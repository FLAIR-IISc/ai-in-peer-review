- Proposes a deep dynamic Poisson factorization model with a simplified RNN-like temporal mechanism, evaluated on five datasets.  
- Overall idea is promising but execution and presentation need significant improvement for a venue like NIPS.  
- Eq. 4 is not a standard RNN: h_t^{(n)} lacks within-layer temporal dependence; clarify motivation and implications.  
- Section 2 omits key model details: Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4 and the distribution generating θ_t in Eq. 3 are unspecified.  
- The paper’s claim of “implicit distributions” is incorrect: Eqs. 9–10 are explicit Gamma distributions computable in closed form.  
- Line 30–31 is unclear: explain “different pattern long-time dependence” and the meaning of “fanatical data” or rephrase with examples.  
- Subsection (lines 133–155) is unclear: define the “loss function” in Eq. 15 precisely (negative ELBO, predictive objective, etc.) and justify it.  
- Evaluation uses inappropriate metrics (MSE/PMSE); report predictive log-likelihood and ranking metrics (e.g., recall@k) instead.  
- Choice of latent dimensionality K is too small; explore larger values (e.g., K ≥ 100) and perform sensitivity analysis.  
- Discussion (lines 224–232) does not clearly relate to Figure 3; explicitly state which figure aspects support the claims.  
- Move detailed variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) to supplement and provide a high-level summary in the main text.  
- Figure 4 caption and visuals are hard to read: enlarge fonts, improve contrast, and make captions self-contained.  
- Writing and style need major fixes: reduce passive voice, correct numerous typos/grammar errors, and improve sentence clarity.  
- Summary recommendation: fix precise model specification, clarify temporal architecture, correct distributional claims, strengthen evaluation, and substantially improve writing.
