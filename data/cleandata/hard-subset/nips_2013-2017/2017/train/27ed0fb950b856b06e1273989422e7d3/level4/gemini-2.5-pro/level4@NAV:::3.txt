This manuscript presents a deep Poisson factorization model designed to capture long-range temporal dependencies. In a departure from previous dynamic Poisson factorization methods, the proposed approach utilizes a structure analogous to a recurrent neural network to model these dependencies. Parameter estimation is performed using variational inference, coupled with optimization routines for the neural network components. While the technical substance of the paper is strong, its quality is diminished by several errors in language and factual accuracy, as detailed below:

1.  The claim on lines 86-87 regarding the modeling of non-negative numbers is incorrect. The method being described corresponds to the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution.

2.  For improved readability, the best-performing results in Table 2 should be highlighted, for instance by using boldface.

3.  In the experiments in Section 4.2, held-out perplexity would have served as a more compelling evaluation metric. Although the reported MSE/PMSE metrics are acceptable, perplexity is often a stronger indicator for this class of models.

4.  The paper effectively addresses the documented limitations of prior work (references 1, 2) in modeling long-range dependencies. However, the justification for the model's multi-level hierarchy could be strengthened. A baseline comparison against a dynamic Poisson Factorization model with a single-level deep architecture would more clearly demonstrate the specific benefits of the proposed hierarchical structure. While the comparison to an LSTM is informative, the fundamental differences in their learning algorithms make it an indirect comparison. Consequently, this experiment does not conclusively validate the necessity of the proposed model's deep hierarchical design.
