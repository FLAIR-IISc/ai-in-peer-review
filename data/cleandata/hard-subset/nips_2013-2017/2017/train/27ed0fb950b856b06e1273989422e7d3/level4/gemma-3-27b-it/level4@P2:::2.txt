This paper introduces a novel Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) designed to capture complex, long-term dependencies in sequential count data. The core idea is to leverage recurrent neural networks to model the implicit distributions within a Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) framework, using gamma distributions to represent temporal dependencies. Specifically, the shape parameters of these gamma distributions are approximated by neural networks, integrating information from both prior (higher layers) and likelihood (lower layers). The authors employ a variational inference approach, updating network parameters based on expectations of latent variables to improve computational efficiency and reduce variance. This approach offers a compelling and potentially effective method for addressing the challenge of long-range dependencies in dynamic models.

The paper’s strength lies in its innovative combination of PFA with deep learning techniques to model temporal dynamics. The use of recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions is a promising direction, and the variational inference approach is a sensible choice for handling the resulting complexity. The motivation for the work – addressing limitations of existing PFA and related models in capturing long-time dependencies – is well-articulated and relevant to the NIPS community, building on prior work in Bayesian factor analysis and deep generative modeling. The paper clearly identifies the shortcomings of models like PGDS [2] which rely on simpler transition structures, and positions DDPFA as a more flexible alternative. It also connects to the broader trend of integrating deep learning with Bayesian methods as seen in works like Deep Exponential Families [8] and Deep Poisson Factor Analysis [9, 10].

However, the manuscript exhibits some areas needing improvement. First, there are several typographical errors, such as in Equation 10, which detract from the paper’s polish and may indicate insufficient attention to detail. Second, the authors’ decision to fix the scale parameter and only update the shape parameter within the gamma distributions warrants a more thorough justification. A detailed discussion of the implications of this choice, and a potential analysis of alternative parameterizations, would strengthen the technical contribution. Third, the experimental evaluation is somewhat limited. The authors only present results for a two-layer model, without a comparative analysis involving single-layer or deeper architectures. This limits our understanding of how the model’s performance scales with depth and whether the increased complexity offers tangible benefits. Furthermore, a sensitivity analysis exploring the impact of other key hyperparameters – such as window size and the number of latent factors – is missing. Including such analyses would improve the robustness and reproducibility of the findings. Finally, while the introduction mentions related work, it omits recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis such as [2, 3], which should be discussed to properly contextualize the contribution. A graphical illustration of the complete model, akin to Figure 1 in [1], would also substantially improve clarity and accessibility.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of PFA with recurrent neural networks for modeling temporal dependencies is a novel approach.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The variational inference framework appears appropriately applied, and the motivation for the method is strong.
*   **Potential Significance:** The model addresses a challenging problem (long-term dependencies in count data) and has the potential to be useful in various applications.
*   **Empirical Results:** The experiments demonstrate promising performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets, outperforming baseline methods in several cases.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Clarity:** The manuscript contains typos and lacks sufficient detail in certain areas (e.g., justification for fixing the gamma scale parameter).
*   **Completeness of Evaluation:** The experimental evaluation is limited, lacking a comprehensive analysis of model depth and hyperparameter sensitivity.
*   **Related Work:** The literature review is not exhaustive and omits recent relevant publications.
*   **Presentation:** The absence of a clear graphical illustration hinders understanding of the model’s architecture.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite its flaws, this paper presents an interesting and potentially valuable contribution to the field. The core idea is sound, and the initial results are promising. However, the manuscript requires significant revisions to address the issues of clarity, completeness, and presentation. I recommend **weak reject** with encouragement to revise and resubmit. Addressing the points raised above – particularly the experimental evaluation and discussion of key design choices – would substantially improve the paper’s quality and increase its likelihood of acceptance in a future iteration.
