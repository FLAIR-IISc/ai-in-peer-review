- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) combining Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs and deep structure to model short- and long-term dependencies in sequential count data.
- Uses variational inference and gradient descent to estimate parameters and optimizes neural network components.
- Evaluates on synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.
- Addresses a relevant, challenging problem important for recommendations, text, and time series with high-dimensional sparse counts.
- Builds on prior deep Bayesian models and correctly identifies limitations of existing methods like PGDS and shallow models.
- Technical formulation and inference procedure appear sound and are clearly articulated.
- Experiments (including synthetic, real data, and ablation study on depth) support the model’s effectiveness and provide useful insights.
- Detailed experimental setup and hyperparameter reporting are valuable for reproducibility.
- Factual inaccuracy: claims about modeling nonnegative numbers are misstated — Poisson-Gamma handles that, not the model as described.
- Writing quality suffers from numerous grammatical errors and typos, reducing readability.
- Comparison to LSTM is limited/unfair due to differing learning algorithms, making hierarchical benefits unclear.
- Missing comparison to a single-layer deep PF model prevents isolating advantages of the deep architecture.
- Evaluation metrics could be strengthened by adding held-out perplexity, especially for real-world experiments.
- Tables are hard to interpret; best results should be bolded for clarity.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, sound methodology, convincing experiments, and helpful ablation study.
- Arguments against acceptance: factual errors, poor writing, unfair baseline comparison, missing shallow baseline, and incomplete evaluation metrics.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept contingent on substantial revisions to fix errors, improve presentation, and strengthen experimental comparisons.
- Specific suggestions: correct the factual inaccuracy, thoroughly proofread, add single-layer PF baseline, include held-out perplexity, bold best results in tables, and clarify links to differentiable variational inference and mean-field limitations.
