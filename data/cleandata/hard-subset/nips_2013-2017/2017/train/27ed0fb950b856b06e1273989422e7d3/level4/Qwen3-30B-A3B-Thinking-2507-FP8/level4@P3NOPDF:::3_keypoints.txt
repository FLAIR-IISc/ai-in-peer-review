- Proposes a novel extension: deep recurrent-like architecture for dynamic Poisson factorization to capture long-range temporal dependencies.  
- Combines Poisson factorization with variational inference and neural-network-style temporal modeling — methodologically sound.  
- Critical factual error: incorrectly states data are modeled with a Poisson distribution; the model uses the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution and this must be corrected.  
- Evaluation metrics are suboptimal: replace or supplement MSE/PMSE with held-out perplexity for better assessment of Poisson models.  
- LSTM comparison is misleading because optimization procedures differ; cannot attribute gains to architecture without controlling inference.  
- Require a controlled baseline: include a single-level (shallow) dynamic PF using the same inference to justify multi-level hierarchy.  
- Numerous grammatical errors and typos throughout the manuscript that impede readability and need thorough proofreading.  
- Presentation fixes: bold best results in Table 2 to improve clarity.  
- Experimental clarity: explain how the deep structure (e.g., number of layers) is selected and report its impact on performance.  
- Limitations section should explicitly state how the proposed approach overcomes prior-work shortcomings.  
- Overall: idea is promising and well-motivated but weakened by factual inaccuracies, poor experimental choices, and presentation issues.  
- Recommendation: Reject, with strong encouragement to resubmit after major revisions addressing the factual error, metric choice, baseline comparisons, grammar, and table formatting.
