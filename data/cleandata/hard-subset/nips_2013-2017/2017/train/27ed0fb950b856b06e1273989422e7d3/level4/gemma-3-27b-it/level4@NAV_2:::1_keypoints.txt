- Paper proposes a deep dynamic Poisson factorization model using a simplified RNN and variational inference, evaluated on five datasets.  
- Eq. 4 does not follow a standard RNN design; author must justify the simplified structure where h_t depends only on top-layer memory vectors.  
- Several model equations are incomplete (e.g., Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4 and the generative distribution for θ_t in Eq. 3), harming reproducibility.  
- The claim that certain distributions are “implicit” is incorrect—Eqs. 9 and 10 specify explicit Gamma distributions and θ_tk can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^(0).  
- The statement about weaknesses on “fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear and needs concrete explanation and examples.  
- The subsection around lines 133–155 lacks clarity; specifically, the meaning and role of the “loss function” in Eq. 15 must be explained.  
- MSE and PMSE are inappropriate evaluation metrics for Poisson factorization models; predictive log-likelihood (at minimum) should be reported.  
- The chosen latent dimensionality K is small; authors should test larger K (e.g., K ≥ 100) to capture richer patterns.  
- The relationship between the discussion in lines 224–232 and Fig. 3 is unclear and requires clarification.  
- Detailed variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) could be moved to the supplement to streamline the main text.  
- Fig. 2’s caption is hard to read and should be improved for clarity.  
- Overall writing quality is poor: excessive passive voice, numerous typos, grammatical errors, and incomplete sentences throughout the manuscript.  
- Positive: the problem is relevant, integrating neural networks with Poisson factorization is novel, and experiments show potential.  
- Negative: incomplete technical details, weak theoretical justification for design choices, inappropriate evaluation metrics, and poor presentation argue against acceptance.  
- Overall recommendation: reject pending substantial revisions to clarity, completeness, evaluation, and writing quality.
