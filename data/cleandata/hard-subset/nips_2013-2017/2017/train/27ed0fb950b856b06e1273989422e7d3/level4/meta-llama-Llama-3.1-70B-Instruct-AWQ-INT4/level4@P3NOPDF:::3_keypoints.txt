- Proposes a novel RNN-like deep Poisson factorization model to capture long-range temporal dependencies, with variational inference and optimization for network parameters.
- Strong technical contribution but weakened by multiple grammatical and factual errors.
- Incorrect factual claim: the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is used for modeling nonnegative numbers, contradicting the paper's statement (lines 86–87).
- Presentation improvement: bold the best results in Table 2 to clarify comparisons.
- Evaluation critique: paper uses MSE and PMSE, but held-out perplexity would be a stronger performance metric.
- Comparison gap: should include a baseline dynamic PF with a one-level deep architecture to justify multiple hierarchy levels.
- LSTM comparison insufficient because differing learning algorithms make it hard to justify multi-level hierarchy benefits.
- Many grammatical errors and typos throughout (examples: "unit the data"→"until the data"; "Examples for this including"→"Examples of this include"; "V-dimesnion sequentially"→"V-dimension sequentially"; "recommend systems"→"recommendation systems"), which impede readability.
- Overall: a valuable and promising method with effective results, but needs clearer justification, more thorough comparisons, and substantial editorial fixes.
