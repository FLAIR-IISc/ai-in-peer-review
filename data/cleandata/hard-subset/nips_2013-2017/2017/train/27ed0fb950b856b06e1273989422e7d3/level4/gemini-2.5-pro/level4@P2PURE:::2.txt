This paper proposes a novel method for capturing long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count data. The core idea is to use a recurrent neural network to model the parameters of a sequence of Gamma distributions. The RNN approximates the time-varying shape parameters of these distributions, integrating top-down information from higher layers (the prior) and bottom-up information from the data (the likelihood). The neural network parameters are updated using the expectation of the latent variables, which effectively reduces sampling variance and improves computational efficiency.

The proposed model is an interesting and intelligent approach to modeling long-term dependencies in dynamic systems. While the core idea is compelling, the manuscript appears to have been prepared in haste. I have the following comments and suggestions for improvement:

1.  There appear to be critical typos in the manuscript, for instance, in Equation 10.
2.  The model fixes the scale parameter of the Gamma distributions and only learns the shape parameter. The motivation and implications of this design choice require more detailed discussion and justification.
3.  The experimental evaluation is limited to a two-layer version of the proposed model. To better understand the impact of model depth, the experiments should include a comparison with single-layer and deeper architectures. Additionally, an analysis of the model's sensitivity to other key hyperparameters, such as the window size and the number of factors, would strengthen the results.
4.  To improve clarity and aid reader understanding, I strongly recommend that the authors include a detailed graphical illustration of the overall model architecture. Figure 1 in [1] serves as a good example of an effective diagram for this type of model.
5.  The introduction should discuss recent and relevant advancements in deep Poisson factor analysis, including models such as those proposed in [2] and [3].
