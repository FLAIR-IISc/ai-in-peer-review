The authors propose a novel method for capturing complex long-time dependencies over count vectors by leveraging recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, specifically through the gamma distribution. The shape parameters of these gamma distributions, which capture time dependence, are approximated by neural networks that aim to integrate information from both prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer). The parameters in the neural network are updated based on the expectation of latent variables, which helps reduce variance caused by sampling and increase computational efficiency. This model provides an interesting and effective approach to handling the long dependency problem in dynamic models. Although I find the idea compelling, the manuscript appears to be somewhat rushed, and I have several comments:

1. There are some critical typos, notably in Equation 10, that need correction.
2. The authors consider the scale parameter as fixed and only update the shape parameter in the gamma distributions. This decision warrants more detailed discussion and analysis to justify the choice.
3. The experimental results only demonstrate the performance of the proposed model with two layers, without comparing it to models with a single layer or more layers. This limited comparison is insufficient to understand the influence of the number of layers on performance. Furthermore, other parameters, such as the size of the window and the number of factors, require more thorough discussion.
4. To facilitate better understanding, I suggest the authors provide detailed graphical illustrations of the entire model. For reference, the authors can consult Figure 1 in [1].
5. Recently, new deep Poisson factor analysis models have been proposed, such as those in [2] and [3]. These models should be discussed in the Introduction to provide a more comprehensive context for the proposed method.

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research, 17(163), 1-44, 2016.
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou, Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC, ICML 2017.
