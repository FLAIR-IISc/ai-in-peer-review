## Review of NIPS 2017 Submission

This paper presents a novel approach to incorporating long-range temporal dependencies within a Poisson Factorization (PF) model by leveraging a deep, recurrent neural network structure. Existing dynamic PF methods often struggle to capture these long-term relationships; this work addresses this limitation through a compellingly structured inference procedure utilizing variational inference and optimization techniques tailored for the neural network parameters. The technical content is generally strong, demonstrating a clear understanding of both PF and recurrent neural networks. However, the paper suffers from several grammatical errors and minor factual inaccuracies that detract from its overall clarity and impact.

The core contribution lies in moving beyond traditional dynamic PF formulations by replacing simpler temporal models with a more expressive recurrent architecture. This allows the model to learn and exploit complex, long-term patterns within the data. The proposed method shows promise, and the results presented are encouraging. 

However, several points require attention. First, the statement on lines 86-87 regarding modeling nonnegative numbers is inaccurate. The Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution, not the method described, is typically used for this purpose. This should be corrected. Second, for improved readability, please **bold** the best results in Table 2 to clearly highlight performance gains. 

While the experimental evaluation in Section 4.2 demonstrates reasonable performance using MSE and PMSE, held-out perplexity would have served as a more informative metric for assessing the model’s ability to capture the underlying data distribution and long-range dependencies. Including perplexity would strengthen the analysis.

The paper effectively addresses the limitations of existing work (references 1 and 2) in modeling long-range dependency. However, a more direct comparison would be beneficial. Specifically, a comparison against a dynamic PF model with a single-layer deep architecture could better illustrate the advantage of the proposed multi-level hierarchy. The comparison with LSTM is valuable, but due to the fundamentally different learning algorithms employed (variational inference vs. backpropagation), it doesn’t fully justify the need for the proposed hierarchical structure. Demonstrating improvement over a more closely related baseline would provide stronger support for the design choices.

Finally, the manuscript contains numerous grammatical errors and typos that hinder readability. While not individually critical, their cumulative effect is significant. Specific examples include:

*   "unit the data"
*   "Noticing the lack of capturing .."
*   "PFA factorized a count matrix" (inconsistent tense)
*   "other data with different pattern long-time dependence .."
*   "Examples for this including .."
*   "shows excellent results on predict and .."
*   "However, it is rough for them .."
*   "V-dimesnion sequentially .."
*   "K-dimension latent variable .."
*   "due to the including of feature-wise"
*   "recommend systems" etc.

A thorough proofread is strongly recommended to address these issues.

**Overall:**

This paper presents an interesting and technically sound contribution to the field of dynamic Poisson factorization. The proposed method offers a potentially powerful approach to modeling long-range temporal dependencies. However, addressing the factual inaccuracy, strengthening the experimental evaluation with perplexity, and including a more direct comparison to a single-layer dynamic PF model would significantly enhance the paper’s impact. Furthermore, careful attention to grammatical detail is crucial to improve clarity and readability. 

I recommend acceptance contingent upon addressing the points raised above.
