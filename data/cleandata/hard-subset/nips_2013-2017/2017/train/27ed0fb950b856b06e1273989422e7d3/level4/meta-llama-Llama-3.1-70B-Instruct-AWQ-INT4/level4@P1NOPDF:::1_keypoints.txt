- Overall assessment: idea is interesting but the paper’s execution and clarity need substantial improvement before acceptance.

- Model/RNN structure: Eq. 4 does not match a standard RNN — h_t^{(n)} depends only on top-layer memory vectors and not on previous time steps for the same layer; author should justify this simplification.

- Missing model details: Section 2 omits equations/parameterizations (e.g., Gamma in Eq. 4 and the generative form of theta_t in Eq. 3), making the model unclear.

- Mislabeling of distributions: Claims about “implicit distributions” are incorrect — Eqs. 9 and 10 are explicit Gamma distributions and not implicit.

- Unclear example/limitations: The sentence about “fanatical data and disaster data” is vague — clarify what data types are meant and why the method might fail on them.

- Loss function ambiguity: The subsection around lines 133–155 is unclear — explicitly state what Eq. 15’s loss is and whether it’s a standard VI objective.

- Evaluation metric: MSE/PMSE is not appropriate for PF-like models; include predictive log-likelihood (at minimum) for a more complete evaluation.

- Latent dimensionality K too small: Experimental K is likely too low; recommend using larger values (e.g., K ≥ 100) to capture structure.

- Figure-text mismatch: The discussion in lines 224–232 does not clearly connect to Fig. 3 — clarify how the figure supports the claims.

- Move derivations to supplement: The variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) are non-essential and can be moved to the appendix.

- Figure readability: Fig. 4’s caption/labels are hard to read — increase font size and improve description.

- Writing and style: Use active voice (especially in the abstract) and improve overall writing quality.

- Typos and language errors: Many grammatical errors and word-choice issues throughout (several line-specific examples provided) that require revision.
