- Proposes a dynamic count model using RNNs to parameterize gamma latent-variable shape parameters to capture long-range temporal dependencies.
- Fuses higher-layer priors and lower-layer likelihoods through the RNN to produce time-varying shapes while keeping gamma scale fixed.
- Updates neural-network parameters using expectations of latent variables to reduce gradient variance and improve efficiency.
- Novel idea: RNN-driven gamma-shape parameterization for dynamic counts addressing long-term dependencies.
- Practical variance reduction by updating with expected latents rather than sampled latents.
- Modeling choice is appropriate: gamma–Poisson structure captures over-dispersion and is extendable to deeper hierarchies.
- Typographical and clarity issues: critical typos (e.g., Eq.10) and ambiguous notation—define scale vs. rate and indices early.
- Fixed scale parameter needs justification: discuss identifiability, training stability, expressiveness loss, sensitivity, and ablation (fixed vs. learned or shared scales).
- Experimental weaknesses: require depth ablations (1–3+ layers), sensitivity analyses (window size, factors, hidden dims), stronger baselines, and forecasting/predictive metrics.
- Efficiency and stability: report wall-clock time, memory by sequence length/depth, gradient norms, and early-stopping behavior.
- Model description should include a clear graphical model, detailed update equations (how expectations are computed), E/M analogy, gradient bias, and positivity constraints.
- Address posterior collapse/degenerate solutions and clarify enforcement of positivity when mapping RNN outputs to gamma parameters.
- Expand related work to include recent deep Poisson/gamma models, gamma belief networks, deep LDA with adaptive MCMC, and recurrent latent variable models; clarify distinctions.
- Discuss negative-binomial interpretation, time-varying dispersion, handling zero-inflation or very sparse counts, and robustness considerations.
- Reproducibility: provide training details (optimizers, batch sizes, learning rates, initialization), early-stopping criteria, and code release.
- Minor fixes: correct Eq.10 and numbering, ensure consistent notation for time/layer/parameters, clarify datasets and preprocessing, improve writing, and add limitations/future work.
- Overall: promising and timely framework with practical variance-reduction, but needs stronger empirical validation, clearer exposition, and justification for fixing the gamma scale.
