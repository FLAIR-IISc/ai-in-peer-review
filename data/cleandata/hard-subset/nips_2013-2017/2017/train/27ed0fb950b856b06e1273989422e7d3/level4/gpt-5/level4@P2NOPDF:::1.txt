This paper introduces a deep dynamic Poisson factorization model that extends PF to capture temporal dependencies. Unlike prior dynamic PF work, it employs a simplified recurrent neural network to model long-term dependencies. Inference is performed via variational inference with an additional optimization step for the neural network parameters. Experiments are presented on five real-world datasets.

Overall, the idea is potentially interesting, but the current execution requires significant clarification and revision before it is ready for a venue like NIPS. Detailed comments follow.

- RNN structure (Eq. 4): The formulation does not reflect a standard RNN because h_t^{(n)} appears to depend only on the top-layer memory vectors, without recurrent dependence on previous time steps within the same layer. Please justify this simplified architecture and discuss its implications for modeling temporal dynamics and long-term dependencies.

- Missing model details (Section 2): Some key equations are absent or underspecified. For example, it is unclear how the Gamma in Eq. 4 is parameterized (which variables determine its shape and rate), and what distribution generates theta_t in Eq. 3. Please provide the full generative process with explicit parameterizations.

- “Implicit distributions” (lines 110–118): I disagree with the characterization. Eqs. 9 and 10 define Gamma distributions with explicit shapes and rates. The distribution of theta_tk (Eq. 9), conditioned on h^{(0)}, is available in closed form and therefore is not implicit; the same applies to h in Eq. 10. Please revise the terminology and the accompanying discussion.

- Ambiguity (lines 30–31): The sentence “This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear. Please clarify what types of data you mean (e.g., if “fanatical” is a typo, correct it), what specific dependence structures are challenging, and why.

- Learning objective (lines 133–155; Eq. 15): The subsection is hard to follow. What precisely is the “loss function” in Eq. 15? Is it the negative ELBO, a surrogate bound, or something else? Please define all terms, state constraints, and describe the optimization strategy (e.g., coordinate ascent, stochastic gradients, alternating updates) and how the neural network parameters are integrated.

- Evaluation metrics: MSE/PMSE are typically not informative for PF-like models. Please report predictive log-likelihood on held-out data (or held-out perplexity) and, if relevant, ranking metrics (e.g., AUC/NDCG) for recommendation-style tasks.

- Model capacity (line 207): The latent dimensionality K seems too small. Please include results with larger K (e.g., K ≥ 100) and provide a sensitivity analysis to demonstrate robustness and capacity-accuracy trade-offs.

- Interpretation of Fig. 3 (lines 224–232): The text does not clearly connect to the figure. Please explain how the plotted quantities correspond to the described phenomena, label axes and units, and provide a concise takeaway.

- Placement of update equations (Eqs. 11–14): These routine variational update equations can be moved to the supplement to improve readability; the main text can summarize their form and intuition.

- Figure quality: The caption of Fig. 4 is difficult to read. Please increase font size, improve contrast, and ensure all elements are legible in grayscale and color.

- Writing and style: The manuscript would benefit from substantial editing. In particular, reduce passive voice (especially in the abstract), fix incomplete sentences, and add missing articles. A few examples of typos and phrasing issues:
  - Line 15: “high-dimensional”
  - Line 22: “factorize”
  - Line 24: “property of the”
  - Lines 29, 34: “Dirichlet”
  - Line 34: “nested”
  - Fig. 1: “visual representation”
  - Fig. 1: “transmission” may not be the appropriate term
  - Line 49: incomplete sentence
  - Frequent missing articles (e.g., “Although the Dirichlet distribution is often used as a prior distribution”)

In summary, the modeling direction is promising, but the paper needs clearer model specification, corrected terminology, a precise training objective, stronger and more appropriate evaluation metrics, improved figures, and substantial language editing.
