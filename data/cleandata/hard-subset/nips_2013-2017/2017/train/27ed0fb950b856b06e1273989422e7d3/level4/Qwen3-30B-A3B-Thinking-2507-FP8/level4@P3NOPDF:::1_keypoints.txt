- Paper proposes DDPF (deep dynamic Poisson factorization) using a simplified RNN to model temporal dependencies; core idea is promising but underdeveloped.  
- RNN design (Eq. 4) is flawed: hidden state h_t depends only on top-layer memory vectors and not on h_{t-1}, contradicting standard recurrence.  
- Authors must justify the non-recurrent simplification or revise the model to include temporal dependence.  
- Key model details are missing: Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4 and the distribution generating θ_t (Eq. 3) are unspecified.  
- The claim about “implicit distributions” is incorrect—Eqs. 9–10 explicitly define Gamma distributions, so the terminology is misleading.  
- Experimental evaluation is inadequate: MSE/PMSE is not appropriate for PF models; predictive log-likelihood should be the primary metric.  
- Model capacity testing is insufficient: using K=10 is unrealistically small for real-world datasets; test K≥100 (or larger) as in PF literature.  
- Figure and result interpretation issues: discussion of Fig. 3 is disconnected from the figure and must be explicitly explained.  
- Loss function in Eq. 15 is undefined—components and derivation need clarification.  
- Writing quality is poor with many grammatical errors, typos, unclear phrases (e.g., “fanatical data”), and excessive passive voice.  
- Figures are poorly presented—Fig. 4’s caption is illegible and figures need clearer references and explanations.  
- Recommended revisions: clarify/repair RNN structure, fully specify generative model and Gamma parameters, and correct the “implicit” distribution claim.  
- Recommended evaluation fixes: report predictive log-likelihood, test larger K values, and align metrics with PF conventions.  
- Presentation fixes: correct writing, clarify ambiguous terms, improve figure quality/captions, and consider moving variational update equations to the supplement.  
- Conclusion: DDPF has potential but currently contains fundamental methodological gaps, inadequate evaluation, and poor presentation; recommend rejection pending major revision.
