- Paper introduces deep dynamic Poisson factorization with a simplified RNN-like structure, variational inference plus optimization of neural network parameters, and experiments on five datasets; idea is potentially interesting but needs substantial improvements.

- Eq. 4 does not implement a standard RNN: h_t^(n) depends only on top-layer memory vectors, not on previous time steps within the same layer—reason for this simplification unclear.

- Several equations in Section 2 are incomplete or underspecified (e.g., Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4 and the distribution generating θ_t in Eq. 3).

- The authors’ claim about “implicit distributions” is incorrect: Eqs. 9 and 10 define explicit Gamma distributions and θ_tk can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^(0).

- The sentence about weaknesses for “fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear and needs elaboration on the specific weaknesses and data types affected.

- The subsection covering lines 133–155 is unclear; it is not stated what precisely constitutes the “loss function” in Eq. 15.

- MSE/PMSE are inappropriate evaluation metrics for PF-like models; at minimum, predictive log-likelihood should be reported.

- The chosen latent dimension K in experiments appears too small; authors should evaluate larger values (e.g., K = 100 or higher).

- The connection between the discussion in lines 224–232 and Figure 3 is unclear and requires clarification.

- The variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) are low-level details that could be moved to the supplementary material.

- The caption for Figure 4 is illegible and must be fixed.

- Abstract and overall writing rely overly on passive voice; authors should revise for clearer active phrasing.

- Overall writing quality is poor with numerous typos, incomplete sentences, and missing articles; several specific wording errors and figure label issues are listed by the reviewer.
