- Summary: Proposes a deep recurrent extension of Poisson factorization to capture long-range temporal dependence, with variational inference and gradient-based optimization for neural parameters.

- Strength: Addresses limited temporal memory in dynamic PF by introducing a recurrent mechanism.

- Strength: Hierarchical multi-level latent structure is well-motivated and may improve expressivity for complex temporal patterns.

- Strength: Empirical results show gains over several baselines, indicating practical value.

- Concern 1 (factual): Correct the claim about modeling nonnegative numbers — the Poisson randomized gamma (PRG) distribution is used for nonnegative real-valued data; revise text and cite appropriately.

- Concern 2 (evaluation): Add held-out predictive likelihood/perplexity with a clear held-out protocol across time and dimensions; keep MSE/PMSE as secondary metrics.

- Concern 3 (ablation/baselines): Include a depth=1 dynamic PF baseline and ablations over depth (e.g., 1,2,3 layers), reporting performance and parameter counts to control for capacity.

- Concern 4 (RNN design): Clarify recurrent mechanism and how vanishing gradients are mitigated (gating, skips, residuals, layer norm, dilations) and report sensitivity to sequence length/backprop-through-time horizon.

- Concern 5 (variational inference & reproducibility): Provide the explicit ELBO (including recurrent transition terms), variational family, amortization/reparameterization details, KL annealing/gradient clipping, optimization settings, initialization, convergence diagnostics, complexity per iteration (T,K,V), and wall-clock/memory usage; consider releasing code/pseudocode.

- Concern 6 (data & experiments): Describe preprocessing and train/validation/test splits across time, held-out construction for perplexity, include uncertainty estimates (std. errors over runs), and control for total parameter count across baselines.

- Concern 7 (presentation): Improve exposition of generative process and inference (e.g., compact diagram or step-by-step listing) and bold best results in Table 2 for readability.

- Typos/language: Fix numerous phrasing errors (e.g., “unit the data,” “Noticing the lack…,” “V-dimesnion”) and improve tense/wording consistency throughout.

- Overall: Promising and timely idea that would be significantly strengthened by correcting PRG statements, adding held-out perplexity, depth ablations, clearer recurrent/inference details, and careful proofreading for reproducibility and clarity.
