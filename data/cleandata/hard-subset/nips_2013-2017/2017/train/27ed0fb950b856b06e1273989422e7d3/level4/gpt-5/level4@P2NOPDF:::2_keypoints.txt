- Proposes modeling long-range temporal dependence in count vectors via RNN-parameterized gamma latent variables, with temporal dependence captured in the gamma shape parameters.  
- Shape parameters are approximated by neural networks that integrate prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) information.  
- Network parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables, reducing sampling variance and improving computational efficiency.  
- Overall approach is promising and offers a clever way to handle long-term dependencies in dynamic count models.  
- Manuscript appears rushed and contains critical typos and notation errors (e.g., Equation (10)); needs careful proofreading.  
- The model fixes the gamma scale parameter and updates only the shape—this choice must be justified theoretically and empirically; include ablation vs. learned scales or alternative parameterizations.  
- Experiments only evaluate a two-layer model; compare against single-layer and deeper variants and analyze sensitivity to hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).  
- Include a detailed graphical illustration of the full model showing hierarchical structure and temporal dependencies.  
- Introduction should discuss recent deep Poisson factorization and related work (e.g., Augmentable Gamma Belief Networks, Deep LDA) and clarify contributions relative to that literature.
