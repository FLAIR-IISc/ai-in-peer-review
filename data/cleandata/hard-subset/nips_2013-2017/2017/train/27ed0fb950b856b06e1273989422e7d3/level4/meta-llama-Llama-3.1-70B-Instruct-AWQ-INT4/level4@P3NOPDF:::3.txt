This paper proposes a novel approach to incorporating long-range temporal dependence in a Poisson factorization model with deep structure. Unlike previous works on dynamic Poisson Factorization (PF), the authors employ a recurrent neural network-like structure to model long-term dependencies. The inference is carried out using variational inference with optimization routines to find the parameters of the neural network.

The paper has good technical content, but it is marred by several grammatical and factual errors. Specifically:

1. The statement on lines 86-87 regarding the modeling of nonnegative numbers is incorrect. The Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is actually used for this purpose.
2. To improve the clarity of the results, it would be helpful to bold the best results in Table 2.
3. While the authors use Mean Squared Error (MSE) and Predicted Mean Squared Error (PMSE) as evaluation metrics in Section 4.2, held-out perplexity would have been a stronger indicator of the model's performance.
4. The paper adequately addresses the limitations of existing works in modeling long-range dependencies (references 1 and 2). However, to demonstrate the utility of the proposed method with multiple levels of hierarchy, a comparison with dynamic PF using a one-level deep architecture would have been useful. Although the comparison with Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) is provided, the learning algorithms are different, making it difficult to justify the need for multiple levels of hierarchy in the proposed method.
5. There are several grammatical errors and typos throughout the paper, including:
   - "unit the data" should be "until the data"
   - "Noticing the lack of capturing" should be rephrased for clarity
   - The tense of the sentence "PFA factorized a count matrix" is inconsistent with the rest of the paragraph
   - "other data with different pattern long-time dependence" should be rephrased for clarity
   - "Examples for this including" should be "Examples of this include"
   - "shows excellent results on predict and" should be "shows excellent results in predicting and"
   - "However, it is rough for them" should be rephrased for clarity
   - "V-dimesnion sequentially" should be "V-dimension sequentially"
   - "K-dimension latent variable" should be "K-dimensional latent variable"
   - "due to the including of feature-wise" should be rephrased for clarity
   - "recommend systems" should be "recommendation systems"

These errors make the paper somewhat difficult to read and understand. Addressing these issues would significantly improve the overall quality of the paper.

In terms of the paper's content, the authors have made a valuable contribution to the field of dynamic Poisson Factorization by proposing a novel approach to modeling long-range temporal dependencies. The use of a recurrent neural network-like structure is a promising direction, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison with existing methods and a clearer justification of the need for multiple levels of hierarchy.
