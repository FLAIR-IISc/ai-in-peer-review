- Introduces DDPFA: a deep hierarchical Poisson Factorization using RNNs to model complex, long-range temporal dependencies with hybrid inference (mean-field variational inference + gradient-based NN optimization).  
- Strength: effectively addresses limitation of prior dynamic PF models by integrating RNN-like structure to capture dependencies beyond a single time step.  
- Weakness: Insufficient justification for the deep hierarchy—no ablation study comparing to a shallow (single-layer) DDPFA to isolate the benefit of depth.  
- Weakness: Comparison to an LSTM is not fully convincing because the models use fundamentally different learning algorithms (variational inference vs. BPTT).  
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics are weak—uses MSE/PMSE; should report held-out perplexity or log-likelihood for probabilistic count models.  
- Weakness: Factual correction needed—modeling non-count real-valued data via compound Poisson/Gamma networks is better stated using the Poisson-Randomized Gamma distribution.  
- Weakness: Clarity and readability suffer from numerous grammatical errors and typos; requires thorough proofreading (many example phrases cited).  
- Weakness: Presentation of results could be improved (e.g., highlight best-performing method in Table 2 with boldface).  
- Overall: Technically interesting contribution but experimental validation and manuscript presentation need substantial improvement.
