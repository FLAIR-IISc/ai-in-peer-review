This paper introduces a deep Poisson factorization model incorporating recurrent neural network structures to capture long-range temporal dependencies. Unlike prior work on dynamic Poisson factorization, this approach leverages a recurrent architecture for modeling these dependencies, with parameter inference performed via variational inference and associated optimization techniques. While the paper presents sound technical content, it contains several grammatical and factual inaccuracies, detailed below:

1. The assertion regarding the modeling of nonnegative numbers (lines 86-87) is inaccurate; the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is the appropriate method for this purpose.
2. For improved clarity, please bold the best-performing results in Table 2.
3. Held-out perplexity would have provided a more compelling evaluation metric in Section 4.2, though the reported MSE and PMSE results are acceptable.
4. The paper effectively addresses limitations of existing methods for modeling long-range dependencies (references 1, 2). However, to better demonstrate the benefits of the proposed multi-level hierarchical structure, a comparison with a dynamic Poisson factorization model employing a single-layer deep architecture would be beneficial. The comparison to LSTM is valuable, but differences in learning algorithms limit its ability to directly justify the necessity of multiple hierarchical levels.
