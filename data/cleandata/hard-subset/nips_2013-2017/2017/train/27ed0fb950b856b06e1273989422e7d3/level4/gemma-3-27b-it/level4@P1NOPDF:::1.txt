This paper introduces a deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, which builds upon Poisson factorization to model temporal dependencies. Unlike previous work on dynamic Poisson factorization, this paper employs a simplified recurrent neural network to capture long-term dependencies. Inference is performed using variational inference, with an additional step to optimize the neural network parameters. The paper reports experimental results on five real-world datasets.

Overall, I find the idea potentially interesting, but I believe the paper requires further refinement before it would be suitable for acceptance at a conference like NIPS. My specific comments are detailed below.

+The formulation in Eq. 4 does not correspond to a standard RNN structure. The hidden state h<sub>t</sub><sup>(n)</sup> depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and not on previous time steps within the same layer, as would be expected in an RNN. Is there a specific reason for this simplification?

+In Section 2, several model equations are missing crucial details. For example, it is unclear how the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 is parameterized, and the distribution generating θ<sub>t</sub> in Eq. 3 is not specified.

+I disagree with the discussion in lines 110-118 regarding “implicit distributions.” Equations 9 and 10 are, in fact, explicit: they represent Gamma distributions with defined shapes and rates. Since the distribution of θ<sub>tk</sub> (Eq. 9) can be computed in closed form conditioned on h<sup>(0)</sup>, it cannot be considered implicit. The same applies to h in Eq. 10.

+Lines 30-31: The sentence “This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear. Could the authors please elaborate on these limitations?

+The subsection in lines 133-155 is not readily understandable. Specifically, what is the “loss function” in Eq. 15?

+MSE/PMSE are typically not ideal metrics for Poisson factorization-like models. The results should also include at least the predictive log-likelihood.

+The value of K used in the experiments (line 207) appears too small. I suggest exploring larger values, such as K=100 or greater.

+I was unable to connect the discussion in lines 224-232 to Fig. 3; the relationship is not clear.

+The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11-14) can be moved to the supplementary material.

+The caption for Fig. 4 is difficult to read.

+I strongly recommend that the authors consult general writing guidelines regarding the use of the passive voice, particularly in the abstract.

+The writing quality needs substantial improvement, and the paper contains numerous typos. Here are a few examples:
 -Line 15, “high-dimensional”
 -Line 22, “factorize” should be “factorize” or “decompose”
 -Line 24, “property of the”
 -Lines 29, 34, “Dirichlet” (ensure consistency in capitalization/use of definite article - e.g., "the Dirichlet distribution")
 -Line 34, “nested”
 -Fig. 1, “visual representation”
 -Fig. 1, “transmission” (consider a more appropriate term)
 -Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
 -Missing articles (e.g., “Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution” should be “Although the Dirichlet distribution is often used as a prior distribution”)
