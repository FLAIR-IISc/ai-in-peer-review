This paper explores the integration of long-range temporal dependence into a Poisson factorization model with a deep structure. Notably, it diverges from previous dynamic PF works by utilizing a recurrent neural network-like structure to model long-term dependencies. Variational inference, accompanied by optimization routines for parameter estimation, is employed for inference. While the paper showcases promising technical content, it is marred by grammatical and factual inaccuracies.

Specifically, the following issues were identified:
1. The assertion regarding the modeling of nonnegative numbers on lines 86-87 is incorrect; it is actually the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution that facilitates this modeling.
2. It would be beneficial to highlight the best results in **bold** in Table 2 for clarity.
3. Held-out perplexity could have served as a more robust indicator in the experiments presented in Section 4.2, although MSE/PMSE are acceptable.
4. The paper adequately addresses the limitations of existing works in modeling long-range dependencies (references 1 and 2). However, a comparison with dynamic PF featuring a one-level deep architecture would have been valuable in demonstrating the utility of the proposed method with multiple levels of hierarchy. While the comparison with LSTM is useful, the differing learning algorithms render this comparison inconclusive in justifying the necessity of multiple levels of hierarchy in the proposed method.
