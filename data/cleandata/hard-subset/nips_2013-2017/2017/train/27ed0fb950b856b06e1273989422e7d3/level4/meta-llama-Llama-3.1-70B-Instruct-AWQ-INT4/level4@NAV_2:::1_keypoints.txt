This paper introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) to extend Poisson Factor Analysis for temporal count data using a recurrent neural network structure and variational inference.

Model leverages a deep RNN-like structure to capture complex temporal dependencies between latent factors.

Authors claim to represent implicit distributions with the RNN and use variational inference for parameter estimation and training.

Thorough related-work review highlighting limitations of existing models in capturing long-range temporal dependencies.

Experimental evaluation on synthetic and real datasets shows DDPFA outperforms PGDS, LSTM, and PFA in the presented experiments.

Strength: novel approach to modeling temporal dependencies in count data.

Strength: extensive evaluation across multiple datasets demonstrating empirical effectiveness.

Strength: ability to capture complex relationships between latent factors over time.

Weakness: RNN formulation (Eq.4) is nonstandard—depends only on top-layer memory vectors and not on previous time steps for the same layer; needs clarification.

Weakness: several model equations are missing or unclear (e.g., Gamma parameterization in Eq.4 and the distribution generating theta_t in Eq.3); request more transparent derivations.

Weakness: description of "implicit distributions" is misleading since Eqs.9–10 specify explicit Gamma distributions; this section should be revised.

Weakness: writing quality and clarity are poor with typos, incomplete sentences, and missing articles; needs thorough proofreading.

Argument for acceptance: novel methodology with promising empirical results and potential impact on Poisson Factor Analysis.

Argument against acceptance: significant issues in clarity, transparency, RNN formulation, and missing evaluation metrics (e.g., predictive log-likelihood) that require revision.
