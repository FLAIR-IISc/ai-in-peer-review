## Review of NIPS 2017 Submission

This paper proposes a novel method for modeling complex long-time dependencies in count vector data using recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to represent implicit distributions. Specifically, the authors leverage gamma distributions, approximating their shape parameters with neural networks that integrate information from both prior (higher layers) and likelihood (lower layers). A key contribution lies in updating the network parameters based on the expectation of latent variables, reducing variance and improving computational efficiency compared to sampling-based approaches. The proposed model offers a promising and insightful approach to address the challenge of long-range dependencies within dynamic models and, overall, I find the core idea compelling. However, the manuscript requires some refinement in terms of clarity, detail, and experimental analysis.

Here are my detailed comments:

**1. Presentation and Typos:** The manuscript exhibits a degree of haste in its presentation. I encountered several typos, including one in Equation 10, which need to be carefully addressed. A thorough proofread is strongly recommended.

**2. Model Assumptions & Justification:** The authors’ decision to fix the scale parameter of the gamma distributions while only updating the shape parameter warrants more detailed discussion and analysis. The rationale behind this design choice should be clearly articulated. What are the potential implications of fixing the scale parameter? How might it impact model flexibility and performance? A brief sensitivity analysis, or at least a discussion of its potential limitations, would strengthen the paper.

**3. Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental results are currently insufficient to fully evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The authors only present results for a two-layer model. A comparative analysis with models utilizing a single layer, and potentially models with more layers, is crucial to understand the impact of network depth on performance. This would help to ascertain whether the benefits observed are truly attributable to the recurrent structure and the specific parameterization, or merely a result of model capacity. Furthermore, a discussion of the sensitivity to other key hyperparameters – such as window size and the number of latent factors – is needed. How do these parameters affect the model’s behavior and performance?

**4. Model Visualization:** A clearer visual representation of the complete model architecture would significantly aid understanding. While the description is adequate, a detailed graphical illustration would be invaluable. The authors could draw inspiration from Figure 1 in Chung et al. (2015) [1] as a starting point for structuring such a diagram. A clear illustration showing the flow of information and the interactions between the RNN, gamma distributions, and latent variables is highly recommended.

**5. Related Work:** The introduction should be expanded to include a discussion of recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis. Specifically, the authors should acknowledge and contrast their work with models such as those presented in Zhou et al. (2016) [2] and Cong et al. (2017) [3]. Positioning the proposed model within the broader landscape of related research will highlight its unique contributions and differentiate it from existing approaches.

**Overall:**

This paper presents an interesting and potentially impactful approach to modeling temporal dependencies in count data. The core idea of utilizing RNNs to parameterize implicit distributions is novel and promising. However, the manuscript would benefit from a more careful presentation, a more thorough justification of key design choices, a more comprehensive experimental evaluation, and a more complete review of related work. Addressing these points will substantially strengthen the paper and increase its potential for positive impact.



**References:**

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research,17(163), 1-44, 2016.
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou, Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC, ICML 2017.
