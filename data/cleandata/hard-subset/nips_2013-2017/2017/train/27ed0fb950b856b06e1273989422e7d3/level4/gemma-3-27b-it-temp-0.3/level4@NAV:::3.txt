This paper introduces a novel approach to incorporating long-range temporal dependencies within a Poisson factorization (PF) model, leveraging a deep, recurrent neural network structure. Unlike prior work in dynamic PF, this method explicitly models long-term dependencies using a recurrent architecture, with parameter inference performed via variational inference and associated optimization techniques. The paper presents strong technical content, though it suffers from several grammatical and factual inaccuracies, detailed below.

1. The assertion regarding the modeling of nonnegative numbers (lines 86-87) is inaccurate; the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is the appropriate method for this purpose.
2. For clarity, please highlight the best performing results in bold within Table 2.
3. While the reported MSE/PMSE results are acceptable, the held-out perplexity metric in Section 4.2 should demonstrate a more substantial improvement to convincingly validate the experimental findings.
4. The paper effectively addresses limitations of existing methods (references 1, 2) regarding the modeling of long-range dependencies. However, to further demonstrate the benefits of the proposed hierarchical structure, a comparison against a dynamic PF model with a single-layer deep architecture would be valuable. The comparison to LSTM is insightful, but differences in learning algorithms limit its ability to fully justify the necessity of the proposed multi-level hierarchy.
