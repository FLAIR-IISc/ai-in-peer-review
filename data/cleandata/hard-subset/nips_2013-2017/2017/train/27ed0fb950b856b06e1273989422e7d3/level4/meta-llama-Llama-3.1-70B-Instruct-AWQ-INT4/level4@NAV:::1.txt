This paper introduces a deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, extending traditional Poisson factorization (PF) to incorporate temporal dependencies. Unlike previous dynamic PF works, this model employs a simplified recurrent neural network (RNN) structure to capture long-term dependencies. Variational inference is used for model inference, with an additional step to optimize the neural network parameters. Experimental results are presented on five real-world datasets.

While I found the idea intriguing, I believe the paper requires improvements in execution to meet the standards of a conference like NIPS. My detailed comments are as follows:

* The formulation in Eq. 4 does not align with a traditional RNN structure, as the hidden state h_t^(n) only depends on top-layer memory vectors and not on previous time steps for the same layer. Is there a specific reason for adopting this simplified structure?
* Section 2 lacks some model equations, such as the parameterization of the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 and the distribution generating theta_t in Eq. 3. Providing these details would enhance clarity.
* I disagree with the discussion on "implicit distributions" in lines 110-118. Eqs. 9 and 10 explicitly define Gamma distributions with given shapes and rates. Since the distribution of theta_tk (Eq. 9) can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^(0), it cannot be considered implicit. A similar comment applies to h in Eq. 10.
* The sentence in lines 30-31, "This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data," is unclear. Could the authors provide further explanation?
* The subsection in lines 133-155 is unclear, particularly the definition of the "loss function" in Eq. 15.
* Mean squared error (MSE) and predictive mean squared error (PMSE) are not ideal metrics for PF-like models. The authors should consider including predictive log-likelihood in the results.
* The chosen value of K in the experiments (line 207) appears too small. I recommend using larger values, such as K=100 (at least).
* The discussion in lines 224-232 does not clearly connect with Fig. 3.
* The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11-14) could be moved to the supplement without affecting the main text.
* The caption for Fig. 4 is difficult to read.
* I strongly advise the authors to follow general writing guidelines, particularly regarding the use of the passive voice (especially in the abstract).
* The writing quality is poor, with numerous typos and grammatical errors, including:
