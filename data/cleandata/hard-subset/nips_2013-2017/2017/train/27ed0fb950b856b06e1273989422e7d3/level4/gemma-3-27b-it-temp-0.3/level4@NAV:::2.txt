The authors present a novel approach to model long-term dependencies in count vector data by leveraging recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions. Specifically, they utilize gamma distributions, with their shape parameters – which govern temporal dynamics – approximated by neural networks integrating information from both prior (higher layers) and likelihood (lower layers). A key contribution is the use of expectation-based updates for the neural network parameters, effectively reducing sampling variance and improving computational efficiency. This model offers a compelling and insightful solution to the challenge of long-range dependencies in dynamic models. However, the manuscript appears somewhat hastily written, and I have several suggestions for improvement:

1. The manuscript contains notable typographical errors, such as in Equation 10.
2. The authors’ decision to fix the scale parameter of the gamma distributions while only updating the shape parameter requires further justification and analysis. A more detailed discussion of this choice is needed.
3. The experimental evaluation focuses solely on a two-layer model. To fully understand the impact of network depth, the authors should include comparisons with single-layer and deeper architectures. Furthermore, a discussion of the sensitivity to other key parameters, such as window size and the number of latent factors, is necessary.
4. A clear, detailed graphical illustration of the complete model would significantly enhance understanding. The authors might find Figure 1 of [1] a useful reference point.
5. The introduction should acknowledge recent advancements in deep Poisson factor analysis, including models such as those presented in [2] and [3].

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research,17(163), 1-44, 2016.
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou, Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC, ICML 2017.
