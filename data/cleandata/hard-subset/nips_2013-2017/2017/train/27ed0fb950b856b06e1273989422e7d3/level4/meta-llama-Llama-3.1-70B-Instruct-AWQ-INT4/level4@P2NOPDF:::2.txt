The authors propose a novel method to capture complex long-time dependencies over count vectors by leveraging recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, realized through the gamma distribution. The shape parameters of these gamma distributions, which capture time dependence, are approximated by neural networks that incorporate information from both prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) sources. The parameters in the neural network are updated based on the expectation of latent variables, which helps reduce variance caused by sampling and increase computational efficiency. This model provides an interesting and intelligent approach to addressing the long dependency problem in dynamic models.

Although I find the proposed method intriguing, the manuscript appears to be somewhat hastily written, and I have several comments for improvement:

1. There are some critical typos, such as in Equation 10, which need to be corrected.
2. The authors consider the scale parameter as fixed and only update the shape parameter in the gamma distributions. This approach requires more detailed discussion and analysis to justify the decision.
3. The experimental evaluation only demonstrates the performance of the proposed model with two layers, without comparing it to models with a single layer or more layers. This limited comparison is insufficient to understand the impact of layer depth on performance. Furthermore, other parameters, such as the window size and the number of factors, also require discussion.
4. To facilitate better understanding, I suggest the authors provide detailed graphical illustrations of the entire model. The authors can refer to Figure 1 in [1] for inspiration.
5. Recently, new deep Poisson factor analysis models have been proposed, such as [2] and [3]. These models should be discussed in the Introduction to provide a more comprehensive context for the proposed method.
