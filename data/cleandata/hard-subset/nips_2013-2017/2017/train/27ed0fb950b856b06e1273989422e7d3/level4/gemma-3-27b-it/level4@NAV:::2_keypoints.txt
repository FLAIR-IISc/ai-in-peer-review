- Novel approach: models long-term dependencies in count-vector data using RNNs with gamma-distributed latent variables.  
- Method detail: neural nets approximate gamma shape parameters by integrating prior (higher-layer) and likelihood (lower-layer) information.  
- Strength: updating network parameters via expectations of latent variables reduces sampling variance and improves computational efficiency.  
- Presentation issue: manuscript appears hastily written with typographical errors (e.g., Equation 10).  
- Modeling concern: fixing the gamma scale parameter while only updating the shape parameter lacks sufficient justification and analysis.  
- Experimental limitation: evaluation only uses a two-layer model; needs comparisons to single-layer and deeper architectures and sensitivity analyses (window size, number of factors).  
- Clarity improvement: include a detailed graphical illustration of the complete model (e.g., similar to Fig.1 in Chung et al. 2015).  
- Related work: introduction should cite recent deep Poisson factor analysis advances (Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017).
