- Proposes a deep Poisson factorization model with an RNN-like structure to capture long-range temporal dependencies, using variational inference to learn neural network parameters.

- Paper contains solid technical content but is undermined by factual inaccuracies and many grammatical/typo issues.

- Factual error: the claim on lines 86–87 about modeling nonnegative numbers is wrong — the Poisson Randomized Gamma (PRG) distribution is used for nonnegative modeling.

- Presentation suggestion: highlight best results in Table 2 in bold for clarity.

- Experimental metric suggestion: held-out perplexity would be a stronger indicator than (or in addition to) MSE/PMSE.

- Methodological suggestion: while the paper addresses limitations of prior work on long-range dependencies, it should compare against a one-level deep dynamic Poisson factorization to justify multi-level hierarchy.

- Critique of LSTM comparison: comparing to LSTM is informative but less convincing because the learning algorithms differ, weakening the case for multiple hierarchy levels.

- Readability issue: numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrases (many examples provided) need correction to improve clarity.
