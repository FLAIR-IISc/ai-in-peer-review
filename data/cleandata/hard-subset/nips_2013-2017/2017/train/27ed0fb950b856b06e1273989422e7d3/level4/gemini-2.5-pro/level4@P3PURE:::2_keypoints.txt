- Novel method: RNNs model shape parameters of implicit Gamma distributions over latent variables to capture complex long-term dependencies in sequences of count vectors.

- Architecture integrates top-down prior and bottom-up likelihood; neural network parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables to reduce sampling variance and improve efficiency.

- Reviewer is positive about the core contribution but notes the manuscript appears rushed and needs substantial improvement.

- Critical typos (e.g., Eq. 10) hinder understanding; careful proofreading required.

- Authors fix the Gamma scale parameter and only learn the shape—motivation and implications of this choice need more detailed discussion and analysis.

- Experiments only use two layers; ablation comparing single-layer and deeper (>2) models is needed to assess impact of depth.

- Experimental analysis should also evaluate key hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).

- Include a detailed graphical illustration of the overall model architecture to improve clarity (e.g., similar to Fig.1 in Chung et al., 2015).

- Introduction should discuss recent related work on deep Poisson factor analysis (e.g., Zhou 2016; Cong et al. 2017) to better position the contribution.
