- Proposes RNN-parameterized gamma distributions to model long-term dependencies in count-sequence data, using top-down and bottom-up neural integration and expectation-based updates to reduce variance.  
- Manuscript appears rushed and contains critical typos and inconsistently defined symbols (e.g., Equation (10)); needs careful proofreading.  
- The gamma scale parameter is held fixed; the authors must justify this design, analyze effects on expressiveness and identifiability, and provide sensitivity studies for learned vs. fixed scales.  
- Experiments only evaluate a two-layer model; include ablations on model depth (1 vs. 2 vs. deeper), and sensitivity analyses for other hyperparameters like window size and number of factors.  
- Add a detailed graphical schematic of the full model showing temporal dynamics and inter-layer interactions (similar to Fig.1 of Chung et al.).  
- Introduction should discuss and compare to recent deep Poisson/gamma factor models (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016, Cong et al. 2017) and clearly position the proposed method relative to them.
