- Proposes DDPFA, a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model for sequential count vectors.  
- Extends Poisson Factor Analysis with a deep structure and uses an RNN for implicit distributions plus variational inference.  
- Well-motivated with clear discussion of limitations of existing methods for long-range dependencies.  
- Technical contribution is sound with a detailed description of the model and inference algorithm.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets show DDPFA outperforms PGDS and LSTM on MSE and PMSE.  
- Needs clearer explanation and analysis of how the deep structure captures long-time dependence.  
- Lacks details on optimization routines (specific SGD variant, hyperparameters, tuning).  
- Fails to address how the model performs under highly noisy or non-stationary data.  
- Paper contains grammatical errors and typos and needs careful proofreading.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 7/10, Originality 8/10, Impact 8/10, Clarity 6/10, Presentation 7/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model, strong empirical results, thorough method description.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient deep-structure justification, missing optimization/limitation details, and presentation issues.
