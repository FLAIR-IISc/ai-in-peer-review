The authors propose a novel method for capturing complex long-term dependencies in count vector data by leveraging recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions. Specifically, they model these distributions using gamma distributions, with the shape parameters – which encode the time dependence – approximated by neural networks. These networks integrate information from both prior (higher) layers and likelihood (lower) layers. The network parameters are updated using the expectation of the latent variables, which effectively reduces variance stemming from sampling and improves computational efficiency. The model offers an interesting and clever approach to addressing the long-dependency problem in dynamic models, and I find it promising. However, the manuscript appears somewhat rushed, and I have several comments for improvement:

1. There are several critical typos throughout the text, for example, in equation 10.
2. The authors fix the scale parameter of the gamma distributions and only update the shape parameter. This design choice requires a more detailed discussion and analysis to justify its effectiveness.
3. The experiments presented focus solely on a two-layer model. To better understand the impact of network depth on performance, the authors should compare the two-layer model to both single-layer and deeper models. Additionally, further discussion is needed regarding other key parameters, such as window size and the number of factors.
4. To enhance understanding, I recommend including a detailed graphical illustration of the complete model. The authors could refer to Figure 1 in [1] as a useful example.
5. Recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis, such as the models presented in [2] and [3], should be discussed in the Introduction to provide a more comprehensive context for the proposed work.

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research,17(163), 1-44, 2016.
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou, Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC, ICML 2017.
