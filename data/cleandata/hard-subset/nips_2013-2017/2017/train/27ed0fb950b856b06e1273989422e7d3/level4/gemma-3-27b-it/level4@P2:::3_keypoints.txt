- Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) to capture long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count data.
- Extends Poisson Factor Analysis by integrating recurrent neural networks and a deep structure to model temporal dependencies.
- Uses variational inference for latent variables and gradient descent to optimize network parameters.
- Evaluates on synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.
- Core contribution: applying deep learning within a probabilistic factorization framework for temporal dynamics.
- Addresses limitations of previous dynamic PFA models that assume temporal independence.
- RNNs enable modeling of complex, non-linear temporal dependencies; experimental setup is comprehensive.
- Numerous grammatical errors and typos reduce readability and indicate need for polishing.
- Technical inaccuracy: claim about modeling nonnegative numbers is incorrect—Poisson-Gamma, not Gamma alone, is the correct mechanism.
- Comparison to LSTM is limited by differing learning algorithms and doesn't isolate benefits of hierarchical depth.
- A direct comparison to a single-hidden-layer dynamic PFA would better demonstrate advantages of depth.
- Evaluation uses MSE/PMSE but omits held-out perplexity, a standard metric for generative models.
- Analysis of learned latent factors (Figures 2 & 3) is compelling but could further explore dataset-specific interpretability.
- Discussion of pruning and limitations of mean-field variational inference is insightful and notes future work.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel application, addresses temporal dependency gaps, shows performance improvements, valuable for topic modeling/sequential analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues, a minor technical error, limited direct comparisons, and missing held-out perplexity.
- Overall: technically sound and potentially significant; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to writing, technical clarity, and experimental comparisons.
