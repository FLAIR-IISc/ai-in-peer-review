

The paper proposes a novel deep Poisson factorization model incorporating long-range temporal dependencies through a recurrent neural network-like structure. While the technical contribution is promising, the manuscript contains significant grammatical errors and factual inaccuracies that undermine clarity and credibility. Key issues are outlined below:

1. **Technical Correction**: Line 86–87 incorrectly states that "nonnegative numbers are modeled" via "Poisson Randomized Gamma." The model actually employs a Poisson-Gamma latent structure (standard for nonnegative count data), and this misstatement must be corrected to avoid confusion.

2. **Table Presentation**: Table 2 should bold the best-performing results to improve readability and highlight key findings for readers.

3. **Evaluation Metrics**: Section 4.2 prioritizes MSE/PMSE over held-out perplexity, which is suboptimal. For count data, held-out perplexity is a more appropriate metric for assessing predictive performance and should be emphasized in the experiments.

4. **Architectural Comparison**: The paper effectively addresses limitations in prior work (refs. 1–2) but omits a critical comparison: a single-level dynamic Poisson factorization (PF) architecture. The LSTM comparison is valid for performance benchmarking but fails to justify the necessity of multiple hierarchical layers, as the learning algorithms differ fundamentally (variational inference vs. backpropagation through time). A single-level dynamic PF comparison would better demonstrate the utility of the proposed multi-level hierarchy.

5. **Grammatical and Typographical Errors**: The manuscript contains numerous errors that impede readability, including:  
   - "unit the data" (should be "unite the data")  
   - "Noticing the lack of capturing..." (should be "Noticing the lack of capturing...")  
   - "PFA factorized a count matrix" (tense inconsistency; should be "PFA factorizes")  
   - "other data with different pattern long-time dependence" (should be "data exhibiting long-term dependencies with varying patterns")  
   - "Examples for this including..." (should be "Examples include...")  
   - "shows excellent results on predict and..." (should be "shows excellent predictive results...")  
   - "However, it is rough for them..." (should be "However, this is challenging for them...")  
   - "V-dimesnion sequentially" (should be "V-dimensional sequentially")  
   - "K-dimension latent variable" (should be "K-dimensional latent variable")  
   - "due to the including of feature-wise" (should be "due to the inclusion of feature-wise")  
   - "recommend systems" (should be "recommendation systems")  

The core methodology—leveraging RNN-like structures for long-term dependencies in Poisson factorization—is a meaningful extension of prior dynamic PF work. The variational inference approach is well-motivated, and the model’s potential for capturing complex temporal dynamics is evident. However, the current presentation lacks precision, and the missing architectural comparison weakens the argument for multi-level hierarchy. Strengthening the evaluation with perplexity metrics and addressing the grammatical errors are essential for clarity. The technical content is sound, but these revisions are necessary to meet NIPS standards. The paper merits acceptance only after these issues are resolved.
