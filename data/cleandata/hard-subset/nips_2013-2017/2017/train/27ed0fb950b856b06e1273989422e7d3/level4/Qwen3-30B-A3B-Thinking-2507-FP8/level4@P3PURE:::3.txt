

The paper proposes incorporating long-range temporal dependencies into a Poisson factorization model through a deep recurrent structure, distinguishing itself from prior dynamic Poisson factorization (PF) work by employing an RNN-like architecture for modeling long-term temporal dynamics. Inference is performed via variational inference with optimization routines for neural network parameters. While the technical contributions are sound, the paper contains grammatical and typographical errors that impede readability. The following points require attention:

1.  **Technical Correction (Lines 86-87):** The statement regarding modeling nonnegative numbers is inaccurate. The Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution, not the Poisson distribution alone, is used for modeling nonnegative data. This must be corrected.

2.  **Table Presentation (Table 2):** Please bold the best-performing results in Table 2 for improved clarity.

3.  **Evaluation Metric (Section 4.2):** Held-out perplexity would provide a stronger evaluation metric for the temporal modeling task than MSE/PMSE, though the latter are acceptable. The authors should prioritize perplexity.

4.  **Hierarchy Justification:** The paper adequately addresses limitations in existing works (Refs 1, 2) for long-range dependency modeling. However, to demonstrate the utility of the proposed multi-level hierarchy, a comparison with a single-level dynamic PF architecture is essential. While the LSTM comparison is valuable, the different optimization procedures render it insufficient for justifying the need for multiple hierarchical levels in the proposed method.

5.  **Grammatical and Typographical Errors:** Several errors throughout the manuscript hinder readability. Specific instances include:
    *   "unit the data" → "unite the data" (or "preprocess the data" if context allows)
    *   "Noticing the lack of capturing..." → "Failing to capture..."
    *   "PFA factorized a count matrix" → "PFA factorizes a count matrix" (tense consistency)
    *   "other data with different pattern long-time dependence..." → "other data exhibiting different long-term dependence patterns..."
    *   "Examples for this including..." → "Examples include..."
    *   "shows excellent results on predict and..." → "shows excellent results on prediction and..."
    *   "However, it is rough for them..." → "However, it is difficult for them..."
    *   "V-dimesnion sequentially..." → "V-dimensional sequentially..."
    *   "K-dimension latent variable..." → "K-dimensional latent variable..."
    *   "due to the including of feature-wise" → "due to the inclusion of feature-wise"
    *   "recommend systems" → "recommendation systems"
