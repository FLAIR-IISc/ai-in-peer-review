Review- The authors present a novel approach to model complex long-term dependencies in count vectors by leveraging recurrent neural networks to parameterize implicit distributions instantiated as gamma distributions. The shape parameters of these gamma distributions, which encode temporal dependence, are approximated by neural networks that aggregate information from the prior (upper layer) and the likelihood (lower layer). The neural network parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables, which helps reduce sampling-induced variance and improves computational efficiency. The model offers a clever and appealing way to handle long-range dependencies in a dynamic model. I like it, but the manuscript seems somewhat rushed, and I have the following comments:
1. There are critical typos, e.g., in Eq. (10).
2. The scale parameter is fixed while only the shape parameter of the gamma distributions is updated; this design choice requires more detailed discussion and analysis.
3. The experiments report only a two-layer model and do not compare with single-layer or deeper variants, which is insufficient to understand the effect of depth on performance. Other hyperparameters also warrant discussion, such as window size and number of factors.
4. For clarity, I recommend including a detailed graphical illustration of the entire model; see Fig. 1 in [1] as a reference.
5. Recent deep Poisson factor analysis models, such as [2] and [3], should be discussed in the Introduction.
 
[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research,17(163), 1-44, 2016.
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou, Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC, ICML 2017.
