This paper introduces the Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization model, a model based on PF that allows for temporal dependencies. Unlike previous work on dynamic PF, this paper uses a simplified recurrent neural network for long-term dependencies. Inference is performed through an additional step to maximize the neural network parameters. The paper presents results from experiments conducted on 5 real-world datasets.

Overall, I found the concept potentially appealing, but I think some improvements are needed in its execution before it can be adopted at conferences like NIPS. Please see my comments below.

+The structure of equation 4 is not consistent with an RNN structure, as h_t^{(n)} depends only on the top-layer memory vector, and it does not depend on the previous time step for the same layer as in the RNN setup. Is there any reason for this simplified structure?
+In paragraph 2, some equations in the model are missing (e.g., it is not clear how to parameterize gamma in equation 4, or what the distribution is that creates theta_t in equation 3).
+I disagree with the discussion of "implicit distribution" in lines 110-118. Equations 9 and 10 are actually clear: these are gamma with specific shapes and rates. Since the distribution of theta_tk (equation 9) can be computed in a closed form conditioned on h^{(0)}, it cannot be called implicit. The same comment applies to h in equation 10.
+Lines 30-31: "This method may have weak points in analyzing various patterned long-term dependencies, such as fanatical data and disaster data, among other data" – this sentence was not clear to me; can you elaborate on this?
+The sub-paragraph in lines 133-155 is not clear to me. What exactly is the "loss function" in equation 15?
+MSE/PMSE is generally not a good metric for models like PF. The results should additionally include at least predictive log-likelihood.
+The value of K in the experiment (line 207) seems very small to me. I suggest the authors use a larger value like K=100 (at least).
+I could not reconcile the discussion in lines 224-232 with figure 3: it is not clear.
+The update equations for the variational inference method (equations 11-14) can be safely removed in the supplement.
+The caption of figure 4 is not clearly readable.
+I strongly recommend following the general writing advice regarding the use of passive voice (especially for abstracts).
+The writing quality is poor, and the paper has many typos. Here are some examples:

 -Line 22, "Factorize"
 -Line 24, "Property of the"
 -Lines 29, 34, "Direkhlet"

 -Figure 1, "Transmission" (however, I am not sure if this is the appropriate term)
 -Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
 -Many articles are missing (e.g., "Although the Dirichlet distribution is often used as a prior distribution")
