- Contribution: Proposes a deep Poisson factorization model with an RNN-like structure to capture long-range temporal dependencies, using variational inference plus optimization for neural network parameters.
- Incorrect factual claim: The paper's statement that it models nonnegative numbers (lines 86–87) is wrong — the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution performs that role.
- Presentation: Best results in Table 2 should be highlighted in bold to improve clarity.
- Evaluation metric: Experiments should place more emphasis on held-out perplexity as a performance indicator (MSE/PMSE are acceptable but insufficient alone).
- Comparisons: Missing a baseline comparison to dynamic PF with a single-level deep architecture; the LSTM comparison is weakened by differing learning algorithms and does not justify the need for multiple hierarchical levels.
- Readability: Numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrasings detract from the paper (examples: "unit the data", "Noticing the lack of capturing", tense issues like "PFA factorized a count matrix", "other data with different pattern long-time dependence", "Examples for this including", "shows excellent results on predict and", "V-dimesnion sequentially", "K-dimension latent variable", "due to the including of feature-wise", and "recommend systems" → "recommendation systems").
