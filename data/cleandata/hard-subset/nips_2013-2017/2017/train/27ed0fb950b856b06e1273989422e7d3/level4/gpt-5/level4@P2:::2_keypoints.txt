- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): RNNs parameterize Gamma shape parameters in a hierarchical temporal Poisson factorization for sequential count vectors.
- Uses Φ, Θ, and component-/feature-specific shrinkage variables Ψ and Λ to factorize Poisson rates.
- Temporal dependencies in Θ are modeled via multi-layer latent variables h(n)t with RNNs for prior and posterior Gamma conditionals.
- Inference alternates conjugate mean-field updates (with auxiliary counts) and gradient-based learning of RNN parameters using a “probability autoencoder” loss.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, Ebola, etc.) shows improved fit and predictions and interpretable factors compared to PGDS, PFA, and LSTM.
- Contribution connects to dynamic count factor models, deep exponential families, and RNN-based latent-variable sequence models.
- Strength: Tackles long-range temporal dependence by combining Poisson–Gamma conjugacy with RNN-parameterized priors/posteriors.
- Strength: Uses conjugacy and auxiliary-count augmentation for efficient mean-field updates, reducing variance versus sampling methods.
- Strength: Hierarchical temporal structure and ψ/λ shrinkage aim to balance sparsity, interpretability, and flexibility.
- Strength: Qualitative analyses suggest meaningful temporal factors across datasets.
- Weakness: Manuscript clarity is poor—typos, notational inconsistencies, and hard-to-parse definitions (e.g., Eq. 10).
- Weakness: RNNs output only Gamma shape parameters while rates are fixed/shared; this choice lacks justification and empirical analysis.
- Weakness: The “probability autoencoder” loss is loosely connected to a principled ELBO, introduces bias, and lacks convergence guarantees.
- Weakness: Positivity constraints on Gamma parameters and reasons for not using reparameterization/score-function estimators are not specified.
- Weakness: Insufficient ablation studies on depth, window size c, number of factors K, hidden sizes, and prior/posterior network choices.
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to recent deep count models (e.g., Augmentable Gamma Belief Networks, deep LDA with SGR-MCMC) and recurrent latent-variable baselines.
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics rely on MSE/PMSE; held-out predictive log-likelihood/perplexity, calibration, runtime, and memory comparisons are missing.
- Quality: Modeling idea is sensible and plausible, but the training objective and fixed-rate choice need stronger theoretical and empirical grounding.
- Clarity: Requires careful editing, consistent notation, a complete generative story, and a comprehensive graphical model.
- Originality: Novel combination of deep temporal Gamma-chain structure with RNN-parameterized implicit distributions within Poisson factorization.
- Significance: Potentially useful for sequential count applications if clarified and better validated; empirical gains are encouraging.
- Key suggestions: fix typos/notation, add graphical model and generative summary, justify/test fixed-rate choice, strengthen inference connection to ELBO, add systematic ablations, report probabilistic metrics, and expand related work and comparisons.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses an important problem, integrates conjugacy with RNNs cleverly, and shows strong empirical and qualitative results.
- Cons against acceptance: exposition problems, insufficient theoretical justification for inference objective, and limited ablations/baselines/metrics.
