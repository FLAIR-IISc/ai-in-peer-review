- Overall: core idea (deep dynamic Poisson factorization) is promising but paper needs significant revisions to meet NIPS standards.
- Model design: Equation (4) does not follow a standard RNN — h_t^(n) depends only on top-layer memory vectors, not prior same-layer timesteps; rationale for this simplified design must be clarified.
- Missing model details: Section 2 omits key specifications (e.g., Gamma parameterization in Eq.4 and generative distribution for θ_t in Eq.3).
- Incorrect terminology: Equations (9) and (10) define explicit Gamma distributions, so calling them "implicit distributions" is incorrect; θ_tk and h have closed-form conditionals.
- Ambiguous claim: Lines 30–31 about the method’s weaknesses on “fanatical data and disaster data” are unclear and need elaboration.
- Unclear subsection: Lines 133–155 are confusing; the referenced "loss function" (Eq.15) must be explicitly defined.
- Evaluation metrics: MSE/PMSE are inappropriate for PF-like models — report predictive log-likelihood (or other likelihood-based metrics).
- Experimental setup: K used in experiments (line 207) is too small; recommend testing much larger K (e.g., K≥100).
- Results clarity: Discussion in lines 224–232 does not clearly connect to Figure 3 and needs better alignment.
- Presentation of inference: Variational update equations (Eqs.11–14) are detailed and may be moved to the supplement to improve flow.
- Figures and captions: Figure 4’s caption is illegible; several figure captions contain redundant or questionable terminology (e.g., Figure 1).
- Writing style: Overuse of passive voice throughout; rewrite in active voice.
- Writing quality: Numerous typographical, grammatical, and phrasing errors throughout the paper (missing articles, inconsistent capitalization, incomplete phrases/sentences); many specific examples provided by reviewer.
- Experimental claims: Paper reports experiments on five real-world datasets but methodological and reporting issues (metrics, K, clarity) undermine the empirical contribution.
- Inference procedure: Uses variational inference with an additional maximization step over neural network parameters — clarify optimization details and convergence behavior.
