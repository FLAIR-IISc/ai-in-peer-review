1. Proposes a deep dynamic Poisson factorization with a simplified RNN to capture temporal dependencies; inference via variational methods plus an M-step for network parameters; experiments on five datasets.

2. Idea is potentially interesting but execution needs improvement before NIPS-level acceptance.

3. Eq. 4 is not a standard RNN: h_t depends only on top-layer memory vectors, not the same layer’s previous state — rationale for this design is missing.

4. Model definitions are incomplete: how the Gamma in Eq. 4 is parameterized and what distribution generates theta_t in Eq. 3 are not specified.

5. The paper’s claim about “implicit distributions” is incorrect: Eqs. 9–10 are explicit Gamma distributions and theta_tk has a closed-form conditional distribution.

6. The sentence about “weak points … fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear and needs elaboration.

7. Subsection (lines 133–155) is unclear — specifically, what exactly is the “loss function” in Eq. 15?

8. Evaluation uses MSE/PMSE, which is inappropriate for PF-like models; predictive log-likelihood should be reported at minimum.

9. Latent dimension K is set too small in experiments; recommend larger values (e.g., K = 100).

10. Discussion in lines 224–232 is not clearly linked to Fig. 3; the connection is unclear.

11. Variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) are better placed in the supplement.

12. Fig. 4 caption is not legible and needs fixing.

13. Strong recommendation to minimize passive voice, especially in the abstract.

14. Writing quality is weak with many typos and missing articles; numerous example corrections are listed (e.g., lines 15, 22, 24, 29, 34, Fig. 1, line 49).
