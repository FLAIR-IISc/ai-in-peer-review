- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) using RNNs to parameterize hierarchical, time-varying Gamma latent factors for sequential count data.  
- Aims to capture complex, long-range temporal dependencies beyond simple Markovian dynamics.  
- Core innovation: combine Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs that output Gamma shape parameters across time and layers.  
- Inference uses a two-stage procedure: mean-field variational inference for latent variables and gradient-based optimization of RNN parameters with a VAE-inspired loss.  
- Empirically outperforms PGDS and standard LSTMs on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Novel and significant hybrid of deep learning (RNNs) and Bayesian matrix factorization with flexible, non-linear conditional priors.  
- Elegant technical design using separate RNNs for top-down priors and bottom-up approximate posteriors ("probability autoencoder").  
- Practical training choice: optimizing network parameters on latent expectations likely improves efficiency and stability.  
- Major clarity and presentation problems: confusing notation, opaque derivations (especially Eqs. 10–18), and unclear connection to standard VAE framework.  
- Lacks a clear graphical model showing dependencies among θ, h, RNN parameters, and data; current figures are too abstract.  
- Missing discussion of important related work (Chung et al. 2015, Zhou et al. 2016, Cong et al. 2017) weakening originality claims.  
- Key modeling choice—fixing Gamma rate parameter βh while learning shape via RNNs—is unjustified and limits flexibility.  
- Empirical issues: adding layers doesn't improve performance and latent dimension "pruning" causes inactive factors, challenging the benefit of depth.  
- Experimental evaluation incomplete: lacks ablation studies (e.g., context window size c) and deeper analysis of failure modes.  
- Recommendation: lean reject due to clarity, related-work omissions, and insufficient technical/experimental justification; encourages revision and resubmission.
