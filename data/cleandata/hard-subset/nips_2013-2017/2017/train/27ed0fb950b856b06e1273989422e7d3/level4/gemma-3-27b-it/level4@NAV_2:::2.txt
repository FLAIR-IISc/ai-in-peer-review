The authors propose a novel method to capture the complex long-time dependence in sequential count data by exploiting recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, realized through gamma distributions. The shape parameters of these gamma distributions, capturing the time dependence, are approximated by neural networks, attempting to integrate information from both prior (higher layers) and likelihood (lower layers). Parameters in the neural networks are updated based on the expectation of latent variables, aiming to reduce variance and improve computational efficiency. The model presents an interesting and insightful approach to addressing the long-dependency problem in dynamic models. However, the manuscript appears somewhat rushed, and I have several comments below.

1. Several typographical errors are present, such as in Equation 10.
2. The authors fix the scale parameter while only updating the shape parameter in the gamma distributions. This choice requires a more detailed justification and analysis. What is the rationale behind this decision, and how might varying the scale parameter affect the model’s behavior?
3. The experimental evaluation only presents results for a two-layer model. A more thorough analysis would compare performance with single-layer and deeper models to better understand the impact of network depth. Further investigation into the sensitivity of results to parameters like window size and the number of factors (K) is also needed.
4. To enhance clarity, I recommend including a detailed graphical illustration of the complete model. The authors could draw inspiration from Figure 1 in [1] as a starting point.
5. Recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis, such as those presented in [2] and [3], are not discussed in the introduction. These works should be acknowledged and differentiated from the proposed approach.

**Summary of Strengths:**

*   **Novel Approach:** The integration of recurrent neural networks with Poisson Factor Analysis is a promising and innovative approach to modeling sequential count data.
*   **Efficient Inference:** Utilizing expectation propagation instead of sampling-based methods is a practical choice for improving computational efficiency.
*   **Interpretability:** The model aims to provide interpretable latent structures, which can be valuable for understanding underlying dynamics in the data.
*   **Empirical Results:** The experimental results demonstrate promising performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

*   **Typographical Errors:** Presence of typos detracts from the overall polish of the manuscript.
*   **Parameter Choice Justification:** Lack of a thorough justification for fixing the scale parameter in the gamma distributions.
*   **Limited Ablation Study:** Insufficient exploration of the impact of network depth and hyperparameter settings.
*   **Missing Related Work:** Failure to cite and discuss recent relevant advancements in deep Poisson factor analysis.
*   **Clarity of Model Diagram:** The current description of the model architecture could benefit from a detailed graphical illustration.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The model presents a novel and potentially impactful approach to modeling sequential count data.
*   The experimental results are promising and suggest the model is competitive with existing methods.
*   The use of variational inference makes the model scalable to larger datasets.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The manuscript lacks polish due to typographical errors.
*   Key design choices (e.g., fixed scale parameter) are not adequately justified.
*   The experimental evaluation is incomplete and lacks a comprehensive ablation study.
*   Important related work is missing from the introduction.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper has the potential to be a valuable contribution to the field, but it requires significant revision to address the weaknesses outlined above. Currently, the paper is borderline acceptable. I recommend **rejection with encouragement to resubmit** after addressing the reviewers’ comments. Specifically, the authors should focus on improving the clarity of the manuscript, justifying their design choices, conducting a more thorough experimental evaluation, and acknowledging relevant related work.
