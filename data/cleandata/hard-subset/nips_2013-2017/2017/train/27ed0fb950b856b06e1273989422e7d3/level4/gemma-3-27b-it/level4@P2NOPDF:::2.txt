This paper introduces a novel approach to modeling complex long-term dependencies in count data using recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions. Specifically, the authors leverage gamma distributions, approximating their shape parameters with neural networks that integrate information from both prior (higher layers) and likelihood (lower layers). A key contribution is the use of expectation-based updates for the neural network parameters, which effectively reduces variance and improves computational efficiency compared to sampling-based methods. The proposed model offers a promising and insightful solution to the challenge of long-range dependencies in dynamic models. However, the manuscript could benefit from further refinement and clarification.

Here are several points for the authors to consider:

1. **Presentation & Technical Accuracy:** The manuscript contains several typographical errors, such as in equation 10, which need to be addressed carefully.

2. **Model Design & Justification:** The decision to fix the scale parameter of the gamma distributions while only updating the shape parameter requires more detailed justification and analysis. The authors should explain the rationale behind this choice and discuss its potential impact on the model’s performance and representational capacity. 

3. **Experimental Evaluation:** The experiments currently focus on a two-layer model. A more comprehensive evaluation would include comparisons to models with varying numbers of layers (single layer and more than two layers) to demonstrate the effect of network depth on performance. Furthermore, a discussion of the sensitivity to other hyperparameters, such as window size and the number of latent factors, is necessary. Providing a sensitivity analysis will strengthen the conclusions drawn from the experiments.

4. **Model Visualization:** A clear graphical illustration of the complete model architecture would significantly improve understanding. The authors could benefit from referencing and adapting the style of Figure 1 from Chung et al. (2015) [1] as a starting point for their visualization. A diagram would help readers grasp the flow of information and the interactions between different components.

5. **Related Work:** The introduction should include a discussion of recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis, such as the work by Zhou et al. (2016) [2] and Cong et al. (2017) [3]. Positioning the proposed method within the broader context of related research will highlight its contributions and differentiate it from existing approaches.
