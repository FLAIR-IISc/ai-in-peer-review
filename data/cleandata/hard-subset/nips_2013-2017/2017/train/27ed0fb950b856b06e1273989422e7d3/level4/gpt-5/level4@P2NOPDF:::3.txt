This paper introduces a deep Poisson factorization model that captures long-range temporal dependence using an RNN-like architecture. Inference is performed with variational methods coupled with optimization to learn the neural parameters. The technical ideas are sound and relevant, but the manuscript contains a factual error and several presentation issues that hinder readability.

Specific comments:
1) Lines 86–87 contain an incorrect statement about modeling nonnegative data. The appropriate distributional construction is the Poisson randomized Gamma distribution for nonnegative counts.
2) Please highlight the best results in Table 2 in bold to improve readability.
3) In Section 4.2, held-out perplexity would be a more informative primary metric for predictive performance on count data; MSE/PMSE are acceptable as secondary measures but are less standard for probabilistic models of counts.
4) The paper addresses the limitations of prior dynamic PF approaches (e.g., references 1 and 2) regarding long-range dependencies. However, to demonstrate the benefit of multiple hierarchical layers, a comparison against a dynamic PF with a single deep layer (i.e., a shallower version of your model) would be valuable. The comparison to LSTM is informative, but because the learning algorithms and objectives differ, it does not by itself justify the need for multiple hierarchical levels in the proposed model.

Writing and presentation issues:
- The manuscript contains many grammatical errors and typos that impede comprehension. Examples include:
  - “unit the data” → “unite the data” or “until the data,” depending on intent
  - “Noticing the lack of capturing ..” → “Noting the lack of ability to capture …”
  - “PFA factorized a count matrix” → “PFA factorizes a count matrix” (use consistent present tense)
  - “other data with different pattern long-time dependence ..” → “other data with different patterns of long-term dependence …”
  - “Examples for this including ..” → “Examples include …”
  - “shows excellent results on predict and ..” → “shows excellent results on prediction and …”
  - “However, it is rough for them ..” → “However, it is difficult for them …”
  - “V-dimesnion sequentially ..” → “V-dimension sequentially …” (and consider “V-dimensional sequence …”)
  - “K-dimension latent variable ..” → “K-dimensional latent variable …”
  - “due to the including of feature-wise” → “due to the inclusion of feature-wise …”
  - “recommend systems” → “recommender systems”
- Please proofread the entire paper to ensure consistent tense, correct pluralization, and proper article usage (e.g., “a,” “an,” “the”).
- Consider adding brief definitions for less common terminology on first use and ensuring that notation is introduced before it appears in equations.

Overall assessment:
- Strengths: The model is well-motivated, the use of an RNN-like mechanism to encode long-range temporal structure within a Poisson factorization framework is novel and promising, and the variational inference setup is technically competent.
- Weaknesses: One factual misstatement, limited experimental validation of the benefit of depth relative to a shallower dynamic PF baseline, and substantial writing issues.

Recommendations:
- Correct the distributional claim on lines 86–87.
- Emphasize held-out perplexity in the evaluation.
- Include a comparison to a single-layer dynamic PF to isolate the effect of hierarchical depth.
- Improve presentation by addressing the listed grammatical errors, standardizing tense, and polishing the prose throughout.
- Update tables/figures for clarity (e.g., bold the best results in Table 2).
