This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization (DDPF) model, extending Poisson factorization (PF) to incorporate temporal dependencies. Unlike prior work on dynamic PF, this approach employs a simplified recurrent neural network structure intended to capture long-term relationships. Inference is performed via variational inference, augmented with a parameter optimization step for the neural network. The model is evaluated on five real-world datasets.

Overall, the idea presents a potentially valuable contribution, but significant revisions are needed before the paper reaches the standard expected for a conference like NIPS. Detailed comments are provided below.

+The formulation presented in Eq. 4 does not fully align with a standard recurrent neural network (RNN) architecture. Specifically, h_t^{(n)} appears to depend only on top-layer memory vectors and lacks the dependency on previous time steps within the same layer characteristic of RNNs. Clarification regarding the rationale for this simplification would be beneficial.

+Section 2 contains incomplete model definitions. The parameterization of the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 is not specified, nor is the distribution from which theta_t is drawn in Eq. 3.

+I disagree with the characterization of the distributions discussed in lines 110-118 as “implicit.” Equations 9 and 10 provide explicit Gamma distributions with defined shape and rate parameters. Given that the distribution of theta_tk (Eq. 9) can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^{(0)}, it cannot be accurately described as implicit; the same applies to h in Eq. 10.

+The statement in lines 30-31 regarding limitations when analyzing data with “different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data” lacks clarity. Please provide a more detailed explanation of these potential weaknesses.

+The subsection spanning lines 133-155 is unclear. Specifically, the definition of the “loss function” in Eq. 15 requires further elaboration.

+Mean Squared Error (MSE) and Predictive MSE (PMSE) are generally not well-suited metrics for evaluating PF-like models. The results should include, at a minimum, predictive log-likelihood to provide a more informative evaluation.

+The chosen value of K in the experiments (line 207) appears insufficiently large. Consideration should be given to using larger values, such as K=100 or greater.

+The connection between the discussion in lines 224-232 and Fig. 3 is not readily apparent. Further clarification is needed to elucidate this relationship.

+The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11-14) can be moved to the supplementary materials.

+The caption for Fig. 4 is illegible and needs to be revised for clarity.

+I recommend addressing the writing style by following established guidelines for reducing passive voice, particularly within the abstract.

+The paper exhibits numerous instances of poor writing quality and typographical errors. Specific examples include:
 -Line 15: “high-dimensional”
 -Line 22: “factorize”
 -Line 24: “property of the”
 -Lines 29, 34: “Dirichlet”
 -Line 34: “nested”
 -Fig. 1: “visual representation”
 -Fig. 1: “transmission” (the appropriateness of this term is questionable)
 -Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
 -Missing articles (e.g., “Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution”)
