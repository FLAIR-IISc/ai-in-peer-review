- Method: Uses RNNs to parameterize implicit gamma distributions for count vectors; shape parameters (capturing temporal dependence) are approximated by neural nets that integrate prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer), with updates using latent-variable expectations to reduce sampling variance and improve efficiency.
- Overall impression: Interesting and clever approach to modeling long-term dependencies, but the manuscript appears rushed.
- Typographical errors: Critical typos present (e.g., Equation (10)) that must be fixed.
- Gamma scale parameter: The model fixes the gamma scale and only updates the shape—this design choice needs deeper justification, discussion, and empirical analysis.
- Experimental scope: Results only for a two-layer model—need comparisons to single-layer and deeper variants and ablations on hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).
- Clarity/figures: Include a full graphical illustration of the model for clarity (e.g., similar to Figure 1 in Chung et al., 2015).
- Related work: Introduction should discuss recent deep Poisson factor analysis models (e.g., Zhou et al., 2016; Cong et al., 2017).
- Recommendation: Addressing the above points would substantially improve clarity and empirical support.
