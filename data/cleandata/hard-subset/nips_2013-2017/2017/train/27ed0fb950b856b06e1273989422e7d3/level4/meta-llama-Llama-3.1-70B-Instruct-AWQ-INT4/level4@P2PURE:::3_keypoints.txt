- Proposes a novel deep Poisson factorization model with an RNN-like structure to capture long-range temporal dependence; inference is done via variational inference with optimization of neural network parameters.

- Strong technical contribution but contains a factual error: the claim on lines 86–87 about modeling nonnegative numbers is incorrect — the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is actually used.

- Presentation issue: best results in Table 2 should be highlighted in bold for clarity.

- Evaluation critique: held-out perplexity would be a more robust metric in Section 4.2, though MSE/PMSE are acceptable.

- Comparison shortcoming: while it addresses limitations of prior works on long-range dependencies, the paper lacks a comparison to dynamic PF with a single-level deep architecture; the LSTM comparison is insufficient because the learning algorithms differ and do not justify multi-level hierarchy.

- Writing quality: numerous grammatical errors and typos hinder readability (examples include “unit the data,” “Noticing the lack of capturing …,” “PFA factorized a count matrix,” “other data with different pattern long-time dependence …,” “Examples for this including …,” “shows excellent results on predict and …,” “However, it is rough for them …,” “V-dimension sequentially …,” “K-dimension latent variable …,” “due to the including of feature-wise,” “recommend systems”).
