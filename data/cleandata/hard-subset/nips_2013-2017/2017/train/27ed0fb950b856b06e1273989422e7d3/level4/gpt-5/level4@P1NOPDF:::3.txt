This paper incorporates long-range temporal dependence into a Poisson factorization model using a deep architecture. Unlike prior work on dynamic PF, it employs an RNN-like structure to model long-term dependencies. Inference is performed via variational methods, with gradient-based optimization to learn the neural network parameters. The paper has strong technical content but also contains several grammatical and factual issues. Detailed comments follow:

1. The statement about modeling nonnegative numbers on lines 86–87 is incorrect. It is the Poisson randomized gamma distribution that is used for modeling nonnegative numbers.

2. Please bold the best results in Table 2.

3. Held-out perplexity should have been a stronger indicator in the experiments in Section 4.2, though MSE/PMSE are acceptable.

4. The limitations of existing approaches for modeling long-range dependency (references 1 and 2) are adequately addressed. However, to demonstrate the utility of the proposed method with multiple hierarchical levels, a comparison with a dynamic PF model using a single-level deep architecture would be helpful. The comparison with an LSTM is useful, but because the learning algorithms differ, it does not by itself justify the need for multiple hierarchical levels in the proposed method.

5. There are numerous grammatical errors and typos that make the paper difficult to read. Examples include:
- "unit the data"
- "Noticing the lack of capturing .."
- "PFA factorized a count matrix" (the tense here is inconsistent with the rest of the paragraph)
- "other data with different pattern long-time dependence .."
- "Examples for this including .."
- "shows excellent results on predict and .."
- "However, it is rough for them .."
- "V-dimesnion sequentially .."
- "K-dimension latent variable .."
- "due to the including of feature-wise"
- "recommend systems"

Overall, the idea of integrating an RNN-like mechanism with deep Poisson factorization to capture long-range temporal dependencies is interesting and relevant. The proposed inference framework appears sound. Addressing the factual error noted above, strengthening the evaluation with held-out perplexity, adding an ablation or baseline that isolates the benefit of multiple hierarchy levels (e.g., a single-level dynamic PF), and thoroughly revising the writing for grammar and clarity would substantially improve the paper.
