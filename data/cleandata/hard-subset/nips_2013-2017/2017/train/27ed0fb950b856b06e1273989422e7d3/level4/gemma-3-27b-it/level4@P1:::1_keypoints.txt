- Addresses an important problem: modeling sequential count data with complex temporal dependencies.  
- Combines Poisson Factorization with neural networks to capture both local and long-range dependencies.  
- Experimental evaluation is performed on five real-world datasets.  

- Model description is unclear, especially the recurrent structure in Eq. 4, which deviates from standard RNNs.  
- Several equations lack sufficient detail (e.g., Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4, distribution for θ_t in Eq. 3), hurting reproducibility.  
- The term “implicit distributions” is misleading since Eqs. 9 & 10 define explicit Gamma distributions.  
- The comment about temporal Dirichlet processes being weak for “fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear and unjustified.  
- The loss function subsection and Eq. 15 are unclear about what is being optimized and the meaning of terms.  
- Evaluation uses MSE/PMSE, which are not ideal for PF-like models; predictive log-likelihood should be included.  
- The chosen latent dimension K may be too small; larger K (e.g., 100) should be tried to test capacity.  
- The link between the text (lines 224–232) and Fig. 3 is weak and needs clearer explanation.  
- Detailed variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) are better placed in supplementary material.  
- Fig. 4 caption is hard to read and should be revised for clarity.  
- Writing quality is uneven with numerous typos, grammatical errors, and missing articles.  

- Pro acceptance: novel combination of PF and neural networks for sequential count data.  
- Pro acceptance: experimental results show potential across diverse datasets.  

- Con acceptance: unclear model description hinders understanding and reproducibility.  
- Con acceptance: incomplete evaluation lacking predictive log-likelihood and possibly insufficient K.  
- Con acceptance: poor writing quality makes the paper difficult to follow.  

- Overall recommendation: reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit after major improvements in clarity, detail, evaluation, and writing.
