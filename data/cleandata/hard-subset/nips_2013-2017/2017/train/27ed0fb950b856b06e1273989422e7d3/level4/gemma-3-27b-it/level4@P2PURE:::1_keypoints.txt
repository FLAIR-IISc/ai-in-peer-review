- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPF) extending Poisson factorization to temporal data using a simplified RNN, with variational inference plus neural network parameter optimization, evaluated on five real-world datasets.

- The paper is potentially interesting but needs substantial improvements before acceptance at a competitive conference.

- The RNN formulation (Eq.4) is nonstandard: h_t^{(n)} appears to depend only on top-layer memory vectors, not previous time steps within the same layer—rationale unclear.

- Section 2 omits key model equations: Gamma parameterization in Eq.4 and the distribution generating theta_t in Eq.3 are not defined.

- The claim that certain distributions are “implicit” is incorrect because Eqs.9–10 give explicit Gamma forms and theta_t|h^{(0)} is computable in closed form.

- The statement about weaknesses for “fanatical data and disaster data” is vague and needs clarification and elaboration.

- The subsection around lines 133–155 lacks clarity; the loss function in Eq.15 is not precisely defined.

- MSE and PMSE are inappropriate metrics for Poisson factorization; the experiments should report predictive log-likelihood or other suitable metrics.

- The chosen latent dimensionality K (line 207) is likely too small; experiments should explore much larger K (e.g., K≥100).

- The discussion in lines 224–232 does not clearly relate to Fig.3; the connection between text and figure is unclear.

- The variational update equations (Eqs.11–14) would be better placed in the supplementary material.

- The caption for Fig.4 is illegible and must be fixed.

- The abstract and paper overuse passive voice; the authors should consult writing guidelines.

- Overall writing quality is poor with many typos, missing articles, and awkward phrasing (numerous line-specific examples provided).
