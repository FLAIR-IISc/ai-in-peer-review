The authors propose a novel method for capturing long-term temporal dependencies in count data. At the core of their approach is a recurrent neural network (RNN) used to model the time-varying parameters of latent gamma distributions. The RNN models the shape parameters of these gamma distributions, effectively capturing temporal dynamics by integrating information from both the prior (from higher layers) and the likelihood (from lower layers). The neural network parameters are updated using the expectation of the latent variables, a technique that reduces the variance associated with sampling and improves computational efficiency.

Overall, the model presents an interesting and intelligent approach to handling long-range dependencies in dynamic models. I find the core idea promising. However, the manuscript appears to have been written in haste and would benefit from several revisions before it is ready for publication. My main comments are listed below:

1.  The manuscript contains several typos, with a particularly critical one in Equation 10 that should be corrected.

2.  The authors fix the scale parameter of the gamma distributions and only update the shape parameter. This is a significant modeling choice that requires more detailed discussion and justification. Why was this decision made, and what are its implications for the model's expressiveness and performance?

3.  The experimental evaluation is insufficient to fully understand the model's properties. The authors only report results for a two-layer architecture, without comparing it to models with a single layer or more than two layers. This makes it difficult to assess the impact of model depth on performance. An ablation study analyzing the sensitivity to other key hyperparameters, such as the window size and the number of factors, is also necessary.

4.  To improve clarity and aid the reader's understanding, I strongly suggest that the authors include a detailed graphical illustration of the proposed model. A figure similar in style to Figure 1 in [1] would be highly beneficial for visualizing the data flow and dependencies.

5.  The introduction and related work section should be updated to discuss recent, relevant work on deep Poisson factor analysis, such as the models proposed in [2] and [3]. Situating the proposed method within the context of these recent advancements is crucial.

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research, 17(163), 1-44, 2016.
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou, Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC, ICML 2017.
