- Summary: Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), a hierarchical recurrent generative model for sequential count vectors using Gamma variables and variational inference with RNN-parameterized conditional distributions.  
- Novelty: Combines deep recurrent priors with Poisson factorization and a VI scheme that couples bottom-up and top-down networks.  
- Datasets & baselines: Evaluated on synthetic data and five real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, Annual Sheep Population) against PGDS, PFA, and LSTM using MSE/PMSE.  
- Strength: Explicitly models long-range temporal dependencies missing from shallow dynamic PF models.  
- Strength: Integrates hierarchical structure with recurrent neural parameterizations to capture complex temporal patterns.  
- Strength: Introduces feature-wise and component-wise Gamma priors (Ψ, Λ) to improve sparsity and interpretability.  
- Strength: Broad empirical evaluation showing DDPFA often outperforms baselines in fitting and prediction.  
- Strength: Qualitative analysis indicates more persistent factor activations aligning with real-world expectations.  
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics are suboptimal—held-out perplexity or predictive log-likelihood should be reported in addition to MSE/PMSE.  
- Weakness: No ablation isolating depth; compare to a one-layer dynamic PF to justify multi-level hierarchy.  
- Weakness: Several grammatical issues and a factual error that reduce readability and may confuse readers—needs thorough language revision.  
- Weakness: The “probability autoencoder” objectives (Eqs. 15–18) are heuristic and not clearly derived from a principled ELBO; clarify assumptions and links to amortized VI/IWAE.  
- Weakness: Strong mean-field factorization may cause pruning/underutilization; discuss structured variational families or auxiliary-variable fixes.  
- Reporting suggestion: Highlight best results in tables (bold minima) and provide standard deviations/confidence intervals over multiple runs.  
- Quality: Modeling idea is sound and leverages Poisson–Gamma conjugacy and auxiliary count augmentation correctly.  
- Quality concern: Inference section is under-specified—provide derivations for Gamma posteriors (Eqs. 9–14), RNN output parameterizations, and optimization details (learning rates, schedules, initialization, regularization).  
- Complexity: Discuss computational complexity, convergence behavior, and sensitivity to window size c, number of layers, and K.  
- Specific correction: Fix incorrect statement about modeling nonnegative numbers—clarify the Gamma–Poisson relationship and cite appropriately.  
- Reproducibility: Release code, hyperparameter settings, and training details (architectures, optimizers, seeds, early stopping, VI iterations).  
- Limitations & remedies: Discuss pruning and depth usefulness and consider ladder connections, auxiliary variables, or structured VI as potential remedies.  
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses long-range dependence, conceptually appealing integration of deep recurrent priors with conjugate updates, and generally strong empirical/interpretability results.  
- Cons for acceptance: Clarity and factual errors, missing standard metrics and depth ablations, and a heuristic neural objective needing clearer theoretical grounding.
