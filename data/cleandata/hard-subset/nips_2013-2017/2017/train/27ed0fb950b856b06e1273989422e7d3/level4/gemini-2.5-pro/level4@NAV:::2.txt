The authors present a novel approach for modeling complex long-range temporal dependencies in count vector sequences. The method leverages recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, which are realized through gamma distributions. The shape parameters of these gamma distributions, which capture the temporal dynamics, are approximated by a neural network that integrates information from both a prior (higher layer) and the likelihood (lower layer). The network parameters are updated based on the expectation of latent variables, which serves to reduce sampling-induced variance and improve computational efficiency. The model provides an elegant and effective solution to the long-dependency problem in dynamic models. However, the manuscript appears to have been prepared hastily and would benefit from the following revisions:

1.  The manuscript contains several typos that need correction, including a critical error in Equation 10.

2.  A significant modeling choice is the decision to fix the scale parameter of the gamma distributions while only updating the shape parameter. This requires a more detailed discussion and justification, including an analysis of the potential impact of this design choice.

3.  The experimental evaluation is not sufficiently comprehensive. The authors report performance for a two-layer model but do not compare it against variants with a single layer or additional layers. This analysis is crucial for understanding the influence of model depth on performance. Furthermore, a sensitivity analysis of other key hyperparameters, such as the window size and the number of factors, should be included.

4.  To enhance the reader's understanding, I strongly suggest the authors include a detailed graphical illustration of the complete model architecture. A diagram similar to Figure 1 in [1] would be highly effective for this purpose.

5.  The introduction should be updated to discuss recent and relevant work in deep Poisson factor analysis, including key models such as those presented in [2] and [3].

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research,17(163), 1-44, 2016.
