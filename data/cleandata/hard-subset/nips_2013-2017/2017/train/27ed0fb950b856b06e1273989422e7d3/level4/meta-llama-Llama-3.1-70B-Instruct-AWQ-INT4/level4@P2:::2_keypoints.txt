- Proposes DDPFA: a novel Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model using RNNs with Poisson Factor Analysis to capture long-term dependence in count vectors.  
- Uses a deep structure and RNN-based implicit distributions to learn complex short- and long-term factor relationships.  
- Employs variational inference and gradient-descent optimization for latent variable estimation.  
- Thorough background review of existing sequential count-vector methods and their limitations.  
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets showing improved performance over baselines.  
- Strengths: novelty, clear motivation, well-explained model, solid organization, and convincing empirical results.  
- Main weakness: insufficient analysis of fixing the gamma scale parameter while only updating the shape parameter.  
- Experimental evaluation needs to explore model variants (different layer counts) and sensitivity to window size and number of factors.  
- Paper would benefit from more detailed graphical illustrations of the overall model for reader clarity.  
- Missing discussion of recent related work on deep Poisson factor analysis (e.g., refs [2] and [3]) to situate contributions.  
- NIPS criteria: scores high on novelty, clarity, and significance but lower on thoroughness due to experimental and related-work gaps.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated model with clear exposition and strong empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of analysis on key modeling choices, incomplete experimental ablations, and missing related-work comparisons.
