- Proposes a deep dynamic Poisson factorization (DDPFA) using RNN-like structure and hierarchical layers to capture long-range and multi-scale temporal dependence in count data.  
- Inference uses variational methods with optimization of neural network parameters.  
- Factual correction: lines 86–87 incorrectly state modeling nonnegative numbers; the Poisson-randomized Gamma distribution is used instead.  
- Presentation: request to bold best results in Table 2.  
- Evaluation metrics: recommend reporting held-out perplexity (standard for topic-like count data) in addition to/instead of MSE/PMSE.  
- Empirical validation of depth: need direct comparison to a single-layer (shallow) dynamic PF to justify the utility of multiple hierarchical layers.  
- LSTM comparison is useful but insufficient because differing learning paradigms don’t directly justify hierarchical depth.  
- Writing quality: numerous grammatical errors, typos, and inconsistent tense make the paper hard to read and require thorough proofreading and notation standardization.  
- Quality assessment: modeling idea is technically sound; combine Poisson factorization with RNN-like implicit priors and variational inference appropriately, but clearer derivations and assumption discussion are needed.  
- Originality: novel combination relative to prior dynamic PF/PGDS work, building on known deep probabilistic modeling ideas.  
- Significance: promising empirical application to synthetic and real data, but needs stronger empirical evidence (held-out perplexity, ablation vs. single-layer) to establish the contribution of depth.
