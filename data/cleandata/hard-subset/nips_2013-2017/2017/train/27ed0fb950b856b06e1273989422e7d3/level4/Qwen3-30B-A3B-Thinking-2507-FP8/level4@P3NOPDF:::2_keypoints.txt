- Proposes using RNNs to parameterize gamma-distributed implicit posteriors’ shape parameters to model long-term temporal dependencies in count data.  
- Uses expectations of latent variables (instead of sampling) to reduce variance and improve computational efficiency.  
- Strength: core idea is well-motivated and addresses long-dependency challenges in dynamic count models.  
- Presentation issues and typos (e.g., Eq. (10)) reduce clarity; manuscript needs careful proofreading and clearer definitions (e.g., explicitly state gamma within a hierarchical Poisson-gamma model).  
- Methodological concern: authors fix the gamma scale parameter but give no theoretical or empirical justification for its impact on flexibility or why it shouldn’t be learned.  
- Experimental gaps: no ablation studies on depth (single-layer vs. multi-layer) or hyperparameters (temporal window size, number of latent factors), preventing assessment of architectural and hyperparameter effects.  
- Missing model visualization: no schematic showing layer interactions, RNN role in updating shape parameters, or the inference flow, which hampers comprehension.  
- Related work is outdated/incomplete; must compare and contrast with recent deep Poisson factor analysis (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016, Cong et al. 2017) and clarify differences in priors and architectures.  
- Overall: promising and computationally efficient idea but requires major revisions in technical justification, experiments, presentation, and literature context.  
- Recommendation: Major revision—address fixed-scale justification, add comprehensive ablations, include a clear model diagram, and update related-work discussion.
