The paper proposes incorporating long-range temporal dependence into a Poisson factorization model with a deep hierarchical structure. Unlike prior dynamic PF approaches, it uses a recurrent neural network–like architecture to model long-term dependencies. Inference is performed via variational methods, with additional optimization to estimate the neural network parameters. The paper offers solid technical content but is hampered by several grammatical and factual issues. My specific comments are:

1. The statement about modeling nonnegative numbers on lines 86–87 is incorrect. It is the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution that is used to model nonnegative numbers.

2. Please bold the best results in Table 2.

3. In Section 4.2, held-out perplexity would have been a stronger evaluation metric; MSE/PMSE are acceptable but less informative.

4. The limitations of prior work on long-range dependency (references 1 and 2) are adequately addressed. However, to demonstrate the benefit of multiple hierarchical levels, a comparison against a dynamic PF with a single-level (one-layer) architecture would be valuable. The LSTM comparison is useful, but because the learning algorithms differ, it does not convincingly justify the need for multiple hierarchical levels in the proposed method.

5. Numerous grammatical errors and typos make the paper difficult to read. Examples include:

- "Noticing the lack of capturing .."
- "PFA factorized a count matrix" (the tense is inconsistent with the rest of the paragraph)
- "other data with different pattern long-time dependence .."
- "Examples for this including .."
- "shows excellent results on predict and .."
- "However, it is rough for them .."
- "V-dimesnion sequentially .."
- "K-dimension latent variable .."
- "due to the including of feature-wise"
- "recommend systems" etc.
