This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model (DDPFA), a model that builds on Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) to allow for temporal dependencies. In contrast to previous works on dynamic PFA, this paper uses a simplified version of a recurrent neural network to allow for long-term dependencies. Inference is carried out via variational inference, with an extra step to maximize over the neural network parameters. The paper reports experiments on five real-world datasets.

Overall, I found the idea potentially interesting, but I still think the paper needs some improvements in its execution before it can be accepted in a conference like NIPS. Please find my comments below.

The formulation in Equation 4 does not correspond to a traditional RNN structure, as $h_t^{(n)}$ depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and does not depend on previous time steps for the same layer, as in the RNN setup. Is there any specific reason for this simplified structure?

In Section 2, some of the model equations are missing. For instance, it is not clear how to parameterize the Gamma distribution in Equation 4 or what distribution generates $\theta_t$ in Equation 3. Providing these details would enhance the clarity of the model.

I disagree with the discussion regarding "implicit distributions" in lines 110-118. Equations 9 and 10 are actually explicit, as they are Gammas with given shapes and rates. Since the distribution of $\theta_{tk}$ (Equation 9) can be computed in closed form conditioned on $h^{(0)}$, it cannot be called implicit. The same comment applies to $h$ in Equation 10.

The sentence "This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data" (lines 30-31) is unclear. Could you provide more context or elaborate on this statement?

The subsection in lines 133-155 is not clear, particularly the explanation of the "loss function" in Equation 15. A more detailed explanation would be beneficial.

Mean squared error (MSE) and predictive mean squared error (PMSE) are not ideal metrics for evaluating PFA-like models. The results should include at least predictive log-likelihood to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.

The value of K in the experiments (line 207) seems too small. I suggest the authors use larger values, such as K=100 (at least), to better assess the model's performance.

The discussion in lines 224-232 is not clearly connected to Figure 3. It would be helpful to clarify the relationship between the text and the figure.

The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Equations 11-14) can be safely moved to the supplement, as they are not crucial to understanding the main ideas of the paper.

The caption in Figure 4 is difficult to read. I recommend revising it for better clarity.

I strongly recommend following general writing advice about the use of the passive voice, especially in the abstract.

The writing quality is poor, and the paper contains many typos. Here are some examples:
- Line 15, "high-dimensional" (should be "high-dimensional data")
- Line 22, "factorize" (should be "factorization")
- Line 24, "property of the" (should be "property of")
- Lines 29, 34, "Dirichlet" (should be "Dirichlet distribution")
- Line 34, "nested" (should be "nested structure")
- Figure 1, "visual representation" (should be "schematic representation")
- Figure 1, "transmission" (not sure if that's an appropriate word; consider "transition")
- Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
- Many missing articles (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution")

Strengths:
- The paper introduces a new model that combines PFA with a recurrent neural network to capture temporal dependencies.
- The model is evaluated on five real-world datasets, providing a comprehensive assessment of its performance.

Weaknesses:
- The writing quality is poor, and the paper contains many typos.
- Some model equations are missing, and the explanation of the loss function is unclear.
- The discussion regarding "implicit distributions" is incorrect.
- The value of K in the experiments is too small.

Arguments for acceptance:
- The paper introduces a new and potentially interesting model that combines PFA with a recurrent neural network.
- The model is evaluated on a variety of real-world datasets, providing a comprehensive assessment of its performance.

Arguments for rejection:
- The writing quality is poor, and the paper contains many typos.
- Some model equations are missing, and the explanation of the loss function is unclear.
- The discussion regarding "implicit distributions" is incorrect.
- The value of K in the experiments is too small.
