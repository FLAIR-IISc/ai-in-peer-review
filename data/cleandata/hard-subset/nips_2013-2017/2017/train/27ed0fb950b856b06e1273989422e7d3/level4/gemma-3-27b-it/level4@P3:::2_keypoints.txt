- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that combines Poisson Factor Analysis with recurrent neural networks to model sequential count vectors.
- Core idea: use RNNs to capture short- and long-term temporal dependencies in latent factors and represent implicit distributions with neural networks.
- Uses variational inference (mean-field) for parameter estimation to provide scalability over MCMC.
- Claimed advantages include novelty, better long-range dependency modeling, and interpretability from latent factors.
- The generative model and variational procedure are clearly presented, but ELBO derivation and coordinate updates could be explained in more depth.
- The decision to update only Gamma shape parameters while fixing scale parameters lacks justification and needs analysis.
- Manuscript contains minor typos and clarity issues (e.g., Equation 10) and requires careful editing.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: only a two-layer architecture tested and no systematic ablation over layers, window sizes, or factor counts.
- Comparisons are limited (PGDS, LSTM, PFA); more extensive benchmarking against state-of-the-art deep count models is needed.
- Visual presentation is lacking; the paper needs a detailed graphical illustration of the full model.
- Mean-field variational choice is noted but its limitations should be acknowledged and discussed further.
- Related work coverage should be expanded to include recent deep Poisson factor analysis literature and suggested citations [1–3].
- Experimental results are promising on synthetic and real datasets but are insufficient to fully validate the approach.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with promising empirical performance.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues, insufficient experiments, and unjustified modeling assumptions.
- Overall assessment: valuable and promising contribution but requires substantial refinement.
- Recommendation: weak accept with major revisions, including fixing typos, justifying Gamma parameter choices, running comprehensive ablations, adding a model figure, and expanding related work.
