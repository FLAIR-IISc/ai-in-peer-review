The paper proposes a novel approach to incorporating long-range temporal dependence in a Poisson factorization model with a deep structure. Unlike previous works on dynamic Poisson factorization, this paper utilizes a recurrent neural network-like structure to model long-term dependencies. The inference process is carried out using variational inference with optimization routines to determine the neural network parameters. While the paper exhibits good technical content, it is marred by grammatical and factual errors, which are listed below:

1. The statement on lines 86-87 regarding the modeling of nonnegative numbers is incorrect. The Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is actually employed for this purpose.

2. The best results in Table 2 should be highlighted in bold for clarity.

3. In the experiments presented in Section 4.2, held-out perplexity would have been a more robust indicator, although MSE/PMSE are acceptable.

4. The limitations of existing works in modeling long-range dependencies (references 1 and 2) are adequately addressed in this paper. However, to demonstrate the utility of the proposed method with multiple levels of hierarchy, a comparison with dynamic PF using a single-level deep architecture would have been beneficial. While the comparison with LSTM is useful, the learning algorithms differ, rendering this comparison insufficient to justify the need for multiple levels of hierarchy in the proposed method.
