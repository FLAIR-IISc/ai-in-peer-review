This paper introduces a deep dynamic Poisson factorization (PF) model that extends PF to capture temporal dependencies. Unlike prior dynamic PF work, it uses a simplified recurrent neural network (RNN)–like mechanism to model long-term dependencies. Inference is performed via variational inference, with an additional maximization step over the neural network parameters. The paper evaluates the model on five real-world datasets.

Overall, the idea is potentially interesting, but the execution and presentation need significant improvement before the paper is suitable for a venue like NIPS. My detailed comments follow.

- Equation 4 does not correspond to an RNN structure: h_t^{(n)} depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and not on the previous time step within the same layer, as is standard in RNNs. What motivates this simplified architecture? Please clarify the design choice and its implications for modeling temporal dependencies.

- Section 2 omits key parts of the model specification. For example, it’s unclear how the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 is parameterized (what are the shape and rate), and what distribution generates θ_t in Eq. 3. The generative process should be fully and unambiguously specified.

- I disagree with the discussion in lines 110–118 regarding “implicit distributions.” Equations 9 and 10 are explicit Gamma distributions with specified shapes and rates. Since the distribution of θ_{tk} in Eq. 9 can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^{(0)}, it should not be called implicit. The same applies to h in Eq. 10.

- Lines 30–31: The sentence “This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear. Please explain what is meant by “different pattern long-time dependence” and “fanatical data,” and provide concrete examples or rephrase.

- The subsection in lines 133–155 is not clear. What exactly is the “loss function” in Eq. 15? Is it the negative ELBO, a predictive objective, or something else? Please define it precisely and justify its use.

- MSE/PMSE are typically not appropriate metrics for PF-like models. Please report predictive log-likelihood (or per-event log-likelihood) and, if applicable, ranking-based metrics (e.g., recall/precision at k) for recommendation-like settings.

- The choice of K in the experiments (line 207) seems too small. I recommend exploring larger values, such as K = 100 (at least), and performing sensitivity analysis to show robustness to K.

- The discussion in lines 224–232 does not clearly connect to Figure 3. Please explain what aspects of the figure the text refers to (e.g., patterns over time, layer-specific dynamics) and how they support your claims.

- The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11–14) could be moved to the supplement to improve readability, with a high-level summary in the main text and clear references.

- The caption of Figure 4 is difficult to read. Please enlarge the font, improve contrast, and ensure the caption is self-contained.

- Writing and style: I strongly recommend reducing the overuse of the passive voice, especially in the abstract, and improving sentence structure throughout. The paper contains many typos and grammatical errors, which impede understanding. Examples include:
  - Line 15: “high-dimensional”
  - Line 22: “factorize”
  - Line 24: “property of the”
  - Lines 29, 34: “Dirichlet”
  - Line 34: “nested”
  - Figure 1: “visual representation”
  - Figure 1: “transmission” (this term may not be appropriate in context)
  - Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
  - Missing articles (e.g., “Although the Dirichlet distribution is often used as a prior distribution”)

In summary, the paper proposes a promising direction—combining dynamic PF with a deep temporal mechanism—but the current version lacks a precise and complete model specification, makes questionable claims about “implicit” distributions, relies on suboptimal evaluation metrics, and suffers from clarity and presentation issues. Addressing the modeling gaps, clarifying the temporal architecture, strengthening the evaluation (including larger K and predictive log-likelihood), and substantially improving the writing would significantly improve the paper.
