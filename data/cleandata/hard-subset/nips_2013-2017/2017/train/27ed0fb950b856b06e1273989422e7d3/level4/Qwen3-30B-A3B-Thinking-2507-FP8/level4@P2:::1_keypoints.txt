- Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) using RNNs and variational inference to model sequential count data and long-term temporal dependencies.  
- Experimental comparison against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA on synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS corpus, Ebola).  
- Relevance: tackles an important problem with applications in recommender systems, text, and time series analysis.  
- Novelty appears limited — mainly an implementation of neural networks within existing Poisson factorization frameworks.  
- Major technical issue: Equation 4 does not implement a standard RNN — hidden state h_t^(n) lacks dependency on previous timesteps for the same layer.  
- Model equations are incomplete/confusing: Gamma parameterizations and generation details (Eq. 3, Eq. 4) are unspecified.  
- Misleading claim about “implicit distributions”: Equations 9–10 are explicit Gamma distributions, contrary to the text.  
- Several unclear or vague statements (e.g., “fanatical data and disaster data”, unclear discussion of pruning and layer usefulness).  
- Inappropriate evaluation metrics: MSE and PMSE are not standard for Poisson factorization; predictive log-likelihood or probabilistic metrics should be used.  
- Experimental setup concerns: choosing K=3 is unrealistically small for large datasets (e.g., NIPS V=14,036); larger K should be tested or justified.  
- Poor writing quality with grammatical errors, typos, and passive constructions; abstract needs rewriting.  
- Loss function and optimization are not properly defined (Eq. 15 and derivation lines 133–155) — need clearer explanation of NN parameter optimization.  
- Problematic figures and captions: Figure 4 caption unreadable and Figure 2’s relation to text is unclear.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but can be reconsidered after substantial revisions — clarify/correct technical formulation, use appropriate metrics, and improve writing and presentation.
