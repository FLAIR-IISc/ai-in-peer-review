- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), a novel extension of Poisson Factor Analysis for sequential count vectors.  
- Incorporates a deep structure to capture long-range temporal dependencies.  
- Uses an RNN-like structure for temporal modeling and variational inference for parameter estimation.  
- Well-motivated with a clear overview of existing dynamic Poisson factorization literature.  
- Strong technical content with detailed model and inference algorithm description.  
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Empirical results show DDPFA outperforms PGDS and LSTM baselines.  
- Lacks detailed comparison to single-level dynamic Poisson factor models to justify multi-level hierarchy.  
- Needs more insight into interpretability of learned factors and how they capture long-range dependencies.  
- Paper contains grammatical errors and typos that reduce readability.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model, clear presentation, comprehensive experiments with strong results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient baseline comparisons, limited interpretability analysis, and language issues.  
- Reviewer rating 7/10, confidence 8/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
