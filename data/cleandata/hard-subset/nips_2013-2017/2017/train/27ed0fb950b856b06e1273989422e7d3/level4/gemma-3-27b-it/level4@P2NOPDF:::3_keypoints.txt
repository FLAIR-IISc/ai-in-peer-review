- Contribution: Introduces RNN-based deep recurrent structure into Poisson Factorization to model long-range temporal dependencies, using variational inference and NN optimization; technically strong.

- Factual inaccuracy: The paper’s claim about modeling nonnegative numbers (lines 86–87) is incorrect — the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is the appropriate method.

- Table presentation: Bold the best-performing results in Table 2 to improve readability and highlight key findings.

- Evaluation metrics: Experiments rely on MSE/PMSE; add held-out perplexity as a stronger, more standard evaluation for this task.

- Ablation/comparison: Require a direct comparison to a dynamic PF with a single-level deep architecture to isolate benefits of the proposed multi-level hierarchy; LSTM comparison alone is insufficient.

- Language and clarity: Numerous grammatical errors and typos (e.g., “unit the data”, “PFA factorized a count matrix”, “V-dimesnion sequentially”, etc.) hinder readability and need thorough proofreading.

- Recommendation: Novel and potentially valuable work but needs correction of factual errors, stronger intra-framework comparisons, and substantial copyediting before publication.
