This paper proposes a novel method for capturing complex long-term dependencies in sequences of count vectors. The core idea is to use recurrent neural networks to model the parameters of implicit Gamma distributions over latent variables. Specifically, the shape parameters of these Gamma distributions, which capture the temporal dependencies, are modeled by a neural network that integrates top-down information from the prior (higher layers) and bottom-up information from the likelihood (lower layers). The neural network parameters are updated using the expectation of the latent variables, which helps reduce the variance associated with sampling-based methods and increases computational efficiency.

The model provides an interesting and clever approach to handling the problem of long-term dependencies in dynamic models. While I am positive about the core contribution, the manuscript appears to have been written in haste and could be significantly improved. I have the following comments:

1.  The paper contains some critical typos that hinder understanding, for example, in Equation 10. The manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading.

2.  The authors fix the scale parameter of the Gamma distributions and only learn the shape parameter. The motivation and implications of this modeling choice require more detailed discussion and analysis.

3.  The experiments only evaluate the proposed model with two layers. To better understand the impact of model depth, an ablation study comparing performance with a single layer and with more than two layers would be beneficial. Additionally, the influence of other key hyperparameters, such as the window size and the number of factors, should be analyzed.

4.  To improve clarity, I suggest the authors include a detailed graphical illustration of the overall model architecture. The authors could refer to Figure 1 in [1] for an example of an effective model diagram.

5.  The introduction should discuss recent related work on deep Poisson factor analysis models, such as those presented in [2] and [3], to better position the contribution of this work.

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research, 17(163), 1-44, 2016.
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou, Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC, ICML 2017.
