- Proposed method: uses RNNs to parameterize gamma shape parameters to model long-term dependencies in count vectors, updating NN parameters via expectations of latent variables to reduce sampling variance and improve efficiency.

- Strength: innovative and promising approach for capturing complex long-time dependencies in dynamic models.

- Manuscript quality: contains critical typos (notably Equation 10) that must be corrected for clarity and correctness.

- Modeling assumption: treating the gamma scale parameter as fixed while only updating shape parameters needs deeper justification and analysis of limitations.

- Experiments: evaluation is limited to only two-layer models with no comparisons to single-layer or deeper variants, making the effect of layer depth unclear.

- Experimental analysis: other hyperparameters (window size, number of factors) are not sufficiently explored or discussed.

- Presentation: include detailed graphical illustrations of the full model (e.g., similar to Fig.1 in Chung et al. 2015) to improve readability.

- Related work: recent deep Poisson/gamma belief network papers ([2], [3]) should be discussed in the Introduction to situate the contribution.
