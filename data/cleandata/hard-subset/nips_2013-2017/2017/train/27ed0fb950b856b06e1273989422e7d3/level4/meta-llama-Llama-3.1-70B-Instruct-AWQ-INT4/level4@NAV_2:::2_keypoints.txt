- Proposes a novel method using RNNs to represent implicit gamma-distributed latent variables to capture complex long-term dependence in count vectors.  
- Shape parameters of the gamma distributions are approximated by neural networks that integrate prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) information.  
- Parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables, which reduces sampling variance and improves computational efficiency.  
- The model offers an interesting and effective approach to handling long-range dependency in dynamic Poisson factor analysis.  
- The paper is well-structured and generally explains the model and components clearly.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and demonstrate the proposed model’s effectiveness compared to existing methods.  
- Manuscript needs careful proofreading to remove typos and grammatical errors (e.g., typo in Equation 10).  
- Treating the gamma scale parameter as fixed (only updating shape) needs detailed discussion and justification.  
- Provide more comprehensive comparisons across different numbers of layers and report effects of other hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).  
- Include detailed graphical illustrations of the full model to aid reader understanding (e.g., similar to Figure 1 in cited work).  
- Discuss recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis (e.g., cited [2], [3]) in the introduction to contextualize the contribution.  
- Recommendation: minor revision — address the above issues to strengthen the paper’s contribution and clarity.
