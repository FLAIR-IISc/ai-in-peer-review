

This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, which extends Poisson factorization (PF) to incorporate temporal dependencies. Unlike prior work on dynamic PF, this approach employs a simplified recurrent neural network (RNN) structure to capture long-term dependencies. Inference is performed via variational inference, with an additional step to maximize over the neural network parameters. The paper reports experiments on five real-world datasets.

Overall, the core idea holds potential, but the paper requires significant revisions to meet NIPS standards. Below are specific concerns:

+ Equation (4) does not represent a standard RNN structure, as $h_t^{(n)}$ depends solely on top-layer memory vectors and not on prior time steps for the same layer. Please clarify the rationale for this simplified design.
+ Section 2 omits key model specifications (e.g., parameterization of the Gamma distribution in Equation 4, and the generative distribution for $\theta_t$ in Equation 3).
+ I disagree with the discussion in lines 110–118 regarding "implicit distributions." Equations (9) and (10) are explicit: they define Gamma distributions with specified shape and rate parameters. Since $\theta_{tk}$ (Equation 9) has a closed-form expression conditioned on $h^{(0)}$, it cannot be termed "implicit." The same applies to $h$ in Equation (10).
+ Lines 30–31 state: "This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data." The phrasing is unclear; please elaborate.
+ The subsection in lines 133–155 is unclear. Specifically, the "loss function" referenced in Equation (15) requires explicit definition.
+ MSE/PMSE is inappropriate for PF-like models. Results must include predictive log-likelihood alongside these metrics.
+ The value of $K$ used in experiments (line 207) appears too small. I recommend testing larger values, e.g., $K=100$ (at minimum).
+ The discussion in lines 224–232 does not clearly connect to Figure 3.
+ The variational inference update equations (Equations 11–14) may be moved to the supplement.
+ Figure 4’s caption is illegible.
+ The abstract (and throughout) overuses passive voice; please adhere to active voice conventions.
+ The writing quality is poor, containing numerous typographical errors and grammatical issues. Examples include:
  - Line 15: "high-dimensional" (missing article: "a high-dimensional")
  - Line 22: "factorize" (should be "factorization")
  - Line 24: "property of the" (incomplete phrase)
  - Lines 29, 34: "Dirichlet" (inconsistent capitalization)
  - Line 34: "nested" (likely a typo for "nested" or "nested")
  - Figure 1 caption: "visual representation" (redundant)
  - Figure 1 caption: "transmission" (questionable terminology)
  - Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
  - Missing articles (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution")
