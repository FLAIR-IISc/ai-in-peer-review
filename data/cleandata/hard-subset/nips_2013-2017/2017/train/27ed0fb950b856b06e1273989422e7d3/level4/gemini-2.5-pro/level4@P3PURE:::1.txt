This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, which extends Poisson Factorization (PF) to incorporate temporal dependencies. Unlike previous dynamic PF models, this work employs a simplified recurrent neural network (RNN) structure to capture long-term temporal patterns. Model inference is performed using variational inference, augmented with a maximization step for the neural network parameters. The proposed model is evaluated on five real-world datasets.

While the core idea of integrating deep recurrent structures with Poisson factorization is promising, the paper suffers from significant issues in its current form, including a lack of clarity in the model description, questionable experimental choices, and poor overall presentation. I believe the paper requires substantial revision before it can meet the standards for publication at NIPS.

My detailed comments are below.

**Major Issues with Model Formulation and Clarity**

*   **RNN Structure:** The model's recurrent structure, as formulated in Eq. 4, is unclear. Specifically, h_t^{(n)} appears to depend only on the top-layer memory vectors and not on its own state from the previous time step, h_{t-1}^{(n)}. This deviates from a standard RNN architecture. The authors should clarify the motivation behind this simplified structure and discuss its implications.
*   **Incomplete Model Specification:** The model description in Section 2 is incomplete, making it difficult to fully understand and reproduce the work. For instance, it is not specified how the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 is parameterized, nor is the distribution that generates `theta_t` in Eq. 3 explicitly defined.
*   **Misuse of "Implicit Distributions":** I disagree with the characterization of the distributions in lines 110-118 as "implicit." The distributions for `theta_tk` (Eq. 9) and `h` (Eq. 10) are conditional Gamma distributions whose parameters are explicitly defined. Since their densities can be computed in closed form, the term "implicit" is misleading.
*   **Unclear Inference Procedure:** The discussion of the inference procedure in lines 133-155 is confusing. In particular, the definition and role of the "loss function" in Eq. 15 are not clearly explained.

**Issues with Experimental Design and Evaluation**

*   **Inappropriate Evaluation Metric:** The choice of Mean Squared Error (MSE/PMSE) is not a suitable metric for Poisson factorization models, which are designed for count data. The evaluation should include metrics better suited to probabilistic models, such as predictive log-likelihood.
*   **Choice of K:** The number of latent factors used in the experiments (K=20, line 207) seems too small for the datasets in question. A more thorough evaluation would involve experimenting with larger values (e.g., K=100 at a minimum) to better assess the model's capacity.
*   **Figure Interpretation:** The connection between the discussion in lines 224-232 and the results presented in Figure 3 is not clear. The authors should explicitly guide the reader in interpreting the figure in the context of their claims.

**Presentation and Writing Quality**

*   **Clarity of Motivation:** The motivation provided in lines 30-31 is difficult to understand. The sentence, "This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data," requires clarification and elaboration.
*   **Paper Structure:** The variational update equations (Eqs. 11-14) are standard and could be moved to the supplementary material to improve the flow of the main text.
*   **Figure Quality:** The caption for Figure 4 is illegible.
*   **Writing Style and Grammar:** The paper's overall writing quality is poor and needs significant improvement.
    *   I strongly recommend revising the paper to use the active voice where appropriate, especially in the abstract, to improve clarity.
    *   The manuscript is replete with grammatical errors, typos, and awkward phrasing. Below is a non-exhaustive list of examples:
        *   Line 15: "high-dimensional"
        *   Line 22: "factorize"
        *   Line 24: "property of the"
        *   Lines 29, 34: "Dirichlet"
        *   Line 34: "nested"
        *   Fig. 1: "visual representation"
        *   Fig. 1: The word "transmission" seems inappropriate in this context.
        *   Line 49: Incomplete sentence.
        *   Numerous missing articles throughout (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution...").
