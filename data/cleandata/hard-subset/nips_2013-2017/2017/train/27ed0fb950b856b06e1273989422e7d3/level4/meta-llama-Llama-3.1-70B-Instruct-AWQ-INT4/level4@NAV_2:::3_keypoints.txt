- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model to analyze sequential count vectors using Poisson Factor Analysis with neural-network-based temporal dependence.  
- Represents temporal dependence via implicit distributions learned by neural networks.  
- Demonstrates strong predictive and fitting performance on synthetic and real-world datasets with interpretable latent factors.  
- Positions work relative to Bayesian factor analysis and sequential count models, arguing prior models struggle with long-range time dependence.  
- Introduces a deep, dynamic temporal structure to capture complex time dependencies beyond existing models.  
- Strength: captures complex time dependence effectively.  
- Strength: uses variational inference for computational efficiency.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation including comparisons to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  
- Weakness: writing quality suffers from grammatical errors, typos, and occasional unclearness.  
- Weakness: paper lacks sufficient background/context about the problem and relevance of the approach.  
- Suggestion: clarify the statement on lines 86–87 about modeling nonnegative numbers.  
- Suggestion: bold the best results in Table 2 for readability.  
- Suggestion: consider reporting held-out perplexity as a stronger experimental metric in Section 4.2.  
- Suggestion: add a comparison with a one-level dynamic Poisson factor model to justify the multi-level hierarchy.  
- Suggestion: correct grammatical errors and typos throughout the manuscript.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel and innovative approach with thorough evaluation and potential contribution to Bayesian factor analysis and deep learning.  
- Argument against acceptance: unclear writing, insufficient contextual motivation, and the LSTM comparison doesn’t fully justify the need for multiple hierarchical levels.
