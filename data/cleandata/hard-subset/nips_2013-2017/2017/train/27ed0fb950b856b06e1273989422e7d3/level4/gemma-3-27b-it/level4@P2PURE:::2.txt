The authors present a novel method for modeling complex long-term dependencies in count vector data by leveraging recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions. Specifically, they model these distributions with gamma distributions, where the shape parameters – capturing temporal dependencies – are approximated by neural networks. These networks integrate information from both prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) components. To improve computational efficiency and reduce variance, network parameters are updated using the expectation of latent variables. This approach offers an interesting and promising solution to the long-dependency problem in dynamic models. However, the manuscript appears somewhat rushed and I have the following comments:

1. There are several critical typos present, such as in equation 10.
2. The authors fix the scale parameter of the gamma distributions and only update the shape parameter. This design choice requires more detailed justification and analysis.
3. The experiments only evaluate a two-layer model; a comparison with single-layer and deeper models is necessary to understand the impact of network depth on performance. Furthermore, a discussion of other relevant parameters, such as window size and the number of factors, is needed.
4. For clarity, I recommend including a detailed graphical illustration of the complete model. The authors may find Figure 1 in [1] to be a useful reference.
5. Recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis, such as those presented in [2] and [3], should be discussed in the Introduction to provide appropriate context.

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research,17(163), 1-44, 2016.
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou, Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC, ICML 2017.
