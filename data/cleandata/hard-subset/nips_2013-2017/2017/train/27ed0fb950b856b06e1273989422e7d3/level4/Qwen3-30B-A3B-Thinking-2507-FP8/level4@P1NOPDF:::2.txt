

The authors propose a novel method to model complex long-term dependencies in count vector data by leveraging recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to represent implicit distributions via gamma distributions. Specifically, the shape parameters of these gamma distributions—which encode temporal dependencies—are approximated using neural networks that integrate information from the prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer). Parameters are updated based on the expectation of latent variables, reducing variance from sampling and improving computational efficiency. The approach offers a promising solution to long-dependency challenges in dynamic models. While the core idea is compelling, the manuscript requires refinement for clarity and completeness. My specific comments follow:

1.  **Typos and Notation**: Equation (10) contains critical typographical errors that require correction.
2.  **Fixed Scale Parameter**: The authors fix the scale parameter while updating only the shape parameter in the gamma distributions. A detailed justification for this choice—particularly its impact on model flexibility and performance—is needed.
3.  **Layer Architecture Analysis**: Experiments evaluate only a two-layer model, omitting comparisons with single-layer and multi-layer variants. This gap prevents understanding how layer count influences performance. Similarly, key hyperparameters like window size and factor dimensionality lack thorough discussion.
4.  **Model Visualization**: A detailed graphical illustration of the full architecture would significantly improve clarity. The authors should consider adopting a structure similar to Figure 1 in [1] for better exposition.
5.  **Related Work**: The introduction should discuss recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis, including [2] and [3], to position the work within the current literature.

[1] Chung, J., Kastner, K., Dinh, L., et al. (2015). A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. *Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems*, 28.  
[2] Zhou, M., Cong, Y., & Chen, B. (2016). Augmentable Gamma Belief Networks. *Journal of Machine Learning Research*, 17(163), 1–44.  
[3] Cong, Y., Chen, B., Liu, H., & Zhou, M. (2017). Deep Latent Dirichlet Allocation with Topic-Layer-Adaptive Stochastic Gradient Riemannian MCMC. *Proceedings of the 34th International Conference on Machine Learning*, 1482–1491.
