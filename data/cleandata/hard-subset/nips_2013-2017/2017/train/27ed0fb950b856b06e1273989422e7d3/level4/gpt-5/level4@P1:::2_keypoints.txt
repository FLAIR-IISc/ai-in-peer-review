- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) — Poisson factorization with Gamma priors whose shapes are RNN-defined hierarchical latent variables, inferred via two-stage VI + “Probability AutoEncoder” training.

- Strength: Addresses modeling long-range dependencies in high-dimensional, sparse count time series by combining interpretable Poisson–Gamma models with flexible RNN temporal priors.

- Strength: Inference leverages Poisson–Gamma conjugacy and expectations under Gamma variational distributions to avoid high-variance gradient estimators.

- Strength: Empirical results show improved fit/prediction (MSE/PMSE) over PFA and LSTM on several datasets and produce interpretable temporal factors.

- Strength: Authors candidly discuss pruning and limited benefits of added depth, which is useful.

- Weakness: Manuscript contains numerous typos, unclear statements, and inconsistent notation (e.g., Eq.10, shape vs rate/scale), making the paper hard to follow.

- Weakness: Model appears to fix Gamma rate/scale parameters while only learning shapes via RNNs; this design choice lacks justification and analysis of sensitivity, identifiability, scaling ambiguities, and effect on posterior variance.

- Weakness: The “Probability AutoEncoder” objective departs from standard ELBO training; its derivation is informal and the role of auxiliary variables and expectation matching is not rigorously justified.

- Clarification needed: Explain how the training objective relates to the marginal likelihood or a defined divergence, and why reparameterized gradients or differentiable VB for Gamma variables are unsuitable.

- Clarification needed: Clarify whether layer-wise training plus fine-tuning optimizes a coherent objective and how convergence is monitored.

- Weakness: Insufficient ablations and hyperparameter sensitivity analyses — need controlled comparisons for depth (1 vs 2 vs deeper) with matched parameter counts, window size c, number of factors K, and inclusion/exclusion of λk and ψv.

- Clarification needed: Analyze sensitivity to fixed rate/scale parameters and priors, and report training time and memory comparisons.

- Weakness: Baselines and related work coverage should be improved — explicitly compare and discuss Gamma Belief Networks, deep Poisson factorization work, sequential VAEs (Chung et al. 2015), and recurrent Poisson factorization baselines.

- Reproducibility: Provide precise architectural and optimization details (RNN type, hidden sizes, activations, learning rates, batch sizes, regularization, stopping criteria), handling of zeros/missing counts, and multinomial augmentation implementation for large V,T.

- Specific comment: Provide a detailed graphical model figure showing Φ, Θ, Λ, Ψ, h(n), and RNN dependencies, and fix critical typos and inconsistent notation.

- Specific comment: Expand and quantify the “ladder” variant results (when deeper layers help), referencing relevant prior work (e.g., Zhao et al., 2017).

- Specific comment: Analyze ICEWS forecasting where PGDS outperforms DDPFA — possible causes (overfitting, window length, horizon mismatch) and remedies like smoothing or regularization.

- Specific comment: Provide credible intervals or uncertainty measures for forecasts given the probabilistic model.

- Evaluation — Quality: Modeling idea is sound and conjugacy is used correctly, but neural-component training needs clearer derivation and justification.

- Evaluation — Clarity: Paper requires substantial editing to fix typos, notation issues, and missing/unclear figures.

- Evaluation — Originality: Novel combination of RNN-defined Gamma priors with Poisson factorization; training approach is unconventional.

- Evaluation — Significance: Potentially significant for sequential count modeling if clarified and supported by stronger ablations.

- Pros for acceptance: Creative integration of deep temporal priors with count modeling and strong empirical performance with interpretable factors.

- Cons for acceptance: Clarity and correctness issues, insufficient theoretical justification for training objective, and limited ablations/sensitivity analyses.

- Overall recommendation: Promising idea but requires revision to address clarity, rigorous treatment of the learning objective, comprehensive ablations, and justification for design choices (especially fixed rate parameters and depth).
