- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that adds RNN-style temporal modeling to Poisson Factor Analysis for sequential count data.
- Uses variational inference plus neural-network parameter optimization; evaluated on four synthetic and five real-world datasets.
- Strength: tackles an important problem—modeling sequential count data.
- Strength: novel integration of recurrent neural networks with PFA to capture short- and long-term dependencies.
- Strength: empirical evaluation includes multiple real-world datasets.
- Weakness: RNN formulation is nonstandard—hidden state at time t doesn’t depend on previous same-layer states, with no justification.
- Weakness: several model equations are incomplete or ambiguous (Gamma parameterization, distribution for θ_t).
- Weakness: incorrect terminology—distributions labeled “implicit” are actually explicit Gamma distributions in Eqs. 9–10.
- Weakness: unclear explanations in multiple passages (e.g., “fanatical data and disaster data”) and a poorly explained loss-function subsection.
- Weakness: evaluation relies only on MSE/PMSE; predictive log-likelihood and other probabilistic metrics are missing.
- Weakness: limited exploration of factor number K (appears small); sensitivity to K (e.g., K=100) not examined.
- Weakness: figures and captions lack clarity or connection to the text (Figure 3 discussion and illegible Figure 4 caption).
- Weakness: writing quality is poor with many typos, grammatical errors, and excessive passive voice.
- Argument for acceptance: idea is novel and relevant and shows promising empirical results.
- Argument against acceptance: technical presentation is flawed, unclear, and missing key evaluations and metrics.
- Overall assessment: interesting concept but currently insufficient for acceptance; requires major revision.
- Required fixes: provide complete model specifications, justify architectural choices, improve clarity and writing, and strengthen empirical evaluation (include predictive log-likelihood and sensitivity analyses).
