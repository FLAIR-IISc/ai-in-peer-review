- Paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA): a Gamma-Poisson hierarchical generative model with neural-network–parameterized temporal priors and hybrid inference.
- Reviewer finds the core idea of integrating deep nets into dynamic Poisson factorization interesting but not yet ready for acceptance.
- Model formulation is ambiguous: described RNN structure appears nonstandard and may omit dependence on previous layer/time-step; authors should clarify and justify.
- Key distributional forms are missing or underspecified (e.g., how Gamma parameters are output by the network).
- Terminology misuse: conditional Gamma distributions are treated as "implicit" despite being closed-form; this is confusing and should be corrected.
- Inference description is unclear: the "Probability AutoEncoder" objective (Eq.15) is not standard and its connection to later L2 losses is not derived or justified.
- Vague claims about prior methods (e.g., being weak for "fanatical data and disaster data") lack explanation of the motivating temporal patterns.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: MSE/PMSE are inappropriate for count data; predictive log-likelihood on held-out data should be reported.
- Experiments use unrealistically small K (3 or 10) for high-dimensional corpora like NIPS; scalability and performance with larger K (e.g., ~100) are not tested.
- Qualitative claims about learned factors are unsupported by Figure 3, which lacks labels/context to link visualizations to asserted events.
- Writing and presentation are poor: model/readability would benefit from moving routine variational updates to an appendix and thorough proofreading.
- Numerous grammatical errors, typos, awkward phrasing, and overuse of passive voice impede comprehension.
- Overall conclusion: promising idea but undermined by lack of clarity, weak experiments, and poor writing; not ready for publication in current form.
