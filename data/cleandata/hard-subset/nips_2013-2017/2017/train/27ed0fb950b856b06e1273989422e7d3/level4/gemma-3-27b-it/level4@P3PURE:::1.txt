This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, which builds upon Poisson Factorization (PF) to model temporal dependencies. In contrast to previous work on dynamic PF, this paper employs a simplified recurrent neural network structure to capture long-term dependencies. Inference is performed via variational inference, with an additional optimization step to maximize the neural network parameters. The authors report experiments on five real-world datasets.

Overall, I find the core idea potentially interesting, but believe the paper requires substantial improvements in its execution before it would be suitable for acceptance at a conference like NIPS. Please see my detailed comments below.

+ The formulation in Eq. 4 does not correspond to a standard RNN structure. Specifically, h<sub>t</sub><sup>(n)</sup> depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and not on previous time steps within the same layer, as would be expected in a recurrent setup. Is there a specific reason for this simplification?

+ In Section 2, several model equations are incomplete, hindering understanding. For example, the parameterization of the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 is not specified, nor is the distribution from which θ<sub>t</sub> is generated in Eq. 3.

+ I disagree with the discussion regarding “implicit distributions” in lines 110-118. Equations 9 and 10 are, in fact, explicit: they define Gamma distributions with specified shape and rate parameters. Since the distribution of θ<sub>tk</sub> (Eq. 9) can be computed in closed form conditioned on h<sup>(0)</sup>, it cannot be considered implicit. The same argument applies to h in Eq. 10.

+ Lines 30-31 contain the sentence “This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data,” which is unclear. Could the authors elaborate on these potential weaknesses and the types of data to which they apply?

+ The subsection in lines 133-155 is unclear. What exactly constitutes the “loss function” in Eq. 15?

+ MSE/PMSE are not typically appropriate metrics for evaluating PF-like models. The results should include, at a minimum, predictive log-likelihood.

+ The value of K used in the experiments (line 207) appears insufficiently large. I suggest the authors consider larger values, such as K=100, or higher.

+ I was unable to establish a clear connection between the discussion in lines 224-232 and Fig. 3. Clarification is needed.

+ The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11-14) could be safely moved to the supplementary material.

+ The caption in Fig. 4 is illegible.

+ I strongly recommend that the authors review general writing advice regarding the use of the passive voice, particularly in the abstract.

+ The overall writing quality is poor, and the paper contains numerous typos. Some examples include:
 - Line 15: “high-dimensional”
 - Line 22: “factorize”
 - Line 24: “property of the”
 - Lines 29, 34: “Dirichlet”
 - Line 34: “nested”
 - Fig. 1: “visual representation”
 - Fig. 1: “transmission” (the appropriateness of this term is questionable)
 - Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
 - Missing articles (e.g., “Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution”)
