

This paper proposes a deep dynamic Poisson factorization (DDPF) model that extends Poisson factorization (PF) to capture temporal dependencies using a simplified recurrent neural network (RNN) architecture. Unlike prior dynamic PF approaches, the method employs a streamlined RNN structure to model long-term dependencies, with inference performed via variational inference augmented by optimization over neural network parameters. The authors evaluate the model on five real-world datasets. While the core idea shows promise, significant revisions are required to address technical gaps, presentation issues, and methodological weaknesses before consideration for NIPS.

**Major Technical Concerns**  
1.  **RNN Formulation (Eq. 4):** The model’s recurrence (h_t^{(n)} depending solely on top-layer memory vectors, not prior timesteps for the same layer) deviates from standard RNN structure. The authors must justify this simplification and clarify how it enables long-term dependencies.  
2.  **Missing Model Specification (Section 2):** Key equations lack critical details. For instance, the parameterization of the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 is undefined, and the generative process for θ_t (Eq. 3) is unclear.  
3.  **Mischaracterization of Distributions (Lines 110-118):** The claim that Eqs. 9–10 define "implicit distributions" is incorrect. Both are explicit Gamma distributions with closed-form parameterizations (given h^{(0)} for θ_tk in Eq. 9, and given h in Eq. 10).  
4.  **Unclear Terminology (Lines 30-31):** The phrase "fanatical data and disaster data" appears to contain a typo (e.g., "fan-out" or "fanatic"?) and lacks context. Please clarify the intended meaning.  
5.  **Loss Function Ambiguity (Lines 133-155):** The "loss function" referenced in Eq. 15 requires explicit definition. Is this the variational lower bound, or a distinct objective?  
6.  **Inappropriate Evaluation Metric (Section 4.1):** Reporting MSE/PMSE is inadequate for PF models. Predictive log-likelihood must be included as a primary metric to properly assess model fit.  
7.  **Underpowered Experiment (Line 207):** The choice of K=100 appears excessively large for typical PF applications (e.g., K=10–50 is standard). The authors should justify this value or test smaller K to ensure meaningful results.  
8.  **Fig. 3 Misalignment (Lines 224-232):** The discussion fails to connect with Fig. 3’s visualization. Explicitly state which aspects of the figure support the claims made.  

**Presentation and Editorial Issues**  
- **Writing Quality:** The manuscript contains pervasive grammatical errors, typos, and poor phrasing (e.g., "high-dimensional" [Line 15], "factorize" [Line 22], "property of the" [Line 24], missing articles like "the" before "Dirichlet distribution" [Line 34], and incomplete sentences [Line 49]).  
- **Figure 4 Caption:** The text is illegible and requires revision for readability.  
- **Passive Voice (Abstract):** The abstract uses excessive passive voice (e.g., "it is not clear how..."). Adhere to active voice conventions throughout.  
- **Supplemental Material:** The variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) are mechanical and should be moved to the supplement.  

**Recommendations for Revision**  
1.  **Clarify the RNN design** in Eq. 4 and justify its departure from standard recurrence.  
2.  **Complete all model specifications** in Section 2 (Gamma parameterization, θ_t generation).  
3.  **Correct the mischaracterization** of Eqs. 9–10 as "implicit" and explicitly state their closed-form nature.  
4.  **Revise ambiguous phrases** (e.g., "fanatical data") with clear, technical terms.  
5.  **Define the loss function** (Eq. 15) precisely and replace MSE/PMSE with predictive log-likelihood.  
6.  **Justify the choice of K** (Line 207) or conduct experiments with standard values (e.g., K=10–50).  
7.  **Align Fig. 3 discussion** with the figure’s content.  
8.  **Thoroughly proofread** the manuscript to eliminate grammatical errors, typos, and passive voice.  

The core contribution—leveraging a simplified RNN for temporal dependencies in PF—has potential, but the current presentation lacks technical rigor and clarity. Addressing these points would substantially strengthen the paper. Without revisions to the model justification, evaluation metrics, and writing quality, the work does not meet NIPS standards. I recommend rejection pending major revisions.
