This paper introduces a deep dynamic Poisson factorization (PF) model that extends PF to capture temporal dependencies. Unlike prior work on dynamic PF, it employs a simplified recurrent neural network (RNN)–like component to model long-term dependencies. Inference is performed via variational inference, with an additional optimization step over the neural network parameters. Experiments are reported on five real-world datasets.

Overall assessment
- The idea is potentially interesting and relevant, but the execution and presentation need significant improvement before the work is ready for a venue like NIPS. Several aspects of the model specification, interpretation, and evaluation are unclear or incomplete.

Major comments
- RNN structure (Eq. 4): The formulation in Eq. 4 does not correspond to an RNN, because h_t^{(n)} depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and not on the previous time step for the same layer, as in a standard RNN. Please justify this simplified structure and discuss its implications for modeling temporal dependencies.
- Missing/unclear model equations (Section 2): Some key details are missing. For example, it is unclear how the Gamma in Eq. 4 is parameterized, and what distribution generates theta_t in Eq. 3. Please provide the complete generative process with precise parameterizations.
- “Implicit distributions” (lines 110–118): I disagree with the characterization of Eqs. 9 and 10 as “implicit.” Both are explicit Gamma distributions with specified shapes and rates. Since the distribution of theta_{t,k} (Eq. 9) can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^{(0)}, it should not be called implicit; the same applies to h in Eq. 10. Please revise the discussion accordingly.
- Clarity of claims (lines 30–31): The sentence “This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear. Please clarify what “fanatical data” refers to, what “pattern long-time dependence” means in this context, and how these data challenge the method.
- Loss function (lines 133–155; Eq. 15): The subsection is unclear, especially the definition and role of the “loss function” in Eq. 15. Please define the objective precisely, describe how it relates to the ELBO or other criteria, and specify how it is optimized with respect to both variational and neural network parameters.
- Evaluation metrics: MSE/PMSE are typically not well aligned with PF-like models. Please include predictive log-likelihood (or held-out Poisson log-likelihood) as a primary metric. If using ranking or recommendation metrics, please justify and include them alongside log-likelihood.
- Model capacity (K; line 207): The chosen K appears too small. Please include experiments with larger K (e.g., K = 100 at least) and analyze sensitivity to K to ensure adequate capacity for the datasets.
- Figure/discussion mismatch (lines 224–232 vs. Fig. 3): I could not connect the discussion in lines 224–232 with Fig. 3. Please clarify what the figure is intended to illustrate and how it supports the claims in that section.
- Variational updates in the main text: The update equations for variational inference (Eqs. 11–14) can be moved to the supplement and summarized succinctly in the main paper to free space for clarifying the model and experiments.
- Figure quality: The caption of Fig. 4 is hard to read. Please improve figure and caption legibility (font size, contrast, and self-contained descriptions).

Writing and presentation
- Please reduce excessive passive voice, especially in the abstract.
- The writing quality needs substantial improvement; there are many typos and grammatical issues. Examples include:
  - Line 15: “high-dimensional”
  - Line 22: “factorize”
  - Line 24: “property of the”
  - Lines 29, 34: “Dirichlet”
  - Line 34: “nested”
  - Fig. 1: “visual representation”
  - Fig. 1: “transmission” (this term may not be appropriate)
  - Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
  - Missing articles (e.g., “Although the Dirichlet distribution is often used as a prior distribution”)
- Please also proofread all figures and captions for clarity and correctness.

Summary
- Strengths: Aims to capture long-term temporal dependencies in PF via a deep, RNN-inspired architecture; evaluated on multiple real datasets.
- Weaknesses: Incomplete/unclear model specification; RNN component not truly recurrent across layers/time as claimed; mischaracterization of “implicit” distributions; unclear objective; suboptimal evaluation metrics; potentially underparameterized model; mismatches between text and figures; poor writing quality.

Addressing the modeling clarity, revising the “implicit distribution” discussion, defining and justifying the optimization objective, expanding the evaluation (including predictive log-likelihood), exploring larger K, and improving the writing and figures would significantly strengthen the paper.
