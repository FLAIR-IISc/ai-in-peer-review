- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that augments Poisson Factor Analysis with recurrent neural networks to model sequential count vectors.  
- Demonstrates improved performance over baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA) on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Clear, concise introduction and thorough explanation of the model, including RNN-based implicit distributions and variational inference.  
- Experiments are well-presented and show the model captures complex temporal dependencies and long-term patterns.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a small number of datasets and lacks comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Needs more detailed analysis of results, especially interpretability of learned factors and sensitivity to hyperparameters.  
- Recommend evaluating on more challenging or realistic datasets to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Paper quality is high (clear writing), but contains minor errors and typos to correct.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; strengths: novelty and effectiveness; weaknesses: limited experiments and analysis.
