This paper introduces the Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization model, which extends Poisson Factorization (PF) to incorporate temporal dependencies. Unlike previous dynamic PF models, the proposed approach uses a simplified recurrent neural network structure to capture long-term dynamics. The authors develop a variational inference algorithm, which includes a maximization step for the neural network parameters, and evaluate their model on five real-world datasets.

While the core idea of integrating a recurrent structure into Poisson factorization is promising, I believe the paper requires significant improvements in its current form to meet the standards of a top-tier conference like NIPS. The model's exposition is unclear in several key areas, the experimental evaluation is lacking, and the overall presentation needs substantial revision. My detailed comments are provided below.

**Major Comments**

1.  **Clarity on the Model's Recurrent Structure:** The structure described in Equation 4 does not appear to be a standard recurrent neural network. Specifically, the hidden state at a given layer, h_t^{(n)}, depends on the layer above it but not on its own state at the previous timestep, h_{t-1}^{(n)}. This deviates from the typical RNN formulation. Could the authors clarify the motivation behind this simplified design choice and discuss its implications?

2.  **Incomplete Model Specification:** The model specification in Section 2 is incomplete. For instance, the paper does not specify how the Gamma distribution in Equation 4 is parameterized. It is also unclear what distribution is used to generate θ_t in Equation 3. These details are essential for understanding and reproducing the proposed generative model.

3.  **Misleading Use of "Implicit Distributions":** I found the discussion of "implicit distributions" in lines 110-118 to be misleading. The distributions for θ_tk (Eq. 9) and h (Eq. 10) are, in fact, explicit Gamma distributions. Their shape and rate parameters are deterministic functions of the neural network's output. Since their conditional distributions can be written in closed form, it is inaccurate to describe them as implicit.

4.  **Inadequate Evaluation Metric:** The choice of MSE/PMSE as an evaluation metric is not appropriate for Poisson factorization models, which model count data. A more standard and informative metric for this class of models is the predictive log-likelihood on held-out data. I strongly recommend that the authors include this metric in their experimental results to allow for a fair comparison with related work.

5.  **Insufficient Experimental Scale:** The number of latent factors used in the experiments (K=20, line 207) seems quite small for the datasets considered. This could limit the model's capacity and may not reflect its full potential. I suggest the authors conduct experiments with larger values, such as K=100 or more, to provide a more thorough evaluation.

**Minor Comments and Suggestions for Improvement**

*   **Clarity of the Loss Function:** The explanation in the subsection covering lines 133-155 is difficult to follow. In particular, what does the "loss function" in Equation 15 represent? Is this the negative evidence lower bound (ELBO), or a component of it? Clarifying the terminology would improve the reader's understanding of the optimization procedure.

*   **Unclear Statements:** The sentence in lines 30-31 ("This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data") is unclear. Could the authors please elaborate on what is meant by "fanatical data" and "disaster data" and clarify the point being made here?

*   **Clarity of Figures:**
    *   The connection between the discussion in lines 224-232 and the content of Figure 3 is unclear. The text does not seem to explain what the figure is showing, making it difficult to interpret the results presented.
    *   The caption for Figure 4 is illegible.

*   **Paper Organization:** To improve the readability and flow of the main paper, the detailed variational update equations (Eqs. 11-14) could be moved to the supplementary material. This would allow the main text to focus on the higher-level concepts of the model and inference procedure.

*   **Writing and Presentation:** The paper would benefit from a thorough proofreading to correct numerous typos and grammatical errors. The quality of the writing currently detracts from the paper's contributions. Some examples include: "high-dimentional" (line 15), "factorize" (line 22), missing articles (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used..."), and incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49). I would also suggest revising the text to use the active voice more frequently, particularly in the abstract, to improve clarity and impact.
