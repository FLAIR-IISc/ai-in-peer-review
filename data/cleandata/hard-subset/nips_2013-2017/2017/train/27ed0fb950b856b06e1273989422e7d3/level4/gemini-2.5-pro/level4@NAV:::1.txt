This manuscript introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, an extension of Poisson Factorization (PF) designed to capture temporal dependencies. In contrast to previous dynamic PF approaches, the proposed model utilizes a simplified recurrent neural network structure to model long-term temporal patterns. Model inference is performed using variational inference, augmented with a maximization step for the neural network weights. The method is evaluated on five real-world datasets.

While the core idea is potentially interesting, I find that the paper's execution requires significant improvement before it meets the standards for a conference like NeurIPS. My specific comments are outlined below.

+ The model formulation in Equation 4 does not seem to correspond to a standard RNN structure. The hidden state h_t^{(n)} is conditioned only on the top-layer memory vectors and lacks a dependency on the previous hidden state at the same layer (h_{t-1}^{(n)}). Could the authors provide the rationale for choosing this simplified architecture over a conventional RNN?

+ The model specification in Section 2 is incomplete. Key distributional assumptions are missing. For example, it is unclear how the Gamma distribution in Equation 4 is parameterized, and the prior distribution for θ_t in Equation 3 is not specified.

+ I disagree with the discussion of "implicit distributions" in lines 110-118. Equations 9 and 10 define explicit Gamma distributions with given shape and rate parameters. Because the conditional distribution of θ_tk (Eq. 9) given h^(0) can be computed in closed form, it cannot be considered implicit. The same applies to h in Equation 10.

+ The sentence in lines 30-31, "This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data," was unclear. Please elaborate on what is meant by this.

+ The subsection spanning lines 133-155 is difficult to follow. What, precisely, is the "loss function" being referred to in Equation 15?

+ Metrics such as MSE/PMSE are generally not well-suited for evaluating PF-based models on count data. The experimental results should be supplemented with more appropriate metrics, such as the predictive log-likelihood.

+ The value of K chosen for the experiments (line 207) appears to be quite small. I suggest that the authors evaluate the model's performance with larger values, such as K=100 at a minimum.

+ The connection between the textual discussion in lines 224-232 and Figure 3 is not apparent. Please revise the text to clarify how it relates to the figure.

+ The detailed variational inference update equations (Eqs. 11-14) are standard and could be moved to the supplementary material to improve the readability of the main text.

+ The caption for Figure 4 is illegible.

+ I strongly recommend revising the paper to reduce the use of passive voice, especially in the abstract, in line with general scientific writing conventions.

+ The manuscript's writing quality is low and requires thorough proofreading for numerous typos and grammatical errors. For example:
    - Line 15: "high-dimensional"
    - Line 22: "factorize"
    - Line 24: "property of the"
    - Lines 29, 34: "Dirichlet"
    - Line 34: "nested"
    - Fig. 1: "visual representation"
    - Fig. 1: "transmission" (this may not be the most appropriate term)
    - Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
    - Frequent missing articles (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution")
