This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, a model that extends Poisson factorization (PF) to account for temporal dependencies. Unlike previous works on dynamic PF, this paper uses a simplified version of a recurrent neural network to capture long-term dependencies. Inference is performed via variational inference, with an additional step to maximize over the neural network parameters. The paper reports experiments on five real-world datasets.

Overall, I found the idea potentially interesting, but I still think the paper needs some improvements in its execution before it can be accepted in a conference like NIPS. Please find my comments below.

* The formulation in Eq. 4 does not correspond to a standard RNN structure, as h_t^{(n)} depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and does not depend on previous time steps for the same layer, as in a typical RNN setup. Is there any reason for this simplified structure?

* In Section 2, some of the model equations are missing (e.g., it is not clear how to parameterize the Gamma in Eq. 4, or what distribution generates theta_t in Eq. 3).

* I disagree with the discussion in lines 110-118 regarding "implicit distributions". Eqs. 9 and 10 are actually explicit: they are Gammas with given shapes and rates. Since the distribution of theta_tk (Eq. 9) can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^{(0)}, it cannot be called implicit. The same comment applies to h in Eq. 10.

* Lines 30-31: The sentence "This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data" is unclear to me; can you elaborate? What types of data are referred to as "fanatical data and disaster data", and how might the method be limited in analyzing them?

* The subsection in lines 133-155 is unclear to me. What exactly is the "loss function" in Eq. 15? Is it a standard loss function used in variational inference, or a custom-defined function?

* MSE/PMSE is typically not a good metric for PF-like models. The results should additionally include at least predictive log-likelihood to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance.

* The value of K in the experiments (line 207) seems too small to me. I suggest the authors use larger values, such as K=100 (at least), to better capture the underlying patterns in the data.

* I couldn't connect the discussion in lines 224-232 with Fig. 3: it is not clear how the figure supports the claims made in the text. Can you provide more explanation or clarify the relationship between the figure and the discussion?

* The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11-14) can safely be moved to the supplement, as they are not essential to understanding the main ideas of the paper.

* The caption in Fig. 4 is difficult to read. Can you increase the font size or provide a clearer description of the figure?

* I strongly recommend following general writing advice about the use of the passive voice, especially in the abstract. Using active voice can make the writing more engaging and easier to read.

* The writing quality is poor, and the paper contains many typos. Here are some examples:
  - Line 15, "high-dimensional" should be "high-dimensionality"
  - Line 22, "factorize" should be "factorization"
  - Line 24, "property of the" is a phrase that can be rephrased for better clarity
  - Lines 29, 34, "Dirichlet" should be "Dirichlet distribution"
  - Line 34, "nested" is unclear in this context; can you provide more explanation?
  - Fig. 1, "visual representation" is a vague term; can you provide more description?
  - Fig. 1, "transmission" is not sure if that's an appropriate word; can you suggest alternative wording?
  - Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49) should be revised for better clarity
  - Many missing articles (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution") should be added to improve the writing quality
