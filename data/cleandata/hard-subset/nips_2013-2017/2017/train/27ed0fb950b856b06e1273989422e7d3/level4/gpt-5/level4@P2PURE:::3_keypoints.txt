- Paper proposes modeling long-range temporal dependence in Poisson factorization via a deep, RNN-like hierarchical architecture with variational inference and optimization for neural parameters; technically solid but marred by issues.

- Incorrect claim: lines 86–87 state nonnegative numbers are modeled by Poisson, but it is the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution that models nonnegative values.

- Presentation: bold the best results in Table 2 for clarity.

- Evaluation: recommend held-out perplexity as a stronger metric; MSE/PMSE are acceptable but less informative.

- Experiments: prior long-range dependency limitations are addressed, but need an ablation comparing to a single-layer dynamic PF to justify multiple hierarchical levels; LSTM comparison alone is insufficient due to differing learning algorithms.

- Writing quality: numerous grammatical errors and typos throughout (many examples provided), which make the paper difficult to read and require thorough proofreading.
