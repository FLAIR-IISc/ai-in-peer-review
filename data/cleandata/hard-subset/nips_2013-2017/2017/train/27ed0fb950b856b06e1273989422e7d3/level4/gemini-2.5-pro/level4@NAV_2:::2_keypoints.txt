- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): extends Poisson Factor Analysis with deep RNNs that parameterize Gamma shape parameters to model long-range temporal dependencies in count sequences.  
- Inference combines mean-field variational updates for latent variables with gradient-based optimization of RNN parameters, resembling a variational autoencoder-style training.  
- Strength: original and significant — moves beyond first-order Markov assumptions and could impact sequential count-data tasks (e.g., event analysis, dynamic topic modeling).  
- Strength: pragmatic inference design — learning RNNs from latent expectations likely improves computational efficiency and gradient stability versus heavy sampling.  
- Presentation lacks clarity — manuscript needs a more detailed graphical model (e.g., Chung et al. 2015 style) and correction of typographical errors (notably Equation 10).  
- Methodological choice unmotivated — authors learn only Gamma shape parameters via RNNs while fixing scale/rate parameters; this simplification requires justification and analysis of its effect on flexibility/performance.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient — missing crucial baselines (single-layer version) and a thorough ablation across number of layers (1, 2, >2) to validate the deep architecture.  
- Need systematic sensitivity analysis — investigate key hyperparameters such as temporal window size c and number of factors K.  
- Related work is incomplete — must discuss and differentiate from recent deep generative count models (e.g., Augmentable Gamma Belief Networks, Deep Latent Dirichlet Allocation) to contextualize novelty.
