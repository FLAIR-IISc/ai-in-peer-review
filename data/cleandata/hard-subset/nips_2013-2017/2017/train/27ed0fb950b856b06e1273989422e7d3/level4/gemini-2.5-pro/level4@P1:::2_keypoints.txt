- Paper summary: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), a hierarchical Gamma-based model using RNNs to parameterize temporal dynamics for sequential count data.  
- Core novelty: RNNs output Gamma shape parameters to learn flexible, non-linear, long-range transitions beyond first-order Markov assumptions.  
- Inference: Uses mean-field variational inference for latents plus gradient-based optimization for RNNs, avoiding high-variance sampling.  
- Strength: Combines interpretable Bayesian factorization with expressive deep recurrent networks, offering a promising approach for dynamic topic modeling.  
- Strength: The RNN-parameterized Gamma prior is an elegant method to capture complex temporal dynamics.  
- Strength: The inference strategy (updating neural nets via expectations) is well-motivated and computationally efficient.  
- Weakness: Presentation needs improvement—graphical model and some equations (e.g., Eq.10) are unclear or possibly contain typos.  
- Weakness: Key design choice unexplained—RNNs only model Gamma shape parameters while scale/rate seem fixed or simply updated; rationale and implications are missing.  
- Weakness: Experimental analysis is shallow—deeper layers show little benefit but lack in-depth investigation into causes (pruning, optimization, or data issues).  
- Weakness: Lacks thorough ablation on hyperparameters (window size c, number of factors K) to assess sensitivity and behavior.  
- Weakness: Related-work coverage is incomplete; should compare and discuss models like Augmentable Gamma Belief Networks and Deep LDA.  
- Overall: Innovative and promising but requires substantial revision for clarity, justification of design choices, and more comprehensive experiments.
