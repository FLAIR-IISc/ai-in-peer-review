- Incorrect distribution statement: The claim that the model "models nonnegative numbers" is inaccurate—specifically the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is used for that purpose.

- Table presentation: Table 2 should highlight the best results (e.g., bold them) for improved readability and impact.

- Evaluation metric: Held-out perplexity would be a more meaningful metric than MSE/PMSE for the sequential prediction task.

- Hierarchy justification: The multi-level hierarchy needs stronger validation—comparison to a single-level dynamic PF (rather than only an LSTM, which uses different learning algorithms) is needed to justify multiple hierarchical levels.

- Grammatical errors and typos: Numerous language and typographical errors throughout the paper (many examples listed) significantly impair clarity and must be corrected.

- Overall assessment: Core technical contribution is promising but factual inaccuracies and presentation issues require substantial refinement before publication.
