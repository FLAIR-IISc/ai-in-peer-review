The authors present a novel approach for modeling long-term dependencies in count vector data. Their method leverages recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, specifically employing gamma distributions to capture temporal dynamics. The shape parameters of these gamma distributions, which govern the time dependence, are approximated using neural networks that integrate information from both prior (higher layers) and likelihood (lower layers) components. A key strength lies in updating network parameters via the expectation of latent variables, effectively reducing sampling variance and improving computational efficiency. This model offers a compelling and insightful solution to the challenge of long-range dependencies in dynamic models. However, the manuscript appears somewhat hastily written and would benefit from addressing the following points:

1. Several critical typographical errors exist, such as in Equation 10.
2. The authors’ decision to fix the scale parameter while only updating the shape parameter within the gamma distributions requires more in-depth justification and analysis.
3. The experimental evaluation focuses solely on a two-layer model; a comparative analysis against single-layer and deeper architectures is necessary to fully understand the impact of network depth on performance. Further discussion of parameter sensitivity, including the window size and number of factors, is also warranted. 
4. To enhance clarity, I recommend including a detailed graphical illustration of the complete model. The authors could find inspiration in Figure 1 of [1].
5. The introduction should acknowledge recent advancements in deep Poisson factor analysis models, such as those presented in [2] and [3].
