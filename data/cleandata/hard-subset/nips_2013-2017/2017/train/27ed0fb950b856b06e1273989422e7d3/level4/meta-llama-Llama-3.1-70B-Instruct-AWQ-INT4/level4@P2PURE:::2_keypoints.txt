- Proposes using RNNs to represent implicit gamma-distributed latent variables for modeling complex long-time dependencies in count data.  
- Approximates gamma shape parameters with neural networks that integrate information from both higher-layer priors and lower-layer likelihoods.  
- Updates network parameters using expectations of latent variables to reduce sampling variance and improve computational efficiency.  
- Offers an interesting and seemingly effective approach to handling long-range dependencies in dynamic models.  
- Manuscript appears rushed and needs polishing.  
- Contains critical typos (notably in Equation 10) that must be corrected.  
- Treats the gamma scale parameter as fixed while only updating the shape parameter; this choice requires deeper justification and analysis.  
- Experiments only evaluate a two-layer model; lacking comparisons to single-layer or deeper models and insufficient discussion of other hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).  
- Recommends adding detailed graphical illustrations of the full model for clarity.  
- Suggests citing and discussing recent related deep Poisson/gamma factor models (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) in the Introduction.
