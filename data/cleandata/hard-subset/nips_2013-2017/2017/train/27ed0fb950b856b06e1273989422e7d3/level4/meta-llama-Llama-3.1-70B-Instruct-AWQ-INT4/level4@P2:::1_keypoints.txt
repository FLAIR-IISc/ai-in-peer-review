- Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), extending Poisson Factor Analysis to model temporal dependencies in count data.  
- Uses recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions and variational inference to learn latent variables and network parameters.  
- Experimental results on real-world datasets are promising.  
- Eq. 4 does not match a standard RNN structure; authors should clarify the simplified design and its effect on long-term dependency capture.  
- Several model equations are missing or unclear (e.g., Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4 and the distribution generating θ_t in Eq. 3); provide full, transparent equations.  
- The "implicit distributions" discussion is misleading since Eqs. 9 and 10 specify explicit Gamma distributions; revise wording for accuracy.  
- Loss function in Eq. 15 is unclear and needs a detailed derivation and link to the variational inference procedure.  
- Evaluation uses only MSE and PMSE, which may be inappropriate for PFA-like models; add metrics like predictive log-likelihood.  
- Latent dimensionality K used in experiments appears too small; consider larger K (e.g., K=100) to better capture structure.  
- The link between the discussion (lines 224–232) and Fig. 3 is unclear; explicitly explain how the figure supports the claims.  
- Update equations for variational inference (Eqs. 11–14) could be moved to the supplement to improve readability.  
- Writing quality needs significant improvement—numerous typos, grammatical errors, and unclear sentences must be fixed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel temporal extension of PFA and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear model formulation, limited evaluation metrics, and poor writing quality.  
- Recommendation: paper has potential but requires substantial revisions to presentation, experiments, and writing before acceptance.
