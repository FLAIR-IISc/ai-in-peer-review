This paper proposes a method for modeling long-range temporal dependence in count vectors by using recurrent neural networks to parameterize gamma-distributed latent variables. Temporal dependence is captured via the gamma shape parameters, which are approximated by neural networks that integrate information from the prior (higher layer) and the likelihood (lower layer). The network parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables, which reduces sampling variance and improves computational efficiency. Overall, the approach is promising and offers a clever way to address long-term dependencies in dynamic count models. However, the manuscript appears rushed, and several issues need attention:

1. Critical typos and notation errors remain (e.g., in Equation (10)). Please proofread carefully.

2. The model fixes the gamma scale parameter and updates only the shape. This design choice should be justified and analyzed. What are the theoretical or empirical implications? An ablation comparing fixed vs. learned scales (or alternative parameterizations) would be helpful.

3. Experiments report results only for a two-layer model. Please compare against single-layer and deeper variants to assess the effect of depth on performance. Also analyze sensitivity to other key hyperparameters, such as window size and number of factors.

4. For clarity, include a detailed graphical illustration of the full model, showing the hierarchical structure and temporal dependencies. See Figure 1 in [1] for a helpful reference.

5. The Introduction should discuss recent deep Poisson factorization and related models, including [2] and [3], and clarify the contributions relative to this literature.

With these revisions—especially clearer presentation, justification of modeling choices, and more comprehensive experiments—the paper would be significantly strengthened.
