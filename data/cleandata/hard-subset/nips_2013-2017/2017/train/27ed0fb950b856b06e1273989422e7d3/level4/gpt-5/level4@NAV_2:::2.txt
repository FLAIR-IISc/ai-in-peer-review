The paper introduces a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) model for sequential count vectors. Building on Poisson factorization, the method uses recurrent neural networks to parameterize implicit conditional distributions that are instantiated as Gamma distributions. Specifically, the networks output shape parameters that encode temporal dependencies, integrating information from both top-down priors (higher layers) and bottom-up signals (lower layers). Learning proceeds via variational inference: latent variables are updated using conjugate mean-field updates, and the neural network parameters are optimized using expectations of latent variables, which reduces sampling variance and improves computational efficiency. The approach provides a compelling mechanism for modeling long-range temporal structure in dynamic count data. I find the idea promising; however, the manuscript appears somewhat rushed, and I have the following comments:

1. There are several important typographical and notational errors, e.g., in Equation (10).

2. The model fixes the rate (scale) parameters and only updates the shape parameters in the Gamma conditionals; this design choice warrants deeper discussion and analysis.

3. The experiments evaluate only a two-layer version of the model; comparisons to a single-layer variant and deeper configurations are needed to clarify the effect of depth. Additional ablations on key hyperparameters (e.g., window size, number of factors) would also be informative.

4. For clarity, please include a detailed graphical depiction of the entire model. A reference structure akin to Figure 1 in [1] could help.

5. Recent deep Poisson factorization–related models, such as [2] and [3], should be discussed in the Introduction to better situate this work within current literature.

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research, 17(163), 1–44, 2016.
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou, Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC, ICML 2017.
