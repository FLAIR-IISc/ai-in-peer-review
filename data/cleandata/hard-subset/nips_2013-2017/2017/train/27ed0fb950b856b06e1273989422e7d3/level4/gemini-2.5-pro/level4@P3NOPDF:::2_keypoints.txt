- Proposes using an RNN to model latent gamma-distributed variables for capturing long-range temporal dependencies in count sequences.  
- RNN learns shape parameters of gamma distributions, combining top-down priors and bottom-up likelihoods at each time step.  
- Inference uses expectations of latent variables to reduce sampling variance and improve computational efficiency.  
- Method is elegant and effective for dynamic topic modeling with long-range dependencies.  
- Manuscript needs careful proofreading (typos, e.g., Eq. (10)) and a detailed graphical illustration of the model/generative process.  
- Key modeling choice—fixing the gamma scale parameter and only learning the shape—lacks sufficient justification or analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: requires ablation on model depth, comparisons to single- and deeper-layer variants, and sensitivity analyses for hyperparameters (temporal window, number of factors).  
- Related work is incomplete; must discuss recent deep Poisson factor analysis advances (e.g., Augmentable Gamma Belief Network, Deep LDA with topic-layer-adaptive SGR MCMC).  
- Overall promising contribution but needs improved clarity, stronger experimental validation, and better positioning within current literature.
