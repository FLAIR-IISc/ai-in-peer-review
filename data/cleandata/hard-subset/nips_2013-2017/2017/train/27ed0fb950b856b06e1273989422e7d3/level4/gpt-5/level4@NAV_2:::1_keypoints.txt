- Overall: Idea is promising but the submission needs significant refinement before being suitable for NIPS.  
- Model description: Equation (4) does not implement a conventional RNN — it omits same-layer temporal recursion; motivation for this simplified architecture is unclear.  
- Missing details: Section 2 omits key specification details (how the Gamma in Eq. 4 is parameterized and the exact distribution generating θ_t in Eq. 3).  
- Terminology error: The paper’s characterization of “implicit distributions” is incorrect—Eqs. 9 and 10 are explicit Gamma distributions available in closed form.  
- Unclear phrasing: The sentence about “fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear and needs clarification (what is “fanatical data” and the intended claim).  
- Optimization clarity: The subsection around lines 133–155 is hard to follow; it’s unclear what is being optimized in Eq. 15 and how the “loss function” should be interpreted.  
- Evaluation metric: MSE/PMSE is not ideal for Poisson factorization—report predictive log-likelihood or held-out perplexity for probabilistic fit.  
- Capacity: The choice of K in experiments appears too small; recommend testing much larger K (e.g., K ≥ 100) to assess capacity and robustness.  
- Figure/text alignment: The discussion in lines 224–232 does not clearly map to Figure 3; the connection between text and depicted factors is not evident.  
- Paper organization: Variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) could be moved to the appendix/supplement to improve main-text clarity.  
- Figure issues: The caption in Figure 4 is not legible.  
- Writing style: Strongly recommend reducing passive voice (especially in the abstract).  
- Writing quality: Overall writing needs improvement and there are numerous typos and grammar errors (multiple line-specific examples provided).
