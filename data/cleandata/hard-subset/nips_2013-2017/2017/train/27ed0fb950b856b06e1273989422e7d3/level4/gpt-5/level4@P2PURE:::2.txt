The authors propose a method to model long-range temporal dependencies in count vectors by using recurrent neural networks to parameterize implicit distributions instantiated as gamma distributions. The gamma shape parameters, which capture temporal dependence, are approximated by neural networks that combine information from the higher-layer prior and the lower-layer likelihood. The network parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables, reducing sampling variance and improving computational efficiency. Overall, the model offers an interesting and effective approach to handling long-term dependencies in dynamic models. I like the idea, but the manuscript appears somewhat rushed; I have the following comments:

1. There are critical typos, e.g., in Equation (10).
2. The gamma scale parameter is treated as fixed and only the shape parameter is updated. This design choice requires more detailed discussion and analysis.
3. The experiments report results only for a two-layer model and do not compare against a single-layer variant or deeper models, making it difficult to assess the impact of depth on performance. Other hyperparameters also merit discussion, such as the window size and the number of factors.
4. For clarity, please include a detailed graphical illustration of the full model. For reference, see Figure 1 in [1].
5. Recent deep Poisson factor analysis models such as [2] and [3] should be discussed in the Introduction.

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research, 17(163), 1-44, 2016.
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou, Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC, ICML 2017.
