- Proposes a novel method using RNNs to model gamma-distributed latent shape parameters for count vectors, aiming to capture complex long-term temporal dependencies.  
- Neural networks approximate shape parameters by integrating prior (higher-layer) and likelihood (lower-layer) information.  
- Parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables, reducing sampling variance and improving computational efficiency.  
- Manuscript is promising but appears hastily written and needs polish.  
- Critical typographical errors exist, including in Equation 10.  
- The choice to fix the gamma scale parameter while only updating shape parameters needs rigorous justification and analysis.  
- Experiments only evaluate a two-layer model; missing comparisons to single-layer and deeper configurations and lacking discussion of hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).  
- A detailed graphical illustration of the complete model architecture is needed (e.g., refer to Figure 1 in Chung et al., 2015).  
- The Introduction should discuss recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis (e.g., Zhou 2016; Cong et al. 2017) to better contextualize the contribution.
