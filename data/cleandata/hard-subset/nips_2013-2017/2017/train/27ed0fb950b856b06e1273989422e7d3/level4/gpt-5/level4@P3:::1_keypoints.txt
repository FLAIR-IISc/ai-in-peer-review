- Proposal: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) extends Poisson Factor Analysis with a deep, temporally structured latent hierarchy and neural-network–parameterized conditional distributions.  
- Likelihood & params: Observations use a Poisson likelihood with loadings Φ, time-varying scores Θ, and scale params Ψ, Λ.  
- Temporal design: Temporal dependence via multiple latent layers H(n) parameterized by neural nets intended to capture short- and long-range dependencies.  
- Inference: Two-stage procedure—(i) mean-field variational inference for Gamma–Poisson structure with auxiliary counts, (ii) neural-network parameter optimization via a "Probability AutoEncoder" loss.  
- Experiments: Evaluated on synthetic and five real datasets, compared against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA using MSE/PMSE.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on PFA/Gamma–Poisson models, temporal extensions (PGDS, recurrent PF), deep count models, and VAE/Ladder VAE literature.  
- Strength: Addresses modeling long-range temporal dependencies in sparse high-dimensional count data.  
- Strength: Integrates deep temporal structure with Poisson likelihood aiming to retain factor interpretability.  
- Strength: Uses conjugate VI for the count part, which is computationally attractive.  
- Strength: Empirical gains on several datasets and useful qualitative factor analysis.  
- Strength: Thoughtful discussion of practical issues (pruning, utility of deeper layers).  
- Weakness: Model specification incomplete/ambiguous—conditional structures (Eqs.3–4) do not clearly define an RNN and omit same-layer temporal recurrence.  
- Weakness: Gamma distribution parameterizations (shapes/rates) are not fully specified for several conditionals.  
- Weakness: Misuse of "implicit distributions"—Eqs.9–10 are explicit Gamma posteriors and should be described clearly if only neural-parameterized.  
- Weakness: Objective/inference not well justified—the "Probability AutoEncoder" loss is under-specified and lacks a derived ELBO or explanation of how the two stages optimize a coherent objective.  
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics problematic—MSE/PMSE are nonstandard for Poisson models; held-out predictive log-likelihood/perplexity and count-based scoring rules are needed.  
- Weakness: Limited experimental scope—small K, missing sensitivity analyses (K, window c, number of layers), and missing relevant baselines (HPF, dynamic topic models, recurrent PF).  
- Weakness: Figure quality, captions, and narrative links (e.g., Fig.3, Fig.4) are unclear or unreadable.  
- Weakness: Paper suffers from many grammatical errors, typos, passive voice overuse, and ambiguous phrasing; needs careful proofreading.  
- Quality concern: Technical soundness is hard to assess without precise conditional definitions and a clear ELBO; Gamma–Poisson core is standard but neural coupling lacks probabilistic interpretation.  
- Clarity concern: Paper is hard to follow; needs unified notation, explicit distributions, and a principled inference objective.  
- Originality: Combining PFA with deep temporal parameterizations is interesting but several related works exist; stronger contribution needs a principled objective and fuller comparisons.  
- Significance: Potentially significant if predictive performance and interpretability are convincingly demonstrated, but current evidence is not conclusive.  
- Questions for authors: Provide a formal ELBO and show optimization relation to both inference stages; justify omission of same-layer recurrence; report sensitivity to K/c/layers; clarify role of Ψ and Λ.  
- Pros for acceptance: Relevant problem, promising empirical performance, and qualitative insights.  
- Cons for acceptance: Incomplete model spec, unclear inference objective, poor metric choices and baselines, and writing/figure issues.  
- Suggestions: Fully specify the probabilistic model, derive an ELBO or principled objective, report held-out predictive likelihoods, expand baselines and sensitivity analyses, move routine VI updates to the appendix, improve figures/captions, and release code.
