- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): extends Poisson Factor Analysis with neural nets to capture short-term complex relationships and long-term temporal dependencies.  
- Positions work relative to PGDS and deep Poisson factorization, introducing a neural-network-based temporal model instead of Gamma-Poisson transitions.  
- Experimental evaluation on synthetic and real datasets (NIPS, International Disaster, EBOLA) shows consistent improvements over baselines (PGDS, PFA, LSTM).  
- Inference uses variational methods combined with neural networks for parameter learning; reported results (Table 2) are favorable.  
- Technical clarity issues: RNN formulation ambiguous and inconsistent (h_t depends only on top-layer memory), Gamma distribution parameterization unclear, and “implicit distributions” are actually explicit.  
- Experimental design concerns: choice of K=3 is unrealistically small for high-dimensional data (e.g., V=14,036) and lacks justification or experiments with larger K.  
- Evaluation metric problem: relies solely on MSE/PMSE, which are inappropriate for count models; predictive log-likelihood is recommended.  
- Writing quality poor: grammatical errors, inconsistent terminology, unclear phrases (e.g., “fanatical data”), and some unreadable figure captions.  
- Lacks critical analysis: does not sufficiently discuss limitations or explain observed “pruning” and diminishing returns from additional layers.  
- For acceptance: addresses a meaningful problem with a novel, well-motivated approach and shows empirical gains across datasets.  
- Against acceptance: significant technical ambiguities, weak experimental choices/metrics, and poor writing hinder reproducibility and clarity.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form; requires substantial revision to fix technical, experimental, and writing issues.  
- Specific improvement suggestions: clarify RNN structure or justify simplification; provide full Gamma parameterizations; use predictive log-likelihood; justify or increase K (e.g., K=100); fix writing and figures; analyze pruning/diminishing returns; consider moving detailed variational updates to the supplement.
