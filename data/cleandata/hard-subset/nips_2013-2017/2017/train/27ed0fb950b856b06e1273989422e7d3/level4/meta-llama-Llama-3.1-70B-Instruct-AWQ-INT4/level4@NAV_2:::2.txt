This paper proposes a novel method to capture the complex long-time dependence over the count vectors by exploiting recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, which is realized by the gamma distribution. The shape parameters of those gamma distributions, capturing the time dependence, are approximated by neural networks, which try to cover the information from prior (from higher layer) and likelihood (from low layer). The parameters in neural network are updated based on the expectation of latent variables, which is helpful to reduce the variance caused by sampling and increase the computational efficiency. The model provides an interesting and smart way to handle the long dependency problem in the dynamic model.

The paper is well-structured and the authors have made a good effort to explain the proposed model and its components. The experiments are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model in comparison to existing methods. However, there are some areas that require attention.

Firstly, the authors should proofread the manuscript carefully to eliminate typos and grammatical errors. For example, there is a typo in equation 10.

Secondly, the authors consider the scale parameter as fixed and only update the shape parameter in those gamma distributions. This requires some detailed discussion and analysis to justify this choice.

Thirdly, the authors should provide a more comprehensive comparison of the proposed model with different numbers of layers. The current results only show the performance of the proposed model with two layers, which is not enough to understand the influence of the layers on the performance. Other parameters such as the size of the window and the number of factors also need to be discussed.

Fourthly, the authors should provide detailed graphical illustrations of the whole model to help readers understand the proposed method better. The authors can refer to figure 1 in [1] for an example of how to illustrate the model.

Lastly, the authors should discuss recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis models, such as [2] and [3], in the introduction to provide a more comprehensive background for the proposed method.

Overall, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of dynamic Poisson factor analysis. With some revisions to address the above-mentioned issues, the paper can be further improved.

Strengths:

* The proposed model provides a novel way to capture the complex long-time dependence over the count vectors.
* The experiments are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model in comparison to existing methods.
* The paper is well-structured and the authors have made a good effort to explain the proposed model and its components.

Weaknesses:

* The manuscript requires careful proofreading to eliminate typos and grammatical errors.
* The authors should provide a more comprehensive comparison of the proposed model with different numbers of layers.
* The authors should provide detailed graphical illustrations of the whole model to help readers understand the proposed method better.
* The authors should discuss recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis models in the introduction.

Acceptance:

Based on the current state of the manuscript, I would recommend a minor revision. The authors need to address the above-mentioned issues to further improve the paper. With some revisions, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of dynamic Poisson factor analysis.
