- Model depiction deviates from standard RNNs: hidden state h_t^(n) lacks dependence on h_{t-1} and this design choice needs justification and implications discussed.
- Model specification is incomplete: Gamma distribution parameterization in Eq.4 and the generative process for topic proportions θ_t (Eq.3) are not explicitly defined.
- Description of “implicit distributions” is inaccurate: θ_tk and h are presented as explicit Gamma distributions and should be characterized correctly.
- Neural network optimization is unclear: the loss L(W,b) (Eq.15) and its role within the variational inference procedure are not well explained.
- Evaluation metrics are inappropriate: MSE and PMSE are ill-suited for Poisson/count models; predictive log-likelihood on held-out data should be reported.
- Latent dimensionality K is likely too small (K=20); authors should justify or report results for larger K (e.g., 50, 100) to test capacity and robustness.
- Vague claims about competing methods and “fanatical data/disaster data”: authors must define terms and specify the long-term dependence patterns they refer to.
- Figure 3 discussion is confusing and does not clearly link visual patterns to claims about temporal dynamics.
- Detailed variational update equations (Eqs.11–14) could be moved to supplementary material to improve readability.
- Figure 4 caption is illegible due to formatting/resolution issues and must be fixed.
- Overall writing quality is poor with numerous grammatical errors, typos, and awkward phrasing; the manuscript needs thorough proofreading and reduced passive voice.
- Multiple specific wording/terminology problems (e.g., “high-dimensional”, “factorize”, misuse of “transmission” in Fig.1) and several incomplete sentences that require correction.
