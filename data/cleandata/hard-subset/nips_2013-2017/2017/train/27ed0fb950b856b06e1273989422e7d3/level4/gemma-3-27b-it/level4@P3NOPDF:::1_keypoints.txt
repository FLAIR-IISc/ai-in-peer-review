- Paper introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPF), extending Poisson factorization with a simplified RNN to model temporal dependencies and uses variational inference plus NN parameter optimization, evaluated on five real datasets.
- Overall assessment: idea is potentially interesting but the paper needs significant revisions before being suitable for NeurIPS.
- Major: Proposed RNN formulation (Eq. 4) does not match standard RNNs; hidden state lacks temporal recurrence and needs justification or clarification.
- Major: Model specification in Section 2 is incomplete—Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4 and the generative distribution for θ_t in Eq. 3 are unspecified, harming reproducibility.
- Major: Claiming Eqs. 9–10 define “implicit distributions” is incorrect since they explicitly specify Gamma distributions with computable conditionals.
- Major: The derivation, purpose, and components of the “loss function” in Eq. 15 are unclear and require a detailed explanation.
- Minor: The comment on difficulties with “fanatical data and disaster data” is vague; authors should explain the specific challenges and why DDPF may struggle.
- Minor: Evaluation metrics are inadequate—MSE/PMSE are weak for PF-like models; predictive log-likelihood should be reported as a primary metric.
- Minor: Chosen latent dimensionality K is small; experiments should explore larger K (e.g., ≥100) to evaluate scalability and performance.
- Minor: The text discussion (lines 224–232) does not clearly connect to Fig. 3; authors should explicitly state how the figure supports the claims.
- Minor: Variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) would be better moved to supplementary material to streamline the main text.
- Minor: Fig. 4 caption is unreadable and must be rewritten for clarity.
- Minor: Writing style overuses passive voice; revise for active voice to improve clarity and conciseness.
- Minor: Numerous grammar errors and typos throughout (examples listed); thorough proofreading and correction are needed.
