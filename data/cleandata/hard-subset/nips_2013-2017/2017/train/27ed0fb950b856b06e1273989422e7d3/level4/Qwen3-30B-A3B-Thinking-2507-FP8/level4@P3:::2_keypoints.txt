- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), extending Poisson Factor Analysis with deep recurrent structures to model sequential count data.  
- Novelty: Uses RNNs to parameterize latent Gamma distributions, claiming improved long-term dependency capture via a deep latent hierarchy.  
- Evaluation: Tested on synthetic data and five real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, disaster events, sheep counts) and compared to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  

Strengths:
- Addresses an important problem in sequential count modeling where long-term dependencies matter.  
- Demonstrates promising and interpretable latent structures on several datasets (e.g., ICEWS temporal patterns).  
- Combines variational inference for latent variables with gradient-based learning for neural components.  
- Experimental design is reasonably comprehensive with multiple baselines.

Weaknesses:
- Paper contains critical typos (e.g., Equation 10) that reduce clarity and professionalism.  
- Fixes Gamma distribution scale parameter while only updating shape, a modeling choice that lacks sufficient justification and discussion.  
- Ablation studies are insufficient: only two-layer tests, no single-layer or deeper-layer comparisons, and limited analysis of "pruning" phenomena.  
- Insufficient systematic analysis of key hyperparameters (window size, number of factors) and their effect on performance.  
- Fails to adequately contextualize and compare against recent deep Poisson factorization work (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017).  
- Poor ICEWS prediction results are reported but not explained.

Quality:
- Technical approach and variational framework are sound, but empirical support for claims about deep structure benefits is weak due to missing ablations.

Clarity:
- Generally well-structured but hampered by typos and unclear explanations of architecture; needs a detailed graphical model and clearer description of how RNNs parameterize Gamma distributions.

Originality:
- Novel combination of deep latent structure and Poisson factorization, but contribution is somewhat incremental given recent related work.

Significance:
- Potentially useful for recommendation, text, and event prediction tasks; real-data results show promise but inconsistencies reduce confidence.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Tackles an important problem, offers a novel deep structure approach, shows good results on multiple datasets, and yields interpretable latent factors.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Lacks sufficient ablation and hyperparameter analyses, insufficient comparison to recent related work, and contains technical/typo issues undermining credibility.

Recommendations:
- Fix typos and clarify Gamma parameterization (shape vs. scale) with justification.  
- Run comprehensive ablations comparing single-, two-, and multi-layer models and investigate pruning effects.  
- Systematically analyze hyperparameters (window size, number of factors) and report sensitivity.  
- Expand related work to include recent deep Poisson factorization papers and contrast methods.  
- Add a detailed graphical illustration of the full model and clarify RNN-to-Gamma parameterization.
