

The authors propose a novel method to capture complex long-time dependencies in count vectors by leveraging recurrent neural networks to model implicit distributions via the gamma distribution. The shape parameters of these gamma distributions, which encode temporal dependencies, are approximated by neural networks that integrate information from prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer). Network parameters are updated using the expectation of latent variables, reducing variance from sampling and enhancing computational efficiency. The model offers an elegant approach to addressing long dependency challenges in dynamic systems. I find it compelling, though the manuscript appears somewhat hurried, with the following comments:

1. Critical typographical errors, including in Equation 10.  
2. The scale parameter is treated as fixed while only the shape parameter is updated; this assumption requires deeper discussion and analysis.  
3. Experiments evaluate only a two-layer architecture without comparison to single-layer or multi-layer variants, insufficient to assess layer impact. Additional parameters (e.g., window size, number of factors) also warrant exploration.  
4. Detailed schematic diagrams of the full model would significantly improve clarity; refer to Figure 1 in [1] for guidance.  
5. Recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis (e.g., [2], [3]) should be addressed in the Introduction.  

[1] Chung J, Kastner K, Dinh L, et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.  
[2] Mingyuan Zhou, Yulai Cong, and Bo Chen, Augmentable gamma belief networks, Journal of Machine Learning Research, 17(163), 2016.  
[3] Yulai Cong, Bo Chen, Hongwei Liu, and Mingyuan Zhou, Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC, ICML 2017.
