This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization (DDPF) model, an extension of Poisson factorization designed to capture temporal dependencies. Unlike prior work on dynamic Poisson factorization, this paper employs a simplified recurrent neural network structure to model long-range dependencies. Inference is performed via variational inference, augmented with a parameter optimization step for the neural network. The model is evaluated on five real-world datasets.

Overall, the idea is potentially interesting, but I believe the paper requires substantial improvements before it would be suitable for acceptance at a competitive conference like NIPS. My detailed comments are provided below.

+The formulation presented in Eq. 4 does not fully correspond to a standard RNN structure. The hidden state h_t^{(n)} appears to depend only on the top-layer memory vectors and not on previous time steps within the same layer, as is typical in an RNN. Could the authors explain the rationale for this simplification?

+Section 2 contains missing model equations that hinder understanding. Specifically, the parameterization of the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4, and the distribution from which theta_t in Eq. 3 is generated, are not clearly defined. 

+I disagree with the authors’ characterization of the distributions in lines 110-118 as “implicit.” Equations 9 and 10 provide explicit Gamma distributions with defined shape and rate parameters. Given that the distribution of theta_tk (Eq. 9) can be computed in closed form conditional on h^{(0)}, it cannot be considered implicit; the same applies to h in Eq. 10.

+The statement in lines 30-31 – “This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data” – is unclear. The authors should elaborate on these potential limitations.

+The subsection in lines 133-155 lacks clarity. The precise definition of the “loss function” in Eq. 15 is not readily apparent.

+Mean Squared Error (MSE) and Percentage Mean Squared Error (PMSE) are not ideal metrics for Poisson factorization models. The results should also include a more appropriate metric, such as predictive log-likelihood.

+The value of K used in the experiments (line 207) seems insufficiently large. I recommend exploring larger values, such as K=100 or greater.

+I found it difficult to connect the discussion in lines 224-232 to the information presented in Fig. 3; the relationship is not clear.

+The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11-14) could be moved to the supplementary material.

+The caption for Fig. 4 is illegible.

+I strongly advise the authors to consult general writing guidelines regarding the use of the passive voice, particularly in the abstract.

+The writing quality needs improvement, and the paper contains numerous typos. Examples include:
 -Line 15, “high-dimensional”
 -Line 22, “factorize”
 -Line 24, “property of the”
 -Lines 29, 34, “Dirichlet”
 -Line 34, “nested”
 -Fig. 1, “visual representation”
 -Fig. 1, “transmission” (the appropriateness of this term is questionable)
 -Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
 -Missing articles (e.g., “Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution”)
