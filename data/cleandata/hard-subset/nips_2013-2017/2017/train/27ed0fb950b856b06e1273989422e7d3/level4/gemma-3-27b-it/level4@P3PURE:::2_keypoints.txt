- Proposes using RNNs to model implicit gamma-distributed latent variables (shape parameter learned by neural nets combining prior and likelihood) and updates neural parameters via expectations to reduce sampling variance and improve efficiency, targeting long-range dependencies in count data.

- Manuscript contains critical typos (e.g., equation 10) that need correction.

- Authors fix the gamma scale parameter and only learn the shape — this design choice requires deeper justification and analysis of its effectiveness and limitations.

- Experiments are limited to a two-layer model; need comparisons with single-layer and deeper models and analysis of key hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).

- Add a detailed graphical illustration of the complete model architecture (e.g., inspired by Figure 1 in Chung et al. 2015) to improve clarity.

- Review related work in deep Poisson/gamma factor models (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) in the Introduction to better situate the contribution.
