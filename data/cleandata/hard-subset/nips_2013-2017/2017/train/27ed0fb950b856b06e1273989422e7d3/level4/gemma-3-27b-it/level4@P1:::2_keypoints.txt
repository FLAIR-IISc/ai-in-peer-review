- Proposed method: use RNNs to parameterize Gamma shape parameters to capture complex long-term dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Modeling choice: shape parameters integrate information from prior (higher layers) and likelihood (lower layers) via neural networks.  
- Learning: neural network parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables, reducing variance and improving computational efficiency.  
- Novelty/strength: combines PFA, RNNs, and deep hierarchical structure to model dynamic count data.  
- Strength: explicitly targets long-term dependency modeling, addressing a key weakness of prior PFA models.  
- Strength: employs variational inference as a computationally efficient alternative to MCMC.  
- Strength: experiments on synthetic and real data show promising predictive performance and interpretable latent factors.  
- Weakness: manuscript contains typographical errors (e.g., Equation 10) that need correction.  
- Design concern: authors fix the Gamma scale parameter and only update shape — requires clearer justification and analysis of impact on expressiveness and performance.  
- Experimental limitation: evaluation only uses two-layer models; needs comparisons to single-layer and deeper models to assess depth effects.  
- Experimental limitation: lacks sensitivity analyses for key hyperparameters (e.g., window size, number of factors).  
- Clarity issue: paper would benefit from a detailed graphical illustration of the full model and information flow (e.g., inspired by Chung et al. 2015 Figure 1).  
- Related work omission: recent deep Poisson factor analysis models (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) are not discussed and should be included.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with promising empirical results addressing long-term dependencies.  
- Argument against acceptance: manuscript needs fixes (typos), stronger justification for modeling choices, more thorough experiments, and inclusion of related work.  
- Overall recommendation: conditional acceptance pending revisions to justify scale fixation, expand experiments and sensitivity analyses, include missing related work, and proofread.
