- Contribution: Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) — a deep recurrent extension of Poisson Factor Analysis for modeling long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count data using variational inference.
- Method: Uses neural networks to model implicit distributions across layers and leverages Gamma–Poisson conjugacy within a deep variational framework.
- Experiments: Evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS corpus, EBOLA, etc.) with comparisons to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.
- Strength: Robust experimental design covering diverse, high-dimensional datasets and relevant baselines.
- Strength: Strong predictive performance on most datasets, particularly where long-term patterns matter (e.g., EBOLA, ASP).
- Strength: Good interpretability of latent factors, showing multi-factor temporal dynamics missed by PGDS.
- Strength: Technical approach is well-motivated and aligned with recent deep Bayesian modeling advances.
- Weakness: Insufficient technical justification for depth — no comparison to a single-layer deep variant to prove necessity of multiple hierarchical layers.
- Weakness: Evaluation relies only on MSE/PMSE; lacks held-out perplexity or other generative-quality metrics suited for count data.
- Weakness: ICEWS prediction failure is unexplained, while PGDS outperforms DDPFA on that dataset.
- Weakness: Presentation issues and grammatical errors reduce clarity; inconsistent formatting (e.g., Section 4.1).
- Weakness: Pruning behavior (Table 3) attributed to mean-field VI is noted but not analyzed or mitigated.
- Suggestion: Bold best results in Table 2 for readability.
- Suggestion: Add a single-layer deep PF comparison to validate the benefit of depth.
- Suggestion: Supplement MSE with held-out perplexity (e.g., last 20% time steps) and clarify ICEWS discrepancy.
- Suggestion: Fix grammatical errors and formatting inconsistencies.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — meaningful contribution but requires clarifications on depth justification, improved evaluation metrics, and language polish.
