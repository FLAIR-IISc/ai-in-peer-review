- Novel approach modeling long-range temporal dependencies in count vector sequences using RNNs realized through gamma distributions.  
- Shape parameters capture temporal dynamics and are approximated by a neural network integrating prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) information.  
- Network parameters updated using the expectation of latent variables to reduce sampling variance and improve computational efficiency.  
- Model provides an elegant and effective solution to the long-dependency problem in dynamic models.  
- Manuscript contains several typos and a critical error in Equation 10 that must be corrected.  
- Fixing the gamma scale parameter while only updating the shape parameter requires deeper discussion and analysis of its impact.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: no comparisons across model depths (single-layer vs. two-layer vs. deeper) and no sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).  
- Include a detailed graphical illustration of the full model architecture (e.g., similar to Figure 1 in Chung et al., 2015).  
- Update the introduction to discuss recent relevant work in deep Poisson factor analysis, including Zhou et al. (2016) and Cong et al. (2017).
