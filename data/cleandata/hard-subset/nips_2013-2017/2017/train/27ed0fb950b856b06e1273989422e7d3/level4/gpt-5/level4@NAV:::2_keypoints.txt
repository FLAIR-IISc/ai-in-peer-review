- Novel approach: uses RNNs to parameterize gamma distributions for modeling complex long-term dependencies in count vectors.
- Shape parameters encode temporal dependence and are approximated by neural nets that aggregate prior (upper layer) and likelihood (lower layer) information.
- Neural network parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables, reducing sampling variance and improving computational efficiency.
- Overall method is clever and appealing for handling long-range dependencies, but the manuscript feels rushed.
- Critical typos present (e.g., Eq. (10)) that need correction.
- Design choice of fixing the gamma scale parameter while only updating shape requires fuller discussion and analysis.
- Experiments only evaluate a two-layer model and fail to compare with single-layer or deeper variants; hyperparameters (window size, number of factors) are not analyzed.
- Recommend including a detailed graphical illustration of the full model (cf. Fig. 1 in Chung et al., 2015).
- Recent related work (e.g., augmentable gamma belief networks and deep latent Dirichlet allocation) should be cited and discussed in the Introduction.
