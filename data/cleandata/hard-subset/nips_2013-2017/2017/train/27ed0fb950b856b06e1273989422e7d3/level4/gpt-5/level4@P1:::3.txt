This paper proposes a deep dynamic Poisson factorization (DDPFA) model to capture long-range temporal dependence in sequential count data. Unlike prior dynamic Poisson factorization approaches, it uses a recurrent neural network–like structure to model long-term dependencies among latent factors, with a hierarchical architecture to capture multi-scale temporal structure. Inference is performed via variational methods, with additional optimization to learn the neural network parameters. The paper contains solid technical ideas, but the presentation needs substantial polishing and one factual correction.

Comments and suggestions:

1) Factual correction. The statement about modeling nonnegative numbers on lines 86–87 is incorrect. It is the Poisson-randomized Gamma distribution that is used for modeling nonnegative numbers.

2) Presentation of results. Please make the best results bold in Table 2.

3) Evaluation metrics. For the experiments in Section 4.2, held-out perplexity would be a stronger and more standard indicator for topic-like count data, although MSE/PMSE are acceptable.

4) Empirical validation of depth. The paper convincingly discusses limitations of prior work (e.g., [1, 2]) in capturing long-range dependence and shows that the proposed method addresses them. However, to demonstrate the utility of multiple hierarchical layers in DDPFA, a direct comparison against a dynamic PF with a single-layer (shallow) deep architecture would be informative. The LSTM comparison is useful, but because the learning paradigms differ, it does not directly justify the need for multiple hierarchical levels in the proposed model.

5) Writing quality. There are numerous grammatical errors and typos that make the paper hard to read. Below are examples that should be corrected:
- “unit the data”
- “Noticing the lack of capturing …”
- “PFA factorized a count matrix” (tense inconsistent with surrounding text; use present tense consistently)
- “other data with different pattern long-time dependence …”
- “Examples for this including …”
- “shows excellent results on predict and …”
- “However, it is rough for them …”
- “V-dimesnion sequentially …”
- “K-dimension latent variable …”
- “due to the including of feature-wise …”
- “recommend systems” → “recommendation systems”

Overall assessment:
- Quality: The modeling idea is technically sound—combining Poisson factorization with RNN-based implicit distributions and a hierarchical structure is reasonable, and the variational inference scheme is appropriate. The paper would benefit from clearer derivations and more careful discussion of assumptions (e.g., how the neural networks parametrize Gamma shapes/rates and stability considerations).
- Clarity: The paper is currently difficult to read due to grammar and organization issues. Tightening the exposition, standardizing notation, and fixing typos are necessary.
- Originality: Using an RNN-like implicit prior within a deep Poisson factorization framework is a novel combination relative to prior dynamic PF and PGDS work, though it builds on known deep probabilistic modeling ideas.
- Significance: The application to both synthetic and real datasets is promising. Strengthening the empirical evidence with held-out perplexity and an ablation comparing single- vs multi-layer DDPFA would better establish the contribution of depth.

With improved writing and a clearer empirical justification of the hierarchical depth, this work could make a meaningful contribution.
