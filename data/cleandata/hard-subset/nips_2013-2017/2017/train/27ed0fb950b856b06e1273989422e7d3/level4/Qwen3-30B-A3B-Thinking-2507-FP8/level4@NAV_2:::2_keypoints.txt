- Introduces DDPFA: extends Poisson Factor Analysis with RNN-parameterized gamma latent variables to capture long-term temporal dependencies.  
- Combines variational inference with neural-network optimization and is validated on synthetic and real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA).  
- Originality (High): RNNs modeling implicit gamma distributions meaningfully extend prior dynamic Poisson models and capture sustained activations missed by PGDS.  
- Significance (Moderate-High): Strong predictive and fit performance on diverse, high-dimensional sparse data; produces interpretable latent factors useful for applications.  
- Quality (Moderate): Variational inference is sound and experiments robust; ablation study identifies pruning and diminishing returns from deeper layers.  
- Clarity (Moderate weakness): Critical typos and unclear derivations reduce reproducibility (e.g., Eq.10 error); fixed gamma scale β=1 lacks justification.  
- Experimental gaps (Moderate weakness): Ablations incomplete—missing 1-layer DDPFA baseline and sensitivity analyses over window sizes and factor counts.  
- Contextualization (Moderate weakness): Inadequate comparison to recent deep Poisson factorization work (e.g., Recurrent Poisson Factorization [25], HPF [26]).  
- Dataset-specific limitation: PGDS outperforms DDPFA on ICEWS prediction, indicating trade-offs not fully discussed.  
- Pro summary: Novel deep latent structure for long-range dependence, efficient variational inference, interpretable factors, strong empirical results.  
- Con summary: Missing ablation/hyperparameter analysis, technical typos, unexplained fixed scale parameter, and insufficient related-work contrast.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—innovative and validated but needs technical clarifications and expanded experiments.
