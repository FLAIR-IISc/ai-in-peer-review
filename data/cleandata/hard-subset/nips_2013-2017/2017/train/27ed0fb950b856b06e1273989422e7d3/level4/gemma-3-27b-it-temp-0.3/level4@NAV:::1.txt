This paper presents a deep dynamic Poisson factorization (DDPF) model, extending Poisson factorization to incorporate temporal dependencies. Unlike prior work in dynamic PF, the authors employ a simplified recurrent neural network structure to capture long-range dependencies. Variational inference is used for model fitting, with an additional optimization step applied to the neural network parameters. The model is evaluated on five real-world datasets.

Overall, the core idea shows promise, but significant revisions are needed before this work would be suitable for a venue like NIPS. Specific concerns are detailed below:

+The formulation presented in Eq. 4 does not align with a standard recurrent neural network (RNN) architecture. The hidden state, h_t^(n), appears to depend only on top-layer memory vectors, lacking the temporal connection to previous time steps within the same layer characteristic of RNNs. Justification for this simplification would be helpful.

+Section 2 contains incomplete model specifications. The parameterization of the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 and the generative distribution for theta_t in Eq. 3 are not clearly defined.

+I disagree with the characterization of the distributions discussed in lines 110-118 as “implicit.” Equations 9 and 10 provide explicit Gamma distributions with defined shape and rate parameters. Given the closed-form computation of the theta_tk distribution (Eq. 9) conditional on h^(0), it cannot be considered implicit. A similar argument applies to h in Eq. 10.

+The statement in lines 30-31 regarding limitations when analyzing data with “different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear and requires further elaboration.

+The subsection spanning lines 133-155 lacks clarity. The precise definition of the “loss function” in Eq. 15 is not readily apparent.

+Mean Squared Error (MSE) and Percentage Mean Squared Error (PMSE) are not ideal evaluation metrics for Poisson factorization models. Inclusion of predictive log-likelihood is strongly recommended.

+The chosen value of K in the experiments (line 207) appears insufficient. Exploring larger values, such as K=100 or greater, is advisable.

+The connection between the discussion in lines 224-232 and Fig. 3 is unclear and needs to be better established.

+The variational inference update equations (Eqs. 11-14) can be relegated to the supplementary material.

+The caption for Fig. 4 is illegible and requires correction.

+I recommend the authors review general writing guidelines regarding the use of the passive voice, particularly in the abstract.

+The manuscript’s writing quality requires substantial improvement, as it contains numerous typos and grammatical errors. Examples include:
 -Line 15, “high-dimensional”
 -Line 22, “factorize”
 -Line 24, “property of the”
 -Lines 29, 34, “Dirichlet”
 -Line 34, “nested”
 -Fig. 1, “visual representation”
 -Fig. 1, “transmission” (the appropriateness of this term is questionable)
 -Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
 -Missing articles (e.g., “Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution”)
