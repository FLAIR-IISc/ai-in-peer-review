- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), augmenting Poisson factorization with multilayer temporal neural parameterizations for Gamma latent variables.
- Inference: Uses mean-field variational inference for latents plus a separate neural-network training stage with an autoencoder-like objective.
- Experiments: Evaluates against PGDS, LSTM, and static PFA on synthetic data and five real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disasters, Annual Sheep Population).
- Relation to prior work: Sits between dynamic PF, deep latent factor models, and RNN-based sequence models; novelty claimed in ladder-like temporal conditioning and ψ/λ sparsity controls.
- Strength — ambition: Attempts to integrate deep temporal structure with PF for sequential counts.
- Strength — conjugacy: Leverages Gamma–Poisson conjugacy and auxiliary counts for tractable, scalable VI updates.
- Strength — sparsity controls: Introduces feature- and component-level Gamma variables (ψ, λ) to encourage sparsity and interpretability.
- Strength — empirical breadth: Tests on diverse real-world datasets and offers qualitative factor interpretations.
- Major concern — temporal architecture: Temporal conditioning is nonstandard (cross-layer windowed dependence); authors must justify design and contrast with intra-layer recurrence.
- Major concern — incomplete model spec: Key distributions (Eqs. 3–4) and neural parameterizations are underspecified (shapes/rates, activations, window indices).
- Major concern — “implicit” terminology: Eqs. 9–10 are explicit Gamma conditionals; wording is misleading and needs correction or clarification.
- Major concern — autoencoder objective: Eq. 15–18 derivation and substitutions to squared-error are unclear; need connection to ELBO/amortized VI and discussion of estimator bias.
- Major concern — evaluation metrics: MSE/PMSE are insufficient; report predictive log-likelihood, perplexity, Poisson/NB deviance, and uncertainty-sensitive metrics.
- Major concern — model capacity: Chosen K is too small for datasets like ICEWS/NIPS; require experiments with larger K and sensitivity analyses for K, depth N, and window c.
- Major concern — empirical clarity: Figure 3 narrative and factor explanations are unclear; captions/legends need to be self-contained and legible.
- Major concern — derivation placement: VI update algebra could be moved to supplement to free main text for modeling/learning exposition.
- Major concern — reproducibility: Missing training details (optimizers, lr, init, early stopping), compute budgets, runtimes, ladder variant and ablation settings.
- Quality: Core idea plausible but presentation leaves distributions and objectives insufficiently specified; predictive-likelihood gains and ablations needed to support claims.
- Clarity: Paper requires substantial editing for grammar, precise notation, and figure readability; avoid mislabeling explicit conditionals as implicit.
- Originality: Interesting combination but overlaps prior recurrent PF and deep EF work; novelty mainly in ladder temporal conditioning and ψ/λ—need clearer differentiation.
- Significance: Potentially useful if shown to outperform baselines on proper metrics at realistic K; current evidence is mixed and underpowered by capacity/metrics choices.
- Pros for acceptance: Integrates deep temporal structure with PF, retains conjugacy, empirical breadth, and interpretability claims.
- Cons against acceptance: Insufficient model/objective specification, misuse of terminology, inadequate evaluation metrics/capacity, and unclear exposition.
- Suggested revisions: Precisely specify conditionals, justify cross-layer windowing, adopt/elaborate ELBO-based training, run larger-K and ablation studies, improve figures, move algebra to supplement, and substantially edit prose.
- Writing/minor issues: Numerous typos and unclear phrases (e.g., “fanatical data”); fix terminology, grammar, hyphenation, and figure captions/axes.
- Overall recommendation: Borderline reject; promising idea but requires clearer specification, principled training objective, stronger evaluations, and major editing.
