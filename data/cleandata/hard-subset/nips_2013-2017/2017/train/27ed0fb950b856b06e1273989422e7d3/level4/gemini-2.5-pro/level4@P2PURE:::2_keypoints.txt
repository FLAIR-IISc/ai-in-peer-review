- Proposes a novel RNN-based method that models time-varying Gamma shape parameters for sequential count data, integrating top-down priors and bottom-up likelihoods and updating via expectations to reduce sampling variance and improve efficiency.  
- Core idea is compelling for capturing long-term dependencies, but the manuscript appears hastily prepared.  
- Critical typos (e.g., Equation 10) need correction.  
- The model fixes the Gamma scale parameter and only learns the shape—motivation and implications require more detailed justification.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a two-layer model; include comparisons with single-layer and deeper architectures and sensitivity analyses for key hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).  
- Add a detailed graphical illustration of the full model architecture to improve clarity (e.g., similar to Fig.1 in Chung et al. 2015).  
- Introduction should discuss recent deep Poisson factor analysis advances, citing works such as Zhou et al. (2016) and Cong et al. (2017).
