- Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) to model long-term temporal dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Extends prior work (dynamic PFA, PGDS) and builds on Deep Exponential Families and Deep Poisson Factor Analysis.  
- Proposes a hierarchical neural network architecture to model implicit temporal distributions enabling short- and long-term dependencies.  
- Uses variational inference with neural network parameters and a novel loss derived from the variational distributions.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and multiple real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, ASP).  
- Shows competitive prediction and fitting performance versus PGDS, LSTM, and PFA baselines, with strong synthetic-data results.  
- Demonstrates interpretable latent structures (e.g., sustained conflict factor in ICEWS).  
- Technical approach is well-motivated and fills a clear gap in modeling long-range dependencies for count data.  
- Inference algorithm is soundly derived and the paper includes an honest discussion of limitations (pruning, layer redundancy).  
- Contains significant grammatical and technical errors and numerous typos that impair clarity.  
- Incorrect terminology (e.g., misattributing “Poisson Randomized Gamma” vs. Gamma-Poisson).  
- Lacks a comparison with a single-layer dynamic PFA, leaving unclear whether depth provides real benefit.  
- LSTM comparison is weakened by differing training paradigms (variational inference vs. SGD).  
- Uses MSE/PMSE instead of held-out perplexity, an uncommon choice for count-data predictive evaluation.  
- ICEWS prediction results are notably worse than PGDS and the paper does not adequately explain this anomaly.  
- Overall: scientifically valid contribution but requires revisions to fix errors, add key comparisons, justify deep structure, and clarify evaluation.
