- Proposes using RNNs to parameterize gamma distributions to capture complex long-time dependence in sequential count data.  
- Shape parameters are approximated by neural networks that integrate prior (higher layers) and likelihood (lower layers) information.  
- Network parameters are updated using expectations of latent variables to reduce variance and improve computational efficiency.  
- Manuscript presents an interesting and insightful approach but reads somewhat rushed.  
- Typographical errors present (e.g., Equation 10) that need correction.  
- Authors fix the gamma scale parameter and only update the shape; this design choice lacks detailed justification and analysis.  
- Experiments are limited to a two-layer model; need comparisons with single-layer and deeper models and sensitivity analyses (window size, number of factors K).  
- Recommend adding a detailed graphical illustration of the full model for clarity (e.g., inspired by Figure 1 in Chung et al. 2015).  
- Recent related work in deep Poisson factor analysis (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) is missing and should be discussed and differentiated.  
- Strength: novel integration of RNNs with Poisson Factor Analysis for sequential count modeling.  
- Strength: efficient inference via expectation propagation rather than sampling.  
- Strength: aims for interpretable latent structures.  
- Strength: promising empirical results on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Weakness: typographical issues reduce manuscript polish.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for fixing the gamma scale parameter.  
- Weakness: limited ablation studies and hyperparameter sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: omission of recent relevant related work.  
- Weakness: model architecture description lacks a clear diagram.  
- For acceptance: novel potentially impactful method, competitive empirical performance, and scalable variational inference.  
- Against acceptance: manuscript lacks polish, key design choices unjustified, incomplete experimental evaluation, and missing related work.  
- Overall assessment: promising contribution but requires significant revision; recommend rejection with encouragement to resubmit after addressing concerns.
