- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) for sequential count vectors.  
- Uses recurrent neural networks to output Gamma shape parameters that encode temporal dependencies and integrate top-down priors with bottom-up signals.  
- Learning via variational inference with conjugate mean-field updates for latents and expectation-based NN optimization, reducing sampling variance and improving efficiency.  
- Claims to model long-range temporal structure in dynamic count data effectively.  
- Manuscript appears rushed with several typographical and notational errors (e.g., Equation (10)).  
- The design choice to fix Gamma rate (scale) parameters and only update shape parameters needs deeper discussion and empirical analysis.  
- Experiments are limited to a two-layer model; comparisons to single-layer and deeper variants plus ablations on hyperparameters (window size, number of factors) are needed.  
- Include a detailed graphical depiction of the full model for clarity (reference-style figure suggested).  
- Related recent deep Poisson factorization works (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) should be discussed in the Introduction.
