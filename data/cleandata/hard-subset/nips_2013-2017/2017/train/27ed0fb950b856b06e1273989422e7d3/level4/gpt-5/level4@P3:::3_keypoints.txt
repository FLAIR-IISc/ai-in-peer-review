- Summary: Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), combining Poisson factorization with deep recurrent Gamma conditionals and a probability autoencoder to model short- and long-range temporal dependencies in count data.  
- Relation to prior work: Positions DDPFA against PGDS and deep latent variable/autoregressive models, claiming better long-range modeling through hierarchical recurrent structure.  

- Strength: Tackles an important problem of temporal modeling in high-dimensional sparse counts.  
- Strength: Novel integration of conjugate Poisson factorization with deep, recurrently parameterized Gamma conditionals.  
- Strength: Practical two-stage inference (mean-field conjugate updates + neural training) that scales to sizable datasets.  
- Strength: Empirical improvements over PGDS, PFA, and LSTM on several datasets with qualitative evidence of more persistent factors.  
- Strength: Discusses architectural choices (ladder structure) and practical issues like pruning.  

- Weakness: Training objective/inference unclear — squared-error surrogates replace a clear ELBO without derivation or justification, risking bias or double-counting.  
- Weakness: Identifiability and scaling ambiguities among Φ, Θ, Λ, Ψ are not resolved; effects on interpretability and optimization are unclear.  
- Weakness: Evaluation uses MSE/PMSE; lacks standard probabilistic metrics (held-out predictive log-likelihood, perplexity, Poisson/neg-bin deviance).  
- Weakness: Ablations insufficient to justify depth — limited gains from deeper layers in reported results and ladder variant confounds hierarchy vs recurrence.  
- Weakness: Missing relevant baseline (Recurrent Poisson Factorization) to isolate benefits of hierarchical structure vs RNN dynamics.  
- Weakness: Experimental protocol details (hyperparameter tuning, validation splits, early stopping, runtime) are missing, risking unfair comparisons.  
- Weakness: Possible Bernoulli–Poisson link typo (b = 1(x > 1) vs typical b = 1(x > 0)) needs confirmation.  
- Weakness: References incomplete or malformed throughout the manuscript.  

- Quality: Modeling is technically interesting and conjugate mean-field updates are sound, but the probabilistic grounding of the training objective is weak.  
- Clarity: Organization is reasonable but grammar, inconsistent notation, and an under-derived “Probability AutoEncoder” section hinder understanding.  
- Originality: Novel combination of deep recurrent Gamma conditionals within a PF framework and ladder-style coupling in this context.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful for sequential count data if objective is clarified and depth is empirically justified.  

- Specific request: Provide the exact ELBO and demonstrate how the squared-error objectives approximate or bound it, or adopt end-to-end VI (e.g., reparameterizations for Gamma).  
- Specific request: Add held-out predictive log-likelihood/perplexity and Poisson/negative-binomial deviance with confidence intervals.  
- Specific request: Include ablations isolating depth vs recurrence: single-layer dynamic PF with RNN, DDPFA with 1 vs 2/3 layers, and non-recurrent deep PF.  
- Specific request: Add Recurrent Poisson Factorization as a baseline.  
- Specific request: Detail hyperparameter tuning, validation splits, network sizes, K and window ranges, early stopping, and runtime/convergence diagnostics.  
- Specific request: Confirm/correct Bernoulli–Poisson threshold and bold best numbers/standardize significant figures in tables.  

- Typos/grammar: Multiple phrasing and spelling errors (examples provided) — thorough proofreading and notation consistency required.  

- Pros for acceptance: Creative modeling approach, promising empirical gains, scalable practical inference, and useful qualitative analyses.  
- Cons for acceptance: Unjustified training objective, missing key ablations, lacking standard probabilistic evaluation, and clarity/notation errors.  

- Recommendation: Weak reject — promising work needing clearer probabilistic grounding, stronger/fairer evaluation and ablations, and substantial editing before acceptance.
