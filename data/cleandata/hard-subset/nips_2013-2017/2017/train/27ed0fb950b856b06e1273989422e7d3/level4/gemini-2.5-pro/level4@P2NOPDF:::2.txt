This paper proposes a novel deep generative model for sequential count data, designed to capture complex, long-range temporal dependencies. The core idea is to employ a Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) to model the dynamics of latent variables. Specifically, the model assumes a hierarchy of gamma-distributed latent variables where the shape parameters are determined by an RNN at each time step. This architecture allows the model to integrate top-down information from the prior (higher layers) and bottom-up information from the data likelihood (lower layers). For parameter estimation, the authors use an update scheme based on the expectation of the latent variables, which cleverly reduces the variance associated with sampling-based methods and enhances computational efficiency.

Overall, the model presents an elegant and promising approach for dynamic topic modeling. The core idea is strong and well-motivated. However, the manuscript appears to have been written in haste and could be significantly strengthened by addressing the following points:

1.  **Clarity and Presentation:** The manuscript's clarity could be substantially improved. I strongly recommend including a detailed graphical illustration of the full generative and inference model to help readers visualize the complex dependencies and the flow of information between layers and across time steps. A diagram similar to Figure 1 in Chung et al. (2015) [1] would be highly beneficial. Furthermore, the paper contains several typos that impede comprehension; for instance, Equation 10 appears to contain a critical error that should be corrected.

2.  **Justification of Modeling Choices:** The rationale behind certain modeling choices requires further justification. The authors fix the scale parameter of the gamma distributions and only learn the shape parameter. A detailed discussion on the motivation for this constraint and an analysis of its potential impact on model flexibility and performance are needed to convince the reader of this design choice.

3.  **Insufficient Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental validation is not sufficiently thorough to fully support the paper's claims. The authors only report results for a two-layer architecture. To properly assess the contribution of the proposed deep hierarchical structure, an ablation study is essential. This study should include comparisons with a single-layer baseline and, if computationally feasible, a deeper model (e.g., three layers) to understand the impact of model depth on performance. Additionally, the paper would be strengthened by a sensitivity analysis of other key hyperparameters, such as the RNN window size and the number of latent factors.

4.  **Incomplete Discussion of Related Work:** The discussion of related work should be expanded to situate the proposed method within the context of recent advancements. Several highly relevant deep generative models for count data have been recently proposed, including Gamma Belief Networks [2] and Deep Latent Dirichlet Allocation [3]. The introduction and related work sections should be updated to include and differentiate the proposed method from these models. This is crucial for clarifying the paper's unique contributions to the field.
