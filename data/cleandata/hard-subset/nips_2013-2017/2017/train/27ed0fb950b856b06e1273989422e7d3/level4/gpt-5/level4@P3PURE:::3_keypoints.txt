- Paper proposes a deep Poisson factorization model with an RNN-like hierarchical structure to capture long-range temporal dependence; inference via variational inference plus optimization of neural network parameters.  
- Technical correction: the claim about modeling nonnegative numbers is wrong — it is the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution that models nonnegative numbers.  
- Experimental metrics: emphasize held-out perplexity as the primary performance metric for count data; MSE/PMSE are secondary.  
- Hierarchy-depth justification: include a comparison to a single-level dynamic Poisson factorization to demonstrate the benefit of multiple hierarchical levels; the LSTM comparison alone is insufficient.  
- Presentation: bold the best results in Table 2 to improve readability.  
- Writing and grammar: numerous typos and awkward phrases require thorough proofreading (examples listed in the review).  
- Overall assessment: promising and technically interesting approach; fix the factual error, emphasize perplexity, add single-level comparison, and correct writing to substantially strengthen the paper.
