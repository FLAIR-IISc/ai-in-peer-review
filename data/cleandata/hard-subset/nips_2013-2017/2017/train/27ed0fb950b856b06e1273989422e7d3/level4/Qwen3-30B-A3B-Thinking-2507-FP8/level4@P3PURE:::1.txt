

This paper introduces a deep dynamic Poisson factorization model that extends Poisson factorization (PF) to incorporate temporal dependencies. Unlike prior work on dynamic PF, this approach employs a simplified recurrent neural network (RNN) structure to capture long-term dependencies. Inference is performed via variational inference, with an additional optimization step over the neural network parameters. The authors report experiments across five real-world datasets.

The core idea is potentially interesting, but the paper requires significant revisions to address technical and presentation issues before it can be considered for publication at NIPS. Below are specific comments:

+ **Eq. 4**: The formulation does not align with a standard RNN structure, as \( h_t^{(n)} \) depends solely on top-layer memory vectors without incorporating prior time steps for the same layer. Is there a specific rationale for this simplification?
+ **Section 2**: Key model equations are incomplete (e.g., the parameterization of the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 is unclear, and the generative process for \( \theta_t \) in Eq. 3 is unspecified).
+ **Lines 110–118**: The discussion of "implicit distributions" is incorrect. Equations 9 and 10 are explicit: \( \theta_{tk} \) (Eq. 9) is a Gamma distribution with closed-form shape and rate parameters conditioned on \( h^{(0)} \), and \( h \) (Eq. 10) similarly admits explicit computation. Neither qualifies as "implicit."
+ **Lines 30–31**: The phrase "fanatical data" appears to be a typo (e.g., likely "financial" or "fashion" data). Please clarify the intended meaning and its relevance to long-term dependencies.
+ **Lines 133–155**: The "loss function" referenced in Eq. 15 is not clearly defined. Please specify its exact form and relationship to the model.
+ **Evaluation Metrics**: MSE/PMSE is generally unsuitable for PF-like models. Results must include predictive log-likelihood alongside these metrics.
+ **Value of \( K \)**: The experimental value of \( K \) (line 207) appears unrealistically small. I recommend testing larger values (e.g., \( K = 100 \)) to assess scalability.
+ **Lines 224–232 & Fig. 3**: The discussion fails to connect with Figure 3. Clarify the relationship between the text and the figure.
+ **Variational Update Equations (Eqs. 11–14)**: These can be safely moved to the supplementary material.
+ **Fig. 4 Caption**: The caption is illegible; please revise for clarity.
+ **Passive Voice**: The abstract (and other sections) overuses passive voice. Adhere to active voice conventions throughout.
+ **Writing Quality**: The paper contains numerous grammatical errors and typos. Examples include:
  - Line 15: "high-dimensional" (correct, but inconsistent with common usage; consider "high-dimensional" or "high-dimensional data" for clarity)
  - Line 22: "factorize" → should be "factorization"
  - Line 24: "property of the" → incomplete phrase
  - Lines 29, 34: "Dirichlet" (repeated; ensure consistent capitalization as "Dirichlet")
  - Line 34: "nested" → unclear context
  - Fig. 1: "visual representation" (redundant; "visualization" suffices)
  - Fig. 1: "transmission" (questionable term; reconsider)
  - Line 49: Incomplete sentence
  - "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution" → missing article ("a prior distribution")
