This paper investigates incorporating long-range temporal dependencies into a Poisson Factorization (PF) model by introducing a deep, recurrent neural network structure. Unlike previous work on dynamic PF, which often relies on simpler temporal models, this approach leverages the representational power of RNNs to capture long-term relationships in the data. Inference is performed using variational inference, combined with optimization techniques for training the neural network parameters. The paper presents strong technical content, though it suffers from several grammatical and factual inaccuracies that detract from its clarity.

Specifically, I identified the following issues:

1. The claim made regarding the modeling of nonnegative numbers in lines 86-87 is inaccurate. The Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution, not the method described, is typically used for this purpose.

2. For improved readability, please bold the best-performing results in Table 2 to clearly highlight the key findings.

3. While the use of MSE and PMSE is reasonable, the experiments in Section 4.2 would benefit from a more prominent evaluation using held-out perplexity, which is generally a stronger indicator of model performance for this type of task.

4. The paper effectively addresses limitations of existing approaches (references 1 & 2) in modeling long-range dependencies. However, to better demonstrate the benefit of the proposed multi-level hierarchical structure, a comparison against a dynamic PF model with a single-level deep architecture would be valuable. The comparison to LSTM is useful, but the fundamentally different learning algorithms make it difficult to directly attribute performance gains to the hierarchical structure itself. A more direct comparison within the PF framework would strengthen this justification.

5. Numerous grammatical errors and typos hinder readability. A thorough proofread is strongly recommended. Examples include:

* “unit the data”
* “Noticing the lack of capturing ..”
* “PFA factorized a count matrix” (inconsistent tense)
* “other data with different pattern long-time dependence ..”
* “Examples for this including ..”
* “shows excellent results on predict and ..”
* “However, it is rough for them ..”
* “V-dimesnion sequentially ..”
* “K-dimension latent variable ..”
* “due to the including of feature-wise”
* “recommend systems” 

Addressing these errors would significantly improve the paper's overall presentation and impact.

In summary, the paper presents a novel and potentially valuable approach to dynamic Poisson factorization. However, the identified factual inaccuracy, the need for a more compelling demonstration of the hierarchical structure’s benefits, and the numerous grammatical errors require attention before publication. I recommend addressing these points before a final decision is made.
