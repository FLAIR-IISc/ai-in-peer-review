## Review of Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model

This paper proposes a novel Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) for analyzing sequential count vectors. The core idea is to leverage recurrent neural networks to model the temporal dependencies inherent in Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA), effectively capturing both short-term and long-term relationships between factors. By representing implicit distributions with neural networks and employing variational inference for parameter estimation, the authors aim to overcome the limitations of existing dynamic count models like PGDS and temporal Dirichlet processes. The approach is interesting and offers a potentially powerful way to address the challenges of modeling complex temporal patterns in count data.

The paper begins by outlining the problem of analyzing sequential count vectors, highlighting their prevalence in applications like recommendation systems and text analysis. It correctly identifies the shortcomings of existing Bayesian factor analysis methods, particularly their difficulty in capturing long-range dependencies. The introduction provides a good overview of relevant prior work, including deep models for Bayesian inference and recent advancements in deep Poisson factor analysis. However, it could benefit from a more thorough discussion of recent deep Poisson factor analysis models, specifically citing [2] and [3] as suggested by the author, as well as potentially [1] for graphical model inspiration.

The proposed DDPFA model is clearly presented, with a well-defined generative process. The use of recurrent neural networks to model the temporal evolution of latent variables is a key contribution. The explanation of the variational inference procedure is generally understandable, though a deeper dive into the derivation of the ELBO and the coordinate ascent updates might be beneficial for some readers. The authors’ decision to focus on mean-field variational inference is noted, and acknowledging the potential limitations associated with this choice, as alluded to in the paper's conclusion, is prudent. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of PFA with deep recurrent neural networks is a novel approach to modeling sequential count data.
* **Potential for capturing long-term dependencies:** The deep structure and recurrent connections are designed to address the limitations of existing models in capturing long-range temporal dependencies.
* **Variational Inference:** Utilizing variational inference provides a scalable alternative to MCMC methods, making the model applicable to larger datasets.
* **Interpretability:**  The latent factor structure of PFA contributes to the interpretability of the model.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity & Polish:** The manuscript contains several minor typos (e.g., in Equation 10) and could benefit from more careful editing for clarity and conciseness.
* **Model Assumptions:** The decision to only update the shape parameter of the Gamma distributions while keeping the scale parameter fixed requires more justification and analysis. The authors should discuss the implications of this choice and potentially explore alternative approaches.
* **Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental evaluation is somewhat limited.  The authors only explore a two-layer architecture and do not systematically compare performance with single-layer or deeper models. A more comprehensive ablation study varying the number of layers, window size, and number of factors is needed to understand the sensitivity of the model to these parameters.
* **Visualizations:** The paper lacks a detailed graphical illustration of the entire model. A figure similar to Figure 1 in [1] would greatly improve understanding.
* **Limited Comparison:** While comparisons are made with PGDS, LSTM, and PFA, a more extensive comparison with state-of-the-art deep count models would be valuable.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper proposes a novel and potentially impactful approach to modeling sequential count data.
* The theoretical framework is sound and well-motivated.
* The experimental results, while limited, demonstrate promising performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The manuscript suffers from minor clarity issues and requires polishing.
* The experimental evaluation is not sufficiently comprehensive to fully validate the proposed approach.
* The model assumptions and parameter choices require further justification.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field. The proposed DDPFA model is a promising approach for capturing complex temporal dependencies in sequential count data. However, the manuscript requires further refinement in terms of clarity, experimental evaluation, and justification of model assumptions. 

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **weak accept** with a request for major revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  Address the identified typos and clarity issues.
2.  Provide a more thorough justification for the fixed scale parameter in the Gamma distributions.
3.  Conduct a more comprehensive experimental evaluation, including ablation studies with varying numbers of layers, window sizes, and factors.
4.  Include a detailed graphical illustration of the model.
5.  Expand the related work section to include more recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis.
