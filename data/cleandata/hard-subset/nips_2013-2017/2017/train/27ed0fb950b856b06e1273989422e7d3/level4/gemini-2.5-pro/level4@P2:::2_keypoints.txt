- Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that integrates RNNs to generate Gamma shape parameters for hierarchical latent variables to model long-range temporal dependencies in count data.  
- Builds on Poisson Factorization Analysis (PFA) and aims to overcome first-order Markov limitations of models like PGDS by using a deep, multi-layer latent structure.  
- Uses a hybrid two-stage inference: mean-field variational inference for latent variables and gradient-based optimization for RNN parameters.  
- Novelty: parameterizing priors of dynamic latent variables with RNNs is a compelling and original idea for capturing complex temporal structure.  
- Model design: stacked latent layers can capture multi-scale temporal patterns and the “Probabilistic Autoencoder” balances information between prior and posterior layers.  
- Inference scheme: the two-stage procedure is sensible, likely more stable and lower-variance than sampling-based updates.  
- Clarity/presentation: manuscript is hard to follow and lacks a graphical model illustrating variables and dependencies.  
- Notation/equations: some equations are confusing or ambiguous (e.g., Equation 10’s fixed rate βh), and important modeling choices (fixed vs. learned rates) are not justified.  
- Inference description: the “Probabilistic Autoencoder” objective and the L2 expectation loss (Equation 16) are tersely presented and not connected to a principled variational objective.  
- Experiments: insufficient validation of the deep structure — missing a 1-layer ablation and limited analysis isolating depth benefits.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: no analysis of key hyperparameters (window size c, number of factors K, RNN architecture) to assess robustness.  
- Related work: misses discussion and comparison to relevant recent deep count models (e.g., AGBN, DeepLDA) and contains at least one incorrect citation/title to fix.  
- Recommendation: promising contribution but requires major revision for clarity, stronger experiments (especially ablations and sensitivity analyses), and better literature positioning before acceptance.
