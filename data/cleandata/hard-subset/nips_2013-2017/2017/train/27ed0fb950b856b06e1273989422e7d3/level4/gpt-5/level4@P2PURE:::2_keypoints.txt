- Positive contribution: proposes an interesting, effective method using RNN-parameterized gamma shape parameters to model long-range temporal dependencies in count vectors.  
- Technical advantage: updating network parameters via expectations of latent variables reduces sampling variance and improves computational efficiency.  
- Manuscript quality: paper appears somewhat rushed and contains critical typos (e.g., Equation (10)).  
- Modeling choice concern: the gamma scale parameter is fixed while only the shape is learned—this design needs deeper discussion and empirical analysis.  
- Experimental limitations: results are only for a two-layer model with no comparisons to single-layer or deeper variants; ablations on depth, window size, and number of factors are missing.  
- Presentation request: include a detailed graphical illustration of the full model for clarity.  
- Related work: recent deep Poisson/factor analysis models (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) should be discussed in the Introduction.
