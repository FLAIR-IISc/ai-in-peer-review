This paper introduces the deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, a model that builds on Poisson factorization (PF) to allow for temporal dependencies. In contrast to previous works on dynamic PF, this paper uses a simplified version of a recurrent neural network to allow for long-term dependencies. Inference is carried out via variational inference, with an extra step to maximize over the neural network parameters. The paper reports experiments on five real-world datasets.

Overall, I found the idea potentially interesting, but I still think the paper needs some improvements in its execution before it can be accepted in a conference like NIPS. Please find my comments below.

Firstly, the formulation in Eq. 4 does not correspond to a traditional RNN structure, as h_t^{(n)} depends only on the top-layer memory vectors and does not depend on previous time steps for the same layer, as in the RNN setup. Is there any reason for this simplified structure? The authors should provide a clear justification for this design choice.

Furthermore, some of the model equations in Section 2 are missing or unclear. For instance, it is not specified how to parameterize the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4 or what distribution generates theta_t in Eq. 3. The authors should provide complete and explicit equations for all components of the model.

I also disagree with the discussion in lines 110-118 regarding "implicit distributions." Eqs. 9 and 10 are actually explicit: they are Gamma distributions with given shapes and rates. Since the distribution of theta_tk (Eq. 9) can be computed in closed form conditioned on h^{(0)}, it cannot be called implicit. The same comment applies to h in Eq. 10.

Additionally, the sentence in lines 30-31, "This method may have weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data," is unclear. The authors should elaborate on this statement and provide specific examples or explanations.

The subsection in lines 133-155 is also unclear, particularly with regards to the "loss function" in Eq. 15. The authors should provide a clear definition and explanation of this loss function and its role in the model.

In terms of evaluation metrics, MSE/PMSE is typically not a good metric for PF-like models. The results should additionally include at least predictive log-likelihood to provide a more comprehensive assessment of the model's performance.

The value of K in the experiments (line 207) seems too small. I suggest the authors use larger values, such as K=100 (at least), to demonstrate the model's scalability and robustness.

I had difficulty connecting the discussion in lines 224-232 with Fig. 3. The authors should revise the text to provide a clearer explanation of the figure and its relationship to the surrounding discussion.

The update equations for the variational inference procedure (Eqs. 11-14) can safely be moved to the supplement, as they are not essential to understanding the main ideas of the paper.

The caption in Fig. 4 is difficult to read. The authors should revise the caption to make it clearer and more concise.

I strongly recommend that the authors follow general writing advice about the use of the passive voice, especially in the abstract. The writing quality is poor, and the paper contains many typos. Here are some examples:

* Line 15: "high-dimensional"
* Line 22: "factorize"
* Line 24: "property of the"
* Lines 29, 34: "Dirichlet"
* Line 34: "nested"
* Fig. 1: "visual representation"
* Fig. 1: "transmission" (not sure if that's an appropriate word)
* Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)
* Many missing articles (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution")

Overall, while the idea presented in this paper is interesting, the execution and writing quality need significant improvement before it can be considered for acceptance in a conference like NIPS.
