The authors propose a novel method to capture complex long-time dependencies over count vectors by leveraging recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, specifically using the gamma distribution. The shape parameters of these gamma distributions, which capture time dependencies, are approximated by neural networks that attempt to incorporate information from both prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) sources. The parameters in the neural network are updated based on the expectation of latent variables, which helps reduce the variance caused by sampling and increase computational efficiency. This model provides an interesting and innovative approach to handling the long dependency problem in dynamic models.

Although the idea is compelling, the manuscript appears to have been written in haste, and several aspects require attention. Below are some specific comments and suggestions for improvement:

1. The manuscript contains several critical typos, including Equation 10, which need to be corrected to ensure clarity and accuracy.

2. The authors consider the scale parameter as fixed and only update the shape parameter in the gamma distributions. This assumption requires more detailed discussion and analysis to justify its validity and potential limitations.

3. The experimental results only demonstrate the performance of the proposed model with two layers, without comparing it to models with a single layer or more layers. This limited comparison makes it difficult to understand the influence of the number of layers on performance. Additionally, other parameters, such as the size of the window and the number of factors, require further discussion and analysis.

4. To facilitate better understanding, it would be beneficial to include detailed graphical illustrations of the entire model. The authors may refer to Figure 1 in [1] for inspiration.

5. Recent advances in deep Poisson factor analysis models, such as those proposed in [2] and [3], should be discussed in the Introduction to provide a more comprehensive context for the proposed method.

References:
[1] Chung, J., Kastner, K., Dinh, L., et al. A Recurrent Latent Variable Model for Sequential Data. NIPS, 2015.

[2] Zhou, M., Cong, Y., & Chen, B. Augmentable gamma belief networks. Journal of Machine Learning Research, 17(163), 1-44, 2016.

[3] Cong, Y., Chen, B., Liu, H., & Zhou, M. Deep latent Dirichlet allocation with topic-layer-adaptive stochastic gradient Riemannian MCMC. ICML 2017.

Overall, the proposed model has the potential to effectively capture complex long-time dependencies in dynamic models. With revisions to address the mentioned concerns, the manuscript can be improved to provide a clearer and more comprehensive presentation of the method and its contributions.
