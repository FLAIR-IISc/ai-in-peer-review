1. Proposes modeling complex long-term dependencies in count data by using RNNs to output gamma-distribution shape parameters (integrating prior and likelihood), updating network parameters via expectations to reduce sampling variance.  
2. The approach is clever and promising for addressing long-range dependency in dynamic models.  
3. Manuscript appears rushed and contains critical typos (e.g., equation 10).  
4. The choice to fix the gamma scale parameter and only update the shape parameter requires more detailed justification and analysis.  
5. Experiments are limited to a two-layer model — authors should compare single-layer and deeper models and analyze effects of window size and number of factors.  
6. Include a detailed graphical illustration of the complete model (e.g., similar to Fig. 1 in Chung et al., 2015).  
7. Discuss recent related work in deep Poisson factor analysis (e.g., Zhou et al., 2016; Cong et al., 2017) in the Introduction for better context.
