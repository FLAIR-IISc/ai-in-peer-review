- Paper introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), extending Poisson factorization to sequential counts via a multi-layer, RNN-inspired mechanism.  
- Uses neural networks to parameterize latent-factor dynamics and mean-field variational inference with an additional VAE-like optimization for network parameters.  
- Evaluated on synthetic data and five real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, Annual Sheep Population).  

Strengths:
- Combining PF with neural networks to capture longer-term dependencies addresses limitations of Markovian dynamic PF.  
- Many latent-variable updates have closed-form variational updates, which is computationally appealing.  
- Broad empirical evaluation across five real-world datasets demonstrates practical effort.  

Weaknesses / Detailed concerns:
- The “RNN” in Eq. 4 is not a standard RNN since h(n)_t does not depend on h(n)_{t-1}, removing within-layer temporal recurrence.  
- Key model specifications are missing (e.g., Gamma parameterization in Eq. 4, exact distribution generating θ_t in Eq. 3).  
- Calling Eqs. 9–10 “implicit” is incorrect: they are explicit Gamma distributions with closed-form conditionals.  
- Unclear phrasing (e.g., “different pattern long-time dependence,” “fanatical data”) needs clarification and concrete examples.  
- The Probability AutoEncoder objective (Eq. 15) is poorly defined; Eq. 16 looks like a heuristic and lacks ELBO/likelihood justification.  
- Notation and assumptions for H(0)' and related terms are unclear and need clarification.  
- Evaluation uses MSE/PMSE, which misalign with Poisson probabilistic goals; predictive log-likelihood or held-out perplexity should be reported.  
- Model capacity appears small (K likely too low); results with larger K (e.g., K=100) are requested.  
- Discussion (lines 224–232) does not clearly connect to Fig. 3; which factors map to which events and how long-term activation is measured is unclear.  
- Variational-update equations (Eqs. 11–14) could be moved to the supplement to improve readability.  
- Fig. 4 caption/quality is illegible and needs improvement.  
- Writing quality, grammar, and clarity need substantial editing; many typos, missing articles, and awkward phrasing hinder comprehension.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Interesting conceptual direction injecting neural structure into PF for longer-term dependencies.  
- Variational inference with closed-form updates is attractive.  
- Broad experimental coverage across multiple real datasets.  

Cons for acceptance:
- The “RNN” structure is not truly recurrent and key modeling choices are underspecified.  
- Mischaracterization of explicit distributions as “implicit.”  
- Probability AutoEncoder objective is not rigorously derived and appears ad hoc.  
- Evaluation metrics are inappropriate for probabilistic modeling; likely underparameterization and unclear figures.  
- Poor figure quality and writing hamper clarity.  

Recommendation:
- Promising idea but requires substantial revision: fix temporal recurrence and explicit parameterizations, provide principled neural-objective derivation, report predictive log-likelihood and larger K experiments, improve figures, and thoroughly revise writing.
