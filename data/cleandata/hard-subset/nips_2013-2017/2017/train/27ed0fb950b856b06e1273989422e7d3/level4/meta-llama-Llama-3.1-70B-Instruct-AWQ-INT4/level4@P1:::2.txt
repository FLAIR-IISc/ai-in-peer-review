The authors propose a novel method to capture the complex long-time dependence over the count vectors by exploiting recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, which is realized by the gamma distribution. The shape parameters of those gamma distributions, capturing the time dependence, are approximated by neural networks, which try to cover the information from prior (from higher layer) and likelihood (from low layer). The parameters in neural network are updated based on the expectation of latent variables, which is helpful to reduce the variance caused by sampling and increase the computational efficiency. The model provides an interesting and smart way to handle the long dependency problem in the dynamic model.

The paper is well-motivated and the problem of modeling complex long-time dependence in count vectors is well-justified. The authors provide a thorough review of existing methods and clearly explain the limitations of current approaches. The proposed Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) model is well-described, and the use of recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions is a novel and promising approach.

The experimental evaluation of the proposed model is thorough, with four multi-dimensional synthetic datasets and five real-world datasets used to assess its performance. The results show that the DDPFA model outperforms existing methods, including Poisson-Gamma Dynamical System (PGDS), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), and Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA), in most cases.

However, there are some issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, there are some typos in the manuscript, such as in Equation 10. Secondly, the authors consider the scale parameter as fixed and only update the shape parameter in those gamma distributions, which needs more detailed discussion and analysis. Thirdly, the experimental evaluation only considers the performance of the proposed model with two layers, and does not compare it with the model with a single layer and more layers. This makes it difficult to understand the influence of the layers on the performance.

Additionally, some other parameters, such as the size of the window and the number of factors, also need to be discussed. Furthermore, the manuscript would benefit from more detailed graphical illustrations of the whole model, such as a figure showing the architecture of the DDPFA model.

Finally, the authors should discuss some recent deep Poisson factor analysis models, such as those proposed in [2] and [3], in the Introduction.

Overall, the paper is well-written and the proposed model is a significant contribution to the field. However, there are some issues that need to be addressed to make the manuscript more complete and polished.
