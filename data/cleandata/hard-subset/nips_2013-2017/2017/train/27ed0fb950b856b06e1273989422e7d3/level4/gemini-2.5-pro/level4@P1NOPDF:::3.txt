This paper proposes a deep Poisson factorization model designed to incorporate long-range temporal dependencies. Unlike previous work on dynamic Poisson factorization, the proposed model employs an RNN-like architecture to capture these long-term patterns. Inference is performed using a variational framework to learn the neural network parameters. While the paper presents a solid technical contribution, its clarity is diminished by several grammatical and factual errors, which are detailed below.

1.  The statement on lines 86-87 regarding the modeling of non-negative numbers is incorrect. The paper should clarify that it is the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution that is used for this purpose.

2.  For improved readability, please bold the best-performing results in Table 2.

3.  Held-out perplexity would be a more conventional and arguably stronger evaluation metric for the experiments in Section 4.2. While the reported Mean Squared Error (MSE) and Poisson Mean Squared Error (PMSE) are acceptable, perplexity is a standard measure for this class of models and would provide more convincing results.

4.  The paper effectively addresses the limitations of prior work [1, 2] in modeling long-range dependencies. However, the experimental design could be strengthened to better validate the model's key contribution. To properly demonstrate the utility of the proposed multi-level hierarchy, a comparison against a dynamic Poisson Factorization (PF) model with a single-level deep architecture would be a more direct and informative baseline. The current comparison to an LSTM is not ideal, as the models use different learning algorithms, making it difficult to isolate the contribution of the hierarchical structure. As a result, this comparison does not sufficiently justify the need for multiple levels of depth in the proposed model.
