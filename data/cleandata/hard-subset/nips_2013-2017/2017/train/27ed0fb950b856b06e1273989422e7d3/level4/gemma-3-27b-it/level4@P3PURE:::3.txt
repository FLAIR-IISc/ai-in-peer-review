The paper addresses incorporating long-range temporal dependencies within a Poisson factorization model, leveraging a deep structure. Unlike prior work on dynamic PF, this paper employs a recurrent neural network-like structure to model these long-term dependencies, with inference performed using variational inference and optimization routines for the neural network parameters. The paper contains strong technical content but suffers from several grammatical and factual inaccuracies, detailed below.

1. The statement regarding modeling nonnegative numbers on lines 86-87 is incorrect. The Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is the appropriate model for nonnegative data.
2. Please bold the best results in Table 2 for clarity.
3. While MSE/PMSE are acceptable metrics, held-out perplexity would have provided a stronger indicator of performance in the experiments presented in Section 4.2.
4. The limitations of existing methods for modeling long-range dependency (references 1, 2) are adequately addressed. However, to better illustrate the benefit of the proposed method’s multi-level hierarchy, a comparison with a dynamic PF employing a single-level deep architecture would have been valuable. The comparison with LSTM is useful, but differing learning algorithms make it difficult to directly attribute performance gains to the hierarchical structure of the proposed method. Therefore, the comparison does not fully justify the need for multiple levels of hierarchy.
5. Numerous grammatical errors and typos impede readability. Examples include:
-- “unit the data”
-- “Noticing the lack of capturing ..”
-- “PFA factorized a count matrix” (inconsistent tense with surrounding sentences)
-- “other data with different pattern long-time dependence ..”
-- “Examples for this including ..”
-- “shows excellent results on predict and ..”
-- “However, it is rough for them ..”
-- “V-dimesnion sequentially ..”
-- “K-dimension latent variable ..”
-- “due to the including of feature-wise”
-- “recommend systems,” etc.
