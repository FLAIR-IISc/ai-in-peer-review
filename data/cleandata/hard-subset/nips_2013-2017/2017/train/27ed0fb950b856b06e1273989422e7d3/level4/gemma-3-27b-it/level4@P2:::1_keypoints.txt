- Introduces DDPFA: a novel deep dynamic Poisson factorization using a simplified RNN to model temporal dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Variational inference with an extra optimization step for neural net parameters is used for parameter estimation.  
- Evaluated on five real-world datasets, showing promising practical results.  
- Core idea is interesting but the manuscript needs substantial refinement to meet NIPS standards.  
- Equation 4’s formulation does not match standard RNNs: hidden state depends only on top-layer memory vectors, lacking within-layer temporal recurrence; motivation for this simplification is missing.  
- Section 2 omits crucial model details (e.g., Gamma parameterization in Eq.4 and distribution generating θ_t in Eq.3), hindering reproducibility.  
- Description of “implicit distributions” (lines 110–118) is inaccurate: Eqs.9–10 define explicit Gamma distributions and are not implicit.  
- Vague claim about prior methods failing on "fanatical data and disaster data" (lines 30–31) needs concrete examples or clearer explanation.  
- Subsection (lines 133–155) and derivation/meaning of the loss function (Eq.15) are unclear and require a detailed breakdown.  
- Exclusive use of MSE and PMSE is insufficient; predictive log-likelihood should be reported for probabilistic factorization models.  
- Number of factors K in experiments is too small; larger values (e.g., K≥100) should be tested to assess model capacity.  
- Connection between text (lines 224–232) and Figure 3 is unclear and must be clarified.  
- Detailed variational update equations (Eqs.11–14) could be moved to supplementary material to improve paper flow.  
- Figure 4’s caption is unreadable and needs revision for clarity.  
- Writing uses excessive passive voice; switching to active voice would improve readability.  
- Paper contains numerous typos and grammatical errors; a thorough proofread is necessary.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel combination of PF and deep learning for sequential counts, promising experimental results, and a comprehensive variational inference attempt.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear formulation and presentation, incomplete/weak empirical evaluation, unconventional unjustified RNN implementation, and poor writing quality.  
- Overall recommendation: Reject in current form; substantive revisions required, but reviewer is open to reconsidering a revised submission.
