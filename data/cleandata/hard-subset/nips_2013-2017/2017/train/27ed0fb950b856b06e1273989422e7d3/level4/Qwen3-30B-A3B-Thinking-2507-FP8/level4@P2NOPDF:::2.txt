

The authors introduce a novel approach for modeling long-term dependencies in count data sequences by leveraging recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to parameterize implicit distributions via gamma distributions. Specifically, RNNs approximate the shape parameters of these gamma distributions, which encode temporal dependencies, by integrating information from prior (higher-layer) and likelihood (lower-layer) components. The model updates neural network parameters using expectations of latent variables, reducing sampling variance and enhancing computational efficiency. This provides a promising solution to the long-dependency challenge in dynamic count models. While the core idea is compelling, the manuscript requires significant refinement for clarity and rigor. Below are specific concerns:

1.  **Typos and Technical Errors:** Equation (10) contains critical typographical errors that obscure the methodological description. These must be corrected to ensure reproducibility and precision.

2.  **Fixed Scale Parameter Justification:** The manuscript fixes the scale parameter of the gamma distributions while only updating the shape parameters. This critical design choice requires thorough theoretical justification and analysis. The authors should discuss *why* fixing the scale is appropriate (e.g., identifiability, computational constraints) and explore potential consequences of relaxing this assumption.

3.  **Insufficient Experimental Analysis:** The experiments only evaluate the two-layer model, omitting comparisons with single-layer and deeper architectures. This omission prevents assessment of how layer depth influences performance. Additionally, key hyperparameters like window size and the number of latent factors lack systematic analysis. The authors should include ablation studies varying these parameters to demonstrate their impact on model efficacy.

4.  **Model Visualization:** A detailed graphical illustration of the complete model architecture is essential for reader comprehension. The current description is abstract; a figure analogous to Figure 1 in Chung et al. (NIPS 2015) would clarify the flow of information between layers, the RNN’s role in parameterizing the gamma distributions, and the hierarchical structure.

5.  **Literature Context:** The introduction should contextualize the work within recent advances in deep probabilistic models for count data. Specifically, the manuscript overlooks key related contributions: Zhou et al. (JMLR 2016) on Augmentable Gamma Belief Networks and Cong et al. (ICML 2017) on Deep Latent Dirichlet Allocation with adaptive MCMC. A comparative discussion of how this work differs from and advances beyond these methods is necessary to establish novelty.

The proposed method addresses a significant challenge in dynamic count modeling with a technically sound core idea. However, the current manuscript’s lack of depth in justifying key design choices (fixed scale, layer depth), insufficient experimental validation, and inadequate visualization hinder its clarity and impact. Addressing these points—particularly the justification for the fixed scale parameter, the inclusion of ablation studies on model depth and hyperparameters, and the addition of a clear model diagram—would substantially strengthen the contribution. The references to [2] and [3] should also be accurately positioned within the literature on deep gamma-based models, not Poisson factor analysis. Overall, the work has potential but requires substantial revision to meet NIPS standards for technical rigor and clarity.
