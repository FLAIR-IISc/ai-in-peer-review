

This paper addresses the incorporation of long-range temporal dependencies into a Poisson factorization model with deep structure, proposing a recurrent neural network-like architecture for modeling such dependencies. Inference employs variational methods with specialized optimization for neural network parameters. While the technical content is strong, the paper contains grammatical errors and factual inaccuracies that impede clarity. Key issues are detailed below:

1.  **Incorrect distribution statement (lines 86-87):** The claim that the model "models nonnegative numbers" is inaccurate. The Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is specifically used for this purpose.

2.  **Table presentation (Table 2):** The best results should be bolded for improved readability and impact.

3.  **Evaluation metric (Section 4.2):** Held-out perplexity would have provided a more meaningful evaluation metric than MSE/PMSE for the sequential prediction task, though the latter are acceptable.

4.  **Hierarchy justification (Section 4.1):** The paper effectively addresses limitations in prior work (refs 1, 2) regarding long-range dependencies. However, the proposed multi-level hierarchy requires stronger validation. While the LSTM comparison is useful, the differing learning algorithms (variational inference vs. backpropagation) mean this comparison does not adequately demonstrate the necessity of multiple hierarchical levels. A comparison with a single-level dynamic PF architecture would better illustrate the utility of the proposed hierarchical structure.

5.  **Grammatical errors and typos:** Numerous errors throughout the text significantly impair readability. Examples include:
    *   "unit the data" (should be "unitize the data" or "unite the data" depending on context)
    *   "Noticing the lack of capturing..." (awkward phrasing, likely "Noticing the lack of capture...")
    *   "PFA factorized a count matrix" (tense inconsistency: "factorizes" or "factorized" should match surrounding context)
    *   "other data with different pattern long-time dependence..." (missing words: "with different patterns of long-term dependence")
    *   "Examples for this including..." (should be "Examples include...")
    *   "shows excellent results on predict and..." (should be "shows excellent results in prediction and...")
    *   "However, it is rough for them..." (incorrect idiom: "However, it is difficult for them...")
    *   "V-dimesnion sequentially..." (typo: "V-dimensional sequentially...")
    *   "K-dimension latent variable..." (typo: "K-dimensional latent variable...")
    *   "due to the including of feature-wise" (incorrect phrasing: "due to the inclusion of feature-wise...")
    *   "recommend systems" (typo: "recommendation systems")

The core technical contribution is promising, but the grammatical and factual issues detract from the paper's clarity and professionalism. Addressing these errors is essential for publication. The proposed method shows potential, but the current presentation requires significant refinement.
