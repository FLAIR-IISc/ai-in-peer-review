This paper explores the incorporation of long-range temporal dependence in a Poisson factorization model with a deep structure. Unlike previous works on dynamic Poisson factorization, this paper utilizes a recurrent neural network-like structure to model long-term dependencies. The inference is carried out using variational inference with optimization routines to find the neural network parameters. The paper presents good technical content but is marred by grammatical and factual errors.

Several errors and suggestions for improvement are noted below:

1. The statement on lines 86-87 regarding the modeling of nonnegative numbers is incorrect. The Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is actually used for modeling nonnegative numbers.

2. It would be beneficial to highlight the best results in bold in Table 2 for clarity.

3. Held-out perplexity should have been a stronger indicator in the experiments in Section 4.2, although MSE/PMSE are acceptable.

4. The limitations of existing works in modeling long-range dependencies (references 1 and 2) are adequately addressed in this paper. However, to demonstrate the utility of the proposed method with multiple levels of hierarchy, a comparison with dynamic Poisson factorization using a one-level deep architecture would have been useful. The comparison with LSTM is useful, but the learning algorithms are different, making this comparison less convincing in justifying the need for multiple levels of hierarchy in the proposed method.

5. Several grammatical errors and typos detract from the paper's readability, including:
- "unit the data" 
- "Noticing the lack of capturing .."
- "PFA factorized a count matrix" (the sentence tense is inconsistent with the surrounding paragraph)
- "other data with different pattern long-time dependence .."
- "Examples for this including .."
- "shows excellent results on predict and .."
- "However, it is rough for them .."
- "V-dimension sequentially .."
- "K-dimension latent variable .."
- "due to the including of feature-wise"
- "recommend systems"

Addressing these errors and suggestions would improve the overall quality and clarity of the paper.
