- Proposes using RNNs to model gamma shape parameters for count-vector data to capture complex long-term dependencies.  
- Shape parameters are approximated by neural nets that integrate prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) information.  
- Parameters are updated via expectations of latent variables, reducing sampling variance and improving computational efficiency.  
- Core idea is promising but manuscript needs refinement for clarity and completeness.  
- Typos/notation issue: Equation (10) contains critical typographical errors that must be corrected.  
- Fixed scale parameter: authors fix the gamma scale while updating only the shape—need detailed justification and discussion of effects on flexibility/performance.  
- Layer-architecture evaluation is incomplete: experiments only use two layers, lacking single-layer and deeper comparisons and discussion of hyperparameters (window size, factor dimensionality).  
- Model visualization requested: include a detailed graphical illustration of the full architecture (e.g., similar to Chung et al. Fig. 1).  
- Related work missing: introduction should discuss recent deep Poisson factor analysis literature, including Zhou et al. (2016) and Cong et al. (2017).
