

This paper introduces a deep dynamic Poisson factorization (DDPF) model that extends Poisson factorization (PF) to capture temporal dependencies. Unlike prior dynamic PF approaches, the authors employ a simplified recurrent neural network (RNN) structure to model long-term dependencies, with inference performed via variational inference augmented by optimization over neural network parameters. The method is evaluated on five real-world datasets. While the core idea is promising, the paper requires significant revisions to address technical and presentation flaws before it can be considered for NIPS.  

**Technical concerns:**  
1. **Eq. 4 lacks standard RNN dynamics**: The formulation for $h_t^{(n)}$ depends solely on top-layer memory vectors and ignores temporal dependencies within the same layer (e.g., no recurrence across $t$). This deviates from typical RNN structure. The authors should justify this simplification or clarify its relationship to standard RNNs.  
2. **Missing model specifications**: Section 2 omits critical details. For instance, Eq. 4 does not specify how the Gamma distribution for $\lambda_{tk}$ is parameterized, and Eq. 3 lacks the generative distribution for $\theta_t$. These omissions hinder reproducibility.  
3. **Mischaracterization of "implicit distributions"**: The discussion in lines 110–118 incorrectly labels Eqs. 9–10 as "implicit." Both are explicit Gamma distributions with closed-form conditional densities (e.g., $\theta_{tk} \mid h^{(0)} \sim \text{Gamma}(\alpha_{tk}, \beta_{tk})$). The same applies to $h$ in Eq. 10.  
4. **Unclear technical claims**: The phrase "weak points in analyzing other data with different pattern long-time dependence, such as fanatical data and disaster data" (lines 30–31) is ambiguous. Please clarify the intended meaning (e.g., "financial data" or "factual data" is likely intended).  
5. **Ambiguous loss function**: The "loss function" referenced in lines 133–155 (Eq. 15) is not defined. Is this the ELBO, a prediction loss, or something else? The paper must explicitly state this.  
6. **Inadequate evaluation metrics**: MSE/PMSE is inappropriate for PF-like models. The authors must report predictive log-likelihood alongside MSE to properly assess model performance.  
7. **Insufficient latent dimension**: The choice of $K=10$ (line 207) is unrealistically small for real-world datasets. The authors should test larger values (e.g., $K=100$) to demonstrate scalability.  
8. **Figure interpretation**: The discussion in lines 224–232 does not clearly connect to Fig. 3. The relationship between the text and the figure must be explicitly explained.  

**Presentation and writing issues:**  
- The update equations for variational inference (Eqs. 11–14) can be safely moved to the supplement.  
- Fig. 4’s caption is illegible and requires revision.  
- The abstract and other sections overuse passive voice (e.g., "it is shown that"). The paper should adopt active voice for clarity.  
- Writing quality is poor, with numerous grammatical errors and typos:  
  • "high-dimensional" (line 15, missing hyphen)  
  • "factorize" (line 22, should be "factorization")  
  • "property of the" (line 24, redundant)  
  • "Dirichlet" misspelled as "Dirichlet" (lines 29, 34)  
  • "nested" (line 34, unclear context)  
  • "visual representation" (Fig. 1, redundant)  
  • "transmission" (Fig. 1, likely typo for "transition")  
  • Incomplete sentences (e.g., line 49)  
  • Missing articles (e.g., "Although Dirichlet distribution is often used as *a* prior distribution").  

**Recommendation:**  
The paper presents an interesting extension to dynamic PF but currently fails to meet NIPS’s standards due to unresolved technical gaps, unclear exposition, and poor writing. The authors must address all points above, particularly the RNN structure justification, model specifications, metric choice, and writing errors. Without these revisions, the paper is not suitable for acceptance.
