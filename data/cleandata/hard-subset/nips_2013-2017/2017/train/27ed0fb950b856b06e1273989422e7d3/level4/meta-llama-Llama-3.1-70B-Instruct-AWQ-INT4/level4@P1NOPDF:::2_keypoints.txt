1. Proposes using RNNs to parameterize implicit gamma distributions for count vectors to capture complex long-time dependencies.  
2. Shape parameters (time-dependent) are approximated by neural nets that combine prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) information.  
3. Updates to network parameters use expectations of latent variables to reduce sampling variance and improve computational efficiency.  
4. Method is interesting and promising for handling long dependencies, but the manuscript appears hastily written.  
5. Critical typos exist, notably an error in Equation 10.  
6. Treating the gamma scale parameter as fixed while updating only the shape requires more justification and analysis.  
7. Experimental evaluation is limited: only two-layer results are shown, with no comparisons to single-layer or deeper models, and other hyperparameters (window size, number of factors) are not explored.  
8. The paper needs detailed graphical illustrations of the full model (e.g., similar to Figure 1 in Chung et al. 2015) for clarity.  
9. Recent related work on deep Poisson factor analysis (Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) should be discussed in the Introduction for proper context.
