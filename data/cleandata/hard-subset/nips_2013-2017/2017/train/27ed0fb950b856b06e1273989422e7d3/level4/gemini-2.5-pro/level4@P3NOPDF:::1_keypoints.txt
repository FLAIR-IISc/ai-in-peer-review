- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPF) with a simplified RNN to model temporal dependencies and a variational inference algorithm with an extra optimization step for NN parameters; evaluated on five real datasets.

- Model formulation is incomplete and confusing; key components and motivations are not clearly specified.

- RNN architecture deviates from standard design (hidden state depends on layer above, not previous time step); authors must justify this simplification and discuss implications.

- Generative process omissions: Gamma distribution parameterization in Eq.4 and the prior for topic proportions theta_t (Eq.3) are not defined, harming reproducibility.

- Incorrect use of “implicit distributions”: Eqs.9–10 define explicit Gamma conditionals, so the implicit-distribution claim is misleading.

- Unclear “loss function”: Eq.15 and the role of the RNN parameter optimization within the variational/ELBO framework are not properly explained.

- Evaluation metric choice (MSE/PMSE) is inappropriate for count/Poisson models; should report predictive log-likelihood or perplexity on held-out data.

- Model capacity likely too small (K=20); authors should perform sensitivity analyses with larger K (e.g., 50, 100, 200).

- Results presentation is unclear; the link between textual claims and Figure 3’s visualization is not explained.

- Writing quality is poor with many typos, grammatical errors, and vague sentences that impede comprehension.

- Presentation issues: standard variational updates should be moved to supplementary material, Figure 4 caption is illegible, passive voice is overused, and wording in Figure 1/caption is inappropriate.
