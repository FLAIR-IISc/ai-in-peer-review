This paper proposes a deep Poisson factorization model that incorporates long-range temporal dependencies. In contrast to previous work on dynamic Poisson factorization, the proposed method employs a recurrent neural network-like structure to model these dependencies. Inference is performed using variational methods, with dedicated optimization routines for the neural network parameters. The paper presents a strong technical contribution, but its quality is diminished by several grammatical issues and some factual or experimental weaknesses.

**Major Comments:**

1.  The experimental evaluation could be significantly strengthened to better justify the proposed model's deep architecture. While the paper adequately addresses the limitations of existing works [1, 2] in modeling long-range dependencies, a crucial baseline is missing. A comparison against a dynamic Poisson factorization model with a single-level deep architecture would serve as a direct ablation study, clearly demonstrating the utility of the proposed multi-level hierarchy. The current comparison with an LSTM is useful, but because the learning algorithms differ, it does not provide a direct justification for the hierarchical structure.

2.  For the experiments in Section 4.2, held-out perplexity would be a more standard and arguably stronger performance indicator for this class of models than the reported Mean Squared Error (MSE) and Poisson Mean Squared Error (PMSE).

3.  There appears to be a factual error on lines 86-87 concerning the modeling of non-negative numbers. The text should be corrected to state that the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution is used for this purpose.
