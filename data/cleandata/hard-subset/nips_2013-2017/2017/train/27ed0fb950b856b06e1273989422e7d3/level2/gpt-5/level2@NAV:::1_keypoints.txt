- Proposal: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) augments Poisson factorization with a multi-layer dynamic prior over factor scores using RNNs to parameterize Gamma conditionals.  
- Inference: Uses mean-field variational updates for conjugate parts via latent count augmentation plus an auxiliary "Probability AutoEncoder" objective to train RNN parameters.  
- Evaluation: Tested on four synthetic and five real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, Ebola, International Disasters, Annual Sheep Population) against PGDS, PFA, and LSTM, reporting improved fit, prediction, and interpretable factors.  
- Strength (quality): Probabilistic core (Poisson–Gamma conjugacy with auxiliary counts) is sound and tackles richer temporal structure than Markovian PGDS.  
- Main methodological concern: The neural training objective (Eqs.15–18) departs from a principled ELBO—replacing intractable terms with squared-error penalties risks biased inference and unclear convergence guarantees.  
- Model specification issue: Generative process for the deep hierarchy is not fully specified (exact RNN→Gamma parameterizations, identifiability with λ, ψ, Φ unclear).  
- Derivation gap: Mean-field updates assume conjugacy under RNN-driven parameters but lack explicit derivations.  
- Reproducibility: Experimental details are insufficient (network architectures, optimizer settings beyond epochs, early stopping, preprocessing) and no code/hyperparameters provided.  
- Uncertainty reporting: No statistical significance tests, uncertainty estimates, or calibration/runtime analyses reported.  
- Clarity problems: Readable overall but suffers from grammatical errors, imprecise/inconsistent notation, confusing use of “implicit distribution,” and poorly described figures/tables.  
- Originality: Combining deep hierarchical temporal priors with Poisson factorization is interesting and distinct from related work; ladder-style regularization is a useful idea.  
- Significance: Approach could be valuable for topic/event/recommendation modeling if made principled and reproducible; current empirical evidence is promising but not conclusive.  
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses long-range dependence in count time series, conceptually appealing deep dynamic prior with interpretable factors, and broad relevant applications with competitive results.  
- Cons for acceptance: Lack of principled ELBO/theoretical guarantees, incomplete specifications/derivations, reproducibility concerns, limited baselines, no significance tests, and weak ablations.  
- Suggestions: Provide a coherent ELBO or IWAE-style objective with amortized inference and clarified parameterizations to ensure claimed conjugacy.  
- Suggestions: Expand baselines (other dynamic/recurrent PFs), report statistical significance, calibration, and runtime comparisons.  
- Suggestions: Release code, full hyperparameters, architectures, and training schedules; detail preprocessing and early stopping.  
- Suggestions: Analyze identifiability and roles of λ and ψ; perform stronger ablations on depth, window size, and ladder connections.  
- Suggestions: Improve writing, correct/improve notation, and complete references; better describe figures and ablation details.
