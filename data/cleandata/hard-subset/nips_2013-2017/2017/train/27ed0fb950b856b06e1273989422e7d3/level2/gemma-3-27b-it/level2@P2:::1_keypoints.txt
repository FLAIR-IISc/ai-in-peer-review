- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) combining Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs to model sequential count vectors.
- Method: Uses variational inference for parameter estimation and integrates short- and long-term temporal dependencies.
- Experiments: Evaluated on synthetic and real datasets, compared against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA, with factor visualizations.
- Foundation & Motivation: Builds on Bayesian factor analysis and deep sequential models with clear motivation and appropriate citations.
- Novelty: Novel integration of PFA with deep recurrent networks for sequential count data.
- Technical Soundness: Model formulation and use of variational inference are reasonable and mathematically grounded.
- Experimental Validation Strength: Results and visualizations demonstrate the model’s potential and provide useful benchmarks.
- Clear Problem Statement: Introduction clearly frames the need to capture complex temporal dependencies in count data.
- Variational Inference Detail Lacking: Section 3 is high-level; derivations, variational distributions, and optimization steps need more explanation.
- Hyperparameter Sensitivity Missing: No thorough analysis of how hyperparameter choices affect performance.
- Scalability Discussion Limited: Insufficient discussion of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.
- ICEWS Performance Concern: DDPFA underperforms PGDS on ICEWS predictions; causes and remedies are not investigated.
- Notation Clarity: Dense notation with many subscripts/superscripts makes equations hard to follow.
- Acceptance Arguments: Novel, promising results and solid technical grounding support acceptance.
- Rejection Concerns: Insufficient VI details, hyperparameter analysis, scalability discussion, and ICEWS result issues argue against acceptance.
- Overall Recommendation: Solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address VI details, hyperparameter sensitivity, and ICEWS analysis.
