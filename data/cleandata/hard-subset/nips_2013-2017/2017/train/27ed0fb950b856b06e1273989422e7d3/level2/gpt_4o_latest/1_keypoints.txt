- Strength — Novelty: Integrates Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs and a deep hierarchical structure to model short- and long-term dependencies.
- Strength — Technical soundness: Bayesian formulation with appropriate variational inference; clear derivation of loss and NN integration.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real-world data show DDPFA outperforms baselines in most cases.
- Strength — Interpretability: Learned factors are interpretable and capture long-term trends (e.g., ICEWS).
- Strength — Clarity: Well-organized paper with clear explanations, figures, and tables.

- Weakness — Limited discussion of limitations: Brief treatment of pruning and limited utility of extra layers.
- Weakness — Scalability concerns: Computational complexity with increasing layers/dimensions is not thoroughly addressed.
- Weakness — Performance on ICEWS: Underperforms vs. PGDS with insufficient analysis of causes or remedies.
- Weakness — Reproducibility: Sparse practical implementation details (hyperparameter tuning, initialization) that may hinder replication.

- Suggestion — Analyze pruning: Provide deeper analysis of pruning and explore alternative inference methods to mitigate it.
- Suggestion — Address scalability: Discuss computational efficiency and propose strategies for scaling to larger datasets.
- Suggestion — Investigate ICEWS failure modes: Study why DDPFA struggles on ICEWS and consider incorporating PGDS elements (e.g., transition matrices).
- Suggestion — Improve reproducibility: Include implementation details or release code.

- Recommendation — Overall: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the identified concerns.
