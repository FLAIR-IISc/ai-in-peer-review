- Proposal: DDPFA extends Poisson factor analysis with deep hierarchical Gamma latents whose conditional parameters are produced by RNNs, using Poisson likelihood with per-feature (ψ) and per-factor (λ) scales and sliding-window time dependence; inference is two-stage: mean-field Gamma–Poisson updates and a “Probability AutoEncoder” squared-loss training for RNNs.

- Related work: builds on dynamic Poisson/Gamma factor models (e.g., PGDS), deep count models (deep exponential families, gamma belief networks), and neural autoregressive/recurrent topic/count models.

Strengths
- Ambitious integration of conjugate Poisson–Gamma factor models with deep recurrent priors to capture long-range temporal dependencies.
- Uses explicit per-feature and per-factor scaling (ψ, λ) to promote sparsity and control factor importance.
- Empirical gains over shallow PFA and PGDS on several datasets and some interpretable factors (e.g., ICEWS).
- Authors acknowledge training pathologies (pruning, limited benefit from depth) and propose a ladder-style fix.

Major weaknesses
- Inference objective is not principled: the PAE squared-loss surrogate is ad hoc, not shown to be an ELBO or to improve the variational objective; alternating scheme lacks a coherent global objective and convergence guarantees.
- Model specification is ambiguous and inconsistent: unclear which Gamma parameters are RNN outputs, how rates/γ are handled, and several equations contain duplicated/missing terms and mismatched indices, impeding reproducibility and conjugacy claims.
- Evaluation uses inappropriate metrics (MSE/PMSE) for count data; should report predictive log-likelihood/perplexity and calibration instead.
- Baselines and fairness issues: LSTM is not a calibrated probabilistic baseline for sparse counts; missing relevant baselines (dynamic topic models, recurrent Poisson factorization, neural autoregressive count models) and hyperparameter tuning appears uneven.
- Limited scalability and ablation analyses: experiments use small K (≤10), no runtime/memory reporting, and insufficient ablations on Λ, Ψ, window size c, and depth N.
- Presentation problems: numerous grammatical errors, undefined notation, incomplete/malformed citations, and incomplete figures/tables.

Quality
- Modeling idea is interesting but undermined by weak theoretical justification for inference, missing ELBO/gradient derivations, and erroneous equations; empirical evaluation is suggestive but not rigorous.

Clarity
- Paper is difficult to follow; needs formal specification of temporal/layer conditioning, exact RNN-to-Gamma parameter mappings, and precise definition of the mean-field family; figures/tables/references must be fixed.

Originality
- Combining deep hierarchical Gamma priors with RNN-parameterized dependencies is novel, but the paper must better position itself relative to recurrent/dynamic Poisson factor and topic models.

Significance
- Potentially impactful if made principled and scalable; current lack of sound inference and rigorous evaluation limits contribution.

Key suggestions
- Derive a coherent ELBO for the full model (including RNN-parameterized priors) and optimize it, or prove surrogate losses bound/improve the ELBO.
- Fully specify and correct model equations: p(θt|h(0)t−c:t), p(h(n)t|·), which Gamma parameters RNNs output, and fix notation errors.
- Evaluate using predictive log-likelihood/perplexity and calibration metrics; compare to appropriate baselines per dataset.
- Provide full architecture/optimization details, code, runtime/sensitivity analyses, and comprehensive ablations on Λ, Ψ, c, K, and depth.
- Enhance qualitative results by showing top features per factor over time and reporting inferred Λ/Ψ effects.

Minor points
- Fix typos and grammar; state RNN type (LSTM/GRU/vanilla) and whether window conditioning uses future context; clarify whether future info is used at training vs. prediction.

Pros for acceptance
- Addresses an important problem (dynamic modeling of sparse counts) with a conceptually interesting model and some empirical/interpretability strengths.

Cons for acceptance
- Unprincipled inference, ambiguous model specification, inadequate evaluation/baselines, and reproducibility issues.

Overall recommendation
- Lean toward rejection in current form, but promising direction if revised with principled ELBO-based inference, clearer modeling, stronger evaluation, and better presentation.
