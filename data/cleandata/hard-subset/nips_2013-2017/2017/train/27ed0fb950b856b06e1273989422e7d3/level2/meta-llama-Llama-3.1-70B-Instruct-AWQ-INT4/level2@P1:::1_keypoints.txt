- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) combining Poisson Factor Analysis with recurrent neural networks for sequential count vectors.
- Demonstrates DDPFA outperforms PGDS, LSTM, and PFA on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a thorough introduction and background.
- Provides a clear explanation of model architecture and inference algorithm.
- Technical soundness is strong with a solid theoretical foundation and well-designed experiments.
- Includes thorough result analysis, comparisons with existing methods, and interpretability evaluation.
- Strength: effectively captures complex temporal dependencies and long-term patterns via RNNs.
- Novelty: applying recurrent neural networks within PFA for sequential count data is an original contribution.
- Significance: high relevance and applicability across a range of real-world datasets.
- Limitation: computational cost can be high due to RNN components.
- Limitation: model may suffer from "pruning" and layer “uselessness” affecting performance.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — address computational costs, pruning issues, and provide more experimental/hyperparameter details.
