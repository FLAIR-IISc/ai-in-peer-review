This paper proposes a new model, Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), for analyzing sequential count vectors. The model is based on Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) and includes recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, capturing complicated relationships between different factors over short time periods. The deep structure of the model is designed to capture long-time dependence. The authors use variational inference to infer latent variables and gradient descent to learn the parameters of the neural networks.

The main idea of the paper is to extend PFA by incorporating a deep and dynamic structure to capture complex time dependence. The authors use a hierarchical model with multiple layers, where each layer represents a different level of abstraction. The model is trained using a combination of mean-field variational inference and gradient descent.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their model and the underlying mathematics. The experimental results show that the proposed model outperforms existing methods, including PGDS, LSTM, and PFA, on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
