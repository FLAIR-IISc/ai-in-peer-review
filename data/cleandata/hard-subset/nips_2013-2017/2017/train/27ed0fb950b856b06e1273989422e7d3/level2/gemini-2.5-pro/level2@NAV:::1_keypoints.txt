- Proposes DDPFA: extends Poisson Factor Analysis with a deep hierarchical model for sequential count data.  
- Main contribution: uses RNNs to parameterize implicit conditional distributions of latent factors to capture flexible, non-linear temporal dynamics.  
- Improves over PGDS by moving beyond conjugate linear transitions to model complex temporal dependencies.  
- Strength: well-motivated, novel combination of a classic probabilistic model and deep learning.  
- Strength: RNN transitions elegantly relax Markovian and linear assumptions of prior models.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets showing DDPFA generally outperforms baselines (PGDS, LSTM) in fitting and prediction.  
- Strength: authors candidly discuss limitations (e.g., pruning effect, diminishing returns from deeper layers).  
- Weakness: inference procedure is unclear and lacks rigorous justification, shifting away from ELBO/variational inference to a seemingly ad-hoc deterministic loss.  
- Weakness: the proposed "Probability AutoEncoder" and squared-error loss are not clearly derived from the probabilistic objective, undermining technical soundness.  
- Weakness: key RNN architectural details are omitted, hindering reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on substantial revision of the inference section to provide a principled derivation and clear link to the variational framework.
