Proposes DDPFA: integrates Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs to model sequential count vectors using variational inference.

Well-motivated extension addressing limitations of PGDS and temporal Dirichlet processes.

RNNs used to capture short- and long-term temporal dependencies; model formulation appears sound.

Evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets with competitive performance against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA baselines.

Factor activation patterns (Figure 2) offer meaningful interpretability insights.

Authors honestly acknowledge limitations like “pruning” and MF-VI inadequacies.

Insufficient comparison to prior deep Poisson factor analysis work ([9,10]); novelty vs. prior art is unclear.

Variational inference description is high-level; key equations lack derivation and implementation details.

Experimental setup lacks clarity: hyperparameter choices are given without rationale or selection procedure.

Choice of number of factors K for real datasets is unjustified and appears ad hoc.

Limited analysis of learned latent variables beyond activation patterns; deeper interpretability needed.

Reliance on MF-VI is concerning and alternatives (e.g., auxiliary MCMC) are only suggested, not explored.

Claim that more layers/variables don’t improve performance is not thoroughly investigated; systematic hyperparameter search missing.

Overall: promising and potentially valuable contribution but requires major revision to address related work, inference details, experimental clarity, and alternative inference methods.
