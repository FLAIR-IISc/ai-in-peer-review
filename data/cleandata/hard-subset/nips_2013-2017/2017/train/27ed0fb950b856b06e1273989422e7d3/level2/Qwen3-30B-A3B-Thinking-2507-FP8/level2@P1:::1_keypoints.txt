- Proposes DDPFA: combines Poisson Factor Analysis with deep neural networks and RNNs to model sequential count vectors and capture short- and long-term dependencies.  
- Builds on PGDS and Deep PFA but introduces a novel deep latent structure with recurrent modeling across time.  
- Technically sound contribution with a well-motivated two-stage inference (mean-field variational inference then neural network parameter learning).  
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic (SDS1–4) and five real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, ASP) using appropriate baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA).  
- Empirical results show DDPFA outperforms baselines in most cases and better captures long-term patterns (notably ICEWS and ASP where PGDS fails).  
- Latent factors are interpretable; figures show meaningful temporal patterns missed by PGDS.  
- Insufficient comparison and clear differentiation from closely related work, especially the referenced Deep PFA with recurrent structure [12].  
- The “deep” claim is misleading: model uses only two layers (N=2) and Table 3 shows diminishing returns from additional layers.  
- Fails to address the pruning issue where some latent dimensions contain only noise, which may hurt practical utility.  
- Paper is generally well written and organized with clear math, but some network descriptions (Section 3, eqs. 3–4) lack intuitive explanation.  
- Need clearer justification for why a deep structure is required for long-term dependencies versus a single recurrent layer.  
- LSTM experimental setup (hidden size 40) is not justified and may be too small for fair comparison.  
- Combining deep learning with PFA is a novel idea, but the practical depth and gains are limited in the current evaluation.  
- Problem is significant for recommendation, text, and event prediction; DDPFA addresses a gap in modeling long-term dependencies for count data.  
- Significance is tempered by limited real-world datasets and occasions where gains are not substantial (e.g., ICEWS prediction).  
- Suggested improvements: clarify distinction from [12] and justify depth; analyze and mitigate pruning; add ablation comparing deep vs single-layer recurrent models.  
- Additional suggestions: justify and test larger LSTM hidden sizes for fair baseline comparison and discuss computational complexity versus baselines.  
- Overall judgment: technically sound and promising contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
