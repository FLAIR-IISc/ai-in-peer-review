- Proposal: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) combining Poisson Factor Analysis and recurrent neural networks, with a variational inference algorithm.  
- Claim: DDPFA captures long-term and complex temporal dependencies in count time series.  
- Claim: DDPFA outperforms PGDS, LSTM, and PFA on fitting and prediction tasks.  
- Evidence: Experiments on 4 synthetic and 5 real-world datasets show better performance in most cases, notably for complex temporal dependencies.  
- Strength: Novel combination of PFA and neural networks to model complex temporal dynamics.  
- Strength: Development of a variational inference algorithm to estimate latent variables and neural network parameters.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation on both synthetic and real datasets.  
- Weakness: Underperforms on the ICEWS dataset, possibly due to smoothing from PGDS transition matrix.  
- Weakness: Faces “pruning” issue and adding network layers is often ineffective; authors propose a ladder-like architecture fix.  
- Weakness: Reliance on mean-field variational inference may be suboptimal; alternative inference methods suggested.  
- Overall: Promising model with solid evaluation but notable limitations that require future work.  
- Quality: 8/10 — well-written, minor writing and implementation detail issues.  
- Clarity: 9/10 — well-organized, but some technical details may challenge non-experts.  
- Originality: 8/10 — new combination of PFA and neural nets, though temporal NN use is not entirely novel.  
- Significance: 8/10 — applicable to recommendation systems, text analysis, network analysis, etc.  
- Acceptance arguments: effectively captures long-term dependencies; outperforms baselines; thorough evaluation and discussion of limitations.  
- Rejection arguments: poor ICEWS performance; pruning and ineffective layer additions; mean-field VI may be inadequate.
