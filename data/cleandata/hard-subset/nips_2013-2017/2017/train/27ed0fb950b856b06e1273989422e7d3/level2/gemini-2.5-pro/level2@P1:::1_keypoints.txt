- Proposes DDPFA: a deep hierarchical Poisson factorization with RNN-parameterized temporal dynamics for count data.
- Main contribution: models factor scores Θ with multiple latent layers and RNNs to capture complex, long-range temporal patterns.
- Inference uses a hybrid scheme alternating mean-field variational updates for latent variables and gradient-based updates for RNNs with a custom loss.
- Empirically outperforms PFA, PGDS, and LSTM on several synthetic and real-world datasets and shows interpretable learned factors.
- Strength: novel integration of deep RNNs into probabilistic dynamic count modeling, addressing PGDS’s first-order Markov limitation.
- Strength: elegant hierarchical generative model where latent h layers inform θ across time scales, combining Bayesian structure with RNN expressivity.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation and qualitative analysis supporting claims.
- Weakness: loss in Equations 16–18 appears ad-hoc and not derived from a principled objective like the ELBO, lacking theoretical justification.
- Weakness: “Probability AutoEncoder” and backward network fback are confusing; notation conflates posterior and generative distributions and needs standard q-variational notation.
- Weakness: layer-wise training plus fine-tuning reads as a heuristic engineering solution rather than a coherent Bayesian inference algorithm.
- Weakness: incomplete analysis of limitations (latent collapse/pruning, limited benefit of deeper layers) and unsupported claim about a ladder architecture.
- Minor clarity: role of window size c and how RNNs process the c+1 vectors from the layer above is underspecified.
- Minor clarity: top-layer inference update depends on future values (non-causal); needs explicit distinction that this is part of the inference network.
- Summary: promising and valuable core idea with strong empirical results, but undermined by a confusing, theoretically weak inference procedure—reframing within a VAE/ELBO framework is recommended.
