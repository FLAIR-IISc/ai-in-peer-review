- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) using RNN-parameterized, time-dependent Gamma priors over Poisson factor scores.  
- Inference: Alternates mean-field variational updates for Gamma latent variables with neural-network training using a squared-error “probability autoencoder” objective.  
- Experiments: Evaluated on synthetic and five real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, Ebola, disasters, sheep); reported better performance than PGDS, PFA, and LSTM in most settings.  
- Strength: Tackles an important temporal count-data problem where both local and long-range dependencies matter.  
- Strength: Promising integration of Poisson factorization with deep temporal structure via RNN-parameterized Gamma conditionals.  
- Strength: Practical coordinate-ascent scheme and broad empirical coverage including one-step prediction and discussion of interpretability.  
- Weakness: Training objective is ad hoc, not shown to be an ELBO or principled surrogate, and uses squared error mismatched to Poisson likelihood.  
- Weakness: Several key update equations (e.g., additive Gamma shape updates) are stated without derivation or justification from a joint model.  
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics (MSE/PMSE) are inappropriate for count models; likelihood-based metrics (predictive log-likelihood, deviance, perplexity) are missing.  
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons and hyperparameter tuning are unclear or insufficient; runtime/memory comparisons are absent.  
- Weakness: Missing experimental and implementation details (RNN type, hidden sizes, activations to ensure positivity, optimization, initialization), harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: References and connections to amortized VI and auxiliary-variable methods are incomplete or missing.  
- Clarity issue: Notation, definitions (e.g., “implicit distribution,” α vs. γ), and the training schedule/alternation scheme need clearer exposition.  
- Originality: Combination of ideas is interesting but related to existing work (deep exponential families, deep Poisson factor analysis, recurrent PF, ladder/auxiliary VAEs); main novelty is limited by ad hoc training.  
- Significance: Empirical gains are promising but undermined by lack of principled objective, weak evaluation, and unclear baselines.  
- Suggestion: Provide a clear joint generative model and derive an ELBO; consider auxiliary-variable or reparameterization techniques for Gamma.  
- Suggestion: Replace squared-error network loss with likelihood-based objectives (negative log-Poisson/NB) and report predictive log-likelihood and deviance.  
- Suggestion: Justify or derive Eqns 10–14 or relate them to established auxiliary/ladder inference frameworks; add ablations (window size, depth, K) and stronger baselines.  
- Suggestion: Fully specify architectures/optimization, tune via validation, report runtimes, and release code for reproducibility.  
- Overall verdict: Promising direction but needs a sound variational objective, clearer derivations, better evaluation, and stronger empirical validation before publication readiness.
