- Introduces DDPFA: a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization model that uses hierarchical latent variables and RNN-parameterized conditional distributions to model sequential count data.  
- Extends Poisson Factor Analysis with deep, non-linear, long-range temporal dynamics instead of simple Markovian transitions.  
- Inference uses a two-stage alternating scheme: mean-field variational updates for latents and gradient-based learning for RNN parameters via a VAE-like loss.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets generally outperform PGDS, standard PFA, and LSTMs.  
- Technically solid model definition and compelling motivation for deep recurrent structure.  
- Experimental validation is thorough with appropriate baselines and diverse datasets; claims largely supported by tables.  
- Honest discussion of limitations (e.g., pruning/posterior collapse and diminishing returns from more layers) enhances credibility.  
- Treatment of the "Probability AutoEncoder" and derivation of the loss (Eq.16) is confusing and lacks formal justification from the stated objective.  
- Reproducibility is hindered by missing RNN architecture details (cell type, hidden sizes, activations) and some hyperparameters (e.g., Gamma prior βh).  
- The crucial "ladder-like architecture" is reported to help but is not described, leaving a gap in understanding the effective model variant.  
- Original contribution: novel combination of PFA and deep RNN-parameterized transitions, departing from conjugate-exponential dynamics.  
- Significant: addresses modeling of complex, long-range dependencies in count time series with potential broad applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated model; comprehensive empirical evaluation; transparent discussion of limitations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear loss derivation for neural components; missing architectural/hyperparameter details; unexplained role of ladder architecture.  
- Essential suggestions: formally clarify/derive the loss (Eqs.16–18) or justify the heuristic; describe the ladder-like architecture in main text or appendix; provide full experimental/RNN hyperparameter details for reproducibility.
