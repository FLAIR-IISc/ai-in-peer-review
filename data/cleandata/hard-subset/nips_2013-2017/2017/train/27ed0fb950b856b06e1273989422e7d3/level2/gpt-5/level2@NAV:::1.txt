This paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), a generative model for sequential count vectors that augments Poisson factorization with a multi-layer dynamic prior over factor scores. Temporal dependence is modeled via recurrent neural networks that parameterize (Gamma) conditional distributions across a sliding window, aiming to capture both short-range interactions and long-range dependencies through depth. Inference uses a mean-field variational scheme for conjugate parts (via latent count augmentation) and an auxiliary “Probability AutoEncoder” objective to train the RNN parameters. The paper evaluates on four synthetic datasets and five real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS corpus, Ebola, International Disasters, Annual Sheep Population), comparing against PGDS, PFA, and LSTM, reporting stronger fitting and predictive performance and interpretable factors.

Quality: The probabilistic core (Poisson–Gamma conjugacy with auxiliary counts) is sound, and the model addresses an important gap (capturing richer temporal structure than Markovian PGDS). However, the training objective for the neural components (Eqs. 15–18) departs from a principled ELBO: replacing intractable terms with squared-error penalties between expectations risks biased inference and unclear convergence guarantees. The generative process for the deep hierarchy is not fully specified (e.g., exact parameterizations of Gamma shape/rate from RNNs, identifiability with λ, ψ, Φ), and the mean-field updates assume conjugacy under RNN-driven parameters without derivations. Experimental details are insufficient for reproducibility (network architectures, optimizer settings beyond epochs, early stopping, data preprocessing), and no statistical significance or uncertainty is reported.

Clarity: The paper is readable but suffers from grammatical issues, imprecise notation (e.g., inconsistent indices and shapes), and incomplete references. The “implicit distribution” terminology is confusing given explicit Gamma forms. Figures/tables are referenced but not well described; ablations are mentioned without sufficient detail.

Originality: Combining deep hierarchical temporal priors with Poisson factorization is interesting and related to, but distinct from, PGDS, deep Poisson factorization, deep exponential families, and recurrent Poisson factorization. The proposed ladder-style regularization is a useful angle, though the training objective overlaps conceptually with VAEs without a clear variational derivation.

Significance: If made principled and reproducible, the approach could be useful for topic modeling, event counts, and recommendation with temporal dynamics. Current empirical evidence is promising but not conclusive.

Pros for acceptance:
- Addresses an important problem (long-range dependence in count time series).
- Conceptually appealing deep dynamic prior; interpretable factors shown.
- Broad and relevant applications; competitive results reported.

Cons for acceptance:
- Lack of a principled ELBO or theoretical guarantees for the “Probability AutoEncoder” objective.
- Incomplete model specification and derivations; reproducibility concerns.
- Limited and sometimes unfavorable comparisons (e.g., other dynamic PF baselines), no significance tests, and weak ablations.

Constructive suggestions:
- Provide a coherent ELBO (or IWAE-style) objective with amortized inference; clarify parameterizations and ensure conjugacy where claimed.
- Expand baselines (dynamic PF variants, recurrent PF) and report statistical significance, calibration, and runtime.
- Release code and hyperparameters; detail architectures and training schedules.
- Analyze identifiability and the roles of λ and ψ; include stronger ablations on depth, window size, and ladder connections.
- Improve writing and complete/correct references.
