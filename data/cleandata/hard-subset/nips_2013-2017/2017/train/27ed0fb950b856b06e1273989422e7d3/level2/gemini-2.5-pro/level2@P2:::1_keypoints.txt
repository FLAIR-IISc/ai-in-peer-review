- Proposal: DDPFA extends Poisson Factor Analysis with a deep, RNN-parameterized hierarchical temporal model for time-varying scores Θ.  
- Inference: Hybrid approach—mean-field variational updates for latent variables and gradient-trained neural nets with a VAE-like (L2) loss for network parameters.  
- Relation: Connects to deep generative models combining Bayesian latent variables and neural nets (e.g., Deep Exponential Families, VAEs).  

Strengths:
- Novelty: Addresses dynamic count data with a novel, well-motivated deep RNN-based temporal hierarchy beyond first-order Markov models.  
- Empirical results: Thorough experiments on multiple synthetic and real datasets show consistently better fitting and prediction than strong baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA).  
- Interpretability: Qualitative analyses indicate learned factors/topics are meaningful and persistent over time, improving interpretability vs. black-box models.

Weaknesses:
- Inference justification: The “Probability AutoEncoder” L2 loss is poorly explained and not theoretically justified as a substitute for the standard ELBO; derivation (Eq.15→16) is opaque.  
- Defense of alternatives: The claim that differentiable variational Bayes is unsuitable is weakly defended despite existing reparameterization methods for Gamma-like distributions.  
- Missing architectural details: Key model choices (RNN type, layers/units, ladder architecture) and training hyperparameters (optimizer, learning rates) are omitted, harming reproducibility.  
- Ladder architecture unspecified: The reported “ladder-like” variant that improves results is not described in the model section.  
- Minor clarity issues: Typos (e.g., “fanatical data”) and newly coined terms (Probability AutoEncoder) are not clearly related to existing literature.

Suggestions:
- Rewrite inference section: Ground the learning objective in established principles (preferably ELBO) or rigorously derive and justify the proposed L2 objective and its link to data likelihood.  
- Clarify network roles: Explicitly state which networks act as recognition/inference vs. generative/decoder (ffeed vs. fback).  
- Improve reproducibility: Provide full architectural descriptions, ladder-variant details, and training hyperparameters.  
- Address pruning: Discuss standard VAE remedies (e.g., KL annealing) for hierarchical “pruning” and explain applicability to this model.

Overall verdict:
- Promising and empirically strong model, but the theoretically unjustified and poorly explained inference procedure is a critical flaw requiring major revisions.
