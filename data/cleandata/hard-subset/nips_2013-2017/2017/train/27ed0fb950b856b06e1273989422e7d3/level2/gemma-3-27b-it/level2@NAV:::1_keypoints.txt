- Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) combining PFA with recurrent neural networks to model sequential count vectors.  
- Aims to capture both short- and long-term dependencies in high-dimensional, sparse sequential data.  
- Core idea of using deep learning for temporal evolution of latent factors is promising and a logical extension of prior work.  
- Paper clearly presents motivation, model formulation, and variational inference procedure, including a training loss for the networks.  
- Uses RNNs to represent implicit distributions and a deep structure to capture long-term dependencies.  
- Experimental evaluation includes synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS) and comparisons to PGDS and LSTM.  
- Strength: novel combination of PFA and deep learning with encouraging synthetic-data results and interpretable factor activations.  
- Weakness: explanations of the neural network components are somewhat superficial and need more depth.  
- Ablation studies reveal issues (pruning, limited benefit from added layers) that highlight limitations of the variational inference approach.  
- Discussion of those limitations is brief; paper should analyze causes and potential alternatives (e.g., MCMC) more thoroughly.  
- Performance gains over strong baselines are moderate and DDPFA’s underperformance on ICEWS is insufficiently explained.  
- Clarity could be improved with more concise writing and additional visual aids to illustrate the architecture.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify network components, deepen discussion of limitations, and explore fixes for variational inference issues.
