- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), a novel model combining Poisson Factor Analysis with recurrent neural networks for sequential count vectors.
- Model designed to capture complex temporal dependencies in sequential count data.
- Demonstrated empirical superiority over baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA) on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Paper is well-structured with clear mathematical formulation and an explained inference algorithm.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough with detailed comparisons to existing methods.
- Authors discuss limitations, notably a "pruning" problem and the idea of using ladder-like architectures.
- Strength: novel integration of PFA and RNNs for temporal count modeling.
- Strength: effective performance across synthetic and real datasets.
- Strength: comprehensive baseline comparisons.
- Strength: transparent discussion of limitations and future directions.
- Weakness: model complexity may hinder implementation and interpretability for non-experts.
- Weakness: risk of overfitting due to deep neural network components.
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning process is not clearly explained, reducing reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with broad applicability but needs clearer reproducibility and potential simplification.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, and thoughtful limitations discussion.
- Arguments against acceptance: complexity, overfitting risk, and lack of hyperparameter detail.
- Recommendation: provide detailed hyperparameter tuning procedures to improve reproducibility.
- Recommendation: investigate model simplifications that retain effectiveness.
- Recommendation: add more visualizations and examples to improve model interpretability.
