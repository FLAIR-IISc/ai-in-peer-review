- Main idea: DDPFA extends Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs and a deep hierarchical structure to capture short- and long-term dependencies in sequential count vectors.  
- Training/inference: Uses mean-field variational inference for latent variables and gradient descent to learn neural network parameters.  
- Experimental result: Outperforms PGDS, LSTM, and PFA on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Strength: Proposes a novel, interesting model for sequential counts.  
- Strength: Clear explanation and thorough mathematical exposition.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance across datasets.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detailed comparison with existing methods and their trade-offs.  
- Weakness: Limited insight into interpretability of learned factors and their relation to data.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity relative to alternatives.  
- Originality: Novel hierarchical multi-layer extension of PFA to model complex temporal dependence.  
- Significance: Addresses an important problem with broad potential applications (recommendation, text, network analysis).  
- Quality: Well-written paper with comprehensive experiments demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Clarity: Well-organized presentation with clear notation and a glossary.  
- Arguments for acceptance: New model, strong empirical gains, and relevance to important problems.  
- Arguments for rejection: Needs deeper comparative analysis, interpretability discussion, and complexity assessment.  
- Recommendation: Rating 8/10 — accept with minor revisions to address the listed weaknesses.
