- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) using deep RNNs to model temporal dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Claim: Hierarchical structure captures short-term patterns via local implicit distributions and long-term dependencies via deep latent layers.  
- Empirical claim: Reported to outperform baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA) on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Strength: Well-motivated problem statement with connections to PFA and PGDS.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments on 4 synthetic and 5 real datasets (including ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA).  
- Strength: Demonstrated interpretability through factor activation patterns (Figure 2).  
- Strength: Variational inference framework is technically detailed and algorithmically sound (Section 3).  
- Strength: Ablation studies indicate ladder architectures improve performance over naive deepening (Table 3).  
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing for reported performance differences (MSE/PMSE tables).  
- Weakness: LSTM baseline appears poorly configured (hidden size mismatch), undermining comparative claims.  
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental and closely resembles prior Deep PFA and Deep Exponential Families without clear differentiation.  
- Weakness: Ladder architecture justification is weak and mostly reiterates known hierarchical VAE issues rather than offering new solutions.  
- Weakness: Poor ICEWS prediction performance challenges the claim of robust long-term dependence modeling.  
- Weakness: Paper acknowledges pruning and other limitations but offers no concrete remedies beyond vague MCMC references.  
- Weakness: Limited theoretical guarantees and no real-world deployment evidence, constraining practical impact.  
- Recommendation: Strengthen statistical validation, better justify architecture relative to prior deep count models, and clarify/fix LSTM baseline; current contribution is borderline.
