- Proposes DDPFA: a Poisson factorization model with a deep hierarchical RNN prior to model temporal factor scores.  
- Uses stacked RNNs as implicit conditional distributions to capture complex, non-linear and long-range temporal dependencies.  
- Inference is two-stage and iterative: mean-field variational updates for latents with fixed RNNs, then gradient-based updates for RNN parameters.  
- Empirically outperforms PFA, PGDS, and LSTM on synthetic and real-world datasets for fitting and prediction.  
- Strength: novel architecture combining Poisson factorization with deep recurrent hierarchical priors.  
- Strength: addresses an important problem—modeling dynamic count data and long-range temporal patterns.  
- Strength: thorough experiments showing consistent performance gains over baselines.  
- Weakness: inference procedure (Section 3) is unclear, lacking a clear description of the interplay between VI and gradient steps.  
- Weakness: training loss departs from ELBO—uses MSE on expectations of latents—without sufficient justification, leaving the objective ambiguous.  
- Weakness: loss formulation contains unclear terms (e.g., expectation of a distribution) and uses squared-error for count data instead of a Poisson likelihood.  
- Weakness: overall clarity and notation are poor; key RNN architectural details are omitted, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: inadequate positioning relative to broader deep probabilistic sequence models (e.g., recurrent VAEs, deep Kalman filters).  
- Argument for acceptance: well-motivated, novel model with strong empirical evidence of effectiveness.  
- Argument against acceptance: poorly explained and theoretically unjustified inference, ad-hoc loss, and limited reproducibility and positioning.  
- Conclusion: promising idea but requires major revision to clarify and theoretically ground the inference algorithm before acceptance.
