- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): RNN-parameterized Gamma transitions over a sliding window + hierarchical depth to model sequential count vectors.  
- Builds on Poisson Factor Analysis, using latent count augmentation and Poisson–Gamma conjugacy.  
- Inference alternates conjugate mean-field updates with neural-parameter optimization.  
- Neural component trained with a VAE-like “probability autoencoder” squared-error loss on expectations rather than a derived ELBO.  
- The squared-error objective and the “collapse” replacing Θ by X (Eq. 18) are insufficiently justified and lack theoretical guarantees.  
- Experiments on synthetic data and real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, Sheep) compare to PGDS, PFA, and LSTM; DDPFA generally performs best except PGDS on ICEWS prediction.  
- Authors discuss practical issues (latent pruning, limited depth utility) and propose a ladder-style architecture as mitigation.  
- Connects to prior work on Poisson/negative-binomial processes, deep exponential families, amortized VI for sequences (VRNN, DKF), and recurrent Poisson factorization.  
- Quality: conjugate MF-VI component is standard and sound, but neural training is ad hoc with no ELBO or convergence guarantee.  
- Experimental protocol lacks likelihood-based metrics for counts (predictive log-likelihood/Poisson deviance), statistical significance, and tuning details for LSTM/PGDS.  
- Clarity/reproducibility issues: indexing/typos, incomplete references, undefined design choices (Gamma shape/rate, role of λ and ψ), and missing figures/tables.  
- Originality: promising and novel hybrid/ladder framing, but needs stronger theoretical grounding for the neural objective.  
- Significance: addresses important long-range dependence in sparse count sequences; empirical gains are suggestive but not conclusive without stronger evaluation and ablations.  
- Pros: interesting conjugate+neural hybrid, generally strong empirical results, interpretable factors, thoughtful discussion of pitfalls.  
- Cons: no principled ELBO for neural part, clarity/reproducibility problems, insufficient evaluation/baselines, limited ablation/architecture analysis.
