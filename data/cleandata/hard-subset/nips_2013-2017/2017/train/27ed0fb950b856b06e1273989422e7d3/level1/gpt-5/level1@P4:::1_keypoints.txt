- Summary: Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) using Poisson observations with factor loadings and time-varying Gamma factor scores governed by a multi-layer recurrent Gamma hierarchy whose shape parameters are outputs of RNNs.
- Inference approach: Alternates mean-field variational updates exploiting Gamma–Poisson conjugacy with learning RNN priors via a “Probability AutoEncoder” objective aligning expectations of prior/posterior conditionals.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Poisson/Gamma factorization and dynamic variants (e.g., PGDS), deep latent count models (DEFs, deep Poisson factorization), and recurrent temporal count models; echoes hierarchical VAEs.
- Strength — relevance: Tackles an important problem class (sequential counts) relevant to recommendation, text, and event data.
- Strength — count-native modeling: Uses Poisson factorization with Gamma priors and latent count augmentation appropriate for counts and scalable inference.
- Strength — temporal modeling: Multi-layer recurrent prior aims to capture longer-range temporal dependencies beyond first-order gamma transitions.
- Strength — empirical scope: Evaluated on multiple synthetic and real datasets with reports of outperforming some baselines and offering interpretability.
- Strength — partial conjugacy: Exploits partially conjugate VI to make some updates efficient.
- Weakness — training objective: The Probability AutoEncoder loss is not derived from a principled ELBO, so there is no guarantee of consistency, convergence, or a coherent posterior approximation.
- Weakness — unspecified conditionals: Implicit Gamma conditionals are insufficiently specified (unclear role of shape/rate, summing shape parameters, and top-layer prior).
- Weakness — notation/derivation errors: Multiple indexing and formula inconsistencies (e.g., ψv vs ψk, wrong β terms, typos in multinomial allocation) raise correctness concerns.
- Weakness — gradient justification: Avoiding reparameterization/score-function gradients is not justified; stochastic gradient VI methods exist and were not properly considered.
- Weakness — evaluation metrics: Uses in-sample and one-step MSEs inappropriate for counts; should report Poisson log-likelihood/held-out perplexity and uncertainty calibration.
- Weakness — experimental details: Missing network architecture, activations, initialization, learning rates, batch sizes, runtime, and only single-step prediction tested.
- Weakness — clarity and presentation: Manuscript has grammatical errors, inconsistent notation, missing algorithm pseudocode, incomplete references, and figures/tables not clearly presented.
- Weakness — novelty: Combination of ideas is reasonable but novelty relative to PGDS, DEFs, and recurrent PF is not clearly established, especially given the ad-hoc training objective.
- Significance concern: Potentially impactful if technically solid, but current ad-hoc training, unclear semantics, limited metrics, and weak baselines undermine claimed contributions.
- Key suggestions: Derive and optimize a principled ELBO with proper gradient estimators; fix and fully specify all conditional distributions and priors; provide model/architectural details and pseudocode.
- Key suggestions (cont.): Report likelihood-based metrics, multi-step forecasting, stronger baselines, ablations (layers, window size, K), and runtime/memory analysis.
- Pros (summary): Important problem, count-native model, attempts long-/short-term dependency modeling, partially conjugate updates, diverse datasets.
- Cons (summary): Invalid/unsupported neural-prior objective, derivation/notation ambiguities, insufficient evaluation and baselines, unclear novelty.
