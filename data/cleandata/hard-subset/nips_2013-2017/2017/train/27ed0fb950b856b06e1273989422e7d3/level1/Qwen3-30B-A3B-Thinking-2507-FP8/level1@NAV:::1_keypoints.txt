- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) combining PFA/PGDS with RNNs to model sequential count data via variational inference.
- Method detail: Uses neural-network-based variational inference to capture short-term complex relationships and long-term temporal dependencies.
- Evaluation: Tested on synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS corpus, EBOLA).
- Strength — technical soundness: Proper variational inference implementation and clear experimental design.
- Strength — empirical performance: Consistent improvements over PFA and PGDS on most datasets.
- Strength — interpretability: Learns interpretable latent structures and better multi-factor activations (e.g., Figure 2).
- Strength — clarity: Well-organized presentation of model architecture and inference procedure.
- Weakness — novelty: Incremental combination of existing techniques rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- Weakness — architectural issues: Authors note problems with “pruning” and unnecessary network layers; benefits diminish with added layers.
- Weakness — inconsistent results: Underperforms PGDS on ICEWS prediction, raising concerns about robustness.
- Weakness — evaluation gaps: Lacks thorough ablation studies on layer depth and network architecture.
- Conclusion/recommendation: Reasonable but incremental contribution with unresolved issues; reviewer recommends rejection due to limited novelty and practical weaknesses.
