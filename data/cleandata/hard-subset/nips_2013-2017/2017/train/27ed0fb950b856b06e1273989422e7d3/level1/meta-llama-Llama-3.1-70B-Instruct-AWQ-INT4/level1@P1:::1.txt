This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) for analyzing sequential count vectors. The model extends the Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) method by incorporating recurrent neural networks to capture complex temporal dependence among time steps. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing improved performance in prediction and fitting tasks compared to existing methods.

The main idea of the paper is to capture the complex temporal dependence among time steps by using a deep and dynamic structure over time steps. The authors achieve this by introducing a hierarchical structure, where each layer represents a different level of abstraction. The model uses variational inference to approximate the latent variables and parameters in the neural networks.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their model and its components. The experimental results are also well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough comparison with existing methods.
