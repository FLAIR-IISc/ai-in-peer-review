- Proposes DDPFA: extends Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs to model short- and long-term dependencies in sequential count data using variational inference.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets, compared to baselines including PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  
- Builds on established Bayesian factor analysis and integrates deep learning (RNNs) for temporal modeling.  
- Motivation for deep models and choice of variational inference are clearly articulated and sensible.  
- Novelty: first significant integration of dynamic structure via RNNs into PFA for sequential count data.  
- Methodology: theoretically well-motivated, combining PFA strengths with deep learning and variational learning.  
- Experiments: comprehensive evaluation across diverse datasets with relevant baselines.  
- Interpretability: authors provide analysis of learned latent factors (e.g., ICEWS) offering useful insights.  
- Writing: generally clear and well-organized with accessible mathematical notation.  
- Lacks detailed comparison to other sequential deep models (e.g., GRUs, Transformers) and rationale for choosing RNNs.  
- Limited detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity analysis, weakening reproducibility and robustness claims.  
- Variational inference limitations (mean-field pruning) are acknowledged but not explored with alternative solutions.  
- Scalability concerns: computational cost with VI and deep networks not adequately addressed for large datasets.  
- Latent factor analysis is present but somewhat limited; deeper visualization/analysis would strengthen interpretability.  
- Some dense technical sections (inference) need clarification; Eq. (18) seems overly specific and could be generalized.  
- Overall: strong, novel contribution with compelling results; recommend accept with minor revisions to address comparisons, VI discussion, scalability, and hyperparameter details.
