

# Review of "Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model"

## Summary
This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) for analyzing sequential count vectors. The model builds upon Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) and Poisson-Gamma Dynamical Systems (PGDS) by incorporating deep neural networks to capture complex temporal dependencies. The authors extend previous work on dynamic Poisson factorization by using recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions and a deep latent structure to model long-time dependence. The paper claims to demonstrate superior performance over baseline methods (PGDS, LSTM, and standard PFA) on both synthetic and real-world datasets, with interpretable latent structures.

The paper relates its work to several previous NIPS contributions, including Deep Exponential Families (Ranganath et al., 2014), Deep Poisson Factor Analysis based on RBM/SBN (Gan et al., 2015), and Dynamic Non-Parametric Mixture Models (Ahmed & Xing, 2012). It acknowledges the limitations of existing models in capturing long-time dependence and complex temporal relationships, which the authors aim to address through their deep structure.

## Strengths
1. **Technical Approach**: The paper presents a well-structured extension of dynamic Poisson factorization that combines Bayesian modeling with deep learning. The use of a "ladder-like architecture" to connect layers of latent variables is a thoughtful approach to the hierarchical structure of the problem.

2. **Experimental Validation**: The paper evaluates the model on both synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, and Annual Sheep Population), demonstrating the model's effectiveness in various applications. The comparison with multiple baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA) provides a comprehensive evaluation.

3. **Interpretability**: The paper demonstrates that the latent factors learned by DDPFA are more interpretable than those from PGDS, as shown in Figure 2 and Figure 3, where the model captures multiple active factors at each time step rather than just one.

4. **Clarity of Model Architecture**: The mathematical formulation of the model (Equations 1-4) is generally well-organized and clearly presented, with a helpful illustration in Figure 1.

## Weaknesses
1. **Theoretical Justification**: The paper lacks sufficient theoretical analysis to support its claim that the deep structure is necessary for capturing long-time dependence. The paper does not provide a formal proof or theoretical analysis showing why a deep structure outperforms a shallow structure with a larger time window.

2. **Experimental Limitations**: The paper does not provide statistical significance tests for the performance improvements over baselines. The improvements claimed (e.g., "best performance in fitting and prediction task") are not quantified with appropriate error bars or p-values. Additionally, the paper does not compare with recent deep models for count data, such as those using neural networks for Poisson factorization.

3. **Limited Discussion of Limitations**: The paper acknowledges two problems ("pruning" and uselessness of adding network layers) but does not thoroughly address how these limitations affect the model's practical utility. The authors note that "pruning" is a known issue in variational inference but don't propose specific solutions beyond suggesting "trying other inference methods."

4. **Scalability Concerns**: The paper does not discuss how the model scales to very large datasets, which is particularly relevant for applications like recommendation systems or text analysis where datasets can be massive. The computational complexity of the variational inference with neural networks is not addressed.

## Arguments for and against Acceptance

**Arguments for acceptance:**
- The paper makes a clear contribution to the field by extending Poisson factorization with a deep structure to capture complex temporal dependencies.
- The model is well-motivated by the limitations of existing approaches in the literature.
- The experimental results are generally positive and demonstrate the model's effectiveness on multiple datasets.
- The interpretability of the learned factors is a valuable practical contribution.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
- The theoretical justification for the deep structure is weak and lacks sufficient analysis.
- The experimental results are not rigorously evaluated with statistical significance tests.
- The paper does not adequately address the limitations of the approach, particularly the "pruning" problem and the inefficiency of adding more layers.
- The paper does not compare with recent deep learning approaches for count data, making it difficult to assess the novelty relative to current state-of-the-art.

## Conclusion
The paper presents a reasonable extension of dynamic Poisson factorization with a deep neural network component. While the approach has some merits in terms of modeling complex temporal dependencies and providing interpretable results, the paper falls short in providing sufficient theoretical justification and rigorous experimental validation. The lack of statistical significance testing for the performance improvements and the limited discussion of the model's limitations are significant drawbacks.

The contribution is incremental rather than transformative, and the novelty compared to recent deep learning approaches for count data is not clearly established. While the paper makes a reasonable attempt to address an important problem, it does not sufficiently advance the state of the art in a demonstrable way. The paper would benefit from stronger theoretical analysis, more rigorous experimental validation, and a more thorough discussion of limitations.

Based on the four criteria:
- **Quality**: The paper is technically sound but lacks sufficient theoretical analysis and rigorous experimental validation.
- **Clarity**: The paper is well-written and organized, though some technical aspects (like the variational inference process) could be clearer.
- **Originality**: The approach extends existing work but does not provide a sufficiently novel perspective on deep modeling of count data.
- **Significance**: The results are promising but not sufficiently impactful to be considered a significant contribution to the field.

Given the above assessment, I recommend rejection. The paper's contributions are incremental and not sufficiently supported by the provided analysis and experiments.
