This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) for analyzing sequential count vectors. The model extends the traditional Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) by incorporating a deep neural network structure to capture the complex temporal dependence among the time steps.

The main idea of the paper is to model the sequential count data using a Poisson distribution, where the latent variables are represented by a deep neural network. The authors propose a variational inference algorithm to estimate the latent variables and the parameters of the neural network. The model is evaluated on both synthetic and real-world datasets, and the results show that it outperforms several baseline methods, including PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.
