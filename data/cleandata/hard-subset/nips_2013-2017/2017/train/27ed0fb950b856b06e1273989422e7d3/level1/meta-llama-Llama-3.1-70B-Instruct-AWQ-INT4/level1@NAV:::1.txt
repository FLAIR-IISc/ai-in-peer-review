This paper proposes a novel model, Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), for analyzing sequential count vectors. The model is based on Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) and incorporates recurrent neural networks to capture complex temporal dependencies. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on both synthetic and real-world datasets, outperforming existing methods such as PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of their model and its components. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors also carefully evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of their model.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and well-organized. The authors provide a clear explanation of their model and its components, making it easy to follow. The paper is also adequately informative, providing enough details for an expert reader to reproduce the results.

**Originality:** The paper proposes a novel combination of familiar techniques, incorporating recurrent neural networks into PFA to capture complex temporal dependencies. The authors also provide a unique approach to modeling sequential count data, which differs from previous contributions.

**Significance:** The results are important, and the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on real-world datasets, showing its potential for practical applications.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

1. The paper proposes a novel and effective model for analyzing sequential count vectors.
2. The authors provide a thorough explanation of their model and its components.
3. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the model on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
4. The authors carefully evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of their model.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
