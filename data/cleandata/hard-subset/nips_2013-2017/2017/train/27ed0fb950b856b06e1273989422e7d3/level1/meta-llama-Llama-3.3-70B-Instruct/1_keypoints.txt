- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), combining Poisson Factor Analysis with recurrent neural networks to model sequential count vectors.  
- Employs variational inference for efficient and scalable posterior estimation.  
- Demonstrates superior performance to baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA) on most synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with detailed model and inference descriptions.  
- Provides detailed experimental analysis and useful visualizations (Figure 2 and 3) that support interpretability.  
- Experiments highlight both strengths and weaknesses of the model.  
- Underperforms on the ICEWS dataset, likely due to the dataset’s complexity.  
- Model experiences “pruning” and added network layers often prove useless, possibly due to mean-field variational inference.  
- Lacks more extensive comparisons to other relevant methods (e.g., temporal Dirichlet process, deep exponential families).  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the noted weaknesses.  
- Pro-acceptance points: novel/effective model, strong empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Con-acceptance points: ICEWS performance, pruning/layer issues, insufficient method comparisons.
