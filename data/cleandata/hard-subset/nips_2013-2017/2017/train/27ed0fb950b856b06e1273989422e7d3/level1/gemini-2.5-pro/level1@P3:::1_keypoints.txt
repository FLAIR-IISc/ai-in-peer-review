- Introduces DDPFA: deep hierarchical Poisson factorization with RNN-parameterized temporal dynamics for sequential count data.
- Uses a two-stage hybrid inference: mean-field variational updates for latent variables and gradient-based training of RNNs with an autoencoder-like loss.
- Empirically outperforms baselines (PFA, PGDS, LSTM) on multiple synthetic and real-world datasets for fitting and prediction.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated melding of PFA interpretability with expressive deep RNN temporal priors.
- Strength: strong and comprehensive empirical evaluation across diverse datasets and baselines.
- Strength: candid analysis of limitations, acknowledging pruning/posterior collapse and diminishing returns from adding layers.
- Weakness: inference for neural-network parameters (Probability AutoEncoder) is confusing and lacks theoretical justification.
- Weakness: derivation from Eq.(15) to Eq.(16) is ad-hoc and not grounded in a principled ELBO/AEVB framework.
- Weakness: unsupported claim that differentiable variational Bayes is unsuitable; a joint ELBO approach is arguably more appropriate.
- Weakness: missing crucial reproducibility details for RNN architectures (unit type, layers, hidden sizes, activations, optimizers, learning rates).
- Weakness: alternating VI and gradient descent training may be unstable; replacing latent Θ with observed X at the bottom layer is questionable.
- For acceptance: novel impactful model with strong empirical gains and careful discussion of known hierarchical-model issues.
- For rejection: poorly explained/justified inference, lack of reproducibility details, and unclear training description in Section 3.
- Suggested fixes: clarify or rigorously derive the inference (preferably via ELBO/AEVB), provide full RNN architecture and hyperparameter details, and improve notation/explanations for ffeed/fback and latent flows.
