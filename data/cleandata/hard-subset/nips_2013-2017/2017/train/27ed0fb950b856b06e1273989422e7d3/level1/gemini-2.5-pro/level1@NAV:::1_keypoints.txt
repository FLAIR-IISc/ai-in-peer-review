- Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA): extends Poisson Factor Analysis by using RNNs to model temporal evolution of latent factors.  
- RNN-based temporal prior aims to capture complex, long-range dependencies compared to Markovian PGDS.  
- Inference uses a two-stage alternating procedure: mean-field variational updates for latents and gradient descent for RNN parameters.  
- Strength — originality/significance: novel, well-motivated combination of PFA with deep RNN temporal priors for sequential count data.  
- Strength — empirical performance: strong results on multiple synthetic and real-world datasets, often outperforming baselines.  
- Strength — quality/transparency: comprehensive experiments and honest discussion of limitations (e.g., pruning, diminishing returns from extra layers).  
- Weakness — clarity: Section 3 is dense and hard to follow, making the method difficult to understand.  
- Weakness — technical soundness: derivation and motivation of the “Probability AutoEncoder” loss (Eq.16–18) are unclear and appear ad-hoc.  
- Weakness — objective choice: unclear why squared error loss is used instead of an ELBO-based principled variational objective.  
- Weakness — reproducibility: unclear theoretical grounding of inference raises concerns about reproducibility and trust.  
- Weakness — practical significance: performance gains are not always decisive (e.g., ICEWS prediction task).  
- Weakness — adoption risk: complex and theoretically questionable inference may limit practical adoption.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture for an important problem, strong empirical evidence across datasets, and candid discussion of limitations.  
- Arguments for rejection: inference algorithm lacks clear justification and a unified variational objective, and the unclear inference section impedes reproducibility.  
- Overall: promising core modeling idea but significant concerns remain about the inference procedure’s clarity and theoretical soundness.
