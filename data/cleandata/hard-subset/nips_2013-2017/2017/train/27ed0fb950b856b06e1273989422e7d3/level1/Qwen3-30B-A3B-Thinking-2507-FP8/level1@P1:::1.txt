

# Peer Review: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model

This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) for analyzing sequential count vectors, extending previous work on Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) and Poisson-Gamma Dynamical Systems (PGDS). The authors aim to capture both short-term complicated relationships and long-term dependencies in time series count data by incorporating deep latent structures with recurrent neural networks. The model builds upon the standard PFA framework (X ~ Poisson(ΦΘ^T)) by introducing a deep structure where each layer of latent variables h^(n) is connected through neural network-based implicit distributions. The inference algorithm combines variational inference with probability autoencoders for training the neural network parameters.

The paper relates its work to previous NIPS contributions on dynamic Poisson factorization, such as PGDS [2] and the Dynamic Non-Parametric Mixture Models [1], as well as deeper models like Deep Exponential Families [8] and Deep Poisson Factor Analysis [9, 10]. However, the novelty claim regarding the deep structure is not sufficiently differentiated from these prior works, particularly since [9, 10] already propose deep Poisson factorization models.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach with a well-defined probabilistic model and inference algorithm. The variational inference framework is correctly derived from the Poisson-Gamma conjugacy, and the experimental evaluation on both synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, and Annual Sheep Population) provides reasonable support for the claims. However, the paper lacks thorough analysis of the model's limitations. The "pruning" issue mentioned in Section 5 is acknowledged but not properly addressed as a limitation, and the authors don't sufficiently explore why adding more layers doesn't consistently improve performance. The comparison with LSTM is reasonable, but the paper doesn't adequately explain why DDPFA outperforms LSTM on the EBOLA and ASP datasets where LSTM typically excels with sequence modeling.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written, with detailed mathematical formulations of the model and inference algorithm. The flow from problem statement to model to experiments is logical. However, some aspects of the model description could be clearer. For instance, the relationship between the layers in the deep structure (especially the role of h^(N)) and the exact mechanism of how the neural networks model the implicit distributions would benefit from more detailed explanation. The figure (Figure 1) would help clarify the model structure, but its absence in the provided text makes it difficult to fully assess.

**Originality:** While the deep structure with neural networks to capture temporal dependencies represents a reasonable extension of previous work, the paper doesn't sufficiently establish how this differs from existing deep Poisson factorization approaches like [9, 10, 12]. The ladder-like architecture mentioned in Section 5 is a notable contribution, but its novelty is not well-established relative to other hierarchical approaches. The paper cites recent work on deep Poisson factorization but doesn't clearly articulate the specific gaps that DDPFA fills. The authors should better position their work within the NIPS literature on deep hierarchical models.

**Significance:** The problem of modeling sequential count data is important across multiple domains (recommendation systems, text analysis, epidemiology), and the paper demonstrates reasonable performance improvements over baselines on several datasets. The interpretable latent structure is a significant advantage for practitioners. However, the significance is somewhat limited by inconsistent performance (e.g., PGDS outperforms DDPFA on ICEWS prediction) and the lack of clear demonstration of how the deep structure specifically contributes to the improvements. The paper doesn't convincingly show that the deep structure is necessary rather than just beneficial.

**Strengths:**
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation on both synthetic and real-world datasets
- Well-motivated problem statement addressing limitations of previous dynamic Poisson factorization approaches
- Clear demonstration of interpretable latent structures in real-world applications
- Detailed mathematical derivation of the inference algorithm
- Good comparison with multiple relevant baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA)

**Weaknesses:**
- Insufficient differentiation from prior deep Poisson factorization work (e.g., [9, 10, 12])
- Inadequate discussion of limitations, particularly the "pruning" issue and limited benefit of additional layers
- Theoretical justification for the deep structure is limited; not clear why the deep structure is necessary
- ICEWS results where DDPFA underperforms PGDS are not adequately explained
- The paper doesn't thoroughly address why the deep structure provides better performance than simpler alternatives

**Recommendation:** The paper presents a reasonable extension of existing work on dynamic Poisson factorization with a technically sound approach. However, the novelty claim is not sufficiently supported, and the paper would benefit from clearer differentiation from prior deep Poisson factorization work. The significance is somewhat limited by inconsistent performance across datasets and lack of thorough theoretical justification for the deep structure. I recommend rejection due to insufficient novelty and incomplete analysis of limitations, though the work is technically sound and could be strengthened with additional analysis and clearer positioning against prior work.

**Pro Acceptance Arguments:**
- Well-executed experiments on diverse datasets
- Provides interpretable latent structures for practitioners
- Addresses an important problem in sequential count data analysis
- Reasonable extension of existing Poisson factorization frameworks

**Con Acceptance Arguments:**
- Insufficient novelty relative to recent deep Poisson factorization work
- Incomplete analysis of model limitations
- Inconsistent performance compared to baselines
- Theoretical justification for the deep structure is lacking
- Limited differentiation from prior work in the literature
