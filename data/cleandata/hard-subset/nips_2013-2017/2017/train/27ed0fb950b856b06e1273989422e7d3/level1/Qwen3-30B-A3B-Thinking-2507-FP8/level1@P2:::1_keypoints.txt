- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): extends Poisson Factor Analysis with deep neural networks and RNNs to model temporal count data.  
- Claims DDPFA captures short-term (via RNNs) and long-term (via deep structure) dependencies and outperforms PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  
- Positions work relative to prior deep Poisson and exponential-family models, arguing existing dynamic models are too shallow.  

- Quality: technically sound with clear math and a well-described variational inference algorithm.  
- Quality concern: lacks theoretical analysis justifying the claimed advantages of the deep structure.  
- Experimental concern: unfair comparison—DDPFA uses 100 iterations vs. PGDS 1000 Gibbs iterations, biasing results.  
- Empirical issue: DDPFA performs poorly on ICEWS prediction and the paper does not explain this failure.  
- Empirical issue: Table 3 shows minimal performance gains from adding deep layers, weakening the main claim.  

- Clarity: generally well-written, consistent notation, and effective figures; datasets and metrics are clearly described.  
- Clarity concern: variational inference details and relationship between network parameters and variational distributions need clearer explanation; probability autoencoder needs implementation details.  

- Originality: integrates deep nets with Poisson factorization and uses RNNs for implicit temporal distributions.  
- Originality concern: not sufficiently distinguished from prior recurrent Poisson models (e.g., [12]); deep aspect yields limited practical gains.  

- Significance: tackles an important problem with interpretable latent structures shown in figures.  
- Significance concern: modest and inconsistent improvements across datasets and unclear scalability to very large datasets.  

- Strengths: well-motivated problem, good experimental mix (synthetic + real), interpretable visualizations, clear relation to prior work.  
- Weaknesses: minimal gains from depth, inconsistent dataset performance, insufficient theoretical justification, unfair experimental comparisons, limited scalability discussion.  

- Arguments for acceptance: meaningful problem, interpretable latent structures, and a reasonable integration of deep learning and Poisson factorization that could inspire follow-up work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak impact of the deep contribution, unfair baselines, insufficient differentiation from related work, and lack of theoretical support.  

- Overall assessment: technically sound but the deep contribution is not convincingly supported; modest/inconsistent gains and methodological concerns lead reviewer to recommend rejection without further theoretical or experimental strengthening.
