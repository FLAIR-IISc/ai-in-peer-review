- Summary: Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) using recurrent nets and deep latent structure to model sequential count vectors, claiming better fit and interpretability than PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.

- Strength: Technical approach: Well-structured extension combining Bayesian Poisson factorization with a ladder-like deep architecture to capture hierarchical temporal structure.

- Strength: Experimental validation: Evaluated on synthetic and multiple real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, Annual Sheep Population) with comparisons to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.

- Strength: Interpretability: Learned latent factors are shown to be more interpretable and capture multiple active factors per time step.

- Strength: Clarity: Mathematical formulation and model illustration are generally well-organized and clearly presented.

- Weakness: Theoretical justification: No formal analysis or proof that a deep structure is necessary or superior to a shallow model with larger time windows.

- Weakness: Experimental limitations: Lacks statistical significance tests, error bars or p-values, and omits comparison to recent deep models for count data.

- Weakness: Limited discussion of limitations: Mentions pruning and ineffectiveness of extra layers but does not assess practical impact or provide concrete remedies.

- Weakness: Scalability concerns: No discussion of computational complexity or how variational inference with neural nets scales to very large datasets.

- For acceptance: Extends Poisson factorization to capture complex temporal dependencies and produces interpretable factors; experiments show promising results across datasets.

- For acceptance: Well-motivated by limitations of existing methods and provides a thoughtful hierarchical deep architecture.

- Against acceptance: Weak theoretical support for the deep design and insufficient experimental rigor (no significance testing or comparisons to recent deep count models).

- Against acceptance: Does not adequately address known limitations (pruning, layer inefficiency) or scalability, reducing practical applicability.

- Conclusion: Contribution is incremental, lacks rigorous theoretical and experimental support, novelty vs. recent deep count models is unclear, and reviewer recommends rejection.
