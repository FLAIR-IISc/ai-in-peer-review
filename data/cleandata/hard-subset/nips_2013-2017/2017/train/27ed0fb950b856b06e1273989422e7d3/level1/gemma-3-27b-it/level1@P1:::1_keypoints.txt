- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), combining RNNs with Poisson Factor Analysis to model sequential count vectors.  
- Inference: Uses variational inference to estimate parameters and latent dynamics.  
- Novelty: Novel combination of RNNs and PFA within a variational framework to capture short- and long-term dependencies.  
- Related work: Builds on PFA and dynamic extensions; related deep Bayesian models cited, but could include newer temporal topic models and VAEs.  
- Technical soundness: Model formulation and derivations appear plausible though some update details are omitted.  
- Experiments: Evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets with baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA), showing promising predictive and fit performance.  
- Interpretability: Claims and visualizations indicate interpretable latent factors and temporal activations.  
- Acknowledgement of limits: Authors note issues like pruning and MF-variational limitations and suggest future work.  
- Clarity/detail weakness: Inference procedure and neural network training (Eqs.15–18) are condensed and hard to follow; pseudocode would help.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter effects (window size, learning rates, architectures).  
- Comparison depth: Baseline tuning and ablation studies (RNN depth, inclusion of Ψ/Λ) are lacking, possibly underrepresenting competitors like LSTM.  
- Scalability concerns: Computational complexity and applicability to large/high-dimensional datasets are not adequately addressed.  
- Theoretical justification: Prior choices (e.g., Gamma priors) lack strong theoretical motivation.  
- Recommendation and assessment: Interesting and potentially valuable contribution but current clarity, experimental depth, and scalability issues lead to a weak reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit.
