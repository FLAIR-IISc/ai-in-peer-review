- Proposes DDPFA: extends Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs to model temporal dependencies in sequential count vectors using variational inference; evaluated on synthetic and real data and compares to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  
- Well-motivated approach that addresses limitations of existing dynamic count models and aligns with using deep learning for Bayesian modeling.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive with multiple baselines and includes visualizations (Figures 2 & 3) for interpreting learned factors.  
- Mathematical derivations—especially variational inference steps (Eqs. 11–18)—lack sufficient detail on expectation computations and loss-function choice.  
- The use of a mean-field approximation is stated but its implications for tractability and limitations are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Experiments report MSE/PMSE differences without statistical significance testing (no p-values or confidence intervals).  
- Hyperparameter settings are briefly described but not justified; unclear if hyperparameter search was performed.  
- Discussion of the pruning problem and MF-VI limitations feels tacked on and not integrated into limitations/future work earlier.  
- Comparison to related work is incomplete and omits recent advances (e.g., attention mechanisms, newer dynamic topic models like [1],[2]).  
- Dataset choices and descriptions are brief; lacks analysis linking dataset characteristics to DDPFA’s strengths and weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel PFA+RNN combination, competitive empirical performance, and relevance to sequential data modeling.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete mathematical derivations, lack of statistical rigor in experiments, insufficient related-work coverage, and delayed discussion of limitations.
