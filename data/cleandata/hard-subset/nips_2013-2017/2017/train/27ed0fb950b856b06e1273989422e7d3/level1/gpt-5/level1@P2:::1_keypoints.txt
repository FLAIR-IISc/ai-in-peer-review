- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) modeling sequences of count vectors with Poisson likelihood and deep hierarchical RNN-defined temporal priors.  
- Inference: Alternates mean-field Gamma updates using auxiliary counts with an RNN-fitting “Probability AutoEncoder” that minimizes L2 losses between prior/posterior expectations and reconstruction.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Poisson factorization, Gamma–Poisson/NB processes, dynamic PF (e.g., PGDS), deep count models (DEFs, GBNs), and recurrent topic/PF models.  
- Technical correctness concern: The two-stage training objective is heuristic (L2 losses), not an ELBO or likelihood-based method, breaking conditional conjugacy and risking biased/unstable inference with no convergence guarantees.  
- Missing/unclear details: Exact RNN-to-Gamma parameterization, positivity/stability constraints, use of backward auxiliary posterior, windowing/layer architectures, and precise algorithmic steps are underspecified.  
- Evaluation metrics: Reliance on MSE/PMSE for counts is suboptimal; predictive log-likelihood or perplexity would be more appropriate.  
- Empirics concerns: Limited details on preprocessing, splits, hyperparameter tuning, runtimes, possible unfair baseline comparisons, missing recent deep count and recurrent PF baselines, and no uncertainty assessment.  
- Ablation gap: No ablation studies for the Probability AutoEncoder loss terms, window size, depth, or contributions of λ and ψ.  
- Clarity issues: Grammar, typos, inconsistent notation, incomplete references, and unclear derivations make the method and training hard to follow.  
- Originality assessment: The blend of deep hierarchical layers, bidirectional RNN conditionals, and alternating training is novel, but novelty over prior deep/recurrent PF work is not clearly isolated.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful if formalized and evaluated rigorously, but current heuristic training and limited evaluation weaken its practical significance.  
- Pros: Tackles important sequential count modeling, combines interpretability with deep temporal structure, shows empirical improvements on several datasets, and discusses failure modes.  
- Cons: Heuristic training lacking theoretical foundation, unclear presentation and derivations, limited/possibly unfair evaluation metrics and baselines, unclear novelty separation, and no scalability analysis.  
- Key suggestions: Derive a principled variational objective including RNN parameters (e.g., ELBO with reparameterization/implicit gradients), clarify model/algorithmic details, report likelihood-based metrics and runtimes, add ablations and stronger baselines, and improve writing and citations.  
- Overall recommendation: Interesting direction but currently insufficient for acceptance; recommend rejection pending principled training, clearer exposition, and stronger evaluation.
