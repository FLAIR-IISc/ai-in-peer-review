- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), combining PFA with deep RNN-based hierarchical latent structure to model temporal count data.  
- Positions work relative to recent deep Bayesian/deep Poisson models but insufficiently differentiates from prior RNN-based Poisson factorization [12].  
- Strength: technically sound model with well-motivated variational inference and appropriate synthetic and real-data experiments.  
- Strength: Demonstrates improved performance over PGDS, LSTM, and PFA on several datasets, especially where long-term dependencies matter.  
- Strength: Learned factors are interpretable, supported by illustrative figures.  
- Strength: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and reproducible with provided dataset and metric details.  
- Strength: Addresses a significant problem with practical applications (recommendation, text, networks) and shows meaningful gains on challenging datasets.  
- Weakness: Core novelty is limited; RNN use for temporal Poisson factorization overlaps with prior work and differences are not clearly articulated.  
- Weakness: Ladder-like architecture and claims about solving "pruning" lack justification and comparison to existing hierarchical models.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation lacks statistical significance testing and does not assess computational scalability to very large datasets.  
- Weakness: The "pruning" problem is noted but not thoroughly investigated or mitigated beyond speculative attribution to mean-field VI.  
- Weakness: Model description omits clear details on RNN structure interactions with hierarchical latents and the complex loss lacks intuitive explanation.  
- Specific criticism: No theoretical justification that the deep hierarchy specifically improves long-term dependency modeling over simpler RNNs/LSTMs.  
- Specific criticism: Comparison to LSTM is superficial and doesn’t explore whether stronger RNN architectures (LSTM/GRU) would close the gap.  
- Specific criticism: Authors do not explore alternative inference methods (e.g., MCMC, advanced variational methods) to address pruning.  
- Specific criticism: Ablation study shows diminishing returns from added layers, contradicting the motivation for depth and not adequately explained.  
- For acceptance: Tackles an important problem, shows empirical improvements, offers interpretability, and is clearly presented.  
- Against acceptance: Limited novelty, insufficient experimental rigor, unaddressed limitations, and ablation results weaken the deep-architecture claim.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on clearer differentiation from prior work, statistical significance testing, and thorough investigation of pruning and depth-related findings.
