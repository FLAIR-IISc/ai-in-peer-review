- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): Gamma sparsity priors, deep windowed recurrent latent hierarchy, and a two-stage inference with mean-field updates plus a “Probability AutoEncoder” (PAE).
- Builds on Poisson factorization and neural sequence models to capture short- and long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count data.
- Addresses an important problem: modeling sequential, sparse count data across diverse domains with interpretable factors.
- Combines PF interpretability with recurrent/deep structure to model long-range temporal dependence beyond shallow PF transitions.
- Leverages conjugacy for many variables, making several coordinate updates simple and compatible with PF machinery.
- Empirical results (synthetic + five datasets) show improved MSE/PMSE versus PGDS, PFA, and LSTM in several settings and produce qualitatively interpretable factors.
- The PAE training objective is ad hoc: squared-error moment-matching between conditional means lacks a principled ELBO or likelihood justification.
- Ambiguity between calling the RNN-parameterized conditionals “implicit” versus specifying them as Gamma; a principled ELBO or conjugate-computation VI could be derived but is not.
- Missing/ambiguous modeling and inference details: exact RNN type, nonlinearities, shape vs. rate parameterization, positivity enforcement, window size, layer counts, and runtime/complexity.
- Equations contain typos/inconsistencies (e.g., ψv vs. ψk, Eq. 8, Bernoulli-Poisson link, normalization), hindering reproducibility.
- Evaluation uses MSE/PMSE which are suboptimal for counts; held-out predictive log-likelihood/perplexity, uncertainty estimates, significance tests, and runtimes are absent.
- Comparisons to closely related neural/dynamic topic and deep PF models are limited or missing; closest prior work should be clearly differentiated and compared.
- Ablation analysis is incomplete: limited gains from deeper upper layers are noted, but the ladder remedy and quantitative ablations are under-described.
- Exposition and notation are unclear in places; paper needs a clear generative process, consistent notation table, and separation of coordinate updates vs. neural parameter optimization.
- Suggestions: derive and optimize a principled ELBO (conjugate-computation VI or black-box VI), clarify full distributional parameterizations, report log-likelihood/perplexity and runtimes, include detailed architecture and code, and run thorough ablations (window size, layers, ladder, pruning).
- Pros for acceptance: addresses an important problem, interpretable model, reported empirical gains, novel combination of PF with deep bidirectional temporal structure.
- Cons against acceptance: non-principled inference objective, clarity/reproducibility issues, insufficient evaluation and baselines, and limited evidence that depth provides consistent benefits.
