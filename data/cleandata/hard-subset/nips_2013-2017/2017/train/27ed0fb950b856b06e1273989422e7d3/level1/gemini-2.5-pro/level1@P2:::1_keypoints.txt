- Proposes DDPFA: integrates hierarchical RNNs into Poisson Factor Analysis to model complex temporal dynamics in count time-series.  
- Uses a two-stage hybrid inference: mean-field variational updates for latent variables and gradient-based training for RNN parameters.  
- Empirical results claim improved fit and prediction over PFA, PGDS, and LSTM baselines on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Core technical contribution (inference) is ad-hoc and lacks a clear global objective or theoretical justification.  
- The "Probability AutoEncoder" squared-error loss (Eq. 16) is hand-wavy, not derived from a proper probabilistic objective (ELBO), and omits KL regularization.  
- The justification for avoiding standard differentiable variational inference is unconvincing and contradicts common practice.  
- Authors acknowledge issues like "pruning" (posterior collapse) and limited gains from deeper layers, indicating possible fundamental flaws.  
- Model description is vague; the generative process and RNN-based equations are not clearly specified step-by-step.  
- A critical Figure 1 is missing, making the hierarchical RNN architecture hard to visualize and understand.  
- The inference section is confusing; the roles of the "prior" (ffeed) and "posterior" (fback) networks are muddled and do not map cleanly to a VAE encoder-decoder.  
- Manuscript suffers from poor writing, grammatical errors, typos, and awkward phrasing that impede readability and reproducibility.  
- References are incomplete and poorly formatted.  
- The integration of deep RNNs into PFA is novel and intuitive, representing a potentially valuable idea for dynamic count modeling.  
- Novelty is weakened by the ad-hoc inference scheme and insufficient comparison/discussion with related work (e.g., Recurrent Poisson Factorization).  
- The problem addressed is significant and results are promising, but lack of methodological rigor undermines trust and reuse by the community.  
- Recommendation: reject—paper needs a principled inference reformulation (e.g., amortized variational inference/VAE) and substantial clarity and reproducibility improvements before resubmission.
