- Paper analyzes anonymity of Bitcoin P2P network, comparing pre-2015 trickle and post-2015 diffusion under an eavesdropper adversary.
- Main contributions: model of Bitcoin P2P and adversary, deanonymization probability analysis for trickle and diffusion, and comparative anonymity evaluation.
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis supported by simulations; new models and estimators; methods clearly explained for reproducibility.
- Strength: emphasizes importance of graph structure in source-estimation/deanonymization attacks.
- Weakness: poor writing quality—excessive passive voice, typos, and incomplete/unclear sentences; needs substantial editing.
- Specific unclear items: sentence at lines 30–31 about method limitations, subsection on loss function (Eq. 15), and discussion lines 224–232 vis-à-vis Fig. 3.
- Metric concern: MSE/PMSE is insufficient for particle-filter-like models; predictive log-likelihood should be added.
- Experimental concern: reported K is too small; recommend increasing to at least K = 100.
- Presentation suggestion: move variational inference update equations to the supplement to improve readability.
- Figure issue: caption in Fig. 4 is unclear and should be revised for readability.
- Conference-fit: technically sound, original, significant, and reproducible; meets conference criteria in substance.
- Overall verdict: valuable and potentially high-quality with revisions, but requires major improvements in execution, clarity, and writing before acceptance.
