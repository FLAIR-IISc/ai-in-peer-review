- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): uses RNNs to model latent gamma shape parameters for sequential count data.  
- Builds on PFA and PGDS but introduces neural networks to capture complex long-term dependencies.  
- Technical approach is generally sound with a well-structured variational inference algorithm and theoretical derivations.  
- Major modeling concern: fixes gamma scale parameters (β) and only updates shape parameters (α) without sufficient justification.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: only a two-layer model tested, no comparisons to single-layer or deeper variants.  
- Lacks hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., window size, number of factors).  
- Manuscript contains typos (e.g., Equation 10) and needs polishing.  
- Presentation issues: confusing notation around the deep structure and inter-layer relationships.  
- Missing a clear graphical illustration of the full model, which would aid understanding.  
- Novelty: using neural networks to represent implicit latent distributions is promising, but distinction from Zhou et al. (2016) and Cong et al. (2017) is not made explicit.  
- Significance: important problem and promising results, but significance is limited by restricted experiments and limited comparisons to recent sequence models.  
- Strengths: addresses long-dependency problem, combines neural nets with probabilistic modeling, shows good real-world performance, and yields interpretable latent temporal patterns.  
- Weaknesses: limited experiments, unjustified fixed scale parameter, typos, insufficient differentiation from related work, and no clear model diagram.  
- Recommendation: reject; revise to compare depths, analyze hyperparameter sensitivity, justify fixed β, expand related-work discussion, and include a clear graphical model before resubmission.
