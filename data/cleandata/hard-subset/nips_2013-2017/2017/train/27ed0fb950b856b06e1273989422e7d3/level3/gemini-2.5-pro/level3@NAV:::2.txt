This paper proposes the Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA), a novel approach for modeling sequential count data. It extends the standard Poisson Factor Analysis framework by using Recurrent Neural Networks to represent implicit distributions over the latent temporal factors. Specifically, the model uses RNNs to approximate the shape parameters of gamma distributions, combining information from higher-level priors and lower-level likelihoods. This approach aims to overcome the limitations of models with simpler Markovian dynamics (e.g., PGDS) by capturing more complex and long-range temporal dependencies. The inference method updates neural network parameters based on the expectations of latent variables, which is a practical choice to improve efficiency.

The core contribution is both original and significant. The idea of using neural networks to parameterize the priors of a probabilistic model in this dynamic setting is a clever and powerful way to combine the flexibility of deep learning with the interpretability of Bayesian matrix factorization. It presents an interesting and smart solution to the challenging long-dependency problem in dynamic models for count data, which could be impactful for researchers in time-series analysis and topic modeling.

However, the paper has several weaknesses in its current form that should be addressed.

**Quality & Clarity:** The manuscript appears rushed and would benefit from significant polishing for clarity. There are critical typos that need correction (e.g., in Equation 10), which undermine confidence in the technical details. A key methodological choice requires more justification: the model assumes fixed gamma scale parameters and only updates the shape parameters. The authors should provide a detailed discussion on the rationale for this design choice and an analysis of its potential impact. The experimental validation also feels preliminary. A more thorough evaluation would include comparisons against a single-layer variant to clearly demonstrate the benefit of the "deep" structure, as well as an ablation study on key hyperparameters like the window size and the number of factors.

**Originality & Presentation:** The introduction should be updated to discuss more recent and highly relevant work in deep Poisson factor analysis, such as augmentable gamma belief networks (Zhou et al., 2016; Cong et al., 2017), to better situate the contribution within the current state of the art. Furthermore, the model's complexity makes it difficult to understand fully. A detailed graphical illustration of the full model architecture (e.g., similar to Fig. 1 in Chung et al., 2015) would greatly enhance the paper's clarity.

In summary, while the core idea is strong, the paper's execution in terms of presentation, methodological justification, and experimental rigor is not yet at the level required for this conference.
