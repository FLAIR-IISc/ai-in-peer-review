1. Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) using a deep hierarchical RNN-based architecture to model long-term dependencies in sequential count data.  
2. Uses neural networks to approximate gamma distribution shape parameters, combining prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) information.  
3. Employs variational inference with a mean-field approximation and neural-network-updated parameters to reduce sampling variance.  
4. Demonstrates improved performance over PGDS, LSTM, and standard PFA on synthetic and real datasets, capturing meaningful long-term patterns (e.g., ICEWS events).  
5. Contains critical typos and unclear exposition (notably Equation 10) and needs clearer notation and graphical illustrations of the architecture.  
6. Assumes fixed gamma scale parameters and updates only shape parameters without theoretical or empirical justification.  
7. Experiments are limited to a two-layer model; lacks comparisons to single-layer and deeper architectures and hyperparameter analyses (window size, number of factors).  
8. Results suggest added layers may not help, but the paper fails to investigate this "pruning" or layer uselessness phenomenon in depth.  
9. Related work discussion is inadequate, missing recent deep Poisson factor analysis papers (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016, Cong et al. 2017).  
10. Claims of interpretability and practical superiority are not convincingly demonstrated.  
11. Recommends substantial revisions: fix typos, add detailed illustrations, justify scale-parameter choice, expand experiments, analyze hyperparameters, discuss related work, and examine limitations.  
12. Overall: promising core idea with moderate originality, but significant technical, experimental, and exposition weaknesses—recommend rejection pending major revision.
