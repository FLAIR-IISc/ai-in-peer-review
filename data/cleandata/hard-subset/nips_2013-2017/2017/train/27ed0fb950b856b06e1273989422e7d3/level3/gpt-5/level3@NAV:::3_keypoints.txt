- Proposal: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) — hierarchical Poisson factor model using RNN-based implicit conditional priors to capture short- and long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Technical design: Preserves Gamma–Poisson conjugacy for local variables and uses mean-field VI for latent factors.  
- Training objective: Neural-network parameters trained via a probability autoencoder objective that matches forward/backward conditional moments (ELBO presentation unclear).  
- Baselines/datasets: Compared to PGDS, PFA, and LSTM on synthetic and real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, ID, ASP).  
- Quality: Modeling contribution is technically solid; hierarchical depth + RNN conditionals meaningfully address limitations of first-order Markov dynamics.  
- Factual error: Lines 86–87 misstate the distribution for nonnegative reals — should be the Poisson Randomized Gamma (PRG) distribution (Zhou et al.).  
- Objective clarity: Eqs. (15–18) are not presented as a valid ELBO — need probabilistic justification, explanation of auxiliary variables, and convergence guarantees.  
- Reproducibility: Paper needs code release and precise data splits for replication.  
- Clarity: Manuscript has numerous grammatical errors and typos; requires thorough language editing (many example phrases listed).  
- Originality: Using neural conditionals within a Gamma–Poisson deep hierarchy for temporal counts is novel relative to PGDS and deep PF variants.  
- Significance: Results are promising but evaluation could be stronger and more appropriate for count models.  
- Metrics: Must report held-out predictive log-likelihood/perplexity (in addition to MSE/PMSE) and emphasize them.  
- Presentation: Table 2 should boldface best results; formatting needs minor fixes.  
- Baselines/ablations: Include a single-level dynamic PF with the same RNN transition to isolate depth benefits, plus ablations over depth and window size.  
- Pros: Addresses long-range dependence with a principled count model; leverages conjugacy and RNNs; provides broad empirical evaluation and interpretability claims.  
- Cons: PRG factual error, unclear learning objective derivation, missing perplexity and limited baselines/ablations, significant clarity/grammar issues.
