- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), combining Poisson factorization with RNN-parameterized Gamma shape priors and bidirectional “probability autoencoder” inference.  
- Generative/inference design: Observations Poisson with factorized rates; RNNs output Gamma shapes; inference alternates conjugate mean-field updates with gradient training of RNNs using squared-error on expectations.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends dynamic PF/PGDS with deep RNN priors; related to deep exponential families, gamma belief networks, deep state-space models, and recurrent PF variants.  
- Technical soundness (positive): Conjugate MF updates over Φ, Θ, Λ, Ψ are standard and appear correct.  
- Technical concern — parameterization: Only learning Gamma shape (fixing rates) needs stronger justification due to mean–variance and identifiability issues; recommend toy experiments and sensitivity analysis to βh, βθ.  
- Training objective concern: The “probability autoencoder” squared-error on expectations is heuristic and not a principled ELBO; compare to proper variational bounds or reparameterization/score estimators.  
- Derivations/notation issues: Several unclear/erroneous equations and inconsistent indices (notably Eq. 10 and Eq. 11); symbols and updates must be aligned and corrected.  
- Clarity and presentation: Manuscript reads rushed with typos, inconsistent “shape/rate” notation, missing indices, and unclear time-window indexing—needs thorough proofreading.  
- Visuals and algorithms missing: Add plate/schematic diagrams for model and forward/backward RNNs, and provide pseudocode for the two-stage inference and NN architecture/details.  
- Originality: The RNN-parameterized Gamma prior idea is novel and appealing but should be positioned more clearly against recent deep Poisson/gamma and recurrent PF works.  
- Empirical evaluation: Shows improved MSE/PMSE and interpretable factors, but metrics are suboptimal for counts—recommend predictive log-likelihood, Poisson deviance, perplexity, plus runtime and convergence reporting.  
- Empirical gaps: Limited ablations (only two-layer shown), no study of window size c, K, Λ/Ψ roles, or learning rate parameters; ICEWS case where PGDS wins needs analysis.  
- Pros for acceptance: Conceptually neat combination preserving conjugacy, captures long-range dependence, interpretable factors, competitive on diverse datasets.  
- Cons against acceptance: Heuristic training objective, limited ablations, clarity/typo issues, missing figures/pseudocode, and baselines not fully aligned with count likelihoods.  
- Recommendations: Expand related work, run ablations (layers, c, K, learn rates), analyze identifiability/scale, use proper predictive metrics, consider implicit reparameterization for Gamma or justify squared-error, and fix notation/figures/pseudocode.  
- Overall takeaway: Promising and interesting approach that needs stronger empirical analysis, clearer exposition, corrected derivations, and a more principled or justified training objective.
