- Proposal: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) combining Poisson factorization with deep RNNs that output Gamma shape parameters for count factors.
- Architecture: Uses a “prior” RNN aggregating higher-layer context and a “posterior” RNN aggregating data-driven signals in a probabilistic autoencoder-style loop.
- Inference: Mean-field Gamma posteriors with closed-form updates; RNNs trained against expectations of latent variables to reduce sampling variance and runtime.
- Experiments: Evaluation on synthetic data and five real datasets, compared to PGDS, PFA, and LSTM; qualitative analyses reported.
- Relation to prior work: Related to VRNN-style implicit priors for sequences and to deep Poisson factorization / gamma belief networks (Zhou et al., 2016; Cong et al., 2017); these links need fuller discussion.
- Strength: Principled, interpretable Gamma–Poisson construction addressing long-range temporal dependence in dynamic count models.
- Strength: Clever design of RNN-produced Gamma shapes with fixed scales enabling efficient, closed-form mean-field updates.
- Strength: Empirical gains over baselines on several datasets and interesting qualitative insights.
- Weakness: The RNN learning objective uses squared-error terms rather than a clear ELBO contribution; theoretical VI link unclear.
- Weakness: Identifiability concerns from fixing scale parameters; should provide ablations with learnable scales or justify fixing β.
- Clarity issue: Manuscript reads rushed with inconsistent notation and critical typos (e.g., Eq. 10); needs proofreading and unified notation.
- Presentation request: Add a full graphical model / inference-flow illustration (similar to Chung et al., 2015) to clarify ffeed/fback, α/γ, and windowing.
- Originality concern: Must better position relative to deep Poisson factorization, gamma belief networks, and recent dynamic PFAs.
- Empirics limitation: Experiments limited to a two-layer model; add comparisons to single-layer and deeper variants.
- Hyperparameter analysis: Need sensitivity analyses for window size c, number of factors K, and effects of Λ/Ψ; report runtime and variance/reliability metrics.
- Pro for acceptance: Novel and practical solution for long-range dependencies in dynamic count models.
- Pro for acceptance: Interpretable model with computationally efficient inference.
- Con for acceptance: Clarity issues and typos hinder reproducibility.
- Con for acceptance: Limited ablations and hyperparameter analysis; missing related-work discussion.
- Con for acceptance: The principled variational-inference objective connection requires tightening.
