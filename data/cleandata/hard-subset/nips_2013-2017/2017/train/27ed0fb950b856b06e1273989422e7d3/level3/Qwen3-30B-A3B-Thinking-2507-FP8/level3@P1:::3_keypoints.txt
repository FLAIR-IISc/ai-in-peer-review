- Proposal: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) integrates Poisson Factor Analysis with deep recurrent networks to model sequential count data and long-range temporal dependencies.

- Core innovation: deep recurrent architecture + variational inference to capture both short-term local and long-term dependencies across hierarchical latent layers.

- Technical strength: addresses shallow-structure limitations of prior dynamic PF methods (e.g., PGDS) for long-range temporal dependence.

- Inference strength: variational framework is well-motivated with clear derivations and neural-network-based parameter updates.

- Empirical strength: outperforms PGDS, PFA, and LSTM on synthetic and real datasets; learns interpretable long-term factor activations (e.g., ICEWS).

- Scalability strength: performs well on high-dimensional, sparse data (e.g., NIPS corpus with V=14,036).

- Critical error: incorrectly claims use of a "Poisson Randomized Gamma" distribution instead of relying on standard Gamma–Poisson conjugacy; must be corrected.

- Evaluation weakness: reports only MSE/PMSE and omits held-out perplexity, a standard metric for count data, weakening significance of results.

- Baseline weakness: comparison to LSTM is incomplete and missing a single-layer deep PF baseline to justify hierarchical depth; Table 3 shows limited gains from extra layers.

- Presentation issues: Table 2 lacks clear formatting of best results and the paper contains numerous grammatical errors reducing clarity.

- Originality: novel application of deep hierarchical structures to dynamic Poisson factorization, distinct from prior shallow or non-temporal deep PF work.

- Significance: meaningful real-world applications demonstrated (text, disaster, event data), though ICEWS prediction weakness and unexplained "pruning" reduce impact.

- Quality & clarity: technically rigorous overall, but the Gamma distribution error and dense, under-motivated variational details require revision and clearer exposition.

- Recommendation: promising but not accept; require fixes for the Gamma misstatement, addition of perplexity metrics, inclusion of a single-layer baseline, clarification of pruning, and language edits.

- Pro/Con summary: Pros — novel deep architecture, strong empirical results, interpretable factors, rigorous inference; Cons — critical Gamma error, insufficient evaluation metrics, missing baseline, grammatical issues, unexplained pruning.
