- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) using RNNs within PFA to model sequential count data.  
- Leverages variational inference and gradient descent to learn latent variables and network parameters.  
- Motivation well-explained; addresses PFA limitations in capturing long-range dependencies.  
- Related work section incomplete—missing key deep PFA works (Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017).  
- Should discuss trade-offs between RNNs and other sequence models (e.g., Transformers).  
- Novelty: combines PFA with deep recurrent networks and RNN-parameterized Gamma distributions.  
- Theoretical soundness: model formulation builds on Bayesian Poisson–Gamma principles and uses reasonable variational inference.  
- Potential interpretability: factorization plus RNNs can yield meaningful latent representations (demonstrated on ICEWS).  
- Experiments: includes synthetic and real datasets with baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA).  
- Clarity and presentation issues: confusing notation, insufficient inference details, typographical errors (e.g., Eq. 10), and need for a clear model diagram.  
- Unjustified modeling choice: only shape parameters of Gamma updated while scale is fixed—needs rationale and analysis of implications.  
- Limited experimentation: primarily two-layer models; lacks depth comparison and hyperparameter sensitivity analyses (window size, number of factors).  
- No ablation study to quantify contributions of deep structure and RNN components.  
- Pruning issue (noisy latent dimensions) acknowledged but under-discussed; should connect to VAE literature and potential fixes (e.g., auxiliary MCMC).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: manuscript clarity problems, unexplained modeling choices, and limited/insufficient experimental evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: interesting contribution but requires substantial revision and clarification; reviewer rates Weak Reject but would reconsider a revised submission.
