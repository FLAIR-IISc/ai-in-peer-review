- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) combining Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs to model sequential count vectors.  
- Uses RNNs to approximate gamma shape parameters and represent implicit distributions over counts via gamma distributions.  
- Captures complex long-time dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Clear and concise problem introduction and thorough explanation of the proposed method.  
- Neural network parameters are updated based on expectations of latent variables.  
- Experimental results show DDPFA often outperforms PGDS and LSTM.  
- Manuscript appears rushed and needs polishing, with critical typos (e.g., equation 10).  
- Authors assume fixed gamma scale parameters and only update shape parameters; this choice requires deeper discussion and analysis.  
- Experiments are limited: only a two-layer model is evaluated; comparisons to single-layer and deeper models are missing.  
- Lacks analysis of hyperparameters such as window size and number of factors.  
- Could benefit from more discussion of recent related work (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017).  
- Would be clearer with more graphical illustrations of the full model.  
- Technically sound, well-motivated method with significant contribution to sequential count analysis.  
- For acceptance: novel RNN+PFA approach and strong empirical performance; Against: presentation issues, missing experiments, and insufficient hyperparameter/assumption analysis.
