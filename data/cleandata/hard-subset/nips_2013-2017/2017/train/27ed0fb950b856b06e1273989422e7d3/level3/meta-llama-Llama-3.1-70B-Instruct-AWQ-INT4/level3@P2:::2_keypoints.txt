- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) combining Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs to model sequential count vectors.  
- Models latent counts with gamma distributions parameterized by recurrent neural networks to capture complex temporal dependencies.  
- Uses variational inference to approximate latent variables and train neural network parameters.  
- Paper is well-organized, clear, and uses helpful mathematical notation and equations.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets, outperforming PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  
- Strength: captures complex long-time dependence among time steps.  
- Experiments are limited to a two-layer model without comparisons to single-layer or deeper variants.  
- Lacks analysis of key hyperparameters such as window size and number of factors.  
- Assumes fixed gamma scale parameters and updates only shape parameters; this assumption needs more discussion and analysis.  
- Would benefit from more detailed graphical illustration of the full model (e.g., similar to Chung et al. 2015).  
- Related work discussion should include recent deep Poisson factor analysis (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017).  
- Quality: technically sound but experimental evaluation is limited.  
- Clarity: clear exposition and helpful mathematical detail.  
- Originality: novel method for sequential count modeling and a meaningful contribution.  
- Significance: promising empirical results showing improvement over existing methods.  
- Recommendations: add model diagrams, expand related work, run deeper/more varied experiments, and analyze the gamma parameter assumptions and other hyperparameters.
