This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) for analyzing sequential count vectors, leveraging RNNs to model implicit distributions and capture long-time dependencies. The core idea of using RNNs within a Poisson Factor Analysis framework to learn temporal dynamics is promising and addresses a known limitation of previous approaches like PGDS, which struggle with long-range dependencies due to independence assumptions. The variational inference approach with gradient-based learning for the neural network parameters is a reasonable strategy for tackling the inference challenges.

However, while the core idea is sound, the execution and presentation leave much to be desired. The paper feels rushed, lacks sufficient detail in critical areas, and contains demonstrable errors.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of RNNs and Poisson Factor Analysis for dynamic count data is a novel approach, offering a potential solution to the long-dependency problem.
* **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The motivation drawing from Bayesian factor analysis and deep models is clear, and the proposed method builds logically upon prior work. The use of Gamma distributions to model latent variables is well-justified within the Poisson framework.
* **Potential Significance:** If the limitations were addressed, this work could significantly impact fields dealing with sequential count data, like recommendation systems, text analysis, and time series analysis. The model's interpretability, stemming from the latent factor structure, is a valuable aspect.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Technical Soundness & Completeness:** Equation 10 contains a critical typo, immediately undermining confidence in the authors' attention to detail. The assumption of fixed Gamma scale parameters is a significant limitation. While the authors likely had reasons for this simplification, the paper *must* contain a detailed discussion and analysis of this choice, including its potential impact on model performance and expressiveness. It's unclear why the scale parameter was fixed, and a justification is vital.
* **Clarity & Presentation:** The manuscript is poorly polished and lacks clarity in several areas.  A detailed graphical illustration of the full model, akin to Fig. 1 in Chung et al. (2015), is crucial for understanding the relationships between the various layers and components. The notation is occasionally dense and could benefit from more explanation.
* **Experimental Evaluation:** The experiments are limited and insufficient to convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method. The authors only present results for a two-layer model. A systematic comparison with single-layer and deeper models is necessary to understand the benefits of depth. Crucially, an analysis of other hyperparameters – window size, number of factors (K) – is missing. The choice of K=3 for some datasets and K=10 for others feels arbitrary and lacks justification.
* **Related Work:** The paper doesn't adequately address recent related work. The authors should discuss Zhou et al. (2016) and Cong et al. (2017) on deep Poisson factor analysis in the introduction. These papers present alternative deep learning approaches for similar problems and should be positioned against within the context of the proposed work.
* **MF-VI Limitations:** The authors briefly acknowledge the problems associated with mean-field variational inference ("pruning") but don't propose solutions or engage in a deeper discussion of the implications.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The core idea is novel and potentially impactful.
* The theoretical framework is solid.
* The experiments, though limited, show promising results.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Critical technical errors (e.g., Eq. 10) raise concerns about technical soundness.
* The assumption of fixed Gamma scale parameters is a major limitation without sufficient justification.
* Insufficient experimental validation and hyperparameter analysis.
* Poor clarity and presentation.
* Lack of discussion of closely related recent work.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents an interesting idea with the potential to advance the field. However, the current manuscript is not ready for publication in NIPS. The significant weaknesses regarding technical correctness, experimental validation, and clarity outweigh the strengths. 

**Recommendation:** **Reject**

I recommend rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing the points raised in this review. Specifically, the authors must:

1.  **Correct the typo in Equation 10.**
2.  **Provide a detailed justification for fixing the Gamma scale parameter and analyze the implications of this choice.**
3.  **Expand the experimental evaluation to include comparisons with single-layer and deeper models, and perform a hyperparameter analysis.**
4.  **Include a detailed graphical illustration of the model.**
5.  **Discuss relevant recent work (Zhou et al., 2016; Cong et al., 2017).**
6.  **Thoroughly proofread and polish the manuscript for clarity and conciseness.**

Addressing these issues will significantly improve the quality and impact of the work.
