- Paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): deep temporal Gamma latent structure parameterized by bidirectional RNNs for sequential count vectors.  
- Inference is two-stage: MFVI for count/Gamma variables treating RNN outputs as fixed, then train RNNs with a "probability autoencoder" objective coupling reconstruction and forward/backward consistency.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and five real datasets against PGDS, PFA, and LSTM using MSE and one-step PMSE; reports qualitative interpretable factors.  
- Strength: addresses shallow dynamic PF limitations by modeling long-range temporal dependencies with a principled deep temporal structure.  
- Strength: retains conjugacy-based MFVI updates for counts and Gammas, making inference computationally attractive.  
- Strength: empirical gains over PGDS/PFA on several tasks and diverse datasets; interpretability demonstrated qualitatively.  
- Strength: discussion of pruning and ladder architectures shows awareness of hierarchical variational pitfalls.  
- Weakness: the "probability autoencoder" objective lacks derivation as a valid ELBO or bound and has no guarantees of consistent inference.  
- Weakness: evaluation uses MSE/PMSE only; predictive log-likelihood/held-out perplexity (standard in PF/topic models) is missing.  
- Weakness: limited ablations — need depth/number-of-layers studies and a shallow RNN-PF baseline to isolate hierarchical benefits.  
- Weakness: LSTM comparison is not definitive because it uses different likelihoods/objectives (MSE vs. count likelihood).  
- Weakness: notational inconsistencies, grammatical errors, and apparent equation typos reduce clarity.  
- Weakness: identifiability and scaling issues among Φ, Θ, Λ, Ψ are not addressed, risking unstable learning without constraints.  
- Weakness: factual inaccuracy about modeling nonnegative reals; should cite Poisson Randomized Gamma (PRG) for Poisson-compound constructions.  
- Quality: PF/MFVI parts are standard and correct if RNN outputs are fixed, but joint RNN training lacks principled variational justification and may bias posteriors.  
- Empirics: promising but need likelihood-based metrics, uncertainty estimates, ablations, and runtime/scalability analysis.  
- Clarity: paper is difficult to read; needs fixes to notation, terminology ("implicit distribution", "Probability AutoEncoder"), figures/tables and training detail disclosure.  
- Originality: novel combination of PF with deep bidirectional RNN-parameterized Gamma conditionals, but incremental positioning relative to recurrent PF/PGDS/DTM should be clarified.  
- Significance: could be useful for long-horizon count time series if validated with likelihood metrics and stronger ablations; current evidence is suggestive but not decisive.  
- Main questions: provide an explicit ELBO including RNNs or justify L2 objective; explain identifiability/scaling fixes; report runtime and complexity scaling; sensitivity to window/layers/hidden sizes; report predictive log-likelihood with std.  
- Suggestions: add baselines (single RNN-PF, recurrent PF, DTM), report predictive likelihood/perplexity and uncertainties, derive principled variational objective (amortized inference or reparameterization), improve tables/figures and fix grammar/notation.  
- Suggestions: clarify architecture/training details (RNN type, hidden sizes, activations, optimizer, LR, batch size, init) and release code.  
- Pros for acceptance: interesting modeling idea for long-range temporal structure and encouraging empirical gains with interpretable factors.  
- Cons against acceptance: no principled variational objective for RNNs, lack of ELBO/likelihood evaluation, insufficient ablations, unclear/erroneous presentation.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject (high reviewer confidence).
