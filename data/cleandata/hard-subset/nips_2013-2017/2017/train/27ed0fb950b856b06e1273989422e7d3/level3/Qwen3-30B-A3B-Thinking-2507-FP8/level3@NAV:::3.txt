

This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that extends dynamic Poisson factorization by incorporating recurrent neural networks to capture long-range temporal dependencies. The authors build upon prior work in Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) and dynamic models like Poisson-Gamma Dynamical System (PGDS), addressing their limitations in modeling complex long-term dependencies through a hierarchical deep structure. The model uses variational inference for parameter learning and demonstrates improved performance on synthetic and real-world count data.

The technical content is strong, with a clear motivation for addressing limitations in prior dynamic PF works. The model architecture effectively leverages deep latent structures to capture complex temporal patterns, and the variational inference approach is well-motivated. However, the manuscript contains a significant factual error: the authors incorrectly reference a "Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution" (lines 86-87) when they actually employ the Poisson-Gamma distribution, which is a standard conjugate pair for count data modeling. This needs immediate correction.

The experimental evaluation would be significantly strengthened by emphasizing held-out perplexity as the primary metric alongside MSE/PMSE, as requested by the reviewers. Table 2 should be formatted with best results in bold for clarity. The authors should include a single-level deep dynamic PF baseline to properly justify the benefits of their multi-level hierarchical approach, as current comparisons with LSTM are not definitive due to differing learning algorithms.

The manuscript suffers from numerous grammatical errors and typos that impede readability (e.g., "unit the data," "PFA factorized a count matrix," "V-dimesnion sequentially"), which must be corrected. These issues, combined with the technical errors, significantly impact the paper's presentation and credibility.

The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the literature on dynamic count data modeling, with clear potential for applications in recommendation systems, text analysis, and time series analysis. While the core idea is promising, the current manuscript requires substantial revisions to address the technical inaccuracies, improve experimental reporting, and fix the language issues before it can be considered for publication. The model's interpretability and performance on real-world datasets (particularly EBOLA and ASP) demonstrate its value, but these strengths are currently undermined by the manuscript's technical and presentation flaws.
