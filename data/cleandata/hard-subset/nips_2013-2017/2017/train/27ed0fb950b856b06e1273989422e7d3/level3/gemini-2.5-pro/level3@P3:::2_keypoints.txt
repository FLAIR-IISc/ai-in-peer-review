- Proposes DDPFA: a novel deep, dynamic Poisson factorization model using RNNs to parameterize Gamma shape transitions for sequential count data.
- Extends PFA by replacing simple Markovian transitions with expressive, non-linear RNN-based priors to capture long-range temporal dependencies.
- Inference uses a hybrid approach: mean-field variational updates for latents and gradient descent on RNN parameters using expectations to avoid high-variance sampling.
- Strength — originality/significance: novel synthesis of RNNs with hierarchical Bayesian models that could impact dynamic topic models and time-series count analysis.
- Strength — technical approach: pragmatic inference that leverages expectations to reduce gradient variance and uses a probabilistic autoencoder-like structure for training.
- Clarity issue: model description lacks a clear graphical illustration of temporal and hierarchical dependencies (e.g., layered/time-sliced plate/graph).
- Clarity issue: derivation and motivation for the loss (Eqs. 16–18) are abrupt and need rigorous justification—unclear if it approximates a variational bound.
- Literature/context: introduction omits relevant recent works (Gamma belief network, augmentable Gamma belief networks) on deep generative models for count data.
- Technical error: probable critical typo in Eq. 10 where a latent variable is incorrectly conditioned on observed data X, violating conditional independencies.
- Modeling/design concern: RNNs output only Gamma shape parameters while scale/rate parameters are fixed scalars, restricting latent variance—no justification or ablation provided.
- Experimental weaknesses: lack of ablation studies (e.g., single-layer vs. multi-layer) and sensitivity analysis to layers, window size c, and number of factors K to justify “deep” claim.
- Baselines and evaluation: comparing to LSTM is potentially misleading; should include other deep generative sequence models and deeper qualitative analysis of interpretability and long-term trends.
- Overall verdict: promising and original model but manuscript feels rushed and incomplete, requiring clearer presentation, technical fixes, stronger justification, and more comprehensive experiments.
