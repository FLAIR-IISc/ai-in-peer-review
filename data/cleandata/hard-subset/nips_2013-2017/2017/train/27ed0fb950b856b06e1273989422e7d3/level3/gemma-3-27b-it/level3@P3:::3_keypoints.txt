- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) using recurrent neural networks within a Poisson factorization framework with variational inference to model long-range temporal dependencies in count data.
- Motivation: Aims to overcome short-range dependence limitations of prior dynamic Poisson Factor Analysis by modeling factor transitions with RNNs.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on PFA and dynamic extensions, and aligns with deep Bayesian and neural factor-analysis literature.
- Technical soundness: Model formulation and variational inference derivations appear reasonable and technically solid.
- Addressing long-range dependency: Use of recurrent networks is a logical approach to capture long-range temporal relationships.
- Experiments: Evaluated on diverse synthetic and real-world datasets, covering multiple scenarios.
- Interpretability: Includes factor analysis and visualizations (Figures 2 and 3) to provide insight into learned representations.
- Critical factual error: Paper incorrectly describes the Gamma distribution usage; it actually requires the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution, undermining methodological validity.
- Incomplete evaluation: Reports MSE/PMSE but omits held-out perplexity, which is a more appropriate metric for generative probabilistic models.
- Missing baseline: No comparison to a single-level deep dynamic PF (e.g., feedforward baseline) to justify hierarchical depth benefits.
- LSTM comparison limitations: Comparison to LSTM is inconclusive due to different learning algorithms and lack of hierarchical LSTM baselines.
- Presentation issues: Numerous grammatical errors and typos significantly reduce readability and must be corrected.
- Table formatting: Table 2 lacks clear formatting and should highlight best results (e.g., bolding) for easy comparison.
- Limited discussion on pruning/ladder architectures: Brief and underdeveloped treatment of pruning issues and ladder-like architectures without implementation details or expected improvements.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem, proposes a novel RNN + Poisson factorization approach, and provides diverse experimental coverage.
- Arguments against acceptance: Major concerns include the factual Gamma error, poor writing quality, incomplete evaluation, missing baselines, and weak justification for hierarchical depth.
- Overall assessment: Interesting and promising idea with solid foundation but not ready for publication due to correctness, clarity, and evaluation shortcomings.
- Recommendation and required revisions: Reject in current form; major revisions needed to fix the Gamma error, proofread manuscript, add single-level baseline, report held-out perplexity, improve table formatting, and expand pruning/ladder discussion.
