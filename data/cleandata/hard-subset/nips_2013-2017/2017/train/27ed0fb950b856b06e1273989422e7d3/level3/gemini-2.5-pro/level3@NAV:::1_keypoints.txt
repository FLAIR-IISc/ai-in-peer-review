- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), extending Poisson factorization with a deep, recurrent-like structure to model complex temporal dependencies in count data.  
- Promising direction: combines interpretability of Poisson factorization with expressive deep recurrent structures to capture non-Markovian dynamics.  
- Evaluation across five real-world datasets shows a serious attempt to validate the model’s utility.  
- Major revision required: significant weaknesses in technical clarity, experimental evaluation, and presentation.  
- Model specification is unclear: key parameterizations (e.g., distributions in Eq. 3 and Eq. 4, role of Gamma) are omitted or confusing.  
- Incorrect claim about posterior form: Eqs. 9–10 are presented as “implicit” but appear to be standard explicit Gamma distributions.  
- Architectural mischaracterization: Eq. 4 is not a true RNN since a layer’s state h_t^(n) doesn’t depend on its own previous state h_{t-1}^(n); this simplification needs strong justification.  
- Inference and loss explanation opaque: Eq. 15 (loss) is not clearly explained.  
- Inappropriate evaluation metrics: using MSE/PMSE is unsuitable for Poisson models; predictive log-likelihood is the standard and necessary metric.  
- Experimental design concern: number of latent factors (K=10) seems too small for high-dimensional datasets like NIPS and may limit performance.  
- Presentation is poor: many typos, grammatical errors, unclear sentences, pervasive passive voice, and unreadable figure captions (e.g., Fig. 4).  
- Specific disconnects in the text: e.g., discussion in lines 224–232 is disconnected from Figure 3.  
- Overall recommendation: not ready for publication—authors must clarify model and inference, justify architectural choices, redo experiments with appropriate metrics and hyperparameters, and thoroughly revise the manuscript for clarity and correctness.
