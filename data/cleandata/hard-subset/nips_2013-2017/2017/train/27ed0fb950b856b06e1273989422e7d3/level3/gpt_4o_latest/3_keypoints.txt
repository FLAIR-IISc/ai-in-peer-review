1. Proposes DDPFA: a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization model combining Poisson factorization with a deep, RNN-like hierarchical structure to capture short- and long-term temporal dependencies.  
2. Uses variational inference and demonstrates strong performance on synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA).  
3. Compares DDPFA to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA, reporting competitive or superior results in most cases.  
4. Strength — Technical novelty: innovative integration of deep hierarchical structure with Poisson factorization to model long-range temporal dependencies.  
5. Strength — Comprehensive evaluation across diverse datasets, showing robustness and domain applicability.  
6. Strength — Interpretability: model yields interpretable latent factors (e.g., ICEWS factor analysis).  
7. Strength — Inclusion of baseline comparisons provides performance context, though some comparisons (e.g., LSTM) are not fully aligned.  
8. Weakness — Numerous grammatical and factual errors (e.g., incorrect statement about modeling nonnegative numbers/Poisson Randomized Gamma) reduce readability and accuracy.  
9. Weakness — Evaluation metrics are limited to MSE/PMSE; missing held-out perplexity would strengthen evaluation for text datasets like NIPS.  
10. Weakness — Lacks direct comparison with single-level dynamic Poisson factorization models, weakening justification for multiple hierarchical levels.  
11. Weakness — Presentation issues: best results not highlighted in tables, organization and explanation of complex equations and inference need improvement.  
12. Weakness — Scalability concerns: “pruning” and limited gains from extra layers suggest potential scalability issues that are not fully addressed.  
13. Recommendation — Accept conditional on revisions: correct factual/grammatical errors, improve clarity and presentation, justify the hierarchical design, highlight best results, and strengthen evaluation metrics.
