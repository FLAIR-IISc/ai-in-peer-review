- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) extending Poisson Factor Analysis with a simplified RNN-like structure for temporal count data.  
- Builds on Poisson-Gamma Dynamical Systems and deep Poisson factorization to model long-term dependencies via hierarchical latent structure.  
- Core idea is promising: deeper structures may capture complex temporal patterns better than shallow models.  
- Major technical flaw: claimed RNN in Eq. 4 is not truly recurrent—h_t^(n) depends only on top-layer vectors, not previous time steps—needs substantial justification.  
- Model description is incomplete: key parameterizations (e.g., Gamma distribution details in Eq. 4) are omitted and some explicit Gammas are mislabeled as “implicit.”  
- Evaluation metrics are inappropriate: using MSE/PMSE for Poisson models is flawed; predictive log-likelihood should be reported.  
- Latent factor count is unrealistically small (K=3–10); authors should evaluate much larger K (≈100) for real-world data.  
- Discussion of “fanatical data and disaster data” is vague and requires clearer explanation.  
- The link between Figure 3 and the text (lines 224–232) is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Writing quality is poor with excessive passive voice, grammatical errors, and typos throughout.  
- Variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) disrupt flow and should be moved to the supplement.  
- Figure 4’s caption is unreadable and must be rewritten for clarity.  
- Claim about “pruning” in Table 3 lacks sufficient justification and evidence.  
- Overall judgment: paper shows potential but needs substantial revision to fix technical inaccuracies, clarify specifications, adopt proper evaluation, and improve writing before publication.
