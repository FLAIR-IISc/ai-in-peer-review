- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): Poisson likelihood with deep RNN-parameterized hierarchical latent factors for sequential counts.
- Novelty: combines Poisson factorization with a deep recurrent prior to capture longer-range temporal dependencies than first-order gamma transitions.
- Inference: alternates closed-form variational updates for conjugate Gamma–Poisson parts with gradient-based learning of neural shape-parameter mappings via a probability autoencoder objective.
- Empirical evaluation: synthetic stress tests and diverse real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, Ebola, International Disaster, Annual Sheep Population) comparing to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.
- Strength — clear motivation: targets prior dynamic PF methods’ weakness at long-range temporal dependencies.
- Strength — technically sound: leverages conjugacy for closed-form updates while learning neural parameterizations; autoencoder objective is a reasonable surrogate.
- Strength — empirical breadth and interpretability: synthetic tests and qualitative factor-trajectory analyses across varied datasets.
- Strength — addresses hierarchy/posterior-collapse issues with ladder-style connections and pruning discussion.
- Weakness — factual error: incorrect description of modeling nonnegative real-valued data; should reference the Poisson Randomized Gamma (PRG) construction and correct citations.
- Weakness — evaluation metrics: MSE/PMSE insufficient; add held-out predictive log-likelihood, per-token perplexity (text), normalized predictive deviance, and ranking metrics (MAP/NDCG) for recommendation-like tasks.
- Weakness — hierarchy baselines: need ablations with recurrent prior but N=1 and comparisons across depths (N=1,2,3) under identical budgets to justify multiple layers.
- Weakness — LSTM baseline: LSTMs use different likelihoods; add an RNN-Poisson GLM (Poisson output with log link) trained on same loss for fair comparison and clarify LSTM limitations.
- Weakness — reporting: Table 2 should bold best per-row results, include standard errors over runs, and report wall-clock time and compute budgets for all methods.
- Weakness — notation/derivation issues: fix Eq.7 (repeat φ), Eq.5 (q(ψv|·) correctness), index ranges (k∈{1,…,K}), consistent shape/rate parametrization, priors for βt, βh, and exact RNN/window/α,γ positivity constraints.
- Weakness — experimental design: provide sensitivity analyses for K, window size c, ladder connections, and dataset-specific hyperparameters; report topic coherence/held-out perplexity for NIPS.
- Weakness — writing quality: numerous grammatical errors and typos reduce clarity and must be corrected.
- Quality assessment: technically solid and original combination, but needs clearer notation and stronger probabilistic evaluations and ablations.
- Clarity assessment: below conference standard due to grammar and ambiguous equations; fix wording and algorithmic details to meet standards.
- Originality: novel integration of deep hierarchical RNN priors with conjugate PF and a probability autoencoder training loop.
- Significance: potentially impactful for sequential count modeling if validated with stronger baselines and probabilistic metrics.
- Specific language corrections: multiple example typos provided (e.g., “unit the data” → “units of the data,” “PFA factorized” → “PFA factorizes,” “Natrually” → “Naturally”).
- Pros for acceptance: strong idea addressing long-range dependence, elegant conjugate VI + neural parameterization, promising results and interpretability.
- Cons for acceptance: writing/notational issues, missing key baselines/ablations, incomplete probabilistic evaluation, and factual PRG error.
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on correcting factual error, improving clarity, adding probabilistic metrics (perplexity/log-likelihood), marking best results and standard errors in tables, and including N=1 vs deeper ablations plus an RNN-Poisson baseline.
