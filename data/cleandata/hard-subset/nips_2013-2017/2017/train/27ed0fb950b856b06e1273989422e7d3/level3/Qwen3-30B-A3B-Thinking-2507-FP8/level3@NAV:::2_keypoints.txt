- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) combining Poisson factor analysis with recurrent neural networks to capture long-range dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Represents implicit distributions over count vectors with gamma distributions; neural networks approximate gamma shape parameters using prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) information.  
- Uses variational inference with neural networks and a loss based on expectations of latent variables to reduce sampling variance.  
- Addresses long-dependency problems through a deep latent structure, a noteworthy contribution.  
- Empirically outperforms baselines (PGDS, LSTM, shallow PFA) on synthetic and real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA).  
- Demonstrates interpretability via factor activation patterns that capture meaningful temporal relationships.  
- Manuscript needs significant polishing, including fixing critical typos (e.g., Equation 10).  
- Assumption of fixed gamma scale parameters is unexplained and requires detailed justification and analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: only a two-layer model is shown with no comparisons to single-layer or deeper architectures.  
- Missing hyperparameter analyses (window size, number of factors) weaken experimental conclusions.  
- Paper should include a clear graphical model illustration (similar to Chung et al. 2015 Fig. 1) and discuss recent related work (Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017).  
- Core idea is promising and technically sound, but incomplete presentation and limited experiments reduce the paper’s impact.
