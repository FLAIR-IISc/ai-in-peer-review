- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) using RNNs within Poisson factor analysis to model sequential count vectors and long-range dependencies.  
- Uses variational inference with gradient-based learning for neural network parameters to handle inference challenges.  
- Novel combination of RNNs and Poisson factor analysis addressing long-dependency limitations of prior methods like PGDS.  
- Sound theoretical motivation building on Bayesian factor analysis and deep models; Gamma latent variables are appropriate for Poisson framework.  
- Potentially significant impact for recommendation systems, text and time-series analysis due to interpretable latent factors.  
- Manuscript execution and presentation are rushed, lacking sufficient detail and containing demonstrable errors.  
- Critical typo in Equation 10 undermines confidence in technical accuracy.  
- Fixing Gamma scale parameters is a significant and unjustified limitation requiring detailed discussion and analysis.  
- Clarity issues: notation is dense and the paper needs a clear graphical illustration of the full model.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: only two-layer results shown with no comparison to single-layer or deeper variants.  
- Missing systematic hyperparameter analysis (window size, number of factors K) and arbitrary/unsupported choices of K.  
- Related work is inadequately discussed; must cite and compare to Zhou et al. (2016) and Cong et al. (2017).  
- Mean-field variational inference limitations (pruning) are noted but not addressed or analyzed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, solid theoretical framework, and promising (though limited) experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: technical errors, unjustified modeling choices, insufficient experiments, poor clarity, and missing related work.  
- Overall assessment: interesting but not ready for publication; recommend rejection with invitation to revise and resubmit.  
- Required revisions suggested: fix Eq. 10, justify/analyze Gamma scale choice, expand experiments and hyperparameter sweeps, add detailed model diagram, discuss recent related work, and thoroughly proofread.
