- Introduces DDPFA: a deep hierarchical extension of Poisson Factor Analysis using RNN-parameterized implicit temporal priors for sequential count data.
- Novelty/significance: RNN-based temporal priors within a hierarchical PFA address limitations of first-order Markov assumptions and enable modeling long-range dependencies.
- Inference: hybrid approach combining coordinate-ascent mean-field variational inference for latent variables with gradient-based optimization of neural network parameters using a VAE-inspired loss.
- Technical quality: model formulation and inference strategy are well-motivated; authors acknowledge and address pitfalls like pruning via architectural choices (ladder structure).
- Empirical results: DDPFA generally outperforms PFA and PGDS on synthetic and real datasets and provides interpretable learned factors (Figures 2–3).
- Main clarity issue: paper contains many grammatical errors, typos, and awkward sentences that impede comprehension; needs thorough proofreading.
- Evaluation metric concern: primary reported metrics (MSE/PMSE) are insufficient for probabilistic count models; should report perplexity or test-set log-likelihood.
- Ablation missing: no controlled comparison against a shallow (single-layer RNN) variant to isolate the benefit of the proposed deep hierarchy.
- LSTM comparison caveat: LSTM is a discriminative point-estimate baseline, so performance differences may stem from modeling paradigm/inference, not just architecture depth.
- Minor technical inaccuracy: statement linking non-count real-valued matrices to latent counts via compound Poisson or Gamma belief networks is imprecise and should be corrected.
- Formatting suggestion: clarify result tables by bolding best-performing models per dataset to improve readability.
- Overall conclusion: promising and novel contribution, but hampered by poor writing and the need for more rigorous experimental validation (perplexity metric and ablation studies).
