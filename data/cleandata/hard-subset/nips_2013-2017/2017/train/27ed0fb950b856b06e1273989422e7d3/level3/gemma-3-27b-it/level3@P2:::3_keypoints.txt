- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that augments Poisson Factor Analysis with recurrent neural networks to capture short- and long-term dependencies in sequential count data.
- Uses variational inference to learn latent variables and neural network parameters, building on Bayesian factor analysis and deep learning.
- Well-motivated positioning relative to prior work (e.g., PGDS, temporal Dirichlet processes) and correctly identifies their limitations in modeling long-range dependencies.
- Technically sound combination of PFA and RNNs with a reasonable variational inference procedure and loss formulation.
- Addresses a relevant and challenging problem with applications in recommendations, text analysis, and time series.
- Novel contribution in integrating deep learning (RNNs) with PFA for dynamic count data.
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets, comparing against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA baselines.
- Discusses interpretability of learned latent factors as a valuable feature.
- Contains a factual error on lines 86–87 regarding the model used for non-negative numbers that must be corrected.
- Evaluation metrics are limited to MSE and PMSE; reviewer recommends reporting held-out perplexity for tasks like topic modeling.
- Insufficient baselines: missing a dynamic PF baseline with a single deep layer to justify multi-layer hierarchy benefits.
- LSTM comparison is inconclusive due to differing learning algorithms and optimization strategies; hierarchical depth benefit over well-tuned LSTM is not clearly shown.
- Manuscript has numerous grammatical errors and typos that hinder readability and require thorough proofreading.
- Table 2 formatting lacks clear indication of best-performing results (e.g., bolding the best entries).
- Ladder architecture and claimed benefit for addressing "pruning" need clearer explanation and stronger justification.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with promising experimental results and interpretability potential.
- Arguments for rejection: factual and grammatical errors, weak evaluation/baselines, unclear justification for deep hierarchy, and unresolved pruning issue.
- Overall assessment: promising work recommended as weak accept conditional on fixing factual and grammatical errors, enhancing baselines and evaluation, and better justifying hierarchical design.
