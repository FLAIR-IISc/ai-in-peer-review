- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs to model sequential count vectors via variational inference.  
- Evaluated on four synthetic and five real-world datasets, compared to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  
- Main aim: capture long-range temporal dependencies in count time series using a deep dynamic structure.  
- Strength: clear problem introduction and motivation for addressing limitations of existing models.  
- Strength: DDPFA has a clear theoretical foundation.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation on both synthetic and real datasets.  
- Weakness: Simplified RNN-like structure is not fully justified or motivated.  
- Weakness: Model equations and parameterizations are incompletely specified, reducing clarity and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics (MSE and PMSE) are inappropriate for Poisson models; predictive log-likelihood should be reported.  
- Weakness: Experiments use too small a number of latent factors K; larger K should be tested.  
- Weakness: Writing quality is poor with many typos, grammar issues, and unclear sentences.  
- Originality: Novel application of neural networks to Poisson Factor Analysis, but neural temporal modeling itself is not new; related work discussion is insufficient.  
- Significance: Potentially useful for time-series count analysis but requires revisions before publication.  
- Recommended revisions: justify RNN design, fully specify model math and parameters, report predictive log-likelihood, try larger K, and substantially improve writing.
