- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factor Analysis (DDPFA): extends Poisson Factor Analysis with hierarchical RNNs to model long-range temporal dependencies in count sequences.  
- Technically solid model formulation with variational inference and meaningful extension over prior dynamic PF methods.  
- Core innovation: hierarchical neural network representing implicit distributions over latent factors to capture long-time dependencies.  
- Critical factual error: paper incorrectly refers to a "Poisson Randomized Gamma" distribution instead of the Poisson-Gamma distribution.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks held-out perplexity, an important and standard metric for count data and topic models.  
- Comparison with LSTM is informative but not definitive because learning algorithms differ (variational inference vs. BPTT).  
- Missing crucial baseline: a dynamic PF with a single-level deep architecture to justify multi-level hierarchy benefits.  
- Results are competitive on multiple datasets, and the model provides interpretable latent structure.  
- Significance is promising for sequential count-data applications (recommendation, text, event prediction) but weakened by methodological and evaluation gaps.  
- Paper contains numerous grammatical errors and typos that hinder readability and professionalism.  
- Table 2 needs formatting improvements (e.g., bold the best results) to enhance clarity.  
- Pros: strong technical contribution, well-motivated hierarchical structure, good empirical performance, appropriate inference method.  
- Cons: factual distribution error, missing baseline and perplexity metric, many grammatical issues, table formatting problems, and inconclusive LSTM comparison.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form due to critical errors and omissions preventing NIPS-quality acceptance.  
- Required fixes recommended: correct the distribution error, add single-level baseline, include held-out perplexity, fix grammar/typos, and improve table formatting.
