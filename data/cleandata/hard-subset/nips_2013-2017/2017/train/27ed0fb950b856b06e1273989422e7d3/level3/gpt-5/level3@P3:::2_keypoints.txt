- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): RNN-parameterized Gamma shape parameters for time-varying Poisson factor scores in a deep ladder hierarchy.  
- Relates to deep Poisson/gamma belief networks and recurrent latent-variable models (distinct from PGDS by nonlinear RNN transitions over Gamma shapes).  

Strengths:
- Novel idea: using RNNs to parameterize implicit conditional Gamma distributions to capture complex long-term dependencies while preserving conjugacy.  
- Clear modeling intuition: top-down (α) and bottom-up (γ) shape aggregation in a ladder-style deep latent model.  
- Training stability: updates network parameters from expectations (probability autoencoder) to reduce variance and improve efficiency versus sampling-based VI.  
- Empirical promise: often outperforms PFA and PGDS on synthetic and real datasets, especially for long-dependency regimes.

Weaknesses:
- Technical clarity issues: notational inconsistencies, missing definitions, and critical typos (e.g., Eq. 10) that impede understanding.  
- Heuristic objective: the “probability autoencoder” loss lacks clear ELBO connection or theoretical justification.  
- Fixed Gamma scales: learning only shapes (fixed rate/scale) limits flexibility, affects dispersion/identifiability, and needs analysis or ablations.  
- Limited experiments: sparse ablations (depth, window size c, number of factors K, hyperparameters) and only a two-layer focus; deeper variants and comparisons missing.  
- Missing diagram: no full graphical/ unrolled RNN plate diagram to illustrate model and recognition/prior networks.

Quality and reproducibility:
- Conceptually sound but training objective and implementation details (optimizer, learning rates, initialization, runtime) are under-specified, reducing reproducibility.  

Clarity:
- Generally understandable but requires thorough proofreading, unified notation, symbol definitions, and clear boundary/window handling for RNNs.

Originality:
- The RNN-parameterized Gamma-shape ladder scheme is novel and distinct from prior Poisson/gamma and VRNN approaches.

Significance:
- Potentially useful for practitioners modeling sequential counts if stabilized and better validated; current evidence limited by missing ablations and objective justification.

Key suggestions:
- Add a complete graphical model and unrolled RNN diagram (prior and recognition) for clarity.  
- Fix typos and unify notation (α/β vs shp/rte; define aggregated sums and indices).  
- Justify or analyze the fixed-scale design and provide ablations (shape-only vs shape+rate vs mean/dispersion).  
- Expand experiments: single-layer vs deeper models (3–4 layers), ablate window size c, K, hidden sizes, compare NB-link and recent deep count models, and report runtime/memory and variance reduction evidence.  
- Strengthen related work discussion (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016, Cong et al. 2017) and clarify distinctions.

Minor comments/questions:
- Clarify boundary handling for t < c (padding/causal masking).  
- Explain identifiability and roles of Λ and Ψ relative to ΦΘ, and any constraints/normalizations.  
- Provide pseudocode for alternating MF-VI and network updates and specify optimizer/batch/stop criteria.  
- Report predictive calibration (e.g., predictive log-likelihood) in addition to MSE/PMSE.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel mechanism for long-term dependencies in count data, preserves conjugacy for efficient VI, and shows promising empirical results.

Cons against acceptance:
- Heuristic objective lacking variational bound, insufficient ablations/experimental scope, clarity/typo issues, and unanalyzed fixed-scale assumption.

Overall recommendation:
- Weak accept conditional on fixing clarity/typos, adding a comprehensive model diagram, and including key ablations (depth, window size, shape vs rate); otherwise weak reject.
