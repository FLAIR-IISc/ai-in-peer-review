This paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), a dynamic count model that marries Poisson factorization with a deep recurrent architecture. The core idea is to represent implicit conditional distributions over count factors via Gamma distributions whose shape parameters are produced by RNNs. A “prior” RNN aggregates information from higher layers (long-range structure) while a “posterior” RNN aggregates information from lower layers (data-driven signals), yielding a probability autoencoder–style training loop. Variational updates use mean-field posteriors for conjugate Gamma–Poisson components; the neural nets are trained against expectations of latent variables, which reduces sampling variance and runtime. Experiments on synthetic and five real datasets compare against PGDS, PFA, and LSTM.

Relation to prior work: The approach is related to VRNN-style implicit priors for sequences (e.g., Chung et al., 2015) but tailored to count data via Gamma–Poisson structure. It also connects to deep Poisson factorization and gamma belief networks (e.g., Zhou et al., 2016; Cong et al., 2017), which should be discussed more explicitly.

Strengths
- Addresses long-range temporal dependence in dynamic count models with a principled, interpretable Gamma–Poisson construction.
- Clever use of RNNs to produce Gamma shapes while keeping scales fixed enables closed-form mean-field updates and efficient training via expectations.
- Empirical results suggest gains over PGDS/PFA/LSTM on several datasets; qualitative analyses are interesting.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Quality: The learning objective for the RNNs uses squared-error terms rather than a clear ELBO contribution; please clarify the theoretical link to variational inference and discuss identifiability when scales are fixed. Provide ablations with learnable scales, or analysis showing why fixing β is beneficial.
- Clarity: The manuscript reads rushed; notation is inconsistent and there are critical typos (e.g., Eq. 10). Please proofread and unify subscripts/superscripts. Add a full graphical illustration of the model and inference flow (akin to Fig. 1 in Chung et al., 2015) to disambiguate ffeed/fback, α/γ, and windowing.
- Originality: Position the work relative to deep Poisson factorization and gamma belief networks (Zhou et al., 2016; Cong et al., 2017) and recent dynamic PFAs.
- Significance/Empirics: Experiments are limited to a two-layer model. Add comparisons to single-layer and deeper variants; analyze window size c, number of factors K, and the effect of Λ/Ψ; report runtime and variance/reliability metrics.

Pros for acceptance
- Novel and practical solution to long-dependency in dynamic count models
- Interpretable and computationally efficient inference

Cons for acceptance
- Clarity issues and typos hinder reproducibility
- Limited ablations/hyperparameter analysis; missing related work
- Objective/principled VI connection needs tightening
