- Introduces DDPFA: extends Poisson Factor Analysis with recurrent nets for short-term and deep structure for long-term dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Uses variational inference for latent estimation and gradient descent to train neural network parameters.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real datasets, showing promising predictive and fitting performance.  
- Builds on prior Bayesian factor analysis and recent deep probabilistic models, but novelty within that landscape is unclear.  
- Strength: Combining PFA with RNNs to model temporal dependencies is a reasonable and potentially valuable idea.  
- Strength: Variational inference provides a scalable alternative to MCMC for this model.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation across synthetic and real data is a positive aspect.  
- Strength: Exploring deep hierarchical structure to capture long-term dependencies is worthwhile.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks clarity and technical soundness in key areas, undermining the core contribution.  
- Weakness: Equation 4 does not implement a true RNN—hidden states don’t depend on previous same-layer time steps; this simplified structure needs justification.  
- Weakness: Critical model equations and parameterizations are missing (e.g., distributions for θ_t and Gamma parameters).  
- Weakness: Calling explicit Gamma distributions “implicit” (Eqs. 9–10) is incorrect and misleading.  
- Weakness: Motivation is vague (e.g., “fanatical data and disaster data”) and explanations of objectives/loss (Eq. 15) are unclear.  
- Weakness: Evaluation uses inappropriate metrics (MSE/PMSE) for probabilistic factor models; predictive log-likelihood should be reported.  
- Weakness: Experimental scope limited—number of latent factors K seems too small given dataset dimensionality.  
- Weakness: Writing quality is poor with many typos, grammatical errors, passive voice, and awkward phrasing.  
- Weakness: Figures problematic (e.g., unreadable caption for Fig. 4) and need fixing.  
- For acceptance: Core idea is promising and the paper attempts reasonable empirical evaluation.  
- Against acceptance: Major clarity, technical, evaluation, and writing issues; simplified RNN needs strong justification.  
- Overall assessment: Idea has potential but paper is not ready for NIPS—recommend reject and substantial revision (clarify math, fix evaluation, proofread).
