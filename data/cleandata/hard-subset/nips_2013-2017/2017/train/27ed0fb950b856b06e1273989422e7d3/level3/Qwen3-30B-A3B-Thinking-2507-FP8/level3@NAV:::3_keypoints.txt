- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) using RNNs to capture long-range temporal dependencies in dynamic Poisson factorization.
- Builds on PFA and PGDS with a hierarchical deep latent structure to model complex long-term dynamics.
- Uses variational inference for parameter learning and shows improved performance on synthetic and real-world count data.
- Technical motivation and model architecture are strong and well-justified.
- Significant factual error: incorrectly refers to a "Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution" but actually uses the standard Poisson–Gamma conjugate; must be corrected.
- Experimental evaluation should emphasize held-out perplexity as the primary metric alongside MSE/PMSE.
- Table 2 needs formatting improvements (best results bolded) for clarity.
- Authors should include a single-level deep dynamic PF baseline to justify benefits of the multi-level hierarchical design.
- Comparisons with LSTM are inconclusive due to differing learning algorithms and lack of appropriate baselines.
- Manuscript contains numerous grammatical errors and typos that impede readability and credibility.
- Paper makes a meaningful contribution with potential applications in recommendation systems, text analysis, and time series.
- Core idea is promising but the manuscript requires substantial revisions to fix technical inaccuracies, improve experiments, and correct language before publication.
- Model interpretability and performance on real-world datasets (EBOLA and ASP) are strengths but are currently undermined by presentation and technical flaws.
