This paper proposes a novel model, Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), for analyzing sequential count vectors. The model extends traditional Poisson Factor Analysis by incorporating a deep neural network structure to capture long-range temporal dependencies. The authors provide a thorough technical description of the model, including the variational inference method used for learning the neural network parameters.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors adequately address the limitations of prior dynamic Poisson factorization works in capturing long-range dependencies. The technical content is strong, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the model and its components. The experimental evaluation is also thorough, with a comparison of the proposed model to several baselines, including Poisson-Gamma Dynamical System (PGDS), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), and traditional Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA).

However, there are several areas for improvement. Firstly, the manuscript contains a factual error on lines 86-87, where the authors incorrectly state the model used for modeling nonnegative numbers. Secondly, the experimental reporting could be improved by emphasizing held-out perplexity as a stronger evaluation metric alongside Mean Squared Error (MSE) and Predicted Mean Squared Error (PMSE). Additionally, Table 2 requires formatting improvement, with the best results marked in bold.

Furthermore, the authors should consider including a baseline of dynamic PF with a single-level deep architecture to justify the benefit of multiple hierarchy levels. The comparison with LSTM is informative but not definitive, as the learning algorithms differ and do not fully justify the hierarchical depth. Finally, the paper requires a thorough proofread to correct numerous grammatical errors and typos that make the paper harder to read.

In terms of the criteria described in the Qualitative Evaluation section, the paper scores well on Quality, as the technical content is strong, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the model and its components. The paper also scores well on Originality, as the proposed model is novel and extends traditional Poisson Factor Analysis in a meaningful way. However, the paper could be improved in terms of Clarity, as the writing is sometimes unclear, and the paper requires a thorough proofread to correct grammatical errors and typos. Finally, the paper scores well on Significance, as the proposed model has the potential to be widely applicable in various domains, including recommendation systems, text analysis, and network analysis.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel model that extends traditional Poisson Factor Analysis in a meaningful way.
* The technical content is strong, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the model and its components.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough, with a comparison of the proposed model to several baselines.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The manuscript contains a factual error on lines 86-87.
* The experimental reporting could be improved by emphasizing held-out perplexity as a stronger evaluation metric.
* The paper requires a thorough proofread to correct numerous grammatical errors and typos.
* The comparison with LSTM is not definitive, and the authors should consider including a baseline of dynamic PF with a single-level deep architecture.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should correct the factual error, improve the experimental reporting, and proofread the paper to correct grammatical errors and typos.
