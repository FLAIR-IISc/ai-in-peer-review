- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): Poisson factorization augmented with a deep, RNN-parameterized temporal hierarchy for sequential count vectors.  
- Models counts with factors Φ, temporal scores Θ, and scalings Ψ and Λ, and introduces latent ladder variables h(n)t to capture long-range dependencies.  
- Uses a two-stage inference/training: mean-field variational updates for PF variables (with auxiliary counts xvtk) and RNN parameter learning via a “probability autoencoder” loss.  
- Demonstrates improved MSE/PMSE and interpretable, longer-lasting factor activations on synthetic and five real datasets versus PGDS, LSTM, and static PFA.  
- Novelty: combines PF with a deep ladder-like temporal hierarchy whose conditionals are RNN-parameterized and couples conjugate VI with neural learning.  
- Strength: addresses short-range limitation of prior dynamic PFs by introducing a deep, flexible temporal hierarchy.  
- Strength: derives practical conjugate mean-field updates and integrates them with neural parameter learning.  
- Strength: empirical gains across diverse datasets and interpretable temporal factors.  
- Strength: acknowledges hierarchical VAE issues (pruning) and adopts a ladder-like architecture to mitigate them.  
- Major weakness: the probability autoencoder losses are heuristic and not shown to form a coherent ELBO; unclear whether RNN training biases mean-field inference.  
- Evaluation concern: uses scale-dependent MSE/PMSE; should report held-out predictive log-likelihood/perplexity and ranking metrics (MAP/NDCG) where appropriate.  
- Ablation missing: need comparisons to single-layer RNN prior and depth-1 hierarchical variants, plus performance vs. number of layers N and window size c.  
- Baseline concern: LSTM baseline optimizes a different objective; should compare to recurrent Poisson factorization and non-Markovian dynamic topic models.  
- Identifiability/scaling: multiplicative positivity of Φ, Θ, Λ, Ψ induces scaling indeterminacies; recommend normalization/constraints and report post-processing for interpretability.  
- Complexity/scalability: storing xvtk for all v,t,k is expensive; discuss sparse updates, memory/time complexity, and report runtimes for all methods.  
- Experimental protocol: justify K per dataset, provide sensitivity analyses, ensure hyperparameter tuning parity and report variability (std/CI) over runs.  
- Clarity/notation: ambiguous definitions (Gamma parameterization, ffeed/fback, βh vs βθ), missing architecture/training details, and request pseudocode for two-stage training.  
- Factual correction: misstatement about modeling nonnegative reals—should reference the Poisson randomized Gamma (PRG) distribution and correct citations.  
- Presentation issues: Table/Figure readability, highlighting best results, and numerous grammatical errors/typos requiring proofreading.  
- Qualitative assessment: PF component and mean-field updates are sound, but neural training departs from principled ELBO-based optimization, lowering confidence without likelihood evidence.  
- Originality & significance: novel and potentially impactful if validated with stronger metrics and ablations; current evidence promising but incomplete.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important limitation of dynamic PF, shows consistent empirical improvements, and offers a flexible, interpretable framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of likelihood-based evaluation and ablations, heuristic neural objective without probabilistic justification, and clarity/presentation issues.  
- Overall recommendation: promising idea with good empirical signs, but needs theoretical justification of the training objective, likelihood-based evaluations, ablations, and improved clarity to be a strong contribution.
