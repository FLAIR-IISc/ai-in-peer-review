- Introduces DDPFA: a novel model combining Poisson Factor Analysis with a simplified RNN and deep hierarchical structure for sequential count data.  
- Uses variational inference with an additional optimization step for neural network parameters.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real datasets, showing superior prediction and interpretability versus PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  
- Strength: addresses a key limitation by modeling complex temporal dependencies—novel and significant contribution.  
- Strength: experimental validation across diverse datasets demonstrates robustness and interpretability.  
- Strength: hierarchical design captures long-term dependencies and yields interpretable latent factors.  
- Weakness: technical clarity issues—Gamma parameterization (Eq.4), theta distribution (Eq.3), and loss (Eq.15) are poorly explained.  
- Weakness: misleading claim about “implicit distributions” despite Eqs.9–10 explicitly defining Gamma distributions.  
- Weakness: evaluation metrics (MSE, PMSE) are inadequate for Poisson models; predictive log-likelihood should be reported.  
- Weakness: experimental design uses too small K (e.g., K=3); K should be much larger (suggested ≥100) for meaningful results.  
- Weakness: poor writing quality (excessive passive voice, typos, grammatical errors) reduces readability.  
- Pro: promising, potentially impactful model with strong interpretability and empirical results.  
- Con: incomplete technical presentation and suboptimal evaluation/experimental choices undermine the contribution.  
- Recommendation: major revision required to improve clarity, correct inaccuracies, and enhance experiments before acceptance.
