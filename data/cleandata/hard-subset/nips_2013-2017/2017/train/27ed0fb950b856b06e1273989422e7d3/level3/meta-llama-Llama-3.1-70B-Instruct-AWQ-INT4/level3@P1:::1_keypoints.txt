- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) combining Poisson Factor Analysis with a deep network to model sequential count vectors.
- Claims deep network captures long-term dependencies and complex factor relationships beyond traditional PF methods.
- Uses variational inference to estimate latent variables and optimize neural network parameters.
- Model structure is not a true RNN: hidden states depend only on top-layer memory vectors, not previous same-layer time steps; this simplification needs justification.
- Key model equations and parameterizations are omitted or unclear (e.g., distribution generating θt in Eq.3 and details of the Gamma in Eq.4).
- Incorrect claim that some distributions are "implicit" — Eqs.9-10 are explicit Gamma distributions with closed-form conditionals and this should be corrected.
- Evaluation metrics are inappropriate for Poisson factor models; predictive log-likelihood (at minimum) should be reported.
- Experiments on real-world datasets are a strength and support empirical evaluation.
- Paper is well-motivated and provides a clear high-level explanation of the problem and proposed solution.
- Writing quality is poor with many typos, grammatical errors, and unclear sentences; paper needs revision for clarity.
- Strength: novel approach to modeling sequential count data with a deep neural structure.
- Weaknesses: not-a-true-RNN architecture, missing equations/parameterizations, incorrect distribution claims, unsuitable evaluation metrics, and poor writing.
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, clear problem framing, and real-world experiments.
- Rejection arguments: architectural justification lacking, incomplete/incorrect technical presentation, poor evaluation choices, and low writing quality.
- Recommendation: revise to justify architecture, provide complete model equations and correct distribution statements, report predictive log-likelihood, and improve writing before resubmission.
