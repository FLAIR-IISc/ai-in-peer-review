- Paper introduces DDPFA: a Poisson factorization model with deep recurrent priors to capture long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Uses hybrid inference combining mean-field variational inference for latent variables with gradient-based learning for neural network components.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real datasets, showing improved fit and prediction over PFA, PGDS, and LSTM baselines.  
- Strength: novel and significant integration of deep recurrent priors into a Bayesian PFA framework to model non-Markovian temporal structure.  
- Strength: technically sound generative model and reasonable hybrid inference scheme that builds on prior dynamic topic and deep generative work.  
- Strength: empirical validation demonstrates superior ability to capture non-local temporal dynamics and provides interpretable factor analyses.  
- Weakness: paper clarity suffers from numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrasing; needs thorough proofreading and clearer result presentation (e.g., bold best results in tables).  
- Weakness: experimental rigor gaps — missing held-out perplexity metric which is standard for probabilistic count models.  
- Weakness: missing ablation study comparing deep vs. shallow DDPFA to justify the benefit of multiple hierarchical layers.  
- Weakness: LSTM comparison is not fully informative because learning objectives differ (generative variational vs. discriminative backprop), complicating conclusions about model advantages.  
- Minor technical inaccuracy: suggestion to use compound Poisson for non-count real-valued data is questionable — Poisson Randomized Gamma is a more appropriate option.  
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance pending major revision to fix writing and strengthen experiments; reviewer willing to raise score if issues are addressed.
