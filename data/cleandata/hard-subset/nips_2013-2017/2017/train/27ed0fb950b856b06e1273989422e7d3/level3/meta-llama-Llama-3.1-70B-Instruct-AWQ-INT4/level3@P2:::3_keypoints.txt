- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), extending Poisson Factor Analysis with deep neural nets to capture long-range temporal dependencies.
- Provides a detailed technical description, including a variational inference method for learning neural network parameters.
- Well-motivated work that addresses limitations of prior dynamic Poisson factorization in modeling long-range dependencies.
- Strong technical content with a clear explanation of the model and its components.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares DDPFA to baselines (PGDS, LSTM, traditional PFA).
- Manuscript contains a factual error on lines 86–87 regarding the model used for nonnegative numbers.
- Experimental reporting should emphasize held-out perplexity as a stronger evaluation metric alongside MSE and PMSE.
- Table 2 requires formatting improvement; best results should be marked in bold.
- Should include a baseline of dynamic PF with a single-level deep architecture to justify the benefit of multiple hierarchy levels.
- Comparison with LSTM is informative but not definitive, since different learning algorithms and setups don’t fully justify hierarchical depth.
- Paper needs thorough proofreading to correct numerous grammatical errors and typos that hinder clarity.
- Qualitative scores: strong on Quality, Originality, and Significance; weaker on Clarity due to writing issues.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — fix the factual error, improve experimental reporting, add suggested baseline, and proofread.
