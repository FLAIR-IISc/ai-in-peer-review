- Introduces DDPFA: combines Poisson Factor Analysis with RNN-parameterized Gamma distributions to model complex, long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count data.
- Uses variational inference with mean-field approximations to iteratively update latent variables and neural network parameters.
- Empirically evaluated on synthetic and real datasets, showing improved prediction and fit over baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA).
- Positions work within Bayesian factor analysis and deep generative models, citing related dynamic count and deep Poisson factor methods.
- Novelty: first to combine PFA with RNN-parameterized Gamma dynamics, offering flexible modeling of temporal relationships.
- Soundness: derivations and variational inference appear theoretically reasonable and clearly presented.
- Empirical strength: consistent outperformance of baselines suggests deep structure benefits for temporal dependency capture.
- Interpretability: claims of interpretable latent factors supported by visualizations (Figures 2 and 3).
- Clarity & polish: manuscript is rushed with typos and grammatical issues; needs careful editing.
- Gamma parameterization: fixing Gamma scale parameters and only updating shapes lacks sufficient justification and sensitivity analysis.
- Limited experimentation: only two-layer model evaluated; missing comparisons to single-layer and deeper models and hyperparameter studies (window size, K).
- Model visualization: Figure 1 is helpful but needs a more detailed graphical depiction similar to prior works (e.g., Chung et al. 2015).
- Related work: introduction should better cover recent deep Poisson factor methods (Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) that use neural networks.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with strong empirical results and potential impact on recommendations, text, and time series tasks.
- Arguments against acceptance: paper lacks polish, experimental depth is limited, and Gamma parameter choice needs further justification.
- Overall: promising contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, justify Gamma choices, and expand experimental comparisons.
