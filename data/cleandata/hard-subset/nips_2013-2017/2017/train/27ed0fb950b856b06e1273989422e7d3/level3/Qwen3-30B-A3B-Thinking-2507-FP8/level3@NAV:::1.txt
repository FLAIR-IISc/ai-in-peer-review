

This paper proposes a "Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization" (DDPFA) model that extends Poisson Factor Analysis with a simplified RNN-like structure to capture temporal dependencies in count data. The authors build upon previous work on Poisson-Gamma Dynamical Systems (PGDS) and deep Poisson factorization models, aiming to improve long-term dependence modeling through hierarchical latent structures. 

The core idea has potential value, as deep structures could better capture complex temporal patterns than shallow models. However, the paper suffers from significant technical and presentation flaws that undermine its contribution. The claimed RNN structure in Eq. 4 does not implement a true recurrent mechanism (h_t^(n) depends only on top-layer vectors, not previous time steps), which requires substantial justification. The model's technical description is incomplete, omitting key parameterizations (e.g., Gamma distribution details in Eq. 4) and mislabeling explicit Gamma distributions as "implicit" (Eqs. 9-10). 

Evaluation is particularly problematic: MSE/PMSE metrics are inappropriate for Poisson models, and the paper should report predictive log-likelihood instead. The small latent factor count (K=3-10) is inadequate for real-world datasets, and the authors should test larger values (K≈100). The discussion of "fanatical data and disaster data" is vague and requires elaboration, while the connection between Figure 3 and the text (lines 224-232) is unclear. 

Writing quality is poor, with excessive passive voice, grammatical errors, and typos throughout (e.g., "high-dimensional" instead of "high-dimensional," missing articles). The variational update equations (Eqs. 11-14) should be moved to the supplement to improve flow. Figure 4's caption is unreadable, and the claim about "pruning" in Table 3 lacks sufficient justification. 

While the core idea shows promise, the paper requires substantial revision to address these technical inaccuracies, clarify model specification, adopt appropriate evaluation metrics, and improve writing quality before it could be considered for publication. The current version does not sufficiently demonstrate the claimed advantages over existing methods.
