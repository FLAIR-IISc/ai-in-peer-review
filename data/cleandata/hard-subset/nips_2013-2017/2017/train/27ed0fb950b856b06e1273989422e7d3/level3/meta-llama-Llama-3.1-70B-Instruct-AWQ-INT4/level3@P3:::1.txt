This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) model for analyzing sequential count vectors. The model extends the Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) by incorporating a recurrent neural network to capture complex temporal dependencies among the time steps. The authors also employ variational inference to estimate the latent variables and approximate the parameters in the neural networks.

The main contribution of this paper is the introduction of a deep dynamic structure to capture long-time dependencies in sequential count data. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showing improved performance compared to existing methods such as PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.

However, there are several concerns and suggestions that need to be addressed to improve the quality and clarity of the paper.

Firstly, the authors should justify the use of a simplified RNN-like structure in Equation 4, where h_t^(n) only depends on top-layer memory vectors and not on previous same-layer time steps. This simplification may lead to a loss of temporal information and affect the model's performance.

Secondly, the authors should provide more details on the model equations and parameterizations, such as the distribution generating θ_t in Equation 3 and the Gamma distribution in Equation 4. These omissions make it difficult to understand the model's underlying assumptions and mechanisms.

Thirdly, the authors claim that certain distributions are "implicit," but Equations 9-10 are explicit Gamma distributions with closed-form conditionals. This inconsistency needs to be clarified.

Fourthly, the sentence about potential weaknesses on "fanatical data and disaster data" is unclear and needs elaboration. What specific challenges do these types of data pose to the model, and how can they be addressed?

Fifthly, the subsection around lines 133-155 is unclear, particularly the definition of the "loss function" in Equation 15. The authors should provide more explanation and mathematical derivations to support their claims.

Sixthly, the evaluation metrics used in the paper, such as MSE and PMSE, are not suitable for Poisson Factorization models. The authors should report predictive log-likelihood (at least) for proper evaluation.

Seventhly, the number of latent factors K in the experiments is too small. The authors should try larger values (e.g., K ≈ 100) to demonstrate the model's scalability and robustness.

Eighthly, the discussion in lines 224-232 is not clearly connected to Figure 3 and needs clarification. The authors should provide more explanation and analysis of the results.

Lastly, the writing quality needs improvement. The authors should avoid passive voice, correct typos and grammatical errors, and rephrase unclear sentences.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel combination of PFA and RNNs to capture complex temporal dependencies. However, the authors should provide more references to related work and clarify how their model differs from existing approaches.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses a challenging problem in sequential count data analysis and provides a promising solution. However, the authors should discuss the potential impact and applications of their model in more detail.

Overall, the paper has potential, but it requires substantial clarification, corrections, and stronger experiments to be conference-ready. With revisions addressing the above concerns, the paper can provide a more convincing and robust contribution to the field.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper proposes a novel and promising approach to sequential count data analysis.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
* The paper addresses a challenging problem in the field and provides a potential solution.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper has several concerns and suggestions that need to be addressed to improve its quality and clarity.
* The evaluation metrics used in the paper are not suitable for Poisson Factorization models.
* The number of latent factors K in the experiments is too small.
* The writing quality needs improvement.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend that the authors revise their paper to address the above concerns and suggestions. With substantial revisions, the paper has the potential to be a strong contribution to the field. However, in its current form, the paper is not yet ready for acceptance.
