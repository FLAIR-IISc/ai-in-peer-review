- Paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): Poisson factorization with neural-net–parameterized multi-layer latent hierarchy and two-stage inference (mean-field VI + gradient-trained nets).
- Relates to Poisson/gamma factorization, PGDS, deep Poisson topic models, deep exponential families, and recurrent Poisson factorization.
- Strength: tackles long-range temporal dependence in sparse count data and combines PF conjugacy with neural nets to capture nonlinear patterns.
- Strength: evaluation uses multiple real datasets across domains and includes qualitative factor analysis for interpretability.
- Eq. 4 is not a true RNN—within-layer temporal recurrence is omitted; authors must justify design or provide ablation with same-layer recurrence.
- Paper omits exact parameterizations: needs explicit Gamma parameter forms (shapes/rates) for θt and h(n)t, and full specification of top-layer conditioning window.
- Clarify role and parameterization of Λ and Ψ in the Poisson rate and state all priors (shapes/rates) to avoid ambiguity.
- The term “implicit” distributions is misleading; if neural nets output distribution parameters, call it “amortized parameterization of Gamma conditionals.”
- The Probability Autoencoder objective is not a principled ELBO; surrogate L2 losses are ad hoc—authors should state and optimize a single ELBO or justify the two-stage scheme with exact objectives.
- Multinomial augmentation (Eq. 7) has index/normalization/typo errors; correct denominator and numerator and ensure consistency (ψv appears duplicated).
- Prior over Φ and identifiability concerns (scale non-identifiability among Φ, Θ, Λ, Ψ) are not addressed—explain normalization/rate-tying/ARD choices.
- Intro sentence about “fanatical data and disaster data” is unclear; rewrite to explain bursty/rare-event characteristics and limitations of fixed-decay temporal processes.
- Evaluation metrics are inappropriate: report held-out predictive log-likelihood or perplexity (and uncertainty), not MSE/PMSE on raw counts.
- Model capacity is underpowered: K is too small (K=3 or 10); run experiments with larger K (e.g., 50–200) and report sensitivity or use ARD/nonparametric shrinkage.
- Comparisons are incomplete: add deep PF baselines and recurrent PF baselines where relevant.
- Fig. 3 interpretation is weak—annotate events/time periods and show factor loadings on representative features to substantiate claims.
- Report runtime, convergence criteria, and release code/hyperparameters for reproducibility.
- Presentation: move long derivations to supplement, include a concise algorithm box summarizing the two-stage procedure.
- Figures need improvement: rewrite Fig. 4 caption, enlarge fonts/axes, and fix Fig. 1 labels and unreadable captions.
- Writing quality requires substantial editing: fix grammar, typos, passive voice, inconsistent notation, and define symbols on first use.
- Quality: core idea is interesting but technical soundness is lacking due to underspecified model and unclear objective.
- Originality: the top-down windowed temporal parameterization is a novel twist but closely related to existing deep PF and DEF work.
- Significance: potentially useful if clarified and properly evaluated; current experiments and presentation limit impact.
- Pros: timely problem, attempt to bridge deep nets with conjugate PF, multi-dataset evaluation, some interpretability.
- Cons: ad hoc objective, missing/inconsistent equations, inappropriate metrics, underpowered models, and poor writing/figures.
- Recommendation: promising but not conference-ready; recommend rejection now with guidance to: adopt ELBO-based training, fully specify model, strengthen evaluation (predictive log-likelihood, larger K, more baselines), and revise writing/figures.
