- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): a deep, neural-network–parameterized temporal extension of Poisson factorization for sequential count data with hybrid variational inference.  
- Novelty: integrating deep temporal neural structure into PFA to model long-range, non‑Markovian dependencies is a significant idea.  
- Authors acknowledge model limitations (e.g., component pruning, diminishing returns from more layers) and discuss them in the paper.  
- Ambitious and relevant goal: learning deep, interpretable representations for dynamic count data with applications in recommender systems, text, and social science.  
- Model specification is unclear: Eqs. 3–4 ambiguous about how neural nets parameterize (shape/rate) Gamma conditionals; misuse of the term “implicit distribution”; architecture is not a true RNN and needs justification.  
- Inference description is confusing: the “Probability AutoEncoder” objective (Eq.15) is ill-defined and the derivation/connection to the final loss (Eq.16) is missing.  
- Evaluation metrics are inappropriate: using MSE/PMSE is ill-suited for probabilistic count models; predictive log-likelihood on held-out data should be the primary metric.  
- Experimental setup likely under-parameterized: very small K (e.g., K=3 or 10) for high-dimensional datasets (V≫1000) is unrealistic; experiments should include larger K (e.g., 50–100).  
- Poor writing and presentation: numerous grammatical errors, unclear phrases (e.g., “fanatical data”), unreadable figure captions, and vague figure discussions hinder comprehension.  
- Suggestion — Model: rewrite Section 2 with a clear, step-by-step generative process and precise mathematical parameterizations via neural networks.  
- Suggestion — Inference: rewrite the autoencoder/inference subsection with a proper VAE-style derivation (ELBO) and clear optimization objective.  
- Suggestion — Evaluation: re-run experiments using held-out predictive log-likelihood and a wider, more appropriate range of latent factors K.  
- Suggestion — Presentation: thorough proofreading by a native speaker; move standard variational updates to an appendix to improve main-text flow.  
- Baselines: compare against other deep generative sequential models (not just discriminative LSTM), such as deep exponential-family models with suitable observation models.  
- Overall verdict: interesting and promising direction but not ready for publication — requires major revisions to clarity, technical exposition, and experimental methodology.
