This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) model for analyzing sequential count vectors. The model combines Poisson Factor Analysis with a deep neural network structure to capture complex temporal dependencies. The authors use variational inference to estimate the latent variables and optimize the neural network parameters.

The main idea of the paper is to extend traditional Poisson Factor Analysis by incorporating a deep neural network structure to model the temporal dependencies in the data. The authors argue that this approach can capture long-time dependencies and complicated relationships between factors, which is not possible with traditional methods.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. However, there are several issues with the paper that need to be addressed.

Firstly, the authors claim that their model is a deep dynamic Poisson factorization model, but the structure they propose is not a true Recurrent Neural Network (RNN). The hidden state at each time step only depends on the top-layer memory vectors and not on previous same-layer time steps. This simplification needs to be justified, and the authors should explain why this structure is sufficient for their problem.

Secondly, the paper omits key model equations and parameterizations. For example, the distribution generating θt in Eq. 3 is unclear, and the authors should provide more details about the Gamma distribution in Eq. 4.

Thirdly, the authors claim that certain distributions are "implicit," but this is incorrect. Eqs. 9-10 are explicit Gamma distributions with closed-form conditionals. The authors should correct this statement and provide more clarity on their model.

Fourthly, the evaluation metrics used in the paper are not suitable for Poisson Factor models. The authors should report predictive log-likelihood (at least) for proper evaluation.

Lastly, the writing quality of the paper is poor, with many typos, grammatical errors, and unclear sentences. The authors should revise the paper to improve its clarity and readability.
