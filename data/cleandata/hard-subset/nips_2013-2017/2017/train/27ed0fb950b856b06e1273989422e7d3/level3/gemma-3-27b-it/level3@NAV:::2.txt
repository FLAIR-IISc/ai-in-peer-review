## Review of Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model

This paper introduces the Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA), a novel approach to modeling sequential count data. The core idea – leveraging RNNs to represent implicit distributions over count vectors within a Poisson Factor Analysis framework, specifically using Gamma distributions – is a compelling attempt to address the long-dependency problem in dynamic models. By incorporating a deep structure, the authors aim to capture complex temporal relationships beyond the limitations of traditional approaches like PGDS. This is a valuable direction, as accurately modeling temporal dependencies is crucial in many real-world applications dealing with count data.

The approach of approximating Gamma shape parameters using neural networks, and updating these parameters using expectations of latent variables to improve computational efficiency, demonstrates a thoughtful design. The use of variational inference is also well-justified given the complexity of the model. The paper clearly positions itself within the existing literature, referencing relevant work on PFA, deep learning for Bayesian models, and related dynamic count models. 

However, the manuscript feels somewhat rushed. While the core idea is strong, the presentation lacks polish. Several critical typos were identified (e.g., equation 10), indicating a need for careful proofreading. The decision to fix Gamma scale parameters requires more thorough justification and analysis. While the authors acknowledge this, a more detailed discussion of its impact on the model’s flexibility and potential limitations is necessary. 

Furthermore, the experimental evaluation is limited. Demonstrating results only with a two-layer model is insufficient. Comparisons to single-layer and deeper architectures are crucial to validate the benefit of the proposed deep structure. Analysis of other hyperparameters, such as window size and the number of factors, is also missing. The graphical illustrations, while present, could benefit from a more detailed depiction of the full model, akin to the clarity offered by Fig. 1 in Chung et al. (2015). The discussion would also be strengthened by referencing recent related work on deep Poisson factor analysis (Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) in the introduction.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and potentially impactful approach to modeling dynamic count data.
*   Addresses a significant challenge – capturing long-term dependencies.
*   Combines established methods (PFA, RNNs, Variational Inference) in a creative manner.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Manuscript requires significant polishing (typos, clarity issues).
*   Limited experimental validation.
*   Lack of justification for fixed Gamma scale parameters.
*   Missing comparisons to related work and model variants.



**Overall Assessment:** While the paper presents a promising idea, the lack of thorough experimentation and the need for significant polishing prevent a strong recommendation for acceptance in its current form. I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing the outlined weaknesses. A more rigorous experimental evaluation and a clearer, more polished presentation could substantially improve the paper's contribution and impact.
