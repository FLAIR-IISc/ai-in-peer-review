- Proposes a novel approach to analyzing Bitcoin P2P anonymity, comparing trickle vs. diffusion spreading.  
- Models the Bitcoin network as a graph and an eavesdropper adversary to evaluate deanonymization probability.  
- Main technical contributions: analysis of deanonymization under trickle propagation, under diffusion propagation, and the overall P2P/eavesdropper model.  
- Uses theoretical tools (nonlinear differential equations, generalized Pòlya urns) for rigorous analysis.  
- Provides simulation results on regular trees and a 2015 Bitcoin network snapshot to validate claims.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Technical claims are well-supported; paper is original and significant for Bitcoin anonymity research.  
- Key weakness: analysis assumes a regular tree topology, which may not reflect real Bitcoin network structure.  
- Additional weakness: limited focus on estimators (first-timestamp and MLE); other estimators are not explored.  
- Suggestion: more analysis of graph irregularity effects is needed to strengthen real-world relevance.  
- Overall assessment: high quality and significance, meeting conference criteria; arguments both for and against acceptance hinge on realism of topology assumptions and estimator scope.
