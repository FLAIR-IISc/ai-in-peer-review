- Proposes DDPFA: a deep dynamic Poisson factorization model that analyzes sequential count vectors.  
- Extends Poisson factor analysis by incorporating an RNN to capture long-range temporal dependencies.  
- Uses variational inference to learn parameters, improving over MCMC and other approximations.  
- Technical content is strong with thorough discussion of model components and inference algorithm.  
- RNN-based temporal modeling is novel and captures complex temporal patterns.  
- Provides detailed comparisons with PGDS and LSTM.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world data show DDPFA outperforms existing methods, especially for long-range dependencies.  
- Deep (multi-layer) architecture improves performance significantly over shallower versions.  
- Writing quality is unclear with grammatical errors and awkwardly worded sentences, hindering readability.  
- Lacks emphasis on held-out perplexity as an important evaluation metric for generalization.  
- Needs additional baselines, e.g., single-level dynamic Poisson factorization, to justify multi-level hierarchy.  
- Comparison with LSTM is not definitive because learning algorithms differ and hierarchical depth isn’t fully justified.  
- Discussion of model limitations (e.g., pruning problem) is insufficient and should be expanded.  
- Originality: novel approach to temporal modeling in sequential count data with clear practical significance.  
- Overall contribution is significant and applicable to recommendation, text, and time-series tasks.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; Rating 8/10, Confidence 8/10.
