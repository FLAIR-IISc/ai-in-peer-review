- Proposes a novel deep dynamic Poisson factor analysis (DDPFA) that models sequential count vectors using RNNs to capture long-term dependencies.  
- Uses recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, enabling modeling of complex temporal dependence.  
- Employs variational inference to estimate latent variables and approximate neural network parameters, with clear motivation and explanation.  
- Demonstrates improved performance and interpretability on synthetic and real-world datasets versus existing methods.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and provides convincing experimental results and comparisons.  
- Strength: model can extract meaningful, interpretable factors from real-world sequential count data.  
- Manuscript appears rushed and needs polishing; several typos reported, including a critical error in equation 10.  
- Assumes fixed gamma scale parameters while only updating shape parameters; this choice requires deeper discussion and analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited—only two-layer models shown with no comparisons to single-layer or deeper architectures.  
- Lacks analysis of other hyperparameters (e.g., window size, number of factors).  
- Would benefit from a detailed graphical illustration of the full model for improved clarity (e.g., similar to Chung et al. 2015 Fig.1).  
- Related work coverage should include recent deep Poisson factor analysis (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016, Cong et al. 2017).  
- Overall contribution is significant; recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing typos, gamma assumption, expanded experiments, and improved figures.  
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 8/10.
