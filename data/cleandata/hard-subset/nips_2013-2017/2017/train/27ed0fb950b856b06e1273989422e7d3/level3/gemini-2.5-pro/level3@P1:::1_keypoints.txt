- Addresses an important problem: modeling dynamic, high-dimensional count data with complex temporal dependencies.  
- Novel idea: integrates deep, neurally-parameterized priors into Poisson factorization to capture richer dynamics than Markovian models.  
- Model formulation is unclear/incomplete: key distributional forms (e.g., p(h(n)t|·)) and neural-network parameterizations are not explicitly specified.  
- Misuse of "implicit distribution": some conditionals are explicit conjugate Gammas, so calling them implicit is misleading.  
- Model described as recurrent is misleading: dependencies connect across layers, not temporally to h(n)t−1, so it's a deep hierarchical model, not an RNN.  
- Inference explanation is confusing: the "Probability AutoEncoder" loss and its derivation are hand-wavy and lack motivation/rigor.  
- Unclear why L2-norm objective is appropriate for optimizing Gamma-prior networks; VAE connection is tenuous and under-explained.  
- Evaluation uses inappropriate metrics (MSE/PMSE) for count data; predictive log-likelihood would be the correct metric.  
- Experimental setup likely underpowered: very small K (3 or 10) is unrealistic for high-dimensional datasets (e.g., V=14036); scalability and effectiveness not demonstrated.  
- Writing and presentation are poor: grammar errors, awkward phrasing, unclear figure discussion, and bad paper flow hinder comprehension.  
- Too much standard derivation in main text (variational updates); these could be moved to an appendix to focus on novel contributions.  
- Acceptance arguments: tackles a relevant problem and presents an original, potentially significant approach.  
- Rejection arguments: poor technical exposition, flawed experimental methodology, unconvincing experimental setup, and inadequate presentation warrant major revision.
