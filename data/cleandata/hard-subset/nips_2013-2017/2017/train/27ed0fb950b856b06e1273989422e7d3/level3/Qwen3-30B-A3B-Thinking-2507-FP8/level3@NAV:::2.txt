

This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that extends Poisson Factor Analysis with recurrent neural networks to capture complex long-time dependencies in sequential count data. The authors represent implicit distributions over count vectors using gamma distributions, with neural networks approximating gamma shape parameters by combining information from prior (higher layer) and likelihood (lower layer) distributions. The method uses variational inference with neural networks trained via a loss function based on expectations of latent variables to reduce sampling variance.

The paper makes an interesting contribution by addressing the long-dependency problem in dynamic models through a deep latent structure, which appears to outperform baselines like PGDS, LSTM, and shallow PFA on both synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, etc.). The model's interpretability is demonstrated through factor activation patterns that capture meaningful temporal relationships.

However, the manuscript requires significant polishing. Critical typos (e.g., Equation 10) must be fixed, and the assumption of fixed gamma scale parameters needs detailed justification and analysis. The experimental section is notably limited: only a two-layer model is shown without comparisons to single-layer or deeper architectures, and hyperparameter analysis (window size, number of factors) is missing. The authors should include detailed graphical illustrations of the full model structure similar to Fig. 1 in Chung et al. (2015) for clarity. Additionally, the introduction should discuss recent related work, particularly Zhou et al. (2016) and Cong et al. (2017) on deep Poisson factor analysis.

While the core idea is promising and technically sound, the current presentation lacks completeness. The limited experimental scope and insufficient discussion of key design choices weaken the paper's impact. The authors should address these concerns before the paper can be considered for publication. Despite these issues, the proposed method represents a meaningful step toward capturing complex temporal dependencies in count data through deep probabilistic modeling.
