- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that extends Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs to model complex temporal dependencies in count data.  
- Novelty: combines RNNs with Bayesian PFA to parameterize priors and move beyond first-order Markov assumptions for long-range, non-linear dynamics.  
- Inference: hybrid scheme using mean-field variational updates for latent variables and gradient-based optimization for RNNs.  
- Technical approach strength: trains RNNs on expected latent values to avoid high-variance Monte Carlo gradients, improving stability and efficiency.  
- Uses a "probability autoencoder" style formulation for learning implicit distributions via feed-forward and backward networks.  
- Presentation is unclear and rushed, with dense notation and an abstract Figure 1 that fails to show variable dependencies across layers and time.  
- Paper contains confusing/contradictory statements (e.g., use of future information in updates) that need clarification—particularly whether fback is generative or only an inference network.  
- Key modeling choice of fixing Gamma scale (rate) parameters (βθ, βh) and only learning shapes is unjustified and overly constraining; implications on variance-to-mean ratio are not discussed.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: only two-layer models shown and missing crucial ablations comparing single-layer vs. deep hierarchical variants.  
- Results (Table 3) suggest adding layers yields little benefit, undermining the claimed advantage of depth without further analysis.  
- Missing sensitivity analyses for important hyperparameters (window size c, number of factors K) and computational cost trade-offs.  
- Comparisons to related deep Poisson factor models (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017) are insufficiently detailed to highlight novelty.  
- Recommendation: promising and significant core idea but not ready for publication; requires major revision with clearer presentation, justification for modeling choices, and more comprehensive experiments.
