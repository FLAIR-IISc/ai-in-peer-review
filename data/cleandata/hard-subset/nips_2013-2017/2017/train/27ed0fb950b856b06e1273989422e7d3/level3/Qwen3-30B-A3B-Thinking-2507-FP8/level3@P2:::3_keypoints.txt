- Summary: Proposes DDPFA, a deep hierarchical Poisson factorization with RNNs and variational inference to model sequential count vectors and long-range temporal dependencies.

- Strength: Technically sound extension that captures complex temporal patterns beyond shallow models.

- Strength: Well-grounded theoretically with appropriate references to Bayesian factor analysis and deep latent variable models.

- Strength: Experiments on synthetic and real data show effectiveness, especially for long-term dependencies where PGDS and LSTM struggle.

- Strength: Clear explanation of model structure and inference with sufficient mathematical formalization.

- Weakness: Factual error calling the distribution "Poisson Randomized Gamma" instead of Poisson-Gamma undermines credibility.

- Weakness: Evaluation uses only MSE/PMSE, which are not standard for count data; held-out perplexity is missing.

- Weakness: Lacks a single-layer deep baseline to justify the benefit of multiple hierarchical levels.

- Weakness: Comparison with LSTM is not sufficiently justified given differing learning algorithms and architecture depth issues.

- Weakness: Manuscript contains numerous grammatical errors and typos that reduce readability and professionalism.

- Weakness: Table 2 presentation should highlight best results (e.g., bold) for easier comparison.

- Pro arguments: Addresses a genuine limitation, yields interpretable long-term latent features, and shows clear empirical improvements.

- Con arguments: Technical error, inadequate metrics, missing baseline, poor writing, and insufficiently justified LSTM comparison.

- Recommendation: Major Revision.

- Required revisions: correct the distribution terminology; add held-out perplexity; include a single-layer deep baseline; fix grammatical errors/typos; reformat Table 2 to highlight best results.
