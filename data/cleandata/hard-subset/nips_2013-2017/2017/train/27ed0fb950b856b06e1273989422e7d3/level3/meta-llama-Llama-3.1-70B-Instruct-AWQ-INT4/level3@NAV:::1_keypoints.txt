- Proposes DDPFA: a deep dynamic Poisson factorization using neural nets to model time dependence and a variational inference algorithm for latent variables and network parameters.
- Claims the model captures long-time dependence and learns complex short-time factor relationships in sequential count data.
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear problem statement and solution description.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets show improved prediction and fit versus existing methods.
- Evaluation uses MSE and PMSE, which are unsuitable for assessing long-time dependence; predictive log-likelihood is recommended.
- Table 3 shows little improvement from increasing layers, questioning the claim that deeper structure learns complex short-time relationships.
- The use of a simplified RNN-like structure is not justified; authors should explain this design choice and its effect on performance.
- Writing quality is poor with multiple typos and grammatical errors; careful proofreading is needed.
- Paper scores well on originality and significance but poorly on quality and clarity.
- Revision suggestions: adopt metrics for long-time dependence, justify the simplified RNN, proofread, and use suitable metrics for short-time relationship evaluation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel combination of techniques, potential to advance sequential count data analysis, clear explanation of approach.
- Arguments against acceptance: inappropriate evaluation metrics, poor writing, and lack of justification for modeling choices.
