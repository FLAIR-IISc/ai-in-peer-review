- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) that integrates RNNs into Poisson Factor Analysis to model long-term dependencies in sequential count data.  
- Model detail: RNNs represent implicit gamma distributions over latent factor scores, enabling hierarchical temporal modeling.  
- Inference: Uses variational inference with coordinate-ascent updates for latent variables and neural network parameter learning via a variational-loss objective.  
- Experiments: Evaluated on synthetic and real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, ASP) and compared to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA baselines.  
- Empirical result: DDPFA shows competitive or superior prediction and fitting performance on most datasets (e.g., NIPS, EBOLA, ASP).  
- Innovation strength: Core novelty is using RNNs to model gamma shape parameters, addressing long-dependency limits of shallow dynamic models.  
- Methodological strength: Effectively combines higher-layer priors and lower-layer likelihoods through neural-network-informed variational updates.  
- Interpretability strength: Learned latent factors are interpretable and reveal meaningful temporal patterns missed by PGDS (e.g., ICEWS examples).  
- Computational strength: Variational loss reduces sampling variance and is more efficient than MCMC-style approaches.  
- Manuscript issues: Contains significant errors (e.g., incorrect indices in Eq. 10) and lacks a clear graphical model illustration, harming clarity.  
- Unjustified assumptions: Fixing gamma scale parameters (β=1) for all variables is unexplained and may reduce model flexibility.  
- Experimental gaps: Only two-layer models tested; missing comparisons to single-layer and deeper architectures (e.g., 3 layers).  
- Hyperparameter analysis weak: Ablation on window size and number of factors K is superficial and limited.  
- Result unexplained: ICEWS prediction failure—where PGDS outperforms DDPFA—is not analyzed or explained.  
- Missing related work: Fails to cite/compare with recent deep Poisson factorization methods (e.g., Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017).  
- Pruning problem: Latent-dimension pruning (dimensions becoming noise) is observed but not addressed beyond attributing it to mean-field VI.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with meaningful progress on long-time dependency modeling, but limited experiments and unresolved assumptions lower confidence.  
- Significance assessment: Moderate significance—promising idea and interpretability but limited by scope, missing validations, and pruning issue.  
- Recommendation: Reject due to critical flaws, incomplete experiments, and manuscript errors.  
- Required fixes for rebuttal: Correct typos and add a clear model diagram; justify or analyze fixed gamma scales; expand experiments (depth ablation, window/K analysis, comparisons to Zhou/Cong); discuss/mitigate pruning (e.g., auxiliary MCMC); cite and contrast recent deep Poisson factorization work.  
- Pro: Novel RNN-based implicit distribution for long-term dependence, strong empirical results on several datasets, and interpretable latent factors.  
- Con: Unjustified gamma assumption, limited depth/hyperparameter experiments, manuscript errors, missing related work, and unresolved pruning issue.
