- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): Poisson factorization with RNN-parameterized Gamma conditionals in a ladder-style deep temporal latent structure.
- Builds on Poisson factorization, PGDS, deep exponential families, and recurrent VAEs, comparing to PGDS, PFA, and LSTM on several datasets.
- Strength: Novel integration of RNNs to parameterize Gamma shape parameters, capturing short- and long-range temporal dependencies while keeping conjugacy.
- Strength: Ladder-style top-down priors and bottom-up auxiliary posteriors via two RNNs per layer helps long-dependency modeling and higher-layer utilization.
- Strength: Training against expectations reduces sampling variance and avoids difficult Gamma reparameterization.
- Strength: Empirically improves MSE/PMSE over PGDS/PFA and is competitive with LSTM; shows more persistent/overlapping factor activations qualitatively.
- Weakness: Inference objective (“probability autoencoder” losses) is under-specified, not shown to be an ELBO or globally coherent objective.
- Weakness: Fixing Gamma rates (β) and only learning shapes via RNNs is unjustified and may limit expressivity, identifiability, and uncertainty calibration.
- Weakness: Potential over-parameterization and scaling redundancies (λk, ψv, φ, θ) threaten identifiability and interpretability without normalization/shrinkage.
- Weakness: Manuscript has typos, inconsistent notation, and insufficient specification of core components (ffeed/fback, RNN architectures, windowing).
- Weakness: Limited ablation studies—insufficient comparisons across depth, window size, factor counts, λ/ψ choices, and pruning behavior.
- Weakness: Baselines incomplete; should include hierarchical/HPF, deep PF variants, augmentable gamma belief networks, and recurrent PF methods; report training time and preprocessing.
- Quality: Model design and conjugate updates are sound, but RNN learning objective lacks theoretical grounding and convergence guarantees.
- Clarity: Paper needs significant editing, precise conditional derivations, consistent symbol definitions, and full graphical/temporal plates.
- Originality: The specific combo of Poisson factorization with RNN-parameterized Gamma conditionals in a ladder-style is novel and nontrivial.
- Significance: Potentially impactful for dynamic count modeling if theoretical grounding and evaluations are strengthened.
- Suggestion: Provide a single consistent ELBO or justify the two-stage objective and connect Eqs. 15–18 to a bound (e.g., amortized VI with auxiliaries).
- Suggestion: Ablate fixed vs learned β (rates) and report effects on fit, prediction, and calibration.
- Suggestion: Add sensitivity analyses for window size c, number of layers, K, and report pruning/skip/residual strategies.
- Suggestion: Expand related work to include deep PF (Gan et al.), augmentable gamma belief networks (Zhou et al.), and recurrent PF literature.
- Suggestion: Provide full graphical model, temporal plates, and a training algorithm box with all updates and hyperparameters.
- Suggestion: Include runtime, convergence plots, random seeds, and release code for reproducibility.
- Suggestion: Add quantitative interpretability metrics (e.g., topic coherence for NIPS, event labeling for ICEWS).
- Noted typos/notation issues: Eq.7 denominator repeats φv; Eq.8 repeated Φ and unclear aggregates; Eqs.9–10 mix α/γ and need consistent Gamma parameterization; inconsistent expectation/digamma notation.
- Pros for acceptance: Interesting idea, elegant RNN use, promising empirical results.
- Cons against acceptance: Unclear learning objective, unjustified design choices, reproducibility issues, limited ablations and baselines.
- Recommendation: Leaning weak reject in current form but promising if ELBO/justification, corrected notation, and thorough ablations are provided.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
