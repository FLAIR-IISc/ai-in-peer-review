- Proposes a novel analysis of Bitcoin P2P anonymity, focusing on the 2015 transition from trickle to diffusion spreading.  
- Models the Bitcoin network as a graph and analyzes deanonymization probability under an eavesdropper adversary for both propagation rules.  
- Delivers significant technical contributions: new estimators and performance analysis on regular trees.  
- Compares estimators (first-timestamp and maximum-likelihood) and validates results with simulations on a 2015 Bitcoin snapshot.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis and clear presentation of main results.  
- Strength: significant implications for designing more anonymous P2P networks.  
- Weakness: writing is sometimes unclear and contains grammatical errors/typos.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison with existing epidemic source detection literature.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations of the proposed approach.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and significance; technically sound with well-supported claims.  
- Recommendation: accept, but require revisions to improve writing, add comparisons with related work, and expand limitations discussion.
