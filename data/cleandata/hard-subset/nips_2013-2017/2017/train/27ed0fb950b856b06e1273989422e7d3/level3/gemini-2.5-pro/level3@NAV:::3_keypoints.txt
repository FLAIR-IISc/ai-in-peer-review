- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): a deep, recurrent Poisson factorization model using variational inference to capture long-range temporal dependencies in count data.
- Novel contribution: deep hierarchical recurrent architecture extending dynamic PF models (e.g., PGDS) to model complex temporal patterns.
- Strength: addresses a known limitation of existing dynamic PF methods; technically solid, well-motivated, and well-situated in related work.
- Weakness (clarity): manuscript has numerous grammatical errors and typos that hinder readability; needs thorough proofreading and Table 2 should mark best results in bold.
- Weakness (factual accuracy): apparent error on lines 86–87—modeling of non-negative numbers corresponds to the Poisson Randomized Gamma distribution, not the stated model.
- Weakness (evaluation metrics): experimental evaluation should emphasize held-out perplexity, a more standard and robust metric for probabilistic sequence models.
- Weakness (baselines): lacks a crucial baseline—a dynamic PF with a single-level deep architecture—so benefits of multi-level hierarchy are not empirically shown.
- Weakness (LSTM comparison): comparison to LSTM is inconclusive because models use different learning algorithms, preventing a definitive assessment of architectural advantages.
- Overall: promising core idea but requires major revisions in writing, factual detail, and experimental validation before publication.
