This paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), a hierarchical Poisson factor model that introduces RNN-based implicit conditional priors to capture both short-range and long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count data. The model preserves Gamma–Poisson conjugacy for local variables, performs mean-field VI for latent factors, and trains neural-network parameters via a probability autoencoder objective that matches forward/backward conditional moments. The work is positioned against dynamic Poisson factorization (e.g., PGDS) and deep latent variable models (deep exponential families, deep PFA), and compares empirically to PGDS, PFA, and LSTM on synthetic and real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, ID, ASP).

Quality: The modeling contribution is technically solid, and the combination of hierarchical depth with RNN conditionals addresses known limitations of first-order Markov dynamics for long-range dependence. However, there is a factual error: lines 86–87 misstate the distribution for modeling nonnegative reals; the correct object is the Poisson Randomized Gamma (PRG) distribution (see Zhou et al., NIPS 2012; TPAMI 2015). The learning objective in Eqs. (15–18) is not presented as a valid ELBO; please clarify its probabilistic justification, the role of the auxiliary variables, and convergence guarantees. Reproducibility would benefit from code and precise data splits.

Clarity: The manuscript is hard to read due to numerous grammatical errors and typos; a thorough language edit is necessary. Examples to correct include: “unit the data,” “Noticing the lack of capturing ..,” “PFA factorized a count matrix,” “other data with different pattern long-time dependence ..,” “Examples for this including ..,” “shows excellent results on predict and ..,” “However, it is rough for them ..,” “V-dimesnion sequentially ..,” “K-dimension latent variable ..,” “due to the including of feature-wise,” “recommend systems.”

Originality: The idea of using neural conditionals within a Gamma–Poisson deep hierarchy for temporal counts is novel relative to PGDS and deep PF variants; the hierarchical temporal design is a meaningful extension.

Significance: Results are promising, but the evaluation could be stronger. For count models, held-out predictive log-likelihood/perplexity should be reported and emphasized alongside MSE/PMSE. Table 2 should boldface the best results. The LSTM comparison is informative but not definitive given different likelihoods/training. Crucially, include a baseline with a single-level (no hierarchy) dynamic PF using the same RNN transition to isolate the benefit of depth; also provide ablations over depth and window size.

Pros:
- Addresses long-range dependence with a principled count model.
- Strong technical construction leveraging conjugacy and RNNs.
- Broad empirical evaluation and interpretability claims.

Cons:
- Factual PRG error; unclear learning objective derivation.
- Missing perplexity; limited baselines/ablations to justify hierarchy.
- Significant clarity/grammar issues; minor formatting (Table 2) needs fixing.
