This paper proposes a novel Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) for analyzing sequential count vectors. The model uses recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions, capturing complex long-time dependence among time steps. The authors provide a clear and well-organized introduction to the problem, discussing the limitations of existing methods and the benefits of their approach.

The technical soundness of the paper is good, with a well-supported theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a detailed explanation of their model, including the use of variational inference and neural networks to approximate the gamma shape parameters. The experiments are thorough, with four multi-dimensional synthetic datasets and five real-world datasets used to evaluate the performance of the proposed model.

However, there are some areas for improvement. The manuscript appears a bit rushed and needs polishing, with some critical typos (e.g., equation 10) that must be fixed. Additionally, the authors assume fixed gamma scale parameters and update only shape parameters, which requires a more detailed discussion and analysis. The experiments are limited, with only two-layer models shown, and missing comparisons to single-layer and deeper models, as well as analysis of other hyperparameters (window size, number of factors).

The originality of the paper is high, as it proposes a novel combination of techniques to address the long-dependency problem in dynamic models. The authors provide a clear explanation of how their work differs from previous contributions and adequately reference related work.

The significance of the paper is also high, as it addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The results are important, and other researchers and practitioners are likely to use these ideas or build on them. The paper provides a unique solution to the long-dependency problem in dynamic models, which is a significant contribution to the field.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, but some sections could be improved. The authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results, but some explanations could be more detailed. For example, the use of ladder-like architecture could be explained more clearly.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. With some revisions to address the areas mentioned above, it has the potential to be a top-rated paper.
