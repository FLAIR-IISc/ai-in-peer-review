- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): RNNs + Gamma within Poisson Factor Analysis to model long-range dependencies in sequential count data.
- Novelty: uses neural networks to approximate Gamma shape parameters and updates them via expectations for computational efficiency; employs variational inference.
- Well-situated in literature but should reference recent deep Poisson factor analysis work (Zhou et al. 2016; Cong et al. 2017).
- Manuscript presentation is rushed with critical typos (e.g., Eq. 10) and needs careful proofreading.
- Decision to fix Gamma scale parameters is insufficiently justified and requires analysis of its impact on model flexibility.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a two-layer model; lacks comparisons to single-layer and deeper variants.
- Missing hyperparameter studies (window size, number of factors) to validate robustness and design choices.
- Graphical illustrations are insufficient; recommend a clearer full-model diagram similar to Chung et al. (2015) Fig. 1.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, addresses long-term dependencies, and creatively combines PFA, RNNs, and variational inference.
- Arguments against acceptance: needs substantial polishing, broader experiments, justification for fixed scale params, and more comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form but encourage revision and resubmission after addressing weaknesses.
