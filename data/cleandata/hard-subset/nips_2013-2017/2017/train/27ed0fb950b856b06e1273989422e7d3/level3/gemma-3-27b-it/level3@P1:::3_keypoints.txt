- Proposed DDPFA: integrates recurrent neural networks into Poisson Factor Analysis to model long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count vectors, with variational inference and gradient descent for training.
- Builds on prior dynamic PFA and deep probabilistic modeling work and targets limitations from independence assumptions in traditional PFA.
- Technical soundness: model is well-motivated and logically constructed; RNN integration and variational inference are appropriate choices.
- Addresses a relevant problem: modeling sequential count data across domains (recommendation, text, time series) where long-range dependencies matter.
- Experimental validation: evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets, compared against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA, showing improved prediction and fit.
- Interpretability: yields interpretable latent factors and clear factor activation patterns (illustrated in figures).
- Factual error: paper incorrectly references a “Poisson Randomized Gamma” distribution, indicating a necessary correction and possible misunderstanding.
- Evaluation metric: relies on MSE/PMSE but should include held-out perplexity, which is more appropriate for count-data predictive evaluation.
- Insufficient baselines: missing a single-level deep PFA baseline to isolate the benefit of the recurrent component.
- Comparison with LSTM: current comparison is inconclusive because DDPFA and LSTM use different learning algorithms; needs deeper analysis to justify advantages.
- Clarity and presentation: manuscript contains many grammatical errors, typos, and awkward phrasing that hinder readability and require careful proofreading.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: paper does not investigate how sensitive performance is to hyperparameter choices, limiting insight into robustness.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach addressing a relevant problem with empirical support and interpretable representations.
- Arguments for rejection: factual error, weak baseline/evaluation design, presentation issues, and lack of hyperparameter analysis undermine the submission.
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially valuable contribution but requires correction of factual errors, stronger experiments, and improved clarity before acceptance.
- Recommendation: reject but encourage revision and resubmission after addressing the listed weaknesses.
