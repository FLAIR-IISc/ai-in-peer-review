- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) using RNNs to model implicit distributions for sequential count vectors.  
- Uses variational inference and neural networks to approximate gamma shape parameters in a principled way.  
- Claims to capture complex long-term dependencies across time steps better than prior dynamic models.  
- Well-organized introduction that clearly explains problem motivation and limitations of existing methods.  
- Strong technical soundness with supporting theoretical analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation on four synthetic and five real-world datasets demonstrating promising results.  
- Original contribution by combining deep recurrent architectures with Poisson factorization to address long-dependency issues.  
- Paper significance is high; approach is likely useful to researchers and practitioners.  
- Manuscript needs polishing and contains critical typos (e.g., equation 10) that must be fixed.  
- Authors fix gamma scale parameters and update only shape parameters; this choice needs deeper justification and analysis.  
- Experiments are limited to two-layer models; missing comparisons to single-layer and deeper architectures.  
- Lacks analysis of key hyperparameters such as window size and number of factors.  
- Some explanations (e.g., ladder-like architecture) are insufficiently detailed for non-expert readers.  
- Reproducibility is reasonable for expert readers but could benefit from more implementation details.
