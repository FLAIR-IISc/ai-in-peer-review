- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) to model sequential count data by adding a deep latent structure to Poisson Factor Analysis.  
- Core idea uses a simplified recurrent-like structure over latent layers (Eq. 4) to capture temporal dependencies, building on PGDS and prior deep PFA work.  
- Technical execution and evaluation require significant clarification and correction.  

- Strength: tackles a relevant problem (time-dependent count data) and explores integrating deep structures into Poisson factorization.  
- Strength: experiments span diverse datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA) and claim interpretable topics over time.  
- Strength: variational inference approach is reasonable for scalability.  

- Weakness (architecture): Eq. 4 is flawed—h^(n)_t depends only on top-layer vectors, not on prior same-layer timesteps, so it is not a true RNN and lacks temporal recurrence.  
- Weakness (equations): Key model equations are incomplete or inconsistent (Gamma prior undefined in Eq. 4; "implicit" distributions in Eqs. 9–10 are actually closed-form Gammas).  
- Weakness (metrics): Evaluation uses inappropriate metrics (MSE/PMSE) for counts; predictive log-likelihood for Poisson models is required.  
- Weakness (capacity): Latent factor K is unrealistically small (K=3 or 10); larger K (≈100) should be tested for realism and scalability.  
- Weakness (results): DDPFA outperforms baselines only on synthetic data and underperforms on real datasets (PGDS/LSTM often win), weakening claims of superiority.  
- Weakness (depth claim): The "deep" contribution is overstated—structure is not hierarchical and added layers give marginal gains, so long-term dependency claims are unsupported.  
- Weakness (novelty): Paper does not clearly advance state-of-the-art given related deep Poisson models and technical obscurities.  

- Clarity issue: Writing has grammatical errors, awkward phrasing, and misuse of terms (e.g., "factorize" vs. "factorization").  
- Clarity issue: Key derivations and the loss (Eqs. 15–18) are unclear or intractable as presented.  
- Clarity issue: Figures and captions are problematic (Fig. 4 caption illegible, Fig. 1 labels inconsistent).  
- Clarity issue: Undefined or erroneous references (e.g., "fanatical data") must be removed.  

- Recommendation: Paper is not suitable for acceptance in current form due to critical technical errors, invalid metrics, inadequate experiments, and poor exposition.  
- Required fixes: Correct architecture to implement a true RNN or justify simplification; define missing priors and correct derivations.  
- Required fixes: Report predictive log-likelihood (not MSE), evaluate larger K values, and compare properly to baselines.  
- Required fixes: Rewrite unclear sections, fix typos/figures, and remove unsupported claims before resubmission.
