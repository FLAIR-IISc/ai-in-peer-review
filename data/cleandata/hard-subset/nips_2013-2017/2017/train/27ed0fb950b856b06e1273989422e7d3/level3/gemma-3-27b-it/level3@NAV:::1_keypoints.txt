- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) combining a simplified RNN with Poisson Factor Analysis using variational inference and gradient descent.  
- Potentially interesting idea of merging deep learning with Poisson factorization for dynamic count data.  
- Simplified RNN (Eq. 4) unjustified: hidden state depends only on top-layer memory vectors, not previous time steps within the same layer.  
- Section 2 lacks critical parametrization details for θ_t (Eq. 3) and the Gamma parameter in Eq. 4, hindering understanding and reproducibility.  
- Incorrect claim about “implicit distributions”: Eqs. 9–10 explicitly define Gamma distributions and their conditionals are known.  
- Writing quality poor: heavy passive voice in abstract, many typos, grammatical errors, and misuse of terms (e.g., “factorize”, “Dirichlet”).  
- Specific sections unclear (e.g., lines 133–155 on the loss function and Eq. 15) and require substantial revision.  
- Vague or confusing phrasing (e.g., “fanatical data and disaster data” in lines 30–31) needs clarification.  
- Evaluation methodology flawed: MSE/PMSE inappropriate for Poisson models; predictive log-likelihood should be reported.  
- Experimental setup limited: using K=3 latent factors is too small; should explore much larger K (e.g., ≈100).  
- Unclear connection between discussion in lines 224–232 and Figure 3; Fig. 4 caption is unreadable.  
- Suggest moving variational update equations (Eqs. 11–14) to the supplement to improve flow.  
- Arguments for rejection: lack of clarity, technical weaknesses (RNN justification and inappropriate metric), insufficient experimentation, and poor writing.  
- Weak arguments for acceptance: novelty of combining deep learning with Poisson factorization and some empirical performance gains, but not convincing.  
- Overall recommendation: paper has significant flaws in clarity, technical soundness, and experiments; recommend strong rejection.
