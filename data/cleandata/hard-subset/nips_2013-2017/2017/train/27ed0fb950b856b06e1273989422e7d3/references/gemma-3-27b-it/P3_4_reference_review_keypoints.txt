- Overall assessment: Proposes a novel DDPFA combining PFA with deep RNNs but lacks rigorous validation; reviewer leans toward weak reject.
- Problem addressed: Models sequential count vectors by integrating RNNs into PFA to capture temporal evolution and long-range dependencies.
- Relation to prior work: Positions itself within deep probabilistic modeling, building on PFA, PGDS, and related deep Poisson/factor methods.
- Novelty: Combining PFA with deep recurrent networks for dynamic count data is a potentially valuable contribution.
- Model flexibility: Deep, layered architecture can capture hierarchical and nonlinear dependencies beyond shallow models.
- Theoretical foundation: Provides clear model derivation and uses variational inference suitably for model complexity.
- Datasets: Evaluated on a diverse set of synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Lack of rigorous comparisons and ablations: Missing ablation studies and sensitivity analyses to isolate benefits of depth vs. RNNs and baselines.
- Clarity and presentation issues: Key equations (e.g., Eq.11, Eq.18) and figures are poorly explained and require clearer exposition.
- Hyperparameter and experimental setup concerns: Hyperparameters and choice of K appear arbitrary with no systematic search or justification.
- Scalability concerns: No discussion of computational complexity or practicality for high-dimensional/long time-series data.
- Overstated claims: "Excellent results" claim is not fully supported; improvements are inconsistent and sometimes marginal.
- Pruning and useless layers: Identifies pruning and inactive layers but offers no mitigation strategies or remedies.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel integration idea, solid probabilistic formulation, and some promising empirical gains.
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient experimental rigor, presentation clarity, scalability discussion, and unsupported claims.
- Recommendations: Add ablation studies, systematic hyperparameter search, clearer exposition, scalability analysis, balanced results discussion, and explore fixes for pruning/layer uselessness.
- Conclusion: Interesting and potentially valuable work but requires substantial revisions; recommend weak rejection pending major improvements.
