Summary
The paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) for sequential count data. It factorizes a V×T count matrix X as Poisson(ΦΘT) with additional per-feature and per-factor Gamma variables ψv and λk to encourage sparsity/importance, and it models temporal dependencies of the factor scores Θ through a multi-layer latent hierarchy H(n) with recurrent neural networks defining “implicit” conditional Gamma distributions. Inference alternates between (i) mean-field variational updates for conjugate parts (using latent Poisson allocations xvtk) and (ii) optimizing RNN parameters via a “Probability AutoEncoder” objective that matches expectations between encoder/decoder Gamma distributions using L2 losses, rather than a standard ELBO. Experiments on synthetic datasets and five real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, Ebola, International Disaster, Sheep) compare against PGDS, PFA, and LSTM using MSE and one-step prediction MSE.

Relation to prior work: The model builds on Poisson factorization, dynamic Gamma-Poisson models (e.g., PGDS), deep latent variable models for counts (Gamma belief networks, deep Poisson factor models, deep exponential families), and recurrent models for count data. The stated novelty is a deep temporal hierarchy with RNN-defined conditional Gamma distributions and a two-stage VI-plus-autoencoder training scheme.

Strengths
- Ambition and scope: addresses long-range temporal dependence and factor interactions for high-dimensional sparse counts, a relevant NeurIPS topic.
- Uses conjugacy where available: the multinomial-Poisson augmentation and Gamma conditionals are standard and allow efficient mean-field updates for Φ, Θ, λ, ψ.
- Empirical coverage: multiple datasets, including challenging real-world time series, and explicit acknowledgment of limitations (e.g., pruning, depth effectiveness).

Weaknesses and detailed reasoning
Quality (technical soundness, support for claims)
- Inference objective is not well-founded. The “Probability AutoEncoder” replaces a principled ELBO with ad-hoc L2 distances between expectations of Gamma encoders/decoders (Eq. 16–18). This is not a bound on the marginal likelihood, offers no convergence or calibration guarantees, and breaks the variational interpretation of the two-stage procedure. Claims about “variational inference” for the RNN parameters thus lack justification.
- Generative model is unclear/invalid in places. Lower-layer conditionals depend on both past and future windows (ffeed and fback consuming t−c: t and t: t+c), which compromises causality in a directed generative model. The top layer uses only past, but intermediate layers using future information in the “prior” blur the line between prior and posterior. As written, the joint distribution is not precisely defined.
- Identifiability and scaling: With ψv, λk, θtk, and φvk all positive and multiplicatively combined, the factorization is unidentifiable without constraints/normalization. The paper does not address this, nor show that priors resolve it in practice.
- Missing ELBO and derivations. There is no explicit ELBO, no gradients with respect to network parameters under a proper objective, and no complexity analysis. Several conditionals (Eq. 8–10) are stated without derivation and have indexing/notation inconsistencies.
- Evaluation metrics and protocol: Using MSE/PMSE for count data is suboptimal; held-out log-likelihood/perplexity is standard. Train/val/test splits, hyperparameter tuning, uncertainty estimates, and significance tests are absent. Comparisons to stronger baselines (dynamic topic models, gamma belief networks, neural Hawkes/point processes, recurrent PF variants) are missing.
- The admission that additional depth often does not help (Table 3) undermines the central claim that the deep hierarchy captures long-term dependence better than shallow dynamics. The “ladder” fix is asserted but not specified or rigorously evaluated.

Clarity
- The paper is difficult to follow: numerous grammatical errors, inconsistent notation (e.g., subvectors, sums, indices), and incomplete references. Key design choices (RNN architecture, window size rationale, activation functions, rate vs shape parameterization, optimization details, stopping criteria) are insufficiently documented for reproducibility. Figures/tables referenced are not provided here, and in the manuscript they appear under-explained.
- The probabilistic semantics of ffeed/fback and the role of “auxiliary posterior” are not clearly defined.

Originality
- The idea of combining Poisson factorization with deep/recurrent structure is not entirely new (e.g., deep exponential families; deep Poisson factor models; augmentable gamma belief networks; recurrent Poisson factorization for temporal recommendation). The claimed novelty rests on the particular hierarchical RNN-driven Gamma priors and the training scheme, but these are not convincingly justified or shown to outperform established variational objectives (e.g., amortized VI with reparameterization for Gamma via implicit reparameterization or Weibull approximations).

Significance
- Potential significance is high if a principled and effective deep temporal Poisson factorization were achieved. However, due to the theoretical and empirical issues above, it is hard to conclude that the method advances the state of the art. The lack of robust baselines, proper likelihood-based metrics, and ablations reduces impact.

Pros
- Addresses an important problem (dynamic modeling of sparse counts).
- Leverages conjugacy for efficient updates where applicable.
- Empirical breadth across datasets.
- Honest discussion of limitations (pruning, depth utility).

Cons
- Unclear and arguably invalid probabilistic model (use of future in “priors”).
- Non-standard, ad-hoc training objective without guarantees.
- Missing ELBO/derivations, weak clarity/reproducibility.
- Limited/less appropriate evaluation and baselines.
- Depth benefit not demonstrated; main claim undercut.

Questions for authors
1) Can you define the full generative model precisely, ensuring causality (no future in priors), and write down the ELBO you optimize? How are RNN parameters trained under that ELBO?
2) Why not use amortized VI with reparameterization (e.g., implicit reparameterization for Gamma, Weibull approximations, or black-box VI with control variates)? What prevented you from optimizing a proper bound?
3) How do you address identifiability among ψ, λ, θ, φ? Any constraints or normalization?
4) Please report held-out log-likelihood/perplexity and compare to dynamic topic models, gamma belief networks, and recurrent Poisson factorization baselines. Provide validation-based hyperparameter selection and confidence intervals.
5) Clarify the ladder architecture and provide ablations showing its incremental effect.

Recommendation
Reject. While the problem is important and the direction is interesting, the paper in its current form lacks a coherent probabilistic objective for the deep/recurrent components, has clarity and methodological issues, and does not demonstrate clear, reproducible improvements over strong baselines with appropriate metrics.

Constructive suggestions
- Recast the model with a strictly causal prior and a single coherent ELBO; use amortized VI with proper gradients for Gamma families.
- Simplify: start with one recurrent prior on Θ (no extra layers) and demonstrate gains under log-likelihood/perplexity and forecasting metrics; only then motivate deeper hierarchies.
- Provide thorough comparisons and ablations, code, and complete derivations. Improve writing, notation, and references (include Ladder VAE, implicit reparameterization, dynamic topic models).
