- Paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): a Poisson factor model with an RNN to capture temporal dependencies, evaluated on synthetic and five real datasets with reported improvements over PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.

- Strength: Addresses an important problem in sequential count data with applications to recommender systems, text, and network analysis.

- Strength: Experimental results show consistent improvements on most datasets and provide interpretable factors (e.g., conflict patterns).

- Strength: Authors acknowledge limitations such as the "pruning" issue and limited gains from adding layers.

- Weakness: The "deep" claim is misleading—model uses a single recurrent layer and depth arises from hierarchical variational structure, not a true deep temporal architecture.

- Weakness: Insufficient theoretical justification for why the proposed approach should outperform simpler alternatives or standard RNN-based temporal models.

- Weakness: Model formulation introduces seemingly redundant parameters (λk, ψv) without clear justification or explanation of added benefit over standard PFA sparsity via Gamma priors.

- Weakness: Performance gains are modest overall and DDPFA performs worse than PGDS on the important ICEWS dataset, weakening the long-term dependence claim.

- Weakness: The pruning problem (noisy latent dimensions) is identified but no novel or evaluated solution is provided; auxiliary MCMC is mentioned but not implemented.

- Technical concern: Variational inference updates (Equations 11–14) lack sufficient derivation detail to verify correctness.

- Technical concern: The "ladder architecture" proposed to address pruning is not clearly explained or justified.

- Technical concern: Key hyperparameters (window size c=4, layer count N=2) are chosen arbitrarily with no ablation study or justification.

- Recommendation: Weak reject—paper is relevant and shows modest empirical gains but overstates novelty and lacks theoretical/technical rigor; major revisions needed (clarify depth, contrast with prior deep PFA, strengthen theory, address pruning).
