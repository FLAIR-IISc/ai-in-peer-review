Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factor Analysis (DDPFA), a model for sequential count data that combines Poisson factorization with a deep, hierarchical temporal structure. The observation model is standard Poisson factorization with Gamma priors over factor loadings and component/feature scales (φ, λ, ψ). The novelty is in modeling the temporal evolution of the factor scores Θ via a multi-layer latent hierarchy H(0:N) where, at each layer, the conditional distribution is parameterized by recurrent neural networks over a sliding window of past (and, for the auxiliary posterior, future) latent states. The prior for θt is Gamma with shape parameters produced by an RNN taking h(0)t−c:t as input, and higher layers are similarly linked, forming a deep temporal dependency. Inference uses a mean-field VI for conjugate parts (leveraging Gamma–Poisson conjugacy after introducing latent allocations), and a “Probability AutoEncoder” training stage that fits the RNN parameters via MSE losses between expectations of prior/posterior conditionals, akin to a ladder autoencoder objective rather than a strict ELBO. Experiments on synthetic data and five real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disasters, Annual Sheep Population) compare DDPFA to PGDS, PFA, and LSTM using MSE/PMSE metrics.

The work relates to: Poisson factorization for counts (BNBP/BPFA); dynamic Poisson-Gamma models including PGDS; deep Poisson factorization and Gamma belief networks; neural amortization for latent-variable models (VAE/ladder VAE); and RNN-based temporal factor models (e.g., recurrent Poisson factorization for recommendation). The paper’s core thrust is to marry deep temporal hierarchical latent variables with PF under RNN-parameterized Gamma conditionals and a bespoke training objective.

Strengths
- Ambitious, novel direction: a deep hierarchical temporal prior for count factors, with RNNs producing Gamma parameters, goes beyond shallow Markov assumptions (e.g., PGDS) and beyond single-layer recurrent PF models.
- The idea of using forward (prior) and backward (auxiliary posterior) RNNs to encourage consistency across layers/time is interesting and could enable long-range dependencies while keeping conjugacy for the PF part.
- Practical modeling touches (ψ and λ scales, Bernoulli–Poisson link) broaden applicability.
- Empirical results suggest improvements over PGDS/PFA/LSTM on several datasets, especially where long-range, complex temporal patterns matter.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Objective and theory: The “Probability AutoEncoder” replaces a principled ELBO with squared-error losses between expectations of conditional distributions. This is nonstandard and lacks theoretical justification (what is being optimized? relation to ELBO/likelihood? any bias analysis?). Without a clear variational objective or a bound, it is hard to assess correctness and convergence.
- Inference consistency: Treating Gamma shape parameters as outputs of RNNs is fine conditionally, but the paper alternates between calling these “implicit distributions” and claiming conjugate updates without clearly specifying the full joint (including amortized inference networks). The role of the γ terms (“posterior information”) added to Gamma shapes is ad hoc and not derived.
- Clarity and notation: The exposition is difficult to follow, with numerous typos, inconsistent indexing (e.g., q(h(n)tk) vs h(n)t), and underspecified conditionals (e.g., h(N) prior uses t−c−1:t−1 only). Figures/tables are referenced but not fully described. The training algorithm is not presented clearly (no pseudocode, no complexity/runtime discussion).
- Experimental evaluation: 
  - Metrics (MSE/PMSE) are limited; predictive log-likelihood/perplexity on held-out counts is a more standard measure for PF/topic models.
  - Comparisons omit strong deep baselines (deep PF with SBN/RBM, gamma belief networks with temporal coupling, recurrent PF for recommendation).
  - Hyperparameter fairness and computational budget are unclear; DDPFA seems to get substantially more training per iteration than baselines. No runtime/complexity report.
  - No ablation on the window size c, the ladder/auxiliary posterior, or the necessity of λ/ψ.
- Related work and citations: Several references are incomplete; some related lines (dynamic topic models, amortized VI for Gamma variables, implicit reparameterization) are not discussed. The paper claims issues with temporal Dirichlet process without precise citations.
- Reproducibility: Many implementation details are missing (architectures, regularization, initialization, optimization settings); no code availability.

Quality
Technically ambitious but the learning objective is not grounded in a clear likelihood-bound framework. Derivations for the hybrid VI/autoencoder approach are informal. Experimental evidence is suggestive but not rigorous.

Clarity
Below average. The paper needs substantial editing for English, notation consistency, and methodological precision. The training procedure should be laid out clearly, with an explicit objective.

Originality
Moderate to good. Combining deep hierarchical temporal priors with PF via RNN-parameterized Gamma conditionals is a fresh angle, though related to prior recurrent PF and deep PF work. The “Probability AutoEncoder” idea is unusual, but its novelty is undermined by lack of theory.

Significance
Potentially useful if stabilized and better justified, as long-range temporal modeling for counts is important. Current empirical support is promising but not decisive.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide a principled training objective. Derive an ELBO with amortized inference networks for h and θ, or justify the proposed MSE objective as a bound/surrogate. Consider implicit reparameterization for Gamma or BBVI with control variates.
- Clarify the full generative and variational model, including the auxiliary posterior networks, with precise conditional forms and a coherent factorization.
- Add predictive log-likelihood evaluations, calibration metrics, and ablations (depth, window size, ladder vs non-ladder, effect of λ/ψ, with/without backward RNN).
- Compare against stronger baselines: deep PF (SBN/RBM), gamma belief networks, recurrent PF for recommendation, dynamic topic models with RNN priors.
- Improve writing, fix notation, complete references (years/venues), and include algorithm pseudocode and complexity/runtime analysis. Release code.

Pros
- Bold, novel modeling of deep temporal dependencies for Poisson factorization.
- Maintains conjugacy for PF parts; practical design choices.
- Promising empirical gains on several datasets.

Cons
- Objective not theoretically grounded; unclear learning dynamics.
- Clarity/notation issues impede understanding.
- Limited and potentially unfair comparisons; missing standard metrics.

Recommendation
Overall score: 5 (weak reject). While I appreciate the ambition and see potential in the core idea, the current presentation lacks a principled objective and sufficient experimental rigor. With a clearer variational formulation and stronger evaluation, this line of work could be impactful.

Confidence: 0.7 (reasonably confident in assessment).
