Summary
The paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Analysis (DDPFA), a sequential count model that augments Poisson factorization with (i) per-feature (ψv) and per-factor (λk) Gamma scale variables to encourage sparsity/importance weighting, and (ii) a deep recurrent structure over time for the factor scores θt. The deep temporal prior is implemented via multiple layers h(n)t, where Gamma distribution parameters (e.g., shapes) are generated by forward and backward RNNs over fixed-size time windows. Inference is described as a two-stage procedure: mean-field variational updates for the conjugate parts (using latent count allocations xvtk), followed by learning the RNN parameters via an auxiliary “Probability AutoEncoder” objective that penalizes squared error between conditional expectations rather than optimizing a principled ELBO. Experiments on synthetic data and five real datasets compare to PGDS, PFA, and LSTM, claiming improved fit/prediction.

Relation to prior work
The model builds on Poisson factorization for counts and dynamic extensions like PGDS; it also relates to deep latent variable models (DEFs, GBNs) and amortized VI (VAE, Ladder VAE). There is also relevant work in recurrent Poisson factorization for temporal recommendation and deep Markov models. The paper cites some of this literature but incompletely and with inconsistencies.

Strengths
- Targets an important setting (dynamic high-dimensional count data) with a model that keeps Poisson likelihoods rather than Gaussian surrogates.
- Attempts to capture longer temporal structure by combining a deep temporal hierarchy with recurrent parameterizations.
- Includes a feature-wise and factor-wise scaling (ψv, λk) for sparsity/importance control.
- Evaluates on several datasets and discusses interpretability on ICEWS.

Weaknesses

Quality (technical soundness)
- The probabilistic specification of the deep temporal prior is unclear and mixes ad hoc components. The Gamma “posteriors” with shape αh(n)tk + γh(n)tk and fixed rate βh are not derived from a well-defined conjugate model; α and γ are outputs of neural nets, but the corresponding prior and likelihood that yield such a posterior are not specified. This undermines the validity of the claimed coordinate-ascent VI for the deep layers.
- The “Probability AutoEncoder” objective (Eqs. 15–18) is not a variational bound; it minimizes squared differences between conditional expectations and a reconstruction error. No guarantee is provided that this optimizes the marginal likelihood or an ELBO. The statement that “differentiable variational Bayes is not suitable” is incorrect; reparameterization for Gamma distributions is well-studied, and score-function estimators or implicit reparameterization can be used.
- The dynamic dependence is confined to fixed windows of size c per layer; stacking layers may increase receptive field, but there is no analysis showing effective long-range memory, stability, or identifiability.
- Identifiability is exacerbated by introducing both ψv and λk together with Φ and Θ in a purely multiplicative model; no constraints (e.g., column normalization of Φ, sum-to-one scaling) or regularization strategies are described to address scale indeterminacy.
- Important algorithmic issues are unaddressed: computational complexity and memory for latent count allocations on large datasets (e.g., ICEWS), convergence diagnostics, and sensitivity to K, c, and depth.

Clarity
- The paper suffers from numerous notation errors, typos, and inconsistencies that make it difficult to follow. Examples include:
  - Eq. 7 probability uses “θtφvλφv” (duplicated φv).
  - In the mean-field factorization, q(ψk|·) is used though ψ is indexed by v elsewhere.
  - Complete conditionals: “λk|Θ,Φ,Φ” (duplicate Φ); rates use βθ without being clearly defined.
  - Bernoulli–Poisson link defines b=1(x>1) instead of b=1(x>0).
  - k ∈ {0,…,K} (off-by-one).
  - Multiple grammatical errors (“Natrually”, “fanatical data”, “nest hierarchical”).
- Critical pieces (Tables 1–3, Figures 1–3) are referenced but not present, preventing assessment of results.
- The role and dimensionality of h(n)t relative to θt, how ffeed/fback are parameterized (RNN type, outputs, constraints to ensure positivity), and how ladder connections are implemented are under-specified.

Originality
- The idea of combining Poisson factorization with recurrent/deep temporal structure is reasonable but not novel per se; related work includes PGDS, recurrent Poisson factorization for recommendations, deep Markov models with count likelihoods, and gamma belief networks. The contribution is primarily an ad hoc training objective rather than a new principled inference scheme. The paper does not clearly differentiate its approach from existing amortized VI for deep latent count models.

Significance
- Because the training objective is not principled and the experimental evidence is incomplete (missing tables/figures, no predictive log likelihoods, no ablations on ψ/λ, no runtime or scalability), it is hard to conclude that the method advances the state of the art. Claims of improved performance are not substantiated quantitatively in the submission.
- The interpretability arguments would be more convincing with thorough factor analyses, stability checks, and comparisons under a common likelihood.

Detailed comments and minor issues
- Provide a precise generative model with all conditionals, including how α and β parameters are generated and constrained; ensure conjugacy-based updates are actually valid.
- Replace the squared-error “Probability AutoEncoder” with a proper ELBO involving recognition models p(h(n)|·), and use reparameterization or score-function estimators for Gamma variables.
- Report predictive log likelihoods (or held-out perplexity) in addition to MSE/PMSE; for count data, MSE is mismatched to the Poisson likelihood.
- Ensure fair baselines and hyperparameter tuning (e.g., LSTM with Poisson/negative binomial output, PGDS settings tuned to each dataset).
- Discuss computational complexity and provide runtime/memory measurements.
- Conduct ablations on Λ and Ψ, window size c, depth N, and ladder connections.
- Fix the Bernoulli–Poisson link and other typos; standardize notation; complete and correct references (years, venues, authors).
- Provide code or sufficient detail for reproducibility.

Pros
- Addresses an important problem (dynamic counts).
- Uses count likelihoods and attempts to integrate deep temporal structure.
- Broad experimental scope.

Cons
- Method lacks a principled objective and clear derivation.
- Numerous notation/formatting errors and missing figures/tables.
- Insufficient and non-reproducible empirical validation.
- Identifiability and scalability concerns unaddressed.

Recommendation
Reject. The paper in its current form is not technically sound or clearly presented enough for NeurIPS. A revised version should provide a rigorous probabilistic specification, a principled variational objective, complete experiments with fair baselines, and substantial improvements in clarity and referencing.

Confidence
High.
