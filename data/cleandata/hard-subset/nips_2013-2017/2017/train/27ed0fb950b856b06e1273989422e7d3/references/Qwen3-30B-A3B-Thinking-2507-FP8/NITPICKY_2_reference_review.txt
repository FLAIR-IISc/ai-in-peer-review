

# Review of "Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model"

## Summary
This paper proposes the Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) model, which extends Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) by incorporating deep recurrent structures to capture complex temporal dependencies in count data. The authors claim their model outperforms existing methods like PGDS, LSTM, and standard PFA on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The model uses variational inference with a deep neural network structure to model the temporal dynamics of the latent factors.

## Strengths
The paper addresses a relevant problem in time series analysis of count data. The idea of combining deep learning with Poisson factorization is potentially interesting, and the authors do attempt to incorporate some novel elements (deep structure for long-term dependencies). The experimental section includes multiple datasets, which is a positive aspect.

## Major Weaknesses

### Technical Inconsistencies and Errors
The paper contains numerous technical errors and notational inconsistencies that undermine its credibility. In Equation (1), the Poisson parameter includes λkψv, but λk and ψv are defined as separate variables with inconsistent notation throughout the paper (sometimes λk, sometimes λ_k). Equation (12) incorrectly uses "βθ" without defining it earlier. The description of the variational inference (Equation 11) is mathematically questionable - the update for xvtk should involve expectations of the latent variables, but the derivation appears incorrect.

### Poorly Defined Model
The deep structure described in Equations (3)-(4) is extremely vague. The paper states "h^(n)_t ∼ p(h^(n)_t |h^(n+1)_t-c, ..., h^(n+1)_t)" but doesn't specify what h^(n)_t represents or how the layers interact. The window size "c" is never defined or justified. The paper claims to use "recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions" but doesn't specify the architecture, activation functions, or how the RNNs are trained.

### Inadequate Experimental Evaluation
The experimental evaluation has significant flaws. The paper claims DDPFA "has the best performance in fitting and prediction task of all the datasets" (Table 1), but fails to provide statistical significance testing for these results. The ICEWS prediction task is explicitly noted to be "not good enough" for DDPFA, yet the paper doesn't address this weakness. The comparison with baselines is superficial - no ablation studies are presented to show which components of the model contribute to performance improvements.

### Overstated Claims
The paper claims DDPFA "has better effectivity and interpretability" but provides no evidence for this claim. The assertion that "the factors learnt by our model are not activated locally compared to PGDS" (Figure 2) is not supported by quantitative analysis. The paper also claims to address "long-time dependence" but fails to demonstrate how this is achieved beyond the vague description of the deep structure.

### Formatting and Writing Issues
The paper contains numerous formatting inconsistencies: inconsistent variable notation (θtk vs. θ_t,k), missing references (e.g., "as in [2]" without specifying which part), and incomplete sentences. The reference list is poorly formatted with inconsistent citation styles and missing page numbers. The paper's description of related work is superficial and fails to properly distinguish this work from existing deep Poisson factorization approaches.

## Specific Technical Concerns

1. The paper states "Dirichlet distribution is often used as prior distribution over φvk in previous works, a Gamma distribution is exploited in our model due to the including of feature-wise parameter ψv." This justification is insufficient and fails to address why Gamma is better than Dirichlet for this purpose.

2. The loss function in Equation (16) is incorrectly formulated as a norm of expectations rather than a proper variational loss. The paper claims "similar to variational AutoEncoder," but the derivation is mathematically unsound.

3. The "pruning" issue mentioned in Section 5 is not properly addressed in the experimental results. The paper claims to have identified a problem but doesn't show how it affects model performance.

4. The paper references "non-count real-valued matrix can also be linked to a latent count matrix via a compound Poisson distribution or a Gamma belief network [20]" but fails to explain how this relates to their model or provide necessary citations for the reference.

## Recommendation

This paper contains numerous technical errors, inconsistent notation, and unsubstantiated claims that significantly undermine its contribution. While the problem being addressed is relevant, the paper fails to provide a clear, rigorous, and novel solution. The experimental evaluation is insufficient and does not properly validate the claims made. The writing is poor with numerous formatting and notational inconsistencies.

The paper appears to be a straightforward combination of existing techniques (PFA + deep structures) without sufficient justification for why this combination is novel or particularly effective. The claims of "better interpretability" and "capturing long-time dependence" are not supported by the evidence provided.

**Recommendation: Reject**
