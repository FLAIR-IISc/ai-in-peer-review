- Summary: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), combining Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs and deep hierarchy to model sequential count data via variational inference, evaluated on synthetic and real datasets.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on PFA and PGDS, addressing their limited long-range dependency modeling by applying deep learning ideas (RNNs, deep Bayesian models).

- Novelty: Combines PFA with deep RNNs and hierarchical depth to capture short- and long-term temporal dependencies.

- Theoretical foundation: Grounded in a probabilistic Poisson factorization framework with a variational inference training approach.

- Empirical validation: Experiments on multiple datasets show DDPFA outperforming baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA) on several tasks.

- Interpretability (potential): PFA factor structure offers some interpretability; visualizations hint at meaningful factor patterns.

- Addresses previous limitations: Explicitly targets shortcomings of models like PGDS in handling complex temporal dependencies.

- Lack of detailed derivation: Key variational inference derivations (e.g., conditional distributions in Eqs. 8–10) are glossed over, hindering reproducibility and correctness assessment.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter selection and impact, leaving model sensitivity unclear.

- Computational complexity: Scalability and runtime for training/inference are not adequately addressed, raising practical concerns for large datasets.

- Overly complex objective: Addition of a Probability AutoEncoder and pruning discussion complicates the objective without clear justification of benefit.

- Inconsistent performance: DDPFA underperforms PGDS on the ICEWS prediction task, questioning robustness.

- Limited factor analysis: Paper lacks a thorough examination of learned factors and what they represent in real datasets.

- Evaluation metric limitation: Reliance on MSE alone for count data is inadequate; metrics like MAE or Poisson deviance are suggested.

- Pro-accept arguments: Novel and potentially powerful model, empirically strong on several datasets, addresses an important time-series modeling challenge.

- Con-accept arguments: Missing derivations, unclear hyperparameter tuning, computational/training instability, and mixed empirical robustness weaken the contribution.

- Overall assessment: Interesting and potentially valuable work but needs more technical detail, scalability discussion, and factor analysis; reviewer leans weak accept.

- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
