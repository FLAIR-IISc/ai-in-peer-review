- Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), combining Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs and a deep structure to model temporal count data.  
- Proposes a variational inference algorithm for parameter estimation.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic and several real-world datasets against baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA).  
- Positions DDPFA within Bayesian factor analysis and recent deep Bayesian models, addressing limitations of models like PGDS.  
- Novelty: integration of RNNs with PFA for long-term dependencies is a significant contribution.  
- Technical soundness: model formulation and variational updates are well-motivated using Poisson/Gamma properties.  
- Empirical validation is extensive, with comparisons to relevant baselines.  
- Interpretability: learned factors and activation patterns suggest potential for interpretability.  
- Main weakness: reliance on mean-field variational inference risks “pruning” and uninformative latents, with suggested fixes not implemented.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: many hyperparameters (priors, window size, network architecture) with inadequate tuning/sensitivity details.  
- Limited ablation studies to isolate contributions of depth, RNN choice, and other components.  
- DDPFA underperforms PGDS on ICEWS prediction; causes not sufficiently investigated.  
- Clarity: insufficient detail on specific RNN architecture (cell type, hidden units).  
- Originality: approach is original in this specific application, though neural networks in Bayesian factor models are not entirely new.  
- Significance: high potential for dynamic count modeling if variational issues are resolved.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — expand VI discussion, report hyperparameter tuning, add ablations, and investigate ICEWS performance.
