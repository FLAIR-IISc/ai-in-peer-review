Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factor Analysis (DDPFA) for sequential count data. The observation model is a Poisson factorization with positive factors Φ (feature loadings), Θ (time-varying factor scores), and two additional positive scaling terms Λ (per-factor) and Ψ (per-feature) to encourage sparsity/smoothness. To capture complex temporal dependence, Θ is driven by a deep hierarchical set of latent variables H(n) with recurrent connections: p(θt|h(0)t−c:t) and, recursively, p(h(n)t |h(n+1)t−c:t) for n<N, with the top layer depending only on past h(N). The conditional distributions are parameterized by RNNs that output Gamma parameters. Inference is performed via a two-stage procedure: (i) mean-field variational inference (coordinate ascent) with conjugate updates for Φ, Λ, Ψ, Θ, H using auxiliary Poisson count allocations xvtk; (ii) training the RNN parameters with a “Probability AutoEncoder” loss that aligns top-down “prior” networks and bottom-up “posterior” networks via L2 discrepancies between expectations of the respective distributions, plus a reconstruction term for X.

Experiments compare DDPFA against PGDS (Poisson-Gamma Dynamical System), LSTM, and PFA on four synthetic datasets and five real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, Ebola, International Disaster, Annual Sheep Population) using MSE/PMSE. DDPFA often improves over baselines, with exceptions (ICEWS prediction).

Relative to prior work, the model builds on Poisson factorization and NB-process-based dynamic models (e.g., PGDS), deep latent variable models (DEFs, deep Poisson factorization, gamma belief networks), and recurrent models (LSTM, recurrent Poisson factorization). The claimed novelty lies in combining a deep hierarchical temporal prior (via RNNs) over PFAs with a bespoke variational training objective.

Strengths
- Ambitious attempt to marry Poisson factorization with deep hierarchical temporal structure to capture longer-range dependencies.
- Leverages conjugacy for parts of the model (Poisson-Gamma) and proposes a practical alternating optimization scheme.
- Empirical results indicate improved MSE/PMSE on several datasets, including challenging multi-year text and event data.
- Discussion of practical issues (“pruning,” ineffective additional layers) and architectural mitigations (ladder-like connections) shows awareness of hierarchical VAE challenges.

Weaknesses

Quality (technical soundness)
- The inference scheme is insufficiently justified. While the PF part is conjugate, the deep temporal prior/posterior are “implicit” RNN-parameterized distributions. The paper alternates mean-field VI with a non-ELBO “Probability AutoEncoder” loss that minimizes L2 differences between expectations (Eqs. 16–18). There is no derivation showing that this optimizes or lower-bounds the marginal likelihood. No monotonic improvement or convergence guarantees are provided.
- The Gamma updates for h(n) (Eq. 10, 14) appear ad hoc (shape = α + γ). It is unclear from a probabilistic model why both top-down and bottom-up contributions combine as additive shape parameters, and how the conditional posteriors are derived. The generative model for H is not fully specified, making the coordinate updates difficult to verify.
- Several mathematical inaccuracies/typos: multinomial parameters in Eq. 7 repeat φ; support for k includes 0; λ conditional in Eq. 8 lists Φ twice; Bernoulli-Poisson link uses b=1(x>1) instead of x≥1; inconsistent notation for rates/sums. These hinder reproducibility and cast doubt on derivations.
- The rationale for not using reparameterization gradients (“sampling all latent variables is high-cost and useless”) is unconvincing given existing methods for Gamma reparameterization/implicit reparameterization, or using amortized variational distributions q with stochastic optimization.
- No calibration of uncertainty or predictive log-likelihood; only MSE/PMSE are reported, which is suboptimal for count models.

Clarity
- The paper contains numerous grammatical issues, incomplete/inaccurate references, and ambiguous notation. Key components (network architectures, exact parameterizations/activations ensuring positivity, window size effects) are under-specified. The training schedule (number of alternations, stopping criteria) is vague. Figures/tables are referenced but not clearly described in text.

Originality
- Combining temporal PF with deep hierarchical latent structures and RNN parameterizations is a natural extension of prior PF, DEF, and recurrent PF work. Prior art includes PGDS (dynamic PF), deep PF (Gan et al.), gamma belief networks (Zhou et al.), temporal topic models, and recurrent Poisson factorization for recommendation. The main novelty appears to be the specific ladder-like deep temporal hierarchy plus the Probability AutoEncoder loss. However, the latter is not standard and lacks theoretical grounding.

Significance
- Empirical gains (in MSE/PMSE) on several datasets are promising but modest and not systematically characterized. The study lacks comparisons to stronger temporal count baselines (e.g., dynamic topic models, Hawkes/GP-modulated Poisson process models, temporal gamma belief networks), predictive likelihood metrics, and runtime/complexity analyses. Without a sound inference objective, it is difficult to assess the generality and reliability of the approach.

Questions for the authors
- Provide a complete generative model for H and Θ, and derive the conditional posteriors (Eqs. 9–14). Under what assumptions is h(n) ~ Gamma(α+γ, β) valid?
- Can you derive an ELBO for the full model and show how your training objective relates to it? Does alternating your two stages increase the ELBO?
- Why not use amortized variational inference with reparameterization or implicit reparameterization for Gamma? What prevents using standard VAE/SGVI for the deep parts?
- How sensitive are results to K, window size c, number of layers, and hidden sizes? Provide ablations and uncertainty (e.g., standard errors).
- Report predictive log-likelihood on held-out time steps and per-token perplexity for text.

Suggestions for improvement
- Formalize the generative model and provide a principled variational objective (ELBO) with either reparameterization or score-function/variance-reduction techniques; alternatively, justify the proposed loss as a bound.
- Clean up notation and fix mathematical/typographical errors; include full references.
- Add stronger baselines and metrics (predictive log-likelihood), and report computational costs.
- Provide ablations for ladder connections and depth; include code and reproducibility details.

Arguments for acceptance
- Addresses an important problem: modeling long-range temporal dependencies in sequential count data within a PF framework.
- Empirical improvements on several datasets; interpretable factors discussed.

Arguments against acceptance
- Inference objective is not theoretically grounded; key updates appear ad hoc without derivations.
- Clarity and presentation issues (notation, typos, incomplete references) impede verification and reproducibility.
- Limited evaluation metrics and baselines; significance is uncertain.
- Novelty over existing deep/recurrent PF methods is incremental.

Overall, while the idea of deep recurrent priors for Poisson factorization is appealing, the current submission lacks theoretical rigor and clarity to support its claims, and the empirical evaluation is not sufficiently comprehensive.
