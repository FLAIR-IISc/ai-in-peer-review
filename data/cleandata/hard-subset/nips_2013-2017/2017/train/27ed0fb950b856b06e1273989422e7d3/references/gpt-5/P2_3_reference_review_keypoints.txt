1. Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): Poisson factorization with Gamma sparsity variables and RNN-defined multi-layer latent Gamma dynamics for sequential count data.  
2. Inference alternates mean-field updates for conjugate parts and an RNN-trained “Probability AutoEncoder” using L2 matching of encoder/decoder Gamma expectations instead of a standard ELBO.  
3. Empirical evaluation on synthetic and five real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, Ebola, International Disaster, Sheep) using MSE and one-step prediction MSE, compared to PGDS, PFA, and LSTM.  
4. Strength: tackles long-range temporal dependence and factor interactions in high-dimensional sparse counts, a relevant and ambitious problem.  
5. Strength: leverages conjugacy (Poisson–multinomial augmentation, Gamma conditionals) for efficient mean-field updates of Φ, Θ, λ, ψ.  
6. Strength: broad empirical coverage and explicit acknowledgment of limitations (pruning, limited depth benefits).  
7. Major weakness: training objective is ad-hoc—L2 expectation matching (“Probability AutoEncoder”) replaces a principled ELBO, lacks marginal likelihood bound, convergence guarantees, and variational justification.  
8. Major weakness: generative model is unclear/invalid—intermediate layer conditionals use future data (ffeed/fback), breaking causality and leaving the joint distribution undefined.  
9. Major weakness: identifiability/scaling not addressed—multiplicative factors (ψ, λ, θ, φ) make decomposition unidentifiable without constraints or normalization.  
10. Major weakness: missing ELBO, derivations, gradient justification, and complexity analysis; some conditionals have indexing/notation errors.  
11. Major weakness: evaluation uses suboptimal metrics (MSE/PMSE) for counts; lacks held-out log-likelihood/perplexity, train/val/test protocol, uncertainty estimates, and significance testing.  
12. Major weakness: comparisons omit stronger baselines (dynamic topic models, gamma belief networks, neural Hawkes/point processes, recurrent PF variants).  
13. Empirical concern: additional depth often does not help (Table 3), undermining the paper’s main claim about deep hierarchies capturing long-term dependence.  
14. Clarity issues: numerous grammatical errors, inconsistent notation, incomplete references, and insufficient documentation of design/optimization details hindering reproducibility.  
15. Originality: combining Poisson factorization with deep/recurrent structure is not entirely novel; claimed novelty (RNN-driven Gamma priors and training) is not convincingly justified.  
16. Significance: potential high impact if made principled and effective, but current theoretical and empirical issues limit the paper’s contribution.  
17. Overall recommendation: Reject due to incoherent probabilistic objective, methodological/clarity issues, and lack of reproducible improvements over strong baselines.  
18. Key questions for authors: define a causal full generative model and ELBO; justify not using amortized VI/reparameterization; address identifiability; report held-out likelihoods and stronger baselines; clarify and ablate the ladder architecture.  
19. Constructive suggestions: recast model with causal prior and single coherent ELBO using amortized VI, start with a single recurrent prior before adding depth, and provide full derivations, ablations, code, and improved writing.
