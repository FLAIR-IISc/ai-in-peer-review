

# Peer Review: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model

## Summary
This paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), a novel model for analyzing sequentially observed count vectors. The authors extend Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) by incorporating a deep recurrent structure to capture complex long-term temporal dependencies, addressing limitations in existing models like Poisson-Gamma Dynamical Systems (PGDS) and standard LSTM approaches. The model uses a hierarchical structure with recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions over latent variables, with inference performed via variational methods. The paper evaluates DDPFA on synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS corpus, Ebola outbreak data, International Disaster data, and Annual Sheep Population), showing competitive results compared to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA baselines.

## Strengths

1. **Problem Relevance**: The paper tackles an important problem in analyzing sequential count data, which is prevalent in recommendation systems, text analysis, and time series analysis. The authors correctly identify limitations in current approaches for capturing long-term temporal dependencies.

2. **Model Structure**: The incorporation of a deep structure to model complex temporal dependencies is conceptually interesting and addresses a genuine gap in the literature. The use of recurrent neural networks to model implicit distributions represents a novel application in this domain.

3. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The paper evaluates the model on both synthetic and diverse real-world datasets, providing a thorough empirical assessment. The results generally demonstrate that DDPFA outperforms baselines in most tasks.

4. **Theoretical Foundation**: The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the model, including detailed derivations of the variational inference procedure and the use of Gamma-Poisson conjugacy.

## Weaknesses

1. **Questionable Necessity of Deep Structure**: The most significant weakness is the lack of compelling evidence that the deep structure is necessary. Table 3 shows that adding more layers or expanding dimensions in upper layers yields minimal performance gains. This directly contradicts the paper's core motivation of using "deep structure to capture long-time dependence." The paper acknowledges this issue but fails to adequately address why the deep structure is beneficial when the experiments suggest otherwise.

2. **Inadequate Comparison with Related Work**: The paper cites Deep Poisson Factor Analysis based on RBM/SBN [9,10] and Deep Poisson Factor Modeling with recurrent structure [12], but does not sufficiently differentiate DDPFA from these approaches. The paper fails to establish how the proposed deep recurrent structure offers advantages over these prior deep Poisson factorization models.

3. **Weak Theoretical Justification**: The paper claims that the deep structure captures "long-time dependence" but provides insufficient theoretical justification. The authors do not clearly explain why a deep structure is better than simply increasing the window size in a shallow model. The transition from Eq. 3 to Eq. 4 is not well-motivated.

4. **Inference Limitations**: The paper acknowledges issues with "pruning" (where some latent dimensions contain only noise) and notes this is likely due to the mean-field variational inference. This is a significant limitation that isn't properly addressed or mitigated in the proposed approach.

5. **Overstated Claims**: The paper claims DDPFA "has better effectivity and interpretability" but provides limited evidence for the interpretability claim. The visualization in Figure 2 shows factors are "not activated locally," but doesn't convincingly demonstrate that this leads to better interpretability.

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a reasonable contribution to the field of sequential count data modeling, but the novelty is overstated. The core idea of using deep structures to capture temporal dependencies is not new, and the experimental evidence does not sufficiently support the claim that the deep structure provides meaningful advantages over simpler alternatives. The paper's main contribution appears to be a specific implementation of a deep recurrent structure within a Poisson factorization framework, rather than a fundamental advance.

The authors' claim that "the independence assumption is made on θt−1 and θt+1 if θt is given" in previous models is inaccurate—this is a standard Markov assumption, not an independence assumption. This mischaracterization weakens the paper's motivation.

The paper's main weakness is that it fails to demonstrate that the deep structure is necessary. If Table 3 is taken at face value, the model's performance doesn't improve significantly with deeper layers, and the "ladder-like architecture" mentioned in the paper is only beneficial in limited circumstances. This suggests the proposed model may not be substantially better than simpler alternatives.

## Recommendation

**Weak Accept**

The paper presents a well-structured model for sequential count data with reasonable empirical results. However, the novelty is limited by the lack of compelling evidence that the deep structure is necessary, and the paper fails to adequately distinguish itself from prior deep Poisson factorization work. The authors should address these concerns by:

1. Conducting additional experiments to demonstrate the necessity of the deep structure (e.g., comparing with a shallow model that uses a larger window size)
2. Clarifying how DDPFA differs from prior deep Poisson factorization models [9,10,12]
3. Providing a more rigorous theoretical justification for the deep structure
4. Addressing the "pruning" issue in the inference procedure

Without these improvements, the paper's contribution is marginal, as the performance gains over simpler models like PGDS are not substantial enough to justify the added complexity. The paper would be significantly stronger if it could convincingly demonstrate the unique value of the deep structure rather than merely adding it as a black-box component.

## Overall Assessment

The paper is technically sound with reasonable experimental results, but the novelty is overstated and the necessity of the deep structure is not adequately demonstrated. The model represents a reasonable extension of previous work but doesn't advance the state-of-the-art in a significant way. The paper would benefit from a more focused contribution that clearly establishes the value of the deep structure in capturing temporal dependencies.
