- Overall: DDPFA integrates RNNs into Poisson Factor Analysis to model sequential count data; idea is promising but empirical gains are not compelling, so reviewer recommends weak reject.

- Problem & contribution: Targets sequential count data, extending PFA with deep recurrent networks to capture short- and long-range dependencies; related work coverage is adequate.

- Novelty: Combining PFA with deep recurrent networks is a novel and potentially valuable contribution.

- Technical soundness: Mathematical formulation appears correct and Gamma priors are reasonable.

- Model scope: DDPFA is a comprehensive framework (Poisson likelihood, factor analysis, RNNs, variational inference).

- Dataset coverage: Evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets, showing breadth of application.

- Empirical weakness: Improvements over simpler baselines (PFA, LSTM, PGDS) are inconsistent and not substantially better; performance on ICEWS is worse than PGDS.

- Experimental concerns: Hyperparameter tuning and settings for baselines are insufficiently described, raising fairness questions.

- Presentation issues: Notation is dense and inference explanation (variational steps) is hard to follow; Figure 1 needs more integrated explanation.

- Missing ablations: No ablation studies to isolate the contributions of recurrent networks or depth.

- Inference complexity: Variational inference and coordinate-ascent updates seem overly complex and are not fully justified over alternatives.

- Acceptance arguments (pro): Model is a useful extension, technically sound, and explores deep learning for sequential count data.

- Acceptance arguments (con): Weak experimental evidence, clarity problems, complex inference, and lack of component-level analysis argue against acceptance.

- Experimentation suggestions: Perform thorough hyperparameter searches, include statistical significance testing, and evaluate on larger/higher-dimensional datasets.

- Ablation suggestion: Run ablation studies to quantify the impact of each model component (e.g., remove RNNs, reduce depth).

- Clarity suggestion: Rewrite inference sections with clearer, more intuitive notation and step-by-step explanations.

- Justification suggestion: Provide stronger justification for chosen hyperparameters and model configurations.

- Discussion suggestion: Expand on model limitations and outline future research directions.
