- Summary: Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) using RNN-driven Gamma hierarchies and a two-stage inference (MFVI with conjugacy + a “Probability AutoEncoder” squared-error network training); reports improved MSE/PMSE on several datasets.

- Relation to prior work: Positions DDPFA against PGDS, DEFs, deep PFA and RNN models but bibliography is incomplete and key technical distinctions are unclear.

- Strength: Ambitious integration of Poisson factorization with deep/recurrent priors to capture long-range temporal dependencies.

- Strength: Uses practical MFVI updates exploiting Gamma–Poisson conjugacy and appropriate multinomial allocations for latent counts.

- Strength: Empirical results often improve over PFA/PGDS/LSTM and include qualitative temporal-factor analyses.

- Strength: Paper honestly reports limitations (pruning, limited depth benefits) and explores a ladder-like variant.

- Weakness: Training objective/inference is ad hoc—the “Probability AutoEncoder” uses squared-error between expectations, not a principled ELBO, and the two-stage procedure lacks convergence guarantees.

- Weakness: Derivational gaps—posterior updates for deep latent h(n) are heuristic and not derived from a coherent generative model or Bayes’ rule.

- Weakness: Clarity and rigor problems—notation errors, ambiguous equations, full joint/ELBO never stated, and RNN/parameter details (architectures, activations, positivity enforcement) are missing.

- Weakness: Evaluation methodology is weak—MSE/PMSE on counts is nonstandard, predictive log-likelihood/Poisson deviance and statistical significance are missing, and key baselines are omitted.

- Weakness: Identifiability and scaling concerns—introducing both λk and ψv increases scale indeterminacy without normalization or constraints.

- Weakness: References are incomplete and miss relevant recent work on variational families and amortized VI for counts.

- Quality assessment: Empirically promising but undermined by an unprincipled learning objective, incomplete inference derivations, and lack of theoretical guarantees.

- Clarity assessment: Paper is hard to follow; needs explicit joint model, ELBO, pseudocode, and full optimization/hyperparameter/runtime details.

- Originality: Direction is reasonable but not novel enough given related work; main novelty (Probability AutoEncoder) appears heuristic rather than principled.

- Significance: Potentially impactful for dynamic high-dimensional counts if made rigorous; current impact limited by methodological and evaluation shortcomings.

- Questions/requests: Provide the full joint distribution and ELBO or justify the alternative objective and its convergence properties.

- Questions/requests: Derive posterior forms for h(n)tk or state the modeling assumption validating h(n)tk ~ Gamma(α+γ, βh).

- Questions/requests: Specify RNN type (GRU/LSTM/vanilla), hidden sizes, activations, how Gamma parameters are constrained/kept positive, and optimization details.

- Questions/requests: Explain how scale indeterminacy among Φ, Θ, Λ, Ψ is prevented (e.g., normalization or rescaling).

- Questions/requests: Report predictive log-likelihood/Poisson deviance, calibration, and include stronger baselines (HPF/GBN, INGARCH, neural count models).

- Questions/requests: Provide sensitivity analyses for window size c, K, depth, runtime comparisons to PGDS/LSTM, and standard deviations over multiple runs.

- Pros for acceptance: Tackles an important problem, shows promising empirical results, and attempts to blend deep/recurrent structure with Poisson factorization while acknowledging pitfalls.

- Cons for acceptance: Lacks a principled training objective, relies on heuristic derivations for deep updates, and suffers from clarity and incomplete experimental evaluation.

- Recommendation: Lean reject; advise reformulating a principled ELBO/amortized VI, enforcing identifiability, evaluating with likelihood metrics and broader baselines, and substantially improving exposition and references.
