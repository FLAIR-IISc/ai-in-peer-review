- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factor Analysis (DDPFA): Poisson factorization with a deep latent hierarchy and RNN-parameterized Gamma priors; inference alternates mean-field VI for conjugate blocks with a “Probability AutoEncoder” L2 training of RNNs.
- Evaluated on four synthetic and five real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, Annual Sheep Population) and compared to PGDS, PFA, and LSTM using MSE/PMSE; reports improvements in most cases.
- Builds on prior work in Poisson factorization, dynamic PF, deep PF, and neural temporal models, but fails to cite and compare to several closely related temporal topic and recurrent PF models.
- Strength: Targets high-dimensional, sparse sequential count data with long-range dependencies, a practical and important setting.
- Strength: Conceptually appealing integration of Poisson factorization with deep temporal structure via RNN-parameterized Gamma priors.
- Strength: Retains conjugacy for efficient coordinate-ascent VI on core latent variables.
- Strength: Includes synthetic and multiple real datasets plus qualitative factor-activation analyses.
- Strength: Identifies practical training challenges (pruning, deeper-layer inefficacy) and suggests remedies (ladder architecture).
- Major weakness: Neural-component training objective is ad hoc — L2 “Probability AutoEncoder” losses lack derivation from a principled ELBO or likelihood.
- Major weakness: Alternating VI and neural training lacks a unified objective, gradients, convergence guarantees, or discussion of bias from decoupled training.
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics (MSE/PMSE) are inappropriate for count models; predictive log-likelihood, Poisson deviance, or held-out perplexity and uncertainty calibration are missing.
- Weakness: Baselines are limited; missing comparisons to dynamic topic models, neural dynamic topic models, deep PF with temporal priors, and recurrent Poisson factorization baselines.
- Weakness: Presentation issues — inconsistent notation, typos, unclear role of scaling variables (λk, ψv), and poorly specified deep hierarchy and boundary conditions.
- Weakness: Reproducibility is weak — missing architectural details (RNN type, hidden sizes, window c), hyperparameters, tuning, computational cost, and no code release.
- Weakness: Empirical rigor is lacking — no standard errors, significance tests, or extensive ablations; forecasting evaluated on very short sequences in some cases (e.g., T=17).
- Quality assessment: Technical soundness is fair-to-weak — conjugate updates are standard but neural training is not grounded in a proper variational bound.
- Empirical support: Mixed; reported improvements are undermined by inappropriate metrics, weak baselines, and incomplete reporting.
- Clarity: Below average — ambiguous notation, grammar/typos, and under-specified design choices, especially the relation between prior and posterior RNNs.
- Originality: Moderate — interesting combination of ideas, but related approaches exist and the novel “Probability AutoEncoder” lacks justification.
- Significance: Limited in current form due to weak theoretical grounding and empirical evaluation; could be impactful with a principled ELBO-based training and stronger experiments.
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses a relevant problem, modular architecture preserves conjugacy, and qualitative analyses suggest interpretability improvements.
- Cons for acceptance: Lack of principled neural training objective, insufficient baselines/metrics, clarity and reproducibility issues, and unresolved identifiability/scaling concerns.
- Key author-requested actions: Derive a unified ELBO and end-to-end training, justify L2 losses vs. likelihood-based objectives, clarify Gamma parameter constraints and positivity transforms, report predictive log-likelihood/Poisson deviance, compare to stronger baselines, analyze sensitivity to c/K/depth, address identifiability among Φ/Λ/Ψ/Θ, and provide computational cost and code.
- Overall recommendation: Reject in current form; encourage revision with unified ELBO, clearer specification, stronger experiments, and improved writing.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
