- Overall: Proposes DDPFA—RNNs integrated with Poisson Factorization—to model temporal dependencies in count data; promising idea but not ready for acceptance.
- Novelty: Combining deep RNNs with Poisson factorization for temporal modeling is novel and potentially powerful.
- Model formulation: Mathematical presentation is clear, using Poisson likelihoods and Gamma priors appropriately.
- Related work: Correctly positions DDPFA relative to PFA, PGDS, temporal Dirichlet processes, and deep Bayesian models.
- Empirical results: Experimental gains are marginal and inconsistent; DDPFA often does not outperform PGDS and performs worse on ICEWS.
- Comparisons: Authors compare against relevant baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA) on synthetic and real datasets.
- Qualitative insight: Visualizations of factor activations suggest DDPFA can capture nuanced dependencies.
- Implementation details: Key details (RNN architecture, layers/units, activations, optimizer, learning rates, initialization) are missing, harming reproducibility.
- Hyperparameter tuning: Hyperparameter choices are weakly justified; thorough search and sensitivity analysis are needed.
- Inference concerns: Reliance on mean-field variational inference raises issues (pruning, local optima) with no clear alternatives proposed.
- Scalability: Computational complexity, training time, and memory usage of DDPFA are not discussed or compared to baselines.
- Ablation: Missing systematic ablation study to quantify contributions of deep structure, RNNs, and other components.
- Significance: Approach is moderately original but significance is limited by lack of clear performance advantage.
- Recommendation: Reject; authors should improve experiments, provide implementation/hyperparameter details, explore alternative inference, and add ablation and scalability analyses.
