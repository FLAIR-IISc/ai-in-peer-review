## Review of Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model

This paper introduces the Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) model, aiming to capture both short-term and long-term dependencies in sequentially observed count data. The model builds upon Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) by incorporating recurrent neural networks to model the temporal evolution of latent factors, and employs variational inference for parameter estimation. While the idea of leveraging deep learning to enhance dynamic factor models is appealing and relevant to several NIPS audiences, the paper suffers from a multitude of shortcomings, ranging from superficial novelty to significant methodological inconsistencies and a lack of rigorous evaluation.

**Summary of Strengths:**

* **Relevant Problem:** Analyzing sequential count data is a significant challenge with applications across diverse fields.
* **Intuitive Extension:** The idea of integrating RNNs into a PFA framework to capture temporal dependencies is conceptually sound.
* **Variational Inference:** Employing variational inference is a practical choice for the proposed complex model.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty:** The combination of PFA and RNNs is not groundbreaking. Several prior works (e.g., [12] cited in the paper) already explore similar concepts. The depth introduced is primarily in stacking RNN layers, which feels incremental rather than a substantial conceptual leap.
* **Methodological Concerns:** The theoretical justification for the model components feels underdeveloped. The choice of Gamma distributions for priors feels somewhat arbitrary and lacks detailed motivation beyond citing existing literature. The abrupt switch to a Bernoulli-Poisson link for binary data is not convincingly justified.
* **Inconsistent Notation and Definitions:** The paper contains several notational inconsistencies (e.g., different uses of subscript and superscript notation) that make it challenging to follow. Definitions are sometimes imprecise.  For example, the explanation of how Λ and Ψ are "absorbed" into Θ is vague and potentially misleading.
* **Incomplete Inference Details:** The inference section provides a high-level overview, but lacks crucial implementation details. The loss function for training the neural networks is presented, but the specifics of optimization (learning rate, batch size, optimizer) are absent. The impact of the variational approximation quality is not discussed. The coordinate ascent method and its convergence properties are not elaborated upon.
* **Insufficient Experimental Validation:** While the paper presents results on both synthetic and real-world datasets, the experimental evaluation is flawed. The synthetic datasets are overly simplistic and don't adequately stress-test the model's capabilities. The comparison with baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA) is not comprehensive. The parameter settings for the baseline models are vaguely described ("set in [2]"), raising concerns about fairness. Crucially, there is no statistical significance testing to demonstrate that the improvements observed are not due to chance.
* **Overstated Claims:** The paper consistently claims “excellent results” without providing sufficient quantitative evidence to support these assertions. The discussion of Figure 2 is subjective and lacks rigorous analysis.
* **Problematic Discussion of "Pruning":** The discussion on "pruning" and the limitations of MF-VI feels tacked on and doesn't offer concrete solutions. 
* **Formatting and Writing Issues:** Numerous minor grammatical errors and awkward phrasing detract from the paper’s readability.



**Detailed Critique:**

* **Equation (1):** The notation is confusing. What is the dimensionality of Φ, Θ, λ, and ψ? A clear statement is necessary.
* **Equation (2):** The decomposition into latent counts *xvtk* is standard, but the justification for introducing Λ and Ψ feels weak. 
* **Equation (3 & 4):** The recurrent structure is described, but the specific type of RNN (e.g., LSTM, GRU) is not specified, which impacts reproducibility.  The lack of detail regarding the activation functions and weight initialization schemes is also a concern.
* **Section 3 (Inference):**  The ELBO maximization process is mentioned, but the specific updates for each variational parameter are presented without sufficient context. The connection between the Gamma distributions and the conjugate properties is asserted without detailed derivation.
* **Section 4 (Experiments):** The hyperparameter settings are poorly documented. The choice of K (number of factors) is also arbitrarily defined for each dataset. The MSE metric is adequate but not informative enough on its own. Further analysis of the factors themselves is needed. The claim about LSTM’s performance on SDS4 due to noise is speculative and requires further investigation.
* **Table 3:** The analysis of different model sizes lacks depth. The observations about pruning are anecdotal and need to be supported by more rigorous analysis.




**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**Against Acceptance:**

* The paper's contributions are incremental and do not represent a significant advance in the field.
* The methodological details are insufficient for reproducibility.
* The experimental validation is flawed and unconvincing.
* The writing is unclear and contains numerous errors.

**For Acceptance:**

* The problem addressed is relevant and important.
* The idea of combining PFA with RNNs is intuitively appealing.
* The use of variational inference is a reasonable choice.




**Overall Assessment:**

Despite addressing a relevant research problem, this paper suffers from significant deficiencies in terms of novelty, methodological rigor, experimental validation, and presentation. The paper feels more like a preliminary investigation than a complete and polished research contribution. Therefore, I **strongly recommend rejection**. The authors should address the concerns raised in this review, provide more detailed methodological explanations, conduct a more thorough experimental evaluation, and improve the clarity and precision of their writing before resubmitting.
