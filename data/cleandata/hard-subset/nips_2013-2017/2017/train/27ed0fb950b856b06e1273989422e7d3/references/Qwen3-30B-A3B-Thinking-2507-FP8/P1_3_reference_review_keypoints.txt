- Summary: Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factor Analysis (DDPFA), a deep hierarchical Poisson factor model using RNNs across layers to capture short- and long-term temporal dependencies in count data.

- Related work: Builds on PFA, PGDS, and deep Bayesian models, claiming to overcome shallow temporal-structure limitations of prior approaches.

- Strength — Problem relevance: Tackles an important, well-motivated problem with applications in recommendation, text, and network analysis.

- Strength — Model innovation: Introduces a layered RNN-based hierarchy to model multi-scale temporal dependence, extending prior Poisson factor models.

- Strength — Experimental validation: Evaluated on synthetic and five real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, Annual Sheep), outperforming PGDS, LSTM, and PFA on most metrics, especially long-term patterns.

- Strength — Interpretability: Latent factors reveal meaningful temporal patterns (e.g., Israel-Palestine conflict, six-party talks) demonstrating model interpretability.

- Strength — Technical soundness: Uses Gamma–Poisson conjugacy and variational inference with a clear inference algorithm.

- Weakness — Theoretical justification: No strong theoretical argument that the deep structure is necessary for capturing long-range dependence.

- Weakness — Comparison to related work: Poorly articulated relation to deep Bayesian models (e.g., Deep Exponential Families, RBM/SBN-based DPFA) and how DDPFA improves on them.

- Weakness — Pruning issue: Some latent dimensions contain noise; paper acknowledges but does not adequately analyze impact or mitigation strategies.

- Weakness — Limited experimental scope: Only five datasets and no statistical significance testing for reported improvements.

- Weakness — Reproducibility concerns: Insufficient details on neural network architectures, hyperparameters beyond Table 2, and training procedures.

- Evaluation — Quality: Technically solid but needs deeper theoretical analysis, more ablations, and statistical tests.

- Evaluation — Clarity: Well-organized and readable, though some inference technicalities need clearer explanation.

- Evaluation — Originality: Novel integration of deep nets with Poisson factorization, but not a dramatic departure from existing deep Bayesian approaches.

- Evaluation — Significance: Promising practical utility, but impact limited by dataset breadth and lack of compelling necessity for deep structure.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Required revisions: Provide stronger theoretical justification for deep structure; clarify novelty vs. prior deep models; address pruning (analysis/solutions); add statistical validation; supply full implementation/training details for reproducibility.
