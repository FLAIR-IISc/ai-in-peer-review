- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): RNN-parameterized Gamma dynamic factors, multi-layer hierarchy, and feature/component gamma scales for sequential count data.
- Generative likelihood: Poisson with rate ∑k θtk φvk λk ψv; temporal priors are implicit via RNN outputs and hierarchical RNN-conditioned Gamma layers.
- Inference alternates closed-form mean-field VI for conjugate blocks using auxiliary counts and RNN training via a "Probability AutoEncoder" reconstruction objective.
- Builds on PF, PGDS, Gamma Belief Networks, deep PF, and deep sequential latent-variable models; novelty claimed in combining conjugacy with RNN-parameterized Gamma priors and ladder structure.
- Strength: Ambitious combination of PF interpretability and conjugacy with flexible RNN temporal structure.
- Strength: Hierarchical, windowed design aims to capture longer-range temporal dependencies beyond first-order Markov.
- Strength: Computational appeal from alternating closed-form updates and learned implicit priors; experiments across multiple datasets and tasks.
- Major weakness: Probabilistic model specification is unclear/inconsistent; prior and posterior terms are mixed in Gamma parameters without a coherent generative model.
- Major weakness: Training objective mismatch—RNNs trained with squared-error losses, not a valid ELBO; no theoretical guarantee or single unifying objective.
- Major weakness: Missing comparisons to modern deep sequential baselines (VRNN, DMM with Poisson emissions) and recurrent PF variants; incomplete/mislabeled citations.
- Major weakness: Evaluation uses MSE/PMSE which are inappropriate for counts; lacks predictive log-likelihood, uncertainty-aware metrics, and clear hyperparameter tuning protocol.
- Major weakness: Identifiability and scaling issues due to Λ and Ψ additions; no constraints or normalization described to address non-identifiability.
- Major weakness: Notational errors, equation typos, and omitted/incorrect figures reduce clarity and correctness.
- Major weakness: Practical details (RNN architecture, window size sensitivity, K, depth ablations, runtime) are insufficiently reported; limited ablation studies.
- Technical soundness is questionable due to unclear generative story and non-ELBO training, undermining principled Bayesian inference claims.
- Clarity and reproducibility are low: ambiguous definitions, missing implementation details, and grammatical/equation errors.
- Originality is limited: RNN-parameterized Gamma priors are interesting but similar ideas exist; the Probability AutoEncoder idea is novel but not well-justified.
- Significance is potential but limited by methodological and empirical weaknesses; requires principled ELBO training and stronger baselines.
- Key suggestions: Recast as coherent top-down generative model with amortized variational posteriors and optimize a valid ELBO; use held-out log-likelihood and systematic ablations.
- Questions for authors include: provide a coherent generative model and derivation of Eqs. 10/14, justify metric choices and hyperparameter selection, give exact RNN architectures and complexity, address non-identifiability, and compare to VRNN/DMM baselines.
- Recommendation: Reject in current form; revise with principled objective, clearer modeling/inference, stronger baselines, rigorous evaluation, and improved clarity.
