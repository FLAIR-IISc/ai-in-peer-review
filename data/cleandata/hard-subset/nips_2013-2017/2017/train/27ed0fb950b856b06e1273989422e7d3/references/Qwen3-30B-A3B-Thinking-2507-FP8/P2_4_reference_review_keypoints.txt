- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that integrates deep neural networks and RNNs into Poisson Factor Analysis to model temporal dependencies in sequential count data.
- Compares DDPFA to PGDS, LSTM, and standard PFA on synthetic and five real-world datasets.

- Technically sound model with a justified variational inference and mean-field approximation.
- Experimental design is reasonable and evaluates multiple datasets.

- Theoretical justification for why a deep structure outperforms shallow models is weak.
- Experimental results lack statistical significance testing, undermining claims of improvement.
- Insufficient differentiation from prior work (notably [12]), weakening the novelty claim.
- Neural network components are vaguely described (RNN type unspecified), making their contribution unclear.

- Paper is generally well-organized with clear notation and logical model presentation.
- “Ladder-like architecture” and differences from prior work are not clearly explained.
- More detailed explanation of the neural network components and their necessity is needed.

- Combining deep learning with Poisson factorization is reasonable but appears incremental.
- Claim that DDPFA captures long-time dependence is overstated without evidence that depth is necessary.

- The problem is important and potential practical value exists if performance gains are real.
- Marginal performance gains and lack of evidence for necessity/generalizability limit significance.
- Interpretability claims are not convincingly supported by the provided results.

- Strengths: addresses a meaningful problem, decent empirical evaluation, candid discussion of limitations, interesting integration of deep learning and probabilistic modeling.
- Weaknesses: limited novelty, weak theoretical support for depth, no significance testing, poor differentiation from prior work, vague NN description.

- Recommendation: weak acceptance with reservations; requires clearer distinction from prior work, statistical testing, stronger theoretical motivation for depth, and clarification of neural architecture and contributions.
