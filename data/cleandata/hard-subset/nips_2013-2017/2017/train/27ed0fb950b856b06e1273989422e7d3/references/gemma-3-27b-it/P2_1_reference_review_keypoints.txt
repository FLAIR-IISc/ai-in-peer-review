- Paper introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), combining RNNs with hierarchical Poisson factorization to model sequential count data using variational inference and compares to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.

- Addresses an important problem: modeling temporal dependencies in sequential count data.

- Novel approach: integrates RNNs into Poisson factorization to capture short- and long-term dependencies via a deep structure.

- Thorough experimentation across synthetic and real-world datasets with comparisons to established baselines.

- Clear mathematical model formulation building on PFA foundations.

- Visualization and analysis of learned factors enhance interpretability (Figures 2 and 3).

- Variational inference limitations: reliance on mean-field VI and the “pruning” problem may hinder deep hierarchical learning.

- Limited improvement with increased depth (Table 3), questioning the effectiveness of the deep architecture and need for better regularization/training.

- Poor prediction performance on ICEWS dataset with insufficient analysis of root causes (data vs. model architecture).

- Lack of hyperparameter sensitivity analysis to assess robustness.

- Some sections (inference process and loss function) are dense and need clearer explanations.

- Qualitative scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 7.5/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with promising experimental results on multiple datasets.

- Arguments against acceptance: MF-VI reliance, limited depth gains, and poor ICEWS performance needing further investigation.

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Requested revisions: discuss alternative inference methods (e.g., advanced VI or MCMC); analyze why depth doesn’t help and explore regularization/architectures; investigate ICEWS failure modes; include hyperparameter sensitivity discussion.
