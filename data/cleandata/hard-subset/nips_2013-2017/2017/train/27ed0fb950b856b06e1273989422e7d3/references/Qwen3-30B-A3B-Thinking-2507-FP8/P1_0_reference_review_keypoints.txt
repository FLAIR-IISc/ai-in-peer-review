- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) — a hierarchical, RNN‑based extension of Poisson factor models for sequential count data using variational inference.

- Strength — Relevance: Addresses an important problem (time series of counts) with well-motivated need to capture long-term dependencies.

- Strength — Evaluation: Experiments on synthetic data and five real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, Annual Sheep) show consistent improvements over several baselines.

- Strength — Presentation: Model architecture and inference are clearly described, and the “ladder-like” hierarchical design is a thoughtful contribution.

- Strength — Transparency: Authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., latent-dimension “pruning”) and discuss potential remedies.

- Weakness — Theory: Paper lacks rigorous theoretical analysis (convergence, guarantees, model capacity).

- Weakness — Novelty: Contribution is incremental; insufficiently differentiated from recent deep Poisson factorization work.

- Weakness — Baselines: Experimental comparisons are inadequate — no statistical significance testing and weak/underexplained LSTM baseline.

- Weakness — Computation: No discussion of computational complexity or training time relative to baselines.

- Weakness — Claims: Some claims (e.g., “excellent results”) are overstated given modest gains in cases like ICEWS.

- Assessment — Quality: Technically sound implementation but weakened by limited theory and sparse statistical validation.

- Assessment — Clarity: Well organized overall, but the ladder architecture and neural inference components need clearer differentiation and detail.

- Assessment — Originality: Some novelty from combining deep recurrent structure with Poisson factorization, but largely incremental.

- Assessment — Significance: Addresses a meaningful problem but improvements are not substantial enough to be a major advance.

- Recommendation: Reject; paper would benefit from stronger theoretical grounding, clearer novelty claims, more rigorous experiments (incl. significance testing), computational analysis, and clearer explanation of the ladder architecture.
