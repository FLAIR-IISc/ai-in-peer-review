- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): factorizes count tensor with per-time scores and a deep RNN-based hierarchy to capture long-range temporal dependencies.
- Uses Poisson–Gamma augmentation and alternating updates: mean-field coordinate updates for conjugate variables and RNN training via a heuristic “Probability AutoEncoder” squared-loss objective.
- Strength: addresses long-range temporal dependence in count data while preserving factor interpretability and tractable Poisson/gamma core.
- Strength: hierarchical temporal structure is intuitive for multi-scale dependencies and experiments span multiple synthetic and real datasets with qualitative factor analysis.
- Major weakness: the Probability AutoEncoder objective is heuristic, not shown to be an ELBO or valid marginal-likelihood bound.
- Major weakness: posterior updates for deep latents inject network-produced “prior” and “posterior” terms (γ), risking double-counting and lacking coherent generative semantics.
- Major weakness: claims of conjugacy are overstated—dual networks and ladder coupling break standard conjugacy and mean-field optimality.
- Methodological gap: avoids reparameterization for Gamma/Poisson without convincing justification; principled amortized VI would be preferable.
- Evaluation issues: uses MSE/PMSE inappropriate for counts; lacks predictive log-likelihood, uncertainty estimates, and statistical testing.
- Baseline/experimental shortcomings: comparisons are limited/uneven (e.g., LSTM trained with square loss), missing stronger baselines like recurrent PF and deep PF with amortized VI, and hyperparameter parity is unclear.
- Presentation problems: inconsistent notation, typos, duplicated symbols, missing architectural details, and undefined choices for enforcing positivity in network outputs.
- Scalability concern: storing and updating latent counts x_{v,t,k} can be prohibitive; no minibatching, sparsity exploitation, or scalable updates described.
- Quality assessment: technical soundness is questionable due to heuristic objective and unclear posterior updates; empirical gains are undermined by weak metrics and baselines.
- Clarity: manuscript is hard to follow; needs a clear generative graph, ELBO derivation, and full network/training details for reproducibility.
- Originality: idea of combining PF with deep recurrent structure is interesting but related to existing work; the novel training objective lacks theoretical justification.
- Suggested fixes: provide a precise generative model, derive and optimize a valid ELBO with amortized inference, and use reparameterization or suitable augmentations for Gamma.
- Suggested fixes: replace squared-loss objective with likelihood-based training, justify or remove γ terms, or follow ladder VAE derivations for coherent variational families.
- Suggested fixes: evaluate with predictive log-likelihood/perplexity, report uncertainty and ablations, compare to stronger baselines, and ensure fair hyperparameter tuning.
- Suggested fixes: address scalability via sparsity, minibatching, collapsed/amortized handling of x_{v,t,k}, and clarify roles of Λ and Ψ with ablation studies.
- Overall verdict: promising idea but currently undermined by heuristic training, unclear probabilistic semantics, incomplete evaluation, presentation and reproducibility issues — overall rating 4 (leaning reject).
