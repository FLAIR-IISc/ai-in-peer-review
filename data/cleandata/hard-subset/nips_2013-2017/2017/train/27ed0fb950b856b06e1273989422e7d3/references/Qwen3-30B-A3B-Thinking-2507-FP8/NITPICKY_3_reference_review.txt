

# Review of "Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model"

## Summary
This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) model to analyze sequentially observed count data, extending previous work on Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) and Poisson-Gamma Dynamical Systems (PGDS) by incorporating deep neural networks to capture long-time dependence. The model uses a hierarchical structure with multiple layers of latent variables, where each layer's distribution is modeled using recurrent neural networks. Inference is performed via variational inference with a mean-field approximation, and neural networks are trained using a loss function similar to variational autoencoders.

## Strengths
The paper addresses a relevant problem in analyzing sequential count data, which has applications in recommendation systems, text analysis, and network analysis. The authors provide a clear motivation for extending existing models with deep structures to capture long-time dependence. The experimental results on both synthetic and real-world datasets show that DDPFA outperforms baseline methods (PGDS, LSTM, PFA) in most cases, particularly for capturing complex temporal patterns.

## Weaknesses

### Technical Inconsistencies
1. **Model Formulation**: Equation (1) states xvt ∼ Poisson(∑K k=1 θtkφvkλkψv), but this formulation is inconsistent with standard Poisson Factor Analysis (X ∼ Poisson(ΦΘ^T)). More critically, Equation (2) claims xvt = ∑K k=1 xvtk with xvtk ∼ Poisson(θtkφvkλkψv), which is mathematically invalid because the Poisson rate parameter for xvtk depends on k, making the decomposition impossible (the sum of independent Poisson variables with different rates is not Poisson).

2. **Notation Inconsistency**: The paper uses inconsistent notation throughout (e.g., h^(n)_t vs h(n)t vs h(n)t), making it difficult to follow the model. The paper also uses inconsistent notation for variational parameters (φ*vk vs φ*).

3. **Inference Algorithm**: Equation (11) contains typos: "Ψ(θshptk )− logθ rte tk" should be "Ψ(θshp tk) - log(θrte tk)". The paper claims "the distribution mentioned in Eq. 9 can be regarded as an implicit conditional distribution," but this is not properly justified.

### Experimental Flaws
1. **Lack of Statistical Significance**: The paper claims "DDPFA has the best performance" without providing statistical significance tests for the differences between models. This weakens the experimental claims significantly.

2. **Inconsistent Dataset Descriptions**: In the ICEWS dataset description, "all pairs with fewer than twenty-five total events is discarded" should be "are discarded" (subject-verb agreement error).

3. **Missing Baseline Comparison**: The paper compares with PGDS, LSTM, and PFA, but fails to compare with recent deep Poisson factorization models like "Scalable Deep Poisson Factor Analysis for Topic Modeling" (Gan et al., 2015) or "Deep Poisson Factor Modeling with Recurrent Structure" (Hosseini et al., 2017), which would better contextualize the novelty.

### Formatting and Clarity Issues
1. **Multiple Typos**: The paper contains numerous typos (e.g., "st Conference" in the title, "st" instead of "the" in "st Conference", inconsistent use of "rte" vs "rate").

2. **Incomplete References**: The reference list has formatting issues (e.g., "A. Ayan, J. Ghosh, M. Zhou, , , " with unnecessary commas).

3. **Confusing Explanation**: The description of the deep structure (Equations 3-4) is confusingly written, with unclear relationships between layers and inconsistent notation.

## Overall Assessment

While the paper addresses a relevant problem and shows promising empirical results, it contains multiple critical technical inconsistencies, particularly in the model formulation (Equations 1-2), which undermine the theoretical foundation of the proposed approach. The notational inconsistencies and numerous typos throughout the paper significantly reduce its clarity and professionalism. The experimental section lacks statistical validation of the results, which is essential for a claims of superior performance.

The paper's claim of novelty is also questionable. The "deep" aspect of the model is not sufficiently differentiated from existing deep Poisson factorization models, and the paper fails to properly contextualize its contribution relative to the state of the art.

The technical flaws in the model formulation (particularly the invalid Poisson decomposition in Equation 2) are particularly concerning. A model that cannot properly represent the data-generating process cannot be expected to work well in practice, regardless of the inference algorithm.

## Recommendation

Reject. The paper presents a potentially interesting idea but contains critical technical flaws in its model formulation that invalidate the core contribution. The numerous notational inconsistencies and typos further reduce its quality and credibility. While the empirical results are promising, the theoretical foundation is too weak to support the claims made in the paper. A revised version addressing these fundamental issues would be required for reconsideration.
