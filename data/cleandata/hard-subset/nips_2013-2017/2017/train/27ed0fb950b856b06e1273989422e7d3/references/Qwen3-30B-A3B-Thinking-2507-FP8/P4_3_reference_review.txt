

# Review of "Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model"

## Summary of Main Ideas
This paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), a model for analyzing sequentially observed count data. The authors extend previous work on Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) and Poisson-Gamma Dynamical Systems (PGDS) by incorporating deep recurrent neural networks to capture complex temporal dependencies. The model uses a multi-layered structure where each layer's latent variables are connected through recurrent neural networks, allowing the model to capture both short-term and long-term temporal patterns in count data. The paper builds on prior work including "Deep Poisson Factor Analysis based on RBM or SBN" (Gan et al., NIPS 2015) and "Deep Exponential Families" (Ranganath et al., AISTATS 2014), but claims novelty in using deep structures specifically for temporal dependencies in count data.

## Strengths

1. **Technical Approach**: The paper provides a well-structured variational inference approach for the proposed model, with clear mathematical formulations for the model and inference process. The use of mean-field variational inference with recurrent neural networks for approximating complex distributions is technically sound.

2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The authors evaluate the model on four synthetic datasets and five real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS corpus, EBOLA, International Disaster, and Annual Sheep Population), providing a diverse test bed for the model's performance.

3. **Clear Identification of Limitations**: The paper acknowledges significant limitations, including "pruning" (where some latent dimensions contain only noise) and the "uselessness of adding network layers," which demonstrates scientific honesty.

4. **Practical Application**: The model is applied to several real-world applications including recommendation systems, text analysis, and event prediction, demonstrating potential practical utility.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Novelty**: The paper's novelty claim is weak as it doesn't clearly distinguish from prior work, particularly "Deep Poisson Factor Analysis based on RBM or SBN" (Gan et al., NIPS 2015) and "Recurrent Poisson Factorization" (Hosseini et al., NIPS 2016). The paper states that "Deep structure is included in order to capture the long-time dependence" but doesn't provide a convincing theoretical argument for why a deep structure is necessary for temporal dependencies, rather than a shallow recurrent structure.

2. **Inconsistent Experimental Results**: The paper reports that DDPFA outperforms baselines on most datasets, but the performance on the ICEWS prediction task is "not good enough" compared to PGDS. This inconsistency weakens the claim of superiority. Additionally, the paper notes that "expanding the dimension in bottom layer is more useful than in upper layers" and that "performance cannot be improved distinctly by adding more layers," suggesting the deep structure may not provide the claimed advantages.

3. **Lack of Theoretical Justification**: The paper does not provide a theoretical analysis of why the deep structure improves performance for temporal dependencies in count data. The "ladder-like architecture" is mentioned as a solution to "hierarchical latent variable models do not take advantage of the structure" (citing [25]), but the paper doesn't clearly explain how this architecture specifically addresses the limitations.

4. **Insufficient Detail on Implementation**: The paper lacks sufficient detail on the neural network architecture, hyperparameter selection, and training procedures. For instance, it states "the size of the window is 4" without explaining how this was chosen or whether it's optimal.

## Quality Assessment
The paper is technically sound with a well-structured inference approach. However, the experimental results don't consistently support the claimed advantages of the deep structure, and the paper fails to adequately justify why a deep architecture is necessary for temporal dependencies in count data. The identification of limitations like "pruning" and the "uselessness of adding network layers" suggests the model may not be as effective as claimed.

## Clarity Assessment
The paper is generally well-organized with clear sections. However, some parts are confusing, particularly the relationship between the different layers and how the deep structure specifically improves performance. The "ladder-like architecture" is mentioned but not clearly explained, and some notations (like the relationship between h^(n)_t and θ_t) are unclear.

## Originality Assessment
The paper's novelty is limited. While it extends previous work on Poisson factorization, it doesn't sufficiently differentiate itself from prior deep Poisson factorization models. The paper cites "Deep Poisson Factor Analysis based on RBM or SBN" [9,10] but doesn't clearly explain how DDPFA differs from these approaches. The claim that DDPFA captures "long-time dependence" is not convincingly supported by the experimental results, as the model shows inconsistent performance across datasets.

## Significance Assessment
The model has potential significance for applications in recommendation systems, text analysis, and time series analysis. However, the inconsistent experimental results and the identification of significant limitations (pruning, uselessness of adding layers) weaken the claimed significance. The paper does not demonstrate clear advantages over state-of-the-art methods in a consistent manner across multiple datasets.

## Recommendation
The paper presents a technically sound approach but has significant limitations in novelty and experimental validation. The model's claimed advantages are not consistently demonstrated, and the paper fails to adequately justify the deep structure's necessity. While the work is interesting and has some merit, it does not provide sufficient evidence of a significant advance over previous work.

**Arguments for Acceptance**: Well-structured model, comprehensive evaluation on multiple datasets, honest identification of limitations.

**Arguments against Acceptance**: Limited novelty, inconsistent experimental results, lack of theoretical justification for deep structure, insufficient differentiation from prior work.

The paper would benefit from stronger theoretical justification for the deep structure, clearer differentiation from prior work, and more consistent experimental results demonstrating the advantages of the deep approach. As it stands, the paper does not make a sufficiently compelling case for its novelty and significance to be accepted at NIPS.
