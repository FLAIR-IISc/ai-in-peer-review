- Proposes DDPFA: a generative model for sequential count data using Poisson factorization with time-varying scores and deep recurrent latent hierarchy.  
- Inference uses a two-stage variational scheme: mean-field Gamma–Poisson updates plus RNN training via a “Probability AutoEncoder” L2 loss.  
- Experiments on synthetic and five real datasets compare to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA, reporting MSE/PMSE gains and qualitative factor visualizations.  
- Claims to combine Poisson factorization with deep/recurrent latent structures, but relation to prior work (recurrent PF, DEFs, deep PF variants) is not clearly delineated and references are incomplete.  
- Strength: tackles long-range, nonlinear temporal dependence in high-dimensional sparse counts.  
- Strength: modular design retains some Poisson–Gamma conjugacy enabling efficient coordinate updates for φ, λ, ψ, θ.  
- Strength: attempts to address hierarchical VAE issues (e.g., pruning) and explores ladder-like connections.  
- Strength: empirical gains over baselines on several datasets and potentially insightful qualitative analyses.  
- Weakness: “Probability AutoEncoder” loss is not a principled ELBO or variational bound, undermining probabilistic learning claims.  
- Weakness: Conjugacy and Gamma posterior forms with RNN-derived priors are ad hoc and lack a coherent joint-model derivation.  
- Weakness: Notation and clarity problems (ambiguous definitions, unspecified networks/activations, typos) hinder understanding and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Missing code, hyperparameter/training details, and incomplete specification of network parameterizations.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to MSE/PMSE; lacks predictive log-likelihood/perplexity and stronger, relevant baselines (DEFs, deep PF RBM/SBN, sequential VAEs, NB-process dynamical models).  
- Weakness: Insufficient ablations (Λ, Ψ, depth, window size c, ladder connections) and no rigorous diagnosis of pruning/limited depth gains.  
- Weakness: No analysis of computational complexity, memory scaling, or runtime comparisons to competitors.  
- Quality assessment: interesting design but inference objective and some updates are heuristic, making technical soundness questionable.  
- Clarity assessment: manuscript hard to follow due to inconsistent notation and missing modeling/inference details.  
- Originality assessment: plausible novel combination but overlap with existing deep/recurrent PF work not clearly separated.  
- Significance assessment: potentially useful direction, but impact limited by lack of principled training objective and weak evaluations.  
- Questions for authors: provide the exact joint model and ELBO, and show how RNN parameters are trained under a principled variational scheme.  
- Questions for authors: how is positivity enforced for shape/rate outputs and how are valid Gamma parameters ensured during training?  
- Questions for authors: what is per-iteration computational complexity and how do runtimes compare to PGDS/PFA/LSTM?  
- Questions for authors: please report predictive log-likelihoods and include stronger baselines (DEF, deep PF SBN/RBM, sequential VAEs, NB-process dynamics).  
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a relevant problem, shows promising empirical improvements, and proposes a novel direction combining deep recurrent structure with PF.  
- Cons for acceptance: lacks theoretical grounding for inference, ambiguous model/specification, limited evaluations, and reproducibility/writing issues.  
- Recommendation: do not accept in current form; requires a principled ELBO or justified objective, precise model/inference specification, stronger likelihood-based evaluations and baselines, and improved clarity/reproducibility.
