- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): Poisson factorization with RNN-parameterized Gamma priors for time-varying factors and a two-stage inference (mean-field VI + “Probability AutoEncoder” training).

- Builds on Poisson factorization and deep temporal models (GBN, Deep Exponential Families, DMM/VRNN); novelty claimed in replacing parametric Markov dynamics with RNN-parameterized Gamma priors but training objective is ad hoc.

- Strength: tackles long-term dependencies in high-dimensional sparse count time series by combining interpretable Poisson factorization with flexible neural dynamics.

- Strength: conjugate updates for Φ, Λ, Ψ, and Θ can be efficient and qualitative factor trajectories look useful.

- Major weakness: generative model and inference are not rigorously specified—unclear rate parameters, positivity enforcement, inconsistent indexing, and invalid Bayesian update by adding prior/posterior shape parameters.

- Major weakness: the Probability AutoEncoder objective is ad hoc, not an ELBO or principled bound, and the dismissal of variational methods is unconvincing.

- Major weakness: ambiguity about temporal dynamics and “deep” structure—unclear whether true recurrence/BPTT is used, top-layer transitions unspecified, and depth appears unnecessary per reported results.

- Major weakness: experimental evaluation is insufficient—suboptimal metrics (MSE/PMSE), missing baselines (DMM/VRNN, dynamic topic models, RPF), insufficient hyperparameter/architecture/runtime details, and lacking multi-step forecasts and uncertainty quantification.

- Major weakness: claim that global scalars Ψ and Λ impose smoothness is unsupported.

- Clarity/reproducibility: paper has notation inconsistencies, grammatical errors, missing definitions, and no code or reproducibility plan; needs a thorough rewrite and pseudocode.

- Originality/significance: idea is interesting but not clearly differentiated from existing work and lacks principled training and strong empirical validation.

- Suggestions: derive a proper ELBO and use amortized variational inference (reparameterization or BBVI), clarify temporal model choice, use appropriate metrics and stronger baselines, report runtimes/ablations, and provide uncertainty analyses.

- Pros: addresses an important problem, conceptually appealing PF+neural dynamics, and shows some promising results.

- Cons: inference objective unjustified, technical/notation errors, weak experiments and baselines, depth not convincingly beneficial, and poor clarity.

- Recommendation: Reject (high confidence).
