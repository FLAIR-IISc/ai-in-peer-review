- Paper proposes DDPFA: Poisson factorization for sequential counts with RNN-parameterized hierarchical Gamma latent states (prior and posterior networks).
- Inference alternates conjugate mean-field Gamma-Poisson updates via latent-count augmentation and RNN training with a custom "Probability AutoEncoder" L2-style objective instead of an ELBO.
- Experiments on synthetic and five real datasets compare DDPFA to PGDS, PFA, and LSTM using MSE/PMSE; authors claim DDPFA wins on most datasets.
- Builds on PF, dynamic PF (PGDS), deep latent models (DEFs/GBNs), and amortized inference; similar ideas exist in recurrent Poisson/recommendation literature.
- Strength: tackles long-range dependencies in high-dimensional sparse count sequences, a timely and important problem.
- Strength: combines conjugate PF structure with amortized neural temporal components while retaining tractable updates for many latent variables.
- Strength: attempts multi-scale temporal modeling via depth/windows and provides some interpretability anecdotes (e.g., ICEWS).
- Major concern: the "Probability AutoEncoder" objective is ad hoc with no theoretical justification as an ELBO or consistent estimator.
- Major concern: hierarchical Gamma updates that sum shape parameters from two RNNs are nonstandard and lack a clear derivation or probabilistic semantics.
- Major concern: posterior networks use future windows (smoothing) during training but the paper fails to specify a prediction protocol that prevents future data leakage.
- Major concern: notation and conditional equations contain typos, index ambiguities, and dimensionally unclear expressions.
- Clarity issues: writing, undefined notation, and incomplete references; generative model and inference factorization are not precisely specified.
- Reproducibility issues: training algorithm details (prediction pipeline, batching, initialization, optimizer, runtime/complexity) are insufficient.
- Originality: idea is a reasonable extension of prior work but not strongly novel; main novelty is the two-network Gamma-shape parameterization and custom objective, which lacks grounding.
- Evaluation weaknesses: MSE/PMSE are not ideal for counts; predictive log-likelihood or Poisson deviance and rolling multi-step forecasts with uncertainty are needed.
- Baselines and tuning: LSTM details unclear, important baselines (dynamic topic models, recurrent PF, Hawkes/Poisson RNNs) are missing, and fairness of hyperparameter tuning is not addressed.
- Empirical claims about depth and long-range dependence are weakly supported; ablations are limited and inconclusive.
- Suggested fixes: derive a principled ELBO or use established VI/SG-MCMC, clarify generative conditionals and parametrization, and define a leak-free prediction protocol.
- Suggested experiments: report predictive log-likelihood/Poisson deviance, rolling forecasts, confidence intervals, stronger baselines, and comprehensive ablations (window size, depth, components).
- Suggested implementation/scale work: provide algorithm pseudocode, complexity analysis, sparse/minibatch SVI approach, and training time comparisons.
- Pros summary: timely problem, principled use of conjugacy where possible, ladder-style idea, and some qualitative interpretability.
- Cons summary: unprincipled inference objective, potential data leakage, unclear probabilistic semantics, poor clarity, and insufficient empirical validation.
- Recommendation: Weak reject; promising direction but needs ELBO-based inference, clearer derivations, better writing, and stronger experiments to be publishable.
