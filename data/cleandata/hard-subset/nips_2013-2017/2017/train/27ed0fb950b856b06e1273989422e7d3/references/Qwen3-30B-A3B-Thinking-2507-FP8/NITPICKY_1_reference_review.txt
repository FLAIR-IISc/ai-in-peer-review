

# Review of "Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model"

This paper proposes a "Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model" (DDPFA) that extends Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) with a deep, hierarchical structure to capture complex temporal dependencies in count data. The authors claim their model outperforms existing methods like PGDS, LSTM, and PFA on synthetic and real-world datasets.

## Major Technical Issues

The paper contains several significant technical inconsistencies that undermine the credibility of the proposed model:

1. **Model Formulation Errors**: The core model equation (1) states "xvt ∼ Poisson(∑K k=1 θtkφvkλkψv)" but Equation (2) claims "xvtk ∼ Poisson(θtkφvkλkψv)". This implies λk and ψv are scaling factors for each factor and feature, which contradicts standard Poisson factorization where the parameter should be θtkφvk. The paper never properly justifies why λk and ψv are needed as additional scaling parameters.

2. **Inconsistent Notation**: The paper uses inconsistent spacing around mathematical operators (e.g., "∑K k=1" instead of "∑_{k=1}^K") and inconsistent use of subscripts (e.g., "θtk" vs "θ_t_k"). This makes the mathematical formulation difficult to follow.

3. **Variational Inference Errors**: Equation (11) for the auxiliary variables contains incorrect terms for the Gamma distribution parameters. The update should depend on the shape parameters, but the paper incorrectly includes both shape and rate parameters in the same expression.

4. **Misrepresentation of "Deep" Structure**: The paper claims to use "deep structure" but the hierarchical layers described in Equations (3)-(4) are not truly deep learning components. The "neural networks" are used to parameterize Gamma distributions but don't actually learn complex representations. The model is more accurately described as a hierarchical Bayesian model rather than a deep learning model.

5. **Inadequate Experimental Analysis**: The paper claims "excellent results" but provides minimal statistical evidence. For instance, in Table 2, DDPFA outperforms baselines on most datasets but the paper doesn't provide p-values or confidence intervals to determine if these differences are statistically significant. The claim that "PGDS fails in catching complicated time dependence" in EBOLA and ASP datasets is unsubstantiated.

## Minor but Significant Issues

1. **Formatting Inconsistencies**: The paper contains numerous spacing errors around mathematical operators (e.g., "xvt = ∑K k=1 xvtk" should be "x_{vt} = \sum_{k=1}^K x_{vtk}"). These minor formatting issues accumulate to create an impression of carelessness.

2. **Inconsistent Terminology**: The paper uses "Poisson Factorize Analysis" in the introduction but later refers to "Poisson Factor Analysis" - a critical inconsistency in technical terminology.

3. **Overstated Contributions**: The paper claims to "capture the long-time dependence" but the model uses a fixed window size (c=4) as defined in Equation (3), which limits the effective time horizon the model can capture. This contradicts the claim of "long-time dependence."

4. **Missing Details**: The paper doesn't provide sufficient information about the neural network architectures used (number of hidden units, activation functions, etc.), making replication impossible.

## Strengths

The paper makes a reasonable attempt to extend existing Poisson factorization models to capture temporal dependencies. The experimental design is generally sound, and the authors correctly acknowledge limitations like the "pruning" problem in their model.

## Overall Assessment

While the paper presents a novel extension to Poisson factorization models, the technical errors, inconsistent notation, and overstatement of the "deep" aspects of the model significantly undermine its contribution. The paper fails to properly justify why λk and ψv are necessary components, and the model's "deep" structure is not truly deep learning. The experimental results are not presented with sufficient statistical rigor to support the claims of superiority.

The paper would benefit from major revisions to correct the mathematical formulation, clarify the model's relationship to existing deep learning models, and provide more rigorous statistical analysis of the results. As written, the technical inconsistencies make it difficult to assess the true contribution of the work.

## Recommendation

**Reject**. The paper contains multiple technical errors that fundamentally undermine the proposed model's validity. While the problem addressed is relevant to NIPS, the paper fails to present a technically sound solution. The numerous minor formatting and notational inconsistencies further indicate a lack of attention to detail that is unacceptable for a NIPS submission. The authors would need to substantially revise the paper to correct the mathematical formulation before it could be considered for publication.
