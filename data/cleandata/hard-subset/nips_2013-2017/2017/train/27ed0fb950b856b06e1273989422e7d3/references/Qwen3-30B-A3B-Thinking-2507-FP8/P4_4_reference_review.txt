

# Review: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model

## Summary
This paper proposes the Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Analysis (DDPFA) model for analyzing sequentially observed count data. The authors build upon previous work in Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) and Poisson-Gamma Dynamical Systems (PGDS), incorporating deep recurrent structures to capture long-time dependence in time series count data. The model extends PFA by introducing a deep hierarchical structure with multiple layers of latent variables connected through recurrent neural networks, while maintaining the Gamma-Poisson conjugacy for inference. The paper compares DDPFA with PGDS, LSTM, and standard PFA on synthetic and real-world datasets, claiming improved performance in most cases.

## Quality
The technical approach is sound, utilizing a well-established Gamma-Poisson conjugate structure with variational inference, which is appropriate for the problem domain. The model description is mathematically rigorous, and the inference algorithm is clearly detailed. However, the paper lacks theoretical justification for why the deep structure is necessary for capturing long-time dependence, and the experimental design has limitations. The comparison only includes three baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA), omitting more recent deep learning approaches for time series count data. While the paper demonstrates improved performance on several datasets, it does not provide statistical significance testing for the results, and the failure case in ICEWS prediction is not adequately analyzed. The paper acknowledges limitations like "pruning" and the "uselessness of adding network layers" but doesn't provide sufficient evidence or analysis to support these claims.

## Clarity
The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a logical flow from problem statement to model description to experiments. The mathematical notation is consistent, though dense, and the model description would benefit from more intuitive explanations of the deep structure's purpose. The experimental section clearly presents results with tables, though the lack of visualizations (e.g., Figure 2 and 3 are referenced but not provided) makes it difficult to fully assess the qualitative claims about factor activation. The paper provides sufficient detail for an expert to reproduce the model, though some aspects of the neural network architecture and training procedure could be clarified.

## Originality
The paper combines Poisson factorization with deep learning structures, which has some novelty. However, the paper does not sufficiently differentiate its approach from prior work. The introduction cites references [9, 10, 12, 23] on deep Poisson factorization, but fails to clearly articulate how DDPFA differs from these methods. The paper claims "deep structure is included to capture long-time dependence," but doesn't provide a theoretical foundation for why this specific deep structure is superior to alternatives. The ladder-like architecture mentioned in Section 5 is noted as a solution to "pruning," but the paper doesn't adequately reference or compare with similar approaches in recent literature. Compared to NIPS proceedings, this work appears to be a modest extension of existing deep Poisson factorization approaches rather than a significant new contribution.

## Significance
The problem addressed (analyzing time series count data) is important and has applications in multiple domains. The experimental results show promising performance improvements over baselines on several datasets, suggesting potential utility for practitioners. However, the significance is limited by the narrow scope of baselines compared, the lack of comparison with more recent methods, and the failure to demonstrate clear advantages in all cases (e.g., poor performance on ICEWS prediction). The paper doesn't convincingly demonstrate how this work advances the state-of-the-art beyond incremental improvements over PGDS or PFA.

## Strengths
- Addresses a relevant problem with practical applications
- Well-structured model description with appropriate mathematical foundation
- Provides comprehensive experimental results on both synthetic and real-world datasets
- Clear description of the inference algorithm using variational methods
- Acknowledges limitations and potential problems (e.g., "pruning" issue)

## Weaknesses
- Insufficient theoretical justification for the deep structure
- Limited baseline comparison (only three methods)
- Failure case in ICEWS prediction not adequately analyzed
- Doesn't clearly differentiate from prior deep Poisson factorization work
- Lacks statistical significance testing for experimental results
- Doesn't provide sufficient evidence for claimed "pruning" and "uselessness of adding layers" issues

## Arguments for and against acceptance
**For acceptance**: The paper presents a well-constructed model for a meaningful problem with promising experimental results. The deep structure approach shows potential for capturing complex temporal dependencies in count data, and the technical details are sound. The work builds logically on previous literature and provides a valuable contribution to the area.

**Against acceptance**: The novelty is marginal compared to recent work in deep Poisson factorization, and the paper fails to adequately distinguish itself from prior methods. The experimental evaluation is limited in scope, and the theoretical foundation for the proposed approach is weak. The paper doesn't convincingly demonstrate that the deep structure provides significant advantages over simpler alternatives.

## Overall Assessment
The paper presents a technically sound approach to time series count data analysis with some promising experimental results. However, the novelty is limited, the comparison with prior work is insufficient, and the theoretical justification for the deep structure is weak. While the work is a reasonable extension of existing methods, it does not represent a significant advancement of the state-of-the-art in a way that would justify acceptance at NIPS. The paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison with recent deep learning approaches for time series count data, stronger theoretical justification for the deep structure, and more comprehensive analysis of the limitations and failure cases.
