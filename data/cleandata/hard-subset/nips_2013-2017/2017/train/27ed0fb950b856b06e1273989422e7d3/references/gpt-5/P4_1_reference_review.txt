Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Analysis (DDPFA) for sequential count data. Observations xvt are modeled via a Poisson factorization with feature- and component-wise scaling: xvtk ~ Poisson(θtk φvk λk ψv), so xvt ~ Poisson(∑k θtk φvk λk ψv). Temporal dependence is introduced by a deep hierarchy of latent variables h(n)t, where each layer uses a recurrent neural network (RNN) to parameterize implicit conditional distributions over the next layer below within a finite window of size c. The bottom layer provides a Gamma prior p(θt | h(0)t−c:t) with shape controlled by an RNN, and intermediate layers have Gamma priors/posteriors parameterized by feedforward/backward RNNs. Inference is performed in two stages: (i) mean-field variational updates for the Gamma-Poisson part (using standard auxiliary-count allocations xvtk and conjugacy) while holding the RNN parameters fixed; (ii) updating RNN parameters via a “Probability AutoEncoder” loss that matches expectations of encoder/decoder distributions and a reconstruction term.

This work is positioned relative to Poisson factorization and dynamic count models (e.g., Beta-Negative Binomial/PFA, PGDS), deep latent variable models (Deep Exponential Families, Gamma Belief Networks, deep PF via RBM/SBN), temporal deep models (NADE, LSTM), and recurrent Poisson factorization for recommendation. The goal is to capture both short-term nonlinear dynamics and long-range dependence via depth, aiming to improve prediction and interpretability over PGDS/PFA and LSTM.

Quality
Strengths
- The base count model builds on well-studied Poisson-Gamma conjugacy with auxiliary latent allocations, enabling scalable variational updates for φ, λ, ψ, θ given fixed RNN-induced priors.
- The idea of using RNNs to parameterize time-varying Gamma priors to enrich temporal dynamics is sensible and has potential to capture nonlinear dependencies beyond Markovian Gamma chains.

Weaknesses
- The inference objective for the neural components is not derived from a valid ELBO. Equations (15–18) introduce an ad-hoc squared-error loss between expectations of encoder/decoder distributions and a reconstruction term that replaces likelihood with ‖X − E[Ψ]E[Λ]E[Φ]E[p(Θ|H(0))]‖^2. This departs from a principled variational objective and lacks guarantees. No derivation connects it to maximizing a lower bound on marginal likelihood.
- The paper claims conditional conjugacy for θ with implicit, RNN-parameterized priors. While Gamma-Gamma-Poisson can be conjugate if the prior is Gamma with fixed parameters, here the shape parameter is a random function of latent h. The presented mean-field updates effectively condition on expectations from q(h), but this approximation is not rigorously justified, and no ELBO is written for the full model coupling Θ and H.
- The “Probability AutoEncoder” training alternates with coordinate-ascent VI, but convergence properties, stability, and effect on the global objective are unclear. No ablation compares this to a standard amortized VI/ELBO with reparameterization where possible, score-function estimators, or alternative regularizers (e.g., KLs between Gamma distributions).
- Experimental evaluation lacks detail and rigor: tables/figures are referenced but not fully specified in the text; no standard predictive metrics (e.g., held-out Poisson log-likelihood/perplexity) or uncertainty quantification; no confidence intervals; unclear train/validation/test splits; hyperparameter selection and fairness across methods are insufficiently discussed.
- Several mathematical inaccuracies/typos affect clarity and credibility (see Clarity).

Clarity
- The manuscript contains numerous typos and notational inconsistencies (e.g., Eq. 7 appears to duplicate φv; Eq. 8 mixes φ/Φ and βθ/β; “Bernoulli-Poisson link” uses 1(x>1) instead of 1(x>0); ambiguous use of dots in xv·k; inconsistent subscripts).
- The role of λk and ψv could be better motivated with ablations (what is gained beyond absorbing into Θ or Φ?).
- The hierarchical temporal structure (what is h(N) vs h(0), exact dependencies, windowing, and how depth induces long-term memory) needs a clearer graphical model and precise conditionals.
- References are frequently incomplete; key related work (e.g., PGDS: Schein et al., NeurIPS 2016; deep gamma belief networks; recurrent PF for recommendation) should be fully cited and contrasted.

Originality
- Using RNNs to parameterize Gamma priors for Poisson factorization dynamics is a reasonable extension that differentiates from PGDS’s linear transitions and from static deep PF models. However, related ideas exist in recurrent Poisson factorization and deep exponential families; the novelty resides mainly in the particular hierarchical RNN-Gamma construction and the proposed training scheme. The latter is unconventional but insufficiently justified.

Significance
- If made principled and scalable, the approach could be useful for sequential count modeling with complex temporal dependencies, relevant to text/event streams and recommendations. However, the current empirical evidence is limited: improvements are reported on a few datasets, but without strong baselines (e.g., PGDS with VI; DEF/GBN dynamics; state-space PF with nonlinear transitions) or robust metrics. The lack of a principled objective diminishes reproducibility and potential impact.

Pros
- Addresses an important problem: long-range, nonlinear dynamics in count data.
- Combines Poisson factorization with learnable temporal priors, potentially yielding better predictive performance and interpretability.
- Uses scalable variational updates for the conjugate parts.

Cons
- Inference objective for neural components is ad-hoc and not tied to an ELBO; theoretical soundness is weak.
- Clarity issues, numerous typos, and incomplete references.
- Experimental evaluation lacks standard likelihood-based metrics, ablations, and statistical rigor.
- Missing discussion of computational complexity and scalability, and no code availability.

Suggestions for improvement
- Derive a proper ELBO for the full model, including Θ and H, and optimize it jointly. Consider reparameterizing Gamma variables (or using implicit reparameterization) or score-function estimators with control variates; alternatively, use auxiliary-variable bounds tailored for Gamma-Poisson models (e.g., augment-and-conquer tricks).
- Provide a clear graphical model, precise conditionals, and constraints ensuring positive Gamma parameters from RNNs.
- Strengthen experiments: report held-out Poisson log-likelihood/perplexity, predictive log-likelihood for next time step, with train/val/test splits and error bars; include ablations (no λ/ψ; shallow vs deep; window size; with/without ladder connections; alternative inference); compare to stronger baselines (PGDS with VI; recurrent Poisson factorization; deep gamma belief networks with temporal dependencies).
- Fix mathematical errors and complete references. Correct the Bernoulli-Poisson link and multinomial allocation expressions.
- Discuss computational cost, memory, and runtime scaling with V, T, K, depth, and window size; include complexity analysis.

Recommendation
The paper tackles a relevant problem and proposes an interesting direction (RNN-parameterized Gamma priors for dynamic Poisson factorization). However, the current submission lacks a principled inference objective, contains clarity and correctness issues, and does not provide sufficiently rigorous empirical validation. Substantial revisions are needed to meet NeurIPS standards.
