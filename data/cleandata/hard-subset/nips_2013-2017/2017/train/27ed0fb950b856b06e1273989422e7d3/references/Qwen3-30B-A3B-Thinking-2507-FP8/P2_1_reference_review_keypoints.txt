- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): a deep hierarchical Poisson factor model with RNN-based implicit distributions and variational inference for sequential count data.  
- Builds on PFA and PGDS to capture long-term temporal dependencies via multiple latent layers.  
- Uses mean-field variational inference and evaluates on synthetic data plus five real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, ASP) against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  
- Technically sound formulation with proper variational framework and comprehensive experimental design (MSE and PMSE metrics).  
- Weak theoretical justification for why a deep hierarchical structure is required over simpler recurrent models like LSTM.  
- DDPFA underperforms PGDS on the ICEWS prediction task, and the paper fails to adequately explain this discrepancy.  
- Acknowledges a "pruning" issue where some latent dimensions capture only noise, but provides insufficient analysis or mitigation strategies.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, but dense mathematical notation and limited intuitive explanations hinder accessibility.  
- Experimental details are mostly reproducible, but some hyperparameters and NN architecture specifics are under-specified.  
- The core idea of combining deep models with Poisson factorization for temporal counts is novel and meaningful.  
- Novelty limited by prior deep factor models (e.g., Deep Exponential Families, Deep Poisson Factor Analysis); differences vs. prior deep temporal methods are not fully articulated.  
- Significance is high due to important application domains, but performance gains are inconsistent across datasets and adding layers does not always help.  
- Strengths: addresses an important problem, introduces multilayer latent structure, and provides broad empirical evaluation while acknowledging limitations.  
- Weaknesses: weak theoretical motivation for depth, limited analysis of failure cases (ICEWS, pruning), and insufficient comparison to recent deep time-series approaches.  
- Recommendation: weak accept — a solid, technically sound contribution that needs stronger theoretical justification, deeper failure analysis, and broader benchmarking.  
- Suggested improvements: provide rigorous theoretical reasons for depth, explain ICEWS performance, compare to modern deep time-series models, and analyze/mitigate the pruning issue.
