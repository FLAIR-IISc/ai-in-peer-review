- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): integrates Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs to model sequential count data.
- Learns parameters via variational inference and compares performance to PFA, PGDS, and LSTM on synthetic and real datasets.
- Situates work well within related literature on dynamic count models and deep Bayesian/deep Poisson factorization approaches.
- Novelty: combining PFA with deep recurrent networks to capture temporal dependencies is a new contribution.
- Flexibility: DDPFA can model short- and long-term dependencies and learn hierarchical temporal representations.
- Empirical strength: often outperforms baselines in fitting tasks; factor activation visualizations provide intuitive support.
- Clear problem formulation and motivation for a more expressive model than existing methods.
- Uses variational inference for tractable, scalable estimation in a complex model.
- Inference clarity: derivations and variational update explanations are dense and difficult to follow; mean-field treatment is underexplained.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: lacks systematic exploration; reported hyperparameters appear somewhat arbitrary.
- Limitations discussion is incomplete — computational cost and specific failures (e.g., ICEWS prediction) are not thoroughly analyzed.
- Pruning and proposed ladder architecture are mentioned but not explored in detail; discussion feels tacked on.
- Baselines: should compare to stronger modern sequence models (e.g., Transformers) for a more comprehensive benchmark.
- Overall assessment: promising and potentially significant, but needs clearer inference exposition, hyperparameter analysis, and fuller limitation discussion.
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on revisions addressing clarity, hyperparameter robustness, and limitations.
