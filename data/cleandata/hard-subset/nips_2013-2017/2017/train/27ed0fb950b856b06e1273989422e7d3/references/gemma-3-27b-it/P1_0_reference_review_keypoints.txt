- Proposal: DDPFA replaces Gamma transitions in Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs to model factor time series for sequential count data.  
- Training/inference: Uses variational inference (coordinate ascent) and compares against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Novelty: Combines PFA with deep RNNs to capture short- and long-range temporal dependencies not handled by traditional dynamic PFA.  
- Probabilistic framework: Retains Poisson likelihood and Gamma priors, enabling principled uncertainty quantification versus deterministic RNNs.  
- Experimental validation: Reasonably comprehensive experiments show benefits on datasets with complex temporal patterns.  
- Motivation & exposition: Clear motivation and detailed description of the model and inference updates.  
- Hyperparameter concern: Insufficient discussion and sensitivity analysis of many hyperparameters (including RNN architecture), hurting reproducibility and generalizability.  
- Scalability concern: Limited analysis of computational cost and scalability of training deep RNNs with variational inference on very large datasets.  
- Ladder architecture: Ladder-like extension is introduced with sparse implementation detail and little experimental backing, making its contribution unclear.  
- Interpretability: Claims of improved interpretability are under-supported; more detailed examples linking latent factors to events/features are needed.  
- Evaluation metric: Reliance on MSE alone is inadequate for count data; count-specific metrics (e.g., perplexity) should be included.  
- Inconsistent results: DDPFA underperforms on ICEWS, and the paper lacks a deeper investigation into this discrepancy.  
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses an important problem, offers a novel and technically sound approach, shows promising results, and provides detailed methodology.  
- Cons for acceptance: Hyperparameter sensitivity, scalability, unclear ladder impact, limited interpretability analysis, and overreliance on MSE.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—request hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, controlled evaluation of the ladder architecture, deeper factor interpretation, additional count-specific metrics, and investigation of ICEWS performance.
