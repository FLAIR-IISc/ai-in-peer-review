- Paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): Poisson factorization with deep RNN-parameterized Gamma temporal hierarchy for sequential count matrices.
- Claimed novelty: combining PF conjugacy with RNN-based deep temporal dependencies to model long-range structure.
- Strength: tackles long-range temporal dependence in high-dimensional sparse count time series.
- Strength: tries to leverage conjugacy in PF alongside flexible RNN temporal models.
- Strength: experiments cover diverse synthetic and real datasets and report fit and one-step prediction.
- Strength: authors transparently discuss issues observed (e.g., pruning, limited utility of deeper layers).
- Weakness: generative model is not rigorously specified—implicit RNN-Gamma conditionals, parameter roles, positivity/stability, and top-layer treatment are unclear.
- Weakness: training objective is ad hoc—claims of VI lack an ELBO, and the “Probability AutoEncoder” squared-error loss is not a principled likelihood or divergence.
- Weakness: derivations and notation contain typos and gaps; key steps (e.g., how RNN outputs produce Gamma posteriors) are not derived.
- Weakness: evaluation uses MSE/PMSE on raw counts rather than held-out predictive log-likelihood/perplexity suitable for probabilistic models.
- Weakness: baselines are incomplete or mismatched (e.g., LSTM not adapted to counts); recent strong related methods are omitted.
- Weakness: hyperparameters (K, window c) lack cross-validation/justification and experimental fairness to baselines is unclear.
- Weakness: clarity and presentation are poor—grammar/typos, incomplete references, missing architectural and optimization details, and no uncertainty/statistical testing.
- Weakness: authors report deeper layers add little and pruning occurs, undermining the central motivation for depth.
- Quality assessment: technical soundness is questionable due to missing ELBO and ad hoc training; evidence is mixed and metrics weak.
- Originality: idea of RNN-parameterized Gamma conditionals is somewhat novel but hard to assess without a principled objective.
- Significance: potentially valuable if formalized, but current form limits impact and does not establish state-of-the-art.
- Key requests for authors: provide full generative model, a precise training objective/ELBO, positivity/enforcement details, RNN architectures/nonlinearities, derivation for Gamma posteriors, held-out log-likelihoods and stronger baselines, cross-validation/ablations, and code to reproduce results.
- Pros summary: addresses an important problem, interesting idea combining PF with deep temporal latent variables, broad datasets, some improvements reported.
- Cons summary: unclear/incomplete model and objective, weak baselines/metrics, poor writing and missing details, and unsupported claim that depth helps.
- Recommendation: Reject.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium-high.
