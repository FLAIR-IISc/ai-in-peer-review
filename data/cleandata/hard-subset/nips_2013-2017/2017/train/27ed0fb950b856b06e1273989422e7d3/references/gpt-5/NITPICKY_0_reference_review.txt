Summary
This paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), a hierarchical dynamic Poisson factor model for sequential count data. Counts x_vt are modeled as Poisson with rate sum_k θ_tk φ_vk λ_k ψ_v, where θ_tk are time-varying factor scores. To capture complex short- and long-range temporal dependencies, the authors introduce a deep hierarchy of latent variables h^(n)_t with windowed recurrent neural networks specifying “implicit” conditional priors p(θ_t | h^(0)_{t−c:t}) and p(h^(n)_t | h^(n+1)_{t−c:t}). Mean-field VI with auxiliary latent counts leverages Poisson–Gamma conjugacy for Φ, Λ, Ψ, Θ, while the RNN parameters are trained via a “Probability AutoEncoder” objective based on L2 distances between expectations rather than an ELBO. Experiments on synthetic data and several real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS corpus, Ebola, International Disaster, Sheep) compare against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA using MSE/PMSE, suggesting improvements in many settings.

Relation to prior work
The model builds on Poisson factorization for counts (e.g., Zhou et al., Beta-NB/PFA; Gopalan et al., HPF), dynamic PFA with gamma Markov or transition matrices (e.g., PGDS), and deep generative models (DEFs, deep PFA, GBN). It attempts to fuse RNN-based temporal priors with Poisson factorization. However, the paper does not clearly position itself against recurrent Poisson factorization variants, nor does it provide a principled variational objective akin to VAE/SGVB for the deep temporal prior.

Strengths
- Ambitious attempt to combine PFAs with deep, windowed RNN-based temporal priors to capture long-range dependencies.
- Maintains conjugacy for Φ, Λ, Ψ, Θ via auxiliary counts, enabling efficient coordinate updates conditional on RNN parameters.
- Incorporates feature-wise sparsity (ψ_v) and component weights (λ_k), which can improve interpretability sparsity-wise.
- Empirical results on diverse datasets suggest potential gains over PGDS/PFA in some tasks.

Weaknesses and technical concerns
Quality/technical soundness
- Variational objective: Eq. (6) minimizes KL(p||q), which is intractable; standard mean-field VI minimizes KL(q||p). The text then claims this “is equal to maximize the ELBO,” which contradicts the stated objective.
- “Probability AutoEncoder”: The training loss (Eqs. 15–18) is ad hoc—L2 between expectations of conditional distributions and reconstruction losses. No derivation as an ELBO or bound; no justification that this objective yields consistent inference or calibrates uncertainty. Dismissing reparameterization/BBVI because “few samples are drawn” is unconvincing.
- Implicit priors: Conjugate updates for θ require Gamma priors; the paper sets the Gamma shape α_θtk from an RNN and a fixed rate β, but does not specify how positivity is enforced or how network outputs are regularized. Similar ambiguity for α_h, γ_h.
- Incorrect/ inconsistent updates:
  - Eq. (8): For λ_k posterior, the rate is written as βθ + θ·k Σ_v ψ_v φ_vk; it should involve β_λ, not β_θ.
  - Eq. (7) multinomial responsibility includes φ twice and appears to omit ψ_v (typo and dimension mismatch).
  - Eq. (5) uses q(ψ_k|ψ*_k) although ψ is indexed by v elsewhere; also duplicates Φ in the conditioning of λ_k in Eq. (8).
- Hierarchical/top-layer dynamics: h^(N)_t is said to be generated from a Gamma distribution with h^(N)_{t−c−1:t−1} as prior information, but the exact conditional form is unspecified, and its relationship to the RNN parametrization is unclear.
- The claimed “ladder-like architecture” is introduced post hoc in the experiments without a clear definition earlier.
- No discussion of identifiability or scale confounding among θ, φ, λ, ψ. Without constraints or normalization, the model may be ill-conditioned.

Clarity/presentation
- Numerous grammatical errors, typos, and notational inconsistencies (e.g., “recommend systems”, “fanatical data”, “nest hierarchical Dirichlet”, “Natrually”, “thi figure”; inconsistent indices for ψ, misuse of β subscripts).
- Figures and tables (1–3, Tables 1–3) are referenced but not provided here; the narrative relies on them.
- Many references are incomplete or inaccurate (e.g., “W. Max” for Kingma & Welling; missing venues/years; several entries lack publication details). Prior art coverage is incomplete.

Originality
- Using RNNs to parametrize Gamma prior parameters for temporal Poisson factor scores is a reasonable idea, but related efforts exist (e.g., recurrent PF for recommendations, DEF-based count models, gamma belief networks with temporal structure). The novelty is less in the components than in their particular combination and the ad hoc training loss. The paper does not convincingly differentiate from PGDS variants or prior deep PFAs.

Significance
- Potentially useful if it robustly improves long-range temporal modeling in high-dimensional counts. However, due to the lack of a principled objective, implementation details, and strong baselines/evaluations, it is hard to assess impact. The authors themselves acknowledge “pruning” and limited gains from added layers.

Empirical evaluation
- Metrics (MSE/PMSE) are not standard for topic models/time-count models; predictive log-likelihood/held-out perplexity is more common. No uncertainty-aware metrics.
- Baselines: LSTM details (likelihood, link function for counts) are unclear; training/tuning fairness not established. Only one split; no error bars or statistical tests.
- ICEWS/NIPS preprocessing choices not fully justified; ICEWS restricted to 2003; for NIPS, standard document-topic evaluations are missing.

Questions for authors
- Provide a principled variational objective (ELBO) that includes the RNN-parameterized priors. Why not use reparameterization for Gamma (e.g., implicit reparametrization) or BBVI with control variates?
- Clarify the exact functional forms of p(θ_t|h) and p(h^(n)_t|h^(n+1)), including positivity constraints and parameterization.
- Correct Eq. (6), Eq. (7), and Eq. (8), and reconcile index inconsistencies in Eq. (5).
- Define the ladder architecture precisely and how it alters inference/training.
- Report predictive log-likelihood on held-out counts; add stronger temporal baselines; provide ablations over window size c, number of layers, and regularization.
- Release code or detailed pseudocode and full hyperparameter settings.

Pros
- Ambitious integration of RNN temporal priors with Poisson factorization.
- Exploits conjugacy where possible to keep parts of inference tractable.
- Addresses interpretability via factor/feature scales.

Cons
- Fundamental issues with the variational objective and training methodology.
- Multiple mathematical/notation errors and incomplete derivations.
- Weak/atypical evaluation and baseline setup; limited reproducibility.
- Poor writing and incomplete references.

Recommendation
Reject. The idea is promising, but the paper in its current form has significant methodological and presentation shortcomings. A future version with a principled ELBO-based training, corrected derivations, clearer model specification, and stronger, standardized evaluations could be much more compelling.
