- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): Poisson observations with factors φ, time-varying scores θ, and Gamma-scaled λ (component importance) and ψ (feature sparsity), with deep recurrent Gamma priors for θ.  
- Inference uses two stages: coordinate-ascent updates for Gamma variables via Poisson–Gamma conjugacy, and RNN parameter learning via an ad-hoc “Probability AutoEncoder” L2 matching plus reconstruction loss.  
- Claimed novelty is a deep, windowed recurrent prior/posterior for θ and the Probability AutoEncoder training objective.  
- Coordinate updates for Gamma variables are standard, but the Probability AutoEncoder objective is not a derived ELBO or bound, weakening probabilistic justification.  
- Derivations and notation contain errors and ambiguities (mislabelled hyperparameters, unclear Gamma parameterization, shape/rate confusion).  
- Positivity constraints and parameterization choices for RNN outputs are not described, raising implementation concerns.  
- Model suffers additional non-identifiability from both λ and ψ without discussed constraints or regularization.  
- Deep temporal structure details are insufficient: RNN variant, activations for positivity, window size rationale, α/γ combination, top-layer prior, and runtime complexity are unspecified.  
- Experimental metrics (MSE/PMSE) are suboptimal for sparse counts; predictive log-likelihood/perplexity and uncertainty reporting are missing.  
- Experimental methodology lacks train/validation splits, early stopping, hyperparameter selection, variance/statistical significance, and runtime or sensitivity analyses.  
- Comparisons omit several strong related baselines (recurrent PF, deep gamma belief nets, VRNN/DKS with Poisson), limiting assessment of gains.  
- Writing quality, inconsistent notation, missing definitions, and incomplete references hinder reproducibility and comprehension.  
- The Probability AutoEncoder loss appears novel but ad-hoc and potentially weaker than amortized variational methods.  
- If validated, deep dynamic PFs could be useful, but current lack of principled inference, derivational clarity, and empirical validation undermines significance.  
- Strengths: tackles long-range temporal dependencies in sparse counts, leverages conjugacy for efficient updates, and offers interpretable factor structure with some qualitative insights.  
- Weaknesses: non-ELBO training objective, derivational errors, unaddressed identifiability/positivity, omitted baselines, suboptimal metrics, and poor writing/reproducibility.  
- Key questions for authors include deriving a principled ELBO, ensuring positivity of Gamma parameters from RNNs, justification for both λ and ψ, reporting predictive log-likelihoods and stronger baselines, and ablation/runtime analyses.  
- Pros for acceptance: relevant problem and intuitive idea with some claimed empirical improvements.  
- Cons for acceptance: weak theoretical grounding, technical errors, poor clarity/reproducibility, missing baselines and metrics, and mixed empirical results.  
- Recommendation: Reject (leaning weak reject); encourage resubmission after principled variational objective, cleaner derivations, stronger comparisons, and improved presentation.
