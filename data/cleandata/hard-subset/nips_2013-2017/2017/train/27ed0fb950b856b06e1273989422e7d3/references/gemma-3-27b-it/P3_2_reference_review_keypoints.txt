- Overall: Proposes DDPFA, combining Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs to model dynamic count data; promising results but needs deeper analysis and comparisons.
- Core contribution: Integrates RNNs into a probabilistic PFA framework to capture complex, non-linear temporal dynamics of latent factors.
- Motivation: Addresses limitations of existing Bayesian factor models (PFA, PGDS) in modeling long-range temporal dependencies.
- Novelty: The RNN-within-PFA formulation is a novel and interesting approach to sequential count modeling.
- Methodology: Model is well-formulated with variational inference and sensible extensions (Λ and Ψ) for feature-wise and temporal smoothness.
- Empirical validation: Extensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets show competitive or superior performance to PFA/PGDS and comparable to LSTM on synthetic data.
- Interpretability: Learned latent factors have potential interpretability, as illustrated in the paper's figures.
- Relation to prior work: Places work within deep Bayesian models and cites relevant literature, but could discuss sequential count methods (e.g., HMMs for counts) more.
- Weakness — NN analysis: Lacks detailed justification and ablations for RNN architecture, cell type, depth, and hidden units.
- Weakness — baselines: Comparison to LSTM and other time-series baselines is superficial and lacks thorough hyperparameter tuning.
- Weakness — variational inference: Raises concerns about mean-field VI (pruning, correlation neglect) without exploring alternatives or mitigation strategies.
- Weakness — scalability: Does not discuss computational cost or scalability of variational inference with deep RNNs to large datasets.
- Weakness — loss clarity: Equation 18 and the modified loss to address pruning are confusing and need clearer explanation.
- Originality: Reasonably original integration of deep learning and probabilistic factor modeling for dynamic counts.
- Significance: Potentially impactful across domains, but practical significance is limited by modest gains and lack of deeper analysis.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, sound approach with promising empirical results and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient NN analysis, weak baseline comparisons, VI and scalability concerns, and modest improvements on some datasets.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing highlighted weaknesses.
- Required revisions: Provide detailed RNN analysis and ablations; rigorously tune and compare baselines (including LSTM); discuss alternatives to mean-field VI; include scalability discussion; and clarify the modified loss function.
