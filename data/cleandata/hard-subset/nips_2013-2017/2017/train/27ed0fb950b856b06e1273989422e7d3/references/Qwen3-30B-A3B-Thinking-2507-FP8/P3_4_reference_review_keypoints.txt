- Summary: Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA), adding multi-layer latent structure and RNNs to Poisson factor analysis for sequential count data using variational inference.

- Strength: Addresses a relevant problem with applications in recommendations, text, and time series.

- Strength: Uses a deep multi-layer latent structure to model complex temporal dependencies beyond simple Markov assumptions.

- Strength: Performs comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets (e.g., ICEWS, EBOLA) and compares against sensible baselines.

- Strength: Provides substantial technical detail on the inference algorithm, including mean-field variational inference and the probability autoencoder.

- Weakness: Lacks compelling theoretical justification for why a deep structure is necessary versus simpler models like PGDS with longer lags.

- Weakness: Fails to adequately compare against prior deep Poisson factorization models (RBM/SBN-based), leaving the novelty unclear.

- Weakness: The “deep” aspect appears superficial—only marginal empirical gains and diminishing returns with additional layers; pruning issues noted.

- Weakness: Methodological concerns about combining variational inference with neural networks and an unjustified/ad hoc loss function for Poisson data.

- Weakness: Overstates claims of “excellent results” and interpretability; some tasks (ICEWS) show DDPFA performing worse than baselines like PGDS.

- Weakness: Reproducibility issues due to missing details on network architectures, training procedures, hyperparameters, and unjustified design choices (e.g., window size).

- Critical assessment: Contribution is modest and not well justified; added complexity is not shown to be warranted and novelty over prior deep Poisson work is unclear.

- Recommendation: Reject—insufficient novelty, theoretical justification, and convincing empirical improvement to warrant publication.

- Suggested improvements: Compare thoroughly with existing deep Poisson factorization, provide theoretical motivation for depth, justify architectural choices, and perform more rigorous statistical evaluation.

- Final assessment: Quality moderate (methodology sound but weak theory), Clarity moderate (dense notation), Originality low, Significance low (marginal improvements).
