Summary
The paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Analysis (DDPFA), a hierarchical model for sequential count matrices. Building on Poisson factorization, it augments time dynamics via multiple latent “layers” h(n) with windowed recurrent neural networks (RNNs) that parameterize (implicitly) Gamma conditional distributions for lower layers. A feature-wise sparsity term ψv and component importance λk are introduced. Inference is two-stage: (i) mean-field variational updates for Gamma/Possion-conjugate parts using auxiliary latent counts xvtk; (ii) learning RNN parameters via a “Probability AutoEncoder” loss that matches expectations of prior/posterior conditionals (using L2 between expectations), rather than optimizing an ELBO. Experiments on synthetic data and five real datasets compare with PGDS, PFA, and LSTM, claiming better reconstruction MSE and one-step PMSE, with qualitative analysis on ICEWS.

Relation to prior work
The paper sits between dynamic Poisson factorization (PGDS; Poisson-Gamma state evolution), deep Poisson factorization (e.g., DEFs, deep PFA), and deep sequential latent variable models (VRNN, SRNN, Deep Markov Models) with Poisson/count likelihoods. However, the paper does not adequately position itself against recent amortized-inference deep sequence models, nor dynamic topic models, variational RNNs, or deep Markov models (Krishnan et al., 2017), nor recurrent Poisson factorization for recommendations. The “ladder” discussion references hierarchical VAEs but lacks precise connections.

Strengths
- Tackles a relevant problem: long-range temporal dependence in high-dimensional sparse count time series.
- Attempts to combine conjugate PFA structure with deep temporal latent hierarchies.
- Introduces ψv and λk to control feature sparsity/component importance, potentially useful in practice.
- Empirical scope spans several datasets; qualitative factor interpretations are discussed.

Weaknesses (technical correctness and clarity)
Major concerns:
1) Objective/Inference: The “Probability AutoEncoder” (Eqs. 15–18) is not a principled ELBO or variational bound; the use of L2 between expectations of distributions as a training loss is ad hoc, lacks justification, and offers no guarantee of optimizing any log-evidence. There is no derivation linking this loss to the stated probabilistic model or to a valid lower bound. This undermines claims of “variational inference.”
2) Generative model specification: The deep hierarchical temporal prior is vague. Are conditionals factorized over k? What are the exact forms of p(h(n) t | h(n+1) t−c:t) and p(θt | h(0) t−c:t)? How are positivity and identifiability ensured? The top layer prior h(N) t ∼ Gamma(·) “with h(N) t−c−1:t−1 as prior information” is underspecified.
3) Mean-field updates: Several equations contain errors/ambiguities:
- Eq. 7: the multinomial parameter shows θtφvλφv instead of θtk φvk λk ψv; normalization not clearly written.
- Eqs. 8–14: repeated symbols, inconsistent indices, duplicated Φ, and unclear dependence (e.g., βθ appearing as both rate parameter and hyperparameter). The shape parameters αθtk and αh(n)tk produced by RNNs must be positive; this constraint and parameterization (e.g., softplus) are not specified.
- Bernoulli-Poisson link: b = 1(x > 1) should be b = 1(x ≥ 1).
4) Lack of a clear ELBO: The paper first claims mean-field VI (Eq. 6) and then optimizes network parameters with a non-ELBO loss, breaking variational consistency. No gradients of an ELBO, no reparameterization/discrete relaxation, and no justification for mixing coordinate ascent with separate MSE losses.
5) Empirics: Key quantitative results (Tables 1–3, Figures) are not provided in the submission text; there are no numbers, error bars, or statistical tests. MSE/PMSE are suboptimal metrics for counts; predictive log likelihood, Poisson deviance, or held-out perplexity are standard. LSTM baseline likely mismatched (MSE on counts, no count-specific likelihood). No ablations for ψ/λ, window size c, or hierarchy depth, beyond a brief claim and a missing table. No runtime or scalability analysis. No code or reproducibility details.

Additional issues (numerous minor flaws)
- Many typos/formatting errors: inconsistent notation (Φ,Φ; φ vs φv; shp vs shpv), grammar, missing equation references, ambiguous subscripts/superscripts.
- Figures and tables are referenced but not included; a NeurIPS submission must be self-contained.
- References are incomplete and in several cases incorrect or missing venues/years; significant related work is not cited (DMM, VRNN/SRNN, dynamic topic models).
- The text claims conjugacy “given implicit priors,” but once priors are neural nets, conjugacy only holds conditional on fixed network outputs; this subtlety should be made explicit.
- The “ladder” explanation is informal and does not clarify the actual architecture used.

Quality
Due to the unclear and likely incorrect inference objective, unspecified model details, and missing empirical results, the work is not technically solid as presented.

Clarity
The paper is difficult to follow, with substantial notation errors, missing definitions, and English/formatting issues. It is not currently reproducible.

Originality
The high-level idea (deep temporal hierarchy for Poisson factorization) is interesting, but overlaps with established deep sequential latent variable models with Poisson emissions. The paper does not adequately differentiate from or compare to that literature.

Significance
Potentially useful if made sound and reproducible, but current presentation and methodology fall short. The experimental section does not convincingly establish state-of-the-art performance.

Questions for authors
- Provide a precise ELBO for the full model and show how network parameters are learned by maximizing it (or provide a principled alternative with guarantees).
- Specify all conditional distributions in closed form, including factorization over k and positivity constraints.
- Detail RNN architectures (LSTM/GRU/vanilla, hidden sizes), activation functions, parameterizations ensuring positivity of Gamma parameters.
- Report predictive log-likelihoods or Poisson deviance with uncertainty, and include strong baselines (DMM/VRNN/SRNN, recurrent Poisson factorization).
- Provide ablations for ψv, λk, depth N, window c; runtime and memory.

Recommendation
Reject. The paper has an interesting goal, but the inference is not principled, the model is insufficiently specified, and the empirical evaluation is incomplete and not compelling. Addressing the objective, clarity, and experimental rigor would be necessary for reconsideration.
