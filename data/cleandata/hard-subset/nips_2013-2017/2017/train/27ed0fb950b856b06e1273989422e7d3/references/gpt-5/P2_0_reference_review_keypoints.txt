- Proposal: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA), combining Poisson factorization with a deep recurrent prior over time for sequential count data.  
- Generative/inference summary: Models counts via Poisson factorization with RNN-parameterized Gamma priors over time-varying factor scores; inference uses auxiliary-count mean-field VI and a “Probability AutoEncoder” objective to learn RNNs.  

Technical soundness issues:
- Generative model and inference are not clearly or rigorously specified; key equations imply implicit Gamma conditionals without a coherent joint model.  
- The “Probability AutoEncoder” objective is ad hoc, replacing a probabilistic objective with squared-error expectation matching and lacks theoretical justification as an ELBO.  
- Conditional conjugacy claims only hold if priors are fixed; using network-produced prior parameters breaks standard derivations and the provided updates are inconsistent.  
- Notational and conditioning inconsistencies (e.g., unusual posterior dependencies) undermine the correctness of derivations.  
- Evaluation metrics (MSE/PMSE) are inappropriate for counts; predictive log-likelihood or held-out Poisson deviance would be more suitable.  

Experimental concerns:
- Key experimental details (tables/figures, confidence intervals, data splits, prediction protocols) are missing or insufficiently described.  
- Baselines appear poorly tuned or unfairly compared; some relevant dynamic count models are omitted.  
- Reproducibility is weak: no code, incomplete optimizer/architecture/hyperparameter details, and ambiguous modeling choices.  

Clarity problems:
- Manuscript is difficult to follow due to grammatical errors, ambiguous notation, typos, and inconsistent references.  
- Roles of generative vs. inference networks and the deep temporal hierarchy are not precisely described, impeding reproducibility.  

Originality critique:
- Using RNNs for Gamma priors in dynamic PF is a natural, incremental extension of existing work rather than a major novelty.  
- The Probability AutoEncoder resembles expectation-matching heuristics without novel theoretical grounding.  

Significance assessment:
- The idea could be useful if rigorously formulated, but current modeling and evaluation issues prevent assessing impact or adoption.  

Pros:
- Addresses an important problem: high-dimensional, sparse, dynamic count data.  
- Attempts to combine interpretable Poisson factorization with deep recurrent structure for long-term dependencies.  
- Evaluates on multiple real datasets and discusses factor interpretability.  

Cons:
- Ambiguous/partially incorrect derivations and unclear probabilistic foundation.  
- Ad hoc training objective lacking guarantees.  
- Poor clarity, notation errors, and missing reproducibility details.  
- Suboptimal evaluation metrics and incomplete baseline comparisons.  
- Incomplete related-work coverage and limited novelty.  

Questions for authors:
- What is the exact full joint p(X, Θ, Φ, Λ, Ψ, H) and which networks are generative vs. inference?  
- Can you provide an ELBO or principled justification for the squared-error objective, and why not use standard amortized VI?  
- How are positivity constraints enforced for network outputs and how are shapes vs. rates parameterized?  
- What is the precise prediction protocol, and can you report predictive log-likelihood with tuned baselines?  
- Can you include ablations (no λ/ψ, shallow vs. deep, ladder vs. no ladder), more modern baselines, and sensitivity analyses?  

Typos / clarifications:
- Eq.7 likely has λ φv typo and should be λ ψv; several posterior expressions mix indices/constants and need correction.  
- References are incomplete and inconsistent; citations in text may be missing from the bibliography.  

Overall recommendation:
- Reject: the paper lacks a rigorous probabilistic formulation, uses an ad hoc training objective, and provides insufficient empirical evidence; substantial revision is needed.
