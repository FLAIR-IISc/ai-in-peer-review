- Proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that combines Poisson factorization with deep hierarchical RNNs to model temporal count data.
- Uses a ladder-like deep architecture and two-stage variational inference (coordinate ascent for latents, then neural network training) to capture complex temporal dependencies.
- Evaluated on four synthetic and five real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, International Disaster, Annual Sheep Population) against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA baselines using MSE/PMSE.

- Strength (Quality): Technically sound model and inference with comprehensive experimental design and appropriate baselines/metrics.
- Strength (Clarity): Well-organized paper, consistent notation, and illustrative figures (e.g., factor activation patterns) aiding reproducibility.
- Strength (Originality): Meaningful extension of Poisson factorization to deep temporal models with a ladder architecture addressing latent “pruning.”
- Strength (Significance): Addresses practical sequential count-data problems and shows improved long-range dependency modeling in some datasets.

- Weakness (Quality): Lacks rigorous theoretical justification for why deeper architecture improves performance; ablation shows diminishing returns.
- Weakness (Clarity): Inference section is dense and the connection between the ladder architecture and standard VAEs is not clearly explained.
- Weakness (Originality): Insufficient differentiation from related deep probabilistic models (Deep Exponential Families, Deep Poisson Factor Analysis).
- Weakness (Significance): Performance gains are often marginal and computational cost/scalability are not discussed.

- For acceptance: Addresses an important problem, shows consistent empirical improvements, uses comprehensive evaluations, and proposes a thoughtful ladder architecture.
- Against acceptance: Insufficient theoretical grounding, marginal empirical gains, unclear novelty relative to prior deep probabilistic work, and ablation suggests depth may be unnecessary.

- Recommendation: Weak accept — promising but not strong; needs stronger theory, clearer differentiation, computational cost analysis, and better explanation of ladder-VAE relationship.
- Suggested improvements: provide rigorous theoretical justification for depth, deeper comparisons with related models, discuss computational costs/scalability, and clarify link between ladder architecture and standard VAEs.
