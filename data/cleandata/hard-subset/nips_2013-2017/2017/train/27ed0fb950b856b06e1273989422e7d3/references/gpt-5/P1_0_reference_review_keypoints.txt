Proposes DDPFA: Poisson factorization combined with a deep, temporally structured hierarchy of latent Gamma variables parameterized by RNNs for sequential count data.

Observation model: x_vt ~ Poisson(sum_k θ_tk φ_vk λ_k ψ_v) with N-layer latent h^(n)_t (K-dim) using windowed context c and forward/backward RNNs.

Inference alternates mean-field Poisson–Gamma updates with auxiliary allocations x_vtk and RNN training via a “probability autoencoder” squared-error objective instead of an ELBO.

Empirical results: DDPFA generally outperforms PGDS, LSTM, and static PFA on several datasets, but PGDS is competitive on ICEWS prediction.

Strength: addresses high-dimensional sparse sequential counts, combining interpretability and conjugacy of Poisson factorization with flexible RNN temporal dynamics.

Strength: qualitative ICEWS factors are interpretable; paper explores practical issues (pruning, ladder connections).

Main weakness: training neural networks that define implicit priors/posteriors is not grounded in a principled variational objective—squared-error losses lack probabilistic semantics and ELBO guarantees.

Inference inconsistency: mean-field conjugate updates assume conjugacy but network outputs break conjugacy; gradients w.r.t. network weights are not derived under a joint ELBO and Stage 1/Stage 2 consistency is unexplained.

Identifiability: multiplicative confounding among φ, ψ, λ, and θ causes scale indeterminacy; no normalization/rescaling or identifiability strategy provided.

Bernoulli–Poisson link section contains errors (indicator and degenerate-zero handling) and appears unused in experiments.

Experimental issues: evaluation uses only MSE/PMSE (nonstandard), lacks heldout predictive log-likelihood/perplexity, unclear PMSE definition and train/test splits.

Baseline concerns: LSTM and other baselines likely under-tuned; architecture and regularization choices are under-specified.

No uncertainty quantification or significance testing; results lack variance across runs.

Scalability: auxiliary x_vtk has O(V*T*K) complexity; computational/memory costs and sparsity/optimization tricks are not analyzed.

Clarity problems: typos, notation inconsistencies, missing architecture/training details (RNN cell type, activations for positive Gamma params), and vague “probability autoencoder” definition.

Related work: incomplete discussion (missing Deep Markov Models, dynamic PF variants, some recurrent count models) and incomplete citation metadata.

Originality limited: combination of Poisson factorization and RNN temporal modeling resembles prior PGDS, recurrent PF, and deep count models; novelty hinges on ad-hoc autoencoder training.

Significance limited: empirical gains are modest and task-dependent; lack of likelihood-based evaluation limits comparability and impact.

Key questions for authors include: provide a proper ELBO or justify squared-error objective; how Gamma params are parameterized/kept positive; exact PMSE/split definitions; sensitivity to K/N/c; identifiability handling; and computational costs.

Suggested fixes: derive a principled ELBO and use implicit reparameterization or score-function estimators (or augmentation); evaluate predictive log-likelihoods and topic coherence; add ablations, hyperparameter sweeps, stronger baselines, and release code.

Pros for acceptance: important problem, interpretable count modeling with temporal structure, and promising hybrid approach.

Cons for acceptance: unsound training objective, clarity/reproducibility issues, limited/nonstandard evaluation, and only incremental novelty.

Recommendation: Reject in current form—needs a principled variational derivation, clearer methodology, and stronger experiments to be competitive.
