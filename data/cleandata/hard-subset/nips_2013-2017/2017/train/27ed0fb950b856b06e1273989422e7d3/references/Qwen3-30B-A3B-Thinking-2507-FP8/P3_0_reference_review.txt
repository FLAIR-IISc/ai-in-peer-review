

# Peer Review: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model

## Summary and Context
This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) model that extends Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) with a deep hierarchical structure to capture complex temporal dependencies in sequential count data. The authors build on prior work including Poisson-Gamma Dynamical Systems (PGDS) and Deep Exponential Families, arguing that their deep structure can better model long-term dependencies than previous shallow approaches. The model uses recurrent neural networks to represent implicit distributions between layers, with variational inference for parameter estimation.

## Strengths

1. **Problem Relevance**: The paper addresses an important problem in analyzing sequential count data, which is common in recommendation systems, text analysis, and network analysis. The authors provide a clear motivation for why existing approaches (like PGDS) may be insufficient for capturing complex temporal patterns.

2. **Experimental Validation**: The authors conduct thorough experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, EBOLA, etc.), demonstrating consistent improvements over baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA) in most cases. The results on synthetic datasets with known temporal patterns (SDS1-4) provide convincing evidence of the model's ability to capture complex temporal dependencies.

3. **Model Integration**: The integration of deep structures with Poisson factorization is well-motivated and builds logically on previous work in deep Bayesian models (e.g., Deep Exponential Families, Deep Poisson Factor Analysis).

## Weaknesses and Concerns

1. **Lack of Theoretical Justification**: The paper claims that the deep structure "captures long-time dependence" but provides no theoretical analysis to support this claim. Why does a deep hierarchical structure specifically improve modeling of temporal dependencies compared to other approaches? The paper doesn't establish a clear connection between the model's architecture and its ability to capture long-term dependencies.

2. **Superficial Comparison with Related Work**: The paper cites relevant prior work (e.g., [9, 10, 12]) but doesn't clearly differentiate DDPFA from these approaches. For example, Deep Poisson Factor Analysis with RBM/SBN [9, 10] and Deep Poisson Factor Modeling with recurrent structure [12] already incorporate deep structures for Poisson factorization. The paper doesn't explain how DDPFA differs in a meaningful way from these prior deep Poisson models.

3. **Inadequate Analysis of Limitations**: The authors identify "pruning" and "uselessness of adding network layers" as limitations (Section 5) but don't provide sufficient evidence or analysis. The experimental results in Table 3 show minimal improvements with additional layers, yet they don't explore why this occurs or how it might be addressed. This is a significant oversight for a method claiming to be "deep."

4. **Inference Algorithm Details**: The variational inference procedure is described but lacks critical details. How do the neural networks in the probability autoencoder differ from standard variational autoencoders? What specific architectures (number of hidden units, activation functions) are used? The paper mentions "ladder-like architecture" but doesn't explain how it differs from standard hierarchical variational models.

5. **Questionable Baseline Comparison**: The comparison with LSTM is problematic. LSTM is a general-purpose sequence model, while DDPFA is specifically designed for count data with Poisson assumptions. A more appropriate comparison would be with specialized models for count data, such as Recurrent Poisson Factorization [12] or Dynamic Poisson-Gamma Systems [2].

## Specific Recommendations

1. **Clarify the Novel Contribution**: The authors should explicitly state how DDPFA differs from existing deep Poisson factorization approaches (e.g., [9, 10, 12]) and why their specific architecture provides advantages.

2. **Add Theoretical Analysis**: Provide a theoretical argument for why deep hierarchical structure is beneficial for modeling temporal dependencies in count data, possibly through analysis of the model's capacity to capture long-range dependencies.

3. **Improve Baseline Comparison**: Replace LSTM with more appropriate baselines for count data, such as Recurrent Poisson Factorization [12] or Dynamic Poisson-Gamma Systems [2].

4. **Expand Limitation Analysis**: Provide deeper analysis of the "pruning" phenomenon and why adding layers doesn't improve performance. This could include visualizations of the latent space or analysis of the neural network weights.

5. **Include More Implementation Details**: Provide specifics about the neural network architectures (number of hidden units, activation functions) used in the probability autoencoder.

## Overall Assessment

The paper presents a well-motivated extension to Poisson factorization for sequential count data with a deep structure. While the experimental results are promising, the paper lacks sufficient theoretical grounding and fails to clearly articulate its novelty compared to existing deep Poisson factorization approaches. The identification of limitations is valuable but not sufficiently supported by the analysis.

**Recommendation**: Weak Accept (with major revisions). The core idea has merit, but the paper requires significant revision to clarify its contributions, provide stronger theoretical justification, and improve the analysis of limitations. The authors should address the concerns outlined above before the paper can be considered for publication at NIPS.

**Key Arguments for Acceptance**:
- Addressed an important problem with sequential count data
- Demonstrated consistent improvements over baselines on multiple datasets
- Well-structured and technically sound model

**Key Arguments for Rejection**:
- Lacks clear differentiation from existing deep Poisson factorization work
- Insufficient theoretical justification for the deep structure
- Inadequate analysis of limitations and their causes
- Superficial comparison with relevant baselines

The paper has potential but needs significant refinement to demonstrate its novelty and theoretical contributions. With the suggested revisions, it could be a strong contribution to the field.
