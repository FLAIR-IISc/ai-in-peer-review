

# Peer Review: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model

## Summary

This paper introduces the Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA) model for analyzing sequentially observed count data. The authors build upon Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) and Poisson-Gamma Dynamical Systems (PGDS) by incorporating deep recurrent neural networks to model complex temporal dependencies in count data. The model represents time-series count data using a hierarchical structure where latent variables at each layer depend on previous layers through RNNs, allowing the model to capture both short-term and long-term temporal patterns. The authors propose a variational inference approach to learn the model parameters and demonstrate superior performance over baselines (PGDS, LSTM, and standard PFA) on both synthetic and real-world datasets including ICEWS, NIPS corpus, and Ebola outbreak data.

The paper makes a reasonable contribution to the growing literature on Bayesian time-series models and deep probabilistic models, building on prior work such as Deep Exponential Families [8] and Deep Poisson Factor Analysis [9, 10]. However, the paper's "deep" aspect is limited to just two layers, which may not fully justify the "deep" designation.

## Quality

The paper presents a technically sound model with clear mathematical formulations. The variational inference approach is well-motivated and correctly applied to the model structure. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, with four synthetic datasets and five real-world datasets, comparing against three relevant baselines. The results consistently show DDPFA outperforming other methods in most cases, particularly in capturing long-term temporal dependencies.

However, the paper lacks sufficient theoretical justification for why the deep structure improves performance. The authors mention that the deep structure helps capture long-time dependence, but don't provide formal analysis of the model's capacity to learn such dependencies. The mean-field variational approximation may be too strong an assumption for this complex model, potentially limiting the quality of inference. The authors acknowledge this limitation in Section 5 (the "pruning" problem), but don't provide a thorough analysis of how this affects performance.

## Clarity

The paper is generally well-written and well-organized, following a standard structure for machine learning papers. The mathematical formulations are clear, and the model is well-explained with appropriate equations. The experimental setup is adequately described, though some implementation details (e.g., specific RNN architectures, hyperparameter tuning procedures) could be more detailed to improve reproducibility.

The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of how the deep structure specifically helps with long-term dependencies compared to simpler approaches. The connection between the model's architecture and its ability to capture complex temporal patterns isn't sufficiently elaborated.

## Originality

The paper combines deep learning with Poisson factorization for time-series count data, which is a reasonable extension of existing work. The use of deep recurrent structures to model temporal dependencies in Poisson factor analysis represents a novel approach compared to previous models that rely on simpler Markov assumptions (like PGDS).

However, the novelty is somewhat limited, as the paper doesn't compare with more recent deep probabilistic models that might address similar problems, such as Deep Markov Models [25] or Deep Factor Analysis [26]. The paper also doesn't clearly distinguish itself from other deep Poisson factorization approaches mentioned in the references.

## Significance

The problem of modeling sequential count data is important in many applications (recommendation systems, text analysis, epidemiology), and the paper addresses a meaningful limitation in existing models (inability to capture complex long-term temporal dependencies). The experimental results demonstrate that DDPFA can capture more nuanced temporal patterns than existing methods, particularly in cases where multiple factors co-occur over time (as shown in Figure 2).

However, the performance gains over baselines are not consistently dramatic across all datasets. For instance, DDPFA's performance on the ICEWS prediction task is not as strong as PGDS, which suggests the model may not universally outperform simpler approaches. The paper could strengthen its significance claim by providing more detailed analysis of when and why the deep structure provides advantages.

## Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across diverse datasets
- Clear motivation for addressing limitations of existing models
- Good comparison with relevant baselines (PGDS, LSTM, PFA)
- Acknowledgement of limitations and potential problems (pruning issue)
- Practical demonstration of model interpretability (Figure 2)

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited theoretical justification for the deep structure
- "Deep" model uses only 2 layers, which is not very deep
- Insufficient comparison with more recent deep probabilistic models
- Mean-field variational approximation may be too strong
- Limited discussion of when the model might not work well

## Recommendation

I recommend **weak acceptance** for this paper. The paper presents a solid contribution to the field of time-series count data modeling with a clear experimental validation. The model is well-motivated and demonstrates improved performance over baselines in most cases. However, the novelty is somewhat limited by the shallow depth of the model and the lack of comparison with more recent deep probabilistic approaches. The paper would benefit from stronger theoretical justification, more detailed experimental comparisons, and a clearer explanation of the advantages of the deep structure.

I suggest the authors strengthen the paper by:
1. Adding a more detailed theoretical analysis of why the deep structure helps
2. Including a comparison with more recent deep probabilistic models
3. Providing more detail about the RNN architecture and training procedures
4. Exploring the relationship between model depth and performance more thoroughly

The paper is of good quality and makes a reasonable contribution to the field, though it doesn't represent a major advance over existing work. It would be a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings.
