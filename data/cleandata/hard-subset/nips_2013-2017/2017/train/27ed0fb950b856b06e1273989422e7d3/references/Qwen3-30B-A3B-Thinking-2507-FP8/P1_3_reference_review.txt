

# Peer Review: Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model

## Summary of Main Ideas

This paper introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factor Analysis (DDPFA), a novel model for analyzing sequentially observed count data. The authors build upon Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) and Poisson-Gamma Dynamical Systems (PGDS) by incorporating deep neural network structures to capture complex temporal dependencies. The key innovation is a deep hierarchical structure where latent variables are connected through recurrent neural networks across multiple layers, allowing the model to capture both short-term dependencies (via the bottom layer) and long-term temporal patterns (via higher layers).

The paper relates its work to several previous contributions: PFA for count data modeling, PGDS for dynamic Poisson factorization with Gamma transitions, and various deep Bayesian models like Deep Exponential Families and Deep Gaussian Processes. The authors position their work as addressing limitations in previous models, particularly their inability to capture long-time dependence due to shallow temporal structures.

## Strengths

1. **Problem Relevance**: The paper addresses an important problem in analyzing sequential count data, with applications in recommendation systems, text analysis, and network analysis. The problem is well-motivated and clearly articulated.

2. **Model Innovation**: The use of a deep hierarchical structure with recurrent neural networks to capture temporal dependencies represents a meaningful extension beyond previous work. The layered approach to modeling time dependence is conceptually sound.

3. **Experimental Validation**: The authors provide thorough empirical validation on both synthetic and real-world datasets (ICEWS, NIPS corpus, EBOLA, International Disaster, and Annual Sheep Population). The results consistently show DDPFA outperforming PGDS, LSTM, and PFA on most metrics, particularly in capturing long-term patterns.

4. **Interpretability**: The model demonstrates good interpretability, as shown in Figure 2 and 3, where meaningful temporal patterns (e.g., Israel-Palestinian conflict, six-party talks in ICEWS) are captured by the latent factors.

5. **Technical Soundness**: The model uses standard Bayesian techniques (Gamma-Poisson conjugacy) and variational inference in a well-justified manner. The inference algorithm is clearly described and logically structured.

## Weaknesses

1. **Theoretical Justification**: The paper lacks a strong theoretical argument for why the deep structure is necessary. The claim that "deep structure is included to capture long-time dependence" is not sufficiently supported with theoretical analysis or comparison to simpler alternatives.

2. **Comparison to Related Work**: The relationship to deep Bayesian models (e.g., Deep Exponential Families) is not clearly articulated. The paper mentions "Deep Poisson Factor Analysis based on RBM or SBN" but doesn't explain how DDPFA differs or improves upon these approaches.

3. **Pruning Issue**: The paper acknowledges a "pruning" problem where some latent dimensions contain only noise (Section 5), but doesn't adequately address how this affects model performance or how it might be mitigated. This is a significant concern for model reliability.

4. **Limited Experimental Scope**: While the 5 real-world datasets are diverse, they are relatively few for a model making broad claims about performance. The paper doesn't provide statistical significance tests for the reported improvements.

5. **Reproducibility Concerns**: The paper lacks sufficient detail about neural network architectures, hyperparameters beyond what's listed in Table 2, and training procedures for the neural networks. This makes replication difficult.

## Evaluation by Criteria

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound but lacks depth in theoretical analysis. The experimental results are well-conducted but could be strengthened with statistical significance testing and more thorough ablation studies. The authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., the "pruning" issue), which demonstrates honesty.

**Clarity**: The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The model description is logically structured, though some technical details in the inference section could benefit from more explanation. The figures (not shown here but referenced) appear to be helpful for understanding the model.

**Originality**: The integration of deep neural networks with Poisson factorization for temporal dependence is novel, but the specific approach doesn't represent a dramatic departure from existing deep Bayesian models. The paper could better articulate the novelty relative to recent deep learning approaches for count data.

**Significance**: The model addresses an important problem with potential applications across multiple domains. The results demonstrate clear improvements over baselines on several datasets, suggesting practical utility. However, the significance is somewhat limited by the small number of datasets tested and the lack of a compelling argument for why deep structure is essential rather than merely beneficial.

## Recommendation

I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. The paper presents a well-motivated, technically sound model that makes a meaningful contribution to the field of sequential count data analysis. The experimental results are promising and demonstrate clear advantages over existing approaches.

However, the paper requires revisions to strengthen its theoretical justification, better articulate the novelty relative to prior work, and address the "pruning" issue more thoroughly. The authors should provide more detailed experimental results (including statistical significance) and improve the reproducibility of their approach.

The paper would benefit from:
1. A more precise theoretical argument for the deep structure
2. A clearer comparison to relevant deep learning approaches for count data
3. More detailed discussion of the "pruning" problem and potential solutions
4. Additional statistical validation of the results
5. More complete implementation details for reproducibility

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the analysis of sequential count data, and with the suggested revisions, would make an excellent contribution to NIPS.
