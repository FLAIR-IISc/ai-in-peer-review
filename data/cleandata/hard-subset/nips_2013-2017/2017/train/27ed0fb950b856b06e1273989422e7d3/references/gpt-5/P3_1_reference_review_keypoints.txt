- Paper proposes Deep Dynamic Poisson Factor Analysis (DDPFA): deep recurrent Gamma–Poisson model for sequential count data with RNN-parameterized “implicit” Gamma conditionals and alternating VI + “Probability AutoEncoder” training.
- Sits at intersection of Poisson factorization, dynamic count models, deep latent variable models, and RNN temporal models, but not clearly positioned vs DTM, neural autoregressive topic models, or amortized VI for Gamma/Poisson.
- Strength: ambitious attempt to capture short- and long-range temporal dependencies via a deep hierarchical recurrent structure.
- Strength: retains conjugacy for many components, enabling relatively simple mean-field updates for φ, λ, ψ, and θ given neural parameters.
- Strength: reports improved MSE/PMSE on several datasets with some qualitative interpretability examples (e.g., ICEWS factors).
- Major weakness: training objective for RNN parameters is ad hoc — the “Probability AutoEncoder” squared-error loss is not a valid ELBO or likelihood surrogate and lacks guarantees about marginal likelihood improvement.
- Major weakness: inference is a two-stage, partially decoupled procedure with no coherent global objective, leaving unclear what posterior is being approximated or whether network parameters match the generative model.
- Technical/notation problems: many derivational errors and ambiguities (errors in Eqs. 7–11, unclear positivity constraints for Gamma params, typos), and Λ/Ψ introduce unjustified scale non-identifiability.
- Missing computational analysis: latent count allocations xvtk scale as O(VTK) and potential scalability / sparse augmentation tricks are not discussed.
- Evaluation issues: uses MSE/PMSE instead of held-out predictive log-likelihood or perplexity, lacks uncertainty-aware metrics, unclear hyperparameter tuning and fairness across baselines, and no runtime comparisons.
- Clarity problems: paper is hard to follow due to typos, inconsistent notation, incomplete formalization of the “Probability AutoEncoder,” and incomplete/incorrect references.
- Originality concern: idea of RNN-parameterized deep Gamma–Poisson conditionals is interesting but related prior work (DEFs with temporal structure, Gamma Belief Nets, Recurrent PFA) reduces novelty; main novelty (training scheme) is not theoretically grounded.
- Significance concern: problem is important, but unclear objective, limited evaluation, and weak baselines make it hard to claim state-of-the-art impact.
- Key suggestions: derive a principled ELBO with amortized variational inference or reparameterized gradients for Gamma; consider recognition networks q(h,θ|x) and optimize a true ELBO.
- Key suggestions: justify and address identifiability of Λ and Ψ (hierarchical sparsity, normalization), and include ablations (depth, window, ladder vs non-ladder, Λ/Ψ removal).
- Key suggestions: use held-out predictive log-likelihood/perplexity, compare to stronger baselines (DTM, neural autoregressive topic models, deep PF with recurrence), and report runtimes.
- Key suggestions: clarify positivity enforcement for Gamma params, specify RNN architectures/activations/optimizers, and explain how bidirectional conditioning affects prediction (ensure future info not used at test time).
- Key suggestions: fix notation and derivation errors, complete references, and release code for reproducibility.
- Pros for acceptance: tackles a relevant problem, interesting model blend of hierarchical gamma-Poisson and RNNs, some empirical gains and qualitative analyses.
- Cons for acceptance: ad hoc training objective, derivational/notation errors, limited evaluation and baselines, lack of likelihood-based assessment and runtime analysis, and clarity/referencing issues.
- Overall recommendation: lean toward rejection in current form due to lack of coherent probabilistic training objective and insufficiently rigorous evaluation, though the direction could become solid with a principled ELBO, stronger baselines, and clearer exposition.
