

# Review of "Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model"

## Summary
This paper proposes a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) that extends Poisson factor analysis to capture complex temporal dependencies in sequential count data. The authors claim to incorporate deep learning structures to model long-time dependence, using a hierarchical structure with recurrent neural networks for inference. They compare their model against PGDS, LSTM, and PFA on synthetic and real-world datasets.

## Major Technical and Presentation Issues

The paper suffers from numerous technical inconsistencies and formatting errors that undermine its credibility and make it difficult to assess the proposed method.

1. **Severe Notation Inconsistency**: The paper uses inconsistent notation throughout (e.g., h(n)t vs. h^{(n)}_t, θtk vs. θ_{tk}, θshptk vs. θ_{shp}^{tk}). This makes the mathematical formulation nearly impossible to follow. For example, in Equation (11), the notation "Ψ(θshptk )− logθ rte tk" is completely non-standard and confusing.

2. **Critical Mathematical Errors**: The paper states "k ∈ {0, . . . ,K}" in Equation (2), but k=0 would be an empty sum, making this notation incorrect. The paper also claims Λ and Ψ can be absorbed into Θ (Section 2), yet later claims they're included in the model for "sparsity extraction" (Section 2), creating a logical contradiction.

3. **Inconsistent Dataset Descriptions**: The ICEWS dataset description states "T = 365, V = 6197, and 475646 events for the matrix" but 365×6197 = 2,262,905, which is inconsistent with the reported 475,646 events. This suggests either a fundamental error in data processing or poor description.

4. **Lack of Experimental Detail**: The paper claims "DDPFA has the best performance" but doesn't provide actual numerical results in the paper (Table 1 and 2 are referenced but not included). The statement "it is obviously that DDPFA has the best performance" is unacceptable for a scientific paper.

5. **Inconsistent Terminology**: The paper refers to "Poisson-Gamma Dynamical System (PGDS)" in Section 1 but later calls it "dynamic Poisson-Gamma system" (Section 4.2), creating confusion about the model name.

6. **Formatting Errors**: The title incorrectly references "st Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS 2017)" rather than "The 31st Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS 2017)".

7. **Incomplete References**: Many references have missing publication details (e.g., "Nonparametric Bayesian Factor Analysis for Dynamic Count Matrices, A. Ayan, J. Ghosh, M. Zhou, , , ").

## Minor but Significant Issues

1. **Unclear Model Justification**: The paper claims the deep structure captures "long-time dependence" but provides no theoretical justification for why a deep architecture is better than existing methods like LSTM for this specific task.

2. **Inconsistent Experimental Setup**: The paper states "K = 3 for ID and ASP datasets, while set K = 10 for the others" but doesn't explain why these different values were chosen.

3. **Overstated Claims**: The paper claims "excellent results are obtained" without providing sufficient quantitative evidence to support this claim.

4. **Confusing Inference Description**: The description of the inference algorithm is unclear, particularly regarding how the "probability autoencoder" is trained and why it's necessary.

## Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths**: 
- The problem of modeling sequential count data is relevant.
- The idea of combining Poisson factor analysis with deep learning is potentially interesting.

**Weaknesses**:
- The paper is riddled with technical errors and inconsistent notation.
- Experimental results are not properly presented.
- The paper fails to clearly distinguish its contribution from prior work (e.g., Deep Poisson Factor Analysis [9, 10]).
- The theoretical justification for the proposed architecture is weak.

## Overall Assessment

While the problem addressed is relevant to NIPS, the paper's technical flaws and poor presentation make it impossible to evaluate the scientific contribution. The numerous mathematical inconsistencies, formatting errors, and lack of detailed experimental results significantly undermine the paper's credibility. The authors have made a serious effort to propose a novel model, but the execution is so poor that it fails to meet NIPS's standards for technical soundness.

The paper does not sufficiently differentiate itself from prior work on Deep Poisson Factor Analysis [9, 10] or other dynamic Poisson models [1, 2]. The claims about the model's performance are unsubstantiated due to the lack of detailed results.

## Recommendation

Reject. The paper contains too many technical errors and presentation issues to be considered for publication at NIPS. The authors should substantially revise the paper, correcting all mathematical inconsistencies, providing clear experimental results, and properly justifying their model's novelty before resubmission.
