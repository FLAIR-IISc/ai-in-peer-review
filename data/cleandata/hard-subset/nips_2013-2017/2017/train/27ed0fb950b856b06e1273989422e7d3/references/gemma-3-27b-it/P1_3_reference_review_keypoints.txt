- Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization (DDPFA): combines Poisson Factor Analysis with RNNs and a deep structure to model sequential count data via variational inference.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real datasets (ICEWS, NIPS, Ebola, International Disaster, Annual Sheep Population) and compared to PGDS, LSTM, and PFA.  
- Properly positions work within dynamic count factorization and deep Bayesian models, noting related methods like PGDS, Deep Gaussian Processes, and temporal Dirichlet processes.  
- Novelty: first to integrate PFA with deep RNNs to capture short- and long-term temporal dependencies in count data.  
- The model has a solid probabilistic foundation with clear variational inference derivations and added feature/temporal smoothness via Ψ and Λ.  
- Empirical validation is comprehensive, comparing to relevant baselines across multiple datasets.  
- Visualizations provide interpretability insights into learned factors and temporal dynamics.  
- Paper is generally well written and organized.  
- Limitation: mean-field variational inference may cause "pruning" and inactive latents; alternative inference like MCMC might help.  
- Lacks detailed hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity analysis; choices are not well justified.  
- Limited ablation study—insufficient analysis of contributions from RNN depth and Ψ/Λ (e.g., benefit of a second RNN layer).  
- DDPFA underperforms PGDS on ICEWS; the cause is not sufficiently investigated.  
- Scalability concerns: training deep models with variational inference may be costly for very large datasets and is not discussed.  
- Metric inconsistency: reporting both MSE and PMSE is confusing; a single standardized metric would improve clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound model with strong empirical results that advance sequential count analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on MF-VI, insufficient hyperparameter/ablation analysis, and questionable generalization on ICEWS.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution deserving acceptance but needing minor revisions to address empirical and inference concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions and address hyperparameter discussion, a detailed ablation study, ICEWS investigation, scalability notes, and metric standardization.
