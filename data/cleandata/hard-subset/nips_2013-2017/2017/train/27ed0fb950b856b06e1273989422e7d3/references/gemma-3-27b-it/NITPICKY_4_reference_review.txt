## Review of Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model

This paper presents a Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization Model (DDPFA) designed to capture both short- and long-term dependencies in sequentially observed count data. The model extends Poisson Factor Analysis (PFA) by incorporating recurrent neural networks to model temporal dynamics at multiple layers, aiming to improve upon the limitations of previous approaches like PGDS and temporal Dirichlet processes. Variational inference is employed for parameter estimation.

While the motivation for addressing temporal dependencies in count data is sound, and the idea of utilizing deep learning to capture complex relationships is appealing, this paper suffers from a multitude of minor, yet collectively significant, flaws that detract from its overall quality. The writing is often imprecise, the methodological details are sometimes unclear or incomplete, and the experimental validation, while extensive, lacks sufficient depth in analysis.

**Strengths:**

*   **Motivation:** The problem of modeling dynamic count data with complex dependencies is relevant and important.
*   **Model Idea:** The core idea of combining PFA with RNNs to capture both short- and long-term dependencies is a reasonable approach.
*   **Experimental Setup:** The authors evaluate their model on a diverse set of datasets, including both synthetic and real-world data.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity and Precision:** The writing is frequently ambiguous. For example, the connection between equation (3) and (4) is not fully explained. What precisely is 'c' representing? Is it a fixed window size used in all layers? The explanation is cursory. Similarly, the description of the “ladder-like architecture” introduced to address “pruning” is presented too late in the paper (end of section 4) and without sufficient detail to understand its implementation.
*   **Methodological Details:** Several aspects of the methodology are vaguely defined. The choice of activation functions within the RNNs is not mentioned, which is crucial for performance. The specific variational inference updates are described at a high level; more detail would be beneficial for reproducibility. The Gamma distributions used for priors—the choice of α and β values—are arbitrarily set and not justified.
*   **Lack of Theoretical Justification:** The paper lacks a rigorous theoretical analysis of the model. A discussion of the identifiability of the model, or the potential for overfitting due to the deep structure, is missing. The authors claim to address the limitations of Gamma-Gamma-Poisson transitions by using deep learning, but they don’t demonstrate *why* a deep structure is inherently better equipped to capture complex temporal dependencies compared to a carefully designed shallow model.
*   **Experimental Analysis:** The experimental results, while comprehensive, are presented without sufficient critical analysis. The MSE/PMSE metrics are reported, but a more nuanced evaluation, perhaps incorporating metrics specific to each dataset (e.g., precision/recall for recommendation systems), would be more informative. The impact of different hyperparameters, beyond those mentioned, is not explored. The statement that "DDPFA has the best performance in most experiments" is an overstatement; PGDS performs better in the ICEWS prediction task, as acknowledged.
*   **Formatting and Typos:** Numerous minor formatting inconsistencies and typos are present throughout the paper. Examples include inconsistent notation (e.g., θt vs. θtk) and grammatical errors. While individually minor, these errors contribute to a lack of polish and detract from the paper's credibility. The reference formatting is also not consistent with NIPS style.
*   **Related Work:** The related work section is somewhat superficial. While several relevant papers are cited, the discussion lacks depth and fails to adequately position the DDPFA model within the existing literature.

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Against:**

*   The numerous minor flaws significantly detract from the paper’s overall quality.
*   The lack of theoretical analysis and justification weakens the scientific contribution.
*   The experimental analysis lacks sufficient depth and critical evaluation.
*   The writing is often imprecise and unclear.

**For:**

*   The core idea of combining PFA and RNNs is potentially promising.
*   The experimental setup is reasonably comprehensive.
*   The model demonstrates competitive performance on several datasets.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite presenting a potentially interesting idea, the numerous deficiencies in this paper prevent me from recommending acceptance. The lack of rigor, clarity, and thoroughness in the methodology and analysis are substantial concerns. While the authors demonstrate that their model can achieve competitive performance, the presented work feels more like a preliminary investigation than a polished, complete contribution. 

**Recommendation:** **Reject**

I recommend that the authors address the weaknesses outlined above—particularly focusing on clarifying the methodology, providing theoretical justification, and conducting a more thorough experimental analysis—before resubmitting. Improving the writing quality and addressing the formatting issues are also crucial.
