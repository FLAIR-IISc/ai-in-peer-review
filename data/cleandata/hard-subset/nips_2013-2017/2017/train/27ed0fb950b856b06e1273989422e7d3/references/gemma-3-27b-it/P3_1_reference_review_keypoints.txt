- Overall assessment: Proposes DDPFA (RNNs + PFA) but reviewer leans to a weak reject due to clarity, novelty, and validation issues.
- Problem addressed: Models sequential count vectors to capture complex temporal dependencies lacking in models like PGDS.
- Method: Introduces Deep Dynamic Poisson Factorization using RNNs within a PFA framework and variational inference.
- Related work: Coverage is broad but lacks deeper comparison to RNN approaches for count time series.
- Strength — Concept: Leveraging RNNs for temporal dynamics in PFA is a natural and potentially powerful extension.
- Strength — Foundation: Builds on established models (PFA) and uses standard techniques like variational inference.
- Strength — Datasets: Evaluated on diverse synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Strength — Motivation: Correctly identifies and attempts to address limitations of prior dynamic Poisson models.
- Weakness — Clarity: Presentation, dense notation, and inference descriptions (Eqs.15–18, ladder architecture) are hard to follow.
- Weakness — Novelty: Core contribution feels incremental; unclear what unique benefit depth provides beyond adding RNNs.
- Weakness — Experiments: Mixed results with modest gains over baselines, overstated claims (e.g., ICEWS), and missing ablation/hyperparameter studies.
- Weakness — LSTM analysis: Superficial explanation for LSTM underperformance and no mitigation strategies explored.
- Weakness — Computational cost: No discussion of runtime or resource demands compared to simpler baselines.
- Weakness — Pruning & MCMC: Use of "pruning" and suggestion of auxiliary MCMC raises concerns about variational inference reliability and reported results.
- Weakness — Inference complexity: Inference is overly complex without convincing justification or stability/ convergence analysis.
- Significance & originality: Moderate potential but limited originality — a clever combination rather than a fundamental advance.
- For acceptance: Reasonable idea, diverse datasets, and addresses known model limitations.
- Against acceptance: Lack of clarity, limited novelty, inconsistent empirical gains, complex/unstable inference, and missing cost analysis.
- Recommendations: Improve clarity, detail variational inference, justify novelty, run ablation/hyperparameter studies, compare to more RNN baselines, analyze ICEWS, report computational cost, and address pruning/inference issues (e.g., auxiliary MCMC).
- Conclusion: Paper is interesting but needs significant revision on clarity, novelty claims, experiments, and inference before being suitable for publication; weak reject recommended.
