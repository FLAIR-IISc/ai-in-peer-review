- Introduces the "Radon machine": train base learners on many small disjoint subsets, then hierarchically aggregate hypotheses via Radon points in a tree.

- Main theoretical result: Radon point aggregation reduces failure probability doubly-exponentially with tree height, enabling polylogarithmic runtime given quasi-polynomial processors.

- Claims connection to NC-learnability, advancing the theoretical question of which learning problems are in Nick’s Class.

- Empirical evaluation shows large speedups (80x–700x) over sequential learners and favorable comparisons to averaging and Spark MLlib.

- Strength — Originality: Using Radon points for hypothesis aggregation is novel and links practical parallelization to fundamental complexity theory.

- Strength — Technical quality: Theoretical analysis (Proposition 2, Theorem 3) is sound and elegant; empirical comparisons are strong and convincing.

- Strength — Clarity and generality: Paper is well-written; Radon machine is a black-box method applicable to consistent, convex-space learners without modifying base algorithms.

- Weakness — Impractical assumptions: Polylogarithmic runtime requires a quasi-polynomial number of processors, an unrealistic assumption not fully analyzed for fixed processor budgets.

- Weakness — Curse of dimensionality: Radon number r = d+2 makes the method impractical in high dimensions due to exponential data/subset requirements and r^3 Radon-point cost; experiments use relatively low-dimensional data.

- Weakness — Clarity of derivations: Key derivations (choice of h in Section 3, proof of Proposition 2) are compressed and need more detailed exposition.

- Suggestion: Add a corollary/discussion stating runtime and speedup for a fixed number of processors c < r^h to clarify practical trade-offs.

- Suggestion: Include at least one experiment on a higher-dimensional dataset to assess limitations and the impact of dimensionality reduction.

- Suggestion: Expand derivations in Section 3 and the proof of Proposition 2 to improve readability and self-containment.

- Overall: High-quality, novel, theoretically grounded, and empirically effective work with strong contributions despite practical and dimensionality limitations; recommend acceptance.
