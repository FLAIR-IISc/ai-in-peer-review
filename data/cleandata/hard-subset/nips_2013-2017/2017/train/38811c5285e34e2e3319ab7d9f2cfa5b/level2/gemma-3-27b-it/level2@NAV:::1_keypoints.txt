- Introduces a novel parallelisation scheme using iterative Radon point aggregation to achieve polylogarithmic runtime on a quasi-polynomial number of processors, addressing efficient parallelisation/NC questions.  
- Claims a broadly applicable, black-box approach requiring no algorithm-specific derivations with theoretical performance guarantees.  
- Theoretical novelty: runtime reduction from polynomial to polylogarithmic and a doubly-exponential confidence boost over averaging methods.  
- Theoretical results depend on strong conditions: need for a quasi-polynomial number of processors and potentially larger sample complexity.  
- Empirical results (Spark, WEKA/scikit-learn) show substantial speedups (up to 700x) and competitive performance versus Spark MLlib.  
- Major practical concern: the quasi-polynomial processor requirement poses a significant hurdle to applicability.  
- Communication complexity is mentioned but lacks a detailed, realistic distributed-environment analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope: datasets are modest and comparisons to Spark MLlib are not exhaustive.  
- Analysis of key parameters (Radon number r and aggregation parameter h) and their impact on performance and scalability is insufficient.  
- Treatment of high-dimensional data via embeddings is cursory and needs a more thorough examination.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand empirical evaluation to larger datasets, provide detailed communication-cost analysis, and elaborate handling of high-dimensional data.
