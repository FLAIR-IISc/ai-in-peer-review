The paper "Effective Parallelisation for Machine Learning" presents a novel parallelisation scheme for machine learning algorithms, leveraging Radon partitioning to aggregate weak learners. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis, accompanied by empirical results that demonstrate improvements in training time and/or AUC over multiple baseline algorithms across various datasets.

The theoretical analysis is well-motivated and effectively supports the proposed algorithm. The authors address several questions and concerns in the Evaluation and Discussion sections, showcasing the robustness of their approach.

The empirical evaluation is comprehensive, comparing the Radon machine to state-of-the-art parallel machine learning algorithms from the Spark machine learning library, as well as the natural baseline of averaging hypotheses. The results indicate that the Radon machine achieves a substantial reduction in computation time while maintaining comparable predictive performance.

The authors also discuss the applicability of their scheme to high-dimensional models, non-linear models, and distributed data streams, highlighting its potential for widespread adoption.

Strengths:

* Novel parallelisation scheme leveraging Radon partitioning
* Thorough theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation
* Robust performance across various datasets and baseline algorithms
* Potential for applicability to high-dimensional models, non-linear models, and distributed data streams

Weaknesses:

* Minor formatting issues (e.g., font size, figure text size)
* Inconsistent citation style
* Some results could be more effectively presented (e.g., comparison with AverageAtTheEnd method)

Quality: The paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims and a clear presentation of the proposed algorithm and its evaluation.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow for readers familiar with machine learning and parallel computing.

Originality: The paper presents a novel parallelisation scheme, and the authors effectively demonstrate its advantages over existing approaches.

Significance: The results have significant implications for the field of machine learning, as they demonstrate the potential for efficient parallelisation of machine learning algorithms. The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.
