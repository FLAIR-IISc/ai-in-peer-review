

The authors propose a method for parallelizing learners (under specific assumptions) using Radon points. The paper's contribution relative to prior work lies in employing Radon points instead of averaging, which serves as the baseline approach. Performance is evaluated against three baselines: no parallelization, the averaging schema, and Spark-based algorithms. Experiments demonstrate reduced learning time while achieving comparable or higher accuracy than the competing methods. However, the theoretical foundation for the parallelization approach appears to have been previously established in the authors' paper "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which is not cited. The paper must clarify how its theoretical contribution differs from or extends this prior work.
