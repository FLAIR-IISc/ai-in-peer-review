The authors present a method for parallelizing learners, operating under specific assumptions, leveraging Radon points. The key novelty of this work lies in the application of Radon points for parallelization, distinguishing it from prior approaches that typically rely on averaging – a method used here as a baseline. The experimental evaluation compares the proposed approach against scenarios with no parallelization, an averaging scheme, and Spark-based algorithms, demonstrating reductions in learning time alongside comparable or improved accuracy. 

However, the theoretical foundations appear closely related to those explored in the authors’ prior work, “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” which is not cited within the manuscript. The paper would benefit from a clear articulation of its unique theoretical contributions in relation to this existing research.
