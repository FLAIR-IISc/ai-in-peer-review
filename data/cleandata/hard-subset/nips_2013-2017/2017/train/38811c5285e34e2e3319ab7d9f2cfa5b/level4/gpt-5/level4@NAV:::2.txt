The authors present a method for parallelizing learners (under certain assumptions) based on Radon points. The paper’s contribution relative to similar work is the use of Radon points in place of averaging (which serves as the baseline approach in the paper). Performance is compared against “no parallelization,” an “averaging scheme,” and “Spark-based” algorithms. The experiments show reduced training time and comparable or better accuracy than the other methods.

It appears that the theoretical component has already been studied in another paper, “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” likely by the same authors. This prior work is not cited in the current manuscript, and the paper should clarify what its specific theoretical contribution is beyond that result.
