- Proposes a method to parallelize learners under certain assumptions by leveraging Radon points.  
- Primary contribution is using Radon points as an alternative to simple averaging.  
- Empirically compared against three baselines: no parallelization, averaging, and Spark-based algorithms.  
- Experiments report reduced learning time and comparable or better accuracy than competitors.  
- Theoretical foundations seem to be treated in a separate paper ("Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization"), likely by the same authors.  
- The manuscript fails to cite that related work and does not clearly state its novel theoretical contributions.
