The authors proposed an approach to parallelize learners (who have some notions) based on Radon points. The contribution of this paper to other similar tasks is to use Radon points instead of averaging (which is used as the basic approach in the paper). The performance is compared to 'no parallelism', 'averaging schema', and 'spark-based' algorithms. They have experimentally shown lower learning time and high or equal accuracy to other algorithms.

It seems that the theoretical part has already been examined in another paper: "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization", which may be by the same authors. However, it is not cited in the current paper and needs to be elaborated on to explain what the paper's contribution is to this part.
