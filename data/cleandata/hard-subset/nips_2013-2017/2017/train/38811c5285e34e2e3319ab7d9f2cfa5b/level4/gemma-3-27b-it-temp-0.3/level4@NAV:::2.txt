The paper presents a method for parallelizing learners – operating under specific assumptions – utilizing Radon points. The key novelty lies in employing Radon points for parallelization, distinguishing it from prior work that typically relies on averaging as a baseline, as demonstrated within this study. The authors evaluate their approach against scenarios with ‘no parallelization’, an ‘averaging schema’, and ‘Spark-based’ algorithms, reporting experimental results indicating reduced learning time alongside comparable or improved accuracy. 

However, the theoretical foundations appear closely related to work previously published in “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization” (potentially by the same authors), and this connection is not explicitly acknowledged. The paper would benefit from a clear articulation of its unique theoretical contributions in relation to that prior work.
