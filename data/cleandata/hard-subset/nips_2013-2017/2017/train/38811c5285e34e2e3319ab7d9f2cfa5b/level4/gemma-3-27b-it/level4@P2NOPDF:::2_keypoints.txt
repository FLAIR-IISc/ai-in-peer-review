- Introduces a parallelization method for ML algorithms using Radon points under specific assumptions.  
- Main contribution: replacing standard averaging with a Radon-point-based aggregation technique.  
- Empirical results show reduced learning time while maintaining comparable or improved accuracy.  
- Comparisons include non-parallelized, averaging-based parallelization, and a Spark-based algorithm.  
- Experiments demonstrate clear computational-efficiency benefits.  
- Theoretical foundations appear closely related to the authors’ prior work “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” which is not cited.  
- The relationship to the previous paper is unclear—uncertain if this work builds on, extends, or simply applies earlier theory.  
- The manuscript lacks a detailed explanation of novel theoretical contributions differentiating it from prior work.  
- Without that theoretical clarification, the independent merit of the paper’s theoretical components is difficult to assess despite valuable practical results.
