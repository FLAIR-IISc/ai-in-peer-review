The authors present a method for parallelizing learners, operating under specific assumptions, using Radon points. The primary contribution of this work, relative to existing approaches, lies in the utilization of Radon points for parallelization, as opposed to the averaging schemes commonly used as a baseline – and indeed, employed as a comparison point here. The experimental evaluation compares the proposed approach to strategies involving no parallelization, averaging, and a Spark-based implementation, demonstrating reduced learning time and comparable or improved accuracy. 

However, the theoretical foundations appear closely related to work presented in “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” potentially by the same authors. This prior work is not cited, and the paper would benefit from a clear explanation of its novelty and contribution with respect to the theoretical results already established therein.
