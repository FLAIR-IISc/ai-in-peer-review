This paper proposes a method for parallelizing a class of learning algorithms using Radon points. The primary contribution, relative to prior work, is the use of Radon points to combine the outputs of parallel learners, offering an alternative to the more common model averaging approach, which the authors include as a baseline. The performance of the proposed method is compared against several alternatives: a sequential (non-parallelized) learner, a model averaging scheme, and a Spark-based algorithm. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method reduces learning time while achieving accuracy comparable to or higher than the baseline algorithms.

A significant concern, however, is the novelty of the theoretical contribution. The theoretical foundation of this work appears to overlap significantly with a prior paper titled "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which may be from the same authors. This potential prior work is not cited in the current manuscript. The authors must address this omission and clearly delineate the unique theoretical contributions of the present paper in light of this previous research.
