- Proposes a novel "Radon machine" parallelisation scheme that iteratively computes Radon points of hypotheses from random data subsets.  
- Claims potential for significant runtime reduction (possibly polylogarithmic) while preserving theoretical performance guarantees at the cost of higher sample complexity.  
- Supports claims with theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation, comparing to averaging and Spark MLlib implementations.  
- Offers a black-box parallelisation approach requiring minimal changes to existing learning algorithms.  
- Theoretical contributions include a link to Nick’s Class and complexity bounds; Theorem 3 shows doubly exponential reduction in error probability per iteration.  
- Empirical results demonstrate substantial speedups over sequential and existing parallel methods, with useful comparison to Spark MLlib.  
- Radon point concepts and their role are clearly explained, though unfamiliar to many practitioners.  
- Main strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and clear exposition of the Radon machine mechanics.  
- Limitation: empirical evaluation centers on relatively low-dimensional datasets.  
- Suggested remedies like random projections and low-rank approximations are discussed but not empirically validated within the framework.  
- Communication complexity is acknowledged but only briefly discussed; costs in distributed settings need deeper analysis.  
- Application to non-linear models is only lightly treated; practical challenges and limitations require more detail.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, strong guarantees, empirical speedups, and broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited high-dimensional experiments, insufficient communication complexity treatment, and underdeveloped discussion of non-linear model application.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution; accept with minor revisions to expand high-dimensional empirical evaluation and to deepen discussion of communication costs and non-linear model applicability.
