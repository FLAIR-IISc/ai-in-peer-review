- Proposes a novel parallelization method using Radon points instead of conventional averaging for learners.
- Main contribution is this alternative parallelization strategy positioned as a refinement over averaging baselines.
- Experiments show substantially reduced training time while maintaining comparable or better accuracy versus no parallelization, averaging, and Spark.
- Empirical validation across multiple datasets demonstrates practical utility, especially when communication overhead is a bottleneck.
- Major concern: apparent close overlap with authors’ prior work "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization" and omission of citation.
- Authors must explicitly cite and acknowledge the prior work and its relevance to the current manuscript.
- Paper should clearly state whether the Radon point approach provides new theoretical guarantees beyond the prior work.
- Need to clarify how assumptions or problem scope differ from the earlier publication.
- Must articulate why the theoretical analysis here is distinct from or complementary to the prior results.
- Experimental section needs deeper analysis explaining why Radon points outperform averaging (e.g., handling non-convexity or heterogeneous data).
- Should discuss trade-offs between computational efficiency and statistical accuracy to strengthen the narrative.
- Writing is generally clear but contains minor grammatical issues (e.g., "use of Radon points," "training time").
- Comparison with Spark-based methods is relevant but lacks implementation details (data partitioning, communication protocol).
- Overall, the empirical contribution is promising and well-motivated, but theoretical novelty is unclear due to missing scholarly context.
- Recommendation: revise to address citation gap and explicitly delineate theoretical advancement; resubmission would then be strongly recommended.
