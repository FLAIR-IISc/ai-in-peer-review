The authors propose an approach for parallelizing learners that rely on specific assumptions, leveraging Radon points as a key component. The primary contribution of this paper, relative to similar works, lies in its use of Radon points as an alternative to the baseline approach of averaging. The authors evaluate the performance of their method by comparing it to three alternatives: 'no parallelization', 'averaging schema', and 'Spark-based' algorithms. Their experimental results demonstrate reduced learning times and comparable or superior accuracy to the competing methods.

However, it appears that the theoretical foundation of this work has been previously explored in another paper, "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization." Notably, this paper is not cited in the current manuscript, and the authors should clarify the novel contributions of their work in this context. Specifically, they need to elaborate on what new insights or advancements their paper brings to the theoretical aspects of parallelizing randomized convex optimization.
