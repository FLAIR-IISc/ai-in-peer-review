The authors propose an approach for parallelizing learners that rely on certain assumptions, leveraging Radon points. The primary contribution of this paper, relative to similar works, lies in its use of Radon points as an alternative to averaging, which serves as the baseline approach. The authors evaluate the performance of their method against three benchmarks: no parallelization, averaging schema, and Spark-based algorithms. Their experimental results demonstrate reduced learning time and comparable or superior accuracy to the competing algorithms.

However, it appears that the theoretical foundations of this work have been explored in a separate paper, "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which may be authored by the same researchers. This paper fails to cite the aforementioned work and does not provide a clear explanation of its novel contributions to the theoretical aspects of parallelization.
