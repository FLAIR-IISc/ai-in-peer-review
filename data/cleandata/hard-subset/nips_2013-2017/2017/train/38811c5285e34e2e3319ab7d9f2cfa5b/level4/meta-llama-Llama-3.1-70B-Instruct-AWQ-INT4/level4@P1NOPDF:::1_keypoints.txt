- Main contribution: novel parallel ML framework that combines subsets of hypotheses via their Radon point instead of simple averaging.  
- Methodology: black-box approach for combining weak learners, presented as an alternative to bagging.  
- Theoretical results: complexity bounds showing more efficient parallelization by splitting samples, plus a derived PAC bound for the Radon machine.  
- Empirical evaluation: comparisons made against parallel algorithms in Spark and base linear models in Weka.  
- Overall assessment: theoretical contributions are solid.  
- Major concern: practicality on high-dimensional data is unclear.  
- Experimental limitation: datasets used are very low-dimensional (≈18 features), not representative of modern problems.  
- Recommendation: authors should demonstrate or explain how the method scales to and handles high-dimensional data to increase impact.
