- Introduces a scheme for parallelizing learning algorithms using Radon points (under stated assumptions).  
- Main contribution is replacing simple averaging with Radon-point aggregation; averaging used as baseline in experiments.  
- Empirical evaluation compares against no parallelization, an averaging-based scheme, and Spark-based methods.  
- Results show reduced training time and comparable or better accuracy than the alternatives.  
- The theoretical component appears to overlap a prior work (“Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization”), which is not cited; the manuscript should clarify the relationship and its unique theoretical contributions.
