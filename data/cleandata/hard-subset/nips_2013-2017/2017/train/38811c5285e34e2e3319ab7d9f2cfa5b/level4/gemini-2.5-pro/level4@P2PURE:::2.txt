This paper proposes an approach for parallelizing certain learning algorithms using Radon points. The primary contribution, in contrast to similar work, is the use of Radon points instead of parameter averaging, which the authors include as a baseline. The method's performance is compared against a non-parallelized baseline, an averaging scheme, and existing Spark-based algorithms. The experiments demonstrate that the proposed method reduces learning time while achieving accuracy comparable or superior to the other approaches.

A significant concern is that the theoretical foundation of this work appears to have been previously investigated in another paper, "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which may be by the same authors. Critically, this prior work is not cited in the current submission. The authors must clarify what novel theoretical contributions this paper makes beyond those presented in the earlier work.
