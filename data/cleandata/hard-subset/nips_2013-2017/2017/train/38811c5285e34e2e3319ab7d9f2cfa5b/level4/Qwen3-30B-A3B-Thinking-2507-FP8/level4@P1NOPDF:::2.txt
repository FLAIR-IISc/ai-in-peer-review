

The authors propose a method for parallelizing learners under specific assumptions using Radon points. The key contribution lies in replacing the standard averaging approach (used as a baseline) with Radon points, which they demonstrate through experiments. They compare their approach against three baselines: no parallelization, the averaging schema, and Spark-based implementations. Results show reduced training time while maintaining comparable or superior accuracy relative to these baselines.

However, a significant concern arises regarding the theoretical foundation. The core theoretical framework appears to overlap substantially with the authors' prior work, "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which has not been cited in the current paper. The authors must explicitly clarify how this work advances beyond their previous contribution. Specifically, they should address whether the current paper introduces novel theoretical guarantees, extends the scope of assumptions, or provides a distinct analytical perspective that justifies its independent publication. The absence of this citation and clarification undermines the stated novelty of the theoretical component. Without this, the paper risks presenting incremental work rather than a meaningful advancement. The reviewers urge the authors to revise the manuscript to explicitly position their theoretical contribution against this prior publication, detailing the specific gaps or extensions that justify the current submission.
