The authors have presented a parallelization algorithm for aggregating weak learners based on Radon partitioning, accompanied by a theoretical analysis that motivates the algorithm and empirical results that support the theory. The theoretical analysis is interesting, and the empirical results demonstrate improvements in training time and/or AUC over multiple baseline algorithms across multiple datasets. The authors also preemptively and convincingly address several questions and concerns in the Evaluation and Discussion sections. I recommend acceptance.

Specific Notes
--------------

- Line 51: There is a minor typo ("the the") that should be corrected to "the".
- Line 79: The font size of the mathematical notation appears to be smaller than elsewhere in the paper, making it somewhat difficult to read.
- Figures 1-3: The text in the figure images is too small and should be increased in size for better readability.
- Figure 3: This figure may be challenging to distinguish when printed in black and white, which could be a concern for some readers.
- Lines 318-320: To clarify, is the statement "the Radon machine outperforms the averaging baseline" based on an average of paired comparisons?
- Line 324: It would be more precise to avoid using the word "significant" in this context, as it typically implies statistical significance, which is not the intended meaning here.
- Lines 340-341: There are minor formatting issues ("machineachieved" and "machineis") that should be corrected by adding spaces.
- Lines 341-349: The difference in performance between the proposed method and the AverageAtTheEnd method does not seem substantial. It would be helpful to include this result in Figure 1 for a more comprehensive comparison. The authors could also provide more justification for their method in this context.
- Line 377: The citation style for "Long and servedio [22]" appears to be inconsistent with the citation style used elsewhere in the paper, including the immediately following citations.
