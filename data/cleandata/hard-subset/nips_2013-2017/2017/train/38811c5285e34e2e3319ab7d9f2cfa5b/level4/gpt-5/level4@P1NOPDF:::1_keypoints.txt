- Proposes a new parallelization framework that aggregates base learners by replacing subsets of hypotheses with their Radon point.  
- Claims this aggregation is a more effective alternative to simple averaging (e.g., bagging) for combining weak learners.  
- Provides complexity bounds showing the parallel Radon machine can be more efficient than training a base learner on the full dataset.  
- Derives a PAC bound for the Radon machine.  
- Empirically compares the method to parallel algorithms implemented in Spark and to base linear models in Weka.  
- Reviewer finds the approach interesting and the theoretical contributions solid.  
- Main concern: practical applicability to high-dimensional data is unclear.  
- Experiments use very low-dimensional datasets (~18 features), so results may not generalize to modern high-dimensional problems; authors should clarify or provide empirical/discussion on high-dimensional scaling.
