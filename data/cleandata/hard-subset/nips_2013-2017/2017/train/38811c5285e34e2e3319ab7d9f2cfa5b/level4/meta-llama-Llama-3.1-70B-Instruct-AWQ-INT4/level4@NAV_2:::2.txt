The paper "Effective Parallelisation for Machine Learning" presents a novel approach to parallelising machine learning algorithms using Radon points. The authors propose a parallelisation scheme that can be applied to a broad class of learning algorithms without requiring further mathematical derivations or dedicated code. The scheme achieves a reduction in runtime from polynomial to polylogarithmic time on quasi-polynomially many processing units.

The main theoretical contribution of the paper is to show that algorithms satisfying certain conditions can be parallelised effectively using Radon points. The authors provide a guarantee on the quality of Radon points and show that the probability of obtaining an ε-bad hypothesis is doubly exponentially reduced. The paper also presents an empirical evaluation of the proposed parallelisation scheme, comparing it to state-of-the-art parallel machine learning algorithms from the Spark machine learning library.

The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of parallel machine learning, providing a new approach to parallelising learning algorithms. The use of Radon points instead of averaging is a key innovation, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness through theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation.
