The authors propose a method for parallelizing students based on Radon points (which assumes certain assumptions). The contribution of this paper compared to other similar works is the use of Radon points instead of averaging (which was used as a basis in this paper). The effectiveness is compared with `no paralysis', `averaging schema', and `spark-based' algorithms. They experimentally show that learning time is reduced compared to other algorithms, and accuracy is almost the same or higher.

It seems that the theoretical part has already been explored in another paper: "Paralizing Randomized Convex Optimization", which may be from the same authors. However, it is not cited in the current paper, and it is necessary to explain in detail what the contribution of the paper is for this part.
