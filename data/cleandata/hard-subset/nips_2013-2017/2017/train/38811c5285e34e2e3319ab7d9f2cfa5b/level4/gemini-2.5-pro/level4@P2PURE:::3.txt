This paper introduces a parallelization algorithm for aggregating weak learners based on Radon partitioning. The algorithm is motivated by a sound theoretical analysis and supported by empirical results that demonstrate improvements in training time and/or AUC over several baselines across multiple datasets. The theoretical contributions are interesting, and I commend the authors for proactively and convincingly addressing potential concerns in the Evaluation and Discussion sections. I recommend acceptance.

Specific Notes
--------------
*   Line 51: A minor typo, "the the" should be "the".
*   Line 79: The math font appears smaller than in other parts of the paper, which affects readability.
*   Figures 1-3: The text within the figures is too small and should be enlarged for legibility.
*   Figure 3: The color scheme may not be distinguishable when printed in grayscale. Please consider using different markers or line styles for better accessibility.
*   Lines 318-320: The claim that "the Radon machine outperforms the averaging baseline" needs clarification. Is this conclusion based on an average of paired comparisons across datasets?
*   Line 324: The term "significant" is used, but it is unclear if this refers to a statistically significant result. It is best to reserve this term for cases where statistical tests have been performed.
*   Lines 340-341: There are missing spaces in "machineachieved" and "machineis".
*   Lines 341-349: The performance gap between the proposed method and the "AverageAtTheEnd" baseline appears marginal. It would be helpful if the authors could elaborate on the practical importance of this difference. Additionally, why were these results omitted from Figure 1? A stronger justification for the proposed method over this specific baseline would strengthen the paper.
*   Line 377: The citation style "Long and servedio [22]" appears inconsistent with the numbered citation format used elsewhere in the paper.
