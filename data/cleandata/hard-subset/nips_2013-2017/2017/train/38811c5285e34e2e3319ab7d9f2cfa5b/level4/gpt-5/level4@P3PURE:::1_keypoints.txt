- Introduces a new parallel ML framework that aggregates base learners by replacing hypothesis subsets with their Radon point.  
- Provides complexity bounds and a PAC bound for the Radon machine.  
- Empirically compares the method to parallel algorithms in Spark and to base linear models in Weka.  
- Presents a largely black-box aggregation approach that serves as an alternative to bagging.  
- Theoretical contributions (complexity analysis and PAC guarantee) are strong.  
- Main concern: practical performance and scalability in high-dimensional settings are unclear.  
- Experiments use very low-dimensional datasets (~18 features), so results may not generalize to modern high-dimensional applications; need empirical evidence or concrete discussion of computational and statistical behavior in high dimensions.
