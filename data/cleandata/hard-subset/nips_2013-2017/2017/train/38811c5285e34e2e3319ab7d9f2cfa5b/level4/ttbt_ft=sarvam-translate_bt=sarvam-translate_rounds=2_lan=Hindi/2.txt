The authors proposed an approach to parallelize learners (who have some assumptions) based on Radon points. The contribution of this paper to other similar tasks is using Radon points instead of averages (which is used as the basic approach in the paper). The performance is compared to 'no parallelism', 'averaging schema', and 'spark-based' algorithms. They have experimentally shown lower learning time and higher or equal accuracy for other algorithms.

It seems that the theoretical part has already been investigated in another paper: "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization", which may be by the same authors. However, it is not cited in the current paper and needs to be elaborated to explain what the paper's contribution is in this part.
