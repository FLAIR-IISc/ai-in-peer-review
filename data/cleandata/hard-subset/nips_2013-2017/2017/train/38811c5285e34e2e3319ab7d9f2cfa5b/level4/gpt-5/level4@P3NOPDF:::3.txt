Summary
This paper presents a parallelization algorithm for aggregating weak learners via Radon partitioning (“Radon Machine”). The authors provide solid theoretical motivation alongside an empirical evaluation across multiple datasets and baselines. The theory is interesting and clearly explained, and the experiments show improvements in training time and/or AUC relative to strong baselines. The Evaluation and Discussion sections anticipate and address several likely concerns. I recommend acceptance.

Strengths
- Clear theoretical foundation that motivates the algorithmic design.
- Broad and sensible empirical evaluation demonstrating either accuracy (AUC) gains or training-time reductions on multiple datasets.
- Strong positioning relative to baselines, with thoughtful discussion of practical considerations and limitations.
- Generally well written and organized; most claims are tied to supporting evidence.

Questions and requests for clarification
- Lines 318–320: “the Radon machine outperforms the averaging baseline...” Please clarify precisely how this statement is computed. Is it based on an average of paired comparisons across datasets/seeds? Report the aggregation method (e.g., mean rank, mean paired difference), and, if applicable, include confidence intervals or p-values.
- Statistical claims: Where you use “significant” to describe performance differences, please report formal statistical significance tests (test type, correction if multiple comparisons, and p-values). Otherwise, use alternative wording such as “substantial” or “pronounced” (see note on Line 324 below).
- AverageAtTheEnd comparison: The reported differences versus the AverageAtTheEnd method (Lines 341–349) seem modest. Please strengthen the case by (a) quantifying effect sizes, (b) clarifying the conditions under which Radon offers clear advantages, and (c) explaining why those results were not included in Figure 1. If the differences are small, consider discussing when the simpler approach may suffice.

Suggestions for improvement
- Figures 1–3: Increase font sizes and axis label readability. Ensure line widths and markers are legible when printed. For Figure 3, use styles that remain distinguishable in grayscale (e.g., marker shapes, dashed vs. solid lines) to support black-and-white printing.
- Math typesetting: Ensure mathematical symbols and equations (e.g., Line 79) use a consistent, sufficiently large font size for readability.
- Consistency in citations and capitalization: Unify the citation style throughout the paper. Avoid mixing “Author and Author [22]” with bracketed references alone, and ensure proper capitalization (see Line 377).

Minor comments and typos
- Line 51: “the the” → “the.”
- Line 79: The math font appears smaller than elsewhere; make the size consistent.
- Figures 1–3: Increase font sizes for figure text; ensure legends and axis labels are readable.
- Figure 3: May be difficult to distinguish in black and white; adjust styles accordingly.
- Lines 318–320: Clarify whether the statement “Radon machine outperforms the averaging baseline” is based on paired comparisons; if so, specify the pairing and statistical test.
- Line 324: Avoid using “significant” unless reporting statistical significance. Consider alternatives such as “notable,” “meaningful,” or “large.”
- Lines 340–341: Insert missing spaces: “machine achieved,” and “machine is.”
- Lines 341–349: The difference versus AverageAtTheEnd seems small. Please motivate the practical relevance, report effect sizes and statistical tests, and consider adding these results to Figure 1 or the main text for visibility.
- Line 377: “Long and servedio [22]” → “Long and Servedio [22].” Also ensure consistency with the citation style used elsewhere (e.g., either author-year in text or bracketed numeric references, but not a mixture).

Overall assessment
This is a well-executed paper that combines a principled theoretical contribution with an informative empirical study. With improved figure readability, clearer statistical reporting, and stronger positioning relative to the AverageAtTheEnd baseline, the work will be even more compelling. I recommend acceptance.
