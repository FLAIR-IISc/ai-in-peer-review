This paper introduces the Radon Machine, a novel algorithm for the parallelized aggregation of weak learners, grounded in the principles of Radon partitioning. The authors provide a compelling theoretical analysis to motivate their approach and validate it with empirical results across multiple datasets. The experiments demonstrate that the proposed method can achieve improvements in training time and/or AUC compared to several established baseline algorithms. Furthermore, the paper is well-structured, with the Evaluation and Discussion sections proactively addressing potential questions and limitations, which strengthens the overall contribution. I find the work to be sound and valuable, and I recommend acceptance.

Below are more specific comments and suggestions for improvement.

### Strengths

1.  **Novelty and Theoretical Grounding:** The core idea of using Radon partitioning as a mechanism for aggregating models in an ensemble is both interesting and novel. The theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the algorithm, connecting geometric concepts to a practical machine learning problem.
2.  **Strong Empirical Validation:** The experimental results are comprehensive and generally convincing. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the Radon Machine on multiple datasets, comparing it against relevant baselines and showing tangible benefits in terms of either predictive performance (AUC) or computational efficiency.
3.  **Clarity and Proactive Discussion:** The paper is clearly written. I particularly commend the authors for anticipating and addressing several potential concerns in the main body of the paper, which shows a thorough consideration of their method's context and limitations.

### Suggestions for Improvement and Clarifications

While the paper is strong overall, a few points could be clarified or strengthened to enhance its impact.

*   **Baseline Comparison Methodology (Lines 318-320):** The text states, "the Radon machine outperforms the averaging baseline...". To make this claim more precise, please clarify the methodology behind this comparison. Is this conclusion based on an average of paired comparisons across all datasets, a sign test, or another form of aggregate analysis? A brief explanation would add rigor to the statement.

*   **Comparison with "AverageAtTheEnd" (Lines 341-349):** The discussion of the `AverageAtTheEnd` baseline is appreciated, but the reported performance difference does not appear to be substantial. This raises the question of why this result was not included alongside the other baselines in Figure 1 for a direct comparison. I would encourage the authors to either:
    1.  Add this baseline to the main figures to provide a more complete picture, or
    2.  Expand the discussion to better advocate for the Radon Machine in this context. If the advantage is not purely in AUC, perhaps there are other benefits (e.g., theoretical guarantees, robustness, memory efficiency) that make it a preferable choice over this simple and effective baseline.

*   **Use of "Significant" (Line 324):** A minor pet peeve is the use of the word "significant" in scientific papers when not referring to a formal statistical test. To avoid ambiguity, I suggest replacing it with a more descriptive term like "substantial," "notable," or "meaningful," unless statistical significance has been established.

### Minor Comments and Typos

*   **Figures 1-3:** The text within the figures (e.g., axis labels, legends) is too small, which makes them difficult to read. Increasing the font size would significantly improve the paper's readability.
*   **Figure 3:** The choice of colors in this figure may be difficult to distinguish if the paper is printed in black and white. I suggest using different markers or line styles in addition to color to ensure accessibility.
*   **Line 51:** Typo: "the the" should be "the".
*   **Line 79:** The font size used for the mathematical notation in this line appears smaller than elsewhere in the paper, affecting readability. Please check for consistency.
*   **Lines 340-341:** Missing spaces in "machineachieved" and "machineis."
*   **Line 377:** The citation style "Long and servedio [22]" is inconsistent with the numeric-only style used in most other places (including the immediately following citation). Please ensure a consistent citation format throughout the manuscript.
