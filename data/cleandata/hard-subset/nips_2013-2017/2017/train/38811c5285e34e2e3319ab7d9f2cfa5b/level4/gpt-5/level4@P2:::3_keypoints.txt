- Proposal: Introduces the Radon machine, a black-box parallelization that trains base learners on iid subsets and aggregates hypotheses via iterated Radon points along a tree instead of simple averaging.  
- Theory: Shows that under convexity, consistency, and finite Radon number the bad-hypothesis probability drops doubly exponentially with tree height, achieving (ε, Δ) guarantees in polylogarithmic time with quasi-polynomial processors.  
- Empirics: Reports large wall-clock speedups (often 1–2 orders of magnitude) with comparable predictive performance and generally better AUC than averaging-at-the-end and Spark baselines.  
- Context: Places the work relative to Nick’s Class, averaging schemes, and distributed SGD approaches.  
- Quality: Theoretical results are well-motivated and clearly linked to algorithm design; assumptions are explicit and experiments support claims.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-organized with clear algorithm description and discussion of limitations, but needs fixes for figure readability, minor typos, and citation consistency.  
- Originality: Novel use of Radon points for hypothesis aggregation yielding double-exponential confidence amplification and NC-style parallelization.  
- Significance: Offers both theoretical insight (Radon-number-based parallelization) and a practical plug-in method that could change how convex learners are parallelized when high confidence is needed.  
- Pros: General black-box applicability, strong theoretical guarantees, solid empirical speedups and AUC improvements, thoughtful scope/limitations discussion.  
- Cons: Strongest runtime guarantees require quasi-polynomial processors; reliance on Radon number ties performance to dimensionality (may need random projections); some empirical “significant” claims lack reported statistical tests.  
- Specific issues: Fix typo “the the”; unify math font sizes; enlarge figure labels/legends and ensure grayscale-friendly color schemes; clarify whether AUC improvements are paired/statistically tested; avoid using “significant” without tests; fix missing spaces and citation formatting.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
