The authors propose a method for parallelizing learners, under certain assumptions, using Radon points. The paper’s main contribution relative to prior work is replacing averaging with Radon points; averaging is used as the baseline in the experiments. Performance is compared against no parallelization, an averaging scheme, and Spark-based algorithms. Empirically, the method achieves reduced training time and comparable or better accuracy than these alternatives.

It appears the theoretical component has already been studied in another paper, “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” likely by the same authors. This prior work is not cited here, and the manuscript should clarify what the novel theoretical contributions of the current paper are.
