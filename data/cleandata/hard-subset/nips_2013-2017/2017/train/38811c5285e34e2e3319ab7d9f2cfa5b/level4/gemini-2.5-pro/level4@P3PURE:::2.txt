This paper proposes a method for parallelizing a class of learning algorithms based on Radon points. The primary contribution, in contrast to related work, is the use of Radon points instead of the standard model averaging technique, which serves as a baseline in the paper's experiments. The authors compare their approach against several baselines: no parallelization, a simple averaging scheme, and a Spark-based algorithm. The experimental results indicate that the proposed method reduces learning time while achieving accuracy comparable to or greater than the other methods.

A significant concern is that the theoretical foundation of this work appears to have been previously investigated in a paper titled "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which may be from the same authors. This prior work is not cited in the current manuscript. The authors should address this omission and clearly elaborate on the novel theoretical contributions of this paper relative to the existing work.
