This paper introduces a novel parallelization algorithm for aggregating weak learners, grounded in the concept of Radon partitioning. The authors provide a compelling theoretical analysis to motivate their approach and support it with empirical results across multiple datasets. The experiments demonstrate improvements in training time and/or AUC over several baseline methods. The paper is well-written, and the authors proactively address potential concerns in the Evaluation and Discussion sections. Overall, this is a strong submission, and I recommend acceptance.

### Major Points

*   **Comparison to "AverageAtTheEnd" (Lines 341-349):** In the discussion of the 'AverageAtTheEnd' method, the reported performance difference does not appear to be substantial. Could the authors provide a stronger justification for the advantages of their method in this specific comparison? Additionally, it would be helpful to understand why this particular result was not included in Figure 1 alongside the other baselines.

*   **Clarification on Comparison Methodology (Lines 318-320):** The paper claims that "the Radon machine outperforms the averaging baseline." For clarity, could the authors specify how this comparison was performed? For instance, is this conclusion based on an average of paired comparisons across datasets?

*   **Use of the word "significant" (Line 324):** The word "significant" is used to describe an improvement. To avoid ambiguity, it would be clearer if this term were reserved for claims of statistical significance, or if the authors could clarify the intended meaning (e.g., "a substantial improvement").

### Minor Comments & Typos

*   **Figure Legibility:** The legibility of Figures 1-3 could be improved by increasing the font size of the text and labels. Furthermore, Figure 3 may be difficult to interpret if printed in black and white; using different markers or line styles in addition to color would enhance its accessibility.

*   **Line 51:** Typo, "the the" should be "the".

*   **Line 79:** The math font appears smaller than in other parts of the text, making it slightly difficult to read. Please check for consistency.

*   **Lines 340-341:** Missing spaces in "machineachieved" and "machineis."

*   **Line 377:** The citation style for "Long and servedio [22]," which names the authors in the text, seems inconsistent with other citations in the paper.
