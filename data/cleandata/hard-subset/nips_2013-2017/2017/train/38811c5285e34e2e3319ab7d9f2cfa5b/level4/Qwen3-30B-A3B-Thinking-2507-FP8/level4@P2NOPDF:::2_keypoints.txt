- Proposes a parallelization framework using Radon points instead of averaging, with experiments showing reduced training time and comparable or better accuracy vs "no parallelization," "averaging-based," and "Spark-based" baselines.  
- Major concern: theoretical novelty is unclear because core theory appears addressed in the authors' prior work ("Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization"), which is not adequately cited or differentiated.  
- The paper must clarify whether its contribution is (i) an application to a new domain, (ii) a refinement of theoretical guarantees, or (iii) a novel implementation for specific learner architectures.  
- Experimental comparisons are adequate but lack analysis of why Radon points outperform averaging (e.g., sensitivity to data distribution or communication overhead).  
- The Spark-based baseline is unspecified (which implementation, e.g., MLlib), harming reproducibility.  
- Overall: empirical results are promising, but omission of key citations and unclear theoretical distinction weaken the paper; revision is needed to contextualize novelty and deepen explanatory analysis.
