This paper introduces a novel parallel algorithm for aggregating weak learners, founded on the principle of Radon partitioning. The proposed method is well-motivated by theoretical analysis and is further supported by empirical evidence. The theory presented is compelling, and the experimental results showcase improvements in either training efficiency or AUC when compared against several baselines across a variety of datasets. Furthermore, the authors effectively anticipate and address potential concerns within their evaluation and discussion, strengthening the submission. Overall, I recommend this paper for acceptance.

Specific Notes
--------------
-   Line 51: A minor typo ("the the") should be corrected to "the".
-   Line 79: The mathematical font appears smaller than in other parts of the text, which affects readability.

-   Figure 3: The choice of colors may make this figure difficult to interpret if printed in black and white.

-   Line 324: The term "significant" is used in a non-statistical context. It is generally advisable to reserve this word for statistically validated claims to avoid ambiguity.
-   Lines 340-341: There are missing spaces in "machineachieved" and "machineis."
-   Lines 341-349: The performance gap with the AverageAtTheEnd baseline appears marginal. It would be helpful if the authors could elaborate on the advantages of their method in this context and provide a stronger argument for its utility. Additionally, could they explain why this specific comparison was omitted from Figure 1?
-   Line 377: The citation style ("Long and servedio [22]") seems inconsistent with the numbered-only format used elsewhere in the manuscript.
