This paper proposes a parallelization scheme for a class of learning algorithms (under certain assumptions) that aggregates models using Radon points rather than simple averaging. The main claimed contribution over prior parallelization-by-averaging approaches is the use of Radon points for aggregation. The empirical evaluation compares against three baselines: no parallelization, an averaging-at-the-end scheme, and Spark-based algorithms. The results demonstrate substantially reduced training time and comparable or higher predictive performance relative to these baselines.

A concern is the theoretical component: it appears to overlap with prior work, specifically “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” which seems likely to be by the same authors. This prior work is not cited, and the paper does not clearly delineate what is new here. The authors should explicitly cite that paper and clarify the incremental theoretical contribution of the present work beyond it.
