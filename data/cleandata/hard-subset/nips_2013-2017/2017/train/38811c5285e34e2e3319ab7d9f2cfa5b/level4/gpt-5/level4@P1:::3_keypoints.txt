- Proposes the Radon machine: a black-box parallelization that aggregates weak hypotheses via Radon points instead of averaging.  
- Runs base learners on iid subsets and aggregates up a tree to amplify confidence doubly exponentially with tree height.  
- Theoretically yields polylogarithmic runtime (in target sample complexity) on quasi-polynomially many processors, at the cost of increased sample complexity.  
- Empirically shows substantial training-time reductions and competitive or improved AUC versus averaging and Spark MLlib baselines across datasets.  
- Situates work relative to averaging-based parallelization, distributed SGD, and NC-style parallel efficiency; discusses practical concerns (high dimensionality, limited processors).
