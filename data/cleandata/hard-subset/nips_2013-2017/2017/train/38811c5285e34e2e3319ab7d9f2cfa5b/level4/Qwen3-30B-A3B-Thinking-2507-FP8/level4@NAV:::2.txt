

The authors present a method for parallelizing learners that rely on specific assumptions, utilizing Radon points instead of the conventional averaging approach. The paper's key contribution lies in this alternative to the averaging baseline, distinguishing it from related work. Experimental results demonstrate reduced training time while maintaining or improving accuracy compared to three baselines: no parallelization, the averaging schema, and Spark-based algorithms.  
The theoretical foundation appears to overlap with the authors' prior work, "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which addresses similar concepts. This prior publication is not cited in the current manuscript, and the paper requires explicit clarification of how its theoretical contribution advances beyond this existing work.
