The paper proposes a method for parallelizing learning algorithms, under specified assumptions, by aggregating models via Radon points. The main contribution relative to prior work is the use of Radon-point aggregation instead of simple averaging, which serves as the baseline in the paper. The evaluation compares three settings: no parallelization, an averaging scheme, and Spark-based implementations. Empirical results show notable reductions in training time while maintaining comparable or superior accuracy to these alternatives.

However, the theoretical component appears closely related to results in “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” likely by the same authors. This prior work is not cited. The manuscript should clearly position its theoretical contributions with respect to that paper: specify what is new, what assumptions differ, and which guarantees are strengthened or generalized. Without this clarification and proper citation, it is difficult to assess the novelty and significance of the theoretical results.
