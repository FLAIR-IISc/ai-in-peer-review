- Proposes a Radon point–based combination scheme to parallelize a class of ML algorithms as an alternative to model averaging.  
- Evaluates the method against three baselines: non-parallelized, standard model averaging, and a Spark implementation.  
- Empirical results show substantial training speedups while achieving comparable or better accuracy than baselines.  
- The application of Radon points to parallel learning is novel and provides a principled alternative to simple averaging.  
- Experiments are well-designed and effectively demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Major weakness: the theoretical foundation appears closely related to prior work ("Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization") which is not cited.  
- Authors must cite and discuss the relationship to that prior work.  
- Authors must clarify whether their contribution is new theory, a novel application, empirical validation, or an extension of existing theory.  
- Without addressing the prior work, the paper’s claims of theoretical novelty are unsubstantiated.  
- Recommendation: thoroughly revise to situate the work in existing literature and clearly delineate the novel contributions.
