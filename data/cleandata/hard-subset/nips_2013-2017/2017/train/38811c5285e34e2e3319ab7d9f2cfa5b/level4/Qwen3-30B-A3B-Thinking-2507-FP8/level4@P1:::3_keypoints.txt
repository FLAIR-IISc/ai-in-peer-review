- Introduces the Radon machine, a novel parallelization scheme enabling effective parallel execution of many learning algorithms with theoretical guarantees.  
- Addresses the open question of performing consistent regularized risk minimization in polylogarithmic time on polynomially many processors (NC).  
- Theoretical result: reduces runtime from polynomial to polylogarithmic time on quasi-polynomially many processors.  
- Theoretical result: doubly-exponential improvement in confidence with the height of the aggregation tree.  
- Key insight: aggregate hypotheses using Radon points instead of averaging, yielding robustness when some hypotheses perform poorly.  
- Empirical result: achieves 80–700× speedups on multiple datasets (including SUSY with 5M instances) versus sequential implementations.  
- Empirical result: maintains comparable predictive performance to state-of-the-art Spark MLlib algorithms.  
- Empirical result: outperforms averaging-at-the-end baselines by ≥10% AUC while keeping similar runtime.  
- Practical comparison: often substantially faster or yields better predictive performance than Spark MLlib on multiple datasets.
