The authors propose a novel parallelisation scheme for machine learning algorithms based on Radon points, which simplifies the adaptation of learning algorithms to growing amounts of data and the need for accurate and confident predictions. The main contribution of the paper is the development of a parallelisation scheme that can be applied to a broad class of learning algorithms without requiring further mathematical derivations or dedicated code, while maintaining theoretical performance guarantees.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their parallelisation scheme, including a proof of its consistency and efficiency. They also present an empirical evaluation of their scheme, comparing it to state-of-the-art parallel machine learning algorithms from the Spark machine learning library and the natural baseline of averaging hypotheses instead of calculating their Radon point. The results show that the proposed parallelisation scheme achieves a substantial reduction in computation time while maintaining similar or higher predictive performance compared to the other algorithms.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the theoretical concepts and empirical results. The authors provide a detailed discussion of the limitations and potential extensions of their work, including the application of their scheme to high-dimensional models and non-linear models.

One potential criticism of the paper is that the theoretical part of the work may have been previously investigated in another paper, "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization", which is not cited in the current paper. The authors should clarify the contribution of their paper with respect to this previous work.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of parallel machine learning, providing a novel and effective parallelisation scheme that can be applied to a broad class of learning algorithms. The empirical results demonstrate the potential of the scheme in practical settings, and the authors provide a clear and thorough discussion of the limitations and potential extensions of their work.
