- Proposes a parallelization method using Radon points instead of conventional averaging for learners with particular assumptions.  
- Key contribution is the Radon-point aggregation as an alternative baseline to averaging.  
- Experiments report reduced training time while maintaining or improving accuracy versus three baselines: no parallelization, the averaging schema, and Spark-based algorithms.  
- Theoretical foundation appears to overlap with the authors' prior work "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which is not cited.  
- Paper needs to explicitly clarify how its theoretical contribution advances beyond that existing prior work.
