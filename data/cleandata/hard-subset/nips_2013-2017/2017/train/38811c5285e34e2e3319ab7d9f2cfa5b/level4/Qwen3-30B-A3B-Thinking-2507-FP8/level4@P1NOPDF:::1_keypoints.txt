- Proposes the "Radon machine": ensemble aggregation via Radon points instead of averaging.  
- Provides theoretical complexity analysis and a PAC bound for the Radon machine.  
- Achieves computational savings by partitioning data and parallel processing compared to a single base learner.  
- Offers a black-box method for combining weak learners that leverages Radon's theorem (geometric aggregation).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to low-dimensional datasets (e.g., ~18 features).  
- Unclear scalability to high-dimensional data; Radon point computation may be unstable or inefficient as dimensionality grows.  
- Suggests addressing high-dimensional concerns via experiments on >100-feature datasets, dimensionality reduction or randomized projections, or theoretical analysis in high dimensions.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution but narrow empirical validation limits practical relevance; handling high-dimensional data would significantly strengthen the work.
