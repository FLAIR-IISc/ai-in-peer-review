- Proposes a method for parallelizing learners using Radon points instead of averaging.
- Main novelty claimed is the use of Radon points; experiments compare to no parallelization, averaging, and Spark baselines.
- Experimental results show reduced learning time and comparable or improved accuracy, with encouraging speedups.
- Theoretical foundations appear similar to prior work (“Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization”), which is not cited.
- Paper must clarify its specific theoretical contribution relative to the prior work (extension, new perspective, or novel application).
- Needs a detailed discussion of the assumptions behind Radon point-based parallelization and sensitivity to assumption violations in practice.
- Should include a computational-complexity analysis of the proposed method versus baselines.
- Lacks analysis of scalability: performance behavior as number of learners and dataset size increase.
- Experimental setup and the Spark comparison are solid and valuable for benchmarking.
- Writing quality needs improvement (avoid "w.r.t.", fix grammatical issues); overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
