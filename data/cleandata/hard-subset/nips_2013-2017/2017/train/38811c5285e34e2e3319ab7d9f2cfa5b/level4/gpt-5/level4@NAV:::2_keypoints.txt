- Proposes a method for parallelizing learners (under certain assumptions) using Radon points.
- Main contribution relative to similar work is using Radon points instead of averaging (the baseline).
- Empirical comparisons are made against no parallelization, an averaging scheme, and Spark-based algorithms.
- Experiments report reduced training time and comparable or better accuracy than the other methods.
- The theoretical component appears to have been studied already in “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” likely by the same authors.
- That prior work is not cited; the paper should clarify what theoretical contribution is new beyond that result.
