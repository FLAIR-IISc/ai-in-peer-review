- Core contribution: a new framework for parallelizing ML that aggregates base hypotheses by replacing subsets with their Radon point instead of averaging.  
- Provides complexity guarantees for the method and empirical comparisons against Spark’s parallel algorithms and Weka linear models.  
- Strength: offers a largely black-box way to combine weak learners, serving as an alternative to bagging ensembles.  
- Theoretical contributions: analysis showing partitioning enables substantially more efficient parallel training and a PAC-style bound for the Radon machine.  
- Theoretical results appear strong.  
- Primary concern: unclear practical performance and scalability in high-dimensional settings—experiments focus on low-dimensional data (~18 features).  
- Recommendation: clarify or demonstrate how the method handles high-dimensional cases to strengthen the paper.
