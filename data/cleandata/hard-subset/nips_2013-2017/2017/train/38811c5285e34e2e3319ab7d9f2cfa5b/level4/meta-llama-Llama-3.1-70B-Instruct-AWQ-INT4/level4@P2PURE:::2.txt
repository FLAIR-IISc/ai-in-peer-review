The authors propose an approach to parallelizing learners that rely on certain assumptions, leveraging Radon points as a key innovation. The primary contribution of this paper, relative to similar works, lies in the use of Radon points as an alternative to the traditional averaging method, which serves as a baseline in the paper. The performance of the proposed approach is evaluated against three alternatives: no parallelization, averaging schema, and Spark-based algorithms, demonstrating reduced learning time and comparable or superior accuracy.

However, it appears that the theoretical foundations of this work have been previously explored in the paper "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization", potentially by the same authors. Unfortunately, this preceding work is not cited in the current paper, and it remains unclear what novel contributions this paper makes to the theoretical aspects of the approach.
