The paper introduces a scheme for parallelizing learning algorithms (under stated assumptions) using Radon points. The main contribution relative to related work is replacing simple averaging with Radon-point aggregation (with averaging used as a baseline in the study). The empirical evaluation compares against no parallelization, an averaging-based scheme, and Spark-based methods. The results show reduced training time and comparable or better accuracy than the alternatives.

The theoretical component appears to overlap with prior work titled “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” potentially by the same authors. This work is not cited, and the manuscript should clarify the relationship and specify the unique theoretical contributions of the present paper.
