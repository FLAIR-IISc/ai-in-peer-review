The authors propose a method for parallelizing learners – operating under certain assumptions – based on Radon points. The paper’s primary contribution lies in utilizing Radon points for parallelization, distinguishing it from prior work that typically relies on averaging as a baseline approach, as demonstrated within this paper. Performance is evaluated against “no parallelization,” an averaging scheme, and Spark-based algorithms, with experimental results showing reduced learning time and comparable or improved accuracy. 

However, the theoretical foundations appear to be closely related to the work presented in “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” potentially by the same authors. This prior work is not cited, and the paper needs to explicitly clarify its contribution with respect to that existing theory. What novel aspects does this work add to the theoretical understanding, or how does it build upon the previously established results?
