- Proposes parallelizing a class of learning algorithms using Radon points instead of standard model averaging.  
- Primary contribution is replacing model averaging with Radon points as the aggregation method.  
- Compares against baselines: no parallelization, simple averaging, and a Spark-based algorithm.  
- Experiments show reduced learning time and accuracy comparable to or better than baselines.  
- Major concern: theoretical foundation appears to overlap with prior work "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which is not cited.  
- Authors must address the omitted citation and clearly state the novel theoretical contributions relative to that prior work.
