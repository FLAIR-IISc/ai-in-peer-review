- Proposes parallelizing learning algorithms by aggregating locally trained models using Radon points.  
- Main contribution: use of Radon points as an alternative to conventional parameter-averaging.  
- Empirical evaluation compares the method to a non-parallel baseline, parameter-averaging, and an existing Spark-based algorithm.  
- Results: substantially reduced learning time while maintaining comparable or superior accuracy.  
- Major concern: theoretical contributions may overlap with prior work "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which is not cited.  
- Authors must clarify novelty relative to that prior work and explicitly cite and discuss it.  
- If the theoretical framework is already established, the contribution should be reframed as empirical implementation and extensive validation of Radon point-based parallelization.  
- A detailed comparison between this submission and the uncited related work is required for proper evaluation.
