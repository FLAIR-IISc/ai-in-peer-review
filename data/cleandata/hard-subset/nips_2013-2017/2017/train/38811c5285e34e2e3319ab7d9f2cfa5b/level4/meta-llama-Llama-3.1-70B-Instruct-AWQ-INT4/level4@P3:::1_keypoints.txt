- Proposes the Radon machine, a novel black-box parallelisation scheme for a broad class of learning algorithms that preserves theoretical performance guarantees.  
- Main contribution: using Radon points to aggregate hypotheses without extra derivations or dedicated code.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a clear motivation and explanation of the scheme.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including sample and runtime complexity.  
- Empirical evaluation compares against Spark ML algorithms and a hypothesis-averaging baseline.  
- Results show substantial reduction in computation time while maintaining comparable predictive performance.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by theory and experiments.  
- The approach is original in applying Radon points for hypothesis aggregation and relates clearly to prior parallel ML work.  
- Addresses a significant problem—efficient parallelisation of learning algorithms—with meaningful practical implications.  
- Major concern: experiments are limited to very low-dimensional datasets (18 features), limiting practical relevance.  
- Unclear how the method scales or performs on high-dimensional data; authors should clarify and demonstrate handling of such cases.  
- Recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing practical/high-dimensional concerns and providing additional experiments or explanations.
