The authors present a novel approach to parallelizing learners that rely on specific assumptions, leveraging Radon points as a key differentiator from existing methods. Notably, this work diverges from similar studies by employing Radon points in lieu of the traditional averaging baseline. The authors conduct a thorough comparison with three alternative methods: no parallelization, averaging schema, and Spark-based algorithms. Their experimental results demonstrate a reduction in learning time while achieving comparable or superior accuracy to state-of-the-art algorithms.

However, it appears that the theoretical foundation of this work may have been previously explored in the paper "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," potentially by the same authors. Unfortunately, this preceding work is not cited in the current manuscript, and the authors should clarify the specific contributions of this paper in relation to the earlier study.
