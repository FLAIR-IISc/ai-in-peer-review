

The authors propose a method for parallelizing learners under specific assumptions, utilizing Radon points instead of the conventional averaging approach. The key contribution lies in this alternative parallelization strategy, distinct from prior work that relies on averaging. The authors evaluate their approach against three baselines: no parallelization, the averaging schema, and Spark-based algorithms. Experimental results demonstrate reduced training time while maintaining comparable or superior accuracy relative to these baselines.  

However, the theoretical foundation for the parallelization framework appears to be covered in the authors' prior work, "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization." This prior publication is not cited in the current paper, and the authors must explicitly clarify how the present contribution advances or differs from this earlier theoretical treatment. The absence of citation and distinction constitutes a significant oversight that requires resolution.
