The paper "Effective Parallelisation for Machine Learning" presents a novel parallelisation scheme for a broad class of learning algorithms. The authors propose a method based on Radon points to effectively parallelise consistent and efficient learning algorithms, achieving a reduction in runtime from polynomial to polylogarithmic.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of the Radon machine, a parallelisation scheme that treats the underlying learning algorithm as a black-box. This approach allows for the parallelisation of learning algorithms without requiring further mathematical derivations or dedicated code. The Radon machine is shown to be effective in achieving the same confidence and error bounds as the sequential execution of the algorithm in much shorter time.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the Radon machine, including its sample and runtime complexity. They also present an empirical evaluation of the Radon machine, comparing its performance to state-of-the-art parallel machine learning algorithms from the Spark machine learning library and the natural baseline of averaging hypotheses instead of calculating their Radon point.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the theoretical and practical aspects of the Radon machine. The authors provide a detailed discussion of the related work and the contributions of their paper.
