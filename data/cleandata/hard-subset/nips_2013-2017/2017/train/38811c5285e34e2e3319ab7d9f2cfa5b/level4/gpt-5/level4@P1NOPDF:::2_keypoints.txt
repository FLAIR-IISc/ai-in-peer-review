- Proposes parallelizing learning algorithms using Radon points for model aggregation under certain assumptions.  
- Main contribution is using Radon-point aggregation instead of simple averaging (which serves as the baseline).  
- Empirical evaluation compares against no parallelization, an averaging scheme, and Spark-based algorithms.  
- Experiments show reduced training time and comparable or higher accuracy than the baselines.  
- The theoretical analysis appears to overlap with prior work “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” which is not cited.  
- Authors should explicitly cite the earlier paper and clarify what theoretical contributions here are new versus reused.
