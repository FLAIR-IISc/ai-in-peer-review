- Introduces the Radon Machine: partition data, train base learners in parallel, and iteratively aggregate hypotheses using Radon points instead of averaging.  
- Theoretical result: aggregation via Radon points reduces the probability of an ε-bad hypothesis doubly-exponentially with tree height.  
- Claims runtime can drop from polynomial to polylogarithmic under a quasi-polynomial number of processors, at the cost of increased sample complexity.  
- Empirical results show substantial speedups (80–700x) over sequential base learners with comparable predictive performance.  
- Competitive or superior runtime and accuracy compared to Spark MLlib and averaging-based aggregation baselines on several datasets.  
- Quality: technically sound with formal proofs (Proposition 2, Theorems 3 and 4) supporting the theoretical claims.  
- Quality: empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing against multiple relevant baselines on large-scale datasets.  
- Quality: authors candidly discuss costs and limitations, including sample complexity and processor requirements.  
- Quality: strong Discussion/Future Work section addressing high-dimensional data, non-linear models, and limited processor availability.
