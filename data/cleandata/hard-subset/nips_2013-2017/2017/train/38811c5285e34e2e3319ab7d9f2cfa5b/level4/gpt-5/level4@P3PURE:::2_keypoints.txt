- Proposes parallelizing learners under certain assumptions by aggregating models with Radon points instead of simple averaging.  
- Main claimed contribution is replacing averaging with Radon-point aggregation, with averaging used as a baseline.  
- Empirical evaluation compares to no parallelization, averaging, and Spark-based algorithms.  
- Experiments show reduced learning time and comparable or higher accuracy versus the alternatives.  
- Theoretical material appears to substantially overlap prior work “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” which is not cited.  
- Paper should explicitly cite that prior work and clearly delineate what is conceptually and technically new here, and spell out the specific theoretical contributions.
