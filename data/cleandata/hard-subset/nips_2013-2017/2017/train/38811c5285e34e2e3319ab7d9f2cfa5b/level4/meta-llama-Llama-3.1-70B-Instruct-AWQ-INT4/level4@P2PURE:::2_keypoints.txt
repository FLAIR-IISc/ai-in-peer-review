- Proposes parallelizing learners under certain assumptions using Radon points as the core innovation.
- Main contribution is replacing traditional averaging with Radon points as the aggregation method.
- Empirical evaluation vs no-parallelization, averaging, and Spark-based algorithms shows reduced training time and comparable or superior accuracy.
- Theoretical foundations appear to overlap with a prior paper "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which is not cited.
- Unclear what novel theoretical contributions this paper adds beyond the earlier work.
