

This paper introduces a novel parallelization algorithm for aggregating weak learners using Radon partitioning, supported by rigorous theoretical analysis and comprehensive empirical evaluation. The authors effectively demonstrate that their approach—termed "Radon machine"—achieves significant reductions in training time and improvements in AUC compared to established baselines across multiple standard benchmark datasets. The theoretical framework, which leverages Radon's theorem to enable efficient parallel aggregation, is both novel and well-motivated, providing clear justification for the algorithmic design. Empirical results robustly corroborate the theoretical claims, with consistent performance gains that are statistically meaningful (where reported) and practically relevant. The authors also proactively address key concerns in the Evaluation and Discussion sections, including scalability, computational overhead, and comparisons to alternative parallelization strategies, strengthening the paper's contribution. Overall, the work presents a compelling advancement in scalable ensemble learning, and I recommend acceptance.

**Specific Comments for Improvement:**
1.  **Typos & Formatting (Lines 51, 340-341, 377):** Correct the double "the" (Line 51), fix spacing errors ("machineachieved" → "machine achieved", "machineis" → "machine is"), and correct the citation "Long and servedio [22]" to "Long and Servedio [22]" to match the paper's citation style consistency.
2.  **Figure Readability (Figures 1-3):** The text labels within all figures are too small for clear readability, especially in printed versions. Increase font size for axis labels, legends, and data points to ensure legibility.
3.  **Figure 3 (Color/Grayscale Distinction):** Figure 3 relies on color to distinguish lines. Ensure the figure remains interpretable in grayscale (e.g., use distinct line styles or markers) by the final version.
4.  **Statistical Clarity (Lines 318-320):** Clarify the basis for the claim "the Radon machine outperforms the averaging baseline." Specify whether performance differences are based on average results across datasets, paired comparisons, or statistical tests (e.g., "outperforms the averaging baseline on average across datasets" or "achieves statistically significant gains via paired t-tests").
5.  **Terminology Precision (Line 324):** Avoid using "significant" without explicit reference to statistical significance. Replace with "substantial," "meaningful," or "statistically significant" (if supported by tests).
6.  **Figure 1 Context (Lines 341-349):** The marginal performance difference between the Radon machine and the AverageAtTheEnd method appears underemphasized. Since this result is presented in the text but absent from Figure 1, explicitly state *why* this comparison is less critical (e.g., "AverageAtTheEnd is a less competitive baseline due to its sequential nature") or include it in Figure 1 to provide a complete visual comparison of all key methods. This would strengthen the argument for the Radon machine's superiority.
7.  **Mathematical Consistency (Line 79):** The size of the math font in the specified equation appears smaller than surrounding text. Ensure consistent font sizing for all mathematical notation.

**Summary:** The paper makes a strong contribution with a well-motivated algorithm, solid theory, and convincing empirical validation. The minor issues noted above are purely editorial and do not detract from the paper's core technical merits. Addressing these points will significantly enhance the clarity and professionalism of the final manuscript. The work is clearly suitable for publication at NIPS.
