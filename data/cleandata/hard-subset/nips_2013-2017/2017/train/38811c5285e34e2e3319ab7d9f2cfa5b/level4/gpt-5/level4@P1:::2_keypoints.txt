- Proposes a parallelization scheme that aggregates models via Radon points (under certain assumptions) rather than simple averaging.  
- Main claimed contribution is the use of Radon-point aggregation over prior averaging-based parallelization methods.  
- Empirical evaluation vs three baselines (no parallelization, averaging-at-end, Spark-based algorithms) shows substantially reduced training time and comparable or better predictive performance.  
- The theoretical component appears to overlap with prior work (“Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization”), which is not cited.  
- Authors should explicitly cite that prior paper and clarify what new theoretical contributions this work provides beyond it.
