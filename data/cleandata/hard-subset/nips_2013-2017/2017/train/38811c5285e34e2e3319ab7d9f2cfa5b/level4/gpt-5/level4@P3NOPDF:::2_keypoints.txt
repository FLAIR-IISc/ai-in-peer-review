- Summary: Proposes parallelization that aggregates local models via Radon points instead of averaging, claiming reduced training time with equal/better accuracy.
- Novelty: Conceptually novel use of Radon-point aggregation, potentially more robust than arithmetic averaging under convexity assumptions.
- Generality: Method is claimed to apply to a family of randomized convex learners, suggesting broad applicability.
- Empirical gains: Experiments show wall-clock time reductions and matching/improved accuracy versus averaging and Spark baselines.
- Theory-practice link: Paper ties aggregation to geometric/convex analysis, attempting to bridge theory and practice.
- Missing citation/positioning: Fails to cite and clearly position relative to “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization”; novelty vs prior work is unclear.
- Ambiguous assumptions: Does not precisely state required properties of base learners (convexity, Lipschitz/smoothness, strong convexity, stability, randomness).
- Radon computation cost: Computational and numerical overhead of computing Radon points in high dimensions is not quantified or discussed.
- Baseline strength/fairness: Comparisons to Spark are under-specified; stronger distributed optimization baselines (PS-SGD, ADMM/CoCoA, variance-reduced methods) are missing.
- Scalability analysis lacking: No thorough strong/weak scaling curves or breakdown of communication vs computation overhead.
- Robustness concerns: Unclear performance under non-i.i.d. partitions, imbalanced partition sizes, or heterogeneous worker speeds.
- Statistical rigor: Experiments lack multiple runs, confidence intervals, and statistical significance reporting.
- Clarity/motivation: Needs better geometric intuition or small synthetic examples explaining why Radon beats averaging.
- Reproducibility: Code, scripts, datasets, preprocessing, and hyperparameter details are not specified for reproducibility.
- Requests for theory/algorithms: Clarify whether there are new theorems, tighter bounds, improved Radon computation, or communication-efficient variants.
- Complexity analysis requested: Provide complexity of Radon aggregation in terms of dimension d, number of models m, and aggregation rounds (including hierarchical recursion).
- Non-convex applicability: Must state applicability limits to convex problems and provide guidance or experiments for non-convex models if claimed.
- Ablation suggestions: Compare Radon vs mean vs geometric median/trimmed mean, vary partitions/sample sizes, and test synthetic distribution shifts.
- Related work expansion: Need comparisons to federated/distributed optimization literature (parameter averaging, ADMM, CoCoA, SVRG/SAGA, elastic averaging).
- Wall-clock breakdown and Spark details: Provide time breakdown (training/communication/aggregation) and clarify Spark baseline configuration, model/data-parallelism, and straggler handling.
- Overall assessment: Idea is clean and promising with encouraging results, but novelty, assumptions, computational overhead, and experimental thoroughness must be addressed—currently borderline.
