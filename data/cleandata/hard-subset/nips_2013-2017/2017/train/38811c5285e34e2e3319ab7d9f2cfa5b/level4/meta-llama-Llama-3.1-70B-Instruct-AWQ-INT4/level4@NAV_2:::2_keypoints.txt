- Proposes a novel parallelisation scheme for ML using Radon points that applies broadly without bespoke math or code changes.  
- Claims runtime reduction from polynomial to polylogarithmic using quasi-polynomially many processors.  
- Theoretical contribution: shows algorithms meeting certain conditions can be effectively parallelised via Radon points.  
- Provides guarantees on Radon point quality and shows ε-bad hypothesis probability is doubly exponentially reduced.  
- Empirical evaluation compares the scheme to Spark ML baselines and is comprehensive.  
- Key innovation: using Radon points instead of averaging for aggregation.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis supporting the method.  
- Strength: comprehensive and well-designed empirical comparisons.  
- Weakness: explanation of Radon point concept and its application lacks clarity.  
- Weakness: limited implementation details for the proposed parallelisation scheme.  
- Weakness: high mathematical prerequisite makes the paper hard for non-experts to follow.  
- Originality: introduces a distinct, new approach to parallelising learning algorithms.  
- Significance: method could be widely applicable and impactful for efficient parallel ML.  
- Quality: paper is well-written, clearly structured, and theoretically solid.  
- Acceptance pros: novel method, strong theory, and solid empirical results.  
- Acceptance cons: accessibility issues for non-experts and insufficient implementation/clarity details.
