## Review for NIPS 2017 - [Paper ID Redacted]

This paper presents a novel parallelization algorithm for aggregating weak learners, grounded in Radon partitioning. The authors provide a compelling theoretical analysis to motivate their approach and support it with empirical results demonstrating improvements in both training time and/or AUC performance compared to several baseline algorithms across multiple datasets. Importantly, the authors proactively address potential questions and concerns within the Evaluation and Discussion sections, strengthening the overall presentation. I recommend acceptance of this paper.

**Summary of Strengths:**

The core contribution of this work – a parallelizable aggregation scheme for weak learners based on Radon transforms – is both theoretically sound and practically effective. The theoretical analysis offers valuable insights into the properties of the proposed algorithm. The empirical validation is well-executed, showcasing consistent gains over competitive baselines. Furthermore, the authors’ anticipation and direct response to potential criticisms significantly enhance the paper’s clarity and credibility. This work has the potential to be impactful in scenarios where scalability and efficiency in boosting-style algorithms are critical.

**Specific Feedback & Suggestions:**

* **Minor Textual Issues:** Line 51 contains a typographical error ("the the" should be "the"). Lines 340-341 require spacing corrections ("machineachieved" and "machineis" should be “machine achieved” and “machine is”).

* **Presentation of Mathematical Notation:** Line 79: Please ensure the mathematical font size is consistent throughout the paper. The current size appears smaller and less readable than elsewhere.

* **Figure Readability:** Figures 1-3 would benefit from larger font sizes for the text within the images. This is particularly important for Figure 3, as the distinctions between elements may be difficult to discern if the paper is printed in grayscale. Consider including a color version of Figure 3 with the final submission to avoid any ambiguity.

* **Clarification of Results (Line 318-320):** The statement "the Radon machine outperforms the averaging baseline…" requires clarification. Is this conclusion based on an average of paired comparisons, or another statistical measure? Providing this detail will strengthen the claim.

* **Precise Language (Line 324):** While the observed performance improvements are promising, the use of the word "significant" should be reserved for instances where statistical significance has been formally established and reported. Consider rephrasing this statement to avoid potential misinterpretation.

* **Justification of Performance Differences (Lines 341-349):** The performance difference between the proposed method and the AverageAtTheEnd baseline appears relatively small. A more robust discussion justifying the merit of these gains would be valuable. Moreover, the results relating to AverageAtTheEnd don't seem to be visually represented in Figure 1. Including a comparison in Figure 1, or referencing a supplementary figure, could further advocate for the benefits of the proposed algorithm. Exploring *why* the performance difference is observed – even if subtle – will increase the paper’s impact.

* **Citation Consistency (Line 377):** The citation format in “Long and servedio [22]” is inconsistent with the other citations in the paper. Please standardize the citation style throughout.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a well-written and technically sound paper. The authors have made a valuable contribution to the field of boosting and parallel machine learning. The strengths of the work significantly outweigh the minor points raised above, and I confidently recommend acceptance to NIPS 2017. Addressing the specific feedback regarding figure readability, result clarification, and citation consistency will further refine the paper’s presentation.
