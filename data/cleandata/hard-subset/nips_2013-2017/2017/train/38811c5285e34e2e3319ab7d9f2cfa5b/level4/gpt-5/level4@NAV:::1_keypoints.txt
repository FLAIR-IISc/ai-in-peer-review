- Primary contribution: a new parallel ML framework that aggregates base learners by replacing subsets of hypotheses with their Radon point.  
- Empirical setup: complexity bounds provided and comparisons against parallel Spark algorithms using linear base models in Weka.  
- Methodary value: offers a black-box aggregation mechanism for weak learners, positioned as an alternative to bagging.  
- Theoretical strengths: analysis of the Radon machine’s complexity showing efficiency from partitioning samples versus training on the full dataset.  
- Provides a PAC bound for the Radon machine.  
- Overall theory is generally strong.  
- Major concern: practical performance on high-dimensional data is unclear.  
- Experiments focus on very low-dimensional datasets (~18 features), which may not represent modern ML problems.  
- Recommendation: clarify or demonstrate how the method handles high-dimensional data to strengthen the paper.
