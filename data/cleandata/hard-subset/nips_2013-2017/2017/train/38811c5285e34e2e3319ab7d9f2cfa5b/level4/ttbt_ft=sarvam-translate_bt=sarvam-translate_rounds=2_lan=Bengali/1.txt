The main contribution of this paper is a new framework for parallel machine learning algorithms. The idea is to combine base learners more effectively than simply averaging them. Specifically, subsets of estimates are replaced with their Radon points. They prove the limit of complexity of their methods and experimentally compare their results using parallel algorithms in Spark and base linear models in Weka.

The paper is quite interesting because it essentially proposes a black-box method for combining weak learners. This method seems like an alternative to bagging weak learners together. The theoretical contribution is an analysis of the complexity of Radon machines, where since the original samples can be divided into multiple parts, the resulting parallel machine is much more efficient than base learners working on all the original samples. They also show PAC bounds on Radon machines. The theoretical contributions seem quite good in the paper. A major concern was the practical aspect, especially for high-dimensional data. It is not clear how well the proposed method works here. The experiments seem to be for very low-dimensional datasets (18 features?), which is probably not realistic for most modern machine learning problems. The paper would have been stronger if the authors had emphasized how much high-dimensional data their method can handle.
