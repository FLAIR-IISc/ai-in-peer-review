The authors propose a method for parallelizing students based on Radon points (which assumes some assumptions). The contribution of this paper compared to other similar works is using Radon points instead of averaging (which was used as the basis in this paper). The effectiveness is compared with `no paralysis', `averaging schema', and `spark-based' algorithms. They experimentally show that learning time decreases compared to other algorithms and accuracy is almost the same or higher.

It seems that the theoretical part has already been explored in another paper: "Paralizing Randomized Convex Optimization", which may be by the same author. However, it is not quoted in the current paper and it is necessary to explain in detail what the contribution of the paper is for this part.
