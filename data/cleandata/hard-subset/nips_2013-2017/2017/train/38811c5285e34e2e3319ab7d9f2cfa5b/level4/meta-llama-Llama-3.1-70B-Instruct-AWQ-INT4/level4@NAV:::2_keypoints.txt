- Presents a novel parallelization approach for learners that uses Radon points as the main differentiator from existing methods.  
- Replaces traditional averaging baselines with Radon points to aggregate local models.  
- Compares thoroughly against three alternatives: no parallelization, averaging schema, and Spark-based algorithms.  
- Experiments show reduced learning time and comparable or better accuracy than state-of-the-art methods.  
- Raises concern that the theoretical basis may overlap with a prior paper ("Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization") that is not cited; authors should clarify differences and specific contributions.
