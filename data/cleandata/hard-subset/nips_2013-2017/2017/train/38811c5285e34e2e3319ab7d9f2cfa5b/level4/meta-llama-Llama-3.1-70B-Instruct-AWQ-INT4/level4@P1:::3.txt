The authors have presented a novel parallelisation scheme for aggregating weak learners based on Radon partitioning. They provide theoretical analysis to motivate the algorithm, as well as empirical results to support the theory. The theoretical analysis is interesting, and the empirical results demonstrate training time and/or AUC improvements over multiple baseline algorithms, on multiple datasets. The authors also preemptively and convincingly address several questions/concerns in the Evaluation and Discussion sections.

The paper contributes to the understanding of the extent to which efficient parallelisation of polynomial time learning algorithms is possible. It relates to Nick's Class of parallelisable decision problems and its variants. The proposed Radon machine is a black-box parallelisation scheme that can be applied to a wide range of machine learning algorithms without further mathematical derivations and without writing dedicated code.

The authors compare the Radon machine to state-of-the-art parallel machine learning algorithms from the Spark machine learning library, as well as the natural baseline of averaging hypotheses instead of calculating their Radon point. The empirical evaluation indicates that the Radon machine achieves a substantial speed-up or higher predictive performance than other parallel machine learning algorithms.

The paper also discusses the applicability of the Radon machine to high-dimensional models, non-linear models, and its potential use in distributed data streams. The authors mention that the scheme can be applied to more general randomized convex optimization algorithms with unknown and random target functions.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their proposed parallelisation scheme. However, there are a few minor issues with the text and figures.

Specific Notes
--------------

* Line 51: "the the" should be corrected to "the".
* Line 79: The font size seems small and hard to read. It would be better to use the same size as elsewhere in the paper.
* Figures 1-3: The text in the figure images is too small and should be increased in size.
* Figure 3: This figure may be hard to distinguish if printed in black and white. It would be better to use different colors or shading to make it clearer.
* Lines 318-320: The authors mention that the Radon machine outperforms the averaging baseline, but it is not clear if this is based on an average of paired comparisons. This should be clarified.
* Line 324: The use of the word "significant" is not referring to statistical significance results. It would be better to rephrase this sentence to avoid confusion.
* Lines 340-341: There are missing spaces in the text, which should be corrected.
* Lines 341-349: The difference in performance with the AverageAtTheEnd method does not seem meaningful. The authors should advocate more for their method and explain why this result is important.
* Line 377: The citation for Long and Servedio is inconsistent with other citations in the paper. It would be better to use a consistent citation style throughout.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed parallelisation scheme. The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the Radon machine, and the authors provide a good discussion of the potential applications and limitations of their method. I recommend acceptance.
