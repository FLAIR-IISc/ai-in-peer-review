- Main contribution: a novel framework (Radon machine) for parallel ML that combines base learners by replacing hypothesis subsets with their Radon point.
- Core idea: more effective combination than averaging by aggregating subsets of hypotheses via Radon points.
- Theoretical results: complexity bounds and a PAC bound for the Radon machine are provided.
- Efficiency claim: decomposition of the dataset enables the parallel machine to be significantly more efficient than a base learner on the full dataset.
- Empirical evaluation: compared against parallel algorithms in Spark and base linear models in Weka.
- Positioning: presented as a black-box alternative to bagging for constructing ensembles of weak learners.
- Major concern: unclear practical applicability and scalability to high-dimensional data.
- Experimental limitation: experiments focus on low-dimensional datasets (~18 features), which may not represent modern ML problems.
- Suggested improvement: the paper should theoretically discuss or empirically demonstrate performance on higher-dimensional data to strengthen its claims.
