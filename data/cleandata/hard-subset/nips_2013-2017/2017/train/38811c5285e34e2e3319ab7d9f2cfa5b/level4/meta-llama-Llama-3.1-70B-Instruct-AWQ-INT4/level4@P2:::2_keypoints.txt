- Proposes a novel parallelization scheme (Radon machine) for ML algorithms based on Radon points.  
- Shows algorithms meeting certain conditions can be parallelized to retain same confidence/error bounds as sequential execution.  
- Claims runtime reduction from polynomial to polylogarithmic, at the cost of increased data size and number of processing units.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis including sample and runtime complexity.  
- Empirically compares the Radon machine to Spark ML algorithms and an averaging-hypotheses baseline.  
- Experimental results: substantial computation-time reduction with comparable predictive performance to Spark algorithms.  
- Demonstrates applicability to regression and multi-class classification tasks.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of the scheme and theoretical underpinnings.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and provides fair comparisons.  
- Weakness: needs more context and background on the theoretical parts.  
- Weakness: should clarify contribution relative to prior work (e.g., "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization").  
- Weakness: lacks details on applying the Radon machine to non-linear models and high-dimensional data.
