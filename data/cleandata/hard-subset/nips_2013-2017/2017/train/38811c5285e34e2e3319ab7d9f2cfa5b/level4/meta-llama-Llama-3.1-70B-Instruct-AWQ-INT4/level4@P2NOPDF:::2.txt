The authors propose a novel approach to parallelizing learners that rely on specific assumptions, leveraging Radon points as a key component. A notable contribution of this paper, distinguishing it from similar works, is the utilization of Radon points as an alternative to the traditional averaging method, which is employed as a baseline approach for comparison. The performance of the proposed method is evaluated against three benchmarks: no parallelization, averaging schema, and Spark-based algorithms. Experimental results demonstrate a reduction in learning time while achieving comparable or superior accuracy to the other algorithms.

However, it appears that the theoretical foundation of this work may have been previously explored in another paper, "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," potentially by the same authors. Unfortunately, this preceding work is not cited in the current paper, leaving a gap in the explanation of the novel contributions made in the theoretical aspects of this research.
