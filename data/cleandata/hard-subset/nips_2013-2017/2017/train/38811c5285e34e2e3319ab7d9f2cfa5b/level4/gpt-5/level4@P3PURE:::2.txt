This paper proposes an approach to parallelizing learners—under certain assumptions—using Radon points for model aggregation. Relative to prior work, the key contribution is to replace simple averaging with Radon-point aggregation; the paper uses averaging as a baseline for comparison. The empirical evaluation contrasts the proposed method against no parallelization, an averaging scheme, and Spark-based algorithms. The experiments indicate reduced learning time and comparable or higher accuracy than these alternatives.

However, the theoretical component appears to overlap substantially with prior work, specifically “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” likely by the same authors. This prior work is not cited in the current submission. The paper should explicitly cite it and clearly delineate what is new here—both conceptually and technically—relative to that earlier theory, and elaborate on the specific contributions of this paper on the theoretical side.
