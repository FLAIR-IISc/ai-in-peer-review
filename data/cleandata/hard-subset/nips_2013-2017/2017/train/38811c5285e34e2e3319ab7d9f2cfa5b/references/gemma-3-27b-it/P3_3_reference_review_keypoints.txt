- Overall assessment: Recommends acceptance with minor revisions; theory is sound and approach is practically promising but paper is dense and missing some practical discussion.  
- Core idea: Proposes the "Radon machine," using Radon point aggregation to parallelize regularized risk minimization with provable polylogarithmic runtime gains.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Maintains sequential algorithm's confidence and error bounds and offers doubly-exponential improvement in confidence (Theorem 3).  
- Positioning: Properly situates work relative to averaging-based methods, Rademacher complexity, convex optimization, and relevant complexity classes.  
- Strength — Strong theoretical foundation: Rigorous analysis with concrete bounds on sample complexity and runtime.  
- Strength — Practical relevance: Empirical results show significant speedups on a Spark cluster and favorable comparison to Spark MLlib.  
- Strength — Black-box applicability: Can wrap existing learning algorithms as black boxes, increasing versatility and ease of implementation.  
- Strength — Novelty: Novel application of Radon points for aggregation, offering a provably superior convergence rate to averaging.  
- Strength — Clear problem definition: Precisely defines effective parallelization and related success criteria.  
- Weakness — Density & accessibility: Paper is mathematically dense and may be hard for a broader audience to follow.  
- Weakness — Communication costs under-discussed: Needs deeper analysis of communication overheads in distributed settings.  
- Weakness — Radon point computation: Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity for computing Radon points in high dimensions.  
- Weakness — Non-convex applicability: Focuses on convex problems and does not discuss extensions or limitations for non-convex models.  
- Weakness — Limited experiments: Evaluation is narrow; should include high-dimensional data, non-linear models, and varied cluster setups.  
- Weakness — "Quasi-polynomial" vagueness: Frequent use of the term without precise dependence on parameters (e.g., 1/ε, log(1/Δ)).  
- Minor issues: Contains small typos and formatting errors that should be corrected.  
- Recommendations: Improve clarity with intuitive explanations/visuals, expand discussion on communication and Radon computation, explore non-convex extensions, broaden experiments, clarify "quasi-polynomial" terms, and fix minor edits.
