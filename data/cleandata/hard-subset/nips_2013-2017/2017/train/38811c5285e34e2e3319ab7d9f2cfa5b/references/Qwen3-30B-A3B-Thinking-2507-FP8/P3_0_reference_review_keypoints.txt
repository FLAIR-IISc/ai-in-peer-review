- Introduces the "Radon Machine": split data, run base learners in parallel, iteratively aggregate via Radon points to get polylogarithmic runtime and doubly exponential error reduction.  
- Empirical results claim 80–700× speedups on 150 processors with predictive performance comparable to Spark MLlib.

- Strength: strong theoretical contribution addressing an open problem, with rigorous proofs and doubly exponential error reduction.  
- Strength: black-box scheme that requires no changes to base learning algorithms—practically appealing.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical validation against a baseline and Spark MLlib.  
- Strength: connects the parallelization problem to complexity theory (Nick’s Class/NC).

- Weakness: relies on low-dimensional hypothesis spaces (Radon number r = d+2), making it impractical for high-dimensional real-world data.  
- Weakness: proposed fixes for high-dimensional data (random projections, low-rank approximations) are underdeveloped.  
- Weakness: no analysis or measurements of communication complexity; claims of low communication are unsubstantiated.  
- Weakness: minimal implementation details on Spark integration and practical challenges; repo link alone is insufficient.  
- Weakness: lacks concrete examples or analysis for non-linear models and how the method performs there.  
- Weakness: insufficient differentiation from existing parallel methods (e.g., SGD, distributed optimization).

- Overall: solid theoretical advance but practical significance limited by dimensionality and missing communication/implementation analyses.  
- Requested revisions: analyze communication complexity, justify or mitigate dimensionality assumption, provide concrete high-dimensional/non-linear examples, and add implementation details.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions contingent on addressing dimensionality and communication concerns.
