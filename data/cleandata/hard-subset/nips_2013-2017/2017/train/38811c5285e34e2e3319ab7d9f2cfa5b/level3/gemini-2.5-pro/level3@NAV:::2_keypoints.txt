- Proposes the "Radon Machine," a black-box parallelization method that partitions data, trains base learners in parallel, and aggregates hypotheses using Radon points.  
- Builds on prior parallel learning via averaging but claims Radon-point aggregation gives doubly-exponential confidence improvement per aggregation step.  
- Theoretical aim is polylogarithmic runtime on quasi-polynomially many processors, connecting to complexity concepts like Nick's Class.  
- Strength: elegant, broadly applicable approach treating the base learner as a black box.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation comparing to no parallelization, simple averaging, and Spark MLlib.  
- Strength: impressive empirical results, showing up to 700x speed-ups with comparable or improved predictive accuracy.  
- Main weakness: originality concerns—aggregation-to-boost-confidence theory resembles Agarwal & Duchi (COLT 2011), which is not cited.  
- Omission of Agarwal & Duchi undermines the claimed novelty of the theoretical results.  
- Recommendation: major revision required to discuss Agarwal & Duchi and clearly delineate the novel theoretical contributions.  
- Conclusion: with a convincing clarification of theoretical novelty, the paper would be a strong candidate for acceptance.
