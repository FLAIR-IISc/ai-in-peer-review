This paper proposes a novel parallelisation scheme, called the Radon machine, for a broad class of learning algorithms. The main theoretical contribution is to show that algorithms satisfying certain conditions can be parallelised effectively, achieving the same confidence and error bounds as the sequential execution of that algorithm in much shorter time. The parallelisation scheme is based on the concept of Radon points, which are used to aggregate hypotheses in a way that increases the confidence in the aggregate doubly-exponentially with the height of the aggregation tree.

The paper is well-motivated and clearly written, with a good introduction to the problem and the proposed solution. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the theoretical properties of the Radon machine, including its sample and runtime complexity. The empirical evaluation of the Radon machine is also comprehensive, comparing its performance to state-of-the-art parallel machine learning algorithms from the Spark machine learning library.

* The proposed parallelisation scheme is novel and has a strong theoretical foundation.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the theoretical properties of the Radon machine.
