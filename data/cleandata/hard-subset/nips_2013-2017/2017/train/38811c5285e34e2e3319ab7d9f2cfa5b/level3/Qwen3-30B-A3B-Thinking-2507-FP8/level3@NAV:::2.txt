

This paper proposes a parallelization scheme called the "Radon machine" that uses Radon points for aggregating hypotheses instead of standard averaging. The authors claim this approach reduces learning time from polynomial to polylogarithmic while maintaining theoretical guarantees. The method is applied to regularized risk minimization problems and evaluated against sequential learning, averaging-based parallelization, and Spark-based algorithms.

The paper makes a technically sound contribution with clear theoretical analysis (Theorems 2-4) showing how Radon points can doubly-exponentially increase confidence with aggregation tree height. The empirical evaluation demonstrates impressive speedups (80-700x) with comparable or better accuracy than baselines. The black-box nature of the approach is a significant practical advantage.

However, the paper's originality claim is problematic. The core theoretical concept of using Radon points for aggregation appears to be previously studied in "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization" (by Vitter and Lin, 1985), which is not cited. The authors must clarify how their work differs from this foundational paper. The paper claims to address an "open question" about efficient parallelization of machine learning, but this claim is weakened by the lack of proper contextualization of prior work.

The quality is strong in empirical evaluation and practical implementation, though the theoretical novelty requires significant clarification. The paper is clearly written with good organization, though the relation to prior work needs substantial improvement. The significance is high given the practical speedups demonstrated, but the theoretical contribution is diminished by the unaddressed prior work.

The authors should:
1) Cite and discuss Vitter and Lin's work on parallel randomized convex optimization
2) Explicitly clarify how their Radon point approach differs from prior applications
3) Better contextualize their contribution within the existing literature on parallel learning

Without these revisions, the paper risks being seen as incremental rather than novel. The empirical results are compelling, but the theoretical contribution must be properly positioned to justify the significance claim. The paper has strong potential but needs to address these critical gaps in the literature review.
