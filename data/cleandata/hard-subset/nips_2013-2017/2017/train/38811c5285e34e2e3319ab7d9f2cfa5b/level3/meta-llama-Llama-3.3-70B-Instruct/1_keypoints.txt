- Proposes a novel unbiased ELBO estimator with zero variance when the variational approximation is exact.  
- Removes the score-function term from the reparameterized gradient to reduce variance.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of the estimator’s properties.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on MNIST and Omniglot.  
- Well-written with clear motivation and derivation.  
- Thorough comparison to existing methods and detailed related-work discussion.  
- Empirically outperforms the standard reparameterized gradient, especially for complex approximate posteriors.  
- Strengths: clear presentation, rigorous analysis, and strong experimental results.  
- Limitation: estimator is specific to variational inference and may not generalize to other stochastic optimization settings.  
- Limitation: no detailed analysis of computational cost.  
- Limitation: potential lack of robustness to noise/outliers and relies on the (often unmet) assumption of exact variational approximation.  
- Paper meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Recommendation: accept, with encouragement to address noted limitations in future work.
