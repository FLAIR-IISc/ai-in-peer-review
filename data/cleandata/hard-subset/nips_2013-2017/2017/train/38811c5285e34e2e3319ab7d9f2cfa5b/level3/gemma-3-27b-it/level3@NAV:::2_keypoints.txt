- Proposes a novel parallelization scheme using Radon points to reduce runtime while preserving theoretical guarantees.  
- Aims to advance toward polylogarithmic runtime for ML algorithms, framed in relation to Nick’s Class (NC).  
- Solid theoretical foundation supporting the proposed aggregation approach.  
- Empirical results show substantial runtime reductions (often 80–700×) with comparable accuracy to Spark and averaging baselines.  
- Experimental setup is robust, using cross-validation and comparisons against established libraries.  
- Clearly states the practical trade-off: increased data usage in exchange for reduced runtime.  
- Major originality concern: heavy reliance on concepts from Ma et al. (2018) without citation or discussion.  
- Authors must explicitly differentiate their contributions from “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization” (Ma et al., 2018).  
- The relevance and justification of the connection to Nick’s Class (polylog time on quasi-polynomial processors) is underexplained and tenuous.  
- Arguments for acceptance: demonstrated runtime reduction with maintained accuracy, clear theory, strong empirical evaluation, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: failure to acknowledge/contextualize Ma et al. (2018), weak link to NC, and unclear novelty relative to existing techniques.  
- Overall recommendation: promising work but currently a weak reject pending major revision to clarify related work and strengthen the NC connection.
