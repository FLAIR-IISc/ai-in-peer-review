

This paper presents a novel parallelization scheme called the Radon machine that effectively parallelizes a broad class of learning algorithms through Radon partitioning. The approach aggregates hypotheses in a tree structure where each inner node computes a Radon point of its children's hypotheses, enabling doubly-exponential confidence improvement with tree height. The authors establish that this parallelization reduces runtime from polynomial to polylogarithmic while maintaining theoretical guarantees, representing a significant step toward answering the open question of efficient parallel machine learning in Nick's Class.

The theoretical contribution is strong, with well-motivated proofs showing the Radon machine achieves polylogarithmic runtime on quasi-polynomially many processors. The paper clearly distinguishes its approach from averaging-based parallelizations and provides rigorous bounds on error probability. Empirically, the Radon machine demonstrates substantial speedups (80-700x) compared to sequential learning while maintaining comparable AUC performance across multiple datasets. It outperforms averaging-at-the-end baselines by at least 10% in AUC and achieves similar or better performance than Spark MLlib algorithms while requiring significantly less runtime.

The paper excels in clarity, with a well-organized structure that effectively connects the theoretical results to practical implementation. The authors proactively address potential concerns regarding applicability to high-dimensional models and non-linear learning, and the empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing.

Minor issues include a typo ("the the" on Line 51), inconsistent math font formatting (Line 79), and readability concerns for figures (especially Figure 3 in black and white). The presentation of results comparing to AverageAtTheEnd (Lines 341-349) could be clearer, and one citation style inconsistency (Line 377) needs correction.

The paper scores highly across all four evaluation criteria: it's technically sound with strong theoretical and empirical support (Quality); clearly written with excellent organization (Clarity); offers a novel combination of Radon points with parallel learning (Originality); and addresses a significant open problem in machine learning with practical implications (Significance). I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the formatting and presentation issues noted above.
