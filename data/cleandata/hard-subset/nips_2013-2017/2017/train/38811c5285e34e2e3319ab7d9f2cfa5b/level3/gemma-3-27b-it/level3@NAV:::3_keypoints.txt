- Proposes a novel parallelisation scheme for ML using Radon partitioning to aggregate hypotheses from random data subsets.  
- Core idea: apply base learner to random subsets and iteratively aggregate hypotheses via Radon points for parallelisation.  
- Theoretical contribution: formalisation and analysis showing potential runtime reduction to polylogarithmic time using quasi-polynomial processors.  
- Motivated by need for confident predictions in critical applications and limits of existing parallelisation methods.  
- Empirical evaluation, though limited by base-learner scalability, shows substantial speedups over sequential execution.  
- Empirical results are competitive with existing Spark-based parallel implementations.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and outline future research directions.  
- Minor presentation issues: typos (e.g., line 51 “the the”; lines 340–341 “machineachieved”, “machineis.”).  
- Formatting/readability issues: small math font on line 79 and small figure fonts in Figures 1–3; Figure 3 may be unclear in grayscale.  
- Ambiguity in results (Lines 318–320): unclear if Radon machine outperforms averaging via paired comparisons or by average.  
- Use of “significant” on line 324 should be avoided unless statistical significance is established.  
- Results on lines 341–349 show only minor differences with AverageAtTheEnd and should be included in Figure 1 or justified more robustly.  
- Citation style inconsistency noted (line 377: “Long and servedio [22]”).  
- Recommendation: accept — strengths (novel theory and promising empirical support) outweigh minor issues; represents valuable contribution.
