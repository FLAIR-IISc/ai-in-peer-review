This paper proposes a novel parallelization scheme for machine learning algorithms using Radon points. The authors claim that their method can effectively parallelize a broad class of learning algorithms without requiring further mathematical derivations or dedicated code, while maintaining theoretical performance guarantees.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem of parallelizing machine learning algorithms and the limitations of existing approaches. They also provide a detailed explanation of their proposed method, including the theoretical foundations and empirical evaluation.

The main contribution of the paper is the use of Radon points instead of standard averaging as the main approach for parallelizing learning algorithms. The authors show that their method can reduce the learning time and achieve similar or improved accuracy compared to baselines.

The empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing, with experiments on multiple datasets and comparisons to state-of-the-art parallel machine learning algorithms. The results show that the proposed method can achieve significant speedups while maintaining similar predictive performance.

However, there are some concerns regarding the originality of the paper. The theoretical part of the paper appears to be previously studied in "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization" (not cited), and the authors need to clarify their original contribution and relation to that work.
