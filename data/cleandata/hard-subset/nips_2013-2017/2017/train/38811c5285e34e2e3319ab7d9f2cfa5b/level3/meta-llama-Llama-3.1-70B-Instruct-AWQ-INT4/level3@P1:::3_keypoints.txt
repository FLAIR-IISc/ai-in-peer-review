- Proposes a novel parallelisation scheme for ML based on Radon partitioning.  
- Provides a theoretical framework for parallelising consistent, efficient regularised risk minimisation algorithms.  
- Shows theoretical runtime reduction from polynomial to polylogarithmic time using quasi-polynomially many processors.  
- Empirically compares the scheme to Spark ML algorithms and an averaging-hypotheses baseline.  
- Empirical results: substantial computation time reduction while maintaining comparable predictive performance.  
- Paper is well-written with thorough, well-motivated theoretical analysis.  
- Authors address potential concerns and limitations convincingly.  
- Minor issues: typos and formatting problems.  
- Some figures need improved readability.  
- Needs clearer explanation/clarification on comparisons (e.g., Radon machine vs averaging baseline).  
- Original contribution and theoretical analysis are novel and significant.  
- Work addresses an important problem (efficient ML parallelisation) with potential major impact.  
- Overall paper quality is high with sound theory and convincing experiments.  
- Recommendation: accept, despite minor presentation issues.
