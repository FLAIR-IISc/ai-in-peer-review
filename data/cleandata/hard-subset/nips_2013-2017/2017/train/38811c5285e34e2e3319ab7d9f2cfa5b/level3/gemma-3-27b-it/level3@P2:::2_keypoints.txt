- Proposes a novel parallelisation scheme (“Radon machine”) that aggregates hypotheses via Radon points to achieve polylogarithmic runtime using many processors at the cost of increased sample complexity.  
- Frames the problem in regularised risk minimisation and connects parallelisation challenges to NC (Nick’s Class).  
- Black-box approach: can wrap existing learning algorithms without modifying them.  
- Theoretical analysis gives bounds relating the base learner’s sample and runtime complexity to the parallelised algorithm.  
- Empirical results show substantial runtime speedups over sequential learning and Spark MLlib while maintaining competitive or better accuracy.  
- Builds on prior work in parallel learning and randomized convex optimization and uses Radon number/quasi-convexity for rigor.  
- Paper is generally well-written with a clear Algorithm 1 overview.  
- Novelty is plausible but needs clarification, particularly relative to closely related methods.  
- Major omission: fails to cite/discuss Allen-Zhu et al. (2017) “Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization,” which directly addresses similar problems and aggregation schemes.  
- Without addressing Allen-Zhu et al., claims of originality are weakened and must be explicitly positioned relative to that work.  
- Computational cost of calculating Radon points (r^3) may be a bottleneck in high-dimensional hypothesis spaces and needs deeper analysis or approximations.  
- Assumes availability of many processors (rh); more discussion needed on performance when fewer processors are available (de-parallelisation effects).  
- Empirical evaluation is limited mainly to linear models; extending to non-linear models or providing preliminary results would strengthen generality claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach (if related work is addressed), solid theoretical foundation, practical runtime benefits, and wide applicability due to black-box design.  
- Arguments against acceptance: critical related-work omission, potential scalability limits from Radon computation, and limited experimental scope.  
- Overall recommendation: weak reject pending major revision to address related work, practical Radon computation concerns, and broader experiments.  
- Reviewer confidence: 6/10.
