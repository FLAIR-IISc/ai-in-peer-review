- Proposes a novel parallelisation scheme that aggregates hypotheses from random data subsets using Radon points.  
- Radon machine (Alg.1) parallelises consistent and efficient learners, claiming runtime reduction from polynomial to polylogarithmic.  
- Provides theoretical analysis with a bound on the probability of an ε-bad hypothesis that is doubly exponentially reduced in parameter h.  
- Empirical evaluation compares Radon machine to Spark ML algorithms and to simple averaging of hypotheses.  
- Results show substantial computation-time reduction with similar predictive performance to Spark baselines.  
- Demonstrates general applicability by applying the scheme to regression and multi-class classification.  
- Main technical contribution: using Radon points instead of averaging for more robust and efficient aggregation.  
- Weakness: theoretical results appear to overlap with prior work ("Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization") which is not cited.  
- Weakness: needs clearer statement of original contribution and relation to the prior work.  
- Weakness: lacks more detailed comparisons with other parallelisation schemes (e.g., stochastic gradient descent).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel aggregation idea, solid theoretical guarantees, and convincing empirical speedups across tasks.  
- Arguments against acceptance: apparent overlap with prior work and insufficient comparative evaluation; these issues should be resolved.  
- Overall: significant contribution but requires clarification of novelty and additional comparative experiments.
