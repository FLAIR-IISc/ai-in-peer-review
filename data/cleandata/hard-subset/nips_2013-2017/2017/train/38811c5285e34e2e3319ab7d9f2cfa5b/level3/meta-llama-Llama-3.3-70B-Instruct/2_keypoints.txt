- Proposes a novel unbiased ELBO estimator that has zero variance when the variational approximation is exact.  
- Provides a simple, general implementation requiring a single change to the computation graph compatible with standard automatic-differentiation tools.  
- Generalizes the gradient estimator to mixture and importance-weighted lower bounds.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized with a helpful introduction motivating the method.  
- Technical sections are detailed and provide a thorough analysis of the estimator.  
- Strength: estimator is unbiased and attains zero variance in the exact-approximation case, improving over existing estimators.  
- Strength: method is simple to implement and easy to integrate into existing software.  
- Strength: authors analyze estimator behavior across scenarios and relate it to existing methods.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed empirical and computational comparisons to existing methods, especially on efficiency and scalability.  
- Weakness: some notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to readers without strong variational-inference backgrounds.  
- Weakness: paper could use more discussion of potential applications and practical implications.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and technical soundness.  
- Recommendation: accept, subject to addressing comparisons, notation clarity, and discussion of applications.
