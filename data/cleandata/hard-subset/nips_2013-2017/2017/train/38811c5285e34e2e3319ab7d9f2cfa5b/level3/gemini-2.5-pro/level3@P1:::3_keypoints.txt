- Paper proposes the "Radon Machine": partition data, train base learners in parallel, iteratively aggregate hypotheses via Radon points in a tree to get doubly-exponentially increased confidence and polylogarithmic runtime (at cost of many processors and increased sample complexity); strong empirical speedups and accuracy.  
- Originality/significance: novel application of iterated Radon points for aggregation and a meaningful connection to NC parallelizability.  
- Technical quality: rigorous theoretical analysis (Theorems 3 and 4) showing doubly-exponential error reduction and complexity claims.  
- Empirical evaluation: comprehensive benchmarks vs sequential base learners, averaging baseline, and Spark MLlib showing large speedups (80x–700x) and competitive or better AUC.  
- Clarity and discussion: well-written, anticipates limitations (high-dimensional/nonlinear models, limited processors, communication) and practical implications.  
- Presentation/readability issues: small figure fonts, Figure 3 hard to read in B/W, and inconsistent math font sizing.  
- Empirical-claim clarity: need to explain how the "at least 10%" AUC improvement is computed and avoid informal use of "significant" without statistical support.  
- Results presentation: multiclass experiment gains are minor and should be better integrated into figures or more strongly justified.  
- Minor typos/formatting: small errors (e.g., "the the", missing spaces, inconsistent citation styling) that should be fixed.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — novel, theoretically sound, empirically validated; mostly minor presentation fixes needed.
