- Main contribution: proposes a novel parallelization scheme for ML algorithms using Radon points.  
- Theoretical analysis: provides thorough analysis of sample and runtime complexity.  
- Empirical evaluation: strong experiments comparing Radon machine to Spark ML algorithms showing large speedups with similar predictive performance.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and explains technical details and the Radon machine clearly.  
- Novelty: using Radon points for parallelization is a novel approach compared to averaging-based methods.  
- Significance: the method is potentially widely applicable and makes a significant contribution to parallel ML.  
- Concern: theoretical aspects may overlap with prior work ("Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization") which is not cited.  
- Requested clarification: authors should clarify the original contribution and relation to the alleged prior work.  
- Suggested improvements: provide a more detailed comparison to other parallel ML algorithms.  
- Suggested improvements: include additional empirical evaluations to further demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel scheme, solid theoretical results, thorough empirical validation, and practical significance.  
- Rejection arguments: potential prior-art overlap and unclear distinction from earlier theoretical work.
