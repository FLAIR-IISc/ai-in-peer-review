- Proposes the Radon machine: a novel parallelisation scheme that aggregates hypotheses via Radon points to boost confidence doubly-exponentially with aggregation tree height.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis of sample and runtime complexity, showing polylogarithmic runtime on quasi-polynomially many processors.  
- Empirically compares Radon machine to Spark ML algorithms and to simple averaging, demonstrating large computation time reductions.  
- Shows Radon machine maintains similar predictive performance to Spark algorithms and outperforms averaging-at-the-end.  
- Discusses limitations: applicability to high-dimensional and non-linear models is restricted and needs extensions.  
- Strength: introduces a novel, effective method for parallelising learning algorithms with solid theory.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical treatment clarifying sample/runtime behavior.  
- Strength: strong empirical evaluation against state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Strength: authors acknowledge limitations and propose possible extensions.  
- Weakness: unclear positioning of original contribution relative to prior work on parallelising randomized convex optimization.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion of practical implications, real-world applications, and limitations.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation could include more datasets and additional parallel ML baselines.  
- Originality: using Radon points for hypothesis aggregation is a novel and well-analyzed idea.  
- Significance: contributes meaningfully to parallel ML, especially for critical applications needing confident predictions.
