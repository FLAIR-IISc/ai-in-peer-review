**Paper Review: Effective Parallelisation for Machine Learning**

**Summary**

This paper presents a novel parallelisation scheme, called the Radon machine, for a broad class of learning algorithms. The scheme simplifies the adaptation of learning algorithms to growing amounts of data and maintains theoretical performance guarantees. The Radon machine can reduce the runtime of many learning algorithms to polylogarithmic time on quasi-polynomially many processing units, making it a significant step towards answering an open question on the efficient parallelisation of machine learning algorithms.

**Strengths**

1. **Theoretical contribution**: The paper provides a well-motivated and interesting theoretical contribution, showing that algorithms satisfying certain conditions can be parallelised effectively.
2. **Empirical results**: The empirical evaluation confirms the potential of the Radon machine, demonstrating a substantial reduction in computation time and comparable predictive performance to state-of-the-art parallel machine learning algorithms.
3. **Originality**: The paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, using Radon points to aggregate hypotheses in a parallelisation scheme.
4. **Clarity**: The paper is well-written and clearly explains the concepts and ideas.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Formatting issues**: There are some formatting issues, such as smaller math font and hard-to-read figure text, which need to be addressed.
2. **Typos and minor errors**: There are a few typos and minor errors, such as "the the" instead of "the" and missing spaces.
3. **Clarification needed**: Some parts of the paper, such as the explanation of the Radon machine's outperforming the averaging baseline, could be clarified.
4. **Language**: The paper could benefit from more precise language, avoiding phrases like "significant" unless referring to statistical significance.

**Recommendation**

Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The authors should address the formatting issues, typos, and minor errors, and provide clarification where needed.

**Pro and Con Acceptance**

**Pro:**

* The paper provides a significant theoretical contribution to the field of machine learning.
* The empirical evaluation demonstrates the potential of the Radon machine.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the concepts and ideas.

**Con:**

* There are some formatting issues and minor errors that need to be addressed.
* Some parts of the paper could be clarified.

Overall, the paper's strengths outweigh its weaknesses, and with minor revisions, it has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field of machine learning.
