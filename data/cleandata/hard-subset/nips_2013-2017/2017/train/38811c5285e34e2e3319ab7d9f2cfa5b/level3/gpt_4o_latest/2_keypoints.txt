- Novelty: Uses Radon points for aggregation instead of averaging, yielding doubly-exponential confidence improvement with aggregation-tree height.  
- Theoretical contributions: Proves the Radon machine attains polylogarithmic runtime on quasi-polynomial processors with guarantees on hypothesis quality, addressing an NC-related open question.  
- Empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments show up to 700x speed-ups and comparable or better accuracy versus Spark and averaging baselines.  
- Generality: Black-box parallelization applicable to many learning algorithms without modifying the underlying methods.  
- Related-work overlap: The theory overlaps with "Parallelizing Randomized Convex Optimization," which is not cited; authors must clarify differences and attribution.  
- Scalability concerns: Reliance on quasi-polynomial processor counts may limit practical use; deparallelization discussion should be expanded.  
- High-dimensional data limitation: Method needs preprocessing (random projections or low-rank approximations) for high-dimensional or non-linear models and should explicitly acknowledge this.  
- Clarity: Some proofs and theoretical derivations are dense and would benefit from clearer explanations or examples.  
- Argument for acceptance: Addresses a fundamental parallel ML problem with an innovative aggregation method and strong theoretical plus empirical results.  
- Argument against acceptance: Unclear originality due to prior overlap and insufficient treatment of scalability and high-dimensional applicability.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on resolving related-work overlap and expanding discussion on scalability and high-dimensional data.
