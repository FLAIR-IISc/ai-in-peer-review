- Presents a novel, generally applicable parallelization scheme (Radon machine) that reduces runtime to polylogarithmic time at the cost of increased sample complexity.  
- Treats base learning algorithms as black boxes and aggregates hypotheses using Radon points.  
- Strong theoretical analysis with guarantees on runtime and sample complexity; connects to parallel complexity class NC.  
- Clear motivation and formulation within regularized risk minimization; definitions of consistency and efficiency are provided.  
- Algorithmic description of the Radon machine is clear and accompanied by theoretical proofs.  
- Empirical evaluation shows substantial speedups and comparable performance versus standard algorithms and Spark MLlib.  
- Novelty: general applicability without algorithm-specific derivations.  
- Strength: compelling empirical results and solid theoretical backing.  
- Weakness: brief treatment of communication complexity, a key practical concern in distributed systems.  
- Weakness: handling of high-dimensional data (random projections, low-rank approximations) is underdeveloped and somewhat hand-wavy.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation uses relatively small datasets; scalability to truly massive/high-dim datasets not fully demonstrated.  
- Weakness: practical overhead of computing Radon points and accessibility for readers unfamiliar with Radon geometry are not thoroughly explored.  
- Recommendation: overall strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the noted practical and exposition concerns.
