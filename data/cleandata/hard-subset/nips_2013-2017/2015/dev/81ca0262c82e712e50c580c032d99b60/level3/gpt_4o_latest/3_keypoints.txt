- Introduces a novel GP-based forward-backward scheme that extends Path Integral control to uncertain systems and derives analytic control laws without policy parameterization.  
- Claims high sample efficiency by avoiding extensive forward sampling typical of PI methods.  
- Enables compositionality-based generalization of learned controllers to new tasks without re-sampling.  
- Demonstrates empirical efficiency and generalizability on cart-pole swing-up, double pendulum on a cart, and PUMA-560, outperforming PILCO and PDDP in experiments.  

- Strength: Significant conceptual contribution by avoiding heavy sampling in PI control via a forward-backward approach.  
- Strength: Strong sample efficiency suitable for real-world, data-scarce applications.  
- Strength: Valuable compositional generalization ability to transfer controllers to new tasks.  
- Strength: Analytic control updates remove need for restrictive policy parameterization.  
- Strength: Comparative experiments highlight performance and computational advantages over state-of-the-art model-based methods.  

- Weakness: Dual optimization over u and δu is poorly explained, causing confusion about core mechanics.  
- Weakness: Paper organization and readability need improvement; Section 3 (especially 3.1) has contradictory GP definitions and unclear initialization.  
- Weakness: GP model update seems to ignore the cost of the current control sequence, raising concerns about consistency with iterative PI control.  
- Weakness: Forward inference via belief propagation may fail for non-Gaussian dynamics, a limitation not sufficiently addressed.  
- Weakness: Notation is unclear about model-based aspects, and comparisons to related methods (e.g., PI², Feedback PI) contain inaccuracies.  

- For acceptance: Novelty, sample efficiency, and generalizability are significant contributions with convincing experimental support.  
- For acceptance: Empirical results demonstrate advantages that align with the conference’s focus on RL and control advances.  

- Against acceptance: Poorly explained problem formulation and organizational issues impede comprehension.  
- Against acceptance: Inadequate treatment of non-Gaussian dynamics and implications of ignoring the current control cost.  
- Against acceptance: Inaccurate or unclear comparisons to related work could mislead readers.  

- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions to improve clarity, address technical limitations, and correct/clarify comparisons with related work.
