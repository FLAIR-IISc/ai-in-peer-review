- Proposes a novel, sample-optimal and computationally efficient approach for testing membership of discrete distributions in various shape-restricted classes.
- Provides testers for monotonicity, independence, log-concavity, unimodality, and monotone hazard rate, improving on prior work.
- Key technical insight: a robust L2-to-L1 identity test that distinguishes small L2 distance from large total variation distance.
- Delivers optimal sample complexity results and matching lower bounds, showing testers are information-theoretically optimal.
- Presents a unified framework that generalizes across multiple distribution classes.
- Detailed proofs and analyses support correctness and optimality of the methods.
- Paper is well-written and well-organized, with clear sections guiding the reader.
- Minor issues: some notation and terminology need clarification.
- Minor issues: a few grammatical errors throughout the text.
- Minor issues: certain technical details (e.g., dynamics and reward functions) require further elaboration.
- Overall assessment: significant, original contribution and a strong candidate for acceptance.
