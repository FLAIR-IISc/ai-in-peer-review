- Proposes a novel testing approach for whether a discrete distribution belongs to a class or is far from it.
- Introduces sample-optimal and computationally efficient testers for monotonicity, independence, log-concavity, unimodality, and monotone hazard rate.
- Technique is based on a robust 2-statistic / 2-`1 identity test distinguishing small 2-distance vs large total variation distance.
- Develops a forward-backward approach as a notable methodological contribution.
- Provides matching lower bounds for most classes, demonstrating optimality of the testers.
- Supplies a useful building block for proper learning of discrete log-concave and monotone hazard rate distributions.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and demonstrates thorough understanding of related work.
- Technical correctness is high and results significantly advance the state of the art.
- Recommends acceptance to the conference, subject to minor revisions.
- Needs more clarity in derivations, notably the recursive form of Φ and the transition from equation 7 to 8.
- Should number all equations to facilitate references in reviews and discussions.
