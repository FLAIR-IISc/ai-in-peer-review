- Introduces a novel PI-control method combining GP-learned dynamics with a forward-backward optimization that yields analytic controls without policy parameterization.
- Key contribution: uses compositionality theory to generalize learned controllers to new tasks without re-sampling.
- Demonstrates sample efficiency and generalizability on three simulated robotics tasks, compared to PILCO and PDDP.
- Strength: novel combination of PI control, probabilistic model-based RL, and linearly solvable optimal control with an innovative forward-backward scheme.
- Strength: addresses sample efficiency and multi-task generalization, enabling controller reuse across tasks.
- Strength: experimental results show superior sample efficiency and competitive performance versus SOTA.
- Strength: clear motivation and effective positioning relative to existing PI-related methods.
- Weakness: some derivations and notation (desirability function, Psi) are hard to follow; open-loop vs feedback distinction is unclear.
- Weakness: experiments lack critical details (hyperparameters, trial counts, compute) and do not report average costs.
- Weakness: method assumes partial knowledge of control matrix G and does not address uncertainty in G, limiting applicability.
- Weakness: grammar, formatting, and notation inconsistencies; vague description of \tilde{G} and actuator details.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on clarifying derivations and improving experimental reporting.
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically grounded novelty, demonstrated improvements in sample efficiency and task generalization, strong robotics relevance.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear derivations, sparse experimental/computational details, reliance on partial knowledge of G.
- Overall: meaningful scientific contribution that needs improved presentation and experimental rigor to maximize impact.
