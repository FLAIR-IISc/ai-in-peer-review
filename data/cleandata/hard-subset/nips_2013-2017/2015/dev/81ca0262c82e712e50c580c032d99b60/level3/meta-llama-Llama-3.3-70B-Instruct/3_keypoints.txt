- Novel approach: uses a Gaussian process to model trajectories for stochastic optimal control.
- Probabilistic formulation: interprets control problem probabilistically to enable efficient optimal-policy computation.
- Experimental validation: experiments show the method competes with existing approaches.
- Presentation: paper is generally well-organized and clearly written with a good introduction and detailed methodology.
- Confusing problem formulation: two optimizations are presented and the relationship between optimal control and proposal control is unclear.
- Paper organization: subsections should be rearranged to improve readability and clarity.
- GP definition mismatch: section 3.1’s GP definition contradicts Eq. (2) and GP initialization is not clearly specified.
- GP update concern: the GP model update appears to ignore the cost of the current control sequence; unclear if this generalizes PI control or is another policy search.
- Belief propagation limitation: forward inference via belief propagation may fail for non-Gaussian dynamics and needs discussion or alternatives.
- Notation ambiguity: unclear notation—authors should distinguish model-based vs. model-free terms more clearly.
- Relationship to PI methods: comparisons to PI-related methods are useful but require explicit remarks clarifying relationships.
- Minor issues: contains unclear sentences, typos, and formatting errors that need correction.
- Overall verdict: interesting, working idea with competitive results but requires clarifications and fixes to be more convincing.
