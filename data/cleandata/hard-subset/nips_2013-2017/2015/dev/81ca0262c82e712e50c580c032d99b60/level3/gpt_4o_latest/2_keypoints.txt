- Novel forward-backward formulation of path integral (PI) control using an exponential value function enabling analytical solutions and backward evaluation of path integrals.  
- Forward-backward optimization combines GP-based forward dynamics modeling with backward integration of path costs and derivatives, avoiding forward sampling.  
- Method produces analytic control laws without policy parameterization, reducing computational cost and sample needs versus traditional PI methods.  
- Use of Gaussian Processes to model dynamics uncertainty and adapt control cost weights is an innovative component.  
- Demonstrated improvements in sample efficiency, optimality, and generalizability compared to state-of-the-art model-based methods (PILCO, PDDP).  
- Ability to generalize to new tasks via compositionality theory is empirically supported.  
- Paper is well-organized and provides valuable insights at the intersection of probabilistic RL and optimal control.  
- Baseline comparisons may be unfair: proposed method uses far fewer samples than iterative PI, complicating direct performance comparisons.  
- Key mathematical derivations lack clarity (notably transitions like Equation 7→8 and integral transformations on page 5), risking reproducibility.  
- The novelty claim is tempered because GP use is not new; forward-backward originality needs clearer contextualization relative to prior work.  
- Lack of equation numbering makes referencing and discussion difficult.  
- Occasional readability issues and insufficiently explained transitions detract from clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, analytically grounded, computationally efficient approach with robust experimental validation and real-world potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potentially unfair baselines, unclear derivations hindering reproducibility, and presentation issues; overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify derivations and improve presentation (e.g., equation numbering).
