Presents a novel data-driven optimal control framework from PI control using an iterative forward-backward scheme that computes analytic control laws without policy parameterization.

Forward-backward optimization generalizes PI methods and adapts control cost weights based on explicit uncertainty.

Method demonstrates superior sample efficiency compared to PILCO and PDDP in experiments.

Uses compositionality theory to generalize learned controllers to new tasks without re-sampling.

Theoretical contribution extends PI control to uncertain systems and derives a linear Chapman–Kolmogorov PDE.

Experimental validation on cart-pole, double pendulum, and PUMA-560 shows efficiency, computational feasibility, and generalizability.

Paper is dense with heavy mathematical derivations and lacks intuitive explanations or visual aids, hurting accessibility.

Relies on partial knowledge of the control matrix G, which limits applicability when G is unknown and no remedy is provided.

More computationally expensive than PDDP due to global optimization; scalability to higher-dimensional systems is not fully explored.

Experiments are limited to simulated environments; real-world validation and handling of uncertainty in G are not demonstrated.

Significant theoretical and practical contribution by combining PI control theory with probabilistic model-based RL.

Addresses key community-relevant challenges of sample efficiency and generalizability.

Experimental results are compelling and outperform state-of-the-art model-based RL baselines.

Main drawbacks are reliance on partial G knowledge and lack of real-world experiments, limiting immediate applicability.

Dense mathematical presentation may reduce the paper’s accessibility to a broader audience.

Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and add discussion/insights on real-world applicability.
