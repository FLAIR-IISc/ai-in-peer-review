- Presents a novel data-driven optimal control framework combining path integral control theory with probabilistic model-based RL.  
- Algorithm operates in a forward-backward manner, unlike sampling-based path integral methods that use forward sampling.  
- Claims significantly higher sample efficiency than sampling-based path integral control and comparable sample/computational efficiency to state-of-the-art model-based methods.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides thorough background and related work.  
- Includes detailed derivation: linearized Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman equation and Feynman–Kac formula.  
- Experimental results demonstrate sample efficiency and generalizability to new tasks.  
- Strength: Novel framework that merges path integral control and probabilistic model-based RL.  
- Strength: Thorough theoretical derivation and supporting experiments.  
- Strength: Shown to be sample efficient and generalizable compared to other path integral methods.  
- Weakness: Assumes the control matrix G is known, which may not hold in many real-world applications.  
- Weakness: Method is limited to systems with uncertain dynamics; unclear performance under other uncertainty types.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison with model-free path integral-related methods to clarify trade-offs.  
- Overall: Significant contribution with potential wide applicability, but further work needed to address limitations and scalability to more complex systems.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novelty, sample efficiency, generalizability, and thorough derivation/experiments.  
- Rejection arguments: Known G assumption, limited uncertainty scope, and missing comparisons with model-free alternatives.
