1. Proposes a novel path integral control formulation using an exponential value function that allows analytic path integrals and backward evaluation.  
2. Introduces a forward-backward optimization: GP models paths forward in time, backward integration computes path costs and derivatives, then controls are updated numerically.  
3. Paper is technically sound and of relatively high quality; reviewer learned something new.  
4. Surprising empirical outperformance of iterative PI may be due to low baseline sample sizes (10^3 for cart-pole, 10^4 for double pendulum), so baseline evaluation needs care.  
5. Clarity issues: key derivations are rushed—transition from Equation 7 to 8 and the integral-to-recursive form derivation around mid-page 5 are hard to follow.  
6. Originality: using a GP for the model is not new, but the forward-backward scheme and using the GP to infer integrals for changed value functions is novel.  
7. Significance: represents an interesting and substantial improvement to existing path integral methods.  
8. Recommendation to number all equations to aid referencing in reviews and future work.  
9. Overall: good paper with important contributions, but somewhat hard to read due to space limitations.
