- Proposes a novel path integral control method that learns uncontrolled dynamics with a GP while using known control matrix G, allowing noise and controls to act in different subspaces.  
- Shows task generalization by linearly combining reward exponents, but the derivation (eqs. 13–14) is unclear and possibly incorrect—needs clarification on how value exponents combine.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on three simulated robotics tasks against SOTA methods, but some experimental details are missing for reproducibility.  
- Missing experimental details: dynamics and reward functions, trajectory lengths, and more implementation specifics (possibly should be in appendix).  
- Comparison/benchmarking concerns: unclear whether iterative PI control is included in Table 1 and how qualitative aspects compare across baselines.  
- Plots (Figures 1–2) need error bars; reporting psi (exponent of value) may be misleading—average or cumulative reward should be reported as an objective metric.  
- Ambiguity whether reported psi is computed by the robot and risk of optimistic bias if the robot overestimates psi.  
- GP modeling inconsistencies: regression targets differ between eq. (2) and line 208 (absence of reference controls); hyperparameter settings and the meaning of “constraining the joint distribution” (conditioning?) must be explained.  
- Algorithm 1 is confusing about open-loop u1...uT vs. learning a feedback controller; notation and returned output should be clarified to show how noise is handled.  
- Originality: combines known components (learned stochastic forward model + PI control) in a novel way and shows significant empirical improvements.  
- Rebuttal issues: (14) still seems to have a typo (Psi vs Phi) and task-generalization explanation remains unclear; confidence/score was reduced accordingly.  
- Requests to report average cost in addition to psi, possibly in supplementary material.  
- Minor editorial and formatting issues: grammar errors, empty sections, margin/formatting problems, bracket scaling in eq. (8), inconsistent bold/italic notation in eq. (13), and table/line labeling (e.g., Table 1, line references).  
- Other minor clarifications requested: explain how to obtain \tilde{G} from G, correct wording about PILCO’s policy improvement/evaluation, and clarify actuator/joint phrasing.
