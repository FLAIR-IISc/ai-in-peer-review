- Novel PI-related method: uses a Gaussian process to model trajectories under a proposal control and optimizes a control update δu via a Bellman equation; evaluated on three simulated tasks.  
- Main conceptual confusion: problem formulation mixes two optimizations (optimizing u and optimizing δu) and implies the optimal u is known with a proposal u^old — authors must clarify.  
- Paper organization/readability issue: proposed approach appears earlier than subsection 3.1; recommend moving 3.1 after 2.1 since Eq.(9) depends on 3.1’s inference.  
- Inconsistency in GP definition: section 3.1 defines the GP for the uncontrolled process (u=0) conflicting with Eq.(2) which depends on the current proposal control.  
- GP initialization concern: if GP is initialized from uncontrolled samples, clarify effects of poor conditioning / small effective sample size on initialization.  
- Potential omission of control-cost term: formulation appears to ignore the cost of the current control sequence (Eq.(5)); need to identify the equivalent of the Radon–Nikodym derivative term from iterative PI and justify the PI-generalization claim.  
- Inference fragility: using belief propagation for forward inference may fail for non-Gaussian dynamics or abrupt changes (e.g., obstacles); authors should clarify limitations and whether experiments reflect such cases.  
- Notation/modeling clarity: although presented as model-based, it’s unclear whether a dynamics model is used beyond a trajectory GP — suggest clearer notation to distinguish model vs. trajectory distribution.  
- Relation to prior PI methods: reviewer notes inaccuracies/omissions in comparisons — e.g., PI^2’s constraint argument, Feedback PI uses parameterized feedback not policy, PI-REPS is model-based, Iterative PI is original PI with importance sampling — revise related work/text and Table 1 accordingly.  
- Minor writing/typo issues to fix: lines 120 (“minimize(s)”), 144–145 (unclear sentence), 146 (“(to) act”), 229 (“based on (a) probabilistic”).  
- Overall verdict: promising, competitive method, but presentation, clarity on theoretical connections to PI vs. policy search, and several technical clarifications are required.
