- Introduces a novel data-driven optimal control framework combining Path Integral control with probabilistic model-based RL and linearly solvable optimal control.  
- Proposes an iterative forward-backward optimization scheme that derives analytic control laws without policy parameterization.  
- Claims to address PI-related limitations: sample inefficiency, poor generalizability, and heavy forward sampling requirements.  
- Demonstrates efficiency and generalizability on cart-pole, double pendulum, and PUMA-560 simulated tasks.  
- Shows ability to generalize learned controllers to new tasks without re-sampling via compositionality theory.  
- Technical soundness: rigorous mathematical derivations and a novel departure from traditional PI methods.  
- Sample efficiency: outperforms PILCO and PDDP in experiments, valuable for data-scarce real-world scenarios.  
- Generalizability: effectively motivates and demonstrates compositional controller reuse across tasks.  
- Clarity and organization: well-structured paper with detailed derivations and reproducible algorithmic steps.  
- Assumes partial knowledge of dynamics (e.g., control matrix G) and does not model uncertainty in G, limiting applicability.  
- Computational complexity: more expensive than PDDP due to global optimization despite being cheaper than PILCO.  
- Experimental scope limited to simulations; no real-world system validation provided.  
- Lacks comparisons to model-free PI approaches and other recent RL algorithms for broader benchmarking.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, technically solid, improves sample efficiency and generalizability, and shows convincing experimental gains.  
- Rejection concerns: reliance on partial dynamics knowledge, missing real-world experiments, and potential computational issues in high-dimensional or real-time settings.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address noted limitations; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
