- Proposes a novel data-driven optimal control framework that combines path integral (PI) control theory with probabilistic model-based RL.  
- Introduces a forward-backward algorithmic approach that differs from PI methods that rely on forward sampling.  
- Demonstrates efficiency and generalizability on three tasks, outperforming state-of-the-art model-based methods in sample and computational efficiency.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, thorough related-work analysis, and well-explained technical details.  
- Strength: novel algorithmic combination and strong experimental validation.  
- Weakness: assumes partial knowledge of the dynamics (G matrix), which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Weakness: more computationally expensive than some baselines (e.g., PDDP).  
- Weakness: does not account for uncertainty in the control matrix G.  
- Recommendation: accept; overall scores — Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Suggestion: authors should address the dynamics-assumption and incorporate/control for uncertainty in G.
