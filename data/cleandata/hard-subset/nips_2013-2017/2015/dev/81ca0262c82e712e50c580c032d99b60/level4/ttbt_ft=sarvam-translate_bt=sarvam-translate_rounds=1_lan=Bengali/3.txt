This paper presents a new PI-related approach for stochastic optimal control. The core idea is to use a Gaussian process to model the trajectory resulting from the application of a proposed control $u^old$, which is applied to the uncontrollable dynamics. The proposed Bellman equation is defined to optimize for the control update $\delta u$ in each iteration. This method takes advantage of the analytical method for GP estimation to efficiently update the GP parameters, and it has been experimentally evaluated in three simulated tasks.

The concept is interesting, and the authors seem to have a working approach that could compete with existing methods. In general, I liked the paper, but I think the following points need attention:

The problem formulation section is somewhat confusing because it involves two optimizations: one is optimizing over $u$ to find the optimal control, and the other is optimizing over $\delta u$. From 2.1, it seems that the optimal u is given with a proposed control u^old, which is clearly not correct because finding the optimal u is the main task. The authors should clarify this point.

The organization of the paper could be significantly improved: since the proposed method is presented before 3.1, rather than in 3.1, I would like to place sub-section 3.1 after sub-section 2.1. This would also improve readability because the assumption step explained in equation (9) involves sub-section 3.1.

I also found section 3.1 confusing, where the GP is defined for the uncontrollable process (u=0) (line 208), which conflicts with the definition of equation (2), which depends on the current proposed control. Furthermore, if the GP is initialized using sample data from the uncontrollable process, the authors should also clarify how the weak conditioning of the uncontrollable process in terms of the effective sample size affects this initialization process.

Regarding how the GP model is updated, it seems that the formulation ignores the cost of the current control sequence, see equation (5). In the recursive PI control, this term enters the equation as a Radon-Nicodim derivative [16,17]. Could the authors mention the equivalent term in their formulation? Can we still talk about a generalization of PI control or is it another policy search, a PI-related approach?

The use of belief propagation for forward estimation can fail when the dynamics around the reference suddenly change, i.e., they become non-Gaussian. This occurs in the presence of constraints, for example, which do not seem to occur in the presented experiment. The authors should clarify whether this is a potential problem.

Regarding the notation, although the authors have presented the method as model-based, it is not clear whether a model of the dynamics has been used (they only have a probability distribution over the trajectory through the GP). I suggest different notation for clarity.

The authors have done a good job of relating their method to existing PI-related approaches, which is a difficult task considering the (already large) current literature on PI control. I have the following comments on this:

- Limitations are not ignored in PI^2 [8], but PI^2 is an open-loop policy search method, so limitations are meaningless and the same logic applies as in PI^2-CMA. - Feedback PI [17] uses a parameterized feedback term (second-order correction), but not a parameterized policy. - PI-REPS [11] is model-based (although the model can be learned, e.g., in guided policy search [23]) - recursive PI [16] is the real PI with a sample of importance and therefore: model-based, with the same structural limitations as PI and without a parameterized policy.

These comments involve not only Table 1 but are written throughout the paper.

Minor errors:
Line 120: minimize(s) Line 144-145: The sentence is ambiguous: "while (...)." Line 146: (to) act Line 229: based on (a) probabilistic.

The paper presents a new PI-related approach for stochastic optimal control that uses a Gaussian process to model the trajectory. The method is promising, but it is not clear whether it is a generalization of PI control or a policy search, a PI-related approach. The presentation should be improved, and some more points need to be clarified.
