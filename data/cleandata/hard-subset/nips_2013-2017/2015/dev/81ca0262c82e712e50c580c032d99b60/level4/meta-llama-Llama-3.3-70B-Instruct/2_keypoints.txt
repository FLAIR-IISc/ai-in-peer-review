- Novel approach: use an exponential form of the value function to obtain analytical path integrals and enable backward evaluation.
- Introduces a forward-backward optimization: GP models paths forward, backward integration yields path costs and derivatives, then controls are updated numerically.
- Technical correctness is high; the paper is enjoyable and informative.
- Comparison to iterative path integral baselines is questionable because outperformance may stem from low sample counts (10^3 for cart-pole, 10^4 for double pendulum).
- Key derivation steps are rushed and hard to follow, notably the transition from Eq. 7 to 8 and the derivation from the initial Ψt integral to the recursive Φ_{t+dt}Ψ_{t+dt} form.
- Using a GP for modeling is not novel, but the forward-backward GP application and using the GP to infer integrals for changed value functions are innovative.
- The proposed method represents a significant improvement over existing path integral techniques.
- Recommend numbering all equations to simplify referencing and improve clarity.
- Overall: a good, valuable contribution with important innovations but some sections need clarification (likely due to page limits).
