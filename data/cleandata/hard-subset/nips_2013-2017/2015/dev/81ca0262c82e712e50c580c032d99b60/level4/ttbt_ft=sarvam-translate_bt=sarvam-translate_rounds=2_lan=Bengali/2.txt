The authors have proposed a new formula for controlling path integrals, where they use an exponential form of the value function, which allows them to evaluate integrals in reverse order, and specifically, in the backward direction. This gives them the ability to optimize using a forward-backward approach, where they use a GP to model the paths over time, and then perform backward integration to obtain the path costs and derivatives, and then use a numerical technique to update the controls.

Quality: The paper's quality is relatively high, in terms of technical accuracy. I enjoyed reading the paper and learned something new.

I was surprised that this technique exceeded repeated PI – if I didn't miss something, it is almost certainly a sample size problem (10^3 for cart-poles, 10^4 for double pendulums), which seems relatively small. Undoubtedly, the new technique is more efficient, but the authors need to be careful about their baseline evaluation.

Clarity: My main comment about the paper is that the authors skip some key steps quickly. In particular, going from equations 7 to 8 is non-trivial, and this is the key to the overall paper. Similarly, going from the initial integral of \Psit (page 5) to the final repeated form of \Phi{t+dt}\Psi_{t+dt} is even more difficult.

Novelty: Using a GP for the model is not new, but forward-backward is new, moreover, the ability to use a GP to estimate integrals for modified value functions is new to me at least.

Significance: An interesting and substantial improvement over existing path integral methods.

Please provide all equation numbers – I understand that there is a school of thought that equations should only be numbered if they are mentioned in the text, but that stylistic idea creates a major problem when others want to mention equations outside the paper's text, either in reviews (like this one), other papers that mention it, or in discussions. The authors have proposed a new method that takes the industry forward. Overall a good paper, although some places are not easy to read. This is mainly due to page limitations, so I cannot blame the authors too much for this.
