**Abstract**

This paper presents a new method for path integral control. The proposed method takes advantage of the fact that the system control matrix G is often known, and the uncontrolled dynamics (or dynamics under a reference controller) can be learned using the Gaussian process. The restriction on the reward function takes on a more general shape compared to previous PI approaches, meaning that noise and control can operate in different subdomains. The authors also show how their framework can be used for generalization from known to new tasks, and evaluate the method on three simulated robotics problems, where their method is compatible with SOTA reinforcement learning and control methods.

**Quality**

Generally, the derivation seems correct and theoretical (although I am not sure about task generalization, see below). The related tasks are adequately discussed, the authors point out the similarities and differences of the related task, and compare with the state of the art methods. The experiments are reassuring, although some details are missing (see below). The task generalization seems odd: (13) states that the exponent of the reward function will be linearly combined to generate the exponent of the reward function of the new task. However, it is not clear to me how this changes the exponent of the value function into a similar linear combination (14). The experiments consider relevant and realistic tasks, and compare with SOTA methods.
