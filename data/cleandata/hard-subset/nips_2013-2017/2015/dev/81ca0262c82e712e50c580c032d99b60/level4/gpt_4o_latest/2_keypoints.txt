- Introduces a novel path-integral control formulation using an exponential value-function representation that allows analytical path-integral solutions and backward evaluation.  
- Proposes a forward-backward optimization: GP models forward trajectories, backward integration computes path costs/derivatives, and a numerical method updates controls.  
- Technical correctness is strong; the work is intellectually stimulating and informative.  
- Reported performance gains over iterative PI are surprising and may be due to low sample counts (1e3 for cart-pole, 1e4 for double pendulum), risking unfair baseline comparison.  
- Clarity concerns: key derivations progress too quickly—transition from Eq.7 to Eq.8 and the derivation leading to Φ_{t+dt}Ψ_{t+dt} are hard to follow.  
- Originality: using a GP for dynamics is not new, but the forward-backward integration and using the GP to infer integrals for a modified value function are novel.  
- Significance: represents a meaningful advancement in path-integral methods.  
- Practical suggestion: number all equations to ease discussion, review, and future citation.  
- Overall assessment: solid, state-of-the-art contribution, though some sections are difficult to follow (likely due to space constraints).
