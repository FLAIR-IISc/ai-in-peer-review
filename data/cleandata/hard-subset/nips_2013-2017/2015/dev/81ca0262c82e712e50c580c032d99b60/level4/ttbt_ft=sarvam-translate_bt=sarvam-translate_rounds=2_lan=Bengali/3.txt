This paper presents a new PI-related approach for stochastic optimal control. The core idea is to use a Gaussian process to model the trajectory resulting from applying a proposed control $u^old$, which is applied to the uncontrollable dynamics. The proposed Bellman equation is defined to optimize the control update $\delta u$ in each iteration. This method takes advantage of the analytical approach for GP estimation to efficiently update the GP parameters, and it has been experimentally evaluated on three simulated tasks.

The concept is interesting, and the authors seem to have a working approach that could compete with existing methods. In general, I liked the paper, but I think the following points need attention:

The problem formulation section is somewhat confusing because it involves two optimizations: one is optimizing over $u$ to find the optimal control, and the other is optimizing over $\delta u$. From 2.1, it seems that the optimal u is given along with a proposed control u^old, which is clearly not correct because finding the optimal u is the main task. The authors should clarify this point.

The organization of the paper could be significantly improved: since the proposed method was presented before 3.1, instead of 3.1, I would like to place sub-section 3.1 after sub-section 2.1. This would also improve readability because sub-section 3.1 involves the assumption explained in equation (9).

I also found the 3.1 section confusing, where the GP is defined for the uncontrollable process (u=0) (line 208), which contradicts the definition of equation (2), which depends on the current proposed control. Furthermore, if the GP is initialized using sample data from the uncontrollable process, the authors should also clarify how the weak conditioning of the uncontrollable process affects this initialization process in terms of effective sample size.

Regarding how the GP model is updated, it seems that the formulation ignores the cost of the current control sequence, see equation (5). In the recursive PI control, this term enters the equation as a Radon-Nicodim derivative [16,17]. Could the authors mention the equivalent term in their formulation? Can we still talk about a generalization of PI control or is it another principle search, a PI-related approach?

The use of trust propagation for forward estimation can fail when the dynamics around the reference suddenly change, i.e., they become non-Gaussian. This occurs in the presence of constraints, for example, which does not seem to occur in the presented experiment. The authors should clarify whether this is a potential problem.

Regarding notation, although the authors have presented the method as model-based, it is not clear whether a model of the dynamics has been used (they only have a probability distribution over the trajectory through the GP). I suggest various notations for clarity.

The authors have done a good job of relating their method to existing PI-related approaches, which is a difficult task considering the current literature on PI control (already large). I have the following comments on this subject:

- Constraints are not ignored in PI^2 [8], but PI^2 is a principle search method, so the constraints are meaningless and the same logic applies as in PI^2-CMA. - Response PI [17] uses a parameterized response term (second-order correction), but not a parameterized principle. - PI-REPS [11] is model-based (although the model can be learned, for example, in guided policy search [23]) - recursive PI [16] has the original PI along with a sample of importance and therefore: model-based, with the same structural constraints as PI and without a parameterized principle.

These comments are written throughout the entire paper, not just in Table 1.

Minor errors:
Line 120: minimize(s) Line 144-145: The sentence is ambiguous: "while (...)." Line 146: (to) act Line 229: based on (a) probabilistic.

The paper presents a new PI-related approach for stochastic optimal control that uses a Gaussian process to model the trajectory. The method is promising, but it is not clear whether it is a generalization of PI control or a principle search, a PI-related approach. The presentation should be improved and some more points need to be clarified.
