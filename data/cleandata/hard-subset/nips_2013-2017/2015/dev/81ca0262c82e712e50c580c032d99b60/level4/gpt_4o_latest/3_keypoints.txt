- Introduces a novel PI-related method using a Gaussian process to model trajectories for stochastic optimal control and reports experiments on three simulated tasks.
- Problem formulation is unclear due to two optimization processes (optimizing u vs optimizing δu); claim that optimal u is given is misleading and needs clarification.
- Paper organization should be improved; move subsection 3.1 to follow 2.1 because Eq.(9) depends on inference in 3.1.
- Section 3.1 is confusing: the GP is defined for the uncontrolled process (u=0) but Eq.(2) uses the current proposal control, creating an inconsistency.
- If the GP is initialized from uncontrolled samples, authors must explain effects of poor conditioning/effective sample size on initialization.
- GP model update appears to omit the cost of the current control sequence (Radon–Nikodym derivative term used in iterative PI); authors should identify the corresponding term or clarify method taxonomy.
- If that term is missing, clarify whether the method is truly a PI generalization or should be categorized as policy search / PI-related.
- Belief propagation for forward inference may fail when dynamics change abruptly or are non-Gaussian (e.g., obstacles); the paper should acknowledge or address this limitation.
- Notation is unclear about whether an explicit dynamics model is used, since the GP gives distributions over trajectories; revise notation for clarity.
- Literature comparisons need corrections: PI^2/PI^2-CMA are open-loop policy search, Feedback PI uses parameterized feedback (not a parameterized policy), PI-REPS is model-based, and Iterative PI is model-based with importance sampling.
- Table 1 and related text should be updated to reflect the correct distinctions among PI variants and related methods.
- Minor editorial issues/typos to fix: "minimize(s)" (line 120), unclear sentence at lines 144–145, "(to) act" (line 146), and "based on (a) probabilistic" (line 229).
- Overall: promising, functional method but needs clearer presentation, tighter theoretical connections to PI, and responses to the points above.
