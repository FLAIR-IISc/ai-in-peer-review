The authors have proposed a new formula for path integral control, where they use an exponential form of the value function, which allows them to analytically solve the path integrals of the cost and, in particular, they can evaluate the path integrals in reverse. This gives them the ability to optimize using a forward-backward method, where they use a GP to model the paths over time and then run backward integration to obtain the path cost and derivatives, and then use a numerical technique to update the controls.

Quality: The paper's quality is relatively high, in terms of technical accuracy. I enjoyed reading the paper and learned something new.

I was surprised that this technique surpassed the recursive PI – unless I missed something, it is almost certainly a problem of sample size (10^3 for cart-poles, 10^4 for double pendulums), which seems relatively minor. Undoubtedly, the new technique is more efficient, but the authors need to be careful about their baseline assessment.

Clarity: My main comment about the paper is that the authors go through some key steps quickly. In particular, going from equations 7 to 8 is non-trivial, and this is the key to the overall paper. Similarly, going from the initial integral of \Psit (mid-page 5) to the final recursive form of \Phi{t+dt}\Psi_{t+dt} is more difficult.

Originality: The use of GP for the model is not new, but forward-backward is new, moreover, the ability to use GP to estimate integrals for modified value functions is new to me at least.

Significance: An interesting and substantial improvement over existing path integral methods.

Please provide all equation numbers – I understand that there is a school of thought that equations should only be numbered if they are mentioned in the text, but that stylistic idea creates a big problem when others want to mention equations outside the paper text, either in reviews (as this one), other papers that mention it, or in discussions. The authors have proposed a new method that advances the art. Overall a good paper, although some places are not easy to read. This is mainly due to page limitations, so I cannot blame the authors too much for this.
