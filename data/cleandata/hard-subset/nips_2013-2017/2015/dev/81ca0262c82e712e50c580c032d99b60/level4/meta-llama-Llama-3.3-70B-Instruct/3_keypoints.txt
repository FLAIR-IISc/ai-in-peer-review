1. Problem formulation is confusing: paper mixes two optimizations (finding optimal u and optimizing δu) and misleadingly treats optimal u as if provided alongside proposal u^{old} — clarify objective and roles of u and u^{old}.  
2. Organization needs improvement: reorder subsections (move 3.1 after 2.1) because Eq.(9) depends on inference explained in 3.1 to improve readability.  
3. GP definition and update unclear: section 3.1 defines a GP for the uncontrolled process (u=0) conflicting with Eq.(2) that depends on the proposal control; clarify the GP’s dependence on control and how poor conditioning from uncontrolled-data initialization is handled.  
4. Formulation and generalization ambiguous: the cost of the current control sequence appears omitted (e.g., Radon–Nikodym term in iterative PI); identify the equivalent term or justify omission and clearly state whether this truly generalizes PI control or is a PI-related policy search variant.  
5. Belief propagation may fail under non-Gaussian/abrupt dynamics: forward inference could be brittle with obstacles or multimodal transitions, and experiments do not evaluate such scenarios — address robustness limits.  
6. Notation and model clarity lacking: it is unclear if an explicit dynamics model is used vs. only a GP over trajectories; adopt clearer notation to resolve this ambiguity.  
7. Relation to existing PI methods needs precision: correct or clarify claims about PI^2 (open-loop), Feedback PI (uses parameterized feedback term not a parameterized policy), PI-REPS (model-based/learned model possible), and Iterative PI (original importance-sampling PI, model-based).  
8. Minor issues: several unclear sentences and notational choices that should be revised for clarity.  
9. Overall: promising and competitive method but requires clearer presentation, theoretical clarification, and experiments covering challenging dynamics.
