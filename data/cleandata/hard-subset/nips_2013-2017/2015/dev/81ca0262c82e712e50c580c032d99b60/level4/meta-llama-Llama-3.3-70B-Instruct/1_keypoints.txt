- Introduces a novel path integral control method using known control matrix G and learning uncontrolled dynamics with a Gaussian Process, allowing noise and controls to act in different subspaces.
- Enables a more general reward-function constraint than prior path integral approaches.
- Framework can generalize from known to new tasks and is demonstrated on three simulated robotics problems.
- Empirical results compare favorably to state-of-the-art reinforcement learning and control methods.
- Derivations appear correct and principled, though there are concerns about the scope of task generalization.
- Related work is sufficiently discussed, with clear similarities and differences and comparisons to baselines.
- Experiments are convincing but missing important details (dynamics, reward functions, trajectory lengths) needed for reproducibility.
- Task generalization seems specific to target-defined tasks, though other variations may be possible with a task-similarity kernel.
- Comparison plots should include error bars and report average or cumulative reward rather than the exponent of the value function.
- Paper is generally well-written but contains grammatical/formatting errors and unclear points (Algorithm 1 notation, GP hyperparameters).
- Originality/significance lie in the novel combination of existing components, yielding strong performance on realistic benchmarks.
- Overall convincing contribution with minor issues that require clarification and an author response to fully assess significance.
