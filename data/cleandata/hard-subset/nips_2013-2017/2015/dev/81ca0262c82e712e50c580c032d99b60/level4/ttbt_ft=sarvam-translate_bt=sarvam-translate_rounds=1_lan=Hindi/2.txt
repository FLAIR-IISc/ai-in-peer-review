The authors propose a new formula for path integral control, where they use an exponential form of the value function, which allows them to analytically solve the path integrals of the cost, and specifically, they can evaluate the path integrals backward. This gives them the ability to optimize using a forward-backward approach, where they use a GP to model the forward paths over time, and then run backward integration to obtain the path cost and derivatives, and then use a numerical technique to update the controls.

Quality: In terms of technical accuracy, the paper's quality is relatively high. I enjoyed reading the paper and learned something new.
I was surprised that this technique performed better than iterative PI – unless I'm missing something, it's almost certainly a problem with the number of samples (10^3 for cart-pole, 10^4 for double pendulum), which seems relatively minor. There's no doubt that the new technique is more efficient, but the authors need to be careful with their baseline evaluation.

Clarity: My main comment about the paper is that the authors skip some key steps quickly. In particular, the changes from equations 7 to 8 are non-trivial, and this is important for the overall paper. Similarly, the change from the initial integral of \Psit (middle of page 5) to the final recursive form of \Phi{t+dt}\Psi_{t+dt} is even more difficult.

Novelty: Using a GP for the model is not new, but the forward-backward approach is new, in addition to the ability to use a GP to estimate the integral for the changed value function is new to me at least.

Importance: An interesting and substantial improvement over existing path integral methods.

Please number all equations – I understand that there is an ideology that equations should only be numbered when they are referenced in the text, but that stylistic notion creates big problems when other people want to mention equations outside the text of the paper, either in reviews (like this one), in other papers referencing this one, or in discussion. The authors have proposed a new method that advances the state of the art. Overall a good paper, although not easy to read in some places. This is largely due to page limits so I can't blame the authors too much for this.
