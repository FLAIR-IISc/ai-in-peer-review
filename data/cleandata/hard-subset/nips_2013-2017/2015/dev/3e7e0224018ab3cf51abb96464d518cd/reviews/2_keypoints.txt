- Background: POA bounds under smoothness exist for full-information games (including coarse correlated equilibria), while incomplete-information results only covered Bayes-Nash equilibria.
- Main contribution: The paper claims POA bounds for coarse correlated equilibria in Bayesian games by reducing them to several full-information games.
- Readability/generalizability: Paper is hard to read and seems to lack generality; the setup is not clearly explained.
- Ambiguity in definitions: Unclear what "Fi be the empirical distributions of values in population Pi" means—whose samples define these empirical distributions?
- Lemma 10 hypotheses unclear: D^T is a random measure, so statements about convergence in distribution and choice of subsequence are ambiguous (a.s. vs. in distribution, same subsequence for all realizations).
- Questionable limit interchange: The step claiming E_{s~D^T}[f_T(s)] → E_{s~D}[f(s)] from f_T → f a.s. and D^T → D in distribution is not justified and needs a rigorous explanation.
- Conditional acceptance: Apart from Lemma 10 concerns, the rest seems correct and the reviewer would consider acceptance if those issues are fixed.
- Relevance/verdict: The topic may not strongly interest the NIPS community; overall the paper is promising but hard to read and possibly contains a flawed proof.
