- Paper's goal: extend price of anarchy bounds from smoothness to learning outcomes in Bayesian games.  
- Method: model a Bayesian game as a stochastic complete-information game with a large population of participants.  
- Result 1: Under no-regret learning in the stochastic game, outcomes converge to a form of Bayesian coarse correlated equilibrium (BCCE).  
- Result 2: If the original game or mechanism is smooth, the constructed stochastic game is also smooth, so smoothness-based PoA bounds carry over.  
- Writing and audience: paper is hard to read, targeted more to the Game Theory community than NIPS, and reads like a direct cut-and-paste from an extended version.  
- Incomplete/unclear exposition: last section claims to analyze convergence but provides no specific convergence details.  
- Missing proofs/material: supplemental material only contains proofs for Theorems 10 and 13—other theorems/lemmas are not addressed.  
- Conceptual question: why use Forges' version of correlated equilibrium specifically?  
- Theorem clarity: Theorem 11 is unclear—does it assert convergence to BCCE? Needs elaboration.  
- Realism concern: reliance on correlated-equilibrium-style solutions (implying a central coordinator) may not realistically model selfish players.
