- Extends the concept of coarse correlated equilibria to Bayesian games.
- Two main technical results: (1) near-optimal welfare in Bayesian games follows from smoothness when private information is public, and (2) no-regret dynamics converge to Bayesian coarse correlated equilibrium.
- Uses a population interpretation of Bayesian games as a stochastic complete-information game to extend Roughgarden’s smoothness framework.
- Shows average welfare of any no-regret dynamics achieves a near-optimal fraction (parametrized by smoothness λ,µ) of the average optimal welfare, almost surely.
- Provides finite-time analysis and convergence rates; approximate efficiency holds with ε-regret.
- Results have important implications for mechanism design in online ad-auction marketplaces and other Bayesian-game applications.
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction, organized technical sections, and rigorous, accessible proofs.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Limitation: assumes finite type space Vi, which may not hold in all applications.
- Limitation: lacks a clear comparison with existing literature to contextualize contributions.
- Limitation: could benefit from numerical examples or simulations to illustrate practical implications.
