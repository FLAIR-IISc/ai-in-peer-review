- Main contribution: Extends price of anarchy bounds to Bayesian games via stochastic games with large populations.  
- Two technical results: convergence to Bayesian coarse correlated equilibrium under no-regret learning, and preservation of smoothness from the base game to the stochastic game.  
- Introduces t-level auctions as a novel mechanism that interpolates between simple and optimal auctions, enabling a tunable trade-off between near-optimality and learnability.  
- Strength: balances expressivity and simplicity in auction design, providing a principled way to trade off revenue approximation and simplicity.  
- Strength: significant implications for the quality of learning outcomes in Bayesian games and for simple approximately revenue-maximizing auctions.  
- Presentation weakness: writing is geared toward the game theory community and may be hard for non-experts to follow.  
- Presentation weakness: paper reads like a cut-and-paste from an extended version, leading to occasional lack of specificity and clarity.  
- Technical concern: choice of correlated equilibrium is not fully justified in the paper.  
- Technical concern: some theorems and lemmas are stated without proofs, raising questions about completeness of the technical argument.  
- Practical concern: realism of using a central entity to learn the auction may be limited in practice.  
- Evaluation: meets conference criteria for quality, originality, and significance; claims are largely supported by theoretical analysis.  
- Recommendation: overall valuable contribution that, with improved clarity and filling missing proofs, could be a strong addition to the conference.
