- Paper extends price of anarchy (PoA) analysis from full-information to Bayesian games, focusing on coarse correlated equilibria (CCE).
- Introduces a reduction that views Bayesian games as full-information/stochastic games, allowing smoothness arguments to bound Bayesian PoA.
- Shows no-regret learning dynamics converge to Bayesian CCE, implying near-optimal welfare in repeated Bayesian games.
- Theoretical contributions are significant for auctions and resource allocation where Bayesian models are common.
- Novel and creative reinterpretation of Bayesian games as stochastic games of complete information.
- Provides a rigorous framework showing smoothness properties carry over to the Bayesian setting; Theorem 9 and its learning implications are notable.
- Results are relevant to mechanism design and potentially applicable to online ad auctions and related domains.
- Paper is hard to read: dense setup and notation, and key concepts need clearer, more intuitive exposition.
- Lemma 10 is critical but its proof is unclear; hypotheses, convergence definitions, and product distribution claims lack rigorous justification.
- Equation (10) and its convergence argument are insufficiently explained and overly reliant on empirical process theory without full support.
- Theoretical focus may have limited appeal to the broader NIPS community that favors empirical/computational work.
- Recommendation: an OK contribution that requires significant revisions to clarity and proof details; consider a more specialized venue if not addressed.
