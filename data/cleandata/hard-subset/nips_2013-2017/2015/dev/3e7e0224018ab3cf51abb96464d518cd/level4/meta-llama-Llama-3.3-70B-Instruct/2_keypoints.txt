- Context: PoA bounds exist for full-information games and many coarse correlated equilibria, but incomplete-information results are limited to Bayes-Nash equilibria.  
- Contribution: Provides a PoA bound for coarse correlated equilibria in Bayesian games by reducing to multiple full-information games.  
- Readability and clarity are poor, limiting accessibility and perceived generality.  
- Setup ambiguity: “Fi as empirical distributions of values in population Pi” is unclear about which samples define Fi.  
- Major technical concern: Lemma 10’s hypotheses are not clearly stated concerning D^T being a random measure.  
- Unclear whether subsequence convergence of D^T is meant almost surely or must hold uniformly across realizations.  
- Questionable justification in equation (10): the paper appears to deduce expectation convergence from almost-sure function convergence plus convergence in distribution without adequate proof.  
- Rest of the paper seems correct; resolving Lemma 10’s issues is crucial for validity.  
- Conditional recommendation: could be acceptable if the proof/clarity issues are fixed, though the topic’s fit for NIPS is marginal.  
- Overall: a reasonable extension of PoA results to Bayesian games but hindered by readability and a potential proof flaw.
