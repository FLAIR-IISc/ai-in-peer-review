- Generalizes price of anarchy bounds from smoothness to learning outcomes in Bayesian games.  
- Models a Bayesian game as a stochastic complete-information game with a large population of potential participants.  
- Shows outcomes converge to a form of Bayesian coarse correlated equilibrium when players use no-regret learning in the stochastic game.  
- Proves the stochastic game is smooth if the underlying game or mechanism is smooth.  
- Concludes smoothness-based price of anarchy bounds extend to no-regret learning outcomes in Bayesian games.  
- Manuscript is difficult to follow and appears targeted more to the Game Theory community than to NIPS.  
- The paper seems to be a direct adaptation of an extended version, not specifically written for the conference; final section on convergence is underdeveloped.  
- Question: why do supplementary materials only include proofs for Theorems 10 and 13, omitting others?  
- Question: unclear motivation for choosing Forges' correlated equilibrium.  
- Question: Theorem 11 lacks clarity—does it imply convergence to a BCCE? Needs elaboration.  
- Concern: managing selfish players is hard except when a central entity (e.g., correlated equilibrium) is introduced, which may be unrealistic.  
- Primary contribution is extending price of anarchy bounds to learning outcomes in Bayesian games, despite presentation and audience-fit issues.
