In game theory, one can show that under some smooth assumptions, the indifference value of a complete information game is bounded by the indifference value of the so-called indifference value of the complete information game. The indifference value is the ratio of the optimal social welfare to the minimum social welfare that can be achieved under a given type of equilibrium. The literature on this subject is quite extensive and largely shows the limits of the indifference value for weakly connected equilibria. However, these results do not exist in the world of incomplete information games, and only the indifference value limits of the Bayes-Nash equilibrium exist.

The main contribution of this paper is to provide the limits of the indifference value for widely connected equilibria. The way to do this is to reduce these games to a few complete information games.
