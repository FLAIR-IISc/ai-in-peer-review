- Paper goal: extend price of anarchy (PoA) bounds from the smoothness framework to learning outcomes in Bayesian games by modeling a Bayesian game as a stochastic complete-information population game.  
- Main theoretical results: (1) no-regret learning in the stochastic game yields convergence to a variant of Bayesian coarse correlated equilibrium (BCCE); (2) smoothness of the underlying game/mechanism implies smoothness of the stochastic game, so PoA bounds carry over.  
- Writing and presentation: manuscript is hard to read and evaluate.  
- Audience fit: more appropriate for the Game Theory community than NIPS, based on references and tone.  
- Presentation critique: paper reads like a “cut-and-paste” from an extended version and lacks detail in the convergence analysis section.  
- Missing supplementary proofs: supplementary material only contains proofs for Theorems 10 and 13 — proofs for other theorems/lemmas are absent.  
- Equilibrium choice question: unclear why the correlated equilibrium notion used is the one defined by Forges.  
- Theorem clarity: statement of Theorem 11 is unclear—need explicit clarification on whether it implies convergence to BCCE.  
- Practicality concern: relying on correlated equilibria assumes a central entity or coordination; question whether this realistically models practical scenarios.  
- Overall summary: contribution is showing PoA bounds via smoothness extend to learning in Bayesian games, but paper’s fit and presentation for NIPS are questionable.
