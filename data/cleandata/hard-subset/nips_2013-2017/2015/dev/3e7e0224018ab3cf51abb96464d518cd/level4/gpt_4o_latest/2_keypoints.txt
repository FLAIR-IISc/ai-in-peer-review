- Background: Price of anarchy bounds exist for full-information games under smoothness; prior work on incomplete-information games only covers Bayes-Nash equilibria.  
- Contribution: Paper claims a bound on the price of anarchy for coarse correlated equilibria in incomplete-information games via reduction to full-information games.  
- Readability and scope: Paper is hard to read and appears to lack generality; even the problem setup is not entirely clear.  
- Specific clarity issue: Definition of empirical distributions Fi is unclear—uncertain which samples define these distributions.  
- Major technical concern (Lemma 10): Hypotheses are poorly articulated; D^T is a random measure so convergence in distribution needs clarification (e.g., whether it is almost sure).  
- Subsequence ambiguity: It’s unclear whether a convergent subsequence must be the same across all realizations of D^T.  
- Questionable limit interchange (Equation 10): The claim that E_{s∼D^T}[f_T(s)] → E_{s∼D}[f(s)] from f_T→f a.s. and D^T→D in distribution is not justified and may be false without stronger conditions.  
- Overall judgment: Rest of the paper appears correct; if Lemma 10 issues are fixed, reviewer might recommend acceptance, but doubts remain about fit for NIPS and possible proof error.
