Proposes a novel mechanism for purchasing sequential participants' information, usable for private data points or richer information.

Leverages prediction-market principles, operates with a bounded budget, and minimizes generalization error for Bregman divergence losses.

Provides a privacy-preserving modification ensuring current prices and predictions reveal almost no information about any single participant (differential privacy).

Builds on prior work (e.g., Abernethy et al.) and connects to prediction markets, crowdsourcing contests, and differential privacy literature.

Introduces innovations including conditional markets, nonparametric securities, and integrated privacy guarantees.

Mechanism is designed to be efficient, incentive-compatible, and private, with theoretical performance guarantees.

Strengths: novel incentivized data aggregation, privacy guarantees, solid theoretical analysis, and clear, well-organized presentation.

Weaknesses: mechanism complexity and potential scalability issues.

Assumes participants have access to private samples from the true underlying distribution, which may not always hold.

Relies on multiple parameters and hyperparameters that may require careful tuning in practice.

Paper could benefit from more experimental evaluations and comparisons to existing methods.

Reviewer recommends acceptance but asks authors to address the noted concerns.

Reported scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8.5/10.
