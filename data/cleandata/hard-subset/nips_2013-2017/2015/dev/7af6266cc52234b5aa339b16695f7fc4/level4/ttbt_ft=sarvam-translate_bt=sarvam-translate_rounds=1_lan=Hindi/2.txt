Summary: The authors propose optimizing tools from the design and analysis of prediction markets to solve the problem of learning a good hypothesis when training data is distributed across multiple parties. The authors also propose modifications to guarantee differential privacy. In more detail, the authors propose maintaining a current hypothesis, allowing participants to update this hypothesis (considering various "betting languages" to do so), and charging/rewarding participants after applying the final hypothesis to a test data point using a family of convex cost functions (parameters by a domain element x). (The choice of cost functions is determined by the loss function.) Differential privacy is added by adapting state-of-the-art techniques to the "continuous observation" model (where privacy is required at each step of time, without linearly paying for the number of time steps). Quality: Combining different models and techniques has been done efficiently. The paper on motivation seems a bit weak. Clarity: The quality of writing is reasonably good. Originality: None of the tools used are original. Some of their combinations seem original here.
