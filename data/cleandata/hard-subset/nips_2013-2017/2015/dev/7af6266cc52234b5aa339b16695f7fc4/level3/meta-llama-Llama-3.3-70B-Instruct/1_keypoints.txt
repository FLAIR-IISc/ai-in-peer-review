- Proposes a novel approach to adaptive stochastic optimization (ASO) over sets and paths using ideas from machine learning.
- Introduces two conditions, the marginal likelihood rate bound (MLRB) and the marginal likelihood bound (MLB), to enable efficient ASO approximation for pointwise submodular objectives.
- Proposes the Recursive Adaptive Coverage (RAC) algorithm with near-optimal guarantees when the objective satisfies MLRB/MLB and is pointwise monotone submodular.
- Generalizes prior adaptive submodular optimization results to path domains, expanding applicability.
- Applies the method to two robot planning tasks under uncertainty and reports promising empirical results.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis and performance guarantees for the proposed algorithm.
- Paper is well-written and well-structured, clearly presenting the approach and limitations.
- Limitation: approach depends on timely availability of correct true samples, which may be impractical if samples arrive much later.
- Limitation: proposed market algorithm requires a known loss function, reducing practicality when prediction targets are unknown.
- Risk: the system can be gamed if past true samples or prediction divergence measures are used to value new training data, creating a catch-22 for cost setup.
- Needs further experimentation and evaluation to fully demonstrate practical effectiveness.
