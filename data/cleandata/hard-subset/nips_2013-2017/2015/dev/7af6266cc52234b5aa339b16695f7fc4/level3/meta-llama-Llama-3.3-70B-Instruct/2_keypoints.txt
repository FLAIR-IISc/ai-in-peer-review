- Introduces two conditions (MLRB and MLB) enabling efficient approximate solutions for adaptive stochastic optimization (ASO).  
- Proposes RAC, a polynomial-time Recursive Adaptive Coverage algorithm with near-optimal guarantees over sets and paths.  
- Empirically evaluated on two robot planning tasks under uncertainty, showing practical effectiveness.  
- Integrates various models and techniques into a comprehensive ASO framework.  
- Writing is clear and easy to follow overall, despite an untraditional narrative.  
- Results are modestly significant and connect to learning with distributed data and differential privacy.  
- Motivation for the approach is weak and could be strengthened.  
- Narrative style is untraditional and may be hard for some readers to follow.  
- Theoretical tools used are not novel, though some combinations are new.  
- Theoretical analysis needs more detailed explanation.  
- Experimental results could be more comprehensive and better explained.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, viewing the paper as a significant contribution to ASO.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
