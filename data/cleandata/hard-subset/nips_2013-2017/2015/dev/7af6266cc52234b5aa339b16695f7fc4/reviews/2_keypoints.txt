- Proposal: adapt tools from prediction market design/analysis to learn hypotheses when training data is distributed across many parties.  
- Additions: propose modifications to guarantee differential privacy.  
- Mechanism: maintain a current hypothesis and allow participants to update it via various "betting languages."  
- Payments: use a family of convex cost functions (parameterized by domain element x) to charge/reward participants after the final hypothesis is applied to a test point.  
- Design detail: the choice of cost functions is determined by the loss function.  
- Differential privacy approach: adapt continual-observation privacy techniques to avoid linear privacy costs over time.  
- Quality: the integration of models and techniques is competently executed.  
- Motivation: paper is weak on motivating the problem and framing why it matters.  
- Narrative/impact: results feel like a collection of feasible techniques ("here's what we know how to do") rather than a strongly motivated, comparative solution.  
- Clarity: writing quality is reasonably good.  
- Originality: individual tools are not new, though some combinations appear novel.  
- Significance: results are of modest significance.
