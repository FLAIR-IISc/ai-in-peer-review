- Problem: tests whether a discrete distribution p belongs to a class C or is far in total variation distance.  
- Contribution: a general framework producing sample-optimal, computationally efficient testers for many distribution classes.  
- Core technique: novel use of the χ^2-statistic to achieve optimal sample complexity.  
- Covered classes: monotonicity, independence, log-concavity, unimodality, and monotone hazard rate (MHR).  
- Optimality: matching lower bounds are proved for all considered classes.  
- Learning result: provides computationally efficient proper learning algorithms for discrete log-concave and MHR distributions.  
- Improvement over prior work: removes logarithmic factors, tightens sample complexity, and handles low-sample computational efficiency.  
- Strength: technically deep, novel, broadly applicable, and well-organized with detailed proofs.  
- Weakness: presentation is dense in places (notably the χ^2-statistic application) and could use more intuition/examples.  
- Weakness: no empirical validation or experiments to assess practical performance.  
- Weakness: comparisons to recent related work (e.g., Canonne et al. [32]) could be more detailed.  
- Recommendation & scores: recommend acceptance; Quality 9/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
