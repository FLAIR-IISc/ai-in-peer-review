- Main contribution: presents sample-optimal, computationally efficient testers for several shape-restricted discrete distribution classes.  
- Key technique: novel use of the L2 statistic enabling optimal sample complexity.  
- Tested properties: monotonicity, log-concavity, monotone hazard rate (MHR), and unimodality.  
- Organization and exposition: paper is well-organized with a clear overview, consistent notation, and helpful definitions.  
- Writing: generally clear but some sections are dense and require careful reading.  
- Technical thoroughness: provides detailed proofs and matching lower bounds demonstrating optimality.  
- Related work: includes discussion and clear comparison to existing results.  
- Concern on stats literature: authors downplay statistics work inaccurately—many classical tests can be made exact via null distribution simulation.  
- Finite-sample claim: claim of a “true finite sample” test is unconvincing since the test still assumes sufficiently large samples to control Type I error.  
- Practicality of domain: using a discrete cube for monotonicity testing may be impractical; applicability to continuous variables is unclear.  
- Overall assessment: significant and innovative contribution but limitations and claims need careful qualification.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—address the statistical literature critique, finite-sample claim, practical implications, and potential extensions to continuous settings.
