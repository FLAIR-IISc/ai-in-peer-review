- Introduces Bayesian active model selection (BAMS) that selects queries by mutual information between observation and model posterior.  
- Major advantage: candidate points can be evaluated without retraining models.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and organized technical sections.  
- Applies BAMS to Gaussian processes with a clear explanation of practical use.  
- Derives an analytical approximation to the BAMS criterion using a Laplace approximation to model evidence and hyperparameter posterior.  
- Approximation enables efficient computation of mutual information and automatic learning of GP model class with arbitrary observation likelihoods.  
- Demonstrates strong real-world results on diagnosing noise-induced hearing loss, requiring far fewer samples and enabling real-time diagnosis.  
- Unclear assumptions/limitations regarding restrictions to monotonic, log-concave, unimodal, and monotonic hazard rate distributions need clarification.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art active model selection methods.  
- May need additional experimental results to establish robustness across settings.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, impactful approach; clear exposition; practical, real-world validation.  
- Rejection concerns: unclear limitations, insufficient comparisons, and potentially limited empirical validation.
