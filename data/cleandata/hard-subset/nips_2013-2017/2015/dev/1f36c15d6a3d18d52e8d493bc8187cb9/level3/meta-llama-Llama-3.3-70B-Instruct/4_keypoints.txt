- Introduces Bayesian Active Model Selection (BAMS) that chooses the next observation by maximizing mutual information between the observation and model posterior.  
- Key idea: avoid costly model retraining using a Laplace approximation to the hyperparameter posterior and a marginal Gaussian process (MGP) approximation to the predictive distribution.  
- BAMS framework is general and applicable to various models, notably Gaussian processes with arbitrary observation likelihoods.  
- Demonstrated strong empirical performance on a real-world rapid screening task for noise-induced hearing loss (NIHL), requiring far fewer samples than existing methods.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and provides clear, concise explanations of methods and derivations.  
- Experiments are well-designed, thoroughly evaluated, and supported by a comprehensive related-work review.  
- Strength: balances theoretical rigor with practical application and highlights the importance of model selection in ML/statistics.  
- Weakness: lacks more detailed empirical comparisons to other active learning and model selection methods, especially for NIHL.  
- Limitation: reliance on a Gaussian process model may reduce applicability to domains where GPs are unsuitable.  
- Suggestion: explore extensions or evaluations in other domains (e.g., image classification, NLP) to demonstrate broader applicability.  
- Meets conference criteria for a strong scientific contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
