- Proposes Bayesian active model selection (BAMS) that selects observations by maximizing mutual information between observations and the model posterior.  
- Key technical contribution is an analytical approximation to the BAMS criterion enabling efficient computation without model retraining.  
- Applies BAMS to Gaussian process models with sound technical development.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, background, and a thorough related-work review distinguishing the method from existing approaches.  
- Experiments demonstrate strong performance on diagnosing noise-induced hearing loss with few observations and include a detailed, intuitive analysis of the sampling strategy.  
- Assumes a discrete distribution whose support grows with observations — an uncommon statistical assumption that requires more discussion and justification.  
- Incorrect claim that any log-concave distribution is unimodal; the paper’s definition of log-concavity permits counterexamples and needs correction.  
- Lacks a clear explanation of the distinction between continuous and discrete cases.  
- Citation to Birgé (1987) is misleading since it addresses density estimation; the paper should state the correct rate for testing log-concavity and clarify relevance.  
- Overall, a significant and promising contribution but requires revisions to address the statistical assumptions, log-concavity errors, and citation/continuity clarifications.
