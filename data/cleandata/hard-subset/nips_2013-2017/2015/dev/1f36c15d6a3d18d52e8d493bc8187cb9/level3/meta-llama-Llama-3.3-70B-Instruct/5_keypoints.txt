- Proposes BAMS: a Bayesian active model selection framework that selects next data by maximizing mutual information between observation and model posterior.  
- Key efficiency insight: does not require retraining models for each candidate point.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- Demonstrated on noise-induced hearing loss (NIHL) diagnosis with high accuracy.  
- Achieves diagnostic performance using significantly fewer samples than existing methods.  
- Method is theoretically sound with supporting analysis and reproducible experiments.  
- Original contribution: novel active model selection approach that combines familiar techniques in a new way.  
- Significant potential impact for NIHL diagnosis and broader ML/statistics applications.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical relevance of the testing problems and extensions to non-discrete distributions.  
- Weakness: needs more comparisons to existing methods and a more detailed analysis of results.  
- Weakness: some notation/terminology may be unfamiliar to non-experts.  
- Overall recommendation from reviewer: accept.
