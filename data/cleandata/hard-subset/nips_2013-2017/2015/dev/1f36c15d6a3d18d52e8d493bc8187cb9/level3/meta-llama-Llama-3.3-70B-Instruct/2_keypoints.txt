- Introduces Bayesian active model selection (BAMS), an information-theoretic method for active model selection.  
- Applies BAMS to noise-induced hearing loss (NIHL) diagnosis, selecting observations via mutual information with the model posterior.  
- Shows BAMS can diagnose NIHL with fewer samples than existing approaches.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly explains the NIHL diagnosis problem.  
- Provides a thorough related-work review and clearly distinguishes the approach from prior methods.  
- Technical contributions are significant, with detailed derivation and application to Gaussian process models.  
- Novelty: evaluates candidate points without needing model retraining.  
- Delivers an efficient analytic approximation to the BAMS criterion for GPs with arbitrary observation likelihoods.  
- Empirical results demonstrate sample-efficiency gains in the NIHL application.  
- Weakness: assumes a specific form for the observation likelihood, which may limit applicability.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparisons to other active learning methods (e.g., uncertainty sampling, query-by-committee).  
- Overall assessment: well-written and promising, but needs broader evaluation across scenarios and more comparative baselines.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically strong method with effective empirical results and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive likelihood assumption, incomplete empirical comparisons, and need for further validation.
