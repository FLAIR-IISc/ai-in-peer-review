- Presents a novel, comprehensive framework for testing membership in discrete distribution classes (monotone, log-concave, unimodal, MHR) with both upper and lower sample-complexity bounds.  
- Introduces a novel use of the χ² statistic to achieve optimal sample complexity in the small-sample regime.  
- Provides matching lower bounds, establishing the tightness of the proposed testers.  
- Tackles multiple distribution classes and multi-dimensional independence testing, increasing the work’s breadth and significance.  
- Well-written with thorough comparisons to prior work, showing clear improvements in sample and computational complexity.  
- Validated empirically via simulations on synthetic data, supporting theoretical claims.  
- Limitation: restricted to discrete distributions with no discussion of extensions to continuous settings or real-world deployment.  
- Limitation: some basic definitions are missing or placed in the appendix, reducing self-containment and accessibility.  
- Limitation: paper does not explore concrete real-world applications or practical extensions, limiting practical relevance.  
- Argument for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution with sample-optimal testers and matching lower bounds.  
- Argument for acceptance: methodological breakthrough via χ² use in small-sample regime.  
- Argument against acceptance: narrow focus on discrete distributions and lack of discussion on continuous/practical extensions.  
- Argument against acceptance: reliance on appendix for foundational definitions may hinder accessibility.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and address practical relevance.
