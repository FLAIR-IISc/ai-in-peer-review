- Presents sample-optimal, computationally efficient testers for many shape-restricted discrete distributions.
- Novel use of the L2 statistic enables achieving optimal sample complexity.
- Theoretical analysis and (claimed) experimental results support the main claims.
- Provides a general framework for testing class membership versus being far from a class.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on monotone, log-concave, monotone hazard rate, and unimodal distributions.
- Paper is well-written, with detailed and well-organized technical sections.
- Delivers the first sample-optimal testers for many considered classes.
- Provides matching lower bounds that establish optimality of the testers.
- Efficiently handles high-dimensional distributions, notably testing monotonicity in high dimensions.
- The tester can serve as a useful subroutine for future distribution-testing algorithms.
- Assumes discrete distributions, which may limit direct applicability to continuous settings (though discretization is possible).
- Reviewer notes a lack of experimental validation of practical effectiveness and limited direct applicability to all areas of statistics/ML.
