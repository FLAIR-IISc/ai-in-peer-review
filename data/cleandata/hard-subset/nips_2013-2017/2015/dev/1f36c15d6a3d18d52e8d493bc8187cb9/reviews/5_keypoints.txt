- Proposes a new method and derives new bounds for testing membership in distribution classes (e.g., monotone, log-concave, uni-modal) for discrete distributions.  
- Provides both upper and lower bounds for the considered classes and shows the upper bounds are essentially tight.  
- Includes simulations on synthetic data to evaluate the proposed algorithm.  
- Paper is generally well written and the results appear correct.  
- Authors give adequate comparison to prior bounds and carefully discuss regimes where their rates improve.  
- Main concern: analysis is restricted to discrete distributions only.  
- Suggestion: add motivational discussion on whether these testing problems arise in practice.  
- Suggestion: discuss potential extensions to non-discrete (continuous) distributions, possibly under density assumptions.  
- Minor issue: paper is not fully self-contained—some basic definitions referenced as being in the appendix are missing.
