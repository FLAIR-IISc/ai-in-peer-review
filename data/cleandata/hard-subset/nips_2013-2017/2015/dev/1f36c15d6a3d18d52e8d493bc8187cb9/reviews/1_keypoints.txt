- Overall positive: reviewer likes the paper and finds the results interesting.
- Practical relevance concern: testing monotonicity on a discrete cube seems unrealistic—asks for real-data or scientific examples where this setting matters.
- Literature critique: dismissing statistics literature is misleading; many classical tests can be made exact by simulating the null and asymptotics are used to get precise power results.
- Request for references: reviewer demands more statistical citations (gives Dümbgen & Walther 2008 as an example).
- Finite-sample claim questioned: the existence of N0 with Type I error < α only for N>N0 feels asymptotic, so the test may not be practically finite-sample verifiable.
- Summary concern: not convinced the problem arises in practice and statistical connections are too readily dismissed.
