- Problem: testing whether an unknown distribution belongs to monotone, log-concave, unimodal, or monotone hazard rate classes (over [n], or [n]^d for monotonicity).  
- Method: two-step framework — estimate q assuming p lies in the class, then apply a modified chi-squared test to compare p and q.  
- Novelty: the estimators for the first step are novel and tailored to each distribution class.  
- Advantage: this avoids costly alternatives (e.g., testing against a net) and yields efficient algorithms with improved/optimal sample complexity.  
- Remarkable finding: the optimal sample complexity rates for the four classes are identical (for d=1 monotone), and the same construction is used for the matching lower bounds.  
- Presentation issue: paper should include definitions (e.g., partial order for hypergrid monotonicity); promised distance definitions in the appendix appear missing.  
- Overall: paper makes significant contributions to testing shape-constrained discrete distributions.
