- Studies testing whether an unknown distribution belongs to monotone, log-concave, unimodal, or monotone hazard rate classes over [n] (and [n]^d for monotonicity).
- Proposes a unified two-step framework: (1) obtain an initial estimate q assuming p is in the class to reduce sample complexity, (2) run a modified chi-squared test to check closeness of p and q.
- Constructs novel, class-specific estimators for the initial-estimate step.
- Avoids costly alternatives (e.g., testing equality to a net over the class), reducing computational and statistical overhead.
- Produces efficient algorithms with improved—and optimal—sample complexities.
- Shows the optimal rates are identical across the four classes (for d=1 in monotone case), with the same lower-bound construction used for each.
- Suggests the paper needs clearer definitions for some concepts (e.g., partial order for hypergrid monotonicity) and missing distance definitions referenced in the appendix.
- Overall, the paper makes substantial contributions to testing shape-constrained discrete distributions.
