This paper introduces a new method and new limits to determine whether any distribution belongs to a specific class (e.g., unimodel, log-convex, one-mode). The authors provide both upper and lower bounds for the class they consider (which shows that their upper bounds are essentially strict). They also provide some simulations on synthetic data for their algorithm.

The paper is generally well-written, and the results seem accurate. The authors also provide sufficient comparisons with previous limits and carefully discuss under what circumstances their results outperform them.

My main concern about this work is that it only considers discrete distributions. It would be better if the authors had provided some encouraging discussion on whether the actual test problems they considered actually occur in practice. Furthermore, it would be good to see some discussion on whether the results could be extended to non-discrete distributions under certain assumptions related to density.

Minor comment: The authors could have tried harder to make it a standalone text. For example, on page 3, they point readers to an appendix for some basic definitions, but those definitions are not in the appendix.
