- Introduces a novel method and new bounds for testing membership in distribution classes (monotone, log-concave, unimodal).
- Provides both upper and lower bounds, with upper bounds shown to be nearly tight.
- Includes simulations on synthetic data to evaluate algorithm performance.
- Paper is well-written and results appear correct.
- Sufficient comparisons with prior bounds and clear discussion of regimes where rates improve.
- Major concern: work is restricted to discrete distributions; needs motivational discussion of practical relevance.
- Suggestion: explore extensions to non-discrete distributions, possibly under density assumptions.
- Minor issue: paper lacks promised basic definitions in the appendix, reducing self-containedness.
