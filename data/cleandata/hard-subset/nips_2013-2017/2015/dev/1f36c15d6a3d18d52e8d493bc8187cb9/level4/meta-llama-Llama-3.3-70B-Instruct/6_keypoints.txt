- Manuscript studies sample complexity to distinguish an unknown discrete distribution p from a class C (monotone, log-concave, unimodal, monotone hazard rate) under TV distance ≥ ε.  
- Motivation: Need clearer justification for imposing shape constraints on discrete distributions since ordinal structure may not naturally apply to many discrete settings.  
- B1: Theorem 1’s proof must explicitly state/invoke independence between observed samples X_i and any newly drawn distribution q used in the argument.  
- B2: In the monotone case, application of Lemma 5 is flawed because it provides E[χ²(p,q)] rather than the actual χ²(p,q) needed for Theorem 1.  
- B3: Distinction between continuous and discrete settings (and relation to Birgé 1987) is unclear, since Birgé mainly addresses density estimation in continuous domains.  
- B4: Claim that any log-concave distribution is unimodal is incorrect under the provided definitions; counterexamples exist.  
- B5: The stated testing rate for log-concavity O(√n/ε²) lacks precise conditions, especially as ε varies.  
- Connections to statistics: The discrete [n] support assumption is more CS-focused; relevant statistical work (e.g., Jankowski & Wellner 2009, Balabdaoui et al. 2013) on discrete monotone/log-concave estimation/testing should be cited and connected.  
- Novelty and rigor: Builds on Valiant & Valiant (2014) but in substantially different settings; main contributions are theoretical and interesting but require more rigorous proof verification.
