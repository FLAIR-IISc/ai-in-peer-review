- Positive overall: paper is engaging and presents interesting results.  
- Concern 1: Using a discrete cube sample space may lack practical relevance; request a concrete scientific example where this setting is important.  
- Concern 2: The claim that statistics focuses mainly on large-sample regimes is misleading; many tests use simulated nulls and asymptotics often give precise limits, not just bounds.  
- Concern 3: References to statistical literature are sparse and should be expanded (e.g., Dümbgen, Lutz, and G. Walther, 2008).  
- Concern 4: Doubt that the proposed test is truly finite-sample—results imply a sample-size threshold N0 for type I error control, which mirrors asymptotic statements.  
- Concern 5: In practice there is no guarantee the sample exceeds N0, so the distinction from asymptotic tests appears negligible.  
- Overall judgment: intriguing work but practical relevance is questionable and connections to existing statistical literature are dismissed too readily.
