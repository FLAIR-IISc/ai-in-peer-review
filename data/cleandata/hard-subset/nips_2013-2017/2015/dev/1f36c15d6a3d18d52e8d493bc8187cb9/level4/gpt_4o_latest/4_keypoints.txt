- Addresses testing whether an unknown distribution belongs to shape-constrained classes (monotone, log-concave, unimodal, MHR) over [n] (and [n]^d for monotonicity).  
- Proposes a two-step framework: estimate q under the class assumption to reduce sample complexity, then run a modified chi-squared test to check closeness to p.  
- Develops innovative, class-specific estimators for the first step.  
- Two-step approach avoids costly alternatives (e.g., testing against a net) and yields efficient algorithms with improved/optimal sample complexity.  
- Shows identical (optimal) rates for all four classes in the d=1 monotone case, using the same construction for matching lower bounds.  
- Notes missing/unclear definitions that would aid clarity (partial order for hypergrid monotonicity; referenced distance definitions in appendix are absent).  
- Concludes the paper makes substantial contributions to testing shape-constrained discrete distributions.
