- Introduces a novel approach and new bounds for testing whether a distribution belongs to classes like monotone, log-concave, or uni-modal.  
- Provides comprehensive analysis with both upper and lower bounds; upper bounds are essentially tight.  
- Validates the algorithm via simulations on synthetic data.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and results appear correct.  
- Offers a thorough comparison to existing bounds and discusses regimes where their rates improve.  
- Major limitation: analysis is restricted to discrete distributions only.  
- Recommendation: add motivational discussion on the practical relevance of the addressed testing problems.  
- Recommendation: discuss potential extensions to non-discrete distributions, possibly under density assumptions.  
- Minor issue: manuscript should be more self-contained—definitions referenced on page 3 are not actually in the appendix.  
- Overall: presents a new method and bounds with well-presented results.
