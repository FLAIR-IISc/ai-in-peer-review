This paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) that addresses the propensity overfitting problem. The authors build upon their previous work, Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), and provide a significantly improved algorithm.

The quality of the research presented in the paper is above average. The proposed extensions result in substantial improvements in training the original POEM algorithm. The authors have made a concerted effort to provide a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and its implications on BLBF.

For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow. The authors have done an excellent job of explaining the technical details of their approach, making the paper accessible to a broad audience. The organization of the paper is logical, and the writing style is concise and free of unnecessary jargon.

The paper's originality is one of its strongest aspects. The authors provide a significantly new formulation to the original POEM algorithm for the BLBF problem. The introduction of the self-normalized risk estimator and its application to BLBF is a novel contribution that addresses a critical issue in the field.

The significance of the results presented in the paper is substantial. The authors demonstrate that their proposed algorithm, Norm-POEM, outperforms the original POEM in various experiments. Moreover, they show that Norm-POEM is computationally efficient, making it a viable solution for practical applications. The fact that Norm-POEM is faster than the original POEM despite having more regularization mechanisms built into it is a notable achievement.

Overall, this is a good paper that is clear and well-written. The authors have made a valuable contribution to the field of BLBF, and their work is likely to have a significant impact on the development of new algorithms and techniques.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper addresses a critical issue in BLBF, namely the propensity overfitting problem.
* The proposed algorithm, Norm-POEM, provides significant improvements in training and generalization performance.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the technical details of their approach.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
* The results presented in the paper are substantial and have the potential to impact the development of new algorithms and techniques.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper may benefit from additional experiments to further demonstrate the efficacy of Norm-POEM.
* Some readers may find the technical details of the paper challenging to follow, particularly in sections 4 and 5.
* The authors could provide more discussion on the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research.

Rating: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
