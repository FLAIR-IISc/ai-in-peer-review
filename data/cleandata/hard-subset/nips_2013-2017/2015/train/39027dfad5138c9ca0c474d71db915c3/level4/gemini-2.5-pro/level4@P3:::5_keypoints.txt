- Identifies a novel failure mode called "Propensity Overfitting" where IPS can be exploited by models to achieve artificially low empirical risk.  
- Provides a simple, intuitive example demonstrating how IPS can be manipulated by assigning extreme probabilities to logged actions.  
- Proposes replacing IPS with a self-normalized (SN) estimator motivated by multiplicative control variates.  
- Shows SN estimator is equivariant to loss shifts and bounded within observed loss range, addressing IPS anomalies.  
- Instantiates the SN estimator in a new algorithm, Norm-POEM, for CRM-based learning.  
- Empirically demonstrates Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM across multi-label classification tasks.  
- Includes diagnostic experiments showing Norm-POEM keeps the sum of weights near the expected value (mitigating propensity overfitting).  
- Demonstrates robustness of Norm-POEM to loss translation and explains faster convergence due to smaller-norm solutions.  
- Notes SN objective precludes stochastic optimization, requiring batch optimizers (e.g., L-BFGS), raising potential scalability concerns.  
- Suggests scalability limitations could matter for extremely large datasets and asks about possible approximations or alternative optimizers.  
- Points out evaluation follows Supervised-to-Bandit protocol on multi-label tasks but recommends testing on native bandit settings (e.g., ads or recommendations).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem identification, principled solution, strong theoretical motivation, thorough diagnostics, and clear empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: viewed as incremental over POEM and reliance on batch optimization may limit scalability.  
- Overall recommendation: strong endorsement for acceptance based on clear conceptual contribution and convincing empirical validation.
