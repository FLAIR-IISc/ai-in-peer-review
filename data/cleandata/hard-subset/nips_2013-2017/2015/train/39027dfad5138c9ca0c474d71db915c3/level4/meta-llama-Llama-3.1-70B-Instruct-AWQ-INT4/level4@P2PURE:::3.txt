Summary
The paper addresses the problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, which is particularly relevant in applications such as ad ranking. It identifies an "overfitting" issue in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a solution using multiplicative control variates, resulting in a biased estimator.

Quality
The paper is of high quality and well-motivated. However, as it builds upon previous work [1], I would have expected additional experiments and a more in-depth analysis of the optimization of the training objective, providing more details than those presented in the paragraph on line 415.

Clarity
The presentation is clear, and the paper is well-written.

Originality
While the paper is a follow-up to [1], it introduces a significant modification to the risk estimator by making it self-normalizing, which appears to substantially impact the experimental results.

Significance
The problem of learning from logged data is highly relevant, but it is challenging to determine how the experimental improvements will translate to more real-world applications.

Various Remarks
Line 365: It would be beneficial to include a measure of variance, in addition to the results of multiple repetitions. The paper successfully identifies a problem in the CRM principle and proposes a solution. Overall, the paper is solid, well-written, and the work is relevant and novel. Nevertheless, I would have appreciated extended experiments, additional applications beyond those in [1], and ideally, some actual real-world applications to further demonstrate the impact of the proposed method.
