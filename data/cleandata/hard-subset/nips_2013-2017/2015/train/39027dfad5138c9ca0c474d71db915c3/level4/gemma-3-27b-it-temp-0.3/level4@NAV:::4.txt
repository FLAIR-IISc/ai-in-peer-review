== Other comments ==
- The setup for “batch learning from logged bandit feedback” in Section 3 (and in [1]) appears to treat feedback as a deterministic function on line 110. I believe it should be modeled as a random variable. Consider a scenario where feedback originates from a classification problem with unknown labels, where loss is observed through prediction and comparison to a true label. If Delta(y', y) represents the true classification loss, and delta(x, y) corresponds to Delta(y', y) for a given y', the same input x and played action y could yield different delta(x, y) values due to potential noise in the labeling process. Therefore, delta(x, y) should generally be considered a random variable, with its mean representing the expected loss given the true, potentially noisy, labeling distribution p(y'|x). While this may not affect the risk estimator (2), the setup in (1) defining the true risk should include an expectation over the randomness of delta for greater generality.
- Line 170-171: The expression should likely be h(y|x) / h_0(y|x). Additionally, it would be beneficial to acknowledge that the lack of linearity was previously identified in [1].
- Line 276: Replace “argmax” with “argmin” as the objective is risk minimization.
- Line 319: Maintain consistency with prior notation by replacing bars with hats.
