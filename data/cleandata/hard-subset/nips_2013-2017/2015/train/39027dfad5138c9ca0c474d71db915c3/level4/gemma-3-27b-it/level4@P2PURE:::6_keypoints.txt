- Counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) handles bias in learning from logged bandit data.
- The paper identifies an overfitting problem where hypotheses overfit to the data-generation/logging probabilities.
- To mitigate this, the authors introduce a multiplicative parameter that bounds variance while preserving consistency.
- They propose a self-normalized estimator tailored for counterfactual learning in logged bandit settings.
- The method specifically addresses a novel overfitting mode where the hypothesis unduly favors logged data.
- Experiments show the normalized counterfactual technique outperforms the non-normalized baseline.
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance, though some uncertainty remains.
