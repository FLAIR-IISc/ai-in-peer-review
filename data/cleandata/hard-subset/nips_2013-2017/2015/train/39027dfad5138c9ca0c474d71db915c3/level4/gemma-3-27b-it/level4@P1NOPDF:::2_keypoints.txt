- Notation and commentary around equation (2) are incorrect or misleading regarding what constitutes an expectation.  
- The loss in (2) is not the expected quantity, so the claim that (2) shows an “anomaly” from lack of translation invariance is technically inaccurate.  
- Example 1 is based on that flawed premise and therefore does not hold.  
- The estimator in (2) is sensitive to its denominator π, which can be zero for previously unobserved samples.  
- The modified estimator in (7) seems superior in principle because the double denominator mitigates effects of π.  
- Reviewer requests the authors revise their commentary if the paper is accepted.  
- Experimental results show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and performs close to a CRF trained with full multi-label information.  
- The paper frames “counterfactual learning” as a specialization of multi-label learning where feedback is single-label/bandit-like rather than full 0/1 annotations.  
- The core contribution is improving the POEM estimator (ICML 2015); overall the reviewer finds the paper convincing and worthy of publication, with several specific remarks.
