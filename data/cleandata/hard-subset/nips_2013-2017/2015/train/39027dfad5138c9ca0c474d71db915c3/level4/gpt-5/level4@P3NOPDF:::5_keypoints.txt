- Proposes a BLBF method that mitigates propensity overfitting by substantially revising POEM with additional regularization.  
- Claims improved training stability and performance, and faster training despite added regularization.  
- Technical quality is above average with a clear problem statement and well-motivated solution.  
- Empirical results indicate meaningful optimization and generalization improvements over POEM.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized; minor exposition additions would help.  
- Provides a significant and original reformulation of POEM for the BLBF setting.  
- Addresses an important practical problem with potential broad impact in recommendation/search.  
- Strength: principled regularizer targeting propensity overfitting.  
- Strength: conceptually appealing revised formulation with strong empirical effectiveness.  
- Strength: demonstrated improvements in training efficiency despite extra regularization.  
- Strength: generally strong presentation and organization.  
- Weakness: needs deeper intuition and theoretical guarantees (e.g., generalization bounds, consistency).  
- Weakness: lacks sensitivity analyses and ablation studies for hyperparameters and components.  
- Weakness: limited baselines—should compare to other counterfactual estimators and regularization schemes.  
- Weakness: unclear assumptions on logging policy and robustness to misspecified/noisy propensities.  
- Weakness: requires clearer description of per-iteration complexity, memory, and why it is faster than POEM.  
- Weakness: reproducibility would improve with code and detailed experimental settings.  
- Clarity suggestions: define BLBF and “propensity overfitting” upfront, keep consistent notation, and explicitly show how the objective differs from POEM (derivation/pseudocode).  
- Practical suggestion: discuss failure modes (e.g., extreme propensity sparsity) and recommended practitioner practices.  
- Overall: a good, convincing, and practical advance over POEM that would benefit from stronger theory and broader empirical analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
