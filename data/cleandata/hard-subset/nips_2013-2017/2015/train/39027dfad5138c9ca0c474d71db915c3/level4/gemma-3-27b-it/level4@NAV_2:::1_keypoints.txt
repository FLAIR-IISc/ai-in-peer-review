- Identifies a significant problem in BLBF: "propensity overfitting" in counterfactual risk estimators.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (using control variates) to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Builds on CRM principle and frames BLBF as a causal inference problem.
- Connects the work to off-policy evaluation in RL and domain adaptation, situating it in prior literature.
- Points out that importance sampling methods are vulnerable to a distinct form of overfitting beyond loss overfitting.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm using the self-normalized estimator.
- Provides theoretical justification by linking to established importance sampling results.
- Empirical evaluation on multi-label classification shows consistent improvement over POEM.
- Code release enhances reproducibility and impact.
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and thoroughly connects to related work.
- Needs deeper exploration of the relationship between propensity overfitting and high variance of importance weights.
- Example illustrating propensity overfitting relies on specific conditions (distinct xi values) and could be broadened.
- Claim that prior works didn’t consider clipping/variance-reduction methods needs clarification, since many use such techniques.
- More detailed computational/runtime analysis is needed, especially regarding the cost of computing the partition function Z(x).
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor issues; accept for presentation.
