This paper presents a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that addresses the propensity overfitting problem. Building upon the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the authors provide a significantly improved algorithm.

Quality: The research presented is of high quality. The proposed extensions demonstrably lead to substantial improvements in the training of the original POEM algorithm. 

Clarity: The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and easy to understand.

Originality: The paper introduces a significantly novel formulation of the POEM algorithm specifically for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback problem. I find the contributions to be highly original.

Significance: The results are compelling and warrant further investigation. I was particularly impressed by the algorithm’s faster performance compared to the original POEM, despite incorporating additional regularization. This algorithm has the potential for widespread adoption in the near future.

Overall, this is a strong paper that is both clear and well-written.
