The commentary and notation surrounding the main equation (2) appear incorrect or at least misleading. An expectation has the form ∑x f(x) p(x): the expected quantity is f(x), while p(x) is the probability measure. In Eq. (2), the loss is clearly part of the integrand, not the measure. Therefore, the statement on page 2, second paragraph, that Eq. (2) exhibits an “anomaly” because it is not invariant to translations of the loss is technically incorrect. Example 1 then relies on this mistaken premise and, unsurprisingly, does not hold.

Practically, the estimator in (2) is fragile with respect to its denominator, π, which can be 0 if a sample has never been observed under the prior. The modified estimator in (7) seems preferable in principle because the effects of π are mollified through the double denominator.

I hope the authors will graciously revise their commentary if the paper is accepted.

The experimental results show that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and comes close to a CRF trained with full multi-label information. The paper addresses “counterfactual learning,” a learning scenario that can be seen as a specialization of multi-label learning: whereas in standard multi-label learning the ground-truth annotation provides a 0/1 label for each class, here only one class label is observed (i.e., bandit feedback). However, this serves primarily as motivation. In practice, the paper’s precise aim is to improve the POEM estimator recently introduced in [1] (ICML 2015). Overall, the paper is convincing and worthy of publication, with additional remarks provided in the “Comments to authors” field.
