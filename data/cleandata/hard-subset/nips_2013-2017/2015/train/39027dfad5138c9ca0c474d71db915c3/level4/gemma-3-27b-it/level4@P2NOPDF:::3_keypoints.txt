- Addresses important problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback (e.g., ad ranking).  
- Identifies potential overfitting in the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) framework.  
- Proposes a novel solution using multiplicative control variates that introduce bias into the risk estimator.  
- Work is high-quality, well-motivated, and a continuation of the authors’ previous paper.  
- Reviewer expected more extensive experiments and deeper analysis of the training objective’s optimization (beyond line 415).  
- Current experiments are promising but require further investigation into the optimization process to strengthen claims.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written.  
- Original contribution: self-normalizing modification of the risk estimator yields substantial experimental improvements over prior work.  
- Practical impact on real-world applications is unclear; more context on real-world relevance needed.  
- Specific request: report variance measures given repeated runs (comment on line 365).  
- Overall: solid, novel work that would benefit from more comprehensive experiments, including real-world datasets.
