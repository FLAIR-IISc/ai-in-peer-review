- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), relevant to ad ranking and recommendation systems.  
- Identifies a novel "propensity overfitting" problem within Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM).  
- Proposes a solution using multiplicative control variates that yields a self-normalized (biased) risk estimator.  
- Builds directly on the authors’ prior work and represents a clear, original refinement of existing BLBF techniques.  
- Experimental results show substantial improvements over existing methods.  
- Paper is well-written and presents complex concepts clearly and accessibly.  
- Novelty is confirmed relative to recent NIPS proceedings and related work.  
- Main weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to datasets similar to prior work [1].  
- Lacks demonstration of impact on diverse or real-world datasets to better assess practical significance.  
- Optimization analysis is brief; deeper exploration of the training objective and convergence behavior is needed.  
- Variance of experimental results across repetitions is not reported, leaving robustness unclear.  
- Overall assessment: solid, technically sound contribution worthy of consideration with recommended expansions in experiments and optimization analysis.
