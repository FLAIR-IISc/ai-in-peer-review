Counterfactual risk minimization is a method designed for learning in a bandit setting from logged data by correcting for the bias introduced by the logging policy. While it is consistent, the paper shows that it suffers from an overfitting issue in which a hypothesis class can overfit based on the example’s generation probability. The authors propose to address this with a multiplicative parameter that bounds the variance while maintaining consistency. The experimental results indicate that the multiplicative (i.e., normalized) counterfactual approach outperforms the non-normalized alternative.

The paper introduces a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting. It presents ideas for handling a specific overfitting phenomenon where the hypothesis can overfit by assigning greater support to the logged data. This is a strong paper and should be accepted, although I am not completely certain.
