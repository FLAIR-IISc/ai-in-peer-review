- Addresses overfitting in counterfactual risk minimization for learning from logged bandit data (correcting logging policy bias).  
- Shows the standard estimator is consistent but suffers high variance.  
- Identifies overfitting mechanism: hypotheses can exploit generation probabilities of logged examples.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator with a multiplicative parameter to control variance.  
- The proposed estimator bounds variance while preserving consistency.  
- Experiments show the normalized counterfactual method outperforms the non-normalized version.  
- Paper is well-motivated and tackles a subtle but important support-overreliance issue; reviewer leans toward acceptance but not fully certain.
