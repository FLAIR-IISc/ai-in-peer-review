Other comments:

- The setup for "batch learning from logged bandit feedback" in Section 3 (and also in [1]) appears to be incomplete. I believe that the feedback should be treated as a random variable rather than a deterministic function, as presented on line 110. My reasoning is as follows. Suppose that the partial feedback originates from a classification problem where the labels are unknown, and we only receive feedback by making a prediction and observing the resulting loss. Assume that there is a true classification loss Delta(y',y) and that the feedback generation process involves making a prediction y using h_0 for a given input x, while a "god agent" also labels the input as y' and provides the loss Delta(y',y) incurred (in this case, delta(x,y) is equivalent to Delta(y',y) for the given y'). However, in classification problems, it is common to assume that labeling can be noisy, resulting in non-unique labels for each input x. Consequently, elsewhere in the log, the same input x and played y may be associated with different delta(x,y) values, as the "god agent" may assign a different label. This suggests that delta(x,y) should be viewed as a random variable, with its mean representing the expectation of Delta(y',y) for y' distributed according to p(y'|x), the true noisy labeling distribution.

While this may not affect the risk estimator (2), I believe that the setup in (1) with the true risk should be revised to include an additional expectation over the randomness of delta, ensuring greater generality.

- On line 276, it would be more accurate to use argmin instead of argmax, as the risk is being minimized.
