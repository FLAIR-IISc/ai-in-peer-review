The commentary and notation surrounding the main equation (2) appear to be incorrect or misleading. An expectation is typically defined as a quantity of the form ∑f(x)p(x), where f(x) is the expected quantity and p(x) is the probability measure. In equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity, which renders the subsequent comment on page 2, second paragraph, technically incorrect. Specifically, the claim that equation (2) exhibits an "anomaly" due to its non-invariance to translation of the loss is unfounded. Consequently, Example 1, which relies on this incorrect assumption, does not hold.

Furthermore, the estimator in equation (2) is sensitive to its denominator, π, which can be zero if the sample has never been observed in the prior. In contrast, the modified estimator in equation (7) appears to be more robust in principle, as the effects of π are mitigated by the double denominator.

I hope the authors will revise their commentary accordingly if the paper is accepted.

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and achieves performance comparable to a Conditional Random Field (CRF) using full multi-label information. This paper proposes an approach for "counterfactual learning," a specialized form of multi-label learning where only the label for one class (i.e., bandit feedback) is available, as opposed to traditional multi-label learning where ground-truth annotations provide 0/1 labels for each class. However, the primary objective of this paper is to improve upon the POEM estimator recently introduced in reference [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, I find the paper convincing and worthy of publication, although I have raised some concerns in the Comments to authors field.
