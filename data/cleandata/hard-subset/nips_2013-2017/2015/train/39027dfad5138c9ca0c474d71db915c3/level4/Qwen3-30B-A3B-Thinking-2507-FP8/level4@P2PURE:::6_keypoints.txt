- Counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) trains in bandit settings from logged data to correct bias from the logging policy.
- CRM is consistent but can overfit to the logged data’s generation probabilities.
- Overfitting occurs when hypotheses assign excessive weight to examples with high logging probabilities.
- The authors introduce a multiplicative parameter to bound variance while preserving consistency.
- They propose a self-normalized (normalized) counterfactual estimator for logged bandit learning.
- Experiments show the normalized estimator outperforms the non-normalized counterpart.
- The contribution is strong and warrants acceptance, with only minor reservations.
