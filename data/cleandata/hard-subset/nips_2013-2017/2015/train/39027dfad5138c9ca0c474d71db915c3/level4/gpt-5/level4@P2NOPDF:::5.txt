The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that mitigates the propensity overfitting problem. Building on Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), it delivers a substantially improved algorithm over POEM.

Quality: The research quality is above average. The proposed extensions lead to significant gains in training effectiveness and robustness compared to the original POEM, with convincing empirical support.

Clarity: The paper is generally clear and easy to follow. The motivation, methodological details, and results are presented coherently.

Originality: The paper offers a substantially new formulation of the original POEM algorithm for the BLBF setting. The contributions appear to be genuinely original.

Significance: The results are compelling and merit further research. It is notable that the proposed algorithm is faster than POEM despite incorporating additional regularization mechanisms. The method has strong practical potential and may see widespread use in the near future.

Overall, this is a strong paper. It is clear, well-structured, and persuasive.
