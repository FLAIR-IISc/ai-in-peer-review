Other comments:

- The setup for "batch learning from logged bandit feedback" in Section 3 (or from [1]) appears to be incomplete. Specifically, the feedback should be treated as a random variable rather than a deterministic function, as presented on line 110. My reasoning is as follows. Suppose the partial feedback comes from a classification problem where the true labels are unknown, and we only receive feedback by making a prediction and observing the resulting loss. Assume there is a true classification loss Delta(y',y), and the feedback is generated by predicting y using h_0, then having a "god agent" label the input as y' and provide the loss Delta(y',y) incurred (in this case, delta(x,y) is Delta(y',y) for the given y'). However, in classification problems, labeling is often noisy, and there may not be a unique y' assigned to each x. This means that elsewhere in the log, the same x can appear as input, with the same y played, but with a different delta(x,y) as the "god agent" assigned a different label. Therefore, I believe that delta(x,y) should be treated as a random variable (in the classification example above, the mean of this random variable for a fixed x and played y should be the expectation of Delta(y',y) for y' distributed according to p(y'|x), the true noisy labeling distribution).

This modification may not affect the risk estimator (2), but at least the setup in equation (1) with the true risk should include an additional expectation over the randomness of delta to be more general.

- Line 276 should be corrected to use argmin instead of argmax, as the risk is being minimized.
