- Identifies a severe propensity overfitting problem in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used for BLBF.  
- Proposes replacing the conventional estimator with a self-normalized (multiplicative control variate) estimator to avoid these degeneracies.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm for structured output prediction using the self-normalized estimator and linear rules.  
- Theoretically, the self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss translation, bounded within the loss range, and provably avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multi-label datasets, showing better generalization, robustness, and faster convergence.  
- Recaps BLBF setting: only bandit feedback is observed, importance sampling yields an unbiased but high-variance estimator when logging probabilities are small.  
- Demonstrates propensity overfitting via an example where empirical risk can be arbitrarily lower than true risk due to importance weights.  
- Uses Ŝ(h) = 1/n ∑ h(yi|xi)/pi as a diagnostic/control variate; deviations from 1 indicate potential overfitting and motivate self-normalization.  
- Positions the work within prior importance-sampling and variance-reduction literature (weight clipping, regression/doubly robust estimators).  
- Norm-POEM implementation: stochastic linear rules with joint feature map φ(x,y), approximate variance estimator, optimized with L-BFGS.  
- Experimental protocol: Supervised→Bandit conversion on multi-label classification; analysis shows Norm-POEM keeps Ŝ(h) close to its expectation.
