Summary
The paper addresses the problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant issue in applications such as ad ranking. The authors identify an "overfitting" problem in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and propose a solution based on multiplicative control variates, which results in a biased estimator.

Quality
This is a high-quality and well-motivated paper. As a follow-up work on [1], I would have expected additional experiments and a more in-depth analysis of the training objective optimization (beyond the details provided in the paragraph on line 415).

Clarity
The presentation is clear, and the paper is well-written.

Originality
Although this paper builds upon [1], the modification of the risk estimator to be self-normalizing appears to make a significant difference in the experiments presented by the authors.

Significance
The problem of learning from logged data is relevant; however, it is challenging to determine how the substantial experimental improvements translate to more real-world applications.

Various Remarks
Line 365: When presenting results from multiple repetitions, it would be helpful to include a measure of variance. The paper identifies a problem in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and introduces a solution to this issue. Overall, the paper is solid, well-written, and the work is relevant and novel. Nevertheless, I would have appreciated more extensive experiments, particularly additional applications beyond those in [1], including actual real-world applications.
