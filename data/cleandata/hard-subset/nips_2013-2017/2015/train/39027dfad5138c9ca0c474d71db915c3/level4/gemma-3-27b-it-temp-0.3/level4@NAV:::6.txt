Counterfactual risk minimization addresses learning from logged data in a bandit setting by mitigating the bias inherent in how those examples were generated. This paper identifies an overfitting issue within this framework – specifically, the potential for a hypothesis class to overfit based on the probability of an example’s generation – despite the method’s consistency. To address this, the authors introduce a multiplicative parameter to bound variance while preserving consistency. Empirical results demonstrate the superior performance of this normalized counterfactual technique compared to its non-normalized counterpart.

In essence, the paper offers a method for learning a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning within the logged bandit problem. It tackles a specific overfitting challenge where hypotheses can exhibit undue confidence in the observed, logged data. This is a strong contribution and, while not without minor reservations, merits acceptance.
