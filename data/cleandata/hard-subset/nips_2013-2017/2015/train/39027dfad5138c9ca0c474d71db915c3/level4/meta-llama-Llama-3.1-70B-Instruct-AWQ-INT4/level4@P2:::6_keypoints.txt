- Identifies a new overfitting mode called "Propensity Overfitting" in counterfactual risk estimation for logged bandit feedback.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using a multiplicative control variate to reduce estimator variance.
- Provides a theoretical consistency proof for the proposed estimator.
- Demonstrates empirically that the self-normalized estimator outperforms the conventional estimator on several multi-label classification tasks.
- Well-motivated problem statement with clear explanations and illustrative examples.
- Paper is well-written and the related work discussion is thorough.
- Strength: novel, theoretically sound solution to a significant BLBF problem.
- Strength: convincing experimental validation across tasks.
- Weakness: assumes logged data comes from a stationary policy, which may be unrealistic.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity.
- Weakness: needs a more detailed discussion of limitations and future work.
- Recommendation: good fit for NIPS and should be accepted, conditional on addressing the weaknesses.
