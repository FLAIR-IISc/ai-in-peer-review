The paper studies the problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant issue in various applications, such as ad ranking. The authors identify an "overfitting" problem in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and propose a solution based on multiplicative control variates, which results in a biased estimator.

Quality: 8/10
The paper is of high quality and well-motivated, building upon the work presented in [1]. However, as a follow-up study, I would have expected to see additional experiments and a more in-depth analysis of the training objective optimization, providing more details than the brief discussion on line 415.

Clarity: 9/10
The presentation is clear, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the proposed methodology.

Originality: 7/10
While the paper is a follow-up work on [1], the authors introduce a novel modification to the risk estimator by making it self-normalizing, which appears to have a significant impact on the experimental results.

Significance: 8/10
The problem of learning from logged data is highly relevant, but it is challenging to determine how the substantial experimental improvements demonstrated in the paper will translate to more real-world applications.

Various Remarks:
On line 365, the authors may want to consider including a measure of variance, given that they have already run multiple repetitions. The paper successfully identifies a problem in the CRM principle and proposes a solution. Overall, the paper is solid, well-written, and presents relevant and novel work. To further enhance the paper, I would have appreciated more extensive experiments, additional applications beyond those presented in [1], and some actual real-world applications to demonstrate the practical impact of the proposed methodology.
