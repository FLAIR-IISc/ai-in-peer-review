

The paper identifies a critical issue with the standard counterfactual risk estimator used in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), proposing a self-normalized estimator that avoids this problem. The authors demonstrate that conventional estimators suffer from propensity overfitting when learning over complex hypothesis spaces, and they introduce Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm that employs the self-normalized estimator. Empirical results on multi-label classification problems show consistent improvements over existing methods.

A key clarification is needed regarding loss scaling: Section 4.1 of [1] states the CRM approach requires loss in [-1,0], yet the paper's Table 1 appears to use delta > 0. Please confirm whether the same loss rescaling applies to Norm-POEM, and clarify the interpretation of results using delta > 0.

Additional comments:
- The paper presents delta(x,y) as a deterministic function (line 110), but in classification settings with noisy labeling, delta should be treated as a random variable (with expectation over the true labeling distribution). The risk definition in Equation (1) should include an additional expectation over delta's randomness.
- Line 170-171 should read h(y|x)/h₀(y|x) (not h(y|x)h₀(y|x)).
- Line 276 should use argmin instead of argmax, as the paper minimizes risk.
- Line 319 should use hats instead of bars for consistency with prior notation.

After the authors' clarifications, this is a solid contribution that effectively addresses propensity overfitting through a self-normalized estimator. The analysis is novel, the results consistently outperform existing methods, and the approach is well-motivated. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the growing literature on BLBF.
