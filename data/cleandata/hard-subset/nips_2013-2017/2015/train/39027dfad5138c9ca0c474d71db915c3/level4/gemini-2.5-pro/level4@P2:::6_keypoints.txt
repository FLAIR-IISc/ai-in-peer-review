- Paper identifies a novel pathology in CRM called "Propensity Overfitting," where hypotheses exploit propensity scores to minimize estimated risk.
- Shows a simple example where IPS can be driven to artificially low risk (e.g., assigning zero probability to observed actions), causing biased estimates and poor model selection.
- Proposes replacing the unbiased IPS estimator with a self-normalized estimator derived via multiplicative control variates to avoid this degeneracy.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm using the self-normalized estimator, and derives its variance-regularized CRM objective.
- Proves desirable theoretical properties for the estimator/algorithm, including consistency and equivariance to loss translations.
- Demonstrates empirical improvements: Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across multiple multi-label datasets.
- Empirical evidence shows Norm-POEM keeps the sum of importance weights near 1 and is robust to loss translations, validating mitigation of propensity overfitting.
- Reports that variance regularization remains useful and Norm-POEM is computationally efficient, often converging faster than POEM.
- Limitation: experiments are confined to multi-label classification and could be broadened to other structured prediction or RL-style tasks.
- Limitation: variance estimator is an approximate delta/Normal-method ratio approximation; potential inaccuracies and downstream effects are not discussed.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — significant, well-motivated contribution with strong theoretical and empirical support.
