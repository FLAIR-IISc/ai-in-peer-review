- Identifies overfitting in Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM): hypotheses can “game” CRM by fitting high-probability logged examples, causing high variance and poor generalization.  
- Proposes a self-normalized CRM estimator using a multiplicative normalization term that bounds variance while preserving consistency.  
- Experiments show the normalized estimator consistently outperforms standard non-normalized CRM and reduces overfitting.  
- Strength: Clear, precise problem formulation highlighting the inverse-propensity-score-driven variance issue.  
- Strength: Elegant, principled solution that directly targets variance without losing consistency.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation across settings supporting the method’s practical benefits.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to other off-policy variance-reduction techniques (e.g., doubly robust estimators).  
- Weakness: Needs discussion of sensitivity and limitations, e.g., behavior when propensity scores are extremely small.  
- Conclusion: Solid, well-written contribution with minor clarifications needed; recommended for acceptance.
