- Paper identifies propensity overfitting in counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF and proposes a self-normalized estimator as a remedy.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm using the self-normalized estimator that empirically outperforms POEM on multi-label tasks.  
- Strong theoretical justification showing robustness and equivariance advantages of the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical validation is thorough and shows consistent improvements, approaching full-information CRF performance.  
- Well-motivated paper connecting CRM to causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation.  
- Clear presentation and good exposition of technical details and related work.  
- Practical relevance: addresses a key issue for leveraging logged bandit data in online systems.  
- Initial imprecision in the presentation around Equation (2) undermined confidence, though authors acknowledged and corrected it.  
- Computational cost unclear: need more detailed complexity analysis, especially for variance estimation.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity not fully explored; more guidance on tuning M and λ is needed.  
- Limited exploration of failure cases or scenarios where Norm-POEM may be outperformed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, is theoretically sound, empirically validated, and well written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: initial technical imprecision and insufficient analysis of complexity and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with minor weaknesses; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested author actions: clarify technical details (Eq. (2)), provide complexity analysis, discuss hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning effort, explore failure cases or harder comparisons, and fix minor wording/typos.
