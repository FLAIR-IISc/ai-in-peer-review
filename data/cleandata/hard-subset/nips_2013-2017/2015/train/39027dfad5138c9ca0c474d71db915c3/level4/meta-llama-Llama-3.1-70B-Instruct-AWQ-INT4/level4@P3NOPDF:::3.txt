Summary
The paper addresses the problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant issue in various applications, such as ad ranking. The authors identify an "overfitting" problem in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and propose a solution based on multiplicative control variates, which results in a biased estimator.

Quality
This is a high-quality and well-motivated paper. As a follow-up work on [1], I would have expected additional experiments and a more in-depth analysis of the optimization of the training objective, providing more details than the brief discussion on line 415. The inclusion of these elements would have strengthened the paper.

Clarity
The presentation is clear, and the paper is well-written. The authors effectively convey their ideas and provide a logical flow of information, making the paper easy to follow.

Originality
Although this paper builds upon previous work [1], the authors' modification of the risk estimator to be self-normalizing appears to make a significant difference in the experiments presented. This contribution demonstrates a notable level of originality.

Significance
The problem of learning from logged data is highly relevant, particularly in real-world applications. However, it is challenging to determine how the substantial experimental improvements reported in the paper will translate to more practical applications.

Various Remarks
On line 365, I suggest including a measure of variance in addition to the results of multiple repetitions. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the results. The paper successfully identifies a problem in the recently proposed CRM principle and introduces a solution to address this issue. Overall, the paper is solid, well-written, and presents relevant and novel work. To further enhance the paper, I would have appreciated additional experiments, especially those involving real-world applications, beyond what was presented in [1].
