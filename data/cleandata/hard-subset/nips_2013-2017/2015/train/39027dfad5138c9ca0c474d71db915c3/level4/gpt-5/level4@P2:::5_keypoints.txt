- Paper identifies propensity overfitting of IPS within Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) for batch learning from logged bandit feedback.
- Proposes a self-normalized importance sampling estimator (ratio estimator) that is bounded, affine-equivariant, and strongly consistent.
- Integrates the estimator into CRM to create Norm-POEM for structured prediction with exponential-family linear models.
- Provides an approximate variance estimate, uses clipping for variance control, and optimizes with L-BFGS.
- Empirical evaluation on multi-label datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL) using a supervised-to-bandit protocol shows consistent gains over POEM.
- Norm-POEM better controls Ŝ(h) around 1, is robust to loss translations, and often converges faster than POEM.
- Builds on Swaminathan & Joachims (ICML’15) and connects to importance sampling, variance reduction, and doubly robust estimators.
- Use of self-normalization is known in MC/RL, but its analysis and application to mitigate propensity overfitting in learning is novel.
- Quality: arguments and properties (boundedness, equivariance, consistency) are clearly presented and experiments are well executed.
- Clarity: paper is generally well written; discussion and diagnostics for propensity overfitting are persuasive.
- Originality: integrating self-normalization into CRM to form Norm-POEM is a novel and well-motivated contribution.
- Significance: addresses a practical BLBF failure mode and offers a robust, adoptable alternative with clear empirical benefits.
- Strength: clear diagnosis and principled replacement of IPS that addresses key IPS anomalies.
- Strength: strong consistency guarantee and practical variance estimate enabling a CRM-style objective.
- Strength: consistent empirical improvements across datasets and robustness to loss translations; competitive runtimes.
- Weakness: comparisons limited mainly to POEM and a CRF skyline; missing stronger baselines like clipped IPS, doubly robust, or defensive mixtures.
- Weakness: lacks a generalization bound for the self-normalized estimator within CRM analogous to prior empirical-Bernstein bounds.
- Weakness: assumes known propensities with full support; no experiments or discussion on estimated or misspecified propensities.
- Weakness: needs more systematic sensitivity analysis for clipping threshold M, variance regularizer λ, and bias-variance trade-offs.
- Weakness: evaluations limited to supervised-to-bandit multi-label tasks; broader domains would strengthen generality.
- Weakness: optimization is non-convex and uses L-BFGS; more discussion of convergence and local minima risks would help.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a real failure mode with a simple, principled fix, supported by theory and consistent empirical gains.
- Cons for acceptance: limited baselines/task diversity, missing tailored generalization bound, and reliance on clipping/approximate variance without deeper bias analysis.
- Overall: strong, timely paper with clear contribution and convincing evidence; recommend acceptance subject to broader comparisons and additional theoretical analysis.
