- Identifies a critical failure mode in batch learning from logged bandit feedback called "Propensity Overfitting," where models manipulate importance weights to get artificially low IPS risk estimates and poor generalization.  
- Proposes replacing IPS with a self-normalized importance sampling estimator, yielding Norm-POEM, which is equivariant to loss shifts and bounds risk estimates within observed loss range.  
- Theoretical properties (equivariance, boundedness, consistency) are shown to directly address IPS weaknesses and underpin the proposed algorithm.  
- Empirical results across multi-label classification tasks show Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM and mitigates propensity overfitting (e.g., weight sums, robustness to loss translation).  
- Strength: High significance and impact by formalizing a previously under-appreciated risk in counterfactual learning with clear practical relevance.  
- Strength: Excellent clarity and presentation, with a simple illustrative example (Example 1) and convincing motivation linking classic statistics to modern ML.  
- Strength: Solid technical contribution—elegant derivation of Norm-POEM and its variance estimator.  
- Strength: Thorough, diagnostic empirical evaluation that credibly supports claims.  
- Weakness: The self-normalized estimator itself is a well-established statistical tool; novelty mainly lies in its application and algorithmic integration.  
- Weakness: Optimization concerns—training objective is non-convex and optimized with L-BFGS; deeper analysis of landscape and local minima would be helpful.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — paper is clear, technically sound, impactful, and supported by strong experiments.
