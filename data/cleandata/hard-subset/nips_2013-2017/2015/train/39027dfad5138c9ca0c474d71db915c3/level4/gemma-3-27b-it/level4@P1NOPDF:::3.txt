Summary
This paper addresses the important problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a scenario highly relevant to applications such as ad ranking. The authors identify an overfitting issue within the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and introduce a solution based on multiplicative control variates – a method that introduces a biased estimator.

Quality
This is a high-quality and well-motivated work. As a follow-up to [1], I would have expected to see additional experiments, and more in-depth analysis of the training objective’s optimization (beyond the detail provided in lines 415-420).

Clarity
The presentation is clear and the paper is well-written.

Originality
While building on previous work [1], the modification of the risk estimator to be self-normalizing appears to yield a significant improvement in the experimental results presented by the authors. 

Significance
Learning from logged data is a relevant and challenging problem. However, it remains difficult to assess how the substantial experimental improvements demonstrated here will translate to real-world applications.

Various Remarks
Line 365: If multiple repetitions have been performed, please include a measure of variance to quantify the results. The paper correctly identifies a limitation of the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and presents a viable solution. Overall, this is a solid and well-written paper addressing a relevant and novel research direction. I would, however, have appreciated more extensive experimentation, and particularly, applications beyond those previously explored in [1], including some evaluation on actual real-world datasets.
