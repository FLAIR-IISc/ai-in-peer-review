- Identifies "propensity overfitting": the counterfactual risk estimator can be minimized by exploiting importance weights, not just by finding low-loss actions.  
- Shows how expressive hypothesis spaces can assign high probability to actions with small logging propensities, producing pathological solutions.  
- Proposes using a self-normalized (ratio) estimator that uses the sum of importance weights as a multiplicative control variate.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new CRM-based learning algorithm employing the self-normalized estimator.  
- Provides theoretical justification via the control variates connection from Monte Carlo literature, resolving issues like loss-translation equivariance and unboundedness.  
- Empirically evaluates via Supervised→Bandit conversion on multi-label tasks and shows Norm-POEM outperforms POEM and is more robust.  
- Experimental results (Tables 1–3, Fig. 1) and Ŝ(h) values support that improvements stem from mitigating propensity overfitting.  
- Strength: clear identification and illustration of a subtle, important failure mode beyond typical high-variance discussions.  
- Strength: solution is principled, elegant, and well-grounded technically.  
- Strength: experiments are well-designed, controlled, convincing, and code is available.  
- Weakness: needs clearer discussion of relationship between propensity overfitting and high-variance importance sampling—are they the same root issue manifesting during ERM?  
- Weakness: overstates prior work focus on unbiased estimators; should acknowledge biased techniques (e.g., weight clipping/truncation) and clarify differences.  
- Weakness: Example 1’s calculation for R̂(hoverfit) should be walked through more explicitly to avoid confusion.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept with minor clarifications requested.
