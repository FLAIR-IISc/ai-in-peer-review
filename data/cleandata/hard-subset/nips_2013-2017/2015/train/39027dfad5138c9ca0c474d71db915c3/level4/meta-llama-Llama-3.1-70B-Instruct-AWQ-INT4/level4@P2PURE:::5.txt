The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that effectively addresses the propensity overfitting problem. Building upon the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the authors provide a significantly improved algorithm.

Quality: The research presented in the paper is of above-average quality. The proposed extensions result in substantial improvements to the original POEM algorithm, demonstrating the effectiveness of the modifications.

Clarity: The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers familiar with the subject matter.

Originality: The paper offers a significantly new formulation of the original POEM algorithm for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. In my opinion, the contributions are substantially original and represent a notable advancement in the field.

Significance: I find the results presented in the paper to be very interesting and worthy of further research. Notably, the proposed algorithm outperforms the original POEM in terms of speed, despite incorporating additional regularization mechanisms. This improved algorithm has the potential to see widespread adoption in the near future.

Overall, this is a well-written paper that effectively communicates its ideas and contributions.
