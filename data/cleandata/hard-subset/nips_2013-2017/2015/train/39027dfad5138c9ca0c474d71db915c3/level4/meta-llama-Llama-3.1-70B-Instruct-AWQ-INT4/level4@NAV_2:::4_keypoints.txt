- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to address propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Identifies a severe degeneracy in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator that leads to overfitting under ERM.
- Shows that conventional estimator can overfit even when the loss is transformed.
- Introduces a multiplicative control variate in the self-normalized estimator to reduce variance and avoid degeneracy.
- Provides theoretical guarantees for the estimator, including equivariance and strong consistency.
- Derives a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on CRM using the self-normalized estimator.
- Experimental results indicate Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting and generalizes better than POEM.
- Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on several multi-label classification problems.
- Strength: identifies a significant problem with the conventional estimator and offers a novel solution.
- Strength: analysis of propensity overfitting is thorough and well-motivated.
- Strength: estimator enjoys desirable theoretical properties.
- Strength: empirical evaluation supports the claimed benefits of Norm-POEM.
- Weakness: assumes a deterministic loss function, which may not hold in many real-world settings.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to specific multi-label tasks, so broader applicability is unclear.
- Weakness: Norm-POEM has higher computational cost than POEM due to the multiplicative control variate.
- Review scores: Quality 8, Clarity 8, Originality 9, Significance 9.
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested follow-up to address deterministic loss assumption and computational cost.
