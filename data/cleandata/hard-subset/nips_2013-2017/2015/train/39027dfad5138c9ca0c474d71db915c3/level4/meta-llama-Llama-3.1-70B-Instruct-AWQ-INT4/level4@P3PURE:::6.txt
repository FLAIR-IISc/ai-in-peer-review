This paper introduces a technique for counterfactual risk minimization, a method aimed at learning from logged data in a bandit setting by correcting for the bias inherent in generating logged examples. Although this approach is consistent, the authors demonstrate that it is susceptible to an overfitting problem, where a hypothesis class can overfit based on the generation probability of an example. To address this issue, the authors propose a solution involving a multiplicative parameter that bounds the variance while maintaining consistency. The experimental results show that the multiplicative (or normalized) counterfactual technique outperforms the non-normalized one.

The paper presents a technique for learning self-normalized estimators for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting, offering insights into dealing with a form of overfitting where the hypothesis can overfit by having stronger support for the logged data. Overall, this is a good paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field, and I believe it should be accepted, although I am not absolutely certain.
