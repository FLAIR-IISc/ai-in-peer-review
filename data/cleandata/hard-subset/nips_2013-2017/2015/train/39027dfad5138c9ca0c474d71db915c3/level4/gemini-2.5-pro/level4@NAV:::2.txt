The commentary and notation related to the primary equation, (2), appear to be incorrect or, at a minimum, confusing. By definition, an expectation is a value of the form \sum f(x) p(x), where f(x) represents the quantity being averaged and p(x) is the probability measure. In Equation (2), the loss term is clearly not the quantity over which the expectation is taken. Consequently, the subsequent argument on page 2 that Equation (2) exhibits an "anomaly" due to its lack of invariance to loss translation is based on a flawed premise. This flawed assumption also undermines the subsequent Example 1, which, as a result, fails to hold.

From a practical standpoint, the estimator in (2) exhibits a significant weakness: its sensitivity to the denominator, pi, which can become zero for samples not observed in the prior distribution. The proposed modified estimator in (7) appears conceptually superior, as the influence of pi is effectively mitigated by the double denominator structure. It is strongly recommended that the authors thoroughly revise this commentary if the paper moves toward publication.

The experiments demonstrate that Norm-POEM consistently surpasses POEM across all datasets and performs competitively with a CRF model that utilizes full multi-label information. This paper introduces a method for "counterfactual learning," a scenario framed as a specialization of multi-label learning where, unlike standard multi-label settings that provide a 0/1 label for all classes, feedback is provided for only a single class (i.e., bandit feedback). While this provides the motivating context, the paper's primary contribution is a specific and targeted improvement to the POEM estimator introduced in reference [1] (ICML 2015). Overall, the work is compelling and warrants publication, contingent upon addressing the critical remarks detailed above.
