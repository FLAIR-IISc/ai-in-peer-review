

The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that avoids the propensity overfitting problem. It builds on Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) and provides a significantly improved algorithm.  
Quality: The research quality is above average. The proposed extensions yield substantial improvements in training the original POEM algorithm.  
Clarity: The paper is generally clearly written and well-structured.  
Originality: The paper introduces a substantially new formulation for the original POEM algorithm within the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. The contributions are highly original.  
Significance: The results are compelling and warrant further investigation. The proposed algorithm achieves faster training than the original POEM despite incorporating a more extensive regularization mechanism, suggesting potential for widespread adoption.  
This is a strong paper that is clear and well-written.
