- The annotations and discussion around equation (2) are inaccurate or misleading relative to the standard definition of an expectation.
- Equation (2) incorrectly treats the loss as the expected quantity, so the claim that it is anomalous due to non-invariance to loss translation is technically flawed.
- Example 1, which depends on the incorrect assumption about equation (2), does not hold.
- The estimator in equation (2) is sensitive to the denominator π, which can be zero if a sample was never seen in the prior.
- The modified estimator in equation (7) is preferable in principle because the double denominator mitigates the effects of π.
- The reviewer requests the authors revise their commentary on these points if the paper is accepted.
- Experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and is comparable to a CRF trained with full multi-label information.
- The paper addresses counterfactual learning in a multi-label/bandit-feedback setting, but the justification for this specialization is preliminary.
- The work aims to improve the POEM estimator introduced in ICML 2015.
- Overall the reviewer finds the paper convincing and worthy of publication, though several remarks were raised for the authors.
