- Introduces "propensity overfitting" in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and shows conventional unbiased estimators can produce degenerate risk estimates.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator to avoid anomalies caused by propensity overfitting.
- Derives the estimator using control variates (importance sampling technique) and explains its properties (equivariance, boundedness).
- Presents a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, that uses the self-normalized estimator with counterfactual risk minimization.
- Provides extensive empirical evaluation on multi-label classification showing Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional estimator and avoids propensity overfitting.
- Thorough discussion of related work, linking to importance sampling and control variates.
- Acknowledges need for variance regularization as a potential limitation of the approach.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and technically thorough.
- Assumes high technical expertise, which may limit accessibility for non-experts.
- Some notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to readers outside the field.
- Could use more discussion of the broader implications and potential impact of propensity overfitting and the solution.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty, significance, and convincing empirical results.
