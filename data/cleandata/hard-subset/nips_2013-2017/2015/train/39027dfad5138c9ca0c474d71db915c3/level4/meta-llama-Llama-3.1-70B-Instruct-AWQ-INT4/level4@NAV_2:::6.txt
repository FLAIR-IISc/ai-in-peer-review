This paper proposes a novel approach to batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) by introducing a self-normalized estimator that addresses the problem of propensity overfitting in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the limitations of the traditional estimator and demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed method through extensive experiments.

The paper relates to previous work on BLBF, causal inference, and off-policy evaluation in reinforcement learning. The authors build upon the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle, which offers a general framework for designing BLBF algorithms. They identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, namely propensity overfitting, and propose a solution using a self-normalized estimator.

The strengths of the paper include its clear and concise presentation, thorough analysis of the problem, and well-designed experiments. The authors provide a detailed discussion of the related work, which helps to situate their contribution within the broader context of BLBF and causal inference. The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the superiority of the self-normalized estimator over the conventional one.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it may benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of propensity overfitting in real-world applications. While the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, it is not entirely clear how widespread this issue is in practice. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to the conventional estimator.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of BLBF and causal inference. The proposed self-normalized estimator offers a promising solution to the problem of propensity overfitting, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their ideas.
