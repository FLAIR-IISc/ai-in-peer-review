- Presents a counterfactual risk minimization method for learning in the logged-bandit setting that accounts for bias in logged example generation.  
- Method is consistent but susceptible to overfitting, where hypotheses over-specialize based on example-generation probabilities.  
- Introduces a multiplicative parameter that bounds variance while preserving consistency.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to address overfitting caused by stronger support in logged data.  
- Experiments show the normalized counterfactual technique outperforms the non-normalized counterpart.  
- Overall evaluation: strong paper deserving acceptance, with only minor uncertainties remaining.
