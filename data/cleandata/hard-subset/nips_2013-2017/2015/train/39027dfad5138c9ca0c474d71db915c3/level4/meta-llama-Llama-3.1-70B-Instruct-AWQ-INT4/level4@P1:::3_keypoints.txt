- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a relevant problem for applications like ad ranking.  
- Identifies an overfitting issue in the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle.  
- Proposes a solution using multiplicative control variates that yields a biased, self-normalizing risk estimator.  
- High-quality and well-motivated work, but is follow-up on [1] and feels incremental to some readers.  
- Presentation is clear and the paper is well-written.  
- The self-normalizing modification appears to produce significant experimental improvements.  
- Unclear how the experimental gains translate to real-world applications; practical impact is uncertain.  
- Reviewer expected more experiments and deeper analysis of optimization (paragraph around line 415).  
- Suggests reporting variance if multiple repetitions were run (comment at line 365).  
- Requests extended experiments and additional real-world applications beyond those in [1].  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a relevant problem, proposes a novel solution, shows solid experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: perceived incremental nature, insufficient optimization analysis, and lack of demonstrated real-world impact.
