- Identifies "propensity overfitting" as a failure mode of IPS in CRM for logged bandit feedback.
- Proposes a self-normalized (SN) IPS estimator using a multiplicative control variate (sum of importance weights).
- Argues SN estimator is bounded, equivariant under loss translations, and avoids IPS degenerate behavior.
- Introduces Norm-POEM: CRM instantiated for linear-exponential (CRF-like) models using SN and an approximate variance regularizer.
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multiple multi-label datasets and is more robust to loss translations.
- Strength: Clear analysis and illustration of propensity overfitting distinct from conventional loss overfitting.
- Strength: Principled use of multiplicative control variates yielding desirable estimator properties (boundedness, equivariance, consistency).
- Strength: Concrete algorithm (Norm-POEM) that integrates SN estimation and shows consistent empirical gains.
- Strength: Strong relevance and connection to BLBF, off-policy learning, importance weighting, and variance-reduction literature.
- Weakness: Some setup and notation need clarification, especially regarding loss scaling and stochasticity of feedback.
- Clarification requested: whether losses are rescaled to δ ∈ [−1,0] as in prior work and how reported "δ > 0" experiment values relate to rescaling and self-normalization.
- Comment on setup: δ(x,y) should be treated as a random variable in many BLBF settings, with R(h)=E_x E_{y∼h(x)} E[δ | x,y] being more general.
- Minor correctness/notation issues: fix h(y|x)/h0(y|x) wording, note prior mention of IPS non-equivariance, use argmin not argmax, and replace overbars with hats for consistency.
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer thanks authors for clarifications and updates assessment.
- Numerical ratings: quality 6/10, clarity 6/10, originality 8/10, significance 8/10.
- Pro acceptance: formalizes propensity overfitting, provides principled SN estimator with strong properties, demonstrates consistent empirical gains and robustness, and builds on established variance-reduction methods.
- Con acceptance: presentation clarity issues (loss scaling, δ>0 interpretation, deterministic feedback treatment), notational slips, and limited discussion of variance-approximation implications and asymptotic conditions.
- Overall: With clarifications and minor revisions, the work is a meaningful contribution to counterfactual learning from logged bandit feedback.
