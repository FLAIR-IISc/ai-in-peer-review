- Notation/commentary around equation (2) is incorrect/misleading: the loss in (2) is not an expectation of the form ∑ f(x)p(x), so the subsequent claim about translation invariance is technically wrong.  
- Example 1, which relies on the incorrect presumption about (2), does not hold.  
- The estimator in (2) is sensitive to its denominator π, which can be zero for unobserved samples in the prior.  
- The modified estimator in equation (7) appears improved, as the double denominator mitigates the effects of π.  
- Reviewer requests the authors revise their commentary on these technical points if the paper is accepted.  
- Experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and is comparable to a CRF trained with full multi-label information.  
- The paper addresses counterfactual (bandit-feedback) multi-label learning and aims primarily to improve the POEM estimator from ICML 2015.  
- Overall recommendation: paper is convincing and worthy of publication, subject to the noted remarks.
