Counterfactual risk minimization is a technique for learning in a bandit setting from logged data, addressing the bias inherent in how those examples were generated. While the method is theoretically consistent, this paper demonstrates that it can suffer from an overfitting problem: the hypothesis class may overfit based on the generation probability of individual examples. To mitigate this, the authors propose a modification incorporating a multiplicative parameter that bounds the variance while preserving consistency. Experimental results demonstrate that this multiplicative (or normalized) counterfactual technique outperforms its non-normalized counterpart.

This paper presents a technique for learning a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting. It addresses a specific form of overfitting where the hypothesis can develop undue support for the observed, logged data. Overall, this is a strong paper and should be accepted, though I retain some minor reservations.
