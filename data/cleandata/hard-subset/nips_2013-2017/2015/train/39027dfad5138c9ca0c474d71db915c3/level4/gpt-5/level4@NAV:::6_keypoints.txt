- Counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) is consistent but can overfit due to bias from the logging policy.
- The paper identifies an overfitting phenomenon where hypotheses exploit example generation probabilities by upweighting logged data.
- Authors propose a multiplicative (self-normalized) parameter/estimator that bounds variance while preserving consistency.
- Experimental results show the normalized (self-normalized) counterfactual approach outperforms the non-normalized alternative.
- The paper contributes a self-normalized estimator specifically for logged bandit counterfactual learning.
- Overall assessment: strong paper and recommended for acceptance, though the reviewer retains some uncertainty.
