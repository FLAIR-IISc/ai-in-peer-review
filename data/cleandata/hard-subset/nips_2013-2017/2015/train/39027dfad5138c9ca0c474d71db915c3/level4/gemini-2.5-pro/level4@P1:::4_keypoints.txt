- Identifies "propensity overfitting": IPS-based counterfactual risk estimators can be manipulated by hypotheses that game propensity weights, yielding biased risk estimates and poor generalization.
- Proposes a self-normalized importance-weighted estimator (multiplicative control variate) that is equivariant to loss translations, bounded by the loss range, and remedies the identified anomalies.
- Instantiates the approach within CRM to produce Norm-POEM, which empirically and consistently outperforms POEM on multi-label classification benchmarks.
- Strength: Clear identification and illustrative analysis of propensity overfitting, including an effective toy example showing arbitrarily low estimated risk disconnected from true performance.
- Strength: Elegant, principled solution linking self-normalization to multiplicative control variates with desirable theoretical properties (equivariance, boundedness, consistency).
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence and diagnostics showing Norm-POEM avoids pathological behaviors (robust to loss translation, keeps sum of weights near 1).
- Strength: Significant and impactful follow-up to CRM with broad applicability to logged-data learning in recommendation/search/advertising.
- Weakness/Question: Clarify whether Norm-POEM requires the CRM loss-scaling assumption (δ in [-1,0]); experiments with translated (positive) losses need explanation relative to theory.
- Weakness/Question: Recommend formalizing stochastic feedback by defining loss as a random variable (use expectation over δ) to better capture noisy real-world logs.
- Minor: Line 170–171 likely typo — weight term should be a ratio h(y|x)/h0(y|x), not a product.
- Minor: Note that lack of equivariance of the unbiased estimator was previously mentioned in [5].
- Minor: Consistency of notation in Var(·) estimate: use hats rather than bars for δi − R̂SN(h).
- Conclusion: Well-written, valuable contribution that fixes a significant flaw in BLBF; recommendation to accept.
