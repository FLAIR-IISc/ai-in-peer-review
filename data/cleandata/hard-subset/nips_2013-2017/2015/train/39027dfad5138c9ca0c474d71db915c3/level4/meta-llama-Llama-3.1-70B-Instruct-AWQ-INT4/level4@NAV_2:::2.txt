The paper "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning" presents an approach for improving the counterfactual risk estimator used in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional estimator, which they term "propensity overfitting," and propose an alternative estimator that avoids this issue. The new estimator, called the self-normalized estimator, is shown to be more robust and effective in practice.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the problem with the conventional estimator and the benefits of the proposed self-normalized estimator. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the estimator's properties and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on several multi-label classification datasets. The results show that the new estimator, used in the Norm-POEM algorithm, outperforms the conventional estimator used in POEM.

The paper is closely related to previous work on BLBF, and the authors provide a clear discussion of the connections to this work. The paper also provides a thorough review of related work on counterfactual estimation and learning.
