Counterfactual risk minimization is a technique aimed at learning from logged data in a bandit setting by correcting the bias of generating logged examples. While consistent, the paper shows that it suffers from the problem of overfitting, where a hypothesis class can overfit based on the probability of an example's origin. The authors propose correcting this by using a qualitative parameter that limits variance while maintaining stability. Experimental results indicate that the qualitative (or generalized) counterfactual technique performs better than the non-generalized technique.

This paper presents a technique for learning self-generalized estimators for counterfactual learning in a logged bandit setting. It presents an idea to deal with a form of overfitting where strong support for logged data can cause the hypothesis to overfit. It is a good paper and should be accepted, although I am not entirely certain.
