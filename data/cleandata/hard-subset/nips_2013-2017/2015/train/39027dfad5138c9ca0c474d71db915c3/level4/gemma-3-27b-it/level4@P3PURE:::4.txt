== Other comments ==
- There is a potential issue with the setup for "batch learning from logged bandit feedback" in Section 3 (or from [1]). I believe the feedback should be modeled as a random variable, rather than a deterministic function as presented on line 110. My reasoning is as follows. Suppose the partial feedback comes from a classification problem where labels are unknown, and feedback is obtained by making a prediction and observing the resulting loss. Let Delta(y', y) represent the true classification loss, where y' is the true label and y is the prediction made by h_0.  The feedback, delta(x, y), is then Delta(y', y) for the given y'. However, in classification tasks, labeling can often be noisy, meaning a single input x may not have a unique y' assigned to it. Consequently, the same x and y might appear in the log with different delta(x, y) values due to varying labels assigned by the "god agent."  Therefore, I suggest that delta(x, y) should be considered a random variable. In the classification example, the mean of this random variable for fixed x and y should be the expectation of Delta(y', y) with y' distributed according to p(y'|x), the true noisy labeling distribution. This may not change the risk estimator (2), but the setup in (1) relating to the true risk should be presented with an additional expectation over the randomness of delta to be more general.
- Line 170-171: I believe it should be h(y|x) / h_0(y|x). It might also be worthwhile to mention that the lack of linearity was already pointed out in [1].
- Line 276: You should use argmin instead of argmax, given that you are minimizing the risk.
- Line 319: Replace bars with hats in the notation to maintain consistency with the previous convention.
