- Fundamental technical error: expectation misuse — Equation (2) treats a loss as the expected quantity, conflating f(x) with p(x).  
- Consequence: the claim that Equation (2) shows an "anomaly" from lack of translation invariance is technically flawed.  
- Downstream impact: Example 1 is based on the incorrect premise and therefore does not hold.  
- Practical issue: the estimator in Equation (2) is highly sensitive to denominator π_i, which can be zero for unobserved samples.  
- Fix in manuscript: Equation (7) mitigates the π_i sensitivity via its double-denominator structure and should be emphasized.  
- Experimental finding: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across all datasets.  
- Empirical comparison: Norm-POEM achieves performance close to a CRF trained with full multi-label information.  
- Framing critique: the paper is essentially an improvement of the POEM estimator from ICML 2015, framed as counterfactual learning (a specialization of multi-label learning).  
- Recommendation: the paper is convincing and merits publication after the noted technical corrections.
