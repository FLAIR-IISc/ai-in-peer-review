

The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that addresses the propensity overfitting issue. It extends Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) and delivers a substantially improved algorithm.  
Quality: The research quality is above average. The proposed extensions yield substantial improvements in training efficiency for the original POEM algorithm.  
Clarity: The paper is well-written and accessible throughout.  
Originality: The work presents a substantially novel formulation of the POEM algorithm for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem, representing a significant original contribution.  
Significance: The findings are compelling and warrant further investigation. Notably, the proposed algorithm achieves faster training than the original POEM despite incorporating additional regularization, suggesting potential for widespread adoption in future applications.  
This is a strong paper that is clear and well-written.
