This paper proposes a novel approach to batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) by introducing a self-normalized estimator to address the problem of propensity overfitting. The authors identify a severe issue with the conventional unbiased risk estimator used in prior works on BLBF, which can lead to degeneracies when used in empirical risk minimization (ERM). They provide a thorough analysis of the problem and propose a solution using a multiplicative control variate, resulting in a new learning algorithm called Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models (Norm-POEM).

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the problem, related work, and the proposed solution. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the self-normalized estimator, including its theoretical properties and an empirical evaluation on several multi-label classification problems. The results show that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional estimator, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The paper contributes to the field of BLBF by introducing a new estimator that addresses the problem of propensity overfitting, which is a common issue in this setting. The authors also provide a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, which is based on the counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) principle and uses the self-normalized estimator.

The strengths of the paper include:

* A clear and thorough analysis of the problem of propensity overfitting in BLBF
* A novel solution using a multiplicative control variate, resulting in a new learning algorithm called Norm-POEM
* A detailed empirical evaluation of the proposed approach on several multi-label classification problems

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The paper assumes a certain level of familiarity with BLBF and the CRM principle, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow
* The authors could provide more discussion on the relation between propensity overfitting and the large variance of the weights, as these two problems seem related but distinct

Overall, the paper is well-written and contributes to the field of BLBF by introducing a new estimator and learning algorithm. However, the reviewer did not have time to thoroughly check the technical soundness of the paper and suggests acceptance based on the interesting ideas presented.
