- Identifies a significant overfitting problem in counterfactual risk minimization where estimators can fit propensity scores instead of true rewards.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator using a multiplicative control variate to bound variance while preserving consistency.  
- Theoretical analysis (Theorems 1 and 2) rigorously justifies the proposed estimator and its avoidance of propensity overfitting.  
- Empirical results show the normalized estimator consistently outperforms the conventional POEM baseline on multiple multi-label classification datasets.  
- Ablation study supports the claimed benefits and clarifies components of the approach.  
- Writing and organization are clear, making complex concepts accessible.  
- Concern about computational cost and need for more discussion of complexity, especially variance estimation (Equation 8).  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (M and λ) and reliance on hold-out calibration; automated tuning discussion would be beneficial.  
- Experimental scope is limited mainly to multi-label classification; broader domains (e.g., recommender systems) are not explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a novel, important problem; solution is theoretically sound and empirically validated; clear, consistent improvements and good presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential scalability/complexity limits for very large datasets and limited cross-domain generalizability of experiments.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution that merits acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
