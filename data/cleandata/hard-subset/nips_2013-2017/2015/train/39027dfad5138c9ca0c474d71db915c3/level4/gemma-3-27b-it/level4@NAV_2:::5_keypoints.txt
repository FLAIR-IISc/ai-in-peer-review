- Identifies propensity overfitting in the standard counterfactual risk estimator for BLBF and proposes a self-normalized estimator (Norm-POEM) to address it.
- High technical quality: clear problem statement, sound theoretical justification, and rigorous empirical evaluation (Theorems 1 & 2 appear correct).
- Well-connected to related areas (causal inference, importance sampling, off-policy evaluation) with appropriate citations.
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM improves performance and stability over POEM.
- Norm-POEM can be computationally faster than POEM despite added regularization, suggesting practical deployment advantages.
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent, accessible notation.
- Some sections need clearer explanation—particularly variance estimation and the practical implications of the Normal approximation in Section 6.
- Original contribution: clearly identifies a distinct phenomenon (propensity overfitting) and develops a novel estimator and algorithm to combat it.
- Significant work: addresses an important real-world problem (ads, recommendations) and is likely to influence future BLBF and off-policy research.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a critical problem, provides theoretically sound and empirically validated solution, shows substantial improvements, and is computationally efficient and well-presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: variance estimation discussion is underdeveloped and experiments could include a broader range of datasets/scenarios.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; recommend acceptance.
