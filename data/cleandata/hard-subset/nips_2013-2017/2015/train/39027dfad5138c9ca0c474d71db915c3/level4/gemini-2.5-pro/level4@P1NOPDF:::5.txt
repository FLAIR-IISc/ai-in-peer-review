This paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that effectively avoids the propensity overfitting problem. The work builds upon Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) and introduces a significantly improved algorithm.

**Quality:** The quality of the research presented in this paper is high. The proposed extensions to the original POEM algorithm result in significant improvements to the training process. The technical contributions are well-motivated and sound.

**Clarity:** For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow. The arguments are well-structured, and the core ideas are communicated effectively.

**Originality:** The paper presents a novel reformulation of the POEM algorithm for the Batch Learning from Bandit Feedback (BLBF) problem. I believe the contributions are significant and original, offering a fresh perspective on a known challenge.

**Significance:** The results are very promising and certainly warrant further investigation. It is particularly impressive that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM, despite incorporating additional regularization mechanisms. This combination of improved performance and efficiency suggests the algorithm has the potential for widespread adoption in the near future.

In summary, this is a strong paper that is well-written and makes a valuable contribution.
