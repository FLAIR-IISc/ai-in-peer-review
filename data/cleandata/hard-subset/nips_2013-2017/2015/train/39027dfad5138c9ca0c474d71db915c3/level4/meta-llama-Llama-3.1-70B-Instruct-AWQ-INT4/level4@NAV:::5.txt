The paper presents a novel approach to batch learning from logged bandit feedback, effectively mitigating the propensity overfitting issue. Building upon the foundation established by Swaminathan and Joachims in 2015, this method offers substantial enhancements over existing algorithms.

Quality: The research quality presented in this paper is commendable, surpassing average standards. The proposed extensions yield notable improvements in the training of the original POEM algorithm, demonstrating the efficacy of the new approach.

Clarity: The paper is, for the most part, well-structured and easily comprehensible, facilitating a smooth understanding of the concepts presented.

Originality: The paper introduces a significantly novel formulation of the original POEM algorithm, tailored to address the Batch Learning from Bandit Feedback (BLBF) problem. The contributions are substantial and original, offering a fresh perspective on the existing methodology.

Significance: The results presented in the paper are intriguing and warrant further investigation. The proposed algorithm's ability to outperform the original POEM in terms of speed, despite incorporating additional regularization mechanisms, is particularly impressive. This algorithm has the potential for widespread adoption in the near future.

Overall, this is a well-crafted paper that is clear, engaging, and effectively conveys its message.
