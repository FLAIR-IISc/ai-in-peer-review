Summary
The paper investigates batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant setting for applications such as ad ranking. It identifies an overfitting issue within the recently proposed CRM principle when using the standard counterfactual estimator, and introduces a remedy based on multiplicative control variates that yields a self-normalized (and thus biased) estimator.

Quality
The work is technically strong and well motivated. Since it extends [1], I would have anticipated broader experiments and a deeper treatment of the optimization aspects of the training objective (beyond the brief discussion around line 415).

Clarity
The presentation is clear and the manuscript is well organized.

Originality
This is a follow-up to [1], with the main innovation being the adoption of a self-normalized risk estimator. That change appears to have a substantial empirical impact in the reported studies.

Significance
Learning from logged data is important; however, it remains uncertain how the notable empirical gains here will translate to more realistic, large-scale deployments.

Various Remarks
- Line 365: since multiple repetitions are reported, please include a measure of variability (e.g., variance or confidence intervals).
- The paper pinpoints a problem in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization framework and presents a solution. Overall, the work is solid, clearly written, relevant, and contributes novel insights. I would, however, have liked to see more extensive experiments and, in particular, applications beyond those in [1], including some real-world use cases.
