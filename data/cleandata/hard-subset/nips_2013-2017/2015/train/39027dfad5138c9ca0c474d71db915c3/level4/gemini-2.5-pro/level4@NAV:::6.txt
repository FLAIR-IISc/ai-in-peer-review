This work investigates Counterfactual Risk Minimization, a standard approach for learning from logged bandit data that corrects for selection bias. The authors demonstrate that while this method is consistent, it is prone to a form of overfitting driven by the data's generation probabilities. To address this, the paper introduces a normalization technique that uses a multiplicative parameter to bound the estimator's variance while preserving consistency. The empirical evaluation validates this approach, showing that the proposed normalized counterfactual estimator outperforms its non-normalized counterpart.

The paper's core contribution is a method for learning a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning within the logged bandit framework. It offers a principled solution to the problem of a hypothesis overfitting by disproportionately favoring the logged data. This is a strong paper that I recommend for acceptance, though my confidence in this assessment is not absolute.
