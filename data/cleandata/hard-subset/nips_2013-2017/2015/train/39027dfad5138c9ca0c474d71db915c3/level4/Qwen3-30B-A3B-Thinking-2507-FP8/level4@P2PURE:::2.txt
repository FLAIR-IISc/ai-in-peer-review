

The commentary and notation regarding the main equation (2) are incorrect or misleading. An expectation is defined as $\sum f(x) p(x)$, where $f(x)$ is the quantity being averaged and distinct from $p(x)$, the probability measure. The loss in equation (2) is not the expected quantity, rendering the claim (page 2, second paragraph) that (2) exhibits an "anomaly" due to lack of translation invariance technically incorrect. Consequently, Example 1, which relies on this erroneous premise, fails to hold. Practically, the estimator in (2) is highly sensitive to its denominator $\pi$, which may be zero if the sample was unobserved in the prior. The modified estimator in (7) is preferable in principle, as it mitigates the influence of $\pi$ through its double denominator. I strongly urge the authors to revise this commentary should the paper be accepted.  

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across all datasets and achieves performance competitive with a CRF using full multi-label information. While the paper positions its approach as "counterfactual learning"—a specialization of multi-label learning where ground-truth annotations provide bandit feedback (one label per sample rather than full multi-label labels)—its core contribution is precisely to improve the POEM estimator introduced in reference [1] (ICML 2015). Overall, the paper is convincing and suitable for publication, though the aforementioned technical issues require correction.
