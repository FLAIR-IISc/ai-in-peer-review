This paper studies counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) for learning from logged bandit feedback, where bias from the logging policy is corrected using propensity-based reweighting. Although CRM is consistent, the authors show it can overfit by exploiting examples with small generation probabilities, effectively letting a hypothesis overemphasize logged data it happens to support. To address this, they propose a self-normalized (multiplicative) estimator that bounds variance while preserving consistency. The method normalizes importance weights, curbing the instability that leads to overfitting without introducing asymptotic bias.

The contribution is clear and well motivated: it identifies a concrete overfitting failure mode in standard CRM and provides a principled fix via normalization. The empirical results support the claim that the normalized (multiplicative) counterfactual estimator outperforms the unnormalized baseline across the reported settings. Overall, this is a solid paper with a useful advance for counterfactual learning from logged bandit data. I recommend acceptance, acknowledging a small degree of uncertainty.
