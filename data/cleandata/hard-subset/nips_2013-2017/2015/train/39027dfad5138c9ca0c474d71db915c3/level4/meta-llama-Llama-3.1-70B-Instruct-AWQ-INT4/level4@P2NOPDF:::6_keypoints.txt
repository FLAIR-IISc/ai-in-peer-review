- Paper proposes a self-normalized (multiplicative) estimator for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting.  
- Addresses overfitting caused by hypotheses gaining stronger support from logged data due to example generation probabilities.  
- Introduces a multiplicative parameter that bounds variance while preserving consistency.  
- Experimental results show the normalized (multiplicative) estimator outperforms the non-normalized version.  
- Contributes meaningfully to counterfactual risk minimization by correcting logging bias in bandit learning from logged data.  
- Notes that the original consistent estimator suffers from overfitting, which the proposed method mitigates.  
- Writing is clear and experiments are convincing.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, though not absolutely certain.
