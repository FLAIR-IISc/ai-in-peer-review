- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (e.g., ad ranking) and identifies overfitting in the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle.  
- Proposes a solution using multiplicative control variates that yields a biased, self-normalizing risk estimator.  
- Work is well motivated, high quality, and modifies the CRM estimator in a way that shows substantial empirical improvement.  
- The paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- It is follow-up work on [1], building on prior methods rather than introducing a wholly new direction.  
- Reviewer expected broader experiments and deeper analysis of optimization beyond the brief discussion around line 415.  
- Concern about how the reported experimental gains will generalize to broader real-world applications and deployments.  
- Suggests reporting variance when multiple repetitions are run (line 365).  
- Overall assessment: solid, relevant, and novel, but would benefit from more extensive experiments and real-world evaluations.
