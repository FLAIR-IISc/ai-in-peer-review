- Tackles batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and exposes propensity overfitting as a key failure mode of IPS-based ERM.
- Proposes replacing IPS with a self-normalized (SN) estimator using multiplicative control variates to prevent overfitting.
- Shows the SN estimator is bounded, equivariant to loss translations, and strongly consistent.
- Derives Norm-POEM, a new CRM-based algorithm for structured prediction with linear-exponential models.
- Provides an approximate variance estimator for the SN ratio estimator and optimizes the non-convex objective with L-BFGS.
- Empirical evaluation using supervised-to-bandit methodology on multi-label datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL).
- Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across runs and sample sizes and is robust to loss translations.
- Norm-POEM achieves faster optimization in practice despite added normalization and variance regularization.
- Diagnostics (sum of normalized weights Ŝ(h)) show Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting (Ŝ ≈ 1), whereas POEM can deviate severely.
- Builds on CRM/POEM and connects to importance sampling and control variate literature; SN idea adapted from Monte Carlo methods.
- The adaptation of SN to counterfactual learning and the resulting Norm-POEM algorithm are original contributions in this context.
- Theoretical properties and variance approximation are appropriate and well-motivated for the setting.
- Paper is clearly written, with well-explained pathology and experimental setup.
- No substantive weaknesses noted; overall a strong, significant, and practically impactful paper.
