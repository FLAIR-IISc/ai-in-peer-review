- Counterfactual risk minimization is used to learn from logged bandit data by correcting for bias and is a consistent approach.
- The paper identifies an overfitting issue where hypotheses can overfit by exploiting the generation probabilities of logged examples.
- To address this, the authors introduce a multiplicative (normalization) parameter that bounds variance while preserving consistency.
- They present a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning that mitigates the identified overfitting mode.
- Experimental results show the multiplicative/normalized counterfactual method outperforms the non-normalized version.
- The reviewer considers the paper good and recommends acceptance, albeit with some uncertainty.
