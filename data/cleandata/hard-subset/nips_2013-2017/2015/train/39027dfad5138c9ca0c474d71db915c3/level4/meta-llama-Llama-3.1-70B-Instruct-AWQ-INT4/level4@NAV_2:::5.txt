This paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that addresses the propensity overfitting problem, a significant issue with the conventional unbiased risk estimator used in prior works on this topic. The authors build upon the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle introduced in Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) and develop a new learning algorithm called Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models (Norm-POEM). This algorithm replaces the conventional estimator with a self-normalized risk estimator, which provably avoids the propensity overfitting problem.

The paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear and concise overview of the related work, the problem formulation, and the proposed solution. The technical content is sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the self-normalized risk estimator, including its theoretical properties and empirical evaluation.

The paper is clearly written, and the authors provide sufficient background information for readers unfamiliar with the topic.
