== Other comments ==
- The setup for batch learning from logged bandit feedback in Section 3 (and in [1]) seems to present δ(x, y) as deterministic (line 110), but in many practical settings the feedback is inherently stochastic. For instance, consider a classification scenario where labels are noisy: a historical policy h0 predicts y for input x; an oracle draws a label y′ from a noise model p(y′ | x); and the observed feedback is the true loss Δ(y′, y). The same x and chosen y could therefore yield different δ(x, y) across log entries due to label noise. This suggests modeling δ(x, y) as a random variable, with E[δ | x, y] = Ey′∼p(·|x)[Δ(y′, y)]. While this does not alter the importance-weighted estimator in (2), the risk in (1) would more generally include an extra expectation over the randomness of δ.
- Lines 170–171: the ratio should read h(y | x) / h0(y | x). It may also be useful to note that the failure of equivariance (lack of translation invariance) was already remarked in [1].
- Line 276: use argmin rather than argmax, since the objective is a risk minimization.
- Line 319: replace bars with hats to align with the earlier notation.
