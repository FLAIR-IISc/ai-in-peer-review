- Equation (2) is incorrectly/misleadingly described: the expectation notation is wrong and the loss is not the expected quantity, so the claim about translation-invariance “anomaly” is technically incorrect.  
- Example 1 is based on that faulty premise and therefore does not hold.  
- Estimator (2) is practically vulnerable because its denominator pi can be zero for unseen samples; estimator (7) is preferable as the double denominator attenuates pi’s influence.  
- Experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and approaches a CRF trained with full multi-label information.  
- Counterfactual learning here is a specialization of multi-label learning (only one label observed); the paper’s concrete goal is improving the POEM estimator from ICML 2015.  
- Overall the paper is convincing and worthy of publication, subject to the noted revisions.
