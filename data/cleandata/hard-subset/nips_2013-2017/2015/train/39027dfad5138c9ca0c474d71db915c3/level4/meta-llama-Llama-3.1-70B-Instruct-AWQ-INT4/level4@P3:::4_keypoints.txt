- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to fix propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Shows conventional unbiased risk estimator can produce degeneracies in ERM for BLBF.  
- Demonstrates the self-normalized estimator avoids these anomalies and improves generalization and robustness.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and thoroughly analyzes propensity overfitting.  
- Theoretical results supporting the proposed estimator are sound and well-motivated.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and shows the effectiveness of the approach.  
- Strength: identifies a significant BLBF problem and provides a novel, clever solution.  
- Strength: comprehensive analysis and strong empirical support.  
- Weakness: assumes deterministic feedback while real-world feedback may be stochastic; implications need discussion.  
- Weakness: some inconsistent notation and minor errors (e.g., lines 170–171, 276, 319).  
- Originality: introduces a new self-normalized estimator to address propensity overfitting.  
- Significance: addresses an important BLBF issue with potential practical impact.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on fixing notation errors and discussing deterministic feedback assumption.
