

The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that avoids the propensity overfitting problem. It builds on Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) and presents a significantly improved algorithm for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem.  

Quality: The quality of research is above average. The proposed extensions result in significant improvements in training stability and performance over the original POEM algorithm.  

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and well-organized, making it easy to follow the technical contributions.  

Originality: The paper introduces a substantially new formulation for the POEM algorithm within the BLBF framework. The contributions are highly original and represent a meaningful advancement over prior work.  

Significance: The results are highly interesting and warrant further investigation. The proposed algorithm achieves faster training than POEM despite incorporating a more complex regularization mechanism, a notable practical advantage. This algorithm has strong potential for widespread adoption in real-world applications.  

This is a strong paper that is clearly written and makes a valuable contribution to the field.
