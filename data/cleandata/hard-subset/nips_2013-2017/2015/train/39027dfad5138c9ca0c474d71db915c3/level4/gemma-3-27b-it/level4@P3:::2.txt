## Review of "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning"

This paper addresses a critical issue in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF): propensity overfitting in the commonly used counterfactual risk estimator. The authors demonstrate that this estimator, while unbiased, can exhibit poor behavior, particularly with complex hypothesis spaces, and propose a self-normalized estimator as a more robust alternative. This leads to a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, which is empirically shown to outperform the original POEM algorithm on several multi-label classification problems.

The paper is well-motivated, building upon the established framework of counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) and connecting it to related work in causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation. The problem of propensity overfitting is clearly articulated, and the proposed solution – the self-normalized estimator – is presented with a strong theoretical justification. The experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, demonstrating consistent improvements over the baseline POEM algorithm and approaching the performance of a full-information CRF.

**Strengths:**

* **Important Problem:** The identified issue of propensity overfitting is a significant concern for the practical application of BLBF, and addressing it is a valuable contribution.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the proposed self-normalized estimator, demonstrating its advantages over the conventional estimator in terms of robustness and equivariance.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results consistently demonstrate the effectiveness of Norm-POEM across multiple datasets, showcasing its improved generalization performance.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the technical details of the proposed method.  The connection to existing work is well-established.
* **Practical Relevance:**  BLBF is a crucial technique for leveraging historical data in online learning systems, making this work relevant to a wide range of applications.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Initial Technical Imprecision:** The original commentary surrounding Equation (2) contained an inaccurate description of expectation and its relation to probability measures. While not central to the core contribution, this initially undermines confidence in the authors’ technical rigor.  (The authors have since acknowledged this issue in their response to reviewers.)
* **Computational Cost:** While the authors state that Norm-POEM is competitive in runtime with POEM, a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity, particularly concerning the variance estimation, would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions calibrating hyperparameters on a hold-out set, but a more in-depth discussion of the sensitivity of performance to these hyperparameters would be valuable.  How much effort is required for successful tuning?
* **Limited Exploration of Failure Cases:** The experiments demonstrate strong performance on the selected datasets, but it would be insightful to investigate scenarios where Norm-POEM might struggle or be outperformed by other methods.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant and practical problem in BLBF.
* The proposed solution is theoretically sound and empirically validated.
* The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate substantial improvements over the existing state-of-the-art.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The initial technical imprecision in the presentation of the core estimator, while corrected in author responses, raises concerns about attention to detail.
* The lack of detailed complexity analysis and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.




**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the minor weaknesses noted above, I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of batch learning from logged bandit feedback. The identification of propensity overfitting and the proposed self-normalized estimator are significant advancements. The strong empirical results and clear presentation further solidify the paper's merit. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the points raised in the "Comments to Authors" section.



**Comments to Authors:**

1. **Clarify Technical Details:**  Please carefully review the technical details in the introduction and related work sections to ensure accuracy and precision.  Specifically, refine the explanation surrounding Equation (2) to accurately reflect the concept of expectation.
2. **Computational Complexity:** Provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of Norm-POEM, particularly in relation to the variance estimation step.
3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Discuss the sensitivity of performance to the regularization hyperparameters (M and λ).  Provide guidance on how to effectively tune these parameters in practice.
4. **Exploration of Failure Cases:** Consider exploring scenarios where Norm-POEM might struggle, or comparing its performance to alternative approaches in more challenging settings.
5. **Minor Typo/Clarity:** Consider rephrasing the sentence "This is in stark contrast to overfitting in supervised learning, which we call Loss Overfitting" for better clarity. It might be more appropriate to say this is different *from* loss overfitting.
