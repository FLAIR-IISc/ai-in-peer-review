The paper identifies a significant problem – propensity overfitting – in the commonly used counterfactual risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and proposes a self-normalized estimator to address it. Building upon previous work by Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the authors introduce a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, demonstrating substantial improvements over the original POEM approach.

**Quality:** The research presented is of high quality. The authors clearly articulate the limitations of existing methods and provide a compelling theoretical justification for their proposed solution. The empirical results consistently demonstrate significant improvements in generalization performance compared to the conventional estimator, supporting the claims made. The thorough experimental evaluation, including comparisons across multiple datasets and analyses of the self-normalized estimator’s properties, strengthens the validity of the findings.

**Clarity:** The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and easy to follow. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and contextualizes the contribution within the broader fields of causal inference, off-policy evaluation, and reinforcement learning. The mathematical notation is generally well-defined, and the flow of ideas is logical. However, some sections, particularly those detailing the theoretical proofs (e.g., Theorem 1 and Theorem 2), could benefit from further simplification or more intuitive explanations for readers less familiar with the underlying mathematical concepts.

**Originality:** The paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of BLBF. The identification of “propensity overfitting” as a distinct form of overfitting is insightful and addresses a critical limitation of existing methods. The self-normalized estimator offers a practical and theoretically sound solution to this problem. The combination of theoretical analysis and empirical validation further strengthens the originality of the work, demonstrating a clear advancement over previous approaches. The authors effectively build upon related work (e.g., importance sampling, control variates) while presenting a genuinely new perspective and solution.

**Significance:** The results presented in this paper are highly interesting and have the potential to significantly impact the field of BLBF. The improved performance and robustness of Norm-POEM, coupled with its surprisingly faster training speed compared to the original POEM, suggest that this algorithm could see widespread adoption in practical applications. The ability to avoid propensity overfitting is a crucial advancement, as it directly addresses a core challenge in learning from historical log data. The theoretical insights provided by the authors contribute to a deeper understanding of the limitations of existing methods and pave the way for future research in this area. The provided software implementation further enhances the practical impact of the work.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant and previously unrecognized problem (propensity overfitting) in BLBF.
*   Proposes a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution (self-normalized estimator and Norm-POEM).
*   Demonstrates substantial improvements in generalization performance and training speed compared to existing methods.
*   Clear and well-written presentation of the problem, proposed solution, and experimental results.
*   Provides a publicly available software implementation, facilitating reproducibility and adoption.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The theoretical sections could benefit from further simplification and more intuitive explanations.
*   While the experiments are comprehensive, exploring additional datasets or application domains could further strengthen the generalizability of the findings.

**Overall:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of batch learning from logged bandit feedback. The authors have identified a critical problem, proposed an effective solution, and provided compelling evidence to support their claims. Despite minor areas for improvement, the paper is well-written, technically sound, and highly significant. I recommend acceptance with enthusiasm.
