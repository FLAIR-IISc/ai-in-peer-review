Due to my current travel schedule, I was only able to provide a brief review of this submission. I found the paper engaging, but I may have overlooked or misinterpreted certain aspects. I apologize if this is the case.

When evaluating an alternative hypothesis based on observed data, it is often necessary to assess the loss under the hypothesis being investigated. This can be achieved by reweighting the data points, as shown in the equation between (1) and (2), particularly when dealing with non-deterministic hypotheses such as conditional distributions.

The authors appear to address a problem related to, yet distinct from, the issue of large variance in weights. They refer to this problem as "propensity overfitting." The provided examples suggest that this issue arises when there is insufficient exploration, resulting in limited or no observations of y corresponding to the new hypothesis being evaluated for a given x. As a similar argument explains the large variance of weights, a more detailed explanation of the relationship between these two problems would be beneficial.

Although I did not have the opportunity to thoroughly examine the details or assess the novelty of the proposed solution, the underlying idea seems reasonable.

I appreciate that the authors have made their code available.

In summary, while I was unable to verify the technical soundness of the paper, it undoubtedly presents interesting ideas that warrant further consideration. Therefore, I recommend acceptance.
