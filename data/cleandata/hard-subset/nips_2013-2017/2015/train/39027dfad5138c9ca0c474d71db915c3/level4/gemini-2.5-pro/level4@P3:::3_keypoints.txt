- Addresses Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback (BLBF), building on Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) and IPS estimators.  
- Identifies "Propensity Overfitting": models can exploit IPS by manipulating propensity weights, yielding arbitrarily poor risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self‑normalized (ratio) estimator via multiplicative control variates that introduces small bias but is strongly consistent.  
- The new estimator is equivariant to loss translations and bounded by observed loss range, preventing IPS pathologies.  
- Develops Norm‑POEM (CRM with the new estimator) for structured output prediction with linear models.  
- Empirically, Norm‑POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multi‑label tasks and is robust to loss translations.  
- Strength: clear diagnosis and an illustrative example that exposes a subtle but severe failure mode.  
- Strength: principled, statistically grounded solution with theoretical analysis addressing IPS anomalies.  
- Strength: strong, convincing empirical results demonstrating practical benefits and robustness.  
- Strength: paper is well‑written, clearly motivated, and well organized.  
- Weakness: contribution is incremental—modifies only the risk estimator within existing CRM/POEM framework.  
- Weakness: experiments use supervised‑to‑bandit on academic datasets; unclear generalization to real‑world logging policies and noisy data.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on optimization behavior of Norm‑POEM (objective landscape, convergence comparisons).  
- Weakness: reporting could include variance (e.g., standard deviations) across runs for fuller result interpretation.  
- Overall: solid, well‑executed, likely influential work that merits acceptance.
