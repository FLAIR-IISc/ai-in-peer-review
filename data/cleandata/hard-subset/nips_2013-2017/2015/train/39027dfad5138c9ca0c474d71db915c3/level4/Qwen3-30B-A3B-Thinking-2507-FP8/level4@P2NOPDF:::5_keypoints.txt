- Proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Builds on and substantially improves POEM (Swaminathan & Joachims, 2015).  
- Introduces a novel reformulation of POEM with a new regularization mechanism.  
- Theoretical advance providing meaningful originality beyond existing approaches.  
- Empirical results show significant improvements in training the original POEM algorithm.  
- Achieves faster training despite added regularization, a counterintuitive efficiency gain.  
- Work is robust and has strong potential for real-world adoption (recommendation systems, online advertising).  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and experimentally clear.  
- Research quality is above average and the contribution is significant.  
- Overall: a strong, well-executed contribution that advances the state of the art.
