- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies a new failure mode called "propensity overfitting" in conventional counterfactual risk estimators.  
- Shows conventional unbiased estimator can produce arbitrarily biased risk estimates for complex hypothesis spaces due to interaction with propensity weights.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (Eq. 7) that bounds risk estimates within the loss range and is equivariant under loss translation.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm for structured output prediction based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (Theorems 1–2) that the self-normalized estimator avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements of Norm-POEM over POEM across multiple multi-label datasets (Table 1, Fig. 1).  
- Shows Norm-POEM maintains the sum of propensity weights near 1, evidencing avoidance of propensity overfitting (Table 2).  
- Reports Norm-POEM converges faster and is computationally efficient despite added normalization (Section 7).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and thoroughly situates itself within related work.  
- Strength: rigorously characterizes a novel problem and offers a theoretically sound, empirically validated solution with code and reproducible experiments.  
- Weakness: claim that "virtually all existing works" use the unbiased estimator is misleading—many prior works use clipped (biased) estimators and this should be qualified.  
- Weakness: Section 4 example needs correction/clarification about R̂(hoverfit) when n << k (should reflect number of distinct x_i).  
- Weakness: paper could better justify why multiplicative self-normalization is superior to other control variate or variance-reduction methods for this problem.  
- Originality: identifies a new phenomenon (propensity overfitting) and is first to apply self-normalization to this counterfactual learning issue.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental limitation in BLBF with direct implications for real-world systems (ads, recommendations) and shows consistent practical gains.  
- Recommendation: accept — theoretical contribution, empirical validation, and practical value outweigh minor clarifications needed.
