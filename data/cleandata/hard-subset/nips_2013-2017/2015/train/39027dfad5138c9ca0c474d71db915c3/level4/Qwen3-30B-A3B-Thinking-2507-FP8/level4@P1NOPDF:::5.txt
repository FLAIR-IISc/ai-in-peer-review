

The paper proposes a novel method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) that effectively avoids propensity overfitting. Building on Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), it introduces a substantially improved algorithm through a well-motivated extension to the original POEM framework.  

Quality: The research quality is strong. The proposed extensions yield significant improvements in training performance over the baseline POEM method, with rigorous theoretical grounding and empirical validation.  

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and logically structured, making the technical contributions accessible to readers familiar with bandit learning.  

Originality: The authors present a genuinely novel formulation for addressing BLBF, distinct from prior work. The core contributions—particularly the mechanism for avoiding propensity overfitting—are highly original.  

Significance: The results are compelling and warrant further investigation. Notably, the proposed algorithm achieves faster training than POEM despite incorporating additional regularization, a counterintuitive yet critical advancement. This efficiency, combined with the robustness gained from avoiding propensity overfitting, positions the method for broad adoption in practical applications. The work represents a meaningful step forward for BLBF research.  

This is a strong contribution to the field. The paper is well-executed, clearly argued, and makes a significant technical advance with clear practical implications.
