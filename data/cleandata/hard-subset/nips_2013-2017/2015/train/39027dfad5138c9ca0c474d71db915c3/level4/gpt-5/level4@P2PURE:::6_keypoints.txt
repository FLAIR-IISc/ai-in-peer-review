- Paper studies counterfactual risk minimization for learning from logged bandit data and correcting logging bias.
- Identifies an overfitting failure mode where hypotheses exploit example-generation probabilities to overemphasize well-supported logged examples.
- Proposes a multiplicative normalization parameter to create a self-normalized estimator that bounds variance.
- The normalization preserves consistency of the counterfactual estimator while reducing variance-driven overfitting.
- Experimental results show the normalized (multiplicative) counterfactual method outperforms the non-normalized alternative.
- Presents a practical technique for counterfactual learning that addresses bias/variance trade-offs in logged bandit settings.
- Reviewer verdict: positive—good paper recommended for acceptance, though not with absolute certainty.
