- Identifies "propensity overfitting" in BLBF where hypothesis spaces overfit logged propensity weights, biasing counterfactual risk estimates.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and concrete examples showing severe bias in ERM with the standard estimator (Section 4).  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates that avoids propensity overfitting and is consistent (Theorem 2).  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, applying the self-normalized estimator within the CRM framework for structured output prediction.  
- Empirical results on multiple multi-label datasets show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM, achieves lower test loss, Ŝ(ĥ) ≈ 1, and is robust to loss translations (Section 7).  
- Extends prior CRM work by characterizing and addressing a previously unrecognized failure mode in BLBF learning.  
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical guarantees (equivariance, bounded estimates, consistency) and thorough empirical validation (hyperparameter sensitivity, ablations).  
- Strength: Novelity in defining propensity overfitting and introducing a principled self-normalized estimator for BLBF.  
- Strength: Practical significance for real-world systems (ads, recommendations) with demonstrated improvements and no reported major computational penalty.  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured exposition with precise definitions, intuitive examples, and strong theory–experiment linkage.  
- Weakness: Needs clearer contrast between multiplicative control variates and additive alternatives (e.g., doubly robust estimators).  
- Weakness: Computational-cost discussion lacks quantified per-iteration overhead of normalization versus POEM’s variance estimator.  
- Recommendation: Strong candidate for acceptance; remaining issues are minor and addressable in revision.
