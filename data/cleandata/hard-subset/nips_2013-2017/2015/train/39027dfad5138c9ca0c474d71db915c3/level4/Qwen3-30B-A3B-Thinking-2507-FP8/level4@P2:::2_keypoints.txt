- Identifies propensity overfitting: the conventional inverse propensity estimator can become arbitrarily biased when used in ERM for BLBF.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (Eq. 7) using multiplicative control variates to eliminate propensity overfitting.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm that implements the self-normalized estimator within the CRM framework.  
- Provides a compelling counterexample (Example 1) showing the standard estimator can produce risk estimates arbitrarily lower than true minimum risk.  
- Proves key properties of the self-normalized estimator: equivariance to loss translations and boundedness within the loss range.  
- Offers formal theoretical guarantees (Theorems 1 and 2) supporting the new estimator and algorithm.  
- Empirical results across multiple multi-label datasets show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and approaches full-information CRF performance.  
- Experimental methodology is sound, using the Supervised→Bandit conversion to create controlled logged-bandit datasets.  
- Paper is technically rigorous and clearly written, with accessible notation and logical flow.  
- Novelty: uncovers and resolves a previously unrecognized problem in BLBF and situates work appropriately within related literature.  
- Significance: practical implications for ad placement, recommendation systems, and other applications relying on logged bandit feedback.  
- Minor weakness: could better link theoretical properties (equivariance, boundedness) directly to observed empirical improvements.  
- Overall: convincing, well-executed work with minor narrative improvements suggested; suitable for NIPS publication.
