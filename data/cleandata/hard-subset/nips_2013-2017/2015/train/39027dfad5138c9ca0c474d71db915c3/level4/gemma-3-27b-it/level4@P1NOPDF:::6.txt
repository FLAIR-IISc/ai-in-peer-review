Counterfactual risk minimization is a technique for learning in a bandit setting from logged data, addressing the bias inherent in how such data is generated. While the method is consistent, this paper demonstrates that it can suffer from overfitting – specifically, a hypothesis class can overfit based on the probability of generating a given example. To address this, the authors propose introducing a multiplicative parameter to bound the variance, while preserving consistency. Experimental results show that this multiplicative (or normalized) counterfactual technique outperforms its non-normalized counterpart.

This paper presents a method for learning a self-normalized estimator within a counterfactual learning framework for logged bandit problems. It identifies and tackles a form of overfitting where the hypothesis can become overly confident in logged data. This is a strong paper and, despite some lingering reservations, I believe it should be accepted.
