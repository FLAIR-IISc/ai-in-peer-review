- Paper identifies "propensity overfitting" in conventional unbiased counterfactual risk estimators, where minimization can focus on weight sums rather than low-loss examples.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator using a multiplicative control variate to reduce Monte Carlo variance and mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains the problem and proposed solution.  
- Provides thorough analysis of the conventional estimator's limitations.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness via experiments on multi-label classification tasks.  
- Self-normalized estimator is a sensible and well-motivated approach.  
- Claim that conventional estimator has unbounded variance needs clearer connection to propensity overfitting.  
- Statement that prior BLBF work focused on unbiased estimators should acknowledge that biased techniques (e.g., clipping) may have been used.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough but lacks a detailed analysis of computational efficiency.  
- Original contribution: novel application of a self-normalized estimator to the propensity overfitting problem.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with implications for more robust counterfactual learning algorithms.  
- Recommendation: accept, subject to clarifications on variance-overfitting linkage, prior-work claims, and computational analysis.
