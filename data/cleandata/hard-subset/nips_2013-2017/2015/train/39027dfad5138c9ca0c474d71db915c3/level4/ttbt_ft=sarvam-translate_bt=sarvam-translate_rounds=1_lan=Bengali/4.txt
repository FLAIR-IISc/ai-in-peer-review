== Other Comments ==
- In the setup of "Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback" in Section 3 (or from [1]), I can't find something: I think the feedback should be seen as a random variable rather than an unspecified function, as presented in line 110. Here's my reasoning. Suppose the partial feedback comes from a classification problem where we don't know the labels; we only make a prediction and get feedback based on the amount of loss. Suppose there's a true classification loss delta(y',y), and the method of generating the feedback is, for a given input x, we make a prediction y using h_0; an agent labels the input as y'; and then our loss amount delta(y',y) tells us (so here delta(x,y) is delta(y',y) for y' in this example). On the other hand, often in classification, we assume that labeling can be messy and therefore a unique y' may not be assigned to each x. This means that elsewhere in the log, we may have the same x as our input, the same y that we used, but another delta(x,y) because the agent gave a different label for this input... For this reason, I think delta(x,y) should generally be seen as a random number (in the classification example above, the average of this random variable for a given x and the y used should be the expectation of y' according to p(y'|x), which is the actual messy labeling distribution).

This probably doesn't change anything about the risk estimator (2); however, at least an additional expectation on the randomness of the delta with the actual risk in the setup of (1) should be presented, so that it becomes more general.
