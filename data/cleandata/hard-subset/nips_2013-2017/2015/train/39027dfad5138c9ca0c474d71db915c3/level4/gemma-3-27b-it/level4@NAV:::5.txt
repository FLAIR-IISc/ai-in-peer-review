This paper introduces a novel method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback, effectively addressing the propensity overfitting issue. Building upon the foundation laid by Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the proposed algorithm demonstrates substantial improvements over existing approaches.

**Quality:** The research presented is of high quality. The extensions to the original POEM algorithm yield significant gains in training performance.

**Clarity:** The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and readily understandable.

**Originality:** This work provides a significantly new formulation of the POEM algorithm specifically tailored for the batch learning from bandit feedback problem. The contributions are demonstrably original and represent a valuable advancement in the field.

**Significance:** The results are compelling and warrant further investigation. Notably, the proposed algorithm achieves faster performance than the original POEM, even with the incorporation of additional regularization. This efficiency suggests potential for widespread adoption in practical applications. 

Overall, this is a strong paper, well-written and easy to follow.
