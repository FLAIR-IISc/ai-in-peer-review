This paper proposes a novel approach to batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) by introducing a self-normalized estimator that avoids the propensity overfitting problem inherent in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the limitations of the existing estimator and demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed approach through extensive experiments.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning. The introduction provides a concise overview of the BLBF problem and the limitations of existing approaches. The related work section is comprehensive and provides a thorough understanding of the context in which the proposed approach is situated.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant. The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, namely propensity overfitting, and propose a novel solution using a self-normalized estimator. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in improving generalization performance.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem of BLBF is of significant interest to the machine learning community. The authors demonstrate the relevance of their approach to real-world problems, such as training online systems using historical logs.
