This paper addresses a critical issue in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF): propensity overfitting in the commonly used counterfactual risk estimator. The authors convincingly demonstrate this problem and propose a self-normalized estimator as a solution, leading to a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, which consistently outperforms the original POEM algorithm (Swaminathan & Joachims, 2015). The work builds directly upon prior research in counterfactual learning, importance sampling, and causal inference, presenting a significant and well-motivated contribution.

**Quality:** The technical quality of this paper is high. The identification of propensity overfitting is a novel and important insight. The proposed self-normalized estimator is well-justified theoretically, drawing connections to existing control variate techniques, and the empirical results strongly support its effectiveness. The authors carefully consider and address potential issues such as the computational cost of the new estimator, and demonstrate that Norm-POEM can, surprisingly, converge faster than POEM. The inclusion of theoretical guarantees (Theorem 1 & 2) adds to the rigor of the work. The paper appears complete and presents a comprehensive evaluation.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly organized. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and situates the work within the broader context of BLBF. The mathematical notation is generally accessible, and the explanations are concise without sacrificing clarity. However, the derivation of the variance estimator in Section 6 could benefit from further elaboration. While the authors reference existing work on approximate variance estimation, a slightly more detailed explanation of the specific application to the self-normalized estimator would be helpful.

**Originality:** The contribution of this paper is significant. While building upon existing techniques from causal inference and importance sampling, the identification and formalization of “propensity overfitting” represents a novel contribution. The application of a self-normalized estimator to address this issue in the context of BLBF is also original. The empirical validation across multiple datasets further strengthens the originality of the work. The paper thoroughly references related work, demonstrating an awareness of the existing literature.

**Significance:** The results presented in this paper are highly significant. Propensity overfitting represents a fundamental challenge for BLBF algorithms, and the proposed solution offers a practical and effective way to mitigate this problem. The consistent performance improvements of Norm-POEM over POEM, coupled with its potential for faster convergence, suggest that this algorithm could have a substantial impact on the field. The problem addressed is relevant to a wide range of applications, including ad placement, recommendation systems, and web search. The availability of the software implementation is also a positive aspect, encouraging further research and adoption.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant and previously unrecognized problem in BLBF (propensity overfitting).
*   Proposes a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution (self-normalized estimator and Norm-POEM).
*   Demonstrates substantial performance improvements over the state-of-the-art (POEM).
*   Presents a well-written and clearly organized paper with a thorough evaluation.
*   Provides an open-source implementation, facilitating reproducibility and further research.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The derivation of the variance estimator in Section 6 could benefit from further clarification.
*   While the speed-up observed in the experiments is intriguing, a deeper analysis of the computational complexity and scalability of Norm-POEM would be beneficial.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of batch learning from logged bandit feedback. The identification of propensity overfitting is a significant insight, and the proposed solution is both theoretically sound and empirically effective. I strongly recommend accepting this paper for NIPS 2017.

**References:**
