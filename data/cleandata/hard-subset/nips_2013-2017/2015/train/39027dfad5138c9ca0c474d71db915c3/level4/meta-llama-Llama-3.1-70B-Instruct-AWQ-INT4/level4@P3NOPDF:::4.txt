**Reviewer's Report**

**Clarification Request**
In Section 4.1 of [1], it is mentioned that the CRM approach is only applicable when delta is in the interval [-1,0], and the authors propose rescaling a general loss to this range. I assume that the same applies to NORM-POEM, implying that equations (8) and (3) both utilize the rescaled delta in [-1,0]. Could you please clarify why "delta > 0" is used in the experiment in Table 1, as it seems inconsistent with this assumption?

**Other Comments**

The setup for "batch learning from logged bandit feedback" in Section 3 (and in [1]) appears to be incomplete. Specifically, the feedback should be treated as a random variable rather than a deterministic function, as presented on line 110. To illustrate this, consider a classification problem where the true labels are unknown, and the feedback is obtained by making a prediction and observing the resulting loss. In this scenario, the partial feedback is generated by predicting y using h_0, and a "god agent" labels the input as y' and provides the loss Delta(y',y). However, due to noisy labeling, the same input x can have different labels y' and corresponding losses delta(x,y) in the log. Therefore, delta(x,y) should be considered a random variable, and the setup in equation (1) should include an additional expectation over the randomness of delta to account for this generality.

Minor corrections and suggestions:

* Line 170-171: The expression should be h(y|x) / h_0(y|x). It is also worth noting that the non-linearity issue was previously pointed out in [1].
* Line 276: Replace "argmax" with "argmin" since the risk is being minimized.
* Line 319: Use hats instead of bars in the notation to maintain consistency with the previous convention.

**Update after Rebuttal**
I appreciate the clarifications provided by the authors. Overall, I believe this is an interesting follow-up work on [1], offering valuable insights into "propensity overfitting" and introducing a multiplicative control variate to mitigate it. The results demonstrate a clear improvement over POEM in the context of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a problem of growing interest.
