Summary
The paper "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning" studies the problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a relevant problem in various applications such as ad ranking. The authors identify a severe "overfitting" problem in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and propose a solution based on multiplicative control variates, resulting in a self-normalized estimator.

Relation to Previous Work
The paper builds upon previous work on CRM [1] and extends it by introducing a new risk estimator that addresses the propensity overfitting problem. The authors also draw connections to other relevant areas, such as importance sampling, control variates, and counterfactual evaluation.

Quality
The paper is of high quality and well-motivated. The authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, propose a solution, and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments. However, as this is follow-up work on [1], additional experiments and analysis on the optimization of the training objective would have been appreciated.

Clarity
The presentation is clear, and the paper is well-written. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the proposed method, including the theoretical foundations and the experimental setup.

Originality
While the paper builds upon previous work, the introduction of the self-normalized estimator is novel and seems to make a significant difference in the experiments. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through a thorough experimental evaluation.

Significance
The problem of learning from logged data is relevant, and the proposed solution has the potential to improve the performance of various applications. However, it is difficult to say how the substantial experimental improvements translate to more real-world applications. Additional experiments and case studies would be necessary to fully assess the significance of the proposed approach.

Various Remarks
The authors should consider including measures of variance in their experimental results, especially when running multiple repetitions. Additionally, exploring more real-world applications and comparing the proposed approach to other state-of-the-art methods would strengthen the paper.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper addresses a relevant problem in batch learning from logged bandit feedback.
* The proposed solution, the self-normalized estimator, is novel and effective.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough and demonstrates the advantages of the proposed approach.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper is follow-up work on [1], and some additional experiments and analysis would have been appreciated.
* The significance of the proposed approach in real-world applications is not fully assessed.

Overall, the paper is solid, well-written, and relevant. With some additional experiments and analysis, it has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
