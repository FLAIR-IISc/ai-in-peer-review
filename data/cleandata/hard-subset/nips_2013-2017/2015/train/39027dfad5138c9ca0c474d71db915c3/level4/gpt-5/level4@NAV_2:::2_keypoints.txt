- Notation and discussion around estimator (2) are incorrect/misleading: the expression does not follow the expected form ∑ f(x)p(x) and the loss shown is not an expectation.  
- The claim that (2) exhibits an “anomaly” (non‑invariance under loss translations) is technically incorrect.  
- Example 1 is based on the faulty premise and therefore does not hold.  
- Practical weakness of (2): denominator pi can be zero if the logging policy never observed an event.  
- The modified estimator (7) is preferable in principle because its additional normalization reduces the impact of pi.  
- Reviewer requests the authors revise this part of the exposition if the paper is accepted.  
- Experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and performs close to a CRF trained with full multi-label supervision.  
- The paper situates counterfactual learning as a specialization of multi-label learning and targets improving the POEM estimator from ICML 2015.  
- Overall assessment: convincing and worthy of publication, subject to the raised caveats.
