Counterfactual risk minimization is a technique for learning in a logged bandit setting, addressing the bias inherent in using logged data. This paper identifies an overfitting issue within this framework: the hypothesis class can overfit to the data generation probability. To mitigate this, the authors propose a method employing a multiplicative parameter to bound the variance, while preserving consistency. Experimental results demonstrate that this normalized counterfactual technique outperforms its non-normalized counterpart. 

The paper introduces a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning specifically designed for logged bandit problems. It tackles a novel form of overfitting where the hypothesis unduly favors logged data. Overall, this is a strong paper and recommended for acceptance, though with some remaining uncertainty.
