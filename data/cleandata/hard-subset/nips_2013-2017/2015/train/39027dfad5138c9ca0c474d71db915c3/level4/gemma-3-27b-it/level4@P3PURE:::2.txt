The commentary and notations concerning the main equation – (2) – appear incorrect or at least misleading. An expectation is a quantity of the form \sum f(x) p(x), where the expected quantity, f(x), is everything *except* the probability measure p(x). In equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity. Consequently, the claim on page 2, second paragraph, that equation (2) exhibits an “anomaly” due to a lack of invariance to translation of the loss is technically inaccurate. The ensuing Example 1 is predicated on this flawed assumption and, unsurprisingly, does not hold.

In practical terms, the estimator in (2) is sensitive to its denominator, π<sub>i</sub>, which can be zero if a sample has never been observed with a particular prior. The modified estimator in (7) appears superior in principle, as the effects of π<sub>i</sub> are mitigated by the double denominator. 

I hope the authors will consider revising their commentary should the paper be accepted.

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across all datasets and achieves performance close to that of a CRF utilizing full multi-label information. This paper presents an approach for “counterfactual learning,” a learning scenario that can be viewed as a specialization of multi-label learning. While standard multi-label learning employs 0/1 ground-truth annotations for each class, this work instead utilizes the label associated with a single class – analogous to bandit feedback. However, this framing serves primarily as justification; the paper’s core objective is to improve an estimator (POEM) recently introduced in reference [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, the paper is convincing and worthy of publication, though I have included further remarks in the “Comments to Authors” section.
