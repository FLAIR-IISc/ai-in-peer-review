This paper presents an approach for "counterfactual learning," a scenario that can be viewed as a specialization of multi-label learning where feedback is limited to a single label per sample (i.e., bandit feedback). The paper's precise aim is to improve the POEM estimator recently presented by Swaminathan and Joachims (ICML 2015). Overall, the paper is convincing and appears worthy of publication, but several remarks must be addressed.

The primary concern relates to the commentary and notation for the main equation (2), which seem incorrect or at least misleading. An expectation is a quantity of the form `\sum f(x) p(x)`, where the expected quantity, `f(x)`, is distinct from the probability measure, `p(x)`. In Equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity but is incorporated into the probability measure. Therefore, the subsequent comment (page 2, second paragraph) that Equation (2) has an "anomaly" because it is not invariant to a translation of the loss is technically incorrect. This property is a direct consequence of the estimator's definition, not an anomaly. The ensuing Example 1 is based on this flawed premise and, unsurprisingly, its conclusion does not hold. I strongly urge the authors to revise this commentary should the paper be accepted.

From a practical perspective, the estimator in Equation (2) is vulnerable to its denominator, `pi`, which can be zero if a sample has never been observed in the prior data. The modified estimator in Equation (7) seems better in principle, as the effects of `pi` are mitigated through the normalization in the denominator.

The experimental results show that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and its performance is not far from that of a CRF using full multi-label information. These strong empirical results are a key strength of the paper.
