- Paper identifies "propensity overfitting" in BLBF: learners overfit importance weights, producing degenerate empirical risks when optimizing IPS over rich hypothesis classes.
- Proposes a self-normalized (SN) counterfactual risk estimator (IPS ratio with multiplicative control variates) that is bounded, equivariant to additive loss shifts, and strongly consistent.
- Integrates SN estimator into CRM to create Norm-POEM for structured-output prediction with exponential-family models.
- Experiments on multi-label datasets show consistent gains over POEM, better robustness to loss translations, competitive runtime, and provided code.
- Builds on IPS, variance reduction, and doubly robust literature; novelty is diagnosing propensity overfitting and applying SN within CRM for learning.
- Would benefit from clearer positioning relative to doubly robust and weighted/self-normalized DR estimators.
- Theory: proves strong consistency and argues boundedness/equivariance; uses delta-method approximation for variance in CRM but lacks a full generalization bound for the SN-CRM objective.
- Empirics: appropriate Supervised→Bandit protocol, consistent results, and convincing diagnostics (Ŝ(h)) showing mitigation of propensity overfitting.
- Empirics would be stronger with comparisons to clipped IPS and (weighted) doubly robust baselines.
- Reproducibility is good: clear setup and available code.
- Clarity: overall well written with a good motivating example and diagnostics.
- Clarity: needs explicit discussion of relation between "variance explosion" in IPS and "propensity overfitting" to clarify similarities/differences.
- Clarity: clarify interplay between self-normalization and CRM’s variance regularization — when each helps and whether either alone suffices.
- Originality: using SN IS is not novel, but identifying propensity overfitting and showing SN+CRM improves learning in BLBF is a useful contribution.
- Significance: practical importance for off-policy learning; simple estimator change improves robustness and generalization.
- Specific: clarify Example 1 (n ≪ k) and the derivation showing R̂(hoverfit) ≤ −k versus R̂(h*) to avoid confusion.
- Specific: soften claim that prior works used "unbiased" estimators, and compare to common biased practices like clipping/truncation.
- Specific: discuss finite-sample bias–variance trade-off of SN, especially under clipping and interaction with CRM variance penalty.
- Specific: comment on the full-support logging assumption and robustness when support is imperfect (e.g., need for smoothing or exploration scavenging).
- Specific: note conditions for accuracy of the delta-method variance approximation (existence of second moments under clipping/moderate dispersion).
- Specific: report model norm of w and training loss traces for POEM and Norm-POEM to illustrate overfitting dynamics.
- Pros: clear identification/diagnosis of a real failure mode and a simple principled fix with theoretical and intuitive support.
- Pros: consistent empirical gains, robustness to loss translation, informative diagnostic Ŝ(h), practical algorithm with code.
- Cons: missing comparisons to doubly robust/weighted DR baselines.
- Cons: theoretical treatment of CRM with ratio estimators is approximate; needs a more detailed bound/analysis.
- Cons: relies on full-support logging assumption; discussion of violations could be expanded.
- Recommendation: reviewer finds ideas compelling and evidence persuasive and recommends acceptance.
