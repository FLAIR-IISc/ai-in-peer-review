Other comments:
- I believe the setup for “batch learning from logged bandit feedback” in Section 3 (and possibly in [1]) is missing an important detail: the feedback should be treated as a random variable rather than a deterministic function, as presented around line 110. Rationale: consider a classification setting where labels are unknown and we only observe loss after making a prediction. Let there be a true classification loss Δ(y′, y). For a given input x, the logging policy h0 selects an action y; a “god” agent also produces a label y′; and the observed feedback is Δ(y′, y), so δ(x, y) equals Δ(y′, y) for the y′ associated with that instance. In many classification problems, labeling is noisy, so there may not be a unique y′ for each x. Consequently, the same x and the same played y could appear elsewhere in the log with a different δ(x, y), simply because the “god” agent provided a different y′. Therefore, in general δ(x, y) should be modeled as a random variable. In this example, for fixed x and played y, the mean of δ(x, y) is E_{y′|x}[Δ(y′, y)], where y′ is drawn from the true (possibly noisy) labeling distribution p(y′|x). This may not affect the estimator in (2), but the true risk in (1) should include an additional expectation over the randomness of δ to reflect this generality.
- Lines 170–171: I think the ratio should be h(y|x) / h0(y|x). It is also worth noting that the lack of linearity was already pointed out in [1].
- Line 276: You should use argmin rather than argmax, since you are minimizing risk.
- Line 319: Replace bars with hats in the notation to be consistent with your earlier convention.
