- Paper addresses Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback (BLBF) and identifies a critical flaw in the standard unbiased counterfactual risk estimator called "propensity overfitting."

- Propensity overfitting occurs when complex hypothesis classes allow minimizing the estimator by manipulating propensity weights instead of fitting true loss, yielding arbitrarily poor risk estimates.

- Proposed remedy: replace the standard estimator with a self-normalized risk estimator derived via multiplicative control variates.

- The self-normalized estimator is equivariant to translations in the loss and bounded by the observed loss range, avoiding propensity overfitting.

- Building on CRM, the authors introduce Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm using the improved estimator.

- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM across multiple multi-label classification datasets and training sizes.

- Table 2 analysis demonstrates Norm-POEM keeps the sum of propensity weights Ŝ(ĥ) stable and close to its expected value of 1, supporting mitigation of propensity overfitting.

- Strength: clear identification and articulation of a novel, important failure mode in importance-sampling-based estimators.

- Strength: principled, literature-grounded solution using control variates and importance-sampling ideas.

- Strength: strong empirical validation showing substantial generalization improvements.

- Strength: high practical relevance to search, recommendation, and advertising, making the contribution significant.

- Weakness: unclear treatment of loss scaling—CRM theory assumes losses in [-1,0]; experiments using positive Hamming loss need clarification on how delta was handled.

- Weakness: problem formulation treats delta(x,y) as deterministic; analysis should generalize to stochastic feedback by adding an expectation over delta.

- Minor corrections: suspected typo in Line 170-171 (should likely use a ratio h/h0), note that lack of equivariance was previously mentioned in [1], and suggest consistent notation (use hat for test loss R̂). 

- Summary/recommendation: solid, well-motivated contribution that identifies propensity overfitting and proposes an effective solution; minor clarifications needed but overall a significant advance.
