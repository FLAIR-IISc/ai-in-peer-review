- Paper proposes a counterfactual learning method (special case of multi-label learning with bandit feedback) aiming to improve the POEM estimator (Swaminathan & Joachims, ICML 2015).
- Overall the paper is convincing and appears worthy of publication, but several issues must be addressed.
- Primary concern: the commentary and notation for Equation (2) are incorrect or misleading—an expectation should be ∑f(x)p(x), but Equation (2) mixes the loss into the probability measure.
- The claim that Equation (2) has an “anomaly” because it isn’t invariant to translation of the loss is technically incorrect; that property follows from the estimator’s definition, not an anomaly.
- Example 1 is based on the flawed premise about Equation (2), so its conclusion does not hold; the authors should revise this commentary.
- Practical issue: the estimator in Equation (2) is vulnerable to its denominator π, which can be zero if a sample was never observed in prior data.
- The modified estimator in Equation (7) is preferable in principle, as it mitigates the effects of π via normalization in the denominator.
- Empirically, Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and performs close to a fully supervised CRF, which is a key strength of the paper.
