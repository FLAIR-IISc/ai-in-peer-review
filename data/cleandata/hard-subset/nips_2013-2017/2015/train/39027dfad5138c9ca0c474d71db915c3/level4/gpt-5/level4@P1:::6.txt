This paper studies counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) for batch learning from logged bandit feedback and identifies a critical overfitting issue with the conventional inverse propensity scoring (IPS) estimator. The authors show that, although IPS is consistent, empirical risk minimization over complex hypothesis classes can exploit variation in logging propensities, leading to “propensity overfitting,” where hypotheses effectively overfit by concentrating or avoiding mass on logged actions rather than truly minimizing loss. To address this, the paper proposes a self-normalized estimator based on a multiplicative control variate (normalizing by the sum of importance weights), which reduces sensitivity to weight fluctuations while preserving consistency. Building on CRM, the authors instantiate a learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, and empirically demonstrate that the normalized approach reliably outperforms the non-normalized baseline on multilabel classification tasks.

Relation to prior work: The paper is grounded in importance sampling and CRM for learning from logged bandit data, and it connects naturally to variance reduction techniques. The use of multiplicative control variates (self-normalization) is well known in the Monte Carlo literature, and the paper adapts this to counterfactual learning to mitigate the specific failure mode of IPS under ERM. The contribution is thus a principled modification of the standard estimator that is directly usable within CRM.

Quality: The core technical argument is clear: the IPS objective can be driven down via propensity manipulation rather than loss reduction, and self-normalization counteracts this by making the estimator equivariant and bounded by the loss range, which prevents extreme empirical risk artifacts. The proposed estimator maintains consistency and is paired with a practical variance estimate suitable for CRM-style variance regularization. The empirical results consistently favor the normalized method over the conventional one.

Clarity: The paper explains the propensity overfitting phenomenon and the motivation for the self-normalized estimator clearly. The presentation of Norm-POEM and the experimental methodology is straightforward and easy to follow.

Originality: The identification of propensity overfitting as a distinct failure mode in BLBF and the application of a self-normalized estimator within CRM constitute a meaningful and original contribution in this context.

Significance: The problem addressed is important for off-policy learning from logs, and the proposed fix appears robust and practically impactful. The results suggest the normalized estimator is a safer default for CRM-based learning.

Pros:
- Clearly identifies and articulates a real and consequential overfitting problem in IPS-based CRM.
- Proposes a simple, principled self-normalized alternative that maintains consistency and improves robustness.
- Demonstrates consistent empirical gains over the non-normalized estimator.

Cons:
- While I find the approach compelling and the evidence positive, I am not absolutely certain; my confidence is moderate.

Recommendation: This is a good paper and should be accepted.
