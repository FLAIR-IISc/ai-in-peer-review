- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Proposes a self-normalized counterfactual risk estimator using a multiplicative control variate.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm built on the self-normalized estimator.
- The estimator is more stable and equivariant, reducing propensity overfitting.
- Theoretical justification provided (Theorem 1 and Theorem 2) for the proposed method.
- Empirical evaluation on multi-label classification shows consistent gains over POEM.
- Norm-POEM often achieves better generalization and faster convergence in training.
- Paper is technically sound with rigorous analysis and thorough experiments.
- Overall clarity is good, though some derivations (e.g., variance estimator, Eq. 8) may be hard to follow.
- Contribution is original and substantial, not a minor tweak to existing importance-sampling methods.
- Results are significant with potential practical impact in ads, recommendations, and search.
- Arguments for acceptance: identifies a key problem, offers a theoretically and empirically validated solution, demonstrates consistent performance gains, and is well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: variance estimator derivation can be difficult to follow, experiments could cover more datasets/domains, and computational complexity needs deeper analysis.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with high confidence.
