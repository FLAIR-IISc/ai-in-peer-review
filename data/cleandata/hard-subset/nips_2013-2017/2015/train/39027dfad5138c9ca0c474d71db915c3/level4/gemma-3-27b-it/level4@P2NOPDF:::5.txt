This paper presents a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that addresses the propensity overfitting problem. Building upon the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the authors propose a significantly improved algorithm.

**Quality:** The research presented is of high quality. The extensions to the original POEM algorithm demonstrably lead to substantial improvements in training performance. The paper provides convincing evidence of these gains.

**Clarity:** The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and easy to understand. The explanations are generally concise and the technical details are presented in a logical manner.

**Originality:** This work offers a novel formulation of the POEM algorithm specifically tailored for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. The contributions are demonstrably original and represent a meaningful advancement in the field.

**Significance:** The results presented are compelling and warrant further investigation. I am particularly impressed by the algorithm’s efficiency; it achieves faster training times than the original POEM implementation *despite* incorporating additional regularization. This improved performance suggests the potential for widespread adoption of the proposed method in practical applications. The work addresses a critical issue in offline learning – propensity overfitting – and offers a promising solution. 

Overall, this is a strong paper with clear writing and a valuable contribution to the literature. The authors have successfully addressed an important problem with a well-designed and thoroughly evaluated algorithm. It is a worthwhile contribution to NIPS 2015 and has the potential to influence future research in this area.
