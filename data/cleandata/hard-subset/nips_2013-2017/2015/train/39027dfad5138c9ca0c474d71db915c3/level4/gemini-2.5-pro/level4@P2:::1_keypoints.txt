- Identifies "propensity overfitting": IPS can be minimized by hypotheses that exploit propensity weights, yielding arbitrarily low risk estimates in high-capacity hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes replacing IPS with a self-normalized estimator (control-variate style) that normalizes weighted losses by sum of importance weights.  
- Self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss shifts and bounded by the loss range, preventing propensity overfitting.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a CRM algorithm using the self-normalized estimator for exponential models.  
- Empirical evaluation (Supervised→Bandit protocol) shows Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM.  
- Code release supports reproducibility.  
- Strength — originality/significance: clearly diagnoses a subtle, important failure mode distinct from conventional IPS variance concerns.  
- Strength — solution quality: elegant, principled, well-justified connection to multiplicative control variates.  
- Strength — clarity: well-written paper with effective motivating examples and concise theoretical arguments.  
- Strength — empirical soundness: thorough experiments confirming improved generalization and robustness to loss transformations.  
- Weakness — relation to high variance: needs deeper discussion of how propensity overfitting relates to IPS high-variance issues (both aggravated by small propensities).  
- Weakness — prior work characterization: claiming most BLBF work focuses on a single unbiased estimator is too strong; many methods use biased variance-control fixes (e.g., clipping).  
- Weakness — Example 1 clarity: the derivation of R̂(hoverfit) ≤ −k should be spelled out step-by-step for readers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem identification, simple theoretically sound fix, strong empirical gains, clear presentation, likely impactful.  
- Argument against acceptance: need a sharper distinction between propensity overfitting and classic importance-sampling variance problems.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
