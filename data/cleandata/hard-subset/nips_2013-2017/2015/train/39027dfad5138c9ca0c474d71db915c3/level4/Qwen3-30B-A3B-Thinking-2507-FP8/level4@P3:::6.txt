

This paper identifies a significant limitation in the counterfactual risk estimation approach used in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), a setting critical for improving interactive systems using historical logs. The authors demonstrate that the conventional unbiased counterfactual risk estimator suffers from "propensity overfitting" when applied to complex hypothesis spaces, where the estimator's risk can become arbitrarily biased due to the interaction between the propensity weights and the loss function. They propose a self-normalized risk estimator that avoids this problem by leveraging multiplicative control variates, and derive a new learning algorithm called Norm-POEM for structured output prediction using linear rules.

The paper makes several important contributions. First, it identifies a previously unrecognized problem in the BLBF literature that can lead to severe degeneracies in empirical risk minimization. The propensity overfitting phenomenon is well-documented through theoretical analysis and concrete examples, showing that conventional estimators can produce risk estimates that are arbitrarily lower (or higher) than the true risk, unlike standard overfitting in supervised learning. Second, the authors provide a theoretically sound solution through the self-normalized estimator, which is shown to be equivariant under loss translation and bounded within the loss range, properties that the conventional estimator lacks. Third, they derive Norm-POEM, a practical learning algorithm that incorporates the new estimator, and demonstrate through extensive experiments that it consistently outperforms the conventional approach across multiple datasets.

The paper is technically sound and well-structured. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, with clear proofs of the estimator's consistency and properties. The empirical evaluation is comprehensive, using multiple multi-label classification datasets from the LibSVM repository, and the experimental design follows the established Supervised 7→Bandit methodology. The results consistently show that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM (the baseline using the conventional estimator) across all datasets and evaluation metrics, with the self-normalized estimator successfully avoiding propensity overfitting as evidenced by the stability of the propensity weight sum Ŝ(h) around its expected value of 1.

The paper makes an original contribution by identifying and solving a previously overlooked problem in BLBF. While the use of self-normalized estimators has been explored in importance sampling, the authors are the first to recognize and address their relevance to propensity overfitting in counterfactual learning. The paper adequately references related work on counterfactual estimation, causal inference, and variance reduction techniques, though it could better connect to recent advances in off-policy evaluation in reinforcement learning.

The significance of this work is substantial. BLBF is a critical technique for improving real-world interactive systems using historical logs, and the propensity overfitting problem represents a fundamental limitation in current practice. The authors' solution not only resolves this issue but also leads to improved generalization performance without compromising computational efficiency. The Norm-POEM algorithm achieves better results than the conventional approach while maintaining similar computational complexity, making it practically useful for real-world applications.

The paper is clearly written with a logical flow from problem identification to theoretical solution to empirical validation. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-explained, and the experimental section provides sufficient detail for reproducibility. The only minor clarity issue is the discussion of the variance estimator in Section 6, where the connection between the self-normalized estimator and the variance calculation could be more explicit.

Strengths:
- Identifies and solves a significant, previously unrecognized problem in BLBF
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-motivated
- Empirical results are comprehensive and consistently demonstrate superiority of the proposed approach
- Clear connection between theory and practice through the Norm-POEM algorithm
- Well-written and well-organized, with sufficient detail for reproducibility

Weaknesses:
- The connection to recent advances in off-policy evaluation could be more explicit
- The discussion of computational efficiency could include more detailed runtime comparisons
- The theoretical analysis of the self-normalized estimator's variance properties could be expanded

The paper makes a strong contribution to the field by addressing a fundamental limitation in counterfactual learning. The propensity overfitting problem is significant because it can lead to systematically misleading risk estimates that degrade learning performance. The authors' solution is both theoretically sound and empirically validated, demonstrating clear practical benefits. The paper's contributions align well with NIPS's focus on high-quality scientific contributions that advance the state of the art in machine learning.

Pro-acceptance arguments:
1. The paper identifies a fundamental problem in a widely used technique (counterfactual risk minimization) that has been overlooked in prior work
2. The proposed solution (self-normalized estimator) is theoretically sound and effectively resolves the identified problem
3. The empirical results consistently demonstrate significant improvements over the state-of-the-art
4. The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility
5. The work has clear practical significance for improving interactive systems using historical logs

Con-acceptance arguments:
1. The paper could better connect to recent advances in off-policy evaluation in reinforcement learning
2. The computational efficiency claims could be more thoroughly validated with runtime comparisons
3. The theoretical analysis of the self-normalized estimator's variance properties could be expanded

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of counterfactual learning. The problem identified is important, the solution is well-motivated and rigorously analyzed, and the empirical results consistently demonstrate its superiority. The paper meets all the NIPS quality criteria and should be accepted.
