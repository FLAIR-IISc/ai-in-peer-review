The paper "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning" studies the problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a relevant problem in various applications such as ad ranking. The authors identify a severe issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in prior works on batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), specifically a propensity overfitting problem that causes severely biased risk estimates.

The authors propose an alternative estimator, the self-normalized estimator, which provably avoids this problem. They derive a new learning algorithm, Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models (Norm-POEM), based on the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle using the new estimator. The experimental results show that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional estimator, especially in terms of generalization performance.

The paper is well-written and clearly presents the problem, the proposed solution, and the experimental results. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and its implications on the CRM principle. The self-normalized estimator is a novel contribution, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness in addressing the propensity overfitting issue.

The paper's quality is high, and the authors provide a solid motivation for their work. The presentation is clear, and the paper is well-organized. The originality of the paper lies in the modification of the risk estimator to be self-normalizing, which seems to make a significant difference in the experiments.

The significance of the paper is relevant, as the problem of learning from logged data is an important one in many applications. However, it is difficult to say how the substantial experimental improvements translate to more real-world applications. The authors could have provided more insights into the practical implications of their work and its potential applications in various domains.

One minor suggestion is to include some measure of variance in the experimental results, especially since the authors run several repetitions of the experiments. Additionally, it would be interesting to see some extended experiments and additional applications beyond those in [1], including some actual real-world applications.

Overall, the paper is solid, well-written, and relevant, and the work is novel and makes a significant contribution to the field of counterfactual learning. With some additional experiments and analysis, the paper could be even stronger.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper identifies a significant issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator and proposes a novel solution.
* The self-normalized estimator is a new contribution that addresses the propensity overfitting problem.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed solution.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presents the problem, the proposed solution, and the experimental results.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from more insights into the practical implications of the work and its potential applications in various domains.
* The authors could have provided more extended experiments and additional applications beyond those in [1].
* The paper could be stronger with some additional analysis on the optimization of the training objective.
