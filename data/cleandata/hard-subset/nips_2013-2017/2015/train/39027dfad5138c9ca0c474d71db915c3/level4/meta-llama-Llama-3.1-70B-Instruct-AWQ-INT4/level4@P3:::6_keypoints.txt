- Identifies propensity overfitting in conventional counterfactual risk estimators, causing degenerate risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (Norm-POEM) to avoid propensity overfitting and yield more robust risk estimates.  
- Provides theoretical explanation and empirical evidence for the propensity overfitting issue.  
- Demonstrates Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple datasets in comprehensive experiments.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly explains the problem and proposed solution.  
- Technical exposition is clear, with consistent notation and easy-to-follow mathematical derivations.  
- Related work discussion is thorough and contributions are clearly articulated.  
- Strengths: addresses a significant limitation, well-justified estimator, comprehensive experiments, clear writing.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with counterfactual learning and logged bandits, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation could include additional baselines or comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, strong justification, and consistent empirical improvements.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited accessibility for non-experts and incomplete experimental comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing accessibility and expanding experimental comparisons.
