This paper addresses an overfitting problem inherent in counterfactual risk minimization (CRM), a technique for learning from logged bandit data by correcting for the sampling bias of the logging policy. The authors show that while the standard CRM estimator is consistent, it is prone to overfitting, where a hypothesis can be unduly influenced by examples with low generation probabilities.

To mitigate this issue, the paper proposes a self-normalized estimator. This approach introduces a multiplicative parameter that bounds the variance of the risk estimate while importantly preserving the estimator's consistency. The experimental results validate this technique, demonstrating that the proposed normalized counterfactual risk minimization consistently outperforms the non-normalized version.

Overall, this is a well-motivated paper that addresses a significant problem in counterfactual learning. The proposed solution is elegant and empirically verified. While my confidence is not absolute, I believe the paper makes a solid contribution and recommend it for acceptance.
