- Identifies propensity overfitting in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator when applying ERM to complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator that avoids propensity overfitting, leading to the Norm-POEM algorithm for structured output prediction.  
- Shows the conventional estimator exhibits severe anomalies in risk estimation and rigorously characterizes this phenomenon (Section 4).  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis proving the self-normalized estimator resolves the problem while maintaining consistency (Theorem 2).  
- Empirical evaluation on multiple multi-label datasets demonstrates improved generalization performance and robustness (Section 7, Table 1, Figure 1).  
- Addresses computational considerations and shows Norm-POEM converges faster than POEM despite a more complex estimator.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with consistent notation and sufficient experimental details for reproducibility (hyperparameters, L-BFGS).  
- Novel contribution that meaningfully advances BLBF by solving a previously unrecognized limitation of the standard estimator, distinct from prior CRM/POEM work.  
- Results have strong practical significance for real-world BLBF applications (ads, recommender systems) and potential for widespread adoption.  
- Pro: Theoretically sound solution, significant empirical improvements, and practical algorithmic benefits.  
- Con: Does not explore theoretical limits of the self-normalized estimator in extremely high-dimensional settings.
