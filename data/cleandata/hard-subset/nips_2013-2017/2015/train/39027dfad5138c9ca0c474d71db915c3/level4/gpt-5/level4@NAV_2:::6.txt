Counterfactual risk minimization targets learning from logged bandit data by correcting for the selection bias introduced by the logging policy. Although consistent, the paper shows that the standard inverse-propensity estimator can overfit by exploiting the generation probabilities of the logged examples (“propensity overfitting”). The authors propose addressing this with a multiplicative control variate—self-normalization of the importance weights—that mitigates variance and preserves consistency. Empirically, the self-normalized (normalized) counterfactual estimator consistently outperforms the unnormalized baseline.

The paper introduces a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting and tackles a form of overfitting where hypotheses gain advantage by aligning with the logged data’s support. This is a strong paper and should be accepted, though I am not absolutely certain.
