This paper addresses a critical issue in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF): the propensity overfitting problem inherent in the commonly used counterfactual risk estimator. The authors identify that this estimator, while unbiased, can exhibit high variance and lead to poor generalization, particularly with complex hypothesis spaces. They propose a self-normalized estimator as a solution, demonstrating its theoretical advantages and empirical effectiveness through a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, on several multi-label classification problems. Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the baseline POEM algorithm and approaches the performance of a full-information CRF.

The work builds on the established framework of counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) [1] and connects to broader areas of causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation. The authors appropriately contextualize their contribution within this landscape, citing relevant prior work. The problem of propensity overfitting is well-motivated, and the proposed self-normalized estimator offers a compelling alternative. The derivation of Norm-POEM is clear, and the experimental setup closely follows established methodologies in the field, allowing for direct comparison with previous results. The authors also thoughtfully provide implementation details.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses an Important Problem:** The paper tackles a significant practical issue in BLBF, namely the instability of the standard counterfactual risk estimator.
*   **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The introduction of the self-normalized estimator is backed by a clear theoretical analysis, demonstrating its ability to avoid propensity overfitting.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple datasets, demonstrating the practical benefits of the proposed approach. The near-CRF performance is particularly noteworthy.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the core ideas accessible.
*   **Reproducibility:** The authors provide a link to the implementation, facilitating reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Initial Commentary on Equation (2):** A portion of the initial review focuses on a perceived technical inaccuracy in the paper’s discussion of Equation (2) and the associated claims about invariance to loss translation. While the author's technical assessment is presented as potentially incorrect, it highlights a potential area of confusion. The core contribution of the paper remains valid irrespective of this specific point.
*   **Computational Complexity:** While the authors mention that Norm-POEM’s runtime is comparable to a full-information CRF, a more detailed discussion of the computational cost, especially in relation to the variance regularization step, would be beneficial.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter calibration but doesn’t delve into the sensitivity of the results to the choice of *M* and *λ*. A brief analysis of this sensitivity would strengthen the evaluation.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound solution to a practical problem in BLBF.
*   The empirical results are strong and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented, making the ideas accessible to a broad audience.
*   The code availability promotes reproducibility and further research.
*   The contribution is significant and likely to be of interest to researchers and practitioners working in the field of learning from logged data.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The initial commentary regarding Equation (2) indicates a potential for misinterpretation, though not fatal to the core contribution.
*   A more detailed analysis of computational complexity and hyperparameter sensitivity would strengthen the paper.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of learning from logged bandit feedback. While the identified weaknesses are present, they are relatively minor and do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. The theoretical and empirical evidence presented convincingly demonstrates the benefits of the proposed self-normalized estimator and the Norm-POEM algorithm. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised in the “Comments to authors” section.



**Comments to Authors:**

1.  **Clarify Equation (2) Discussion:** Acknowledge and briefly address the concerns raised regarding the commentary on Equation (2). Perhaps a sentence clarifying the scope of the analysis and reaffirming the validity of the main arguments.
2.  **Computational Cost:** Provide a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity of Norm-POEM, particularly the variance regularization step.
3.  **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Include a brief analysis of the sensitivity of the results to the choice of *M* and *λ*. Show how performance changes with different settings.
4.  **Typographical Errors:** Proofread carefully for minor typos and grammatical errors.
