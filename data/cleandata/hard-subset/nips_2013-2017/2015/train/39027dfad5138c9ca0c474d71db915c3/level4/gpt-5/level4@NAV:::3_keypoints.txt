- Tackles batch learning from logged bandit feedback with applications like ad ranking.
- Identifies an overfitting issue in the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle.
- Proposes multiplicative control variates, producing a self-normalizing (biased) estimator as a remedy.
- High-quality and well-motivated work but is follow-up to prior paper [1].
- Reviewer expected more extensive experiments and deeper analysis of training-objective optimization (beyond brief discussion around line 415).
- Presentation is clear and well written.
- The self-normalizing risk estimator modification appears to yield large empirical improvements.
- Significance is high given the problem, but it is uncertain how gains will generalize to broader real-world applications.
- Request to report variability (e.g., variance or error bars) if multiple repetitions were run.
- Overall: solid, relevant, and novel contribution, but would benefit from more experiments and real-world deployments beyond those in [1].
