- Paper identifies a critical failure mode in CRM called "Propensity Overfitting," where learners manipulate propensity weights to artificially lower empirical risk.  
- Propensity overfitting arises with complex hypothesis spaces by placing mass on unobserved actions or concentrating on logged actions.  
- Authors propose replacing the IPS estimator with a self-normalized (ratio) estimator derived via a multiplicative control variate.  
- The self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss shifts and bounded by observed loss range, preventing the identified anomalies.  
- Theoretical results include consistency (Theorem 2) and analysis of desirable estimator properties.  
- The authors develop Norm-POEM, a normalized policy optimizer for exponential models, instantiating the idea for structured output prediction.  
- Experiments on multi-label datasets show Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM, validating the practical impact.  
- Empirical tests directly measure propensity weight sums and robustness to loss translation, supporting the core hypothesis.  
- Strength: novel and important diagnosis of propensity overfitting, with a clear illustrative example.  
- Strength: elegant, principled solution with solid theoretical and empirical support.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing connecting theory and experiments effectively.  
- Weakness: finite-sample bias of the self-normalized estimator needs discussion on potential drawbacks and trade-offs.  
- Weakness: experiments rely on supervised→bandit simulation; evaluation on real logged bandit data would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: variance estimate for the ratio estimator uses approximations (delta/Normal); alternatives like bootstrap could be considered.  
- Final recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance.
