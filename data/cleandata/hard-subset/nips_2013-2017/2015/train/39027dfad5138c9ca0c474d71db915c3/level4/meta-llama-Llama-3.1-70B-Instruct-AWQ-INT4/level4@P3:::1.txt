This paper proposes an alternative estimator for counterfactual learning in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional unbiased counterfactual risk estimator, which they term "propensity overfitting." This issue arises when the estimator can be minimized by maximizing or minimizing the sum of the weights, rather than by putting large probability mass on examples with low loss. The proposed self-normalized estimator addresses this problem by using a multiplicative control variate to reduce the variance of the Monte Carlo approximation.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the problem and the proposed solution. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the conventional estimator's limitations and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on multi-label classification problems. The use of a self-normalized estimator is a sensible solution, and the authors provide a clear explanation of its benefits.

However, there are a few points that require clarification. For instance, the authors mention that the conventional estimator has unbounded variance, but it is not entirely clear how this relates to the propensity overfitting problem. Additionally, the authors claim that prior works on BLBF have focused on an unbiased estimator, but it is possible that some of these works have used techniques like clipping to reduce variance, which would result in biased estimation.

The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the authors provide a clear comparison of their approach with the conventional estimator. However, it would be beneficial to see a more detailed analysis of the results, particularly in terms of the computational efficiency of the proposed method.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting solution to a significant problem in counterfactual learning. While there are some minor points that require clarification, the paper is well-written, and the proposed approach is sensible. Therefore, I suggest acceptance.
