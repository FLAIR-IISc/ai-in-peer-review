This paper addresses the important problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a common scenario in applications like ad ranking. The authors identify a potential overfitting issue within the recently introduced Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) framework and propose a novel solution utilizing multiplicative control variates. This approach introduces a bias into the risk estimator, which the paper explores.

The work is of high quality and well-motivated. As a continuation of the authors’ previous work [1], I anticipated more extensive experimentation and deeper analysis of the training objective’s optimization – beyond the discussion on line 415. While the current experiments demonstrate promising results, further investigation into the optimization process would strengthen the contribution.

The paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, demonstrating a strong understanding of the relevant concepts and presenting them effectively.

The originality lies in modifying the risk estimator to achieve self-normalization. This appears to yield substantial improvements in the reported experimental results. While building upon [1], this modification represents a significant step forward.

The problem of learning from logged data is highly relevant, but assessing the practical impact of the observed experimental improvements on real-world applications remains a challenge. More context on how these gains translate to realistic scenarios would be valuable. 

Specific comments: On line 365, given the reported repetitions, including a measure of variance in the results would be beneficial. 

Overall, this is a solid and well-written paper presenting relevant and novel research. I would have appreciated a more comprehensive set of experiments, ideally extending beyond those presented in [1] to include some genuine real-world applications and datasets. This would better illustrate the practical benefits and limitations of the proposed approach.
