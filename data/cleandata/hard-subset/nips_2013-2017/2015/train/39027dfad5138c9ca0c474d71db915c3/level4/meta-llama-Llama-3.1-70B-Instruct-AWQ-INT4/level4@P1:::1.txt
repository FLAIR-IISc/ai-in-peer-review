This paper discusses a problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe issue with this estimator, which they term "propensity overfitting." This problem arises when the estimator is used for learning over complex hypothesis spaces, and it can lead to degenerate risk estimates.

The authors propose an alternative estimator, called the self-normalized estimator, which avoids this problem. They derive a new learning algorithm, called Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models (Norm-POEM), based on the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle using the self-normalized estimator. The Norm-POEM algorithm is designed for structured output prediction and is shown to outperform the conventional estimator in several multi-label classification problems.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and its solution. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the self-normalized estimator and the Norm-POEM algorithm through extensive empirical evaluations. They also provide a detailed comparison with the conventional estimator and other related methods.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and their proposed solution. The use of control variates to derive the self-normalized estimator is a nice touch, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of its properties.

One potential issue with the paper is that it assumes a certain level of familiarity with BLBF and the CRM principle. While the authors provide a brief introduction to these topics, readers who are not familiar with them may find it difficult to follow the paper.

Overall, I found the paper to be interesting and well-written. The authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and their proposed solution, and the empirical evaluations are thorough and convincing. I suggest acceptance.
