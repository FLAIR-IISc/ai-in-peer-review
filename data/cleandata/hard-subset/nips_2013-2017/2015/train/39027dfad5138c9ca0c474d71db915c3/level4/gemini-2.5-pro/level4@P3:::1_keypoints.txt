- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies "propensity overfitting" where models exploit IPS propensity weights to drive empirical risk artificially low.  
- Shows propensity overfitting produces degenerate solutions that over-concentrate or avoid training examples depending on loss sign.  
- Proposes using the self-normalized (SN) estimator from importance sampling, which is equivariant to loss shifts and bounded by the loss range.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, integrating the SN estimator and its variance into the CRM objective.  
- Theoretical claims include equivariance, boundedness, and strong consistency of the SN estimator (Theorem 2).  
- Empirical evaluation (Supervised -> Bandit protocol) shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets and data sizes.  
- Experiments verify robustness to loss translation and that Norm-POEM’s importance weight sum stays near 1 while POEM is biased and sensitive.  
- Ablation study indicates variance regularization remains beneficial even with the improved estimator.  
- Strength: clearly identifies and illustrates a novel, important failure mode (propensity overfitting) distinct from IPS high variance.  
- Strength: solution is principled, grounded in statistical literature (control variates/importance sampling).  
- Strength: thorough, well-designed experiments and reproducible code; paper is clearly written and organized.  
- Weakness: relationship between propensity overfitting and IPS high variance needs more detailed discussion, since both are exacerbated by tiny propensities.  
- Weakness: terminology around the "unbiased counterfactual risk estimator" could be clearer given common use of clipped/biased IPS variants in prior work.  
- Weakness: the self-normalized estimator itself is classical; novelty lies mainly in diagnosing the problem and applying the estimator to CRM.  
- Argument for acceptance: formalizes and names a subtle failure mode, and offers a simple, theoretically sound remedy.  
- Argument for acceptance: strong empirical evidence that Norm-POEM resolves the issue and improves generalization over state-of-the-art.  
- Argument against acceptance: core estimator is not new; contribution is primarily in application and problem framing.  
- Argument against acceptance: paper would benefit from deeper clarification of how propensity overfitting relates to variance reduction techniques.  
- Conclusion: high-quality, impactful paper with strengths outweighing minor weaknesses; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
