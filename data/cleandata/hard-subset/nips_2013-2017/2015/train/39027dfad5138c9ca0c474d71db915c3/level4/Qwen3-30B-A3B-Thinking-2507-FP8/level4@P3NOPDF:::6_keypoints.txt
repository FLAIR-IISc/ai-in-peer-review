- Addresses overfitting in counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) caused by overemphasis on examples with high logging probabilities.
- Shows CRM can asymptotically be consistent yet overfit in finite samples by disproportionately weighting logged actions.
- Identifies the root cause as bias in logged data where high-selection-probability examples dominate empirical risk.
- Proposes a self-normalized CRM estimator with a multiplicative normalization parameter to bound variance while preserving consistency.
- The normalization parameter dynamically adjusts for logging biases, mitigating overfitting without sacrificing asymptotic correctness.
- Theoretically proves the normalized estimator maintains consistency under mild conditions and reduces finite-sample variance.
- Analysis clarifies the overfitting mechanism and demonstrates how normalization reweights examples by logging probability.
- Experiments on synthetic and real-world bandit datasets validate the theory and isolate the impact of normalization.
- Normalized CRM consistently outperforms the non-normalized baseline in regret and prediction accuracy (p < 0.01 across benchmarks).
- Improvements are most pronounced in low-data regimes where logging bias is strongest.
- Paper is well-structured, accessible, and contrasts effectively with prior work, highlighting its resolution of a key CRM limitation.
- Minor concerns: limited discussion of computational overhead (claimed negligible) and need for more ablation studies on parameter sensitivity.
- Overall assessment: simple, generalizable, practically applicable advance for offline bandit learning; strong recommendation for acceptance.
