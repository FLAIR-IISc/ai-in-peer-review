This paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that addresses the propensity overfitting problem. Building upon the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the authors present a significantly improved algorithm for this challenging problem.

**Quality:** The research presented is of high quality. The extensions to the original Pathwise Optimization and Exploration in Multi-armed Bandits (POEM) algorithm demonstrably yield substantial improvements in training performance. The authors provide compelling empirical evidence supporting these gains, and the theoretical justification for the modifications appears sound. The experimental setup is well-defined and the comparisons to existing methods are thorough. The inclusion of multiple datasets strengthens the validity of the results. 

**Clarity:** The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and easy to follow. The core concepts are explained in a relatively accessible manner, and the notation is consistent throughout. However, a more detailed explanation of the intuition behind certain design choices—particularly regarding the specific regularization terms—would further enhance clarity. While the mathematical derivations are presented logically, some readers might benefit from a slightly more verbose explanation of the key steps.

**Originality:** This paper presents a significantly novel formulation of the POEM algorithm specifically tailored for the Batch Learning from Bandit Feedback (BLBF) problem. The modifications introduced, particularly the regularization techniques, represent a considerable advancement over existing approaches. I believe the contributions are genuinely original and address a critical limitation of the original POEM method. The approach of directly addressing propensity overfitting within the optimization framework, rather than relying on post-hoc adjustments, is a particularly insightful contribution.

**Significance:** The results presented in this paper are highly interesting and warrant further investigation. The fact that the proposed algorithm outperforms the original POEM, *despite* incorporating additional regularization, is particularly impressive. This suggests a highly effective regularization strategy and a deeper understanding of the underlying optimization landscape. The potential for faster training times, combined with improved accuracy, makes this algorithm a strong candidate for widespread adoption in practical applications of logged bandit feedback learning. This work has the potential to impact various fields, including recommendation systems, ad placement, and personalized medicine. 

**Minor Issues:** While generally well-written, a few minor stylistic improvements could be made. For example, clarifying the connection between the proposed method and other regularization techniques used in related fields would be beneficial. Additionally, a brief discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential avenues for future research would further strengthen the paper. 

**Overall:** This is a strong paper with significant contributions to the field of bandit learning. The proposed method is a clear improvement over existing techniques, and the results are compelling. I recommend acceptance.
