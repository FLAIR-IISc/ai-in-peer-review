This paper presents an approach for "counterfactual learning," a scenario that can be viewed as a specialization of multi-label learning where only partial feedback (i.e., bandit feedback for a single class) is available for each sample. While the paper is framed within this context, its core technical contribution is to improve upon the POEM estimator recently introduced in [1]. The experimental results show that the proposed Norm-POEM method consistently outperforms the original POEM on all tested datasets and achieves performance comparable to a CRF model trained with full multi-label information. Overall, the paper is convincing and presents a valuable contribution worthy of publication, but I have several remarks that the authors should address.

My primary concern relates to the commentary and notation surrounding Equation (2), which I believe are incorrect or, at a minimum, misleading. An expectation is formally a quantity of the form \sum f(x) p(x), where f(x) is the quantity whose expectation is being computed and p(x) is the probability measure. In Equation (2), the loss function clearly does not represent the quantity f(x) over which the expectation is being taken.

Consequently, the authors' subsequent claim (page 2, second paragraph) that Equation (2) exhibits an "anomaly" due to its lack of invariance to loss translation is technically incorrect. This claim stems from a misapplication of the definition of an expected value. Example 1, which follows this claim, is therefore predicated on this flawed premise, and its conclusions are unsound. I strongly encourage the authors to revise this commentary should the paper be accepted.

From a practical standpoint, the estimator in Equation (2) is sensitive to its denominator, pi, which can be zero if a sample was never observed in the historical data. The modified estimator in Equation (7) appears more robust in principle, as the influence of small pi values is mitigated by the self-normalizing denominator structure. This practical improvement is well-motivated and is the paper's key strength.
