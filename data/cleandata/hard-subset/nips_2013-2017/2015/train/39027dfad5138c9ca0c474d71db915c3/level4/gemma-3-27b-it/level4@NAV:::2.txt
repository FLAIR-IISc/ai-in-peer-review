The discussion surrounding the core equation (2) contains inaccuracies or potential misinterpretations. An expectation value fundamentally takes the form of \sum f(x) p(x), where f(x) represents the quantity being expected, distinct from the probability measure p(x). In equation (2), the term identified as the 'loss' does not align with this definition of an expected quantity. Consequently, the claim of an “anomaly” in equation (2) due to its lack of translation invariance (page 2, second paragraph) is technically unfounded. The subsequent Example 1 is similarly built upon this flawed premise, and predictably, fails to hold.

From a practical standpoint, the estimator in (2) exhibits sensitivity to its denominator, pi, which can become zero when a sample is absent from the prior. The revised estimator in (7) appears more robust, as the influence of pi is mitigated by the double denominator. 

I encourage the authors to revisit and refine their commentary if this paper is accepted for publication.

The experimental findings demonstrate that Norm-POEM consistently surpasses POEM across all evaluated datasets and achieves performance competitive with a CRF leveraging complete multi-label information. This work addresses “counterfactual learning,” which can be viewed as a specialized application within the broader field of multi-label learning. While standard multi-label learning utilizes 0/1 ground-truth labels for all classes, counterfactual learning operates with feedback limited to a single class (akin to bandit feedback). This framing serves primarily as motivation. The primary objective of this paper is, in fact, to refine an estimator (POEM) originally introduced in [1] at ICML 2015.  Overall, I find the paper to be compelling and suitable for publication, pending attention to the points detailed in the ‘Comments to Authors’ section.
