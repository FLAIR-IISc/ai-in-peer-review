- Studies batch learning from logged bandit feedback and identifies "propensity overfitting" in Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM).  
- Proposes a solution using multiplicative control variates that yields a self-normalized estimator.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound and the new overfitting phenomenon is a significant contribution.  
- Paper is high-quality, well-motivated, and carefully evaluates strengths and weaknesses.  
- Presentation is clear, well-written, and logically organized, making complex concepts understandable.  
- Builds on prior work by modifying the risk estimator, producing substantial experimental improvements.  
- Use of multiplicative control variates in this context is novel and effective.  
- Experimental setup largely mirrors prior work ([1]), limiting diversity of datasets and scenarios.  
- More extensive experiments and exploration of real-world applications would strengthen impact.  
- Discussion of optimization and training objective is limited and could use more detail.  
- Results should report variance across repetitions to better convey experimental stability.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, relevant, and novel work with promising results but needs broader evaluation and deeper optimization analysis.
