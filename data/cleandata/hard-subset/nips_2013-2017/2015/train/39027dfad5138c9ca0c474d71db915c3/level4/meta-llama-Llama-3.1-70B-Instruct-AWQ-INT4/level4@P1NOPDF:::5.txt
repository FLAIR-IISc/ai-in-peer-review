The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that avoids the propensity overfitting problem. Building on the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), it presents a significantly improved algorithm.

Quality: I found the quality of research discussed in the paper to be above average. The extensions proposed in the paper result in significant improvements in training the original POEM algorithm, demonstrating a clear advancement in the field.

Clarity: For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow. The authors have done a commendable job in presenting their ideas and methodology in a coherent and well-structured manner.

Originality: The paper offers a significantly new formulation of the original POEM algorithm for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. I believe the contributions are substantially original and represent a meaningful departure from existing work.

Significance: I think the results in the paper are very interesting and certainly warrant further research. I am impressed that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM, despite incorporating more regularization mechanisms. This algorithm may see widespread use in the near future, given its potential to overcome the propensity overfitting problem.

Overall, this is a good paper that is clear and engaging to read. The authors have made a valuable contribution to the field, and their work is likely to have a lasting impact.
