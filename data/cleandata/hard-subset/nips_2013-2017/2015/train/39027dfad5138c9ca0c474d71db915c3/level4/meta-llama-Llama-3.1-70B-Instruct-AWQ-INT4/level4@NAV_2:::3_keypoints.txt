- Identifies propensity overfitting in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for batch learning from logged bandit feedback.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator that avoids the propensity overfitting problem.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models, replacing POEM’s estimator with the self-normalized version.  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing the self-normalized estimator resolves problems of the unbiased estimator.  
- Details the Norm-POEM algorithm: hypothesis space, variance estimator, and training objective.  
- Experimental evaluation compares Norm-POEM to POEM on multi-label tasks and shows consistent improvement.  
- Reports detailed experimental analyses including selected-hypothesis Ŝ(ĥ) and test prediction error.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides thorough background and related work.  
- Contribution is novel and significant for learning from logged data with practical relevance.  
- Strengths: high-quality motivation, clarity, novelty, thorough analysis, and solid experimental design.  
- Weaknesses: experiments limited to multi-label classification, lacking broader or real-world applications.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient details on optimization (hyper-parameters, convergence) of the training objective.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, thorough analysis, strong empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental scope and missing optimization/real-world application details.
