- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new counterfactual learning estimator (specialized multi-label learning with bandit feedback).  
- Empirical results are strong: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms original POEM and is competitive with fully supervised CRF.  
- Main claimed theoretical motivation is that POEM has an "anomaly" of non-invariance to loss translation (Equation (2)).  
- That claim appears technically incorrect: the loss is part of the probability measure, so translation-invariance of the expected quantity does not apply here.  
- The paper’s expectation/translation argument stems from a misinterpretation of the estimator’s structure and thus is invalid.  
- Example 1 and subsequent theoretical conclusions built on that anomaly are therefore unconvincing.  
- Practically motivated modification (Equation (7)) is sensible: normalization reduces sensitivity to small or zero propensity scores (pi) and stabilizes variance.  
- The normalization’s “double denominator” effectively mollifies influence of tiny pi values, providing a sound practical justification.  
- Recommendation: replace the flawed theoretical motivation with the stability/variance-based justification and revise the core argument before publication.
