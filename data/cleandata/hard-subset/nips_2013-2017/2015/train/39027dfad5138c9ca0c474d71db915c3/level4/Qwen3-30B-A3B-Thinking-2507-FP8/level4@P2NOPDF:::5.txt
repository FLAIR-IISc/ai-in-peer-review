

This paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that avoids propensity overfitting, building upon Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) with a substantially improved algorithm.  

Quality: The research quality is above average. The proposed extensions yield significant improvements in training the original POEM algorithm for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), addressing a key limitation in prior work.  

Clarity: The paper is well-written, logically structured, and accessible, with clear explanations of technical contributions and experimental design.  

Originality: The paper introduces a significantly novel reformulation of the POEM algorithm for the BLBF problem, representing a substantial theoretical advance beyond existing approaches. The core idea—integrating a new regularization mechanism to prevent propensity overfitting—demonstrates meaningful originality.  

Significance: The results are compelling. The proposed algorithm achieves faster training than POEM despite incorporating additional regularization, a counterintuitive yet crucial efficiency gain. This combination of robustness and speed suggests strong potential for real-world adoption in applications like recommendation systems and online advertising. The work merits further investigation and could influence future research in offline bandit learning.  

Overall, this is a strong contribution that advances the state of the art with clear theoretical and practical value. The paper is well-executed, concise, and makes a meaningful impact on the field.
