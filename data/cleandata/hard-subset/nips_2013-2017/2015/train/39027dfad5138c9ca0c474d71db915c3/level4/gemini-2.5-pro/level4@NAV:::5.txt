This paper introduces a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that successfully addresses the propensity overfitting problem. The work extends the foundational research of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), offering a substantially improved algorithm.

Quality: The research quality presented in this manuscript is commendable. The proposed extensions yield considerable enhancements to the training process of the original POEM algorithm.

Clarity: The paper is, for the most part, lucid and well-structured, making the core concepts and contributions straightforward to comprehend.

Originality: This work puts forward a genuinely novel reformulation of the POEM algorithm for the batch learning from bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. The contributions are, in my view, of significant originality.

Significance: The results presented are highly compelling and certainly warrant further investigation. A particularly impressive achievement is that the proposed algorithm is computationally faster than the original POEM, even with the inclusion of additional regularization mechanisms. This method has strong potential for widespread adoption in the near future.

Overall, this is a strong submission that is clearly articulated and well-presented.
