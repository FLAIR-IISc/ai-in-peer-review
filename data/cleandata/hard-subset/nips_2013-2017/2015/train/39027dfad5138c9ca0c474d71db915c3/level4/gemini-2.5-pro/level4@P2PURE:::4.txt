This paper presents an interesting follow-up to the work in [1], addressing the increasingly relevant problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback. The analysis of "propensity overfitting" is a valuable contribution, and the proposed multiplicative control variate is a clever technique for mitigating it. The empirical results demonstrate a clear improvement over the POEM baseline. The authors have satisfactorily addressed my initial questions.

**Questions for Rebuttal:**

In [1] (Section 4.1), the authors state that the CRM approach is only valid for a loss `delta` in the range [-1, 0] and propose a rescaling for general losses. I presume the NORM-POEM approach presented here has the same requirement, meaning that `delta` in Equations (3) and (8) should also be rescaled to [-1, 0]. If this is correct, could you please clarify the use of `delta > 0` in the experiments presented in Table 1?

**Other Comments:**

1.  The setup for "batch learning from logged bandit feedback" in Section 3 seems incomplete. The feedback `delta(x,y)` is treated as a deterministic function (line 110), whereas it would be more realistic to model it as a random variable. For example, in a classification problem with noisy labels, the same input `x` and action `y` could yield different observed losses `delta(x,y)` across the log due to the stochastic nature of the ground-truth labeling process, `p(y'|x)`. While this may not change the risk estimator in Equation (2), the definition of the true risk in Equation (1) should ideally include an expectation over the randomness of `delta` to be more general.

2.  **Lines 170-171:** The ratio should be `h(y|x) / h_0(y|x)`. It is also worth noting that the lack of linearity mentioned here was previously identified in [1].

3.  **Line 276:** Since the objective is to minimize the risk, this should be `argmin` instead of `argmax`.

4.  **Line 319:** To maintain consistency with prior notation, please replace the bars with hats.
