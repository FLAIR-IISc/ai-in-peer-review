- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback and identifies a significant overfitting issue in the CRM principle.
- Proposes multiplicative control variates to create a self-normalizing (biased) estimator to mitigate overfitting.
- Theoretical justification is sound and technical details are carefully considered.
- High-quality, well-motivated contribution that builds on prior work [1].
- Use of multiplicative control variates in this context appears novel.
- Presentation is exceptionally clear with consistent, well-defined notation.
- Experimental results show demonstrable improvements but are limited in scope.
- Paper needs more extensive experiments and sensitivity analysis of optimization parameters and strategies.
- Results should report variance measures (e.g., standard deviation or confidence intervals) across repetitions.
- Unclear how gains translate to larger, more heterogeneous, real-world datasets; scalability and deployment evidence needed.
- Should discuss the potential impact of the introduced bias on the final policy and possible mitigation methods.
- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept/Accept conditional on addressing expanded experimental validation and discussion of bias; reviewer confidence medium–high.
