The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that addresses the propensity overfitting problem. Building upon the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), it presents a significantly improved algorithm.

Quality: I found the quality of the research to be above average. The extensions proposed yield significant improvements in the training of the original POEM algorithm. 

Clarity: For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

Originality: The paper offers a significantly novel formulation of the original POEM algorithm for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. I believe the contributions are substantially original.

Significance: The results presented are very interesting and warrant further research. I am particularly impressed that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM despite incorporating additional regularization. This algorithm has the potential for widespread use in the near future. 

Overall, this is a strong paper that is clear and well-written.
