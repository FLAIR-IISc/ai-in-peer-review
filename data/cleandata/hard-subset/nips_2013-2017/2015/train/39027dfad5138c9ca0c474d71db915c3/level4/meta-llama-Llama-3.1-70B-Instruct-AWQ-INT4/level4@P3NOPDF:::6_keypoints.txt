- Proposes a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual risk minimization in logged bandit settings.
- Targets overfitting that occurs when hypotheses exploit example generation probabilities from logged data.
- Introduces a multiplicative parameter that bounds estimator variance while preserving consistency.
- Presents a well-motivated problem statement with clear explanation of counterfactual risk minimization challenges.
- Shows standard CRM can overfit, especially with complex hypothesis classes.
- Experimental results indicate the self-normalized estimator outperforms standard CRM and is more robust to overfitting.
- Concludes the approach provides better generalization and is a valuable contribution to counterfactual learning.
- Recommends acceptance, though notes some uncertainty about the paper’s broader impact and relevance.
