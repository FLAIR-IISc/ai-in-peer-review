

Summary  
This paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback—a highly relevant problem in applications like ad ranking. It identifies an overfitting issue in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a solution using multiplicative control variates, resulting in a biased estimator.  

Quality  
The work is high-quality and well-motivated. As a follow-up to [1], the paper would benefit from additional experiments and more detailed analysis of the training objective’s optimization, beyond the brief mention at line 415.  

Clarity  
The presentation is clear and the paper is well-written.  

Originality  
While building on [1], the key novelty lies in modifying the risk estimator to be self-normalizing, which significantly improves performance in the authors’ experiments.  

Significance  
The problem of learning from logged data remains highly relevant, but it is unclear whether the substantial experimental gains translate to broader real-world applications. Additional validation on diverse, real-world datasets would strengthen the impact.  

Major Comments  
- **Line 365**: If multiple repetitions are reported, please include variance measures to assess statistical stability.  
- **Experiments**: The authors should expand beyond the benchmarks in [1]. Including real-world applications (e.g., large-scale ad platforms or recommendation systems) would better demonstrate practical utility. The current experiments, while rigorous, lack direct evidence of real-world applicability.  

Minor Comments  
- **Line 415**: The optimization analysis of the training objective requires deeper elaboration.  
- **Terminology**: "Conterfactual" (line 365) should be corrected to "Counterfactual."  

Overall, the paper presents a solid contribution with a clear technical insight. The self-normalizing estimator effectively addresses CRM’s overfitting issue, and the experimental results are convincing within the studied settings. However, the significance would be substantially elevated by real-world validation and more comprehensive analysis of the optimization process. The work is relevant and novel in its approach, but the path to practical deployment requires further empirical grounding.
