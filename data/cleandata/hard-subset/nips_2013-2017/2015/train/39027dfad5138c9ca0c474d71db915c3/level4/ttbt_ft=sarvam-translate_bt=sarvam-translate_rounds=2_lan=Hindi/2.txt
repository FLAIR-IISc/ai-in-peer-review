The comments and notations regarding the main equation (2) seem incorrect or at least misleading:

An expectation is a quantity of the type \sum f (x) p (x). The expected quantity, f (x), is everything else besides p (x), which is the probability measure.

In equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity! Therefore, the following comment (page 2, second paragraph) that there is a "discreteness" in (2) because the loss is not invertible to translate it is technically incorrect. Furthermore, the example 1 given later is based on the misconception that, surprisingly, this is not correct.

Practically, the estimator in (2) is weak for its denominator, pi, which can be 0 if the sample has never been seen before. The modified estimator in (7) seems better in theory because the effects of pi are reduced through the double denominator.

I really hope the author will be kind enough to revise their comment on the acceptance of their paper.

Experimental results show that Norm-Poem performs better than Poem on all datasets and is not far from CRF using full multi-label information. This paper presents an approach to "Counterfactual Learning," a learning scenario that is a specialization of multi-label learning. In multi-label learning, a ground-truth annotation for a sample provides 0/1 labels for each class; here, instead, only one class is labeled (i.e., the bandit response). However, the above is only a justification proposal. In fact, this paper has a precise purpose: to improve an estimator (PoEM) in ICML 2015, recently referenced [1]. I would say that, overall, the paper is convincing and worthy of publication, but with some comments that I raise in the comments in the authors' area.
