- Identifies propensity overfitting: IPS-based CRM can be minimized by manipulating importance weights rather than reducing loss, yielding degenerate, unbounded, non-equivariant estimates.
- Proposes SNIPS (self-normalized IPS) using a multiplicative control variate that normalizes by total importance weight to remedy the pathology.
- Shows SNIPS is bounded in the loss range, equivariant to additive loss shifts, and strongly consistent.
- Develops Norm-POEM by replacing IPS with SNIPS in POEM’s risk and variance terms and optimizing a non-convex objective with L-BFGS.
- Provides an approximate variance estimator for the ratio estimator and uses clipping to ensure finite variance, discussing bias–variance trade-offs.
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple multilabel datasets under a supervised-to-bandit protocol.
- Introduces diagnostic Ŝ(h) (sum of normalized weights) to detect propensity overfitting and shows SNIPS avoids related pathologies.
- Situates work within importance weighting, control variates, off-policy evaluation, covariate shift, and prior BLBF literature.
- Paper is technically sound, well-motivated, and the diagnosis plus theoretical grounding is compelling.
- Optimization is a weakness: objective non-convexity/non-smoothness needs deeper analysis on convergence, initialization, and sensitivity to M and λ.
- Empirical section is careful but would be stronger with additional baselines (e.g., doubly robust estimators) and uncertainty quantification.
- Clarity is good overall; minor issues include a typo (“Conterfactual”), inconsistent naming (Norm-POEM vs NormPOEM), and notation/assumption presentation.
- Originality: SNIPS is classical but its targeted use to fix propensity overfitting in CRM and the Norm-POEM algorithm are novel contributions.
- Significance: addresses an important practical failure mode in CRM; results are impactful but would benefit from broader experiments and scalability discussion.
- Pros: clear failure-mode identification, principled self-normalization solution with solid theory, practical algorithm with empirical gains, useful diagnostics, and topical relevance.
- Cons: experiments mirror prior work’s setup, missing important baselines, limited optimization/hyperparameter analysis, approximate variance estimator, and potential scalability issues.
- Suggested improvements: add error bars/statistical tests, include more baselines (clipped IPS, doubly robust, offset tree), expand optimization details and hyperparameter guidance, and analyze regimes where SNIPS may underperform.
- Also suggested: study robustness to support violations/extreme propensities, provide ablations on clipping, add extra datasets or a real-world case study, and fix minor editorial issues.
- Overall assessment: solid, relevant paper that meaningfully advances BLBF by diagnosing propensity overfitting and proposing a principled fix; leans toward acceptance pending broader experiments and deeper optimization analysis.
