- Reviewer had limited time and may have missed or misinterpreted aspects of the paper.
- Paper studies evaluating a new hypothesis from data collected under a different hypothesis using reweighting for nondeterministic hypotheses.
- Authors introduce "propensity overfitting," which appears related to but distinct from the well-known problem of large importance-weight variance.
- Examples suggest propensity overfitting stems from insufficient exploration (for some x, few or no y samples for the target hypothesis).
- The reviewer requests a clear clarification of the precise relationship between propensity overfitting and weight variance.
- Reviewer could not fully verify technical novelty or correctness, though the core idea seems sound.
- Technical concern: Line 199 — if n is small relative to k, the sign/value claim about \hat{R}(\hat{h}) appears inconsistent (should be smaller than -2, not larger).
- Citation concern: Claim that prior BLBF works use an "unbiased counterfactual risk estimator" is misleading since those works often apply variance-reducing (biased) techniques like clipping.
- Reviewer commends the authors for providing code.
- Recommendation: accept pending clarifications on the relationship between propensity overfitting and weight variance and on the cited methodology.
