- Identifies propensity overfitting in BLBF: conventional unbiased risk estimators can severely overfit propensities, yielding biased risk estimates, especially with complex hypothesis spaces.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates to avoid this degeneracy.
- Self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss translations and ties to variance-reduction and self-normalization techniques from causal inference and off-policy evaluation.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on the proposed estimator.
- Provides theoretical guarantee: proof of consistency (Theorem 2).
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multi-label classification tasks.
- Ablation study on variance regularization offers insightful analysis of components.
- Paper is clearly written and well-presented.
- Code is publicly available, aiding reproducibility.
- Computational complexity concern: need more detailed discussion of estimator cost for large-scale datasets and complex hypothesis spaces.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: impact of clipping parameter M and regularization λ needs deeper exploration.
- Limited experimental scope: evaluations are confined to multi-label classification; broader structured prediction tasks are not tested.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a novel, important problem with sound theory and strong empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: potential scalability issues and narrow experimental evaluation.
- Overall assessment/recommendation: strong paper with minor limitations; recommend acceptance.
