- Equation (2) is incorrectly or misleadingly presented: the expectation notation is misused, since the loss shown is not an expectation E_p[f(X)] = Σ f(x)p(x).  
- The paper’s claim that (2) exhibits an “anomaly” (non-invariance to loss translations) is technically incorrect given the notation issue.  
- Example 1 is built on that mistaken premise, so its conclusion does not follow.  
- The estimator in (2) is practically fragile because it depends on π in the denominator, which can be zero or very small, yielding undefined estimates or high variance.  
- The modified estimator in (7) is preferable: its normalization (“double denominator”) dampens the effect of small π values.  
- The authors should revise the related discussion and correct these technical points if the paper is accepted.  
- Experimental results are strong: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets and approaches the performance of a CRF with full multi-label annotations.  
- The paper addresses counterfactual (bandit-feedback) multi-label learning rather than standard fully supervised multi-label settings, with a concrete goal of improving the POEM estimator (ICML 2015).  
- Overall the submission is convincing and merits publication, subject to the noted technical corrections and additional author-addressed comments.
