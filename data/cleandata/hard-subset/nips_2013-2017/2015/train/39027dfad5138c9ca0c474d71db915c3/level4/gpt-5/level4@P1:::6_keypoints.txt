- Paper studies counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) for batch learning from logged bandit feedback and identifies a critical overfitting issue with the IPS estimator.
- Shows that IPS, though consistent, can lead to “propensity overfitting” under ERM, where hypotheses exploit logging propensities instead of reducing loss.
- Demonstrates that ERM can drive the IPS objective down by concentrating or avoiding mass on logged actions, producing misleadingly low empirical risk.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (multiplicative control variate) that normalizes by the sum of importance weights to reduce sensitivity to weight fluctuations.
- The self-normalized estimator preserves consistency while being equivariant and bounded by the loss range, preventing extreme empirical risk artifacts.
- Introduces Norm-POEM as a CRM-instantiated algorithm using the normalized estimator and a practical variance estimate for variance regularization.
- Empirical results on multilabel classification show the normalized approach consistently outperforms the non-normalized baseline.
- Paper is grounded in importance sampling and CRM literature and connects naturally to variance reduction techniques.
- Use of multiplicative control variates (self-normalization) is well-known in Monte Carlo but is meaningfully adapted here for counterfactual learning.
- Core technical argument is clear and convincing: IPS objective can be manipulated via propensities and self-normalization counteracts this.
- The proposed estimator maintains consistency and comes with a variance estimate suitable for CRM-style regularization.
- Presentation is clear; the propensity overfitting phenomenon, Norm-POEM, and experimental methodology are easy to follow.
- Original contribution: identification of propensity overfitting as a distinct failure mode in BLBF and adapting self-normalization within CRM.
- Significance: addresses an important off-policy learning problem with a robust, practically impactful fix that should be a safer default.
- Pros: clearly articulated problem, simple principled solution that maintains consistency, and consistent empirical gains.
- Con: reviewer has moderate (not absolute) confidence in the findings.
- Recommendation: the paper is good and should be accepted.
