By observing data for a specific assumption, we may be interested in evaluating another assumption. If these assumptions are not certain (e.g., conditional distributions), we can re-weight the data points and estimate the loss under the assumption being investigated, see equations (1) and (2).

The authors discussed a related but somewhat different problem (see below), which differs from the problem of large weight variance. The authors called this problem "pregnancy overfitting." The examples suggest that this problem occurs if there is not enough exploration, that is, for a given x, we see very few or even no y's that would be consistent with the new assumption to evaluate. Since a similar argument explains large weight variance, it is better to explain the relationship between these two problems in a little more detail.
