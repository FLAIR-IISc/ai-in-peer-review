Counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) aims to learn in a bandit setting from logged data by correcting for the bias introduced by the logging policy’s generation of examples. While CRM is consistent, the paper shows that it can suffer from overfitting, where a hypothesis class overfits by exploiting the generation probability (propensity) of examples—effectively giving stronger support to the logged data it prefers. The authors propose to address this with a multiplicative self-normalization parameter that bounds variance while maintaining consistency. Empirically, the multiplicative (normalized) counterfactual method outperforms the non-normalized approach.

Overall, the paper presents a technique for learning a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting and offers a clear strategy for mitigating this form of overfitting. This is a good paper and should be accepted, although I am not absolutely certain.
