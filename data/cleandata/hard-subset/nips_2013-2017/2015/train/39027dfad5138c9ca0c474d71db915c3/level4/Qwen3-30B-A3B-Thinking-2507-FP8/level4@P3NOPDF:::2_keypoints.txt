- Addresses an important technical challenge in multi-label learning with bandit feedback and proposes Norm-POEM.  
- Empirical results are compelling: Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across datasets and approaches CRF performance.  
- Theoretical analysis contains a critical error around Equation (2) that must be corrected before publication.  
- Authors incorrectly claim a "translation anomaly" by treating a loss as if it were an expectation; the loss need not be translation-invariant.  
- The conflation of loss and expectation invalidates the paper’s Example 1 and the anomaly argument.  
- The real practical issue with Equation (2) is denominator instability: division by zero if a class is unobserved (π̂_i = 0).  
- Equation (7)’s double-denominator (π̂_i + ε) correctly mitigates this instability, making the estimator more robust.  
- The paper should replace references to "translation anomaly" with a clear explanation of the division-by-zero/denominator problem and how Equation (7) fixes it.  
- Framing "counterfactual learning" as a distinct scenario is misleading; bandit feedback is a special case of multi-label learning, and the contribution is a refinement of POEM.  
- Requested revisions: (1) correct the theoretical error and remove "translation anomaly" language, (2) reframe the contribution as a POEM refinement under bandit feedback.  
- Recommendation: strong empirical contribution and practical utility, accept only if the above corrections are implemented.
