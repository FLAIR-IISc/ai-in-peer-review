- Identifies "propensity overfitting" in BLBF: IPS estimator can be minimized by manipulating propensity weights, producing degenerate solutions and poor generalization.  
- Proposes replacing IPS with a self-normalized estimator using the sum of propensity weights as a control variate to avoid this anomaly.  
- Shows the self-normalized estimator is translation-equivariant and bounded within the loss range, preventing the issues causing propensity overfitting.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, which integrates the self-normalized estimator with a variance regularizer into CRM for structured prediction.  
- Builds directly on and significantly extends prior POEM work by replacing the conventional unbiased estimator with the self-normalized variant.  
- Provides theoretical support (consistency proof / Theorem 2) and rigorous analysis for the proposed estimator and method.  
- Empirical evaluation on benchmarks is thorough and controlled, demonstrating Norm-POEM's superior generalization and sometimes faster convergence.  
- Method is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly explained with a strong illustrative example (Example 1).  
- Novelty arises from applying a known importance-sampling technique to a newly identified counterfactual learning problem.  
- Significant practical impact for applications using logged bandit data (search, recommendation, advertising) due to improved accuracy, robustness, and efficiency.  
- Limitation: variance estimator in Norm-POEM does not admit stochastic optimization, raising scalability concerns for extremely large-scale problems.  
- Limitation: lacks a finite-sample generalization bound analogous to POEM's Theorem 1, beyond the shown strong consistency.  
- Limitation/question: sensitivity to the quality of the logging policy h0 (e.g., when h0 differs greatly from optimal) and resulting high-variance importance weights remains to be evaluated.
