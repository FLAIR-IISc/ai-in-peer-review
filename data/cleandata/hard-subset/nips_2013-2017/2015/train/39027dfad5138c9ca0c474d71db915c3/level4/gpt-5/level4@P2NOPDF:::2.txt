The commentary and notation around the main equation (2) appear incorrect or at least misleading. By definition, an expectation has the form E_p[f(X)] = Σ_x f(x) p(x), where f(X) is the random variable (the “quantity being averaged”) and p is the probability measure. In (2), the loss is plainly not the expectation itself. Consequently, the statement on page 2, second paragraph, that (2) exhibits an “anomaly” because it is not invariant to translations of the loss is technically incorrect. Example 1 then builds on this mistaken premise, so its conclusion does not follow.

From a practical standpoint, the estimator in (2) is fragile due to its dependence on the denominator π, which can be zero (or very small) when a sample/action has negligible propensity under the logging policy, leading to undefined estimates or excessive variance. The modified estimator in (7) is preferable in principle because the normalization (the “double denominator”) dampens the influence of small π values.

I encourage the authors to revise the corresponding discussion and correct these points if the paper is accepted.

The experimental results are strong: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across all datasets and performs close to a CRF trained with full multi-label annotations. The paper addresses “counterfactual learning,” a specialization of multi-label learning. In standard multi-label settings, ground-truth provides a 0/1 label per class; here, only a single label per instance is observed (bandit feedback). While this context is useful background, the paper’s concrete objective is to improve the POEM estimator introduced in [1] (ICML 2015). Overall, the submission is convincing and merits publication, subject to the technical corrections noted above and additional remarks provided in the Comments to Authors.
