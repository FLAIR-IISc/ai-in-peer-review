

The paper proposes Norm-POEM, a modification of the POEM estimator [1] for counterfactual learning. While the experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across all datasets and achieves performance comparable to CRF baselines using full multi-label information, the mathematical justification for the proposed estimator contains critical errors that require correction.  

The core issue lies in the authors' interpretation of Equation (2). The authors claim this equation exhibits an "anomaly" due to lack of translation invariance (page 2, second paragraph), arguing that the loss should be invariant under translation of the loss function. However, this premise is fundamentally flawed. An expectation is defined as $\sum f(x) p(x)$, where $f(x)$ is the random variable (the "expected quantity") and $p(x)$ is the probability measure. In Equation (2), the loss function $L$ is treated as the expectation variable, yet it is clearly distinct from the probability measure $\pi$. Consequently, the loss is not an expectation, and the authors' claim of an "anomaly" based on translation non-invariance is technically incorrect. This error invalidates Example 1, which relies on this misinterpretation.  

Furthermore, Equation (2) suffers from a practical vulnerability: it is undefined when $\pi_i = 0$ (i.e., when a sample was never observed in the prior). The modified estimator in Equation (7) addresses this by introducing a double denominator structure, which effectively avoids division by zero and stabilizes the estimate. The authors’ commentary should clarify that Equation (7) resolves this critical limitation of Equation (2), rather than implying an "anomaly" in the original formulation.  

The paper positions counterfactual learning as a specialization of multi-label learning, where ground-truth annotations typically provide 0/1 labels for all classes, but the model receives bandit feedback (only one label per sample). While this framing is acceptable, the authors’ primary contribution is not the counterfactual learning framework itself but a practical improvement to the POEM estimator [1]. The paper convincingly demonstrates that Norm-POEM enhances POEM’s robustness and performance, though the justification for the modification requires correction.  

In summary, the experimental results are strong and the approach is valuable. However, the mathematical errors in the commentary must be addressed to ensure technical correctness. We recommend the authors:  
1. Correct the mischaracterization of Equation (2) as an expectation and remove the invalid "anomaly" claim;  
2. Clarify that Equation (7) resolves the denominator instability issue in Equation (2).  

With these revisions, the paper would be a solid contribution to the literature. As presented, the technical inaccuracies undermine the paper’s foundation, and we urge the authors to address them prior to publication. The experimental validation of Norm-POEM’s performance is compelling, but the theoretical justification must be rigorously corrected.
