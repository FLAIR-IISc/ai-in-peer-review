- Addresses the important problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) relevant to ad ranking and recommendation.  
- Identifies a novel “propensity overfitting” issue within Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM).  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates, producing a biased but more robust estimator.  
- High-quality work with strong theoretical motivation and empirical validation.  
- Logical extension of prior work [1], refining the CRM principle.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough but would benefit from expanded experiments on optimization, hyperparameter sensitivity, and ablation studies.  
- Exceptionally well-written and clearly presented with consistent notation and accessible derivations.  
- Original contribution: self-normalizing modification and clear demonstration of its performance gains.  
- Significant application potential, though practical transferability, scalability, and robustness to larger/real-world datasets remain to be shown.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, identifies a novel issue, offers a solid theoretical and empirical solution, shows consistent improvements, and is clearly written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experiments rely heavily on datasets/methods from [1]; needs broader datasets, deeper optimization/hyperparameter analysis, and discussion of practical limitations.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution that merits acceptance, with suggested additional experiments and analysis to strengthen the paper.
