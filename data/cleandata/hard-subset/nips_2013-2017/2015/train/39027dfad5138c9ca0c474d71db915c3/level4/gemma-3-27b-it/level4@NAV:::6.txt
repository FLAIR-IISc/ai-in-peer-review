Counterfactual risk minimization addresses learning from logged data in a bandit setting by mitigating bias inherent in example generation. The paper identifies an overfitting issue within this framework, demonstrating that hypothesis classes can overfit based on the probability of generating observed examples, despite consistency guarantees. To address this, the authors introduce a multiplicative parameter to bound variance while preserving consistency. Experimental results confirm the superior performance of this normalized counterfactual approach compared to its non-normalized counterpart. 

This work presents a method for learning a self-normalized estimator specifically for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit scenario. It offers a valuable solution to an overfitting problem arising from excessive support for logged data. Overall, this is a strong paper and recommended for acceptance, though with some remaining uncertainty.
