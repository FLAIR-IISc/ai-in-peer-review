The discussion and notation around the main equation (2) appear incorrect or at least misleading: an expectation is of the form ∑ f(x) p(x). The expected quantity is f(x); p(x) is the probability measure, not the quantity being expected. In equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity. Therefore, the subsequent claim (page 2, second paragraph) that (2) has an “anomaly” due to non-invariance under translations of the loss is technically wrong. Likewise, Example 1 rests on this incorrect premise and, unsurprisingly, does not hold.

Practically speaking, the estimator in (2) is fragile due to its denominator, pi, which can be 0 if the sample was never observed under the prior. The modified estimator in (7) appears preferable in principle because the influence of pi is tempered via the double denominator.

I sincerely hope the authors will revise their commentary should the paper be accepted.

The experiments show that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and is close to a CRF that uses full multi-label supervision. The paper addresses “counterfactual learning,” a setting that can be seen as a specialization of multi-label learning: in multi-label learning, the ground truth provides a 0/1 label for each class; here, only a single class label is observed (bandit feedback). However, this serves mainly as motivation. In fact, the paper’s concrete goal is to improve an estimator (POEM) recently proposed in [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, the paper is convincing and worthy of publication, with the caveats detailed in the Comments to authors.
