The comments and notation regarding the main equation - (2) - seem incorrect or at least misleading:

An expectation is a quantity of the type \sum f(x) p(x). The expected quantity, f(x), is everything else besides p(x), which is the probability measure.

In equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity! Therefore, the following comment (page 2, second paragraph) that there is a "discreteness" in (2) because it is not invertible to translate the loss is technically incorrect. Furthermore, the example 1 given later is based on the misconception that, surprisingly, it is not correct.

Practically, the estimator in (2) is weak for its denominator, pi, which can be 0 if the sample has never been seen before. The modified estimator in (7) seems better in theory because the effects of pi are reduced through the double denominator.

I really hope that the author will be kind enough to revise his comment upon acceptance of his paper.

Experimental results show that Norm-PoEM performs better than PoEM on all datasets and is not far from CRF using full multi-label information. This paper presents an approach to "Counterfactual Learning," a learning scenario that is a specialization of multi-label learning. In multi-label learning, a ground-truth annotation for a sample provides 0/1 labels for each class; here, instead, only one class is labeled (i.e., bandit feedback). However, the above is only a justification proposal. In fact, this paper has a precise purpose: to improve an estimator (PoEM) recently presented by reference [1] in ICML 2015. I would say that, overall, the paper is convincing and worthy of publication, but with some comments that I raise in the comments in the authors' area.
