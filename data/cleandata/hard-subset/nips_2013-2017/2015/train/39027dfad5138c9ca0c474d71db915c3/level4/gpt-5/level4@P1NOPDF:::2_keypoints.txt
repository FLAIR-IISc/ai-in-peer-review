- The paper’s notation and commentary around Eq. (2) are incorrect/misleading: the expectation was mischaracterized and the loss is part of the integrand, not the measure.  
- The claim that Eq. (2) exhibits an “anomaly” (non-invariance to loss translations) is technically incorrect.  
- Example 1 is based on the mistaken premise about Eq. (2) and therefore does not hold.  
- The estimator in (2) is fragile because its denominator π can be zero for unobserved samples.  
- The modified estimator in (7) is preferable in principle since it mitigates π’s effects via a double denominator.  
- Reviewer requests the authors revise their commentary if the paper is accepted.  
- Experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and approaches CRF performance with full multi-label information.  
- The paper frames “counterfactual learning” as a specialization of multi-label learning (bandit feedback vs full labels), mainly as motivation.  
- The paper’s primary contribution is improving the POEM estimator from ICML 2015.  
- Overall assessment: convincing and worthy of publication, with additional detailed comments provided to the authors.
