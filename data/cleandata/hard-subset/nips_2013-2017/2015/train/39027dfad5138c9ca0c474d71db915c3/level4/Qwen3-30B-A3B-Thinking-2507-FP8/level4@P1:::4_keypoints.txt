- Identifies propensity overfitting as a critical flaw in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator that avoids propensity overfitting via multiplicative control variates.
- Shows the self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss translations and yields bounded risk estimates, fixing anomalies of the conventional estimator.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, which replaces POEM’s risk estimator within the same CRM framework and hypothesis space.
- Provides rigorous theoretical justification (Theorem 2) for the self-normalized estimator.
- Empirical results across multiple multi-label datasets show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and reduces propensity overfitting.
- Norm-POEM has practical computational costs comparable to POEM and the code is publicly available.
- Clarification needed on loss rescaling: inconsistency between CRM’s [-1,0] requirement and reported positive delta results.
- Clarification needed to explicitly model feedback delta(x,y) as a random variable reflecting noisy/unobserved labels.
- Minor notation issues: Line 276 uses "argmax" but should be "argmin".
- Minor notation consistency: Equation 8 should use hats for estimated quantities (e.g., \hat{S}(h)).
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and impactful contribution to BLBF with robust empirical support; recommendation: Accept.
