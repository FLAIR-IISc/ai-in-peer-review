Summary
This paper investigates batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant problem with applications in areas such as ad ranking. The authors identify an overfitting issue within the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and introduce a solution based on multiplicative control variates, acknowledging this results in a biased estimator.

Quality
This is a high-quality and well-motivated work. As a follow-up to [1], I would have anticipated more extensive experimentation and a deeper analysis of the training objective’s optimization – beyond the discussion on line 415.

Clarity
The presentation is clear and the paper is well-written.

Originality
While building upon [1], the modification of the risk estimator to be self-normalizing appears to yield significant improvements in the experimental results presented by the authors.

Significance
The problem of learning from logged data is indeed relevant; however, the extent to which the substantial experimental improvements observed translate to practical, real-world applications remains unclear.

Various Remarks
Line 365: Given the multiple repetitions already performed, please include a measure of variance to quantify the results. The paper correctly identifies a limitation of the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a solution. Overall, this is a solid, well-written paper presenting relevant and novel work. I would have appreciated more extensive experimentation, and particularly the inclusion of applications beyond those explored in [1], including some evaluation on actual real-world datasets.
