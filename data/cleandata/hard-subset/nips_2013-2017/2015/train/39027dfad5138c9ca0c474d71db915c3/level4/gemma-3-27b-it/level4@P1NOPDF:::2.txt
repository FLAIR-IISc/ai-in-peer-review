The commentary and notations surrounding the main equation – (2) – appear incorrect, or at least misleading. An expectation is a quantity of the form \sum f(x) p(x), where the expected quantity, f(x), is everything *except* the probability measure p(x). In equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity. Consequently, the claim on page 2, second paragraph, that (2) exhibits an “anomaly” due to a lack of invariance to translation of the loss is technically inaccurate. The subsequent Example 1 is based on this flawed premise and, unsurprisingly, does not hold. 

In practical terms, the estimator in (2) is sensitive to its denominator, π, which can be zero if a sample has never been observed in the prior. The modified estimator in (7) appears superior in principle, as the effects of π are mitigated by the double denominator. 

I hope the authors will consider revising their commentary should the paper be accepted.

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across all datasets and achieves performance close to that of a CRF utilizing full multi-label information. This paper presents an approach for “counterfactual learning,” a learning scenario that can be viewed as a specialization of multi-label learning. In traditional multi-label learning, ground-truth annotations provide 0/1 labels for each class; here, the feedback is limited to the label for a single class, resembling bandit feedback. However, this is primarily a justification for the problem setting. The core aim of this paper is to improve an estimator (POEM) recently introduced in reference [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, I find the paper convincing and worthy of publication, though I have several specific remarks outlined in the “Comments to Authors” section.
