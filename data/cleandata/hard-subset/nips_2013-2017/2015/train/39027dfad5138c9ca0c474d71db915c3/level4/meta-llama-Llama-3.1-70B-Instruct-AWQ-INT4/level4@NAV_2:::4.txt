This paper proposes a novel estimator, the self-normalized estimator, to address the propensity overfitting problem in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, which can lead to degeneracies when used in empirical risk minimization (ERM). They provide a thorough analysis of the problem, demonstrating that the conventional estimator can result in overfitting, even when the loss is transformed.

The proposed self-normalized estimator is shown to avoid this issue by using a multiplicative control variate to reduce the variance of the estimator. The authors provide theoretical guarantees for the self-normalized estimator, including its equivariance and strong consistency. They also derive a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on the CRM principle using the self-normalized estimator.

The experimental evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of Norm-POEM in avoiding propensity overfitting and achieving improved generalization performance compared to the conventional POEM algorithm. The results show that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on several multi-label classification problems, and that the improved estimator leads to better generalization performance.

Strengths:

* The paper identifies a significant issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator and proposes a novel solution.
* The analysis of the propensity overfitting problem is thorough and well-motivated.
* The self-normalized estimator is shown to have desirable theoretical properties, including equivariance and strong consistency.
* The experimental evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of Norm-POEM in avoiding propensity overfitting and achieving improved generalization performance.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the loss function is deterministic, whereas in many real-world applications, the loss function may be stochastic.
* The experimental evaluation is limited to a specific set of multi-label classification problems, and it is unclear how well the results generalize to other domains.
* The computational cost of Norm-POEM is higher than POEM due to the use of a multiplicative control variate, which may be a concern in large-scale applications.
