The commentary and notation surrounding the main equation (2) appear to be incorrect or misleading. Specifically, an expectation is typically expressed as a quantity of the form ∑f(x)p(x), where the expected quantity, f(x), is distinct from the probability measure, p(x). In equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity, which renders the subsequent comment on page 2 (second paragraph) technically incorrect. This comment suggests that equation (2) has an "anomaly" due to its non-invariance to translation of the loss. Furthermore, Example 1 is based on this incorrect assumption, which unsurprisingly does not hold.

From a practical perspective, the estimator in equation (2) is vulnerable to its denominator, π, which can be zero if the sample has never been observed in the prior. In contrast, the modified estimator in equation (7) appears to be an improvement in principle, as the effects of π are mitigated through the double denominator.

I hope the authors will revise their commentary if the paper is accepted.

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and achieves performance comparable to that of a Conditional Random Field (CRF) using full multi-label information. This paper presents an approach to "counterfactual learning," a specialized form of multi-label learning. In traditional multi-label learning, the ground-truth annotation for a sample provides a 0/1 label for each class; in contrast, this paper focuses on a scenario where only the label for one class (i.e., bandit feedback) is available. However, this is merely a preamble to justify the approach. In reality, the paper aims to improve upon a recently proposed estimator (POEM) presented in reference [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, I find the paper convincing and worthy of publication, but with some remarks that I raise in the Comments to authors field.
