- Identifies "propensity overfitting" in standard counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF, especially with complex hypothesis spaces and negative losses.  
- Shows the conventional unbiased estimator (Eq.2) can produce arbitrarily biased finite-sample risk estimates despite being unbiased in expectation.  
- Demonstrates the estimator fails equivariance under loss translation, unlike supervised learning, motivating a fix.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (Eq.7) that restores equivariance and bounds risk estimates within the loss range.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, applying the self-normalized estimator for structured output prediction.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and proofs that the self-normalized estimator resolves the identified anomalies.  
- Example 1 empirically illustrates how the standard estimator enables overfitting to propensities.  
- Empirical results on multi-label datasets show consistent improvements over POEM and performance close to a full-information CRF baseline.  
- Normalized propensity weights stay near 1, evidencing reduced propensity overfitting.  
- Notes Norm-POEM converges faster than POEM despite the normalization step.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.  
- Minor concern: clarify that the anomaly is a finite-sample behavior issue (estimator unbiased in expectation).  
- Minor concern: include more detailed comparisons with recent variance-reduction techniques for counterfactual evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: significant, well-justified contribution; recommends acceptance.
