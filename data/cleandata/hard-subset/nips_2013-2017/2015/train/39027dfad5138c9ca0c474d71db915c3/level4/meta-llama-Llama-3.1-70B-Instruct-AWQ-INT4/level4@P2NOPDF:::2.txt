The commentary and notation surrounding the main equation (2) appear to be incorrect or misleading. Specifically, an expectation is typically defined as a quantity of the form ∑f(x)p(x), where f(x) is the quantity being expected and p(x) is the probability measure. In equation (2), the loss is not the expected quantity, which raises questions about the subsequent comment on page 2, second paragraph, regarding the "anomaly" of equation (2) not being invariant to translation of the loss. This criticism is technically incorrect, and Example 1, which is based on this presumption, does not hold.

Furthermore, the estimator in equation (2) is sensitive to its denominator, π, which can be zero if the sample has never been observed in the prior. In contrast, the modified estimator in equation (7) appears to be an improvement, as the effects of π are mitigated by the double denominator.

I hope the authors will consider revising their commentary if the paper is accepted.

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and is comparable to a Conditional Random Field (CRF) using full multi-label information. This paper presents an approach to "counterfactual learning," a specialized form of multi-label learning where only the label for one class (i.e., bandit feedback) is available, rather than the traditional 0/1 label for each class. However, the primary aim of this paper is to improve upon the POEM estimator recently introduced in reference [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, I find the paper convincing and worthy of publication, but with some remarks that I have noted in the Comments to authors field.
