- Addresses propensity overfitting in counterfactual learning from logged bandit feedback within the CRM framework and proposes a mitigation.
- Proposes a self-normalized importance sampling estimator and derives Norm-POEM for structured output prediction with exponential-family models.
- Builds on POEM (ICML 2015) and connects to variance control in importance sampling via control variates; contrasts with clipping/truncation and empirical Bernstein bounds.
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multi-label datasets and approaches supervised CRF performance.
- Equation (2) exposition is misleading: the review notes a confusion between integrand and probability measure that should be corrected.
- The claim that non-invariance to loss translations is an “anomaly” is presented unclearly and rests on a technically incorrect framing.
- Example 1 relies on the problematic framing around Equation (2) and should be revised for technical correctness.
- IPS estimator vulnerability is practically due to tiny or zero propensities, causing unstable estimates.
- Self-normalized estimator reduces sensitivity to small propensities by dividing by the sum of weights, damping extreme weights.
- Recommend revising the text around Equation (2) and Example 1 to avoid conflating integrand and measure and to define “anomaly” precisely.
- Experiments use a supervised-to-bandit protocol, demonstrate consistent gains, show runtime observations, and analyze Ŝ(ĥ) to support normalization claims.
- Strengths: clear BLBF/CRM formulation and principled use of multiplicative control variates.
- Strengths: simple, broadly applicable self-normalized estimator with an intuitive diagnostic (Ŝ(h) ≈ 1).
- Strengths: consistent empirical improvements and claimed robustness to loss translations; useful discussion of optimization/variance trade-offs.
- Weaknesses: confusing theoretical exposition around Eq. (2), invariance claims, and Example 1 that risks misleading readers.
- Weaknesses: need to clarify logging policy support assumptions and explicit handling of pi = 0 (clipping/thresholding).
- Weaknesses: variance estimator for the ratio is approximate; discuss its accuracy and conditions for validity.
- Weaknesses: limited empirical comparison to other variance-reduction methods (stronger clipping baselines, doubly robust estimators).
- Quality: algorithmic idea is sound and well-connected to importance sampling literature; empirical evidence is strong.
- Clarity: generally well written but key motivational parts (Eq.2, “anomaly,” Example 1) require precise rewriting.
- Originality: novel application of self-normalized importance sampling within CRM and integration into Norm-POEM.
- Significance: high practical relevance for off-policy learning in recommendation/search/ads with demonstrated gains and robustness.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses an important failure mode, offers a simple principled estimator, and shows strong empirical benefits.
- Cons for acceptance: misleading exposition on IPS properties and limited comparison/discussion of variance approximation limitations.
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance conditional on fixing Eq.(2)/Example 1 exposition, clarifying handling of small/zero propensities, and discussing the variance approximation.
