- Tackles batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), identifies "propensity overfitting" in IPS-based ERM, and proposes Norm-POEM using a self-normalized importance sampling estimator with consistency and empirical gains.
- Builds on prior off-policy/importance-sampling work by applying a known self-normalized estimator to CRM and articulating a new failure mode of IPS in BLBF.
- Technical soundness: compelling diagnosis and illustrative example; self-normalized estimator is well-motivated, bounded, equivariant, and consistency proof is appropriate; variance approximation and clipping are reasonable.
- Empirics: controlled supervised-to-bandit experiments show consistent risk/stability improvements across datasets and training sizes, with Ŝ(h) analysis supporting the mechanism.
- Weakness: lacks a finite-sample/generalization bound tailored to the self-normalized estimator within CRM.
- Weakness: experimental baselines omit relevant competitors like doubly robust and regression-based estimators.
- Weakness: insufficient details on optimizing the non-convex objective (initialization, stopping, sensitivity to λ and clipping M) harming reproducibility.
- Clarity: clear, well-organized exposition; optimization section needs more practical implementation details (L-BFGS settings, gradients, runtime).
- Originality: estimator itself is known, but applying it to CRM, identifying propensity overfitting, and producing Norm-POEM is a meaningful, impactful contribution.
- Significance: addresses a practical IPS instability problem with substantial empirical gains, but scope limited to multi-label classification and lacks real-world logged data or broader domains.
- Pros: clear identification/demonstration of the pathology; well-motivated bounded estimator; consistent empirical improvements and robustness; useful Ŝ(h) diagnostic; code and reasonable compute footprint.
- Cons: no tailored finite-sample theory; limited domain and missing strong baselines; sparse optimization/hyperparameter analysis; real-world applicability not shown.
- Suggestions: provide CRM bounds or discussion for self-normalized estimator, add doubly robust and clipped IPS baselines, report standard errors/confidence intervals and Ŝ(h) variability, include ablations on λ/M and logging-policy quality, detail optimization and runtime, and evaluate at least one real-world logged dataset.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper that identifies a real IPS weakness and offers a principled, practical fix with strong empirical benefits; broader baselines, more optimization detail, and real-world evidence would increase impact.
