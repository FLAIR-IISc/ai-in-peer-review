This is a good, well-written paper. It proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that addresses the problem of propensity overfitting. Building upon Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the authors introduce a significantly improved algorithm.

**Quality:** The quality of the research is high. The proposed extensions result in significant improvements to the training of the original POEM algorithm.

**Clarity:** For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

**Originality:** The paper presents a novel reformulation of the original POEM algorithm for the batch learning from bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. The contributions are both significant and original.

**Significance:** The results are interesting and warrant further investigation. It is particularly impressive that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM, despite incorporating additional regularization mechanisms. This algorithm has the potential for widespread adoption in the near future.
