This paper presents an approach for "counterfactual learning," a specialization of multi-label learning where feedback is limited to a single label per sample (i.e., bandit feedback). The paper's primary contribution is to improve upon the POEM estimator recently introduced in [1]. Overall, the paper is convincing and presents a valuable contribution, but its theoretical justification contains significant flaws that should be addressed.

The experimental results are a clear strength. The proposed Norm-POEM estimator is shown to outperform the original POEM on all datasets and achieves performance competitive with a CRF trained on full multi-label information.

My main criticism concerns the commentary and mathematical interpretation of Equation (2). The authors claim that this equation has an "anomaly" because it is not invariant to translations of the loss function. This claim appears to be technically incorrect and based on a misinterpretation of the expectation operator. An expectation is a quantity of the form `\sum f(x) p(x)`, where `f(x)` is the random variable and `p(x)` is the probability measure. In Equation (2), the loss function is not the random variable over which the expectation is taken. Therefore, the argument that the estimator should be invariant to a translation of the loss is invalid. The subsequent Example 1 is built upon this flawed premise and is therefore unconvincing. I strongly urge the authors to revise this commentary if the paper is accepted.

Despite this flawed motivation, the proposed estimator is practically well-founded. The estimator in Equation (2) is vulnerable to instability since its denominator, `pi`, can be zero if a sample has not been observed in the prior data. The modified estimator in Equation (7) appears superior in principle, as its normalization structure (the "double denominator") mitigates the influence of small `pi` values and improves stability.
