- Paper addresses counterfactual learning (a specialization of multi-label learning with bandit/partial feedback).  
- Core technical contribution: proposes Norm-POEM, an improvement over the POEM estimator from [1].  
- Experiments show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and matches a fully supervised CRF on tested datasets.  
- Overall assessment: convincing and valuable contribution, but several issues need addressing.  
- Major concern: commentary and notation around Equation (2) are incorrect or misleading regarding expectations.  
- Specifically, the loss in Equation (2) is not the f(x) in a formal expectation \sum f(x)p(x), so the authors’ interpretation is flawed.  
- The claim that Equation (2) shows an "anomaly" due to non-invariance to loss translation is technically incorrect and misapplies expected-value definitions.  
- Example 1 is based on this flawed premise, making its conclusions unsound; the reviewer urges revision.  
- Practical point: the estimator in Equation (2) is sensitive to the denominator pi (which can be zero for unseen samples).  
- The modified estimator in Equation (7) with a self-normalizing denominator is more robust to small or zero pi values and is the paper’s key strength.
