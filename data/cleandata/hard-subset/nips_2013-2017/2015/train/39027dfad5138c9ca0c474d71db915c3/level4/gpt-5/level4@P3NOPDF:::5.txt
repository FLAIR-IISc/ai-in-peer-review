Summary:
This paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) that mitigates the propensity overfitting problem. Building on Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), it presents a substantially revised formulation of the original POEM algorithm, incorporating additional regularization that improves training stability and performance. Empirically, the proposed approach achieves better results than POEM and is faster to train despite the extra regularization.

Quality:
Overall, the technical quality is above average. The paper articulates a clear problem—propensity overfitting in counterfactual risk minimization—and provides a well-motivated solution. The extensions to POEM appear to lead to meaningful improvements in both optimization and generalization. The empirical evaluation, as presented, indicates significant gains over the original POEM and suggests the approach is practical.

Clarity:
The paper is generally clear and easy to follow. The problem setting, motivation, and high-level ideas are well explained. With a few small additions (see suggestions below), the exposition could be even stronger.

Originality:
The paper provides a significantly new formulation of POEM for the BLBF setting. While it builds on prior work, the proposed regularization and learning framework represent a substantive and original contribution beyond Swaminathan and Joachims (2015).

Significance:
The work addresses an important and timely problem in offline learning with bandit feedback. The proposed method appears both more accurate and faster than POEM, which enhances its practical utility. Given the prevalence of logged bandit data in applications such as recommendation and search, the method has the potential for broad impact and adoption. The results are intriguing and invite further research.

Strengths:
- Clear focus on a core limitation of POEM (propensity overfitting) and a principled remedy via regularization.
- A substantially revised formulation that is both conceptually appealing and empirically effective.
- Demonstrated improvements in training efficiency despite added regularization.
- Generally strong presentation and organization.

Weaknesses and questions:
- Theory and intuition: The paper would benefit from deeper intuition for how the proposed regularizer curbs propensity overfitting. Formal guarantees (e.g., generalization bounds or consistency under specified assumptions) would further strengthen the contribution.
- Sensitivity and ablations: Please provide sensitivity analyses for key hyperparameters (e.g., regularization strength, any clipping or constraints) and ablations isolating the contributions of the new components relative to POEM.
- Baselines and scope: While POEM is the primary prior art, consider including additional baselines or variants (e.g., alternative counterfactual estimators, regularization schemes) to contextualize the gains.
- Assumptions and robustness: Clarify assumptions on the logging policy and propensity estimation. How robust is the method to misspecified or noisy propensities? Are there diagnostics or safeguards (e.g., clipping) and how do they affect performance?
- Computational complexity: Provide a clearer description of per-iteration complexity and memory requirements, and explain why the method is faster in practice than POEM despite additional regularization.
- Reproducibility: Code and detailed experimental settings (including initialization, optimization parameters, and data preprocessing) would enhance reproducibility.

Suggestions for clarity:
- Define BLBF and “propensity overfitting” upfront and maintain consistent notation for propensities and counterfactual risk.
- Highlight precisely how the proposed objective differs from POEM (e.g., additional terms, constraints), ideally with a concise derivation or pseudocode.
- Include a brief discussion of when the method may not help (e.g., extreme propensity sparsity) and recommended practices for practitioners.

Overall assessment:
This is a good paper: it is clearly written, well motivated, and presents a meaningful and practical advance over POEM for batch learning from logged bandit feedback. The approach addresses a central weakness in existing methods and shows both accuracy and speed advantages, which is impressive given the additional regularization. While I would like to see stronger theoretical grounding and more extensive empirical analysis (sensitivity, ablations, broader baselines), the current results are convincing and the contribution is significant.

Recommendation: Accept.
