- Identifies a severe propensity overfitting problem in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for batch learning from logged bandit feedback.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator that provably avoids the propensity overfitting bias.  
- Derives a new algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on CRM using the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms conventional estimators, particularly in generalization.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and well-organized.  
- Thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting issue and its implications for CRM.  
- The self-normalized estimator is a novel and significant methodological contribution.  
- Significance is high for learning from logged data, but real-world applicability is not fully demonstrated.  
- Suggestion: report variance measures for experiments since multiple repetitions were run.  
- Suggestion: include extended experiments and additional (ideally real-world) applications beyond prior work.  
- Suggestion: provide further analysis on optimization of the training objective.  
- Overall assessment: solid, novel, and relevant work that could be strengthened with more experiments and practical insights.
