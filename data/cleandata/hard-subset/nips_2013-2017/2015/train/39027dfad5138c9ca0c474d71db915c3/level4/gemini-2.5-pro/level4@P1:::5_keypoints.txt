- Identifies a critical flaw ("propensity overfitting") in the standard unbiased counterfactual risk estimator that causes biased and degenerate risk estimates in complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes replacing the standard estimator with a self-normalized risk estimator using the sum of propensity weights as a multiplicative control variate.  
- Shows the self-normalized estimator remedies anomalies of the unbiased estimator (e.g., lack of equivariance to loss shifts) with theoretical arguments.  
- Develops Norm-POEM, a principled extension of POEM based on the improved estimator.  
- Provides strong empirical evidence: Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM on multiple multi-label classification datasets.  
- Demonstrates the improvement is due to avoiding propensity overfitting, with Norm-POEM keeping the sum of propensity weights near its expected value.  
- Reports high practical significance: the method is more robust and, surprisingly, computationally faster due to quicker convergence.  
- Paper is very well-written, clearly organized, and accessible.  
- Minor concern: the self-normalized estimator is biased in finite samples; discussion of effects in very small-sample regimes would be useful.  
- Minor concern: empirical evaluation could be broadened to other structured prediction tasks (e.g., sequence labeling) to further validate generality.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, practically significant contribution; strongly recommended for acceptance.
