### Review

**Summary of the Paper**

This paper addresses the problem of Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback (BLBF), a setting crucial for improving interactive systems like search engines and recommender systems using historical data. The standard approach in this area is Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM), which relies on an unbiased inverse propensity score (IPS) estimator to evaluate the performance of new policies.

The central contribution of this work is the identification and clear articulation of a critical flaw in this conventional estimator, which the authors term "Propensity Overfitting." They demonstrate that, unlike traditional overfitting which exploits spurious patterns in the loss, propensity overfitting occurs when a complex hypothesis class minimizes the estimated risk by exploiting the propensity scores themselves. For instance, with positive losses, a hypothesis can achieve an artificially low risk by assigning zero probability to all actions observed in the training data. The authors provide a compelling and simple example to illustrate this degeneracy, showing how it can lead to severely biased risk estimates and poor model selection.

To remedy this problem, the paper proposes replacing the standard unbiased estimator with a self-normalized estimator. This alternative estimator, derived using multiplicative control variates, is a ratio of the standard IPS estimator to the sum of the importance weights. The authors show that this self-normalized estimator is equivariant to translations in the loss function and is always bounded by the range of the observed losses, thereby provably avoiding the anomalies that cause propensity overfitting. Building on this improved estimator, they introduce a new learning algorithm, the Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models (Norm-POEM), and demonstrate its empirical effectiveness on several multi-label classification tasks, showing consistent and significant improvements over the previous state-of-the-art method (POEM).

**Strengths (Arguments for Acceptance)**

1.  **Originality and Significance:** The identification of "Propensity Overfitting" is a novel, insightful, and significant contribution to the field of counterfactual learning. It highlights a fundamental and previously overlooked pathology in the standard IPS estimator that can cause learning algorithms to fail catastrophically. This is not an incremental improvement but rather a diagnosis of a core problem with a widely used methodology. The proposed solution is both elegant and effective, advancing the state of the art in a demonstrable way.

2.  **Technical Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The proposed solution—using a self-normalized estimator—is well-motivated by established principles in Monte Carlo methods (control variates) and is principled. The authors provide clear theoretical arguments for its desirable properties, such as consistency (Theorem 2) and equivariance to loss-shifting. The derivation of the Norm-POEM algorithm is a straightforward and logical application of the CRM principle with the new estimator and an appropriate variance approximation.

3.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The "Propensity Overfitting" problem is introduced with a simple, intuitive, and powerful example (Example 1) that makes the issue crystal clear. The motivation for the self-normalized estimator is presented logically, and the connection to its theoretical properties is well-explained. The overall structure is coherent, guiding the reader from the problem identification to the solution and its empirical validation.

4.  **Thorough Empirical Evaluation:** The experiments are well-designed and convincingly support the paper's claims.
    *   They directly demonstrate that Norm-POEM achieves better generalization performance than the baseline POEM across multiple datasets (Table 1, Figure 1).
    *   Crucially, they validate that this improvement is indeed due to mitigating propensity overfitting by showing that the sum of weights `Ŝ(h)` for the learned hypothesis remains close to its expected value of 1, unlike for the baseline (Table 2).
    *   The experiments also confirm the theoretical claim of equivariance, showing that Norm-POEM's performance is robust to translations of the loss function, while the baseline's performance degrades severely (Table 2).
    *   The authors also show that variance regularization remains beneficial (Table 3) and that Norm-POEM is computationally efficient, often converging faster than its predecessor.

**Weaknesses (Arguments against Acceptance)**

1.  **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The empirical validation, while thorough within its domain, is confined to multi-label classification tasks. Although the proposed method is general, demonstrating its effectiveness on a broader range of problems, such as other structured prediction tasks or problems more traditionally associated with reinforcement learning, would further strengthen the paper's claims of general applicability.

2.  **Approximate Variance Estimator:** The variance estimator used for Norm-POEM (Equation 8) is an approximation based on the delta method or a Normal approximation. While this is a standard and reasonable choice for ratio estimators, the paper does not discuss potential limitations or inaccuracies of this approximation and their downstream effects on the CRM objective. This is a minor point, as the empirical results are strong, but a brief discussion could have added more depth.

**Recommendation**

This is an excellent paper that makes a clear and important contribution. It identifies a significant, previously unarticulated problem in a popular counterfactual learning framework and proposes a principled, effective, and empirically validated solution. The strengths—particularly the novelty of identifying propensity overfitting and the quality of the solution and its evaluation—far outweigh the minor weaknesses related to the scope of experimentation. The work is likely to be highly influential for researchers and practitioners working with logged bandit data.

I strongly recommend acceptance.
