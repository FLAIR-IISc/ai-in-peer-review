- Problem: Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), improving policies from historical interaction data.
- Key contribution: Identifies and formalizes "propensity overfitting"—the unbiased CRM estimator can be exploited by complex hypothesis spaces to produce degenerate solutions.
- Evidence: Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and a simple illustrative example showing how propensity weights cause overfitting.
- Solution: Proposes using a self-normalized risk estimator (multiplicative control variate) to replace the conventional unbiased estimator.
- Rationale: The self-normalized estimator is translation-equivariant and bounded by observed loss range, directly preventing the identified pathologies.
- Algorithm: Introduces Norm-POEM for structured prediction with linear models, using the self-normalized estimator.
- Empirical results: Experiments on multi-label datasets show Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM and controls propensity overfitting.
- Strength — significance/originality: Exposes a subtle but severe flaw in a widely used estimator; applying self-normalization to this problem is novel and insightful.
- Strength — quality: Theoretical claims are sound, analysis is rigorous, and experiments are well-controlled and supportive.
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written and logically develops motivation, example, solution, and algorithm.
- Weakness — optimization: Norm-POEM objective is non-convex with a variance term unsuited to stochastic optimization; reliance on batch optimizers may limit scalability.
- Weakness — model scope: Focuses on linear exponential family models; extension to non-linear/deep models is not addressed.
- Arguments for acceptance: Clearly identifies a fundamental problem, offers a principled fix, provides strong empirical support, and has high practical significance.
- Arguments against acceptance: Core statistical tool is existing (reducing novelty) and potential computational/scalability concerns with optimization.
- Recommendation: Strong acceptance—strengths substantially outweigh minor weaknesses.
