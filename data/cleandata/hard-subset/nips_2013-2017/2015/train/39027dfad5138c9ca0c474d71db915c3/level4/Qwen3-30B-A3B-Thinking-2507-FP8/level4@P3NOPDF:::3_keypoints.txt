- Tackles batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a high-impact real-world problem (e.g., ad ranking).  
- Identifies an overfitting issue in the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle.  
- Proposes multiplicative control variates to produce a self-normalizing risk estimator.  
- Core contribution: the estimator modification that demonstrably improves empirical performance.  
- Builds logically on prior work [1] and is well-motivated.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of optimization dynamics (convergence behavior, hyperparameter sensitivity).  
- Experimental scope is limited (mostly synthetic/benchmarks); needs more diverse or real-world evaluations.  
- Must report variance measures (standard deviations, confidence intervals) across runs to assess robustness.  
- Should analyze the bias/variance trade-off of the self-normalizing estimator and justify the introduced bias.  
- Presentation is clear, well-written, and well-organized.  
- Originality: a follow-up to [1] but with an impactful, distinguishing modification.  
- Significance claim needs tempering—unclear if gains scale to large, noisy, high-dimensional real-world systems.  
- Recommendation: include at least one non-synthetic application or detailed deployment discussion to strengthen practical impact.
