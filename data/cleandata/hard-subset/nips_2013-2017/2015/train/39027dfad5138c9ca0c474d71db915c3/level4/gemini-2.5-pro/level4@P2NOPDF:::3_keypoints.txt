- Addresses learning from logged bandit feedback, identifies overfitting in CRM, and proposes a biased self-normalizing estimator using multiplicative control variates that shows empirical improvements.  
- High-quality, technically sound paper tackling a relevant problem with important practical implications.  
- Strength: tackles a well-established, important problem in reinforcement learning and applications.  
- Strength: clearly motivates the work by identifying the overfitting issue in the standard CRM estimator.  
- Strength: proposes a novel, clever self-normalizing estimator as an empirically strong alternative to prior work.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and presentation is clear.  
- Weakness: limited empirical scope—experiments largely repeat prior settings; needs new datasets or real-world applications to demonstrate broader significance.  
- Weakness: lacks variance reporting—results need standard deviations or confidence intervals to assess significance and stability.  
- Weakness: brief discussion of optimization—needs more analysis of optimization landscape and convergence properties.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of the estimator's bias and the bias–variance trade-offs and their implications.  
- Conclusion: solid, elegant contribution whose impact would be substantially increased by broader experiments, variance reporting, and deeper theoretical/optimization analysis.
