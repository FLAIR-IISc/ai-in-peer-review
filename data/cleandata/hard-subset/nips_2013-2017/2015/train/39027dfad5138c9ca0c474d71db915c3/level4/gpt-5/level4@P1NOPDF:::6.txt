Counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) targets learning in the bandit setting from logged data by correcting for the bias induced by the logging policy. While CRM is consistent, the paper shows it can overfit: a hypothesis class may exploit the example-generation probabilities (propensities), effectively overfitting to regions of the data that receive higher support from the logging policy. To address this, the authors introduce a multiplicative normalization parameter that bounds the estimator’s variance while maintaining consistency. Empirically, the multiplicative (self-normalized) counterfactual method outperforms the non-normalized alternative.

The paper thus presents a technique for learning a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning with logged bandit feedback and provides a principled way to mitigate overfitting driven by propensity-weighted support. The ideas are sound, and the experiments support the claims. Overall, this is a good paper and should be accepted, though I am not entirely certain.
