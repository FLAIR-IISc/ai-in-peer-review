- Paper studies counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) for learning from logged bandit feedback using propensity-based reweighting.
- CRM is consistent but can overfit by exploiting examples with very small logging probabilities.
- Overfitting occurs when hypotheses overemphasize logged examples they happen to support via large inverse-propensity weights.
- Authors propose a self-normalized (multiplicative) importance-weighted estimator to bound variance.
- The normalized estimator preserves consistency and avoids introducing asymptotic bias.
- Normalization curbs the instability that leads to CRM overfitting.
- Empirical results show the normalized estimator outperforms the unnormalized CRM baseline across reported settings.
- Contribution is clear and well-motivated: identifies a concrete failure mode and offers a principled fix.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, with a small degree of remaining uncertainty.
