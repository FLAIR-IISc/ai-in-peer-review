- Addresses propensity overfitting in counterfactual risk estimators for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Demonstrates the common estimator can be severely biased, especially with complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirically shows Norm-POEM outperforms existing methods on multi-label classification tasks.  
- Formalizes propensity overfitting and proves consistency for the self-normalized estimator (Theorem 2).  
- Adapts multiplicative control variates from finance/RL to reduce estimator bias/variance.  
- Provides ablation study showing variance regularization remains beneficial even with the new estimator.  
- Includes runtime comparisons and overall clear, well-written presentation.  
- Lacks a more rigorous mathematical explanation for why propensity overfitting emerges.  
- Needs further elaboration on how hypothesis space capacity affects overfitting severity.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited mainly to POEM; broader BLBF baselines needed.  
- Should discuss scenarios where Norm-POEM’s benefits might diminish given the supervised 7→bandit setup.  
- Computational complexity discussion is brief; a component-level cost breakdown would be useful.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel, theoretically sound paper with compelling empirical results—recommend acceptance.
