**Questions for Rebuttal**

*   Reference [1] (Section 4.1) states that the CRM approach is applicable when the loss `delta` is scaled to the range [-1, 0]. I assume this requirement also applies to the proposed NORM-POEM estimator, meaning that the `delta` used in Equations (3) and (8) must be rescaled to [-1, 0]. Given this assumption, I am confused by the experimental setup in Table 1, which includes results for "delta > 0". Could the authors please clarify this discrepancy?

**Other Comments**

*   The problem formulation for "batch learning from logged bandit feedback" in Section 3 appears to treat the feedback `delta(x,y)` as a deterministic function (line 110). I believe a more general and realistic setup would model this feedback as a random variable. To illustrate, consider a classification task with noisy labels. For a given input `x` and action `y` (from policy `h_0`), the observed loss depends on the true (and potentially stochastic) label `y'`. If the same input `x` appears multiple times in the log, it could be associated with different true labels `y'`, leading to different observed losses `delta(x,y)` even for the same action `y`. This suggests that `delta(x,y)` should be treated as a random variable. While this may not affect the risk estimator in Equation (2), the definition of the true risk in Equation (1) should ideally include an additional expectation over the randomness of `delta` to be more general.
*   On lines 170-171, the ratio appears to be inverted; I believe it should be `h(y|x) / h_0(y|x)`. Additionally, it would be appropriate to mention that the lack of linearity discussed here was also noted in [1].
*   On line 276, `argmax` should be `argmin`, as the objective is to minimize the risk.
*   On line 319, the notation should be changed from bars to hats to maintain consistency with the rest of the paper.

**Update After Rebuttal**

I thank the authors for their clarifications. This is a valuable follow-up to the work in [1]. The analysis of "propensity overfitting" is insightful, and the proposed multiplicative control variate is a clever approach to mitigate it. The empirical results demonstrate a clear improvement over the POEM baseline on the important and increasingly relevant problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback.
