- Addresses off-policy evaluation: estimating target-hypothesis risk from data collected under a different behavior policy — a compelling problem.  
- Proposes regularization to mitigate "propensity overfitting," which is promising for limited-exploration settings.  
- Needs clearer articulation of how propensity overfitting relates to high-variance importance weights to establish novelty and scope.  
- Defines propensity overfitting as a modeling error from sparse data causing overfitted propensity scores, but the paper doesn't explicitly connect or distinguish this from weight variance.  
- Authors should state whether propensity overfitting causes high weight variance (e.g., by amplifying instability) or is a distinct issue requiring separate mitigation.  
- Reviewer could not fully verify novelty or correctness due to time constraints, though the core idea of regularizing propensity estimation seems reasonable.  
- Line 199 is confusing: the estimator \hat{R}(h)= (1/n) Σ I(x_i = y_i) \hat{w}_i and the example about being "even smaller than -2" appears incorrect or a typo and needs clarification.  
- The claim that prior works use an "unbiased counterfactual risk estimator" is overstated; practical implementations often clip/truncate weights (introducing bias), so the paper should acknowledge common variance-reduction practices.  
- The paper’s identification of propensity overfitting as a challenge distinct from weight variance is an important contribution.  
- Regularizing propensity-score estimation is conceptually sound, aligns with robust off-policy learning trends, and the provided code is a strong reproducibility asset.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, but require revisions clarifying the relation to weight variance and fixing the noted technical/claim issues.
