Summary
The paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant problem (e.g., in ad ranking). It identifies an overfitting issue in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a remedy using multiplicative control variates, yielding a self-normalizing but biased estimator.

Quality
The work is high quality and well motivated. However, as a follow-up to [1], I expected additional experiments and a deeper analysis of the optimization of the training objective—more detail than the brief discussion around line 415.

Clarity
The presentation is clear and the paper is well written.

Originality
While it builds on [1], the modification of the risk estimator to be self-normalizing is a meaningful contribution and appears to make a substantial difference in the authors’ experiments.

Significance
Learning from logged data is an important and timely problem. However, it is difficult to assess how the substantial experimental improvements will translate to broader, real-world applications.

Remarks
- Line 365: If you already performed several repetitions, please include a measure of variability (e.g., standard deviations or confidence intervals).
- The paper clearly identifies a problem in CRM and introduces a principled solution based on multiplicative control variates. Overall, the work is solid, well written, and both relevant and novel. I would have appreciated more extensive experiments and, especially, applications beyond those in [1], including at least one real-world application or dataset.
