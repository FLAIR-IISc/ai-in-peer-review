- Addresses counterfactual risk minimization for learning from logged bandit data to mitigate generation bias.
- Identifies an overfitting issue where hypothesis classes can overfit based on the probability of generating observed examples despite consistency guarantees.
- Proposes a multiplicative parameter (self-normalized estimator) to bound variance while preserving consistency.
- Shows experimental evidence that the normalized counterfactual approach outperforms the non-normalized alternative.
- Frames the method as a solution to overfitting caused by excessive support for logged data.
- Overall assessment: strong paper, recommended for acceptance but with some remaining uncertainty.
