When observing data under a given hypothesis, we may wish to evaluate a different target hypothesis. For non-deterministic hypotheses (e.g., conditional distributions), the loss under the target hypothesis can be estimated by reweighting the data points, as indicated between equations (1) and (2).  

The authors address a problem related but distinct from the well-known issue of large weight variance. They term this "propensity overfitting," which arises when insufficient exploration occurs—specifically, for a given input \(x\), few or no corresponding outputs \(y\) exist under the target hypothesis being evaluated. Since this phenomenon shares conceptual similarities with large weight variance, a more detailed explanation of the relationship between these two issues would be valuable.
