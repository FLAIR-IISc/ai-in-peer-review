This paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) that avoids the propensity overfitting problem. Building on Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), it provides a significantly better algorithm.

Quality: The quality of the research is above average. The extensions proposed in the paper result in significant improvements in training over the original POEM.

Clarity: For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

Originality: The paper offers a significantly new formulation of the original POEM algorithm for the BLBF problem. I believe the contributions are significantly original.

Significance: The results are very interesting and certainly require further research. I am impressed that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM despite having more regularization mechanisms built into it. This algorithm may see widespread use in the near future.

Overall: This is a good paper. It is clear and reads well.
