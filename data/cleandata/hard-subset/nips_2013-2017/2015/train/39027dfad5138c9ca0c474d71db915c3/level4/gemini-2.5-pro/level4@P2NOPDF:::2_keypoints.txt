- Paper proposes Norm-POEM, a modification of POEM for counterfactual/multi-label learning with bandit feedback.  
- Norm-POEM empirically outperforms POEM across datasets and approaches CRF performance with full supervision.  
- Clear practical motivation: POEM is unstable when propensity scores pi are zero or near-zero, causing high variance.  
- Norm-POEM introduces a normalization term (Equation 7) that effectively reduces the influence of small propensity scores.  
- Major theoretical concern: authors misunderstand expectation—treating the loss as the expected quantity rather than part of the probability measure.  
- Consequently, the claim that Equation (2) has an “anomaly” due to lack of invariance to loss translation is incorrect.  
- Example 1 and subsequent theoretical motivation are built on this flawed premise and are therefore unsound.  
- Despite the flawed theory, the estimator’s empirical success stands, driven by practical variance reduction rather than the claimed invariance issue.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revising the theoretical commentary to correctly frame the motivation around estimator instability from small propensities, not loss-translation invariance.
