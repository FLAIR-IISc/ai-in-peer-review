The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that avoids the propensity overfitting problem. It builds on Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) and provides a significantly better algorithm.

Quality: I found the quality of the research to be above average. The extensions proposed in the paper result in significant improvements in training over the original POEM.

Clarity: For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

Originality: The paper presents a substantially new formulation of the original POEM algorithm for the BLBF problem. I believe the contributions are significantly original.

Significance: The results are very interesting and certainly warrant further research. I am impressed that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM despite incorporating additional regularization mechanisms. This algorithm may see widespread use in the near future.

This is a good paper. It is clear and reads well.
