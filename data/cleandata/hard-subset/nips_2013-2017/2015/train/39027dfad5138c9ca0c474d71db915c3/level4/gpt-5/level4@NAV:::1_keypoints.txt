- Authors introduce/target a problem called “propensity overfitting,” related to but not identical with high-variance importance weights.
- Propensity overfitting is said to arise from insufficient exploration: for some x, very few or no y consistent with the target hypothesis are observed.
- Reviewer asks for clearer distinction and deeper clarification of the relationship between propensity overfitting and the classic large-variance issue.
- Reviewer did not verify technical details or assess novelty but finds the core idea reasonable.
