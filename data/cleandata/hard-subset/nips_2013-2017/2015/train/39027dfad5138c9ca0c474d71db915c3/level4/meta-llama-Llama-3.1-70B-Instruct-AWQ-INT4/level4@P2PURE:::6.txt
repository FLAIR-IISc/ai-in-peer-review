This paper introduces a technique for counterfactual risk minimization, which aims to learn from logged data in a bandit setting by correcting for the bias inherent in the data generation process. While the proposed method is consistent, it is shown to be susceptible to overfitting, where the hypothesis class can overfit to the data by exploiting the generation probabilities of the logged examples. To address this issue, the authors propose a multiplicative parameter that bounds the variance of the estimator while maintaining consistency. The experimental results demonstrate that the multiplicative (or normalized) counterfactual technique outperforms its non-normalized counterpart.

The paper presents a novel approach to learning self-normalized estimators for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting. It offers valuable insights into a specific form of overfitting, where the hypothesis can overfit by having stronger support for the logged data. Overall, this is a well-written and solid paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the field, and I believe it should be accepted, although I am not entirely certain about its impact.
