- Identifies propensity overfitting in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF and proposes a self-normalized estimator to fix it.  
- Notes a novel anomaly: conventional estimator lacks equivariance under loss translation and can severely overestimate risk with complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator, and demonstrates consistent improvements over POEM on multi-label tasks.  
- Theoretical strength: rigorous analysis with Theorem 2 showing strong consistency and illustrative examples exposing the overfitting mechanism (e.g., Example 1).  
- Empirical strength: thorough experiments on standard BLBF benchmarks (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL) with consistent outperformance across datasets and training sizes.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with clear definitions, intuitive examples, concise derivation of Norm-POEM, and available code for reproducibility.  
- Originality: frames propensity overfitting as a novel problem distinct from known IS variance issues and applies multiplicative control variates in a new way to BLBF.  
- Significance: addresses practical deployment concerns (ad placement, recommendations) by demonstrating Norm-POEM’s robustness and computational efficiency.  
- Weakness: inaccurate claim that prior works use an “unbiased estimator”; POEM and others use clipped (biased) estimators and this should be clarified.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 assumes full support of h0; the paper should discuss implications for real-world datasets with sparse exploration.  
- Weakness: Example 1 in Section 4.1 needs clearer wording when n << k (R̂(hoverfit) = −k) to avoid confusion.  
- Weakness: the relationship between propensity overfitting and weight variance requires deeper elaboration in Section 4.  
- Weakness: impact would be stronger with runtime comparisons to recent BLBF methods (e.g., [11]), although current analysis shows competitiveness with POEM.  
- Overall: recommends acceptance for novel problem identification, rigorous theory, strong empirical validation, and practical relevance, noting only minor clarifications needed.
