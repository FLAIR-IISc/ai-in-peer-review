- Identifies propensity overfitting in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in BLBF.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator and implements it in Norm-POEM to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Frames BLBF as a crucial framework for leveraging logged bandit feedback when full-information labels are unavailable.  
- Shows the widely adopted unbiased estimator can be unstable in complex hypothesis spaces, producing biased risk estimates.  
- Empirically validates Norm-POEM on multi-label classification, consistently outperforming the conventional estimator.  
- Contextualizes BLBF within causal inference and off-policy evaluation, linking to propensity score matching and importance sampling.  
- Introduces multiplicative control variates and derives the self-normalized estimator as a principled variance-reduction solution.  
- Thoroughly reviews related work on counterfactual estimation and variance reduction techniques.  
- Strength: insightful identification of a significant, previously underrecognized problem in BLBF.  
- Strength: theoretically grounded and practically effective solution with consistent empirical improvements.  
- Strength: experimental setup mirrors prior supervised-to-bandit methodology, enabling fair comparisons with POEM.  
- Strength: analysis of Ŝ(h) invariance to loss translation supports theoretical claims; presentation and derivations are clear.  
- Weakness: contains a technical inaccuracy in framing the equivariance claim—problem arises from small propensities, not the estimator's expectation.  
- Weakness: experiments would benefit from deeper dataset analysis and identification of scenarios where Norm-POEM excels.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison of computational cost relative to other variance-reduction methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify the technical justification about estimator equivariance.
