- Identifies a critical flaw in CRM for BLBF: "propensity overfitting" where ERM exploits propensity weights instead of true losses.  
- Shows the conventional unbiased estimator can produce arbitrarily biased risk estimates via a counterexample (Example 1).  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates (Equation 7) to fix propensity overfitting.  
- The self-normalized estimator is equivariant under loss translation and bounded within the loss range, unlike the conventional estimator.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a practical algorithm for structured prediction based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong and supported by rigorous proofs (Theorems 1–2) showing consistency and desirable properties.  
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple multi-label datasets and matches CRF runtime.  
- Paper is very well written and clearly connects estimator properties to observed failure modes, with intuitive examples.  
- Originality: novel and significant technical fix to foundational CRM methods, not merely incremental.  
- Weakness: optimization analysis is underdeveloped—no explanation why self-normalization improves convergence or how non-convexity affects generalization.  
- Weakness: experiments omit variance metrics (e.g., standard deviations), weakening statistical significance claims.  
- Weakness: limited explicit discussion of why propensity overfitting is especially problematic in structured prediction versus simpler settings.  
- Recommendation: accept, with camera-ready fixes to add variance reporting and clarify optimization/thresholding details.
