- Problem: Learning from logged bandit data where historical policy bias must be corrected for when training a new policy.
- Standard approach: Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) uses importance weighting to correct bias and is consistent.
- Identified issue: CRM can overfit by favoring examples that were highly probable under the logging policy, producing high-variance estimators that generalize poorly.
- Proposed fix: A self-normalized CRM estimator that normalizes importance-weighted risk by the sum of importance weights.
- Effect of fix: Normalization reduces variance by preventing dominance of a few high-weight examples while preserving consistency.
- Empirical results: Experiments show the normalized estimator consistently outperforms the standard non-normalized CRM.
- Strength: Clearly identifies a significant practical problem in off-policy learning and provides sharp analysis of its manifestation.
- Strength: The self-normalized estimator is simple, intuitive, effective, and theoretically grounded.
- Strength: Claims are well-supported by clear empirical evidence.
- Weakness: The paper would benefit from deeper theoretical analysis of variance reduction or finite-sample bounds.
- Recommendation: Strong paper; recommend acceptance.
