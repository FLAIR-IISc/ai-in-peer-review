- Paper studies batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies "propensity overfitting" with the inverse propensity score estimator.
- Authors propose a self-normalized estimator and Norm-POEM algorithm for structured output prediction.
- Paper builds on Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) and importance sampling for policy risk estimation.
- Reviewers note the conventional estimator has unbounded variance due to small propensity scores, a known issue addressed previously by clipping.
- The authors' motivation claims R̂(h) is not equivariant to loss translation, which the reviewer says is incorrect and undermines the "propensity overfitting" argument.
- Example 1 and parts of the paper's motivation are based on this fundamental misunderstanding about loss translation.
- The self-normalized estimator R̂SN(h) is a valid alternative that is bounded and equivariant to loss translation.
- Theoretical analysis of the self-normalized estimator (Theorem 2) is sound.
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM consistently across multiple datasets.
- Strengths: identifies a real limitation in BLBF, proposes a theoretically sound estimator, strong empirical validation, and clear writing.
- Weaknesses: flawed motivation due to technical error, insufficient discussion why prior work missed this issue, and limited positioning relative to other variance reduction methods (e.g., control variates).
- Quality: strong empirical and theoretical parts, but weakened by the motivation error.
- Originality: "propensity overfitting" framing is novel; the self-normalized estimator itself is known but its BLBF application and experiments are original.
- Significance: addresses a practical BLBF problem with real-world relevance and demonstrable empirical gains.
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to fix the technical error in the motivation and better situate the contribution within prior variance-reduction literature.
