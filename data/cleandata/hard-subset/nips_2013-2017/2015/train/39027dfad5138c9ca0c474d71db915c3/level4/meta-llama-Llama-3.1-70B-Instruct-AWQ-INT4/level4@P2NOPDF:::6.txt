This paper presents a technique for learning a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting. The authors address the issue of overfitting, where a hypothesis class can overfit by having stronger support for the logged data, due to the generation probability of an example. They propose a solution using a multiplicative parameter that bounds the variance while maintaining consistency. The experimental results demonstrate that the multiplicative (or normalized) counterfactual technique outperforms the non-normalized one.

The paper provides a valuable contribution to the field of counterfactual risk minimization, a technique aimed at learning in a bandit setting from logged data by correcting for the bias of generating logged examples. While the original technique is consistent, it suffers from an overfitting problem. The authors' proposed solution effectively mitigates this issue.

Overall, this is a good paper that presents a well-motivated and well-executed idea. The writing is clear, and the results are convincing. I recommend acceptance, although I am not absolutely certain.
