This paper introduces a technique for counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) that addresses a severe overfitting problem in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a propensity overfitting issue that arises when using the conventional unbiased risk estimator, which can lead to degenerate risk estimates. To mitigate this, they propose a self-normalized risk estimator that incorporates a multiplicative control variate, resulting in a new learning algorithm called Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models (Norm-POEM).

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the propensity overfitting problem and its implications for BLBF. The proposed self-normalized estimator is theoretically sound, and the authors provide a proof of its strong consistency. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of Norm-POEM in avoiding propensity overfitting and achieving better generalization performance compared to the conventional CRM approach.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough review of related work in the field. The experimental setup is well-designed, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the computational efficiency of Norm-POEM, which is an important consideration for practical applications.

Strengths:

* The paper introduces a novel technique for addressing propensity overfitting in BLBF, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed self-normalized estimator, including a proof of its strong consistency.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of Norm-POEM in avoiding propensity overfitting and achieving better generalization performance.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a relatively high level of background knowledge in CRM and BLBF, which may make it challenging for non-experts to follow.
* The authors could provide more discussion on the potential limitations of Norm-POEM, such as its sensitivity to hyperparameter tuning.

Overall, this is a well-written and technically sound paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of CRM and BLBF. The proposed self-normalized estimator is a valuable technique for addressing propensity overfitting, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness.
