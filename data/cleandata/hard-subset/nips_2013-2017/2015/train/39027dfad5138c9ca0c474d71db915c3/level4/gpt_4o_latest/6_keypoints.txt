- Counterfactual risk minimization is a consistent method for learning in bandit settings from logged data with bias correction.
- The paper identifies an overfitting issue where hypotheses can overfit by exploiting generation probabilities of logged examples.
- Authors propose a multiplicative (self-normalized) parameter to control variance while preserving consistency.
- Experimental results show the multiplicative/normalized estimator outperforms the non-normalized approach.
- The method introduces a self-normalized estimator specifically for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting.
- The approach explicitly tackles hypotheses placing excessive weight on logged data to avoid overfitting.
- Reviewer considers the paper strong and meriting acceptance, though not completely certain.
