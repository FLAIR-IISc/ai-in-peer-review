- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Identifies a severe degeneracy in the conventional unbiased risk estimator when used in empirical risk minimization (ERM).
- Introduces a multiplicative control variate and a new algorithm, Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models (Norm-POEM).
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis of the self-normalized estimator and its properties.
- Empirically evaluates Norm-POEM on several multi-label classification tasks, showing consistent improvements over the conventional estimator.
- Frames the algorithm within the counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) principle.
- Strength: clear, well-structured exposition and detailed empirical evaluation.
- Strength: novel solution addressing a common BLBF issue (propensity overfitting).
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with BLBF and CRM, which may hinder accessibility for non-experts.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion on the relationship between propensity overfitting and large weight variance.
- Reviewer did not fully verify technical soundness but recommends acceptance based on the paper's interesting ideas.
- Minor: clarify the explanation of propensity overfitting around Line 199.
- Minor: add more discussion comparing the novelty of the solution to existing approaches.
- Minor: code availability is appreciated.
