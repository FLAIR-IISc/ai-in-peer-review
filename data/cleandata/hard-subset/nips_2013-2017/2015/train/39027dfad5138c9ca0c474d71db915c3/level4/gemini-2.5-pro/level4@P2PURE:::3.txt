### Summary

This paper addresses the problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, which is highly relevant to applications such as ad ranking. The authors identify an "overfitting" issue with the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and propose a solution using a biased estimator based on multiplicative control variates.

### Quality

The work is high-quality and well-motivated. However, given that it is a follow-up to [1], the paper would be stronger with more extensive experiments. Additionally, a more detailed analysis of the optimization of the training objective, beyond the brief discussion on line 415, would be beneficial.

### Clarity

The paper is well-written and the presentation is clear.

### Originality

While this is follow-up work on [1], it introduces a novel modification to the risk estimator by making it self-normalizing. This change appears to yield significant improvements in the authors' experiments.

### Significance

The problem of learning from logged data is significant. However, it remains unclear how the substantial experimental improvements demonstrated in the paper will translate to performance gains in real-world applications.

### Various Remarks

Overall, this is a solid, well-written, relevant, and novel paper that identifies and addresses a key problem in the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle. The work could be strengthened with more extensive experiments, particularly by exploring applications beyond those presented in [1] and including results from real-world scenarios.

A specific suggestion for the current experiments: on line 365, since several repetitions were run, please include a measure of variance (e.g., standard deviation or error bars) to accompany the results.
