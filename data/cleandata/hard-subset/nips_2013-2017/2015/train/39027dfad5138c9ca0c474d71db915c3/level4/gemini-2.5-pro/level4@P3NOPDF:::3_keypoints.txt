- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback; identifies overfitting from high-variance importance-weighted CRM estimator and proposes a self-normalizing estimator using multiplicative control variates (introduces bias but empirically effective).
- Paper is high-quality, well-motivated, clearly written, and tackles a significant, practical problem in offline policy learning.
- Proposed self-normalizing modification is elegant and yields substantial empirical performance gains over the baseline.
- Experimental scope is limited — evaluation relies on datasets studied in prior work [1] and should include a broader range of new, real-world applications to demonstrate generalizability.
- Optimization analysis is brief (around line 415); the paper needs deeper exploration of optimization landscape, convergence properties, and hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Experimental reporting omits variance measures (e.g., error bars or confidence intervals despite multiple runs around line 365); adding these would clarify stability and significance.
- Originality is strong: the self-normalizing term is a novel, impactful fix to an unaddressed weakness in the CRM framework.
