Other comments
- On the setup in Section 3 (“batch learning from logged bandit feedback”): It seems incomplete (in your paper or in [1]) to model the feedback as a deterministic function, as on line 110. In many realistic settings, feedback is stochastic. For example, consider a classification problem where true labels are unknown and we only observe the incurred loss after making a prediction. Let Delta(y’, y) be the true classification loss. For a given x, the logging policy h0 selects y, a notional oracle labels x with y’, and we observe the loss Delta(y’, y). With noisy labels, the same x and the same played y could yield different observed losses across occurrences, because the oracle might assign different y’. Thus delta(x, y) should generally be treated as a random variable; for fixed x and y, its mean would be the expectation of Delta(y’, y) under the true labeling distribution p(y’ | x). This perspective likely does not change estimator (2), but the definition of the true risk in (1) should include an additional expectation over the randomness in delta to be fully general.
