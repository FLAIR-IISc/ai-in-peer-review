- Introduces a technique for counterfactual risk minimization to learn from logged bandit data by correcting generation bias.  
- The proposed estimator is consistent but vulnerable to overfitting by exploiting logged-example probabilities.  
- Identifies a specific overfitting mode where hypotheses gain by placing stronger support on logged data.  
- Proposes a multiplicative (self-normalization) parameter that bounds estimator variance while preserving consistency.  
- Experimental results show the normalized/multiplicative counterfactual estimator outperforms the non-normalized version.  
- Presents a novel approach to learning self-normalized estimators in the logged bandit setting.  
- Paper is well-written and a meaningful contribution; recommended for acceptance though overall impact is somewhat uncertain.
