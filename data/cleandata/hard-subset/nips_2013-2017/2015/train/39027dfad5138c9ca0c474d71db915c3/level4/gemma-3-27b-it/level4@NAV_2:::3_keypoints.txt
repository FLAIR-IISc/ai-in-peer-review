- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a crucial problem for ad ranking and recommendation systems.  
- Identifies a novel "propensity overfitting" issue within the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) framework.  
- Proposes multiplicative control variates to produce a self-normalized (biased) estimator as a solution.  
- Builds on prior work [1] with a significant and novel modification to the risk estimator.  
- Empirical results report consistent performance improvements over existing methods.  
- Paper is high-quality, well-motivated, clearly written, and demonstrates strong conceptual understanding.  
- Experimental scope is limited—needs more extensive evaluation beyond the settings in [1].  
- Lacks deeper analysis of the optimization/training objective (discussion beyond line 415 is insufficient).  
- Should report variance measures given multiple repetitions (comment at line 365).  
- Unclear how well the reported improvements generalize to broader, real-world applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel discovery, theoretically grounded and empirically validated, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited real-world exploration, insufficient optimization analysis, and need for additional experiments.
