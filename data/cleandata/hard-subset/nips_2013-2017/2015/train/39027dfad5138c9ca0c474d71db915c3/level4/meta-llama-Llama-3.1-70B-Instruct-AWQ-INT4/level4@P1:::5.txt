The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that effectively addresses the propensity overfitting problem. Building upon the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the authors present a significantly improved algorithm.

Quality: The research discussed in the paper is of above-average quality. The proposed extensions result in substantial improvements in the training of the original POEM algorithm. The authors demonstrate a thorough understanding of the underlying concepts and provide a rigorous analysis of the propensity overfitting problem.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method, including the mathematical derivations and algorithmic details. The text is well-organized, and the authors use appropriate headings and subheadings to guide the reader through the paper.

Originality: The paper presents a significantly new formulation of the original POEM algorithm for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. The authors' contributions are substantial and original, providing a novel solution to the propensity overfitting problem.

Significance: The results presented in the paper are interesting and warrant further research. The proposed algorithm's improved performance and computational efficiency make it a promising approach for BLBF problems. The authors' finding that the algorithm is faster than the original POEM despite having more regularization mechanisms built into it is particularly noteworthy. This algorithm may see widespread use in the near future.

Overall, this is a good paper that is clear and well-written. The authors' contributions are significant, and their work has the potential to make a substantial impact in the field.
