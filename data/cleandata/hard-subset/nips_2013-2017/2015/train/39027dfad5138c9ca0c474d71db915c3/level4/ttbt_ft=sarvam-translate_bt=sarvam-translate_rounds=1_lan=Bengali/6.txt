Counterfactual risk reduction is a technique aimed at learning from a bandit environment by correcting biases in the creation of logged examples. Although consistent, the paper shows that it suffers from an overfitting problem where a hypothesis class can overfit based on the generation probability of an example. The authors propose a solution to this problem by using a multiplicative parameter that maintains consistency while limiting variance. Experimental results show that the multiplicative (or normalized) counterfactual technique performs better than the non-normalized technique.

The paper presents a technique for learning self-normalized estimators for counterfactual learning in a logged bandit environment. It presents an idea to combat a form of overfitting where the hypothesis may overfit due to strong support for logged data. It is a good paper and should be adopted, although I am not entirely sure.
