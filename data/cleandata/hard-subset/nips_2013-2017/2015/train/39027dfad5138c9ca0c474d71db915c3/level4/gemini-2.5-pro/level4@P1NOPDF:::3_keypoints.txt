- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (e.g., ad ranking) and identifies overfitting in Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM).
- Proposes multiplicative control variates / a self-normalizing modification to the risk estimator.
- Modified estimator is biased but empirically more stable and yields significant performance gains in the authors' experiments.
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and of high quality.
- Work is follow-up to prior paper but introduces a novel self-normalizing risk estimator.
- Needs deeper analysis of optimization of the training objective (briefly discussed around line 415).
- Experimental evaluation should be broadened beyond prior applications and include more/different datasets.
- Recommend adding real-world dataset results to strengthen practical claims.
- Plots and tables should report variance measures (error bars or standard deviations) to assess stability and statistical significance.
