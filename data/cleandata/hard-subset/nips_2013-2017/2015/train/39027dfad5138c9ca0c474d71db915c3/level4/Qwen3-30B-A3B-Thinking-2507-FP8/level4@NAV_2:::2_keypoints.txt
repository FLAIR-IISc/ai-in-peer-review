- Identifies a previously overlooked issue—“propensity overfitting”—with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Proposes a self-normalized counterfactual risk estimator that avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm based on counterfactual risk minimization using the self-normalized estimator.  
- Provides theoretical justification for the estimator, including Theorem 2 proving strong consistency.  
- Shows empirically that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms conventional POEM across multiple multi-label classification datasets and training sizes.  
- Connects and builds on prior Counterfactual Risk Minimization work (Swaminathan & Joachims, ICML 2015) while distinguishing the specific propensity overfitting problem.  
- Argues that prior work focused on variance reduction (e.g., importance weight clipping) and did not address propensity overfitting.  
- Strength: Novel identification of propensity overfitting with solid theoretical analysis and clear proofs.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation demonstrating consistent improvements and practical relevance (e.g., ads, recommendations).  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured writing that makes contributions accessible.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of computational trade-offs of the self-normalized estimator versus the conventional approach.  
- Weakness: Insufficient exploration of when the self-normalized estimator yields the largest gains and limited discussion of broader theoretical connections.  
- Acceptance arguments: Solves a previously unrecognized problem, offers rigorous theory, strong empirical gains, clear exposition, and practical significance.  
- Rejection arguments: Lacks comprehensive computational cost analysis and deeper exploration of the approach’s limitations.
