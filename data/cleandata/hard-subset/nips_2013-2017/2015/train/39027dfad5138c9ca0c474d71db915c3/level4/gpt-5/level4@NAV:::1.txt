Given observations drawn under one hypothesis, we may wish to evaluate another. When the hypotheses are stochastic (e.g., conditional distributions), one can estimate the loss for the target hypothesis by reweighting samples, as noted in the equation between (1) and (2).

The paper appears to tackle a problem related to—but not identical with—the classic high-variance issue in importance weights. The authors term their focus “propensity overfitting.” The examples suggest this arises under insufficient exploration: for a given x, we observe very few—or possibly no—y consistent with the target hypothesis. Because a similar rationale underlies the large-variance phenomenon, it would be helpful to clarify the precise relationship between these two issues in more depth.

I didn’t have time to verify technical details or assess novelty, but the core idea seems reasonable.
