- Problem: Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback under CRM and failure of IPS-based ERM over rich hypothesis classes.
- Failure mode: Identifies "propensity overfitting" where ERM manipulates importance weights instead of modeling low loss, causing biased empirical risks and non-equivariance under loss shifts.
- Proposed fix: Introduces a self-normalized (multiplicative control variate) importance-weighted risk estimator that enforces Ŝ(h)≈1.
- Estimator properties: Self-normalized estimator is bounded, equivariant to loss translations, and strongly consistent.
- Algorithm: Proposes Norm-POEM — CRM instantiated with the self-normalized estimator for exponential-family policies plus a variance-regularizer using an approximate variance estimate.
- Experiments: On multi-label datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL) Norm-POEM shows consistent gains over POEM, improved stability under loss translations, better Ŝ(h) diagnostics, and competitive runtime.
- Relation to prior work: Connects to self-normalized importance sampling, importance weighting for covariate shift, off-policy evaluation, clipping/DR estimators, and CRM literature.
- Novelty: Main novelty is articulating propensity overfitting as distinct from variance blow-up and integrating self-normalized IS into the CRM learning objective.
- Quality of analysis: Examples clearly illustrate the failure mode; approach aligns with Monte Carlo theory though full proof checks were not completed by reviewer.
- Theoretical gap: Paper should more explicitly compare propensity overfitting to classic large-variance issues and clarify how self-normalization prevents ERM exploiting Ŝ(h).
- Clarity: Generally well written; suggestions to clarify the Example 1 bound and conditions for the variance approximation (Equation 8).
- Technical caution: Variance estimate relies on standard ratio-estimator assumptions (finite second moment, possible need for clipping) and Normal approximation conditions should be stated.
- Empirical suggestions: Report distributions or confidence intervals for Ŝ(ĥ) across runs and state whether hyperparameters (M, λ) were tuned identically for POEM and Norm-POEM.
- Relation to practical baselines: Should qualify statement about "unbiased" IPS in prior work given widespread use of clipping/truncation and doubly robust estimators.
- Pros: Clear identification of a real failure mode, principled self-normalized fix, solid empirical robustness, practical algorithm with released code.
- Cons: Limited theoretical comparison to variance-based remedies, need for clearer variance-approximation conditions, and some unverified derivational details due to reviewer time constraints.
- Recommendation: Accept — novel, practically significant contribution with sound motivation, theory alignment, and convincing empirical evidence.
