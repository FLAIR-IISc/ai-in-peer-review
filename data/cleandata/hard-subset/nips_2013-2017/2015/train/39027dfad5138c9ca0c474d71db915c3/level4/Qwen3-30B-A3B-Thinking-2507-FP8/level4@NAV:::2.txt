

The commentary and notations regarding the main equation (2) appear incorrect or misleading:  
An expectation is defined as \(\sum f(x) p(x)\), where \(f(x)\) is the expected quantity and \(p(x)\) is the probability measure—these are distinct entities. Equation (2) does not represent an expected quantity, rendering the subsequent claim (page 2, second paragraph) that it exhibits "an anomaly" due to lack of translation invariance technically erroneous. Consequently, Example 1, which relies on this flawed premise, fails to hold.  

Practically, the estimator in (2) is highly sensitive to its denominator \(\pi\), which may be zero if the sample was absent in the prior distribution. The modified estimator in (7) is preferable in principle, as its double denominator mitigates the impact of \(\pi\). I sincerely hope the authors will revise this commentary should the paper be accepted.  

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across all datasets and achieves results comparable to a CRF using full multi-label supervision. While the paper positions itself within "counterfactual learning"—a specialization of multi-label learning where only bandit feedback (one class label per sample) is available—the core contribution is specifically an improvement to the POEM estimator introduced in reference [1] (ICML 2015). Overall, the paper is convincing and suitable for publication, pending the author’s attention to the remarks below.
