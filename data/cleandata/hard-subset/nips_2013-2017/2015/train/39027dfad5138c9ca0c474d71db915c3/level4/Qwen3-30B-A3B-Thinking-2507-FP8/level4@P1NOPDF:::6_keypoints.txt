- CRM corrects for selection bias in learning from logged bandit data.  
- The paper identifies a critical overfitting issue: hypothesis classes can overfit to examples with high logging probability, harming generalization.  
- Authors propose a multiplicative normalization parameter that bounds variance while preserving consistency.  
- Normalized CRM effectively mitigates the overfitting problem without sacrificing theoretical guarantees.  
- Experimental results show normalized CRM consistently outperforms non-normalized CRM across multiple datasets.  
- The paper formally diagnoses the overfitting mechanism and offers a simple, effective remedy.  
- Technical arguments are sound, experimental design is rigorous, and results are well-supported.  
- Writing is generally clear but some sections need tighter phrasing.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor reservations about scalability analysis for extremely large-scale settings.  
- Practical takeaway: the proposed normalization is a valuable enhancement that should be adopted in CRM implementations.
