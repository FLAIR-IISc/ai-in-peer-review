- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback and identifies "propensity overfitting" as a failure mode of the IPS estimator.  
- Propensity overfitting: high-capacity models exploit propensity weights instead of minimizing true loss, causing biased risk estimates and poor generalization.  
- Proposes replacing IPS with a self-normalized importance sampling estimator and introduces the Norm-POEM learning algorithm.  
- The self-normalized estimator is provably robust to the identified issue, equivariant to loss shifts, and bounded by observed loss range.  
- Empirical results on multi-label tasks show Norm-POEM outperforms the IPS-based predecessor and is more robust (Ŝ(h) stays near 1).  
- Authors provide code, improving reproducibility and adoption.  
- Strength: Clearly identifies and well-motivates a subtle, important problem with intuitive examples.  
- Strength: Proposed solution is elegant, principled, and directly targets the diagnosed failure mode.  
- Strength: Experimental evaluation is convincing and supports claims of better generalization and robustness.  
- Weakness: Needs clearer discussion of relationship between propensity overfitting and the well-known high-variance problem in importance sampling.  
- Weakness: Overstates novelty relative to prior work—should acknowledge existing biased, variance-reduction variants (clipping/thresholding).  
- Weakness/clarification: Example 1’s derivation (R̂(hoverfit) ≤ −k) needs more detail—does it assume all sampled losses δi = −1?  
- Overall: Solid, well-executed contribution with strong empirical support; reviewer recommends acceptance.
