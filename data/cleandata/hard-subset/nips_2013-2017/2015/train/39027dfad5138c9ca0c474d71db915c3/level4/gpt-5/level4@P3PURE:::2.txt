The commentary and notation surrounding the main equation (2) appear incorrect or at least misleading. An expectation has the form ∑ f(x) p(x), where the expected quantity is the integrand f(x), and p(x) is the probability measure. In equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity. Consequently, the statement on page 2 (second paragraph) that (2) exhibits an “anomaly” because it is not invariant to translations of the loss is technically incorrect. Example 1 then builds on this mistaken premise—which, unsurprisingly, does not hold.

In practical terms, the estimator in (2) is overly sensitive to its denominator, π, which can be zero if the sample has never been observed under the prior. The modified estimator in (7) is preferable in principle because the influence of π is mollified through the double denominator.

I sincerely hope the authors will revise their commentary accordingly should the paper be accepted.

The experimental results show that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across all datasets and performs not far from a CRF trained with full multi-label information. The paper addresses “counterfactual learning,” a specialization of multi-label learning: whereas standard multi-label learning provides a 0/1 label for each class, here only a single class label is observed (bandit feedback). However, this serves mainly as a justification preamble. In practice, the paper’s concrete goal is to improve the POEM estimator introduced in [1] (ICML 2015). Overall, the paper is convincing and merits publication, with the remarks above to be addressed (and further details in the Comments to authors).
