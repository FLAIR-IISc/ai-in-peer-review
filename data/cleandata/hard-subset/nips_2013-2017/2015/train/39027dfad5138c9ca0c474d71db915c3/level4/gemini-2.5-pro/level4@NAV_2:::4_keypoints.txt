- Identifies "propensity overfitting": IPS can be exploited to produce arbitrarily low estimated risk rather than fitting losses.  
- Builds on CRM (Swaminathan & Joachims 2015) which uses an unbiased IPS estimator.  
- Proposes a self-normalized IPS estimator framed as multiplicative control variates, yielding the Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Theoretically principled: fixes anomalies of standard IPS (non-equivariance to loss translations, unboundedness).  
- Empirically strong: Norm-POEM consistently and substantially outperforms POEM across multiple multi-label datasets and data sizes.  
- Experiments validate the central claim: Norm-POEM avoids biased propensity weight sums and is robust to loss translations.  
- High impact: relevant to recommender systems, ad placement, and search—improves robustness and generalization in BLBF.  
- Weakness: Problem setup treats feedback δ(x,y) as deterministic; should consider stochastic/noisy feedback and frame risk accordingly.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether Norm-POEM requires losses rescaled to [-1,0] as in CRM or supports general loss ranges; experiments use translated (positive) losses.  
- Minor: The estimator's non-equivariance under translation was noted previously in POEM and could be highlighted.  
- Minor: Check correctness of argmin/argmax usage in objective functions.  
- Minor: Ensure notational consistency (e.g., empirical estimates denoted with hats).
