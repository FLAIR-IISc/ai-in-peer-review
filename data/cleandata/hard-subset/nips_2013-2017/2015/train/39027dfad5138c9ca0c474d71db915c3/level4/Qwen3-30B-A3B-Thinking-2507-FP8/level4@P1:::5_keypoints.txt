- Identifies a critical flaw ("propensity overfitting") in conventional inverse-propensity counterfactual risk estimators for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Shows propensity overfitting arises from unbounded variance in importance weights and can bias risk estimates arbitrarily across hypothesis spaces.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (Equation 7) using multiplicative control variates that is equivariant under loss translation and bounded within the loss range.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, which replaces the conventional estimator in the CRM objective to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Provides a concrete counterexample (Example 1) that rigorously characterizes propensity overfitting.
- Proves consistency of the self-normalized estimator (Theorem 2) and argues the denominator’s convergence ensures estimator consistency.
- Empirical results on four multi-label datasets show consistent improvements over POEM and avoid propensity overfitting (Ŝ(ĥ) ≈ 1).
- Demonstrates improvements are due to mitigating propensity overfitting rather than hyperparameter tuning (Table 2).
- Shows Norm-POEM converges faster in iterations despite higher per-iteration cost, with computational analysis addressing scalability concerns.
- Compares favorably to thresholded and additive alternatives (e.g., R̂_M, doubly robust estimators), with a novel application of self-normalized importance sampling to BLBF.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and structured; figures and tables effectively communicate results.
- Minor gap: lacks theoretical analysis of the variance estimator’s convergence rate.
- Notes a small scalability caveat (e.g., extremely large output spaces Y) but treats it as minor.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, empirically validated contribution with high practical significance and a strong recommendation for acceptance.
