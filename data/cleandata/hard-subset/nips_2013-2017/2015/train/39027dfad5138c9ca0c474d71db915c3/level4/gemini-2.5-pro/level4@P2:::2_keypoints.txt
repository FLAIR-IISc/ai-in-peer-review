- Summary: Identifies "Propensity Overfitting" in Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback and proposes using a self-normalized IPS estimator and Norm-POEM to mitigate it.  
- Key insight: Standard IPS can be exploited by high-capacity models, producing biased risk estimates and poor generalization.  
- Proposed solution: Replace unbiased IPS with a self-normalized estimator that is equivariant to loss translations and bounded, avoiding the identified anomalies.  
- Algorithm: Introduces Norm-POEM, adapting CRM to use self-normalized risk and a variance estimate.  
- Empirical strength: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple multi-label datasets and approaches fully supervised CRF performance.  
- Problem significance: Learning from logged bandit data is important for practical interactive systems, and estimator instability is a central challenge.  
- Diagnosis & presentation: Clear examples demonstrate how conventional IPS can fail and motivate the self-normalized approach.  
- Main criticism: Framing the unbiased IPS’s lack of shift-invariance as an "anomaly" is potentially misleading and mathematically unfounded.  
- Suggested reframing: Emphasize IPS’s high-variance and sensitivity to small propensities as the fundamental issue, with self-normalization reducing extreme importance weights.  
- Overall assessment: Well-written, convincing empirical results, solid contribution despite minor theoretical framing issues; recommend acceptance.
