- Identifies a new failure mode called "propensity overfitting" in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF.  
- Shows the standard unbiased ERM estimator can be arbitrarily biased for complex hypothesis spaces, even with bounded losses.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates and introduces the Norm-POEM algorithm for structured outputs.  
- Theoretical analysis demonstrates the standard estimator is non-equivariant, enabling propensity overfitting.  
- Proves the self-normalized estimator is equivariant, bounded within the loss range, and strongly consistent, resolving the anomalies.  
- Empirical evaluation (Supervised 7→Bandit on multi-label datasets) shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets and sample sizes.  
- Table 2 evidence: Norm-POEM’s propensity weight sum Ŝ(h) is near 1 while POEM’s is severely biased.  
- Norm-POEM is robust to loss translations; POEM’s performance can degrade catastrophically (e.g., TMC dataset).  
- Norm-POEM converges faster because bounded risk estimates prevent extreme weight magnitudes.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly defining the problem, analysis, solution, and validation.  
- Related work is well contextualized, acknowledging connections to control variates and self-normalized importance sampling.  
- Weakness: comparison to other variance-reduction methods (e.g., doubly robust estimators) could be deeper.  
- Weakness: discussion of computational complexity for the self-normalized variance estimator is brief despite noting efficient convergence.  
- Significance: high practical and theoretical impact for BLBF and interactive systems; recommends acceptance.
