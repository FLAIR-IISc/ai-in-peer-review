- CRM corrects selection bias in logged bandit data but can overfit by exploiting logging probabilities.  
- Identified overfitting mechanism: hypotheses overweight logged examples with high logging probability instead of learning true rewards.  
- Authors propose normalized CRM with a multiplicative parameter to bound estimator variance while preserving consistency.  
- Normalization explicitly accounts for the logging distribution, preventing disproportionate favoring of certain logged examples.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the normalized estimator is sound and superior to standard CRM.  
- Empirical evaluations across multiple bandit datasets consistently favor normalized CRM over non-normalized CRM.  
- Results show significant improvements in accuracy and robustness, especially with sparse logging or skewed action distributions.  
- Experimental design is thorough, including ablation studies isolating the effect of normalization and comparisons to relevant baselines.  
- Contribution is novel, practically significant, and builds cleanly on existing CRM frameworks.  
- Paper is well-written, technically solid, and immediately applicable to real-world logged bandit settings (e.g., recommendation, advertising).  
- Minor concern: limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity in experiments.  
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS) due to strong theoretical backing and compelling empirical validation.
