- Counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) learns from biased logged bandit data and is consistent but can overfit.  
- Overfitting occurs because hypotheses can exploit the probability of generating specific logged examples.  
- Authors introduce a multiplicative (self-normalizing) parameter to bound variance while preserving consistency.  
- Normalized (multiplicative) counterfactual estimator empirically outperforms the non-normalized version.  
- Paper frames the solution as learning a self-normalized estimator to prevent hypothesis overconfidence on logged data.  
- Reviewer views the paper as strong and recommends acceptance despite some reservations.
