- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and propensity overfitting when using unbiased inverse propensity estimators.  
- Proposes a self-normalized (multiplicative control variate) estimator that is biased but equivariant, bounded, and strongly consistent.  
- Integrates the estimator into Norm-POEM for structured output prediction with exponential-family linear rules.  
- Derives an approximate variance estimator for the self-normalized risk and applies CRM with variance regularization.  
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on several multi-label datasets and is robust to loss translations.  
- Shows favorable runtime behavior and that variance regularization remains useful with the self-normalized estimator.  
- Connects to prior work on importance sampling, self-normalized IS, variance reduction, and off-policy evaluation; extends CRM by replacing the risk estimator.  
- Technically solid and well-motivated; theoretical properties and motivation are clearly articulated.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of the optimization procedure (convergence behavior, iteration counts, L-BFGS vs stochastic methods).  
- Needs sensitivity analysis for clipping parameter M and variance regularization λ and reporting of variability across runs.  
- Experimental scope is narrow and largely mirrors prior work; real-world logged-data experiments are missing.  
- Requests: report standard errors/confidence intervals, expand optimization discussion, include ablations on M and λ, add real-world dataset(s), and diagnostic plots of Ŝ(ĥ) distributions.  
- Minor issues: fix small typos (e.g., “Conterfactual” → “Counterfactual”).
