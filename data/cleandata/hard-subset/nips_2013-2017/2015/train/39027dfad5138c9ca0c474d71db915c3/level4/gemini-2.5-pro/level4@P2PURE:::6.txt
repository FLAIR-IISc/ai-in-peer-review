This paper addresses an overfitting problem within counterfactual risk minimization, a technique for learning from logged bandit data by correcting for the logging policy's bias. The authors demonstrate that while this method is consistent, it is susceptible to high variance, as a hypothesis can overfit by exploiting the generation probabilities of the logged examples. To remedy this, they introduce a self-normalized estimator with a multiplicative parameter that effectively bounds the variance while preserving consistency. Experimental results confirm that this normalized counterfactual technique outperforms the standard, non-normalized version.

The paper presents a well-motivated solution to a subtle but important overfitting problem in counterfactual learning, specifically how a model can become over-reliant on the support of the logged data. This is a good paper that should be accepted, although I am not absolutely certain of this recommendation.
