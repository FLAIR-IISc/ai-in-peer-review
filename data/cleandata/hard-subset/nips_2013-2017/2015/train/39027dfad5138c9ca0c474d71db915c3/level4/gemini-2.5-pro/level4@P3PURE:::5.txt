This paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that avoids the propensity overfitting problem. Building on the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), it presents a significantly improved algorithm.

**Quality:** The quality of the research is high. The proposed extensions result in significant improvements to the training process of the original POEM algorithm.

**Clarity:** For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

**Originality:** The paper presents a novel reformulation of the original POEM algorithm for the batch learning from bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. The contributions are significantly original.

**Significance:** The results are very interesting and warrant further investigation. It is particularly impressive that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM, despite incorporating additional regularization mechanisms. This algorithm has the potential for widespread adoption in the near future.

This is a good paper. It is clear and reads well.
