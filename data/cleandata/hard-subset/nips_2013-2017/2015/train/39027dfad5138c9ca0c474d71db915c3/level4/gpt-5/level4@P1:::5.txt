Summary
This paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies a key failure mode of the conventional inverse-propensity (IPS) counterfactual risk estimator when used for empirical risk minimization (ERM): propensity overfitting. The authors propose replacing the standard estimator with a self-normalized (SN) estimator that uses multiplicative control variates. The SN estimator is bounded and equivariant to translations of the loss, and is strongly consistent. Building on the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), they instantiate a new algorithm, Norm-POEM, for structured prediction with linear-exponential models. They also provide an approximate variance estimator for the SN ratio estimator and optimize the resulting (non-convex) objective with L-BFGS.

Empirically, using the supervised-to-bandit methodology, they evaluate on multi-label datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL). Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across runs and sample sizes, appears robust to translations of the loss, and achieves faster optimization in practice despite the added normalization and variance terms. Diagnostics based on the sum of normalized weights Ŝ(h) show that Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting (Ŝ ≈ 1), whereas POEM can severely deviate, leading to poor generalization.

Relation to prior work
The paper builds directly on CRM and POEM (Swaminathan & Joachims, 2015), and connects to importance sampling, propensity score methods, and variance reduction via control variates. The proposed SN estimator has a long history in Monte Carlo and importance sampling; here it is carefully adapted to the BLBF learning setting to resolve the degeneracies of IPS within ERM.

Evaluation

- Quality: Above average. The paper clearly motivates the pathology of IPS in ERM (propensity overfitting) and argues for the SN estimator as a principled fix. Theoretical properties are appropriate for the setting (equivariance, boundedness, strong consistency), and the variance estimator is a reasonable approximation for a ratio estimator. The empirical evaluation follows established supervised-to-bandit protocols and shows consistent gains and improved robustness. The extensions to POEM yield significant improvements in training and generalization.

- Clarity: For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow. The presentation of propensity overfitting and the rationale for self-normalization are well explained, and the experimental setup is described in sufficient detail.

- Originality: The paper provides a significantly new formulation of CRM/POEM for BLBF by replacing the IPS objective with a self-normalized estimator and integrating a corresponding variance regularizer. While the SN idea itself is known in Monte Carlo, applying it in this way to counterfactual learning to prevent propensity overfitting, and deriving the Norm-POEM algorithm, are original contributions in this context.

- Significance: The results are interesting and point to a robust improvement over POEM. It is notable that the proposed approach is faster in practice than POEM despite the additional regularization and normalization, and that it remains stable under loss translations. This method is likely to see widespread use in BLBF applications and warrants further research.

Pros
- Identifies and clearly explains a practical and important failure mode (propensity overfitting) of IPS-based ERM in BLBF.
- Proposes a principled remedy via a self-normalized estimator with strong theoretical properties (boundedness, equivariance, consistency).
- Derives a concrete learning algorithm (Norm-POEM) and an approximate variance estimator consistent with CRM.
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements over POEM across datasets and training sizes, with evidence of better robustness and optimization behavior.
- Practical implications: potentially faster training and more stable learning in real BLBF systems.

Cons
- None substantive noted.

Overall assessment
This is a good paper. It is clear, well-motivated, and reads well. The proposed approach offers a significantly better algorithmic formulation than POEM for BLBF, with strong empirical performance and sound theoretical underpinnings.
