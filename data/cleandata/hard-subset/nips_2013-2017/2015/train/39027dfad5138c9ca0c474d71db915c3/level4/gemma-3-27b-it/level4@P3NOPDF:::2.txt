This paper introduces Norm-POEM, an improvement to the POEM estimator presented in [1] (ICML 2015), within the framework of “counterfactual learning,” which the authors position as a specialization of multi-label learning. The core aim of the work is to address limitations of the original POEM estimator, and the experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across several datasets, achieving performance close to a CRF utilizing full multi-label information. Overall, I find the paper convincing and believe it represents a worthwhile contribution, contingent on addressing the comments detailed below.

My primary concern lies with the mathematical commentary surrounding the main equation (2). The authors’ interpretation of expectation appears inaccurate, or at least misleading. An expectation is fundamentally of the form  ∑ f(x) p(x), where p(x) represents the probability measure and f(x) is the quantity being expected. Equation (2) clearly involves a loss function, which is not the expected quantity itself but *what is being minimized* via expectation. Consequently, the claim on page 2, second paragraph, that equation (2) exhibits an “anomaly” due to a lack of invariance to translation of the loss is technically incorrect. Example 1, presented subsequent to this claim, is built upon this flawed premise and therefore its conclusions are questionable.

Furthermore, the original POEM estimator, as expressed in equation (2), is vulnerable to issues arising from the denominator, π, which represents the prior probability. If a sample has never been observed under the prior, π can be zero, leading to instability. The modified estimator in equation (7) appears to mitigate this problem by introducing a double denominator, effectively smoothing the influence of π. This is a positive aspect of the proposed Norm-POEM.

The authors effectively contextualize their work by drawing a parallel to multi-label learning. While acknowledging the difference – bandit feedback providing a single label versus the 0/1 labels common in multi-label learning – they appropriately frame counterfactual learning as a specific instance of the broader multi-label problem. However, it's important to recognize that this framing largely serves as justification for the problem setting and doesn't significantly drive the technical contributions. The primary focus and tangible achievement of this paper are demonstrably the improvements to the POEM estimator.

The experimental results are well-presented and clearly demonstrate the superiority of Norm-POEM over the original POEM. The comparison to a CRF provides a useful benchmark, highlighting the practical effectiveness of the proposed approach. 

I strongly encourage the authors to revisit the mathematical commentary surrounding equation (2) and revise it for accuracy if the paper is accepted. A more precise explanation would significantly enhance the clarity and rigor of the presentation.



**Comments to Authors:**

*   **Equation (2) and related commentary:** Please carefully review the explanation of expectation and the discussion of invariance in the vicinity of equation (2) and on page 2. The current formulation is misleading and detracts from the overall quality of the paper.
*   **Prior Probability (π):** While the improvement in stability due to the modified estimator in (7) is noted, a more detailed discussion of the sensitivity of the original estimator to the prior, and the impact of the normalization in (7), would be valuable.
*   **Clarify contribution:** While the connection to multi-label learning is interesting, emphasize that the core contribution of the paper is the improvement of the POEM estimator through Norm-POEM.
*   **Consider Ablation Study:** While the results are positive, an ablation study demonstrating the impact of the specific normalization used in Norm-POEM could further strengthen the analysis.
