Summary
This paper addresses the important problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, with applications in areas such as ad ranking. The authors identify an overfitting issue within the recently introduced Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) framework and propose a solution utilizing multiplicative control variates, acknowledging this introduces a biased estimator.

Quality
This is a high-quality and well-motivated work. As a continuation of [1], I expected to see more extensive experimentation, and a deeper analysis of the training objective’s optimization – beyond the details provided on line 415.

Clarity
The presentation is clear and the paper is well-written.

Originality
While building upon [1], the modification of the risk estimator to be self-normalizing appears to significantly impact performance, as demonstrated by the experimental results. 

Significance
Learning from logged data is a relevant and practical problem; however, the extent to which the observed experimental improvements generalize to real-world applications remains unclear.

Various Remarks
Line 365: Given that multiple repetitions are already performed, please include a measure of variance in the results. The paper correctly identifies a limitation of the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and offers a compelling solution. Overall, this is a solid, well-written, and relevant contribution with novel aspects. I would have benefited from more extensive experiments, and particularly, applications beyond those presented in [1], including some evaluations on actual real-world datasets.
