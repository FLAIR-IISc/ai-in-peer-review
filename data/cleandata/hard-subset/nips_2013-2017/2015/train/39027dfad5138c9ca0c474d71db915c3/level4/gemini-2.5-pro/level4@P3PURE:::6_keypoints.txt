- Identifies an overfitting problem in counterfactual risk minimization: hypotheses can be unduly influenced by examples with low generation probabilities.
- Notes that the standard CRM estimator is consistent but prone to overfitting due to high-variance importance weights.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator that introduces a multiplicative parameter to bound the variance of the risk estimate.
- Emphasizes that the normalization preserves estimator consistency while reducing variance.
- Experimental results validate that normalized CRM consistently outperforms the non-normalized version.
- Paper is well-motivated, presents an elegant solution, and is empirically verified.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, though with less than absolute confidence.
