- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies "propensity overfitting," where models exploit importance weights to drive down empirical risk.  
- Proposes replacing the unbiased IPS estimator with a self-normalized (ratio) estimator to prevent weight-manipulation-based overfitting.  
- Integrates the self-normalized estimator into a new CRM algorithm, Norm-POEM, for structured output prediction with linear models.  
- The self-normalized estimator is theoretically motivated: equivariant to loss translations and bounded by observed loss range, preventing degenerate estimates.  
- Norm-POEM is positioned as a direct improvement over POEM by swapping the core risk estimator.  
- Empirical evaluation shows Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM on multi-label benchmarks.  
- Results indicate Norm-POEM is robust to loss transformations and approaches fully supervised CRF performance ("skyline").  
- Paper is well-written and organized; Example 1 effectively illustrates propensity overfitting.  
- Critique: framing the IPS estimator's lack of equivariance as an "anomaly" is debatable—finite-sample nonlinearity is expected given importance weighting.  
- Suggestion: soften the framing and emphasize that large hypothesis spaces allow exploitation of this property, rather than treating it as a technical flaw.  
- Alternative framing: highlight high variance from small propensities and note that the self-normalized estimator mitigates this by normalizing weights.  
- Overall assessment: solid, novel, and empirically convincing contribution to counterfactual learning; recommendation for acceptance.
