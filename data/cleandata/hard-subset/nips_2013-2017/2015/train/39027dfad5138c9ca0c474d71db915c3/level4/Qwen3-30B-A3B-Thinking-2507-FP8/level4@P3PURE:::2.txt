

The commentary surrounding Equation (2) contains a fundamental technical error that undermines key claims. An expectation is defined as $\mathbb{E}[f(x)] = \sum f(x) p(x)$, where $f(x)$ (the quantity being averaged) is distinct from $p(x)$ (the probability measure). Equation (2) explicitly defines a loss function, which is clearly not the expected quantity. Consequently, the assertion on page 2, second paragraph, that Equation (2) exhibits an "anomaly" due to lack of translation invariance is technically flawed. The subsequent Example 1 relies on this incorrect premise and thus fails to hold.  

Practically, the estimator in Equation (2) is highly sensitive to its denominator $\pi_i$, which may be zero if the sample was unobserved in the prior distribution. The modified estimator in Equation (7) addresses this issue by mitigating the influence of $\pi_i$ through its double denominator structure. The authors should correct this mischaracterization in the revised manuscript.  

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across all datasets and achieves performance near that of a CRF using full multi-label information. While the paper positions its work as addressing "counterfactual learning"—a specialization of multi-label learning where only a single class label (bandit feedback) is observed—the core contribution is precisely the improvement of the POEM estimator introduced by reference [1] at ICML 2015. The paper is convincing and merits publication, but requires the technical corrections noted above.
