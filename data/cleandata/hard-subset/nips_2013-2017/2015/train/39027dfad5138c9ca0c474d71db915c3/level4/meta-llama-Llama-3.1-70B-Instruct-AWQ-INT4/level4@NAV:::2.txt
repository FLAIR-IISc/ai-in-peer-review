The discussion surrounding equation (2) appears to be incorrect or misleading: 
an expectation is typically expressed as \sum f(x) p(x), where f(x) represents the expected quantity, distinct from the probability measure p(x).
The loss in equation (2) is clearly not the expected quantity, rendering the subsequent comment on page 2 (second paragraph) regarding the "anomaly" due to non-invariance to loss translation technically inaccurate. Consequently, Example 1, which relies on this incorrect assumption, does not hold.
In practical terms, the estimator in equation (2) is sensitive to its denominator, pi, which can be 0 if the sample has not been observed in the prior. In contrast, the modified estimator in equation (7) appears to be an improvement, as the effects of pi are mitigated by the double denominator.
I hope the authors will revise their discussion if the paper is accepted.
The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across all datasets and is comparable to a CRF using full multi-label information. This paper proposes an approach to "counterfactual learning," a specialized form of multi-label learning where, unlike traditional multi-label learning, only a single class label (i.e., bandit feedback) is provided for each sample.
In essence, the primary objective of this paper is to enhance the POEM estimator, recently introduced in reference [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, I find the paper convincing and worthy of publication, although I have raised some points for consideration in the Comments to authors field.
