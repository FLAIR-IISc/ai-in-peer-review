

Summary
This paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a critically relevant problem in applications such as ad ranking. It identifies an "overfitting" issue in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) framework and proposes a solution using multiplicative control variates, resulting in a biased risk estimator.

Quality
The work is high-quality and well motivated. As a follow-up to [1], I would have expected additional experiments and deeper analysis regarding the optimization of the training objective (beyond the brief discussion at line 415).

Clarity
The presentation is clear and the paper is well written.

Originality
This constitutes follow-up work on [1], but the modification of the risk estimator to be self-normalizing appears to yield substantial empirical improvements, which is the core novelty.

Significance
While the problem of learning from logged data is highly relevant, it remains unclear how the substantial experimental improvements translate to broader real-world applications.

Specific Comments
- Line 365: If multiple repetitions are run, please report a measure of variance (e.g., standard deviation) alongside means.
- The paper identifies a problem in the CRM principle and introduces a solution. Overall, the work is solid, well written, and relevant. However, additional experiments—particularly including real-world applications beyond those in [1]—would strengthen the contribution.
