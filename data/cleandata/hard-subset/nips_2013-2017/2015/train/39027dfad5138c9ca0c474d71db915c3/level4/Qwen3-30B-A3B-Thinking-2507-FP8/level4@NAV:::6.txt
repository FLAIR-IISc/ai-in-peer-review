

Counterfactual risk minimization is a technique for learning in bandit settings from logged data, addressing bias in the logged examples' generation process. While consistent, the paper identifies an overfitting issue wherein a hypothesis class may overfit to the generation probabilities of examples. The authors propose a solution using a multiplicative parameter that bounds the variance while preserving consistency. Experimental results demonstrate that the multiplicative (or normalized) counterfactual approach outperforms the non-normalized variant.

The paper introduces a method for learning self-normalized estimators in counterfactual learning under logged bandit settings, addressing a specific overfitting scenario where hypotheses overfit by exhibiting stronger alignment with logged data. This is a strong paper deserving of acceptance, though I remain not entirely certain.
