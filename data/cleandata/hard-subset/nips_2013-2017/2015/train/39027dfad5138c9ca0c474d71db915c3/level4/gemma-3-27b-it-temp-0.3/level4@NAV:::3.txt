## Review

**Summary:**
This paper addresses the important problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, with direct relevance to applications like ad ranking. The authors identify an overfitting issue within the recently introduced Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) framework and propose a solution leveraging multiplicative control variates – acknowledging this introduces a biased estimator.

**Quality:**
The work is of high quality and well-motivated. As a continuation of [1], I anticipated a more extensive experimental evaluation and deeper analysis of the training objective’s optimization, beyond the discussion on line 415. 

**Clarity:**
The presentation is clear and the paper is well-written.

**Originality:**
This work builds upon [1] by modifying the risk estimator to achieve self-normalization, a change that demonstrably improves performance in the authors’ experimental setup.

**Significance:**
Learning from logged data remains a significant challenge. However, the practical impact of the observed experimental improvements on broader, real-world applications requires further investigation.

**Various Remarks:**
Line 365: When performing multiple repetitions of an experiment, please include a measure of variance in the results. The paper correctly identifies a limitation of the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and presents a viable solution. Overall, this is a solid, well-written, relevant, and novel contribution. I would have benefited from a more comprehensive experimental analysis, particularly extending beyond the applications presented in [1] to include some evaluation on actual real-world datasets.
