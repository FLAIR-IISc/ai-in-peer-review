Counterfactual risk minimization is a technique for learning in a bandit setting from logged data by correcting for the bias induced by the logging process. While consistent, the paper shows that it can overfit because a hypothesis class may exploit the example-generation probabilities. The authors propose a remedy that introduces a multiplicative normalization parameter to bound variance while preserving consistency. The experimental results indicate that this normalized (multiplicative) counterfactual method outperforms the non-normalized alternative.

The paper presents a technique for learning a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting. It offers ideas for addressing a form of overfitting in which a hypothesis overemphasizes logged examples with stronger support. This is a good paper and should be accepted, although I am not absolutely certain.
