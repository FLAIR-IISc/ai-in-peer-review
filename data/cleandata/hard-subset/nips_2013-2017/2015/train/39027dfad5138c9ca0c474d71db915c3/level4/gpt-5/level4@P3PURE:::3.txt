Summary
The paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant problem, for example in ad ranking. It identifies an overfitting issue in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a solution based on multiplicative control variates, resulting in a biased, self-normalizing estimator.

Quality
High quality and well motivated. As a follow-up to [1], I would have expected additional experiments and more detailed analysis of the optimization of the training objective—beyond what is provided in the paragraph around line 415.

Clarity
The presentation is clear and the paper is well written.

Originality
This is follow-up work on [1], but it modifies the risk estimator to be self-normalizing, which appears to make a substantial difference in the experiments reported by the authors.

Significance
Learning from logged data is an important problem. However, it remains difficult to assess how the substantial experimental improvements demonstrated here will translate to broader real-world applications.

Various remarks
- Line 365: If you already run several repetitions, please include a measure of variance.
- The paper identifies a problem in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization principle and introduces a solution. Overall, the work is solid, well written, relevant, and novel. I would nevertheless have appreciated extended experiments and, in particular, applications beyond those in [1], including some actual real-world applications.
