Counterfactual risk minimization is a technique designed to learn from logged data in a bandit setting by correcting for the bias inherent in the generation of logged examples. While this approach is consistent, the paper highlights its susceptibility to an overfitting problem, where a hypothesis class can overfit based on the generation probability of an example. To address this issue, the authors propose introducing a multiplicative parameter that bounds the variance while maintaining consistency. The experimental results demonstrate that the multiplicative (or normalized) counterfactual technique outperforms its non-normalized counterpart.

The paper presents a technique for learning a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting. It introduces ideas for mitigating a form of overfitting, where the hypothesis can overfit by having stronger support for the logged data. This is a good paper, and I believe it should be accepted, although I am not entirely certain.
