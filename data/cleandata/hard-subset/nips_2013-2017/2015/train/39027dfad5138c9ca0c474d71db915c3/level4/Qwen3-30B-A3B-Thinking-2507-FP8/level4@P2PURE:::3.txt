

Summary
The paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant problem in applications such as ad ranking. It identifies an "overfitting" issue in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a solution using multiplicative control variates, resulting in a biased estimator.

Quality
The work is high-quality and well motivated. As this is follow-up work on [1], I would have expected additional experiments and deeper analysis regarding the optimization of the training objective (beyond the brief discussion on line 415).

Clarity
The presentation is clear and the paper is well written.

Originality
This is follow-up work on [1], but it modifies the risk estimator to be self-normalizing, which appears to yield significant improvements in the experiments conducted by the authors.

Significance
While the problem of learning from logged data is relevant, it is difficult to assess how the substantial experimental improvements translate to broader real-world applicability.

Various Remarks
Line 365: If multiple repetitions have been run, please include a measure of variance. The paper identifies an issue in the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a solution. Overall, the paper is solid, well written, and addresses a relevant problem. However, I would have appreciated extended experiments, particularly additional applications beyond those in [1], including real-world case studies.
