- The notation and commentary around equation (2) are incorrect/misleading: expectations should be of the form ∑ f(x) p(x), and the paper treats the loss incorrectly as the expected quantity.
- The claim that (2) exhibits an “anomaly” because it is not invariant to translations of the loss is technically incorrect.
- Example 1 is built on the mistaken premise about (2) and therefore does not hold.
- The estimator in (2) is overly sensitive to its denominator π, which can be zero if a sample was never observed under the prior.
- The modified estimator in (7) is preferable because its double denominator mollifies the influence of π.
- The reviewer requests the authors revise their commentary on these points if the paper is accepted.
- Experimental results show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across all datasets.
- Norm-POEM performs close to a fully supervised CRF trained with full multi-label information.
- The paper frames the problem as counterfactual learning (bandit feedback) versus standard multi-label learning, but this serves mainly as a preamble.
- The concrete contribution is an improvement of the POEM estimator (ICML 2015), and the paper is convincing and merits publication pending addressing the remarks.
