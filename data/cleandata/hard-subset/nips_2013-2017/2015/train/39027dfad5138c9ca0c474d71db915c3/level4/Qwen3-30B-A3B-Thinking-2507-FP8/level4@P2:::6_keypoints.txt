- Identifies a critical flaw ("propensity overfitting") in CRM for batch learning from logged bandit feedback when applied to complex hypothesis spaces.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator that avoids propensity overfitting.
- Provides theoretical analysis showing conventional unbiased estimators can produce risk estimates arbitrarily lower than true risk, causing poor generalization.
- Demonstrates the self-normalized estimator resolves theoretical anomalies, maintaining equivariance under loss transformations and bounding risk estimates.
- Proves strong consistency of the estimator and derives a principled learning algorithm (Norm-POEM).
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing Norm-POEM to POEM across multiple multi-label LibSVM datasets.
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets, training sizes, and loss transformations.
- Validation shows the self-normalized estimator effectively prevents propensity overfitting (Ŝ(ĥ) close to 1).
- Work is original, identifying an overlooked vulnerability and using multiplicative control variates for a novel self-normalized estimator.
- Builds on prior CRM and importance sampling literature (e.g., Swaminathan & Joachims 2015) while significantly extending it.
- Significance is high for real-world BLBF applications (ads, recommendations) and the paper includes code to facilitate adoption.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, with effective exposition and concrete examples illustrating the problem.
- Theoretical results are well-motivated, contextualized, and connected to empirical findings.
- Minor weakness: limited analysis of the computational cost and complexity of the self-normalized estimator.
- Minor weakness: could better contextualize relations to other variance reduction techniques (e.g., doubly robust estimators).
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, novel contribution that satisfies acceptance criteria; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
