The paper addresses the critical problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant challenge in areas like ad ranking and recommendation systems. It identifies a novel "propensity overfitting" issue within the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle, a recently proposed framework for this setting, and introduces a solution leveraging multiplicative control variates – resulting in a self-normalized, albeit biased, estimator.

This is a high-quality and well-motivated work. As a follow-up to [1], one might expect a more extensive empirical evaluation, alongside deeper analysis of the training objective’s optimization – perhaps beyond the discussion on line 415. 

The presentation is clear and the paper is well-written, demonstrating a strong grasp of the relevant concepts. 

The work builds upon [1], introducing a significant modification to the risk estimator via self-normalization, which demonstrably improves performance in the reported experiments. This appears to be a novel and insightful contribution.

The problem of learning from logged data is undeniably important. However, assessing the generalizability of the substantial experimental improvements to broader, real-world applications remains a challenge.
