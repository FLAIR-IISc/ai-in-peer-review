The paper proposes an improvement to the POEM estimator introduced in [1], framing the work as a contribution to “counterfactual learning,” a specialization of multi-label learning. While the core aim—enhancing POEM—is clearly articulated and the experimental results demonstrate a performance gain, I have significant concerns regarding the theoretical justification presented, specifically surrounding equation (2) and its interpretation.

My primary concern lies with the commentary accompanying equation (2). The authors characterize a perceived “anomaly” related to translation invariance. However, their understanding of the equation's components appears flawed. An expectation, fundamentally, takes the form of a sum over outcomes weighted by their probabilities:  ∑ f(x) p(x). In this context, *f(x)* represents the quantity being estimated, while *p(x)* is the probability measure. Equation (2) clearly estimates a loss, which is *f(x)*, and is not itself an expected quantity. Therefore, the assertion of a lack of translation invariance is technically incorrect. Consequently, Example 1, built upon this initial mischaracterization, does not logically follow. 

From a practical perspective, the original estimator in (2) exhibits sensitivity to its denominator, π<sub>i</sub>. This is problematic because π<sub>i</sub> can evaluate to zero when a sample has not been observed under the prior, leading to instability. The modified estimator presented in (7) offers a potential improvement by mitigating this issue through the introduction of a double denominator, effectively smoothing the influence of π<sub>i</sub>.

The experimental results presented are compelling. Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across all tested datasets and achieves performance close to a conditional random field (CRF) leveraging full multi-label information. This suggests the proposed modification is practically effective. 

Overall, I find the paper convincing and believe it warrants publication, *provided* the authors address the theoretical inaccuracies outlined above. I strongly encourage them to revisit the commentary surrounding equation (2) and correct the misinterpretation of expected values. Clarifying this point will significantly strengthen the paper’s theoretical foundation. Further details and specific suggestions are provided in the “Comments to Authors” section.
