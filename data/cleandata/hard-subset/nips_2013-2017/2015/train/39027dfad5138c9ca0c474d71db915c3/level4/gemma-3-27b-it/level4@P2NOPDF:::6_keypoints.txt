- CRM tackles learning from biased, logged bandit data by correcting for the data-generation bias.  
- The paper identifies a significant overfitting issue: hypotheses can become overly sensitive to logging probabilities, harming generalization.  
- Authors introduce a multiplicative parameter that bounds estimator variance while preserving CRM consistency.  
- Core contribution: a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning to reduce overfitting from excessive confidence in logged data.  
- Normalization of counterfactual estimates yields a more robust, generalizable model.  
- Experiments show the normalized CRM consistently outperforms the standard non-normalized variant.  
- Controlling variance via the multiplicative parameter is crucial for practical performance.  
- Overall: theoretically sound, empirically validated, addresses an important limitation, and recommended for acceptance.
