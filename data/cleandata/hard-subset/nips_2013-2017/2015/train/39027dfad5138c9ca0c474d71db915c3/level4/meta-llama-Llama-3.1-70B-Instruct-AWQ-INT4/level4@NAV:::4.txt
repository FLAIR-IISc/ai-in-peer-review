== Other comments ==
- I believe the setup for "batch learning from logged bandit feedback" in Section 3 (and in [1]) is incomplete. In my opinion, the feedback should be treated as a random variable rather than a deterministic function, as presented on line 110. My reasoning is as follows: Suppose the partial feedback originates from a classification problem, where we are unaware of the labels, and only receive feedback by making a prediction and observing the resulting loss. Assume there is a true classification loss Delta(y',y), and the feedback generation process involves predicting y using h_0, while a "god agent" also labels the input as y' and informs us of the incurred loss Delta(y',y), which corresponds to delta(x,y) for the given y'. However, in classification problems, labeling is often noisy, and there may not be a unique y' assigned to each x. This implies that, elsewhere in the log, we could have the same x as input, the same y played, but with a different delta(x,y) due to the "god agent" assigning a different label. Therefore, I think delta(x,y) should generally be regarded as a random variable (in the classification example above, the mean of this random variable for a fixed x and played y should be the expectation of Delta(y',y) for y' distributed according to p(y'|x), the true noisy labeling distribution).
This may not affect the risk estimator (2), but the setup in (1) with the true risk should be presented with an additional expectation over the randomness of delta to be more general.

- Line 276: You should probably use argmin instead of argmax, as you are minimizing the risk.
