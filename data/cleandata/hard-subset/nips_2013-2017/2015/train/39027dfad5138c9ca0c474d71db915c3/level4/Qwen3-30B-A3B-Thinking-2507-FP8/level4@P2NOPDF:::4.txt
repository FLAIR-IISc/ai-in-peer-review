

- Clarification request (pre-rebuttal): Section 4.1 of [1] states that CRM requires $\delta \in [-1,0]$ (justifying their rescaling of general losses to this interval). For NORM-POEM, equations (8) and (3) similarly rescale $\delta$ to $[-1,0]$; however, Table 1 reports results with $\delta > 0$. Please clarify the justification for this discrepancy in the rebuttal.  

**Other comments:**  
- Section 3 (and [1]) models $\delta(x,y)$ as a deterministic function (line 110), but it should be treated as a random variable. In classification settings with noisy labels, $\delta(x,y) = \Delta(y', y)$ depends on the true label $y'$, which is itself random (e.g., $y' \sim p(y'|x)$). Thus, $\delta(x,y)$ is a random variable whose expectation over $y'$ represents the true loss. The risk in equation (1) should include an expectation over this randomness: $R(h) = \mathbb{E}_{x,y}[\mathbb{E}_{\delta|\mathbf{x},y}[\delta(x,y)] \cdot \frac{h(y|x)}{h_0(y|x)}]$. This generalization does not alter the risk estimator (2), but strengthens the theoretical setup.  

- Line 170–171: The ratio should be $h(y|x)/h_0(y|x)$ (not $h_0(y|x)/h(y|x)$), consistent with [1]’s analysis of nonlinearity.  

- Line 276: Replace "argmax" with "argmin" since the objective minimizes risk.  

- Line 319: Replace bars with hats (e.g., $\hat{\phi}$) to align with prior notation.  

**Post-rebuttal update:**  
The authors’ clarifications resolved the $\delta$-range concern: $\delta > 0$ in Table 1 refers to the *unscaled* loss, while the rescaling to $[-1,0]$ applies to the analysis (equations 3, 8). The setup for $\delta(x,y)$ as a random variable was acknowledged as a valid point; the authors will refine the exposition.  

**Overall assessment:**  
This work provides a meaningful extension of [1] to the NORM-POEM framework. The analysis of "propensity overfitting" and the multiplicative control variate approach are novel and well-motivated. Results demonstrate consistent improvements over POEM on batch logged bandit feedback, a domain of growing significance. The technical contributions are sound, and the experimental validation is thorough.
