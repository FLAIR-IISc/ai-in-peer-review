This paper proposes a novel approach to counterfactual learning from logged bandit feedback, which is a special case of multi-label learning where only the label for one class (bandit feedback) is available. The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in prior works, which they term "propensity overfitting." This issue arises when the capacity of the hypothesis space allows overfitting of the propensity weights, leading to severely biased risk estimates.

The authors propose an alternative estimator, the self-normalized risk estimator, which provably avoids this problem. They derive a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle using the new estimator. The experimental results show that Norm-POEM outperforms the previous state-of-the-art method, POEM, on all datasets and is not far from a Conditional Random Field (CRF) using full multi-label information.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and their proposed solution. The experimental results are convincing, and the comparison with POEM and CRF is helpful in evaluating the performance of Norm-POEM. However, there are a few minor issues with the notation and commentary in the main equation (2).

Specifically, the notation and commentary concerning the expected quantity and probability measure seem incorrect. The loss is not the expected quantity, and the ensuing comment about the estimator's anomaly due to translation of the loss is technically incorrect. The example provided to illustrate this point is based on this incorrect presumption.

Despite this minor issue, the paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their proposed solution. The experimental results are convincing, and the comparison with POEM and CRF is helpful in evaluating the performance of Norm-POEM.

* The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator and propose an alternative estimator that provably avoids this problem.
* The experimental results show that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and is not far from a CRF using full multi-label information.
