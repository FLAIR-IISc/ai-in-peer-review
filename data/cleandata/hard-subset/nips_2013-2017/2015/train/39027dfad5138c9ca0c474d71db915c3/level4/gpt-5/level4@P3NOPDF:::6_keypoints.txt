- Summary: Addresses counterfactual risk minimization from logged bandit feedback, shows IPS overfits via small propensities, and proposes a self-normalized multiplicative estimator with variance control that empirically outperforms IPS.

- Clear motivation: Highlights practical overfitting risk of propensity-weighted ERM in logged bandit settings.

- Principled estimator: Self-normalization reduces variance by normalizing propensity weights and uses a multiplicative variance-control parameter to stabilize learning.

- Theoretical grounding: Claims the procedure preserves consistency while reducing variance-related overfitting.

- Empirical support: Experiments show consistent gains of the normalized approach over the non-normalized baseline.

- Clarity issue: Needs a more explicit presentation of the optimized objective, the exact normalized risk form, and the role of the multiplicative parameter.

- Logging policy assumptions: Should state assumptions (accurate propensities, positivity) clearly and discuss robustness to propensity misspecification.

- Bias–variance trade-off: Requires discussion of finite-sample bias from normalization and guidance on selecting the multiplicative parameter and its sensitivity.

- Experimental scope limited: Would benefit from broader tasks, ablations varying propensity distributions and rare-action frequencies, and sensitivity analyses.

- Relation to prior work: Needs clearer positioning relative to IPS variants (clipped IPS, self-normalized IPS) and variance-regularized objectives to clarify novelty.

- Quality assessment: Important problem, sound and practically relevant solution with empirical-theory alignment.

- Clarity recommendations: Make normalized estimator, unbiasedness/consistency claims, training objective, and logging assumptions explicit.

- Impact: Offers a practical, theoretically motivated improvement useful for counterfactual policy learning and off-policy evaluation.

- Recommendation: Weak accept—meaningful contribution but requires clearer exposition and parameter guidance.

- Minor suggestions: Provide pseudocode/algorithm, tuning guidance for multiplicative parameter, report confidence intervals/statistical tests, and contrast normalization with clipping and other variance-reduction techniques.
