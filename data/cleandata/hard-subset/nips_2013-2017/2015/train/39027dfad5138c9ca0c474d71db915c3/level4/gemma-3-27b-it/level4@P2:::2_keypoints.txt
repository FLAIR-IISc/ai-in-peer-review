- Problem: Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) due to high-variance counterfactual risk estimators.  
- Solution: Proposes a self-normalized estimator and a new algorithm, Norm-POEM.  
- Theory: Provides clear theoretical analysis showing the self-normalized estimator avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Empirics: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and approaches full-information CRF on multi-label datasets.  
- Context: Builds on counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) and ties to causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation.  
- Presentation: Well-written, clear derivation, experiments follow established methodologies, and implementation details provided.  
- Strength: Tackles an important practical issue in BLBF with sound theory and strong empirical support; code available for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Potential confusion in the paper’s commentary on Equation (2) regarding loss-translation invariance.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity, especially the variance-regularization cost.  
- Weakness: No thorough analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity for M and λ.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, theoretically grounded, strong empirical results, clear presentation, reproducible code, and significant contribution to the field.  
- Rejection risks: Possible misinterpretation around Eq(2) and need for more analysis of runtime and hyperparameter robustness.  
- Overall: Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- To authors: Clarify Eq(2) discussion, detail computational cost (variance regularization), analyze sensitivity to M and λ, and fix typos.
