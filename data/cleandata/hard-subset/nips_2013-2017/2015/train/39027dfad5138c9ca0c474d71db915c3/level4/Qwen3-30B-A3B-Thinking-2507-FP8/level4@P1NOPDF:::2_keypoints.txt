1. Fundamental conceptual error: Equation (2) is mischaracterized as an expectation, but the loss is an estimator depending on the prior π, not an expected value.  
2. The claim that Equation (2) shows an "anomaly" from lack of translation invariance is incorrect because translation-invariance does not apply to this estimator.  
3. Example 1 is undermined because it relies on the flawed premise that the loss should be translation-invariant.  
4. Practical instability: the estimator in Equation (2) can be undefined if π is zero for an unobserved sample.  
5. Equation (7) improves robustness by mitigating π's influence via a double denominator.  
6. Authors should correct the treatment of Equation (2) and revise the expectation/translation-invariance discussion before acceptance.  
7. Empirical result: Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across all datasets.  
8. Norm-POEM achieves performance competitive with a fully supervised CRF using full multi-label annotations.  
9. Framing: the paper is essentially an improvement to the POEM estimator from ICML 2015, with counterfactual learning serving mainly as context.  
10. Overall verdict: well-motivated, technically sound, and empirically convincing, but requires correction of the Equation (2) conceptual error.
