**Additional comments:**  
- Section 3 (and [1]) treats the feedback delta(x,y) as deterministic (line 110), but it should be modeled as a random variable. In classification with noisy labels, identical (x,y) pairs may yield different delta(x,y) due to label variability (e.g., p(y'|x) governing true labels). Thus, delta(x,y) is inherently random, and the true risk in Equation (1) should include an expectation over this randomness to capture general settings. This does not affect the estimator (2), but strengthens the setup.  
- Line 170–171: The ratio should be h(y|x)/h₀(y|x), not h(y|x)/h₀(y|x). Also note that the non-linearity of this ratio was previously highlighted in [1].  
- Line 276: Use "argmin" instead of "argmax" since the goal is risk minimization.  
- Line 319: Replace bars with hats (e.g., ̂θ) to align with prior notation.
