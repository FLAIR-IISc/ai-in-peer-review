

The paper addresses an important technical challenge in multi-label learning with bandit feedback, presenting a modified estimator (Norm-POEM) that improves upon the POEM framework from [1]. While the experimental results are compelling—Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across all datasets and approaches CRF performance using full multi-label information—the theoretical foundation contains a critical error that requires correction before publication.  

The core issue lies in the analysis of Equation (2). The authors claim this estimator exhibits an "anomaly" due to lack of translation invariance, arguing that the loss is not invariant under shifts of the loss function. This claim is fundamentally flawed. By definition, an expectation is of the form $\mathbb{E}[f(X)] = \sum_x f(x) p(x)$, where $f(x)$ is the *function* being averaged and $p(x)$ is the probability measure. Equation (2) defines a *loss* (not an expectation), specifically $\sum_i \ell_i \frac{\pi_i}{\hat{\pi}_i}$, where $\ell_i$ is the loss for class $i$ and $\pi_i$ is the prior. The authors erroneously conflate the loss with the expectation, leading to the incorrect assertion that the loss should be translation-invariant. Consequently, the purported "anomaly" is a mischaracterization, and Example 1—which relies on this error—fails to hold.  

This theoretical misstep has practical implications. Equation (2) is highly sensitive to the denominator $\hat{\pi}_i$, which may be zero if a class was unobserved in the prior, rendering the estimator undefined. The modified estimator in Equation (7) properly mitigates this issue through its double denominator ($\hat{\pi}_i + \epsilon$ in both numerator and denominator), making it more robust. The paper’s current commentary, however, incorrectly frames Equation (2) as having a "translation anomaly" rather than acknowledging its denominator instability. This must be revised: the critical flaw is the potential for division by zero, not translation non-invariance.  

The paper’s framing of "counterfactual learning" as a distinct learning scenario is also misleading. While multi-label learning typically provides full 0/1 annotations, this work operates under bandit feedback (only one label observed per sample), which is a *special case* of multi-label learning. However, the paper’s primary contribution is not the counterfactual setting itself but a technical refinement of POEM [1]. The experimental section correctly demonstrates Norm-POEM’s superiority over POEM and near-optimality relative to CRF, but the theoretical justification must align with this focus.  

Despite these issues, the paper is otherwise strong. The derivation of Norm-POEM is sound, the empirical results are thorough and well-presented, and the method’s practical utility is clear. The authors’ aim—to improve POEM’s robustness—remains valid and impactful. The requested revisions are minor but essential for correctness:  
1. **Correct the theoretical error**: Remove all references to "translation anomaly" and replace them with a clear explanation of the denominator instability in Equation (2) and how Equation (7) resolves it.  
2. **Reframe the contribution**: Clarify that the work is a refinement of POEM [1] within the context of bandit feedback, not a new "counterfactual learning" framework.  

I strongly recommend acceptance *only if* these revisions are implemented. The current draft’s theoretical misstep risks misleading readers about the estimator’s properties, while the experimental validation remains robust. A concise correction would strengthen the paper’s technical rigor without altering its core contribution. The authors should be gracious in addressing this, as it is a straightforward clarification of a fundamental misunderstanding.  

In summary: The paper presents a valuable improvement to POEM with strong empirical results. However, the theoretical discussion of Equation (2) contains a critical error that must be corrected to avoid misrepresenting the estimator’s behavior. Once revised, the paper will be a solid contribution to the field.
