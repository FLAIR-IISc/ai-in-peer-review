The paper "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning" presents a novel approach to improving the estimation of counterfactual risk in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional unbiased risk estimator used in prior works on BLBF, which they term "propensity overfitting." This problem arises when the estimator is not equivariant to translations of the loss function, leading to degenerate risk estimates.

The authors propose a new estimator, called the self-normalized estimator, which addresses this issue by incorporating a multiplicative control variate. This estimator is shown to be equivariant to translations of the loss function and has a lower variance than the conventional estimator. The authors also derive a new learning algorithm, called Norm-POEM, which uses the self-normalized estimator and is based on the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle.

The experimental results show that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM, a recently presented estimator at ICML 2015, on all datasets and is not far from a Conditional Random Field (CRF) using full multi-label information. The authors also demonstrate that the self-normalized estimator is more robust to propensity overfitting than the conventional estimator.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem and their proposed solution. The experimental results are thorough and convincing, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the performance of their algorithm.

However, there are some minor issues with the notation and commentary in the paper. Specifically, the authors' notation and commentary concerning the main equation (2) seem misleading. The loss is not the expected quantity, and the ensuing Example 1 is based on this incorrect presumption. The authors should modify their commentary to reflect this.

Additionally, the authors could provide more context and background information on the CRM principle and the POEM estimator, as well as a more detailed explanation of the self-normalized estimator and its properties.

Overall, the paper is convincing and worth publication, but with some minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to improving the estimation of counterfactual risk in BLBF.
* The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional unbiased risk estimator used in prior works on BLBF and propose a new estimator that addresses this issue.
* The experimental results show that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and is not far from a CRF using full multi-label information.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem and their proposed solution.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The notation and commentary concerning the main equation (2) seem misleading.
* The authors could provide more context and background information on the CRM principle and the POEM estimator.
* The authors could provide a more detailed explanation of the self-normalized estimator and its properties.

Rating: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
