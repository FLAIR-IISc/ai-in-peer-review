This paper proposes an alternative estimator, the self-normalized estimator, to address the propensity overfitting problem in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors demonstrate that the conventional unbiased risk estimator used in prior works on BLBF suffers from severe anomalies that can lead to degeneracies when used in empirical risk minimization (ERM). They show that the self-normalized estimator neatly avoids these problems and provides improved generalization performance and robustness.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and its implications for BLBF. The proposed self-normalized estimator is well-motivated, and the theoretical results are sound. The experimental evaluation is thorough and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Strengths:

* The paper identifies a significant problem in BLBF and proposes a novel solution.
* The analysis of propensity overfitting is thorough and well-motivated.
* The self-normalized estimator is a clever solution that addresses the propensity overfitting problem.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the feedback is a deterministic function, whereas in many cases, it may be a random variable. This assumption may not hold in practice, and the authors should discuss the implications of this assumption.
* The notation is not always consistent, and some minor errors were found (e.g., line 170-171, line 276, line 319).
