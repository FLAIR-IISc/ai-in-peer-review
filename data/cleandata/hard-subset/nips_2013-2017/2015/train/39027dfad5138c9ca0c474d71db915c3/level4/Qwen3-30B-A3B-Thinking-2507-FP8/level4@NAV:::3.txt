

Review Summary  
The paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a critical problem in applications such as ad ranking. It identifies an "overfitting" issue in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a solution using multiplicative control variates, yielding a biased estimator.  

Quality  
High-quality and well motivated. As a follow-up to [1], additional experiments and deeper analysis of the training objective optimization (beyond the details in line 415) were expected.  

Clarity  
The presentation is clear, and the paper is well written.  

Originality  
This work extends [1] by modifying the risk estimator to be self-normalizing, which substantially impacts the experimental results.  

Significance  
While the problem of learning from logged data is relevant, it remains unclear how the significant experimental improvements translate to practical real-world applications.  

Various Remarks  
Line 365: If multiple repetitions are conducted, please include a measure of variance. The paper identifies a problem in the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a solution. Overall, the paper is solid, well written, and the work is relevant and novel. However, extended experiments—particularly additional applications beyond those in [1], including real-world cases—would have been valuable.
