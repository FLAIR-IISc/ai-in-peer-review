- The notation and commentary around equation (2) are incorrect/misleading: an expectation is mischaracterized and the loss in (2) is not the expected quantity.
- The paper’s claim that equation (2) exhibits an "anomaly" due to non-invariance to translation of the loss is unfounded.
- Example 1, which depends on the above incorrect assumption about (2), does not hold.
- The estimator in equation (2) is sensitive to its denominator π, which can be zero for unseen samples.
- The modified estimator in equation (7) is more robust in principle because the double denominator mitigates the effects of π.
- The authors should revise their commentary if the paper is accepted to correct these technical issues.
- Experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and performs comparably to a CRF trained with full multi-label information.
- The paper addresses counterfactual (bandit-feedback) multi-label learning and primarily aims to improve the POEM estimator from ICML 2015; overall convincing and worthy of publication despite noted concerns.
