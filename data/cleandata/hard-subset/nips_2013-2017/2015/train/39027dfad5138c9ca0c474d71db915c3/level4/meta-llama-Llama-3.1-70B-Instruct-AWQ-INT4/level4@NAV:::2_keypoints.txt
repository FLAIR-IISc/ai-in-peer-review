- The paper’s discussion of equation (2) is incorrect/misleading: an expectation should be written as ∑ f(x)p(x) with f(x) distinct from the probability measure p(x).
- The loss in equation (2) is not an expected quantity, so the claim that an "anomaly" arises from non-invariance to loss translation is technically inaccurate.
- Example 1, which depends on the incorrect expectation assumption, does not hold.
- The estimator in equation (2) is sensitive to its denominator π, which can be zero if a sample was not observed in the prior.
- The modified estimator in equation (7) appears to improve robustness by mitigating π’s effects via a double denominator.
- Reviewer asks authors to revise the discussion if the paper is accepted.
- Experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and is comparable to a CRF trained with full multi-label information.
- The paper addresses counterfactual learning (bandit feedback: one label per sample) rather than traditional multi-label learning.
- The main contribution is an enhancement of the POEM estimator (ICML 2015), and the reviewer finds the paper convincing and publishable despite listed concerns.
