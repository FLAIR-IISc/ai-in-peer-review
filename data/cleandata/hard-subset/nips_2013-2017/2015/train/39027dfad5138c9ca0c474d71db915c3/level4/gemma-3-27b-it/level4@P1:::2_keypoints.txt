- Identifies propensity overfitting in the commonly used counterfactual risk estimator for BLBF and shows it biases learning in complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator derived via control variates that avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm using the self-normalized estimator that outperforms conventional methods.  
- Motivates the problem well, connecting BLBF to off-policy evaluation and causal inference.  
- Related work is thorough, situating the contribution among counterfactual estimators, variance reduction, and domain adaptation.  
- Provides a clear formalization of BLBF and counterfactual estimators with well-defined notation.  
- The propensity overfitting section convincingly demonstrates the new form of overfitting with an insightful example.  
- Shows why the standard estimator is not equivariant and how that leads to overfitting.  
- Uses S(h) as a control variate with a clear justification for the self-normalized estimator.  
- Theorem 2 establishes consistency of the self-normalized estimator, strengthening theoretical claims.  
- Norm-POEM is described clearly, including hypothesis space, variance estimator, and computational considerations.  
- Experiments show consistent outperformance of Norm-POEM over POEM across multiple multi-label classification datasets.  
- Analysis of Ŝ(h) indicates Norm-POEM is less sensitive to bias from loss translation; comparison to full-information CRF is informative.  
- Paper is technically sound, complete, and represents a substantial contribution to BLBF.  
- Generally well-written and organized, though some sections need additional clarification.  
- Original work: identification of propensity overfitting and the self-normalized estimator are novel advances.  
- Significant potential impact for online learning, recommendation systems, and learning from logged data.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a critical problem, offers a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution, and shows consistent gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: initial technical description of the anomaly is imprecise and scalability to very large datasets needs further study.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address technical clarifications and scalability discussion.
