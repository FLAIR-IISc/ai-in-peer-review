- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback and identifies "propensity overfitting" where high-capacity models exploit propensity weights to minimize the unbiased estimator.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator derived from multiplicative control variates that is equivariant to loss translations and bounded by the observed loss range.  
- Instantiates the idea within CRM as the Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Experiments on multi-label classification show Norm-POEM consistently and substantially outperforms POEM and mitigates propensity overfitting; loss-translation invariance is empirically validated.  
- Strength: novel and significant identification of propensity overfitting as a key failure mode for counterfactual learning.  
- Strength: principled, theoretically grounded solution with desirable properties (equivariance, bounded risk).  
- Strength: strong, thorough empirical validation linking improved generalization to mitigation of propensity overfitting.  
- Weakness: unclear whether Norm-POEM inherits the CRM requirement that losses be scaled to [-1,0]; the loss-translation experiments need clarification relative to theory.  
- Weakness: formulation treats feedback δ(x,y) as deterministic; should discuss extension to stochastic/noisy feedback (expected risk over δ).  
- Weakness: minor presentation issues — typo in control variates discussion (importance weight is a ratio, not product), note equivariance was briefly mentioned in [1], and check notational/typographical inconsistencies.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, well-justified contribution with strong experiments; recommended for acceptance.
