

This paper presents NORM-POEM, a follow-up to [1] addressing propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback. The approach leverages a multiplicative control variate to reduce variance, with promising empirical results. The technical contributions are sound, and the analysis of propensity overfitting is insightful. However, several clarifications and minor corrections are needed before acceptance.

**Major Concerns:**  
1. **Delta Range Consistency (Section 4.1):** The CRM approach in [1] restricts δ to [-1, 0] (see Section 4.1), requiring loss rescaling. The paper states NORM-POEM uses the same rescaling (Equations 3 and 8), yet Table 1 experiments employ δ > 0. This contradicts the theoretical setup. Please clarify:  
   - Is the δ > 0 in Table 1 a typo, or does NORM-POEM *actually* support δ > 0?  
   - If the latter, how does this align with the [-1, 0] requirement in [1] and Equations (3)/(8)?  
   *This is critical for reproducibility and theoretical coherence.*  

2. **Randomness in Feedback (Section 3):** The setup treats δ(x, y) as a deterministic function (line 110), but in practice (e.g., classification with noisy labels), δ(x, y) should be a random variable. Specifically:  
   - For input *x*, the true label *y'* may vary stochastically (e.g., due to noise), making δ(x, y) = Δ(y', y) random for fixed *x*, *y*.  
   - The true risk (Equation 1) should explicitly include an expectation over this randomness: *R(h) = E_{x,y'}[Δ(y', h(x))]*, not a deterministic *Δ(y', y)*.  
   *While this does not invalidate the risk estimator (2), the setup is incomplete without acknowledging this generalization.*  

**Minor Corrections:**  
- Line 170–171: Replace *h_0(y|x)/h(y|x)* with *h(y|x)/h_0(y|x)* (to match standard importance weighting). Also note that the non-linearity of the risk estimator was already highlighted in [1].  
- Line 276: Replace *argmax* with *argmin* (since the goal is risk minimization).  
- Line 319: Use *hats* (e.g., *ĥ*) instead of *bars* for consistency with prior notation.  

**Overall Assessment:**  
The work is a valuable extension of [1], with clear empirical improvements over POEM on the batch bandit problem—a growing area of interest. The propensity overfitting analysis is compelling and well-motivated. The technical contributions (e.g., the multiplicative control variate) are original and significant.  

**Rebuttal Response:**  
The authors clarified the delta range issue: NORM-POEM *does* require δ ∈ [-1, 0], and Table 1’s δ > 0 was a typo (using unrescaled loss values). This aligns with the theoretical setup. The feedback randomness point remains valid but does not affect the estimator’s correctness under the standard model (where δ is deterministic).  

**Final Recommendation:**  
This is a strong contribution to a relevant problem. With the minor clarifications above (especially the delta range typo), it merits acceptance. The analysis and results are robust, and the method is well-motivated.
