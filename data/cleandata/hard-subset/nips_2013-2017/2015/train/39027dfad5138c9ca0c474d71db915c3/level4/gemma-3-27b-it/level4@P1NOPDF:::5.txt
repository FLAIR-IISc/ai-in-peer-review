The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that avoids the propensity overfitting problem. It builds upon the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) and presents a significantly improved algorithm.

Quality: I found the research presented in this paper to be above average. The extensions proposed do result in significant improvements in training the original POEM algorithm.

Clarity: For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow. 

Originality: The paper introduces a significantly new formulation to the original POEM algorithm for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. I believe the contributions are substantially original.

Significance: I think the results presented in the paper are very interesting and warrant further research. I am particularly impressed that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM despite incorporating more regularization. This algorithm has the potential for widespread use in the near future. 

Overall, this is a strong paper. It is clear, well-written, and presents a valuable contribution to the field.
