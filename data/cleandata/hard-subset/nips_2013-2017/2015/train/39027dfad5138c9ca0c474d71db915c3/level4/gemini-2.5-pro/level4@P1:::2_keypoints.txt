- Paper tackles batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies "propensity overfitting" where IPS-based learners exploit propensity weights to get degenerate solutions.  
- Authors propose replacing the unbiased IPS estimator with a self-normalized estimator derived from multiplicative control variates.  
- The new estimator is claimed to be equivariant to loss translations and bounded by the loss range, avoiding anomalies that cause propensity overfitting.  
- Based on this estimator, the paper introduces Norm-POEM, a Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models adapting the CRM principle.  
- Extensive experiments on multi-label classification show Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM and approaches a full-information skyline.  
- Strength: Identifies and formalizes propensity overfitting, a subtle but important problem in counterfactual/off-policy learning.  
- Strength: Provides a principled, technically sound solution grounded in control variates and ratio estimators, with theoretical analysis of consistency and properties.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing, demonstrating robustness to loss translations where POEM fails.  
- Main weakness: The paper's central theoretical motivation—framing IPS lack of translation equivariance as an "anomaly"—is technically misleading.  
- Critique: Translation equivariance does not hold for the importance-weighted term by construction (the averaged quantity includes the importance weight), so Section 4 and Example 1 rest on an imprecise interpretation.  
- Practical defense: Regardless of the exposition, the self-normalized estimator is preferable in practice because it normalizes by the sum of importance weights, mitigating issues from tiny propensities.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept; authors should revise Section 4 to provide a more precise theoretical justification for the self-normalized estimator.
