## Review:

This paper addresses the important problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, with direct relevance to applications like ad ranking. The authors identify an overfitting issue within the recently introduced Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) framework and propose a solution leveraging multiplicative control variates, acknowledging the resulting estimator bias.

The work is of high quality and well-motivated. As a continuation of [1], further experimentation and more in-depth analysis of the training objective’s optimization – beyond the discussion on line 415 – would have been beneficial. 

The presentation is clear and the paper is well-written.

While building upon [1], the modification of the risk estimator to achieve self-normalization represents a significant contribution, demonstrably impacting performance in the authors’ experimental evaluations.

The problem addressed is highly relevant; however, assessing the scalability of the observed experimental improvements to broader, real-world scenarios remains a challenge.

Specific comments: On line 365, if multiple repetitions are already performed, including a measure of variance would strengthen the results. The paper successfully identifies a limitation of Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) and introduces a compelling solution. Overall, this is a solid, well-written, relevant, and novel contribution. I would encourage the authors to expand their experimental analysis, particularly beyond the applications presented in [1], ideally including evaluations using real-world datasets.
