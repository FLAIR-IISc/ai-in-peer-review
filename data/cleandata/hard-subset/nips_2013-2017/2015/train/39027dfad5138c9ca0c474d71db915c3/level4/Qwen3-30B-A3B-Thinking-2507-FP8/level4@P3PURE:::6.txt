

Counterfactual risk minimization is a technique for learning in bandit settings from logged data, designed to correct for bias in the generation of logged examples. While the approach is consistent, the paper demonstrates that it suffers from an overfitting problem: a hypothesis class can overfit to logged data by exploiting the generation probability of individual examples. The authors propose a solution using a multiplicative parameter that bounds the variance while preserving consistency. Experimental results show that the normalized (multiplicative) counterfactual technique significantly outperforms the non-normalized variant. This is a strong paper that addresses a meaningful challenge in counterfactual learning and should be accepted, though I remain somewhat uncertain about the generality of the overfitting analysis.
