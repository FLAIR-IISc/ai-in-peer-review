- Paper is engaging but some technical aspects were not fully verified.  
- Evaluating an alternative hypothesis requires reweighting data (as between (1) and (2)), especially for non-deterministic conditional distributions.  
- Authors tackle a problem called "propensity overfitting," related to but distinct from large-variance weights.  
- Propensity overfitting appears to occur when there is insufficient exploration and few/no y observations for the new hypothesis at given x.  
- The relationship between propensity overfitting and large-weight variance needs clearer, more detailed explanation.  
- The core idea seems reasonable, though the novelty and technical details were not thoroughly assessed.  
- Line 199 is unclear: if n << k we expect few xi = yi — shouldn’t \hat R(h^) be even smaller than -2?  
- The claim that prior BLBF works used an "unbiased counterfactual risk estimator" may be inaccurate since methods like clipping can introduce bias to reduce variance.  
- Reviewer appreciates that the authors released their code.  
- Recommendation: accept, despite inability to fully verify technical soundness.
