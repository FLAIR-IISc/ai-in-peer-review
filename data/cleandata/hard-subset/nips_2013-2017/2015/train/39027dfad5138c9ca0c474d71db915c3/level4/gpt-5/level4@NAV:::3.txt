Review- Summary
The paper tackles batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant problem with applications such as ad ranking. It highlights an overfitting issue in the recently proposed CRM principle and offers a remedy based on multiplicative control variates, which introduces a biased estimator.

Quality
High-quality and well-motivated. Since this is follow-up work to [1], I expected more extensive experiments and additional analysis of the optimization of the training objective (more detail than the brief discussion around line 415).

Clarity
The presentation is clear and the paper is well written.

Originality
This is follow-up work on [1], but it modifies the risk estimator to be self-normalizing, which appears to produce large improvements in the authors’ experiments.

Significance
Learning from logged data is important; however, it is uncertain how the substantial empirical gains will carry over to broader real-world applications.

Various Remarks
- Line 365: if multiple repetitions were run, please report a measure of variability (e.g., variance or error bars).
- The paper identifies an issue in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and introduces a solution.
- Overall, the paper is solid, well written, and the contributions are relevant and novel. I would have liked more extensive experiments and especially applications beyond those in [1], including real-world deployments.
