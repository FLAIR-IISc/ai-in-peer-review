Summary
The paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant problem (e.g., in ad ranking). It identifies an overfitting issue in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) framework and proposes a remedy using multiplicative control variates, yielding a biased but self-normalizing estimator.

Quality
The work is high quality and well motivated. However, as a follow-up to [1], I expected more extensive experiments and a deeper analysis of the optimization of the training objective. The brief discussion around line 415 is insufficient; more detail on optimization behavior and practical considerations would be valuable.

Clarity
The presentation is clear and the paper is well written.

Originality
While it builds on [1], the modification of the risk estimator to a self-normalizing variant is a meaningful contribution and appears to drive substantial gains in the reported experiments.

Significance
Learning from logged data is important and timely. The empirical improvements are substantial, but it remains unclear how these gains translate to broader or real-world applications.

Various Remarks
- Line 365: Since multiple repetitions are run, please report variability (e.g., standard errors or confidence intervals).
- The paper clearly identifies a limitation of CRM and introduces a principled solution. Overall, it is solid, well written, relevant, and novel. I would nonetheless have appreciated expanded experiments and additional applications beyond those in [1], ideally including at least one real-world deployment or dataset.
