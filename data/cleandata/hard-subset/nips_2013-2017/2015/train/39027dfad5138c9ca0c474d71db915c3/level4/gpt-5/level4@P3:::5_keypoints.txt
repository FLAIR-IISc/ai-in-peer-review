- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) where IPS-based ERM can be driven to degenerate solutions.
- Proposes using self-normalized importance sampling (SNIPS) as a bounded, translation-equivariant counterfactual risk estimator with a simple consistency argument.
- Introduces Norm-POEM: a CRM-based algorithm for structured exponential-family policies, with an approximate variance estimator and L-BFGS optimization.
- Empirical evaluation on supervised-to-bandit multi-label tasks shows Norm-POEM outperforms POEM, is robust to loss translations, and has reasonable runtime.
- Situates work within BLBF, causal inference, IPS clipping, variance reduction, and doubly robust literature; novelty is applying multiplicative control variates to BLBF objectives.
- Clear identification and illustrative examples of propensity overfitting, highlighting IPS unboundedness and non-equivariance.
- SNIPS offers a simple principled fix that bounds estimates, enforces translation equivariance, and controls weight-sum deviation.
- Provides consistency for SNIPS and a diagnostic Ŝ(h) that matches empirical behavior.
- Presents a concrete, tractable algorithm (Norm-POEM) and practical variance approximation for structured policies.
- Strong empirical gains over POEM across datasets and training sizes, with controlled optimizer comparisons.
- Lacks finite-sample generalization bounds for the SNIPS-based CRM objective analogous to prior Bernstein-style CRM bounds.
- Variance estimate uses asymptotic approximations; sensitivity in small-n or heavy-tailed settings is unclear.
- SNIPS is biased in finite samples; sensitivity to clipping parameter M and regularizer λ (and interaction with policy support) needs systematic analysis.
- Empirical baselines are limited to POEM; comparisons to clipped IPS and doubly robust estimators are missing.
- Evaluation scope is narrow (supervised-to-bandit multi-label); tests on real logs or other structured tasks like ranking would improve external validity.
- Method assumes accurate propensities and logging policy support; robustness to misspecified propensities is not studied.
- Paper includes a public code link that may break double-blind review and should be anonymized.
- Technically sound derivation, sensible variance approximation, and convincing propensity overfitting analysis supported by experiments.
- Well written and organized; recommend clarifying variance approximation reliability, comparison to doubly robust methods, and hyperparameter selection guidance for M and λ.
- Applying self-normalization to BLBF learning is a novel, insightful contribution; Norm-POEM and Ŝ(h) diagnostic are original in this context.
- Contribution is significant and practically relevant, likely to influence counterfactual learning methods.
- Pros for acceptance: identifies key failure mode, offers principled fix with consistency, shows consistent empirical gains, and has practical impact.
- Cons for acceptance: missing finite-sample learning bound, limited baselines/domains, and reliance on asymptotic variance approximations.
- Suggested improvements: add finite-sample bound or discussion, compare to clipped IPS and doubly robust baselines, perform sensitivity analyses for M and λ, test robustness to propensity noise, and anonymize code.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated, and practically relevant paper that would be an excellent fit with minor additions and clarifications.
