Counterfactual risk reduction is a technique aimed at learning from a bandit environment by correcting bias in creating logged examples. Although consistent, the paper shows that it suffers from an overfitting problem where a hypothesis class can overfit based on the probability of generating an example. The authors propose a solution to this problem using a multiplicative parameter that maintains consistency while also limiting variance. Experimental results show that the multiplicative (or normalized) counterfactual technique performs better than the non-normalized technique.

The paper presents a self-normalized assumption learning technique for counterfactual learning in a logged bandit environment. It presents an idea to combat a form of overfitting where the hypothesis may overfit due to strong support for logged data. It's a good paper and should be accepted, although I'm not entirely sure.
