- Proposes Norm-POEM: a new algorithm for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that addresses propensity overfitting.
- Builds on Counterfactual Risk Minimization (Swaminathan & Joachims, 2015) and reformulates POEM for exponential models.
- Replaces the conventional estimator with a self-normalized risk estimator that provably avoids propensity overfitting.
- Technical content is sound with clear problem formulation, well-defined notation, and easy-to-follow derivations.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis of the self-normalized estimator and its properties.
- Empirical evaluation shows consistent and significant improvements over the original POEM on multi-label classification tasks.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and accessible to readers unfamiliar with the topic.
- Originality is significant; contribution is substantial and likely to see widespread use.
- Significance is high since it tackles a critical BLBF issue and yields a more robust, efficient algorithm.
- Arguments for acceptance: critical problem addressed, strong theory, extensive experiments, clear writing, and practical impact.
- No arguments against acceptance were provided.
