This paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) that addresses the propensity overfitting problem. Building on the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the authors provide a significantly improved algorithm.

The quality of research discussed in the paper is above average. The extensions proposed in the paper result in significant improvements in the training of the original POEM algorithm. The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in BLBF and propose an alternative estimator that avoids this issue. The new estimator, called the Self-Normalized Risk Estimator, is shown to be more robust and effective in avoiding propensity overfitting.

The paper is clearly written and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers with a background in machine learning and BLBF. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the problem, the proposed solution, and the experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.

The contributions of the paper are significantly original. The authors provide a new formulation of the POEM algorithm for the BLBF problem, which addresses the propensity overfitting issue. The proposed algorithm, called Norm-POEM, is shown to be more effective and efficient than the original POEM algorithm.

The results in the paper are very interesting and certainly require further research. The authors demonstrate that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM despite having more regularization mechanisms built into it. This algorithm may see widespread use in the near future, particularly in applications where BLBF is a critical component.

Overall, this is a good paper that is clear and well-written. The authors provide a significant contribution to the field of machine learning and BLBF, and their work is likely to have a lasting impact.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel solution to the propensity overfitting problem in BLBF.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.
* The contributions of the paper are significantly original and are likely to have a lasting impact on the field.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper builds on previous work, and some readers may question the novelty of the contributions.
* The experimental results may not be comprehensive, and further evaluation may be necessary to fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

However, overall, the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses, and I believe that it is a good candidate for acceptance.
