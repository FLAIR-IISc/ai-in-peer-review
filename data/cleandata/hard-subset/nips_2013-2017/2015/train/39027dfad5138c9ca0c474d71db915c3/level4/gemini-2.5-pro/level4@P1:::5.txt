This paper addresses the problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies a crucial flaw, termed "propensity overfitting," in the conventional unbiased counterfactual risk estimator. The authors demonstrate that this standard estimator can produce severely biased and degenerate risk estimates, particularly when learning over complex hypothesis spaces.

The core contribution is the proposal to replace the standard estimator with a self-normalized risk estimator. This alternative approach uses the sum of propensity weights as a multiplicative control variate, a technique which provably remedies the anomalies of the unbiased estimator, such as its lack of equivariance to shifts in the loss function. Building on this improved estimator, the paper develops a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, as a direct and principled enhancement of the POEM algorithm (Swaminathan & Joachims, 2015). The paper's claims are well-supported by both theoretical arguments regarding the estimator's desirable properties and a strong empirical evaluation on several multi-label classification datasets, where Norm-POEM is shown to consistently and significantly outperform its predecessor.

**Strengths / Arguments for Acceptance:**

1.  **Clear Problem Identification and Solution:** The paper excels at identifying a subtle yet severe problem in a widely-used methodology. The "propensity overfitting" phenomenon is explained with exceptional clarity, aided by a simple and effective illustrative example (Example 1). The proposed solution—employing a self-normalized estimator—is not an ad-hoc fix but a theoretically-grounded and elegant solution that directly targets the root cause of the problem.

2.  **Significant Empirical Improvements:** The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate a clear, practical benefit. Across all multi-label classification tasks, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the original POEM algorithm. The experiments convincingly show that this improvement is a direct result of avoiding propensity overfitting, as evidenced by the sum of propensity weights Ŝ(ĥ) remaining close to its expected value of 1 for Norm-POEM, unlike for POEM.

3.  **High Practical Significance and Efficiency:** The contributions are of high significance to researchers and practitioners working with logged bandit data (e.g., in recommendation systems, ad placement, or search). Surprisingly, despite the more complex form of the variance regularization term, the proposed Norm-POEM algorithm is reported to be computationally *faster* than the original POEM due to faster convergence. This makes the proposed method not only more robust and accurate but also more practical to deploy.

4.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is very well-written and easy to follow. The arguments are presented logically, starting from the problem definition, proceeding to the proposed solution, and culminating in a new algorithm and thorough empirical validation. This makes the paper a strong contribution that is accessible and compelling.

**Weaknesses / Minor Points for Improvement:**

The paper presents a very complete and compelling piece of work, and it is difficult to find significant weaknesses. However, for the sake of offering constructive feedback, the following minor points could be considered:

1.  While the paper notes that the self-normalized estimator is biased for finite samples (though strongly consistent), a brief discussion on the potential impact of this bias in extremely small sample-size scenarios could add further nuance. The experiments presented seem to operate in a regime where this is not an issue, but acknowledging this theoretical trade-off would be valuable.

2.  The empirical evaluation, while thorough on the chosen multi-label classification tasks, could be further strengthened by demonstrating its effectiveness on other types of structured prediction problems where the original POEM was also applied (e.g., sequence labeling). This would further solidify the claims of general applicability.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is an excellent paper that makes a significant and practical contribution to the field of counterfactual learning. It clearly identifies an important problem with existing methods and proposes an elegant, well-motivated, and empirically superior solution. The resulting algorithm, Norm-POEM, represents a clear advance over prior work. The paper is well-written, the experiments are thorough, and the results are impressive. I strongly recommend acceptance.
