This paper proposes a novel method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback, effectively addressing the propensity overfitting problem. Building upon the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the authors introduce significant improvements to the original algorithm.

Quality: The research presented in this paper is of above-average quality. The proposed extensions to the original algorithm result in substantial improvements in training the POEM (Policy Optimization by Multiple Estimators) algorithm, demonstrating a clear understanding of the subject matter.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow for the most part. The authors have done an excellent job of presenting complex ideas in a clear and concise manner, making the paper accessible to a broad audience.

Originality: The paper presents a significantly new formulation of the original POEM algorithm for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. The contributions are substantial and original, offering a fresh perspective on the existing work.

Significance: The results presented in the paper are intriguing and warrant further research. The proposed algorithm's ability to outperform the original POEM in terms of speed, despite incorporating additional regularization mechanisms, is particularly impressive. This algorithm has the potential to see widespread adoption in the near future, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

Overall, this is a well-written and engaging paper that effectively communicates the authors' ideas. The research is of high quality, and the contributions are significant and original. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise, making it a pleasure to read.
