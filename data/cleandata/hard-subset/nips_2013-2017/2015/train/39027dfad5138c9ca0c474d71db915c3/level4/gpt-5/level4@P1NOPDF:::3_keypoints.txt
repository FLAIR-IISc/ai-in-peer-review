- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback, identifies overfitting in CRM, and proposes multiplicative control variates yielding a self-normalizing (biased) estimator.  
- Work is high quality and well motivated, but more experiments and deeper analysis of optimization (beyond the brief discussion around line 415) are expected.  
- Presentation is clear and the paper is well written.  
- Builds on [1], but the self-normalizing risk estimator is a meaningful, novel modification that substantially improves results in experiments.  
- Learning from logged data is important and timely, though it’s unclear how experimental gains translate to real-world applications.  
- Request to report variability (e.g., standard deviations or confidence intervals) for repeated experiments.  
- Overall solid, relevant, and novel work, but needs more extensive experiments and at least one real-world dataset/application.
