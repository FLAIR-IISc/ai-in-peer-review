- Counterfactual risk minimization addresses learning from logged bandit data by correcting selection bias from the logging policy.  
- The standard inverse-propensity estimator, though consistent, can overfit by exploiting logged examples’ generation probabilities (“propensity overfitting”).  
- The authors propose a multiplicative control variate—self-normalization of importance weights—to mitigate variance while preserving consistency.  
- Empirical results show the self-normalized counterfactual estimator consistently outperforms the unnormalized baseline.  
- The paper introduces the self-normalized estimator and addresses overfitting caused by hypotheses aligning with the logged data’s support.  
- Reviewer assessment: strong paper that should be accepted, though with some residual uncertainty.
