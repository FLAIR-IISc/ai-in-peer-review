- Paper identifies propensity overfitting: IPS can be driven arbitrarily low by exploiting high-variance importance weights, causing degenerate ERM behavior.  
- Proposes replacing IPS with SNIPS (self-normalized importance sampling) to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- SNIPS properties: bounded within loss range, equivariant to additive loss translations, and strongly consistent under standard support assumptions.  
- Integrates SNIPS into a CRM-style objective with an approximate variance penalty, yielding the Norm-POEM algorithm for structured prediction.  
- Empirical evaluation on LibSVM multi-label datasets shows consistent improvements over POEM (IPS), robustness to loss translation, and good computational behavior.  
- Theory: constructed example and diagnostics (Ŝ(h)) clearly illustrate propensity overfitting; SNIPS theoretical claims are supported by standard arguments.  
- Variance estimate for SNIPS uses a normal/delta-method approximation and clipping, which is reasonable but approximate.  
- Limitation: no generalization bound for SNIPS analogous to CRM bounds for IPS; theoretical link to out-of-sample performance is heuristic.  
- Assumptions (accurate logged propensities, full support) are standard but strong and need more explicit discussion for practice.  
- Experiments: “Supervised → Bandit” protocol is sound and diagnostic Ŝ(ĥ) is compelling.  
- Empirical comparisons should include clipped-IPS baselines with tuned thresholds and doubly robust (DR) methods for a fuller evaluation.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear; should explicitly separate estimation guarantees (consistency, boundedness) from learning guarantees (generalization).  
- Suggest expanding discussion of when SNIPS helps most (high weight variance, modest loss variance) and when its bias may matter.  
- Originality: novelty lies in formalizing propensity overfitting, applying SNIPS in CRM-style learning, and introducing the Ŝ(h) diagnostic.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical failure mode in BLBF and offers a robust, practically useful remedy likely to influence practice.  
- Pros: clear identification of problem, well-motivated estimator, practical algorithm (Norm-POEM), consistent empirical gains, and available code.  
- Cons: missing SNIPS learning-theoretic guarantees, omitted DR/clipped-IPS baselines, strong propensity/support assumptions, and approximate variance estimator.  
- Suggestions: provide learning-theoretic analysis or discussion of bounds, add clipped-IPS and DR baselines with significance tests, analyze sensitivity to hyperparameters and logging policy, and discuss robustness to noisy/unknown propensities and partial support.  
- Minor fixes: complete bibliographic entries, fix typos, and verify dataset statistics.  
- Recommendation: reviewer leans toward acceptance given the practical and empirical contributions, pending stronger theoretical guarantees and broader empirical comparisons.
