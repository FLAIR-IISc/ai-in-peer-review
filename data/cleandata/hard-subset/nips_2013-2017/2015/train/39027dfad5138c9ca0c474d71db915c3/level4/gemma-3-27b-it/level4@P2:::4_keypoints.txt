- Identifies propensity overfitting in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (multiplicative control variate) to remove propensity overfitting.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm using the self-normalized estimator that outperforms POEM empirically.
- Demonstrates consistent empirical gains on multi-label classification tasks, supporting theoretical claims.
- Clearly situates the work within causal inference, off-policy evaluation, importance sampling, and prior CRM literature (Swaminathan & Joachims, 2013).
- Provides an elegant, theoretically justified solution with comprehensive analysis of computational efficiency and robustness.
- Strength: clear problem identification with insightful examples showing bias and poor generalization of the standard estimator.
- Strength: strong empirical validation showing consistent superiority of Norm-POEM over existing methods.
- Strength: good connection to related work and appropriate citations.
- Weakness: needs clarification on the loss range assumption (loss in [-1,0]) and the experimental "delta > 0" description.
- Weakness: treats delta(x,y) as deterministic; should acknowledge and possibly model delta as a random variable for noisy labels.
- Weakness: minor notational inconsistencies and a typographical error (argmin vs argmax).
- Weakness: variance estimation in Norm-POEM has higher computational complexity and needs more discussion on practical implications and optimizations.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel identification of propensity overfitting, significant practical impact, sound theoretical and empirical support, and generally clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: minor but important technical clarifications (loss range, delta randomness) and notational issues that affect completeness and clarity.
- Overall assessment: strong paper, valuable contribution, minor clarifications needed; recommended for acceptance (Quality 7/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10).
