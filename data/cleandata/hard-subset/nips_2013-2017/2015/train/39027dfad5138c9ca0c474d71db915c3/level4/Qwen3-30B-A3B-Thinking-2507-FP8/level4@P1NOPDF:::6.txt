

Counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) addresses the challenge of learning from logged bandit data by correcting for selection bias introduced during logging. While the method is theoretically consistent, the paper identifies a critical overfitting issue: hypothesis classes can disproportionately overfit to examples with high generation probability, leading to poor generalization. The authors propose a solution by introducing a multiplicative normalization parameter that bounds variance while preserving consistency. This normalized CRM approach effectively mitigates the overfitting problem without compromising theoretical guarantees. Experimental results demonstrate that the normalized technique consistently outperforms its non-normalized counterpart across multiple datasets, validating the practical efficacy of the proposed correction. The paper makes a clear contribution by formally diagnosing this overfitting mechanism and providing a simple yet effective remedy. The technical arguments are sound, the experimental design is rigorous, and the results are well-supported. The writing is generally clear, though some sections could benefit from tighter phrasing. Overall, this is a strong contribution to the bandit learning literature. I recommend acceptance with minor reservations regarding the scalability analysis for extremely large-scale settings, which could be addressed in future work. The proposed normalization is a valuable practical enhancement that should be adopted in standard CRM implementations.
