- Identifies a significant problem, "propensity overfitting," in the common counterfactual risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting and derives a new algorithm, Norm-POEM.  
- Shows theoretical arguments that the self-normalized estimator avoids bias in complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Empirically demonstrates consistent improvements of Norm-POEM over baseline POEM on multiple multi-label classification datasets.  
- Paper is well-motivated and properly connects to causal inference, off-policy evaluation, and importance sampling literature.  
- The identification of propensity overfitting is novel and an important contribution to BLBF.  
- The proposed estimator appears theoretically sound and practically effective.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and includes discussion of computational efficiency.  
- Presentation and notation lack clarity for readers unfamiliar with BLBF, particularly around Equation (1) and the randomness of feedback δ(x,y).  
- Authors need to clarify the rescaling of δ to [-1,0] within the CRM framework and its use in experiments.  
- The connection to prior work (e.g., use of control variates) needs stronger differentiation to highlight the paper’s specific novelty.  
- Reliance on the fully supportive historical policy (h0) assumption should be more thoroughly discussed, including implications if violated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a practical problem with a novel, justified solution and strong empirical evidence.  
- Arguments for rejection: clarity issues may hinder comprehension and robustness under varied conditions needs further study.  
- Overall recommendation: accept contingent on addressing clarity and contextualization concerns.  
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
