- Identifies "propensity overfitting" in conventional CRM estimators, causing severe bias when optimizing over complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates and provides theoretical justification (Theorems 1 & 2).  
- Introduces Norm-POEM algorithm and empirically shows it consistently outperforms POEM across datasets and sample sizes.  
- Empirical evidence shows the self-normalized estimator prevents propensity overfitting (Ŝ(ĥ) ≈ 1).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and demonstrates the conventional estimator can be arbitrarily biased even with variance regularization.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and reproducible, though reported results lack standard deviations.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized; examples (Example 1) effectively illustrate the problem.  
- Originality: novel, practically useful modification to CRM by adapting multiplicative control variates from importance sampling literature.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental BLBF limitation with general theoretical insights and competitive empirical performance versus full-information CRFs.  
- Minor weaknesses: limited analysis of Norm-POEM’s optimization landscape and convergence given non-convexity.  
- Suggested additions: report standard deviations, discuss computational efficiency (per-iteration cost vs. convergence), and compare more thoroughly to other variance-reduction techniques (e.g., clipped importance weights).  
- Recommendation: strong accept; minor experimental/analysis extensions would strengthen the paper.
