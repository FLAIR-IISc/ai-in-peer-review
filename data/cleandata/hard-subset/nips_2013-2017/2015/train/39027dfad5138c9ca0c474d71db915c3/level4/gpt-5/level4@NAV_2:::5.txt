The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that mitigates the propensity overfitting problem. It builds on Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) and delivers a substantially improved algorithm.

Quality: The technical quality of the work is above average. The proposed extensions lead to meaningful gains in training over the original POEM.

Clarity: The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and easy to follow.

Originality: The paper presents a notably new formulation of the original POEM algorithm for the BLBF setting. I regard the contributions as genuinely original.

Significance: The results are compelling and merit further investigation. I am impressed that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM despite incorporating additional regularization mechanisms. This approach may see broad adoption in the near future.

This is a strong paper. It is clear and reads well.
