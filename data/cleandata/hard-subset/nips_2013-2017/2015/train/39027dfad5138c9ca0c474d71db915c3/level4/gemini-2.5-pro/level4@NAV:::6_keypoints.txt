- Studies Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) for learning from logged bandit data to correct selection bias.
- Shows CRM is consistent but can overfit due to the data generation probabilities.
- Introduces a multiplicative normalization technique that bounds estimator variance while preserving consistency.
- Empirical results show the normalized counterfactual estimator outperforms the non-normalized version.
- Main contribution: a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting.
- Provides a principled fix for hypotheses that overfit by disproportionately favoring logged data.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, though with limited confidence.
