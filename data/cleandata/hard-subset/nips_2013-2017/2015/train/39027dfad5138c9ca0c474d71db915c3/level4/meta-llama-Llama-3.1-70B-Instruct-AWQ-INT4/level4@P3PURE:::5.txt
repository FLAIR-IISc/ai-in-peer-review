The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that avoids the propensity overfitting problem, building upon the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) and providing a significantly better algorithm.

Quality: I found the quality of research discussed in the paper to be above average. The extensions proposed in the paper do result in significant improvements in training over the original POEM.

Clarity: For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

Originality: The paper presents a significantly new formulation to the original POEM algorithm for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. I believe the contributions are significantly original.

Significance: I think the results in the paper are very interesting and certainly warrant further research. I am impressed that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM despite having more regularization mechanisms built into it. This algorithm may see widespread use in the near future.

Overall, this is a good paper that is clear and reads well.
