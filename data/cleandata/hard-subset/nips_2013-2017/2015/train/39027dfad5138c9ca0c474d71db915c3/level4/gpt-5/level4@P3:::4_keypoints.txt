- Paper identifies propensity overfitting in BLBF where IPS-based ERM is exploited by hypotheses via importance weights rather than loss signal.
- Proposes using a self-normalized (SN) importance sampling estimator (multiplicative control variate) that is bounded, equivariant to additive loss shifts, and strongly consistent.
- Introduces Norm-POEM: a CRM instantiation using SN estimator plus an approximate variance regularizer and tractable optimization.
- Empirical results on supervised-to-bandit multi-label experiments show consistent improvements over POEM, reduced sensitivity to loss translations, and competitive runtime.
- Positions work at intersection of counterfactual learning, importance-weighting domain adaptation, and off-policy RL, building on CRM and classical SN/variance-reduction ideas.
- Strength: clearly diagnoses a genuine failure mode (propensity overfitting) for IPS-based learning over rich hypothesis classes.
- Strength: principled remedy via SN estimator with intuitive diagnostic Ŝ(h) (expected sum of weights = 1).
- Strength: practical algorithm (Norm-POEM) with variance-regularized CRM objective tailored to SN and feasible optimization.
- Strength: solid empirical gains and stability evidence across datasets and training sizes.
- Weakness: lacks a CRM-style finite-sample generalization bound for the SN estimator (Theorem 1 covers capped IPS only).
- Weakness: limited baselines — missing comparisons to doubly robust estimators, defensive mixtures, or weighted IS variants.
- Weakness: more discussion needed on SN bias–variance trade-off, when SN dominates, and sensitivity to clipping (M).
- Weakness: feedback δ modeled deterministically in Section 3; should formalize δ as stochastic to reflect noisy rewards/labels.
- Request: clarify and explicitly state loss rescaling to [−1,0] (or consistent convention) throughout experiments and claims about equivariance/boundedness.
- Request: cite prior discussion of IPS nonlinearity/equivariance (e.g., CRM [1]) when highlighting equivariance issues.
- Notational fixes requested: correct importance weight ratio order, use argmin for risk minimization, and consistently use hats for empirical estimates.
- Assumptions to state: h0 full support and use of clipping to ensure finite variance for SN estimator, and where results rely on them.
- Assessment — Quality: sound formulation and use of SN sampling; would be stronger with tighter finite-sample bounds.
- Assessment — Clarity: generally clear and organized but needs fixes on loss scaling, stochastic feedback modeling, and notation.
- Assessment — Originality: novel identification of propensity overfitting and SN-based learning objective as an extension of CRM.
- Assessment — Significance: relevant to practical BLBF; ideas likely to influence future counterfactual learning work.
- Pros: identifies impactful failure mode, offers principled remedy, shows consistent empirical gains, and provides a practical algorithm.
- Cons: missing finite-sample CRM bound for SN, limited comparisons to advanced off-policy estimators, and presentation inconsistencies.
- Post-rebuttal: loss-scaling clarified; reviewer’s view is positive and recommends weak accept.
- Scores: Quality 6/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10; Recommendation: Weak Accept.
