- Counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) learns in logged bandit settings to correct bias from how examples were generated.
- CRM is theoretically consistent but can overfit due to hypothesis picking up on individual examples' generation probabilities.
- The paper proposes a multiplicative (self-normalized) parameter to bound variance while preserving consistency.
- The proposed normalized counterfactual estimator mitigates the specific overfitting where hypotheses give undue support to logged data.
- Experimental results show the normalized method outperforms the non-normalized CRM.
- Overall assessment: strong paper and recommended for acceptance, with minor reservations.
