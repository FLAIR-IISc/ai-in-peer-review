- Identifies a significant limitation ("propensity overfitting") in counterfactual risk estimation for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Shows conventional unbiased counterfactual risk estimators can become arbitrarily biased in complex hypothesis spaces due to interaction of propensity weights and loss.
- Provides theoretical analysis and concrete examples documenting propensity overfitting and its degeneracies.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates to avoid propensity overfitting.
- Derives Norm-POEM, a practical learning algorithm for structured output prediction that incorporates the self-normalized estimator.
- Proves desirable properties of the self-normalized estimator (equivariance under loss translation, bounded within loss range, consistency).
- Empirically evaluates on multiple multi-label datasets (LibSVM) using the Supervised→Bandit methodology.
- Experimental results consistently show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across datasets and metrics.
- Demonstrates stability of propensity weight sum Ŝ(h) around its expected value of 1 when using the self-normalized estimator.
- Novel contribution: first to identify propensity overfitting in BLBF and to apply self-normalized estimators to this problem.
- References related work appropriately but could better connect to recent off-policy evaluation advances in reinforcement learning.
- Paper is technically sound, rigorous, well-structured, and clearly written with reproducible experimental details.
- Minor clarity issue: connection between the self-normalized estimator and the variance estimator in Section 6 could be made more explicit.
- Weakness: computational efficiency claims would benefit from detailed runtime comparisons.
- Weakness: theoretical analysis of the self-normalized estimator's variance properties could be expanded.
- Pro-acceptance: identifies a fundamental overlooked problem, provides a sound solution, strong empirical gains, clear writing, and practical significance.
- Con-acceptance: needs stronger links to off-policy RL literature, more runtime validation, and deeper variance analysis.
