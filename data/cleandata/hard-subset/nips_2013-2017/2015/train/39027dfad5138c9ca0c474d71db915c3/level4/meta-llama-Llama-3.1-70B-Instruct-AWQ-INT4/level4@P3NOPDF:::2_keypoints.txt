Paper proposes Norm-POEM for counterfactual learning (a specialization of multi-label learning) aiming to improve POEM.  
Experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and approaches CRF performance using full multi-label labels.  
Overall assessment: convincing work and potentially worth publication.  
Technical critique: notation/commentary around equation (2) is misleading—expectation is mischaracterized and the loss is not the expected quantity as described.  
The claim that (2) has an "anomaly" (non-invariance to loss translation) is technically incorrect based on the wrong expectation interpretation.  
Example 1 is based on the incorrect presumption and therefore does not hold.  
Practical concern: estimator (2) is fragile to its denominator π, which can be zero for previously unobserved samples.  
Methodological improvement: modified estimator (7) is preferable since it mollifies the effect of π via a double denominator.  
Authors should revise explanations around (2)/(7) to avoid confusion and preserve credibility.  
Suggestion: expand discussion of the relationship between counterfactual learning and multi-label learning and implications for general multi-label problems.  
Request: provide more experimental details (datasets, sample counts, class distributions) to clarify results and reproducibility.
