The paper "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning" identifies a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), specifically propensity overfitting, and proposes an alternative estimator that avoids this issue. The authors introduce a self-normalized risk estimator, which they use to develop a new learning algorithm called Norm-POEM for structured output prediction.

The main idea of the paper is closely related to previous work on BLBF and off-policy evaluation in reinforcement learning. The authors provide a clear summary of the existing approaches and their limitations, making it easy for readers to understand the context and the contributions of the paper. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed estimator and its properties.

The self-normalized risk estimator is a significant contribution to the field, as it addresses the propensity overfitting problem that can occur when using the conventional unbiased risk estimator. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the estimator's properties, including its equivariance and boundedness. They also show that the estimator is strongly consistent and approaches the desired expectation when the sample size is large.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed Norm-POEM algorithm in improving generalization performance and robustness compared to the conventional POEM algorithm. The authors also provide a detailed comparison of the two algorithms, including their computational efficiency and sensitivity to hyper-parameter tuning.

One minor criticism is that the notation and commentary surrounding the main equation (2) could be improved. Specifically, the loss is not the expected quantity, and the ensuing comment about the estimator's anomaly due to translation of the loss is technically incorrect. However, this does not affect the overall validity of the paper's contributions.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and detailed analysis of the proposed estimator and its properties. The experimental results are convincing, and the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of BLBF and off-policy evaluation.
