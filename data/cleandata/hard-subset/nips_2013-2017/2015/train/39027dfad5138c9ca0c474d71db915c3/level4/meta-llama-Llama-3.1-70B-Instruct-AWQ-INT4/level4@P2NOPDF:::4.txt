Other comments:

The setup for "batch learning from logged bandit feedback" in Section 3 appears to be incomplete. The feedback should be treated as a random variable rather than a deterministic function, as presented on line 110. This is because the partial feedback may come from a classification problem where the true labels are unknown, and the feedback is generated by trying a prediction and observing the loss. The true classification loss Delta(y',y) is unknown, and the feedback is Delta(y',y) for a given y'. However, in classification problems, labeling can be noisy, and there may not be a unique y' assigned to each x. This means that the same x and y can have different delta(x,y) values in the log. Therefore, delta(x,y) should be considered a random variable, and the setup in equation (1) should include an additional expectation over the randomness of delta to be more general.
