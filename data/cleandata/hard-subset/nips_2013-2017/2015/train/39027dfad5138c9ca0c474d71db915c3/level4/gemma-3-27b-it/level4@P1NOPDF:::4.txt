== Other comments ==
- I believe the setup for “batch learning from logged bandit feedback” in Section 3 (and in [1]) is missing a crucial element. The feedback should be modeled as a random variable, rather than the deterministic function presented on line 110. Here’s my reasoning: Imagine the partial feedback originates from a classification problem where true labels are unknown, and feedback is obtained by making a prediction and observing the resulting loss. Let's say there’s a true classification loss, Delta(y', y), and the feedback generation process works as follows: given an input x, we make a prediction y using h_0; a “god agent” also labels the input as y'; then, we’re informed of the incurred loss, Delta(y', y) – effectively, delta(x, y) becomes Delta(y', y) for the given y'. However, in classification tasks, labeling is often noisy, meaning a single x isn't necessarily assigned a unique y'. Consequently, we might encounter the same x and y in the log, but with different delta(x, y) values due to the "god agent" assigning different labels on different occasions. This suggests that delta(x, y) should generally be treated as a random variable. In the classification example, the mean of this random variable, fixed x & played y, should correspond to the expectation of Delta(y', y) given y' distributed according to p(y'|x), the true noisy labeling distribution. 
This consideration may not impact the risk estimator (2), but the setup in (1) representing the true risk should include an additional expectation over the randomness of delta for greater generality.
