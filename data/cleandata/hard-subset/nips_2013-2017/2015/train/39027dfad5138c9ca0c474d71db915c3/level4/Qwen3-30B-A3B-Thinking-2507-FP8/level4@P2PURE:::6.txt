

Counterfactual risk minimization is a technique for learning in bandit settings from logged data, addressing bias introduced by the logging process. While consistent, the paper demonstrates that this approach suffers from an overfitting problem: a hypothesis class may overfit to the generation probabilities of logged examples. The authors propose resolving this by introducing a multiplicative parameter that bounds variance while preserving consistency. Experimental results indicate that the normalized counterfactual estimator outperforms its non-normalized counterpart. The paper presents a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in logged bandit settings and addresses overfitting where hypotheses overfit to logged data by assigning excessive weight to examples with high generation probabilities. This is a strong contribution that warrants acceptance, though I have minor reservations.
