Summary
The paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant problem, for example in ad ranking. It identifies an overfitting issue in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a solution based on multiplicative control variates, which yields a biased estimator.

Quality
The work is high quality and well motivated. However, as a follow-up to [1], I would have expected a broader set of experiments and more detailed analysis of the optimization of the training objective (beyond the brief discussion around line 415).

Clarity
The presentation is clear and the paper is well written.

Originality
This is follow-up work on [1], but it modifies the risk estimator to be self-normalizing, which appears to make a substantial difference in the authors’ experiments.

Significance
Learning from logged data is an important problem. That said, it is difficult to assess how the substantial experimental gains reported here will translate to broader real-world applications.

Various Remarks
- Line 365: If multiple repetitions are run, please also report a measure of variance.
- The paper identifies a problem in the recently proposed CRM principle and introduces a corresponding solution.
- Overall, the paper is solid, well written, and both relevant and novel. I would have appreciated more extensive experiments and, in particular, applications beyond those in [1], including real-world deployments.
