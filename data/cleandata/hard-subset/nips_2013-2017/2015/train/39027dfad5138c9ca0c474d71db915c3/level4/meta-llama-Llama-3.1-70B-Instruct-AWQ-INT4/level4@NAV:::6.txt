This paper introduces a novel approach to counterfactual risk minimization, a technique designed to learn from logged data in a bandit setting by correcting for the bias inherent in the generation of logged examples. Although the method is consistent, it is susceptible to overfitting, where the hypothesis class may overfit based on the probability of generating an example. To address this issue, the authors propose a solution involving a multiplicative parameter that bounds variance while maintaining consistency. The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed multiplicative (or normalized) counterfactual technique over its non-normalized counterpart.

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field by developing a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting. It also offers insightful solutions to mitigate a specific form of overfitting, where the hypothesis may overfit due to stronger support for the logged data. Overall, this is a well-written and impactful paper that warrants acceptance, although some minor reservations remain.
