== Other comments ==
- The setup for “batch learning from logged bandit feedback” in Section 3 (and as presented in [1]) appears to overlook a crucial element: the feedback should be modeled as a random variable, rather than a deterministic function as stated on line 110. Consider a scenario where feedback derives from a classification problem with unknown labels, where loss is observed only through prediction and comparison to a true label. If we define Delta(y', y) as the true classification loss, the feedback delta(x, y) represents Delta(y', y) for a specific y' assigned to input x. However, labeling is often noisy, meaning a single input x and prediction y might have different delta(x, y) values in the log due to varying labels assigned by a “god agent.” Consequently, delta(x, y) should generally be treated as a random variable; for a fixed x and y, its mean should represent the expected value of Delta(y', y) given y' is distributed according to the true, noisy labeling distribution p(y'|x). While this may not impact the risk estimator (2), the setup in (1), relating to the true risk, should explicitly include an expectation over the randomness of delta to enhance generality.
- Line 170-171: I believe the correct expression should be h(y|x) / h_0(y|x). It would also be beneficial to acknowledge that the lack of linearity was previously identified in [1].
- Line 276: Given that the objective is risk minimization, “argmin” should be used instead of “argmax.”
- Line 319: For consistency with prior notation, replace vertical bars with hats.
