- Addresses learning from logged bandit data using counterfactual risk minimization (CRM).
- Shows CRM is consistent but vulnerable to a specific form of overfitting.
- Overfitting occurs when hypotheses are dominated by examples with low logging probabilities that receive large weights.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator with a multiplicative parameter to bound CRM estimator variance.
- The normalization mitigates overfitting while maintaining consistency.
- Experiments show the normalized estimator significantly outperforms the non-normalized CRM.
- Identifies an important practical issue for propensity-weighted estimators and offers an elegant, well-motivated remedy.
- Reviewer views the paper as a solid, valuable contribution and recommends acceptance.
