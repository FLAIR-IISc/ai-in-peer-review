The paper addresses a problem in evaluating hypotheses given observed data. Specifically, when dealing with non-deterministic hypotheses (e.g., those involving conditional distributions), estimating the loss under a new hypothesis can be approached by reweighting data points, as shown in the equation between (1) and (2).

The authors tackle a challenge related to, but distinct from, the issue of large variance in weights. They term this problem “propensity overfitting,” which appears to occur when there is insufficient exploration – that is, when, for a given *x*, very few or no *y* values are observed that align with the hypothesis being evaluated. Since a similar rationale explains the high variance of the weights, a more detailed discussion of the relationship between these two problems would be beneficial. 

I haven’t had sufficient time to delve into the solution's details or assess its novelty, but the core idea seems sensible.
