This article discusses the problem of learning from logged data, which is a highly relevant problem in areas such as advertising ranking. The article identifies an "overfitting" problem in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a solution based on multiplicative control variables (resulting in a biased estimator).

Quality

High quality and well-motivated. Since this is a follow-up work of [1], I expected some additional tests and some additional analysis on training objective optimization (more detailed than the paragraph at line 415).

Clarity

The presentation is clear and the article is well-written.

Originality

Follow-up work of [1], but with changes to normalize the risk estimator, which seems to have made a significant difference in the experiment conducted by the authors.

Significance

The problem of learning from logged data is relevant, but it is difficult to say how sufficient experimental improvements translate into real-world applications.

Various comments

365: If you have already done several iterations, please include some measurements of variance as well. This article identifies a problem in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and presents a solution to the problem. Overall, the article is challenging, well-written, and the work is relevant and new. However, I would appreciate some additional tests and especially the inclusion of some real-world applications instead of the real-world applications mentioned in [1].
