### Summary
This work addresses the challenge of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a problem of significant practical importance in domains like ad ranking. The authors identify an "overfitting" issue within the recently introduced Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and propose a solution that leverages multiplicative control variates, resulting in a biased estimator.

### Quality
The work is well-motivated and of high quality. However, as this research builds directly on [1], a more extensive set of experiments would be beneficial. The paper would also be strengthened by a deeper analysis of the training objective's optimization, expanding on the details provided in the paragraph at line 415.

### Clarity
The manuscript is well-written and the presentation is clear throughout.

### Originality
This research extends the work presented in [1]. Its primary contribution is the introduction of a self-normalizing risk estimator, a modification that demonstrates a significant performance impact in the authors' experiments.

### Significance
The addressed problem of learning from logged data is highly relevant. However, it is unclear how the substantial performance gains observed in the experiments will transfer to more complex, real-world applications.

### Various Remarks
Line 365: Given that the experiments were repeated, reporting a measure of variance is essential. In summary, the paper effectively pinpoints a shortcoming in the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and proposes a viable solution. The work is solid, clearly presented, relevant, and makes a novel contribution. My primary critique is the scope of the evaluation; I would have liked to see more extensive experiments, particularly on new applications not covered in [1] and on real-world datasets.
