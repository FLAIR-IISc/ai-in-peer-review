The commentary and notation related to the main equation (2) seem incorrect or at least misleading:

One expectation is that an expression of the type \sum f(x) p(x) is expected. The expected expression, f(x), is everything except p(x), which is the probability measure.

In equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected expression! Therefore, the following comment (page 2, second paragraph) that there is an "inconsistency" in (2) because the translation of the loss is not invariant is technically incorrect. Again, the subsequent example 1 is based on this incorrect concept - which, unsurprisingly, is not true.

In practice, the assumed parameter in (2) is weaker than its denominator, pi, which can be 0 if the sample has never been observed before. The modified parameter in (7) seems better in principle because the effects of pi are mitigated by the double denominator.

I truly hope that the authors would be kind enough to correct their commentary if the paper is accepted.

Experimental results show that Norm-POEM performs better than POEM in all datasets and is not far from a CRF using complete multi-label information. This paper presents a method for "counterfactual learning," which is a special form of multi-label learning. In multi-label learning, a ground-truth label for a sample provides a 0/1 label for each class; here, instead, only one label is provided for a class (i.e., the robber response). However, the above is only a fairness proposition. In fact, this paper has a specific goal: to improve a parameter (POEM) recently presented by ICML 2015 [1]. I would say that, overall, the paper is credible and worthy of publication, but there are some comments that I raise in the context of the authors' comments.
