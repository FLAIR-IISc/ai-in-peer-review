- Equation (2) and its notation are incorrect/misleading: the expectation is misrepresented (p(x) is a measure, not the expected quantity) and the loss in (2) is not the expected quantity.  
- The claim that (2) exhibits an “anomaly” due to non-invariance under translations of the loss is technically wrong.  
- Example 1 is based on the incorrect premise about (2) and therefore does not hold.  
- The estimator in (2) is practically fragile because its denominator, πi, can be zero if a sample was never observed under the prior.  
- The modified estimator in (7) is preferable in principle because it tempers the influence of πi via a double denominator.  
- Reviewer urges the authors to revise their commentary if the paper is accepted.  
- Experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and approaches a fully supervised CRF.  
- The paper frames counterfactual learning as a specialization of multi-label learning and mainly aims to improve the POEM estimator from ICML 2015.  
- Overall assessment: convincing and worthy of publication, subject to the caveats noted above.
