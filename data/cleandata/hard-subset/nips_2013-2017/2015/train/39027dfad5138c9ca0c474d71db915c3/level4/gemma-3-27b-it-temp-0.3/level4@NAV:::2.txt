The discussion surrounding the core equation (2) appears inaccurate or, at best, presents a misleading interpretation. An expectation value is fundamentally represented as a sum of the form \sum f(x) p(x), where f(x) represents the quantity being expected and p(x) is the probability measure. In equation (2), the loss function clearly does not function as the expected quantity. Consequently, the assertion on page 2, second paragraph, regarding an “anomaly” in equation (2) due to its lack of translation invariance of the loss is technically flawed. The subsequent Example 1 is built upon this incorrect premise and, predictably, yields invalid results.

From a practical perspective, the estimator in (2) exhibits sensitivity to its denominator, π, which can equal zero when a sample is absent from the prior. The revised estimator in (7) appears more robust, as the influence of π is mitigated by the double denominator. 

I encourage the authors to reconsider their commentary in light of these points should the paper be accepted for publication.

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM consistently surpasses POEM across all datasets and achieves performance comparable to a CRF leveraging complete multi-label information. This work introduces a method for “counterfactual learning,” which can be understood as a specialized application within the broader field of multi-label learning. While traditional multi-label learning utilizes 0/1 ground-truth labels for each class, this approach focuses on bandit feedback – a single label per sample. However, this framing serves primarily as contextual motivation. The primary objective of this paper is to refine an estimator (POEM) originally introduced in [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, the paper presents a compelling contribution and is suitable for publication, contingent upon addressing the detailed comments provided in the “Comments to Authors” section.
