- Proposes a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Identifies "propensity overfitting" as a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator.
- Shows the self-normalized estimator avoids propensity overfitting and is more robust in practice.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the problem and proposed solution.
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed estimator's properties.
- Empirical results on multiple multi-label classification datasets show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM.
- Clearly situates the work within prior BLBF and counterfactual estimation literature.
- Assumes substantial background knowledge in BLBF and counterfactual estimation, limiting accessibility for non-experts.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to specific multi-label datasets, so generalization to other domains is unclear.
- Original contribution: a new estimator addressing a significant flaw in the conventional estimator.
- Potentially significant impact on counterfactual estimation and learning in BLBF applications.
- Overall high quality, well-organized, and recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- Suggests authors address accessibility assumptions and broaden experimental validation.
