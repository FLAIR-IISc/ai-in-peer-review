The authors’ explanation and notation surrounding the main equation (2) appear inaccurate or, at best, misleading. An expectation takes the form \sum f(x) p(x), where f(x) represents the quantity being expected and p(x) is the probability measure. Consequently, in equation (2), the loss function is not the expected quantity. Therefore, the claim on page 2, second paragraph, that equation (2) exhibits an “anomaly” due to a lack of invariance to translation of the loss is technically incorrect. Example 1, which follows, is predicated on this flawed assumption and, predictably, does not hold.

From a practical standpoint, the estimator in (2) is sensitive to its denominator, π, which can be zero when a sample has not been observed under the prior. The modified estimator in (7) appears more robust, as the double denominator mitigates the impact of π. 

I encourage the authors to reconsider their commentary should this paper be accepted.

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across all datasets and achieves performance close to that of a CRF utilizing complete multi-label information. This paper addresses “counterfactual learning,” a specialized instance of multi-label learning. While traditional multi-label learning employs 0/1 labels for each class in the ground truth, counterfactual learning receives only the label for a single class, mirroring bandit feedback. However, this framing serves primarily as motivation. The core contribution of this work is to refine an estimator (POEM) originally presented in reference [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, the paper is well-supported and suitable for publication, contingent upon addressing the concerns detailed in the Comments to Authors section.
