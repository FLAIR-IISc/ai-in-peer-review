- Introduces a counterfactual risk minimization technique for learning from biased logged bandit data.  
- Corrects for generation bias in logged examples and is consistent in estimation.  
- Identifies an overfitting issue where hypotheses can overfit via generation probabilities of examples.  
- Proposes a multiplicative (normalization) parameter to bound variance while preserving consistency.  
- Develops self-normalized estimators for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting.  
- Experimental results show the normalized (multiplicative) method outperforms the non-normalized one.  
- Provides useful insights into overfitting caused by models having stronger support for logged data.  
- Reviewer views the paper as a valuable contribution and recommends acceptance, though not with full certainty.
