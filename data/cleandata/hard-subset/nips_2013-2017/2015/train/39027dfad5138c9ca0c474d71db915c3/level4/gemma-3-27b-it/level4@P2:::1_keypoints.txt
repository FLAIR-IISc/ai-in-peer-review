- Identifies "propensity overfitting" in counterfactual risk estimators for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Shows existing unbiased estimators can be misleadingly optimistic due to overfitting to propensity scores, especially with limited action exploration.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator using control variates and introduces the Norm-POEM algorithm to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Derivation is theoretically sound and grounded in importance sampling and variance-reduction literature.  
- Empirical results demonstrate Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on multi-label classification tasks and stabilizes the estimated risk Ŝ(ĥ).  
- Paper situates work well within causal inference, covariate shift, and off-policy evaluation prior work.  
- Practical considerations are addressed: computational costs, hyperparameter tuning, optimization, and code availability.  
- Novel contribution: highlights a distinct overfitting phenomenon beyond the known high-variance issue.  
- Weakness: needs deeper discussion clarifying how propensity overfitting differs from standard variance-reduction issues.  
- Weakness: Section 4 example lacks sufficient detail on why R̂(hoverfit) becomes highly negative and the role of distinct xi.  
- Weakness: claim that prior works don’t use techniques like clipping may be overstated and needs more nuance.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to specific multi-label classification tasks; broader BLBF scenarios would strengthen generalizability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important, previously unrecognized problem with a principled, empirically validated solution and reproducible code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: requires clarifications on relation to variance reduction, the illustrative example, and broader experimental validation.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarifications and broaden experiments.
