- Identifies a severe issue in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF, termed "propensity overfitting," causing degenerate risk estimates in complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator that avoids propensity overfitting by using control variates.  
- Derives Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on CRM with the self-normalized estimator for structured output prediction.  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM outperforms the conventional estimator on multiple multi-label classification tasks.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical and empirical analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and the proposed solution.  
- Clear, well-written exposition with consistent technical notation.  
- Use of control variates to derive the self-normalized estimator is a novel contribution.  
- Addresses an important problem in BLBF with potential broad impact on related applications.  
- Empirical evaluations are extensive and convincing, supporting the claims.  
- Assumes reader familiarity with BLBF and CRM, which may make the paper hard to follow for newcomers.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting, technically sound work; suggests acceptance.
