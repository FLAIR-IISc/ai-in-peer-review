The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that effectively addresses the propensity overfitting problem. Building upon the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the authors present a significantly improved algorithm.

Quality: The research discussed in the paper is of above-average quality. The proposed extensions result in substantial improvements to the original POEM algorithm, demonstrating the effectiveness of the authors' approach.

Clarity: The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and easy to follow. The authors successfully convey their ideas, making the content accessible to readers.

Originality: The paper offers a novel formulation of the original POEM algorithm for the batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) problem. The contributions are significant and original, providing a fresh perspective on the topic.

Significance: The results presented in the paper are intriguing and warrant further research. Notably, the proposed algorithm outperforms the original POEM in terms of speed, despite incorporating additional regularization mechanisms. This improved performance suggests that the algorithm may have widespread applications in the near future.

Overall, this is a well-written paper that effectively communicates its ideas. The clarity and readability of the text make it a pleasure to read, and the contributions are substantial and impactful.
