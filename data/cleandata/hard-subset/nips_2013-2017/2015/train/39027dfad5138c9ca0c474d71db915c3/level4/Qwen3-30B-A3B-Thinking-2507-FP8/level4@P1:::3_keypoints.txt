- Identifies "propensity overfitting" in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) where unbiased CRM estimators become severely biased in complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Shows the conventional counterfactual risk estimator can lead learning algorithms to prefer hypotheses with arbitrarily low estimated risk.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (Equation 7) to resolve these anomalies and avoid propensity overfitting.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm for structured output prediction using the self-normalized estimator.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical support (Theorem 2) showing strong consistency and desirable properties (equivariance, boundedness) of the self-normalized estimator.  
- Demonstrates Norm-POEM consistently outperforms prior POEM on multiple multi-label classification datasets using the Supervised→Bandit evaluation protocol.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and well-designed, convincingly showing empirical improvements are substantial, not marginal.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and uses illustrative examples that make the technical points accessible.  
- Contribution is original: identifies a previously unrecognized problem in BLBF and offers a simple, theoretically grounded fix.  
- Significance is high for practical online systems that rely on logged bandit feedback, as the approach improves generalization and robustness.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, convincing empirical results, clear writing, and practical relevance.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient analysis of optimization challenges for Norm-POEM (variance estimator not amenable to stochastic optimization), limited exploration of alternatives to L-BFGS, and lack of sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (M and λ).  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper and recommended for acceptance.
