- Counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) learns from logged bandit data to correct for bias and is consistent.
- The paper shows CRM can overfit by exploiting the generation probability (propensity) of individual examples.
- Authors introduce a multiplicative parameter that bounds variance while preserving consistency.
- Experiments: normalized (multiplicative) CRM significantly outperforms the non-normalized variant.
- Reviewer: strong paper addressing a meaningful challenge and should be accepted.
- Reviewer expresses some uncertainty about the generality of the overfitting analysis.
