- Identifies propensity overfitting in the counterfactual risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- BLBF setting is practically important for applications like ad placement and recommender systems.
- Shows conventional estimator can produce degenerate risk estimates; self-normalized estimator avoids this.
- Provides theoretical guarantees for the proposed estimator.
- Demonstrates empirical improvements from the self-normalized estimator across datasets.
- Paper is well-structured with a clear introduction linking BLBF and counterfactual reasoning.
- Effectively situates work within causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation literature.
- Presents a strong, clarifying example (Example 1) illustrating propensity overfitting.
- Derivation of the self-normalized estimator builds on importance sampling and control variates and is logically presented.
- Connects the method to the CRM principle and motivates the Norm-POEM algorithm.
- Thorough empirical validation using the Supervised → Bandit methodology.
- Ablation studies and analyses (e.g., Ŝ(ĥ) in Table 2) support the method’s benefits.
- Acknowledges computational costs and shows robustness gains often outweigh them, sometimes yielding faster convergence.
- Weakness: theoretical discussion of consistency could be more detailed.
- Weakness: sensitivity and practical selection of hyperparameter M (clipping threshold) not fully explored; adaptive strategies not provided.
- Weakness: limited comparison to other BLBF algorithms (e.g., doubly robust estimators).
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses critical issue, offers a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution, clear presentation, strong experiments, and practical relevance.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete consistency discussion, insufficient hyperparameter analysis, and limited baseline comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, significant contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions.
