- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback, identifies CRM overfitting, and proposes multiplicative control variates yielding a biased self-normalizing risk estimator.  
- High-quality, well-motivated work that builds logically on prior work but would benefit from more experiments and deeper optimization analysis.  
- Exceptionally well-written with clear structure and precise technical exposition.  
- Novelty: main contribution is a self-normalizing risk estimator that empirically outperforms prior approaches.  
- Significance: problem is relevant, but real-world applicability and deployment relevance remain insufficiently justified.  
- Major: reports mean results without variance/confidence intervals—statistical significance is unclear.  
- Major: experiments largely replicate prior settings; needs validation on real-world or public ad-click datasets to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Major: the bias introduced by the self-normalizing estimator is noted but not analyzed—need discussion of how bias scales with dataset size/logging policies and its effect on regret.  
- Minor: optimization details (algorithm, convergence criteria) are insufficiently described for the biased objective.  
- Minor: clarify what is meant by "overfitting" (overfitting to logging policy vs. noise in logged data).  
- Minor: add an intuitive explanation for why multiplicative control variates resolve the overfitting issue beyond the math.  
- Overall: solid contribution with meaningful empirical gains, but requires variance metrics, broader experimental scope, and clearer bias analysis to strengthen claims of real-world impact.
