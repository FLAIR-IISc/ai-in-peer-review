Problem setting and contribution
Given data generated under one hypothesis, we may wish to evaluate a different hypothesis. When these hypotheses are stochastic (e.g., conditional distributions), we can estimate the loss under the target hypothesis by reweighting the observed data points (importance weighting), as in the equation between (1) and (2). The paper addresses a related but somewhat different issue from the classic large-variance problem of importance weights, which the authors call “propensity overfitting.” From the examples, this appears to arise when exploration is insufficient: for a given x, we observe very few or even no y values that correspond to the new hypothesis being evaluated. Because a similar mechanism also causes large-variance weights, it would be helpful if the paper clarified in more detail how propensity overfitting relates to, differs from, or subsumes the standard large-variance problem.

I did not have time to verify all technical details or check the novelty thoroughly, but the core idea seems sensible.
