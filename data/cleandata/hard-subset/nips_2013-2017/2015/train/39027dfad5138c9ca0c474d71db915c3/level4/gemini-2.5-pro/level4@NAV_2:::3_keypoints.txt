- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and its relevance to deployed systems.
- Identifies a novel failure mode called "Propensity Overfitting" where CRM/IPS objectives can be minimized by exploiting propensity weights rather than fitting true losses.
- Demonstrates Propensity Overfitting with a compelling illustrative example.
- Proposes replacing unbiased IPS with a self-normalized estimator using the sum of propensity weights as a control variate.
- Shows the self-normalized estimator is translation-equivariant, bounded by observed loss range, and consistent, avoiding the overfitting anomaly.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, which combines the self-normalized estimator with a variance regularizer within the CRM framework.
- Positions the work as a direct and corrective extension of Swaminathan & Joachims (2015) POEM.
- Technically sound and well-motivated application of importance-sampling ideas to off-policy learning.
- Strong theoretical analysis establishing desirable estimator properties.
- Exceptionally clear writing and logical organization.
- Empirical results convincingly show Norm-POEM mitigates propensity overfitting and improves generalization versus POEM.
- Ablation studies support robustness to loss translation and the benefit of variance regularization.
- Evaluation scope is limited to semi-synthetic datasets from the original POEM paper; lacks broader or real-world logged bandit benchmarks.
- Optimization discussion is brief despite non-convexity introduced by the self-normalized estimator; more on convergence and sensitivity is needed.
- Experimental variance (e.g., standard deviation or error) is not reported for results averaged over runs.
- Overall: solid, valuable contribution likely to influence off-policy evaluation and learning, with primary weaknesses in empirical validation details.
