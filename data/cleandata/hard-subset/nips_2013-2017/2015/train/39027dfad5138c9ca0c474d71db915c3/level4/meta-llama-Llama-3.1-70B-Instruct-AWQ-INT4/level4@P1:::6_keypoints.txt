- Introduces a technique for counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) to address severe overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Identifies a propensity overfitting issue with the conventional unbiased risk estimator that can produce degenerate risk estimates.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using a multiplicative control variate to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Presents a new algorithm, Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models (Norm-POEM).
- Provides a clear motivation and explanation of the propensity overfitting problem and its implications for BLBF.
- Offers a thorough theoretical analysis, including a proof of strong consistency for the self-normalized estimator.
- Experimental results show Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting and achieves better generalization than conventional CRM.
- Paper is well-written with a comprehensive related-work review and a clear, well-designed experimental setup.
- Includes a detailed analysis of Norm-POEM’s computational efficiency, relevant for practical use.
- Weakness: assumes substantial background knowledge in CRM and BLBF, which may hinder accessibility for non-experts.
- Weakness: lacks sufficient discussion of potential limitations, notably sensitivity to hyperparameter tuning.
- Overall recommendation: strengths (novelty, theory, experiments) outweigh weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
