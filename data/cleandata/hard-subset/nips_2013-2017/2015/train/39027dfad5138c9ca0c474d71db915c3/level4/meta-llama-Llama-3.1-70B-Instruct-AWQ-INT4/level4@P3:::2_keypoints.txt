- Novel contribution: introduces a self-normalized estimator to improve counterfactual risk estimation in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Identified problem: "propensity overfitting" in conventional unbiased risk estimators, caused by non-equivariance to translations of the loss.
- Proposed fix: self-normalized estimator using a multiplicative control variate that is equivariant to loss translations.
- Theoretical benefit: self-normalized estimator has lower variance than the conventional estimator.
- Algorithm: derives Norm-POEM, a CRM-based learning algorithm that uses the self-normalized estimator.
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and approaches CRF performance using full multi-label information.
- Robustness: self-normalized estimator is more resistant to propensity overfitting than the conventional estimator.
- Presentation: paper is well-written with clear explanations and thorough experimental analysis.
- Notation issue: commentary around main equation (2) is misleading—loss is not the expected quantity and Example 1 builds on this incorrect assumption.
- Requested clarifications: need more background on CRM and POEM and a more detailed explanation of the self-normalized estimator and its properties.
- Verdict: convincing paper, recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- Review score: 8/10.
