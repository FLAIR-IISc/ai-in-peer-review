This paper presents a novel method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback, effectively addressing the propensity overfitting issue. Building upon the foundation laid by Swaminathan and Joachims (2015), the proposed algorithm demonstrates substantial performance gains.

**Quality:** The research presented is of high quality, with the extensions to the original POEM algorithm yielding significant improvements in training performance.

**Clarity:** The paper is, for the most part, well-written and readily understandable. 

**Originality:** The work offers a significantly new formulation of POEM specifically tailored for the batch learning from bandit feedback problem, representing a substantial and original contribution to the field.

**Significance:** The results are compelling and warrant further investigation. Notably, the proposed algorithm achieves faster training times than the original POEM, despite incorporating additional regularization. This efficiency suggests potential for broad adoption in practical applications. 

Overall, this is a strong paper, clearly presented and well-articulated.
