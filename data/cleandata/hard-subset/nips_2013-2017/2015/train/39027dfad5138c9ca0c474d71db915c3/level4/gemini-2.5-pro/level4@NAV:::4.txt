A point requiring clarification in the rebuttal concerns the range of the delta parameter. Reference [1] (Section 4.1) suggests that the CRM approach is principled for delta within the range [-1, 0], prompting them to rescale general losses accordingly. == Other comments ==
-   The formulation for "batch learning from logged bandit feedback" in Section 3 appears to be missing a key element. The feedback, delta(x,y), is presented as a deterministic function (line 110), whereas it seems more general to model it as a random variable. For instance, consider a classification task where feedback is the loss from a prediction. If the ground-truth labeling is noisy, the same input `x` and action `y` could yield different loss values on different occasions in the log. This is because the underlying "true" label `y'` could vary. In such a case, delta(x,y) for a fixed `x` and `y` would be a random variable whose mean is the expectation over the true noisy labeling distribution p(y'|x). While this may not alter the risk estimator in (2), the true risk formulation in (1) should ideally be presented with an additional expectation over the randomness of delta to be more comprehensive.
-   Lines 170-171: The ratio appears to be inverted; it should likely be h(y|x) / h_0(y|x). Additionally, it would be appropriate to credit [1] for previously pointing out the lack of linearity.
-   Line 276: Given that the objective is risk minimization, "argmin" should be used instead of "argmax".
-   Line 319: For notational consistency with the rest of the paper, the bars should be replaced with hats.
