The commentary and notation around the main equation (2) appear incorrect or at least misleading. An expectation has the form ∑ f(x) p(x): the quantity being averaged is f(x), whereas p(x) is the probability measure. In equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity. Therefore, the statement on page 2, second paragraph—that (2) has an “anomaly” because it is not invariant to translating the loss—is technically incorrect. Likewise, Example 1 is built on this faulty premise and, unsurprisingly, does not hold.

Practically, the estimator in (2) is vulnerable to its denominator, pi, which can be 0 if the sample has never been observed in the prior. The modified estimator in (7) is preferable in principle because the influence of pi is attenuated via the double denominator. I hope the authors will revise their commentary accordingly if the paper is accepted.

The experimental results indicate that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and is close to a CRF trained with full multi-label information. The paper studies “counterfactual learning,” a learning setup that is a specialization of multi-label learning: in standard multi-label learning, each sample has a 0/1 label for every class, whereas here only one class’s label is observed (bandit feedback). However, this serves mainly as motivation. In effect, the paper’s concrete goal is to improve the estimator (POEM) recently introduced in reference [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, the paper is convincing and worthy of publication, with the remarks noted in the Comments to authors.
