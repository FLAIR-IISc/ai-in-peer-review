- Problem setting: Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback (BLBF) and reliance on CRM/POEM with IPS estimators.  
- Key failure mode: Identification of "Propensity Overfitting" where models exploit propensity weights to get artificially low empirical risk.  
- Mechanism of failure: Models can up-weight negative-loss examples or zero-out positive-loss examples, producing severely biased risk estimates and poor generalization.  
- Proposed solution: Replace unbiased IPS estimator with a self-normalized risk estimator derived via multiplicative control variates (ratio of weighted losses to weighted propensities).  
- Estimator properties: Equivariant to loss translations, bounded by observed loss range, and strongly consistent, preventing propensity overfitting.  
- Algorithm: Introduction of Norm-POEM (Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models) based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical results: Extensive multi-label experiments show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM, is more robust, and often converges faster.  
- Technical quality: High—sound theoretical analysis and well-designed experiments, including supervised→bandit evaluation protocol.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally clear writing, strong motivation (Section 4 example), and useful distinction between loss overfitting and propensity overfitting.  
- Originality: Novel application of self-normalized estimators to solve a newly identified failure mode in counterfactual learning (not merely incremental).  
- Significance: High practical impact; findings and Norm-POEM are likely to influence off-policy evaluation and learning practice.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel problem identification, principled solution, strong empirical validation, practical impact, and exemplary clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Minor caveat that self-normalized estimators are known in statistics and the work uses approximate variance estimation and non-convex optimization (standard issues).  
- Recommendation: Strong accept—paper is significant, well-supported, and practically valuable.
