== Other comments ==  
- The setup for "batch learning from logged bandit feedback" in Section 3 (and [1]) omits that the feedback $\delta(x,y)$ should be treated as a random variable, not a deterministic function (as stated on line 110). This is critical in settings like classification with noisy labels: for a fixed input $x$ and prediction $y$, $\delta(x,y)$ arises from a true loss $\Delta(y',y)$ where $y'$ is drawn from $p(y'|x)$ (the noisy labeling distribution). Thus, $\delta(x,y)$ is random with $\mathbb{E}[\delta(x,y) \mid x,y] = \mathbb{E}_{y' \sim p(y'|x)}[\Delta(y',y)]$. While this does not affect the risk estimator (2), the true risk in (1) should include an expectation over this randomness to be general.  
- Lines 170–171: The expression should be $h(y|x) / h_0(y|x)$, not the reverse. It is also worth noting that the non-linearity of this ratio was already highlighted in [1].  
- Line 276: Use $\arg\min$ instead of $\arg\max$ since the goal is risk minimization.  
- Line 319: Replace bars with hats ($\hat{\cdot}$) for notation consistency with prior conventions.
