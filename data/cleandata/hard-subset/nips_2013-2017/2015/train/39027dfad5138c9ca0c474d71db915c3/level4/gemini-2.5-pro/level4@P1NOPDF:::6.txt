This paper addresses the problem of learning from logged data in a bandit setting using counterfactual risk minimization (CRM). The authors first show that while CRM is a consistent estimation technique, it is susceptible to a specific form of overfitting. This issue arises when the learned hypothesis is overly influenced by examples with low generation probabilities under the logging policy, which are assigned large weights by the estimator.

To address this problem, the authors propose a novel self-normalized estimator. This approach introduces a multiplicative parameter that effectively bounds the variance of the CRM estimator, thereby mitigating the risk of overfitting while crucially maintaining consistency. The experimental results demonstrate that this normalized counterfactual technique significantly outperforms its non-normalized counterpart.

Overall, this paper clearly identifies an important practical issue with a widely used technique and offers an elegant, well-motivated solution. The work presents a valuable contribution to the field of counterfactual learning by highlighting a form of overfitting specific to propensity-weighted estimators and providing an effective remedy. This is a solid paper, and I recommend it for acceptance.
