This paper proposes a novel technique for counterfactual learning in the logged bandit setting, addressing a significant limitation of existing methods. The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, namely propensity overfitting, which can lead to degenerate risk estimates. To overcome this issue, they introduce a self-normalized estimator that avoids this problem and provides a more robust estimate of the risk.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem of propensity overfitting is clearly explained. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the issue, including a theoretical explanation and empirical evidence. The proposed self-normalized estimator is well-justified, and its advantages over the conventional estimator are clearly demonstrated.

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering multiple datasets and comparing the proposed method, Norm-POEM, to the conventional method, POEM. The results show that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM, demonstrating the effectiveness of the self-normalized estimator in avoiding propensity overfitting.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the technical details. The notation is consistent, and the mathematical derivations are easy to follow. The related work section is thorough, and the authors provide a clear discussion of the contributions of their work.

Strengths:

* The paper addresses a significant limitation of existing counterfactual learning methods.
* The self-normalized estimator is well-justified, and its advantages over the conventional estimator are clearly demonstrated.
* The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, and the results show that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the technical details.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a basic understanding of counterfactual learning and the logged bandit setting. While the authors provide a brief introduction, readers without prior knowledge may find it challenging to follow.
* The experimental evaluation could be improved by including additional baselines or comparing the proposed method to other state-of-the-art techniques.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper addresses a significant limitation of existing counterfactual learning methods.
* The proposed self-normalized estimator is well-justified, and its advantages over the conventional estimator are clearly demonstrated.
* The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, and the results show that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM.

Arguments for rejection:

* The paper assumes a basic understanding of counterfactual learning and the logged bandit setting, which may limit its accessibility to readers without prior knowledge.
* The experimental evaluation could be improved by including additional baselines or comparing the proposed method to other state-of-the-art techniques.

Overall, I believe that the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of counterfactual learning and should be accepted. However, I would like to see the authors address the weaknesses mentioned above, particularly by providing additional context for readers without prior knowledge and improving the experimental evaluation.
