Identifies propensity overfitting in the standard counterfactual risk estimator for BLBF as a significant problem.

Proposes a self-normalized estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting.

Introduces Norm-POEM (Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models) which consistently outperforms the conventional estimator on multi-label classification.

Effectively motivates BLBF and connects the work to causal inference, domain adaptation, and reinforcement learning.

Related work appropriately positions the paper and highlights distinctions between propensity overfitting and loss overfitting.

Technical presentation is strong: problem formulation, derivation of the estimator, and theoretical results (Theorems 1 & 2) are well-defined.

Norm-POEM’s connection to Conditional Random Fields is clearly explained.

Experimental setup follows established methodologies and demonstrates consistent empirical gains.

Code release improves reproducibility and encourages further research.

Needs clearer discussion of how propensity overfitting relates to high-variance importance weights and existing variance-reduction techniques.

Section 4’s illustrative example should be made more intuitive, especially the “R̂(hoverfit) should be even smaller than -2?” statement.

Should explicitly acknowledge and compare to variance-reduction methods (e.g., clipping) and discuss their limitations relative to the proposed approach.

Strengths: novel problem identification, sound theory, empirical validation, clear writing, and available code.

Weaknesses: limited discussion of variance-reduction literature and a less-clear illustrative example.

Arguments for acceptance: important problem, theoretically sound solution, empirical support, and clear presentation.

Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis of links to variance-reduction techniques and clarity issues in the example.

Overall recommendation: accept.
