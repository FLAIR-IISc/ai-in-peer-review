- Proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) addressing propensity overfitting.  
- Builds on Swaminathan & Joachims (2015) and provides a significantly improved algorithm.  
- Research quality is above average with meaningful extensions to POEM.  
- Identifies a severe flaw in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in BLBF.  
- Introduces a Self-Normalized Risk Estimator that is more robust and avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Presents a new formulation called Norm-POEM that addresses the propensity overfitting issue.  
- Norm-POEM is more effective and efficient than the original POEM.  
- Experimental results show Norm-POEM is faster despite additional regularization mechanisms.  
- Paper is clearly written and accessible to ML and BLBF readers.  
- Contributions are significantly original and likely to have lasting impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel solution, thorough evaluation, clear writing, and original contributions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: perceived limited novelty over prior work and potentially insufficient experimental breadth.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses and the paper is a good candidate for acceptance.
