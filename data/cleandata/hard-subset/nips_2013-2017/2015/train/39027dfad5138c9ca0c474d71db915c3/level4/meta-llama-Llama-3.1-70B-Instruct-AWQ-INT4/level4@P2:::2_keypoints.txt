- Proposes a novel method for counterfactual learning from logged bandit feedback (a special case of multi-label learning).  
- Identifies "propensity overfitting": conventional counterfactual risk estimators can be severely biased when hypothesis spaces allow overfitting of propensity weights.  
- Introduces a self-normalized risk estimator that provably avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Derives Norm-POEM, a new CRM-based learning algorithm using the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and approaches CRF performance that uses full labels.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of problem, solution, and empirical evaluation.  
- Comparison with POEM and CRF is informative and convincing.  
- Minor but important issues in notation/commentary around equation (2): the description of the expected quantity and probability measure appears incorrect.  
- The example illustrating the estimator anomaly (translation of the loss) is technically incorrect due to the mistaken presumption in equation (2).  
- Paper would benefit from clearer exposition of the relationship between the proposed approach and other multi-label learning methods.  
- Originality: the self-normalized risk estimator is a novel contribution.  
- Significance: addresses a substantial problem in counterfactual learning and has practical potential to improve methods.  
- Recommendation: publishable after addressing the minor notation/commentary issues in equation (2) and clarifying connections to multi-label learning.
