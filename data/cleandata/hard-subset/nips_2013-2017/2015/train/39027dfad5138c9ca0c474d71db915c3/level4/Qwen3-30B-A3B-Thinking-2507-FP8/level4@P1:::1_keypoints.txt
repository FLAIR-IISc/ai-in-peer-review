- Identifies a critical flaw in standard counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF called "propensity overfitting."  
- Propensity overfitting occurs when empirical risk minimization minimizes sum of propensity weights Ŝ(h) instead of true risk, producing unbounded/bad estimates.  
- Shows conventional unbiased estimators (e.g., IPS) and even clipped variants can produce severely biased risk under ERM in complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Formally defines the phenomenon and demonstrates it violates loss equivariance.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator R̂_SN(h) to resolve propensity overfitting.  
- Proves theoretical guarantees for R̂_SN: strong consistency, equivariance under loss translation, and boundedness within loss range.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new algorithm for structured prediction built on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical results across multi-label datasets show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms prior POEM in generalization and robustness.  
- Experiments demonstrate avoidance of propensity overfitting and faster convergence despite higher per-iteration cost.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical identification and separation of propensity overfitting from variance-related issues.  
- Strength: thorough experimental design (Supervised→Bandit protocol, held-out validation, ablations) and reproducible code.  
- Weakness: overstates prior work by implying most BLBF used only the unbiased estimator; clipped estimators used in practice and are biased but different.  
- Weakness: Example 1 (Section 4) is confusingly explained and needs correction about the risk value when n ≪ k.  
- Weakness: understates computational cost of Norm-POEM’s variance estimator (Equation 8) despite empirical compensation.  
- Overall: novel, significant contribution with strong theoretical and empirical support; recommendation to accept.
