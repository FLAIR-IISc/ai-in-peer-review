- Proposes an improvement to the POEM estimator for counterfactual/multi-label learning; aim is clear and experiments show performance gains.  
- Major theoretical concern: the authors misinterpret equation (2) and its commentary on translation invariance.  
- Correct expectation form is ∑ f(x)p(x); equation (2) estimates the loss f(x) itself, not an expected quantity.  
- Therefore the claimed lack of translation invariance and Example 1 do not logically follow.  
- Practical problem: estimator (2) is sensitive to denominator π_i, which can be zero and cause instability.  
- The modified estimator (7) uses a double denominator that smooths π_i and reduces instability.  
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and nears CRF performance that uses full multi-label information.  
- Recommendation: paper is convincing and publishable provided the authors correct the theoretical inaccuracies around equation (2) and clarify the expected-value interpretation.
