- Counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) addresses bias in logged bandit data and is consistent.  
- The paper identifies an overfitting issue where hypotheses can overfit to the generation probabilities of examples.  
- Authors propose a multiplicative parameter that bounds variance while preserving consistency.  
- Experimental results show the multiplicative/normalized counterfactual approach outperforms the non-normalized variant.  
- Introduces a method for learning self-normalized estimators in counterfactual learning under logged bandit settings, targeting overfitting from alignment with logged data.  
- Reviewer judgment: strong paper deserving acceptance, though the reviewer is not entirely certain.
