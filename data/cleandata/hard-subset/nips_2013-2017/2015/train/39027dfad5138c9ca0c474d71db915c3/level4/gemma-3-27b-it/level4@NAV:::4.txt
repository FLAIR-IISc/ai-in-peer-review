== Other comments ==
- The setup for “batch learning from logged bandit feedback” in Section 3 (and as presented in [1]) appears to treat feedback as deterministic on line 110. I believe it should be modeled as a random variable. Consider a scenario where feedback originates from a classification problem with unknown labels, where loss is observed through prediction and comparison to a true label. Let Delta(y', y) represent the true classification loss, and assume a “god agent” provides the label y' for a given input x, resulting in a loss delta(x, y) = Delta(y', y). However, labels are often noisy, meaning multiple labels y' may be associated with the same input x. Consequently, the same x and y pairing could yield different delta(x, y) values due to variations in the god agent’s labeling. Therefore, delta(x, y) should generally be considered a random variable. In the classification example, its mean (for fixed x & y) should represent the expectation of Delta(y', y) given y' distributed according to the true, noisy labeling distribution p(y'|x). This might not impact the risk estimator (2), but the setup in (1) defining the true risk should incorporate an expectation over the randomness of delta for broader applicability.
- Line 170-171: I believe the correct expression should be h(y|x) / h_0(y|x). It would also be beneficial to acknowledge that the lack of linearity was previously identified in [1].
- Line 276: Please change “argmax” to “argmin” as the objective is risk minimization.
- Line 319: To maintain consistency with previous notation, replace bars with hats.
