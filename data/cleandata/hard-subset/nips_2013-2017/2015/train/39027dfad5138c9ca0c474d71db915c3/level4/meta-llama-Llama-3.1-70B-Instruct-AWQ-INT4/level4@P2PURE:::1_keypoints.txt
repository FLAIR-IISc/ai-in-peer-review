- Reviewer had limited time and may have missed or misinterpreted details, but found the paper engaging.
- Paper tackles estimating loss under alternative (non-deterministic) hypotheses via reweighting data points.
- Authors introduce "propensity overfitting," a problem related to but distinct from large variance in importance weights.
- Examples suggest propensity overfitting arises from insufficient exploration and few/no observations of y under the new hypothesis for some x.
- Reviewer requests clearer discussion of the relationship between propensity overfitting and large-weight variance.
- Due to time constraints, reviewer could not fully assess technical details or novelty, but finds the core idea reasonable.
- Specific concern: Line 199 — unclear statement about n small vs k and the implication for \hat R(h^) being smaller than -2; needs clarification.
- Specific concern: Claim that prior BLBF works [4,5,1] use unbiased estimators may be incorrect; prior methods might use clipping and thus introduce bias.

- Overall recommendation: ideas are interesting and paper should be accepted despite unverified technical soundness.
