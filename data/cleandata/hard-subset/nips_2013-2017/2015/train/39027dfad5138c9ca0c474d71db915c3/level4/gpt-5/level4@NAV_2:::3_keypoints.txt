- Studies batch learning from logged bandit feedback (relevant to ad ranking).  
- Finds overfitting in Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) when using the standard counterfactual estimator.  
- Proposes multiplicative control variates that produce a self-normalized (biased) estimator as a remedy.  
- Work is technically strong and well motivated; builds on and extends prior work [1].  
- Reviewer expected broader experiments and deeper treatment of optimization beyond the brief discussion.  
- Presentation is clear and the manuscript is well organized.  
- Main innovation is the self-normalized risk estimator, which shows substantial empirical impact.  
- Significance is high for logged-data learning, but uncertain how gains translate to large-scale real-world deployments.  
- Request to report variability measures (e.g., variance or confidence intervals) for repeated experiments.  
- Overall: solid, relevant, and novel contribution but would benefit from more extensive experiments and real-world use cases beyond [1].
