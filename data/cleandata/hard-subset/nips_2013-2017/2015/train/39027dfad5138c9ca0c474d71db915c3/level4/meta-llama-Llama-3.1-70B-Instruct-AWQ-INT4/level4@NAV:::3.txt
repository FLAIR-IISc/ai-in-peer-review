Review - Summary
This paper tackles the challenge of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant issue in applications such as ad ranking. The authors identify an "overfitting" issue in the recently proposed Conterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and propose a solution using multiplicative control variates, resulting in a biased estimator.

Quality
The paper is of high quality and well-motivated, building upon previous work in [1]. However, as a follow-up study, it would have been beneficial to include additional experiments and a more in-depth analysis of the training objective optimization, providing more details than what is currently presented in the paragraph on line 415.

Clarity
The presentation is clear, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.

Originality
While this paper is a follow-up to [1], the authors introduce a novel modification to the risk estimator by making it self-normalizing, which significantly impacts the experimental results.

Significance
The problem of learning from logged data is highly relevant, but it is challenging to determine how the substantial experimental improvements will translate to real-world applications.

Various Remarks
On line 365, it would be beneficial to include a measure of variance when presenting results from multiple repetitions. The authors successfully identify a problem in the CRM principle and propose a solution. Overall, the paper is solid, well-written, and relevant, with novel contributions. However, additional experiments, particularly those involving real-world applications beyond [1], would have strengthened the paper.
