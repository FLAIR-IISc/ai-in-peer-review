- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and its importance for interactive systems like ads and recommendations.  
- Identifies a novel "propensity overfitting" failure mode in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator R̂(h).  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator R̂SN(h) using multiplicative control variates to eliminate anomalies (non-equivariance, unbounded estimates) while retaining consistency.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm for structured output prediction based on R̂SN(h).  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and guarantees (Theorem 2) resolving the identified failure mode.  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multi-label classification datasets.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear derivations (Sections 4–6) and computational/runtime comparisons.  
- Experiments lack variance metrics (e.g., standard deviations) and deeper analysis of optimization dynamics (convergence behavior of ∥w∥).  
- Writing and presentation are excellent; key concepts and connections between theory and results are clearly explained.  
- Minor clarity suggestions: discuss computational cost of the R̂SN(h) denominator and justify using L-BFGS over stochastic methods.  
- Original contribution: novel application of multiplicative control variates to counterfactual learning, distinct from prior variance-reduction approaches.  
- Significant practical relevance, but would benefit from explicit discussion of deployment aspects (sensitivity to logging policy quality and scalability to real-world data).  
- Pros: resolves fundamental flaw, strong theoretical and empirical support, computationally efficient, advances prior work.  
- Cons: limited experimental scope (only simulated multi-label tasks), missing variance reporting, insufficient exploration of optimization behavior.
