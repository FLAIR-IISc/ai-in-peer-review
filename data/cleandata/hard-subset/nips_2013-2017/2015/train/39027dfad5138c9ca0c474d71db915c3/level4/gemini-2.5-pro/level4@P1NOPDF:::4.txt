This paper presents an interesting follow-up to the work in [1]. The analysis of "propensity overfitting" is insightful, and the proposed solution of using a multiplicative control variate to reduce it is a novel and compelling idea. The empirical results demonstrate a clear improvement over the original POEM method on the increasingly important problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback.

However, there are a few points that require clarification and revision:

**Major Comments**

1.  **Stochasticity of the Feedback Signal:** A point of concern is the problem formulation in Section 3, which appears to treat the feedback `delta(x, y)` as a deterministic function (line 110). This may not be a sufficiently general model. For instance, in a classification setting with noisy labels, the same input `x` and action `y` could result in different observed losses on different occasions. In such cases, `delta(x, y)` is more accurately modeled as a random variable. The expectation of this variable for a fixed `x` and `y` would correspond to E_{y'~p(y'|x)}[Loss(y',y)], where `p(y'|x)` is the true, potentially noisy, labeling distribution. While this might not alter the final risk estimator in Equation (2), the definition of the true risk in Equation (1) should, for greater generality, include an expectation over the randomness of `delta`.

2.  **Range of the Loss Function:** There appears to be a contradiction regarding the range of the loss `delta`. As noted in [1] (Section 4.1), the underlying Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) approach is applicable only when the loss is scaled to the range `[-1, 0]`. I assume this same constraint applies to the NORM-POEM method presented here, and that the `delta` in Equations (3) and (8) is similarly rescaled. If this is the case, the use of `delta > 0` in the experiments described in Table 1 is confusing and requires clarification.

**Minor Corrections**

*   **Lines 170-171:** The ratio appears to be inverted; it should likely be `h(y|x) / h_0(y|x)`. It would also be worth noting that the lack of linearity was previously pointed out in [1].
*   **Line 276:** As the objective is risk minimization, the operator should be `argmin`, not `argmax`.
*   **Line 319:** The notation should use hats instead of bars for consistency with the rest of the paper.
