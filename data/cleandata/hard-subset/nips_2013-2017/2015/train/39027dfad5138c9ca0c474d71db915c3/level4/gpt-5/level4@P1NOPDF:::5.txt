The paper proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that avoids the propensity overfitting problem. It builds on Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) and delivers a substantially improved algorithm over the original POEM.

Quality: The research quality is above average. The extensions introduced lead to significant improvements in training compared to the original POEM.

Clarity: For the most part, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

Originality: The paper presents a substantially new formulation of the original POEM algorithm for the BLBF problem. I believe the contributions are significant and original.

Significance: The results are compelling and merit further investigation. I am particularly impressed that the proposed algorithm is faster than the original POEM despite incorporating additional regularization mechanisms. This method has strong potential for widespread adoption in the near future.

Overall, this is a good paper: it is clear, well organized, and reads well.
