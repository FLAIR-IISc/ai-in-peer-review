- Proposes a method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback that directly addresses propensity overfitting.  
- Builds on Swaminathan & Joachims (2015) and presents a significantly improved algorithm over POEM.  
- Introduces novel regularization techniques within the optimization framework rather than as post-hoc fixes.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical improvements in training performance with compelling evidence across multiple datasets.  
- Provides sound theoretical justification for the proposed modifications.  
- Experimental setup and comparisons to existing methods are thorough and well-defined.  
- Paper is mostly clear with consistent notation, but some design-choice intuition (especially for regularization terms) needs more explanation.  
- Mathematical derivations are logical but could use slightly more verbose explanations of key steps for some readers.  
- The approach is original and addresses a critical limitation of the original POEM method.  
- Results are significant: better accuracy and potentially faster training, with broad application potential (recommendation systems, ads, personalized medicine).  
- Minor issues: stylistic clarifications, clearer connections to other regularization techniques, and a brief discussion of limitations and future work would help.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with compelling results; recommendation for acceptance.
