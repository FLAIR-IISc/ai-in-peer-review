The commentary and notation surrounding the main equation (2) appear to be incorrect or misleading. An expectation is typically a quantity of the form \sum f(x) p(x), where the expected quantity, f(x), is distinct from the probability measure, p(x). In equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity. Consequently, the subsequent comment on page 2, second paragraph, stating that equation (2) has an "anomaly" due to its non-invariance to translation of the loss, is technically incorrect. Furthermore, Example 1, which is based on this incorrect assumption, does not hold.

From a practical perspective, the estimator in equation (2) is sensitive to its denominator, pi, which can be zero if the sample has never been observed in the prior. In contrast, the modified estimator in equation (7) appears to be an improvement, as the effects of pi are mitigated through the double denominator.

I hope the authors will consider revising their commentary if the paper is accepted.

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and achieves performance comparable to a Conditional Random Field (CRF) using full multi-label information. This paper proposes an approach for "counterfactual learning", a specialized form of multi-label learning where, instead of receiving ground-truth annotations for all classes, only the label for one class (i.e., bandit feedback) is provided. While this setup is presented as a justification for the work, the paper's primary aim is to improve upon the POEM estimator recently introduced in reference [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, I find the paper convincing and worthy of publication, although I have raised some points for consideration in the Comments to authors field.
