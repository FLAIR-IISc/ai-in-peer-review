- Addresses Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback (BLBF) and identifies "Propensity Overfitting" where high-capacity models exploit importance-sampling weights to lower empirical risk.  
- Proposes replacing the inverse propensity score estimator with a self-normalized (ratio) estimator that is equivariant to loss shifts and bounds risk within observed loss range.  
- Integrates the self-normalized estimator into CRM to create Norm-POEM, an algorithm for exponential models.  
- Provides theoretical support showing the self-normalized estimator is strongly consistent and resolves anomalies of the unbiased estimator.  
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM across four datasets, various sample sizes, and logging policies.  
- Norm-POEM produces well-calibrated sum-of-weights close to 1, while POEM exhibits severe bias in weight sums.  
- Demonstrates Norm-POEM’s invariance to loss translation and robustness across settings.  
- Ablation shows variance regularization remains beneficial even with the self-normalized estimator.  
- Critiques framing the unbiased estimator’s “anomalies” as potentially overstated; they stem from known finite-sample importance-sampling behavior and should be framed as a practical vulnerability.  
- Notes the paper is framed mainly as an improvement over POEM, and a broader discussion on applicability to other importance-sampling-based methods would strengthen impact.  
- Raises computational scalability concerns: the self-normalized variance term complicates stochastic optimization, and L-BFGS may not scale to massive datasets.  
- For acceptance: identifies and solves a significant practical problem with a simple, well-motivated solution backed by strong theory and experiments.  
- Against acceptance: core technical change is substituting a known estimator into an existing framework, which some may view as incremental despite the novel insight.  
- Conclusion: strong, well-executed paper that meaningfully improves robustness in off-policy learning; recommendation is to accept.
