- Identifies a significant problem—propensity overfitting—in counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator and a new algorithm, Norm-POEM, to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Builds on prior work by Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) while introducing meaningful methodological advances.  
- Theoretical justification is compelling and well-argued, supporting the proposed approach.  
- Empirical results show consistent, substantial improvements in generalization over conventional estimators.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation across multiple datasets strengthens the validity of findings.  
- Paper is mostly clear and well-written, with a logical flow and well-defined notation.  
- Theoretical proofs (e.g., Theorems 1 and 2) are dense and could use simpler or more intuitive explanations.  
- Presents a novel contribution by defining and addressing propensity overfitting with a self-normalized estimator.  
- Norm-POEM offers improved performance, robustness, and unexpectedly faster training than POEM.  
- Provided software implementation enhances reproducibility and practical impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a critical problem, offers a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution, improves performance and speed, and is clearly presented with available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: theoretical sections need simplification and experiments could cover more datasets/domains for broader generalizability.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, technically sound and significant; recommend acceptance with enthusiasm.
