- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies "propensity overfitting" as a critical problem.
- Shows the conventional unbiased importance-weighted estimator can be manipulated to achieve arbitrarily low empirical risk.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator via multiplicative control variates (Norm-POEM) that is biased but consistent, equivariant to loss translations, and bounded.
- Empirically, Norm-POEM yields substantial improvements over POEM on several semi-synthetic multi-label classification tasks.
- Paper is high quality, well-motivated, and technically sound; formalization of propensity overfitting is novel and important.
- Main weakness: evaluation is limited to the same experimental setup and datasets as prior work, reducing generality of claims.
- Optimization discussion is brief; more analysis of the non-convex objective and justification for using L-BFGS (and the claimed faster convergence) is needed.
- Presentation is clear and well-written, with a simple, effective example and enough detail for reproduction.
- Originality: meaningful and novel application of a known self-normalized estimator technique to counterfactual learning.
- Significance: topic is highly relevant to research and industry, but real-world impact is uncertain without broader empirical validation.
- Experimental results are strong but should report variance (e.g., standard deviation) for the averages presented in Table 1.
- Recommendation: broaden experimental evaluation to include more diverse or real-world applications to strengthen practical significance.
