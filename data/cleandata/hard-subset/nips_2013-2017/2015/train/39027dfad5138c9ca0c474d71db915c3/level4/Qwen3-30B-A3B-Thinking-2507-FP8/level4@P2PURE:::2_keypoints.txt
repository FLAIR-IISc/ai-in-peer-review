- The commentary and notation around equation (2) are incorrect/misleading regarding expectations.
- Expectation should be defined as Σ f(x) p(x) with f(x) distinct from p(x); the loss in (2) is not an expectation.
- The claim that (2) exhibits an "anomaly" due to lack of translation invariance is technically incorrect.
- Example 1, which depends on that erroneous premise, therefore does not hold.
- The estimator in (2) is practically highly sensitive to its denominator π, which can be zero if the sample was unobserved under the prior.
- The modified estimator in (7) is preferable in principle because its double denominator mitigates influence of π.
- The authors should revise the misleading commentary if the paper is accepted.
- Experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across all datasets.
- Norm-POEM achieves performance competitive with a CRF trained with full multi-label information.
- The paper’s framing as "counterfactual learning" is essentially a specialization of multi-label learning; its core contribution is improving the POEM estimator (ICML 2015).
- Overall the paper is convincing and suitable for publication, provided the noted technical issues are corrected.
