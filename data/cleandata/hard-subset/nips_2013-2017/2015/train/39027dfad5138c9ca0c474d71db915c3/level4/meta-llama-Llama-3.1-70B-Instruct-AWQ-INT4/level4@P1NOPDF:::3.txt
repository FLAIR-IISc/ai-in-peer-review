Summary
The paper addresses the problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback, a highly relevant issue in fields such as ad ranking. The authors identify an "overfitting" problem inherent to the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and propose a solution based on multiplicative control variates, resulting in a biased estimator.

Quality
The paper is of high quality and well-motivated. However, as a follow-up to [1], I would have expected more comprehensive experiments and a deeper analysis of the training objective optimization, providing more details than those presented in the paragraph on line 415.

Clarity
The presentation is clear, and the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and methodology.

Originality
Although the paper builds upon [1], the modification of the risk estimator to be self-normalizing appears to make a significant difference in the experiments presented by the authors, demonstrating a notable contribution.

Significance
The problem of learning from logged data is highly relevant; however, it is challenging to determine how the substantial experimental improvements will translate to real-world applications.

Various Remarks
Line 365: When presenting the results of multiple repetitions, it would be beneficial to include a measure of variance to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the results. The paper identifies a problem in the recently proposed Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and introduces a solution to address this issue. Overall, the paper is solid, well-written, and the work is relevant and novel. Nevertheless, I would have appreciated more extensive experiments, particularly those involving additional applications beyond those presented in [1], including actual real-world applications to further demonstrate the practical implications of the proposed method.
