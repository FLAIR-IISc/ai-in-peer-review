- Problem & fix: Identifies "Propensity Overfitting" in batch learning from logged bandit feedback and proposes replacing IPS with a self-normalized (ratio) estimator, yielding the Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Theoretical benefit: The self-normalized estimator is principled (control variates), bounded by loss range and equivariant to loss translations, directly addressing estimator anomalies.  
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM across multiple multi-label datasets and training sizes.  
- Illustrative examples: Paper uses simple, effective examples to clearly demonstrate how the unbiased IPS estimator can produce arbitrarily poor risk estimates.  
- Clarity: The paper is well-written, logically organized, and easy to follow.  
- Experimental limitation: Experiments convert supervised datasets to bandit settings (simulated); performance on real-world logged bandit data is not shown.  
- Incremental contribution: The core change is substituting a known statistical estimator into an existing CRM framework, so the work is more a crucial correction than a wholly new framework.  
- Optimization detail missing: Norm-POEM's more complex objective and its surprising faster convergence (via L-BFGS) lack deeper analysis or explanation.  
- Reporting detail: Results are averaged over 10 runs but lack variance measures (e.g., standard deviation) to show stability.  
- Overall judgment: Solid, important contribution that corrects a serious flaw and is recommended for acceptance despite the noted limitations.
