- Paper identifies propensity overfitting in Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) and proposes a self-normalized estimator using multiplicative control variates.  
- Builds on CRM and connects to importance sampling, control variates, and counterfactual evaluation.  
- High-quality, well-motivated work with clear problem statement and demonstrated empirical effectiveness.  
- Would benefit from additional experiments and deeper analysis of training objective optimization.  
- Presentation is clear and well-written with solid theoretical and experimental exposition.  
- Introduction of the self-normalized estimator is novel and experimentally impactful.  
- Problem is significant for learning from logged data, but real-world impact is not fully assessed.  
- Recommend reporting variance measures across repetitions in experiments.  
- Suggest exploring more real-world applications and comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: relevant problem, novel effective solution, thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: primarily follow-up work on prior paper [1] and insufficient assessment of real-world significance.  
- Overall: solid and relevant paper that could be strengthened with more experiments and analysis.
