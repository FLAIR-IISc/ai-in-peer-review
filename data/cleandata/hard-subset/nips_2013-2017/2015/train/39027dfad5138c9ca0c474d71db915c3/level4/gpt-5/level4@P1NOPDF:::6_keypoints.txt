- CRM learns from logged bandit data by correcting bias from the logging policy.
- Although CRM is consistent, it can overfit by exploiting example-generation propensities.
- Overfitting arises because hypotheses can focus on regions with higher logging-policy support.
- The authors introduce a multiplicative (self-normalized) parameter that bounds estimator variance while preserving consistency.
- Empirical results show the self-normalized counterfactual method outperforms the non-normalized alternative.
- The paper provides a principled technique to mitigate propensity-weighted support–driven overfitting.
- Overall the ideas are sound, experiments support the claims, and the paper is a good candidate for acceptance (reviewer not fully certain).
