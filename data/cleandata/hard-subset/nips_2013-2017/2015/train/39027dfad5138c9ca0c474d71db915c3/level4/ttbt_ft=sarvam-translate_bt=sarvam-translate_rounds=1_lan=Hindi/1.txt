While observing data from a given hypothesis, we might be interested in evaluating a different hypothesis. If these hypotheses are non-deterministic (e.g., conditional distributions), we can estimate the loss under the hypothesis under investigation by re-weighting the data points, see between equations (1) and (2).

The author seems to be solving a problem that is related but somewhat different from the problem of large variance of weights (see below). The author calls this problem "progenity overfitting." The example illustrates that this problem occurs when there is not enough search, that is, for a given x, we only see very few or even no y's that would correspond to the new hypothesis to be evaluated. Since a similar argument explains large variance of weights, it would be good to explain the relationship between these two problems in a little more detail.

I didn't have time to look at the details or check the novelty of the proposed solution, but the idea seems sensible.
