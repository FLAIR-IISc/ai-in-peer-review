

The commentary and notation surrounding Equation (2) contain a fundamental conceptual error. An expectation takes the form \(\sum f(x) p(x)\), where \(f(x)\) is the function being averaged and \(p(x)\) is the probability measure. In Equation (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity; rather, it is an estimator that depends on the prior \(\pi\). Consequently, the claim on page 2 (second paragraph) that Equation (2) exhibits an "anomaly" due to lack of translation invariance is technically incorrect. This error underpins Example 1, which relies on the flawed premise that the loss should be translation-invariant—a condition that does not apply here.  

The estimator in Equation (2) also suffers from practical instability: its denominator \(\pi\) can vanish if the sample was unobserved in the prior, rendering the estimate undefined. The modified estimator in Equation (7) addresses this issue by mitigating \(\pi\)'s influence through a double denominator, making it more robust in practice. I strongly recommend the authors correct this error in the final version should the paper be accepted.  

The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across all datasets and achieves performance competitive with a CRF using full multi-label annotations. While the paper positions itself within "counterfactual learning"—a specialized multi-label setting where only one class label (bandit feedback) is observed per sample—the core contribution is more precisely framed as an improvement to the POEM estimator introduced by reference [1] at ICML 2015. The contextual justification for counterfactual learning serves as a preamble, but the paper’s primary aim is to refine POEM’s estimator.  

Overall, the work is well-motivated, technically sound in its core proposal, and supported by convincing empirical results. I find the paper to be a worthwhile contribution to the literature. However, the conceptual error in the treatment of Equation (2) must be addressed before acceptance. The authors should revise their discussion of the expectation structure and the translation-invariance claim to align with the mathematical definition. The proposed modification in Equation (7) is a clear improvement, and the experimental validation of Norm-POEM is compelling. With these clarifications, the paper would be suitable for publication.
