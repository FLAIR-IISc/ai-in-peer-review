Summary
The paper tackles counterfactual (bandit-feedback) learning in a multi-label classification setting and proposes Norm-POEM as an improvement over POEM. Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets and performs competitively with a fully supervised CRF baseline despite having access only to bandit feedback. The overall contribution is solid and practically relevant.

Major comments
- Conceptual and notational issues around Eq. (2): An expectation has the form E_p[f(X)] = Σ_x f(x) p(x), where f(x) is the quantity being averaged and p(x) is the probability measure. In Eq. (2), the loss is part of f(·), not the measure. The paper’s discussion (p. 2, second paragraph) characterizes Eq. (2) as having an “anomaly” because it is not invariant to translations of the loss. This is technically incorrect: adding a constant c to the loss shifts the expected value by c; invariance to translation is not a property one should expect here, nor is lack of it an anomaly. Example 1 appears to be built on this mistaken premise and therefore does not convincingly support the claim. Please revise the commentary and example accordingly.
- Sensitivity to propensities in Eq. (2): The estimator in Eq. (2) is fragile with respect to the propensity π in the denominator. If π(a|x) is zero (or extremely small) for an action observed under the logging policy, the estimator becomes undefined (or exhibits high variance). Any valid application must explicitly assume sufficient support (π(a|x) > 0 for actions with nonzero target probability) and/or employ safeguards such as propensity clipping, lower bounds, or enforced exploration. Norm-POEM in Eq. (7) is preferable in principle: by normalizing, it mitigates the volatility induced by small propensities and reduces variance. However, the paper should clearly state the assumptions under which Eq. (7) is consistent, and discuss the bias–variance trade-off introduced by normalization.
- Framing of “counterfactual learning”: The motivation via multi-label learning is helpful, but the paper’s true aim is to stabilize and improve a specific off-policy estimator (POEM). I suggest sharpening the narrative: (i) define the counterfactual risk minimization objective; (ii) present Eq. (2) as a standard IPS-style estimator; (iii) explain why normalization (Eq. (7)) addresses variance and numerical stability; and (iv) position Norm-POEM relative to prior work (including [1]) with clear theoretical and empirical claims.

Empirical evaluation
- Strengths: The results demonstrate consistent gains of Norm-POEM over POEM across datasets and show that Norm-POEM approaches the performance of a fully supervised CRF. This is compelling evidence of practical benefit.
- Suggestions:
  - Report how zero or near-zero propensities are handled in practice (e.g., clipping thresholds, minimum propensity floors, or data filtering). This is critical for reproducibility and to assess robustness.
  - Include variance estimates or confidence intervals around performance metrics, since the central claim is variance reduction and stability.
  - Provide sensitivity analyses with respect to propensity accuracy (if propensities are estimated) and to normalization strength, to illuminate when Norm-POEM helps most.
  - Clarify whether normalization induces bias in your setting and, if so, quantify its magnitude empirically.

Clarity and presentation
- Revise the discussion around expectations and translation of losses to avoid the misleading “anomaly” claim and correct Example 1 accordingly.
- Tighten notation: define all random variables and distributions at first use; denote propensities clearly as π(a|x) and specify the source distribution with respect to which expectations are taken (logging vs. target).
- Separate the high-level motivation (bandit feedback as partial multi-label supervision) from the core contribution (a normalized estimator), so the reader can follow the logical progression from problem to method to guarantees.

Overall assessment
The paper addresses an important and practically frequent learning scenario and proposes a method that is simple, intuitive, and empirically strong. With corrections to the theoretical commentary around Eq. (2) and Example 1, and with clearer statements of assumptions and robustness practices for handling small propensities, the paper would be a strong contribution.

Recommendation: Accept, contingent on revising the discussion of expectations and translation invariance, clarifying the assumptions and safeguards regarding propensities, and strengthening the empirical reporting on variance and robustness.

Minor comments
- Please ensure consistent symbols across the paper and avoid overloading notation for losses, propensities, and measures.
- Consider adding a brief related-work paragraph connecting Norm-POEM to self-normalized importance sampling and other variance-reduction techniques to further ground the contribution.
- Proofread for minor grammatical issues and spacing (e.g., stray spaces before punctuation; “bandit feedback).However” → “bandit feedback). However”).
