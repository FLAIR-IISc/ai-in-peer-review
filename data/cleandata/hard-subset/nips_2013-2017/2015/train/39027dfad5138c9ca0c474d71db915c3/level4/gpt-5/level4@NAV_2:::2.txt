The discussion and notation around the main estimator (2) appear incorrect or at least misleading: an expectation has the form ∑ f(x) p(x), where f(x) is the quantity being averaged and p(x) is the probability measure. In (2), the loss is clearly not the expected quantity. Consequently, the subsequent remark (page 2, second paragraph) that (2) exhibits an “anomaly” because it is not invariant under translations of the loss is, in my view, technically incorrect. The ensuing Example 1 builds on this faulty premise and, unsurprisingly, does not hold. In practical terms, the weakness of (2) stems from its denominator, pi, which can be 0 if the event has never been observed under the logging policy. The modified estimator in (7) appears preferable in principle because the influence of pi is tempered through the additional normalization. I sincerely hope the authors will revise this part of the exposition should the paper be accepted.

The experimental results show that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and performs close to a CRF trained with full multi-label supervision. The paper addresses “counterfactual learning,” a setting that can be viewed as a specialization of multi-label learning: in standard multi-label problems the ground-truth annotation provides a 0/1 label for each class, whereas here only the feedback for a single chosen label (bandit feedback) is observed. Nevertheless, the paper’s aim is quite precise: to improve the estimator underlying POEM, recently proposed in [1] at ICML 2015. Overall, the paper is convincing and worthy of publication, with the caveats raised in the Comments to authors.
