- Identifies a critical flaw in the standard counterfactual risk estimator used in BLBF: "propensity overfitting" when learning over complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Defines propensity overfitting as a phenomenon where risk estimates can become arbitrarily biased, distinct from conventional supervised overfitting.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates that maintains equivariance and bounds risk estimates.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator that consistently outperforms conventional POEM.  
- Theoretical contribution (Theorem 2) shows the self-normalized estimator is consistent and resolves anomalies of the conventional estimator.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough with ablation studies showing Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting (Ŝ(h) values near 1) and generalizes better across datasets.  
- Paper situates contributions within related NIPS/ICML work and references CRM (Swaminathan & Joachims) appropriately.  
- Strengths: novel problem identification, strong theoretical foundation, compelling empirical results, practical significance for real-world bandit learning.  
- Computationally efficient: Norm-POEM converges faster than POEM despite the normalization step.  
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and provides sufficient technical detail for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: experimental scope is limited to multi-label classification datasets.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of estimator behavior under highly skewed propensity scores.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to recent BLBF methods such as Doubly Robust estimators (Dudik et al., ICML 2014).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, rigorous analysis, strong empirical validation, and practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental scope and minimal comparisons with state-of-the-art BLBF methods.
