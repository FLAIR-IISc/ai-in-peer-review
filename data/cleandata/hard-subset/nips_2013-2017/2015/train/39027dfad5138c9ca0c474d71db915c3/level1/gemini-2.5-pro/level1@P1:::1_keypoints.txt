- Identifies "Propensity Overfitting": IPS-based CRM can be gamed by complex learners to produce artificially low empirical risk, causing biased estimates and poor generalization.  
- Proposes replacing IPS with a self-normalized importance sampling estimator to fix this pathology by bounding and making risk estimates equivariant to loss translations.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM: integrates the self-normalized estimator into CRM for structured prediction, instantiated for linear exponential family models.  
- Empirical results on multi-label tasks show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM, controls sum of propensity weights, and is robust to loss translations.  
- Situates work in off-policy learning/CRM literature; estimator is classical in Monte Carlo/statistics, but novelty is identifying propensity overfitting and applying the fix for learning.  
- Strength — Significance/Originality: clearly uncovers a fundamental, previously overlooked flaw with strong illustrative example.  
- Strength — Technical Quality: solution is simple, principled (control variates), and analyzed for equivariance, boundedness, and consistency.  
- Strength — Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and motivates experiments that directly test claims.  
- Strength — Empirical Evaluation: thorough experiments validate mitigation of propensity overfitting and improved generalization.  
- Weakness — Limited theoretical analysis: lacks finite-sample/generalization bounds showing impact of bias introduced by self-normalization on CRM’s guarantees.  
- Weakness — Computational scalability: variance estimate hinders stochastic optimization, forcing batch methods (L-BFGS), which may not scale to very large datasets.  
- Weakness — Real-world validation: experiments use supervised-to-bandit conversions; evaluation on real logged bandit data would strengthen claims.  
- Pro-acceptance: exposes important problem, offers principled/simple fix with strong empirical support and clear exposition.  
- Con-acceptance: estimator itself is not novel; theory could be deeper and scalability/real-data applicability are concerns.  
- Conclusion: Strong, well-executed contribution that meaningfully advances BLBF; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
