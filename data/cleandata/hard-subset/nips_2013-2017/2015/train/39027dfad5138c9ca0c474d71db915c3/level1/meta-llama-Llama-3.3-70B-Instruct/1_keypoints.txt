- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) to avoid propensity overfitting.  
- Identifies severe anomalies of the conventional unbiased estimator, including unbounded variance and non-equivariance leading to degenerate risk estimates.  
- Shows the self-normalized estimator is equivariant, bounded, and strongly consistent.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM and demonstrates its effectiveness on several multi-label classification tasks.  
- Paper is well-structured with a clear, concise introduction to BLBF and existing limitations.  
- Related work is thorough, linking BLBF to causal inference and importance sampling.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including a proof of strong consistency (with some proofs in appendices).  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and supports the proposed method.  
- Strength: significant technical contribution that can improve robustness and reliability of BLBF algorithms.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with BLBF and importance sampling, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: several technical proofs/derivations are relegated to appendices or external references.  
- Accept arguments: novel approach addressing a key limitation, rigorous theory and experiments, potential field impact.  
- Reject arguments: limited accessibility for non-experts and omitted in-paper proofs may hinder full evaluation.
