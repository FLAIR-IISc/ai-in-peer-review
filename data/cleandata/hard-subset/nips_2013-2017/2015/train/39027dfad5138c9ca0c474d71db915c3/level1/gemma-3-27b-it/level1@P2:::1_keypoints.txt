- Identifies propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and shows the unbiased counterfactual risk estimator generalizes poorly.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator as a remedy, with theoretical justification and empirical validation on multi-label classification tasks.  
- Technically sound work: novel identification of the problem and rigorous theoretical analysis showing why the self-normalized estimator avoids standard estimator pitfalls.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation using established datasets and the "Supervised 7→ Bandit" methodology.  
- Ablation studies isolate the impact of variance regularization and demonstrate equivariance, strengthening the empirical claims.  
- Direct comparisons with POEM and a full-information CRF provide valuable performance context.  
- Runtime analysis included, showing Norm-POEM achieves competitive runtime despite increased per-iteration cost.  
- Minor concern: variance estimation for the self-normalized estimator is complex, though faster convergence mitigates this.  
- Clarity is generally good: well-written introduction, standard notation, and helpful examples; Section 5 derivations could be streamlined for accessibility.  
- Would benefit from a more intuitive explanation of why the self-normalized estimator works before the formal derivations.  
- Originality: novel application and theoretical analysis of the self-normalized estimator in BLBF and the Norm-POEM algorithm as a new CRM instantiation.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental BLBF problem with broadly applicable solutions and strong empirical support; overall recommendation is acceptance.
