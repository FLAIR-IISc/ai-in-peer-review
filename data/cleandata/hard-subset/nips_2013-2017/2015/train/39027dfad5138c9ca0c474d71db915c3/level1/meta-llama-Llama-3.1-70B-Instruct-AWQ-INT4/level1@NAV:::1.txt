**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel estimator, called the Self-Normalized Estimator, for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, known as Propensity Overfitting, which can lead to degenerate risk estimates. The proposed estimator avoids this problem and is shown to have desirable theoretical properties. The authors also introduce a new learning algorithm, called Norm-POEM, which uses the Self-Normalized Estimator and achieves improved generalization performance and robustness.

**Strengths**

1. **Identification of Propensity Overfitting**: The authors identify a significant problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, which is a crucial contribution to the field of BLBF.
2. **Theoretical Analysis**: The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the Self-Normalized Estimator, including its properties and advantages over the conventional estimator.
3. **Improved Generalization Performance**: The experimental results demonstrate that Norm-POEM achieves better generalization performance than the conventional POEM algorithm.
4. **Robustness**: The Self-Normalized Estimator is shown to be more robust to propensity overfitting, which is a significant advantage in BLBF.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational Cost**: The Self-Normalized Estimator requires more computational resources than the conventional estimator, which may be a limitation for large-scale applications.
2. **Optimization Complexity**: The optimization problem for Norm-POEM is non-convex, which may require more sophisticated optimization techniques.
3. **Limited Experimental Evaluation**: The experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets and may not be representative of all possible scenarios.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel estimator and a new learning algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field of BLBF. The identification of Propensity Overfitting and the development of a solution to address this problem are original and impactful contributions.

**Significance**

The paper has significant implications for the field of BLBF, as it provides a new estimator and a learning algorithm that can improve generalization performance and robustness. The results have the potential to impact various applications, including online systems, such as ad placement, web search, and recommendation systems.
