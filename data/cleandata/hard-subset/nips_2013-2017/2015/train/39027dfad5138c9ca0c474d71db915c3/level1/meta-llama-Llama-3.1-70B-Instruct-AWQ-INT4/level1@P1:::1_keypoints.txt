- Identifies that the conventional counterfactual risk estimator causes propensity overfitting in BLBF.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator that avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Derives Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm using CRM with the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional estimator on multi-label classification tasks.  
- Connects to prior BLBF, propensity score matching, and off-policy evaluation in RL; highlights limitations of previous estimators.  
- Strength: clearly identifies a significant problem in BLBF.  
- Strength: introduces a novel self-normalized estimator addressing that problem.  
- Strength: provides a well-motivated derivation of Norm-POEM.  
- Strength: offers thorough and convincing empirical evaluation.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis of the estimator and algorithm is limited.  
- Weakness: computational cost of the self-normalized estimator / Norm-POEM is mentioned but not quantified.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of hyperparameter effects on performance.  
- Quality: technically sound with clear derivations and convincing results, but would benefit from more theory and hyperparameter study.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with consistent notation and helpful figures/tables.  
- Originality: presents an original and well-motivated solution to a key BLBF problem.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential impact on online systems and recommendation systems.  
- Pro acceptance: novel solution, significant problem addressed, strong empirical support.  
- Con acceptance: needs expanded theoretical analysis, hyperparameter exploration, and computational cost details.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
