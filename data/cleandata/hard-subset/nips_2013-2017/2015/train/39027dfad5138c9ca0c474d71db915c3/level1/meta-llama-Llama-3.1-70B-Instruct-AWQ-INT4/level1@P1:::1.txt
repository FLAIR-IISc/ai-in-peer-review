**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper identifies a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), which leads to propensity overfitting. The authors propose an alternative estimator, called the self-normalized estimator, which avoids this problem. They derive a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle using the self-normalized estimator. The paper evaluates the empirical effectiveness of Norm-POEM on several multi-label classification problems and shows that it consistently outperforms the conventional estimator.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on BLBF, which is an instance of causal inference. The authors draw connections to classic inference techniques, such as propensity score matching, and to off-policy evaluation in reinforcement learning. They also discuss the limitations of previous counterfactual estimators and the importance of addressing propensity overfitting.

**Strengths**

1. **Identification of a key problem**: The paper identifies a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Proposal of a new estimator**: The self-normalized estimator is a novel solution that addresses the problem of propensity overfitting.
3. **Derivation of a new learning algorithm**: Norm-POEM is a well-motivated learning algorithm that combines the self-normalized estimator with the CRM principle.
4. **Empirical evaluation**: The paper provides a thorough empirical evaluation of Norm-POEM on several multi-label classification problems, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited theoretical analysis**: While the paper provides some theoretical insights, a more comprehensive analysis of the self-normalized estimator and Norm-POEM would be beneficial.
2. **Computational cost**: The paper mentions that the self-normalized estimator comes at a computational cost, but it would be helpful to provide more details on the computational complexity of Norm-POEM.
3. **Limited exploration of hyperparameters**: The paper does not thoroughly explore the impact of hyperparameters on the performance of Norm-POEM.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-motivated derivation of the self-normalized estimator and Norm-POEM. The empirical evaluation is thorough, and the results are convincing. However, some additional theoretical analysis and exploration of hyperparameters would strengthen the paper.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed solution, and the empirical evaluation. The notation is consistent, and the figures and tables are helpful.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel solution to a significant problem in BLBF, which is a substantial contribution to the field. The self-normalized estimator and Norm-POEM are original and well-motivated.

**Significance**

The paper addresses a key problem in BLBF, which is an important area of research. The proposed solution has the potential to improve the performance of BLBF algorithms in various applications, such as online systems and recommendation systems.

**Pro acceptance**:
