- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to address propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Shows the self-normalized estimator avoids propensity overfitting and yields more robust risk estimates.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of the limitations of the conventional counterfactual estimator.  
- Supports claims with both theoretical results and empirical experiments.  
- Main contribution: identification of propensity overfitting in BLBF and a novel estimator to mitigate it.  
- Experiments (multi-label classification) demonstrate effectiveness of the proposed method.  
- Method balances theoretical grounding and empirical validation.  
- Strong connection to related work and clear differentiation from existing methods.  
- Norm-POEM is presented as a significant contribution that improves generalization.  
- Originality lies in the novel estimator addressing a critical BLBF issue.  
- Significance: high potential applicability across recommendation systems, advertising, healthcare.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, well-analyzed, empirically validated, and clearly presented contribution.  
- Concerns: assumes a stationary policy h0 with full support, which may be unrealistic.  
- Concerns: lacks comprehensive analysis of Norm-POEM’s computational complexity for large-scale use.  
- Concerns: evaluation focused mainly on multi-label classification, possibly limiting generality.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; rating 9/10, confidence 8/10.
