## Review of "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning"

This paper addresses a critical issue in Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback (BLBF): propensity overfitting in the commonly used counterfactual risk estimator. The authors demonstrate, both theoretically and empirically, that this estimator can lead to severely biased risk estimates and propose a self-normalized estimator as a solution. This new estimator forms the basis for a novel learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, which is evaluated on multi-label classification tasks and shown to outperform the standard approach (POEM) consistently. 

The work builds upon the established CRM principle [1] and relates to broader areas like causal inference [7], domain adaptation [8], and off-policy evaluation [4]. It correctly identifies limitations within the existing literature focused on importance sampling techniques [6, 5], pinpointing a previously unrecognized form of overfitting. The authors clearly articulate the problem, offering a compelling explanation with a helpful example demonstrating the estimator's failure modes.  The self-normalized estimator, borrowed from financial applications [17], is well-justified as a potential remedy and shown to possess desirable properties like equivariance.

**Strengths:** 

* **Important Problem:** Propensity overfitting is a significant issue in BLBF, and the paper effectively highlights its consequences.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The analysis is rigorous, with a clear explanation of the estimator's properties and a theoretical guarantee of consistency.
* **Empirical Validation:** Extensive experiments demonstrate the practical benefits of Norm-POEM, showcasing improved performance and robustness across different datasets. The comparisons against POEM are convincing.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and the explanations are easy to follow, even for readers unfamiliar with the nuances of BLBF.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Cost:** While the authors mention the computational cost, a more detailed analysis of the trade-offs between the self-normalized estimator and the original estimator would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The dependence on hyperparameter tuning (M, λ) is acknowledged but could be explored further. A discussion of strategies for efficient hyperparameter selection would be valuable.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments focus on a specific type of hypothesis space (linear rules). Evaluating Norm-POEM with more complex models would strengthen the conclusions.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a significant and previously overlooked problem in a relevant field.
* Presents a theoretically sound and practically effective solution.
* Provides strong empirical evidence supporting the claims.
* Well-written and clearly explains complex concepts.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* Computational considerations are not fully addressed.
* The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of learning from logged bandit feedback. The identified problem of propensity overfitting is important, and the proposed solution is both theoretically justified and empirically effective. Despite minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper clearly outweigh its shortcomings, and I **recommend acceptance** to NIPS.
