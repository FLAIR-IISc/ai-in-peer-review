- Proposes a novel self-normalized estimator to address propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Identifies a severe degeneracy issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator when used in ERM.  
- Introduces a multiplicative control variate to derive the self-normalized estimator that provably avoids these degeneracies.  
- Theoretical motivation and properties of the self-normalized estimator are clearly explained.  
- Paper is well-written, with a good introduction, thorough related work, and organized technical sections.  
- Authors present an extensive empirical evaluation (Norm-POEM) across multi-label classification tasks.  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional POEM estimator.  
- Strength: identifies a significant practical problem and provides a novel, theoretically sound solution.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation is extensive and convincing.  
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in BLBF and CRM, which may hinder non-experts.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of computational complexity of Norm-POEM versus POEM.  
- Weakness: could include more theoretical analysis (convergence rates, robustness to misspecification).  
- Recommendation: suitable for NIPS; reviewer recommends acceptance but asks authors to address weaknesses in the final version.  
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
