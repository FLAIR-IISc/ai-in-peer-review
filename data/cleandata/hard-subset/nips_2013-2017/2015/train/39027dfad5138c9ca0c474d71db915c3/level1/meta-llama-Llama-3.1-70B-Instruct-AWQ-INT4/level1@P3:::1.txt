This paper proposes an alternative estimator for counterfactual learning in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), addressing a severe problem of the conventional counterfactual risk estimator. The authors introduce the self-normalized estimator, which avoids the propensity overfitting problem and provides a more robust risk estimate.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the concepts and the proposed method. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the conventional estimator's limitations and demonstrate the benefits of the self-normalized estimator through theoretical and empirical results.

The main contribution of this paper is the identification of the propensity overfitting problem in BLBF and the proposal of a novel estimator that addresses this issue. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the problem and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on multi-label classification problems.

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem, related work, and the proposed method. The experimental results are well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of the results.

One of the strengths of this paper is its ability to balance theoretical and empirical results. The authors provide a solid theoretical foundation for their approach and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments.

The paper is also well-connected to related work in the field. The authors provide a thorough review of existing methods and clearly explain how their approach differs from and improves upon existing methods.

The proposed method, Norm-POEM, is a significant contribution to the field of BLBF. The authors demonstrate its effectiveness in avoiding propensity overfitting and achieving better generalization performance.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written and easy to understand. The authors provide clear explanations of complex concepts and use appropriate notation and terminology.

The originality of this paper lies in its proposal of a novel estimator that addresses the propensity overfitting problem in BLBF. The authors provide a unique perspective on the problem and propose a solution that is both theoretically sound and empirically effective.

The significance of this paper is high, as it addresses a critical problem in BLBF and provides a solution that can be widely applied in various fields, such as recommendation systems, advertising, and healthcare.

Arguments against acceptance:

3. The paper focuses primarily on multi-label classification problems and may not be directly applicable to other types of problems.
