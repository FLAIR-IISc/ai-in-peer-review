- Identifies a new problem—propensity overfitting—in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF.  
- Shows standard inverse propensity weighting (IPW) estimator can give severely biased risk estimates in empirical risk minimization.  
- Demonstrates this differs from supervised "loss overfitting" because the estimator is not equivariant to loss translations and can produce estimates outside the loss range.  
- Provides a concrete example (Example 1) where the conventional estimator yields arbitrarily low risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (Equation 7) that avoids these anomalies.  
- Proves strong consistency of the self-normalized estimator (Theorem 2).  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new algorithm using the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical results across multiple multi-label datasets show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and is more robust to loss translations.  
- Evaluation uses established Supervised→Bandit methodology and is thorough and reproducible.  
- Notes normalization increases per-iteration computation but leads to faster optimization convergence and better control over propensity overfitting.  
- Acknowledges variance regularization remains beneficial even with the new estimator.  
- Paper is technically sound, exceptionally clear, and well-organized, with reproducible experimental details.  
- Original contribution: first to identify propensity overfitting in BLBF and apply self-normalization to address it.  
- Significance: practical, drop-in improvement for BLBF methods with potential real-world impact (ads, recommendations, search).  
- Weaknesses: could discuss bias of the self-normalized estimator in more detail, provide more precise runtime comparisons, and better connect to control variates literature.  
- Overall conclusion: high-quality, novel, significant work with a strong recommendation for acceptance.
