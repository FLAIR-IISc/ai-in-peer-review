- Identifies a critical failure mode called "Propensity Overfitting" in the IPS estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback.
- Shows models can exploit propensity weights to minimize empirical risk, producing unboundedly optimistic risk estimates in complex hypothesis spaces.
- Proposes replacing IPS with a self-normalized (ratio) estimator from Monte Carlo literature to correct this failure.
- The self-normalized estimator normalizes weighted losses by the sum of weights, yielding bounded estimates.
- Integrates the robust estimator into the CRM framework to create the Norm-POEM algorithm.
- Positions the work as a direct, important extension of prior counterfactual learning research with high relevance to the community.
- Strength: Clearly and intuitively explains Propensity Overfitting with a compelling simple example.
- Strength: The solution is elegant, grounded in statistical theory (control variates), equivariant to loss translation, and provably resolves IPS anomalies.
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments show Norm-POEM generalizes better, is robust to loss-shifting pathologies, and converges faster in practice.
- Weakness: The self-normalized estimator itself is not novel; novelty is in diagnosing the problem and analyzing benefits in the learning setting.
- Weakness: Variance estimation precludes stochastic optimization; paper could better discuss scalability to massive-scale problems requiring stochastic methods.
- Overall recommendation: High-quality paper with strong theoretical and empirical contributions; strongly recommend acceptance.
