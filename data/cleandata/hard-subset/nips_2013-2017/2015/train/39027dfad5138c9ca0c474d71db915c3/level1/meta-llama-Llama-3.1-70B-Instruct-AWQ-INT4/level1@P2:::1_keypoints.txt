- Paper identifies a significant flaw (propensity overfitting) in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Authors propose a self-normalized risk estimator that provably avoids propensity overfitting.  
- The new estimator leads to improved generalization performance and robustness empirically.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis is provided to justify the proposed estimator.  
- Extensive experimental evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations, notation, and figures.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and limitations of prior methods are discussed.  
- Strength: clear identification of a significant problem and a concrete solution.  
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding and extensive empirical validation.  
- Strength: strong writing and exposition.  
- Weakness: assumption that the historical policy has full support over the action space, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other methods that might also mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Recommendation: accept, with request to address full-support assumption and add comparisons to alternative approaches.
