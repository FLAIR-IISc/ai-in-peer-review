- Identifies "propensity overfitting" in the standard inverse-propensity estimator for BLBF, which can produce severely biased (arbitrarily low) risk estimates even with bounded loss.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator that avoids this flaw, preserves equivariance under loss translation, and bounds overfitted risk.  
- Derives Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Theoretical contributions include clear examples illustrating the problem and a consistency proof for the self-normalized estimator (Theorem 2).  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently improves over POEM across multiple datasets and avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the distinction between loss overfitting and propensity overfitting.  
- Authors transparently note practical considerations, including the need for variance regularization and computational trade-offs.  
- Weakness: no theoretical generalization bounds for the self-normalized estimator beyond consistency.  
- Weakness: empirical comparisons are limited—missing baselines such as doubly robust estimators and the offset tree.  
- Weakness: computational complexity claims (per-iteration cost parity with POEM) are not fully justified given the normalization step.  
- Weakness: consistency proof requires the logging policy to have full support over Y; behavior when this assumption fails is not addressed.  
- Overall assessment: significant and practically important contribution to BLBF with strong theory and empirical validation, meriting publication despite some theoretical and comparative gaps.
