- Problem: Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and instability of IPS within risk minimization.
- Main diagnosis: Identifies "Propensity Overfitting" where models exploit propensity weights to drive empirical risk artificially low.
- Pathology example: With negative losses, models can assign high probability to training points, pushing IPS risk toward negative infinity.
- Proposed fix: Replace IPS with the self-normalized estimator, using the sum of importance weights as a multiplicative control variate.
- Theoretical benefits: Self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss shifts and yields estimates bounded by observed loss range.
- Algorithm: Introduces Norm-POEM, integrating the self-normalized estimator and a variance estimate into the CRM framework.
- Theoretical support: Provides consistency (Theorem 2) and motivates variance control via control variates.
- Empirical validation: Extensive experiments on multi-label classification show Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM.
- Positioning: Builds on CRM and classic importance-sampling tools; application to propensity overfitting is novel and well-argued.
- Strength — quality: Paper is technically sound, with a clear illustrative example and strong empirical/theoretical support.
- Strength — clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized; distinguishes loss vs. propensity overfitting clearly.
- Strength — impact: Significant practical importance for recommendation/search/ads; likely high research and practitioner impact.
- Weakness: Variance estimator relies on asymptotic approximation (delta method); potential issues in small-to-medium samples are not discussed.
- Weakness: Final optimization is non-convex and potential local minima or sensitivity to initialization are not analyzed.
- For acceptance: Novel problem identification, principled elegant solution, strong empirical gains, and high-quality presentation.
- Against acceptance: Core estimator is a classic tool (not new) and theoretical analysis of optimization/variance approximation is limited.
- Recommendation: Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance — strengths substantially outweigh minor weaknesses.
