- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies a new problem: "propensity overfitting."
- Propensity overfitting: unbiased importance sampling estimator gives severely biased risk estimates for high-capacity hypotheses, especially with negative losses.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (Eq. 7) using multiplicative control variates, yielding the Norm-POEM algorithm.
- Theoretically and empirically demonstrates that Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting and produces more reliable risk estimates.
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets, training sizes, and loss transformations.
- Positions work within CRM and importance sampling literature and distinguishes propensity overfitting from standard loss overfitting.
- Correctly compares to clipped importance sampling and doubly robust estimators; novelty is multiplicative vs additive control variates.
- Strength: clear, formal definition and compelling examples of propensity overfitting (Section 4).
- Strength: sound theoretical analysis (Theorems 1–2) supporting the self-normalized estimator.
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation on multiple standard multi-label classification datasets.
- Strength: detailed analysis of how Norm-POEM avoids overfitting via behavior of Ŝ(h) (Table 2).
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational cost of the self-normalized estimator.
- Weakness: variance estimate (Eq. 8) does not admit stochastic optimization; paper lacks sufficient justification for using L-BFGS in practice.
- Weakness: limited exploration of sensitivity to choice of control variate beyond theoretical correlation assumption.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation limited to multi-label classification; lacks experiments on more complex structured prediction tasks.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and technically rigorous with reproducible experimental setup.
- Contribution is novel and significant for practical BLBF applications, enabling more reliable learning from logged data.
- Claims minimal computational overhead supported by runtime analysis, but optimization details remain underexplained.
- Pro acceptance: identifies new problem, provides sound theory, comprehensive experiments, practical algorithm, high relevance.
- Con acceptance: limited structured prediction experiments, incomplete optimization/complexity discussion, need more on control variate choice.
- Overall verdict in review: high-quality paper with minor weaknesses; should be accepted to NIPS.
