- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and its impact on counterfactual risk minimization (CRM).
- Identifies standard unbiased estimator’s vulnerability to propensity overfitting, especially with complex hypothesis spaces.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator as a remedy and introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on it.
- Provides theoretical justification for the approach (Theorems 1 & 2) explaining why the conventional estimator fails.
- Connects to and builds on prior work in BLBF, causal inference, off-policy evaluation, and importance sampling.
- Appropriately references variance reduction techniques and multiplicative control variates from related literature.
- Empirically demonstrates superior performance of Norm-POEM over POEM on multi-label classification tasks.
- Includes extensive experiments, ablation study, and analysis of Ŝ(ĥ) to support empirical claims.
- Novel contribution by identifying propensity overfitting as a specific problem and proposing a direct solution.
- Technical soundness: solid mathematical analysis and insightful failure analysis of conventional estimators.
- Clear writing, consistent notation, and well-explained problem, solution, and experiments.
- Significant practical impact potential by improving reliability and generalization of learned policies.
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational complexity and potentially complex variance calculations.
- Weakness: insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for M and λ.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to LibSVM multi-label datasets, reducing evidence of generalizability.
- Weakness: could deepen discussion of equivariance literature and its relevance to BLBF.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant, previously unrecognized problem with strong theoretical and empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: computational cost concerns, limited dataset diversity, and need for more hyperparameter analysis.
- Overall assessment: valuable, novel, and well-supported contribution with minor weaknesses.
- Recommendation: Accept.
