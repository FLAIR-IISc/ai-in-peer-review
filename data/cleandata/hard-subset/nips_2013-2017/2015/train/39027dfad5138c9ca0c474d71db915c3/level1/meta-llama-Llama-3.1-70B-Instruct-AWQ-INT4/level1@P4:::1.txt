This paper proposes a novel estimator, the self-normalized estimator, to address the propensity overfitting problem in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in prior works on BLBF, which can lead to degeneracies when used in Empirical Risk Minimization (ERM). They introduce a multiplicative control variate to derive the self-normalized estimator, which provably avoids these degeneracies.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem and their proposed solution. The introduction provides a good background on BLBF and the CRM principle, and the related work section is thorough and well-referenced. The technical sections are well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the self-normalized estimator and its properties.

The authors also provide an extensive empirical evaluation of their proposed method, Norm-POEM, which uses the self-normalized estimator. The results show that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional estimator, POEM, on several multi-label classification problems.

Strengths:

* The paper identifies a significant problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in prior works on BLBF and proposes a novel solution.
* The self-normalized estimator is well-motivated and theoretically sound.
* The empirical evaluation is extensive and well-designed, and the results are convincing.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a certain level of background knowledge in BLBF and the CRM principle, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors could provide more discussion on the computational complexity of Norm-POEM and how it compares to POEM.
* The paper could benefit from more analysis on the theoretical properties of the self-normalized estimator, such as its convergence rate and robustness to model misspecification.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and theoretically sound solution to a significant problem in BLBF.
* The empirical evaluation is extensive and well-designed, and the results are convincing.
* The paper is well-written and well-organized, making it easy to follow for experts in the field.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper assumes a certain level of background knowledge in BLBF and the CRM principle, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors could provide more discussion on the computational complexity of Norm-POEM and how it compares to POEM.
* The paper could benefit from more analysis on the theoretical properties of the self-normalized estimator.

Overall, I believe that the paper is well-suited for publication in NIPS, and I recommend acceptance. However, I suggest that the authors address the weaknesses mentioned above in their final version.
