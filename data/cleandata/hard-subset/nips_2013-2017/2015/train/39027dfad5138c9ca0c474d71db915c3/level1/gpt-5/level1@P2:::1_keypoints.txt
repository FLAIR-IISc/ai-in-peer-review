- Addresses BLBF/CRM, identifies "propensity overfitting" in IPS, and proposes SNIPS plus Norm-POEM with a variance penalty for structured stochastic policies.
- Situates work within Monte Carlo/IS and off-policy learning literature, extending prior CRM and connecting to domain adaptation bounds.
- Clear theoretical diagnosis and constructed example showing IPS pathologies (unboundedness, non-equivariance); SNIPS is motivated by boundedness, equivariance, and consistency.
- Variance penalty for SNIPS follows standard asymptotic/Delta-method derivations, but the paper lacks finite-sample generalization bounds for the SNIPS-based objective.
- Main theoretical bound covers capped IPS, not SNIPS; missing discussion/analysis of SNIPS bias and how variance regularization trades off bias vs. variance.
- Experimental design is solid and comparable to prior work; results consistently favor Norm-POEM and show translation-invariance and Ŝ(ĥ) ≈ 1 diagnostics.
- Empirical baselines are limited—missing doubly robust (DR), weighted/self-normalized DR, defensive mixtures, and well-tuned clipping comparisons—and no real logged data or synthetic propensity-controlled tests.
- Writing and organization are clear; the propensity overfitting example and Ŝ(h) connection to SNIPS are especially effective.
- Suggestions for clarity: explicitly state clipping within SNIPS and how M enters equations, provide Norm-POEM pseudo-code (objective, gradients, training loop), and report convergence criteria.
- Suggest reporting standard deviations/confidence intervals and clarifying the validation protocol for λ and M to avoid bias in counterfactual evaluation.
- Originality: diagnosing propensity overfitting and using SNIPS as both diagnostic and learning objective in CRM is novel and useful, despite SNIPS being a classical estimator.
- Significance: practically impactful for IPS-based BLBF; replacement with SNIPS improves robustness and accuracy and could influence practice, but impact would grow with broader baselines and real data.
- Pros: clear identification of failure mode, principled/simple fix, consistent empirical gains, useful diagnostic (Ŝ(h)), faster convergence and smaller norms, open-source code.
- Cons: no finite-sample theory for SNIPS-CRM, limited baselines, supervised-to-bandit-only experiments, and some unclear implementation details (clipping interaction with SNIPS and variance estimator).
- Recommended improvements: derive or discuss bounds for SNIPS under clipping, add DR/weighted-DR/defensive-mixture baselines, report sensitivity to M and λ with error bars, include a real-logs or synthetic-propensity experiment, and add training pseudo-code.
- Overall: strong paper with a clear diagnosis and effective solution; the reviewer leans toward acceptance contingent on stronger baselines and clearer theory/exposition.
