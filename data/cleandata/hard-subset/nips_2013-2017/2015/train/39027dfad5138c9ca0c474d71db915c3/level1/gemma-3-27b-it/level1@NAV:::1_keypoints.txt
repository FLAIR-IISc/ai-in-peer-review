- Addresses propensity overfitting in Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback (BLBF) causing biased counterfactual risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting and bases a new algorithm, Norm-POEM, on it.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides a consistency guarantee for the proposed estimator.  
- Empirical results on multi-label classification show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the standard POEM approach.  
- Clearly connects to CRM, causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation literature.  
- Identifies a previously unrecognized form of overfitting in importance-sampling-based estimators.  
- Provides a compelling explanatory example demonstrating estimator failure modes.  
- Adopts the self-normalized estimator from financial applications and demonstrates desirable properties like equivariance.  
- Strength: tackles an important and impactful problem in BLBF.  
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding and rigorous analysis.  
- Strength: extensive empirical validation with convincing comparisons to POEM.  
- Strength: well-written and accessible presentation.  
- Weakness: insufficiently detailed analysis of computational trade-offs between estimators.  
- Weakness: reliance on hyperparameter tuning (M, λ) needs deeper exploration and efficient selection strategies.  
- Weakness: experimental scope limited to linear hypothesis spaces; more complex models should be evaluated.  
- Recommendation: strong paper with minor weaknesses, reviewer recommends acceptance.
