- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and improving interactive systems using historical data.
- Identifies a critical failure mode called "propensity overfitting" where IPS-based ERM can minimize empirical risk by manipulating importance weights rather than true loss.
- Shows propensity overfitting can produce degenerate solutions with poor generalization and provides a clear illustrative example.
- Proposes replacing the unbiased IPS estimator with the self-normalized estimator from importance sampling to prevent propensity overfitting.
- Highlights desirable properties of the self-normalized estimator: boundedness within observed loss range and equivariance to constant loss shifts.
- Notes the self-normalized estimator is biased for finite samples but is strongly consistent.
- Develops Norm-POEM by integrating the self-normalized estimator and its variance estimate into the CRM framework.
- Empirical results on multi-label tasks demonstrate Norm-POEM is robust to propensity overfitting and outperforms the IPS-based predecessor.
- Strength: significant conceptual contribution by identifying propensity overfitting and providing strong intuition and examples.
- Strength: paper is very clear, well-organized, and distinguishes "loss overfitting" from "propensity overfitting."
- Strength: technically rigorous with proofs of estimator properties and thorough, controlled experiments validating claims.
- Strength: original application of a known estimator as a practical remedy in counterfactual learning.
- Weakness: limited discussion and characterization of the finite-sample bias and the bias-variance trade-off.
- Weakness: scalability concern because the variance term is not SGD-friendly, leading to reliance on L-BFGS for optimization.
- Weakness: experiments focus on linear exponential models; need discussion of applicability to high-capacity models like deep networks.
- Overall: strong, well-argued paper with minor weaknesses; results and insights merit acceptance.
