- Summary: Proposes a Self-Normalized Estimator to fix propensity overfitting in BLBF and introduces Norm-POEM, yielding improved generalization and robustness.

- Strength: Identifies propensity overfitting as a severe problem in conventional counterfactual risk estimation.

- Strength: Provides thorough theoretical analysis showing advantages of the Self-Normalized Estimator.

- Strength: Empirical results show Norm-POEM outperforms conventional POEM in generalization.

- Strength: The estimator is more robust to propensity overfitting.

- Weakness: Self-Normalized Estimator incurs higher computational cost, limiting large-scale applicability.

- Weakness: Norm-POEM’s optimization is non-convex and may require sophisticated optimization techniques.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited to a few datasets and may not generalize.

- Originality: Novel estimator and algorithm with original identification and mitigation of propensity overfitting.

- Significance: Findings have important implications for BLBF and applications like ad placement, web search, and recommender systems.

- Pro (accept): Novel, theoretically grounded approach with improved empirical performance and practical relevance.

- Con (reject concerns): Scalability, optimization difficulty, and narrow experimental scope warrant caution.

- Recommendation: Accept, but authors should address scalability, optimization, and broader evaluation in future work.
