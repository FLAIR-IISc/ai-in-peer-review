- Problem: Identifies propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and limitations of conventional counterfactual risk estimators used in CRM.
- Proposal: Introduces a self-normalized risk estimator and a new algorithm, Norm-POEM, to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Theoretical claim: Proves the self-normalized estimator is strongly consistent and avoids anomalies of the conventional estimator.
- Empirical claim: Shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple datasets and stabilizes propensity weight sums.
- Novelty: First identification of propensity overfitting in BLBF and novel application of self-normalization from importance sampling.
- Theoretical rigor: Provides thorough mathematical derivations and proofs supporting the estimator’s properties.
- Empirical validation: Experiments demonstrate superior generalization and empirical mitigation of propensity overfitting.
- Practical relevance: Method is computationally efficient and accompanied by open-source software.
- Weakness — clarity: Paper is dense; derivations (especially of the estimator and its variance) are hard to follow for non-experts.
- Weakness — experimental scope: Experiments are limited to multi-label classification; broader BLBF tasks (e.g., ad placement, recommendations) are not tested.
- Weakness — hyperparameters: Lacks in-depth analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, which may worry practitioners.
- Arguments for acceptance: Well-motivated problem, novel and theoretically sound solution, strong empirical results, and practical efficiency.
- Arguments against acceptance: Presentation clarity needs improvement and experimental scope is somewhat narrow.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance, with suggested improvements in clarity and broader experimental validation.
