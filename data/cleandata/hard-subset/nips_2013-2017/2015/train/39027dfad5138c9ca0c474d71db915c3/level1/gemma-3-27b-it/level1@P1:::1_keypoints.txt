- Problem addressed: Identifies propensity overfitting in counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF and proposes a self-normalized estimator to reduce bias.  
- Main claim: Self-normalized estimator has theoretical advantages and empirical superiority over the conventional estimator.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and mathematically rigorous with good connections to related work.  
- Experimental setup: Follows POEM for fair comparison; Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multi-label classification with ablations and Ŝ(ĥ) analysis.  
- Strength — Importance: Highlights a significant, under-addressed issue (propensity overfitting) in BLBF.  
- Strength — Theory: Provides a solid theoretical foundation for the self-normalized estimator’s bias/variance improvements.  
- Strength — Empirics: Empirical results across datasets support practical benefits; analysis clarifies mechanism.  
- Strength — Reproducibility: Code availability promotes reproducibility and further research.  
- Weakness — Computational analysis: Insufficient detail on runtime/complexity and scaling of the self-normalized estimator and variance estimation.  
- Weakness — Limited experiments: Evaluation limited to multi-label classification and CRF hypothesis space; robustness to other domains/logging policies not shown.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Sensitivity to M and λ not systematically explored.  
- Weakness — Literature connection: Could compare more deeply with other importance-sampling/variance-reduction techniques.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important issue, presents a sound solution with empirical validation, and is clearly presented with available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Inadequate computational complexity analysis, limited experimental scope, and insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity study.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand computational discussion, broaden experiments, and perform systematic hyperparameter studies.
