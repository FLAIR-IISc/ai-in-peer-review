- Problem addressed: identifies "propensity overfitting" in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), where standard counterfactual risk estimators become severely biased with complex hypothesis classes.
- Main contribution: proposes a self-normalized estimator and implements it in a new algorithm, Norm-POEM, to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Novelty/positioning: adapts self-normalized importance sampling ideas to BLBF—a novel application contrasted with prior unbiased estimators in CRM and related work.
- Theoretical support: provides strong theoretical justification, including Theorem 2 establishing consistency of the self-normalized estimator.
- Empirical validation: comprehensive experiments using standard methodology show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets.
- Ablation and analysis: useful ablation studies and direct analysis of Ŝ(ĥ) support the claim of reduced propensity overfitting.
- Clarity and presentation: paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses consistent notation; code is provided to aid reproducibility.
- Practical considerations: authors discuss computational efficiency and supply code, enhancing practical impact.
- Weakness — hyperparameters: hyperparameter tuning (M and λ) is underexplored; more guidance and sensitivity analysis are needed.
- Weakness — experimental scope: experiments are limited to multi-label classification with a specific feature map; broader hypothesis spaces and tasks are not tested.
- Weakness — variance estimator cost: the empirical variance estimator appears computationally expensive and lacks proposed approximations or efficient implementations.
- Weakness — relation to other methods: limited comparison/analysis against other variance-reduction approaches (e.g., clipped importance sampling) leaves relative advantages unclear.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important overlooked problem, offers a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution, and is well-presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental scope, computational complexity concerns, and insufficient hyperparameter discussion.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to expand hyperparameter discussion and explore variance-estimator approximations.
