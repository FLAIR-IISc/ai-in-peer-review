- Identifies "propensity overfitting" where IPS-based ERM exploits importance-weight variability to drive empirical risk arbitrarily low/high, harming generalization.
- Proposes replacing IPS with self-normalized importance sampling (SNIPS), treating the sum of weights as a multiplicative control variate.
- Argues SNIPS provides bounded, loss-translation-equivariant, and strongly consistent estimates, avoiding IPS anomalies.
- Instantiates CRM with SNIPS and an approximate variance estimator to produce Norm-POEM for structured prediction in exponential-family models.
- Empirical results (supervised→bandit protocol) show consistent improvements over POEM, robustness to loss translation, and useful diagnostic Ŝ(h)≈1.
- Experiments report competitive runtime and code availability.
- Theory: clear explanation and proof of SNIPS strong consistency, but no finite-sample generalization bound for SNIPS-based CRM analogous to prior IPS results.
- Theoretical issues: variance regularizer is approximate; interaction of clipping with SNIPS and small-denominator bias not formally bounded.
- Methodology: Norm-POEM objective is well specified and L-BFGS for nonconvex optimization is reasonable.
- Experiments: appropriate protocol and datasets but baselines are limited—missing doubly robust/normalized DR and other variance-reduced IPS variants.
- Experimental analyses lack sufficient sensitivity studies (clipping M, λ), ablations, and statistical error bars or significance tests.
- Clarity: well written and organized; phenomenon and benefits are explained intuitively and formally; some implementation details (factorization, hyperparameter search, numerical safeguards) need expansion.
- Originality: SNIPS itself is known; main novelty is identifying propensity overfitting and applying SNIPS/Norm-POEM as a principled remedy.
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem in off-policy learning with a simple, broadly applicable fix that improves robustness.
- Strengths: clear identification of failure mode, simple principled fix, practical algorithm with empirical gains and diagnostics.
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical guarantees, incomplete experimental baselines and sensitivity analyses, scalability to truly structured outputs not demonstrated.
- Suggestions: derive empirical Bernstein-style generalization bounds for SNIPS CRM, include sensitivity plots and CIs, compare to DR/weighted DR, test partial-support logging, clarify model factorization and scalability, add denominator safeguards.
- Arguments for acceptance: important failure mode identified, simple effective remedy with empirical support, clear writing and practical impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: estimator is known and novelty is application-focused, missing finite-sample theory, and incomplete experimental evaluation.
- Overall recommendation: practice-relevant contribution with consistent empirical gains and clear exposition; leans toward acceptance pending stronger theory and baseline analyses.
