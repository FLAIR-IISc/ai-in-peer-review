- Counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) learns from logged bandit data by correcting for logging bias.
- The paper identifies an overfitting failure mode where hypotheses exploit example generation probabilities.
- Overfitting occurs when a hypothesis gains stronger support from the logged data via importance weights.
- Proposed solution: a multiplicative (self-normalized) parameter that bounds variance while maintaining consistency.
- Theoretical result: the self-normalized estimator preserves consistency and controls variance.
- Empirically, the normalized (multiplicative) CRM outperforms the non-normalized variant.
- Reviewer opinion: overall a good paper and should be accepted, though the reviewer is not fully certain.
