- The paper's commentary and notation around equation (2) are misleading: the loss in (2) is not the expected quantity and the expectation interpretation is incorrect.  
- The claim that (2) has an "anomaly" (non-invariance to translation of the loss) is technically incorrect given the above misunderstanding.  
- Example 1 is based on this wrong presumption and therefore does not hold.  
- The estimator in (2) is sensitive to its denominator π, which can be zero if a sample was never seen in the prior.  
- The modified estimator in (7) is preferable in principle because the double denominator mollifies the effects of π.  
- Reviewer requests the authors revise their commentary if the paper is accepted.  
- Experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and approaches CRF performance using full multi-label information.  
- The paper focuses on counterfactual learning (a specialization of multi-label learning with bandit feedback) and aims to improve the POEM estimator from ICML 2015.  
- Overall the paper is convincing and worth publication, subject to the noted remarks.
