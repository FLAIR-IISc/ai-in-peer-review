- Identifies a new form of overfitting in BLBF called "propensity overfitting" that biases conventional CRM counterfactual risk estimators.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates, leading to the Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Shows conventional estimator can give risk estimates outside the loss range and is not equivariant under loss translation, enabling overfitting to propensity weights.  
- Demonstrates the self-normalized estimator is bounded within the loss range, equivariant under loss translation, and strongly consistent.  
- Provides strong theoretical contributions with clear proofs (Theorem 2) and illustrative examples (Section 4) analyzing propensity overfitting.  
- Derives Norm-POEM from CRM principles and gives a tractable implementation using L-BFGS optimization.  
- Empirical evaluation on four multi-label datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL) shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM in generalization.  
- Presents evidence that Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting (Ŝ(ĥ) values close to 1) and that variance regularization remains useful.  
- Reports faster convergence for Norm-POEM compared to POEM due to improved optimization properties.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with effective illustrative examples and a good connection to importance-sampling control variates.  
- Appropriately situates work within related literature (e.g., Swaminathan & Joachims) and causal/off-policy evaluation contexts.  
- Main novelty is the self-normalized estimator—a meaningful refinement to CRM rather than an entirely new framework.  
- Significance is moderate: relevant to applications like ads/recommendation, but real-world impact is not convincingly demonstrated beyond controlled datasets.  
- Limitations: lacks variance estimates across runs, needs sensitivity experiments to different logging policies (h0), and broader real-world validation; recommends acceptance contingent on additional experiments.
