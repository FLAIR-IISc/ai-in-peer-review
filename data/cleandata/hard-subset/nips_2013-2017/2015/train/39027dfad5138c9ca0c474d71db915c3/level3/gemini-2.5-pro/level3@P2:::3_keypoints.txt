- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback and identifies "propensity overfitting" as a major flaw in the standard unbiased CRM estimator.  
- Propensity overfitting occurs when high-capacity models exploit importance weights instead of minimizing true loss, producing biased/degenerate risk estimates.  
- Proposes using a self-normalized risk estimator (control variate using sum of importance weights) to mitigate this failure mode.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, an adaptation of the POEM algorithm that uses the self-normalized estimator.  
- Provides theoretical properties of the estimator: equivariant, bounded, biased but consistent, and avoids anomalies of the unbiased estimator.  
- Supplies a variance estimator suitable for inclusion in the CRM objective.  
- Empirical results on multi-label classification datasets show Norm-POEM consistently and substantially outperforms POEM.  
- Novelty: main insight is identifying propensity overfitting; technical fix is applying a known Monte Carlo self-normalized estimator to CRM.  
- Strength — Quality: technically sound, clear example illustrating the problem, and well-principled solution using control variates.  
- Strength — Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and situates itself clearly relative to prior work.  
- Strength — Significance: problem is practically important and demonstrated performance gains suggest real impact.  
- Weakness — Originality: core technical contribution is an application of an existing estimator, making the work an incremental correction rather than a major conceptual advance.  
- Weakness — Experimental scope: evaluations reuse simulated "Supervised -> Bandit" setups and datasets from POEM; lacks additional datasets or real-world logged bandit experiments.  
- Weakness — Optimization analysis: observation that Norm-POEM converges faster than POEM is interesting but underanalyzed; deeper study of optimization behavior is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: identifies and fixes a subtle critical flaw, offers a simple/theoretically motivated solution, shows consistent empirical gains, and is clearly presented.  
- Arguments for rejection: contribution may be seen as incremental follow-up, and experimental validation is limited to simulations rather than real-world logs.  
- Detailed suggestions: report standard deviations/errors (not just means) for Table 1; comment on robustness to loss scaling as well as translation; further analyze optimization/convergence behavior.
