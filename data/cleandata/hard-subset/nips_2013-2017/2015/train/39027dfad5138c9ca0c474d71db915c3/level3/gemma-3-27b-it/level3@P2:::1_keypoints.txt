- Identifies propensity overfitting as a critical issue in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Shows standard importance-sampling estimators are unbiased but vulnerable to overfitting when historical data lacks exploration.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (Norm-POEM) to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Provides theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation on multi-label classification demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Builds on Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) and connects to causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation.  
- Distinguishes propensity overfitting from mere large weight variance, highlighting hypothesis-space capacity as an exacerbating factor.  
- Strength: addresses an important and underappreciated problem in BLBF.  
- Strength: offers a sensible, pragmatic solution with appealing properties (e.g., equivariance).  
- Strength: empirical results show Norm-POEM outperforms conventional approaches and includes a full-information CRF benchmark.  
- Strength: code is provided, aiding reproducibility.  
- Strength: paper is generally well-written and logically structured.  
- Weakness: needs clearer explanation of how large weight variance concretely leads to propensity overfitting.  
- Weakness: claim that prior BLBF work universally used unbiased estimators overlooks common clipping/variance-reduction practices.  
- Weakness: Example 1 (Line 199) needs clarification on why small n relative to k allows extreme empirical risk for overfit hypotheses.  
- Weakness: deeper theoretical analysis of the self-normalized estimator, particularly w.r.t. hypothesis-space capacity, would strengthen the paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a relevant problem, proposes a motivated solution, shows strong empirical performance, provides code, and enhances understanding of off-policy evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: variance–overfitting connection unclear, prior-work claim needs refinement, and more theoretical depth desired.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with insightful identification of propensity overfitting and a practical remedy, despite minor weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
