- Paper tackles batch learning from logged bandit feedback, diagnoses "propensity overfitting" in IPS-based ERM, and proposes SNIPS-based Norm-POEM with consistency proofs and empirical gains.
- Main contribution: reframe self-normalized IPS (SNIPS) as a learning-time fix to propensity overfitting and integrate it into CRM (Norm-POEM).
- Strength: Clearly identifies propensity overfitting as a distinct ERM pathology tied to empirical sum of weights Ŝ(h).
- Strength: Sensible remedy using self-normalization that yields bounded, loss-translation-equivariant finite-sample behavior.
- Strength: Provides theoretical sanity checks (strong consistency) and a practical variance estimate via delta/Normal approximation.
- Strength: Empirical results consistently favor Norm-POEM and diagnostics (Ŝ(ĥ) near 1) support the proposed mechanism; code is provided.
- Weakness/ask: Relationship between propensity overfitting and high weight variance is underspecified and needs clarification.
- Weakness/ask: When does large weight variance imply high risk of propensity overfitting for rich hypothesis classes H?
- Weakness/ask: Can |Ŝ(h) − 1| be bounded in terms of weight variance, higher moments, or ESS; request error decomposition into loss vs weight contributions.
- Weakness/ask: Are there cases where weight variance is large but SNIPS gives limited protection, or moderate variance yet Ŝ(h) still causes overfitting?
- Weakness/ask: Example 1 (Line 199) is confusing—clarify whether claim about hoverfit selecting holds for pure ERM (λ=0) or under CRM as well.
- Weakness/ask: Explain why CRM’s variance penalty (and clipping M) wouldn’t prevent hoverfit selection, and how ties among hoverfit′ are broken in practice.
- Weakness/ask: Suggests a small synthetic study varying n/k, λ, and M to illustrate ERM vs CRM vs Norm-POEM behavior on hoverfit.
- Weakness/ask: Prior-work claim that “virtually all BLBF works” use unbiased estimators is overstated—need to nuance and position relative to clipping/truncation, DR, and weighted/normalized variants.
- Weakness/ask: Reviewer did not verify technical details: delta/Normal variance estimate (Eq. 8), asymptotic normality under clipping, and L-BFGS optimization for nonconvex objectives.
- Weakness/ask: Missing baselines—include DR, truncated IPS (+CRM), and SNIPS without CRM to isolate normalization vs CRM effects.
- Weakness/ask: Request sensitivity analyses for clipping threshold M and regularization λ, plus reporting ESS and distribution of Ŝ(h) across hypotheses during training.
- Quality: Idea is technically sound and well-motivated; proofs plausible though not fully checked; estimator properties support claims.
- Clarity: Generally clear and organized, but needs sharper discussion linking weight variance, ESS, and propensity overfitting and clarification in Example 1.
- Originality: SNIPS is classical; novelty lies in diagnosing propensity overfitting and integrating SNIPS into CRM with analysis and experiments.
- Significance: Practical and important for training from logs in ranking/ads/recommendation; solution is simple and likely adoptable.
- Pros: Identifies an under-discussed failure mode, provides a simple principled remedy, shows consistent empirical gains, and shares code.
- Cons: Lacks formal ESS-based analysis, overstates prior-unbiased claims, has limited baselines/sensitivity studies, and contains clarity issues (Example 1, tie-breaking).
- Recommendation: Accept—idea is sound, problem important, experiments convincing, and community would benefit despite some open technical/verificational questions.
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate-to-low due to limited time to verify all technical details.
