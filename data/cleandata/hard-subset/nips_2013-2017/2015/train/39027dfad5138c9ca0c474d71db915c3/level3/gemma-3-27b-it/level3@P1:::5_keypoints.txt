- Addresses propensity overfitting in the counterfactual risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator and derives Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm to mitigate this bias.  
- Theoretical derivation is rigorous; Theorem 2 establishes estimator consistency.  
- Situates work well within CRM, causal inference, domain adaptation, and importance sampling literature.  
- Identifies “propensity overfitting” as a distinct issue and applies self-normalization insightfully.  
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multi-label classification datasets and is faster in practice.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, includes illustrative examples, and provides software for reproducibility.  
- Theoretical/computational complexity of the variance estimation (Eq. 8) is not fully analyzed.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (λ and M) is underexplored; lacks guidance for selection.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to multi-label classification; missing tests on continuous actions or complex rewards.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, offers a sound theory-backed solution, shows empirical improvements, and has practical applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential computational limitations and limited experimental scope.  
- Overall: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address complexity analysis and hyperparameter sensitivity.
