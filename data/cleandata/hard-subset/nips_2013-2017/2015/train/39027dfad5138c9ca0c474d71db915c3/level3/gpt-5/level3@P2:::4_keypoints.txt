- Identifies propensity overfitting in IPS-based empirical risk minimization where importance weight variability is exploited to reduce weight sum rather than true loss.  
- Proposes self-normalized IPS (SNIPS) using a multiplicative control variate to obtain a bounded, equivariant estimator that remedies IPS anomalies.  
- Proves strong consistency for SNIPS and provides a practical variance estimator within the CRM framework.  
- Instantiates CRM with SNIPS to create Norm-POEM, an algorithm for structured prediction with variance-regularized objectives.  
- Empirical results on multi-label datasets show consistent improvements of Norm-POEM over POEM, with sanity checks on translation equivariance and weight sums.  
- Situates work relative to prior importance-weighting and counterfactual learning literature (IPS/clipping, CRM, doubly robust, classical self-normalized IS).  
- Strength: Clear diagnosis and theoretical framing of propensity overfitting with intuitive examples.  
- Strength: Well-motivated use of self-normalized IS; boundedness and equivariance directly address IPS failures.  
- Strength: Consistency proof and practical variance estimator are sound and appropriately adapted.  
- Strength: Concrete algorithm with empirical gains and relevant diagnostics for BLBF applications.  
- Weakness: Some clarity and notation issues, including loss scaling/sign conventions and minor typos.  
- Weakness: Limited comparisons to alternative variance-reduction methods (notably doubly robust and hybrids).  
- Weakness: Limited sensitivity analyses (e.g., clipping threshold M and regularization λ).  
- Weakness: Theoretical generalization bounds are inherited from CRM with IPS; lacks a bound tailored to SNIPS within CRM.  
- Quality: Technically sound overall; empirical evaluation supports claims but could include DR baselines and more bias–variance exploration.  
- Clarity: Generally readable and well organized, but needs clarification on loss range/sign, stochastic loss modeling, and variance-approximation details.  
- Originality: Novel application of multiplicative control variates/SNIPS to prevent propensity overfitting within CRM and demonstrated impact on BLBF.  
- Significance: Practically important for counterfactual learning from logs with likely adoption and extension potential.  
- Requested clarification: Precisely explain loss scaling/sign conventions and how δ is rescaled in experiments (δ ∈ [−1,0] vs positive losses).  
- Requested clarification: Formalize δ as a random variable and define true risk with nested expectations to account for label/noise stochasticity.  
- Requested corrections: Fix typos/notation (importance weight ratio, argmin vs argmax, hats vs bars for empirical quantities) and acknowledge prior notes on IPS nonlinearity.  
- Methodology suggestions: Compare to doubly robust and self-normalized DR variants, report sensitivity to clipping M and λ, and analyze robustness to near-zero propensities.  
- Computational questions: Clarify per-iteration cost vs POEM, number of L-BFGS iterations, and why SNIPS leads to smaller weight norms in practice.  
- Overall assessment: Valuable and original contribution diagnosing propensity overfitting and offering a principled SNIPS-based fix; recommend acceptance after addressing clarity and comparison points.  
- Post-rebuttal ratings: Quality 6, Clarity 6, Originality 8, Significance 8.
