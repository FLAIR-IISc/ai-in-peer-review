- Identifies propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) as a distinct problem from large importance-weight variance.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting and motivates it well.  
- Provides clear, concise exposition of the BLBF setting and the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle.  
- Offers a thorough analysis of causes and consequences of propensity overfitting.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Related work is detailed and the contribution is clearly differentiated from prior research.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing across multiple settings.  
- Authors release code, aiding reproducibility and adoption.  
- Needs clearer explanation of how large weight variance and propensity overfitting are related.  
- Clarification requested for behavior of the estimator (line 199) when sample size n is small relative to number of actions k.  
- Questions the claim that prior BLBF works universally used unbiased counterfactual risk estimators; some used clipping and introduced bias.  
- Recommends more nuanced discussion of bias–variance trade-offs in existing estimators.  
- Reviewer leans to accept: idea is sensible, experiments convincing, but did not fully verify all technical details.
