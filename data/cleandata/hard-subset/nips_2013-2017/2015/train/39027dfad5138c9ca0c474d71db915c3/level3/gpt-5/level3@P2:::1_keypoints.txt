- Paper tackles learning from logged bandit feedback via CRM and identifies a failure mode called propensity overfitting where IPS-based ERM exploits logging propensities.  
- Propensity overfitting can produce degenerate empirical risks outside the loss range or sensitive to additive loss translations.  
- Proposed remedy: use a multiplicative control variate leading to a self-normalized IPS (SNIPS) estimator inside CRM.  
- Theoretical contributions include a strong consistency proof and analysis of finite-sample properties (boundedness and equivariance).  
- Algorithmic contribution: Norm-POEM for exponential-family policies with variance-regularized CRM using an approximate variance formula for the ratio estimator.  
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multilabel datasets, is robust to loss translations, and yields diagnostic Ŝ(ĥ) ≈ 1.  
- Practical impact: SNIPS is an easy drop-in replacement for IPS and the authors provide code for reproducibility.  
- Paper situates itself relative to prior BLBF/CRM work (POEM) and off-policy evaluation techniques (IPS, clipping, DR).  
- Novelty lies in highlighting propensity overfitting as a learning-time pathology and integrating SNIPS into the learning objective.  
- Reviewer requests clearer delineation of how SNIPS differs from prior clipping and DR-based approaches.  
- Strength: identifies a real, under-discussed failure mode distinct from mere high variance.  
- Strength: SNIPS offers a simple, principled fix with clear finite-sample advantages.  
- Strength: provides a reasonable approximate variance estimate to enable CRM regularization.  
- Strength: experiments are clean, include useful diagnostics, and show robust improvements; code available.  
- Weakness: relationship between propensity overfitting and importance-weight variance is not fully clarified and needs an explicit statement.  
- Weakness: lacks a dedicated generalization bound for SNIPS-CRM analogous to prior CRM results.  
- Weakness: limited empirical baselines—should compare to clipped-IPS POEM, DR-based learners, and self-normalized DR.  
- Weakness: wording about “unbiased estimator” in prior work should be clarified given prevalent clipping practices.  
- Weakness: the illustrative “k vs n” example needs a clearer derivation to aid reader understanding.  
- Weakness: brief discussion on scalability to very large action spaces and stochastic optimization; more practical guidance needed.  
- Quality: technically sound and well written overall but missing a formal learning bound for the SNIPS-CRM objective.  
- Suggestion: add a lemma or visualization relating propensity overfitting to importance-weight variance and show how ERM can push Ŝ(h) away from 1.  
- Suggestion: provide a generalization result or discussion on when the approximate variance is reliable.  
- Suggestion: include additional baselines (clipped-IPS POEM, DR learners, self-normalized DR) in experiments.  
- Suggestion: clarify “unbiased” language, expand the k-vs-n example derivation, and report sensitivity to M and λ and validation bias concerns.  
- Reproducibility: code, standard datasets, and hyperparameter tuning protocol are provided.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear identification of an important failure mode, principled remedy, strong empirical evidence, and practical adoptability.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing theoretical generalization bound for SNIPS-CRM and limited baselines; some exposition could be clearer.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: moderate.
