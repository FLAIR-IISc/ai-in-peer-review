This paper, "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning", presents a significant contribution to the field of batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, known as Propensity Overfitting, and propose a novel self-normalized risk estimator to address this issue. The new estimator is shown to be more robust and effective in avoiding overfitting, and is used to develop a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the technical details. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain the limitations of existing approaches. The proposed self-normalized risk estimator is rigorously analyzed, and its advantages over the conventional estimator are demonstrated through theoretical and empirical results.

The experiments presented in the paper are comprehensive and well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of Norm-POEM in various multi-label classification tasks. The results show that Norm-POEM outperforms the conventional POEM algorithm and is competitive with a full-information CRF.

However, there are a few issues with the paper that need to be addressed. Firstly, the notation and terminology used in the paper are not always clear, and some definitions are missing. For example, the definition of the hypothesis space H is not provided until Section 3. Additionally, the authors' claim that the conventional estimator has an "anomaly" (non-invariance to translation of the loss) is technically incorrect, and the example provided in Section 4 is based on this wrong presumption.

Furthermore, the estimator in Equation (2) is sensitive to its denominator π, which can be zero if a sample was never seen in the prior. The authors should provide more discussion on how to handle this issue.

Despite these minor issues, the paper is convincing and worth publication, subject to revisions. The authors' contributions to the field of BLBF are significant, and their work has the potential to impact the development of more robust and effective learning algorithms.

**Strengths:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective solution to the problem of Propensity Overfitting in BLBF.
* The self-normalized risk estimator is rigorously analyzed, and its advantages over the conventional estimator are demonstrated through theoretical and empirical results.
* The experiments are comprehensive and well-designed, demonstrating the effectiveness of Norm-POEM in various multi-label classification tasks.

**Weaknesses:**

* The notation and terminology used in the paper are not always clear, and some definitions are missing.
* The authors' claim that the conventional estimator has an "anomaly" (non-invariance to translation of the loss) is technically incorrect.
* The estimator in Equation (2) is sensitive to its denominator π, which can be zero if a sample was never seen in the prior.

**Suggestions for improvement:**

* Provide clear definitions for all notation and terminology used in the paper.
* Correct the technical error in the authors' claim about the conventional estimator's anomaly.
* Provide more discussion on how to handle the issue of the estimator in Equation (2) being sensitive to its denominator π.
* Consider adding more experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of Norm-POEM in different scenarios.
