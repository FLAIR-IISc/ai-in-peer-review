- Addresses propensity overfitting in counterfactual risk estimators for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Demonstrates that standard estimator can produce severely biased risk estimates, especially with complex hypothesis spaces.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting, yielding the Norm-POEM algorithm.
- Empirically shows Norm-POEM outperforms existing approaches on the evaluated benchmarks.
- Theoretical support provided, with Theorem 2 guaranteeing strong consistency.
- Builds on the authors’ prior work and connects to causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation.
- Paper is well-written, with clear, concise mathematical presentation and thorough related work.
- Experimental evaluation is limited, relying on the same datasets as prior work [1], raising generalizability concerns.
- Unclear real-world impact; needs validation on more diverse or industry-sourced datasets.
- Insufficient analysis of the training-objective optimization process and choice of L-BFGS.
- Missing variance reporting across repeated runs, leaving algorithm stability unquantified.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel important problem, elegant theoretically sound solution, empirical gains, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, unclear practical impact, weak optimization analysis, no variance estimates.
- Overall: solid contribution but recommends Weak Accept contingent on major revisions (expand experiments and report variance).
