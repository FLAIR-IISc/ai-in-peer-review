- Paper is an interesting, well-written contribution to batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Identifies a significant problem termed "propensity overfitting" with the conventional unbiased risk estimator.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator as a novel, more robust solution.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making arguments and technical details easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough related-work review that situates the contribution within BLBF literature.  
- Balances theoretical and empirical contributions effectively.  
- Gives rigorous theoretical analysis, including consistency proof and a variance bound for the estimator.  
- Presents extensive empirical evaluation demonstrating practical effectiveness.  
- Code is provided to facilitate replication and extension.  
- Suggests clearer explanation of how large weight variance relates to propensity overfitting.  
- Requests clarification of estimator behavior when sample size n is small relative to k (line 199).  
- Recommends addressing the claim that prior BLBF works used unbiased estimators, since some used clipping (introducing bias).  
- Recommends acceptance, noting the contribution’s strengths despite not fully verifying technical details.
