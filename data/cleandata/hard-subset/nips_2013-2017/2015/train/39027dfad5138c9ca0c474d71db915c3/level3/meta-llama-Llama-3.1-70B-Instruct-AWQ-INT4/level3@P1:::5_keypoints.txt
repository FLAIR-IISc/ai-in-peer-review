- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies a critical failure mode in prior estimators.
- Identifies "propensity overfitting": conventional counterfactual risk estimators are not equivariant and can misestimate risk under constant translations.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator derived from a multiplicative control variate that is equivariant and reduces variance.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a CRM-based learning algorithm that uses the self-normalized estimator.
- Provides theoretical analysis proving consistency and giving a variance bound for the proposed estimator and algorithm.
- Empirical evaluation on several multi-label classification tasks shows Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting and outperforms original POEM.
- Contributions are original, meaningfully extend POEM, and offer a significantly new formulation with practical improvements.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and situates the work within related literature effectively.
- Strength: novel method that addresses a concrete failure mode and yields better generalization empirically.
- Strength: thorough theoretical treatment and variance reduction argument for the estimator.
- Weakness: assumes substantial ML/statistics background, potentially limiting accessibility to non-experts.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a few multi-label tasks, leaving generality to other problem types unclear.
- Weakness: lacks clear comparisons with other state-of-the-art BLBF algorithms beyond POEM.
- Overall recommendation: acceptance — significant contribution warranting further research.
