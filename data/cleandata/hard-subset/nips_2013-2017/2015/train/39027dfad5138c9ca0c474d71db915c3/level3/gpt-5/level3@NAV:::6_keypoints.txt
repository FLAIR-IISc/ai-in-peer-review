- Problem: studies batch learning from logged bandit feedback under CRM and identifies propensity overfitting of IPS estimators.
- Failure mode: hypotheses can “game” the sum of importance weights, causing propensity overfitting.
- Proposed fix: replace IPS with a multiplicative control variate (self-normalized importance sampling) to form a self-normalized risk estimator.
- Instantiation: introduce Norm-POEM, a structured prediction learner using the self-normalized estimator.
- Theoretical properties: estimator is equivariant to loss translation, bounded, and strongly consistent.
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on several multi-label datasets; diagnostics (Ŝ ≈ 1) support the mechanism.
- Positioning: well-situated relative to prior work on importance weighting, CRM, variance reduction, doubly robust methods, and self-normalized IS.
- Main conceptual contribution: diagnosing propensity overfitting via weight-sum deviations and addressing it with a ratio estimator in the learning objective.
- Quality: theoretical claims are correct and relevant; empirical evidence is solid.
- Limitation — theory: no finite-sample generalization bounds for the normalized CRM objective (unlike IPS-motivated CRM).
- Limitation — variance estimation: approximate variance estimator relies on asymptotic arguments.
- Limitation — experiments: limited baselines (no doubly robust or clipping/DR comparisons) and experiments only on multi-label classification.
- Clarity: paper is clearly written, well-organized, and exposition is compelling.
- Originality: applying classical self-normalized IS inside CRM and formalizing propensity overfitting to derive Norm-POEM is novel.
- Significance: simple, broadly applicable method addressing an important practical issue with consistent empirical gains.
- Recommendation: reviewer leans to accept, with reservations about missing finite-sample theory and limited baselines/experiments.
