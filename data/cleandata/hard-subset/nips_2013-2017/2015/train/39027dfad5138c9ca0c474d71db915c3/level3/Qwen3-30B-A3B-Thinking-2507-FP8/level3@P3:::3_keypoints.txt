- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies "propensity overfitting" in standard CRM estimators.
- Shows the standard unbiased risk estimator can produce arbitrarily low risk estimates for some hypotheses.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates to avoid propensity overfitting.
- Provides a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on the self-normalized estimator.
- Theoretical analysis is solid, with concrete examples and a consistency proof (Theorem 2).
- Properly connects to control variates and importance sampling literature, citing relevant prior work.
- Empirical results show consistent improvements of Norm-POEM over POEM on multiple multi-label datasets.
- Analysis of the propensity weight sum Ŝ(h) supports that the self-normalized estimator avoids propensity overfitting.
- Paper is clear and well-written; examples effectively illustrate the problem's severity.
- Experimental scope is limited: only synthetic bandit feedback on standard datasets, no real-world logged data.
- Unclear how modest empirical gains translate to practical improvements in deployed systems.
- Lacks reporting of variance/repeated runs to establish statistical significance of results.
- Needs more discussion of training objective optimization and computational efficiency claims.
- Should compare against more recent BLBF approaches and address practical implementation challenges.
- Overall: technically sound, valuable incremental contribution; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing experimental and analysis limitations.
