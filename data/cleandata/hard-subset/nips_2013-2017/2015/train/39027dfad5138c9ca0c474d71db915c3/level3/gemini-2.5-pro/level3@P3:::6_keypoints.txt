- Problem addressed: learning from logged bandit feedback; identifies "propensity overfitting" where complex hypotheses exploit importance weights to get artificially low empirical risk.  
- Standard approach: Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) uses an importance-weighted estimator that can fail via propensity overfitting.  
- Proposed solution: replace unbiased estimator with a self-normalized (ratio) estimator motivated by multiplicative control variates.  
- Estimator properties: self-normalized estimator is consistent, equivariant to loss shifts, and bounds estimated risk within observed loss range.  
- Algorithm: instantiate approach as Norm-POEM and evaluate on multi-label classification tasks.  
- Empirical result: Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms standard POEM; diagnostics confirm the mechanism (Tables/Figures).  
- Strength: Clear identification and simple illustrative example of a significant failure mode (propensity overfitting).  
- Strength: Principled, well-motivated solution connecting to established statistical techniques.  
- Strength: Strong and convincing empirical evaluation, including diagnostics showing robustness and correction of propensity bias.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and well-organized.  
- Weakness: Theoretical concern: substituting a biased self-normalized estimator into CRM’s bound lacks formal justification—implications for the CRM principle are unclear.  
- Weakness: Needs deeper discussion of finite-sample bias of the ratio estimator and its bias–variance trade-off, especially for small samples.  
- Weakness: Scope/generalization: applicability and challenges for higher-capacity models (e.g., deep nets) are not fully discussed.  
- Overall summary: Strong, valuable contribution with solid empirical support; would be improved by deeper theoretical analysis of the biased estimator within CRM.
