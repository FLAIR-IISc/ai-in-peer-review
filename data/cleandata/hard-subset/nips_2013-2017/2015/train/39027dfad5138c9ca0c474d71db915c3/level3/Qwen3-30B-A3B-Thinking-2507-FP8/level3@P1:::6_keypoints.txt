- Identifies "propensity overfitting" in counterfactual risk estimation for BLBF, where importance-weighted estimators can produce arbitrarily low risk estimates.  
- Propensity overfitting arises because the estimator depends on the sum of propensity weights Ŝ(h), which can deviate significantly from its expected value of 1.  
- This failure mode persists even after loss translation or clipping, unlike standard supervised loss overfitting.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator R̂SN(h) = [Σ δi h(yi|xi)/pi] / [Σ h(yi|xi)/pi] using multiplicative control variates to bound estimates and ensure equivariance under loss translation.  
- Theoretical results (Theorems 1–2) establish consistency and provide formal diagnostics for the problem.  
- Empirical results on four multi-label datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL) show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM.  
- Norm-POEM avoids the propensity overfitting symptom (Ŝ(h) ≈ 1) and achieves faster convergence despite higher per-iteration cost.  
- Most dramatic empirical gain on TMC where POEM fails catastrophically (worse than random).  
- Originality: novel identification of propensity overfitting for BLBF and novel application of self-normalized estimators to CRM.  
- Quality: sound theoretical arguments, clear diagnostics (Example 1, Table 2), thorough experiments with statistical significance and ablations.  
- Significance: advances BLBF reliability for real-world systems, achieving near-supervised "skyline" performance.  
- Clarity: well-structured exposition and clear theory–practice connection, though Example 1 could use concrete numeric illustration.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to multi-label classification; broader structured-prediction validation (ranking, sequence labeling) is missing.  
- Weakness: lacks explicit runtime comparison to stochastic-optimization variants of POEM despite claiming faster convergence.  
- Weakness: minor clarity improvement possible by introducing the term "propensity" earlier for non-expert readers.  
- Pro: elegant, provably consistent solution with strong empirical support and direct practical relevance.  
- Con: limited experimental generality and minor missed opportunity to numerically illustrate Example 1.  
- Overall verdict: technically sound, impactful contribution; strongly recommended for acceptance.
