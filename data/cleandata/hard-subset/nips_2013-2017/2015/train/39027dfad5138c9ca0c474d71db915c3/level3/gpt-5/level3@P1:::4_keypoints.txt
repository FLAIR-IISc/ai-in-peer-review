- Paper diagnoses "propensity overfitting" in BLBF/CRM where IPS-based ERM exploits weight variability rather than true loss.  
- Proposes SNIPS (self-normalized IPS) as a multiplicative control variate to obtain bounded, equivariant risk estimates that mitigate this degeneracy.  
- Instantiates SNIPS within CRM as Norm-POEM for exponential-family (CRF-like) policies and reports consistent empirical gains over POEM on multi-label tasks.  
- Approach connects to classical importance-sampling variance reduction (SNIS), off-policy evaluation, and doubly robust estimators.  
- Strength: Clear formalization and a compelling toy example that makes propensity overfitting concrete.  
- Strength: SNIPS provides boundedness and translation equivariance, directly addressing IPS anomalies in ERM.  
- Strength: Consistency result for the estimator and a reasonable variance approximation for CRM regularization.  
- Strength: Practical Norm-POEM algorithm with tractable gradients and competitive runtime.  
- Strength: Solid empirical evidence and diagnostics (Ŝ(ĥ) ≈ 1) that align with the theory.  
- Weakness: No finite-sample generalization bound for the SNIPS-based CRM objective despite SNIPS bias; need bias/variance decomposition and role of clipping M and λ.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to doubly robust (DR) or weighted-DR baselines, which also mitigate weight variance.  
- Weakness: Limited sensitivity analysis for clipping M and variance regularizer λ and lack of documented failure cases.  
- Weakness: Results rely on full support; paper should discuss effects of support violations and the potential benefit of a defensive-mixture logging policy.  
- Clarification requested: Explain loss scaling—whether δ is rescaled to [−1,0] and confirm SNIPS equivariance vs IPS sensitivity to translations.  
- Clarification requested: Model stochastic losses explicitly as δ(x,y) with E[δ|x,y] and define true risk accordingly to handle noisy feedback.  
- Clarification requested: State conditions for the asymptotic variance approximation (Eq. 8) and whether clipping is applied inside variance computation.  
- Specific correction: Line 170–171—use h(y|x)/h0(y|x) inside min{·,·} for u_h and note added nonconvexity from normalization.  
- Specific correction: Line 276—use argmin (not argmax) for training objective.  
- Specific correction: Line 319—use hats (e.g., V̂) not bars for empirical estimates; ensure consistent hats for R̂, Ŝ and clarify clipping in numerator/denominator.  
- Quality assessment: Methodology is sound and insightful but missing finite-sample bounds; empirics convincing but could be broadened.  
- Clarity assessment: Generally clear; fix noted typos/notation and add a “Practical tuning” paragraph for M, λ and Ŝ diagnostics.  
- Originality assessment: Novel identification of propensity overfitting and principled use of SNIPS in CRM despite SNIPS being classical.  
- Significance assessment: Impactful for BLBF in large-scale systems; simple, principled change yields robust gains and a practical diagnostic.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Identifies a real failure mode, provides a principled fix with consistency, and shows consistent empirical improvements.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Lacks finite-sample SNIPS-CRM bounds, limited baseline and sensitivity analyses, and minor clarity/notation issues.  
- Post-rebuttal ratings: Quality 6, Clarity 6, Originality 8, Significance 8; overall recommendation: accept after addressing clarifications and corrections.
