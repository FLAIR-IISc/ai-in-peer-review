- Problem: Identifies propensity overfitting in counterfactual risk estimation for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), causing high variance and poor generalization.  
- Proposal: Introduces a self-normalized estimator and the Norm-POEM algorithm using a multiplicative control variate to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Theory & Empirics: Provides theoretical justification and empirical results showing improved robustness and variance reduction; Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and approaches supervised performance.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on CRM and off-policy evaluation literature (e.g., Swaminathan & Joachims), citing propensity score matching and importance sampling foundations.  
- Strengths: Clear motivation, important identification of propensity overfitting, well-justified estimator, strong experiments across datasets, ablation and runtime studies, and public code for reproducibility.  
- Technical issues: Contains inaccuracies in the mathematical discussion of equation (2), misstates the estimator’s loss-translation invariance, and Example 1 is based on this flawed premise.  
- Novelty claim: Overstates novelty of the variance issue — sensitivity to small propensity weights is a known importance-sampling problem.  
- Impact: Despite technical commentary errors, the self-normalized estimator and Norm-POEM are valid, pragmatic, and impactful contributions.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance conditional on correcting the technical inaccuracies and tempering novelty claims about the estimator’s “anomalies.”  
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses a significant problem, theoretically sound fix, strong empirical gains, clear presentation, and reproducible code.  
- Cons against acceptance: Technical inaccuracies in estimator discussion and overstated novelty regarding known variance issues.
