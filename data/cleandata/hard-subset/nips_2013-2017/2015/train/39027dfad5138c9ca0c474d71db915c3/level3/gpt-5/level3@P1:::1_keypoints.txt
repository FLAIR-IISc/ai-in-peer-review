- Paper studies batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), identifies "propensity overfitting" where ERM exploits propensity randomness, and proposes self-normalized IS (SN-IS) integrated into Norm-POEM to mitigate it.  
- Main theoretical claim: IPS-based ERM can be pathological (non-equivariant, unbounded finite-sample range, ERM can optimize Ŝ(h)); SN-IS restores boundedness, equivariance, and strong consistency.  
- Theoretical gap: no dedicated finite-sample CRM-style generalization bound for the SN-IPS (ratio) estimator or clear translation of variance control into the learning guarantee.  
- Empirics: Norm-POEM consistently improves generalization over POEM under fair experimental protocol and shows robustness to loss translations and replay count.  
- Empirical shortcomings: missing DR-CRM and tuned clipped-IPS baselines, ablations isolating normalization vs clipping vs variance regularization, and no wall-clock runtimes or iteration counts.  
- Technical correctness: derivations appear standard for SN-IS and variance approximations; full-support logging-policy assumption is stated and necessary.  
- Clarity: paper is well written but should better distinguish propensity overfitting from mere high variance and explicitly spell out the toy example computation around line 199.  
- Originality: SN-IS and control variates are classical; novelty lies in framing propensity overfitting, showing SN-IS fixes equivariance/range anomalies, and integrating it into a structured-prediction CRM learner.  
- Significance: Addressing estimator instability in BLBF is important; the simple estimator change is practical and likely to influence practice.  
- Request: qualify claims that "virtually all" prior works used unbiased estimators, since many use clipping or DR.  
- Request: provide a high-probability generalization bound or discussion for variance-regularized SN objectives controlling excess risk.  
- Request: recommend Ŝ(h)≈1 as a general diagnostic and show Ŝ(h) vs risk plots across models.  
- Request: add DR-CRM and clipped-IPS-CRM baselines, report sensitivity to clipping M and regularization λ, and include confidence intervals.  
- Request: report computational metrics (wall-clock time, iteration counts, norms of w) to substantiate optimization claims.  
- Request: include code reproduction instructions and random seeds for experiments.  
- Pros: clear identification of propensity overfitting, simple principled SN-IS fix, consistent empirical improvements, useful diagnostic Ŝ(h), and code release.  
- Cons: lacks finite-sample SN bound, limited baselines and sensitivity analyses, and a few overgeneralized prior-work claims and under-explained derivations.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
