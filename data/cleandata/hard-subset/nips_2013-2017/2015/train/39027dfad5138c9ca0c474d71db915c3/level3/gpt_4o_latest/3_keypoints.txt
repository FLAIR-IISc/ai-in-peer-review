- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies propensity overfitting in conventional CRM estimators.  
- Proposes a novel self-normalized risk estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Integrates the estimator into the Norm-POEM algorithm with theoretical grounding (consistency proof).  
- Clearly written paper with detailed theoretical analysis and transparent experimental methodology.  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM outperforms baseline POEM across multiple datasets and stabilizes propensity weight sums.  
- Potential broad impact on causal inference and reinforcement learning research.  
- Experiments are mostly on multi-label classification datasets and lack real-world domain evaluations (e.g., ad ranking, recommender systems).  
- Limited comparison/analysis with other variance reduction techniques and their computational trade-offs.  
- Insufficient detail on why Norm-POEM converges faster and lack of comprehensive runtime/optimization analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, and empirically effective solution to an important BLBF problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need for broader real-world experiments and deeper analysis of variance and optimization trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with encouragement to expand experiments and provide more detailed variance/optimization analyses in future work.
