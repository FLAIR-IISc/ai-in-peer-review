- Proposes Norm-POEM, a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in batch logged bandit feedback to address "propensity overfitting."
- Builds on POEM (ICML 2015) and extends it with a new estimator that uses double denominators (Equation 7).
- Empirical validation is strong: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple multi-label datasets and approaches CRF performance.
- Theoretical justification for the self-normalized estimator is largely sound and clearly shows improved boundedness and equivariance properties.
- Significant technical errors: commentary around Equation (2) is misleading—the loss there is not the expected quantity and the expectation interpretation is incorrect.
- The claim that Equation (2) has an "anomaly" (non-invariance to translation) is technically incorrect, invalidating Example 1 based on that claim.
- The sensitivity of the estimator in Equation (2) to its denominator π (which can be zero for unseen samples) is inadequately addressed.
- Equation (7)'s double denominator mitigates the π sensitivity, making the modified estimator preferable.
- Clarity is generally good, but the technical mistakes create confusion and require correction.
- The work is original and significant for real-world BLBF applications; recommend acceptance with major revisions to fix theoretical inaccuracies.
