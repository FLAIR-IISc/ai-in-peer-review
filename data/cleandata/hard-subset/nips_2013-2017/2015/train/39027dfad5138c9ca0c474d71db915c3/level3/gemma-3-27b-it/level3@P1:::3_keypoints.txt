- Identifies a critical issue—propensity overfitting—in counterfactual risk estimators for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Shows propensity overfitting leads to degenerate risk estimates, especially with complex hypothesis spaces.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator using multiplicative control variates that provably avoids propensity overfitting.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm derived from the self-normalized estimator.
- Empirical evaluation on multi-label classification shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms prior methods using the conventional estimator.
- Extends prior CRM work and situates BLBF within off-policy evaluation and causal inference contexts.
- Highlights novelty: propensity overfitting as a BLBF-specific problem tied to hypothesis space capacity.
- Applies established importance sampling ideas in a novel way to mitigate the BLBF problem.
- Technical analysis appears sound and provides strong theoretical justification for the estimator.
- Presentation is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and notationally consistent.
- Code release is a significant positive for reproducibility.
- Experiments are limited to benchmark datasets and lack broader diversity.
- No demonstration on real-world applications (e.g., ads, recommender systems), limiting practical claims.
- Optimization discussion is brief; more details on convergence and hyperparameter sensitivity are needed.
- Variance/robustness across runs is not reported, reducing insight into algorithm stability.
- Lacks deeper analysis explaining why Norm-POEM converges faster than POEM and lacks detailed computational-efficiency benchmarks.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution that merits acceptance pending minor revisions (variance reporting and clearer optimization/efficiency discussion).
- Recommendation: Accept.
