This paper proposes an alternative estimator for counterfactual learning in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, which they term "propensity overfitting." This problem occurs when the estimator is sensitive to the propensity weights, leading to severely biased risk estimates. To address this issue, the authors propose a self-normalized estimator that uses a multiplicative control variate to reduce the variance of the estimator.

The self-normalized estimator is shown to be more robust and accurate than the conventional estimator, and it is used to derive a new learning algorithm called Norm-POEM. The authors evaluate the performance of Norm-POEM on several multi-label classification problems and find that it consistently outperforms the conventional estimator.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the problem with the conventional estimator and the benefits of the self-normalized estimator. The experiments are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of Norm-POEM. However, there are some issues with the notation and commentary around equation (2), which may be misleading.
