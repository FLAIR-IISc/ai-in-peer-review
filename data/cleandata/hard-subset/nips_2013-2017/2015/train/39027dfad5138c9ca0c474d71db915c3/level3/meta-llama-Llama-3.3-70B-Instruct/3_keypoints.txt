- Proposes a novel hybrid 2D-3D ConvNet architecture for neuronal boundary detection in serial section EM images.  
- Introduces a recursive training framework where a second ConvNet is trained on the output of a first to refine predictions.  
- Demonstrates significant improvement in boundary detection accuracy compared to existing 2D ConvNet methods.  
- Provides detailed analysis of feature maps to illustrate the network’s effectiveness.  
- Leverages both 2D and 3D contextual information, which is an innovative design choice.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and includes a thorough review of prior work.  
- Lacks experiments and analysis on optimization of the training objective (fixed learning rate and momentum).  
- Does not explore the effects of different hyperparameters on network performance.  
- Could include more discussion and experiments on applicability to other image types, such as video.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality contribution with significant implications for neuronal boundary detection and neuroscience.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, improved accuracy, clarity, and potential scientific impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient hyperparameter/optimization analysis and limited discussion of broader applicability.
