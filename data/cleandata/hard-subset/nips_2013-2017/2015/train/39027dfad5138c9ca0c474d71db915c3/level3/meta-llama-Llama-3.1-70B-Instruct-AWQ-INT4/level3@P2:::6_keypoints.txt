- Proposes a self-normalized (multiplicative control variate) estimator for counterfactual risk minimization in batch learning from logged bandit feedback.  
- Identifies and characterizes "propensity overfitting", where importance weights cause hypotheses to overfit logged data.  
- Shows the self-normalized estimator preserves consistency and controls variance compared to the conventional unbiased estimator.  
- Empirical results demonstrate the normalized CRM outperforms the non-normalized variant.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm based on the proposed estimator.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains the problem, and provides consistent notation.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and the proofs/results are sound.  
- Experimental setup is extensive and results are presented clearly.  
- Lacks a detailed breakdown of the computational complexity of the proposed approach.  
- Could benefit from a deeper discussion of practical implications and application scenarios.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution to BLBF with strong theory and empirical support (rating 8/10, confidence 8/10).
