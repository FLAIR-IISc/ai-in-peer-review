This paper introduces a novel approach to batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), a problem that arises in many online systems where the goal is to learn from historical logs without having access to full-information feedback. The authors identify a severe issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in prior works on BLBF, which they term "propensity overfitting." This problem occurs when the estimator overfits the propensity weights, leading to severely biased risk estimates.

The authors propose a new estimator, called the self-normalized estimator, which avoids this problem by using a multiplicative control variate. They show that this estimator is equivariant and has a lower variance than the conventional estimator. They also derive a new learning algorithm, called Norm-POEM, which uses the self-normalized estimator and the counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) principle.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and its consequences, as well as a detailed derivation of the self-normalized estimator and the Norm-POEM algorithm. The authors also provide an extensive empirical evaluation of the proposed approach, demonstrating its effectiveness in several multi-label classification problems.

The reviewer found the paper interesting and worth reading, and appreciates the authors' efforts in providing a clear and detailed explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. However, the reviewer notes that they did not have enough time to fully verify the technical details and novelty of the paper.
