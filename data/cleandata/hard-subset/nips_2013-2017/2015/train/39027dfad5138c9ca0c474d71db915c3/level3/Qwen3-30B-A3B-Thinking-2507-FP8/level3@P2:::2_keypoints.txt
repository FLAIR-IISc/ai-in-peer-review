- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback and proposes a self-normalized estimator (Norm-POEM).
- Builds on Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) and extends POEM for structured output prediction.
- Main technical contribution: identify a problem with the conventional estimator (Eq.2) and propose a self-normalized alternative (Eq.7).
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple multi-label datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL).
- Shows empirical evidence Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting (Ŝ(h) ≈ 1) where POEM fails (Table 2).
- Performance approaches full-information CRF (“skyline”) on evaluated datasets, indicating strong practical gains.
- Theoretical support: Theorem 2 provides strong consistency for the self-normalized estimator.
- Practical significance: relevant to real-world applications like ad placement and recommendation systems.
- Quality of experiments: thorough, well-designed, and convincing across datasets.
- Major weakness: technical error in analysis of the conventional estimator—misinterprets finite-sample estimator properties as expectation properties.
- Example 1 and the claim of non-invariance to loss translation are invalid because the authors conflate true risk equivariance with finite-sample behavior.
- Paper needs clearer exposition distinguishing true risk (equivariant) from finite-sample estimator (not equivariant) and correction of commentary around Eq. (2) and Example 1.
- Originality: novel application of self-normalization in counterfactual learning, differentiating it from prior work.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to correct the theoretical misinterpretation and clarify the exposition.
