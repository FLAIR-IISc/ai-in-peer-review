- Introduces a self-normalized estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) to combat propensity overfitting.
- Identifies a severe flaw in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator that can cause degeneracies and overfitting.
- Provides a clear, well-structured introduction and concise related-work overview.
- Proposes Norm-POEM and presents a thorough theoretical analysis of the estimator’s properties.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and well-designed across multiple datasets.
- Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and shows better generalization performance.
- Paper is well-written; mathematical notation is clear and references are relevant.
- Technical quality is high: claims are supported by theoretical and empirical evidence, with honest evaluation of strengths/weaknesses.
- Original contribution: the self-normalized estimator addresses a previously unexplored issue in BLBF.
- Significance: findings are important and likely to impact counterfactual learning and BLBF practices.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid theory, strong experiments, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: relies on the assumption that the logging policy is stationary with full support over Y, which may not hold in practice.
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution recommended for acceptance, with suggested future work to address noted limitations.
