- Identifies "propensity overfitting" as a new, critical failure mode of the conventional counterfactual risk estimator in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (Eq. 7) that avoids propensity overfitting and resolves anomalies of the conventional estimator.  
- Builds on Swaminathan & Joachims' CRM framework and extends it with deeper theoretical analysis.  
- Demonstrates the conventional estimator can produce severely biased risk estimates even for simple hypothesis spaces.  
- Shows the self-normalized estimator is consistent and equivariant, with formal proof of consistency (Theorem 2).  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a practical algorithm implementing the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough (Supervised→Bandit methodology) and Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple multi-label datasets.  
- Provides convincing evidence that Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting (Table 2).  
- Strength: novel identification of propensity overfitting addressing a key gap in counterfactual learning.  
- Strength: theoretically sound estimator that resolves multiple anomalies of the conventional approach.  
- Strength: compelling, reproducible empirical results and clear implementation/computational details.  
- Weakness: clarify delta range—theory uses δ ∈ [-1,0] while experiments use translated losses (δ>0); explain equivariance and translation in text.  
- Weakness: treat loss δ(x,y) as potentially noisy and define true risk as E[δ(x,y)] over data and loss noise.  
- Corrections: fix typo in equation (denominator should be h_0(y|x)); change Eq. 276 optimization to argmin with variance regularizer; maintain notation consistency (use hats not bars, e.g., R̂SN).  
- Overall: high-quality, well-written, significant and original contribution likely to be influential; reviewer recommends acceptance (scores: Quality 6, Clarity 6, Originality 8, Significance 8).
