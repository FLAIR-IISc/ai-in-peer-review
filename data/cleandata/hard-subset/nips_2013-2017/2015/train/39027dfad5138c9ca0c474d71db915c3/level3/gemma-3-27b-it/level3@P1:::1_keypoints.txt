- Identifies "propensity overfitting" as a critical issue in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- BLBF is important for leveraging logged data when interactive control is unavailable (e.g., ads, recommendations).
- Standard importance-sampling counterfactual risk estimators are unbiased but can have high variance and be prone to overfitting propensities.
- The paper argues propensity overfitting is distinct from mere large-variance problems and harms generalization.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator using multiplicative control variates to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- The estimator is bounded and equivariant, reducing susceptibility to propensity-driven overfitting.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, an algorithm built on the estimator, which empirically outperforms prior methods.
- Work is well-positioned within causal inference, off-policy evaluation, and BLBF literature, building on Swaminathan & Joachims and IS/variance reduction techniques.
- Reviewer suggests more detailed comparison to clipping techniques and scrutiny of the claim that prior work used only unbiased estimators.
- Strength: Novel identification of propensity overfitting with a theoretically motivated fix.
- Strength: Strong empirical results across multiple datasets and an informative ablation study; code is available.
- Weakness: The link between large importance weight variance and propensity overfitting needs clearer, more intuitive explanation.
- Weakness: Example 1 and related exposition are hard to follow; a clearer illustration or visualization is recommended.
- Weakness: Assumes a fully supportive historical policy; implications and limitations of this assumption need more discussion.
- Reviewer could not fully verify all technical details due to time constraints, raising minor concerns about technical verification.
- Overall recommendation: Accept, subject to addressing clarity issues and clarifying the variance–overfitting relationship.
