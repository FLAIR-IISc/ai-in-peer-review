- Problem identified: conventional counterfactual risk estimator suffers from "propensity overfitting," producing severely biased risk estimates due to sensitivity to propensity weights.
- Proposal: a self-normalized estimator using a multiplicative control variate to reduce variance and improve robustness.
- Algorithm: the self-normalized estimator is used to derive a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM.
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional estimator on several multi-label classification problems.
- Writing and clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow; experiments are thorough.
- Notation concern: commentary around equation (2) is misleading— the loss in (2) is not the expected quantity and the expectation interpretation is incorrect.
- Technical claim issue: the paper's claim that (2) exhibits an "anomaly" (non-invariance to translation of the loss) is technically incorrect.
- Significance: the self-normalized estimator is a significant contribution with potential to improve accuracy and robustness in counterfactual learning.
- Accessibility concern: paper assumes high familiarity with counterfactual learning and may be hard for non-experts to follow.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel solution to propensity overfitting, strong experimental support, clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: notation/interpretation issues around eq (2) and limited accessibility for non-experts.
- Recommendations: revise and correct notation/commentary around eq (2), add more background on counterfactual learning, and consider additional experiments in varied scenarios.
