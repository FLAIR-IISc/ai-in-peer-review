- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator as an alternative to the conventional counterfactual risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Identifies a serious issue called "propensity overfitting," where hypothesis-space capacity allows overfitting of propensity weights and biases risk estimates.
- Demonstrates experimentally that the self-normalized estimator mitigates propensity overfitting and outperforms the conventional estimator.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.
- Introduction and related work provide clear motivation and a comprehensive literature overview.
- Technical contributions are significant, with a thorough analysis of propensity overfitting.
- Experimental results are convincing and support the proposed estimator's superiority.
- Lacks a clear, detailed theoretical explanation of why the self-normalized estimator avoids propensity overfitting.
- Does not provide detailed comparisons with other existing BLBF methods to contextualize strengths and weaknesses.
- Recommends specific clarifications/corrections: clarify meaning of "delta > 0" in Table 1 for NORM-POEM and explain rescaled delta in [-1,0].
- Suggests modeling delta(x,y) as a random variable and adding an expectation over delta in the true risk formulation.
- Requests mathematical corrections: use h(y|x)/h_0(y|x) (not product) at lines ~170–171, use argmin (not argmax) at line ~276, and replace bars with hats in notation at line ~319.
