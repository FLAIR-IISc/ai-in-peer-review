- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and its impact on counterfactual risk estimation.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator as a remedy, claiming theoretical advantages (equivariance, bounded risk).
- Empirically validates the method on multi-label classification datasets with strong results.
- Well-motivated paper with solid connections to causal inference, domain adaptation, and importance sampling literature.
- Introduces "Propensity Overfitting" as a distinct phenomenon; Example 1 is intended to illustrate the issue.
- Derivation of the self-normalized estimator and discussion of equivariance are clear and valuable.
- Commentary around Equation (2) is misleading: the loss in (2) is the expected quantity and translation non-invariance claim is incorrect.
- The incorrect claim about Equation (2) undermines Example 1 and weakens the paper’s initial theoretical justification.
- Sensitivity to the denominator π is a general importance-sampling issue, not unique to the proposed estimator.
- The modified estimator in (7) is sensible and empirically effective, despite flaws in the initial theoretical argument.
- Experimental results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and approaches full-information CRF performance.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, proposes a plausible solution, strong empirical gains, clear writing.
- Argument against acceptance: core theoretical claim regarding Equation (2) is fundamentally incorrect and needs correction.
- Overall: valuable contribution contingent on revising the incorrect theoretical commentary around Equation (2) and clarifying the theoretical foundation.
