This paper addresses the problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies a severe issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in prior works on BLBF, which the authors term "propensity overfitting." The paper proposes a novel solution, a self-normalized risk estimator that uses multiplicative control variates to avoid this problem. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through an extensive empirical evaluation.

The paper is well-motivated and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' argument. The presentation is well-organized, and the authors provide enough information for the reader to reproduce their results. The paper is a follow-up work on prior research [1], and the main novelty is the self-normalized risk estimator, which improves empirical performance.

The paper's contribution is significant, as it addresses a relevant problem in the field of BLBF. However, the significance of the paper is uncertain, as it is unclear how the experimental gains will translate to real-world applications. The authors' use of simulated bandit feedback datasets from supervised multi-label classification problems may not accurately reflect the complexities of real-world applications.

The paper's technical soundness is good, with a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and a well-motivated solution. The authors provide a theoretical justification for their approach and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments. However, the reviewer expected more experiments and a deeper analysis of the training-objective optimization.

The paper's originality is good, as it proposes a novel solution to the propensity overfitting problem. The authors draw on diagnostic tools used in importance sampling to detect and avoid propensity overfitting, which is a new and interesting approach. However, the paper's relation to prior work is not entirely clear, and the authors could have provided more context and comparisons to existing methods.

The paper's clarity is excellent, with clear and concise writing that makes it easy to follow the authors' argument. The presentation is well-organized, and the authors provide enough information for the reader to reproduce their results.
