This paper proposes a novel approach to batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) by introducing a self-normalized risk estimator that addresses the problem of propensity overfitting. The authors identify a severe issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in prior works on BLBF, which can lead to degeneracies and overfitting.

The paper is well-motivated and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the proposed solution. The introduction provides a thorough background on BLBF and the limitations of existing approaches, setting the stage for the proposed method.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant, as the authors provide a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and propose a novel solution using a self-normalized risk estimator. The theoretical results are well-supported by empirical evaluations on several multi-label classification problems, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method, including the self-normalized risk estimator and the learning algorithm, Norm-POEM. The experiments are well-designed, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to address a specific problem in BLBF and provide a novel solution that improves upon existing approaches. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the limitations of existing methods and demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed approach.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The authors could have provided more experiments and deeper analysis of the training-objective optimization. Additionally, the significance of the results is uncertain, as it is unclear how the experimental gains translate to real-world applications.

Overall, I would rate this paper as follows:

* Quality: 8/10 (well-supported by theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations)
* Clarity: 9/10 (clearly written and easy to follow)
* Originality: 8/10 (novel solution to a specific problem in BLBF)
* Significance: 7/10 (unclear how experimental gains translate to real-world applications)
