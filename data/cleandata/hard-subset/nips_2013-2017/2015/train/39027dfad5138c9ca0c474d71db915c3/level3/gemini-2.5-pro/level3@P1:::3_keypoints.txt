- Identifies "Propensity Overfitting" in BLBF where IPS-based CRM exploits propensity weights instead of true loss.  
- Proposes a self-normalized IPS estimator (ratio of IPS risk to IPS sum of weights) derived via a multiplicative control variate.  
- Shows the self-normalized estimator is translation-equivariant, bounded by observed losses, biased in finite samples but strongly consistent.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM: CRM objective using the self-normalized estimator plus a variance estimate.  
- Empirical results (Supervised-to-Bandit on multi-label datasets) show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM.  
- Paper is very well-written, clearly motivated, and uses an intuitive illustrative example for the problem.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and grounded in control variate / Monte Carlo principles.  
- Ablations demonstrate robustness to loss translation and continued benefit from variance regularization.  
- Main contribution is incremental: replaces IPS with self-normalized estimator within existing CRM/POEM framework.  
- Experimental scope is narrow, relying on the same datasets and simulation protocol as prior work.  
- Lacks evaluation on real-world logged bandit feedback to establish practical impact.  
- Notes faster practical convergence of Norm-POEM but provides limited analysis of optimization behavior.  
- Requests: report variance/error bars for results, add real-world BLBF experiments, and include convergence plots for POEM vs Norm-POEM.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed work addressing a genuine issue with a recommend-accept verdict despite incremental novelty and limited empirical scope.
