- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), a practically important problem.  
- Identifies sensitivity of the standard counterfactual risk estimator to propensity scores, which can produce degenerate risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self-normalized counterfactual estimator as a remedy to propensity-induced instability.  
- Instantiates the estimator in a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM.  
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and in some cases approaches full-information CRF performance on multi-label tasks.  
- The connection to importance sampling and control variates provides solid theoretical grounding for the approach.  
- The paper is generally well-written and organized.  
- Main technical concern: the paper’s claim that the conventional estimator exhibits an “anomaly” (loss-translation non-invariance) is based on a misunderstanding.  
- Example 1 and related discussion around Equation (2) are flawed because they conflate sample estimates with true expected risk.  
- While the translation-anomaly claim is incorrect, the estimator’s sensitivity to small or zero propensities is real and problematic.  
- The self-normalized estimator’s double normalization intrinsically mitigates the influence of small propensity weights, improving robustness.  
- The core contribution is an effective application of a known technique (self-normalization) to BLBF, so novelty may be overstated.  
- Acceptance arguments: substantial and well-demonstrated empirical improvement, relevance to a growing field, and a well-motivated method.  
- Rejection/concern arguments: flawed theoretical reasoning could mislead readers and reduces credibility; paper should clarify and correct claims about Equation (2).  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to correct the theoretical justification and clarify the commentary on the conventional estimator.
