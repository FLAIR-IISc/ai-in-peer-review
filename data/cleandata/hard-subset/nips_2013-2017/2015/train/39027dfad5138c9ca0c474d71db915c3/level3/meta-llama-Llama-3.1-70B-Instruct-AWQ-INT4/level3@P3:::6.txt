This paper proposes an alternative estimator for counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, which can lead to overfitting due to the exploitation of example generation probabilities. They introduce a self-normalized estimator that avoids this problem and demonstrate its effectiveness through theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the conventional estimator's limitations and demonstrate how their proposed estimator addresses these issues. The theoretical results are well-supported, and the empirical evaluation is extensive and convincing.

The main contribution of this paper is the introduction of the self-normalized estimator, which is a novel solution to the problem of propensity overfitting in CRM. The authors demonstrate that this estimator is more robust and effective than the conventional estimator, especially in scenarios where the logging policy is biased.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the related work and the context in which their contribution is situated. The writing is clear, and the notation is well-defined.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their proposed estimator. While they mention that the estimator has a higher per-iteration computation cost, they do not provide a thorough analysis of the trade-offs between computation cost and accuracy.

Another potential weakness is that the authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other existing methods that address the problem of propensity overfitting. While they mention some related work, they do not provide a thorough analysis of how their proposed estimator compares to these methods.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of CRM and BLBF. The authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution, and the empirical evaluation is extensive and convincing. The paper is well-written, and the notation is well-defined.
