- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and builds on Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM).
- Identifies a critical failure mode of the IPS estimator called "propensity overfitting" where high-capacity models exploit propensity weights.
- Shows with a clear example that models can achieve low estimated risk by manipulating propensity weights, harming generalization.
- Proposes replacing the unbiased IPS estimator with a self-normalized importance sampling estimator derived via multiplicative control variates.
- New estimator is equivariant to loss translations and bounded by the observed loss range, preventing IPS pathologies.
- Integrates the estimator into CRM to form Norm-POEM, an extension of the prior POEM algorithm.
- Provides theoretical support (e.g., consistency) and principled derivation from control variates and ratio-estimator literature.
- Empirical evaluation on multi-label datasets shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM in generalization, robustness, and often training speed.
- Uses the standard Supervised→Bandit conversion for controlled, repeatable experimental comparisons.
- Paper is high technical quality with rigorous arguments and comprehensive experiments.
- Writing and organization are very clear; Section 4 effectively illustrates propensity overfitting.
- Contribution is original: reframing IPS pathologies as "propensity overfitting" and applying self-normalized estimators to learning-time overfitting is novel.
- Results are significant and likely impactful for practice (e.g., advertising, recommendations) and future research.
- Minor downside: experiments use benchmark conversions rather than real-world industrial logs, but this is a standard and acceptable methodology.
