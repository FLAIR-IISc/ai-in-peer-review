- Identifies a previously overlooked problem in BLBF called "Propensity Overfitting," where importance-weighted estimators can be exploited by high-capacity models to drive estimated risk artificially low.  
- Explains the mechanism: for negative losses models inflate weights on seen actions; for positive losses they predict unseen actions to drive estimates toward zero.  
- Proposes replacing the unbiased counterfactual risk estimator with a self-normalized importance sampling estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Highlights desirable properties of the self-normalized estimator: equivariant to constant loss shifts and bounded within observed loss range.  
- Notes the self-normalized estimator is biased in finite samples but is strongly consistent.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, which integrates the self-normalized risk and a corresponding variance estimator into the CRM framework.  
- Provides theoretical motivation connecting estimator properties to the practical propensity overfitting problem.  
- Empirical results across multi-label tasks show Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM.  
- Diagnostic experiments confirm Norm-POEM controls propensity overfitting (Ŝ(ĥ) ≈ 1) and is robust to loss translations, unlike POEM.  
- Finds Norm-POEM is often computationally faster than POEM despite a more complex objective.  
- Strength: clear, significant contribution with elegant, well-motivated solution and strong empirical support.  
- Strength: paper is exceptionally clear with an illustrative toy example that builds strong intuition.  
- Weakness: evaluation uses supervised-to-bandit conversion rather than real-world logged bandit datasets with unknown logging policies.  
- Weakness: variance estimator relies on a delta-method approximation and the optimization is non-convex; discussion of sensitivity to initialization/local minima is limited.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, practically important advance; strongly recommended for acceptance.
