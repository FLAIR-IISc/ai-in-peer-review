- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback and identifies a flaw in the unbiased counterfactual risk estimator.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (multiplicative control variate) to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical results on multi-label classification consistently outperform POEM and are convincing.  
- Builds on and is a compelling follow-up to Swaminathan & Joachims (ICML 2013) with insightful analysis.  
- The connection to importance sampling and control variates provides strong theoretical grounding.  
- Experimental setup mirrors POEM for fair comparison and includes useful runtime comparisons.  
- Unclear whether Norm-POEM uses a rescaled delta in [-1,0] like CRM; clarification needed for interpreting "delta > 0" in Table 1.  
- Suggests modeling delta(x,y) as a random variable (with expectation in true risk) to capture feedback noise and potentially tighten bounds.  
- Critical correction: lines 170-171 should use the likelihood ratio h(y|x)/h0(y|x), not h(y|x)-h0(y|x).  
- Note that non-linearity of the likelihood ratio was previously observed in Beygelzimer & Langford [2] and should be acknowledged.  
- Typographical error: line 276 should be "argmin" (risk minimization), not "argmax".  
- Notation fix: line 319 should replace bars with hats to match earlier notation.  
- Strengths summarized: technically sound, clear presentation, original identification of propensity overfitting, and significant empirical improvement.  
- Weaknesses summarized: delta rescaling ambiguity, deterministic treatment of delta, minor typographical/notation errors, and a missing citation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important BLBF problem, strong theory and experiments, clear writing, and substantial improvement over prior methods.  
- Arguments against acceptance: requires minor clarifications/corrections and could benefit from exploring delta as a random variable.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 6, Clarity 6, Originality 8, Significance 8; overall recommendation to accept pending minor fixes.
