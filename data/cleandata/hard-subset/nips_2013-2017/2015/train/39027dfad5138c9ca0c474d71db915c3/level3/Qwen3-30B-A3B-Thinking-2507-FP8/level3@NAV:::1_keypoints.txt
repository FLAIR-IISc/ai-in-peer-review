- Identifies "propensity overfitting" in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) as distinct from standard importance weight variance and proposes replacing the unbiased counterfactual estimator with a self-normalized estimator.  
- Introduces the Norm-POEM algorithm for structured output prediction based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Builds on the CRM principle and extends prior importance-sampling-based BLBF work.  
- Strength: clearly identifies a previously unrecognized failure mode (propensity overfitting).  
- Strength: elegant solution using self-normalized estimators that resolves anomalies of conventional approaches.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation across multiple datasets with provided code and consistent gains over baselines.  
- Strength: theoretical motivation for the self-normalized estimator (equivariance property and bounded risk estimates) is convincing.  
- Concern: the relationship between large importance weight variance and propensity overfitting needs clearer, more explicit explanation.  
- Concern: unclear analysis at line 199—behavior of \hat R(h^) when n is small relative to k requires clarification.  
- Concern: the claim that prior BLBF works used "unbiased" estimators is problematic since many likely used clipping (introducing bias) as noted in prior references.  
- Reviewer did not verify all technical details but finds the core idea sensible and empirical results convincing; recommends acceptance while requesting minor revisions.
