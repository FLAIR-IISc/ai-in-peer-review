- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and limitations of counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) with IPS estimators.
- Identifies and formalizes "propensity overfitting": high-capacity hypotheses can manipulate importance weights to drive estimated risk arbitrarily low.
- Illustrates the failure mode with an example where negative losses and concentrated weights yield degenerate solutions.
- Proposes a self-normalized IPS estimator using the sum of importance weights as a multiplicative control variate (ratio estimator).
- Shows the self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss translations, bounded within the loss range, and consistent—properties that mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Instantiates the estimator in CRM to create Norm-POEM for structured output prediction with linear models.
- Empirical results on multi-label tasks show Norm-POEM consistently and substantially outperforms POEM (standard IPS-based method).
- Code is released, aiding reproducibility and adoption.
- Strength: tackles a significant, practical problem relevant to industry applications.
- Strength: novel conceptual contribution distinguishing propensity overfitting from high variance; solution is elegant and theoretically grounded.
- Strength: clear exposition and strong experimental validation following the "Supervised to Bandit" protocol.
- Weakness: relationship between propensity overfitting and high variance needs clearer discussion—does variance regularization in CRM partially address it?
- Weakness: Example 1’s argument for n small relative to k could use a clearer, more explicit walk-through.
- Weakness: positioning relative to prior work should acknowledge common biased variance-reduction practices (e.g., clipping) and clarify how the contribution differs.
- Overall evaluation: high quality, clarity, originality, and significance; the paper is recommended for acceptance.
