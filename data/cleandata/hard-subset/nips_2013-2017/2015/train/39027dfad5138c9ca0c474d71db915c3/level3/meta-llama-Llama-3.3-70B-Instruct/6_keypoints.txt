- Proposes VD2D3D, a recursively trained 2D-3D ConvNet for neuronal boundary detection in serial-section EM images.  
- Key innovations: deeper network architecture, incorporation of 3D filters, and a recursive training framework.  
- Recursive framework uses preliminary network output as input to a second network, improving boundary detection accuracy.  
- VD2D3D significantly reduces pixel-wise classification error and improves Rand F-scores versus N4 and VD2D.  
- Qualitative results show better boundary detection in ambiguous regions where 2D models fail.  
- Introduces ZNN, a multicore-CPU implementation of 3D ConvNets, as a viable alternative to GPU frameworks.  
- ZNN enables training of large networks and additional computations like multi-level feature integration.  
- Paper is well-written with clear descriptions of architecture, training, and evaluation.  
- Strengths: novel approach, significant accuracy improvements, clear exposition, and promising ZNN tool.  
- Weaknesses: evaluation limited to a single dataset and lacks comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Additional weakness: some sections could be clearer or more readable.  
- Recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses.
