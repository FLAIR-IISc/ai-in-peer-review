- Identifies a new problem "propensity overfitting" in batch learning from logged bandit feedback where conventional unbiased estimators can under-report risk.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator that avoids propensity overfitting and derives a new algorithm, Norm-POEM.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the self-normalized estimator resolves anomalies of the conventional estimator (lack of equivariance, unbounded estimates).  
- Empirical results on multi-label datasets show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms conventional POEM and stabilizes the self-normalizer around its expected value.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and connects theory to experiments effectively.  
- Authors provide code for reproducibility.  
- Theoretical arguments are rigorous and proofs are sound.  
- Variance regularization remains beneficial even with the self-normalized estimator.  
- Originality: reframes self-normalization in the BLBF context and distinguishes propensity overfitting from importance-weight variance issues.  
- Significance: practical implications for real-world systems (ads, recommendations) with consistent empirical improvements.  
- Concern: claim that prior BLBF work used unbiased estimators is questionable because many use clipped importance weights—needs clarification.  
- Concern: the relationship between large importance weight variance and propensity overfitting needs clearer explanation.  
- Concern: the example in Section 4 and line 199 (behavior when n is small relative to k) should be explained more thoroughly.  
- Recommendation: accept, pending minor clarifications on prior-work claims and clearer exposition of examples and variance-overfitting connection.
