Summary: The paper diagnoses “propensity overfitting” in counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) with inverse propensity scoring (IPS) and proposes a self-normalized (SN) risk estimator as a multiplicative control variate. Building on [1] (POEM), the authors derive Norm-POEM by plugging the SN estimator and its variance approximation into the CRM objective, proving consistency and highlighting desirable properties (equivariance, boundedness). Experiments on multi-label tasks show consistent gains over POEM and clear diagnostics (Ŝ(ĥ)≈1), with competitive runtime.

Quality: The theoretical motivation for SN-IPS is sound and well-linked to classical Monte Carlo variance reduction. However, some clarifications/corrections would strengthen rigor: (i) Please clarify whether Norm-POEM, in Eqs. (3) and (8), assumes the rescaled loss δ∈[−1,0] as in the CRM bound; if so, state explicitly how δ is rescaled in training and evaluation. Relatedly, Table 1 and Sec. 7 refer to “δ>0” conditions—please explain precisely what δ>0 means in the experiments (e.g., an additive translation of Hamming loss, and by how much), and whether normalization uses raw or shifted δ. (ii) δ(x,y) should be modeled as a random variable; the true risk should read R(h)=Ex Ey|h E[δ|x,y], with explicit expectation over label noise. (iii) The variance formula (8) is an asymptotic approximation; please specify clipping M used in all runs and report sensitivity. (iv) A small empirical comparison to doubly-robust/weighted control-variate baselines would contextualize gains.

Clarity: Generally clear, but please fix the following:
- Line 170–171: ratio should be h(y|x)/h0(y|x). Also, note that nonlinearity of the CRM objective was previously discussed in [1].
- Line 276: use argmin (risk is minimized), not argmax.
- Line 319: replace bars with hats to match earlier notation.

Originality: High. Framing propensity overfitting and using multiplicative control variates for learning (not just evaluation) is a neat, impactful twist on IPS-based CRM.

Significance: Solid. The analysis is insightful, the estimator is practical, and the empirical gains over POEM on a widely studied problem are convincing.

Pros:
- Interesting follow-up to [1] with clear conceptual advance.
- Insightful analysis of propensity overfitting; effective diagnostic via Ŝ(h).
- Multiplicative control variate is principled and yields bounded, equivariant estimates.
- Consistent empirical improvements and good runtime.

Cons:
- Need clearer treatment of loss scaling/translation and δ stochasticity.
- Variance approximation and clipping choices underreported.
- Missing comparison to DR/control-variate baselines.

Post-rebuttal assessment: Quality 6, Clarity 6, Originality 8, Significance 8. Overall positive.
