- Identifies a new problem in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) called "propensity overfitting," where conventional counterfactual estimators can give severely biased risk estimates for some hypotheses.
- Shows via a compelling example that the standard unbiased importance sampling estimator can produce arbitrarily low (misleading) risk estimates for overfitted hypotheses when exploration is insufficient.
- Proposes replacing the standard estimator with a self-normalized estimator (Equation 7) and derives a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM.
- Proves theoretical properties of the self-normalized estimator: equivariance under loss translation, boundedness within the loss range, and avoidance of degeneracies of the conventional estimator.
- Empirical evaluation across multiple datasets shows consistent improvements of Norm-POEM over POEM and evidence that Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting (Table 2).
- Provides code to facilitate reproducibility and adoption.
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly explains practical relevance to applications like ad placement and recommendation systems.
- Unclear relationship between large importance weight variance and propensity overfitting; the connection to standard IS variance issues needs clarification.
- The analysis in Section 4 (claim about empirical risk concentrating around -2) seems questionable for small n relative to k and needs clearer justification.
- The paper's claim that prior BLBF works used "unbiased" estimators is imprecise, since some prior methods used clipped (biased) importance weights; this requires clarification.
- The theoretical analysis of the self-normalized estimator (Theorem 2) is correct but terse; stronger connection to practical benefits would help.
- Overall quality: sound theoretical argument and convincing empirical support, though some technical details were not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Clarity: generally well-written and organized with an effective illustrative example, but could better explain ties to IS variance issues.
- Originality: novel contribution by identifying propensity overfitting in BLBF and applying the self-normalized estimator in this context.
- Significance: problem has direct practical impact; solution is simple to adopt and shows consistent empirical gains.
- Pro: genuine, previously unrecognized problem with an elegant, practical solution and strong empirical evidence.
- Con: theoretical connections to importance-weight variance and the prior-work unbiasedness claim need clarification.
- Recommendation: accept, with minor revisions to clarify theoretical connections and prior-work claims.
