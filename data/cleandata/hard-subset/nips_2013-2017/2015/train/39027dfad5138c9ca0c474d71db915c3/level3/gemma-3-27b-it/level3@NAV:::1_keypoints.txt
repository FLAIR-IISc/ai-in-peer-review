- Paper identifies propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) as inability to reliably estimate risk when exploration is insufficient, distinct from just large importance-weight variance.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Theoretical arguments and empirical evaluation show the estimator reduces propensity overfitting and improves multi-label classification performance.
- Motivation and presentation are clear; the illustrative example in Section 4 is particularly helpful.
- The self-normalized approach is sensible and draws on established importance-sampling variance-reduction techniques.
- Strong connection to prior work in causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation.
- Empirical results consistently outperform standard methods; ablation studies (Table 3) clarify the role of variance regularization.
- Code is available, aiding reproducibility.
- Reviewer had limited time for the extra review and may have missed technical nuances.
- The paper does not fully explain how large weight variance concretely leads to propensity overfitting; a more direct explanation is needed.
- Line 199’s discussion about n small relative to k is unclear; the behavior of the estimated risk R̂(h^) in the n<k regime requires clarification.
- The claim that prior BLBF works used an unbiased counterfactual risk estimator is questionable given common weight-clipping practices that introduce bias.
- Acceptance arguments: novel and important problem, theoretically grounded and effective solution, clear presentation, empirical gains, and code availability.
- Rejection/concern arguments: limited verification time, unclear variance–overfitting relationship, questionable unbiasedness claim about prior work, and unclear estimator behavior when n<k.
- Despite concerns, the reviewer recommends acceptance.
