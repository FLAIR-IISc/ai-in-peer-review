- Paper proposes Norm-POEM: CRM with a self-normalized importance sampling estimator to address finite-sample issues in IPS for logged bandit structured prediction.  
- Norm-POEM connects to classic importance sampling, control variates, and SN-IS estimators and empirically outperforms POEM, approaching full-information CRF performance.  
- The commentary around Eq. (2) is misleading: IPS is an unbiased estimator of the expectation, but its finite-sample behavior depends on realized importance-weight sums.  
- It is incorrect to treat expectation-level invariances (e.g., translation equivariance) as properties that must hold of finite-sample unnormalized IS estimates.  
- The paper’s claim of an “anomaly” (non-invariance to translation) for Eq. (2) is technically inaccurate and should be reframed as a known finite-sample consequence of unnormalized IS.  
- Example 1 relies on the incorrect expectation-level interpretation and should be revised to illustrate finite-sample instability driven by weight variability instead.  
- The IPS estimator is highly sensitive to small or zero logging policy probabilities π, which can cause infinite or undefined importance weights.  
- The paper should explicitly state the positivity/full-support assumption and discuss practical safeguards (exploration, clipping, floors on π, model constraints).  
- The self-normalized estimator introduces a double denominator that bounds estimates, yields translation equivariance, and reduces variance when weight fluctuations dominate—standard benefits of SN-IS.  
- The methodological choice of SN-IPS in CRM is principled and appropriate, and the tailored variance proxy and optimizer are useful contributions.  
- Empirical results are strong and consistent, showing robustness to loss translation and competitive runtime; CRM regularization remains helpful.  
- Theoretical commentary should be corrected around Eq. (2), and the paper should discuss bias–variance trade-offs and conditions where SN-IPS dominates IPS (including role of clipping M).  
- Clarity would improve by explicitly stating assumptions about π, how π=0 cases are excluded, and how clipping interacts with self-normalization.  
- Originality is moderate: SN estimator is classic, but its integration into CRM for structured outputs is a novel and practical synthesis.  
- Significance is high: consistent empirical gains in counterfactual learning are likely to influence practitioner estimator choices.  
- Pros: principled estimator swap, strong empirical improvements, practical training recipe, and good positioning within prior work.  
- Cons: mischaracterization of IPS finite-sample properties and “anomaly” language, Example 1 needs revision, limited discussion of π≈0 handling, and sparse theoretical guidance on clipping and SN-IPS bias.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on revisions that correct Eq. (2) discussion, revise Example 1, and clarify safeguards and bias–variance/clipping guidance.  
- Additional suggestions: add ablation on clipping thresholds and consider or discuss comparison to doubly robust estimators.
