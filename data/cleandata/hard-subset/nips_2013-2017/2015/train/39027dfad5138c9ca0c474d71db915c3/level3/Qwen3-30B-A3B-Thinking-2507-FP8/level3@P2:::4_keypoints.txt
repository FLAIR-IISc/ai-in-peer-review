- Identifies a critical flaw ("propensity overfitting") in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator for BLBF when used in ERM over complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator and the Norm-POEM algorithm to avoid propensity overfitting.  
- Builds on Swaminathan & Joachims' CRM framework and uncovers a previously overlooked issue in that framework.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous: proves self-normalized estimator avoids propensity overfitting, is equivariant under loss translation, and stays bounded within the loss range.  
- Empirical results consistently show Norm-POEM outperforms the conventional POEM across multiple datasets.  
- Notational mistakes need correction (e.g., line 170–171 should use h(y|x)/h₀(y|x); line 276 should use argmin).  
- Recommend modeling delta(x,y) as a random variable with an expectation to better reflect label noise in real settings.  
- Clarity is generally good, but some notational inconsistencies (line 319 hats vs bars) and ambiguous loss statements (e.g., "delta > 0" in Table 1) require clarification.  
- The paper explains propensity overfitting clearly and insightfully connects the solution to control variates from importance sampling.  
- Originality is high: distinguishes propensity overfitting from standard overfitting and applies multiplicative control variates in a novel way.  
- Significance is high: findings have practical impact on BLBF applications (ads, recommenders) and broader causal inference.  
- Weakness: paper acknowledges the self-normalized estimator is biased but does not fully analyze when the bias is acceptable or quantify bias vs variance trade-offs.  
- Strengths: principled solution, strong theoretical grounding, and comprehensive empirical evaluation using standard supervised→bandit methodology.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix notation and clarify the loss/setup.
