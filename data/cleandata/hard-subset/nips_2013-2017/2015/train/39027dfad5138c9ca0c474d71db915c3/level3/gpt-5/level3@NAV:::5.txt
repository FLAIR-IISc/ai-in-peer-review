This paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) by diagnosing “propensity overfitting” in the standard inverse propensity scoring (IPS) estimator used within Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM). Building directly on Swaminathan & Joachims (2015), the authors propose a self-normalized counterfactual risk estimator (a multiplicative control variate) and instantiate it in a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, for structured prediction with exponential-family policies. The formulation is closely related to importance-weight self-normalization in off-policy evaluation (e.g., Hesterberg; Owen) and to prior work on variance reduction, clipping, and doubly robust estimators (Dudík, Langford, Li; Cortes, Mansour, Mohri; Thomas et al.). The paper argues that self-normalization fixes the nonequivariant, unbounded behavior of IPS that leads to propensity overfitting, proves strong consistency, and integrates an approximate variance estimate into a CRM-style objective. Empirically, on multi-label classification benchmarks under the supervised→bandit protocol, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM, is robust to loss translations, and converges faster in practice.

Quality: Technically sound overall. The propensity-overfitting diagnosis is compelling, the self-normalized estimator is appropriate, and the empirical study is careful with multiple datasets and hyperparameter validation. However, the theoretical treatment could be strengthened: there is no finite-sample generalization bound for CRM with the ratio estimator, and the variance plug-in is asymptotic. Comparisons to strong baselines like doubly robust and clipped-IPS learners are limited.

Clarity: Clear and well organized. The exposition of anomalies (equivariance and boundedness) is pedagogical; suggestions: explicitly separate bias sources from clipping vs. normalization, and give pseudo-code for Norm-POEM for reproducibility.

Originality: High. While self-normalized IS is classical, its use to directly remedy propensity overfitting within CRM and the resulting formulation of Norm-POEM are novel and meaningfully extend POEM.

Significance: Strong. The method is likely to be adopted in BLBF and off-policy learning pipelines; the runtime improvements despite added regularization are noteworthy.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear identification and principled remedy of propensity overfitting.
- Original and practical reformulation of POEM with consistent empirical gains.
- Robustness to loss translations and improved training stability/speed.
- Solid connection to prior NeurIPS literature on importance weighting and off-policy evaluation.

Cons against acceptance:
- Lacks finite-sample learning bounds for the self-normalized CRM objective.
- Baselines omit modern doubly robust learners and high-confidence OPE methods.
- Experiments are limited to supervised→bandit multi-label tasks; no real logged data.

Overall: I recommend acceptance. Adding a finite-sample analysis for the ratio estimator within CRM and broader baselines would further strengthen the paper.
