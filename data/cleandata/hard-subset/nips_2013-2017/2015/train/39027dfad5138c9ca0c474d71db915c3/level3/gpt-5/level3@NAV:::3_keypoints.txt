- Identifies propensity overfitting in IPS-based CRM: ERM exploits importance-weight variability, producing degenerate solutions and loss-shift non-equivariance.  
- Proposes multiplicative control-variate / self-normalized IPS (SNIPS/weighted IPS) as a biased but consistent, bounded, and equivariant estimator to mitigate this failure mode.  
- Instantiates CRM with SNIPS to produce Norm-POEM for structured prediction using exponential-family policies.  
- Theoretical justification: SNIPS gives consistency, boundedness, and equivariance; paper draws on self-normalized IS and variance-reduction literature.  
- Missing theoretical element: no finite-sample generalization bound for the self-normalized CRM objective analogous to prior POEM results.  
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM improves generalization and stabilizes the sum-of-weights diagnostic on multilabel benchmarks from prior work.  
- Experiments are limited in scope (datasets from [1] only); practical impact uncertain without broader/real-world domains (recommendation, search, ads, public logs).  
- Important baselines are absent: doubly robust (DR), clipped-IPS variants, and self-normalized DR are not compared.  
- Experimental reporting gaps: need run-to-run variability (std/CI), ablations over clipping M and λ, and sensitivity analyses to logging policy quality/support.  
- Clarity is high: writing, organization, and diagnostics (propensity-overfitting examples, Ŝ) are illuminating.  
- Presentation could be improved with deeper discussion of the nonconvex CRM objective, optimization stability, and why L-BFGS converges faster in practice.  
- Originality: integration of self-normalized IS into CRM and articulation of propensity overfitting is a useful, incremental advance over POEM.  
- Significance: addresses an important pathology in BLBF, but broader empirical validation is needed to establish practical relevance.
