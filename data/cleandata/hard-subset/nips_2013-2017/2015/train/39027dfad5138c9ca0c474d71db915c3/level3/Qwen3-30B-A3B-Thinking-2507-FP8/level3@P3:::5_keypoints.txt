- Identifies a critical problem in BLBF called "propensity overfitting" where propensity weights are fit instead of the loss.  
- Builds on Swaminathan & Joachims (2015) and proposes a self-normalized risk estimator to address this issue.  
- Shows the conventional unbiased estimator can produce arbitrarily biased (overly optimistic) risk estimates for high-capacity hypothesis spaces.  
- The self-normalized estimator (Eq. 7) bounds risk within the loss range and is equivariant to loss translations.  
- Provides a theoretical proof of strong consistency for the self-normalized estimator (Theorem 2).  
- Demonstrates the standard estimator lacks equivariance and can be driven to low risk by overfitting propensities.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a practical CRM implementation using the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL).  
- Shows Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting by keeping Ŝ(ĥ) near its expected value of 1, whereas POEM is severely biased (Table 2).  
- Norm-POEM is computationally efficient and often converges faster than POEM despite normalization.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates the work within prior counterfactual learning and importance-sampling literature.  
- Strengths: novel problem identification, solid theory, strong empirical gains, faster convergence, and clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of limitations (e.g., denominator near zero), brief theoretical treatment of the variance estimator (Eq. 8), and experiments could explore more diverse hypothesis spaces.  
- Meets NIPS criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance) and the reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
