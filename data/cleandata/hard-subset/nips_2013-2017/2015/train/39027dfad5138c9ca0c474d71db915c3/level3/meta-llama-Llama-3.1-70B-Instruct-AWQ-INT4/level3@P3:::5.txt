This paper proposes a novel method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) that addresses a severe issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, namely propensity overfitting. The authors introduce a self-normalized risk estimator that avoids this problem and develop a new learning algorithm, Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models (Norm-POEM), for structured output prediction.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem of propensity overfitting is clearly explained through examples. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the issue and its implications for BLBF. The proposed self-normalized risk estimator is well-justified, and its properties are rigorously analyzed. The Norm-POEM algorithm is a natural extension of the previous work on POEM, and its advantages over the original POEM are clearly demonstrated.
