- Paper proposes a new self-normalized estimator for counterfactual risk and a learning algorithm Norm-POEM for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Identifies "propensity overfitting": hypothesis spaces can overfit propensity weights, biasing conventional unbiased risk estimators.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator that is equivariant and always bounded within the loss range.
- Shows the self-normalized estimator is strongly consistent and converges to the desired expectation as sample size grows.
- Norm-POEM, based on the new estimator, is extensively evaluated on multi-label classification tasks.
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional POEM estimator in generalization and robustness.
- Paper provides a clear explanation of the relationship between the self-normalized estimator and propensity overfitting.
- Strengths: thorough analysis, well-motivated estimator, extensive empirical evaluation, and released code.
- Weakness: explanation of how large weight variance leads to propensity overfitting is unclear and needs more detail.
- Weakness: reviewer questions the claim that prior BLBF work used unbiased estimators, noting many used clipping (which introduces bias).
- Quality: technically sound, clear and well-organized presentation with adequate background and references.
- Clarity: generally clear but needs improvement on the weight-variance vs. overfitting explanation.
- Originality: introduces a novel estimator and algorithm, making a significant contribution.
- Significance: addresses an important BLBF problem and offers a practical improvement with potential wide use.
- Recommendation: accept paper despite not fully checking technical details, given the sensible idea, thorough analysis, and strong empirical support.
