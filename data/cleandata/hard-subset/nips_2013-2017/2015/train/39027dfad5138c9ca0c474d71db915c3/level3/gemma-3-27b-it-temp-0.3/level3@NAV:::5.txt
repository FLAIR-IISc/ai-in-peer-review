## Review of "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning"

This paper addresses a critical issue in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF): propensity overfitting in the commonly used counterfactual risk estimator. The authors convincingly demonstrate that this estimator can lead to severely biased risk estimates, particularly with complex hypothesis spaces. Their proposed solution, a self-normalized estimator, elegantly avoids this problem and forms the basis for a new algorithm, Norm-POEM, which significantly improves upon the original POEM algorithm. 

The work builds directly upon Swaminathan & Joachims (2015) and extends it with a meaningful and theoretically grounded modification. The problem of propensity overfitting is clearly articulated and the proposed solution is well-motivated. The authors effectively leverage control variate techniques from importance sampling to arrive at a more robust estimator. The theoretical analysis, including the proof of consistency for the self-normalized estimator, adds substantial weight to the contribution. 

The experimental evaluation is thorough and demonstrates consistent performance gains of Norm-POEM over POEM across multiple datasets. The ablation study, showing the continued need for variance regularization even with the improved estimator, is insightful. Furthermore, the observation that Norm-POEM can be faster than the original POEM despite the added regularization is a significant practical advantage. The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and easy to follow, with a logical progression of ideas. 

However, while the paper is strong, a more detailed discussion of the computational cost of the variance estimation in Norm-POEM would be beneficial. Although the authors mention faster convergence, a more precise comparison of per-iteration costs would be valuable. Additionally, exploring the sensitivity of Norm-POEM to the choice of the clipping parameter *M* could further strengthen the analysis.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The self-normalized estimator is a significantly new formulation of POEM, addressing a previously unrecognised issue.
*   **Significance:** The results are compelling and demonstrate a substantial improvement in BLBF performance. The potential for widespread use due to the algorithm's speed is high.
*   **Quality:** The technical analysis is sound, and the experimental validation is robust.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Minor: A more detailed analysis of the computational cost of variance estimation would be beneficial.



**Overall:** This is a high-quality paper with a significant contribution to the field of learning from logged bandit feedback. I strongly recommend acceptance.
