- Tackles learning from logged bandit feedback, extending the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) framework.
- Identifies and names "propensity overfitting," where models exploit importance weights instead of minimizing true loss.
- Clarifies a novel and significant failure mode that can destabilize off-policy learning.
- Proposes using a self-normalized estimator in place of the standard unbiased risk estimator to mitigate this failure.
- Applies a known importance-sampling technique in a new, effective way within CRM.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: consistency, equivariance to loss translations, and boundedness by the loss range.
- Empirical results are strong on several multi-label classification tasks, supporting the claims.
- Strength: original conceptual contribution in diagnosing propensity overfitting.
- Strength: technically sound, well-motivated solution with links to multiplicative control variates.
- Strength: clear writing and an effective motivating example.
- Weakness: the self-normalized estimator itself is not novel.
- Weakness: experimental validation is concentrated on multi-label classification; lacks broader bandit domains (e.g., recommender systems, ad placement).
- Overall: strong paper with practical and theoretical impact; recommended for acceptance.
