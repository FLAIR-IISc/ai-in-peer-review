- Proposes Norm-POEM, a novel batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) method that avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Introduces a self-normalized risk estimator that provably prevents degeneracies in conventional counterfactual risk estimation.  
- Builds on Swaminathan & Joachims (2015) and yields significant improvements over original POEM.  
- Experimental results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM in generalization and robustness.  
- Norm-POEM is faster in training than original POEM despite added regularization.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible to expert readers.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of propensity overfitting and the effectiveness of the proposed solution.  
- Contributions are original and technically sound, with theoretical support and reproducible details.  
- Results are interesting, significant, and warrant further research.  
- Pro-accept arguments: novel approach, strong analysis, consistent empirical gains, faster algorithm, and originality.  
- Con-accept arguments: assumes logged policy is stationary with full support over Y, which may not hold in practice.  
- Con-accept arguments: lacks thorough analysis of computational complexity for large-scale use.  
- Con-accept arguments: does not compare against other state-of-the-art BLBF algorithms.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication.
