- Proposes a hybrid 2D-3D ConvNet (VD2D3D) with recursive training for neuronal boundary detection in serial-section EM.  
- Builds on 2D max-pooling ConvNet, adding increased depth, 3D filters, and recursive refinement.  
- VD2D3D achieves significant improvements in pixel-wise classification error and Rand F-score vs. N4 and VD2D.  
- Paper is well-written with clear methodology, thorough background, and detailed dataset/evaluation descriptions.  
- Strength: effectively leverages both 2D and 3D contextual information to improve boundary detection.  
- Strength: recursive training refines predictions using preliminary boundary maps.  
- Strength: demonstrates substantial accuracy gains over prior state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: provides publicly available dataset and code to facilitate further research.  
- Weakness: high computational cost of training may limit applicability to larger datasets or tasks.  
- Weakness: potential for overfitting due to many parameters and a relatively small training set.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other state-of-the-art approaches (e.g., recurrent networks or graphical models).  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with important implications for image segmentation research.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, clarity, and released resources.  
- Arguments against acceptance: computational burden, overfitting risk, and limited comparative evaluation.
