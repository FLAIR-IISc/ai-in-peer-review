- Addresses learning from logged bandit feedback and identifies a pathology in IPS called "propensity overfitting."
- Propensity overfitting: high-capacity models exploit propensity weights to minimize empirical risk rather than fitting loss.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (multiplicative control variates) that is equivariant to loss translations and bounded, countering the pathology.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, integrating the self-normalized estimator into the CRM framework, with consistency guarantees.
- Empirical results on multi-label tasks show Norm-POEM consistently and substantially outperforms POEM, improving generalization and robustness.
- Strength: clear and convincing diagnosis of propensity overfitting with illustrative analysis in Section 4.
- Strength: principled, well-motivated solution grounded in control variate theory with desirable theoretical properties.
- Strength: strong experimental design and ablation studies demonstrating that gains stem from controlling propensity overfitting and that variance regularization remains useful.
- Strength: significant follow-up to prior CRM work, making the approach more robust and practically applicable.
- Weakness: theoretical justification (Theorem 1) assumes losses in [-1,0], but experiments use non-negative Hamming loss—need clarification on applicability of guarantees.
- Weakness: should model δ(x,y) as a random variable and define true risk with an expectation to reflect loss stochasticity more clearly.
- Weakness / minor corrections: typos and notation issues—correlation description (lines 170–171), argmin vs argmax (line 276), and consistent hat notation for learned hypothesis (line 319).
- Overall: well-executed, original, and significant work with minor clarifications needed; recommendation to accept (Quality 6/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10).
