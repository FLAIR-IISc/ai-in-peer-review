

This paper presents an insightful follow-up to the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) framework [1], addressing a previously overlooked issue in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify and characterize "propensity overfitting" - a phenomenon where conventional unbiased counterfactual estimators produce severely biased risk estimates when used in empirical risk minimization. Their key contribution is proposing a self-normalized risk estimator that avoids this problem by using multiplicative control variates, leading to the Norm-POEM algorithm.

The paper makes several important technical contributions. The theoretical analysis of propensity overfitting (Section 4) is particularly strong, with clear examples demonstrating how the conventional estimator fails to maintain bounded risk estimates. The self-normalized estimator (Equation 7) provides a principled solution that resolves the equivariance and boundedness issues of the conventional approach. The empirical evaluation (Section 7) convincingly shows Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across multiple datasets, with consistent improvements in generalization performance.

I have a few minor concerns that should be addressed. First, the paper should clarify whether the delta in Equations (8) and (3) is rescaled to [-1,0] as in CRM, and explicitly explain the meaning of "delta > 0" in Table 1 experiments. Second, the authors should model delta(x,y) as a random variable with expectation over label noise rather than a deterministic function, and incorporate this expectation into the true risk setup. Third, there are several minor corrections needed: Line 170-171 should use h(y|x)/h_0(y|x) instead of h_0(y|x)/h(y|x); Line 276 should use argmin instead of argmax; and Line 319 should replace bars with hats to match earlier notation.

Despite these minor issues, the paper makes a significant contribution to the BLBF literature. The analysis of propensity overfitting is novel, the self-normalized estimator is theoretically sound, and the empirical results demonstrate clear practical advantages. The work builds effectively on [1] while addressing a critical limitation of prior approaches. The paper is well-written and the technical content is sound.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper with strong theoretical contributions and compelling empirical results. It represents a meaningful advancement in the field with clear potential for practical impact. The authors have provided a valuable analysis of a previously overlooked problem, and their solution effectively resolves it with elegant theoretical properties and demonstrable empirical improvements. The paper deserves acceptance at NIPS.
