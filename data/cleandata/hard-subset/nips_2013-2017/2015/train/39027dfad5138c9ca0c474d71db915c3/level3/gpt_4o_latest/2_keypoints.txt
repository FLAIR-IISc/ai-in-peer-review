- Paper identifies propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and proposes mitigating it by replacing the conventional unbiased risk estimator with a self-normalized risk estimator.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator that shows superior generalization in multi-label classification tasks.
- Work is well-motivated, technically rigorous, and provides empirical evidence supporting the proposed method.
- Novelty/significance: identifies a previously unaddressed issue in counterfactual risk estimation and offers a principled solution.
- Theoretical soundness: self-normalized estimator is rigorously derived with properties like strong consistency and boundedness.
- Empirical validation: Norm-POEM outperforms POEM and approaches full-information CRF performance across multiple datasets.
- Practical impact: method is applicable to interactive systems (ads, search, recommendation) and relevant to real-world scenarios.
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with clear explanations of problem, methodology, and results.
- Incorrect commentary on Equation (2): notation and interpretation are misleading, making Example 1 based on a faulty presumption.
- Estimator sensitivity: original estimator is unstable when propensity p_i is zero for unobserved samples; modified estimator (Eq.7) addresses this but the weakness needs deeper analysis.
- Computational complexity: paper lacks a detailed analysis of computational trade-offs introduced by the self-normalized estimator despite faster practical convergence.
- Limited related work discussion: could better contextualize contributions within broader counterfactual learning and variance reduction literature.
- Pro/Con for acceptance: pro—addresses critical problem with a novel, theoretically sound, empirically validated solution; con—issues with Eq.(2)/Example 1 and insufficient computational analysis.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to fix Eq.(2) commentary and clarify computational trade-offs.
