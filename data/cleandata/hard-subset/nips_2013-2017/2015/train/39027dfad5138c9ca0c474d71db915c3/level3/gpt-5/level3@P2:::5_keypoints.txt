- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) in the CRM framework and identifies a new failure mode called propensity overfitting.
- Shows prior IPS-based CRM is unbiased but high-variance and can lead to degenerate ERM solutions exploiting importance-weight variability.
- Diagnoses propensity overfitting via a constructive example linking IPS non-equivariance and anomalous weight-sum Ŝ(h) behavior.
- Proposes using a multiplicative control variate to form a self-normalized IS estimator (SNIPS) that is bounded, equivariant, and consistent though biased.
- Integrates SNIPS into CRM, derives a sample variance estimator for the ratio estimator, and instantiates Norm-POEM for structured prediction using L-BFGS.
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multi-label datasets in supervised-to-bandit simulations and is more robust to loss translation.
- Norm-POEM avoids severe deviations in Ŝ(ĥ) and often converges faster despite higher per-iteration cost.
- Situates work against relevant literature on importance weighting, variance reduction, doubly robust methods, off-policy evaluation, and self-normalized IS.
- Theory: convincingly illustrates propensity overfitting and motivates SNIPS via control variates; consistency proof follows from SLLN.
- Theory limitation: no new finite-sample generalization bound for SNIPS-CRM analogous to IPS-CRM empirical Bernstein-style bounds.
- Bias from clipping (M) and finite-sample behavior of SNIPS is discussed pragmatically but not bounded.
- Empirics: good experimental design, multiple datasets, loss-translation and variance-regularization analyses; Norm-POEM dominates POEM.
- Empirics limitation: missing comparisons to doubly robust baselines, only simulated bandit logs (no real logs), and limited reporting of error bars and sensitivity analyses.
- Algorithmic detail: optimization is non-convex; L-BFGS use is reasonable and reliance on tractable partition function is explicit.
- Clarity: paper is well written, organized, and the propensity overfitting example is clear and illuminating.
- Minor clarity suggestions: clarify adaptation of CRM generalization bounds to SNIPS, add runtimes and stddevs, discuss sensitivity to mis-specified propensities.
- Originality: high — diagnosis of propensity overfitting and applying SNIPS within CRM (Norm-POEM) is novel despite SNIPS being classical.
- Significance: high — practical, broadly applicable fix with stable empirical gains likely to be adopted in BLBF settings.
- Pros: clear diagnosis, principled bounded estimator, practical algorithm with variance regularization, strong empirical gains, competitive runtime.
- Cons: lack of finite-sample guarantees, missing DR baseline comparisons, simulations only (no real logs), limited analysis of propensity misspecification/support violations.
- Suggested improvements: provide partial finite-sample analysis or concentration bounds, add DR baselines (with/without normalization/clipping), test robustness to noisy/bias propensities and partial support, report error bars, sensitivity to M and λ, and wall-clock runtimes.
- Recommendation: Accept — original, clear, significant contribution despite theoretical and experimental gaps.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium-high (3.5/5).
