The paper "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning" presents a novel approach to batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in BLBF, which they term "propensity overfitting." They propose an alternative estimator, the self-normalized estimator, which avoids this problem and leads to improved generalization performance.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the limitations of the conventional estimator and demonstrate the effectiveness of the self-normalized estimator through extensive empirical evaluations. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the technical details.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of the self-normalized estimator, which is a multiplicative control variate that addresses the propensity overfitting problem. The authors show that this estimator is equivariant, meaning that it is invariant to translations of the loss function, and that it is strongly consistent, meaning that it converges to the true risk as the sample size increases.

The authors also derive a new learning algorithm, called Norm-POEM, which uses the self-normalized estimator and the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle. They demonstrate the effectiveness of Norm-POEM through experiments on several multi-label classification problems, showing that it consistently outperforms the conventional estimator.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the technical details. The writing is clear, and the notation is consistent throughout the paper.
