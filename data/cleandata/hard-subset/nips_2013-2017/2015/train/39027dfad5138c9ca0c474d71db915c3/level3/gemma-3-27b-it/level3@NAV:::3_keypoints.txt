- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback and the propensity overfitting issue in counterfactual risk estimation.  
- Demonstrates propensity overfitting convincingly with a clear motivating example.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator and derives the Norm-POEM algorithm to mitigate overfitting.  
- Builds directly on the authors’ prior work [1] and extends it theoretically and empirically.  
- Presents rigorous theoretical derivation and properties of the self-normalized estimator.  
- Clearly explains the connection to control variates.  
- Empirically shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across evaluated datasets.  
- Ablation study indicates variance regularization remains beneficial; analysis of Ŝ(ĥ) supports claims.  
- Experimental setup largely replicates [1], limiting novelty of empirical evaluation.  
- Real-world application domains (e.g., advertising, recommender systems) are mentioned but not experimentally explored.  
- Results are averaged over 10 runs but lack reported standard deviations or confidence intervals.  
- Claims of computational efficiency would benefit from more detailed runtime comparisons with other BLBF methods.  
- Argument for acceptance: tackles a relevant problem with a novel, theoretically grounded solution.  
- Argument for acceptance: empirical evidence of consistent improvement and clear presentation.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited experimental scope and replication of prior setup.  
- Argument against acceptance: absence of variance reporting weakens assessment of statistical reliability.  
- Argument against acceptance: unclear practical significance due to lack of real-world validations.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to broaden experiments and include variance/runtime reporting.
