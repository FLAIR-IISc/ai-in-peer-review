- Addresses counterfactual learning from logged bandit feedback, building on POEM (ICML 2015).  
- Identifies "propensity overfitting" in standard IPS estimators and proposes a fix.  
- Proposes Norm-POEM: replace IPS with a self-normalized IPS estimator.  
- Extensive experiments show Norm-POEM is more robust and generalizes better than POEM.  
- Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across reported multi-label classification datasets.  
- Performance approaches that of a Conditional Random Field trained with full-information feedback.  
- Self-normalized estimator (Eq. 7) is principled: double-denominator reduces sensitivity to small propensities.  
- Major flaw in theoretical motivation: Eq. (2) is a finite-sample Monte Carlo estimate, not the expected risk.  
- The claim that the estimator's lack of invariance to constant loss translation is an "anomaly" is incorrect.  
- Example 1 is based on the flawed premise and does not validly illustrate propensity overfitting.  
- Despite flawed motivation, the estimator and empirical results are sound and the paper is a valuable contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revising motivation to emphasize known benefits (equivariance, boundedness, robustness to propensity-weight variance).
