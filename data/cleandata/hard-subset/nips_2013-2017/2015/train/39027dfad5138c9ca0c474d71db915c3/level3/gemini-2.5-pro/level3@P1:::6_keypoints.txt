- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and limitations of Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) using IPS.  
- Identifies a critical failure mode called "Propensity Overfitting" where expressive models manipulate importance weights to minimize empirical IPS risk.  
- Demonstrates the failure with an intuitive illustrative example showing arbitrarily low empirical risk via overfitting to logged actions.  
- Proposes replacing IPS with the self-normalized importance sampling (SNIS) estimator to prevent propensity overfitting.  
- Notes SNIS properties: bounded by loss range, equivariant to constant loss shifts, biased in finite samples but strongly consistent.  
- Instantiates CRM with SNIS to create the Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Empirical results on multi-label tasks show Norm-POEM consistently and substantially outperforms POEM and is robust to propensity overfitting.  
- Experimental methodology (Supervised → Bandit) is sound and controlled, and loss-translation experiments strongly support theoretical claims.  
- Paper is high technical quality with clear theoretical arguments and strong empirical validation.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with a logical structure and strong motivating example.  
- Originality lies in framing and identifying propensity overfitting in counterfactual learning and applying SNIS as a principled fix.  
- Contribution is significant for practical logged-data learning, especially as models become more expressive.  
- Weakness: Norm-POEM’s variance estimate is not amenable to stochastic optimization, raising scalability concerns for very large datasets.  
- Weakness: Theoretical depth could be improved with direct generalization bounds or analysis specific to SNIS-based learning.  
- Arguments for acceptance: identifies a novel practical problem, offers a simple principled solution, provides convincing empirical evidence, and has high significance and clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: core technique (SNIS) is well-known in statistics, limiting novelty, and the optimization approach may not scale to massive datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — the strengths substantially outweigh the minor weaknesses.
