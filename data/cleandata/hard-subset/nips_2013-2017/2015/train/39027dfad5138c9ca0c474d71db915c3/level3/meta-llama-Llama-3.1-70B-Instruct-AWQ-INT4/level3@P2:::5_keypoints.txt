- Proposes a novel self-normalized risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) to prevent propensity overfitting.  
- Builds on Swaminathan & Joachims (2015) and formulates a new version of POEM addressing its key limitation.  
- Well-motivated problem statement with clear explanation of significance.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis showing advantages over the conventional unbiased estimator.  
- Experimental results show improved generalization, robustness, and faster training compared to original POEM.  
- Research quality is above average with meaningful extensions and careful evaluation of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent notation with clear figures/tables.  
- Contributions are original—a substantially new formulation of POEM with practical impact potential.  
- Results are significant and likely to advance BLBF, warranting further research and real-world use.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel approach, sound theory, strong empirical improvements, and faster algorithm.  
- Con-acceptance: incremental over authors' prior work for some readers, limited dataset diversity in experiments.  
- Con-acceptance: assumes a specific BLBF setting so generalization to other settings is uncertain.
