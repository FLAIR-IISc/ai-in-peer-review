- Identifies a novel problem: "propensity overfitting" in counterfactual risk estimation for BLBF.  
- Shows standard importance-sampling estimator can produce arbitrarily extreme risk estimates due to large importance weights.  
- Demonstrates the conventional estimator effectively minimizes the sum of weights Ŝ(h) instead of true risk, causing overfitting.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator R̂SN(h) that is loss-translation equivariant and bounded within the loss range.  
- Derives Norm-POEM for structured output prediction using the self-normalized estimator.  
- Provides theoretical justification including a consistency proof (Theorem 2) and a multiplicative control variate derivation.  
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multiple multi-label datasets with statistically significant gains.  
- Presents evidence that Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting (Ŝ(ĥ) stays near 1 vs. severe bias for POEM).  
- Code is released, improving reproducibility.  
- Critique: framing is misleading—prior BLBF work often used clipped importance weights (biased), not exclusively the unclipped "unbiased" estimator.  
- Requests clearer scope: clarify whether critique targets the unclipped estimator specifically or the general BLBF approach.  
- Asks for deeper analysis of how large weight variance causally leads to the identified overfitting, especially in regimes with small n relative to k (Example 1 unclear).  
- Notes empirical ambiguity: unclear how much improvement comes from self-normalization alone versus interaction with CRM variance regularization.  
- Observes runtime/convergence findings are interesting but the connection to propensity overfitting is not explicitly made.  
- Overall: well-written, original in identifying propensity overfitting, significant practical impact, minor framing/explanation issues; recommends acceptance.
