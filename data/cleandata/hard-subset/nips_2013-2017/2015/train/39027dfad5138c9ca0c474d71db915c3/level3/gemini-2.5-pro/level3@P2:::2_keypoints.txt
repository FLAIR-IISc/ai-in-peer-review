- Paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and builds on Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM).  
- Main claim: IPS estimator suffers from "propensity overfitting"; authors propose a self-normalized estimator and introduce Norm-POEM.  
- Self-normalized estimator normalizes by sum of importance weights, is bounded and equivariant to loss translations, and avoids IPS anomalies.  
- Experiments on multi-label datasets show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and approaches fully supervised performance.  
- Strength: tackles an important, practical problem with high potential impact in industry settings.  
- Strength: thorough and convincing empirical evaluation demonstrating substantial gains over prior state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: applying a self-normalized estimator within CRM is a principled, novel, and natural modification (control variate motivation).  
- Weakness: theoretical framing in Section 4 is misleading, conflating finite-sample behavior with properties of the estimator's expectation.  
- Weakness: Example 1 and the "propensity overfitting" argument overstate the issue; observed behavior is better explained by IPS's high variance when weights are large.  
- Recommendation: motivation should be reframed in terms of variance reduction and sensitivity to small logging propensities, which better justifies the self-normalized estimator.  
- For acceptance: simple, effective method with strong empirical results and clear practical value.  
- Against acceptance: flawed theoretical argumentation weakens clarity and technical soundness of the core claim.  
- Conclusion: overall strong paper; recommend acceptance with revision to Section 4 to reframe the motivation around variance reduction.
