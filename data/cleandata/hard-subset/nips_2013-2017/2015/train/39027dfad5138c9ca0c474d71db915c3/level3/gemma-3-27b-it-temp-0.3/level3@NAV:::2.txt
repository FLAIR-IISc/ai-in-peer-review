## Review of "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning"

This paper addresses a critical issue in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF): propensity overfitting in the commonly used counterfactual risk estimator. The authors convincingly argue that this estimator, while unbiased, can lead to degenerate risk estimates and poor generalization performance, particularly with complex hypothesis spaces. They propose a self-normalized estimator as a remedy, demonstrating its theoretical advantages – specifically, equivariance – and empirical superiority through experiments on multi-label classification datasets. The proposed Norm-POEM algorithm, built upon this estimator, consistently outperforms the standard POEM algorithm and approaches the performance of a full-information CRF.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly situating the problem within the broader context of causal inference, off-policy evaluation, and multi-label learning. The connection to existing work in importance sampling and control variates is thoughtfully discussed. The identification of propensity overfitting as a distinct phenomenon is a valuable contribution, and the examples provided effectively illustrate the issue. The theoretical justification for the self-normalized estimator, including the proof of its consistency, is solid. 

However, the commentary surrounding Equation (2) and the subsequent claim of “anomaly” are misleading. The loss in (2) *is* the expected quantity under the proposed importance weighting scheme, and the expectation interpretation is correct. The assertion of non-invariance to translation is technically incorrect; the estimator *is* translation invariant in expectation. Consequently, Example 1 is based on a false premise and does not hold. Furthermore, the sensitivity of the estimator to the denominator π (which can be zero) is a valid concern, but the paper correctly points out that the modified estimator in (7) addresses this through a double denominator. 

Despite these issues with the initial framing, the core contribution – the self-normalized estimator and its application in Norm-POEM – remains significant and the experimental results are compelling. The paper demonstrates a clear improvement over existing methods and offers a practical solution to a challenging problem. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant problem in BLBF.
*   Proposes a theoretically sound and empirically effective solution.
*   Clear and well-written (aside from the issues noted above).
*   Strong experimental validation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Misleading commentary and incorrect claims regarding Equation (2) and its properties.
*   Example 1 is based on a flawed premise.



**Recommendation:** Accept with revisions. The authors should carefully revise their commentary around Equation (2) to accurately reflect the properties of the estimator. Removing the claims of “anomaly” and correcting the reasoning in Example 1 are crucial for the paper’s technical soundness.
