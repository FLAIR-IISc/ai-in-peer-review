This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), a crucial problem in many real-world applications. The authors identify a severe issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in prior works, known as propensity overfitting, and propose a novel solution using a self-normalized estimator. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and their proposed solution.

The key idea of the paper is to replace the conventional estimator with a self-normalized estimator, which is shown to avoid the propensity overfitting problem. The authors provide a detailed derivation of the self-normalized estimator and demonstrate its effectiveness in a series of experiments. The results show that the proposed estimator, known as Norm-POEM, outperforms the conventional estimator in terms of generalization performance and robustness.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity and organization. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, followed by a thorough analysis of the conventional estimator and its limitations. The proposed solution is then presented, along with a detailed derivation and analysis of its properties. The experimental results are well-presented and provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed estimator.

The paper also makes a significant contribution to the field by highlighting the importance of propensity overfitting in BLBF and providing a novel solution to this problem. The authors demonstrate that the conventional estimator can suffer from severe overfitting, leading to poor generalization performance, and show that the proposed estimator can avoid this issue.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. One minor issue is the notation, which can be confusing at times. The authors use a range of different symbols and notation, which can make it difficult to follow the derivations and analysis. Additionally, some of the sections could be more clearly organized, with more explicit headings and subheadings.
