This paper presents a novel approach to batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), a problem that arises in various online systems such as ad placement, web search, and recommendation. The authors identify a severe issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in prior works on BLBF, which they term "propensity overfitting." This problem occurs when the estimator is not equivariant, meaning that its estimate can be arbitrarily far away from the true risk, even when the loss function is translated by a constant.

To address this issue, the authors propose a new estimator, called the self-normalized risk estimator, which is derived from a multiplicative control variate. This estimator is shown to be equivariant and to have a lower variance than the conventional estimator. The authors also develop a new learning algorithm, called Norm-POEM, which is based on the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle and uses the self-normalized risk estimator.

The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed estimator and algorithm, including a proof of its consistency and a bound on its variance. The authors also conduct extensive experiments on several multi-label classification problems, which demonstrate the effectiveness of Norm-POEM in avoiding propensity overfitting and achieving better generalization performance than the original POEM algorithm.
