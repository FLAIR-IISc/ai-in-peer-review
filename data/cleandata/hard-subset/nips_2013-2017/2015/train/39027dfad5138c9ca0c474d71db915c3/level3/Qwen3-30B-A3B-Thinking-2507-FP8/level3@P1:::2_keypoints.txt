- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and proposes a self-normalized estimator (Eq.7) and Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Builds on prior counterfactual learning work, especially POEM (ICML 2015).  
- Main claimed contribution: identifies a problem with the conventional IPS estimator (Eq.2) and replaces it with a self-normalized variant.  
- Fundamental technical error: authors misinterpret Eq.2’s expectation—δ(x,y) is an observed loss, not an expected quantity—invalidating their Example 1 and central “propensity overfitting” claim.  
- Correct point: conventional estimator is sensitive to small/zero logging propensities π = h0(y|x).  
- Self-normalized estimator legitimately improves robustness by normalizing propensity weights and addressing extreme π values.  
- Empirical results are strong: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL) and approaches full-information CRF performance.  
- Norm-POEM maintains Ŝ(h) near its expected value of 1 while POEM shows severe bias; Norm-POEM also converges faster in runtime.  
- Theoretical analysis (Theorem 2) is sound and establishes consistency of the self-normalized estimator.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and reproducible.  
- Connection to control variates and importance sampling is appropriate, but prior work on self-normalized estimators should be more explicitly acknowledged.  
- Main weakness: the misinterpretation of Eq.2 undermines the paper’s motivation and requires correction.  
- Originality is limited in the broader importance-sampling context, but the specific application and empirical validation in counterfactual learning are valuable.  
- Significance is high for real-world BLBF applications (ads, recommendations); with corrected motivation, the paper is a strong publication candidate.
