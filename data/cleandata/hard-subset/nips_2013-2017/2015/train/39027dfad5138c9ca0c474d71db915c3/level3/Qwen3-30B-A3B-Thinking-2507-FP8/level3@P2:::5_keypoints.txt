- Identifies a critical flaw—propensity overfitting—in counterfactual risk estimation for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Shows conventional unbiased estimators can produce arbitrarily biased risk estimates and degenerate solutions when minimizing empirical risk over complex hypothesis spaces.
- Demonstrates the problem is distinct from standard loss overfitting and persists under loss translation, with rigorous examples (Section 4).
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (Eq. 7) using multiplicative control variates to fix these anomalies and ensure equivariance under loss translation.
- Proves strong consistency of the self-normalized estimator (Theorem 2).
- Introduces Norm-POEM by replacing the risk estimator in POEM while retaining the same computational complexity.
- Empirically evaluates on standard BLBF benchmarks (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL) and shows consistent improvements over POEM across datasets and training sizes.
- Runtime analysis shows Norm-POEM converges faster than POEM with better-behaved optimization (shorter ||w|| and bounded overfit risk).
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with an intuitive motivating example and reproducible experimental setup.
- Original contribution: identifies a novel failure mode in BLBF and adapts self-normalized importance sampling ideas to counterfactual learning.
- Significant practical impact for real-world BLBF applications (ads, recommendations) with minimal implementation changes and broader implications for off-policy evaluation.
- Minor weaknesses: could briefly discuss why prior work missed propensity overfitting; variance regularization (λ) remains necessary.
- Overall recommendation: strong acceptance—robust theoretical and empirical results, high significance, and no substantial cons.
