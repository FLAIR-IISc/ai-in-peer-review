This paper proposes a novel approach to batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) that avoids propensity overfitting, a severe problem that can lead to degeneracies in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator. The authors build upon the work of Swaminathan and Joachims (2015) and introduce a self-normalized risk estimator that provably avoids these degeneracies. The proposed approach, called Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models (Norm-POEM), yields a significantly better algorithm than the original POEM.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed solution, and the experimental results. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach in avoiding it. The experimental results show that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the original POEM in terms of generalization performance and robustness.

The research quality is above average, with meaningful extensions to POEM. The proposed extensions lead to significant improvements in training the original POEM, and the paper provides a thorough analysis of the results. The algorithm is faster than the original POEM despite added regularization, which is a significant advantage.

The paper is original and contributes to the field of batch learning from logged bandit feedback. The self-normalized risk estimator is a novel contribution, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in avoiding propensity overfitting. The paper is well-organized and provides enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

The results are interesting and warrant further research. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach in a well-controlled environment, and the results suggest that Norm-POEM can be a useful tool for batch learning from logged bandit feedback.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of Norm-POEM, which may be a concern for large-scale applications.
* The paper does not provide a comparison with other state-of-the-art algorithms for batch learning from logged bandit feedback, which may be a limitation of the study.
