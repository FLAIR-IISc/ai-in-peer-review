This paper proposes an alternative estimator to the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional estimator, which they term "propensity overfitting." This problem occurs when the capacity of the hypothesis space allows for overfitting of the propensity weights, leading to severely biased risk estimates. The authors propose a self-normalized risk estimator that avoids this problem and demonstrate its effectiveness in a series of experiments.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The introduction provides a clear motivation for the work and sets the stage for the rest of the paper. The related work section is comprehensive and provides a good overview of the existing literature on BLBF.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and demonstrate that the self-normalized risk estimator is a effective solution. The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate the superiority of the proposed estimator over the conventional estimator.

One potential criticism of the paper is that it does not provide a clear explanation of why the self-normalized risk estimator is effective in avoiding propensity overfitting. While the authors provide some intuition about why the estimator works, a more detailed analysis would be helpful.

Another potential criticism is that the paper does not provide a clear comparison with other existing methods for BLBF. While the authors mention some related work, a more detailed comparison with other methods would be helpful in understanding the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.

In terms of the conference reviewing guidelines, I would rate the paper as follows:

* Quality: 8/10. The paper is well-written and clearly organized, and the technical contributions are significant.
* Clarity: 9/10. The paper is easy to follow, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their ideas.
* Originality: 9/10. The paper proposes a new estimator for BLBF, and the technical contributions are significant.
* Significance: 9/10. The paper addresses an important problem in BLBF and provides a effective solution.

Overall, I would recommend accepting the paper for publication. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field of BLBF, and the paper is well-written and clearly organized.
