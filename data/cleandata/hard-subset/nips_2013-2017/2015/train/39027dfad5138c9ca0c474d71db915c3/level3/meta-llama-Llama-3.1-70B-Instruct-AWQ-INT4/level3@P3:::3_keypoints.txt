- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies "propensity overfitting" as a severe issue with conventional counterfactual risk estimators.
- Proposes a novel self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Provides extensive empirical evaluation demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed estimator.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and well-organized, with reproducible experimental details.
- Technical soundness is good: thorough analysis of propensity overfitting and theoretical justification for the proposed solution.
- Originality is strong: adapts diagnostic tools from importance sampling to detect and avoid propensity overfitting.
- Main novelty centers on the self-normalized estimator and improved empirical performance.
- Significance is uncertain: unclear how experimental gains on simulated datasets translate to real-world applications.
- Experiments use simulated bandit feedback from supervised multi-label classification, which may not reflect real-world complexity.
- Reviewer expected more experiments and a deeper analysis of the training-objective optimization.
- Relation to prior work is not fully clarified; more context and comparisons to existing methods are needed.
- Suggested improvements: add more experiments, deeper optimization analysis, clearer comparisons to prior work, and discussion of real-world limitations.
- Scores well on quality, clarity, and originality, but weaker on demonstrated significance and depth of empirical/optimization analysis.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address experimental breadth, optimization analysis, and contextualization with prior work.
