- Paper diagnoses "propensity overfitting" in CRM with IPS and proposes a self-normalized (SN) risk estimator; derives Norm-POEM with consistency and properties (equivariance, boundedness) and shows empirical gains over POEM.  
- Theoretical motivation for SN-IPS is sound and well-connected to Monte Carlo variance reduction.  
- Clarification needed whether Norm-POEM assumes rescaled loss δ∈[−1,0] (state how δ is rescaled in training/evaluation) and explain references to “δ>0” in experiments (how δ is shifted and whether normalization uses raw or shifted δ).  
- δ(x,y) should be treated as a random variable; the true risk should explicitly include expectation over label noise.  
- The variance formula (Eq. 8) is an asymptotic approximation; authors should specify the clipping M used and report sensitivity to M.  
- Add empirical comparisons to doubly-robust or weighted control-variate baselines to contextualize gains.  
- Clarity corrections: fix ratio to h(y|x)/h0(y|x) (lines 170–171) and note CRM nonlinearity was discussed in [1].  
- Additional clarity fixes: use argmin (line 276) not argmax, and replace bars with hats to match notation (line 319).  
- Originality: high — framing propensity overfitting and using multiplicative control variates for learning is a neat, impactful twist.  
- Significance: solid — analysis is insightful, estimator practical, and empirical gains convincing.  
- Pros: clear conceptual advance over [1], useful diagnostic Ŝ(h), principled bounded/equivariant estimator, consistent empirical improvements and competitive runtime.  
- Cons: unclear treatment of loss scaling/translation and δ stochasticity, underreported variance approximation/clipping choices, and missing DR/control-variate baseline comparisons.  
- Post-rebuttal assessment scores: Quality 6, Clarity 6, Originality 8, Significance 8.
