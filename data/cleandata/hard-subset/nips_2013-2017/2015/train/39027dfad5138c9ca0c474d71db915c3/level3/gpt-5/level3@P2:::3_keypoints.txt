- Paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) under CRM and diagnoses "propensity overfitting" where IPS-driven ERM exploits importance weight variability.
- Proposes replacing unbiased IPS with a multiplicative control-variate (self-normalized) estimator (SN-IPS/WIS) that is biased but consistent, bounded, and equivariant to loss translations.
- Introduces Norm-POEM: a CRM-style algorithm in a linear-exponential hypothesis class using SN-IPS and an approximate variance penalty for the ratio estimator.
- Empirical results on multi-label datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL) show consistent improvements over POEM, reduced sensitivity to loss translation, better Ŝ(h) diagnostics, and competitive runtime.
- Builds on CRM/POEM and classical importance sampling/control-variate literature, connecting BLBF to OPE and domain adaptation.
- Primary novelty is diagnosing propensity overfitting in learning and integrating SN-IPS into a CRM learning objective for structured prediction.
- Strength: clear problem identification with convincing examples and Ŝ(h) diagnostics.
- Strength: methodologically sound use of multiplicative control variates; boundedness and equivariance are compelling for learning.
- Strength: concrete algorithmic instantiation (Norm-POEM) with a tractable objective and practical training.
- Strength: consistent empirical gains across datasets and replay counts, and good presentation/writing.
- Weakness: limited experimental scope—datasets mirror prior work and lack large-action real-world BLBF tasks (ads, recommender, search).
- Weakness: missing important baselines such as doubly robust (DR) methods, regression control variates, and weight-clipping variants.
- Weakness: reporting averages without variability—needs confidence intervals, standard deviations, and effective sample size (ESS).
- Weakness: optimization analysis is limited—nonconvex objective with ratio-term needs diagnostics on landscapes, convergence, and initialization sensitivity.
- Weakness: theoretical gap—no generalization bound for the self-normalized CRM objective analogous to prior work; bias–variance discussion is missing.
- Requests: report run variability (std/CI), ESS, and Ŝ(h) distributions and test statistical significance of gains.
- Requests: add DR baselines (DR-IPS, DR-WIS, clipped variants) and regression control variates for comparison.
- Requests: provide ablations and sensitivity curves for clipping M and variance-penalty λ.
- Requests: include at least one real-world or semi-synthetic large-action experiment to test heavy-tailed propensities and support violations.
- Requests: calibrate the approximate variance estimator against empirical variance and report any under/over-confidence.
- Requests: discuss or provide a generalization guarantee for the SN-CRM objective and highlight bias–variance trade-offs.
- Requests: report optimization details—iteration counts, convergence criteria, initialization, and wall-clock/time breakdowns.
- Overall: solid, well-presented follow-up to prior work with a principled fix and consistent gains, but requires broader experiments, DR baselines, and stronger variability/theoretical analyses to confirm real-world impact.
