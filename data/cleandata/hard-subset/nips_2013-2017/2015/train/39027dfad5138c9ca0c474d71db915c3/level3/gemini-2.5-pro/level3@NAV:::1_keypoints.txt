- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies a key failure mode of IPS called "propensity overfitting."
- Propensity overfitting occurs when complex models exploit the logging policy to achieve artificially low empirical risk, distinct from high importance-weight variance.
- Proposes using the self-normalized importance sampling estimator to mitigate this failure mode, leading to the Norm-POEM algorithm within the CRM framework.
- Norm-POEM is empirically shown to significantly and consistently outperform prior methods (e.g., POEM).
- Strength: clear identification and explanation of a subtle, practical problem with useful conceptual framing.
- Strength: solution is well-motivated by importance sampling literature and directly applicable to CRM.
- Experiments are strong and code release aids reproducibility.
- Originality: applying the self-normalized estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting is novel, though the estimator itself is not new.
- Clarity: paper is well-written with an effective illustrative example and available code.
- Clarification requested: better explain the relationship and interplay between propensity overfitting and high weight variance.
- Clarification requested: nuance the claim that prior BLBF works used "unbiased" estimators since clipping/thresholding introduce bias to control variance.
- Clarification requested: elaborate the example regarding ERM behavior when sample size n is small relative to number of actions k.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution and recommend acceptance.
