This paper proposes a novel estimator, the self-normalized estimator, for counterfactual learning in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, which they term "propensity overfitting." They argue that this problem can lead to degenerate risk estimates and propose the use of a multiplicative control variate to derive the self-normalized risk estimator. This new estimator is shown to avoid the anomalies of the conventional estimator and is used to develop a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, for structured output prediction.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problems with the conventional estimator. The proposed self-normalized estimator is intuitive and well-justified, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of related work and clearly explain how their approach differs from previous methods.

The paper's technical contributions are significant, and the authors provide a rigorous analysis of the self-normalized estimator. Theorem 2, which shows that the self-normalized estimator is strongly consistent, is a notable result. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the CRM principle and how it is used to develop the Norm-POEM algorithm.

However, there are some issues with the paper's notation and commentary. Specifically, the notation and commentary around equation (2) are misleading, and the claim that (2) has an "anomaly" (non-invariance to translation of the loss) is technically incorrect. Example 1 is also based on this wrong presumption and therefore does not hold. The authors should revise their commentary to correct these errors.

The experimental results are convincing, and the authors demonstrate that Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on all datasets and approaches CRF performance using full multi-label information.
