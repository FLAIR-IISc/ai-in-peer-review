- Problem: Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies propensity overfitting in conventional counterfactual risk estimators.  
- Issue: Propensity overfitting occurs when hypothesis capacity allows overfitting propensity weights, causing severely biased risk estimates.  
- Solution: Introduces a multiplicative control variate that yields a self-normalized (SN) risk estimator.  
- SN estimator benefit: More accurate than the unbiased estimator when sampling-weight fluctuations dominate loss fluctuations.  
- Equivariance: The SN estimator is invariant to translations of the loss function.  
- Algorithm: Derives Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm using the SN estimator and Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM).  
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM outperforms conventional POEM on several multi-label classification problems.  
- Analysis/quality: Paper is well-written, technically sound, and provides a thorough analysis of the SN estimator and its properties.  
- Originality: Main novelty is the self-normalizing risk estimator addressing propensity overfitting.  
- Related work: Follow-up to prior work [1]; clearly positions contribution relative to literature.  
- Experimental limitations: Reviewer requests more experiments, especially on additional and real-world applications beyond those in [1].  
- Reproducibility/variance: Requests variance reporting from repeated runs to assess stability of gains.  
- Optimization analysis: Desires deeper analysis of the training-objective optimization and Norm-POEM behavior across scenarios.  
- Significance uncertainty: Unclear how empirical gains translate to real-world impact; further experiments/analysis needed.
