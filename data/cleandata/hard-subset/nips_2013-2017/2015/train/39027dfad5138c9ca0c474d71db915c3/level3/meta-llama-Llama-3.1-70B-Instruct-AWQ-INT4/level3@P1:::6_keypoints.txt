- Introduces a self-normalized estimator to address propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Well-motivated problem statement with clear explanation of significance.
- Thorough and knowledgeable review of related work.
- Sound technical analysis including proof of consistency and a variance bound for the estimator.
- Proposes a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, derived from the estimator.
- Experimental evaluation is well-designed and uses multiple datasets.
- Empirical results show the self-normalized estimator and Norm-POEM reduce propensity overfitting and improve generalization.
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and accessible to a broad audience.
- The approach is original and makes a meaningful contribution to BLBF.
- The paper has high practical significance and applicability across settings.
- Quality of presentation and experimental reporting is strong.
- Limitation: assumes reader familiarity with BLBF and counterfactual learning, which may hinder accessibility for some.
- Limitation: experimental scope limited to selected datasets; generalization to other datasets is unclear.
- Limitation: lacks detailed comparisons to alternative estimators (e.g., doubly robust, weighted importance sampling).
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance based on strengths outweighing limitations.
