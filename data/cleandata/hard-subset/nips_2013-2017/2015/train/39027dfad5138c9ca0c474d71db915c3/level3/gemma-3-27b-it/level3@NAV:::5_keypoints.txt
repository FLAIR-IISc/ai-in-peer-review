- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) that biases counterfactual risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator that avoids propensity overfitting and yields the Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Norm-POEM consistently outperforms original POEM empirically and trains faster despite added regularization.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and clearly explains how the self-normalized estimator mitigates the problem.  
- Builds on Swaminathan & Joachims (2015) and situates the work within CRM, covariate shift, and off-policy evaluation literature.  
- Empirical evaluation across multiple datasets is convincing and shows consistent performance gains.  
- Writing is clear with a logical progression from problem to solution and validation.  
- The formulation is original and has meaningful practical impact on training POEM.  
- Minor weakness: limited depth of comparison to other propensity-weighting mitigation methods (e.g., weight clipping).  
- Minor weakness: lacks a direct comparison to a full-information CRF trained on the same limited supervised data.  
- Arguments for acceptance: identifies an important problem, provides a novel theoretically grounded solution, shows strong empirical results and faster training, and significantly improves POEM.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs more detailed comparisons to alternative mitigation strategies and to CRF baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address comparison gaps.
