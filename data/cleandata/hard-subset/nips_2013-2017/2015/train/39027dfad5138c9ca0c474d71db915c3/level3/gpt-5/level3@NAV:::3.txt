Summary: The paper tackles batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) within the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) paradigm. It identifies a failure mode of the conventional inverse propensity scoring (IPS) estimator—propensity overfitting—where ERM exploits variability in importance weights, leading to degenerate solutions and lack of equivariance to loss shifts. The authors propose a multiplicative control-variate approach (self-normalization, a.k.a. weighted IPS/SNIPS) to form a biased but consistent estimator that is bounded and equivariant. Instantiating CRM with this estimator yields Norm-POEM for structured prediction with exponential-family policies. Empirically, on multilabel benchmarks used in prior work, Norm-POEM improves generalization and stabilizes the diagnostic sum of weights Ŝ(h), addressing the identified pathology. The work builds directly on POEM [1] and draws from classic self-normalized importance sampling (e.g., Hesterberg), DR/variance-reduction literature, and empirical Bernstein bounds.

Quality: The problem is well-motivated and the estimator choice is theoretically justified (consistency, boundedness, equivariance). However, the CRM objective now uses an approximate variance for a ratio estimator; a finite-sample generalization guarantee analogous to [1] for the self-normalized case is not provided. Experimental support is solid but limited in scope; key baselines (e.g., doubly robust/DR, clipped-IPS variants, self-normalized DR) are missing. Please report variability across repeated runs (std/CI), add ablations over clipping M and λ, and include sensitivity to logging policy quality/support.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written, well organized, and the propensity-overfitting examples and Ŝ diagnostics are illuminating. Presentation could benefit from a deeper discussion of the nonconvex objective, optimization stability, and why L-BFGS converges faster in practice.

Originality: Self-normalized IS is known, but its systematic integration into CRM for learning (not just evaluation), the articulation of propensity overfitting, and the Norm-POEM instantiation constitute a thoughtful and useful incremental contribution over [1].

Significance: BLBF is important, and mitigating IPS pathologies matters. That said, the practical impact remains uncertain without evidence beyond the datasets of [1]. Real-world or at least additional domains (e.g., recommendation, search, ads; public logged datasets) would strengthen claims.

Pros (arguments for acceptance):
- Clear identification and analysis of a real pathology; principled, simple fix.
- Stronger empirical robustness and consistent improvements over POEM.
- Clear writing; useful diagnostics and insights for practitioners.

Cons (arguments against acceptance):
- Incremental advance over [1]; estimator is standard.
- Missing baselines (DR, clipped/normalized variants) and run-to-run variance.
- No finite-sample CRM bound for the self-normalized objective; limited application scope.
