- Presents a follow-up to Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) addressing a gap in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Identifies and characterizes "propensity overfitting": conventional unbiased counterfactual estimators produce severely biased risk estimates in ERM.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates, yielding the Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Theoretical analysis (Section 4) is strong, with clear examples showing failure modes of the conventional estimator.  
- The self-normalized estimator (Eq. 7) resolves equivariance and boundedness issues of the conventional approach.  
- Empirical evaluation (Section 7) shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple datasets and improves generalization.  
- Requests clarification whether delta in Eqs. (8) and (3) is rescaled to [-1,0] as in CRM and what "delta > 0" means in Table 1.  
- Suggests modeling delta(x,y) as a random variable (expectation over label noise) and incorporating that into the true risk setup.  
- Notes minor corrections: Line 170–171 ratio should be h(y|x)/h_0(y|x); Line 276 should use argmin not argmax; Line 319 should use hats not bars.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, novel, and makes a significant contribution with clear practical impact.  
- Recommendation: accept to NIPS.
