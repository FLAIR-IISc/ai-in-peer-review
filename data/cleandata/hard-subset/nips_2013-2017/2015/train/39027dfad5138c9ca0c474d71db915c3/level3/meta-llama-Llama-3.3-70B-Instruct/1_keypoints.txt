- Identifies a severe propensity overfitting problem in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) that can produce degenerate risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (Norm-POEM) to avoid degeneracy and provide more robust risk estimates.  
- Norm-POEM demonstrates better generalization and outperforms conventional POEM in experiments on several multi-label classification tasks.  
- Paper provides thorough analysis and clear explanation of propensity overfitting and the proposed solution.  
- Experimental results are well-presented and convincingly show the effectiveness of the method.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and accessible, which aids comprehension.  
- Availability of code for the proposed algorithm facilitates reproducibility and adoption.  
- Recommends acceptance based on quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Would benefit from more discussion linking propensity overfitting to large variance of importance weights.  
- Needs clarification on the correctness of Equation (2) and the prior claim of unbiased counterfactual risk estimation.  
- Minor notation and formatting issues should be cleaned up.
