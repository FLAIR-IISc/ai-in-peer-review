- Identifies and names "Propensity Overfitting," where IPS weights can be manipulated to lower empirical risk without truly improving loss.  
- Proposes replacing IPS with a self-normalized risk estimator (ratio form using a multiplicative control variate) to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Shows the self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss shifts, bounded by observed losses, and strongly consistent.  
- Integrates the estimator into CRM to produce Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm.  
- Empirical results on multi-label tasks show Norm-POEM consistently and substantially outperforms POEM.  
- Motivating example in Section 4 clearly and effectively illustrates the propensity overfitting problem.  
- Solution is principled and well-grounded in control variates and ratio-estimator literature, not an ad-hoc fix.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and connects clearly to prior work with sufficient technical detail.  
- Main novelty is incremental—application of a known self-normalized estimator to CRM—so contribution feels like an important correction rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks variance/error bars and statistical significance reporting despite averaging over runs.  
- Experiments use the same limited set of datasets as prior work; broader, real-world logged bandit datasets would strengthen claims.  
- Analysis of why Norm-POEM converges faster and yields better-behaved solutions is brief; deeper optimization analysis or visualizations are needed.
