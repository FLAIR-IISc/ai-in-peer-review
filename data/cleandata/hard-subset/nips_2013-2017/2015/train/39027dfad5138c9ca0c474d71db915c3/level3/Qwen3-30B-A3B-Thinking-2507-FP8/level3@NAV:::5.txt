

This paper identifies a critical flaw in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), namely propensity overfitting, and proposes a novel self-normalized estimator that avoids this problem. The authors build upon Swaminathan & Joachims (2015) to develop Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm that replaces the standard unbiased estimator with a self-normalized version. The paper demonstrates theoretically that the self-normalized estimator resolves key anomalies of the conventional estimator, including non-equivariance and unbounded risk estimates, while maintaining strong consistency.

The research quality is excellent, with rigorous theoretical analysis that clearly demonstrates how the proposed estimator avoids propensity overfitting. The authors provide a compelling theoretical argument supported by extensive empirical validation across multiple multi-label classification datasets. The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the problem, proposed solution, and experimental results.

The originality is significant, as the self-normalized estimator represents a fundamentally new approach to counterfactual learning that differs substantially from previous work. The paper makes a clear contribution to the field by identifying and solving a previously unrecognized problem in BLBF. The significance is high, as the method not only outperforms existing approaches but also offers computational advantages—Norm-POEM trains faster than POEM despite adding regularization, making it highly practical for real-world applications.

The results are compelling and warrant further research, with consistent improvements across multiple datasets and experimental conditions. The paper addresses a challenging problem in a demonstrably better way than previous research, advancing the state of the art in counterfactual learning. While the paper doesn't explicitly discuss limitations, the thorough experimental validation and clear theoretical justification make this a strong contribution.

Pro: Strong theoretical foundation, significant empirical improvements, clear writing, practical algorithm, meaningful extension to POEM. Con: None identified in the paper's current form. This paper represents a substantial contribution to the field with high potential for widespread adoption. I recommend acceptance.
