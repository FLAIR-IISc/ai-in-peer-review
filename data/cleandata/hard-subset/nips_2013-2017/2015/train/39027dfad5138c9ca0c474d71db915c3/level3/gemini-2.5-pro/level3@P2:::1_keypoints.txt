- Addresses Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback (BLBF) and limitations of standard Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM).  
- Identifies and names a critical failure mode: "Propensity Overfitting" where learners exploit propensity weights instead of fitting losses.  
- Shows propensity overfitting can produce arbitrarily poor risk estimates unrelated to true loss range.  
- Proposes replacing the inverse propensity score estimator with a self-normalized (ratio) estimator from Monte Carlo literature.  
- Motivates the self-normalized estimator as using the sum of importance weights as a multiplicative control variate.  
- The self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss translations, bounded by observed loss range, and consistent.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, an algorithm instantiating the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical results on multi-label classification show Norm-POEM is more robust and generalizes better than POEM.  
- Strength: novel and significant identification and analysis of propensity overfitting distinct from standard loss overfitting.  
- Strength: proposed solution is simple, elegant, theoretically well-motivated, and reproducible (code provided).  
- Strength: clear writing and an effective illustrative example that builds intuition.  
- Weakness: needs clearer discussion of the relationship between propensity overfitting and classic high-variance importance weights.  
- Weakness: should clarify applicability of propensity overfitting to biased variance-reduction estimators like clipping and expand the claim that clipping can exacerbate the effect.  
- Weakness: Example 1’s derivation (how hoverfit achieves arbitrarily low risk) should be spelled out more explicitly for clarity.  
- Overall evaluation: technically sound, original, significant, and recommended for acceptance despite minor clarifications needed.
