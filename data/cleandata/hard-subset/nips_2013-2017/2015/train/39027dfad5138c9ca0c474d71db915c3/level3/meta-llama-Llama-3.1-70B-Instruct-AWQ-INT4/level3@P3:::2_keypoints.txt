- Proposes a self-normalized estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Identifies a severe degeneracy issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator when used in ERM.  
- The self-normalized estimator provably avoids these degeneracies and is empirically more robust.  
- Paper is well-motivated and propensity overfitting is clearly illustrated with examples.  
- Thorough literature review covering counterfactual estimators, importance sampling, and control variates.  
- The estimator is based on a multiplicative control variate to reduce variance.  
- Norm-POEM (proposed method) consistently outperforms conventional POEM on several multi-label datasets.  
- Experiments are thorough, well-designed, and show better generalization and robustness of Norm-POEM.  
- Commentary and notation around equation (2) are misleading; the loss in (2) is not the expected quantity.  
- The claim that (2) has an "anomaly" (non-invariance to translation) is technically incorrect.  
- Example 1 relies on the incorrect presumption about (2) and therefore does not hold.  
- The estimator in (2) is sensitive to its denominator π and can be problematic if π is zero for unseen samples.  
- The modified estimator in (7) is preferable since its double denominator mollifies effects of π.  
- Overall assessment: convincing and worth publication, subject to revisions addressing the noted issues.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revising commentary/notation around (2) and addressing estimator sensitivity to π.
