- Paper addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and proposes a self-normalized estimator (Equation 7) and Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Authors claim a previously overlooked flaw in the conventional importance-weighted estimator (Equation 2) that can cause overfitting.  
- Reviewer identifies a critical technical error: Equation (2) is an importance-weighted estimate, not a plain expectation, so its non-equivariance to loss translation is expected, not anomalous.  
- This misunderstanding invalidates Example 1 and the paper’s theoretical claim that Equation (2)’s behavior constitutes a new “propensity overfitting” phenomenon.  
- The self-normalized estimator is nevertheless valuable: double denominator mollifies small propensities and bounds the estimate within the loss range.  
- Empirical results are strong: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and approaches CRF performance using only bandit feedback.  
- Experiments show Norm-POEM keeps the sum of propensity weights close to 1, while POEM is severely biased, and verify equivariance to loss translation.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and connects cleanly to prior work (POEM, CRM).  
- Main weakness is the theoretical misinterpretation of Equation (2); authors should revise discussion and Example 1 accordingly.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to correct the theoretical interpretation and discussion of the “anomaly.”
