- Paper studies batch learning from logged bandit feedback under CRM, identifies "propensity overfitting" (hypotheses exploit rare/missing logged actions), and proposes using self-normalized importance weights (SNIPS) instantiated as Norm-POEM with code and empirical gains.  
- Core idea is sensible: self-normalization mitigates weight-fluctuation pathologies, addressing lack of equivariance and bounding that exacerbate propensity overfitting.  
- Empirical evaluation is careful and aligns with theory (e.g., Ŝ(h) near 1), though reviewer had limited time to verify all technical details and originality claims.  
- Reviewer requests a clearer formal link between weight variance and propensity overfitting—ideally a proposition tying Ŝ(h) deviations to bias of R̂(h) and Var(weights).  
- Reviewer asks for clearer assumptions and derivation in Example 1 (line 199), especially when n is small relative to k, and reconciliation with earlier loss-range assumptions.  
- Prior BLBF literature often used clipping/biased estimators; the paper should qualify the claim that "virtually all" used unbiased estimators.  
- Originality: naming and systematically addressing propensity overfitting in BLBF is novel and useful, even though SNIPS itself is known.  
- Significance: practical, timely contribution likely to influence off-policy learning practice; code availability is appreciated.  
- Pros: clear problem formulation, practical fix with strong intuition, bounded/equivariant estimator, consistency, and empirical improvements across datasets.  
- Cons: theory needs tightening to connect propensity overfitting to variance and generalization bounds; some prior-work claims need nuance; a few derivations are unclear.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
