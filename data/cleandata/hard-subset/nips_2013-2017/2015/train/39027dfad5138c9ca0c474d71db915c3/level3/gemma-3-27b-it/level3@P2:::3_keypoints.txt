- Identifies propensity overfitting in CRM for batch learning from logged bandit feedback and shows the standard unbiased risk estimator is vulnerable.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator based on control variates to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Develops a new algorithm, Norm-POEM, implementing the self-normalized estimator.
- Empirical results on multi-label classification datasets show consistent performance gains of Norm-POEM over POEM.
- Builds logically on prior BLBF work and situates contributions within causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation.
- Introduction effectively motivates the problem and limitations of existing approaches.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and accessible to a broad NIPS audience.
- Mathematical derivations are concise, clear, and connect well to importance sampling and related techniques.
- Authors thoroughly acknowledge and discuss related work, including variance reduction and propensity score methods.
- Strong theoretical motivation: formalizes propensity overfitting and justifies the self-normalized alternative.
- Solid empirical validation with a fair experimental setup mirroring prior methodology.
- Presents Norm-POEM as a practical algorithm with clear implementation details.
- Limited experimental scope: primarily replicates prior settings and multi-label datasets, lacking diverse or real-world applications.
- Insufficient analysis of training dynamics, e.g., why Norm-POEM converges faster and how optimization/gradients behave.
- Missing reporting of result variance across multiple runs, reducing clarity on robustness.
- Improvements are positive but the magnitude of gains is moderate and real-world impact is not fully established.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important theoretical problem, provides a sound solution, strong writing, and available code.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental breadth, unclear significance of gains, and shallow analysis of training behavior.
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution that merits acceptance with a recommendation to broaden experiments and deepen training analysis.
- Recommendation: Accept.
