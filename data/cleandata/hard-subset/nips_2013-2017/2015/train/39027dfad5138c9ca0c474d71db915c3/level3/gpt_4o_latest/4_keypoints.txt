- Paper proposes a novel self-normalized risk estimator and Norm-POEM algorithm to fix propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Norm-POEM consistently outperforms conventional POEM on multi-label classification in experiments, improving generalization and robustness.  
- The self-normalized estimator is a significant and original application of importance sampling and control variates to BLBF.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong, providing proofs of consistency and boundedness and using propensity weight sums (Ŝ(h)) as a diagnostic for overfitting.  
- Empirical validation is compelling across multiple datasets and shows mitigation of catastrophic failures seen in POEM.  
- Method is computationally efficient with runtimes comparable to existing approaches, making it practically relevant for recommendation and ad-placement tasks.  
- Results are clearly presented, showing robustness to loss transformations and improved stability.  
- Presentation needs clarifications: ambiguity about whether Norm-POEM rescales δ to [-1,0] and what "δ > 0" means in Table 1.  
- Treatment of feedback δ as deterministic in Section 3 misses noisy labeling scenarios; modeling δ as random would improve generality.  
- The true risk setup should include an expectation over δ's randomness to strengthen the theoretical framework.  
- Notational inconsistencies: ratio should be h(y|x)/h0(y|x) (lines 170–171), use argmin not argmax for the objective (line 276), and replace bars with hats for estimates (line 319).  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a critical BLBF problem with a theoretically sound, empirically validated solution that advances counterfactual learning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: requires minor revisions for clarity and notation and extension to noisy feedback scenarios for broader applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address clarity, notation, and handling of noisy feedback.
