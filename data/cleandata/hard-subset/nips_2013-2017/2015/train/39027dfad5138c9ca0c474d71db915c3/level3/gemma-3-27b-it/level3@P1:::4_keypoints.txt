- Identifies propensity overfitting in counterfactual risk estimation for BLBF: unbiased estimator can overfit in complex hypothesis spaces and generalize poorly.
- Shows the conventional unbiased estimator leads to severely biased risk estimates and poor performance via a compelling example and experiments.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (multiplicative control variates) as a remedy to propensity overfitting.
- Introduces Norm-POEM (Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models) and applies it to multi-label classification tasks.
- Demonstrates theoretical advantages of the self-normalized estimator and empirical effectiveness: Norm-POEM outperforms POEM [1].
- Builds on prior BLBF work (especially [1]) and connects to causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation.
- Strength: insightful analysis and convincing experimental validation of the propensity overfitting problem.
- Strength: elegant use of self-normalization / multiplicative control variates as a practical and theoretical fix.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation following supervised-to-bandit methodology enabling clear comparison.
- Clarification needed: formally model the loss δ(x,y) as a random variable and express true risk as E[δ(x,y)] to clarify expectations.
- Clarification needed: explicitly define what "delta > 0" means in Table 1.
- Clarification needed: state whether Norm-POEM rescales δ to [-1,0] like CRM, since this affects theoretical guarantees and interpretation.
- Technical correction: importance weight ratio should be h(y|x) / h0(y|x) (lines 170–171).
- Attribution/correction: acknowledge that the cited nonlinearity was previously noted in [1].
- Typo correction: line 276 should read argmin (objective minimizes risk), not argmax.
- Notation correction: line 319 should use hats (^) for empirical estimates instead of bars (|) to be consistent.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel identification/analysis of propensity overfitting, significant practical impact, sound theoretical and empirical support, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: minor technical/notation clarifications needed and potential for deeper theoretical analysis of conditions guaranteeing improvement.
- Overall assessment: strong, important contribution with minor fixes; recommendation to accept.
