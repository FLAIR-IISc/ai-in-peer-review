- Identifies propensity overfitting in the standard counterfactual risk estimator for BLBF, causing poor generalization even with well-behaved losses.
- Shows that existing fixes like thresholding are insufficient through a compelling motivating example.
- Proposes a self-normalized importance-sampling estimator and the Norm-POEM algorithm to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Grounds the estimator in importance sampling theory and provides theoretical consistency guarantees.
- Presents Norm-POEM using linear rules and a CRF-like hypothesis space with clear algorithmic presentation.
- Empirically demonstrates consistent improvements over POEM on multi-label classification benchmarks.
- Includes thorough experiments, ablation studies, and analyses (Tables 2–3) that explain how Norm-POEM avoids overfitting.
- Strengths: novel identification of a subtle flaw, rigorous analysis/proofs, clear writing, and publicly available code.
- Weakness: theoretical analysis lacks detailed finite-sample/statistical characterizations beyond asymptotic consistency.
- Weakness: experiments are concentrated on multi-label classification; applicability to other settings (e.g., continuous-action contextual bandits) is not shown.
- Weakness: computational cost and practical complexity (approximate variance estimate, L-BFGS use) are not analyzed in depth.
- Weakness: relation to broader importance sampling literature could be discussed more nuancedly to clarify extensions/deviations.
- Observation: variance regularization (λ) still helps with the self-normalized estimator, suggesting propensity overfitting may be reduced but not fully eliminated.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand empirical scope, deepen theoretical/statistical analysis, and discuss computational costs.
