Summary
This paper identifies and analyzes “propensity overfitting” when using inverse propensity scoring (IPS) within Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) for batch learning from logged bandit feedback. The authors propose replacing the conventional IPS estimator with a self-normalized (SN) importance sampling estimator, yielding a bounded, translation-equivariant risk estimate that avoids the anomalous behavior of IPS. They instantiate this idea in Norm-POEM, which uses the SN estimator together with a CRM-style variance regularizer, derive a practical variance approximation for the ratio estimator, and evaluate on multi-label classification tasks. Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently improves over POEM across datasets and is robust to translations of the loss.

Relation to prior work
The paper builds directly on CRM [1], which advocated variance-regularized IPS with clipping. It connects to classic work on self-normalized IS and multiplicative control variates, and complements doubly robust/weighted estimators from off-policy evaluation. The contribution is a focused, well-motivated replacement of the estimator used within CRM, along with an analysis showing why SN-IS is a better fit in BLBF due to propensity overfitting.

Technical questions and clarifications
- Normalization of δ in Norm-POEM (Eqs. (8) and (3)) and “δ > 0” in experiments:
  • The CRM bound and objective in [1] assume a bounded loss, often δ ∈ [−1,0] after a sign flip/shift; this is used to control variance in the empirical Bernstein argument. Norm-POEM’s SN estimator and its variance approximation (Eq. (8)) do not require δ to be negative, only bounded. If the CRM-style variance regularizer (Eq. (3)) is invoked as a generalization bound, δ should be scaled into a fixed bounded range (e.g., map Hamming loss to [0,1] and optionally negate to [−1,0]) to match assumptions. If used purely as a regularizer, any bounded δ is acceptable.
  • In Table 1 and related experiments, “δ > 0” refers to using the standard nonnegative Hamming loss (no sign flip). The paper later studies translated losses (additive shifts) to demonstrate SN’s translation equivariance; the “positive δ” condition indicates the original, nonnegated loss convention.
- Modeling δ(x,y) as a random variable:
  • The paper partly acknowledges noise via “set δ(x,y) = E[δ | x,y]”, but it is cleaner to model δ as stochastic. The true risk should be written as R(h) = E_{x∼Pr(X)} E_{y∼h(y|x)} E_{δ|x,y}[δ], with expectations over label/noise processes explicit. Estimators then target this nested expectation, and assumptions about boundedness apply to δ’s support or moments.
- On propensity overfitting:
  • The paper convincingly shows IPS can minimize the sum of importance weights Ŝ(h) rather than align with low-loss examples, causing degenerate ERM solutions. Using Ŝ(h) as a multiplicative control variate (SN-IS) directly addresses this failure mode by bounding and making the estimator equivariant, which aligns the ERM landscape with the intended loss structure.

Strengths
- Clear identification and explanation of propensity overfitting in IPS+ERM, with an illustrative example and empirical diagnostics (Ŝ(h)).
- Well-justified use of the self-normalized estimator as a multiplicative control variate that restores boundedness and translation equivariance, and is strongly consistent.
- Practical variance approximation and integration into a POEM-like learning pipeline (Norm-POEM).
- Empirical results show consistent and notable gains over POEM, and robustness to loss translations—an effect predicted by the theory.
- Conceptually simple change with high practical impact on an important problem (BLBF).

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Theory is primarily about replacing the estimator; the generalization bound is inherited in spirit from [1] but the precise conditions for the CRM-regularized objective with SN-IS (including clipping) could be stated more explicitly. Clarify when δ must be rescaled to apply the same bound.
- Optimization details: Eq. (8) leads to nonconvex objectives without stochastic optimization; while empirically fine, a brief discussion of convergence guarantees or practical heuristics (e.g., line search, initialization sensitivity) would help reproducibility.
- Ablations beyond clipping M and λ (e.g., effect of scale/shift of δ, or comparing to doubly robust baselines) would further strengthen claims.

Specific corrections and notation
- Lines 170–171: The importance ratio should be h(y|x) / h0(y|x), not the inverse. Also, the induced nonlinearity in the CRM objective due to clipping/variance regularization was already noted in [1]; please credit this prior observation.
- Line 276: Use argmin (not argmax) because the learning objective minimizes risk plus variance penalty.
- Line 319: Replace overbars with hats to be consistent with earlier notation for empirical estimates (e.g., R̂, V̂ar).
- Risk definition (Sec. 3): Prefer R(h) = E_{x} E_{y∼h(y|x)} E_{δ|x,y}[δ] to make the label/noise expectation explicit.
- δ scaling: State explicitly whether, in Norm-POEM’s objective, δ is rescaled to [−1,0] (as assumed in the CRM bound) or kept as the native Hamming loss in [0,1]. Clarify that “δ > 0” in Table 1 means using the native, nonnegated Hamming loss.

Pros for acceptance
- Interesting and timely follow-up to [1] with a crisp insight (propensity overfitting) and an effective remedy (SN-IS).
- Strong conceptual link between diagnostics (Ŝ(h)) and the estimator design.
- Substantial empirical gains and robustness on realistic multi-label benchmarks.
- Practical method with minimal changes to existing pipelines.

Cons for acceptance
- Theoretical treatment around the CRM bound with SN-IS could be tightened, especially regarding δ scaling and conditions for variance control.
- Limited comparison to alternative variance-reduced or doubly robust estimators in learning (not just evaluation).

Overall evaluation
A solid, well-argued paper that diagnoses a real shortcoming of IPS-based CRM and proposes a principled, effective fix via a multiplicative control variate. The analysis is insightful, and the empirical improvement over POEM is clear. I recommend acceptance after making the noted clarifications and minor corrections.
