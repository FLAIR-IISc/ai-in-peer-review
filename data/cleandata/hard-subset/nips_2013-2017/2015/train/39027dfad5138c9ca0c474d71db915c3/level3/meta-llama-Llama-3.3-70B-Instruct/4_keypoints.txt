- Introduces a hybrid 2D-3D ConvNet with a recursive training framework where a 2D preliminary boundary map is refined by a 3D ConvNet.  
- Leveraging 3D context via the recursive design yields significant improvements in neuronal boundary detection accuracy over prior 2D-only methods.  
- Implementation uses ZNN to efficiently train very deep 3D ConvNets, enabling exploration of novel architectures.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, methodology, and results.  
- Authors provide a biologically inspired rationale based on interactions between primary and higher visual cortical areas.  
- Quantitative results show reduced pixel-wise classification error and improved Rand scores compared to previous approaches.  
- Qualitative results demonstrate fewer false detections and more accurate boundary delineation.  
- Approach is broadly applicable to other anisotropic 3D images (e.g., video) and general image labeling problems.  
- Minor issues: Table 1’s "delta > 0" should be rescaled to [-1,0] consistent with CRM.  
- Minor issues: feedback in "batch learning from logged bandit feedback" should be modeled as a random variable to account for noisy labeling.  
- Minor issues: equation (1) should include an additional expectation over the randomness of delta; notation inconsistencies and a typo appear in the optimization problem.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — novel, well-motivated, significant empirical improvements with manageable minor corrections.
