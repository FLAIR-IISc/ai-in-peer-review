- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Identifies anomalous behavior of the standard unbiased importance sampling estimator (Eq. 2) in empirical risk minimization.  
- Shows Eq. 2 yields severely biased risk estimates for complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (Eq. 7) using multiplicative control variates.  
- Proves the self-normalized estimator avoids degeneracies while preserving strong consistency.  
- Develops Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on the proposed estimator.  
- Empirical results show improvements of Norm-POEM over conventional POEM on multiple multi-label datasets.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and the paper builds sensibly on prior work.  
- Experiments are well-designed and the paper is clearly and accessibly written.  
- Significance is uncertain due to experiments being limited to simulated bandit feedback on standard supervised datasets.  
- Lacks real-world application experiments and deeper analysis of practical translation of gains.  
- Missing variance reporting across repeated runs (e.g., standard deviations) and limited discussion of optimization dynamics.  
- Does not sufficiently explore scaling to more complex hypothesis spaces or larger-scale applications.  
- Novelty is incremental rather than introducing a fundamentally new paradigm.  
- Overall: technically sound and well-motivated but impact weakened by limited experimental scope and real-world validation.
