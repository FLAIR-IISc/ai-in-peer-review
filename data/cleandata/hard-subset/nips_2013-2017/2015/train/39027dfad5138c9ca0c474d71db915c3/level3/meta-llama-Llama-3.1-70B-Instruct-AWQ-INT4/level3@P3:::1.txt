This paper identifies a severe problem in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and proposes an alternative estimator to mitigate it. The authors focus on the issue of "propensity overfitting," which arises when the capacity of the hypothesis space allows overfitting of the propensity weights. This problem is distinct from the large importance-weight variance typically associated with importance sampling.

The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the BLBF setting and the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle. They also offer a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem, including its causes and consequences. The proposed self-normalized estimator is well-motivated and effectively addresses the issues with the conventional unbiased risk estimator.

The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise. The authors provide a detailed explanation of the related work and clearly differentiate their contribution from existing research. The experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach in various settings.

One potential area for improvement is the explanation of the relationship between large weight variance and propensity overfitting. While the authors mention that these issues are related, a clearer explanation of how they are connected would be beneficial.

I also have some confusion about line 199, where the behavior of \hat R(h^) seems unclear when n is small relative to k. A more detailed explanation or clarification of this point would be helpful.

Furthermore, I question the claim that prior BLBF works used an unbiased counterfactual risk estimator. It is possible that some of these works used clipping, which introduces bias. A more nuanced discussion of the trade-offs between bias and variance in these estimators would be valuable.

Despite these minor issues, I appreciate the authors' effort to provide code for their proposed method. This will undoubtedly facilitate the reproduction of their results and the adoption of their approach by other researchers.

Overall, I recommend accepting this paper despite not having fully checked its technical soundness due to time constraints. The proposed idea is sensible, and the experimental evaluation is convincing. The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a clear effort to explain their contribution and its significance.
