- Summary: Proposes Norm-POEM, replacing IPS with self-normalized importance sampling in CRM for structured prediction to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Motivation: Connects POEM/CRM to SN importance weighting and off-policy evaluation literature (Monte Carlo, RL).  
- Strength: Timely and relevant to off-policy learning and multi-label prediction research.  
- Strength: Using a self-normalized estimator in CRM is sensible, yielding bounded and translation-equivariant empirical risks and improved robustness.  
- Strength: Experiments are careful and on multiple multi-label datasets; Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM and nears full-information CRF.  
- Weakness: Discussion and notation around Eq. (2) are misleading—averaged loss description and expectation/translation claims are imprecise.  
- Weakness: Claim that Eq. (2) shows an “anomaly” (non-invariance to translation) is technically incorrect; finite-sample non-equivariance stems from normalization mismatch.  
- Weakness: Example 1 depends on the above misunderstanding and does not hold as stated.  
- Weakness: The estimator in (2) is highly sensitive to small or zero logging propensities π; practical instability requires explicit clipping/safeguards.  
- Suggestion: Eq. (7) with double normalization is preferable; clarify conditions (support, clipping, finite-sample behavior) for consistency and variance results.  
- Quality: Empirically strong, but theoretical framing and expectations/normalization need greater precision.  
- Clarity: Generally well written, but must correct the discussion around (2) and Example 1.  
- Originality: Thoughtful specialization of known self-normalized estimators to CRM/BLBF with a new algorithmic instantiation.  
- Significance: Likely to influence counterfactual learning practice.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear empirical gains, principled variance reduction, and relevance to NeurIPS topics.  
- Cons: misleading exposition around key equations and insufficient treatment of propensity denominator safeguards.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, contingent on revising the commentary, examples, and clarifying support/clipping/finite-sample claims.
