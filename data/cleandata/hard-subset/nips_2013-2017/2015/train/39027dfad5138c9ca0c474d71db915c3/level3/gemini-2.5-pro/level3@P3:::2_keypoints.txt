- Paper identifies "propensity overfitting" in counterfactual learning from logged bandit feedback and builds on CRM/POEM to address it.  
- Main proposal: replace IPS with a self-normalized importance sampling estimator, yielding the Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Provides theoretical motivation for the self-normalized estimator and derives Norm-POEM via CRM principles.  
- Empirical results: extensive experiments showing Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM and approaches fully-supervised CRF performance.  
- Diagnostic experiments demonstrate Norm-POEM avoids the identified overfitting pathology and is robust to loss transformations.  
- Strength: novel and significant identification of a practical failure mode (propensity overfitting) relevant to real-world systems.  
- Strength: technically well-motivated estimator with desirable properties (equivariance to loss translations, bounded estimates).  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation supporting claims across datasets.  
- Weakness: misleading/imprecise commentary around Eq.(2) — confuses an estimator with its expectation and misstates unbiasedness.  
- Weakness: framing the IPS non-invariance to loss translation as an "anomaly" is questionable; it is a finite-sample property and should be framed as an ERM vulnerability.  
- Weakness: Example 1 is compelling but motivated by the imprecise "anomaly" framing and should be re-motivated focusing on practical consequences for ERM.  
- Weakness/suggestion: emphasize more directly that self-normalization improves robustness to tiny/zero propensities by stabilizing the denominator.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revisions to clarify IPS properties, reframe the "anomaly" claims, and tighten the motivation for Example 1.
