- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), showing standard counterfactual risk estimators can be severely biased.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator that avoids propensity overfitting and yields the Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Builds on prior BLBF work (Swaminathan & Joachims 2015) and situates contributions within causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation.  
- Correctly identifies limitations of importance-sampling estimators and connects to propensity score matching and doubly robust methods.  
- Theorem 2 establishes strong consistency of the self-normalized estimator, providing solid theoretical justification.  
- Provides an insightful explanation and illustrative example of why conventional estimators fail due to propensity overfitting.  
- Novel application of self-normalization to BLBF, producing a meaningful refinement of the POEM framework.  
- Empirically demonstrates Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple datasets using a rigorous Supervised→Bandit evaluation.  
- Ablation study and runtime comparisons strengthen empirical claims; Norm-POEM shows faster training despite variance estimator costs.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and accessible, with good notation and helpful examples.  
- Weakness: increased computational complexity of the variance estimator needs more detailed analysis (e.g., relation to dataset size and feature dimensionality).  
- Weakness: limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly for λ and M.  
- Weakness: connections to more recent variance reduction techniques could be more explicitly drawn.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important overlooked problem, theoretically sound and empirically validated solution, clear writing, practical algorithm with adoption potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: variance-estimation complexity and insufficient hyperparameter-sensitivity analysis may hinder adoption.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution likely to have lasting impact; strong recommendation for acceptance.
