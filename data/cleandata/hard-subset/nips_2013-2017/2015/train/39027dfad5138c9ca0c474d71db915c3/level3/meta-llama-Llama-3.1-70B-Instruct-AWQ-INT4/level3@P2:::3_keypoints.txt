- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), an important practical problem.  
- Identifies propensity overfitting as a severe flaw in the conventional counterfactual risk estimator.  
- Proposes a novel self-normalized risk estimator to avoid propensity overfitting.  
- The self-normalized estimator is robust to loss translation and shown to outperform the conventional estimator.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and well-defined notation.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis supporting the proposed estimator.  
- Empirical evaluations on several multi-label classification tasks show consistent improvements.  
- Main contribution is the self-normalizing risk estimator, a novel follow-up to prior work.  
- Significance uncertain because experiments use simulated bandit feedback and may not generalize to real-world settings.  
- Results lack variance reporting across repeated runs, hindering assessment of reliability.  
- Recommends more extensive experiments including real-world datasets and additional applications.  
- Authors should provide more details on optimization, hyperparameter choices, and convergence behavior.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, well-motivated, and promising work that warrants further investigation.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
