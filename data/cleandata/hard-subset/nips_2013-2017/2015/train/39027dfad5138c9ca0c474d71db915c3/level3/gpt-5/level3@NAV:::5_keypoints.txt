- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback by proposing a self-normalized counterfactual risk estimator and Norm-POEM for structured prediction.
- Builds on Swaminathan & Joachims (2015) and relates to classical importance-weight self-normalization and variance-reduction/clipping/doubly-robust work.
- Argues self-normalization fixes IPS’s nonequivariant, unbounded behavior, proves strong consistency, and integrates an approximate variance estimate into the CRM objective.
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on supervised→bandit multi-label benchmarks, is robust to loss translations, and converges faster.
- Technical quality: generally sound; compelling diagnosis and appropriate estimator, with careful empirical validation across datasets and hyperparameter tuning.
- Theoretical limitations: no finite-sample generalization bound for CRM with the ratio estimator and the variance plug-in is asymptotic.
- Baseline limitations: comparisons omit strong modern baselines like doubly robust learners, clipped-IPS, and high-confidence OPE methods.
- Experimental scope: limited to supervised→bandit multi-label tasks with no evaluation on real logged datasets.
- Clarity: paper is clear and well organized; exposition of anomalies is pedagogical.
- Suggested clarity improvements: explicitly separate bias sources from clipping vs. normalization and provide pseudo-code for Norm-POEM for reproducibility.
- Originality: high — novel use of self-normalized IS to remedy propensity overfitting within CRM and meaningful extension of POEM.
- Significance: strong — likely to be adopted in BLBF/off-policy pipelines with notable runtime and stability benefits.
- Recommendation: accept, but strengthen by adding finite-sample analysis for the ratio estimator and comparisons to broader, stronger baselines (and real logged data).
