- Proposes a novel self-normalized risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Identifies severe degeneracies and overfitting in conventional counterfactual risk estimators used in prior BLBF work.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written, with a thorough BLBF background and limitations of existing methods.  
- Technical contribution: thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and a novel estimator-based solution.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM as the learning algorithm implementing the self-normalized estimator.  
- Theoretical results are supported by empirical evaluations on several multi-label classification problems.  
- Experiments are well-designed and results are clearly presented.  
- Strength: addresses a specific BLBF problem and demonstrably improves upon existing approaches.  
- Limitation: more experiments and deeper analysis of the training-objective optimization are needed.  
- Limitation: unclear how experimental gains translate to real-world applications (significance uncertain).  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, significant technical contributions, and supportive empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental depth and unclear real-world impact.  
- Recommendations: add more experiments and deeper optimization analysis, clarify real-world applicability, and provide broader BLBF background for accessibility.
