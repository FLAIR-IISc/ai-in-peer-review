- Addresses overfitting in learning from logged bandit feedback due to instability of standard counterfactual risk estimators.  
- Identifies a new failure mode, "Propensity Overfitting," where estimators exploit historical policy sampling probabilities.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator motivated by control variates and the Norm-POEM learning algorithm.  
- Theoretical claims: estimator is consistent and offers improved variance control.  
- Empirical claim: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms existing methods on multi-label classification benchmarks.  
- Builds on CRM framework and extensive prior work in importance sampling and off-policy evaluation.  
- Clearly contrasts Propensity Overfitting with traditional Loss Overfitting in supervised learning.  
- Strengths: rigorous technical analysis, strong theoretical grounding, and compelling empirical validation.  
- Provides diagnostic analysis confirming reduced Propensity Overfitting and makes code available.  
- Weakness: computational cost of the self-normalized estimator and reliance on L-BFGS may hurt scalability.  
- Weakness: connection to doubly-robust estimators is not fully explored.  
- Weakness: limited sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (M and λ) and focus mostly on multi-label classification.  
- Overall: strong paper with significant contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the noted concerns.
