- Problem: learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and failure mode of IPS called "propensity overfitting" distinct from high-variance importance weights.  
- Propensity overfitting: hypotheses can exploit logging probabilities to achieve artificially low empirical risk (e.g., upweight logged low-loss actions or assign prob to unlogged actions to avoid positive losses).  
- Solution: replace IPS with a self-normalized (ratio) estimator that is shift-equivariant and bounded by observed losses, directly countering propensity overfitting.  
- Algorithm: introduce Norm-POEM, instantiating the self-normalized estimator in a CRM framework.  
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM substantially and consistently outperforms strong baseline POEM (clipped IPS) on multiple multi-label datasets.  
- Evidence: Table 2 and analysis of summed propensity weights Ŝ(ĥ) support that the method fixes the targeted pathology.  
- Strength: clearly identifies and names an underappreciated pathology, with a principled connection to control variates.  
- Strength: elegant, well-motivated estimator and reproducible code release.  
- Weakness: need clearer explanation of the relationship between propensity overfitting and high-variance weights—can they occur independently?  
- Weakness: clarify why CRM’s variance-regularization (Eq.3) fails to prevent the hoverfit pathology despite expected high empirical variance.  
- Weakness: paper should more directly argue why clipping (biased IPS) is insufficient specifically for preventing propensity overfitting.  
- Recommendation: interesting, well-executed, and practically significant paper; recommend acceptance.
