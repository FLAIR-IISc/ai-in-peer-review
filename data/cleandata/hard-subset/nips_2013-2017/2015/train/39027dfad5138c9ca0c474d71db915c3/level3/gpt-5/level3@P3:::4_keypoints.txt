- Paper identifies "propensity overfitting" in IPS+ERM for CRM and proposes using a self-normalized (SN) importance sampling estimator (Norm-POEM) to fix it.
- SN estimator yields a bounded, translation-equivariant risk estimate that avoids IPS’s anomalous behavior.
- Norm-POEM combines SN-IS with a CRM-style variance regularizer and a practical variance approximation for the ratio estimator.
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multi-label benchmarks and is robust to loss translations.
- Builds on CRM [1], connects to classic self-normalized IS and multiplicative control variates, and complements doubly robust/weighted estimators.
- Clarify δ normalization: SN-IS and its variance approximation only require bounded δ, but CRM generalization bounds assume δ scaled (e.g., to [−1,0]).
- In experiments, “δ > 0” denotes using the native nonnegative Hamming loss; translations are later used to show SN equivariance.
- Recommend modeling δ as stochastic and write risk as R(h)=E_x E_{y∼h} E_{δ|x,y}[δ] so estimators target the nested expectation and boundedness assumptions are clear.
- Propensity overfitting diagnosis: IPS can reduce importance-weight sum Ŝ(h) leading to degenerate solutions; SN-IS acts as a multiplicative control variate to prevent this.
- Strength: clear identification and diagnostic (Ŝ(h)) demonstrating the failure mode of IPS+ERM.
- Strength: well-justified use of SN-IS that restores boundedness, translation equivariance, and maintains consistency.
- Strength: practical variance approximation and seamless integration into a POEM-like pipeline.
- Strength: consistent empirical gains and predicted robustness to loss translations.
- Strength: conceptually simple change with substantial practical impact for BLBF.
- Weakness: theoretical treatment is mainly an estimator replacement; need explicit conditions for CRM-regularized objective with SN-IS (including clipping) and when δ must be rescaled.
- Weakness: optimization objective from Eq. (8) is nonconvex; add discussion of convergence guarantees or practical heuristics (line search, initialization).
- Weakness: additional ablations suggested (effects of δ scale/shift, comparisons to doubly robust baselines) to strengthen claims.
- Correction: importance ratio should be h(y|x)/h0(y|x) (not inverse); credit [1] for noting induced nonlinearity from clipping/variance regularization.
- Correction: use argmin (not argmax) for the learning objective at line 276.
- Correction: replace overbars with hats for empirical estimates (e.g., R̂, V̂ar) for consistency.
- Correction: explicitly define risk as R(h)=E_x E_{y∼h} E_{δ|x,y}[δ] to make label/noise expectation explicit.
- Correction: state whether δ in Norm-POEM is rescaled to [−1,0] for CRM bounds or kept as native [0,1]; clarify “δ > 0” notation in Table 1.
- Pros for acceptance: timely follow-up to CRM with a crisp insight and effective remedy; strong diagnostics-estimator connection; notable empirical gains; minimal changes required for practice.
- Cons for acceptance: need tighter theoretical treatment of CRM bounds with SN-IS and broader comparisons to other variance-reduced/doubly robust learning estimators.

- Overall recommendation: accept after addressing clarifications and minor corrections.
