- Identifies a novel failure mode—propensity overfitting—where IPS-based objectives exploit logging probabilities instead of true loss structure.  
- Shows pathologies from IPS (empirical risks outside loss range, non-equivariance to affine loss transforms).  
- Proposes using a multiplicative control variate: the self-normalized IPS estimator (SNIPS) to stabilize importance weights.  
- Proves strong consistency of the self-normalized risk estimator and derives an approximate variance estimator for CRM regularization.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a CRM algorithm for structured prediction using SNIPS and a variance regularizer.  
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multilabel datasets and data sizes.  
- Diagnostics confirm Ŝ(h) ≈ 1 with normalization, indicating desired weight stabilization.  
- The fix is bounded and equivariant to affine loss transformations while preserving consistency.  
- Builds on CRM, importance weighting, and off-policy evaluation literature; distinguishes multiplicative self-normalization from clipping and additive control variates.  
- Code is publicly available and runtime is reasonable for the reported experiments.  
- Theoretical development stops at strong consistency; lacks a new high-probability generalization bound for SNIPS+CRM.  
- Empirical comparisons omit important baselines (doubly-robust, SWITCH-DR, carefully tuned weight clipping).  
- Experiments use supervised→bandit simulation on multilabel tasks; no real logged-data or large-action-space contextual bandit results.  
- Sensitivity analysis for clipping parameter M and variance regularization λ is limited; more practitioner guidance is needed.  
- Optimization is non-convex and uses full-batch L-BFGS; scalability and stochastic optimization alternatives are not fully discussed.  
- Writing and organization are clear; propensity overfitting examples and discussion of equivariance/boundedness are compelling.  
- Originality: applying classical self-normalization to CRM, naming/diagnosing propensity overfitting, and deriving Norm-POEM is novel and timely.  
- Significance: addresses an important BLBF failure mode with a simple, widely applicable solution likely to influence practice.  
- Key requested additions: a high-probability generalization bound for SNIPS, comparisons to DR/SWITCH estimators, sensitivity plots for M and λ, and scalability/stochastic optimization discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept — solid, impactful contribution despite gaps in theory, baselines, and real-data validation.  
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
