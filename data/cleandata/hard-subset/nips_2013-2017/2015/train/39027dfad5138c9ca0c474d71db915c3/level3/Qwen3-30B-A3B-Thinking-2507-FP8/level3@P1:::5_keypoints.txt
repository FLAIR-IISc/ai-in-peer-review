- Identifies a critical flaw in IPS-based counterfactual risk estimation called "propensity overfitting" that yields arbitrarily biased risk estimates in BLBF/ERM.  
- Propensity overfitting is a novel phenomenon distinct from standard supervised overfitting; risk estimates can fall outside the loss function's natural range.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates and introduces the Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Proves the self-normalized estimator is equivariant and its risk estimates remain bounded within the loss range.  
- Shows the estimator avoids propensity overfitting by construction, with empirical Ŝ(h) staying close to the expected value 1.  
- Provides a concise consistency proof (Theorem 2) and rigorous theoretical treatment of the problem.  
- Empirical results on Scene, Yeast, TMC, and LYRL demonstrate Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across training sizes and losses.  
- Norm-POEM avoids the bias seen in POEM and converges faster despite the normalization step.  
- Experimental setup is carefully designed to isolate estimator effects; empirical validation is comprehensive and convincing.  
- Writing and presentation are clear, well-structured, and accessible with appropriate notation.  
- Contribution is original: extends CRM principles and uncovers a previously unrecognized vulnerability in BLBF methods.  
- High practical significance for real-world BLBF applications (ads, recommendations) and immediate applicability to existing systems.  
- Minor weakness: limited analysis of theoretical limitations or conditions under which the self-normalized estimator might fail.  
- Acceptance pros: identifies/solves a critical flaw, rigorous theory, strong experiments, faster and more robust algorithm, clear presentation.  
- Acceptance cons: relatively narrow focus on a specific estimator in BLBF and limited discussion of theoretical boundaries.
