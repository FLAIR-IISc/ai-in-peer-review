- Identifies "propensity overfitting" as a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator in batch learning from logged bandit feedback.  
- Proposes the self-normalized estimator, a multiplicative control variate, to avoid propensity overfitting and improve generalization.  
- Proves desirable properties of the estimator: equivariance (invariance to loss translations) and strong consistency (convergence to true risk).  
- Derives Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm that applies the self-normalized estimator within the CRM framework.  
- Demonstrates through extensive experiments on multi-label classification that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional estimator.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of limitations of the conventional estimator and clear, concise technical exposition.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses consistent notation overall.  
- Main contribution (self-normalized estimator) is novel and significant for BLBF research.  
- Weakness: assumes deterministic loss functions instead of modeling stochastic label noise; should consider expectation over loss randomness.  
- Weakness: some notation choices could be improved (e.g., hats vs. bars) to match conventions.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion of Norm-POEM’s computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.  
- Reviewer recommendation: recommend acceptance; scores — Quality: 6, Clarity: 6, Originality: 8, Significance: 8.
