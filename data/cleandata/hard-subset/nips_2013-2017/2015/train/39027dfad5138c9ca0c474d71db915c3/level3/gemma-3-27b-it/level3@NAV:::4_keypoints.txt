- Paper tackles propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and proposes a self-normalized estimator as a remedy.
- Authors demonstrate that the standard counterfactual risk estimator can produce severely biased risk estimates, especially with complex hypothesis spaces.
- The work is well-positioned with respect to prior literature in causal inference, off-policy evaluation, and counterfactual learning.
- Core strength: insightful analysis of propensity overfitting and an elegant self-normalized estimator solution.
- Examples (e.g., Example 1) effectively illustrate the propensity overfitting issue.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and consistently shows Norm-POEM outperforming POEM across multiple datasets.
- Ablation study (Table 3) shows variance regularization remains necessary even with the self-normalized estimator.
- Clarification needed: how delta is rescaled to [-1,0] in equation (8).
- Clarification needed: what “delta > 0” denotes in Table 1 experiments.
- Minor corrections requested: fix h(y|x)/h0(y|x) at lines 170–171, use argmin instead of argmax at line 276, and change bars to hats at line 319.
- Theoretical concern: stronger argument needed for why modeling delta(x,y) as deterministic is problematic and why it should be treated as a random variable (expectation over label noise).
- Suggestion to include an expectation over delta in the true risk formulation to strengthen theoretical foundations.
- Prior work noting nonlinearity is mentioned but not fully contextualized—more discussion of its relevance is recommended.
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful paper with solid theory and empirical support; minor clarifications and theoretical strengthening would improve it.
