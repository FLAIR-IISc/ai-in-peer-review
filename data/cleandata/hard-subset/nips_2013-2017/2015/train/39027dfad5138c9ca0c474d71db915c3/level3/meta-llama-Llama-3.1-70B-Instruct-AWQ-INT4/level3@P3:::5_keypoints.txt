- Proposes a novel method for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) addressing propensity overfitting.  
- Identifies propensity overfitting as a severe issue with conventional counterfactual risk estimators.  
- Introduces a self-normalized risk estimator that avoids propensity overfitting and is well-justified.  
- Develops Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm for structured output prediction based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Norm-POEM is a natural extension of POEM and shows clear advantages over the original algorithm.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis of the estimator’s properties and implications for BLBF.  
- Balances theoretical analysis with empirical evaluation effectively.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and results are convincing, especially comparisons to POEM.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- Research quality and originality are high; the method is a significant improvement with potential wide adoption.  
- Assumes a stationary historical policy, which may not hold in practice.  
- Lacks thorough analysis of computational complexity for large-scale applications.  
- Experimental validation is limited to a few datasets, so generalization to other datasets is uncertain.
