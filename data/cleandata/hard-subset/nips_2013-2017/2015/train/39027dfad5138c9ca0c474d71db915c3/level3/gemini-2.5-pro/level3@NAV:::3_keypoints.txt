- Problem: Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) for improving interactive systems using historical data.
- Pathology identified: "Propensity overfitting"—models exploit propensity weights to minimize counterfactual risk instead of fitting true loss.
- Solution: Replace unbiased risk estimator with a self-normalized estimator using the sum of importance weights as a multiplicative control variate.
- Theoretical contribution: Self-normalized estimator is theoretically sound and addresses equivariance and unboundedness issues.
- Clarity and writing: Paper is high-quality, well-written, and makes a complex topic accessible.
- Motivation and intuition: Excellent motivation and a clear, simple example that illustrates propensity overfitting.
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms baseline POEM on benchmark datasets.
- Originality concern: Contribution is largely incremental—applying a known importance-sampling technique to CRM.
- Significance concern: Practical significance of empirical gains is uncertain for large-scale, complex real-world settings.
- Experimental limitations: Experiments reuse multi-label-to-bandit conversion from prior work; broader dataset variety is lacking.
- Suggested experiment improvements: Report variance/standard deviation across runs and include additional, real-world logged bandit datasets.
- Optimization analysis: A brief analysis of the non-convex training objective and why L-BFGS works would strengthen the paper.
