- Identifies propensity overfitting as a critical flaw in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF.
- Proposes a novel self-normalized estimator that avoids propensity overfitting.
- Develops Norm-POEM by building on Swaminathan & Joachims (2015), replacing the unbiased estimator with a self-normalized version.
- Theoretically shows the self-normalized estimator resolves non-equivariance and unbounded risk estimates while preserving strong consistency.
- Research quality is excellent with rigorous theoretical analysis linking the estimator to avoidance of propensity overfitting.
- Provides extensive empirical validation across multiple multi-label classification datasets supporting the theoretical claims.
- Paper is exceptionally well written and easy to follow, with clear problem statement, solution, and experiments.
- Originality is significant: the self-normalized estimator is a fundamentally new approach in counterfactual learning.
- Significance is high: Norm-POEM outperforms existing methods and offers practical computational advantages.
- Norm-POEM trains faster than POEM despite added regularization, making it practical for real-world use.
- Results are compelling and consistent across datasets, warranting further research.
- No limitations or weaknesses identified by the reviewer; recommends acceptance.
