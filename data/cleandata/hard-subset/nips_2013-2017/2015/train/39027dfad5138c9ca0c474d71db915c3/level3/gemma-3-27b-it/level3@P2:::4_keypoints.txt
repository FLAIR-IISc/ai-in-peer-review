- Identifies propensity overfitting in counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF and proposes a self-normalized estimator (Norm-POEM) to mitigate it.  
- Builds on Swaminathan & Joachims (ICML 2015) with a compelling theoretical analysis and convincing empirical results.  
- Clearly frames BLBF in the context of causal inference, off-policy evaluation, and domain adaptation.  
- Demonstrates propensity overfitting with an illustrative example showing risk estimates can be arbitrarily manipulated via propensity weights.  
- Introduces a multiplicative control variate (self-normalized estimator) that yields more stable and equivariant risk estimates.  
- Theoretical work is sound, connecting to importance sampling and variance-reduction techniques.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, using standard benchmarks and consistently showing Norm-POEM outperforming POEM across datasets.  
- Ablation study shows continued benefits of variance regularization and computational efficiency gains.  
- Needs clarification on whether Norm-POEM assumes δ(x,y) ∈ [−1,0] and how “δ > 0” reported losses are interpreted/handled.  
- Recommends modeling δ(x,y) as a random variable and including expectation over label noise in the true risk formulation.  
- Minor technical corrections: ratio should be h(y|x)/h0(y|x) (lines 170–171); “argmax” → “argmin” (line 276); replace bars with hats for consistency (line 319).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper, significant contribution; scores Quality 6, Clarity 6, Originality 8, Significance 8; recommendation: Accept.
