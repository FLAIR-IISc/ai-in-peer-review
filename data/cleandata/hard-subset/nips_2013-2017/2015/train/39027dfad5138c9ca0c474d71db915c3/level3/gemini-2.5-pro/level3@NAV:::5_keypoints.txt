- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) for improving interactive systems.  
- Identifies "propensity overfitting," where CRM’s unbiased estimator is exploited via propensity weights.  
- Proposes using a self-normalized importance-sampling risk estimator to mitigate this issue.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new algorithm based on POEM that uses the self-normalized estimator.  
- Theoretical guarantees: the proposed estimator is equivariant and bounded, addressing the flaw.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation shows Norm-POEM is more robust and outperforms the baseline on benchmarks.  
- Surprisingly often computationally faster than original POEM despite a more complex objective.  
- Original contribution: novel application of self-normalized estimator within CRM to resolve propensity overfitting.  
- Overall quality: technically sound, well-written, claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Minor suggestion: expand on the choice of L-BFGS for optimizing the non-convex objective.  
- Final verdict: strong recommendation for acceptance.
