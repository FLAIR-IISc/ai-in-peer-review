This paper proposes an alternative estimator for counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a severe problem with the conventional unbiased estimator used in prior works on BLBF, which they term "propensity overfitting". This occurs when a hypothesis gains stronger support from the logged data via importance weights, leading to overfitting. The proposed solution is a multiplicative control variate that leads to the self-normalized risk estimator. This estimator is shown to preserve consistency and control variance, and empirical results demonstrate that the normalized CRM outperforms the non-normalized variant.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the problem of propensity overfitting and the proposed solution. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the conventional estimator and its limitations, and the theoretical results are sound. The empirical evaluation is extensive and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The main contributions of the paper are the identification of the propensity overfitting problem and the proposal of the self-normalized estimator as a solution. The authors also derive a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on the CRM principle using the new estimator.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the background and related work, and the notation is consistent throughout the paper. The experimental setup is well-described, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach. While they mention that the per-iteration cost of optimizing the Norm-POEM objective has the same complexity as the per-iteration cost of POEM with L-BFGS, they do not provide a detailed breakdown of the computational costs.

Overall, I believe that this is a good paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of batch learning from logged bandit feedback. The authors identify a important problem and propose a effective solution, and the empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper identifies a important problem with the conventional unbiased estimator used in prior works on BLBF and proposes a effective solution.
* The theoretical results are sound and demonstrate the consistency and variance control of the proposed estimator.
* The empirical evaluation is extensive and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the problem and the proposed solution.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach.
* The paper may benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the proposed approach for practical applications.
