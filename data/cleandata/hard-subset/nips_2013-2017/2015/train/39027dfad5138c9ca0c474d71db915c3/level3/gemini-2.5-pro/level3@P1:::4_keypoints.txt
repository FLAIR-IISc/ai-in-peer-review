- Identifies and articulates a new failure mode in BLBF called "propensity overfitting," where hypotheses exploit IPS propensity weights instead of true losses.
- Shows the standard unbiased IPS estimator can give arbitrarily poor risk estimates for complex hypothesis spaces.
- Proposes a principled fix: replace IPS with a self-normalized estimator derived from multiplicative control variates.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, the algorithm using the self-normalized estimator.
- Provides strong empirical evidence (Supervised-to-Bandit protocol) that Norm-POEM generalizes better and is more robust than POEM.
- Demonstrates Norm-POEM is invariant to loss translation and keeps total propensity weights near their expected value, validating the theoretical claims.
- Strength: insightful and significant identification of a subtle but severe failure mode, supported by a clear illustrative example.
- Strength: elegant, well-motivated solution connecting to control variates with desirable theoretical properties (boundedness, shift equivariance).
- Strength: convincing and well-designed experiments that directly verify theoretical improvements.
- Weakness: current formalism treats loss δ(x,y) as deterministic; should model δ as stochastic with an extra expectation for realism.
- Weakness: unclear whether Norm-POEM’s variance regularizer requires losses scaled to [-1,0]; need clarification on loss transformations used in experiments (e.g., "δ > 0").
- Weakness: minor corrections needed—clarify that the minimizer of R̂(h) can change under translation, explicitly define importance weight as h(y|x)/h0(y|x), fix argmax→argmin typo, and unify hat/bar notation for Ŝ. 
- Overall: well-written, impactful follow-up to [1] with minor fixable issues; strong recommend accept with high quality, clarity, originality, and significance scores.
