- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Shows conventional unbiased risk estimators are vulnerable to overfitting, especially with complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator as a remedy for propensity overfitting.  
- Theoretically demonstrates the estimator’s consistency and variance control.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, an algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical results on multi-label classification show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM.  
- Clearly connects to prior work in off-policy evaluation, importance sampling, and self-normalization.  
- Provides illustrative examples that effectively identify and explain the new failure mode.  
- Demonstrates robustness to loss translation and insightful analysis (e.g., Ŝ(h) values in Table 2).  
- Practical implications of variance estimation and challenges with stochastic optimization are underexplored.  
- Lacks a detailed ablation study separating self-normalization and variance regularization effects.  
- Experimental evaluation is somewhat limited in dataset diversity and hypothesis spaces.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution and recommend acceptance with targeted revisions.
