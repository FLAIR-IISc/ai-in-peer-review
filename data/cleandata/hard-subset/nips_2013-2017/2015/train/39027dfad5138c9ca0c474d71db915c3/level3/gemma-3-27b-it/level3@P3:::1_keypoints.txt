- Identifies a novel problem "propensity overfitting" in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and explains its significance.
- Core insight: minimizing empirical importance-weighted risk can overfit propensity scores instead of true loss.
- Situates work within causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation literature with relevant citations.
- Demonstrates limitations of existing unbiased counterfactual risk estimators, showing degeneracy and poor performance in complex hypothesis spaces.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator and derives the Norm-POEM algorithm as a logical solution.
- Provides empirical evaluation showing Norm-POEM’s effectiveness.
- Releases implementation code to support reproducibility and adoption.
- Paper is generally well-written and concepts are clearly explained.
- Unclear relationship between large importance weight variance and propensity overfitting (cause, symptom, or correlation).
- Needs clarification of behavior of R̂(h^) when sample size n is small relative to number of labels k (line 199 concern).
- Claim that prior BLBF works used unbiased counterfactual risk estimators is misleading due to practices like clipping and should be qualified.
- Limited exploration of how variance regularization interacts with the self-normalized estimator; more analysis needed.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem with a novel, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated solution plus available code.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear weight-variance relationship, specific clarification needed, and overstated claims about prior unbiased estimators.
- Overall recommendation: accept, subject to addressing the minor clarifications and qualification of prior-work claims.
