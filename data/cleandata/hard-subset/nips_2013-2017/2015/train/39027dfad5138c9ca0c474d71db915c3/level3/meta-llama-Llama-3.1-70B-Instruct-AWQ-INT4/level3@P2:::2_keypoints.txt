- Proposes a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual learning in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Identifies "propensity overfitting" as a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator that can produce degenerate risk estimates.  
- Introduces a multiplicative control variate to derive the self-normalized risk estimator.  
- Shows the self-normalized estimator avoids anomalies of the conventional estimator and yields the Norm-POEM algorithm for structured output prediction.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear explanation of the conventional estimator's problems.  
- The proposed estimator is intuitive, well-justified, and supported by convincing experimental results.  
- Provides a thorough related-work discussion and clearly differentiates the approach from prior methods.  
- Technical contributions are significant and rigorously analyzed; Theorem 2 establishes strong consistency of the self-normalized estimator.  
- Explains the CRM principle and how it informs the development of Norm-POEM.  
- Notation and commentary around equation (2) are misleading; the claimed "anomaly" (non-invariance to translation of the loss) is technically incorrect.  
- Example 1 is based on the incorrect presumption about (2) and therefore does not hold.  
- The estimator in (2) is sensitive to its denominator π, which can be zero for unseen samples; the modified estimator (7) with a double denominator is preferable.  
- Experimental results show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM across datasets and approaches CRF performance with full multi-label information.  
- Overall well-written, but authors should revise commentary and notation to correct the identified errors.  
- Quality: technically sound with rigorous analysis and convincing experiments.  
- Clarity: generally clear but requires revision on notation/commentary issues.  
- Originality: presents a novel, significant solution to propensity overfitting in BLBF.  
- Significance: well-motivated focus on counterfactual learning; Norm-POEM is an effective solution.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel methodology, strong empirical results, and substantial technical contributions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: misleading claims and example related to equation (2) that must be corrected.  
- Recommendation: Accept, subject to revision to correct the noted errors.
