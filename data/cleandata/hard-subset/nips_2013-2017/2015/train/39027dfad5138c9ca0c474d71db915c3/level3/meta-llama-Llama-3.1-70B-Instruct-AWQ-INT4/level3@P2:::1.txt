This paper proposes an alternative estimator for counterfactual learning in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and presents a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on this estimator. The authors identify a problem with the conventional unbiased risk estimator used in prior works on BLBF, which they term "propensity overfitting." This occurs when the capacity of the hypothesis space allows overfitting of the propensity weights, leading to severely biased risk estimates.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and propose a self-normalized risk estimator that avoids this issue. They show that this estimator is equivariant and always bounded within the range of the loss function, unlike the conventional unbiased estimator. The self-normalized estimator is also strongly consistent and approaches the desired expectation when the sample size is large.

The paper presents an extensive empirical evaluation of the proposed algorithm, Norm-POEM, on several multi-label classification problems. The results show that Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional estimator, POEM, in terms of generalization performance and robustness. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the relationship between the self-normalized estimator and propensity overfitting.

The strengths of the paper include its thorough analysis of the problem, the proposal of a sensible and well-motivated alternative estimator, and the extensive empirical evaluation of the proposed algorithm. The authors also provide code for the proposed algorithm, which is appreciated.

However, there are a few weaknesses and areas for improvement. The reviewer found the explanation of the relationship between large weight variance and propensity overfitting to be unclear and would like a more detailed explanation. Additionally, the reviewer questions the claim that prior BLBF works used an unbiased counterfactual risk estimator, noting that they may have used clipping, which introduces bias.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their ideas. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide sufficient background information and references to related work.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-organized, and the authors provide clear explanations of their ideas. However, there are a few areas where the explanation could be improved, such as the relationship between large weight variance and propensity overfitting.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a new estimator and a new learning algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the problem and a clear explanation of the relationship between the self-normalized estimator and propensity overfitting.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in BLBF and provides a significant improvement over prior works. The proposed algorithm, Norm-POEM, has the potential to be widely used in practice, and the authors provide a clear explanation of its advantages and limitations.
