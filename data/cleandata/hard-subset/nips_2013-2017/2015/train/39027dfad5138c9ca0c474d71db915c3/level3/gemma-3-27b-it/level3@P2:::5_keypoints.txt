- Problem: Identifies propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), causing biased risk estimates with standard counterfactual estimators.
- Prior-art limitation: Shows that the commonly used estimator (Swaminathan & Joachims, 2015) exhibits anomalies that existing regularization only partially fixes.
- Solution: Proposes a self-normalized estimator and derives Norm-POEM (Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models) to address propensity overfitting.
- Theory: Provides sound theoretical justification and consistency guarantees (Theorem 2) for the self-normalized estimator.
- Empirics: Extensive experiments on multi-label classification show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms original POEM.
- Originality: Novel identification of propensity overfitting as a distinct BLBF problem and a new self-normalized mitigation approach.
- Related work: Well-positioned within causal inference, domain adaptation, off-policy evaluation, and importance sampling literature.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and includes helpful illustrative examples and accessible notation.
- Significance: Demonstrates substantial practical improvements and better computational efficiency, advancing BLBF methodology.
- Computational cost: Variance estimation in Norm-POEM increases computational complexity and needs deeper discussion.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Lacks a systematic analysis of sensitivity to regularization hyperparameters (λ and M).
- Dataset scope: Experimental evaluation is limited to a small set of LibSVM datasets, limiting claims about generalizability.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important overlooked problem, is theoretically sound, empirically validated, and clearly presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: Potential scalability issue due to variance estimation cost and the need for broader experimental validation.
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommended for acceptance (NIPS 2015).
