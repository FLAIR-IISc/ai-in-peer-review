- Identifies a severe problem in BLBF: conventional unbiased risk estimator suffers from "propensity overfitting" when hypothesis space capacity allows overfitting of propensity weights.  
- Frames the problem within BLBF and the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle as background.  
- Proposes a multiplicative control variate leading to a self-normalized risk estimator that avoids the conventional estimator's anomalies and is strongly consistent.  
- Derives a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on CRM with the self-normalized estimator.  
- Extensive empirical evaluation shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on several multi-label classification tasks.  
- Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting: selected hypotheses have average sum of propensity weights close to the expected value of 1.  
- Self-normalized estimator removes the need for variance regularization and yields surprisingly faster runtime than POEM.  
- Strengths: insightful analysis of propensity overfitting, effective use of multiplicative control variate, clear empirical improvements, and well-written presentation.  
- Minor issues to address: clarify whether Norm-POEM uses a rescaled delta in [-1,0] and explain "delta > 0" in Table 1.  
- Suggests modeling delta(x,y) as a random variable with expectation over label noise and adding that expectation into the true risk setup.  
- Related work is thorough, but the paper should better differentiate its approach from other control-variate methods like the doubly robust estimator.  
- Some mathematical derivations need more elaboration and more intuition behind the multiplicative control variate.  
- Overall assessment: significant and publishable contribution pending minor revisions; quality 6, clarity 6, originality 8, significance 8.
