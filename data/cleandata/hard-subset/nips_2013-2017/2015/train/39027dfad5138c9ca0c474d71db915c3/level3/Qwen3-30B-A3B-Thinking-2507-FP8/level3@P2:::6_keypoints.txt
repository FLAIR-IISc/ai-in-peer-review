- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), a previously overlooked failure mode.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator and the Norm-POEM algorithm to avoid propensity overfitting.  
- Shows conventional CRM/POEM estimators can be driven to arbitrarily low risk by exploiting propensity weights.  
- The self-normalized estimator uses multiplicative control variates, proving strong consistency and equivariance.  
- Theoretical analysis demonstrates the estimator resolves propensity overfitting while remaining consistent.  
- Empirical evaluation on multi-label datasets shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms conventional POEM.  
- Empirical evidence: propensity sum estimates Ŝ(h) remain close to 1 under the self-normalized estimator.  
- Strength: identifies a novel, significant failure mode in BLBF not previously recognized.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical proofs and clear linkage between theory and empirical results.  
- Strength: thorough empirical validation across datasets and sample sizes.  
- Weakness: computational complexity of the self-normalized estimator is not explored in detail.  
- Weakness: connections to recent work on control variates in importance sampling could be strengthened with more citations.  
- Weakness: applicability beyond linear hypothesis spaces and to other structured outputs is not thoroughly explored.  
- Quality/clrty: technically sound, well-written, and well-organized with an effective illustrative example.  
- Originality/significance: novel identification of propensity overfitting and a meaningful application of self-normalized estimators; recommended for acceptance.
