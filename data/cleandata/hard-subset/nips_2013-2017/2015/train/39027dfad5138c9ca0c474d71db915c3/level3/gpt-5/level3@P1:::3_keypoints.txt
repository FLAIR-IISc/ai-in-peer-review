- Problem: Identifies "propensity overfitting" in Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) where IPS allows hypotheses to game importance weights, producing pathological empirical risks and sensitivity to loss translations.  
- Proposal: Replace IPS with self-normalized importance sampling (SNIPS) and instantiate within CRM to obtain Norm-POEM for structured (CRF-like) policies.  
- Diagnostic: Proposes monitoring the sum of weights Ŝ(h) as a grounded diagnostic (E[Ŝ(h)] = 1) for propensity overfitting.  
- Theoretical: Provides a consistency theorem for SNIPS, showing strong consistency and addressing non-equivariance and boundedness issues of IPS.  
- Variance plug-in: Derives an approximate variance formula for the SNIPS ratio estimator to use in CRM’s empirical-Bernstein-style regularizer, with clipping to ensure finite variance.  
- Empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multiple multi-label supervised→bandit datasets.  
- Robustness: Shows Norm-POEM is invariant to loss translations and yields better-conditioned optimization objectives with improved runtime behavior.  
- Experimental limitations: Experiments lack variance reporting (means±std or CIs), ablations, and several important baselines.  
- Baselines missing: Recommends comparisons to doubly robust/regression estimators (with/without clipping) and truncated IPS to isolate SNIPS effects.  
- Optimization choices: Uses L-BFGS on a non-convex, non-stochastic objective due to the ratio’s variance estimate; plausible but needs deeper analysis of convergence, initialization sensitivity, and scalability.  
- Optimization gaps: No comparison to scalable stochastic/quasi-stochastic methods or detailed convergence/stability diagnostics.  
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written, well organized, and pedagogical on equivariance and control variates; code pointers aid reproducibility.  
- Clarity suggestions: Ask for guidance on selecting M and λ, gradient derivation appendix for SNIPS-CRM, and explicit computational complexity per iteration (wall-clock and iteration counts).  
- Originality: Ingredients (SNIPS, CRM, CRF policies) are known; novelty is identifying propensity overfitting and arguing/practically demonstrating SNIPS within CRM—incremental but meaningful.  
- Significance: Problem is important for recommendation/ads/search and normalization is a low-friction change with potential impact.  
- Significance caveats: Would be stronger with DR/truncated comparisons, real logged data or other domains, and statistical significance/sensitivity analyses (exploration rate, tail-heaviness, clipping, λ).  
- Pros summary: Clearly identifies failure mode, provides principled fix, shows empirical improvements and robustness, connects to IS theory, supplies consistency result and variance plug-in, good diagnostics, and available code.  
- Cons summary: Limited experimental scope (same tasks as prior work), few baselines, no real-world logs, missing uncertainty reporting, limited theoretical bounds for ratio estimator, and light optimization analysis.  
- Requested revisions (experiments): Report mean±std or 95% CI over runs, add significance tests, include DR/truncated IPS baselines, and add an ablation isolating normalization.  
- Requested revisions (sensitivity): Provide sensitivity studies for clipping M and λ, exploration in h0, support violations, and heavy-tailed weight scenarios.  
- Requested revisions (data): Include at least one real-world BLBF task or synthetic off-policy RL domain to broaden empirical scope.  
- Requested revisions (theory/optimization): Discuss theoretical bias of SNIPS under clipping, provide practical guidance (e.g., bounds via coefficient of variation), and expand optimization analysis (initialization, scaling, comparison to stochastic methods).  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — addresses a real problem with a principled, practical solution and consistent improvements, but needs stronger experimental and theoretical support.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
