- Identifies a critical failure mode in CRM for BLBF called "propensity overfitting," where hypotheses exploit example-generation probabilities to reduce estimated risk artificially.  
- Shows the standard importance-weighted risk estimator in ERM can cause severe overfitting, with risk estimates deviating arbitrarily from true risk.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator using a multiplicative control variate that provably preserves consistency while controlling variance.  
- Derives a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on the self-normalized estimator, with theoretical guarantees.  
- Provides empirical validation across multiple multi-label classification datasets demonstrating Norm-POEM improves generalization and avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Analyzes how the self-normalized estimator maintains equivariance under loss transformations, a key advantage over conventional estimators.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound, with clear proofs of consistency and variance control.  
- Strengths: clear identification of a novel problem, elegant theoretical solution, and convincing empirical results.  
- Paper is well-organized and properly contextualized with related BLBF work (e.g., POEM, off-policy evaluation).  
- Implementation details and experimental setup are thorough and well-motivated.  
- Main weakness: limited discussion of computational cost trade-offs and incomplete theoretical complexity analysis.  
- Evaluation scope is narrow (primarily multi-label classification); broader application domains would strengthen significance.  
- Contribution is highly original and significant, addressing a previously overlooked fundamental issue in CRM.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper with strong theoretical and empirical support; recommended for acceptance despite slight uncertainty about broader applicability.
