- Tackles propensity overfitting in counterfactual risk minimization for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Proposes a novel self-normalized risk estimator and integrates it into a new algorithm, Norm-POEM.
- Theoretical analysis shows consistency and boundedness of the proposed estimator.
- Provides proofs that the self-normalized estimator mitigates propensity overfitting.
- Norm-POEM demonstrates faster convergence than POEM in experiments.
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms baseline POEM across multiple datasets.
- Experiments confirm stable propensity weight sums and improved test performance with the self-normalized estimator.
- Novel application of self-normalization to CRM in BLBF, advancing prior work.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with detailed experimental setup for reproducibility.
- Concern: increased per-iteration computational cost from normalization may limit large-scale applicability; runtime scalability analysis is lacking.
- Concern: evaluation primarily on multi-label classification; limited testing on other BLBF domains (e.g., recommender systems, ads).
- Concern: hyperparameter sensitivity (clipping threshold, regularization) is only briefly discussed; deeper analysis would help practitioners.
- Recommendation: accept — strengths and contributions outweigh minor concerns.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, offers novel theory-backed solution, and shows strong empirical performance.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evaluation scope and insufficient exploration of computational scalability.
