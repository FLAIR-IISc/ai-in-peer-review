- Identifies propensity overfitting in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator to avoid anomalies and lack of equivariance.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM (Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models) as the resulting algorithm.  
- Employs multiplicative control variates and provides a rigorous theoretical foundation for the estimator.  
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms baseline POEM on multi-label classification tasks.  
- Demonstrates improved generalization, robustness, and maintained computational efficiency.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and includes sufficient detail for reproducibility (public datasets/software).  
- Original contribution: novel formulation addressing a previously unrecognized form of overfitting in BLBF.  
- Comprehensive related-work discussion situates the method within counterfactual learning and importance sampling.  
- Potentially high practical impact for applications like ad placement, recommendation, and web search.  
- Weakness: computational trade-offs of the self-normalized estimator could be explored in more depth.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to multi-label classification; broader BLBF scenario experiments would strengthen generality.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant theoretical and empirical advance.
