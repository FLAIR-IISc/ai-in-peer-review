- Proposes a self-normalized estimator for counterfactual risk minimization (CRM) in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Identifies a severe overfitting issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator caused by exploiting example generation probabilities (propensity overfitting).  
- Shows the self-normalized estimator avoids propensity overfitting and is more robust, especially with biased logging policies.  
- Provides solid theoretical analysis supporting the proposed estimator’s properties.  
- Offers extensive and convincing empirical evaluation demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and uses well-defined notation.  
- Main contribution: novel self-normalized estimator that addresses propensity overfitting in CRM.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational complexity analysis and trade-off discussion despite noting higher per-iteration cost.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough empirical or theoretical comparison with other existing methods for addressing propensity overfitting.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on revisions to address complexity analysis and comparisons with related methods.
