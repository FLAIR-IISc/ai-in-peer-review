- Overall assessment: Excellent paper making a significant contribution to batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Problem identified: Reveals a fundamental failure mode of the standard IPS estimator, termed "Propensity Overfitting."  
- Failure mode detail: With complex hypothesis spaces, IPS can be minimized by manipulating propensity weights, producing arbitrarily poor risk estimates and degenerate solutions.  
- Proposed solution: Replace standard IPS with a self-normalized estimator derived via a multiplicative control variate.  
- Theoretical properties: Self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss translations, bounded by observed loss range, and strongly consistent.  
- Practical algorithm: Introduces Norm-POEM, integrating the self-normalized estimator into CRM for structured output prediction.  
- Empirical results: Extensive experiments on multi-label tasks show Norm-POEM consistently and substantially outperforms POEM and converges faster.  
- Methodology: Experimental approach is sound, using "Supervised -> Bandit" conversion for controlled, reproducible comparisons.  
- Quality and analysis: High technical quality; insightful analysis and an effective illustrative example for propensity overfitting.  
- Clarity: Very well-written and organized; motivates and explains the core idea clearly.  
- Originality: Novel identification of propensity overfitting in BLBF and creative application of self-normalization to solve it.  
- Significance: Addresses a highly practical and important problem; solution is easy to implement and likely to be widely adopted.  
- Acceptance arguments: Clear analysis of a prior oversight, principled simple solution, strong empirical gains, and high practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: No major objections; only minor suggestions for improvement.  
- Suggested minor improvements: Clarify theoretical assumptions about loss range vs. experiments (Hamming loss), explicitly handle stochastic loss notation, add a citation regarding estimator non-equivariance, and fix a few notational/argmin-argmax typos.
