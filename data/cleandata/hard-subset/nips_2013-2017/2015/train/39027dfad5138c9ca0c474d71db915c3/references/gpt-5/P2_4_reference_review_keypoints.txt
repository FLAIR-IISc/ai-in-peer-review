- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback and off-policy evaluation, diagnosing a new pathology called "propensity overfitting."
- Core observation: IPS ERM can be driven by manipulating the sum of importance weights Ŝ(h), producing extreme, non-equivariant estimates.
- Proposes self-normalized IPS (SNIPS) using the sum of weights as a multiplicative control variate and introduces Norm-POEM that plugs SNIPS and an approximate variance into CRM.
- Theoretical claims: SNIPS yields boundedness, equivariance, and almost sure consistency (with full support).
- Empirical claim: Norm-POEM improves generalization over POEM on simulated logged multilabel datasets and is robust to loss translation and computationally competitive.
- Notes SN/self-normalized IS is well-known in Monte Carlo and RL; doubly robust and clipped IPS baselines are established elsewhere.
- Main novelty is framing and diagnosing "propensity overfitting" in BLBF ERM and showing SNIPS mitigates it in theory and practice.
- Technical soundness: identification of propensity overfitting via Ŝ(h) is compelling; Example 1 effectively illustrates IPS ERM failure modes.
- SNIPS properties are standard and correctly stated; consistency proof is straightforward.
- Variance estimator for the ratio is reasonable but approximate; clipping is used to ensure finite variance.
- Claim that SNIPS “resolves all problems” is too strong—SNIPS introduces finite-sample bias and can fail when denominators are small.
- The paper addresses key anomalies (lack of equivariance, unbounded empirical risk) and supports robustness improvements with experiments.
- Completeness: provides full algorithm, training details, and empirical evaluation but omits comparisons to strong alternatives like DR and weighted/normalized DR.
- No formal generalization bound for CRM with SNIPS is provided, weakening theoretical completeness.
- Clarity: generally clear and well-structured; propensity overfitting and translation pathology are well explained though notation is sometimes heavy.
- Suggested clarity improvements: state full-support requirement for consistency earlier and discuss exploration implications.
- Additional practical suggestions: provide gradient derivation and tricks to avoid small denominators; clarify hyperparameter selection and validation protocol.
- Recommend reporting confidence intervals, effect sizes, and visualizing weight distributions or Ŝ(h) histograms to show mitigation of propensity overfitting.
- Originality: moderate—estimator is classical, novelty lies in articulating the pathology and showing SNIPS within CRM is practical and stable.
- Significance: addresses an important problem for recommender systems and contextual bandits; diagnosis and simple remedy are likely useful to practitioners.
- Limitations: missing DR baselines, no real-world logged data, limited ablations (sensitivity to M and weight distributions), and light guidance on clipping vs bias trade-offs.
- Suggested improvements: add DR/truncated DR baselines, provide CRM-style bound or high-level argument, analyze effect of M and weight distributions, and clarify model selection to avoid validation-overfitting.
- Overall assessment: well-argued, practically relevant contribution diagnosing IPS-ERM failure and offering a simple effective remedy with theory and experiments.
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
