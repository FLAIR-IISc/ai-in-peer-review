- Identifies Propensity Overfitting as a critical, previously unrecognized flaw in BLBF that can yield arbitrarily low (misleading) risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates and a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM.  
- Provides a compelling counterexample (Example 1) demonstrating the severity of Propensity Overfitting.  
- Proves the self-normalized estimator is equivariant under loss translation, bounded within the loss range, and strongly consistent (Theorem 2).  
- Highlights the key insight connecting E[Ŝ(h)] = 1 to the design of the self-normalized estimator.  
- Notes the paper stops short of an explicit formal proof that the self-normalized estimator completely avoids Propensity Overfitting.  
- Empirically validates methods using the Supervised 7→ Bandit protocol across multiple datasets.  
- Shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets and improves with more training data.  
- Demonstrates Norm-POEM maintains Ŝ(ĥ) close to 1 while POEM exhibits severe bias (Table 2).  
- Reports Norm-POEM converges faster than POEM and yields better optimization conditioning (smaller ||w||).  
- Claims originality in identifying Propensity Overfitting and applying multiplicative control variates to counterfactual learning.  
- Suggests the paper should better distinguish its approach from related variance-reduction methods (e.g., doubly robust estimators).  
- Lists critical weaknesses: lack of full proof of avoidance, experiments limited to multi-label classification, and limited comparison to other variance-reduction techniques.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed contribution with practical impact and a recommendation to accept.
