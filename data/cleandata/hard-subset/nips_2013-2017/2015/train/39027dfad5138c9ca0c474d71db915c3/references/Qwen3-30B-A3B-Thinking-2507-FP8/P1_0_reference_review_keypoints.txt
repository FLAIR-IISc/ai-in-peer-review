- Identifies a critical flaw in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) called "Propensity Overfitting" where importance-sampling risk estimators become biased under ERM.
- Proposes a novel self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates to prevent Propensity Overfitting.
- Shows the self-normalized estimator is provably better: equivariant, bounded within the loss range, and avoids the identified overfitting issue.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including Theorem 2 proving consistency of the self-normalized estimator.
- Presents a compelling illustrative example (Example 1) demonstrating Propensity Overfitting in practice.
- Empirical evaluation across multiple LibSVM datasets shows Norm-POEM outperforms the prior state-of-the-art (POEM).
- Experiments include analysis via the Ŝ(h) statistic and follow standard Supervised→Bandit methodology for comparability.
- Acknowledges limitations such as continued need for variance regularization and increased computational cost.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and uses figures/tables effectively to convey results.
- Originality is strong: first to identify Propensity Overfitting in BLBF and to apply self-normalized estimators to this problem.
- Results are significant for real-world applications (ads, recommendations) and are easy to integrate into existing frameworks.
- Strengths: novel problem identification, simple elegant theory, consistent empirical gains, computational efficiency, clear writing.
- Weaknesses: limited exploration of extreme covariate shift or limited logging-policy support, need for more ablation studies, and limited discussion on practical benefits of equivariance.
- Pro-accept: novel important problem, sound theory, clear empirical improvements, easy implementation, thorough evaluation.
- Con-accept: theoretical analysis could be more comprehensive, more ablations desired, limitations in challenging scenarios not fully explored.
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance at NIPS due to theoretical and empirical contributions and practical significance.
