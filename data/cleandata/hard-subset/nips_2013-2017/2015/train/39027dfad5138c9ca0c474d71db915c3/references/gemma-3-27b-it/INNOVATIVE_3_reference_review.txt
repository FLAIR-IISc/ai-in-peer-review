This paper tackles a critical issue in learning from logged data – propensity overfitting – which is a novel and significant observation. The problem is clearly articulated, and the proposed solution, a self-normalized estimator, is theoretically sound and intuitively appealing, drawing parallels to established techniques in other fields (finance, RL). The empirical validation is extensive and convincingly demonstrates the benefits of the proposed approach over existing methods. While the theoretical analysis builds on prior work, identifying and addressing this specific overfitting phenomenon constitutes a genuine contribution. The paper is well-written and the connection to existing literature is well established.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The identification of "propensity overfitting" is a novel and important contribution to the field of learning from logged bandit feedback. This isn't simply an incremental improvement, but a fundamentally new problem identified within a well-studied setting.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The self-normalized estimator is well-motivated and grounded in established statistical principles (control variates, importance sampling). The theoretical justification and proof of consistency are solid.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments are comprehensive and clearly demonstrate the superior performance of Norm-POEM compared to POEM across various datasets. The ablation studies (e.g., impact of variance regularization) are insightful.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The technical concepts are explained clearly and concisely, making the paper accessible to a broad audience.
*   **Significance:** Addressing propensity overfitting has significant implications for real-world applications of learning from logged data, such as ad placement, recommender systems, and personalized medicine.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Computational Complexity:** While the paper mentions the computational cost of the self-normalized estimator, a more detailed discussion of its scalability to very large datasets and high-dimensional feature spaces would be beneficial. Though the empirical results suggest that convergence is faster, a formal analysis of the computational trade-offs would strengthen the paper.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper briefly mentions hyperparameter tuning, but a more in-depth exploration of the sensitivity of Norm-POEM to different hyperparameter settings would be valuable. A discussion of best practices for hyperparameter selection would be helpful for practitioners.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experimental evaluation is extensive, it is primarily focused on a specific set of datasets and a particular hypothesis space (CRF). Exploring the performance of Norm-POEM in other settings (e.g., different datasets, different hypothesis spaces) could further strengthen the claims.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper identifies a novel and important problem—propensity overfitting—that has been overlooked in previous work.
*   The proposed solution, the self-normalized estimator, is theoretically sound and empirically validated.
*   The paper is well-written, clear, and concise.
*   The potential impact of this work is significant for applications of learning from logged data.
*   The observed improved robustness and faster convergence are compelling.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The computational complexity of the self-normalized estimator is a potential concern.
*   The experimental evaluation could be broadened to include more diverse datasets and hypothesis spaces.
*   A deeper dive into hyperparameter sensitivity would be helpful.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of learning from logged bandit feedback. The identification of propensity overfitting is a novel and important observation, and the proposed solution is both theoretically sound and empirically validated. While there are a few minor weaknesses, they do not detract from the overall quality of the work. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance, despite not being polished to perfection. The novelty and potential impact of the work outweigh the minor shortcomings. I would rate this as a **strong accept**. It's a paper I'd be excited to see presented at NIPS.
