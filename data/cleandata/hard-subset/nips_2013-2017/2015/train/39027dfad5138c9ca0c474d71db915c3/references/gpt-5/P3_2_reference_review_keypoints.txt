- Paper studies batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies "propensity overfitting" in IPS-based ERM.  
- Root cause: IPS estimator is not range-bounded or translation-equivariant, letting ERM exploit weight sums.  
- Proposed fix: use a multiplicative control variate → self-normalized IPS (SNIPS), which is bounded, equivariant, and strongly consistent.  
- Algorithm instantiation: Norm-POEM replaces IPS in CRM with SNIPS and an approximate variance estimate, optimized via L-BFGS.  
- Experiments (supervised→bandit replay) show consistent gains over POEM, better stability under loss translations, and competitive runtimes.  

- Builds on CRM/POEM and classic importance sampling variance-reduction literature; explicitly uses self-normalization inside learning objectives (novel application).  
- Cites empirical Bernstein/high-confidence OPE foundations, but lacks experimental comparison to modern DR/WDR and hybrid clipping-switch estimators.  

- Strength: Clear identification and diagnostic (Ŝ(h)) for propensity overfitting with a synthetic example.  
- Strength: SNIPS is a principled, simple fix with boundedness/equivariance addressing the pathology.  
- Strength: Strong consistency proof and empirically meaningful, robust gains across datasets and translations.  
- Strength: Well-motivated integration into CRM and a tractable Norm-POEM implementation; runtime observations encouraging.  
- Strength: Diagnostics (Ŝ(h) ≈ 1 for Norm-POEM) support the main hypothesis.  

- Weakness: Theoretical learning guarantees are underdeveloped — CRM bound applies to capped IPS, not SNIPS; no high-probability generalization bound capturing SNIPS bias.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to doubly robust (DR), weighted DR (WDR), SWITCH-DR, and clipping-only baselines, weakening empirical claims.  
- Weakness: Experimental protocol (replay from CRF on 5% data) doesn’t test partial/no support or propensity misspecification; behavior under insufficient coverage unexplored.  
- Weakness: Nonconvex ratio optimization details sparse — initialization, convergence diagnostics, and sensitivity to λ and M need ablation.  
- Weakness: Variance estimator (Eq. 8) is asymptotic/approximate and used as a regularizer on the same data; stability under heavy-tailed weights/clipping is not discussed or empirically validated.  
- Weakness: Related work should better contrast with conservative/trust-region policy improvement methods that regularize toward the logging policy.  

- Clarity: Paper is well written; example and Ŝ(h) diagnostic are helpful.  
- Clarity suggestion: Explicitly distinguish IPS, clipped IPS, and SNIPS early and state which is used where.  
- Clarity suggestion: State assumptions for Theorem 2 precisely and provide gradients of Norm-POEM in the appendix.  

- Significance: Identification of propensity overfitting and use of self-normalization in learning are conceptually important and practically impactful.  
- Reproducibility: Datasets, protocol, hyperparameters, and code availability are adequate; include seeds, preprocessing, and full hyperparameter grids for full reproducibility.  

- Pros for acceptance: clear problem diagnosis, principled fix grounded in IS theory, practical algorithm with demonstrable gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing SNIPS learning generalization bounds, absent DR/WDR baselines, and limited evaluation under coverage/misspecification issues.  

- Recommendation: Weak accept pending stronger theoretical guarantees for SNIPS-based learning and broader empirical baselines.
