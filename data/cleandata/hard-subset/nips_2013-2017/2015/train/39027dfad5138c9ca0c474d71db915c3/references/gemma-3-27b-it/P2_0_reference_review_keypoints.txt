- Summary: Identifies propensity overfitting in logged bandit feedback and proposes a self-normalized (SN) estimator to mitigate biased counterfactual risk estimates, with theoretical justification and empirical validation.  
- Novel Problem: Highlights a previously unrecognized and important issue (propensity overfitting) in BLBF.  
- Solution: Proposes an elegant, principled SN estimator based on importance sampling control variates.  
- Theoretical Support: Provides guarantees for consistency and addresses variance-related concerns.  
- Empirical Validation: Extensive experiments (Supervised → Bandit) show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM and is invariant to loss translation.  
- Clarity & Organization: Paper is well-written, logically organized, and explains motivation and methods clearly.  
- Reproducibility: Code is provided, facilitating replication and further research.  
- Computational Concerns: Potentially higher computational complexity (variance estimator) needs clearer runtime breakdown.  
- Hyperparameter Sensitivity: CRM effectiveness depends on tuning (M and λ); more guidance on sensitivity is needed.  
- Limited Experimental Scope: Evaluation is confined to specific datasets and setup; broader BLBF scenarios would strengthen claims.  
- Quality Assessment: Technically sound, complete work with well-supported claims.  
- Originality: Novel application and analysis of SN estimator in BLBF despite estimator roots in importance sampling.  
- Significance: Results are practically important and likely to impact researchers and practitioners using historical data.  
- Arguments For Acceptance: Addresses a critical problem with a practical, theoretically justified solution and reproducible results.  
- Arguments Against Acceptance: Concerns about computational cost and limited experimental breadth.  
- Overall Recommendation: Strong accept — significant contribution with minor areas for improvement.
