- Identifies propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) causing biased counterfactual risk estimates.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator using importance sampling and control variates to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Provides theoretical justification and clear intuition for why the self-normalized estimator improves properties over the standard estimator.
- Empirically demonstrates Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple datasets.
- Includes ablation studies and analysis of the self-normalized term that support theoretical claims.
- Positions the work relative to BLBF, off-policy evaluation, doubly-robust estimators, and POEM, highlighting novelty in identifying propensity overfitting.
- References are comprehensive and the paper is clearly written and well organized.
- Offers practical implications for real-world logged bandit applications (e.g., ads, recommender systems).
- Increased per-iteration computational cost is acknowledged but lacks detailed runtime scaling analysis.
- Experiments are primarily limited to adapting the POEM framework; broader algorithm/hypothesis-space validation is needed.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (λ and M) is not thoroughly explored; more sensitivity analysis would be useful.
- The equivariance claim depends on accurate estimation of the self-normalized term; biased estimates could undermine equivariance.
- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically grounded, and empirically convincing paper; rated Strong Accept (8/10).
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant unrecognized problem, offers theoretically grounded solution with compelling empirical support, clear presentation, and practical impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: computational complexity, limited experiment scope, and insufficient parameter sensitivity analysis.
