- Tackles propensity overfitting in learning from logged bandit feedback and proposes a self-normalized risk estimator to mitigate it.  
- Self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss translations, inherently bounded, and borrowed from finance/RL literature.  
- Theoretical analysis demonstrates why standard importance-sampling estimators fail and proves consistency of the proposed estimator.  
- Empirical results across multiple datasets show improved performance and robustness over POEM and CRF baselines.  
- Paper situates itself well within BLBF, off-policy evaluation, and importance-sampling literature and contrasts propensity overfitting with standard loss overfitting.  
- Connection to prior work and variance-reduction techniques is well-explained and appropriately cited.  
- Novel identification and analysis of "propensity overfitting" is a significant contribution.  
- Theoretical soundness and proofs (including consistency) strengthen the proposal.  
- Practical impact: Norm-POEM improves performance and appears computationally efficient in experiments.  
- Clear writing, organized presentation, and helpful illustrative examples (e.g., Example 1).  
- Thorough experimentation following Supervised->Bandit methodology, with useful ablation on variance regularization.  
- Concern about computational complexity of variance estimation (Equation 8) and claim it "does not admit stochastic optimization."  
- Sensitivity to clipping parameter M is not fully analyzed; calibration via hold-out is mentioned but not studied in depth.  
- Experiments are limited to datasets used in prior work; broader dataset diversity is lacking.  
- Missing implementation details for Norm-POEM’s L-BFGS optimization (learning rate, convergence criteria) reduce reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with solid theory and experiments; minor weaknesses do not undermine contribution.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: detail computational complexity of variance estimation, analyze sensitivity to M, and clarify L-BFGS implementation details.
