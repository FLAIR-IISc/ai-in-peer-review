- Addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies a failure mode—Propensity Overfitting—when IPS is used inside ERM.  
- Proposes a multiplicative control variate yielding the self-normalized IPS (SNIPS) estimator and integrates it into Norm-POEM with an approximate variance penalty.  
- Proves SNIPS remedies IPS anomalies: boundedness, equivariance to loss shifts, and strong consistency (via SLLN); variance estimator uses standard delta/normal approximations.  
- Empirically, Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on multilabel supervised-to-bandit simulations, stabilizes the sum of weights, is robust to loss translations, and has competitive runtime.  
- Situates work in prior IS and off-policy evaluation literature; novelty lies in formalizing Propensity Overfitting and adopting SNIPS within CRM rather than inventing SNIPS itself.  
- Technical strengths: convincing diagnostic example, correct theoretical claims, and reasonable empirical support for stability and improved risk.  
- Key limitations: lacks finite-sample generalization bounds for SNIPS-CRM and no uniform analysis of bias–variance tradeoffs from normalization/clipping.  
- Variance estimator is approximate and its optimization/calibration impact is not theoretically characterized.  
- Critical assumptions (full support, accurately estimated logging propensities) are necessary and should be emphasized; behavior under small/misspecified propensities is unclear.  
- Missing empirical comparisons to doubly robust (DR/wDR) and other stabilized estimators and limited diversity of logging policies/action spaces in evaluation.  
- Clarity is good overall, but the paper should add pseudocode, clearer training objective/gradients, and explicitly state model factorization/tractability of Z(x).  
- Suggestions: derive finite-sample/ratio-concentration bounds, add DR/wDR baselines and sensitivity analyses for clipping M and λ, and discuss propensity misspecification effects.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well written, conceptually useful and empirically effective; recommendation to accept.
