- Identifies a novel problem in BLBF called "Propensity Overfitting" affecting conventional counterfactual estimators.  
- Proposes a Self-Normalized Risk Estimator using multiplicative control variates to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the estimator avoids anomalies of conventional importance sampling (unbounded risks, lack of equivariance).  
- Theorem 2 provides formal guarantees for the self-normalized estimator.  
- Derives Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple standard BLBF datasets.  
- Demonstrates robustness to different dataset sizes and loss transformations.  
- Clear, well-organized writing with consistent notation and reproducible experimental setup.  
- Provides a compelling example and empirical evidence illustrating propensity overfitting prevalence.  
- Original contribution: formalizes propensity overfitting and applies multiplicative control variates in a new BLBF context.  
- Builds on CRM principles but advances beyond prior work by identifying and addressing a previously unrecognized problem.  
- Significant practical implications for recommendation and ad systems that use logged bandit feedback.  
- Addresses potential weaknesses candidly, including computational cost and need for variance regularization.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation and detailed analysis of why the approach avoids conventional estimator anomalies.  
- Weakness: limited discussion on cases where the self-normalized denominator may approach zero in practice.  
- Weakness: theoretical variance analysis of the self-normalized estimator could be more detailed.  
- Weakness: computational cost is higher than conventional estimators and not thoroughly compared to POEM.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound paper with significant contributions and a recommendation for acceptance.
