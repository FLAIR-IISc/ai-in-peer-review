- Identifies "Propensity Overfitting" in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF): conventional counterfactual estimators can produce arbitrarily biased risk estimates by overemphasizing samples with certain propensities.

- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates and proves it avoids the anomalies of conventional estimators.

- Derives the Norm-POEM learning algorithm and demonstrates superior empirical performance across multiple LibSVM datasets.

- Strength: Novel problem identification with effective examples showing conventional estimators can under- or over-estimate risk arbitrarily.

- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis—proves consistency and equivariance; well-motivated connection to control variates in importance sampling.

- Strength: Comprehensive empirical evaluation showing consistent improvements of Norm-POEM and evidence that the estimator avoids Propensity Overfitting (Ŝ(h) analysis).

- Weakness: Paper does not adequately explain why prior work failed to identify Propensity Overfitting or how it differs from previously addressed overfitting issues.

- Weakness: Lacks finite-sample variance bounds or a comparative variance analysis for the self-normalized estimator versus conventional estimators.

- Weakness: Does not discuss limitations such as handling logging policies with very low support for some actions (extremely small propensity scores).

- Weakness: Runtime analysis is superficial; insufficient explanation for why Norm-POEM converges faster and how smaller ||w|| leads to faster convergence.

- Overall assessment: Valuable contribution with strong theory and convincing experiments; significant advance in BLBF despite some gaps.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Suggestion: Expand related work to explain why Propensity Overfitting was previously overlooked and contrast with past overfitting analyses.

- Suggestion: Add theoretical analysis of the variance of the self-normalized estimator, including finite-sample bounds.

- Suggestion: Discuss estimator behavior when the logging policy has very low support or extremely small p_i values.

- Suggestion: Provide a more detailed explanation of why Norm-POEM converges faster, particularly the relationship between ||w|| and convergence rate.

- Suggestion: Clarify connections between the self-normalized estimator and other recent counterfactual approaches (e.g., doubly robust estimators).
