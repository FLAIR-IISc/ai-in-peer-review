- Identifies a new problem in BLBF, "Propensity Overfitting," where IPS estimators can produce unbounded, non-translation-equivariant risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self-normalized IPS estimator using multiplicative control variates to fix the identified anomaly.  
- Theoretical analysis (Section 4) demonstrates how conventional estimators can be arbitrarily misled by overfitting and shows strong consistency of the new estimator (Theorem 2).  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple datasets and is robust to loss translations.  
- Draws useful connections to importance sampling and prior use of self-normalization in other fields (e.g., finance).  
- Lacks a detailed bias–variance analysis quantifying the bias of the self-normalized estimator and why it doesn’t harm performance.  
- Computational claims are weakly supported: no rigorous runtime or complexity comparison with POEM despite extra per-iteration computation.  
- Empirical evaluation omits challenging real-world conditions (e.g., logging policies with low support, very high-dimensional features).  
- Contribution is original in applying the self-normalized estimator to BLBF and formally identifying Propensity Overfitting.  
- Significant practical impact for industry applications (ads, recommendations) and strong overall theoretical + empirical case.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper with important conceptual advance; recommended for acceptance despite noted gaps.
