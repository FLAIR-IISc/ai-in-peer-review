- Identifies a novel problem: propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (Norm-POEM) to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Provides theoretical justification, including consistency (Theorem 2) and links to control variates/self-normalization.
- Empirically validates Norm-POEM across datasets, showing consistent improvement over POEM and reduced propensity overfitting.
- Demonstrates an equivariance property of the self-normalized estimator as an advantage over conventional estimators.
- Paper is clear, well-written, and well-organized.
- Code is released, aiding reproducibility and practical adoption.
- Computational complexity explanation is weak; needs detailed analysis and profiling, and discussion of optimization challenges (L-BFGS vs stochastic).
- Experimental scope is limited to low-dimensional datasets and a specific feature map; needs evaluation on larger, more complex datasets and representations.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (parameters M and λ) is insufficiently analyzed.
- Could better connect to broader literature on robust estimation and outlier handling for extreme importance weights.
- Variance estimation in Norm-POEM is approximate; potential impacts of this approximation are not discussed.
- Originality: identifying propensity overfitting is novel; applying self-normalization to BLBF and combining with CRM is an original contribution.
- Significance: work is highly significant with likely impact on recommender systems and ad platforms.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses critical, previously unrecognized problem; offers a theoretically grounded solution with strong empirical results and reproducible code.
- Arguments against acceptance: needs more detailed computational analysis, broader experiments, and hyperparameter sensitivity studies.
- Overall assessment: Strong accept with rating 8.5/10.
