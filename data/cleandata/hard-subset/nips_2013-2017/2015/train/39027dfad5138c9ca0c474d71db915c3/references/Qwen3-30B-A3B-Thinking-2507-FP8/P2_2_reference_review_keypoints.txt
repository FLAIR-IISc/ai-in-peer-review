- Paper identifies a novel problem "Propensity Overfitting" in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and proposes a self-normalized estimator to fix it.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm using multiplicative control variates to avoid propensity overfitting.  
- Provides a compelling theoretical example showing conventional importance-sampling estimators can give arbitrarily low risk estimates.  
- Proves consistency of the self-normalized estimator (Theorem 2).  
- Empirical evaluation follows established "Supervised→Bandit" methodology and is comprehensive across multiple datasets.  
- Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM in experiments, with evidence linking gains to avoiding propensity overfitting (Table 2).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and technically accessible with proper placement of theorems and proofs.  
- The concept of propensity overfitting is original and the application of self-normalized estimators to BLBF is novel relative to prior work.  
- The contribution is significant for real-world counterfactual learning systems (recommendation, ads), addressing a fundamental reliability issue.  
- Lacks finite-sample generalization bounds or error guarantees for Norm-POEM beyond consistency.  
- Insufficient computational complexity analysis despite claims that Norm-POEM is "surprisingly faster" than POEM.  
- Relies on the strong assumption that the logging policy has full support; robustness when this is violated is not discussed.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution with sound theory and strong empirical results.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
