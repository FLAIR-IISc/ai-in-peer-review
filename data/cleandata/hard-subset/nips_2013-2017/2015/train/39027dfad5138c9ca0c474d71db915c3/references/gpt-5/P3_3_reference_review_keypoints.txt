- Problem identified: IPS-based ERM in BLBF suffers "propensity overfitting" where importance weights, not low loss, drive empirical risk.
- Core insight: IPS empirical risk can be manipulated via the sum of importance weights; SNIPS (self-normalized IS) fixes this via a multiplicative control variate.
- Proposed method: Integrate SNIPS into CRM to form Norm-POEM and provide an approximate variance estimator for the ratio estimator.
- Theoretical claims: E[Ŝ(h)]=1, SNIPS yields a bounded, translation-equivariant estimator, and the estimator is strongly consistent.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on CRM, importance weighting, and known SNIPS literature; connects E[Ŝ]=1 diagnostic to propensity overfitting.
- Missing comparisons: No experiments versus doubly robust (DR) or weighted-DR learners, clipped-IPS+regularization, or other modern counterfactual methods.
- Strength — diagnosis: Clear articulation and simple examples demonstrating IPS pathologies (unboundedness, non-equivariance).
- Strength — remedy: SNIPS is a principled multiplicative control variate that directly addresses the identified anomalies.
- Strength — integration: Norm-POEM is a natural instantiation; the variance estimator is standard and appropriate in principle.
- Empirical strengths: Consistent improvements over POEM, robustness to loss translation, and Ŝ(ĥ) aligns better with expectation 1.
- Practical notes: Observed optimization benefits (LBFGS convergence, smaller parameter norms) useful for practitioners.
- Theoretical weakness: Modest novelty overall — SNIPS is classical and no new finite-sample generalization bounds are provided.
- Missing theory: No CRM-style finite-sample learning bound or concentration result tailored to the SNIPS objective.
- Variance-estimator concern: Used asymptotic approximation without discussion of finite-sample bias/variance or how clipping is applied to numerator/denominator.
- Experimental limitations: Supervised-to-bandit with multilabel Hamming loss, small/factorized action spaces — limited realism and scope.
- Robustness concerns: No tests with heavy-tailed propensities, large/non-factorized action spaces, or wall-clock runtime reporting.
- Assumptions questioned: Full-support logging and i.i.d. samples may be unrealistic; sensitivity to support violations and exploration rates not addressed.
- Clarity suggestions: State whether SNIPS weights are clipped (numerator and denominator), give variance-approximation derivation/reference, and link CRM penalty to Ŝ(h) fluctuations.
- Suggested improvements: Add DR and clipped-IPS baselines, sensitivity analyses for M/λ/loss scaling, clarify variance-regularizer implementation, and discuss applicability to larger/real logs.
- Overall verdict: Technically sound and practically useful with moderate originality; promising empirical gains but tempered by missing theory and stronger baselines — recommendation: weak accept.
