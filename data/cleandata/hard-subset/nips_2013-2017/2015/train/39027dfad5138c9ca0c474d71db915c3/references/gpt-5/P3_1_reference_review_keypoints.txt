- Paper studies batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and a failure mode of IPS-based ERM called “propensity overfitting.”
- Identifies IPS issues: non-equivariance and unboundedness that let hypotheses exploit importance weights to drive degenerate empirical risks.
- Proposes using the self-normalized importance sampling estimator (SNIPS) as a multiplicative control variate to obtain bounded, translation-equivariant, and consistent risk estimates.
- Introduces Norm-POEM: a CRM-style learning objective using SNIPS with an approximate variance penalty and weight clipping for stability.
- Connects SNIPS to classic importance sampling literature and prior off-policy learning and BLBF/CRM work (e.g., Dudík et al., Swaminathan & Joachims).
- Strength: Clear articulation and simple illustrative example of the propensity overfitting failure mode.
- Strength: Sound use of self-normalization as a variance-reduction/control variate technique; consistency and boundedness claims are correct.
- Strength: Integrates estimator into a practical algorithm with coherent computational discussion (Norm-POEM).
- Strength: Empirical results favor Norm-POEM over POEM across datasets, with diagnostics (Ŝ ≈ 1) supporting mitigation of overfitting.
- Strength: Loss-translation experiments convincingly show POEM’s brittleness vs. Norm-POEM’s invariance.
- Weakness: Core estimator (SNIPS/WIS) is standard; novelty is mainly framing propensity overfitting and applying SNIPS within CRM.
- Weakness: Lacks deeper theory—no finite-sample generalization bound for learning with SNIPS/ratio estimator analogous to CRM bounds for IPS.
- Weakness: Missing key baselines (Doubly Robust, self-normalized DR/WDR), clipped IPS/SNIPS ablations, and other robust off-policy learning methods.
- Weakness: Clipping treatment is underspecified—need consistent clipping definition for numerator and denominator and discussion of bias implications.
- Weakness: Theoretical claim “provably avoids degeneracies” omits discussion of finite-sample errors, high-variance propensities, and applicability of empirical Bernstein bounds to ratios.
- Weakness: Experimental scope limited—synthetic-to-bandit simulation only; needs stress tests with heavy-tailed/near-deterministic propensities and real logged data; statistical reporting is light.
- Weakness: Modeling clarity missing—unclear factorization of exponential-family model over multilabel outputs and tractability of partition function Z(x).
- Suggestions: Provide finite-sample learning bounds or high-level ratio-concentration arguments for the SNIPS-based CRM objective.
- Suggestions: Add DR/WDR baselines and ablations for clipping and variance-regularization sensitivity (M and λ).
- Suggestions: Clarify exact clipped SNIPS objective used, consistent clipping protocol, and induced bias.
- Suggestions: Expand experiments to extreme propensity regimes and real logged data; report standard errors/confidence intervals and clarify unusual data splits.
- Suggestions: Explicitly state the factorization used for tractable normalization in multilabel settings and avoid misleading CRF terminology.
- Overall assessment: Solid empirical paper with modest theoretical novelty; correct but incomplete theory and missing important baselines.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on clarifying clipping, adding DR/WDR comparisons, and tightening theoretical/expository gaps.
