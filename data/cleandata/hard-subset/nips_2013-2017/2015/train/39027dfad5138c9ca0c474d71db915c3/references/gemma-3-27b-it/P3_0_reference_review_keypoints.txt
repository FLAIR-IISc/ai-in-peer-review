- Identifies propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and shows standard counterfactual estimators can be driven far from true risk.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using importance sampling and control variates to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Provides theoretical justification including consistency and proofs that the estimator avoids the identified overfitting.  
- Empirically validates Norm-POEM outperforming POEM across multiple datasets, showing better generalization, robustness, and reasonable runtime.  
- Situates work well within BLBF, causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation literatures with comprehensive references.  
- Novel contribution: recognizing propensity overfitting as a new problem in BLBF.  
- Elegant solution: self-normalized estimator with strong theoretical connections to control variates.  
- Practical benefit: code release enables reproducibility.  
- Weakness: computational complexity discussion is superficial and needs deeper analysis of scaling and convergence claims (e.g., why shorter ‖w‖ speeds convergence).  
- Weakness: uses an approximate variance estimate (Eq. 8); impact on theory and empirical trustworthiness is not fully explored.  
- Weakness: lacks sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters M and λ.  
- Weakness: experiments use supervised datasets converted to BLBF; more realistic logged bandit datasets would strengthen claims.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-written paper with significant theoretical and empirical contributions; minor weaknesses can be addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental issue, offers a novel theoretically grounded solution, strong empirical results, clarity, and available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete computational complexity analysis, reliance on approximate variance estimate, and limited experimental scope.  
- Recommendation/rating: Strong Accept (8/10).
