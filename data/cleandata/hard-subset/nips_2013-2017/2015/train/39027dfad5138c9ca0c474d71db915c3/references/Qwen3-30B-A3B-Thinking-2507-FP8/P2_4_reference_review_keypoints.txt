- Identifies "Propensity Overfitting" in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) where the standard IPS estimator yields severely biased risk estimates for the empirical risk minimizer.  
- Shows the conventional inverse propensity score estimator can produce risk estimates arbitrarily lower than true risk (Example 1).  
- Proposes a Self-Normalized Risk Estimator using multiplicative control variates that provably avoids those anomalies (Equation 7).  
- Develops Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis (e.g., Theorem 2 proving strong consistency) supporting the new estimator.  
- Empirical evaluation across multiple LibSVM datasets (supervised→bandit protocol) demonstrates Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM.  
- Claims computational advantages for Norm-POEM and includes detailed computational analysis, though not exhaustive.  
- Original contribution: novel problem identification and novel application of control variates in counterfactual learning, distinct from prior POEM-related work.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, with clear notation and an effective illustrative example; results are reproducible.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental practical issue for recommender systems and ad platforms with substantial empirical gains.  
- Weakness: lacks a formal quantification of the magnitude by which propensity overfitting is reduced.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of computational complexity and trade-offs despite performance claims.  
- Weakness: assumes logging policy has full support over Y and provides little discussion of violations of this assumption.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly accept — significant, well-supported contribution with clear practical and theoretical benefits.
