- Paper identifies "propensity overfitting": IPS used inside ERM can be exploited to produce degenerate empirical risks (unbounded, non-equivariant under loss translations).
- Proposes using a multiplicative control variate (self-normalized importance sampling, SNIS) to obtain a bounded, equivariant, and strongly consistent risk estimator.
- Implements Norm-POEM by substituting SNIS and an approximate variance regularizer into the CRM objective and optimizing with L-BFGS.
- Theoretical contribution: clean construction (Example 1) convincingly demonstrates propensity overfitting; SNIS properties (boundedness, equivariance, consistency) are correctly argued.
- The paper lacks a finite-sample generalization bound for the SNIS-based learning objective analogous to existing CRM bounds.
- The variance expression (Eq. 8) is a standard delta-method approximation and thus heuristic in finite samples.
- The role and theoretical analysis of clipping M under SNIS and its bias–variance tradeoff are not provided.
- Methodologically, Norm-POEM is a straightforward, well-motivated substitution and is implementable when partition functions are tractable.
- Diagnostic Ŝ(h)≈1 is a useful practical sanity check for reduced propensity overfitting.
- Experiments using supervised→bandit simulation show consistent gains over POEM and robustness to loss translations.
- Experimental weaknesses: limited baselines (notably missing DR, Switch-DR, regression-based estimators) and no real logged datasets or larger action spaces.
- Ablations are partial: Norm-IPS included, but the separate contributions of normalization vs variance regularization and sensitivity to M and λ are insufficiently explored.
- Clarity and presentation are good overall; motivation and exposition are crisp, though minor typos (e.g., δ range) and hyperparameter detail omissions exist.
- Originality is moderate: SNIS is well-known, but formalizing propensity overfitting and showing SNIS mitigates it in learning is a novel framing.
- Significance: practically relevant for BLBF/off-policy learning; likely to influence practitioners toward self-normalized estimators with regularization.
- Pros: clear diagnosis, simple/principled remedy, consistent empirical gains, practical algorithm with available code and quick convergence.
- Cons: limited estimator novelty, standard/heuristic theoretical results, missing finite-sample learning bounds, incomplete experimental baselines, and minor notation/tuning details.
- Suggestions: provide a tailored generalization/empirical-Bernstein bound for SNIS, add DR/Switch-DR baselines, report effective sample size (ESS), and analyze sensitivity/selection of M and λ.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — meaningful practical contribution but would benefit from stronger theoretical guarantees and broader empirical comparisons.
