- Identifies a novel problem in BLBF called "propensity overfitting" where standard counterfactual estimators produce biased risk estimates and poor generalization.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator that is equivariant to loss translations and bounds the risk estimate to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Provides theoretical justification including equivariance arguments and a consistency result (Theorem 2).  
- Incorporates the estimator into a practical algorithm, Norm-POEM, for training from logged bandit feedback.  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multiple benchmark datasets and is robust to loss translations.  
- Ablation study indicates variance regularization remains necessary even with the self-normalized estimator.  
- Situates the work within prior BLBF, causal inference, and off-policy RL literature, connecting to importance sampling and variance reduction techniques.  
- Novelty lies in identifying propensity overfitting and applying self-normalization specifically to the BLBF setting.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains motivations and methods.  
- Code release improves reproducibility and impact.  
- Computational complexity discussion is insufficient, especially around variance estimation (Eq. 8) and L-BFGS use compared to POEM.  
- Limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (M and lambda) and their effect on performance.  
- Experiments use standard but relatively small datasets; scalability to larger, more complex data is not shown.  
- Theoretical connection to the CRM principle is stated but could be strengthened with explicit bounds under the new estimator.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution and convincing results.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
