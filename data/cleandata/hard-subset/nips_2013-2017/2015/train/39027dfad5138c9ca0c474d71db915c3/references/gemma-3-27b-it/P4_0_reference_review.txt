This paper addresses the problem of batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies a novel issue called “Propensity Overfitting” in the commonly used counterfactual risk estimator. The authors propose a “Self-Normalized Risk Estimator” based on multiplicative control variates to mitigate this overfitting and demonstrate its effectiveness both theoretically and empirically.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly establishing the connection between BLBF, off-policy evaluation, and causal inference. The identification of Propensity Overfitting is a significant contribution, as it reveals a subtle but critical flaw in previous approaches. The proposed self-normalized estimator is a reasonable and theoretically sound solution, drawing upon established techniques from importance sampling and control variates. The experimental validation using standard datasets and a well-defined experimental setup (following the “Supervised 7→ Bandit” methodology) convincingly demonstrates the benefits of the proposed approach over the existing POEM algorithm. The paper is well-written and the mathematical derivations are presented clearly. The comparison to existing work is thorough.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The identification of Propensity Overfitting is a novel and important contribution to the field of BLBF.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed self-normalized estimator is theoretically well-motivated and justified. The proofs provided, while referencing existing work, appear sound.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the problem, the proposed solution, and the experimental results.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The extensive experimental evaluation provides strong evidence supporting the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The comparison to the existing POEM algorithm is particularly compelling.
*   **Practicality:**  The implementation details and runtime analysis suggest the proposed method is practical for real-world applications.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experiments are thorough, they are limited to a specific set of datasets and a particular experimental setup.  Exploring different dataset characteristics or bandit feedback scenarios could strengthen the results.
*   **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper mentions hyperparameter calibration but provides limited details about the tuning process. A more detailed discussion of the sensitivity to hyperparameters would be beneficial.
*   **Theoretical Limitations:** The theoretical analysis focuses on asymptotic consistency. Providing tighter bounds or exploring finite-sample guarantees would enhance the theoretical contributions.
*   **Connection to Existing Literature:** The paper mentions the self-normalized estimator being used in finance but doesn’t delve into the potential adaptations or limitations when applying it to the BLBF setting. A more in-depth discussion of these nuances would be valuable.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant and previously unrecognised problem in BLBF.
*   Proposes a theoretically sound and practically effective solution.
*   Demonstrates clear empirical improvements over the state-of-the-art.
*   The writing is exceptionally clear and the paper is well-organized.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The experimental evaluation could be expanded to cover a wider range of scenarios.
*   The theoretical analysis is limited to asymptotic consistency.
*   The discussion of the connection to existing literature could be more in-depth.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of batch learning from logged bandit feedback. The identification of Propensity Overfitting is a novel and important insight, and the proposed self-normalized estimator provides an effective solution. The paper is well-written, theoretically sound, and empirically validated. Despite some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper clearly outweigh its shortcomings. I recommend accepting this paper to NIPS.
