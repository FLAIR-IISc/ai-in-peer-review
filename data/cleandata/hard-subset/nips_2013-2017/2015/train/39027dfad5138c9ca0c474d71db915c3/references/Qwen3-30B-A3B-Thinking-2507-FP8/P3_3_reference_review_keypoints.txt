- Identifies Propensity Overfitting in Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback and proposes a self-normalized estimator plus Norm-POEM.
- Demonstrates with examples that conventional estimators can produce arbitrarily low risk estimates, violating bounded loss assumptions.
- Theorem 2 proves consistency of the self-normalized estimator and explains how it resolves the equivariance problem.
- Connects the estimator to multiplicative control variates/importance sampling and cites relevant literature.
- Empirically, Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets and confirms theoretical claims (e.g., invariance to loss translations).
- Claims practical applicability with minimal changes to existing frameworks (Norm-POEM analogous to POEM).
- Lacks finite-sample bounds or rates of convergence; theoretical guarantees beyond consistency are missing.
- Computational efficiency claim is under-supported—needs wall-clock time and memory comparisons to complement iteration counts.
- Insufficient analysis of behavior under extremely small propensity scores; thresholding treatment is superficial.
- Limited engagement with prior importance sampling techniques (e.g., deeper comparison to Hesterberg’s defensive mixture).
- Overall assessment: significant and well-motivated contribution but would benefit from deeper theoretical analysis for small-sample regimes.
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, builds on POEM, and transparently acknowledges limitations.
- Significance: addresses a fundamental bias in counterfactual learning with clear practical impact for real systems.
- Recommendation: Accept.
