

# Peer Review: "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning"

## Summary

This paper addresses a critical issue in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), a fundamental problem in interactive systems like recommendation engines and ad platforms. The authors identify a previously unrecognized form of overfitting they term "propensity overfitting" that plagues conventional counterfactual risk estimators used in BLBF. They propose a self-normalized risk estimator based on multiplicative control variates that resolves this problem, leading to a new learning algorithm called Norm-POEM. The paper provides theoretical analysis showing the estimator's desirable properties (equivariance, bounded risk estimates) and extensive empirical validation demonstrating consistent improvements over the state-of-the-art POEM algorithm across multiple datasets.

The work builds on prior literature in counterfactual learning (e.g., [5, 1, 6]) and importance sampling (e.g., [19, 22]), but makes a novel contribution by identifying and solving a previously unaddressed issue with conventional estimators. The authors properly contextualize their work within the broader literature on off-policy evaluation in reinforcement learning and causal inference.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Contribution**: The paper makes a strong theoretical contribution by identifying and formally defining "propensity overfitting," a previously unrecognized problem in BLBF. The authors prove that their self-normalized estimator resolves the key anomalies (non-equivariance, unbounded risk estimates) of the conventional estimator, which is a significant theoretical advancement.

2. **Empirical Validation**: The empirical evaluation is thorough and well-designed. The authors use the Supervised 7→ Bandit methodology to test their approach on multiple benchmark datasets, demonstrating consistent improvements across all settings. The ablation studies (Table 2) provide compelling evidence that Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting as theoretically predicted.

3. **Practical Impact**: The paper shows that the improved estimator leads to better generalization performance without significantly increasing computational complexity. The runtime analysis is particularly convincing, showing that Norm-POEM converges faster than POEM despite the increased per-iteration cost.

4. **Clarity and Organization**: The paper is exceptionally well-written, with a clear problem statement, logical flow, and effective use of examples (e.g., Example 1). The connection between the self-normalized estimator and control variates is well-motivated, and the theoretical results are presented with sufficient detail.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Theoretical Analysis of Generalization**: While the paper proves strong consistency of the estimator, it does not provide a formal generalization bound for Norm-POEM. The CRM principle is used, but it's not clear how the self-normalized estimator affects the generalization error bound derived in Theorem 1.

2. **Comparison to Recent Alternatives**: The paper references important work on counterfactual learning but could better position itself against more recent approaches like Doubly Robust estimators [6] or the more recent work on offline RL [13, 14]. The paper should more explicitly address how Norm-POEM compares to these approaches.

3. **Implementation Details**: While the paper mentions that Norm-POEM can be implemented efficiently, the specific computational details for handling large-scale datasets (e.g., the TMC dataset with 30k+ features) are somewhat vague. More concrete information about scalability would strengthen the practical impact.

## Overall Assessment

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of counterfactual learning. The identification of propensity overfitting is a novel insight with practical implications for the field. The proposed solution is theoretically sound, empirically validated, and leads to meaningful improvements in performance.

The paper excels in both theoretical rigor and practical applicability. The authors have clearly identified a gap in the literature, provided a well-motivated solution, and demonstrated its effectiveness through thorough experimentation. The writing is clear and the organization is logical.

While the paper could benefit from more detailed comparison to recent alternatives and a more complete theoretical analysis of generalization bounds, these are minor concerns that don't detract from the paper's overall contribution.

## Recommendation

I strongly recommend acceptance. The paper addresses a real problem with significant practical implications, provides a theoretically sound solution, and demonstrates clear empirical improvements over the state of the art. The work represents a meaningful advancement in the field of counterfactual learning and would be valuable to both researchers and practitioners working with logged bandit feedback.

The paper meets all four criteria for a NeurIPS paper:
- **Quality**: The paper is technically sound with strong theoretical and empirical support.
- **Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized.
- **Originality**: The identification of propensity overfitting and the proposed solution represent a novel contribution.
- **Significance**: The problem addressed is important for real-world applications, and the solution has clear practical value.

This paper advances the state of the art in counterfactual learning and would be a valuable addition to the NeurIPS proceedings.
