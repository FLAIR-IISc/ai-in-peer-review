- Identifies and addresses "Propensity Overfitting" in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and shows it harms generalization.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (inspired by finance) and the Norm-POEM algorithm to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Provides theoretical arguments, including consistency, explaining why the self-normalized estimator is more robust.  
- Empirically validates Norm-POEM with comprehensive experiments showing improved performance and estimator stability across datasets.  
- Connects to prior work in importance sampling, counterfactual estimation, and self-normalization, positioning the contribution relative to CRM and off-policy evaluation.  
- Novel contribution: formal identification and analysis of propensity overfitting as a previously unarticulated problem.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with helpful examples illustrating the problem.  
- Strength: practical relevance for real-world systems like recommenders and advertising.  
- Strength: ablation studies that show variance regularization remains necessary.  
- Weakness: discussion of computational complexity is superficial; needs deeper analysis of bottlenecks (especially variance estimation).  
- Weakness: lacks guidance and sensitivity analysis for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Weakness: experimental scope is limited, focusing mainly on classification rather than structured prediction tasks.  
- Weakness: the equivariance argument, while logical, is not well integrated with the variance-mitigation motivation.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with sound theory, convincing experiments, and high practical impact, deserving acceptance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem identification, theoretically/practically effective solution, strong empirical support, clear writing, and real-world impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential computational concerns at scale, limited experimental scope, and unresolved hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Reviewer rating: Strong Accept (8/10).
