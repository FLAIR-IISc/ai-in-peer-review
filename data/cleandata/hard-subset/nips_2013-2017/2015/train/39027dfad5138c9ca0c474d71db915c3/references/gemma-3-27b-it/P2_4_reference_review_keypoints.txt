- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (Norm-POEM).
- The self-normalized estimator is theoretically analyzed, with Theorem 2 proving strong consistency and links to CRM and importance sampling.
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple datasets.
- Ablation study (Table 3) demonstrates variance regularization remains necessary alongside self-normalization.
- Provides a practical, well-defined algorithm with available implementation and improved computational efficiency.
- Identifies propensity overfitting as a novel and significant problem for learning from logged data.
- Theoretical foundation is sound and well-justified, borrowing and adapting techniques from other fields.
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, conveying key ideas effectively.
- Some theoretical derivations (e.g., variance estimator in Eq. 8) are dense and need clearer exposition.
- Experiments are limited to supervised datasets converted to BLBF; lacks evaluation on real-world logged bandit datasets.
- Hyperparameter calibration (M and λ) and sensitivity analysis are insufficiently detailed; automated tuning strategies not explored.
- Overall assessment: technically strong, significant, and practically useful — reviewer recommends strong accept.
- Pro: important problem, strong theory, consistent empirical gains, clear writing, available implementation.
- Con: accessibility of theory, limited dataset diversity, and incomplete hyperparameter discussion.
