- Paper studies batch learning from logged bandit feedback and identifies "propensity overfitting" in IPS-based CRM methods, proposing SNIPS and Norm-POEM to mitigate it.
- Propensity overfitting: IPS risk can be driven to degenerate values by manipulating importance weights, causing extreme bias and loss-translation non-equivariance.
- Proposal: replace IPS with self-normalized IPS (SNIPS) using multiplicative control variates, prove equivariance, boundedness, and strong consistency, and incorporate a variance regularizer into CRM (Norm-POEM).
- Empirically, Norm-POEM outperforms POEM on multilabel datasets under a supervised-to-bandit protocol, shows robustness to loss translations, and better calibrates the sum of weights.
- Connects to existing importance sampling and OPE literature; novelty is in diagnosing propensity overfitting in BLBF and operationalizing SNIPS within CRM rather than inventing a new estimator.
- Strength: Clear identification and simple illustrative example of propensity overfitting distinct from loss overfitting.
- Strength: Well-motivated adoption of SNIPS—restores equivariance, bounds empirical risk within loss range, and targets degeneracy via E[Ŝ]=1 identity.
- Strength: Practical algorithmic integration (Norm-POEM) with a justified approximate variance estimator for regularization.
- Strength: Consistent empirical gains, robustness checks (loss translation), improved Ŝ calibration, and competitive runtime; writing is clear and structured.
- Weakness: Theoretical contribution is modest—properties are mostly known and only asymptotic; no new finite-sample generalization bound for SNIPS-CRM.
- Weakness: Variance estimator is asymptotic and relies on clipping; no analysis of bias-variance trade-off from self-normalization plus clipping in learning.
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to doubly robust (DR) methods and weighted/normalized DR, leaving open whether normalization or model-based control variates drive gains.
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to supervised-to-bandit simulations with a CRF logging policy and no real logged data or broader tasks; limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: Minor notational inconsistencies and small typos (e.g., δ ranges, mixing loss/reward language, loss range typo).
- Quality assessment: Technically sound diagnosis and application of SNIPS, but learning-theory support is incomplete and experiments lack broader baselines.
- Clarity assessment: Clear exposition and reproducible algorithmic description, with some minor clarity issues to fix.
- Originality assessment: Incremental—estimator not new, but contribution lies in identifying a practical failure mode and integrating SNIPS into CRM.
- Significance assessment: Practical and likely adoptable due to addressing an important failure mode, though tempered by limited theory and comparisons.
- Suggestions: Provide finite-sample bounds or concentration results for SNIPS-CRM; compare to DR-based learners and normalized DR variants.
- Suggestions: Add sensitivity analyses for clipping M and regularizer λ, ablations for normalization/clipping/variance regularization, and report run variability/confidence intervals.
- Suggestions: Fix notational inconsistencies and unify loss-range assumptions.
- Pros summary: Identifies a real failure mode, provides a simple principled fix (SNIPS), and demonstrates consistent empirical improvements with modest computation cost.
- Cons summary: Limited novelty in estimator, main theory is asymptotic without new finite-sample bounds, missing DR comparisons, and evaluation scope is narrow.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — practical and useful contribution despite incremental theory and limited experimental breadth.
