- Identifies a new problem, "Propensity Overfitting," where conventional counterfactual risk estimators overfit by exploiting propensity weights.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator that avoids the identified anomalies (boundedness and equivariance properties).  
- Derives Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm that uses the self-normalized estimator.  
- Provides theoretical support (Theorem 2) proving consistency of the self-normalized estimator.  
- Connects the solution to multiplicative control variates / importance sampling techniques (cites Hesterberg).  
- Presents clear motivating examples (Example 1) that illustrate the severity of propensity overfitting.  
- Carefully compares to prior work (POEM) using the Supervised 7→ Bandit methodology to isolate estimator effects.  
- Empirical validation is extensive and convincing: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets and training sizes.  
- Acknowledges that variance regularization is still necessary despite normalization (Table 3).  
- Notes computational trade-offs: normalization increases per-iteration cost but can speed overall convergence.  
- Weaknesses: lacks finite-sample bounds or detailed error-rate comparisons and has limited computational-cost analysis.  
- Experiments are restricted to classification datasets; broader validation would strengthen claims.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept for NeurIPS due to novel insight, solid theory, and consistent empirical gains.
