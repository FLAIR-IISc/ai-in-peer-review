- Identifies propensity overfitting: IPS inside ERM can be exploited to drive empirical risk estimates outside true loss range or be sensitive to loss translations.
- Proposes replacing IPS with self-normalized IPS (SNIPS) as a multiplicative control variate to enforce sample propensity sum near its expectation.
- Shows SNIPS is bounded, equivariant to loss translations, and strongly consistent.
- Instantiates Norm-POEM by plugging SNIPS and an approximate variance estimator into CRM and optimizing an exponential-family hypothesis with L-BFGS.
- Experiments on multi-label datasets show improved generalization, robustness vs POEM, reduced sensitivity to loss translation, better calibration of propensity sums, and competitive runtime.
- Situates work in OPE and CRM literature; SNIPS itself is well-known but its use within CRM to avoid propensity overfitting is the novel contribution.
- Draws connections to variance reduction techniques (clipping), additive control variates (DR), and empirical-Bernstein bounds.
- Technical solidity: clear articulation and motivating example for propensity overfitting; proofs of SNIPS properties are correct and relevant.
- Theoretical limitation: main consistency result is standard and no finite-sample generalization/bias-variance bounds for SNIPS-based CRM are provided.
- Regularization relies on an approximate ratio variance estimator without high-probability guarantees.
- Interplay between clipping and normalization (bias/variance trade-offs) is not analyzed theoretically.
- Experiments are well executed (supervised-to-bandit protocol, multiple datasets, diagnostics), and results align with theoretical claims.
- Empirical comparisons omit doubly robust (DR) learning baselines and DR+SNIPS variants, which are directly relevant competitors.
- Clarity and reproducibility are strong: paper is well written, example and derivations are clear, algorithmic details and code provided; some experimental details could be expanded.
- Originality is incremental: SNIPS is not new, but identifying propensity overfitting and integrating SNIPS into CRM with variance regularization is a useful practical contribution.
- Significance: practical impact for BLBF and off-policy learning is high due to simplicity and consistent empirical gains; theoretical novelty is limited.
- Suggested improvements: provide finite-sample bounds or bias/variance analysis, compare to DR baselines, analyze clipping sensitivity and heavy-tailed regimes, consider Ŝ(h) constraints, and include optimization/scale ablations.
- Overall assessment: well-executed and practically relevant paper with consistent empirical gains; leans toward acceptance despite limited new theory and missing DR comparisons (score 7/10).
