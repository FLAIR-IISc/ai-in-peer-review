## Review of "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses a critical issue in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF): propensity overfitting. The authors demonstrate that standard counterfactual risk estimators, while theoretically sound, are prone to producing severely biased risk estimates, leading to poor generalization. They propose a novel “self-normalized” risk estimator, inspired by techniques from financial mathematics and reinforcement learning, which mitigates this problem.  The authors provide both theoretical justification (showing consistency) and empirical validation across several datasets, demonstrating improved robustness and generalization performance compared to existing methods (specifically, POEM).

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds directly on the work of Swaminathan & Joachims (2016) on counterfactual risk minimization for BLBF. It correctly positions itself within the broader context of causal inference, off-policy evaluation, and domain adaptation, drawing connections to propensity score matching, importance sampling, and related variance reduction techniques. The authors acknowledge prior work on doubly-robust estimators and highlight how their approach differs, focusing on a multiplicative control variate that directly addresses the propensity overfitting issue. The reference to Hesterberg (2005) regarding self-normalization is key and appropriately placed. 

**Strengths:**

* **Identification of a Novel Problem:** The paper clearly articulates and formalizes “propensity overfitting,” a previously unrecognized issue in BLBF. The examples provided are insightful and convincingly demonstrate the potential for the standard estimator to produce misleading results.
* **Elegant Solution:** The proposed self-normalized estimator is a clever and theoretically grounded solution. Its connection to established techniques (self-normalization in importance sampling) enhances its credibility.
* **Strong Theoretical Justification:** The authors provide a consistency guarantee for the estimator, demonstrating its asymptotic correctness. 
* **Comprehensive Empirical Evaluation:** The experimental setup is thorough, following the established “Supervised → Bandit” methodology. The consistently superior performance of Norm-POEM across different datasets and replay counts is compelling.
* **Clear and Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written. The notation is consistent, and the explanations are accessible.
* **Practicality:** The authors show that, despite the increased per-iteration cost, the improved convergence properties of Norm-POEM make it computationally efficient in practice.



**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Discussion of Practical Implications of Variance Estimation:** While the authors acknowledge the complexity of estimating the variance for the CRM objective, they don’t delve deeply into practical approximations or efficient computation strategies beyond mentioning L-BFGS. A more detailed discussion of scalable variance estimation methods would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance of Norm-POEM still relies on careful tuning of hyperparameters (M and lambda). The paper acknowledges this, but a more detailed sensitivity analysis or guidance on hyperparameter selection would strengthen the work.
* **Assumption of Full Support:** The assumption of full support for the historical policy (h0) is crucial for the theoretical guarantees. While standard in this literature, the paper could briefly discuss the implications of violating this assumption in practice.  What happens if h0 isn’t truly full support?  
* **Equivariance Argument Could be Strengthened:** While the authors state the estimator is equivariant, the explanation feels slightly hand-wavy. A more formal demonstration of this property would be valuable.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that addresses a significant and previously overlooked problem in batch learning from bandit feedback. The self-normalized estimator is a clever and effective solution, supported by both theoretical analysis and compelling empirical results. The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation, methodology, and implications of the work. 

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Solves a significant and novel problem with clear theoretical and empirical support.
* The proposed solution is elegant and well-motivated.
* The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate a clear improvement over existing methods.
* The paper is well-written and accessible.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The discussion of practical variance estimation is limited.
* Some aspects of the theory (e.g., equivariance) could be strengthened.



**Recommendation:**

**Strong Accept.**  This paper is a valuable contribution to the field and represents a significant advance in counterfactual learning. The weaknesses are relatively minor and do not detract from the overall quality of the work. I believe it is well-suited for presentation at NIPS.



**Confidence:** 9/10
