

# Review of "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning"

## Summary
This paper addresses a critical problem in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), a setting crucial for improving interactive systems like recommender systems and ad platforms. The authors identify a previously unrecognized issue they term "Propensity Overfitting" that plagues conventional counterfactual risk estimators, causing severe biases in empirical risk minimization. They propose a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates that provably avoids these degeneracies. The authors derive a new learning algorithm (Norm-POEM) based on this estimator and demonstrate its superior performance through extensive experiments on multiple benchmark datasets.

## Strengths
1. **Novel Problem Identification**: The identification of Propensity Overfitting as a distinct problem from conventional Loss Overfitting is a significant theoretical contribution. The paper effectively demonstrates this problem through well-constructed examples and empirical evidence.

2. **Theoretical Rigor**: The paper provides strong theoretical guarantees for the self-normalized estimator, including a proof of strong consistency (Theorem 2) and clear explanation of how it resolves the anomalies of the conventional estimator.

3. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The empirical evaluation is thorough, with experiments across multiple datasets, ablation studies, and careful analysis of how the estimator behaves under different conditions (including loss translation). The results consistently show Norm-POEM outperforming prior methods.

4. **Practical Impact**: The paper directly addresses a real-world problem with clear implications for industry applications. The authors provide practical implementation details and demonstrate that Norm-POEM can be efficiently implemented with existing optimization techniques.

5. **Clear Writing and Organization**: The paper is well-structured, with clear explanations of both the problem and the solution. The mathematical derivations are carefully presented and easy to follow.

## Weaknesses
1. **Theoretical Limitations**: While the paper proves strong consistency of the self-normalized estimator, it doesn't fully address the behavior when the denominator in Equation (7) approaches zero, which could occur in practice. A more detailed analysis of the estimator's variance under various conditions would strengthen the theoretical contribution.

2. **Scalability Considerations**: The paper mentions computational efficiency but doesn't thoroughly address how Norm-POEM scales to extremely large datasets (e.g., billion-scale ad platforms). While the runtime is comparable to POEM, the paper could better quantify this for real-world applications.

3. **Comparison with Related Work**: While the paper references self-normalized importance sampling, it doesn't sufficiently contrast its approach with recent work on variance reduction techniques for counterfactual estimation, such as doubly robust estimators. A more comprehensive comparison would better position the contribution.

4. **Assumption of Full Support**: The paper assumes the logging policy has full support over Y, which is a strong assumption that may not hold in many practical settings. The authors should discuss how their method would behave when this assumption is violated.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with well-supported claims. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the empirical evaluation is comprehensive. The authors are careful to evaluate both strengths and weaknesses of their approach, particularly noting that variance regularization remains beneficial even with the self-normalized estimator.

## Clarity
The paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized. The problem statement, theoretical contribution, and experimental results are presented logically and accessibly. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-explained. The paper provides sufficient detail for experts to reproduce the results.

## Originality
The paper makes a significant original contribution by identifying Propensity Overfitting as a distinct problem in BLBF and proposing a novel solution using multiplicative control variates. While self-normalized importance sampling has been used in other contexts, the application to BLBF and the specific focus on resolving Propensity Overfitting is novel.

## Significance
The significance of this work is high. Propensity Overfitting represents a fundamental limitation in current counterfactual learning methods that has not been previously recognized. The proposed solution directly addresses this limitation and demonstrates clear improvements in generalization performance across multiple datasets. This work has immediate practical implications for improving real-world interactive systems.

## Recommendation
I recommend acceptance of this paper. It makes a clear, significant contribution to the field of counterfactual learning with strong theoretical foundations and compelling empirical evidence. The paper is well-written and addresses an important problem that has practical implications for industry applications.

## Suggestions for Improvement
1. Expand the theoretical analysis to address the behavior when the denominator in Equation (7) approaches zero, which could be a practical concern.

2. Include a more detailed discussion of how the method handles cases where the logging policy does not have full support over Y.

3. Add a comparison with recent variance reduction techniques for counterfactual estimation, such as doubly robust estimators, to better position the contribution.

4. Include a brief analysis of the scalability of the method to extremely large datasets, which would be valuable for industry adoption.

The paper is strong and makes a valuable contribution to the field. With the suggested improvements, it would be an excellent addition to the NeurIPS proceedings.
