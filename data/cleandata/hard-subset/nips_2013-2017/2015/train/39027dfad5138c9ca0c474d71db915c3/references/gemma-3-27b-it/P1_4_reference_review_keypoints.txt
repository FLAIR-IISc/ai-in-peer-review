- Paper addresses batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and identifies a novel failure mode called "Propensity Overfitting."  
- Proposes a Self-Normalized Risk Estimator using multiplicative control variates to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing consistency and robustness of the self-normalized estimator, connecting to existing results (e.g., Maurer & Pontil).  
- Translates the estimator into a practical algorithm (Norm-POEM) and implements it with standard optimization (L-BFGS).  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple datasets and h0 policy settings.  
- Ablation studies indicate variance regularization remains necessary even with self-normalization.  
- Positions the work well within causal inference, off-policy evaluation, and prior counterfactual estimators, citing comprehensive related work.  
- Novelty: identifies a specific overfitting mode and applies self-normalization from other fields into BLBF.  
- Paper is generally clear, well-organized, and includes publicly available code for reproducibility.  
- Theoretical sections are complex; the link between variance regularization and propensity overfitting could be clearer and more accessible.  
- Assumes a stationary logging policy with full support; sensitivity to violations of these assumptions is not thoroughly discussed.  
- Computational cost may be higher in very large-scale or complex hypothesis spaces despite claimed comparable runtime to POEM.  
- Experiments are limited to stochastic linear hypothesis spaces, leaving generalization to more complex models uncertain.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; recommendation: Strong Accept, with suggested clarifications on theory, assumptions, and computational trade-offs.
