- Identifies propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF): standard counterfactual estimators can give severely biased risk estimates and poor generalization.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (Norm-POEM), inspired by importance-sampling self-normalization, to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Provides theoretical justification showing consistency/asymptotic correctness of the estimator; claims equivariance but the argument could be formalized further.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive (Supervised → Bandit protocol) and shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across datasets and replay counts.  
- Places work in context of Swaminathan & Joachims (2016), causal inference, off-policy evaluation, importance sampling, and doubly-robust methods; cites Hesterberg (2005) appropriately.  
- Strength: clearly formalizes a novel problem with insightful examples demonstrating the failure modes of standard estimators.  
- Strength: presents an elegant, theoretically grounded solution with practical computational efficiency despite higher per-iteration cost.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical and scalable variance estimation methods beyond mentioning L-BFGS.  
- Weakness: performance depends on careful tuning of hyperparameters (M and λ); lacks detailed sensitivity analysis or guidance.  
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees assume full support of the historical policy (h0); the paper should discuss implications when this assumption is violated.  
- Weakness: equivariance claim is somewhat hand-wavy and would benefit from a more formal demonstration.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-written paper that makes a significant contribution to counterfactual learning; reviewer recommends Strong Accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
