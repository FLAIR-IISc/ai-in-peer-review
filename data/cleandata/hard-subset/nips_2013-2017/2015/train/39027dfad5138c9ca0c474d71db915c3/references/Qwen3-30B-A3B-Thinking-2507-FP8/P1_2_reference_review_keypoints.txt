- Paper identifies "Propensity Overfitting" in standard counterfactual risk estimators causing arbitrarily biased risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates to fix the problem.  
- Presents Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Theoretical results: estimator is consistent, equivariant, bounded, and resolves anomalies of conventional estimators (Theorem 2).  
- Conceptual distinction and motivation between Propensity Overfitting and Loss Overfitting.  
- Empirical evaluation: extensive experiments across multiple datasets show Norm-POEM outperforms POEM and avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Demonstrates necessity of variance regularization and faster convergence of Norm-POEM versus POEM.  
- Situates work within BLBF literature and links to prior control variate methods.  
- Practical impact: immediately applicable to systems using logged bandit feedback (ads, recommenders).  
- Lacks detailed computational complexity analysis and discussion of scalability for large datasets.  
- Paper does not analyze sensitivity of performance to hyperparameters M and λ.  
- Limited comparison with other variance-reduction techniques (e.g., additive control variates, doubly robust estimators).  
- Experiments rely on supervised→bandit conversion; no validation on genuine real-world bandit logs.  
- Does not explore theoretical or practical scenarios where the self-normalized estimator may not help.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, reproducible, and significant contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept—strong theoretical and empirical contributions outweigh minor gaps.
