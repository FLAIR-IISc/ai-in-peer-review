- Summary: Tackles batch learning from logged bandit feedback, identifies "Propensity Overfitting," and proposes a self-normalized estimator using multiplicative control variates with theoretical and empirical support.

- Relation to Previous Work: Well-situated in causal inference and off-policy evaluation literature, clearly linked to Dudík/Bottou/POEM and the self-normalized importance-sampling literature.

- Novelty: Identifies a previously unrecognized and important issue (Propensity Overfitting) in BLBF.

- Theoretical Rigor: Provides clear theoretical justification and a concise proof (Theorem 2) showing improved properties.

- Empirical Validation: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple datasets, with analysis of Ŝ(h) supporting claims.

- Clarity of Explanation: Paper is well-written and explains the estimator and intuition clearly.

- Practical Relevance: Problem is highly relevant to real-world logged-data applications and computational efficiency is discussed.

- Equivariance Property: Demonstrates estimator’s equivariance to loss translations as a useful theoretical/practical insight.

- Computational Complexity (Weakness): Claim of POEM-comparable runtime is undermined by harder variance estimation (Eq. 8); needs more detailed cost analysis and comparison to other variance-reduction methods.

- Hyperparameter Sensitivity (Weakness): Requires more detailed sensitivity analysis for clipping parameter M and regularization λ and robustness to suboptimal settings.

- Limited Experimental Scope (Weakness): Experiments rely mainly on LibSVM datasets and should include more realistic domains (e.g., recommender systems, advertising).

- Full Support Assumption (Weakness): Assumes historical policy h0 has full support but does not analyze practical consequences when this is violated.

- Theoretical Link to CRM (Weakness): Needs clearer exposition of how the self-normalized estimator tightens CRM-derived bounds (Theorem 1).

- Arguments For Acceptance: Significant, novel identification of a problem with a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution that is well-presented and impactful.

- Arguments Against Acceptance: Incomplete analysis of computational cost, limited dataset diversity, and insufficient investigation of hyperparameter robustness.

- Overall Assessment: Strong contribution with minor weaknesses that do not outweigh the paper’s strengths.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing computational complexity, hyperparameter sensitivity, and broader experiments.
