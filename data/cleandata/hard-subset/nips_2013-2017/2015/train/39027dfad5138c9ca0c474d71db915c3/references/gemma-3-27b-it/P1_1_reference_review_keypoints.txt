- Tackles batch learning from logged bandit feedback and identifies a novel issue termed "Propensity Overfitting."
- Proposes a Self-Normalized Risk Estimator (SNRE) inspired by control variates to mitigate propensity overfitting.
- Provides theoretical analysis showing improved properties of SNRE, including a consistency guarantee (Theorem 2).
- Empirically demonstrates Norm-POEM (using SNRE) outperforms POEM across multiple benchmarks and converges faster.
- Ablation studies show the necessity of variance regularization for performance gains.
- Positions the work within BLBF, counterfactual estimation, importance sampling, and related causal/doma in adaptation literature.
- Highlights the novelty and significance of formalizing "Propensity Overfitting."
- Paper is well-written with helpful examples illustrating the problem and clear technical exposition.
- Releases code for Norm-POEM, aiding reproducibility.
- Notes an equivariance property of SNRE under loss translation that reduces bias concerns.
- Lacks detailed computational complexity analysis, especially regarding variance estimation and why it converges faster.
- Does not thoroughly analyze sensitivity to hyperparameters (M and λ) or robustness to sub-optimal tuning.
- Comparisons are mainly against POEM; broader baselines (e.g., doubly robust estimators) are missing.
- Theoretical guarantees assume full support of the logging policy; practical implications of relaxing this are not discussed.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with solid theory and experiments; recommendation to accept (confidence 8/10).
