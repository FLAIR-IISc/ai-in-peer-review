- Paper identifies "Propensity Overfitting" in BLBF where conventional IPS estimators can degenerate under overfitting.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates to avoid this degeneracy.
- Derives Norm-POEM, replacing POEM’s estimator with the self-normalized version.
- Theoretical results show the estimator is equivariant, bounded, and provably avoids propensity overfitting.
- Empirical results across benchmarks show Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM (Table 1, Fig. 1).
- Strong clarity and organization; examples effectively illustrate the new phenomenon.
- Experimental design follows established Supervised→Bandit methodology with thorough ablation studies.
- Practical impact: the estimator provides robustness to loss translations and mitigates propensity overfitting.
- Novelty concern: self-normalized estimators are established in importance sampling; contribution is mainly applying them to BLBF.
- Empirical scope is limited to LibSVM multi-label datasets; lacking real-world ad/recommendation benchmarks.
- Computational efficiency claim is vague and unsupported by concrete runtime/iteration comparisons.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (M and λ) is under-discussed; no analysis of robustness to those choices.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution and solid evidence; recommended for acceptance.
- Suggested future work: test on more diverse/real-world datasets and provide concrete efficiency and hyperparameter sensitivity analyses.
