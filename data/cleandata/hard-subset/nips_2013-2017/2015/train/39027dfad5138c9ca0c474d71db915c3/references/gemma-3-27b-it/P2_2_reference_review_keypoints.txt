- Identifies propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) as a critical issue.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (Norm-POEM) to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Provides theoretical analysis linking to importance sampling and control variates; Theorem 2 establishes consistency.  
- Empirical validation shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across several benchmarks.  
- Includes analysis (Table 2) demonstrating the practical impact of propensity overfitting.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and well-organized.  
- Norm-POEM is a practical, simple modification to existing BLBF algorithms with comparable runtime.  
- Core technique (self-normalization) is known from importance sampling; novelty is in its application to BLBF.  
- Variance estimation (Equation 8) may increase computational complexity; scalability needs more analysis.  
- Hyperparameter tuning (M and lambda) is only briefly discussed; sensitivity analysis is missing.  
- Discussion of related work, especially doubly robust estimators, is cursory and could be expanded.  
- Quality: technically sound with strong empirical support.  
- Clarity: excellent presentation and exposition.  
- Originality: application and problem identification are novel, though technique itself is not entirely new.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and offers a practically adoptable solution.  
- Acceptance arguments: highlights a previously unrecognized problem, offers theoretical and empirical solution, and has high adoption potential.  
- Rejection concerns: limited novelty of the core technique, potential computational cost, and insufficient hyperparameter/related-work discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept, with request to address computational complexity and hyperparameter sensitivity in the final version.
