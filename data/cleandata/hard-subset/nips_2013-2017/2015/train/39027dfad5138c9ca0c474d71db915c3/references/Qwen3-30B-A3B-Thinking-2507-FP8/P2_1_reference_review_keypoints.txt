- Identifies "Propensity Overfitting" in BLBF where conventional counterfactual risk estimators can yield arbitrarily low risk due to propensity weights.  
- Proposes a Self-Normalized Risk Estimator using multiplicative control variates that is consistent and avoids anomalies of the conventional estimator.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator, and shows it outperforms POEM in experiments.  
- Paper is technically sound with solid theoretical support (e.g., Theorem 2) and a compelling illustrative Example 1 demonstrating the problem.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and reproducible, using the Supervised 7→ Bandit protocol across multiple datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL).  
- Results consistently show statistically significant improvements of Norm-POEM over POEM and faster convergence with smaller weight norms.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting, with the self-normalized estimator Ŝ(h) remaining close to its expected value of 1.  
- The paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, with precise mathematical exposition and consistent notation.  
- Original contribution: names and formalizes Propensity Overfitting and novel application of self-normalized estimators to counterfactual BLBF.  
- Significance: addresses a critical practical problem for recommendation/ad systems with an elegant, implementable fix that is computationally efficient.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of bias–variance tradeoff of the self-normalized estimator versus clipped unbiased estimators.  
- Weakness: theoretical focus on consistency without detailed bias/variance comparison, and minimal discussion of sensitivity to the clipping parameter M.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — significant, rigorous, and practically relevant contribution to counterfactual learning.
