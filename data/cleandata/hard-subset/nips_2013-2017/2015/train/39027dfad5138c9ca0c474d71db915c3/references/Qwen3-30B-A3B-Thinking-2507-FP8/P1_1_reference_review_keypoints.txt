- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback and proposes a fix.
- Introduces a self-normalized risk estimator using multiplicative control variates and the Norm-POEM algorithm.
- Proves desirable theoretical properties (equivariance, bounded risk estimates, strong consistency).
- Empirically outperforms POEM across multiple benchmark datasets using Supervised 7→ Bandit methodology.
- Ablation studies support the claim that Norm-POEM avoids propensity overfitting as predicted.
- Shows practical benefits: better generalization and faster convergence without large runtime penalties.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and situates itself in prior counterfactual learning and importance-sampling literature.
- Weakness: lacks a formal generalization bound for Norm-POEM and unclear effect on CRM-derived bounds.
- Weakness: comparisons to recent alternatives (e.g., Doubly Robust estimators, newer offline RL work) are insufficient.
- Weakness: implementation/scalability details for very large feature sets (e.g., TMC dataset) are vague.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel, practically significant work with strong theoretical and empirical support.
- Recommendation: strong accept.
