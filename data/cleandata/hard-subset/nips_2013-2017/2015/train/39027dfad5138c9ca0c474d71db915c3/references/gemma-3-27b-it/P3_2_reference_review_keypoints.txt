- Presents propensity overfitting, a novel bias in counterfactual risk estimation for learning from logged bandit feedback.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator using multiplicative control variates to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (consistency) for the proposed estimator.  
- Empirically demonstrates improved performance and robustness over POEM across several datasets.  
- Connects to prior work in propensity scoring, importance sampling, doubly-robust estimators, and self-normalization techniques from finance and RL.  
- Properly cites foundational and recent relevant literature, including POEM, Bottou et al., Dudík et al., Thomas et al., and Li et al.  
- Clearly written, well-organized paper with consistent notation and accessible technical details.  
- Code is available, supporting reproducibility.  
- Identifies and clearly illustrates the propensity overfitting problem with illustrative examples.  
- The self-normalized estimator is elegant, mathematically sound, and has desirable properties like equivariance.  
- Strong theoretical foundation complements the empirical results.  
- Experiments convincingly show advantages, particularly when propensity overfitting is severe.  
- Computational complexity and practical overhead of variance estimation are not fully analyzed.  
- Performance may be sensitive to the clipping parameter M; guidance on choosing M is limited.  
- Experimental scope is limited (primarily LibSVM datasets); more real-world log data would strengthen claims.  
- Unclear why variance regularization remains necessary despite the improved estimator; deeper discussion is lacking.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, offers a solid theoretical and practical solution, and includes reproducible code.  
- Against acceptance: potential practical limitations due to computational cost, parameter sensitivity, and limited experimental diversity.  
- Overall assessment: strong, influential contribution deserving acceptance (Rating: Accept Strongly).
