1. Identifies a new failure mode called "Propensity Overfitting" where IPS-based learners can manipulate importance weights to achieve artificially low estimated risk.  
2. Shows that complex hypothesis spaces enable degenerate solutions that generalize poorly by exploiting IPS weights.  
3. Proposes replacing the unbiased IPS estimator with the self-normalized (SN) estimator as a principled fix.  
4. Motivates the SN estimator as a multiplicative control variate from Monte Carlo literature.  
5. Proves SN estimator is equivariant to constant loss shifts and its estimates are bounded within observed loss range.  
6. Argues these SN properties provably prevent catastrophic propensity overfitting.  
7. Introduces Norm-POEM: integrates the SN estimator with a variance regularizer in the CRM framework for structured prediction.  
8. Empirical results on multi-label tasks show Norm-POEM consistently and significantly outperforms POEM.  
9. Experiments use the supervised→bandit protocol and include diagnostics that directly confirm theoretical claims (bias in propensity sums, sensitivity to loss translation).  
10. Contribution is high-impact for BLBF and industrial applications (recommendation, advertising).  
11. Solution is simple, elegant, and well-justified theoretically.  
12. Paper is exceptionally clear, with a strong illustrative example and flawless logical flow.  
13. Minor weakness: variance estimator (Eq. 8) is a delta-method approximation and could use brief discussion or citations on its quality.  
14. Minor weakness: computational aspects—variance estimator does not admit stochastic optimization and relies on L-BFGS, raising potential large-scale limitations.  
15. Overall recommendation: strong accept; caveat that the SN estimator itself is not new, though its application here is novel and impactful.
