- Identifies "Propensity Overfitting," where high-capacity models game importance weights to get artificially low risk estimates.
- Proposes using a self-normalized importance sampling estimator from Monte Carlo literature to counteract this pathology.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, integrating the self-normalized estimator into the CRM framework and improving on prior work like POEM.
- Significance/originality: exposes a subtle, severe vulnerability in BLBF and offers an elegant, principled remedy.
- Quality: provides an intuitive example, rigorous theoretical analysis (equivariance and consistency), and thorough empirical validation.
- Empirics: experiments across multiple datasets show superior generalization and avoidance of catastrophic failures of previous methods.
- Clarity: manuscript is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed discussion of computational trade-offs—self-normalized estimator is more complex per iteration despite claimed faster convergence.
- Weakness: experiments rely on supervised-to-bandit conversion; needs discussion of challenges with real-world, non-stationary logged data.
- Overall: outstanding, well-validated contribution that meaningfully advances off-policy learning.
