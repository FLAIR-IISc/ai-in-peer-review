- Important problem: Identifies propensity overfitting in counterfactual risk estimators as a novel and significant issue for BLBF.  
- Clear explanation: Uses an illustrative example to make the propensity overfitting intuition accessible.  
- Theoretical strength: Introduces a self-normalized estimator with strong theoretical motivation and a consistency proof.  
- Connection to literature: Relates the estimator to control variates and importance sampling.  
- Empirical validation: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple multi-label datasets, with supportive ablations and runtime comparisons.  
- Writing and organization: Paper is well-written, logically organized, and clearly connects to related work.  
- Limited experiment scope: Experiments focus mainly on multi-label classification, limiting claims about generality to other domains (regression, ranking).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient analysis and detail on robustness to hyperparameter choices.  
- Computational complexity: Need for more precise discussion/analysis of the computational cost, especially variance estimation.  
- Missing literature links: Does not deeply connect to broader robust estimation work applied to importance sampling.  
- Intro suggestion: Could strengthen motivation by noting real-world impacts (e.g., ad placement).  
- Section strengths: CRM recap is helpful; Section 4 example effectively demonstrates the problem.  
- Method presentation: Derivation of self-normalized estimator and Norm-POEM description are clear and well-presented.  
- Experimental suggestion: Add error bars to performance plots to show variability; Ŝ(ĥ) table is informative.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a significant unrecognized problem, offers a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution, and is clearly presented.  
- Arguments for rejection: Limited experimental domains, incomplete hyperparameter and complexity analyses, and weaker ties to robust estimation literature.  
- Recommendation: Overall a significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments and clarify hyperparameter/complexity analysis.
