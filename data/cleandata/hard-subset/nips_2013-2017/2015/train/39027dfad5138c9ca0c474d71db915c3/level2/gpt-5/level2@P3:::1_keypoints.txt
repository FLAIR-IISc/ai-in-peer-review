- Paper studies BLBF in CRM and identifies propensity overfitting in IPS estimators.  
- Proposes replacing IPS with SNIPS (self-normalized IS) to bound risks and ensure equivariance to loss shifts.  
- Shows SNIPS yields empirical risk as a convex combination of observed losses and provides a strong consistency result.  
- Derives Norm-POEM: a CRM-style algorithm for exponential-family policies with an approximate variance penalty for the ratio estimator.  
- Experiments (supervised-to-bandit CRF logging) show consistent improvements over IPS-based POEM and robustness to loss translations.  
- SNIPS stabilizes the diagnostic Ŝ(h) near 1 and shows competitive runtime and fewer optimization pathologies.  
- Builds on prior CRM/POEM, importance weighting literature, and OPE estimators; SNIPS itself is a known estimator applied here to CRM.  
- Main conceptual contribution is diagnosing propensity overfitting in learning and integrating SNIPS into CRM with variance penalization.  
- Strength: clear articulation and examples of IPS failure modes (non-equivariance, unbounded overfitting).  
- Strength: self-normalization addresses boundedness, equivariance, and yields strong consistency (Theorem 2).  
- Strength: seamless integration into CRM with a practical approximate variance estimator.  
- Strength: empirical evidence across datasets showing improved generalization and robustness across data sizes and replay counts.  
- Weakness: lacks a finite-sample generalization bound for SNIPS-based CRM analogous to IPS results.  
- Weakness: variance penalty relies on asymptotic approximation for a ratio estimator; finite-sample concentration results are needed.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to doubly robust (DR) and clipped-DR baselines for learning and evaluation.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to supervised-to-bandit simulations; needs experiments with non-uniform logging policies or real logged data.  
- Weakness: objective is non-convex and not amenable to stochastic optimization; more details on initialization, convergence, and scaling are needed.  
- Weakness: sensitivity analyses for hyperparameters (λ, M) and optimization choices are lacking.  
- Caveat: self-normalization does not eliminate variance blow-up under poor overlap; clipping and model selection remain necessary.  
- Quality: technical claims are largely sound; strong consistency holds, but finite-sample theory is missing.  
- Clarity: well written and organized; variance approximation is cited but would benefit from a brief derivation or conditions for reliability.  
- Originality: using SNIPS in CRM is novel in diagnosing and mitigating propensity overfitting for structured prediction.  
- Significance: simple, drop-in estimator change with practical impact likely to influence practice and follow-up research.  
- Arguments for acceptance: identifies an important failure mode and offers a principled, empirically validated fix that integrates into CRM.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks finite-sample bounds, DR baselines, and real-world/logging-policy-diverse evaluations.  
- Recommended improvements: add finite-sample bounds, include DR/clipped-DR baselines, perform sensitivity analyses, detail optimization/scaling, and temper absolute claims.
