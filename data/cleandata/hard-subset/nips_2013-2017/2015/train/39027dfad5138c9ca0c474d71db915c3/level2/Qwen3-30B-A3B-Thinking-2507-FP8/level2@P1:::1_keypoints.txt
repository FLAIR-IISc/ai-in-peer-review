- Identifies propensity overfitting in inverse propensity weighting for CRM in BLBF, causing arbitrarily biased risk estimates.  
- Proves bias can occur even with bounded loss functions when optimizing over complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (R̂SN(h)) using multiplicative control variates to fix the issue.  
- Shows the self-normalized estimator maintains equivariance under loss translation and guarantees bounded risk estimates.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM algorithm for structured prediction based on the new estimator.  
- Empirically validates Norm-POEM on multi-label classification datasets, showing consistent improvement over POEM.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant and rigorously analyzes a previously unrecognized problem.  
- Equation 7 (self-normalized estimator) elegantly resolves anomalies of the conventional estimator, supported by Theorem 2.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and demonstrates avoidance of propensity overfitting (Tables/Figures cited).  
- Demonstrates robustness under loss translations and maintains computational feasibility.  
- Connects the method to control variates in importance sampling, aligning with established techniques.  
- Lacks clear contextualization of novelty relative to prior self-normalized estimators in importance sampling literature.  
- Empirical section could better address scalability to larger datasets and longer training runs (runtime vs. absolute scalability).  
- Does not explore whether the self-normalized estimator enables more aggressive variance regularization strategies.  
- Suggested improvements: explicitly clarify novelty versus prior Monte Carlo uses, expand runtime/scale experiments, and strengthen related-work citations.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, impactful, and recommended for acceptance due to robust theoretical and empirical contributions.
