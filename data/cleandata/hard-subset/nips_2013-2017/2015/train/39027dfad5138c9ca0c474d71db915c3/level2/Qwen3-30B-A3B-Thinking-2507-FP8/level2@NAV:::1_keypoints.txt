- Identifies "propensity overfitting" as a significant issue with conventional inverse propensity estimators in BLBF.  
- Shows standard inverse propensity estimator produces biased risk estimates under ERM, especially for complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator that provably avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Introduces the Norm-POEM algorithm for structured prediction based on the self-normalized estimator.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical proofs demonstrating consistency and resolution of propensity overfitting.  
- Empirical evaluation on multiple multi-label datasets supports theoretical claims with consistent generalization improvements over POEM.  
- Experiments are well-designed using Supervised 7→ Bandit methodology and validate that gains stem from handling propensity overfitting.  
- Demonstrates strong knowledge of BLBF literature and appropriately cites prior work.  
- Novelty is significant: propensity overfitting was previously uncharacterized and the estimator is a meaningful advancement.  
- High practical significance for real-world BLBF applications like ads and recommendation systems.  
- Clarity and exposition are excellent with careful definitions and organization.  
- Limitation: the self-normalized estimator requires more computation per iteration, though it converges faster overall.  
- Minor weakness: paper could provide more intuition on why propensity overfitting occurs in BLBF but not in standard supervised learning.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, theoretically sound, empirically validated contribution suitable for NIPS.
