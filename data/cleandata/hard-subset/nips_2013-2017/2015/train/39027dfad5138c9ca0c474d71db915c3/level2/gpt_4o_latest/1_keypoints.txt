- Problem: Identifies propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and limitations of the conventional counterfactual risk estimator.  
- Contribution: Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator to mitigate propensity overfitting.  
- Algorithm: Introduces Norm-POEM, a practical CRM-based algorithm leveraging the self-normalized estimator.  
- Theoretical strength: Provides strong theoretical guarantees (consistency, boundedness) and control-variates justification.  
- Empirical strength: Demonstrates Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple structured output prediction datasets.  
- Practical integration: Norm-POEM is computationally efficient and integrates with models like Conditional Random Fields (CRFs).  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with a logical flow and detailed theoretical and empirical sections.  
- Experimental limitation: Experiments are limited to multi-label classification and lack broader BLBF tasks (e.g., recommendation, ads).  
- Computational analysis gap: Claims of efficiency lack thorough runtime and scalability analysis for large datasets or complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Regularization gap: Notes that variance regularization is still necessary but does not explore alternative regularizers.  
- Broader impact gap: Insufficient discussion on implications for related fields like causal inference and reinforcement learning.  
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses a significant BLBF problem with theoretically sound and empirically validated solutions.  
- Cons against acceptance: Experimental scope is narrow and computational-efficiency claims need more evidence.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance based on substantial contribution and likely influence on BLBF research and applications.
