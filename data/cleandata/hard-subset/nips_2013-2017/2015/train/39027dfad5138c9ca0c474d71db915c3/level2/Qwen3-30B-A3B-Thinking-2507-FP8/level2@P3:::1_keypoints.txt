- Identifies "propensity overfitting": a critical flaw where the standard importance-sampling estimator yields degenerate empirical risks arbitrarily lower than true risk.  
- Shows conventional unbiased estimator (Eq.2) can produce catastrophic, misleading risk estimates in BLBF.  
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator (Eq.7) that is equivariant and bounded within the loss range, fixing those anomalies.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, replacing the conventional estimator with the self-normalized version while preserving CRM principles.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (Theorem 2) that the self-normalized estimator is strongly consistent.  
- Empirically demonstrates Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM across multiple multi-label datasets (Scene, Yeast, TMC, LYRL).  
- Shows empirical evidence that improvements stem from better control of propensity overfitting (Table 2).  
- Novelty: reframes a variance-focused literature by identifying a distinct, fundamental estimator pathology not addressed by clipping/thresholding.  
- Builds on importance-sampling control variate ideas (Hesterberg) but applies them novelly to BLBF.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound with clear theorems, proofs, and illustrative examples (Example 1).  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and uses standard BLBF methodology with multiple datasets and runs.  
- Weakness: slightly more complex computation for variance estimation (Eq.8), though practical convergence is faster.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of interactions with other variance-reduction techniques and extension to non-structured hypothesis spaces.  
- Significance: substantial practical impact for real-world BLBF systems (ads, recommendations); paper is well-written, original, and meets criteria for quality.
