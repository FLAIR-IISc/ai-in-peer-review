- Addresses propensity overfitting in Batch Learning from Logged Bandit Feedback (BLBF), a critical issue in counterfactual risk estimation.  
- Shows the commonly used counterfactual estimator can produce severely biased risk estimates, especially for complex hypothesis spaces.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator that is well-motivated and avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Connects BLBF to related fields (causal inference, off-policy evaluation) with a clear introduction.  
- Core contribution: identification and mitigation of propensity overfitting with solid theoretical grounding.  
- Section 4 example effectively illustrates the propensity overfitting problem.  
- Theoretical justification builds on importance sampling and is sound (Theorem 2 guarantees consistency).  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a practical algorithm leveraging the self-normalized estimator.  
- Strong empirical results: Norm-POEM consistently outperforms POEM on multi-label classification tasks.  
- Ablation studies and runtime comparisons strengthen empirical claims and show practical efficiency.  
- Limited discussion of convergence rate and finite-sample rates for the estimator.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (M and λ) is only briefly discussed and needs deeper analysis.  
- Computational cost of the variance estimate (Equation 8) requires more detailed treatment.  
- Recommendation: high-quality paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address the above limitations.
