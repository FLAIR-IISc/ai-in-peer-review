- Identifies "propensity overfitting": the IPS estimator can be manipulated via propensity weights to produce artificially low empirical risk and degenerate policies.  
- Presents a clear toy/example (Section 4) demonstrating the severity and plausibility of this failure mode.  
- Proposes using the self-normalized estimator as a principled replacement, motivated as a multiplicative control variate.  
- Self-normalized estimator is equivariant to loss translations and yields estimates bounded within the observed loss range, preventing IPS pathologies.  
- Notes the self-normalized estimator is biased for finite samples but is strongly consistent asymptotically.  
- Integrates the estimator into CRM to form the Norm-POEM learning algorithm.  
- Empirical results show Norm-POEM consistently and substantially outperforms POEM on multi-label classification tasks.  
- Diagnostic experiments (sum of propensity weights, robustness to loss shifts) support the claim that Norm-POEM avoids weight-manipulation.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and carefully motivated.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of how the finite-sample bias of the self-normalized estimator interacts with the learning objective and whether it systematically favors certain policies.  
- Weakness: Computational scalability concerns—variance estimate impedes stochastic optimization, necessitating batch methods like L-BFGS.  
- Overall: High quality, originality, and significance; Norm-POEM is a practical and influential improvement for learning from logged bandit feedback.
