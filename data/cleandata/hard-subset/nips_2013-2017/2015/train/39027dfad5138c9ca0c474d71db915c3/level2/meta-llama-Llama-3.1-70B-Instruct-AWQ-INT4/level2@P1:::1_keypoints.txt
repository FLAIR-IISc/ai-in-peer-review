- Identifies a severe issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator (propensity overfitting) that causes degeneracies and biased risk estimates.  
- Proposes the Self-Normalized Risk Estimator as an alternative that provably avoids these degeneracies.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise explanation of the problem and proposed solution.  
- Technical content is sound, with thorough theoretical analysis of the Self-Normalized Risk Estimator.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and shows significantly better generalization than the conventional estimator.  
- Provides a simple, effective illustrative example that clarifies the impact of propensity overfitting.  
- Includes a thorough related-work review that situates the contribution within BLBF and counterfactual learning.  
- Weakness: assumes a relatively high level of background knowledge in BLBF and counterfactual learning, which may hinder accessibility.  
- Weakness: could provide a more detailed discussion of implications for real-world applications.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with solid empirical and theoretical support (Quality 9/10, Clarity 8.5/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective solution, strong empirical evidence, significant impact on BLBF and counterfactual learning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns for non-experts and insufficient discussion of practical application implications.
