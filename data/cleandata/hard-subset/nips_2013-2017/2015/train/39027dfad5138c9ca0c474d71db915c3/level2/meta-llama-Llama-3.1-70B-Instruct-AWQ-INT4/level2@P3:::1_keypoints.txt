- Identifies propensity overfitting in conventional counterfactual risk estimators for BLBF, causing degenerate risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator that provably avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Derives Norm-POEM, a CRM-based learning algorithm using the self-normalized estimator for exponential models.  
- Demonstrates Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional estimator on several multi-label classification tasks.  
- Builds on prior BLBF and CRM work, leveraging importance sampling and variance-reduction techniques like control variates.  
- Strength: clearly identifies a significant practical/theoretical problem and offers a well-motivated solution.  
- Strength: self-normalized estimator has desirable theoretical properties (equivariance, bounded range).  
- Strength: empirical results support improved robustness and generalization of Norm-POEM.  
- Strength: provides thorough analysis of propensity overfitting and its consequences.  
- Weakness: assumes historical policy has full support over outputs, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Weakness: self-normalized estimator is biased (not unbiased), potentially problematic for some applications.  
- Weakness: Norm-POEM incurs higher computational cost than the conventional estimator.  
- Originality: introduces a new estimator addressing propensity overfitting and a novel Norm-POEM algorithm.  
- Significance: contributions could impact BLBF and related fields (reinforcement learning, causal inference) and broader importance-sampling tasks.  
- Pro-acceptance: theoretically sound solution with empirical improvements and clear problem identification.  
- Con-acceptance: concerns about full-support assumption, estimator bias, and increased computational cost.  
- Revision suggestions: relax or address the full-support assumption, discuss implications of estimator bias, and add experiments showing robustness to settings and hyperparameters.
