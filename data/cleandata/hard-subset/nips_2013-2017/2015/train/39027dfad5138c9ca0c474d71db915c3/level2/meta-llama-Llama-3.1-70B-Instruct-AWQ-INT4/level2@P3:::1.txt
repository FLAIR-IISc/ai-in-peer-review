**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper identifies a severe problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), specifically a propensity overfitting issue that can lead to degenerate risk estimates. The authors propose an alternative estimator, the self-normalized estimator, which provably avoids this problem. They then derive a new learning algorithm, Normalized Policy Optimizer for Exponential Models (Norm-POEM), based on the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle using the new estimator. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of Norm-POEM in several multi-label classification problems, showing that it consistently outperforms the conventional estimator.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on BLBF, specifically the CRM principle and the use of counterfactual estimators. The authors also draw on techniques from importance sampling and variance reduction, such as control variates. The paper's contribution is the identification of the propensity overfitting problem and the proposal of a new estimator that addresses this issue.

**Strengths**

* The paper identifies a significant problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator and proposes a well-motivated solution.
* The self-normalized estimator is theoretically sound and has desirable properties, such as being equivariant and having a bounded range.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of Norm-POEM in several multi-label classification problems.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and its consequences.

**Weaknesses**

* The paper assumes that the historical policy has full support over the output space, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The self-normalized estimator is not unbiased, which may be a concern in some applications.
* The computational cost of Norm-POEM is higher than that of the conventional estimator, although the authors argue that this is offset by the improved robustness and generalization performance.

**Originality**

* The paper proposes a new estimator that addresses the propensity overfitting problem, which is a significant contribution to the field of BLBF.
* The derivation of Norm-POEM and its application to multi-label classification problems is original and well-motivated.

**Significance**

* The paper's contribution has the potential to impact the field of BLBF and related areas, such as reinforcement learning and causal inference.
* The self-normalized estimator and Norm-POEM may be applicable to other problems beyond BLBF, such as importance sampling and variance reduction.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of BLBF by identifying and addressing the propensity overfitting problem.
* The self-normalized estimator and Norm-POEM are well-motivated and theoretically sound.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of Norm-POEM in several multi-label classification problems.

Con:

* The paper assumes that the historical policy has full support over the output space, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The self-normalized estimator is not unbiased, which may be a concern in some applications.
* The computational cost of Norm-POEM is higher than that of the conventional estimator.

**Recommendations for Revision**

* Consider addressing the assumption of full support over the output space and exploring ways to relax this assumption.
* Provide more discussion on the implications of the self-normalized estimator being not unbiased.
* Consider providing more experimental results to demonstrate the robustness of Norm-POEM to different problem settings and hyper-parameter choices.
