**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning" identifies a severe problem of the conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF), called Propensity Overfitting. The authors propose an alternative estimator, the Self-Normalized Risk Estimator, which avoids this problem. They also derive a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on the Counterfactual Risk Minimization (CRM) principle using the new estimator.

**Claims**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. The conventional counterfactual risk estimator used in BLBF exhibits Propensity Overfitting, which can lead to degeneracies when used in Empirical Risk Minimization (ERM).
2. The proposed Self-Normalized Risk Estimator avoids Propensity Overfitting and provides a more robust estimate of the risk.
3. The Norm-POEM algorithm, which uses the Self-Normalized Risk Estimator, achieves significantly improved generalization performance compared to the conventional CRM algorithm.

**Support for Claims**

The authors provide theoretical analysis and empirical evidence to support their claims. They show that the Self-Normalized Risk Estimator is equivariant and has a lower variance than the conventional estimator. They also demonstrate the effectiveness of Norm-POEM on several multi-label classification problems, where it consistently outperforms the conventional CRM algorithm.

**Usefulness**

The ideas presented in the paper are practically useful for batch learning from logged bandit feedback, which is a common problem in online systems such as ad placement, web search, and recommendation.

**Field Knowledge**

The paper reflects common knowledge in the field of machine learning and counterfactual learning. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and demonstrate a good understanding of the relevant literature.

**Novelty**

The paper presents a significant improvement over existing approaches to BLBF. The Self-Normalized Risk Estimator is a novel contribution, and the Norm-POEM algorithm is a new learning algorithm that achieves state-of-the-art performance on several benchmark datasets.

**Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility. The authors describe the experimental setup, the datasets used, and the hyper-parameters tuned.

**Limitations**

The authors acknowledge that the Self-Normalized Risk Estimator is not unbiased, but they show that it is strongly consistent and approaches the desired expectation when the sample size is large.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

**Pro:**

* The paper presents a significant improvement over existing approaches to BLBF.
* The Self-Normalized Risk Estimator is a novel contribution that avoids Propensity Overfitting.
* The Norm-POEM algorithm achieves state-of-the-art performance on several benchmark datasets.

**Con:**

* The paper assumes that the historical algorithm whose predictions are recorded in the logged data is a stationary policy with full support over Y.
* The Self-Normalized Risk Estimator is not unbiased, which may be a limitation in certain applications.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting the paper for publication. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field of counterfactual learning, and their ideas have the potential to impact the development of online systems that rely on batch learning from logged bandit feedback.
