The paper "The Self-Normalized Estimator for Counterfactual Learning" presents a significant contribution to the field of batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF). The authors identify a critical issue with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator, known as propensity overfitting, and propose a novel solution using a self-normalized risk estimator.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the technical details. The introduction provides a concise overview of the BLBF setting and the importance of counterfactual learning. The related work section is comprehensive and provides a thorough review of existing methods and their limitations.

The technical contribution of the paper is substantial. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed self-normalized risk estimator in addressing this issue. The theoretical results are well-supported by empirical evaluations on several multi-label classification problems, showing that the new estimator leads to improved generalization performance and robustness.

The paper also introduces a new learning algorithm, Norm-POEM, which incorporates the self-normalized risk estimator and the CRM principle. The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of Norm-POEM over the conventional POEM algorithm in terms of generalization performance and robustness.

The strengths of the paper include:

1. Identification of a critical issue in BLBF: The authors identify a significant problem with the conventional counterfactual risk estimator and propose a novel solution.
2. Rigorous theoretical analysis: The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and the effectiveness of the proposed self-normalized risk estimator.
3. Comprehensive empirical evaluation: The authors conduct extensive experiments on several multi-label classification problems, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed method.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

1. Limited scope: The paper focuses primarily on the BLBF setting and may not be directly applicable to other areas of machine learning.
2. Computational cost: The self-normalized risk estimator may incur additional computational costs, which could be a limitation in large-scale applications.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of BLBF and counterfactual learning. The proposed self-normalized risk estimator and the Norm-POEM algorithm have the potential to improve the performance and robustness of BLBF methods in various applications.
