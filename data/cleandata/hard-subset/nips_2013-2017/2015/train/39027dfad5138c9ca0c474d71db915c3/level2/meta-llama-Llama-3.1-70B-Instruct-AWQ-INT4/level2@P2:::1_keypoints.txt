- Paper identifies propensity overfitting in conventional counterfactual risk estimators and proposes a self-normalized risk estimator as a solution.  
- Manuscript is well-organized and clearly written, with a concise BLBF introduction and comprehensive related work.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis of propensity overfitting and the self-normalized estimator.  
- Empirical evaluations on multi-label classification tasks show improved generalization and robustness.  
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a new algorithm combining the self-normalized estimator with the CRM principle.  
- Experiments demonstrate Norm-POEM outperforms conventional POEM on generalization and robustness.  
- Strength: Identification of a critical issue in BLBF and a novel corrective estimator.  
- Strength: Thorough and rigorous theoretical treatment of the problem and solution.  
- Strength: Comprehensive empirical evaluation across multiple datasets.  
- Weakness: Focus is primarily on BLBF and may not generalize to other ML settings.  
- Weakness: Self-normalized estimator may increase computational cost, limiting large-scale applicability.  
- Pro-accept: Significant contribution addressing a key BLBF issue with strong theoretical and empirical support.  
- Con-accept: Limited scope beyond BLBF and potential computational overhead for large datasets.  
- Rating: 8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Minor revisions requested: detail computational cost and scalability, discuss extensions to online/RL settings, and provide additional experiments on more datasets/applications.
