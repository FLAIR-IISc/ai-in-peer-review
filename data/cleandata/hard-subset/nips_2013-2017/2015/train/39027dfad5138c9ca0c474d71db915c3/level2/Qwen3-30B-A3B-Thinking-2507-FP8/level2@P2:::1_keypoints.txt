- Identifies a previously overlooked problem in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) called "propensity overfitting."
- Shows conventional inverse propensity score estimators can be severely biased due to overfitting to propensity weights, not the loss.
- Proposes a self-normalized risk estimator that is equivariant to loss translations and bounded within the loss range.
- Derives a new algorithm, Norm-POEM, based on the self-normalized estimator.
- Provides theoretical analysis proving consistency and shows the estimator avoids key anomalies of the conventional estimator.
- Empirical evaluation across multiple datasets shows consistent improvements and robustness to loss translation.
- Strength: clear identification and rigorous characterization of propensity overfitting, with a compelling illustrative example.
- Weakness: practical significance of propensity overfitting is not convincingly established—lack of evidence on how common it is in real-world BLBF.
- Weakness: unclear whether empirical gains stem specifically from avoiding propensity overfitting or from other factors.
- Weakness: insufficient connection and comparison to existing variance-reduction techniques (e.g., control variates, doubly robust estimators, clipped importance sampling).
- Weakness: theoretical analysis incomplete—no generalization error bound, rate of convergence, or sample-complexity discussion for Norm-POEM.
- Writing is clear and structure is good, but the related work section should more thoroughly position the approach relative to prior variance-reduction methods.
- Significance judged moderate: useful incremental advance applying self-normalized importance sampling to BLBF, but not a major breakthrough.
- Recommendation: solid paper worthy of acceptance after addressing practical significance, comparative baselines, and stronger theoretical guarantees.
