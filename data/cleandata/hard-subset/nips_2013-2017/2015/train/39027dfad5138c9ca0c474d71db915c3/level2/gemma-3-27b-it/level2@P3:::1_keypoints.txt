- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF).  
- Shows conventional counterfactual risk estimator can produce severely biased risk estimates.  
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator (Norm-POEM) as a remedy.  
- Provides strong theoretical justification (Theorems 1 and 2) for the proposed estimator.  
- Empirically validates Norm-POEM across several multi-label classification problems with consistent outperformance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and motivates BLBF within causal inference and off-policy evaluation.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and appropriately cites prior literature.  
- Example 1 effectively illustrates the propensity overfitting phenomenon.  
- Experimental setup mirrors prior work (POEM), enabling fair comparison and reproducibility.  
- Results are thoroughly presented with insightful analysis and discussion of computational efficiency.  
- Norm-POEM particularly outperforms when the logging policy is suboptimal; Table 2 supports mitigation of propensity overfitting.  
- Limitations discussion for the self-normalized estimator is brief; more coverage of failure modes (e.g., extreme weights, high-dimensional features) is needed.  
- Explanation for faster convergence of Norm-POEM despite higher per-iteration cost could be expanded (beyond mentioning shorter ‖w‖).  
- Scalability concerns due to reliance on L-BFGS should be addressed, and alternative optimization strategies discussed.  
- Dataset selection could be broadened to include varying degrees of logging-policy bias to further demonstrate robustness.  
- Overall: novel, significant, technically sound, and clear work deserving acceptance, with minor improvements suggested.
