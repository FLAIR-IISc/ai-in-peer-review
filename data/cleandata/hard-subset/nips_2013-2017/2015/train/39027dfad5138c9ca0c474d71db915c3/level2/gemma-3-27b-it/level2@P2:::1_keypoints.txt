- Addresses propensity overfitting in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) and shows counterfactual risk estimator can be severely biased.
- Proposes a self-normalized estimator as a remedy to reduce bias in risk estimates.
- Introduces Norm-POEM, a learning algorithm based on the self-normalized estimator.
- Empirically demonstrates Norm-POEM outperforms existing methods on multi-label classification and yields more stable risk estimates.
- Positions work appropriately within causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation literature, citing relevant prior work.
- Connects to importance sampling and control variates; cites prior self-normalized estimator work (e.g., [19]).
- Theoretical contribution: provides a strong consistency guarantee (Theorem 2) for the estimator.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, using supervised-to-bandit conversion and ablation studies to validate design choices.
- Paper is technically sound and insightful in identifying propensity overfitting as a distinct problem.
- Writing is generally clear and well-organized, with consistent mathematical notation.
- Clarity could be improved: Section 4’s example and Section 6’s variance estimator derivation need more detailed explanations.
- Originality: novel application of self-normalized estimators to BLBF and introduction of Norm-POEM.
- Significance: addresses a fundamental BLBF challenge, has practical impact, and releases code for reproducibility.
- Argument for acceptance: important problem, solid theory, strong empirical gains, clear presentation, and available code.
- Argument against acceptance: variance estimation is computationally expensive and relies on L-BFGS, which may not scale; some derivations need expansion.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity in Sections 4 and 6.
