- Identifies "Propensity Overfitting": IPS can be gamed by models to lower empirical risk via importance-weight manipulation rather than modeling loss.
- Shows Propensity Overfitting causes degenerate solutions that either overfit or avoid training data depending on loss sign.
- Proposes self-normalized importance sampling as a remedy: equivariant to loss shifts, bounded by observed loss range, mitigates the pathology.
- Notes the self-normalized estimator is biased in finite samples but strongly consistent.
- Introduces Norm-POEM: integrates self-normalized estimator and a variance approximation into CRM for structured-output learning.
- Theoretical grounding uses multiplicative control variates and analyzes equivariance/consistency properties.
- Experimental evaluation (Supervised-to-Bandit protocol) shows Norm-POEM consistently and substantially outperforms POEM on multi-label tasks.
- Ablation study confirms CRM's variance regularization remains necessary even with the new estimator.
- Paper quality is high: well-written, clear motivation, and a straightforward derivation of Norm-POEM.
- Original contribution: formalizing and naming Propensity Overfitting and applying a known estimator to this new ML pathology.
- Significance: findings are highly relevant to BLBF applications (search, recommendation, advertising) and likely to influence practice.
- Suggestion: briefly discuss the quality/impact of the variance approximation (e.g., delta method) on the CRM regularizer.
- Suggestion: validate on settings more directly tied to applications (ad placement or recommendation) to strengthen practical claims.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, principled/effective solution, strong empirical validation, high practical impact potential.
- Argument against acceptance: the self-normalized estimator itself is not new, though its application here is novel.
