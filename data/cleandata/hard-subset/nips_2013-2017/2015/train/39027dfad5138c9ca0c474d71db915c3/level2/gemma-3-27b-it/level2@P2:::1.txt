This paper addresses a critical issue in batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF): propensity overfitting in the commonly used counterfactual risk estimator. The authors convincingly demonstrate that this estimator can lead to severely biased risk estimates and propose a self-normalized estimator as a remedy. They introduce Norm-POEM, a new learning algorithm based on this estimator, and empirically show its superior performance on multi-label classification problems compared to existing methods.

**Summary of Related Work:** The paper correctly positions its work within the broader context of causal inference, domain adaptation, and off-policy evaluation. The references to key works in these areas (e.g., [7, 8, 4]) are appropriate. The connection to importance sampling and control variates is well-explained, and the citation of [19] regarding the self-normalized estimator is particularly relevant. The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the existing literature.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and the core idea – addressing propensity overfitting – is insightful. The theoretical justification for the self-normalized estimator is well-presented, and Theorem 2 provides a strong guarantee of consistency. The empirical evaluation is thorough and well-designed, employing a standard supervised-to-bandit conversion methodology. The experiments convincingly demonstrate the benefits of Norm-POEM, not just in terms of performance but also in terms of the stability of the estimated risk (Ŝ(ĥ) closer to 1). The inclusion of ablation studies (Table 3) adds to the rigor of the evaluation. The authors appropriately acknowledge limitations, such as the computational cost associated with the variance estimation.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation for the work is clearly articulated in the introduction. The mathematical notation is clear and consistent. However, some sections could benefit from slightly more detailed explanations. For instance, the connection between Propensity Overfitting and the specific behavior of the estimator in the example (Section 4) could be further elucidated.  The derivation of the variance estimator in Section 6 could also be expanded for readers less familiar with the topic.

**Originality:** The identification of "propensity overfitting" as a distinct issue in BLBF is a novel contribution. While self-normalized estimators are known in the importance sampling literature, their application to address this specific problem in the context of BLBF and CRM is original. The Norm-POEM algorithm, based on this estimator, is a direct result of this insight.

**Significance:** This work has the potential to significantly impact the field of BLBF. The propensity overfitting problem is a fundamental challenge in learning from logged data, and the proposed solution offers a promising way to address it. The improved performance of Norm-POEM demonstrates the practical value of this approach. The insights gained from this work could also inform the development of more robust learning algorithms in related areas, such as off-policy evaluation in reinforcement learning.  The readily available code adds to its practical value.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a crucial, previously unrecognized problem in BLBF.
*   Provides a theoretically sound solution with strong guarantees.
*   Empirically demonstrates significant performance gains.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.
*   Code availability promotes reproducibility and adoption.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The variance estimation in Norm-POEM is computationally expensive and requires L-BFGS, which may not scale well to very large datasets. (Though the authors show it's competitive.)
*   Some sections (e.g., derivation of variance estimator) could benefit from more detailed explanations.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that addresses an important problem with a novel and well-supported solution. The empirical results are compelling, and the theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the proposed approach.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the clarity issues mentioned above. Specifically, I suggest expanding the explanations in sections 4 and 6.
