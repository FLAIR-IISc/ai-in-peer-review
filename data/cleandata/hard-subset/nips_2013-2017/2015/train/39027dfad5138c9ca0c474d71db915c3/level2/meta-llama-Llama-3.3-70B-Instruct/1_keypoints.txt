- Proposes Norm-POEM: a self-normalized risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) to avoid propensity overfitting.
- Claims conventional unbiased risk estimator suffers from propensity overfitting causing severely biased risk estimates.
- Claims the self-normalized risk estimator provably avoids propensity overfitting.
- Claims Norm-POEM achieves significantly better generalization than conventional approaches.
- Provides thorough analysis demonstrating propensity overfitting across datasets.
- Derives the self-normalized estimator in detail and shows theoretical advantages over the conventional estimator.
- Presents comprehensive, well-designed empirical evaluations on multi-label classification tasks supporting effectiveness of Norm-POEM.
- Paper is well-written with clear technical explanations and a thorough related work discussion.
- The method is potentially useful for applications like online advertising and recommendation systems.
- Authors demonstrate strong field knowledge and understanding of BLBF literature and challenges.
- Novel contribution: the self-normalized risk estimator and new insights into propensity overfitting.
- Paper is complete in derivation, experiments, and discussion of limitations.
- Acknowledged limitation: potential high computational cost of the self-normalized estimator.
- Additional limitation: assumption that the historical policy has full support over the action space may not hold in practice.
- Suggestion: paper could include more detailed discussion of practical applications and limitations.
