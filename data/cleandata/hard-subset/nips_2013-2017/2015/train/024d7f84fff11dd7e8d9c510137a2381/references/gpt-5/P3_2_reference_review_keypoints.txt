- Paper develops policy-gradient methods for coherent risk measures in both static and time-consistent dynamic Markov settings using the dual (risk-envelope) representation.
- Derives a general static gradient formula for coherent risk ρ(Z) depending on the saddle point (ξ*, λ*), unifying prior special-case results and enabling sampling-based estimators.
- Proposes a sample-average-approximation (SAA) estimator: solve a finite convex program from samples to get ξ* and KKT multipliers, plug into gradient, and prove consistency under mild assumptions.
- Presents a dynamic policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk: gradient is an expectation over trajectories under an adversarially reweighted kernel with a stage-wise cost hθ that includes value and multiplier terms.
- Proposes an actor-critic scheme where the critic estimates a robust/risk-sensitive value function and the actor uses the new gradient formula.
- Strength: Broad generality — framework covers all coherent risks and subsumes prior specific-risk gradient results (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance).
- Strength: Conceptually clean variational foundation using convex duality and envelope theorems; SAA with KKT multipliers is natural and consistency is valuable.
- Strength: Novel extension to dynamic, time-consistent coherent risk with clear link to robust MDPs and a reasonable critic approach.
- Strength: Clear exposition of main ideas and illustrative portfolio example showing flexibility beyond variance-based criteria.
- Weakness: Practicality concerns — actor requires sampling under reweighted kernel Pθ ξ* and computing derivatives of envelope constraints w.r.t. P, which may need a generative model or importance-sampling not provided.
- Weakness: Computational burden — repeated convex program solves (static and per-state dynamic) raise scalability issues for large/continuous spaces; approximation effects are deferred.
- Weakness: Limited experiments — only a toy portfolio example, no dynamic MDP benchmarks, no comparisons to risk-averse baselines, and no sample-complexity or runtime analysis.
- Weakness: Technical conditions under-specified — finite-state assumptions, extension to Lp not developed, and issues like non-unique saddle points and measurability/stability of ξ*(θ) are not addressed.
- Weakness: Potentially high gradient variance for tail-focused risks; variance-reduction and importance-sampling techniques are not integrated.
- Quality: Theoretical approach is sound at a high level, but many proofs deferred and practical sampling/model-free details under-specified.
- Clarity: Generally well written with clear notation and examples, though dynamic-constraint notation and actor-critic details are dense/brief.
- Originality: High — provides a general policy-gradient and novel dynamic theorem for coherent risks beyond prior specific-risk work.
- Significance: Potentially high for researchers, but immediate practical impact limited by computational and sampling requirements plus weak experiments.
- Question: How to implement sampling from Pθ ξ* in practice with only a simulator; request importance-sampling scheme and variance analysis.
- Question: Provide explicit forms of constraint derivatives (dfi/dp, dge/dp) for common envelopes and discuss sample-based estimation without knowing P.
- Question: Can ξ* and multipliers be obtained via online primal-dual updates (e.g., mirror descent) to avoid repeated convex solves; provide complexity/convergence results.
- Suggestion: Add dynamic MDP benchmarks and comparisons (e.g., CVaR-RL, robust MDPs) with ablations on estimators, tail weight, and runtime.
- Suggestion: Discuss selection rules and continuity for ξ* when saddle points are non-unique and impact on SGD stability.
- Minor: Clarify that normalization ∑x′ ξ(x′)Pθ(x′|x)=1 implies a proper reweighted kernel and state finite-space assumptions up front.
- Minor: Develop extensions to continuous spaces (function approximation/sampling) or state assumptions precisely.
- Minor: Fix incomplete references (years/pages).
- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel and unifying theoretical contribution but needs stronger empirical validation and more practical guidance for dynamic settings.
