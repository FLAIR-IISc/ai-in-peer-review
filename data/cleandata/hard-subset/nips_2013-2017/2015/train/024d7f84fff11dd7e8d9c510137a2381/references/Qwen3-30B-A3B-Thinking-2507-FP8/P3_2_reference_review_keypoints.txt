- Presents a unified policy-gradient framework for coherent risk measures, covering static and Markov coherent risk.  
- Derives a general gradient formula for static coherent risk (Theorem 4.2) implementable via sampling and convex programming.  
- Proposes a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk (Theorem 5.2), extending classical policy gradient to risk-sensitive settings.  
- Leverages the representation theorem for coherent risk measures and connects envelope theorem with likelihood ratio methods.  
- Shows the framework recovers known results for specific risks (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation).  
- Provides a sampling-based estimator with a consistency result (Proposition 4.4).  
- Includes a numerical trading example demonstrating different risk preferences yield different optimal policies.  
- Theoretical foundation is strong and well-motivated, with clear assumptions (Assumptions 2.2, 4.1).  
- Gradient derivations are novel, insightful, and generalize prior specific-case work.  
- Sampling approach is practical for large-scale problems and supported by consistency theory.  
- Numerical example effectively illustrates practical importance of risk choice.  
- Computational complexity of required convex programming is not thoroughly analyzed or scaled.  
- Convergence analysis of the actor-critic algorithm is not detailed in the main paper.  
- Empirical validation is limited to a single trading example; broader benchmarks are lacking.  
- The connection between coherent risk measures and model uncertainty is mentioned but underdeveloped.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with good explanations of core concepts.  
- Some important technical proofs are deferred to supplementary material without sufficient emphasis.  
- The two-phase sampling procedure for the actor-critic algorithm is described vaguely and needs clarification.  
- Lacks explicit comparison of computational efficiency against existing risk-sensitive RL methods.  
- Originality is high: generalizes risk-sensitive RL to the entire class of coherent risk measures.  
- The Markov coherent risk policy gradient theorem is a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Does not fully leverage the connection to robust MDPs or existing stochastic programming work.  
- Practical implications are significant, but scalability and sensitivity to risk-envelope choices are not fully explored.  
- Recommendations: expand experiments, analyze computational complexity, develop model-uncertainty link, clarify actor-critic procedure, and explicitly discuss limitations.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution worth accepting; recommendation: Accept.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel unified framework, solid theory, generalization of prior results, useful numerical illustration.  
- Rejection concerns: limited experiments, unclear computational scaling, underdeveloped model-uncertainty connection.
