- Overall assessment: Strong, novel contribution generalizing policy gradients to coherent risk measures; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Main contribution: Unified framework for policy gradients applicable to any coherent risk measure, covering static and dynamic cases.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends and generalizes risk-sensitive RL and robust MDP literature, showing special cases (e.g., CVaR) fit into the framework.  
- Theoretical rigor: Gradient derivations (static and dynamic) are sound and leverage the Envelope Theorem for sampling-based estimators.  
- Novelty: Provides new gradient formula for static coherent risk (Thm 4.2) and policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk (Thm 5.2).  
- Practicality: Sampling-plus-convex-program approach is suited to large-scale problems where dynamic programming is infeasible.  
- Writing and organization: Clear exposition, definitions, and logical structure.  
- Illustrative example: Simple numerical example effectively shows how different risk measures affect policy choice.  
- Implementation complexity: Dynamic-case actor-critic details, function approximation choices, and hyperparameter tuning are under-specified; two-phase sampling is hand-wavy.  
- Assumption dependence: Heavy reliance on Assumption 2.2 (risk envelope form); practical verification and failure modes need discussion.  
- Scalability concerns: Repeated convex program solves may bottleneck performance in large state/action spaces.  
- Variance analysis missing: No detailed study of gradient estimator variance, especially for the dynamic two-phase sampling approach.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant theoretical advance, broad applicability, novel tractable gradient formulas, and meaningful generalization of prior work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Sparse dynamic implementation details, dependency on Assumption 2.2, scalability of convex solves, and lack of variance analysis.  
- Recommendations: Add detailed actor-critic pseudo-code and implementation specifics for the dynamic case.  
- Recommendations: Discuss limitations or alternatives if Assumption 2.2 does not hold.  
- Recommendations: Address convex-program scalability (approximation or decomposition techniques).  
- Recommendations: Provide (even preliminary) variance analysis for gradient estimators.  
- Recommendations: Clarify and justify the two-phase sampling procedure and compare to single-phase sampling.
