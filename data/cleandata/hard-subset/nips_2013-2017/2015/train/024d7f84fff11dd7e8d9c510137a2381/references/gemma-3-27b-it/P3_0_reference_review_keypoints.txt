- Overall assessment: Strong, novel contribution unifying policy-gradient methods for coherent risk measures; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
- Paper tackles optimizing policies under coherent risk measures, unifying and generalizing prior work on specific risk metrics and robust MDPs.
- Positions work well relative to prior literature, noting limitations of dynamic programming and links to stochastic programming.
- Strength: Generality — framework applies to a broad class of coherent risk measures.
- Strength: Theoretical rigor — rigorous derivation of gradient formulas for static and dynamic coherent risk.
- Strength: Practicality — sampling-based approach addresses computational scalability.
- Strength: Novel contributions — new gradient formula, sampling algorithm, and policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk.
- Strength: Clear presentation — well-written and organized despite mathematical complexity.
- Strength: Illustrative example — numerical example shows different behaviors under different risk criteria.
- Weakness: Accessibility — requires strong background in stochastic optimization and mathematical finance; needs more intuitive explanations.
- Weakness: Implementation details lacking — actor-critic algorithm description needs practical specifics (function approximation, hyperparameters).
- Weakness: Limited empirical evaluation — only a simple numerical example; needs benchmarks and comparisons to existing methods.
- Weakness: Assumption 2.2 may be restrictive — needs discussion of practical implications and alternatives for other risk envelopes.
- Weakness: Computational cost not fully analyzed — more detail needed on complexity, especially convex programming within sampling.
- For acceptance: Strong theory, unification of risk-sensitive RL, scalable sampling approach, clear motivation.
- Against acceptance: Steep learning curve, limited empirical validation, potentially restrictive Assumption 2.2.
- Recommendation: Add an intuitive section explaining coherent risk measures for RL audiences.
- Recommendation: Provide detailed implementation guidance for the actor-critic algorithm (value approximation, tuning).
- Recommendation: Expand empirical evaluation on benchmark RL tasks and compare to existing risk-sensitive methods.
- Recommendation: Discuss Assumption 2.2’s practical impact and ways to handle nonconforming risk envelopes.
- Recommendation: Provide computational complexity analysis, especially for the convex optimization step.
- Recommendation: Suggest exploring importance sampling or other variance-reduction methods for gradient estimation in future work.
