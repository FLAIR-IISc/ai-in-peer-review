- Develops policy gradient algorithms for RL with coherent risk objectives, giving a unified framework for the entire class of coherent risk measures.
- Main contributions: (1) new gradient formula for static coherent risk enabling sampling-based estimation, (2) a sampling + convex-programming estimator for static coherent risk gradients, (3) a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures extending to dynamic risk-sensitive settings.
- Builds on Artzner et al. and Ruszczynski & Shapiro, generalizing prior work focused on specific risk measures.
- Technical quality: proofs are rigorous, assumptions stated clearly, and consistency of gradient estimators is established.
- Clarity: well-organized, consistent notation, clear progression from static to dynamic risk, with effective examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation).
- Originality: first unified policy-gradient framework covering all coherent risk measures; existing specific-results shown as special cases.
- Significance: important for applications caring about tail risk and variability; framework enables easier experimentation with different risk preferences.
- Algorithmic implementation details are limited; actor-critic algorithm and scalability of convex-programming steps need more practical guidance.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a simple three-asset trading example and lacks experiments on standard RL benchmarks.
- Comparison with specialized methods (e.g., CVaR estimators) is insufficient—missing analyses of sample complexity and computational cost.
- Recommendation: Accept — strong, novel theoretical contribution despite empirical limitations.
- Pros: unified framework, rigorous and general theory, clear link to dynamic programming, well-written with illustrative examples, and a numerical demonstration of risk-selection effects.
- Cons: limited empirical validation, missing large-scale implementation details, and shallow comparison to specialized approaches.
- Final assessment: a significant, likely influential theoretical contribution bridging coherent risk theory and RL, suitable for NIPS.
