- Unified framework for policy gradient RL that applies to the entire class of coherent risk measures (static and dynamic).
- Derives novel gradient formulas using the representation theorem and envelope theorem (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2).
- Provides a sampling-based gradient estimator with consistency guarantees (Proposition 4.4).
- Supplies a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and examples for CVaR and mean-semideviation.
- Includes a numerical trading example illustrating different risk preferences' policy effects.
- Builds on and generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL and finance literature, rederiving specific cases in greater generality.
- Theoretical foundations are solid and assumptions (e.g., Assumptions 2.2, 4.1) are reasonable and well-motivated.
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized writing, consistent notation, and reproducible algorithmic detail.
- Original contribution is the generalization to all coherent risk measures rather than a single measure.
- Significant potential impact for risk-sensitive decision-making in real-world domains and links risk to model uncertainty.
- Strengths: unified theory, rigorous proofs, practical algorithms, clear exposition, thorough related work, and a practical demo.
- Weaknesses: simplistic numerical evaluation, limited computational complexity analysis, simulator requirement for sampling may limit applicability, and limited treatment of learning the risk envelope from data.
- Recommendation: Accept to NeurIPS.
- Final scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
