- Presents a unified framework for policy gradient optimization over the full class of coherent risk measures (static and dynamic).  
- Derives novel gradient formulas for static coherent risk (Theorem 4.2) and time-consistent dynamic risk (Theorem 5.2).  
- Uses a sampling-based approach and convex programming to make the method scalable to large MDPs.  
- Shows existing specific-risk results (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance) as special cases, demonstrating generality.  
- Provides theoretical consistency results for the sampling-based estimator (Proposition 4.4).  
- Numerical illustration demonstrates how different risk preferences affect policies, though example is simple.  
- Positions work well relative to prior risk-sensitive RL, robust MDPs, and stochastic programming literature with comprehensive references.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor and mathematically sound derivations leveraging coherent risk representation.  
- Strength: extends gradient calculations to dynamic, time-consistent risk, enabling advanced risk-aware control.  
- Strength: generally clear presentation and consistent notation, with useful motivating discussion.  
- Weakness: mathematical formalism is dense and may be hard to follow for non-experts; needs more intuitive explanations.  
- Weakness: limited implementation details for the actor-critic algorithm and reliance on a simulator for transitions.  
- Weakness: numerical experiment is limited and not sufficient to demonstrate scalability or benchmark performance.  
- Weakness: key assumptions (e.g., smoothness) are strong and need deeper discussion of limitations and impacts.  
- Weakness: connection to robust MDPs acknowledged but not fully explored or contrasted in depth.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, novel gradient formulas, scalable sampling approach, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high mathematical complexity, insufficient implementation/experimental validation, and assumptions needing justification.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution to risk-sensitive RL; should be accepted with minor revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: add intuitive explanations, expand actor-critic implementation details and optimizations, run broader benchmark experiments, and discuss assumption limitations.
