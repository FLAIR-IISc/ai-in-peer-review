- Overall: Valuable, technically sound paper generalizing policy gradients to coherent risk measures; recommend Accept with minor revisions.  
- Main contribution: Unified framework for policy gradient methods under coherent risk, covering static and dynamic settings.  
- Main contribution: Novel gradient formula for static coherent risk tailored for sampling.  
- Main contribution: Sampling-based algorithm for static risk with consistency guarantees (SAA approach).  
- Main contribution: Policy gradient theorem for dynamic Markov coherent risk and an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on and generalizes risk-sensitive RL and robust MDP literature; links to stochastic programming highlighted.  
- Strength: Generality and unification—encompasses many risk preferences in one framework.  
- Strength: Theoretical rigor—sound derivations (Theorems 4.2, 5.2) and consistency results (Proposition 4.4).  
- Strength: Sampling-based approach is practical and scalable for large MDPs.  
- Strength: Dynamic risk treatment addresses time consistency in multi-stage problems.  
- Strength: Clear and thorough connections to existing literature.  
- Weakness: Numerical evaluation is limited—small illustrative example without comprehensive baselines or harder benchmarks.  
- Weakness: Implementation complexity—actor-critic scheme requires convex programming, sampling, and function approximation, making tuning challenging.  
- Weakness: Dependence on Assumption 2.2 (risk envelope) may limit practical applicability if convex formulations are unavailable.  
- Weakness: Some notation (e.g., Lagrangian multipliers) is dense and needs clearer explanation.  
- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Pro arguments: General, theoretically sound framework; covers static and dynamic risk; sampling-based methods suitable for large problems; fills a gap by unifying methods.  
- Con arguments: Insufficient experiments, potential implementation barriers, and reliance on the risk-envelope assumption.  
- Recommendations: Accept with minor revisions—expand experimental validation, add implementation and complexity details, clarify notation, and discuss practical handling of Assumption 2.2.
