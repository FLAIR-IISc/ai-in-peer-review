The paper addresses the problem of policy gradient optimization for coherent risk measures in reinforcement learning. It generalizes existing approaches by considering the entire class of coherent risk measures, both static and dynamic, and provides novel gradient formulas for both cases. The authors present a new formula for the static coherent risk gradient, an algorithm involving convex programming for general static risk, and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk along with a corresponding actor-critic algorithm.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly articulating the limitations of existing risk-sensitive RL methods focused on specific risk measures and advocating for a more general framework based on coherent risk. The connection to robust MDPs and the discussion of time consistency are also valuable. The technical contributions, particularly the gradient formulas, are substantial and build upon existing literature in stochastic programming and optimization. The demonstration of how existing results are special cases of their more general approach strengthens the contribution. The numerical example provides a compelling illustration of the importance of choosing an appropriate risk measure based on the specific application and highlights the nuanced behavior of different risk-averse policies.

However, the paper suffers from a few weaknesses. While the theoretical contributions are significant, the presentation is dense and requires a strong background in optimization and risk theory. The assumptions, particularly Assumption 2.2 regarding the structure of the risk envelope, are not thoroughly justified. It’s not immediately clear how widely applicable this assumption is in practice, and a discussion of scenarios where it might not hold would be beneficial. The actor-critic algorithm is briefly described, and a more detailed explanation of the practical implementation challenges and computational complexity would be valuable. The convergence analysis is mentioned but relegated to the supplementary material, hindering a thorough evaluation within the main text. The connection between the proposed method and existing robust RL approaches could be further elaborated. While acknowledging the connection in the introduction, a more in-depth comparison of their strengths and weaknesses would be helpful. The code is not provided, limiting reproducibility.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The generalization to the entire class of coherent risk measures is a significant contribution, providing a unifying framework for risk-sensitive RL.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The gradient formulas are derived rigorously and build upon established results in optimization.
*   **Significance:** The work addresses an important problem in RL and has the potential to impact applications in finance, robotics, and other domains where risk management is crucial.
*   **Clarity:** Despite the technical density, the core ideas are clearly presented and the paper is well-organized.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Assumption 2.2:** The assumption about the structure of the risk envelope is strong and its applicability needs further discussion.
*   **Algorithm Detail:** The actor-critic algorithm description is brief, lacking details on implementation challenges and computational complexity.
*   **Convergence Analysis:** The convergence analysis is relegated to the supplementary material.
*   **Lack of Code:**  The absence of code hinders reproducibility.

**Overall assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The theoretical results are significant, and the proposed framework offers a unifying approach to handling coherent risk measures. While the paper has some weaknesses related to the presentation, assumptions, and algorithm details, the strengths outweigh these concerns. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should: 1) provide a more detailed discussion of the assumptions, including scenarios where they might not hold; 2) expand the description of the actor-critic algorithm, addressing implementation challenges and computational complexity; and 3) consider moving key aspects of the convergence analysis into the main text or providing a more comprehensive summary. Providing code would further enhance the reproducibility and impact of the work.
