- Presents a unified framework for policy gradient methods with coherent risk measures, deriving gradient formulas for static and dynamic Markov coherent risk.  
- Main contributions: a new gradient formula for static coherent risk enabling sampling, a convex-programming-based gradient estimator, and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk.  
- Builds on coherent risk theory and dynamic risk measures, generalizing prior specific-risk results (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation).  
- Technically sound with rigorous proofs for key theorems and consistency results for gradient estimators; representation theorem is the key insight.  
- Careful technical treatment with clear assumptions (e.g., risk envelopes); numerical example shows relevance but is limited.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized exposition with consistent notation and useful supplementary material; extension to continuous state spaces could be discussed more.  
- Original and novel: unifies fragmented prior work by applying coherent risk envelopes to RL policy gradients.  
- Significant for RL: enables designing risk-sensitive algorithms for any coherent risk measure and connects to robust MDPs, though empirical validation is sparse.  
- Strengths: unification, rigorous theory, practical example, clear generalization of prior work, strong ties to finance risk theory.  
- Weaknesses: limited empirical validation, little analysis of computational complexity, high technical depth may hinder accessibility, excludes non-coherent risks.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance to NIPS.
