This paper presents a compelling and broadly applicable framework for policy gradient methods in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning, specifically addressing coherent risk measures. The core idea – leveraging the duality representation of coherent risk to formulate gradients – is both elegant and powerful. The generalization of existing results to the entire class of coherent risk measures is a significant contribution, and the derived formulas, particularly Theorem 4.2, appear quite useful. While the paper is mathematically dense, the presentation is generally clear, and the connection to existing literature is well-established. The numerical example, while simple, effectively demonstrates the impact of different risk preferences.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The unification of policy gradient methods under a coherent risk framework is highly novel. Existing work typically focuses on specific risk measures. This paper provides a general recipe.
*   **Generality:** The applicability to *all* coherent risk measures is a major strength.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of the gradient formulas is rigorous, building on established results from coherent risk theory and optimization.
*   **Potential Impact:** This work could significantly broaden the application of risk-sensitive RL by providing a more flexible and principled approach to risk management.
*   **Clear Connection to Existing Work:** The paper meticulously positions itself within the existing literature, clearly highlighting its contributions and generalizing previous results.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Computational Complexity:** The reliance on solving a convex optimization problem (Eq. 7) at each iteration of a policy gradient algorithm is computationally demanding, especially for high-dimensional state spaces. The paper acknowledges this but could benefit from a more detailed discussion of scalability concerns and potential approximation strategies.
*   **Limited Empirical Validation:** The numerical example is illustrative but limited in scope.  More extensive experiments on benchmark RL environments would strengthen the paper.  Specifically, demonstrating performance gains compared to standard policy gradients and other risk-sensitive approaches (e.g., CVaR-based methods) is crucial.
*   **Assumptions:** Several assumptions, particularly Assumption 2.2 regarding the structure of the risk envelope, are strong. While justified by many common coherent risk measures, their generality requires more discussion. Are there practical risk measures for which these assumptions do not hold?
*   **Actor-Critic Algorithm Details:** The description of the actor-critic algorithm (Section 5) is relatively brief. A more detailed algorithmic description and analysis of its convergence properties would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The core idea is genuinely novel and addresses an important gap in the literature.
*   The theoretical framework is well-developed and provides a solid foundation for future research.
*   The potential for broad applicability to various risk-sensitive RL problems is high.
*   The authors demonstrate a deep understanding of both coherent risk theory and reinforcement learning.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly articulates its contributions.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The computational cost of the proposed approach may limit its practical applicability.
*   The lack of extensive empirical validation raises concerns about the algorithm's performance in real-world scenarios.
*   The strong assumptions regarding the risk envelope could restrict the generality of the framework.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some limitations, I strongly recommend accepting this paper. The novelty and generality of the proposed approach significantly outweigh the weaknesses. The computational complexity and lack of extensive empirical validation are valid concerns, but they do not detract from the fundamental contribution of this work. The paper introduces a powerful and flexible framework for risk-sensitive RL that has the potential to significantly advance the field.  Minor revisions addressing the concerns regarding scalability and empirical validation could further strengthen the paper.  I believe this work represents a significant step forward and is appropriate for presentation at NIPS.



**Rating:** Accept (with minor revisions)
