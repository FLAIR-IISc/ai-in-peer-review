This paper introduces policy gradient algorithms for reinforcement learning with coherent risk objectives, aiming to generalize existing approaches by applying to the entire class of coherent risk measures. The core contributions are a new gradient formula for static coherent risk, a sampling-based algorithm for its computation, and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk accompanied by an actor-critic algorithm. The paper is well-motivated, positioning itself within the growing field of risk-sensitive RL and highlighting the limitations of previous work focused on specific risk measures.

**Strengths:**

* **Generality:** The paper’s primary strength lies in its broad applicability to the class of coherent risk measures. This provides a unified framework encompassing many existing approaches as special cases.
* **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the gradient formulas, based on the envelope theorem and duality results from coherent risk theory, appears technically sound. The link to robust MDPs and the utilization of existing convex optimization techniques add to the credibility.
* **Clear Presentation (for experts):** The paper is generally well-structured and clearly written *for an audience familiar with both RL and coherent risk measures*.  Mathematical notation is standard and the key concepts are well-defined.
* **Potential for Scalability:**  The proposed sampling-based algorithms and the use of function approximation suggest potential scalability to large MDPs, addressing a key limitation of dynamic programming approaches.
* **Illustrative Example:** The numerical example demonstrates the impact of different risk measures on policy selection, providing a concrete illustration of the framework’s utility.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Accessibility:** While clear for experts, the paper has a steep learning curve for those unfamiliar with both reinforcement learning *and* coherent risk theory.  More intuitive explanations of key concepts (e.g., coherent risk, risk envelopes) would broaden its appeal.
* **Implementation Details:** The actor-critic algorithm is described at a high level. Crucial implementation details – such as how the risk envelope is computed and how function approximation is applied – are relegated to the supplementary material, hindering a full understanding of the practical challenges.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The numerical example is relatively simple and focuses on demonstrating the qualitative impact of different risk measures. A more comprehensive empirical evaluation, including comparisons to existing risk-sensitive RL algorithms on benchmark MDPs, would significantly strengthen the paper.
* **Assumptions:** The paper relies on several assumptions (e.g., Assumption 2.2 regarding the risk envelope, Assumption 4.1 on the likelihood ratio) that are not fully justified. A discussion of the sensitivity of the results to these assumptions would be valuable.
* **Convergence Analysis:** The convergence analysis of the actor-critic algorithm, described briefly as being available in the supplement, requires detailed examination. The quality of the convergence guarantees is critical for assessing the practical viability of the approach.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound framework for risk-sensitive RL.
* The generality of the approach is a significant contribution, unifying existing work and offering a flexible platform for future research.
* The proposed algorithms have the potential to scale to large and complex MDPs.
* The paper addresses a challenging and important problem in reinforcement learning.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper's accessibility is limited by its technical depth and the assumed background knowledge.
* The empirical evaluation is insufficient to demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.
* Several crucial implementation details and convergence guarantees are only available in the supplementary material.
* The assumptions made by the paper require further justification and sensitivity analysis.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a solid theoretical contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The generality and technical soundness of the proposed framework are commendable. However, the limited empirical evaluation and the lack of detailed implementation guidance raise concerns about its practical impact. The supplementary material is crucial for assessing the full value of the work, and reviewers should carefully examine it.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  The authors should:

1.  Expand the discussion of the key concepts (coherent risk, risk envelopes) to improve accessibility.
2.  Provide more details on the implementation of the actor-critic algorithm, particularly regarding function approximation and risk envelope computation.
3.  Conduct a more comprehensive empirical evaluation, comparing the proposed algorithms to existing methods on benchmark MDPs.
4.  Provide a more thorough justification of the key assumptions and analyze the sensitivity of the results to these assumptions.
5.  Ensure that the convergence analysis presented in the supplementary material is rigorous and complete.
