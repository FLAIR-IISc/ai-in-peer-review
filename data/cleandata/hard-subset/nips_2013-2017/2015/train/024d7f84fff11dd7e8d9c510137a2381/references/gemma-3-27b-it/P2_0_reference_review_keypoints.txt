- Summary: Proposes a unified policy-gradient framework for the full class of coherent risk measures, with gradient formulas for static and dynamic (time-consistent) risk and practical algorithms using sampling and convex duality.

- Strength — Generality: Provides a unified approach covering many coherent risk measures, generalizing prior work focused on specific risks like CVaR.

- Strength — Theoretical soundness: Derivations use coherent risk theory and convex duality and are supported by proofs (in supplementary material).

- Strength — Practical algorithms: Presents a sampling-based estimator for static risk and an actor-critic algorithm for Markov coherent risk, making theory implementable.

- Strength — Connections to prior work: Clearly situates the contributions relative to existing literature and shows how prior results are special cases.

- Strength — Illustrative example: Asset-allocation example demonstrates the importance of choosing appropriate risk measures and provides intuition.

- Weakness — Complexity: Algorithms require convex programming and two-phase sampling, which may hinder scalability and implementation.

- Weakness — Strong assumptions: Results rely on assumptions (e.g., bounded likelihood ratios, smoothness) that may not hold in practice and need discussion.

- Weakness — Limited empirical evaluation: Only a simple numerical example is shown; more complex RL environment experiments are needed to validate scalability and effectiveness.

- Weakness — Convergence analysis: Convergence guarantees (e.g., for the actor-critic) are incomplete or relegated to supplements and need more thorough treatment.

- Weakness — Clarity in dynamic risk: Description of the dynamic risk actor-critic, critic implementation, and weighted-transition gradient estimation is brief and could be expanded.

- NIPS Evaluation — Quality: Technically sound with supporting theory; overall strong.

- NIPS Evaluation — Clarity: Generally well-written but some dynamic-risk sections need more detail; rated good.

- NIPS Evaluation — Originality: Novel unified treatment of coherent risk in policy gradients; rated strong.

- NIPS Evaluation — Significance: Potentially important and broadly applicable across domains; rated good.

- Arguments For: Novel, theoretically solid contribution; highly general framework with wide applicability; concrete algorithms enable practical use.

- Arguments Against: Algorithmic complexity may limit large-scale use; empirical validation is insufficient; convergence analysis needs strengthening.

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Requested revisions: 1) Expand empirical evaluation on more complex RL environments; 2) Provide more implementation and convergence details for the dynamic-risk actor-critic; 3) Discuss practical implications of the paper's assumptions.
