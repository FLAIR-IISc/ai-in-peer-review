- Paper generalizes policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures (static and dynamic) and derives gradient formulas for both.  
- Key contributions: static risk gradient formula, sampling-plus-convex-program algorithm for static risk, and a Markov coherent risk policy-gradient theorem enabling actor-critic methods.  
- Strength — Generality: framework applies to any coherent risk measure, subsuming prior specialized approaches (e.g., CVaR, variance).  
- Strength — Theoretical soundness: derivations are rigorous, leveraging coherent risk theory and the envelope theorem with proofs in the supplement.  
- Strength — Practicality: sampling-based static algorithm and function approximation for dynamic case offer routes to scale to large/continuous MDPs.  
- Strength — Connections: paper situates itself clearly within risk-sensitive RL, robust MDPs, and stochastic programming literature.  
- Strength — Illustrative example: numerical example shows how different risk measures (mean-semideviation vs. variance) affect policies.  
- Weakness — Complexity: theory is dense and requires strong background in coherent risk and optimization, making it hard to follow for some readers.  
- Weakness — Limited empirical validation: only a simple numerical example; lacks experiments on standard RL benchmarks or broader comparisons.  
- Weakness — Computational cost: static-risk algorithm requires solving a convex program each iteration, potentially expensive and not fully addressed.  
- Weakness — Reliance on assumptions: results depend on assumptions (e.g., risk envelope) that may not hold in all practical settings; limitations not fully discussed.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound and well-researched, but empirical gaps leave concerns about practical impact.  
- Clarity assessment: generally clear writing but dense notation and equations; would benefit from more intuitive explanations.  
- Originality assessment: novel and significant generalization of risk-sensitive RL with original gradient formulas.  
- Significance assessment: high potential impact if scalability and empirical benefits of different coherent risk choices are demonstrated.  
- Pro for acceptance: significant theoretical generalization, novel algorithms, and strong literature connections.  
- Con against acceptance: limited experiments, potential convex-programming bottleneck, and high mathematical complexity limiting accessibility.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: expand empirical evaluation on benchmark tasks, address computational scalability of convex-program step, and improve clarity/simplify notation with more intuition.
