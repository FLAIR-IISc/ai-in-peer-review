- Summary: Develops policy-gradient methods for risk-sensitive RL under coherent risk measures using dual representation, envelope theorem, and likelihood-ratio tricks to derive gradients.

- Contribution (static): Presents a general gradient formula (Theorem 4.2) via the Lagrangian saddle point, recovers CVaR and gives a closed-form for mean-semideviation.

- Contribution (static estimator): Proposes an SAA gradient estimator combining sampling with a convex program and proves consistency under mild assumptions.

- Contribution (dynamic): Derives a policy-gradient theorem (Theorem 5.2) for time-consistent Markov coherent risk tying ∇θVθ(x) to a value function and ξ*-reweighted transitions.

- Contribution (algorithm): Proposes an actor-critic style algorithm where the critic leverages robust MDP equivalence and the actor uses ξ*-reweighted trajectories.

- Experiment: Small asset-selection experiment shows different coherent risks yield distinct policies but empirical evaluation is minimal.

- Relation to prior work: Unifies and generalizes risk-sensitive PG approaches (mean-variance, CVaR, exponential utility) and connects dynamic coherent risk to robust MDPs.

- Technical soundness: Static gradient derivation and SAA consistency appear correct under stated assumptions; dynamic theorem is a substantial, plausible extension aligning with robust MDP duality.

- Assumptions: Requires explicit, differentiable, convex risk envelopes and computable saddle points/KKT multipliers; finite Ω assumption restricts scope.

- Practical limitations: Dynamic method requires sampling under ξ*-reweighted transitions or model access, limiting purely model-free single-trajectory applicability.

- Completeness: Many proofs, algorithmic details, and convergence analyses are deferred to the supplement, hindering reproduction from the main text.

- Clarity: Writing and theorem statements are clear, but the actor-critic procedure is only sketched and needs pseudocode and complexity discussion.

- Implementation detail gaps: Derivatives dfi/dp, dge/dp need more concrete examples beyond CVaR; sampling under ξ*-weighted dynamics needs discussion (e.g., importance sampling).

- Originality: The unified static gradient, SAA estimator, and especially the policy-gradient theorem for dynamic coherent risk are novel and significant.

- Significance: Provides a general toolkit for time-consistent risk-sensitive RL with connections to robust MDPs, but practical adoption depends on algorithmic feasibility and empirical validation.

- Strengths: Broad, unifying framework; clean duality-based derivations; recovers/simplifies CVaR; new dynamic PG theorem with actor-critic interpretation; theoretical consistency guarantees.

- Weaknesses: Heavy reliance on explicit envelopes and KKT multipliers; computational cost of convex programs per gradient not assessed; dynamic sampling requirements are strong; limited experiments and reproducibility details.

- Suggestions: Add detailed pseudocode and complexity analysis, demonstrate sampling techniques without full model access (importance sampling, doubly robust estimators), expand empirical evaluation on dynamic tasks, and clarify Lp extension and example derivatives beyond CVaR.

- Recommendation: Borderline accept — theoretically strong and novel but weakened by limited experiments and insufficient algorithmic details.

- Reviewer confidence: High on theory; moderate on practical feasibility pending more experiments.
