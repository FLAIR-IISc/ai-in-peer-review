- Summary: Proposes a general policy-gradient framework for any coherent risk measure, with gradient formulas, sampling-based approximation, and an actor-critic for Markov coherent risk.  
- Strength — Generality: Handles the entire class of coherent risk measures, not just specific ones like CVaR.  
- Strength — Theoretical foundation: Strong connections to coherent risk theory, robust MDPs, and dynamic programming (notably Theorems 4.2 and 5.2).  
- Strength — Novel gradients: Elegant gradient formulas (e.g., Theorem 4.2) suitable for sampling-based estimation.  
- Strength — Algorithms: Sampling-based algorithms for static and dynamic risk that aim to scale beyond DP methods.  
- Strength — Robustness link: Clarifies the relation between coherent risk and robust MDPs, enabling cross-methodology use.  
- Strength — Illustrative example: Numerical example shows how different risk criteria affect policy choice.  
- Weakness — Complexity: Dense mathematical presentation that may be hard to follow without more intuition.  
- Weakness — Implementation details: Insufficient details on actor-critic implementation, function approximation, hyperparameters, and sampling strategies.  
- Weakness — Empirical evaluation: Limited experiments; needs broader benchmarks and comparisons (e.g., CVaR-based methods).  
- Weakness — Assumption dependence: Results rely on assumptions (e.g., risk envelope) with limited discussion of practical implications.  
- Weakness — Computational cost: Potentially expensive sampling and convex programming steps not thoroughly addressed.  
- Qualitative — Quality: 8/10 (technically sound but empirically limited).  
- Qualitative — Clarity: 6/10 (precise but dense; heavy notation).  
- Qualitative — Originality: 9/10 (significant generalization/unification).  
- Qualitative — Significance: 8/10 (promising scalability and applicability; robust MDP link valuable).  
- Accept arguments: General, theoretically sound framework; novel gradient estimation; unifies prior work; opens new research directions.  
- Reject/concern arguments: Lacks extensive empirical validation; sparse implementation and computational-cost discussion; dense presentation; reliance on restrictive assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Requested revisions: Add detailed actor-critic implementation info, expand empirical benchmarks and comparisons, discuss computational costs/optimizations, clarify assumption implications/robustness, and improve presentation with intuitive explanations and examples.
