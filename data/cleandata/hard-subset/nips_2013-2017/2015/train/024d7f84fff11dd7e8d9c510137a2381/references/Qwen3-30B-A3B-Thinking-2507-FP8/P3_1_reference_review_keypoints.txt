- Proposes a unified policy-gradient framework for reinforcement learning with the full class of coherent risk measures, generalizing beyond specific risks like CVaR or mean-variance.
- Main technical contributions: new gradient formula for static coherent risk enabling sampling-based estimation.
- Main technical contributions: a general gradient estimation algorithm that combines sampling with convex programming.
- Main technical contributions: a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent (time-consistent dynamic) risk measures.
- Leverages the representation theorem for coherent risks (ρ(Z)=max_ξ E_ξ[Z] over a convex risk envelope) to derive and unify gradient formulas.
- Theoretical development is rigorous with well-motivated assumptions and carefully derived theorems.
- Conceptual novelty in unifying risk-sensitive RL under coherent risk measures, simplifying and generalizing prior specific-case work.
- Provides clear examples and a numerical illustration showing how different coherent risk choices lead to different policies.
- Draws relevant connections between coherent risk measures and model uncertainty, deepening conceptual understanding.
- Proposes a practical two-step sampling + convex programming procedure with a consistency result (Proposition 4.4).
- Lacks comprehensive empirical comparisons against specialized algorithms for specific risk measures (e.g., CVaR baselines).
- Does not adequately discuss computational complexity of the convex programming step, especially as the risk envelope grows.
- Omits full algorithmic details (actor-critic) in the main text due to space, which is problematic for reproducibility.
- Convergence analysis for the dynamic risk actor-critic is relegated to the supplement and lacks discussion on critic approximation errors’ impact.
- Assumes the risk envelope is known in canonical convex form (Assumption 2.2) without discussing how to obtain or approximate it for complex measures.
- Arguments for acceptance: high theoretical quality, strong conceptual contribution, clear practical implications, novelty, and relevance to the RL community.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation, incomplete algorithm description, underdeveloped computational analysis, and potentially overstated scalability claims.
- Overall assessment: theoretically sound and conceptually important work that substantially advances risk-sensitive RL, despite empirical and practical detail gaps.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Suggested improvements: add comparisons with specialized risk algorithms (e.g., CVaR), provide detailed actor-critic algorithm and critic approximation method.
- Suggested improvements: discuss computational complexity and scaling of the convex programming step in depth.
- Suggested improvements: expand numerical experiments with convergence analysis and final performance metrics.
- Suggested improvements: discuss how to obtain or learn/approximate the risk envelope when it isn’t available in canonical form.
