- Overall assessment: Strong, significant contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem addressed: Policy gradients for minimizing coherent risk measures in MDPs, unifying many prior approaches.  
- Positioning: Correctly relates to risk-sensitive RL, robust MDPs, and stochastic programming, highlighting generalization over prior work.  
- Generality strength: Method handles the full class of coherent risk measures, a major advantage over prior specialized methods.  
- Theoretical soundness: Gradient derivations (e.g., Theorem 4.2) and sampling-consistency (Proposition 4.4) are rigorous and well-founded.  
- Practical algorithms: Combines sampling-based gradient estimation with convex programming and proposes an actor-critic method for dynamic risk.  
- Writing and organization: Generally clear, consistent notation, and well-structured exposition.  
- Connection to robust MDPs: Highlighted as a useful theoretical bridge and avenue for leveraging existing techniques.  
- Limited numerical evaluation: Empirical tests are simple and single-scenario; broader, more challenging experiments and baselines are needed.  
- Implementation complexity: Actor-critic implementation details, hyperparameter tuning, and function approximation choices need more practical discussion.  
- Strong assumption: Assuming a known risk envelope (Assumption 2.2) is restrictive; methods for relaxing or learning it should be discussed.  
- Minor clarifications needed: Some derivations (e.g., link between (7) and gradient estimator (8)) require clearer exposition.  
- For acceptance: Significant theoretical unification, broad applicability, sound algorithms, and clear writing.  
- Against acceptance: Insufficient empirical validation, strong risk-envelope assumption, and incomplete discussion of implementation challenges.  
- Specific requests: Discuss sample size vs. gradient accuracy (Sec 4.3), detail function approximation choices and effects (Sec 5), provide intuition for complex terms like hθ(x,a) (Thm 5.2), and compare to baseline risk-averse methods in experiments.
