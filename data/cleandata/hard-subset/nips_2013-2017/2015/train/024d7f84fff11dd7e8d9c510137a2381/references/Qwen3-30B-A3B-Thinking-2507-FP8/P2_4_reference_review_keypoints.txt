- Summary: Introduces policy gradient methods for the full class of coherent risk measures, deriving gradient formulas for static and dynamic risk and proposing sampling-based and actor-critic algorithms.  
- Key contributions: (1) gradient formula for static coherent risk enabling sampling, (2) sampling + convex-programming algorithm for static risk, (3) policy gradient theorem and actor-critic method for Markov coherent risk.  
- Quality: Technically rigorous with proven theorems (representation, gradient formulas) and a consistency result supporting sampling estimators.  
- Empirical validation: Numerical portfolio optimization shows different coherent risks yield different policies, supporting practical relevance.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, with consistent notation and illustrative examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation).  
- Originality: Novel unification of risk-sensitive RL across all coherent risk measures, with new gradient results not previously known.  
- Significance: Substantially advances risk-sensitive RL by enabling general-purpose risk-averse policy design and linking to model uncertainty/robustness.  
- Strengths: Strong theoretical foundation, clear unification of prior work, practical numerical demonstration, transparent assumptions and limitations, and high-quality presentation.  
- Weaknesses: Convex-programming step may be computationally costly for large problems, limited empirical comparisons to state-of-the-art benchmarks, and narrower demonstration of application domains beyond the portfolio example.  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, original, and significant contribution; recommended for acceptance.  
- Pro (accept): Unifies prior work, provides strong theoretical results, and demonstrates practical utility.  
- Con (concerns): Scalability due to convex programming, limited empirical benchmarking, and need for broader application examples.
