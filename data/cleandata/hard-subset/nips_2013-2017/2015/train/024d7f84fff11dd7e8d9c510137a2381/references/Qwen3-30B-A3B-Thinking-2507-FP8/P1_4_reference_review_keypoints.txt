- Introduces policy gradient algorithms for the entire class of coherent risk measures, generalizing prior work limited to specific measures.  
- Provides two main theoretical contributions: a sampling-friendly gradient formula for static coherent risk and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures.  
- Leverages the representation theorem for coherent risk and the envelope theorem; proofs (Theorems 4.2, 5.2) are rigorous and well-supported in supplements.  
- Establishes consistency of the gradient estimator (Proposition 4.4) and gives theoretical guarantees for sampling-based methods.  
- Numerical example demonstrates practical importance of choosing appropriate risk measures and shows different measures lead to markedly different policies.  
- Acknowledges limitations such as the need for a simulator to sample multiple transitions and provides clear, justified assumptions.  
- Well-written and organized with consistent notation; suggests adding a high-level diagram to illustrate static vs dynamic risk and their gradients.  
- Original and novel: unifies risk-sensitive RL across all coherent risk measures and recovers known special-case results.  
- Extends the classical policy gradient theorem to dynamic coherent risk measures — a non-trivial theoretical advance.  
- Significant practical relevance for applications requiring risk management and opens connections to model uncertainty.  
- Strengths: unified framework, rigorous theory, practical demonstration, clear links to prior work, principled approach to risk selection.  
- Weaknesses: actor-critic algorithmic details are brief (space-limited), simulator requirement may limit real-world applicability, and empirical comparisons between estimators need more detail.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept for NIPS; final decision: Accept.  
- Pro-accept: general framework, rigorous theoretical results, compelling numerical example, connection to model uncertainty.  
- Con-accept: limited algorithmic detail in paper and reliance on simulator for multiple transition sampling.
