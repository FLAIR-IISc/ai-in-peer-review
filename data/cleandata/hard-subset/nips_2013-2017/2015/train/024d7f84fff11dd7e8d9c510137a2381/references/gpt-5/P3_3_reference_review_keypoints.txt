- Develops policy gradient methods for coherent risk in RL for static risks and time-consistent Markov coherent (dynamic) risks.

- Derives a general gradient formula for static coherent risk via the dual (risk envelope) and envelope theorem: expectation under adversarial density ξ* plus KKT-weighted envelope-derivative terms (Thm. 4.2).

- Instantiates the formula for CVaR and mean-semideviation, recovering/simplifying known results and producing sampling-friendly expressions.

- Proposes an SAA scheme: estimate ξ* and multipliers by solving a convex program on samples, then plug into the gradient; provides a consistency result.

- Proves a policy gradient theorem for dynamic Markov coherent risk (Thm. 5.2): ∇θV equals an expectation over ξ*-reweighted trajectories of a score times a stage cost hθ.

- Proposes an actor-critic algorithm: a robust-MDP-style critic with function approximation and an actor using ξ* and hθ to estimate gradients.

- Presents a toy experiment comparing risk-neutral, mean-semideviation, and mean-variance policies in a heavy-tailed bandit.

- Unifies prior work across exponential utility, mean-variance, CVaR, dynamic coherent risk, and robust MDPs; builds on Shapiro–Dentcheva–Ruszczyński’s theory adapted to Pθ-dependent distributions.

- Strength: strong conceptual generality—provides a unifying recipe for policy gradients across coherent risks.

- Strength: methodological novelty—using the risk-envelope Lagrangian and envelope theorem; broadly applicable SAA estimator with consistency.

- Strength: meaningful dynamic extension aligning risk-sensitive dynamic programming with actor-critic methods.

- Strength: leverages connections to robust MDPs for the critic and reuse of scalable methods.

- Weakness: computational practicality—each gradient needs convex solves (potentially per state and as Vθ changes); no complexity/runtime analysis or warm-start strategies.

- Weakness: sampling under ξ*-reweighted dynamics assumes access to generative model or multiple next-state samples; strong assumption not analyzed for variance/bias.

- Weakness: non-smoothness—insufficient treatment of set-valued gradients/subgradients and KKT-multiplier uniqueness at non-smooth points.

- Weakness: reliance on explicit, differentiable envelope constraints and access to their derivatives limits applicability to tractable risks.

- Weakness: limited empirical validation—only a static bandit, no dynamic MDP experiments, no variance/sample-efficiency/scalability studies.

- Weakness: finite probability space assumption—extensions to continuous/Lp settings are claimed but details and measurability/differentiability issues are omitted.

- Quality: theoretical development is sound and grounded in convex analysis, but several important details are deferred to the supplement and dynamic results rely on strong sampling assumptions.

- Clarity: generally clear given density, but lacks actor-critic pseudocode and explicit summary of practical requirements (generative model need, per-state convex solves, envelope-derivative computation).

- Originality: original and valuable generalization to the full class of coherent risks and to time-consistent dynamic risk.

- Significance: could be a reference framework if practical challenges are addressed; current empirical and computational gaps limit immediate impact.

- Pros for acceptance: unifying, elegant theoretical framework; generalizes several prior results; introduces new dynamic policy gradient theorem.

- Cons for acceptance: insufficient empirical validation, potentially impractical sampling/computational requirements, and inadequate discussion of non-smoothness and multiplier uniqueness.

- Suggestions: add dynamic MDP experiments and runtime/variance analysis; provide algorithm boxes and implementation details, warm-starting/amortization strategies.

- Suggestions: clarify subgradient treatment and KKT uniqueness; discuss single-trajectory settings and analyze importance-sampling variance.

- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on strengthened empirical section and clarification of practical assumptions.

- Reviewer confidence: High.
