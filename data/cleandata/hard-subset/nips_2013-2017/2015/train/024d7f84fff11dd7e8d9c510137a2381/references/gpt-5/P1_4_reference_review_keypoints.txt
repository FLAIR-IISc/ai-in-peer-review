- Paper develops policy-gradient methods for static coherent risk and time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk in RL.
- Core contribution 1: general likelihood-ratio-style gradient formula for static coherent risk using the dual (risk envelope) and envelope theorem.
- Core contribution 2: sample average approximation (SAA) scheme computing saddle points via convex programming and using KKT multipliers for consistent gradient estimation.
- Core contribution 3: a dynamic “policy gradient theorem” expressing gradients as expectations under ξ*-reweighted transitions, enabling an actor-critic algorithm.
- Positions work against prior risk-sensitive RL (exponential utility, mean-variance, CVaR, robust MDPs) and leverages dual coherent-risk theory and robust MDP connections.
- Static gradient derivation is elegant and appears sound under stated assumptions, recovering CVaR and deriving mean-semideviation correctly.
- SAA-based estimator and consistency result are natural and technically reasonable under standard stochastic programming assumptions.
- Dynamic gradient theorem is novel and carefully accounts for risk-envelope derivative effects and changed sampling distribution.
- Practical issues: requires solving convex programs (possibly per state) to get ξ* and KKT multipliers, which can be expensive.
- Sampling concerns: dynamic method needs sampling from ξ*-weighted model or importance sampling, which is assumed but not resolved.
- Critic uses function approximation for a robust MDP, but convergence and implementation details are deferred to the supplement.
- Empirical evaluation is very limited (toy bandit), with no dynamic MDP experiments or comparisons to risk-sensitive baselines.
- No evaluation of estimator variance, computational cost, or scalability in experiments.
- Clarity is good overall, but heavy notation; more guidance needed on computing constraint derivatives for common envelopes.
- Missing explicit Markov chain assumptions (ergodicity/mixing) for ξ*-weighted kernel and conditions for interchanging gradient and infinite sums.
- Non-differentiability and non-uniqueness issues (e.g., CVaR ties, saddle points) are not discussed; need statement about subgradients.
- Paper lacks pseudocode for SAA gradient and actor-critic and a complexity/runtime discussion to aid reproducibility.
- Originality: unifying envelope-theorem-based gradient and dynamic policy gradient for coherent risk are novel and significant extensions.
- Potential significance: could broadly impact risk-sensitive RL if made practical, but current algorithmic and empirical gaps limit immediate applicability.
- Pros: broad principled framework, clean theoretical results, recovers known cases and yields new ones, ties to robust MDPs.
- Cons: minimal experiments, strong assumptions (finite spaces, smooth known envelopes, bounded score), practical burden of convex solves and derivative computations, missing subgradient/ergodicity discussion.
- Suggestions: add dynamic MDP experiments and baselines, provide pseudocode and implementation details for ξ*/KKT and derivatives, discuss subgradients and ergodicity, and explore importance sampling and variance reduction strategies.
- Recommendation: Weak reject due to insufficient empirical validation and practical gaps despite strong theoretical contributions.
