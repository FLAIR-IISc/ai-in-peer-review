- Overall: valuable, theoretically solid contribution on policy gradients for coherent risk; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Main idea: optimize MDP policies under coherent risk measures instead of expected reward.
- Method: use the representation theorem for coherent risk to derive policy gradient formulas for static and dynamic risk.
- Contribution: provides a sampling-friendly gradient formula for static coherent risk with consistency results.
- Contribution: develops a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and an actor-critic approach for the dynamic case.
- Generality: framework handles the full class of coherent risk measures, generalizing CVaR and mean-variance approaches.
- Theoretical soundness: derivations are rigorous, leveraging Envelope Theorem and likelihood ratios.
- Connection: links coherent risk RL to robust MDPs, enabling cross-fertilization with robust methods.
- Scalability: sampling-based methods plus function approximation suggest potential to scale to large MDPs.
- Practical relevance: trading-agent numerical example demonstrates impact of different risk criteria.
- Clarity issues: presentation is dense; key theorems (e.g., Theorem 4.2) need more intuitive explanation and intermediate steps.
- Algorithmic detail lacking: actor-critic algorithm needs fuller specification, pseudo-code, and details on critic estimation and ξ* computation.
- Assumption 2.2: needs clearer discussion of practical implications and explicit examples of risk measures that satisfy it.
- Experimental limitations: empirical evaluation is limited; more benchmarks and comparisons are needed to support scalability and performance claims.
- SAA estimator: clarify implementation choices (sample size N, convergence criteria for convex program).
- Section 5: provide explicit details on value function estimation and ξ* computation, preferably with pseudo-code.
- Notation: fix inconsistent state notation (use either ω or x throughout).
- Overall recommendation restated: accept with minor revisions contingent on improved clarity, algorithmic detail, and expanded experiments.
