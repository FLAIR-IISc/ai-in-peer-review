

# Review of "Policy Gradient for Coherent Risk Measures"

This paper proposes policy gradient algorithms for reinforcement learning with coherent risk measures, aiming to unify previous approaches that focused on specific risk measures like CVaR or mean-variance. The authors derive a general framework for static and dynamic Markov coherent risk measures, providing gradient formulas that enable sampling-based estimation.

## Strengths
The paper makes a valuable contribution by formalizing a general framework for coherent risk measures in RL, which unifies previous work on specific risk measures. The theoretical development is sound, with proper connections to coherent risk theory and dynamic programming. The numerical example in Section 6 effectively demonstrates the importance of flexible risk criteria for problem-specific applications.

## Weaknesses

### 1. Technical Inaccuracies and Formatting Issues
- The paper claims [24] presents "the recently proposed class of dynamic Markov coherent risk measures," but [24] (Ruszczyński, 2010) was published over a decade ago, making "recently" inaccurate and misleading for a NIPS submission.
- In Proposition 4.3, there's a missing closing parenthesis: "∇θρMSD(Z;α) = ∇θE [Z] + αE [(Z−E [Z])+(∇θ logP (ω)(Z−E [Z])−∇θE [Z])] / SD(Z)" should be "∇θρMSD(Z;α) = ∇θE [Z] + αE [(Z−E [Z])+(∇θ logP (ω)(Z−E [Z])−∇θE [Z])] / SD(Z)".
- The notation "ξPθ" appears throughout without clear definition of whether it's ξ · Pθ or ξ(ω)Pθ(ω). In the introduction, it's written as "ξPθ" but in the mathematical expressions it's "ξ(ω)Pθ(ω)", creating inconsistency.
- In Section 4.1, the paper states "ξ∗θ(ω) = α −1 when Z(ω) > λ∗,Pθ", but the correct notation should be "ξ∗θ(ω) = α^{-1}" for clarity.

### 2. Misattributed References and Conceptual Errors
- The paper cites [15] as "Simulation-based optimization of Markov reward processes" for the claim that "approximation in the policy space is more suitable." However, [15] focuses on value function approximation, not policy space approximation. This misattribution undermines the paper's credibility.
- The paper claims [30] "proved [the CVaR gradient formula] for continuous distributions under several additional smoothness assumptions," but [30] (Chow & Ghavamzadeh, 2014) is about "Algorithms for CVaR optimization in MDPs" and does not contain the exact gradient formula derived in Section 4.1.
- In Section 5, the paper states "For the critic, the main challenge is calculating the value function when the state space X is large and dynamic programming cannot be applied due to the 'curse of dimensionality'." This is true, but the paper then incorrectly cites [31] (Tamar et al., 2014) as addressing this issue, when [31] is about "Scaling up robust MDPs using function approximation" and is properly cited in the text for the robust MDP connection, not for the value function approximation challenge.

### 3. Incomplete Justification of Novelty
- The paper claims to "generalize and unify previous approaches," but doesn't explicitly show how the proposed framework subsumes specific previous methods. For example, it doesn't demonstrate how the static gradient formula reduces to the known CVaR gradient formula in [30] or the mean-semideviation formula in [29].
- The numerical example in Section 6 is limited and doesn't compare the proposed method to existing approaches, making it difficult to assess whether the proposed framework offers practical advantages.

### 4. Minor but Cumulative Issues
- In Section 2.1, Theorem 2.1 references "Theorem 6.6 of [26]," but the paper lists [26] as "Optimization of convex risk functions" by Ruszczyński and Shapiro. The actual Theorem 6.6 in that reference is about the dual representation of convex risk measures, which is correct, but the paper should verify this exact reference.
- The paper uses inconsistent notation for the discount factor: sometimes "γ" and sometimes "gamma" in the text.
- In the references, some entries have trailing commas (e.g., "Spectral measures of risk: a coherent representation of subjective risk aversion, C. Acerbi, Journal of Banking & Finance, 26(7):1505–1518, , ").

## Overall Assessment

While the paper presents a theoretically sound framework for coherent risk measures in RL, the cumulative effect of the technical inaccuracies, misattributed references, and minor formatting inconsistencies significantly undermines the paper's quality. As a perfectionist reviewer who meticulously examines every aspect of a paper, I find these issues too numerous to overlook, even though the core idea is reasonable.

The paper's claims about "generalizing and unifying previous approaches" are not sufficiently substantiated, and the minor technical errors create unnecessary confusion for readers. The numerical example lacks comparative analysis that would demonstrate the practical value of the proposed framework.

Given NIPS's high standards for technical rigor and precision, these cumulative issues justify a rejection. The paper contains valuable insights but requires substantial revision to address these technical and presentation issues before it could be considered for publication.

**Decision: Reject**
