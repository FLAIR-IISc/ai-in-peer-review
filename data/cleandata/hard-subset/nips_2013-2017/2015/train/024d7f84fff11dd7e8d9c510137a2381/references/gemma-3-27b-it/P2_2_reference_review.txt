## Review of "Policy Gradient for Coherent Risk Measures"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel framework for applying policy gradient methods to reinforcement learning problems with coherent risk objectives. It generalizes existing risk-sensitive RL approaches by providing a unified treatment for the entire class of coherent risk measures, both static and dynamic (time-consistent). The core contributions include a new gradient formula for static coherent risk, an algorithm for its estimation involving convex programming, and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk leading to an actor-critic algorithm. The authors successfully bridge the gap between theoretical coherence properties and practical policy optimization.

**Strengths:**

* **Generality & Unification:** The work provides a very general framework, subsuming previous results for specific risk measures like CVaR and mean-variance as special cases. This is a significant strength, offering a unified perspective on risk-sensitive RL.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper builds on a strong theoretical foundation, leveraging results from coherent risk theory and utilizing the envelope theorem to derive novel gradient formulas. The proofs appear rigorous (though a full check requires reviewing the supplementary material).
* **Practical Algorithms:** The derived gradient formulas are amenable to sampling-based estimation, making the approach applicable to large-scale and continuous MDPs.  The proposed algorithm incorporating convex programming appears well-motivated.
* **Clear Connections to Existing Work:**  The paper thoroughly reviews related work in risk-sensitive RL and robust MDPs, clearly positioning its contributions.
* **Illustrative Example:** The numerical example demonstrates the practical impact of choosing different coherent risk measures and highlights the potential for nuanced control over risk preferences.
* **Addresses a key challenge:** The paper directly tackles the challenge of applying coherent risk measures – which are theoretically appealing but often computationally difficult – to the setting of policy gradients.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The approach, while general, is relatively complex. The need for convex programming within the gradient estimation process adds overhead. While the authors claim scalability, the practical computational cost relative to simpler risk-sensitive methods isn't fully explored.
* **Assumption Dependence:** The results rely on assumptions like Assumption 2.2 (the general form of the risk envelope) and 4.1 (bounded likelihood ratio). The sensitivity of the method to violations of these assumptions isn't discussed.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The numerical illustration is limited in scope.  A more comprehensive empirical evaluation on benchmark RL tasks, comparing performance against existing risk-sensitive RL algorithms (e.g., those based on exponential utility) would significantly strengthen the paper.  It's unclear how sensitive performance is to the approximations used in the actor-critic algorithm.
* **Dynamic Risk Algorithm Details:** The description of the actor-critic algorithm for the dynamic case is somewhat high-level.  More details on the function approximation scheme for the value function and the sampling strategy would be helpful.



**Review Criteria Assessment:**

* **Quality:**  High. The paper is technically sound, with a clear connection between theory and algorithms. The derivations appear correct, and the method builds upon established results. The assumption checks needed to fully assess quality require deeper review of the supplementary material.
* **Clarity:** Good. The paper is generally well-written, though some sections (particularly those detailing the dynamic risk algorithm) could benefit from more elaboration. The notation is consistent, and the authors clearly define their terms.
* **Originality:** High. The generalization to the entire class of coherent risk measures and the corresponding gradient formulas are novel contributions. While building on existing ideas, the paper provides a significant advance in the field.
* **Significance:** Good. The ability to apply coherent risk measures to policy gradient methods has the potential to be impactful.  This work opens up new avenues for designing more sophisticated and robust RL agents that can explicitly manage risk.


**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Provides a unifying framework for risk-sensitive RL.
* Derives novel and theoretically sound gradient formulas.
* Offers a practical algorithm leveraging sampling and convex programming.
* Addresses a significant challenge in applying coherent risk measures.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Computational complexity may limit scalability.
* Limited empirical evaluation hinders assessment of practical performance.
* The details of the dynamic risk algorithm could be more fleshed out.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions. The work is a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. Addressing the weaknesses regarding empirical evaluation (adding more benchmarks) and algorithm details (providing a clearer description of the dynamic risk actor-critic procedure) would further strengthen the paper. The authors should also briefly discuss the potential impact of violating key assumptions. Despite these minor points, the paper’s originality, theoretical soundness, and potential impact make it a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings.
