- Develops policy-gradient methods for optimizing coherent risk objectives using dual representation and an envelope-theorem argument.  
- Static result: gives a general gradient formula (Theorem 4.2) for coherent risks with convex-form risk envelopes.  
- Static: recovers CVaR, derives a new mean–semideviation gradient, and presents an SAA estimator with consistency (Proposition 4.4).  
- Dynamic result: provides a policy-gradient theorem (Theorem 5.2) for time-consistent Markov coherent risk using ξ-weighted transitions and a stage-wise hθ term.  
- Proposes an actor-critic sketch where the critic solves a robust-MDP value and the actor uses the new gradient; illustrated by a small toy showing risk-dependent policies.  
- Prior work is well cited; paper unifies and generalizes prior RL risk-sensitive and robust MDP results.  
- Static theoretical derivations are technically sound under stated assumptions (finite Ω, Slater-type conditions, differentiability).  
- The SAA estimator and consistency follow standard but useful lines; assumptions are reasonable for CVaR, mean-semideviation, spectral risks.  
- Dynamic theorem is conceptually strong but depends on access to per-state saddle points (ξ*, λ*) and the ability to sample from ξ*-reweighted transitions.  
- The actor-critic algorithm is only sketched; convergence guarantees, function-approximation error analysis, ergodicity under reweighted dynamics, and ξ* approximation bias are deferred.  
- Empirical evaluation is minimal (single-step static toy); no dynamic MDP experiments, variance analysis, or runtime/solver overhead reported.  
- Lacks finite-sample error analysis (variance bounds, sample complexity) and practical variance-reduction techniques beyond noting importance sampling.  
- Paper is generally clear and organized; the role of the risk envelope is well explained.  
- Notation is heavy; providing explicit pseudocode for the static estimator and actor-critic would improve reproducibility.  
- Assumption 2.2 is central and should be instantiated in the main text for CVaR, mean-semideviation, and a spectral risk (including dfi/dp).  
- The requirement for a simulator that can generate multiple next-state samples and simulate ξ*-weighted transitions should be highlighted earlier and practicality discussed for single on-policy rollouts.  
- Originality is high: extends policy-gradient methods to the full class of coherent risks and introduces a dynamic PG theorem while recovering known cases.  
- Significance is broad for risk-sensitive RL and robust control, but practical impact is tempered by computational and access requirements.  
- Pros: general unifying framework, clean duality/envelope derivations, SAA consistency, recovers/simplifies CVaR, new mean–semideviation gradient, links to robust MDPs.  
- Cons: sparse empirical validation, per-state convex-program/ξ* computation and sampling challenges, many proofs/assumptions in supplement, missing finite-sample analysis, thin algorithmic details.  
- Suggestions: clarify sampling from ξ* without a generative model (importance sampling/variance control), provide explicit dfi/dp examples in main text, discuss ergodicity of ξ*-reweighted chains, add at least one dynamic MDP experiment with runtime/solver metrics, and address variance reduction and CVaR quantile non-differentiability.  
- Minor: fix incomplete bibliographic entries and reduce disruptive footnotes.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — strong theoretical novelty and generality but limited practicality and empirical validation.
