## Peer Review: Policy Gradient for Coherent Risk Measures

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the growing field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. It offers a unifying framework for applying policy gradient methods to a broad class of coherent risk measures, generalizing previous work focused on specific risk criteria. The theoretical results are solid, and the connection to robust MDPs and the potential for scaling to large problems are significant. However, the presentation could benefit from improved clarity and a more detailed explanation of the algorithmic implications. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**.

**1. Summary of Main Ideas:**

The paper tackles the problem of optimizing policies in Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) under coherent risk measures. Unlike standard RL which focuses on expected reward, this framework aims to manage the variability of costs.  The authors leverage the representation theorem for coherent risk measures (expressing them as worst-case expectations) to derive novel policy gradient formulas for both static and dynamic coherent risk. Key contributions include: a convenient gradient formula for static coherent risk amenable to sampling, a consistency result for its estimation, and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk leading to an actor-critic algorithm.  The work effectively generalizes existing approaches (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance) and provides a unifying theoretical foundation.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Generality:** The framework’s ability to handle the entire class of coherent risk measures is a significant strength. This offers flexibility to practitioners and researchers in choosing a risk criterion appropriate for their application.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivations of the gradient formulas appear rigorous, building on established results from coherent risk theory and policy gradient methods. The use of the Envelope Theorem and likelihood ratios is appropriate and well-executed.
* **Connection to Robust MDPs:**  The identified connection to robust MDPs highlights the potential for leveraging existing techniques from that field and opens avenues for further research.
* **Scalability Potential:**  The sampling-based approach, combined with function approximation for the dynamic case, offers a pathway to scale the algorithms to large, complex MDPs.
* **Practical Relevance:** The numerical illustration with a trading agent effectively demonstrates the impact of different risk criteria and the potential benefits of the proposed framework.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity & Organization:**  The presentation is dense, and certain sections (e.g., Theorem 4.2 and its application) would benefit from more intuitive explanations and intermediate steps. The jump from theoretical results to algorithmic implications could be smoother.
* **Algorithm Detail:** While the actor-critic algorithm is mentioned, it lacks a detailed specification. A more complete description, including pseudo-code, would enhance the paper’s practical impact.  Specifically, the details of how the critic estimates the value function in the dynamic case, and how the ξ* is calculated, are lacking.
* **Assumption 2.2:** The generality of Assumption 2.2 is appreciated, but a clearer discussion of its practical implications and potential limitations would be valuable. It is stated that it holds for many popular risk measures, but a more explicit list of examples would be helpful.
* **Experimental Evaluation:**  The numerical illustration is insightful, but limited. A more comprehensive empirical evaluation on benchmark MDPs with varying state/action spaces and different risk criteria would strengthen the claims of scalability and performance.

**4. Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:**  The paper is technically sound and demonstrates a thorough understanding of the relevant literature. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis. It is a complete piece of work in terms of theoretical development.
* **Clarity:**  As noted above, clarity is an area for improvement.  The mathematical notation is standard but dense, and explanations could be more intuitive.
* **Originality:**  The work presents a novel combination of coherent risk theory and policy gradient methods, offering a significant generalization of previous work. The derived gradient formulas and the actor-critic algorithm represent original contributions.
* **Significance:** The results are important as they provide a principled framework for risk-sensitive RL with broad applicability. The potential for scaling to large problems and the connection to robust MDPs further enhance the significance of this work. Researchers and practitioners interested in risk management and decision-making under uncertainty are likely to find this paper valuable.



**5. Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Provides a unifying framework for risk-sensitive RL.
* Derives novel and theoretically sound gradient formulas.
* Offers a path towards scalable algorithms for large MDPs.
* Connects to existing research in robust MDPs.
* Demonstrates practical relevance through a compelling example.

**Con:**

* The presentation could be clearer and more intuitive.
* The algorithm specification is incomplete.
* The empirical evaluation is limited.




**6. Specific Comments & Suggestions:**

* **Section 4.3:** Clarify the practical considerations for implementing the SAA estimator (e.g., choice of N, convergence criteria for the convex program).
* **Section 5:**  Provide more details on the critic’s estimation of the value function in the dynamic case and the computation of ξ*. Pseudo-code would be very helpful.
* **Assumption 2.2:**  Expand on its implications and provide specific examples of risk measures that satisfy it.
* **Experimentation:** Include results on standard MDP benchmark tasks.  Compare the performance of the proposed algorithms to existing risk-sensitive RL methods.
* **Minor:** Throughout the paper, consistently use either "ω" or "x" for states (the notation is somewhat mixed).




**7. Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with minor revisions.**  The paper presents a strong theoretical contribution with significant potential impact. Addressing the identified weaknesses, particularly improving clarity and providing a more detailed algorithmic specification, will further enhance its value to the NIPS community.
