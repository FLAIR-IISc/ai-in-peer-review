This paper tackles the important problem of policy gradient optimization for reinforcement learning with coherent risk measures, generalizing previous work focused on specific risk metrics. The authors present a comprehensive framework, providing gradient formulas for both static and dynamic coherent risk, and propose an actor-critic algorithm for dynamic risk optimization. The work is well-motivated, clearly written, and demonstrates a strong understanding of the relevant literature in risk-sensitive RL and coherent risk theory.

**Strengths:**

* **Generality:** The primary strength is the broad applicability of the proposed approach. By working directly with the framework of coherent risk measures, the authors unify a diverse range of existing risk-sensitive RL methods as special cases.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of the gradient formulas, especially Theorem 4.2 and Theorem 5.2, appears technically sound and leverages the duality results from coherent risk theory effectively.  The connection to robust MDPs is also a valuable insight.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is consistent, and the concepts are explained clearly, making it accessible to researchers familiar with RL and optimization.
* **Practical Algorithm:**  The proposed actor-critic algorithm, despite its complexity, offers a practical pathway to apply the theoretical results to large-scale MDPs, utilizing function approximation techniques.
* **Illustrative Example:** The numerical illustration provides a compelling demonstration of the practical impact of different coherent risk measures on policy selection and highlights the benefit of framework’s flexibility.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Cost:** The reliance on convex programming to compute the saddle point of the Lagrangian (as required for the gradient estimation in the general case) could be computationally expensive, especially for high-dimensional state spaces. The authors acknowledge this but a more detailed discussion of potential approximations or efficient solvers would be beneficial.
* **Assumptions:**  While the assumptions made (e.g., Assumption 2.2, Assumption 4.1, Assumption 5.1) are reasonable, their impact on the practical applicability of the approach needs further discussion.  The smoothness requirement on the constraints in Assumption 2.2, while common, may not hold for all coherent risk measures.
* **Empirical Evaluation:** The numerical example, while insightful, is relatively simple. A more comprehensive empirical evaluation on benchmark RL tasks with different risk preferences would strengthen the claim of practical utility.  Comparing against existing risk-sensitive RL algorithms (e.g., CVaR-based methods) on standard tasks would be particularly valuable.
* **Convergence Analysis:** While the paper mentions convergence analysis in the supplementary material, a more concise and accessible summary of the key convergence results within the main body of the paper would improve its readability.
* **Implementation Details:** The paper lacks detailed information regarding implementation choices. This includes details on the convex programming solver used, the function approximator employed in the actor-critic algorithm, and specific hyperparameter settings.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses an important and open problem in reinforcement learning with a novel and theoretically sound approach.
* The generality of the framework and the unification of existing methods are significant contributions.
* The clear writing and organization make the paper accessible and easy to understand.
* The potential for practical application, supported by the illustrative example, is promising.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The computational cost associated with the convex programming step could limit the scalability of the approach.
* The empirical evaluation is limited and lacks comparison to existing methods on standard benchmarks.
* The detailed convergence analysis is relegated to the supplementary material, making it less accessible.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant contributions to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The generality, theoretical soundness, and clear presentation make it a valuable addition to the NIPS program. While the computational cost and limited empirical evaluation are valid concerns, they do not outweigh the strengths of the work. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. 

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Discuss potential approximations for the convex programming step** to reduce the computational burden.
* **Expand the empirical evaluation** to include more challenging RL tasks and comparison against existing risk-sensitive algorithms.
* **Provide a concise summary of the key convergence results** within the main body of the paper.
* **Include more implementation details** in the supplementary material to facilitate reproducibility.
* **Elaborate on the practical implications of the assumptions** made and discuss potential strategies for handling cases where these assumptions are not fully satisfied.
