

# Review of "Policy Gradient for Coherent Risk Measures"

This paper presents a significant conceptual contribution by developing policy gradient methods for reinforcement learning with coherent risk measures. The authors generalize previous work on risk-sensitive RL, which has typically focused on specific risk measures like CVaR or mean-variance, to the entire class of coherent risk measures. This is a substantial theoretical unification that provides a single framework for handling diverse risk preferences in sequential decision-making.

The paper makes two key theoretical contributions: (1) a new gradient formula for static coherent risk measures that enables sampling-based estimation, and (2) a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures that extends the classical policy gradient theorem to risk-sensitive settings. The authors demonstrate how their approach generalizes and simplifies previous results for specific risk measures (like CVaR and mean-semideviation), providing a unified perspective that is both theoretically elegant and practically useful.

The paper builds on the well-established theory of coherent risk measures (Artzner et al., 1999) and Markov coherent risk measures (Pflug & Pichler, 2012), but makes a novel connection to reinforcement learning through a careful application of the envelope theorem and the representation theorem for coherent risk measures. The technical development is sound, with clear theorems and proofs (referenced to supplementary material), and the authors demonstrate the utility of their approach through concrete examples.

The significance of this work is substantial. Risk-sensitive decision-making is crucial in many real-world applications (finance, operations research, etc.), and the paper provides a principled framework for incorporating a wide range of risk preferences into reinforcement learning. The authors' numerical example (Section 6) effectively illustrates how different risk measures can lead to different policy behaviors, highlighting the importance of flexible risk criteria. This is particularly valuable for applications where decision-makers need to balance expected performance with risk management.

Strengths:
- Strong theoretical contribution that unifies previous work on risk-sensitive RL
- Novel gradient formulas that generalize across the entire class of coherent risk measures
- Clear exposition of the mathematical framework and its connection to previous work
- Demonstrated utility through concrete examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation)
- Potential for broad impact across risk-sensitive RL applications

Weaknesses:
- The empirical evaluation is limited to a single illustrative example (trading agent)
- The computational complexity of the two-step sampling/convex programming approach isn't thoroughly analyzed
- The assumption that the risk envelope is known in canonical form (Assumption 2.2) might limit practical applicability for some risk measures
- The connection to practical implementation challenges (e.g., scalability to large state spaces) is somewhat superficial

However, as a reviewer who prioritizes novelty and bold conceptual contributions, I find the paper's theoretical advance to be sufficiently significant to outweigh these methodological limitations. The work represents a meaningful step toward a more general understanding of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning, moving beyond the narrow focus on specific risk measures that has characterized much of the prior literature.

The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and makes a compelling case for the value of the coherent risk framework in reinforcement learning. The connection to model uncertainty (Section 7) provides an intriguing direction for future work that could further enhance the paper's significance.

While the empirical evaluation could be strengthened with more comprehensive experiments, the theoretical contribution is substantial enough to stand on its own. The paper does not merely refine existing methods but introduces a new conceptual framework that will likely inspire future work in risk-sensitive RL.

I believe this paper makes a significant contribution to the field, advancing the state of the art by providing a unified theoretical foundation for risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The authors successfully demonstrate how their approach generalizes and simplifies previous work, while opening new avenues for research in risk-aware decision-making.

Given the paper's strong theoretical novelty, clear exposition, and significant potential impact, I recommend acceptance. The work represents a thoughtful and bold contribution that aligns well with NIPS's interest in novel theoretical advances that open new directions in machine learning.
