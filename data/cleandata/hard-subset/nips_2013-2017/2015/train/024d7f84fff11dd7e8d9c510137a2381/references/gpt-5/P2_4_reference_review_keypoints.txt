- Paper develops policy-gradient methods for coherent risk measures with (i) a general static gradient formula, (ii) an SAA estimator with consistency, and (iii) a dynamic actor–critic via Markov coherent risk.
- Uses dual (risk-envelope) representation and the envelope theorem to derive sampling-based static gradient estimators.
- Proposes an SAA sampling-plus-convex-programming estimator for cases without closed-form saddle points, with a consistency result (Prop. 4.4).
- Presents a dynamic policy-gradient theorem for time-consistent Markov coherent risk, yielding a robust-MDP-based actor–critic approach.
- Recovers known special cases (e.g., CVaR) and provides a mean-semideviation gradient formula.
- Novelty: unifies prior risk-specific RL results into a general coherent-risk policy-gradient framework using established convex-analytic tools.
- Strength: generality and unification; envelope-theorem-based formula subsumes earlier derivations.
- Strength: practical SAA estimator with mild assumptions and consistency guarantees.
- Strength: dynamic theorem conceptually parallels classic policy gradient and links to robust MDP critics.
- Strength: solid grounding in convex analysis and likelihood-ratio methods, contextualizing prior work.
- Weakness: experimental validation is minimal — single static portfolio example, no dynamic benchmarks or scalability tests.
- Weakness: practicality concerns for the dynamic actor — requires ξ*-reweighted sampling and multiple next-state samples, implying a simulator/generative model.
- Weakness: missing variance and sample-complexity analysis for potentially high-variance likelihood-ratio estimators, especially for tail risks.
- Weakness: reliance on explicit risk envelopes and derivatives dfi/dp, which may be nontrivial for some spectral measures; limited worked examples beyond CVaR.
- Weakness: some strong/under-discussed assumptions (bounded likelihood ratios, finite probability spaces, access to KKT multipliers) and unquantified computational overhead of per-iteration convex solves.
- Quality: technically sound derivations and standard SAA assumptions, but key algorithmic details and proofs deferred to the supplement.
- Clarity: generally clear but heavy notation; missing main-text pseudo-code and concrete derivative examples hinder reproducibility.
- Originality: high — a novel synthesis providing general coherent-risk PG methods with implementable estimators.
- Significance: potentially high for risk-aware RL, but immediate impact limited by lack of dynamic experiments and practical guidance.
- Suggestions: add at least one dynamic MDP experiment, clarify ξ*-weighted sampling in practice, provide derivative examples for spectral risks, discuss variance-reduction techniques, and include main-paper pseudo-code.
- Pros: elegant, general gradient formulas; consistent SAA estimator; principled dynamic PG theorem enabling actor–critic designs.
- Cons: very limited experiments; practical constraints (simulator requirement, convex solves, variance) underexplored; some strong assumptions left unquantified.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with potential impact but insufficient empirical validation — reviewer leans weak accept for theory focus, else weak reject.
