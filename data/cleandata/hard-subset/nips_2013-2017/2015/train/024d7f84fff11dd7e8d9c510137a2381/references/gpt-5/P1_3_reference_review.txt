Summary
The paper develops policy-gradient methods for optimizing coherent risk measures in reinforcement learning. It treats (i) static coherent risk of a random cost Z, and (ii) time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk in MDPs. The key technical lever is the dual (risk-envelope) representation of coherent risk (Artzner et al.; Ruszczyński & Shapiro): ρ(Z)=maxξ∈U Eξ[Z]. For static risk, the authors derive a general gradient formula via the envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio trick (Theorem 4.2), which expresses ∇θρ(Z) in terms of expectations under an adversarial density ξ* and Lagrange multipliers of the risk-envelope constraints. This yields closed-form gradients for CVaR and mean-semideviation and, when no analytic saddle-point is available, a two-step estimator that combines sampling with solving a convex program (SAA) and is shown to be consistent (Proposition 4.4). For dynamic risk, they exploit the time-consistent nested form of Markov coherent risk and its Bellman equation to derive a policy gradient theorem (Theorem 5.2), expressing ∇θVθ(x) as an expectation of REINFORCE terms with a modified stage cost hθ and trajectories under ξ*-reweighted transitions. They sketch an actor-critic algorithm that uses function approximation for the critic (via the equivalence to robust MDPs) and a two-phase sampling scheme for the actor. A toy trading example illustrates how different coherent risks can induce different policies.

Relation to prior work
The paper unifies and generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL work focused on specific risks (mean-variance, entropic risk, CVaR), recovering CVaR gradient results (e.g., Tamar et al.; Chow et al.) and mean-variance style methods, and connecting to dynamic Markov coherent risk and robust MDPs (Ruszczyński; Osogami; Petrik & Subramanian). The robust MDP connection is known (e.g., Iyengar 2005; Nilim & El Ghaoui 2005) and is used for the critic.

Strengths
- Generality: A principled, unified gradient framework for the entire class of coherent risks, both static and time-consistent dynamic. The dual risk-envelope perspective and envelope theorem are used elegantly.
- Theory: Clear derivations for static gradients (Theorem 4.2) and consistency of the SAA-based estimator under mild assumptions. The dynamic policy-gradient theorem (Theorem 5.2) is new and extends the classic policy-gradient theorem to Markov coherent risks.
- Connections: Makes explicit the link between coherent risk, worst-case reweighting, and robust MDPs; subsumes previous special cases (e.g., CVaR).
- Practicality for static risk: When analytic ξ* is unavailable, the SAA estimator is implementable with standard convex optimization and provides a general recipe.
- Motivation: The flexibility of coherent risk is well-motivated, and the toy example illustrates how risk choice matters.

Weaknesses and questions
- Empirical evaluation is very limited. There are no experiments demonstrating the dynamic actor-critic on even small/medium MDPs, nor comparisons against baselines (risk-neutral, CVaR-specific methods, robust MDP planning, mean-variance). The toy asset example does not validate the dynamic gradient theorem or the two-phase sampling scheme.
- Practical sampling under ξ*-weighted dynamics: Theorem 5.2 requires generating trajectories from the ξ*-reweighted kernel. In typical model-free RL, one cannot change the environment dynamics. Can this be implemented via importance sampling with acceptable variance? The paper mentions a simulator with multiple next-state samples; please quantify the requirements and variance implications, especially for tail-sensitive risks.
- Computational overhead: For static risks (and for each state in dynamic risk), the method solves a convex program to obtain ξ* and multipliers. How does this scale with large/continuous state/action spaces? What are the per-update complexities in the dynamic actor-critic, and how often must ξ* be recomputed as θ changes?
- Assumptions and scope: The finite probability space assumption is strong; extensions to Lp spaces are claimed but omitted. For dynamic risk, are measurability/continuity conditions ensuring the existence and stability of ξ* across states and θ provided? Many risk envelopes depend on P; the method needs sensitivities dfi/dp and dge/dp—are these available in common coherent risks beyond CVaR and spectral/semideviation?
- Convergence guarantees: The actor-critic convergence and the bias from using an approximate critic V̂ are deferred to the supplement. A summary of assumptions (e.g., compatibility conditions, function approximation class, step-size schedules) and the main theorem statement should be in the main text.
- Variance and sample complexity: Likelihood-ratio estimators for rare-event risks (e.g., small-α CVaR) can have very high variance. The paper mentions importance sampling as future work; some guidance or control-variates/baselines for variance reduction would strengthen the practical side.
- Related work: The robust MDP equivalence could cite Iyengar (2005) and Nilim & El Ghaoui (2005). There is also a literature on entropic/exponential utility risk-sensitive PG and twisted measures; a brief connection would help position the dynamic gradient result.

Quality
Technically sound and well-grounded in convex analysis and stochastic approximation. The static results are careful; the dynamic theorem is compelling but its practical estimation and convergence properties are not demonstrated empirically. More details of the actor-critic algorithm and its guarantees in the main text would improve completeness.

Clarity
Generally clear and well organized. The notation is dense; including algorithmic pseudocode for the SAA estimator and the actor-critic would help reproducibility. Clarify the meaning of Eξ* over “ξ*-weighted transitions” (change of measure vs. modified simulator), and explicitly state requirements on access to a generative model and on computing dfi/dp. Fix minor footnote/formatting issues.

Originality
High. The general coherent-risk PG formula and the dynamic policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk are, to my knowledge, new and unify several strands of prior work.

Significance
Potentially high. A general, sampling-friendly recipe for coherent risk optimization in RL is valuable theoretically and could influence practical risk-aware RL. However, the paper’s impact would be strengthened by empirical validation on dynamic tasks and a discussion of computational/variance tradeoffs.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add at least one dynamic MDP experiment (e.g., inventory control, gridworld with risk of rare catastrophic costs), compare against risk-neutral PG, CVaR-specific methods, and robust MDP baselines.
- Provide pseudocode and complexity analysis for the SAA gradient and dynamic actor-critic; discuss how often the convex program is solved and how to warm-start multipliers.
- Elaborate on implementing ξ*-weighted sampling via importance sampling when environment dynamics cannot be modified; include a baseline/control variate.
- Summarize the actor-critic convergence theorem and assumptions in the main text.
- Expand related work to include Iyengar (2005), Nilim & El Ghaoui (2005), and clarify relation to entropic risk PG.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel, general theoretical contribution unifying risk-sensitive PG across coherent risks.
- Clean derivations and consistency guarantees.
- Bridges coherent risk and robust MDPs in a policy-gradient framework.

Arguments against acceptance
- Lack of substantive empirical validation, especially for the dynamic method.
- Practical feasibility questions (sampling under ξ*, per-iteration convex programs) not fully addressed.
- Some assumptions and necessary sensitivities for envelopes may limit applicability.

Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on strengthening the experimental section and clarifying practical aspects; otherwise borderline. Confidence: medium.
