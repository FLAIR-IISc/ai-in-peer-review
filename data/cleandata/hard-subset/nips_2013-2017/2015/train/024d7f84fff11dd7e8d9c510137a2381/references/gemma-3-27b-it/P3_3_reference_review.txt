## NIPS Review: Policy Gradient for Coherent Risk Measures

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a significant and well-executed contribution to the growing field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. It provides a unifying framework for policy gradient methods applied to coherent risk measures, generalizing existing work and offering novel theoretical and algorithmic results. The paper is technically sound, clearly written, and addresses an important problem. While the numerical illustration is limited, the theoretical contributions and potential impact are substantial. I recommend **acceptance**, with minor revisions as detailed below.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper tackles the problem of optimizing policies under coherent risk measures in Markov Decision Processes (MDPs). Unlike traditional RL which focuses on expected reward, this work addresses scenarios where controlling the *variability* of outcomes is crucial. The authors leverage the theory of coherent risk measures, establishing a general framework for deriving policy gradient algorithms applicable to any coherent risk measure. They provide novel gradient formulas for both static and dynamic (time-consistent) coherent risk, alongside an actor-critic algorithm for the dynamic case. 

The related work section is comprehensive and accurately positions the paper within the existing literature. The authors correctly highlight previous work on specific risk measures (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance) and contrast their general approach with these. They also acknowledge connections to robust MDPs and stochastic programming, clearly delineating the differences and benefits of their framework.  The authors do a good job recognizing and addressing the limitations of prior work and explaining how their approach offers a more general and scalable solution.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Generality:** The core strength of this work is its unifying framework. By operating within the theory of coherent risk measures, the authors provide a general methodology applicable to a wide class of risk preferences, rather than being limited to specific instances.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The derivation of the policy gradient formulas (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2) is mathematically sound and well-motivated. The use of the Envelope Theorem and the Lagrangian duality approach are elegant and effective. 
* **Sampling-Based Approach:** The emphasis on sampling-based estimation is crucial for scalability. Many risk measures are intractable to compute directly, making a sampling-based approach essential for applying these methods to large or continuous MDPs.
* **Dynamic Risk Treatment:** The development of a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk is a significant contribution. The proposed actor-critic algorithm provides a practical approach for implementing this in dynamic settings.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains complex concepts in a concise manner. The notation is consistent, and the arguments are easy to follow.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Numerical Illustration:**  The numerical example is somewhat limited in scope. While it demonstrates the differences in policy choices based on different risk preferences (mean-semideviation vs. variance), it lacks a more thorough evaluation of the performance and scalability of the proposed algorithms. Comparing against baseline risk-neutral or risk-averse algorithms on more complex environments would strengthen the paper.
* **Complexity of Implementation:** While theoretically sound, implementing the actor-critic algorithm for dynamic risk seems potentially complex, requiring careful tuning of the sampling procedures and function approximation schemes.  A more detailed discussion of the practical considerations and potential challenges of implementation would be valuable.
* **Assumptions:**  The paper relies on several assumptions (e.g., Assumption 2.2 on the risk envelope structure, Assumption 4.1 & 5.1 on the likelihood ratio). While these are reasonable, their limitations and potential impact on the applicability of the results should be more explicitly discussed.
* **Convergence Analysis:** The convergence analysis of the actor-critic algorithm is mentioned but summarized briefly. A more detailed presentation of the convergence guarantees would be highly desirable.



**4. Originality & Significance:**

The paper is highly original. While risk-sensitive RL is an active area, the authors’ generalization to the entire class of coherent risk measures, coupled with the development of accompanying gradient formulas and algorithms, represents a significant advance.  This work has the potential to be highly influential, providing a foundational framework for future research in risk-sensitive decision-making. The connections to robust MDPs and the potential for handling model uncertainty (as briefly mentioned in the conclusion) are particularly intriguing and suggest a broader impact beyond standard RL.

**5. Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*  Significant theoretical contributions to risk-sensitive RL.
*  Generalization to a broad class of coherent risk measures.
*  Practical sampling-based approach.
*  Clear and well-written paper.
*  Addresses a crucial problem with broad applicability.

**Con:**

* Limited numerical evaluation.
* Implementation complexity not fully addressed.
* Convergence analysis could be more detailed.



**6. Recommendations for Improvement:**

* **Expand Numerical Experiments:**  Include more comprehensive experiments on benchmark RL environments (e.g., OpenAI Gym) to demonstrate the performance of the proposed algorithms.  Compare against existing risk-averse RL baselines.
* **Implementation Details:** Provide more discussion on the practical challenges and considerations involved in implementing the actor-critic algorithm, including hyperparameter tuning and function approximation choices.
* **Address Assumption Limitations:** Discuss the limitations of the assumptions made in the paper and how they might affect the applicability of the results.
* **Elaborate on Convergence:** Include a more detailed explanation of the convergence analysis of the actor-critic algorithm.
* **Future Work:** Expand on the discussion of potential connections to robust MDPs and model uncertainty.

**Final Verdict:**  **Accept**.  This paper makes a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The theoretical results are strong, the framework is general, and the sampling-based approach offers a practical pathway for implementation. Addressing the suggested improvements would further enhance the paper's impact.
