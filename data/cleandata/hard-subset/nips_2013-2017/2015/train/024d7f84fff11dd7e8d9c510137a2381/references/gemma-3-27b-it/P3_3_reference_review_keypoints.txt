- Overall assessment: Significant, well-executed contribution to risk-sensitive RL; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem addressed: Optimizes policies under coherent risk measures in MDPs, focusing on variability control rather than just expected reward.  
- Technical contribution: Provides a unifying framework and novel gradient formulas for static and time-consistent dynamic coherent risk.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Proposes an actor-critic algorithm for Markov coherent risk measures with sampling-based estimation.  
- Related work: Comprehensive review that correctly situates the paper relative to CVaR, mean-variance, robust MDPs, and stochastic programming.  
- Strength — generality: Framework applies to a wide class of coherent risk measures rather than specific instances.  
- Strength — theoretical rigor: Derivations (Theorems 4.2, 5.2) are sound, leveraging Envelope Theorem and Lagrangian duality.  
- Strength — sampling emphasis: Sampling-based estimation makes the approach scalable to large or continuous MDPs.  
- Strength — dynamic treatment: Policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and corresponding practical algorithm are significant advances.  
- Strength — clarity: Paper is very well written with clear notation and explanations.  
- Weakness — limited experiments: Numerical illustration is narrow and lacks comparisons on complex benchmarks and baselines.  
- Weakness — implementation complexity: Actor-critic implementation may be complex and require careful tuning and approximations.  
- Weakness — assumptions: Several assumptions (risk envelope, likelihood ratios) need clearer discussion about limits and impact.  
- Weakness — convergence analysis: Convergence results for the actor-critic algorithm are summarized too briefly and need expansion.  
- Originality & significance: Highly original generalization with potential for strong influence and connections to robust MDPs and model uncertainty.  
- Pro acceptance points: Significant theory, broad applicability, practical sampling approach, and clear exposition.  
- Con acceptance points: Insufficient empirical evaluation, implementation and assumption issues, and limited convergence detail.  
- Recommendations: Expand numerical experiments and baselines, add implementation guidance and hyperparameter discussion, address assumption limitations, and elaborate convergence analysis.  
- Final verdict: Accept, pending minor revisions to strengthen empirical evaluation and practical/convergence details.
