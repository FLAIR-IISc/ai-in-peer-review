- Paper develops policy-gradient methods for risk-sensitive RL under coherent risk measures using dual risk-envelope representation and envelope theorem to derive sample-friendly gradients.
- Static case: Theorem 4.2 gives a general coherent-risk gradient, recovers CVaR and yields mean-semideviation formula; proposes SAA with convex program + KKT multipliers for consistent gradient estimation.
- Dynamic case: Theorem 5.2 gives a policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent (time-consistent) risk using ξ-weighted transitions and a stage-wise cost hθ; proposes an actor-critic using robust-MDP critic and trajectory-based actor.
- Unifies prior strands: exponential utility, mean-variance, CVaR PG methods; builds on robust MDP and CVaR literature; dynamic PG for general Markov coherent risk appears novel.
- Theory: Derivations are technically sound under stated assumptions; SAA + KKT consistency argument is reasonable; dynamic result leverages robust Bellman equation properly.
- Assumptions: Relies on finite probability space, bounded likelihood ratios, smooth dependence of envelopes on base distribution, and existence/uniqueness of saddle points—standard but nontrivial.
- Practical requirements: Dynamic result needs access to ξ-weighted transitions and derivatives of constraint functions dfi/dp, dge/dp, which may be strong in practice.
- Empirics: Experimental evaluation is minimal (single toy asset-selection example) with no benchmarks or comparisons to specialized methods.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written and organized but notation is heavy and many algorithmic details and proofs are deferred to the supplement.
- Missing details: Needs explicit computation guidance for ∇θ ge and ∇θ fi for common envelopes and discussion of computational overhead.
- Originality: Main contributions (general static coherent-risk gradient and dynamic PG theorem) are original and nontrivial, subsuming known results.
- Significance: Conceptually important and likely influential for risk-sensitive RL, but practical impact is uncertain without stronger empirical evidence.
- Strengths: General theoretical framework, static gradient for any coherent risk with SAA estimator, novel dynamic PG result, and good use of robust MDP connections.
- Weaknesses: Sparse experiments, strong practical requirements (convex solves, per-state saddle problems, ξ-weighted sampling), potentially restrictive assumptions, and limited analysis of variance/sample complexity.
- Suggestions: Explore off-policy estimation without ξ-weighted simulators (importance sampling/density-ratio), provide experiments on standard RL tasks vs CVaR/mean-variance baselines, and extend discussion to continuous spaces and spectral risk derivatives.
- Recommendation: Borderline reject—strong theory but insufficient empirical validation and practical guidance.
- Confidence: High on theory and related-work assessment; medium on practicality due to missing experiments.
