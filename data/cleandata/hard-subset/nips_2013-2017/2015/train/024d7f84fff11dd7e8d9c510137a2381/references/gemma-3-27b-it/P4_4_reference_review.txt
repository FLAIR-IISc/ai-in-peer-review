This paper presents policy gradient algorithms for reinforcement learning with coherent risk objectives, generalizing previous approaches by applying to the entire class of coherent risk measures. The authors formulate risk-sensitive policy gradients for both static and time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk, providing a new gradient formula for static risk suitable for sampling, a sampling-based algorithm for the static case with a consistency result, and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk coupled with an actor-critic algorithm.

The paper is well-motivated, positioning itself within the growing body of work on risk-sensitive RL and highlighting the limitations of existing methods focused on specific risk measures. The introduction clearly articulates the benefits of coherent risk measures and time consistency. The authors appropriately acknowledge prior work, referencing key papers in the field and clarifying how their approach extends existing results. The mathematical notation is generally clear, and the paper is logically structured, progressing from preliminary definitions to gradient formulations and algorithms.

**Strengths:**

*   **Generality:** The focus on the entire class of coherent risk measures is a significant strength, offering a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of the gradient formulas, based on the envelope theorem and leveraging the properties of coherent risk measures, appears technically sound.  The consistency result for the static case provides a guarantee on the accuracy of the sampling-based algorithm.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The development of sampling-based algorithms is crucial for addressing the scalability challenges of applying coherent risk measures to large MDPs. The actor-critic algorithm for the dynamic case provides a potential solution for complex sequential decision-making problems.
*   **Connection to Robust MDPs:** The acknowledgement of the relationship between dynamic coherent risk and robust MDPs adds further depth and context to the work.
*   **Illustrative Example:** The numerical illustration showcases the potential of the approach, demonstrating how different risk criteria can lead to different investment strategies.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Computational Complexity:** While the paper emphasizes sampling-based algorithms, the computational cost of solving the convex optimization problem within the sampling loop (for estimating the Lagrangian multipliers) is not fully discussed. The complexity of this step could be a bottleneck in practice.
*   **Assumptions:** The assumptions regarding the smoothness of the constraints in Assumption 2.2, while standard, may not hold for all coherent risk measures. A discussion of the practical implications of violating these assumptions would be beneficial.
*   **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The numerical example is limited in scope, focusing on a simple trading scenario. A more comprehensive empirical evaluation on benchmark RL tasks would strengthen the paper’s claims.
*   **Actor-Critic Details:** The description of the actor-critic algorithm is somewhat high-level. More details on the function approximation methods, learning rates, and convergence analysis would be valuable.
*   **Lack of comparison with state-of-the-art:** The authors should more clearly compare the performance of their proposed algorithm with state-of-the-art algorithms for risk-sensitive reinforcement learning.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to risk-sensitive RL, generalizing existing methods and providing a unified framework for coherent risk measures.
*   The development of sampling-based algorithms addresses a key limitation of previous approaches, enabling the application of coherent risk measures to large-scale problems.
*   The actor-critic algorithm for dynamic risk provides a promising approach to sequential decision-making under uncertainty.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the contributions and limitations of the work.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The computational complexity of the sampling-based algorithms, particularly the convex optimization step, is a potential concern.
*   The limited empirical evaluation and the lack of comparison with state-of-the-art algorithms weaken the paper’s claims.
*   The level of detail in the actor-critic algorithm description is insufficient for replication.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The generality of the approach, the theoretical soundness of the derivations, and the development of practical algorithms are all strong points. While the computational complexity and limited empirical evaluation are concerns, the potential benefits of this work outweigh these drawbacks.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly a more detailed discussion of the computational costs and a more comprehensive empirical evaluation.
