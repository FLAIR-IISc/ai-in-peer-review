- Paper develops policy gradient methods for the full class of coherent risk measures, generalizing prior work focused on specific metrics like CVaR or mean-variance.  
- Uses the representation theorem for coherent risk measures to derive gradient formulas for static and dynamic Markov coherent risk, extending the policy gradient theorem to risk-sensitive settings.  

Strengths:
- Theoretical novelty: provides a unified framework and key theorems (4.2, 5.2) that extend policy gradient to coherent risk.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: leverages coherent risk theory with reasonable assumptions (e.g., Assumption 2.2).  
- Practical algorithm design: proposes a sampling plus convex-programming gradient estimator with a consistency result (Proposition 4.4).  
- Clear exposition: well-organized paper with illustrative examples showing recovery of prior methods.  

Weaknesses:
- Computational complexity: gradient estimator requires solving a convex program per sample, potentially prohibitive for large-scale problems; runtime scaling with risk envelope complexity is not analyzed.  
- Limited empirical validation: experiments are simplistic and lack challenging RL benchmarks and quantitative comparisons of risk measures.  
- Assumption limitations: requires explicit convex-form risk envelope (Assumption 2.2), which may not hold or be available for all risk measures.  
- Dynamic risk implementation: actor-critic algorithm needs multiple transitions per state, which is infeasible in many real-world environments and not addressed.  
- Incomplete comparisons: lacks thorough empirical and computational comparison with existing risk-sensitive RL methods ([20,21,29,30]).  

Overall assessment:
- Strong and novel theoretical contribution that unifies risk-sensitive RL under coherent risk measures, but practical utility is limited by computational and empirical shortcomings.  

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing computational analysis and empirical validation.  

Suggested improvements:
- Add computational complexity analysis of the algorithm.  
- Provide more substantial empirical results on standard RL benchmarks.  
- Discuss trade-offs between risk-measure flexibility and computational cost.  
- Include detailed comparisons with existing risk-sensitive RL methods.
