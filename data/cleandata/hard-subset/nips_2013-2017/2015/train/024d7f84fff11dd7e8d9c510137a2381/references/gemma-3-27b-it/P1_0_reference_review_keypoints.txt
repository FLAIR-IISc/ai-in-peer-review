- Overall: Valuable, general contribution to risk-sensitive RL; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem & scope: Optimizes policies in MDPs under the full class of coherent risk measures, unifying static and dynamic cases.  
- Main contributions: Novel gradient formulas for static coherent risk, a policy-gradient theorem for dynamic Markov coherent risk, and an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends prior CVaR/mean-variance and robust MDP approaches by providing a unified, sampling-based framework.  
- Generality strength: Framework applies to any coherent risk measure, offering broad applicability.  
- Theoretical rigor: Derivations and proofs are mathematically sound and grounded in convex optimization and stochastic gradient literature.  
- Sampling-based approach: SAA-based gradient estimation and sampling make the methods scalable to large MDPs.  
- Practical algorithm: Presents an actor-critic method for dynamic risk, providing a concrete implementation path.  
- Illustrative example: Numerical example (financial application) highlights importance of risk-measure choice and demonstrates framework potential.  
- Accessibility weakness: Paper is dense with heavy notation and assumes strong background; needs more intuition and simplified notation.  
- Assumption concerns: Key assumptions (risk envelope form, bounded likelihood ratios) should be discussed further for their limitations.  
- Computational complexity: Convex programming step in static risk gradient estimation may be expensive in very high dimensions; needs cost discussion/approximations.  
- Empirical evaluation limited: Single/simple numerical illustration; requires more experiments on standard RL benchmarks with various risk measures.  
- Actor-critic detail lacking: Main paper gives high-level algorithm; more detailed algorithmic description should be included in the main text.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound and complete work (strong).  
- Clarity assessment: Reasonably clear but dense; would benefit from more intuition (good).  
- Originality assessment: Original generalization to coherent risk measures with novel gradient results (strong).  
- Significance assessment: Likely important for risk-sensitive RL applications in finance, robotics, healthcare (strong).  
- Arguments for acceptance: General, theoretically sound framework; novel contributions; practical importance; scalable sampling approach.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Dense presentation; limited empirical validation; potential computational cost concerns.  
- Recommendation actions: Improve clarity and notation, discuss assumptions, expand empirical evaluation, and elaborate actor-critic algorithm in the main paper.
