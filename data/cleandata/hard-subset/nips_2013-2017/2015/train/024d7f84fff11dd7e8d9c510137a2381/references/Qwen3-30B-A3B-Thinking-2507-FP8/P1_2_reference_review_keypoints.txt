- Summary: Develops policy-gradient methods for the full class of coherent risk measures, with sampling-friendly gradient formula (Theorem 4.2) and a dynamic extension (Theorem 5.2) that generalizes prior CVaR/mean-variance results.

- Strength: Strong theoretical contribution providing a unified framework and novel gradient theorems for coherent risk measures.

- Strength: Clearly generalizes prior work (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) and recovers known results under weaker assumptions.

- Strength: Well-structured technical development from representation theorem to gradient formulas and actor-critic algorithm.

- Strength: Numerical example illustrates how different risk measures yield different policies in a trading scenario.

- Strength: Provides consistency results for the proposed gradient estimators (Proposition 4.4).

- Weakness: Experimental validation is limited to a brief numerical example and lacks extensive empirical comparisons.

- Weakness: Potential computational expense of the two-step sampling-plus-convex-programming approach is not analyzed.

- Weakness: No empirical comparison with recent state-of-the-art risk-sensitive RL methods.

- Weakness: Practical implementation issues (need for simulator, sampling multiple transitions per state) are noted but not addressed.

- Weakness: Connection to model uncertainty is mentioned but underdeveloped and lacks examples.

- Quality: Technically sound with solid theoretical guarantees but would benefit from stronger empirical support.

- Clarity: Well-written, logically organized, consistent notation, and helpful supplementary material for reproduction.

- Originality: Novel unified framework and new gradient formulas (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2) that extend prior risk-sensitive RL work.

- Significance: Addresses an important RL problem and enables flexible risk-criteria design, though practical impact is limited by sparse experiments.

- Recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical advancement justifies acceptance despite limited empirical evaluation.

- For acceptance: Unified framework, novel theorems, generalization of prior work, theoretical guarantees, and meaningful numerical illustration.

- Against acceptance: Limited experiments, lack of SOTA comparisons, unresolved computational/scalability concerns, and weak treatment of model uncertainty.

- Suggestion: Add extensive experiments comparing to existing methods for specific risk measures (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance).

- Suggestion: Analyze and report computational complexity and scalability of the sampling + convex programming approach.

- Suggestion: Expand numerical examples to include concrete performance metrics and baselines.

- Suggestion: Discuss adaptations for single-trajectory rollouts or limited-data settings to address practical sampling constraints.

- Suggestion: Develop the model-uncertainty connection into a more substantial discussion or future-work section.
