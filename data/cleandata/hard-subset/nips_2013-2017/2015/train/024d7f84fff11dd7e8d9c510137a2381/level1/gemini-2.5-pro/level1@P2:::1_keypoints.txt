- Presents a unified policy-gradient framework for the entire class of coherent risk measures in RL.  
- Leverages the dual representation (risk envelope) and the envelope theorem to derive novel gradient formulas.  
- Addresses both static risk (risk on total discounted cost) and dynamic, time-consistent Markov coherent risk.  
- Static-case gradient requires solving a convex optimization over the risk envelope; sampling + convex programming algorithm proposed.  
- Dynamic-case yields a new policy-gradient theorem analogous to the standard theorem and leads to an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Re-derives known results (e.g., CVaR) simply and derives new gradients for measures like mean-semideviation.  
- Theoretical contributions (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2) are elegant, technically sound, and substantial.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains coherent risk measures and static vs. dynamic distinctions effectively.  
- Highly original: first unified framework for coherent risks in policy gradients and novel sampling + convex-programming technique.  
- Significant impact potential: enables broad "risk shaping" in RL and connects coherent risk, robust MDPs, and policy gradients.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a simple bandit, failing to stress-test sequential decision-making and scalability.  
- Practicality concerns: convex program must be solved at each gradient estimate, with no analysis of computational overhead.  
- Key algorithmic details and convergence analysis for the dynamic actor-critic are deferred to supplement, hindering assessment.  
- Potential applicability limits: actor requires multiple next-state samples per state, which may be impractical in many settings.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: despite empirical gaps, the paper's theoretical and conceptual advances justify strong acceptance.
