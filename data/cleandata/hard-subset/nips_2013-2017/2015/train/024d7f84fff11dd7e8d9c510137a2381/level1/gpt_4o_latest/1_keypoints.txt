- Paper extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, covering static and time-consistent dynamic cases, and proposes gradient estimation and actor-critic algorithms.  
- Theoretical contributions include rigorous gradient theorems using the envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio trick, with consistency guarantees under reasonable assumptions.  
- Generalizes prior work by enabling risk-sensitive RL for any coherent risk measure rather than specific cases like CVaR or mean-variance.  
- Novel unified framework and policy gradient results, with impactful extension to dynamic, time-consistent risk measures.  
- Practical relevance demonstrated via a numerical example showing the flexibility of coherent risk measures for problem-specific risk preferences (e.g., finance).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with supplementary material for proofs and additional details.  
- Scalability concerns: reliance on convex programming and sampling may not scale to large or high-dimensional problems, with no concrete solutions provided.  
- Empirical validation is limited: the single illustrative example is simple and does not demonstrate robustness or scalability in complex RL environments.  
- Some theoretical assumptions (explicit risk envelope form, simulator availability) may restrict practical applicability.  
- Actor-critic algorithm for dynamic risk lacks sufficient detail in the main text, hindering assessment of implementation practicality.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial theoretical advance, unified generalization of prior work, and clear practical implications for risk management in RL.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical evaluation of scalability/robustness, limiting assumptions, and incomplete algorithmic details.  
- Overall conclusion: strong theoretical contribution deserving acceptance, with recommendation to address scalability and provide more extensive empirical results in future revisions.
