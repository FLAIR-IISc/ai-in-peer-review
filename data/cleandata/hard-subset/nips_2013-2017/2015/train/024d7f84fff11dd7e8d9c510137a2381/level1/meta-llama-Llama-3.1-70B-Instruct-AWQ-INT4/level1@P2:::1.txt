This paper presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that generalizes and extends previous results. The authors develop policy gradient algorithms for coherent risk measures, which is a widely accepted class of risk measures in finance and operations research. The paper provides a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk and an algorithm for estimating the gradient of general static coherent risk using sampling with convex programming. The authors also derive a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk, relating the gradient to a suitable value function and a corresponding actor-critic algorithm.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the technical details. The use of examples, such as the CVaR and mean-semideviation, helps to illustrate the applicability of the proposed approach. The numerical illustration in Section 6 demonstrates the importance of flexibility in designing risk criteria and shows how the proposed approach can be used to optimize risk-sensitive policies.

The paper's contributions are significant, as it provides a general framework for risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that can be applied to a wide range of risk measures. The authors' use of convex programming and sampling-based estimation algorithms makes the approach scalable and efficient.
