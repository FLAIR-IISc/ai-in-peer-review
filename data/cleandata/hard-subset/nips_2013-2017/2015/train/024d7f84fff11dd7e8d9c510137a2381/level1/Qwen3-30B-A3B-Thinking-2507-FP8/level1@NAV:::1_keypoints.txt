- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, beyond specific measures like CVaR or variance.  
- Presents a unified framework covering both static and time-consistent dynamic coherent risk measures.  
- Core technical results: Theorem 4.2 (gradient formula for static coherent risk enabling sampling) and Theorem 5.2 (policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk).  
- Demonstrates that prior approaches (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) are special cases of the proposed framework.  
- Theoretical development is technically sound with consistent proofs.  
- Exposition is clear and well-organized, with precise definitions and strong mathematical structure.  
- Provides a numerical trading example that effectively illustrates practical value in selecting risk measures.  
- Original and significant contribution, offering a flexible, theoretically grounded method for risk-sensitive RL and handling model uncertainty.  
- Appropriately cites relevant prior work (e.g., Chow & Ghavamzadeh; Tamar et al.).  
- Minor weakness: limited empirical evaluation (only a simple trading example).  
- Minor weakness: computational complexity of the convex-programming steps may challenge very large-scale problems.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution, well-suited for NIPS and valuable to researchers in risk-sensitive decision making.
