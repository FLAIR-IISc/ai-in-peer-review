- Paper develops policy-gradient methods for optimizing coherent risk measures in RL for both static trajectory risk and dynamic time-consistent Markov coherent risk.
- Static-risk approach uses dual representation of coherent risk and the envelope theorem plus likelihood ratios to derive a general gradient formula.
- Static contributions include closed-form gradients for special cases (CVaR, mean-semi-deviation) and a two-step SAA-based estimator with consistency guarantees for general coherent risks.
- Dynamic contribution is a novel policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk expressing ∇θVθ via log-policy gradients and state-dependent stage costs from dual saddle points.
- Authors connect the dynamic value function to robust MDPs and propose an actor-critic algorithm using ξ-weighted transitions and function approximation.
- Small experiment illustrates how different coherent risks change learned portfolio choices.
- Work unifies and generalizes prior risk-sensitive PG methods (exponential/entropic, mean-variance, CVaR) and extends classical policy gradient theory to coherent risks.
- Strength: General and unifying framework covering the whole class of coherent risk measures with new derivations.
- Strength: Technically solid use of convex duality and envelope theorem; SAA estimator is natural and theoretically supported.
- Strength: Practical implementability via sampling plus small convex programs and use of KKT multipliers.
- Strength: Clear connection to robust MDP literature and leveraging of scalable critic methods.
- Strength: Clarifies behavioral impact of different risk choices with illustrative examples.
- Weakness: Exposition restricted to finite probability spaces; claimed extensions to Lp spaces are omitted.
- Weakness: Results require smoothness and canonical-form envelopes that may not hold or be obtainable for all coherent risks.
- Weakness: Computational burden of solving convex programs per gradient estimate and per-state in dynamic case raises scalability concerns.
- Weakness: Requires a generative model to sample multiple next states and ξ-weighted transitions; not directly applicable to single-trajectory online RL.
- Weakness: Limited empirical validation—no dynamic MDP experiments, baseline comparisons, or variance/sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: Some technical details, proofs, and pseudocode deferred to supplement; stability/uniqueness of saddle points and non-smooth subgradient handling need more discussion.
- Quality: Technical core is sound; static gradient and SAA consistency are convincing; dynamic theorem insightful but practical deployment depends on KKT access and function-approximation analysis.
- Clarity: Generally clear and well organized, but heavy notation and omitted algorithmic details reduce readability; finite-space assumptions should be prominent.
- Originality: High—first to extend PG to entire class of coherent risks and derive dynamic PG theorem for Markov coherent risk.
- Significance: Potentially significant for OR/finance and risk-sensitive RL, but impact limited by scalability and generative-model requirements.
- Suggested improvements: Add pseudocode and complexity analysis, include a dynamic MDP experiment with function approximation and baselines, and discuss saddle-point non-uniqueness and spectral risk implementation.
- Suggested improvements: Address extensions beyond finite spaces and propose variance-reduction techniques and practical guidance for envelope misspecification.
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong, general theoretical contribution, novel dynamic PG theorem, and sound use of convex duality and SAA.
- Arguments against acceptance: Weak empirical validation, reliance on generative model and many convex solves, and key details relegated to supplement.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—theoretical novelty merits publication but stronger experiments and clearer scalability paths are needed.
