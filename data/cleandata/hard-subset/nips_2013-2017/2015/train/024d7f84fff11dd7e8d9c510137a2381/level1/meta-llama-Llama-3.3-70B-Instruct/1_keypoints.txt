- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Presents a unified framework that generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL work (e.g., variance, CVaR).  
- Derives a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk measures.  
- Proves a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Proposes a sampling-based algorithm to estimate gradients of dynamic risk measures, avoiding dynamic programming.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and demonstrates clear understanding of prior work and motivations.  
- Provides clear assumptions, proofs, and concise explanations of technical results.  
- Numerical illustration highlights the importance and flexibility of choosing appropriate risk criteria.  
- Assumes the risk envelope is given in a canonical convex programming form, which may not always hold in practice.  
- The gradient-estimation algorithm requires a simulator capable of sampling multiple transitions per state, limiting feasibility in some settings.  
- Lacks comprehensive empirical comparison to existing methods for thorough performance evaluation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial, well-motivated technical results, clarity, and useful numerical demonstration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumption on risk envelope, simulator requirements, and insufficient empirical comparisons.
