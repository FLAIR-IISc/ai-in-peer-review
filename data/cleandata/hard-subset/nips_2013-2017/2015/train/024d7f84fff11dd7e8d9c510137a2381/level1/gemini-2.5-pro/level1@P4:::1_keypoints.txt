- Introduces a unified policy-gradient framework for reinforcement learning with coherent risk measures instead of expected returns.  
- Leverages the dual representation of coherent risk (risk envelope) and applies the Envelope Theorem to derive gradients.  
- Static-risk result (Theorem 4.2): new policy-gradient formula involving optimal dual solution and Lagrange multipliers.  
- Proposes a sampling + convex-programming SAA estimator for the static gradient and proves consistency (Proposition 4.4).  
- Recovers known CVaR gradient formulas under weaker assumptions and provides a new mean-semideviation gradient.  
- Extends to time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk and derives a dynamic policy-gradient theorem (Theorem 5.2).  
- Proposes an actor-critic algorithm for the dynamic case where the critic estimates a risk-sensitive value function.  
- Theoretical development is strong and technically sound, with elegant use of the Envelope Theorem.  
- Main weakness: dynamic-case algorithm and sampling/value-approximation details are under-specified in the main text (left to supplement).  
- Clarity is high—well-written, well-organized, good motivation, and effective examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation).  
- Section 5 is concise to the point of ambiguity; more detail on the actor sampling scheme would help reproducibility.  
- Originality is high: unifies and generalizes prior case-by-case risk-sensitive RL results into a single framework.  
- Significance: provides a principled, extensible toolkit for risk-sensitive RL and clarifies relationships among prior methods.  
- Practical concern: computational cost—requires solving a convex optimization per gradient estimate; scalability not evaluated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, general, technically sound, well-written, and potentially impactful.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dynamic algorithm underspecified in main paper and potential computational scalability issues.
