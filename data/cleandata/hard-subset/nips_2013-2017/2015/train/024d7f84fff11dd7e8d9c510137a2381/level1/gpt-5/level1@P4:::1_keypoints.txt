- Paper develops policy-gradient methods for coherent risk (static and time-consistent dynamic) using dual risk-envelope representation and likelihood-ratio differentiation.
- Static-case: derives a general gradient formula via a saddle point of the convex envelope program, recovers CVaR and gives new mean-semideviation expression.
- Proposes SAA procedure solving empirical convex programs and using KKT multipliers to form consistent gradient estimators when saddle point unavailable.
- Dynamic-case: proves a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk using Bellman characterization and proposes an actor-critic scheme under ξ*-reweighted dynamics.
- Actor-critic: critic approximates risk-sensitive value function with function approximation; actor estimates gradient from ξ*-weighted trajectory samples.
- Empirical evaluation: small portfolio toy demonstrates differing policies from different risk measures but lacks dynamic-risk experiments.
- Static technical soundness: derivations are grounded, assumptions reasonable for many coherent risks, SAA consistency plausible.
- Dynamic technical soundness: theorem aligns with Bellman view but proofs, convergence with function approximation, and ergodicity under ξ* are deferred to supplement.
- Practical sampling: implementing ξ*-weighted dynamics requires simulator access and multiple next-state samples; practical approach under-specified.
- Computational concerns: per-batch convex solves and per-state KKT computations add nontrivial overhead that is not analyzed.
- Clarity: paper is well organized with heavy but standard notation; many implementation details and proofs are in the supplement.
- Originality: unified gradient formulas for broad coherent risks and a dynamic PG theorem are novel and generalize prior, risk-specific work.
- Significance: framework could influence risk-sensitive RL and connects to robust MDPs, but practical adoption limited by assumptions and simulator requirements.
- Strengths: general, theoretically grounded results; unifies prior results (e.g., CVaR); consistent SAA estimator; extension to time-consistent dynamic risk.
- Weaknesses: limited empirical validation (no dynamic experiments), unclear ξ*-sampling practicality, computational cost concerns, restrictive assumptions (finite spaces), and only sketched convergence with function approximation.
- Suggestions: add dynamic benchmark experiments and comparisons, clarify ξ*-sampling implementation (importance/rejection sampling and variance), analyze computational complexity and scalable approximations, provide SAA sensitivity/ablation studies, and discuss continuous-state extensions and ergodicity conditions.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong, general theoretical contributions with clear derivations and relevance to risk/robust control communities.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experiments to establish practicality, unresolved implementation and computational feasibility, and key details deferred to supplement.
- Overall assessment: solid, novel theoretical contribution offering a unified framework for coherent-risk policy gradients, but limited by lack of empirical validation and practical guidance for dynamic settings.
