- Presents a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL covering the entire class of coherent risk measures.  
- Main contributions: a gradient formula for static coherent risk combining sampling and convex programming, and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk leading to an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Builds on Artzner et al. (1999) and recent risk-sensitive RL work, correctly situating the contribution relative to robust MDPs and optimization literature.  
- Technical results (Theorems 4.2, 5.2) are rigorous with proofs in the supplement and well-motivated assumptions.  
- Provides a consistency result (Proposition 4.4) guaranteeing the sampling-based estimator.  
- Demonstrates practical utility via a financial trading numerical example comparing different risk-sensitive policies.  
- Clearly written and well-structured with consistent notation, helpful examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation), and useful supplementary material for reproducibility.  
- Novelty: first to generalize policy-gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures using the worst-case expectation representation.  
- Significance: broad applicability to finance and operations, simplifies implementing risk-sensitive RL across measures, and connects to model uncertainty.  
- Strengths include unified framework, rigorous theory, practical illustration, and solid literature contextualization.  
- Weaknesses: convex-programming step may be computationally costly for large-scale problems.  
- Applicability limited by need for a simulator to sample multiple transitions and lack of computational-efficiency comparisons with existing methods.  
- Theoretical assumptions include finite probability spaces, which may not directly extend to continuous settings.  
- Overall assessment: significant, technically sound, clearly written contribution; recommendation: Accept.
