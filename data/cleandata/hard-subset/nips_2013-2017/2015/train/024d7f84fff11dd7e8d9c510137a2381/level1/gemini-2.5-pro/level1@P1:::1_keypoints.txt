- Presents a unified policy-gradient framework for the entire class of coherent risk measures, generalizing prior risk-sensitive RL work.  
- Treats two settings: static risk (risk on full-trajectory discounted cost) and dynamic risk (time-consistent Markov coherent risk).  
- Static case: uses the dual representation of coherent risk and the Envelope Theorem to derive a general gradient formula (Thm 4.2).  
- Proposes a static-risk algorithm combining empirical sampling with convex programming to solve the dual and estimate gradients, with a consistency proof (Prop 4.4).  
- Dynamic case: derives a risk-sensitive policy gradient theorem (Thm 5.2) and an actor-critic algorithm where the critic solves a risk-sensitive Bellman equation.  
- Gradient for dynamic risk involves expectations under a re-weighted MDP using the worst-case density found by the critic.  
- Demonstrates generality by recovering CVaR results and deriving formulas for other measures like mean-semideviation.  
- Technical strengths: original, elegant theoretical derivations leveraging duality and the Envelope Theorem, with strong formal results.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates contributions relative to prior work.  
- Framework is broadly applicable whenever the risk envelope can be expressed via convex constraints.  
- Weak empirical validation: only a simple multi-armed bandit experiment; no experiments for the dynamic actor-critic algorithm.  
- Practicality concern: dynamic algorithm requires a generative model (multiple i.i.d. next-state samples), limiting applicability in single-trajectory/model-free settings.  
- Completeness concern: key algorithmic details and convergence analysis for the actor-critic are deferred to the supplement, making the paper less self-contained.  
- Recommendation summary: theoretically strong and likely influential; recommends acceptance despite weak empirical validation, but notes practical/sample limitations.
