**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that generalizes and extends previous results. The authors focus on coherent risk measures, which are widely accepted in finance and operations research, and develop policy gradient algorithms for both static and dynamic risk measures. The paper provides a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk, an algorithm for the gradient of general static coherent risk, and a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk. The authors also demonstrate the utility of their approach through numerical examples and comparisons with previous results.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on risk-sensitive optimization in reinforcement learning, including studies on specific risk functions such as variance, conditional value-at-risk (CVaR), and mean-semideviation. The authors extend these results to the whole class of coherent risk measures, providing a more general and unified approach. The paper also relates to previous work on dynamic programming and optimal control, as well as to studies on robust Markov decision processes.
