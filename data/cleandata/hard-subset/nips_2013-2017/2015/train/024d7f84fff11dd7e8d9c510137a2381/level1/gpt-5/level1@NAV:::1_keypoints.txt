- Contribution: Develops policy-gradient methods for the full class of coherent risk measures, covering static and time-consistent dynamic (Markov coherent) settings.  
- Static gradient: Derives a new gradient formula via the envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio, giving sampling estimators that combine Monte Carlo with a small convex program over the risk envelope.  
- Static guarantees: Shows consistency of the static estimator via an SAA argument and recovers CVaR and mean-semi-deviation as special cases.  
- Dynamic gradient: Establishes a policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk by coupling the static gradient with a risk-sensitive Bellman equation.  
- Algorithm: Proposes an actor-critic scheme using robust-MDP-style value approximation and sampling under ξ-weighted transition kernels.  
- Empirics: Demonstrates differences in induced policies on a small trading example; empirical validation is limited to this toy case.  
- Theoretical quality: Technical development is careful and sound under stated assumptions (finite spaces, Slater condition, bounded likelihood ratios, differentiable envelope constraints).  
- Missing theory: Key proofs and the full actor-critic algorithm are deferred to the supplement; no finite-sample or high-probability error bounds provided.  
- Practical issues: Computing KKT multipliers, handling non-unique saddle points, and sampling from ξ-weighted kernels need deeper analysis.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written and organized but notation-heavy; dynamic section would benefit from pseudocode and clearer discussion of per-update computational cost, variance-reduction, and sampling guidance.  
- Originality: High — unified coherent-risk gradient and Markov coherent policy-gradient theorem appear novel and subsume prior risk-specific methods.  
- Significance: Potentially substantial as a general risk-sensitive RL toolkit linked to robust control, but scalability and broader impact remain unproven.  
- Pros for acceptance: Unifying theoretical contribution, clear connections to prior work, and consistency guarantees for static estimator.  
- Cons for acceptance: Sparse experiments, practical implementation concerns (convex program and ξ-weighted sampling per update), and missing finite-sample analysis and some main-text proofs.
