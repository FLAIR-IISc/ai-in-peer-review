## Review of "Policy Gradient for Coherent Risk Measures"

This paper presents a unified framework for policy gradient methods in reinforcement learning (RL) that extends beyond expected cost minimization to encompass the entire class of coherent risk measures. This is a significant generalization of previous work, which often focused on specific risk measures like variance or CVaR. The authors address both static and time-consistent dynamic risk measures, offering novel gradient formulas and algorithms utilizing sampling and convex programming. The work builds upon foundational concepts in coherent risk theory (Artzner et al., 1997) and connects them to recent advances in risk-sensitive RL (e.g., Chow et al., 2017; Prashanth & Ghavamzadeh, 2016). 

The key strength of this paper lies in its generality and theoretical rigor. By framing risk-sensitive RL within the coherent risk framework, the authors provide a principled way to analyze and optimize for a broad range of risk preferences. The derivation of gradient formulas, particularly for the dynamic case, is a significant contribution. The use of sampling-based methods addresses the scalability challenges often associated with dynamic programming in large MDPs, as highlighted by Petrik & Subramanian (2009). The inclusion of a numerical example demonstrating the impact of different risk measures (mean-semideviation vs. standard deviation) reinforces the practical relevance of the work. 

However, the paper's clarity could be improved. The mathematical notation is dense, and while the authors attempt to provide intuition, a more accessible explanation of the core concepts would benefit readers unfamiliar with coherent risk theory. Furthermore, the discussion of the actor-critic algorithm in Section 5 feels somewhat abbreviated. More details on the implementation and convergence analysis (beyond what is alluded to in the supplementary material) would strengthen the contribution. While the authors acknowledge the need for further work on importance sampling, a more detailed discussion of its potential benefits and challenges within this framework would be valuable. Finally, the connection to robust MDPs (mentioned in relation to dynamic risk) could be explored more deeply.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

* **Novelty:** Provides a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL based on coherent risk measures.
* **Generality:** Extends existing methods to a wider class of risk preferences.
* **Theoretical Soundness:**  Rigorous derivation of gradient formulas.
* **Practical Relevance:** Demonstrated through a numerical example.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* **Clarity:** Dense mathematical notation and limited explanation of core concepts.
* **Completeness:** Abbreviated discussion of the actor-critic algorithm and convergence analysis.



**Overall:** I believe this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of risk-sensitive RL. While improvements in clarity and completeness would be beneficial, the novelty, generality, and theoretical rigor of the work justify acceptance. I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions.
