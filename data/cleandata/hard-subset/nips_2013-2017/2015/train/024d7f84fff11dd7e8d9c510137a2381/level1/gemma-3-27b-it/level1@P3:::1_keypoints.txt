- Generalizes risk-sensitive policy gradients to the full class of coherent risk measures, covering both static and dynamic cases.  
- Unifies previous work (variance, CVaR, mean-variance) under a coherent risk framework.  
- Main technical contribution: novel gradient formulas suitable for sampling-based approximation in large MDPs.  
- Derivations are technically sound and leverage convex duality and the envelope theorem effectively.  
- Strong theoretical grounding, including connections to robust MDPs and discussion of time consistency.  
- Assumption on smoothness of constraints (Assumption 2.2) is reasonable and supported by prior work.  
- Proposition on SAA estimator convergence gives a solid foundation but lacks detailed discussion of convergence rates/conditions.  
- Empirical validation is limited and mostly illustrative; needs broader comparisons on standard benchmarks.  
- Unclear whether the approach offers practical computational advantages due to solving convex programs each iteration.  
- Paper is generally well-written but dense; notation can be overwhelming for readers.  
- Theoretical results are precise but would benefit from more intuitive explanations and illustrative examples.  
- Needs clearer explanation of how choice of the risk envelope affects resulting policies.  
- Actor-critic algorithm description is brief; more implementation details (function approximation, sampling) are needed.  
- Originality: significant in generalization and in deriving gradient formulas; novelty largely in combining and extending existing techniques.  
- Potential significance: high impact for applications requiring robust risk management (finance, robotics, healthcare).  
- Identified promising link between risk-shaping and model uncertainty.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, increased flexibility, novel gradients, important robustness connection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, potential computational complexity, and dense presentation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; add more experiments and clarify computational/practical limitations.
