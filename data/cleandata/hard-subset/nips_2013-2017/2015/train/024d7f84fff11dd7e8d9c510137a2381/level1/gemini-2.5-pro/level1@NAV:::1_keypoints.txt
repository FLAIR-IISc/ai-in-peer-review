- Proposes a general framework extending policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, unifying prior work on specific risks like mean-variance and CVaR.  
- Core contributions: (1) a novel static gradient formula via dual representation and the Envelope theorem, enabling sampling combined with convex programming; (2) an extension to time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk with a new policy gradient theorem and an actor-critic outline.  
- Elegantly re-derives the CVaR gradient as a special case, demonstrating framework generality.  
- Theoretical development appears rigorous, leveraging duality and the Envelope theorem, with a consistency result for the static risk estimator (Proposition 4.4).  
- High originality and significance by enabling a broad, principled set of risk criteria from finance/OR to be used in RL.  
- Main clarity issue: the actor-critic algorithm for dynamic risk is presented at a high level with key details and convergence analysis deferred to supplementary material.  
- Major empirical weakness: only a single, simple portfolio selection experiment is provided, not a sequential RL task.  
- The empirical results do not convincingly validate the proposed policy gradient or actor-critic methods in standard RL environments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical unification, elegant and general technical approach, and high potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minimal empirical evaluation and underdeveloped presentation of the dynamic risk algorithm in the main paper.
