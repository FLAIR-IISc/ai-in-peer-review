- Generalizes risk-sensitive policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, covering both static and time-consistent dynamic cases.  
- Proposes a unified approach using sampling + convex programming for static risks and an actor-critic style algorithm for dynamic risks.  
- Derives new risk-sensitive policy gradient formulas (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2) and associated algorithms.  
- Correctly situates work among prior risk-sensitive RL research (variance, CVaR, exponential utility) and foundational coherent risk literature (Artzner et al.).  
- Connects coherent risk to robust MDPs and scalable planning methods, strengthening theoretical context.  
- Technical derivations appear sound; key steps are clear in the main text with detailed proofs in the supplement.  
- Relies on standard but strong assumptions (bounded likelihood ratio, smooth risk envelope); discussion of violations is needed.  
- Numerical illustration provides limited empirical validation; more extensive experiments on scalability and performance are needed.  
- Algorithmic complexity is high, which, combined with limited experiments, raises practical adoption concerns.  
- Writing is generally good, but the dynamic risk section and actor-critic algorithm are dense and need more intuitive explanation and examples.  
- Notation is mostly consistent, but the transition to experiments is abrupt and experimental details/results could be clearer.  
- Highly original contribution with significant potential impact in finance, operations research, and uncertainty-aware RL.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial generalization, novel and mathematically sound results, unified framework, and broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy assumptions, algorithm complexity, limited empirical validation, and clarity issues in key sections.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions for clarity and further empirical investigation.
