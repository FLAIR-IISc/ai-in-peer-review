- Major theoretical contribution: develops policy gradient methods for the entire class of coherent risk measures rather than only specific risks.  
- Unified framework: uses the representation theorem to generalize and subsume previous results (CVaR, variance, mean-semideviation).  
- Main results: gradient formula for static coherent risk (Theorem 4.2) enabling sampling, and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk (Theorem 5.2) leading to an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Technical soundness: rigorous theoretical development with clear proofs and consistency results for gradient estimators (Proposition 4.4).  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-organized and clearly written with precise notation and illustrative examples.  
- Originality: substantial theoretical advance and fundamental unification of risk-sensitive RL methods.  
- Significance: broad practical impact for domains requiring risk management (finance, robotics, safety-critical systems) and connections to model uncertainty.  
- Empirical validation: includes a numerical trading example showing different risk measures yield different policies, but limited in scope.  
- Comparative evaluation: lacks comprehensive empirical comparisons with prior methods for specific risk measures.  
- Computational concerns: does not thoroughly analyze computational complexity or scalability of the proposed approaches.  
- Model uncertainty link: connection is intriguing but remains conceptual without concrete examples or initial theoretical results.  
- Recommendation balance: strong candidate for acceptance due to theoretical contribution, with empirical and complexity analyses identified as areas for strengthening.
