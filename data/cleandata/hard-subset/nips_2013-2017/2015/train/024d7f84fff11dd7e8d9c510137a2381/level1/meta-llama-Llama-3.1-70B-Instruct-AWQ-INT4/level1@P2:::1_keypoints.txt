- Presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that generalizes and extends prior results.  
- Develops policy gradient algorithms specifically for coherent risk measures.  
- Provides a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk.  
- Proposes an algorithm to estimate gradients of general static coherent risk via sampling combined with convex programming.  
- Derives a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk, linking the gradient to a value function and yielding an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written with clear technical exposition.  
- Uses examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation) to illustrate applicability.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate the value of flexible risk criteria and optimizing risk-sensitive policies.  
- Uses convex programming and sampling-based estimation, making the approach scalable and efficient.  
- Assumes the risk envelope is known in explicit form, which may not hold in practice.  
- Does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms.  
- Lacks discussion of potential limitations, such as sensitivity to choice of risk measure.  
- Approach is novel and generalizes previous work in risk-sensitive RL.  
- The use of convex programming and sampling-based estimators is a new contribution.  
- Contributions have potential high impact across finance, operations research, and AI.  
- Technical results are sound and supported by theoretical analysis and numerical illustrations.  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address weaknesses.  
- Requests for authors: provide more comprehensive computational complexity analysis and discuss limitations/context of results.
