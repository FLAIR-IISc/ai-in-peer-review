- Paper proposes a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL covering the class of coherent risk measures.  
- Two main contributions: a sampling-based gradient estimator for static coherent risk via convex programming, and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk enabling actor-critic methods.  
- Builds on Artzner et al. (1999) and RL foundations, generalizing prior work on specific risk measures (CVaR, variance, mean-semideviation).  
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous proofs for main theorems (4.2, 5.2) and elegant use of the coherent risk representation theorem.  
- Unification: framework subsumes previous approaches and recovers known gradient formulas for special cases.  
- Practical algorithm: motivated sampling-based gradient estimator with a consistency guarantee (Proposition 4.4).  
- Clear illustration: numerical example demonstrates how different risk measures yield different policy choices in a financial setting.  
- Computational complexity concern: requires solving convex programs per gradient estimate, with limited discussion of scalability to large problems.  
- Finite-space assumption: theoretical development restricted to finite probability spaces, with only brief mention of extensions to continuous/Lp spaces.  
- Limited empirical comparison: numerical example is illustrative but lacks comparisons of sample efficiency or computational cost against prior methods.  
- Underdeveloped model-uncertainty link: paper mentions connections to model uncertainty but does not elaborate or develop them.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution that unifies risk-sensitive RL and merits acceptance despite practical limitations.
