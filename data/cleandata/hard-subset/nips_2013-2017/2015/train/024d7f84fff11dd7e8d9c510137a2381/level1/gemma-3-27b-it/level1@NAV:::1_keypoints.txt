- Paper presents a unified policy-gradient framework for RL that optimizes the full class of coherent risk measures, generalizing beyond expected cost.
- Handles both static and time-consistent dynamic risk measures, deriving novel gradient formulas.
- Proposes sampling-based algorithms and convex-programming approaches to address scalability in large MDPs.
- Builds on coherent risk theory and connects to recent risk-sensitive RL literature.
- Theoretical contribution is strong: rigorous derivations of gradient formulas, especially for the dynamic case.
- Numerical example demonstrates practical impact by comparing different risk measures (mean-semideviation vs. standard deviation).
- Clarity is an issue: dense mathematical notation and limited intuitive explanation hinder accessibility.
- Actor-critic algorithm discussion is brief; implementation details and convergence analysis are insufficient in the main text.
- Treatment of importance sampling is acknowledged but lacks depth on benefits and challenges within the framework.
- Connection to robust MDPs (in the dynamic risk context) is mentioned but underexplored.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to novelty, generality, and theoretical rigor, contingent on clearer exposition and more complete algorithmic details.
