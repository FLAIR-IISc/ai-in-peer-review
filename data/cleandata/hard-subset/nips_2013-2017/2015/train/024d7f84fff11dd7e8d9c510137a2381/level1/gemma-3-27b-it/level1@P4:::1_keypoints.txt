- Presents a policy-gradient method for reinforcement learning under coherent risk measures, generalizing beyond variance/CVaR.
- Provides a unified framework covering static coherent risks (sampling + convex programming) and time-consistent dynamic risks (actor-critic with value approximation).
- Core technical contribution: formulation and derivation of a risk-sensitive policy gradient within the coherent-risk framework.
- Theorem 4.2: a general gradient formula for static risk measures that subsumes prior specific-results.
- Theorem 5.2: extension to dynamic risk measures with an actor-critic style algorithm and value-function approximation.
- Well-motivated positioning with clear problem formulation and assumptions, linking to finance and operations research.
- Derivations appear mathematically sound and are supported by relevant theorems and references.
- Novel combination of sampling with convex programming (static case) and actor-critic methods (dynamic case).
- Clear exposition and organization, making concepts and derivations accessible.
- Insightful connection drawn between dynamic coherent risk and robust MDP formulations.
- Major practical concern: algorithms require solving convex programs inside gradient loops, raising computational complexity issues for large-scale problems.
- Lacks detailed implementation guidance (e.g., choice of function approximators for value functions) and practical considerations.
- Empirical evaluation is limited and simple; scalability and comparative performance on benchmarks are not demonstrated.
- Theoretical results depend on assumptions (e.g., smoothness of risk-envelope constraints), but sensitivity to assumption violations is not explored.
- Potential sample inefficiency in the dynamic-case two-phase sampling; variance reduction and sampling-efficiency strategies are not investigated.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, principled general framework, solid derivations, and new research directions.
- Arguments against acceptance: practical applicability concerns due to computational cost, sparse empirical validation, and missing implementation detail.
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to add implementation guidance, expanded experiments, complexity discussion, and variance-reduction suggestions.
