- Presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive RL using coherent risk measures, with policy gradient algorithms for static and dynamic cases and new gradient formulas plus a Markov coherent risk theorem, validated by numerical examples.  
- Extends prior RL risk work (variance, CVaR, mean-semideviation) to the full class of coherent risk measures and connects to dynamic programming and robust MDPs.  
- Strength: provides a unified framework that generalizes and extends previous results.  
- Strength: focuses on coherent risk measures, widely accepted in finance and operations research.  
- Strength: derives new gradient formulas for static and dynamic coherent risks.  
- Strength: includes numerical examples and comparisons demonstrating utility.  
- Weakness: high technical complexity, requiring strong expertise in convex optimization, dynamic programming, and RL.  
- Weakness: limited scope by focusing only on coherent risk measures, which may not cover all risk-sensitive settings.  
- Weakness: proposed algorithms may be computationally expensive and may not scale well to large problems.  
- Originality: novel and original generalization of existing risk-sensitive RL results with new gradient formulas.  
- Significance: meaningful implications for making risk-sensitive RL more generally applicable to real-world problems.  
- Quality: technically sound, clear, well-organized, and thoroughly analyzed.  
- Clarity: well-written with a clear introduction, literature review, and statement of contributions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel unified approach, significant practical relevance, and strong technical contributions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: steep technical requirements, computational costs, and limited applicability beyond coherent risks.  
- Recommendation: Accept for publication at NIPS.
