- Proposes a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that generalizes and extends prior work under a coherent-risk framework.  
- Considers both static and time-consistent dynamic risk measures and develops policy gradient algorithms for coherent risk measures.  
- Main contribution is the formulation of the risk-sensitive policy gradient under the coherent-risk framework.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and a thorough related work section explaining differences from prior work.  
- Technical soundness is strong: clear derivation of the static-risk gradient formula and a provided proof for the dynamic-risk gradient.  
- Demonstrates how the dynamic-risk gradient yields an actor-critic style algorithm for the dynamic risk problem.  
- High novelty: presents a new unified framework that generalizes previous results and offers new insights into risk-sensitive optimization.  
- High significance: potential applications to real-world problems (e.g., portfolio optimization) and promises more robust, reliable solutions.  
- Strengths summarized: clear exposition, thorough related work, strong technical derivations, high novelty and practical relevance.  
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in RL and convex optimization, limiting accessibility to non-experts.  
- Weakness: limited numerical examples and simulations to empirically demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations and potential drawbacks of the approach.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, novel contribution with strong technical foundations and real-world applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns, lack of empirical validation, and inadequate discussion of limitations.
