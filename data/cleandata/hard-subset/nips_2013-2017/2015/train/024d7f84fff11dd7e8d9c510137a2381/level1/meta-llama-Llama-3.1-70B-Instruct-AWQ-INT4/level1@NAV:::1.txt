This paper presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) using coherent risk measures, which generalizes and extends previous results. The authors develop policy gradient algorithms for RL with coherent risk objectives, applicable to both static and dynamic risk measures.

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper introduces a new framework for risk-sensitive RL, building upon the concept of coherent risk measures. The authors derive a formula for the gradient of static coherent risk measures and propose a sampling-based estimator for the gradient. They also develop an actor-critic style algorithm for dynamic Markov coherent risk measures. The approach is demonstrated through a numerical example, showcasing the importance of flexibility in designing risk criteria.

**Strengths**

1. **Unified framework**: The paper provides a unified approach to risk-sensitive RL, encompassing various risk measures and generalizing previous results.
2. **Theoretical foundations**: The authors establish a solid theoretical foundation for their approach, leveraging the concept of coherent risk measures and the Envelope Theorem.
3. **Practical algorithms**: The paper presents practical algorithms for estimating the gradient of static and dynamic coherent risk measures, making the approach applicable to real-world problems.
4. **Numerical illustration**: The authors provide a numerical example demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach and the importance of flexibility in designing risk criteria.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Assumptions**: The paper relies on several assumptions, such as the existence of a compact set C and the smoothness of the constraints, which may not hold in all cases.
2. **Computational complexity**: The algorithms proposed in the paper may be computationally intensive, particularly for large-scale problems.
3. **Limited scope**: The paper focuses primarily on coherent risk measures, which may not be the only relevant risk measures in all applications.
