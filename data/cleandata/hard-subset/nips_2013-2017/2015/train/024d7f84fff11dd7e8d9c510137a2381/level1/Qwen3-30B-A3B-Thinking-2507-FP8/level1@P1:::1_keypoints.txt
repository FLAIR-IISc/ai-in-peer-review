- Presents a unified policy-gradient framework for the entire class of coherent risk measures (static and time-consistent dynamic).  
- Extends prior RL work (CVaR, variance, mean-variance) to a general coherent-risk setting using Artzner et al.’s characterization.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous; Theorem 2.1 and supporting proofs connect coherent risk measures to policy-gradient formulas.  
- Provides a sampling-based gradient estimator for static coherent risk combining convex programming with likelihood-ratio techniques.  
- Derives a dynamic programming policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and proposes an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Establishes a consistency result for the proposed estimators/algorithms (Proposition 4.4).  
- Numerical financial trading example illustrates that different coherent risk measures produce different, practically relevant policies.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with consistent notation and useful supplementary implementation details.  
- Original and novel: first unified RL framework for coherent risk measures, recovering previous methods as special cases.  
- Innovative use of the risk-envelope representation to derive gradient formulas for RL.  
- Significant practical relevance for finance, operations, and safety-critical systems; simplifies risk-sensitive RL algorithm design.  
- Connects to model uncertainty and robust decision-making, suggesting broader impact.  
- Main limitation: requires the risk envelope to be explicitly known (Assumption 2.2), which may not hold or may need estimation.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scale; does not fully demonstrate scalability to large or continuous MDPs.  
- Actor-critic algorithm lacks full implementation detail in the paper due to space constraints.  
- No comparison of computational efficiency with existing methods is provided.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant theoretical contribution with practical utility despite noted limitations.
