- Presents a unified extension of policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Derives a new, sampling-friendly formula for the gradient of static coherent risk.  
- Develops an algorithm to approximate the gradient of general static coherent risk.  
- Proposes a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk linking the gradient to a suitable value function.  
- Introduces a corresponding actor-critic algorithm for Markov coherent risk.  
- Well-motivated problem formulation that logically extends prior work and addresses limitations of specific risk methods.  
- High technical soundness with clear equations and thorough theoretical discussion.  
- Above-average clarity and organization; algorithms are described in detail and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Numerical illustration on a toy finance example shows risk-averse behavior and flexibility across risk types.  
- Experiments are simple and static, limiting empirical validation and generality.  
- Lacks a more comprehensive evaluation and deeper discussion of method limitations and future directions.  
- Original contribution is nontrivial and a natural extension of existing methods.  
- Paper’s significance is high, addressing an important problem in risk-sensitive RL with potential field impact.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
