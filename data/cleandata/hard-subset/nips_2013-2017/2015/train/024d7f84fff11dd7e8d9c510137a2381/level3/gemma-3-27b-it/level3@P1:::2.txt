This paper tackles the important problem of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning, extending existing methods to a more general and theoretically sound framework based on coherent risk measures. Recent work has explored risk-sensitive policy gradients using specific risk measures like variance or Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR). This paper significantly generalizes these results by considering the entire class of coherent risk measures, widely accepted in finance and operations research, offering a unified approach. The core contribution lies in formulating policy gradient algorithms applicable to both static and time-consistent dynamic coherent risk, combining sampling techniques with convex programming for static cases and an actor-critic style approach for dynamic scenarios.

The paper is well-structured, beginning with a clear introduction to risk-sensitive optimization and motivation for using coherent risk measures. It effectively positions the work within the existing literature, referencing key papers in risk management and reinforcement learning (e.g., Artzner et al. [2], Rockafellar & Uryasev [23], Sutton & Barto [27]). The mathematical preliminaries are presented concisely, and the definitions of coherent risk measures and their properties are clearly explained. The subsequent sections detailing the gradient formulas for both static and dynamic risk are technically rigorous and build logically upon the foundational definitions.  A crucial aspect is the use of Theorem 2.1, which establishes the relationship between coherent risk and risk envelopes, enabling the derivation of sampling-based gradient estimators. The introduction of Proposition 4.4 provides crucial conditions for ensuring the consistency of these estimators, lending further theoretical weight to the approach.

The strengths of the paper are numerous. Technically, it is sound, with a clear derivation of the gradient formulas.  The authors demonstrate a careful approach to evaluation, acknowledging the computational challenges and proposing practical algorithms leveraging sampling and convex programming. The paper effectively bridges the gap between the theoretical foundations of coherent risk and the practical demands of reinforcement learning. The originality stems from the generalization to the entire class of coherent risk measures, which has not been previously addressed in the RL literature. The significance is high, as a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL could have a broad impact on applications ranging from robotics and finance to healthcare and autonomous systems. The inclusion of a numerical example, albeit simple, demonstrates the practical implications of the approach and the flexibility in selecting risk criteria.

However, the paper is not without some limitations. The numerical example is relatively basic and lacks a comparison with existing risk-sensitive RL algorithms on standard benchmarks. While the theoretical framework is strong, the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms, particularly for dynamic risk measures with large state spaces, is not thoroughly addressed. The actor-critic algorithm is briefly outlined, and a more detailed analysis of its convergence properties and practical performance would strengthen the contribution.  Furthermore, the discussion on handling model uncertainty, mentioned towards the end, feels somewhat tangential and could benefit from a more focused exploration. The assumption of known risk envelopes (Assumption 2.2) might be restrictive in some real-world scenarios, and exploring methods to learn or estimate these envelopes would be a valuable extension.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Strong theoretical foundation:** The paper builds on well-established concepts from coherent risk theory and provides rigorous mathematical derivations.
*   **Generalization and unification:** The extension to the entire class of coherent risk measures is a significant contribution, providing a more general and flexible framework for risk-sensitive RL.
*   **Practical algorithms:** The proposed policy gradient and actor-critic algorithms are concrete and provide a starting point for implementing risk-sensitive RL in various applications.
*   **Clear writing and organization:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making the complex concepts accessible to a broad audience.
*   **Potential impact:** The unified framework has the potential to advance the field of risk-sensitive RL and facilitate the development of more robust and reliable AI systems.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited empirical evaluation:** The numerical example is simple and does not provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithms.
*   **Computational complexity:** The paper does not thoroughly address the computational challenges of applying the algorithms to large-scale problems.
*   **Restricted assumptions:** The assumption of known risk envelopes may limit the applicability of the approach in some scenarios.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the limitations, I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The theoretical rigor, the generalization to coherent risk measures, and the proposal of practical algorithms make it a valuable addition to the NIPS workshop. The potential impact of the work is considerable, and the minor weaknesses can be addressed in future research. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, primarily focusing on expanding the empirical evaluation and discussing the computational complexity in more detail.
