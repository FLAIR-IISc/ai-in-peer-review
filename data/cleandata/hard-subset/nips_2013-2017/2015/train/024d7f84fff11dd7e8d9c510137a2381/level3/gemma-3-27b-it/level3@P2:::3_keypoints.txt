- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, covering static and time-consistent dynamic forms.  
- Generalizes prior work (e.g., CVaR) into a unified framework with new gradient formulas suitable for sampling-based approximation.  
- Uses the representation theorem for coherent risk and the envelope theorem to derive tractable gradient expressions.  
- Proposes an actor-critic approach for dynamic coherent risk measures, expanding applicability in RL.  
- Technical derivations are rigorous and build on established convex optimization and RL results.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written despite technical complexity.  
- Sampling-based gradient estimation algorithms are practical for large-scale problems without analytical solutions.  
- Highlights a connection between dynamic coherent risk and robust MDPs as a promising future direction.  
- Notational clarity issue: the term “ξPθ” in Theorem 2.1 needs clarification and alignment with Ruszczynski & Shapiro [26].  
- Minor typo present (line 141: "risk enevlop[e]").  
- Tractability discussion should be refined—computational challenges stem more from policy parametrization and state-space dimensionality than the risk measure itself.  
- Numerical illustration is simple and empirical evaluation is limited; more benchmarks would strengthen the paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound generalization with significant potential impact and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: contributions may be viewed as incremental and empirical validation is limited.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions (address notation, tractability discussion, and consider expanding empirical evaluation).
