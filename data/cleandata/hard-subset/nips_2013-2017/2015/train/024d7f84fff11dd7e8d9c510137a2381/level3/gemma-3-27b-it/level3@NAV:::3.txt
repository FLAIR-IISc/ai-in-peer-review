## Review of "Policy Gradient for Coherent Risk Measures"

This paper presents a significant extension to risk-sensitive policy gradient methods by generalizing existing approaches – previously focused on specific risk measures like CVaR – to the broader class of coherent risk measures, encompassing both static and dynamically consistent settings. The authors provide gradient formulas for both static and dynamic cases, leveraging concepts from coherent risk theory and establishing a unified framework for risk-sensitive reinforcement learning.

The work builds on recent advances in risk-sensitive RL, acknowledging contributions from authors exploring variance, CVaR, and other risk metrics. The core contribution lies in the elegant extension of policy gradient techniques to the entire class of coherent risk measures, offering a more general and theoretically grounded approach.  The paper clearly articulates the benefits of coherent risk measures – stemming from their desirable properties as outlined by Artzner et al. – and highlights the importance of time-consistency for dynamic problems. The organization is excellent, with clear definitions, motivations, and a logical progression of ideas. The mathematical exposition is rigorous and well-written, making the paper easy to follow despite the technical depth. The results appear correct, and the inclusion of example applications like CVaR demonstrates the generality of the proposed framework.

However, a few minor points could be improved. The notation "ξPθ" in Theorem 2.1 is indeed confusing, and adopting the notation from Theorem 6.6 in [26] as suggested would enhance clarity. A typo exists on line 141 ("risk enevlop[e]"). Furthermore, the paper would benefit from explicitly clarifying that tractability issues in applying these methods stem from the policy parametrization (and associated function approximation) rather than inherent complexities within Markov risk measures themselves.  MDPs *with* Markov risk measures *are* tractable, and this distinction is important. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Solid Theoretical Contribution:** The paper provides a valuable extension to existing risk-sensitive RL methods with strong theoretical backing.
*   **Generality & Unification:** The framework generalizes previous results, providing a more comprehensive approach to risk management in RL.
*   **Clarity & Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and easy to understand.
*   **Correctness:** The mathematical results appear sound and well-justified.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Minor Notation Issue:** The "ξPθ" notation could be confusing.
*   **Potential Misinterpretation:** The connection between tractability and Markov risk measures needs clarification.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a minor but solid and important extension of existing results. The paper addresses a relevant and challenging problem with a rigorous and well-presented solution. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points mentioned above. The work has the potential to significantly impact the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning by providing a more general and theoretically sound foundation for future research.
