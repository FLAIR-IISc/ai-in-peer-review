- Proposes a significant extension: policy gradient methods for the broad class of coherent risk measures, unifying prior work on variance/CVaR.  
- Derives concrete policy gradient and actor-critic algorithms from the unified formulation.  
- Addresses dynamic, time-consistent risk via an actor-critic extension.  
- Well-situated in prior literature with clear motivation for using coherent risk measures in RL.  
- Technically sound presentation with clear equations and a rigorous gradient derivation (Theorem 4.2).  
- Demonstrates risk-averse behavior on a simple three-asset portfolio example, showing different risk measures yield different strategies.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and static; lacks complex, dynamic environments (e.g., time-varying trading scenarios).  
- Missing ablation studies on hyperparameters and choice of convex programming solvers.  
- Practical implications of selecting different coherent risk measures are underexplored.  
- Reliance on Assumption 2.2 (form of the risk envelope) may restrict applicability in some settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel unified contribution, clarity, and technical rigor.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments and unresolved practical/assumption-related concerns.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with encouragement to expand experimental validation in future work.
