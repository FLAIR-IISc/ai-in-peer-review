- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, covering both static and dynamic cases.
- Leverages the dual representation (Theorem 2.1) that expresses coherent risk as a max expected value over a convex risk envelope.
- Derives a general gradient formula for static coherent risk (Theorem 4.2).
- Proves a policy gradient theorem for dynamic Markov coherent risk (Theorem 5.2).
- Provides a unified framework that generalizes prior special-case results (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation).
- Strength: strong theoretical contribution with rigorous proofs and a consistency result (Proposition 4.4).
- Strength: highly original extension of the literature beyond previous NIPS work on specific risk measures.
- Strength: paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, making complex material accessible.
- Strength: framework gives practical flexibility in risk-criterion selection and connects coherent risk to model uncertainty (Section 7).
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited to a single illustrative example and lacks comprehensive comparisons.
- Weakness: preliminaries need clearer explanation of the roles of Pθ and ξ and the relationship between U(Pθ) and (Ω,F,Pθ).
- Weakness: Equation 2 appears more restrictive than Theorem 2.1; the convex programming assumption needs justification.
- Weakness: focuses on finite probability spaces; lacks explicit discussion of extension to continuous distributions common in RL.
- Pro acceptance: unified theory, practical sampling/convex-programming gradient estimators, and significant theoretical impact.
- Con acceptance: limited empirical validation, unresolved technical clarifications, and unclear computational complexity of proposed algorithms.
- Overall: high-quality theoretical paper, within NIPS scope, recommended for acceptance provided authors address risk-envelope and continuous-distribution clarifications.
