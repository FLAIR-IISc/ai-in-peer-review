- Paper studies risk-sensitive policy optimization using coherent risk measures via their dual (robust) representation ρ(Z)=maxξ∈U(Pθ) Eξ[Z].
- Main contributions: policy-gradient formulas for static risks and time-consistent dynamic (Markov) coherent risk, plus an actor-critic scheme for the dynamic case.
- Central assumption: the risk envelope U(Pθ) admits an explicit convex program with smooth, twice-differentiable, M-Lipschitz dependence on Pθ.
- Provides analytic gradient derivations for specific risks (CVaR, mean-semideviation) and a general SAA-based sample+convex-program estimator.
- Dynamic case yields a policy-gradient theorem under Markov coherent risk using a weighted transition kernel and a saddle-point-dependent stage cost hθ.
- Unifies and extends prior work on mean-variance, CVaR gradients, and robust MDP connections; builds on coherent risk duality and dynamic time-consistent risk literature.
- Strength: unified framework producing gradient estimators across a broad class of coherent risks (static and dynamic).
- Strength: sound variational foundation using dual representation and envelope theorem to account for Pθ and U(Pθ) interaction.
- Strength: practical, generally consistent SAA-based gradient estimation algorithm under mild conditions.
- Strength: dynamic extension connects risk-sensitive RL to robust MDP value functions and proposes an actor-critic design.
- Strength: clear exposition; accessible to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited—dynamic algorithm not tested at scale and lacks benchmark comparisons or variance/efficiency analyses.
- Weakness: actor requires multiple next-state samples per state (simulator access), limiting online/trajectory-only applicability.
- Weakness: potential high variance of likelihood-ratio gradients for tail-sensitive risks (e.g., small-α CVaR); importance sampling not explored.
- Weakness: Assumption 2.2 (explicit smooth convex envelope) is more restrictive than existence guarantees and needs clarification on practical breadth.
- Weakness: preliminaries should clarify roles of Pθ (policy-induced base distribution) and ξ (adversarial reweighting density ratio) earlier.
- Weakness: Eq. (2) is a constructive but restrictive subset of envelopes from Theorem 2.1; paper should explicitly state and justify this restrictiveness.
- Weakness: Theorem 4.2 relies on smoothness/Lipschitz of constraints for differentiating saddle-points—paper should sketch where these properties are used.
- Weakness: finite Ω restriction—add discussion on generalization to continuous distributions, appropriate function spaces, and SAA discretization issues.
- Quality: technically sound derivations consistent with convex analysis and policy-gradient techniques, but some proofs are deferred and require careful verification.
- Originality: high—novel, general policy-gradient treatment for coherent risks that subsumes prior special cases.
- Significance: high—broad applicability to risk-sensitive RL and strong connections to robust RL and safety-critical applications.
- Suggestions: expand empirical evaluation (larger MDPs, baselines, variance/sample-efficiency analyses, ablations on SAA).
- Suggestions: explore variance reduction (importance sampling, control variates, tailored baselines) and provide guidance on modeling U(Pθ) and verifying Assumption 2.2.
- Acceptance arguments: strong unified theoretical contribution, new dynamic theorem and actor-critic framework, subsumes prior special cases, and high relevance to robust/safety RL.
- Rejection concerns: limited empirical validation, restrictive technical assumptions needing more discussion/examples, and deferred proof details hinging on regularity conditions.
- Overall: strong theoretical paper with significant contributions; leans toward acceptance subject to clarifying assumptions, addressing continuous cases, and strengthening empirical evidence.
