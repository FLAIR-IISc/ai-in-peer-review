This paper presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning by extending policy gradient methods to the whole class of coherent risk measures. The authors provide a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk and an algorithm for the gradient of general static coherent risk that involves sampling with convex programming. They also derive a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and propose an actor-critic style algorithm for solving dynamic risk problems.

The paper is well-written and organized, making it accessible even to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to coherent risk measures and their properties, which is helpful for readers unfamiliar with the topic. The technical derivations are sound and well-presented, with the proofs relegated to the supplementary material.

One of the main strengths of the paper is its unified analysis, which covers a broad class of risk measures, including most well-known ones. The authors demonstrate the generality of their approach by deriving previously known results as special cases of their framework. This is a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning.

However, the paper could benefit from a deeper empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm. While the authors provide a numerical illustration of their approach, more extensive experiments would be helpful in demonstrating the effectiveness of their method.

There are a few minor issues with the presentation. For example, the roles of Pθ and ξ in the preliminaries could be clarified further. Additionally, the authors claim that Equation 2 is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1, but it is not entirely clear why this is the case.

From a theoretical perspective, the paper is strong, and the results are compelling. However, the proofs require verification, and some readers may find the technical derivations challenging to follow.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The authors' unified approach to coherent risk measures is a major strength, and their results have the potential to impact a wide range of applications.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to coherent risk measures and their properties.
* The technical derivations are sound and well-presented.
* The paper demonstrates the generality of the approach by deriving previously known results as special cases of their framework.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a deeper empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.
* Some readers may find the technical derivations challenging to follow.
* The roles of Pθ and ξ in the preliminaries could be clarified further.

Recommendation:

I recommend accepting this paper, but with the suggestion that the authors provide additional empirical results to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach. Additionally, the authors may want to consider clarifying the roles of Pθ and ξ in the preliminaries and providing more context for Equation 2.
