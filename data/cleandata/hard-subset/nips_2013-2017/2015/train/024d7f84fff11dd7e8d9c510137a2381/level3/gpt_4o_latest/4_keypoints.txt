- Significant theoretical contribution: extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures for risk-sensitive RL.
- Unified framework: handles both static and time-consistent dynamic risk measures, generalizing prior work on CVaR and mean-variance.
- Core technical innovation: uses the dual representation of coherent risk (convex hull of probability distributions) to define risk as a max expected value.
- Gradient derivation: provides novel gradient formulas for risk-sensitive optimization based on the dual representation.
- Algorithmic development: proposes sampling-based gradient estimation algorithms applicable to large-scale MDPs.
- Clarity and presentation: paper is well-written, organized, and includes illustrative examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation).
- Broad significance: approach is likely impactful across domains since coherent risk measures are widely used and flexible.
- Weakness — empirical evaluation: limited empirical analysis, especially for dynamic risk measures and algorithm behavior.
- Weakness — notation/preliminaries: roles of P_theta and ξ are not clearly explained, hindering comprehension for some readers.
- Weakness — theory linkages: connection between Equation 2 (convex envelope) and Theorem 2.1 is insufficiently clarified.
- Weakness — continuous variables: no explicit discussion on extending the analysis to continuous distributions.
- Weakness — technical assumptions: the role of constraint smoothness in proving Theorem 4.2 is not adequately discussed.
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to clarify notation, theoretical links, smoothness assumptions, and to expand empirical validation.
