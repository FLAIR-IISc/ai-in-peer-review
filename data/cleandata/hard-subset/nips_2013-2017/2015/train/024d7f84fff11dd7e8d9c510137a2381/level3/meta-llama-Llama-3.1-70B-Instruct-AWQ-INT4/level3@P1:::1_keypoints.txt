- Proposes extending policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, covering both static and dynamic cases.  
- Main contribution: a sampling-friendly gradient formula for static coherent risk.  
- Provides an algorithm for static coherent risk gradients using sampling plus convex programming, with a consistency result.  
- Derives a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and proposes a corresponding actor-critic algorithm.  
- Paper is technically sound and generally well-written with clear technical explanations.  
- Tries to cover too many topics in limited space, making parts hard to follow.  
- Dynamic risk section is high-level and lacks algorithmic details and experimental validation, raising practicality concerns.  
- Unified derivation is a major strength and a valuable contribution to risk-sensitive decision making.  
- Novel expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures are interesting and original.  
- Lacks empirical validation of the sample-based approximation, an important omission.  
- No experimental comparison between sample-based and analytical gradients to assess trade-offs.  
- Lack of comprehensive experiments limits assessment of impact and practicality; consider splitting the work or focusing on static measures.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
