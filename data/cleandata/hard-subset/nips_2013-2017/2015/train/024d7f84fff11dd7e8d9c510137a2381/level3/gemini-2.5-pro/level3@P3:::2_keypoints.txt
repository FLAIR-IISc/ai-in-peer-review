- Develops policy gradient methods for the entire class of coherent risk measures.  
- Addresses two settings: static coherent risk of cumulative cost and dynamic, time-consistent Markov coherent risk.  
- Core idea: estimate policy gradients via the dual representation of coherent risks (risk envelope as worst-case expectation over probability distortions).  
- Static-case algorithm: combines sampling with convex programming to estimate the gradient.  
- Dynamic-case contribution: a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and a corresponding actor-critic algorithm.  
- Unifies and generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL work (e.g., mean-variance, CVaR) to a principled coherent-risk framework.  
- Technical quality is high: rigorous, elegant derivations (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2) combining envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio trick.  
- Algorithms are natural consequences of the theory and are clearly presented.  
- Main weakness: empirical evaluation is limited to a simple static financial portfolio example.  
- The proposed dynamic actor-critic algorithm is not empirically validated; scalability and sample efficiency remain unevaluated.  
- Clarity is excellent: well-written, well-organized, and places contributions in context.  
- Originality: a novel synthesis extending dual coherent-risk representations to policy gradients.  
- Significance: high practical importance for deploying RL under worst-case and variability concerns (finance, autonomous systems, etc.).  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — theoretical contribution is substantial and outweighs limited experiments.
