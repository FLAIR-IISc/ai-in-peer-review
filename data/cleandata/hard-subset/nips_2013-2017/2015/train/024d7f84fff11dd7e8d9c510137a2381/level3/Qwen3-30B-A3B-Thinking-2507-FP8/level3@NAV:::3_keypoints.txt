- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, unifying prior work on specific risks like CVaR and mean-variance.
- Derives gradient formulas for both static and time-consistent dynamic coherent risk measures, generalizing previous results.
- Maintains computational tractability: static case combines sampling with convex programming.
- Dynamic case uses an actor-critic framework with explicit value function approximation.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, accessible to the RL community.
- Technical derivations appear correct and theoretically well-grounded.
- Clarifies that tractability issues arise from policy parametrization rather than Markov risk measures themselves.
- Numerical trading example effectively shows different risk measures lead to different policy choices.
- Minor issues: confusing notation "ξP_θ" in Theorem 2.1 should match Theorem 6.6 of [26], and a typo on line 141 ("risk enevlop[e]").
- Contribution is valuable and practical though not revolutionary; overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
