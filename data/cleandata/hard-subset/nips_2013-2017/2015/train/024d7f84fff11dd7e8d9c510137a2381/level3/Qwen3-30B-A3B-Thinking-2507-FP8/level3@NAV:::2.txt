

This paper presents a significant extension to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning by developing policy gradient methods for the entire class of coherent risk measures, rather than focusing on specific measures like CVaR or variance as previous work has done. The authors derive a unified framework that encompasses both static and dynamic coherent risk measures, providing novel gradient formulas that enable sampling-based estimation and actor-critic algorithms. Their approach naturally extends prior work while offering a more general theoretical foundation.

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with rigorous derivations, sound theoretical results (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2), and a clear connection to existing literature on coherent risk measures. The experiments, while simple, effectively illustrate the risk-averse behavior and flexibility of their approach across different risk types using a toy finance example with three assets having distinct return distributions. The clarity of writing is above average, with well-structured presentation and comprehensive references.

The originality is substantial, as the extension to the general class of coherent risk measures represents a nontrivial contribution that unifies previous specialized approaches. The significance is high, addressing an important problem in risk-sensitive RL with meaningful contributions that advance the state of the art. The paper carefully acknowledges limitations and builds logically on prior work.

Strengths: Unified theoretical framework, technically sound contributions, clear exposition, strong experimental validation of core concepts. Weaknesses: Experiments are relatively simple and static, though this is understandable given the focus on theoretical foundations. The paper would benefit from more discussion of computational complexity and scalability to larger problems.

The work is well-positioned to influence future research in risk-sensitive RL, providing a principled approach that connects risk management with reinforcement learning. It meets all four quality criteria with exceptional clarity, significant contribution, and technical soundness. This paper represents a high-quality scientific contribution that would be valuable to the NIPS community.
