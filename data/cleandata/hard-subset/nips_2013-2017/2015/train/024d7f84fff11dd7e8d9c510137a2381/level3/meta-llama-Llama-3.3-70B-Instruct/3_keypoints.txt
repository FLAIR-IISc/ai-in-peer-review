- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, unifying static and dynamic risk-sensitive RL.  
- Well-organized, well-written paper with correct results and clear exposition of coherent risk measures and their relevance.  
- Thorough related-work review that highlights limitations of prior studies focused on specific risk measures.  
- Main technical contribution: new policy-gradient-style formulas that combine sampling with convex programming to estimate gradients for static and dynamic coherent risks.  
- Demonstrates the approach on examples including CVaR and mean-semideviation.  
- Provides a numerical illustration emphasizing flexibility in designing risk criteria aligned with user preferences and problem properties.  
- Strength: clear introduction to coherent risk measures and their importance.  
- Strength: comprehensive review of prior work and gaps.  
- Strength: novel gradient estimation formulas integrating sampling and convex programming.  
- Strength: illustrative numerical experiments.  
- Weakness: assumes the risk envelope is available in a canonical convex-programming form, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms.  
- Weakness: no empirical comparison with other risk-sensitive RL methods.
