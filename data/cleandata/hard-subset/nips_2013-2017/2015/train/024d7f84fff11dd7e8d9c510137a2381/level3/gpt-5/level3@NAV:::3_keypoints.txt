- Contribution: Extends policy-gradient methods to all coherent risk measures for both static and time-consistent Markov coherent risks, unifying prior CVaR/mean-variance work.  
- Static result: Derives a gradient formula via the Ruszczyński–Shapiro risk-envelope dual and the envelope theorem, yielding a likelihood-ratio estimator combined with SAA + KKT multipliers.  
- Dynamic result: Provides a policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk using robust-MDP equivalence and proposes an actor–critic style algorithm.  
- Technical soundness: Development appears correct; static derivation is clean and dynamic extension follows naturally with standard assumptions (bounded likelihood ratios, compact envelopes).  
- Empirics: Numerical illustration is minimal but coherent; SAA consistency claim is plausible.  
- Clarity: Paper is well organized and readable, though Theorem 2.1’s “ξ Pθ” notation is confusing and a minor typo on line 141 exists.  
- Originality: Modest but meaningful generalization that lifts CVaR/mean-semideviation results to general coherent risk in a unified way.  
- Significance: Useful unification for practitioners and a practical SAA/KKT recipe, but impact limited by incremental novelty and sparse experiments.  
- Suggestion: Clarify that tractability issues arise from policy parametrization/sampling rather than Markov coherent risks themselves, and note DP tractability per [24].  
- Suggestion: Move empirical SAA vs analytical CVaR comparisons from supplement to main text.  
- Suggestion: Discuss computational cost (per-iteration convex program, KKT extraction), sample complexity, and when analytical envelopes (e.g., spectral risks) avoid optimization.  
- Pros: Clear, correct, unifying treatment; general gradient forms; practical sampling + convex-program recipe.  
- Cons: Modest novelty, limited experiments, notational confusion, minor typos, and requires multi-sample simulators plus convex solves per batch.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
