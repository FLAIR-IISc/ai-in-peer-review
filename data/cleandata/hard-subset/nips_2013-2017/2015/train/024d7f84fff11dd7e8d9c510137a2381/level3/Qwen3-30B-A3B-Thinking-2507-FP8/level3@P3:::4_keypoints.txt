- Presents a unified policy-gradient framework for risk-sensitive RL covering the full class of coherent risk measures using their dual representation.  
- Extends prior work by handling both static (e.g., CVaR of discounted cost) and dynamic (Markov coherent) risk settings, generalizing many recent specific-measure approaches.  
- Theoretical contribution: unifies and extends gradient methods to most well-known coherent risk measures (CVaR, mean-semideviation, spectral).  
- Uses the dual/convex-hull representation of coherent risks to derive gradient estimation algorithms.  
- Provides clear, well-organized exposition and mathematically transparent derivations, accessible to non-experts.  
- Develops practical gradient estimation algorithms combining sampling with convex programming, including a general algorithm (Section 4.3).  
- Example sections effectively show the framework recovers and simplifies prior results (e.g., CVaR).  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; deeper experiments comparing to existing methods are needed.  
- Preliminaries unclear: the roles and dependence of P_θ and ξ (risk envelope) need explicit clarification.  
- Technical clarity issue: relationship between Equation 2 and Theorem 2.1 (claimed restrictiveness) is not well explained.  
- Theorem/proof gap: smoothness of constraint functions is referenced but not included in theorem assumptions; link to M-Lipschitz Assumption 2.2 needs elaboration.  
- Does not address extension to continuous distributions, limiting applicability to some real-world problems.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original theoretical paper with significant unifying contribution and clear writing; main weaknesses are limited empirical work and some technical clarifications.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
