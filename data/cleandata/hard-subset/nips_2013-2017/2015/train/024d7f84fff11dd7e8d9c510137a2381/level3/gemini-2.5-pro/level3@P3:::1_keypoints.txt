- Proposes a unified framework to compute policy gradients for the full class of coherent risk measures, generalizing prior risk-sensitive RL work.  
- Core contribution: a new gradient formula for static coherent risks derived from their dual worst-case expectation representation.  
- Framework yields analytic gradients for some measures (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) and a general sampling-based algorithm when no closed form exists.  
- Provides a consistency proof for the sampling-based estimator and a clear derivation of the main gradient result (Theorem 4.2, Proposition 4.4).  
- Extends results to the dynamic setting with a policy gradient theorem for time-consistent Markov coherent dynamic risk measures, enabling actor-critic development.  
- Strength: original, elegant, and potentially high-impact approach that unifies and simplifies many prior disparate results.  
- Weakness: limited empirical validation — the general sampling algorithm is not evaluated in the main text or compared in detail to analytical gradients.  
- Weakness: overly ambitious scope reduces depth and clarity; the dynamic-risk section is high-level and lacks algorithms, pseudocode, and experiments.  
- Weakness: analytical solutions rely on closed-form saddle points, which many coherent risks lack, limiting the practical reach of the analytic results.  
- Overall: theoretically sound and promising, but practical significance is currently constrained by missing experiments and incomplete treatment of dynamic methods.
