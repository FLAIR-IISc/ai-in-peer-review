- Proposes a unified framework for policy gradient algorithms for reinforcement learning with objectives defined by coherent risk measures (static and dynamic).
- Extends prior risk-sensitive RL methods (e.g., variance-based, CVaR, robust MDPs) to the broader class of coherent risks using coherent risk theory.
- Key technical contribution: a novel formula for static coherent risk gradients allowing analytical derivations for some measures and a sampling-based algorithm for others with proven consistency.
- Provides policy gradient expressions for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- Theorems 4.2 and 5.2 and related theoretical derivations appear sound and well-connected to stochastic programming and robust optimization.
- Strengths: theoretical completeness, unified derivation across many risk measures, rigorous mathematics, and clear writing.
- Major weakness: complete lack of comprehensive empirical validation—only a limited numerical example without quantitative comparisons or scalability assessment.
- Dynamic-risk section is high-level, lacks algorithmic details and experimental validation, leaving practicality unclear.
- Paper’s scope is potentially too ambitious for the page limit, causing clarity issues and incomplete presentation.
- Recommendation: split focus (static with full experiments or fully develop dynamic algorithm) and provide thorough empirical evaluation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically solid, useful extensions of policy gradient methods, rigorous derivations.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experiments, limited practical demonstration of dynamic algorithms, and overly broad scope; reviewer recommends rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit.
