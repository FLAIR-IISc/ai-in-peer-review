- Extends policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures, a significant contribution to risk-sensitive RL.
- Provides a unified view of existing risk-sensitive RL methods and introduces new algorithms.
- Demonstrates sufficient originality and technical soundness in the theoretical development.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, with comprehensive references.
- Presents a toy finance-domain example showing risk-averse behavior and flexibility across risk types.
- Example underscores the importance of choosing problem-specific risk criteria to manage cost variability.
- Generalizes and extends previous results, offering a unified approach applicable to multiple applications.
- Shows practical significance by enabling optimization of risk-sensitive objectives in varied settings.
- Weakness: convergence analysis of the actor-critic algorithm is relegated to the supplementary material, which may hinder readability.
- Weakness / future work: extending the approach to single-trajectory roll-outs is noted as an open direction.
- Overall recommendation: accept — meets criteria of quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
