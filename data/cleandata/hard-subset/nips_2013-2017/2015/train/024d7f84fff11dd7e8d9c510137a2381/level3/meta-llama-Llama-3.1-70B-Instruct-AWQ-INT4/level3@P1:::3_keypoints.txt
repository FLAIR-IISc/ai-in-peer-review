- Extends policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified risk-sensitive RL framework.  
- Derives gradient formulas for both static and dynamic coherent risk measures, generalizing prior CVaR-specific and risk-neutral results.  
- Results appear correct with thorough analysis and proofs provided in the supplementary material.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow with good notation overall.  
- Main strength: clear presentation and concise introduction followed by detailed analysis.  
- Weakness: confusing notation "\xi P_\theta" in Theorem 2.1—should align with Theorem 6.6 in [26].  
- Minor typo on line 141: "risk enevlop[e]" should be "risk envelope."  
- Authors should clarify that tractability issues stem from policy parametrization, not from Markov risk measures (MDPs with Markov risk measures are tractable).  
- Overall assessment: a minor but solid and potentially widely applicable extension.  
- Numerical ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: unified approach, generalization of prior results (including CVaR), clear writing, and correct results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: only a minor extension over existing work, plus minor notation/typo and clarification issues.
