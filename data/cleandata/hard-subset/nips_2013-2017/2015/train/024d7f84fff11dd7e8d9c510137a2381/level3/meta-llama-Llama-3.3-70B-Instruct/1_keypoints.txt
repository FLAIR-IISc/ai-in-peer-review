- Proposes a novel extension of policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures.
- Derives a unified formula for the gradient of static coherent risk measures.
- Introduces a sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions.
- Develops a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures and a corresponding actor-critic algorithm.
- Main strengths: originality, technical soundness, and significance.
- Unified derivation is a valuable contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making literature.
- The sample-based approximation scheme is promising and backed by consistency guarantees.
- Clarity suffers from trying to address multiple issues in limited space, notably Section 5.
- Paper would benefit from a narrower focus or an expanded journal version with more detailed explanations and examples.
- Significance: generalizes and extends prior risk-sensitive RL results and demonstrates practical benefits via a numerical example.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, unified theory, sampling scheme with guarantees, and empirical illustration.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues, insufficient explanatory detail/examples, and potentially limited appeal beyond coherent risk measures.
- Overall recommendation: accept, subject to revisions improving clarity and providing more detailed explanations and examples.
