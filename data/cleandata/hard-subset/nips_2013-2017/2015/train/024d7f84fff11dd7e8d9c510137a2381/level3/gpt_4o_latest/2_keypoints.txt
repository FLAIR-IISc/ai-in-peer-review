- Paper extends policy-gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, covering both static and dynamic settings.  
- Proposes novel algorithms: a sampling-based method for static coherent risks and an actor-critic style algorithm for dynamic Markov coherent risks.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical derivations, including new gradient formulas and convergence guarantees.  
- Presentation is clear, well-structured, and technically sound, making complex derivations accessible.  
- Original and significant: unifies and generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL work, enabling flexible risk choices across domains.  
- Practical relevance demonstrated via a finance-domain example showing the framework captures diverse risk preferences.  
- Experimental validation is limited to a toy, static finance example, reducing empirical strength.  
- Computational scalability concerns due to reliance on convex programming and sampling for high-dimensional/continuous spaces.  
- Actor-critic algorithm for dynamic risks is only briefly outlined; key implementation and convergence details are deferred to supplementary material.  
- Recommendation: accept — theoretical contributions, originality, and clarity outweigh empirical and scalability limitations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial theoretical advance, unified framework, and clear technical presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, scalability issues, and insufficient algorithmic detail for the actor-critic method.
