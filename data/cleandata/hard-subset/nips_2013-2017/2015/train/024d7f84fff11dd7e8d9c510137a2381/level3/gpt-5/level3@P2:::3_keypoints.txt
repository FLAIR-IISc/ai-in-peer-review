- Paper studies risk-averse RL for static coherent risks and time-consistent Markov coherent dynamic risks, using dual representations.
- Derives a general policy-gradient formula for static coherent risk measures and sampling-based estimators that recover the risk-envelope saddle point via a small convex program.
- Proves a policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures and proposes an actor-critic style algorithm using robust/ξ-reweighted dynamics.
- Results unify and generalize prior specific-risk work (CVaR, mean-variance, exponential utility) and connect to robust MDP literature.
- Main derivations appear correct and rely on standard, sound tools; assumptions (finite spaces, Slater, bounded scores) are clear and reasonable.
- Static estimator includes consistency via SAA and KKT multiplier analysis; dynamic theorem is motivated by risk-sensitive Bellman equation and robust-MDP equivalence.
- Empirical validation is limited to a static toy trading example; no dynamic experiments in the main text, weakening practical evidence.
- Paper is well-written and organized; intuition on coherent risk and time consistency is clearly conveyed.
- Notational issue: “ξ Pθ” in Theorem 2.1 is confusing and should be replaced with explicit densities ξ ∈ U(Pθ) and Eξ[Z] notation.
- Minor typo: “risk enevlop[e]” on line 141 needs correction.
- Clarify that tractability challenges stem from policy parametrization and large state spaces; Markov coherent risk DP is tractable for small MDPs.
- Conceptual novelty is incremental — generalizing known duality + envelope + likelihood-ratio ideas — but the dynamic policy-gradient extension is a neat generalization.
- The unified framework and combining sampling with an inner convex problem to recover ξ and multipliers is useful and broadly applicable.
- Practical impact tempered by costs: per-state inner convex solves and KKT multipliers, need for simulator to sample next states, and limited experiments.
- Arguments for acceptance: clear, correct, unified treatment; generalizes prior results; provides consistency guarantees and a new dynamic theorem; well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, minimal experimental validation, and practicality concerns for large-scale settings.
- Detailed suggestions: fix Theorem 2.1 notation, correct typo, clarify intractability claims, provide complexity estimates for the inner convex program, and give practical guidance for common envelopes.
- Implementation questions to address: how to compute ξ* and KKT multipliers per state with function approximation, stability with approximate Vθ, and sensitivity to envelope gradients.
- Suggest discussing variance reduction (baselines/advantages) applicability to the dynamic gradient and whether control variates can be used bias-free.
- Request at least one dynamic benchmark (even in supplement) to demonstrate actor-critic with Markov coherent risk on a moderate MDP.
- Questions for authors: scalability when envelopes have many constraints and heuristics to amortize inner solves; possibility of model-free single-trajectory methods via importance sampling; other envelopes with closed-form saddle points.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — minor but solid and useful extension with correct theory and clear exposition, though empirical support and large-scale practicality need work.
