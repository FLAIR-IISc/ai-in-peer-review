- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified generalization of prior risk-sensitive RL work.  
- Paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow with a clear problem introduction and related-work review.  
- Derives a new gradient formula for static coherent risk measures that is amenable to sampling-based approximation.  
- Presents an algorithm for gradients of general static coherent risk measures using sampling plus convex programming, with a consistency result.  
- Derives a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures, linking the gradient to a value function and proposing an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Provides numerical experiments demonstrating the method’s flexibility, ability to train risk-averse policies, and handling of heavy-tailed distributions.  
- Technical contributions are substantial and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Paper is technically sound and authors transparently discuss strengths and weaknesses.  
- Originality: novel combination of techniques extending policy gradient to all coherent risk measures.  
- Significance: results are important and potentially impactful for finance, operations research, and RL.
