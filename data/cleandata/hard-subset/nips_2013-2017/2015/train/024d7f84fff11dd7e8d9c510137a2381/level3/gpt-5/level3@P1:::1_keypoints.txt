- Develops policy-gradient methods for the full class of coherent risk measures in static and time-consistent dynamic settings.  
- Uses dual representation of coherent risks plus the envelope theorem to derive a general gradient formula for static coherent risks.  
- Provides closed-form gradient identities for CVaR and mean-semideviation and generalizes prior results to other risks (e.g., spectral measures).  
- Proposes a sampling-plus-convex-programming (SAA) estimator for gradients with consistency guarantees when analytic saddle points are unavailable.  
- Proves a policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent (time-consistent) risk measures, expressing gradients as expectations under a ξ-weighted Markov chain.  
- Derives an actor-critic style algorithm for dynamic risk via equivalence to robust MDPs.  
- Conceptually unifies many prior risk-sensitive PG methods (mean-variance, CVaR, exponential utility) under one framework.  
- Positions contributions against prior work that focused on specific risks; claims broader scope and novelty.  

Strengths:
- Elegant, general envelope-theorem-based gradient identity that subsumes known results and enables new cases.  
- Concrete estimation pathway (SAA + convex programming) with technically sound consistency under mild conditions.  
- Dynamic extension clarifies ξ-weighted dynamics and provides a promising actor-critic outline.  
- High potential impact by letting practitioners choose among a wider set of risk measures.  

Weaknesses:
- Very limited empirical validation: only a small static example and no dynamic experiments, so practical viability and sample complexity are unclear.  
- Missing algorithmic detail: dynamic actor-critic and nontrivial implementation steps deferred to supplement, reducing implementability.  
- Scalability concerns from repeatedly solving inner convex programs (state-wise) without guidance on solvers, sizes, or warm-starts.  
- Assumptions (finite outcome spaces, smooth constraints) limit demonstrated scope; extensions to continuous spaces are not empirically shown.  

Quality:
- Theoretical development appears sound and aligns with robust MDP interpretations.  
- Lack of experimental stress-testing leaves open questions about robustness, variance, and function-approximation bias.  

Clarity:
- Generally well written but attempts too much in limited space; dynamic section is too terse and not actionable.  

Originality:
- High — moving from specific risk measures to coherent class and deriving a dynamic PG theorem is novel.  

Significance:
- Potentially high if the SAA-plus-convex-program approach proves efficient and stable in practice.  

Suggested improvements:
- Add pseudocode for static SAA gradient and dynamic actor-critic, including saddle-point reuse and ξ-weighted sampling procedures.  
- Empirically compare analytic vs SAA gradients (variance/accuracy vs sample size) and report timing for convex subproblems.  
- Include dynamic benchmark experiments and comparisons to risk-neutral, mean-variance, and CVaR baselines.  
- Discuss variance reduction, importance sampling, and sensitivity to choice of coherent risk.  
- Clarify assumptions and demonstrate extensions to continuous state/action problems.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Unified, general theoretical framework subsuming many risk measures.  
- New dynamic PG theorem with principled actor-critic pathway.  
- Conceptually significant bridge between coherent risk theory and RL.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Insufficient experimental evidence and missing algorithmic details to assess practicality and scalability.  
- Over-reliance on supplemental material for key validation.  
- Potential implementation complexity not addressed empirically.  

Overall recommendation:
- Borderline reject: strong theoretical contributions but needs substantially more empirical validation and implementation detail for acceptance.
