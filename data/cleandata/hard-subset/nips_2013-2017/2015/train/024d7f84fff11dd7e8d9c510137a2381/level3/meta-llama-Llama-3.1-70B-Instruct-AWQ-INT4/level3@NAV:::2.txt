This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) by extending the policy gradient method to the whole class of coherent risk measures. The authors provide a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL, which generalizes and extends previous results.

The paper is well-written, and the equations are technically sound. The authors have done an excellent job of relating their work to previous studies in the field, and the references provided are relevant and up-to-date. The clarity of the paper is above average, making it easier for readers to follow the authors' arguments.

The originality of the paper is sufficient, as the extension to coherent risk measures is non-trivial and provides a new perspective on risk-sensitive RL. The significance of the paper is high, as it addresses an important problem in RL and presents clear, meaningful contributions to the field.

The authors demonstrate the approach on a toy finance example, which shows the algorithm's risk-averse behavior and flexibility across different risk types. While the experiments are simple and static, they effectively illustrate the potential of the proposed method.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL. The authors achieve this by proposing concrete algorithms (policy gradient, actor-critic) derived naturally from prior work and the problem formulation. The paper also presents a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk, which is convenient for approximation using sampling.

The paper's weaknesses are minor. One potential criticism is that the experiments are limited to a simple finance example, and it would be beneficial to see the approach applied to more complex and dynamic environments. Additionally, the paper assumes that the risk envelope is known in an explicit form, which may not always be the case in practice.
