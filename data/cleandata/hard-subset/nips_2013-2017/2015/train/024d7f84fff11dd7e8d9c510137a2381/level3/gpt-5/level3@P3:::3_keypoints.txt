- Develops policy-gradient methods for coherent risk objectives, covering static and time-consistent dynamic (Markov coherent) risks.
- Static case: uses dual (risk-envelope) representation and envelope theorem to derive likelihood-ratio style gradient depending on saddle-point densities and KKT multipliers.
- Proposes an SAA scheme to estimate the envelope and multipliers from samples when closed-form saddle points are unavailable.
- Dynamic case: leverages Bellman equation for Markov coherent risk to derive a policy-gradient theorem that reweights transitions and modifies stage costs, yielding an actor–critic algorithm with a robust-MDP-style critic.
- Framework generalizes prior CVaR- and mean-variance-specific PG results to the full class of coherent risks (CVaR, spectral, mean-semi-deviation, etc.).
- Conceptually simple extension of risk-neutral PG to worst-case envelope reweightings, providing a unifying estimator/algorithmic template.
- Static gradient (Theorem 4.2) and SAA consistency (Proposition 4.4) are technically sound and reproduce known CVaR results.
- Dynamic PG theorem (Theorem 5.2) aligns with robust-MDP interpretation and mirrors classical policy-gradient structure.
- Assumptions (finite spaces for exposition, differentiability of envelope constraints) are explicit and extendable to Lp spaces.
- Practical costs: each gradient step requires solving a convex program and obtaining KKT multipliers; dynamic method needs ξ-weighted sampling and value approximation.
- No variance reduction or sample-complexity analysis beyond LLN-style guarantees; importance sampling noted as future work.
- Experiments are small-scale and illustrative; broader empirical validation would strengthen the paper.
- Writing is clear and well organized; dual-representation and robust-MDP connection aid readability.
- Notation issue: use of “ξ Pθ” is confusing; recommend representing envelope as a set of probability vectors q ∈ Q(Pθ) to avoid overloading ξ.
- Minor issues: typo (“risk enevlop[e]”), clarity needed on tractability statement regarding tabular/model-known DP versus RL sampling/parametrization challenges.
- Suggested additions: algorithm box for SAA static gradient, high-level pseudocode for actor–critic, and reiteration of assumptions enabling ξ-weighted sampling.
- Novelty is modest (generalizing CVaR derivations) but nontrivial to execute and broadly useful; main novelty is unification and dynamic PG theorem.
- Significance: provides a reusable template for many coherent risks and links risk-sensitive RL to robust MDPs; likely to influence future work.
- Pros: unified treatment, generalization of special cases, dynamic PG theorem and actor–critic recipe, clear presentation and explicit assumptions.
- Cons: computational overhead (convex solves, KKT access, weighted sampling), limited empirical validation, and some notational/clarity issues.
- Specific actionable suggestions: change Theorem 2.1 notation to q ∈ Q(Pθ), fix typo, clarify DP tractability in Sec.3, discuss variance-reduction/importance-sampling, and note how to retrieve KKT multipliers and regularization strategies.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, minor revisions recommended (notation, typo, tractability clarification); recommend acceptance.
