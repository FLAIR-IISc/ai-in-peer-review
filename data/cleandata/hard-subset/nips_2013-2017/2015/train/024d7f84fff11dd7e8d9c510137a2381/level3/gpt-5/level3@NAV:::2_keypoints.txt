- Develops policy-gradient estimators for risk-sensitive RL over the full class of coherent risk measures (static and time-consistent dynamic).
- Core technique: uses the dual (risk-envelope) representation and the envelope theorem to derive a general static-risk gradient formula.
- Provides concrete instantiations (CVaR, mean-semideviation) and a sample-average approximation coupling Monte Carlo with a small convex program.
- For Markov coherent (dynamic) risk, proves a policy-gradient theorem linking the gradient to a risk-sensitive Bellman value function.
- Proposes an actor-critic algorithm that leverages robust-MDP structure to estimate values and samples under ξ-weighted transitions to estimate gradients.
- Includes a toy finance example showing different coherent risks induce distinct, interpretable behaviors.
- Theoretical derivations are technically sound, grounded in convex analysis, and include consistency results for the SAA estimator.
- Recovers known special cases (validates framework) and unifies/extends prior CVaR and risk-sensitive RL work.
- Main empirical weakness: dynamic algorithm lacks experiments; toy example is small and static, leaving sample-efficiency and stability questions unanswered.
- Practicality concerns: per-update convex subproblems and saddle-point multipliers may introduce computational overhead; finite-space and simulator assumptions limit applicability.
- Clarity is high, but reviewers request discussion of saddle-point (non)uniqueness and its effect on estimator variance.
- Ask for per-step computational complexity of the convex program and procedure to retrieve KKT multipliers.
- Need clarification on assumptions and practical mechanisms for sampling from ξ-weighted transitions.
- Originality: substantial and nontrivial extension from specific measures to the coherent-risk class.
- Significance: high — broadly useful for flexible risk modeling beyond expectation/CVaR.
- Suggested experiments: add dynamic benchmarks (gridworld/control), test multiple coherent risks (e.g., spectral), report variance and wall-clock, compare to CVaR actor-critic and mean-variance methods, and study sensitivity to envelope choices and function-approximation error.
