This paper extends policy-gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, treating both static risks and time-consistent Markov coherent risks. The static case derives a gradient formula via the risk-envelope dual representation (Ruszczyński–Shapiro) and the envelope theorem, yielding a likelihood-ratio estimator combined with a convex program (SAA + KKT multipliers). The dynamic case provides a policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk, leveraging the robust-MDP equivalence and an actor–critic style algorithm. The results subsume and generalize prior work focused on particular risks (e.g., CVaR and mean-variance) and connect to risk-sensitive actor–critic (e.g., Prashanth & Ghavamzadeh), robust MDPs (Petrik & Subramanian), and dynamic coherent risk DP (Ruszczyński).

Quality: The technical development appears sound; the static gradient follows cleanly from duality and the envelope theorem, and the dynamic result is a natural extension of the standard policy gradient theorem under ξ-weighted transitions. Assumptions are standard (bounded likelihood ratios, compact envelopes), and the SAA consistency claim is plausible. The numerical illustration is minimal but coherent.

Clarity: The paper is well organized and readable. Notational nit: in Theorem 2.1, the expression “ξ Pθ” is confusing; I recommend adopting the notation of Theorem 6.6 in [26], e.g., write Q ∈ U(Pθ) with dQ/dPθ = ξ, or keep ξ as the RN derivative and write expectations with respect to Q explicitly. Minor typo on line 141: “risk enevlop[e]”.

Originality: The contribution is a minor but meaningful generalization: it lifts CVaR-specific and mean-semideviation policy gradients to any coherent risk via a simple and unified extension of risk-neutral PG. The dynamic theorem similarly extends familiar machinery to Markov coherent risk.

Significance: Despite being incremental, the unification is useful for practitioners seeking risk-sensitive RL beyond CVaR, and the SAA/KKT recipe is practical. Impact would be strengthened by more empirical validation.

Specific suggestions:
- Clarify that tractability challenges primarily stem from policy parametrization and sampling, not from Markov coherent risk measures per se; MDPs with Markov risk measures are tractable via DP ([24]).
- Move the empirical comparison between SAA and analytical CVaR estimators from the supplement into the main text.
- Discuss computational cost (per-iteration convex program, KKT extraction) and sample complexity, and when analytical envelopes (e.g., spectral risks) avoid optimization.

Pros:
- Clear, correct, and unifying treatment.
- General gradient forms for static and dynamic coherent risks.
- Useful practical recipe (sampling + convex programming).

Cons:
- Modest novelty; limited experiments.
- Notational confusion in Theorem 2.1; minor typos.
- Requires multi-sample simulators and solves convex programs per batch.

Recommendation: Weak accept.
