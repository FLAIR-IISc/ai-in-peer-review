- Presents a principled, general framework extending policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures.
- Derives a new gradient formula for static coherent risk measures using the dual representation and the Envelope Theorem (Theorem 4.2).
- Proposes a general policy gradient algorithm that combines sampling with convex programming for static risks.
- Extends the approach to dynamic, time-consistent Markov coherent risk measures and derives a policy gradient theorem for this case.
- Proposes an actor-critic algorithm for the dynamic risk setting.
- Unifies and generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL results, subsuming special cases like CVaR and variance.
- Theoretical contributions are technically sound, well-founded, and elegantly derived, with clear assumptions (e.g., risk envelope, simulator access).
- Mathematical exposition is precise, well-organized, and supported by illustrative examples that recover known results.
- Novelty lies in the unified treatment and the nontrivial combination of dual representation with the Envelope Theorem.
- High significance: provides a flexible, principled toolkit for risk-aware policy optimization likely to influence future work.
- Main weakness: experimental validation is limited to a simple static multi-asset trading problem.
- Significant gap: the dynamic/actor-critic algorithm is not empirically tested on sequential decision-making benchmarks.
- Overall judgment: theoretical strengths and originality far outweigh limited experiments; the paper is a substantial contribution to risk-sensitive RL.
