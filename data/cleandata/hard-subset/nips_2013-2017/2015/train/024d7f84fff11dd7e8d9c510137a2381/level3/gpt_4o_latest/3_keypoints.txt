- Paper extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures (static and dynamic), generalizing prior CVaR-focused work.  
- Proposes new gradient formulas for static and dynamic coherent risk measures.  
- Uses sampling combined with convex programming for static risk measures.  
- Uses an actor-critic approach for dynamic coherent risk measures.  
- Provides a unified, flexible framework for managing cost variability with potential applications in finance and operations research.  
- Strength: technically rigorous with well-supported theoretical derivations.  
- Strength: paper is clear, well-organized, and provides sufficient background.  
- Strength: original generalization beyond specific measures like CVaR.  
- Strength: practically significant and likely to inspire further research and applications.  
- Weakness: notation in Theorem 2.1 is confusing and should align with established references (e.g., Theorem 6.6 in [26]).  
- Weakness: should clarify that intractability arises from policy parameterization, not the risk measure itself.  
- Weakness: minor typo noted (Line 141: "risk enevlop[e]").  
- Weakness: contribution is somewhat incremental over CVaR work and needs more empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to fix notation, clarify intractability, and correct typos.
