- Presents a unified framework for deriving policy gradients for the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Core contribution: novel gradient formula for static coherent risk measures derived from their dual representation.  
- Enables analytical derivation of policy gradients for specific measures and a general sampling-based algorithm when analytics are intractable.  
- Extends framework to dynamic, time-consistent Markov coherent risk measures.  
- Strength: original and significant contribution that generalizes and simplifies prior piecemeal results (e.g., CVaR).  
- Strength: elegant, principled derivation with strong theoretical quality.  
- Strength: provides a sampling-based gradient estimator and proves its consistency.  
- Weakness: major lack of empirical validation, weakening assessment of practical value.  
- Weakness: proposed sample-based algorithm is not experimentally tested.  
- Suggested experiment: compare sample-based estimator to analytical CVaR gradient to assess performance and sample efficiency.  
- Weakness: clarity suffers from ambitious scope and dense presentation within eight pages.  
- Weakness: dynamic risk section is high-level and lacks algorithmic detail and experiments, feeling preliminary.  
- Recommendation: consider focusing on static risk to allow fuller presentation and detailed empirical evaluation.  
- Conclusion: theoretically promising work but incomplete without comprehensive experiments.
