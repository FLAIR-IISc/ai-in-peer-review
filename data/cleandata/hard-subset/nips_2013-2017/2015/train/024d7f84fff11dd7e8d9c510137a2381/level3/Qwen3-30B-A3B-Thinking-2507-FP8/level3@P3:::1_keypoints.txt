- Develops a unified policy-gradient framework for the entire class of coherent risk measures, extending prior CVaR/mean-variance work.  
- Main contributions: new static coherent-risk gradient formula, a sampling-based algorithm with consistency proof, and policy-gradient expressions for Markov coherent dynamic risk.  
- Builds on Artzner et al. coherent-risk theory and connects to dynamic risk and robust MDP literature.  
- Strength: novel unified theoretical framework that subsumes prior special-case results.  
- Strength: elegant analytic derivations for static risk measures that simplify previous complex results.  
- Strength: sampling-based algorithm with a rigorous consistency proof for cases lacking analytic solutions.  
- Strength: clear examples and good linkage to established risk-theory concepts.  
- Weakness: paper is overly ambitious for 8 pages; dynamic risk section is high-level and lacks algorithmic detail.  
- Weakness: many risk measures lack closed-form Lagrangian saddle-point solutions, and the paper does not adequately address this limitation.  
- Weakness: sampling-based approximation lacks empirical validation and no experimental comparison with analytical gradients is provided.  
- Weakness: numerical illustration is insufficient and does not validate the gradient-estimation algorithms.  
- Quality: theoretical contributions and proofs are sound, but practical quality is limited by missing experiments.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but tries to cover too much; dynamic risk methods need more implementation detail—consider splitting into two papers.  
- Originality: unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures is genuinely novel.  
- Significance: has strong potential impact, but lack of comprehensive empirical evaluation prevents full assessment of practical significance.  
- Overall: interesting theoretical advance but insufficient experimental support and overly broad scope for current length, so not a strong accept recommendation.
