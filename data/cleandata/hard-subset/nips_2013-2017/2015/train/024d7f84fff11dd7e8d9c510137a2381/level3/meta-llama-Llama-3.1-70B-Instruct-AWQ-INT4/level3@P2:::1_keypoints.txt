- Proposes a novel extension of policy gradient methods to the class of coherent risk measures.  
- Derives a new analytic formula for the gradient of static coherent risk measures.  
- Presents a unified framework that covers both static and dynamic risk measures.  
- Provides a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions.  
- Proves consistency of the policy gradient estimates for the sampling-based method.  
- Demonstrates applicability to common risk measures (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) and derives new gradient formulas.  
- Paper is well-written and technically organized, with clear explanations of derivations.  
- Strength: unifies previous results and generalizes risk-sensitive RL methods.  
- Limitation: no empirical validation of the sample-based approximation, especially concerning rare-event-sensitive criteria like CVaR.  
- Limitation: dynamic risk policy gradients section is high-level and lacks algorithmic details and experiments.  
- Limitation: eight-page scope attempts to cover too much, making some parts hard to follow.  
- Suggestion: consider splitting the work or focusing on static risk measures to allow deeper empirical evaluation.  
- Overall quality: technically sound with solid proofs, but incomplete empirical assessment.  
- Significance/originality: potentially significant and original contribution to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning.
