This paper presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) by extending existing methods to the general class of coherent risk measures. The authors provide a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk, an algorithm for estimating this gradient using sampling and convex programming, and a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.

The paper is well-written, and the equations are technically sound. The authors provide a clear motivation for their work, and the problem formulation and algorithms follow logically from prior work. The paper is well-organized, and the authors adequately inform the reader about the technical details of their approach.

The originality of the paper is sufficient, as the extension to coherent risk measures is non-trivial. The authors provide a general method for the whole class of coherent risk measures, which is not limited to a specific choice within that class or to particular system dynamics.

The significance of the paper is high, as it addresses an important problem in risk-sensitive RL and presents clear, meaningful contributions to the field. The authors demonstrate the approach on a toy finance example, where each asset has its own return distribution, and show that the algorithm exhibits risk-averse behavior and flexibility across different risk types.

The experiments are simple and static, but they show the potential of the approach. The authors acknowledge that the experiments are limited and that more extensive evaluations are needed to fully assess the performance of their approach.

The paper is related to previous work on risk-sensitive optimization in RL, but it provides a more general and unified approach. The authors cite relevant work and provide a clear comparison with existing methods.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive RL, and it has the potential to impact various applications, including finance and operations research.
