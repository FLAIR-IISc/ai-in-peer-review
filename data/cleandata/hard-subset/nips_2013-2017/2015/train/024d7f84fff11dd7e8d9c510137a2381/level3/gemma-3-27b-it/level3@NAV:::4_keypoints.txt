- Extends policy gradient methods to a broad class of coherent risk measures beyond special cases like variance or CVaR.
- Leverages the dual representation of coherent risk measures to derive analytic solutions and gradient estimation algorithms.
- Provides gradient formulas and sampling-based estimators (Theorem 4.2) making the approach scalable.
- Extends results to the dynamic setting with an actor-critic algorithm.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and accessible to non-experts.
- Builds effectively on robust MDP and stochastic programming literature, offering a valuable synthesis.
- Numerical example shows different risk preferences can yield divergent policies, demonstrating practical potential.
- Empirical evaluation is limited and could be more extensive.
- Needs direct comparisons (variance and computational cost) of the proposed estimator to existing methods (e.g., CVaR-specific algorithms).
- Preliminaries (Section 2) are dense; roles of Pθ and ξ require more intuitive explanation.
- Clarification needed on Equation 2 and why it is claimed to be "more restrictive" than Theorem 2.1.
- Suggestion to comment on how the analysis generalizes to continuous distributions.
- Request to state the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution and unified framework; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions and expanded experiments.
