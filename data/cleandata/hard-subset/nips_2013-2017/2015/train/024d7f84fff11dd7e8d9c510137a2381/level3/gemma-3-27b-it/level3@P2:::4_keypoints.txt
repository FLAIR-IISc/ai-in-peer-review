- Contribution: Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, unifying static and dynamic risk optimization.  
- Theoretical soundness: Builds on dual representation of coherent risk measures and uses the envelope theorem to derive rigorous policy-gradient formulas.  
- Unified framework: Provides a single framework covering both static and dynamic risk settings, simplifying analysis and connections between them.  
- Practical algorithms: Develops sampling-based gradient estimation methods applicable to large-scale problems where analytical solutions are infeasible.  
- Clarity and presentation: Well-structured, clearly written, and accessible even to readers not deeply familiar with risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Example applications: Includes useful illustrations (CVaR, mean-semideviation) that demonstrate the approach’s generality.  
- Limited empirical evaluation: Experiments are narrow (single trading example); broader benchmarks and risk-preference tests are needed to demonstrate scalability and performance.  
- Preliminaries notation: Notation around P_θ and the role of ξ is unclear and requires more intuitive explanation.  
- Eq. 2 vs Theorem 2.1: The claim that Eq. 2 is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1 needs justification—possible restatement of convex envelope should be clarified.  
- Smoothness assumption: Role of Assumption 2.2 (smoothness of constraint functions) should be explicitly tied to the proof of Theorem 4.2.  
- Continuous distributions: Paper focuses on finite probability spaces; discussion of challenges and modifications for continuous distributions is missing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, significant generalization with strong theoretical contributions and practical algorithmic pathways.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited empirical validation and minor clarity issues reduce confidence in practical impact.  
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical paper with high potential impact; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing clarity issues, discussing continuous extensions, and adding stronger empirical validation.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10, noting intricate proofs require careful verification.
