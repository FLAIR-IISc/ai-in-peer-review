- Presents a unified approach extending policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Derives gradient expressions for both static and dynamic coherent risk measures.  
- Provides a general algorithm to estimate gradients of static coherent risk measures via sampling and convex programming.  
- Develops an actor-critic style algorithm to estimate gradients for dynamic Markov coherent risk measures.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with an accessible introduction to risk-sensitive RL.  
- Thorough review of related work and clear motivation for considering coherent risk measures.  
- Numerical illustration demonstrates that different risk measures yield different optimal policies and highlights the value of risk-averse solutions.  
- Technical contributions are significant and novel, offering a general framework for risk-sensitive RL.  
- Strengths: strong technical results, clarity, thorough literature treatment, and illustrative experiments.  
- Weakness: notation in Theorem 2.1 is confusing and should align with Theorem 6.6 in [26].  
- Weakness: typo on line 141 ("risk enevlop[e]" → "risk envelope").  
- Weakness: authors should clarify that tractability issues stem from policy parametrization rather than Markov risk measures.  
- Overall assessment: a minor but solid and important extension with potential wide applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, general derivations, clear writing, comprehensive related work, and illustrative example.  
- Arguments for rejection: confusing notation, small typographical errors, and need for clarification on tractability source.
