This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) by extending the policy gradient method to the whole class of coherent risk measures. The authors provide a unified approach to risk-sensitive RL, generalizing and extending previous results that focused on specific risk measures.

The paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of risk-sensitive optimization in RL, discussing the importance of managing variability in cost, in addition to its expected outcome. They also provide a thorough review of related work, highlighting the limitations of previous approaches and the need for a more general framework.

The technical contributions of the paper are substantial. The authors derive a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk measures, which is convenient for approximation using sampling. They also provide an algorithm for the gradient of general static coherent risk measures, involving sampling with convex programming and a corresponding consistency result. Furthermore, they derive a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures, relating the gradient to a suitable value function and a corresponding actor-critic algorithm.

The authors also provide a numerical illustration of their approach, demonstrating the importance of flexibility in designing risk criteria for selecting an appropriate risk measure. They show that their approach can be used to train policies that are risk-averse and can handle heavy-tailed distributions, which are common in financial modeling.

Overall, this paper presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results in risk-sensitive RL. The authors' contributions are significant, and their approach has the potential to be widely applicable in various fields, including finance, operations research, and RL.
