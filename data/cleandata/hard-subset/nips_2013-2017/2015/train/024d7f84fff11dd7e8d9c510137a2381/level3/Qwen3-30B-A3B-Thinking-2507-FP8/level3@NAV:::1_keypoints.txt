- Presents a unified framework for policy gradient methods under coherent risk measures, extending beyond CVaR and mean-variance.  
- Derives a new formula for static coherent risk gradients that enables analytic derivation for many common risk measures.  
- Develops a general sampling-based algorithm for coherent risk gradients with consistency guarantees.  
- Provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures, generalizing prior dynamic-risk results.  
- Theorem 4.2 is a key theoretical contribution that unifies computation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures.  
- Proposition 4.4 offers a technically sound consistency proof for the sampling-based estimator.  
- Highlights a valuable conceptual connection between coherent risk measures and model uncertainty (Section 7).  
- Main weakness: lacks comprehensive empirical validation in the main paper, limiting assessment of practical value.  
- No experiments comparing analytical versus sampling-based gradients or evaluations across different risk measures (CVaR comparison relegated to supplement).  
- Dynamic risk section is underdeveloped, missing algorithmic details and experimental validation.  
- Attempting to cover both static and dynamic risks in an eight-page paper results in insufficient depth for each topic.  
- Recommendation: focus on static risk measures or split the work to allow more detailed analysis and thorough experiments.
