- Presents a unified framework for policy gradient methods under coherent risk measures, filling a gap in risk-sensitive RL.
- Leverages dual representation (convex hull of distributions) to derive gradient estimators for static and dynamic risk settings.
- Generalizes prior work on specific risk measures (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance) and grounds risk-sensitive policy optimization theoretically.
- Theorem 4.2 gives a general gradient formula that unifies disparate risk measures.
- Strong technical quality with rigorous proofs of consistency (Proposition 4.4) and connection to established risk theory (Theorem 2.1).
- Presentation is exceptionally clear and accessible for non-specialists.
- Empirical evaluation is insufficient: lacks comparisons to existing risk-sensitive methods and quantitative convergence metrics.
- Preliminaries unclear about the roles and definitions of P_θ and ξ; need more precise definitions.
- Technical concern: Equation 2 appears to be the convex envelope definition, yet authors claim it’s more restrictive than Theorem 2.1 — requires clarification.
- Needs discussion of extension to continuous distributions.
- Proof of Theorem 4.2 should explicitly state any smoothness assumptions used.
- Overall: highly original and significant theoretical contribution but limited practical assessment due to weak empirical validation and unclear technical points.
