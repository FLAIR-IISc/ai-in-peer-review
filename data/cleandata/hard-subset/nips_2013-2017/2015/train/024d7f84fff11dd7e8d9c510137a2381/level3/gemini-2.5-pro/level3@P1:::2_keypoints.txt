- Novel unified framework extending policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures.  
- Addresses both static risk (coherent risk of total discounted cost) and dynamic risk (time-consistent Markov coherent risk).  
- Derives a new policy gradient formula for the static case and proposes a sampling + convex programming algorithm.  
- Develops a policy gradient theorem and an actor-critic style algorithm for the dynamic case.  
- Uses the dual representation of coherent risks and the envelope theorem to obtain general, sampling-friendly gradient formulas (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2).  
- Framework subsumes prior specific results (e.g., mean-variance, CVaR) as special cases.  
- High technical quality with sound derivations, clear formulation, and stated assumptions (Assumptions 2.2, 4.1).  
- Provides a consistency result for the general gradient estimator (Proposition 4.4).  
- Exceptionally clear, well-organized writing with concise preliminaries and illustrative examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation).  
- Highly original: non-trivial synthesis of coherent risk theory and policy-gradient/actor-critic methods.  
- Significant contribution: enables principled, flexible risk-sensitive RL beyond standard risk measures.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a static, single-step portfolio problem and does not validate the dynamic actor-critic algorithm.  
- Lack of experiments on standard sequential RL benchmarks weakens empirical support for the dynamic setting.  
- Overall: strong theoretical and conceptual contribution that merits acceptance despite limited experimental validation.
