- Proposes a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that generalizes and extends prior work.
- Develops policy gradient algorithms for a broad class of coherent risk measures, covering static and dynamic cases.
- Leverages the dual representation of coherent risk measures as a convex hull of probability distributions; defines risk as the maximum expected value over the hull.
- Provides a new gradient formula for static coherent risk that is amenable to sampling-based approximation.
- Presents a general gradient estimation algorithm for both static and dynamic settings that combines sampling with convex programming.
- Paper is well-written and well-organized, accessible to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization.
- Includes several examples and numerical illustrations demonstrating the proposed approach.
- Offers a thorough discussion of related work and the significance of the contributions.
- Theoretical claims are technically sound and supported by analysis.
- Proofs require verification for correctness and completeness.
- Insufficient empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm to demonstrate practical effectiveness.
- Paper would benefit from more discussion of limitations and potential extensions.
- Suggested improvements: verify proofs, provide more empirical evaluations, and expand mathematical derivation details.
