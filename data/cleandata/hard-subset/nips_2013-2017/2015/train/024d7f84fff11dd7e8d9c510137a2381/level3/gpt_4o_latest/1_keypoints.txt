- Paper extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, covering static and dynamic settings.  
- Unified approach generalizes prior work focused on specific risks like variance or CVaR.  
- Derives analytical policy-gradient expressions for static coherent risk measures using dual representation and envelope theorem.  
- Proposes a sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions, with consistency guarantees for gradient estimates.  
- Introduces a policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risks and an actor-critic style algorithm for dynamic risk measures.  
- Mathematical derivations are detailed, well-structured, and theoretically rigorous.  
- Novelty and generality represent a notable advancement for risk-sensitive RL.  
- Limited empirical validation; missing experimental comparisons between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR).  
- Presentation attempts to cover both static and dynamic risks, leading to density and reduced clarity.  
- Actor-critic algorithm for dynamic risk is high-level with insufficient implementation details and convergence analysis.  
- Practical utility of the sampling-based approach is unclear without empirical evidence.  
- Reliance on Lagrangian saddle-points limits analytical solutions to specific risk measures, reducing general applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept conditionally if authors add experimental comparisons (especially CVaR), provide detailed algorithmic descriptions and convergence analysis for actor-critic, and consider narrowing scope or splitting the paper.  
- Pro: rigorous, general framework with potential high impact; Con: limited experiments, insufficient algorithmic detail for dynamic risk, and clarity issues from broad scope.
