- Introduces a unified framework for policy-gradient RL with the full class of coherent risk measures, generalizing beyond variance/CVaR.  
- Core contributions: (1) new gradient formula for static coherent risk via dual representation and envelope theorem.  
- Core contributions: (2) a sampling-based SAA + convex-programming algorithm to estimate gradients when no analytic solution exists, with a consistency proof.  
- Core contributions: (3) extension to dynamic, time-consistent Markov coherent risk measures with a policy-gradient theorem and an actor-critic algorithm sketch.  
- Strength — originality/significance: framework is general and unifies prior risk-sensitive gradient results, offering an axiomatically justified approach.  
- Strength — technical quality: derivations (Theorem 4.2), illustrative examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation), and consistency proof (Proposition 4.4) are sound and novel.  
- Strength — potential impact: gives practitioners flexible risk choices; experiments show different risk measures can yield qualitatively different, more rational policies under heavy tails.  
- Weakness — clarity/scope: paper is overly broad for eight pages; the dynamic-risk section is high-level and lacks detail, making that contribution hard to assess.  
- Weakness — lack of empirical validation: only a simple three-armed bandit experiment; no dynamic experiments and no main-paper evaluation of the SAA estimator.  
- Weakness — practicality concerns: SAA requires solving a convex problem per step and dynamic method may need multi-sample rollouts, raising computational and real-world applicability questions.  
- Suggestion: split into two papers or focus on static risk to provide detailed SAA exposition, pseudocode, and comprehensive empirical comparisons.  
- Summary pro: novel, elegant, unified theoretical framework that generalizes previous results and provides technically strong theorems.  
- Summary con: overbroad scope, superficial treatment of dynamics, and insufficient experiments leave practical performance unclear.
