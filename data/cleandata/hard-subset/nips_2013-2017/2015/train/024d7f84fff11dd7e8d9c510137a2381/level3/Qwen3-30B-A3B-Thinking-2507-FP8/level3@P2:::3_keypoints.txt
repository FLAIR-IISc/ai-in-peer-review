- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, covering both static and time-consistent dynamic cases.  
- Static-risk result: derives a gradient formula that combines sampling with convex programming.  
- Dynamic-risk result: proposes an actor-critic algorithm that explicitly approximates the value function.  
- Unifies and generalizes prior specific-risk results (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) as special cases.  
- Well-organized and clearly written, making the technical development easy to follow.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including a consistency result for the gradient estimator (Proposition 4.4).  
- Demonstrates practical relevance with numerical illustrations showing importance of choosing appropriate risk measures.  
- Technically sound with correct derivations and appropriate theoretical guarantees; authors acknowledge computational trade-offs.  
- Main conceptual contribution is theoretical unification of risk-sensitive policy gradient methods under coherent risk.  
- Notational issue: "\xi P_\theta" in Theorem 2.1 is confusing and should follow the standard notation used in [26].  
- Minor typo: "risk enevlop[e]" on line 141 needs correction.  
- Clarify that tractability concerns stem from policy parameterization, not from Markov risk measures themselves.  
- Quality assessment: meets criteria of Quality, Clarity, Originality, and Significance with rigorous analysis and practical relevance.  
- Pros for acceptance: unified framework, generalization of prior work, clear writing, practical example, theoretical guarantees.  
- Cons: perceived incremental nature by some reviewers and minor notational/typo fixes needed.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
