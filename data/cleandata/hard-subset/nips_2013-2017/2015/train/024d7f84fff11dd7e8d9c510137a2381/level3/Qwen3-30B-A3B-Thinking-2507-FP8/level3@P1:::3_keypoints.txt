- Presents a unified policy-gradient framework for the entire class of coherent risk measures instead of just specific ones like CVaR or variance.  
- Formulates both static and dynamic Markov coherent risk measures within the policy gradient setting.  
- Derives novel gradient formulas using the representation theorem for coherent risk measures, combining sampling with convex programming.  
- Provides a sampling-based estimator for static risk that mixes convex optimization and likelihood-ratio estimation.  
- Extends the policy gradient theorem to Markov coherent risk measures and proposes an actor-critic algorithm for the dynamic case.  
- Generalizes and unifies prior results, showing CVaR and mean-semideviation are special cases of the framework.  
- Technical content is sound with consistent proofs and conditions for estimator consistency (Proposition 4.4).  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate flexibility in selecting risk measures and their impact on policy choices (e.g., financial trading example).  
- Well-organized and clearly written, with logical flow from formulation to theory to experiments.  
- Originality lies in the principled unification of coherent risk measures within risk-sensitive RL, providing broader applicability.  
- Significant practical value by allowing practitioners to tailor risk preferences and linking coherent risk to model uncertainty.  
- Main limitation: extending from specific measures to the general class is relatively straightforward, though still valuable conceptually.  
- Algorithms are practical: static case uses standard convex programming; dynamic case builds on established actor-critic approaches.  
- Notation issue: confusing use of "\xi P_\theta" in Theorem 2.1 inconsistent with standard U(Pθ) notation and should be fixed.  
- Minor clarity issues: typo "risk enevlop" and need to clarify that tractability concerns stem from policy parametrization, not Markov risk measures themselves.
