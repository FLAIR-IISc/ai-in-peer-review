- Develops policy-gradient methods for risk-sensitive RL over the full class of coherent risk measures using the dual (risk-envelope) representation and an envelope theorem.  
- Static-risk result: derives a gradient identity via the Lagrangian saddle point and proposes an SAA sampling-plus-convex-program estimator with consistency guarantees.  
- Shows classic cases arise as special instances: CVaR reduces to conditional REINFORCE on tail events; derives an explicit mean-semideviation estimator.  
- Dynamic/time-consistent result: extends the policy gradient theorem to Markov coherent risk via a robust Bellman operator and proposes an actor-critic method.  
- Actor-critic design: critic estimates a robust value function with function approximation; actor requires sampling under ξ-weighted transitions induced by the risk envelope.  
- Empirical evaluation: minimal and limited to static experiments; no dynamic MDP benchmarks, no scalability or variance/sample-complexity studies.  
- Practicality concerns: needs knowledge of risk envelopes and derivatives, solving convex programs (and extracting KKT multipliers) per batch/state, and ability to sample ξ-weighted transitions.  
- Technical soundness: derivations are clean and justified; assumptions (bounded likelihood ratios, finite Ω, smooth constraints, generative model) are clearly stated.  
- Clarity: well written with clean notation, thorough connections to prior work, and clear derivation sketches with supplementary details.  
- Originality: nontrivial, elegant generalization unifying disparate risk-sensitive RL results; dynamic policy-gradient for Markov coherent risk appears novel.  
- Significance: conceptually important foundation enabling plug-in coherent risks for RL, bridging risk-sensitive control and RL; impact limited by sparse experiments.  
- Pros: unified static/dynamic framework, clear gradient formulas recovering known cases, SAA consistency guarantee, principled actor-critic, strong theoretical links to robust MDPs.  
- Cons: very limited experiments, potentially heavy per-iteration computation, reliance on generative model and ξ-sampling, finite/discrete and smoothness assumptions may limit applicability.  
- Suggestions: add pseudo-code and complexity analysis for SAA and actor-critic, detail construction of U(Pθ) for common risks, and clarify state-dependent dynamic risk handling.  
- Questions for authors: practical schemes for sampling ξ-weighted transitions (importance sampling/variance analysis), sensitivity to critic/saddle-point errors, dynamic benchmark results, and extensions to continuous spaces and neural approximators.  
- Recommendation: Accept — technically sound and broadly useful despite limited empirical validation.  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
