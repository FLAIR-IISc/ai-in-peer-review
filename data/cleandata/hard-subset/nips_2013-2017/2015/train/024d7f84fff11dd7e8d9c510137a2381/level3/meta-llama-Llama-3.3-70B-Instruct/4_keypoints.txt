- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified approach to risk-sensitive RL.  
- Uses the dual representation of coherent risk measures (convex hull of probability distributions) as the key insight for deriving optimal policy parameters and gradients.  
- Assumes M-Lipschitz continuity of the convex hull's borders to enable analytical derivations.  
- Derives a gradient estimation algorithm and analytic expressions for optimal policy parameters under the coherence assumption.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of both static and dynamic risk measures, including a new gradient formula for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Numerical illustration (Section 6) demonstrates flexibility in designing risk criteria and training risk-averse policies.  
- Empirical comparison shows benefits of coherent risk measures over alternatives like mean–standard deviation.  
- Strength: unified framework that generalizes and extends prior, measure-specific results.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation and detailed theoretical analysis.  
- Strength: numerical example supports practical relevance of the approach.  
- Weakness: M-Lipschitz continuity assumption may not hold for all coherent risk measure families.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of convergence rates for the proposed gradient estimates.  
- Weakness: numerical validation is limited to a simple example; more complex scenarios are needed.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution with strong clarity and significance (Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10).  
- Suggested future work: improve convergence analysis of gradient estimates and develop principled procedures for risk-shaping.
