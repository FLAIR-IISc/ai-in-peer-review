- Presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive RL that generalizes and extends prior results.  
- Develops a policy gradient method for a broad class of coherent risk measures.  
- Uses the dual representation of coherent risk measures (convex hull of distributions) to derive gradient estimates.  
- Derives an analytic expression for optimal policy parameters and a general gradient estimation algorithm for static and dynamic risks.  
- Provides a clear problem definition, assumptions, and thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, accessible to non-experts.  
- Numerical illustration demonstrates the importance of flexible risk criteria.  
- Unified analysis covers a broad class of well-known risk measures.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a simple example and needs deeper, more extensive experiments.  
- Preliminaries need clarification, specifically the roles and interpretation of Pθ and ξ.  
- Unclear claim that Equation 2 is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1—needs explanation.  
- Lacks detail on how the analysis generalizes to continuous distributions and extension to Lp-normed spaces.  
- Overall strong theoretical contribution with potential impact, but requires minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10.
