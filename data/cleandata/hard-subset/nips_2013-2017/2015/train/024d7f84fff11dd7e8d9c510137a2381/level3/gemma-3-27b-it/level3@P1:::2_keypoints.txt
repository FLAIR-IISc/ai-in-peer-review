- Tackles risk-sensitive RL by generalizing from variance/CVaR to the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Provides a unified, theoretically sound framework for both static and time-consistent dynamic coherent risk.  
- Core technical contribution: policy-gradient algorithms for static cases (sampling + convex programming) and an actor-critic approach for dynamic cases.  
- Paper is well-structured with a clear introduction and good positioning within existing literature.  
- Mathematical preliminaries and definitions of coherent risk measures are concisely and clearly presented.  
- Theorem 2.1 connects coherent risk with risk envelopes, enabling sampling-based gradient estimators.  
- Proposition 4.4 supplies conditions ensuring consistency of the gradient estimators.  
- Derivations of the gradient formulas are technically rigorous and logically built on the foundations.  
- Authors propose practical algorithms, thoughtfully acknowledging computational challenges.  
- Originality: significant generalization to the entire class of coherent risk measures not previously addressed in RL.  
- Potentially high impact across applications (robotics, finance, healthcare, autonomous systems).  
- Includes a numerical example showing practical implications and flexibility, but the example is simple.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to existing risk-sensitive RL baselines on standard benchmarks.  
- Computational complexity, especially for dynamic risk in large state spaces, is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Actor-critic algorithm is outlined only briefly; convergence properties and empirical performance need deeper study.  
- Discussion of model uncertainty is underdeveloped and feels tangential.  
- Assumption of known risk envelopes (Assumption 2.2) may be restrictive; methods to learn/estimate envelopes are needed.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, emphasizing expanded empirical evaluation and more discussion of computational complexity.
