- Presents a unified framework applying policy gradient methods to all coherent risk measures via their dual representation (risk envelope).
- Derives a general analytic formula for the gradient of the coherent-risk objective (Theorem 4.2).
- Proposes a practical gradient estimation algorithm combining sampling with convex programming for both static and dynamic decision problems.
- Significantly generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL work that focused on specific measures like CVaR or mean-variance.
- Strength: strong originality and significance by subsuming many prior measure-specific results into a principled framework.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical development, notably the use of the envelope theorem to obtain the main gradient result.
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing with illustrative examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation).
- Weakness: limited empirical validation; numerical illustration is insufficient to assess estimator variance, sample complexity, or performance on challenging benchmarks.
- Weakness: Section 2 could more clearly explain the roles of the base measure P_{\theta} and the re-weighting density ξ.
- Question: clarify whether Equation 2 in Assumption 2.2 is simply the convex risk envelope definition and why it’s claimed more restrictive than Theorem 2.1’s representation.
- Question: explicitly state how the smoothness of constraint functions (g_e, f_i) is used in the proof of Theorem 4.2.
- Question: add a brief comment on how the analysis extends to continuous distributions.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with high impact; recommend acceptance pending clarifications and stronger empirical support.
