- Proposes a unified approach to risk-sensitive RL that generalizes prior work for coherent risk measures.  
- Derives a new formula for the gradient of static (one-step) coherent risk.  
- Presents a sampling + convex-programming algorithm to estimate gradients for general static coherent risk.  
- Derives a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and proposes a corresponding actor-critic algorithm.  

- Strength: Provides unified analysis covering a broad class of risk measures, including most well-known ones.  
- Strength: Proposes a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static and dynamic settings.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and organized, accessible to non-experts.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical results, including derivation of optimal policy parameters under the risk measure.  

- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited and would benefit from deeper experiments.  
- Weakness: Preliminaries are unclear about the roles of Pθ and ξ and need better explanation.

- Weakness: Limited discussion on extending the analysis to continuous distributions.

- Originality: The unified approach to risk-sensitive RL is novel and important.  
- Originality: Generalizes and extends previous results in risk-sensitive optimization.
