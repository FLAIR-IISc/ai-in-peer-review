

# Review of "Policy Gradient for Coherent Risk Measures"

This paper presents a unified framework for risk-sensitive reinforcement learning by extending policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures, rather than focusing on specific risk measures like variance or CVaR as in previous work. The authors develop both static and dynamic risk-sensitive policy gradient algorithms that generalize previous approaches. For static risk, they provide a sampling-based estimator combined with convex programming, while for dynamic risk, they develop an actor-critic style algorithm that leverages the representation theorem for coherent risk measures. The paper demonstrates that previous results for specific risk measures (CVaR, mean-semideviation, etc.) are special cases of their general framework.

The paper makes a technically sound contribution to the field. The authors establish a solid theoretical foundation by connecting the representation theorem for coherent risk measures (Theorem 2.1) to policy gradient estimation. The gradient formulas derived in Theorems 4.2 and 5.2 are novel and well-motivated, with the static case building on the envelope theorem and the dynamic case extending the standard policy gradient theorem to Markov coherent risk. The consistency result for their gradient estimator (Proposition 4.4) provides strong theoretical support for their approach. The numerical example in Section 6 effectively demonstrates the practical importance of choosing appropriate risk measures for specific problems, showing how different risk criteria lead to different investment policies in a simple financial example.

The paper is well-written with clear organization and good technical exposition. The connection between the proposed framework and prior work is well-explained, and the authors provide appropriate citations to relevant literature. The mathematical presentation is rigorous, with careful definitions and assumptions. The supplementary material appears to contain the necessary proofs and additional details for verification.

The originality of the paper is significant. While risk-sensitive RL has been studied with specific risk measures (e.g., CVaR in [8, 30], mean-variance in [18, 29]), this work provides a general framework that encompasses these approaches and extends them to the entire class of coherent risk measures. This generalization is nontrivial and addresses an important gap in the literature. The paper's connection to model uncertainty through the representation duality of coherent risk measures (Section 7) provides additional conceptual value beyond the immediate technical contribution.

The significance of the work is high. Risk-sensitive RL is an important area with applications in finance, operations research, and other domains where managing variability is crucial. The proposed framework simplifies the development of risk-sensitive RL algorithms and provides decision-makers with flexibility in designing risk preferences. The paper's demonstration of how different risk criteria lead to different behaviors in the financial example highlights the practical relevance of this flexibility. The connection to model uncertainty (Section 7) suggests broader implications for handling model misspecification in dynamic decision making.

Strengths:
- Unified framework for coherent risk measures that generalizes previous approaches
- Rigorous theoretical foundation with theorems and consistency results
- Clear connection to prior work and proper citation of relevant literature
- Well-written and technically sound presentation
- Demonstrates practical importance through a meaningful numerical example
- Provides both static and dynamic risk formulations

Weaknesses:
- The numerical example is relatively simple and could be expanded to demonstrate more complex scenarios
- The computational complexity of the convex programming step in the static case is not fully addressed
- The extension to continuous state/action spaces is not explicitly discussed
- The comparison with state-of-the-art risk-sensitive RL methods could be more comprehensive

Arguments for acceptance:
- Provides a significant theoretical extension to risk-sensitive RL
- Offers a unified framework that generalizes previous specific approaches
- Demonstrates practical relevance with a clear numerical example
- Well-written with strong theoretical foundation
- Addresses an important problem in reinforcement learning with clear significance

Arguments against acceptance:
- The numerical validation is limited to a simple example
- The computational aspects of the proposed algorithms are not thoroughly analyzed

This paper makes a meaningful contribution to the field of reinforcement learning by providing a general framework for risk-sensitive optimization that encompasses previous specific approaches. The theoretical developments are sound and well-motivated, and the practical implications are clearly demonstrated. The work is of high quality, original, and significant for the field. I recommend acceptance.
