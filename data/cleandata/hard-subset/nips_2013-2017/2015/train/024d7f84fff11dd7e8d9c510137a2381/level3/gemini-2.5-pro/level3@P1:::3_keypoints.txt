Paper presents a general policy-gradient framework for risk-sensitive RL covering all coherent risk measures via their dual (risk-envelope) representation.

Static case: derives a novel policy-gradient formula using the Envelope Theorem, leading to a practical algorithm that combines sampling with convex programming to find the worst-case density (generalizes CVaR).

Dynamic case: derives a time-consistent policy-gradient theorem, yielding an actor-critic algorithm where the critic solves a robust Bellman equation for the risk-sensitive value function.

Numerical example: shows choosing different coherent risk measures can produce more rational policies under heavy-tailed distributions compared to mean-variance.

Strength — Generalization/unification: extends previous specific-risk approaches to the full class of coherent risks, providing a principled unified framework.

Strength — Technical soundness/elegance: correct, insightful derivations using duality and the Envelope Theorem; practical algorithmic pathway (sampling + convex optimization).

Strength — Clarity/organization: well-written, logically presented, and connects general theory to concrete examples like CVaR.

Weakness — Originality framing: core tools (likelihood-ratio, dual representation) are standard; novelty is primarily the synthesis and application to RL and should be acknowledged as such.

Weakness — Tractability clarification: should clarify that dynamic coherent-risk problems are tractable in tabular/known MDPs (equivalent to robust MDPs); intractability arises from large/continuous state spaces and function approximation.

Weakness — Notation: replace the unusual ξPθ notation with a standard reweighted measure notation (e.g., define Q with dQ/dPθ = ξ) for clarity.

Overall assessment: high-quality, significant and influential contribution with minor revisions suggested; strong recommendation for acceptance.
