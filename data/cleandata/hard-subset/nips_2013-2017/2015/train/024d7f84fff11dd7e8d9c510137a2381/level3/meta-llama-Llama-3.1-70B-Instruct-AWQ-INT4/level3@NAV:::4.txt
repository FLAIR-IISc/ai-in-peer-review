This paper presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning, generalizing and extending previous results. The authors develop policy gradient algorithms for a broad class of coherent risk measures, including both static and dynamic risk measures. The approach is based on the dual representation of coherent risk measures as a convex hull of probability distributions, and the risk is defined as the maximum expected value over the hull.

The paper is well-written and organized, making it accessible even to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, followed by a detailed presentation of their approach and results.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a unified analysis covering a broad class of risk measures, including most well-known ones. The authors provide a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk, which is convenient for approximation using sampling. They also develop a general gradient estimation algorithm for both static and dynamic cases, which involves sampling with convex programming.

The paper includes several examples and numerical illustrations, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of related work and the significance of their contributions.

Overall, this is a strong theoretical paper with compelling results. However, the proofs require verification, and the authors should consider providing more empirical evaluations of the proposed gradient algorithm to demonstrate its effectiveness in practice.
