- Presents a unified framework extending policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Well-motivated: choice of risk measure is problem-dependent, so a general approach is needed.  
- Main technical contribution: new gradient formula for static coherent risk enabling sampling-based estimation.  
- Develops a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk, supporting actor-critic style algorithms for dynamic (time-consistent) risk.  
- Generalizes prior work (CVaR, mean-semideviation), showing those as special cases of the framework.  
- Theoretical rigor: sound mathematical derivations using the representation theorem and consistency results for estimators.  
- Addresses time consistency challenges in dynamic settings with appropriate proofs and assumptions.  
- Experiments: simple portfolio selection task that illustrates how different risk measures yield different optimal policies.  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written, organized, and connects clearly to prior literature.  
- Originality: significant—especially the extension to Markov coherent risk and handling of dynamic/time-consistent risk.  
- Significance: high relevance to finance, operations research, and uncertainty-aware decision making; promising link to model uncertainty.  
- Positioning: well-situated within NIPS literature and cites relevant prior work thoroughly.  
- Strengths: unified generalization, rigorous theory, estimator consistency, clear exposition, and illustrative experiments.  
- Weaknesses: limited experimental scope (needs more complex benchmarks), insufficient discussion of computational complexity trade-offs, and underdeveloped practical connection to model uncertainty.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution and recommend acceptance.
