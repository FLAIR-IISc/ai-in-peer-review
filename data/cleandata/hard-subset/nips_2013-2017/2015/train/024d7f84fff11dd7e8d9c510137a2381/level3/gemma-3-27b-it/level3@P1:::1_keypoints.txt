- Proposes a novel unified approach extending policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures, covering static and time-consistent dynamic cases.  
- Main contribution: risk-sensitive policy gradients within a coherent risk framework, including a static risk gradient formula and a Markov coherent risk policy gradient theorem.  
- Introduces a convex-programming-based algorithm and an actor-critic method for Markov coherent risk.  
- Generalizes prior work limited to specific measures (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance) to a broader, theoretically grounded class.  
- Leverages Artzner et al. (1997) coherence properties and connects clearly to robust MDP and stochastic programming literature.  
- Derives a new sampling-friendly gradient formula for static coherent risk and re-derives CVaR and mean-semideviation results as validation.  
- Proposes a sample average approximation (SAA) algorithm for cases without analytical solutions, with Proposition 4.4 giving estimator consistency conditions.  
- Major weakness: insufficient experimental validation — only a numerical example that illustrates policy differences but does not evaluate performance or scalability.  
- Lacks empirical comparison of sampling-based gradient estimates to analytical solutions (e.g., CVaR) to assess SAA accuracy and efficiency.  
- Treatment of dynamic risk measures is underdeveloped, with Section 5 presented at a high level and few algorithmic details.  
- Actor-critic algorithm complexity and reliance on function approximation raise practical applicability concerns that are not empirically addressed.  
- Paper attempts to cover too many facets within page limits, reducing clarity in parts; tighter focus on static or dynamic settings would help.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on improved experimental validation and more detailed exposition of dynamic-risk algorithms.
