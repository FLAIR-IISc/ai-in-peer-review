- Presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive RL by extending methods to the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Derives a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk.  
- Proposes an algorithm to estimate this gradient using sampling and convex programming.  
- Proves a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Paper is well-written, equations are technically sound, and motivation and formulation are clear.  
- Extension to coherent risk measures is non-trivial and sufficiently original.  
- Method is general across the entire class of coherent risks and not tied to specific dynamics.  
- Significance is high: addresses an important problem with meaningful contributions.  
- Demonstrated on a toy finance example showing risk-averse behavior and flexibility across risk types.  
- Experiments are simple and static; more extensive evaluations are needed.  
- Assumes the risk envelope is known explicitly, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Related work is cited and compared clearly; paper offers a more general unified approach.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address experimental limitations and the risk-envelope assumption.
