This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning by extending the policy gradient method to the whole class of coherent risk measures. The authors provide a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning, generalizing and extending previous results that focused on specific risk measures.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the concepts and notation. The results appear to be correct, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem.

The main contribution of the paper is the derivation of the gradient formula for both static and dynamic coherent risk measures. The authors show that their approach generalizes previous results, including the CVaR-specific results, and provides a minor but useful extension of the risk-neutral policy gradient.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity and organization. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, followed by a detailed analysis of the static and dynamic risk measures. The notation is well-defined, and the proofs are provided in the supplementary material.

However, there are a few minor issues with the paper. The notation "\xi P_\theta" in Theorem 2.1 is confusing and could be improved by using the same notation as Theorem 6.6 in [26]. Additionally, there is a typo on line 141, where "risk enevlop[e]" should be corrected to "risk envelope".

Furthermore, the authors could clarify that tractability issues arise from policy parametrization, not from Markov risk measures. MDPs with Markov risk measures are tractable, but the policy parametrization can make the problem intractable.

Overall, the paper provides a minor but solid and important extension of existing results. The authors' approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning is unified and general, and their results have the potential to be widely applicable.
