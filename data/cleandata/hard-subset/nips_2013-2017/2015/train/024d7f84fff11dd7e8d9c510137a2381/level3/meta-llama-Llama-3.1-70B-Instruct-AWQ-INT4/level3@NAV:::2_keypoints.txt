- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified framework that generalizes prior work.  
- Provides concrete algorithms (policy gradient, actor-critic) derived naturally from the formulation.  
- Introduces a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk, suitable for sampling-based approximation.  
- Paper is well-written with technically sound equations.  
- Strong relation to prior work with relevant, up-to-date references.  
- Clarity is above average, making the arguments easy to follow.  
- Originality is non-trivial; the extension to coherent risk measures offers a new perspective.  
- Significance is high, addressing an important problem in risk-sensitive RL.  
- Experiments are limited to a simple, static finance toy but effectively illustrate risk-averse behavior and method flexibility.  
- Weakness: assumes the risk envelope is known explicitly, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited and would benefit from more complex, dynamic environments.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the experimental scope and the explicit risk-envelope assumption.
