- Presents a unified extension of policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, not just CVaR or variance.  
- Derives gradient formulas for static coherent risk measures enabling sampling-based approximation.  
- Provides a policy gradient theorem for time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk measures, supporting actor-critic style algorithms.  
- Proposes a sampling-based gradient estimation algorithm that uses convex programming.  
- Theoretical foundation is rigorous, building on the representation theorem for coherent risk measures (Theorem 2.1).  
- Shows prior specific-risk results (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) are special cases of the general framework with detailed examples.  
- Numerical illustration demonstrates that choice of risk measure significantly affects policy behavior and can be counter-intuitive.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and accessible to the RL community.  
- Points out practical limitation: approach requires a simulator for sampling multiple state transitions.  
- Notation issue: "\xi P_\theta" in Theorem 2.1 is confusing; recommend using P_ξ as in Theorem 6.6 of [26].  
- Minor typo on line 141 ("risk enevlop[e]") and need to clarify that tractability issues stem from policy parameterization, not Markov risk measures themselves.  
- Originality rated moderate (natural but meaningful generalization); overall significance substantial due to practical flexibility offered.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept — solid, technically sound, minor but important extension with clear practical implications.
