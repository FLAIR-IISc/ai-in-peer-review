- Presents a general policy gradient framework for MDPs with coherent risk objectives, covering static and dynamic cases.  
- Derives policy gradient formulas for static coherent risk of total discounted cost and for time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk.  
- Generalizes prior risk-sensitive PG work (e.g., variance, CVaR) into a unified framework.  
- For static risk, combines sampling with convex programming using the dual representation of coherent risk to estimate gradients.  
- For dynamic risk, develops a risk-sensitive policy gradient theorem and an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Theoretical derivations are sound; Theorem 4.2 uses the envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio trick correctly.  
- Provides a consistency result (Proposition 4.4) for the gradient estimation algorithm, strengthening theoretical grounding.  
- Extension to time-consistent dynamic risk is well-founded and naturally extends risk-neutral results.  
- Paper includes complete contributions: theory, algorithms, and a numerical illustration.  
- Well-organized and clearly written; introduction and preliminaries effectively motivate coherent risk for RL.  
- Notation "ξPθ" is confusing; recommend standard Radon–Nikodym derivative notation or ξ(ω) for clarity.  
- Need clearer explanation that computational intractability mainly arises from large/continuous state-action spaces and function approximation, not from dynamic programming in principle.  
- Minor typo noted ("risk enevlop[e]").  
- Originality: novel and insightful synthesis of existing techniques with dual representation to create a general risk-sensitive PG algorithm.  
- Techniques used (likelihood-ratio, SAA, actor-critic) are standard, so contribution may be seen as incremental by some.  
- Significance: provides a flexible, general toolbox for designing risk objectives and avoids bespoke algorithms for each risk measure.  
- Numerical experiment effectively demonstrates the importance of choosing appropriate risk measures.  
- Recommends acceptance: technical quality, clarity, novelty, and practical importance outweigh the incremental-ness concern.
