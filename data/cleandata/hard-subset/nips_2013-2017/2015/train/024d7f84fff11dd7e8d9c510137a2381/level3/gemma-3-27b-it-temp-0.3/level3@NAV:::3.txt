## Review of "Policy Gradient for Coherent Risk Measures"

This paper presents a valuable extension of policy gradient methods to the broader class of coherent risk measures, encompassing static and dynamically consistent formulations. The authors successfully generalize existing work focused on specific risk measures like CVaR, framing their approach as a natural extension of risk-neutral policy gradient techniques. The core contribution lies in deriving gradient forms for both static and dynamic coherent risk, enabling the application of policy gradient algorithms in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning settings.

The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, making the complex theoretical concepts accessible. The notation is generally clear, and the logical flow of the derivations is easy to follow. The results appear theoretically sound, and the connection to existing literature, particularly in stochastic programming and robust MDPs, is well-established. The inclusion of a numerical example demonstrating the impact of different risk measures on policy selection is a strong point, illustrating the practical relevance of the work.

However, the notation “ξ Pθ” in Theorem 2.1 could indeed be confusing and would benefit from alignment with the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of [26], as suggested. A minor typo on line 141 ("risk enevlop[e]") should be corrected. Furthermore, while the paper acknowledges tractability issues, clarifying that these primarily stem from the policy parametrization rather than inherent limitations of Markov risk measures themselves would be beneficial. Emphasizing that MDPs *with* Markov risk measures are, in fact, tractable would strengthen this point.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Solid Theoretical Contribution:** The paper provides a mathematically rigorous extension of policy gradient methods to a broader class of risk measures.
*   **Generality & Unification:** It generalizes existing results, offering a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL.
*   **Clarity & Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The numerical example demonstrates the potential impact of different risk preferences on policy selection.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Incremental Nature:** While valuable, the extension is relatively incremental. The core ideas build directly on existing policy gradient techniques.
*   **Minor Notational/Typographical Issues:** The suggested corrections, while minor, should be addressed.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and important extension of existing results in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The paper addresses a relevant problem with a technically sound and well-presented solution. The minor weaknesses identified are easily addressed, and the strengths significantly outweigh the drawbacks. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
