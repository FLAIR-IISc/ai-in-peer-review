**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that generalizes and extends previous results. It focuses on coherent risk measures, which are widely accepted in finance and operations research, among other fields. The authors provide a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk and an algorithm for the gradient of general static coherent risk that involves sampling with convex programming. They also derive a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk, relating the gradient to a suitable value function and a corresponding actor-critic algorithm.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Unified analysis**: The paper provides a unified analysis covering a broad class of risk measures, including most well-known ones.
2. **General gradient estimation algorithm**: The authors propose a general gradient estimation algorithm for both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) cases.
3. **Well-written and organized**: The paper is well-written and organized, making it accessible even to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization.
4. **Strong theoretical results**: The paper presents strong theoretical results, including the derivation of optimal policy parameters under the risk measure.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Lack of deeper empirical evaluation**: The paper could benefit from a more extensive empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.
2. **Unclear preliminaries**: The roles of Pθ and ξ in the preliminaries section need clearer explanation.
3. **Technical clarification**: Equation 2 is claimed to be more restrictive than Theorem 2.1, but it is unclear why this is the case.
4. **Limited discussion of continuous distributions**: The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of how the analysis generalizes to continuous distributions.

**Originality and Significance**

2. **Unified approach**: The paper provides a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning, which is a novel and important contribution.
