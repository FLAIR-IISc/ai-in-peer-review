This paper presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning by extending the policy gradient method to the whole class of coherent risk measures, both static and dynamic. The authors provide a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk and an algorithm for the gradient of general static coherent risk that involves sampling with convex programming. They also derive a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and propose an actor-critic algorithm for solving dynamic risk problems.

The paper is well-organized and well-written, making it easy to follow. The results appear to be correct, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The use of coherent risk measures is a significant contribution, as it provides a more comprehensive framework for risk-sensitive reinforcement learning.

The authors' generalization of prior CVaR-specific results is a minor but useful extension of risk-neutral policy gradient methods. The extension is straightforward, and the authors demonstrate its applicability to various risk measures.

The paper's technical contributions are sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach. However, some minor issues need to be addressed. For instance, the notation "\xi P_\theta" in Theorem 2.1 is confusing and should be changed to match the notation used in Theorem 6.6 in [26]. Additionally, there is a typo on line 141, where "risk enevlop[e]" should be corrected to "risk envelope".

The authors should also clarify that tractability issues arise from policy parametrization, not from Markov risk measures. MDPs with Markov risk measures are tractable, and the authors' approach is aimed at addressing the challenges posed by policy parametrization.

Overall, this paper presents a solid and important extension of existing results in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The authors' contribution to the field is significant, and their approach has the potential to be widely applicable.
