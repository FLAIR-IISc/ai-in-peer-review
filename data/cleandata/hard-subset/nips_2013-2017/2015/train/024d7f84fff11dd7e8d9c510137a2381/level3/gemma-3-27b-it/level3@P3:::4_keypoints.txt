- Presents a unified theoretical framework extending policy gradients to all coherent risk measures via a dual max-expectation representation.  
- Well-written, organized, and accessible even to readers not expert in risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Correctly situates the work within prior literature and shows existing results as special cases.  
- Major strength: generality — covers the entire class of coherent risk measures.  
- Major strength: theoretical soundness — derivations of optimal policy parameters and gradient estimation appear well-motivated.  
- Major strength: novelty — extending policy gradients to coherent risk is a novel contribution.  
- Major strength: practicality — provides a gradient estimation algorithm for implementation.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited to a single example and needs broader experiments, convergence rates, and comparisons to baselines.  
- Weakness: preliminaries (Section 2) are dense and need clearer motivation for parameterizing Pθ and the role of ξ.  
- Weakness/technical: Equation 2’s claim about being “more restrictive” than Theorem 2.1 is unclear and needs justification about restrictions on U(Pθ).  
- Weakness: analysis is restricted to finite distributions; extension to continuous distributions and related challenges should be discussed.  
- Suggestion: rewrite Section 2 to intuitively explain Pθ and ξ.  
- Suggestion: clarify why the structure of U(Pθ) is necessary or restrictive compared to Theorem 2.1.  
- Suggestion: discuss computational complexity of solving the convex program in the SAA estimator (Section 4.3).  
- Suggestion: note challenges and approximation techniques for estimating ξ*_{θ,x} in practice (Section 5).  
- Suggestion: explicitly state that smoothness assumptions in Assumption 2.2 are crucial for Theorem 4.2 and other proofs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, unification of approaches, clear presentation, and practical gradient algorithms.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation and unresolved technical clarifications (Equation 2, preliminaries).  
- Overall: strong theoretical paper recommended for acceptance pending minor clarifications and expanded experimental validation.
