- Novel, theoretically grounded extension of policy gradient methods to the class of coherent risk measures using their dual representation.  
- Leverages dual representation to derive analytic gradient formulas and a general sampling-based gradient estimation algorithm for static and dynamic risk.  
- Unifies and generalizes prior work (e.g., variance, CVaR), showing those as special cases of the framework.  
- Proposes an actor-critic style algorithm for dynamic risk measures built on the static gradient results.  
- Algorithms combine sampling with convex programming and appear practical and scalable for large or continuous MDPs.  
- Technical presentation is clear, well-organized, and mathematically rigorous (though full proof verification is needed).  
- Thorough literature review that correctly situates the contribution within existing research.  
- Includes a numerical example that effectively illustrates the impact of different risk preferences, but the example is brief.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; needs broader MDP environments and detailed comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Preliminaries notation is dense; the roles of P_theta and the weighting function ξ require clearer explanation.  
- The claim that Assumption 2.2 is “more restrictive” than Theorem 2.1 is counterintuitive and needs further justification.  
- Should comment on generalization to continuous distributions beyond the finite probability-space setting.  
- Should explicitly acknowledge and discuss the importance of smoothness of constraint functions in proving Theorem 4.2.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, unifying, clear exposition, potentially scalable algorithms, and relevance to an important research area.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, minor clarity issues, missing discussion on continuous generalization, and need for proof verification.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept, contingent on addressing the minor concerns and providing additional justification where noted.
