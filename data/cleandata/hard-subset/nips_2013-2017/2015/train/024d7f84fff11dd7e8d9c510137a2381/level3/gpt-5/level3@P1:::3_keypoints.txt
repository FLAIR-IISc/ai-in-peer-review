- Develops policy-gradient methods for the full class of coherent risk measures, covering static and time-consistent dynamic (Markov coherent) risks.  
- Static case uses the dual (risk-envelope) representation to express the objective as a convex program and differentiates its Lagrangian via the envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio trick.  
- Produces a generic sampling-based gradient estimator and an SAA variant with a proved consistency theorem under reasonable assumptions.  
- Recovers and simplifies known special cases (CVaR, mean-semideviation) and handles spectral and other risks whose envelopes meet mild conditions.  
- Dynamic case proves a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk by coupling the static gradient with a Bellman recursion.  
- Proposes an actor-critic scheme leveraging the equivalence between dynamic coherent risk and robust MDPs for value-function estimation.  
- Unifies prior risk-sensitive PG results that were specific to particular measures and connects to robust MDP planning.  
- Theoretical development is sound; static gradient derivation via KKT multipliers is clear and SAA consistency is well stated.  
- Dynamic PG theorem is plausible and consistent with robust-MDP value characterization; proofs and references are appropriate.  
- Paper is well organized and readable, reusing familiar PG and convex duality tools; minor notation issues remain.  
- Originality is incremental conceptually (uses dual characterization/envelope theorem) but nontrivial and practically useful.  
- Significance is moderate: provides a general recipe for practitioners but practical impact limited by computational overhead and simulator requirements.  
- Main practical concern: per-iteration convex programs and extracting KKT multipliers may be costly in high-dimensional or continuous outcome spaces.  
- Dynamic algorithm requires sampling multiple next states per visited state; single-trajectory settings are not handled.  
- Empirical evaluation is minimal (small toy example); lacks scaling experiments and comparisons to tailored CVaR/variance baselines.  
- Some assumptions (finite probability spaces; differentiability of envelope constraints) may restrict applicability; extension to Lp spaces deferred.  
- Detailed suggestions: clarify notation for ξPθ, fix typo “risk enevlop[e]”, make explicit that convex programs/KKT multipliers are computed at each θ/state, and discuss solver strategies (warm starts, parametric sensitivity).  
- Additional suggestions: clarify tractability vs. function approximation issues, define ξ-weighted dynamics and sampling in actor-critic, discuss bias from Vθ approximation, and add larger experiments (e.g., continuous control) with runtime/sample-efficiency comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — solid useful generalization with clean theory, but needs stronger empirical validation and clearer discussion of tractability/notation.
