This paper develops policy-gradient methods for risk-sensitive RL that apply to the full class of coherent risk measures. For static risk, it introduces a new gradient formula based on the dual (risk-envelope) representation and an envelope-theorem argument, yielding analytic gradients for several common risks (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation). For general cases where closed-form saddle points are unavailable, it proposes a sampling-plus-convex-programming estimator and proves consistency. For dynamic settings, it provides a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures, linking gradients to a value function and suggesting an actor-critic algorithm. The work unifies and generalizes prior results that targeted individual risks (e.g., mean-variance, CVaR), and connects to robust MDP formulations of dynamic coherent risk.

Quality: The theoretical development is solid and technically careful, with appropriate use of the coherent risk duality [Artzner et al.; Ruszczyński & Shapiro], likelihood-ratio identities, and dynamic programming for Markov risk [Ruszczyński; Chow & Pavone]. The consistency proof for the sample-based estimator is valuable. However, empirical support is minimal: there is no experimental validation of the SAA-based gradient, nor comparisons against analytical gradients where available (e.g., CVaR), and the dynamic-section lacks algorithmic details and experiments.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written but over-ambitious for eight pages. The static section is clearer; the dynamic section is high-level (no pseudo-code, sampling/projection details, simulator requirements, or complexity discussion), which leaves practicality unclear. Splitting the work or focusing this submission on static risks with a thorough empirical study would improve clarity.

Originality: High. The unified derivation for coherent risks and the extension to Markov coherent risk gradients appear novel and go beyond prior risk-specific treatments (e.g., Tamar et al. on CVaR, mean-variance policy gradients, Borkar/Konda-Tsitsiklis actor-critic).

Significance: Potentially broad impact for risk-sensitive decision making and robust RL, provided the methods are demonstrated empirically and made practical.

Pros:
- Unified, elegant derivation covering a wide class of coherent risks.
- Consistency of sampling-based gradient estimator.
- Extension to dynamic Markov coherent risk with a policy-gradient theorem.

Cons:
- Closed-form saddle-point solutions exist only for some risks; scope of analytic applicability is limited.
- No empirical validation of the SAA estimator or comparison to analytic gradients (e.g., CVaR).
- Dynamic section lacks algorithmic detail and experiments; computational practicality unclear.
- Paper attempts too much, which hurts clarity.

Suggestions: Provide experiments comparing SAA vs analytic gradients (variance, bias, wall-clock); include dynamic-risk actor-critic experiments; detail algorithmic steps, computational costs, and assumptions; consider narrowing the scope to static risks for a comprehensive empirical evaluation.
