- Tackles risk-sensitive RL by extending policy gradient methods to general coherent risk measures.
- Presents a unified framework generalizing prior work focused on specific risks like CVaR.
- Derives a novel formula for static coherent risks enabling analytical gradient computation in some cases.
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm for gradients with consistency proofs for complex cases.
- Extends the framework to dynamic Markov coherent risk measures, addressing time-consistency at a high level.
- Notes a promising connection to robust MDPs and potential for handling model uncertainty.
- Major weakness: very limited empirical validation prevents assessing method effectiveness.
- Lacks experimental comparison between sampling-based and analytical gradient approaches (e.g., for CVaR).
- Numerical illustration (Section 6) shows risk preference effects but does not evaluate the policy gradient algorithms.
- Paper attempts to cover static/dynamic and analytical/sampling methods within eight pages, causing lack of depth.
- Dynamic risk section (Section 5) is concise and lacks algorithmic detail, pseudocode, and experimental support.
- Hard to assess computational complexity and scalability of the actor-critic algorithm without implementation details.
- Writing and derivations are generally good but explanations sometimes lack clarity and detail.
- Important assumptions (e.g., Assumption 2.2) need clearer, more detailed explanation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty in unified framework, strong theoretical derivations, broad potential significance, and linkage to robust control.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical evidence, limited depth due to scope, underdeveloped dynamic-risk treatment, and missing method comparisons.
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially impactful theory, but insufficient experiments lead to a rejection recommendation.
- Recommendation: reject but encourage resubmission after substantially expanding experiments and algorithmic details.
- Suggested revisions: provide detailed empirical comparison (sampling vs analytical for CVaR), flesh out actor-critic with pseudocode and experiments, or split into two papers for deeper treatment.
