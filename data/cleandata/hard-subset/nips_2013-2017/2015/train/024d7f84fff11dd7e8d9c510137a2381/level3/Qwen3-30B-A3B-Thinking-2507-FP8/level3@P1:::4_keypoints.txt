- Presents a unified policy-gradient framework for the entire class of coherent risk measures, covering both static and dynamic settings.  
- Leverages the dual representation of coherent risk (Theorem 2.1) to derive general gradient formulas.  
- Proposes a sampling-based estimator for static risk combining convex programming with likelihood-ratio techniques (Theorem 4.2).  
- Extends the policy gradient theorem to Markov coherent risk measures and derives an actor-critic algorithm (Theorem 5.2).  
- Demonstrates that many specific risk methods (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) are special cases of the framework, simplifying prior derivations.  
- Well-organized, clear mathematical exposition accessible beyond the risk-sensitive RL niche.  
- Numerical example effectively shows how different risk measures yield different policies and the importance of risk-measure choice.  
- Assumptions are reasonable, but preliminaries need clearer notation/explanation for Pθ (policy-induced distribution) and ξ (risk-envelope weight).  
- Important technical question whether Equation 2 is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1; convex-program formulation is standard but should be discussed.  
- Main weakness: limited empirical evaluation and lacking comprehensive comparisons to baselines (e.g., SAA in Sec.4.3 vs analytical Sec.4.1 for CVaR).  
- No discussion of extending the approach to continuous distributions; finite probability-space assumption should be addressed.  
- Some theoretical proofs require careful verification (as noted by reviewer).  
- High significance and broad applicability (finance, operations research); promising connection to model uncertainty.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify preliminaries and strengthen empirical analysis.
