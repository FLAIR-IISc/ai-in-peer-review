- Extends policy gradient methods from CVaR to the full class of coherent risk measures, covering static and dynamic settings.  
- Provides unified gradient derivations for static and dynamically consistent coherent risk using Envelope Theorem and convex duality.  
- Builds on recent risk-sensitive RL work and connects to finance and operations research literature.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized, clearly written, and accessible with generally good notation.  
- Numerical illustration (Section 6) demonstrates practical relevance and aids understanding.  
- Technical presentation is high-quality; theoretical claims are well-supported and complete.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and outline future work appropriately.  
- Strength: generality in moving beyond CVaR to all coherent risk measures.  
- Strength: theoretical soundness of gradient derivations and underlying assumptions.  
- Strength: clarity, organization, and potential real-world applicability.  
- Weakness: confusing notation “ξ Pθ” in Theorem 2.1; should adopt clearer notation (e.g., from Theorem 6.6 in [26]).  
- Weakness: minor typo on line 141 (“risk enevlop[e]”) needs correction.  
- Weakness: needs clearer distinction between tractability issues from policy parametrization versus inherent limits of Markov risk measures.  
- Argument for acceptance: important, theoretically grounded extension likely to stimulate further research.  
- Argument against acceptance: extension may be seen as incremental for a top-tier venue despite its generality and clarity.  
- Argument against acceptance: computational complexity of solving convex programs for gradient estimation is not fully explored.  
- Overall assessment: minor but solid and important extension; recommend acceptance with suggested minor revisions.
