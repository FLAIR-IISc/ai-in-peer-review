- Proposes a unified framework extending policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Derives gradient formulas for both static and dynamic coherent risk measures.  
- Leverages the representation theorem for coherent risk measures to enable sampling-based gradient estimators.  
- Main contributions: new static coherent-risk gradient formula, a general sampling-based algorithm with consistency proofs, and a policy-gradient theorem plus actor-critic for Markov coherent risk.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical development and proofs, well-grounded in risk theory and RL.  
- Correctly situates the work with related literature (robust MDPs and prior risk-sensitive policy-gradient work).  
- Originality is strong—generalizes prior results that targeted specific risk measures (e.g., CVaR).  
- Significance is high due to applicability in finance/operations and potential for model-uncertainty handling.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; numerical illustrations do not convincingly demonstrate algorithm effectiveness.  
- Missing direct comparison between analytical gradient approaches (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) and the general sampling-based method.  
- Dynamic-risk section is high-level and underdeveloped, lacking full algorithmic details and convergence analysis.  
- Paper attempts to cover too much within page limits; suggests splitting or focusing on static or dynamic risk.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical unification and technical soundness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical validation, unclear dynamic-risk practicality, and overambitious scope compromising depth.  
- Overall: theoretically important and promising, but needs more comprehensive experiments and clearer development of the dynamic-risk approach.
