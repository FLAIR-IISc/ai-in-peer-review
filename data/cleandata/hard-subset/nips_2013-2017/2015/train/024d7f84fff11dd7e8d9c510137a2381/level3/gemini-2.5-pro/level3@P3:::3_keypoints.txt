- Presents a general policy-gradient framework for MDPs under the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Generalizes prior risk-sensitive policy gradients (e.g., CVaR, variance) to a unified approach.  
- Derives gradient theorems for two settings: static coherent risk of total discounted cost and time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk.  
- Static-case algorithm: combines sampling with convex programming to estimate gradients.  
- Dynamic-case algorithm: develops an actor-critic style method.  
- Unifies and simplifies several prior results, showing them as special cases of the general framework.  
- Theoretical results are technically sound, leveraging dual representation of coherent risk and the Envelope Theorem.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Includes useful examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation) that demonstrate the generality of Theorem 4.2.  
- Contribution is a useful, if relatively direct, extension of existing techniques—minor but important.  
- Significance: increases accessibility and applicability of risk-sensitive RL and connects coherent risk to model uncertainty.  
- Suggestion: clarify notation for ξPθ in Theorem 2.1 to better reflect density/measure representation.  
- Minor typo to fix ("risk enevlop[e]").  
- Suggestion: clarify that SRP/DRP intractability mainly stems from complex, nonconvex dependency of state-visitation distributions on policy parameters; note tabular dynamic Markov risk MDPs are tractable via dynamic programming.  
- Overall: technically solid, clearly written, and a strong contribution suitable for NIPS.
