- Presents a unified extension of policy gradient methods to general coherent risk measures, motivated by gaps in prior work.  
- Leverages the representation theorem for coherent risk measures to derive sampling-based risk-sensitive policy gradients (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2).  
- Proposes concrete algorithms (policy gradient and actor-critic) for both static and dynamic coherent risk settings.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous, using convex optimization and the envelope theorem with clear assumptions.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well organized, with concise explanations and notation.  
- Original contribution: non-trivial generalization of existing risk-sensitive RL methods to the full class of coherent risks.  
- Significant potential impact for applications in finance, robotics, and other domains requiring risk-sensitive decisions.  
- Comprehensive treatment covering both static and dynamic risk measures, including an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to simple toy finance examples and lacks benchmark RL experiments.  
- Sampling-based dynamic algorithms require repeated convex solves, raising scalability and computational-cost concerns.  
- Some assumptions (e.g., bounded likelihood ratios) may limit practical applicability and are not thoroughly explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical novelty, unified framework, clear derivations, and potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical validation and unresolved practical computational limitations.  
- Overall: strong, well-written paper with important theoretical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability and expand experiments.
