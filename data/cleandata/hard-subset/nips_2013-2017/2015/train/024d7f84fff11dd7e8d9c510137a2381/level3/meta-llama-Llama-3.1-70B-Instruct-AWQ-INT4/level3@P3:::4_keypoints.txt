- Presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive RL by extending policy gradients to the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Derives a new gradient formula for static coherent risk and an algorithm using sampling combined with convex programming.  
- Proposes a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and an actor-critic style algorithm for dynamic risk problems.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and accessible to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Provides a clear, concise introduction to coherent risk measures and their properties.  
- Technical derivations are sound and well-presented, with proofs in the supplementary material.  
- Unified analysis covers a broad class of risk measures and recovers previously known results as special cases.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited — more extensive experiments are needed to demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Minor presentation issues: the roles of Pθ and ξ in preliminaries need clarification.  
- Claim that Equation 2 is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1 is not clearly justified.  
- Theoretical results are strong but proofs require verification and may be challenging for some readers.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on additional empirical results and clarifications; overall scores: Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance = 9/10.
