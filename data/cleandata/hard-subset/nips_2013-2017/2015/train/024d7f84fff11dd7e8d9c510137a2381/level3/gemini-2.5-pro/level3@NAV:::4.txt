This paper presents a novel and unified framework for applying policy gradient methods to a general class of coherent risk measures. The core idea is to leverage the dual representation of coherent risk, where any such measure can be expressed as the maximum expected value over a convex set of probability distributions, known as the risk envelope. Building on this, the authors derive a general analytic formula for the gradient of the risk objective. This leads to a practical gradient estimation algorithm, combining sampling with convex programming, applicable to both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) decision-making problems. The work significantly generalizes previous research in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning, which has largely focused on specific measures like Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR) or mean-variance criteria.

**Strengths:**
*   **Originality and Significance:** The paper's primary contribution is its unified theoretical analysis. By treating the entire class of coherent risk measures, it provides a principled and elegant framework that subsumes and simplifies many prior, measure-specific results. This is a significant step forward for risk-sensitive RL.
*   **Quality:** The theoretical development appears sound, with the application of the envelope theorem to derive the main gradient formula (Theorem 4.2) being particularly compelling. The approach is rigorous and provides a complete piece of work, covering both static and dynamic settings.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written, organized, and accessible even to readers not deeply versed in risk-sensitive optimization. The motivation is clear, and the examples of CVaR and mean-semideviation effectively illustrate the framework's utility.

**Weaknesses and Questions for the Authors:**
*   **Empirical Evaluation:** The paper's main weakness is the limited empirical validation. The numerical illustration serves as a good motivational example but does not sufficiently evaluate the proposed gradient estimation algorithm's performance (e.g., variance, sample complexity) on a challenging benchmark. A more in-depth empirical study would substantially strengthen the claims.
*   **Clarity in Preliminaries:** The roles of the base probability measure P_{\theta} and the re-weighting density ξ could be explained more clearly in Section 2.
*   **Technical Clarifications:**
    1.  Regarding Assumption 2.2, is Equation 2 simply the definition of the convex risk envelope? If so, please clarify why it is claimed to be more restrictive than the representation in Theorem 2.1.
    2.  It would be helpful to explicitly mention the role that the smoothness of the constraint functions (ge, fi) plays in the proof of Theorem 4.2.
    3.  A brief comment on how the analysis generalizes to continuous distributions would be a valuable addition.

**Overall Assessment:**
This is a strong theoretical paper with compelling results and high potential impact. The unified framework is a significant contribution to the field. While the proofs require thorough verification and the empirical support is currently thin, the theoretical novelty and importance of the work are clear. I recommend acceptance, with the expectation that the authors will address the requested clarifications.
