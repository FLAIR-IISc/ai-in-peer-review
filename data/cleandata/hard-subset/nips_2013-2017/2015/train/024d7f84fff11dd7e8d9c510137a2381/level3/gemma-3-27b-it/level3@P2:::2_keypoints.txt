- Extends policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified risk-sensitive RL framework.
- Provides theoretical derivations for gradients of both static and dynamic coherent risk measures.
- Static-case gradient uses the envelope theorem and generalizes results for CVaR and mean-semideviation.
- Proposes a sampling-based estimator combined with convex programming for situations without analytical gradients.
- Derives a novel policy gradient theorem for dynamic coherent risk and proposes an actor-critic algorithm.
- Connects the dynamic-risk formulation to robust MDPs and uses function approximation to mitigate dimensionality issues.
- Motivation and problem framing are clear, emphasizing importance for finance and operations research.
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous and well-justified, building on established optimization and RL theory.
- Algorithms for both static and dynamic risk are concrete and practically oriented.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and uses consistent, clear notation.
- Authors situate the work well within related literature, highlighting novel contributions.
- Demonstrates practical relevance via a finance example that shows different risk preferences.
- Computational complexity is a concern: convex programming step and nested sampling may not scale to large problems.
- Empirical evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons on standard benchmark tasks to demonstrate performance.
- Algorithm performance depends on assumptions (e.g., smoothness of risk envelope constraints, simulator availability); sensitivity to violations is under-discussed.
- Key details of the dynamic actor-critic algorithm are deferred to supplementary material and could be more complete in the main text.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, novel and substantial contributions, rigorous analysis, practical algorithms, strong presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: potential scalability issues and insufficient empirical validation on challenging benchmarks.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to discuss computational efficiency, expand empirical evaluation, and include more actor-critic details in the main text.
