- Novel extension of policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Unified framework covering both static and dynamic coherent risk measures.  
- New gradient formula for static coherent risk measures suited for sampling-based approximation.  
- General sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions, with consistency proof for estimates.  
- Derived expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Strong technical contribution using the Envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio trick.  
- Provides illustrative examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation) demonstrating applicability.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for some risk measures, limiting closed-form use.  
- Sample-based approximation lacks empirical validation within the paper.  
- No experimental comparison between sample-based and analytical gradient methods.  
- Dynamic risk policy gradient section is high-level, lacking algorithmic detail and experiments.  
- Paper attempts to cover too many topics in eight pages, which reduces clarity in places.  
- Suggestion to split the work or focus on static measures to allow more detail and empirical evaluation.  
- Significant and original contribution with potential impact across finance, operations research, and RL.  
- Overall positive assessment but needs more comprehensive experiments; rating 8/10, confidence 8/10.
