- Proposes a general policy-gradient framework for optimizing coherent risk measures using their dual (risk envelope) representation.  
- Uses the dual representation to develop gradient-based methods applicable to the whole class of coherent risk measures.  
- Derives a static policy gradient formula (Theorem 4.2) via the Envelope Theorem applied to the Lagrangian of the dual problem.  
- Presents a sample-based gradient estimation algorithm that combines sampling with convex programming when saddle points are not analytic (SAA-based estimator).  
- Extends the analysis to time-consistent Markov coherent risk measures and provides a dynamic policy gradient theorem (Theorem 5.2).  
- Forms the basis for an actor-critic algorithm through a risk-sensitive value function in the dynamic setting.  
- Unifies and subsumes prior specialized results (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance), placing them within a single principled framework.  
- Strength: significant and original theoretical contribution that could standardize risk-sensitive RL methods.  
- Strength: technically elegant and sound development; key insight is combining duality with the Envelope Theorem.  
- Strength: clear writing and structure, with illustrative examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation) that aid understanding.  
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation; numerical illustration does not validate the gradient estimator or actor-critic performance.  
- Needs experiments comparing SAA estimator to analytical estimators (sample efficiency, bias, variance) and testing actor-critic on nontrivial tasks.  
- Raises concern about computational overhead of solving a convex program at each gradient step and lack of empirical cost analysis.  
- Preliminaries should more clearly explain the interplay between policy-induced measure P_theta and the re-weighting density ξ as the “worst-case” distribution.  
- Questions about Assumption 2.2: whether the convex-program representation of the risk envelope is restrictive or just a computational form of the general definition.  
- Requests discussion on extending results to continuous spaces / L_p settings and how SAA convex programs would be handled if infinite-dimensional.  
- Suggests explicitly linking smoothness assumptions on constraint functions to the applicability of the Envelope Theorem in proofs.  
- Overall: strong, influential theoretical paper deserving acceptance despite insufficient empirical validation.
