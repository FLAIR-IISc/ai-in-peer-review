This paper presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that generalizes and extends previous results. The authors provide a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk measures, which is convenient for approximation using sampling, and an algorithm for the gradient of general static coherent risk measures that involves sampling with convex programming. They also derive a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures, relating the gradient to a suitable value function and a corresponding actor-critic algorithm.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning and the concept of coherent risk measures. The technical sections of the paper are also well-written, with clear explanations of the mathematical derivations and algorithms.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that can handle a wide range of coherent risk measures. This is a significant contribution to the field, as previous work has focused on specific risk measures such as variance or conditional value at risk (CVaR).

The authors also provide a numerical illustration of their approach, which demonstrates the importance of flexibility in designing risk criteria for selecting an appropriate risk measure. The example shows that a risk-averse policy with a mean-semideviation objective can lead to different results than a risk-averse policy with a mean-standard deviation objective.

Overall, I believe that this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of reinforcement learning and risk-sensitive optimization. The authors' unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning has the potential to be widely applicable and to lead to new insights and applications in the field.
