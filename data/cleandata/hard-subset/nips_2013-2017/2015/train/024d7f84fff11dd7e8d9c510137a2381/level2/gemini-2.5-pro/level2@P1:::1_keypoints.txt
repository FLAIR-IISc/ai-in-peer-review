- Presents a unified policy-gradient framework for the full class of coherent risk measures, covering static and time-consistent dynamic settings.  
- Derives a novel general policy-gradient formula using the dual representation of coherent risk and the Envelope Theorem.  
- Static case: recovers known gradients for measures with analytic worst-case densities (e.g., CVaR) and proposes an SAA+convex-programming estimator for the general case with a consistency result.  
- Dynamic case: proves a policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risks and proposes an actor-critic algorithm based on a risk-sensitive value function.  
- Strength — generality and unification: significant theoretical advance unifying piecemeal prior work into a single coherent framework.  
- Strength — technical novelty and elegance: clean derivation of gradient results (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2) using principled mathematical tools.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written, motivated, and logically organized from static to dynamic cases.  
- Strength — potential impact: enables designing or selecting problem-specific coherent risk measures beyond standard choices.  
- Weakness — computational cost: SAA-based gradient estimator requires solving a convex program per update, which may be a practical bottleneck as sample size grows.  
- Weakness — actor-critic details: many implementation/analysis details are deferred to the supplement, leaving sample-efficiency and practical complexity unclear (e.g., two-phase sampling).  
- Weakness — limited experiments: evaluation only on a stateless bandit; lacks MDP benchmarks to demonstrate scalability and performance of the actor-critic method.  
- Question — practicality of SAA estimator: how does its computational cost compare to direct estimators for specific risks like CVaR?  
- Question — need for generative model: does the actor-critic require sampling under ξ*-weighted transitions (implying a simulator), and can it be adapted to model-free single-stream or off-policy learning?  
- Question — include CVaR baseline: how does CVaR perform in the illustrative example, given its coherence and insensitivity to upside gains?  
- Overall judgment: strong, elegant theoretical contribution with limited empirical validation and potential computational challenges; recommendation to accept.
