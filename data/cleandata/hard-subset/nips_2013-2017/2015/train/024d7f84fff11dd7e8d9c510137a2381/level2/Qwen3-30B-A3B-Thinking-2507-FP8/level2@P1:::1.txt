

This paper presents a unified framework for risk-sensitive reinforcement learning using coherent risk measures, extending previous work that focused on specific risk measures like CVaR or mean-variance. The authors make two key contributions: (1) a gradient formula for static coherent risk that enables sampling-based estimation, and (2) a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk that leads to an actor-critic algorithm for dynamic risk. The paper is well-motivated and positions itself clearly within the literature, with a thorough discussion of related work showing how their approach generalizes previous results.

The theoretical contributions are strong. Theorem 4.2 provides a new gradient formula for static coherent risk that is particularly convenient for sampling-based approximation, while Theorem 5.2 extends the policy gradient theorem to Markov coherent risk. The consistency result in Proposition 4.4 provides theoretical grounding for the sampling-based estimator. The authors correctly identify that their approach generalizes previous results on specific risk measures, which is a significant contribution.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with appropriate mathematical notation and well-defined concepts. The numerical example in Section 6 effectively demonstrates the practical utility of the framework, showing how different risk measures lead to different policy choices in a portfolio optimization problem. The connection between coherent risk and model uncertainty in the conclusion is particularly insightful.

The main strength of the paper is its conceptual contribution: providing a unified framework that encompasses and extends previous work on specific risk measures. This flexibility is valuable for practitioners who need to select appropriate risk measures based on their specific problem characteristics. The paper advances the state of the art in risk-sensitive RL by providing a general method applicable to the entire class of coherent risk measures.

I have a few suggestions to improve the paper:
1. The comparison with closely related work on dynamic coherent risk (e.g., [20, 9]) could be more detailed. While the authors mention these works, a more precise discussion of how their approach differs would strengthen the paper's novelty claim.
2. The numerical example would be more compelling with additional performance metrics, such as convergence rates and final risk values for the different policies.
3. The authors note their approach requires a simulator for sampling multiple state-transitions from each state. It would be valuable to discuss potential limitations in settings where such a simulator is unavailable.

The paper is technically sound, with appropriate assumptions and well-justified theoretical results. The writing is clear and accessible to readers familiar with reinforcement learning and risk measures. The significance of the work is demonstrated through both the theoretical framework and the practical numerical example.

The paper meets all four NIPS criteria:
- Quality: The technical results are sound, well-supported by theory, and the approach is complete.
- Clarity: The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides sufficient detail for replication.
- Originality: The unified framework for coherent risk measures represents a significant generalization of previous work.
- Significance: The framework provides flexibility for decision-makers to select appropriate risk measures, with potential applications in finance, operations research, and other risk-sensitive domains.

This is a strong contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and makes a significant advance over previous work. I recommend acceptance at NIPS.
