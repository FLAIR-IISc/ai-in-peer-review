- Extends policy gradient methods to the broad class of coherent risk measures, generalizing beyond VaR/CVaR.  
- Proposes a unified framework giving gradient formulas for static and time-consistent dynamic coherent risk measures.  
- Theorem 5.2 linking the dynamic Markov coherent risk gradient to the value function is a key theoretical result.  
- Mathematical development is rigorous and grounded in stochastic programming and optimization.  
- Shows previous risk-gradient results as special cases and connects formulas to sampling-based algorithms for tractability.  
- Numerical results illustrate how different coherent risk measures change learned policies, though examples are brief.  
- Strength: provides a principled, general approach to risk management in RL with potential for large-scale application.  
- Strength: strong literature coverage and theoretical soundness.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational complexity, particularly for the dynamic case.  
- Weakness: limited numerical evaluation and lack of comparisons to strong baseline risk-sensitive RL algorithms.  
- Weakness: scalability and implementation challenges (including function approximation effects on convergence) are not fully addressed.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worth acceptance with minor revisions to address complexity and expanded empirical validation.
