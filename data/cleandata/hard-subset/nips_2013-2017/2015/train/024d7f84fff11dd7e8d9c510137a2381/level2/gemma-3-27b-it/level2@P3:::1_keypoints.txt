- Tackles policy gradient optimization under the full class of coherent risk measures, extending beyond variance/CVaR.  
- Core contribution: unified framework for static and time-consistent dynamic coherent risk measures.  
- Derives new gradient formulas and algorithms combining sampling with convex programming; generalizes existing results.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear motivation, problem formulation, and presentation of main results.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and cites relevant literature in risk-sensitive RL.  
- Numerical illustration provides intuition but is limited in scope and depth.  
- Major strength: generalization to the entire class of coherent risk measures, not just specific instances.  
- Gradient derivations, especially for the dynamic case, are mathematically sound and leverage robust MDP concepts.  
- Practical algorithms (sampling + convex programming, actor-critic) appear promising for large-scale problems.  
- Insightful discussion connecting coherent risk to model uncertainty and robust MDPs in the conclusion.  
- Weakness: practical implications and computational complexity are not fully analyzed.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited; needs broader benchmarks and comparisons to existing methods.  
- Assumption of smoothness (Assumption 2.2) may be restrictive; implications of relaxing it are not explored.  
- Discussion of the actor-critic algorithm is abbreviated; more detail on function approximation and convergence analysis needed.  
- Justification for chosen numerical test problems is weak and should be clarified.  
- Future work: extend approach to explicitly handle model uncertainty and model misspecification with coherent risk.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel/general approach, sound theory, scalable sampling-based algorithms, and strong generalization of prior work.  
- Rejection concerns: limited experiments, brief algorithmic discussion, potentially restrictive assumptions, and unclear computational costs.  
- Overall recommendation: technically strong paper; revise to address experimental and practical weaknesses.
