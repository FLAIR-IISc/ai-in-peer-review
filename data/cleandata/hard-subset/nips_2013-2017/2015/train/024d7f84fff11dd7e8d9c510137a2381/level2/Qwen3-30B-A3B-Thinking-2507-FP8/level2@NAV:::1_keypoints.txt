- Presents a unified framework extending policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures (static and dynamic).
- Main contributions: (1) new gradient formula for static coherent risk enabling sampling-based estimation.
- Main contributions: (2) convex-programming-based algorithm for gradient estimation.
- Main contributions: (3) policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures and an associated actor-critic algorithm.
- Strong theoretical grounding, identifying coherent risk as a natural extension of prior CVaR/mean-variance work.
- Properly cites and situates work within seminal literature (Artzner et al., Ruszczynski & Shapiro).
- Theoretical derivations (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2) are well-supported with proofs in the supplement.
- Examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation) effectively demonstrate generalization of prior results.
- Numerical illustration (Section 6) compellingly shows different risk criteria yield substantially different policies in a financial trading task.
- Demonstrates practical utility for decision-makers needing flexible risk preferences.
- High paper quality: technically sound with clear connection between theory and implementation.
- Excellent clarity and well-organized exposition progressing logically from preliminaries to main results.
- Significant originality by generalizing beyond specific risk measures rather than replicating them.
- Significant potential impact for managing cost variability across diverse applications.
- Main limitation: actor-critic algorithm requires a simulator to sample multiple transitions per state, limiting applicability in low-simulation settings.
- Authors acknowledge this limitation and propose extending to single-trajectory roll-outs as future work.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution that meets NIPS quality criteria; recommendation for acceptance.
