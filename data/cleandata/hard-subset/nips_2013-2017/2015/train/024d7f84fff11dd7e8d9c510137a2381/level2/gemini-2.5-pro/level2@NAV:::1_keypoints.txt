- Proposes a general framework extending policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures for risk-sensitive RL.  
- Unifies and generalizes prior work that targeted specific risk measures (e.g., variance, CVaR).  
- Derives novel gradient formulas for static coherent risk and time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk.  
- Static-case algorithm combines sampling with convex programming.  
- Dynamic-case solution is a new actor-critic algorithm based on a risk-sensitive policy gradient theorem.  
- Major strength: elegant, significant theoretical contribution leveraging the dual representation of coherent risks.  
- Connects RL to established finance and operations-research theory, providing a principled foundation.  
- Gradient derivations recover known results (e.g., CVaR), validating the approach.  
- Offers a versatile toolbox for encoding a wide range of rational risk preferences in agents.  
- Main weakness: limited empirical validation—only a three-asset portfolio illustration.  
- Lacks evaluation of performance, scalability, and sample efficiency on challenging, high-dimensional RL benchmarks.  
- Needs comparisons with existing specialized algorithms to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Practical limitation: the actor-critic method requires a simulator to sample multiple next-state transitions.  
- Overall: strong theoretical advance with solid foundations, but experimental section should be strengthened.
