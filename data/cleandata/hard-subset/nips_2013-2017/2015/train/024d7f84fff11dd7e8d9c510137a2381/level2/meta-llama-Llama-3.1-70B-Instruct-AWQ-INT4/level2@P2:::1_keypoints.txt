- Introduces a unified approach to risk-sensitive RL for coherent risk measures with new gradient formulas for static risk and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent dynamic risk.  
- Covers both static and dynamic coherent risk measures widely used in finance and operations research.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides a clear, concise introduction and technical exposition.  
- Method unifies and generalizes prior work, with the ability to rederive previous results more simply.  
- Provides a clear overview of coherent risk measures and their properties for non-expert readers.  
- Balances theoretical results and practical applications effectively.  
- Numerical evaluation is limited (only one illustration in Section 6) and lacks broader simulations or comparisons.  
- Lacks sufficient details about computational complexity and practical implementation costs.  
- Technical quality rated 8/10 (technically sound).  
- Clarity rated 9/10.  
- Originality rated 9/10.  
- Significance rated 8/10, but could be strengthened with more experiments.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant, general contribution with wide applicability and strong exposition.  
- Rejection arguments: insufficient empirical validation and missing complexity analysis.
