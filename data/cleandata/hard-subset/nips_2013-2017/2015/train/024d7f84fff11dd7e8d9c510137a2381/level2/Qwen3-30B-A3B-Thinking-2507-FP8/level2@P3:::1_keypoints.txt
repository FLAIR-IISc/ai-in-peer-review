- Main contributions: new gradient formula for static coherent risk (sampling + convex programming) and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk enabling an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Theoretical foundation is strong, leveraging the representation theorem for coherent risk measures to derive gradient formulas.  
- Proofs are rigorous; the consistency result (Prop. 4.4) is well-justified and situated within prior literature.  
- Numerical example effectively shows that different risk measures lead to distinct policy choices and highlights the importance of risk selection.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, developing theory before presenting main results.  
- Connections to related work are explicit, with prior results framed as special cases of the proposed framework.  
- Concrete examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation) make the theoretical results more accessible.  
- Originality is strong: the paper generalizes prior work to the full class of coherent risk measures and novelly applies the representation theorem to policy gradients.  
- Significance is clear: provides a flexible framework for decision-makers with varying risk preferences and links coherent risk to model uncertainty.  
- Minor weaknesses: would benefit from broader comparisons on standard benchmarks, quantitative comparisons to prior methods, more accessible algorithmic details (currently in supplement), and a discussion of computational complexity.  
- Overall judgment: technically sound, practically relevant, and recommended for acceptance (fits NIPS focus and has promising application directions).
