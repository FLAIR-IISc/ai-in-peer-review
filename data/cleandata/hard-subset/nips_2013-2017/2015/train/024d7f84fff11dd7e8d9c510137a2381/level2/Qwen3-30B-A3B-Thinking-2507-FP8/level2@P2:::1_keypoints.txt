- Proposes a unified framework extending policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures.  
- Derives gradient formulas for both static and dynamic coherent risk measures, subsuming prior specific measures like CVaR and mean-variance.  
- Presents a sampling-based estimator for static risk using convex programming.  
- Develops a new policy gradient theorem for dynamic risk and an associated actor-critic algorithm.  
- Shows that several previous results are special cases of the general framework.  
- Main strength: strong theoretical contribution and principled, general treatment of risk preferences in RL.  
- Elegant use of the risk envelope representation (Theorem 2.1) to derive general gradient formulas.  
- CVaR example effectively demonstrates simplification and generalization of prior complex derivations.  
- Numerical example (financial trading) illustrates that different risk measures yield different policy choices.  
- Paper is well-written, rigorous, and connects to prior work, but could better address very recent NIPS papers (e.g., Tamar et al. 2014; Chow & Ghavamzadeh 2014).  
- Major limitation: computational complexity not thoroughly analyzed; convex programs per iteration may be expensive for large-scale problems.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a single small example (three assets), so scalability and benchmark performance are not demonstrated.  
- Practical applicability limited by Assumption 2.2 that the risk envelope U(Pθ) is given explicitly, which may not hold for all coherent risks.  
- Significance is moderate: theoretically promising but lacks convincing empirical superiority on standard RL benchmarks.  
- Connection to model uncertainty is mentioned but remains undeveloped.  
- Suggested improvements: more detailed comparisons to recent work, complexity/scalability analysis, additional benchmark experiments, and guidance on selecting risk measures.
