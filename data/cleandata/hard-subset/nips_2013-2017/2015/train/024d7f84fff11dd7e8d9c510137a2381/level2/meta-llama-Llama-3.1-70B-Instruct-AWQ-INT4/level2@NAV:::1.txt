**Review**

This paper presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) by extending the policy gradient method to the whole class of coherent risk measures. The authors provide a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk and an algorithm for the gradient of general static coherent risk, which involves sampling with convex programming. They also derive a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and propose an actor-critic style algorithm for solving dynamic risk problems.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the coherent risk measures and their application to RL. The proposed algorithms are novel and have the potential to generalize previous results.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the organization is logical. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem and the proposed approach. The notation is consistent, and the mathematical derivations are easy to follow.

**Originality**

The paper presents a significant improvement over existing approaches by providing a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL. The authors' contribution is original, and the proposed algorithms are novel.

**Significance**

The results have the potential to impact the field of RL and risk-sensitive optimization. The proposed approach can be applied to various applications, such as finance and operations research, where risk management is crucial.

**Usefulness**

The ideas presented in the paper are practically useful, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how to implement the proposed algorithms. The paper also includes a numerical illustration that demonstrates the importance of flexibility in designing risk criteria.

**Field Knowledge**

The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the field of RL and risk-sensitive optimization. They provide a comprehensive review of related work and clearly explain the contributions of their paper.

**Novelty**

The paper presents a novel approach to risk-sensitive RL, and the proposed algorithms are original. The authors' contribution is significant, and the paper has the potential to impact the field.

**Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility, and the authors include a comprehensive review of related work. However, some of the proofs and technical details are omitted and included in the supplementary material.

**Limitations**

The authors acknowledge the limitations of their approach, such as the need for a simulator for sampling multiple state-transitions from each state. They also mention that extending their approach to work with a single trajectory roll-out is an interesting direction for future research.

**Constructive Criticism**

One potential criticism is that the paper could benefit from more experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms. Additionally, the authors could provide more insights into the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms and their scalability to large problems.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Arguments Pro Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of RL and risk-sensitive optimization.
* The proposed algorithms are original and have the potential to generalize previous results.
* The paper is well-written, and the organization is logical.

Arguments Con Acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from more experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.
* The authors could provide more insights into the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms and their scalability to large problems.

Overall, I recommend accepting the paper with minor revisions to address the constructive criticism mentioned above.
