- Paper introduces a unified policy-gradient framework for the full class of coherent risk measures, covering static and time-consistent dynamic settings.  
- For the static case, derives a general gradient formula using the dual (risk-envelope) representation, requiring solving a convex program and using its Lagrange multipliers.  
- For the dynamic case, proves a policy-gradient theorem relating the gradient to a risk-weighted advantage term and proposes an actor-critic algorithm.  
- The framework recovers and simplifies prior results for specific measures like CVaR and mean-semideviation.  
- Strength: significant and original generalization from specific risk measures to coherent risk measures, linking RL to established risk literature.  
- Strength: technically sound and elegant theory (use of envelope theorem, Theorems 4.2 and 5.2), with clear connections to robust MDPs and risk-sensitive Bellman equations.  
- Strength: well-written and organized paper with useful motivating examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation).  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is very limited (stateless bandit only) and does not test the proposed dynamic actor-critic on sequential benchmarks.  
- Weakness: practical viability concerns — solving a convex program per static-gradient update and two-phase/ξ-weighted sampling for dynamic case may be computationally and sample inefficient and require a simulator.  
- Weakness: important algorithmic and convergence details (critic update, full analysis) are deferred to the supplement, limiting assessment from the main paper.  
- Suggestion: include empirical results on a standard MDP/control task to evaluate the dynamic actor-critic and behavior under different coherent risks.  
- Suggestion: add analysis of computational complexity and scaling of the convex-program-based gradient estimator versus specific-risk methods.  
- Suggestion: provide more implementation intuition for the actor-critic, especially how ξ-weighted trajectory sampling and h(x,a) estimation are performed.  
- Suggestion: emphasize the robustness-to-model-uncertainty motivation earlier (e.g., in the introduction) to highlight the framework’s practical relevance.  
- Overall: a strong, novel theoretical contribution with limited empirical validation; promising paper and good candidate for acceptance if empirical/practical concerns are addressed.
