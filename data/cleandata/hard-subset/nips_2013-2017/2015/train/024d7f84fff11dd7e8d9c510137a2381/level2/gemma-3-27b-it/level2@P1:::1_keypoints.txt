- Proposes a unified policy-gradient approach for coherent risk measures, generalizing prior work on variance and CVaR.
- Covers both static and time-consistent dynamic coherent risk measures.
- Uses sampling plus convex programming for static risk and actor-critic methods for dynamic risk.
- Well-motivated with clear connections to finance and operations research.
- Situates work appropriately in existing literature and cites key prior papers.
- Theorem 4.2 provides a significant, general gradient formula for risk-sensitive policy optimization.
- Discussion of dynamic risk and links to robust MDPs and dynamic programming are valuable.
- Numerical illustration offers some intuition but is very limited in scope.
- Presentation is dense and occasionally hard to follow; many proofs are in the supplement.
- Scalability of the actor-critic algorithm and the dynamic sampling procedure is not adequately addressed.
- Discussion of function approximation is brief and omits detailed impact of approximation error on convergence.
- Empirical validation is weak; more comprehensive experiments on benchmark RL tasks are needed.
- Proposition 4.4 gives SAA consistency conditions, but their practical verifiability is questionable.
- The paper relies on Assumption 2.2 for the risk envelope, limiting applicability to certain coherent risks.
- A more thorough exploration of connections to robust MDP literature would strengthen the work.
- Strengths: novel, general framework; extension to all coherent risk measures; solid theoretical foundation; links to robust MDPs.
- Weaknesses: dense presentation, limited scalability/approximation discussion, weak empirical validation, SAA practicality concerns, reliance on Assumption 2.2.
- Overall assessment: theoretically valuable but borderline due to practical and empirical shortcomings; reviewer leans toward rejection unless concerns are addressed.
