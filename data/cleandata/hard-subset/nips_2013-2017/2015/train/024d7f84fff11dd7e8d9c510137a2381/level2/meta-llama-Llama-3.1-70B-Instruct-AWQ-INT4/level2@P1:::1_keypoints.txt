- Presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that generalizes and extends prior results.  
- Derives a new gradient formula for static coherent risk measures that is amenable to sampling-based approximation.  
- Proposes an algorithm for gradients of static coherent risk measures combining sampling with convex programming.  
- Introduces a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures and a corresponding actor-critic algorithm.  
- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow with clear technical derivations and algorithm descriptions.  
- Provides a concise introduction to risk-sensitive RL and the concept of coherent risk measures.  
- Main contribution: handles a wide range of coherent risk measures beyond variance and CVaR.  
- Numerical illustration shows designing flexible risk criteria matters (mean–semideviation vs mean–standard deviation produce different policies).  
- Technically sound with well-explained mathematical derivations and clear algorithmic presentation.  
- Assumes substantial background in RL and risk-sensitive optimization, which may limit accessibility for non-experts.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion of limitations and potential avenues for future research.  
- Evaluation metrics: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: unified approach, clear exposition, strong technical sections, and illustrative experiment.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns for non-experts and limited discussion of limitations/future work.
