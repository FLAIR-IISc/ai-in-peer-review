**Summary of the Paper**

This paper presents a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) that generalizes and extends previous results. The authors focus on coherent risk measures, which are widely accepted in finance and operations research, and develop policy gradient algorithms for RL with a coherent risk objective. They consider both static and dynamic risk measures and provide a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk and a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent dynamic risk. The paper also includes a numerical illustration of the approach using a trading agent example.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Unified approach**: The paper provides a unified approach to risk-sensitive RL that generalizes and extends previous results, making it a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Coherent risk measures**: The authors focus on coherent risk measures, which are widely accepted in finance and operations research, making the approach more applicable to real-world problems.
3. **New formulas and theorems**: The paper provides new formulas and theorems for the gradient of static coherent risk and Markov coherent dynamic risk, which are essential for developing policy gradient algorithms.
4. **Numerical illustration**: The paper includes a numerical illustration of the approach using a trading agent example, which helps to demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Technical complexity**: The paper is technically complex, and the mathematical derivations may be challenging for non-experts to follow.
2. **Limited scope**: The paper focuses on coherent risk measures, which may not be applicable to all types of risk-sensitive RL problems.
3. **Assumptions**: The paper makes several assumptions, such as the existence of a strictly feasible point in the risk envelope, which may not always hold in practice.
