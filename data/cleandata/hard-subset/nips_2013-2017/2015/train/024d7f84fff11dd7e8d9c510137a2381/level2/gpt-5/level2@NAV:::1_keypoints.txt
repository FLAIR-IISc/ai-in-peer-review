- Paper develops risk-sensitive policy-gradient methods for the full class of coherent risks, covering static and time-consistent Markov coherent settings.  
- Main claims: new gradient formula for static coherent risks enabling sampling via likelihood-ratio plus convex programming.  
- Main claims (cont.): a sample-average-approximation (SAA) estimator with consistency guarantees.  
- Main claims (cont.): a policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk that leads to an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Theoretical development is sound, grounded in dual representations of coherent risks and the envelope theorem.  
- Static gradient formula cleanly recovers known special cases (e.g., CVaR); SAA consistency plausible under mild regularity.  
- Dynamic extension (policy gradient for Markov coherent risk) is a notable technical contribution.  
- Empirical evaluation is minimal (small trading toy) and lacks ablations on scalability and baselines.  
- Algorithmic details and convergence proofs are deferred to the supplement, reducing immediate reproducibility.  
- Computational overhead (convex optimization to obtain ξ* and multipliers, potentially per state) and sample complexity are not quantified.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and literature-situated, with stated assumptions (finite Ω, envelope smoothness, availability of dfi/dp).  
- Clarity issues: guidance on verifying assumptions for common envelopes and implications for continuous spaces is lacking; actor-critic is only sketched.  
- Originality: high — unifies prior risk-specific work and extends policy-gradient theory to Markov coherent risk.  
- Significance: potentially high, but practical impact depends on computational feasibility and broader empirical validation.  
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually unifying framework, broad generality, solid theoretical support and recovery of known cases.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited experiments, heavy inner-loop optimization, several practical assumptions/details left to supplement.  
- Constructive suggestions: add experiments on standard RL benchmarks and comparisons to CVaR-PG/mean-variance/robust MDPs.  
- Constructive suggestions (cont.): provide complexity and wall-clock analyses, discuss variance reduction and importance sampling.  
- Constructive suggestions (cont.): clarify computation of multipliers and constraint derivatives in model-free and continuous settings; explore single-trajectory and off-policy estimators.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical paper; recommend acceptance conditional on strengthening empirical validation.
