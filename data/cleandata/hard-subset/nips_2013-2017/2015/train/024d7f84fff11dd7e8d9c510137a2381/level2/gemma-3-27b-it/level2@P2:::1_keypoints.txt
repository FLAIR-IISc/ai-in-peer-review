- Presents a unified policy gradient framework for coherent risk measures in RL, generalizing beyond CVaR/variance.  
- Derives gradient formulas for static and dynamic coherent risk using sampling and convex programming.  
- Leverages coherent risk theory and the envelope theorem to provide a sound theoretical foundation.  
- Connects framework to robust MDPs and discusses model uncertainty implications.  
- Includes a simple numerical example illustrating effects of different risk preferences.  

- Strength: Generality — extends to the full class of coherent risk measures, a significant theoretical advance.  
- Strength: Sound theoretical foundation grounded in established coherent risk literature.  
- Strength: Practical algorithmic proposals (sampling + convex programs, actor-critic) for large-scale MDPs.  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation with rigorous derivations (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2).  
- Strength: Insightful discussion on model uncertainty and robust MDP connections.  

- Weakness: Computational complexity — repeatedly solving convex programs in gradient estimation may not scale and is not fully addressed.  
- Weakness: Limited empirical evaluation — only a single illustrative example, lacking benchmarks and broader tasks.  
- Weakness: Dependence on assumptions (e.g., risk envelope form) that may not hold for all coherent risk measures.  
- Weakness: Dynamic risk actor-critic description is brief and lacks details on function approximation, sampling, and convergence.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detailed comparison to robust MDP methods in terms of cost and performance.  

- Argument for acceptance: Addresses an important RL problem with substantial theoretical generalization and viable algorithmic directions.  
- Argument against acceptance: Scalability concerns and limited empirical validation make practical benefits unclear.  
- Argument against acceptance: Technical details for the dynamic-risk algorithm are insufficient.  

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-written contribution with significant theoretical novelty; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  

- Suggested revision: Provide detailed analysis of computational cost, especially the convex programming step.  
- Suggested revision: Expand empirical evaluation on standard RL benchmarks across risk preferences.  
- Suggested revision: Elaborate the dynamic actor-critic algorithm, including function approximation, sampling, and convergence analysis.  
- Suggested revision: Offer a more detailed comparison to robust MDP approaches (cost and performance).  
- Suggested revision: Discuss practical implications and limitations of key assumptions (e.g., Assumption 2.2).
