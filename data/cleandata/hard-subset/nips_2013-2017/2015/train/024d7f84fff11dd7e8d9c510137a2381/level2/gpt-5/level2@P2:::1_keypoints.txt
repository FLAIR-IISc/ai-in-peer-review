- Paper develops policy-gradient methods for the full class of coherent risk measures in both static and time-consistent dynamic settings.  
- Static result (Theorem 4.2): general gradient formula for ρ(Z) via the dual risk-envelope and envelope theorem, enabling sampling-based estimators.  
- Provides concrete instances: CVaR (recovers/simplifies prior work) and mean-semideviation (new gradient formula).  
- Proposes a sample-average approximation (SAA) combining Monte Carlo with a convex program to recover envelope optimizers and KKT multipliers; proves estimator consistency.  
- Dynamic result (Theorem 5.2): policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk linking ∇θVθ(x) to expectations under a ξ-weighted Markov chain where ξ is the one-step envelope optimizer.  
- Derives an actor-critic style algorithm: critic uses robust MDP tools with function approximation; actor forms ξ, samples reweighted dynamics, and updates θ.  
- Small trading example shows behavioral differences between risk-neutral, mean-semideviation, and mean-standard-deviation objectives.  
- Generalizes prior risk-sensitive PG work (variance, CVaR, exponential utility) and connects to Ruszczyński’s dynamic coherent risk and robust MDP literature.  
- Strength: generality and unification across coherent risks, recovering known cases and yielding new ones.  
- Strength: principled variational foundation using the dual envelope and envelope theorem with mild assumptions.  
- Strength: novel dynamic PG theorem bridging to robust MDPs and enabling policy-space approximation.  
- Strength: practical estimators (likelihood-ratio and SAA with convex programming) with consistency guarantees.  
- Weakness: very limited empirical evaluation — no dynamic MDP experiments, no baselines, no sample-efficiency or scalability studies.  
- Weakness: computational burden — per-batch convex solves, per-state envelope problems, and need for ξ-weighted sampling; overall complexity and stability not quantified.  
- Weakness: clarity gaps in estimator implementation, especially how derivative terms w.r.t. p and θ are estimated from samples and how KKT multipliers are obtained.  
- Weakness: finite probability space assumption and requirement for simulator access for ξ-weighted sampling limit applicability; single-trajectory on-policy learning not addressed.  
- Weakness: no discussion or analysis of estimator variance or finite-sample behavior for tail-sensitive risk measures.  
- Quality: theory appears sound and SAA consistency appropriate, but actor-critic is only sketched and algorithmic costs are under-described.  
- Clarity: generally well written with clear theorems, but many implementation details deferred to the supplement and some derivative notation is unclear.  
- Originality: high — unified gradient formula for coherent risks and dynamic PG theorem for Markov coherent risk are novel.  
- Significance: potentially high for RL and risk-aware applications, but immediate impact limited by lack of empirical validation and scalability analysis.  
- Key suggestions: add dynamic experiments and baselines, provide algorithm pseudocode and complexity, clarify derivative/KKT estimation, discuss variance reduction, address single-trajectory learning and extensions beyond finite Ω.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong, novel, unifying theoretical contributions and clear connections to prior work.  
- Cons against acceptance: insufficient empirical validation, unclear practicality/compute costs, and under-specified implementation details.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept — strong theory but needs more empirical evidence and clearer algorithmic exposition to strengthen the case.
