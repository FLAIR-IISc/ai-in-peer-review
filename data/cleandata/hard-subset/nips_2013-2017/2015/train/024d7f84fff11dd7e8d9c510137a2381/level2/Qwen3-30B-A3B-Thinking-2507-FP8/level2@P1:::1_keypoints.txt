- Presents a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL using coherent risk measures, generalizing beyond CVaR/mean-variance.  
- Two main contributions: (1) gradient formula for static coherent risk enabling sampling-based estimation.  
- (2) Policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk, yielding an actor-critic algorithm for dynamic risk.  
- Well-motivated positioning and thorough related-work discussion showing generalization of prior results.  
- Theorem 4.2: new gradient formula for static coherent risk suitable for sampling approximation.  
- Theorem 5.2: extension of the policy gradient theorem to Markov coherent risk.  
- Proposition 4.4: consistency result providing theoretical grounding for the sampling estimator.  
- Correctly identifies and demonstrates that the approach generalizes previous specific-risk-measure results.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and uses appropriate mathematical notation and definitions.  
- Numerical example (portfolio optimization) effectively shows different risk measures produce different policy choices.  
- Insightful connection drawn between coherent risk and model uncertainty in the conclusion.  
- Main strength: conceptual contribution — a flexible, unified method applicable to the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed comparison with closely related dynamic coherent risk works (e.g., [20, 9]).  
- Suggestion: enhance numerical results with additional metrics (convergence rates, final risk values) for different policies.  
- Suggestion: discuss limitations when a simulator for sampling multiple state-transitions per state is unavailable.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, meets NIPS criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance), recommend acceptance.
