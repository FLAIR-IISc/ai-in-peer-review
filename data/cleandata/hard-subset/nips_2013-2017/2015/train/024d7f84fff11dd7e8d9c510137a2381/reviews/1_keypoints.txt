- Proposes a new formula for static coherent risks enabling analytic derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.
- Presents a general sampling-based algorithm for cases lacking analytical solutions and proves consistency of the policy gradient estimates.
- Provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- Unified derivation is novel and a valuable contribution to risk-sensitive decision making.
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for some risk measures, limiting closed-form applicability.
- The sample-based approximation could extend applicability but lacks empirical validation in the paper.
- No experimental comparison between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., for CVaR) is provided but would be helpful.
- Paper is generally well written but attempts too many issues in eight pages, hurting clarity.
- Section on dynamic risk policy gradients is high-level and lacks algorithmic details and experiments, making practicality unclear.
- Suggestion to split the work or focus on static risk measures to allow more details and comprehensive empirical evaluation.
- Overall interesting and potentially broadly applicable, but the lack of comprehensive experiments prevents full assessment.
