- Problem: Optimize policy parameters to minimize a general class of coherent risk measures (convex, monotone, translation invariant).
- Approach: Use dual representation of coherent risk measures as a convex hull of probability distributions; risk is max expected value over the hull.
- Assumption: Bound on maximum derivatives of the convex hull borders (M-Lipschitz continuity).
- Results: Analytic derivation of optimal policy parameters under the risk measure.
- Results: General gradient estimation algorithm for both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) cases.
- Presentation: Paper is well-written and organized; accessible even to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization.
- Contribution: Unified analysis covering a broad class of risk measures, including most well-known ones.
- Empirical critique: Deeper empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm is needed.
- Clarification request: Preliminaries unclear—roles of P_{\theta} and ξ need clearer explanation.
- Technical question: Is Equation 2 simply the convex envelope definition, and why is it claimed to be more restrictive than Theorem 2.1?
- Extension suggestion: Add comment on how the analysis generalizes to continuous distributions.
- Theoretical note: Mention the role of smoothness of constraint functions in proving Theorem 4.2.
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical paper with compelling results, but proofs require verification.
