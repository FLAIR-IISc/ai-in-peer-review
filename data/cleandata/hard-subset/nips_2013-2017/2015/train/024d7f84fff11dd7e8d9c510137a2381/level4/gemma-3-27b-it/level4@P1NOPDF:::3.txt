The authors analyze risk-averse Markov decision processes using both static and dynamically consistent risk measures. The primary contribution of this work is the derivation of the gradient for both the dynamic and static settings.

This paper generalizes previous results that specifically focused on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR). This extension represents a minor, but useful, contribution, as the results largely follow from a straightforward extension of policy gradient methods in the risk-neutral case.

The paper is well organized, clearly written, and easy to follow. 

The results appear to be correct.

Minor comments:

- Theorem 2.1: The notation ξP<sub>θ</sub> is somewhat confusing. I suggest referencing Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting the notation used there.
- Line 141: “risk enevlop[e]” – In Section 3, to avoid confusing readers less familiar with the topic, it may be helpful to clarify that Markov decision processes with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability of the problem addressed in this paper stems from the policy parametrization, and not from the risk measure itself. 

Overall, the paper presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results.
