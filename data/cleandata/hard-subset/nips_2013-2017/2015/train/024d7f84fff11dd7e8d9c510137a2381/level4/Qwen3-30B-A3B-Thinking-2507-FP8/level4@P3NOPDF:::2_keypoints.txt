- Introduces a unified framework for policy gradient estimation in risk-sensitive RL under coherent risk measures.  
- Presents novel algorithms integrating coherent risk optimization with policy gradient and actor-critic methods.  
- Demonstrates applicability via a finance-focused toy example: static portfolio optimization with distinct asset return distributions.  
- Motivation is compelling and clearly articulated, addressing the need for flexible risk-sensitive sequential decision-making.  
- Problem formulation and derivations are logically grounded in coherent risk theory and prior RL work, with technical rigor.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with precise mathematical exposition and comprehensive references.  
- Clarity and narrative flow are standout strengths, making concepts accessible and reproducible.  
- Experiments are limited to a static setting but effectively show the algorithm’s risk-averse behavior aligning with theory.  
- Results confirm adaptability across different coherent risk measures and expected reward–risk trade-offs.  
- Originality is strong: non-trivial extension from specific risk measures to a general coherent-risk framework that unifies prior work.  
- Significance is substantial for high-stakes domains (finance, robotics), offering a practical, theoretically grounded toolkit for risk-aware policies.  
- Sole noted limitation is the lack of dynamic/sequential experimental validation, framed as future work; overall recommendation: accept.
