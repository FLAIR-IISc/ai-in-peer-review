- Presents a unified approach to policy gradient methods for reinforcement learning using coherent risk measures, generalizing prior work on specific risk objectives.  
- Leverages the dual representation of coherent risk measures (Theorem 2.1) to build a general framework covering both static and dynamic risk measures.  
- Derives a gradient formula for static coherent risks using convex programming combined with sampling.  
- Establishes a policy gradient theorem for dynamic Markov coherent risks, extending standard policy gradient to risk-sensitive settings.  
- Provides a practical gradient estimation algorithm that integrates sampling with convex optimization for both static and dynamic cases.  
- Proves theoretical consistency results for the proposed gradient estimators under mild assumptions.  
- Demonstrates practical utility via numerical experiments (financial trading) showing distinct policies from different risk preferences.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and mathematically rigorous; the dual-representation connection is clearly motivated.  
- Mathematical notation is consistent and supplementary material gives helpful additional detail.  
- Original contribution: first unified framework across the class of coherent risk measures, showing CVaR and mean-variance as special cases.  
- Significance: addresses important risk-sensitive RL problems (finance, robotics) and supports time-consistent risk management.  
- Strength: unifies theory, provides practical algorithms, proves consistency, and links to prior work clearly.  
- Weakness: assumes a finite probability space, limiting immediate applicability to continuous domains (authors acknowledge this).  
- Weakness: computational complexity of convex programming steps in the gradient estimator is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is illustrative but could include more comprehensive comparisons with existing methods.  
- Weakness: discussion of model uncertainty (Section 7) is promising but needs more concrete development.  
- Clarification: reviewer’s earlier critique of unclear preliminaries could be more constructive; the paper clarifies roles of P_θ and ξ.  
- Technical note: Equation 2 specifies the risk envelope form under Assumption 2.2 and is necessary for the gradient derivation.  
- Technical note: smoothness of constraint functions is crucial for Theorem 4.2 and is addressed by Assumption 2.2.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, NIPS-suitable paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions to risk-sensitive RL.
