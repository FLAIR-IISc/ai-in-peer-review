- Problem: Finding policy parameters that minimize coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).
- Approach: Uses the dual representation of coherent risks as a convex hull of probability distributions where risk = max expected value over the hull.
- Assumption: Imposes M‑Lipschitz continuity (bounds on max derivatives) of the convex hull’s boundaries.
- Theoretical result: Analytically derives optimal policy parameters under the Lipschitz assumption.
- Algorithmic result: Proposes a general gradient-estimation algorithm applicable to both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) settings.
- Presentation: Paper is well-written and clearly organized; explanations are mostly accessible.
- Contribution/significance: Provides a unified analysis covering a broad class of risk measures—considered a significant advance.
- Empirical limitation: Needs a more comprehensive empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.
- Clarification needed: Roles of P_θ and ξ in the preliminaries are unclear and should be clarified.
- Question on Eq.2: Ask whether Equation 2 is just the convex envelope and why it’s viewed as more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.
- Extension request: Include discussion on how the analysis generalizes to distributions over continuous variables.
- Proof detail request: In the short paper, clarify the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2.
- Overall judgment: Strong theoretical paper with promising results, but proofs require verification.
