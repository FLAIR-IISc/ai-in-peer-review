- Analyzes risk-averse MDPs with static and dynamically consistent risk measures; main contribution is deriving the gradient form for both settings.  
- Generalizes prior CVaR-specific results into a unified approach for risk-sensitive reinforcement learning.  
- Paper is well-organized, well-written, technically sound, and results appear correct.  
- Provides a clear introduction to coherent risk measures and includes illustrative examples.  
- Framework applies to various risk measures (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) and a numerical example shows the value of flexible risk criteria.  
- Contribution is useful but not groundbreaking—largely an extension of existing work.  
- The policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures is a natural extension of the standard policy gradient theorem.  
- Minor comment: notation ξPθ in Theorem 2.1 is confusing; suggest using the notation from Theorem 6.6 in [26].  
- Minor comment: typo in line 141 ("risk enevlop[e]" → "risk envelope").  
- Minor comment: clarify in Section 3 that MDPs with Markov risk measures are tractable and intractability arises from policy parametrization, not the risk measure.  
- Recommendation: accept, subject to addressing minor comments and adding context on the significance of the contribution.
