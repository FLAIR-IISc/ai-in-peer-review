This paper introduces methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning problems using coherent risk measures, and proposes corresponding RL algorithms. A key contribution lies in extending existing techniques, previously limited to specific risk measures, to the more general class of coherent risk measures, thereby offering a unified framework. The proposed algorithms are illustrated through a toy example in a financial setting, where each asset possesses its own return distribution.

The motivation is clearly articulated, and the problem formulation and algorithms logically follow from this motivation and related prior work. While the experimental evaluation focuses on a relatively simple static risk problem, the results appear to validate the algorithm’s effectiveness in achieving risk-averse behavior and its flexibility across different risk types. 

**Quality:** The paper is well-written and technically sound, with equations presented accurately and all relevant references included.

**Clarity:** The paper’s clarity is notably high.

**Originality:** This work introduces novel algorithms for risk-sensitive RL, building upon existing research in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to a coherent risk measure framework is not trivial, and the paper demonstrates sufficient originality.

**Significance:** Risk-sensitive RL is a compelling and important research area, and this paper makes a significant contribution by presenting clear algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL problems with coherent risk measures. The authors demonstrate the behavior of trained policies under varying risk settings within a static risk problem. The proposed approach is well-motivated and represents a natural progression from existing algorithms and problem settings, encompassing policy gradients, actor-critic methods, and coherent risk measure optimization.
