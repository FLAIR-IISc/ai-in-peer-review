- Addresses identifying policy parameters that minimize a specified risk measure.  
- Considers a broad class of coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Leverages the dual representation of coherent risks as the convex hull of probability distributions.  
- Characterizes risk as the maximum expected value over that convex hull.  
- Imposes an M‑Lipschitz assumption on the convex hull boundary derivatives.  
- Derives analytical expressions for optimal policy parameters under that assumption.  
- Proposes a general gradient estimation algorithm for static and dynamic settings.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written; explanations are mostly comprehensible.  
- Main contribution: unified analysis covering many widely used risk measures.  
- Recommends a more comprehensive empirical evaluation of the gradient algorithm.  
- Comment: preliminaries need clarification on the roles of Pθ and ξ.  
- Question: Is Equation 2 the convex envelope, and does it impose stricter conditions than Theorem 2.1?  
- Suggestion: discuss extension of the analysis to continuous distributions.  
- Suggestion: briefly explain how smoothness of constraint functions aids the proof of Theorem 4.2.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with promising results, but proofs require careful verification.
