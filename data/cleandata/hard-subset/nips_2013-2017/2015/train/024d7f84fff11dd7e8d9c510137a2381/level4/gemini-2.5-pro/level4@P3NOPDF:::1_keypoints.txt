- Introduces a unified framework deriving policy gradients for static coherent risk measures, yielding analytical gradients for several common risks.  
- Presents a sampling-based algorithm and consistency guarantees when analytical saddle-point solutions are unavailable.  
- Extends the approach to derive policy gradients for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Theoretical development is elegant, novel, and a significant contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for a subset of risk measures, limiting closed-form applicability.  
- The sample-based approximation is critical for general use but is not empirically validated in the paper.  
- No experiments are provided, preventing assessment of estimator performance, stability, or sample complexity.  
- The paper’s breadth within eight pages harms clarity and leads to superficial treatment of key concepts.  
- Section 5 on dynamic risk measures is underdeveloped, lacking concrete algorithmic details and experimental evaluation.  
- Recommendation: narrow the submission to static risk measures to allow fuller theoretical exposition and comprehensive experiments.  
- Suggestion: include empirical comparisons (e.g., sample-based vs analytical CVaR gradients) and move dynamic-risk material to a longer journal version.
