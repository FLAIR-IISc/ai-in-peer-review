The paper introduces a unified expression for the class of static coherent risk measures that enables analytical derivations of policy gradients for many standard risks. It also proposes a general sampling-based method for situations lacking closed-form solutions and establishes consistency guarantees for the resulting policy-gradient estimators. Finally, the paper provides formulas for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.

Significance and quality:
The unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risks is compelling and represents a valuable, novel contribution to risk-sensitive decision making. However, analytic solutions to the Lagrangian saddle points are available only for a subset of risk measures. The proposed sampling-based approximation could extend applicability to general coherent risks by avoiding explicit saddle-point derivations, but the paper lacks empirical evidence supporting the practical viability of this estimator. A comparative evaluation of sampling-based versus analytical policy gradients across tasks—e.g., for CVaR—would be very helpful.

Originality:
To the best of my knowledge, the policy-gradient results in this level of generality are new.

Clarity:
The paper is generally well written, but the clarity of the main text is hampered by the number of issues tackled within eight pages. In particular, the dynamic-risk policy gradients in Section 5 are presented at a very high level, and the absence of algorithmic details and experimental results prevents the reader from assessing whether the proposed gradients yield practical methods. This material seems better suited to a longer journal article. Alternatively, narrowing the scope to static risk measures would make the paper more self-contained and allow for greater detail and a more comprehensive empirical study beyond the current basic toy example. This is an interesting paper with potentially broadly applicable results, but the lack of thorough experimental evaluation makes it difficult to judge. A tighter focus could also alleviate clarity issues due to space constraints.
