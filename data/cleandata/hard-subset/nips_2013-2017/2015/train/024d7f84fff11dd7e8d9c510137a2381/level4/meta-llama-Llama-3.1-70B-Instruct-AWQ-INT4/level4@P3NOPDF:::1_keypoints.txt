- Proposes a new unified formula for static coherent risks that yields analytical policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Introduces a general sampling-based algorithm for coherent risks lacking analytical saddle-point solutions.  
- Provides consistency guarantees for the sampling-based policy gradient estimates.  
- Derives policy gradient expressions for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Novelty: the general-form derivation of policy gradients for coherent risks is a meaningful contribution.  
- Limitation: analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for some risk measures.  
- Major weakness: lack of empirical evidence demonstrating the practicality of the sampling-based estimation scheme.  
- Recommends experimental comparisons between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR) to assess viability.  
- Clarity issue: paper attempts many topics within page limits, which reduces clarity and detail.  
- Dynamic risk gradients (Section 5) are presented at a high level without algorithmic details or experiments.  
- Suggests possibly splitting the work or focusing on static risks with fuller empirical evaluation for greater impact.
