- Problem: Optimize policy parameters to minimize coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).
- Methodology: Uses dual representation of coherent risk as the maximum expected value over a convex hull of probability distributions.
- Assumption: Introduces M-Lipschitz continuity of convex-hull boundaries to enable analysis.
- Results: Analytically derives optimal policy parameters under the assumption.
- Algorithm: Develops a general gradient estimation method applicable to both static (single-stage) and dynamic (sequential) settings.
- Presentation: Paper is clearly written and well-organized, accessible to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization.
- Contribution: Provides a unified framework covering a broad class of coherent risk measures, representing a significant theoretical advance.
- Empirical gap: More extensive empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient estimator is needed to strengthen the work.
- Clarification 1: Preliminaries need clearer explanation of the roles of Pθ and ξ.
- Clarification 2: Explain why Equation (2) (convex envelope) is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1’s representation.
- Clarification 3: Discuss how the analysis extends to continuous distributions.
- Clarification 4: Explicitly justify the significance of smoothness assumptions on constraint functions in Theorem 4.2’s proof.
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contribution with compelling results, though proofs warrant careful verification.
