- Main contribution: extends existing methods from specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified framework.  
- Algorithm illustrated on a toy finance example with assets having distinct return distributions.  
- Motivation is clear; problem formulation and algorithms logically follow prior work.  
- Experiment is simple/static but effectively demonstrates risk-averse behavior and flexibility across risk measures.  
- Paper is well-written, equations are technically sound, and relevant references are included.  
- Clarity is well above average.  
- Originality: non-trivial extension to coherent risk measures; sufficient novelty.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem, provides clear algorithms for optimizing coherent risk measures, and represents a natural extension of policy-gradient/actor-critic methods.
