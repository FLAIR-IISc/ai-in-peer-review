The authors analyze risk-averse Markov decision processes using both static and dynamically consistent risk measures. The primary contribution of this work is the derivation of the gradient form for both the dynamic and static settings.

This paper generalizes previous results that were specifically focused on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR). This extension represents a minor, yet useful, contribution, as the results can be seen as a straightforward extension of policy gradient methods in risk-neutral settings.

The paper is well organized, clearly written, and easy to follow. 

The results appear to be correct.

Minor comments:
- Theorem 2.1: The notation \xi P_\theta is potentially confusing. I recommend referencing Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting the corresponding notation.
- Line 141: “risk enevlop[e]” - In Section 3, to assist readers less familiar with the topic, it may be beneficial to clarify that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability of the problem addressed in this paper stems from the policy parametrization, and not from the risk measure itself. The paper presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results.
