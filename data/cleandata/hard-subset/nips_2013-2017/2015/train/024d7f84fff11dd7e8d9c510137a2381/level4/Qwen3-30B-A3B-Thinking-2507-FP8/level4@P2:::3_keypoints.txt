- Addresses risk-sensitive RL by developing policy gradient methods for the entire class of coherent risk measures, not just CVaR or variance.
- Presents a unified framework that generalizes and extends existing static and dynamic risk formulations with theoretically grounded gradient estimators.
- Builds on the coherent risk foundation of Artzner et al. and extends risk-neutral policy gradients to risk-sensitive contexts.
- Major contribution: a single unified method for handling all coherent risk measures, allowing practitioners to choose problem-appropriate risk preferences.
- Numerical example (Section 6) demonstrates that different risk measures (e.g., mean-semideviation vs mean-standard-deviation) produce substantially different policy choices.
- Quality: technically sound analysis with correct proofs, gradient derivations via convex optimization and envelope theorem, and consistent sampling-based estimators.
- Clarity: well-organized and clearly written, with useful examples, but notation (e.g., ξP_θ) is confusing and should reference Theorem 6.6 in [26]; relation to robust MDP [19] and dynamic programming [24] needs clearer discussion.
- Originality: meaningful unification of prior work (CVaR, mean-semideviation) rather than trivial extension, positioning previous results as special cases.
- Significance: practical importance for finance, robotics, and other domains where managing cost variability matters; framework enables tailored risk selection.
- Strengths: unified theoretical framework, sound technical development, clear gradient formulas, consistency results, effective numerical illustration, and good exposition.
- Weaknesses: paper downplays its technical contribution as a "simple extension" and needs a more detailed comparison with robust MDP and dynamic programming approaches concerning computational advantages.
- Recommendation: solid contribution meeting conference standards; accept with minor revisions to clarify notation and strengthen related-work comparisons.
