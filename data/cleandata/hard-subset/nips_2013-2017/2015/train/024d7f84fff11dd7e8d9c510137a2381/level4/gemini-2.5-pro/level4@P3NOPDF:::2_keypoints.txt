- Proposes a framework for estimating policy gradients for the general class of coherent risk measures and derives corresponding policy gradient theorems.  
- Introduces practical actor-critic algorithms grounded in the derived theory.  
- Extends prior work by generalizing from specific risk measures (e.g., CVaR) to the broad axiomatic class of coherent risks.  
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous and technically sound.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and explanations of complex concepts are accessible.  
- Thoroughly situates the work within existing literature with appropriate citations.  
- Key contributions: (1) a principled theoretical framework for optimizing coherent risk measures via policy gradients, and (2) actionable algorithms.  
- Empirical demonstration on a financial portfolio optimization task shows learned risk-averse behaviors and framework flexibility.  
- Experiments are limited to a relatively simple static problem; applying to complex, sequential control benchmarks would strengthen claims.  
- Overall assessment: a strong, significant contribution that provides a solid foundation for future risk-sensitive RL research.
