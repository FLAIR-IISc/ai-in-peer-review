

The authors address the problem of optimizing policy parameters to minimize a general class of coherent risk measures, defined via standard properties including convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance. The approach leverages a dual representation of coherent risk measures as the convex hull of probability distributions, where the risk of a random cost variable is expressed as the maximum expected value over distributions in this hull. Under the key assumption that the boundaries of the convex hull are M-Lipschitz continuous (i.e., the maximum derivatives of the hull's boundaries are bounded), the authors derive analytical expressions for optimal policy parameters and a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) decision settings.  

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with the exposition accessible even to readers unfamiliar with risk-sensitive optimization. The main contribution—a unified analysis of a broad class of risk measures encompassing most well-known examples—represents a significant advancement. However, a more comprehensive empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm would substantially strengthen the work.  

Specific comments:  
1. The roles of $P_\theta$ and $\xi$ in the preliminaries section are unclear and require clarification.  
2. Equation (2) appears to be the standard definition of a convex envelope; its relationship to Theorem 2.1 warrants explicit justification.  
3. A discussion of how the analysis extends to continuous domains would be valuable.  
4. The role of constraint smoothness in establishing Theorem 4.2 should be explicitly noted in the short paper.  

This is a strong theoretical contribution that provides a general framework for risk-sensitive decision-making. The results are compelling, though the proofs require careful verification.
