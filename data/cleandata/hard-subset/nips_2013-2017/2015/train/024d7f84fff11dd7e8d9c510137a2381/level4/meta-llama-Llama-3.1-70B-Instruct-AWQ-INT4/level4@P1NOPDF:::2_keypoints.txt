- Proposes methods to estimate policy gradients for risk-sensitive problems using coherent risk measures and introduces RL algorithms built on these methods.
- Extends prior work from specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified framework.
- Demonstrates the approach on a toy finance example where each asset has its own return distribution.
- Motivation is clear and problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from prior work.
- Experiment is relatively simple/static but supports the algorithm’s risk-averse behavior and flexibility across risk types.
- Paper quality is high: equations are technically sound, references are comprehensive, and presentation is clear.
- Originality is sufficient: new algorithms are nontrivial extensions of related risk-sensitive planning and RL work.
- Significance: meaningful contribution to risk-sensitive RL, providing clear algorithms (policy gradient, actor-critic) for coherent risk measure optimization and demonstrating their behavior.
