- Addresses policy optimization in risk-averse MDPs for both static and dynamically consistent coherent risk measures.  
- Main contribution: derives policy gradient theorems for both settings, enabling gradient-based optimization under general risk-averse objectives.  
- Generalizes prior CVaR-specific results to a much broader class of risk mappings.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound.  
- Key strength: the generalization provides a foundational tool for risk-averse reinforcement learning.  
- Contribution is incremental but valuable, facilitating development of more practical risk-sensitive algorithms.  
- Minor: notation `\xi P_\theta` in Theorem 2.1 may be confusing; suggest aligning with Shapiro et al. conventions.  
- Minor: typo at Line 141 — "enevlope" should be "envelope."  
- Clarification requested in Section 3: explicitly state that the intractability arises from policy parameterization, not from risk measures (MDPs with Markov risk measures are tractable via dynamic programming).
