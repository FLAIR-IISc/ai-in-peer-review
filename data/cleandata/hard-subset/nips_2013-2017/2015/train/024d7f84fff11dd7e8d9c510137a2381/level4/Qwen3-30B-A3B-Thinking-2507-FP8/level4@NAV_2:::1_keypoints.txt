- Introduces a unified policy-gradient framework for coherent risk measures, generalizing prior CVaR and mean-variance work.  
- Covers both static coherent risk (sampling + convex programming) and dynamic Markov coherent risk (actor-critic approach).  
- Shows many existing risk-sensitive RL methods are special cases of the general formulation.  
- Theoretical derivations (gradient formulas) are rigorous and sampling estimators have consistency guarantees.  
- Major weakness: no empirical validation or practical demonstrations of the proposed algorithms.  
- Clarity suffers from trying to cover static and dynamic risks in 8 pages; the dynamic section is high-level and lacks algorithmic/implementation detail.  
- Recommends splitting into two papers (static with experiments, dynamic with detailed analysis) to improve clarity and empirical support.  
- Originality is strong: novel general formulation for policy gradients over the entire class of coherent risk measures.  
- Potential significance is high for risk-sensitive RL, but practical impact is unclear without experiments.  
- Acceptance arguments: substantial and novel theoretical contribution; rejection arguments: insufficient empirical evidence and inadequate detail for the dynamic component.
