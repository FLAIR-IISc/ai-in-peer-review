- Proposes a novel unified framework for computing policy gradients for the class of coherent risk measures.  
- Derives a new analytical formula for static coherent risk policy gradients, enabling computation for common risk measures.  
- Presents a general sampling-based algorithm for cases lacking analytical solutions, with consistency guarantees for gradient estimates.  
- Provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Unified derivation across the entire class of coherent risk measures is a significant and original contribution.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are available only for specific risk measures, limiting closed-form applicability.  
- The sample-based approximation scheme enables application without analytic saddle-point derivations.  
- Paper lacks empirical evidence validating the sample-based estimator’s practical viability.  
- Recommends experimental comparison between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., for CVaR) to strengthen the work.  
- Generally well-written but breadth of topics within page limits reduces clarity, especially for Section 5 on dynamic risk gradients.  
- Missing detailed algorithmic descriptions and extensive experimental evaluations hinder assessment of implementability.  
- Suggests narrowing scope (e.g., focus on static risks) or expanding to a longer journal version for fuller exposition.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, and potential high impact in finance and robust control.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical validation, limited algorithmic detail, and breadth-over-depth reducing clarity.
