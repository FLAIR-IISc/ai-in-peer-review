- Paper addresses selecting policy parameters to minimize coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).
- Uses dual representation of coherent risk as max expected value over a convex hull of probability distributions.
- Assumes the hull boundary is M-Lipschitz (bound on maximal derivatives) to enable analysis.
- Analytically derives optimal policy parameters under this assumption.
- Presents a general gradient-estimation algorithm for both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) settings.
- Paper is well written and organized; exposition is mostly understandable even to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization.
- Main contribution: unified analysis covering a broad class of risk measures, subsuming many known measures.
- Empirical evaluation is limited; a more extensive empirical study of the gradient algorithm is recommended.
- Preliminaries unclear: roles of P_{θ} and ξ need clarification.
- Question: Is Equation 2 merely the convex envelope definition, and how is it more restrictive than Theorem 2.1?
- Request to explain extension of analysis to continuous-variable distributions.
- Suggestion to state the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2 (short paper).
- Overall assessment: strong theory paper with compelling results, but proofs should be carefully verified.
