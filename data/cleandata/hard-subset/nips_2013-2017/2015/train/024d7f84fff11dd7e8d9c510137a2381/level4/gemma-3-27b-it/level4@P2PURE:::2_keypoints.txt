- Proposes methods to estimate policy gradients for risk-sensitive RL using coherent risk measures and presents corresponding RL algorithms.  
- Extends prior techniques (previously limited to specific risk measures) to the general class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified framework.  
- Demonstrates the approach on a toy financial example where each asset has its own return distribution.  
- Motivation is clear and the problem formulation and algorithms logically follow from related work.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a relatively simple, static risk problem.  
- Results indicate the algorithms achieve risk-averse behavior and are flexible across different risk types.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound, with accurate equations and complete references.  
- Presentation clarity is notably high.  
- Originality: introduces novel algorithms and a non-trivial extension to coherent risk measures.  
- Significance: makes a meaningful contribution to risk-sensitive RL, integrating policy gradients, actor-critic methods, and coherent risk optimization.
