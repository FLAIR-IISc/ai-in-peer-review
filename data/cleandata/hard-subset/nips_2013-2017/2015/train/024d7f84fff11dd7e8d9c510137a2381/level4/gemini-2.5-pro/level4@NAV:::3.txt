This paper investigates risk-averse Markov Decision Processes (MDPs), considering both static and dynamically consistent risk measures. The primary contribution is the derivation of the policy gradient's form within these two settings.

The work generalizes prior results that were limited to the Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR) measure. This generalization represents a modest but useful contribution, serving as a straightforward extension of the policy gradient from the risk-neutral case. Overall, this paper presents a solid and important extension of existing work.

The manuscript is well-structured and clearly written, making the arguments easy to comprehend. To the best of my assessment, the technical results are sound.

Minor comments:
*   **Theorem 2.1:** The notation `\xi P_\theta` may be unclear to readers. For improved clarity, I suggest aligning it with the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of reference [26].
*   **Line 141:** There is a typo: "enevlope" should be "envelope".
*   **Section 3:** To prevent misunderstanding for readers less familiar with the topic, it would be beneficial to explicitly state that MDPs utilizing Markov risk measures are generally tractable. Clarifying that the intractability in this paper's problem stems from the policy parametrization, rather than the risk measure itself, would be a valuable addition.
