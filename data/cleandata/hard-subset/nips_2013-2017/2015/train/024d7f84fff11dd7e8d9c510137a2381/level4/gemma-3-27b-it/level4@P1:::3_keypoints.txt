- Main contribution: derives gradient forms for risk-averse MDPs under both static and dynamically consistent coherent risk measures.  
- Generalization: extends prior CVaR-specific results to a broader class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified framework.  
- Core technical result: straightforward extension of the policy gradient theorem to risk-sensitive settings.  
- Writing and organization: paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow.  
- Correctness: technical arguments and derived gradient formulas appear correct.  
- Novelty assessment: useful but relatively minor/incremental contribution over existing work.  
- Numerical evaluation: illustrative but limited in scope.  
- Theoretical gaps: lacks detailed convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithms (noted as future work).  
- Minor comment 1: The notation ξPθ in Theorem 2.1 is potentially confusing; recommend using notation from Theorem 6.6 in [26].  
- Minor comment 2: Typo at Line 141: “risk enevlop” should be “envelope.”  
- Minor comment 3: Suggest clarifying that tractability issues arise from policy parametrization rather than from using coherent Markov risk measures.  
- Acceptance pros: addresses relevant RL problem, offers valuable generalization, provides correct/useful gradient formulas, and is clearly presented.  
- Acceptance cons: core result is a straightforward extension, numerical example limited, and theoretical analysis could be strengthened.
