The authors tackle the problem of determining policy parameters that minimize a specified risk measure, focusing on a broad class of risk measures defined by fundamental coherence properties such as convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance. The approach presented relies on the dual representation of any coherent risk measure as the convex hull of probability distributions, where the risk associated with a random variable is given by the maximum expected value within this hull. By assuming the family of coherent risk measures has M-Lipschitz continuous convex hull borders, the authors derive the optimal policy parameters with respect to the risk measure and propose a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static and dynamic cases.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it accessible even to readers unfamiliar with risk-sensitive optimization. The primary contribution lies in the unified analysis of a general class of risk measures encompassing most well-known measures, which is a significant achievement. However, further empirical analysis of the proposed gradient algorithm would be beneficial.

Some comments and questions include: 
1. Clarification is needed in the preliminaries section regarding the roles of P_{\theta} and \ksi.
2. It appears that Equation 2 is essentially the definition of a convex envelope; an explanation of its restrictiveness compared to Theorem 2.1 would be helpful.
3. A comment on the generalizability of the analysis to distributions on continuous variables would be appreciated.
4. Mentioning the role of the smoothness of the constraint functions in proving Theorem 4.2 would enhance the paper.

Overall, the paper presents a robust theoretical framework for risk-sensitive decision-making, with compelling results that warrant verification of the proofs.
