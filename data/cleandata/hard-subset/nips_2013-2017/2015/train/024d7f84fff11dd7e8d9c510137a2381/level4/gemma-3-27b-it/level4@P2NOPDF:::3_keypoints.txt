- Analyzes risk-averse MDPs using both static and dynamically consistent risk measures; main contribution is deriving the gradient for both settings.  
- Generalizes previous results that were restricted to CVaR—an incremental but valuable extension.  
- Results can be seen as a straightforward extension of policy-gradient methods from risk-neutral MDPs.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound.  
- Theorem 2.1: notation ξ P_θ is unclear; recommend referencing Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting that notation.  
- Line 141: typo “risk enevlop[e]” should be corrected to “risk envelope.”  
- Section 3: clarify that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally tractable and that intractability here stems from policy parametrization, not the risk measure.  
- Overall: a minor but solid and important extension providing useful generalization and clear gradient derivations.
