- Proposes methods to estimate policy gradients for risk-sensitive problems using coherent risk measures and derives corresponding RL algorithms.  
- Extends existing risk-sensitive approaches to the general class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified framework.  
- Algorithmic development is a natural extension of prior work and includes policy-gradient and actor-critic style methods.  
- Paper motivation is clear and problem formulation/algorithms follow logically from prior literature.  
- Experimental validation uses a toy finance domain with per-asset return distributions to illustrate risk-averse behavior.  
- Experiments are simple and static but support the algorithm’s ability to produce different risk-sensitive policies.  
- Manuscript is well-written, equations are technically sound, and relevant references are comprehensive.  
- Clarity is high; complex concepts are explained well with sufficient background for non-experts.  
- The extension to coherent risk measures is non-trivial and represents a meaningful original contribution.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem in risk-sensitive RL and offers a broadly applicable solution.  
- Main strength is the unified treatment of risk-sensitive RL across coherent risk measures.  
- Limitation: experimental section is limited—needs more complex scenarios and comparisons to other methods.  
- Limitation: could include more discussion of method limitations and directions for future work.  
- Overall: technically solid and significant contribution, making the paper a strong candidate for publication.
