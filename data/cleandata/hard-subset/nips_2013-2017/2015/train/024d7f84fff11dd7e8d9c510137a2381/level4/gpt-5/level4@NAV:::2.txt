This paper develops methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive settings with coherent risk measures and proposes RL algorithms based on these estimators. It generalizes existing approaches for specific risk measures to the broader class of coherent risk measures, thereby providing a unified perspective. The algorithm is illustrated with a toy example in the finance domain, where each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation is very clear, and the problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from this motivation and prior work. The experiment is relatively simple, as it focuses only on a static risk problem. However, the results appear to support the quality of the algorithm in terms of its risk-averse behavior as well as its flexibility across different risk types.

Quality: The paper is well written. The equations are technically sound, and all relevant references are provided.

Clarity: The clarity of this paper is well above average.

Originality: The paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL based on related work in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to the proposed approach is nontrivial. The level of originality seems sufficient.

Significance: Risk-sensitive RL is an interesting and important problem, and this paper makes significant contributions. It presents clear algorithms that address risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures and demonstrates the behavior of trained policies under different risk settings on a static risk problem. The proposed approach is clearly motivated by the authors and is a natural extension of existing algorithms and problems (policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization).
