- Tackles policy optimization under coherent risk measures via a unified framework using a dual representation (risk envelopes) and combines sampling with convex programming to derive gradient formulas for static and dynamic settings.  
- Generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL work (e.g., CVaR) and provides a tractable gradient estimation algorithm for a broad class of risk measures.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Primary contribution is the theoretical generalization and development of gradient formulas/algorithms for coherent risks.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor and generality, successfully extending policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Strength: derivation of dynamic-case gradients is significant and the link to robust MDPs offers useful insights and future directions.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited; needs more comprehensive experiments on benchmarks and comparisons to existing methods.  
- Weakness: preliminaries need clearer notation and explanations, particularly the roles of Pθ and ξ and how ξ weights Pθ.  
- Clarity issue: the connection between Equation 2 and Theorem 2.1 should be made more explicit, with assumptions for the tighter formulation stated.  
- Missing discussion: implications of smoothness assumptions on constraint functions for the validity of Theorem 4.2 should be acknowledged in the main text.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical paper with high potential; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address empirical and presentation concerns.
