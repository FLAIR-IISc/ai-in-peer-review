The authors analyze the risk-averse Markov decision process setting with static and dynamically consistent risk measures, providing a comprehensive examination of this topic. The main contribution of the paper is to derive the form of the gradient for both dynamic and static settings, which is a notable achievement.

The paper builds upon previous results, specifically those focused on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR), and extends them to a broader class of risk measures. While this extension may be considered minor, it represents a useful contribution to the field. The results presented in the paper can be seen as a natural extension of the policy gradient in risk-neutral settings.

The organization and writing style of the paper are clear and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers. As far as I can tell, the results presented in the paper are correct.

I have a few minor comments to suggest improvements:

- In Theorem 2.1, the notation \xi P_\theta may be confusing to some readers. I recommend adopting the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of reference [26] to maintain consistency and clarity.
- On line 141, there is a minor typo: "risk enevlop[e]" should be corrected to "risk envelope."
- In Section 3, it may be helpful to point out that Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability of the problem presented in the paper stems from the policy parametrization, rather than the risk measure itself. This clarification can help avoid confusion among readers unfamiliar with the topic.

Overall, the paper presents a solid and important extension of existing results, providing a valuable contribution to the field.
