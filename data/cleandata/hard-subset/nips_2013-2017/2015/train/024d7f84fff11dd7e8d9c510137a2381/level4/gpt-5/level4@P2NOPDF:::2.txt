This paper develops policy-gradient estimators for risk-sensitive objectives defined by coherent risk measures and builds corresponding reinforcement learning algorithms on top of them. It generalizes prior methods tailored to specific risk measures to the broader class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified framework. The approach is illustrated on a toy financial portfolio problem in which each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation is clear, and the problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from it and from prior work. The experimental evaluation is relatively simple, focusing on a static risk setting, but the results support the method’s risk-averse behavior and its flexibility across different risk specifications.

Quality: The paper is well written. The derivations appear technically sound, and the references are relevant and complete.

Clarity: The presentation is well above average, with clear notation and organization.

Originality: The paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL grounded in prior work on risk-sensitive planning and RL. Extending existing methods to handle coherent risk measures in a unified way is nontrivial, and the level of novelty is, in my view, sufficient.

Significance: Risk-sensitive RL is an important and timely topic. This work makes a meaningful contribution by presenting clear algorithms for optimizing coherent risk objectives and by demonstrating how trained policies behave under different risk settings in a static risk problem. The approach is well motivated and represents a natural extension of established techniques (policy gradient, actor-critic) to coherent risk optimization. While the empirical evaluation is limited in scope, the results are consistent with the intended risk-averse behavior and highlight the flexibility of the framework across multiple risk types.
