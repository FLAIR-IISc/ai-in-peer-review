- Presents a unified framework extending policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures in RL, not just specific instances like variance or CVaR.  
- Uses the dual representation (risk envelope as a convex set of distributions) to derive gradient formulas for static and dynamic coherent risks.  
- Key contributions: general gradient formula for static coherent risk enabling sampling-based estimation.  
- Key contributions: a convex-programming-based gradient estimation algorithm with consistency guarantees.  
- Key contributions: a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk enabling actor-critic style algorithms for dynamic risk.  
- Unifies and generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL results, showing specific previous methods are special cases.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides rigorous theoretical analysis including consistency proofs.  
- Provides a compelling numerical example illustrating how different risk preferences produce distinct policies in a financial setting.  
- Preliminaries need clarification, particularly the distinction and relationship between the policy-parameterized measure P_θ and the dual risk envelope ξ.  
- The claim that Assumption 2.2 is "more restrictive" than Theorem 2.1 is misleading; the paper should clarify that Assumption 2.2 specifies a convenient risk-envelope form for tractability, not a narrower class of risk measures.  
- Insufficient detail on extending the approach to continuous probability spaces (e.g., Lp-normed spaces) for practical applications.  
- The role of constraint smoothness in Theorem 4.2 and its connection to consistency results needs more explicit explanation.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: should include broader comparisons on standard RL benchmarks, especially for dynamic risk measures, to better demonstrate practical advantages.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution, practically implementable framework, and recommended for acceptance.
