- Proposes a unified formula for static coherent risk measures enabling analytical derivation of many policy gradients.
- Presents a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical saddle-point solutions, with consistency guarantees.
- Derives policy gradient expressions for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- Contribution is intriguing and valuable for risk-sensitive decision-making literature.
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for a subset of risk measures.
- Paper lacks empirical evidence demonstrating the practicality of the sampling-based estimation scheme.
- Recommends experimental comparisons (e.g., CVaR) between sample-based and analytical gradients to assess utility.
- Claims novelty: the general-form derived policy gradients appear to be new.
- Writing is generally good but scope is too broad for an eight-page paper, reducing clarity.
- Treatment of dynamic-risk gradients (Section 5) is high-level and lacks algorithmic detail.
- Lack of experiments and algorithmic specifics prevents judging practical applicability.
- Suggests narrowing focus to static risk measures for a more thorough, self-contained presentation and stronger empirical evaluation.
