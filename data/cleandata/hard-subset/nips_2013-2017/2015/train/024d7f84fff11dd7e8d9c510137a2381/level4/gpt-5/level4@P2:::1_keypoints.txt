- Paper develops policy-gradient methods for the full class of coherent risk measures, deriving general gradient formulas for static risks and a policy-gradient theorem for time-consistent Markov coherent risk.
- For static coherent risk, authors use the risk-envelope dual representation and the envelope theorem to recover known results (e.g., CVaR) and derive new gradients (e.g., mean-semideviation).
- When saddle points are not analytically solvable, the paper proposes a sampling-plus-convex-programming estimator and proves its consistency.
- For dynamic, time-consistent risk, the paper presents an actor–critic style approach relying on a risk-sensitive value function and ξ-weighted transition sampling, connecting to robust MDPs.
- The work unifies and extends prior risk-sensitive RL results (mean-variance, exponential utility, CVaR) into a single framework producing gradients across coherent risks.
- Strength: novel, unified, and principled derivation of policy gradients for coherent risks that is conceptually valuable.
- Strength: elegant use of risk-envelope representation simplifying and generalizing past results.
- Strength: sample-based estimator with consistency guarantees is broadly applicable when analytical solutions are unavailable.
- Strength: dynamic extension and policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk are theoretically interesting and potentially impactful.
- Weakness: analytical saddle points exist only for select risks, so practical performance depends on the sample-based estimator.
- Weakness: empirical validation of the sample-based estimator is weak or missing in the main paper.
- Weakness: dynamic-risk section is high-level, lacking algorithmic details (actor–critic steps, function approximation, sampling, computational cost) and experiments.
- Quality: theoretical development appears sound with standard assumptions, but empirical support is insufficient to assess efficiency, variance, and robustness.
- Clarity: writing is generally clear but overly compressed; Section 5 needs concrete pseudocode, complexity, and practical guidance (sampling ξ, sample counts, variance reduction).
- Originality: the unified gradient formulas and policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk appear novel and extend prior special-case results.
- Significance: framework could influence risk-sensitive RL if empirically validated on larger/continuous MDPs and compared to baselines.
- Suggestions: add empirical comparisons to analytical gradients, runtime/variance/sample-complexity studies, and ablations on sample counts and convex-solver accuracy.
- Suggestions: provide a concrete actor–critic algorithm, implementation details, experiments on benchmarks, and discussion of simulator requirements for ξ-weighted sampling.
- Suggestions: include pseudocode and computational complexity analyses for static and dynamic algorithms.
- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful, but lacks thorough experiments and algorithmic detail—recommend focusing on static risks with richer experiments or expanding to a longer format to fully cover the dynamic component.
