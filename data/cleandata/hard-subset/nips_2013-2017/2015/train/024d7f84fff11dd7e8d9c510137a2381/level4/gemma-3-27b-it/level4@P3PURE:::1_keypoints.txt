- Proposes a novel formula for computing policy gradients for static coherent risk measures, enabling analytical derivations for many common risks.  
- Presents a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions, with consistency guarantees for the policy gradient estimates.  
- Derives expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Unified derivation is an intriguing and valuable contribution to risk-sensitive decision making.  
- Analytical saddle-point solutions are limited to only certain risk measures.  
- The sample-based approximation is promising for broader applicability but lacks empirical validation.  
- Paper needs experimental comparisons between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR) to demonstrate viability.  
- Claims of originality: the general-form policy gradients appear novel.  
- Generally well-written but breadth of topics within eight pages reduces clarity.  
- Section 5 on dynamic risk gradients is high-level and misses algorithmic details and evaluations.  
- Suggests either a longer journal version or narrowing focus to static risk measures for a more self-contained, empirically thorough paper.
