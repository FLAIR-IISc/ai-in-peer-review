- Presents a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL under coherent risk measures, generalizing beyond specific measures like variance or CVaR.  
- Develops policy gradient algorithms for both static and time-consistent dynamic coherent risk measures.  
- For static coherent risk, derives a gradient formula using the convex risk-envelope representation enabling sampling-based gradient estimation via convex programming.  
- Extends the policy gradient theorem to Markov coherent risk measures and proposes an actor-critic–style algorithm combining value approximation with the static risk gradient framework.  
- Theorem 2.1 connects coherent risk measures to RL via the risk envelope; Proposition 4.4 gives a consistent sampling-based gradient estimator with convergence guarantees; Theorem 5.2 generalizes policy gradients to dynamic risk.  
- Demonstrates that prior approaches for specific risk measures (CVaR, mean-semideviation) are special cases of the unified framework.  
- Theoretical development is sound with rigorous proofs and careful treatment of technical details.  
- Main weakness: lack of empirical validation for the sampling-based gradient estimators and limited experiments (a simplistic toy portfolio example).  
- Dynamic-risk section is under-specified (actor-critic algorithm and implementation details omitted due to space), harming practical assessability.  
- Paper is highly original and significant for risk-sensitive RL, enabling flexible choice of risk measures for applications managing tail risk or variability.  
- Recommendation: focus future work/submission on static coherent risk with deeper theory and comprehensive experiments; current submission recommended for rejection due to insufficient empirical evidence.
