- Problem: finding policy parameters that minimize a general coherent risk measure over costs.

- Risk class: considers general coherent risk measures defined by convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance.

- Approach: uses dual representation of coherent risks as a convex hull of probability distributions; risk = max expected value over the hull.

- Technical assumption: the convex-hull borders are M-Lipschitz (bounded maximum derivatives).

- Results: analytically derive optimal policy parameters under the assumption.

- Algorithmic contribution: derive a general gradient estimation algorithm for both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) cases.

- Writing and accessibility: paper is well-written and organized; explanations are generally followable even for non-experts.

- Empirical concern: lacks deeper empirical analysis of the proposed gradient algorithm.

- Clarification needed: preliminaries should better explain roles of Pθ and ξ for non-expert readers.

- Equation/Theory comment: Equation 2 (convex envelope) needs clarification on why it is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1 and its role.

- Generalization: authors should comment on extending analysis to continuous distributions.

- Smoothness remark: paper should state the role of constraint-function smoothness in proving Theorem 4.2.

- Overall judgment: strong theoretical contribution that unifies many risk measures, but proofs need verification.

- Acceptance arguments: unified analysis of a broad class of risk measures; general gradient algorithm for static and dynamic settings; clear presentation.

- Rejection/weakness arguments: insufficient empirical evaluation; some sections need clarification for readability; unclear generalizability to continuous distributions.
