- Introduces policy gradient methods for risk-sensitive RL under coherent risk measures, unifying prior specific-risk approaches.  
- Derives new gradient formulas for both static and dynamic coherent risk measures.  
- Static-risk result: novel gradient formula combining sampling with convex programming.  
- Dynamic-risk result: policy gradient theorem that extends actor-critic methods to risk-sensitive settings.  
- Proves consistency of the proposed gradient estimators and shows recovery/simplification of prior results (variance, CVaR, mean-semideviation).  
- Establishes the theoretical foundation for using coherent risk measures in MDPs and decision making.  
- Experimental validation limited to a static-risk trading agent example demonstrating different strategies under different risk preferences.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and mathematically rigorous.  
- Strength: unified coherent-risk framework is a significant theoretical advance and provides practical flexibility.  
- Strength: algorithms are well-motivated and supported by rigorous proofs.  
- Strength: experiments effectively illustrate practical utility despite their scope.  
- Weakness: experiments do not include dynamic-risk problems; empirical evaluation is limited.  
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability to large state spaces are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness: lacks direct comparison to the most recent risk-sensitive RL methods.  
- Overall: technically sound, original, and significant—recommended for acceptance pending expanded empirical and complexity analysis.
