- Novel unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures; a valuable, novel contribution.
- New formula enables analytical policy gradients for many common risk measures.
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions.
- Provides consistency guarantees for the sampling-based policy gradient estimates.
- Derives expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are available only for some risk measures.
- No empirical evidence that the proposed sampling-based estimation is practical or effective.
- Missing experimental comparison between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR) to validate performance.
- Paper’s clarity is hindered by covering many topics within eight pages; dynamic risk section is presented too high-level.
- Lacks algorithmic details and comprehensive experiments, making practical viability hard to assess.
- Recommendation to split the work and focus on static risk measures to allow fuller exposition and empirical evaluation.
