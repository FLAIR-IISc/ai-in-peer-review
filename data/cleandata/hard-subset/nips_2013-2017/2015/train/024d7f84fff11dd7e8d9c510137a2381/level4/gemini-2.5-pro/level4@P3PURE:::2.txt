This paper presents methods for estimating policy gradients for risk-sensitive problems formulated with coherent risk measures, and proposes corresponding reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms. A key contribution is the extension of existing methods, which are limited to specific risk measures, to the general class of coherent risk measures, thereby providing a unified framework. The proposed algorithms are illustrated on a toy example from the finance domain, involving a portfolio of assets with distinct return distributions.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem formulation and proposed algorithms are a natural progression from previous work. The experimental evaluation is somewhat limited, as it is confined to a static risk problem. Nevertheless, the results effectively demonstrate the algorithm's ability to produce risk-averse behavior and its flexibility in handling different types of risk.

**Quality:** The paper is well-written, the mathematical derivations are technically sound, and the authors have provided all relevant citations.

**Clarity:** The clarity of the paper is well above average.

**Originality:** The paper proposes novel algorithms for risk-sensitive RL, building upon related work in risk-sensitive planning and reinforcement learning. The extension from prior work to the proposed approach is non-trivial, and the originality is sufficient for this venue.

**Significance:** Risk-sensitive reinforcement learning is an important and challenging problem, and this paper makes a significant contribution to the field. The authors present clear algorithms for risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures and demonstrate the behavior of the resulting policies under different risk settings on a static risk problem. The proposed approach is well-motivated and represents a natural extension of existing work on policy gradient methods, actor-critic algorithms, and coherent risk measure optimization.
