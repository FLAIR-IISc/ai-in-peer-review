- Main contribution: derive gradient formulas for both static and dynamically consistent risk measures in risk-averse MDPs.
- Novelty: minor but useful generalization of previous CVaR-specific results; extension is straightforward from risk-neutral policy gradients.
- Correctness and clarity: results appear correct; the paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow.
- Technical soundness: clear, concise derivations; sound use of sampling-based estimators and convex programming for gradient estimation.
- Applicability: approach demonstrated for multiple risk measures including CVaR and mean-semideviation.
- Notation issue: ξPθ in Theorem 2.1 is confusing; prefer notation consistent with Theorem 6.6 in [26].
- Tractability remark: clarify that MDPs with Markov risk measures are tractable and the intractability arises from the policy parametrization, not the risk measure.
- Strength: clear derivation of gradient formulas for static and dynamic risk measures.
- Strength: effective application of sampling-based estimators and convex programming.
- Strength: well-organized and accessible presentation.
- Weakness: confusing notation (ξPθ) in Theorem 2.1.
- Weakness: need clearer explanation about tractability of MDPs with Markov risk measures.
- Acceptance support: solid, incremental extension of existing results with demonstrated applicability and good presentation.
- Acceptance concern: contribution not highly novel—essentially a simple extension of risk-neutral policy gradients.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address notation and clarify tractability.
