This paper presents methods for estimating policy probabilities in risk-sensitive problems with consistent risk measures, and proposes RL algorithms based on them. The paper specifically extends existing methods for specific risk measures to the general class of consistent risk measures, thus providing an integrated approach. The algorithm is illustrated with a toy example in the finance domain, where each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation is very clear, and the problem formulation and algorithm are naturally derived from this motivation and previous work. The experiment is relatively simple because it is only performed on a static risk problem. However, the results seem to support the quality of the algorithm in terms of risk-averse behavior as well as flexibility in different risk types.

Quality: This paper is well-written. The equations are technically sound, and all relevant references are provided.
Clarity: The clarity of this paper is far above average.
Originality: This paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems based on risk-sensitive planning and related work on RL. The proposed approach is not straightforward in its extension. I find the originality of this paper sufficient.
Importance: Risk-sensitive RL is an interesting and important problem, and this paper makes a significant contribution. This paper presents clear algorithms that solve risk-sensitive RL with consistent risk measures, and demonstrates the behavior of trained policies with different risk settings on a static risk problem. The proposed approach is clearly inspired by the authors and is a natural extension of existing algorithms and problems (policy probability, actor-critic, and consistent risk measure optimization).
