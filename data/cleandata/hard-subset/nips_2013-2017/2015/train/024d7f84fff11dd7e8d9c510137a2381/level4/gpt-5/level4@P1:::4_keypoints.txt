- Paper studies risk-sensitive policy optimization for coherent risk measures using their dual representation as worst-case expectations over a convex risk envelope U(Pθ).

- Main contributions: (i) a general gradient formula for static coherent risks under an explicit convex-program envelope description, and (ii) a policy-gradient theorem for time-consistent Markov coherent risks with a Bellman-type decomposition.

- For static risk, shows sampling + convex-program estimators for gradients, recovering and generalizing prior cases like CVaR and mean-semideviation.

- For dynamic risk, proposes an actor-critic algorithm that approximates the risk-sensitive value and uses the new gradient for policy updates.

- Relates and unifies prior work on mean-variance, CVaR, robust MDPs, and time-consistent Markov coherent risk frameworks.

- Strength: novel, unified gradient framework for the entire class of coherent risks covering static and dynamic settings.

- Strength: elegant static gradient derivation using the envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio trick that subsumes known results with weaker assumptions.

- Strength: substantive actor-critic extension for dynamic Markov coherent risk beyond risk-specific treatments.

- Strength: careful assumptions and a consistent SAA estimator when the saddle point is not analytic.

- Weakness: empirical evaluation is minimal; lacks systematic assessment of estimator variance, sample complexity, and scalability.

- Weakness: practicality limited for model-free settings—dynamic algorithm requires a simulator and sampling multiple next states or ξ-weighted transitions.

- Weakness: preliminaries can confuse Pθ (nominal distribution) vs ξ (adversarial reweighting); dependence of U(Pθ) not always explicit.

- Weakness: assumption motivation—affine/convex envelope constraints with bounded derivatives—could be better explained and edge cases discussed.

- Weakness: finite-space restriction needs at least a sketch for continuous extensions (Lp spaces, Radon–Nikodym, SAA translation).

- Quality: technical development appears sound and builds on convex duality and policy-gradient techniques; proofs are nontrivial and merit careful verification.

- Clarity: paper is mostly clear and well organized but should disambiguate Pθ vs ξ and explicitly state the role of constraint smoothness in key theorems.

- Originality: high—gives a unified treatment and general policy-gradient formulas for coherent risks beyond prior risk-specific work.

- Significance: high—relevant to finance/OR and risk-sensitive RL; the dynamic actor-critic result is especially impactful for large MDPs.

- Specific request: clarify roles of Pθ and ξ and emphasize state dependence of ξ in the dynamic case.

- Specific request: explain why Eq. (2)’s structural/smoothness assumptions are stronger than Theorem 2.1’s existence guarantee and needed for envelope-theorem application.

- Specific request: add a short subsection/appendix sketching continuous-space extensions (Lp, absolute continuity, SAA adaptations).

- Specific request: explicitly connect bounded derivatives of constraint maps to Theorem 4.2 and the gradient derivation.

- Specific request: discuss computational overhead of solving the convex program per iteration and suggest warm-starts, and address feasibility with single-trajectory data and variance-reduction ideas.

- Pros for acceptance: unified, principled gradient framework; generalizes/simplifies prior results; solid theoretical development.

- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical work; practical constraints requiring simulators; some clarity and assumption gaps.

- Overall: strong theory paper that merits publication pending proof checks; would be improved by stronger empirical results and clearer exposition of preliminaries and assumptions.
