This paper presents methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems with coherent risk measures and proposes reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms based on them. The paper particularly extends existing methods for specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified view. The algorithm is illustrated with a toy example in the finance domain, where each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation is very clear, and the problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from this motivation and previous works. The experiment, although relatively simple as it is done in only a static risk problem, demonstrates the effectiveness of the algorithm. The results seem to support the quality of the algorithm in terms of its risk-averse behavior as well as its flexibility in handling different risk types.

The quality of the paper is high. The equations are technically sound, and all relevant references are provided. The clarity of the paper is well above average, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and understand the proposed methods.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems based on related works on risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to the proposed approach is not straightforward, indicating a significant contribution to the field. The originality of this paper seems sufficient.

The significance of the paper lies in its contribution to the important problem of risk-sensitive RL. The authors provide clear algorithms that solve risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures and demonstrate the behavior of trained policies with different risk settings on a static risk problem. The proposed approach is clearly motivated by the authors and represents a natural extension to existing algorithms and problems, including policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization.
