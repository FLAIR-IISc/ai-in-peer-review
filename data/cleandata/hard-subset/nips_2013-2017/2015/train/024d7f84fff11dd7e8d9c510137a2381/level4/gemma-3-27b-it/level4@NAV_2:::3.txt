The authors analyze the risk-sensitive Markov decision process setting with coherent risk measures, considering both static and time-consistent dynamic formulations. The primary contribution is a derivation of gradient formulas for both static and dynamic risk, enabling policy gradient methods in this more general framework.

This work generalizes previous results that focused on specific risk measures like CVaR. This extension represents a valuable, though incremental, contribution, as it broadens the applicability of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The derived results can be seen as a straightforward extension of policy gradient methods commonly used in risk-neutral settings.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow. The technical presentation is sound, and the authors appropriately connect their work to relevant literature in reinforcement learning, stochastic programming, and risk management. 

The results appear technically correct. The proofs are not included in the main text but are referenced to supplementary material, which is standard practice. 

Minor comments:
- Theorem 2.1: The notation ξPθ is potentially confusing. Referring to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting their notation might improve clarity.
- Line 141: “risk enevlop[e]” – Typographical error.
- Section 3: To avoid confusion for readers unfamiliar with the topic, it would be beneficial to clarify that MDPs with Markov risk measures are inherently tractable. The intractability of the problem addressed here stems primarily from the policy parametrization, rather than the risk measure itself.

Overall, the paper presents a solid and important extension of existing results in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper builds upon established results in coherent risk measures and policy gradient methods, extending them in a mathematically sound manner.
*   **Generality:** The work provides a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL, encompassing a wide class of coherent risk measures beyond those previously considered.
*   **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly explains the technical details.
*   **Potential Impact:** The results could facilitate the application of risk-sensitive RL to a broader range of real-world problems where coherent risk measures are preferred.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Incremental Contribution:** While valuable, the contribution is incremental rather than a radical departure from existing work. It’s an extension of known results rather than a completely new paradigm.
*   **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The numerical example is relatively simple and limited. A more extensive empirical evaluation demonstrating the practical benefits of the proposed approach would strengthen the paper.
