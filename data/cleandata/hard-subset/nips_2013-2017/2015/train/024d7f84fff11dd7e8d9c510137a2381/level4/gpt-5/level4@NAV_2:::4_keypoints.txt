- Problem: selecting policy parameters that minimize a coherent risk measure (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance, positive homogeneity).  
- Method: leverages dual representation of coherent risk as a max expectation over adversarially reweighted densities from a risk envelope.  
- Key assumption: envelope boundary sensitivity is bounded (M-Lipschitz in Pθ).  
- Theory: derives analytical expressions enabling gradient-based optimization over policy parameters.  
- Algorithms: presents general gradient estimation procedures for both static (single-step) and dynamic/sequential settings.  
- Writing: paper is well written, structured, and accessible even without deep prior exposure.  
- Main contribution: unified framework covering a wide spectrum of coherent risk measures, subsuming many standard choices.  
- Empirical gap: more extensive empirical study of the proposed gradient estimators is needed.  
- Clarification request 1: better separate roles of Pθ (policy-induced distribution) and ξ (adversarial reweighting) and indicate where each appears in gradients.  
- Clarification request 2: clarify whether Equation (2) is the finite-setting definition of a convex risk envelope and how it is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.  
- Clarification request 3: discuss extension to continuous outcome spaces, required regularity, interchange of limits/integration, and technical conditions on the envelope.  
- Clarification request 4: explicitly state how smoothness assumptions on constraint functions are used in Theorem 4.2 (envelope theorem and differentiation under the max).  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution and promising results, but proofs should be carefully checked.
