- Proposes policy gradient estimation methods and RL algorithms for the general class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified framework that generalizes prior risk-specific methods.
- Motivation is clear and the problem formulation and algorithms build naturally from prior work.
- Demonstrates the approach on a toy financial example where each asset has its own return distribution.
- Experimental evaluation is simple and static but shows the algorithm’s risk-averse behavior and flexibility across risk types.
- Paper is technically sound and well-written, with clear equations and comprehensive references.
- Theoretical contributions are solid and well-connected to existing literature.
- Clarity is above average: complex concepts are explained accessibly, organization is logical, and notation is consistent.
- Novelty: presents nontrivial extensions to coherent risk measures and new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL; originality appears sufficient for NIPS.
- Significance: meaningful contribution to risk-sensitive RL, extending policy gradient and actor-critic methods to coherent risk measures.
- Arguments for acceptance: unified framework, novel gradient and actor-critic methods, theoretical rigor, and a motivating illustrative example.
- Arguments against acceptance: experiments limited to a simple static problem, lacking evaluation in complex dynamic environments.
- Arguments against acceptance: convergence analysis of the actor-critic algorithm is relegated to the supplement and needs a concise summary in the main text.
