

This paper introduces methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning problems using coherent risk measures, and proposes corresponding RL algorithms. It extends existing techniques for specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified framework. The algorithm is demonstrated via a toy example in finance, where each asset exhibits a distinct return distribution.  

The motivation is clear, and the problem formulation and algorithms logically follow from this motivation and prior work. While the experiments are conducted in a static risk setting—limiting complexity—the results effectively demonstrate the algorithm's risk-averse behavior and flexibility across diverse risk types.  

Quality: The paper is well-structured, with rigorous technical derivations and comprehensive references.  
Clarity: The exposition is exceptionally clear.  
Originality: The paper develops novel risk-sensitive RL algorithms by extending prior work on risk-sensitive planning and RL. The generalization to coherent risk measures constitutes a non-trivial contribution, establishing sufficient originality.  
Significance: Risk-sensitive RL represents a significant and timely challenge. This work makes a meaningful contribution by presenting clear algorithms for coherent risk measure-based risk-sensitive RL, demonstrating policy behavior under varied risk settings in a static setting. The approach is well-motivated, representing a natural extension of existing methods (policy gradient, actor-critic) to coherent risk measure optimization.
