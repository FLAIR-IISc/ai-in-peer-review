- Paper studies risk-averse MDPs under static and time-consistent (Markov) risk measures and derives policy-gradient expressions for both settings.
- Main contribution: generalized policy-gradient formulas beyond prior CVaR-focused results to a broader class of risk measures.
- The derived gradients are natural analogs of the standard risk-neutral policy gradient.
- The extension is incremental but useful to the community.
- Paper is well organized, clearly written, and easy to follow.
- Results appear correct to the reviewer.
- Notation issue: ξ P_θ in Theorem 2.1 is confusing; recommend aligning with Theorem 6.6 of [26].
- Typo: “risk enevlop[e]” at line 141 should be “risk envelope.”
- Clarification suggested: explicitly note that Markov/time-consistent risk-measure MDPs are generally tractable and that intractability here arises from the policy parametrization.
- Presentation suggestion: briefly contrast derived gradients with the classical risk-neutral policy gradient to better position the contribution and clarify scope.
- Overall assessment: a minor but solid and useful extension with clear exposition and sound technical development, relevant to risk-averse RL and MDP optimization researchers.
