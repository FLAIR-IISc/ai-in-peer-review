- Introduces novel methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems using coherent risk measures.  
- Proposes corresponding RL algorithms that generalize existing approaches to a unified coherent-risk framework.  
- Demonstrates the approach with a financial toy example where assets have distinct return distributions.  
- Motivation, problem formulation, and algorithm design are clearly articulated and logically derived from prior work.  
- Experimental setup is simple and static but shows the algorithm captures risk-averse behavior and adapts to different risk types.  
- Paper is well-written with technically sound equations and comprehensive referencing; clarity is above average.  
- Originality stems from novel algorithms and non-trivial extensions of prior risk-sensitive planning and RL work.  
- Significance lies in advancing risk-sensitive RL with clear algorithms and demonstrations extending policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk optimization.
