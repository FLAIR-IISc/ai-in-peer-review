- Presents methods for estimating policy gradients for risk-sensitive problems using coherent risk measures and proposes corresponding RL algorithms.
- Extends existing risk-specific methods to a unified framework for the general class of coherent risk measures.
- Demonstrated on a simple financial-domain static risk example showing risk-averse behavior and flexibility across risk types.
- Motivation, problem formulation, and algorithms are clear and flow naturally from prior work.
- Theoretical contributions appear rigorous, equations are clear, and relevant references are cited.
- Paper is well-written and concepts are accessible to researchers familiar with RL and optimization.
- The extension to coherent risk measures is non-trivial and represents novel algorithmic contributions.
- The work is significant given the active interest in risk-sensitive RL and provides clear algorithms (policy gradient, actor-critic) for this setting.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, provides a unifying framework, has sound theory, and builds on established techniques.
- Arguments against acceptance: empirical validation is limited to a relatively simple example and could be expanded to more complex environments.
- Convergence analysis of the actor-critic algorithm is deferred to supplementary material and needs more discussion in the main paper.
- Overall, the paper focuses mainly on theoretical development and would benefit from a more extensive empirical evaluation.
