- Addresses finding policy parameters that minimize a risk measure using coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance) and their dual representation.  
- Defines risk as the maximum expected cost over a convex hull of probability distributions.  
- Introduces an assumption bounding the convex hull boundary derivatives via an M-Lipschitz continuity constraint.  
- Analytically derives optimal policy parameters under this assumption.  
- Proposes a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) decision problems.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized; arguments are followable even for readers with limited background.  
- Main contribution: unified analysis covering a broad class of risk measures, including many known examples.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; more comprehensive experiments for the gradient algorithm are needed.  
- Preliminaries lack clarity—roles of Pθ and ξ are unclear.  
- Question whether Equation 2 is just the convex envelope definition and why it’s considered more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.  
- Request to discuss extension of the analysis to continuous distributions.  
- Suggest mentioning the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2 given the concise format.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with compelling results, but proofs should be thoroughly verified.
