- Proposes a new formulation for static coherent risks enabling analytic derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Presents a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytic saddle-point solutions.  
- Provides consistency guarantees for the sampling-based policy gradient estimates.  
- Derives expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Contribution is novel: unified derivation of policy gradients in this general form.  
- Analytical solutions to the Lagrangian saddle-points exist only for a limited subset of risk measures.  
- The sampling-based scheme could broaden applicability but lacks any empirical validation in the paper.  
- No experimental comparison between sample-based gradients and analytical solutions (e.g., CVaR) is provided but would be highly informative.  
- Paper is generally well-written but attempts to cover too many topics within an eight-page limit.  
- Section on dynamic risk policy gradients is high-level and lacks algorithmic details and evaluation.  
- Recommends narrowing focus to static risk measures to allow a self-contained presentation and more thorough empirical analysis.
