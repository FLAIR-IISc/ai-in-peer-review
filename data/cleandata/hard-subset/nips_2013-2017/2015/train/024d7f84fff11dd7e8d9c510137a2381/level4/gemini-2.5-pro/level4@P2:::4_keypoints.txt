- Paper introduces a general policy-gradient framework for all coherent risk measures using their dual (risk-envelope) representation.
- Presents a novel, sample-friendly policy-gradient formula for the static case that requires solving a convex program to find the worst-case distribution and Lagrange multipliers.
- Extends the static result to time-consistent Markov coherent risk measures, deriving a policy-gradient theorem and an actor-critic algorithm for the dynamic case.
- Unifies and generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL results (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) as special cases within a simpler framework.
- Strength: high originality and significance by covering the entire class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified theoretical foundation.
- Strength: strong technical quality with an elegant application of the Envelope Theorem linking policy gradients, convex optimization, and risk theory.
- Strength: clear, well-organized exposition that builds intuition via static-to-dynamic development and illustrative examples.
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation—numerical illustration is motivational but lacks experiments on gradient estimator convergence, sample efficiency, or variance.
- Weakness: preliminaries could better clarify the relationship between the policy-induced measure P_theta and the adversarial test density xi from the risk envelope.
- Detailed concern: clarify Assumption 2.2’s tractable functional form for the risk envelope versus the general existence guaranteed in Theorem 2.1.
- Detailed suggestions: discuss extension challenges to Lp/continuous spaces, and explain smoothness assumptions used with the Envelope Theorem in the main text.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution, sound results, recommend acceptance with suggestions to strengthen empirical validation and clarify preliminaries.
