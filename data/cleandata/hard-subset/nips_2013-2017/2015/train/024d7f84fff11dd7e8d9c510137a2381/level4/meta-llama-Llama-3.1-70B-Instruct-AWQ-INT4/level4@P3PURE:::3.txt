The authors analyze the risk-averse Markov decision process (MDP) setting with static and dynamically consistent risk measures (mappings). The main contribution of this work is to derive the form of the gradient for both dynamic and static settings.

The paper generalizes previous results that focused specifically on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR). This extension represents a minor yet useful contribution. The results can be seen as a straightforward extension of the policy gradient in risk-neutral settings.

The paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow. The results appear to be correct as far as I can verify.

Minor comments:

- The notation $\xi P_\theta$ in Theorem 2.1 is confusing. I suggest referring to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting the same notation for clarity.
- On line 141, there is a typo ("enevlop[e]") that should be corrected to "envelope."
- In Section 3, it may be helpful to point out that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability of the problem in this paper stems from the policy parametrization, rather than the risk measure itself. This clarification can help avoid confusing readers unfamiliar with the topic.

Overall, the paper presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results.
