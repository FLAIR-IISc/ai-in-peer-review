

This paper introduces methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) problems using coherent risk measures, proposing novel RL algorithms based on this framework. The key contribution lies in generalizing existing approaches for specific risk measures to the broad class of coherent risk measures, thereby establishing a unified theoretical foundation. The approach is demonstrated through a finance-inspired toy example involving assets with distinct return distributions.

The motivation is compelling and logically builds upon prior work in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from this motivation. While the experiments focus on a single-stage risk problem (not dynamic sequential settings), the results effectively demonstrate the algorithm's ability to produce risk-averse policies and its flexibility across different risk types.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, with well-justified equations and comprehensive references. The writing is clear and precise.

Clarity: The exposition is exceptionally clear, making complex concepts accessible without sacrificing rigor.

Originality: The extension to coherent risk measures represents a non-trivial advancement over prior risk-sensitive RL methods. The proposed algorithms constitute a meaningful generalization, and the originality is sufficient for publication.

Significance: Risk-sensitive RL is a vital area, and this work makes a clear contribution by providing a unified framework for coherent risk measures. The authors offer well-motivated algorithms that solve risk-sensitive RL problems, clearly illustrating policy behavior under varying risk preferences in a static setting. The approach naturally extends established techniques (policy gradient, actor-critic) to coherent risk optimization, offering a valuable theoretical and practical bridge.
