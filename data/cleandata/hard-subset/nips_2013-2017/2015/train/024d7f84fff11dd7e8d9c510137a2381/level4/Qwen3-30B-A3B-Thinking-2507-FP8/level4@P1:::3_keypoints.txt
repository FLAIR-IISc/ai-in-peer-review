- Addresses risk-sensitive RL using the full class of coherent risk measures, both static and dynamically consistent.
- Presents a unified policy-gradient framework for any coherent risk measure, extending beyond CVaR and mean-variance.
- Key insight: leverages the representation theorem for coherent risk measures to derive general gradient-based methods.
- Contribution: new gradient formula for static coherent risk measures (Theorem 4.2) enabling sampling-based estimation via convex programming and likelihood ratios.
- Contribution: policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk measures (Theorem 5.2), extending the classic policy gradient to risk-sensitive settings.
- Contribution: practical algorithms — two-step sampling + convex program for static risk, and an actor-critic approach for dynamic risk.
- Shows that prior specialized results (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) are recovered as special cases of the framework.
- Technical arguments are sound, with precise mathematical exposition and supporting theorems.
- Numerical illustration demonstrates the importance of flexible risk criteria for decision-making.
- Well-written and well-organized; makes a significant and generalizable theoretical contribution to RL.
- Originality: unifies disparate risk-sensitive RL approaches under coherent risk measures and allows problem-dependent choice of risk measure.
- Main weakness: weakly developed connection to robust MDPs and the model-uncertainty interpretation of coherent risk.
- Main weakness: numerical example lacks quantitative comparisons between different risk-averse policies.
- Recommendation: well-suited for NIPS due to theoretical novelty and practical implications.
- Arguments for acceptance: general, theoretically sound framework; novel theorems; clear exposition; practical utility.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient development of model-uncertainty/robust optimization links and limited quantitative evaluation.
- Suggested fixes: clarify notation ξPθ per Theorem 6.6 of [26] and explain it when introduced.
- Suggested fixes: state explicitly that Markov risk-measure MDPs are tractable in principle but policy parametrization causes the challenge.
- Suggested fixes: briefly connect the framework to the “risk envelope” interpretation and model uncertainty in the main text.
