- Introduces a novel formula for static coherent risks that enables analytical derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Presents a sampling-based algorithm to handle coherent risk measures without analytical saddle-point solutions.  
- Provides consistency guarantees for the proposed policy gradient estimates.  
- Extends the analysis to derive policy gradients for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Unified approach to policy gradients for coherent risks is a valuable and innovative contribution.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are available only for a limited set of risk measures.  
- The sampling-based approximation is promising but lacks empirical validation of practical viability.  
- Recommends comparative experiments between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR) to assess performance.  
- Claims novelty: the general-form derived policy gradients appear to be original.  
- Paper is generally well-written but overambitious for an eight-page format, reducing clarity.  
- Dynamic risk policy gradients (Section 5) are presented too concisely with insufficient algorithmic detail.  
- Absence of experimental evaluations and algorithmic specifics limits the reader’s ability to assess practical impact; suggests a longer journal version or a tighter focus on static measures.
