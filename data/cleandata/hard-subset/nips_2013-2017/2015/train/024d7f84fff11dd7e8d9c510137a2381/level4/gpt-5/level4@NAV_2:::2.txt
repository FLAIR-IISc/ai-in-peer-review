This paper develops techniques to compute policy gradients for risk-sensitive objectives defined via coherent risk measures and builds RL algorithms on top of these gradients. It lifts prior methods tailored to particular risk metrics to the entire class of coherent risks, providing a unified framework. The approach is illustrated with a small financial example in which each asset has a distinct return distribution.

The motivation is compelling, and the problem setup and algorithms follow naturally from it and from prior work. The empirical study is relatively modest—focused solely on a static-risk scenario—but the results support the method’s risk-averse behavior and demonstrate flexibility across different risk specifications.

Quality: The manuscript is well written; the derivations are technically sound, and the related literature is cited appropriately.

Clarity: The presentation is very clear and well organized.

Originality: The paper introduces new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL grounded in prior research on risk-aware planning and RL. Generalizing to the full coherent-risk setting is nontrivial, and the contribution appears sufficiently original.

Significance: Risk-sensitive RL is an important area, and this work offers a substantive contribution. The paper presents clear algorithms for handling coherent risk measures in RL and shows policy behavior under different risk preferences in a static example. The approach is well motivated and represents a natural extension of established techniques (policy gradients, actor-critic, and coherent-risk optimization).
