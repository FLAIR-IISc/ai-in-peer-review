- Problem: determine policy parameters that minimize a broad class of risk measures (convex, monotone, translation invariant).
- Approach: use dual representation of coherent risk measures as a convex hull of probability distributions; risk = maximum expected value over this hull.
- Assumption: the convex hull borders have bounded maximum derivatives (M-Lipschitz continuity).
- Contribution: derive optimal policy parameters analytically with respect to the risk measure.
- Algorithm: propose a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static and dynamic settings.
- Presentation: paper is well-structured and clearly written, accessible to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization.
- Main novelty: unified analysis covering a general class of risk measures, encompassing most well-known measures.
- Empirical gap: further empirical analysis/experiments of the proposed gradient algorithm would strengthen impact.
- Clarification needed: clearer explanation of the roles of P_θ and ξ in the preliminaries.
- Clarification needed: Equation 2 appears to define a convex envelope; explain its restrictiveness compared to Theorem 2.1.
- Clarification needed: discuss how the analysis extends to continuous distributions.
- Clarification needed: state the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution, but proofs require verification.
