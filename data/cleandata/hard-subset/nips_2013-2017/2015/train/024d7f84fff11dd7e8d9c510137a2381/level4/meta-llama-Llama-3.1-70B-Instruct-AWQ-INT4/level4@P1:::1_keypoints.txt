- Proposes a new formula for static coherent risks enabling analytical derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Presents a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions and proves consistency of the policy gradient estimates.  
- Provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- The unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures is novel and a valuable contribution.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are available only for some risk measures, limiting closed-form applicability.  
- The sample-based approximation scheme could extend applicability to general coherent risks but lacks empirical validation.  
- No experiments compare sample-based policy gradients to analytical ones (e.g., CVaR), leaving practical viability unclear.  
- Section 5 on dynamic risk policy gradients is presented at a high level and omits algorithmic details and evaluations.  
- The paper attempts too many issues in eight pages, which harms clarity and depth.  
- Suggestion to split the work or focus on static risk measures to allow more detailed exposition and comprehensive empirical evaluation.  
- Well written overall, but lack of comprehensive experiments makes the contribution hard to judge.
