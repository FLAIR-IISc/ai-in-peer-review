- Presents a unified framework for policy gradient methods with the entire class of coherent risk measures (static and time-consistent dynamic settings).
- Provides two main theoretical contributions: a sampling-capable gradient formula for static coherent risk and a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk.
- Subsumes prior risk-sensitive RL results (CVaR, mean-semideviation, etc.) under a general, theoretically grounded framework.
- Technical development is rigorous with proofs and a consistency result for the sampling-based gradient estimator.
- Numerical example (financial trading) shows different risk measures produce qualitatively different policies, demonstrating practical utility.
- Writing and organization are excellent; concepts and examples are clearly defined and the supplement enables replication.
- Minor clarity issue: notation ξPθ in Theorem 2.1 should be clarified, possibly by referencing Ruszczyński & Shapiro (2006) Theorem 6.6.
- Originality: novel unification of risk-sensitive RL under coherent risk measures, extending beyond measure-specific prior work.
- Significance: enables flexible design of risk preferences in applications and links coherent risk to model uncertainty and robustness.
- Main weakness: contribution is mainly a generalization of existing work rather than a radical new technique.
- Suggested clarification: explicitly state that intractability stems from policy parameterization θ, not from the risk measure itself.
- Review ratings: Quality — Strong; Clarity — Excellent; Originality — Good; Significance — Good.
- Pros: unified theoretical framework, generalizes/simplifies prior results, clear numerical demonstration, well-written and technical.
- Cons: primarily a generalization (moderate novelty), does not substantially push the state-of-the-art beyond known risk measures.
- Recommendation: Accept (well-suited for NIPS; valuable theoretical contribution to risk-sensitive RL).
