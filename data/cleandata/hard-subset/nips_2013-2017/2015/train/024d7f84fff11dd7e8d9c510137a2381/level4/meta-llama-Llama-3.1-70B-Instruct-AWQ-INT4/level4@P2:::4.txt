This paper proposes a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that generalizes and extends previous results. The authors consider a general class of coherent risk measures, which is widely accepted in finance and operations research, among other fields. They develop policy gradient algorithms for both static and dynamic risk measures, and provide a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk that is convenient for approximation using sampling.

The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the explanations. The main contribution of this work is the unified analysis of a general class of risk measures that include most well-known measures. This is a significant contribution, as it provides a general framework for risk-sensitive decision-making.

The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of risk-sensitive optimization, and explain the coherence properties of risk measures. They also provide a detailed derivation of the gradient formula for static coherent risk, and show how it can be used to develop a general gradient estimation algorithm.

The paper also discusses the dynamic case, and provides a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk. The authors show how this theorem can be used to develop an actor-critic style algorithm for solving the dynamic risk problem.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a general framework for risk-sensitive decision-making. The authors show how their approach can be used to generalize and extend previous results, and provide a unified analysis of a general class of risk measures.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. The preliminaries section could be clarified, particularly with regards to the roles of Pθ and ξ. Additionally, the authors could provide more details on how the analysis generalizes to distributions on continuous variables.

Overall, this is a strong theory paper that provides a general approach to risk-sensitive decision-making. The results seem compelling, but the proofs need to be verified. With some minor revisions to address the above comments, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper provides a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning that generalizes and extends previous results.
* The authors develop policy gradient algorithms for both static and dynamic risk measures.
* The paper provides a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk that is convenient for approximation using sampling.
* The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of risk-sensitive optimization.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The preliminaries section could be clarified.
* The authors could provide more details on how the analysis generalizes to distributions on continuous variables.
* The proofs need to be verified.

Rating: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
