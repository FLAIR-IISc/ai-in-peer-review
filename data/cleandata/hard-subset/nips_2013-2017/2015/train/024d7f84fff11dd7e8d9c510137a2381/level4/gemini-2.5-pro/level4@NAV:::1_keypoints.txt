- Introduces a novel unified formulation enabling analytical derivation of policy gradients for many static coherent risk measures.
- Provides a general sample-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions, with consistency guarantees for the estimators.
- Derives expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are available only for a subset of risk measures, limiting closed-form applicability.
- The sample-based approximation potentially extends applicability to general coherent risks but its practical viability is untested.
- Major shortcoming: no empirical evidence or experiments validating the sampling scheme or comparing it to analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR).
- Section 5’s treatment of dynamic risk gradients is high-level and lacks algorithmic details and experimental validation.
- Breadth of topics hurts clarity under page limits; material on dynamics may be better suited to a longer journal paper.
- Recommend narrowing scope to static risk measures to allow deeper exposition and thorough empirical evaluation.
