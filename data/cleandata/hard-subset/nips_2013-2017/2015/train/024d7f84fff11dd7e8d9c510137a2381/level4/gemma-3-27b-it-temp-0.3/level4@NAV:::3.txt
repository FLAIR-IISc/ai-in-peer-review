The authors investigate risk-averse Markov decision processes utilizing both static and dynamically consistent risk measures. A key contribution lies in the derivation of the gradient form for both the dynamic and static cases. 

This work generalizes prior research concentrated on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR), representing a modest yet valuable advancement. The findings effectively extend the policy gradient approach commonly used in risk-neutral environments.

The paper is clearly structured, well-written, and readily comprehensible. 

To the best of my knowledge, the presented results are accurate.

Minor comments:
- Theorem 2.1: The notation \xi P_\theta is potentially unclear. I recommend aligning with the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of [26].
- Line 141: Typo – “risk enevlop[e]”. - Section 3: For readers less familiar with the field, it would be beneficial to clarify that the intractability of the problem stems from the policy parametrization, and not from the use of Markov risk measures, as MDPs *with* Markov risk measures are generally tractable.  Overall, this paper presents a minor, but sound and significant, extension to existing literature.
