- Proposes a new policy gradient formula for static coherent risk measures, enabling analytic gradients for many common risk measures.
- Presents a sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytic saddle-point solutions and provides consistency guarantees for the estimates.
- Derives policy gradient expressions for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- Makes a significant, novel contribution by unifying policy-gradient derivations across the class of coherent risk measures.
- Generalization across the coherent-risk class is a valuable advancement.
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are available only for a subset of risk measures.
- The sample-based approximation is a promising way to handle general coherent risk measures without analytic solutions.
- Lacks empirical validation of the sample-based estimation scheme in practice.
- Recommends experimental comparison between sample-based and analytic gradients (e.g., using CVaR) to demonstrate viability.
- Originality of the general-form policy gradients appears strong.
- Writing is generally good but clarity suffers from covering too many topics within the page limit.
- Presentation of dynamic risk policy gradients (Section 5) is too high-level and missing algorithmic detail.
- Absence of detailed algorithms and experiments makes practical applicability hard to assess.
- Suggests either a longer journal version or narrowing focus (e.g., static risk measures) to allow clearer presentation and fuller empirical evaluation.
- Overall promising work with generally applicable results but weakened by insufficient experimental validation; a more focused presentation would improve impact.
