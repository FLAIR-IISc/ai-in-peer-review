This paper presents a novel approach to estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems, utilizing coherent risk measures, and proposes reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms based on these methods. The authors extend existing techniques, which were previously limited to specific risk measures, to the broader class of coherent risk measures, thereby providing a unified framework. A toy example in the finance domain is used to illustrate the algorithm, where each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation behind the paper is clear, and the problem formulation and algorithms logically follow from this motivation and previous works. Although the experiment is relatively simple, as it only addresses a static risk problem, the results appear to support the quality of the algorithm in terms of its risk-averse behavior and flexibility in handling different risk types.

In terms of quality, the paper is well-written, with technically sound equations and a comprehensive list of relevant references. The clarity of the paper is well above average, making it easy to follow and understand.

The originality of the paper lies in its proposal of new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems, building upon related works on risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to the proposed approach is not straightforward, and the originality of this paper seems sufficient.

The significance of the paper is substantial, as risk-sensitive RL is an interesting and important problem. The authors provide significant contributions by presenting clear algorithms that solve risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures and demonstrating the behavior of trained policies with different risk settings on a static risk problem. The proposed approach is clearly motivated by the authors and represents a natural extension to existing algorithms and problems, including policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization.
