- Presents a unified framework for deriving policy gradients for the broad class of coherent risk measures (static and dynamic).  
- Derives novel gradient formulas that generalize existing methods focused on specific risks like CVaR.  
- Develops a sampling-based algorithm for cases where analytical solutions are intractable, with consistency guarantees for gradient estimates.  
- Situates work well within prior literature (mean-variance, VaR, CVaR, dynamic coherent risk) and notes connection to robust MDPs.  
- Theoretical results (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2) extend policy gradient and envelope theorems to coherent risk contexts.  
- Major strength is the generality of the approach across coherent risk measures.  
- Mathematical derivations are sound and the sampling-based method offers practical implementation potential.  
- Addresses dynamic risk and time-consistent policies, an important sequential decision-making concern.  
- Lacks sufficient empirical validation; numerical illustration is limited in scope.  
- Needs comprehensive experimental comparison to existing methods (e.g., CVaR) and benchmarks.  
- Sampling-based approach (Section 4.3) requires stronger empirical demonstration.  
- Dynamic risk section (Section 5) is high-level and lacks algorithmic details or experimental results.  
- Paper is generally clear but dense; rapid introduction of concepts and assumptions can challenge readers.  
- Originality is high: the unified derivation for coherent risk policy gradients is a significant novel contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand empirical evaluation or narrow scope to static risk, and clarify/expand the dynamic-risk presentation.
