- Presents a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL using coherent risk measures, generalizing beyond CVaR and mean-variance.  
- Develops policy gradient algorithms for both static and dynamic Markov coherent risk measures.  
- Provides rigorous derivations and consistency results for gradient formulas, supported by appropriate theorems.  
- Proposes a sampling-based gradient estimator combining convex programming with likelihood-ratio techniques.  
- Numerical illustration demonstrates practical effects of choosing different risk criteria in a financial application.  
- Correctly identifies tractability challenges arising from policy parametrization rather than the risk measure.  
- Exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, with accessible technical exposition and helpful supplementary material.  
- Clearly connects to prior work and shows CVaR and mean-semideviation as special cases of the framework.  
- Primary originality is conceptual generalization to coherent risks; technical approach naturally extends existing policy gradient methods.  
- Significant practical value by enabling flexible risk shaping and addressing model uncertainty in dynamic decision making.  
- Strength: unified framework that extends previous specific-risk approaches.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation and well-supported gradient formulas.  
- Strength: clear demonstration that prior results are special cases and a relevant numerical example.  
- Weakness: technical novelty is incremental rather than revolutionary.  
- Weakness: dynamic risk approach needs a simulator for multiple transitions, limiting applicability.  
- Weakness: connection to model uncertainty could be explored more deeply.  
- Pro acceptance: solid, well-executed contribution with clear practical significance and strong presentation.  
- Con rejection: contribution viewed as straightforward generalization without substantially new theoretical insights.
