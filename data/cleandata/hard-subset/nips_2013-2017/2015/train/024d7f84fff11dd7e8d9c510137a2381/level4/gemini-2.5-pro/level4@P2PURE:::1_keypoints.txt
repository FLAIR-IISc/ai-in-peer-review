- Introduces a unified framework for deriving policy gradients for static coherent risk measures, yielding analytic expressions for several common risks.  
- Novel and significant contribution: general-form policy gradient derivation for coherent risk measures.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for a subset of coherent risk measures, limiting direct applicability.  
- Proposes a sampling-based approximation algorithm with consistency guarantees to handle cases without analytic solutions.  
- No empirical validation provided for the sampling scheme; practical viability is untested.  
- Recommends experimental comparison between sample-based and analytic gradients (e.g., CVaR) to strengthen the paper.  
- Paper is generally well-written but attempts to cover too many topics within the eight-page limit, reducing clarity.  
- Dynamic (Markov) risk section is high-level and lacks algorithmic detail and experimental evaluation, hurting assessability.  
- Suggests focusing on static risk measures for the conference version to allow more detail and comprehensive empirical evaluation.
