Policy Gradient for Coherent Risk Measures

The authors consider the problem of finding policy parameters that minimize a given risk measure. The class of risk measures considered in the paper is general, and defined by simple coherence properties such as convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance. The presented approach is based on a dual representation of any coherent risk measures as a convex hull of probability distributions. The risk of a random variable (such as a cost) is given by the maximum expected value in the hull.

The paper makes an assumption on the family of coherent risk measures, by bounding the maximum derivatives of the convex hull's borders (i.e., the borders of the hull are M-Lipschitz continuous). Using this assumption, the authors analytically derive the optimal policy parameters (w.r.t the risk measure), as well as a general gradient estimation algorithm for both the static case (one-step decision), and the dynamic (or sequential) case.

The paper is well-written and organized. Although I am not familiar with risk-sensitive optimization, I was able to follow most of the explanations. The main contribution of this work is the unified analysis of a general class of risk measures that include most well-known measures. I believe that this is a significant contribution.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The proofs of the theorems are not included in the main paper, but are instead relegated to the supplementary material. However, based on the explanations provided, the results seem compelling.

Clarity: The paper is well-written, and the authors do a good job of explaining the technical details of their approach. However, there are a few places where the text could be improved for clarity. For example, the preliminaries section could be expanded to provide more background information on risk-sensitive optimization.

Originality: The paper presents a novel approach to risk-sensitive decision-making, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their contributions. The use of a dual representation of coherent risk measures is a new and interesting approach, and the authors do a good job of explaining how their results generalize to different types of risk measures.

Significance: The paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of risk-sensitive decision-making. The authors provide a unified analysis of a general class of risk measures, which could be useful in a wide range of applications. The results of the paper could also be used to improve the performance of existing risk-sensitive decision-making algorithms.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to risk-sensitive decision-making.
* The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their contributions.
* The results of the paper have the potential to make a significant impact in the field.
* The paper is well-written and organized.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
