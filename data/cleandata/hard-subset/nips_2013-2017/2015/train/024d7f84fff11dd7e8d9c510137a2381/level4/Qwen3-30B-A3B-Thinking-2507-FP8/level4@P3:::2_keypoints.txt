- Presents a unified framework for policy gradients in risk-sensitive RL covering the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Derives gradient formulas for static (Theorem 4.2) and dynamic Markov coherent risk (Theorem 5.2) using the representation theorem.  
- Proposes a convex-programming-based sampling estimator for static risk and a novel actor-critic algorithm for dynamic risk.  
- Shows prior results (CVaR, mean-variance) as special cases of the unified framework.  
- Numerical example (financial asset allocation) illustrates risk-shaping flexibility but is limited to the static-risk algorithm.  
- Technical derivations are rigorous, leveraging convex optimization and the envelope theorem.  
- Provides a consistency proof (Proposition 4.4) for sampling-based estimation.  
- Experimental validation is limited: dynamic actor-critic is not evaluated and no comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Clarity is high: well-organized exposition, readable math, and adequate supplementary material for reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity gaps: need brief discussion of computational complexity for the convex step and clearer motivation for risk-envelope assumptions.  
- Original and conceptually significant: identifies a common gradient estimation structure across all coherent risk measures.  
- High practical significance for finance, operations, and safety-critical systems due to generality and theoretical grounding.  
- Pros: theoretical unification, novel gradient formulas, practical sampling estimators, rigorous assumptions.  
- Cons: limited empirical scope (static-only), lack of baseline comparisons, unclear scalability of convex-programming step.  
- Overall recommendation: significant, high-quality contribution worthy of acceptance pending stronger empirical evaluation.
