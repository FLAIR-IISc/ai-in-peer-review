- Proposes a unified policy-gradient formula for static coherent risk measures, enabling analytic gradients for many common risks.
- Introduces a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytic saddle-point solutions.
- Provides consistency guarantees for the sampling-based policy-gradient estimates.
- Extends results to derive expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- Novelty: the general-form derived policy gradients appear to be a new contribution to the literature.
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are available only for some risk measures, limiting closed-form applicability.
- No empirical evidence is provided that the sampling-based estimation scheme is practical or effective.
- Experimental evaluation is very limited (basic toy example), hindering assessment of real-world viability.
- Dynamic risk policy-gradient section is presented at a high level with insufficient algorithmic detail.
- Paper attempts to address too many issues within eight pages, reducing clarity and depth.
- Suggestion: split the work or focus on static risk measures to allow more detail and comprehensive experiments.
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, unified derivation, general sampling algorithm, and consistency guarantees.
- Rejection arguments: lack of empirical validation, limited experiments, and clarity issues due to scope.
