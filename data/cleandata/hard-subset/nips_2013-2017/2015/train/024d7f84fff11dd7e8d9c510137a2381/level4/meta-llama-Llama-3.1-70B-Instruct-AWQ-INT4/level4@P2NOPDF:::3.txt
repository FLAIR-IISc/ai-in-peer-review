The authors analyze the risk-averse Markov decision process (MDP) setting with both static and dynamically consistent risk measures, providing a comprehensive examination of the topic. The primary contribution of the paper is the derivation of the gradient form for both dynamic and static settings, which represents a valuable extension of existing results.

This work builds upon previous studies that focused specifically on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR) and generalizes those findings to a broader class of risk measures. While this extension may be considered minor, it is nonetheless a useful contribution to the field. The results presented in the paper can be seen as a straightforward extension of policy gradient methods in risk-neutral settings.

The organization and writing style of the paper are clear and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers. Upon reviewing the content, the results appear to be correct.

Some minor suggestions for improvement are noted below:

- In Theorem 2.1, the notation \xi P_\theta may be confusing to some readers. It may be beneficial to refer to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopt a similar notation to maintain consistency.
- On line 141, there is a typo ("enevlop[e]") that should be corrected to "envelope."
- In Section 3, it may be helpful to provide additional context for readers unfamiliar with the topic. Specifically, it would be beneficial to note that Markov decision processes with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability of the problem presented in the paper stems from the policy parametrization, rather than the risk measure itself. This clarification can help avoid confusion and provide a clearer understanding of the paper's contributions.

Overall, the paper presents a solid and important extension of existing results, providing a valuable contribution to the field of risk-averse MDPs.
