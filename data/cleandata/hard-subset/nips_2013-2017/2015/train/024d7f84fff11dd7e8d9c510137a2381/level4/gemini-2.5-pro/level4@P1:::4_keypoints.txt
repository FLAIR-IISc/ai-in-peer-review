- Presents a unified policy-gradient framework for reinforcement learning under coherent risk measures using the dual (risk envelope) representation.  
- Leverages the dual representation to derive novel gradient formulas for static and time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk measures.  
- Proposes a sampling + convex programming algorithm for the static case and an actor-critic style algorithm for the dynamic case.  
- Primary strength: strong generality that subsumes prior risk-sensitive RL methods tailored to specific measures.  
- Theoretical contribution: elegant connection between the dual risk representation and the Envelope Theorem for gradient derivation.  
- Paper is well-written and provides a compelling theoretical foundation for broad risk-sensitive problems.  
- Main weakness: limited empirical evaluation; lacks deeper analysis of gradient estimators’ convergence, sample efficiency, or variance.  
- Suggestion: compare empirical properties (convergence, sample complexity, variance) against specialized methods like CVaR.  
- Comment: clarify early that the test density ξ is an adversarial re-weighting of P_θ from the risk envelope.  
- Comment: explain whether Assumption 2.2’s convex program structure restricts the class of coherent risk measures or is fully general on finite spaces.  
- Comment: discuss challenges and necessary modifications for extending results to continuous outcome spaces.  
- Comment: briefly state in the main text how smoothness assumptions in Assumption 2.2 enable the Envelope Theorem application used in the proof.  
- Overall summary: strong theoretical paper that substantially generalizes risk-sensitive policy gradients and is a clear candidate for acceptance despite limited experiments.
