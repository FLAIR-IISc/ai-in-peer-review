- Paper proposes a unified framework for deriving policy gradients for static coherent risk measures, gives analytical solutions for some metrics, a sampling-based algorithm with consistency guarantees, and an extension to Markov dynamic risk measures.  
- The unified derivation is a significant and novel theoretical contribution to risk-sensitive RL.  
- Analytical results are limited to risk measures where Lagrangian saddle-points can be solved explicitly.  
- The sampling-based scheme is promising but the paper provides no empirical validation of its practical viability.  
- Crucially missing are experiments comparing sample-based gradients to analytical solutions (e.g., CVaR) across problems — a major weakness.  
- The general form of the derived policy gradients appears original compared to prior work.  
- Writing is generally clear but the paper suffers from scope inflation within eight pages, creating a disjointed narrative.  
- Section 5 (dynamic risk) is presented too high-level, lacking algorithmic details and experimental validation, preventing assessment of practicality.  
- The single toy example is insufficient to support the claims or demonstrate usefulness.  
- Reviewer recommends focusing the paper exclusively on static coherent risk measures to allow deeper theory and comprehensive experiments.  
- Suggested revisions: thorough empirical evaluation (analytical vs sampling across key risks and domains), detailed algorithmic/implementation details for the sampling scheme, and a clearer, more cohesive narrative.  
- Current submission is conceptually interesting but unverified in practice; requires substantial revision before suitability for publication.
