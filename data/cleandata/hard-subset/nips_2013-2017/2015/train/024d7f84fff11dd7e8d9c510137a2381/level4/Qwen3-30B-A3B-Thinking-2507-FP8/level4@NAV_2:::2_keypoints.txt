- Introduces a unified policy gradient framework for risk-sensitive RL that covers the full class of coherent risk measures.
- Provides static and dynamic formulations: sampling plus convex programming for static risk, and an actor-critic style algorithm with value approximation for dynamic risk.
- Derives gradient formulas using the risk envelope representation, unifying and generalizing prior special-case results (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance).
- Theory is rigorous, with well-constructed theorems and supporting proofs.
- Experimental validation is limited to a static risk problem but demonstrates flexible selection of risk measures and risk-averse policy behavior.
- Builds naturally on prior NIPS work and clearly positions the contribution relative to CVaR and mean-variance studies.
- Related work section effectively contextualizes the paper and shows how existing results are special cases.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and mathematically accessible to experts; supplementary material supports reproducibility.
- Novelty lies in extending policy gradient methods to all coherent risk measures and deriving gradients via the risk envelope approach.
- The dynamic extension via Markov coherent risk is a meaningful advance beyond prior work.
- Significant practical relevance for domains requiring risk management (finance, operations research, etc.).
- Pro accept points: unified framework, rigorous theory, clear links to prior work, explicit algorithms, and illustrative numerical example.
- Con accept points: experimental validation focuses only on a static problem and the numerical example is relatively simple.
- Overall assessment: valuable, technically strong, clearly presented work with minor experimental limitations.
