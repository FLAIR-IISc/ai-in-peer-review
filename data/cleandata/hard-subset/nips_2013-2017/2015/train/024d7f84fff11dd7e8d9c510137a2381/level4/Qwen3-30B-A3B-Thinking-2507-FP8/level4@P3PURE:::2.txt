

This paper presents novel methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning problems using coherent risk measures, along with corresponding RL algorithms. The work provides a significant unification by extending existing techniques for specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, establishing a cohesive theoretical framework. The algorithm is demonstrated through a finance domain toy example, where each asset exhibits a distinct return distribution.

The motivation is clearly articulated, and the problem formulation, along with the proposed algorithms, logically follows from this motivation and builds naturally upon prior work. While the experimental evaluation is limited to a static risk problem, the results robustly demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness in achieving risk-averse behavior and its flexibility across diverse risk preferences.

Quality: The paper is exceptionally well-written, with technically sound equations and comprehensive references.

Clarity: The clarity is outstanding, significantly exceeding the average for the venue.

Originality: The paper introduces new risk-sensitive RL algorithms grounded in related work on risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to the general class of coherent risk measures represents a non-trivial contribution, and the originality is sufficient for publication.

Significance: Risk-sensitive RL constitutes a critical and timely research area. This paper makes substantial contributions by providing clear, implementable algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures. It effectively demonstrates how trained policies adapt to different risk settings within a static risk problem, offering a natural and well-motivated extension to existing approaches in policy gradient methods, actor-critic frameworks, and coherent risk measure optimization.
