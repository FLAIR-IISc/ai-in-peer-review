- Presents a unified policy-gradient framework for the full class of coherent risk measures using their dual representation and the Envelope Theorem (Theorem 4.2).
- Derives analytical gradient formulas for specific risk measures (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) and gives a general sampling-based SAA algorithm when no closed-form exists.
- Extends the framework to dynamic, time-consistent Markov coherent risk measures and proposes an actor-critic algorithm for the dynamic case.
- Strength: principled, axiomatically justified unification that connects and generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL results.
- Strength: the main gradient derivation is novel and provides a powerful, general theoretical tool for risk-sensitive optimization.
- Major weakness: the general SAA-based gradient estimator is not empirically validated; only a motivating numerical example is provided.
- Lacks experimental comparison between the SAA estimator and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR) to demonstrate practical viability, sample efficiency, and convergence.
- Scope is overly ambitious for an 8-page conference paper, causing the dynamic-risk contributions to be compressed and underdeveloped.
- The actor-critic algorithm for dynamic risk is presented at a high level with crucial implementation and analysis details deferred to a “full paper” version.
- Clarity suffers from dense presentation and insufficient algorithmic/experimental detail, making reproducibility and assessment of the dynamic case difficult.
- Recommendation: narrow the paper to static coherent risk measures to allow fuller theoretical exposition and comprehensive empirical evaluation of the SAA estimator.
- Arguments for acceptance: original, elegant theoretical contribution that generalizes and simplifies many previous results.
- Arguments against acceptance: central algorithmic proposal lacks empirical proof-of-concept; breadth prevents adequate treatment of the dynamic setting.
