- Tackles policy parameter optimization to minimize a specified coherent risk measure.  
- Focuses on coherent risk measures characterized by convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance.  
- Uses the dual representation: risk as the maximum expected value over a convex hull of probability distributions.  
- Introduces an M-Lipschitz assumption bounding derivatives of the convex hull’s boundaries.  
- Leveraging that assumption, derives analytical solutions for optimal policy parameters under the risk measure.  
- Develops a general gradient-estimation algorithm applicable to both single-step (static) and sequential (dynamic) decision problems.  
- Paper is clearly written and logically structured; explanations are accessible even to non-experts.  
- Main strength is a unified framework covering a broad class of risk measures, including many existing ones.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; more extensive experiments would strengthen the contribution.  
- Request for clearer explanation of the roles of P_theta and ksi in the preliminaries.  
- Seeks clarification why Equation 2 (convex envelope) is presented as more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.  
- Suggests discussion on extending analysis to continuous distributions and noting the impact of constraint smoothness on Theorem 4.2’s proof.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with promising results, but recommends thorough verification of proofs.
