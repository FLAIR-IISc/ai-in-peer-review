- Develops policy-gradient methods for coherent risk objectives using the dual (risk-envelope) representation for static and time-consistent dynamic settings.

- Static result: envelope-theorem-based gradient formula combining likelihood-ratio sampling with a convex program whose KKT multipliers enter the gradient; recovers CVaR and mean-semideviation and supports SAA.

- Dynamic result: policy-gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and an actor-critic method where the critic estimates a robust value function and the actor updates under reweighted dynamics.

- Technical quality: careful and elegant derivations, especially the static Lagrangian/saddle-point approach; some proofs deferred and not fully verified by reviewer.

- Empirical evaluation: limited and small-scale; needs more experiments to assess variance, bias, and sample efficiency.

- Clarity: generally readable but preliminaries should better distinguish Pθ (nominal distribution) from ξ (adversarial reweighting) and state where smoothness assumptions are used.

- Originality: novel unified treatment of the whole class of coherent risks that subsumes prior specific-risk results.

- Significance: likely impactful for risk-sensitive and robust RL, though practical requirement of multiple next-state samples per state is a limitation in some domains.

- Strengths: unified framework, duality-based practical estimators, recovers known cases, principled actor-critic connection to robust MDPs.

- Weaknesses: sparse empirical validation, potentially restrictive smoothness/derivative and sampling assumptions, and some notational/preliminaries clarity issues.

- Specific comment 1: Clarify roles of Pθ (policy-induced nominal distribution) vs. ξ (adversarial reweighting) with a short illustrative example.

- Specific comment 2: Explain why the canonical constraint form in Eq.(2) isn’t unduly restrictive and show examples (CVaR, spectral risk, mean-semideviation) that fit it.

- Specific comment 3: Explicitly state where differentiability/bounded-sensitivity of constraint functions are used (envelope theorem, interchange of limits, SAA consistency) and discuss failure modes.

- Specific comment 4: Summarize required modifications for continuous-state/action spaces (densities vs mass functions, measurability, compactness of envelopes, SAA conditions, handling unbounded support).

- Specific comment 5: Discuss practical workarounds when only single-trajectory rollouts are available (importance sampling, off-policy corrections, kernel estimators) and their variance impact.

- Specific comment 6: Expand experiments to compare closed-form vs. SAA gradient estimators and different risk envelopes in dynamic actor-critic to evaluate variance, bias, and sample efficiency.

- Pros for acceptance: strong, general theoretical framework with clear algorithms and high potential impact.

- Cons for acceptance: minimal empirical validation and some technical/practical assumptions that limit immediate applicability.

- Overall recommendation: Strong theory paper; recommend acceptance conditional on clarifying preliminaries, smoothness assumptions, continuous-space guidance, and careful proof checking.
