This paper presents methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems with coherent risk measures, and proposes reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms based on them. The paper particularly extends the existing methods for specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified view. The algorithm is illustrated with a toy example in the finance domain, where each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation is very clear, and the problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from this motivation and previous works. The experiment is relatively simple, as it is done in only a static risk problem. However, the results seem to support the quality of the algorithm in terms of its risk-aversive behavior as well as the flexibility in different risk types.

Quality: The paper is well-written, and the equations are technically sound. All relevant references are given, demonstrating the authors' thorough understanding of the field.

Clarity: The clarity of this paper is well above average. The authors have done an excellent job in explaining the concepts, and the paper is easy to follow.

Originality: The paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems based on related works on risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to the proposed approach is not straightforward. The originality of this paper seems sufficient.

Significance: Risk-sensitive RL is an interesting and important problem, and this paper provides significant contributions. This paper presents clear algorithms that solve risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures and demonstrates the behavior of trained policies with different risk settings on a static risk problem. The proposed approach is clearly motivated by the authors and is a natural extension to existing algorithms and problems (policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization).

Arguments For Acceptance:
- The paper provides a unified view of risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures.
- The proposed algorithms are well-motivated and clearly explained.
- The experiment demonstrates the quality of the algorithm in terms of its risk-aversive behavior and flexibility in different risk types.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
- The experiment is relatively simple, and it would be beneficial to see more complex experiments or real-world applications.
- Some readers may find the technical details and equations challenging to follow, although the authors have done an excellent job in explaining the concepts.

Overall, I believe that this paper is a valuable contribution to the field of RL and risk-sensitive optimization. The authors have presented a clear and well-motivated approach to solving risk-sensitive RL problems with coherent risk measures, and the experiment demonstrates the quality of the algorithm. I recommend accepting this paper.
