The authors analyze risk-averse Markov decision processes (MDPs) using both static and dynamically consistent risk measures. The paper's main contribution is deriving the form of the policy gradient for both the static and dynamic settings. This work generalizes previous results that focused specifically on the Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR) measure. While the result is a relatively straightforward extension of the policy gradient from the risk-neutral setting, this generalization represents a minor but solid and useful contribution.

The paper is well organized, well written, and easy to follow. The technical results appear to be correct.

**Minor Comments:**

*   **Theorem 2.1:** The notation \xi P_\theta may be confusing. For improved clarity, I suggest the authors refer to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and consider adopting a similar notation.
*   **Line 141:** There is a typo: "enevlope" should be "envelope".
*   **Section 3:** To avoid potential confusion for readers less familiar with this topic, it would be helpful to clarify that the problem's intractability stems from the policy parametrization, not the risk measure itself. The authors could point out that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally tractable; this clarification would help to frame the paper's contribution more precisely.
