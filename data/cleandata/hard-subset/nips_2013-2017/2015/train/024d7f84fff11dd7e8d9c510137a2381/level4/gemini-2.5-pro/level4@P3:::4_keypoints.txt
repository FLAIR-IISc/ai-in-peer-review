- Proposes a general framework for risk-sensitive policy gradient methods covering all coherent risk measures via their dual risk-envelope representation.  
- Derives a policy gradient formula and a sample+convex-program estimator for the static risk (total cost) setting.  
- Extends to time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk measures and provides a risk-sensitive policy gradient theorem plus an actor-critic algorithm for sequential problems.  
- Unifies and generalizes prior specific-risk results (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) as special cases of the framework.  
- Strength: high significance and originality by providing a unified, general theoretical framework for risk-sensitive RL.  
- Strength: strong technical development using the dual representation and Envelope Theorem, with sound derivations (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2).  
- Strength: clear writing and organization, with illustrative examples that aid accessibility.  
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation—experiments are minimal (one-step problem) and lack comparisons (e.g., general estimator vs analytical CVaR estimator) and complex sequential tasks.  
- Weakness: preliminaries need clearer explanation of roles of Pθ vs ξ (risk-envelope densities), ideally with a simple illustrative example.  
- Weakness: need clarification on Assumption 2.2—how restrictive its affine/convex differentiable constraint form is and whether it excludes common coherent risks.  
- Weakness: paper focuses on finite probability spaces; should briefly discuss challenges/extensions to continuous state/action spaces.  
- Weakness: should explicitly connect smoothness assumptions to the applicability of the Envelope Theorem in the main text.  
- Conclusion: strong and elegant theoretical contribution warranting acceptance, but would benefit from expanded experiments and clearer exposition on assumptions and preliminaries.
