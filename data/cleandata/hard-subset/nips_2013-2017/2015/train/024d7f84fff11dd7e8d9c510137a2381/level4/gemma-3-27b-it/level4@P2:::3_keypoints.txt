- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures for risk-sensitive MDPs, covering static and dynamically consistent formulations.  
- Main contribution: derivation of gradient formulas generalizing prior CVaR-focused work.  
- Leverages duality representation of coherent risk, connecting to stochastic programming and enabling sampling-based gradient estimation.  
- Builds on and generalizes established risk-sensitive RL results; provides a unifying, flexible framework.  
- Gradient formulas are clear and are a natural extension of risk-neutral policy gradients.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and accessible to RL and optimization readers.  
- Mathematical derivations appear sound and logically presented.  
- Strength — Generality: covers entire class of coherent risk measures.  
- Strength — Theoretical soundness: rigorous derivations grounded in convex optimization and stochastic programming.  
- Strength — Clear presentation and effective literature positioning.  
- Weakness — Incremental contribution: extension is useful but not groundbreaking.  
- Weakness — Scalability concerns: computational challenges for large state spaces and reliance on function approximation need more discussion.  
- Weakness — Implementation details lacking: actor-critic algorithm and function approximation/sampling specifics are under-specified.  
- For acceptance: technically sound, useful gradient tools, clear writing, tackles relevant problem.  
- Against acceptance: incremental impact, scalability issues not fully addressed, and limited reproducibility due to missing implementation details.
