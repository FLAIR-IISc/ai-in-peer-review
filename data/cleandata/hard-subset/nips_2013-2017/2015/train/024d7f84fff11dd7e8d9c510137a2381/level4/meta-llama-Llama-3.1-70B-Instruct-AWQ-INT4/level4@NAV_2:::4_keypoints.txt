- Paper studies optimizing policy parameters under a broad class of coherent risk measures defined by convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance.  
- Approach uses the dual representation of coherent risk measures as a convex hull of probability distributions, where risk equals the maximum expected value over the hull.  
- Authors assume the convex-hull borders are M-Lipschitz (bounded maximum derivatives) to enable analysis.  
- Under this assumption they derive analytic optimal policy parameters and a general gradient-estimation algorithm for both one-step (static) and sequential (dynamic) problems.  
- Presentation is well-written, organized, and accessible even to readers not expert in risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Main contribution is a unified analysis covering many well-known risk measures, which is significant and novel.  
- Theoretical development uses convex analysis and duality appropriately; proofs are in the supplement and appear correct but need verification.  
- Reviewer requests deeper empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.  
- Preliminaries section is unclear—specifically the roles of P_{\theta} and \xi need clarification.  
- Question whether Equation 2 is merely the convex envelope definition and how it relates to Theorem 2.1 (potential redundancy/restriction).  
- Suggest adding discussion on extending the analysis to continuous distributions.  
- Recommend briefly noting the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2.  
- Paper assumes familiarity with convex analysis/duality, which may limit accessibility for some readers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, novel theoretical contribution; technically sound; broad implications for risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Arguments for rejection: needs stronger empirical validation; some clarity issues in preliminaries; potential accessibility concerns.
