- Addresses policy parameter optimization under general risk measures for decision-making.
- Presents a unifying framework for coherent risk measures defined by convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance.
- Leverages the dual representation of coherent risk as the convex hull of probability distributions (risk = max expected value within hull).
- Provides analytical derivation of optimal policy parameters with respect to chosen risk measures.
- Proposes a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) problems.
- Imposes a Lipschitz continuity constraint on convex-hull boundaries to enable tractable analysis.
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and accessible despite reviewer’s limited prior exposure to the topic.
- Strength lies in generality and unified analysis across many established risk measures; theoretical results are compelling.
- Main weakness: insufficient empirical evaluation—needs benchmarks and comparisons to existing risk-sensitive/robust methods.
- Preliminaries need clarification, specifically the roles and intuition for P_θ and ξ.
- Clarify why Equation 2 (convex envelope) is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1 to avoid confusion.
- Discuss generalization to continuous-variable distributions and required modifications.
- Explain the role of smoothness in constraint functions and its connection to the proof of Theorem 4.2 (existence/properties of gradients).
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical paper; accept contingent on addressing comments, expanding empirical validation, and verifying proofs.
