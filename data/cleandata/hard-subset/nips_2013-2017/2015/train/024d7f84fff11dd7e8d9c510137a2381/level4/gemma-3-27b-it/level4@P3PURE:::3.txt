The authors analyze the risk-averse Markov decision process setting with both static and dynamically consistent risk measures (mappings). The main contribution is, in particular, to derive the form of the gradient for both the dynamic and static settings. 

This paper generalizes previous results, which focused specifically on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR). This extension represents a minor, but useful, contribution, as the results are a straightforward extension of policy gradient methods in risk-neutral settings.

The paper is well organized, well written, and easy to follow. 

The results appear correct.

Minor comments:

- Theorem 2.1: The notation \xi P_\theta is confusing. I suggest referring to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting the same notation.
- Line 141: “risk enevlop[e]” – Section 3: To avoid confusing readers unfamiliar with the topic, it may be worthwhile to point out that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally very tractable. The intractability of the problem addressed in this paper stems from the policy parametrization, and not from the risk measure itself. The paper presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results.
