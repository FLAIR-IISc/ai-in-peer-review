- Introduces a unified formulation for static coherent risk measures yielding closed-form policy gradients for many common risks.  
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical saddle-point solutions.  
- Establishes consistency guarantees for gradient estimates produced by the sampling-based method.  
- Derives policy-gradient expressions for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- The unified derivation is a novel and valuable contribution to risk-sensitive decision making.  
- Closed-form analytical solutions exist only for a subset of risk measures.  
- No empirical evidence is provided to show the practicality of the sampling-based estimator.  
- Recommends experimental comparison between sampling-based and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR) across multiple problems.  
- Paper is generally well written but covers many topics in eight pages, harming clarity.  
- Dynamic-risk policy gradients are presented at a high level with missing algorithmic details and evaluation.  
- Suggests narrowing scope to static risk measures or expanding to a journal article to allow more detail and comprehensive experiments.
