- Paper analyzes risk-averse MDPs using both static and dynamically consistent risk measures.
- Main contribution: derivation of the policy gradient form for both dynamic and static risk settings.
- Generalizes prior CVaR-specific results to a broader class of risk measures.
- Contribution is minor but useful, largely a straightforward extension of risk-neutral policy gradient methods.
- Paper is well organized, clearly written, and easy to follow.
- Results appear to be correct.
- Notation issue: "\xi P_\theta" in Theorem 2.1 is potentially confusing; recommend referencing Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting that notation.
- Typo: "risk enevlop[e]" at line 141.
- Suggestion: clarify that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally tractable and that intractability here stems from policy parametrization, not the risk measure.
- Overall assessment: minor but solid and important extension of existing results.
