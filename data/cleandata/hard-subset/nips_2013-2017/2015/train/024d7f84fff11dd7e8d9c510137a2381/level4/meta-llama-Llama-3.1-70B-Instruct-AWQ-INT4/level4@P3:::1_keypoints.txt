- Proposes a unified policy-gradient approach for the class of coherent risk measures and derives a new gradient formula that generalizes variance and CVaR results.  
- Presents a sampling-based algorithm to estimate gradients for static coherent risk measures.  
- Provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Novel and technically sound contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making literature.  
- Sampling scheme enables application without closed-form saddle points.  
- Lacks empirical evidence demonstrating the estimation scheme’s practical viability.  
- Dynamic risk-gradient section is high-level and omits algorithmic details and experiments.  
- Paper attempts multiple objectives, which reduces clarity and makes practical assessment difficult.  
- Recommends focusing on static risk measures for deeper empirical evaluation to improve clarity.  
- Alternatively, recommends expanding details and experiments for dynamic risk gradients or splitting the paper.
