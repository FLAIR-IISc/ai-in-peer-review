- Introduces novel methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive RL using coherent risk measures and presents corresponding RL algorithms.  
- Systematically extends prior work on specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, creating a unified framework.  
- Builds algorithms based on established approaches (policy gradient, actor-critic) and coherent risk optimization.  
- Demonstrates the approach on a finance-domain toy example with assets having distinct return distributions.  
- Motivation and problem formulation are clearly articulated and logically derived from existing literature.  
- Experiments are limited to a static risk problem but effectively show risk-averse behavior and adaptability across different risk types.  
- Paper is well-written, with technically sound equations and comprehensive references.  
- Exposition and clarity are excellent, exceeding average standards.  
- The generalization to coherent risk measures is a non-trivial, original contribution.  
- Work is significant and timely, providing clear algorithms and empirical validation for risk-sensitive RL despite limited experimental scope.
