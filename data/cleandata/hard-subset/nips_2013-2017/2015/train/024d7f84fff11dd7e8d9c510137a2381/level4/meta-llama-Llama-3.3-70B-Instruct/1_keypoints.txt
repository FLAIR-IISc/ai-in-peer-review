- Introduces a novel formula for static coherent risks that enables analytical derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.
- Proposes a sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions, with consistency guarantees for policy gradient estimates.
- Derives expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- Unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures is a valuable and innovative contribution.
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are available only for specific risk measures.
- The sample-based approximation offers a potential general solution but lacks empirical validation.
- Paper misses comparative experiments between sample-based and analytical policy gradients (e.g., CVaR), leaving practical performance unclear.
- Clarity is generally good but hampered by the eight-page limit; Section 5 (dynamic risks) is overly concise and lacks algorithmic detail and evaluations.
- Recommends either focusing on static risk measures for a self-contained paper with stronger experiments or expanding to a journal version to fully develop dynamic-risk content.
