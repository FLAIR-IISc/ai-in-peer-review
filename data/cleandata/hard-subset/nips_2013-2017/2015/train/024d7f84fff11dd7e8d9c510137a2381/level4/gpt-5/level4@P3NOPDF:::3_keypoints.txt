- Studies risk-averse MDPs with static and time-consistent (Markov) risk measures and derives policy gradient formulas generalizing beyond CVaR.  
- Extends the standard policy gradient theorem conceptually to a broad class of risk measures.

- Presentation is clear and well organized.  
- Theoretical results appear correct and technically sound.  
- Generalization beyond CVaR is useful and offers a unified view of policy gradients.  
- Results should be of practical interest for risk-sensitive reinforcement learning.

- Contribution is incremental, mainly adapting known risk-neutral and CVaR techniques to more general risk measures.  
- Paper should better specify the scope and precise assumptions on risk measures and MDPs required for the gradient forms.

- Clarify that tractability issues stem from policy parameterization rather than from Markov risk measures (state this explicitly in Section 3).  
- Explicitly relate the new gradient formulas to the classical policy gradient theorem, highlighting changed vs unchanged terms.  
- If possible, add a short illustrative example contrasting gradients for static vs dynamic risk measures.  
- State required assumptions on differentiability, integrability, and risk-measure structure (e.g., coherent/convex, risk-envelope representations).

- Fix notation in Theorem 2.1 (ξ P_θ is confusing; align with Theorem 6.6 of [26]).  
- Correct typo “risk enevlop[e]” → “risk envelope” and note computational challenges arise from policy parameterization.

- Overall: solid, well-written paper with modest novelty but a clean, useful generalization; minor clarifications would strengthen the work.
