This paper presents methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) problems and proposes RL algorithms based on these estimators. The main contribution is extending existing methods from specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, thereby providing a unified view. The proposed algorithm is illustrated with a toy example in the finance domain, involving assets with distinct return distributions.

The motivation is clear, and the problem formulation and algorithms logically follow from this motivation and prior work. While the experiment is relatively simple, being confined to a static risk problem, the results effectively demonstrate the algorithm's risk-averse behavior and its flexibility across different risk measures.

**Quality:** The paper is well-written, the equations are technically sound, and all relevant references are provided.

**Clarity:** The clarity of this paper is well above average.

**Originality:** The paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL, building upon related work in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to the general class of coherent risk measures is non-trivial, and the paper's originality appears sufficient.

**Significance:** Risk-sensitive RL is an interesting and important problem, and this paper makes a significant contribution to the field. It presents clear algorithms for optimizing coherent risk measures in RL and demonstrates the behavior of the resulting policies under various risk settings. The proposed approach is well-motivated and represents a natural extension of existing policy gradient and actor-critic methods to this problem domain.
