Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper studies risk-averse reinforcement learning for both static and time-consistent dynamic settings within the general class of coherent risk measures. The main technical contributions are: (i) a new gradient formula for static coherent risk that combines the envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio methods; (ii) a sampling-based algorithm that estimates this gradient by solving a convex program defined by the risk envelope, with a consistency result via sample average approximation (SAA); and (iii) a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent dynamic risk, leading to an actor-critic style algorithm where the critic computes a risk-sensitive value function (equivalently, a robust MDP value function) and the actor uses a ξ-weighted trajectory distribution to estimate the gradient.

The paper unifies and generalizes prior risk-sensitive policy gradient methods that focused on specific risk measures such as variance/mean-variance, exponential utility, and especially CVaR. For static risk, it recovers and simplifies known results (e.g., the CVaR gradient) and provides a general recipe when the risk envelope is given in a convex form. For dynamic risk, it extends the policy gradient theorem to Markov coherent risk measures [Ruszczyński 2010], tying into the robust MDP viewpoint [Osogami; Petrik & Subramanian] and policy gradient literature [Sutton et al.; Konda & Tsitsiklis]. The numerical illustration highlights how different risk criteria (mean-semideviation vs. mean-standard deviation) can lead to qualitatively different policies, especially with heavy-tailed returns.

Strengths:
- Clear, well-organized exposition with correct technical development as far as I can tell.
- A unifying treatment of policy gradients for the full class of coherent risk measures, covering both static and Markov dynamic cases.
- The static gradient formula is simple and practical, and the SAA-based estimator is natural; the consistency result is useful.
- The dynamic result provides a clean extension of the policy gradient theorem to time-consistent Markov coherent risk, and the actor-critic construction is sensible given the robust MDP equivalence.

Weaknesses:
- The contribution is incremental: largely a straightforward extension of risk-neutral policy gradients and prior CVaR-specific results to general coherent risk measures.
- Empirical validation is limited to a small illustrative example; there is no evaluation of computational overhead (e.g., solving a convex program per iteration/state) or scalability.
- The approach relies on access to the risk envelope in explicit convex form and, for the dynamic method, on the ability to sample multiple next states and to simulate ξ-weighted transitions, which may limit applicability in some domains.

Quality:
- The paper appears technically sound. The use of the envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio trick is standard and appropriate. The static examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation) are correct and align with known special cases. The SAA consistency assumptions are reasonable for common coherent risk measures.

Clarity:
- The paper is well written and easy to follow. Assumptions are stated clearly (finite probability space; likelihood ratios bounded; convex risk envelope with Slater condition). The connection to robust MDPs for the critic is helpful.

Originality:
- The work primarily generalizes existing results from specific risk measures (especially CVaR) to the broader coherent class. The dynamic policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk is a useful formalization but follows naturally from prior robust MDP and risk-sensitive DP frameworks.

Significance:
- The unified treatment is a minor but useful contribution: it provides a general recipe for risk-sensitive policy gradients under coherent risk and may simplify future development across different risk measures. Practical impact depends on computational scalability and availability of risk envelopes.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Clear, correct, and well-presented unification of static and dynamic risk-sensitive policy gradients for coherent risk measures.
- Provides generally applicable gradient estimators with a consistency guarantee under broad conditions.
- Extends policy gradient theory in a way that will be useful for researchers working with different coherent risk criteria.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Incremental contribution relative to prior CVaR/risk-sensitive policy gradient work; largely a straightforward extension.
- Limited empirical evidence and no thorough study of computational trade-offs or large-scale performance.
- Assumptions (explicit risk envelope, simulator access for ξ-weighted transitions) may restrict applicability.

Minor comments and suggestions:
- Theorem 2.1: The notation ξ Pθ is confusing. I suggest aligning with the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of [26] to avoid ambiguity.
- Typo at line 141: “risk enevlop[e]” should be corrected to “risk envelope.”
- Section 3: It would help readers unfamiliar with the area to note explicitly that MDPs with Markov coherent risk measures are tractable via dynamic programming; the intractability addressed here stems from policy parameterization and the need for sampling-based estimation, not from the risk measure itself.
- Consider adding a brief discussion of computational costs (solving the convex program for the static gradient; per-state saddle points in the dynamic method) and how these scale with problem size.
- If space allows, expand the experiments to include at least one control task or larger MDP to illustrate scalability and to compare against CVaR-specific baselines.

Overall evaluation: The paper presents a minor but solid and important extension that unifies risk-sensitive policy gradients under the coherent risk framework and offers clear static and dynamic gradient formulas with practical estimation procedures.
