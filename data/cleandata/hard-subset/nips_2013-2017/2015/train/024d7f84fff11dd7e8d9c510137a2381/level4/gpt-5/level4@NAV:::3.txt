The authors study the risk-averse Markov decision process framework with both static and dynamically consistent risk measures (mappings). The main contribution is, in particular, to derive the form of the gradient for both the dynamic and static cases.
The paper generalizes prior results that focused specifically on CVaR. This extension is modest but useful. The outcome is a straightforward extension of the policy gradient in risk-neutral settings.
The paper is well structured, clearly written, and easy to follow.
The results appear correct to the best of my knowledge.
Minor comments:
- Theorem 2.1: The notation ξ P_θ is unclear. I recommend referring to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting the same notation.
- Line 141: “risk envelope” — Section 3: To avoid confusing readers unfamiliar with the topic, it may be helpful to note here that MDPs with Markov risk measures are very tractable. The difficulty in this paper arises from the policy parametrization and not from the risk measure. The paper offers a minor but solid and meaningful extension of existing results.
