This paper introduces methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems using coherent risk measures and proposes reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms based on these methods. The authors extend existing approaches tailored to specific risk measures to the broader class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified framework. The algorithm is demonstrated through a toy example in the finance domain, where each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation behind the work is clearly articulated, and the problem formulation and algorithms logically follow from this motivation and previous research. Although the experiment is relatively simple, focusing on a static risk problem, the results appear to validate the algorithm's quality in terms of its risk-averse behavior and flexibility in accommodating different risk types.

Quality: The paper is well-written, with technically sound equations and a comprehensive list of relevant references.

Clarity: The paper's clarity is above average, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the proposed approach.

Originality: The paper proposes novel algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems, building upon related works on risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension of the proposed approach is non-trivial, and the originality of the paper seems sufficient.

Significance: Risk-sensitive RL is an interesting and important problem, and this paper makes significant contributions to the field. The authors present clear algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL problems with coherent risk measures and demonstrate the behavior of trained policies under different risk settings in a static risk problem. The proposed approach is well-motivated and naturally extends existing algorithms and problems, including policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization.
