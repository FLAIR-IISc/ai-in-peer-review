## Review of NIPS 2015 Submission: [Paper Title - *Please Replace*]

This paper addresses the important problem of policy parameter optimization under general risk measures. The authors provide a unifying framework for risk-sensitive decision-making, encompassing a broad class of coherent risk measures defined by properties such as convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance. The core idea leverages the dual representation of coherent risk measures as the convex hull of probability distributions, defining risk as the maximum expected value within this hull. A key contribution is the analytical derivation of optimal policy parameters – with respect to the chosen risk measure – alongside a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) decision problems. This is achieved by imposing a Lipschitz continuity constraint on the boundaries of the convex hull, which allows for tractable analysis.

The paper is well-written and logically organized. Despite my limited prior exposure to risk-sensitive optimization, I found the explanations generally accessible. The strength of this work lies in its generality; providing a unified analysis applicable to a wide range of established risk measures represents a significant contribution to the field. The theoretical results appear compelling and offer a valuable tool for analyzing decision-making problems under uncertainty. 

However, while the theoretical foundations are strong, a more thorough empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient estimation algorithm would substantially strengthen the paper. Demonstrating performance on a range of benchmark tasks, and comparing against existing risk-sensitive or robust optimization methods, would provide critical validation of the practical benefits of the proposed approach.

I have several specific comments and questions that may further improve the paper:

1. **Preliminaries Clarity:** The preliminaries section requires clarification. The roles of $P_{\theta}$ and $\ksi$ were initially unclear to me. Providing more intuitive explanations and potentially illustrating their connection with a simple example would be beneficial. 

2. **Equation 2 and Theorem 2.1:** Equation 2 appears to be a standard definition of a convex envelope. Could the authors explain why this definition is considered more restrictive than Theorem 2.1? Clarifying this point will avoid potential confusion.

3. **Continuous Variable Generalization:** The paper primarily focuses on discrete distributions. It would be valuable to discuss, even briefly, how the analysis generalizes to distributions defined over continuous variables. What additional considerations or modifications would be necessary?

4. **Smoothness and Theorem 4.2:** The relationship between the smoothness of the constraint functions and the proof of Theorem 4.2 is not explicitly stated in the paper. Including a comment acknowledging this connection would provide a more complete understanding of the theoretical underpinnings. Specifically, mentioning how smoothness ensures the existence and properties of the necessary gradients would be helpful.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical paper that provides a general and promising approach to risk-sensitive decision-making. While the theoretical results seem compelling, a more comprehensive empirical validation is necessary to fully assess the practical impact of the proposed method. I recommend acceptance contingent upon addressing the comments outlined above and including a more extensive empirical evaluation. The proofs should be carefully verified to ensure their correctness.
