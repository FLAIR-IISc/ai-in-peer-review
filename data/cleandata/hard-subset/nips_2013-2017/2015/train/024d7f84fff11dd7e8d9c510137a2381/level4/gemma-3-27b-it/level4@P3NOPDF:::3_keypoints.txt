- Paper analyzes risk-averse MDPs for both static and dynamically consistent risk measures and derives policy-gradient formulas for both settings.
- Main contribution: generalizes prior CVaR-focused work by deriving gradients for a broader class of risk measures.
- Extension is incremental but useful, representing a natural extension of risk-neutral policy gradient methods.
- Results appear technically sound, correct, and the derivations are straightforward.
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized, clearly written, and maintains consistent mathematical rigor.
- Strength lies in increased generality, broadening applicability of risk-averse policy gradient methods beyond CVaR.
- Authors correctly identify optimization challenges under general risk measures and provide a tractable gradient-based approach.
- Minor issue: notation “ξ P<sub>θ</sub>” in Theorem 2.1 is confusing; recommend adopting notation from Theorem 6.6 in [26].
- Typographical error at Line 141: “risk enevlop[e]” should be “risk envelope.”
- Suggestion for Section 3: explicitly state that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally tractable and that the main computational difficulty arises from policy parameterization, not the risk measure.
- Overall assessment: well-executed, theoretically sound, incremental but valuable contribution to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning; recommend acceptance.
