- Addresses minimizing risk over policy parameters for a general class of coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).
- Uses dual representation of coherent risk as a convex hull of probability distributions; risk equals the maximum expected value in the hull.
- Assumes bounded maximum derivatives of the convex hull borders, implying M-Lipschitz continuity of the hull boundaries.
- Analytically derives optimal policy parameters and proposes a general gradient estimation algorithm for both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) cases.
- Paper is well-written and organized, accessible to readers new to risk-sensitive optimization.
- Main contribution: a unified analysis covering a broad class of risk measures, including most well-known ones.
- Requests deeper empirical analysis to better demonstrate the proposed gradient algorithm’s effectiveness and robustness.
- Suggests clarifying the preliminaries, especially the roles of P_{\theta} and \ksi, with more explanation or examples.
- Requests an explanation why Equation 2 is considered more restrictive than Theorem 2.1, since it resembles the convex envelope definition.
- Recommends discussing how the analysis extends to distributions over continuous variables to broaden applicability.
- Suggests mentioning the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2 for additional theoretical insight.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper with compelling results, but proofs need verification and minor revisions could increase impact.
