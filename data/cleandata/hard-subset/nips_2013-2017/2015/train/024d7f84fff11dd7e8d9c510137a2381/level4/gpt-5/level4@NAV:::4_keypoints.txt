- Problem/setup: Studies selecting policy parameters to minimize coherent risk measures using the dual representation as the convex hull of probability distributions and assumes an M‑Lipschitz boundary of that hull.  
- Methods/results: Derives analytic expressions for risk‑optimal policy parameters and provides a general gradient estimation procedure for both static and dynamic settings.  
- Contribution: Presents a unified analysis covering a broad class of commonly used coherent risk measures.  
- Clarity/organization: Paper is clearly written and well organized and understandable to non‑experts.  
- Empirical evaluation: More thorough empirical study of the proposed gradient algorithm is needed.  
- Preliminaries: Roles of P_{\theta} and ξ (ksi) are not sufficiently clear in the preliminaries.  
- Equation/claim clarity: Question whether Equation 2 is essentially the convex envelope and how it is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.  
- Continuous distributions: Request to comment on how the analysis extends to continuous variable distributions.  
- Smoothness assumptions: Ask to note the role of constraint‑function smoothness in proving Theorem 4.2.  
- Verification: Theoretical results are strong but the proofs should be carefully verified.
