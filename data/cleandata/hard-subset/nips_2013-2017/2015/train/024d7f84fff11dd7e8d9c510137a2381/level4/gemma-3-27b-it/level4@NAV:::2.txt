This paper introduces novel methodologies for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning problems utilizing coherent risk measures, and develops corresponding RL algorithms. A key contribution lies in generalizing existing techniques—previously limited to specific risk measures—to encompass the broader class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified framework. The proposed algorithms are demonstrated through a financial toy example, modeling assets with individual return distributions.

The motivation is clearly articulated, and the subsequent problem formulation and algorithmic development logically extend from this motivation and relevant prior work. While the experimental evaluation focuses on a relatively simple, static risk scenario, the results validate the algorithm's ability to achieve risk-averse behavior and adapt to different risk preferences. 

In terms of quality, the paper is well-written and technically rigorous, with sound equations and a comprehensive bibliography. The clarity of presentation is notably high.

The work exhibits sufficient originality, proposing new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL that build upon existing research in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The generalization presented is not trivial. 

Finally, the paper addresses a significant and increasingly important problem – risk-sensitive RL – and makes a substantial contribution through the development of clear algorithms for tackling this problem with coherent risk measures. The demonstrated behavior of learned policies across varying risk settings, even within the limitations of a static risk problem, highlights the potential of this approach as a natural and well-motivated extension of existing techniques in policy gradient methods, actor-critic frameworks, and coherent risk measure optimization.
