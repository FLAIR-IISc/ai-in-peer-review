- Addresses finding policy parameters that minimize a general class of coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Uses the dual representation of risk as the maximum expected value over a convex hull of probability distributions.  
- Introduces a key assumption that the convex-hull boundaries are M-Lipschitz continuous, bounding their derivatives.  
- Derives explicit optimal policy parameters under the Lipschitz-bound assumption.  
- Develops a general gradient-estimation algorithm applicable to both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) decision settings.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and accessible even to readers less familiar with risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Main contribution is a unified theoretical analysis covering a broad class of widely used risk measures.  
- Empirical evaluation is insufficient; a more thorough empirical study of the gradient algorithm is needed.  
- Clarify the roles and relationship of Pθ and ξ in the preliminaries for improved readability.  
- Explain why Equation (2) (convex envelope restatement) is more restrictive than the setting in Theorem 2.1.  
- Discuss extension to continuous distributions, especially implications for the dual representation and Lipschitz assumptions.  
- For Theorem 4.2, state whether smoothness of the constraint functions is necessary for the proof.  
- Proofs appear compelling but require careful verification.
