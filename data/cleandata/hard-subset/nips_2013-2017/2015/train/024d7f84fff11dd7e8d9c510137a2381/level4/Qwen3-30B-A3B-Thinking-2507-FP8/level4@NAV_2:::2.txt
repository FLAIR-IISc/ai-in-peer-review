

This paper introduces a unified framework for policy gradient methods in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning, extending previous approaches focused on specific risk measures (such as variance or CVaR) to the entire class of coherent risk measures. The authors present both static and dynamic formulations: for static risk, they combine standard sampling with convex programming to estimate gradients, while for dynamic risk, they develop an actor-critic style algorithm involving explicit value function approximation. The work significantly generalizes and unifies prior results, as specific risk measures become special cases of the proposed framework.

The paper builds naturally on previous NIPS work in risk-sensitive RL (e.g., [8, 30] for CVaR, [18, 29] for mean-variance), while addressing the gap in generalizing to the full class of coherent risk measures. The related work section effectively contextualizes the contribution within the NIPS literature, noting that prior studies were limited to specific risk measures or particular system dynamics, and the paper clearly demonstrates how existing results follow as special cases.

Quality: The paper is technically sound with rigorous theoretical development supported by well-constructed theorems and proofs. The gradient formulas for both static and dynamic coherent risk measures are derived using the risk envelope representation, which provides a solid foundation for the proposed methods. The experimental validation, while limited to a static risk problem, effectively demonstrates the framework's ability to flexibly select appropriate risk measures based on problem characteristics (e.g., asset return distributions in the numerical example), with results supporting the risk-averse behavior of the trained policies.

Clarity: The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear definitions of key concepts (e.g., coherent risk measures, Markov coherent risk) and a logical progression from problem formulation to solution. The mathematical exposition is accessible to experts in the field, and the supplementary material provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.

Originality: The work makes a significant contribution by generalizing risk-sensitive policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures, rather than focusing on specific risk metrics as previous studies did. The derivation of the gradient formulas using the risk envelope representation is novel, and the paper clearly demonstrates how prior results (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) are special cases of the proposed framework. The extension to dynamic coherent risk measures via the Markov risk formulation represents a meaningful advance beyond previous work.

Significance: Risk-sensitive reinforcement learning is an important area with practical applications in finance, operations research, and other domains. This paper provides a substantial contribution by enabling practitioners to use any coherent risk measure in RL settings, rather than being limited to specific risk metrics. The ability to flexibly select risk measures based on problem characteristics (demonstrated in the numerical example) represents a valuable advance beyond previous approaches. The framework has clear potential for real-world applications where risk management is critical.

Pro arguments for acceptance:
- Provides a unified framework extending previous work to the entire class of coherent risk measures
- Theoretical development is rigorous and well-supported with clear connections to prior work
- Demonstrates how previous results are special cases of the proposed framework
- Presents clear algorithms that solve risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures
- Numerical example effectively illustrates the flexibility of the approach

Con arguments against acceptance:
- The experimental validation is limited to a static risk problem rather than demonstrating the dynamic risk formulation in practice
- While the numerical example is illustrative, it's relatively simple and may not fully capture the complexity of real-world risk-sensitive RL applications

Overall, this paper makes a valuable contribution to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning by providing a unified framework for coherent risk measures. The technical quality is high, the presentation is clear, and the significance is substantial. The minor limitation in the experimental validation does not detract from the overall quality of the work.
