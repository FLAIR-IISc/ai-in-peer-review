- Problem: Finds policy parameters that minimize risk for a broad class of coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Method: Leverages dual representation of coherent risk measures as the convex hull of probability distributions, expressing risk as the maximum expected value over this hull.  
- Assumption: Imposes an upper bound on the maximum derivatives of the convex hull’s boundaries, ensuring M‑Lipschitz continuity.  
- Results: Analytically derives optimal policy parameters under the Lipschitz assumption.  
- Algorithm: Proposes a general gradient estimation method applicable to both static (one‑step) and dynamic (sequential) settings.  
- Writing: Paper is well‑structured and clearly written; exposition was largely followable even by a reviewer not expert in risk‑sensitive optimization.  
- Contribution: Significant unified analysis covering a general class of risk measures that subsumes many established measures.  
- Empirical weakness: Needs more thorough empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.  
- Clarity issues/questions: Preliminaries unclear about roles of P_theta and ξ.  
- Technical question: Equation (2) looks like a convex envelope—why is this more restrictive than Theorem 2.1?  
- Extension request: Recommend discussion on extending the analysis to continuous distributions.  
- Proof detail: The role of constraint function smoothness in proving Theorem 4.2 should be explicitly stated.  
- Overall verdict: Strong theoretical contribution with compelling results, though proofs require careful verification.
