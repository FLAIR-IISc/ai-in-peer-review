The paper proposes a novel formula for the class of static coherent risks, from which policy gradients for many common risk measures can be derived analytically. Additionally, it presents a general sampling-based algorithm for cases where no analytical solution is available and demonstrates consistency guarantees for the policy gradient estimates. Furthermore, the paper provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.

Significance and quality:
The unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures is a valuable and novel contribution to the risk-sensitive decision-making literature, offering a significant improvement in this field. However, it is worth noting that analytical solutions of the Lagrangian saddle-points are only available for a subset of risk measures. The proposed sample-based approximation scheme has the potential to extend the applicability of the derived results to general coherent risk measures by avoiding the need to derive analytical forms of the saddle points. Nevertheless, the paper lacks empirical evidence to demonstrate the viability of the proposed estimation scheme in practice. A comparative experimental evaluation between the sample-based policy gradients and the analytical ones in various problems, such as CVaR, would be highly beneficial in assessing the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Originality: To the best of my knowledge, the derived policy gradients, in their general form, are novel and represent a new contribution to the field.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written, but its clarity is compromised by attempting to address multiple issues within the limited eight-page constraint. Specifically, the presentation of dynamic risk policy gradients in Section 5 is overly concise, lacking algorithmic details and experimental evaluations, which hinders the reader's ability to assess the practical viability of the derived policy gradients. Considering the breadth of topics covered, this content may be more suitable for a longer journal publication. Alternatively, focusing solely on static risk measures could enhance the paper's standalone quality, allowing for more detailed explanations and a more comprehensive empirical evaluation beyond the basic toy example presented. While the paper showcases potentially groundbreaking results, the lack of comprehensive experimental evaluations makes it challenging to fully appreciate its significance. A narrower focus could help alleviate the clarity issues stemming from space constraints.
