- Proposes a novel unified formula for static coherent risks enabling analytical derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Presents a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical saddle-point solutions and proves consistency of the policy gradient estimates.  
- Provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Unified derivation is a valuable and novel contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making literature.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are only available for a subset of risk measures, limiting direct applicability.  
- The sample-based approximation can potentially extend applicability but lacks empirical validation.  
- Paper should include comparative experiments (e.g., CVaR) between sample-based and analytical policy gradients to assess effectiveness.  
- Writing is generally good but constrained by the eight-page limit, reducing clarity in places.  
- Section 5 on dynamic risk policy gradients is overly concise, missing algorithmic details and experimental evaluation.  
- Recommendation: either expand into a longer journal paper or narrow focus to static risk measures to allow more detailed exposition and comprehensive experiments.
