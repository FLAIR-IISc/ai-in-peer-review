- Introduces an analytical framework for deriving policy gradients across static coherent risk measures, yielding closed-form solutions for many common metrics.  
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions and proves consistency of the gradient estimates.  
- Derives policy gradients for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- The unified derivation is novel and a valuable contribution to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning.  
- Closed-form (Lagrangian saddle-point) solutions are available only for specific risk measures, limiting immediate applicability.  
- The proposed sampling scheme lacks empirical validation in the paper.  
- A comparative empirical study (especially for CVaR) between sample-based and analytical gradients across diverse tasks is necessary.  
- Paper is generally well-written but overambitious for the 8-page limit, covering too many distinct topics.  
- Section 5 (dynamic risk measures) is overly abstract, lacking algorithmic details and experimental validation, preventing assessment of practicality.  
- Recommendation to split the work and focus on static risk measures to improve coherence and enable detailed experiments.  
- Core contribution is promising, but the absence of thorough experimental evaluation across multiple risk metrics hinders assessment of practical impact.
