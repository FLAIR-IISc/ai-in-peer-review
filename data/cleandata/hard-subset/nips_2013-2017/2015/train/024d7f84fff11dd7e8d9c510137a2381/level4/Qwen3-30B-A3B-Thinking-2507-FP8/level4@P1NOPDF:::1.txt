

The paper introduces a novel analytical framework for deriving policy gradients across static coherent risk measures, enabling closed-form solutions for many common risk metrics. It further proposes a general sampling-based algorithm for cases lacking analytical solutions, establishing consistency guarantees for the resulting gradient estimates, and derives policy gradients for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.

Significance and quality: The unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures represents a valuable and novel contribution to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. However, analytical solutions for the Lagrangian saddle points remain limited to specific risk measures, constraining immediate applicability. While the proposed sampling scheme theoretically extends the approach to general coherent risk measures by bypassing analytical saddle-point derivations, the paper lacks empirical validation of this estimation method. A comparative empirical study—particularly for CVaR—between sample-based and analytical policy gradients across diverse problems would be essential to demonstrate practical viability.

Originality: To the best of my knowledge, the derived policy gradients in this general form are novel.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written but suffers from overambition within the standard 8-page limit. The inclusion of multiple distinct topics—static risk gradients, sampling algorithms, and dynamic risk extensions—undermines focus. Section 5 (dynamic risk measures) is presented at an excessively high level of abstraction, with no algorithmic details or experimental validation. This prevents meaningful assessment of whether the derived gradients yield computationally feasible, practically viable methods. The current content would better suit a journal submission with expanded space for implementation and evaluation. I recommend splitting the paper to focus exclusively on static risk measures. This narrower scope would strengthen the paper’s coherence, allow for more detailed exposition of the analytical and sampling approaches, and support a comprehensive empirical evaluation beyond the current submission’s single toy example. The core contribution for static risks is promising, but the absence of thorough experimental validation across multiple risk metrics significantly hinders evaluation of its practical impact. A focused revision addressing these gaps would substantially elevate the work.
