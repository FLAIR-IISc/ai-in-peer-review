- Investigates risk-averse MDPs under static and dynamically consistent risk measures; primary contribution is deriving policy-gradient forms for both settings, generalizing beyond CVaR.  
- Extension from CVaR to general risk measures is technically straightforward but useful, broadening applicability of risk-averse policy gradients.  
- Results are a natural extension of risk-neutral policy gradient techniques; derivations are clear and rigorous.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with sound mathematical exposition and correct technical arguments; good connection to prior work (especially [26]).  
- Section 3 contains a misleading claim that "MDPs with Markov risk measures are intractable"; intractability actually comes from parametric policy optimization, not the risk measure—this must be clarified.  
- Minor issues: non-standard notation ξPθ in Theorem 2.1 (recommend using notation from Theorem 6.6 of [26]), typo "risk enevlop" → "risk envelope," and the tractability clarification in Section 3.  
- Technical contribution is modest but valuable—formalizes gradients for general risk measures but offers limited novelty (no new algorithms, measures, or empirical insights).  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on minor revisions (particularly the tractability clarification).
