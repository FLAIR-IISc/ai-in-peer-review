- Study of selecting policy parameters to minimize coherent risk measures under standard axioms (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).
- Focus on a broad class of coherent risk measures and use of their dual representation as supremum of expected values over a convex risk envelope.
- Key assumption: the boundary of the risk envelope is M-Lipschitz (bounded derivatives).
- Under the Lipschitz assumption, the paper derives optimality conditions for policy parameters and develops a general gradient-estimation algorithm.
- Algorithmic results cover both static (one-shot) and dynamic (sequential) settings.
- Paper is well written, well organized, and accessible despite the reviewer not being an expert in risk-sensitive optimization.
- Principal contribution: unified analysis subsuming many well-known special-case risk measures—generality is significant.
- Empirical evaluation is weak; a more thorough empirical study of the gradient algorithm is needed.
- Clarification requested: the precise roles of P_theta and xi (policy-induced distribution vs. latent/noise/index in the risk envelope).
- Clarification requested: whether Equation 2 is just the convex envelope definition and how it is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.
- Clarification requested: how analysis extends to continuous outcome spaces (measurability, integrability, regularity required).
- Clarification requested: explicit statement of how smoothness/Lipschitz/differentiability of constraint functions is used in the proof of Theorem 4.2.
- Overall summary: strong theoretical contribution with compelling results, but proofs need careful checking and empirical validation should be strengthened.
