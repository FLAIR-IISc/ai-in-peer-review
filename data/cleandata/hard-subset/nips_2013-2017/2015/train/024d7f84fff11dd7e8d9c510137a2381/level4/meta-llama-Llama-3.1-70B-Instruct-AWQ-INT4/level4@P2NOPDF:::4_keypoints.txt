- Problem addressed: finding policy parameters that minimize coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).
- Method: leverages dual representation of coherent risks as a convex hull of probability distributions; risk = max expected value over that hull.
- Key assumption: convex-hull borders have bounded derivatives (M‑Lipschitz continuity).
- Contributions: analytic derivation of optimal policy parameters and a general gradient-estimation algorithm for static and dynamic settings.
- Presentation: well-structured and clearly written, accessible to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization.
- Main novelty: unified analysis covering a broad class of common risk measures—significant theoretical advance.
- Empirical shortcoming: paper needs deeper experimental evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.
- Clarification requested: expand preliminaries to clarify roles of P_{θ} and ξ.
- Clarification requested: explain Equation 2 (convex envelope) and why it is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.
- Extension requested: comment on generalization to continuous-variable distributions.
- Clarification requested: state the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution, but proofs require verification and empirical support.
