- Introduces a unified framework deriving policy-gradient formulas for the full class of static coherent risk measures, enabling analytical gradients for common risks.
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm to handle cases without analytical solutions, with consistency guarantees for the gradient estimates.
- Extends the framework to policy gradients for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures, but this extension is presented at a high level.
- The unified derivation is novel and meaningfully connects coherent risk measures with policy-gradient RL methods.
- Practical usefulness hinges on the viability of the sampling-based approximation because analytical Lagrangian saddle-points are limited.
- The paper lacks empirical validation demonstrating the effectiveness of the sampling scheme and the proposed methods in practice.
- Reviewer recommends experiments comparing sample-based gradients to analytical counterparts (e.g., CVaR) and evaluations on benchmark tasks.
- Clarity is reduced by the paper’s breadth under tight page limits; dynamic-risk material lacks algorithmic detail and empirical support.
- Suggestion to narrow scope (focus on static risk measures) to allow deeper technical exposition and more thorough empirical evaluation.
- Strengths: novelty, generality across risk measures, and theoretical soundness (consistency guarantees).
- Weaknesses: no experiments, insufficient detail for dynamic risks, and trade-off between breadth and depth.
- Overall recommendation: weak reject — promising theoretical work but requires empirical validation and/or a tighter, more focused presentation.
