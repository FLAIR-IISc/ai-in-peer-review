The authors investigate risk-averse Markov decision processes utilizing both static and dynamically consistent risk measures. A key contribution lies in the derivation of the gradient form for both the dynamic and static cases. 

This work generalizes prior research primarily focused on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR), representing a modest yet valuable extension. The obtained results can be viewed as a straightforward adaptation of policy gradient methods commonly employed in risk-neutral environments.

The paper is clearly structured, well-written, and readily comprehensible. 

Based on my assessment, the presented results appear to be accurate.

Minor comments:

- Theorem 2.1: The notation \xi P_\theta could benefit from clarification. I recommend aligning it with the notation presented in Theorem 6.6 of [26].
- Line 141: Typo - "risk enevlop[e]". - Section 3: For readers potentially unfamiliar with the field, it would be helpful to explicitly state that the intractability of the problem stems from the policy parametrization, and not from the use of Markov risk measures, as MDPs *with* Markov risk measures are generally tractable. Overall, this paper presents a minor but sound and significant advancement to existing literature.
