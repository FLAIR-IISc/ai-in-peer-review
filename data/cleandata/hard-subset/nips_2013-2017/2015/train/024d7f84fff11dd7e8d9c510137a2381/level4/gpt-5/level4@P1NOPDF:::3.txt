This paper analyzes risk-averse Markov decision processes with both static and dynamically consistent (Markov) risk measures. The main contribution is the derivation of policy-gradient formulas for both the dynamic and static settings.

The work generalizes prior results that focused specifically on CVaR. This extension is minor but useful: the resulting gradients are essentially straightforward extensions of the standard policy-gradient expressions from the risk-neutral setting.

The paper is well organized, clearly written, and easy to follow. As far as I can tell, the results are correct.

Minor comments:
- Theorem 2.1: The notation ξ P_θ is confusing. I suggest referring to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting the same notation for clarity.
- Line 141: “risk enevlop[e]” should be “risk envelope.”
- Section 3: To avoid confusing readers who are not familiar with the area, it would be helpful to emphasize that MDPs with Markov risk measures are very tractable. The intractability in this paper arises from the policy parametrization, not from the risk measure itself. Within that context, the paper provides a minor but solid and important extension of existing results.
