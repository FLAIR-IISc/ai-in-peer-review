This paper analyzes risk-averse Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) utilizing both static and dynamically consistent risk measures. The primary contribution lies in deriving the gradient for both dynamic and static settings within this framework. 

The work generalizes existing results, which were previously limited to Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR). This extension, while incremental, is a valuable addition to the literature. Notably, the presented results can be viewed as a straightforward extension of policy gradient methods commonly employed in risk-neutral MDPs.

The paper is exceptionally well-organized, clearly written, and readily comprehensible. The technical results appear correct based on my understanding.

I have a few minor comments that could further enhance the paper:

*   **Theorem 2.1:** The notation \xi P_\theta is somewhat unclear. I recommend referencing Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting the notation used there for improved consistency and clarity.

*   **Line 141:** Typo – “risk enevlop[e]” should be corrected to “risk envelope”. 

*   **Section 3:** To assist readers less familiar with the nuances of risk-sensitive MDPs, it would be beneficial to explicitly state that MDPs equipped with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability of the problem addressed in this paper stems from the policy parametrization, and not from the use of a more general risk measure itself. Highlighting this distinction will prevent potential misinterpretations.

Overall, this paper presents a minor, but solid and important extension of existing results in risk-averse reinforcement learning. It provides a useful generalization and clear derivation of relevant gradients, building upon established techniques in the field.
