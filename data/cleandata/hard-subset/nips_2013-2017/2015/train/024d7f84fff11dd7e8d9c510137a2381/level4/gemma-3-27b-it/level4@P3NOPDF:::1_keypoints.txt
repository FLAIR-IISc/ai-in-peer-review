- Presents a novel unified framework for deriving policy gradients for a broad class of static coherent risks, with extensions to dynamic risk measures.  
- Core contribution is a general formula enabling analytical policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Proposes a sampling-based algorithm with consistency guarantees for cases lacking analytical solutions.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant and novel, synthesizing coherent risk measures with policy gradient methods.  
- Practical impact is limited because analytical solutions are not universally available.  
- Major weakness: complete absence of empirical validation for the sampling-based scheme.  
- Recommends empirical comparisons (e.g., against analytical CVaR gradients) to evaluate viability and computational cost.  
- Paper is well-written but overly ambitious in scope, harming clarity—Section 5 (dynamic risks) is particularly abstract and under-detailed.  
- Suggests focusing on static risk measures or splitting into two papers to allow fuller development and experiments.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful work that needs more focused presentation and comprehensive experimental evaluation.
