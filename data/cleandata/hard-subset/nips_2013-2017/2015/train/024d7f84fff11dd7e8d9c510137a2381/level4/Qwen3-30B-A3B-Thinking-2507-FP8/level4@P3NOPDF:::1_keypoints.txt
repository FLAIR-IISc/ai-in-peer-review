- Introduces a unified framework for deriving policy gradients for static coherent risk measures.  
- Provides analytical solutions for some common risk metrics (e.g., CVaR).  
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm for risk measures lacking closed-form gradients.  
- Extends the approach to Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant and generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL work.  
- Novelty is strong but partly limited because analytical solutions are only available for a few special cases.  
- Major shortcoming: no empirical validation of the sampling-based method to demonstrate viability or scalability.  
- Claims of "consistency guarantees" are unsupported by proofs, sketches, or experiments in the paper.  
- Dynamic risk extension (Section 5) is underdeveloped, lacking algorithmic detail, implementation guidance, and examples.  
- Paper tries to cover too much within 8 pages, resulting in a disjoint and superficial treatment of topics.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form due to lack of empirical and algorithmic substantiation.  
- Suggested fixes: focus on static risks, add comprehensive experiments (convergence, performance, efficiency), and include algorithm descriptions and proof sketches for consistency.
