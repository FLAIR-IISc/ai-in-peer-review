This paper introduces methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems using coherent risk measures, and proposes reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms based on these estimations. The key contribution lies in extending existing methods, previously specific to individual risk measures, to the more general class of coherent risk measures, thereby providing a unified framework. The algorithms are illustrated with a toy example in a financial setting, where each asset possesses its own return distribution.

The motivation is clearly articulated, and the problem formulation and algorithms flow logically from this motivation and relevant prior work. The experimental evaluation is currently limited to a static risk problem, which is relatively simple. However, the results appear to validate the algorithm’s quality, demonstrating both its risk-averse behavior and flexibility across different risk types.

**Quality:** The paper is well-written and technically sound, with equations that are clearly presented and all relevant references appropriately cited.

**Clarity:** The clarity of this paper is notably high, exceeding the average.

**Originality:** The authors propose novel algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems, building on related work in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to the proposed approach is not trivial, and the level of originality appears sufficient for publication.

**Significance:** Risk-sensitive RL is an important and increasingly relevant problem, and this paper makes a significant contribution to the field. Specifically, the paper presents clear algorithms for addressing risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures, and demonstrates the resulting behavior of trained policies under varying risk settings within a static risk problem. The proposed approach is well-motivated by the authors and represents a natural extension to existing algorithms and problem settings – namely, policy gradient methods, actor-critic algorithms, and coherent risk measure optimization.
