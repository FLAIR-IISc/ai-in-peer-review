**Summary and Relation to Previous Work**

The paper "Policy Gradient for Coherent Risk Measures" proposes a unified approach to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning by extending policy gradient methods to the class of coherent risk measures. The authors derive a new formula for the gradient of static coherent risk measures, which generalizes previously known results for specific risk measures such as variance and conditional value at risk (CVaR). They also present a general sampling-based algorithm for estimating the gradient of static coherent risk measures and provide expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.

The paper relates to previous work on risk-sensitive optimization in reinforcement learning, which has focused on specific risk functions such as exponential utility functions, mean-variance models, and CVaR. The authors' contribution is a general method for the whole class of coherent risk measures, which is not limited to a specific choice within that class.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures is a valuable and novel contribution to the risk-sensitive decision-making literature.
* The proposed sampling-based approximation scheme allows for the application of the derived results to general coherent risk measures without requiring analytical forms of the saddle points.

Weaknesses:

* The paper lacks empirical evidence to demonstrate the viability of the proposed estimation scheme in practice.
* The presentation of dynamic risk policy gradients is high-level and lacks algorithm details and experimental evaluations.
* The paper attempts to address multiple issues, which hinders clarity and makes it difficult to judge the practicality of the derived policy gradients.

**Quality, Originality, and Clarity**

Quality: The paper presents a technically sound contribution to the risk-sensitive decision-making literature. However, the lack of empirical evidence and comprehensive experimental evaluations makes it hard to judge the practicality of the proposed approach.

Originality: To the best of my knowledge, the derived policy gradients are novel in this general form.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written, but the clarity is hindered by the attempt to address multiple issues in a limited space. A narrower focus on static risk measures, with more details and comprehensive empirical evaluations, would improve the paper's clarity and make it more standalone.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper presents a novel and valuable contribution to the risk-sensitive decision-making literature.
* The proposed sampling-based approximation scheme has the potential to be widely applicable.

Con:

* The lack of empirical evidence and comprehensive experimental evaluations makes it hard to judge the practicality of the proposed approach.
* The presentation of dynamic risk policy gradients is high-level and lacks algorithm details and experimental evaluations.

**Recommendation**

I recommend that the authors consider splitting the paper and focusing on static risk measures, which would allow for a more comprehensive empirical evaluation and improve the paper's clarity. Alternatively, the authors could provide more details and experimental evaluations for the dynamic risk policy gradients to demonstrate the practicality of the proposed approach.
