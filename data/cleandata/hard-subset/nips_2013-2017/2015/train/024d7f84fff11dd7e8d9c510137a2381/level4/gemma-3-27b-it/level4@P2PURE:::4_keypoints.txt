- Addresses policy parameter optimization under a general class of coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Uses the dual representation of coherent risk as the convex hull of probability distributions, defining risk as the maximum expected value over that hull.  
- Introduces an M-Lipschitz smoothness assumption on the convex hull boundary and leverages it to derive optimal policy parameters.  
- Provides analytical solutions for both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) decision problems.  
- Presents a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static and dynamic settings.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and largely accessible despite reviewer’s limited background in risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Main contribution: a unified theoretical analysis covering a broad class of commonly used risk measures.  
- Suggests the need for a more extensive empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.  
- Requests clarification in preliminaries: roles of P_θ and ξ are unclear.  
- Questions Equation 2: appears to state convex envelope definition—ask why it’s presented as more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.  
- Asks for discussion on extending the analysis to continuous-variable distributions.  
- Requests explicit explanation of how constraint-function smoothness is used in the proof of Theorem 4.2.  
- Recommends careful verification of the proofs despite promising theoretical results.
