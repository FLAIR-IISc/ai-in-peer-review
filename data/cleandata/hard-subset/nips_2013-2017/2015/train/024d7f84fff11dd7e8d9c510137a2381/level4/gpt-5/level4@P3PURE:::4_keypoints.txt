- Studies finding policy parameters that minimize coherent risk measures satisfying convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance.
- Considers a broad class of coherent risk measures and uses their dual representation as a convex hull of probability distributions.
- Defines risk as the maximum expected value over the convex hull of distributions.
- Assumes the hull boundary is M-Lipschitz (bound on maximum derivatives) to enable analysis.
- Analytically derives optimal policy parameters under that Lipschitz assumption.
- Proposes a general gradient estimation algorithm for both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) settings.
- Paper is well written and well organized; exposition is mostly followable even for non-experts.
- Main theoretical contribution: unified analysis covering many well-known risk measures.
- Empirical evaluation is weak—needs deeper empirical analysis of the proposed gradient algorithm.
- Preliminaries are unclear on the roles of P_θ and ξ; clarification requested.
- Questions whether Equation (2) is just the convex envelope and why it’s more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.
- Requests a comment on extending the analysis to continuous distributions.
- Asks to mention the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2.
- Overall judged a strong theory paper, but proofs require careful verification.
