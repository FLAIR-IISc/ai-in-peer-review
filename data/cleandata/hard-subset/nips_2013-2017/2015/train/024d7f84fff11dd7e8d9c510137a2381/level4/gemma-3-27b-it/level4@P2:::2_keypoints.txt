- Proposes novel methods to estimate policy gradients for risk-sensitive RL using coherent risk measures and presents corresponding algorithms.  
- Generalizes prior work by extending techniques from specific risk measures to the full class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified framework.  
- Demonstrates the approach with a financial example that shows different asset selections under different risk preferences.  
- Motivation is clear and the problem formulation and algorithm development logically follow from prior work.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a static risk problem but shows the algorithm yields risk-averse behavior and adapts to different risk types.  
- Technical quality is high: equations are well-defined, references comprehensive, and theoretical grounding rigorous.  
- Paper clarity is strong with straightforward explanations, consistent notation, and good organization.  
- Originality is sufficient: introduces new algorithms and a non-trivial extension of existing risk-sensitive RL methods.  
- Significance: provides practical algorithms for risk-sensitive RL and is a natural extension of policy-gradient and actor-critic methods to coherent risk measures.  
- Arguments for acceptance: successful generalization, theoretical soundness, clear presentation, and potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental scope is limited; needs more diverse, dynamic, or complex evaluations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity, particularly for the convex programming components.  
- Overall verdict: strong contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
