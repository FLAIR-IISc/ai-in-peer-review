- Paper develops policy-gradient methods for the full class of coherent risk measures, covering static discounted-return risk and time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk.  
- Core idea leverages the dual representation of coherent risk as a worst-case expectation over adversarial reweightings ξ within a convex risk envelope U(Pθ).  
- Derives a general static coherent-risk gradient formula using the envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio trick.  
- Provides practical estimators: closed-form gradients for CVaR and mean–semideviation, plus an SAA scheme that solves an inner convex program and uses KKT multipliers.  
- Proves consistency of the SAA-based estimator under mild assumptions.  
- Extends to dynamic Markov coherent risk with a policy-gradient theorem expressing gradients via a value function and ξ*-weighted trajectory distribution, enabling an actor–critic algorithm.  
- Unifies and extends prior risk-sensitive RL work (mean–variance, CVaR, exponential utility, special-case dynamic coherent risk) and connects to robust MDP literature.  
- Originality: clean, unified treatment of coherent risks and novel extension of the policy-gradient theorem to dynamic Markov coherent risk.  
- Technical quality: principled derivations using duality, envelope theorem, and KKT conditions; dynamic result ties to a Bellman equation.  
- Clarity and organization: generally well written and accessible, with useful connections to prior work.  
- Significance: offers a general toolkit subsuming CVaR/mean–variance methods and potential high impact for time-consistent risk in RL.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and small-scale; more extensive experiments and comparisons would strengthen the paper.  
- Practicality concerns: dynamic method requires sampling under ξ*-weighted transitions and multiple next-state samples per state, limiting offline or single-trajectory applicability.  
- Assumptions on the risk envelope require explicit convex parametrizations and smooth dependence on Pθ; the paper should clarify which coherent risks fit or do not fit this template.  
- Notational load: roles of Pθ and ξ can be confusing, especially when moving from static to dynamic settings; minor clarifications would help.  
- Quality: static formulas recover known results and extend them; SAA + KKT estimator is appealing and supported by a consistency proof under Slater, compactness, continuity.  
- Clarity suggestions: better distinguish expectations under Pθ vs Eξ and explicitly note state-dependent envelopes U(x,Pθ(·|x)) in the dynamic case.  
- Specific requested clarifications: (1) explicit roles of Pθ and ξ; (2) explain Eq.(2) as a parametric risk-envelope representation and its relation to Theorem 2.1; (3) sketch conditions/changes for continuous distributions and SAA; (4) state how smoothness of constraint functions is used in Theorem 4.2; (5) discuss computational cost and variance of inner convex solves/KKT extraction; (6) comment on importance sampling/off-policy or single-trajectory extensions for dynamic algorithm.  
- Pros for acceptance: unifies coherent-risk policy gradients, novel dynamic policy-gradient theorem and actor–critic design, clear robust-MDP connections, and recovers known special cases.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical validation, practical requirements (inner convex programs, ξ*-weighted dynamics, multi-sample simulator) may limit applicability, and some envelope-smoothness/representation assumptions need clearer discussion.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with high originality and potential impact; recommend acceptance pending requested clarifications and modestly expanded experiments.
