- Problem: optimize policy parameters to minimize a given coherent risk measure.  
- Scope: focuses on a general class of coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Approach: uses the dual representation of a coherent risk as the convex hull of a set of probability distributions, with risk equal to the max expected value over that hull.  
- Key assumption: boundaries of the convex hull are M-Lipschitz continuous.  
- Method: derives a general gradient estimation algorithm leveraging the M-Lipschitz assumption.  
- Settings: develops the algorithm for both static (single-step) and dynamic (sequential) decision problems.  
- Writing: paper is well-written and organized, accessible to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Main contribution: a unified theoretical analysis that includes many well-known risk measures — a significant contribution.  
- Empirical weakness: paper lacks a comprehensive empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.  
- Clarity issue 1: Preliminaries are unclear — reviewer confused about the roles of P_{\theta} and \ksi.  
- Clarity issue 2: Question whether Equation 2 is just the convex envelope and why it’s more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.  
- Suggestion 1: Add discussion on how analysis generalizes to distributions over continuous variables.  
- Suggestion 2: Explain the role of smoothness of the constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2.  
- Overall verdict: strong theoretical paper with compelling results, though proofs need careful verification.
