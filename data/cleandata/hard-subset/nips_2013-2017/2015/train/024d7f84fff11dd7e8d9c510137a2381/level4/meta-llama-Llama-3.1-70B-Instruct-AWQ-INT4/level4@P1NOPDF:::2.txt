This paper presents methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems using coherent risk measures and proposes reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms based on these methods. The paper extends existing approaches for specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified view. A toy example in the finance domain is used to illustrate the algorithm, where each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation behind the paper is clear, and the problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from this motivation and previous works. The experiment, although relatively simple as it only addresses a static risk problem, appears to support the quality of the algorithm in terms of its risk-averse behavior and flexibility in handling different risk types.

The quality of the paper is high. The equations are technically sound, and all relevant references are provided. The clarity of the paper is well above average, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. 

In terms of originality, the paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems based on related works on risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension of the proposed approach is not straightforward, indicating sufficient originality.

The significance of the paper lies in its contribution to the field of risk-sensitive RL, an interesting and important problem. The paper presents clear algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures and demonstrates the behavior of trained policies with different risk settings on a static risk problem. The proposed approach is clearly motivated by the authors and represents a natural extension of existing algorithms and problems, including policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization.
