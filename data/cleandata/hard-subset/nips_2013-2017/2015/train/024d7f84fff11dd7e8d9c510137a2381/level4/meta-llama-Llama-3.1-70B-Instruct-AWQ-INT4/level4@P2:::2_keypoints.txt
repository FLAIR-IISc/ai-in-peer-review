- Proposes methods to estimate policy gradients for risk-sensitive problems using coherent risk measures and develops corresponding RL algorithms.  
- Extends prior work on specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified framework.  
- Validated on a toy finance example where each asset has its own return distribution.  
- Motivation is clear and the problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from previous work.  
- Experiment is simple (static risk problem) but demonstrates risk-averse behavior and flexibility across risk types.  
- Paper is well-written, with technically sound equations and comprehensive references.  
- Clarity is above average; explanations are excellent and the paper is easy to follow.  
- Originality is sufficient; the extension to coherent risk measures is nontrivial.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and provides clear algorithms (policy gradient, actor-critic) for coherent risk optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: unified view, well-motivated and clearly explained algorithms, experimental demonstration of behavior.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experiments are relatively simple; need more complex or real-world validations.  
- Minor concern: some readers may find technical details and equations challenging despite good exposition.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution and recommended for acceptance.
