The authors analyze the risk-averse Markov decision process setting with static and dynamically consistent risk measures. The main contribution is, in particular, to derive the form of the gradient for both the dynamic and static settings.

The paper is a generalization of previous results, which focused specifically on CVaR. This extension represents a useful contribution, albeit a relatively minor one. The core result is a straightforward extension of the policy gradient theorem to risk-neutral settings, broadened to encompass a wider class of coherent risk measures.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow. The technical arguments appear correct.

Minor comments:

*   Theorem 2.1: The notation ξPθ is potentially confusing. I suggest referring to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and using the same notation for clarity.
*   Line 141: “risk enevlop” – Typo: should be “envelope.”
*   Section 3: To avoid confusing readers unfamiliar with the topic, it may be worthwhile to point out that MDPs *with* Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability of the problem addressed in this paper stems from the policy parametrization, and not inherently from the choice of a coherent risk measure.

Overall, the paper presents a solid and important, though incremental, extension of existing results. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a relevant problem in reinforcement learning – risk-sensitive optimization.
*   The generalization to coherent risk measures is a valuable contribution, providing a unified framework.
*   The derived gradient formulas are correct and potentially useful for practitioners.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The core result is a relatively straightforward extension of existing policy gradient methods.
*   The numerical example, while illustrative, is limited in scope.
*   The theoretical analysis, while sound, could be further strengthened with more detailed convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithms (acknowledged as future work by the authors).
