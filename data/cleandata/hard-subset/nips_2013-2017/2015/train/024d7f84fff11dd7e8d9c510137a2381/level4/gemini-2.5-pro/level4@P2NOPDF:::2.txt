This paper introduces a principled framework for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning problems, focusing on the general class of coherent risk measures. Based on these estimators, the authors propose practical actor-critic algorithms. A key contribution of this work is the generalization of existing methods, which are often tailored to specific risk measures, into a unified framework. This provides a more encompassing view of risk-sensitive policy optimization. The proposed approach is illustrated with an empirical evaluation on a financial portfolio optimization task, where assets are characterized by distinct return distributions.

**Strengths:**

*   **Significance and Novelty:** The paper tackles the important and challenging problem of risk-sensitive RL. The extension from specific risk measures to the broad and theoretically grounded class of coherent risk measures is a significant and non-trivial contribution. This generalization provides a valuable, unified perspective that advances the field. The proposed algorithms are novel and well-motivated by prior work in risk-sensitive planning and optimization.

*   **Technical Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The mathematical derivations are clear and appear correct, and the work is well-situated within the context of relevant literature, with appropriate citations provided. The proposed algorithms are a natural and well-reasoned extension of existing policy gradient and actor-critic methods.

*   **Clarity and Presentation:** The manuscript is exceptionally well-written and organized. The motivation for a unified approach to coherent risk measures is clearly articulated, and the problem formulation and subsequent algorithmic development flow logically. This high degree of clarity makes the paper's contributions easy to understand and appreciate.

*   **Empirical Demonstration:** While the experimental setting is a static risk problem, the results effectively support the paper's claims. The experiments successfully demonstrate the algorithm's ability to produce policies with varying degrees of risk aversion, and showcase its flexibility in handling different risk preferences by simply changing the underlying risk measure.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Experimental Scope:** The primary weakness of the paper is the limited scope of the empirical evaluation. The experiments are confined to a static (single-step) portfolio optimization problem. While this serves as a good proof of concept, it does not fully demonstrate the algorithm's efficacy in the more general, sequential decision-making settings for which reinforcement learning is typically designed. Evaluating the approach on more complex, dynamic environments where a policy's risk sensitivity influences an entire trajectory of outcomes would significantly strengthen the paper's empirical contributions.

In summary, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. It is well-written, technically solid, and presents a novel, unified approach to optimizing for coherent risk measures. Despite the somewhat limited experimental validation, the theoretical contribution is strong and provides a solid foundation for future work.
