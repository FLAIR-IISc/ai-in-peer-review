- Presents a unified framework for risk-sensitive RL using coherent risk measures, generalizing beyond specific measures like variance or CVaR.  
- Develops policy gradient algorithms for the full class of coherent risk measures in both static and time-consistent dynamic settings.  
- Proposes a sampling-based convex-programming approach for static risk and an actor-critic style algorithm for dynamic risk.  
- Theorems (e.g., Thm 2.1, 4.2, 5.2) rigorously underpin the approach, using the worst-case expectation representation, envelope theorem, and likelihood-ratio techniques.  
- Mathematical derivations and proofs are technically sound and well-detailed, with sufficient supplementary material for verification.  
- Exposition and notation are clear and consistent, with effective use of examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation) to recover prior results.  
- Numerical illustration demonstrates practical effects of different risk preferences on policy selection and highlights trade-offs (e.g., heavy-tailed asset choice).  
- Originality is strong: extending risk-sensitive RL to the entire coherent risk class is a significant conceptual advance.  
- Significance is substantial for applications in finance, operations research, and robust decision-making under uncertainty.  
- Connects coherent risk measures to model uncertainty, suggesting broader implications and future work on risk shaping.  
- Main limitation: experimental validation is limited to a static risk problem; dynamic risk algorithms remain largely theoretical.  
- Well-referenced and contextualized relative to prior CVaR and mean-variance work; reviewer recommends acceptance.
