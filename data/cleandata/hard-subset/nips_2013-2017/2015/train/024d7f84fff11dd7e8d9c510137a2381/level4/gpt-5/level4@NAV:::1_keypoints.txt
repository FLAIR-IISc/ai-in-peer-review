- Introduces a unified expression for static coherent risk measures enabling analytic policy-gradient derivations for many standard risks.  
- Proposes a general sampling-based method for cases without closed-form solutions and proves consistency of the resulting policy-gradient estimators.  
- Provides formulas for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Unified analytic derivation is a novel and valuable contribution to risk-sensitive decision making.  
- Analytic Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for a subset of risk measures.  
- The sampling-based approximation could broaden applicability but lacks empirical evidence of practical viability.  
- Recommends comparative evaluation of sampling-based vs analytical gradients (e.g., for CVaR).  
- Claims originality: policy-gradient results at this level of generality appear new.  
- Paper is generally well written but overly dense for eight pages; many issues tackled reduce clarity.  
- Dynamic-risk policy-gradient section is presented at a high level with missing algorithmic details and experiments.  
- Suggests either expand into a longer journal article or narrow scope to static risk measures to allow more detail and empirical study.
