This paper studies risk-averse Markov decision processes under both static risk measures and dynamically consistent (time-consistent) risk measures. The main contribution is to derive the policy-gradient expressions for both settings. The work generalizes prior results that focused on CVaR, extending them to a broader class of risk measures. While the extension is incremental, it is useful, and the resulting gradients are natural analogs of the standard risk-neutral policy gradient.

The paper is well organized, clearly written, and easy to follow. To the best of my knowledge, the results are correct.

Minor comments:
- Theorem 2.1: The notation ξ P_θ is confusing. I recommend aligning with the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of [26] to improve clarity.
- Line 141: Typo: “risk enevlop[e]” should be “risk envelope.”
- Section 3 (and possibly earlier): To prevent confusion among readers less familiar with risk-averse MDPs, it would help to explicitly note that MDPs with Markov (time-consistent) risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability in this paper arises from the policy parametrization, not from the use of Markov risk measures themselves.
- Presentation: It may be worth briefly contrasting the derived gradients with the classical risk-neutral policy gradient, emphasizing that the extension is straightforward once the appropriate risk mappings and envelopes are specified. This would help position the contribution and clarify its scope.

Overall, the paper presents a minor but solid and useful extension of existing policy-gradient results to general static and dynamically consistent risk measures. The exposition is clear, the technical development appears sound, and the contribution should be of interest to researchers working on risk-averse RL and MDP optimization.
