

This paper addresses risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) by developing policy gradient estimation methods for coherent risk measures, presenting novel algorithms that generalize beyond specific risk metrics. The core contribution lies in unifying existing approaches for specialized risk measures into a single framework applicable to the entire class of coherent risk measures, offering a principled and flexible foundation for risk-sensitive RL. The authors demonstrate the approach using a simplified financial portfolio example, where each asset's return follows a distinct distribution, effectively illustrating policy behavior under varying risk preferences.

The motivation is compelling and clearly articulated, directly linking the problem to real-world applications where risk management is critical. The problem formulation and resulting algorithms logically extend from this motivation and build upon established work in risk-sensitive planning and RL. While the experimental validation is conducted in a static risk setting—a simplification that limits scalability to dynamic environments—the results robustly demonstrate the algorithm's ability to produce risk-averse policies and adapt to diverse risk types (e.g., CVaR, entropic risk). This provides strong empirical support for the method's core functionality.

Quality: The paper is technically sound and well-structured. Equations are rigorously derived and well-justified, with a comprehensive literature review that appropriately contextualizes the work within existing risk-sensitive RL and coherent risk literature.

Clarity: The exposition is exceptionally clear, exceeding typical standards for the field. Concepts are introduced logically, notation is consistent, and the narrative flows smoothly from motivation to methodology to results.

Originality: The extension from specific risk measures to the general coherent risk framework represents a non-trivial theoretical advancement. While building on prior work, the authors successfully generalize the approach, moving beyond incremental improvements to establish a unified methodology. This core extension is sufficiently original for publication.

Significance: The paper makes a meaningful contribution to an important and growing area of RL research. Risk-sensitive decision-making is crucial in applications like finance, robotics, and healthcare, where suboptimal risk exposure can have severe consequences. By providing clear, implementable algorithms for coherent risk measures and demonstrating their behavior across risk settings, the work offers practical value to both researchers and practitioners. The natural integration with established RL paradigms (policy gradients, actor-critic) further enhances its applicability and potential impact. The contribution is significant not only for its technical advance but also for its role in advancing the theoretical understanding of risk in sequential decision-making.

Overall, this work presents a well-motivated, technically rigorous, and clearly articulated solution to a relevant problem. The unified framework for coherent risk measures is a notable theoretical contribution, and the empirical results, though limited to a static setting, effectively validate the core claims. The paper is a strong candidate for publication at NeurIPS, offering valuable tools and insights for the RL community.
