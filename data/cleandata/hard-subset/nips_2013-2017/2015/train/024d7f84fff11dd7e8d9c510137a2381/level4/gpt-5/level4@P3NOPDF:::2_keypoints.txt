- Summary: Proposes policy-gradient and actor-critic methods for risk-sensitive RL covering the full class of coherent risk measures, demonstrated on a static portfolio selection task.

- Strength: Clear motivation and problem formulation, with a natural flow from setup to algorithm design.

- Strength: General and unifying approach that extends prior work on specific risk measures to all coherent risks.

- Strength: Technically sound derivations, well-specified notation and assumptions, and accurate literature connections.

- Strength: Writing is clear and organized; algorithms and concepts are easy to follow.

- Strength: Empirical illustration shows plausible risk-averse behavior and flexibility across risk choices.

- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is narrow—limited to a static problem rather than dynamic MDPs or standard sequential benchmarks.

- Weakness: Missing comparisons to established baselines (e.g., CVaR policy gradients, risk-neutral methods) to contextualize performance and sample efficiency.

- Weakness: No sensitivity analysis over risk parameters or algorithm hyperparameters to assess robustness.

- Weakness: Lacks discussion and empirical data on computational cost, gradient-variance, and variance-reduction or runtime/sample-complexity trends.

- Weakness: Little practical guidance on choosing among coherent risks or trade-offs observed in experiments.

- Quality: Paper is technically solid with clear algorithms and appropriate theoretical framing.

- Clarity: Exposition, notation, and figures are strong and aid understanding.

- Originality: Novel and nontrivial extension of policy-gradient methods to the broad class of coherent risks.

- Significance: Important contribution for safety-critical and finance applications, with strong conceptual impact despite limited experiments.

- Question: Please comment on variance properties of the gradient estimators and any control variates used.

- Question: How does the approach scale with large state/action spaces and function approximation (e.g., deep networks)?

- Question: Do the estimators reduce to known formulas for specific risks (e.g., CVaR), and do empirical results match specialized methods?

- Question: For dynamic settings, do you assume time-consistent coherent risks and how does that affect guarantees?

- Minor: Provide pseudocode for both policy-gradient and actor-critic variants to improve reproducibility.

- Minor: Add a summary table of coherent risks, their properties, and parameterizations.

- Overall assessment: Well-written, original, and significant work; recommend acceptance if empirical scope, baselines, and computational/variance guidance are addressed.
