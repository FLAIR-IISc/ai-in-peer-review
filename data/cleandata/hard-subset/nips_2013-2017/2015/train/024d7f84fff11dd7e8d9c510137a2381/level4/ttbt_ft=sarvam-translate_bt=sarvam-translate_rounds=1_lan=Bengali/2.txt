This paper presents a method for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems through consistent risk measurement and proposes an RL algorithm based on it. This paper specifically expands the existing methods for specific risk measurement in the general category of consistent risk measurement and provides an integrated approach through it. The algorithm is illustrated with a toy example of finance, where each asset has its own return distribution.

The purpose behind this is very clear, and the problem structure and algorithms are naturally followed from this purpose and previous work. The experiment is relatively simple, as it is only done in a static risk problem. However, the results seem to support the quality of the algorithm in terms of risk-averse behavior and flexibility in different types of risks.

Quality: The paper is well written. The equations are technically correct, and all relevant references are provided.
Clarity: The clarity of this paper is much higher than the average.
Originality: This paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems based on risk-sensitive planning and related work in RL. The proposed method is not a direct extension. The originality of this paper seems sufficient to me.
Importance: Risk-sensitive RL is an interesting and important problem, and this paper makes a significant contribution. This paper presents a clear algorithm that solves risk-sensitive RL through consistent risk measurement and demonstrates the behavior of trained policies with different risk settings in a static risk problem. The proposed method is clearly inspired by the authors and is a natural extension of existing algorithms and problems (policy gradients, actor-criticism, and consistent risk measurement optimization).
