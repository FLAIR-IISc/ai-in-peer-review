This paper presents methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive settings with coherent risk measures and proposes RL algorithms based on these estimators. It extends existing approaches that target specific risk measures to the broader class of coherent risk measures, thereby providing a unified framework. The algorithm is illustrated with a toy example in the finance domain, where each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation is clear, and the problem formulation and resulting algorithms follow naturally from this motivation and prior work. The experimental evaluation is relatively simple, as it is restricted to a static risk problem. Nevertheless, the results appear to support the effectiveness of the approach in terms of its risk-averse behavior and its flexibility across different risk types.

Quality: The paper is well written. The equations are technically sound, and all relevant references are provided.

Clarity: The clarity of the presentation is well above average.

Originality: The paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems, grounded in related work on risk-sensitive planning and RL. The proposed extension to coherent risk measures is nontrivial. The level of originality seems sufficient.

Significance: Risk-sensitive RL is an important and timely topic, and this paper makes a significant contribution. It presents clear algorithms for addressing risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures and demonstrates the behavior of trained policies under different risk settings on a static risk problem. The approach is clearly motivated by the authors and represents a natural extension of existing algorithms and formulations (policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization).
