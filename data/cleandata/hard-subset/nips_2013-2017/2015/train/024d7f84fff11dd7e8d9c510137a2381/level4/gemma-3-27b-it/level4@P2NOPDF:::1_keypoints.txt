- Introduces a framework for computing policy gradients for a broad class of static coherent risks with analytical derivations for many common risk measures.
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm to estimate policy gradients when analytical solutions are unavailable, with consistency guarantees.
- Presents policy gradient expressions for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- Unified derivation is a valuable and original contribution to risk-sensitive decision making.
- Analytical solutions are limited to specific risk measures due to complexity of solving Lagrangian saddle-points.
- Major weakness: complete absence of empirical validation for the proposed sampling-based scheme.
- Recommends experimental comparison between sample-based gradients and existing analytical methods, particularly CVaR.
- Clarity is hampered by ambitious scope and page limits; Section 5 on dynamic risks feels underdeveloped.
- Lack of algorithmic details and experimental results makes it difficult to assess practicality and benefits.
- Suggests narrowing focus to static risk measures for a more self-contained paper with thorough empirical evaluation.
- Alternatively recommends splitting into two publications: one on static risks with experiments, another on dynamic risks.
- Overall: interesting and potentially broadly applicable work but requires a more focused approach and robust empirical support.
