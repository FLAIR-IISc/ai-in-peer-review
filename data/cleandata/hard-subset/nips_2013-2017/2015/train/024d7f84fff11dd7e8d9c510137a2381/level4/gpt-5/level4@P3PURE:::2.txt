This paper presents methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems with coherent risk measures and proposes reinforcement learning algorithms based on these estimators. It extends prior methods designed for specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, thereby providing a unified framework. The approach is illustrated with a toy finance example in which each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation is clear, and the problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from both the motivation and prior work. The experimental evaluation is relatively simple, as it addresses only a static risk problem. Nevertheless, the results appear to support the quality of the proposed algorithm in terms of risk-averse behavior and flexibility across different risk types.

Quality: The paper is well written. The equations are technically sound, and all relevant references are provided.

Clarity: The clarity of the paper is well above average.

Originality: The paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems, building on related work in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to the proposed approach is not straightforward, and the level of originality appears sufficient.

Significance: Risk-sensitive RL is an important and timely topic, and this work makes a significant contribution. The paper presents clear algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures and demonstrates the behavior of trained policies under different risk settings on a static risk problem. The approach is clearly motivated and represents a natural extension of existing algorithms and problems (policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization).
