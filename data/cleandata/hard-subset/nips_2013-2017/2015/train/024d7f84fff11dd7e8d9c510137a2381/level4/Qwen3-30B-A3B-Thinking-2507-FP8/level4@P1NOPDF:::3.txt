

The authors analyze risk-averse Markov decision processes under both static and dynamically consistent risk measures (mappings), with the primary contribution being a derivation of the gradient form for both dynamic and static settings. The paper generalizes prior work, which focused exclusively on CVaR, by extending the framework to broader risk measures. This extension constitutes a minor yet useful contribution, representing a straightforward adaptation of policy gradient methods from risk-neutral to risk-averse settings.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and logically structured, making it easy to follow. The technical results appear correct based on my review. The main strength lies in formalizing the gradient computation for these risk measures, which enables practical application of policy gradient methods in risk-averse contexts.

Minor suggestions for clarity:
- **Theorem 2.1**: The notation $\xi P_\theta$ is ambiguous. To align with standard usage, I recommend adopting the notation from Theorem 6.6 in [26] for consistency.
- **Line 141 and Section 3**: The phrase "risk envelope" is misspelled as "risk enevlop[e]". More importantly, to prevent confusion for readers unfamiliar with the topic, it should be clarified that Markov decision processes with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability addressed in this paper stems solely from the policy parametrization, not from the risk measure itself. Explicitly stating this distinction would strengthen the presentation.

Overall, the paper provides a solid and important extension of existing methodology. While the technical novelty is incremental, the work effectively bridges a gap in applying policy gradient approaches to general risk measures beyond CVaR, and the results are presented with sufficient rigor. The minor notational and explanatory improvements suggested above would further enhance the paper’s clarity and accessibility.
