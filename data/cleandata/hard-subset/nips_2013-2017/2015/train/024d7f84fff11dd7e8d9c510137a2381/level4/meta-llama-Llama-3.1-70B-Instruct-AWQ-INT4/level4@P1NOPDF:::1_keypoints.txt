- Proposes a new formula for static coherent risks enabling analytical policy gradients for many common risk measures.
- Introduces a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions and proves consistency of the policy gradient estimates.
- Provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- Unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures is an intriguing and valuable contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making.
- Analytical solutions of the Lagrangian saddle-points are available only for some risk measures.
- The sample-based approximation could extend the method to general coherent risks but lacks empirical validation.
- No experimental comparison between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR) is provided, limiting assessment of practical effectiveness.
- The general-form policy gradients appear novel.
- Clarity is hindered by attempting to cover multiple topics within eight pages; dynamic risk section is high-level.
- Paper lacks algorithmic details and comprehensive experimental evaluation, making practical value difficult to judge.
- Recommendation: either expand to a longer journal version or focus on static risk measures to allow deeper exposition and experiments.
