- Presents a theoretically sound extension of policy gradient methods to the class of coherent risk measures using duality.  
- Derives novel gradient formulas for static and dynamic risk optimization (notably Theorems 4.2 and 5.2).  
- Unifies prior work (e.g., CVaR, variance) under a general coherent-risk framework.  
- Combines sampling + convex programming for static cases and an actor-critic/value-function approximation approach for dynamic cases.  
- Numerical illustration shows how different risk criteria affect policy choice, but is brief.  
- Strength: broad generality covering a wide class of coherent risk measures.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor, leveraging convex optimization and risk theory.  
- Strength: novel contribution extending policy gradients to coherent risk measures.  
- Strength: generally clear and well-structured exposition despite technical complexity.  
- Strength: insightful connection between Markov coherent risk and robust MDPs.  
- Weakness: preliminaries notation (Pθ, ξ) needs clearer, more intuitive explanation.  
- Weakness: Assumption 2.2 is insufficiently motivated and its implications (e.g., Lipschitz relation) need clarification.  
- Weakness: paper restricts to finite probability spaces; lacks discussion of extending to continuous settings and associated challenges.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited; needs broader benchmarks and comparisons to existing risk-sensitive RL methods.  
- Weakness: actor-critic algorithm lacks crucial implementation details (function approximation, learning rates) affecting reproducibility.  
- Accept arguments: addresses a significant problem with a novel, general, and rigorous approach that opens new research directions.  
- Reject arguments: practical applicability concerns due to limited experiments and missing algorithmic specifics; some clarity issues remain.  
- Overall: strong submission recommended for acceptance conditional on addressing weaknesses—provide more actor-critic details, expand empirical evaluation, and discuss sensitivity to the smoothness parameter M.
