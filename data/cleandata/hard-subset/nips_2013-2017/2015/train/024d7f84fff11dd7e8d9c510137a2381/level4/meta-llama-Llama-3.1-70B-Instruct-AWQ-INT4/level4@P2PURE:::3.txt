The authors analyze the risk-averse Markov decision process setting with static and dynamically consistent risk measures, providing a generalization of previous results that focused specifically on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR). The main contribution of this work lies in deriving the form of the gradient for both dynamic and static settings.

This extension represents a minor, yet useful, contribution to the field. The results can be seen as a straightforward extension of the policy gradient in risk-neutral settings. The paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow, making it accessible to readers.

Upon reviewing the paper, I found the results to be correct as far as I can verify. However, I do have some minor comments to improve the clarity and consistency of the presentation.

Firstly, the notation $\xi P_\theta$ in Theorem 2.1 may be confusing. I suggest adopting the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of reference [26] to maintain consistency.

Additionally, on line 141, there is a minor typo ("risk enevlop[e]"). More importantly, in Section 3, it would be beneficial to point out that Markov decision processes (MDPs) with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability of the problem presented in the paper stems from the policy parametrization, rather than the risk measure itself. This clarification would help avoid confusing readers unfamiliar with the topic.

Overall, the paper presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results, providing a valuable contribution to the field.
