- Presents a novel, general framework extending policy gradient methods to the class of coherent risk measures via their dual (risk-envelope) representation.  
- Main contributions: a general static-policy gradient formula using the envelope theorem and a time-consistent Markov coherent-risk policy gradient theorem enabling an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Unifies and generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL results (e.g., variance, CVaR) under a single principled framework.  
- Theoretical development is elegant and sound, integrating convex optimization (dual representation, envelope theorem) with RL techniques.  
- Formulation is general and applicable to any coherent risk with a convex-constraint description of its risk envelope.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides useful background and illustrative examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation).  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a simple bandit-style example and does not assess sample complexity, gradient variance, or scalability on standard RL benchmarks.  
- Some technical assumptions and their practical implications need clearer explanation for broader accessibility.  
- Clarify that ξ reweights Pθ to produce the worst-case distribution from U, making the duality intuition explicit.  
- Make explicit that Assumption 2.2 operationalizes Theorem 2.1 by enabling solving for ξ and Lagrange multipliers needed in the gradient formula.  
- If claiming extension to Lp spaces, briefly discuss required technical tools (e.g., Radon–Nikodym derivatives) and challenges.  
- Connect smoothness conditions in Assumption 2.2 to envelope-theorem prerequisites to justify differentiability of the optimization value w.r.t. θ.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution unifying risk-sensitive policy optimization, but needs stronger empirical validation and clearer exposition of technical assumptions.
