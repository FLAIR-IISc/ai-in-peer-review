This paper introduces methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) problems utilizing coherent risk measures, and subsequently proposes RL algorithms built upon these estimations. A key contribution lies in generalizing existing techniques, previously limited to specific risk measures, to encompass the broader class of coherent risk measures, providing a unifying framework. The algorithms are demonstrated through a toy example within a financial application, modeling a portfolio of assets each with independent return distributions.

The motivation for this work is compelling and clearly articulated. The problem formulation and algorithmic development flow logically from this motivation and build effectively upon prior research in the field. While the experimental evaluation is currently limited to a static risk scenario, the results convincingly demonstrate the algorithm’s ability to achieve risk-averse behavior and its adaptability to various risk preferences as defined by different coherent risk measures. 

**Quality:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and technically sound. The mathematical derivations are rigorous, and the authors provide a comprehensive set of relevant references.

**Clarity:** The paper's clarity is notably high, exceeding the standard for conference submissions. The concepts are explained in a readily understandable manner, and the presentation is well-organized.

**Originality:** The authors present novel algorithms for tackling risk-sensitive RL problems, drawing from and extending established work in risk-sensitive planning and RL.  The generalization to coherent risk measures represents a non-trivial extension and demonstrates a sufficient level of originality.

**Significance:** Risk-sensitive RL is a crucial and actively researched area. This paper makes a significant contribution by providing concrete algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL problems using coherent risk measures. The demonstration of trained policies exhibiting varied risk behaviors within the static risk testbed further strengthens this contribution. The proposed approach is well-motivated, representing a natural and logical progression from existing algorithms and problem settings – specifically, integrating policy gradient methods, actor-critic architectures, and coherent risk measure optimization.
