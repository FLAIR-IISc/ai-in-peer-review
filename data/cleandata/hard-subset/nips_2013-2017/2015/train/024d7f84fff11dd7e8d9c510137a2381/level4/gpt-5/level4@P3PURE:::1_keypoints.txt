- Proposes a new formula for static coherent risks that allows analytical derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Introduces a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions and proves consistency of the resulting policy gradient estimates.  
- Derives expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- The unified derivation is novel and a valuable contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making literature.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are available only for some risk measures.  
- No empirical evidence demonstrating practicality of the sampling-based approximation.  
- Missing experimental comparisons between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., for CVaR).  
- Paper is generally well written but clarity is hindered by covering many distinct issues within eight pages.  
- Dynamic risk policy gradients are presented at a high level with insufficient algorithmic detail.  
- Lack of algorithmic details and comprehensive experiments prevents judging practical impact; recommend focusing on static measures or expanding to a journal version with more evaluation.
