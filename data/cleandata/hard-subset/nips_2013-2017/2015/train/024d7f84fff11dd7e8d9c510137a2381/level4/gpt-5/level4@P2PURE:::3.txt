The authors analyze risk-averse Markov decision processes with both static and dynamically consistent risk measures (mappings). The main contribution is to derive the form of the gradient for both the static and dynamic settings.
The paper generalizes prior results that focused specifically on CVaR. This extension is minor but useful. The results are a straightforward extension of the policy gradient in risk-neutral settings.
The paper is well organized, well written, and easy to follow.
The results are correct as far as I can tell.

Minor comments:
- Theorem 2.1: The notation \xi P_\theta is confusing. I suggest referring to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and using the same notation.
- Line 141: “risk enevlop[e]” appears to be a typo.
- Section 3: To avoid confusing readers not familiar with the topic, it may be worth pointing out that MDPs with Markov risk measures are very tractable. The reason the problem in the paper is not tractable is the policy parametrization, not the risk measure. The paper presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results.
