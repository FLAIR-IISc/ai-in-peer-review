This paper analyzes risk-averse Markov decision processes (MDPs) using both static and dynamically consistent risk measures. The primary contribution is the derivation of the policy gradient's form for both the static and dynamic settings.

The work generalizes previous results that focused specifically on the CVaR risk measure. The derived gradient is a straightforward extension of the standard policy gradient from the risk-neutral setting. Consequently, this extension represents a minor, but solid and useful, contribution. The paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow, and its technical results appear to be correct.

Minor Comments:
-   In Theorem 2.1, the notation `\xi P_\theta` is confusing. For improved clarity, I suggest the authors refer to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and consider adopting its notation.
-   Line 141: There is a typo: "risk enevlope" should be "risk envelope".
-   Section 3: To avoid confusion for readers unfamiliar with this topic, it would be beneficial to state explicitly that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The authors should clarify that the intractability of the problem studied here arises from the policy parameterization, not from the risk measure itself.
