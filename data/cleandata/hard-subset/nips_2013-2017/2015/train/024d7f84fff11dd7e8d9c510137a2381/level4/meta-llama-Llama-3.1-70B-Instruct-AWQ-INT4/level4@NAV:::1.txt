The paper introduces a novel formula for static coherent risks, enabling the analytical derivation of policy gradients for various common risk measures. Additionally, it presents a sampling-based algorithm for scenarios lacking analytical solutions and establishes consistency guarantees for policy gradient estimates. The paper further extends this work by deriving policy gradients for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.

Significance and quality:
The unified approach to deriving policy gradients for coherent risk measures is a valuable and innovative contribution to the risk-sensitive decision-making literature. However, analytical solutions for Lagrangian saddle-points are limited to specific risk measures. The proposed sampling-based approximation scheme offers a potential solution for general coherent risk measures by circumventing the need for analytical saddle-point derivations. Nevertheless, the paper lacks empirical evidence supporting the viability of the proposed estimation scheme in practice. A comparative analysis of sample-based and analytical policy gradients in various problems, such as CVaR, would be highly beneficial.

Originality:
To my knowledge, the derived policy gradients, in their general form, are novel.

Clarity:
While the paper is generally well-written, its clarity is compromised by the attempt to address multiple issues within the eight-page limit. The presentation of dynamic risk policy gradients in Section 5 is overly concise, and the absence of algorithmic details and experimental evaluations hinders the reader's ability to assess the practical viability of the derived policy gradients. This content may be better suited for a longer journal publication. Alternatively, focusing on static risk measures could enhance the paper's standalone quality, allowing for more detailed explanations and comprehensive empirical evaluations beyond the basic toy example. The paper's potential is evident, but the lack of thorough experimental evaluations and narrow focus makes it challenging to fully appreciate its contributions.
