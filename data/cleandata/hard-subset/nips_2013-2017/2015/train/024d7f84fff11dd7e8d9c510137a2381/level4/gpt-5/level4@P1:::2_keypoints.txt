- Develops policy gradient methods for risk-sensitive RL covering the full class of coherent risk measures (static and time-consistent dynamic).
- Key technical idea: use the dual (risk envelope) representation of coherent risks and the envelope theorem to derive sampling-friendly gradient formulas.
- Static case: gradient expresses as an expectation under a worst-case reweighting involving Lagrange multipliers from the dual problem.
- Provides an SAA procedure combining Monte Carlo sampling with a convex program to estimate the risk envelope and gradient consistently.
- Recovers known special cases (e.g., CVaR, mean-semideviation) and offers a unifying view across many popular risk criteria.
- Dynamic case: derives a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk via a risk-sensitive value function that solves a robust Bellman equation.
- Proposes an actor-critic algorithm: critic uses robust MDP techniques to approximate the risk-sensitive value; actor estimates gradients using ξ-weighted transitions.
- Empirical demo: a small portfolio selection example shows coherent-risk choices yield distinct, interpretable policies in heavy-tailed settings, but experiments are limited.
- Relation to prior work: generalizes prior risk-specific RL (mean-variance, CVaR, exponential utility) and connects to robust MDP and robust planning literature.
- Strength: unified, principled framework that cleanly generalizes several known results via the dual representation.
- Strength: technically sound gradient derivations with clear conditions (likelihood-ratio assumptions, Slater-type feasibility) and SAA consistency.
- Strength: clear exposition, helpful examples, and concrete algorithmic proposals (sampling + convex programming; actor-critic design).
- Weakness: limited empirical scope—no dynamic MDP experiments demonstrating the actor-critic with function approximation.
- Weakness: computational concerns—static estimator needs solving a convex program per batch; runtime, scaling, and solver choices need discussion.
- Weakness: sampling requirements—assumes simulator and ξ-weighted sampling; needs clarification for single-trajectory/off-policy settings and ergodicity/absolute continuity conditions.
- Weakness: several assumptions and convergence analyses are deferred to the supplement; key conditions (Slater, bounded multipliers, stability) should be restated in main text.
- Weakness: variance and rare-event issues (e.g., CVaR at small α) lack empirical/theoretical treatment and variance-reduction guidance; compare SAA vs closed-form estimators when available.
- Weakness: needs more systematic comparisons and baselines (e.g., CVaR/mean-variance algorithms) on benchmark problems.
- Evaluation: technically solid, clear, original, and significant, but practical computational and statistical details should be expanded.
- Recommendation: positive—paper merits acceptance, with suggestions to add at least one dynamic experiment and clarify computational, sampling, and variance-reduction issues.
