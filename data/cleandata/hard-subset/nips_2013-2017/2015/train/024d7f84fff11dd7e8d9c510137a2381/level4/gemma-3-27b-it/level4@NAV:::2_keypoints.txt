- Introduces novel methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive RL using coherent risk measures.  
- Develops corresponding RL algorithms implementing these gradient estimators.  
- Generalizes prior techniques from specific risk measures to the broader class of coherent risk measures, creating a unified framework.  
- Demonstrates the approach on a financial toy example modeling assets with individual return distributions.  
- Motivation is clearly articulated and the problem formulation follows logically from prior work.  
- Algorithmic development is coherent, well-justified, and technically sound.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a relatively simple, static risk scenario.  
- Results validate the algorithms’ ability to produce risk-averse behavior and adapt to different risk preferences.  
- Paper is well-written, technically rigorous, with sound equations and a comprehensive bibliography.  
- Presentation clarity is notably high.  
- The contribution is original and non-trivial, extending existing risk-sensitive planning and RL research.  
- Addresses an important problem and presents a promising, natural extension of policy-gradient and actor-critic methods to coherent risk measures.
