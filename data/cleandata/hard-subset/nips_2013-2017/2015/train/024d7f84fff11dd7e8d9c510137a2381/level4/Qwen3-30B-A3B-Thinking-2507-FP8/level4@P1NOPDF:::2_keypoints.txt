- Introduces methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive RL using coherent risk measures and proposes novel algorithms.  
- Generalizes existing approaches for specific risk measures to the broad class of coherent risk measures, creating a unified theoretical framework.  
- Demonstrates the approach on a finance-inspired toy example with assets having distinct return distributions.  
- Motivation is compelling and builds logically on prior work in risk-sensitive planning and RL.  
- Problem formulation and algorithms naturally follow from the motivation.  
- Experiments are limited to a single-stage (static) risk problem rather than dynamic sequential settings.  
- Empirical results show the algorithm can produce risk-averse policies and handle different risk types.  
- Paper is technically sound with well-justified equations and comprehensive references.  
- Exposition is exceptionally clear, making complex concepts accessible without losing rigor.  
- Extension to coherent risk measures is a non-trivial, original contribution that meaningfully generalizes prior methods and merits publication.  
- Work is significant for providing a unified framework that extends policy-gradient and actor-critic techniques to coherent risk optimization, offering theoretical and practical value.
