- Addresses optimizing policy parameters for a broad class of coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Uses dual representation: any coherent risk = max expected value over a convex hull of probability distributions.  
- Introduces an assumption bounding maximum derivatives of the convex-hull borders (M-Lipschitz continuity).  
- Analytically derives optimal policy parameters under that Lipschitz assumption.  
- Proposes a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) settings.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible to readers new to risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Main contribution: unified analysis covering most well-known risk measures — significant theoretical advance.  
- Lacks sufficient empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.  
- Preliminaries need clarification, specifically the roles of P_{\theta} and \ksi.  
- Question whether Equation 2 is just the convex envelope and how it is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.  
- Suggestion to discuss extension of analysis to continuous-variable distributions.  
- Recommend explicitly noting the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2.  
- Overall: strong theory paper with compelling results, but proofs should be carefully verified.
