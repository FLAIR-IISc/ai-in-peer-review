- Addresses policy optimization under a broad class of coherent risk measures using their dual representation as a convex hull of distributions.
- Leverages M-Lipschitz smoothness of the convex-hull boundaries to derive analytical optimal policies and a gradient-estimation algorithm.
- Provides methods applicable to both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) decision problems.
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured, making core ideas accessible even to non-experts in risk-sensitive optimization.
- Primary strength: unified theoretical treatment that subsumes many common coherent risk measures.
- Theoretical results are compelling but would benefit from further empirical validation of the gradient-estimation algorithm.
- Preliminaries need clarification—roles of P_θ and ξ are initially unclear and should be explained more explicitly.
- Equation 2 seems to restate the convex envelope definition; clarify why this is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1 and explain their relationship.
- Analysis mainly treats discrete distributions; discuss generalizability and extension to continuous distributions.
- Theorem 4.2’s proof appears to depend on smoothness of constraint functions—state this dependency explicitly in the paper.
- Recommend thorough verification of proofs and a more extensive empirical evaluation to strengthen the paper’s impact.
