- Proposes a new formula for a broad class of static coherent risk measures enabling analytical derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Presents a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions and proves consistency guarantees for the policy gradient estimates.  
- Provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures is novel and a valuable contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making literature.  
- Analytical saddle-point solutions exist only for some risk measures, limiting direct applicability of analytical gradients.  
- The sampling-based approximation could extend applicability to general coherent risk measures by avoiding analytical saddle-point derivations.  
- No empirical evidence is provided to show the practicality of the sampling-based estimation scheme.  
- Experimental comparison between sampling-based and analytical policy gradients (e.g., for CVaR) is needed.  
- Paper appears original: policy gradient derivations in this general form are novel.  
- Generally well written, but clarity suffers from addressing many distinct issues within eight pages.  
- Dynamic risk policy gradients (Section 5) are presented at a high level with insufficient algorithmic detail and no experimental evaluation.  
- Recommendation: either expand into a longer journal version or narrow focus to static risk measures to allow more detailed exposition and comprehensive empirical evaluation.
