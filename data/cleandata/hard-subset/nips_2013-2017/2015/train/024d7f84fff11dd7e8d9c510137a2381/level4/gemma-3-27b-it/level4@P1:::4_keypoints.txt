- Problem: finding policy parameters that minimize a general coherent risk measure for decisions.  
- Scope: considers a broad class of coherent risk measures defined by convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance.  
- Approach: uses dual representation of coherent risk measures as a convex hull of probability distributions; risk = max expected value over that hull.  
- Key assumption: boundaries of the convex hull have bounded derivatives (M‑Lipschitz continuity).  
- Results: derives analytical policy-gradient expressions and a general gradient-estimation algorithm for both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) cases.  
- Writing: paper is well-written and organized, accessible despite reviewer’s limited background in risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Main contribution: unified theoretical analysis covering many known risk measures — a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Empirical limitation: numerical illustration is limited; more extensive experiments are needed to show practical effectiveness.  
- Clarity issue 1: preliminaries unclear—roles of Pθ and ξ are confusing.  
- Clarity issue 2: question whether Equation 2 is just the convex envelope and why it’s considered more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.  
- Generalization question: request to comment on how analysis extends to continuous random variables.  
- Technical detail request: ask to briefly state the role of smoothness of constraint functions in proving Theorem 4.2.  
- Proofs: results appear compelling but proofs require careful verification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: generality, theoretical rigor building on convex optimization and RL, and potential impact in finance/robotics/control.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation and some technical details that may be hard for nonexperts; clearer preliminaries needed.
