Presents methods to estimate policy gradients for risk-sensitive problems using general coherent risk measures.  
Extends prior work by providing a unified framework that covers a broad class of coherent risk measures.  
Proposes RL algorithms (including policy-gradient and actor-critic variants) based on these gradient estimators.  
Illustrates the approach with a toy finance example where each asset has its own return distribution.  
Motivation is clear and problem formulation/algorithms follow naturally from prior work.  
Experiments are simple/static but demonstrate effective risk-averse behavior and flexibility across risk types.  
Technical content and equations are sound, relevant references are included, and the paper is very clear.  
Originality is sufficient: extending to general coherent risk measures is nontrivial and constitutes a meaningful contribution.  
Significance: advances risk-sensitive RL by providing concrete algorithms for coherent risk optimization.  
Limitation: empirical evaluation is limited to a static toy problem, not complex or sequential RL tasks.
