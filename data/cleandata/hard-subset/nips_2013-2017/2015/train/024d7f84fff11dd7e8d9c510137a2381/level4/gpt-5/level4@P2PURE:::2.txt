This paper develops methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive settings with coherent risk measures and proposes RL algorithms based on these estimates. It generalizes prior techniques designed for specific risk measures to the broader class of coherent risk measures, yielding a unified perspective. The algorithm is illustrated on a toy finance example in which each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation is very clear, and the problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from it and from prior work. The experimental evaluation is relatively modest, as it focuses only on a static risk problem. Nevertheless, the results appear to support the algorithm’s risk-averse behavior and its flexibility across different types of risk.

Quality: The paper is well written. The equations are technically sound, and all relevant references are provided.

Clarity: The clarity of the paper is well above average.

Originality: The paper introduces new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL, building on related work in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to the proposed approach is nontrivial. The level of originality appears sufficient.

Significance: Risk-sensitive RL is an important and timely topic, and this paper makes significant contributions. It presents clear algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures and demonstrates the behavior of learned policies under different risk settings on a static risk problem. The approach is well motivated and represents a natural extension of existing algorithms and frameworks (policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization).
