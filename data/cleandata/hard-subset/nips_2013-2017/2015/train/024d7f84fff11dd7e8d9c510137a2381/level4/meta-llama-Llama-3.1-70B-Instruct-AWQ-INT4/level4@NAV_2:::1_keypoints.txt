- Proposes a new formula for static coherent risks enabling analytical policy gradients for many common risk measures.
- Introduces a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions and proves consistency of the policy gradient estimates.
- Provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- The unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risks is novel and valuable to risk-sensitive decision making.
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are available only for some risk measures.
- The sample-based approximation could extend applicability but no empirical evidence is provided for its practical viability.
- Lacks experimental comparison between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., for CVaR), which would be helpful.
- Dynamic risk policy gradients (Section 5) are presented at a very high level with no algorithmic details or evaluations.
- Paper attempts too many topics within eight pages, harming clarity.
- Recommends splitting the work or focusing on static risk measures to allow more detail and comprehensive empirical evaluation.
- Overall interesting and potentially broadly applicable, but the lack of comprehensive experiments makes it hard to judge.
