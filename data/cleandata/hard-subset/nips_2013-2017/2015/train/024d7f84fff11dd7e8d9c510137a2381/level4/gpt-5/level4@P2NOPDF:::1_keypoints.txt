- Introduces a new representation for static coherent risk measures that enables closed-form policy gradient derivations for many common risks.  
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytic solutions and proves consistency of the gradient estimators.  
- Provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Unified derivation is compelling, novel, and a valuable contribution to risk-sensitive decision making.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for certain risk measures, limiting direct applicability.  
- The sampling-based approximation could broaden applicability by avoiding explicit saddle-point solutions.  
- Major weakness: lacks empirical evidence that the estimator is practical or competitive.  
- Needs experimental comparisons (e.g., sample-based vs analytical gradients for CVaR) to assess accuracy, sample efficiency, and computational cost.  
- Novelty: deriving policy gradients for coherent risk measures in this general form appears original.  
- Clarity: generally well written but eight-page format tries to cover too much, impairing clarity.  
- Section 5’s treatment of dynamic risk is high-level and missing algorithmic details and empirical validation.  
- Suggest narrowing scope to static risk measures for a clearer, self-contained paper with a comprehensive empirical evaluation.  
- Overall: interesting paper with broad potential, but insufficient experiments and algorithmic detail; tighter focus or more validation would strengthen the submission.
