- Novel unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures; valuable contribution.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for a subset of risk measures — key limitation.  
- Authors propose a sampling-based approximation to handle general coherent risk measures.  
- No empirical evidence is provided to validate the sampling-based estimator in practice — major omission.  
- Reviewer recommends experiments comparing sample-based and analytical gradients on benchmarks (e.g., CVaR).  
- Originality: the generalized policy-gradient form appears to be novel.  
- Clarity suffers from trying to cover too much within eight pages; dynamic risk section is high-level.  
- Lack of algorithmic details and experimental results prevents judging practical applicability.  
- Suggest narrowing scope to static risk measures to allow fuller exposition and empirical evaluation.  
- Overall: interesting theoretical work with potential, but practical significance is unclear without stronger experiments and clearer presentation.
