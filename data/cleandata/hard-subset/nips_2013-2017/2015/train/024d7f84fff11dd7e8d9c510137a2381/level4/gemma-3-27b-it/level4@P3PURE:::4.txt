The authors address the problem of finding policy parameters that minimize a given risk measure. They consider a general class of risk measures defined by coherence properties – convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance – and leverage a dual representation of these coherent risk measures as a convex hull of probability distributions. Specifically, the risk of a random variable (e.g., a cost) is defined as the maximum expected value within this hull. The paper introduces an assumption regarding the family of coherent risk measures, bounding the maximum derivatives of the convex hull’s boundaries with an M-Lipschitz continuity constraint. Under this assumption, the authors analytically derive optimal policy parameters with respect to the risk measure and propose a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static (one-step decision) and dynamic (sequential) cases.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. Despite my limited background in risk-sensitive optimization, I was able to follow the core arguments. The principal contribution of this work lies in its unified analysis of a broad class of risk measures encompassing many well-known examples, which I consider a significant advance. However, a more comprehensive empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm would strengthen the paper.

I have the following comments and questions: 1) The preliminaries section lacks clarity. I found the roles of $P_{\theta}$ and $\ksi$ unclear. 2) Is Equation 2 simply the definition of a convex envelope? If so, why is this considered more restrictive than Theorem 2.1? 3) The authors should comment on the extension of their analysis to distributions over continuous variables. 4) In the concise paper format, mentioning the role of the smoothness of the constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2 would be beneficial. 

Overall, this is a strong theoretical paper offering a general approach to risk-sensitive decision-making. The results appear compelling, but a thorough verification of the proofs is recommended.
