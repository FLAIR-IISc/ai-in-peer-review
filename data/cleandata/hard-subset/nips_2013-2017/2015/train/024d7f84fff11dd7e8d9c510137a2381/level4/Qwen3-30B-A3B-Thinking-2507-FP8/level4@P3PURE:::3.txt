

The authors analyze risk-averse Markov decision processes under static and dynamically consistent risk measures (mappings), with the primary contribution being the derivation of the gradient form for both dynamic and static settings. This work generalizes prior results that focused exclusively on CVaR, representing a minor but useful extension. The results constitute a straightforward extension of policy gradient methods from risk-neutral settings. The paper is well organized, clearly written, and easy to follow. The technical results appear correct.  

Minor comments:  
- Theorem 2.1: The notation $\xi P_\theta$ is ambiguous. We suggest adopting the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of [26] for consistency.  
- Line 141: "risk enevlop" should be "risk envelope". Section 3 should clarify that MDPs with Markov risk measures are tractable, while the intractability in this paper arises solely from the policy parametrization—not from the risk measure itself. This distinction is important for readers unfamiliar with the topic.  

The paper presents a minor but significant extension of existing results.
