1. Problem: optimizing policy parameters to minimize coherent risk measures (convex, monotone, translation invariant).  
2. Approach: leverages dual representation of coherent risks as the convex hull of probability distributions (risk = max expected value).  
3. Key assumption: convex-hull boundary functions are M-Lipschitz (bounded maximum derivatives).  
4. Results: derive analytical expressions for optimal policy parameters under the assumption.  
5. Results: propose a general gradient-estimation algorithm applicable to static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) settings.  
6. Presentation: paper is well-structured and clearly written, accessible to non-specialists in risk-sensitive optimization.  
7. Contribution: unified analysis covering a broad class of risk measures, representing a significant theoretical advance.  
8. Empirical weakness: more comprehensive empirical evaluation of the gradient algorithm is needed.  
9. Clarification needed: roles of P_θ and ξ in the preliminaries are unclear and should be clarified.  
10. Clarification needed: Equation (2) appears to be the standard convex envelope; its relationship to Theorem 2.1 needs explicit justification.  
11. Extension requested: discuss how the analysis extends to continuous domains.  
12. Clarification requested: explicitly state the role of constraint smoothness in establishing Theorem 4.2.  
13. Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with compelling results, but proofs require careful verification.
