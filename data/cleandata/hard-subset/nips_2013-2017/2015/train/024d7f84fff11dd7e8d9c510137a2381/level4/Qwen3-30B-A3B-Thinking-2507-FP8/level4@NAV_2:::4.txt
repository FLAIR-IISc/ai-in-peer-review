

The authors address the problem of optimizing policy parameters to minimize risk measures characterized by coherence properties including convexity, monotonicity, and translation invariance. The methodology leverages a dual representation of coherent risk measures as a convex hull of probability distributions, where risk corresponds to the maximum expected value within this hull. The authors introduce an assumption that the convex hull boundaries are M-Lipschitz continuous, which enables them to analytically derive optimal policy parameters and develop a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static (single-stage) and dynamic (sequential) settings.

The paper is clearly written and well-organized, making it accessible even to readers unfamiliar with risk-sensitive optimization. The primary contribution lies in providing a unified framework for a broad class of coherent risk measures, encompassing many established measures. This represents a significant advancement, though a more thorough empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient estimation algorithm would strengthen the work.

The following clarifications would improve the paper:
1. The preliminaries section requires greater clarity regarding the roles of Pθ and ξ.
2. Equation (2) appears to define a convex envelope; it would be beneficial to explain why this formulation is more restrictive than the representation in Theorem 2.1.
3. A discussion of how the analysis extends to continuous distributions would be valuable.
4. The significance of the smoothness assumptions on constraint functions for the proof of Theorem 4.2 should be explicitly addressed.

This is a strong theoretical contribution that establishes a general framework for risk-sensitive decision-making. The results are compelling, though the proofs require careful verification.
