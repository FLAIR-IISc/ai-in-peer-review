- Introduces a novel formula for static coherent risks that enables analytical derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm to estimate policy gradients when analytical solutions are unavailable, with consistency guarantees.  
- Extends results to derive policy gradient expressions for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risk measures is a valuable and original contribution to risk-sensitive decision making.  
- Analytical saddle-point solutions are currently limited to specific risk measures, restricting direct applicability.  
- The sampling-based approximation is promising for broader classes of coherent risks but needs practical validation.  
- Paper lacks empirical validation of the sampling-based estimation scheme, weakening assessment of feasibility.  
- Recommends experimental comparison between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR) to strengthen the work.  
- Generally well-written but attempts too much in limited space, reducing clarity and depth.  
- Section on dynamic risk policy gradients is high-level and missing algorithmic details and experiments.  
- Suggests either expanding into a journal for full treatment or narrowing focus to static risk measures for a more self-contained paper.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful results, but limited experiments and broad scope make full assessment difficult.
