- Proposes a unified framework extending policy gradient methods to the entire class of coherent risk measures, covering static and dynamic settings.
- Static case: derives a new policy gradient formula via the dual representation and envelope theorem, yielding a two-step estimator combining sampling and convex programming.
- Dynamic case: derives a risk-sensitive policy gradient theorem relating gradients to a risk-sensitive value function and proposes an actor-critic algorithm.
- Generalizes and unifies prior bespoke results (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance), often simplifying earlier proofs.
- High technical quality with elegant derivations and a consistency proof for the gradient estimator (Proposition 4.4).
- Numerical example effectively illustrates conceptual points but is not a comprehensive empirical evaluation.
- Lacks benchmarking on sample efficiency, convergence speed, and computational overhead versus specialized methods.
- Exceptionally clear writing and structure, with accessible background on coherent risk measures.
- Suggest clarifying in Section 3 that intractability arises from large state spaces/policy parameterizations, not from coherent risk measures per se.
- Original and significant: enables tailoring risk-sensitive agents to domain-specific needs and should guide future research.
- Pros for acceptance: novel general framework, technical soundness, high potential impact, and simplification of specific-risk derivations.
- Cons against acceptance: may be viewed as an incremental extension of policy gradient theory and suffers from limited experimental validation.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, substantial contribution, strongly recommend acceptance despite limited experiments.
- Minor comments: clarify notation ξPθ (or define Q with dQ/dPθ=ξ), fix typo "risk enevlop[e]," and add the suggested sentence in Section 3.
