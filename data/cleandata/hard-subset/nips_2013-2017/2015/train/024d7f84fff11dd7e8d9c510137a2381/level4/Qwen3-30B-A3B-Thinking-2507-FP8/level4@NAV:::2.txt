

This paper develops methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning problems using coherent risk measures, introducing novel RL algorithms grounded in these approaches. It systematically extends prior work on specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, establishing a unified framework. The proposed algorithm is demonstrated through a finance-domain toy example involving assets with distinct return distributions.  

The motivation is clearly articulated, and the problem formulation and algorithms logically derive from this motivation and existing literature. While the experiments address only a static risk problem, the results effectively demonstrate the algorithm's risk-averse behavior and adaptability across diverse risk types.  

Quality: The paper is well-written, with technically sound equations and comprehensive references.  
Clarity: The exposition is of excellent clarity, exceeding average standards.  
Originality: The paper introduces new risk-sensitive RL algorithms by extending prior work on risk-sensitive planning and RL; the generalization to coherent risk measures constitutes a non-trivial contribution, establishing sufficient originality.  
Significance: Risk-sensitive RL represents a significant and timely research area, and this work provides substantial contributions. It delivers clear algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures, empirically validating policy behavior under varying risk settings in a static problem. The approach is well-motivated, naturally extends existing methods (policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk optimization), and addresses an important problem domain.
