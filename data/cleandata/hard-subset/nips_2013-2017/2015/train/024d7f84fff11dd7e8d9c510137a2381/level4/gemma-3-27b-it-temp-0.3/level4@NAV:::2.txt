This paper introduces novel methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning problems utilizing coherent risk measures, and subsequently develops RL algorithms grounded in these methods. A key contribution lies in generalizing existing techniques, previously limited to specific risk measures, to the broader class of coherent risk measures, offering a unifying framework. The proposed algorithms are demonstrated through a financial toy example featuring assets with individual return distributions.

The motivation is compelling, and the problem formulation and algorithmic development logically follow from both this motivation and prior research. While the experimental evaluation focuses on a relatively simple static risk scenario, the results effectively demonstrate the algorithm’s capacity for risk-averse behavior and adaptability across various risk preferences. 

The paper is technically sound, well-written, and comprehensively referenced.

The clarity of presentation is notably high.

The work demonstrates sufficient originality by proposing new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL, building upon existing literature in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to a general coherent risk measure framework represents a non-trivial advancement.

This paper makes a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive RL, an area of growing importance. It provides well-defined algorithms for addressing risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures and illustrates the resulting policy behavior under different risk settings within a static risk problem. The approach is clearly justified by the authors and represents a natural progression from existing algorithms and problem formulations related to policy gradients, actor-critic methods, and coherent risk measure optimization.
