

The authors analyze risk-averse Markov decision processes under static and dynamically consistent risk measures (mappings). The main contribution is to derive the form of the gradient for both the dynamic and static settings. The paper generalizes previous results that focused specifically on CVaR, representing a minor but useful extension. The results constitute a straightforward extension of policy gradient methods in risk-neutral settings. The paper is well organized, well written, and easy to follow. The results appear correct. Minor comments: - Theorem 2.1: The notation $\xi P_\theta$ is confusing. I suggest adopting the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of [26]. - Line 141: "risk envelope" (correcting typo). Section 3: To avoid confusing readers unfamiliar with the topic, it would be helpful to clarify that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally tractable; the intractability in this paper arises solely from the policy parametrization, not from the risk measure itself. The paper presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results.
