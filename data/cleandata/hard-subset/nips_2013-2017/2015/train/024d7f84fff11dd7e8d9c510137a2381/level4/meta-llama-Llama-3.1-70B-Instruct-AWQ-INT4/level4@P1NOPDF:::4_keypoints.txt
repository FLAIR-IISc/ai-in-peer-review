- Problem: optimizing policy parameters to minimize general coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).
- Approach: uses dual representation of coherent risk as a convex hull of probability distributions, with risk = maximum expected value over the hull.
- Assumption: bounds on maximum derivatives of the convex-hull borders, i.e., M-Lipschitz continuity of hull boundaries.
- Results: analytic derivation of optimal policy parameters under the risk measure.
- Results: general gradient-estimation algorithm derived for both one-step (static) and sequential (dynamic) cases.
- Writing: paper is well-written and organized and mostly accessible to non-experts.
- Main contribution: unified theoretical analysis covering a broad class of common risk measures.
- Empirical gap: requests deeper empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.
- Clarification needed: preliminaries should clarify the roles of P_{\theta} and \ksi.
- Question: Equation 2 appears to define a convex envelope—why is it claimed to be more restrictive than Theorem 2.1?
- Extension: ask for commentary on generalization to continuous-variable distributions.
- Technical note: suggest stating the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2.
- Overall verdict: strong theory paper with compelling results, but proofs need verification.
