- Addresses finding policy parameters that minimize coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Focuses on a general class of coherent risk measures using their dual representation as a max expected value over a convex set of distributions.  
- Introduces an M-Lipschitz assumption on boundary-defining functions of the convex set, enabling analytic derivation of optimal policy parameters.  
- Develops a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static (single-step) and dynamic (sequential) decision problems.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and makes a significant theoretical contribution via a unified analysis encompassing many known risk measures.  
- Primary strength: a unified, general framework for risk-sensitive optimization with compelling theoretical results.  
- Main weakness: lacks a comprehensive empirical evaluation to demonstrate practical performance of the gradient algorithm.  
- Comment 1: Preliminaries need clearer explanation of the roles and relationship between the policy distribution P_theta and the random outcome ξ.  
- Comment 2: Equation 2 seems to define a convex envelope—state this explicitly and clarify its relation to Theorem 2.1 and any extra restrictions.  
- Comment 3: Current analysis appears limited to finite-support distributions; add discussion on generalization to continuous random variables.  
- Comment 4: Theorem 4.2 depends on smoothness of certain constraint functions—this assumption should be highlighted in the main text, not only the appendix.  
- Overall: Strong theoretical paper and principled approach to risk-sensitive decision-making, but proofs require thorough verification and more empirical validation.
