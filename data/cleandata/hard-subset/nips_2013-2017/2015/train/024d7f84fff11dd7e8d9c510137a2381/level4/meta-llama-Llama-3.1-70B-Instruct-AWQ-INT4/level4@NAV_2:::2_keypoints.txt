- Proposes methods to estimate policy gradients for risk-sensitive problems with coherent risk measures and derives RL algorithms (policy gradient, actor-critic).
- Extends prior methods for specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified framework.
- Validates the approach on a toy finance example where each asset has its own return distribution.
- Motivation is clear and problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from prior work.
- Experiments are simple/static but show risk-averse behavior and flexibility across different risk types.
- Paper is well written; equations are technically sound and references are comprehensive.
- Clarity is above average.
- Originality is sufficient; the extension to coherent risk measures is nontrivial.
- Significance: provides important contributions to risk-sensitive RL with clear algorithms and demonstrations.
- Arguments for acceptance: unified view, well-motivated algorithms, and demonstrated policy behavior under different risk settings.
- Arguments against acceptance: experiments limited to a static problem; lacks evaluations on dynamic risk problems and detailed convergence-rate analysis.
