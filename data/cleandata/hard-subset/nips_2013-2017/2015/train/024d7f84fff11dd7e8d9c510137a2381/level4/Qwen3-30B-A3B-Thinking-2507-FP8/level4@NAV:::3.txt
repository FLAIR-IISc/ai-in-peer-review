

The authors investigate risk-averse Markov decision processes under both static and dynamically consistent risk measures (mappings). The primary contribution lies in deriving the explicit gradient form for both dynamic and static settings.  
The work generalizes prior results restricted to CVaR, representing a minor yet valuable extension. The results constitute a straightforward adaptation of policy gradient methods from risk-neutral frameworks.  
The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and logically presented.  
The findings appear correct based on my assessment.  
Minor suggestions:  
- Theorem 2.1: The notation $\xi P_\theta$ is ambiguous. I recommend adopting the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of [26] for consistency.  
- Line 141: "risk enve[op]lo" → "risk envelope". Section 3 should clarify that Markov decision processes with Markov risk measures are generally tractable; the intractability in this paper arises solely from policy parametrization, not the risk measure itself.  
The paper offers a minor but robust and significant extension of existing literature.
