- Paper presents a unified framework for deriving policy gradients for static coherent risk measures, enabling analytical solutions for many common metrics.  
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm for coherent risk measures lacking analytical saddle-point solutions.  
- Establishes consistency guarantees for the proposed sampling-based policy gradient estimates.  
- Derives policy gradients for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Novelty: the general-form policy gradient derivations appear original.  
- Significance: unified derivation is a valuable contribution to risk-sensitive RL.  
- Limitation: analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are available only for specific risk measures.  
- Practical gap: the sampling-based scheme lacks empirical validation of its viability.  
- Suggestion: an experimental comparison between analytical and sample-based gradients (e.g., CVaR) across problems would strengthen claims.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written but over-scoped for eight pages, reducing depth and readability.  
- Section 5 (dynamic risk gradients) is high-level, missing algorithmic details and experiments, and may be better suited to a journal extension.  
- Recommendation: narrow focus to static risk measures to allow deeper empirical evaluation and improved clarity.
