- Derives gradient formulas for static and time-consistent dynamic coherent risk measures in risk-sensitive MDPs, enabling policy gradient methods.  
- Generalizes prior work (e.g., CVaR) to a broad class of coherent risk measures; useful but incremental.  
- Results are a straightforward extension of risk-neutral policy gradient methods.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and technically sound with appropriate literature connections.  
- Proofs are provided in supplementary material; results appear correct.  
- Minor issues: notation ξPθ is potentially confusing—follow Theorem 6.6 in [26]; typo “risk enevlop[e]”.  
- Suggest clarifying that tractability issues arise from policy parametrization, not from Markov risk measures themselves.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technical soundness, generality of framework, clarity of presentation, and potential real-world impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: contribution is incremental and empirical evaluation is limited/simple.
