- Introduces a novel formula for static coherent risks enabling analytical policy-gradient derivations for several common risk measures.  
- Derivations of policy gradients for coherent risks are an intriguing and significant contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are provided only for a subset of risk measures.  
- Proposes a general sampling-based approximation algorithm for cases lacking analytical solutions.  
- Establishes consistency guarantees for the estimated policy gradients from the sampling scheme.  
- Lacks empirical validation comparing the sampling-based gradients to analytical counterparts, limiting assessment of practical utility.  
- Recommends experiments (e.g., CVaR) to strengthen the paper by demonstrating practical performance.  
- Presentation suffers from breadth within an eight-page limit; Section 5 on dynamic risk is high-level and missing algorithmic details.  
- Suggests narrowing scope to static risks or expanding to a longer paper to improve clarity and allow thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Claims of novelty appear well-founded: the general-form policy gradients seem original.
