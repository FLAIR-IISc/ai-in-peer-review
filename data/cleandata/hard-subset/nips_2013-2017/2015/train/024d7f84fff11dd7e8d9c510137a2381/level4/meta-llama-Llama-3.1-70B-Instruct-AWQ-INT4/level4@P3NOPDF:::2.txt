This paper presents novel methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems with coherent risk measures and proposes reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms based on these methods. The authors extend existing methods for specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified view. The algorithm is illustrated with a toy example in the finance domain, where each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation behind the paper is clear, and the problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from this motivation and previous works. The experiment, although relatively simple as it only addresses a static risk problem, demonstrates the quality of the algorithm in terms of its risk-aversive behavior and flexibility in handling different risk types.

The quality of the paper is high. The equations are technically sound, and all relevant references are provided. The writing is clear and concise, making the paper easy to follow. The clarity of the paper is well above average, with the authors doing an excellent job of explaining complex concepts and ideas.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems based on related works on risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension to the proposed approach is not straightforward, and the originality of the paper seems sufficient.

The significance of the paper lies in its contribution to the field of risk-sensitive RL, an interesting and important problem. The authors provide clear algorithms that solve risk-sensitive RL with coherent risk measures and demonstrate the behavior of trained policies with different risk settings on a static risk problem. The proposed approach is clearly motivated by the authors and represents a natural extension to existing algorithms and problems, including policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors have made a significant contribution to the field of risk-sensitive RL. The paper's clarity, originality, and significance make it a valuable addition to the literature.
