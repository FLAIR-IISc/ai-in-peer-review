- Extends policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, covering static and dynamic settings.  
- Static case: uses dual representation and the Envelope Theorem to derive a general policy gradient (Theorem 4.2) and a sampling+convex-programming estimator.  
- Dynamic case: derives a policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk (Theorem 5.2) and proposes an actor-critic algorithm tied to a risk-sensitive value function.  
- Generalization/unification: provides a single principled framework subsuming many prior risk-sensitive RL approaches.  
- Technical quality: sound, elegant use of duality and Envelope Theorem leading to insightful gradient formulas.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized with helpful background on coherent risk measures.  
- Incremental contribution: largely a synthesis and extension of established tools rather than a fundamentally new method.  
- Limited experiments: only a simple contextual bandit; lacks empirical validation of performance, sample complexity, and scalability (especially actor-critic).  
- Notation issue: `ξP_θ` is unconventional and should be defined or aligned with the cited source.  
- Problem framing: clarify that intractability stems from policy parametrization and large state spaces; tabular dynamic Markov risk problems are solvable via dynamic programming.  
- Typo: fix "risk enevlop[e]" on line 141.  
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable theoretical contribution that unifies prior work and merits acceptance, but would be stronger with more extensive empirical validation.
