- Addresses optimizing policy parameters to minimize coherent risk measures using their dual representation.
- Uses dual form (max expected cost over a convex set of distributions) to derive analytic characterization and a gradient estimation algorithm.
- Key technical assumption: boundaries of the convex set are M-Lipschitz continuous, which enables the results.
- Algorithm applies to both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) decision problems.
- Paper is well-written and presents a unified framework that covers many common coherent risk measures.
- Empirical evaluation is limited; stronger experimental analysis of the proposed gradient algorithm is needed.
- Preliminaries unclear: distinction between policy distribution P_theta and underlying random variable xi should be clarified earlier.
- Clarify Equation 2: explain why the convex envelope representation is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.
- The analysis appears to assume discrete distributions; discuss generalization to continuous random variables.
- Theorem 4.2 seems to rely on smoothness of constraint functions—state this assumption explicitly in the main text.
- Strong theoretical contribution overall, but full verification of proofs is warranted.
