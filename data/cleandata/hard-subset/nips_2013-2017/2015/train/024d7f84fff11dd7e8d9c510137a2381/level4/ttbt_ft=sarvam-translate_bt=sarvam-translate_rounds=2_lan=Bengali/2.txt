This paper presents a method for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems using continuous risk measurement and proposes an RL algorithm based on it. This paper specifically extends existing risk measurement methods in the general class of continuous risk measurement and provides an integrated approach. The algorithm is illustrated with a toy example of financing, where each asset has its own return distribution.

The purpose behind this is very clear, and the problem structure and algorithms are naturally followed from this purpose and previous work. The experiment is relatively simple because it is done on a static risk problem. However, the results seem to support the quality of the algorithm in terms of risk-resistant behavior and flexibility in various types of risks.

Quality: The paper is well-written. The equations are technically correct, and all relevant references are provided.
Clarity: The clarity of this paper is much above average.
Originality: This paper proposes a new algorithm for risk-sensitive RL problems based on risk-sensitive planning and RL-related work. The proposed method is not a direct extension. The originality of this paper seems sufficient to me.
Importance: Risk-sensitive RL is an interesting and important problem, and this paper makes a significant contribution. This paper presents a clear algorithm that solves risk-sensitive RL through continuous risk measurement and demonstrates the behavior of trained policies with different risk settings on a static risk problem. The proposed method is clearly inspired by the authors and is a natural extension of existing algorithms and problems (policy gradients, actor-criticism, and continuous risk measurement optimization).
