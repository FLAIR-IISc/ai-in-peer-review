- Introduces a novel formula for static coherent risk measures enabling analytical derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical solutions and proves consistency of the policy gradient estimates.  
- Derives policy gradients for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures, extending results to dynamic settings.  
- Unified derivation for coherent risk measure gradients is a valuable and novel contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are limited to specific risk measures, restricting closed-form applicability.  
- The sampling-based approximation could broaden applicability but lacks empirical validation of practical viability.  
- Recommends experimental comparison between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR) to demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Paper is generally well-written but tries to cover too many topics within an eight-page limit, harming clarity.  
- Section 5’s treatment of dynamic risk gradients is high-level with insufficient algorithmic detail and no experiments.  
- Suggests splitting the work to focus on static risk measures for deeper technical detail and comprehensive empirical validation.  
- Absence of thorough experimental evaluations beyond a rudimentary toy example prevents assessment of practical performance.
