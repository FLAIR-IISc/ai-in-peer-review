- Presents a unified framework extending policy gradient methods to all coherent risk measures using the dual representation and the envelope theorem.  
- Derives general policy gradient formulas for static coherent risk and for time-consistent Markov coherent (dynamic) risk (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2).  
- Static case: provides formulas generalizing CVaR, derives mean-semideviation gradient, and proposes a sampling + convex programming estimator when no closed-form exists.  
- Dynamic case: develops a risk-sensitive policy gradient theorem and proposes an actor-critic algorithm based on a risk-sensitive value function.  
- Empirical illustration on a simple portfolio optimization problem showing different risk measures yield qualitatively different strategies.  
- Theoretical development is sound, leveraging established stochastic programming and coherent risk theory; main theorems appear correct.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear motivation and accessible preliminaries for non-experts.  
- Examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation) effectively demonstrate specialization of the general framework.  
- Novel contribution: first unified, general policy gradient framework for the entire class of coherent risk measures.  
- The sampling + convex programming approach to gradient estimation is a novel practical contribution.  
- Significant potential impact for risk-sensitive RL in real-world domains (finance, robotics, operations research).  
- Major weakness: lack of comprehensive empirical evaluation, especially no experiments validating the proposed dynamic actor-critic algorithm.  
- Practical concerns not addressed: potential high variance in gradient estimates for tail risks and computational overhead of solving a convex program per step.  
- Overall, strong theoretical and expository contribution with limited experimental validation, warranting acceptance but needing more empirical study.
