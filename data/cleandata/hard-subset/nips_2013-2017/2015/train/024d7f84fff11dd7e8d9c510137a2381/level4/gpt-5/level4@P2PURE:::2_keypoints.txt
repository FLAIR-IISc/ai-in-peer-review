- Develops methods to estimate policy gradients for risk-sensitive RL using coherent risk measures and proposes corresponding RL algorithms (including policy gradient and actor-critic variants).
- Generalizes prior techniques for specific risk measures to the broader class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified perspective.
- Illustrates the approach on a toy finance example where each asset has its own return distribution.
- Motivation is very clear and the problem formulation and algorithms follow naturally from prior work.
- Experimental evaluation is modest, focusing only on a static risk problem.
- Empirical results support the algorithm’s risk-averse behavior and its flexibility across different risk types.
- Paper is well written; equations are technically sound and relevant references are provided.
- Clarity is well above average.
- The extension is nontrivial and the level of originality is sufficient.
- The work is a significant and timely contribution to risk-sensitive RL, naturally extending policy gradient/actor-critic and coherent risk optimization frameworks.
