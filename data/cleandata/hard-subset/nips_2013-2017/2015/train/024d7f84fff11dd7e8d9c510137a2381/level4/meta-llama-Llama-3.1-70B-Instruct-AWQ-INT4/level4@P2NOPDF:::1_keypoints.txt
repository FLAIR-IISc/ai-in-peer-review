- Proposes a new unified formula for static coherent risks enabling analytical derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Presents a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical saddle-point solutions, with consistency guarantees for the gradient estimates.  
- Derives policy-gradient expressions for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Unified derivation of policy gradients is a valuable and novel contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for some risk measures, limiting closed-form applicability.  
- The sample-based approximation could extend applicability to general coherent risks by avoiding analytical saddle-point derivations.  
- The paper lacks empirical evidence demonstrating practical viability of the sampling scheme.  
- A comparative experimental study (e.g., between sample-based and analytical gradients for CVaR) is recommended to assess practicality.  
- Clarity is hurt by addressing multiple issues within an eight-page limit; Section 5 (dynamic risks) is too high-level and lacks algorithmic details.  
- Suggest narrowing focus (e.g., static risk measures) or expanding to a longer journal version to enable fuller empirical evaluation and clearer presentation.
