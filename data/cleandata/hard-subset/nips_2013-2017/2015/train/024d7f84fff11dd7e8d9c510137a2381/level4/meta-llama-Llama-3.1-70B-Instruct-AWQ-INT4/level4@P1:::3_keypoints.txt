- Main contribution: derive the form of the policy gradient for both dynamic and static risk-averse MDPs under general coherent risk measures.  
- Generalization: extends prior CVaR-specific results to the entire class of coherent risk measures.  
- Technical novelty: results are a straightforward extension of risk-neutral policy gradient methods (minor but useful).  
- Presentation: paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow.  
- Correctness: theoretical results appear correct and are supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Related work: provides a thorough discussion and clear differentiation from existing methods.  
- Notation issue: ξPθ in Theorem 2.1 is confusing; recommend adopting notation from Theorem 6.6 in [26].  
- Tractability clarification: note that MDPs with Markov risk measures are tractable and the difficulty comes from policy parameterization, not the risk measure—should be stated in Section 3.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound with honest evaluation of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Clarity assessment: clear writing with sufficient detail for reproducibility by experts.  
- Originality assessment: novel in combining familiar techniques to cover all coherent risk measures rather than a single risk measure.  
- Significance assessment: results are useful and likely to be built upon by other researchers/practitioners.  
- Pros: solid extension, well-motivated approach, clear exposition.  
- Cons: extension is incremental; more experiments or comparisons would strengthen the empirical case.  
- Recommendation: accept—useful contribution to risk-sensitive reinforcement learning.
