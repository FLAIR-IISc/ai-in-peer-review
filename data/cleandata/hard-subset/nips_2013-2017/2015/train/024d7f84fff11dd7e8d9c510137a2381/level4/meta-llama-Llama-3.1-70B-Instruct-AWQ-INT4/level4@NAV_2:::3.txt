The authors analyze the risk averse Markov decision process setting with static and dynamically consistent risk measures (mappings). The main contribution is, in particular, to show the form of the gradient for both the dynamic and static setting. The paper is a generalization of previous results, which focused specifically on CVaR. This extension represents a minor, but useful, contribution. The results are a simple extension of the policy gradient in risk-neutral settings.

The paper is well organized, well written, and easy to follow. The results are correct as far as I can tell. The authors provide a thorough overview of the problem, and the proofs are sound and well-explained.

The main strength of the paper is its ability to generalize previous results to a broader class of risk measures. This is a significant contribution, as it provides a more comprehensive understanding of risk-sensitive Markov decision processes. The authors also provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem and the proposed solution.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the results may not be entirely surprising, given the existing literature on policy gradients and risk-sensitive Markov decision processes. However, the authors do provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem and the proposed solution, which makes the paper easy to follow.

Minor comments:

- Theorem 2.1: The notation ξPθ is confusing. I suggest referring to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and using the same notation.
- Line 141: risk envelope - Section 3: To avoid confusing readers not familiar with the topic, it may be worth pointing out here that MDPs with Markov risk measures are very tractable. The reason the problem in the paper is not tractable is because of the policy parametrization and NOT because of the risk measure.
