- Introduces a unified framework for policy gradients under coherent risk measures, extending beyond specific metrics like CVaR and mean-variance.  
- Main contributions: (1) sampling-based gradient estimator for static coherent risk using convex programming with consistency guarantees.  
- Main contributions: (2) dynamic programming-based policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk enabling actor-critic style algorithms.  
- Theoretical results generalize prior methods and show existing approaches as special cases, demonstrating novelty.  
- Significant theoretical advance but lacks empirical validation of the proposed sampling-based estimator’s practical viability.  
- Toy experiment in Section 6 is insufficient to assess scalability or real-world applicability, especially for dynamic risk.  
- Recommends experimental comparison between analytical and sample-based gradient estimators (e.g., CVaR in non-trivial domains).  
- Dynamic section (Section 5) is overly abstract due to space limits, omitting algorithmic details, convergence analysis, and empirical validation.  
- Two-phase sampling procedure for gradient estimation is described only briefly, with no discussion of computational cost or implementation challenges.  
- Suggests narrowing focus to static risk (or removing dynamic section) to allow deeper treatment, rigorous experiments, and clearer exposition.  
- As submitted, claims about practical utility are unverified; dynamic component lacks sufficient detail to assess novelty or applicability.  
- Overall recommendation: weak reject due to insufficient empirical evidence and clarity limitations from overambitious scope.
