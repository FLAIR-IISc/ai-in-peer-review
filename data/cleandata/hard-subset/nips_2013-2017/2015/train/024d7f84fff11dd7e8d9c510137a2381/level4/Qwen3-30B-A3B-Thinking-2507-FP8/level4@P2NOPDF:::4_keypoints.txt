- Addresses optimizing policy parameters to minimize general coherent risk measures using dual representation over a convex set of probability measures.  
- Assumes M-Lipschitz continuity on the boundary of the dual set to derive analytical optimal policy parameters and unified gradient estimators.  
- Proposes a unified gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both single-step (static) and sequential (dynamic) decision settings.  
- Paper is well-structured and accessible, aiding readers unfamiliar with risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Primary contribution: unifies analysis of a broad class of risk measures, covering most well-known examples—a significant theoretical advance.  
- Lacks comprehensive empirical validation to demonstrate practical utility of the proposed gradient algorithm.  
- Specific comment: clarify roles and interplay of P_theta (policy parameterization) and xi (random variable) in Section 2.  
- Specific comment: justify the claim that Equation 2 is "more restrictive" than Theorem 2.1, since convex envelope typically gives the dual representation.  
- Specific comment: extend or discuss generalization from discrete to continuous distributions (measure-theoretic extensions or approximations).  
- Specific comment: explicitly state the required smoothness of constraint functions used in the proof of Theorem 4.2.  
- Contextualize the Lipschitz assumption with respect to practical feasibility in real-world risk-averse settings.  
- Proofs appear sound but recommend verification of technical steps, particularly in Theorems 3.1 and 4.2.  
- Overall: strong, general, and rigorous theoretical contribution; addressing notation precision and empirical validation would strengthen the paper for NeurIPS.
