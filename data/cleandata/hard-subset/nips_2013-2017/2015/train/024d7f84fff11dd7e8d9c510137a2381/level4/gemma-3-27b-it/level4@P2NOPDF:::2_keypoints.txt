- Introduces methods for estimating policy gradients for risk-sensitive RL using coherent risk measures and proposes corresponding RL algorithms.  
- Generalizes prior techniques (previously limited to specific risk measures) to the broader class of coherent risk measures, offering a unifying framework.  
- Demonstrates algorithms on a toy financial portfolio example with independent asset return distributions.  
- Motivation is compelling and clearly articulated, with logical problem formulation and algorithmic development building on prior work.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a static risk scenario but shows the method achieves risk-averse behavior and adapts to different coherent risk preferences.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and technically sound, with rigorous mathematical derivations.  
- Provides a comprehensive set of relevant references supporting the work.  
- Clarity of presentation is high; concepts are explained understandably and organization exceeds typical conference standards.  
- Presents novel algorithms and a non-trivial generalization to coherent risk measures, indicating sufficient originality.  
- Makes a significant contribution to risk-sensitive RL by integrating policy gradient, actor-critic methods, and coherent risk measure optimization.
