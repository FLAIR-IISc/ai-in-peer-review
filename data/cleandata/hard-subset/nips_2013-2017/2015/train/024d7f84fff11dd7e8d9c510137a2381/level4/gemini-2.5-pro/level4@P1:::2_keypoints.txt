- Proposes a general framework extending policy-gradient RL to the full class of coherent risk measures.  
- Unifies treatment of static (total discounted return) and time-consistent dynamic risk in MDPs.  
- For the static case, derives a new policy gradient formula via the dual representation of coherent risk.  
- Introduces an algorithm for the static case combining sampling with convex programming.  
- For the dynamic case, presents a novel policy gradient theorem and an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Major strengths: originality and significant generalization of prior risk-sensitive methods.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous, elegant, and leverages duality and the envelope theorem.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and makes the complex material accessible.  
- Algorithms are natural extensions of standard policy gradient and actor-critic methods, aiding adoption.  
- Empirical validation is limited: experiments only cover a static portfolio selection example.  
- Lacks empirical evaluation of the proposed actor-critic method on complex sequential tasks.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with practical potential, but experimental coverage should be expanded.
