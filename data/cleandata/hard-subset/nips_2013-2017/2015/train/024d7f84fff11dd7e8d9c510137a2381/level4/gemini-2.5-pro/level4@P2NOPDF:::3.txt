This paper investigates policy gradient methods for risk-averse Markov Decision Processes (MDPs), considering both static and dynamically consistent risk measures. The primary contribution is the derivation of the policy gradient's analytical form for both of these settings.

The work generalizes previous results, which have primarily focused on specific risk measures such as the Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR), to a broader class of risk mappings. The resulting policy gradient forms are natural extensions of their counterparts in the standard risk-neutral setting. While this is an incremental step, it represents a solid, useful, and important contribution to the field of risk-averse reinforcement learning.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the arguments easy to follow. The theoretical results appear to be sound.

**Minor Comments:**

*   **Line 141:** Typo, "enevlope" should be "envelope".
*   **Theorem 2.1:** The notation \\(\xi P_\theta\\) may be confusing. For improved clarity, I suggest the authors consider adopting the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of reference [26], which addresses a similar concept.
*   **Section 3:** To avoid potential confusion for readers less familiar with this area, it would be beneficial to explicitly clarify the source of the problem's intractability. The authors should state that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The computational difficulty addressed in this work arises specifically from the policy parametrization, not from the inherent nature of the risk measures themselves. This clarification would strengthen the paper's motivation.
