This paper presents methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems with coherent risk measures, and proposes RL algorithms based on them. The paper particularly extends existing methods tailored to specific risk measures to the general class of coherent risk measures, thus providing a unified framework. The approach is illustrated with a simple example in a financial domain, focusing on a static risk problem.

The motivation is clear, the problem formulation and algorithms flow naturally from the motivation and prior work. While the experiment is relatively simple, the results support the quality of the algorithm, demonstrating its risk-averse behavior and flexibility across different risk types.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and well-written, with equations clearly presented and all relevant references cited. The theoretical contributions appear rigorous. 

**Clarity:** The paper exhibits excellent clarity. The concepts are explained in a manner accessible to researchers familiar with reinforcement learning and optimization. 

**Originality:** The paper proposes novel algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems, building upon prior work in risk-sensitive planning and RL. Extending existing techniques to the general coherent risk measure framework is non-trivial, and the level of originality appears sufficient for publication.

**Significance:** Risk-sensitive RL is an important and actively researched area. This paper makes a significant contribution by presenting clear algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL problems with coherent risk measures, and demonstrating the behavior of trained policies with different risk settings. The proposed approach is well-motivated and represents a natural extension of existing algorithms and problems (policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization).

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses an important problem in reinforcement learning – risk-sensitive decision making.
*   It provides a unifying framework for dealing with a broad class of risk measures (coherent risk measures).
*   The theoretical results appear sound and are supported by a numerical example.
*   The algorithms are clearly presented and build upon established techniques.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The numerical example is relatively simple and could be expanded to more complex environments.
*   The convergence analysis of the actor-critic algorithm is relegated to the supplementary material and could benefit from more detailed discussion in the main paper.
*   The paper primarily focuses on theoretical developments; a more extensive empirical evaluation could strengthen the contribution.
