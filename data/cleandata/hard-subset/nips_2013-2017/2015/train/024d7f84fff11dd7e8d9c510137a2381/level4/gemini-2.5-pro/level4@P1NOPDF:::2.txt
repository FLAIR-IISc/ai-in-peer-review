This paper presents methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems involving coherent risk measures, and proposes corresponding reinforcement learning algorithms. The work notably extends existing methods, which were designed for specific risk measures, to the general class of coherent risk measures, thereby providing a unified framework. The proposed algorithms are illustrated with a simple example in the financial domain, where a portfolio is constructed from assets with distinct return distributions.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem formulation and algorithms follow logically from this motivation and prior work. The experimental evaluation is relatively simple, as it is confined to a static risk problem. However, the results effectively support the algorithm's performance, demonstrating both its intended risk-averse behavior and its flexibility when applied to different risk measures.

**Quality:** The paper is well-written and technically sound. The mathematical derivations appear correct, and the authors provide comprehensive citations to relevant work.

**Clarity:** The paper is written with well-above-average clarity.

**Originality:** The paper proposes new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL by building on related work in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The proposed extension is non-trivial, and I find the work to possess sufficient originality for this venue.

**Significance:** This paper makes a significant contribution to the important area of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. It presents clear algorithms for optimizing coherent risk measures and demonstrates that the resulting policies exhibit sensible behavior under different risk settings in a static problem. The approach is well-motivated and represents a natural extension of existing policy gradient and actor-critic methods to this problem class.
