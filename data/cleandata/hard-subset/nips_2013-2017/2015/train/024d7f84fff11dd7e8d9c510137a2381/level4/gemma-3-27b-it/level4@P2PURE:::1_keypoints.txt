- Introduces a novel formula for computing policy gradients for static coherent risk measures, enabling analytical derivations for many common risks.
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical saddle-point solutions.
- Provides consistency guarantees for the sampling-based policy gradient estimates.
- Derives policy gradients for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.
- Unified derivation for coherent-risk policy gradients is a significant and valuable contribution.
- Sample-based approximation potentially extends applicability to a broader class of coherent risks by avoiding analytical saddle-point requirements.
- Paper lacks empirical validation of the proposed estimation scheme’s practical viability.
- Recommends experimental comparison between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR) across tasks.
- Claims novelty: the general-form policy gradients appear to be a novel result.
- Generally well-written but clarity suffers from covering many topics within page limits.
- Dynamic risk section is high-level and missing algorithmic details and experiments; suggests narrowing scope or expanding to a journal for full treatment.
