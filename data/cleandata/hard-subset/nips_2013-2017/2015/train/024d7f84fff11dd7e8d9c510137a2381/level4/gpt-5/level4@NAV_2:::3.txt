The paper investigates risk-averse Markov decision processes using both static coherent risk measures and time-consistent dynamic (Markov coherent) risk measures. The main contribution is to derive policy-gradient expressions for both the static and the dynamic settings.

This work extends prior approaches that focused primarily on CVaR to the broader class of coherent risk measures. The extension is incremental but useful, and the results amount to a relatively direct generalization of the standard risk-neutral policy gradient framework.

The manuscript is clearly written, well structured, and easy to read.

The technical claims appear correct to the best of my assessment.

Minor comments:
- Theorem 2.1: The notation ξ Pθ is potentially confusing. Consider referring to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and aligning the notation accordingly.
- Line 141: “risk enevlop[e]” → “risk envelope.”
- Section 3: To prevent confusion for readers new to the area, it would help to emphasize that MDPs with Markov coherent risk measures are tractable; the intractability in this paper stems from the policy parameterization rather than from the risk measure itself.

Overall, this is a modest yet solid and valuable generalization of existing CVaR-focused results.
