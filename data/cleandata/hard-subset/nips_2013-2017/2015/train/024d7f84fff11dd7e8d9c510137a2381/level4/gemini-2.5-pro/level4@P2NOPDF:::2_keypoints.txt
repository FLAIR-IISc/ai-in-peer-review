- Introduces a principled framework for estimating policy gradients for the general class of coherent risk measures and proposes practical actor-critic algorithms.  
- Generalizes prior risk-sensitive methods into a unified, theoretically grounded framework covering many risk measures.  
- Empirical demonstration on a financial portfolio optimization task shows the method can produce policies with varying risk aversion.  
- Technically sound: clear, correct mathematical derivations and well-situated within relevant literature.  
- Proposes novel, well-motivated extensions of policy gradient and actor-critic methods.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized presentation, making contributions easy to understand.  
- Main weakness: limited experimental scope—evaluation is confined to a static single-step problem and lacks sequential/dynamic RL benchmarks.  
- Overall: a high-quality, significant theoretical contribution that lays a strong foundation for future empirical work.
