This paper introduces novel methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems using coherent risk measures, and proposes corresponding reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms. Notably, the authors generalize existing approaches tailored to specific risk measures, thereby providing a unified framework encompassing the broader class of coherent risk measures. The proposed algorithm is exemplified through a financial toy example, where each asset is characterized by its own return distribution.

The motivation behind this work is clearly articulated, and the problem formulation and algorithm design logically follow from this motivation and prior research. Although the experimental setup is relatively simple, focusing on a static risk problem, the results suggest that the algorithm effectively captures risk-averse behavior and demonstrates flexibility in accommodating various risk types.

In terms of quality, the paper is well-written, with technically sound equations and comprehensive referencing. The clarity of the presentation is above average. The originality of the paper lies in its proposal of novel algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems, building upon related work in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The extension of the proposed approach is non-trivial, and the originality is deemed sufficient.

The significance of this paper stems from its contributions to the important and interesting problem of risk-sensitive RL. The authors provide clear algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL problems with coherent risk measures and demonstrate the behavior of trained policies under different risk settings in a static risk problem. The proposed approach is well-motivated and naturally extends existing algorithms and problems, including policy gradient, actor-critic, and coherent risk measure optimization.
