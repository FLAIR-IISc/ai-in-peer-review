- Introduces a general framework extending policy gradient methods to the full class of coherent risk measures, unifying prior measure-specific approaches.  
- Derives novel policy gradient formulas for static coherent risk measures using the envelope theorem (Theorem 4.2).  
- Proposes a practical algorithm for static risk combining sampling with convex programming.  
- Extends results to time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk measures and provides a corresponding policy gradient theorem (Theorem 5.2).  
- Develops a risk-sensitive actor-critic algorithm for the dynamic Markov coherent risk setting.  
- Demonstrates method flexibility on a portfolio optimization task, handling different risk preferences and return distributions.  
- Theoretical foundations are solid, leveraging convex optimization and the dual representation of coherent risk; derivations appear correct.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent notation with illustrative examples (CVaR, mean-semideviation).  
- Original contribution: first general policy gradient algorithm for the entire class of coherent risk measures in RL.  
- Results are significant and likely influential for risk-sensitive RL in real-world applications.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: lacks demonstration of the proposed actor-critic algorithm on a sequential/dynamic decision problem.
