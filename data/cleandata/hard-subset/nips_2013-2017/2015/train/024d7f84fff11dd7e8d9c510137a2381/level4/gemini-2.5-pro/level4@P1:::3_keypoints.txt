- Paper develops policy gradient methods for the class of coherent risk measures, covering both static and time-consistent dynamic cases.
- Core contribution: novel gradient formulas using the dual representation of coherent risk as an expectation under a worst-case distribution.
- For static risk measures, proposes an algorithm combining sampling with convex programming to estimate gradients.
- For dynamic risk measures, derives a new policy gradient theorem and proposes an actor-critic style algorithm.
- Work generalizes and unifies prior results focused on specific risk measures (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance).
- Strength: high generality and unification of risk-sensitive RL methods into a single framework.
- Strength: paper is well-organized and clearly written, presenting theory from simple to complex cases.
- Strength: theoretical derivations appear correct and elegantly use envelope theorem and likelihood-ratio techniques.
- Weakness: perceived originality is incremental since core techniques (dual representation, likelihood ratios) are established.
- Con/Pro summary: important and well-executed generalization, but methodological novelty may be limited.
- Suggestion: clarify notation ξPθ (e.g., explicitly define as a re-weighting of Pθ) to avoid reader confusion.
- Suggestion: clarify why the problem is intractable here (intractability stems from parameterized policies and function approximation, not the risk measures).
- Minor: fix typo "enevlope" → "envelope" on line 141.
