This paper introduces novel methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning problems employing coherent risk measures, and proposes corresponding RL algorithms. A key contribution is the generalization of existing techniques – previously focused on specific risk measures – to the broader class of coherent risk measures, offering a unified framework. The approach is illustrated with a simple example in a financial setting, showcasing how different assets with varying return distributions are selected under different risk preferences.

The motivation is well-articulated, and the problem formulation and algorithmic development logically follow from this motivation and relevant prior work. While the experimental evaluation is limited to a static risk problem, the results appear to validate the quality of the proposed algorithm, demonstrating its ability to exhibit risk-averse behavior and adapt to different risk types.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and clearly written. Equations are well-defined, and the authors provide comprehensive referencing. The theoretical grounding appears solid, and the extension of existing techniques is presented rigorously.

**Clarity:** The paper’s clarity is notably high. The concepts are explained in a straightforward manner, and the notation is consistent throughout. The organization facilitates understanding the progression of ideas. 

**Originality:** The work presents new algorithms for risk-sensitive RL leveraging coherent risk measures. Extending existing risk-sensitive planning and RL methods to this generalized framework is a non-trivial undertaking. The originality of the approach appears sufficient for publication. The authors effectively build upon existing foundations while introducing meaningful advancements.

**Significance:** Risk-sensitive RL is a valuable and increasingly important research area. This paper provides significant contributions to the field by offering practical algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL problems with coherent risk measures and demonstrating the behavior of trained policies across diverse risk settings. The proposed approach is well-motivated and represents a natural extension of established algorithms and problem domains (policy gradient methods, actor-critic techniques, and coherent risk measure optimization).

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Generalization:** The paper successfully extends existing risk-sensitive RL techniques to the broader class of coherent risk measures, providing a unified framework.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The algorithms are theoretically well-grounded and supported by rigorous analysis.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, making it accessible to a broad audience.
*   **Potential Impact:** The proposed approach has the potential to be widely adopted by researchers and practitioners working on risk-sensitive decision-making problems.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Experimentation:** The experimental evaluation is relatively simple and restricted to a static risk problem. More extensive and diverse experiments, potentially including dynamic risk scenarios and more complex environments, would strengthen the paper.
*   **Computational Complexity:** While the paper presents algorithms, a more detailed discussion of their computational complexity, especially concerning the convex programming steps, would be beneficial.



Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning. The clear presentation, theoretical soundness, and potential impact outweigh the limitations of the current experimental evaluation. I recommend acceptance.
