- Proposes a unified framework deriving policy-gradient formulas for general coherent risk measures, including a new gradient formula for static risks.  
- Extends the framework to time-consistent dynamic Markov coherent risk measures and provides derived gradient expressions.  
- Theoretical contribution is novel and unifies prior work by leveraging the dual representation of coherent risk measures.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for a subset of risk measures, limiting closed-form applicability.  
- Introduces a general sampling-based gradient estimator for cases without analytical solutions and proves its consistency.  
- No empirical evidence is provided to validate the sampling-based estimator’s practical viability.  
- Recommends an experimental comparison between analytical and sample-based estimators (e.g., CVaR) to validate the approximation.  
- Paper’s clarity suffers from trying to cover too many topics within the page limit, especially weakening the presentation of dynamic risk results.  
- Section on dynamic risk is high-level and lacks algorithmic details and experimental results, preventing assessment of practical usefulness.  
- Suggests focusing on static coherent risk measures for a conference submission and adding comprehensive empirical evaluation and implementation details.  
- Conclusion: strong and potentially significant theoretical work, but limited practical impact due to lack of experiments and overly ambitious scope.
