- Introduces a general gradient formula for static coherent risk measures and a sampling-based convex-optimization procedure for cases without closed-form saddle points; establishes consistency and derives dynamic risk gradients.
- Unified derivation of policy gradients for coherent risks is novel and a valuable contribution.
- Explicit Lagrangian saddle-point solutions are given only for some risk measures, limiting direct applicability.
- The proposed sampling-based estimator could broaden applicability but no empirical evidence is provided that it is practical.
- The paper should include experimental comparisons (e.g., sampling-based vs analytic gradients for CVaR) to strengthen claims.
- Presentation appears original in this general form.
- Paper is generally well written, but attempting many topics in eight pages reduces clarity.
- Dynamic risk policy gradients (Section 5) are presented at a high level and lack algorithmic details and empirical validation.
- Suggests either a longer journal version or a narrower focus on static risk measures to allow more detail and stronger experiments.
- Overall interesting and potentially broadly useful, but limited experimental evidence makes practical viability difficult to assess.
