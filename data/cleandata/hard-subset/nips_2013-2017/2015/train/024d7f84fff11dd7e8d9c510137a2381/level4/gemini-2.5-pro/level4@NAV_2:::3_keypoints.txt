- Introduces a policy gradient framework for optimizing coherent risk measures in MDPs, covering both static trajectory-level and time-consistent dynamic risk measures.  
- Presents novel policy gradient formulas for static and dynamic coherent risk settings.  
- Static-risk method combines sampling with convex programming via the dual representation of coherent risk measures.  
- Dynamic-risk contribution: derives a risk-sensitive policy gradient theorem and proposes an actor-critic algorithm.  
- Unifies and generalizes prior risk-sensitive policy gradient work (e.g., CVaR, mean-variance) into a single framework.  
- Strength: high significance and generality, allowing selection of appropriate coherent risk measures for different problems (demonstrated on heavy-tailed example).  
- Strength: strong technical quality; static gradient derivation uses the envelope theorem and the dynamic extension is logically sound.  
- Strength: clear exposition with good background on coherent risk measures.  
- Weakness: contribution may be viewed as incremental since core tools (dual representation, likelihood-ratio trick) are established.  
- Suggestion: authors should more explicitly describe novel technical challenges when generalizing from specific measures (e.g., CVaR) to the full class and how they overcome them.  
- Clarification needed: explicitly state that intractability arises from policy parameterization in large/continuous state-action spaces (tabular MDPs with Markov coherent risk are tractable).  
- Notation issue: the term ξP_θ in Theorem 2.1 is unconventional and should be clarified or replaced with standard notation from [26].  
- Minor typo: "risk enevlop[e]" on line 141.
