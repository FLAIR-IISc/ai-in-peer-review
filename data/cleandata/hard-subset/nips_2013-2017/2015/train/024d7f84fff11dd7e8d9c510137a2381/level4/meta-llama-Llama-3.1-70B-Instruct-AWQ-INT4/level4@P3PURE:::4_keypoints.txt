- Problem: Finding policy parameters that minimize a general class of coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Risk class: Uses a dual representation of any coherent risk measure as a convex hull of probability distributions; risk is the maximum expected value over that hull.  
- Assumption: Authors assume the convex-hull borders are M-Lipschitz (bound on maximum derivatives).  
- Contributions: Analytic derivation of optimal policy parameters under the risk measure and a general gradient-estimation algorithm for static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) cases.  
- Writing: Paper is well-written and organized; accessible even to readers not familiar with risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Significance: Unified analysis covering many well-known risk measures; considered a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Empirical gap: Deeper empirical analysis of the proposed gradient algorithm is needed.  
- Comment 1: Preliminaries unclear—roles of P_theta and ksi need clarification.  
- Comment 2: Question about Equation 2—seems like convex envelope definition and why it is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.  
- Comment 3: Suggestion to discuss extension/generalization to continuous-variable distributions.  
- Comment 4: Suggestion to explicitly state the role of smoothness of constraint functions in proving Theorem 4.2.  
- Overall: Strong theory paper with compelling results, but proofs require verification.
