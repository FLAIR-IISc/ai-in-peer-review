- Extends policy-gradient and actor-critic methods to the general class of coherent risk measures, providing a unifying framework for risk-sensitive RL.  
- Derives novel, theoretically grounded policy gradient formulas applicable to a wide range of coherent risk measures.  
- Technical development is solid: equations are clear, justified, and supported by relevant references.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and mostly reproducible for expert readers, though more implementation detail would help.  
- Motivation and problem formulation are clear and follow naturally from prior work.  
- Illustrative financial example demonstrates risk-averse behavior and flexibility across different risk types.  
- Originality is strong: non-trivial generalization beyond prior work, meriting NIPS-level contribution.  
- Significance is high: practical and theoretical applicability for robust decision-making across domains.  
- Could more explicitly distinguish the approach from robust MDP methods (e.g., [19]) beyond scalability claims.  
- Experimental validation is limited to a simple static risk setting and should be expanded.  
- Scalability to large-scale problems is not demonstrated and remains an open concern.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparisons with existing risk-sensitive RL methods (e.g., CVaR-based approaches).  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggested improvements in experiments, scalability evaluation, and clarification of relations to robust MDPs.
