- Analyzes risk-averse MDPs with both static and dynamically consistent risk measures, providing a comprehensive examination.  
- Main contribution: derivation of gradient form for both dynamic and static settings, extending existing results.  
- Generalizes prior CVaR-specific work to a broader class of risk measures; useful though potentially minor.  
- Results are essentially a straightforward extension of policy-gradient methods from risk-neutral settings.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; results appear correct.  
- Notation concern: ξ P_θ in Theorem 2.1 may be confusing; suggest referencing Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting similar notation.  
- Typo: line 141 "enevlop[e]" should be "envelope."  
- Suggestion for Section 3: clarify that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally tractable and that intractability arises from policy parametrization, not the risk measure.  
- Overall assessment: a solid and important extension to the field of risk-averse MDPs.
