The authors analyze the risk-averse Markov decision process setting with static and dynamically consistent risk measures (mappings). The main contribution is to show the form of the gradient for both the dynamic and static setting.

The paper is a generalization of previous results, which focused specifically on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR). This extension represents a minor, but useful, contribution. The results are a simple extension of the policy gradient in risk-neutral settings.

The paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow. The results are correct as far as I can tell.

The authors provide a thorough overview of the problem, including a detailed introduction to coherent risk measures and their application in Markov decision processes. The paper is divided into clear sections, each addressing a specific aspect of the problem.

The technical contributions of the paper are sound, and the authors provide a clear and concise derivation of the gradient formulas for both static and dynamic risk measures. The use of sampling-based estimators and convex programming is well-motivated and effectively applied.

One minor comment is that the notation ξPθ in Theorem 2.1 is confusing. I suggest referring to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and using the same notation.

Additionally, in Section 3, it may be worth pointing out that Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) with Markov risk measures are very tractable. The reason the problem in the paper is not tractable is because of the policy parametrization and not because of the risk measure.

Overall, the paper presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results. The authors effectively demonstrate the applicability of their approach to a range of risk measures, including CVaR and mean-semideviation.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a clear and concise derivation of the gradient formulas for both static and dynamic risk measures.
* The authors effectively apply sampling-based estimators and convex programming to estimate the gradients.
* The paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow.

Weaknesses:

* The notation ξPθ in Theorem 2.1 is confusing.
* The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the tractability of MDPs with Markov risk measures.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results.
* The authors effectively demonstrate the applicability of their approach to a range of risk measures.
* The paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper does not present a significant new contribution to the field.
* The results are a simple extension of the policy gradient in risk-neutral settings.

Overall, I recommend accepting the paper, as it presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results. However, the authors should address the minor comments and consider revising the paper to improve clarity and readability.
