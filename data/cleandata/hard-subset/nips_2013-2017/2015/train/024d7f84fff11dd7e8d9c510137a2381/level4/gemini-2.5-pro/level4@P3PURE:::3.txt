This paper analyzes risk-averse Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) using both static and dynamically consistent risk measures. The primary contribution is the derivation of the policy gradient for both the static and dynamic settings. This work generalizes previous results that focused specifically on the Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR), providing a straightforward extension of the policy gradient theorem from risk-neutral settings to a broader class of risk measures.

The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow. The technical results appear to be correct. While the extension is incremental, it represents a solid, useful, and important contribution to the field of risk-averse reinforcement learning.

**Minor Comments:**

*   **Theorem 2.1:** The notation `\xi P_\theta` is potentially confusing. For improved clarity, I suggest the authors consider adopting the notation used in Theorem 6.6 of reference [26].
*   **Line 141:** There is a typo in "risk enevlope".
*   **Section 3:** To avoid potential confusion for readers less familiar with this area, it would be beneficial to explicitly state that MDPs with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The authors should clarify that the intractability of the problem addressed in this paper stems from the policy parameterization, rather than from the choice of risk measure itself.
