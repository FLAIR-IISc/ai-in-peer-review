- Addresses optimization of policy parameters to minimize a broad class of coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Uses the dual representation of coherent risks: risk = maximum expected cost over a convex hull of probability distributions.  
- Imposes a central assumption that the convex-hull boundaries are M-Lipschitz continuous.  
- Provides an analytical characterization of optimal policy parameters under this Lipschitz assumption.  
- Proposes a general gradient-estimation algorithm applicable to both single-step (static) and sequential (dynamic) decision problems.  
- Manuscript is well-structured, clearly written, and accessible despite the reviewer’s limited background in risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Principal contribution: unified theoretical analysis covering most commonly used risk measures — a significant theoretical advance.  
- Paper would be strengthened by a more comprehensive empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.  
- Specific comment: preliminaries need clearer distinction between the policy-induced distribution P_θ and the random variable ξ.  
- Specific comment: Equation 2 seems like the standard convex envelope definition — clarify how it is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1.  
- Specific comment: include discussion on extending analysis to continuous-variable distributions.  
- Specific comment: emphasize the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2 in the main text.  
- Overall: strong theoretical paper with compelling results, but proofs require careful verification.
