- Tackles finding policy parameters that minimize a broad class of coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Uses the dual representation of coherent risk measures as the convex hull of probability distributions, with risk = max expected value over that hull.  
- Assumes M-Lipschitz continuity of the convex hull boundaries to derive optimal policy parameters.  
- Proposes a general gradient estimation algorithm applicable to both static and dynamic settings.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, accessible to readers unfamiliar with risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Main contribution: a unified theoretical analysis that covers most well-known risk measures.  
- Lacking: more empirical analysis/experiments to validate the proposed gradient algorithm.  
- Request clarification in preliminaries about the roles of P_{\theta} and \ksi.  
- Note that Equation 2 appears to define a convex envelope; ask for explanation of how restrictive this is compared to Theorem 2.1.  
- Ask whether the analysis generalizes to distributions over continuous variables.  
- Suggest explicitly mentioning the role of smoothness of constraint functions in the proof of Theorem 4.2.  
- Overall: presents a robust theoretical framework with compelling results that require proof verification.
