- Proposes a general formula for policy gradients for static coherent risk measures, enabling derivation for many common risks.  
- Provides a sampling-based algorithm for cases without analytical saddle-point solutions, with consistency guarantees.  
- Extends the framework to derive policy gradients for Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Unified derivation is novel and a valuable contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making.  
- Key limitation: analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for a subset of risk measures.  
- The sample-based approximation is promising but lacks empirical validation in the paper.  
- Missing experimental comparison between sample-based and analytical gradients (e.g., CVaR) to assess practical viability.  
- Clarity is generally good but hampered by covering many topics within an eight-page limit.  
- Section on dynamic risk policy gradients is too high-level and lacks algorithmic details and experiments.  
- Content would fit better in a longer journal paper or be split; focusing on static risks could allow deeper analysis and empirical work.  
- Overall interesting and potentially valuable, but practical impact is unclear due to limited experimental evaluation.
