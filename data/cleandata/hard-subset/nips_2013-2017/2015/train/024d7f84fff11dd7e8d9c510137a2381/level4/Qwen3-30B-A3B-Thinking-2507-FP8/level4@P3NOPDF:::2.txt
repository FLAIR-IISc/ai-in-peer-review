

This paper introduces a unified framework for policy gradient estimation in risk-sensitive reinforcement learning (RL) under coherent risk measures, extending prior work on specific risk metrics to the general class of coherent measures. The authors present novel RL algorithms that integrate coherent risk optimization with standard policy gradient and actor-critic methods, demonstrating their applicability through a finance-focused toy example involving static portfolio optimization with assets exhibiting distinct return distributions.  

The motivation is compelling and clearly articulated, directly addressing the need for flexible risk-sensitive decision-making in sequential settings. The problem formulation and algorithmic derivations follow logically from the established theory of coherent risk measures and prior RL work, with technical rigor evident in the equations and proofs. The paper is exceptionally well-written, with precise mathematical exposition and comprehensive references covering both risk theory and RL literature. Clarity is a standout feature—concepts are introduced accessibly, and the narrative flows smoothly from motivation to implementation.  

The experimental validation, while limited to a static risk problem (i.e., fixed return distributions without sequential dynamics), effectively demonstrates two critical properties: (1) the algorithm’s risk-averse behavior aligns with theoretical expectations across diverse risk profiles (e.g., CVaR, entropic risk), and (2) its adaptability to different coherent risk measures. Results confirm that policies trained under varying risk settings exhibit the expected trade-offs between reward and risk exposure, validating the core contribution. While the static nature of the experiment is a noted limitation, it serves as a sufficient foundation for establishing the algorithm’s conceptual validity before scaling to dynamic environments.  

Originality is strong. The extension from specific risk measures (e.g., CVaR) to the general coherent risk framework is non-trivial, requiring novel adaptations to policy gradient estimation that avoid the computational pitfalls of prior approaches. The authors convincingly position their work as a bridge between risk-sensitive planning and modern RL, resolving fragmentation in the literature. This is not merely a technical tweak but a conceptual unification that enables a broader class of risk-sensitive RL problems to be addressed within a single, coherent methodology. The contribution clearly surpasses incremental improvements and meets the bar for novelty at NIPS.  

Significance is substantial. Risk-sensitive RL is a critical challenge in high-stakes applications (e.g., finance, robotics), where standard RL’s focus on expected reward often leads to suboptimal or unsafe policies. This paper provides a theoretically grounded, practically implementable solution that directly addresses this gap. By unifying coherent risk measures under a single algorithmic umbrella, it offers researchers and practitioners a versatile toolkit for designing risk-aware policies without re-engineering methods for each risk metric. The clarity of the proposed algorithms—explicitly linking risk parameters to policy behavior—enhances reproducibility and adoption. The work naturally extends existing RL paradigms, making it highly relevant to the NIPS community’s interests in scalable, robust decision-making under uncertainty.  

The paper’s sole limitation is the lack of dynamic experimental validation, which would strengthen the case for real-world applicability. However, this is a common first-step constraint in theoretical contributions, and the authors appropriately frame the static example as a proof-of-concept. Future work could explore dynamic risk settings, but the current results sufficiently establish the method’s foundational viability.  

In summary, this is a high-quality contribution that advances risk-sensitive RL through a theoretically sound, unified approach. The work is well-motivated, technically rigorous, and clearly presented, with originality and significance that align strongly with NIPS’ focus on impactful machine learning research. The static experiment, while limited, effectively demonstrates the core innovation, and the framework’s generality provides a clear path for future extensions. I recommend acceptance.
