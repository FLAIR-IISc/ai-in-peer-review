The authors have analyzed the risk aversion Markov decision process with consistent risk measurement (mapping) in a stable and dynamic manner. The main contribution is, in particular, the representation of the gradient for both dynamic and static settings.

This paper is a generalization of previous results, which specifically focused on CVaR. This extension presents a small but useful contribution. The result is a simple extension of the policy gradient in risk-neutral settings.

The paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow.

To my knowledge, the results are correct.

Small comments:
- Theorem 2.1: The symbol \xi P_\theta is misleading. I suggest mentioning theorem 6.6 in [26] and using the same symbol.
- Line 141: Risk envelope[e] - Section 3: To avoid confusion for readers unfamiliar with the subject, it can be mentioned here that MDPs with Markov risk measurement are very solvable. The reason the paper's problem is not solvable is policy parameterization, not risk measurement. The paper presents a small but solid and important extension of existing results.
