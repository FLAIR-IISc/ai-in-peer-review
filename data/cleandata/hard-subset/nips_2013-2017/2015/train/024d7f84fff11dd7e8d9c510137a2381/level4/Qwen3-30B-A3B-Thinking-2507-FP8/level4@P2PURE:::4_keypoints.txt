- Addresses finding policy parameters that minimize a general class of coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Uses dual representation of coherent risks as the convex hull of probability distributions; risk = max expected value over that hull.  
- Assumes convex-hull boundaries are M-Lipschitz (bounded max derivatives) as a key technical condition.  
- Under that assumption, derives analytic optimal policy parameters for the risk measure.  
- Proposes a general gradient-estimation algorithm applicable to both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) problems.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and largely accessible despite reviewer’s limited background in risk-sensitive optimization.  
- Primary contribution is a unified theoretical analysis covering a broad class of risk measures, encompassing most well-known examples.  
- Empirical evaluation is insufficient; more experiments would strengthen the work.  
- Preliminaries unclear about the roles of P_theta and ξ—needs clarification.  
- Question on Equation 2: it looks like the standard convex envelope definition—why is it claimed to be more restrictive than Theorem 2.1?  
- Suggest commenting on extension/generalization to distributions over continuous variables.  
- Recommend explicitly discussing how constraint function smoothness affects Theorem 4.2.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with compelling results, but proofs require careful verification.
