This paper presents methods for estimating policy gradients in risk-sensitive problems using coherent risk measures, and proposes corresponding reinforcement learning (RL) algorithms. The paper notably extends existing methods, previously specific to individual risk measures, to the general class of coherent risk measures, thereby providing a unified framework. The algorithm is illustrated through a toy example in the finance domain, modeling a scenario where each asset has its own return distribution.

The motivation is clear and the problem formulation and algorithms naturally follow from this motivation and related prior work. The experimental evaluation is currently limited to a static risk problem; however, the results appear to support the algorithm's quality in terms of its risk-averse behavior and its flexibility across different risk types. 

**Quality:** The paper is well-written and technically sound, with equations presented correctly and all relevant references included.
**Clarity:** The clarity of this paper is above average.
**Originality:** The paper proposes novel algorithms for risk-sensitive RL problems, building upon related work in risk-sensitive planning and RL. The proposed extension is not straightforward, and the paper demonstrates sufficient originality.
**Significance:** Risk-sensitive RL is an important and interesting problem, and this paper provides significant contributions to the field. It presents clear algorithms for solving risk-sensitive RL problems with coherent risk measures, and demonstrates the behavior of trained policies under different risk settings within a static risk problem. The proposed approach is well-motivated by the authors and represents a natural extension to existing algorithms and problem settings, including policy gradient methods, actor-critic frameworks, and coherent risk measure optimization.
