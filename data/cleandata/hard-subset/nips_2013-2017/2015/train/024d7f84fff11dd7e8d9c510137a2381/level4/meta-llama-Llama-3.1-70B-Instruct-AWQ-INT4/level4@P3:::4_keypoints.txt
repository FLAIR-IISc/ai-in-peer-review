- Problem: Minimizing general coherent risk measures over policy parameters in risk-sensitive decision-making.  
- Approach: Uses dual representation of coherent risk measures as a convex hull of probability distributions, with risk as the maximum expected value over the hull.  
- Assumption: Requires M-Lipschitz continuity (bounded derivatives) of the convex hull’s borders.  
- Results: Analytically derives optimal policy parameters under the risk measure given the assumption.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Provides a general gradient estimation algorithm for both static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) settings.  
- Technical quality: Paper is technically sound; proofs are provided but placed in the supplementary material rather than the main text.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized overall, but some sections (preliminaries) need more background for non-experts.  
- Originality: Novel use of the dual representation for coherent risk measures; generalizes to many known risk measures.  
- Significance: Unified analysis of a broad class of risk measures with potential wide applicability and impact.  
- Strengths for acceptance: Novel approach, clear explanations, strong potential impact, good organization.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: Assumes substantial background in risk-sensitive optimization, lacks main-paper proofs, and limited empirical evaluation.  
- Suggested improvements: Expand preliminaries, clarify some textual points, and state the role of constraint smoothness in Theorem 4.2.  
- Empirical evaluation: Paper would benefit from more experiments validating the proposed gradient algorithm.
