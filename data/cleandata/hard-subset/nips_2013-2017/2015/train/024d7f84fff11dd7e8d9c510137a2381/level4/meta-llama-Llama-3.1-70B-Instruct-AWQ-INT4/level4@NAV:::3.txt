The authors provide an in-depth analysis of the risk-averse Markov decision process framework, incorporating both static and dynamically consistent risk measures. A key contribution of this work lies in deriving the gradient form for both dynamic and static settings.

This paper serves as a generalization of prior studies that focused primarily on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR). Although this extension is not groundbreaking, it represents a valuable addition to the field. The results can be seen as a straightforward extension of policy gradient methods in risk-neutral environments.

The manuscript is well-structured, clearly written, and easy to comprehend. The presented results appear to be accurate, based on my assessment.

Minor suggestions for improvement include:

- In Theorem 2.1, the notation ξ P_θ may be confusing. It may be beneficial to refer to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopt the same notation for clarity.
- On line 141, a typographical error ("enevlop[e]") is present. 
- In Section 3, it may be helpful to note that Markov decision processes with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability of the problem presented in this paper stems from the policy parametrization, rather than the risk measure itself. Overall, the paper presents a solid, albeit minor, extension of existing research.
