- Introduces a novel formula for static coherent risk measures enabling analytical derivation of policy gradients for many common risk measures.  
- Proposes a general sampling-based algorithm for coherent risk measures lacking analytical solutions.  
- Establishes consistency guarantees for the proposed sampling-based policy gradient estimates.  
- Provides expressions for policy gradients of Markov coherent dynamic risk measures.  
- Unified derivation of policy gradients is a significant and novel contribution to risk-sensitive decision-making.  
- Analytical Lagrangian saddle-point solutions exist only for specific risk measures.  
- The sampling-based approximation could enable broader application but lacks empirical evidence of practical viability.  
- Recommends empirical comparison between sample-based and analytical policy gradients across problems (e.g., CVaR).  
- Claims of originality: the derived policy gradients appear novel in this general form.  
- Generally well-written but tries to address too many topics within the eight-page limit, harming clarity.  
- Section 5’s treatment of dynamic risk policy gradients is high-level and lacks algorithmic detail and experiments.  
- Suggests splitting the paper to focus on static risk measures for a self-contained presentation with deeper technical detail.  
- Absence of thorough experimental validation (beyond a toy example) prevents assessment of practical utility.
