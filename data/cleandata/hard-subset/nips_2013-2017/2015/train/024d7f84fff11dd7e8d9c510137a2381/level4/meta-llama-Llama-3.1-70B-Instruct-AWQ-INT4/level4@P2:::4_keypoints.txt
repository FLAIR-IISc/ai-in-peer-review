- Proposes a unified approach to risk-sensitive RL that generalizes and extends prior results.
- Considers a general class of coherent risk measures widely used in finance and OR.
- Develops policy gradient algorithms for both static and dynamic risk measures.
- Introduces a new gradient formula for static coherent risk that is amenable to sampling-based approximation.
- Provides a clear, well-organized presentation and concise introduction to risk-sensitive optimization and coherence properties.
- Gives a detailed derivation of the static coherent risk gradient and a general gradient estimation algorithm.
- Derives a new policy gradient theorem for Markov coherent risk and proposes an actor-critic style algorithm for the dynamic case.
- Main strength: delivers a general framework that unifies and extends many known risk measures and results.
- Weakness: preliminaries need clarification, especially the roles of Pθ and ξ.
- Weakness: lacks detail on extending the analysis to distributions over continuous variables.
- Weakness: theoretical proofs need independent verification.
- Overall assessment: strong theory paper with compelling results; recommendation to accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
