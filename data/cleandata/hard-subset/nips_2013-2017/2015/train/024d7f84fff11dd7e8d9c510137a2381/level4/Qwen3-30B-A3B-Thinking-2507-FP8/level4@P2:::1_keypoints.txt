- Proposes a unified framework for policy gradient methods under coherent risk measures, deriving a general gradient formula for static coherent risk, a sampling-based gradient estimator, and an extension to dynamic Markov coherent risk.

- Theoretical development is rigorous with well-motivated assumptions and clear proofs (Theorems 4.2 and 5.2), building on existing coherent risk and Markov coherent risk literature.

- Provides a consistency guarantee for the sampling-based estimator (Proposition 4.4) but lacks practical validation.

- Empirical validation is insufficient: only a toy trading example with three assets, which does not demonstrate practical utility for non-trivial risk measures.

- Dynamic risk section is superficial and lacks algorithmic details and implementation considerations necessary for replication.

- Paper tries to cover both static and dynamic results within eight pages, leading to reduced depth and clarity.

- Originality: novel unified treatment that generalizes prior risk-sensitive RL work and contextualizes contributions relative to earlier studies.

- Significance: theoretically high potential, but practical impact is unclear without empirical demonstration.

- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically sound unified framework, generalizes previous specific-risk results, provides gradient estimation guarantees, and has clear application potential.

- Arguments against acceptance: missing empirical validation, shallow dynamic section, limited numerical example, and overly ambitious scope for the page limit.

- Recommended improvements: add comprehensive empirical evaluations across multiple risk measures and domains; focus on either static or dynamic risks for depth; include pseudocode and implementation specifics.

- Reviewer recommendation: reject due to insufficient empirical validation and overly ambitious scope limiting practical impact for a conference venue.
