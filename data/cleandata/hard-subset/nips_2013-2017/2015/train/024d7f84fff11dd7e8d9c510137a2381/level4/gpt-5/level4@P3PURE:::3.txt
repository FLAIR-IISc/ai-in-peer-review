Summary:
The paper analyzes risk-averse Markov decision processes with both static risk measures and dynamically consistent risk measures (mappings). The main contribution is to derive the form of the policy gradient for both the dynamic and the static settings. The work generalizes previous results that focused specifically on CVaR, providing a minor but useful extension. The resulting gradients are a straightforward extension of the standard policy gradient in risk-neutral settings.

Evaluation:
- Clarity and organization: The paper is well organized, well written, and easy to follow.
- Technical correctness: The results appear correct as far as I can tell.
- Contribution: While incremental, the extension is solid and useful.

Minor comments and suggestions:
- Theorem 2.1: The notation ξ P_θ is confusing. I suggest referring to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopting the same notation for consistency and clarity.
- Line 141: There is a typo—“risk enevlop[e]” should be “risk envelope.”
- Section 3: To avoid confusing readers not familiar with the area, please emphasize that MDPs with Markov risk measures are very tractable. The intractability in this paper arises from the policy parameterization, not from the risk measure itself. Framing the contribution accordingly will make it clearer that the paper offers a minor but solid and important extension of existing results.
