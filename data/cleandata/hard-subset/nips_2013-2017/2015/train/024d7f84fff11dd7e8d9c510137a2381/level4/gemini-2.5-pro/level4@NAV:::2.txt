This work introduces methods to compute policy gradients for risk-sensitive reinforcement learning problems that utilize coherent risk measures, and on this basis, proposes novel RL algorithms. A key contribution of this work is the generalization of existing approaches, which were limited to specific risk measures, to the broader class of coherent risk measures, thereby offering a more unified framework. The proposed algorithms are demonstrated on an illustrative financial portfolio problem, where individual assets are characterized by distinct return distributions.

The paper's motivation is articulated with great clarity, and both the problem formulation and the proposed algorithms are a logical progression from this motivation and established prior work. The experimental validation, while informative, is somewhat limited as it is confined to a static risk setting. Nevertheless, the empirical results successfully demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness, showcasing its capacity for risk-averse decision-making and its adaptability to various risk measures.

**Quality:** The manuscript is well-composed. The mathematical derivations appear to be correct, and the authors have appropriately cited relevant literature.

**Clarity:** The presentation is exceptionally clear and easy to follow.

**Originality:** This work introduces novel algorithms for risk-sensitive RL, building upon related research in risk-sensitive planning and reinforcement learning. The extension from prior work is non-trivial, and the paper's contribution is sufficiently original.

**Significance:** The paper addresses the important and timely problem of risk-sensitive reinforcement learning, making a notable contribution to the field. It puts forward well-defined algorithms for optimizing coherent risk measures in RL and validates the resulting policies in a static risk environment under various risk preferences. The proposed framework is well-motivated and represents a natural and valuable extension of existing policy gradient and actor-critic methods to the domain of coherent risk optimization.
