- Problem addressed: policy optimization for general coherent risk measures using their dual (max-over-probabilities) representation under an M-Lipschitz boundary assumption.  
- Main technical result: characterization of optimal policy structure and a general gradient estimation algorithm for static and dynamic decision problems.  
- Key assumption: boundaries of the convex risk set are M-Lipschitz continuous.  
- Strength: provides a unified theoretical framework that generalizes many existing risk measures (e.g., CVaR).  
- Strength: well-written and accessible exposition with strong theoretical results.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited; needs broader experiments comparing performance, stability, and computational cost across measures and domains.  
- Weakness: proofs are technical and require careful verification by experts in the subfield.  
- Suggestion 1: clarify preliminaries—precise relationship between the policy-induced distribution P_theta and the random variable ξ (is ξ the environment randomness and how does P_theta transform it?).  
- Suggestion 2: clarify how Equation 2 relates to Theorem 2.1 and whether Eq.2 imposes additional restrictions compared to the general dual representation.  
- Suggestion 3: discuss generalization to continuous random variables and any extra assumptions or technical challenges for the continuous case.  
- Suggestion 4: explicitly state the smoothness requirements (e.g., smooth constraint functions) used in the proof of Theorem 4.2 in the main text.  
- Overall conclusion: strong, novel theoretical contribution and useful foundation for future work, but would benefit from more experiments and presentation clarifications.
