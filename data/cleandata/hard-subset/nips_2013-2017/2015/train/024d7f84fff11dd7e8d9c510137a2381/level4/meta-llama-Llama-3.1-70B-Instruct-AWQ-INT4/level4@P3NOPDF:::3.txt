The authors present an analysis of risk-averse Markov decision processes (MDPs) with static and dynamically consistent risk measures. The primary contribution of the paper is the derivation of the gradient form for both dynamic and static settings.

This work builds upon previous results, which focused specifically on Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR). The extension to more general risk measures represents a minor yet useful contribution. The results are a straightforward extension of the policy gradient in risk-neutral settings.

The paper is well-organized, well-written, and easy to follow. The authors have done an excellent job in presenting their ideas in a clear and concise manner. The results appear to be correct, and the mathematical derivations are sound.

A few minor comments are worth noting:

- In Theorem 2.1, the notation \xi P_\theta is somewhat confusing. It may be beneficial to refer to Theorem 6.6 in [26] and adopt a similar notation to maintain consistency and clarity.
- On line 141, there is a minor typo ("risk enevlop[e]") that should be corrected.
- In Section 3, it may be helpful to provide additional context for readers unfamiliar with the topic. Specifically, it would be worth noting that Markov decision processes with Markov risk measures are generally tractable. The intractability of the problem presented in the paper arises from the policy parametrization, rather than the risk measure itself.

Overall, the paper presents a minor but solid and important extension of existing results. The authors have made a valuable contribution to the field, and their work is likely to be of interest to researchers in risk-averse MDPs.
