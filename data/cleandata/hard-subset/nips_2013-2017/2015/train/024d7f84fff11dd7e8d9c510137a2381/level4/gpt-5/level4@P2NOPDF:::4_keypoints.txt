- Studies selection of policy parameters to minimize coherent risk measures using their dual (supremum-over-distributions) representation.
- Assumes the risk envelope boundary is M-Lipschitz and derives analytical characterizations of risk-optimal policies under this regularity.
- Proposes a general gradient-estimation algorithm applicable to static (one-step) and dynamic (sequential) problems.
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, and offers a unifying treatment covering most standard coherent risk measures.
- Main contribution is the broad, elegant theoretical framework for risk-sensitive decision-making.
- Empirical evaluation is limited; a more extensive empirical study of the gradient algorithm is needed to strengthen practical impact.
- Preliminaries are unclear about roles of P_θ versus ξ — clarify P_θ as the parameterized data-generating distribution/transition kernel and ξ as the adversarial/reweighted distribution in the risk envelope.
- Question whether Equation (2) is simply the convex envelope/support function representation and how it differs or is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1; request explanation of extra assumptions in Eq. (2).
- Ask for extension discussion to continuous state/action spaces and continuous distributions, specifying required technical conditions (measurability, compactness, density differentiability) and whether Lipschitz assumptions extend.
- Request explicit explanation of how smoothness of constraint functions (differentiability, Lipschitz/curvature bounds) is used in the proof of Theorem 4.2 and whether weaker conditions suffice.
- Overall verdict: strong theoretical paper with compelling results, but proofs should be carefully checked and empirical validation expanded.
