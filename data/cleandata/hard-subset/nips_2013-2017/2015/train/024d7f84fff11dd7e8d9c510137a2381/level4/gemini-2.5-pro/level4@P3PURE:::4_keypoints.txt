- Problem: finding policy parameters that minimize general coherent risk measures (convexity, monotonicity, translation invariance).  
- Method: uses dual representation of coherent risk as the maximum expected cost over a convex hull of probability distributions.  
- Key assumption: boundaries of the convex hull are M-Lipschitz continuous.  
- Results: analytic derivation of optimal policy parameters and a general gradient estimation algorithm for static and dynamic settings.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written and organized; explanations are mostly clear even to non-experts.  
- Contribution: unified theoretical analysis covering a broad class of risk measures, including many known examples.  
- Caveat: proofs appear compelling but require careful verification.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough empirical evaluation of the proposed gradient algorithm.  
- Comment 1: preliminaries are unclear—roles of P_{\theta} and \ksi need clarification.  
- Comment 2: Equation 2 looks like a convex envelope—clarify why it is more restrictive than Theorem 2.1’s representation.  
- Comment 3: discuss how the analysis generalizes to continuous distributions.  
- Comment 4: explicitly state the role of constraint-function smoothness (used in Theorem 4.2) in the main text.
