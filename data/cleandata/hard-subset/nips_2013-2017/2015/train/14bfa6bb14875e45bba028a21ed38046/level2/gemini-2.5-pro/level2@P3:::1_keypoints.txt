- Introduces Faster R-CNN with a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional feature maps to generate near-cost-free region proposals via sliding-window anchor predictions.
- RPN predicts objectness scores and refines coordinates for predefined anchor boxes (multiple scales/aspect ratios) from a single-scale feature map.
- Proposes a 4-step alternating training scheme to learn shared features between the RPN and the Fast R-CNN detection network.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art accuracy and large speed improvements (e.g., ~5 fps with VGG-16; proposals ≈10ms) on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012.
- Technically sound and well-executed; RPN is an elegant solution validated by extensive experiments and ablation studies.
- Ablation studies confirm benefits of shared convolutional layers, joint classification/regression outputs, and the two-stage cascade design; multi-task loss is well-motivated.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, with detailed explanations, illustrations, and reproducible training/implementation details.
- Key original contributions: unified feature-sharing RPN and the anchor box mechanism for multi-scale/multi-aspect proposals without an image pyramid.
- Distinct from prior work (OverFeat, MultiBox) by retaining a two-stage cascade and using translation-invariant, parameter-efficient anchors.
- Highly significant: solves the region-proposal bottleneck, enabling practical, near real-time high-accuracy detection and influencing many subsequent advances.
- Suggested improvements: discuss why joint end-to-end training is challenging and analyze sensitivity to anchor configuration hyperparameters.
- No substantial arguments against acceptance; reviewer regards the work as a landmark contribution.
