- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detection network to produce nearly cost-free region proposals.
- RPN is fully convolutional, predicts bounding boxes and objectness scores, and is trained end-to-end with Fast R-CNN using an alternating optimization scheme.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC and a significant speedup, achieving ~5 fps with VGG-16.
- Correctly situates the work relative to prior region-based CNNs and proposal methods (Selective Search, EdgeBoxes) and contrasts with OverFeat and MultiBox.
- Strength — Significant speedup enabling near real-time object detection.
- Strength — Improved accuracy on PASCAL VOC benchmarks.
- Strength — Elegant shared-convolution design and a well-reasoned alternating optimization approach.
- Strength — Clear, thorough experiments and ablation studies that analyze component impacts.
- Strength — Well-written, organized presentation with informative figures and tables.
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity (IoU thresholds, anchor scales/ratios, learning rates) not fully explored.
- Weakness — Evaluation limited to PASCAL VOC; lacks testing on other datasets like COCO or ImageNet.
- Weakness — Anchor box design (3 scales × 3 ratios) appears arbitrary and merits systematic exploration and explanation.
- Weakness — Implementation complexity from alternating training and shared layers could hinder reproducibility despite code release.
- Weakness — Loss weighting (λ = 10) seems empirically chosen without principled justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Pro-accept — Novel, effective approach that meaningfully advances region proposal generation for detection.
- Pro-accept — Compelling empirical results showing clear improvements over existing methods.
- Con-accept — Limited dataset scope and insufficient hyperparameter analysis are notable but minor concerns.
- Con-accept — Implementation complexity could impede adoption, though available code mitigates this.
- Overall — Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address hyperparameter sensitivity and broader evaluation.
