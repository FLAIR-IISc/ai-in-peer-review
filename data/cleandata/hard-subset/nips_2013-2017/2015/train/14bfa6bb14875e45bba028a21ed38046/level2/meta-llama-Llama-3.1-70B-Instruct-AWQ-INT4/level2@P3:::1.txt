**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares full-image convolutional features with the detection network, enabling nearly cost-free region proposals. The RPN is a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position. The authors propose a simple alternating optimization scheme to train the RPN and Fast R-CNN to share convolutional features. The method achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012, with a frame rate of 5fps on a GPU.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon recent advances in object detection, including region proposal methods (e.g., Selective Search) and region-based convolutional neural networks (R-CNNs). The authors also draw inspiration from previous work on using deep networks for locating class-specific or class-agnostic bounding boxes (e.g., OverFeat, MultiBox).

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, with a clear and well-motivated approach to addressing the problem of region proposal generation. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their method, including ablation studies and comparisons to previous work. The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on challenging object detection benchmarks.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components. The authors provide sufficient detail to enable reproduction of the results, including implementation details and hyperparameter settings.

**Originality**

The paper introduces a novel approach to region proposal generation, leveraging a fully-convolutional network to predict object bounds and objectness scores. The authors also propose a simple yet effective alternating optimization scheme to train the RPN and Fast R-CNN to share convolutional features. While the paper builds upon previous work, the combination of these ideas is new and innovative.

**Significance**

The paper has significant implications for object detection research, demonstrating a new approach to region proposal generation that is both efficient and effective. The method has the potential to be widely adopted in the field, particularly given its state-of-the-art performance on challenging benchmarks.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance**

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper introduces a novel and effective approach to region proposal generation.
* The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on challenging object detection benchmarks.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its components.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper may be seen as incremental, building upon previous work in object detection.
* Some readers may find the alternating optimization scheme to be overly simplistic.
