This paper introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with a Fast R-CNN detection network, addressing the bottleneck of region proposal computation in state-of-the-art object detection systems. The RPN is a fully convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores, and is trained end-to-end with Fast R-CNN through alternating optimization. The authors demonstrate significant speed-up and competitive accuracy on PASCAL VOC datasets. This work builds on previous advances in region-based CNNs (R-CNN [6], SPPnet [7], Fast R-CNN [5]) by tackling the remaining computational bottleneck – region proposal generation – with a deep learning approach rather than relying on traditional methods like Selective Search [22] or EdgeBoxes [24]. Related work in using deep networks for bounding box prediction, such as OverFeat [18] and MultiBox [3, 20], is discussed, and the authors highlight the advantages of their shared convolutional feature approach.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a key bottleneck:** The paper directly tackles a significant performance bottleneck in object detection pipelines.  Proposals were clearly the limiting factor after improvements to detection networks, and this work offers a compelling solution.
* **Novel approach:** Introducing a dedicated deep network (RPN) for region proposal generation, specifically designed to share convolutional features with the detection network, is a novel and effective idea.
* **Strong experimental results:** The results on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 demonstrate competitive accuracy and a substantial speed-up compared to previous methods utilizing Selective Search or EdgeBoxes.  The 5fps frame rate with VGG-16 is a notable achievement.
* **Clear explanation and well-organized paper:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the RPN architecture and the training procedure. The ablation studies are helpful in understanding the contribution of different components.
* **Availability of code:** Providing the code is excellent and greatly facilitates reproducibility and further research.
* **Careful analysis:** The discussion comparing to one-stage detectors like OverFeat and the analysis of Recall-to-IoU provide deeper insights into the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter sensitivity:** The paper doesn’t delve deeply into the sensitivity of the method to hyperparameter settings (e.g., anchor scales, aspect ratios, IoU thresholds). A more detailed analysis of these parameters would be beneficial.
* **Limited exploration of alternative training schemes:** While the alternating optimization scheme works well, a discussion of other potential joint training approaches, even if preliminary, could strengthen the paper. The authors acknowledge the difficulty of joint optimization but could offer more speculation.
* **Lack of comparison to more recent proposal methods:** Although Selective Search and EdgeBoxes were state-of-the-art at the time, the paper would be stronger with a comparison to more recently developed proposal methods that might have emerged after the submission deadline but before publication.
* **Potential for bias in anchor design:** The choice of 3 scales and 3 aspect ratios for the anchors seems somewhat arbitrary. A justification for these specific values, or an exploration of their impact on performance, could be included.
* **Clarity on “translation-invariant anchors”:** While the concept is explained, the benefit isn't immediately apparent. Expanding on *why* translation invariance is crucial would be helpful.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a significant advancement in object detection by addressing a critical performance bottleneck.
* The proposed RPN architecture is novel and demonstrably effective.
* The experimental results are compelling and show a clear improvement over existing methods.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details.
* The availability of code promotes reproducibility and future research.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* While the method is effective, the paper doesn't fully explore the sensitivity to hyperparameters or alternative training schemes.
* The comparison to other proposal methods is limited to those prevalent at the time of submission, and doesn't include more recent approaches.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of object detection. The introduction of the RPN is a clever and effective solution to the region proposal bottleneck. While there are minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper significantly outweigh them. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NIPS 2015. I rate this as a **Strong Accept**.
