- Addresses the region proposal bottleneck by integrating proposal generation into the detection network via a Region Proposal Network (RPN).  
- RPN is a fully-convolutional module operating on shared detector feature maps and predicts object proposals with objectness scores at every location.  
- Introduces "anchors" (pre-defined boxes of multiple scales/aspect ratios) to handle multi-scale, multi-shape proposals from a single-scale feature map.  
- Proposes a 4-step alternating optimization scheme to train RPN and Fast R-CNN to share convolutional layers, forming the Faster R-CNN framework.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC while running at ~5 fps with VGG-16, making region proposal nearly cost-free.  
- Strength: High significance and impact — unifies proposal and detection for large speedups and practical, accurate deep detectors.  
- Strength: Excellent originality — learnable, feature-sharing RPN and anchor mechanism avoid image/feature pyramids and improve over prior methods.  
- Strength: Strong technical quality — extensive experiments and ablations validate design choices and outperform Fast R-CNN + Selective Search.  
- Strength: Exceptional clarity — well-written, clearly motivated, with informative figures/tables and detailed experimental setup.  
- Weakness: Training complexity — the 4-step alternating training feels engineered; lacks joint end-to-end training and more explanation of challenges.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to anchors — paper lacks analysis on how anchor number/scales/aspect ratios affect performance or guidance for new datasets.  
- Weakness: Small-object performance — large feature stride (16 px) may hurt detection of very small objects; per-size performance analysis is missing.
