- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with detection networks to remove the region-proposal bottleneck.  
- RPNs implemented as fully-convolutional networks generate high-quality proposals at nearly zero extra cost when integrated with Fast R-CNN.  
- Alternating optimization trains RPNs and detection networks to share conv features, enabling 5 fps on VGG-16 while achieving state-of-the-art results (73.2% mAP on VOC2007, 70.4% on VOC2012).  
- Technically sound architecture with a well-motivated loss balancing classification and regression.  
- Demonstrates large speed and accuracy gains over traditional proposal methods (RPN ~10 ms vs. Selective Search ~1.5 s).  
- Strong ablation studies: classification scores are crucial for maintaining accuracy with fewer proposals (300 vs. 2000).  
- Ablations show the regression layer significantly improves proposal quality over anchor boxes alone.  
- Comparison to one-stage detectors (OverFeat) justifies the two-stage cascade approach.  
- Experiments are thorough (recall-IoU trade-offs, multiple architectures ZF/VGG) and implementation details plus public code support reproducibility.  
- Paper is clear and well-structured, with effective figures and precise writing situating work within prior region-based CNN and proposal literature.  
- Original anchor-based, translation-invariant design is elegant and much more parameter-efficient than MultiBox (2.4M vs. 27M parameters).  
- Argues convincingly that shared computation is superior to merely GPU-accelerating existing proposal methods.  
- Significant practical impact: makes region proposals nearly cost-free, enabling real-time detection with deep networks and influencing later systems (e.g., Mask R-CNN, RetinaNet).  
- Minor weaknesses: limited justification for the 9-anchor (3 scales × 3 ratios) choice and insufficient exploration of how the standard 300 proposals affects performance on specific categories (e.g., small objects).  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, novel, and significant work; recommend acceptance without reservation.
