**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper introduces a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares full-image convolutional features with a detection network, enabling nearly cost-free region proposals. The RPN is a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position. The authors propose a simple training scheme that alternates between fine-tuning for the region proposal task and fine-tuning for object detection, while keeping the proposals fixed. The RPN is evaluated on the PASCAL VOC detection benchmarks and achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy while reducing the computational burden of region proposal generation.

**Evaluation of Support for Claims**

The paper provides strong support for its claims through extensive experiments on the PASCAL VOC detection benchmarks. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their RPN approach by comparing it to other region proposal methods, such as Selective Search and EdgeBoxes. They also show that their method can be used with different networks, including the very deep VGG-16 model, and still achieve high accuracy and speed. The experiments are well-designed and provide a thorough evaluation of the RPN approach.

**Usefulness**

The ideas presented in the paper are practically useful for object detection tasks, particularly for applications that require real-time processing. The RPN approach can be used to improve the efficiency of object detection systems by reducing the computational burden of region proposal generation. The authors also provide code for their implementation, making it easy for others to use and build upon their work.

**Field Knowledge**
