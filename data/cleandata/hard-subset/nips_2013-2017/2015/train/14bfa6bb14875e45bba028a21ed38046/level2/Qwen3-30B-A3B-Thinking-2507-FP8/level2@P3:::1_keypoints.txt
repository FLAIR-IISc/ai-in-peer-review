- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with the detection network to eliminate proposal computation bottleneck.  
- Fully-convolutional, end-to-end proposal generator leverages Fast R-CNN feature maps, making proposal computation nearly free at test time.  
- Presents a unified Faster R-CNN architecture achieving state-of-the-art accuracy (73.2% mAP VOC2007, 70.4% VOC2012) at ~5 fps using only 300 proposals.  
- Outperforms prior proposal methods (Selective Search, EdgeBoxes) by avoiding separate proposal computation through shared features.  
- Comprehensive experiments and rigorous ablation studies dissect RPN components (cls/reg layers, anchor design) and training procedures.  
- Uses a well-motivated 4-step alternating optimization to effectively learn shared features without joint-training pitfalls.  
- RPN proposals outperform Selective Search even with far fewer proposals; recall-to-IoU curves show robustness at low proposal counts.  
- Comparison to one-stage methods (OverFeat emulation) demonstrates the two-stage cascade’s superiority in mAP.  
- Clarity and reproducibility are strong: concise architecture description, precise technical details (anchors, loss, training), and implementation notes.  
- High originality: novel embedding of proposal generation within detection via shared convolutions and a translation-invariant anchor mechanism.  
- Significance is transformative: resolves proposal bottleneck, enables real-time high-accuracy detection, and influenced many subsequent detectors (e.g., Mask R-CNN, FPN).  
- Minor weaknesses: limited analysis of failure cases (small objects, occlusion), no comparison to later one-stage detectors (contextual to 2015), and few qualitative proposal examples.  
- Meets NIPS criteria — technically sound, clearly written, original, significant — and is a strong candidate for acceptance.
