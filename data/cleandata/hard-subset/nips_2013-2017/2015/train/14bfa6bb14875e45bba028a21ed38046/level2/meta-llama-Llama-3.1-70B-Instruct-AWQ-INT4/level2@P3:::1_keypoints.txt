- Proposes a fully-convolutional Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares full-image convolutional features with the detection network to produce nearly cost-free region proposals.
- RPN predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position and is trained with a simple alternating optimization to share features with Fast R-CNN.
- Method achieves state-of-the-art detection on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 and runs at about 5 fps on a GPU.
- Builds on prior region proposal methods (e.g., Selective Search) and region-based CNNs, drawing inspiration from OverFeat and MultiBox.
- Technically sound with thorough analysis, ablation studies, and strong experimental comparisons.
- Experimental results are impressive and demonstrate clear performance gains over previous approaches.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides sufficient implementation and hyperparameter details for reproduction.
- Introduces a novel combination of a fully-convolutional RPN and alternating training, offering a fresh approach despite building on prior ideas.
- The approach is significant for object detection, being both efficient and effective with potential for wide adoption.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective method, state-of-the-art results, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: perceived as incremental over prior work and the alternating optimization may seem overly simplistic.
- Suggested revisions: discuss limitations and future work, provide a detailed computational time breakdown (RPN vs. Fast R-CNN), and add more visualizations of proposals and detections.
