- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detector to generate proposals in ~10ms, forming the Faster R-CNN system.  
- RPN slides a small network over feature maps to regress bounding boxes and predict objectness for multi-scale, multi-aspect anchors.  
- Proposes a pragmatic 4-step alternating training scheme to allow RPN and Fast R-CNN to share convolutional layers.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 while running ~5 FPS with VGG-16.  
- Significance: removes the major bottleneck of external proposal methods, reducing proposal time from seconds to milliseconds.  
- Originality: elegant, novel fully-convolutional RPN design with translation-invariant anchors enabling multi-scale proposals from a single feature map.  
- Technical quality: extensive experiments and ablation studies validate design choices (feature sharing, classification vs regression, two-stage vs one-stage, recall vs IoU).  
- Clarity: clearly written and well-structured with helpful figures and detailed training description.  
- Weakness: the 4-step alternating training is somewhat heuristic and complex, lacking deeper analysis of joint training challenges.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of anchor hyperparameter choices (scales/aspect ratios) and sensitivity to those settings.  
- Suggestion: add a diagram/flowchart illustrating the 4-step alternating training to clarify weight/data flow.  
- Suggestion: report timing for the one-stage baseline to compare speed as well as accuracy.  
- Suggestion: include mAP vs number-of-proposals plot to better illustrate the speed/accuracy trade-off.  
- Conclusion: outstanding, high-impact contribution that substantially advances unified, near-real-time object detection; strongly recommended for acceptance.
