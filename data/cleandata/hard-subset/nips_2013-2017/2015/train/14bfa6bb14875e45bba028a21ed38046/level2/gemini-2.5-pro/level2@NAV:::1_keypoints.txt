- Central contribution: Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional feature maps with the detector to generate high-quality proposals with negligible extra cost, unifying proposal and detection stages.
- Significance & originality: The learnable, feature-sharing proposal network is a novel, impactful solution that removes a major bottleneck and advances object detection toward unified deep models.
- Experimental quality: Strong empirical support on PASCAL VOC with thorough ablation studies demonstrating the benefits of feature sharing, RPN heads, and the two-stage design.
- Performance: Achieves state-of-the-art speed and accuracy for its time (notably ~5 fps with VGG-16), showing practical efficiency improvements over prior methods.
- Clarity and reproducibility: Well-written, logically presented (anchors clearly explained) and backed by publicly released code, facilitating adoption and replication.
- Main weakness: Relies on a somewhat convoluted 4-step alternating training procedure rather than a single end-to-end optimization.
- Suggested improvement: Discuss potential challenges of true joint training (e.g., unstable gradients) and explore simplifying the training pipeline into a unified process.
- Overall verdict: An outstanding, foundational contribution likely to be highly influential and widely adopted despite the minor training-methodology limitation.
