- Proposes Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with Fast R-CNN and predict k anchors per location via classification and regression.
- Introduces translation-invariant anchors enabling low output dimensionality compared to prior proposal nets.
- Uses a multi-task loss with IoU-based positive/negative anchor assignment and a pragmatic 4-step alternating training scheme to enable feature sharing.
- Achieves state-of-the-art VOC mAP (73.2%/70.4%) and practical speed (5 fps with VGG-16; ~10 ms marginal cost for proposals on GPU).
- Thorough experiments and ablations (classification vs regression, NMS, number of proposals, shared vs unshared features) and comparisons to Selective Search and EdgeBoxes.
- Methodology is technically sound and well specified (IoU thresholds, sampling, loss normalization, optimization).
- Alternating training is justified and empirically shown to converge.
- Lacks demonstration of generalization beyond VOC to larger/diverse datasets and object scales.
- Sensitivity to anchor configurations and feature stride is only briefly explored and needs a small study.
- Runtime reporting needs explicit hardware and standardized measurement protocol (batching, I/O, NMS timing) for reproducibility.
- Paper is clearly written with detailed architecture, loss and training descriptions; figures and ablations are informative.
- Suggests adding an algorithm box for the 4-step training and a centralized summary of key hyperparameters and runtime software/hardware.
- Original contribution: novel coupling of proposal generation with detector via shared conv features and anchor design, improving parameter efficiency over MultiBox.
- Significant impact: makes proposals nearly cost-free, removing a major runtime bottleneck and enabling faster, more accurate detection pipelines.
- Essential camera-ready fixes: clarify runtime setup, provide anchor sensitivity analysis, report per-class AP and failure analysis.
- Optional extensions: try joint end-to-end training, smaller stride/dilated convs for small objects, and comparison to GPU-optimized hand-crafted proposal methods.
- Pros: simple and strong idea, elegant anchor design, comprehensive validation, code released.
- Cons: experiments limited to VOC, limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and insufficiently detailed runtime documentation.
- Overall: high-quality, original, and impactful work; recommended for acceptance with minor clarifications.
