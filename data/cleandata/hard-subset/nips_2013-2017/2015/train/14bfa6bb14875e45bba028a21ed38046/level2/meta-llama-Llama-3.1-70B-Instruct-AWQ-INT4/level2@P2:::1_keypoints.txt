- Main contribution: Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with the detection network to generate region proposals efficiently.  
- Method: RPNs are fully-convolutional networks that predict object bounds and objectness scores at each position.  
- Training scheme: Uses an alternating fine-tuning procedure to allow RPN and Fast R-CNN to share convolutional features.  
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012.  
- Efficiency: Greatly reduces computational burden of region proposal generation; RPNs are described as nearly cost-free.  
- Experiments: Thorough experimental evaluation and impressive results with detailed recall-to-IoU analysis and comparisons to prior methods.  
- Clarity and writing: Well-written, well-organized paper with clear explanations, labeled figures/tables, and reproducible experimental details.  
- Strengths: Novel, effective approach that combines high accuracy with reduced computation and clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses: Assumes reader familiarity with Fast R-CNN and RPN concepts; could offer more background for novices.  
- Weaknesses: Lacks a detailed, quantitative breakdown of computational complexity and runtime costs.  
- Originality: Novel combination of RPNs with Fast R-CNN and use of fully-convolutional prediction for proposals.  
- Significance: Results and approach have broad applicability to object detection tasks and represent a significant contribution.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer endorses acceptance based on novelty, strong results, and thorough experiments.
