- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method combining bound-based search and GP optimization.  
- Claims IMGPO achieves exponential convergence rate without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Well-written paper with clear algorithm description and thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Experimental results show IMGPO outperforming SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on benchmarks.  
- Novelty: merges ideas from bound-based methods and GP approaches, related to DIRECT and GP-UCB.  
- Significance: advances the state of the art and offers ideas likely to be used or extended by others.  
- Implementation as a two-stage CNN object detector (RPN + Fast R-CNN) achieving near real-time ~5 FPS.  
- Performance caveat: IMGPO is generally better but slower than SOO.  
- Major concern: alternating optimization uses only a single iteration between RPN and R-CNN and assumes optimal RPN proposals, which may be unrealistic.  
- Concern: applying a pixel-wise sliding window on the CNN top layer may produce inaccurate bounding boxes due to reduced spatial scale.  
- Notation issue: unclear definition of h_a ($h_a$) in lines 173–174; needs clarification.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, subject to addressing the optimization assumptions, bounding-box sampling concerns, and clarifying $h_a$.
