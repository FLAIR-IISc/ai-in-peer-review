- Introduces Faster R-CNN and the Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features to eliminate the region-proposal bottleneck.
- Argues that external proposal methods (Selective Search, EdgeBoxes) limit detection speed and RPN enables nearly cost-free proposals.
- RPNs are trained end-to-end and supply high-quality proposals to Fast R-CNN for detection.
- Achieves strong empirical results: ~5 fps with VGG-16 and state-of-the-art mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007 (73.2%) and 2012 (70.4%).
- Builds on prior work (R-CNN, SPPnet, Fast R-CNN, OverFeat, MultiBox) and distinguishes itself by integrating proposal generation inside the network.
- Significant speed improvement is a central strength, substantially accelerating detection pipelines.
- Maintains strong accuracy despite the speed gains, matching or exceeding prior methods.
- End-to-end (alternating) training of RPN and detector is an important advantage for joint feature learning.
- The anchor mechanism and translation-invariant design effectively handle multiple scales and aspect ratios.
- Experiments and ablations are clear and comprehensive, demonstrating component contributions.
- Alternating optimization is simplistic and insufficiently analyzed; sensitivity to initialization and iterations is unclear.
- Sliding-window proposals on high-level feature maps may produce coarse/inaccurate bounding boxes due to large receptive fields.
- Some notation (e.g., definition of h_a) is unclear and needs clarification for readability.
- Limited comparison to one-stage detectors (e.g., YOLO, SSD) leaves trade-offs between approaches underexplored.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant advance, speed and accuracy improvements, clear writing, public code, reproducibility.
- Arguments against acceptance: underexplored optimization scheme, potential coarse proposals, and insufficient comparison to one-stage methods.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to clarify notation and expand comparisons.
