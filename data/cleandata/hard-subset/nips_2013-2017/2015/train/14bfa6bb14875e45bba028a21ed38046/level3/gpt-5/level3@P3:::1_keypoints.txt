- Proposes Faster R-CNN: integrates a Region Proposal Network (RPN) with Fast R-CNN to share convolutional features for two-stage detection.
- RPN is fully convolutional and predicts k box refinements and objectness scores per spatial location using translation-tied anchors of multiple scales/aspect ratios.
- Introduces a 4-step alternating training scheme to enable feature sharing while keeping Fast R-CNN’s fixed-proposal training requirement.
- Achieves state-of-the-art mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 and ~5 fps with VGG-16 using as few as 300 proposals.
- Replaces CPU-bound, hand-engineered proposal methods with a trainable, GPU-efficient proposal network.
- Strength: Clearly stated goal with strong empirical speed and accuracy improvements (≈10× faster than Fast R-CNN + Selective Search).
- Strength: Novel, parameter-efficient anchor-based, translation-tied proposal mechanism that fits fully convolutional sharing.
- Strength: Solid ablation studies (cls vs reg, NMS, proposal count, shared vs unshared features, one- vs two-stage).
- Strength: Practical and easy to deploy; reduces engineering overhead and code is released.
- Weakness: Exposition is confusing in places (loss normalization/λ, sampling, boundary handling, 4-step training); implementation caveats are scattered and easy to miss.
- Weakness: Translation-invariance claim is unclear and needs precise comparison to prior methods like MultiBox and OverFeat, plus corrected citations.
- Weakness: Many hyperparameters (conv window n, k anchors, IoU thresholds, NMS, sampling ratio, image scale, λ) feel hand-tuned without sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: Training complexity and stability concerns — alternating schedule has many moving parts; no reported variance across runs or exploration of alternatives.
- Weakness: Reproducibility details (normalizations, λ, learning rates, layers frozen, seeds, hardware) are scattered and should be consolidated into a single recipe.
- Quality: Technically sound with thoughtful ablations, but lacks sensitivity analyses and repeated-run stability evidence.
- Clarity: Core idea is simple but manuscript needs a consolidated “Training recipe” and clearer translation-invariance discussion.
- Originality: Novel and elegant anchor-based RPN that unifies proposals with detection via shared features; fresh engineering combination.
- Significance: High — removes a major bottleneck, improves accuracy, and sets a likely new baseline for detectors.
- Questions for authors: clarify translation-invariance relative to MultiBox/OverFeat; sensitivity to n and k; repeated-run variances; attempts at partial/joint training; effects of different NMS or no-NMS.
- Suggestions: provide a consolidated training protocol, add sensitivity analyses for anchors and IoU thresholds, and discuss failure cases (small/elongated objects).
- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical gains, substantial speedup, simple/elegant design, released code, solid ablations.
- Cons for acceptance: confusing exposition/citation issues, heavy reliance on manual hyperparameters, and ad hoc training procedure without stability evidence.
- Recommendation: Accept, with requests to improve clarity and training robustness.
- Reviewer confidence: High.
