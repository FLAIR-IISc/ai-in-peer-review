- Introduces the Region Proposal Network (RPN), a fully convolutional network that generates region proposals within the detector.
- Shares convolutional layers with Fast R-CNN to achieve near real-time performance (~5 fps) while retaining state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC.
- Integrates proposal generation into the deep learning pipeline for end-to-end training, replacing slower external methods like Selective Search.
- Ablation studies effectively validate the contributions of classification/regression layers and feature sharing.
- Paper is generally clear and well-presented.
- Alternating optimization between RPN and Fast R-CNN uses only a single iteration and appears simplistic.
- The authors assume initial RPN proposals are near-optimal but do not study the effect of multiple optimization iterations.
- Sliding-window on the top-level feature map with a relatively large pooling stride may cause bounding box localization inaccuracies; sensitivity to stride is unclear.
- The definition of h_a (lines 173–174) is ambiguous and needs clarification.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and effective method, significant speed improvement, competitive accuracy, clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: simplistic optimization scheme, potential localization issues from stride, unclear parameter definition.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; accept with minor revisions to clarify h_a and possibly expand optimization analysis.
