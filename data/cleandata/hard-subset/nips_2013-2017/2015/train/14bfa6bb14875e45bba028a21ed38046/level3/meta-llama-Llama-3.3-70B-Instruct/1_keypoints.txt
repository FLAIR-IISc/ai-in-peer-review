- Introduces a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares full-image convolutional features with the detection network for nearly cost-free proposals.  
- RPN is a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position and is trained end-to-end.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 with ~5 fps on a GPU.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with thorough explanation of architecture and training.  
- Experimental results are comprehensive and demonstrate effectiveness across comparisons.  
- Detailed comparisons show RPN outperforms Selective Search and EdgeBoxes in accuracy and speed.  
- Lacks a clear explanation of RPN’s translation-invariance relative to methods like Overfeat.  
- Some hyperparameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) are manually set without analysis or justification.  
- Training procedure is complicated and reportedly unstable, raising concerns about result consistency across trials.  
- Technically sound work with claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Novel and original contribution by using a fully-convolutional network for region proposals.  
- Significant impact by addressing the costly region-proposal step and delivering strong accuracy-speed tradeoffs.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but asks authors to address the noted explanations, parameter choices, and training stability.
