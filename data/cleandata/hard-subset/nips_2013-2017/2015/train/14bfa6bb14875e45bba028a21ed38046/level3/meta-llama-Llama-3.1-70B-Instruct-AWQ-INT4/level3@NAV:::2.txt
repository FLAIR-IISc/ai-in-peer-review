This paper proposes a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with a Fast R-CNN object detection network, enabling nearly cost-free region proposals. The RPN is a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position, and is trained end-to-end to generate high-quality region proposals. The authors also introduce a simple alternating optimization algorithm to jointly train the RPN and Fast R-CNN, allowing them to share convolutional features.

The paper is well-written and provides thorough experimental evaluations, demonstrating state-of-the-art detection performance on VOC07 and VOC12. The proposed method greatly reduces region proposal runtime by sharing convolutional layers with the detection network.

Strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed RPN is a novel and efficient approach to region proposal generation, which can be used standalone or in conjunction with Fast R-CNN.
* The method achieves state-of-the-art detection performance on VOC07 and VOC12, with a significant reduction in region proposal runtime.
* The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including ablation studies and comparisons with other region proposal methods.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* The alternating optimization algorithm used to jointly train the RPN and Fast R-CNN is heuristic and lacks a convergence proof.
* The RPN can be used standalone for proposals, but does not clearly outperform Selective Search.

Overall, this is a strong contribution to the field of object detection, improving both efficiency and accuracy. However, the heuristic combination of RPN and Fast R-CNN is a concern, and further work is needed to establish the convergence properties of the alternating optimization algorithm.
