- Proposes a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with an R-CNN for efficient, accurate object detection.  
- RPN is a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position and is trained end-to-end.  
- Introduces a simple alternating optimization scheme to unify and jointly train the RPN with Fast R-CNN.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 benchmarks.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art detection performance while greatly reducing region proposal runtime by sharing convolutional layers.  
- Improves both efficiency and accuracy of object detection in practice.  
- RPN generates high-quality region proposals competitive with other state-of-the-art methods while lowering computational cost.  
- Alternating optimization is a key practical contribution enabling joint training of proposal and detection networks.  
- Alternating optimization is heuristic and lacks a formal convergence proof, raising concerns about training stability.  
- RPN does not clearly outperform other proposal methods (e.g., Selective Search) when used standalone.  
- Overall strong contribution to object detection with thorough experiments and clear writing.  
- Quality: 9/10.  
- Clarity: 9/10.  
- Originality: 8/10.  
- Significance: 9/10.
