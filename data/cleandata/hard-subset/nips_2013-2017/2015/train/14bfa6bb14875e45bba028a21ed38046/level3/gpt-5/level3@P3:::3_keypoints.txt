- Summary: Proposes Faster R-CNN — a two-stage detector with a fully convolutional Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares backbone features with Fast R-CNN and uses translation-invariant anchors to produce fast, accurate proposals.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on SPPnet/Fast R-CNN, replaces hand-crafted CPU proposal methods (Selective Search/EdgeBoxes), differs from one-stage detectors (OverFeat) and improves on MultiBox via anchors; related to FCNs.
- Key innovation: Learning class-agnostic proposals with a lightweight, shared FCN head using multi-scale/aspect-ratio anchors.
- Strength: Eliminates the proposal bottleneck with minimal extra compute (~10 ms) while sharing features between proposal and detection.
- Strength: Elegant, simple RPN design with informative ablations isolating classification, regression, and feature sharing effects.
- Strength: Strong empirical results — state-of-the-art mAP on VOC with ~300 proposals, thorough recall-IoU analysis, and clear speed accounting.
- Strength: Practical, fast-converging training recipe and public code release.
- Weakness: Training uses a 4-step alternating schedule but reports essentially one alternation; needs evidence whether more alternations or joint training improve or destabilize results.
- Weakness: Coarse top-layer stride (16 px with VGG-16) may hurt small-object localization and high-IoU recall; asks for size- and IoU-specific results and ablations with stride-8/dilated convs, denser/shifted anchors, or image pyramids.
- Weakness: Notation ambiguity — request explicit definition that wa and ha are anchor width and height in the bounding-box parameterization.
- Weakness: Runtime comparisons mix CPU (SS/EB) and GPU (detector) timings; requests a GPU-ported baseline or a table separating CPU/GPU components.
- Quality: Method is technically sound with convincing ablations, but limited exploration of optimization schemes weakens the convergence claim.
- Clarity: Overall clear and well organized; minor fixes needed — define ha/wa, remind final feature-map stride, and provide NMS threshold sensitivity analysis.
- Originality: Significant and novel contribution — small FCN proposals with shared features and translation-invariant anchors represent a meaningful advance.
- Significance: High impact — removes hand-crafted proposal step while improving accuracy and speed; likely to be widely adopted.
- Specific suggestions: Add experiments with multiple alternations or joint training; evaluate stride-8/denser anchors and high-IoU (≥0.75) recall and size-based error analysis; report AP75 or IoU-swept recall curves.
- Pros (accept): Conceptually clean, practical importance, strong empirical gains, thorough ablations, public code, high impact.
- Cons (reject concerns): Limited optimization exploration, insufficient analysis of coarse-grid localization/high-IoU performance, and mixed CPU/GPU runtime comparisons.
- Clarification request: Please explicitly confirm that tx=(x−xa)/wa, ty=(y−ya)/ha, tw=log(w/wa), th=log(h/ha) use wa and ha as the anchor box width and height.
- Overall assessment: Excellent paper that substantially advances two-stage detection; minor clarifications and extra experiments on optimization and localization granularity would make it a strong accept.
