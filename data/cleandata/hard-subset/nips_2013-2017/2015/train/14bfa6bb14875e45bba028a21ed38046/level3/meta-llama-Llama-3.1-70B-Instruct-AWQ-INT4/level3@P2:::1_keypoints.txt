- Proposes Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share CNN features with detection for joint classification and bounding-box regression.  
- Uses a fully-convolutional network to predict object bounds and objectness scores at each position and share features with the detection network.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 while being significantly faster than previous methods.  
- Positions the work relative to Selective Search and EdgeBoxes, noting those methods are slower and do not share computation with detectors.  
- Compares to deep-network proposal methods (OverFeat, MultiBox) and highlights their limitations like translation-variance and many anchors.  
- Strength: strong experimental results validating effectiveness of RPNs.  
- Strength: provides a useful recall-to-IoU analysis for diagnosing proposal quality.  
- Strength: introduces a significant, novel idea of sharing CNN features between proposal and detection.  
- Weakness: paper writing is unclear and some explanations are confusing or incomplete.  
- Weakness: does not clearly explain why RPN is translation-invariant while OverFeat is not.  
- Weakness: many hyperparameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) are set manually without justification.  
- Weakness: training procedure appears complicated and potentially unstable; no repeated runs or alternative training ablations reported.  
- Technical quality: method is sound and claims supported by analysis and experiments, but more detail needed on loss and optimization.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to improve clarity, justify hyperparameters, and better document/validate training choices.
