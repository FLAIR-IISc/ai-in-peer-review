This paper proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the Fast R-CNN detection network. The RPN uses two fully connected layers to predict box bounds and objectness at each position, and is trained end-to-end to generate high-quality region proposals. The authors also propose a simple alternating optimization scheme to train the RPN and Fast R-CNN jointly, allowing them to share convolutional features.

The paper is well-written and provides thorough experimental evaluations on the PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 detection benchmarks. The results show that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art detection performance while greatly reducing the region proposal runtime by sharing convolutional layers with the detection network.

One of the main strengths of the paper is its ability to address the computational bottleneck of region proposal generation in object detection systems. By sharing convolutional features with the detection network, the RPN is able to generate high-quality proposals at a significantly lower computational cost. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the recall-to-IoU metric, which is a useful tool for evaluating the quality of region proposals.

However, one of the weaknesses of the paper is the heuristic nature of the alternating optimization scheme used to train the RPN and Fast R-CNN jointly. While the authors provide some justification for this approach, it is not clear whether it is the optimal way to train the two networks jointly. Additionally, the RPN can be used as a standalone proposal method, but it does not clearly outperform Selective Search, which is a widely used proposal method.

Overall, the paper makes a strong contribution to the field of object detection by improving the efficiency and accuracy of region proposal generation. However, further work is needed to fully understand the potential of the proposed method and to address some of the limitations of the current approach.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to region proposal generation that addresses the computational bottleneck of object detection systems.
* The results show that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art detection performance on the PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 detection benchmarks.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the recall-to-IoU metric, which is a useful tool for evaluating the quality of region proposals.
* The proposed method has the potential to improve the efficiency and accuracy of object detection systems.

* The RPN can be used as a standalone proposal method, but it does not clearly outperform Selective Search.
* Further work is needed to fully understand the potential of the proposed method and to address some of the limitations of the current approach.
