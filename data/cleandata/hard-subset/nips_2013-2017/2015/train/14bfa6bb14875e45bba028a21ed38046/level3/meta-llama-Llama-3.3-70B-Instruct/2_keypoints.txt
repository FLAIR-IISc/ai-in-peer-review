- Proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Leverages an unknown bound from continuity at the global optimizer and jointly performs global and local searches over all candidate bounds.  
- Demonstrates empirical superiority to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI across various experiments.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves exponential convergence rate, a significant improvement over prior methods.  
- Provides thorough analysis including effects of UCB tightness and the role of Gaussian processes.  
- Experimental results are strong and include high-dimensional problem evaluations.  
- Weakness: requires tuning multiple hyperparameters (e.g., kernel matrix, η), which may be difficult in practice.  
- Weakness: assumes the objective is a non-convex black-box function, which may not hold in all real-world cases.  
- Weakness: computational cost may limit applicability to very large-scale problems.  
- Overall assessment: significant and original contribution with strong potential for practical use.  
- Recommendation caveat: further work needed to improve scalability and robustness.
