- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that reuses convolutional features to generate nearly cost-free region proposals.  
- RPN is fully convolutional, trained end-to-end, and integrated with Fast R-CNN via a multi-stage joint training procedure.  
- Demonstrates substantial speed-up (~10x faster than R-CNN) with competitive or slightly improved accuracy on PASCAL VOC.  
- Tackles an important bottleneck in object detection — the computational cost of region proposal generation.  
- Conceptually sound idea; sharing convolutional features is a clear strength.  
- Explanation of why the RPN is translation-invariant (vs. referenced work [19]) is incomplete and needs elaboration.  
- Behavior of Overfeat with respect to translation invariance is unclear and insufficiently discussed.  
- Key hyperparameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) are set without adequate justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Training procedure appears complex and potentially unstable; no evidence of repeated runs or alternative training settings.  
- Lacks a detailed ablation study to identify which RPN components drive performance gains.  
- Comparison with Overfeat is superficial and needs deeper analysis.  
- Paper writing, readability, and organization require significant improvement.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution but accept only with minor revisions to clarify methods, justify hyperparameters, and strengthen experimental evaluation.
