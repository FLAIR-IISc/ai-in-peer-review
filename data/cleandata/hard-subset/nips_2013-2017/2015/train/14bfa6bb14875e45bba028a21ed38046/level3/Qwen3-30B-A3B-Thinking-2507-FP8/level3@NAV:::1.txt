

This paper introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) to eliminate the computational bottleneck of region proposal methods in object detection, sharing convolutional features with Fast R-CNN for near-zero marginal cost. The core innovation lies in training a fully-convolutional network to generate proposals *during* detection network training, achieving 5fps on VGG-16 with state-of-the-art VOC 2007 mAP (73.2%). The approach builds on prior work like SPPnet [7] and Fast R-CNN [5] but elegantly unifies proposal and detection through shared features, contrasting with OverFeat [18]’s one-stage detection and MultiBox [20]’s non-translation-invariant anchors.

**Strengths:** The goal is exceptionally clear, and experimental results are robust—RPN+Fast R-CNN outperforms Selective Search/EdgeBoxes while being 10× faster. The feature-sharing mechanism is novel and well-motivated, with compelling speed-accuracy trade-offs (e.g., 70.4% VOC 2012 mAP at 5fps). The 4-step training procedure for joint optimization is pragmatic and effectively demonstrated.

**Weaknesses:** The paper suffers from poor clarity. Critical concepts lack justification: (1) Why RPN is translation-invariant while OverFeat [18] is not is unexplained (Section 3.2), creating confusion about the core novelty. (2) Key hyperparameters (e.g., *n*=3, *k*=9 in Section 3.1) are set manually without empirical justification. (3) The training procedure is unnecessarily complex and unstable (Section 3.3), with no ablation studies exploring simpler alternatives or repeated runs to validate robustness. (4) The "translation-invariant" claim (Section 3.2) contradicts the anchor-based design, requiring clearer elaboration.

**Overall:** This is a high-impact contribution with strong technical execution (Quality), but clarity and justification gaps significantly hinder reproducibility and confidence. While the significance is undeniable (practical real-time detection), the weaknesses prevent it from fully meeting NIPS’ standards for rigor. The work is original (distinguishing from [18,20]) but requires major revisions to clarify key design choices and justify hyperparameters before acceptance. *Pro*: Strong results, elegant feature-sharing. *Con*: Poorly justified choices, confusing explanations.
