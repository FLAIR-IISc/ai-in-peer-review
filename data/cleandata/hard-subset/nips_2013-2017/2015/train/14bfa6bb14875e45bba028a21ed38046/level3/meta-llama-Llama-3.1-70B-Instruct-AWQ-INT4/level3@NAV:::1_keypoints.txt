- Proposes a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with detection to achieve efficient, accurate object detection (Faster R-CNN ~10× faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate).  
- Main idea: a fully-convolutional network predicts object bounds and objectness at each position to generate region proposals used by Fast R-CNN.  
- RPN is trained end-to-end to produce high-quality proposals.  
- Introduces a simple alternating optimization scheme to train RPN and Fast R-CNN to share convolutional features.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and impressive experimental results (state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012).  
- Unclear why the RPN is translation-invariant while the method in [19] is not.  
- No clear justification for hyperparameter choices (e.g., n=3, k=9).  
- Training procedure appears complicated and potentially unstable, with no reported repeated runs or exploration of alternatives.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient SOTA method well-suited for real-time detection and clearly explained.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved clarities (translation invariance, hyperparameters), complicated/unstable training, and lack of justification for training/hyperparameter choices.
