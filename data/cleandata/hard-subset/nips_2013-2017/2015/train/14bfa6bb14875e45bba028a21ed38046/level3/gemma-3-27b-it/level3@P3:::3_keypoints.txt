- Introduces Faster R-CNN using Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) to speed up region proposal generation and achieve near real-time detection (5 fps) with state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC.  
- Shares convolutional layers between RPN and detection network to improve efficiency and reduce computation.  
- Builds on prior region-based CNN work and addresses the computational cost of Selective Search and EdgeBoxes.  
- Distinguishes itself from fully convolutional detection methods via an efficient two-stage approach with shared features.  
- Technically sound with clear experimental improvements in both speed and accuracy.  
- Ablation studies effectively isolate contributions of shared conv layers and RPN classification/regression; loss and parameterizations are well explained.  
- Alternating optimization scheme is pragmatic but potentially suboptimal; deeper theoretical convergence analysis would be beneficial.  
- Inclusion of running time measurements and comparisons strengthens the empirical evaluation.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; figures and RPN integration explanations are clear.  
- The definition of h_a (anchor height) is unclear and needs explicit clarification.  
- Minor implementation details (e.g., specific Caffe configuration) could be explained more fully.  
- Novelty: the RPN + Fast R-CNN integration and shared convs represent a significant, impactful contribution.  
- Translation-invariant anchors simplify the model and reduce parameters compared to MultiBox; two-stage approach shows advantages over OverFeat.  
- Significance: offers broadly applicable principle of sharing conv features, near real-time SOTA performance, and high practical impact (code released).  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant speed improvement, state-of-the-art accuracy, novel and effective approach, thorough evaluation, practical impact and code release.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potentially suboptimal alternating optimization, unclear h_a definition, limited theoretical analysis of optimization.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor issues; strengths outweigh weaknesses and recommendation to accept.
