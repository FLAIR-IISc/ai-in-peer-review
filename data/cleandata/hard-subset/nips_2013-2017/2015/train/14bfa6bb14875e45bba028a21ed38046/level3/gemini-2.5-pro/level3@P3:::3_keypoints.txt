- Introduces the Region Proposal Network (RPN) to generate object proposals from convolutional feature maps and share layers with Fast R-CNN, unifying proposal and detection stages.  
- Employs a pragmatic 4-step alternating optimization scheme to train shared features.  
- Delivers state-of-the-art accuracy and large speed-up (e.g., 73.2% vs 66.9% mAP on VOC2007; proposals ~10ms vs Selective Search ~2s).  
- RPN architecture uses translation-invariant anchors and sibling classification/regression heads; ablation studies justify design choices.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and provides sufficient implementation details for reproduction.  
- Question about convergence: unclear why a single cycle of the alternating optimization is sufficient and whether further iterations help.  
- Concern that the large feature stride (stride 16) yields coarse anchor placement, potentially hurting localization for small objects.  
- Minor clarification needed: explicitly define anchor width/height variables in the bounding-box regression parameterization.  
- Overall conclusion: seminal, technically sound contribution that significantly advances object detection; strong accept.
