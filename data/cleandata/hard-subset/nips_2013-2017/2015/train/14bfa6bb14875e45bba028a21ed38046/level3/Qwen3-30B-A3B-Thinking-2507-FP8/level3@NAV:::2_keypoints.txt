- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to create a unified, efficient detection system.  
- Proposes a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position, trained end-to-end via alternating optimization.  
- Makes region proposal computation nearly cost-free, addressing the Selective Search bottleneck (~2s/image) and enabling ~5 fps on GPU with ~10 ms proposal time.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy: VOC07 73.2% mAP and VOC12 70.4% mAP.  
- Strong technical quality with thorough experimental validation demonstrating superiority over Selective Search and EdgeBoxes in speed and proposal quality.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized, making contributions accessible.  
- Novelty: elegant sharing of convolutional features between proposal and detection networks, distinct from prior methods like MultiBox or OverFeat.  
- Main weakness: heuristic alternating optimization for joint training lacks a convergence proof.  
- Paper does not clearly demonstrate RPN’s standalone superiority over Selective Search on proposal evaluation metrics; ablations show competitiveness but not significant improvement.  
- High significance: enables practical real-time detection systems and advances accuracy while greatly improving efficiency.  
- Code release enhances reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution and recommended for acceptance; training heuristic is a minor concern that does not undermine results.
