Paper introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features between proposal generation and detection to improve efficiency.  
RPN outputs bounding boxes and objectness scores from shared convolutional feature maps.  
RPN is trained end-to-end with Fast R-CNN using a simple alternating optimization scheme that shares features.  
Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art detection on PASCAL VOC 2007 (73.2% mAP) and 2012 (70.4% mAP) at ~5 fps with VGG-16.  
Marginal cost for region proposals is nearly negligible (~10 ms per image), removing the Selective Search bottleneck.  
Thorough experimental validation across datasets and ablation studies supports the claims.  
RPN design (translation-invariant anchors, loss formulation) is well-motivated and technically sound.  
Implementation is well-documented and code is available, aiding reproducibility.  
Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with effective figures and sufficient implementation details.  
Originality is high: sharing convolutional features between proposal and detection networks is a significant conceptual advance.  
Significance is substantial: solves a critical detection bottleneck, enabling faster and more practical detectors.  
Main weakness: alternating optimization is heuristic with no theoretical convergence proof or rigorous justification.  
Minor point: RPN alone does not clearly outperform Selective Search on proposal-quality metrics despite better integrated detection results.  
Overall, strong contribution meeting NIPS criteria (Quality, Clarity, Originality, Significance) with compelling empirical support.
