- Proposes a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the object detection network.  
- Clear goal and strong experimental evidence demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Well-motivated analysis showing existing proposal methods are computationally expensive and do not share conv features.  
- RPN is a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position.  
- Trained end-to-end and uses an alternating optimization scheme to share features with Fast R-CNN.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012.  
- Runs about 10× faster than Fast R-CNN while being slightly more accurate.  
- Paper is hard to follow with several confusing explanations (e.g., translation-invariance claim vs. [19]).  
- Hyperparameter choices (e.g., n=3, k=9) are unjustified.  
- Training procedure appears complicated and potentially unstable; no repeated runs or alternative settings reported.  
- Builds on prior work (Fast R-CNN, OverFeat) but lacks clear comparisons to those methods.  
- Contribution is significant and likely widely useful, but the paper needs clearer exposition and more experimental validation.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify method, justify hyperparameters, and provide more extensive experiments.
