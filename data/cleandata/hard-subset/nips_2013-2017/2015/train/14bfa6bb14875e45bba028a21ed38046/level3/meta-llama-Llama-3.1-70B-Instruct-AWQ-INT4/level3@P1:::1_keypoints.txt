- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features, enabling nearly cost-free region proposals; Faster R-CNN achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 and reduces computation time.  
- Well-motivated goal and thorough related-work review highlighting limits of existing proposal methods and benefits of shared computation.  
- Solid, comprehensive experimental evaluation on PASCAL VOC benchmarks.  
- Paper is hard to follow; some explanations (e.g., why RPN is translation-invariant vs OverFeat) are unclear.  
- Unclear justification for hyperparameter choices (e.g., n=3, k=9).  
- Training procedure appears complicated and unstable, with no reported repeated runs or exploration of alternatives.  
- Proposes multi-stage training to handle localization CNN’s assumption that regions are given.  
- Combined system is about 10× faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate.  
- Originality: novel region proposal method sharing CNN features and a multi-stage training alternative.  
- Significance: achieves SOTA accuracy and reduced runtime, with potential high impact on object detection.  
- Quality: generally well-written and experimentally solid, but hampered by confusing parts and unstable training.  
- Clarity: needs clearer explanations and stronger justification for methodological choices.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, SOTA results, reduced computation, and potential field impact.  
- Arguments for rejection: poor clarity, complicated/unstable training, and insufficient justification of hyperparameters/training settings.
