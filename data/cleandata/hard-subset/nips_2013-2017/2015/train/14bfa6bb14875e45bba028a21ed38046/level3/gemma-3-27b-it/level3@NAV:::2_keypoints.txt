- Introduces Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with detection network to generate nearly cost-free region proposals.  
- RPN predicts object bounds and objectness scores directly from convolutional feature maps.  
- Trains RPN and Fast R-CNN jointly using an alternating optimization strategy.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on VOC07 and VOC12 with strong speed-accuracy trade-offs.  
- Substantially reduces region proposal runtime, moving toward real-time detection.  
- Builds logically on prior region-based CNN work (R-CNN, Fast R-CNN, SPPnet) and related feature-sharing methods.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and reproducible due to released code.  
- Ablation studies are thorough and show contributions of each component.  
- Technically sound with extensive experimental support for claims.  
- Novel integration of a learned region proposal mechanism within a deep detection pipeline.  
- Highly significant work likely to be widely adopted and extended.  
- Main weakness: alternating optimization lacks theoretical convergence justification and is heuristic.  
- RPN does not clearly outperform Selective Search as a standalone proposal method.  
- Arguments for acceptance: SOTA performance, major runtime reduction, clear presentation, public code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: no convergence proof for training scheme and limited standalone RPN advantage.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, impactful contribution — recommend acceptance.
