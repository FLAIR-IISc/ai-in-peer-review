- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with detection, yielding nearly cost-free proposals and real-time detection (5 fps on VGG-16).
- RPN is a fully-convolutional network predicting object bounds and scores at each position using anchors for translation-invariance.
- Strong experimental validation: 73.2% mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 70.4% on VOC 2012.
- Elegant, well-motivated architecture that removes the proposal computation bottleneck by sharing features.
- Thorough ablation studies investigating contributions of classification vs. regression outputs, number of proposals, etc.
- Implementation and training procedures are described in detail.
- Training concern: the 4-step alternating optimization uses only one iteration, raising questions about convergence and whether joint training would be better.
- Localization concern: the stride-16 top feature map produces a coarse sliding-window grid that may hurt bounding-box accuracy for small objects.
- Notation issue: definition of h_a (lines 173–174) is unclear and needs clarification for reproducibility.
- Clarity is generally good with clear figures and anchor-based regression explanation.
- Originality: novel idea of sharing convolutional features for proposal generation, distinct from OverFeat/MultiBox.
- Significance: substantial impact on the field, enabling real-time, high-accuracy detection and influencing later work (e.g., Mask R-CNN, RetinaNet).
- Recommendation: accept — important contribution with addressable concerns for the camera-ready.
- Pro arguments: solves the proposal bottleneck, achieves SOTA accuracy at near real-time speed, simple and widely adopted.
- Con arguments: limited alternating-optimization iterations, potential coarse localization from feature stride, and unclear h_a definition.
