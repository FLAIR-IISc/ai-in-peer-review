

# Review for "Faster R-CNN: Towards Real-Time Object Detection with Region Proposal Networks"

This paper introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs), a novel approach to object detection that eliminates the computational bottleneck of region proposal generation by sharing convolutional features with the detection network. The work builds on prior advances in region-based CNNs (R-CNNs) [6, 5, 7] and addresses the key limitation of previous systems where region proposal computation (e.g., Selective Search [22]) dominated the runtime. The authors propose RPNs as fully convolutional networks that predict object bounds and objectness scores at each position in the feature map, sharing convolutional layers with the detection network (Fast R-CNN [5]). This enables nearly cost-free region proposals, achieving a frame rate of 5fps (VGG-16) while maintaining state-of-the-art accuracy (73.2% mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007, 70.4% on 2012).

The paper makes several key contributions: (1) RPNs as a fully-convolutional network for generating region proposals; (2) a training scheme that shares convolutional features between RPN and Fast R-CNN via alternating optimization; and (3) empirical demonstration that the method achieves both high accuracy and real-time performance. The work is positioned within the context of prior region proposal methods (Selective Search, EdgeBoxes) and related deep learning approaches for detection (OverFeat [18], MultiBox [3, 20]).

**Strengths:**
- **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a well-motivated and thoroughly evaluated approach. The ablation studies (Table 1) convincingly demonstrate the importance of shared features, the classification layer, and the regression layer. The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of proposal quality (Fig. 2) and running time (Table 4).
- **Novelty:** While prior work (e.g., OverFeat [18], MultiBox [3, 20]) has explored using deep networks for detection, the RPN's fully convolutional design with translation-invariant anchors is a significant innovation. The sharing of convolutional features between proposal and detection networks is particularly elegant and practical.
- **Significance:** The paper directly addresses a critical bottleneck in object detection systems, enabling real-time performance without sacrificing accuracy. The method has had a profound impact on the field, as evidenced by its widespread adoption and influence on subsequent work (e.g., Mask R-CNN [13]).
- **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the RPN architecture, loss function, and training procedure. The figures (particularly Fig. 1) effectively illustrate the key concepts.

**Weaknesses:**
- **Training Procedure:** The paper describes a "simple alternating optimization" that involves four steps, but it appears to run only one iteration of the RPN-Fast R-CNN alternation. This implicitly assumes that the initial RPN proposals are optimal, which may not be the case. The authors should clarify whether multiple iterations of alternation were evaluated and why only one iteration was used.
- **Feature Map Resolution:** The sliding window on the top-level feature map (with a stride of 16 pixels) may result in large steps on the input image, potentially leading to coarse or inaccurate bounding box locations. The authors should address whether this limitation was observed in practice and how it was mitigated.
- **Clarity of Formulation:** The definition of h_a in lines 173-174 is unclear and needs clarification. This parameter appears critical to the bounding box regression formulation but is not properly defined.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

*Pro:*
- Solves a critical bottleneck in object detection systems
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy with real-time performance
- Elegant and practical approach that has been widely adopted
- Thorough experimental evaluation with ablation studies
- Well-written and clearly explained

*Con:*
- Training procedure could be better justified (single iteration of alternation)
- Potential limitation from large stride on feature map
- Some unclear definitions in the mathematical formulation

**Overall Assessment:**
This is a high-quality, significant contribution to the field of object detection. The paper presents a technically sound and innovative approach that has had a major impact on the field. While there are minor concerns about the training procedure and a few unclear definitions, these do not undermine the paper's overall contribution. The method has been widely adopted and has influenced subsequent work, including Mask R-CNN [13], which extends Faster R-CNN for instance segmentation.

The paper addresses a critical problem in object detection with a simple yet powerful solution that achieves both high accuracy and real-time performance. The authors have provided a comprehensive evaluation and thorough ablation studies that convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach. The concerns raised are minor and could be addressed with minor revisions to the manuscript.

I recommend acceptance of this paper as it makes a significant contribution to the field of computer vision with practical implications for real-world object detection systems. The paper's influence is already evident in the widespread adoption of Faster R-CNN in both academic research and industry applications.
