- Proposes an RPN that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN, using a small sliding network with classification (objectness) and box-regression heads over translation-invariant anchors.
- Novelty: integrates proposal generation into the detector’s deep feature hierarchy with anchors providing scale/aspect coverage.
- Training: couples RPN and Fast R-CNN via a simple alternating optimization to enable shared conv layers without fully joint training.
- Results: achieves state-of-the-art mAP on PASCAL VOC07/12, cuts proposal time to ~10 ms on GPU, and retains strong accuracy with as few as ~300 proposals.
- Empirical validation: extensive ablations (feature sharing, proposal count, classification vs regression, recall–IoU) support speed and accuracy claims.
- Main weakness: alternating optimization is heuristic and lacks a convergence guarantee; end-to-end joint training is left as future work.
- Comparative weakness: as a standalone proposal method, RPN is competitive but does not unambiguously outperform Selective Search across all settings.
- Clarity: paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides sufficient implementation details (anchors, loss, sampling, NMS) and public code for reproducibility.
- Clarity gap: would benefit from a brief discussion of training stability and failure modes.
- Originality: the shared-feature RPN with translation-invariant anchors and pragmatic training scheme is a novel and impactful combination distinct from prior pipelines.
- Significance: high — advances both accuracy and efficiency and is likely to be widely adopted and extended.
- Pros: elegant integration of proposals with detection, strong SOTA results and speedups, thorough experiments, open code, and simple/general anchor design.
- Cons: heuristic alternating optimization lacks theoretical backing, proposal quality vs Selective Search not decisively superior, evaluation limited to VOC benchmarks.
