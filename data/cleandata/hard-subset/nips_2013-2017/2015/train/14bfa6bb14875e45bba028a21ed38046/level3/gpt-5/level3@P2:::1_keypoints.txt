- Proposal: Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs), a fully-convolutional module that predicts objectness and class-agnostic boxes at each feature-map location, sharing conv layers with Fast R-CNN to make proposals nearly “free” and enable a fast end-to-end two-stage detector (5–17 fps), achieving strong VOC results (VGG-16: 73.2% mAP with 300 proposals).

- Method details: Uses a 3×3 conv mini-network to predict k=9 anchors (3 scales × 3 aspect ratios) per location, trained with a multi-task loss and an alternating 4-step schedule to share conv layers with Fast R-CNN.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on R-CNN/SPPnet/Fast R-CNN by removing CPU proposal methods (Selective Search/EdgeBoxes), contrasts with one-stage detectors (OverFeat) and learned proposal methods (MultiBox), and connects to FCNs and standard smooth-L1 box regression.

- Strength — clear goal: Directly addresses and removes the region-proposal bottleneck by learning proposals that share features with the detector.

- Strength — empirical results: Strong, carefully ablated results showing competitive or superior mAP to SS/EB with much higher speed and robust performance with few proposals.

- Strength — simplicity and applicability: Anchor formulation, dense FCN prediction, and feature sharing are conceptually simple, efficient, and broadly useful.

- Strength — reproducibility: Public code and use of standard backbones/datasets increase reproducibility and impact.

- Weakness — clarity/organization: Paper is hard to follow in parts (especially the 4-step training and comparisons to prior learned proposal methods); needs clearer presentation and a schematic timeline of training/parameter sharing.

- Weakness — translation-invariance claim: Claim that RPNs are translation-invariant vs prior work is unclear/misattributed (MultiBox vs OverFeat) and needs clarification, correct citations, and ideally an experiment or analysis.

- Weakness — hyperparameter justification: Many design choices (receptive field n, k=9 anchors, IoU thresholds, NMS IoU, batch composition, λ, LR schedule, strides) appear hand-picked without sensitivity analyses.

- Weakness — training complexity & stability: Alternating 4-step training suggests joint-training instability but the paper provides no empirical evidence (failed joint runs, convergence plots, variance across runs) to justify this complexity.

- Weakness — limited evaluation scope: Experiments focus on VOC; would benefit from more challenging settings, per-size object analyses, stride ablations, cross-dataset tests, and stronger linkage between recall-to-IoU and final mAP.

- Quality assessment: Method is technically sound and well-motivated with extensive VOC experiments, but lacks justification for key choices and evidence that simpler joint training would fail.

- Clarity suggestions: Provide an algorithm box for the 4-step training, consolidate hyperparameters and defaults, clarify translation-invariance, and give a detailed time breakdown for the “nearly cost-free” claim.

- Originality: Novel, substantive combination of translation-invariant anchors predicted densely by an FCN and tightly integrated with detection via feature sharing and alternating training.

- Significance: High — removes CPU proposal bottleneck while improving accuracy, likely to be widely adopted and foundational for future detectors.

- Pros for acceptance: Strong empirical gains, elegant anchor+FCN mechanism, careful ablations, and open-source release.

- Cons against acceptance: Insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, seemingly ad hoc training scheme without stability evidence, and some confusing comparisons/clarity issues.

- Question 1 for authors: Please report variance across 3–5 runs (different seeds) for main VGG results and ablations.

- Question 2 for authors: Did you try end-to-end joint training of RPN + Fast R-CNN (online proposals)? If so, what failed; if not, why prefer the alternating scheme?

- Question 3 for authors: How sensitive are results to anchor designs (scales/aspect ratios/k), mini-network size n, and IoU thresholds for positives/negatives and NMS?

- Question 4 for authors: Please clarify the translation-invariance argument vs MultiBox and OverFeat, and provide parameter counts/overfitting analysis supporting the claim.

- Minor comments: Consolidate hyperparameters in a table, add a figure illustrating anchor tiling/receptive fields/the 3×3 mini-network, and fix mismatched citation indices.

- Overall recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing clarity, hyperparameter/sensitivity analyses, and training-stability concerns.
