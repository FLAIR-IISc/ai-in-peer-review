- Proposes Faster R-CNN: a two-stage detector with a fully convolutional Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features via anchor-based proposals, enabling efficient proposal generation (~10 ms).
- Shows strong empirical results on PASCAL VOC with state-of-the-art mAP and roughly an order-of-magnitude speedup vs Fast R-CNN + Selective Search.
- Training protocol is complex and potentially unstable (4-step alternating training, boundary-anchor handling, fixed NMS/IoU thresholds).
- Many hyperparameters are manually set (e.g., n=3, k=9, IoU=0.7/0.3, λ, NMS=0.7) with no justification or sensitivity analysis.
- No repeated runs or variance reporting, leaving robustness and reproducibility unclear.
- Manuscript clarity issues: alternation scheme, normalization (Ncls/Nreg), and loss balancing need clearer presentation, ideally an algorithm box.
- The “translation-invariant anchors” claim requires a careful explanation and reconciliation with VGG striding/padding; contrast more directly with MultiBox learned priors.
- Comparison to OverFeat is incomplete; controlled experiments isolating pyramids and proposal counts are needed.
- Originality: meaningful advance by making proposals neural, feature-sharing, and anchor-based; well situated in prior work.
- Significance: high — demonstrable improvement in speed-accuracy trade-offs and likely broad adoption.
- Pros: strong empirical gains, conceptually simple and practical approach, clear advance over external proposal pipelines, code availability.
- Cons: clarity problems, many unmotivated hyperparameters, complex/possibly unstable training, limited exploration of alternative training/anchor settings.
- Suggestions: add sensitivity studies for k, n, IoU/NMS; report repeated runs/variance; clarify invariance claims and OverFeat comparison; provide a concise training algorithm summary.
