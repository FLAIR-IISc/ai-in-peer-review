- Introduces the Region Proposal Network (RPN) to integrate region proposal generation into CNNs, addressing a major computational bottleneck.
- RPN is fully-convolutional, sliding over shared feature maps to predict objectness scores and bounding-box regressions using anchor boxes, enabling nearly cost-free proposals at test time.
- Proposes a multi-step alternating optimization scheme to jointly train the RPN and Fast R-CNN detector.
- Highly original and significant: internal, learnable proposals that share computation yield major speed and accuracy improvements and move toward real-time detection.
- Strong empirical performance on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 with extensive ablation studies demonstrating the benefits of feature sharing.
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear and well-organized, facilitating understanding and reproducibility.
- Primary weakness: the four-step alternating training is heuristic with no theoretical analysis or convergence proof, raising questions about stability and optimality.
- RPN as a standalone proposal generator (without shared features) does not decisively outperform methods like Selective Search; its main advantage stems from feature sharing.
- Overall assessment: landmark contribution with compelling empirical results; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
