- Proposes a novel two-stage detection system combining a Region Proposal Network (RPN) with Fast R-CNN that shares convolutional features.  
- RPN enables nearly cost-free region proposals by sharing conv features with detection network.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 while running at 5–17 FPS.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear overview of prior detection systems and their limitations.  
- RPN architecture is thoroughly described, including translation-invariant anchors, loss function, and optimization.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, with useful ablation studies separating RPN and Fast R-CNN contributions.  
- Comparison with OverFeat provides insight into effectiveness of the two-stage shared-feature approach.  
- Concern: alternating optimization between RPN and Fast R-CNN appears to run only one iteration, assuming initial RPN proposals are optimal.  
- Concern: sliding window on top-level feature map may map to large input-image steps due to pooling, risking coarse/inaccurate bounding boxes.  
- Concern: definition of h_a (lines 173–174) is unclear and requires clarification.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound and claims supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Clarity assessment: well-organized, clear descriptions, and helpful figures/tables.  
- Originality and significance: high—novel integration with potential real-time impact and influence on future detection research.  
- Recommendation: accept, provided the authors address the noted concerns to improve clarity and justification.
