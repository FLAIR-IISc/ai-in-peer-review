This paper proposes Faster R-CNN, a two-stage detector that introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) sharing convolutional features with the detection head. The RPN is a fully convolutional module that predicts objectness and box regression offsets relative to translation-invariant “anchor” boxes at each spatial location, enabling efficient proposal generation (≈10 ms) on top of shared features. The authors train the RPN and detector via an alternating multi-stage scheme that yields a unified network. Relative to prior work at NIPS and elsewhere—OverFeat (one-stage sliding-window detection), MultiBox (proposal nets with k-means anchors), SPPnet and Fast R-CNN (shared conv features with external proposals)—the key novelty is to make the proposal mechanism neural, feature-sharing, and anchor-based, thereby removing the proposal bottleneck. Results on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 show state-of-the-art mAP with a substantial speedup; end-to-end, the system is roughly an order of magnitude faster than Fast R-CNN + Selective Search while being slightly more accurate.

Quality: The goal is clear and the experimental evidence is strong, with ablations on sharing, proposal counts, and cls/reg components. However, the training protocol appears complex and potentially unstable (4-step alternating training, boundary-anchor handling, fixed NMS/IoU thresholds). Many hyperparameters (e.g., n=3, k=9, IoU=0.7/0.3, λ, NMS=0.7) are set manually without justification or sensitivity analysis. No repeated runs or variance are reported, leaving robustness unclear.

Clarity: While the high-level idea is elegant, the manuscript is not easy to follow. The alternation scheme, normalization (Ncls/Nreg), and loss balancing would benefit from a concise algorithm box and clearer motivation. The “translation-invariant anchors” claim needs a careful explanation: please reconcile anchor-level invariance with striding/padding in VGG and contrast more precisely with MultiBox’s learned priors. The comparison to OverFeat would be clearer with controlled experiments isolating pyramids and proposal counts.

Originality: The integration of RPNs with shared features and anchor design is a meaningful advance over Fast R-CNN and MultiBox. The paper situates itself well in the literature, but could more explicitly discuss design alternatives (e.g., different n, k, strides).

Significance: High. The method demonstrably advances speed-accuracy trade-offs and is likely to be widely adopted.

Pros for acceptance:
- Strong empirical gains with compelling efficiency
- Conceptually simple, practical, and general approach
- Clear advance over external proposal pipelines
- Code availability

Cons against acceptance:
- Clarity issues and confusing parts (invariance, training)
- Many unmotivated hyperparameters; no sensitivity/variance
- Complex training; stability not convincingly demonstrated
- Limited exploration of alternative training settings (e.g., joint end-to-end, different anchors)

Suggestions: add sensitivity studies for k, n, IoU/NMS; report repeated runs; clarify invariance claims and OverFeat comparison; provide a concise training algorithm summary.
