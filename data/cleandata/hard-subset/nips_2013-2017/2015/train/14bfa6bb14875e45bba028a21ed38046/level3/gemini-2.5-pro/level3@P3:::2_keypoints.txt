- Introduces Region Proposal Network (RPN) that generates object proposals directly from convolutional feature maps, unifying proposal and detection.  
- Shares convolutional features between RPN and Fast R-CNN detector, making proposal generation marginal in cost.  
- Uses a fully-convolutional sliding-window RPN that predicts objectness and refines predefined anchor boxes of multiple scales/aspect ratios.  
- Anchor box mechanism handles scale/aspect variation from single-scale feature maps and became a standard in detectors.  
- Demonstrates large improvements in speed (e.g., ~5 fps with VGG-16) and state-of-the-art mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012.  
- Provides thorough experimental validation and strong ablation studies supporting feature sharing and component design choices.  
- Proposes a pragmatic 4-step alternating optimization training scheme to train shared features and detector.  
- Training scheme is heuristic, multi-stage, lacks theoretical convergence guarantees, and adds implementation complexity.  
- Paper does not convincingly show RPN proposals are superior standalone to methods like Selective Search in raw recall.  
- Highly original and influential work that changed object detection paradigms; technically sound and clearly presented.  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept — strengths far outweigh minor concerns about training heuristics.
