This paper proposes a novel region proposal method called Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that shares convolutional neural network (CNN) features with object localization, which includes both classification and regression. The main idea is to use a fully-convolutional network to predict object bounds and objectness scores at each position, and then share the convolutional features with the detection network. The authors demonstrate that their method, Faster R-CNN, achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012, while being significantly faster than previous methods.

The paper relates to previous work on region proposal methods, such as Selective Search (SS) and EdgeBoxes (EB), which rely on inexpensive features and economical inference schemes. However, these methods are slow and do not share computation with the detection network. The authors also discuss recent papers that use deep networks for locating class-specific or class-agnostic bounding boxes, such as OverFeat and MultiBox. However, these methods have limitations, such as being translation-variant or requiring a large number of anchors.

The strengths of the paper include its solid experimental results, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the recall-to-IoU metric, which is useful for diagnosing the proposal method. Additionally, the paper proposes a novel region proposal method that shares CNN features with object localization, which is a significant contribution to the field.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. The writing is not clear, and some parts of the paper are confusing. For example, the authors do not clearly explain why RPN is translation-invariant while OverFeat is not. Additionally, many hyperparameters are set manually without justification, such as n=3 and k=9. The training procedure also seems complicated and unstable, and the authors do not report repeated runs or exploration of alternative training settings to justify the chosen procedure.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of some of the technical aspects, such as the loss function and the optimization procedure.

In terms of clarity, the paper is not well-written, and some parts are difficult to follow. The authors could improve the writing by providing more detailed explanations of the technical aspects, and by using clearer and more concise language.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel region proposal method that shares CNN features with object localization, which is a significant contribution to the field. However, the paper builds on previous work, and the authors could provide more context and discussion of how their method relates to previous work.

In terms of significance, the paper demonstrates that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012, while being significantly faster than previous methods. This is a significant contribution to the field, and the paper could have a major impact on the development of object detection systems.
