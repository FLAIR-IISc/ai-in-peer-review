- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detection network to reduce region proposal computation cost.  
- RPN is a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores from shared conv features.  
- Shared computation dramatically lowers the cost of generating region proposals compared to independent methods.  
- Proposes a practical alternating optimization scheme to jointly train the RPN and Fast R-CNN detector, though it is heuristic.  
- Builds on R-CNN/SPPnet/Fast R-CNN and targets region proposal as the remaining computational bottleneck.  
- Prior methods (Selective Search, EdgeBoxes) are slower and do not exploit shared conv features; OverFeat/MultiBox differ in architecture and training.  
- Technical approach is sound, well-motivated, and the RPN architecture is elegant and well-designed.  
- Extensive experiments show state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012.  
- Ablation studies provide useful insights into contributions of shared features and classification/regression outputs.  
- Paper is clearly written and the released code improves reproducibility.  
- Alternating optimization lacks theoretical convergence guarantees and needs deeper analysis.  
- Evaluation of RPN alone does not conclusively outperform Selective Search; improvements mainly arise from the combined system.  
- More detailed comparisons with optimized Selective Search implementations would strengthen the evaluation.  
- The effect of shared layers on generalization is not thoroughly investigated.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with substantial efficiency gains; accept with a minor request to discuss limitations of the alternating optimization.
