The paper "Faster R-CNN: Towards Real-Time Object Detection with Region Proposal Networks" proposes a novel two-stage object detection system that integrates a Region Proposal Network (RPN) with a Fast R-CNN object detection network. The RPN is designed to share convolutional features with the Fast R-CNN network, enabling nearly cost-free region proposals. The authors demonstrate that their system can achieve state-of-the-art object detection accuracy on the PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 datasets while running at 5-17 frames per second.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear overview of the existing object detection systems and their limitations. The proposed RPN is an innovative solution that addresses the computational bottleneck of region proposal generation. The authors provide a thorough description of the RPN architecture, including the translation-invariant anchors, loss function, and optimization procedure.

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the results. The ablation studies help to disentangle the contributions of the RPN and Fast R-CNN components, and the comparison with the OverFeat system provides insight into the effectiveness of the proposed two-stage approach.

However, there are a few concerns and questions that need to be addressed. Firstly, the alternating optimization procedure between the RPN and Fast R-CNN appears to run only one iteration, implicitly assuming that the initial RPN proposals are optimal. It is unclear whether this is a reasonable assumption, and further analysis is needed to justify this choice.

Secondly, the sliding window on the top-level feature map may map to large steps on the input image because of pooling, risking coarse or inaccurate bounding-box locations. The authors should provide more insight into how they handle this issue and whether it affects the overall performance of the system.

Lastly, the definition of h_a in lines 173-174 is unclear and needs clarification.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work, and the paper provides a complete piece of work.

The clarity of the paper is also good, with clear and concise descriptions of the proposed method and experimental evaluation. The paper is well-organized, and the figures and tables are helpful in illustrating the key points.

The originality of the paper is high, as it proposes a novel two-stage object detection system that integrates a RPN with a Fast R-CNN network. The paper also provides new insights into the effectiveness of shared convolutional features and the importance of accurate region proposals for object detection.

The significance of the paper is also high, as it addresses a critical problem in object detection and provides a solution that can be used in real-time applications. The paper has the potential to impact the field of computer vision and inspire further research in object detection.
