- Introduces Faster R-CNN with a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detector to generate proposals efficiently.
- RPN slides a small network over feature maps, regressing bounding boxes and scoring objectness relative to multi-scale, multi-aspect anchors.
- Proposes a 4-step alternating training scheme to learn shared features between RPN and Fast R-CNN.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC (mAP 73.2% on 2007, 70.4% on 2012) using VGG-16 while running ~5 fps.
- Claims a ~10x speed-up over Fast R-CNN with external proposals, making proposal generation nearly cost-free at test time.
- Strength: Highly original and impactful idea that unifies proposal and detection stages, shifting toward integrated end-to-end systems.
- Strength: Strong technical quality with thorough ablation studies demonstrating benefits of feature sharing and the two-stage approach.
- Strength: Well-motivated goal to eliminate the computational bottleneck of region proposal generation.
- Weakness: Paper organization and explanations are sometimes unclear, especially the brief description of the 4-step training.
- Weakness: Lacks justification or sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (anchor config, IoU thresholds, NMS, etc.).
- Weakness: The alternating training procedure appears complex and potentially unstable, with no analysis of robustness or variance across runs.
- Weakness: Explanation of "translation-invariant anchors" is confusing and could better distinguish RPN from methods like MultiBox.
- Overall: Despite exposition and ablation shortcomings, the RPN is a landmark, widely influential contribution that justifies acceptance.
