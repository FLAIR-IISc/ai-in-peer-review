This paper proposes a novel region proposal method called Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the object localization (classification + regression) network. The goal of the paper is clear, and the experimental results are solid, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the limitations of existing region proposal methods. They argue that these methods are computationally expensive and do not take advantage of the shared convolutional features with the object detection network. The proposed RPN method addresses these limitations by sharing convolutional features with the object detection network, making the region proposal step nearly cost-free.

The RPN method is based on a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position. The network is trained end-to-end to generate high-quality region proposals, which are then used by the Fast R-CNN network for object detection. The authors propose a simple alternating optimization scheme to train the RPN and Fast R-CNN networks to share convolutional features.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The authors evaluate their method on the PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 detection benchmarks and achieve state-of-the-art object detection accuracy. The results show that the proposed method is about 10 times faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate.

However, the paper is not easy to follow, and there are several confusing parts. For example, the authors do not clearly explain why the RPN is translation-invariant while the method in [19] is not. Additionally, the authors do not provide a clear justification for the choice of hyperparameters, such as n=3 and k=9.

Furthermore, the training procedure seems complicated and unstable. The authors do not report repeated runs or exploration of alternative training settings to justify the chosen procedure. This makes it difficult to assess the robustness of the proposed method.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel region proposal method that shares convolutional features with the object localization network. This is a significant contribution to the field of object detection. However, the paper builds on previous work, such as Fast R-CNN and OverFeat, and the authors do not provide a clear comparison with these methods.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses a difficult problem in object detection, namely, the region proposal step. The proposed method has the potential to be widely used in object detection applications, and the results demonstrate its effectiveness.

Overall, the paper is well-motivated, and the proposed method is a significant contribution to the field of object detection. However, the paper could be improved by providing a clearer explanation of the method, justifying the choice of hyperparameters, and reporting more extensive experimental results.
