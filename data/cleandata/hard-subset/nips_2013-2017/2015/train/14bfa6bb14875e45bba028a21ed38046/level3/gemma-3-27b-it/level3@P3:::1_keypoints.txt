- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to accelerate object detection.
- Formulates region proposal generation as a learned deep network, enabling near cost-free proposals at test time.
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC with significant speedup (≈5 fps with VGG-16).
- Demonstrates that learning proposals is more effective than reimplementing traditional methods on GPUs.
- Core idea is elegant and the experimental results are compelling and impactful.
- Paper lacks clarity on translation invariance and why VGG/OverFeat differ in this regard.
- Key hyperparameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) are chosen ad hoc without sufficient justification or exploration.
- Training procedure (alternating optimization) is complex, opaquely described, and may be unstable.
- No reported repeated runs, ablation studies, or sensitivity analyses for training schedules, learning rates, or momentum.
- Potential overfitting concerns not adequately addressed given small PASCAL VOC datasets despite ImageNet pretraining.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, significant speed/accuracy improvement, solid benchmark results, clear goal.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues, weak hyperparameter justification, opaque training procedure, limited robustness analysis.
- Overall: strong and significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify translation invariance, justify hyperparameters, and add ablations/robustness analyses.
