- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to eliminate proposal bottleneck.  
- Trains a fully-convolutional network to generate proposals during detection training, enabling near-zero marginal cost.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results (73.2% VOC 2007 mAP) and practical speed (5 fps on VGG-16).  
- Elegantly unifies proposal and detection, outperforming Selective Search/EdgeBoxes while being ~10× faster.  
- Feature-sharing mechanism is novel, well-motivated, and demonstrates attractive speed–accuracy trade-offs (e.g., 70.4% VOC 2012 mAP at 5 fps).  
- Presents a pragmatic 4-step joint training procedure that is effective in experiments.  
- Paper clarity is poor, with critical concepts and justifications lacking, hampering reproducibility.  
- The "translation-invariant" claim for RPN vs. OverFeat is unexplained and appears contradictory given anchor design.  
- Key hyperparameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) are manually chosen without empirical justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Training procedure is complex and potentially unstable; no ablation studies or repeated runs assess robustness.  
- Overall: high-impact and original work with strong results, but requires major revisions to clarify design choices and justify hyperparameters before acceptance.
