This paper introduces a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with object detection networks, enabling nearly cost-free region proposals. The authors demonstrate that their method, Faster R-CNN, achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 while reducing the computational time.

The paper is well-motivated, and the goal is clear. The authors provide a thorough review of related work, highlighting the limitations of existing region proposal methods and the benefits of shared computation. The experimental results are solid, with a comprehensive evaluation on the PASCAL VOC detection benchmarks.

However, the paper is not easy to follow, and some parts are confusing. For example, the explanation of why RPN is translation-invariant while OverFeat [18] is not is unclear. The authors also fail to provide a clear justification for the choice of hyperparameters, such as n=3 and k=9. The training procedure seems complicated and unstable, and the authors do not report repeated runs or exploration of alternative training settings to justify the chosen procedure.

Despite these limitations, the paper proposes a novel region proposal method that shares CNN features with object localization (classification + regression). The combined RCNN is about 10× faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate. The authors also propose a multi-stage training alternative because localization CNN assumes regions are given.

Strengths:

* Novel region proposal method that shares CNN features with object localization
* State-of-the-art object detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012
* Reduced computational time compared to Fast R-CNN
* Comprehensive evaluation on the PASCAL VOC detection benchmarks

Weaknesses:

* Paper is not easy to follow, and some parts are confusing
* Unclear justification for choice of hyperparameters
* Training procedure seems complicated and unstable
* Lack of repeated runs or exploration of alternative training settings

Originality:

* The paper proposes a novel region proposal method that shares CNN features with object localization
* The authors introduce a multi-stage training alternative that addresses the limitation of localization CNN assuming regions are given
