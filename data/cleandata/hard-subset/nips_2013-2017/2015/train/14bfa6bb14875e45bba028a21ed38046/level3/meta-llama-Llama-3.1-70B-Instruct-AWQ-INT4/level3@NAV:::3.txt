This paper proposes a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional layers with a Fast R-CNN object detection network, achieving near real-time object detection with high accuracy. The authors demonstrate that by sharing convolutions at test-time, the marginal cost for computing proposals is small, making the region proposal step nearly cost-free.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the proposed method. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain how their approach differs from previous methods.

The proposed RPN is a fully-convolutional network that simultaneously predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position. The authors introduce a novel loss function for learning region proposals, which is a combination of classification and regression losses. The RPN is trained end-to-end using stochastic gradient descent, and the authors provide implementation details, including the use of non-maximum suppression to reduce redundancy in proposal regions.

The authors evaluate their method on the PASCAL VOC 2007 detection benchmark and demonstrate that it achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy with a frame rate of 5fps on a GPU. They also provide ablation studies to investigate the behavior of RPNs as a proposal method and demonstrate that the proposal quality improves when using a more powerful network.

Strengths of the paper:

* The proposed RPN is a novel and efficient approach to region proposal generation, which achieves high accuracy and near real-time performance.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method, including ablation studies and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
* The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the proposed method.

Weaknesses of the paper:

* The authors assume that the initial RPN proposals are optimal and only run one iteration of alternating optimization between RPN and Fast R-CNN. This may not be sufficient to ensure convergence, and the authors should consider running multiple iterations or using a more robust optimization method.
* The sliding window approach used in the RPN may map to large steps on the input image due to pooling, which could result in coarse or inaccurate bounding-box locations.
* The definition of h_a in lines 173-174 is unclear and needs clarification.
