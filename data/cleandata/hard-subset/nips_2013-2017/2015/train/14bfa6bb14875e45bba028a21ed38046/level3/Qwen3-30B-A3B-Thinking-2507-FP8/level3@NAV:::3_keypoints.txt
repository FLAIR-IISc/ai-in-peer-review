- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional layers between proposal generation and detection.  
- Proposes a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and scores at each feature-map position for nearly cost-free proposals when shared with Fast R-CNN.  
- Achieves 5 fps on VGG-16 and sets state-of-the-art PASCAL VOC results (73.2% mAP 2007, 70.4% 2012).  
- Builds on prior shared-feature work (SPPnet, Fast R-CNN) but makes a significant contribution by integrating proposal generation into the detection network.  
- Technically sound solution addressing a major computational bottleneck in object detection.  
- Compelling experimental validation showing speed and accuracy gains over Selective Search and EdgeBoxes.  
- Alternating optimization scheme is simple and effectively unifies the two networks.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Alternating optimization appears to run only one iteration and needs explicit justification or further treatment.  
- Sliding-window on top-level feature maps may map to large input strides (e.g., 16-pixel on VGG), potentially producing coarse bounding boxes that hurt small-object accuracy.  
- Notation issue: definition of h_a (lines 173–174) is unclear and requires clarification.  
- Overall: high-quality, significant, original work with minor addressable concerns; strong candidate for acceptance.
