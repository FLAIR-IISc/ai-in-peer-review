- Proposes the Region Proposal Network (RPN) to generate object proposals within a CNN, sharing features with the detector.
- Integrates RPN with Fast R-CNN to form Faster R-CNN, enabling near-cost-free proposals and end-to-end training.
- Delivers substantial speed and accuracy gains (about 10× faster than Fast R-CNN; state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC), making high-accuracy detection practical.
- Introduces a novel, learnable, attention-like proposal mechanism that shares computation with the detector and is highly influential.
- Provides solid experimental validation with comprehensive ablation studies and strong baseline comparisons.
- Clarity issues: the 4-step alternating training procedure is convoluted and needs clearer explanation/diagrams; the translation-invariance claim is unclear and mis-cited.
- Reproducibility and justification concerns: anchor hyperparameters chosen without empirical justification, training seems unstable, and results from repeated runs or alternative training schemes are not reported.
- Overall: a very strong, original, and landmark paper despite some clarity and reproducibility weaknesses.
