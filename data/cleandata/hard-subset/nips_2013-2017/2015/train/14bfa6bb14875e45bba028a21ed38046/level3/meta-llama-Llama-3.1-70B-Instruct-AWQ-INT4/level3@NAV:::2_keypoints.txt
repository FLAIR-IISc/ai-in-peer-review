- Proposes a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to produce nearly cost-free region proposals.
- RPN is a fully-convolutional network predicting object bounds and objectness scores at each position, trained end-to-end for high-quality proposals.
- Introduces a simple alternating optimization algorithm to jointly train RPN and Fast R-CNN, allowing shared convolutional features.
- Paper is well-written with thorough experimental evaluations showing state-of-the-art detection on VOC07 and VOC12.
- Method significantly reduces region proposal runtime by sharing convolutional layers with the detection network.
- Strength: RPN is a novel, efficient proposal approach usable standalone or with Fast R-CNN.
- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art detection performance while greatly reducing proposal runtime.
- Strength: Provides thorough evaluations, including ablation studies and comparisons to other proposal methods.
- Weakness: Alternating optimization algorithm is heuristic and lacks a convergence proof.
- Weakness: RPN as a standalone proposal method does not clearly outperform Selective Search.
- Pro-accept: Novel, efficient proposal method with strong empirical results and comprehensive evaluation.
- Con-accept: Heuristic joint-training lacks theoretical guarantees and standalone RPN doesn't surpass Selective Search.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address concerns about the heuristic combination and convergence properties.
