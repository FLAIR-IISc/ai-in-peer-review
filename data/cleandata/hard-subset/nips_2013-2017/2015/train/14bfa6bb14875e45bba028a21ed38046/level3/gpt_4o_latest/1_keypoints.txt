- Introduces an RPN that unifies region proposal and detection by sharing convolutional features, producing nearly cost-free proposals.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC and is ~10× faster than Fast R-CNN (5 fps, 73.2% mAP on VOC2007).
- Proposes a multi-stage training procedure to relax the assumption that localization CNNs need predefined regions.
- Builds on SPPnet/Fast R-CNN and specifically addresses the region-proposal computation bottleneck.
- Strength — Novelty/Impact: feature-sharing RPN eliminates Selective Search bottleneck, improving efficiency and accuracy.
- Strength — Performance: faster and slightly more accurate than Fast R-CNN with strong empirical results.
- Strength — Unified framework: elegant integration of RPN and Fast R-CNN demonstrating practical benefits of shared conv features.
- Strength — Reproducibility: authors provide code.
- Weakness — Clarity/Presentation: parts are hard to follow; explanation of translation-invariance is unclear and OverFeat is not discussed.
- Weakness — Parameter choices: manual settings (e.g., n=3, k=9) lack justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness — Complex training: multi-stage procedure appears complex and unstable with insufficient exploration of alternatives.
- Weakness — Related work: inadequate discussion/comparison to OverFeat, MultiBox, and other relevant methods.
- Pro: Addresses a critical detection bottleneck with a novel, impactful method and strong results.
- Con: Clarity issues, missing parameter analysis, and incomplete related-work discussion reduce overall rigor.
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on addressing clarity, justifying parameter choices, and expanding related-work discussion.
