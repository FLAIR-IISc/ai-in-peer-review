- Proposes an RPN that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN, predicting box bounds and objectness via two fully connected layers and trained end-to-end.
- Introduces a simple alternating optimization scheme to jointly train RPN and Fast R-CNN so they can share convolutional features.
- Shows state-of-the-art detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 while greatly reducing region proposal runtime.
- Provides thorough experimental evaluation and a detailed analysis using the recall-to-IoU metric to assess proposal quality.
- Main strength: addresses the computational bottleneck by generating high-quality proposals with much lower cost through shared conv layers.
- Weakness: the alternating optimization is heuristic, lacks a convergence proof, and its optimality is unclear.
- Weakness: as a standalone proposal method, RPN does not clearly outperform Selective Search.
- Overall contribution: significant improvement in efficiency and accuracy of region proposal generation for object detection.
- Quality score: 8/10 — technically sound and well-supported, but limited by the heuristic training scheme.
- Clarity score: 9/10 — clearly written and thorough, though some technical details may be hard for newcomers.
- Originality score: 8/10 — novel approach building on prior work, with useful recall-to-IoU analysis.
- Significance score: 9/10 — strong potential to improve practical object detection systems.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient proposal method; SOTA results; thorough analysis; practical impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: heuristic joint-training lacking proof, RPN not clearly superior to Selective Search, further validation needed.
