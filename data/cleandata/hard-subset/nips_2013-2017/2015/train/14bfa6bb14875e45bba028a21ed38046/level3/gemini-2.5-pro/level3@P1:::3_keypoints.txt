- Proposes Region Proposal Network (RPN): a fully-convolutional module that predicts object proposals from conv feature maps and shares layers with Fast R-CNN to make proposals nearly cost-free at test time.
- RPN slides a small network over the last conv feature map and predicts objectness scores and bbox regressions for k pre-defined anchors of different scales/aspect ratios.
- Contrasts favorably with Selective Search (slow, CPU) and prior learning methods like MultiBox (less translation-invariant, more parameters).
- Introduces a 4-step alternating optimization training scheme to learn shared features between RPN and Fast R-CNN.
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 while running near real-time (~5 fps with VGG-16), providing large speedups over Fast R-CNN with SS.
- Paper contains thorough experiments and ablation studies validating design choices (feature sharing, cls/reg heads, two-stage vs one-stage) and analyses like recall vs IoU.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains RPN architecture and integration with Fast R-CNN, aided by informative figures.
- Concern: the 4-step “alternating” training seems sequential (single cycle) rather than fully joint; convergence properties and whether further iterations help are not justified.
- Concern: RPN operates on coarse feature maps (stride ~16 pixels), which may limit localization accuracy for small objects despite bbox regression correcting errors.
- Minor clarification needed: bounding-box regression notation uses h_a without explicit definition (presumably anchor height).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, impactful architecture (RPN) that removes proposal bottleneck, strong accuracy+speed tradeoff, shared-feature idea is foundational, and claims are well-supported by experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: training complexity and unexplored convergence, and limited discussion of coarse-resolution localization limitations.
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance — paper is a landmark, high-quality contribution likely of great interest to the community.
