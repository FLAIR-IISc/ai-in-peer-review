- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with the detection network to eliminate the region proposal bottleneck.  
- RPNs are fully-convolutional, predicting object bounds and objectness at each feature-map position and sharing conv layers with Fast R-CNN.  
- Achieves near cost-free proposals with real-time performance (5 fps with VGG-16) while maintaining strong accuracy (73.2% mAP on VOC2007, 70.4% on VOC2012).  
- Key contributions: (1) fully-convolutional RPN for proposals, (2) training scheme that shares conv features via alternating optimization, (3) empirical demonstration of high accuracy and real-time speed.  
- Technical soundness: well-motivated approach with thorough evaluation; ablation studies show importance of shared features, classification, and regression layers.  
- Novelty: fully-conv design with translation-invariant anchors and feature sharing is a significant and practical innovation over prior work.  
- Significance: directly addresses a critical detection bottleneck, enabling real-time detection and influencing subsequent work (e.g., Mask R-CNN).  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with effective figures that explain architecture, loss, and training.  
- Weakness: alternating optimization appears to run only one iteration—needs clarification or justification.  
- Weakness: stride of 16 pixels on top-level feature map may cause coarse localization; authors should report mitigation or observed effects.  
- Weakness: unclear definition of h_a in the bounding-box regression formulation needs clarification.  
- Pro acceptance: solves bottleneck, achieves SOTA with real-time speed, elegant and widely adopted approach, thorough experiments, clear exposition.  
- Con acceptance: training alternation, potential stride-related localization limits, and some unclear mathematical definitions.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution with minor issues; recommend acceptance.
