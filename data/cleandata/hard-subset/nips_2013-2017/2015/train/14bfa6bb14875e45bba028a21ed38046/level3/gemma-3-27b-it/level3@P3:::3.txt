This paper introduces Faster R-CNN, a novel object detection system leveraging Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) to significantly improve the speed of region proposal generation, a key bottleneck in previous approaches like Fast R-CNN. By sharing convolutional layers between the RPN and the detection network, the authors achieve near real-time object detection (5 fps) with state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC datasets. This work builds upon previous advances in region-based CNNs [6, 7, 5] and addresses the computational cost of traditional region proposal methods like Selective Search [22] and EdgeBoxes [24]. It also relates to concurrent work exploring fully convolutional networks for object detection [18, 3, 20], but distinguishes itself through the efficient two-stage approach and shared convolutional features.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and presents a compelling solution to a significant problem in object detection. The experimental results demonstrate a clear improvement in both speed and accuracy compared to previous methods. The ablation studies effectively dissect the contributions of different components, such as shared convolutional layers and the RPN’s classification and regression outputs. The alternating optimization scheme, while pragmatic, is acknowledged as potentially suboptimal, and the authors clearly state their focus is on a practical, working system. The loss function and parameterizations are clearly explained, drawing appropriately from previous work [5]. The inclusion of running time measurements and comparison with existing methods strengthens the evaluation. However, a deeper theoretical analysis of the alternating optimization convergence could be beneficial.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The explanation of the RPN architecture and its integration with Fast R-CNN is clear and concise. The figures are helpful in illustrating the concepts. However, the definition of *h<sub>a</sub>* in lines 173-174 is indeed unclear. It's referenced in the context of anchor box height calculation but isn't explicitly defined or related to previous notation. A clear definition or explanation is necessary. Minor suggestions include more detailed explanations of the implementation details, such as the specific Caffe configuration.

**Originality:** The combination of RPNs with Fast R-CNN and the use of shared convolutional layers represents a novel and impactful contribution. While the individual components – CNNs, region proposals, and Fast R-CNN – are not new, the integrated approach and the efficiency gains are significant. The use of translation-invariant anchors is a clever design choice that simplifies the network and reduces the number of parameters compared to methods like MultiBox [20]. The comparison to OverFeat [18] highlights the advantages of a two-stage approach. The paper adequately references related work, demonstrating an understanding of the field.

**Significance:** Faster R-CNN is a highly significant contribution to the field of object detection. The near real-time performance, combined with state-of-the-art accuracy, makes it a valuable tool for a wide range of applications. The approach has been widely adopted and has served as a foundation for subsequent research in object detection. The idea of sharing convolutional features between different tasks is a generally applicable principle that could be extended to other vision problems. The accessible code repository further enhances the impact and usability of the work.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Significant speed improvement:** Achieves near real-time object detection, a major advance.
*   **State-of-the-art accuracy:** Demonstrates competitive or superior accuracy on standard benchmarks.
*   **Novel approach:** The RPN and shared convolutional layers are a clever and effective combination.
*   **Clear and thorough evaluation:** The ablation studies and comparisons to existing methods are convincing.
*   **Practical and impactful:** The code release and potential applications make this work highly valuable.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Suboptimal optimization:** The alternating optimization scheme is acknowledged as potentially not converging to the global optimum.
*   **Unclear definition:** The definition of *h<sub>a</sub>* requires clarification.
*   **Limited theoretical analysis:** A more rigorous analysis of the optimization process would be beneficial.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of object detection. The strengths far outweigh the weaknesses. Despite the minor concerns regarding the optimization scheme and the unclear definition, the paper is well-written, technically sound, and demonstrates a clear improvement over existing methods. I recommend acceptance.
