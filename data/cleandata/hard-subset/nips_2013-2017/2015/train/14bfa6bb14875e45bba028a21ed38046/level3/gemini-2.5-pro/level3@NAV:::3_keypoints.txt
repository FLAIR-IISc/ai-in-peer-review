- Proposes Faster R-CNN integrating region proposal generation into detection via a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN.  
- RPN replaces slow, external proposal methods (e.g., Selective Search), removing the primary computational bottleneck.  
- Delivers near real-time speeds (~5 fps on VGG-16) and improved mAP on PASCAL VOC compared to strong baselines.  
- High technical quality and originality: unified feature-sharing architecture backed by extensive experiments and ablation studies.  
- High significance: solving the proposal bottleneck and introducing anchors/RPN has become foundational for subsequent detectors.  
- Weakness — Training optimization: the 4-step alternating training is run only once, not fully end-to-end, which may limit feature co-adaptation.  
- Weakness — Localization precision: coarse anchor center grid due to large feature stride may hurt precise localization of small objects and boundary alignment.  
- Weakness — Clarity: the definition of h_a in the bounding-box regression parameterization should be explicitly stated for reproducibility.  
- Overall: a landmark, efficient, and accurate framework despite minor concerns.
