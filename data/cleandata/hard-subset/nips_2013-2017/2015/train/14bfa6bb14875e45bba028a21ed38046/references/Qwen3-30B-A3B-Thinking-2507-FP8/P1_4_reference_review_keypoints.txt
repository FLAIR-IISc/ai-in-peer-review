- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with detection networks to generate nearly cost-free region proposals, forming the Faster R-CNN framework.

- Technically robust with thorough ablation studies and comprehensive experiments on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012.

- Novel idea of sharing convolutional features between proposal and detection networks and translation-invariant anchor design.

- Demonstrates significant speed and accuracy improvements (e.g., 73.2% mAP on VOC2007; detection time reduced from ~1.5s to ~198ms).

- Paper is clear, well-organized, and provides sufficient implementation details and effective figures for reproducibility.

- Lacks deeper theoretical analysis explaining why feature sharing between proposal and detection is optimal.

- Anchor design (3 scales × 3 aspect ratios) appears somewhat arbitrary and not automatically optimized.

- Limited comparison with one-stage methods using stronger architectures (e.g., VGG-16), restricting evaluation of trade-offs.

- Focuses mainly on PASCAL VOC and does not fully address scalability to larger datasets like MS COCO.

- Quality: technically sound with well-supported claims and appropriate ablation studies.

- Clarity: exceptionally clear presentation and logical organization.

- Originality: novel feature-sharing approach and translation-invariant anchors that differ from prior work.

- Significance: addresses a key detection bottleneck, achieving higher accuracy and real-time performance with broad impact.

- Recommendation: strongly recommended for acceptance.

- Pro: solves the region proposal bottleneck in object detection.

- Pro: achieves state-of-the-art accuracy while enabling near real-time performance.

- Pro: elegant and effective feature-sharing solution between proposal and detection networks.

- Pro: thorough experimental evaluation with ablation studies.

- Pro: clear, well-written, and highly reproducible work.

- Con: lacks deeper theoretical justification for feature sharing.

- Con: arbitrary anchor parameter choices without automatic determination.

- Con: limited benchmarking against one-stage methods on stronger backbones.

- Con: limited discussion of scaling to larger datasets.
