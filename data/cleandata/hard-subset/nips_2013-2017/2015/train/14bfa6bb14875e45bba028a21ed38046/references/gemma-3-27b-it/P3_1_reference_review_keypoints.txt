- Overall: Integrates region proposal generation into the detection network via RPNs, removing the Selective Search bottleneck and enabling near real-time detection; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Problem: Addresses slow, CPU-bound region proposal generation that limited end-to-end detection speed.
- Method: Proposes fully convolutional Region Proposal Networks that share convolutional features with the detector to produce nearly cost-free proposals.
- Positioning: Correctly situates work among R-CNN/Fast R-CNN, OverFeat, and MultiBox, highlighting differences in translation invariance and parameter efficiency.
- Speed: Achieves dramatic proposal time reduction (~10 ms) and near real-time detection (~5 fps with VGG-16).
- Accuracy: Improves detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC compared to Selective Search + Fast R-CNN.
- Integration: Elegant and effective sharing of convolutional features between RPN and Fast R-CNN.
- Novelty: Shifts from engineered to learned region proposals, a novel and impactful idea.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and accompanied by informative figures and experiments.
- Ablation: Comprehensive ablation studies isolate the effects of shared features and classification vs. regression outputs.
- Reproducibility: Code is publicly available, aiding reproducibility and adoption.
- Anchor sensitivity: Choice of anchor scales/aspect ratios seems arbitrary and needs deeper analysis or guidance for other datasets.
- Failure analysis: Limited discussion of common failure cases (e.g., small or occluded objects) and remedies.
- IoU thresholds: Positive/negative IoU cutoffs (0.3/0.7) are somewhat ad-hoc and lack justification or exploration of alternatives.
- Training cost: Alternating optimization of RPN and Fast R-CNN may be computationally expensive; training time/resource details are missing.
- Significance: Highly original and significant; parameter-efficient, translation-invariant design likely to be widely adopted and influential.
- Pros: Major speed/accuracy gains, elegant integration, solid experiments, and public code favor acceptance.
- Cons: Needs more analysis on anchor selection, failure modes, and IoU/training cost justification.
- Questions for authors: Rationale and experiments for 3 scales × 3 aspect ratios; qualitative failure analysis; approximate end-to-end training time; guidance for adapting anchors to datasets with different object scales/aspect ratios.
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
