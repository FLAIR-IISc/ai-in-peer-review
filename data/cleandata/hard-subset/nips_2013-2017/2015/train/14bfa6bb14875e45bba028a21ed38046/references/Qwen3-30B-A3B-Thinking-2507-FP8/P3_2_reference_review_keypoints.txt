- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that generate region proposals by sharing convolutional features with the detection network, enabling near cost-free proposals and a unified detection system.
- Proposes a 4-step training algorithm to share conv features between RPN and Fast R-CNN, achieving state-of-the-art accuracy with much faster runtime.

- Novel technical contribution: RPNs effectively address the region proposal bottleneck using shared convolutional features and anchor boxes.
- Strong empirical performance: 73.2% mAP (PASCAL VOC), 5–17 fps on GPU, and dramatic runtime reduction (198ms vs 1510ms for Selective Search).
- Methodologically clear: well-explained RPN architecture, training procedure, and anchor design with scales/aspect ratios.
- Comprehensive evaluation: thorough ablation studies isolating classification/regression contributions and showing importance of shared features.
- High practical impact: RPNs became a standard component in modern detection systems and bridge research to practical application.

- Novelty concerns: prior works (MultiBox, OverFeat) used deep nets for proposals and the distinction could be more explicitly emphasized.
- Lacks deeper theoretical justification for why RPNs and the 4-step alternating training converge so effectively.
- Limited comparisons to other contemporary/shortly following methods (e.g., SSD, R-FCN), weakening broader benchmarking.
- Some implementation details are under-specified (e.g., handling anchors that cross image boundaries).
- Scalability unclear: limited evaluation beyond PASCAL VOC, no COCO results to demonstrate larger-scale performance.

- Overall technically sound with reproducible experiments and well-supported claims.
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized writing with helpful figures and sufficient detail for experts to reproduce results.
- Original contribution: translation-invariant anchors and shared conv features represent a significant advancement despite related prior work.
- Highly significant: solves a major detection bottleneck, enabling real-time detection and influencing many follow-up works.
- Recommendation: Accept.

- Suggested additions: explicitly compare RPNs to MultiBox/OverFeat in Related Work to clarify novel differences.
- Suggested additions: include brief theoretical insights on why shared features work well (e.g., information-theoretic view).
- Suggested clarifications: detail the exact handling and training effect of anchors crossing image boundaries.
- Suggested comparison: evaluate speed-accuracy tradeoffs against SSD.
- Suggested scalability experiment: report or discuss COCO results to show performance on larger datasets.

- Pros for acceptance: solves a key bottleneck, strong speed/accuracy gains, novel and influential, thorough experiments, excellent writing.
- Cons against acceptance: similarities to prior deep-proposal work, lacking theoretical analysis, limited large-scale benchmarking, some missing implementation specifics.

- Final note: Highly influential paper (>10,000 citations) that significantly advanced object detection and is strongly recommended for acceptance.
