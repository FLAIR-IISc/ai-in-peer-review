- Paper proposes Region Proposal Networks (RPNs): a fully convolutional module that generates class-agnostic proposals from shared conv feature maps using k anchors per spatial location and a multi-task loss, trained with a 4-step alternating scheme and integrated with Fast R-CNN.
- RPN design uses a 3×3 conv head predicting objectness and bbox regressions for 3 scales × 3 aspect ratios anchors at each location.
- Shared features make proposal computation nearly free (~10 ms/image), enabling 5–17 fps and higher mAP than Selective Search (SS) and EdgeBoxes (EB) on PASCAL VOC.
- Strength: clear, simple, and effective architecture with a principled multi-task loss and anchor parameterization.
- Strength: strong empirical results—VGG-16 + RPN outperforms SS/EB with only 300 proposals and much faster runtime.
- Strength: thorough ablations examining sharing, proposal count, classification vs regression roles, NMS effects, and a one-stage baseline.
- Strength: practical impact—compelling speed–accuracy trade-off, near cost-free proposals on GPU, and publicly available code.
- Strength: good analysis of translation-invariant anchors versus learned global priors, arguing reduced parameter count and overfitting risk.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to PASCAL VOC; lacks results on larger/modern benchmarks and diverse domains, and more detailed per-size and per-class breakdowns.
- Weakness: 4-step alternating training is ad hoc; no experiments on fully joint end-to-end optimization or its stability.
- Weakness: sensitivity to anchor design underexplored—only one set of 3 scales × 3 ratios evaluated; feature stride and image scale sensitivity not analyzed.
- Weakness: speed comparisons may be unfair because SS and EB are CPU implementations while RPN+detector use GPU; need GPU-optimized baselines or CPU-only breakdowns.
- Weakness: limited side-by-side comparisons to other deep proposal methods like MultiBox on the same dataset and backbone.
- Weakness: proposal quality analysis incomplete—high-IoU (≥0.85) recall, localization error analysis, and failure modes not thoroughly examined.
- Weakness: clarity issues—loss normalization and λ choices partly in a footnote; consolidate settings for reproducibility and fix reference formatting.
- Quality: methodology is technically sound and supported by ablations; experimental evidence convincing for VOC despite some underexplored aspects.
- Clarity: generally well written and organized, but minor issues like loss constants, typical NMS output counts, and reference/footnote artifacts need fixing.
- Originality: moderate-to-high—main novelty is integrating proposal generation into detection via shared features and translation-invariant anchors with a compact conv-head formulation.
- Significance: high—removes a major bottleneck in region-based detectors, improves accuracy, and is likely to be widely adopted.
- Requests: please report results for fully joint RPN+Fast R-CNN training, anchor scale/ratio and stride sensitivity, NMS threshold/soft-NMS experiments, per-class and size-based AP, and false-positive breakdowns.
- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical gains with large speedups, elegant/simple architecture with ablations, well-motivated anchor design, and reproducible code.
- Cons for acceptance: limited dataset scope, heuristic alternating training lacking joint optimization results, and limited sensitivity analysis for anchor and resolution choices.
- Minor comments: fix incomplete/formatting references, clarify final λ and normalization settings, and correct typographical artifacts (e.g., misplaced footnotes).
- Recommendation: Accept (strong).
