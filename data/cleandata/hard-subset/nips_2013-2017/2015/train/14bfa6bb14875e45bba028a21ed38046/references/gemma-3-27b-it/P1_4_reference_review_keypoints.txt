- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that learn region proposals using convolutional features shared with the detector.  
- Sharing computation between RPN and Fast R-CNN makes proposal generation nearly cost-free and enables near real-time detection.  
- Demonstrates improved accuracy over Selective Search baselines (73.2% mAP VOC2007, 70.4% VOC2012 with VGG-16).  
- Positions work well relative to prior methods (R-CNN family, OverFeat, MultiBox) and highlights end-to-end learning benefits.  
- Significant speed improvement: reported ~10 ms for proposal generation and ~5 fps overall with VGG-16.  
- Accuracy improvement indicates higher-quality proposals from learned RPNs versus traditional methods.  
- Alternating optimization for joint training of RPN and detector is effective and converges well.  
- Novelty: learning proposals inside the detection network and leveraging shared conv features is a substantial contribution.  
- Clear presentation and helpful illustrations; paper is well-organized and easy to follow.  
- Thorough evaluation with ablation studies shedding light on component contributions (cls vs. reg, network depth).  
- Public code release supports reproducibility and future research.  
- Weakness: performance depends on anchor box design (scales/aspect ratios) which may be dataset-sensitive.  
- Weakness: method is limited to rectangular regions, which may be suboptimal for some tasks.  
- Weakness: comparison to one-stage methods (e.g., OverFeat) could be deeper; unclear when two-stage will consistently outperform one-stage.  
- Weakness: added architectural complexity and new hyperparameters (anchors, IoU thresholds) increase tuning burden.  
- Weakness: large stride / receptive field on final conv layer could hinder small object detection.  
- Overall assessment: highly impactful, novel, and well-supported work that addresses a major detection bottleneck.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves critical proposal bottleneck, significant speedup, novel shared-computation architecture, strong results, public code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: anchor sensitivity and increased complexity/tuning requirements.  
- Reviewer rating: Strong Accept (8/10) — significant contribution likely to influence future work.
