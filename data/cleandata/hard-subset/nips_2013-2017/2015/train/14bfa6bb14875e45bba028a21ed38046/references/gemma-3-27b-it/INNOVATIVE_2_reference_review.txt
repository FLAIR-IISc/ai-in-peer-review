This paper presents a compelling solution to a critical bottleneck in modern object detection: region proposal generation. The authors convincingly demonstrate that a deep convolutional network – a Region Proposal Network (RPN) – can perform this task nearly cost-free when shared with the detection network, dramatically accelerating the overall process. This is a genuinely novel idea, shifting away from hand-crafted features (like those used in Selective Search) and leveraging the power of learned representations. While the paper doesn’t introduce radically new architectural components, the *application* of fully-convolutional networks to region proposal generation, coupled with the shared computation strategy, is a significant contribution. It addresses a practical, impactful problem with an elegant and effective solution.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The core idea of using a deep network for region proposals, specifically designed to share computation with the detection network, is highly novel. This moves beyond incremental improvements in proposal algorithms and presents a fundamental shift in how the problem is approached.
*   **Impact:** The performance gains are substantial. Achieving near real-time object detection (5fps with VGG-16) is a significant step forward, and the ablation studies clearly demonstrate the benefit of the shared convolutional layers.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and organized. The explanation of the RPN architecture, the training scheme, and the loss function are all clear and concise. The illustrations (especially Fig. 1) are helpful.
*   **Thoroughness:** The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation, including ablation studies, comparisons to existing methods (SS, EdgeBoxes), and results on standard datasets (PASCAL VOC). The analysis of recall-to-IoU is a valuable addition.
*   **Practicality:** The availability of code further enhances the value of this work, making it readily accessible to the research community.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the empirical results are strong, the paper lacks a deeper theoretical analysis of *why* this approach works so well. A more formal understanding of the benefits of shared computation would be valuable.
*   **Anchor Box Sensitivity:** The performance is likely sensitive to the choice of anchor box scales and aspect ratios. While the authors justify their choices, a more systematic exploration of this parameter space could strengthen the results.
*   **Training Complexity:** The 4-step training algorithm, while pragmatic, feels somewhat ad-hoc. Exploring more streamlined or theoretically grounded training procedures could be beneficial.
* **Comparison to One-Stage Detectors:** While the comparison to the Overfeat-style one-stage detector is present, it feels a little dismissive. More in-depth discussion of the trade-offs, particularly in terms of accuracy vs. speed, would be warranted.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a genuinely novel and impactful idea – a region proposal network that shares computation with the detection network.
*   The empirical results are strong and demonstrate significant performance gains.
*   The paper is well-written, clear, and thoroughly evaluated.
*   The practical impact of this work is high, potentially accelerating research in object detection.
*   This paper represents a significant step toward truly real-time object detection.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of deeper theoretical analysis.
*   The training procedure feels somewhat empirical and could benefit from further investigation.
*   The paper, while strong, doesn't revolutionize the field - it's an excellent engineering contribution built on solid foundations.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a very strong paper that represents a significant contribution to the field of object detection. The novelty of the approach, the impressive empirical results, and the clarity of the presentation outweigh the minor weaknesses. I believe this paper is well-suited for NIPS, despite not containing breakthrough theoretical results, as it represents a significant practical advancement and opens up exciting new avenues for research. I **strongly recommend acceptance**. The paper prioritizes a clever and effective solution to a practical problem over complete theoretical rigor, which I believe is appropriate for this work.
