- Paper introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that unify proposal generation and detection by sharing convolutional features for near cost-free proposals.
- RPN+Fast R-CNN achieves 73.2% mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007 and runs at 5 fps with VGG-16, outperforming Selective Search and EdgeBoxes in speed and accuracy.

Strengths:
- Core contribution is technically sound and practically significant, advocating shared conv features over traditional pipelines.
- RPN is an elegant fully convolutional design using translation-invariant anchors for proposal generation.
- Ablation studies effectively isolate component contributions and show objectness scores are critical.
- Comparison to one-stage methods demonstrates advantages of the two-stage cascade.
- Detailed runtime analysis shows RPN adds only ~10ms per image, enabling real-time performance.
- Work eliminates external proposal methods, yielding an end-to-end trainable, faster, and more accurate system.

Weaknesses:
- Anchor configuration (3 scales × 3 ratios) is arbitrary and not thoroughly explored or justified.
- Lacks detailed analysis or qualitative examples explaining why RPN proposals outperform Selective Search.
- The 4-step training procedure is pragmatic but lacks theoretical justification or exploration of alternatives.
- Evaluation is limited on challenging scenarios (small objects, occlusion, crowded scenes) and per-category nuances.

Recommendations:
- Add qualitative comparisons of RPN proposals vs Selective Search with illustrative examples.
- Explore and report effects of alternative anchor configurations, especially per-category impacts.
- Provide a deeper discussion or analysis of the 4-step training procedure and possible principled alternatives.
- Expand evaluation to address small objects, occlusion, and crowded scenes to characterize limitations.

Overall assessment:
- Significant, original contribution that advances speed and accuracy in object detection; technically strong and influential — recommend accept.
- Key arguments for acceptance: novel unified proposal approach, clear speed/accuracy gains, comprehensive evaluations, and solid presentation.
- Key arguments against acceptance: insufficient justification for anchor/training choices, limited analysis on hard categories, and lack of detailed explanation for proposal quality improvements.
