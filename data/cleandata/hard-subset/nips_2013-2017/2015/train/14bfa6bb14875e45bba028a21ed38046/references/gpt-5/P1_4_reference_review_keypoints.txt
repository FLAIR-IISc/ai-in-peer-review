- Proposes Region Proposal Networks (RPNs): a fully convolutional, anchor-based module that generates class-agnostic proposals by sliding over shared conv feature maps.  
- Uses k=9 anchors per location with sibling heads for objectness and bbox regression, trained with multi-task loss and IoU labeling.  
- Shares conv features with Fast R-CNN and uses a 4-step alternating training to produce near-zero-cost proposals for the detector.  
- Advances prior work by replacing CPU-based proposals (Selective Search, EdgeBoxes) and improving on OverFeat/MultiBox via translation-invariant anchors and feature sharing.  
- Technical formulation is clear and principled, with standard choices (smooth L1, sampling, NMS) and a reasonable alternating training strategy.  
- Empirical evaluation includes ablations (shared vs unshared, cls vs reg, NMS/top-N, number of proposals) and two backbones (ZF, VGG16).  
- Demonstrates competitive or superior mAP and much faster runtime (5–17 fps including proposals) on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012.  
- Recall-to-IoU analysis provided to diagnose proposal quality.  
- Reproducibility is strong: implementation details given and code availability stated.  
- Paper is well written; architecture, anchor design, loss, and training scheme are clearly explained.  
- Minor clarity suggestions: move normalization details from footnote to main text, explicitly state number of test-time proposals in tables, and add per-class/small-medium-large breakdowns.  
- Originality lies in a compact, translation-invariant, anchor-based RPN that shares features with the detector and the pragmatic alternating training scheme.  
- Significant impact: removes a major bottleneck, improves accuracy-speed trade-off, and likely to be widely adopted and extended.  
- Pros: elegant, low-overhead design; strong results with as few as 300 proposals; thorough ablations; clear comparisons; reproducible implementation.  
- Cons/questions: no joint end-to-end optimization attempted; sensitivity to anchor design not deeply explored; limited evaluation to PASCAL VOC (no COCO-scale results).  
- Additional concerns: runtime comparisons mix GPU and CPU implementations; stride=16 may hurt small-object detection; limited head-to-head with MultiBox under matched settings.  
- Suggested improvements: add anchor/NMS sensitivity study and small-object results, discuss joint training attempts, include per-class AP and error analysis, and report GPU memory/throughput breakdowns.  
- If feasible, add larger-benchmark (COCO) results or subset experiments to show scalability.  
- Recommendation: Accept — substantial, practical advance with high anticipated community impact.  
- Confidence: High.
