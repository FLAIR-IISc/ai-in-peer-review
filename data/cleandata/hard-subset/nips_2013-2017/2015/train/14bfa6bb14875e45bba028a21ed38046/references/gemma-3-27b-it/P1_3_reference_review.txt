## Review of "Faster R-CNN: Towards Real-Time Object Detection with Region Proposal Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs), a novel approach to generating object detection proposals directly within a deep convolutional neural network. Instead of relying on slower, traditional methods like Selective Search, the authors propose a fully convolutional network that shares convolutional layers with the subsequent object detection network (Fast R-CNN). This shared computation drastically reduces the time spent on proposal generation, bringing object detection closer to real-time performance. The key innovation lies in using anchors – a set of pre-defined boxes with varying scales and aspect ratios – to parameterize proposal generation, enabling a translation-invariant and efficient proposal mechanism.  The paper demonstrates improved accuracy and significantly faster performance on PASCAL VOC benchmarks compared to state-of-the-art methods using traditional proposal techniques.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds upon the rapid advancements in object detection fueled by region-based CNNs (R-CNN, Fast R-CNN). It directly addresses the bottleneck identified in Fast R-CNN – the time-consuming region proposal step. The authors acknowledge and contrast their approach with Selective Search and EdgeBoxes, highlighting their slowness. The paper also situates itself within the broader context of utilizing deep networks for bounding box prediction (OverFeat, MultiBox), differentiating itself by focusing on a unified, shared-computation approach rather than separate networks for proposal generation and detection. The authors correctly position their RPN as a fully convolutional network, relating it to work in semantic segmentation (FCNs).

**Strengths:**

* **Significant Speed Improvement:** The core contribution—reducing the proposal generation time to near-cost-free—is a major achievement. Achieving 10ms per image for proposal generation is a substantial leap forward and enables near real-time object detection (5fps with VGG-16).
* **Improved Accuracy:** The RPN, when combined with Fast R-CNN, delivers competitive and, in many cases, superior accuracy to existing methods employing Selective Search. The ablation studies effectively demonstrate this.
* **Elegant and Unified Approach:** The shared convolutional layers create a seamless integration between proposal generation and detection, simplifying the pipeline and potentially facilitating end-to-end training (though the current approach is alternating).
* **Well-Designed Anchor Mechanism:** The use of anchors with varying scales and aspect ratios is a clever way to handle the diversity of object shapes and sizes, and their translation-invariance is a key strength.
* **Thorough Experimental Evaluation:** The paper includes comprehensive experiments on PASCAL VOC, including ablation studies, comparisons with state-of-the-art methods, and analysis of recall-to-IoU.
* **Clear and Detailed Explanations:**  The paper is well-written and provides a clear description of the RPN architecture, training process, and experimental setup.
* **Code Release:** Providing publicly available code (https://github.com/ShaoqingRen/faster_rcnn) is a significant benefit to the research community.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Alternating Training:** While the alternating training scheme works well, it's not a truly end-to-end optimization. The authors acknowledge this and suggest joint training as a potential future direction, but the current approach is a compromise.  The impact of this non-end-to-end training on ultimate achievable performance remains an open question.
* **Anchor Hyperparameters:** The choice of anchor scales and aspect ratios seems somewhat arbitrary. While the authors justify their choice, a more systematic investigation of these hyperparameters could be beneficial.
* **Limited Scope of Ablation:** While thorough, the ablation studies could be expanded. For instance, analyzing the impact of different numbers of anchors (k) beyond the chosen value of 9 might be insightful.
* **Comparison to One-Stage Detectors:** While the authors briefly discuss and compare to OverFeat, a more detailed comparison to emerging one-stage detectors (like SSD) would provide valuable context.  The trade-offs between two-stage (RPN + Fast R-CNN) and one-stage approaches warrant deeper analysis.



**Arguments for/against Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Solves a critical bottleneck in object detection.
* Achieves state-of-the-art performance in terms of both speed and accuracy.
* Presents a novel and elegant approach to region proposal generation.
* Well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and publicly available.
* Significant impact on the field of object detection.

**Con:**

* Alternating training scheme isn’t fully end-to-end.
* Hyperparameter choices (anchors) could be explored more thoroughly.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a very strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of object detection. The introduction of Region Proposal Networks is a clever and effective solution to the proposal generation bottleneck, and the resulting Faster R-CNN system represents a substantial step towards real-time object detection. While the alternating training scheme and anchor hyperparameters are areas for potential future work, the benefits of the proposed approach are undeniable. 

**Recommendation:**

**Strong Accept.**  This paper is a clear fit for NIPS and should be accepted for presentation.



**Review Criteria Assessment:**

* **Quality:** Excellent. The paper is technically sound, well-supported by experimental results, and represents a complete piece of work.
* **Clarity:** Excellent. The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand.
* **Originality:** Excellent. The RPN concept is novel and significantly different from previous approaches.
* **Significance:** Excellent. The results are important and likely to be widely adopted by the object detection community. This work advances the state-of-the-art in a demonstrable way.
