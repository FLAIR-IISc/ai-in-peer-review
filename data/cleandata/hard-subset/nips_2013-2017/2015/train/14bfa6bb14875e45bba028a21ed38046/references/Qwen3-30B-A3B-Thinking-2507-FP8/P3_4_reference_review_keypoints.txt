- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional feature maps with the detector to generate nearly cost-free region proposals.  
- Proposes a 4-step training scheme to jointly optimize RPN and detection network, producing a unified, end-to-end system.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy (73.2% mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007) and real-time performance (5–17 fps; ~198ms with VGG-16).  
- Thorough experimental validation with comprehensive ablation studies demonstrating importance of shared features, classification/regression components, and proposal count.  
- Novelty in translation-invariant anchor design and a compact parameterization (2.4M vs. MultiBox’s 27M), distinguishing it from prior work (OverFeat, MultiBox).  
- High practical significance: addresses region proposal bottleneck and influenced subsequent detectors (e.g., Mask R-CNN, RetinaNet).  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized with reproducible implementation details (anchors, losses, training procedures).  
- Limited evaluation scope: lacks experiments on more challenging datasets like MS COCO to show scalability and robustness.  
- Anchor configuration (3 scales × 3 ratios, k=9) not thoroughly explored or justified experimentally.  
- The 4-step training procedure is pragmatic but lacks theoretical justification or analysis of alternatives.  
- Potential weakness in small-object detection due to fixed stride (16 pixels) not being discussed or addressed.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, well-executed contribution with minor limitations that do not undermine impact.
