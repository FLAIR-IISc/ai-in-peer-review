- Introduces Region Proposal Network (RPN) to generate object proposals inside a CNN, removing slow external proposal algorithms.  
- RPN is fully convolutional and shares features with Fast R-CNN, sliding a small network to regress boxes and predict objectness for anchor boxes.  
- Significant contribution that enables end-to-end deep detectors with much higher speed (≈5 fps with VGG-16) and strong accuracy.  
- Novel and elegant use of translation-invariant "anchors" as reference boxes, improving over prior non-translation-invariant methods.  
- Technically strong paper with extensive experiments on PASCAL VOC, thorough ablations, and public code for reproducibility.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, well-motivated architecture diagrams, and detailed training procedure.  
- Training requires a 4-step alternating scheme to learn shared features, which is more complex than a single joint optimization.  
- Introduces anchor hyperparameters (counts, scales, aspect ratios) with little analysis of sensitivity or automatic selection for other datasets.  
- Large network stride (16px) may limit detection of very small objects; no detailed size-wise performance analysis provided.  
- Solves a major bottleneck in object detection and unifies proposal and detection into one coherent framework.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy while significantly improving detection speed, enabling near real-time use with deep models.  
- Work is a landmark contribution likely to be widely adopted and extended in future research.  
- Cons: training heuristic complexity and anchor design may require dataset-specific tuning.  
- Overall recommendation: outstanding paper with minor weaknesses; strong accept (oral likely).
