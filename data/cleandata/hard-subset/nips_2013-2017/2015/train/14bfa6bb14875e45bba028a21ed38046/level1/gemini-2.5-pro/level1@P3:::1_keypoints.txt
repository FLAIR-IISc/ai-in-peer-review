- Introduces Region Proposal Network (RPN) that predicts proposals from conv feature maps and shares features with Fast R-CNN, making proposal generation almost free (~10ms) and enabling Faster R-CNN.  
- Integrating a learnable RPN into the detection network is highly original and solves a major computational bottleneck, yielding large speed and accuracy gains.  
- Technical approach is sound: translation-invariant anchors, multi-task loss, and extensive ablations convincingly support the design choices.  
- Paper is clear and well-written with sufficient methodological detail and effective figures/tables for reproducibility.  
- The 4-step alternating training procedure is somewhat convoluted and feels engineered rather than a principled joint optimization.  
- Anchor design introduces critical hyperparameters (number, scales, aspect ratios) whose sensitivity and selection for new datasets are not fully explored.  
- Ablation comparing two-stage vs. one-stage shows a significant 4.8% mAP drop for one-stage, supporting the cascaded refinement approach.  
- Recall-vs-IoU analysis demonstrates RPN’s robust objectness scoring and graceful degradation with fewer proposals compared to Selective Search.  
- Question raised about step 3 of training: why freeze shared conv layers when fine-tuning RPN-specific layers, and would small‑LR updates help or destabilize training?  
- Overall assessment: significant, high-quality contribution that sets a new state-of-the-art; strong accept and recommended for oral presentation.
