- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to produce nearly cost-free region proposals.  
- Identifies region proposal computation as the bottleneck in prior detection pipelines and addresses it with a fully-convolutional RPN predicting bounds and objectness at each position.  
- Uses an alternating 4-step training scheme to enable feature sharing between RPN and Fast R-CNN.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007 (73.2% mAP) and 2012 (70.4% mAP) while running at ~5 fps and producing proposals in ~10 ms/image.  
- Technically sound method with a clear loss formulation and thorough ablation studies validating design choices.  
- Strong experimental comparisons showing improvements over Selective Search and EdgeBoxes in speed and accuracy.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized, with helpful figures and sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Novel contribution: translation-invariant anchor mechanism and integrated proposal+detection pipeline that departs from separate proposal methods.  
- High practical significance and influence: solves a critical bottleneck and enabled real-time, accurate detection adopted by the community.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis explaining why shared-feature RPNs generalize so well.  
- Weakness: evaluation focused mainly on PASCAL VOC; broader benchmarks like MS COCO are missing.  
- Weakness: does not compare to detection methods that appeared after submission (e.g., SSD, YOLO), though timing explains this omission.
