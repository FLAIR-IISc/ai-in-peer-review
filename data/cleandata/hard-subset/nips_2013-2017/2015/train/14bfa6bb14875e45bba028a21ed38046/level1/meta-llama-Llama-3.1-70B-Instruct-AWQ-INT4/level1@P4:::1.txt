This paper introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs), a novel approach for efficient and accurate region proposal generation in object detection. The authors propose a fully-convolutional network that shares convolutional features with the downstream detection network, making the region proposal step nearly cost-free.

The main idea of the paper is to use a deep network to compute proposals, rather than relying on traditional region proposal methods. The authors argue that this approach leads to an elegant and effective solution, where proposal computation is nearly cost-free given the detection network's computation.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain the motivation and contributions of their approach. The technical details of the RPN architecture and training procedure are well-explained, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the experimental results.

The technical soundness of the paper is strong. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach on the PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 benchmarks, and demonstrate that their method achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy while being significantly faster than traditional region proposal methods.

The novelty of the paper is high. The authors propose a new approach to region proposal generation that is significantly different from traditional methods. The use of a deep network to compute proposals is a new and interesting idea, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in achieving state-of-the-art object detection accuracy.

The significance of the paper is also high. The authors demonstrate that their approach can be used to achieve real-time object detection, which is a critical requirement for many applications. The paper also provides a new perspective on the problem of region proposal generation, and the authors' approach has the potential to be widely adopted in the field.
