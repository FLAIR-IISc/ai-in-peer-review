- Summary: Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to generate nearly cost-free region proposals and achieve practical real-time detection.  
- Summary result: Demonstrates state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 and ~5 fps on a GPU.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Selective Search, EdgeBoxes, R-CNN/Fast R-CNN, and OverFeat-style localization, extending shared conv-feature detection.  
- Strength — Efficient proposals: RPN shares conv features with the detector, making region proposals nearly cost-free.  
- Strength — Accuracy: Achieves state-of-the-art detection performance on standard benchmarks.  
- Strength — Speed: Achieves real-time-capable performance (~5 fps on GPU).  
- Weakness — Training complexity: Requires a complex 4-step training procedure to learn shared RPN and Fast R-CNN features.  
- Weakness — Limited analysis: Lacks detailed analysis of the Recall-to-IoU metric and its connection to final detection performance.  
- Originality: Introduces a novel RPN that tightly integrates with Fast R-CNN, distinct from prior proposal methods.  
- Significance: High practical impact with potential adoption in autonomous vehicles, surveillance, and robotics due to accuracy and speed.  
- Pro acceptance: Strong empirical results, real-time performance, and novel approach justify publication.  
- Con acceptance: Complexity of training and insufficient metric analysis are reasons for caution.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS, citing originality, state-of-the-art results, and real-time performance despite limitations.
