- Proposes Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that predict objectness and bbox offsets for translation-invariant anchors on shared conv feature maps, enabling nearly-free proposals and a fast two-stage pipeline.  
- Builds on R-CNN/SPPnet/Fast R-CNN and addresses Selective Search/EdgeBoxes bottlenecks; differs from OverFeat/MultiBox by anchor design and feature sharing.  
- Key contribution: sharing convolutional features between proposal generator and Fast R-CNN detector, reducing test-time and improving mAP with only 300 proposals.  
- Well-justified anchor design (multiple scales/aspect ratios) yields compact, translation-invariant parameterization and better regularization than MultiBox.  
- Thorough ablation studies (sharing vs. unshared, proposal count, NMS, heads on/off, one-stage vs. two-stage) support claims.  
- Practical 4-step alternating training recipe that avoids unstable joint training and converges reliably.  
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 at practical speeds; code release aids reproducibility.  
- Missing joint end-to-end training results; alternating training is pragmatic but leaves potential gains unexplored.  
- Limited exploration of anchor sensitivity (only 3 scales × 3 ratios) and lacking per-class/small-object recall breakdowns.  
- Possible bias: RPN trained on detection categories may not be truly class-agnostic; cross-dataset/class generalization not evaluated.  
- Runtime comparisons partly confounded by CPU vs. GPU implementations of baseline proposal methods, weakening "nearly free" claim.  
- Some training details scattered; consolidation of hyperparameters, loss normalization, and minibatch composition would aid reproduction.  
- Technically sound with standard, appropriate losses and sampling; key disentanglement experiments are informative.  
- Generally well written with clear figures; minor clarity improvements suggested (training hyperparams, training schedule diagram, per-class AP).  
- Moderately high originality: anchor-based, translation-invariant formulation and tight feature sharing are novel and important.  
- High significance: addresses a major detection bottleneck with a general template likely to be widely adopted.  
- Suggested improvements: attempt/report joint end-to-end training, anchor/IoU/NMS sensitivity analyses, cross-dataset transfer tests, per-class AP and recall plots, and clearer runtime baselines.  
- Arguments for acceptance: simple/strong idea, solid ablations, clear advancement over prior methods, and public code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, no joint training results, unclear proposal generalization, and runtime comparison confounds.  
- Overall recommendation: significant, timely contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
