- Introduces Faster R-CNN, integrating region proposal generation directly into the network to remove external proposal methods.  
- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detector, yielding near cost-free proposals.  
- RPN is fully convolutional, predicts object bounds and objectness scores, and is trained end-to-end with Fast R-CNN via alternating optimization.  
- Introduces anchor boxes with multiple scales and aspect ratios to handle diverse object shapes and sizes.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC (73.2% mAP 2007, 70.4% mAP 2012) with substantial speed improvement (≈5 fps with VGG-16).  
- Well-motivated work that clearly identifies and addresses the region proposal bottleneck in prior detectors.  
- Related work is adequately covered and design choices (e.g., GPU vs CPU comparisons) are justified.  
- Technically sound: clear architecture, appropriate loss formulation, and thorough experimental ablations.  
- Significant impact: simplifies the detection pipeline and advances toward real-time, end-to-end trainable detection.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized writing with informative figures.  
- Original contribution: novel integration of RPN with Fast R-CNN and the specific training procedure.  
- Weakness: alternating optimization increases training complexity and may affect robustness across datasets/architectures.  
- Weakness: potential sensitivity to anchor box scale/aspect choices; deeper sensitivity analysis would help.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to some more recent proposal methods or one-stage detectors.  
- Overall: very strong paper with strengths outweighing weaknesses; strongly recommended for acceptance.
