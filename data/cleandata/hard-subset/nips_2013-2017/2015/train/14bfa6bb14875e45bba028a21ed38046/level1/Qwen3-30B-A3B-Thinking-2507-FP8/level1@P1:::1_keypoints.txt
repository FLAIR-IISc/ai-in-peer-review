- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with the detection network, removing the region proposal bottleneck.  
- Builds on R-CNN and Fast R-CNN, using shared conv features to make proposals nearly cost-free.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results (73.2% mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007) and runs at ~5 FPS on a GPU, far faster than Selective Search or EdgeBoxes.  
- Strong technical quality with a well-designed method and no apparent methodological flaws.  
- Extensive ablation studies validate key contributions: feature sharing, classification/regression components, and deeper networks.  
- Training procedure (4-step alternating optimization) is clearly described and experimentally validated.  
- Experiments on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 provide comprehensive and well-supported comparisons to baselines.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and organization; RPN architecture and translation-invariant anchors are well explained and illustrated.  
- Minor clarity weakness: loss function discussion could use slightly more detail.  
- Original contribution: sharing conv features for proposals and translation-invariant anchors, distinct from prior MultiBox/OverFeat approaches.  
- Significant practical impact: removes critical bottleneck, enabling real-time detection without sacrificing accuracy and simplifying the pipeline.  
- Open-sourcing code increases practical adoption and potential influence on future work.  
- Strengths: novel insight on shared features, comprehensive validation, practical speed/accuracy, clear exposition, reproducible implementation details.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of failure cases (small/occluded objects), few explored extensions (e.g., video), and comparison to one-stage detectors could be deeper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel, significant speed and accuracy improvements, well-validated and practically useful.  
- Arguments against acceptance: few compelling reasons; not merely incremental but a fundamental improvement.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution, elegant solution to a core bottleneck, thoroughly evaluated and highly relevant to the vision community.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
