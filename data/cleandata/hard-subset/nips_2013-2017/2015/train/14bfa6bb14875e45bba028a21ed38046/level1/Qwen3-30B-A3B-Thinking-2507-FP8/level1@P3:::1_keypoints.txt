- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features between proposal and detection, removing the Selective Search bottleneck.  
- Proposes a unified, fully-convolutional framework where RPNs share features with Fast R-CNN for proposal generation.  
- Achieves large speedup (5 fps on VGG-16) while maintaining state-of-the-art mAP (73.2% VOC2007, 70.4% VOC2012).  
- Builds on SPPnet and Fast R-CNN and correctly identifies region proposal as the new pipeline bottleneck.  
- Uses translation-invariant anchor design that requires far fewer parameters than MultiBox.  
- Strong technical validation with comprehensive ablation studies (cls/reg branches, network depths, proposal counts).  
- Exceptionally clear presentation and thorough implementation details, including a helpful Figure 1 and the 4-step training algorithm.  
- Empirical evidence shows RPN+VGG outperforms Selective Search and highlights the importance of the RPN cls layer.  
- Limited discussion of limitations, e.g., how anchor scales/aspect ratios generalize to other datasets or extreme aspect ratios.  
- Insufficient comparative analysis with contemporaneous proposal methods like EdgeBoxes.  
- Lacks analysis of failure cases (handling of occlusion, extreme scales, common error modes).  
- Highly original and significant contribution that enabled real-time detection and influenced subsequent work (e.g., Mask R-CNN).  
- Recommendation: strong accept — technically sound, clear, original, and highly impactful; meets NIPS quality, clarity, originality, and significance criteria.
