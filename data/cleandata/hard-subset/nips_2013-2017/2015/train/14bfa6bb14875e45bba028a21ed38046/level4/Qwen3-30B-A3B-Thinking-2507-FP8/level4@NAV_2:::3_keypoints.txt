- Introduces RPNs that share convolutional features with the detection network to enable efficient, near-real-time object detection.  
- Proposes a two-stage system: RPNs generate region proposals on shared features, followed by Fast R-CNN for detection.  
- Shared convolutional computation yields ~5 fps on GPU while achieving state-of-the-art PASCAL VOC accuracy.  
- Concern 1: Discrepancy between Section 3.3 (single-iteration alternating optimization) and Section 3.4’s 4-step multi-iteration training — needs clarification.  
- Concern 2: Unclear how sliding-window step size on the top-layer feature map maps to the original image pixels given CNN downsampling (e.g., VGG-16).  
- Concern 3: Parameter h_a in equations (1) and (2) is not defined and must be explicitly specified.  
- Overall: Technically sound, well-motivated, important contribution with strong experimental results (73.2% mAP on VOC2007, 70.4% on VOC2012).
