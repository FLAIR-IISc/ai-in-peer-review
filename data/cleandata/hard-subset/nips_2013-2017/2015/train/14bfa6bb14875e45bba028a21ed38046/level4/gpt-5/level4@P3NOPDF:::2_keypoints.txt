- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to generate object proposals efficiently.  
- Integrates RPN and Fast R-CNN via an alternating training scheme, creating a unified detection pipeline.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012.  
- Substantially reduces proposal generation time by reusing backbone features, removing a major R-CNN bottleneck.  
- Strength: clear, impactful idea—feature sharing dramatically cuts runtime without losing accuracy.  
- Strength: strong empirical results showing favorable speed–accuracy trade-offs.  
- Strength: practical efficiency—proposal generation becomes extremely fast on shared conv features.  
- Strength: well-written paper with solid experimental validation and ablation studies.  
- Weakness: alternating training is heuristic, lacking convergence analysis or theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: RPN’s standalone proposal quality vs. strong hand-engineered methods is not fully established across IoU thresholds and proposal budgets.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to design choices (anchor scales/aspect ratios, NMS) appears heuristic and under-analyzed.  
- Weakness: potential coupling/error propagation between RPN and detector not deeply analyzed (how proposals evolve and affect AP).  
- Weakness: limited evidence of generalization and robustness beyond VOC (e.g., COCO, small/crowded objects).  
- Requested clarification: provide training curves/statistics to demonstrate stability and convergence of alternating training.  
- Requested clarification: quantify sensitivity to number and shapes of anchors and methods to tune anchors for new domains.  
- Requested clarification: compare RPN vs Selective Search/EdgeBoxes across IoU thresholds and proposal counts (100/300/1000).  
- Requested clarification: give runtime breakdown with/without feature sharing (GPU/CPU) and note whether NMS is a test-time bottleneck.  
- Suggestions: develop a principled end-to-end joint training or stronger empirical justification for alternating training.  
- Suggestions: expand proposal analysis (recall at multiple IoUs, AR, small-object recall, AP/AR vs number of proposals).  
- Suggestions: add ablations for anchor configs, NMS thresholds, sampling ratios, and provide default settings for new datasets.  
- Suggestions: include error analysis comparing pipelines with Selective Search vs RPN to show sources of improvement.  
- Suggestions: discuss memory/compute trade-offs when increasing resolution or using deeper backbones and their impact on runtime/accuracy.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, influential work that advances detection by coupling proposal generation with detection; recommended for acceptance despite concerns.
