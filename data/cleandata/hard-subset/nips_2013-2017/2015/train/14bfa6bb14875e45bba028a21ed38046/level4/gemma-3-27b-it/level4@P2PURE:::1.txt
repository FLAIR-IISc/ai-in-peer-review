The paper presents a clear goal and strong experimental results. However, the presentation could be improved for clarity, as several aspects are currently confusing. 

1. The paper states that the Region Proposal Network (RPN) is translation-invariant, but does not explain why this is the case for RPN while [19] lacks this property. It would be helpful to also discuss the relevance of Overfeat in this context.
2. Several hyperparameters appear to be set arbitrarily without supporting analysis. Specifically, the motivation for choosing n=3 and k=9 is unclear.
3. The training procedure appears complex and potentially unstable. It would be beneficial to understand the robustness of this procedure; have the authors evaluated the results of running the training multiple times? Furthermore, have they explored alternative training settings, and if so, why did they ultimately select the current configuration?

The authors propose a novel region proposal method that leverages shared convolutional neural network (CNN) features with object localization, resulting in a ~10x speedup compared to Fast R-CNN with a slight improvement in accuracy. Because the object localization CNN is trained assuming region proposals are provided, the authors introduce a multi-stage alternative training procedure to address this dependency.
