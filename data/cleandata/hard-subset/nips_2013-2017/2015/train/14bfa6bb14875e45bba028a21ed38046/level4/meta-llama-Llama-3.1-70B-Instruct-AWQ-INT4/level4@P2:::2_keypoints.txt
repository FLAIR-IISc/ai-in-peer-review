- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to cut computation for region proposals.  
- RPN is a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each spatial position and is integrated with Fast R-CNN.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 and achieves ~5 fps on a GPU.  
- Sharing convolutional features is well-motivated and effectively reduces proposal generation runtime.  
- Paper is well-written with thorough experimental evaluations, including ablation studies and comparisons.  
- Strength: novel region proposal method that complements Fast R-CNN.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluations and clear presentation of method components.  
- Weakness: no convergence proof for the alternating optimization used to train RPN and Fast R-CNN.  
- Weakness: RPN does not clearly outperform Selective Search when used independently, questioning standalone effectiveness.  
- Quality: technically sound and well-executed implementation and experiments.  
- Clarity: easy to follow, with helpful figures and tables.  
- Originality: presents a novel implementation of shared-feature proposals, though sharing features is not entirely new.  
- Significance: meaningful contribution to object detection with practical speed improvements for real-time use.  
- Argument for acceptance: new method achieves SOTA detection performance.  
- Argument for acceptance: substantially reduces computational cost for proposal generation.  
- Argument against acceptance: lack of rigorous convergence analysis for the training procedure.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited standalone advantage over established methods like Selective Search.
