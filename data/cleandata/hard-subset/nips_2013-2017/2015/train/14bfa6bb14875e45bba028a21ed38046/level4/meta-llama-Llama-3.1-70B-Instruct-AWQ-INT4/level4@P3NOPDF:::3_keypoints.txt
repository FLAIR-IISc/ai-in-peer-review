- Proposes a two-stage CNN-based object detector: a Region Proposal Network (RPN) using sliding windows on top-layer feature maps, followed by Fast R-CNN, with shared convolutional layers.  
- Claims near real-time performance (≈5 fps) by sharing conv layers and using GPU acceleration.  
- Concern: Optimization appears to use only one iteration of alternating training between RPN and Fast R-CNN, which likely won’t converge to optimal proposals.  
- Concern: Unclear image-level step size when sliding on top-layer feature maps—pooling reduces spatial resolution and may cause large strides and inaccurate bounding boxes.  
- Concern: Definition of h_a (lines 173–174) is missing, preventing understanding of the underlying math.  
- Positive: Method is a natural extension of CNN detectors and the shared convolution idea is innovative and improves efficiency; however, the above issues must be addressed.
