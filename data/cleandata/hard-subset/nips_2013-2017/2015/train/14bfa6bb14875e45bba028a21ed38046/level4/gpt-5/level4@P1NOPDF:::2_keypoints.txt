- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detector and uses two fully connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness at each spatial position.  
- Heuristically combines RPN with Fast R-CNN and achieves state-of-the-art detection on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012.  
- Sharing convolutional layers between proposal and detection stages substantially reduces proposal generation time.  
- Pros: clever feature sharing that reduces runtime, achieves SOTA detection performance, and is clearly written with thorough experiments.  
- Cons: RPN and Fast R-CNN training is only weakly coupled via alternating optimization, with no convergence proof and potential stability/reproducibility concerns.  
- Cons: As a standalone proposal method, RPN does not clearly outperform Selective Search.  
- Overall: strong, impactful, and practical contribution with significant end-to-end detection gains despite heuristic training and limited proposal-stage superiority.
