The paper presents a clear objective and demonstrates robust experimental results. However, the presentation could be improved for clarity, as several sections require further explanation. Specifically:

1. The rationale for the translation invariance of the proposed Region Proposal Network (RPN) – contrasted with the approach in [19] – remains unclear. A discussion of related work, such as Overfeat, would be beneficial here.
2. Several key parameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) are set without sufficient justification or analysis. The basis for these specific choices should be elaborated upon.
3. The training procedure appears complex and potentially unstable. Investigating the consistency of results through multiple training runs is recommended. Furthermore, detailing any parameter sweeps or alternative training configurations explored by the authors, and the reasoning behind selecting the current procedure, would strengthen the paper.

The authors introduce a novel region proposal method that leverages shared convolutional neural network (CNN) features with an object localization module. This method utilizes a classification layer to distinguish between object and non-object regions, alongside a regression layer for bounding box prediction. By sharing feature computation, the proposed Region-based CNN achieves approximately a 10x speedup compared to Fast R-CNN, with a slight improvement in accuracy. Recognizing that the object localization CNN is trained assuming pre-defined regions, the authors propose a multi-stage alternative training procedure to address this dependency.
