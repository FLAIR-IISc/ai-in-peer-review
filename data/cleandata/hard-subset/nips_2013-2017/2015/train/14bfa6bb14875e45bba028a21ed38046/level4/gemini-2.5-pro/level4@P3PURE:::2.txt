This paper presents a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with a Fast R-CNN detection network. At each position on the final convolutional feature map, the RPN utilizes two sibling fully-connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness scores. The RPN is combined with Fast R-CNN for object detection via an alternating training procedure, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 datasets. A key contribution is the significant reduction in the runtime for generating region proposals, which is made possible by sharing the computationally expensive convolutional layers with the detection network.

**Pros:**
- The concept of sharing convolutional layers between the region proposal and detection networks is a clever idea that substantially reduces the computational cost of object proposals.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on standard object detection benchmarks.
- The paper is well-written and provides a thorough experimental evaluation.

**Cons:**
- The training of the RPN and the Fast R-CNN network is combined via an alternating optimization procedure, which appears heuristic and lacks a convergence proof.
- When evaluated as a standalone region proposal method, the RPN does not seem to outperform established methods like Selective Search.

**Overall Comments:**
This is an excellent paper that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN-based object detection. The writing is clear, and the experimental evaluations are thorough. My primary concern is the method used to combine the RPN and Fast R-CNN for training. The proposed alternating optimization scheme is somewhat heuristic, and a more principled, jointly trained framework would strengthen the contribution.
