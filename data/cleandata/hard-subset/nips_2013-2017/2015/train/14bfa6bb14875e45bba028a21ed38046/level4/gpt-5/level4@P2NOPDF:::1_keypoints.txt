- Proposal: Shares convolutional features between region proposal network (RPN) and detector, giving ~10x speedup and slightly higher accuracy than Fast R-CNN.  
- Strength: Clear objective with strong empirical results demonstrating practical impact.  
- Strength: Sharing deep features for proposals and detection is a novel and compelling idea.  

- Concern: Translation-invariance claim unclear — need explanation of architectural/training differences vs. [19] and comparison to OverFeat.  
- Concern: Lack of theoretical or controlled empirical evidence (e.g., controlled translations or performance-vs-translation curves) supporting translation-invariance.  

- Concern: Several hyperparameters appear hand-tuned (e.g., n=3 scales, k=9 anchors) without sensitivity analysis.  
- Request: Provide ablations varying numbers of scales/aspect ratios/anchors and report accuracy vs. runtime trade-offs.  

- Concern: Alternating multi-stage training is complex and may be unstable; rationale for not using single end-to-end training is missing.  
- Request: Report how many alternation cycles are needed, whether performance saturates, run-to-run variance, and results of alternative schedules or optimizer settings.  
- Request: If joint training was attempted and abandoned, summarize results and failure modes.  

- Clarity: Exposition can be hard to follow; include a schematic separating proposal module and detection head with data flows and loss functions.  
- Clarity: Explicitly define all symbols and hyperparameters (e.g., n, k) and provide intuition when first introduced.  
- Clarity: Summarize training steps in an algorithm box, indicating when features are frozen vs. updated and how proposals/targets are regenerated.  
- Clarity: Provide a short discussion of computational cost and the exact sources of the 10x speedup, plus hardware details for fair comparison.  

- Experiments: Include per-class AP or error analysis to identify where sharing helps or hurts.  
- Experiments: Report sensitivity to IoU thresholds for positive/negative sample assignment in both proposal classifier and detector.  
- Experiments: Study NMS thresholds and their impact on recall and final AP, given interactions with anchor design and counts.  
- Experiments: Compare proposal recall and performance against OverFeat-style dense sliding windows to contextualize anchor and feature-sharing gains.  

- Overall: Contribution is significant and timely, but the paper needs deeper analysis of design choices, clearer justification of key claims, and a more transparent, reproducible training procedure.
