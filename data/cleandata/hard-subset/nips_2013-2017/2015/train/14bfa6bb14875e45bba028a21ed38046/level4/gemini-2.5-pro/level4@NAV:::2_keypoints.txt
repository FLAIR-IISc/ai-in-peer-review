- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to predict bounding boxes and objectness at each feature-map location.
- Integrates RPN and Fast R-CNN into a unified detector achieving state-of-the-art performance on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012.
- Sharing the convolutional backbone substantially reduces the computational cost of generating region proposals.
- Novel and effective strategy of sharing layers yields a significant runtime reduction.
- Method achieves leading benchmark performance; paper is clear and well-structured.
- Joint training uses an alternating optimization scheme that is heuristic and lacks a formal convergence proof.
- As a standalone proposal method, RPN does not outperform traditional techniques like Selective Search.
- Main reservation: a more principled joint training approach would strengthen the contribution.
