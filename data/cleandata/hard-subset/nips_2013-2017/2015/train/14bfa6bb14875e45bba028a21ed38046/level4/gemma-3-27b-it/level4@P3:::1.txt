## Review of Faster R-CNN: Towards Real-Time Object Detection with Region Proposal Networks

This paper presents Faster R-CNN, a novel object detection system that significantly improves speed by introducing a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detection network. This approach addresses a key bottleneck in previous methods – the time-consuming region proposal generation step – and achieves near real-time performance (5fps with VGG-16) while maintaining state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012. The work builds on recent advances in region-based CNNs like SPPnet [7] and Fast R-CNN [5], and represents a substantial contribution to the field of object detection.

The core idea of learning region proposals with a deep network, rather than relying on traditional methods like Selective Search [22] or EdgeBoxes [24], is a strong and well-motivated one. The authors effectively demonstrate that this algorithmic change, enabling shared computation, yields significant speedups without sacrificing accuracy. The experimental results are compelling, showcasing a clear improvement over existing approaches, particularly in terms of inference time. The code release is also a valuable asset for the community.

However, the paper suffers from some clarity issues and lacks detailed justification for certain design choices. Specifically:

* **Translation Invariance (Section 3.2):** The discussion of translation invariance feels somewhat muddled. While the explanation is provided, the connection to [19] and OverFeat isn’t clear.  A more direct comparison, explaining why the proposed approach *is* translation invariant while others might not be, would be helpful.  Is the claim about OverFeat accurate, and if so, why isn't it highlighted more explicitly as a difference?
* **Hyperparameter Choices (Section 3.2):** Several critical parameters (n=3, k=9) are set without sufficient justification.  While the authors may have performed ablation studies, these are not presented.  A brief discussion of the sensitivity of performance to these parameters, and the rationale behind the selected values, would strengthen the paper. Why these specific values perform best, or any analysis showing the impact of deviating from them, is missing.
* **Training Procedure (Section 3.4):** The alternating optimization training scheme is complex and requires careful tuning. The paper describes the procedure but doesn't provide insight into its robustness. Were other training schemes considered?  What sensitivity analysis was performed to ensure the convergence and stability of this alternating approach?  It’s unclear if the reported results are consistently reproducible, or if they rely on a carefully tuned configuration. The explanation feels somewhat hand-wavy, and a discussion of potential failure modes or stability issues would be beneficial.
* **Clarity of Anchor Explanation:** The explanation of anchor boxes and their scales/aspect ratios could be clearer. A visual representation alongside the text might help readers grasp the concept more easily.

**Strengths:**

* **Significant Speed Improvement:** The proposed RPN dramatically reduces the computational cost of region proposal generation, enabling near real-time object detection.
* **State-of-the-Art Accuracy:** The method achieves competitive or superior accuracy compared to existing approaches on standard benchmarks.
* **Novel Approach:** The idea of learning region proposals with a deep network is a significant contribution and opens up new avenues for research.
* **Code Release:**  The availability of code will facilitate further research and development in this area.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity:**  The paper contains sections that are difficult to follow and lack sufficient explanation.
* **Justification of Design Choices:**  Several critical parameters are set without clear justification.
* **Training Procedure Complexity:** The alternating optimization training scheme is complex and its robustness is not adequately addressed.
* **Limited Ablation Studies:** The paper lacks detailed ablation studies to support key design choices.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant bottleneck in object detection and proposes a novel and effective solution.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate a clear improvement over existing approaches.
* The method has the potential to be widely adopted and used by other researchers.
* The code release will facilitate further research and development.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The paper suffers from clarity issues and lacks detailed justification for certain design choices.
* The complexity of the training procedure and lack of robustness analysis are concerns.
* The limited ablation studies weaken the support for the proposed approach.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the weaknesses in clarity and justification, the technical contribution of this paper is substantial. The Faster R-CNN represents a significant advance in the field of object detection, and the reported results are impressive. I believe the paper is **accept** with minor revisions. The authors should address the clarity issues, provide more justification for their design choices, and discuss the robustness of their training procedure.  Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and make it more accessible to the research community.  I would particularly encourage the inclusion of an ablation study examining the sensitivity of performance to the parameters *n* and *k*.
