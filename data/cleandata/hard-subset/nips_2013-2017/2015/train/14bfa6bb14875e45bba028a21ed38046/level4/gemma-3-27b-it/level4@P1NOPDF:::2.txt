The paper introduces a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and incorporates two fully connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness scores for each position. This RPN is heuristically integrated with Fast R-CNN for object detection, achieving state-of-the-art performance on VOC07 and VOC12. A key benefit is the significantly reduced runtime for generating region proposals, stemming from the shared convolutional layers between the RPN and the detection network.

**Pros:**

*   The idea of sharing convolutional layers between the RPN and R-CNN is clever and substantially reduces the runtime for generating object proposals.
*   The approach achieves state-of-the-art performance in object detection.
*   The paper is well-written and clear.

**Cons:**

*   The training of the RPN and Fast R-CNN is loosely coupled through alternating optimization, and there is no formal convergence guarantee.
*   While the RPN can be used independently as a region proposal method, it does not appear to outperform Selective Search.

This is a strong contribution that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN based object detection. The paper is well-written and includes thorough experimental evaluations. My primary concern lies with the method used to combine the RPN and Fast R-CNN. The proposed alternating optimization approach feels somewhat heuristic.
