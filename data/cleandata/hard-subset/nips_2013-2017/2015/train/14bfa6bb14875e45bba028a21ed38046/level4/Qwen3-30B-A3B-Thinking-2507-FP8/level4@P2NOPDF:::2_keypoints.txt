- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN and uses two fully connected layers to predict bounding boxes and objectness at each spatial location.  
- Integrates RPN with Fast R-CNN to achieve state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 while greatly reducing proposal generation runtime via shared feature extraction.  
- Efficiency and integration: removes redundant computation between proposal and detection networks, drastically accelerating the pipeline versus prior R-CNN approaches.  
- Performance: achieves competitive detection accuracy on standard benchmarks without compromising precision.  
- Clarity and rigor: paper is well-structured, clearly written, and supported by comprehensive experiments and ablation studies.  
- Concern — training integration: uses alternating optimization for RPN and Fast R-CNN without theoretical justification or convergence guarantees, which may limit scalability.  
- Concern — standalone proposal utility: RPN does not outperform selective search as a standalone proposal method, implying its main benefit may be only when integrated with Fast R-CNN.  
- Overall: a practical, elegant advancement that balances speed and accuracy and is promising for real-world use, but would benefit from convergence analysis and stronger standalone proposal performance.
