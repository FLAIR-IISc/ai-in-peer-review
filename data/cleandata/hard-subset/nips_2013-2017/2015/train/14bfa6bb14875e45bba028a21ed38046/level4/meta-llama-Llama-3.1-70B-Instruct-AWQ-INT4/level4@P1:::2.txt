The paper presents a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and includes two fully connected layers that predict object bounds and objectness scores at each position. RPN is heuristically combined with Fast R-CNN for object detection and achieves state-of-the-art performance on VOC07 and VOC12. The running time of generating region proposals is greatly reduced since RPN shares the convolutional layers with the detection network.

The main idea of this paper is related to previous work at NIPS and elsewhere, such as SPPnet [7] and Fast R-CNN [5], which have reduced the running time of object detection networks by sharing convolutions across proposals. However, these methods still rely on external region proposal algorithms, which can be computationally expensive. The proposed RPN addresses this issue by integrating region proposal generation into the object detection network.

The paper is well-written, and the experimental evaluations are thorough. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of RPN in reducing the running time of object proposal generation while maintaining state-of-the-art performance on object detection.

One of the strengths of this paper is its smart idea of sharing convolutional layers between RPN and R-CNN, which greatly reduces the runtime of object proposals. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the effects of different components of RPN on object detection performance.

However, there are some concerns about the training of RPN and Fast R-CNN. The two networks are weakly combined by alternating optimization, and there is no convergence proof. This may lead to suboptimal performance or instability in certain scenarios.

Additionally, while RPN can be used independently as a region proposal method, its performance does not seem to outperform selective search. This raises questions about the effectiveness of RPN as a standalone region proposal method.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, namely, the integration of region proposal generation into the object detection network. However, the idea of using a deep network for region proposal generation is not entirely new, as seen in previous work such as OverFeat [18] and MultiBox [3, 20].

The significance of this paper lies in its ability to advance the state-of-the-art in object detection while reducing the computational cost of region proposal generation. The proposed RPN has the potential to be widely adopted in object detection applications, particularly those that require real-time processing.

In conclusion, this is a very nice piece of work, which improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN in object detection. The only concern is on how to combine RPN and Fast R-CNN for object detection. The proposed alternating optimization method is a bit heuristic.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* State-of-the-art performance on object detection
* Significant reduction in running time of object proposal generation
* Well-written paper and thorough experimental evaluations
* Novel combination of familiar techniques

Arguments con acceptance:

* Weak combination of RPN and Fast R-CNN training
* No convergence proof for the alternating optimization method
* RPN does not outperform selective search as a standalone region proposal method

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the concerns about the training of RPN and Fast R-CNN.
