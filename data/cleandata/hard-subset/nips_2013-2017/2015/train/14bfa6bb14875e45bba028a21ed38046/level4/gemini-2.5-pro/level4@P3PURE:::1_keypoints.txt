- Clear goal with solid experiments supporting it.  
- Proposes a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detection network (classification + bounding-box regression).  
- Sharing features yields ~10× speedup over Fast R-CNN while slightly improving accuracy.  
- Introduces a multi-stage, alternating training procedure to handle detector reliance on precomputed proposals.  
- Paper is hard to follow in places and several key design choices lack justification.  
- Claim that the RPN is translation-invariant (unlike [19]) needs clearer architectural explanation and comparison to sliding-window methods like Overfeat.  
- Hyperparameters (e.g., n=3 scales, k=9 aspect ratios) are set manually without ablation or sensitivity analysis.  
- Training procedure appears complex; stability and sensitivity to the multi-stage process (and comparison to alternative strategies) are not discussed.
