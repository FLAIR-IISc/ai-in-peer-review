The paper presents a clear goal and solid experimental results. However, there are several areas that hinder the clarity of the manuscript, as outlined below.

Firstly, the authors claim that their Region Proposal Network (RPN) is translation-invariant, whereas [19] is not. It is unclear why this is the case, and it would be beneficial to discuss how Overfeat compares in this regard.

Furthermore, several parameters are set manually without thorough analysis or justification. For instance, the choice of n=3 and k=9 is not explained. Providing a more detailed explanation or analysis of these parameters would strengthen the paper.

Additionally, the training procedure appears complex and unstable. It is unclear whether the authors have applied the same training procedure multiple times to verify the stability of the results. It would be helpful to know whether the authors have experimented with other settings and found the current one to perform the best.

The authors propose a novel region proposal method that shares the CNN features used for object localization. This method consists of a classification layer for distinguishing between objects and non-objects, and a regression layer for predicting object bounding boxes. By sharing feature computation with the object localization module, the combined new RCNN achieves a significant speedup of approximately 10 times compared to Fast RCNN, while also being slightly more accurate. To address the issue that the training procedure of the object localization CNN assumes regions are given, the authors propose a multi-stage alternative training procedure.
