This work presents a logical progression of existing CNN-based object detection methods. However, several aspects warrant further consideration:

1. The optimization section appears to employ a single iteration of alternating optimization between RPN and R-CNN. If this is the case, it implies that the proposals generated by the first iterated RPN are optimal, which seems unrealistic.

2. When applying the pixel-wise sliding window to the top layer of the CNN, it is unclear what the step size is on the image layer. Given that the CNN undergoes multiple rounds of max-pooling, the top level is significantly downscaled compared to the input image. Consequently, the sliding window on the top level may result in a substantial step on the image level, potentially leading to inaccurate bounding box locations.

3. The definition of $h_a$ in lines 173 and 174 is not explicitly stated.

The proposed two-stage object detection algorithm leverages a convolutional neural network (CNN). The first stage involves region proposal, which adapts the traditional sliding window method to operate on the top layer feature map of the CNN (RPN). The second stage applies a fast R-CNN to the proposed regions. By sharing convolution layers between RPN and R-CNN and utilizing GPU acceleration, the algorithm achieves near real-time performance (5fps).
