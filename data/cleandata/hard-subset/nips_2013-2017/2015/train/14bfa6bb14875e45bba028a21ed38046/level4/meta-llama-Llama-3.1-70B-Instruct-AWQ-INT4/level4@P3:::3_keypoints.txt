- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares full-image convolutional features with the detection network, enabling nearly cost-free proposals; Faster R-CNN achieves state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 at ~5 fps.

- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation; fully-convolutional RPN is a key contribution and evaluation is thorough (including recall-to-IoU analysis).

- Builds on Fast R-CNN by introducing shared-feature region proposals for faster and more accurate detection.

- Strength: novel RPN that shares convolutional features with the detection network.

- Strength: comprehensive evaluation and comparisons with existing proposal methods.

- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art detection accuracy while maintaining real-time-ish runtime (~5 fps).

- Concern: alternating optimization between RPN and R-CNN appears to use only one iteration, assuming initial proposals are optimal; multiple iterations should be explored.

- Concern: unclear step size/stride on the image layer when applying pixel-wise sliding window to top CNN layer; pooling reduces resolution and may cause coarse, inaccurate bounding boxes.

- Concern: definition of h_a (lines 173–174) is unclear and needs clarification.

- Argument for acceptance: novel method, strong empirical results, and thorough evaluation.

- Argument for rejection: questionable optimization assumption (single iteration), missing details on sliding-window stride, and unclear notation (h_a).

- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing concerns—explore multiple alternating-optimization iterations, clarify step size/stride and its impact, and clearly define h_a.
