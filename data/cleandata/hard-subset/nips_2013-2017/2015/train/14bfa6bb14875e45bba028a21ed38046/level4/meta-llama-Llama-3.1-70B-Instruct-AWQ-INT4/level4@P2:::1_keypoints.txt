- Proposes Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share CNN features with object localization, combining a classification layer (object vs. non-object) and a regression layer (bounding boxes).  
- Sharing feature computation yields ~10x speedup over Fast R-CNN with slightly better accuracy.  
- Paper is well-motivated and presents solid experimental results on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012.  
- Clarify why RPN is translation-invariant while the method in [19] is not, and include a comparison to OverFeat on this property.  
- Several hyperparameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) are set manually without sufficient justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Training procedure appears complicated and potentially unstable; authors should demonstrate robustness by repeating training and reporting variability.  
- Unclear whether other training/settings were tried and why the current one was chosen as best.  
- Related work discussion covers Selective Search and EdgeBoxes adequately but needs a deeper comparison with OverFeat.  
- Recommend ablation studies to quantify contributions of classification vs. regression layers and other components.  
- Recommend a more detailed discussion and analysis of hyperparameter choices and their impact.  
- Suggest comparing against state-of-the-art detectors, including methods that do not use region proposal networks.  
- Strengths: novel shared-feature RPN design, significant speedup, competitive accuracy, and thorough placement within existing literature.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient analysis of components/hyperparameters, training complexity/robustness concerns, and incomplete comparison with OverFeat and other SOTA methods.
