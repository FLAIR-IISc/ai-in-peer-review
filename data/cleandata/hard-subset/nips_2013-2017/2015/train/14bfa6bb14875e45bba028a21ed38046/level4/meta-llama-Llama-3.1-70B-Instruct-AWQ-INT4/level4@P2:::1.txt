The paper proposes a novel region proposal method, called Region Proposal Networks (RPNs), which shares the convolutional neural network (CNN) features used for object localization. The RPN consists of a classification layer for recognizing objects versus non-objects and a regression layer for predicting object bounding boxes. By sharing feature computation with the object localization module, the combined new R-CNN is approximately 10 times faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate.

The paper is well-motivated, and the experimental results are solid. However, there are a few areas that require clarification to improve the paper's readability. Firstly, the authors should elaborate on why RPN is translation-invariant, whereas the method proposed in [19] is not. Additionally, a comparison with OverFeat in this regard would be beneficial.

Furthermore, several parameters, such as n=3 and k=9, are set manually without thorough analysis. It would be helpful to provide a more detailed explanation for these choices or explore their impact on the results.

The training procedure appears complicated and not entirely stable. The authors may want to consider applying the same training procedure multiple times to demonstrate its robustness. It is also unclear whether the authors have tried other settings and found the current one to perform the best.

In terms of related work, the authors have adequately discussed the connections with previous region proposal methods, such as Selective Search and EdgeBoxes. However, a more in-depth comparison with OverFeat would be beneficial, as both methods utilize deep networks for object localization.

The paper's strengths include its ability to share feature computation between the region proposal and object localization modules, resulting in a significant speedup. The experimental results on the PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

To improve the paper, the authors could provide more analysis on the following aspects:

* Ablation studies to evaluate the impact of individual components, such as the classification and regression layers, on the overall performance.
* A more detailed discussion on the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the results.
* A comparison with state-of-the-art object detection methods, including those that do not use region proposal networks.

Overall, the paper presents a promising approach to region proposal generation and object detection. With some clarifications and additional analysis, it has the potential to become a strong contribution to the field.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective region proposal method that shares feature computation with object localization.
* The experimental results demonstrate a significant speedup and competitive accuracy compared to state-of-the-art methods.
* The paper provides a thorough discussion of related work and adequately situates the proposed method within the context of existing research.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from more analysis and explanation of the proposed method's components and hyperparameters.
* The training procedure appears complicated and may require additional clarification or justification.
* The comparison with OverFeat could be more thorough, and additional comparisons with state-of-the-art object detection methods would strengthen the paper.
