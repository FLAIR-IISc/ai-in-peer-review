- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with detection networks to make region proposal computation nearly cost-free.  
- Builds on Fast R-CNN and targets region proposal as the remaining detection bottleneck.  
- Previous proposal methods (Selective Search, EdgeBoxes) are much slower; RPNs run ≈10ms/image on GPU, enabling a full system at ~5fps with VGG-16.  
- Strong technical quality with thorough experimental validation on PASCAL VOC.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results: 73.2% mAP (VOC2007) and 70.4% mAP (VOC2012) while improving speed.  
- Novel and significant contribution: sharing convolutional features between proposal and detection within a single network.  
- Well-motivated approach with clear advantages and convincing empirical evidence.  
- Strengths: substantial computational reduction, strong empirical performance, clear demonstration of shared features, well-organized presentation.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation for why RPN is translation-invariant compared to OverFeat despite similar use of convolutional features.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter choices (n=3 sliding window, k=9 anchors) lack justification and sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: training procedure (four-step alternating optimization) described as simple but lacks detail on stability, robustness, and sensitivity to initialization/hyperparameters.  
- High significance: enables real-time object detection with state-of-the-art accuracy and effectively integrates proposal and detection.  
- Overall: strong paper with minor issues—clarifications on design choices and training would make it an excellent contribution.  
- Pro: novel feature-sharing, strong empirical results, real-time high accuracy; Con: missing technical explanations, hyperparameter rationale, and training-detail clarity.
