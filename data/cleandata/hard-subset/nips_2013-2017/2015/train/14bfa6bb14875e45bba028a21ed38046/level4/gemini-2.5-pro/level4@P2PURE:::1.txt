This paper proposes a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the main object detection network. By combining a classifier for objectness and a regressor for bounding boxes, the RPN allows the region proposal stage to be nearly cost-free. Consequently, the proposed detector is approximately 10x faster than Fast R-CNN and achieves slightly higher accuracy. The paper's goal is well-defined, and the experimental results are strong.

However, the presentation is not always easy to follow, and several aspects of the methodology could be clarified:

1.  The paper claims that the RPN is translation-invariant, in contrast to methods like Selective Search [19]. This claim requires further justification. A more detailed explanation of why RPN possesses this property, and a comparison with other methods like OverFeat, would be beneficial.

2.  Several key hyperparameters are introduced without sufficient analysis or justification. For instance, the choice of 3 scales (n=3) and 9 aspect ratios (k=9) for the anchors seems arbitrary. An ablation study analyzing the system's sensitivity to these parameters would significantly strengthen the paper.

3.  The proposed multi-stage training procedure appears complex and its stability is not discussed. It is unclear if this specific procedure was chosen after exploring other alternatives. Have the authors experimented with different training schemes? Demonstrating the robustness of the method by reporting results from multiple training runs would also make the findings more convincing.
