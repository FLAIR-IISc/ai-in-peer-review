Summary & relation to prior work: Introduces fully-convolutional Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) with translation-invariant anchors that share conv features with Fast R-CNN, yielding state-of-the-art VOC accuracy with few proposals and near real-time speed.  
Builds on SPPnet/Fast R-CNN and contrasts with OverFeat/MultiBox by using parameter-efficient, translation-invariant anchors and feature sharing.

Strength — Idea: Sharing convolutional features between proposal generation and detection makes proposals nearly free at test time.  
Strength — Performance: RPN+Fast R-CNN achieves state-of-the-art mAP on VOC07/12 with as few as 300 proposals; thorough ablations support claims.  
Strength — Practicality: Fast wall-clock runtime, reduced dependence on external CPU proposals, and available code for reproducibility.  
Strength — Clarity: Well-written, well-organized paper with clear architecture, loss, and implementation details.

Concern — Training integration: Alternating optimization for joint RPN and detector is heuristic with no convergence proof or stability analysis.  
Concern — Standalone proposal quality: RPN does not consistently outperform Selective Search in all configurations (e.g., with ZF), implying gains partly come from feature sharing.

Quality: Technically sound design (cls + smooth L1 regression, anchors, sampling, schedule) with comprehensive experiments; main caveat is lack of alternating-training analysis.  
Clarity: Exposition is clear, with sufficient implementation details for replication.  
Originality: Novel fully-convolutional, anchor-based proposal mechanism that shares features with detectors, offering runtime and parameter advantages.  
Significance: Advances accuracy and speed of region-based detectors and is likely to be widely adopted.

Suggestions for improvement: Provide analysis or evidence on stability/convergence of alternating training or explore end-to-end joint optimization.  
Suggestions for improvement: Clarify settings where RPN outperforms or matches Selective Search across IoU thresholds and proposal counts.  
Suggestions for improvement: Discuss sensitivity to anchor choices (scales/aspect ratios) and IoU thresholds for labeling.

Arguments for acceptance: Strong conceptual contribution (unified, efficient proposal+detection), thorough experiments, clear improvements in accuracy/speed, and practical impact with released code.  
Arguments against acceptance: Reliance on heuristic alternating optimization without theoretical guarantees; RPN not consistently superior to Selective Search as a standalone proposer.

Overall assessment: Very strong paper that meaningfully improves efficiency and accuracy of region-based detectors; heuristic training and mixed standalone comparisons are minor caveats but empirical gains and clarity make it a compelling contribution.
