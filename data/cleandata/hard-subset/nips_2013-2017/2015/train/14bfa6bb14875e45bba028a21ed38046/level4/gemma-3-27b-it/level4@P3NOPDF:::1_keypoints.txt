- Core contribution: integrates Region Proposal Network (RPN) into the CNN pipeline, sharing convolutional features and using classification for objectness plus regression for bounding-box refinement.  
- Empirical result: reports ~10x speedup over Fast R-CNN with a slight accuracy improvement, validated on standard datasets.  
- Motivation: addresses a major bottleneck in object detection by speeding up proposal generation.  
- Presentation: paper lacks clarity in several areas, making some design choices hard to understand.  
- Translation-invariance claim: insufficient explanation for why the RPN is translation-invariant and why the referenced method [19] is not.  
- Overfeat comparison missing: prior shared-feature work (Overfeat) is not adequately discussed or contrasted with the proposed method.  
- Hyperparameters (n=3, k=9) appear arbitrary: no ablation study or rationale provided to justify these values or show sensitivity.  
- Training procedure complexity: multi-stage alternating optimization is complex, under-described, and raises reproducibility concerns.  
- Training stability: unclear whether results are consistent across multiple runs or if alternative training strategies were tried and compared.  
- Novelty vs. elegance: main novelty is feature sharing for proposals; the multi-stage training feels ad hoc and detracts from the core idea.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying translation-invariance, justifying hyperparameters, and detailing/troubleshooting the training procedure.
