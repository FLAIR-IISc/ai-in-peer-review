- Proposal: Two-stage detector combining an RPN sliding over top-layer feature maps with Fast R-CNN, sharing convolutional backbone; reported ~5 fps on GPU.
- Strength: Clear, practical integration of proposal generation and detection within a shared CNN backbone.
- Strength: Conceptually simple extension that removes the need for expensive external proposal methods.
- Strength: Promising speed/accuracy trade-offs with near–real-time performance potential.
- Concern: Optimization/training unclear—paper seems to describe only a single alternation between RPN and detector, which may be a strong assumption.
- Request: Clarify whether multiple rounds of alternating training are used, how many, and the convergence criterion.
- Request: If only one alternation, provide empirical evidence (ablation) showing sufficiency for mAP.
- Request: State whether backbone convolutional layers are shared and updated jointly or frozen during head training, and how gradients are combined for joint training.
- Concern: Sliding-window stride vs. input image not specified; coarse feature-map stride (e.g., 16/32) may harm localization.
- Request: Specify exact feature-map stride relative to the input and the sliding step on the feature map.
- Request: Explain how localization error from coarse stride is mitigated (anchors, multi-scale features, dilated convs, finer-resolution features).
- Request: Provide localization metrics at stricter IoUs (e.g., AP@0.75 or COCO [0.5:0.95]) and ablations vs. stride/feature level.
- Concern: Notation ambiguity—h_a is introduced without definition.
- Request: Define all symbols at first use and give full bounding-box regression equations linking tx,ty,tw,th to anchor (x_a,y_a,w_a,h_a) and ground truth.
- Request: Provide runtime breakdown per stage (RPN forward, proposal generation/NMS, ROI pooling, classification/regression) to justify the 5 fps claim.
- Request: Report RPN proposal quality: recall vs. IoU and number of proposals, effect of NMS, and proposal precision–recall curves.
- Request: Show detector sensitivity to number of proposals (mAP vs. proposals per image) to demonstrate efficiency/accuracy trade-offs.
- Request: Specify anchor scales and aspect ratios, how they were selected, and include an ablation on these choices.
- Request: Clarify sampling and losses: positive/negative sampling strategies for RPN and detector, class-agnostic vs. class-specific bbox regression, and loss weighting when jointly trained.
- Request: Include strong baselines using external proposals (Selective Search, EdgeBoxes) with matched backbones/training to isolate benefits of learned proposals and shared features.
- Overall assessment: Idea is timely, impactful, and computationally advantageous, but key training, stride, and implementation details are underspecified; acceptance recommended conditional on clarifications and modest ablations.
