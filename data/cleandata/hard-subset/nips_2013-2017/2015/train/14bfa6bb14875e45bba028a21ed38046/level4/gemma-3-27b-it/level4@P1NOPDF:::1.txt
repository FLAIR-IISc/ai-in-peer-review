The paper presents a clear goal and strong experimental results. However, the paper's flow could be improved; several sections are confusing, as detailed below.

1. The paper states that the Region Proposal Network (RPN) is translation-invariant, but [19] is not. This distinction isn’t fully explained. What about OverFeat? Further clarification here would be beneficial.

2. Numerous hyperparameters are set without sufficient justification or analysis. For example, why were n=3 and k=9 chosen? A discussion of the sensitivity to these values, or a rationale for their selection, is missing.

3. The training procedure appears complex and potentially unstable. Why wasn’t the training procedure repeated multiple times to assess its robustness? Have the authors explored a wider range of training settings, and if so, why did they ultimately select the current configuration?

The authors propose a novel region proposal method that shares convolutional neural network (CNN) features with the object localization module. This region proposal method employs a classification layer to distinguish between object and non-object regions, and a regression layer to predict object bounding boxes. By sharing feature computation, the resulting R-CNN implementation is approximately 10x faster than Fast R-CNN and achieves a slight improvement in accuracy. Because the training of the object localization CNN relies on pre-defined region proposals, a multi-stage alternating training procedure is proposed to address this dependency.
