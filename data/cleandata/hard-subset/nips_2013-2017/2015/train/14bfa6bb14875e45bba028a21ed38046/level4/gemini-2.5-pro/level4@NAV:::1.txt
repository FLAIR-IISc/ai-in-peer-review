The paper's objective is well-defined and supported by strong experimental results. However, the manuscript's clarity could be improved, as several sections are challenging to comprehend. The primary concerns are as follows:

1.  Could the authors elaborate on the claim that the RPN is translation-invariant? A detailed explanation of why this property holds for the proposed method but not for [19] would be beneficial. A comparison to Overfeat in this context would also be helpful.
2.  Several hyperparameters appear to be set without sufficient analysis or justification. For example, the rationale behind choosing values such as n=3 and k=9 should be provided.
3.  The training process is described as a complex, multi-stage procedure, which raises concerns about its stability. Was the training run multiple times to verify the consistency of the results? Furthermore, were other training configurations explored to determine if the current approach is indeed optimal?

The paper introduces a novel region proposal network that shares convolutional features with the downstream object detection network. This proposal network comprises a classification layer to distinguish between object and background, alongside a regression layer to refine bounding box coordinates. This feature-sharing architecture allows the integrated detector to be approximately 10x faster than Fast R-CNN while also achieving a slight increase in accuracy. To manage the circular dependency wherein the detector network's training requires pre-computed regions, the authors present an alternating, multi-stage training strategy.
