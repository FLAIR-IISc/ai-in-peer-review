- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with a downstream detection network.  
- RPN efficiently predicts rectangular object proposals with associated "objectness" scores directly from feature maps.  
- Integrates RPN with Fast R-CNN to form a unified object detection model.  
- Significantly reduces computational cost of proposal generation, removing the Selective Search bottleneck.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 benchmarks.  
- Sharing convolutional layers between proposal and detection networks is an elegant, impactful innovation that boosts speed.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation convincingly demonstrates the method’s effectiveness.  
- The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.  
- The alternating optimization training procedure is somewhat ad-hoc and lacks theoretical justification or convergence guarantees.  
- As a standalone proposal method, RPN does not always outperform Selective Search in proposal quality, suggesting gains stem from tight integration and shared features.  
- Overall: an excellent, influential contribution with the main concern being the heuristic training scheme, but strengths outweigh weaknesses.
