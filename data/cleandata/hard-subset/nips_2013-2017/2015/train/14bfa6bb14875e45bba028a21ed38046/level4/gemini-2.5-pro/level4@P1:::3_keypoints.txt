- Main contribution: Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional layers with a Fast R-CNN detector, unifying proposal and detection to avoid redundant feature extraction.  
- Result: Faster R-CNN achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC while running near real-time (≈5 FPS with VGG-16).  
- Novelty/significance: Elegant unification of proposal and detection stages replaces slow external proposal methods with a learnable, end-to-end trainable module.  
- Performance/efficiency: Substantially improves detection speed by eliminating external proposal bottlenecks without sacrificing—and slightly improving—mAP.  
- Architecture: Simple, powerful fully-convolutional RPN that uses multi-scale/aspect "anchors" to predict proposals in a translation-invariant, efficient manner.  
- Experiments: Comprehensive evaluation and ablation studies convincingly demonstrate the benefits of layer sharing and the RPN’s classification/regression components; compares favorably to one-stage systems.  
- Clarification 1 (optimization): The 4-step "alternating optimization" is described as a single sequential fine-tuning cycle; unclear whether iterating this process was tried and if further mutual improvement is possible.  
- Clarification 2 (localization): Concern about localization accuracy when RPN operates on coarse final feature maps (large stride like 16 px), especially for small objects; unclear if higher-resolution feature maps were considered.  
- Clarification 3 (notation): The bounding-box parameterization uses h_a without explicit definition (should be defined as anchor height) for clarity.
