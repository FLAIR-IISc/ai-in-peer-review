- Proposes an RPN that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN and uses sibling layers to predict object bounds and objectness at each feature-map position.
- Combines RPN and Fast R-CNN via an alternating training procedure.
- Achieves state-of-the-art detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012.
- Major contribution: substantially reduced runtime for region proposals by sharing expensive convolutional computation.
- Pro: Sharing convolutional layers is a clever way to cut computational cost for proposals.
- Pro: Thorough experimental evaluation demonstrating strong empirical results.
- Pro: Paper is well-written and clear.
- Con: Alternating optimization for joint training is heuristic and lacks a convergence proof.
- Con: As a standalone region proposal method, the RPN does not outperform established methods like Selective Search.
- Overall: Excellent improvement in efficiency and accuracy for R-CNN–based detection, with the main weakness being the ad hoc training scheme.
