- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional layers with Fast R-CNN to replace slow external proposal methods.  
- Designs a fully-convolutional sliding-window that predicts proposals relative to multi-scale, multi-aspect-ratio anchor boxes.  
- Trains the system with a multi-task loss combining objectness classification and bounding-box regression.  
- Uses a pragmatic 4-step alternating training scheme to enable feature sharing between the RPN and the detector.  
- Significant and original: unifies region proposal and detection into a single deep framework, replacing slow external algorithms.  
- Performance and efficiency: achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC and reduces proposal time to ~10ms, enabling near real-time detection (≈5fps with VGG-16).  
- Evaluation quality: extensive experiments and ablation studies strongly support claims and validate design choices (e.g., separate classification/regression outputs).  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and the anchor mechanism and core ideas are clearly presented.  
- Training heuristics concern: the 4-step alternating optimization appears ad-hoc and it’s unclear if it’s iterated or whether final features were tuned on proposals not produced by the final RPN.  
- Feature map stride issue: operating on a coarse final feature map (e.g., stride 16) may limit precise localization, especially for small objects—discussion is limited.  
- Minor reproducibility clarifications: some notational/details (e.g., explicit definitions of anchor width/height w_a, h_a) are omitted and should be stated.
