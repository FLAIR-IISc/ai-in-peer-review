Review—This paper is a natural extension of existing CNN-based object detection. My concerns are as follows.
1. In the optimization section, it seems there is only a single pass of alternating optimization between the RPN and the R-CNN. If so, this effectively assumes the proposals from the first RPN pass are optimal, which is clearly not the case.
2. When a pixel-wise sliding window is applied to the top CNN layer, what is the effective step size on the input image? Due to multiple max-pooling operations, the top-layer feature map is much lower resolution than the input, so a unit step on the feature map corresponds to a large stride on the image, potentially leading to imprecise bounding box localization.
3. What is the definition of $h_a$ in lines 173 and 174?

This work introduces a two-stage CNN-based object detector. The first stage generates region proposals using a traditional sliding-window approach applied to the top-layer CNN feature map (RPN). The second stage applies a Fast R-CNN to these proposals. Because the convolutional layers are shared between the RPN and the R-CNN and computation is accelerated on a GPU, the method achieves near real-time performance (5 fps).
