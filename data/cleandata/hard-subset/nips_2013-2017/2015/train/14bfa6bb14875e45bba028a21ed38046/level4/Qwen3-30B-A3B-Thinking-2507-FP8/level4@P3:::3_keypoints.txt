- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) to remove Selective Search bottleneck via a fully convolutional, feature-sharing proposal module.
- Shares convolutional features between RPN and detection network, enabling nearly cost-free region proposals.
- Trains RPN end-to-end with the detection network using a 4-step alternating optimization to produce a unified system.
- Employs translation-invariant anchor design (9 anchors per location: 3 scales × 3 aspect ratios) instead of k-means anchors.
- Provides a clear theoretical foundation for bounding-box regression, differing from prior feature-map-based methods.
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy (73.2% mAP on VOC2007, 70.4% on VOC2012) at near real-time 5fps using VGG-16.
- Thorough experimental validation and ablation studies isolate contributions of RPN classification vs. regression, proposal quality, and shared features.
- Clear, well-organized presentation with precise math and illustrative figures explaining RPN architecture and anchors.
- Implementation details and released code support reproducibility and community use.
- Strong originality: novel fully convolutional proposal network that meaningfully advances prior detection methods.
- High significance: addresses a critical detection bottleneck, enabling practical real-time applications and broader vision tasks.
- Addresses reviewer concerns: explains 4-step optimization, anchor coverage/receptive field, and definition of anchor height h_a.
- Minor weaknesses: limited discussion of potential limitations (e.g., very small objects) and evaluation primarily on PASCAL VOC.
- Recommendation: accept—technically sound, impactful, and well-presented contribution.
