- Paper proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with a detector, using anchors with dense object/background classification and bbox regression, yielding ~10× speedup and improved accuracy.
- Strength: Clear goal with strong practical impact—unifying proposal generation and detection speeds up inference without losing accuracy.
- Strength: Conceptually simple and deployable—a lightweight sliding-window proposal mechanism on shared features.
- Strength: Solid empirical results showing state-of-the-art accuracy and substantial runtime gains.
- Strength: Broad relevance—addresses a core detection pipeline bottleneck and is likely widely adoptable.

- Concern: “Translation-invariant” claim is undefined; the paper should precisely define the term (e.g., invariance to integer-pixel input shifts) and justify its usage.
- Concern: Need explanation of why the RPN is translation-invariant and which factors (stride, padding, boundary effects, anchor quantization, NMS) might break strict invariance/equivariance.
- Concern: Provide comparison to OverFeat and [19] regarding translation-equivariance and include empirical tests demonstrating consistency under controlled input translations.

- Concern: Many hyperparameters (anchor scales/aspect ratios, IoU thresholds, NMS threshold, number of proposals, minibatch ratio) are set without ablation or sensitivity analysis.
- Request: Include ablations varying number of scales/aspect ratios, feature stride effects on small objects, number of proposals needed (e.g., 100/300/1k), and sensitivity to NMS/IoU thresholds; describe validation tuning protocol.

- Concern: Alternating multi-stage training is complex—justify why alternations stop where they do and show whether performance saturates (plot mAP across alternations).
- Concern: Report training stability and variance (sensitivity to initialization/minibatch ordering), e.g., mean ± std over multiple runs.
- Request: If attempted, summarize end-to-end joint training results and stability; if not attempted, explain practical barriers and any approximate joint-training experiments.

- Concern: Empirical reporting lacks detail—provide per-image runtime breakdown for RPN, detection, NMS, and data transfer with hardware specs, and clarify whether the reported 10× is measured fairly.
- Request: Add proposal recall vs. IoU curves (0.5–0.9), average recall, and comparisons to strong hand-crafted proposal methods and [19] across proposal counts.
- Request: Assess generalization across datasets and backbones (e.g., ZF vs. VGG) and discuss how anchors should adapt to different datasets/resolutions.

- Minor: Clarify terminology by calling the RPN classifier “objectness” to avoid confusion with multi-class classification.
- Minor: Provide concise pseudocode/algorithmic summary of the training schedule including losses, sampling ratios, and IoU thresholds for reproducibility.
- Minor: Unify and define notation for n (scales) and k (anchors/location) and explicitly list scale values and aspect ratios in the main text or appendix.
- Minor: Discuss failure modes (small/thin objects vs. feature stride, crowded scenes and NMS suppression).
- Minor: Fix typos and improve figure legends so the pipeline is self-contained.

- Overall: Strong, influential contribution with clear speed and accuracy benefits; recommend acceptance contingent on clarifying the translation-invariance claim, providing anchor/hyperparameter ablations, and detailing training stability and empirical reporting.
