The paper's goal is clear, and it is supported by solid experimental results. The authors propose a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the object detection network. This RPN consists of a classification layer to distinguish object from background and a regression layer to predict object bounding boxes. By sharing the feature computation with the detection network, the resulting system is approximately 10x faster than Fast R-CNN while achieving slightly higher accuracy. To address the fact that the detector's training requires pre-computed region proposals, the authors introduce a multi-stage, alternating training procedure.

However, the paper is difficult to follow in some areas, and several key design choices lack justification:

1.  The paper claims the RPN is translation-invariant, while the method in [19] is not. This central claim requires further explanation. Could the authors elaborate on the specific architectural properties that enable translation invariance in the RPN and provide a more detailed comparison with other sliding-window methods like Overfeat?

2.  Many hyperparameters are set manually without sufficient analysis. For example, the paper does not justify the choice of `n=3` scales and `k=9` aspect ratios for the anchors. An ablation study or sensitivity analysis regarding these parameters would significantly strengthen the paper's claims.

3.  The proposed training procedure appears complex and its stability is not discussed. Is the model's final performance sensitive to this specific multi-stage process? The authors should clarify whether they explored other training strategies and found the current one to be optimal.
