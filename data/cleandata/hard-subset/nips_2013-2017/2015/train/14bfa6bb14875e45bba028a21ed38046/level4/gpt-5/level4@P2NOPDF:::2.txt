The paper introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detector and employs two fully connected layers to predict bounding boxes and objectness scores at each spatial position. The RPN is integrated with Fast R-CNN in a single pipeline and achieves state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012. Because the RPN reuses the backbone features of the detection network, the cost of generating proposals is greatly reduced.

Pros:
- Clever feature sharing between the RPN and Fast R-CNN substantially reduces the runtime for proposal generation.
- Strong, state-of-the-art object detection performance.
- Clear writing and thorough experimental evaluation.

Cons:
- The integration of RPN and Fast R-CNN relies on alternating optimization without convergence guarantees; the training procedure feels somewhat ad hoc.
- Used as an independent proposal generator, the RPN does not appear to surpass Selective Search.

Overall, this is an excellent piece of work that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN-based object detection. The paper is well written and the experiments are comprehensive. The main concern is the method for combining the RPN with Fast R-CNN: the proposed alternating training strategy is heuristic, and a more principled joint optimization or an analysis of convergence would strengthen the contribution.
