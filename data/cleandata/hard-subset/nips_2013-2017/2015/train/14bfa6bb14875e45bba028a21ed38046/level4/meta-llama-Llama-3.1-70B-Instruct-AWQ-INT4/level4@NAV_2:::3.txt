This work proposes a two-stage object detection algorithm based on convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The first stage is region proposal, which is based on the traditional sliding window method but working on the top layer feature map of a CNN, referred to as a Region Proposal Network (RPN). In the second stage, a Fast R-CNN is applied to the proposed regions. Since the convolutional layers are shared between the RPN and Fast R-CNN, and the calculation is sped up using a GPU, the algorithm can achieve near real-time performance (5fps).

The authors have made significant contributions to the field of object detection, building upon previous work such as SPPnet and Fast R-CNN. The introduction of RPNs is a novel approach to generating region proposals, and the results demonstrate a substantial improvement in speed and accuracy. The use of a shared convolutional layer between the RPN and Fast R-CNN is a key factor in achieving this performance.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology and results. The authors have also provided a thorough analysis of the performance of their algorithm, including comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

However, I do have some concerns regarding the optimization process. Specifically, it appears that there is only one iteration of the alternating optimization between the RPN and Fast R-CNN. This assumes that the proposals generated by the first iterated RPN are optimal, which is not necessarily true. It would be beneficial to see the results of multiple iterations of this process to determine if the performance can be further improved.

Additionally, when applying the pixel-wise sliding window to the top layer of the CNN, the step size on the image layer is not clearly defined. Since the CNN has several rounds of max-pooling, the top layer is in a much smaller scale compared to the input image. The sliding window on the top layer may have a large step on the image level, causing inaccurate bounding box locations.

Furthermore, the definition of $h_a$ in lines 173 and 174 is not clearly explained. It would be helpful to provide a clearer explanation of this variable and its role in the algorithm.

Overall, this work is a significant contribution to the field of object detection, and the results demonstrate a substantial improvement in speed and accuracy. However, addressing the concerns mentioned above would further strengthen the paper.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to generating region proposals using RPNs
* Significant improvement in speed and accuracy
* Thorough analysis of performance
* Well-written and clear explanation of methodology and results

Weaknesses:

* Optimization process may not be fully converged
* Step size on image layer not clearly defined
* Definition of $h_a$ not clearly explained

Arguments for acceptance:

* Significant contribution to the field of object detection
* Novel approach to generating region proposals
* Thorough analysis of performance

Arguments for rejection:

* Optimization process may not be fully converged
* Step size on image layer not clearly defined
* Definition of $h_a$ not clearly explained

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the concerns mentioned above.
