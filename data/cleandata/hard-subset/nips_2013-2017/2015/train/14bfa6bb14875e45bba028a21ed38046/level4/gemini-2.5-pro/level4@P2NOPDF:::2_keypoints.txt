- Introduces the Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares deep convolutional feature maps with a downstream detector (e.g., Fast R-CNN) to generate object proposals efficiently.  
- RPN uses a small sibling network to predict objectness scores and bounding-box regressions at multiple scales and aspect ratios per spatial location.  
- Integration of RPN with Fast R-CNN yields a unified, end-to-end trainable detection system with state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 and much faster inference.  
- Core contribution: sharing convolutional layers between proposal and detection networks elegantly unifies two stages and drastically reduces proposal computation time.  
- Empirical results are strong and thorough, demonstrating improvements in both speed and accuracy on standard benchmarks.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and presents the architecture in a structured, understandable way.  
- Weakness: training relies on an alternating optimization scheme that is heuristic, lacks formal convergence proof, and would benefit from comments on stability or more principled joint training.  
- Weakness: as a standalone proposal generator, RPN’s recall does not consistently outperform methods like Selective Search across all operating points, suggesting its main advantage is tight integration with the detector.  
- Overall: a strong, impactful contribution that addresses a key efficiency bottleneck in two-stage detectors; the heuristic training is a minor concern given the outstanding empirical results.
