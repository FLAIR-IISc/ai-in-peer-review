The paper introduces a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and employs two fully connected layers to predict bounding boxes and objectness scores at each spatial location. The RPN is heuristically integrated with Fast R-CNN for detection and attains state-of-the-art results on VOC07 and VOC12. Proposal generation is significantly faster because the RPN reuses the detector’s convolutional layers.

Pros:
- Clever sharing of convolutional layers between the RPN and R-CNN, which greatly accelerates proposal generation.
- State-of-the-art performance on object detection.
- Clear, well-structured writing.

Cons:
- The linkage between the RPN and Fast R-CNN relies on alternating optimization without a convergence guarantee.
- As an independent proposal method, the RPN does not appear to outperform Selective Search.

This is an excellent piece of work that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN-based object detection. The paper is well written and supported by comprehensive experiments. The primary concern is the integration of RPN with Fast R-CNN; the proposed alternating optimization approach feels somewhat heuristic.
