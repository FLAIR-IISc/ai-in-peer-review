- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with the detection network, removing the traditional proposal bottleneck and achieving 73.2% mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007 at 5 fps with VGG-16.
- RPN is a fully-convolutional network that predicts objectness scores and bounding-box coordinates at each spatial location using anchor boxes.
- The translation-invariant design allows the same function to predict proposals at different locations, making the model parameter-efficient (2.4M vs 27M for MultiBox) and less prone to overfitting.
- Integrates end-to-end trainable proposal generation into the Fast R-CNN pipeline, replacing external methods like Selective Search.
- Strong experimental results with thorough ablation studies that validate key components (e.g., classification vs. regression layers in RPN).
- Training uses an alternating optimization scheme that is simple, converges quickly, and is practically effective.
- Demonstrates a superior speed–accuracy trade-off and near-cost-free proposal computation, enabling practical real-time detection.
- Clarity is generally good but the explanation of translation invariance needs a clearer, explicit contrast with prior methods (e.g., OverFeat, MultiBox).
- The choice of receptive field/window size (n=3) is insufficiently justified and would benefit from deeper analysis.
- The selection of k=9 anchors (3 scales × 3 aspect ratios) lacks exploration of alternative combinations and their effects.
- The rationale for the specific alternating training procedure and evidence of training stability (e.g., multiple runs) could be stronger.
- High significance for both research and industry; code release will facilitate adoption and replication.
- Overall: strong, original technical contribution with excellent empirical support; recommended for acceptance.
