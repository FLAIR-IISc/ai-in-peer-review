This paper introduces a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and includes two fully connected layers that predict object bounds and objectness scores at each spatial position. The RPN is then heuristically combined with Fast R-CNN for object detection and achieves state-of-the-art results on VOC07 and VOC12. Runtime for generating region proposals is greatly reduced because the RPN shares convolutional layers with the detection network.

Pros:
- Smart idea of sharing convolutional layers between the RPN and R-CNN, which greatly reduces the runtime of generating object proposals.
- State-of-the-art performance on object detection.
- Well-written paper.

Cons:
- The training of the RPN and Fast R-CNN is only weakly coupled via alternating optimization, and there is no convergence proof.
- While the RPN can be used independently as a region proposal method, it does not appear to outperform Selective Search.

Overall, this is a very strong piece of work that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN for object detection. The paper is well written with thorough experimental evaluations. The primary concern is the combination strategy for RPN and Fast R-CNN: the proposed alternating optimization is somewhat heuristic and lacks a convergence guarantee.
