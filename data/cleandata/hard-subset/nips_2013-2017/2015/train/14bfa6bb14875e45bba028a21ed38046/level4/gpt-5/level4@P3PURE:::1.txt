The paper’s goal is clear and the experimental results are solid. The authors propose a novel region proposal method that shares CNN features with the object localization module. The proposal network comprises a classification layer for object vs. non-object and a regression layer for bounding-box prediction. By sharing feature computation with the localization module, the combined system is approximately 10× faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate. Because the object localization CNN assumes proposals are given during training, the authors adopt a multi-stage, alternating training procedure.

That said, the paper is not easy to follow, and several points are confusing or insufficiently justified:

1) Translation invariance. The paper claims the RPN is translation-invariant, whereas [19] is not. Please clarify the reasoning behind this distinction and explain how OverFeat fits into this comparison.

2) Manually set parameters. Many parameters are fixed without analysis—for example, n=3 and k=9. Please justify these choices and discuss sensitivity to these settings.

3) Training complexity and stability. The training procedure appears complicated and potentially unstable. Why not apply the same training procedure multiple times? Have the authors explored many alternative settings and found that the current configuration performs best?

Overall, the method is promising, and the empirical results are strong, but the paper would benefit from clearer explanations and justifications for the above points.
