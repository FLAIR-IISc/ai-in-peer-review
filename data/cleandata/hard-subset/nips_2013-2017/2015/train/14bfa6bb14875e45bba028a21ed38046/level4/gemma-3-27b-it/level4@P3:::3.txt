## Review of Faster R-CNN: Towards Real-Time Object Detection with Region Proposal Networks

This paper presents a compelling approach to object detection, addressing a key bottleneck in state-of-the-art systems: region proposal generation. The authors introduce a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that leverages shared convolutional features with a Fast R-CNN detector, enabling near cost-free region proposals and a significant speed-up in detection. This builds directly upon previous work in deep learning for object detection, such as R-CNN [6], SPPnet [7], and Fast R-CNN [5], which progressively reduced computational cost by sharing computations across proposals. The work addresses a well-recognized limitation of existing pipelines – the relative slowness of CPU-based region proposal methods (e.g., Selective Search [22], EdgeBoxes [24]) compared to GPU-accelerated detection networks.

The core idea of learning region proposals with a deep network is novel and well-executed. By formulating the proposal generation as a fully-convolutional network operating on feature maps shared with the detector, the authors elegantly sidestep the performance limitations of hand-engineered feature-based methods. The alternating optimization scheme for training the RPN and Fast R-CNN, while pragmatic, is clearly explained and demonstrates effective convergence. The reported results on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012, achieving state-of-the-art accuracy with a 5fps frame rate using VGG-16, are highly impressive and highlight the practical significance of this work.  The availability of code further strengthens the contribution.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The paper is technically rigorous, with a clear explanation of the RPN architecture, loss function, and training procedure. The ablation studies are particularly helpful in dissecting the contributions of different components.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and organized. The motivation for the work is clearly articulated, and the technical details are presented in a logical and accessible manner. The diagrams (referenced as Fig. 1) are essential and aid understanding.
* **Originality:**  While building on prior work, the concept of learning region proposals within a fully-convolutional network and integrating it seamlessly with an object detector is a novel and significant contribution. The efficient sharing of convolutional features is a key innovation.
* **Significance:** This work represents a substantial advance in the field of object detection. The demonstrated improvement in speed and accuracy has the potential to impact a wide range of applications, making real-time object detection more feasible. The performance on standard benchmarks is strong and well-documented.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Alternating Optimization:** The description of the alternating optimization scheme raises a question regarding its sensitivity to initialization and potential for suboptimal convergence. While the authors state it converges quickly, it would be beneficial to discuss the impact of varying the number of iterations for each step. The assumption that the initial RPN generates optimal proposals seems strong and could be addressed with a more detailed analysis.
* **Sliding Window Step Size:** The paper does not explicitly address the impact of the sliding window step size on the image level, given the downsampling inherent in convolutional neural networks. As the reviewer points out, a large step size could lead to inaccurate bounding box locations. A discussion of how the choice of stride affects performance, and potential mitigation strategies, would strengthen the paper.
* **Typographical Error:** The undefined variable $h_a$ on lines 173 and 174 represents a minor issue, but should be addressed.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* This paper presents a novel and effective approach to object detection that significantly improves upon existing methods.
* The technical contributions are substantial and well-supported by experimental results.
* The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to understand.
* The code availability promotes reproducibility and further research.
* The performance gains are significant and demonstrate the practical impact of the work.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The limited discussion of the alternating optimization scheme's convergence properties and sensitivity.
* The lack of explicit analysis regarding the sliding window step size and its potential impact on accuracy.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of object detection. The strengths of the work clearly outweigh the minor weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the concerns regarding the alternating optimization scheme and the sliding window step size. Specifically, the authors should provide more details about the convergence behavior of the alternating optimization, and discuss the impact of the sliding window stride on performance and potential solutions. Clarifying the definition of $h_a$ is also necessary.
