- Goal is clear and experimental results are solid.
- Paper is not easy to follow; several parts are confusing.
- Claim that the RPN is translation-invariant is unclear and needs clearer comparison to methods like [19] and OverFeat.
- Several parameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) are set manually without justification or sensitivity analysis.
- The four-step training procedure is complicated, appears unstable, and lacks explanation for its choice.
- Authors propose a novel RPN that shares CNN features with object localization, combining classification and regression layers.
- Shared feature computation yields an R-CNN variant about 10× faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate.
- Main contribution: an end-to-end trainable, fully-convolutional RPN predicting object bounds and objectness at each position.
- Method achieves state-of-the-art detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 while reducing proposal computation.
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by thorough experiments and recall-to-IoU analysis.
- Clarity needs improvement; dense explanations and training details should be simplified and supported with visuals.
- Originality is high; the approach meaningfully differs from prior region proposal methods.
- Significance is high due to practical impact on object detection performance and efficiency.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions — justify parameter choices, clarify and simplify training, and add visual aids.
