- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detector to generate proposals nearly cost-free, trained end-to-end with Fast R-CNN via alternating optimization.  
- Reports significant speed (5 fps on VGG-16) and state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012.  
- Builds on SPPnet/Fast R-CNN and relates to OverFeat/MultiBox but uniquely focuses on a cascaded, feature-sharing proposal stage.  
- Strength: Substantial speed improvement bringing object detection closer to real-time.  
- Strength: Strong mAP performance, competitive with or exceeding prior methods.  
- Strength: Novel and well-motivated RPN design that effectively addresses a key bottleneck.  
- Strength: Clear articulation of the problem and proposed solution.  
- Strength: Publicly available code facilitates reproducibility and further research.  
- Weakness: Key hyperparameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) lack justification and no ablation/sensitivity study.  
- Weakness: Alternating training procedure is complex and may be unstable; robustness and alternatives are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Weakness: Claim about translation invariance is unclear and needs clarification and comparison to related work.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of failure cases and common error modes.  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, effective approach with strong empirical results and community-impacting code release.  
- Argument for rejection: Concerns over hyperparameter justification, training complexity/generalizability, and unclear exposition of translation invariance.  
- Overall: Strong paper worthy of acceptance if authors address hyperparameter justification and training/procedure clarity.
