- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to generate proposals more efficiently.  
- RPN uses two fully-connected layers on shared conv feature maps to predict object bounds and objectness scores per position.  
- Feature sharing substantially reduces runtime for region proposal generation.  
- Combined RPN + Fast R-CNN achieves state-of-the-art object detection on VOC07 and VOC12.  
- Paper is clearly written with thorough and convincing experimental evaluation.  
- Training alternates between optimizing RPN and Fast R-CNN and lacks formal convergence guarantees, appearing somewhat ad hoc.  
- RPN as a standalone proposal method does not outperform traditional methods (e.g., Selective Search), suggesting its main benefit is integration into the end-to-end framework.  
- Overall a strong, significant contribution that improves speed and accuracy and merits acceptance despite the heuristic training concern.
