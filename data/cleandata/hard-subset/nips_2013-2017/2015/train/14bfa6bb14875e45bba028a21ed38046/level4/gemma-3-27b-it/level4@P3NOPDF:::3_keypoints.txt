- Method summary: Proposes a two-stage CNN object detector using an RPN (sliding window on top-layer feature map) followed by Fast R-CNN, sharing convolutional layers to achieve near real-time (5 fps) performance.

- Optimization concern: The alternating optimization between RPN and Fast R-CNN appears to use a single iteration, which unrealistically assumes near-optimal initial proposals and may yield suboptimal detection performance; clarification and justification of iteration count needed.

- Localization concern: Sliding windows on heavily downsampled top-layer feature maps may correspond to large steps in image space, causing coarse localization and poor performance on small objects; authors should analyze and/or experiment on the feature-map-to-image step-size relationship.

- Notation issue: The variable h_a referenced in lines 173–174 is undefined and must be clearly defined.

- Positive note: The approach is a logical extension of existing methods, the shared convolutional backbone is efficient, and the reported near real-time speed is noteworthy; addressing the above issues would strengthen the paper.
