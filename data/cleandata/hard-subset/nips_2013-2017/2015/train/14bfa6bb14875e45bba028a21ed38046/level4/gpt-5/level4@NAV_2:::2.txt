The paper introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with an R-CNN-style detector and uses two fully connected heads to predict bounding-box coordinates and objectness scores at each spatial location. The RPN is integrated with Fast R-CNN through an alternating training scheme and achieves state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012. Because the RPN reuses the detector’s convolutional backbone, the cost of generating proposals is greatly reduced.

Pros:
- Elegant idea of sharing convolutional layers between the RPN and R-CNN, substantially cutting the runtime of proposal generation.
- State-of-the-art object detection accuracy.
- Clear and well-organized presentation.

Cons:
- The coupling between RPN and Fast R-CNN relies on an alternating optimization procedure without a convergence guarantee.
- As a stand-alone proposal generator, RPN does not appear to outperform Selective Search.

Overall, this is a strong contribution that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN-based object detection, with clear writing and thorough experiments. The main reservation is the integration strategy: the proposed alternating optimization is somewhat heuristic.
