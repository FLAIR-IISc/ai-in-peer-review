- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to enable a unified, efficient object detection pipeline.  
- RPN predicts bounding boxes and objectness scores at each spatial position using two fully connected layers, generating proposals in a single forward pass.  
- Sharing convolutional layers removes redundant computation and dramatically speeds proposal generation while preserving detection accuracy.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 with a strong speed–accuracy trade-off.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough (mAP, FPS) and the paper is clearly written with well-explained architecture and training protocol.  
- Core innovation is practical and impactful, reducing proposal time to under 0.1s and outperforming selective search in runtime.  
- Training uses an alternating optimization strategy without a unified loss or convergence analysis, lacking theoretical grounding.  
- Standalone RPN performance and proposal-quality analysis (e.g., recall at varying IoUs, ablations) are underexplored.  
- Overall, a significant empirical advance that meaningfully improves detection efficiency, though training methodology and standalone evaluation need further work.  
- Recommendation: Accept — valuable contribution with practical benefits; authors should address training and RPN evaluation in future revisions.
