- Goal is clear and results are solid.  
- Proposes a novel region proposal network (RPN) that shares CNN features with the object localization/detection module.  
- RPN uses a classification layer (object vs non-object) and a regression layer for bounding-box prediction.  
- Sharing features makes the combined system ~10× faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate.  
- Introduces a multi-stage, alternating training procedure because the detector expects region proposals.  
- Paper is hard to follow and several parts are confusing.  
- Translation-invariance claim is unclear: need to state whether RPN’s invariance is theoretical or empirical and why [19] lacks it.  
- Need comparison to OverFeat: is it translation-invariant and which architectural difference breaks invariance?  
- Request controlled experiments or clear theoretical argument to support the translation-invariance claim.  
- Many design choices appear manual (e.g., n=3, k=9); authors must justify these specific choices.  
- Recommend ablation/sensitivity analysis varying n, aspect ratios, and number of anchors to assess robustness.  
- Training procedure seems complicated and potentially unstable; ask why not run additional alternating rounds or use a simpler unified scheme.  
- Request reporting of experiments across other training settings, stability across random initializations, number of alternating iterations, and different datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: clarify translation-invariance, justify hyperparameters, and provide a more transparent account of training and stability.
