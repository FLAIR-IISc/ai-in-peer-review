- Introduces the Region Proposal Network (RPN) to generate high-quality object proposals efficiently by sharing convolutional features with Fast R-CNN.  
- RPN is a fully-convolutional, sliding-window network that regresses bounding boxes and predicts objectness for multi-scale, multi-aspect anchor boxes.  
- Proposes a pragmatic 4-step alternating optimization scheme to train shared features between RPN and the Fast R-CNN detector.  
- Experiments on PASCAL VOC show Faster R-CNN substantially speeds up detection (e.g., 5fps with VGG-16) while improving accuracy and setting new state-of-the-art.  
- High significance and originality: replaces external proposal algorithms with a learned, integrated module that advances detection architecture.  
- Excellent performance and efficiency: achieves SOTA mAP and large speed gains, bringing detection closer to real-time.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation with insightful ablations and analyses (e.g., recall vs. IoU) supporting claims.  
- Clear writing and detailed implementation that facilitate reproducibility.  
- Weakness: the 4-step alternating training is heuristic, complex, and lacks a formal convergence guarantee.  
- Weakness: an independently trained RPN (without feature sharing) is only competitive, not dramatically superior, to Selective Search—main gains come from integration.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, highly impactful, and well-validated work; minor training concerns are outweighed by its significance and merits acceptance.
