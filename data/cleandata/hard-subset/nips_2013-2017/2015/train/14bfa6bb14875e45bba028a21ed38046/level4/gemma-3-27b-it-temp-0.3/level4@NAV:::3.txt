This paper presents a two-stage object detection algorithm leveraging convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The first stage generates region proposals using a region proposal network (RPN) that employs a sliding window approach on the CNN’s top-level feature map. The second stage refines these proposals with a fast R-CNN. By sharing convolutional layers between the RPN and R-CNN and utilizing GPU acceleration, the authors achieve near real-time performance (5fps). 

However, I have several concerns regarding the methodology. First, the optimization procedure appears to utilize only a single iteration of alternating optimization between the RPN and R-CNN. This implicitly assumes the optimality of the proposals generated by the initial RPN iteration, which is unlikely to hold true. Second, the application of a pixel-wise sliding window to the top CNN layer raises questions about the effective step size on the original image. Given the downsampling inherent in CNNs due to max-pooling, a sliding window step on the feature map corresponds to a potentially large step on the input image, potentially leading to imprecise bounding box localization. Finally, the definition of $h_a$ in lines 173 and 174 requires clarification.
