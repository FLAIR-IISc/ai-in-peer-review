**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel region proposal method called Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that shares the convolutional neural network (CNN) features used for object localization. The RPN consists of a classification layer for recognizing objects versus non-objects and a regression layer for predicting object bounding boxes. The authors demonstrate that the combined RPN and Fast R-CNN object detection network is approximately 10 times faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a clear and well-motivated goal of improving the efficiency and accuracy of object detection systems.
2. The experimental results are solid and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed RPN method.
3. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the recall-to-IoU metric and its relation to the ultimate detection accuracy.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

1. The paper is not easy to follow, with some confusing parts that require clarification. For example, the explanation of why RPN is translation-invariant while [19] is not could be improved.
2. Many parameters are set manually without further analysis. It would be beneficial to provide a more detailed explanation of the choices made for these parameters, such as why n=3 and k=9.
3. The training procedure seems complicated and not stable. The authors may want to consider applying the same training procedure multiple times to demonstrate its robustness. Additionally, it would be helpful to know if the authors have tried many other settings and found the current one to perform the best.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper presents a novel region proposal method that shares CNN features with the object localization module, which is a significant contribution to the field of object detection. The proposed RPN method is original and has the potential to improve the efficiency and accuracy of object detection systems.

**Quality and Clarity**

The paper is well-written, but there are some areas that require clarification. The authors may want to consider reorganizing some sections to improve the flow of the paper. Additionally, some of the technical terms and abbreviations could be better explained for a non-expert audience.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance**

Arguments For Acceptance:

**Conclusion**

In conclusion, the paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of object detection. While there are some areas that require clarification and improvement, the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed RPN method.
