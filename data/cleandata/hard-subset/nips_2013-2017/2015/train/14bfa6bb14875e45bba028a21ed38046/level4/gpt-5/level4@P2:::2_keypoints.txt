- Proposes Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional feature maps with Fast R-CNN, predict k anchors’ objectness and box regressions, and—via alternating fine-tuning—achieve SOTA mAP on VOC07/12 with ~300 proposals and ~5 fps.
- Builds on SPPnet/Fast R-CNN by learning proposals from deep features and sharing them; differs from Selective Search/EdgeBoxes and from OverFeat/MultiBox by using translation-invariant anchors with fewer parameters and joint backbone training.
- Technical strength: clean, fully convolutional anchor-based proposal module tightly integrated with the detector; simple and effective multi-scale/aspect-ratio anchor design.
- Efficiency strength: proposal generation is nearly cost-free when sharing features (~10 ms overhead) and requires far fewer proposals than prior methods.
- Empirical strength: strong VOC07/12 mAP scores and thorough ablation studies (sharing, number of proposals, cls vs reg, NMS, backbone).
- Clarity strength: well organized, clearly written, with explicit training/implementation details and released code supporting reproducibility.
- Weakness: reliance on alternating optimization is heuristic; no convergence or stability analysis and no fully joint end-to-end training results.
- Weakness: as a stand-alone proposal method, RPN is not consistently superior to Selective Search with weaker backbones or when detector training proposals are mismatched.
- Weakness: evaluation scope is limited to VOC; more diverse datasets and cross-dataset transfer would strengthen generality claims.
- Quality: method is technically sound (multi-task loss, anchor parameterization, optimization) and ablations convincingly justify design choices.
- Clarity: manuscript is easy to follow and provides sufficient details for reproduction.
- Originality: key novelty is integrating proposal generation into the detector via shared conv features and translation-invariant anchors, yielding a simple, unified two-stage system.
- Significance: meaningfully advances detection accuracy and efficiency and is likely to influence future detector designs.
- Suggestions: provide fully joint end-to-end training results/analysis, expand stand-alone proposal evaluations across backbones/datasets, and analyze sensitivity to anchor scales/aspect ratios and feature stride.
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful contribution that improves efficiency and accuracy despite heuristic training integration; recommend acceptance.
