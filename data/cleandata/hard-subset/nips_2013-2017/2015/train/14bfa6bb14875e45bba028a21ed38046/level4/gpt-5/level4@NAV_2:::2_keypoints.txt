- Introduces an RPN that shares convolutional features with an R-CNN-style detector and uses two fully connected heads to predict bounding-box coordinates and objectness at each spatial location.
- Integrates RPN with Fast R-CNN via an alternating training scheme and achieves state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012.
- Reusing the detector’s convolutional backbone greatly reduces the cost of generating proposals.
- Pros: sharing convolutional layers between RPN and R-CNN substantially cuts proposal-generation runtime.
- Pros: achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy.
- Pros: clear and well-organized presentation.
- Cons: coupling between RPN and Fast R-CNN depends on an alternating optimization procedure without convergence guarantees.
- Cons: as a stand-alone proposal generator, RPN does not outperform Selective Search.
- Overall: strong contribution improving efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN-based detection, with the main reservation that the alternating integration strategy is heuristic.
