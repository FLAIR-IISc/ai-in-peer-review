

This work presents a natural extension to CNN-based object detection by proposing a two-stage algorithm. The first stage employs a Region Proposal Network (RPN) operating on the top-layer feature map of a shared CNN backbone to generate region proposals, while the second stage applies Fast R-CNN to these proposals. Due to shared convolutional layers and GPU acceleration, the method achieves near real-time performance (5 FPS). However, I have the following concerns regarding the technical details:

1.  **Optimization Iteration:** The optimization section describes an alternating procedure between RPN and R-CNN. It appears only a single iteration is performed. This assumes the initial RPN proposals are optimal, which is not generally true. A single iteration may not allow sufficient refinement of proposals or the detection network.

2.  **Sliding Window Step Size:** When applying the pixel-wise sliding window to the top-layer feature map (after multiple max-pooling operations), the step size on the original image scale is not specified. Given the significant spatial reduction from max-pooling, a small step size on the feature map corresponds to a large step size on the input image. This could lead to inaccurate bounding box localization due to insufficient sampling density.

3.  **Definition of $h_a$:** The definition of $h_a$ in lines 173 and 174 is unclear and requires clarification. The exact meaning and computation of this parameter are essential for understanding the proposal generation mechanism.
