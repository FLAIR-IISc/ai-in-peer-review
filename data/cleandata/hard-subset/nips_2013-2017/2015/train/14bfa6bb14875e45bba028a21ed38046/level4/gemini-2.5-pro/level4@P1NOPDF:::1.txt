The paper's goal is well-defined and the experimental results are solid. However, the presentation is difficult to follow in places, with several key design choices requiring further clarification.

1.  The paper claims that the Region Proposal Network (RPN) is translation-invariant. Could the authors elaborate on this point, specifically explaining why the RPN possesses this property while previous methods like [19] and OverFeat do not?

2.  Several key hyperparameters are introduced without sufficient justification. For example, the paper sets the number of anchor scales and aspect ratios to n=3 and k=9, respectively, but provides no analysis to support these choices. The paper would be strengthened by an ablation study or a discussion of how these parameters were selected.

3.  The proposed 4-step alternating training procedure appears complex and raises questions about its stability. Have the authors investigated the variance in results by running the procedure multiple times? Furthermore, it is unclear if this specific multi-stage approach is essential. A justification for this particular training scheme over other, potentially simpler, alternatives would be valuable.

The authors propose a novel region proposal method that shares convolutional features with the object detection network. This Region Proposal Network (RPN) consists of a classification layer for distinguishing objects from background and a regression layer for refining bounding box coordinates. By sharing feature computation with the detection network, the resulting system is approximately 10 times faster than Fast R-CNN while also being slightly more accurate. Because the detection network's training requires pre-computed regions, the authors propose a multi-stage, alternating training procedure to handle this interdependence.
