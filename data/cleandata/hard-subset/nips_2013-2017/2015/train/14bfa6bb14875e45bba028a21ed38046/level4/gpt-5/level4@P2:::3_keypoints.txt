- Proposes Faster R-CNN: integrates Region Proposal Network (RPN) with Fast R-CNN to share convolutional features for end-to-end detection.  
- RPN is a fully-convolutional, anchor-based, translation-invariant module that outputs class-agnostic box proposals and objectness scores.  
- Uses multi-task loss to train box regression and objectness simultaneously; proposals feed Fast R-CNN for classification and refinement.  
- Alternating 4-step training enables shared conv layers between RPN and detector without full joint end-to-end optimization.  
- Anchors at multiple scales/aspect ratios reduce parameters versus MultiBox and mitigate overfitting on small datasets.  
- Ablation studies isolate effects of classification vs regression, NMS, proposal counts, and feature sharing.  
- Strong empirical results on PASCAL VOC with ZF and VGG-16 backbones: state-of-the-art mAP with ~300 proposals and ~5 fps on VGG-16.  
- Runtime analysis shows major speedups over Selective Search and EdgeBoxes while improving accuracy; proposal generation adds ~10 ms.  
- Elegant, principled integration that amortizes proposal cost and has high originality and practical impact.  
- Question on alternating training: unclear if more alternations or joint training were tried and how they affect convergence and accuracy.  
- Concern about stride (16 px) limiting localization granularity for small objects; requests AP on small objects or localization error breakdown.  
- Asks for clarification of variable ha in bounding-box parameterization (anchor height) and explicit definitions for all regression variables.  
- Notes training ignores anchors crossing image boundaries but testing clips them; requests analysis of discarded anchors and impact on convergence.  
- Speed comparison fairness: SS/EB are CPU-only while RPN uses GPU/shared features—requests GPU-accelerated baselines or end-to-end time comparisons.  
- Generalization concern: results only on VOC; requests observations on transfer to datasets with many small/crowded objects (e.g., MS COCO).  
- Clarity suggestions: explicitly state feature-map stride, show mapping of anchor centers to input pixels, and clarify normalization constants and λ.  
- Pros: unifies proposal generation and detection, state-of-the-art accuracy and speed, thorough ablations, simple low-parameter anchor mechanism.  
- Cons: limited optimization discussion beyond single alternation, potential localization limits due to stride, minor clarity issues, and lack of GPU baseline comparisons.  
- Overall: technically sound, original, and significant; with minor clarifications and expanded discussion of stride/localization, reviewer leans toward acceptance.
