This paper presents a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and incorporates two fully connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness scores at each position. The RPN is combined with Fast R-CNN for object detection, achieving state-of-the-art performance on VOC07 and VOC12. By sharing convolutional layers with the detection network, the running time for generating region proposals is significantly reduced.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the idea of sharing convolutional layers between RPN and R-CNN is ingenious, resulting in substantial reductions in runtime for object proposals. Secondly, the proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art performance in object detection. Lastly, the paper is well-written, making it easy to follow and understand.

However, there are some weaknesses that need to be addressed. One major concern is the weak combination of RPN and Fast R-CNN training through alternating optimization, which lacks convergence proof. Furthermore, while RPN can be used as an independent region proposal method, its performance does not seem to surpass that of selective search.

Overall, this is a commendable piece of work that enhances both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN in object detection. The paper is well-written, and the experimental evaluations are thorough. The only concern is the combination of RPN and Fast R-CNN for object detection. The proposed alternating optimization method is somewhat heuristic, and a more rigorous approach may be necessary to fully harness the potential of this framework.
