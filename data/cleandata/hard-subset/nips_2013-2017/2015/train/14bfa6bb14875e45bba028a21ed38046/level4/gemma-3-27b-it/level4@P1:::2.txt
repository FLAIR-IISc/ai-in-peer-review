The paper presents a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN, incorporating two fully-connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness scores at each position. This RPN is integrated with Fast R-CNN for object detection, achieving state-of-the-art performance on VOC07 and VOC12. A key benefit is the significantly reduced runtime for generating region proposals, stemming from the shared convolutional layers.

**Summary:** This paper addresses a critical bottleneck in object detection pipelines – the time-consuming region proposal step. Previous works like SPPnet [7] and Fast R-CNN [5] improved detection speed but left region proposal as a limiting factor. The authors introduce a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that, by sharing convolutional features with the detection network, dramatically reduces the computational cost of generating proposals. The RPN is a fully-convolutional network trained end-to-end to produce high-quality proposals used by Fast R-CNN. An alternating optimization scheme enables the sharing of convolutional features between the RPN and Fast R-CNN. Results demonstrate state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 (73.2% mAP) and 2012 (70.4% mAP) with a frame rate of 5fps on a GPU.

**Strengths:**
* **Clever Design:** The core idea of sharing convolutional layers between the RPN and the detection network is insightful and effectively addresses the computational bottleneck of region proposal.
* **State-of-the-Art Performance:** The proposed system achieves impressive results on standard benchmarks, demonstrating a significant advance in object detection accuracy and speed.
* **Well-Written & Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and experimental setup.
* **Thorough Evaluation:** The authors provide comprehensive experimental results, including ablation studies and comparisons to existing methods, supporting their claims.

**Weaknesses:**
* **Heuristic Training Procedure:** The alternating optimization scheme used to train the RPN and Fast R-CNN, while effective in practice, lacks a formal convergence guarantee. The approach appears somewhat heuristic, and a more principled training method could potentially yield further improvements.
* **RPN as a Standalone Proposal Method:** While the RPN significantly benefits from integration with Fast R-CNN, its performance as a standalone region proposal method doesn't definitively surpass that of established techniques like Selective Search.
* **Anchor Dependence:** The method relies on a set of predefined anchor boxes. The sensitivity of the results to the choice of anchor scales and aspect ratios isn't fully explored, and an adaptive anchor selection strategy could be beneficial.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with clear theoretical grounding and strong experimental support for the claims. The experimental setup is rigorous, and the results are well-presented. While the training procedure is heuristic, the demonstrated performance justifies its use.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized. The authors effectively explain the key concepts and design choices, making it easy for readers to understand the proposed method.

**Originality:** The work is highly original. While building upon previous advances in region-based CNNs [6, 7, 5], the introduction of the RPN and the shared convolutional feature approach represent a significant departure from prior art.

**Significance:** The results are highly significant. The proposed method not only improves object detection accuracy but also dramatically reduces runtime, making real-time object detection more feasible. This work is likely to have a substantial impact on the field and inspire further research in efficient object detection.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Achieves state-of-the-art performance on standard benchmarks.
* Significantly reduces the computational cost of region proposal.
* Presents a novel and well-motivated approach to object detection.
* The paper is well-written and thoroughly evaluated.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The alternating optimization training procedure lacks a formal convergence guarantee.
* Performance as a standalone proposal method doesn't clearly outperform existing techniques.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a strong paper that presents a significant advance in object detection. The proposed RPN is a clever and effective solution to a critical bottleneck in existing pipelines. While the training procedure is somewhat heuristic, the demonstrated performance and clear presentation of the work justify acceptance. I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS 2015.
