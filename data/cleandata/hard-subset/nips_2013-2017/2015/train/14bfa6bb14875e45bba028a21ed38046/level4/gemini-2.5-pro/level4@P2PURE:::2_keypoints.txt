- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with an R-CNN detector and predicts bounding boxes and objectness scores for anchors at each feature-map location.
- RPN is combined with Fast R-CNN through a multi-step training process to form a joint system.
- The combined system achieves state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012.
- Major benefit is a significant reduction in runtime for generating region proposals by sharing expensive convolutional computation.
- Novelty: sharing convolutional layers between proposal and detection stages to drastically cut computational cost.
- Strong empirical result: the complete system achieves top accuracy on standard object detection benchmarks.
- Presentation: paper is well-written and clearly explains contributions and experiments.
- Limitation: training procedure is complex, relying on an alternating optimization heuristic that is not guaranteed to converge or be fully end-to-end.
- Limitation: as a standalone region proposal method, the RPN does not outperform existing methods like Selective Search.
- Overall: excellent and significant contribution to R-CNN-based detection efficiency and accuracy, with reservations about the heuristic training methodology.
