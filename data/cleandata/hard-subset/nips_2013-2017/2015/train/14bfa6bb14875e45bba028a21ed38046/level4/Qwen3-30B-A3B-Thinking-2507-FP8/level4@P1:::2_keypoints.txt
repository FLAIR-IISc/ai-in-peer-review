- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with detection networks to eliminate region-proposal bottleneck.  
- Trains a fully-convolutional network to predict object bounds and objectness at each spatial location using translation-invariant anchors.  
- Integrates RPNs with Fast R-CNN via a simple alternating optimization to form a unified detection system.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy (73.2% mAP on VOC07, 70.4% on VOC12) while running at ~5fps on GPU, far faster than Selective Search.  
- Shared-feature paradigm is elegant and removes redundant computation without sacrificing accuracy.  
- Empirical results show RPNs outperform Selective Search (59.9% vs. 58.7% mAP with 300 proposals) and enable near-real-time detection.  
- Ablation studies thoroughly validate design choices, highlighting the importance of bounding-box regression and objectness scoring.  
- Anchor design is parameter-efficient compared to MultiBox, and alternating optimization converges practically (~80k iterations).  
- Presentation is clear with precise architecture, loss, training protocol, and implementation details that aid reproducibility.  
- Training strategy for coupling RPN and Fast R-CNN is heuristic and lacks theoretical justification; joint optimization is deferred.  
- Standalone proposal quality (recall–IoU) is comparable to EdgeBoxes and not clearly superior to Selective Search; gains mainly come from feature sharing.  
- Runtime comparison should more explicitly consider GPU-accelerated proposal methods, which could narrow the speed gap despite lacking feature sharing.  
- Overall assessment: significant, technically sound, well-validated contribution that advances object detection and merits acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
