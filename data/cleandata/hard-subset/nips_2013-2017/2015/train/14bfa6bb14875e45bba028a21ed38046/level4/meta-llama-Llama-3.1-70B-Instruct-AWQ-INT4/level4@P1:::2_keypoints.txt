- Introduces a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and uses two FC layers to predict object bounds and objectness.
- RPN combined (heuristically) with Fast R-CNN achieves state-of-the-art results on VOC07 and VOC12.
- Sharing convolutional layers greatly reduces region proposal generation time compared to external proposal methods.
- Integrating proposal generation into the detection network removes reliance on external, costly proposal algorithms.
- Paper is well-written with thorough experimental evaluations and detailed component analysis.
- The shared-convolution idea is a smart, effective way to reduce runtime.
- RPN and Fast R-CNN are trained via alternating optimization without a convergence proof.
- The heuristic alternating training may lead to suboptimal performance or instability in some cases.
- RPN as a standalone proposal method does not clearly outperform selective search.
- Using deep networks for proposals is not entirely novel (related to OverFeat, MultiBox, etc.).
- Significant contribution: advances detection accuracy while lowering computational cost, enabling wider real-time use.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address training/combination concerns.
