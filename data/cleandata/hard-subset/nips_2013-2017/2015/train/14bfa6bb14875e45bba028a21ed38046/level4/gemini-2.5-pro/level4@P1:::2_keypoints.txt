- Addresses the computational bottleneck of region proposal generation in object detection by making it nearly cost-free at test time.
- Introduces a fully-convolutional Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares deep convolutional features with the detector.
- RPN slides over the final conv feature map and concurrently regresses bounding boxes and predicts objectness for multiple anchor boxes (scales/aspect ratios).
- Proposes a 4-step alternating optimization scheme to train the RPN and Fast R-CNN to share features.
- The unified system, Faster R-CNN, attains state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 while running ~5 fps with VGG-16.
- Core strength: elegant feature sharing between proposal and detection networks that dramatically reduces computation and enables near real-time detection.
- Strength: achieves improved or state-of-the-art accuracy, showing efficiency gains do not sacrifice performance and can improve detector-tuned proposals.
- Strength: clear writing and thorough experiments, including ablation studies that isolate component contributions.
- Weakness: the 4-step alternating training is heuristic with no theoretical convergence proof; not fully end-to-end principled.
- Weakness: as a standalone proposal generator, RPN is competitive but not clearly superior to methods like Selective Search; main gains come from integration.
- Overall: a strong, influential paper that significantly advances both speed and accuracy in object detection despite some training-scheme limitations.
