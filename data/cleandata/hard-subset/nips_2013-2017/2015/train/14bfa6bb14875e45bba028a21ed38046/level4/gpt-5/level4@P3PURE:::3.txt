This work is a natural extension of existing CNN-based object detection. It proposes a two-stage algorithm: (1) a region proposal stage that uses a traditional sliding-window approach on the top-layer CNN feature map (RPN), and (2) a Fast R-CNN applied to the proposed regions. Because the convolutional layers are shared between the RPN and the R-CNN, and computation is accelerated on the GPU, the system achieves near real-time performance (approximately 5 fps).

My concerns are as follows.
1) In the optimization section, it appears there is only a single iteration of alternating optimization between the RPN and the R-CNN. If this is the case, the method effectively assumes the proposals generated by the first RPN pass are already optimal (or near-optimal), which is not a reasonable assumption. Please clarify whether additional alternations were attempted and, if not, provide justification or empirical evidence that more iterations do not improve performance.

2) When the pixel-wise sliding window is applied to the top CNN layer, what is the corresponding step size on the input image? Because multiple max-pooling operations reduce spatial resolution, the top-level feature map is much smaller than the image. As a result, a sliding window on the top level may correspond to a large step at the image level, potentially causing inaccurate bounding-box localization. Please specify the effective stride and explain how you address any resulting localization errors.

3) The definition of h_a in lines 173–174 is missing. Please provide a clear definition.
