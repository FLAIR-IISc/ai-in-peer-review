- Presents a two-stage detector: RPN on CNN feature maps + Fast R-CNN classifier with a GPU implementation (~5 fps) as a practical contribution.  
- Major concern: optimization uses a single alternating iteration between RPN and detector, which is likely insufficient and unjustified; needs clarification and ablation on iteration count.  
- Major concern: spatial stride of the RPN sliding window at the input image scale is unspecified, risking coarse localization; must define stride and anchor scaling.  
- Major concern: notation $h_a$ is undefined in the text, impairing comprehension of proposal generation.  
- Additional: ambiguous which CNN feature layer(s) the RPN operates on (e.g., conv5-3); the paper should specify and justify the choice.  
- Additional: speed claim (“near real-time, 5 fps”) lacks hardware, input resolution, and baseline comparison details, making it misleading.  
- Additional: unclear how CNN weight sharing between RPN and Fast R-CNN is implemented (joint vs. sequential training), harming reproducibility.  
- Strength: conceptually sound two-stage design that efficiently leverages shared CNN computation and provides a clear high-level pipeline description.  
- Recommendation: reject pending major revisions to justify/validate optimization, specify spatial parameters and feature layers, define notation, and provide implementation details.
