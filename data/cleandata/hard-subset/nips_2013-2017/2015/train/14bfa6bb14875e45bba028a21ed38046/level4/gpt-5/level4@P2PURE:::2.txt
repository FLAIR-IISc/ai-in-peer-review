The paper presents a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and adds two fully connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness scores at each spatial location. The RPN is then heuristically combined with Fast R-CNN for object detection and achieves state-of-the-art performance on VOC07 and VOC12. Because the RPN and detector share convolutional layers, the time required to generate region proposals is greatly reduced.

Pros:
- Clever idea of sharing convolutional layers between the RPN and R-CNN, which substantially reduces the runtime for generating object proposals.
- State-of-the-art object detection performance.
- Well-written paper.

Cons:
- The training of the RPN and Fast R-CNN is only weakly coupled through alternating optimization, and there is no convergence proof.
- While the RPN can be used independently for region proposals, it does not appear to outperform Selective Search.

Overall, this is a very strong piece of work that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN-based object detection, with clear writing and thorough experimental evaluation. The primary concern is how the RPN is combined with Fast R-CNN; the proposed alternating optimization strategy is somewhat heuristic.
