The paper’s objective is well-defined and the empirical results are strong. Nevertheless, the manuscript is somewhat difficult to follow, with several confusing aspects highlighted below.
1. Why is the RPN translation-invariant, whereas [19] is not? How does this compare to OverFeat?
2. Many hyperparameters are chosen manually without further justification. For instance: why n=3 and k=9?
3. The training pipeline appears complex and potentially unstable. Why not repeat the same alternating training schedule multiple times? Did the authors explore other configurations and conclude that the current one performs best?

The authors introduce a new region proposal mechanism that shares CNN features with the object localization network. The proposal module comprises a classification head for object vs. background and a regression head for bounding box prediction. Owing to shared feature computation with the localization component, the integrated RCNN system is approximately 10× faster than Fast R-CNN and marginally more accurate. Because the detector’s training assumes fixed regions, a multi-stage alternating training procedure is proposed.
