Review- The paper’s objective is clear and the experimental results are strong. However, the manuscript is not easy to follow, with several confusing aspects noted below.
1. Why is the RPN translation-invariant, whereas [19] is not? How does this relate to OverFeat?
2. Many parameters are chosen manually without further analysis. For example, why n=3 and k=9?
3. The training procedure appears complicated and potentially unstable. Why not apply the same training schedule multiple times? Did the authors test many other configurations and find that the current one performs best?

The authors present a novel region proposal method that shares the CNN features used for object localization. The proposal component includes a classification layer to distinguish object vs. non-object and a regression layer to predict object bounding boxes. By sharing feature computation with the localization/detection module, the resulting RCNN variant is approximately 10× faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate. Because the detection CNN’s training assumes given regions, a multi-stage alternating training procedure is adopted.
