- Proposes Region Proposal Network (RPN) that generates object proposals from the same convolutional feature maps used by the detector, enabling near-cost-free proposals.  
- RPN slides a small network over feature maps, predicting bounding boxes relative to pre-defined multi-scale, multi-aspect "anchors"; shared features with Fast R-CNN enable end-to-end efficiency.  
- Significant contribution: solves a major bottleneck in two-stage detection, making high-accuracy detection practical for near real-time use.  
- Original and elegant design: reusing convolutional features and translation-invariant anchors to predict multiple proposals per location is novel and efficient.  
- Strong empirical performance: state-of-the-art mAP on PASCAL VOC and large speed-up (≈2s → ≈200ms per image); thorough ablation studies support claims.  
- Training complexity: the 4-step alternating training scheme is ad-hoc and lacks discussion of stability, convergence, and reproducibility.  
- Missing hyperparameter ablations: no study on anchor scales/aspect ratios (k=9) or sliding window size (n=3), leaving sensitivity and design choices unexplored.  
- Clarity on translation-invariance: claim needs clearer explanation and comparison to methods like MultiBox and k-means anchors.  
- For acceptance: introduces a highly novel, impactful RPN that addresses a critical bottleneck in detection.  
- For acceptance: achieves higher accuracy and an order-of-magnitude speed-up over prior systems.  
- For acceptance: feature sharing for proposals and detection is a fundamental idea with high field impact.  
- For acceptance: comprehensive experiments provide strong evidence for the paper's claims.  
- Against acceptance: complex 4-step training lacks strong justification, raising stability and reproducibility concerns.  
- Against acceptance: omission of key hyperparameter ablations (anchor configuration) limits understanding and follow-up work.  
- Recommendation: overall strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance with revisions to clarify training and provide hyperparameter analyses.
