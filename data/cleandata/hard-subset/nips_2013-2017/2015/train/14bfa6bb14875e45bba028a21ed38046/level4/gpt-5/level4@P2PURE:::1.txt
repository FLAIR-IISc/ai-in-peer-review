The paper’s objective is clear, and the experimental results are strong. The authors propose a novel region proposal method that shares CNN features with the object localization module. The proposal network includes a classification layer (object vs. non-object) and a regression layer for bounding-box prediction. By sharing feature computation with the localization module, the combined system (new R-CNN) is approximately 10x faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate. Because training the object localization CNN assumes that region proposals are given, the authors adopt a multi-stage alternating training procedure.

That said, the paper is not easy to follow, with several confusing points:
1. The paper claims the RPN is translation-invariant, whereas [19] is not. Please clarify why this is the case. How does this compare with OverFeat?
2. Many parameters are set manually without further analysis. For example, why choose n=3 and k=9?
3. The training procedure appears complicated and potentially unstable. Why not repeat the same training schedule multiple times? Did the authors explore many alternative settings and find that the current configuration performs best?
