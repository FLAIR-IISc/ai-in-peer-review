The paper presents a clear objective and delivers robust experimental results. However, the presentation could be improved for clarity, as several points require further elaboration. Specifically:

1. The rationale for the translation invariance of the proposed Region Proposal Network (RPN) – and how this differs from the approach in [19] – is unclear. Discussion of Overfeat in this context would also be beneficial.
2. Several key parameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) appear to be set arbitrarily without supporting analysis or justification.
3. The training procedure appears complex and potentially unstable. It would strengthen the work to demonstrate the robustness of this procedure through multiple runs. Furthermore, detailing the range of training settings explored and the reasoning behind selecting the current configuration would be valuable.

The authors introduce a novel region proposal method that leverages shared CNN features with an object localization module. This method utilizes a classification layer to distinguish between object and non-object regions, and a regression layer to refine bounding box predictions. By sharing feature computation, the resulting RCNN implementation achieves approximately a 10x speedup compared to Fast R-CNN, with a slight improvement in accuracy. Recognizing that the object localization CNN relies on pre-defined regions, the authors propose a multi-stage alternative training procedure to address this dependency.
