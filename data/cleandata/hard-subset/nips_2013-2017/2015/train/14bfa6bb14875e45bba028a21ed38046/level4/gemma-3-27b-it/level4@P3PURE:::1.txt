The goal of the paper is clear and the experimental results are strong. However, the paper’s presentation could be improved for clarity, as several aspects are currently confusing. 

1. The paper states that the Region Proposal Network (RPN) is translation-invariant, while [19] is not. This distinction requires further explanation. What about Overfeat – how does its approach to translation invariance compare?

2. Numerous hyperparameters are set without sufficient justification or analysis. For example, the choices of n=3 and k=9 are not explained. 

3. The training procedure appears complex and potentially unstable. It would be beneficial to understand the robustness of this procedure; have the authors applied it multiple times to assess variability? Furthermore, have they explored alternative training settings, and if so, why did they ultimately choose the current configuration?

The authors propose a novel region proposal method that leverages shared convolutional neural network (CNN) features with an object localization module. This region proposal network consists of a classification layer – distinguishing object from non-object – and a regression layer for predicting object bounding boxes. By sharing feature computation, the resulting R-CNN implementation achieves approximately a 10x speedup compared to Fast R-CNN, with a slight improvement in accuracy. Given that the object localization CNN training assumes pre-defined regions, the authors propose a multi-stage alternating training procedure to address this dependency.
