- Proposes a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detection network, is fully convolutional, predicts bounds and objectness, and is trained end-to-end.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art object detection on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 and significantly reduces region proposal runtime.  
- Positions the work relative to prior region proposal methods and R-CNNs, emphasizing differences and benefits.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of RPN architecture, training procedure, and experiments.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and includes detailed comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Strength: RPN is a novel, effective approach to region proposal generation.  
- Strength: Method is well-motivated and clearly addresses limitations of existing approaches.  
- Strength: Improves both efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN–based object detection.  
- Weakness: Joint training of RPN and Fast R-CNN uses alternating optimization without a convergence proof, raising stability/robustness concerns.  
- Weakness: RPN as a standalone region proposal method does not significantly outperform Selective Search.  
- Recommendation: Accept, but encourage addressing convergence proof and standalone RPN performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: SOTA performance, strong motivation, and thorough experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Lack of convergence proof for joint training and limited standalone gains over Selective Search.
