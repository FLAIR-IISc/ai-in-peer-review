- Novel region proposal method shares convolutional features with object localization network, achieving ~10× speedup and slight accuracy gain.  
- Clear goal and seemingly robust experimental results, but overall presentation needs clarity.  
- Claim that the RPN is translation-invariant while [19] is not is unexplained; comparison to Overfeat (which uses region proposals) is missing.  
- Key hyperparameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) are chosen without justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Training procedure is complex, under-described, and may be unstable; robustness is unclear.  
- Authors should report results from multiple runs to demonstrate consistency and variance.  
- Need to explain whether alternative training configurations were tested and why the final procedure was chosen.  
- The feature-sharing idea is strong, but reliance on a multi-stage training pipeline requiring predefined regions requires better justification.  
- A clearer articulation of design choices and demonstration of training stability would significantly strengthen the paper.
