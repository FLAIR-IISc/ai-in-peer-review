The paper presents a novel region proposal method that leverages shared convolutional features with an object localization network, resulting in a significant speedup—approximately 10x faster than Fast R-CNN—with a slight improvement in accuracy. The goal is clearly defined, and the experimental results appear robust. However, the paper’s presentation could be improved for clarity and a more thorough justification of design choices.

Specifically, several aspects of the method require further elaboration. First, the authors claim translation invariance for their Region Proposal Network (RPN) but state that [19] lacks this property. The rationale for this difference is unclear, and a comparison to Overfeat, which also employs region proposals, would be beneficial to contextualize this claim.

Second, many hyperparameters are set without sufficient analysis or justification. For example, the choices of *n*=3 and *k*=9 are not explained. Providing the reasoning behind these settings, or demonstrating sensitivity to their values, would strengthen the paper. Did the authors explore a range of values for these and other hyperparameters?

Third, the proposed training procedure appears complex and potentially unstable. The description lacks sufficient detail to fully assess its robustness. To address this, the authors should consider reporting results from multiple runs with the same training settings to demonstrate consistency. Furthermore, it would be helpful to know if alternative training configurations were explored and why the current procedure was ultimately selected as the most effective.

The core idea of feature sharing is compelling, and the reported speedup is substantial. However, the reliance on a complicated, multi-stage training procedure – necessitated by the assumption of pre-defined regions for object localization CNN training – requires more detailed explanation and justification. A clearer articulation of the rationale behind key design choices and a demonstration of training stability would significantly enhance the paper’s impact.
