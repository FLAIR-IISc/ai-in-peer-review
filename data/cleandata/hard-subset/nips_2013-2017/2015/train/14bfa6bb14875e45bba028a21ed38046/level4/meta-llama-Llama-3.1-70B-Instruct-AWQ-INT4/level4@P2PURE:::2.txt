The paper proposes a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and employs two fully connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness scores at each position. The RPN is combined with Fast R-CNN for object detection, yielding state-of-the-art performance on VOC07 and VOC12. By sharing convolutional layers with the detection network, the running time for generating region proposals is significantly reduced.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the idea of sharing convolutional layers between RPN and R-CNN is ingenious, resulting in substantial runtime savings for object proposal generation. Secondly, the proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art performance in object detection. Lastly, the paper is well-written and clearly presented.

However, there are a few limitations. One major concern is that the training of RPN and Fast R-CNN is combined through alternating optimization, which is a weak form of combination and lacks convergence guarantees. Furthermore, while RPN can be used as a standalone region proposal method, its performance does not appear to surpass that of selective search.

Overall, this is a high-quality work that improves the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN in object detection. The paper is well-written, and the experimental evaluations are thorough. The primary concern lies in the combination of RPN and Fast R-CNN for object detection, where the proposed alternating optimization method seems somewhat heuristic.
