- Paper proposes a two-stage CNN detector: RPN generates dense sliding-window proposals on top-layer feature maps, then Fast R-CNN classifies them, sharing conv layers for near real-time ~5 fps performance.
- Approach is a natural progression of CNN detection pipelines and is practically well-motivated.

Major concerns:
- Optimization: unclear whether alternating optimization between RPN and Fast R-CNN is run for multiple iterations or only once; need clarification.
- If only a single alternation is used, authors must justify why fixing initial RPN proposals is acceptable.
- Provide results showing how detection performance changes with additional alternations or full end-to-end joint training.
- Stride & localization: report the effective stride of the top feature map relative to the input image and how feature-map anchors map to image coordinates.
- Describe any mechanisms used to mitigate coarse spatial sampling (dilated convs, deconvolution/upsampling, multi-scale aggregation, or finer-stride layers).
- Provide ablation showing how different strides or feature-map resolutions affect bounding-box localization and overall mAP.
- Notation: define h_a (and w_a) explicitly and keep consistent parameterization for anchor sizes and box regression.
- Clarifying training alternation, effective stride/localization impacts, and fixing notation would strengthen reproducibility and technical presentation.
