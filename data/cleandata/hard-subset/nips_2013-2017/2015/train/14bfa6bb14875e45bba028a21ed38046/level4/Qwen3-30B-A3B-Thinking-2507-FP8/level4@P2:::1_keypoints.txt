- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features between proposal and detection networks to eliminate the proposal bottleneck.
- RPNs are fully-convolutional and produce high-quality proposals with minimal extra compute (~10 ms/image), enabling ~5 fps on VGG-16.
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy (73.2% mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007, 70.4% on 2012) with large speed gains over Fast R-CNN.
- Builds on SPPnet and Fast R-CNN and advances prior localization work (OverFeat, MultiBox) by generating proposals via shared features rather than separate methods.
- Novelty: the idea of sharing conv features for region proposals is a significant conceptual advance; the translation-invariant anchor design is well-motivated.
- Experimental quality: thorough, technically sound results and ablation studies showing RPNs outperform Selective Search and EdgeBoxes in speed and accuracy.
- Practical significance: makes near-real-time, accurate object detection feasible and demonstrates a ~10× speedup with comparable accuracy.
- Clarity: paper is generally well-organized and clearly written with a logical progression.
- Weakness: explanation of why RPN is translation-invariant vs OverFeat is confusing and needs clearer distinction (fully-convolutional vs fully-connected predictors).
- Weakness: hyperparameter choices (e.g., n=3, k=9) are used without sufficient justification; ablation or rationale on these settings is needed.
- Weakness: training procedure (four-step alternating optimization) is called "simple" but requires clearer motivation and evidence of stability/convergence.
- Weakness/clarification: paper should more explicitly differentiate its approach from OverFeat’s one-stage detection pipeline.
- Overall: strong, well-executed contribution with minor, addressable clarity and parameter-justification issues; recommend acceptance.
