- Introduces the Region Proposal Network (RPN) that generates object proposals within a deep CNN by sharing convolutional feature maps with detection.  
- RPN is a fully-convolutional sliding-window network that predicts objectness and bbox regressions relative to multi-scale, multi-aspect "anchor" boxes.  
- Sharing features makes proposal generation nearly free (~10 ms per image), removing the Selective Search bottleneck.  
- The unified system, Faster R-CNN, achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC and runs near real-time (5–17 fps).  
- Proposes a 4-step alternating training scheme to learn shared features between RPN and Fast R-CNN.  
- Strength: Significant and original solution that unifies proposal and detection into a single trainable network.  
- Strength: Technically sound with extensive experiments and useful ablation studies validating feature sharing and design choices.  
- Strength: Major speed and efficiency improvement enabling real-time high-accuracy detection with deep models (e.g., VGG-16).  
- Weakness: Need clearer explanation of translation invariance of anchors versus methods like MultiBox and comparison to OverFeat.  
- Weakness: Key hyperparameters (sliding window size n=3, anchors k=9) lack analysis or justification and sensitivity studies.  
- Weakness: The 4-step alternating training is complex; the paper should discuss why end-to-end joint training is infeasible, stability across runs, and alternative procedures tested.  
- Conclusion: High-quality, impactful work with minor clarity/training/hyperparameter issues; strong recommendation for acceptance.
