The paper presents a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and includes two fully connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness scores at each position. By heuristically combining RPN with Fast R-CNN for object detection, the authors achieve state-of-the-art performance on VOC07 and VOC12. Notably, the running time for generating region proposals is significantly reduced due to the shared convolutional layers between RPN and the detection network.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the idea of sharing convolutional layers between RPN and R-CNN is ingenious, resulting in a substantial reduction in the runtime of object proposals. Secondly, the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance in object detection. Lastly, the paper is well-written and clearly conveys the authors' ideas.

However, there are a few limitations to the paper. One concern is that the training of RPN and Fast R-CNN are only weakly combined through alternating optimization, and there is no proof of convergence. Furthermore, while RPN can be used independently as a region proposal method, its performance does not appear to surpass that of selective search.

Overall, this is an excellent piece of work that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN in object detection. The paper is well-written, and the experimental evaluations are thorough. The primary concern lies in the combination of RPN and Fast R-CNN for object detection, where the proposed alternating optimization method seems somewhat heuristic.
