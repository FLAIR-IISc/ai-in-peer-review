- Introduces Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detection network to remove the region proposal bottleneck.  
- Achieves near real-time performance (≈5 fps with VGG-16) while maintaining state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012.  
- Learning region proposals with a deep network (vs Selective Search/EdgeBoxes) is a well-motivated and effective idea.  
- Experimental results convincingly show significant speedups without sacrificing detection accuracy.  
- Public code release is a valuable contribution for reproducibility and community adoption.  
- Discussion of translation invariance (Sec 3.2) is unclear and its relation to OverFeat and [19] needs a more direct comparison.  
- Critical hyperparameters (e.g., n=3, k=9) are chosen without sufficient justification or presented ablation studies.  
- Alternating optimization training scheme is complex and the paper lacks analysis of its robustness, convergence, or sensitivity.  
- The explanation of anchor boxes, scales, and aspect ratios is unclear and would benefit from visual illustrations.  
- Paper contains clarity issues in multiple sections that hinder understanding.  
- Limited ablation studies weaken support for key design choices and parameter settings.  
- Strengths summarized: significant speed improvement, state-of-the-art accuracy, novel approach, and code release.  
- Weaknesses summarized: clarity problems, insufficient justification for design choices, complex training procedure, and limited ablation analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses major detection bottleneck, strong empirical results, broad adoption potential, and code availability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear exposition, unaddressed training robustness, and lack of detailed ablations.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—fix clarity, justify design/hyperparameter choices, analyze training robustness, and add ablation studies (especially for n and k).
