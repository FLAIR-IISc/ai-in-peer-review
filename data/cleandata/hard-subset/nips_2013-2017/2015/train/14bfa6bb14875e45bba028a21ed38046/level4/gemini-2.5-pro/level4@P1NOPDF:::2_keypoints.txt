- Proposes an RPN that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN and predicts box coordinates and objectness for anchor boxes at each spatial location.
- Integrates RPN with Fast R-CNN via a multi-stage/alternating training process and achieves state-of-the-art results on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012.
- Major advantage is a large reduction in proposal generation time by reusing the detector’s convolutional features.
- Core contribution (sharing conv layers between proposal and detection networks) is clever, effective, and dramatically speeds up the pipeline.
- The complete system attains state-of-the-art accuracy on standard object detection benchmarks.
- The paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.
- Training is not unified end-to-end; the alternating optimization is heuristic and lacks convergence guarantees.
- As a standalone proposal method, the RPN does not clearly outperform methods like Selective Search in proposal quality.
- Overall an excellent paper that significantly improves both efficiency and accuracy of the R-CNN framework.
- Main reservation: the heuristic alternating training lacks the elegance of full end-to-end training despite strong empirical results.
