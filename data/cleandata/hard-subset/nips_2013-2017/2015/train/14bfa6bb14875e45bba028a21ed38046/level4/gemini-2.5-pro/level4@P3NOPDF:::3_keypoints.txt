- Paper presents a two-stage CNN detector: a Region Proposal Network (RPN) sliding over convolutional feature maps, followed by Fast R-CNN for classification and bbox regression, achieving ~5 fps with high accuracy.  
- Key contribution: RPN and detection network share convolutional layers, enabling an efficient, nearly end-to-end system that removes slow external proposal algorithms.  
- Concern 1 — Alternating optimization: authors perform only one 4-step alternation (train RPN → train Fast R-CNN → fine-tune RPN → fine-tune Fast R-CNN); reviewer asks why more iterations weren’t done, whether further alternations were tried, and if additional iterations give diminishing returns or instability.  
- Concern 2 — Effective stride & localization: large effective stride on input (e.g., 16 px for VGG-16) may harm precise localization, especially for small objects; reviewer requests the actual input-plane step size and explanation/evidence of how accurate localization is maintained despite coarse sampling.  
- Concern 3 — Notation: reviewer asks for clarification/definition of the term h_a used in lines 173–174.  
- Overall: architecture is compelling and effective, but addressing training-iteration rationale and stride/localization impacts would strengthen the paper.
