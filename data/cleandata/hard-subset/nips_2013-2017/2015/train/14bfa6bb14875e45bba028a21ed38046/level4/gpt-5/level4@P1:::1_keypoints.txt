- Summary: Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs), a fully convolutional, anchor-based module that shares features with Fast R-CNN to produce class-agnostic proposals, achieving strong VOC mAP (73.2/70.4) and near real-time speed.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on R-CNN/SPPnet/Fast R-CNN by moving proposals into the network and compares favorably to OverFeat and MultiBox via translation-invariant anchors and parameter sharing.  
- Strength — practical impact: Proposal generation becomes nearly cost-free when sharing features, giving a large runtime speedup with slight accuracy gains.  
- Strength — clear idea: Anchor-based, fully convolutional proposal generation integrated with Fast R-CNN is conceptually simple and effective.  
- Strength — empirical support: Strong mAP on VOC07/12 and informative ablation studies (proposal count, NMS, cls vs reg).  
- Strength — reproducibility: Key training details and code are provided.  
- Weakness — translation-invariance claim unclear: The paper needs to precisely define what is meant by translation invariance and compare explicitly with OverFeat/MultiBox.  
- Weakness — manual hyperparameters: Choices like receptive field n=3, k=9 anchors, IoU labels (0.7/0.3), and NMS threshold (0.7) lack sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness — training complexity/stability: The 4-step alternating training is more complex than standard fine-tuning; stability, variance across runs, and effects of repeated alternation are not reported.  
- Weakness — clarity issues: Explanations of cross-boundary anchors, loss normalizations (Ncls, Nreg) and λ could be streamlined and justified.  
- Quality: Method is technically sound with appropriate losses/sampling; ablations and runtime accounting are careful, but hyperparameter sensitivity and training stability remain concerns.  
- Clarity: Overall readable but needs clearer discussion of translation invariance, anchor design, and a concise description/table of the alternating training steps.  
- Originality: Moving proposal generation into a shared, fully convolutional module is a distinct and timely contribution relative to prior single-stage and multi-box methods.  
- Significance: High — removes proposal bottleneck enabling near real-time detection with strong accuracy and likely wide adoption.  
- Specific request — translation invariance: Clarify how learned functions generalize to spatial shifts and differ from MultiBox/OverFeat.  
- Specific request — hyperparameter sensitivity: Provide experiments varying n, k, anchor scales/aspect ratios, and NMS to show effects on recall/mAP and runtime.  
- Specific request — training experiments: Report whether more alternation cycles help, variance across random seeds, and alternative schedules tried.  
- Specific request — loss weighting: Justify normalization constants and λ and report sensitivity to these choices.  
- Specific request — cross-boundary anchors: Quantify fraction ignored in training and effect of including/clipping them.  
- Pros for acceptance: Strong empirical results, substantial speedup over SS, clear architectural advance, code availability, and thorough ablations.  
- Cons for acceptance: Lack of analysis for key hyperparameters, possibly ad hoc alternating training procedure, and confusion around translation-invariance claims.  
- Overall evaluation: Compelling, impactful paper; leans toward acceptance provided authors clarify translation-invariance and add analyses on hyperparameter sensitivity and training stability.
