- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to produce near cost-free region proposals and speed up detection.  
- Formulates proposal generation as a fully-convolutional network, replacing CPU-based methods like Selective Search and EdgeBoxes.  
- Alternating optimization scheme for joint training of RPN and detector is pragmatic, explained clearly, and shows effective convergence.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 with ~5 fps using VGG-16, demonstrating practical significance.  
- Code release improves reproducibility and strengthens the contribution.  
- Paper is technically rigorous with clear architecture, loss, training details, and helpful ablation studies.  
- Well-written and organized presentation with useful diagrams aiding understanding.  
- Original and significant idea: learning region proposals within a fully-convolutional network and sharing features with the detector.  
- Results represent a substantial advance in speed and accuracy for object detection, enabling real-time applications.  
- Concern about sensitivity and convergence of the alternating optimization scheme; needs analysis of initialization and iteration choices.  
- Missing discussion on the effect of sliding-window step size (stride) due to CNN downsampling and its impact on bounding box accuracy.  
- Minor typographical issue: undefined variable h_a on lines 173–174 should be clarified.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective approach, strong experimental support, clear writing, and available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis of alternating optimization convergence and sliding-window stride effects.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address convergence analysis, stride discussion, and the typographical clarification.
