The paper presents a clear goal and demonstrates solid experimental results. However, some parts of the paper are confusing and require clarification. 

Firstly, the authors claim that their Region Proposal Network (RPN) is translation-invariant, whereas [19] is not. It would be beneficial to provide a more detailed explanation for this difference and also discuss how their approach compares to Overfeat in terms of translation invariance.

Furthermore, several parameters are set manually without thorough analysis or justification. For instance, the choices of n=3 and k=9 seem arbitrary, and it is unclear why these specific values were selected. A more in-depth analysis of these parameters and their impact on the results would strengthen the paper.

Additionally, the training procedure appears complicated and unstable. To improve the robustness of the results, it would be helpful to apply the same training procedure multiple times and report the average performance. It is also unclear whether the authors have experimented with other training settings and found the current one to perform the best.

The authors propose a novel region proposal method that shares the CNN features used for object localization. This method consists of a classification layer for distinguishing between objects and non-objects, and a regression layer for predicting object bounding boxes. By sharing feature computations with the object localization module, the new RCNN is approximately 10 times faster than Fast R-CNN and slightly more accurate. To address the issue that the training procedure of the object localization CNN assumes given regions, the authors propose a multi-stage alternative training procedure.

Overall, the paper has potential, but addressing these concerns would improve its clarity and impact.
