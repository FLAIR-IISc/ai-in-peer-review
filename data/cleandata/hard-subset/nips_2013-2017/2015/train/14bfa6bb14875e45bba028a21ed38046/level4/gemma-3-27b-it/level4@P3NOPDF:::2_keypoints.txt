- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to efficiently generate high-quality region proposals.  
- RPN predicts object bounds and objectness scores for anchor boxes at each feature-map position.  
- Integration with Fast R-CNN achieves state-of-the-art detection on VOC07 and VOC12.  
- Strength: Sharing convolutional features dramatically reduces runtime compared to methods like Selective Search.  
- Strength: Demonstrates compelling state-of-the-art experimental results.  
- Strength: Paper is clear and well-written with informative figures and tables.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation, including ablation studies.  
- Weakness: Training alternates between optimizing RPN and Fast R-CNN (heuristic) and lacks a formal convergence guarantee.  
- Weakness: RPN as a standalone proposal method does not consistently outperform traditional methods like Selective Search.  
- Overall: Strong contribution that effectively addresses the region-proposal bottleneck and improves detection speed and accuracy.  
- Recommendation: Accept — positives outweigh concerns about the training procedure.  
- Future work: Explore more robust/joint training strategies and evaluate RPN beyond tight integration with Fast R-CNN.
