- Proposes a two-stage detector: RPN for region proposals and Fast R-CNN for detection, sharing convolutional features for near real-time performance.
- RPN is a fully-convolutional network that replaces traditional proposal methods and shares features with detection.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the approach.
- Demonstrated effectiveness on PASCAL VOC with detailed experiments and comparisons.
- Evaluation includes recall-vs-IoU analysis and shows state-of-the-art accuracy while being significantly faster than prior methods.
- Concern: only one iteration of alternating optimization between RPN and Fast R-CNN is used, which may not ensure optimal proposals.
- Suggestion: provide convergence analysis or justify why a single iteration suffices.
- Concern: pixel-wise sliding-window step size at the top CNN layer may be too coarse due to pooling, risking inaccurate bounding-box localization.
- Request clarification on the definition of h_a (lines 173–174).
- Strengths summarized: novel RPN+Fast R-CNN approach, clear presentation, strong accuracy and speed.
- Weaknesses summarized: limited optimization iterations, potential localization inaccuracies from stride, unclear notation.
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the above concerns; overall rating 8/10.
