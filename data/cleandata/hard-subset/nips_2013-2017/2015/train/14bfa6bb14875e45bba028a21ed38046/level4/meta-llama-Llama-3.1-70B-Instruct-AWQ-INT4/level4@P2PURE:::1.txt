The paper's objective is well-defined, and the experimental results are robust. However, the text is not easy to follow, with several confusing aspects that require clarification. Firstly, the authors claim that their Region Proposal Network (RPN) is translation-invariant, whereas [19] is not. It would be beneficial to provide a detailed explanation for this distinction and also discuss how their approach compares to Overfeat in this regard. 

Furthermore, several parameters are set manually without a thorough analysis. For instance, the choice of n=3 and k=9 lacks clear justification. It is essential to provide a more in-depth explanation for these choices or acknowledge the need for further investigation.

Additionally, the training procedure appears complicated and unstable. To address this, the authors could consider applying the same training procedure multiple times to evaluate its consistency. It is also unclear whether the authors have experimented with various settings and found the current one to be optimal.

The authors propose a novel region proposal method that shares the CNN features used for object localization. This method consists of a classification layer for distinguishing between objects and non-objects and a regression layer for predicting object bounding boxes. By sharing feature computation with the object localization module, the new RCNN achieves a significant speedup of approximately 10 times compared to Fast RCNN, while also slightly improving accuracy. 

To address the issue that the training procedure for the object localization CNN assumes given regions, the authors propose an alternative training procedure involving multiple stages.
