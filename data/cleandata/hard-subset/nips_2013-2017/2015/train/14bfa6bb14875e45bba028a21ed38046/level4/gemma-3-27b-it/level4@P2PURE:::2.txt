This paper introduces a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and employs two fully connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness scores for each position. The RPN is integrated with Fast R-CNN for object detection, achieving state-of-the-art performance on VOC07 and VOC12. A key benefit is the reduced runtime for generating region proposals, stemming from the shared convolutional layers between the RPN and the detection network.

Pros:
- The sharing of convolutional layers between the RPN and R-CNN is a clever design choice that significantly reduces the computational cost of region proposal generation.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance in object detection.
- The paper is well-written and clear.

Cons:
- The training of the RPN and Fast R-CNN is performed via alternating optimization, lacking a formal convergence guarantee.
- While the RPN can function as a standalone region proposal method, it does not demonstrably outperform selective search in that capacity.

This is a strong contribution that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN-based object detection. The paper is well-written, and the experimental evaluation is thorough. A potential area for improvement lies in the integration of the RPN and Fast R-CNN; the proposed alternating optimization approach feels somewhat heuristic.
