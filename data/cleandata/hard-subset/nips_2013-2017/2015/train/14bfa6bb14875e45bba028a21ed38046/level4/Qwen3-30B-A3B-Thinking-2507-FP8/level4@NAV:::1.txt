

The paper presents a clear objective with robust experimental validation. However, the manuscript is somewhat difficult to follow due to several points requiring clarification:  
1. The translation invariance of RPN is stated, yet [19] is not described as such; this discrepancy requires explanation, particularly in light of Overfeat's properties.  
2. Several hyperparameters are manually selected without justification (e.g., *n*=3, *k*=9). A more thorough analysis of these choices would strengthen the work.  
3. The training procedure appears complex and potentially unstable. The authors should address whether the procedure was repeated to confirm reproducibility and whether alternative configurations were evaluated to justify the selected settings.  

The authors introduce a novel region proposal method that shares the CNN feature map used for object localization. This method employs a classification layer (to distinguish objects from background) and a regression layer (to predict bounding boxes). By leveraging shared feature computation with the object localization module, the integrated architecture achieves approximately 10× speedup over Fast R-CNN while maintaining slightly higher accuracy. Given that the object localization CNN assumes predefined regions during training, a multi-stage training strategy is proposed to address this dependency.
