This paper presents a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and includes two fully connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness scores at each position. The RPN is combined with Fast R-CNN for object detection, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the VOC07 and VOC12 datasets. By sharing convolutional layers with the detection network, the running time of generating region proposals is significantly reduced.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the idea of sharing convolutional layers between RPN and R-CNN is innovative and greatly reduces the runtime of object proposals. Secondly, the proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on object detection tasks. Finally, the paper is well-written and clearly presents the methodology and experimental results.

However, there are a few limitations to the approach. The training of RPN and Fast R-CNN is weakly combined through alternating optimization, and there is no convergence proof provided. Additionally, while RPN can be used independently as a region proposal method, its performance does not seem to surpass that of selective search.

In conclusion, this is a high-quality paper that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN in object detection. The paper is well-written, and the experimental evaluations are thorough. The primary concern is the heuristic combination of RPN and Fast R-CNN for object detection, which relies on alternating optimization. A more rigorous approach to combining these components would strengthen the paper. Nevertheless, the proposed RPN is a significant contribution to the field of object detection, and its impact is likely to be substantial.
