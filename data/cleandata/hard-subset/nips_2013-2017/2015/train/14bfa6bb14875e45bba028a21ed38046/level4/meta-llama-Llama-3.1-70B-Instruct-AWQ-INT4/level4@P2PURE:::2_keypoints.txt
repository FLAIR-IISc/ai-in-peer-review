- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and uses two fully connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness at each position.  
- Integrates RPN with Fast R-CNN, achieving state-of-the-art detection performance on VOC07 and VOC12.  
- Sharing convolutional layers substantially reduces runtime for generating region proposals.  
- Strength: The idea of sharing convolutional layers is ingenious and yields large runtime savings.  
- Strength: The approach achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy.  
- Strength: The paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Limitation: RPN and Fast R-CNN are trained via alternating optimization, which is a weak, heuristic combination without convergence guarantees.  
- Limitation: As a standalone region proposal method, RPN does not clearly outperform selective search.  
- Overall: High-quality work that improves R-CNN efficiency and accuracy, with the main concern being the heuristic/alternating training scheme.
