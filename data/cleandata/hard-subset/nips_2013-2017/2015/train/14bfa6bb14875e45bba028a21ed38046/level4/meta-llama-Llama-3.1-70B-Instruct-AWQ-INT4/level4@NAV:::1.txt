The paper's objective is well-defined, and the experimental results demonstrate a strong foundation. However, there are several areas that hinder the clarity of the manuscript, as outlined below. 

1. The translation invariance of RPN is not clearly justified, particularly in comparison to [19]. Furthermore, the treatment of Overfeat in this context is unclear.
2. Several parameters have been manually set without thorough justification or analysis. For instance, the choice of n=3 and k=9 warrants further explanation.
3. The training procedure appears complex and unstable. It would be beneficial to investigate the effects of repeated training procedures and explore alternative settings to determine whether the current approach is indeed optimal.

The authors propose a novel region proposal method that leverages the shared CNN features for object localization. This method comprises a classification layer for distinguishing between objects and non-objects, as well as a regression layer for predicting object bounding boxes. By sharing feature computations with the object localization module, the new RCNN achieves a significant speedup of approximately 10 times compared to Fast RCNN, while also exhibiting slightly improved accuracy. To address the assumption of given regions in the object localization CNN training procedure, the authors propose an alternative training procedure involving multiple stages.
