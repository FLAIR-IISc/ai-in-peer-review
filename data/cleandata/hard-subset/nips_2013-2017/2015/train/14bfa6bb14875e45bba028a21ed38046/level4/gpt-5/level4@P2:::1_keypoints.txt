- Summary: Proposes Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with Fast R-CNN to generate nearly cost-free, anchor-based region proposals feeding a unified detector trained by alternating fine-tuning.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on R-CNN/SPPnet/Fast R-CNN and contrasts with Selective Search/EdgeBoxes and one-stage or proposal nets like OverFeat and MultiBox via translation-invariant anchors and lightweight heads.

- Strength: Clear motivation — proposal generation is the bottleneck and feature sharing is a natural solution.

- Strength: Strong empirical results — high mAP on PASCAL VOC 2007/2012 with as few as 300 proposals and speedups versus SS/EB pipelines.

- Strength: Simple, effective architecture — small conv window plus sibling 1×1 classification and regression heads with conceptually clean anchor mechanism.

- Strength: Pragmatic training and useful ablations showing effects of sharing, classification vs. regression, and proposal count.

- Strength: Good engineering — GPU-friendly, end-to-end proposal training, and small marginal runtime (~10 ms) for RPN when sharing features.

- Weakness: Translation-invariance claim needs clarification and explicit contrast with MultiBox and OverFeat.

- Weakness: Unclear why the anchor formulation is translation-invariant versus learned priors; manuscript mixes references confusingly.

- Weakness: Hyperparameter choices lack sensitivity analysis (conv window size n, number of anchors k, anchor scales/ratios).

- Weakness: No reported sensitivity to NMS threshold, IoU thresholds for positive/negative labels, or number of test-time proposals.

- Weakness: Training procedure stability is insufficiently analyzed — variability across seeds, learning rates, and pretraining is not reported.

- Weakness: Unclear whether repeating alternation improves performance or why joint end-to-end training diverges; lacks experiments or analysis.

- Comment: Ensure runtime comparisons are fair (GPU vs. CPU) and provide end-to-end timing breakdowns across backbones at fixed proposal counts.

- Comment: Quantify the benefit of per-anchor regressors versus shared regression used in Fast R-CNN.

- Comment: Analyze the effect of ignoring boundary-crossing anchors in training but clipping them at test time on recall and mAP.

- Comment: Generalization is limited to PASCAL VOC — a brief experiment or discussion on transfer to other datasets would strengthen claims.

- Quality: Method is technically sound and experiments on VOC are thorough, but sensitivity and stability analyses are missing.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written but needs clearer exposition on translation-invariance, distinctions from OverFeat/MultiBox, and rationale for design choices and alternation schedule.

- Originality: The anchor parameterization combined with a lightweight fully convolutional head and feature sharing is a notable, distinct contribution despite related prior ideas.

- Significance: High — approach reduces the proposal bottleneck while maintaining or improving accuracy and is likely to be widely adopted.

- Pros: Elegant integration of proposal generation with detection, strong accuracy with few proposals, and substantial speed improvements over SS/EB.

- Cons: Limited analysis of design choices and training stability, need clearer conceptual comparisons, and results confined to VOC.

- Questions for authors: Clarify translation-invariance and explicit comparisons with MultiBox and OverFeat.

- Questions for authors: Provide sensitivity analyses for n, k, anchor scales/ratios, NMS/IoU thresholds, and test-time proposal counts.

- Questions for authors: Discuss stability of alternating training, whether additional alternations help, and any attempts/insights on joint training.
