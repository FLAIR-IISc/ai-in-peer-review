The paper presents a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and includes two fully connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness scores at each position. By heuristically combining RPN with Fast R-CNN for object detection, the authors achieve state-of-the-art performance on VOC07 and VOC12. Notably, the running time of generating region proposals is significantly reduced due to the shared convolutional layers between RPN and the detection network.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the idea of sharing convolutional layers between RPN and R-CNN is ingenious, leading to substantial reductions in the runtime of object proposals. Secondly, the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on object detection tasks. Lastly, the paper is well-written and clearly presents the authors' ideas.

However, there are a few limitations to the approach. The training of RPN and Fast R-CNN is weakly combined through alternating optimization, and there is no convergence proof provided. Furthermore, while RPN can be used independently as a region proposal method, its performance does not seem to surpass that of selective search.

Overall, this is an excellent piece of work that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN in object detection. The paper is well-written, and the experimental evaluations are thorough. The primary concern lies in the method used to combine RPN and Fast R-CNN for object detection, as the proposed alternating optimization approach appears somewhat heuristic.
