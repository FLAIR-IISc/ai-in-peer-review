- Introduces a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN and uses two fully connected layers to predict bounding boxes and objectness at each spatial location.  
- Heuristically integrates RPN with Fast R-CNN, achieving state-of-the-art detection results on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012.  
- Shared convolutional layers substantially reduce runtime for generating region proposals compared to non-shared architectures.  
- Pros: Feature sharing markedly accelerates proposal generation.  
- Pros: Strong empirical performance on standard object detection benchmarks.  
- Pros: Clear writing and thorough experimental validation.  
- Cons: The alternating-optimization training of RPN and Fast R-CNN is heuristic, lacks theoretical foundation, and has no convergence guarantees.  
- Cons: As a standalone proposal method, RPN does not surpass selective search in proposal quality.  
- Overall: Notable contribution improving both efficiency and accuracy, but the heuristic training procedure is the primary concern and needs more rigorous justification.
