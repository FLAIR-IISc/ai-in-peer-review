This paper is a straightforward extension of CNN-based object detection. My main concerns are:

1. In the optimization procedure, it appears that the alternating training between the RPN and the Fast R-CNN detector is performed only once. If so, this effectively presumes that the proposals produced by the RPN after the first alternation are already near-optimal, which is unlikely.

2. When applying a sliding window densely over the top convolutional feature map, what is the effective stride on the input image? Because multiple max-pooling layers substantially downsample the feature map, a unit step on the top layer corresponds to a relatively large step in image pixels, which may impair localization precision of the predicted boxes.

3. Please clarify the definition of $h_a$ in lines 173–174.

This work proposes a two-stage CNN-based detection pipeline. The first stage generates region proposals via a sliding-window mechanism operating on the top-layer CNN feature map (RPN). The second stage applies Fast R-CNN to these proposed regions. By sharing convolutional layers between the RPN and the detector and exploiting GPU acceleration, the system achieves near real-time performance (approximately 5 fps).
