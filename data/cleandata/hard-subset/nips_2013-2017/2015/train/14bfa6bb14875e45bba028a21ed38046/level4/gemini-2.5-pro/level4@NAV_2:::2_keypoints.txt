- Introduces the Region Proposal Network (RPN) that generates object proposals inside a CNN.  
- Shares deep convolutional feature maps between the proposal network and the Fast R-CNN detector.  
- RPN is a fully-convolutional sliding-window network that regresses bounding boxes and predicts objectness at anchor points of multiple scales/aspect ratios.  
- Uses a pragmatic 4-step alternating optimization to train RPN and Fast R-CNN to share features.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC with near real-time speed (5–17 fps).  
- Major conceptual advance: unifies proposal generation and detection, addressing the previous speed bottleneck.  
- Technically sound with extensive experiments and ablation studies that justify design choices.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized, with a well-motivated architecture and training procedure.  
- Weakness: the alternating training scheme is heuristic and not a true end-to-end joint optimization, lacking theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: as a standalone proposal method, RPN does not fundamentally outperform Selective Search; main gains stem from feature sharing and stronger backbones.
