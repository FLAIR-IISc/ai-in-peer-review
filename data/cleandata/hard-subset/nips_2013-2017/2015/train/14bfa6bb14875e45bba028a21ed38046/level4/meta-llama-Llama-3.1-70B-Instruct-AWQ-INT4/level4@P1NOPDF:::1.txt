The paper presents a clear goal and solid experimental results. However, there are several aspects that make it challenging to follow, which are outlined below.

Firstly, the authors claim that their Region Proposal Network (RPN) is translation-invariant, whereas the approach in [19] is not. It would be beneficial to provide a more detailed explanation for this distinction, as well as clarify how their method compares to Overfeat in this regard.

Furthermore, several parameters are set manually without thorough analysis or justification. For instance, the choices of n=3 and k=9 are not adequately explained. It would strengthen the paper to provide a more in-depth discussion on the selection of these parameters and their impact on the overall performance.

Additionally, the training procedure appears to be complicated and unstable. The authors may want to consider applying the same training procedure multiple times to demonstrate its robustness. It is also unclear whether the authors have experimented with various settings and found the current one to be optimal. Providing more details on the exploration of different training settings would enhance the paper's credibility.

The authors propose a novel region proposal method that shares the CNN features used for object localization. This method consists of a classification layer for distinguishing between objects and non-objects, and a regression layer for predicting object bounding boxes. By sharing feature computations with the object localization module, the combined new RCNN achieves a significant speedup of approximately 10 times compared to Fast RCNN, while maintaining slightly higher accuracy. 

To address the limitation that the training procedure of the object localization CNN assumes regions are given, the authors propose an alternative training procedure involving multiple stages.
