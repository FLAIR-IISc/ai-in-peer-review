- Introduces Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share convolutional features with the detection network to eliminate region proposal as a computational bottleneck.  
- RPN is a fully convolutional network that simultaneously predicts object bounds and objectness scores using translation-invariant anchors (9 scale/aspect combinations).  
- Proposes an alternating four-step optimization to jointly train RPN and Fast R-CNN while sharing convolutional features.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC (73.2% mAP 2007, 70.4% mAP 2012) and real-time-like speed (5 fps), with proposals computed in ~10 ms/image.  
- Strong technical contribution addressing a well-identified bottleneck, with comprehensive experiments and ablation studies validating design choices.  
- Original and elegant idea: making proposal computation integral to the detection network and using translation-invariant anchors.  
- Clear presentation of the core idea and logical motivation for the alternating training, supported by experiments.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate necessity of both classification and regression layers in RPNs and importance of feature sharing.  
- Clarity issues: insufficient explanation of why RPN is translation-invariant versus other methods and lack of justification for anchor/window parameter choices (n=3, k=9).  
- Training complexity concern: four-step alternating optimization is somewhat complex and lacks comparison to simpler joint-training alternatives.  
- Limited discussion of relation to one-stage detectors (e.g., SSD, YOLO) and broader placement in the detection literature.  
- Practical impact strengthened by code release and dual achievement of speed and accuracy.  
- Pro-acceptance points: solves a key bottleneck, state-of-the-art accuracy with improved speed, novel approach, thorough experiments, practical implementation.  
- Con-acceptance points: unclear parameter justification, potentially overcomplicated training procedure, limited contextualization with recent one-stage methods.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with high marks for quality, significance, and originality; recommend acceptance.
