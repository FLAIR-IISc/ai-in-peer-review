The paper presents a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with Fast R-CNN and incorporates two fully connected layers to predict object bounds and objectness scores for each position. This RPN is heuristically combined with Fast R-CNN for object detection, achieving state-of-the-art performance on VOC07 and VOC12. A significant benefit is the reduced runtime for generating region proposals, stemming from the shared convolutional layers with the detection network.

**Pros:**
- The sharing of convolutional layers between the RPN and Fast R-CNN is a clever idea that substantially reduces the computational cost of region proposal generation.
- The method achieves state-of-the-art performance in object detection.
- The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Cons:**
- The training of the RPN and Fast R-CNN is weakly coupled through alternating optimization, and no convergence guarantees are provided.
- While the RPN can be used independently as a region proposal method, it does not appear to outperform Selective Search in that capacity. 

This is a strong contribution that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN-based object detection. The paper is well-written and includes thorough experimental evaluations. The primary concern lies in the method used to combine the RPN and Fast R-CNN; the proposed alternating optimization approach feels somewhat heuristic.
