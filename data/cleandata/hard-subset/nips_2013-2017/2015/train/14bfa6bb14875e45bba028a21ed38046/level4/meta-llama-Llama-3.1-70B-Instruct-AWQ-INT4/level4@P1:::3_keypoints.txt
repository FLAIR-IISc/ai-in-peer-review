- Proposes a two-stage CNN-based object detector combining Region Proposal Networks (RPN) and Fast R-CNN.  
- RPNs operate on top-layer CNN feature maps and share full-image convolutional features with the detection network, making proposals nearly cost-free.  
- RPN is a fully-convolutional network that simultaneously predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position.  
- Authors introduce an alternating optimization scheme to train RPN and Fast R-CNN to share convolutional features.  
- The method achieves near real-time performance (~5 FPS) by sharing convolutional layers and using GPU acceleration.  
- Experimental results are state-of-the-art on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012, with thorough ablation studies and comparisons.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Concern: Alternating optimization appears to use only one iteration, raising questions about proposal optimality and training details.  
- Concern: Sliding-window on top-layer feature maps may correspond to large steps on the image layer due to pooling, potentially causing inaccurate bounding boxes.  
- Concern: Definition and role of the variable h_a (lines 173–174) are unclear and need clarification.  
- Strengths summarized: novel RPN approach, strong empirical results, and clear writing.  
- Weaknesses summarized: unclear optimization process, potential localization issues from stride, and unclear notation (h_a).
