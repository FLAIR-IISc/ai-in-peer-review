This paper proposes a two-stage object detection algorithm based on a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). The first stage, a Region Proposal Network (RPN), generates object proposals by applying a sliding window method to the final convolutional feature map. In the second stage, a Fast R-CNN detector processes these proposed regions. By sharing convolutional layers between the RPN and the Fast R-CNN detector and leveraging GPU acceleration, the proposed system achieves near real-time performance of 5 frames per second.

While this work is a logical extension of existing CNN-based object detection methods, I have the following concerns:

1.  The optimization section appears to describe only a single iteration for the alternating optimization between the RPN and the Fast R-CNN. If this is the case, it assumes that the proposals generated by the initial RPN are optimal, which is a strong and potentially incorrect assumption.
2.  When the sliding window is applied to the final convolutional layer, what is the effective step size, or stride, on the input image layer? Due to multiple max-pooling operations, the final feature map is at a much coarser scale than the input image. Consequently, a sliding window on this top layer may correspond to a large stride on the image level, potentially causing inaccurate bounding box localization.
3.  Could the authors please provide the definition for the variable `$h_a$` used in lines 173 and 174?
