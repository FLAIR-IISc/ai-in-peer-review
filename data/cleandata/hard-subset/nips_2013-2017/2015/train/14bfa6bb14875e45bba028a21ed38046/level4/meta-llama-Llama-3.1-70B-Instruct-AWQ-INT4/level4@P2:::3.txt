This work is a natural extension to the existing CNN-based object detection. The authors propose a two-stage object detection algorithm, where the first stage is region proposal using a Region Proposal Network (RPN) and the second stage is object detection using Fast R-CNN. The RPN is a fully-convolutional network that shares convolutional features with the Fast R-CNN, enabling near real-time object detection.

The paper is well-written and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach. The use of RPN for region proposal generation is a significant improvement over traditional methods, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on the PASCAL VOC dataset.

The authors also provide a detailed analysis of their approach, including an evaluation of the recall-to-IoU metric and a comparison with other methods. The results show that the proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy while being significantly faster than other methods.

However, I do have some concerns regarding the optimization process. Specifically, it appears that there is only one iteration of the alternating optimization between RPN and Fast R-CNN. This assumes that the proposals generated by the first iterated RPN are optimal, which is not necessarily true. It would be beneficial to see an analysis of the convergence of the optimization process or an explanation for why a single iteration is sufficient.

Additionally, I am concerned about the step size of the pixel-wise sliding window when applied to the top layer of the CNN. Since the CNN has several rounds of max-pooling, the top layer is at a much smaller scale compared to the input image. This could result in inaccurate bounding box locations.

Finally, I would like clarification on the definition of $h_a$ in lines 173 and 174 of the paper.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of object detection. With some minor revisions to address the concerns mentioned above, this paper has the potential to be a top-rated submission.
