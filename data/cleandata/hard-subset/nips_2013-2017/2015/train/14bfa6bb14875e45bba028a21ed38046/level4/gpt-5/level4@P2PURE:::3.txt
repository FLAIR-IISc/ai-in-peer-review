This paper is a natural extension of existing CNN-based object detection methods. My main concerns are:

1. In the optimization section, the alternating training between the RPN and the R-CNN appears to consist of only a single iteration. If so, it effectively assumes the proposals generated by the first RPN pass are optimal, which is clearly not true.

2. When applying a pixel-wise sliding window on the top CNN layer, what is the corresponding step size on the image? Because the CNN includes several rounds of max-pooling, the top-layer feature map is much smaller than the input image. As a result, a unit step on the top layer may correspond to a large step on the image, potentially leading to inaccurate bounding-box localization.

3. What is the definition of h_a in lines 173 and 174?

This work proposes a two-stage object detection algorithm based on convolutional neural networks. The first stage performs region proposal using a traditional sliding-window approach on the top-layer CNN feature map (RPN). In the second stage, a Fast R-CNN is applied to the proposed regions. Since the convolutional layers are shared between the RPN and R-CNN, and computation is accelerated on a GPU, the method achieves near real-time performance (5 fps).
