- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares full-image convolutional features with a detection network to unify and speed up object detection.
- Replaces slow external proposal methods with a small fully-convolutional network that predicts bounding boxes and objectness scores on a shared feature map.
- Uses anchor boxes (k=9 from 3 scales × 3 aspect ratios) as a core design enabling dense proposals and claimed translation-invariance.
- Introduces a 4-step alternating training procedure to handle interdependence between the RPN and the detection network.
- Reports ~10× test-time speedup over Fast R-CNN while achieving state-of-the-art accuracy on standard benchmarks.
- Strength: an elegant, high-impact solution that addresses a major bottleneck in two-stage detectors with solid experimental results.
- Weakness: the claim of translation-invariance versus methods like MultiBox needs clearer explanation and direct comparison.
- Weakness: key hyperparameters (number, scales, aspect ratios of anchors) lack ablation studies and sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: the complex 4-step training schedule is not fully justified; alternative or simpler joint-training schemes are not explored.
- Suggestion: report training stability (results averaged over multiple runs) and analyze convergence robustness to strengthen claims.
