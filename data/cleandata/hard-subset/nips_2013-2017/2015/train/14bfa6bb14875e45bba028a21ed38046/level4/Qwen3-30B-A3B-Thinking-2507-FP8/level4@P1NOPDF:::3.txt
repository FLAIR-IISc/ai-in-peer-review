

This paper extends CNN-based object detection by proposing a two-stage framework. While the approach leverages shared convolutional layers between the Region Proposal Network (RPN) and Fast R-CNN to accelerate inference, three key concerns require clarification.

First, the optimization procedure appears to use only a single iteration for alternating updates between RPN and R-CNN. This implicitly assumes that the initial RPN proposals are optimal, which is unlikely in practice. The authors should clarify whether multiple iterations are feasible or explain why a single iteration suffices.

Second, the paper applies a sliding window with pixel-level stride to the top feature map of the CNN. Given multiple max-pooling layers, this top feature map operates at a significantly reduced spatial scale (e.g., 1:16 relative to the input image). A stride of 1 pixel on this map corresponds to an effective stride of 16 pixels on the original image, potentially causing substantial inaccuracies in bounding box localization. The authors must specify the exact stride used at the image level or justify why the reduced resolution does not degrade localization precision.

Third, the definition of $h_a$ in lines 173–174 is unclear. Without this definition, it is difficult to verify the mathematical formulation in Equation 12 or subsequent derivations.

The core contribution—using RPN to generate region proposals directly from feature maps, followed by Fast R-CNN processing—builds logically on prior work. The claimed near real-time performance (5 FPS) is plausible given the shared convolutional layers and GPU acceleration, but the technical details in the concerns above must be addressed to validate the method's robustness.
