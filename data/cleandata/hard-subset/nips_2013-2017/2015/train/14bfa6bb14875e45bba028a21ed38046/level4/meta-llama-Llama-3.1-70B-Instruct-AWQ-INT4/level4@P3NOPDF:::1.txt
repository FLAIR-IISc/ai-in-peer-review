The paper presents a clear goal and demonstrates solid experimental results. However, there are a few areas that require clarification to improve the overall flow and understanding of the manuscript.

Firstly, the authors claim that their Region Proposal Network (RPN) is translation-invariant, whereas [19] is not. It would be beneficial to provide a detailed explanation for this distinction, as well as a comparison with Overfeat. Specifically, the authors should elaborate on the key differences between their approach and existing methods that make their RPN more robust to translations.

Furthermore, several parameters are set manually without thorough analysis or justification. For instance, the choices of n=3 and k=9 seem arbitrary, and the authors should provide a more in-depth explanation for these selections. A more comprehensive analysis of the parameter space and the impact of these choices on the overall performance of the model would strengthen the paper.

Additionally, the training procedure appears complicated and unstable. The authors propose a multi-stage alternative training procedure, but it is unclear whether this is the optimal approach. To alleviate these concerns, the authors should provide more details on their training process, including whether they have tried multiple settings and how they arrived at the current configuration.

On a positive note, the authors propose a novel region proposal method that shares the CNN feature used for object localization. This approach consists of a classification layer for distinguishing between objects and non-objects, and a regression layer for predicting object bounding boxes. By sharing feature computation with the object localization module, the combined new RCNN achieves a significant speedup of approximately 10 times compared to Fast RCNN, while maintaining a slight improvement in accuracy.

Overall, the paper demonstrates promise, but addressing the aforementioned concerns would greatly enhance the clarity and impact of the manuscript. With some revisions to address these issues, the authors can strengthen their contribution and provide a more compelling case for their proposed method.
