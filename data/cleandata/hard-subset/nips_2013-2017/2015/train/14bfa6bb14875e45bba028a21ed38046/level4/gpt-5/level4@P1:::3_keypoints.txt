- Proposes a two-stage detector combining a fully-convolutional RPN with Fast R-CNN that shares convolutional features, achieving near real-time ~5 fps (VGG-16) and strong mAP (73.2% VOC2007, 70.4% VOC2012) with 300 proposals.  
- Builds on SPPnet/Fast R-CNN and contrasts with OverFeat/MultiBox by using translation-invariant anchors and tighter feature sharing with fewer parameters.  
- Strength: elegant, practical unification of proposal generation and detection that reduces the proposal bottleneck to ~10 ms per image.  
- Strength: strong empirical results and thorough ablations showing competitive/superior mAP with only 300 proposals.  
- Strength: clear, simple architectural choices (multi-scale/aspect anchors; 3×3 conv + 1×1 conv heads) that are efficient and easy to implement.  
- Strength: sensible training strategy that alternates RPN and Fast R-CNN fine-tuning to enable feature sharing without unstable joint optimization.  
- Weakness: unclear alternating optimization details—number of alternations, effect on mAP and proposal recall, and evidence of convergence are not reported.  
- Weakness: potential localization granularity issues due to coarse top-layer sliding-window stride after pooling, possibly harming small-object localization.  
- Weakness: notation ambiguity in bounding-box parameterization—variables like ha (and wa) should be explicitly defined.  
- Quality: methodology is sound with careful design (anchors, balanced sampling, smooth L1) and informative ablations, but missing convergence analysis under alternating optimization.  
- Clarity: generally well-written; should explicitly state image-level stride, define all bbox symbols, and document alternation counts and convergence behavior.  
- Originality: notable and influential contribution—translation-invariant, fully-convolutional RPN tightly shared with the detector, distinct from prior MultiBox/OverFeat approaches.  
- Significance: high—removes a major bottleneck, yields state-of-the-art accuracy with fewer proposals, and is likely broadly adoptable.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant practical impact, simple/general design transferable across backbones/datasets, and comprehensive empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited analysis of alternating optimization, insufficient discussion of conv stride effects on localization, and minor notation clarity issues.  
- Suggestions: report training results with multiple alternations, provide explicit image-level stride values per backbone and analyze their impact, and clarify all bounding-box parameter variables.
