This work is a natural extension to the existing CNN-based object detection. The authors propose a two-stage object detection algorithm based on convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The first stage is region proposal, which is based on the traditional sliding window method but working on the top layer feature map of a CNN, referred to as Region Proposal Networks (RPN). In the second stage, a Fast R-CNN is applied to the proposed regions. Since the convolution layers are shared between RPN and Fast R-CNN, and the calculation is sped up using a GPU, the algorithm can achieve near real-time performance (5fps).

The authors' main contribution is the introduction of RPNs, which share full-image convolutional features with the detection network, thus enabling nearly cost-free region proposals. The RPN is a fully-convolutional network that simultaneously predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position. The authors also propose a simple alternating optimization scheme to train the RPN and Fast R-CNN to share convolutional features.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their method, including ablation studies and comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods. The experimental results are impressive, with the proposed method achieving state-of-the-art object detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 while maintaining a high frame rate.

However, I have a few concerns regarding the optimization and implementation details.

Firstly, in the optimization section, it appears that there is only one iteration for the alternating optimization between RPN and Fast R-CNN. If this is the case, it assumes that the proposals generated by the first iterated RPN are optimal, which is obviously not true. It would be beneficial to clarify this point and provide more details on the optimization process.

Secondly, when the pixel-wise sliding window is applied to the top layer of the CNN, what is the step size on the image layer? Since the CNN has several rounds of max-pooling, the top level is in a much smaller scale compared to the input image. The sliding window on the top level may have a big step on the image level, causing inaccurate bounding box locations.

Lastly, the definition of $h_a$ in lines 173 and 174 is unclear. It would be helpful to provide a clear explanation of this variable and its role in the proposed method.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes significant contributions to the field of object detection. With some clarifications on the optimization and implementation details, this paper has the potential to be a top-rated submission.

Strengths:
- Novel approach to region proposal generation using RPNs
- Impressive experimental results on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012
- Well-written and easy to follow paper

Weaknesses:
- Unclear optimization process
- Potential issue with sliding window step size on the image layer
- Unclear definition of $h_a$

Arguments for acceptance:
- The paper proposes a novel and effective approach to region proposal generation.
- The experimental results are impressive and demonstrate the potential of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Arguments for rejection:
- The optimization process is unclear and may not be optimal.
- The potential issue with the sliding window step size on the image layer may affect the accuracy of the bounding box locations.
- The unclear definition of $h_a$ may cause confusion for readers.
