This paper proposes a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the downstream detection network, achieving state-of-the-art object detection performance on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012 benchmarks. The RPN is a fully-convolutional network that predicts object bounds and objectness scores at each position, and is trained end-to-end to generate high-quality region proposals.

The authors relate their work to previous research on region proposal methods and region-based convolutional neural networks (R-CNNs), highlighting the limitations of existing approaches and the benefits of their proposed method. They also discuss related work on using deep networks for locating class-specific or class-agnostic bounding boxes, and how their approach differs from these methods.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their method, including the architecture of the RPN, the training procedure, and the experimental evaluation. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, achieving state-of-the-art performance on VOC07 and VOC12, with a significant reduction in running time for generating region proposals.

Strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed RPN is a novel and effective approach to region proposal generation, achieving state-of-the-art performance on object detection benchmarks.
* The method is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of existing approaches and the benefits of their proposed method.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the authors provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* The training of RPN and Fast R-CNN are weakly combined by alternating optimization, and there is no convergence proof. This may raise concerns about the stability and robustness of the proposed method.
* While the RPN achieves state-of-the-art performance when combined with Fast R-CNN, its performance as a standalone region proposal method is not significantly better than Selective Search.

Overall, this is a very nice piece of work, which improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN in object detection. The proposed RPN is a novel and effective approach to region proposal generation, and the experimental evaluation demonstrates its effectiveness. However, the authors may want to consider addressing the limitations of their method, including the lack of convergence proof and the performance of the RPN as a standalone region proposal method.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on object detection benchmarks.
* The method is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of existing approaches and the benefits of their proposed method.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the authors provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The training of RPN and Fast R-CNN are weakly combined by alternating optimization, and there is no convergence proof.
* The RPN does not significantly outperform Selective Search as a standalone region proposal method.

Recommendation:

* Accept the paper, but encourage the authors to address the limitations of their method, including the lack of convergence proof and the performance of the RPN as a standalone region proposal method.
