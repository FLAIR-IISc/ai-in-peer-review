This paper presents a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detector and includes two fully connected layers that predict object bounds and objectness scores at each spatial position. The RPN is heuristically combined with Fast R-CNN for object detection and achieves state-of-the-art performance on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012. Because the proposal stage reuses the shared convolutional layers, the time required to generate region proposals is greatly reduced.

Pros:
- Clever sharing of convolutional layers between the RPN and the detection network (Fast R-CNN), which substantially reduces the runtime for generating proposals.
- State-of-the-art object detection performance.
- Clear writing and thorough experimental evaluation.

Cons:
- The training of the RPN and Fast R-CNN is only weakly coupled via alternating optimization, and there is no convergence proof.
- While the RPN can be used independently as a proposal method, it does not appear to outperform Selective Search.

Overall assessment:
This is a strong and impactful contribution that improves both the efficiency and accuracy of R-CNN–style object detection. The idea of sharing convolutional features between the proposal and detection stages is elegant and highly practical, and the empirical results are compelling. My main concern is the way the RPN and Fast R-CNN are combined: the proposed alternating optimization strategy feels heuristic, and the lack of a convergence guarantee leaves open questions about stability and reproducibility. Additionally, as a standalone proposal generator, the RPN does not seem to surpass Selective Search, which slightly tempers the claim of superiority in the proposal stage itself.

Despite these concerns, the work is well executed and clearly presented, and the gains in end-to-end detection are significant.
