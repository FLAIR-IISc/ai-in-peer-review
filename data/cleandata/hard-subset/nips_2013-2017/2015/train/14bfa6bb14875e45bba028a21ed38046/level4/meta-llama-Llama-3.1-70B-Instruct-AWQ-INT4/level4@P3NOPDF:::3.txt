This paper proposes a two-stage object detection algorithm based on convolutional neural networks (CNNs). The first stage is region proposal, which leverages the traditional sliding window method but operates on the top layer feature map of the CNN, referred to as Region Proposal Network (RPN). In the second stage, a Fast R-CNN is applied to the proposed regions. Since the convolutional layers are shared between the RPN and Fast R-CNN, and the computation is accelerated using a GPU, the algorithm achieves near real-time performance (5fps).

However, I have several concerns regarding the proposed approach:

1. The optimization section appears to employ only one iteration of alternating optimization between the RPN and Fast R-CNN. If this is the case, it assumes that the proposals generated by the first iterated RPN are optimal, which is unlikely to be true. Typically, multiple iterations of alternating optimization are required to converge to a satisfactory solution.

2. When the pixel-wise sliding window is applied to the top layer of the CNN, the step size on the image layer is unclear. Due to the multiple rounds of max-pooling in the CNN, the top level operates on a much smaller scale compared to the input image. Consequently, the sliding window on the top level may result in a large step size on the image level, potentially leading to inaccurate bounding box locations. Clarification on this aspect is necessary to ensure the efficacy of the proposed approach.

3. The definition of $h_a$ in lines 173 and 174 is not provided, making it difficult to understand the underlying mathematics. A clear explanation of this term is essential for a comprehensive grasp of the algorithm.

Despite these concerns, the proposed algorithm is a natural extension to existing CNN-based object detection methods. The idea of sharing convolutional layers between the RPN and Fast R-CNN is innovative and contributes to the efficiency of the approach. Nevertheless, addressing the aforementioned concerns is crucial to ensure the validity and effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
