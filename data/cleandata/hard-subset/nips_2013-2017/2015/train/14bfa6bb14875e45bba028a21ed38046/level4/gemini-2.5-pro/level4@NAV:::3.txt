The proposed method builds upon the established foundation of object detection using Convolutional Neural Networks. My main concerns are detailed below.
1.  Regarding the optimization process, the authors appear to use only a single iteration for the alternating training of the RPN and Fast R-CNN. This implies that the proposals generated by the RPN after one pass are considered optimal, an assumption that is unlikely to be valid.
2.  When the sliding window is applied to the final convolutional layer, clarification is needed on its effective stride in the original image space. Due to repeated max-pooling operations, the feature map is at a much coarser resolution than the input image. Consequently, a pixel-wise slide on this map could result in a large step on the original image, potentially leading to inaccurate bounding box localization.
3.  The term $h_a$ is used in lines 173 and 174 without a prior definition. Please clarify its meaning.
This paper introduces a two-stage object detection framework that utilizes a CNN. In the first stage, a Region Proposal Network (RPN) generates candidate regions by adapting the traditional sliding window approach to operate on the top-level feature map of the CNN. The second stage then applies a Fast R-CNN detector to these proposed regions. By sharing the convolutional layers between the RPN and the detection network and leveraging GPU computation, the proposed algorithm achieves a near real-time performance of 5 fps.
