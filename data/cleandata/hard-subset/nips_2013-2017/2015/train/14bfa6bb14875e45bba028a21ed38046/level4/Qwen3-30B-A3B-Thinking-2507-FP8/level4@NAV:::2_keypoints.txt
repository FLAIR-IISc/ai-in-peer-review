- Introduces a region proposal network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with R-CNN to generate proposals.  
- RPN uses two fully connected layers to predict bounding boxes and objectness scores at every spatial location.  
- Heuristically integrates RPN with Fast R-CNN for detection.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art detection results on VOC07 and VOC12.  
- Proposal generation runtime is significantly reduced by sharing convolutional layers between RPN and the detection network.  
- Pros: effective feature sharing substantially accelerates region proposal generation.  
- Pros: achieves strong, state-of-the-art benchmark performance.  
- Pros: paper is clear, well-structured, and experimentally comprehensive.  
- Cons: training uses alternating optimization without theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Cons: as a standalone proposal method, RPN does not outperform selective search.  
- Main concern: the RPN–Fast R-CNN integration is heuristic and lacks theoretical convergence support.
