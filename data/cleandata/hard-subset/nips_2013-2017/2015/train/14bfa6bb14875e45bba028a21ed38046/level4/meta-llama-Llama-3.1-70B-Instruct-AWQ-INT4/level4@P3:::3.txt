This paper proposes a novel Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares full-image convolutional features with the detection network, enabling nearly cost-free region proposals. The authors demonstrate that their method, Faster R-CNN, achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012, while maintaining a frame rate of 5fps on a GPU.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method and its advantages. The use of a fully-convolutional network for region proposal generation is a key contribution of this work. The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of their method, including comparisons with existing region proposal methods and an analysis of the recall-to-IoU metric.

The paper is closely related to previous work on object detection, particularly the Fast R-CNN method [5]. The authors build upon this work by introducing a novel region proposal method that shares convolutional features with the detection network. This approach enables faster and more accurate object detection.

The strengths of this paper include:

* The introduction of a novel region proposal method that shares convolutional features with the detection network.
* A thorough evaluation of the method, including comparisons with existing region proposal methods.
* The achievement of state-of-the-art object detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012, while maintaining a frame rate of 5fps on a GPU.

However, there are some concerns that need to be addressed:

* In the optimization section, it appears that there is only one iteration for the alternating optimization between RPN and R-CNN. This assumes that the proposals generated by the first iterated RPN are optimal, which may not be the case. It would be beneficial to explore multiple iterations of alternating optimization to improve the performance of the method.
* When the pixel-wise sliding window is applied to the top layer of CNN, the step size on the image layer is not clear. Since CNN has several rounds of max-pooling, the top level is in a much smaller scale compared with the input image. The sliding window on the top level may have a big step on the image level, causing inaccurate bounding box locations. It would be helpful to provide more details on how the step size is determined and its impact on the performance of the method.
* The definition of $h_a$ in lines 173 and 174 is not clear. It would be beneficial to provide a clear explanation of this variable and its role in the method.

Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of object detection, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method. However, addressing the concerns mentioned above would strengthen the paper and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method.

* The method achieves state-of-the-art object detection accuracy on PASCAL VOC 2007 and 2012, while maintaining a frame rate of 5fps on a GPU.
