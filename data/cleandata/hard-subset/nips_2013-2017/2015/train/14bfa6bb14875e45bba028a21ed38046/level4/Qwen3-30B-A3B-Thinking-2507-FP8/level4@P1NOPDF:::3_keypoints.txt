- Proposes a two-stage CNN object detection framework using shared convolutional layers between RPN and Fast R-CNN to speed inference.  
- Concern 1: Optimization appears to use a single alternating update iteration between RPN and R-CNN; authors must clarify feasibility or justification for a single iteration.  
- Concern 1 implication: Single-iteration assumes near-optimal initial RPN proposals, which is unlikely without evidence.  
- Concern 2: Sliding window with stride 1 on top feature map maps to a large image-level stride (e.g., 16 pixels) due to pooling, potentially harming bounding-box localization.  
- Concern 2 request: Specify exact image-level stride used or justify why reduced spatial resolution does not degrade localization precision.  
- Concern 3: Definition of h_a (lines 173–174) is missing/unclear, preventing verification of Equation 12 and downstream derivations.  
- Overall: Core idea is logical and 5 FPS claim is plausible, but the above technical details must be addressed to validate robustness.
