

The paper presents a clear objective with solid experimental results. However, several aspects require clarification to enhance readability and technical rigor. Specifically:  
1. The paper claims RPN is translation-invariant, yet [19] (Overfeat) is not. The authors should explicitly explain this distinction, particularly given Overfeat's relevance as a baseline.  
2. Key parameters (e.g., *n*=3, *k*=9) are set manually without justification or ablation studies. The rationale for these choices should be elaborated.  
3. The training procedure appears complex and lacks stability analysis. The authors do not report multiple training runs or systematic exploration of alternative settings (e.g., "Why not apply the same procedure repeatedly? Did the authors test numerous configurations before selecting the current one?").  

The proposed region proposal method—sharing CNN features with the object localization module—is novel. It employs a classification layer (object vs. non-object) and a regression layer (bounding box prediction). By sharing feature computation, the combined architecture achieves ~10× speedup over Fast R-CNN while maintaining slightly higher accuracy. The authors appropriately address the training challenge (where object localization assumes pre-specified regions) by proposing a multi-stage training procedure.
