- Proposes Region Proposal Networks (RPNs) that share CNN features, with classification and regression layers; combined RPN+Fast R-CNN is ~10× faster and slightly more accurate.
- Clear, well-motivated goal to improve object detection efficiency and accuracy.
- Solid experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of RPN.
- Thorough analysis of recall-to-IoU and its relation to final detection accuracy.
- Paper is sometimes hard to follow; explanations (e.g., why RPN is translation-invariant vs. [19]) need clarification.
- Many parameters are manually chosen without justification (e.g., n=3, k=9); more analysis needed.
- Training procedure appears complicated and unstable; robustness should be demonstrated via repeated runs and exploration of settings.
- Novelty: sharing CNN features for region proposals is an original and significant contribution to object detection.
- Quality: generally well-written but needs reorganizing and clearer explanations of terms for non-experts.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/significant method, strong experimental support, potential to improve detection efficiency and accuracy.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear presentation, unanalysed manual parameter choices, and a complex/possibly unstable training procedure.
- Conclusion: promising and significant work with effective results, but requires clarifications and additional analyses to address weaknesses.
