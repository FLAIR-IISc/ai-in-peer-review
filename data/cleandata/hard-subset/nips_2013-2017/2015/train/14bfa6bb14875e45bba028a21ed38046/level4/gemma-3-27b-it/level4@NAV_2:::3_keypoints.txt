- Proposes a Region Proposal Network (RPN) that shares convolutional features with the detector, addressing the region-proposal bottleneck.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art accuracy on PASCAL VOC with a substantial speed improvement (5 fps on VGG-16).  
- Code release improves reproducibility and practical impact.  
- Paper is well-motivated, technically sound, thoroughly evaluated, and clearly written.  
- Main concern: alternating optimization between RPN and Fast R-CNN is under-specified; using a single iteration may leave RPN proposals suboptimal and sensitivity to the schedule should be studied.  
- Main concern: sliding-window on the top conv layer combined with heavy downsampling can produce large image-level step sizes, risking poor bounding-box localization and missed small or dense objects.  
- Suggestion: discuss/analyze receptive field sizes and the effect of spatial resolution on localization accuracy.  
- Minor issue: definition/typo of h_a (lines 173–174) needs clarification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of RPN, significance (speed + accuracy), strong technical results, clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient optimization details, spatial-resolution/localization concerns, and a minor notation typo.
