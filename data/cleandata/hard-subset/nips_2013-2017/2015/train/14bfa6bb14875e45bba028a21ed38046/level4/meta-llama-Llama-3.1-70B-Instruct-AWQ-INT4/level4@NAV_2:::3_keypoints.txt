- Proposes a two-stage CNN object detector: Region Proposal Network (RPN) on top-layer features + Fast R-CNN for detection.  
- RPN uses a sliding-window on the CNN top feature map and shares convolutional layers with Fast R-CNN.  
- Shared conv layers and GPU acceleration yield near real-time performance (~5 fps) and improved accuracy.  
- Builds on SPPnet and Fast R-CNN; introducing RPN is a novel region-proposal approach.  
- Shared convolutional computation is a key factor in the method’s efficiency.  
- Paper is well-written with clear methodology and thorough experimental comparisons.  
- Concern: alternating optimization between RPN and Fast R-CNN appears to use only one iteration and may not be fully converged.  
- Suggestion: evaluate multiple iterations of alternating optimization to test for further improvements.  
- Concern: sliding-window step size on the image layer is not clearly defined given pooling-induced downsampling, possibly causing coarse/incorrect box locations.  
- Concern: definition of h_a (lines 173–174) is unclear and requires clarification.  
- Strengths: novel RPN approach, significant speed/accuracy gains, thorough analysis, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: possible nonconverged optimization, undefined image-level step size, unclear h_a definition.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the above concerns.  
- Rating: 8/10.
