- RPN generates object proposals inside the CNN, sharing convolutional feature maps with Fast R-CNN for nearly cost-free proposals.
- RPN is a fully convolutional network that regresses bounding boxes and predicts objectness scores; authors use a multi-stage training to produce Faster R-CNN.
- System achieves ~10x speed-up over Fast R-CNN while maintaining or slightly improving state-of-the-art mAP on benchmarks like PASCAL VOC.
- Novelty/impact: integrating proposal generation into the detection network is a major step toward end-to-end object detection.
- Computational efficiency: sharing conv layers makes proposal generation extremely efficient and practical for real-time use.
- Strong empirical performance: clear speed gains and robust accuracy improvements across datasets.
- Clarity issue: need a clearer explanation of RPN’s translation invariance and how it differs from Selective Search or sliding-window methods like OverFeat.
- Hyperparameter justification lacking: no ablation/sensitivity analysis for the 3 scales × 3 aspect ratios (k=9 anchors), NMS threshold, and training mini-batch strategy.
- Training complexity/stability concerns: the 4-step alternating training is intricate; authors should justify it, compare to simpler joint training, and report variance/reproducibility across runs.
