- Proposed method: recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that disentangles view-invariant identity and view-dependent pose, with an RNN applying discrete actions to generate rotation sequences and trained via curriculum learning.  
- Originality/significance: novel use of RNN to traverse pose manifold enabling long, coherent multi-view synthesis and emergent weakly-supervised identity/pose disentangling.  
- Quality: thorough experiments on Multi-PIE and synthetic chairs with strong qualitative results, quantitative comparisons (k-NN), and ablation-like studies on curriculum learning.  
- Clarity: paper is clearly written with well-explained architecture and helpful diagrams.  
- Minor weakness — scope: evaluation limited to single, centered objects on clean backgrounds; lacks discussion of failure modes in cluttered or in-the-wild scenes.  
- Minor weakness — baselines: 3D Morphable Model comparison may be compromised by landmark localization errors and not reflect its best-case performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: introduces a well-motivated recurrent architecture, generates long coherent rotations, demonstrates significant emergent disentangling, and provides comprehensive experimental support.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited evaluation scope leaves generalization to complex scenes an open question.  
- Overall: strengths and novel contributions substantially outweigh minor weaknesses; likely to be a high-quality, impactful submission.
