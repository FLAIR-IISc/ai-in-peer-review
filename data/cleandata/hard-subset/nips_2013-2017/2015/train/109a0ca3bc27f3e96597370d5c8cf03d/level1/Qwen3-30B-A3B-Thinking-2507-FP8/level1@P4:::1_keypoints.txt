- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views by traversing a pose manifold via sequential small rotations.
- Model separates identity units (invariant) and pose units (transformable) with action units controlling rotations at each time step.
- Uses curriculum learning to progressively increase training sequence length, improving long-term synthesis and disentanglement.
- Builds on prior work (Transforming Autoencoder, Inverse Graphics Network, Predictive Gating Pyramid) while adding action inputs and recurrence.
- Demonstrates strong technical soundness with clear ablation studies across curriculum stages (RNN1 to RNN16).
- Quantitative results show improved image synthesis (chairs: 310 MSE -> 179 MSE for RNN16) and better cross-view recognition (RNN6 > RNN1 by 10% on Multi-PIE).
- Novelty: recurrent structure for long-term pose manifold traversal and weakly-supervised disentanglement without class labels.
- Empirically shows identity units encode identity while pose units are near-random for identity recognition, indicating disentanglement.
- Significance: addresses single-image 3D view synthesis with applications in photo editing and vision-based RL; shows class interpolation and novel view synthesis.
- Clarity: well-written paper with clear figures, precise math, and thorough experimental details.
- Limited scope: evaluated only on faces and chairs, not on complex scenes or articulated objects.
- Baseline comparisons could be more equitable (e.g., VGG-16 pretraining vs. task-specific training concerns).
- Limited theoretical grounding: lacks deeper analysis of why recurrence yields better disentanglement or prevents information leakage.
- Pro-acceptance: novel architecture, weakly-supervised disentanglement, strong quantitative results on two datasets, clear methodology, practical applications.
- Con-acceptance: limited object classes tested, potentially unfair baselines, and limited theoretical justification.
- Overall assessment: strong technical contribution that advances 3D view synthesis and representation learning, meeting NIPS quality criteria.
