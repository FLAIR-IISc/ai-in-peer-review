The paper "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis" proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing 3D views of objects from a single image. The model is trained end-to-end on the task of rendering rotated objects starting from a single image, and it demonstrates the quality of its predictions for human faces on the Multi-PIE dataset and for a dataset of 3D chair models.

The main contributions of this work are the development of a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for learning to apply out-of-plane rotations to human faces and 3D chair models, the generation of realistic rotation trajectories with a control signal supplied at each step by the user, and the learning of discriminative view-invariant features without using class labels. The proposed curriculum training by gradually increasing trajectory length of training sequences yields both better image appearance and more discriminative features for pose-invariant recognition.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their model and its components. The experiments are thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner. The paper also provides a good discussion of related work and the contributions of the proposed model.
