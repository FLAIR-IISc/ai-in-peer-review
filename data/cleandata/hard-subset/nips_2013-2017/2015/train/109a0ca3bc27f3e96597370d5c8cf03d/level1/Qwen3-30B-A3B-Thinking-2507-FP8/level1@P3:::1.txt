

# Peer Review: "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis"

This paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for 3D view synthesis from single images, specifically targeting rotation of faces and chairs. The key innovation lies in modeling pose manifold traversal as a sequence of small rotation steps using a recurrent structure with action inputs, enabling long-term dependency learning. The authors demonstrate that their approach achieves weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose factors without explicit class labels, which emerges naturally from the training objective.

The work builds on several prior NIPS contributions. It relates to the transforming autoencoder [12] but replaces capsules with convolutional networks and adds recurrent structure. It extends Predictive Gating Pyramid [19] by incorporating disentangled latent features and control signals. It differs from IGN [17] by using recurrent networks for trajectory generation rather than a VAE framework. The weakly-supervised disentangling also connects to bilinear models [24] and disBM [21], though this paper achieves disentangling without explicit regularization.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel recurrent architecture:** The recurrent pose units with action inputs provide an elegant solution to the long-term dependency problem in pose manifold traversal. The curriculum learning strategy (gradually increasing trajectory length) is well-motivated and effective, as evidenced by the improved performance across RNN stages.

2. **Strong empirical results:** The model achieves high-quality view synthesis for both faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs (CAD models), with visually convincing results. The quantitative MSE comparison (RNN16: ~179 vs. KNN5: ~310) demonstrates significant improvement over the baseline.

3. **Impressive weakly-supervised disentangling:** The most notable contribution is the emergence of disentangled identity and pose representations without explicit regularization. The cross-view recognition results (e.g., RNN6 maintaining ~85% accuracy at 90° offset vs. ~60% for RNN1) provide compelling evidence of the model's ability to learn pose-invariant representations.

4. **Comprehensive evaluation:** The paper thoroughly evaluates the model across multiple dimensions: view synthesis quality, disentangled feature discriminative power, and class interpolation capabilities. The chair interpolation results (Figure 9) are particularly compelling.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited theoretical justification:** While the recurrent structure is well-motivated by the need to handle long-term dependencies, the paper lacks theoretical analysis explaining why this approach is superior to alternatives (e.g., a non-recurrent network with a single large rotation step). The comparison to non-recurrent baselines would strengthen the argument.

2. **Arbitrary action encoding:** The 1-of-3 action encoding (clockwise, noop, counter-clockwise) seems somewhat arbitrary. A more principled approach to action representation (e.g., using continuous rotation angles) would be more elegant and generalizable.

3. **Limited comparison to related work:** For the chair dataset, the KNN baseline is appropriate but the comparison to IGN [17] and other recent view synthesis methods (e.g., [9], [25]) would be valuable. The paper mentions IGN but doesn't directly compare to it.

4. **Failure cases and limitations:** The paper doesn't thoroughly discuss limitations or failure modes. For example, how does the model perform with extreme rotations or complex chair structures? The VGG-16 outperforming RNN16 at large angle offsets (>120°) for chairs suggests potential limitations that warrant discussion.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the field of 3D view synthesis. The core technical contribution—the recurrent architecture for pose manifold traversal with weakly-supervised disentangling—is novel and well-executed. The empirical results are compelling across two distinct datasets, and the weakly-supervised disentangling is particularly noteworthy as it emerges naturally from the training objective.

The paper addresses a significant challenge in computer vision: synthesizing novel views from single images without explicit 3D models. The approach has potential applications in photo editing, virtual reality, and vision-based reinforcement learning. The demonstration of class interpolation (Figure 9) further highlights the model's generative capabilities.

While the paper has some minor weaknesses (primarily limited theoretical justification and action representation), these do not substantially undermine the overall contribution. The work is technically sound, well-motivated, and makes a clear advance over prior approaches for the specific problem of 3D view synthesis with weakly-supervised disentangling.

**Recommendation:** Accept. This paper presents a novel and effective approach to 3D view synthesis with significant contributions to both the technical approach (recurrent pose manifold traversal) and the theoretical understanding of weakly-supervised disentangling. The results are robust, the methodology is sound, and the significance of the contribution is clear. The paper would be a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings.
