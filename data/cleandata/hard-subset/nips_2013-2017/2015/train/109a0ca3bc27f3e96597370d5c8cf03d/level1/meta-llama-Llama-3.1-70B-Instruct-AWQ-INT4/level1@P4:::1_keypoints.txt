- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing 3D views from a single image.  
- Trained end-to-end to render rotated objects step-by-step; evaluated on Multi-PIE (faces) and a 3D chair dataset.  
- Main contributions: recurrent model for out-of-plane rotations, user-controlled rotation trajectories via per-step control signal, and weakly-supervised disentangling to learn view-invariant features without class labels.  
- Curriculum training (gradually increasing trajectory length) improves image quality and yields more discriminative pose-invariant features.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of model components and thorough experiments.  
- Technically sound; experiments are comprehensive and results are clearly presented.  
- Minor issue: unclear how the 1-of-3 encoding for action units is used in the model.  
- Minor issue: no justification or explanation for choosing tradeoff parameter λ = 0.1 for mask prediction loss.  
- Some figures are not clearly labeled and figure captions could be more detailed.  
- Originality: introduces a novel recurrent encoder-decoder approach and curriculum strategy for view synthesis.  
- Significance: addresses an important computer vision problem, achieves state-of-the-art on two datasets, and has broad application potential (robotics, AV, VR).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, strong empirical results, effective curriculum training, and practical significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear action encoding, unexplained hyperparameter choice, and figure clarity issues.  
- Overall recommendation from reviewer: accept.
