- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views from a single image by modeling sequences of transformations.
- Network captures long-term dependencies and applies a control signal at each step to generate realistic rotation trajectories.
- Trained end-to-end to render rotated objects from one image without requiring explicit 3D model recovery.
- Main contributions: novel recurrent architecture, controlled rotation trajectory generation, and learning view-invariant features without class labels.
- Introduces a curriculum training procedure that gradually increases trajectory length to improve performance.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Multi-PIE (faces) and a 3D chair CAD dataset with accurate renderings across repeated rotations.
- Experimental results are strong and often competitive with or better than state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., 3D morphable models).
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and has a comprehensive related work section.
- Strength: RNN formulation effectively models pose-manifold traversal and long-term dependencies.
- Strength: Curriculum training improves both image prediction and pose-invariant recognition.
- Limitation: Assumes objects are isolated against a white background, which reduces real-world applicability.
- Limitation: Trained only on limited object classes (faces and chairs); generalization to other classes is unclear.
- Limitation: No explicit regularization for disentangling; reliance on rotation prediction objective may not be robust across scenarios.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; authors should address limitations and provide additional robustness experiments.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
