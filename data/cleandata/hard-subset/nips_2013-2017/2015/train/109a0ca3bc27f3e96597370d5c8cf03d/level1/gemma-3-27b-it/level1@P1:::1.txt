This paper proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for 3D view synthesis from a single image, specifically focusing on faces and chairs. The core idea is to model pose manifold traversal using a recurrent network, where each step applies a small rotation controlled by an “action unit”. The model learns to disentangle identity and pose factors without explicit label supervision. This work builds on a significant body of research in both graphics and vision, including 3D morphable models [5], view synthesis via multi-view stereo, rendering with explicit 3D models [8, 17], and disentangled representations [21, 24, 29].  Recent advancements like the Inverse Graphics Network [17] and transforming autoencoders [12] tackle similar problems of inferring 3D structure and applying transformations, but often rely on different architectures (capsules) or frameworks (VAE).  The use of a recurrent network to handle long-term dependencies in the rotation sequence, and the weak supervision for disentangling factors, are key contributions.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Approach:** The recurrent structure for modeling pose manifold traversal is a clever idea, addressing the challenge of accumulating errors over multiple rotation steps.
*   **Weakly-Supervised Disentangling:** Learning disentangled representations without explicit labels is a significant achievement and demonstrates the model's ability to capture meaningful latent factors.
*   **Good Experimental Results:**  The paper demonstrates competitive results on both face and chair datasets, comparing favorably against baselines like KNN and 3D morphable models. The visualizations of generated views appear plausible.
*   **Curriculum Learning:** The use of curriculum learning is well-justified and contributes to the stability and performance of the model.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and the architecture is clearly explained with a diagram (Figure 1).

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty in Architecture:** While the *combination* of recurrent networks and convolutional encoders/decoders for this specific task is novel, the individual components are fairly standard. The paper doesn’t introduce any fundamentally new building blocks.
*   **Ablation Studies:** The paper lacks comprehensive ablation studies. It would be beneficial to understand the contribution of each component – the recurrent structure, the action units, the disentangled latent space – more rigorously. For example, how does performance degrade when the recurrence is removed?
*   **Comparison with State-of-the-Art:**  While comparisons are made, they aren't always with the most relevant and up-to-date methods.  For example, comparing to more recent generative models, or more sophisticated view synthesis techniques, could strengthen the analysis. The comparison to the 3D morphable model is a bit dated.
*   **Qualitative Results Could Be Stronger:** While the visualizations are good, a more systematic evaluation of the generated images would be valuable. Metrics like FID or IS could provide a quantitative assessment of image quality.
*   **Discussion of Failure Cases:** The paper lacks a discussion of failure cases. What types of rotations or objects are particularly challenging for the model? Analyzing these limitations would provide valuable insights.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper doesn't discuss the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameters, like learning rate or network depth.




**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses an important and challenging problem in both computer graphics and computer vision.
*   The proposed approach is novel and demonstrates promising results on challenging datasets.
*   The weakly-supervised disentangling aspect is particularly interesting and potentially impactful.
*   The experimental validation is reasonably thorough and demonstrates the effectiveness of the method.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

*   The architectural novelty is relatively incremental.
*   The lack of thorough ablation studies weakens the analysis of the model's components.
*   The comparison with state-of-the-art methods could be more comprehensive.
*   The lack of discussion of failure cases limits the understanding of the model's limitations.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong submission** with a solid contribution to the field. The proposed recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network offers a compelling approach to 3D view synthesis and demonstrates promising results. While the architecture isn't groundbreaking, the combination of techniques and the weakly-supervised disentangling aspect are significant.  The weaknesses are primarily related to the lack of deeper analysis (ablation studies) and more comprehensive comparisons.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The authors should address the weaknesses by adding ablation studies, expanding the comparison to state-of-the-art methods, and providing a more detailed discussion of failure cases.




**Specific Suggestions:**

*   **Add ablation studies:** Remove the recurrent structure and compare the results. Remove the action units and see how that affects performance.
*   **Expand the comparison:** Include comparisons with more recent generative models and view synthesis techniques.
*   **Include quantitative image quality metrics:**  Report FID or IS scores for the generated images.
*   **Discuss failure cases:** Analyze examples where the model fails and provide potential explanations.
*   **Investigate hyperparameter sensitivity:** Briefly discuss the impact of different hyperparameter settings on the results.
