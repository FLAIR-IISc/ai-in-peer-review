- Summary: Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that models 3D view synthesis as sequential small rotations and learns disentangled identity/pose representations in a weakly-supervised way, demonstrated on Multi-PIE faces and chair CAD models.

Strengths:
- Technical soundness and quality: Recurrent structure and curriculum learning effectively handle long-term pose traversal and produce convincing disentanglement without explicit supervision.
- Originality: Introduces action units in a recurrent architecture for longer rotation trajectories, extending prior generative and view-invariant work.
- Significance: Addresses an important problem with strong qualitative and quantitative results, including interpolation between object classes.
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized with clear model diagrams and explanations, making the method accessible.

Weaknesses:
- Limited scope of evaluation: Experiments are restricted to faces and chairs and do not assess performance on more complex objects or scenes.
- Comparison to recent methods: Lacks comparisons to newer generative approaches (e.g., GANs, advanced neural rendering) that emerged after 2015.
- Analysis of disentanglement: Provides limited investigation into the latent space structure beyond basic pose-invariance metrics.
- Handling symmetric objects: Performance drops on symmetric chairs at large angle offsets and the paper does not explore incorporating symmetry priors.

Recommendation:
- Overall recommendation: Accept — solid, novel contribution with strong experimental validation despite some evaluation gaps.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel recurrent architecture tailored for long-term pose manifold traversal.
- Demonstrated weakly-supervised disentangling without explicit labels or regularization.
- Strong experimental results on two challenging datasets with quantitative evaluations.
- Effective curriculum learning strategy and clear methodological presentation.
- Relevant to conference topics in generative models and representation learning.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Evaluation is limited to relatively simple datasets and scenarios.
- Missing comparisons to more recent and advanced generative/view-synthesis methods.
- Insufficient deep analysis of what the model has disentangled in the latent space.

Overall assessment:
- Strong, well-written paper making a meaningful technical contribution to view synthesis and representation learning; recommended for acceptance with noted areas for expanded evaluation and analysis.
