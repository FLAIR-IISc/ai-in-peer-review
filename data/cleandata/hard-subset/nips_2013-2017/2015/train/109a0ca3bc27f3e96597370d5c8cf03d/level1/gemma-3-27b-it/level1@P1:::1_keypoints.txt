- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder for single-image 3D view synthesis (faces and chairs).  
- Models pose manifold traversal with a recurrent network applying small rotations controlled by an “action unit.”  
- Learns to disentangle identity and pose factors using weak supervision (no explicit labels).  
- Builds on prior work in 3D morphable models, multi-view rendering, inverse graphics, and disentangled representations.  
- Recurrence addresses long-term dependencies and accumulation of rotation errors.  
- Recurrent approach for pose traversal is a clever and useful idea.  
- Individual components (conv encoders/decoders, recurrence) are standard; architectural novelty is incremental.  
- Weakly-supervised disentangling is a significant and interesting contribution.  
- Demonstrates competitive experimental results on face and chair datasets versus KNN and 3DMM baselines.  
- Curriculum learning is well-motivated and improves training stability/performance.  
- Paper is clearly written and includes a helpful architecture diagram.  
- Lacks comprehensive ablation studies to quantify each component’s contribution (recurrence, action units, disentangling).  
- Comparisons omit some recent state-of-the-art generative/view-synthesis methods; 3DMM baseline is somewhat dated.  
- Qualitative results are good but need systematic quantitative evaluation (e.g., FID, IS).  
- No discussion or analysis of failure cases and challenging rotations/objects.  
- Does not report sensitivity to hyperparameters (learning rate, network depth, etc.).  
- Pro-acceptance points: important problem, promising and novel approach, strong weakly-supervised disentangling, reasonable experiments.  
- Con-acceptance points: incremental architectural novelty, insufficient ablation and comparisons, missing failure analysis.  
- Overall: strong submission recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to deepen analysis.  
- Specific suggested revisions: add ablations (remove recurrence/action units), expand SOTA comparisons, report image-quality metrics (FID/IS), analyze failure cases, and test hyperparameter sensitivity.
