- Problem & method: Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that disentangles identity (static) and pose (dynamic) latent units and iteratively updates pose via an action signal to synthesize multi-step novel views from a single image.

- Training & results: Trained end-to-end with curriculum learning on rotated image sequences; shows strong qualitative and quantitative results on Multi-PIE faces and rendered chairs.

- Emergent disentangling: Learning sequential transformations yields weakly-supervised disentangled identity features that perform competitively for view-invariant recognition without identity labels.

- Novelty & elegance: Nicely integrates convolutional encoder-decoders with an RNN manipulating a factored latent space for controlled multi-step image generation.

- Experimental thoroughness: Comprehensive evaluation including visual examples, k-NN baseline comparison, cross-view recognition tests, and curriculum learning ablation.

- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear diagrams and transparent discussion of limitations and failure cases.

- Limitation — transformation scope: Demonstrated only on discrete out-of-plane yaw rotations; unclear how it extends to continuous angles or more complex transforms (elevation, lighting, non-rigid).

- Limitation — determinism: Deterministic generation ignores inherent ambiguity (multiple plausible occluded views); lacks probabilistic modeling of uncertainty.

- Limitation — dataset alignment: Experiments use well-centered/cropped data, limiting claims about applicability to in-the-wild images with clutter and background variation.

- Acceptance arguments: Presents a novel, well-motivated architecture, significant weakly-supervised disentangling result, and strong empirical validation likely to inspire follow-up work.

- Rejection arguments: Limited transformation types and deterministic output are notable but minor issues that suggest directions for future work.

- Overall verdict: Strong, impactful contribution with minor limitations; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
