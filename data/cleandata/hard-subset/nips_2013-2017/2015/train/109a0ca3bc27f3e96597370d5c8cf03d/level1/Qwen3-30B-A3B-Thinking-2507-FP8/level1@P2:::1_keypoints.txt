- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for synthesizing 3D views from a single image, focusing on sequential rotations.  
- Introduces curriculum learning to progressively train on longer rotation trajectories (RNN1→RNN16).  
- Curriculum training enables high-quality long-term view synthesis and emergent disentanglement of identity and pose without explicit regularization.  
- Thorough experimental validation on Multi-PIE (faces) and CAD chairs, with qualitative results demonstrating synthesis, disentangling, and interpolation.  
- Quantitative results (MSE) show RNN16 outperforms KNN baselines by ~42%.  
- Model is well-motivated for long-term dependency issues and architecture is clearly described with reproducible details.  
- Paper lacks deeper theoretical justification for why recurrence is necessary and how it avoids manifold drift in sequential prediction.  
- Clarity and organization are excellent; figures and experimental setup are detailed and reproducible.  
- Originality lies in combining recurrent structure, curriculum learning, and weakly-supervised disentanglement for view synthesis.  
- Demonstrated generality across very different object classes (faces and chairs) and potential applications in graphics and recognition.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of failure cases (e.g., complex chair shapes) and imprecise qualification of "weakly-supervised disentangling."  
- Comparison to the most related work (IGN) is limited, and computational efficiency vs. alternatives is not explored.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — technically strong, novel, well-validated, and of clear significance to vision and graphics.
