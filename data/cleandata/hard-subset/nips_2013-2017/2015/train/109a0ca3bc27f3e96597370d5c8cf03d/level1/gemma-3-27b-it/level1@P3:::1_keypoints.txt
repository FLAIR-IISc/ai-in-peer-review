- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that models 3D view synthesis from a single image by traversing the pose manifold with recurrent connections.  
- Focuses experiments on faces and chairs, using recurrence to maintain long-term dependencies across rotation sequences.  
- Trained end-to-end and shows weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose without explicit supervision.  
- Integrates a control/action signal for sequential transformations, related to IGN and PGP but novel in this specific combination.  
- Uses curriculum learning to help with long-term dependency learning.  
- Architecture choice (CNN + RNN within encoder-decoder) is well-motivated and technically sound.  
- Qualitative results show improved view synthesis; quantitative gains demonstrated in cross-view recognition versus KNN baseline.  
- Quantitative comparisons are limited and should include stronger modern baselines (e.g., recent generative models or differentiable renderers).  
- Ablation studies are limited; unclear how much each component (recurrence, curriculum) contributes to performance.  
- Texture synthesis quality is weak compared to shape/pose manipulation success.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized; key equations and diagrams are clear and helpful.  
- Some explanations (e.g., action units) need more intuitive detail and latent space sizes should be stated in the text.  
- Novelty lies in combining recurrent networks with convolutional encoder-decoders for controlled 3D rotations and weakly-supervised disentangling.  
- Significance is high: advances single-image 3D understanding with applications in graphics, AR, and robotics; code release would increase impact.  
- Missing discussion on failure cases, computational cost, and scalability.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on strengthening baseline comparisons and expanding ablation studies in revision.
