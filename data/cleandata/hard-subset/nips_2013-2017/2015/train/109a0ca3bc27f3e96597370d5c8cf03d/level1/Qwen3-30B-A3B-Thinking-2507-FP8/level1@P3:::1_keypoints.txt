- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that models pose manifold traversal as a sequence of small rotation steps with action inputs.  
- Uses curriculum learning (increasing trajectory length) to improve long-term dependency learning.  
- Demonstrates weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose emerging without explicit labels or regularization.  
- Strong empirical view synthesis on Multi-PIE (faces) and CAD (chairs) with notable quantitative gains (e.g., RNN16 MSE ~179 vs KNN5 ~310).  
- Cross-view recognition shows robust pose-invariant identity features (e.g., RNN6 maintains ~85% at 90° offset vs ~60% for RNN1).  
- Evaluation is comprehensive: synthesis quality, discriminative power, and class interpolation (chair interpolation particularly compelling).  
- Lacks theoretical justification for why the recurrent approach is superior to non-recurrent alternatives; needs baseline comparisons.  
- The 1-of-3 discrete action encoding (cw/noop/ccw) is arbitrary; a continuous rotation representation would be more principled.  
- Limited direct comparisons to related recent methods (e.g., IGN and other view-synthesis works) for the chair dataset.  
- Insufficient discussion of failure cases and limitations (e.g., extreme rotations, complex chair structures); VGG-16 outperforming at >120° suggests limits.  
- Overall: technically sound, novel contribution with strong results and emergent disentangling; recommendation—Accept.
