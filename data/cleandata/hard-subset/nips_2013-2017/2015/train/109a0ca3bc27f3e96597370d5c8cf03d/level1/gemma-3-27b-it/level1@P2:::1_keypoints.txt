- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for single-image 3D view synthesis using action units to control pose manifold traversal.
- Trained end-to-end on Multi-PIE (faces) and chairs, showing rotated view generation and emergent disentangled representations.
- Connects to graphics rendering, conv-net image generation, and recurrent sequence modeling, and cites relevant prior work.
- Novel combination of recurrent structure with conv encoders/decoders and action units is a clever and interesting idea.
- Network architecture and curriculum learning are well-motivated to handle long-term dependencies.
- Experiments use standard datasets and baselines (KNN, CNN) and include qualitative and quantitative results.
- Qualitative outputs look plausible; quantitative results indicate disentanglement and competitive performance.
- Emergence of disentangled pose vs. identity features without supervision is a noteworthy finding.
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly motivated, and sufficiently detailed.
- Baseline comparisons are limited; should compare to 3D morphable models and recent generative models (e.g., GANs).
- Insufficient detail on curriculum design and sensitivity to hyperparameter schedules.
- Computational cost and training resource requirements are not discussed.
- Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of recurrence, action units, and curriculum learning.
- Evaluation relies heavily on visualization; needs quantitative image-quality metrics (e.g., SSIM/PSNR).
- For acceptance: novel, technically sound, promising results, and clear presentation addressing a challenging problem.
- Against acceptance: limited baselines, missing hyperparameter/computational analysis, absent ablations, and over-reliance on qualitative evaluation.
- Overall: solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add baselines, curriculum details, ablations, and quantitative metrics.
