This paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for 3D view synthesis, specifically for faces and chairs. The network is trained end-to-end to render rotated objects starting from a single image, and is able to capture long-term dependencies along a sequence of transformations. The authors demonstrate the quality of the network's predictions on the Multi-PIE dataset and a dataset of 3D chair models, and show its ability to disentangle latent data factors without using object class labels.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method, related work, and experimental results. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the network's performance, including quantitative and qualitative evaluations. The use of curriculum training to gradually increase the difficulty of the training sequences is a nice touch, and the results show that this approach improves both the image prediction performance and the pose-invariant recognition performance of the identity features.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to tackle a challenging problem in computer vision, namely 3D view synthesis, and to provide a novel solution that is both effective and efficient. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the related work, including both traditional computer vision methods and deep learning-based approaches.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. One potential weakness is that the network is specifically designed for faces and chairs, and it is not clear how well it would generalize to other object classes. Additionally, the authors could provide more analysis of the network's performance on more challenging datasets, such as those with varying lighting conditions or complex backgrounds.
