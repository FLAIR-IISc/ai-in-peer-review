- Proposed recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to render object views from a single 2D image; evaluated on faces and chairs and includes experiments on curriculum learning, disentangled representations (cross-view recognition), and chair class interpolation.  
- Evaluation is largely qualitative and inadequate; the paper needs quantitative metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Figures/visualizations should be scaled larger for readability.  
- Paper is generally well written and easy to follow but has minor grammatical errors (e.g., "rotate" → "rotated").  
- Experiments are limited to Multi-PIE and Chairs; more diverse datasets are needed to demonstrate generality.  
- Should test generalization to non-static-axis or non-repeating rotation trajectories.  
- Novel and interesting approach, but its significance is undermined by insufficient experimentation and analysis.  
- Lacks sufficient comparison to state-of-the-art geometry-based approaches.
