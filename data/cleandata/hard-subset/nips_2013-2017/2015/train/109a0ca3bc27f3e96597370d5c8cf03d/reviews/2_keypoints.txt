- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects, with encoder disentangling identity and pose, action units controlling pose, and decoder reconstructing rotated image and mask.  
- Extends to a recurrent architecture that keeps identity fixed and updates pose via a sequence of action units to support longer rotation trajectories.  
- Main contribution: unsupervised disentangling of identity/appearance and pose, yielding identity units that act as discriminative, view-invariant features for cross-view recognition.  
- Disentangling and curriculum training improve rendering quality and prediction accuracy for longer rotations.  
- Trained end-to-end; qualitative renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition results support the method.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and explanation of components.  
- Weakness: model supports only discrete rotation angles determined by training data; no method for continuous-angle support is provided.  
- Weakness: network is trained with fixed-length sequences, which conflicts with typical recurrent network flexibility.
