This paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for 3D view synthesis from a single image. The model is trained end-to-end on the task of rendering rotated objects starting from a single image, and it demonstrates the quality of its predictions for human faces and 3D chair models.

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The model is a complete piece of work, and the authors are careful about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the work.

**Originality**: The paper proposes a novel model for 3D view synthesis, which is a significant contribution to the field. The use of a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network with action units is a new approach to this problem. The paper also demonstrates the ability of the model to disentangle latent data factors without using object class labels, which is a notable achievement.

**Significance**: The results of the paper are important and have the potential to impact the field of computer vision and graphics. The ability to synthesize novel views of objects from a single image is a challenging problem, and the proposed model provides a promising solution. The paper also demonstrates the potential of the model for other applications, such as cross-view object recognition and class interpolation.
