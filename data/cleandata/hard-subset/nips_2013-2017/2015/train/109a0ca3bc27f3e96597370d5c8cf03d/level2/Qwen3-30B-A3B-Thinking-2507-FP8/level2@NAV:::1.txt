

This paper proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for 3D view synthesis from single images, with a focus on weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose factors. The main claims are: (1) a novel recurrent architecture that captures long-term dependencies for pose manifold traversal, (2) the ability to generate realistic rotation trajectories with stepwise control signals, and (3) the emergence of view-invariant identity features without explicit class labels.

The paper provides strong experimental support for these claims. For the Multi-PIE face dataset, the authors demonstrate high-quality rotation synthesis with smooth transitions between viewpoints, outperforming a 3D morphable model baseline in robustness. On the chair dataset, their RNN16 model achieves 42% lower MSE than the best KNN baseline. Crucially, the cross-view recognition experiments on both datasets (Figure 8) demonstrate that identity features become increasingly view-invariant with curriculum training, with RNN6 achieving 10% performance gap reduction between 15° and 90° angle offsets. The class interpolation results (Figure 9) further validate the disentangling property.

The work shows solid field knowledge, correctly positioning itself against related approaches like IGN, Predictive Gating Pyramid, and disentangling models. The novelty lies in the recurrent structure for long-term pose trajectory learning and the emergent weakly-supervised disentangling without explicit regularization, which is particularly impressive.

The paper is well-organized and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility. However, the authors could better contextualize their weakly-supervised disentangling claim by comparing against other unsupervised disentangling methods like those in [6] or [21]. The limited scope to faces and chairs (while reasonable for a conference paper) should be explicitly acknowledged as a limitation.

The significance is strong: the method enables novel view synthesis from single images without 3D models, with practical applications in graphics and vision. The quality is excellent with thorough experiments and clear ablation studies.

This is a high-quality contribution that advances the state of the art in view synthesis and disentangled representations. The recurrent architecture for pose manifold traversal is particularly elegant, and the emergent disentangling without explicit regularization is a notable achievement. I recommend acceptance.
