The paper "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis" proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for 3D view synthesis from a single image. The authors tackle the challenging problem of predicting transformed appearances of an object when it is rotated in 3D. The proposed approach is weakly-supervised, meaning it does not require explicit 3D model recovery or object class labels.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise overview of the problem, related work, and their proposed approach. The introduction effectively sets the stage for the rest of the paper, and the related work section provides a thorough review of existing methods.

The proposed recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network is a significant contribution to the field. The authors' use of a recurrent structure to capture long-term dependencies along a sequence of transformations is a novel and effective approach. The incorporation of action units to model the process of pose manifold traversal is also a key innovation.

The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach on both face and chair datasets. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method, including comparisons to state-of-the-art approaches and an analysis of the disentangling property of their model.

The paper's strengths include:

* A clear and concise presentation of the problem and proposed approach
* A thorough review of related work
* A novel and effective approach to 3D view synthesis
* Impressive experimental results
* A thorough evaluation of the proposed method

The paper's weaknesses include:

* The authors could provide more detail on the implementation of their method, such as the specific architecture of the encoder and decoder networks
* The paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the disentangling property of the proposed model
* The authors could provide more context on the significance of their results, such as how they compare to other state-of-the-art approaches in the field

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a significant contribution to the field of 3D view synthesis. The authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their approach, and the experimental results are impressive.
