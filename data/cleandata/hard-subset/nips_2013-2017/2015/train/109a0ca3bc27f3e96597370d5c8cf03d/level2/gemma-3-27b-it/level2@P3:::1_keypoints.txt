- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize 3D views from a single image, focusing on faces and chairs and modeling pose traversal via recurrence.  
- Trains end-to-end and reports that the recurrent structure helps capture long-term dependencies and can disentangle latent factors without class labels.  
- Well-motivated problem at the graphics/vision intersection and a reasonable, potentially effective use of recurrence for pose changes.  
- Curriculum learning is used and experimental results on Multi-PIE and a chair dataset show qualitative improvements and quantitative gains in cross-view recognition.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive; comparisons to a KNN baseline and a 3D morphable model (for faces) strengthen evaluation.  
- Encoder/decoder architecture details (layers, filter sizes, etc.) are buried and should be presented more clearly, e.g., in a summary table.  
- The 1-of-3 encoding for action units seems arbitrary and needs justification or exploration of alternatives.  
- Ablation studies are limited; the impact of removing the recurrent component and sensitivity to curriculum schedule are not analyzed.  
- Disentanglement claim is under-supported; requires more direct evaluation of independence between identity and pose representations.  
- The explanation for VGG-16 outperforming the RNN at large chair angles is incomplete; further analysis of chair symmetries and their effect on performance is needed.  
- Paper lacks error analysis and examples of failure cases; limitations are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Notation (especially equation (1)) could be improved for clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a challenging problem, introduces a novel recurrent approach, demonstrates strong results on two datasets, and shows noteworthy unsupervised disentanglement.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear architectural presentation, insufficient ablation and disentanglement analyses, and missing error/limitation discussion.  
- Overall: significant contribution and likely acceptable, but a major revision is recommended to add ablation studies and more rigorous disentanglement and failure-case analyses.
