- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder with action units to synthesize rotated 3D views from a single image without explicit 3D reconstruction.  
- Leverages recurrence to capture long-term pose manifold traversal and controlled incremental rotations.  
- Reports emergent disentangling of identity and pose factors despite training only for view synthesis.  
- Combines convolutional architectures, recurrence, and action-driven transforms in a novel integration.  
- Demonstrates realistic rotation results on Multi-PIE faces and a chair dataset with useful visualizations and a 3DMM comparison.  
- Uses curriculum learning to stabilize training and improve performance.  
- Lacks detailed ablation studies to isolate the contributions of recurrence, action units, and curriculum learning.  
- Quantitative evaluation is limited (primarily MSE); stronger perceptual metrics (SSIM, LPIPS) are needed.  
- Comparison to recent generative models for 3D view synthesis is limited.  
- Claim of disentangling needs stronger justification and deeper latent-space analysis beyond cross-view recognition.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear, but some hyperparameter and implementation details are missing for reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and novel work deserving acceptance with minor revisions (more ablations, robust metrics, and stronger disentangling evidence).
