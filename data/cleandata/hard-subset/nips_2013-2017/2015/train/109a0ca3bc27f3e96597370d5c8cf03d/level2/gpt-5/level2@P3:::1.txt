Summary
The paper addresses single-image novel view synthesis for object classes (faces and chairs) by learning to traverse a pose manifold using a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder. An encoder maps an input image to two latent partitions: identity units (time-invariant) and pose units (time-evolving). Action inputs (clockwise/no-op/counterclockwise) select linear operators that update pose units over multiple steps; a decoder renders images from identity and pose at each step. Training uses an L2 pixel loss with backpropagation through time and a curriculum that increases sequence length, encouraging long-horizon consistency and stronger disentanglement. Experiments on Multi-PIE faces and a large chair CAD dataset show multi-step view synthesis, emergent identity/pose disentanglement (assessed via cross-view recognition), and interpolation across identities. Comparisons include qualitative results versus a 3D morphable model for faces and quantitative MSE versus a KNN baseline for chairs.

Relation to prior work
The approach builds on transforming autoencoders [Hinton et al. 2011], gating models for learning image transformations and temporal dependencies [Memisevic/Michalski et al.], and class-conditional generators for chairs [Dosovitskiy et al. 2015]. It is closely related to the contemporary Inverse Graphics Network (IGN) [Kulkarni et al. 2015], which learns a graphics code via a VAE but does not perform recurrent, action-conditioned trajectories. It also connects to disentangling methods (bilinear models [Tenenbaum & Freeman 2000], disBM [Reed et al. 2014], Multi-view Perceptron [Zhu et al. 2014]) but emphasizes disentanglement emerging from sequence prediction rather than explicit regularizers. Compared to DeepStereo [Flynn et al. 2015], the focus here is single-image object-centric synthesis rather than multi-view scene synthesis.

Strengths
- Clear architectural formulation of action-conditioned recurrent pose updates with shared identity units, trained end-to-end.
- Well-motivated curriculum learning that demonstrably improves long-horizon synthesis and disentanglement.
- Compelling qualitative results for both faces and chairs; smooth multi-step rotations and identity preservation.
- Quantitative improvements on chairs versus a KNN baseline (MSE reduction ~42% at 16 steps).
- Evidence for weakly supervised disentanglement: identity units support cross-view recognition without identity labels; pose units become non-discriminative for identity.
- Practical training details (upsampling choice, mask branch for chairs) that matter for image quality are documented.

Weaknesses
- Baselines are limited. For chairs, KNN pixel averaging is weak; for faces, only qualitative comparison to a 3D morphable model is provided. No comparison to IGN or to a non-recurrent encoder-decoder trained to predict target viewpoints directly.
- Evaluation metrics focus on MSE, which correlates poorly with perceptual fidelity; more perceptually aligned metrics or human studies would strengthen claims.
- Disentanglement evaluation is mostly indirect (recognition from identity units vs. pose units). More systematic quantification would bolster the claim.
- Scope is restricted to rotation about a single axis under controlled backgrounds and, for faces, pre-aligned images. Generalization to cluttered scenes or illumination changes is not assessed.
- The “weakly-supervised” claim relies on action/viewpoint supervision and curated sequences; the supervision requirements should be more precisely characterized.

Quality
The model is technically sound, with a coherent recurrent formulation and appropriate training via BPTT and ADAM. The curriculum ablation convincingly supports the design choice. The mask auxiliary loss for chairs is a useful detail. However, the empirical study would be more persuasive with stronger baselines (e.g., IGN-style encoder-decoder, Dosovitskiy decoder with learned encoder, or a non-recurrent multi-step predictor) and additional metrics beyond MSE. Claims of superiority over 3D morphable models are not quantitatively established.

Clarity
The paper is clearly written and well organized. Architecture, losses, datasets, and training schedules are described with sufficient detail to reproduce. The role of action units and the recurrent update is explained clearly. Figures (as described) appear to illustrate long-horizon behavior and curriculum effects.

Originality
The combination of (i) action-conditioned recurrent traversal in a deep convolutional encoder-decoder, (ii) shared identity units across time to encourage disentanglement, and (iii) curriculum over trajectory length is a novel and timely contribution relative to contemporaneous work. While conceptually related to prior gating and inverse graphics approaches, the recurrent, controllable trajectory aspect is distinctive.

Significance
The work advances controllable image synthesis for object categories from a single view and shows promise for learning view-invariant features without labels. This is of interest to both graphics and vision communities. Limitations in baselines and metrics temper the strength of the empirical claims, but the approach is likely to influence follow-up work on learned transformations and disentanglement.

Suggestions for improvement
Essential:
- Add stronger baselines: (a) a feed-forward encoder-decoder trained to predict a target view directly without recurrence; (b) an IGN-style model trained on the same data; and (c) a nearest-neighbor feature transfer baseline that warps rather than averages pixels.
- Report additional metrics beyond MSE, such as SSIM, and provide a small-scale human perceptual study for chairs.
- Quantify disentanglement more rigorously: linear probe accuracy of pose from identity units and of identity from pose units; correlation analyses across time steps.

Nice-to-have:
- Provide ablations: without curriculum; without mask branch; with/without no-op action; alternative step sizes.
- Analyze failure modes (e.g., self-occlusion, thin structures) and show error accumulation curves beyond trained horizons.
- Clarify supervision: what labels are required (view indices/actions), and how sensitive training is to noisy action labels.
- Explore simple symmetry priors for chairs (e.g., mirroring augmentation) to address large angle offsets.

Recommendation
Lean toward accept. The paper presents a well-motivated and novel recurrent approach with clear technical contributions and convincing qualitative results, plus promising evidence of emergent disentanglement. Strengthening the empirical comparison and disentanglement quantification would make the case more compelling, but the current version already offers substantial value to the NeurIPS audience.
