

This paper proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing novel 3D views from single images, with a focus on learning disentangled representations of identity and pose through weak supervision. The core idea is to model pose manifold traversal as a sequence of small rotations using a recurrent structure with action units, trained via curriculum learning to handle long-term dependencies. The approach is evaluated on face rotation (Multi-PIE) and chair view synthesis (CAD models), with claims of disentangled identity features emerging without explicit regularization.

**Strengths:** The paper presents a clear technical contribution with a well-motivated recurrent architecture. The curriculum learning strategy effectively improves both image synthesis quality and disentanglement, as evidenced by progressive gains in recognition accuracy across training stages (Figure 8). The qualitative results for face rotation (Figure 3) and chair synthesis (Figure 5) are convincing, demonstrating smooth transformations and preserved object characteristics. The interpolation experiment (Figure 9) compellingly shows generative disentangling of identity and pose. The architecture is well-justified (e.g., avoiding pooling/unpooling), and the paper properly contextualizes related work (IGN, transforming autoencoders, disentanglement methods).

**Major Weaknesses:** The most significant issue is the overstatement of "weakly-supervised disentangling." The paper claims disentanglement emerges without explicit regularization, but the evidence is limited to cross-view recognition performance (Figure 8, Table 1). This does not substantiate disentanglement—true disentanglement requires factor manipulation experiments (e.g., varying identity while holding pose constant, or vice versa), which are absent. The recognition accuracy gap reduction (e.g., RNN6 vs. RNN1) is consistent with improved pose invariance, not necessarily disentangled representations. This mischaracterization undermines a key contribution.

The baseline comparisons are inadequate. For chairs, KNN (with top-K retrieval) is a weak baseline; the paper cites [8] (chair generation) but does not compare against it directly. A more relevant baseline would be the chair synthesis model from [8], which explicitly handles view synthesis. The lack of comparison to recent neural view synthesis work (e.g., [9], [28]) also weakens significance claims. Additionally, the chair dataset (CAD models) is highly controlled—real-world scenes with occlusions or clutter are not addressed, limiting applicability.

**Minor Issues:** The disentanglement claim is conflated with pose invariance (e.g., "identity units can do invariant identity recognition"). These are distinct concepts: invariance does not imply disentanglement. The paper should clarify this. The ablation on curriculum stages (Figure 7) is useful, but the quantitative MSE for RNN16 (179) lacks context—how does it compare to [8]? The "weakly-supervised" label is misleading since the model is fully supervised (rotations are provided as action inputs).

**Overall Assessment:** The technical approach is sound and the results are promising for controlled datasets. However, the overstated disentanglement claim is a critical flaw that misrepresents the contribution. The significance is partially diminished by the limited dataset scope (no real-world images) and weak baselines. While the recurrent structure for pose traversal is novel, the paper fails to demonstrate the claimed disentanglement rigorously. This is a significant issue for a venue like NIPS, where precise claims about disentanglement are expected.

**Recommendation:** **Weak Reject**. The paper makes a valid contribution to view synthesis but misrepresents its disentanglement claim. With substantial revision—adding disentanglement metrics (e.g., factor manipulation), stronger baselines (e.g., [8]), and clearer distinction between invariance and disentanglement—it could be acceptable. As written, the central claim is unsubstantiated, which is critical for this venue. The quality is good, but the significance is overstated due to the disentanglement mischaracterization.
