- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that performs controlled 3D view synthesis by traversing a pose manifold with action-conditioned small steps.  
- Splits latent bottleneck into identity units (shared across time) and pose units (recurrently updated), trained with backpropagation through time and curriculum learning.  
- Demonstrates smooth long-range rotations, preserved identity, improved chair reconstruction over KNN, competitive cross-view face recognition, and plausible identity interpolation.  
- Clear problem framing and a simple, effective architecture enabling multi-step transformations without explicit 3D reconstruction.  
- Recurrent, action-conditioned design naturally models “mental rotation” and updates pose in latent space while sharing identity.  
- Curriculum training is well-motivated and shown to improve synthesis quality and recognition invariance.  
- Disentangling of identity and pose emerges without explicit labels; identity units support strong cross-view recognition.  
- Convincing qualitative long-sequence results (up to 16 steps on chairs) and informative quantitative evaluation versus KNN.  
- Interpolation experiments show smooth, controllable manipulation of identity and pose.  
- Supervision is stronger than claimed: requires paired views with known relative rotations and alignment for faces.  
- Key architectural details (pose recurrence equation, action-conditional parameterization, weight sharing) are underspecified, hurting reproducibility.  
- Weak baselines: chairs compared only to KNN; lacks direct comparisons to contemporaneous generators like Dosovitskiy et al. and IGN.  
- Evaluation metrics limited to MSE; should include perceptual metrics (e.g., SSIM) or user studies and standard recognition metrics.  
- Scope limited to two object categories with constrained backgrounds and known camera rigs; generalization is untested.  
- Deterministic decoder cannot capture multimodal completions for severe occlusions or large rotations.  
- Discrete action granularity (± step, noop) leaves unclear whether continuous or arbitrary step sizes are supported.  
- Overall technically sound with coherent architecture and insightful curriculum ablations, but empirical case weakened by baselines and metric choices.  
- Paper is generally well written; requests clarification on recurrence equation, action matrix dimensionalities, mask branch usage, sequence sampling, and face alignment impact.  
- Novel combination of recurrent, action-conditioned encoder–decoder with emergent disentangling is an original contribution distinct from prior work.  
- Significant for controllable view synthesis and representation learning, though demonstrated on limited domains.  
- Suggested improvements: add stronger baselines (IGN, Dosovitskiy), report SSIM/perceptual metrics, ablation studies (dimensionalities, mask, curriculum), use augmentations/symmetry priors, explore continuous control and stochastic extensions, and release code.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated architecture; clear evidence that curriculum aids disentangling; strong qualitative and reasonable quantitative results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited baselines/metrics, under-specified implementation details, restricted experimental scope.  
- Overall recommendation: lean accept, contingent on addressing clarity and evaluation gaps.
