- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for 3D view synthesis from single images, targeting weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose.
- Claims a novel recurrent architecture that captures long-term dependencies for pose manifold traversal.
- Claims ability to generate realistic rotation trajectories using stepwise control signals.
- Claims emergence of view-invariant identity features without explicit class labels.
- Strong experimental support on Multi-PIE: high-quality rotation synthesis with smooth viewpoint transitions and robustness superior to a 3D morphable model baseline.
- Strong results on the chair dataset: RNN16 achieves 42% lower MSE than the best KNN baseline.
- Cross-view recognition shows identity features become more view-invariant with curriculum training; RNN6 reduces the 15°–90° performance gap by 10%.
- Class interpolation experiments further validate the disentangling of identity and pose.
- Positions itself well against related work (IGN, Predictive Gating Pyramid, disentangling models).
- Novelty lies in the recurrent structure for long-term pose trajectory learning and emergent weakly-supervised disentangling without explicit regularization.
- Paper is well-organized and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- Suggests better contextualization by comparing to other unsupervised disentangling methods (e.g., [6], [21]).
- Notes limitation in scope—experiments are limited to faces and chairs and this should be acknowledged.
- Significance is strong: enables novel view synthesis from single images without 3D models with clear applications in graphics and vision.
- Quality is excellent with thorough experiments and ablations; reviewer recommends acceptance.
