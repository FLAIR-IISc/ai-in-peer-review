## Review of "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of 3D view synthesis from a single image, focusing on faces and chairs. The core idea is a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network trained end-to-end to render rotated objects. The recurrent structure aims to capture long-term dependencies during the rotation process, and the authors demonstrate a degree of disentanglement of latent factors (pose and identity) without explicit supervision.

The paper builds upon existing work in view synthesis, generative modeling, and disentangled representation learning. It correctly identifies limitations of traditional geometry-based approaches and positions itself within the growing trend of learning-based solutions. The connection to the mental rotation phenomenon is a nice touch, providing motivation for the recurrent architecture. Related work is reasonably well-covered, citing key papers in the field (e.g., Dosovitskiy et al., IGN, transforming autoencoders).

**Strengths:** The proposed recurrent architecture is a sensible approach to address the sequential nature of rotations. The curriculum learning strategy appears effective, as demonstrated by the improved performance with increasing sequence length. The results on both faces and chairs are promising, showing visually plausible rotations. The observation of weakly-supervised disentanglement is interesting, suggesting the model learns meaningful representations without explicit labels. The quantitative results, particularly the comparison to KNN on the chair dataset, demonstrate a clear advantage.

**Weaknesses:** While the results are encouraging, the paper lacks a thorough ablation study. It's unclear how much each component (recurrent structure, curriculum learning, disentangled latent spaces) contributes to the overall performance. The discussion of the disentanglement is somewhat superficial; more analysis (e.g., visualization of latent spaces, quantitative metrics for disentanglement) would strengthen this claim. The comparison to existing methods is limited. While KNN is used as a baseline, comparing to more sophisticated generative models or view synthesis techniques would provide a more comprehensive evaluation. The paper also lacks discussion of failure cases and potential biases in the datasets.

**Originality:** The combination of a recurrent convolutional network with action units for 3D view synthesis is a novel contribution. The weakly-supervised disentanglement aspect is also noteworthy.

**Significance:** The work has the potential to advance the field of 3D view synthesis and disentangled representation learning. The ability to generate novel views from a single image has applications in various domains, including graphics, vision, and robotics.

**Arguments for acceptance:** Novel approach, promising results on challenging datasets, potential for broader impact.
**Arguments against acceptance:** Limited ablation study, superficial discussion of disentanglement, limited comparison to state-of-the-art methods.

**Overall:** This is a solid paper with a promising idea. While it has some weaknesses, the strengths outweigh them. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. The authors should conduct a more thorough ablation study, provide a more detailed analysis of the disentangled representations, and include a more comprehensive comparison to existing methods. Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its impact.
