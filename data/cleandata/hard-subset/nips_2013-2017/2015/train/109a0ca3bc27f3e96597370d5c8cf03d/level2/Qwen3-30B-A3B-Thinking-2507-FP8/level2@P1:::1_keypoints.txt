- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder for synthesizing 3D view rotations from single images using action-conditioned recurrent pose units.  
- Uses curriculum learning to progressively train longer rotation trajectories, improving image quality and representation learning.  
- Demonstrates emergent weakly-supervised disentangling: identity units enable near-perfect cross-view recognition while pose units perform near-randomly.  
- Strong empirical results on Multi-PIE faces and CAD-rendered chairs with quantitative (MSE, recognition) and qualitative (generated views, interpolations) evaluations versus meaningful baselines.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with effective architectural and ablation figures supporting the approach.  
- Missing explicit specification of action unit rotation magnitude (e.g., degrees per step), which is needed for reproducibility.  
- Lacks direct quantitative comparison to IGN and a clearer discussion of how the recurrent/deterministic approach differs from prior disentangling methods.  
- No analysis of why identity disentangles strongly while pose units remain uninformative; feature visualization or explanation is suggested.  
- Chair experiments use synthetic CAD renders only; discussion of generalization to real images or domain adaptation is needed.  
- Overall: technically sound, original, and significant contribution to view synthesis and disentangled representation learning.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
