## Review of “Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis”

This paper proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing rotated views of 3D objects (faces and chairs) from a single image, without relying on explicit 3D model recovery. The core idea is to leverage a recurrent structure to capture long-term dependencies during pose manifold traversal, driven by action units representing incremental rotations. The authors also highlight the emergent disentangling of latent factors (identity and pose) despite training solely for view synthesis.

The work builds on existing approaches in 3D view synthesis, encompassing geometry-based methods and recent learning-based techniques like transforming autoencoders and generative CNNs. It distinguishes itself by combining a convolutional architecture with recurrence and action units for controlled transformations, avoiding the need for explicit 3D mesh reconstruction. The paper effectively positions itself within the broader context of disentangled representation learning and relates to the human mental rotation phenomenon.

**Strengths:** The proposed method demonstrates promising results in generating realistic rotations of both faces and chairs. The curriculum learning strategy appears effective in stabilizing training and improving performance.  The emergent disentangling of latent factors is a notable contribution, suggesting the model learns meaningful representations beyond just view synthesis. The experimental evaluation on Multi-PIE and a chair dataset provides a good starting point for assessing the model’s capabilities. The visualization of generated images and the comparison with a 3D morphable model are helpful.

**Weaknesses:** The paper lacks detailed ablation studies to fully understand the contribution of each component (recurrence, action units, curriculum learning).  While the results are visually appealing, the quantitative evaluation relies primarily on MSE. More robust metrics for image quality and perceptual similarity (e.g., SSIM, LPIPS) would be beneficial. The comparison to other recent generative models for 3D view synthesis is limited. The discussion of limitations is brief. The claim of disentangling without explicit regularization needs stronger justification beyond the observed performance in cross-view recognition; further analysis of the latent space is needed.

**Originality & Significance:** The combination of recurrent networks, action units for controlled transformations, and the emergent disentangling property offers a novel approach to 3D view synthesis. While individual components have been explored before, the specific integration and demonstrated results contribute to the field. The potential for learning useful representations without labeled data is significant.

**Clarity & Completeness:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The network architecture and training procedure are clearly explained. However, some details regarding the hyperparameter settings and implementation could be more explicit to facilitate reproducibility.



**Arguments for Acceptance:** 
*   Novel combination of techniques for 3D view synthesis.
*   Promising results on challenging datasets.
*   Emergent disentangling of latent factors.

**Arguments for Rejection:**
*   Limited ablation studies.
*   Insufficient quantitative evaluation.
*   Weak justification of disentangling claims.



**Overall Assessment:**  I believe this paper presents a promising approach to 3D view synthesis with interesting emergent properties. Despite some weaknesses, the novelty and potential significance of the work warrant acceptance *with minor revisions*. I recommend the authors conduct more thorough ablation studies, incorporate more robust evaluation metrics, and provide a stronger justification for their disentangling claims.
