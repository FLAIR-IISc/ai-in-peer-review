- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that disentangles view-invariant identity units and view-dependent pose units for single-image 3D view synthesis.  
- Pose units are iteratively updated by an action-conditioned recurrent mechanism (e.g., "rotate clockwise") while identity units remain fixed; a decoder generates images for each pose.  
- Trained end-to-end with curriculum learning, progressively handling longer transformation sequences.  
- Evaluated on Multi-PIE faces and 3D chair models, showing high-quality rotated view synthesis.  
- Learned identity features are highly discriminative for cross-view recognition, competitive with supervised methods despite no identity labels.  
- Method is well-motivated, technically sound, and supported by extensive qualitative and quantitative experiments (including k-NN baseline comparisons).  
- Curriculum learning convincingly improves synthesis quality and invariance of learned features.  
- Analysis of learned representations via cross-view recognition strongly supports the weakly-supervised disentangling claim.  
- Writing and organization are clear, with helpful diagrams and detailed experimental descriptions.  
- Technical clarity could be improved by a more explicit mathematical formulation of how action units modify pose units (the described "tensor product" is ambiguous).  
- Lacks quantitative comparison to relevant baselines such as 3D Morphable Models for faces; current 3DMM discussion is qualitative and partly biased.  
- Pixel-wise MSE training likely causes less-sharp textures; discussion of this trade-off and limitations (e.g., in-the-wild performance) should be expanded.  
- Novel combination of recurrent structure, action control, and identity/pose separation; key novelty is weakly-supervised disentangling via sequence prediction.  
- Results are significant for generative modeling and representation learning, with broad implications for 3D-aware image manipulation and unsupervised feature learning.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel recurrent architecture, strong results on two datasets, significant weakly-supervised disentangling finding, clear presentation and thorough experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need more quantitative baselines, clearer technical formulation of action-pose interaction, and a more detailed limitations discussion.
