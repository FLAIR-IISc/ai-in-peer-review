- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that separates identity and pose latent units and uses discrete "action" units to apply out-of-plane rotations to single images.  
- Uses curriculum training (start with one-step rotations, progressively longer) to improve image quality and disentanglement.  
- Extends prior work by adding an encoder for generalization, recurrent control for multi-step transformations, and weakly supervised disentangling without explicit labels.  
- Clear architectural idea: action-gated recurrent updates to pose units with shared identity units and a scalable encoder/decoder.  
- Strong qualitative results on Multi-PIE faces and rendered chairs, including long smooth rotation trajectories and improved chair synthesis with mask prediction.  
- Disentangling emerges from the rotation objective and curriculum: identity units are view-invariant and discriminative for cross-view recognition.  
- Solid experiments demonstrating curriculum effects (RNN1→RNN16) with both qualitative and quantitative analyses (MSE, recognition accuracy).  
- Simple, effective control interface via action units and demonstrated identity interpolation with consistent pose traversal.  
- Rotations are discretized to training step size; no mechanism for continuous-angle control or calibration to arbitrary angles.  
- Training staged at fixed sequence lengths limits generalization beyond trained horizons; no explicit variable-length training/evaluation protocol.  
- Baselines are limited and weak (KNN for chairs); missing comparisons to recent neural view-synthesis/inverse graphics methods on chairs.  
- Evaluation relies heavily on MSE, lacking perceptual metrics (SSIM/LPIPS) or user studies to validate visual quality.  
- Scope limited to category-specific, curated/registered data (aligned faces, rendered chairs), single transformation type (rotation), and no illumination/background variation.  
- Paper is technically sound with reproducible architectural/training details; disentangling claim supported by recognition experiments.  
- Well written and organized; figures and descriptions are informative, though action-to-pose transformation and a concise curriculum algorithm could be clearer.  
- Novel combination of convolutional encoder/decoder with action-conditioned recurrent pose updates and curriculum-driven disentangling for this setting.  
- Significant step toward 3D-aware view synthesis and learning view-invariant features without labels, but applicability tempered by discrete rotations and curated data.  
- Suggested improvements: enable continuous-angle control, train with variable-length/randomized sequences, strengthen baselines, add perceptual metrics/user study, and include ablations and robustness discussion.  
- Acceptance arguments: elegant, novel recurrent control formulation, strong qualitative results, and convincing disentangling without labels; rejection arguments: weak baselines, MSE-focused evaluation, and limited generality to real-world variability.
