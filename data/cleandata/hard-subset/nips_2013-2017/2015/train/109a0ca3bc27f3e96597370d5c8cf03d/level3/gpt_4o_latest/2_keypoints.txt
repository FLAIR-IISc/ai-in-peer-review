- Presents a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes rotated 3D object views from a single image by disentangling identity and pose.  
- Recurrent pose units sequentially update pose while identity units remain fixed, enabling longer rotation trajectories.  
- Decoder reconstructs rotated images and object masks, addressing partial observability and ill-posed rotation.  
- Demonstrated high-quality renderings and view-invariant features on Multi-PIE (faces) and a 3D chairs dataset.  
- Key strength: disentangling identity and pose enables learning view-invariant features without class labels.  
- Key strength: recurrent architecture models long-term dependencies in rotation trajectories, improving extended-sequence rendering.  
- Key strength: curriculum training (increasing trajectory length) improves rendering quality and disentanglement.  
- Key strength: strong qualitative and quantitative experiments, with comparisons to KNN and CNN baselines.  
- Key strength: paper is well-written with clear methodology and illustrative figures.  
- Weakness: limited to discrete rotation angles, restricting continuous-transformation tasks.  
- Weakness: fixed-length training may limit generalization to unseen trajectory lengths despite recurrence.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to faces and chairs, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to more recent generative models (e.g., GANs, VAEs).  
- Pro: introduces an effective disentangling approach and practical applications in synthesis and view-invariant recognition.  
- Con: reliance on discrete angles, fixed-length training, and narrow experimental scope may limit flexibility and broader impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address limitations and extend evaluation.
