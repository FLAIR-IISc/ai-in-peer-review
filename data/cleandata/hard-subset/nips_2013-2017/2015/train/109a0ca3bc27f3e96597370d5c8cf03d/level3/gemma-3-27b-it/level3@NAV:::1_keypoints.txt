- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize 3D views from a single 2D image by modeling pose-manifold traversal with action units.
- Integrates recurrence with convolutional networks to capture long-term dependencies for applying rotations.
- Shows emergent disentangled representations for pose and identity without explicit supervision.
- Experiments on Multi-PIE (faces) and Chairs datasets, with curriculum learning and interpolation studies.
- Qualitative results are provided but figures are small and hard to assess visually.
- Lacks quantitative evaluation using standard metrics (e.g., PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS), preventing rigorous assessment of rendering quality.
- Fails to compare against state-of-the-art geometry-based and recent learning-based baselines.
- Dataset choice is narrow, limiting evidence of generality to more diverse object classes.
- Does not explore generalization to more complex or varied rotation trajectories beyond repeated rotations.
- Writing is clear and problem motivation is strong, but insufficient experimentation undermines the contribution.
- Recommendation: rejection in current form, with suggestion to add thorough quantitative evaluations, baselines, broader datasets, and clearer figures before resubmission.
