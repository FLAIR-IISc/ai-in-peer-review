- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for end-to-end 3D view synthesis from a single image.  
- Main contributions: recurrent encoder-decoder architecture, weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose, and user-controlled rotation trajectory generation.  
- Relates to prior work on 3D invariant recognition, disentangled representations, and image generation (e.g., transforming autoencoder, Predictive Gating Pyramid, Inverse Graphics Network).  
- Architecture: deep convolutional encoder, shared identity units, recurrent pose units with rotation action inputs, and deep convolutional decoder.  
- Training: curriculum learning to increase trajectory length gradually and a hidden unit regularizer to encourage disentangling.  
- Evaluation on Multi-PIE (faces) and Chairs datasets to test synthesis across wide rotational angles.  
- Results: generates high-quality rotated images, realistic rotation trajectories, and competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods.  
- Demonstrates interpolation across chair identity manifold at fixed pose and traversing pose manifold while fixing identity.  
- Disentangled identity units show good cross-view recognition performance (discriminative evaluation).  
- Strength: novel end-to-end recurrent convolutional architecture for single-image rotation synthesis.  
- Strength: effective weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose via curriculum training.  
- Strength: user-controllable generation of realistic rotation trajectories and competitive empirical results.  
- Weakness: only supports discrete rotation angles tied to training data; no continuous-angle method provided.  
- Weakness: trained with fixed-length sequences, limiting typical recurrent network flexibility.  
- Overall: novel and effective approach with potential applications in robotics, vision, and graphics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated novel approach, evaluated on two datasets, clear writing and explanations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limitations in rotation continuity and sequence flexibility; needs deeper analysis of limitations.  
- Rating and recommendation: 8/10, accept with minor revisions.  
- Minor revisions requested: provide more detailed analysis of approach and limitations, discuss extensions for continuous-angle rotation, and add more experimental results.
