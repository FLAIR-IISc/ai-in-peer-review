1. Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that disentangles view-invariant identity and view-dependent pose from a single image.  
2. Uses action-controlled pose updates in latent space to model sequential out-of-plane rotations.  
3. Keeps identity fixed and recurrently updates pose to generate long rotation trajectories, decoded to images (and masks).  
4. Trains end-to-end with curriculum learning, gradually increasing rotation sequence length.  
5. Integrates ideas from generative convnets and transforming auto-encoders into a novel recurrent, action-driven framework.  
6. Differs from concurrent work by using a deterministic recurrent model (not a VAE) focused on transformation trajectories.  
7. Demonstrates weakly-supervised disentangling: identity features become view-invariant without identity labels.  
8. Strong qualitative results on Multi-PIE and Chair datasets showing smooth, identity-preserving rotations.  
9. Quantitative gains over a k-NN baseline on chairs and competitive cross-view recognition accuracy versus a supervised CNN.  
10. Cross-view experiments show identity units are discriminative while pose units perform near chance, supporting disentanglement.  
11. Paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and includes helpful diagrams and experimental presentation.  
12. Limitation: supports only discrete rotation steps tied to training step size; continuous or arbitrary-angle control is not addressed.  
13. Limitation: curriculum uses fixed-length sequences; unclear how well the model handles arbitrary-length action sequences beyond training maxima.
