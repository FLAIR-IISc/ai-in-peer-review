- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that disentangles identity and pose to synthesize 3D rotated views from a single image.  
- Uses a recurrent architecture and curriculum learning to handle long-term sequential rotations; trained end-to-end.  
- Integrates ideas from transforming autoencoders, predictive gating pyramids, generative CNNs for chairs, inverse graphics, and disentangling literature.  
- Technically sound with a clearly described architecture and training procedure.  
- Strong qualitative and quantitative results supporting effectiveness.  
- Curriculum learning is justified and empirically beneficial.  
- Ablation studies (varying RNN lengths) are informative and compelling.  
- Quantitative gains on Multi-PIE and chairs datasets versus KNN baseline.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and reproducible with appropriate notation and figures.  
- Novel combination of recurrent CNNs, disentangled latent factors, and action units for pose control.  
- Weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose is a notable achievement.  
- Learned identity units provide view-invariant features useful beyond synthesis.  
- Demonstrates generative capabilities such as interpolation of chair identities and realistic rotation trajectories.  
- Weakness: supports only discrete rotation angles determined by training data.  
- Weakness: uses fixed-length recurrent sequences rather than variable-length, limiting flexibility.  
- Weakness: computational cost of training is not discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated, strong results, clear presentation, and high impact potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: discrete-angle limitation and restrictive fixed-length sequence design.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with strengths outweighing weaknesses; strong recommendation to accept.
