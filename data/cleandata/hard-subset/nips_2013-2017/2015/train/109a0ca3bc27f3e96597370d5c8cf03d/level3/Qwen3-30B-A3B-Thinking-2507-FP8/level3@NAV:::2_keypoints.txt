- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for unsupervised disentanglement of identity and pose in 3D rotation tasks.  
- Architecture uses a fixed shared identity unit and recurrent pose units updated by action units that control small rotation increments.  
- Curriculum training progressively increases sequence length, enabling learning of long rotation trajectories and pose manifold traversal.  
- Key innovation: unsupervised disentanglement produces view-invariant identity features that improve cross-view recognition without class labels.  
- Builds on prior disentangling and recurrent sequence work but makes a clear contribution by applying these ideas to 3D view synthesis.  
- Demonstrates strong qualitative and quantitative results on Multi-PIE faces and chair CAD models.  
- Shows improved rendering quality and cross-view recognition accuracy versus baselines like KNN and VGG-16 features.  
- Curriculum strategy is particularly effective for learning long rotation sequences.  
- Strengths: well-structured methodology, clear experimental validation across datasets, and thorough ablation studies.  
- Paper is very well written with clear explanations of components.  
- Disentangled identity features are an important contribution for unsupervised, view-invariant recognition.  
- Weakness: model supports only discrete rotation angles from training data, lacking continuous-angle interpolation.  
- Weakness: fixed-length sequence training limits the typical flexibility of recurrent nets and may hurt generalization to variable-length transformations.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations but do not provide solutions.  
- Overall assessment: strong technical contribution with high significance and clarity; recommendation to accept.
