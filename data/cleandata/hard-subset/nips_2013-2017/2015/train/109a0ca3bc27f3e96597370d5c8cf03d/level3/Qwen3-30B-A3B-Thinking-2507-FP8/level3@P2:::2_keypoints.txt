- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for 3D view synthesis from single images, modeling pose traversal via small rotational steps.  
- Key innovation: recurrent architecture with action units to control local transformations and learn long-term dependencies without explicit 3D reconstruction.  
- Weakly-supervised disentangling of identity (stable) and pose (changing) learned without class labels, enabling view-invariant recognition.  
- Extends Inverse Graphics Network (IGN) by adding recurrence for sequences and introduces controllable action units for pose traversal.  
- Related to bilinear and disBM models but achieves disentanglement through the learning objective rather than explicit regularization.  
- Contrasts with transforming autoencoders and Predictive Gating Pyramid by emphasizing recurrence and weak supervision.  
- Architecture is well-motivated and clearly explained (encoder, identity units, pose units, action units, decoder).  
- Curriculum learning effectively enables training for longer rotation sequences and improves performance.  
- Ablation studies convincingly show incremental training improves rendering quality and disentanglement.  
- Strong experimental validation on Multi-PIE and chair datasets with compelling qualitative results.  
- Quantitative validation via cross-view recognition and MSE comparisons supports claims.  
- Disentangling demonstrated: identity units yield near-perfect recognition while pose units perform at chance.  
- Learned representations allow practical operations like identity interpolation at fixed pose.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear motivation, organization, and useful figures.  
- Contributions significant: handles longer rotation trajectories without explicit 3D models and advances weakly-supervised disentangled representation learning.  
- Main weakness: supports only discrete rotation angles tied to training data, no continuous-angle method.  
- Second weakness: trained with fixed-length sequences, limiting typical recurrent network flexibility.  
- Limited evaluation scope: demonstrated only on faces and chairs, with no broad generalization claims.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, significant contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
