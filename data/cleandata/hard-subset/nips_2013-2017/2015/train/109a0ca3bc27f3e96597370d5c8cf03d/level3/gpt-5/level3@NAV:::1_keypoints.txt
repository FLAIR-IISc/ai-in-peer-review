- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that synthesizes novel views from a single image using action-conditioned recurrent pose units and shared identity units.  
- Uses curriculum training (increasing sequence length) to stabilize long-horizon predictions.  
- Situates work relative to transforming autoencoders, gating/predictive models, IGN, and chair renderers; emphasizes weakly supervised disentangling.  
- Architecture and training scheme are technically sound and clearly motivated; curriculum-length ablations are informative.  
- Evaluation is predominantly qualitative; quantitative analysis is limited to MSE for chairs and cross-view recognition.  
- Missing perceptual and distributional metrics (PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS, FID/KID), pose error, mask IoU, and uncertainty calibration.  
- Lacks systematic comparisons to strong baselines and geometry-based pipelines (3DMM, SfM/MVS, single-view fit-and-render).  
- Experiments are confined to Multi-PIE faces and rendered Chairs with constrained, static rotation axes.  
- Generalization to other categories, real-world imagery, and non-repeating or non-fixed-axis rotations is untested.  
- Paper is well organized and easy to follow, but figures are too small for detail and there are minor grammatical issues.  
- Novelty: combines recurrent, action-conditioned disentangling within a conv encoder–decoder and extends prior chair/IGN-style work to long sequences with curriculum learning.  
- Significance is limited by constrained experiments and lack of broader validation.  
- Pros: clear, well-motivated model; elegant recurrent/action design and curriculum training; compelling qualitative results and evidence of emergent disentangling.  
- Cons: inadequate quantitative evaluation, narrow datasets, no non-static-axis trajectory tests, small figures, and minor language issues.  
- Suggestions: add rigorous metrics (PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS/FID), pose accuracy, and user studies; compare to 3DMM, single-view 3D fit-and-render, IGN, and KNN/flow/warping baselines.  
- Suggestions (cont.): test SE(3) control beyond yaw and non-repeating trajectories; evaluate on broader datasets (cars, other ShapeNet classes, in-the-wild images).  
- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful but currently below the bar due to limited experimentation; borderline reject.
