- Proposes an action-conditioned recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that disentangles identity and pose for 3D view synthesis from a single image.  
- Encoder separates identity and pose; discrete action units drive local pose updates and the decoder reconstructs images (faces, chairs) and foreground masks (chairs).  
- Recurrent extension keeps identity fixed and repeatedly updates pose to traverse longer rotation trajectories.  
- Trained end-to-end with curriculum learning, producing qualitatively smooth long-horizon renderings.  
- Emergent view-invariant identity codes support cross-view recognition without class supervision.  
- Positioned relative to transforming autoencoders, gating models, chair generators, and inverse graphics, contributing an action-conditioned recurrent traversal with disentanglement.  
- Methodology is sound with coherent architecture choices and clear training objectives; curriculum ablations show gains in rendering and recognition as sequence length increases.  
- Quantitative evidence includes MSE on chairs and cross-view recognition on Multi-PIE and chairs.  
- Baselines are limited (KNN for chairs; supervised CNN for faces) and omit stronger generative baselines and perceptual metrics.  
- Model supports only discrete angles from the training set, limiting continuous control.  
- Recurrent training uses fixed unroll lengths, undermining RNN flexibility and generalization to arbitrary sequence lengths.  
- Paper is clearly written with explicit architectures, training schedules, and reproducible details; figures effectively illustrate behaviors.  
- Originality lies in combining recurrent, action-selective pose updates with weakly supervised disentanglement and demonstrating recognition benefits.  
- Significance: compelling results for faces and chairs and identity units’ discriminative utility increases impact for generative modeling and representation learning.  
- Pros: clear end-to-end model, unsupervised disentanglement with recognition gains, strong long-trajectory renderings, and a simple action interface for controlled pose traversal.  
- Cons: discrete-angle limitation, fixed-length RNN training and limited generalization evaluation, and missing stronger baselines/perceptual metrics.  
- Suggestions: parameterize actions continuously (e.g., angle embeddings or SO(3) maps), train with randomly sampled sequence lengths and scheduled sampling, and add perceptual metrics and stronger baselines.
