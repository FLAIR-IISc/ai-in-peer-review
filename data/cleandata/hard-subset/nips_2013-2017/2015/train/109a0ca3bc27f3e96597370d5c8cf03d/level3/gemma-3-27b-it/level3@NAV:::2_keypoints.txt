- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes rotated 3D views from a single image by disentangling identity and pose in latent space.
- Controls pose via a sequence of action units and uses a recurrent architecture to handle longer rotation trajectories.
- Learns view-invariant identity features useful for cross-view recognition without explicit supervision.
- Employs curriculum learning and end-to-end training to improve learning of long-term pose transformations.
- Demonstrates qualitative results showing smooth, realistic rotations and applies the method to both face and chair datasets.
- Shows strong quantitative performance on cross-view recognition, indicating effective disentanglement.
- Well-written paper with clear motivation, comprehensive component explanation, and thorough experiments.
- Main limitation: supports only discrete rotation angles determined by the training data, lacking continuous-angle modeling.
- Fixed-length sequence requirement for the recurrent network reduces flexibility and may hurt variable trajectory handling.
- Curriculum learning steps (specific length increases) lack deeper justification or analysis.
- Evaluation lacks broader quantitative comparisons to state-of-the-art 3D reconstruction/rendering methods.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address continuous-angle limitation and provide more comprehensive related-work comparisons.
