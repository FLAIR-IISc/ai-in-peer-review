- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for 3D view synthesis that leverages recurrence to traverse pose manifolds.  
- Uses action units for controllable rotation and trajectory prediction, a novel twist on prior work.  
- Demonstrated on Multi-PIE (faces) and a chair dataset, showing rotated view generation and unsupervised disentangling of identity vs. pose.  
- Connects to prior literature (transforming autoencoders, predictive gating, generative CNNs, inverse graphics) and to Shepard & Metzler mental rotation motivation.  
- Strength: novel recurrent architecture for pose traversal and controllable rotations.  
- Strength: model appears to learn disentangled identity and pose representations without explicit supervision, supported by cross-view recognition.  
- Strength: curriculum learning is sensibly used and likely helps long rotation trajectories.  
- Strength: paper is generally well-written and clearly organized.  
- Weakness: insufficient quantitative evaluation—missing metrics like FID, LPIPS, and comprehensive pixel-wise errors.  
- Weakness: limited baselines (only KNN) and no comparisons to strong geometry-based or recent generative (GAN) view-synthesis methods.  
- Weakness: small datasets and limited generalization—only Multi-PIE and one chair dataset tested.  
- Weakness: experiments focus on simple, equally-spaced rotations and do not explore arbitrary 3D rotations or non-static axes.  
- Weakness: visualization quality is poor (figures too small) hindering assessment.  
- Weakness: minor grammatical errors in the text.  
- For acceptance: novel direction, intriguing disentanglement result, and clear presentation.  
- Against acceptance: lack of robust quantitative evidence, weak baselines, limited datasets, and restricted rotation experiments.  
- Overall: promising but undermined by insufficient experimentation and analysis; recommend rejection with encouragement to resubmit after addressing these issues.
