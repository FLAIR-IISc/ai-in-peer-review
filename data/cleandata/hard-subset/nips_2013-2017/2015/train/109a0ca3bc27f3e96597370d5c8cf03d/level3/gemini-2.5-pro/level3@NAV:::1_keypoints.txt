- Proposed method: recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that disentangles identity and pose, sequentially updates pose via actions to synthesize novel 3D views from a single image; trained with curriculum learning and evaluated on Multi-PIE and ShapeNet.  
- Novelty: recurrent architecture for sequential view synthesis is an intuitive and original contribution.  
- Representation: model convincingly learns disentangled identity and pose in a weakly-supervised way (shown by cross-view recognition and class interpolation).  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and clearly explains the model.  
- Evaluation weakness: results are largely qualitative; quantitative analysis is limited to comparison with a simple k-NN baseline.  
- Missing baselines: lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art deep learning and geometry-based view synthesis methods.  
- Limited scope: experiments only on faces and chairs (constrained poses), so generality is unproven.  
- Limited transformations: tested only on simple static-axis rotations; unclear performance on more complex transformations.  
- Presentation issues: many figures are too small and there are minor grammatical errors.  
- Overall conclusion: method is interesting and promising, but insufficient experimental validation and missing relevant comparisons significantly weaken the paper's contribution.
