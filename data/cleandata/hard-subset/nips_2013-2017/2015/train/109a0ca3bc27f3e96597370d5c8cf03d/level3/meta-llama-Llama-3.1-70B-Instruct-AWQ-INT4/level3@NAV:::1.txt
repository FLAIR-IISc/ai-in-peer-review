This paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing novel views of 3D objects from a single image. The model is trained end-to-end on the task of rendering rotated objects starting from a single image, and is evaluated on the Multi-PIE dataset for faces and a dataset of 3D chair models. The results show that the model can generate high-quality images of both faces and chairs in a wide range of rotational angles, and can also learn disentangled features for pose-invariant recognition.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to 3D view synthesis using a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network.
* The model is able to generate high-quality images of both faces and chairs in a wide range of rotational angles.
* The paper demonstrates the ability of the model to learn disentangled features for pose-invariant recognition, which is a challenging task in computer vision.

Weaknesses:

* The evaluation is largely qualitative and inadequate, with no quantitative metrics or comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
* The figures and visualizations are not scaled large enough for readability.
* The paper has minor grammatical errors, such as "rotate" → "rotated".
* The experiments are limited to the Multi-PIE and Chairs datasets, and more diverse datasets are needed to demonstrate the generality of the approach.
* The paper does not test the generalization of the model to non-static-axis or non-repeating rotation trajectories.
* The paper lacks sufficient comparison to state-of-the-art geometry-based approaches.

* The paper proposes a novel approach to 3D view synthesis using a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network.
