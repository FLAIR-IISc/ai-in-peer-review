- Novel recurrent conv encoder-decoder that uses action-conditioned updates to traverse a pose manifold and synthesize multi-step rotations.
- Disentangles identity (view-invariant) and pose (view-dependent) features without explicit supervision.
- Cross-view recognition shows identity units are view-invariant and pose units poorly predict identity, supporting disentanglement.
- Qualitative view-synthesis results on Multi-PIE and chair renders are visually strong, preserving identity across large rotations.
- Chair interpolation experiments indicate a smooth, meaningful latent space for object identity.
- Major weakness: lacks rigorous quantitative evaluation (no PSNR/SSIM or similar metrics against ground truth).
- Only quantitative baseline is KNN, which is weak for generative comparison; missing comparisons to learning-based methods like Dosovitskiy et al. [8] or Inverse Graphics Network [17].
- Comparison to 3D morphable models for faces is purely qualitative; needs quantitative pose-normalization and texture-fidelity evaluation.
- Experiments restricted to aligned faces and rendered chairs with clean backgrounds, limiting claims about generalization to in-the-wild data.
- Tested only on simple, single-axis, discrete-step rotations; unclear if model handles continuous angles, arbitrary axes, or non-repeating trajectories.
- Some figures are too small and there are minor grammatical errors that reduce clarity.
- Curriculum learning is justified and helpful, but the paper should analyze whether gains are in image quality, disentanglement, or sequence stability.
- Introduction downplays geometry-based methods too strongly; a more balanced discussion and direct quantitative trade-off comparison is needed.
- Overall assessment: creative and novel idea with promising qualitative results, but significance is undermined by weak experimental evaluation; recommend rejection unless stronger quantitative baselines and broader experiments are added.
