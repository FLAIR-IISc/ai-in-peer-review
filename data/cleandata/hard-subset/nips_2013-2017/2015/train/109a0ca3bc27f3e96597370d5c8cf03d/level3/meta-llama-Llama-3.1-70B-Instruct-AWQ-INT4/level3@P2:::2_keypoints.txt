- Proposal: Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to synthesize novel 3D object views from a single image.  
- Main idea: Disentangles identity and pose using a shared encoder/decoder and action units that control pose changes.  
- Training: Trained end-to-end with curriculum learning that gradually increases sequence difficulty.  
- Writing/clarity: Paper is well-written with clear motivation and component explanations.  
- Related work: Provides a thorough discussion of recent computer vision and graphics literature.  
- Comparisons: Evaluated against state-of-the-art methods including transforming autoencoder, Predictive Gating Pyramid, and Inverse Graphics Network.  
- Experiments/datasets: Demonstrates effectiveness on Multi-PIE and Chairs datasets with high-quality novel view renderings.  
- Disentanglement utility: Learned identity units support cross-view object recognition.  
- Generative interpolation: Shows ability to generate novel chairs by interpolating between instances.  
- Strengths: Novel, well-motivated approach with thorough experiments and impressive results.  
- Weakness 1: Model only supports discrete rotation angles determined by training data, no continuous-angle method provided.  
- Weakness 2: Trained with fixed-length sequences, which conflicts with the expected flexibility of recurrent networks.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper (Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10) with important limitations to address in future work.  
- Acceptance arguments: Pros—novelty, solid experiments, impressive results; Cons—discrete rotations and fixed-length training sequences.
