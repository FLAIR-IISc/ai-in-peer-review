- Novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for sequential 3D view synthesis from a single image.
- Integrates recurrent networks with conv encoders/decoders to model pose-manifold traversal.
- Learns to disentangle identity and pose factors in a weakly-supervised way without explicit labels.
- Uses curriculum learning to improve training stability and long-term prediction.
- Generally clear, well-organized presentation and helpful diagrams.

- Quantitative evaluation is limited (only MSE reported); lacks stronger metrics like FID or SSIM.
- Insufficient baselines and comparisons to state-of-the-art generative and geometry-based methods.
- Experiments restricted to Multi-PIE and Chairs datasets, limiting demonstrated generality.
- Evaluated mainly on regular rotation trajectories; does not test non-axis or complex motion paths.
- Several figures are small and hard to interpret; visual presentation needs improvement.

- Technically sound approach, but limited evaluation reduces confidence in claims.
- Writing is clear overall with minor grammatical issues; diagrams helpful but should be larger.
- Original combination of recurrent architecture and weak disentangling is novel and valuable.
- Promising potential impact if validated on more datasets and against stronger baselines.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, weakly-supervised disentangling, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient quantitative evaluation, limited datasets, and lack of complex trajectory tests.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept pending more comprehensive quantitative comparisons, broader datasets, trajectory experiments, and improved figure clarity.
