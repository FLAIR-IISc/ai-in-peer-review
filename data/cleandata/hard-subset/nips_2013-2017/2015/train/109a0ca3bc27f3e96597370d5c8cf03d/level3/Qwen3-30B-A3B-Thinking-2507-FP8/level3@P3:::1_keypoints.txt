- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder with action units for long-term 3D view synthesis from a single 2D image.  
- Trains end-to-end using curriculum learning to handle progressively longer rotation trajectories.  
- Claims weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose features and ability to interpolate between object classes.  
- Builds on transforming autoencoders and predictive gating ideas but introduces recurrent action-driven steps for repeated rotations.  
- Demonstrates convincing qualitative results on faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs, showing realistic rotations.  
- Evaluation is largely qualitative and lacks sufficient quantitative metrics (no LPIPS/FID, limited MSE reporting).  
- Fails to compare against state-of-the-art geometry-based and learning-based baselines (e.g., 3DMMs, recent methods).  
- Quantitative chair results (MSE ~179 for RNN16) are presented without comparable baselines on the same dataset.  
- Paper is well-written and reproducible with clear architecture and training descriptions, though some figures are small.  
- Curriculum learning strategy and recurrent structure are novel contributions for trajectory-based prediction.  
- Limited experimental scope (only two datasets) weakens claims of generalizability.  
- No analysis of failure modes, limitations, or generalization to non-static-axis/non-repeating rotations.  
- Weak evidence that disentangling outperforms explicitly regularized approaches; lacks quantitative support.  
- Minor grammatical errors in the manuscript and figure captions.  
- Recommendation: expand quantitative evaluation, include comparisons to state-of-the-art methods, test more diverse datasets, and analyze limitations.
