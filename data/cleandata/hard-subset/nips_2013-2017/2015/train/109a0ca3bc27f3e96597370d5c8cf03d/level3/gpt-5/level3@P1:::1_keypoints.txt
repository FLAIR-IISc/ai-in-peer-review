- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that synthesizes novel views by traversing a latent pose manifold with action-conditioned recurrent updates.  
- Latent code split into identity units (time-invariant) and pose units (updated via a 1-of-3 action: clockwise/no-op/counterclockwise).  
- Uses curriculum learning (increasing RNN trajectory length) to stabilize long-horizon synthesis and encourage disentanglement.  
- Evaluated on Multi-PIE faces and rendered chair CAD models, showing plausible multi-step rotations and smooth trajectories.  
- Identity units support cross-view recognition without labels, improving with longer curriculum training.  
- Class interpolation produces novel chair identities that maintain consistent pose traversals.  
- Combines ideas from transforming autoencoders, gating models, conv encoder–decoders, and inverse graphics, adding action-conditioned recurrence and curriculum.  
- Strength: clear, simple, and well-motivated architecture separating identity and pose.  
- Strength: curriculum training demonstrably stabilizes long-horizon prediction and improves disentangling.  
- Strength: compelling qualitative results for faces and chairs with smooth, identity-preserving rotations.  
- Strength: weakly-supervised disentangling is notable for cross-view recognition.  
- Weakness: evaluation is largely qualitative—lacks PSNR/SSIM, trajectory-averaged metrics, mask IoU, perceptual metrics, and human studies.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparative baselines—needs 3D morphable models for faces, Dosovitskiy/IGN comparisons for chairs, and retrieval+warp baselines.  
- Weakness: narrow experimental scope—only Multi-PIE and synthetic chairs; needs more categories and real-world, in-the-wild tests.  
- Weakness: limited trajectory diversity—only fixed-step, single-axis rotations; should test variable steps, mixed transformations, and non-repeating sequences.  
- Weakness: missing ablations—action vs. unconditioned recurrence, recurrence vs. single-shot, latent dimensionalities, decoder choices, curriculum length, and feedback vs. latent-only recurrence.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of failure modes—drift/identity leakage, symmetry ambiguities, and sensitivity to misalignment.  
- Weakness: figures are small and hard to read; needs per-step error plots and clearer visual comparisons.  
- Quality: methodologically sound and implemented, but empirical evidence is incomplete due to limited metrics, comparisons, and ablations.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized and mostly clear; needs larger figures and a schematic for the action-conditioned update plus minor grammatical polishing.  
- Originality: novel combination of conv encoder–decoder, action-conditioned recurrent pose units, and curriculum training with emergent weak supervision.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for view synthesis and representation learning, but current impact limited by narrow and qualitative evaluation.  
- Pros: simple principled model, effective curriculum, emergent identity/pose separation, and meaningful identity manifold interpolation.  
- Cons: lacks quantitative evaluation, limited datasets and trajectory types, and missing ablations and failure analyses.  
- Minor fixes: enlarge figures, add error bars/per-step plots, fix grammar, and cite/compare to geometry-based pipelines and IGN/Dosovitskiy quantitatively.
