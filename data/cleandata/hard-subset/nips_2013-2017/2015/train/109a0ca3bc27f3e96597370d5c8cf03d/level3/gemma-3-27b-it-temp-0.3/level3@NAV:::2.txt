## Review of “Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis”

This paper presents a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing rotated views of 3D objects (faces and chairs) from a single image. The core idea is to disentangle identity and pose within a latent space, and then use recurrent action units to control pose transformations, enabling the generation of longer rotation trajectories. The authors demonstrate that this approach not only produces plausible renderings but also learns view-invariant identity features useful for cross-view recognition, all in a weakly-supervised manner.

This work builds upon a rich history of 3D view synthesis and disentangled representation learning. Previous approaches often relied on explicit 3D model recovery (multi-view stereo, morphable models – [5]) or strong supervision. More recent work has explored learning-based methods, including transforming autoencoders [12], predictive gating pyramids [19], and inverse graphics networks [17]. This paper distinguishes itself by combining a recurrent architecture for handling sequential transformations with a focus on *weakly-supervised* disentangling of identity and pose, a contribution highlighted by the authors and supported by their experimental results. The use of action units to control pose is a clever mechanism for navigating the pose manifold. 

The strengths of this paper are considerable. The proposed method is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly explained. The experimental results on both faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach, with qualitative renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition results supporting the claims. The curriculum training strategy is also a valuable addition, improving both rendering quality and prediction accuracy. The disentangled identity features are a particularly interesting outcome, offering a potential benefit beyond view synthesis.

However, the paper does have weaknesses. As noted by the authors, the model currently supports only discrete rotation angles dictated by the training data, lacking a mechanism for continuous angle control. This limits the flexibility of the system. Furthermore, the fixed-length sequence training, while simplifying implementation, feels somewhat at odds with the inherent flexibility of recurrent networks. A more dynamic sequence length approach could potentially improve performance. While the results are strong, a comparison to more recent generative models (e.g., GAN-based approaches) would strengthen the evaluation. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel combination of recurrent networks, disentangled representation learning, and action unit control for 3D view synthesis.
*   Strong experimental results on two datasets, demonstrating both rendering quality and the utility of disentangled features.
*   Clear and well-written paper with a compelling motivation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited to discrete rotation angles.
*   Fixed-length sequence training restricts the full potential of the recurrent architecture.



**Overall:** I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field. While the limitations are present, the strengths and novelty of the approach outweigh them. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the points raised above. Specifically, the authors should discuss the limitations of discrete angles and potentially explore avenues for continuous control in future work.
