- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for 3D view synthesis from a single image focusing on object rotation.  
- Tackles the ill-posed problem by training on large class-specific datasets (faces, chairs) and using recurrence to model transformation sequences.  
- Key innovation: disentangles latent identity and pose factors to produce view-invariant features without class labels.  
- Draws on related work (multi-view stereo, SFM, chair generation, Inverse Graphics Network) and mental rotation literature to motivate design choices.  
- Architecture inspired by transforming auto-encoders and predictive gating pyramids, with clear distinctions highlighted.  
- Novelty: combines recurrent structure with disentangled representations and action units for pose manifold traversal.  
- Achieves unsupervised disentangling; identity units are effective for cross-view recognition.  
- Uses curriculum learning to progressively increase rotation sequence length, improving training stability and performance.  
- Provides extensive experiments on Multi-PIE and chair datasets with qualitative and quantitative results; includes KNN baseline comparison.  
- Writing and figures are clear and help explain the model and results.  
- Limitation: relies on discrete, pre-defined rotation steps, lacking continuous-angle control.  
- Limitation: training uses fixed-length sequences, which contradicts RNNs’ variable-length flexibility and is not fully justified.  
- Limitation: focuses only on rotations; does not address other transformations like scaling, translation, or deformation.  
- Limitation: comparisons to more advanced state-of-the-art 3D reconstruction/view-synthesis methods are limited.  
- Recommendation: overall strong paper with significant contributions; recommends acceptance with minor revisions to discuss fixed-sequence rationale and plans for continuous rotation control.
