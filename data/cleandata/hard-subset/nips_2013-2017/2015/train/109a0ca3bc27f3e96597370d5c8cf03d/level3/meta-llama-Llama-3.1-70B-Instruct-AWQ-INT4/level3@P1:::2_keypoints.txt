- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that applies out-of-plane rotations by disentangling identity and pose with action units controlling pose.
- Extends architecture with recurrence to keep identity fixed and update pose over sequences, supporting longer rotation trajectories.
- Main contribution: unsupervised disentangling of identity and pose producing discriminative, view-invariant identity features for cross-view recognition.
- Uses curriculum training to improve rendering quality and long-term rotation prediction.
- Network trained end-to-end; qualitative renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition results support the method.
- Well-written paper with clear motivation and component explanations.
- Thorough discussion of related work and clear positioning of the contribution.
- Strength: novel and effective approach to the challenging problem of 3D view synthesis using recurrence and disentangled representations.
- Strength: ability to learn long-term dependencies and generate view-invariant features useful for applications like face recognition and object manipulation.
- Limitation: supports only discrete rotation angles determined by training data, no continuous-angle interpolation provided.
- Limitation: trained with fixed-length sequences, reducing the typical flexibility expected from recurrent networks.
- Applicability may be restricted in scenarios requiring continuous rotations or variable-length sequences.
- Originality: combination of recurrent nets and disentangled representations is novel in the 3D view synthesis context, though components individually are not entirely new.
- Strong experimental results claimed (both qualitative and quantitative).
- Overall rating: 8/10; reviewer confidence: 8/10.
