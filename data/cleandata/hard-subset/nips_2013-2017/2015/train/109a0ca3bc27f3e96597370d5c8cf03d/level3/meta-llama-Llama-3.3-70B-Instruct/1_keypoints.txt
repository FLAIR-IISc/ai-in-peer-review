- Proposes Norm-POEM, a self-normalized risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) that avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Claims Norm-POEM improves generalization over the conventional unbiased importance-sampling estimator.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with a concise problem and solution presentation.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on several multi-label classification datasets demonstrating improved performance.  
- Detailed analysis of results, including effects of loss translations and the role of variance regularization.  
- Well-motivated theoretical and practical rationale for using a self-normalized estimator in BLBF.  
- Assumes substantial background in BLBF and importance sampling, which may reduce accessibility for non-experts.  
- Experimental scope is limited to multi-label classification, leaving generalization to other problem types uncertain.  
- Lacks a more detailed comparison to other related BLBF and importance-sampling approaches.  
- Overall contribution is significant and potentially broadly useful within BLBF.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated method with convincing empirical results and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility, narrow experimental domain, and insufficient comparison to prior work.
