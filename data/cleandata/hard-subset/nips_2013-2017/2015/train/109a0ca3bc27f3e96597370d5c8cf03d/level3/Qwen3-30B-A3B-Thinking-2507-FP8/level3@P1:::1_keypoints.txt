- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views from a single 2D image by learning disentangled identity and pose representations.  
- Uses sequential transformations and action units in a recurrent structure to traverse the pose manifold and handle long-term dependencies.  
- Employs curriculum learning to train on short trajectories before longer multi-step rotations.  
- Demonstrates qualitative results on Multi-PIE (faces) and chair CAD models with smooth rotation trajectories and preserved identity.  
- Shows weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose that emerges from the training objective without explicit regularization.  
- Cross-view recognition experiments indicate improved robustness with curriculum training (e.g., RNN6 vs RNN1 performance gap).  
- Chair interpolation results suggest the model can generate novel objects by interpolating identity units.  
- Lacks comprehensive quantitative image-quality metrics (e.g., PSNR, SSIM, FID), relying mainly on qualitative observations.  
- KNN baseline comparison is insufficient and limited to specific points rather than a thorough angle-wise comparison.  
- Fails to compare against relevant geometry-based state-of-the-art methods (e.g., 3D morphable models, multi-view stereo), leaving evaluation context incomplete.  
- Experimental scope is narrow (only faces and chairs) and not shown to generalize to more complex objects, non-axis rotations, or scenes with occlusions/multiple objects.  
- Figures are too small for proper visual assessment and the paper contains minor grammatical errors.  
- Needs stronger quantitative evidence for disentanglement (e.g., mutual information between latent factors) and more rigorous baseline comparisons.  
- Well-written and clearly structured, but the inadequate evaluation and limited scope weaken the paper’s claimed significance; more extensive experiments are needed.
