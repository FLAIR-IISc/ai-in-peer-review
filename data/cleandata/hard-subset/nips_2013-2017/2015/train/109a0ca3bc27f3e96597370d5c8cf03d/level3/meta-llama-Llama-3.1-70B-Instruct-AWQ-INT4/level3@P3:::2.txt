This paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for 3D view synthesis, which can be trained end-to-end on the task of rendering rotated objects starting from a single image. The main contributions of this work include a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network, a weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose, and the ability to generate realistic rotation trajectories with a control signal supplied at each step by the user.

The authors relate their work to previous research in 3D invariant recognition, disentangling latent factors of variation, and image generation tasks. They also compare their approach to other methods, such as the transforming autoencoder, the Predictive Gating Pyramid, and the Inverse Graphics Network.

The proposed network architecture consists of a deep convolutional encoder, shared identity units, recurrent pose units with rotation action inputs, and a deep convolutional decoder. The authors use a curriculum training procedure to gradually increase the difficulty of training by increasing the trajectory length. They also employ a novel hidden unit regularizer to disentangle the latent factors of variation.

The authors evaluate their model on two datasets: Multi-PIE and Chairs. They demonstrate the ability of their model to synthesize high-quality images of both face and complex 3D objects in a wide range of rotational angles. They also evaluate the discriminative performance of disentangled identity units through cross-view object recognition.

The results show that the proposed model can generate realistic rotation trajectories and achieve competitive results against state-of-the-art methods. The authors also demonstrate the ability of their model to interpolate across the identity manifold of chairs at fixed pose and traverse the pose manifold while fixing the identity.

The strengths of this paper include:

* A novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network architecture that can be trained end-to-end on the task of rendering rotated objects starting from a single image.
* A weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose that can be achieved through curriculum training.
* The ability to generate realistic rotation trajectories with a control signal supplied at each step by the user.
* Competitive results against state-of-the-art methods on two datasets.

The weaknesses of this paper include:

* The model supports only discrete rotation angles determined by the training data, and no method is provided for continuous-angle support.
* The network is trained with fixed-length sequences, which conflicts with typical recurrent network flexibility.

Overall, this paper presents a novel approach to 3D view synthesis and demonstrates its effectiveness on two datasets. The proposed model has the potential to be applied to a wide range of applications, including robotics, computer vision, and computer graphics.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to 3D view synthesis.
* The proposed model is evaluated on two datasets and demonstrates competitive results against state-of-the-art methods.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed approach and its contributions.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The model has some limitations, including the support for only discrete rotation angles and fixed-length sequences.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the proposed approach and its limitations.
