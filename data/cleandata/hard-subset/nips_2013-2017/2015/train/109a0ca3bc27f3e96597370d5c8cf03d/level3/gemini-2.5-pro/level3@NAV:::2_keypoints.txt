- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views from a single image.
- Disentangles object identity and pose by mapping inputs to separate identity and pose representations.
- Uses sequence of "action units" to recurrently update pose while keeping identity fixed.
- Decoder reconstructs the object's image and mask at each new pose.
- Builds on prior generative work but introduces a recurrent formulation to handle long transformation trajectories.
- Employs curriculum learning to stabilize training and enable longer rotation sequences.
- Main contribution: elegant weakly-supervised disentanglement learned by re-rendering across long rotations.
- Quantitative cross-view recognition shows learned identity features are highly discriminative and competitive with supervised methods.
- Curriculum learning empirically improves both rendering quality and discriminative feature power.
- Strengths: clear writing, well-explained model, strong qualitative and quantitative results on face and chair datasets.
- Limitations: constrained to discrete rotation angles from training data, no method for continuous/arbitrary-angle generalization.
- Limitation: trained with fixed-length sequences, underutilizing recurrent networks' ability to handle variable-length sequences.
- Overall: high-quality, original contribution that advances generative view synthesis and representation learning, with clear directions for future work.
