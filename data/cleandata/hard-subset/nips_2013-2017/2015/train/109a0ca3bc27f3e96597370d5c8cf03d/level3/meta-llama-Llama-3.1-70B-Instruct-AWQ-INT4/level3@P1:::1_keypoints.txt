- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for out-of-plane rotation synthesis from a single image, applied to faces and chairs.  
- Trains the network end-to-end to generate realistic rotation trajectories with user-supplied control signals.  
- Key contributions: recurrent encoder-decoder architecture, realistic rotation trajectories, learning view-invariant features without class labels, and curriculum training.  
- Paper is well-written with clear method descriptions and well-designed experiments.  
- Visual results are clear but figures could be larger for readability.  
- Evaluation is largely qualitative and lacks sufficient quantitative metrics.  
- Missing comparisons to state-of-the-art methods, especially geometry-based approaches.  
- Experiments are limited to Multi-PIE and Chairs datasets, reducing evidence of generality.  
- Needs testing on non-static-axis and non-repeating rotation trajectories to assess generalization.  
- Lacks detailed analysis such as error analysis and ablation studies to explain performance.  
- Minor grammatical errors present that should be corrected.  
- Original and interesting use of recurrent transformations for 3D view synthesis.  
- Potentially significant impact, but significance is weakened by insufficient experimentation and analysis.  
- For acceptance: novelty, curriculum training benefits, and clarity support acceptance.  
- Against acceptance: qualitative evaluation, limited datasets, and insufficient comparisons argue against acceptance.  
- Recommended improvements: add quantitative metrics and SOTA comparisons, expand datasets, perform error/ablation analyses, test broader rotation scenarios, and discuss limitations/extensions.
