The paper "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis" proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to synthesize novel views of a 3D object from a single image. The network is trained end-to-end on the task of rendering rotated objects starting from a single image. The authors demonstrate the quality of its predictions for human faces on the Multi-PIE dataset and for a dataset of 3D chair models.

The paper is closely related to previous work on 3D invariant recognition, disentangling latent factors of variation, and image generation. The authors draw inspiration from mental rotation experiments and propose a recurrent neural network to model the process of pose manifold traversal. The network consists of four components: a deep convolutional encoder, shared identity units, recurrent pose units with rotation action inputs, and a deep convolutional decoder.

The main contributions of this work are the development of a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for learning to apply out-of-plane rotations to human faces and 3D chair models, the generation of realistic rotation trajectories with a control signal supplied at each step by the user, and the learning of discriminative view-invariant features without using class labels.

The authors evaluate their model on the Multi-PIE and Chairs datasets and demonstrate its effectiveness for synthesizing 3D views of unseen object instances. The proposed curriculum training by gradually increasing trajectory length of training sequences yields both better image appearance and more discriminative features for pose-invariant recognition.
