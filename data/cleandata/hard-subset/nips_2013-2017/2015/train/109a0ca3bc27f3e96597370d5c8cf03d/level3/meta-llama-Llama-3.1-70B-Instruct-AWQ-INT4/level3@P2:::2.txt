This paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing novel views of 3D objects from a single image. The main idea is to disentangle the identity and pose of the object using a shared encoder and decoder network, with action units controlling the pose. The network is trained end-to-end using a curriculum learning approach, which gradually increases the difficulty of the training sequences.

The paper is well-written, with clear motivation and explanation of the components. The authors provide a thorough discussion of related work, including recent advances in computer vision and graphics. The proposed approach is compared to several state-of-the-art methods, including the transforming autoencoder, Predictive Gating Pyramid, and Inverse Graphics Network.

The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on two datasets: Multi-PIE and Chairs. The results show that the model can generate high-quality renderings of novel views, and that the disentangled identity units can be used for cross-view object recognition. The authors also demonstrate the ability to generate novel chairs by interpolating between two existing ones.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed approach is novel and well-motivated, with a clear explanation of the components and their roles.
* The experiments are thorough and well-designed, with a clear evaluation of the proposed approach against several state-of-the-art methods.
* The results are impressive, with high-quality renderings and good performance on cross-view object recognition.

However, there are also some weaknesses:

* The model supports only discrete rotation angles determined by the training data, with no method provided for continuous-angle support.
* The network is trained with fixed-length sequences, which conflicts with the typical flexibility of recurrent networks.

Overall, the paper is well-written and well-motivated, with impressive results on two datasets. However, the limitations of the approach, including the discrete rotation angles and fixed-length sequences, should be addressed in future work.
