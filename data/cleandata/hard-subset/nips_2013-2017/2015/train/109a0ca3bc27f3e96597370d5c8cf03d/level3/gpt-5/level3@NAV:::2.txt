This paper proposes an action-conditioned recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for 3D view synthesis from a single image. The encoder disentangles identity and pose; discrete “action units” drive local pose updates; and the decoder reconstructs both an image (faces and chairs) and a foreground mask (chairs). A recurrent extension keeps identity fixed and repeatedly updates pose to traverse longer rotation trajectories. Trained end-to-end with curriculum learning, the model produces qualitatively smooth renderings and, importantly, learns view-invariant identity codes that support cross-view recognition without class supervision. The work is well-positioned with respect to transforming autoencoders, gating models (PGP), chair generators, and inverse graphics (IGN), and contributes a clear action-conditioned, recurrent traversal of the pose manifold with emergent disentanglement.

Quality: The methodology is sound, with coherent architecture choices and a clear training objective. The curriculum ablations convincingly show gains in both rendering fidelity and recognition as sequence length increases. Quantitative evidence includes MSE on chairs and cross-view recognition on Multi-PIE and chairs. However, baselines are limited (KNN for chairs; a supervised CNN for faces) and exclude stronger contemporary generative baselines (e.g., IGN-style decoders or STN-like controls). The model supports only discrete angles determined by the training set, and the recurrent training uses fixed unroll lengths, undercutting the usual flexibility of RNNs.

Clarity: The paper is very clearly written, with explicit architectures, training schedules, and data preprocessing. Figures effectively show long-horizon behavior and curriculum effects. Reproducibility seems feasible given the details provided.

Originality: While related ideas exist in disentangling, inverse graphics, and action-conditioned prediction, combining a recurrent, action-selective pose update with weakly supervised disentanglement and showing recognition benefits is a novel and timely contribution.

Significance: Results are compelling for faces and chairs; the identity units’ discriminative utility increases the impact beyond image synthesis. The approach should be of interest to NIPS audiences in generative modeling and representation learning.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, well-motivated model with end-to-end training
- Unsupervised identity/pose disentanglement with measurable recognition gains
- Strong qualitative long-trajectory renderings; curriculum is effective
- Simple action interface enabling controlled pose traversal

Cons against acceptance:
- Discrete-angle limitation; no mechanism for continuous control
- Fixed-length training contradicts variable-length RNN promise; limited evaluation of generalization beyond trained lengths
- Comparisons to stronger baselines and perceptual metrics are missing

Suggestions: Parameterize actions continuously (e.g., angle embeddings or SO(3) exponential maps) to enable arbitrary rotations; train with randomly sampled sequence lengths (and possibly scheduled sampling) to improve length generalization; add perceptual metrics/baselines to strengthen claims.
