- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that models 3D view synthesis as sequential small rotations with identity and pose units.
- Trains with curriculum learning to increase rotation trajectory complexity and learns disentangled representations without explicit 3D reconstruction or class labels.

- Technically sound architecture designed for long-term pose manifold traversal dependencies.
- Comprehensive experiments on faces and chairs with qualitative and quantitative results.
- Curriculum learning effectively improves synthesis quality and representation disentanglement.
- Cross-view recognition shows identity units become increasingly view-invariant with curriculum training.
- Ablation study demonstrates progressive capability gains as sequence length increases.

- Lacks thorough comparison to recent 3D view synthesis methods (e.g., NeRF and implicit representations).
- Limited theoretical analysis on why disentanglement emerges without explicit regularization.
- Does not address limitations for objects with complex textures, heavy self-occlusion, or articulated parts.
- Mask prediction for chairs is mentioned but not thoroughly evaluated.
- No discussion of computational efficiency relative to other methods.

- Paper is well-organized with clear model description and helpful figures.
- Experimental setup, datasets, architectures, and training procedures are clearly detailed.
- Figures effectively demonstrate model capabilities and curriculum learning progression.

- Connection to Shepard and Metzler’s mental rotation could be more explicitly explained.
- The term "weakly-supervised disentangling" is not clearly defined.
- Action unit encoding (1-of-3) lacks detailed justification.

- Novel combination of recurrent networks and disentangled representations for 3D view synthesis.
- Important insight that disentanglement can emerge from rotation prediction without explicit regularization.
- Offers an alternative to explicit 3D reconstruction by handling long-term dependencies through recurrence.

- Similarity to Predictive Gating Pyramid (PGP) and need for a more detailed comparison weakens novelty claim.
- Curriculum learning use is not novel in itself, though its application here is new.
- Insufficient differentiation from related models like the Inverse Graphics Network (IGN).

- Addresses a significant problem: single-image novel view synthesis without explicit 3D models.
- Learning disentangled representations without class labels is significant for unsupervised learning and transfer.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains over KNN baselines and generative interpolation capabilities.

- Significance limited by evaluation on only faces and chairs rather than arbitrary objects.
- Unclear scalability to complex scenes or articulated objects common in real-world settings.
- Practical applications are mentioned but not concretely demonstrated or compared to alternatives.

- Overall assessment: strong paper with clear technical contribution, thorough experiments, and valuable insights on weakly-supervised disentanglement.

- Recommendation: Accept for NIPS.

- Request to add comparisons with recent NeRF and implicit neural representation methods.
- Clarify and precisely define what is meant by "weakly-supervised disentangling."
- Expand discussion of limitations for complex textures, self-occlusions, and articulated parts.
- Provide evaluation showing the contribution of the mask prediction component for chairs.
- Offer theoretical or empirical analysis explaining why disentanglement emerges without explicit regularization and the role of curriculum learning.
- Better articulate the connection between the model’s behavior and Shepard & Metzler’s mental rotation findings.
