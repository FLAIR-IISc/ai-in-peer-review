- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize 3D views from a single image by modeling pose traversal as sequential small rotations with action units.  
- Uses curriculum learning to progressively train longer rotation sequences, enabling long-term pose modeling.  
- Learns disentangled identity and pose representations weakly supervised, without explicit regularization.  
- Technically sound methodology with well-motivated recurrence for long-term dependencies.  
- Curriculum strategy effectively improves training for longer rotation trajectories and disentangling.  
- Comprehensive experiments on Multi-PIE (faces) and CAD chairs with qualitative and quantitative results.  
- Ablation studies (RNN1 to RNN16) clearly show benefits of longer curriculum stages for synthesis and representation.  
- Careful evaluation includes appropriate baselines (KNN for chairs, CNN for recognition) and acknowledges limitations (e.g., landmark sensitivity).  
- Exceptionally clear writing, organized presentation, and informative figures (Figure 2 unrolled RNN).  
- Original combination of recurrence, action units, and curriculum learning for pose manifold traversal is novel.  
- Weakly-supervised disentangling emerging from rotation objective is a significant and original contribution.  
- Advances state of the art by handling longer rotations, disentangling without supervision, and demonstrating on faces and chairs.  
- Strength: novel recurrent + curriculum approach for 3D view synthesis.  
- Strength: demonstrates weakly-supervised identity/pose disentangling without explicit regularization.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation and strong quantitative improvements with longer training.  
- Strength: clear writing and effective visualizations aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited exploration on highly complex 3D objects beyond chairs.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to more recent 3D-aware generative models (e.g., NeRF variants).  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of sensitivity to facial landmark localization.  
- Weakness: does not explore generalization to other transformations (deformation, lighting).  
- Weakness: lacks quantification of computational efficiency vs. other methods.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound paper with meaningful contributions — recommended for acceptance.  
- Suggestions: analyze landmark sensitivity and mitigation strategies; compare/acknowledge NeRF-like models; discuss extending to other transformations; report computational complexity/training time.
