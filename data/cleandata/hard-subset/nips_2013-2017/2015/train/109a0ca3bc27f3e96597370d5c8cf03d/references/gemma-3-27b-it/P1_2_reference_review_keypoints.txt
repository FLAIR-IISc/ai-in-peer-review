- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views from a single image via simulated rotations.  
- Uses action units to control local movements along a learned nonlinear pose manifold and identity units to preserve object characteristics.  
- Achieves weakly-supervised disentangling of pose and identity without explicit 3D model recovery or labeling.  
- Demonstrates results on faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs with image synthesis, cross-view recognition, and latent-space interpolation.  
- Builds on prior work (transforming autoencoders, predictive gating pyramids, Inverse Graphics Network) and connects to mental rotation psychology.  
- Novelty lies in combining recurrent networks, action units for controlled manifold traversal, and disentangling as a byproduct.  
- Curriculum learning is used and shown to improve performance.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides network/training details aiding reproducibility.  
- Strong empirical evaluation with reasonable comparison to a KNN baseline.  
- Limited to rotation transformations; does not address deformations or lighting changes.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, unit sizes, action encoding).  
- Qualitative evaluation, especially for chairs, is somewhat limited and could use more visual examples or a user study.  
- Comparison is missing against stronger state-of-the-art rendering methods that use explicit 3D reconstruction.  
- Theoretical justification of the pose manifold and action units is limited and could be more rigorous.  
- Overall judged a strong, promising contribution with compelling results and worthy of acceptance (strong accept, 7/10).
