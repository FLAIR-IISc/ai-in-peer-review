## Review of "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for 3D view synthesis from a single image, specifically focusing on rotations. The core idea is to model pose manifold traversal using recurrent units with action inputs, allowing for controlled rotation of the object in the latent space. The authors demonstrate weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose representations, achieving competitive results on face and chair datasets, and showing potential for generating novel objects through interpolation.  The work is motivated by the observation of human mental rotation and aims to bridge the gap between graphics and vision by learning 3D transformations without explicit 3D model recovery.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Approach:** The combination of recurrent networks, action inputs, and a convolutional architecture for controllable 3D rotation is a compelling and relatively novel approach.  It moves beyond traditional geometry-based methods and recent approaches reliant on explicit 3D reconstructions.
* **Weakly-Supervised Disentangling:**  The emergence of disentangled representations for identity and pose *without* explicit supervision is a significant achievement. This is demonstrated through the cross-view recognition experiments, and the ability to interpolate between identities.
* **Curriculum Learning:** The curriculum learning strategy is well-motivated and demonstrably improves performance, particularly for longer rotation sequences. This addresses a key challenge in recurrent modeling of sequential transformations.
* **Solid Experimental Validation:** The experiments on both Multi-PIE and chair datasets are well-designed and provide a good evaluation of the proposed method.  Comparison to a KNN baseline for chair synthesis is particularly helpful.
* **Clear Writing and Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, making it relatively easy to follow the methodology and results.  Figure 1 and 2 are helpful visual aids.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty of Components:** While the *combination* is novel, the individual components (CNN encoders/decoders, recurrent networks) are well-established.  The paper doesn't introduce fundamentally new building blocks.
* **Quantitative Gains Modest:** While the RNN16 model outperforms the KNN baseline on chair synthesis, the margin is not overwhelmingly large.  Further gains, or a more dramatic improvement over existing methods, would strengthen the impact.
* **Interpolation Results Not Fully Convincing:** While the interpolation examples are visually interesting, the qualitative evaluation doesn’t offer strong quantitative measures of interpolation quality.  It’s difficult to assess the fidelity of the generated interpolations without a more rigorous evaluation.
* **Lack of Ablation Studies:**  The paper would benefit from more detailed ablation studies. For example, what is the impact of the action unit encoding (1-of-3)? How sensitive is the model to the choice of curriculum learning schedule?
* **Discussion of Limitations:** While the authors acknowledge some limitations in the conclusion, a more thorough discussion of potential failure cases and avenues for future work could enhance the paper. For instance, the lack of symmetry awareness in the RNN seems to be a limitation on chairs.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with clear mathematical formulations and a logical experimental setup. Claims are generally supported by experimental results, though some quantitative gains could be larger. It’s a complete piece of work, but further ablation studies would increase confidence.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and well-organized, though some sections (e.g., equation (1)) could benefit from more detailed explanation. The authors adequately inform the reader, though reproducing the results might require significant effort due to the complexity of the setup.
* **Originality:**  The approach is novel in its combination of techniques, but the individual components are not groundbreaking. The weakly-supervised disentangling is a particularly original aspect. The authors cite relevant related work.
* **Significance:** The results are significant as they demonstrate a promising approach to 3D view synthesis without explicit 3D modeling. The potential for generating novel objects and learning disentangled representations could be valuable for various applications. However, the practical impact is currently limited by the modest quantitative gains.



**Arguments for/against Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*  Novel combination of techniques addressing a challenging problem.
*  Demonstrated weakly-supervised disentangling is a significant contribution.
*  Solid experimental results on two datasets.
*  Clear and well-written presentation.

**Con:**

*  Quantitative gains are relatively modest.
*  Individual components are not fundamentally new.
*  Lack of thorough ablation studies.
*  Interpolation evaluation is largely qualitative.




**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **accepting** this paper with minor revisions. The work presents a novel and technically sound approach to 3D view synthesis, demonstrating promising results in a challenging problem. While the quantitative gains are not dramatic, the weakly-supervised disentangling aspect and the well-motivated approach are significant contributions. I suggest the authors address the weaknesses outlined above – specifically adding more ablation studies and providing a more quantitative evaluation of the interpolation results – to further strengthen the paper. The clarity of writing and presentation are a definite plus.
