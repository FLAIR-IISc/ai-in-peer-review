- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views from a single image by modeling pose manifold traversal with small rotation control signals.
- Introduces curriculum learning to gradually increase training sequence length, improving long-term rotation prediction and disentanglement.
- Claims weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose, with identity units achieving pose-invariant recognition comparable to supervised CNNs.
- Shows strong quantitative gains on chairs (RNN16 yields 42% MSE improvement over best KNN baseline) and qualitative results with smooth, identity-preserving transformations.
- Demonstrates identity interpolation between chairs, supporting the quality of disentangled representations.
- Thoroughly situates work relative to prior methods (IGN, Transforming Autoencoders, PGP) and uses two diverse datasets (faces, chairs) with careful experimental design.
- Ablation studies justify curriculum learning, showing longer training sequences improve synthesis and disentanglement.
- Comparison with IGN is insufficiently detailed; lacks direct quantitative comparisons on identical datasets and metrics.
- Key component "triangle" operation is under-described mathematically, making its novelty and mechanism unclear.
- Limited scope to rotations; unclear how approach generalizes to scaling, deformation, or more complex motions.
- No comparison of inference time or memory requirements versus baselines, limiting assessment of practical efficiency.
- Lacks formal disentanglement metrics (e.g., mutual information) to quantitatively measure degree of disentanglement.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with sound methodology and compelling results, recommended for accept with minor revisions.
- Required revisions: provide mathematical formulation of the "triangle" operation, direct IGN comparison, computational efficiency metrics, formal disentanglement measures, and clearer discussion of limitations.
