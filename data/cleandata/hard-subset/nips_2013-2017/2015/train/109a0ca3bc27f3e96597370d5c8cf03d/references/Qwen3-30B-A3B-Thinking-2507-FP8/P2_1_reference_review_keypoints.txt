- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views by traversing the pose manifold with sequential small rotations.  
- Key innovation: curriculum learning to grow training sequence length (1-step to long sequences) enabling disentangled identity and pose without explicit regularization.  
- Models rotations as sequences of small steps rather than predicting large rotations directly.  
- Demonstrates strong image synthesis and pose-invariant features on face and chair datasets.  
- Achieves significant quantitative gains (e.g., 42% MSE improvement over best KNN baseline).  
- Relates to prior work (IGN, transforming autoencoders, PGP) but differs by learning multi-step trajectories with control signals.  
- Paper is technically sound with a clear mathematical formulation and justified use of recurrent networks for long-term dependencies.  
- Experimental design is comprehensive with appropriate baselines (KNN, 3DMM), metrics (MSE, recognition accuracy), and ablations (RNN1–RNN16).  
- Ablation studies effectively show curriculum learning benefits for image quality and feature disentanglement.  
- Provides sufficient implementation detail for reproducibility (architectures, training, datasets).  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized; figures effectively illustrate architecture, curriculum, and results.  
- Novelty: weakly-supervised disentangling emerging from training objective and curriculum, not explicit regularizers.  
- Novel application of curriculum learning to 3D view synthesis and insight that longer sequences force identity–pose separation.  
- Significant practical impact for photo editing, VR, and training vision systems; useful disentangled features reduce need for labels.  
- Model can interpolate between object classes and produce smooth view transitions.  
- Limitations: restricted to rotation transformations; limited analysis under lighting changes or occlusion.  
- Theoretical justification for why curriculum learning works is limited; relationship between sequence length and generalization needs more exploration.  
- Recommends more theoretical analysis of curriculum learning, discussion of extensions beyond rotation, stronger quantitative comparison to IGN, and analysis of computational complexity.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, significant work with strong experiments and clear writing; reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.
