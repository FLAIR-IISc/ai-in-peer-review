- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views by traversing a learned pose manifold with action inputs.  
- Learns disentangled identity and pose representations in a weakly-supervised manner without explicit pose labels.  
- Builds on prior work (transforming autoencoders, inverse graphics, bilinear models) and is inspired by mental rotation; differs via recurrent structure and control signals.  
- Novelty: combining recurrent networks with explicit control signals for controlled 3D rotation is a promising contribution.  
- Demonstrates effective weakly-supervised disentanglement of identity and pose.  
- Uses curriculum learning to stabilize training and improve performance.  
- Strong empirical results on Multi-PIE and chairs, with visually plausible generations and quantitative gains (especially for chairs); interpolation demonstrates disentanglement.  
- Paper is clearly written and includes a helpful architecture illustration.  
- Weakness: comparisons are limited—missing modern neural rendering and stronger generative baselines.  
- Weakness: training schedule and architecture choices appear specific; paper lacks hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: latent space analysis is limited; more visualization and perturbation studies are needed.  
- Weakness: computational cost of training and inference is not reported despite potential recurrence overhead.  
- Weakness: evaluated only on single objects with clean backgrounds, limiting generalization to complex scenes.  
- For acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with significant weakly-supervised disentanglement and compelling results.  
- Against acceptance: limited comparisons, insufficient analysis of hyperparameters and latent space, and narrow application scope.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper meriting acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept (minor revisions).  
- Suggested revisions: add stronger baselines, expand latent-space analysis, discuss hyperparameter sensitivity, report computational cost, and provide a point-by-point response to reviewers.
