- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder with identity and recurrent pose units for 3D view synthesis from single images without explicit 3D models.  
- Uses curriculum learning to progressively train from single-step to multi-step rotations, enabling long-term pose traversal.  
- Learns weakly-supervised disentangled identity and pose features without explicit regularization or class labels.  
- Demonstrates strong qualitative and quantitative results on Multi-PIE (faces) and a CAD chair dataset, including smooth rotations and identity interpolation.  
- Compares against appropriate baselines (KNN for chairs, 3D morphable model for faces) and reports MSE metrics and cross-view recognition performance.  
- Ablation studies on curriculum stages (RNN1, RNN2, etc.) show curriculum learning improves pose-invariant identity representation.  
- Acknowledges limitations: poor handling of occlusions, complex lighting, non-rigid deformations, and chair symmetry issues at extreme angles.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides sufficient architectural and experimental details for reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: justify architectural choices (e.g., 5×5 convs with stride 2) versus modern alternatives like ResNet blocks.  
- Novel contribution: recurrent pose manifold traversal with action units for multi-step transformations, extending transforming autoencoders.  
- Significance: practical 3D view synthesis without 3D models and useful weakly-supervised disentangling with broad applications.  
- Strong empirical gains: e.g., RNN16 achieves 42% better MSE than best KNN baseline on chairs.  
- Strengths summarized: novel recurrent+curriculum design, strong empirical results, natural weak supervision, and identity interpolation capability.  
- Weaknesses summarized: limited discussion of limitations, restricted chair dataset, lack of comparisons to recent 3D-aware generative models, and limited disentanglement analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — technically sound, novel, significant contribution with comprehensive experiments despite evaluation scope limitations.
