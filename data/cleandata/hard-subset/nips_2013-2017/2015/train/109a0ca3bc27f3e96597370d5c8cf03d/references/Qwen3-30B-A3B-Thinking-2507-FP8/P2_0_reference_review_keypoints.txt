- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder with action units to model pose manifold traversal as a sequence of small rotations for single-image 3D view synthesis.  
- Uses curriculum learning to train for longer rotation trajectories and maintain identity via shared identity units.  
- Discovers weakly-supervised disentanglement of identity and pose without explicit regularization.  
- Evaluated on Multi-PIE faces and chair CAD models, showing high-quality synthesis and improved pose-invariant recognition.  
- Experiments are comprehensive with qualitative and quantitative results and ablation across curriculum stages (e.g., RNN16 shows strong MSE improvement).  
- Paper is technically sound, well-executed, and well-organized with clear mathematical formulation and reproducible experimental setup.  
- Minor clarity issue: action unit encoding ([100], [010], [001]) could be explained more explicitly.  
- Original contribution: recurrent architecture with control signals for pose traversal and novel weakly-supervised disentanglement finding.  
- Significance: advances view synthesis from single images, generalizes beyond class-specific 3D methods, and has practical applications (VR, photo editing, robotics).  
- Weaknesses: limited to rotations (not other transformations), lacks detailed failure-case analysis, and omits computational complexity discussion.  
- Limitation: tested only on isolated objects; handling complex scenes is not explored.  
- Recommendation: accept, with a suggestion to add discussion of limitations and failure cases in the final version.
