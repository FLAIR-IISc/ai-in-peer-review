- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that models 3D view rotations as sequences of small steps to synthesize novel views from a single image.  
- Uses curriculum learning to gradually increase rotation sequence length, enabling weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose.

- Strength: Recurrent architecture with curriculum learning effectively handles long-term dependencies, improving synthesis and feature learning.  
- Strength: Learns pose-invariant identity representations without class labels, validated by cross-view recognition experiments.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments on faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs show smooth rotations and better quantitative results than KNN baselines.  
- Strength: Clearly differentiates from related work by emphasizing recurrent structure with action inputs for sequence modeling.

- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to recent neural rendering methods (e.g., NeRF variants, 3D-GANs) for stronger benchmarks.  
- Weakness: Does not analyze failure cases or limitations (e.g., large rotations, complex textures, occlusions).  
- Weakness: Ambiguous architectural details (type of recurrent unit, activations, exact pose transformation), hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Limited theoretical justification for why recurrence is necessary versus non-recurrent long sequences.  
- Weakness: Incomplete disentanglement evaluation—no quantitative metrics (e.g., mutual information) provided.

- Overall: Technically sound, novel approach that advances view synthesis and weakly-supervised disentangling, supported by solid experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept — contributions and empirical results merit publication despite noted weaknesses.

- Suggestion: Add comparisons with recent neural rendering approaches for more complete benchmarking.  
- Suggestion: Include a failure analysis section to delineate method limitations and common errors.  
- Suggestion: Provide exact architectural specifications (recurrent unit type, parameters, activations) for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: Add quantitative disentanglement metrics (e.g., mutual information) to better evaluate identity vs. pose separation.  
- Suggestion: Report computational complexity and training time for practical assessment.
