- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that models pose traversal as small rotation steps with action inputs and curriculum learning.
- Novelty: recurrent structure with action inputs and curriculum learning for long-term rotation modeling.
- Achieves weakly-supervised disentanglement of identity and pose without explicit labels or regularization.
- Experiments are comprehensive: visual inspection, MSE, cross-view recognition, and curriculum progression (RNN1→RNN16).
- Demonstrates improved performance with longer-sequence curriculum training.
- Shows ability to interpolate between object identities and pose-invariant recognition capability.
- Comparison to state-of-the-art 3D view synthesis methods is inadequate (KNN for chairs is weak).
- Lacks comparison to modern 3D-aware generative methods (e.g., NeRF, 3D-GAN variants).
- Fails to thoroughly analyze limitations such as handling symmetric objects and performance drop at large angles.
- Connection to broader disentanglement literature (β-VAE, FactorVAE, DIP-VAE) is weak or missing.
- Ambiguous description of action units (1-of-3 encoding) and rationale for encoding choice is unclear.
- Relationship between pose manifold traversal concept and specific network operations needs clearer explanation.
- Evaluation metrics for cross-view recognition (use of cosine distance) are not well-justified.
- Does not discuss computational cost, training/inference time, or comparisons to other approaches on efficiency.
- Insufficient contrast with related models like Inverse Graphics Network (IGN)—differences not deeply explored.
- Suggests exploring alternative curriculum strategies (e.g., random sequence lengths) to improve generalization.
- Chair experiments need visual comparisons to recent 3D view synthesis methods rather than KNN.
- Specific result issues: Table 1’s claim about pose units being near-random lacks statistical tests; Figure 8’s angle behavior needs clearer explanation.
- Paper is generally well-written and organized with clear architecture description and helpful figures.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on stronger SOTA comparisons, clearer limitations discussion, and better positioning within disentanglement literature.
- Required revisions suggested: add comparisons to recent 3D view synthesis methods, expand discussion of symmetric-object limitations, strengthen disentanglement literature connections, and clarify action units/transformation process.
