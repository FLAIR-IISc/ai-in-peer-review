This paper presents a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for 3D view synthesis from a single image, focusing on rotating objects (faces and chairs). The core idea – modeling pose manifold traversal with recurrent units driven by action signals – is a compelling and novel approach to a long-standing problem. It sidesteps the need for explicit 3D reconstruction, which is a major advantage over traditional methods. The weakly-supervised disentangling of pose and identity, achieved as a byproduct of the rotation prediction task, is particularly exciting. The curriculum learning strategy is well-justified and contributes to the model’s effectiveness.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The recurrent approach to view synthesis, coupled with action-based control, is genuinely novel. It frames the problem in a fresh way, drawing inspiration from human mental rotation.
*   **Boldness:** The paper tackles a difficult ill-posed problem without relying on explicit 3D representations, which is a significant departure from many existing approaches.
*   **Weakly-Supervised Disentangling:** The emergence of disentangled identity features without explicit supervision is a very strong result, demonstrating the model's ability to learn meaningful representations.
*   **Curriculum Learning:** The use of curriculum learning is well-motivated and effective, contributing to the stability and performance of the model.
*   **Demonstrated Results:** The qualitative and quantitative results on both face and chair datasets are promising, showing the model’s ability to generate plausible rotated views.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Baselines:** While KNN is used as a baseline for chairs, more sophisticated and directly comparable methods for 3D view synthesis (e.g., other generative models or differentiable rendering approaches) would strengthen the evaluation. The argument that no methods tackle *exactly* the same chair re-rendering problem feels like a deflection.
*   **Evaluation Metrics:**  MSE is a reasonable metric for image reconstruction, but it doesn't fully capture perceptual quality. Metrics like FID or SSIM would provide a more comprehensive assessment.
*   **Ablation Studies:** The paper would benefit from more thorough ablation studies to understand the contribution of different components of the model (e.g., the recurrent units, disentangled latent space, curriculum learning).
*   **Generalization:** While the model shows promise on faces and chairs, its generalization ability to other object categories isn't demonstrated. The statement about "novel images" in the introduction is somewhat misleading, as the model is still trained on a dataset of images.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The connection between the pose manifold traversal and the recurrent network’s dynamics could be elaborated upon with more theoretical insight.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a genuinely novel and potentially impactful approach to 3D view synthesis.
*   The weakly-supervised disentangling result is significant and demonstrates the model's learning capabilities.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presents the key ideas.
*   The empirical results are promising and suggest the approach has merit.
*   The authors acknowledge limitations and suggest future directions.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The evaluation could be more comprehensive with stronger baselines and better metrics.
*   The lack of extensive ablation studies makes it difficult to fully assess the contribution of each component.
*   The generalization ability to unseen object categories remains unclear.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel and promising approach. While the evaluation could be more thorough and certain aspects could be elaborated on, the originality and potential impact of the work outweigh the weaknesses. The successful demonstration of weakly-supervised disentangling is particularly noteworthy. I believe this paper warrants acceptance, despite the minor flaws, as it represents a significant step forward in the field of 3D view synthesis and has the potential to inspire further research. I would encourage the authors to address the limitations identified in the review in future work.

**Recommendation:** Accept.
