- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that models 3D view synthesis as sequential small rotation steps.  
- Uses curriculum learning to train increasing sequence lengths, improving image quality and disentangling.  
- Learns to separate identity and pose features weakly-supervised without explicit regularization.  
- Thorough evaluation on Multi-PIE and Chair CAD shows clear improvements over baselines in both qualitative and quantitative metrics.  
- Technically sound with strong motivation against limitations of 3D model recovery approaches.  
- Recurrent design effectively handles long-term dependencies in pose manifold traversal.  
- Curriculum learning is well-justified and empirically beneficial (ablation studies support this).  
- Experiments include comprehensive qualitative examples and quantitative MSE and cross-view recognition results.  
- Paper is complete and reproducible with detailed model, training, and dataset descriptions.  
- Exceptionally well-written and clearly organized with effective illustrative figures.  
- Provides sufficient implementation detail (network architectures, training stages) for reproduction.  
- Original contribution combining recurrent nets, curriculum learning, and weakly-supervised disentangling for view synthesis.  
- Differentiates itself from prior work by using recurrence for long-term prediction and emergent disentangling.  
- Significant for applications like photo editing, VR, and reinforcement learning; shows notable MSE and recognition gains.  
- Strength: novel method combining recurrence and curriculum to achieve weakly-supervised disentangling.  
- Strength: strong experimental evaluation with clear quantitative and qualitative improvements.  
- Strength: demonstrates disentangling without explicit regularization.  
- Strength: thorough ablation studies validate curriculum training benefits.  
- Strength: well-written and clearly presented paper.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational efficiency and training cost comparisons.  
- Weakness: comparison to 3D morphable face models is qualitative; lacks quantitative evaluation.  
- Weakness: does not address complex scenes/backgrounds in current work.  
- Weakness: more analysis of what identity units capture beyond recognition would be valuable.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong contribution with compelling results.  
- Final rating: 8.5/10 (Strong Accept).  
- Additional suggestion: include computational efficiency details and deeper analysis of identity representations.
