## Review of "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing novel views of 3D objects (faces and chairs) from a single image, achieved through simulated 3D rotations. The approach eschews explicit 3D model recovery, instead learning a mapping between image space and a nonlinear pose manifold. A key element is the use of "action units" to control local movements along this manifold, coupled with shared identity units to preserve object characteristics during rotation. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through image synthesis, cross-view recognition, and even interpolation in the learned latent space.  A notable aspect is the weakly-supervised disentangling of pose and identity factors without explicit supervision.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon a rich body of work in both computer graphics and computer vision.  It correctly positions itself relative to approaches like transforming autoencoders [12], predictive gating pyramids [19], and generative models for chairs [8]. The authors also acknowledge contemporary work like the Inverse Graphics Network [17] and disentangling latent factor approaches [6, 21, 24, 29]. The novelty lies in the specific combination of recurrent neural networks, action units for controlled pose manifold traversal, and the weakly-supervised disentangling achieved as a byproduct of the rotation prediction task.  The connection to the psychology of mental rotation [22] is an interesting and motivating element.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Approach:** The recurrent network with action units offers a compelling alternative to explicit 3D reconstruction for view synthesis.  The controlled traversal of the pose manifold is a conceptually sound idea.
* **Weakly-Supervised Disentangling:**  The fact that the model learns disentangled representations of identity and pose without explicit labeling is a significant achievement. This demonstrates the power of the proposed learning objective.
* **Solid Experimental Evaluation:** The authors provide a thorough empirical evaluation on two datasets (Multi-PIE and Chairs), covering both image synthesis quality and cross-view recognition performance.  The comparisons with a KNN baseline are reasonable.
* **Curriculum Learning:** The use of curriculum learning is well-justified and demonstrably improves performance.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the core ideas accessible.
* **Reproducibility:** Details regarding network architectures and training parameters are provided, enhancing reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Transformations:** The method currently focuses solely on rotations. Expanding to other transformations (e.g., deformations, lighting changes) would be a valuable extension.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper mentions curriculum learning, further analysis of the sensitivity of the approach to hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, unit sizes, action unit encoding) would be beneficial.
* **Qualitative Evaluation:** While quantitative results are presented, the qualitative evaluation, particularly for the chair dataset, could be improved.  More visual examples and perhaps a user study to assess perceptual quality would strengthen the claims.
* **Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** The comparison to the KNN baseline is appropriate, but a comparison to more advanced rendering methods, even if they rely on explicit 3D reconstruction, would provide more context.
* **Theoretical Justification:** While the motivation from mental rotation is intriguing, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the pose manifold and the action units would enhance the paper.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*  The approach presents a novel and potentially impactful method for 3D view synthesis.
* The weakly-supervised disentangling of pose and identity is a significant contribution.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the core ideas.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The method is currently limited to rotations and could benefit from generalization to other transformations.
* A more thorough analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and a more robust qualitative evaluation are needed.
* The theoretical justification could be strengthened.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a novel and promising approach to 3D view synthesis. The authors have successfully demonstrated the effectiveness of their method through a comprehensive set of experiments. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall contribution. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NIPS.  I would rate it as a **strong accept (7/10)**, with the suggestion that the authors consider addressing the weaknesses outlined above in a final revision.
