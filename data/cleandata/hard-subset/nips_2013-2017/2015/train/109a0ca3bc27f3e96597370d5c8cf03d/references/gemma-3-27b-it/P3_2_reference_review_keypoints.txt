- Summary: Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that models 3D rotation as traversal on a pose manifold using action units; learns disentangled pose vs. identity, uses curriculum learning, and is demonstrated on faces and chairs.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on transforming autoencoders, predictive gating, mental rotation models, generative CNNs (e.g., Dosovitskiy), and inverse graphics, positioned as a recurrent, weakly-supervised sequential-transformation approach.  
- Strength — Novel approach: Recurrent pose-manifold traversal with action units offers a compelling way to synthesize 3D views without explicit 3D reconstruction.  
- Strength — Weakly-supervised disentanglement: Learns identity units invariant to pose without explicit supervision.  
- Strength — Curriculum learning: Curriculum strategy is important for training stability and improved performance.  
- Strength — Experimental validation: Good quantitative (MSE) and qualitative results on Multi-PIE and chair datasets.  
- Strength — Interpolation: Latent-space interpolation produces plausible novel identities.  
- Strength — Presentation: Paper is well-written and clear.  
- Weakness — Limited baselines: Comparisons lack stronger neural-rendering or recent image-based 3D view synthesis baselines.  
- Weakness — Action granularity: Uses a coarse 1-of-3 action encoding (cw/no-op/ccw) with no exploration of finer or continuous rotations.  
- Weakness — Limited ablation: Needs more analysis to isolate contributions (e.g., recurrent vs. non-recurrent with same action units).  
- Weakness — Potential mode collapse: Unclear if model explores full pose manifold; diversity of generated views not quantitatively assessed.  
- Weakness — Computational cost: No discussion of training/inference costs for the recurrent model.  
- For acceptance: Novel, technically sound method with significant weakly-supervised disentanglement and convincing experiments.  
- Against acceptance: Needs stronger baselines, deeper ablation/mode-collapse analysis, and justification of action-unit design.  
- Overall assessment: Strong contribution to 3D vision/generative modeling; strengths outweigh weaknesses but requires minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.
