## Review of "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing novel views of 3D objects (faces and chairs) from a single image, specifically focusing on rotating the object in 3D. The key idea is to model the 3D rotation as a traversal of a “pose manifold” using recurrent transformations controlled by action units. The authors demonstrate that this approach can generate realistic rotations, learn disentangled representations (pose vs. identity) without explicit supervision, and even perform interpolation in the latent space to generate novel object instances. The use of curriculum learning is crucial for training stability and performance.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds upon several lines of research. It acknowledges and differentiates itself from: transforming autoencoders (capsules), predictive gating pyramids, methods directly modeling mental rotation, generative CNNs for chair synthesis (e.g., Dosovitskiy et al.), and Inverse Graphics Networks. The authors correctly position their contribution as offering a recurrent, weakly-supervised approach with control signals for sequential transformations, filling a gap between these existing methods. The comparison to disentangled representation learning (e.g., bilinear models, disBM) is also relevant, highlighting the emergent disentanglement achieved through the rotation prediction task.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Approach:** The recurrent pose manifold traversal framework is a compelling way to approach 3D view synthesis without explicitly reconstructing a 3D model.  The use of action units to control local transformations is a clever idea.
* **Weakly-Supervised Disentanglement:** Achieving disentangled representations without explicit supervision is a significant contribution. The experiments demonstrate that the learned identity units are indeed relatively invariant to pose changes.
* **Curriculum Learning:** The curriculum learning strategy is convincingly argued and demonstrably effective, contributing to the stability and performance of the recurrent model.
* **Solid Experimental Validation:** The experiments on both faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs provide good evidence of the method's effectiveness.  The quantitative comparisons (MSE) and qualitative visualizations are well presented.
* **Interpolation Capability:** The demonstration of interpolating between object identities adds to the versatility and potential applications of the learned representations.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed method and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Baseline Comparison:** While the paper compares to a KNN baseline for chairs, a stronger baseline for 3D view synthesis would be beneficial, such as a recent neural rendering technique that doesn’t rely on explicit 3D reconstruction but learns directly from images. The comparison to Dosovitskiy et al. is more of a statement of differences than a direct comparison.
* **Action Unit Granularity:**  The 1-of-3 encoding for rotation (clockwise, no-op, counter-clockwise) seems somewhat coarse.  Exploring finer-grained action units or continuous rotation angles might yield even better results. The justification for this choice is not fully explored.
* **Limited Ablation Study:** A more detailed ablation study would be helpful to understand the contribution of each component of the network. For example, how much does the recurrent structure *really* contribute compared to a non-recurrent model with the same action units?
* **Potential for Mode Collapse:** While the generated images *look* realistic, it’s unclear if the model is truly exploring the full pose manifold or if it’s prone to mode collapse (generating similar views repeatedly).  A more quantitative assessment of the diversity of generated views would be valuable.
* **Computational Cost:** Recurrent networks are generally computationally expensive. A discussion of the training and inference costs of the model would be useful.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to 3D view synthesis.
* The weakly-supervised disentanglement of pose and identity is a significant achievement.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key ideas and contributions.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The baseline comparison could be strengthened.
* A more detailed ablation study and analysis of potential mode collapse would be beneficial.
* The choice of action unit granularity could be better justified.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a strong contribution to the field of 3D vision and generative modeling. The proposed recurrent pose manifold traversal framework is a novel and effective approach to 3D view synthesis, and the weakly-supervised disentanglement of pose and identity is a particularly impressive result.  While there are some areas for improvement (strengthening baselines, more extensive ablation study), the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses. 

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.** I recommend accepting the paper contingent on the authors addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by adding a stronger baseline comparison and performing a more detailed ablation study.  A brief discussion of computational cost would also be valuable.
