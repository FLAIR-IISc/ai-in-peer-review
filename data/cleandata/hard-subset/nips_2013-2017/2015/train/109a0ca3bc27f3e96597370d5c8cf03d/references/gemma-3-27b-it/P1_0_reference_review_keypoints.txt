- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views from a single image.  
- Uses action units to traverse a learned pose manifold for small rotations.  
- Disentangles pose and identity, enabling manipulation of one while preserving the other.  
- Learns view-invariant features as an emergent property without explicit supervision.  
- Employs curriculum learning to handle long rotation sequences.  
- Builds on 3D morphable models, transforming autoencoders, inverse graphics, and disentanglement literature.  
- Novel combination of recurrent architecture, explicit control signals, and implicit disentanglement.  
- Qualitative results show realistic rotations and interpolations (faces and chairs).  
- Quantitative chair rendering results are competitive versus a KNN baseline.  
- Limited to modeling rotations; other transformations (scaling, translation, deformation) are not addressed.  
- Evaluation is largely qualitative; stronger quantitative metrics for image quality and disentanglement are needed.  
- Comparison to state-of-the-art methods is limited and could be expanded.  
- Lacks detailed ablation studies on architectural choices (unit sizes, recurrent cell type) and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Well-written, clear presentation with helpful diagrams.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel contribution with promising results; accept with suggested improvements (more comprehensive evaluation and ablations).  
- Reviewer score: 7.5 / 10.
