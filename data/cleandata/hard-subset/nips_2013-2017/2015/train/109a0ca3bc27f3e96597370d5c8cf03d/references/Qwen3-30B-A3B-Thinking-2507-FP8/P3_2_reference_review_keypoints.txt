- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that models 3D view synthesis as sequential small rotations from a single image.  
- Introduces identity units (view-invariant) and pose units (transformable) to separate factors of variation.  
- Uses curriculum learning to train on progressively longer rotation trajectories.  
- Claims weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose without explicit labels.  
- Strong motivation linking 3D view synthesis to human mental rotation and clear problem formulation.  
- Novel recurrent architecture addressing long-term dependencies in rotation sequences.  
- Comprehensive experiments on Multi-PIE (faces) and chair CAD models with relevant baselines and metrics.  
- Convincing cross-view recognition results showing increasing viewpoint-invariance with longer training.  
- Provides sufficient implementation and training details for reproducibility.  
- Limited novelty; insufficiently differentiated from related work like Inverse Graphics Network (IGN).  
- Unclear mechanism and lack of analysis explaining how disentanglement emerges in the latent space.  
- No theoretical justification for using recurrence over non-recurrent alternatives for manifold traversal.  
- Missing failure-mode analysis, e.g., artifacts in complex chair rotations.  
- The term "weakly-supervised" is ambiguous and potentially misleading given training setup.  
- Limited discussion on generalization to other transformation types beyond rotation.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed contribution with incremental novelty but promising results in disentangling and view synthesis.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: clarify the "weakly-supervised" claim, analyze latent space (e.g., t-SNE), better contrast to IGN, add failure analysis, and justify curriculum learning.
