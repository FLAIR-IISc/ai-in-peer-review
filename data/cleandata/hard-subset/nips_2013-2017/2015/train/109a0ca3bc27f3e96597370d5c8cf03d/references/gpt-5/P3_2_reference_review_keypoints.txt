- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that factors latent space into time-invariant identity and time-evolving pose, driven by discrete action inputs for multi-step 3D view synthesis.
- Novelty lies in action-conditioned recurrent latent transitions plus curriculum learning for long-range pose traversal, differing from prior transforming/gated and IGN-style models.
- Architecture is plausible and well-motivated: recurrence on pose units with shared identity units and latent-space-only feedback reduces exposure bias.
- Curriculum learning is appropriate and empirically shown to improve long-range consistency and disentangling.
- Qualitative results for long rotations on faces and chairs are convincing and interpolation demos are compelling.
- Quantitative gains: lower MSE vs a KNN baseline on chairs, and cross-view recognition using identity units shows emergent view-invariance.
- Ablations over curriculum lengths (RNN1/2/4/8/16) are informative about training dynamics.
- Disentanglement claim is only indirectly supported; stronger quantification (linear probes, MI estimates) is missing.
- Baselines are limited: no non-recurrent multi-step model, no recurrent model without factorization, no IGN/STN comparisons, and no GAN/perceptual baselines.
- Image quality evaluation relies mainly on pixel MSE; perceptual metrics (SSIM, LPIPS) or user studies are absent.
- Mask branch for chairs is underspecified (loss type, effect) and lacks ablation.
- Datasets are simplified (aligned faces, synthetic chairs), so generalization to in-the-wild scenes, complex lighting, occlusion is untested.
- Action space is quantized to fixed step sizes; robustness to variable or continuous actions is not evaluated.
- Use of L2 pixel loss likely causes blur; no adversarial or perceptual losses were explored to improve fidelity.
- Paper is generally clear and reproducible but needs precise details on action-conditioned transforms, mask loss, and supervision across unrolled steps.
- The combination of ideas and emergent weakly-supervised disentangling is original and of research interest, though scope is constrained.
- Strengths: principled architecture, effective curriculum, solid qualitative results, emergent identity invariance, and reproducible details.
- Weaknesses: limited baselines and datasets, indirect disentanglement evaluation, reliance on pixel MSE, and restricted action parametrization.
- Recommended experiments/suggestions: ablations (single-step rollout, no factorization, no curriculum, no mask), compare to IGN/STN, quantify disentanglement with probes/MI, test continuous action parameterization, add perceptual/adversarial losses and evaluate on natural images.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and novel with promising results; leans toward acceptance pending stronger evaluations and clarification of action-conditioned transition details.
