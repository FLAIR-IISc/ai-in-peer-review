## Review of "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing novel views of 3D objects (faces and chairs) from a single input image. The core idea is to model 3D rotation as a traversal of a "pose manifold" using recurrent transformations guided by action units representing small rotational steps.  The model is trained in a weakly-supervised manner, learning disentangled representations of identity and pose without explicit 3D supervision or labeled data.  The authors demonstrate competitive results in both view synthesis and pose-invariant recognition, and show evidence of disentangled representations through interpolation experiments.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds upon several areas: 3D view synthesis, disentangled representation learning, and recurrent neural networks. It acknowledges connections to transforming autoencoders, predictive gating pyramids, and inverse graphics networks. However, it differentiates itself by combining these ideas into a recurrent framework with action inputs for controlled rotation, and by achieving disentanglement *without* explicit regularization or supervision.  The authors accurately position their work within the broader landscape of related research.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Approach:** The recurrent formulation of 3D view synthesis as pose manifold traversal is a novel and interesting idea.  Using action units to control the traversal allows for fine-grained control over the generated views.
* **Weakly-Supervised Disentanglement:** Achieving disentangled representations without explicit supervision is a significant accomplishment. The demonstrated ability to perform pose-invariant recognition suggests genuine disentanglement.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experiments on both faces and chairs demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, achieving competitive results compared to existing methods (e.g., KNN baseline).  The quantitative results (MSE) and qualitative visualizations are compelling.
* **Curriculum Learning:** The use of curriculum learning to train the recurrent network is a sensible and effective strategy.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The network architecture and training procedure are clearly explained.
* **Comprehensive Experimentation:** The authors evaluate their method on two distinct datasets (faces and chairs) and perform experiments covering view synthesis, cross-view recognition, and interpolation.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Transformations:** The method currently focuses solely on rotation. Expanding to other transformations (e.g., scaling, translation, deformation) would broaden the applicability of the approach.
* **Computational Cost:** Recurrent models can be computationally expensive to train and sample from.  The authors don't explicitly discuss computational costs or scalability.
* **Dependence on Action Unit Granularity:** The performance may be sensitive to the granularity of the action units (e.g., 15-degree rotations). It would be valuable to explore different granularities and their impact on results.
* **Qualitative Comparison:** While the quantitative results are good, the qualitative comparison with other methods (e.g., the 3D morphable model) is limited to a single example. More extensive comparisons would strengthen the claims.
* **Interpolation Visualizations:** The interpolation examples are somewhat difficult to interpret. Providing more detailed visualizations or quantitative metrics would help to assess the quality of the interpolated results.




**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The proposed method is well-motivated, and the experimental results are convincing. While not a theoretical breakthrough, the approach demonstrates a practical and effective solution to a challenging problem.
* **Clarity:** The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The diagrams (Figures 1 & 2) are particularly helpful.
* **Originality:** The combination of recurrent networks, action units, and weakly-supervised disentanglement is a novel contribution. It represents a meaningful advancement over existing methods.
* **Significance:** The results are important and have the potential to be used in various applications, such as image editing, 3D modeling, and computer vision tasks requiring view invariance.  The ability to learn disentangled representations without explicit supervision is a valuable contribution to the field.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to 3D view synthesis.
* The experimental results are strong and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The weakly-supervised disentanglement aspect is a significant contribution.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explained.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The current method is limited to rotation transformations.
* The computational cost of recurrent models could be a concern.
* Qualitative comparisons with other methods could be more comprehensive.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a **strong paper** that makes a valuable contribution to the field of 3D view synthesis and disentangled representation learning. The novel approach, strong experimental results, and clear writing make it a worthy candidate for acceptance to NIPS. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not outweigh the strengths of the work.

**Recommendation:** **Accept**
