## Review of "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing novel views of 3D objects (faces and chairs) given a single input image and a desired rotation.  The core idea is to learn a pose manifold traversal using recurrent units, controlled by action inputs representing small rotational steps. The authors emphasize a weakly-supervised learning approach – achieving disentangled representations of pose and identity *without* explicit supervision – as a key contribution. They also employ curriculum learning to improve training stability and performance. The paper demonstrates compelling results in both view synthesis and discriminative feature learning for object recognition.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the growing literature on 3D-aware image manipulation and disentangled representation learning.  It acknowledges relevant works like transforming autoencoders, predictive gating pyramids, inverse graphics networks, and disentangled latent variable models.  The authors effectively differentiate their approach by highlighting the use of recurrent networks for handling multi-step transformations with control signals, and the emphasis on *learning* the 3D representation rather than relying on pre-defined models or strong supervision. The connection to Shepard and Metzler's mental rotation experiments is a clever and insightful motivation.  However, the relation to recent work on Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) and related differentiable rendering techniques (e.g., PixelNeRF, NeRF++) could be acknowledged, as those represent a significant parallel trend in novel view synthesis.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of recurrent networks, action inputs for controlled transformations, and weakly-supervised disentanglement is a strong contribution.  Learning to traverse a pose manifold in this way is a compelling approach to 3D view synthesis without requiring explicit 3D supervision.
* **Empirical Results:** The experimental results on both the Multi-PIE and chair datasets are convincing.  The generated images are visually plausible, and the quantitative results demonstrate significant improvements over baselines (KNN) on the chair dataset.  The cross-view recognition experiments provide strong evidence for the disentangled representations.
* **Curriculum Learning:** The use of curriculum learning is well-motivated and demonstrably effective, addressing the challenges of training recurrent networks for long-term dependencies.
* **Weakly-Supervised Disentanglement:** The ability to learn disentangled representations *without* explicit labels is a notable achievement and aligns with a key trend in unsupervised and self-supervised learning.
* **Clarity:**  The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the proposed method. Figure 1 and 2 are helpful for understanding the network architecture.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Comparison:** While the KNN baseline is a reasonable starting point, the comparison to state-of-the-art view synthesis methods is limited.  Specifically, the paper doesn't compare against NeRF-based approaches, which have become dominant in this field. A comparative analysis would strengthen the claim of novelty and significance.
* **Action Space Simplification:** The 1-of-3 encoding for rotation actions (clockwise, noop, counter-clockwise) is a significant simplification.  While it allows for discrete control, it limits the precision and flexibility of the transformations. Exploring continuous action spaces (e.g., representing rotations as angles) could lead to more accurate and nuanced results.
* **Generalization to Complex Scenes:** The experiments focus on isolated objects (faces and chairs). It's unclear how well this method would generalize to more complex scenes with multiple objects and intricate lighting conditions.
* **Identity Unit Behavior:** The results in Table 1, showing poor performance of the pose units when identity units perform well, raises a question: is the disentanglement truly occurring, or is the network simply prioritizing accurate identity representation at the expense of pose encoding? This warrants further investigation and discussion.  It would be helpful to visualize the latent space representations to gain further insight.
* **Computational Cost:** Recurrent networks, especially with backpropagation through time, can be computationally expensive. The paper doesn't provide detailed information on training time and resource requirements.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to 3D view synthesis.
* The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The weakly-supervised disentanglement aspect is a significant contribution.
* The curriculum learning strategy is well-motivated and improves performance.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The limited comparison to state-of-the-art methods (particularly NeRF-based approaches) weakens the claims of novelty and significance.
* The simplified action space and concerns about true disentanglement warrant further investigation.
* The generalization ability to complex scenes remains unclear.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel and well-executed approach. While the lack of comparison to the most recent state-of-the-art methods is a drawback, the contributions are significant and the experimental results are compelling.  I believe the paper is a valuable addition to the literature on 3D view synthesis and disentangled representation learning and **recommend acceptance with minor revisions**.  

**Suggested Revisions:**

* **Expand the related work section** to include a discussion of NeRF-based approaches and their limitations.
* **Provide a more thorough comparison** to state-of-the-art view synthesis methods.
* **Investigate the behavior of the pose units** further and provide a more detailed analysis of the disentanglement process.  Visualization of latent space representations would be helpful.
* **Discuss the limitations of the simplified action space** and potential avenues for improvement (e.g., continuous action spaces).
* **Provide information on training time and computational resource requirements.**
