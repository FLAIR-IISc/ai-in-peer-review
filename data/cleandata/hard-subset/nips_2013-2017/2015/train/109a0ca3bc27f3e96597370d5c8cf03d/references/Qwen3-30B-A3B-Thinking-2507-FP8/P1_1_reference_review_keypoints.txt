- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that performs 3D view synthesis by traversing a pose manifold using action units for incremental rotations.  
- Uses curriculum learning to train the recurrent model for long rotation trajectories and to improve disentangling of identity and pose.  
- Learns weakly-supervised disentangled identity and pose features without explicit regularization; identity units support cross-view recognition.  
- Evaluated on Multi-PIE faces and a chair CAD dataset, showing high-quality, smooth view synthesis and novel-chair generation via identity interpolation.  
- Quantitatively outperforms baselines, e.g., 42% MSE improvement over the best KNN baseline (RNN16 vs KNN5).  
- Ablation studies demonstrate curriculum training substantially improves long-term rotation synthesis and disentangling.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides sufficient experimental and architectural details for reproducibility.  
- Figures effectively illustrate architecture and qualitative results; results are comprehensive and compelling.  
- Core idea is novel relative to transforming autoencoders, PGP, and IGN due to recurrent action-driven traversal and deterministic training.  
- Significant potential impact for vision, graphics, and AR by enabling view synthesis without explicit 3D models.  
- Strengths: novel recurrent/action-unit architecture, effective curriculum learning, emergent disentangling, strong experiments on two datasets.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient explanation of the "triangle" tensor-product operation transforming pose units.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of handling non-rigid objects, occlusions, and broader 3D reconstruction comparisons.  
- Weaknesses: no analysis of computational complexity or inference speed versus other methods.  
- Minor request: add theoretical discussion on why the recurrent/action-unit design navigates the nonlinear pose manifold.  
- Recommendation: accept — technical quality is high and contributions outweigh minor weaknesses.
