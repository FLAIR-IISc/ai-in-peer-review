- Proposes a recurrent conv encoder–decoder that splits latent space into identity units (fixed) and pose units (recurrently updated) with 1-of-3 action inputs to traverse a pose manifold; trained with pixel L2 and curriculum learning.

- Extends transforming autoencoders/gated models by adding a deep conv encoder, recurrent pose transitions controlled by actions, and curriculum training; differs from IGN via deterministic feedforward training and recurrent control.

- Spatial Transformer Networks (STN) are not cited but represent a relevant alternative transformation mechanism worth discussing.

- Strength: clear, natural recurrent pose-evolution formulation aligning with “mental rotation” intuition.

- Strength: curriculum learning yields weakly supervised disentanglement, improving cross-view recognition without explicit disentangling losses.

- Strength: pragmatic engineering (fixed upsampling, mask branch for chairs) improves stability and quality; training avoids feeding generated pixels back in.

- Strength: models can generalize beyond training horizon reasonably; identity interpolation shows a smooth latent space.

- Weakness: baselines are limited and weak (e.g., KNN for chairs); quantitative comparisons are sparse.

- Weakness: image quality evaluation relies on L2/MSE (and mask-weighted L2) only; no SSIM/PSNR/perceptual metrics or user studies, and L2-induced blur is not quantified.

- Weakness: disentanglement is not rigorously evaluated; needs direct probes (linear predictability, mutual information, classifiers).

- Weakness: action parameterization is coarse (1-of-3 discrete steps) and transition details (matrix/tensor shapes, additive terms) are under-specified, possibly limiting capacity and long-horizon stability.

- Weakness: missing ablation studies isolating recurrence vs. feedforward, action gating vs. autoregression, curriculum effects, and mask-branch contribution.

- Weakness: datasets are constrained (aligned faces within ±45°, synthetic chairs, clean backgrounds); generalization to in-the-wild images, complex backgrounds, illumination, or nonrigid objects is untested.

- Weakness: no cycle-consistency or geometric/symmetry constraints to reduce drift and improve long-rotation identity preservation.

- Quality: technically sensible with careful training and curriculum demonstration, but view-synthesis claims are mostly qualitative and empirical support is limited.

- Clarity: generally clear and organized, but the action–pose transition, mask loss, and some references (STN, multiplicative interactions) need explicit specification and better integration.

- Originality: moderately novel—meaningful combination of conv encoder–decoder, recurrent action-controlled pose updates, and curriculum; disentanglement insight is interesting but under-quantified.

- Significance: addresses an important problem and may inspire follow-up work, but impact is tempered by weak baselines, limited metrics, and narrow evaluation scope.

- Suggestions: add stronger baselines (IGN-style encoder–decoder, STN-based variant, retrieval+warping, non-recurrent multi-step decoder) and comprehensive image/recognition metrics.

- Suggestions: perform ablations on recurrence, action discretization (continuous actions), curriculum, and mask branch; clarify transition parameterization.

- Suggestions: quantitatively analyze disentanglement (linear probes, mutual information, sensitivity plots) and explore cycle-consistency or symmetry priors to reduce drift.

- Pros: simple, well-motivated recurrent formulation with action control; effective curriculum; solid qualitative results and identity interpolation.

- Cons: weak baselines/metrics, incomplete disentanglement analysis, coarse action parametrization, missing ablations, and limited generalization evidence.

- Overall recommendation: Accept (rating 7) with reviewer confidence 0.7.
