

# Peer Review: Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis

## Summary

This paper proposes a novel approach to 3D view synthesis from a single image using a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network. The authors address the challenging problem of synthesizing novel views of objects (faces and chairs) by modeling the pose manifold traversal as a sequence of small rotation steps. The key innovation is the use of a recurrent network with identity units (representing invariant object features) and pose units (representing view-dependent features), trained with curriculum learning to gradually increase the complexity of rotation trajectories. The model is trained without explicit 3D reconstruction or class labels, yet learns disentangled representations that enable both high-quality view synthesis and pose-invariant recognition.

This work builds upon several related lines of research: transforming autoencoders for image transformations [12], predictive gating networks for sequence prediction [19], and disentangled representation learning [24, 21]. It also relates to the mental rotation experiments by Shepard and Metzler [22] that inspired the recurrent approach. The paper makes a clear contribution by combining recurrent networks with disentangled representations for 3D view synthesis, and demonstrating that the disentanglement emerges naturally from the training objective without explicit regularization.

## Quality

**Strengths:**
- The paper presents a technically sound approach with clear theoretical motivation. The model architecture is well-designed to handle the long-term dependencies in pose manifold traversal.
- The authors provide comprehensive experimental validation across two distinct datasets (faces and chairs), with both qualitative and quantitative results.
- The curriculum learning approach is well-justified and effectively demonstrates how longer training sequences improve both image synthesis quality and disentangled representation learning.
- The paper carefully evaluates the model's ability to learn disentangled representations through cross-view recognition, showing that the identity units become increasingly view-invariant with curriculum training.
- The ablation study (Figure 6) effectively demonstrates the progression of the model's capabilities as curriculum training increases sequence length.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with recent methods specifically designed for 3D view synthesis, such as NeRF-based approaches [2] or recent implicit neural representations [3]. While the paper mentions "no previous methods tackle the exact same chair re-rendering problem," this claim needs to be better supported.
- The theoretical analysis of why the disentanglement emerges without explicit regularization is limited. The authors could provide more insight into the optimization dynamics that lead to this weakly-supervised disentanglement.
- The paper does not address the limitations of the approach for objects with significant texture variations or complex self-occlusions (e.g., chairs with intricate patterns or faces with heavy makeup).
- The mask prediction component for chairs is mentioned but not thoroughly evaluated. It's unclear how much this contributes to the overall performance.
- The paper doesn't discuss the computational efficiency of the proposed approach compared to other methods, which is important for practical applications.

## Clarity

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized with clear sections that logically build upon each other.
- The model description (Section 3) is detailed and includes helpful figures (Figures 1-2) that clarify the architecture.
- The experimental setup is clearly described, including dataset details, network architectures, and training procedures.
- The figures (Figures 3-9) effectively demonstrate the model's capabilities and the progression of learning through curriculum training.

**Weaknesses:**
- The connection between the mental rotation experiments by Shepard and Metzler [22] and the proposed recurrent architecture could be more explicitly explained. While the inspiration is mentioned, the paper doesn't clearly articulate how the model's behavior aligns with or differs from human mental rotation.
- The term "weakly-supervised disentangling" is used but not clearly defined. The paper demonstrates that disentanglement emerges without explicit supervision, but the "weakly-supervised" aspect could be better explained.
- Some technical details about the action unit encoding (1-of-3 encoding) could be clarified with a more detailed explanation of why this particular encoding was chosen.

## Originality

**Strengths:**
- The combination of recurrent networks with disentangled representations for 3D view synthesis is novel. While related work exists on disentanglement [24, 21] and sequence prediction [19], the specific application to 3D view synthesis with curriculum learning is original.
- The key insight that disentanglement can emerge from the rotation prediction objective without explicit regularization is a significant contribution that advances the understanding of how disentangled representations can be learned.
- The paper's approach to handling long-term dependencies through recurrence, rather than explicit 3D reconstruction, offers a different perspective from the current mainstream of 3D reconstruction methods.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper's main contribution (recurrent network for pose manifold traversal) is somewhat similar to the Predictive Gating Pyramid (PGP) [19], though the authors do make clear distinctions regarding the action inputs and disentangled representations. A more detailed comparison with PGP would strengthen the novelty claim.
- The use of curriculum learning for sequence prediction is not novel (as the authors note, it's used in [27]), though its application to 3D view synthesis is novel.
- The paper doesn't sufficiently differentiate itself from the Inverse Graphics Network (IGN) [17], which also uses a deep encoder and decoder with a similar objective. The authors mention the differences but could provide a more thorough comparison.

## Significance

**Strengths:**
- The paper addresses an important problem in computer vision: synthesizing novel views of objects from a single image without explicit 3D reconstruction.
- The ability to learn disentangled representations without class labels is significant for unsupervised learning and has implications for transfer learning and representation learning.
- The results demonstrate clear improvements over baselines (KNN) on the chair dataset, with a 42% MSE improvement over the best KNN baseline.
- The model's ability to interpolate between object classes (Figure 9) demonstrates its generative capabilities, which could be useful for applications like 3D model synthesis and virtual try-on.

**Weaknesses:**
- The significance of the work is somewhat limited by the focus on specific object classes (faces and chairs), rather than a more general approach applicable to arbitrary objects.
- The paper doesn't clearly articulate how this approach would scale to more complex scenes or objects with articulated parts, which are common in real-world applications.
- The practical applications of the method are not thoroughly explored. The paper mentions photo editing and game playing agents, but doesn't provide concrete use cases where this approach would be superior to existing methods.

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper with a clear technical contribution to the field of 3D view synthesis and representation learning. The authors present a novel approach that combines recurrence with disentangled representations to model pose manifold traversal, and they demonstrate that disentanglement emerges naturally from the training objective without explicit regularization. The experimental results are comprehensive and well-presented, showing significant improvements over baselines on both face and chair datasets.

The main limitations are the lack of comparison with recent 3D view synthesis methods (e.g., NeRF-based approaches), insufficient discussion of the method's limitations for complex objects, and some unclear explanations of key concepts like "weakly-supervised disentangling." However, these are relatively minor issues compared to the paper's overall strengths.

## Recommendation

I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS. The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of 3D view synthesis and representation learning, with strong technical content, thorough experiments, and clear insights about disentangled representation learning. The work is well-positioned to influence future research in unsupervised 3D representation learning and view synthesis.

## Specific Comments for Authors

1. **Comparison with recent 3D view synthesis methods:** Please add a comparison with recent methods like NeRF [2] and implicit neural representations [3] that have achieved state-of-the-art results in novel view synthesis. While the paper's focus is different (single-image synthesis without explicit 3D models), this comparison would strengthen the paper's positioning in the field.

2. **Clarify the "weakly-supervised" aspect:** The term "weakly-supervised disentangling" is used but not clearly defined. Please provide a more precise definition of what makes this disentanglement "weakly-supervised" compared to other approaches.

3. **Expand on the limitations:** Discuss the limitations of the approach for objects with complex textures, self-occlusions, or articulated parts. How might the model be extended to handle these challenges?

4. **Mask prediction evaluation:** The mask prediction component for chairs is mentioned but not thoroughly evaluated. Please include a brief analysis of how much this contributes to the overall performance.

5. **Theoretical insights:** Provide more theoretical insight into why the disentanglement emerges without explicit regularization. Is this related to the curriculum learning schedule? Could the authors provide some analysis of the learned representations?

6. **Connection to Shepard and Metzler:** More clearly articulate how the model's behavior relates to the mental rotation experiments by Shepard and Metzler. Does the model's performance align with the linear relationship between rotation angle and response time?

## Final Recommendation

Accept

The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of 3D view synthesis and representation learning, with strong technical content, comprehensive experiments, and novel insights about weakly-supervised disentanglement. The work is well-positioned to influence future research in unsupervised 3D representation learning.
