This paper proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for 3D view synthesis from a single image. The core idea is to model pose manifold traversal using recurrent units, where each step applies a small rotation controlled by an action input. The model learns to disentangle identity and pose information, enabling realistic image generation and surprisingly, view-invariant feature learning without explicit supervision. The work builds upon ideas from transforming autoencoders, predictive gating pyramids, and generative models for 3D objects like chairs. It differentiates itself through the recurrent structure for handling multiple rotations, the control signal input, and the focus on weak supervision.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of recurrent networks with action inputs for controlling pose manifold traversal is a novel approach to 3D view synthesis. The weak supervision aspect, achieving view-invariant features without explicit labels, is also a significant contribution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed model is well-defined, and the use of convolutional networks and recurrent units is justified. The curriculum learning strategy is a sensible approach to tackling the long-term dependency problem. The experiments on both faces and chairs demonstrate the model's ability to generate plausible rotated views.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation is clear, and the technical details are explained adequately. Figure 1 and 2 are helpful for understanding the model architecture.
*   **Significance:**  The ability to synthesize novel views from a single image has broad applications in areas like image editing, computer graphics, and robotics. The weakly-supervised disentangling of features is particularly valuable, as it avoids the need for large labeled datasets.  The results on both faces and chairs, including quantitative comparisons with a KNN baseline, demonstrate practical improvements.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Baselines:** While a KNN baseline is used, the comparison is somewhat limited.  More recent and sophisticated 3D reconstruction or view synthesis methods should be considered for a more thorough evaluation. Specifically, the inverse graphics network (IGN) mentioned in related work is a very close approach and would be important to compare against.
*   **Qualitative Evaluation:** The visual examples are good, but a more systematic evaluation of the image quality (e.g., using metrics like FID or SSIM) would strengthen the paper.
*   **Action Space:** The 1-of-3 encoding for action units is relatively simple.  Exploring more nuanced action representations (e.g., continuous angles) could potentially improve the control and precision of the rotations.
*   **Disentanglement Quantification:** While the paper claims disentanglement, it relies on the observation that pose and identity units have different performance in cross-view recognition. A more rigorous evaluation of disentanglement, using metrics like Mutual Information Gap (MIG), would be beneficial.
*   **Computational Cost:** The paper doesn't discuss the computational resources required for training and inference. Providing this information would help readers assess the practical feasibility of the approach.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to 3D view synthesis.
*   The weak supervision aspect is a significant contribution.
*   The experimental results are promising and demonstrate the model's effectiveness.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The comparison with existing methods is limited.
*   The qualitative evaluation could be more systematic.
*   The disentanglement claim could be supported by more rigorous metrics.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel and promising approach to 3D view synthesis. While there are some weaknesses, the strengths outweigh them. The technical contribution is significant, and the experimental results are encouraging. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically addressing the points related to baselines, quantitative evaluation, and disentanglement quantification.  A direct comparison to IGN would be especially important.
