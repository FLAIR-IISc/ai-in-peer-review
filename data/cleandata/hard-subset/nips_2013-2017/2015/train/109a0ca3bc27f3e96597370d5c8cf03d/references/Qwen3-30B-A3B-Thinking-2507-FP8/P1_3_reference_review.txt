

# Peer Review: "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis"

## Summary

This paper proposes a novel approach to 3D view synthesis from single images without explicit 3D model recovery. The authors develop a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network with identity and pose units, where the pose units are made recurrent to handle long-term dependencies during rotation trajectories. The key innovation is using curriculum learning to gradually increase the complexity of training sequences (from single-step to multi-step rotations), which enables the model to learn view-invariant identity features without explicit supervision. The model demonstrates strong performance on both face rotation (Multi-PIE dataset) and chair rotation (CAD model dataset), with the ability to interpolate between chair identities while preserving pose.

This work builds on recent advances in view-invariant recognition (e.g., [29]) and disentangled representations (e.g., [24, 21]), but differs by using a recurrent structure to model continuous pose manifold traversal rather than learning a single transformation. It also extends the "transforming autoencoder" concept [12] by incorporating action units and recurrence for multi-step transformations.

## Quality

The paper presents technically sound work with well-supported claims. The methodology is carefully designed to address the long-term dependency problem in pose manifold traversal through recurrent structure and curriculum learning. The experiments are comprehensive, comparing with appropriate baselines (KNN for chairs, 3D morphable model for faces) and including quantitative metrics (MSE). The ablation studies on curriculum learning stages (RNN1, RNN2, etc.) demonstrate the effectiveness of their training strategy.

The authors are careful to acknowledge limitations: they note that their model doesn't handle occlusions or complex lighting, and they acknowledge that VGG-16 outperforms their model for extreme angle offsets (over 120°) in chair recognition due to symmetry in chairs. The cross-view recognition experiments provide strong evidence for the weakly-supervised disentangling property.

## Clarity

The paper is well-written and well-organized, with clear explanations of the model architecture and training procedure. The figures effectively illustrate the model's capabilities (e.g., Figure 3 showing smooth face rotations, Figure 5 demonstrating chair synthesis, Figure 6 comparing curriculum stages). The technical details of the network architecture (Section 3.2) and experimental setup (Section 4.2) are sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.

A minor improvement would be to provide more intuition about why their specific architecture (e.g., 5×5 convolutions with stride 2) was chosen over alternatives like standard ResNet blocks, which are now common in vision tasks.

## Originality

This work makes a clear contribution to the field by addressing a challenging problem (3D view synthesis from single images without explicit 3D models) with a novel solution (recurrent pose manifold traversal with curriculum learning). The weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose features without explicit regularization is particularly novel and noteworthy.

The paper properly situates itself within the literature, distinguishing from related work on transforming autoencoders [12], predictive gating [19], inverse graphics [17], and disentangled representation learning [24, 21]. The key distinction from prior work is the recurrent structure with action units for handling multiple transformation steps rather than single-step transformations.

## Significance

The significance of this work is strong. The ability to synthesize realistic 3D views from single images without explicit 3D models has broad applications in computer vision, graphics, and augmented reality. The weakly-supervised disentangling capability is particularly valuable as it eliminates the need for class labels, making the approach more practical for real-world applications.

The results demonstrate clear improvements over baselines: for chairs, RNN16 achieves 42% better MSE than the best KNN baseline. The cross-view recognition results (Figure 8) show that the model learns increasingly view-invariant representations with longer curriculum training, achieving competitive results against supervised CNNs without using any class labels.

## Strengths

1. Novel recurrent architecture with curriculum learning effectively solves the long-term dependency problem in pose manifold traversal.
2. Demonstrates strong empirical results on both faces and chairs with clear qualitative and quantitative improvements over baselines.
3. Weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose features is a significant contribution that emerges naturally from the training objective.
4. The ability to interpolate between chair identities while preserving pose is a compelling demonstration of the disentangled representation.

## Weaknesses

1. Limited discussion of the model's limitations beyond what's mentioned in the paper (e.g., handling occlusions, complex lighting, or non-rigid deformations).
2. The chair dataset is limited to a specific CAD model set; it would be valuable to test on more diverse real-world images.
3. The comparison with other deep learning approaches for chair rotation is limited to KNN; including a comparison with recent 3D-aware generative models would strengthen the evaluation.
4. The paper doesn't provide sufficient analysis of the disentanglement quality beyond the cross-view recognition results.

## Recommendation

This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of 3D view synthesis and disentangled representation learning. The recurrent architecture with curriculum learning is well-motivated and effective, and the weakly-supervised disentangling capability is particularly novel. The experiments are comprehensive and well-executed, with clear evidence of the model's capabilities.

The paper is technically sound, well-written, and makes a significant contribution to the field. While there are some minor limitations in the evaluation scope, these do not detract from the overall quality and significance of the work.

**Recommendation: Accept**

The paper addresses an important problem with a novel solution, demonstrates strong empirical results, and makes a clear contribution to the literature on disentangled representations and 3D view synthesis. It advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way and is likely to be influential for future research in the field.
