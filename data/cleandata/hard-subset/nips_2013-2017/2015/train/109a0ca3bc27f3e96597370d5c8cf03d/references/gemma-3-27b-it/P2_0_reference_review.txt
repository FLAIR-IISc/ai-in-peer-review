## Review of "Weakly-supervised Disentangling with Recurrent Transformations for 3D View Synthesis"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for 3D view synthesis from a single image, specifically focusing on rotating objects (faces and chairs). The core idea is to learn a pose manifold traversal using recurrent units and action inputs, enabling generation of novel views. The model is trained weakly, without explicit 3D supervision, and demonstrates emergent disentanglement of identity and pose factors. The authors also employ curriculum learning to improve training stability and performance.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon several established areas: 3D view synthesis (classic geometry-based methods and recent learning-based approaches like IGN), disentangled representation learning (VAE, bilinear models), and recurrent neural networks for sequence modeling (PGP). It differentiates itself by combining these concepts in a specific way – using a recurrent network *specifically* for pose manifold traversal guided by action inputs, and achieving disentanglement as an emergent property of this process, rather than through explicit regularization.  The authors appropriately position their work within these related areas, citing relevant papers and highlighting their novel contributions.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Approach:** The formulation of 3D view synthesis as a pose manifold traversal with recurrent units and action inputs is a fresh perspective. It avoids the difficulties of explicit 3D reconstruction.
* **Weakly-Supervised Disentanglement:** Achieving disentanglement of identity and pose without explicit labels is a significant accomplishment. The observation that the recurrent training process encourages this disentanglement is insightful.
* **Curriculum Learning:**  The use of curriculum learning is well-justified and demonstrably effective, as shown in the experiments.
* **Strong Experimental Results:** The experiments on both faces and chairs demonstrate the model’s ability to generate plausible rotated views. Quantitative results (MSE) and visual comparisons to baselines (KNN, 3D morphable model) are convincing.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the network architecture and training procedure.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Transformations:** The model is currently limited to rotations. While this is a well-defined problem, it restricts the broader applicability of the approach. The authors acknowledge this in the conclusion.
* **Complexity of Network:** The architecture is relatively complex, and the number of hyperparameters (e.g., the size of the identity and pose units) is substantial. A more thorough ablation study exploring the impact of these choices would be beneficial.
* **Qualitative Evaluation:** While the visual results are compelling, a more rigorous qualitative evaluation (e.g., user studies) would strengthen the claims about the realism of the generated images.
* **Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** While comparisons to KNN and a 3D morphable model are provided, a more direct comparison to other recent generative models (e.g., GANs or VAEs tailored for view synthesis) would be valuable.  IGN is mentioned but not thoroughly compared in the experiments.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. Claims are generally supported by experimental results. While not a *complete* solution to 3D view synthesis, it presents a well-defined and thoroughly investigated contribution. The authors are reasonably careful in evaluating strengths and weaknesses.
* **Clarity:** The paper is mostly clear, although the description of the recurrent transformations could benefit from further elaboration with more intuitive explanations. The notation is consistent and the figures are helpful.
* **Originality:** The combination of recurrent networks, action inputs, and weak supervision for 3D view synthesis is a novel approach. The emergent disentanglement is also a noteworthy contribution. The paper cites relevant related work, demonstrating awareness of the existing literature.
* **Significance:** The results are important because they demonstrate a feasible approach to 3D view synthesis without explicit 3D supervision. The emergent disentanglement is also significant, as it could be applied to other tasks. The work advances the state-of-the-art in weakly-supervised 3D understanding.  Researchers and practitioners interested in 3D vision, generative modeling, and representation learning are likely to find this work valuable.



**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Novel and well-motivated approach.
* Strong experimental results on challenging datasets.
* Emergent disentanglement is a significant finding.
* Clear and well-written paper.

**Against:**

* Limited scope of transformations (only rotations).
* Relatively complex network architecture.
* Could benefit from more rigorous qualitative evaluation and comparison to more recent generative models.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

This paper presents a compelling and novel approach to 3D view synthesis. The strengths outweigh the weaknesses, and the work has the potential to significantly impact the field. I recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above (e.g., providing a more thorough comparison to state-of-the-art methods and conducting a more rigorous qualitative evaluation).  Specifically, I would like to see a brief discussion of how the proposed method would compare with recent GAN-based approaches for view synthesis in the revised manuscript.
