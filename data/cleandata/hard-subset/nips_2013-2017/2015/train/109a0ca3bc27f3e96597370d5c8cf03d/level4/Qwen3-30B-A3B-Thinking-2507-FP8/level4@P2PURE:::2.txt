

The paper introduces a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network designed to learn applying out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects, including human faces and 3D chair models. The core architecture begins with a base model where the encoder disentangles the input image into identity and pose representations. Action units are then applied to the pose representation to control rotation direction, and the decoder—comprising convolutional and upsampling layers—reconstructs the rotated object image and its corresponding mask from the identity and pose representations. To handle longer rotation trajectories, the model is extended with a recurrent structure: the identity representation remains fixed across the sequence, while the pose representation is iteratively updated via a sequence of action units. The final identity and pose representations are fed into the decoder to generate the rotated output image.  

A primary contribution is the successful disentanglement of identity/appearance and pose factors. The identity representations are demonstrated to serve as discriminative, view-invariant features for cross-view object recognition. This disentanglement further enhances rendering quality when longer rotation trajectories are employed during curriculum training stages.  

The paper is well-written, logically structured, and clearly motivates each component of the proposed method. Qualitative results for rotated image rendering and quantitative evaluations on the cross-view object recognition task provide strong empirical support, particularly for the disentangled pose and identity representations.  

Minor weaknesses require clarification:  
- The network currently supports only discrete rotation angles, dependent on the training data's predefined angles. How might the authors extend the method to handle continuous rotation angles?  
- The recurrent network is trained on fixed-length sequences, which contradicts the typical flexibility of recurrent neural networks designed for variable-length inputs.  

The work presents a novel end-to-end trained recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for rendering rotated objects from a single image. The key contribution—generative disentanglement of identity and pose factors, emerging from the recurrent rotation prediction objective—is effectively validated through comprehensive qualitative and quantitative evaluations.
