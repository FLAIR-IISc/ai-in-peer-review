- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder architecture to learn out-of-plane rotations of 3D objects from a single image.  
- Encoder disentangles input into identity and pose components; action units applied to pose control rotation direction.  
- Decoder (convolution + upsampling) generates rotated object images and corresponding masks.  
- Extends to a recurrent model for longer rotation trajectories by keeping identity fixed and updating pose via action sequences.  
- Main contribution: learned disentangling of identity/appearance and pose factors emerging from the rotation prediction objective.  
- Identity components serve as discriminative, view-invariant features for cross-view recognition tasks.  
- Curriculum training with longer rotation trajectories improves rendering predictions and disentangling.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.  
- Strong qualitative renderings and quantitative evaluations support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Limitation: supports only discrete rotation angles tied to training data—no current continuous-angle formulation.  
- Limitation: trained with fixed-length sequences, which deviates from typical variable-length recurrent network behavior.
