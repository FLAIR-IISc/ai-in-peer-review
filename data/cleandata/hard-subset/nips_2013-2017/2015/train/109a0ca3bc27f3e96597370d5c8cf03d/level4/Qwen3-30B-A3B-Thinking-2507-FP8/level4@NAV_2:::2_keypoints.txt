- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for synthesizing 3D views from single images (faces and chairs).
- Disentangles identity and pose via a recurrent architecture enabling long-term rotation prediction without explicit 3D models.
- Key contributions: recurrent structure with action units for sequential rotations; weakly-supervised identity/pose disentangling without class labels; curriculum learning for longer rotation trajectories.
- Builds on transforming autoencoders, predictive gating networks, and inverse graphics networks but achieves disentangling emergently from the rotation objective.
- Strong experimental validation on Multi-PIE and chair datasets with both quantitative (MSE) and cross-view recognition evaluations.
- Longer training sequences improve image quality and feature disentanglement; RNN16 achieves 42% better MSE than best KNN baseline.
- Exposition and reproducibility are excellent, with clear architecture diagrams and detailed training protocols.
- Originality: disentangling emerges naturally from prediction task rather than explicit factor-separation regularization used in prior work.
- High significance for graphics and vision—enables realistic rotation trajectories and view-invariant representations without class labels.
- Strengths: novel recurrent pose traversal, effective weak supervision, comprehensive quantitative/qualitative evaluation, curriculum learning benefits, generalization to novel instances.
- Weakness: currently supports only discrete rotation angles tied to training data; extension to continuous rotations is needed.
- Weakness: trained with fixed-length sequences despite RNNs' variable-length flexibility; authors should clarify rationale and generalization implications.
- Overall: a well-executed, technically sound, and significant contribution to weakly-supervised disentangling and 3D view synthesis.
