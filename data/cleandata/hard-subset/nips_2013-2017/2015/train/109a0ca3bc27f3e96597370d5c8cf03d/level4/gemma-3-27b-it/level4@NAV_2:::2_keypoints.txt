- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize out-of-plane rotations from a single image (faces and chairs).
- Encoder disentangles identity and pose; action units control pose transforms; decoder reconstructs rotated images.
- Recurrent architecture enables extended rotation sequences and models long-term dependencies.
- Trained end-to-end; learned identity units show discriminative, view-invariant properties.
- Builds on recent convnet image-generation and RNN ideas; inspired by mental rotation; avoids explicit 3D model recovery.
- Novel architecture and successful application of recurrence to 3D view synthesis.
- Emergent, unsupervised disentangling of latent factors is a significant contribution.
- Strong qualitative results on faces and chairs and quantitative cross-view recognition supporting view invariance.
- Curriculum learning is used effectively; paper is well-written and clear.
- Limitation: relies on discrete rotation angles via action units instead of continuous control.
- Fixed-length sequence training is unconventional for a recurrent network and needs clearer justification.
- Evaluation is limited—lacks comprehensive comparisons to other single-image 3D reconstruction/view-synthesis methods beyond KNN.
- Additional analysis of learned representations (visualizations/ablation studies) would provide deeper insight.
- Reviewer recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses; paper is worthy of acceptance, with suggested rebuttal improvements.
