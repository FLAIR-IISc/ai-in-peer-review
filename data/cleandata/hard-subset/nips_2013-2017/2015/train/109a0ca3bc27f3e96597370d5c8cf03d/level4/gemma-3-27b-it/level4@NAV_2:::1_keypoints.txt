- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that renders novel viewpoints from a single 2D image by learning pose manifold traversal.  
- Demonstrated on Multi-PIE faces and chairs, including class interpolation for chairs.  
- Explores curriculum learning to improve multi-step view synthesis.  
- Shows weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose via cross-view recognition.  
- Recurrent architecture is well-motivated and technically sound for capturing long-term dependencies.  
- Results produce plausible rotated views qualitatively on both datasets.  
- Related work coverage is thorough and presentation is clear.  
- Quantitative evaluation is limited and relies heavily on visual inspection; lacks standard metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS).  
- Missing comparisons to state-of-the-art geometry-based view synthesis methods.  
- Experiments are restricted to single-axis (cyclic) rotations, not exploring more complex transformations.  
- Dataset scope is relatively narrow (only Multi-PIE and chairs).  
- Minor editorial errors (e.g., “rotate” should be “rotated” at line 124).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel recurrent approach, plausible results, disentangling of factors, thorough related work, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited quantitative evaluation, no comparison to geometry-based methods, single-axis rotation only, narrow dataset scope.
