- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize rotated views from a single 2D image; evaluated on faces and chairs, with curriculum learning, disentanglement, and class interpolation experiments.  
- Model description is clear, detailed, and well-presented.  
- Evaluation is largely qualitative (visualizations) and lacks quantitative performance metrics and direct comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Visual figures need to be scaled larger for better readability.  
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with minor grammatical errors (e.g., "rotate" vs. "rotated").  
- Significance is limited by insufficient experimentation and deeper analysis.  
- Should include experiments on additional, diverse datasets to demonstrate generality beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs.  
- Should evaluate generalization to non-repeating rotation trajectories and rotations not around a static axis.  
- Needs quantitative error metrics and comparisons with geometry-based and other SOTA methods to establish competitiveness.
