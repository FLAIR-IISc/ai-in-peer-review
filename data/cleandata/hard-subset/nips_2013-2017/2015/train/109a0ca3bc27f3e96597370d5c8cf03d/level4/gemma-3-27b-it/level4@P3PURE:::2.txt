In this paper, the authors propose a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for learning to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects, such as human faces and 3D chair models. The proposed network begins with a basic model where the encoder network disentangles the input image into identity and pose units. Action units are then applied to the pose units to control the rotation direction, and the decoder network – consisting of convolutional and unsampling layers – decodes the identity and pose units into an image of the rotated object and its corresponding mask. To support longer rotation trajectories, the network is extended with a recurrent architecture where the encoded identity unit remains fixed while the pose unit is updated by a sequence of action units; both units are then fed into the decoder to generate the resulting image.

A key contribution of this paper is learning to disentangle representations for identity/appearance and pose factors. The authors demonstrate that the identity units serve as discriminative, view-invariant features in a cross-view object recognition task. Furthermore, these disentangling properties benefit prediction accuracy and improved rendering, particularly when utilizing longer rotation trajectories during the curriculum training stages of the recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network. 

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear motivation provided for each component of the proposed method. The qualitative results for predicted renderings of rotated images, along with the quantitative evaluation on the cross-view object recognition task, provide strong support for the method, particularly regarding the disentangled representations for pose and identity factors.

Several minor weaknesses are noted below, and it is hoped that the authors can address them during the rebuttal period:

- The proposed network currently supports only discrete rotation angles, dependent on the set of angles present in the training data. Do the authors have any initial ideas on how to extend the method to support continuous rotation angles?
- The recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network is trained with a fixed sequence length, which is somewhat contradictory to the typical flexibility of recurrent neural networks.

Overall, this paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network trained end-to-end for the task of rendering rotated objects from a single image. The main contribution – generatively disentangling identity and pose factors which emerges from the recurrent rotation prediction objective – is well demonstrated through both qualitative and quantitative evaluations.
