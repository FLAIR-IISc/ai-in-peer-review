- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that factors latent space into identity and action-updated pose units to synthesize novel views from a single image.
- Trains end-to-end on rotation sequences using curriculum learning to improve long-range prediction and pose traversal.
- Positioned relative to transforming/gating models, generative CNNs, and disentangling work, adding an encoder, explicit identity/pose split, and action-conditioned recurrence.
- Strength: clear, coherent architecture combining encoder–decoder CNNs with action-conditioned recurrence for long sequences.
- Strength: curriculum learning is well motivated and empirically improves synthesis quality and identity invariance.
- Strength: weakly supervised disentangling emerges, enabling cross-view recognition without labels and improved with longer curricula.
- Strength: compelling qualitative results on Multi-PIE faces and rendered chairs, including identity interpolation with consistent rotation.
- Strength: simple effective design choice to keep identity units constant and only update pose units.
- Weakness: quantitative evaluation is limited — relies on MSE/KNN; lacks perceptual metrics (SSIM/PSNR) and human studies.
- Weakness: baselines are weak or misaligned; missing stronger comparisons (IGN-like decoders, non-recurrent repeated application, gating models) and key ablations.
- Weakness: disentangling claims need more systematic analysis (linear separability, pose regression, mutual information).
- Weakness: experiments use controlled, aligned datasets; robustness to clutter, misalignment, lighting, occlusion is unclear.
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and figures informative but some visuals are too small and minor typos exist.
- Originality: combination of action-conditioned recurrence, identity/pose factorization, and curriculum is novel, but components are close to prior work and need empirical differentiation.
- Significance: promising for single-view synthesis and view-invariant representation learning, but limited by datasets and baselines.
- Suggested improvement: add SSIM/PSNR, identity preservation metrics, and a small human perceptual study.
- Suggested improvement: expand baselines and perform ablations (no curriculum, no actions, non-recurrent, gating/IGN variants).
- Suggested improvement: provide quantitative comparisons to 3D morphable models on Multi-PIE (landmark/photometric errors).
- Suggested improvement: test on more object categories and less-constrained real data to show generality.
- Suggested improvement: analyze disentanglement rigorously (mutual information, pose regression, failure cases).
- Suggested improvement: consider continuous action parameterization and evaluate generalization to unseen step sizes/trajectories.
- Suggested improvement: discuss multi-modality and whether probabilistic decoders (VAE/GAN) could handle one-to-many mappings.
- Minor comments: clarify gradient flow through recurrence vs rendered images, report parameter counts/training time/memory, and fix reference formatting.
- Arguments for acceptance: elegant, well-motivated architecture with solid qualitative results and emergent disentangling without labels.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient quantitative evaluation, weak baselines, limited datasets, and incomplete analysis of failure modes and disentanglement.
