- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to render out-of-plane rotations of 3D objects from a single image.
- Encoder disentangles input into an identity vector (appearance) and a pose vector (orientation); discrete action units modify pose.
- Decoder (convolutions + upsampling) synthesizes rotated images and segmentation masks from identity + modified pose.
- Recurrent extension keeps identity fixed and iteratively updates pose with action sequences to generate longer rotations.
- Disentanglement of identity and pose emerges from the view-synthesis objective without supervision on identity or pose.
- Learned identity features are highly discriminative and view-invariant, achieving strong cross-view recognition performance.
- Qualitative results demonstrate realistic and coherent rotated views of objects.
- Curriculum learning with longer rotation trajectories improves rendering quality and is crucial for the recurrent model’s success.
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation for each component.
- Limitation: model supports only discrete rotation angles defined by training actions.
- Limitation: recurrent network trained on fixed-length action sequences, not exploiting variable-length sequence flexibility.
- Overall: significant contribution toward learning generative, disentangled 3D structure from 2D images, supported by quantitative and qualitative evidence.
