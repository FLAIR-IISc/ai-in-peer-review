- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that generates object appearances from novel viewpoints given a single 2D image.  
- Evaluates model by rendering rotated views of faces and chairs, plus experiments on curriculum learning, disentangled representations (cross-view recognition), and chair class interpolation.  
- Model presentation is thorough and the paper is well written and easy to follow.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and predominantly qualitative, relying on visualizations that need scaling for readability.  
- Lacks quantitative performance metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Minor grammatical errors (e.g., "rotate" should be "rotated" on line 124).  
- Significance is weakened by inadequate experimentation and analysis.  
- Needs experiments on additional datasets beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs to demonstrate broader applicability and reduce the ad-hoc impression of the architecture.  
- Should explore generalization to non-repeating, non-static-axis rotation trajectories and stronger comparison to geometry-based approaches.
