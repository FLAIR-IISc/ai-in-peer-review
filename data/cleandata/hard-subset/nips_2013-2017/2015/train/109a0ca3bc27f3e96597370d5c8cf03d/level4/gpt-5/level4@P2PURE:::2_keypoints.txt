- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that learns to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects from a single image.  
- Encoder disentangles image into identity units and pose units; action units modify pose to control rotation direction.  
- Decoder (convolution + upsampling) reconstructs rotated image and object mask from identity and pose.  
- Recurrent extension updates pose units over a sequence of action units while keeping identity fixed for longer rotation trajectories.  
- Main contribution: unsupervised generative disentanglement of identity and pose, yielding view-invariant identity features for cross-view recognition.  
- Curriculum training of the recurrent model improves rendering quality for longer rotations.  
- Paper is well written, with clear motivation and strong qualitative and quantitative evaluations supporting claims.  
- Minor weakness: model only supports discrete rotation angles tied to the training set.  
- Minor weakness: recurrent encoder–decoder trained on fixed-length sequences, limiting RNN flexibility.  
- Overall: novel end-to-end recurrent model with convincingly demonstrated disentanglement and rendering/recognition benefits.
