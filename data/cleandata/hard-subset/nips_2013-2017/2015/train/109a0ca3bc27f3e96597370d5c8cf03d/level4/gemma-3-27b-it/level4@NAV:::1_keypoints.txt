- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for single-image multi-view rendering, evaluated on face and chair rotation tasks.  
- Includes supplementary studies on curriculum learning, disentangled representations via cross-view recognition, and class interpolation for chairs.  
- Model description is clear and thoroughly detailed.  
- Evaluation is mostly qualitative; visualizations need clearer scaling and presentation.  
- Lacks quantitative performance metrics and comparisons to current state-of-the-art methods.  
- Generally well-written with only minor grammatical issues (e.g., “rotate” → “rotated” on line 124).  
- Experimental scope is limited to Multi-PIE and Chairs; broader datasets are needed to show generalization.  
- Should investigate non-static-axis (non-repeating) rotation trajectories to test robustness.  
- Overall contribution is novel but weakened by insufficient experimental validation and lack of comparability with geometry-based approaches.
