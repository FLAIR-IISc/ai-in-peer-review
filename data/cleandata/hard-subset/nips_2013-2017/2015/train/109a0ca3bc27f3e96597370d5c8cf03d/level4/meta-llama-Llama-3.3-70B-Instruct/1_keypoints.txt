- Paper proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for generating object views from a single 2D image.  
- Evaluated on face and chair rotation synthesis, with experiments on curriculum learning, disentanglement (cross-view recognition), and chair class interpolation.  
- Model architecture is well-detailed and clearly presented.  
- Evaluation relies heavily on qualitative results; quantitative metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art are missing.  
- Visualizations need improvement (figures should be scaled for readability).  
- Lack of appropriate error metrics prevents meaningful performance comparison with prior work.  
- Paper is generally well-written but contains minor grammatical errors (e.g., "rotate" → "rotated" on line 124).  
- Significance is weakened by limited experimental scope and analysis.  
- Experiments should cover more datasets beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs to demonstrate generality.  
- Should investigate handling non-repeating rotation trajectories and rotations about non-static axes.  
- Claims about matching geometry-based approaches (line 51) are not sufficiently supported by experiments.
