- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects from a single image.
- Encoder disentangles input into identity and pose units; action units modify pose; decoder (conv + upsampling) outputs rotated image and object mask.
- Extends to a recurrent architecture where identity stays fixed and pose is updated by a sequence of action units for longer rotation trajectories.
- Disentangled identity units produce discriminative, view-invariant features useful for cross-view object recognition.
- Disentangling and curriculum training with longer rotation trajectories improve rendering quality and representation separation.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.
- Qualitative renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition results provide strong support for the method.
- Limitation: model supports only discrete rotation angles determined by training data; no mechanism for continuous rotations.
- Limitation: network is trained with fixed-length sequences, which conflicts with typical recurrent network usage.
- Overall: main contribution—generative disentangling of identity and pose via recurrent rotation prediction—is well demonstrated.
