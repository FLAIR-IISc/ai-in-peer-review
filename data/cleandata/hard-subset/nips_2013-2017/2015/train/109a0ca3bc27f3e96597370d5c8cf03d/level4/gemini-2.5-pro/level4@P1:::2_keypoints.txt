- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize novel 3D object views from a single image.  
- Encoder disentangles into identity units (view-invariant) and pose units (orientation); an action unit specifies discrete rotation updates in latent space.  
- Recurrent extension keeps identity units fixed and updates pose units over time to generate rotation sequences via the decoder.  
- Curriculum learning (gradually increasing rotation sequence length) improves rendered image quality and identity disentanglement.  
- Weakly-supervised training (no identity labels) produces discriminative, view-invariant identity features useful for cross-view recognition.  
- Extensive experiments on faces and chairs show strong qualitative (smooth, consistent rotations) and quantitative (recognition) results.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow with good explanations of model components.  
- Novelty/significance: modeling sequential 3D transformations with a recurrent network to achieve disentanglement is an important contribution.  
- Strength: strong empirical support and compelling visualizations validating the main claims.  
- Weakness: model uses discrete rotation angles tied to training data; extension to continuous rotations is not addressed.  
- Weakness: training on fixed-length sequences (RNN2/4/6) leaves unclear how well the model generalizes to unseen trajectory lengths.  
- Overall: a compelling, well-executed contribution showing that recurrent view prediction naturally yields disentangled identity and pose representations.
