In this paper, the authors propose a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for learning to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects, such as human faces and 3D chair models. The network begins with a basic encoder that disentangles the input image into identity and pose units. Action units are then applied to the pose units to control the rotation direction. The decoder, comprised of convolutional and upsampling layers, reconstructs an image of the rotated object and a corresponding object mask from the combined identity and pose units. To support longer rotation trajectories, the network is extended with a recurrent architecture, where the encoded identity unit remains fixed while the pose unit is modified by a sequence of action units. Finally, both identity and pose units are fed into the decoder to generate the output image.

A key contribution of this paper is the learning of disentangled representations for identity/appearance and pose factors. The authors demonstrate that the identity units function as discriminative, view-invariant features in a cross-view object recognition task. Furthermore, this disentanglement property enables more accurate rendering, particularly when utilizing longer rotation trajectories during the curriculum training of the recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear motivation provided for each component of the proposed method. Both the qualitative results – demonstrating the predicted rendering of rotated images – and the quantitative evaluation on the cross-view object recognition task provide strong support for the method, especially regarding the disentangled representations of pose and identity.

However, there are a few minor weaknesses that the authors might address during the rebuttal period:

- The proposed network currently supports only discrete rotation angles, determined by the set of angles present in the training data. Do the authors have any preliminary ideas on how to extend the method to accommodate continuous rotation angles?
- The recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network is trained with fixed-length sequences, which seems contrary to the typical behavior of recurrent neural networks. 

Overall, this paper introduces a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network trained end-to-end for rendering rotated objects from a single image. The main contribution – generatively disentangling identity and pose factors through a recurrent rotation prediction objective – is well-supported by both qualitative and quantitative evaluations.
