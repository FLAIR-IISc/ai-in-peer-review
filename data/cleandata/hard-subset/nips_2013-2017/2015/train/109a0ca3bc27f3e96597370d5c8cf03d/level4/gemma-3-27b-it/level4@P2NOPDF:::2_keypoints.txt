- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to apply out-of-plane rotations to objects from a single image.
- Encoder disentangles identity (appearance) and pose representations; action units modify pose.
- Decoder reconstructs images and object masks from combined identity and rotated pose.
- Recurrence holds identity fixed while sequentially updating pose for longer rotation trajectories.
- Demonstrates learned disentanglement: identity units act as discriminative, view-invariant features for cross-view recognition.
- Disentanglement improves rendering quality, especially with longer rotation trajectories and curriculum training.
- Clear, well-written paper with logical motivation for each component.
- Strong qualitative results (realistic rotated renderings) and quantitative evaluation supporting effectiveness.
- Concern: model currently limited to discrete rotation angles tied to training data; extension to continuous angles unclear.
- Concern: training uses fixed-length sequences despite recurrent architecture usually supporting variable-length inputs; rationale needs clarification.
