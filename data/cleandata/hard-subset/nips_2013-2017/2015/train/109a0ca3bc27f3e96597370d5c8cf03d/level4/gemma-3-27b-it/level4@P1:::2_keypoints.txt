- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects (faces, chairs).
- Encoder disentangles input into identity and pose units; action units modify pose; decoder (conv + upsampling) reconstructs rotated image and mask.
- Extends to a recurrent architecture for longer rotation trajectories: identity fixed, pose evolves through a sequence of action units, then decoded.
- Main contribution: learning to disentangle identity/appearance and pose factors via the recurrent rotation prediction objective.
- Identity units provide discriminative, view-invariant features useful for cross-view object recognition.
- Disentangling and curriculum training improve rendering performance, especially for longer rotation sequences.
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation for each component.
- Strong qualitative synthesis and quantitative cross-view recognition results supporting the approach.
- Minor weakness: method currently supports only discrete rotation angles limited by training data—asks for ideas to handle continuous angles.
- Minor weakness: recurrent network trained with fixed-length sequences—requests clarification on this design choice.
- Overall assessment: novel end-to-end method that convincingly demonstrates generative disentangling of identity and pose.
