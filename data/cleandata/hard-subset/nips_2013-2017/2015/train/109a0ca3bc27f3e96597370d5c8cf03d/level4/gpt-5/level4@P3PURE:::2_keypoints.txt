- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder network to synthesize out-of-plane rotations of 3D objects (faces, chairs) from a single image.  
- Encoder disentangles input into identity units and pose units; action units update pose; decoder (conv + upsampling) reconstructs rotated image and object mask.  
- Recurrence: identity is held fixed while pose units are iteratively updated by action sequences to support longer rotation trajectories.  
- Key contribution: learning to disentangle identity/appearance and pose factors emergently from the rotation-prediction objective.  
- Identity units are shown to be discriminative and view-invariant in cross-view object recognition experiments.  
- Curriculum training and the recurrent extension improve long-horizon rotations and rendering quality.  
- Strong empirical support: qualitative renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition results.  
- Writing and motivation are clear and the paper is easy to follow.  
- Minor weakness: model appears to support only discrete rotation angles tied to training data; extension to continuous angles is not addressed.  
- Minor weakness: training uses fixed-length sequences, which seems at odds with the expected flexibility of recurrent networks.  
- Overall: solid demonstration that a recurrent encoder–decoder can generatively disentangle identity and pose for rotation synthesis, supported by experiments.
