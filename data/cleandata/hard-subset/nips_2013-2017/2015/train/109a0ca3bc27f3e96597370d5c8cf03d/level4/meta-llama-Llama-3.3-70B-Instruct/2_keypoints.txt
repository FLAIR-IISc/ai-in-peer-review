- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to learn out-of-plane rotations of 3D objects from a single image.  
- Encodes inputs into disentangled identity and pose units, with action units controlling rotation direction.  
- Decoder (convolutions + upsampling) combines identity and pose to generate rotated images and corresponding masks.  
- Recurrent extension fixes identity and sequentially updates pose to handle longer rotation trajectories.  
- Key contribution: disentangled identity/pose yields view-invariant features with strong cross-view recognition performance.  
- Disentangling also enhances predictive rendering, especially using longer trajectories and curriculum training.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and provides strong qualitative and quantitative evidence.  
- Limitation: supports only discrete rotation angles constrained by training-data ranges; continuous angles not addressed.  
- Limitation: trained on fixed-length sequences rather than handling variable-length sequences typical of RNNs.  
- Overall: novel end-to-end generative approach that effectively disentangles identity and pose for rotation rendering.
