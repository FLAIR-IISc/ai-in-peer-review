- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder to render objects from new viewpoints given a single 2D image; tested on faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs.
- Includes experiments on curriculum learning, cross-view object recognition, and class interpolation for chairs.
- Paper is generally well written and clear, with minor grammatical errors (e.g., line 124: “rotate” → “rotated”).
- Evaluation is largely qualitative; result visualizations need proper scaling for clear assessment.
- Lacks quantitative performance metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods; should report relevant error measures.
- Limited experimentation restricts significance; needs additional datasets beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs to show generalizability.
- Should evaluate handling of non-static-axis or non-repeating rotation trajectories to test robustness.
- Current results are not shown to be comparable to geometry-based state-of-the-art approaches.
