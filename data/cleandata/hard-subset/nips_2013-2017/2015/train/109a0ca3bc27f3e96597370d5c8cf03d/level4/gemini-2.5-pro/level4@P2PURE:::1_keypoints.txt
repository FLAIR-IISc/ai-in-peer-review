- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to synthesize novel object viewpoints from a single image.  
- Demonstrates model outputs on face and chair datasets, generating rotated-view images.  
- Evaluates curriculum learning, representation disentanglement (via cross-view recognition), and class interpolation.  
- Paper is well-written and the model description is clear and detailed.  
- Major weakness: limited experimental evaluation and analysis that undermines the contribution.  
- Evaluation relies heavily on qualitative results, which are insufficient for thorough assessment.  
- Needs quantitative metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods, especially geometry-based approaches.  
- Figures showing qualitative results should be enlarged for better visibility.  
- Contains minor grammatical errors (example: line 124 "rotate" → "rotated").  
- Experiments confined to faces and chairs; evaluation on more diverse object classes is needed to show generality.  
- Should analyze generalization to more complex, non-repeating rotation trajectories beyond simple static-axis rotations.
