- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects (faces, chairs).
- Encoder disentangles identity and pose units; action units modify pose; decoder (conv + upsampling) renders rotated image and mask.
- Recurrent extension fixes identity while iteratively updating pose via action sequence to support longer rotation trajectories.
- Key contribution: learned disentangling of identity/appearance and pose, producing view-invariant identity features useful for cross-view recognition.
- Curriculum training with longer rotation trajectories improves disentangling and rendering quality.
- Qualitative and quantitative results support the method, showing good synthesized rotations and cross-view recognition performance.
- Thorough comparison to prior work (transforming autoencoder, PGP, Inverse Graphics Network) and clear articulation of differences.
- Evaluated on two datasets (Multi-PIE and Chairs) and demonstrates synthesis of 3D views of unseen instances.
- Paper is well-written, well-structured, and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.
- Technically sound; claims supported by analysis and experiments; authors acknowledge strengths and weaknesses honestly.
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques and a new perspective on 3D view synthesis.
- Significance: addresses an important CV problem with potential applications in photo editing, gaming, and robotics.
- Pro-acceptance points: novelty of recurrent encoder-decoder, effective synthesis of unseen instances, thorough evaluation, clear writing and motivation.
- Con-acceptance points: supports only discrete rotation angles tied to training data; trained with fixed-length sequences contradicting general RNN behavior; lacks clear extension to continuous rotations.
