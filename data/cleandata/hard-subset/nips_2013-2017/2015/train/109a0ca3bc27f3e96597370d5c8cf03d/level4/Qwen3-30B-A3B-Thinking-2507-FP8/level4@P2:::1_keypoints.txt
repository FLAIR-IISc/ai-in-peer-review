- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize novel 3D views from single 2D images for faces and chairs.  
- Models pose manifold traversal via sequences of small rotations using a recurrent structure to capture long-term dependencies.  
- Introduces weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose features without explicit class labels.  
- Enables user-controlled, step-by-step generation of realistic rotation trajectories.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with mostly clear figures.  
- Provides a thorough comparison with related work in image generation, 3D recognition, and disentangling.  
- Model architecture is well-motivated, with clear identity, pose, and action units enabling recurrent rotation processing.  
- Curriculum learning is justified and implemented, improving training for long rotation sequences.  
- Strength: novel recurrent architecture effectively handles long-term rotation dependencies.  
- Strength: demonstrates weakly-supervised disentangling and applicability to two datasets (Multi-PIE faces, CAD chairs).  
- Curriculum training enhances both synthesis quality and discriminative power for cross-view recognition.  
- Weakness: insufficient experimental evaluation — largely qualitative results and limited quantitative analysis.  
- Weakness: comparisons are weak (KNN baseline for chairs); lacks metrics like FID, LPIPS, or PSNR against SOTA methods.  
- Weakness: limited generalizability testing — only faces and chairs, no broader object categories.  
- Needs clearer demonstration of advancement over geometry-based approaches and comparisons to methods like NeRF.  
- Should compare against non-recurrent variants to justify necessity of recurrence.  
- Originality: recurrence-driven, naturally emerging disentangling is a novel contribution to latent factor disentanglement.  
- Clarity notes: generally clear but some figures need resizing and a few minor grammatical fixes.  
- Recommendations: add comprehensive quantitative metrics, compare to SOTA view synthesis methods, test more object categories, analyze non-axis rotations, and contrast with geometry-based approaches.  
- Overall: solid contribution but current evaluation gaps and limited scope prevent full demonstration of impact; with improvements it could be a strong NIPS paper.
