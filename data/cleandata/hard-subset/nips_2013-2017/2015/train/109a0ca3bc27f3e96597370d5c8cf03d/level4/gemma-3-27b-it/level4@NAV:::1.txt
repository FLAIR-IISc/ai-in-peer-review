This paper introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network designed to render an object from varying pivoting viewpoints, utilizing only a single 2D input image. The authors demonstrate the model’s capabilities through image generation of faces and chairs under rotation, alongside supplementary studies examining curriculum learning, disentangled representation performance via cross-view object recognition, and class interpolation specifically for chairs.

While the model is clearly presented and thoroughly detailed, the performance evaluation is limited. The experiments supporting the effectiveness of the proposed RNN model rely heavily on qualitative assessment; improving the clarity of presented visualizations through appropriate scaling would be beneficial. Incorporating a quantitative performance metric and comparing results to current state-of-the-art methods would offer a more compelling analysis than visual inspection alone. 

The paper is generally well-written and easily understood, with only minor grammatical issues present (e.g., line 124, “rotate” should be “rotated”).

However, the paper’s overall impact is weakened by insufficient experimentation and analysis. Expanding the experimental datasets beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs would be valuable. Demonstrating the model’s ability to generalize to a wider range of objects would strengthen the design’s validity and reduce its appearance as being specifically tailored to the chosen datasets. Furthermore, investigating the model’s performance with non-static axis object rotation trajectories (i.e., rotations that do not repeat) would be of interest. Ultimately, this work presents a novel deep learning approach to single-image multi-view rendering. However, it currently lacks the comprehensive experimental validation and analysis necessary to fully establish its significance and achieve comparability with established ‘geometry-based approaches’ (line 51).
