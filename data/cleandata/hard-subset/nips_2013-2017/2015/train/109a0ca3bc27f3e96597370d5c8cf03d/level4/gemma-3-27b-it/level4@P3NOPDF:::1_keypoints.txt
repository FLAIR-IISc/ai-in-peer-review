- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that renders novel viewpoints from a single 2D image.
- Demonstrated on face (Multi-PIE) and chair datasets, generating rotated views.
- Experiments include curriculum learning, cross-view recognition to evaluate representations, and chair class interpolation.
- Paper is well-written and the model architecture is clearly described.
- Evaluation is overly qualitative; lacks quantitative error metrics to assess effectiveness.
- Figures need better scaling for clearer visual inspection.
- Should compare against state-of-the-art and geometry-based methods for robust benchmarking.
- Experimental scope is limited to two object categories, reducing perceived generalizability.
- Needs testing on more diverse objects and on complex, non-fixed-axis rotation trajectories to verify true 3D understanding vs memorization.
- Minor grammatical error: “rotate” should be “rotated” (line 124).
- Overall promising approach but requires more comprehensive quantitative evaluation and expanded experiments to strengthen claims.
