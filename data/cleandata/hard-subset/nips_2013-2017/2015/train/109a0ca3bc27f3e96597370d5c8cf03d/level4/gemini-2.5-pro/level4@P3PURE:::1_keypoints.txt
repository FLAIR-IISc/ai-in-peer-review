- Proposal: recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for rendering objects from various viewpoints from a single 2D input; experiments on faces and chairs, curriculum learning, disentanglement via cross-view recognition, and class interpolation for chairs.

- Presentation: model is well-presented and detailed.

- Evaluation critique: experiments are largely qualitative and rely on visual inspection; visualization figures are too small for clear assessment.

- Recommendation: add quantitative performance measures and appropriate error metrics to enable comparison with state-of-the-art methods.

- Scope limitation: experiments are narrow (only Multi-PIE faces and chairs); testing on more object classes would strengthen claims of generality.

- Generalization concern: authors should evaluate performance on more complex, non-repeating rotation trajectories and non-static rotation axes.

- Writing quality: paper is well-written and easy to follow, with only minor grammatical errors (e.g., "rotate" → "rotated" on line 124).

- Overall assessment: novel and interesting deep learning approach but currently lacks sufficient experimentation and rigorous analysis to demonstrate competitiveness with geometry-based approaches.
