- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for learning out-of-plane rotations of 3D objects (faces, chairs).  
- Encoder disentangles input into separate identity and pose representations.  
- Action units modify the pose representation to control rotation.  
- Decoder (convolutions + upsampling) synthesizes rotated object images and masks from identity and modified pose.  
- Recurrent extension updates pose iteratively while keeping identity fixed to generate longer rotation sequences.  
- Key contribution: learned disentanglement of identity and pose factors.  
- Disentanglement validated by discriminative, view-invariant identity features on cross-view recognition.  
- Curriculum training with longer rotational trajectories improves rendering quality.  
- Manuscript is clearly written with well-articulated rationale for design choices.  
- Qualitative rendered images and quantitative cross-view recognition results convincingly support the claims.  
- Minor concerns: model appears limited to discrete training angles—ask for strategies to handle continuous rotations.  
- Minor concerns: recurrent network trained with fixed-length sequences—request justification for not using variable-length training.
