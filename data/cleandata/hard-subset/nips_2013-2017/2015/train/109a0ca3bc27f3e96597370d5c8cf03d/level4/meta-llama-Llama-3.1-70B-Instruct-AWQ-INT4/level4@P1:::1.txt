This paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for rendering an object from different rotational viewpoints given a single 2D image of the object. The authors evaluate the performance of their proposed model via its ability to generate images of faces and chairs from rotated viewpoints. They also perform additional experiments to examine the benefits of curriculum learning, to evaluate the model's performance of disentangled representations through cross-view object recognition, and to explore the model's ability to perform class interpolation with chairs.

The proposed model is well presented and detailed, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of their approach. However, the evaluation of its performance is somewhat limited. The experiments used to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed RNN model are largely qualitative, relying on visualizations of results. While these visualizations are helpful, introducing a quantitative measure of performance with an appropriate error metric would provide a more comprehensive evaluation and allow for comparison with state-of-the-art results.

In general, the paper is well written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach, and the text is free of major grammatical errors.

The significance of this paper is somewhat impaired by its limited experimentation and analysis. To strengthen the paper, the authors could include experiments with additional datasets, demonstrating the model's ability to render rotated viewpoints for different types of objects. This would help to establish the network architecture as a more general solution, rather than one tailored to a specific task. Additionally, it would be interesting to see how the proposed model could generalize to handle object rotation trajectories that deviate from a static axis (non-repeating rotations).

The paper develops a deep learning model for the task of rendering an object from different rotational viewpoints given a single 2D image of that object. Although the approach is novel and interesting, the paper lacks sufficient experimentation and relevant analysis to fully flesh out the model's significance and potential for results that are comparable to state-of-the-art geometry-based approaches.

Strengths:

* The proposed model is well presented and detailed, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of their approach.
