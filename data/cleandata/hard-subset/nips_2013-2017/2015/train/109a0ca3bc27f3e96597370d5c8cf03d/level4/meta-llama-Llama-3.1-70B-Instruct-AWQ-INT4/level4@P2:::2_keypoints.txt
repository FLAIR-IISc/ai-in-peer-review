- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that learns to render out-of-plane rotations from a single image.  
- Encoder disentangles identity and pose; decoder (conv + upsampling) reconstructs rotated image and mask.  
- Action units control pose changes; recurrent extension supports longer rotation trajectories by updating pose while fixing identity.  
- Differs from prior work (transforming autoencoders, PGP, IGN) by using convolutional architecture, action inputs, recurrence, and deterministic training instead of VAE/capsules.  
- Main contribution: emergent disentangling of identity (view-invariant) and pose, useful for cross-view recognition.  
- Curriculum training with longer rotation trajectories improves rendering and disentanglement.  
- Strong qualitative results and quantitative cross-view recognition evaluations support the method.  
- Paper is well-written and motivation for design choices is clear.  
- Limitation: supports only discrete rotation angles tied to training data; lacks continuous-angle modeling.  
- Limitation: trained with fixed-length sequences, which is at odds with general recurrent network flexibility.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10); address continuous rotations and variable-length training.
