This paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for learning to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects, such as human faces and 3D chair models. The network architecture begins with a basic model, where the encoder disentangles the input image into identity units and pose units. The action units are then applied to the pose units to control the rotation direction. Subsequently, the decoder network, comprising convolutional and upsampling layers, decodes the identity and pose units into an image of the rotated object and its corresponding mask.

To support longer rotation trajectories, the proposed network is extended to incorporate a recurrent architecture. In this architecture, the encoded identity unit of the input image remains fixed, while the pose unit is modified by a sequence of action units. Finally, both the identity and pose units are fed into the decoder to generate the resulting image.

One of the primary contributions of this paper is the learning of disentangled representations for identity/appearance and pose factors. The identity units are demonstrated to be discriminative, view-invariant features in the cross-view object recognition task. Furthermore, the disentangling properties of the network are expected to yield better rendering results when using longer rotation trajectories in the curriculum training stages for training the proposed recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network.

The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the motivation for different components of the proposed method is clearly described. The qualitative results for predicted rendering of rotated images and quantitative evaluation on the cross-view object recognition task provide strong support for the method, particularly the disentangled representations for pose and identity factors.

However, there are a few minor weaknesses that the authors may want to address in their rebuttal:

* The proposed network only supports discrete rotation angles, which are dependent on the set of rotation angles present in the training data. It would be beneficial to explore methods to extend the proposed method to support continuous rotation angles.
* The proposed recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network is trained with fixed-length sequences, which contradicts the typical characteristics of general recurrent neural networks.

Overall, this paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network trained end-to-end on the task of rendering rotated objects starting from a single image. The main contribution of generative disentangling of the identity and pose factors, which emerged from the recurrent rotation prediction objective, is well-demonstrated by the qualitative and quantitative evaluations.
