- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to generate out-of-plane rotations of 3D objects from a single image.  
- Encoder disentangles identity and pose; action units manipulate pose; decoder synthesizes rotated images and masks.  
- Recurrent design keeps identity fixed and evolves pose through a chain of action units for longer rotation trajectories.  
- Trained end-to-end with curriculum learning that progressively increases trajectory length.  
- Main contribution: explicit disentanglement of identity and pose representations.  
- Identity features are highly discriminative for view-invariant cross-view recognition, outperforming baselines.  
- Disentanglement improves rendering accuracy and generalization to unseen rotation angles.  
- Qualitative renderings and quantitative recognition results robustly support the approach.  
- Ablation studies effectively isolate and validate the role of disentanglement.  
- Writing, motivation, and method presentation are clear and well-structured.  
- Two points needing clarification in rebuttal: how to support continuous rotation angles (e.g., interpolation or continuous action spaces) and whether fixed-length recurrent training is a constraint or a design choice / if variable-length sequences can be accommodated.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—method is novel, well-executed, impactful; minor issues are addressable.
