- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize 3D rotated views from single images (faces and chairs).  
- Models pose traversal as a sequence of small rotation steps to better preserve identity.  
- Introduces a recurrent architecture capturing long-term dependencies in pose transformations.  
- Achieves weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose factors without class labels.  
- Uses curriculum learning to progressively increase rotation trajectory lengths during training.  
- Demonstrates high-quality rotation trajectories on Multi-PIE (faces) and a 3D chair dataset.  
- Provides competitive quantitative performance in cross-view object recognition.  
- Shows that curriculum learning improves both synthesis quality and identity discriminative power.  
- Finds disentangled representations emerge from the objective without explicit regularization.  
- Ablation studies support disentangling claims: identity units are view-invariant, pose units capture rotation.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and well-situated within related work.  
- Limitation: model is restricted to discrete rotation angles from the training data (not continuous).  
- Limitation: recurrent structure trained with fixed-length sequences; variable-length handling is not addressed.  
- Limitation: struggles with highly complex chair shapes and rotations beyond 180°; failure cases need analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the above concerns.
