- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize novel rotational viewpoints from a single 2D image, tested on faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs.
- Includes qualitative visualizations, curriculum learning ablation, disentangled representation analysis (cross-view recognition), and class interpolation for chairs.
- Model architecture is clearly presented and the writing is generally accessible.
- Evaluation relies almost exclusively on qualitative results and lacks standard quantitative error metrics for comparison to state-of-the-art.
- Figures need proper scaling for readability.
- Experimental scope is limited to Multi-PIE and Chairs, raising concerns about generalizability to diverse object categories.
- Does not evaluate non-static or non-repeating rotation trajectories, limiting real-world applicability assessment.
- Claim of parity with "geometry-based approaches" is unsubstantiated without rigorous quantitative benchmarks or comparisons.
- Minor grammatical issues (e.g., "rotate" → "rotated" at line 124).
- Novel architecture but absence of comprehensive quantitative evaluation, broader dataset validation, and analysis of non-standard rotation dynamics prevents meaningful assessment of contributions.
