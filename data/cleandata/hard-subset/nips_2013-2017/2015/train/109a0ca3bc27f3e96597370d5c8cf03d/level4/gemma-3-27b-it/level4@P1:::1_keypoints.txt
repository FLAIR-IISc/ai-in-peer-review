- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to render objects from new viewpoints given a single 2D image.  
- Demonstrates view synthesis qualitatively on faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs.  
- Evaluates curriculum learning benefits, disentangled representation learning (via cross-view recognition), and class interpolation for chairs.  
- Paper is well presented and easy to follow, with minor grammatical fixes needed.  
- Evaluation is largely qualitative and lacks quantitative metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Visualizations should be scaled appropriately so details are easily discernible.  
- Experiments are limited to two datasets, weakening claims of generalization.  
- Model focuses on rotation around a single axis and lacks experiments for non-repeating or complex rotation trajectories.  
- Insufficient experimentation and analysis to convincingly compare against geometry-based approaches.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel approach avoiding traditional geometry-based limitations.  
- Argument for acceptance: learns disentangled representations without explicit supervision.  
- Argument for acceptance: curriculum learning improves performance for longer rotations.  
- Argument for acceptance: generates realistic and consistent rotations for faces and chairs.  
- Argument against acceptance: lacks comprehensive quantitative comparisons and relevant baselines.  
- Argument against acceptance: unclear generalization to other object categories.  
- Argument against acceptance: needs extension to more complex rotation trajectories and a more detailed limitations discussion.
