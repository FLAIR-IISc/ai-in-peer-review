- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to render objects from different rotational viewpoints from a single 2D image.  
- Evaluated on face and chair datasets, showing generated rotations qualitatively.  
- Includes experiments on curriculum learning, disentangled representations (cross-view recognition), and class interpolation for chairs.  
- Model presentation and technical details are thorough and well explained.  
- Paper is well written and free of major grammatical issues.  
- Evaluation is largely qualitative and lacks quantitative error metrics for comparison.  
- Limited experimentation reduces significance; results may be task- or dataset-specific.  
- Missing experiments on additional datasets to demonstrate generality across object types.  
- Does not explore rotations beyond a static axis or non-repeating rotation trajectories.  
- Lacks sufficient analysis to compare with state-of-the-art geometry-based approaches.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, clear presentation, and well-written exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient quantitative evaluation, limited experiments, and weak demonstration of generalization.
