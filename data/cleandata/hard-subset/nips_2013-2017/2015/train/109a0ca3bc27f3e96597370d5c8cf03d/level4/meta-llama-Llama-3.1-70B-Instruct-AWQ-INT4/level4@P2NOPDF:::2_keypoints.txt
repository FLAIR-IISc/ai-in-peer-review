- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects (faces, chairs).
- Encoder disentangles input into identity units and pose units; action units control pose updates; decoder reconstructs rotated image and mask.
- Recurrent extension fixes identity and updates pose by a sequence of action units to support longer rotation trajectories.
- Main contribution: learning to disentangle identity/appearance and pose factors in a generative model.
- Identity units serve as discriminative, view-invariant features for cross-view object recognition.
- Disentangling and curriculum training improve rendering quality for longer rotation sequences.
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation for model components.
- Strong qualitative results for rotated renderings and quantitative support from cross-view recognition tasks.
- Limitation: model supports only discrete rotation angles tied to the training set; continuous rotations not addressed.
- Limitation: training uses fixed-length recurrent sequences, which conflicts with typical RNN flexibility.
- Overall: novel end-to-end approach with well-demonstrated generative disentangling of identity and pose.
