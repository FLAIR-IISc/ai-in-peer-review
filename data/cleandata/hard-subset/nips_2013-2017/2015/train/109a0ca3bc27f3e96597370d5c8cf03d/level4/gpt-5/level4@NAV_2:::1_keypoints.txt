- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D-rotated views from a single 2D image, evaluated on faces and chairs.  
- Authors investigate curriculum learning, disentanglement via cross-view recognition, and class interpolation for chairs.  
- Model description and technical details are clear and well presented.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak and predominantly qualitative; many visualizations are small and hard to read.  
- Paper lacks quantitative metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines with appropriate error measures.  
- Significance is limited by experiments on only Multi-PIE and Chairs, raising concerns about domain-specific tuning.  
- Should evaluate generalization to non-fixed-axis or non-repeating rotation trajectories.  
- Overall approach is novel and interesting but currently insufficiently evaluated to demonstrate impact or competitiveness with geometry-based methods.  
- Minor grammatical issues noted (example: line 124 “rotate” → “rotated”).
