- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize novel 3D views from a single image, focusing on rotation-based view synthesis.
- Evaluated on Multi-PIE faces and a chairs dataset, demonstrating rotated view generation and class interpolation for chairs.
- Core idea is compelling: recurrent modeling of pose manifold traversal captures long-term dependencies in rotation.
- Leverages curriculum learning to stabilize and improve training.
- Shows emergent disentangled representations for identity and pose without explicit regularization.
- Paper is well-written with clear architecture and training details.
- Evaluation is mostly qualitative, relying on visual inspection rather than objective metrics.
- Lacks quantitative comparisons using accepted metrics (e.g., LPIPS, SSIM) to assess performance objectively.
- Limited baseline comparisons; only KNN for chairs and missing comparisons to state-of-the-art geometry-based methods.
- Experimental scope is narrow—only faces and chairs—raising concerns about generalizability.
- Focuses on single-axis rotations; more complex multi-axis or non-repeating rotations are not explored.
- Authors acknowledge future work but should include initial results on more complex rotations in this submission.
- Recommendation leans toward rejection due to insufficient quantitative evaluation and limited experiments, with encouragement to resubmit after addressing issues.
