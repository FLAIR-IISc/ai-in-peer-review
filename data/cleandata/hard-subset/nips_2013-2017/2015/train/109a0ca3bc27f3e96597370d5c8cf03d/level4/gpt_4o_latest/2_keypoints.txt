- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects (faces, chairs) from a single image.
- Encoder splits input into identity units and pose units; action units modify pose units to control rotation direction.
- Decoder uses convolutional layers and upsampling to reconstruct the rotated image and corresponding mask from identity and pose.
- Recurrent extension keeps identity fixed while evolving pose through a sequence of action units to handle longer rotation trajectories.
- Key contribution: disentangling identity/appearance and pose representations via the recurrent rotation-prediction objective.
- Identity units are shown to be discriminative, view-invariant features for cross-view object recognition.
- Disentanglement and curriculum training improve rendering accuracy, especially for longer rotation sequences.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.
- Qualitative renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition results strongly support the method’s effectiveness.
- Limitation: network currently supports only discrete rotation angles determined by training data.
- Limitation: recurrent network is trained with a fixed sequence length, which reduces the expected flexibility of RNNs.
