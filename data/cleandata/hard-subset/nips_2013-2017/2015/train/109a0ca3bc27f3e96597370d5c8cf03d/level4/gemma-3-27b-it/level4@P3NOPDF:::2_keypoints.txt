- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that renders out-of-plane rotations from a single image by disentangling identity and pose.  
- Uses an action unit to modulate pose units and a decoder (conv + upsampling) to reconstruct rotated images and masks.  
- Recurrent extension: identity units remain fixed while pose units evolve through a sequence of action units to handle longer rotations.  
- Key contribution: learning disentangled identity (appearance) and pose representations.  
- Learned identity units serve as discriminative, view-invariant features for cross-view recognition.  
- Curriculum learning with longer rotation trajectories improves rendering quality and disentanglement.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivations for each component.  
- Qualitative renders and quantitative cross-view recognition results strongly support the method’s effectiveness.  
- Weakness: network is limited to discrete rotation angles from training data; continuous rotations/interpolation not addressed.  
- Weakness: trained with fixed sequence length despite variable-length recurrence being typical; rationale and alternatives need discussion.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to faces and chairs, so generalizability to diverse object categories is unclear.
