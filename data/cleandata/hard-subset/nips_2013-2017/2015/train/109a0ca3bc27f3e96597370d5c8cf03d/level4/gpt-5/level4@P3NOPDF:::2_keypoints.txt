- Summary: Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that decomposes input images into identity and pose units and uses action units plus a recurrent pose update to synthesize out-of-plane rotations and masks from a single image, trained with a curriculum.

- Strength: Clear, well-motivated architecture combining disentangled representations with controllable recurrent transformations.

- Strength: Identity/pose decomposition is effective—identity units are discriminative and view-invariant in cross-view recognition, pose units capture rotation.

- Strength: Action-unit mechanism provides an intuitive and effective interface for controlling rotation direction and magnitude.

- Strength: Recurrent extension enables longer rotation sequences by holding identity fixed and updating pose incrementally.

- Strength: Strong empirical support with both qualitative rendered rotations and quantitative cross-view recognition results.

- Strength: Paper is well written, easy to follow, and explains motivation and training strategy (including curriculum learning).

- Weakness/Question: Model appears limited to discrete rotation angles seen during training; how can it be extended to continuous rotations (e.g., continuous action parameterization or continuous pose manifold)?

- Weakness/Question: Recurrent model trained on fixed-length trajectories—how well does it generalize to variable-length test sequences, and can training be adapted (variable-length curriculum, randomized unrolls, scheduled sampling)?

- Suggestion: Provide more detail and ablations on mechanisms that encourage disentanglement beyond task objective and curriculum (architectural constraints, losses, regularization).

- Suggestion: Include interpolation analyses in identity and pose spaces to further validate factorization.

- Suggestion: Demonstrate or analyze continuous control by smoothly varying action inputs even if trained on discrete angles.

- Suggestion: Discuss failure cases (extreme rotations, occlusions) and identity preservation under long sequences.

- Overall assessment: Novel, elegant, and well-executed approach with convincing evidence; main limitations (discrete angles, fixed-length training) seem addressable—recommend acceptance.
