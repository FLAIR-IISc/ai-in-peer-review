- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that applies out-of-plane rotations to single images of 3D objects.  
- Encoder disentangles input into identity units and pose units; action units modify pose and a convolutional+upsampling decoder renders rotated images and masks.  
- Recurrent extension updates pose units over an action sequence while keeping identity units fixed to enable longer rotation trajectories.  
- Main contribution: learns generative disentanglement of identity (appearance) and pose factors.  
- Identity units serve as discriminative, view-invariant features useful for cross-view object recognition.  
- Disentanglement and curriculum training improve rendering quality for longer rotations.  
- Paper is well written with clear motivation for components.  
- Strong qualitative renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition results that support the approach.  
- Minor weaknesses: model only supports discrete rotation angles present in training data (no continuous-angle extension yet).  
- Minor weakness: trained with a fixed sequence length, which is atypical for recurrent networks.  
- Overall: novel end-to-end recurrent encoder-decoder with well-demonstrated generative disentanglement of identity and pose.
