- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder network to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects from a single image.
- Encoder disentangles input into identity and pose units; action units modify pose units to control rotation direction.
- Decoder (convolution + upsampling) reconstructs the rotated image and its corresponding mask from identity and pose units.
- Recurrent extension updates pose units over a sequence of action units while keeping identity units fixed to support longer rotation trajectories.
- Main contribution: learning to generatively disentangle identity/appearance and pose factors via the recurrent rotation objective.
- Identity units act as discriminative, view-invariant features for cross-view object recognition.
- Disentangling improves prediction accuracy and rendering quality, especially when using longer rotation trajectories and curriculum training.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.
- Qualitative renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition results strongly support the method.
- Limitation: method currently supports only discrete rotation angles tied to training-data angles; extension to continuous angles is not addressed.
- Limitation: the recurrent network is trained with a fixed sequence length, reducing the expected flexibility of RNNs.
