- Novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder architecture is well-explained but its contribution is undermined by poor evaluation.  
- Evaluation relies almost exclusively on qualitative visualizations; quantitative metrics are missing.  
- Figures are too small/poorly scaled, preventing detailed inspection of results.  
- Must report standard image-quality metrics (e.g., PSNR, LPIPS, SSIM) to enable rigorous assessment.  
- No benchmarking against geometry-based baselines (e.g., NeRF, 3DMM) or strong learning-based methods.  
- Experimental scope is narrow (Multi-PIE and Chairs only); generalizability to other object classes is untested.  
- Paper ignores non-axis-aligned and arbitrary rotation trajectories, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Minor grammatical errors persist (example: “rotate” → “rotated”).  
- Disentangled representation claim lacks quantitative analysis (no accuracy scores or t-SNE/metrics).  
- Class interpolation results are purely qualitative; numerical evaluation of interpolation fidelity is absent.  
- Impact of curriculum learning is not rigorously evaluated (no training curves or ablation studies).  
- Authors should include error metrics, statistical significance tests, broader benchmarks, and expanded experiments to validate claims.
