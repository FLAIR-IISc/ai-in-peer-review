- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that renders multiple viewpoints from a single 2D image.  
- Evaluated qualitatively on rotated views of faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs.  
- Includes experiments on curriculum learning, disentangled representations (via cross-view recognition), and class interpolation for chairs.  
- Model description is clear and well-detailed.  
- Performance evaluation is insufficient and primarily qualitative; result figures need larger, readable sizing.  
- Paper lacks quantitative metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Minor grammatical issues (e.g., “rotate” → “rotated” at line 124).  
- Significance limited by insufficient experimentation and analysis.  
- Needs additional datasets and more object categories to show generality beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs.  
- Should test generalization to non-fixed-axis or non-repeating rotation trajectories and compare against geometry-based approaches.
