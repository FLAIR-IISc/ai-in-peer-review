

This paper introduces a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for learning to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects, including human faces and chair models. The architecture begins with a base model where the encoder disentangles input images into identity and pose units. Action units are then applied to the pose units to control rotation direction, and the decoder (combining convolution and upsampling layers) reconstructs the rotated object image and corresponding mask from the identity and pose units. To enable longer rotation trajectories, the network is extended with a recurrent structure: the identity unit remains fixed across sequences, while the pose unit is iteratively updated via a sequence of action units before being decoded into the final rotated image.

A primary contribution is the successful disentanglement of identity/appearance and pose representations, demonstrated by the identity units serving as discriminative view-invariant features in cross-view object recognition. This disentanglement also enhances rendering quality and prediction accuracy when employing longer rotation trajectories during curriculum training.

The paper is well-written, logically structured, and clearly motivates each methodological component. Qualitative results for rotated object rendering and quantitative evaluations on cross-view recognition provide strong empirical support, particularly for the disentangled pose and identity factors.

Minor weaknesses requiring clarification in the rebuttal:
- The network currently supports only discrete rotation angles defined by the training data. How might the authors extend this to continuous rotation angles?
- The recurrent network is trained on fixed-length sequences, which contradicts the typical design of recurrent neural networks.

Overall, this work presents a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder trained end-to-end for rendering rotated objects from single images, with the key contribution of generative disentanglement of identity and pose factors being robustly validated through comprehensive evaluation.
