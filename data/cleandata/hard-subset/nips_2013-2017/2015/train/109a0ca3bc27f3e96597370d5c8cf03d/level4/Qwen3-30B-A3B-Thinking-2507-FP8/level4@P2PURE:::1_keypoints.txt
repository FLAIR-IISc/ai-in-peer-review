- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to render objects from diverse pivoting viewpoints given a single 2D input image.  
- Evaluated on image generation for faces and chairs, plus experiments on curriculum learning, disentangled representations (cross-view recognition), and class interpolation for chairs.  
- Paper is clearly presented and well-structured, with minor grammatical errors (e.g., “rotate” → “rotated” on line 124).  
- Evaluation lacks rigor: relies predominantly on qualitative visualizations and figures are inadequately scaled for readability.  
- Needs quantitative performance metrics and standardized error measures to compare against state-of-the-art.  
- Significance is undermined by insufficient experimentation and analysis.  
- Should validate on additional datasets beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs to demonstrate generality and avoid appearing ad hoc.  
- Should explore generalization to non-static rotation trajectories (e.g., non-repeating rotations or rotations not about a fixed axis).  
- Cannot meaningfully compare to state-of-the-art geometry-based methods due to the absence of comprehensive experiments and relevant analyses.
