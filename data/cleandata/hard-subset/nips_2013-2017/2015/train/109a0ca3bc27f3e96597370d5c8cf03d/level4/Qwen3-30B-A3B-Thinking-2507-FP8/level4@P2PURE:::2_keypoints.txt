- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects (faces, chairs).  
- Encoder disentangles identity and pose; action units alter pose; decoder reconstructs rotated image and mask.  
- Recurrent extension: identity stays fixed while pose is iteratively updated via action sequence for longer rotations.  
- Primary contribution: successful generative disentanglement of identity and pose factors.  
- Learned identity representations are discriminative, view-invariant features for cross-view recognition.  
- Disentanglement and curriculum training improve rendering quality for longer rotation trajectories.  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and components are clearly motivated.  
- Strong qualitative and quantitative results support the method and disentangled representations.  
- Minor weakness: model supports only discrete rotation angles tied to training data; extension to continuous angles is unclear.  
- Minor weakness: recurrent network is trained on fixed-length sequences, which limits the typical RNN flexibility for variable-length inputs.
