- Introduces a novel recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that learns to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects (faces and chairs) from single images.
- Encoder disentangles input into identity and pose units, enabling separate handling of appearance and pose.
- Action units manipulate pose units to control rotation direction; decoder (conv + upsampling) reconstructs rotated image and mask from identity and pose.
- Extends to a recurrent model for longer rotation trajectories: identity units fixed across sequences, pose units iteratively updated by action sequences.
- Primary contribution is successful disentanglement of identity and pose, with identity units serving as discriminative, view-invariant features for cross-view recognition.
- Disentanglement improves rendering quality and prediction accuracy, especially with longer rotation trajectories learned via curriculum training.
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and clearly motivates each methodological component.
- Provides strong empirical support: qualitative rotated renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition evaluations validate the approach.
- Minor weakness: model currently supports only discrete rotation angles tied to training data—extension to continuous rotations is unclear.
- Minor weakness: recurrent network trained on fixed-length sequences, which seems to contradict typical RNN design and requires clarification.
- Overall: presents a novel end-to-end recurrent encoder–decoder with robustly validated generative disentanglement of identity and pose.
