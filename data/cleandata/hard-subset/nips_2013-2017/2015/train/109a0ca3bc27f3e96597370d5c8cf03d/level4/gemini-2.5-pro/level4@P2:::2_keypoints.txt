- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that disentangles view-invariant identity and view-dependent pose, using action vectors to synthesize new 3D views from a single image.  
- Extends to an RNN that holds identity fixed while recurrently updating pose via action sequences, learning disentanglement weakly-supervised and benefiting from curriculum training.  
- Key contribution: emergent disentanglement of identity and pose without labels, a significant result for representation learning.  
- Strong empirical support: realistic, smooth rotations for faces and chairs; better MSE than a k-NN baseline; learned identity features yield competitive cross-view recognition.  
- Curriculum learning is effective, improving both synthesis quality and discriminative power of identity features.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and presents experiments and results effectively.  
- Weakness: model uses discrete rotation steps tied to dataset viewpoints—unclear how to handle continuous rotation angles.  
- Weakness: curriculum training on fixed-length sequences raises questions about generalization to arbitrary-length action sequences at test time.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, and well-executed work with impressive results; strong recommendation for acceptance.
