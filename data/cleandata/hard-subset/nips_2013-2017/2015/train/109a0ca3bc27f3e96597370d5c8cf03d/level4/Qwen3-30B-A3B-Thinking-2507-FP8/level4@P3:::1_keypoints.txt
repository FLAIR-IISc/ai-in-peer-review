- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views from single 2D images by traversing the pose manifold via recurrent pose units and curriculum learning, aiming for weakly-supervised identity/pose disentanglement.  
- Recurrent architecture and curriculum learning effectively address long-term dependency in pose traversal, enabling high-quality multi-step rotations.  
- Disentanglement of identity and pose emerges without explicit regularization, validated by strong cross-view recognition performance comparable to supervised CNNs.  
- Strong experimental design: quantitative MSE gains over KNN baselines, and ablation over curriculum stages (RNN1–RNN16) shows progressive improvements in synthesis and representation.  
- Paper is technically solid, well-motivated, and clearly written, with a notable contribution that disentanglement arises naturally from the rotation-prediction objective.  
- Evaluation scope is limited to faces and chairs; adding more diverse object categories (e.g., ShapeNet/cars/animals) would better demonstrate generalizability.  
- Image-quality assessment relies on MSE; perceptual metrics (LPIPS, SSIM) should be reported to better capture synthesis quality.  
- Comparisons to geometry-based methods could be expanded to include practical aspects like computational efficiency and inference time.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add a sentence about broader dataset applicability and include LPIPS scores in quantitative results.
