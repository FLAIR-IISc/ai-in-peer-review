- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects from a single image.  
- Encoder disentangles input into identity units and pose units; action units control pose updates; decoder (conv + upsampling) reconstructs rotated image and object mask.  
- Recurrent extension: identity code fixed, pose code updated by a sequence of action units to generate multi-step rotations.  
- Curriculum learning is used to train longer rotation sequences in the recurrent model.  
- Main contribution: learned disentanglement of identity and pose yields view-invariant, discriminative identity units for cross-view recognition.  
- Disentanglement also improves rendering quality, particularly for longer rotation trajectories.  
- Paper is well written with clear motivation; qualitative renderings and quantitative recognition results strongly support the approach.  
- Minor weakness: model handles only discrete rotation angles from the training set—no clear extension to continuous rotations.  
- Minor weakness: recurrent model trained with fixed-length sequences—questions about enabling variable-length training and inference.
