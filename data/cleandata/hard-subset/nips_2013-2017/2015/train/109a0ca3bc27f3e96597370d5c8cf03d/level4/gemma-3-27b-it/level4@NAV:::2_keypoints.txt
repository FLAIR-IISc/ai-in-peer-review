- Introduces a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for applying out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects (faces and chairs).  
- Encoder disentangles input into identity and pose components; action units manipulate pose; decoder (convolution + upsampling) reconstructs image and object mask.  
- Recurrence: identity is fixed while pose representation evolves through action units for longer rotation sequences.  
- Key contribution: successfully learns disentangled identity and pose representations.  
- Learned identity representations are view-invariant and effective for cross-view object recognition.  
- Disentanglement improves predictive rendering, especially with longer rotation trajectories and curriculum training.  
- Paper is clearly written, logically structured, and components are well motivated.  
- Qualitative results show realistic rotated image renderings.  
- Quantitative evaluation demonstrates improved cross-view recognition supporting the approach.  
- Minor concern: model is limited to discrete rotation angles determined by training data; extension to continuous angles would be valuable.  
- Minor concern: using a fixed sequence length during training seems inconsistent with typical recurrent network behavior.  
- Overall: a compelling end-to-end recurrent encoder-decoder that demonstrates benefits of generative disentangling for single-image rotated object rendering.
