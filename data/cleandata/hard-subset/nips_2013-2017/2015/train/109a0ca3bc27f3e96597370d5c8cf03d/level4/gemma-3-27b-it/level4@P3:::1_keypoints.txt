- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize continuous 3D views from a single image by traversing a pose manifold.  
- Recurrent structure addresses long-term dependencies in predicting sequences of transformations.  
- Demonstrated on Multi-PIE faces and chairs, showing plausible rendered rotations and emergent disentangled latents without explicit supervision.  
- Builds on prior geometry-based and deep-learning view-synthesis work, distinguishing itself by avoiding explicit 3D model recovery.  
- Novel combination of recurrence and convolution for continuous pose manipulation is a compelling technical contribution.  
- Emergence of weakly-supervised disentangled representations is an interesting and valuable side result.  
- Paper is clearly written with a well-explained architecture and sensible curriculum learning strategy.  
- Qualitative results, especially for chairs, demonstrate the method’s potential.  
- Lacks comprehensive quantitative evaluation (e.g., prominently reported MSE, FID) to rigorously compare with prior work.  
- Experiments are limited to faces and chairs, raising concerns about generality across object categories.  
- Comparisons are limited (only KNN); stronger baselines, including geometry-based methods, should be included.  
- Details of the curriculum learning schedule (how sequence length increases) need clarification.  
- Some figures suffer from low resolution or unclear labeling, reducing clarity.  
- Overall: promising and technically sound but needs more quantitative rigor and broader experiments to be convincing.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject, with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing evaluations and comparisons.
