- Proposed method: recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for synthesizing novel object views from a single 2D image; evaluated on faces and chairs; studies curriculum learning, disentanglement, and chair interpolation.  
- Main weakness: evaluation relies almost entirely on qualitative visualizations, many figures poorly scaled, preventing proper assessment.  
- Missing quantitative validation: must report objective metrics (e.g., PSNR, LPIPS, FID) and compare to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Limited experimental scope: tested only on Multi-PIE (faces) and Chairs, so generalizability to other object classes is unproven.  
- Rotation limitations: does not address arbitrary 3D rotations or non-axis-aligned/non-repeating rotation trajectories relevant to real-world use.  
- Lack of baselines: fails to compare or contextualize results against geometry-based methods (e.g., multi-view stereo, structure-from-motion).  
- Writing issues: minor grammatical error (“rotate” → “rotated”), redundant phrasing, and some awkward sentences that need tightening.  
- Recommendation/summary: strengthen the paper by adding quantitative metrics, expanding datasets and rotation scenarios, and benchmarking against geometric baselines; currently insufficient empirical depth for NIPS standards.
