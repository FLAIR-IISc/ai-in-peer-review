- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize out-of-plane rotations of 3D objects from a single image.  
- Encodes inputs into two latent partitions—identity units and pose units—and uses action units to control rotation before decoding back to an image.  
- Adds auxiliary mask prediction for objects like chairs to aid reconstruction.  
- Extends the model with recurrence: identity units are fixed while pose units are updated step-by-step by action inputs for long rotation trajectories.  
- Main contribution is emergent disentanglement of identity/appearance and pose, with identity units serving as view-invariant, discriminative features.  
- Curriculum recurrent training improves disentanglement and rendering quality for longer sequences.  
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, and motivations for components are explicit.  
- Qualitative multistep view synthesis and quantitative cross-view recognition provide solid evidence for effectiveness.  
- Minor concern: approach seems tied to discrete rotation steps from training data—question about supporting continuous rotation parameterization.  
- Minor concern: training uses fixed-length sequences per curriculum stage—question about generalization to variable-length test sequences and potential benefits of variable-length training.  
- Overall assessment: convincingly demonstrates emergent generative disentanglement and presents a solid end-to-end recurrent encoder-decoder for view synthesis.
