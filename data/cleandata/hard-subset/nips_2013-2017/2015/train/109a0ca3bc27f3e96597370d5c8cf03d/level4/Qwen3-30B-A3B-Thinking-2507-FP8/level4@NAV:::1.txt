

This paper proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for synthesizing object views from diverse pivoting angles based solely on a single 2D input image. The authors evaluate their model using face and chair datasets, and conduct supplementary experiments on curriculum learning benefits, disentangled representations via cross-view recognition, and class interpolation for chairs. While the model is well-described and the writing is clear, the performance evaluation is insufficient. The experiments primarily rely on qualitative visualizations (figures require proper scaling for readability), and the absence of quantitative metrics for comparison with state-of-the-art results limits the assessment. A quantitative analysis would provide greater insight than qualitative assessments alone. Minor grammatical errors exist (e.g., line 124: "rotate" → "rotated"). The paper's significance is substantially weakened by inadequate experimentation and analysis. Additional datasets beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs should be included to demonstrate generalizability across object categories, reducing the perception of an ad hoc architecture. Exploring generalization to non-static rotation trajectories (non-repeating rotations) would also strengthen the contribution. Although the approach is novel, the lack of sufficient experimentation and relevant analysis prevents meaningful comparison with state-of-the-art "geometry-based approaches" (line 51).
