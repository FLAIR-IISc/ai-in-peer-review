- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects from a single 2D image.
- Base model disentangles input into "identity" and "pose" units; an "action" unit modifies pose and a decoder synthesizes the rotated image and segmentation mask.
- Recurrent extension holds identity fixed and iteratively updates pose via action sequence to generate longer rotation trajectories.
- Main contribution is learning unsupervised disentanglement of identity and pose representations.
- Learned identity units are discriminative and view-invariant, yielding strong cross-view object recognition performance.
- Disentanglement plus curriculum learning (progressively longer trajectories) is crucial for generating long rotation sequences.
- Paper is well-written with clear methodology and motivations for each component.
- Qualitative results show coherent rendered rotations; quantitative evaluation supports the disentanglement claim.
- Weakness: model is limited to discrete rotation angles determined by training data; extension to continuous angles is not addressed.
- Weakness: recurrent training uses fixed-length sequences, lacking variable-length sequence processing typical of RNNs; implications and remedies are not discussed.
- Overall: innovative recurrent encoder-decoder with convincing qualitative and quantitative evidence for generative disentanglement.
