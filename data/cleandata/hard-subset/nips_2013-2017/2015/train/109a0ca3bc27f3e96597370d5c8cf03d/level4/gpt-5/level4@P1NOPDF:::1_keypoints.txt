- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder to render novel object views from a single 2D image via simulated rotations.  
- Paper is clearly presented and well written.  
- Empirical evaluation is inadequate and overly qualitative; figures need scaling for visible details.  
- Requires quantitative evaluation with appropriate error metrics and comparisons to strong baselines and state-of-the-art methods.  
- Experimental scope is narrow (only Multi-PIE and Chairs); more datasets and object categories are needed to support generality.  
- Should test rotation trajectories that do not follow a single static axis and may be non-repeating.  
- Supplementary analyses are promising but need deeper treatment: curriculum learning needs quantitative gains and ablations.  
- Disentanglement claims need stronger support via robust cross-view recognition metrics.  
- Class interpolation experiments require more rigorous evaluation to confirm meaningful shape/appearance factors rather than overfitting.  
- Overall approach is novel but limited experimentation and lack of robust quantitative analysis hinder assessment and competitiveness with state-of-the-art geometry-based methods.  
- Minor typo: line 124 “rotate” should be “rotated.”
