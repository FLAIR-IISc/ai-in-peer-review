- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for single-image 3D view synthesis.
- Main contribution: emergent generative disentangling of identity and pose from a recurrent rotation objective.
- Models pose manifold traversal with action units inspired by mental rotation.
- Trains end-to-end without explicit 3D model recovery, mapping image space to a nonlinear manifold.
- Uses curriculum training by gradually increasing rotation trajectory length for better appearance and features.
- Demonstrates strong qualitative and quantitative results on Multi-PIE and 3D chair CAD datasets.
- Identity units produce discriminative, view-invariant features useful for cross-view recognition.
- Paper is well-written and clearly motivated.
- Strengths: novel approach, effective disentangling, strong evaluation, clear presentation.
- Weakness: supports only discrete rotation angles determined by training data.
- Weakness: trained with fixed-length sequences, limiting general recurrent behavior.
- Suggested improvements: extend to continuous rotation angles and variable-length sequences.
- Concern: may not generalize to more complex scenarios beyond tested datasets.
- Overall assessment: promising method with thorough evaluation; rating 8/10, recommend accept with minor revisions.
