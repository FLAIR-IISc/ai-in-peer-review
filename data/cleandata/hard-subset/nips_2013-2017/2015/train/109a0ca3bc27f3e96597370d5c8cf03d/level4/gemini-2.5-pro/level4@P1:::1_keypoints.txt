- Novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views from a single image by traversing a learned pose manifold.
- Encoder disentangles view-invariant "identity" and view-dependent "pose"; recurrent network updates pose via discrete actions while identity stays fixed; decoder generates images.
- Curriculum learning progressively trains on longer transformation sequences, improving generation quality and learned representations.
- Demonstrated on Multi-PIE faces and 3D chairs, producing realistic rotation sequences and strong cross-view recognition without class labels.
- Elegant, novel formulation offering a learned-transformation alternative to geometry-based methods.
- Shows effective weakly-supervised disentanglement by forcing identity constancy across long sequences.
- Ablation study convincingly shows curriculum learning is crucial for handling long-term dependencies and better generalization.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents model architecture and training details.
- Evaluation lacks quantitative comparisons to state-of-the-art and geometry-based baselines and would benefit from established error metrics.
- Experimental scope is limited to faces and chairs; broader, more diverse object categories are needed to demonstrate generality.
- Model is only tested on simple repeated rotations around a static axis; need experiments on complex, multi-axis, or non-repeating transformations.
- Qualitative figures are small and should be scaled up to better assess image quality and fine details.
- Minor typo: "rotate" should likely be "rotated" on line 124.
