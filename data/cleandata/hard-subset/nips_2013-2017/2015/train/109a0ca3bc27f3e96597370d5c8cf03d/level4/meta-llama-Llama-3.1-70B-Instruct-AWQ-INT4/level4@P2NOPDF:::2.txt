This paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network for learning to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects, such as human faces and 3D chair models. The network starts with a basic model where its encoder disentangles the input image into identity units and pose units. The action units are then applied to the pose units to control the rotation direction. The decoder, consisting of convolution and upsampling layers, combines the identity and pose units to generate an image of the rotated object and its corresponding mask.

To support longer rotation trajectories, the proposed network is extended to have a recurrent architecture. In this architecture, the encoded identity unit of the input image is fixed, while the pose unit is updated by a sequence of action units. Both the identity and pose units are then fed into the decoder to generate the resulting image.

One of the main contributions of this paper is learning to disentangle the representations for identity/appearance and pose factors. The identity units are shown to be discriminative view-invariant features in the cross-view object recognition task. This disentangling property benefits the prediction of better rendering when using longer rotation trajectories in the curriculum training stages for training the proposed recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear motivation for different parts of the proposed method. The qualitative results for predicted rendering of rotated images and quantitative evaluation on cross-view object recognition tasks provide good support for the method, particularly the disentangled representations for pose and identity factors.

However, there are some minor weaknesses that the authors may want to address in the rebuttal period:

* The proposed network can only support discrete rotation angles, which are dependent on the set of rotation angles shown in the training data. It would be beneficial to explore how to extend the proposed method to support continuous rotation angles.
* The proposed recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network is trained with a fixed length, which is contradictory to the general properties of recurrent neural networks.

Overall, this paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network trained end-to-end on the task of rendering rotated objects starting from a single image. The main contribution of generative disentangling of the identity and pose factors, which emerged from the recurrent rotation prediction objective, is well demonstrated by the qualitative and quantitative evaluations.
