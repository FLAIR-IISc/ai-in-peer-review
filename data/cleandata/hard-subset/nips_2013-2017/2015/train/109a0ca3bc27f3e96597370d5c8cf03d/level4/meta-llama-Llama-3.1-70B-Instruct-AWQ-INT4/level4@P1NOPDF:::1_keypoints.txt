- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize object views from a single 2D image.  
- Evaluated qualitatively on face (Multi-PIE) and chair datasets for rotated viewpoints.  
- Additional experiments include curriculum learning, disentanglement via cross-view recognition, and chair class interpolation.  
- Model presentation and technical details are clear and well-written.  
- Evaluation is limited and largely qualitative, relying on visualizations rather than metrics.  
- Recommends adding quantitative performance measures and comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Paper has minor grammatical errors but is easy to follow.  
- Significance is weakened by limited experimentation and analysis.  
- Suggests testing on additional datasets and object types to show broader applicability.  
- Recommends investigating generalization to non-static-axis (non-repeating) rotation trajectories.  
- Novel approach but insufficient evidence to claim parity with geometry-based state-of-the-art methods.
