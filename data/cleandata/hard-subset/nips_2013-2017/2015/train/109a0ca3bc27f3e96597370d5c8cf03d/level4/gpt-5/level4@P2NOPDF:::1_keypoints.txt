- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder to synthesize novel object views from a single 2D image via sequential pivot rotations.
- Architecture is clearly presented and technically well detailed.
- Paper is well written and easy to follow.
- Ancillary experiments (curriculum learning, disentanglement, interpolation) are relevant and thoughtfully chosen.
- Evaluation is largely qualitative and over-relies on small visualizations that are hard to inspect.
- No rigorous quantitative comparison to strong baselines or geometry-based state-of-the-art, making effectiveness unclear.
- Experimental scope is narrow (only Multi-PIE and Chairs), raising generalization concerns.
- Method does not address off-axis rotations, non-repeating trajectories, or longer compound rotations common in real scenarios.
- Disentanglement claims lack direct quantitative evidence and rely on qualitative impressions.
- Suggest adding quantitative metrics (PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS) and downstream recognition accuracy across angles and occlusions.
- Suggest comparisons to robust baselines: geometry-based methods, non-recurrent encoder–decoders, and recent view-synthesis models.
- Recommend expanding to more datasets and object categories to demonstrate broader applicability.
- Recommend evaluating off-axis, non-periodic, compound rotations and longer trajectories, plus ablation studies isolating recurrence, latent representation, and curriculum learning.
- Improve figure readability by enlarging key visualizations.
- Minor editorial fix: change “rotate” to “rotated” at line 124.
- Overall: novel and promising approach but significance is undermined by limited experiments and lack of rigorous quantitative analysis; stronger evaluation and broader scope needed.
