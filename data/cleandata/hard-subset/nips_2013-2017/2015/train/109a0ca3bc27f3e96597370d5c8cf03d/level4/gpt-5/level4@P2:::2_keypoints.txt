- Proposed method: action-conditioned recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that splits latent space into identity and pose and applies discrete actions to update pose for multi-step rotations.  
- Training strategy: curriculum learning from single-step to longer sequences to stabilize training and preserve identity across trajectories.  
- Empirical results: high-quality multi-step view synthesis on Multi-PIE faces and rendered chairs, emergent disentangling, improved cross-view recognition, and smooth identity interpolation.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on transforming autoencoders and time-series models, extends Dosovitskiy et al. with an encoder and recurrence, and differs from IGN by using deterministic training and action-conditioned multi-step control.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated formulation and elegant architecture that cleanly separates identity and pose with convincing qualitative/quantitative evidence.  
- Strength: effective use of curriculum learning enabling long-horizon predictions and improved view-invariant recognition.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments across two domains with comparisons to some baselines and compelling interpolation demos.  
- Weakness: discrete 1-of-3 action encoding; no continuous-angle control or learned mapping from real-valued deltas.  
- Weakness: fixed-length training per curriculum stage; unclear robustness to variable-length inference and potential exposure bias (no scheduled sampling discussed).  
- Weakness: limited baselines (3D morphable model for faces, weak KNN for chairs); lacks head-to-head comparisons with IGN-like encoder–decoders.  
- Weakness: evaluation dominated by MSE; missing perceptual metrics (e.g., LPIPS/SSIM) or user studies to assess visual quality.  
- Weakness: dataset scope limited to aligned faces and synthetic chairs; unclear robustness to misalignment, real backgrounds, occlusion, sparse views, or domain shift.  
- Weakness: disentangling not fully characterized — potential leakage between identity and pose representations needs probing.  
- Quality: technically sound and carefully executed with clear description of architecture, training, and curriculum; more ablation studies would help.  
- Clarity: paper is well written with helpful figures; minor editorial clarifications suggested.  
- Originality: meaningful and timely combination of action-conditioned recurrence, convolutional encoder/decoder, and weakly supervised disentangling for multi-step 3D view synthesis.  
- Significance: promising for controllable image synthesis and view-invariant representation learning; broader impact would follow from continuous control and real-scene extensions.  
- Pros (summary): coherent action-conditioned recurrent architecture, emergent disentangling, effective curriculum learning, solid synthesis/recognition/interpolation results, clear exposition.  
- Cons (summary): discrete action space, fixed-length training concerns, limited baselines and perceptual evaluation, constrained datasets.  
- Key questions for authors: can actions be made continuous; how does the model perform under variable-length inference/extrapolation; any perceptual/user evaluations; can it train from sparse/unpaired or in-the-wild data; empirical comparison to IGN?
