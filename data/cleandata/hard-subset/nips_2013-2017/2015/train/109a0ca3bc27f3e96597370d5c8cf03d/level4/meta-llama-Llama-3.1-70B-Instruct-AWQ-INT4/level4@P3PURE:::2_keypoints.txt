- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to learn out-of-plane rotations from a single image.  
- Encoder disentangles input into identity units and pose units; action units modify pose to control rotation.  
- Decoder (convolution + upsampling) reconstructs rotated image and object mask from identity and pose.  
- Recurrent extension fixes identity and updates pose via a sequence of action units for longer rotation trajectories.  
- Identity units emerge as discriminative, view-invariant features useful for cross-view recognition.  
- Disentangling of identity and pose improves rendering quality, especially with longer trajectories and curriculum training.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation for each component.  
- Strong qualitative results for rendered rotations and quantitative support on cross-view recognition.  
- Limitation: supports only discrete rotation angles tied to training data; needs extension for continuous angles.  
- Limitation: trained on fixed-length sequences, which contrasts with the usual flexible definition of recurrent networks.  
- Overall, the generative disentangling contribution is well demonstrated by experiments.
