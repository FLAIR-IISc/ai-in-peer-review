- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize novel 3D views from single images, disentangling identity and pose without class labels.  
- Introduces a novel action-controlled recurrent structure that traverses the pose manifold via sequential small transformations for long-term rotation prediction.  
- Builds on prior image-generation and disentanglement work but differs by using control signals at each step and avoiding explicit 3D model recovery.  
- Uses curriculum learning to train the recurrent network; longer training sequences improve synthesis quality and disentanglement.  
- Demonstrates strong qualitative and quantitative results on Multi-PIE faces and CAD chairs, including a 42% MSE improvement (RNN16 vs RNN4) over KNN baseline.  
- Cross-view recognition experiments show emergent pose-invariant identity features without explicit supervision.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized; architecture, experimental setup, and figures support reproducibility.  
- Significant potential applications: view synthesis, pose-invariant recognition, generative modeling, photo editing, and vision-based RL; generalizes across object classes and enables interpolation-based novel object generation.  
- Major strengths: novel recurrent architecture, emergent disentanglement without supervision, strong multi-dataset validation, cognitive-science connection, and effective curriculum learning.  
- Minor weaknesses: supports only discrete rotation angles, trained on fixed-length sequences without discussion of variable-length testing, and limited comparison to recent explicit-regularization disentanglement methods.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-validated, significant contribution; recommend acceptance.
