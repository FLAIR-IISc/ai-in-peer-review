- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to synthesize novel viewpoints from a single 2D image.  
- Evaluated on rotating faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs, with experiments on curriculum learning, disentanglement via cross-view recognition, and class interpolation.  
- Model presentation and implementation details are clear and well-explained.  
- Evaluation is largely qualitative (visual inspection) and lacks rigorous quantitative metrics.  
- Needs error metrics and quantitative comparisons to state-of-the-art methods for objective validation.  
- Experimental scope is narrow (only Multi-PIE and Chairs); more diverse datasets would strengthen generality claims.  
- Should test more complex, non-axis or non-repeating rotation trajectories to assess generalization.  
- Minor issues: result figures are too small for readability and there are occasional grammatical errors (e.g., "rotate" → "rotated").  
- Overall: interesting and promising approach but insufficient experimentation and analysis to demonstrate significance or parity with geometry-based state-of-the-art.
