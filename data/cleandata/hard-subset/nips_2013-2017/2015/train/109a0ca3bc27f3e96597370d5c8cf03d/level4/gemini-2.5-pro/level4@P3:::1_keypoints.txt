- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that models 3D rotation as sequential traversal on a learned pose manifold to synthesize novel views from one image.  
- Disentangles representation into view-invariant identity units and view-dependent pose units, with action inputs controlling pose transitions.  
- Uses curriculum learning to train on progressively longer rotation sequences, improving image quality and identity feature invariance.  
- Demonstrates plausible view synthesis and competitive cross-view recognition on Multi-PIE faces and a 3D chairs dataset without identity labels.  
- Novelty: reframes view synthesis as action-conditioned recurrent prediction, enabling long-range transformation handling.  
- Shows effective weakly-supervised disentanglement of identity and pose via the curriculum training strategy.  
- Paper is very clear and well-presented, with helpful diagrams and thorough explanations.  
- Qualitative results are strong: coherent rotated sequences preserving identity cues and smooth latent interpolations.  
- Evaluation relies mostly on qualitative results and MSE; lacks rigorous quantitative comparisons (e.g., PSNR/SSIM) against state-of-the-art or geometry-based methods.  
- Experiments are limited to single-axis rotations (yaw/azimuth); unclear generalization to full 6-DoF or non-rigid transformations and continuous multi-axis actions.  
- Baselines are weak: KNN for chairs is insufficient; an ablation to a non-recurrent version would clarify the recurrent contribution.  
- On large-angle chair recognition, a pre-trained VGG-16 outperforms the model, suggesting possible degradation of identity features over long trajectories or symmetry issues.  
- Presentation issues: figures need higher resolution for inspection and there is a minor typo regarding the phrasing of "identity units."  
- Curriculum learning is an effective and convincing component, shown to improve generalization and invariance in results.
