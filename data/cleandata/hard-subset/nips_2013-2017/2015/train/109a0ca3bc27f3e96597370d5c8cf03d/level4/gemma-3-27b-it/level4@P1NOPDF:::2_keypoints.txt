- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects from a single image.  
- Encoder disentangles input into identity and pose units; action units control pose changes.  
- Decoder (convolution + upsampling) reconstructs the rotated image and an object mask from identity+pose.  
- Recurrent extension keeps identity fixed and updates pose via a sequence of action units for longer rotation trajectories.  
- Both identity and pose units are used by the decoder to generate outputs at each step.  
- Main contribution: learning disentangled identity/pose representations that support generative rotation prediction.  
- Identity units serve as discriminative, view-invariant features in cross-view recognition tasks.  
- Disentanglement and curriculum training with longer trajectories improve rendering accuracy.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and strong qualitative and quantitative results.  
- Weakness: model supports only discrete rotation angles tied to training data; continuous angles not addressed.  
- Weakness: trained with fixed-length sequences, which seems atypical for recurrent networks.  
- Overall: novel end-to-end recurrent encoder-decoder with solid support for its main claims.
