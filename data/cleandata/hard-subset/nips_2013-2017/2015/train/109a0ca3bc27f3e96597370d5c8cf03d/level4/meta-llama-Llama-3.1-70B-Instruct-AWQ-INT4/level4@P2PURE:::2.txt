In this paper, the authors introduce a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network designed to learn out-of-plane rotations for 3D objects, such as human faces and 3D chair models. The proposed network begins with a basic model, where the encoder disentangles the input image into identity units and pose units. The action units are then applied to the pose units to control the rotation direction. Subsequently, the decoder network, comprising convolution and upsampling layers, decodes the identity and pose units into an image of the rotated object and its corresponding mask.

To support longer rotation trajectories, the proposed network is extended to incorporate a recurrent architecture. In this setup, the encoded identity unit of the input image remains fixed, while the pose unit is modified by a sequence of action units. Ultimately, both identity and pose units are fed into the decoder to generate the resulting image.

One of the primary contributions of this paper lies in learning to disentangle the representations for identity/appearance and pose factors. The identity units are demonstrated to be discriminative view-invariant features in the cross-view object recognition task. Furthermore, this disentangling property is expected to yield better rendering predictions when utilizing longer rotation trajectories in the curriculum training stages for training the proposed recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network.

The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and the motivation behind each component of the proposed method is clearly described. The qualitative results for predicted rendering of rotated images and quantitative evaluation on the cross-view object recognition task provide strong support for the method, particularly the disentangled representations for pose and identity factors.

However, some minor weaknesses are noted below, and it is hoped that the authors will address these concerns during the rebuttal period:

- The proposed network currently only supports discrete rotation angles, which are dependent on the set of rotation angles presented in the training data. Do the authors have any initial ideas on how to extend the proposed method to accommodate continuous rotation angles?
- The proposed recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network is trained with fixed-length sequences, which contradicts the typical behavior of general recurrent neural networks.

This paper proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network trained end-to-end on the task of rendering rotated objects from a single image. The primary contribution of generative disentangling of identity and pose factors, which emerges from the recurrent rotation prediction objective, is well-demonstrated through both qualitative and quantitative evaluations.
