- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize novel 3D views from single 2D images, avoiding explicit 3D reconstruction.  
- Focuses on weakly-supervised disentanglement of identity and pose, emergent without class labels.  
- Introduces a recurrent structure for long-term pose manifold traversal, improving over single-step transformations.  
- Employs curriculum learning to progressively increase rotation trajectory length and improve disentanglement.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Multi-PIE (faces) and CAD chair datasets with qualitative and quantitative results.  
- Technical formulation is sound with clear ablation studies, cross-view recognition experiments, and MSE comparisons.  
- Model preserves object identity during rotations, supported by empirical results (Table 1).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly describes architecture and training; figures effectively illustrate methods.  
- Experimental scope is limited to faces and chairs, raising questions about generalization to other object classes or complex scenes.  
- Lacks quantitative comparisons to state-of-the-art geometry-based and other learning-based view synthesis methods.  
- Does not analyze non-axis or complex rotation trajectories; significance claims are somewhat overstated without broader validation.  
- Overall: technically solid and clear contribution but limited impact due to narrow experimental validation and insufficient baseline comparisons.
