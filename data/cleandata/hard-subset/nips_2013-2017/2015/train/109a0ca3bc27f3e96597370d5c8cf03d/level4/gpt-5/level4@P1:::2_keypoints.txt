- Paper tackles single-image 3D view synthesis using a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder with action units for incremental rotations.
- Encoder disentangles identity (view-invariant) and pose units; decoder reconstructs images (and masks for chairs); pose updated recurrently while identity is fixed.
- Curriculum learning (increasing sequence length) improves long-term rotation quality and identity invariance.
- Empirical results: smooth plausible renderings on Multi-PIE and chairs, quantitative MSE improvement over a KNN baseline, and strong cross-view recognition using identity units.
- Method positions well relative to prior work: generalizes to novel instances, uses action-conditioned recurrent updates, and emphasizes weakly supervised disentangling.
- Strength: clear, coherent model architecture that naturally extends to recurrent, action-conditioned transformations.
- Strength: curriculum learning is effective and improves both rendering and identity disentanglement.
- Strength: qualitative and quantitative evidence supports the approach, including interpolation of identity codes to synthesize new chairs.
- Demonstrates weakly supervised disentangling: identity units become increasingly pose-invariant without class labels.
- Weakness: supports only discrete rotation steps tied to training data; unclear how to extend to continuous angles.
- Weakness/question: training uses fixed-length sequences—unclear generalization to longer trajectories and whether variable-length or scheduled sampling would help.
- Quality: technically sound architecture and experiments; KNN baseline is simple but comparison gap is convincing.
- Suggestion: discuss the effect of the chair mask branch on training stability and whether it benefits faces.
- Clarity: well written and easy to follow; minor edits suggested (use “upsampling”, state rotation step size for action coding).
- Originality: novel combination of convolutional encoder–decoder, action-conditioned recurrent pose updates, and curriculum learning leading to disentangled identity/pose.
- Significance: practically and scientifically meaningful for graphics and vision, with useful cross-view recognition and synthesis capabilities.
- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated model, strong results, effective curriculum learning, and convincing disentanglement/interpolation.
- Cons for acceptance: limitation to discrete rotations and fixed-length training despite recurrent design.
- Overall: novel, well-supported approach to generative disentangling for single-image rotation; clarification on continuous control and sequence-length generalization would strengthen the paper.
