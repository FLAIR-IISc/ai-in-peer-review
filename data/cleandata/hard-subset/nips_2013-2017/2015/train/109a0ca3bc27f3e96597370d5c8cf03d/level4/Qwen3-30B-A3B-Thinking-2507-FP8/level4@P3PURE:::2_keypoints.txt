- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that learns out-of-plane rotations for 3D objects (faces, chairs).
- Encoder disentangles input into identity units (appearance) and pose units (rotation state).
- Action units modify pose units to control rotation direction; decoder (convs + upsampling) reconstructs rotated image and mask.
- Extends to a recurrent model for longer rotation trajectories: identity fixed, pose iteratively updated by action sequence.
- Identity units act as discriminative, view-invariant features, enabling cross-view object recognition.
- Curriculum training with extended rotation trajectories improves disentanglement and rendering quality.
- Strong empirical support via qualitative rotated renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition results.
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and motivations are compelling.
- Minor weaknesses: supports only discrete training-angle rotations (how to handle continuous angles?).
- Minor weaknesses: recurrent network trained on fixed-length sequences, conflicting with typical variable-length design.
- Main contribution: demonstrates emergent generative disentanglement of identity and pose from the recurrent rotation prediction objective.
