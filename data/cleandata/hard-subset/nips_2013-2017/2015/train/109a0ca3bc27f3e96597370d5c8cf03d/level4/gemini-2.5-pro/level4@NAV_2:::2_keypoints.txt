- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that disentangles identity (view-invariant) and pose (view-dependent) features for single-image 3D view synthesis.  
- Models out-of-plane rotations by applying discrete action inputs to pose units and updates pose via recurrence while keeping identity fixed.  
- Trains end-to-end with curriculum learning that progressively increases rotation sequence length.  
- Builds on generative convnets and transforming auto-encoders but is novel in its recurrent formulation for sequential transformations.  
- Demonstrates strong qualitative results: smooth, realistic long-trajectory rotations that preserve object identity (Multi-PIE faces and 3D chairs).  
- Shows strong quantitative performance, significantly outperforming a k-NN baseline in reconstruction error.  
- Provides evidence that identity features are highly discriminative and view-invariant, rivaling a fully supervised CNN in cross-view recognition.  
- Achieves weakly-supervised disentanglement of identity and pose without explicit latent supervision.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and well-motivated in its architectural choices and experiments.  
- Limitation: uses discrete action steps for rotation, restricting continuous viewpoint control; could consider continuous-valued action inputs.  
- Question: curriculum uses fixed increasing sequence lengths—was variable-length training explored and does curriculum outperform standard RNN training?  
- Overall: novel, effective approach that meaningfully contributes to generative modeling and representation learning via transformation-based proxy tasks.
