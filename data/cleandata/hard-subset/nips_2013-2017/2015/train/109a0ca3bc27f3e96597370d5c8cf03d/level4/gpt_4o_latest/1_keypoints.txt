- Proposal: recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to render objects from novel pivoting viewpoints from a single 2D image.  
- Evaluation: tested qualitatively on faces (Multi-PIE) and chairs.  
- Additional experiments: curriculum learning benefits, disentangled representation via cross-view recognition, and class interpolation on chairs.  
- Model description: clearly and thoroughly articulated.  
- Main critique: performance evaluation is largely qualitative and insufficient.  
- Recommendation: include quantitative metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Figures: visual results need better scaling/readability.  
- Writing: generally clear but contains minor grammatical errors (e.g., “rotate” → “rotated”).  
- Major limitation: limited experiments and analysis reduce the paper’s overall significance.  
- Generalization concerns: should test on additional datasets, more object types, and non-static/non-repeating rotation trajectories.  
- Claim weakness: insufficient evidence to demonstrate results comparable to geometry-based state-of-the-art.
