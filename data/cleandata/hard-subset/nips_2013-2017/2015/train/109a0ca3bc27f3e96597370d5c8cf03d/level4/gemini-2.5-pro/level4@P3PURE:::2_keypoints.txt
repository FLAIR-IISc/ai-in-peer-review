- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects (faces, chairs).
- Encoder disentangles input into separate identity and pose representations; an action unit modifies pose; decoder reconstructs rotated image and segmentation mask.
- Extends to a recurrent architecture where identity is fixed and pose is iteratively updated by action sequences for longer rotation trajectories.
- Main contribution is emergent, learned disentanglement of identity and pose factors from the rotation prediction objective.
- Learned identity features are discriminative and view-invariant, yielding strong cross-view object recognition performance.
- Disentanglement improves rendering quality, particularly for longer rotation trajectories under curriculum learning.
- Paper is well-written with clear motivations, compelling qualitative results, and strong quantitative evaluation.
- Minor weakness: network generates outputs only at discrete rotation angles tied to training data — question about extensions to continuous angles.
- Minor weakness: recurrent network trained with fixed-length sequences — question about the design choice versus variable-length RNN capability.
- Summary: end-to-end trained model that convincingly demonstrates generative disentanglement of identity and pose, supported by qualitative and quantitative results.
