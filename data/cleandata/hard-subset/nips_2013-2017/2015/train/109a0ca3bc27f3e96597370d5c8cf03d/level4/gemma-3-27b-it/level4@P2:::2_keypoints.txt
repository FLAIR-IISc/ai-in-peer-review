- Presents a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel 3D views from a single image by disentangling identity and pose and applying sequential latent rotations.

- Positions work against geometry-based rendering and recent generative/disentangling methods, highlighting recurrent action units for controlled rotation.

- Novelty: combining recurrent conv. architecture with disentangled identity and pose units for view synthesis.

- Technical soundness: clear, logical architecture and sensible use of curriculum learning for long-term dependencies.

- Empirical validation: compelling qualitative and quantitative results on faces and chairs; cross-view recognition demonstrates effective disentanglement.

- Clarity: paper is well-written and clearly explains motivation, method, and experiments.

- Significance: addresses an important problem with applications in photo editing and vision-based reinforcement learning.

- Weakness: relies on discrete rotation angles defined during training, limiting flexibility and continuous control.

- Weakness: uses fixed-length recurrent sequences without sufficient justification despite RNNs’ inherent flexibility.

- Weakness: limited action space (clockwise, counter-clockwise, no rotation); lacks finer-grained or more complex transformations.

- Weakness: baseline comparisons are limited (only KNN); should compare to GANs, VAEs, and other SOTA generative methods.

- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel and sound method, strong empirical results, clear presentation.

- Arguments against acceptance: practical limitations from discrete rotations and fixed-length sequences, and limited comparisons to other generative approaches.

- Overall: strong paper with minor but addressable weaknesses; recommended for acceptance pending rebuttal clarifications.
