- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to render novel object viewpoints from a single 2D image, tested on faces and chairs.  
- Model description is clear and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Experiments are largely qualitative; results would benefit from larger, clearer visualization figures.  
- Lacks quantitative performance metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods; needs appropriate error metrics.  
- Overall significance is limited by insufficient experimentation and analysis.  
- Evaluation should include datasets beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Should test generalization to non-fixed-axis or non-repeating rotation trajectories.  
- Contains minor grammatical errors (e.g., line 124: “rotate” → “rotated”).  
- Approach is novel and interesting but currently not shown to match geometry-based state-of-the-art due to limited results.
