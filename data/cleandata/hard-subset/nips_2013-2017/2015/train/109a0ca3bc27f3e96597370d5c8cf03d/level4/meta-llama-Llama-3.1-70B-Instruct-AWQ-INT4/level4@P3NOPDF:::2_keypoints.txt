- Proposes a novel recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects (faces, chairs).
- Encoder disentangles input into identity units and pose units; action units control rotation direction.
- Decoder (convolution + upsampling) reconstructs the rotated image and its mask from identity and pose units.
- Recurrent extension updates pose units with a sequence of action units while keeping identity fixed for longer rotation trajectories.
- Main contribution: learning disentangled identity and pose representations that emerge from the rotation prediction objective.
- Identity units are shown to be discriminative and view-invariant in cross-view object recognition.
- Curriculum training with progressively longer rotations improves disentangling and rendering quality.
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and component descriptions.
- Strong qualitative renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition results support the method.
- Limitation: only supports discrete rotation angles tied to training-data angles; continuous rotation not addressed.
- Limitation: trained with fixed-length sequences, which contradicts the variable-length capability expected of general RNNs.
