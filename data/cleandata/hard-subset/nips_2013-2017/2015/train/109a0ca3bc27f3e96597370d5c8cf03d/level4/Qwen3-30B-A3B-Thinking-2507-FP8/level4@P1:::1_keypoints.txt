- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for novel 3D view synthesis from a single 2D image, tested on faces and chairs.  
- Key innovation: recurrent structure that captures long-term dependencies for pose manifold traversal.  
- Uses curriculum learning to gradually increase rotation sequence complexity, improving performance.  
- Achieves weakly-supervised disentangling of identity and pose without explicit class labels.  
- Cross-view recognition experiments show identity units learn pose-invariant representations.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides sufficient implementation details for reproduction.  
- Related work is effectively reviewed and situates the contribution in the literature.  
- Strength: novel recurrent architecture handling long-term dependencies in rotation trajectories.  
- Strength: elegant weakly-supervised disentangling and comprehensive evaluation aspects.  
- Weakness: inadequate quantitative evaluation of view synthesis (lack of standard metrics like PSNR/SSIM).  
- Weakness: comparisons (e.g., KNN MSE) are not contextualized against broader literature or SOTA methods.  
- Weakness: limited experimental scope (only Multi-PIE and chairs), reducing generalizability.  
- Weakness: no evaluation of non-axis-aligned rotations to test more complex transformations.  
- Minor issues: a few grammatical errors and inconsistent image scaling in figures hindering visual comparison.  
- Recommendations: add PSNR/SSIM and baseline comparisons, expand object categories, evaluate non-axis-aligned rotations, and benchmark against established methods.
