- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder that synthesizes out-of-plane rotations of 3D objects from a single image.  
- Encoder disentangles input into identity and pose units; action units modify pose; decoder (conv + upsampling) reconstructs rotated image and mask.  
- Recurrent extension keeps identity fixed and updates pose via action sequences to produce longer rotation trajectories.  
- Main contribution: learned disentanglement of identity and pose factors, with identity units shown to be discriminative and view-invariant.  
- Curriculum training (increasing rotation sequence length) improves disentanglement and rendering quality.  
- Experimental support: strong qualitative renderings and quantitative cross-view recognition results validating the approach.  
- Paper is clearly written with well-motivated architectural choices.  
- Limitation: model currently supports only discrete rotation angles present in training data; continuous-angle handling is not addressed.  
- Limitation/question: trained with fixed-length sequences—clarity needed on variable-length handling and generalization to different trajectory lengths.  
- Overall: novel end-to-end recurrent conv encoder–decoder with well-demonstrated emergent disentangling driven by rotation-prediction.
