- Core idea: recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder models 3D rotation as sequential traversal on a learned pose manifold with separate identity and pose latents and action-conditioned recurrent updates.  
- Implementation: encoder disentangles identity/pose, recurrent pose updates per action, decoder generates new views; curriculum learning (increasing sequence length) used.  
- Datasets: evaluated on Multi-PIE faces and 3D chair models.  
- Emergent disentangling: training objective yields identity/pose disentanglement without explicit supervision, validated by cross-view recognition.  
- Novelty: framing view synthesis as a controlled sequential (mental rotation–like) process with explicit stepwise control signals is original and elegant.  
- Technical quality: architecture and curriculum learning are well-designed; longer sequences improve synthesis and feature invariance.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and explanations are clear.  
- Limited evaluation scope: experiments only on two well-aligned, structured classes (faces, chairs), so generality to diverse objects is unclear.  
- Weak baselines/comparisons: comparisons are limited (k-NN for chairs); lacks non-recurrent ablation and comparisons to contemporary learning-based methods (e.g., Inverse Graphics Network).  
- Generalization analysis lacking: only single-axis rotations shown; handling compound rotations or arbitrary axes not analyzed.  
- Qualitative presentation issues: some generated-image figures are small and hard to interpret; larger examples or supplementary material needed.  
- Overall verdict: promising and novel work with strong core idea and emergent disentangling, but evaluation is preliminary and needs broader, stronger experiments to substantiate general-purpose claims.
