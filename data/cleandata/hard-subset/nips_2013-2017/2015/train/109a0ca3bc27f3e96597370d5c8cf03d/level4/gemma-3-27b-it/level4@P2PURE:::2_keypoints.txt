- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to learn out-of-plane rotations on 3D objects (faces and chairs).
- Encoder disentangles input into identity and pose representations; action units modify pose to control rotation direction.
- Decoder (convolution + unsampling) reconstructs rotated image and corresponding mask from combined identity and pose.
- Recurrent extension keeps identity fixed while updating pose through a sequence of action units to produce longer rotation trajectories.
- Main contribution: unsupervised generative disentanglement of identity and pose factors.
- Learned identity features serve as discriminative, view-invariant representations for cross-view recognition.
- Disentanglement and curriculum training improve rendering performance, notably for longer rotations.
- Strong qualitative results and quantitative cross-view recognition experiments support the method.
- Minor weakness: currently handles only discrete rotation angles tied to training data; no continuous-angle extension proposed.
- Minor weakness: the recurrent model is trained with a fixed sequence length rather than supporting variable-length sequences.
