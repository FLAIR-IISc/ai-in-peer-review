- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder to render novel object views from a single 2D image, evaluated on faces and chairs with studies on curriculum learning, disentanglement, and class interpolation.
- Model description is clear and technically detailed.
- Evaluation is largely qualitative; figures should be scaled for legibility.
- Lacks quantitative performance metrics and error measures for comparison to state-of-the-art.
- Significance limited by scarce experiments and analysis.
- Needs additional datasets beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs to demonstrate generality across object categories.
- Should evaluate generalization to non-fixed-axis or non-repeating rotation trajectories.
- Novel and interesting approach but currently insufficiently substantiated against geometry-based or state-of-the-art methods.
- Minor grammatical issues (example: “rotate” -> “rotated” at line 124).
