- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that models large 3D rotations as sequences of incremental latent-space transformations.
- Architecture disentangles identity (time-invariant) and pose (time-varying) in latent space, with actions driving recurrent pose updates and a decoder synthesizing images.
- Inspired by mental rotation and differs from prior work (e.g., IGN) by using a deterministic recurrent structure to model transformation trajectories.
- Demonstrated on face and chair datasets, generating plausible rotation sequences and producing identity features that are increasingly view-invariant.
- Originality: Novel controlled traversal of a pose manifold via recurrent updates and explicit discrete actions in latent space.
- Quality: Strong weakly-supervised disentangling; identity units learned from view synthesis become view-invariant while pose units contain little identity information.
- Quality (training): Effective use of curriculum learning to train for longer transformation trajectories.
- Significance: Enables user-controlled novel-view synthesis with potential applications in graphics, vision, and reinforcement learning; shows disentangling emerges without direct supervision.
- Evaluation weakness: Quantitative comparisons are limited—main baseline is KNN; missing comparisons to other learning-based methods and a non-recurrent ablation.
- Evaluation weakness: Comparison to geometry-based methods is mainly qualitative; lacks quantitative benchmarks against classic approaches for rendering quality and robustness.
- Generalization weakness: Experiments limited to faces and chairs; need broader object categories to support generality claims.
- Generalization weakness: Transformations restricted to discrete rotations around a single fixed axis; does not address multi-axis or non-rigid transformations.
- Clarity: Some figures are dense and images too small for detailed assessment; manuscript needs minor proofreading for grammatical errors.
- Overall: Novel and well-motivated approach that yields emergent disentangled representations, but impact is limited by the scope and depth of experimental evaluation.
