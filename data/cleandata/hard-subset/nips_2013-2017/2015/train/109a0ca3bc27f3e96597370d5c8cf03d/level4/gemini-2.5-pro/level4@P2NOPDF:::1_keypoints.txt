- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder for single-image view synthesis, demonstrated on faces and chairs.  
- Novel, interesting learning-based alternative to geometry methods; model architecture is clearly and reproducibly presented.  
- Primary weakness: limited experimental evaluation and analysis, hindering assessment of significance.  
- Lacks quantitative evaluation—needs metrics like PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS and comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Limited scope of experiments—only two structured categories (faces, chairs); needs more diverse object classes.  
- Evaluated only on simple fixed-axis rotations; should test complex trajectories and out-of-distribution views.  
- Minor issues: some visualization figures are too small and there are a few grammatical errors.  
- Recommendation: add robust quantitative results, broader datasets, and experiments on more complex transformations to strengthen the paper.
