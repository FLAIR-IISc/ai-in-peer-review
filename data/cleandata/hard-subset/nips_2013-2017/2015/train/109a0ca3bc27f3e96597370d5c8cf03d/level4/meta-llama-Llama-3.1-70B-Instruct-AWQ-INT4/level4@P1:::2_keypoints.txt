- Proposal: recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects (faces, chairs).  
- Innovation: uses an RNN to model long-term dependencies in rotation sequences.  
- Innovation: disentangles identity and pose factors without explicit regularization.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear motivation and component descriptions.  
- Results: strong qualitative renderings of rotations and quantitative cross-view recognition performance.  
- Contribution: disentangled representations enable realistic rotation trajectories and competitive recognition.  
- Training: curriculum training progressively increases sequence difficulty to handle longer-term dependencies.  
- Weakness: model supports only discrete rotation angles tied to training data; unclear extension to continuous rotations.  
- Weakness: trained with fixed-length sequences, which contradicts typical RNN usage (partly mitigated by curriculum training).  
- Overall: significant contribution to 3D view synthesis and image generation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach; RNN + disentanglement; strong qualitative/quantitative results; effective curriculum training.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited to discrete angles and fixed-length sequence training.  
- Rating and confidence: 8/10, confidence 9/10.
