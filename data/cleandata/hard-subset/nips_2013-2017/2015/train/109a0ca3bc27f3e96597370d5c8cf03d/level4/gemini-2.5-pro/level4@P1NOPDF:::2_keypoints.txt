- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that renders 3D objects from novel viewpoints given a single input image.  
- Encoder disentangles input into separate identity and pose latent representations.  
- An action unit controls rotation by modifying the pose representation.  
- Decoder (convolution + upsampling) uses identity and modified pose to generate the rotated image and object mask.  
- Extends to a recurrent architecture: identity fixed from the initial image while pose is iteratively updated by action sequences.  
- Uses curriculum learning to train longer and more complex rotation trajectories.  
- Learned disentanglement yields discriminative, view-invariant identity features validated by cross-view recognition.  
- Qualitative results show convincing rotated renderings; quantitative evaluation supports effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and component descriptions.  
- Concern: method appears limited to discrete rotation angles present in the training data—question about extending to continuous rotations.  
- Concern: recurrent network is trained with fixed-length action sequences, raising questions about handling variable-length sequences and the design choice.
