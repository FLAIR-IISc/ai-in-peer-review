This paper introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network designed to render an object from varying pivoting viewpoints using only a single 2D input image. The authors demonstrate their model’s capabilities through image generation of faces and chairs under rotation, alongside supplementary experiments investigating curriculum learning, disentangled representation learning via cross-view object recognition, and class interpolation specifically with chair objects.

While the model is clearly presented and thoroughly detailed, the performance evaluation feels limited. The experiments supporting the effectiveness of the proposed RNN model rely heavily on qualitative assessment (visualizations should be presented at a scale conducive to detailed review). Incorporating quantitative performance metrics and comparing results against existing state-of-the-art methods would provide a more robust and informative evaluation than qualitative analysis alone.

The paper is generally well-written and accessible. A few minor grammatical corrections would improve clarity (e.g., line 124, change "rotate" to "rotated").

The paper’s overall impact is currently constrained by a lack of extensive experimentation and analysis. Expanding the evaluation to include datasets beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs would be beneficial. Demonstrating the model’s ability to render rotated views of a wider variety of objects would strengthen the generalizability of the proposed architecture and reduce the perception of it being tailored to specific object categories. Furthermore, exploring the model’s performance with non-axis-aligned or dynamic rotation trajectories would be a valuable addition. The work presents a novel deep learning approach to single-image, rotational viewpoint rendering. However, the paper would benefit from more comprehensive experimentation and analysis to fully establish the model’s significance and allow for meaningful comparison with established ‘geometry-based approaches’ (line 51).
