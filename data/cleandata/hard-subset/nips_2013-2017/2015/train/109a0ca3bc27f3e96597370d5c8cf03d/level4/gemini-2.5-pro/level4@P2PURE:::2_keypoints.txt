- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to apply out-of-plane rotations to 3D objects (faces, chairs).
- Encoder disentangles input into separate identity and pose representations; action units modify pose; decoder generates rotated image and mask.
- Recurrent extension keeps identity fixed and sequentially updates pose via action units to generate multi-step rotations.
- Uses curriculum learning to train for longer rotation trajectories.
- Learned disentanglement produces discriminative, view-invariant identity features validated on cross-view recognition.
- Qualitative renderings and quantitative recognition results strongly support the method's effectiveness.
- Paper is well-written and components are clearly motivated.
- Minor weakness: model limited to discrete rotation angles from training data—request strategies for continuous rotations.
- Minor weakness: recurrent network trained on fixed-length sequences—request clarification on not using variable-length sequences.
- Overall: novel end-to-end method whose main contribution is emergent generative disentanglement of identity and pose, well-supported by experiments.
