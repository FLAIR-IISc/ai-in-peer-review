- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder for synthesizing out-of-plane rotations of 3D objects (faces and chairs).  
- Non-recurrent core: encoder disentangles input into identity and pose representations.  
- Pose is modified by action units controlling rotation direction; decoder (conv + upsampling) reconstructs rotated image and mask.  
- Recurrent extension: identity stays fixed while pose is iteratively updated through action units for longer rotation sequences.  
- Primary contribution: learning disentangled identity/appearance and pose representations.  
- Identity representations are discriminative, providing view-invariant features for cross-view recognition.  
- Disentanglement enables more accurate rendering when using longer rotation trajectories and curriculum learning.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, with strong qualitative synthesis results.  
- Quantitative evaluations robustly validate cross-view recognition improvements.  
- Weakness 1: model supports only discrete rotation angles from the training set—needs discussion of extensions to continuous rotations.  
- Weakness 2: recurrent network is trained on fixed-length sequences—clarify rationale given typical RNN flexibility.  
- Overall: compelling, well-validated approach that convincingly demonstrates generative disentanglement for rotation prediction.
