This paper introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder network capable of rendering an object from novel pivoting viewpoints given a single 2D input image. The authors demonstrate the model’s performance by generating rotated views of faces and chairs, and include further experiments investigating curriculum learning, disentangled representation learning through cross-view object recognition, and class interpolation with chair images. 

While the proposed model is clearly presented and thoroughly detailed, its evaluation lacks sufficient rigor. The experiments intended to showcase the effectiveness of the RNN model are primarily qualitative; figures visualizing the results should be scaled for improved readability. Incorporating quantitative performance metrics and comparing against state-of-the-art methods would provide a more insightful assessment than qualitative evaluation alone.

The paper is generally well-written and easy to understand, with only minor grammatical issues (e.g., line 124, “rotate” should be “rotated”). 

However, the paper’s significance is limited by insufficient experimentation and analysis. Expanding the evaluation to include datasets beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs would be beneficial. Demonstrating the model’s ability to render rotations of diverse object categories would reduce the perception that the architecture is specifically tailored to the chosen datasets. Furthermore, exploring the model’s capacity to handle non-axis-aligned or dynamic rotation trajectories—rather than solely rotations around a fixed axis—would be valuable. In summary, this work presents a novel and interesting deep learning approach for rendering objects from rotational viewpoints based on a single 2D image. Nevertheless, the paper requires more extensive experimentation and analysis to fully establish its significance and demonstrate results competitive with existing geometry-based approaches (line 51).
