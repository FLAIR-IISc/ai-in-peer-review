- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that generates novel views of objects from a single 2D image.
- Evaluates on face (Multi-PIE) and chair datasets, showing rotated-view image generation qualitatively.
- Additional experiments include curriculum learning benefits, disentangled representation assessment via cross-view recognition, and class interpolation for chairs.
- Main weakness: evaluation is largely qualitative—visualizations are insufficiently scaled and lack quantitative performance metrics.
- Recommends introducing appropriate error metrics to enable comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- Paper is generally well-written and clear, with a few minor grammatical errors (e.g., "rotate" → "rotated").
- Significance is limited by insufficient experimentation and analysis to establish broad applicability.
- Suggests testing on more datasets beyond Multi-PIE and Chairs to avoid an ad hoc impression of the architecture.
- Recommends exploring generalization to non-static or non-repeating rotation trajectories.
- Notes the approach is novel and interesting but currently lacks evidence to compete with geometry-based state-of-the-art methods.
