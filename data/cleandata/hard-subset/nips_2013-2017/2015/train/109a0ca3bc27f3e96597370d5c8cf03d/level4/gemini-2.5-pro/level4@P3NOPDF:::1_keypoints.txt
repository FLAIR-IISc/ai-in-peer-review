- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder to synthesize novel views from a single 2D image, tested on Multi-PIE faces and chairs.  
- Demonstrates curriculum learning, evaluates disentangled representations via cross-view recognition, and shows class interpolation for chairs.  
- Strength: novel combination of RNN and convolutional encoder-decoder to learn 3D transformations without explicit 3D models.  
- Strength: paper is generally well-written with a clear, detailed presentation of the architecture.  
- Major weakness: evaluation is mostly qualitative; lacks quantitative metrics (e.g., PSNR, SSIM, L2) to measure rendering accuracy.  
- Major weakness: no comparisons to existing methods or state-of-the-art baselines, making contribution unclear.  
- Major weakness: limited experimental scope—only two object classes (faces and chairs), limiting claims of generality.  
- Major weakness: tested only on simple fixed-axis rotations; needs evaluation on more complex, non-axis or compound rotations.  
- Minor issue: qualitative figures are too small, obscuring details in generated images.  
- Minor issue: contains a few grammatical errors; needs proofreading.  
- Conclusion: conceptually interesting but currently lacks rigorous experiments—should add quantitative metrics, baseline comparisons, diverse datasets, and more complex transformation tests.
