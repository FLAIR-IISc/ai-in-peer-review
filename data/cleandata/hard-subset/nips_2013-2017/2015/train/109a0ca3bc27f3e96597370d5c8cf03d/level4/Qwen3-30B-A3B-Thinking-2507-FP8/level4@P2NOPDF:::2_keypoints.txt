- Introduces a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes out-of-plane rotations from a single image.
- Architecture disentangles input into identity (appearance) and pose (rotation) units.
- Pose units are modified by action units specifying rotation direction; decoder reconstructs rotated image and mask.
- Recurrent extension keeps identity fixed while pose evolves through sequential actions for longer rotation trajectories.
- Primary contribution: learns disentangled identity and pose representations useful for generation and recognition.
- Identity units act as discriminative, view-invariant features, improving cross-view recognition performance.
- Curriculum learning with progressively longer rotations aids robust rendering and training of the recurrent model.
- Evaluation provides strong empirical support: high-fidelity qualitative synthesis and quantitative recognition gains.
- Minor clarification 1: model currently uses discrete rotation angles; discuss extensions for continuous rotations (e.g., regression or continuous actions).
- Minor clarification 2: recurrent network is trained on fixed-length sequences for trajectory generation; clarify this is a task-specific design, not a contradiction of recurrent networks.
- Overall: solid, well-motivated paper showing generative rotation prediction naturally yields useful disentangled representations.
