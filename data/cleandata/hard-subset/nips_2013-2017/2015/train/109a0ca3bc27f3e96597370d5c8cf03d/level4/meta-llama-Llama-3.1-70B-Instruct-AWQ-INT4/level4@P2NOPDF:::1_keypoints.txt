- Presents a recurrent convolutional encoder-decoder that synthesizes novel viewpoints from a single 2D image.
- Evaluated on face (Multi-PIE) and chair datasets to generate rotated-view images.
- Additional experiments examine curriculum learning benefits and disentangled representations via cross-view recognition.
- Demonstrates class interpolation for chairs to show learned latent space structure.
- Paper is well-written and detailed, with minor grammatical errors noted.
- Evaluation relies mainly on qualitative visualizations that could be scaled up for clarity.
- Lacks quantitative performance metrics and error measures for comparisons with prior work.
- Limited experimentation (only Multi-PIE and Chairs) undermines claims of generality and robustness.
- Needs tests on non-repeating rotations or rotations about non-static axes to assess generalization.
- Approach is novel and interesting but insufficient experimental analysis prevents strong claims versus geometry-based state-of-the-art.
