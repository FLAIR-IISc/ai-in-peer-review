- Proposes a recurrent convolutional encoder–decoder for single-image object view synthesis, evaluated on faces and chairs with experiments on curriculum learning, disentanglement, and class interpolation.

- Paper is clearly written and method/training details are well described, with minor grammatical issues.

- Evaluation is largely qualitative and inadequate for definitive assessment; figures need scaling for legibility.

- Lacks quantitative metrics and comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines, especially geometry-based approaches.

- Claims about disentanglement are unsupported by standard quantitative measures (e.g., cross-view recognition accuracy).

- Dataset scope is narrow (only Multi-PIE and Chairs), limiting claims of generality.

- Does not test robustness to non-static-axis rotations (off-axis, compound, or non-repeating trajectories).

- Overall novel and interesting but impact is limited by insufficient experimentation and analysis; recommends quantitative evaluations, broader datasets, deeper analysis of curriculum/disentanglement, and rotation-trajectory tests.
