- Proposes a novel Bayesian optimization method that avoids auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling by simultaneously exploring multiple semi-metric bounds using GP prior information.  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing exponential convergence rate and a regret bound under reasonable assumptions.  
- Removes the impractical δ-cover sampling requirement from prior work, improving practical applicability.  
- Clear motivation, well-articulated problem setting, and good placement within related bound-based search and exploration–exploitation literature.  
- Empirical results on benchmark functions show promising performance and favorable comparisons to existing methods.  
- Novelty: simultaneous consideration and GP-based pruning of multiple bounds is an original and interesting idea.  
- Theoretical rigor: analysis appears detailed and convincing, supporting the exponential convergence claim.  
- Practical relevance: addressing δ-cover sampling makes theoretical guarantees more usable in practice.  
- Scalability concern: convergence rate depends on input dimension D (λ approaches 1 as D increases), potentially negating exponential convergence in high dimensions.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance depends on Ξ_max, with limited justification for the default value (22) and insufficient analysis of its effect.  
- Limited experimental scope: evaluations are on a small set of benchmarks and lack tests for GP prior misspecification and higher-dimensional problems.  
- Algorithm clarity: steps (i)-(v) and Algorithm 1 are dense and hard to follow; pseudocode needs clearer structure, comments, and illustrative examples.  
- Computational cost: acknowledged higher cost than SOO but lacks detailed complexity comparison to GP-UCB, BaMSOO, and others.  
- Remark 3 and related discussion feel disconnected and need clearer integration and unpacking (especially regarding ρ̃_t).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach that removes a key prior limitation and shows promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dimensionality scalability, hyperparameter sensitivity, limited experiments, and computational cost concerns.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: discuss dimensionality mitigation strategies, analyze Ξ_max impact and justify its choice, expand experimental evaluation, clarify algorithm description/pseudocode, and add a detailed computational cost analysis.
