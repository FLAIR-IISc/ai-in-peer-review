- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Addresses two key limits of prior methods: need for auxiliary non-convex optimization and impractical δ-cover sampling.
- Builds on prior work (de Freitas et al. 2012, Wang et al. 2013) and the SOO framework of Munos (2011).
- Key insight: simultaneously consider all candidate semi-metric bounds while using the GP bound to prune search.
- Theoretical strength: rigorous analysis proving exponential regret O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1.
- Proof is well-structured and leverages Munos’ theoretical framework for SOO.
- Provides careful analysis of the GP prior’s effect and the infinite-metric exploration loss (Remarks 1–3).
- Experimental strength: consistent improvements across eight benchmark functions versus SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.
- Authors acknowledge practical limitation: potential scaling issues with dimensionality (Remark 1).
- Weakness: main theorem (Theorem 1) lacks full proof in the main text and is in the supplementary material.
- Weakness: insufficient clarity on how regret-bound constants (C, D, etc.) scale with dimensionality.
- Weakness: experimental section lacks sensitivity analysis for the Ξmax parameter.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, with clear figures and executable pseudocode; could introduce infinite-metric exploration loss earlier for intuition.
- Originality: novel “infinite-metric” idea that avoids committing to a single bound, a meaningful conceptual advance over GP-UCB and SOO.
- Significance: addresses a known practical limitation, offers theoretical and empirical advantages, and could influence broader optimization work.
- Pros for acceptance: first method with exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization/δ-cover, rigorous theory, strong experiments, practical relevance.
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on supplementary proofs, limited exploration of dimensionality behavior, only eight test functions, and some theoretical exposition may be inaccessible to a general audience.
- Overall recommendation: accept — high scores in quality, clarity, originality, and significance; strong theoretical and practical contribution.
