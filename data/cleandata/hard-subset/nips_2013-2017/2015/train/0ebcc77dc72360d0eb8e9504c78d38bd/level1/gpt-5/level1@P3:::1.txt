Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm for deterministic black-box functions that avoids auxiliary acquisition-function maximization and δ-cover sampling, while claiming an exponential convergence rate (simple regret decays as O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1). The method blends hierarchical partitioning (DIRECT/SOO-style) with GP upper confidence bounds used only as admissible filters, not as an acquisition to optimize. The key conceptual move is to not commit to a single (known) smoothness/metric; instead, IMGPO searches “optimistically” over infinitely many candidate semi-metrics around the global optimum, using the GP-UCB to prune intervals that cannot beat current best values.

The work sits at the intersection of:
- Bound-based global optimization with unknown smoothness: DIRECT [19], DOO/SOO [18].
- GP Bayesian optimization: EI/PI/UCB [3,15] and exponential regret for deterministic GP bandits with impractical δ-cover sampling [1].
- BaMSOO [2], which integrates GP and SOO to obtain polynomial regret without δ-cover.

IMGPO’s analysis introduces an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt (intervals expanded per iteration). When GP-UCB is informative, ρ̄t remains O(1), driving fast depth growth and an exponential-in-N regret, improving over BaMSOO’s polynomial rate. Experiments on a suite of synthetic benchmarks (including a REMBO-based 1000D case) show competitive or superior empirical performance and wall-clock improvements over GP-PI/EI that require auxiliary optimization.

Strengths:
- Conceptual novelty: a clear and appealing principle—treat GP-derived bounds as admissible filters while hedging over unknown smoothness via an infinite family of semi-metrics—bridges the GP-BO and optimistic partitioning literatures.
- Theoretical contribution: an exponential simple-regret bound under deterministic observations, with adaptivity to unknown Hölder parameters (L, α, p). The analysis makes explicit how the GP affects pruning (Ngp and ρ̄t), giving insight into when the GP helps despite possible mis-specification.
- Practicality: avoids solving a hard auxiliary nonconvex subproblem each iteration; the “lazy” use of UCBs to skip evaluations is simple and effective.
- Empirical results: broad benchmark coverage, sensible baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, EI/PI), and a discussion of runtime. The REMBO+IMGPO combination is a useful illustration for high dimensions.

Weaknesses and concerns:
- Assumptions behind UCB validity: The theoretical guarantee relies on P(f(x) ≤ U(x|D) for all calls) ≥ 1−η. However, experiments update GP hyperparameters by empirical Bayes; with misspecified kernels or data-driven hyperparameters, standard high-probability UCB guarantees (e.g., Srinivas et al.) do not directly apply. The paper should clearly state the conditions (fixed kernel/hyperparameters, prior correctness or bounded RKHS norm) under which Theorem 1 holds, and reconcile them with the practical use of empirical Bayes.
- Possible typo/clarity in Theorem 1: λ is written as λ = 3 − α/(2CDρ̄t) < 1, which is dimensionally odd. Presumably λ = 3−α/(2CDρ̄t) should be λ = 3−α/(2CDρ̄t) with an exponent, i.e., λ = 3−(α/(2CDρ̄t)) = exp(−(α ln 3)/(2CDρ̄t)). Please correct and re-derive explicitly.
- Scope of “exponential convergence”: The rate depends critically on ρ̄t (and D). When GP-UCB is uninformative, ρ̄t can grow and the bound degrades. The paper would benefit from empirical reporting of ρt, Ngp, and Ξn over time to substantiate the claimed mechanism (e.g., plots showing sustained small ρ̄t where IMGPO excels).
- Deterministic-only setting: The method and analysis are for noise-free observations. Many BO applications are noisy; it would help to discuss if/how the approach and bounds extend to sub-Gaussian noise or replicates.
- Computational complexity: Repeatedly computing UCBs over T′h+ξ and updating GP hyperparameters each iteration can be costly (O(n^3) Cholesky per update unless warm-started). A complexity analysis and practical details (e.g., whether a nugget is used, per-iteration GP update costs, batching of UCB computations, caching) would strengthen the practical case.
- Double-blindness: The code URL reveals author identity. Please provide an anonymized repository or supplementary zip for the review phase.
- Experimental baselines: While EI/PI are included, GP-UCB is omitted. Given the theoretical context, including GP-UCB with a reasonable multistart optimizer would help position IMGPO. Also report performance with fixed hyperparameters to illustrate sensitivity to empirical Bayes.
- Notation and minor clarity issues: The pseudo-code is dense; several lines (21–24) and definitions (T′h+ξ, C) would benefit from more precise notation. A footnote marker appears mid-text, and some terms (e.g., “Ξ updates”) are under-explained.

Quality:
- The algorithmic idea is sound and well-motivated. The analysis is interesting but its validity hinges on precise UCB assumptions that are not fully spelled out, especially vis-à-vis empirical Bayes. Some typos in the main theorem and lack of conditions reduce confidence. Experiments are generally supportive.

Clarity:
- Overall clear, but the analysis section and pseudo-code need tightening (fix λ, define constants and events precisely, improve readability). Including a brief computational complexity discussion would aid clarity.

Originality:
- The combination of infinite-metric optimistic partitioning with GP-based admissible pruning is original relative to BaMSOO and SOO/DOO/DIRECT. The “infinite-metric exploration loss” is a useful analytic device.

Significance:
- If the theoretical claims are fixed and the assumptions made explicit, this is a meaningful step toward practical BO without auxiliary optimization while retaining fast rates. The impact is moderate-to-high for deterministic BO and optimistic partitioning communities.

Questions and suggestions for the authors:
- State the exact assumption set under which f(x) ≤ U(x|D) holds (fixed kernel, known noise, RKHS norm bound), and reconcile with empirical Bayes updates used in practice.
- Correct and clarify Theorem 1 (λ expression), define C precisely, and provide a short proof sketch in the main text that highlights where GP enters (via Ngp, ρ̄t).
- Report ρt, Ngp, Ξn over time in experiments to connect empirical behavior with the theory. Include GP-UCB as a baseline.
- Provide computational complexity per iteration and any implementation tricks (e.g., incremental Cholesky, limiting UCB evaluations).
- Discuss extensions to noisy observations and whether the pruning step can be adapted with stochastic UCBs (e.g., βt from GP-UCB).
- Anonymize the code link for double-blind review.

Arguments pro acceptance:
- Novel, well-motivated integration of GP bounds with optimistic partitioning.
- Claims to remove two practical obstacles (auxiliary optimization and δ-covers).
- Insightful analysis offering conditions for exponential rates and interpretability.
- Strong empirical performance and practical algorithm.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Theoretical guarantees depend on unstated/unclear UCB assumptions; empirical Bayes may invalidate them.
- Typos/clarity issues in the main theorem; missing details reduce confidence.
- Deterministic-only scope; lack of noisy setting discussion.
- Double-blind violation via code link.

Overall, this is a promising contribution; with corrections to the theory statement, clarified assumptions, and improved presentation, it would be a strong NeurIPS paper.
