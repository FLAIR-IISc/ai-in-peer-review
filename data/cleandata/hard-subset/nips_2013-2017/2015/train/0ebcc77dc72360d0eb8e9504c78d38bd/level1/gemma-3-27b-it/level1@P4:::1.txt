This paper proposes a Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence that avoids auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling, both of which are drawbacks of existing methods. The approach leverages a Gaussian Process (GP) and introduces an “infinite-metric” optimization strategy based on hierarchical partitioning and considering a range of potential Lipschitz-like bounds. The authors claim to achieve exponential convergence without the practical limitations of prior work, particularly the δ-cover sampling requirement of de Freitas et al. [1].

The paper is well-structured, beginning with a clear motivation and overview of the problem. The related work section adequately positions the contribution with respect to existing Bayesian optimization techniques and highlights the limitations of current approaches. The technical details of the proposed algorithm (IMGPO) are presented in a reasonably clear manner, although the explanation of the hierarchical partitioning and the interplay between GP-based bounds and the unknown semi-metric ` requires careful reading. The experimental evaluation compares IMGPO with several baseline algorithms on a range of benchmark functions, demonstrating its effectiveness. The analysis section provides a theoretical justification for the exponential convergence rate.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of GP-based Bayesian optimization with the infinite-metric optimization strategy appears novel. Addressing the δ-cover sampling issue from [1] is a significant contribution, as acknowledged by prior work [2].
*   **Theoretical Guarantees:** The paper provides a theoretical analysis demonstrating exponential convergence, which is a strong result.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results demonstrate that IMGPO outperforms other algorithms on the tested functions, providing empirical support for the proposed method.
*   **Clear Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of existing methods and motivates the need for a new approach.
*   **Code Availability:** Publicly available code is a plus, enabling reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity of Infinite-Metric Concept:** While the core idea of considering a range of bounds is intuitive, the precise mechanism and mathematical justification for the “infinite-metric” optimization are not entirely clear. The explanation could be strengthened with a more illustrative example or diagram.
*   **Scalability Discussion:** While the paper mentions potential scalability issues with increasing dimensionality, the discussion is brief. More detailed analysis or potential solutions for high-dimensional problems would be valuable.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The impact of parameters like Ξmax on performance is not thoroughly investigated. The claim that increasing Ξmax might improve performance for some functions is not fully substantiated.
*   **Comparison to Recent Work:** Although the comparison includes related works like BaMSOO, the paper doesn't fully engage with very recent advancements in Bayesian optimization, particularly those focusing on dimensionality reduction or alternative acquisition functions.
*  **The connection between the unknown semi-metric and the practical implementation is not clear.** While the theoretical framework relies on the assumption of the existence of an unknown semi-metric, it's unclear how the algorithm utilizes this concept in practice beyond the hierarchical partitioning strategy.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant limitation of existing Bayesian optimization methods (δ-cover sampling).
*   It presents a novel approach with strong theoretical guarantees (exponential convergence).
*   The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
*   The code availability promotes reproducibility.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The clarity of the “infinite-metric” optimization concept could be improved.
*   The discussion of scalability issues is limited.
*   The paper doesn’t deeply analyze the parameter sensitivity.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The proposed IMGPO algorithm addresses a critical limitation of existing methods and provides a solid theoretical foundation with empirical support. While there are areas for improvement in terms of clarity and scalability discussion, the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the clarity issues and expand on the scalability discussion. Specifically, I suggest adding a more detailed explanation of the infinite-metric concept and potentially including experiments on higher-dimensional problems to assess scalability.
