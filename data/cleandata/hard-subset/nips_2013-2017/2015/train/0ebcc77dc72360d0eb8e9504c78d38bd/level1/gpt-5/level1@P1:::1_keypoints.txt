- Summary: Proposes IMGPO, a hybrid GP-UCB-pruned infinite-metric hierarchical partitioning method that avoids acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling, claiming exponential simple regret bounds and empirical gains.

- Key idea: Combine optimistic hierarchical partitioning over unknown Hölder-like parameters with GP posterior UCBs to skip or defer function evaluations.

- Theoretical claim: Proves exponential regret O(λ^{N+Ngp}) under local Hölder assumptions and GP-UCB containment, introducing the infinite-metric exploration loss ρt and algorithm-dependent constants.

- Empirical claim: IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI on deterministic benchmarks and integrates with REMBO for 1000-D embedded tasks.

- Strength — Conceptual novelty: Novel, principled hybrid that prunes with GP UCBs while retaining robustness to misspecified smoothness via “infinite-metric” exploration.

- Strength — Theoretical contribution: Provides an exponential regret bound adaptive to unknown smoothness parameters and explicitly relates convergence to D, C, ρ̄t, and GP quantities.

- Strength — Practical design: Avoids inner acquisition optimization, can defer evaluations using UCBs, and the code is open-source.

- Strength — Empirical evidence: Broad benchmark comparisons and high-dimensional REMBO experiments showing competitive and faster performance than standard GP BO.

- Weakness — GP-UCB assumption underspecified: The paper does not clearly state required assumptions for f relative to the GP when hyperparameters are learned online, which can break UCB containment guarantees.

- Weakness — Dependence on algorithm constants: Exponential rate relies on ρ̄t, Ξn, C, and D, which may render λ < 1 weak in high dimensions without extra structure.

- Weakness — Notation and clarity issues: Several malformed expressions, dense definitions (e.g., T′h+ξ(...)), and an under-explained adaptive Ξ update; pseudocode/2D example would help.

- Weakness — Empirical baselines and ablations: EI/PI baselines are disadvantaged by weak acquisition optimizers; missing stronger baselines, ablations on hyperparameter misspecification, and Ξmax effects.

- Weakness — Deterministic-only focus: Analysis and experiments target noise-free functions; robustness to noisy observations is not addressed.

- Quality (NeurIPS): Sound motivation and nontrivial analysis, but the practical-theoretical gap due to hyperparameter learning needs precise assumptions to validate the main theorem.

- Clarity (NeurIPS): Generally organized but hampered by notation/typos and dense algorithmic sections; suggest an invariant for node expansion and UCB placeholder resolution.

- Originality (NeurIPS): High — novel combination of infinite-metric partitioning and GP pruning with exponential regret without δ-cover sampling.

- Significance (NeurIPS): Meaningful removal of practical obstacles and useful insights on when GP bounds help; high-dimensional applicability still requires additional structure.

- Question 1: Precisely state and justify assumptions under which f(x) ≤ μ(x|D)+ςσ(x|D) holds when hyperparameters are re-estimated, or provide a lemma for the chosen ςM.

- Question 2: Report empirical values of ρ̄t and Ξn to connect observed convergence with the theory.

- Question 3: Evaluate sensitivity to initial lengthscales and misspecified kernels, including ARD lengthscale experiments.

- Question 4: Provide practical per-iteration complexity of step (iii) and report how often Ξ reaches Ξmax.

- Pros for acceptance: Novel algorithmic synthesis removing practical obstacles, insightful exponential-rate theory, and strong empirical performance on deterministic benchmarks.

- Cons for acceptance: Unclear probabilistic UCB assumption under hyperparameter learning, clarity/notation issues, limited ablations and weak acquisition-optimizer baselines, and limited high-dimensional guarantees without extra structure.

- Suggestions/minor fixes: Fix typos and λ expression, define constants and Ξ/Ξmax upfront, streamline Algorithm 1 with clearer names/comments, cite HOO/DOO explicitly, and discuss the noisy case.

- Overall recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on clarifying UCB containment under hyperparameter learning and improving clarity and empirical ablations.
