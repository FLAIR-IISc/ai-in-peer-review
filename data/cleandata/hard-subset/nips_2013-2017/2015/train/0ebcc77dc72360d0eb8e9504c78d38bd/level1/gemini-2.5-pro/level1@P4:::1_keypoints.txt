- Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence for simple regret.  
- Avoids auxiliary non-convex acquisition optimization and the impractical δ-cover sampling of prior exponential-convergence work.  
- Combines optimistic partition-based search (like SOO) with GP models, using GP-UCB to prune regions rather than to select points.  

- Theoretical result (Theorem 1) proves exponential regret O(λ^(N+Ngp)) under standard optimistic-optimization assumptions.  
- Analysis leverages established frameworks and standard semi-metric assumptions.  
- Remarks clarify dependence on GP quality via the infinite-metric exploration loss ρ̄t and the advantage of considering infinite bounds.  
- Empirical comparisons against SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI show strong, generally superior performance for IMGPO.  

- Paper is well-organized and motivated, but the algorithm description and pseudocode are dense and hard to follow.  
- Nested loops, depth-based logic, and screening conditions in Algorithm 1 are complex and would benefit from a step-by-step 1D trace.  
- Computing max U over centers in a fully expanded subtree (Line 21) appears computationally demanding; cost management and the role of Ξ_max are insufficiently detailed.  
- The update rule for Ξ (Line 38) seems heuristic and lacks clear justification.  

- Work is highly original, addressing an open problem by delivering a practical GP-based algorithm with proven exponential convergence.  
- Novel synthesis: using GP-UCB for pruning within an optimistic, partition-based search bridges DIRECT/SOO and Bayesian optimization lines.  

- Significant theoretical and practical contribution: constructive proof of exponential convergence and a method that removes the auxiliary acquisition optimization bottleneck.  
- Strong empirical results and a conceptual framework on model-based vs. model-free bounds that could inspire future hybrid methods.  

- Arguments for acceptance: strong novelty/significance, technical soundness, practical impact (no auxiliary optimization), and a valuable conceptual contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear algorithmic presentation, incomplete analysis of computational cost (UCB maximization), and insufficient exploration of parameter tuning and sensitivity (e.g., Ξ_max).
