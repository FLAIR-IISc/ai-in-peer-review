- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. but replaces δ‑cover sampling by exploiting an unknown bound from continuity at the global optimizer.  
- Well-organized paper with clear background, related work, and a helpful Algorithm 1 pseudocode.  
- Clearly relates IMGPO to prior methods such as BaMSOO and GP‑UCB.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis providing exponential convergence guarantee and a regret bound O(λ^N + N_gp) with λ < 1.  
- Explains how the tightness of the GP UCB affects the regret bound.  
- Strong experimental results on standard global‑optimization benchmarks, compared to SOO, BaMSOO, GP‑PI, and GP‑EI.  
- Technically sound work with claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Well‑written and easy to follow, using proper notation and terminology.  
- Original and distinct approach with potential significant impact on Bayesian optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, solid theoretical guarantees, impressive experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: assumes BO/GP familiarity; experiments limited in scope; lacks detailed comparisons with other state‑of‑the‑art algorithms.  
- Suggestions: provide more detailed comparisons, broader experimental evaluations, and clearer exposition of assumptions and limitations (GP prior and continuity bound).
