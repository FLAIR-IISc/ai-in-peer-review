This paper introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization algorithm for deterministic black-box functions that claims exponential simple-regret convergence without auxiliary acquisition optimization and without δ-cover sampling. The core idea is to combine hierarchical partitioning (as in DIRECT and SOO) with GP-based UCB screening: rather than relying on a single known bound, the method implicitly considers infinitely many candidate semi-metrics around the optimizer and uses the GP posterior only to prune candidates. The analysis assumes an unknown semi-metric satisfying a Hölder-type condition around x*, and proves a regret bound of the form O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1, where the rate depends on dimension, an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρ̄t, and a parameter Ξ that caps local subtree lookahead. Empirically, IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-PI/EI on standard benchmarks (Branin, Hartmann, Rosenbrock, etc.), and code is provided.

Relation to prior work: It directly addresses two limitations in de Freitas et al.’s deterministic GP bandits (ICML’12) requiring δ-covers, and improves on BaMSOO (AISTATS’14), which achieved only polynomial rates by coupling GP-UCB with SOO. The “unknown bound” perspective generalizes Munos’ SOO (NIPS’11) and connects to DIRECT’s bound-agnostic search.

Strengths
- Quality: The algorithm is carefully specified with pseudocode; the analysis introduces clear quantities (ρ̄t, Ξ) and provides an exponential-rate theorem under stated assumptions. The empirical study is fair (fixed kernel class, EB hyperparameters) and broad.
- Originality: Novel synthesis of infinite-metric partitioning with GP-based pruning, yielding a new rate claim without δ-covers or auxiliary optimization.
- Significance: If the guarantees hold under practical modeling choices, this removes two key pain points in GP BO and could influence deterministic global optimization practice.
- Clarity: The algorithmic description and intuition (Remarks 1–3) are helpful; connections to DIRECT/SOO are explicit.

Weaknesses
- Assumptions/guarantees: The theorem relies on f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with probability ≥ 1−η, but the paper updates GP hyperparameters via empirical Bayes and does not state conditions (e.g., well-specified GP, fixed kernel, RKHS norm bounds) under which the UCBs are valid. This is a key gap given the deterministic setting and can undermine the probabilistic guarantee.
- Scalability: The rate constant λ worsens with dimension; the analysis acknowledges this, and experiments rely on REMBO for 1000-D, so native high-D performance remains unclear.
- Complexity control: Step (iii)’s dependence on Ξmax and its effect on runtime and guarantees deserve fuller discussion and ablations.
- Clarity: Some notation is heavy; a concise theorem preamble listing all assumptions (domain geometry, kernel conditions, hyperparameter updates) would improve readability.

Pros for acceptance
- Addresses an open problem (exponential regret without δ-cover/auxiliary optimization).
- Solid empirical gains and practical algorithm.
- Clear conceptual contribution bridging two BO traditions.

Cons against acceptance
- Theoretical guarantee’s reliance on UCB validity is underspecified under EB hyperparameter learning.
- Limited analysis of computational complexity vs. Ξ and sensitivity to kernel misspecification.
- High-dimensional limitations not fully resolved.

Suggestion: Clarify and strengthen the conditions ensuring UCB validity under adaptive hyperparameters (or provide a misspecification-robust analysis), and add ablations on Ξmax and η.
