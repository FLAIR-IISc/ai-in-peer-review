**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), which achieves exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors propose a hierarchical partitioning optimization method that leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer. The algorithm simultaneously conducts global and local searches based on all possible candidates of the bounds, using the bound estimated by GP to reduce the number of candidates. The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the algorithm, including a regret bound that depends on the near-optimality dimension.

**Relationship to Previous Work**

The authors relate their work to previous Bayesian optimization methods, including GP-UCB and BaMSOO. They highlight the limitations of these methods, such as the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling, and demonstrate how their approach overcomes these limitations. The paper also discusses the relationship between IMGPO and other bound-based search methods, such as Lipschitz optimization and A\* search.

3. **Improved performance**: The experimental results demonstrate that IMGPO outperforms other Bayesian optimization methods, including GP-UCB and BaMSOO.

**Weaknesses**
