

# Review of "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence"

This paper introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary optimization (a common limitation of methods like GP-UCB) or δ-cover sampling (a requirement of the previous exponential convergence method by de Freitas et al. [1]). The key insight is to consider infinitely many possible upper bounds (semi-metrics) rather than relying on a single bound estimated from the GP prior. The authors formalize this approach through Assumption 1 (existence of an unknown semi-metric bound) and demonstrate that IMGPO's regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1 is superior to the polynomial regret bounds of existing methods like BaMSOO [2]. The paper relates this work to DIRECT [19] and SOO [18], showing how IMGPO combines the benefits of both approaches while addressing their limitations.

**Strengths:**
1. **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical analysis is rigorous, providing a clear exponential regret bound with well-defined assumptions (Assumptions 1 and 2). The proof techniques build upon existing work while introducing novel concepts like the infinite-metric exploration loss. The supplementary material provides complete proofs, which is commendable.

2. **Novelty:** The core contribution of considering infinitely many possible upper bounds (rather than a single bound) is genuinely novel. Previous work either relied on a single bound (GP-UCB) or used a parameter to balance global/local search (SOO, DIRECT). The paper convincingly demonstrates how this approach can be superior even when the GP-based bound is not tight.

3. **Empirical Validation:** The experimental evaluation is thorough, testing on eight diverse benchmark functions across dimensions 1-1000. IMGPO consistently outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, with the authors providing clear explanations for cases where GP-based methods underperform (e.g., when the GP prior mismodels the function).

4. **Theoretical Insights:** The paper provides valuable theoretical insights through Remarks 1-3, explaining how the tightness of the GP-based bound affects performance, the role of GP in reducing regret, and why considering multiple bounds is beneficial even when a bound is known.

**Weaknesses:**
1. **Parameter Sensitivity:** The paper lacks guidance for selecting Ξmax, which significantly affects performance (as shown in Figure 2). While the authors note that increasing Ξmax improves performance at the cost of computational time, they don't provide a principled way to choose this parameter.

2. **Scalability Concerns:** The paper acknowledges that λ approaches 1 as dimension D increases, suggesting scalability challenges for high-dimensional problems. While they mention potential solutions (e.g., leveraging additional assumptions like in [14]), this limitation isn't fully addressed.

3. **Computational Complexity Analysis:** The paper doesn't provide a detailed comparison of computational complexity between IMGPO and existing methods, particularly regarding the cost of considering multiple bounds versus the auxiliary optimization required by GP-UCB.

4. **Practical Implementation Details:** The authors state that they used a single kernel (isotropic Matern with ν=5/2) and fixed hyperparameter initialization, but don't discuss how these choices might affect performance in different scenarios.

**Pro/Con for Acceptance:**

*Pro:*
- Achieves exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling, addressing two significant practical limitations in Bayesian optimization.
- Provides a theoretically rigorous analysis with a clear exponential regret bound, which is a substantial improvement over previous methods.
- Demonstrates empirical superiority across diverse benchmark functions with comprehensive experiments.
- Offers valuable theoretical insights into bound-based optimization that could influence future work beyond Bayesian optimization.

*Con:*
- Requires manual tuning of Ξmax without clear guidelines, which may limit practical adoption.
- Scalability to high dimensions remains a challenge without additional assumptions.
- Computational complexity comparison with existing methods is incomplete.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to Bayesian optimization by introducing a novel approach that achieves exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary optimization or impractical sampling procedures. The technical soundness of the analysis is strong, and the empirical results convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over existing alternatives. While the paper has some limitations regarding parameter selection and scalability, these do not undermine the core theoretical contribution. The insights provided about the benefits of considering multiple bounds are particularly valuable and could influence future work in optimization and related fields.

The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and makes a compelling case for its contributions. It represents a meaningful advancement in the theoretical understanding and practical implementation of Bayesian optimization methods. The work is highly relevant to NIPS, addressing a fundamental problem in optimization with clear theoretical and practical implications.
