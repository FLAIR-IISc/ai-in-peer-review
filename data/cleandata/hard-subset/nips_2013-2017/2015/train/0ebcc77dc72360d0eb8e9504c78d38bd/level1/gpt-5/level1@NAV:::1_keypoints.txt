This paper proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization method combining hierarchical partitioning with GP-based UCB screening to avoid auxiliary acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
Core idea: implicitly consider infinitely many candidate semi-metrics around the optimizer and use the GP posterior only to prune candidates, yielding claimed exponential simple-regret convergence.  
Theoretical setup: assumes an unknown semi-metric with a Hölder-type condition around x*, and proves regret O(λN + Ngp) with λ < 1, depending on dimension, an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρ̄t, and a lookahead cap Ξ.  
Empirics: IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-PI/EI on standard benchmarks (Branin, Hartmann, Rosenbrock, etc.) and the authors provide code.  
Relation to prior work: removes δ-cover and auxiliary optimization requirements from de Freitas et al. (ICML’12) and improves on BaMSOO (AISTATS’14), generalizing Munos’ SOO and relating to DIRECT.  
Strength — Quality: algorithm well-specified with pseudocode; analysis introduces clear quantities (ρ̄t, Ξ) and gives an exponential-rate theorem under stated assumptions.  
Strength — Originality: novel synthesis of infinite-metric partitioning with GP pruning to achieve faster rates without δ-covers.  
Strength — Significance: if practical guarantees hold, the method addresses two main pain points in GP BO and could impact deterministic global optimization.  
Strength — Clarity: helpful algorithmic description and intuitive remarks; explicit connections to DIRECT/SOO.  
Weakness — Assumptions/guarantees: theorem depends on f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with high probability, but GP hyperparameters are updated via empirical Bayes without stated conditions ensuring UCB validity.  
Weakness — Scalability: rate constant λ degrades with dimension; experiments use REMBO for 1000-D so native high-dimensional performance remains unclear.  
Weakness — Complexity control: the role of Ξmax in runtime and theoretical guarantees needs fuller discussion and empirical ablation.  
Weakness — Clarity: notation is heavy and a concise theorem preamble listing all assumptions (domain, kernel, hyperparameter updates) is missing.  
Pros for acceptance: addresses an open problem, shows solid empirical gains, provides practical algorithm and clear conceptual bridge between BO traditions.  
Cons against acceptance: UCB validity under EB hyperparameter learning is underspecified, limited complexity analysis regarding Ξ, and unresolved high-dimensional limitations.  
Suggested improvements: clarify and strengthen conditions ensuring UCB validity under adaptive hyperparameters (or give misspecification-robust analysis), and add ablations on Ξmax and η.
