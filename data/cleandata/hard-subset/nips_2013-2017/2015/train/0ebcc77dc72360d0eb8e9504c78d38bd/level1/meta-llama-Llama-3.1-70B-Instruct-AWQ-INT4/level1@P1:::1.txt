This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method, called Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), which achieves exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors build upon the existing literature on Bayesian optimization, particularly the work of de Freitas et al. [1], which introduced a theoretical procedure with exponential convergence rate but required δ-cover sampling.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the background, related work, and the proposed algorithm. The introduction effectively sets the stage for the problem, and the authors clearly articulate the contributions of their work.

The main idea of IMGPO is to leverage the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer, rather than relying solely on the known upper bound provided by the Gaussian process (GP) prior. This approach allows the algorithm to consider infinitely many possible candidates for the bound, which is shown to be beneficial in the analysis.

The technical detail of the algorithm is well-explained, and the pseudocode provided in Algorithm 1 is helpful in understanding the implementation. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the relationship between IMGPO and previous algorithms, such as BaMSOO [2] and GP-UCB.

The analysis of the algorithm is thorough, and the authors provide a theoretical guarantee of exponential convergence rate. The regret bound is shown to be O(λN+Ngp), where λ < 1, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the effect of the tightness of the UCB by GP on the regret bound.

The experimental results are impressive, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of IMGPO on a range of objective functions, including standard benchmarks for global optimization. The comparison with other algorithms, such as SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, is helpful in understanding the strengths and weaknesses of IMGPO.
