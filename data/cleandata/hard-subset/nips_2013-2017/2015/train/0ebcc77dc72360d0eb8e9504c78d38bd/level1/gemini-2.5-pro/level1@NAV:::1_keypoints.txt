- Introduces IMGPO, a novel GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving an exponential convergence (regret) rate.  
- Solves a known open problem by avoiding impractical δ-cover sampling and expensive auxiliary optimization steps.  
- Combines GP-UCB with infinite-metric hierarchical partitioning (inspired by SOO) to implicitly handle infinite smoothness classes.  
- Uses GP-UCB bounds to efficiently prune unpromising hyperrectangles during search.  
- Major theoretical contribution: first practical GP-based method with an exponential regret bound, advancing beyond polynomial bounds like BaMSOO.  
- Analytical insights (Remarks 1–3) clarify the interplay between GP bounds and infinite-metric optimization.  
- Technically sound with a formal proof of exponential convergence provided in supplementary material.  
- Empirically validated on diverse benchmarks, showing superior or competitive performance versus SOO, BaMSOO, GP-EI/PI.  
- Authors acknowledge scalability limitations in high-dimensional settings.  
- Weakness: algorithm description is dense; mapping prose to Algorithm 1 is hard, hurting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: practical complexity with nested loops and partitioning rules may impede practitioner adoption.  
- Recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses; strong acceptance recommendation.
