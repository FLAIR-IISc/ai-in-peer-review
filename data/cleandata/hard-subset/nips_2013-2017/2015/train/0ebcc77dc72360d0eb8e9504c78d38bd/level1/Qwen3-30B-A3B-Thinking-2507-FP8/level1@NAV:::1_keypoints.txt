- Proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. and Wang et al. to solve the open problem of a GP-based algorithm with exponential convergence that avoids impractical sampling.  
- Key innovation: use of "infinitely many possible bounds" via an unknown semi-metric assumption instead of a single GP-derived bound.  
- Provides theoretical exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ<1, outperforming BaMSOO’s polynomial regret.  
- Strengths: rigorous theoretical analysis with well-motivated assumptions and a clear algorithmic description including pseudocode.  
- Empirical strengths: comprehensive experiments showing consistent performance gains across multiple benchmarks, including higher-dimensional problems.  
- Practical value: removes need for δ‑cover sampling while preserving theoretical guarantees and analyzes how GP bound quality affects regret.  
- Weakness: dense theoretical exposition that needs more intuitive explanation of the infinite-metric exploration loss.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons should include additional recent baselines (e.g., BOHB, TuRBO).  
- Weakness: potential scalability issues as λ approaches 1 with increasing dimensionality, requiring deeper discussion.  
- Overall: significant theoretical and practical contribution that differentiates from prior work (BaMSOO, GP‑UCB); recommendation: accept.
