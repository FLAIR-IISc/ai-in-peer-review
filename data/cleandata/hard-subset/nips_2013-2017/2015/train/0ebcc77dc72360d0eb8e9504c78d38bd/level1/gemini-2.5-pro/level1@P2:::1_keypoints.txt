- Proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a hierarchical partitioning Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence for simple regret.  
- Targets deterministic black-box global optimization, avoiding auxiliary non-convex acquisition optimization.  
- Balances exploration of large partitions and exploitation of small partitions using an implicit infinite family of unknown semi-metrics.  
- Uses a GP model not to guide search but to prune provably suboptimal regions via the GP upper confidence bound.  
- Achieves an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) without δ-cover sampling, addressing an open problem.  
- Theoretical analysis extends Munos (2011) and introduces the "infinite-metric exploration loss" ρt to quantify agnosticism to smoothness.  
- Empirical results show superior performance versus SOO, BaMSOO, and standard GP methods on benchmarks.  
- Acknowledges failure modes: performance can degrade when the GP prior is misleading (e.g., Rosenbrock2).  
- Paper is technically strong, with well-supported claims and thorough experiments.  
- Completeness: presents algorithm, detailed theory, assumptions, and discussion of dimensionality dependence.  
- Clarity: well-written, logically structured, with helpful conceptual overview and illustrative figures.  
- Suggests simplifying some dense notation (e.g., T'h+ξ(...) description) and better motivation for the heuristic Ξ update.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of hierarchical partitioning (SOO/DIRECT) and GP-based pruning rather than guidance.  
- Conceptual contribution: unifies Lipschitz-inspired partitioning and Bayesian modeling via the "infinite-metric" framing.  
- Practical significance: removes costly acquisition optimization, making BO more off-the-shelf and efficient; code provided.  
- Broader impact: pruning learned models could generalize to other AI search domains (planning, RL).  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves an open problem, strong theory, practical value, novel approach, excellent presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: algorithm complexity may hinder adoption and sensitivity to GP model quality.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (Oral/Spotlight).
