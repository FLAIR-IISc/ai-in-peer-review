- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Addresses key limitations: GP-UCB’s need for auxiliary non-convex optimization and de Freitas et al.’s impractical δ-cover sampling.  
- Extends GP-UCB framework and relates to SOO and BaMSOO, correctly positioning against prior work.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1 under reasonable assumptions.  
- Proofs are sound and supplementary material provides sufficient technical detail.  
- Empirical evaluation: compares IMGPO to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI on eight benchmarks, showing consistent improvements.  
- Notes scalability concern: λ approaches 1 as dimensionality increases, indicating challenges in high-dimensional problems.  
- Discusses trade-off between using GP to reduce exploration loss (ρ̄t) and increasing Ξn.  
- Clarity: well-organized, clear motivation, helpful Figure 1, and reproducible pseudocode; some technical definitions may challenge readers.  
- Originality: novel concept of considering infinitely many candidate bounds instead of a single GP-encoded upper bound.  
- Significance: removes practical barriers to theoretically strong methods and may influence other bound-based optimization approaches.  
- Strengths: first to get exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization/δ-cover sampling, strong theory, comprehensive experiments, clear exposition, public code.  
- Weaknesses: limited analysis of high-dimensional scalability beyond theoretical note about λ; Ξmax fixed to 22 without thorough sensitivity analysis.  
- Weaknesses (cont.): lacks statistical significance testing between IMGPO and baselines; connection to REMBO in the 1000-D test is underexplained.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a known practical problem, rigorous theory, consistent empirical gains, and potential broader impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited high-dimensional evaluation, some technical complexity for non-specialists, and unexplored parameter sensitivity.  
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance—significant, technically sound, and practically relevant contribution.
