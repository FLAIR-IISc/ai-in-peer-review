- Proposes IMGPO: a Bayesian optimization method combining Gaussian Processes with an “infinite-metric” hierarchical partitioning strategy to avoid auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling.
- Claims exponential convergence without the δ-cover sampling requirement of prior work (de Freitas et al.).
- Paper is well-structured with clear motivation and a related-work section that positions the contribution relative to existing BO methods.
- Technical presentation of IMGPO is reasonable, but the hierarchical partitioning and GP-bound interplay require careful reading.
- Experimental evaluation compares IMGPO to several baselines on benchmark functions and shows improved performance.
- Provides theoretical analysis that justifies the claimed exponential convergence rate.
- Novelty: the GP + infinite-metric combination and removing δ-cover sampling is a meaningful and novel contribution.
- Theoretical guarantees are strong and notable (exponential convergence).
- Empirical validation supports effectiveness of IMGPO across tested functions.
- Clear motivation and articulation of limitations in prior methods.
- Code is publicly available, aiding reproducibility.
- The “infinite-metric” concept and its mathematical justification are not fully clear and need clearer exposition or examples.
- Scalability discussion is brief; more analysis or solutions for high-dimensional problems are needed.
- Parameter sensitivity (e.g., Ξmax) is insufficiently investigated and claims about its effects are not fully substantiated.
- Comparison misses engagement with very recent BO advances, especially methods focusing on dimensionality reduction or alternative acquisition functions.
- The practical connection between the assumed unknown semi-metric and the algorithm’s implementation is unclear.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant limitation, offers novel theory and empirical results, and provides code.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity of core concept, limited scalability discussion, and insufficient parameter sensitivity analysis.
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution; accept with minor revisions to clarify the infinite-metric idea and expand scalability/experimental evaluation (e.g., higher-dimensional tests).
