- Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm using an unknown semi-metric bound to guide search.  
- Uses hierarchical partitioning and a novel interval selection strategy to balance global and local exploration.  
- Eliminates need for auxiliary optimization and impractical δ-cover sampling used by prior work.  
- Theoretical analysis shows IMGPO achieves exponential convergence/regret bounds, outperforming methods like BaMSOO.  
- Empirical results on benchmark functions demonstrate superior performance and computational efficiency, including a 1000-D experiment using REMBO.  
- Technical novelty: infinite-metric exploration is a new and elegant solution to tightness issues in GP-derived bounds.  
- Theoretical rigor: detailed regret analysis and discussion of GP-bound vs infinite-metric effects.  
- Practical relevance: more practical and computationally efficient than traditional GP-UCB; source code provided.  
- Scalability concern: regret bound worsens with dimensionality and no concrete remedy is proposed beyond REMBO.  
- Algorithm complexity: hierarchical partitioning and interval selection are intricate and may be hard to implement/tune.  
- Limited empirical comparison: missing comparisons with recent BO advances (e.g., neural-network-based surrogates).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: paper lacks thorough exploration of sensitivity to parameters like Ξmax.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental BO challenge with strong theory and empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability, algorithm complexity, and limited SOTA comparisons may limit impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability and hyperparameter sensitivity.
