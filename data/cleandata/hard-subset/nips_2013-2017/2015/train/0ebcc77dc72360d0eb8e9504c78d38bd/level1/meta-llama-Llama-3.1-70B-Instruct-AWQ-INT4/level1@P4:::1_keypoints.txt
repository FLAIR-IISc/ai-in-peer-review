- IMGPO: a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Uses hierarchical partitioning and an unknown continuity-bound at the global optimizer; performs simultaneous global/local search over bound candidates, pruned by GP-estimated bounds.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including a regret bound that depends on the near-optimality dimension.  
- Compares to GP-UCB and BaMSOO, claiming to avoid their need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Positions IMGPO relative to other bound-based searches like Lipschitz optimization and A* search.  
- Strength: Introduces a novel approach combining bound-based search with GP flexibility.  
- Strength: Offers rigorous theoretical guarantees (regret bound).  
- Strength: Empirical results show improved performance over GP-UCB and BaMSOO.  
- Weakness: Computational cost from computing UCB values for multiple intervals.  
- Weakness: Performance sensitive to hyperparameter choices (e.g., Ξmax and η).  
- Weakness: May not scale well to high-dimensional problems without additional assumptions.  
- Pro acceptance: Presents an innovative method for Bayesian optimization.  
- Pro acceptance: Provides strong theoretical understanding of performance.  
- Pro acceptance: Demonstrates empirical improvements over existing methods.  
- Con acceptance: Potentially high computational expense due to multiple UCB evaluations.  
- Con acceptance: Hyperparameter sensitivity could hinder robustness.  
- Con acceptance: Limited scalability to high-dimensional settings.  
- Quality: Technically sound with rigorous analysis and well-presented experiments.  
- Clarity: Well-written and easy to follow, with clear algorithm and analysis exposition.  
- Originality: Novel combination of bound-based and GP-based optimization ideas.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful contribution to Bayesian optimization with broad application relevance.
