- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Positions work relative to GP-UCB and SOO, highlighting limitations of existing methods.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear technical exposition of GP prior and infinite-metric exploration loss.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including a regret bound for IMGPO.  
- Empirical results on benchmark functions show IMGPO outperforms state-of-the-art algorithms.  
- Strength: novel approach addressing limitations of previous Bayesian optimization methods.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation of algorithm and technical details.  
- Strength: theoretical guarantees (regret bound) supporting algorithm effectiveness.  
- Strength: strong experimental performance demonstrating superiority over baselines.  
- Weakness: potential computational expense from computing GP posterior and infinite-metric exploration loss.  
- Weakness: relies on black-box objective function assumption, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of hyperparameter choices and sensitivity.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address hyperparameter discussion and add robustness experiments.
