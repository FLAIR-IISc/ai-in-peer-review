- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Core idea: consider infinitely many possible upper bounds (semi-metrics) instead of a single GP-based bound.  
- Presents formal Assumption 1 (unknown semi-metric bound) and derives an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with complete proofs and novel concepts (e.g., infinite-metric exploration loss).  
- Novelty: genuinely new approach relative to GP-UCB, SOO, and DIRECT by using multiple bounds to balance exploration/exploitation.  
- Relates method to DIRECT and SOO, combining their benefits while addressing their limitations.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough across eight benchmark functions and dimensions 1–1000, showing consistent superiority over SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.  
- Provides theoretical insights (Remarks 1–3) on GP-bound tightness, GP’s role in reducing regret, and advantages of multiple bounds.  
- Lacks guidance for selecting Ξmax, a parameter that strongly affects performance and runtime.  
- Scalability concerns: λ approaches 1 as dimensionality increases, limiting high-dimensional performance without extra assumptions.  
- No detailed computational-complexity comparison between IMGPO and existing methods, especially regarding multi-bound costs vs. auxiliary optimization.  
- Practical implementation details are sparse: single isotropic Matern kernel and fixed hyperparameter initialization used without discussion of broader impacts.  
- Pros for acceptance: solves key practical limitations, offers strong theory and empirical results, and yields insights likely to influence future optimization research.  
- Cons for acceptance: requires manual Ξmax tuning, uncertain scalability to high dimensions, and incomplete complexity analysis.  
- Overall: significant theoretical and empirical contribution to Bayesian optimization, well-written and relevant, despite practical limitations in tuning and scalability.
