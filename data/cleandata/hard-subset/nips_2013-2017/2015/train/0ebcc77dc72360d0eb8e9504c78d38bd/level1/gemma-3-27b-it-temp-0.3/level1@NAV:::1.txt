## Review of "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence"

This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization (BO) method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling, addressing limitations of prior work like de Freitas et al. [1] and Wang et al. [2]. The core idea is to leverage an unknown semi-metric encoding a tighter bound on the objective function, combined with a hierarchical partitioning strategy, to guide exploration. The authors argue that while GP-based methods provide a bound, a tighter, unknown bound likely exists, and their method simultaneously searches based on both.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the drawbacks of existing exponential convergence BO methods (reliance on δ-cover sampling) and the challenges of practical implementation. The proposed Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO) algorithm is described in detail, with a helpful illustration (Figure 1) of the hierarchical partitioning process. The theoretical analysis provides a regret bound demonstrating exponential convergence, and the experimental results on eight functions (including standard benchmarks) show IMGPO generally outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, particularly when the GP-estimated bound is less tight.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The approach of simultaneously considering multiple bounds (including an unknown semi-metric) is a significant contribution.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a theoretical analysis with a proven exponential convergence rate.
*   **Practical Relevance:** Eliminating the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling makes the method more practical.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of IMGPO across a range of benchmark functions.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a clear explanation of the algorithm and its underlying principles.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Scalability Concerns:** While the experiments show promising results, the analysis highlights potential scalability issues in high dimensions (mentioned in Section 4). The authors suggest future work to address this.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The choice of Ξmax (controlling the depth of the search tree) could be sensitive and require tuning. The paper acknowledges this and provides some guidance, but further investigation might be needed.
*   **Assumptions:** The assumption of an unknown semi-metric (Assumption 1) is strong. While reasonable, it's not always guaranteed to hold.
*   **Comparison Limitations:** The comparison with other methods uses fixed hyperparameters for all algorithms, which might not be a fair comparison. A more thorough hyperparameter tuning process for all methods could strengthen the results.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to Bayesian optimization.
*   It addresses a significant limitation of existing exponential convergence methods.
*   The experimental results are promising and demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed algorithm.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The scalability concerns and parameter sensitivity need to be carefully considered.
*   The strong assumption about the existence of a semi-metric could limit the applicability of the method.



**Overall:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. The contributions are significant, the paper is well-written, and the results are promising. The identified weaknesses are acknowledged by the authors and represent potential directions for future research. The paper advances the state-of-the-art in Bayesian optimization and is likely to be of interest to researchers in the field.
