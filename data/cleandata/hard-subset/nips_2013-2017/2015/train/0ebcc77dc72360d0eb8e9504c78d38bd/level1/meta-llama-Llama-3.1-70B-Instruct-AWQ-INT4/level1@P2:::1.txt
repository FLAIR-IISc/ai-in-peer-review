The paper "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence" presents a novel Bayesian optimization method that achieves exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors propose an infinite-metric GP optimization (IMGPO) algorithm that leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical analysis. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the related work and highlight the contributions of their paper.

* The proposed algorithm achieves exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling, which is a significant improvement over existing Bayesian optimization methods.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm, including a proof of the exponential convergence rate and a discussion of the regret bound.
* The paper includes a comprehensive evaluation of the algorithm on several benchmark functions, demonstrating its effectiveness in practice.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The algorithm requires a careful tuning of several hyperparameters, including the kernel parameters, the UCB parameter, and the maximum depth of the search tree. This may limit its applicability in practice.
* The paper assumes that the objective function is deterministic, which may not be the case in many real-world applications.
* The algorithm's performance may degrade in high-dimensional spaces, as the number of possible bounds increases exponentially with the dimensionality.
