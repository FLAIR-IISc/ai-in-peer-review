Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization method for deterministic black-box functions that achieves exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling and without auxiliary global optimization of the acquisition function. The algorithm marries hierarchical optimistic partitioning (as in DIRECT and SOO) with GP posterior upper confidence bounds (UCB) used only to prune/divert evaluations rather than to define the sole search criterion. The key conceptual move is to not commit to a single, potentially loose, global bound (e.g., a Lipschitz constant), but instead to implicitly consider an infinite family of “semi-metric” bounds; the GP UCB serves to rule out candidates that are provably suboptimal with high probability. The theory formalizes the cost of this non-commitment via an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt and proves an exponential regret bound of the form O(λN+Ngp) under Hölder-type continuity near the optimizer. Empirically, IMGPO is compared to SOO, BaMSOO (SOO+GP-UCB), and GP-EI/PI on standard deterministic test functions, typically outperforming them, and also used as the REMBO subroutine in 1000D.

This work builds on: (i) GP-UCB and related GP-BO (Srinivas et al. 2010; Snoek et al. NIPS’12), (ii) the exponential-regret result for deterministic GP bandits with δ-cover (de Freitas et al. ICML’12), (iii) optimistic partitioning (DIRECT; SOO, Munos NIPS’11), and (iv) BaMSOO (Wang et al., AISTATS’14), which achieves polynomial rates. The contribution is to obtain exponential rates without δ-cover and without solving an inner nonconvex problem.

Quality
- Theory: The high-level argument is sensible: combine tree-based optimistic search with GP-UCB pruning and analyze regret under Assumptions 1–2 (existence of an unknown semi-metric yielding Hölder continuity near x*). The notion of ρt cleanly captures the price of hedging over many possible bounds, and the remarks clarify how GP helps by decreasing ρ̄t and increasing Ngp. However, there are issues that require clarification:
  - The expression for λ appears mistyped (“λ = 3 − α2CDρ̄t < 1” cannot be <1 for general α,C,D,ρ̄t); presumably λ = 3−α/(2CDρ̄t). Please fix and restate the bound cleanly.
  - The high-probability UCB guarantee typically assumes fixed kernel/hyperparameters; here, hyperparameters are re-estimated after each iteration. This may invalidate the concentration inequality used to ensure f(x) ≤ U(x|D) w.p. ≥ 1−η across the run. Either (a) state the theorem for fixed hyperparameters and treat adaptation as a heuristic in experiments, or (b) provide a justification that the particular empirical Bayes updates preserve the required coverage.
  - Constants C, β, p, and the assumptions about the division scheme are scattered; a compact theorem statement with all definitions would improve reproducibility.
  - The dependence on dimension is adverse (λ → 1 as D grows), as the authors note. Discussion of near-optimality dimension vs D would help contextualize when exponential rates are meaningful in practice.
- Algorithmic soundness: IMGPO’s integration of GP UCB into SOO-like selection/pruning is well motivated. The “dummy value from UCB to avoid evaluation” trick is a practical speedup. Complexity and implementation details are reasonable, though an explicit discussion of computational cost per iteration (GP updates and the worst-case cost of Step (iii) limited by Ξmax) would be useful.
- Experiments: The benchmarks are standard and the plots indicate consistent improvements. Positives: same kernel class across methods; code availability; sensitivity to Ξmax discussed. Concerns:
  - Acquisition-optimization baselines: GP-EI/PI are optimized with SOO and a local gradient-based method; typical practice uses multi-start L-BFGS-B or CMA-ES. Including a strong GP-UCB baseline with DIRECT/CMA-ES (as suggested in the text) would strengthen claims about avoiding auxiliary optimization.
  - Statistical reporting: Only GP-EI/PI have repeated runs; SOO/IMGPO/BaMSOO are deterministic. Provide wall-clock times (Table 1 is referenced but not shown) and, where applicable, multiple seeds for stochastic baselines.
  - Noisy objectives are out of scope; this should be stated clearly in the abstract and experiments.

Clarity
The paper is generally well organized and readable. The algorithm description and pseudocode are helpful. However, there are several notational/typographical issues (e.g., λ expression; mixing ρt and ρτ; minor inconsistencies in indices and line references). Assumptions and constants should be centralized, and the proof sketch (main ideas) could be summarized in the main text for intuition. Please also clarify the role and adaptation of Ξ, and the conditions under which Step (v) uses UCB values without evaluation.

Originality
High. While BaMSOO already combined SOO and GP-UCB, IMGPO’s use of GP only as a pruning signal within an “infinite-metric” framework, plus the new analysis yielding exponential rates without δ-cover or inner optimization, is novel. The “infinite-metric exploration loss” is a useful analytical device that may be of independent interest.

Significance
Potentially significant. Removing δ-cover and auxiliary optimization while attaining exponential convergence is an appealing theoretical and practical advancement for deterministic BO. The empirical gains on standard testbeds suggest the method can be useful. Limitations (deterministic setting, dimensional dependence) temper the impact, but the approach could inspire further work (e.g., noise-robust variants; better high-D structure assumptions).

Pros for acceptance
- New algorithmic principle (infinite-metric pruning with GP) and clear advancement over BaMSOO.
- Exponential regret bound without δ-cover and without inner acquisition optimization.
- Insightful analysis (role of ρ̄t, Ngp, and the benefit of not committing to a single bound).
- Solid empirical performance and released code.

Cons for acceptance
- Theoretical clarity issues (typo in λ; unaddressed effect of hyperparameter learning on UCB coverage).
- Experimental baselines for acquisition optimization could be stronger; missing runtime table in the main text.
- Scope limited to deterministic objectives; adverse D-dependence acknowledged but not mitigated.
- Several notational/clarity rough edges.

Suggestions for improvement
- Fix theorem statements/typos; consolidate assumptions/constants; add a brief proof sketch.
- Either hold hyperparameters fixed in theory/experiments or justify adaptive updates within the concentration analysis.
- Add a GP-UCB baseline with standard global optimizers; report wall-clock times and sensitivity to kernel/hyperparameters.
- Discuss extensions to noisy observations and to high-D settings beyond REMBO.

Overall, I find the contribution technically interesting, original, and practically relevant, with some clarifications needed. I lean toward acceptance.
