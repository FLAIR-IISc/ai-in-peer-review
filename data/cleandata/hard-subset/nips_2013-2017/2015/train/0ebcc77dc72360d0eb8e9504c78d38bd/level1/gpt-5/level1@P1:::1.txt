Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes IMGPO, a Gaussian-process-based global optimization algorithm that avoids auxiliary acquisition maximization and the impractical δ-cover sampling while claiming an exponential simple regret bound. The key idea is to combine: (i) a bound-based, hierarchical partitioning search (in the spirit of DIRECT, SOO/DOO) that implicitly reasons over an infinite family of possible Lipschitz/Hölder-type bounds (“infinite-metric”), with (ii) GP posterior UCBs used as a filter to prune partitions whose optimistic value is dominated by already-explored regions. The method adaptively divides rectangles along the longest coordinate and may skip objective evaluations by substituting UCB values until later confirmation. The analysis introduces an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt (how many intervals are expanded per iteration), and proves a regret of the form rN ≤ const · exp(−α[(N+Ngp)/(2 C D ρ̄t) − Ξn − 2] ln 3), i.e., O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1, under assumptions that the function satisfies a Hölder-type local bound around the global optimum (unknown L, α, p) and that the GP-UCB upper bounds contain f with high probability. Empirically, IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on a suite of low- to moderate-dimensional deterministic benchmarks, and integrates with REMBO for a 1000-D embedded task. This builds on and extends lines of work on Lipschitz/optimistic partitioning (DIRECT, HOO/DOO/SOO) and GP-based BO (GP-UCB, EI/PI), notably improving upon BaMSOO’s polynomial regret and de Freitas et al.’s exponential rate that required δ-cover sampling.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: A principled hybrid that leverages GP UCBs to prune while retaining robustness to misspecified smoothness via “infinite-metric” exploration; removes the practical barrier of δ-cover sampling and avoids auxiliary acquisition optimization.
- Theoretical contribution: An exponential regret bound (in N+Ngp) that adapts to unknown L, α, p and makes explicit how dimension D, the partition constant C, the exploration loss ρ̄t, and the GP-related quantities affect convergence. The remarks provide useful insight into when GP information helps or hurts.
- Practical design: No inner global optimizer; ability to defer function evaluations using UCBs; open-source code.
- Empirical evidence: Broad comparisons on standard deterministic benchmarks and with REMBO for very high-dimensional embeddings; competitive to strong baselines (SOO, BaMSOO) and faster than standard GP BO that solves inner optimizations.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Assumptions for GP-UCB validity are under-specified. The “f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with probability ≥ 1−η for all U-calls” typically requires that f is a sample from the GP prior or belongs to an RKHS with bounded norm together with a specific βt schedule (Srinivas et al., 2010). The paper sets ςM via a union bound but does not clearly state the needed assumption on f relative to the GP. Moreover, hyperparameters are re-estimated online (empirical Bayes), which generally breaks standard UCB containment guarantees. The theorem should either fix hyperparameters or explicitly state and justify assumptions under which containment still holds with learning.
- Exponential rate depends on algorithm-dependent quantities (ρ̄t, Ξn) and constants with adverse dimensional dependence (C, D). While this is common, the practical tightness of λ < 1 may be weak in high D unless additional structure (e.g., REMBO/additivity) is assumed. The paper acknowledges this but could quantify it more.
- Notational and clarity issues: Some expressions appear malformed (e.g., λ = 3 − α/(2 C D ρ̄t) should likely be λ = 3^{−α/(2 C D ρ̄t)}). Step (iii) and the definition of T′h+ξ(ch,i*h) are dense and could be clarified with pseudocode comments or a small 2D example. The adaptive update of Ξ (line 38) is non-standard and under-explained.
- Empirical methodology: While avoiding hand-tuned kernels is laudable, EI/PI baselines are disadvantaged by using SOO plus a local optimizer for acquisition maximization; stronger baselines (multi-start L-BFGS, DIRECT/CMA-ES for acquisitions) and GP-UCB with standard βt would strengthen the case. An ablation on the impact of GP hyperparameter misspecification and on Ξmax would be helpful.
- Deterministic-only setting: The method and analysis target noise-free functions. Discussion of robustness to small noise and how the algorithm or proof extends would broaden impact.

Evaluation against NeurIPS criteria
- Quality: The algorithm is soundly motivated and the analysis is nontrivial; however, the GP-UCB containment assumption under hyperparameter learning needs to be precisely stated to ensure the main theorem is valid as implemented. Proof details are in the supplement; the main ideas are plausible and connect to prior optimistic partitioning analyses.
- Clarity: Generally well-organized with an illustrative figure and pseudocode, but several notation/typo issues and dense sections (Algorithm 1 lines 20–23; the definition and role of Ξ) hamper readability. Add a succinct invariant describing when a node is expanded and when UCB placeholders are resolved.
- Originality: High. The combination of infinite-metric optimistic partitioning with GP-based pruning and an exponential regret bound without δ-cover is, to my knowledge, novel relative to DIRECT, SOO/DOO, BaMSOO, and GP-UCB literature.
- Significance: Meaningful. Removing auxiliary optimization and δ-cover for exponential-rate BO is theoretically and practically relevant. The insights about when GP bounds help are useful for the community. High-dimensional applicability still relies on additional structure (e.g., REMBO), which is standard.

Questions for the authors
1. Precisely state the assumption under which f(x) ≤ μ(x|D)+ςσ(x|D) holds throughout when hyperparameters are re-estimated. Is f assumed to be a draw from the GP with the evolving hyperparameters, or in a fixed RKHS with a βt schedule? Can you provide a lemma justifying the chosen ςM with learned hyperparameters?
2. Can you report ρ̄t and Ξn empirically to relate observed convergence to the theory?
3. How sensitive is IMGPO to the initial lengthscale and to misspecified kernels? Can you show results with ARD lengthscales?
4. What is the per-iteration complexity of step (iii) in practice, and how often does Ξ hit Ξmax?

Pros for acceptance
- Novel algorithmic synthesis; removes practical obstacles; insightful theory with exponential rate.
- Strong empirical performance across diverse deterministic benchmarks.
- Clear connection to and improvement over BaMSOO and prior δ-cover results.

Cons for acceptance
- Key probabilistic assumption for UCB containment under hyperparameter learning is unclear; potential gap between theory and implementation.
- Some clarity/notation issues; limited ablations and somewhat weak acquisition-optimizer baselines.
- High-dimensional guarantees still limited without extra structure.

Suggestions and minor issues
- Fix typos and clarify λ expression; define all constants (C) and parameters (Ξ, Ξmax) upfront; streamline Algorithm 1 with clearer variable names and comments.
- Cite HOO/DOO (Bubeck et al., Munos) explicitly where appropriate.
- Add a brief discussion of the noisy case and whether IMGPO can be adapted (e.g., using GP-UCB for noisy observations).

Overall recommendation
Lean accept. The paper offers a compelling and original contribution with both theoretical and practical impact for deterministic BO, provided the authors clarify the UCB containment assumption under hyperparameter learning and improve clarity and empirical ablations.
