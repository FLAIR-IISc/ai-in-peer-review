- Proposal: IMGPO — a GP-based Bayesian optimization method combining hierarchical partitioning with GP UCB screening and an "infinite-metric" view to avoid auxiliary acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Main theoretical claim: high-probability exponential simple-regret bound rN = O(λ^{N+Ngp}) with λ<1, explicit dependence on D, ρ̄t, L, α, p, and Ngp (GP proxy uses).  
- Empirics: IMGPO typically outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on standard deterministic benchmarks, with reasonable default kernels and robustness.  

Strengths:
- Conceptual novelty: formalizes "infinite-metric" exploration and the exploration-loss ρ̄t to bridge partition-based and GP-UCB methods.  
- Theoretical contribution: first exponential-rate bound without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization (contrasts with BaMSOO and δ-cover results).  
- Algorithmic practicality: removes need for global acquisition optimization, uses hierarchical partitioning with GP-based skipping to reduce evaluations.  
- Empirical support: shows faster regret reduction on several deterministic test problems and provides open-source code.  

Weaknesses:
- Hyperparameter learning: paper does not justify validity of high-probability UCBs when kernel hyperparameters are updated adaptively (union bound/ςM issues).  
- Dependence on ρ̄t and dimension: λ→1 as D grows and rate sensitive to ρ̄t, limiting scalability and practical guarantees in high dimensions.  
- Ngp in rate: convergence stated in terms of N+Ngp (proxy uses), which may overstate performance relative to actual function evaluations.  
- Clarity/notation: Algorithm 1 and definitions (e.g., T′h+ξ, step (iii) cost, constants like C) are dense or referenced externally, reducing readability.  
- Experimental scope: lacks comparisons with stronger GP-UCB baselines/tuned acquisition optimization, ablations (Ξmax, GP screening), sensitivity to kernel misspecification, and no noisy-function results.  

Quality:
- Generally technically sound under stated assumptions, but main gap is unaddressed impact of adaptive hyperparameter estimation on UCB validity; full proofs are mostly in the supplement.  

Clarity:
- Mostly well organized with helpful 1D walkthrough, but needs clearer definitions, explicit constants, and computational complexity discussion.  

Originality:
- Novel combination of infinite-metric reasoning with GP-based screening yielding exponential-rate bound without δ-cover or acquisition optimization.  

Significance:
- Potentially significant both theoretically and practically if UCB guarantees hold under realistic hyperparameter learning; impact limited by dimensional dependence and deterministic-assumption.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel theoretical result and conceptual framework; practical algorithm with promising results and available code.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Inadequate justification for UCBs under adaptive hyperparameter updates; rate depends on Ngp and ρ̄t; clarity and complexity issues; limited empirical scope.  

Suggestions for improvement:
- Provide formal treatment or explicit assumptions ensuring valid confidence bounds with adaptive hyperparameter estimation, or include fixed-hyperparameter ablations.  
- Add time complexity analysis for step (iii) and per-iteration cost; report wall-clock times alongside regret.  
- Run ablations for Ξmax, kernel/hyperparameter choices, and separate GP screening vs pure SOO to quantify Ngp and ρ̄t effects.  
- Clarify constants and streamline Algorithm 1 pseudocode; restate referenced constants/proofs inline for readability.  
- Extend experiments to noisy settings, higher-dimensional problems, and stronger/tuned GP baselines.
