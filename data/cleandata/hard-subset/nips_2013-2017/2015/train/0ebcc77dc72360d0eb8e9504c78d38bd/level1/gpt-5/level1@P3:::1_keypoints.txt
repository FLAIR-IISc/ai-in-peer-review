- Proposal: IMGPO blends hierarchical partitioning with GP-UCB used as an admissible filter to avoid acquisition-function maximization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Conceptual novelty: optimistic search over an infinite family of semi-metrics around the optimum, using GP bounds only to prune intervals.  
- Theoretical claim: exponential simple-regret decay O(λ^{N+Ngp}) with λ<1, introducing an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt that controls depth growth.  
- Positioning: builds on DIRECT/DOO/SOO (unknown smoothness), GP-BO (EI/PI/UCB), and BaMSOO (GP+SOO integration).  
- Analysis: shows adaptivity to unknown Hölder parameters (L, α, p) and explicates GP’s influence via Ngp and average ρ̄t.  
- Practical advantage: removes the need for costly auxiliary nonconvex optimization each iteration.  
- Empirics: broad synthetic benchmarks (including REMBO 1000D), competitive or superior performance and better wall-clock time than EI/PI that require auxiliary optimization.  
- Strength: clear, appealing principle bridging GP-BO and optimistic partitioning literatures.  
- Strength: theoretical contribution gives conditions for fast (exponential) rates under deterministic observations.  
- Strength: simple, effective “lazy” use of UCBs to skip expensive evaluations.  
- Strength: REMBO+IMGPO illustration is useful for high-dimensional cases.  
- Weakness: UCB-validity assumptions are not clearly stated given empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates—Theorem 1 may not hold in practice without fixed/valid kernel assumptions.  
- Weakness: Possible typo/ambiguous expression for λ in Theorem 1; needs correction and explicit re-derivation.  
- Weakness: Exponential rate hinges on ρ̄t and dimension D; when GP-UCB is uninformative ρ̄t can grow and the bound degrades.  
- Weakness: Deterministic/noise-free setting only; unclear extension to noisy or sub-Gaussian observations.  
- Weakness: Computational cost concerns (per-iteration GP updates, UCB evaluations, O(n^3) Cholesky) need analysis and implementation details (nugget, caching, incremental updates).  
- Weakness: Double-blind violation via public code URL—needs anonymization for review.  
- Weakness: Experimental baselines omit GP-UCB; sensitivity to empirical Bayes vs fixed hyperparameters not reported.  
- Weakness: Notation, pseudo-code, and definitions (e.g., T′h+ξ, C, “Ξ updates”) are dense/unclear and have minor editorial issues.  
- Quality assessment: idea is sound and experiments supportive, but theorem confidence reduced by unstated UCB conditions and typos.  
- Clarity assessment: generally clear, but analysis and pseudo-code require tightening and a brief complexity discussion.  
- Originality: original combination of infinite-metric optimistic partitioning with GP-based pruning; the infinite-metric exploration loss is a useful analytic concept.  
- Significance: moderate-to-high if theoretical statements are corrected and assumptions made explicit; meaningful for deterministic BO and optimistic partitioning.  
- Suggestions: explicitly state UCB assumptions (fixed kernel/noise/RKHS bounds), reconcile with empirical Bayes; fix Theorem 1 and define constants; plot/report ρt, Ngp, Ξn over time; include GP-UCB baseline and fixed-hyperparameter experiments; provide per-iteration complexity and implementation tricks; discuss noisy extensions; anonymize code.  
- Pro-accept: novel, removes practical obstacles, insightful analysis, strong empirical performance.  
- Contra-accept: theoretical dependence on unstated assumptions, theorem typos/clarity issues, deterministic-only scope, double-blind violation.
