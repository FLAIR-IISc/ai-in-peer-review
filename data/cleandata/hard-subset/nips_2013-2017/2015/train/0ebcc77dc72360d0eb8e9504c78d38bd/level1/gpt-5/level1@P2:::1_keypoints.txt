- Summary: Proposes IMGPO, combining hierarchical optimistic partitioning with GP-UCB pruning to get exponential convergence for deterministic black-box optimization without δ-cover or inner acquisition optimization.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on GP-UCB, deterministic GP bandit results, optimistic partitioning (DIRECT/SOO), and BaMSOO, improving convergence rates and avoiding auxiliary optimization.  
- Theory — high level: Sensible combination; introduces “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt and proves exponential regret O(λ^{N+N_gp}) under Hölder-type local continuity.  
- Theory — typo/error: The λ expression appears mistyped (given λ = 3 − α2CDρ̄t < 1); needs correction and a clean restatement of the bound.  
- Theory — hyperparameters: UCB high-probability guarantees usually require fixed kernel/hyperparameters; re-estimating hyperparameters each iteration may invalidate coverage and must be addressed.  
- Theory — presentation: Constants (C, β, p), assumptions, and division-scheme requirements are scattered; a compact theorem with all definitions is needed for reproducibility.  
- Theory — dimension dependence: Dependence on ambient dimension D is adverse (λ → 1 as D grows); discussion of near-optimality dimension vs D would contextualize practical relevance.  
- Algorithmic soundness: Integration of GP UCB into SOO-like search is well motivated; the UCB “dummy value to avoid evaluation” is a useful practical speedup.  
- Algorithmic — complexity: Implementation details are reasonable, but an explicit per-iteration computational-cost discussion (GP updates, worst-case Step (iii) cost bounded by Ξmax) should be added.  
- Experiments — positives: Standard benchmarks, consistent empirical improvements, same kernel class across methods, code release, sensitivity to Ξmax discussed.  
- Experiments — baselines: Acquisition-optimization baselines (GP-EI/PI) were optimized with SOO/local methods; stronger baselines (multi-start L-BFGS-B, CMA-ES, DIRECT for GP-UCB) should be included.  
- Experiments — statistical reporting: Only GP-EI/PI had repeated runs; deterministic methods lack stochastic reporting; wall-clock times (Table 1 missing) and seeds for stochastic baselines should be provided.  
- Experiments — scope: Method is for deterministic objectives; experiments and abstract should explicitly state noisy objectives are out of scope.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally readable with helpful pseudocode, but notational/typographical issues (λ, mixing ρt/ρτ, index inconsistencies) need fixing and assumptions/constants should be centralized.  
- Originality: High — novel use of GP as a pruning signal within an infinite-metric framework and a new analysis achieving exponential rates without δ-cover or inner optimization.  
- Significance: Potentially significant — removes δ-cover and inner optimization while attaining exponential convergence, though deterministic scope and D-dependence limit immediate impact.  
- Pros for acceptance: New algorithmic principle, exponential regret without δ-cover, insightful analysis of ρ̄t and N_gp, solid empirical performance and released code.  
- Cons for acceptance: Theoretical clarity issues (λ typo, hyperparameter learning impact), weaker acquisition-optimization baselines, missing runtime reporting, limited to deterministic settings, and notational rough edges.  
- Suggestions for improvement: Fix theorem typos and consolidate assumptions, either fix hyperparameters in theory or justify adaptive updates, add stronger GP-UCB baselines and wall-clock times, and discuss noisy/high-D extensions.  
- Overall recommendation: Technically interesting, original, and practically relevant with clarifications needed; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
