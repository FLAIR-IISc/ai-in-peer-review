- Summary: Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm combining hierarchical partitioning with GP bounds to achieve exponential simple-regret convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition-function optimization.  
- Summary: Avoids costly acquisition maximization by using partitioning to consider an infinite family of local smoothness assumptions while using GP-UCB to prune branches.

- Strength: High significance and originality—addresses an important open problem by unifying Lipschitz-style partitioning and GP probabilistic models.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical result—Theorem 1 proves exponential convergence O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1 and introduces the "infinite-metric exploration loss" concept.  
- Strength: Improved practicality—eliminates auxiliary acquisition optimization, is deterministic (given kernel/domain), and shows faster runtime than standard GP methods.  
- Strength: Solid experiments—comparisons to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-EI/PI across varied benchmarks (including high-dimensional REMBO) show competitive or superior performance.

- Weakness: Algorithm presentation is unclear and dense; the multi-step filtering and pseudocode (Algorithm 1) lack intuitive walk-through and are hard to parse.  
- Weakness: Potential hidden computational cost—computing max UCB over fully expanded subtrees (step (iii)) may be expensive, which could undermine the "no auxiliary optimization" claim.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns—λ approaches 1 as dimension increases, so the curse of dimensionality limits effectiveness to low-to-moderate dimensions and needs more explicit discussion.  
- Weakness: Parameter sensitivity—new parameters (Ξmax and the adaptive Ξ schedule) lack thorough justification or sensitivity analysis.

- Overall assessment: Excellent, novel, and technically strong paper with practical promise; presentation, complexity analysis, scalability, and parameter studies should be improved but are addressable—strong recommend accept.
