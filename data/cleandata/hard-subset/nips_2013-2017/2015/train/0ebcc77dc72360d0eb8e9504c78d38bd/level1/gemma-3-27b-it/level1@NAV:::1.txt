## Review of “Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence”

This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization (BO) method aiming for exponential convergence without relying on auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling, both drawbacks of existing approaches like de Freitas et al. [1]. The authors address a significant limitation in the field, as δ-cover sampling is often impractical [1, 2].  They propose an “Infinite-Metric GP Optimization” (IMGPO) algorithm leveraging a hierarchical partitioning scheme and exploring a wider range of possible bounds compared to standard GP-UCB.  The core idea is to admit the GP’s bound might be loose and simultaneously explore multiple potential bounds encoded in an unknown semi-metric. 

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the problems with current BO methods and framing their approach as a solution. The authors relate their work to relevant existing literature, including Lipschitz optimization [7, 8], recent work on exponential convergence [1, 2], and bound-based search methods like DIRECT [19].  The description of the IMGPO algorithm is detailed, accompanied by a helpful illustrative figure. They provide a theoretical analysis proving an exponential convergence rate and discuss the impact of key parameters. Finally, they present experimental results on eight benchmark functions, demonstrating IMGPO’s superiority over SOO, BaMSOO, and standard GP-based methods.

**Strengths:**

* **Significant Problem Addressed:** The paper tackles a practical limitation of existing exponential convergence BO methods – the reliance on δ-cover sampling.
* **Novel Approach:**  The idea of simultaneously considering multiple bounds through infinite-metric exploration is innovative and potentially impactful.
* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:**  The authors provide a theoretical convergence guarantee and a detailed analysis.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:**  The experiments on a variety of benchmark functions demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The algorithm and its explanation are quite involved. The many parameters (Ξmax, η, etc.) might make it difficult to implement and tune.
* **Scalability Concerns:** While the authors acknowledge scalability issues, the analysis points to potential difficulties in high dimensions, even with extensions like REMBO [14].
* **Parameter Sensitivity:**  The results seem to be somewhat sensitive to the choice of Ξmax, and a more thorough discussion of its impact would be beneficial.
* **Limited Comparison:** The comparison to other GP-based methods (GP-PI and GP-EI) uses a relatively simple implementation and could be expanded to include more sophisticated approaches.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to Bayesian optimization with a proven exponential convergence rate.
* It addresses a significant practical limitation in the field.
* The experimental results are convincing.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The algorithm is complex and may be difficult to implement.
* Scalability to very high dimensions remains a concern.




**Overall:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization by presenting a novel algorithm with strong theoretical guarantees and demonstrated empirical performance. While the complexity of the method is a concern, the potential benefits outweigh this drawback. Addressing the comments regarding parameter sensitivity and scalability would further strengthen the paper.
