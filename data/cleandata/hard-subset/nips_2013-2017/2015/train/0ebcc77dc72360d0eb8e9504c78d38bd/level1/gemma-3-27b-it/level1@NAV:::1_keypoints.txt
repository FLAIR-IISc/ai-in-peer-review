- Proposes IMGPO, a novel BO method that seeks exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.
- Uses hierarchical partitioning and simultaneously explores multiple possible bounds via an unknown semi-metric.
- Well-motivated relation to prior work (Lipschitz optimization, SOO, BaMSOO, GP-UCB) and clear algorithm description with an illustrative figure.
- Provides theoretical analysis proving exponential convergence and discusses the role of key parameters.
- Empirical results on eight benchmark functions show IMGPO outperforming SOO, BaMSOO, and standard GP methods.
- Strength: Tackles a significant practical limitation of existing exponential-convergence BO methods.
- Strength: Innovative infinite-metric exploration idea that is potentially impactful.
- Strength: Strong theoretical guarantees supporting the approach.
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments demonstrating efficacy across benchmarks.
- Weakness: Algorithm and explanation are complex with many tuning parameters (Ξmax, η, etc.), making implementation/tuning harder.
- Weakness: Scalability concerns in high dimensions, with extensions like REMBO noted but potentially insufficient.
- Weakness: Sensitivity to choice of Ξmax; need more thorough discussion of its impact.
- Weakness: Comparisons to GP-PI and GP-EI use simple implementations and could be expanded to stronger baselines.
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound approach addressing a key limitation with convincing experiments.
- Against acceptance: Complexity and unresolved scalability to very high dimensions.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; address parameter sensitivity and scalability concerns.
