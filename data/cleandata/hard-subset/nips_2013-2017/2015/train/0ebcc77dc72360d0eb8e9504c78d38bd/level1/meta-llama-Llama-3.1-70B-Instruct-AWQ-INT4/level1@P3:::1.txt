**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel Bayesian optimization method, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), that achieves exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The method leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer and considers infinitely many possible candidates of the bounds. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the method, including a regret bound, and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on various objective functions.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper presents a new approach to Bayesian optimization that combines the benefits of bound-based methods and infinite-metric optimization.
2. **Theoretical analysis**: The authors provide a rigorous theoretical analysis of the method, including a regret bound, which is a significant contribution to the field.
3. **Experimental evaluation**: The paper includes a thorough experimental evaluation of the method on various objective functions, demonstrating its effectiveness.
