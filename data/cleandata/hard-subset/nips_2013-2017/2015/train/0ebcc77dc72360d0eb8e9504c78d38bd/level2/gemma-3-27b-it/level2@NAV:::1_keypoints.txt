- Proposes a novel Bayesian optimization method (IMGPO) that attains exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Leverages an unknown semi-metric bound by considering a range of possible bounds while using the GP prior to prune the search.  
- Clearly motivated by impracticality of δ-cover sampling and computational cost of auxiliary optimization.  
- Builds on prior bound-based search methods and situates contribution within BO/global optimization literature.  
- Technical description of IMGPO and its connection to GP-UCB are well explained; pseudocode is provided.  
- Strong theoretical analysis proving exponential convergence and useful discussion on the effect of GP bound tightness (Remarks 1–3).  
- Empirical evaluation on eight functions shows competitive performance versus SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, especially when GP prior is weak.  
- Division/hierarchical partitioning procedure is under-explained and would benefit from clearer visualization and detail.  
- Scalability is a concern: convergence rate approaches 1 as dimensionality increases, and this limitation is acknowledged but not resolved.  
- Limited discussion of potential mitigation strategies or additional assumptions to improve high-dimensional performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid theory, competitive empirical results, and clear motivation/positioning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear partitioning description, scalability issues, and insufficient exploration of solutions to scalability.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify partitioning procedure and further explore scalability remedies.
