- Proposes IMGPO: GP-based Bayesian optimization combining hierarchical partitioning with GP UCBs to prune candidates and defer expensive evaluations by substituting UCBs at cell centers.  
- Main theoretical claim: exponential simple-regret convergence O(λ^(N+Ngp)) under an “unknown semi-metric” continuity assumption and a Lipschitz-like condition (x,x*) ≤ L||x−x*||_p^α.  
- Removes need for auxiliary acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling used in prior exponential-rate results.  
- Identifies and analyzes the “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρ_t, with explicit dependence on dimension D and smoothness α.  
- The theoretical guarantee depends on f(x) ≤ U(x|D) holding with high probability while using empirically learned hyperparameters; conditions for this are not precisely stated.  
- The paper should justify the chosen ς_M schedule and link it to standard GP-UCB β_t constructions under hyperparameter learning and possible model misspecification.  
- Empirical results show competitive or better performance than SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on benchmarks and include ablations on Ξ_max.  
- Missing experiments: no direct comparison to multi-start GP-UCB (without δ-cover) and no noisy-function evaluation benchmarks.  
- Clarity is generally good with helpful intuition and pseudocode, but notation (ρ̄_t, Ξ) could be streamlined.  
- Runtime dependence on Ξ_max and per-iteration complexity require clearer discussion.  
- Originality: the “infinite-metric” selection with GP filtering is a novel tightening over BaMSOO, enabling exponential rates.  
- Significance: achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization is practically important for deterministic BO, though scalability is a limitation.  
- Pros: exponential convergence without δ-cover/aux optimization; clear connection to SOO/BaMSOO/DIRECT; public code and solid empirical results.  
- Cons: gap between theory and practice re: UCB validity with learned hyperparameters; lack of noisy-setting and high-dimensional experiments; missing comparison to strong GP-UCB baselines.  
- Constructive suggestions: state precise conditions guaranteeing f ≤ U under misspecification/EB hyperparameter learning; add multi-start GP-UCB and noisy benchmarks; report wall-clock, ρ_t and Ξ_n traces and include IMGPO-without-GP ablation; discuss kernel sensitivity and guidance for selecting Ξ_max.
