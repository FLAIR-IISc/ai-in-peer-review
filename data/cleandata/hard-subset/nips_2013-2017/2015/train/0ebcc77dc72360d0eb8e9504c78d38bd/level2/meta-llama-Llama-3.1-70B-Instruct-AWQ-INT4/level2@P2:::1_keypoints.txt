- Presents IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Key idea: exploit an unknown bound from continuity at the global optimizer instead of a single GP-provided bound.  
- Considers infinitely many candidate bounds and concurrently performs global and local search over these candidates.  
- Uses GP upper confidence bound to prune candidates and adaptively select intervals to divide.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including a proof of exponential convergence and discussion of UCB tightness effects.  
- Experimental results compare IMGPO to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, showing IMGPO generally outperforms competitors.  
- SOO is competitive on the Rosenbrock2 benchmark.  
- Strength: novel and efficient Bayesian optimization method with provable exponential convergence.  
- Strength: algorithm is well-motivated, clearly explained, and thoroughly analyzed.  
- Strength: convincing experimental evaluation demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Weakness: assumes deterministic objective functions, limiting practical applicability.  
- Weakness: requires specifying a GP prior, which may be hard in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear discussion of computational complexity.  
- Originality: introduces a distinct approach leveraging unknown continuity bounds, different from GP-UCB and related methods.  
- Significance: potentially widely applicable across ML, engineering design, and parameter fitting.  
- Quality: well-written, well-organized, with clear explanations and notation.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing weaknesses (computational complexity and GP prior specification).
