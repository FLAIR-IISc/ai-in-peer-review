- Proposal: IMGPO combines GP posteriors with hierarchical partitioning (SOO/DIRECT-like) to get exponential simple-regret without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- Key idea: Use an infinite family of bounds (an unknown semi-metric around the optimum) and GP-UCB as a filter to prune loose candidates.  
- Algorithm mechanics: Partition domain into hyperrectangles, evaluate at centers, use UCBs to decide evaluation vs deferral with GP-based surrogate labels.  
- Theoretical claim: Proves exponential convergence O(λ^{N+N_gp}) with λ<1 under Hölder-type local smoothness, introducing infinite-metric exploration loss ρ_t to quantify not fixing a bound.  
- Empirics: On several deterministic benchmarks (and REMBO for high-D), IMGPO typically outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI in simple regret and needs no inner optimization.  

Strengths:
- Conceptual: The infinite-metric perspective is a principled hedge against bound misspecification and makes the trade-off analyzable via ρ_t.  
- Theoretical advance: Achieves an exponential regret bound without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization, addressing an open question.  
- Practical algorithm: Evaluates only at rectangle centers, avoids nonconvex inner solves, and includes heuristics (Ξmax, dummy GP labels) to reduce evaluations.  
- Empirical support: Generally faster regret improvement than baselines across deterministic benchmarks.

Weaknesses / Clarifications needed:
- Assumptions/constants: Hölder-like assumptions and “best” constants (L, α, p) need clearer statement and practical interpretation; λ approaches 1 in higher D weakening exponential meaning.  
- Theorem typo/notation: λ is misprinted (shown as “λ = 3 − α/(2CDρ̄t) < 1”); likely intended as λ = 3^{−α/(2CDρ̄t)} and constants should be consolidated.  
- Hyperparameter learning: GP-UCB validity under empirical Bayes hyperparameter refitting is unclear; theory may only hold for fixed hyperparameters—needs clarification.  
- Computational complexity: Need tighter time and GP-cost analysis per iteration, guidance on Ξmax and η, and more ablation on Ξmax.  
- Experimental scope: Experiments are deterministic and low-D; must discuss noisy observations, how IMGPO extends to sub-Gaussian noise, and report wall-clock times and ablations (ρ̄_t, N_gp).  
- Clarity: Algorithm pseudocode and GP-based label indexing are intricate; a worked example and schematic accept/reject logic would improve readability.

Quality:
- Careful analysis connecting to SOO/DOO, BaMSOO, GP-UCB, though hyperparameter-learning caveat weakens airtightness; empirical evaluation is reasonable but could use more ablations.

Clarity:
- Generally well written but hampered by notational slips (notably λ), dense pseudocode, and lack of a consolidated constants/assumptions list and complexity section.

Originality:
- High; novel synthesis of infinite-metric approach with GP pruning and a new exponential-rate result without δ-covering or auxiliary optimization.

Significance:
- Potentially significant for BO if theoretical caveats are addressed and empirical weaknesses (dimension, noise) are clarified.

Pros for acceptance:
- Exponential regret without δ-cover or inner optimization, clear positioning vs prior work, practical algorithm with code, and generally strong empirical results.

Cons for acceptance:
- Exponential-rate interpretation weak in higher D, theorem needs correction and clearer hyperparameter treatment, limited ablations and noisy-setting discussion, and incomplete complexity characterization.

Questions for authors:
- Please correct and restate Theorem 1 with precise λ and all constants.  
- Does the probability guarantee hold with empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates; if not, provide a fixed-hyperparameter version and practical implications.  
- What is per-iteration time complexity in terms of D, depth, Ξmax, and GP kernel operations?  
- Can IMGPO be extended to noisy (sub-Gaussian) observations while retaining exponential rate?  
- Can you report ρ̄_t and N_gp over time to empirically validate the theoretical remarks?

Recommendation:
- Lean accept, conditional on clarifying theorem/λ, addressing hyperparameter-learning caveats, and adding brief complexity/ablation analyses.
