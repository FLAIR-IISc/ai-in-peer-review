- Proposes IMGPO, a practical Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence for simple regret, addressing a key open problem.  
- Avoids impractical δ-cover sampling and the hard auxiliary non-convex optimization required by standard GP methods.  
- Combines hierarchical partitioning (like SOO) with a GP prior and an "infinite-metric" search that implicitly considers many semi-metrics/smoothness levels.  
- Uses GP-UCB not to pick a single sample but to prune candidate regions from the infinite-metric search.  
- Provides a theoretical exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) (Theorem 1), improving over polynomial bounds of related work (e.g., BaMSOO).  
- Remarks/analysis clarify trade-offs, especially how GP reduces "infinite-metric exploration loss" ρ_t and why the combined approach helps.  
- Empirical results on diverse benchmarks show IMGPO is consistently among the best, without per-function kernel/hyperparameter tuning.  
- Includes a high-dimensional REMBO experiment indicating potential use as a subroutine in complex settings.  
- Well-written paper with clear motivation and helpful illustrative examples (e.g., Figure 1).  
- Pseudocode (Algorithm 1) is dense and hard to parse; selection/filtering logic and the `while true` loop need clearer step-by-step explanation or a flowchart.  
- Potentially higher per-iteration computational cost due to nested loops and UCB computations; formal complexity analysis is missing.  
- Unclear how cost of specific steps (e.g., line 22) scales with Ξmax and tree size; needs clarification.  
- Convergence rate degrades with dimensionality D (λ→1); dependence of constant C on D should be clarified to assess the curse of dimensionality.  
- New parameter Ξmax affects performance vs. computation trade-off; a sensitivity analysis or guidance for setting it would improve practicality.  
- Overall recommendation: important, novel, and strong theoretical/empirical contribution; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
