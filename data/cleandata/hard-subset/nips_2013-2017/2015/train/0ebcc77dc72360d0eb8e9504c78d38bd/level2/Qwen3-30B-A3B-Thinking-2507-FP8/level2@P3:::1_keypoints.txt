- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm using an infinite-metric formulation and hierarchical partitioning to avoid auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling.
- Encodes infinitely many possible bounds via an unknown semi-metric instead of a single GP-derived upper bound.
- Uses the GP prior to prune less promising candidate bounds while simultaneously conducting global and local search.
- Theoretical contribution: Theorem 1 proves exponential regret O(λ^(N+N_gp)) with λ < 1, improving on prior polynomial bounds (e.g., BaMSOO).
- Provides analysis (Remarks 1–3) linking GP-bound tightness to performance and explaining when IMGPO outperforms single-bound methods.
- Experimental results on eight benchmarks show consistent improvements and faster convergence compared to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.
- Strong theoretical rigor and clear connection to prior work (DIRECT, SOO), clarifying limitations of GP-UCB and removing δ-cover sampling.
- Algorithm description includes comprehensive pseudocode (Algorithm 1) and sufficient technical detail for reproduction.
- Practical perspective: demonstrates implementation without impractical δ-cover sampling while retaining exponential convergence.
- Discusses effect of GP prior quality on performance and highlights misleading cases (e.g., Rosenbrock2) showing real-world considerations.
- Weakness: dependence on dimensionality (λ → 1 as D increases) needs more discussion and mitigation strategies beyond brief mentions.
- Weakness: limited guidance on choosing Ξmax in practice; experiments show trade-offs between performance and computation but lack deeper analysis.
- Weakness: experimental comparisons could include more recent BO methods (additive structure, dimensionality reduction) to strengthen empirical claims.
- Assumption that default GP kernels/hyperparameters suffice could use more justification when prior knowledge exists.
- Writing and organization are clear, proofs are in supplementary material, though the main paper could concisely present key theoretical insights.
- Overall assessment: significant, original contribution with high significance and quality, meeting NIPS standards despite some practical considerations to address.
