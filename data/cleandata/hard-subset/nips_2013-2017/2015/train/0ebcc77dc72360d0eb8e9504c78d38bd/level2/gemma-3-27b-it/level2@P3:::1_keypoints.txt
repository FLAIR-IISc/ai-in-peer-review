- Core contribution: Proposes IMGPO, an “infinite-metric” BO method that attains exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Method novelty: Uses an unknown semi-metric to guide search, avoiding reliance on tight GP-based bounds.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on GP optimization, UCB acquisition, and connects clearly to SOO and GP-UCB.
- Theoretical strength: Provides a solid theoretical analysis proving exponential convergence under given assumptions.
- Practical promise: Initial experiments indicate improvement over existing methods in tested scenarios.
- Experimental limitations: Empirical evaluation is limited in scope and does not fully demonstrate scalability or robustness.
- Parameter concerns: Sensitivity to parameters (e.g., Ξmax) is not rigorously analyzed and kernel hyperparameters (σ, l) were fixed without strong justification.
- Assumption clarity: Key assumptions (notably Assumption 1 and the unknown semi-metric) need deeper discussion and exploration of violation scenarios.
- Computational cost: The link between ρ̃t and computational cost is not explicitly stated or analyzed.
- Presentation issues: Some sections, especially the description of Algorithm 1, could be more concise; writing can be tightened.
- Related work comparison: Discussion of related methods lacks a nuanced comparison of strengths and weaknesses versus IMGPO.
- Reproducibility detail: More detailed experimental results (including runtimes) and sensitivity analyses are needed.
- Overall recommendation: Valuable theoretical contribution with promising results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments, clarify assumptions, justify parameter choices, and tighten writing.
