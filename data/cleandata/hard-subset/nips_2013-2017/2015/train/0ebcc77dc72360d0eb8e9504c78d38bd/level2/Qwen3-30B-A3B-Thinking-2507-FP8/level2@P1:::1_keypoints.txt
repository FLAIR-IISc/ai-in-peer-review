- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that claims exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Targets two practical limitations of prior methods: the need for auxiliary non-convex optimization and impractical δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: consider "infinitely many" candidate bounds encoded by a semi-metric to enable exponential convergence.  
- Theorem 1 provides a regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, stronger than prior polynomial or δ-cover–dependent results.  
- Remarks elucidate how GP bound tightness, the role of GP, and using multiple bounds affect performance.  
- Empirical evaluation against SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI on eight test functions shows consistent improvements, especially in higher dimensions.  
- Authors acknowledge cases where the GP prior is misleading (e.g., Rosenbrock2), demonstrating appropriate self-critique.  
- Strengths: clear theoretical contribution, comprehensive experiments, and detailed algorithm description with pseudocode.  
- Strength: effectively addresses a recognized practical limitation in Bayesian optimization literature.  
- Weakness: needs clearer contrast with related work (e.g., Kandasamy et al.) that might achieve similar goals.  
- Weakness: theoretical reliance on Assumption 2 (the semi-metric) may be restrictive in practice.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of scalability as dimensionality increases and its practical impact.  
- Weakness: focuses on deterministic functions and lacks treatment or extension to noisy optimization settings.  
- Overall judgment: technically strong, well-written, significant contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
