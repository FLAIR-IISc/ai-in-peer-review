This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors propose a Gaussian process (GP) optimization method that leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer. The method, called Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), simultaneously conducts global and local searches based on all possible candidates of the bounds, using the GP prior to reduce the number of candidates.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed method, and its theoretical analysis. The introduction provides a good overview of the Bayesian optimization literature and the limitations of existing methods. The technical details of the algorithm are presented in a clear and concise manner, and the pseudocode is easy to follow.

The theoretical analysis of the method is thorough, and the authors provide a regret bound that shows the exponential convergence rate of IMGPO. The analysis is based on the infinite-metric exploration loss, which is a novel concept that measures the cost of not committing to any particular upper bound. The authors also provide remarks on the effect of the tightness of the UCB by GP, the effect of GP, and the effect of infinite-metric optimization, which provide additional insights into the behavior of the algorithm.

The experimental results show that IMGPO outperforms other algorithms, including SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, on a variety of test functions. The results are presented in a clear and concise manner, and the authors provide a discussion of the results and the implications of the findings.

Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a novel and effective Bayesian optimization method. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.
