- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Builds on hierarchical partitioning (e.g., SOO) and addresses an open problem left by BaMSOO.  
- Core idea: use GP-UCB to prune branches in a hierarchical search rather than to select next query points.  
- Practical advantage: avoids computationally expensive auxiliary optimization common in GP methods.  
- Theoretical contribution: formal proof of an exponential regret bound (Theorem 1).  
- Empirical results: consistently outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-EI/PI on diverse benchmark functions.  
- Paper is original, significant, reproducible, and code is provided.  
- Main weakness: algorithm description and pseudocode (Section 3.2) are dense and hard to parse.  
- Suggested improvements: add a high-level intuitive walkthrough of selection logic (steps i–iii).  
- Suggested discussion: clarify sensitivity to the Ξmax parameter balancing exploration and cost.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality work, minor issues only, strongly recommended for acceptance.
