- Proposes IMGPO: a partition-based Bayesian optimization that combines hierarchical partitioning with GP-UCB pruning to avoid auxiliary acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling.
- Key idea: use an implicit family of semi-metrics (infinite-metric perspective) around the global optimum to avoid committing to a single smoothness assumption.
- Algorithm: maintain a tree of hyperrectangles, select per-depth centers, prune via GP-UCB lookahead, and split along the longest coordinate into three children; limit lookahead by Ξmax and update GP hyperparameters empirically.
- Theoretically introduces “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt and proves a high-probability exponential simple-regret bound rN = O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1, where Ngp counts GP-based virtual steps.
- Bound depends on dimension D, local semi-metric constants (L, α, p), division constant C, and average exploration loss ρ̄t.
- Positions IMGPO relative to DIRECT, SOO/DOO, and BaMSOO, claiming exponential rates without δ-cover sampling unlike BaMSOO’s polynomial rates.
- Experiments: compares IMGPO to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI (and REMBO 1000D test); IMGPO generally attains lower regret faster and is faster than GP-BO with auxiliary optimization.
- SOO remains competitive on Rosenbrock when the GP prior is misleading; runtime shows IMGPO slower than SOO but substantially faster than GP-BO with auxiliary optimization.
- Technical soundness: hierarchical optimistic scheme and GP-UCB pruning are plausible and the main theorem is insightful, but two clarifications are needed.
- Concern 1: validity of the high-probability UCB guarantee under empirical Bayes/hyperparameter learning is not justified; precise probabilistic assumptions should be stated.
- Concern 2: Ngp and complexity accounting are not tied analytically to N and Ξmax; a bound relating Ngp to N (and an overall per-iteration complexity) is requested.
- Clarity issues: ambiguous notation for λ in Theorem 1, many constants should be collected in the theorem, and probabilistic assumptions behind UCB should be recapped.
- Originality: novel combination of infinite-metric perspective with GP pruning to remove δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization, plus an insightful decomposition of GP’s role via Ngp and ρ̄t.
- Significance: removing auxiliary optimization while achieving exponential convergence is a substantive advance for deterministic BO; REMBO results indicate potential in high-dimensional schemes.
- Essential suggested fixes: justify UCB guarantees under data-dependent hyperparameters or provide a fixed-kernel theorem; provide analytic bounds tying Ngp and UCB computations to N, Ξmax, D; disambiguate constants/typography.
- Nice-to-have suggestions: include DIRECT in experiments, report empirical traces of ρ̄t and Ngp/N and tests varying Ξmax, discuss noisy-setting extensions, and scalability strategies (random embeddings, additive structure).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm achieving exponential regret without δ-cover/auxiliary optimization, clear theory, and strong empirical performance with public code.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete justification for high-probability guarantees under empirical Bayes, unclear complexity/accounting of Ngp relative to evaluation budget, and limited discussion of noisy/high-dimensional extensions.
