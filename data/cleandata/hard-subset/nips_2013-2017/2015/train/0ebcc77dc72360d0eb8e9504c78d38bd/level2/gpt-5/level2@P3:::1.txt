Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization method for deterministic black-box functions that achieves an exponential simple-regret rate without (a) solving the auxiliary global optimization of acquisition functions and (b) relying on δ-cover sampling. The method combines hierarchical partitioning with GP-based optimistic bounds. A key idea is to avoid committing to a single smoothness/metric assumption by considering an implicit family of possible semi-metrics around a global optimum (Assumption 1), while using the GP UCB to prune candidates and to skip expensive function evaluations when the UCB falls below the current best. The algorithm maintains a tree of hyperrectangles, selects per-depth candidates by center values, prunes via GP-UCB lookahead, and divides selected rectangles along the longest coordinate into three children. A parameter Ξmax limits lookahead depth, and GP hyperparameters are updated empirically.

The theoretical analysis introduces the “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt to quantify the overhead from not fixing a single bound and proves a high-probability exponential regret bound rN = O(λN+Ngp), λ < 1, where N is the number of true function evaluations and Ngp counts GP-based “virtual” steps that avoided evaluation. The bound depends on dimensionality D, constants L, α, p in a local semi-metric assumption, the division constant C, and the average exploration loss ρ̄t. The paper positions IMGPO relative to DIRECT [19], SOO/DOO [18], and BaMSOO [2]. Unlike BaMSOO’s polynomial rates, IMGPO achieves exponential rates without δ-cover sampling [1].

Experiments compare IMGPO to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on several standard benchmarks (including a 1000D REMBO setting [14]), using an isotropic Matérn-5/2 kernel with empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates. IMGPO generally attains lower regret faster; SOO is competitive on Rosenbrock when the GP prior is misleading. A runtime table (not shown here) indicates IMGPO is substantially faster than standard GP-BO with auxiliary optimization, though slower than SOO.

Quality:
- Technical soundness: The hierarchical optimistic scheme is well-specified, and the pruning rule using GP-UCB is plausible. The core theorem provides a clear exponential rate under Assumptions 1–2, with insightful remarks on how GP helps via Ngp and reduced ρ̄t. However, two aspects would benefit from clarification to strengthen soundness:
  1) Confidence bound validity with learned hyperparameters. The paper sets ςM so that f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with probability ≥ 1−η across all UCB calls, following the style of GP-UCB [15]. When kernel hyperparameters are updated from data (empirical Bayes), the standard high-probability guarantee typically requires additional assumptions (e.g., RKHS norm bounds with fixed kernel or priors). Stating the precise assumptions under which the high-probability event is measured (fixed prior vs. data-dependent hyperparameters) would make the guarantee crisper.
  2) Complexity and accounting for Ngp. The bound uses N+Ngp in the exponent. Since evaluation cost is dominated by N, it would be helpful to relate Ngp to N and Ξmax analytically (e.g., Ngp ≤ O(ρ̄t Ξmax N) or similar), and to present a regret bound purely in terms of N and algorithm parameters. The worst-case cost of step (iii) (3Ξmax UCB calls per candidate) is mentioned; providing an overall per-iteration complexity bound would improve completeness.
- Empirics: The experimental setup is sensible and avoids hand-tuning kernels per function, which is fair across GP-based baselines. Including DIRECT [19] would further connect with the main narrative. Results are consistent with the claimed advantages; robustness across kernels and ablations (e.g., varying Ξmax and plotting Ngp/N and ρ̄t empirically) would strengthen the empirical story.

Clarity:
The paper is generally clear and well-organized, with a helpful figure and pseudocode. Some notation and typography should be tightened:
- Precisely define λ in Theorem 1 (exponent formatting is ambiguous in the draft).
- Collect all constants (C, β, ρ̄t, Ξn) in a succinct theorem statement, with pointers to assumptions.
- Briefly recap the probabilistic assumptions behind the UCB validity, especially given empirical Bayes.
- A short complexity subsection would aid clarity.

Originality:
The combination of an infinite-metric perspective with GP-based pruning to remove δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization, together with an exponential regret guarantee, appears novel relative to DIRECT, SOO/DOO, and BaMSOO. The analysis that decomposes the effect of GP via Ngp and ρ̄t is an insightful contribution.

Significance:
For deterministic BO, eliminating auxiliary optimization while attaining exponential convergence is a substantive advance. The framework clarifies how even non-tight GP bounds can provide value when combined with partition-based optimism. The dimensional dependence remains a limitation (acknowledged), but the REMBO experiment suggests utility as a subroutine in high-dimensional methods.

Suggestions for improvement:
Essential:
- State and justify the high-probability UCB guarantee under data-dependent hyperparameter learning, or provide a version of the theorem under fixed hyperparameters (with a discussion of the empirical Bayes variant).
- Provide bounds tying Ngp and the per-iteration UCB computations to N, Ξmax, and D, and add a regret bound expressed solely in terms of N and algorithm parameters.
- Disambiguate constants in Theorem 1 and fix minor typographical issues (e.g., the expression for λ).

Nice-to-have:
- Include DIRECT in the empirical comparison and/or a brief runtime/accuracy comparison to a strong GP-UCB optimization backend (e.g., multi-start L-BFGS).
- Report empirical traces of ρ̄t, Ngp/N, and the effect of Ξmax beyond 22 on both performance and wall-clock time.
- Discuss extensions to noisy settings and whether the analysis can be adapted (e.g., via confidence inflation or noise-aware UCB).
- Add a brief discussion of scalability strategies (random embeddings, additive structure [20]) with IMGPO as the core subroutine.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel algorithmic idea yielding exponential regret without δ-cover and without auxiliary optimization.
- Clear theoretical contribution with insightful decomposition of GP’s role.
- Strong empirical performance across diverse benchmarks; public code.

Arguments against acceptance:
- High-probability guarantees under empirical Bayes are not fully justified; assumptions should be explicit.
- Complexity and the role of Ngp in the bound could be better connected to the evaluation budget.
- Limited discussion of noisy objectives and high-dimensional scalability (beyond a REMBO illustration).

Overall, the work is a solid and timely contribution to deterministic Bayesian optimization, with clear novelty and useful analysis. With clarifications on the probabilistic assumptions and complexity, the paper would be a strong addition to the NIPS program.
