- Introduces IMGPO, a new Bayesian optimization algorithm claiming exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Main contribution: theoretical framework that uses infinitely many possible bounds to enable exponential convergence and remove prior practical barriers.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: Theorem 1 proves exponential rate O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ<1, and Remarks 1–3 justify why multiple bounds outperform single‑bound methods.  
- Experimental evaluation on eight benchmark functions shows consistent improvements over SOO, BaMSOO, GP‑PI, and GP‑EI, with IMGPO faster and achieving lower regret.  
- Demonstrates solid knowledge of the literature and correctly addresses limitations of prior approaches (e.g., impracticality of δ‑cover sampling).  
- Novelty is well justified: IMGPO bridges single‑bound methods (like GP‑UCB) and multiple‑bound methods (like DIRECT and SOO).  
- Limitation: convergence degrades as dimensionality increases (λ→1 as D grows), indicating scalability challenges.  
- Limitation: experiments do not provide adequate guidance on selecting the Ξmax parameter, despite its strong impact on performance.  
- Limitation: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and the trade‑off between Ξmax and runtime.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add guidance on Ξmax selection, computational trade‑offs, and clarification of scalability to higher dimensions.
