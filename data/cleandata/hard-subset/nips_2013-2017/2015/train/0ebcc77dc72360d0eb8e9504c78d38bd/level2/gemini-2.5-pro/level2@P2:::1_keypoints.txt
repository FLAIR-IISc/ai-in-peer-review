- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm that attains an exponential simple-regret convergence rate while avoiding auxiliary acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- IMGPO uses hierarchical partitioning and treats an infinite set of smoothness assumptions, integrating a GP by using GP-UCB as a pruning mechanism rather than for acquisition maximization.  
- Theoretical analysis yields an O(λ^(N+Ngp)) regret bound, improving over polynomial rates of prior methods like BaMSOO.  
- Empirical results show IMGPO outperforms several state-of-the-art methods on benchmarks and is computationally faster per iteration.  
- Strong theoretical contribution solving a stated open problem and providing thorough analysis connecting to existing frameworks.  
- Novel, well-motivated algorithm that combines partitioning robustness with GP-based pruning in a clever way.  
- Practical advantage: removes costly auxiliary non-convex optimization at each step, reducing per-iteration computation.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and situates contributions relative to prior work.  
- Main paper lacks an intuitive explanation of why the GP-UCB pruning step yields exponential rate—supplement proof is technical.  
- Scalability concern: regret constant λ approaches 1 as dimensionality increases; REMBO experiment is only a proof-of-concept for higher dimensions.  
- Parameter sensitivity issue: new parameter Ξ_max fixed to 22 with heuristic update rule; needs sensitivity analysis and justification.  
- Overall: excellent, high-quality paper with strong theoretical and practical contributions; weaknesses are minor and addressable.
