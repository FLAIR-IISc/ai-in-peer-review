- Presents a BO method with exponential convergence that removes the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Leverages an unknown semi-metric to encode potential tightness of bounds, broadening modeled function behaviors.  
- Uses a hierarchical partitioning scheme to enable efficient exploration.  
- Well-structured paper with clear motivation, method description, and experimental results.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Theoretical analysis is dense but provides essential foundations for convergence claims.  
- Empirical validation across diverse test functions (including a 1000-D problem) shows strong performance vs. baselines.  
- Public code availability improves reproducibility and impact.  
- Practical relevance: removing auxiliary optimization makes the method more applicable in real-world settings.  
- Scalability concerns: performance may degrade with increasing dimensionality; proposed remedies relying on extra assumptions are not fully detailed.  
- Parameter sensitivity: results depend on hyperparameters like Ξmax; more robust tuning guidance is needed.  
- Limited comparisons to the latest BO advances (e.g., deep GPs, advanced acquisition functions).  
- Assumption 1 (existence of an unknown semi-metric) is strong and its practical implications are insufficiently discussed.  
- Theoretical exposition could be made more accessible with intuitive explanations of key assumptions and implications.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with compelling theory and experiments; recommend acceptance provided scalability, comparison, and clarity on assumptions are addressed.
