## Review of "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence"

This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization (BO) method achieving exponential convergence without relying on auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling, both limitations of existing approaches like de Freitas et al. [1]. The authors address a significant challenge in BO – the impracticality of δ-cover sampling – and propose an “Infinite-Metric GP Optimization” (IMGPO) algorithm. The core idea revolves around leveraging an unknown semi-metric encoding a tighter bound on the objective function, combined with a hierarchical partitioning strategy guided by the GP posterior.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the drawbacks of current BO methods and positioning IMGPO as a solution. The authors effectively connect their work to related areas like bound-based search methods (A*, PAC-MDP) and highlight the benefits of considering a broader range of potential bounds. The technical description of IMGPO is detailed, and the inclusion of pseudocode (Algorithm 1) is helpful. The theoretical analysis, culminating in Theorem 1, provides a formal guarantee of exponential convergence, a key contribution. 

A strength of this work lies in its theoretical grounding and the attempt to provide a comprehensive analysis, including discussions on the impact of the GP prior and the choice of division procedures. The experimental evaluation, while limited in scope, demonstrates IMGPO’s competitive performance across several benchmark functions, outperforming other methods in most cases. The authors acknowledge limitations, such as the potential scalability issues in high dimensions and the sensitivity to the choice of Ξmax.

However, the paper suffers from some weaknesses. The experimental section feels somewhat cursory. While IMGPO performs well, a more thorough comparison with state-of-the-art BO algorithms (e.g., those utilizing recent advancements in acquisition functions or kernel learning) would strengthen the claims. The discussion of the experimental setup is brief, and the justification for the chosen hyperparameters is weak. Furthermore, the connection between the theoretical analysis and the practical implementation could be made more explicit.  The notation is dense and can be challenging to follow without careful reading.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant practical limitation of existing BO methods.
*   Provides a theoretical guarantee of exponential convergence.
*   Demonstrates competitive performance on benchmark functions.
*   Presents a novel and well-motivated approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks a comprehensive comparison.
*   Theoretical analysis is complex and could benefit from further clarification.
*   Notation is dense and potentially hinders readability.



**Overall:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. While the experimental evaluation could be more robust, the theoretical novelty and potential impact of IMGPO warrant acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting a more detailed experimental section and a clearer explanation of the connection between theory and practice.
