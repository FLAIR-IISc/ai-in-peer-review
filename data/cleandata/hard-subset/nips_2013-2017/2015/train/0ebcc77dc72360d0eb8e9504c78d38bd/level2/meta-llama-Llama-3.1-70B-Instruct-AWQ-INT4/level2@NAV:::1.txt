This paper proposes a novel Bayesian optimization method, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), which achieves exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors claim that their approach eliminates the impractical requirements of existing Bayesian optimization methods with exponential convergence.

The main contribution of this paper is the introduction of a new algorithm that leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer. The algorithm simultaneously conducts global and local searches based on all possible candidates of the bounds, using the bound estimated by GP to reduce the number of candidates. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the algorithm, proving an exponential convergence rate and discussing the benefits of considering infinitely many possible bounds.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and its advantages. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The algorithm is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of its advantages over existing methods.
* The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of the benefits of considering infinitely many possible bounds.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a certain level of familiarity with Bayesian optimization and Gaussian processes, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The algorithm may be computationally expensive, especially for high-dimensional problems.
* The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods.

Suggestions for improvement:

* Provide a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods.
* Discuss the computational complexity of the algorithm and potential ways to improve its efficiency.
* Consider providing more experimental results, including comparisons with other methods on a variety of problems.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization, and the proposed algorithm has the potential to be widely adopted. With some minor improvements, the paper could be even stronger.
