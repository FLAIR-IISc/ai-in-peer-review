- Introduces IMGPO: combines hierarchical optimistic partitioning with GP-UCB pruning, claiming exponential simple-regret without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- Assumes an unknown semi-metric upper-bounding suboptimality (Assumption 1) and a Hölder-type global domination (Assumption 2).  
- Algorithm explores a hierarchy of hyperrectangles, maintains multiple candidates per depth ("infinite-metric"), and uses GP-UCB to skip evaluations and prune subtrees.  
- Theoretical result: a regret bound r_N = O(λ^{·} + N_gp) with λ < 1 depending on constants, dimension D, and an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρ̄_t.  
- Empirically outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI on deterministic benchmarks and is competitive in REMBO for 1000-D embeddings.  
- Positions itself as a bridge between DIRECT/SOO/DOO and GP-UCB/BaMSOO, leveraging GP bounds to reduce exploration overhead.  
- Strength: novel decoupling of correctness (unknown local bound) from tightness (GP-UCB pruning), combining optimistic partitioning and probabilistic pruning.  
- Strength: significant theoretical claim of exponential convergence under deterministic observations without δ-cover sampling.  
- Strength: practical relevance—avoids auxiliary acquisition optimization, uses standard kernels and EB updates, code released.  
- Major weakness: pruning/theory depends on UCB validity while adapting GP hyperparameters via empirical Bayes, which can invalidate uniform UCB guarantees in practice.  
- Weakness: main theorem notation and presentation are unclear (ambiguous λ, mixing of N, N_gp, t, ρ̄_t, and Ξ) and hard to interpret without supplement.  
- Weakness: Assumption 2 enforces global Hölder domination, stronger than local smoothness assumptions and narrower than SOO’s near-optimality framework.  
- Weakness: experimental baselines and acquisition optimizers are potentially suboptimal (no GP-UCB baseline, unusual optimizer for PI/EI), with limited sensitivity analysis and no ablations (e.g., without GP pruning or fixed hyperparameters).  
- Weakness: scalability concern—bound degrades with dimension D and high-D results rely on REMBO without broader study.  
- Quality concern: core idea is sound but proofs hinge on uniform UCB under hyperparameter adaptation; needs explicit assumptions or methods ensuring theoretical validity.  
- Clarity issues: typos and inconsistent notation (N vs n vs t), unclear definitions/use of ρ̄_t and Ξ, and messy pseudocode references.  
- Originality: the “infinite-metric” framing and using GP to reduce exploration while achieving exponential regret appear novel.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for deterministic BO if UCB/hyperparameter issues are resolved and experiments strengthened.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel unification of ideas, theoretical advance (no δ-cover), practical algorithm with code and promising empirical results.  
- Cons against acceptance: theoretical guarantee relies on questionable UCB assumptions under EB, unclear theorem/notation, weak baselines and missing ablations, and restrictive assumptions affecting applicability.  
- Main suggestions: state/ensure UCB validity under hyperparameter learning (or fix priors), correct and unify theorem notation and provide a proof sketch, add ablations and stronger baselines (GP-UCB, DIRECT), measure ρ̄_t and N_gp, test kernel/hyperparameter sensitivity, report runtimes, and fix typos and pseudocode.  
- Overall: promising and creative but borderline — would be strong with clarified theory on UCB validity and substantially stronger experiments.
