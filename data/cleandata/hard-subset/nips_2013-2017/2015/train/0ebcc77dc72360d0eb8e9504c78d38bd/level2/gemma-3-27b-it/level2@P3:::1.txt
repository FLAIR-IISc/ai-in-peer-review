This paper presents a Bayesian optimization (BO) method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling, addressing limitations of prior work by de Freitas et al. [1] and Wang et al. [2]. The core idea is an “infinite-metric” approach, leveraging an unknown semi-metric to guide search and mitigating the need for a tight GP-based bound. The work builds on the foundation of GP optimization, UCB acquisition, and bound-based search methods.

The strengths of this work lie in its theoretical contribution and potential practical impact. Achieving exponential convergence without the restrictive requirements of δ-cover sampling is a significant advancement. The motivation is clearly articulated, and the problem statement is well-defined. The introduction provides sufficient background and effectively highlights the gap in existing literature. The proposed algorithm, IMGPO, is clearly described, and the connection to existing methods like SOO and GP-UCB is well-established. The inclusion of a theoretical analysis, demonstrating the exponential convergence rate, strengthens the paper considerably. The experiments, although limited in scope, provide initial evidence of the algorithm's effectiveness compared to other established methods. The discussion section effectively contextualizes the findings and outlines potential future research directions. The thorough referencing is also commendable.

However, several areas could benefit from improvement. While the theoretical analysis is present, the assumptions (particularly Assumption 1) could be discussed in more detail. The practical implications of an unknown semi-metric and how well the algorithm performs when this assumption is violated are not thoroughly explored. The experimental evaluation feels somewhat limited. The choice of functions, while standard, doesn’t fully demonstrate the algorithm’s scalability or robustness. More detailed results, including computational time for each algorithm, would be beneficial. The sensitivity of the performance to the parameters (e.g., Ξmax) should be examined more rigorously. The parameter setting for the Gaussian kernel (σ and l) being fixed across all experiments is a potential concern. While the authors acknowledge this, a brief justification or investigation of its impact would be worthwhile. The writing, while generally clear, could be more concise in sections detailing the algorithm. For instance, the description of Algorithm 1 could be streamlined without sacrificing clarity. The connection between ρ̃t and computational cost isn't explicitly stated and could be clarified. The discussion of related work could also be more nuanced; while existing methods are mentioned, a deeper comparison of their strengths and weaknesses relative to IMGPO would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel theoretical result: achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.
*   Well-motivated problem and clear presentation of the proposed solution.
*   Solid theoretical foundation and analysis.
*   Demonstrated improvement over existing methods in the tested scenarios.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited experimental evaluation and lack of detailed performance analysis.
*   The assumptions underlying the theoretical analysis require further discussion.
*   The writing could be more concise in some sections.
*   The justification for parameter settings in the experiments is limited.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The theoretical results are significant, and the proposed algorithm addresses a genuine limitation of existing methods. While the experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, the initial results are promising. The weaknesses identified are addressable through revisions. Therefore, a recommendation for acceptance with minor revisions is warranted. Specifically, the authors should expand the experimental evaluation, provide a more thorough discussion of the underlying assumptions, and refine the writing for improved clarity and conciseness.
