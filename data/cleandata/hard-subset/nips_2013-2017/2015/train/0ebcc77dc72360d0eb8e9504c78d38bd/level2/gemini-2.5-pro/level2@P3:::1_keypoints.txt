- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential simple-regret convergence without auxiliary acquisition optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Core idea: combine hierarchical partitioning with GP uncertainty by using GP-UCB to prune search-tree branches rather than directly optimize an acquisition function.
- Significance: solves a known open problem by providing strong theoretical guarantees with practical efficiency gains.
- Originality: novel integration of partitioning methods and GP-UCB, using UCB for pruning instead of maximization.
- Theory: Theorem 1 proves an exponential regret bound; remarks explain robustness when GP bounds are uninformative and benefits of the infinite-metric approach.
- Experiments: thorough empirical comparison against strong baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-EI/PI), showing superior sample efficiency and runtime benefits; includes REMBO embedding demonstration.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, motivated, and logically structured with good intuition before technical details.
- Weakness — algorithmic complexity: selection/expansion logic (nested loops and z(h,i*h)) is intricate and needs clearer explanation and complexity analysis.
- Weakness — scalability with dimension: dependence on input dimension (λ→1) is concerning; needs explicit discussion of how constants and exploration loss scale with D.
- Weakness — parameter sensitivity: hyperparameter Ξmax and its update rule are heuristic; include sensitivity/ablation analysis and justification.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, significant work likely to be influential and a strong candidate for acceptance.
