This paper presents a Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence, eliminating the need for auxiliary optimization and the impractical δ-cover sampling required by de Freitas et al. [1]. The authors propose an Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO) algorithm, leveraging an unknown semi-metric to achieve this result. The core idea is to simultaneously search based on all possible bounds, reducing the search space based on the GP-estimated bound.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the limitations of existing Bayesian optimization methods, particularly the reliance on auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The problem statement is well-defined, and the authors effectively position their work within the broader context of global optimization and Bayesian optimization. The introduction provides a good overview of related work, citing relevant papers in the field [1, 2, 3, 7, 8, 13]. The technical description of the algorithm is reasonably clear, although some sections could benefit from further elaboration (see weaknesses). The experiments demonstrate promising results, showcasing IMGPO’s performance on several benchmark functions and comparisons to existing methods like SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI [2, 18].

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The proposed approach eliminates the δ-cover sampling requirement, addressing a significant practical limitation of previous work [1, 2].  Achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization is a substantial contribution.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a theoretical analysis of the algorithm, proving an exponential convergence rate under reasonable assumptions. The detailed discussion relating the convergence rate to parameters like L, α, and p, and the sensitivity analysis (Remarks 1-3) is valuable.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate that IMGPO generally outperforms other algorithms on a range of benchmark functions. The inclusion of high-dimensional test cases (1000 dimensions) is a positive aspect.
* **Clear Motivation and Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of existing methods and motivates the need for an algorithm like IMGPO.
* **Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it relatively easy to follow the main ideas.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity of Algorithm Description:** While the high-level idea is clear, the description of the algorithm (Section 3.2) is somewhat dense and could be improved with more illustrative examples or pseudocode. The iterative steps (i)-(v) are hard to follow without a concrete walkthrough.  The role of Ξmax isn't entirely clear, and its impact is only briefly discussed.
* **Limited Scalability Discussion:** While the authors acknowledge the potential scalability issues with increasing dimensions (related to λ approaching one), the discussion is limited. The use of REMBO for the 1000-dimensional case is mentioned, but a more in-depth analysis of the scalability limitations and potential mitigation strategies would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The experimental setup assumes a "blind" initialization of hyperparameters. A sensitivity analysis of the algorithm's performance to different hyperparameter settings would strengthen the empirical evaluation.  While the authors mention updating hyperparameters using an empirical Bayesian method, the specific details aren’t provided.
* **Comparison to Strong Baselines:**  The comparison to GP-PI and GP-EI uses standard implementations.  However, acknowledging how these methods might be further tuned (beyond the described hyperparameter update) for even stronger performance could give more context to the results.
* **Missing Related Work:** While the paper cites de Freitas et al. [1] and Wang et al. [2], it would be valuable to discuss recent advances in Bayesian optimization, especially those related to handling high-dimensional problems and non-stationary functions. Some work on variance reduction techniques or alternative acquisition functions might be relevant.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant challenge in Bayesian optimization: achieving exponential convergence without the limitations of δ-cover sampling.
* The proposed algorithm (IMGPO) is novel and demonstrates promising empirical performance on a variety of benchmark functions.
* The theoretical analysis provides a strong foundation for the algorithm’s performance guarantees.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presents the main ideas and contributions.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The algorithm description could be clearer and more accessible.
* The scalability discussion is limited, and a more in-depth analysis of potential limitations would be beneficial.
* The hyperparameter sensitivity analysis is lacking.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. While there are some areas for improvement, the novelty, theoretical soundness, and empirical results justify acceptance. The elimination of the δ-cover sampling requirement is a particularly valuable contribution, making this algorithm more practical for real-world applications. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the clarity of the algorithm description and expanding the discussion on scalability and hyperparameter sensitivity.  I suggest the authors consider adding a more illustrative example of the algorithm's execution (perhaps with a simplified 1D function) to aid understanding.
