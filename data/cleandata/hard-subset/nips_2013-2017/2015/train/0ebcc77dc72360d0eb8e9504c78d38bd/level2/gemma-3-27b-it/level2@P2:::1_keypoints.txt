- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Uses an Infinite-Metric GP framework with an unknown semi-metric and searches across all possible bounds via GP-estimated bounds.  
- Well-motivated problem statement that clearly positions the work within global and Bayesian optimization literature.  
- Technical formulation is sound and generally clear, though some sections need more elaboration.  
- Provides a theoretical analysis proving exponential convergence and sensitivity relations to parameters (L, α, p).  
- Empirical results show IMGPO generally outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on benchmark functions.  
- Includes a 1000-dimensional test case (using REMBO), demonstrating scalability efforts.  
- Novelty: removes the impractical δ-cover requirement and achieves exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with detailed discussion and remarks on convergence behavior.  
- Good empirical validation across multiple benchmarks and baselines.  
- Clear motivation and well-written, organized presentation.  
- Algorithm description is dense and could benefit from pseudocode, examples, and a concrete walkthrough.  
- The role and impact of Ξmax are not clearly explained.  
- Limited discussion and analysis of scalability as dimensionality increases; REMBO mention insufficiently analyzed.  
- Lacks a hyperparameter sensitivity study and detailed description of the empirical Bayesian hyperparameter updates.  
- Baseline comparisons use standard implementations and might be improved with stronger tuning for fairness.  
- Missing discussion of recent related work on high-dimensional and non-stationary Bayesian optimization and alternative techniques.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant challenge, novel algorithm, strong theory, promising empirical performance, and clear writing.  
- Arguments for rejection: unclear algorithm description, limited scalability analysis, and missing hyperparameter sensitivity evaluation.  
- Overall: significant contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to clarify algorithm details, expand scalability/hyperparameter discussion, and add an illustrative example.
