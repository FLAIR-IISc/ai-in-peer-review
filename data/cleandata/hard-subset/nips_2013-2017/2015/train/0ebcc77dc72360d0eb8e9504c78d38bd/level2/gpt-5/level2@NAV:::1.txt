This paper proposes IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm that attains exponential simple-regret convergence without auxiliary acquisition optimization and without δ-cover sampling. The method combines hierarchical partitioning (as in DIRECT/SOO) with GP-derived UCBs to prune candidates and to defer expensive evaluations by temporarily substituting UCB values at cell centers. The main claims are: (1) an exponential convergence bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) under an “unknown semi-metric” continuity assumption at the optimizer and a Lipschitz-like condition `(x,x*) ≤ L||x−x*||_p^α, and (2) practical performance competitive with or better than SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on standard benchmarks, while removing auxiliary optimization. The work is well-situated relative to GP-UCB (Srinivas et al.), the δ-cover exponential result (de Freitas et al.), and optimistic hierarchical schemes SOO/DOO and their GP hybrid BaMSOO.

Quality: The theoretical result is interesting and, if correct, advances the state of the art beyond BaMSOO’s polynomial rate. The role of the “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρ_t is clearly identified, and the dependence on D and α is explicit. However, the guarantee relies on f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with high probability while using empirically learned hyperparameters; the conditions under which this inequality holds (model correctness, kernel class, hyperparameter estimation) should be stated precisely, and the linkage to the chosen ς_M schedule justified against standard GP-UCB β_t constructions. Experiments are sensible and include ablations on Ξ_max, but a direct comparison to multi-start GP-UCB (without δ-cover) is missing, and there is no noisy-function evaluation.

Clarity: The algorithm is described with helpful intuition and pseudocode; some notation (ρ̄_t, Ξ) could be streamlined. The dependence of runtime on Ξ_max and the per-iteration complexity merits a clearer discussion.

Originality: The idea of “infinite-metric” selection combined with GP filtering is a novel tightening over BaMSOO, yielding an exponential rate.

Significance: Removing auxiliary optimization while achieving exponential convergence is important for BO practice in deterministic settings. Scalability remains a limitation, acknowledged by the authors.

Pros for acceptance:
- Exponential convergence without δ-cover or auxiliary optimization
- Clear connection and improvement over SOO/BaMSOO/DIRECT
- Public code and solid empirical results

Cons for acceptance:
- Assumption-to-practice gap around UCB validity with learned hyperparameters
- Noisy settings and higher-D scalability not addressed experimentally
- Missing comparison to strong GP-UCB baselines with pragmatic optimization

Constructive suggestions:
- State precise conditions ensuring f ≤ U under misspecification/EB hyperparameter learning
- Add multi-start GP-UCB baselines and a noisy benchmark
- Report wall-clock and ρ_t, Ξ_n traces; include IMGPO without GP as an ablation
- Discuss kernel sensitivity and guidance for Ξ_max selection
