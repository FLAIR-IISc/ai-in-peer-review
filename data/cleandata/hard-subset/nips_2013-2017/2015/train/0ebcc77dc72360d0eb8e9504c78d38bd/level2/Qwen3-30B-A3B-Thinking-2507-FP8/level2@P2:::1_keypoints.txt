- Proposes IMGPO, claiming exponential convergence for Bayesian optimization without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: provides an exponential regret bound without δ-cover sampling and introduces the novel idea of considering infinitely many bounds.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis (Section 4) examining parameter effects on regret.  
- Empirical results show IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on most benchmarks, except Rosenbrock2 due to a misleading GP prior.  
- Major weakness: convergence rate λ = 3^(-α/(2CDρ̄t)) approaches 1 as dimension D increases, indicating poor scalability to high-dimensional problems.  
- Computational complexity is insufficiently addressed; the infinite-metric exploration loss ρt could be large and make the algorithm slow, with no concrete runtime comparisons.  
- The claim of removing δ-cover sampling is not fully substantiated; it’s unclear how the algorithm avoids the underlying requirements that motivated δ-cover sampling.  
- Experiments lack parameter sensitivity analysis, especially for Ξmax (only Ξmax = 22 reported) and its effect across functions.  
- Theoretical reliance on Assumption 1 (Unknown Bound) is vague, with no guidance on verification or practical prevalence.  
- Overall: solid theoretical advance but practical impact limited by scalability and complexity concerns.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to address dimensionality, runtime comparisons, explanation of δ-cover avoidance, and Ξmax sensitivity.
