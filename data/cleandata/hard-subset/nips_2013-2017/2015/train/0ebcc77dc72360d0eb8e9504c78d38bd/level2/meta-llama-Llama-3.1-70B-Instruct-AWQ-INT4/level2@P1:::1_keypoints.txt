- Proposes IMGPO, a novel GP-based Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Leverages an unknown bound from continuity at the global optimizer and conducts simultaneous global and local searches over all bound candidates.  
- Uses the GP prior to reduce the number of candidate bounds considered.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear literature overview, problem statement, algorithm description, and easy-to-follow pseudocode.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, providing a regret bound that demonstrates exponential convergence.  
- Introduces the infinite-metric exploration loss as a novel analysis tool and discusses effects of UCB tightness, GP, and infinite-metric optimization.  
- Experimental results show IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI across several test functions.  
- Strengths: methodological novelty, strong theoretical guarantees, and convincing empirical performance.  
- Weaknesses: assumes deterministic objective functions, depends on a GP prior (may be inappropriate for some tasks), and may have high computational cost for large-scale problems.  
- Originality: introduces a new optimization approach and the infinite-metric exploration loss concept.  
- Significance: meaningful contribution to Bayesian optimization with potential applications in ML, engineering design, and biological model fitting.  
- Overall quality and clarity are high; reviewer rates the paper 9/10.
