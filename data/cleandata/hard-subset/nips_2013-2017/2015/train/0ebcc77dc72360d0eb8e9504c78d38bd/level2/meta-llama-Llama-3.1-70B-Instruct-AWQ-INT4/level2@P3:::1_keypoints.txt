- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling by considering infinitely many possible bounds via continuity at the global optimizer.

- Tackles global optimization of non-convex black-box functions using GP-based hierarchical partitioning and UCB-guided selection over hyperintervals.

- Relates to prior work: GP-UCB (requires auxiliary optimization), BaMSOO (combines SOO+GP-UCB but only polynomial regret), and DIRECT (considers many bounds but only ensures consistency).

- Strength: Theoretical proof of exponential convergence rate, a significant advancement over existing methods.

- Strength: Eliminates the need for auxiliary optimization.

- Strength: Removes requirement for δ-cover sampling, improving practicality.

- Strength: Provides a practical analysis framework explaining why avoiding reliance on a single bound is beneficial and how non-tight bounds can still work.

- Weakness: Computationally expensive due to computing UCBs across many intervals.

- Weakness: Requires tuning hyperparameters (e.g., Ξmax, η), which may be difficult in practice.

- Weakness: Potential scalability issues in high dimensions as the number of intervals grows exponentially.

- Originality: Novel combination of hierarchical partitioning and simultaneous consideration of infinitely many bounds.

- Significance: Offers a broadly useful new approach to Bayesian optimization with practical applications and improved convergence behavior.

- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear descriptions, pseudocode, theoretical analysis, and experiments.

- Quality: High-quality work with thorough theoretical and experimental support.

- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.
