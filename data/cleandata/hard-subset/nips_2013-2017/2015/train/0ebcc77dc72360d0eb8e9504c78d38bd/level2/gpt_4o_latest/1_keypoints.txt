- Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm that removes δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization while achieving exponential convergence.  
- Leverages unknown semi-metric bounds by considering all possible bounds to balance global and local search.  
- Provides strong theoretical analysis with exponential regret bound O(λ^{N+N_gp}) improving on polynomial-regret methods like BaMSOO.  
- Claims computational efficiency relative to traditional GP methods and supplies public code for reproducibility.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical superiority over SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI across benchmark functions, robust to limited hyperparameter tuning.  
- Presents clear algorithm description and well-explained pseudocode.  
- Scalability to high-dimensional problems is not thoroughly addressed; suggested remedies (e.g., REMBO) are only briefly discussed.  
- Performance may depend on initial GP kernel and hyperparameters despite empirical Bayesian updates; this sensitivity is underexplored.  
- Limited discussion of potential trade-offs, such as overhead from considering infinite bounds or effects of suboptimal GP priors.  
- Pro arguments: removes impractical assumptions, maintains exponential convergence, and supports claims with rigorous proofs and strong experiments.  
- Con arguments: high-dimensional scalability concerns and reliance on GP priors could limit practical applicability in some settings.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—significant theoretical and practical contribution with minor areas for improvement.
