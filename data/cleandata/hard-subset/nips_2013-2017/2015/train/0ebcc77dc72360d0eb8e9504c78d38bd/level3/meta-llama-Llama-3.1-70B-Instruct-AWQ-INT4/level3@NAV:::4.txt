This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), which achieves exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm, including a proof of its exponential convergence rate and a discussion of its advantages over existing methods.

The paper is well-written and didactic, making it easy to follow for readers familiar with the topic. The use of graphs and figures effectively illustrates the procedures and helps to clarify the concepts. The authors also provide a clear and concise explanation of the theoretical and experimental results, which convincingly demonstrate the advantages of their approach.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to bridge the gap between two different approaches to global optimization: bound-based methods and methods that do not rely on a particular bound. The authors show that their approach, which combines elements of both, can lead to improved performance and a more robust optimization method.

The experimental results presented in the paper are impressive, with IMGPO outperforming other state-of-the-art algorithms on a range of test functions. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of their method, which is an important consideration in practice.

If I were to offer any criticisms, it would be that the paper assumes a certain level of familiarity with Gaussian processes and global optimization, which may make it difficult for readers without a background in these areas to fully appreciate the contributions of the paper. Additionally, there are a few minor errors in the text, such as the misplaced parenthesis in the function on line 72.

Overall, however, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The authors have presented a novel and effective method that is likely to be of interest to researchers and practitioners in the field.
