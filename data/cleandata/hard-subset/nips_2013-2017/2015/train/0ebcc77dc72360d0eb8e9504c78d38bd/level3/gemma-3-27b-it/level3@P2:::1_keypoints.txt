- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that attains exponential convergence without δ‑cover sampling.  
- Core idea: combine an unknown semi‑metric encoding continuity with GP prior information to guide exploration and avoid sole reliance on GP bounds.  
- Places IMGPO within bound‑based search literature and connects to Lipschitz optimization, A* search, Munos [18], and Wang et al. [2].  
- Paper is exceptionally well written with clear motivation, algorithm details, and theoretical framing.  
- Strong literature positioning, explicitly addressing limitations of δ‑cover sampling.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant: rigorous proof of exponential convergence (detailed in supplementary material).  
- Algorithm description is clear and supported by pseudocode (Algorithm 1).  
- Authors discuss interplay between GP and infinite‑metric strategy and note scalability to higher dimensions as future work.  
- Strengths: theoretical novelty, clarity of presentation, and nuanced analysis of algorithm behavior and applicable conditions.  
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation lacks robustness and statistical rigor.  
- Experiments omit multiple random initializations, a standard Bayesian optimization practice.  
- Missing comparisons to state‑of‑the‑art BO methods (e.g., information‑theoretic approaches like Entropy Search).  
- Empirical tests limited to synthetic benchmarks up to 6D, not demonstrating real‑world or high‑dimensional applicability.  
- Fixed kernel and hyperparameter initialization may introduce bias; effect of increasing Ξmax not thoroughly investigated.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical work but insufficient empirical validation undermines practical viability.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject, with encouragement to substantially strengthen experimental comparisons and evaluations before resubmission.
