- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without δ‑cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. and Wang et al., avoiding their impractical δ‑cover requirement by leveraging an unknown semi‑metric bound (Assumption 1).  
- Theorem 1: establishes an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, improving over BaMSOO’s polynomial regret.  
- Analysis (Remarks 1–3) explains interaction between GP priors and infinite‑metric exploration and why multiple bounds can outperform a single bound.  
- Algorithm is clearly described with well‑structured pseudocode (Algorithm 1) and an illustrative example (Figure 1).  
- Effectively contextualizes IMGPO relative to DIRECT, SOO, and BaMSOO, bridging two theoretical paths in global optimization.  
- Practical implementation details are provided (kernel choices, hyperparameter initialization) and source code is available for reproducibility.  
- Strength: significant theoretical advancement with clean exponential convergence rate.  
- Strength: clear connection to prior work and well‑motivated problem formulation.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical analysis illuminating GP and bound‑based search interaction.  
- Weakness: experimental validation appears limited to 1D benchmarks, with most experiment dimensionalities not explicitly stated.  
- Weakness: 1000D REMBO experiment does not demonstrate IMGPO’s standalone high‑dimensional performance.  
- Weakness: scalability to higher dimensions is not adequately addressed; interval‑partitioning likely suffers from the curse of dimensionality.  
- Weakness: authors do not discuss mitigation strategies or additional assumptions to handle high dimensions.  
- Minor clarity issues: vague phrasing about simultaneous global/local searches, insufficient explanation for Figure 1’s specific behavior, and unclear comment about using UCB “for brevity.”  
- Recommendation: accept—strong theoretical contribution—but authors should address scalability concerns, add higher‑dim experiments or discuss mitigation (e.g., dimensionality reduction/assumptions) in the final version.
