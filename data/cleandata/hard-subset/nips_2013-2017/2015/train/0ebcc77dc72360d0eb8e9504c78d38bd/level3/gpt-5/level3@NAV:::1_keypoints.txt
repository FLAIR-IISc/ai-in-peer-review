- Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization method that achieves exponential simple regret without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- Operates in a bound-based search framework while hedging over infinitely many semi-metrics and using GP-UCB only for pruning to avoid over-commitment.  
- Theoretical regret bound O(λN + Ngp) with λ < 1 under mild continuity assumptions; clarifies effects of infinite-metric exploration loss and GP tightness.  
- Generalizes deterministic BO theory (e.g., SOO, BaMSOO) and connects to GP-UCB analysis (Srinivas et al., 2010).  
- The theoretical contribution is rigorous with an insightful decomposition explaining when GP information helps and a transparent account of dimensional dependence.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak: single-start runs prevent meaningful statistics and lack common BO multi-start/initialization practice.  
- Experimental comparisons omit state-of-the-art information-theoretic BO methods (Entropy Search, Predictive Entropy Search).  
- Benchmarks are limited to synthetic functions up to six dimensions; the 1000D result depends on REMBO rather than native scaling.  
- Missing sensitivity analyses and ablations (e.g., kernel/hyperparameter sensitivity, Ξmax, Ngp) and no wall-clock overhead vs. acquisition optimization comparison.  
- Paper is well written, organized, with explicit pseudocode and assumptions; code availability is a positive.  
- Novelty lies in avoiding δ-cover sampling while retaining exponential regret and the infinite-metric synthesis of GP and partition-based search.  
- Potentially significant for deterministic BO, but practical benefits require stronger empirical validation.  
- Pros: strong theory, well-motivated algorithm, conceptual unification of GP and partition methods.  
- Cons: missing SOTA comparisons (ES/PES), no real-world tasks, no multi-start statistics or robustness checks.  
- Recommendation: Borderline acceptance (score 5); support if authors add multi-start statistics, real-world benchmarks, and SOTA comparisons.
