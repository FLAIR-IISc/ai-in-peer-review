- Proposal: Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm for deterministic black-box global optimization.  
- Method: Combines hierarchical partitioning (DIRECT/SOO-style) with GP-UCB pruning to avoid auxiliary acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Modeling assumption: Assumes existence of an unknown semi-metric around the global maximizer and implicitly considers infinitely many such bounds.  
- Algorithmic details: Interleaves bound-based selection across partition-tree depths with GP-based screening and sometimes substitutes GP-UCB for actual evaluations to save queries.  
- Theoretical contribution: Defines "infinite-metric exploration loss" ρt and proves exponential simple-regret convergence with rate depending on λ<1, holding with probability ≥1−η, tying rates to N (evaluations) and Ngp (GP substitutions).  
- Analysis insight: Makes explicit how GP-UCB tightness affects exploration loss ρ̄t and clarifies roles of dimensionality and smoothness parameters.  
- Empirical evaluation: Compares IMGPO to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI on standard benchmarks using Matérn-5/2 kernel with empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates.  
- Empirical results: Shows faster regret decay, favorable wall-clock time vs GP-EI/PI, and better accuracy than SOO/BaMSOO on most tasks; demonstrates REMBO coupling in 1000-D embedded problem.  
- Relation to prior work: Connects to DIRECT, SOO/DOO, BaMSOO, GP-UCB, and practical BO literature; addresses an open gap about exponential regret without δ-cover.  
- Quality appraisal: Algorithm and pseudocode are clear; main theorem stated precisely with proofs in supplement; experiments and ablations are comprehensive.  
- Limitation noted: Theory assumes noiseless evaluations and a semi-metric bound; impacts of hyperparameter learning and model misspecification on UCB validity are not formally analyzed.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written and didactic with an effective explanatory figure; minor presentation/typo issues remain.  
- Originality: Novel combination of infinite-metric selection and GP-based pruning plus the infinite-metric exploration loss and its analysis.  
- Significance: Addresses a practical/theoretical pain point by removing δ-cover and acquisition optimization needs; ideas are reusable (e.g., REMBO, additive models).  
- Strengths summary: Eliminates δ-cover and acquisition optimization, principled GP integration, interpretable theoretical quantities, strong empirical results, public code, didactic exposition.  
- Weaknesses & suggestions: Need discussion/extensions for noisy evaluations and model misspecification, clearer treatment of hyperparameter learning effects on UCB, more scalability experiments beyond embeddings, and sensitivity guidance for Ξmax and η.  
- Baseline suggestion: Include a strong GP-UCB baseline with global acquisition optimization for contextual comparison.  
- Domain/generalization note: Analysis assumes hyperrectangle domain; supplement mentions generalizations—add a main-text practical note for non-rectangular domains.  
- Acceptance arguments: Resolves an open problem, solid theory with insights, practically relevant algorithm, strong empirical gains, and public code.  
- Rejection arguments: Theory limited to noiseless/semi-metric settings, unclear robustness to noise/misspecification, degraded high-D rates, and limited sensitivity/baseline coverage.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate (informed reading but not a GP/global-optimization specialist).
