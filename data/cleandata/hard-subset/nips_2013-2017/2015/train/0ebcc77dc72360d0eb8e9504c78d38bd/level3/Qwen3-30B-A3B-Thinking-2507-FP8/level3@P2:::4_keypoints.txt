- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.  
- Overcomes limitations of GP-UCB and BaMSOO by considering all possible bounds and leveraging an unknown semi-metric at the global optimizer.  
- Theorem 1 provides a rigorous exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, a notable improvement over BaMSOO’s polynomial bound.  
- Experiments on eight benchmark functions show IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI; includes analysis of GP upper-bound tightness effects.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized; algorithm explanation, Figure 1, and pseudocode are particularly didactic and accessible.  
- Original contribution: the “infinite-metric” idea of considering multiple bounds rather than a single GP-derived bound.  
- Significance: resolves a known open problem by providing practical exponential convergence with implications for costly evaluations.  
- Strengths: strong theoretical analysis, thorough empirical validation, clear exposition, and insightful remarks (e.g., on Ξmax).  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion on scalability to very high dimensions (D > 100); more strategies for scaling would strengthen the work.  
- Minor technical issues: redundant abstract formulation and a misplaced parenthesis noted.  
- Recommendation: accept — meaningful theoretical and practical advance with excellent presentation.
