- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence for deterministic black-box functions.  
- Eliminates the costly auxiliary non-convex optimization step used by many GP-based methods.  
- Removes dependence on impractical δ-cover sampling required by prior theoretical work.  
- Bridges GP-based probabilistic methods with hierarchical partitioning (SOO/DIRECT), implicitly handling an infinite family of semi-metrics.  
- Uses GP-derived probabilistic bounds to efficiently prune the hierarchical search tree.  
- Theorem 1 provides a rigorous proof of exponential convergence; Remarks 1–3 give insightful analysis of the hybrid approach.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, comparing IMGPO against strong baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) including high-dimensional tests.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with a clear introduction, intuitive 1D example, and unambiguous pseudocode for reproducibility.  
- Highly original synthesis of probabilistic GP modeling and deterministic bound-based partitioning.  
- Significant contribution: first practical algorithm with proven exponential convergence and reduced per-iteration computational cost.  
- Potentially impactful beyond BO, inspiring similar hybrid approaches in planning and reinforcement learning.  
- Weakness: scalability degrades with dimensionality (regret bound parameter λ → 1), partially addressed via REMBO but needing more discussion.  
- Weakness: introduces hyperparameter Ξmax without a sensitivity analysis on performance vs. computation time.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves an important open problem, novel, strong theory and empirical results, removes costly steps, clearly presented.  
- Argument against acceptance: primary concern is scalability to high dimensions.  
- Conclusion: excellent, well-rounded contribution with strong recommendation for acceptance.
