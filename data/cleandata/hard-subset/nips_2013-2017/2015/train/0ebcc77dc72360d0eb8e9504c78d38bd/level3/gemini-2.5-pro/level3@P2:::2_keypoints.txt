- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm combining GP priors with hierarchical partitioning to avoid auxiliary acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proves exponential convergence rate for simple regret (Theorem 1).  
- Algorithm selects hyperrectangles to divide using center function values and GP-UCB, implicitly considering infinitely many smoothness semi-metrics.  
- Technical soundness: analysis is complete and clarifies how regret depends on function properties and algorithm behavior.  
- Empirical results (limited scope) support competitiveness with existing methods on tested problems.  
- Clear writing and effective illustrations (Section 3.2, Figure 1) and sufficient pseudocode for implementation.  
- Original synthesis of GP-UCB and hierarchical partitioning is novel and bridges two research lines.  
- Theoretical significance: first practical GP-based algorithm with a proven exponential convergence rate.  
- Practical advantage: eliminates costly auxiliary optimization of the acquisition function, simplifying implementation and saving computation.  
- Major weakness: scalability concerns due to hierarchical partitioning and the curse of dimensionality; core algorithm not shown to scale beyond low dimensions.  
- Experiments mostly 1D–3D; the lone 1000D experiment uses REMBO embedding, so IMGPO itself wasn’t evaluated in high dimensions.  
- Recommendation: add a dedicated discussion on scalability limits, mitigation strategies, and the problem settings where IMGPO is most suitable.  
- Request clarification on whether other acquisition functions (EI, PI) can be integrated beyond UCB.  
- Suggest explicitly stating the UCB < f+ condition in Figure 1’s description for clarity.  
- Minor wording suggestion: rephrase the abstract sentence about conducting global and local searches “based on all the candidates of the bounds” for clarity.  
- Overall verdict: strong theoretical contribution and novelty but limited practical impact until scalability concerns are addressed.
