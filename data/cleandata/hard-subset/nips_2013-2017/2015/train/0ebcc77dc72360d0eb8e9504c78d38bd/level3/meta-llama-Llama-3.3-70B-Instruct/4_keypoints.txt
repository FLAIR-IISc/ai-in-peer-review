- Introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Leverages an unknown bound encoded by continuity at the global optimizer instead of a single bound from the GP prior.  
- Well-written and didactic, with effective graphical explanations that make the method easy to understand.  
- Theoretical and experimental results are convincing and demonstrate advantages over existing methods.  
- Empirical comparisons to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI show improved performance on several benchmarks.  
- Abstract formulation is somewhat redundant and contains technical errors (e.g., misplaced parentheses in equations).  
- Reviewer admits limited familiarity with GP and global optimization, hindering a full assessment of technical soundness.  
- Division procedure in the algorithm could be improved; authors should consider alternative approaches.  
- Strengths: novel approach, clear exposition, and convincing theoretical/experimental evidence.  
- Weaknesses: technical errors, redundancy in abstract, and potential algorithmic improvements needed.  
- Pro-acceptance: innovative method, strong results, and clear presentation.  
- Con-acceptance: presentation/formulation issues and reviewer’s limited domain expertise raise evaluation concerns.  
- Overall: solid scientific contribution that, with revisions addressing technical issues, could become a strong paper.
