- Proposes IMGPO: a GP-based global optimization combining hierarchical partitioning with GP-UCB bounds to avoid acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Proves exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp) (λ<1) that adapts to unknown smoothness (L, α, p) and counts virtual GP screenings Ngp.  
- Introduces infinite-metric exploration loss ρ̄t to quantify cost of not committing to a single bound and shows GP information reduces this loss vs SOO/DOO.  
- Empirical results show favorable performance and reduced wall-clock time on low-D synthetic functions and one REMBO 1000-D embedding case.  
- Positions work relative to GP-UCB, practical BO (Snoek et al.), de Freitas et al. (δ-cover), SOO/DOO and DIRECT, highlighting removal of acquisition optimization and δ-covers.  
- Does not compare against information-theoretic BO methods (ES/PES) or modern Thompson sampling variants.  
- Conceptual novelty: merges partition-based optimism with GP posteriors to eliminate acquisition maximization and δ-covers while retaining exponential convergence.  
- Theoretical contributions: clear assumptions, adaptive regret bound, and new analysis tools (ρ̄t, Ngp, Ξn) elucidating dimension and exploration costs.  
- Paper is well written with helpful intuition, pseudocode, and remarks on when infinite-metric optimism is beneficial.  
- Practical benefit: reduces reliance on hard-to-tune acquisition optimizers; code provided and GP screening can skip costly evaluations.  
- Experiments are below NIPS standards: missing strong baselines (ES/PES, TS) and direct GP-UCB comparisons.  
- Experimental protocol issues: deterministic single-run starts for some methods vs averaged runs for others, leading to uneven comparisons.  
- Limited scope: only synthetic problems up to D=6 (plus one REMBO case) and no real-world hyperparameter tuning or lab tasks.  
- Ablation gaps: limited exploration of sensitivity to Ξmax, kernel misspecification, hyperparameter learning, and limited wall-clock comparisons.  
- Theory–implementation gap: analysis assumes fixed GP prior, but implementation uses adaptive hyperparameter learning; impact on UCB validity is not addressed.  
- Scalability concern: bound degrades with dimension (λ→1 as D grows) and empirical evidence in high-D is limited.  
- Technical quality: sound advance over BaMSOO, but proofs should state assumptions on kernel correctness and hyperparameter learning; complexity per iteration (GP O(n^3)) needs clearer summary.  
- Clarity suggestions: define Ngp and ρ̄t earlier, add a concise complexity table, and clarify worst-case cost of certain steps.  
- Originality: high — exponential deterministic regret without δ-covers or acquisition maximization and the infinite-metric framing are novel.  
- Significance: potentially important for deterministic black-box optimization, but empirical support is insufficient to claim broad practical dominance.  
- Questions for authors: how does the proof handle adaptive hyperparameter learning or can a fixed-hyperparameter variant be provided?  
- Questions for authors: what typical values are observed for ρ̄t and Ξn and how do they correlate with performance?  
- Questions for authors: can wall-clock and regret comparisons vs PES/ES and GP-UCB with robust acquisition maximization be reported?  
- Questions for authors: how sensitive is IMGPO to kernel misspecification compared to BaMSOO/GP-UCB?  
- Suggestions: add comprehensive baselines (ES, PES, TS) with multi-start designs, repeated runs, and statistical reporting.  
- Suggestions: include real-world BO tasks (hyperparameter tuning, robotics, chemistry) to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Suggestions: provide ablations on Ξmax, kernel choice, hyperparameter learning, and time–regret trade-offs.  
- Suggestions: clarify theoretical assumptions about hyperparameters and kernel correctness in the analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm removing δ-covers and acquisition optimization with strong theoretical exponential regret and insightful analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited and uneven experiments, theory–practice mismatch on hyperparameter adaptation, and insufficient empirical scalability evidence.  
- Recommendation: 5 (borderline reject) — interesting theoretical contribution but empirical evaluation needs strengthening.  
- Reviewer confidence: 0.7 (familiar with BO literature and optimistic partitioning methods).
