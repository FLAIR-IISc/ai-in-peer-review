- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential regret without auxiliary optimization or delta-cover sampling.  
- Addresses limitations of prior work: removes impractical delta-cover sampling (de Freitas et al.) and avoids auxiliary non-convex optimization required by GP-UCB.  
- Positions IMGPO within bounded-based search literature, connecting to Lipschitz optimization, A* search, and SOO (Munos, 2011).  
- Main theoretical result: proves exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, improving on BaMSOO’s polynomial bound.  
- Key insight: consider “infinitely many possible bounds” rather than a single GP-derived bound, formalized by Assumption 1 (Unknown Bound).  
- Proof builds on Munos’ SOO framework while incorporating GP properties, offering insights on when single vs. multiple bounds are advantageous (Remarks 1–3).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear organization, precise math, and helpful illustrations (Figure 1).  
- Thoroughly distinguishes the approach from related work, especially BaMSOO, and compares regret bounds to prior results.  
- Strength: first GP-based method with exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or delta-cover sampling.  
- Strength: clear intuition and rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the algorithm design.  
- Strength: excellent exposition that makes complex concepts accessible.  
- Strength: thoughtful discussion of relationships to prior literature.  
- Weakness: experimental section is inadequate — single-run results, no multiple initial evaluations, no error bars or statistical testing.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison with state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., Entropy Search, Predictive Entropy Search).  
- Weakness: experiments limited to synthetic functions (max dim 6); 1000-d embedding is not a real-world evaluation.  
- Weakness: no real-world applications (e.g., hyperparameter tuning) to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of practical factors (e.g., effect of Ξmax on performance).  
- Quality assessment: theoretical contribution is technically sound, but experimental weaknesses undermine the paper’s impact.  
- Originality: the “infinitely many bounds” perspective is novel and generalizes previous work.  
- Significance: strong theoretical importance but limited practical validation due to poor experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong, novel theoretical contribution; clear writing and useful insights on bounded-based search.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental deficiencies (no multiple runs, no modern baselines, no real-world tests) weaken empirical validation of claims.  
- Required improvements: add multiple randomized initializations with statistics, compare to modern BO methods, include at least one real-world application, and analyze Ξmax trade-offs.  
- Final reviewer stance: theoretically promising (would rate ~5) but lowered to 4 (weak accept) because experimental validation is insufficient.
