- Problem: global optimization of black-box functions (e.g., hyperparameter tuning).

- Proposed method: Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO) — combines a GP prior with hierarchical partitioning, using GP-UCB to prune while implicitly considering an infinite family of semi-metrics.

- Avoids impractical steps: eliminates the need for auxiliary non-convex acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling.

- Theoretical result: proves an exponential convergence rate, improving on polynomial bounds (e.g., BaMSOO).

- Methodological contribution: practical, conceptually simpler per-iteration algorithm that resolves an open problem from prior work.

- Clarity and presentation: paper is extremely well-written with a clear 1D illustrative example and implementable pseudocode.

- Theoretical insights: remarks explain why even loose GP bounds help pruning and why infinite-metric consideration is advantageous.

- Novel synthesis: elegantly integrates GP-based Bayesian optimization and DIRECT/SOO-style partitioning approaches.

- Empirical evaluation: thorough benchmarks show IMGPO outperforms or is competitive with strong baselines; includes a REMBO-based high-dimensional proof-of-concept.

- Weakness: scalability with dimensionality — hierarchical partitioning and regret constants depend on D, raising concerns for moderate-to-high dimensions.

- Weakness: parameter sensitivity — introduces Ξmax (set to 22) with limited sensitivity analysis or practical guidance.

- Overall assessment: strong, complete, and likely influential contribution; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
