This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), which achieves exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors provide a clear and concise description of their algorithm, along with a theoretical analysis that demonstrates its exponential-regret improvements over previous work.

The IMGPO algorithm is a variant of Bayesian global optimization that generates future samples via space/interval partitioning. It uses the Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) to avoid the internal global optimization problem of selecting the next sample. The algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example, making it easy to understand and implement.

The theoretical analysis of IMGPO shows that it achieves an exponential convergence rate, which is a significant improvement over previous work. The authors also provide a novel contribution by considering all possible upper bounds, rather than relying on a single estimated bound. This approach is shown to be beneficial in Remarks 1, 2, and 3, which provide a practically oriented analysis framework for understanding the advantages of IMGPO.

However, there are some concerns regarding the scalability of the algorithm to higher dimensions. The interval-partitioning approach used in IMGPO requires a resolution that grows exponentially with the dimensionality of the problem. This could lead to significant computational costs and make the algorithm impractical for high-dimensional problems. The authors do not explicitly address this concern, but it is a significant limitation that should be discussed.

Furthermore, the experimental results presented in the paper appear to be limited to 1D test functions. While the authors claim that IMGPO can be used as a main subroutine to scale up to higher dimensions with additional assumptions, this is not demonstrated in the paper. The lack of experimental results for higher-dimensional problems makes it difficult to assess the practicality of the algorithm.

Additionally, the paper mentions that the method is related to DIRECT, which is known to perform poorly in high dimensions. This issue should be discussed in more detail, as it is a significant concern for the scalability of IMGPO.

In terms of minor issues, the paper is unclear about whether UCB is required or if other criteria (e.g., expected improvement) could be used. The sentence about "simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds" is also unclear. Finally, it is unclear why at n=16 the far-right region has no function evaluation.

Overall, the paper presents a sound and novel work, but the evaluation appears limited to 1D problems. The scalability concerns do not affect the theoretical contribution but are significant for practical use. The authors should address these concerns and provide more experimental results for higher-dimensional problems to demonstrate the practicality of IMGPO.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence.
* The algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example.
* The theoretical analysis demonstrates significant improvements over previous work.
* The paper provides a novel contribution by considering all possible upper bounds.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The experimental results appear to be limited to 1D test functions.
* The interval-partitioning approach may not scale to higher dimensions.
* The method is related to DIRECT, which is known to perform poorly in high dimensions.
* The paper does not address the scalability concerns in detail.

Rating: 7/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the scalability concerns and provide more experimental results for higher-dimensional problems.
