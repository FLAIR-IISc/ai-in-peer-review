- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-guided hierarchical space-partitioning BO algorithm that selects intervals to split instead of optimizing an acquisition function.  
- Novel synthesis linking partitioning methods (SOO, DIRECT) with probabilistic BO by combining UCB with a DIRECT-style partitioning scheme.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proof that IMGPO achieves an exponential convergence rate, improving over BaMSOO and removing impractical sampling requirements.  
- Algorithm presentation is clear and well-written, with a helpful detailed 1D illustrative example.  
- Major scalability concern: interval-partitioning is susceptible to the curse of dimensionality, limiting practical impact as dimension increases.  
- The paper understates dimensionality issues and should discuss and cite DIRECT and related works more thoroughly.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (mostly 1D–6D); the single high-dimensional test uses REMBO, which does not demonstrate the core IMGPO scalability.  
- Unclear whether UCB is required for the theoretical results or if other acquisition functions (e.g., EI) could be used—requires clarification.  
- Wording in Section 3.1 (about simultaneously conducting global and local searches) is vague and should be clarified.  
- Overall: technically sound with a novel theoretical advance, but practical applicability is questionable due to insufficient treatment of scalability.
