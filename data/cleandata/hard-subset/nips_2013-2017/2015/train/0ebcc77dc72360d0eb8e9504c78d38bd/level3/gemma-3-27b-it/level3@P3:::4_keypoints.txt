- Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: combine an unknown semi-metric encoding function continuity with the GP prior to guide exploration.  
- Contrasts with prior work that relied solely on GP-estimated bounds or required δ-cover sampling.  
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous proof (Theorem 1) of exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Empirical validation: experiments show consistent performance gains over competing algorithms on benchmarks.  
- Novelty: simultaneous consideration of a set of possible bounds via an unknown semi-metric is a new approach.  
- Practical significance: removes impractical requirements (auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling), improving real-world applicability.  
- Clarity and presentation: exceptionally well-written, didactic exposition with helpful figures and algorithm description.  
- Reproducibility: authors provide a publicly available codebase.  
- Minor technical issues: abstract redundancy and a misplaced parenthesis in the function definition.  
- Reviewer limitation: reviewer has limited expertise in GPs/global optimization, so theoretical depth assessment is somewhat tentative.  
- Scalability concern: potential issues with increasing dimensionality; further investigation using high-dimensional BO techniques is suggested.  
- Recommendation: strong overall endorsement for acceptance, with minor corrections and further scalability work recommended.
