- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian global optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Combines GP-bound methods (e.g., GP-UCB) and unknown-smoothness methods (e.g., DIRECT, SOO) by considering an infinite set of semi-metrics pruned via the GP UCB.  
- Addresses an open problem from Wang et al. (2014) by providing a GP-based algorithm with exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Highly original and significant contribution to the Bayesian optimization community.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (Theorem 1) supporting the exponential convergence claim.  
- Empirical results are thorough and show superior performance against relevant baselines on multiple benchmarks.  
- Paper is well-written with clear intuition, a helpful illustrative example (Sec. 3.2), and replicable pseudocode (Sec. 3.3).  
- Analysis thoughtfully discusses trade-offs, such as the impact of a loose GP bound on performance.  
- Main weakness is scalability: convergence rate depends on input dimension D, limiting performance in high-dimensional settings.  
- High-dimensional experiments use random embedding (REMBO), suggesting IMGPO is best used as a powerful subroutine rather than a standalone high-D solver.  
- Overall evaluation: strong, technically sound, and likely influential despite the noted dimensionality limitation.
