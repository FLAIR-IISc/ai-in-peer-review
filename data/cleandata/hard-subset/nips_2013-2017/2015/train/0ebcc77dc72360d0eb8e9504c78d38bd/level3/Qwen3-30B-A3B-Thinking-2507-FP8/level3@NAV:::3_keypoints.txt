- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization, addressing major limitations of prior approaches.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. and Wang et al., tackling the impracticality of δ-cover sampling and the computational cost of auxiliary optimization.  
- Core idea: consider infinitely many GP-derived upper bounds instead of a single tight bound, preserving exponential convergence while avoiding problematic requirements.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: Theorem 1 proves an exponential regret bound with rigorous proofs in the supplement.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on eight benchmarks show IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: well-organized paper, intuitive example (Section 3.2, Fig.1), and detailed implementation guidance (Section 3.3).  
- Originality: novel bridge between bound-based methods with and without continuity estimation, addressing practical limitations.  
- Significance: provides a theoretically sound, practically implementable solution with broad applicability in ML, engineering, and science.  
- Limitation: convergence-rate parameter λ approaches 1 as dimensionality increases, indicating potential scalability issues in very high dimensions (authors acknowledge and suggest remedies).  
- Overall judgment: exceptionally well-written, substantial contribution, recommended for acceptance.
