- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence for simple regret without auxiliary acquisition maximization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Combines hierarchical partitioning (SOO-like) with a GP prior and implicitly considers an infinite family of smoothness semi-metrics.  
- Uses GP UCB to prune provably suboptimal branches, balancing exploration of unknown metrics with uncertainty bounds.  
- Theorem 1 provides a formal exponential regret bound, improving on polynomial bounds from related work (e.g., BaMSOO).  
- Theoretical analysis explains trade-offs, including how the GP reduces the "infinite-metric exploration loss."  
- Paper is very clearly written, well-motivated, and situates contributions relative to GP-UCB, SOO, DIRECT, and BaMSOO.  
- Experiments lack statistical rigor: deterministic procedures for some methods vs. 50 random restarts for others, preventing fair comparisons.  
- Missing comparisons to modern state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., Entropy Search, Predictive Entropy Search), limiting empirical credibility.  
- Empirical evaluation is restricted to synthetic, mostly low-dimensional benchmarks and lacks real-world application tests; high-dimensional test relies on REMBO wrapper.  
- Overall: strong, novel theoretical contribution but experimental section is insufficient for NIPS standards.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel solution to an important open problem, significant exponential regret bound, and high clarity.  
- Arguments for rejection: critically flawed experimental protocol, omission of modern baselines, and limited problem scope without real-world validation.  
- Reviewer leans toward rejection unless authors can convincingly address experimental concerns in rebuttal.
