This paper introduces a new global optimization algorithm, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), that achieves exponential regret without relying on delta-cover sampling, thus generalizing the work of de Freitas et al. (2012). The algorithm is well-positioned within the bounded-based search literature, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

The main idea of the paper is to leverage the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer, rather than relying solely on the bound provided by the Gaussian process (GP) prior. This is achieved by simultaneously conducting global and local searches based on all possible candidates of the bounds. The paper provides a clear and detailed description of the algorithm, including its technical details and pseudocode.

The theoretical contributions of the paper are interesting, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's regret bound. The paper also discusses the relationship between the proposed algorithm and previous work, including the BaMSOO algorithm and the DIRECT algorithm.

However, the experimental section of the paper is below the conference standard. The experiments do not use multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, which is a standard practice in Bayesian optimization. This prevents meaningful statistical comparisons between the proposed algorithm and other state-of-the-art methods. Furthermore, the paper does not include comparisons with other state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods, such as information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search.

Additionally, all the experiments in the paper are conducted on synthetic functions, with a maximum dimension of 6. There are no real-world evaluations, such as wet-lab or parameter tuning experiments, which are essential for demonstrating the practicality and effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the lack of thorough experimental evaluation and comparison with state-of-the-art methods reduces the significance of the paper.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the algorithm and its technical details. However, the experimental section could be improved by including more details about the experimental setup and the results.

In terms of originality, the paper introduces a new global optimization algorithm that achieves exponential regret without relying on delta-cover sampling. The idea of leveraging the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer is novel and interesting.

In terms of significance, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of global optimization. However, the lack of thorough experimental evaluation and comparison with state-of-the-art methods reduces the significance of the paper.

Overall, I would give this paper a score of 4. While the paper has some weaknesses in the experimental section, it is technically sound, and the theoretical contributions are interesting. With some improvements to the experimental section, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of global optimization.
