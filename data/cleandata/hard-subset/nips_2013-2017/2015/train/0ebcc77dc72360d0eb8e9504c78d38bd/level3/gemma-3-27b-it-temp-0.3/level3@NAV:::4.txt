## Review of “Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence”

This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization (BO) algorithm achieving exponential convergence without relying on auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling, both limitations of existing methods. The authors address a significant challenge in BO – achieving theoretical guarantees of rapid convergence without computationally expensive auxiliary steps. The core idea involves leveraging an unknown semi-metric to guide exploration, effectively considering a broader range of potential bounds on the objective function. 

The paper is well-written and didactic, clearly explaining the motivation, methodology, and theoretical results. The use of Figure 1 to illustrate the algorithm’s hierarchical partitioning is particularly effective in conveying the core mechanics. The authors convincingly demonstrate the advantages of their approach through both theoretical analysis (establishing an exponential convergence rate) and experimental results on a variety of benchmark functions. The comparison to existing methods like SOO, BaMSOO, and standard GP-based BO demonstrates the practical benefits of their approach. The inclusion of a publicly available code repository is also a positive aspect.

However, the abstract is somewhat redundant, stating the main result without providing much specific detail. Additionally, there’s a misplaced parenthesis in the function definition on line 72, which should be corrected. 

My understanding of Gaussian Processes and global optimization is admittedly limited, and thus my evaluation of the theoretical contributions is based on an educated assessment. However, the authors clearly articulate the novelty of their approach and its relation to prior work, particularly the limitations of de Freitas et al. [1] and the improvements over Wang et al. [2]. The connection to bound-based search methods and the discussion of the infinite-metric exploration loss provide valuable context and insight. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The algorithm offers a significant advancement in BO by removing the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis demonstrating exponential convergence.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Experimental results support the theoretical claims and demonstrate superior performance compared to existing methods.
*   **Clarity & Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Abstract Redundancy:** The abstract could be more informative.
*   **Minor Typo:** The misplaced parenthesis on line 72 needs correction.
*   **Scalability Concerns:** While promising, the analysis highlights potential scalability challenges in high dimensions.



Overall, I believe this paper represents a strong contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses, and I recommend acceptance.
