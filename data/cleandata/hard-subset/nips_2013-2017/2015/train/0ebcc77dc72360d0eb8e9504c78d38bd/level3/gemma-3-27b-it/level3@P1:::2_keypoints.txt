- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.  
- Uses hierarchical partitioning guided by UCB to explore promising regions while implicitly considering varied bounds on the objective.  
- Eliminates the need for a separate global optimization step, simplifying the algorithm and potentially improving computational efficiency.  
- Situates work within Bayesian optimization and related bound-based search methods (Lipschitz optimization, A*, PAC-MDP).  
- Theoretically advantageous compared to BaMSOO and SOO, with stronger regret bounds.  
- Algorithm is clearly described with illustrative example and pseudocode.  
- Explicitly states and analyzes a key assumption: existence of an unknown semi-metric bounding the function’s gradient.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and proofs (supplement) demonstrating exponential convergence and discussing GP-bound vs semi-metric interplay.  
- Discusses practical factors (e.g., Ξmax) affecting performance and connects theory to implementation considerations.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (eight test functions, mainly 1D), raising questions about real-world performance.  
- Scalability concern: hierarchical partitioning’s resolution grows exponentially with dimension, echoing DIRECT’s limitations.  
- Paper needs a more thorough discussion of high-dimensional performance and mitigation strategies (despite mention of REMBO).  
- Minor clarifications requested: whether UCB is necessary or optional, clearer phrasing for simultaneous global/local search, and explanation of Figure 1’s n=16 behavior.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand high-dimensional experiments, discuss scalability/mitigations, and clarify UCB requirement.
