- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm combining hierarchical interval partitioning with Gaussian Process modeling to optimize deterministic black-box functions.  
- Avoids inner-loop acquisition function optimization by selecting intervals using interval-center values and GP-derived UCB.  
- Builds on DIRECT/SOO partitioning methods and provable GP optimization, aiming to improve regret bounds over BaMSOO and de Freitas et al. (2012).  
- Main theoretical contribution: proves an exponential convergence rate (Theorem 1) for simple regret without δ-cover sampling.  
- Introduces the "infinite-metric exploration loss" concept to justify considering a family of smoothness assumptions.  
- Analysis shows GP-UCB helps prune search space and accelerates convergence compared to pure partitioning.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear pseudocode and an effective 1D illustrative example (Figure 1) to build intuition.  
- Practical advantage: removes the expensive auxiliary global optimization step for acquisition maximization, improving efficiency and implementability.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and unclear about dimensionality; core results appear to be on 1D/2D benchmarks, which limits generalizability.  
- Fundamental scalability concern: interval-partitioning likely suffers from the curse of dimensionality, mirroring DIRECT's poor high-dimensional performance.  
- Minor clarity issues: unclear whether theoretical framework depends on UCB exclusively, opaque phrasing in Section 3.1 about global/local searches, and an insufficiently explained step in Figure 1.  
- Overall: theoretically significant and novel but practical utility is questionable due to unaddressed scalability and limited experiments; nevertheless a valuable contribution to GP optimization theory.
