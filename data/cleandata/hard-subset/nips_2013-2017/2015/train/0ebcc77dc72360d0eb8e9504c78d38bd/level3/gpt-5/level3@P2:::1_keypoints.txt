- Proposes IMGPO: combines hierarchical partitioning with GP-UCB, hedging over infinitely many unknown semi-metric bounds to avoid δ-cover sampling and inner acquisition optimization.
- The algorithm adaptively splits hyperrectangles, uses GP-UCB for pruning, sometimes substitutes GP-UCB labels, and updates GP hyperparameters via empirical Bayes.
- Theoretical claim: exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1 w.h.p., improving on BaMSOO’s polynomial rates under Assumptions 1–2.
- Analysis introduces and clarifies roles of infinite-metric exploration loss ρ̄t, depth parameter Ξn, and GP usage Ngp, and discusses dimensionality effects.
- Empirically evaluated on synthetic benchmarks (1–6D) and a 1000D REMBO task; uses Matern 5/2 kernel with empirical Bayes.
- IMGPO generally outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on regret vs. evaluations, runs faster than GP-EI/PI but slower than SOO.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with strong intuition and detailed pseudocode.
- Strength: original theoretical contribution achieving exponential regret without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization.
- Strength: practical algorithmic design (tree partitioning, GP-based pruning) and public code release.
- Strength: careful discussion of when GP information helps and benefits of not committing to a single bound.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited and below BO conference standards.
- Weakness: no comparisons to information-theoretic methods (Entropy Search/PES), Thompson sampling, or practical systems (SMAC, Spearmint).
- Weakness: all experiments are synthetic; no real-world tasks (hyperparameter tuning, engineering, wet-lab).
- Weakness: inconsistent initialization practices and lack of multiple seeds hinder robust statistical comparison.
- Weakness: acquisition optimization fairness and computational budgets are not fully controlled or reported.
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions (unknown semi-metric, GP-UCB high-probability guarantee) may be sensitive to empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates.
- Weakness: “exponential” rate may have λ close to 1 in high dimensions; constants and practical regimes are not quantified.
- Quality: theory appears sound and novel but relies on nontrivial constants and GP-UCB validity under empirical Bayes.
- Clarity: paper is very well written; a few implementation choices and complexity/budget guidance should be summarized more crisply.
- Originality: novel combination of infinite-metric exploration with GP pruning and insightful analysis framing.
- Significance: potentially important for deterministic BO, but limited empirical validation reduces immediate practical impact.
- For acceptance: addresses a theoretical gap, provides implementable algorithm, insightful analysis, and public code.
- Against acceptance: missing strong baseline comparisons, nonstandard experimental protocol, and brittle theoretical assumptions with empirical Bayes.
- Suggestions: add real tasks, compare to Entropy Search/PES/Thompson sampling and GP-UCB with competent optimizers, use standard initial designs and multiple seeds, and run ablations (Ξmax, GP pruning, hyperparameter treatments).
- Questions: sensitivity to kernel/hyperparameter misspecification, scaling of λ/ρ̄t with dimension, and extension to noisy observations.
- Overall recommendation: weak reject (score 5/10); theory is interesting and clear but empirical section needs substantial strengthening.
