- Proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Leverages an unknown semi-metric to encode function continuity and considers all possible bounds during optimization.  
- Well-motivated, clearly presented approach supported by strong experimental results.  
- Thoroughly situates the work in existing literature, acknowledging limitations of Lipschitz-based methods and prior δ-cover sampling approaches.  
- Clearly explains how IMGPO overcomes limitations of previous work and connects to bound-based search methods (A*, PAC-MDP).  
- Technical approach is sound and innovative, using the GP prior to prune implausible candidate bounds.  
- Algorithm description (Section 3.2, Figure 1, Algorithm 1) is detailed and facilitates replication.  
- Theoretical analysis (Section 4) is rigorous, providing a clear convergence guarantee and insights on GP prior and semi-metric interplay.  
- Analysis shows benefits even when the GP prior gives suboptimal bounds.  
- Experimental evaluation (Section 5) is well-designed and demonstrates consistent superior performance versus SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI.  
- Authors acknowledge experimental limitations (fixed hyperparameters) and suggest improvements.  
- Writing is concise, well-organized, and accessible to Bayesian/global optimization audiences.  
- Pseudocode inclusion aids implementability and clarity.  
- The approach is original, combining Bayesian optimization and bound-based search to remove δ-cover sampling.  
- The method is significant and practical, broadening applicability by eliminating auxiliary optimization and providing exponential convergence guarantees.  
- Paper likely to influence future research in Bayesian and global optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel sound approach, solves key limitation, rigorous theory, strong empirical results, clear presentation, high impact potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential scalability issues in very high dimensions and sensitivity to hyperparameter choices.  
- Overall assessment: excellent paper with strong technical, theoretical, and empirical contributions; strong recommend for acceptance.
