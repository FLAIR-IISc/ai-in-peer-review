- Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization method that removes the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Addresses an open problem from Wang et al. by providing a GP-based algorithm with exponential convergence that avoids δ-cover sampling.  
- Key insight: consider infinitely many possible upper bounds encoded by continuity at the global optimizer rather than a single GP prior bound.  
- Formalized via Assumption 1 (Unknown Bound) and Theorem 1, proving an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1.  
- Section 4 analysis is strong; Remarks 1–3 explain effects of GP UCB tightness, GP use, and infinite bounds on regret.  
- Clear presentation and illustrative example (Figure 1) effectively convey algorithm intuition.  
- Algorithm 1 provides complete, well-commented pseudocode and precise hierarchical partitioning details for replication.  
- Thoroughly relates to prior work, clarifying connections and differences with BaMSOO and SOO.  
- Experimental evaluation across eight multimodal benchmarks shows consistent superiority of IMGPO over SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.  
- Experiments used realistic settings (single isotropic Matern kernel, blind initialization), with Rosenbrock2 as the lone exception where GP prior misled.  
- Ablation study indicates IMGPO is significantly faster than traditional GP optimization methods while retaining competitive performance.  
- Theoretical insight: relying exclusively on a known bound can be suboptimal, with implications beyond BO (e.g., planning and PAC-MDP).  
- Limitations: exponential convergence weakens with increasing dimensionality and computational cost from considering infinitely many bounds (Ξmax parameter).  
- Overall: technically sound, practically oriented, and a significant contribution to Bayesian optimization suitable for NIPS.
