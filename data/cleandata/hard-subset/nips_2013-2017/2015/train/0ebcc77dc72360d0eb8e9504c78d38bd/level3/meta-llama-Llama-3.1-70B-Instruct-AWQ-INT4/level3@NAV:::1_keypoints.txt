- Introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- IMGPO uses an unknown bound from continuity at the global optimizer and concurrently performs global and local searches over all possible bound candidates.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis proving exponential convergence and claims superior performance to existing methods.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly positioned within bounded-based search literature, and explains limitations of prior methods.  
- Theoretical contributions are substantial and thoroughly analyzed.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to synthetic functions and lacks any real-world benchmarks.  
- Experiments do not use multiple initial evaluations of the objective, contrary to standard BO practice.  
- No comparisons are made with state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization approaches (e.g., information-theoretic methods like Entropy Search).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method with exponential convergence, strong theoretical analysis, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted experimental validation, missing standard BO practices, and lack of comparisons to modern baselines.  
- Rating: 4 — Accept with minor revisions.  
- Recommendations: expand experiments to real-world problems and state-of-the-art baselines, include multiple initial evaluations, and add implementation/experimental setup details.
