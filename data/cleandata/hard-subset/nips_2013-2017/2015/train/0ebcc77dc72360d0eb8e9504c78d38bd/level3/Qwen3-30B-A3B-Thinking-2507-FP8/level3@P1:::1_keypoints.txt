- Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm that attains exponential regret without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Proposes an "infinite-metric" strategy that simultaneously considers infinitely many candidate bounds encoded by an unknown semi-metric, rather than a single GP-derived upper bound.  
- Main theoretical contribution (Theorem 1): exponential regret bound O(λ^{N+Ngp}) with λ<1 under Assumptions 1–2, showing GP information reduces regret relative to non-GP methods.  
- Algorithmic design: hierarchical partitioning with adaptive interval division; includes clear pseudocode and analysis of infinite-metric exploration loss.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-positioned in the BO literature, contrasting with GP-UCB, BaMSOO, and connecting to DIRECT and SOO.  
- Figures and Algorithm 1 effectively illustrate mechanics; Remarks 1–3 provide a principled discussion of bound tightness trade-offs.  
- Originality: novel conceptual advance reconciling fixed-bound and boundless optimization paths; exponential regret guarantee without impractical sampling is significant.  
- Experimental evaluation is seriously flawed: no multiple initial evaluations were performed, violating standard BO practice and invalidating comparisons.  
- Reported mean/std for GP-PI/GP-EI (50 runs) are incomparable to deterministic runs of other methods, compromising statistical validity.  
- Missing comparisons to key state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., Entropy Search/MES, REMBO), so "outperforming" claims are misleading.  
- Experiments are limited to synthetic functions (max D=6) with no real-world application tests; the 1000-D Sin2 use of REMBO as subroutine is not a meaningful IMGPO evaluation.  
- Paper lacks discussion on scalability beyond D=6 despite theoretical remarks suggesting high-dimensional challenges.  
- Recommendation: add multiple initial evaluations, include comparisons with modern BO baselines, and evaluate at least one real-world task; current empirical gaps justify rejection.
