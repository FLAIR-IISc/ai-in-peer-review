- Introduces IMGPO (Infinite-Metric GP Optimization), a BO algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.  
- Generalizes previous approaches by considering all possible semi-metric bounds instead of a single GP-based bound.  
- Provides a clear, rigorous proof of exponential regret bounds, with rate O(λ^(N+N_gp)) for λ < 1.  
- Analyzes how GP upper-bound tightness and the unknown semi-metric affect performance and problem difficulty.  
- Positions the work well within bounded-based search literature, connecting to DIRECT, SOO, and GP-UCB.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with helpful figures, pseudocode, and detailed hierarchical partitioning description.  
- Originality is strong: novel idea of simultaneously considering infinitely many bounds addressing a known gap.  
- Authors carefully discuss limitations, including scalability to high dimensions and GP/exploration trade-offs.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound and reproducible.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: lacks standard BO practice of multiple initial evaluations, preventing fair statistical comparisons.  
- Missing comparisons with state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., Entropy Search), weakening practical claims.  
- Experiments limited to synthetic functions (max dim 6) and no substantive real-world applications or comprehensive high-dimensional tests.  
- Insufficient statistical validation and scope in experiments undermines the paper’s practical impact.  
- Recommendation: borderline — strong theoretical contribution but experimental shortcomings (need multiple inits, SOTA comparisons, and at least one real-world task) prevent clear acceptance.
