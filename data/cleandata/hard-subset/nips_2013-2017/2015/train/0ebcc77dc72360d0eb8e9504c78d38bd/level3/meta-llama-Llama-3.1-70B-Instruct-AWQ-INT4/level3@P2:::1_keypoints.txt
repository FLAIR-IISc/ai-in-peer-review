- Introduces IMGPO, a new global optimization algorithm achieving exponential regret without delta-cover sampling, generalizing de Freitas et al. (2012).  
- Well-positioned within bound-based search literature; paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Core idea: exploit an unknown continuity-bound at the global optimizer and run simultaneous global/local searches over all candidate bounds.  
- Algorithm description is clear and detailed, with technical exposition and pseudocode.  
- Theoretical contributions are interesting, with a thorough regret-bound analysis.  
- Paper discusses relationships to prior work (BaMSOO, DIRECT) and situates contributions clearly.  
- Experimental section is below conference standard and needs improvement.  
- Experiments omit multiple initial evaluations (standard in Bayesian optimization), preventing meaningful statistical comparisons.  
- No comparisons to state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search).  
- All experiments are on synthetic functions (max dimension 6); no real-world evaluations (e.g., wet-lab or parameter tuning).  
- Technical claims are supported and the paper is sound, but weak experiments reduce significance.  
- Clarity is high overall, though experimental setup and result details are insufficient.  
- Originality is strong: novel use of unknown bound continuity to achieve exponential regret.  
- Potentially significant contribution if experimental evaluation and comparisons are substantially improved.  
- Overall assessment: score 4 — promising theoretical work that requires stronger empirical validation.
