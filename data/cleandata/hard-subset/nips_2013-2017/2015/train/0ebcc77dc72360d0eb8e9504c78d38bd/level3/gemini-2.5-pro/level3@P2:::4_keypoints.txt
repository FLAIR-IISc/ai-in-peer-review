- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence for non-convex black-box optimization.  
- Combines hierarchical partitioning with GP-based UCB pruning to avoid auxiliary global optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Theorem 1 provides a formal exponential regret bound; accompanying remarks give intuition on UCB tightness and the infinite-metric idea.  
- Analysis appears technically sound and addresses a known open problem in the literature.  
- Comprehensive experiments on eight benchmarks (1D to 1000D embedding) compare IMGPO to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.  
- Empirical results show IMGPO generally outperforms baselines and converges faster in practice.  
- Authors transparently note a failure case (Rosenbrock2) where a misleading GP prior made SOO competitive.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and didactic; Figure 1 and detailed pseudocode effectively explain the algorithm.  
- Minor presentation issues: a slightly redundant sentence in the abstract and a typo in the Matern kernel expression.  
- The approach is highly original in synthesizing GP UCBs with infinite-metric partitioning to obtain practical exponential convergence.  
- The contribution is significant and likely influential for Bayesian optimization and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Removes the need for an auxiliary optimization step, improving practical usability and wall-clock performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves an open problem, novel method, strong theory and experiments, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: no major concerns beyond minor typos.
