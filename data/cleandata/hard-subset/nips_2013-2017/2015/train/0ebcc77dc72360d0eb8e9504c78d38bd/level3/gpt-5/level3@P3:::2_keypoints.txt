- Proposes IMGPO: combines GP-UCB screening with hierarchical interval partitioning to avoid auxiliary acquisition optimization by deferring evaluations using UCB "dummy" values.
- Provides clear algorithm description, pseudocode, and a 1D illustrative example.
- Theoretically proves an exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp) (λ < 1) under generalized Lipschitz-like assumptions, improving on BaMSOO and avoiding δ-cover sampling.
- Analysis explains how GP tightness and exploration loss ρ̄t influence rates and leverages GP posteriors to prune candidates.
- Novel combination of infinite-metric partitioning and GP-based screening yielding exponential regret is a substantive theoretical advance.
- Algorithmic clarity is good, with intuitive explanation of when UCBs defer evaluations.
- Practical relevance: avoids inner nonconvex acquisition optimization and may reduce wall-clock time when evaluations are not extremely expensive.
- Major scalability concern: interval partitioning suffers the curse of dimensionality; λ deteriorates with dimension and methods like DIRECT/SOO are known to fail in high-D.
- Experiments appear dominated by 1D/low-D problems; dimensionality is not clearly labeled in figures and high-D benchmarks are missing.
- Needs explicit comparison to strong BO baselines (EI/PI/UCB with multistart/DIRECT/CMA-ES, TuRBO/REMBO) on 5–10D standard functions.
- Should directly address whether GP pruning materially improves performance in D ≥ 6–10 compared to DIRECT/SOO.
- Unclear whether UCB is essential for the theory or if EI/PI could be substituted while retaining guarantees; request clarification.
- Technical soundness is strong, but D-dependence of bounds and semi-metric assumptions require more discussion.
- Reproducibility: pseudocode and code link provided, but hyperparameter schedules (η, Ξmax, ςM) and kernel learning details need documentation.
- Two clarity issues: define "global vs local" search concretely and clarify the n=16 example in the figure (why far-right region lacked evaluation).
- Originality: the approach is novel and well-positioned relative to GP-UCB, BaMSOO, SOO, and DIRECT.
- Significance: high theoretical significance; practical significance depends on moderate-to-high dimensional performance.
- Requested experiments/clarifications: evaluate D ≥ 6 benchmarks, compare to DIRECT/SOO/BaMSOO and strong BO baselines, discuss additive/low-dimensional extensions (REMBO/TuRBO), and clarify role of UCB.
- Pros for acceptance: strong theoretical novelty, clear algorithmic description, potential practical impact when inner optimization is costly.
- Cons for acceptance: limited/ambiguous empirical evidence focused on low-D, scalability concerns from interval partitioning, and unclear necessity of UCB versus other acquisition functions.
- Overall: sound and novel theoretical contribution but requires stronger, clearly labeled multi-dimensional experiments and a fuller discussion of high-dimensional behavior and UCB necessity for acceptance.
