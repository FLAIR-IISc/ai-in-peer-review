This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and the δ-cover sampling. The proposed algorithm, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), achieves an exponential convergence rate by leveraging the information encoded in the GP prior/posterior to reduce the degree of the unknownness of the semi-metric. 

The authors provide a clear and well-organized explanation of the algorithm, including its relationship to previous work, such as the DIRECT algorithm and the SOO algorithm. The paper also includes a thorough analysis of the algorithm's performance, including a proof of its exponential convergence rate and a discussion of the effect of the tightness of the UCB by GP on the regret bound.

One of the strengths of the paper is its use of graphs to explain the procedures and illustrate the performance of the algorithm. The experimental results, which compare IMGPO to other algorithms such as SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, there are a few minor issues with the paper. For example, the abstract formulation is redundant (line 49), and there is a misplaced parenthesis in the function (line 72). Additionally, the reviewer is unfamiliar with Gaussian Processes and global optimization, so the evaluation is an educated guess.

Despite these minor issues, the paper is well-written and didactic, making it easy to follow for readers who are not experts in the field. The authors provide a clear explanation of the algorithm and its performance, and the experimental results are convincing.

In terms of the conference reviewing guidelines, the paper meets the criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
