Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian global optimization algorithm for deterministic black-box functions that achieves an exponential simple-regret rate while avoiding two practical pain points: (i) δ-cover sampling required by de Freitas et al. (ICML’12) for exponential rates, and (ii) auxiliary nonconvex optimization of an acquisition function used by standard GP-UCB/EI/PI. IMGPO builds on hierarchical partitioning and the insight that, even if the GP-derived UCB bound is not tight, there exists an unknown semi-metric around the global maximizer that induces a tighter bound. The algorithm “hedges” over infinitely many such bounds by selecting and dividing rectangles across depths, while using GP-UCB only as a screening mechanism to prune and to skip unnecessary evaluations. The analysis introduces the infinite-metric exploration loss ρt and proves a high-probability exponential regret rate O(λN+Ngp) under Hölder-type continuity (Assumptions 1–2), improving over BaMSOO (polynomial) and connecting DIRECT/SOO-style optimistic partitioning with GP-based BO. Section 3.2 provides an intuitive 1-D walk-through, Section 3.3 supplies pseudocode and practical details, and experiments benchmark IMGPO against SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, including a REMBO-based 1000-D case.

Strengths
- Clear problem framing and contributions: a BO method with exponential convergence rate that avoids δ-cover sampling and acquisition maximization.
- Algorithmic novelty: “infinite-metric” perspective that simultaneously accounts for all candidate continuity bounds, using GP only to screen/prune, not to drive an auxiliary optimizer.
- Strong, coherent presentation: Section 3.2’s illustrative example conveys the search dynamics; Section 3.3’s programmatic details and pseudocode facilitate implementation and replication (code link provided).
- Theory: High-probability exponential regret bound with interpretable dependence on ρ̄t, Ngp, Ξn, D, and Hölder parameters; remarks clarify when GP confidence helps, and why hedging across bounds can outperform committing to a misspecified bound.
- Experiments: Compelling results across diverse multimodal functions; generally superior convergence of IMGPO vs. SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI/EI; no δ-cover or auxiliary optimization needed; discussion of CPU time and Ξmax effects; integration with REMBO for large-D.
- Integrative perspective: Unifies ideas from DIRECT/SOO (continuity without known constants) and GP-UCB/EI/PI (model-based uncertainty), showing how to benefit from both in a practical algorithm.
- Writing quality: Well-organized, careful about assumptions and limitations, with useful discussion sections.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Scalability with dimension: Theoretical rate constant λ approaches 1 as D grows; experiments include a REMBO-based 1000-D illustration, but a more systematic study of higher-D regimes (with and without embeddings/additive structure) would strengthen the claim.
- Noise-free assumption: The analysis targets deterministic observations. Discussion or preliminary results for noisy settings (beyond a brief mention) would broaden impact; clarifying what changes in steps (iii) and (v) and how confidence is calibrated with noise would help.
- Dependence on confidence calibration: Guarantees require f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with high probability; empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates can break calibration. Please discuss robustness (e.g., priors over length-scales, marginalization, or parameter freezing) and whether the ςM schedule compensates for misspecification.
- Computational overhead: Step (iii) may require up to 3^Ξmax UCB computations per candidate; more explicit complexity bounds (per iteration and cumulative) and wall-clock comparisons versus baselines would be useful. An adaptive policy for Ξmax (e.g., budget-aware or performance-triggered) could reduce overhead.
- Sensitivity analyses: While using a standard Matérn-5/2 kernel is reasonable, a brief ablation on kernel choice, initial hyperparameters, and η would better characterize robustness.
- Fairness of comparisons: GP-PI/EI require auxiliary optimization; you implement this with SOO and a local gradient method. Comment on whether different acquisition optimizers materially change their performance in your setting.

Quality
- Technically sound overall; the analysis is carefully tied to algorithmic mechanisms (ρt, Ngp, Ξn), and empirical results substantiate claims. Assumptions are standard (Hölder continuity, deterministic observations) and clearly stated. Some practical aspects (confidence calibration under empirical Bayes; computational complexity details) could be elaborated.

Clarity
- Very well written and organized. The 1-D example and the mapping from steps (i)–(v) to pseudocode lines are helpful. Minor suggestion: consolidate and clearly distinguish n, N, Ngp, t; and add a short explanation on computing T′ sets and handling general Ω beyond hyperrectangles.

Originality
- The infinite-metric viewpoint that leverages GP as a pruning device rather than as the sole driver for selection is novel. Achieving exponential rates without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization advances the state of the art relative to de Freitas et al. and BaMSOO.

Significance
- Important practical and theoretical contribution to BO: removes two major impediments while strengthening guarantees, with an algorithm that practitioners can implement. The conceptual synthesis of “with continuity estimation” (DIRECT/SOO) and “without” (GP-UCB) is likely to influence future work.

Questions for the authors
- Can Ξmax be adapted online based on observed improvements or a CPU budget?
- How sensitive is performance to η and to the ςM schedule? Any guidance for practitioners?
- Do guarantees extend to sub-Gaussian noise with minor modifications? Which steps need to change?
- Can you provide wall-clock plots and complexity accounting per iteration (UCB calls, GP updates)?
- Could you report results with additive/ARD kernels or automatic relevance determination to mitigate high-D issues?

Minor comments
- Clarify the effect of Ngp on both bounds and runtime. A brief table of Ngp per benchmark would help.
- Add a short paragraph on extending beyond hyperrectangles (currently deferred).

Arguments for acceptance
- Strong theoretical advance (exponential rate) with clear algorithmic design, analysis, and empirical validation.
- Eliminates δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization, improving practicality.
- High clarity and good reproducibility; code available.
- Integrates and advances multiple threads in the BO literature.

Arguments against acceptance
- Limited exploration of noisy settings and high-D scalability beyond a REMBO case.
- Some reliance on calibrated GP posteriors under empirical Bayes without a robustness study.
- Partial complexity reporting; step (iii) overhead could be heavy in some regimes.

Recommendation
Accept. This is a well-executed, original, and significant contribution to Bayesian optimization that will be of strong interest to the NIPS community.
