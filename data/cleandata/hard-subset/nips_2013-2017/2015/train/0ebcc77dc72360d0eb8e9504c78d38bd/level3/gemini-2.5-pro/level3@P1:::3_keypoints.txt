- Summary: Proposes IMGPO, a GP + hierarchical partitioning BO algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary non-convex optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Key idea: Combines GP upper bounds with an infinite-family semi-metric partition search to prune the search space efficiently.  
- Theoretical contribution: Proves an exponential regret bound, improving on polynomial bounds of practical baselines like BaMSOO.  
- Empirical claim: Outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, EI, and PI across multiple benchmark functions, with honest discussion of failure cases.  

- Strength — Significance & originality: Solves an open problem by making exponential-rate GP-based BO practical and synthesizes two distinct optimization paradigms.  
- Strength — Theoretical soundness: Provides rigorous analysis and insightful remarks explaining the role of the GP bound and infinite-metric approach.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: Thorough experiments with relevant baselines and transparent discussion of when the GP prior misleads.  
- Strength — Clarity & reproducibility: Well-written, good intuition/example (Sec 3.2, Fig 1), clear pseudocode enabling reproduction.  

- Weakness — Scalability: Convergence constant degrades with dimension (λ → 1), and core IMGPO scalability in high-D remains limited despite a REMBO embedding experiment.  
- Weakness — Complexity & hyperparameters: Algorithm is more complex than GP-UCB/SOO and introduces Ξmax and η; sensitivity of Ξmax and trade-offs are not fully explored (no ablation).  
- Weakness — GP bound reliance: Performance depends on the GP bound holding; a misspecified GP (e.g., Rosenbrock2) can mislead, and interaction with the infinite-metric component needs more analysis (does it degrade gracefully?).  

- Detailed comment: The Ξ update rule (line 38) appears heuristic; a brief intuition (e.g., why powers of 2) would help.  
- Detailed comment: Remark 3 gives a strong intuitive justification for the infinite-metric approach — well explained.  
- Detailed comment: Public code release is commendable and will aid adoption and follow-up work.  

- Overall recommendation: Excellent paper, significant contribution, strong accept.
