This paper proposes a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), that achieves an exponential convergence rate for global optimization of deterministic black-box functions. The key contributions are twofold: the proposed method eliminates the need for a costly auxiliary non-convex optimization step common to many Gaussian Process (GP) based methods (like GP-UCB), and it removes the dependency on the impractical δ-cover sampling procedure required by previous work that achieved similar theoretical guarantees [1]. The paper situates its contribution effectively within the existing literature, bridging two distinct lines of work. It improves upon methods like BaMSOO [2], which also removed the δ-cover requirement but only achieved a polynomial regret bound. The core idea is to combine the probabilistic bounds from a GP with a hierarchical partitioning scheme inspired by algorithms like SOO [18] and DIRECT [19]. This allows the algorithm to implicitly consider an infinite family of possible semi-metrics defining the function's smoothness, using the GP to efficiently prune the search space.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

**Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality. The proposed algorithm is theoretically sound, culminating in Theorem 1, which formally establishes its exponential convergence rate. The proof is a significant theoretical result for this field. The analysis provided in Remarks 1-3 is particularly insightful, clearly articulating the benefits of the proposed hybrid approach and explaining how a GP-based bound, even if not perfectly tight, can be used to accelerate the search. The experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing. The authors compare IMGPO against a strong set of relevant baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) on a diverse suite of benchmark functions, including a high-dimensional test case. The results robustly demonstrate the superior performance of IMGPO. The paper is a complete piece of work, presenting a novel algorithm, a rigorous theoretical analysis, and strong empirical validation.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly motivates the problem and situates the work in the context of prior art. The high-level intuition behind the algorithm is conveyed brilliantly in Section 3.2 through a simple 1D illustrative example (Figure 1), which makes the complex mechanics of the hierarchical selection and pruning steps easy to grasp. The detailed pseudocode in Algorithm 1 provides a clear and unambiguous description of the method, which should greatly facilitate replication of the results.

**Originality:** The work is highly original. It addresses a well-defined and important open problem in Bayesian optimization: achieving exponential regret without impractical sampling schemes or expensive sub-routines. The primary novelty lies in the elegant synthesis of two powerful but previously separate paradigms: the probabilistic modeling of GP-based Bayesian optimization and the deterministic, bound-based hierarchical partitioning of algorithms like SOO. This fusion, where the GP prunes branches in the partitioning tree that would otherwise be explored by the "infinite-metric" logic, is a novel and clever contribution.

**Significance:** The contribution is significant and likely to have a notable impact on both researchers and practitioners. Theoretically, it advances the state of the art by providing the first practical algorithm with an exponential convergence guarantee. Practically, the elimination of the auxiliary optimization step makes the method computationally more efficient per-iteration than standard GP-UCB or GP-EI methods, which is a major advantage when the function evaluation cost is not overwhelmingly dominant. The ideas presented could also inspire similar hybrid approaches in other domains that rely on bound-based search, such as planning and reinforcement learning, as the authors suggest.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**

The paper is very strong, and its weaknesses are minor.

1.  **Scalability:** The paper rightly notes that the convergence rate degrades with dimensionality `D`, as `λ` in the regret bound approaches 1. While this is a common challenge for many global optimization methods and the authors demonstrate a potential solution by using IMGPO within a random embedding framework (REMBO), a more detailed discussion of the scalability of the core IMGPO algorithm would be beneficial.
2.  **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The algorithm introduces a new hyperparameter, `Ξmax`, which controls the lookahead depth for the GP-based pruning step. The experiments use a fixed value, but a sensitivity analysis showing the trade-off between performance and computation time for different values of `Ξmax` would strengthen the practical guidance for users of the algorithm.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Pro:** The paper solves a significant open problem in Bayesian optimization theory.
*   **Pro:** It presents the first practical algorithm with a proven exponential convergence rate, a substantial improvement over previous polynomial bounds.
*   **Pro:** The proposed method is novel, combining ideas from probabilistic and deterministic optimization in an elegant and effective manner.
*   **Pro:** It removes the need for a costly auxiliary optimization step, increasing its practical appeal.
*   **Pro:** The paper is exceptionally clear, with strong theoretical analysis and compelling empirical results.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Con:** The core algorithm's scalability with dimension is a potential limitation, though this is a common issue in the field and partially addressed.

**Conclusion:**

This is an excellent paper that presents a significant advance in the field of Bayesian optimization. The novel IMGPO algorithm is elegant, well-motivated, and supported by both strong theoretical guarantees and impressive empirical performance. It is clearly written and represents a very strong contribution to the NIPS community. I strongly recommend acceptance.
