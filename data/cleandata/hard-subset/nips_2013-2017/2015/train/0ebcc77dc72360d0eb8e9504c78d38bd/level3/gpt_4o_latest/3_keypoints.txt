- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Leverages an unknown semi-metric bound and GP priors to balance global and local search, improving regret bounds over GP-UCB and BaMSOO.
- Theoretical contributions include rigorous proofs and clearly derived exponential regret bounds.
- Empirical results show IMGPO consistently outperforms state-of-the-art methods on benchmark functions.
- Well-written and organized; Section 3.2 provides an intuitive example and Section 3.3 gives clear pseudocode for replication.
- Integrates ideas from bound-based search and Bayesian optimization, bridging previously distinct approaches.
- Originality: considers infinitely many possible bounds, a significant departure from traditional GP methods.
- Significance: likely to influence future research by solving a critical open problem in Bayesian optimization.
- Weakness: scalability may degrade in high dimensions due to exponential dependence on dimensionality in the regret bound.
- Weakness: computational cost — faster than traditional GP methods but slower than SOO; trade-offs need deeper exploration.
- Recommendation: strong contribution recommended for acceptance, though scalability concerns temper the critique.
