- Proposes a novel Bayesian Global Optimization (BGO) variant using space/interval partitioning to generate future samples.  
- Leverages Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) to avoid internal global optimization for next-sample selection.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis showing exponential-regret improvements over prior work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and well-illustrated; paper is well-written.  
- Novel approach: considers all possible bounds rather than relying on a single GP-provided bound (differs from GP-UCB).  
- Regret bound analyzed via infinite-metric exploration loss, showing improved bounds even with loose GP priors.  
- Experiments show IMGPO often outperforms state-of-the-art, but tests are limited to 1D functions.  
- Major concern: interval-partitioning likely does not scale to higher dimensions due to exponential resolution growth.  
- Relatedness to DIRECT is a concern because DIRECT performs poorly in high dimensions and this relation should be discussed.  
- Minor issues: unclear sentences (e.g., simultaneous global/local search phrasing) and unexplained absence of an evaluation at n=16.  
- Meets review criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; technically sound and well-supported.  
- Suitability concern for NIPS: strong theoretical focus and limited empirical evaluation suggest a theory-focused venue might be better.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel contribution, solid theoretical analysis, promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited to 1D experiments, scalability concerns, and similarity to DIRECT’s limitations.
