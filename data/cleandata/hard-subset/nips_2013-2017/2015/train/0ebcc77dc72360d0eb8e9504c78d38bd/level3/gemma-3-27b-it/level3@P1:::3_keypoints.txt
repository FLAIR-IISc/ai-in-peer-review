- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method that combines a GP prior with an infinite set of potential smoothness bounds to guide search without δ-cover sampling.  
- Claims exponential convergence rate while avoiding computationally expensive δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization used by prior methods.  
- Leverages hierarchical partitioning and GP uncertainty to balance exploration and exploitation informed by potentially tighter unknown bounds.  
- Builds on and situates itself within prior work in Bayesian optimization, Lipschitz optimization, and methods relaxing Lipschitz assumptions.  
- Provides a clear, well-motivated introduction that articulates limitations of existing approaches and the open problem addressed.  
- Algorithm presentation (Section 3.2), a 1D illustrative example, and detailed pseudocode make the method easy to understand and reproducible.  
- Theoretical analysis (Section 4) is strong, proving exponential convergence and discussing how GP-derived bound tightness affects performance.  
- Draws useful connections to bound-based search methods like DIRECT and SOO, clarifying theoretical positioning.  
- Empirical evaluation across eight functions shows IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI in many cases.  
- Includes a high-dimensional (1000D REMBO) experiment demonstrating scalability potential as a subroutine.  
- Strength: Novel combination of GP prior with infinite bounds is a genuine contribution.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical rigor and proven exponential convergence.  
- Strength: High clarity and reproducibility through examples and pseudocode.  
- Strength: Empirical results convincingly demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Weakness: Scalability remains a concern for higher input dimensions despite promising experiments.  
- Weakness: Potential sensitivity to fixed hyperparameters; more tuning analysis is needed.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to more recent, highly optimized Bayesian optimization packages and acquisition strategies.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant advancement, technically sound, well-written, and experimentally validated—relevant to the community.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Scalability limitations and insufficient study of hyperparameter sensitivity may restrict applicability.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed contribution deserving acceptance, with a minor recommendation to investigate hyperparameter sensitivity further.
