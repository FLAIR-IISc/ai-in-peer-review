- Presents IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm claiming exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: use an unknown semi-metric to explore a range of potential bounds and avoid needing a precise Lipschitz constant.  
- Positions IMGPO within bound-based search literature, linking to Lipschitz optimization, A* search, and PAC-MDP algorithms.  
- Main strength: strong theoretical contribution—rigorous proof of exponential convergence addressing an open problem.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and logically organized with detailed algorithm description and analysis.  
- Positive reproducibility step: authors provide publicly available code.  
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation is weak and undermines the paper’s impact.  
- Specific experimental flaws: no multiple initial evaluations of f (no statistical robustness), no comparisons to state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., Entropy Search), and experiments only on synthetic functions up to 6 dimensions.  
- Lacks real-world benchmarks (e.g., parameter tuning or wet-lab optimization), raising concerns about practical applicability.  
- Reviewer score: votes 5 but lowers due to critical experimental shortcomings; recommends addressing experiments to strengthen the paper.
