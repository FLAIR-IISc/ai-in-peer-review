Summary
The paper proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization method for deterministic black-box functions that achieves exponential regret decay without (i) solving an auxiliary non-convex optimization (e.g., maximizing an acquisition function) and (ii) relying on the impractical δ-cover sampling used in prior theory. The core idea combines GP posterior upper confidence bounds (UCBs) with hierarchical partitioning and a novel “infinite-metric” strategy: instead of committing to a single smoothness/Lipschitz-type bound, the algorithm implicitly hedges over infinitely many candidate semi-metrics and uses the GP UCB to prune candidates that are provably looser. The analysis introduces the infinite-metric exploration loss ρt to quantify the cost of not committing to a single bound and proves an exponential regret bound O(λN+Ngp) (λ < 1), an improvement over the polynomial bounds of BaMSOO. Empirically, IMGPO shows strong performance on standard multimodal benchmarks and is competitive or better than SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, while avoiding expensive acquisition maximization. The paper relates clearly to prior work: it directly addresses gaps left by de Freitas et al. (exponential bounds with δ-cover) and Wang et al. (BaMSOO with polynomial rates), and builds on optimistic partition-based methods (DIRECT, DOO/SOO).

Quality
- Theory: The analysis is clearly structured and introduces useful constructs (ρt, the roles of Ngp and Ξn) to explain when and why GP UCBs help even if imperfect. The exponential rate is notable compared to BaMSOO’s polynomial rate. It would help to clarify the exact form of λ in Theorem 1 (the current typesetting “λ = 3 − α/(2CDρ̄t)” reads as a subtraction; presumably λ = 3−α/(2CDρ̄t) as an exponent). The assumption of a deterministic objective and the unknown semi-metric with Hölder-type control (Assumption 2) are standard in optimistic optimization, but readers would benefit from more discussion on how empirical Bayes updates of kernel hyperparameters affect the validity of the high-probability UCB condition over the whole run.
- Algorithmic design: The pruning via GP UCBs reduces function evaluations and narrows the set of candidate intervals; capping ξ by Ξmax controls computational blowups. The complexity of step (iii) (potentially 3ξ UCB evaluations) is acknowledged and mitigated by Ξmax; some empirical profiling or ablations on Ξmax are provided but more guidance would be welcome.
- Experiments: The benchmarks are appropriate; comparisons are fair in that kernels/hyperparameters are not hand-tuned, which aligns with practical use. Results consistently favor IMGPO, with SOO competitive when the GP prior is misleading (e.g., Rosenbrock2), which the authors discuss. Wall-clock comparisons (Table 1) and code release improve credibility.

Clarity
The paper is well written and didactic. The step-by-step figure illuminating the partitioning, acceptance criteria, and deferred evaluations via UCB (dummy values) is particularly effective. Pseudocode is detailed and cross-referenced to the narrative. Minor issues:
- Abstract: the first sentence is somewhat redundant; consider tightening to emphasize the two key removals (auxiliary optimization, δ-cover) and the exponential rate. (Line 49: redundant formulation.)
- Typo: misplaced parenthesis in the function definition. (Line 72.)
- Theorem 1: clarify the definition/formatting of λ to avoid ambiguity.
- Consider a brief glossary of symbols (ρt, ρ̄t, Ξ, Ξmax, Ngp, C) to aid readability.

Originality
The “infinite-metric” view and the way GP UCBs are used to prune this set are original and insightful, unifying strands from GP BO and optimistic partitioning. Achieving exponential rates without δ-cover and without auxiliary acquisition maximization is a meaningful theoretical and practical advance. The analysis also isolates how Ngp, ρ̄t, and Ξn trade off, giving interpretability beyond asymptotics.

Significance
The contribution is significant for Bayesian optimization of deterministic functions: it removes two practical pain points while strengthening guarantees. The method is likely to be adopted, especially in settings where acquisition maximization is costly or brittle. The paper also suggests a template for combining loose statistical bounds with structural search bounds, potentially influencing planning and RL.

Weaknesses and questions
- Deterministic setting: Extensions to noisy observations are not discussed; many BO applications are noisy. A brief discussion of whether IMGPO’s logic (especially deferred evaluations and UCB validity) extends to sub-Gaussian noise would broaden impact.
- Hyperparameter learning: Empirical Bayes is used online; please clarify whether the UCB high-probability guarantee is preserved under hyperparameter re-estimation, and if not, whether a conservative inflation of ςM or a split-data strategy would restore it.
- High-dimensionality: Theoretical rate degrades with D, as noted. The REMBO experiment is encouraging; an ablation isolating IMGPO within REMBO versus BaMSOO/GP-UCB under identical embeddings would help quantify subroutine contributions.
- Computational overhead: Provide more granular runtime breakdowns (UCB evaluations, GP updates, partition maintenance) and practical guidance on tuning Ξmax.

Pro/Con list
Pros:
- Exponential convergence without δ-cover and without auxiliary acquisition optimization.
- Clear, well-motivated algorithm; strong empirical results; released code.
- Insightful analysis explaining when imperfect GP bounds still help.
- Didactic figures and pseudocode improve reproducibility.

Cons:
- Theory assumes determinism; no noisy variant or discussion.
- Potential ambiguity in Theorem 1’s λ; minor typos.
- High-dimensional scalability remains challenging; limited guidance on Ξmax and computational costs.
- UCB guarantees under adaptive hyperparameter updates need clarification.

Minor comments and edits
- Line 49: Abstract contains a redundant formulation; suggest tightening to a single sentence highlighting the two eliminations and the exponential rate.
- Line 72: Misplaced parenthesis in the function definition; please correct.
- Theorem 1: clarify λ’s definition; as written it reads like subtraction rather than an exponential factor.
- Consider reporting the empirical infinite-metric exploration loss ρ̄t across functions to validate the qualitative discussion in Remarks 1–3.
- Add a brief sensitivity analysis for Ξmax and its effect on regret/runtime.

Evaluation note
While I am not a specialist in Gaussian processes or global optimization, I have carefully read the paper and cross-checked the claims against cited prior work; any misunderstandings are mine.

Recommendation
Strong accept. The paper addresses an important open gap with a principled, practical method, provides clear theory and convincing experiments, and will be of broad interest to the NIPS community working on BO and global optimization.
