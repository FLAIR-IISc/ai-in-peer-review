- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary global optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Addresses limitations of de Freitas et al. (δ-cover requirement) and BaMSOO (polynomial regret).
- Core innovation: "infinite-metric exploration" considering infinitely many bounds and combining global/local search via interval partitioning.
- Uses GP-UCB to prune candidates and guide exploration without internal optimization.
- Theoretical contribution: Theorem 1 proves exponential regret O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, improving over BaMSOO.
- Assumption 1 (unknown bound) formalizes the framework and underpins the theoretical results.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis demonstrating exponential regret improvement.
- Strength: clear writing, well-described algorithm, helpful illustrative example, and reproducible pseudocode.
- Strength: situates work relative to DIRECT, SOO, and GP-UCB and provides novel insights about not relying on a single upper bound.
- Major limitation: experiments are limited to 1D functions despite claims of D-dimensional applicability.
- Major concern: scalability — λ may approach 1 as dimension D increases, implying poor high-dimensional performance.
- Fails to discuss or evaluate how interval-partitioning scales in higher dimensions; no experiments beyond 1D.
- Does not adequately discuss relationship to DIRECT’s known poor performance in high dimensions.
- Minor clarity issues: unclear phrasing about simultaneous global/local search, unexplained example detail (no eval at n=16), and unclear whether acquisition functions other than UCB (e.g., EI) are applicable.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but practical relevance is limited without higher-dimensional experiments or a thorough scalability discussion.
