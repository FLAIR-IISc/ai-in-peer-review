This paper presents a Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors' approach eliminates both requirements and achieves an exponential convergence rate. The proposed method, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer and simultaneously conducts global and local searches based on all possible candidates of the bounds.

The paper is well-written and didactic, making it easy to follow even for a reviewer unfamiliar with Gaussian Processes and global optimization. The use of graphs to explain the procedures is effective in illustrating the algorithm's steps. The authors provide a clear and concise abstract, introduction, and problem formulation, although the abstract formulation is slightly redundant.

The technical detail of the algorithm is well-explained, and the pseudocode is provided for clarity. The relationship to previous algorithms is discussed, and the authors highlight the advantages of their approach. The analysis section provides a theoretical discussion of the algorithm's performance, and the experimental results convincingly show that the authors' approach is advantageous.

The paper's quality is high, and the authors demonstrate a good understanding of the topic. The writing is clear, and the organization is logical. The use of citations is appropriate, and the references are relevant.
