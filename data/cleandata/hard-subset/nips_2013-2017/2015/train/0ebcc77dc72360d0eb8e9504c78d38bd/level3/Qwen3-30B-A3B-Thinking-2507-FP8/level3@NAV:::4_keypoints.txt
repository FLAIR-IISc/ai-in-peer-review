- Proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Addresses GP-UCB's need for extra non-convex optimization and de Freitas et al.'s impractical δ-cover sampling.  
- Considers all possible unknown semi-metric bounds while using the GP bound to prune candidates, avoiding reliance on a single prior bound.  
- Provides a theoretical exponential regret bound (Theorem 1) with informative discussions in Remarks 1–3.  
- Empirically outperforms baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) across eight benchmark functions.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with effective figures (notably Figure 1) that explain algorithm operation.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis supporting practical algorithmic advances.  
- Minor issues: redundant abstract formulation (line 49) and a misplaced parenthesis in a function definition (line 72).  
- Reviewer notes limited personal expertise in GPs/global optimization but finds the theoretical arguments plausible.  
- Overall high-quality, original, and significant work; recommendation for acceptance.
