- Proposes IMGPO: a Bayesian global optimization algorithm achieving exponential simple regret without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- Blends hierarchical partition-based optimistic search with GP-derived UCBs while avoiding commitment to a single smoothness metric.  
- Assumes an unknown semi-metric near the optimizer and explores an infinite family of candidate bounds, using GP posteriors to prune loose candidates.  
- Extends and contrasts prior work: GP-UCB/GP-EI/GP-PI, de Freitas et al. (δ-covers), SOO/BaMSOO (polynomial rates), and connects to REMBO for high-D.  
- Main idea: simultaneously consider infinitely many semi-metrics via hierarchical partitioning (Assumption 1).  
- Use GP-UCB to screen/prune candidates rather than drive acquisition; operate on partition centers with a tri-section rule.  
- Analyze algorithm using “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt, deriving rN = O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1 under Hölder-type assumptions (Assumption 2).  
- Conceptual novelty: infinite-metric optimism that leverages GP info without a single smoothness metric.  
- Theoretical contribution: exponential regret bound without δ-covers or known Lipschitz/Hölder constants, with clear dependence on problem constants and ρ̄t.  
- Algorithmic clarity: intuition, pseudocode, and UCB union-bound motivation provided for reimplementation.  
- Empirical support: broad comparisons show IMGPO is generally superior or competitive and avoids expensive inner acquisition optimization.  
- Writing quality: clear, coherent, and well organized with helpful illustrative examples and remarks.  
- Weakness: Theorems rely on Hölder-type smoothness around the optimum; clarify constants and the exact form of λ (typographic ambiguity).  
- Weakness: UCB guarantees may break when hyperparameters are re-estimated online via empirical Bayes; discuss validity and robustness to kernel/lengthscale misspecification.  
- Weakness: Computational cost—step (iii) may evaluate up to O(3Ξmax) UCBs; need practical guidance, runtime scaling with D, N, Ξmax, and sensitivity ablation.  
- Weakness: Baselines may be weak—acquisition optimization for PI/EI should use stronger optimizers (multi-start L-BFGS, DIRECT, CMA-ES) for fair comparison.  
- Weakness: Theory and experiments are for deterministic objectives; need discussion/analysis/experiments for sub-Gaussian/noisy settings.  
- Weakness: High-dimensional scaling limited (λ→1 as D grows); clarify when IMGPO should be paired with embeddings/additive structure and whether theory can extend.  
- Minor issues: notational inconsistencies, typos in Algorithm 1 and theorems, and a non-anonymous code URL that may breach double-blind review.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with meaningful proofs, but requires clarifications on constants, UCB validity, and computational overhead.  
- Clarity assessment: Well written; a notation table and brief complexity section would improve readability.  
- Originality: High—novel infinite-metric approach that fills a recognized gap in BO.  
- Significance: High—theoretically impactful and practically relevant for removing δ-covers and auxiliary optimization.  
- Pros for acceptance: Resolves open problem, strong theory and insights, practical algorithm, compelling empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: Ambiguities in theorem constants, limited treatment of hyperparameter learning, noise, high-D scaling, baseline fairness, and runtime analysis.  
- Key questions for authors: clarify λ and constants; explain UCB validity with online hyperparameter learning and mis-specification robustness; provide worst-case/typical cost of step (iii) and wall-clock comparisons; state if analysis extends to sub-Gaussian noise; include ablations (no-GP variant and alternative division schemes).  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept, contingent on addressing clarifications and minor issues.
