This paper proposes a Bayesian global optimization algorithm that replaces auxiliary acquisition maximization with a hierarchical space/interval partitioning scheme guided by GP-based UCB, and proves an exponential simple-regret rate under an unknown semi-metric smoothness assumption. Conceptually, the method blends ideas from GP-UCB [Srinivas et al., 2010] with DIRECT [Jones et al., 1993] and SOO/DOO [Munos, 2011], using the GP to prune partitions while not committing to a single smoothness bound. Theoretical analysis shows an exponential-regret improvement over BaMSOO [Wang et al., 2014], which achieved only polynomial rates. The algorithm is clearly described and the step-by-step example is helpful.

Quality: The theoretical development appears careful and technically sound; the analysis cleanly exposes the roles of the infinite-metric exploration loss and of GP-driven pruning, and it acknowledges that λ can approach 1 as D grows. However, the empirical evaluation seems restricted to 1D test functions (this is not made explicit), which is a major limitation given the partitioning strategy. The paper should either include higher-dimensional experiments (≥3D) or clearly state the scope. The relationship to DIRECT’s known degradation in high dimensions should be discussed, with quantitative expectations for IMGPO’s scaling.

Clarity: Overall well written, but a few points need clarification: (i) Is UCB essential for the theory, or could EI/PI be used with similar guarantees (the text says “for brevity”)? (ii) The sentence “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds” is vague—please formalize “candidates” and how the GP eliminates them. (iii) In the example, explain why at n=16 the far-right region is divided without a function evaluation and how/when such “GP-based” placeholders are resolved.

Originality: The combination of GP-driven bounds with infinite-metric partitioning and the elimination of auxiliary optimization is novel, and the exponential regret bound advances theory beyond BaMSOO. The connection to and differences from DIRECT/SOO are appropriately positioned.

Significance: If borne out empirically beyond 1D, this could influence practical BO by removing inner-loop optimization and offering stronger guarantees. Scalability concerns, however, remain a substantial obstacle for broad adoption.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel algorithm with clear exposition and exponential regret bound.
- Eliminates auxiliary acquisition optimization while leveraging GP information.
- Insightful analysis of bound tightness and exploration loss.

Cons for acceptance:
- Experiments appear limited to 1D; scalability untested.
- Potential exponential blow-up of partitions in higher D; relation to DIRECT’s high-D weaknesses not fully addressed.
- Minor clarity issues (UCB necessity, example details) to fix.
