This paper introduces IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm that achieves exponential simple regret without auxiliary acquisition optimization and, crucially, without the impractical δ-cover sampling required by de Freitas et al. (2012). The method sits squarely in the bound-based search paradigm (DIRECT, SOO, GP-UCB), but departs by hedging over infinitely many candidate semi-metrics while using GP-UCB solely to prune, thereby retaining correctness and avoiding over-commitment to a potentially loose bound. The analysis yields a regret of O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1 under mild continuity assumptions and clarifies how the “infinite-metric exploration loss” and GP tightness impact rates. This generalizes prior deterministic BO theory (e.g., SOO’s polynomial rates and BaMSOO’s bounds) and relates clearly to NIPS work on GP-UCB (Srinivas et al., 2010).

Quality: The theoretical contribution is carefully developed, with an insightful decomposition that explains when and why GP information helps, and a transparent discussion of dimensional dependence. However, the empirical validation is below conference standards. Experiments use single-start runs without the multiple initial evaluations common in BO, precluding meaningful statistics. Comparisons omit state-of-the-art information-theoretic BO (Entropy Search: Hennig & Schuler, 2012; Predictive Entropy Search: Hernández-Lobato et al., 2014), and the study focuses on synthetic functions up to six dimensions (the 1000D case relies on REMBO rather than native scaling). There is limited analysis of sensitivity to kernels/hyperparameters, ablations (e.g., Ξmax, Ngp), or wall-clock overhead versus acquisition optimization.

Clarity: The paper is well written, well organized, and positions IMGPO convincingly within bound-based search. Pseudocode and assumptions are explicit, and code availability is a plus.

Originality: Avoiding δ-cover sampling while retaining exponential regret is novel and addresses a recognized gap raised by Wang et al. (BaMSOO). The infinite-metric perspective is an elegant synthesis of GP and partition-based search.

Significance: Potentially impactful for deterministic BO if empirical claims are validated; practical gains from removing acquisition optimization need stronger evidence.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, rigorous theory with a new exponential regret result without δ-cover.
- Well-motivated algorithm and good placement within the literature.
- Conceptually unifies GP posteriors with partition-based optimism.

Cons for acceptance:
- Missing comparisons to ES/PES (and related SOTA).
- No real-world BO tasks (e.g., hyperparameter tuning, wet-lab).
- No multi-start statistics; limited ablations and robustness checks.

Recommendation: Borderline (score: 5). I would support acceptance if the authors add multi-start statistical evaluations, real-world benchmarks, and SOTA comparisons; otherwise, the empirical evidence is not yet commensurate with the theoretical promise.
