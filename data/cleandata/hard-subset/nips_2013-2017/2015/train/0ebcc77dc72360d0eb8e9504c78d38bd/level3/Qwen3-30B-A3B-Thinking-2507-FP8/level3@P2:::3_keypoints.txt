- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization method that attains exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical contribution with an exponential regret bound (Theorem 1) and careful analysis of GP usage, bound tightness, and infinite-metric exploration.  
- Proposes the "infinite-metric" framework that considers all possible semi-metric bounds rather than committing to one.  
- Experimental evaluation across eight benchmarks shows consistent superiority over SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI (except Rosenbrock2 where GP modeling failed).  
- Includes a clear ablation study on Ξmax and supplementary material that strengthens theoretical claims.  
- Exposition is exceptionally clear: effective illustrative example, well-structured and executable Algorithm 1, and precise writing.  
- Minor clarity suggestions: motivate definition of ρ̄t earlier and add a brief computational-complexity comparison in the supplementary material.  
- Original and nontrivial advance over DIRECT/SOO/GP-UCB by shifting from a single-bound reliance to an all-bounds perspective.  
- Significant practical impact by removing two major barriers (δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization) and potential applicability beyond Bayesian optimization.  
- Weaknesses: exponential rate worsens with higher dimensionality, Ξmax selection not fully resolved in theory, and Theorem 1 assumes Ω is a hyperrectangle.  
- Overall recommendation: strong acceptance — rigorous theory, robust experiments, clear implementation, and meaningful practical/theoretical contributions.
