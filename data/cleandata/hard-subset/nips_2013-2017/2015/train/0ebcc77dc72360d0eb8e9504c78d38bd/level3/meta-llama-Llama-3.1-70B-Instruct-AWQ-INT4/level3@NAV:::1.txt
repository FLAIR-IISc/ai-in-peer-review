This paper introduces a novel Bayesian optimization method that achieves exponential convergence without relying on auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling. The proposed algorithm, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer and simultaneously conducts global and local searches based on all possible candidates of the bounds. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the algorithm, showing that it achieves an exponential convergence rate and outperforms existing methods.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, clearly positioning the algorithm within the bounded-based search literature. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem, discussing the limitations of existing methods and the challenges of achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.

The theoretical contributions of the paper are significant, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the algorithm's performance. The paper also includes experimental results, comparing IMGPO to other state-of-the-art methods on a range of synthetic functions.

However, the experimental section is somewhat limited, as it only includes comparisons on synthetic functions and does not provide any real-world evaluations. Additionally, the experiments do not use multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, which is a standard practice in Bayesian optimization. Furthermore, the paper does not provide any comparisons with state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods, such as information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search.

Overall, while the paper makes significant theoretical contributions, the experimental evaluation is somewhat limited, and the paper could benefit from more comprehensive experiments and comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
