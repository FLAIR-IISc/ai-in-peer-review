- Introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Proposes a hierarchical partitioning of the domain to maintain hyperintervals, leveraging an unknown bound from continuity at the global optimizer.  
- Uses the GP Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) to select query points, avoiding an internal global optimization subproblem.  
- Provides clear, well-illustrated algorithm description that is easy to follow.  
- Theoretical analysis claims exponential-regret improvements over prior work, a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Thorough discussion of connections to prior methods, notably DIRECT and GP-UCB.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to 1D test functions, raising concerns about practical scalability.  
- Notes that required resolution grows exponentially with dimensionality, making interval-partitioning potentially impractical in high dimensions.  
- Authors should explicitly discuss how IMGPO differs from DIRECT and how it could overcome DIRECT’s poor high-dimensional performance.  
- Requests discussion or experiments on higher-dimensional applicability and strategies to mitigate exponential growth in resolution.  
- Minor clarifications needed: whether UCB is required vs. other criteria (e.g., expected improvement), the meaning of the “simultaneously conduct global and local searches…” sentence, and why the far-right region has no evaluation at n=16.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
