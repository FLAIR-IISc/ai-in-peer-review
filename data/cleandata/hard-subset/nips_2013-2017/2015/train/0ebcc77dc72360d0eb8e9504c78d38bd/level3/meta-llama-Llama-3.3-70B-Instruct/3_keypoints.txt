- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian global optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling by combining bound-based search with GP priors to reduce semi-metric uncertainty.

- Paper is well-organized with clear algorithm descriptions, a helpful illustrative example, and easy-to-follow implementation details.

- Experimental results are strong, demonstrating IMGPO’s effectiveness compared to state-of-the-art methods.

- Strength: Achieves exponential convergence rate without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling, marking a unique contribution.

- Strength: Uses a bound-based search that depends less on the estimated bound, offering greater flexibility and robustness.

- Strength: Provides a practical analysis framework that explains why avoiding reliance on a specific bound is beneficial.

- Weakness: Potentially higher computational complexity due to computing the UCB each iteration.

- Weakness: Hyperparameters (e.g., Ξmax) may require careful tuning for best performance.

- Argument for acceptance: Novel and significant contribution to Bayesian optimization.

- Argument for acceptance: Method has potential for broad applicability across domains.

- Argument for acceptance: Empirical comparisons substantiate its superior performance.

- Argument against acceptance: Computational cost may be higher than alternative methods.

- Argument against acceptance: Performance sensitive to hyperparameter selection.

- Overall recommendation: Strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance.
