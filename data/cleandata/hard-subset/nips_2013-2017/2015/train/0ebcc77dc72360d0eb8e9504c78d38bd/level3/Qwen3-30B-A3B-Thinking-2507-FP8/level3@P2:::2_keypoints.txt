- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Tackles limitations of GP-UCB (requires non-convex auxiliary optimization) and de Freitas et al. (relies on impractical δ-cover sampling).  
- Core idea: consider infinitely many possible semi-metric bounds instead of committing to a single GP-provided bound.  
- Positions the work relative to prior art: builds on GP-UCB, BaMSOO, SOO, and DIRECT.  
- Theoretical strength: provides a rigorous proof of exponential convergence (Theorem 1) and detailed analysis of parameter effects.  
- Novelty: the simultaneous consideration of all candidate bounds is a fresh conceptual contribution.  
- Presentation: well-written, consistent notation, clear pseudocode (Algorithm 1) and illustrative figure (Figure 1).  
- Practical discussion: authors discuss trade-offs between using GP information and multiple bounds and when IMGPO is preferable.  
- Major weakness — experiments: empirical evaluation is limited to 1D problems; high-dimensional result only via REMBO embedding, not direct IMGPO performance.  
- Major weakness — scalability: interval-partitioning approach may not scale to high dimensions (DIRECT-like issues); need explicit discussion of dimensionality effects and λ→1.  
- Minor clarifications needed: unclear phrasing about simultaneous global/local search, unexplained missing evaluation at n=16 in Figure 1, and whether other acquisition functions maintain guarantees.  
- Overall assessment: significant theoretical advance but practical relevance weakened by limited high-dimensional validation and insufficient scalability analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing scalability concerns and adding higher-dimensional experiments for camera-ready.  
- Pro: novel theoretical result, rigorous analysis, clear exposition.  
- Con: limited experimental scope (mostly 1D), inadequate scalability discussion, and minor clarity issues.
