- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm that attains exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Addresses an open problem identified by prior work (Wang et al.) by eliminating impractical δ‑cover sampling.  
- Key insight: use an "infinite‑metric" view—consider all possible bounds encoded by an unknown semi‑metric rather than a single GP upper bound.  
- Algorithmically combines hierarchical partitioning with GP-based UCBs to explore efficiently while keeping theoretical guarantees.  
- Paper is clear and well‑organized, with a strong illustrative example (Fig.1) and implementable pseudocode (Algorithm 1).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous; Theorem 1 proves an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, improving on BaMSOO’s polynomial bounds.  
- Remarks 1–3 elucidate how parameters affect the regret bound, demonstrating deep understanding of behavior.  
- Empirical results on eight benchmark functions (1–1000 dims) show IMGPO consistently outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP‑PI, and GP‑EI.  
- Experiments use a fair protocol (same kernel and hyperparameter initialization across methods), avoiding hand‑tuned kernels per function.  
- Paper synthesizes Bayesian optimization approaches with and without continuity estimation, bridging previously divergent lines of work.  
- Situates contributions well relative to key prior methods (DIRECT, SOO, GP‑UCB).  
- Strengths: solves a significant open problem, provides solid theory, practical advantages, clear exposition, and comprehensive validation.  
- Weakness: dimensionality issue—λ approaches 1 as D increases, a practical concern needing more attention.  
- Weakness: impact of different kernel choices on performance is not thoroughly explored.  
- Weakness: computational overhead of the infinite‑metric exploration term (ρ̄t) is not fully analyzed for scaling.  
- Overall strong acceptance recommendation: significant, original, and likely to be widely adopted by the Bayesian optimization community.
