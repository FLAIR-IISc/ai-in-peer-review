- Paper introduces IMGPO: combines a GP prior with hierarchical partitioning to optimize black-box functions, avoiding auxiliary acquisition-function optimization and δ-cover sampling.
- Main theoretical contribution: proof that IMGPO attains an exponential convergence rate for simple regret, improving over prior polynomial bounds (e.g., BaMSOO).
- Strength: first GP-based algorithm with a practical scheme that provably achieves exponential regret without impractical sampling assumptions.
- Strength: practical algorithm design that removes the costly non-convex acquisition maximization, replacing it with deterministic partitioning and selection.
- Strength: clear presentation, good relation to prior work (DIRECT, SOO, BaMSOO), with helpful intuition and illustrative 1D example and pseudocode.
- Major weakness: scalability concerns — hierarchical partitioning likely suffers from the curse of dimensionality and experiments focus on low-dimensional problems, leaving practical performance for D > ~5 unclear.
- Weakness: the 1000D REMBO-style experiment embeds but does not validate scalability of the core IMGPO algorithm itself.
- Weakness: ambiguity whether UCB is essential for the algorithm and theory or if other acquisition functions (e.g., EI) could be used.
- Minor clarity issues: some phrasing (e.g., “simultaneously conduct global and local searches…”) is abstract and the skipped-evaluation behavior could be emphasized more.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound algorithm; solves a known open problem by proving exponential convergence; eliminates auxiliary optimization step; well-written and well-situated in literature.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental validation on higher-dimensional problems, leaving practical impact and scalability unproven.
- Recommendation: accept based on strong theoretical contribution and algorithmic novelty, but authors should explicitly discuss dimensionality limitations in the final version.
