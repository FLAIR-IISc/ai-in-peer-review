- Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without delta-cover sampling.
- Addresses an open problem from Wang et al. (2012), improving on BaMSOO which had only polynomial regret after removing delta-cover.
- Combines hierarchical partitioning with GP upper confidence bounds and an infinite family of semi-metrics to guide search and avoid auxiliary optimization.
- Primary strength is theoretical: first practical GP-based algorithm with an exponential regret bound—original and significant.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written with strong intuition and literature positioning.
- Experimental evaluation is weak, limiting the strength of the recommendation.
- Experiments lack statistical rigor (no multiple random initializations).
- Important baselines are missing (e.g., Entropy Search, Predictive Entropy Search), so practical performance is unclear.
- Evaluation scope is limited to low-dimensional synthetic functions with no real-world or hyperparameter-tuning experiments.
- Overall: a valuable theoretical contribution but empirical claims are insufficiently supported.
