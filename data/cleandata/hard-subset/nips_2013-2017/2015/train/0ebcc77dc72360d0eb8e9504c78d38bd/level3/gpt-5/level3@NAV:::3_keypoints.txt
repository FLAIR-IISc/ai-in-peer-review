- Proposal: IMGPO combines hierarchical partitioning (SOO/DIRECT) with GP-derived UCBs and an infinite family of continuity bounds via an unknown semi-metric.  
- Main theoretical contribution: introduces infinite-metric exploration loss ρt and proves an exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp), improving on BaMSOO’s polynomial rates.  
- Practical novelty: achieves exponential convergence without auxiliary acquisition-function optimization and without δ-cover sampling.  
- Positioning: carefully compared and contrasted with GP-UCB, SOO, DIRECT, and REMBO.  
- Experiments: thorough benchmarks (including a 1000-D REMBO setting) show strong empirical performance and favorable wall-clock trade-offs versus GP baselines.  
- Theoretical quality: analysis is technically sound, assumptions are clearly stated (unknown semi-metric continuity, deterministic evaluations).  
- Bound interpretation: the result transparently highlights the roles of dimension D, exploration loss ρ̄t, and GP usage Ngp; remarks clarify when GP-UCB helps.  
- Reproducibility: Section 3.3 pseudocode and parameterization details aid reimplementation; experiments use fixed kernels with empirical Bayes updates.  
- Ablations: includes studies on Ξmax and related parameters.  
- Clarity: well written and organized; 1-D walkthrough effectively conveys intuition.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: consolidate the roles of Ξ, Ξmax, and η and add complexity accounting for UCB calls in step (iii).  
- Originality: novel idea of exploiting an infinite set of possible bounds and using GP-UCB only for pruning; “exploration loss” is a useful analytic tool.  
- Significance: removes two practical BO pain points (auxiliary optimization and δ-cover) and provides notable theoretical progress plus strong practical results.  
- Weakness — scalability: dependence on D can make λ approach 1; needs more discussion or methods for additive/low-dimensional structure beyond the REMBO demo.  
- Weakness — noise and robustness: current analysis assumes deterministic evaluations; clarify extensions or robustness to noisy observations.  
- Weakness — kernel misspecification and baselines: recommend sensitivity analyses for hyperparameter learning/GP priors and comparison to other BO methods (Thompson sampling, entropy search).  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear, novel method with strong theory and practical algorithm; removes auxiliary optimization/δ-cover assumptions; compelling empirical and expository quality.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited treatment of noise and high-dimensional scalability; some missing baselines and sensitivity analyses.
