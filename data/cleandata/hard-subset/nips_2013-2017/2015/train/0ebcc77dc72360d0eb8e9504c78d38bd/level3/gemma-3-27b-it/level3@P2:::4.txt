This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method, dubbed Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), aiming for exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling – limitations of existing approaches like de Freitas et al. [1]. The core idea is to leverage an unknown semi-metric encoding potential tighter bounds on the objective function than those provided by the Gaussian Process (GP) prior alone, and to simultaneously consider multiple candidate bounds, effectively conducting both global and local searches. The method is accompanied by a theoretical analysis demonstrating exponential convergence and experimental validation on a suite of benchmark functions.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and didactic, providing clear explanations of concepts, particularly for readers potentially less familiar with Bayesian optimization and Gaussian Processes (as is the case for this reviewer). The inclusion of Figure 1, visually illustrating the algorithm’s hierarchical partitioning process, is a significant strength, aiding comprehension significantly. The authors effectively connect their work to previous research, citing relevant papers on Bayesian optimization [3, 13, 14], Lipschitz optimization [7, 8, 9], and related techniques like BaMSOO [2] and SOO [18]. The clear articulation of the problem – the impracticality of δ-cover sampling and the need for efficient GP-based optimization with exponential convergence – sets a strong foundation for the presented solution.

The theoretical contributions, specifically Theorem 1, are a major strength. The proof (provided in the supplementary material) establishes a regret bound demonstrating exponential convergence. The discussion surrounding the effect of the GP prior’s tightness (Remarks 1-3) provides valuable insight into the method’s behavior and limitations. The authors acknowledge potential scalability issues in high dimensions and suggest avenues for future work, demonstrating a realistic assessment of their contribution.

The experimental results presented in Figure 2 and Table 1 convincingly demonstrate IMGPO’s effectiveness. The consistent outperformance compared to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on the tested functions, despite using a standard kernel and hyperparameter initialization, is compelling evidence supporting the claims. The inclusion of a high-dimensional test case (1000 dimensions) combined with REMBO demonstrates the potential for scaling the approach, though further investigation is warranted.

However, there are minor points for consideration. The abstract states the abstract formulation is redundant, which while technically correct, could be removed for conciseness. A misplaced parenthesis on line 72 should be corrected. The paper would benefit from a slightly more detailed discussion of the computational cost associated with the simultaneous consideration of multiple bounds, especially regarding memory requirements and the practical impact of the Ξmax parameter. While the authors touch upon this, a quantitative analysis would be valuable.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis with a demonstrable exponential convergence rate.
*   **Clear and Didactic Presentation:** The writing is exceptionally clear, making the complex topic accessible.
*   **Effective Visualizations:** Figure 1 is a significant aid to understanding the algorithm’s mechanics.
*   **Convincing Experimental Results:**  The results consistently outperform competing methods on a range of benchmark functions.
*   **Honest Discussion of Limitations:** The authors acknowledge potential scalability issues and areas for future research.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Minor Abstract Redundancy:** Abstract formulation is redundant.
*   **Typographical Error:** Misplaced parenthesis on line 72.
*   **Limited Cost Analysis:**  Further discussion of the computational cost associated with considering multiple bounds would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to Bayesian optimization with a proven exponential convergence rate.
*   The experimental results provide strong evidence of the method’s effectiveness.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains complex concepts.
*   The work addresses a significant challenge in the field of Bayesian optimization - achieving exponential convergence without impractical requirements.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The weaknesses are minor and do not fundamentally detract from the paper’s overall quality. There are no compelling arguments for rejection.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The theoretical results are strong, the experimental validation is convincing, and the presentation is exceptionally clear. Despite minor points for improvement, the paper is a valuable addition to the NIPS conference proceedings and is **strongly recommended for acceptance**.
