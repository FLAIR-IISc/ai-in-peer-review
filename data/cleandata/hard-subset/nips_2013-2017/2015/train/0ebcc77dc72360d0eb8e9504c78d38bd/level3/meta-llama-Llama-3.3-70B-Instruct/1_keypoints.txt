- Presents Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization method with claimed exponential convergence.  
- Key idea: consider infinitely many possible bounds instead of a single estimated bound to improve convergence.  
- Avoids δ-cover sampling and generalizes previous work (connects to GP-UCB and SOO).  
- Algorithm is clearly explained and easy to follow.  
- Technically sound and offers a novel combination of familiar techniques.  
- Experimental results show IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on synthetic benchmarks.  
- Experiments are limited to synthetic functions with dimensionality up to 6; no real-world evaluations.  
- Comparisons are not comprehensive and lack statistical analysis.  
- Paper lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis and discussion of scalability to higher dimensions.  
- Authors claim potential for scaling via using IMGPO as a subroutine under extra assumptions, but this is not explored.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing experimental breadth and complexity/scalability analyses.
