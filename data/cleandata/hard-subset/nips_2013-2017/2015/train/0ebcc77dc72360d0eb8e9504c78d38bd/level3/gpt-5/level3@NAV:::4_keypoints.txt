- Proposal: IMGPO — a BO algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary acquisition optimization or δ-cover sampling, combining GP-UCB with hierarchical partitioning over an infinite family of semi-metric bounds and GP-based pruning.  
- Theoretical result: proves exponential simple-regret bound O(λN + Ngp) (λ < 1) under mild continuity, explicitly relating regret to exploration loss and GP tightness.  
- Empirical results: outperforms or matches SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-EI/PI on benchmarks; REMBO demo indicates potential for higher-dimensional problems.  
- Relation to prior work: grounded in and extends ideas from DIRECT, GP-UCB, and deterministic exponential-regret analyses that used δ-covers.  
- Quality of analysis: theoretical claims are clearly stated with a main theorem and insightful remarks; experiments are sensible (fixed Matern kernel, blind hyperparameter updates).  
- Practical gaps: lacks details on per-iteration computational complexity (step iii), sensitivity to Ξmax, and robustness to model misspecification or noise.  
- Clarity: well written and didactic with effective figures; minor issues (redundant abstract wording, misplaced parenthesis) and needs earlier notation clarification and an algorithmic complexity summary.  
- Originality: novel combination of infinite-metric selection with GP-based pruning that removes both δ-covers and auxiliary optimization, distinguishing it from BaMSOO and GP-UCB.  
- Significance: removing auxiliary optimization could make BO more deployable; analysis framework may influence other bound-based methods.  
- Pros: strong theoretical novelty, clear presentation, and solid empirical evidence.  
- Cons: limited discussion of computational scalability and high-dimensional degradation of λ; analysis limited to deterministic/noiseless setting; minor typos.  
- Overall recommendation: in scope for NeurIPS; reviewer leans positive due to theoretical novelty and clear exposition.
