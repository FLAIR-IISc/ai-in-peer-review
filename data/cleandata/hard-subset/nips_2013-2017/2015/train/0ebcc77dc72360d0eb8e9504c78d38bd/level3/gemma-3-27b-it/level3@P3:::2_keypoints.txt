- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: hierarchical partitioning plus maintaining multiple potential bounds to perform global and local search simultaneously.  
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous analysis showing improved exponential regret bounds.  
- Presentation: algorithm is clearly described and supported by a helpful illustrative example.  
- Major empirical limitation: evaluations are conducted exclusively on 1D test functions.  
- Scalability concern: interval-partitioning likely suffers from the curse of dimensionality (required resolution grows exponentially with D; λ→1 as D increases).  
- Insufficient discussion on how IMGPO might overcome high-dimensional issues or be combined with dimensionality-reduction techniques (e.g., methods in [14]).  
- Inadequate comparison to DIRECT, despite methodological similarities and DIRECT’s known poor performance in high dimensions.  
- Practical utility is questionable if the method is limited to low-dimensional problems.  
- Minor clarity: unclear whether UCB is strictly required or if other acquisition functions (e.g., Expected Improvement) could be used.  
- Minor clarity: the phrase "based on all the candidates of the bounds" needs elaboration—what are “candidate bounds” and how are they managed?  
- Minor clarity: Figure 1 needs explanation for why no function evaluation occurs in the far-right region at n=16 (is this due to the UCB criterion?).  
- Recommendation: accept for strong theoretical contribution but require expanded high-dimensional experiments or compelling theoretical justification for scalability, and address the minor clarity points.
