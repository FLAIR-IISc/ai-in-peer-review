Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes IMGPO, a Gaussian process (GP)-based global optimization algorithm that achieves an exponential simple-regret rate without requiring (i) auxiliary optimization of an acquisition function and (ii) δ-cover sampling. The method combines hierarchical partitioning with GP upper confidence bounds (UCBs) and a new “infinite-metric” strategy: instead of committing to a single smoothness metric (e.g., a known Lipschitz constant), it implicitly considers an infinite family of candidate semi-metrics and uses the GP posterior to prune unpromising candidates/regions. Theoretically, the paper proves an exponential regret of the form O(λN+Ngp) for λ < 1 under a deterministic setting and an “unknown bound” assumption that generalizes de Freitas et al. (2012), who required δ-cover sampling. The analysis introduces infinite-metric exploration loss to quantify the cost of not committing to a single bound and clarifies when GP information tightens the rate. The paper positions the work relative to bound-based search (DIRECT, DOO/SOO) and GP-UCB/BaMSOO, improving over BaMSOO’s polynomial-rate guarantee while avoiding auxiliary optimization.

Strengths
- Technical contribution: A clear, nontrivial analysis yielding exponential regret without δ-cover sampling, plus insights via the infinite-metric exploration loss. The discussion of how GP information reduces this loss is illuminating and links bound-based and Bayesian perspectives.
- Conceptual novelty: Avoids auxiliary optimization of acquisition functions while maintaining strong rates; bridges DIRECT/SOO and GP-based BO in a principled way.
- Clarity: Well written and easy to follow; the algorithmic description and intuition (steps (i)–(v)) are clear, and the positioning within bound-based search is excellent.
- Scope: Generalizes prior exponential-rate results beyond δ-cover sampling and improves upon BaMSOO’s rate under the stated assumptions.

Weaknesses
- Experimental evaluation is below conference standard:
  - No multiple initial evaluations/designs of f. Standard BO practice is to start with several initial points (e.g., Latin hypercube or Sobol), and to report statistics across multiple random seeds. Here, deterministic methods are run from a single fixed center and most baselines are not evaluated with multiple-start initial designs, limiting statistical validity.
  - Missing comparisons to state-of-the-art information-theoretic BO (e.g., Entropy Search, Hennig & Schuler 2012; Predictive Entropy Search, Hernández-Lobato et al. 2014), which are important baselines for deterministic functions and would contextualize the claimed advantages.
  - Benchmarks are exclusively synthetic with modest dimensionality (≤6) except for a synthetic REMBO example. There are no real-world evaluations (e.g., wet-lab design or ML hyperparameter tuning), which are customary in BO papers to demonstrate practical impact.
  - Limited statistical analysis: aside from GP-EI/PI mean±std, there is no reporting of confidence intervals or statistical tests across runs for IMGPO/SOO/BaMSOO. Hyperparameter learning in the GP introduces stochasticity; single-run curves do not suffice.
- Assumptions and scope:
  - Deterministic objective; robustness to noise is not evaluated. Many BO applications are noisy.
  - The exponential-rate constant λ worsens with dimension; empirical evidence on higher-D (beyond REMBO) and wall-clock scaling with GP updates would strengthen the practical case.
- Claims vs practice: While avoiding auxiliary optimization is attractive, many BO applications accept acquisition optimization because f is dominant in cost; the paper would benefit from wall-clock comparisons that include GP update time and any savings from skipping evaluations via UCB screening.

Quality
- Theory is solid and original, with clear statements and discussion of constants/assumptions (including the role of α, p, and dimensional dependence).
- Experimental methodology is the weak point: lack of multiple-start designs, limited baselines, and absence of real tasks prevent a compelling empirical case.

Clarity
- Very clear exposition and positioning; proofs are deferred appropriately; pseudo-code is helpful. Minor suggestion: briefly discuss numerical stability/complexity of repeated GP updates and any use of sparse or rank-one updates.

Originality
- High on the theory/algorithmic side: the infinite-metric perspective and exponential-rate guarantee without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization are novel relative to NIPS/ICML prior work.

Significance
- Potentially significant for deterministic global optimization and for unifying bound-based and GP-based BO. However, the current experiments undercut the impact; stronger empirical evidence against ES/PES and on real problems is needed to demonstrate practical utility.

Questions and requests for the authors
- How sensitive is IMGPO to kernel choice and hyperparameter learning? Please include ablations varying kernel lengthscales and priors, and report calibration of η.
- Can you provide results with standard multi-point initial designs (e.g., 5–10 initial evaluations) and multiple random seeds for all methods, including IMGPO/SOO/BaMSOO?
- Please compare against ES and PES on the same deterministic benchmarks; if not feasible, explain why and include at least ES.
- Do you have results on noisy objectives or a discussion of how to adapt IMGPO to noise?
- Provide wall-clock comparisons including GP update time and any savings from deferred evaluations, and report Ngp and ρ̄t over time.

Suggestions for improving the paper
- Add information-theoretic BO baselines (ES, PES) and modern deterministic optimizers (e.g., DIRECT, CMA-ES) where appropriate.
- Use multiple-start initial designs and report mean±std or confidence intervals over ≥20 runs for all methods.
- Include at least one real-world task (e.g., SVM or neural network hyperparameter tuning; or a wet-lab/engineering design task) to validate practical benefits.
- Provide ablations: effect of Ξmax, effect of removing GP (to isolate infinite-metric search), and sensitivity to kernel/hyperparameters.
- Discuss extension to noisy settings and to higher dimensions beyond REMBO (e.g., additive models or embedding strategies).

Pros for acceptance
- Strong and novel theoretical contribution (exponential regret without δ-cover sampling/auxiliary optimization).
- Clear and well-positioned paper that advances understanding of bound-based vs GP-based search.
- Practical algorithmic ideas (skipping evaluations via UCB) that could be impactful.

Cons for acceptance
- Empirical evaluation is not up to NIPS standards: limited baselines, no ES/PES, no real tasks, and insufficient statistical treatment.
- Deterministic-only focus with unclear robustness to noise.
- Limited evidence on scalability and wall-clock performance.

Overall score: 5 (marginally below acceptance; would raise with stronger experiments as above)
Confidence: 0.7 (familiar with BO literature and related theory)
