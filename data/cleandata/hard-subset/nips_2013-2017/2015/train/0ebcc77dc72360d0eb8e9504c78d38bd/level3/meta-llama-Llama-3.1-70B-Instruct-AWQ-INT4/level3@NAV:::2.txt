This paper proposes a novel Bayesian global optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling. The algorithm, called Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), uses a hierarchical partitioning approach to generate future samples and employs the Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) acquisition function to avoid the internal global optimization problem.

The paper provides a clear and well-organized presentation of the algorithm, including a detailed description of the approach, pseudocode, and theoretical analysis. The authors also provide experimental results comparing IMGPO to other state-of-the-art algorithms, demonstrating its superior performance on a range of test functions.

The theoretical analysis of the algorithm is sound and provides a novel contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The authors show that IMGPO achieves an exponential convergence rate, which is a significant improvement over previous work. The analysis also provides insights into the effect of the tightness of the UCB bound and the use of GP on the regret bound.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The experimental results appear to be limited to 1D test functions, which raises concerns about the scalability of the algorithm to higher dimensions. The authors acknowledge this limitation and suggest that additional assumptions may be needed to address this issue.

Another concern is the relationship between IMGPO and other algorithms, such as DIRECT, which is known to perform poorly in high dimensions. The authors should discuss this issue in more detail and provide a clear explanation of how IMGPO addresses the limitations of previous work.

Minor issues include unclear sentences and unclear notation in some places. For example, the sentence "simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds" is unclear, and the notation "T ′h+ξ(ch,i∗h)" is not well-defined.

Overall, the paper presents a sound and novel contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. However, the evaluation appears limited to 1D problems, and the authors should address the scalability concerns and provide a clearer explanation of the relationship between IMGPO and other algorithms.
