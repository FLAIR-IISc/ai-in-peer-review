- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary global optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Addresses GP-UCB’s reliance on an expensive additional global optimization and the impracticality of δ-cover sampling.  
- Leverages interval partitioning and an "infinite-metric exploration" framework to consider infinitely many upper bounds.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical regret analysis showing exponential improvement over BaMSOO under mild assumptions.  
- Effectively relates the method to DIRECT and SOO; algorithm, pseudocode, and illustrative example are clear.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear writing and strong presentation.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to 1D functions, which is a significant shortcoming.  
- Scalability concerns (exponential resolution in higher dimensions) are acknowledged theoretically but remain unaddressed empirically, conflicting with known poor performance of DIRECT-like methods in high dimensions.  
- Authors should explicitly compare to high-dimensional alternatives (e.g., REMBO) rather than merely mentioning them.  
- Minor issues: unclear justification for using UCB, confusing wording about "simultaneously conducting global and local searches," and unexplained omission of evaluation at n=16.  
- Overall: strong and novel theoretical contribution but limited practical significance until broader, higher-dimensional experimental validation is provided.
