This paper proposes a novel Bayesian global optimization method that avoids the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors introduce Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO) as the algorithmic instantiation, which leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer. The main contributions are clearly and coherently presented in the main section, and the paper provides a strong illustrative example in Section 3.2 that conveys high-level intuition. The programmatic details in Section 3.3 facilitate implementation and replication.

The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the method's effectiveness. The authors integrate ideas from the Bayesian optimization literature, both with and without continuity estimation, and provide a well-written paper that represents a strong contribution to the NIPS community.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposal of a novel Bayesian global optimization method that avoids auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling.
* The introduction of IMGPO as the algorithmic instantiation, which leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer.
* The clear and coherent presentation of the main contributions in the main section.
* The provision of a strong illustrative example in Section 3.2 that conveys high-level intuition.
* The facilitation of implementation and replication through the programmatic details in Section 3.3.
* The demonstration of the method's effectiveness through compelling experimental results.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The potential for increased computational complexity due to the consideration of infinitely many possible candidates of the bounds.
* The requirement for careful tuning of the hyperparameters, such as Ξmax and η.
* The need for further analysis and experimentation to fully understand the behavior of the algorithm in different scenarios.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective Bayesian global optimization method that addresses the limitations of existing methods.
* The authors provide a clear and coherent presentation of the main contributions and facilitate implementation and replication.
* The experimental results demonstrate the method's effectiveness and provide a strong contribution to the NIPS community.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The potential for increased computational complexity may limit the applicability of the method in certain scenarios.
* The requirement for careful tuning of the hyperparameters may be a challenge for some users.
* Further analysis and experimentation are needed to fully understand the behavior of the algorithm in different scenarios.

Overall, I recommend acceptance of the paper, as it represents a strong contribution to the NIPS community and proposes a novel and effective Bayesian global optimization method.
