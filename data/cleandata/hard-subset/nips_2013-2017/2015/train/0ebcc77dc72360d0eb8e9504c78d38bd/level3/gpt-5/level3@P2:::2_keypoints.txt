- Paper proposes IMGPO: hierarchical partitioning + GP-UCB pruning to avoid auxiliary acquisition optimization, claiming exponential simple-regret without δ-cover assumptions.  
- Theoretical contribution: high-probability exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp) under unknown semi-metric smoothness, removing de Freitas et al.’s δ-cover requirement.  
- Relation to prior work: connects to DIRECT, optimistic optimization (HOO/DOO/SOO), and BaMSOO, but claims improved regret by “infinite-metric” exploration with GP pruning.  
- Algorithm: selects rectangles by center values, uses GP-UCB checks across scales, and can assign GP-imputed values to child centers to avoid evaluations.  
- Strength — Quality: substantial, careful analysis clarifying roles of ρ̄t, Ngp, and division depth Ξ and quantifying GP tightness effects.  
- Strength — Originality: novel infinite-metric stance synthesizing DIRECT/SOO-style partitioning with GP-based optimism to hedge unknown metric tightness.  
- Strength — Clarity: precise pseudocode, 1D illustration, and clear high-level design rationale.  
- Strength — Significance: removing auxiliary optimization is practically appealing and theoretically advances deterministic GP optimization.  
- Weakness — Empirical scope: experiments appear limited to 1D; lacking 2–10D benchmarks and realistic BO tasks to assess practical impact.  
- Weakness — Scalability: DIRECT-style partitioning likely degrades exponentially with dimension; paper lacks discussion and empirical mitigation strategies.  
- Weakness — Practical complexity: step (iii) can be computationally heavy and GP hyperparameters are re-estimated each iteration; no per-iteration complexity or runtime comparisons given.  
- Weakness — Deterministic setting: method targets deterministic objectives; no discussion/extension to noisy observations common in BO.  
- Minor clarity issues: role/necessity of UCB vs other acquisition functions, formalization of “simultaneous global and local searches,” and GP-surrogate resolution rules need clarification.  
- Assessment — Quality: strong theory but narrow empirical support; parameter sensitivity and practical utility unclear.  
- Assessment — Clarity: generally good but some sentences, notation, and surrogate rules need standardization/clarification.  
- Assessment — Originality: strong and novel relative to relevant literature.  
- Assessment — Significance: high theoretical significance; practical significance depends on scalability and broader experiments.  
- Suggestions: add 2–10D benchmarks and a real BO task, include wall-clock and evaluation comparisons, and perform ablations on Ξmax, η, and kernel learning.  
- Suggestions: provide per-iteration complexity/memory analysis and empirical runtimes.  
- Suggestions: discuss high-dimensional strategies (e.g., REMBO, additive models) and how IMGPO could be integrated or used as a subroutine.  
- Suggestions: discuss or extend the algorithm and analysis to noisy settings and clarify UCB choice implications.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong novel theoretical result (exponential regret w/o δ-cover), clear algorithmic design, and potential practical benefit for deterministic BO.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical evaluation, unresolved scalability concerns, and applicability restricted to deterministic settings.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept conditional on strengthening experiments beyond 1D and clarifying scalability and practical complexity.  
- Confidence: high on theory/prior-work relation, medium on empirical assessment due to limited scope.
