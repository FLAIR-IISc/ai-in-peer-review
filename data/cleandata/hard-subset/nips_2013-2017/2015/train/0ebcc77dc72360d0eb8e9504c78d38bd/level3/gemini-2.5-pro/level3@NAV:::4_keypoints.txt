- Introduces IMGPO, a novel GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence.  
- Removes need for auxiliary non-convex optimization and impractical δ-cover sampling from prior exponential-convergence theory.  
- Synthesizes bound-based hierarchical partitioning (e.g., SOO) with GP-UCB-style probabilistic modeling.  
- Improves on polynomial regret bounds of related methods like BaMSOO.  
- Paper quality and clarity are exceptional; presentation is didactic and well-organized.  
- Figure 1 provides an effective step-by-step visual walkthrough that aids comprehension.  
- Theoretical claims are rigorously analyzed and convincingly supported.  
- Comprehensive experiments demonstrate strong performance versus robust baselines on diverse benchmarks.  
- Minor presentational issues: redundant final abstract sentence and a small typo in the definition of ςM (Section 3.3).  
- Reviewer notes limited personal expertise in GPs/global optimization but judges the work technically sound.  
- Originality and significance are high; core idea of considering infinite bounds and GP-based pruning is novel and impactful.  
- Strong recommendation for acceptance based on significance, technical quality, and presentation.
