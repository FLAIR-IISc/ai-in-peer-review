- Proposes IMGPO, a BO algorithm that attains exponential simple-regret rates while avoiding δ-cover sampling and auxiliary acquisition-function optimization.
- Key idea: hedge over infinitely many unknown semi-metrics to get tighter local bounds and use GP-UCB only to screen/prune, not drive optimization.
- Builds on hierarchical partitioning (DIRECT/SOO) and GP uncertainty (GP-UCB), introducing infinite-metric exploration loss ρt in the analysis.
- Proves a high-probability exponential regret bound O(λN+Ngp) under Hölder-type continuity, improving over BaMSOO’s polynomial rate.
- Provides a 1-D illustrative example, clear pseudocode, practical implementation details, and publicly available code.
- Experiments show generally superior convergence versus SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI, and include a REMBO-based 1000-D illustration.
- Strength: clear problem framing and contributions — exponential convergence without δ-cover or auxiliary optimization.
- Strength: algorithmic novelty — the “infinite-metric” perspective and using GP for pruning rather than selection.
- Strength: strong presentation and reproducibility (illustrative example, pseudocode, code link).
- Strength: solid theory linking algorithmic mechanisms (ρt, Ngp, Ξn) to regret guarantees and interpretable parameters.
- Strength: compelling empirical results across multimodal functions and discussion of CPU time and Ξmax effects.
- Strength: integrative perspective unifying DIRECT/SOO and GP-UCB/EI/PI ideas.
- Strength: well-written, careful about assumptions and limitations.
- Weakness: scalability with dimension — λ→1 as D grows and only a REMBO example; broader high-D study needed.
- Weakness: analysis assumes noise-free observations; need discussion/experiments for noisy settings and required algorithmic changes.
- Weakness: dependence on GP confidence calibration — empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates may break guarantees; robustness strategies should be discussed.
- Weakness: computational overhead — step (iii) can require up to 3^Ξmax UCB computations; provide explicit complexity bounds and wall-clock comparisons.
- Weakness: sensitivity analyses missing — ablations on kernel choice, initial hyperparameters, and η are needed to assess robustness.
- Weakness: fairness of comparisons — clarify whether different acquisition optimizers materially affect GP-PI/EI baselines.
- Quality: technically sound and carefully tied to mechanisms, but practical aspects (calibration, complexity) need elaboration.
- Clarity: very well organized; minor clarifications suggested for notation (n, N, Ngp, t), T′ computation, and non-hyperrectangle domains.
- Originality: novel infinite-metric viewpoint using GP as pruning device and achieving exponential rates without δ-cover or auxiliary optimization.
- Significance: important practical/theoretical advance likely to influence BO research and practice.
- Questions for authors: can Ξmax be adapted online or budget-aware?
- Questions for authors: how sensitive is performance to η and the ςM schedule; any practitioner guidance?
- Questions for authors: do guarantees extend to sub-Gaussian noise and which steps must change?
- Questions for authors: please provide wall-clock plots and per-iteration complexity (UCB calls, GP updates).
- Questions for authors: results with additive/ARD kernels or ARD-based mitigation for high-D?
- Minor comment: clarify Ngp’s effect on bounds and runtime and report Ngp per benchmark.
- Minor comment: add a short paragraph on extending beyond hyperrectangles.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical advance, practical algorithm eliminating two major impediments, clear presentation, reproducible code.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited noisy/high-D exploration, reliance on calibrated GP posteriors without robustness study, and incomplete complexity reporting.
- Recommendation: Accept.
