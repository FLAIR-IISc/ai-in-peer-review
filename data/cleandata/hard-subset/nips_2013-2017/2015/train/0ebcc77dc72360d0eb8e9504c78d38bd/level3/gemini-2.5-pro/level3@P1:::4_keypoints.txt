- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence for simple regret without auxiliary non-convex optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Core idea: combine hierarchical partitioning (DIRECT/SOO-style infinite-metric approach) with GP posterior UCBs used for pruning promising regions rather than guiding expensive auxiliary optimization.
- Addresses an open problem by merging two research lines to get theoretical power and practical appeal.
- Theorem 1 proves an exponential regret bound, improving on polynomial bounds of comparable practical methods like BaMSOO.
- Analysis (Remarks 1–3) explains how the GP reduces "infinite-metric exploration loss" and how UCB tightness affects performance.
- Empirical evaluation compares IMGPO to strong baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) on eight benchmark functions, showing generally superior performance.
- Includes a 1000-D REMBO experiment suggesting IMGPO’s potential as a subroutine in high-dimensional settings.
- Reports computation time, showing IMGPO is faster than GP methods that require auxiliary optimization.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, with intuitive explanations, helpful Figure 1, and reproducible pseudocode (Algorithm 1).
- Minor presentation issues: abstract’s problem formulation is slightly redundant and could be streamlined.
- Typo in Section 5: the fsin1 function expression needs clarification.
- Suggests adding brief discussion on scalability/limitations for moderate dimensions (e.g., D=10–50) without extra structure.
