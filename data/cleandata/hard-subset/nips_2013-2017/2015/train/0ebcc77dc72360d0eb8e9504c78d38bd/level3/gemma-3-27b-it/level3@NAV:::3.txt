## Review of "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence"

This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization (BO) method, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), that achieves exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling – both significant hurdles in existing approaches. The core idea is to leverage an unknown semi-metric governing the function’s smoothness, effectively considering all possible bounds during the optimization process. 

The work builds upon a strong foundation in BO literature, intelligently integrating ideas from both continuity estimation and methods without it. The authors clearly position their contribution within the broader context of global optimization, acknowledging limitations of Lipschitz continuity assumptions and the challenges posed by δ-cover sampling in previous work like de Freitas et al. [1] and Wang et al. [2]. The approach represents a significant refinement over BaMSOO [2] by achieving an exponential convergence rate.

The paper’s strengths are numerous. The main contributions are presented clearly and coherently in the introduction and subsequent sections. Section 3.2 offers a particularly strong illustrative example, effectively conveying the high-level intuition behind considering multiple bounds via hierarchical partitioning. Moreover, Section 3.3 provides sufficient programmatic details for implementation and replication – a crucial aspect often lacking in theoretical works. The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating IMGPO’s effectiveness across a range of benchmark functions, and comparing favorably to SOO, BaMSOO, and standard GP-based methods. The authors thoughtfully acknowledge potential limitations and discuss avenues for future improvement, such as scalability to higher dimensions. The theoretical analysis, while dense, is well-structured and provides a solid justification for the claimed exponential convergence.

A minor weakness lies in the assumption of a square search domain, though the authors acknowledge this can be mitigated through scaling. Additionally, the discussion of parameter sensitivity (e.g., Ξmax) could be expanded to provide more guidance for practical application. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** Addresses a key limitation in BO – the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** Provides a rigorous analysis with a demonstrable exponential convergence rate.
*   **Empirical Validation:**  Demonstrates strong performance on benchmark functions.
*   **Clarity:**  Well-written and clearly explains complex concepts.
*   **Significance:** Advances the state-of-the-art in Bayesian optimization and offers a valuable tool for practitioners.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The assumption of a square search domain, while addressed, could limit applicability.
*   Further analysis of parameter sensitivity would strengthen the practical impact.



Overall, this is a high-quality paper that represents a strong contribution to the NIPS community. I recommend acceptance.
