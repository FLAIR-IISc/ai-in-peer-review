- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential regret without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Claims to fix a limitation in Freitas et al. (2012) by avoiding restrictive sampling requirements.
- Clearly positions the work within bounded-based search and contrasts it with BaMSOO and GP-UCB.
- Main theoretical contribution: considering infinitely many possible bounds yields improved regret bounds over existing methods.
- Paper is very clear with strong theoretical exposition.
- Algorithm description is well-structured with clear pseudocode and insightful intuition.
- Relation to prior work is thoroughly discussed and the mathematical analysis is sound.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: standard BO practice (multiple initial evaluations) was not followed, undermining statistical comparisons.
- Missing comparisons with state-of-the-art baselines such as Entropy Search and GP-UCB variants.
- All experiments use synthetic functions up to 6 dimensions; no real-world applications (e.g., hyperparameter tuning) are tested.
- Overall lack of statistical comparisons, relevant baselines, and real-world benchmarks prevents assessment of practical value.
- Reviewer scores: Originality 4/5, Quality 4/5, Clarity 5/5, Significance 2/5; recommendation: reject despite valuable theoretical insights.
