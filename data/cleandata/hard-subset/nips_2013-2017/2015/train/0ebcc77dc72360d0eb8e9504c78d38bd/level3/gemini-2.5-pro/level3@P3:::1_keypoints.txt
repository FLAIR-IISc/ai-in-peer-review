- Introduces IMGPO, a novel GP-based global optimization algorithm claiming exponential convergence in simple regret.  
- Main innovation: avoids the impractical δ-cover assumption by combining hierarchical partitioning (like SOO/DIRECT) with GP UCBs.  
- Simultaneously considers an infinite family of semi-metrics instead of committing to a single smoothness constant.  
- Practical benefit: no separate acquisition-function maximization step is required.  
- Theoretical contribution is original and significant, addressing an open problem and unifying Lipschitz-style and GP-based methods.  
- Technical analysis appears sound, with strong regret bounds and insightful remarks on how GP bounds reduce exploration loss.  
- Paper is very clear and well-written, with helpful illustrations and a good exposition of related work.  
- Experiments lack statistical rigor: deterministic single-start comparisons vs. randomized baselines averaged over many runs is unfair.  
- Missing comparisons to modern state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., ES, PES, MES), limiting assessment of practical performance.  
- Experimental scope is narrow and unrealistic: mostly low-dimensional synthetic benchmarks and only a preliminary high-dimensional REMBO test.  
- Overall: theoretically valuable work, but empirical validation is weak and needs major revision before top-tier acceptance.
