Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes a new Bayesian global optimization algorithm, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), that combines hierarchical space partitioning with GP-based upper confidence bounds (UCB) to guide search without solving an auxiliary non-convex inner optimization. The key theoretical contribution is an exponential simple-regret bound that does not rely on the δ-cover sampling assumption used by de Freitas et al. for deterministic GP bandits, which has been deemed impractical. The approach is related to DIRECT and to the optimistic optimization line (HOO/DOO/SOO), as well as to BaMSOO (SOO + GP-UCB). Compared to BaMSOO, which yields polynomial regret, IMGPO claims exponential regret by leveraging GP posterior UCBs to filter candidate regions while remaining agnostic to the unknown tightness of Lipschitz-like bounds via an “infinite-metric” exploration strategy.

Algorithmically, the method maintains a hierarchical partitioning of the space, selects promising rectangles by center values and UCB checks across scales, and adaptively decides whether to evaluate or to temporarily assign GP-UCB surrogates at child centers to avoid unnecessary function calls. This avoids the typical acquisition maximization subproblem in GP-based BO.

Strengths
- Quality: The theoretical analysis is substantial. The paper provides a high-probability exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp) under an unknown semi-metric smoothness assumption, removing the δ-cover requirement of prior theory. The analysis clearly articulates the roles of the infinite-metric exploration loss ρ̄t, the number of GP-imputed points Ngp, and the division depth parameter Ξ, including remarks quantifying how GP tightness affects the rate.
- Originality: The “infinite-metric” stance—hedging against unknown tightness by considering all candidate semi-metrics while using the GP to prune—offers a novel synthesis of DIRECT/SOO-style partitioning with GP-based optimism. Achieving exponential regret without δ-cover is new relative to de Freitas et al. and BaMSOO.
- Clarity: The algorithm is precisely described with pseudocode and a step-by-step 1D illustration. The high-level design rationale (bounds may be loose; compensate by exploring across all metrics but prune with GP) is well-motivated.
- Significance: Removing auxiliary optimization is of practical interest for BO, and the theoretical rate improvement advances our understanding of deterministic GP optimization.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Empirical evaluation scope: The experiments appear focused on 1D test functions (or at least this is not explicitly clarified). Given the hierarchical interval partitioning, performance and wall-clock cost can degrade quickly with dimension. Without results beyond 1D (e.g., 2–10D standard benchmarks and realistic BO tasks), it is hard to assess practical impact.
- Scalability: DIRECT-style partitioning and SOO variants are known to struggle in higher dimensions because the number of rectangles grows exponentially with D. While the theory already notes λ approaching 1 with D, the paper should discuss practical scalability, engineering considerations (e.g., embedding, additive structure, REMBO-like methods), and provide empirical evidence beyond 1D. DIRECT’s limitations in high D should be explicitly addressed and contrasted with IMGPO’s mechanisms (use of GP UCB pruning, Ngp, Ξ) that could mitigate, or not.
- Practical complexity: Although auxiliary optimization is avoided, step (iii) can be computationally heavy (max of U over a local fully expanded subtree up to depth ξ), and GP hyperparameters are re-estimated each iteration. A complexity analysis (per-iteration and cumulative, including GP updates) and runtime comparisons would strengthen the practical case.
- Deterministic setting: The method and theory target deterministic objectives. Many BO applications are noisy. A discussion (or extension) for noisy observations—e.g., how to adapt step (iii), UCB choices, and proofs—would increase relevance.
- Minor clarity points:
  - It is stated “for brevity” that UCB is used; is UCB essential for the theory/pruning, or could EI/PI or Thompson sampling be substituted with analogous guarantees?
  - The sentence “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds” is vague; please formalize how this simultaneity manifests algorithmically beyond the selection rules in steps (i)–(iii).
  - In the t=9, n=16 example, clarify precisely why the far-right interval is not evaluated and how the GP-based placeholder values are later resolved; a brief invariant or condition list for when GP surrogates are converted to true evaluations would help.

Assessment by review criteria
- Quality: The theory is careful and advances the state of the art; assumptions (unknown semi-metric smoothness) and probability statements are clear. Empirical support as currently presented appears narrow; broader experiments would improve confidence in practical utility and parameter sensitivity (Ξmax, η).
- Clarity: Generally well-written with good figures and pseudocode. A few sentences and the GP surrogate resolution rule could be clarified. Please also standardize notation for depths and sets (T, T′).
- Originality: Strong. The infinite-metric perspective plus GP-based pruning and the removal of δ-cover/auxiliary optimization is novel relative to NIPS and related literature (Srinivas et al.; de Freitas et al.; SOO/BaMSOO; DIRECT).
- Significance: Theoretical significance is high. Practical significance depends on scalability; more evidence on higher-D benchmarks or BO tasks (e.g., hyperparameter tuning) would increase impact.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add experiments in 2–10D (e.g., Branin, Hartmann3/6, Ackley, Rosenbrock, Levy) and at least one real BO task (ML hyperparameter tuning), with wall-clock times and evaluation counts. Compare to GP-UCB/EI with multi-start, DIRECT, SOO, BaMSOO. Include ablations of Ξmax, η, kernel/hyperparameter learning, and sensitivity to misspecified kernels.
- Provide per-iteration complexity and memory costs, and empirical runtimes.
- Discuss high-dimensional strategies (e.g., REMBO, additive models) and show how IMGPO fits as a subroutine; provide a small demonstration if possible.
- Comment on extending to noisy settings and what changes would be needed in the algorithm and analysis.

Pros for acceptance
- Strong and novel theoretical contribution: exponential regret without δ-cover and without auxiliary optimization.
- Clear algorithm design that connects well to established families (DIRECT/SOO and GP-UCB).
- Potentially impactful for deterministic BO where acquisition maximization is costly.

Cons for acceptance
- Empirical evaluation appears limited; lack of higher-dimensional and practical tasks undermines claims of practical utility.
- Scalability concerns are not fully addressed; relation to known limitations of DIRECT-like methods needs deeper discussion and evidence.
- Deterministic-only setting reduces applicability to common noisy BO scenarios.

Recommendation
Weak Accept, contingent on strengthening the experimental section beyond 1D and clarifying scalability and practical complexity. The theoretical advance is solid and of interest to the NIPS community, but broader empirical validation would significantly improve the paper.

Confidence
High on the theory and relation to prior work; medium on empirical assessment due to the limited scope presented.
