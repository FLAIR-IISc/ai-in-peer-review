- Proposal: IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm that attains exponential simple-regret without auxiliary acquisition optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Method: Combines hierarchical partitioning, GP UCBs, and an “infinite-metric” strategy that implicitly considers many semi-metrics and prunes via the GP posterior.  
- Theory: Proves exponential regret O(λN+Ngp) for λ<1 in a deterministic setting under an “unknown bound” assumption, introducing infinite-metric exploration loss.  
- Relation to prior work: Links and improves upon bound-based methods (DIRECT/DOO/SOO) and GP-based BO (GP-UCB/BaMSOO), surpassing BaMSOO’s polynomial-rate guarantee.  

Strengths
- Technical: Nontrivial analysis delivering exponential regret without δ-cover sampling; novel infinite-metric exploration loss concept.  
- Conceptual novelty: Avoids auxiliary acquisition optimization while preserving strong rates; bridges bound-based and Bayesian BO.  
- Clarity: Well written, clear algorithmic description, good positioning within bound-based search.  
- Scope: Generalizes previous exponential-rate results beyond δ-cover sampling and improves theoretical rates under stated assumptions.  

Weaknesses — experiments
- No multiple initial evaluations or multi-start designs; methods run from a single fixed center, limiting statistical validity.  
- Missing comparisons to information-theoretic BO (Entropy Search, Predictive Entropy Search), important baselines for deterministic problems.  
- Benchmarks are only synthetic and low-dimensional (≤6) except for one REMBO example; no real-world tasks.  
- Limited statistical analysis: IMGPO/SOO/BaMSOO lack mean±std/confidence intervals across runs; GP hyperparameter learning adds stochasticity not accounted for.  

Weaknesses — assumptions & scope
- Assumes deterministic objectives; robustness to noise is not evaluated.  
- Exponential-rate constant λ degrades with dimension; little empirical evidence on higher-dimensional scaling or GP update costs.  
- Claims about avoiding acquisition optimization need wall-clock comparisons including GP update time to assess practical savings.  

Quality
- Theory: Solid, original, with clear constants and assumptions.  
- Experiments: Methodology is the weak point due to limited baselines and statistical treatment.  

Clarity
- Exposition and proofs are clear; pseudo-code is helpful.  
- Minor request: discuss numerical stability/complexity of repeated GP updates and use of sparse/rank-one updates.  

Originality
- High: infinite-metric perspective and exponential-rate guarantee without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization are novel.  

Significance
- Potentially significant for deterministic global optimization and unifying bound-based and GP-based BO, but experiments currently undercut practical impact.  

Requests for authors
- Provide sensitivity analysis to kernel choice and hyperparameter learning, and report calibration of η.  
- Add results using multi-point initial designs (e.g., 5–10) and multiple random seeds for all methods.  
- Compare against Entropy Search / Predictive Entropy Search on the same deterministic benchmarks or explain omission.  
- Provide results or discussion on adapting IMGPO to noisy objectives.  
- Include wall-clock comparisons that report GP update time, Ngp, and ρ̄t over time.  

Suggestions to improve
- Add information-theoretic BO baselines (ES, PES) and modern optimizers (e.g., DIRECT, CMA-ES) where appropriate.  
- Use multiple-start initial designs and report mean±std or confidence intervals over ≥20 runs for all methods.  
- Include at least one real-world optimization task (e.g., ML hyperparameter tuning or wet-lab/engineering design).  
- Provide ablations: effect of Ξmax, removing GP to isolate infinite-metric search, and sensitivity to kernel/hyperparameters.  
- Discuss extensions to noisy settings and higher-dimensional strategies (additive models, embeddings).  

Pros for acceptance
- Strong, novel theoretical contribution and clear advancement of understanding between bound-based and GP-based search.  
- Practical algorithmic idea (skipping evaluations via UCB) with potential impact.  

Cons for acceptance
- Empirical evaluation not up to conference standards: limited baselines, no ES/PES, no real tasks, insufficient statistical rigor.  
- Deterministic-only focus with unclear noise robustness.  
- Insufficient evidence on scalability and wall-clock performance.  

Overall score: 5 (marginal; would improve with stronger experiments).
