The proposed method, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer, allowing it to maintain an exponential convergence rate.

The authors note that this requirement is impractical and propose an alternative approach that eliminates the need for δ-cover sampling.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of IMGPO, which simultaneously conducts global and local searches based on all possible candidates of the bounds. The algorithm uses a hierarchical partitioning approach to maintain hyperintervals and selects the interval with the maximum center value among the intervals of the same size. The authors provide a detailed description of the algorithm, including pseudocode, and discuss its relationship to previous algorithms, such as the BaMSOO algorithm [2].

The paper also presents an analysis of the algorithm's convergence rate, which is shown to be exponential. The authors provide a theoretical discussion of the reason why the novel idea underlying IMGPO is beneficial and introduce the concept of infinite-metric exploration loss. The analysis is supported by experimental results, which demonstrate the effectiveness of IMGPO compared to other algorithms.
