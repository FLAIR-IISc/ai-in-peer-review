- Proposes IMGPO: Bayesian optimization achieving exponential convergence for deterministic black-box optimization without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- Core idea: hierarchical partitioning plus search over an infinite family of candidate bounds implied by an unknown semi-metric, using GP UCB only to prune loose candidates.  
- Proves a high-probability exponential regret bound O(λ^{N+N_gp}) with explicit dependence on Hölder parameters (L, α, p) and analyzes ρ_t, N_gp, and pruning depth Ξ.  
- Unifies bound-based partitioning methods (DIRECT/DOO/SOO) and GP-based BO (GP-UCB, EI/PI), improving on BaMSOO by avoiding acquisition optimization and obtaining exponential regret.  
- Empirical results: IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on multimodal benchmarks and REMBO; SOO can win when GP priors are misleading (Rosenbrock2).  
- Theory quality: clear assumptions and theorem; insightful discussion on when GP pruning helps and comparison to single-bound methods.  
- Theoretical limitation: dependence on dimension is explicit (λ→1 as D increases), limiting high-dimensional performance.  
- Missing theoretical clarification: effects of empirical Bayes hyperparameter estimation on the UCB coverage guarantee need discussion.  
- Experimental quality: fair kernel/hyperparameter treatment and code availability; suggests further sensitivity analyses for Ξmax, kernel choice, and η.  
- Experimental gap: recommend ablation showing IMGPO without GP-pruning and adding a GP-UCB baseline with a competent acquisition optimizer.  
- Reproducibility: algorithm fully specified with pseudocode and clear, didactic figures.  
- Clarity: well written and didactic; figures and regret analysis effectively illustrate concepts.  
- Minor presentation issues: redundant phrasing in abstract, misplaced parenthesis in kernel definition, duplicate word in algorithm, and undefined constant C.  
- Suggestion to add: short time-complexity discussion for step (iii) in terms of Ξmax, dimension, and tree size.  
- Originality: novel “infinite-metric” search over bounds with GP pruning, bridging DIRECT/SOO-style partitioning and GP posteriors.  
- Significance: removes practical bottleneck of auxiliary acquisition optimization and provides stronger guarantees for deterministic GP BO, with potential wider impact in planning/RL.  
- Pros: clear original idea, strong theory with exponential regret, convincing experiments, good exposition, and available code.  
- Cons: scaling with dimension remains challenging, UCB robustness under empirical Bayes is unclear, limited sensitivity/ablation studies, and minor writing/notation issues.  
- Detailed reviewer suggestions: clarify hyperparameter learning impact on UCB guarantees, provide complexity bound/guidance for Ξmax, report empirical proxies for ρ̄t and N_gp, and add a GP-UCB+solver baseline.  
- Recommendation: strong accept.
