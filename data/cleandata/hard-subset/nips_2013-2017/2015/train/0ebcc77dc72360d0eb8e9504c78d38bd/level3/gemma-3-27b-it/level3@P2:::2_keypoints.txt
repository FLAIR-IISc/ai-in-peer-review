- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: hierarchical partitioning combined with UCB while considering many possible bounds instead of relying solely on GP-derived bounds.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains motivation, algorithm, theory, and experiments, with a helpful illustrative example.  
- Theoretical analysis (Theorem 1) provides rigorous justification for exponential convergence and analyzes exploration loss vs. GP-bound quality.  
- Novelty: circumvents computational burdens of prior methods and mitigates inaccurate GP predictions by considering multiple bounds.  
- Theoretical rigor: strong analysis showing convergence under reasonable assumptions.  
- Clarity: precise algorithm description and readable presentation despite technical complexity.  
- Good connection to related work (SOO, BaMSOO, DIRECT) and broader literature in global and Lipschitz optimization.  
- Limited empirical evaluation: experiments focus on 1D functions and a single high-dimensional embedding, lacking broader high-dimensional benchmarks.  
- Scalability concerns: hierarchical partitioning may suffer exponential resolution growth with dimensionality; mitigation strategies are under-discussed and comparison to DIRECT’s issues is insufficient.  
- Minor clarifications needed: ambiguous sentence in Section 3.1, unclear reasoning for missing evaluation at n=16 in Figure 1, and whether acquisition functions beyond UCB are compatible.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, well-written, and addresses a key Bayesian optimization challenge.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate empirical validation in high dimensions and significant scalability questions affecting practical applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand high-dimensional experiments, deepen scalability/mitigation discussion, and resolve the minor clarifications.
