- Proposes IMGPO: combines GP UCBs with hierarchical partitioning and an “infinite-metric” hedge to avoid committing to a single smoothness bound.
- Achieves exponential regret decay O(λ^(N+Ngp)) without (i) auxiliary acquisition maximization and (ii) impractical δ-cover sampling.
- Introduces infinite-metric exploration loss ρt to quantify cost of not committing to a single bound and uses GP UCBs to prune loose candidates.
- Analysis isolates roles of Ngp, Ξn, and ρ̄t, explaining when imperfect GP bounds still help.
- Theorem 1 gives exponential rate but λ’s formatting/definition is ambiguous and should be clarified.
- Assumes deterministic objective and unknown Hölder-type semi-metric (standard in optimistic optimization).
- Needs discussion on how empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates affect the UCB high-probability guarantee over the whole run.
- Algorithmic pruning via GP UCBs reduces evaluations; capping ξ with Ξmax controls computational blowups.
- Step (iii) can require up to 3ξ UCB evaluations; Ξmax mitigates this but more practical guidance is needed.
- Experiments use standard multimodal benchmarks and fair comparisons with untuned kernels/hyperparameters.
- Empirically, IMGPO outperforms or matches SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI; SOO can be competitive when GP prior is misleading.
- Wall-clock time comparisons and released code increase credibility.
- Paper is well written, with effective step-by-step figures and detailed pseudocode.
- Minor clarity issues: redundant abstract sentence, misplaced parenthesis in function definition, and λ formatting in Theorem 1.
- Recommend adding a brief glossary of symbols (ρt, ρ̄t, Ξ, Ξmax, Ngp, C) to aid readability.
- The “infinite-metric” concept and GP pruning mechanism are original and unify GP BO with optimistic partitioning.
- Contribution is significant: removes two practical BO pain points while strengthening theoretical guarantees.
- Lacks discussion/extension to noisy observations; applicability to sub-Gaussian noise should be addressed.
- Hyperparameter re-estimation via empirical Bayes needs clarification regarding preservation of UCB guarantees or conservative alternatives.
- High-dimensional scalability degrades with D; REMBO results are encouraging but more ablations isolating embedding effects are needed.
- Request more granular runtime breakdowns (UCB evals, GP updates, partition maintenance) and sensitivity analysis for Ξmax.
- Pros: exponential convergence without δ-cover or acquisition optimization, clear algorithm, strong empirical results, released code.
- Cons: deterministic-only theory, λ ambiguity and minor typos, scalability and computational guidance limited, UCB guarantees under adaptive hyperparameter updates unclear.
- Suggest reporting empirical ρ̄t across functions and a sensitivity analysis for Ξmax and runtime/regret trade-offs.
- Recommendation: Strong accept — principled, practical method with clear theory and convincing experiments.
