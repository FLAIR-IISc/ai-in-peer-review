- Proposes IMGPO: combines a GP posterior with hierarchical partitioning to explore an “infinite” family of continuity bounds, avoiding δ-cover sampling and auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- Algorithm selects and divides hyperrectangles via three criteria: center-value maximality, cross-scale pruning, and GP-UCB screening; supports lazy GP evaluations to avoid unnecessary function calls.  
- Theoretical claim: exponential regret bound O(exp(−c(N+Ngp))) under a Hölder-type local assumption, with constants depending on dimension, partition scheme, and an “infinite-metric exploration loss.”  
- Analysis highlights when GP helps (through Ngp and tighter implied bounds) and when loose UCBs hurt, connecting to near-optimality dimension and DIRECT’s multi-scale intuition.  
- Novelty: systematically uses an infinite family of candidate bounds pruned by GP uncertainty, bridging optimistic partition methods (SOO/DIRECT) and GP-based BO.  
- Empirical results: IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-EI/PI on multiple benchmarks, including a REMBO-based high-dimensional example; code is provided.  
- Improves on prior theory: achieves exponential rates without impractical δ-cover sampling required by earlier GP bandit work and improves over BaMSOO’s polynomial rates.  
- Concern: high-probability guarantees may not be rigorous under adaptive hyperparameter learning; interaction of union bounds with hyperparameter updates needs clarification.  
- Algorithmic presentation: Section 3.2 and pseudocode are clear and implementation-ready; roles of parameters η and Ξmax are explicit.  
- Experimental setup: reasonable (Matern-5/2 kernel, empirical Bayes), with helpful CPU-time reporting; more detailed runtime and memory scaling would be useful.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized with effective illustrative examples; notation (n/N/t, λ) and semi-metric linkage to near-optimality dimension could be tightened.  
- Significance: fills a practical gap in GP-based global optimization with strong rates and is likely to influence partition-based BO and related areas.  
- Weakness — assumptions: the unknown semi-metric / Hölder-type control should be better contextualized for real-world plausibility.  
- Weakness — high dimensionality: theoretical bounds degrade with dimension; REMBO is promising but more ablations on dimensionality and subspace structure are needed.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter learning: empirical Bayes may invalidate strict UCB coverage; suggest adding robustness lemmas or empirical UCB calibration studies.  
- Weakness — sensitivity: sensitivity to Ξmax, η, and kernel misspecification is not explored; recommend ablation removing GP or infinite-metric components.  
- Weakness — noise: method targets deterministic objectives; no analysis or extension to noisy observations is provided.  
- Pros: removes δ-cover and auxiliary acquisition optimization, achieves exponential convergence, provides practical code and strong empirical evidence, and bridges two research lines.  
- Cons: theoretical coverage under adaptive hyperparameter learning is incomplete, scaling with dimension is limited, new tuning parameters require study, and noisy settings are not addressed.  
- Overall: a strong, well-executed contribution with meaningful theoretical and practical advances; minor theoretical clarifications and additional empirical sensitivity analyses would strengthen the paper.
