- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm that avoids auxiliary acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Replaces acquisition optimization with hierarchical rectangular space partitioning (split longest coordinate) and bound-based interval selection.  
- Combines two optimism sources: GP UCB and an unknown tighter semi-metric bound (Assumptions 1–2, Hölder-type smoothness).  
- Uses an “infinite-metric” approach entertaining infinitely many candidate bounds, pruning those looser than the GP UCB.  
- Maintains the best interval per depth, applies GP-UCB–filtered lookahead over horizon Ξ, and splits accepted intervals into three.  
- Can skip function evaluations when a candidate center’s UCB is below the incumbent.  
- Theoretical result: exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1, removing δ-cover and acquisition-maximization requirements.  
- Improves on BaMSOO’s polynomial regret by achieving exponential regret under stated assumptions.  
- Situates method relative to DIRECT, SOO, GP-UCB and de Freitas et al., generalizing unknown-metric ideas with finite-time exponential guarantees.  
- Theory is careful with explicit assumptions and UCB confidence parameterization ensuring high-probability bounds.  
- Analysis transparently discusses dimension dependence (λ → 1 as D increases) and decomposes roles of ρ̄t, Ξ, and Ngp.  
- Caveat: high-probability guarantees require well-calibrated GP posteriors; empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates may violate this in practice.  
- Experiments show strong empirical performance on benchmarks (Branin, Hartmann3, Rosenbrock2) and a 1000-D REMBO-embedded example, with wall-clock savings.  
- Empirical evaluation limited mostly to low-D tasks; only one high-D example uses REMBO embedding.  
- Clarity is generally good with pseudocode and figures, but several points need clearer operational description (depth-wise selection, skipped centers, computational cost, UCB necessity).  
- Originality: high — novel combination of hierarchical partitioning and GP-UCB pruning without δ-cover or acquisition optimization.  
- Significance: strong theoretical contribution; practical impact is promising but limited by partitioning scalability as D grows.  
- Pros: removes auxiliary optimization and δ-cover, principled GP-UCB pruning and skipping, insightful analytical devices (ρt), solid empirical results.  
- Cons: scalability concerns in high D, limited mid/high-D evaluations, reliance on noise-free and well-calibrated GP assumptions, limited practical discussion of DIRECT differences, minor clarity issues.  
- Requests: clarify whether UCB is essential for practice/proofs and what breaks with EI/PI; provide per-iteration complexity and CPU-time breakdown across Ξ settings.  
- Requests (cont.): expand discussion of DIRECT’s high-D degradation and GP-guided pruning’s practical effects; add mid-to-high-D benchmarks or integrate additive/embedding structure.  
- Requests (cont.): evaluate robustness to kernel mis-specification and impact on UCB calibration; discuss extension to noisy observations and whether exponential rates persist under sub-Gaussian noise.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel and theoretically strong contribution with practical caveats on scalability and evaluation breadth.
