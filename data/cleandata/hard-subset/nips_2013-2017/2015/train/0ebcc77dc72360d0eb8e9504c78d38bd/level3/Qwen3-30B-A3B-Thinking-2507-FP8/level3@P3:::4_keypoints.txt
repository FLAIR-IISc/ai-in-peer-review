- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Key idea: "infinite-metric exploration" — consider all bounds from an unknown semi-metric rather than a single GP-based bound.  
- Theoretical result: provides a regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, improving on polynomial bounds like BaMSOO.  
- Bridges gap between single-bound methods (e.g., GP-UCB) and multi-bound approaches, formalizing practical GP-based infinite-metric optimization.  
- Builds on prior work (GP-UCB, SOO) while addressing their limitations.  
- Strength: exceptionally clear, didactic writing and effective use of Figure 1 to explain hierarchical partitioning.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis with helpful remarks clarifying effects of UCB tightness, GP usage, and infinite-metric optimization.  
- Strength: strong experimental validation showing IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on benchmarks.  
- Quality: technically sound with proofs in supplementary material; acknowledges scalability limitations in higher dimensions.  
- Clarity: overall excellent organization and reproducibility, though GP-specific components may be hard for non-experts.  
- Originality: novel contribution via infinite-metric exploration concept and clear distinction from prior methods.  
- Significance: practical method achieving exponential convergence without impractical sampling, with potential impact beyond optimization.  
- Weakness: minor technical/typo issues (redundant abstract formulation, misplaced parenthesis) that affect presentation.  
- Weakness: reviewer not a GP/optimization expert, so some technical evaluations are educated guesses; more GP detail would help readers.
