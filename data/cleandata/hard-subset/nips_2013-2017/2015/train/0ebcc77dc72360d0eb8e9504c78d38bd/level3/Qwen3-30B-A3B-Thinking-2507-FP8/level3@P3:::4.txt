

# Review of "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence"

This paper proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Gaussian process (GP)-based global optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling. The authors address a gap in the literature identified by Wang et al. [2], who noted that existing GP-based methods with exponential convergence rely on impractical δ-cover sampling. The key insight is to consider all possible bounds encoded by an unknown semi-metric rather than relying on a single GP-based bound, which the authors formalize as "infinite-metric exploration." The paper provides a regret bound of O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, which is superior to the polynomial regret bound of BaMSOO [2] and other existing methods.

The paper builds on previous work in Bayesian optimization, particularly GP-UCB [1] and SOO [18], while addressing their limitations. The authors position their work as bridging the gap between bound-based methods (which rely on a single bound) and methods that consider multiple bounds (which have been shown to be theoretically advantageous but not practically implemented with GP-based optimization).

**Strengths:**
The paper is exceptionally well-written and didactic, making complex concepts accessible to readers. The authors effectively use Figure 1 to explain the algorithm's procedure, which significantly aids understanding of the hierarchical partitioning process. The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides clear insights into why the proposed approach works, particularly in Remarks 1-3, which explain the effects of UCB tightness, GP usage, and infinite-metric optimization. The experimental results convincingly demonstrate that IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI across multiple benchmark functions, with the authors providing a thorough explanation of the performance differences. The paper is well-organized, with a logical progression from problem statement to theoretical analysis to experimental validation.

**Weaknesses:**
The paper contains minor technical errors that affect the quality of presentation. Specifically, the abstract formulation appears redundant (line 49), and there is a misplaced parenthesis in a function definition (line 72). While these are minor issues, they detract from the paper's professionalism. Additionally, the reviewer, while familiar with machine learning, is not an expert in Gaussian processes or global optimization, so the evaluation of certain technical details is necessarily an educated guess. The paper would benefit from more detailed explanations of the GP-based components for readers less familiar with this specific domain.

**Evaluation Criteria:**

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound with well-supported theoretical claims. The regret bound is rigorously proven (with proofs in supplementary material), and the experimental results convincingly support the theoretical claims. The authors are careful to discuss both strengths (exponential convergence) and limitations (scalability challenges in higher dimensions). The minor technical errors slightly reduce the quality but do not undermine the main contributions.

**Clarity:** The paper excels in clarity. The writing is precise, the organization is logical, and the graphical explanation of the algorithm (Figure 1) is particularly effective. The authors provide sufficient detail for experts to reproduce the results. The main weakness in clarity relates to the GP-specific content, which might be challenging for readers without a strong background in Gaussian processes.

**Originality:** The paper makes a clear and significant contribution by introducing the infinite-metric exploration concept, which is novel in the context of GP-based optimization. The authors clearly distinguish their work from previous approaches like GP-UCB [1] and BaMSOO [2], and the theoretical analysis demonstrates how their approach differs fundamentally from existing methods. This represents a meaningful advancement in the field.

**Significance:** The results are highly significant as they provide a practical method for Bayesian optimization with exponential convergence, which has been a long-standing theoretical goal. The approach addresses a practical limitation (δ-cover sampling) while maintaining theoretical guarantees. The paper's insights about not relying on a single bound could influence other bound-based methods beyond optimization, potentially impacting fields like reinforcement learning (as noted in the conclusion). The experimental results demonstrate clear practical advantages over existing methods.

**Recommendation:** I recommend acceptance. The paper makes a strong theoretical contribution with practical implications, is well-written, and provides convincing experimental validation. The minor technical errors are easily fixable and do not detract from the paper's overall quality and significance. The work represents a meaningful advancement in Bayesian optimization that aligns well with NIPS's focus on theoretical and practical contributions to machine learning.

**Pro and Con for Acceptance:**
- Pro: Novel theoretical approach with clear practical advantages, rigorous analysis, strong experimental validation, well-written with effective visual explanations.
- Con: Minor technical errors in the manuscript, and some GP-specific content might be challenging for readers without deep expertise in this domain (though the paper is generally well-explained).
