- Introduces IMGPO, a BO algorithm claiming exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.  
- Frames IMGPO within bound-based search, connecting to Lipschitz optimization, A* search, and PAC-MDP.  
- Core idea: exploit an unknown smoothness bound and concurrently explore multiple candidate bounds guided by a GP prior.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear motivation, logical algorithm description, and detailed theoretical analysis.  
- Thorough literature discussion and publicly available code for reproducibility.  
- Experiments lack multiple initial ("warm-up") evaluations, risking results driven by random initialization and preventing robust statistical comparisons.  
- Does not compare to contemporary state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., Entropy Search, newer GP/deep-GP approaches), a major empirical omission.  
- Evaluation is limited to synthetic functions (≤6D), with no real-world or practical optimization tasks to demonstrate applicability or scalability.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong, but scalability to higher dimensions is not fully addressed.  
- Conclusion: theoretically interesting contribution, but weak experimental validation lowers confidence and the reviewer’s score; recommends adding multiple initializations, SOTA baselines, and real-world benchmarks.
