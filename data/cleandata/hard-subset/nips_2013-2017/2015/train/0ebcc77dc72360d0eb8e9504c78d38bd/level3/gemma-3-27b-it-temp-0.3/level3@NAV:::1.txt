## Review of “Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence”

This paper introduces a novel Bayesian optimization (BO) algorithm, termed Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), designed to achieve exponential convergence without relying on auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling – limitations of existing approaches like Freitas et al. (2012). The authors position their work within the broader context of bound-based search methods, highlighting the importance of tight bounds and proposing a strategy to circumvent the need for a precise, known bound by considering a wider range of possibilities. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly structured. The motivation is well-articulated, the algorithm is explained in a logical manner, and the theoretical analysis is presented with sufficient detail. The connection to existing literature on Bayesian optimization, Lipschitz optimization, and bound-based search is effectively established. The authors correctly identify the challenges with δ-cover sampling and the need for algorithms that can achieve exponential convergence without it. The inclusion of pseudocode enhances reproducibility.

However, despite the strong theoretical contributions and clear presentation, the experimental evaluation significantly weakens the paper. The most critical flaw is the absence of multiple initial evaluations of the objective function *f*. Standard practice in Bayesian optimization involves running the algorithm from several random starting points to obtain statistically meaningful results and account for the inherent stochasticity of the GP. Without this, the reported performance may be overly optimistic or misleading. 

Furthermore, the paper lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art BO methods, such as those employing information-theoretic acquisition functions like Entropy Search. While comparisons to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI are included, the field has progressed beyond these, and a more comprehensive benchmark is expected. The exclusive use of synthetic functions, limited to a maximum dimension of 6, also limits the generalizability of the findings. Real-world evaluations, even on a single practical problem (e.g., hyperparameter tuning or a simple lab experiment), would substantially strengthen the paper.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical contribution: achieves exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.
*   Excellent clarity and presentation.
*   Well-motivated and positioned within the relevant literature.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Weak experimental evaluation: lacks multiple initializations and comparisons to modern BO methods.
*   Limited to synthetic functions and low dimensionality.
*   Experimental results do not convincingly demonstrate superior performance in a statistically robust manner.



**Overall Score:** 5 (lowered due to experimental weaknesses)

I recommend acceptance with major revisions. The authors should address the experimental shortcomings by incorporating multiple initializations, comparing to stronger baselines, and including at least one real-world evaluation. Addressing these points would elevate the paper to a standard suitable for publication at a competitive conference like NIPS.
