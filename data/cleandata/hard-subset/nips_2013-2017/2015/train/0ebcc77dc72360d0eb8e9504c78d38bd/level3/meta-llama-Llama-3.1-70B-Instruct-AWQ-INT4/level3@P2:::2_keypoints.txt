- Introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence.  
- Uses space/interval partitioning and UCB to generate samples, avoiding internal global optimization.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example, making implementation straightforward.  
- Theoretical analysis proves exponential-regret improvements over prior work.  
- Novel contribution: considers all possible upper bounds instead of a single estimated bound (Remarks 1–3).  
- Practical concern: interval-partitioning requires resolution that grows exponentially with dimensionality.  
- Scalability to higher dimensions is not addressed and may incur prohibitive computational costs.  
- Experiments are limited to 1D test functions; no higher-dimensional empirical validation is provided.  
- Claim that IMGPO can scale via being a subroutine is not demonstrated experimentally.  
- Related to DIRECT, which is known to perform poorly in high dimensions—this connection needs deeper discussion.  
- Unclear whether UCB is required or if other acquisition criteria (e.g., EI) could be used.  
- Ambiguous phrasing about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor unclear point: unexplained absence of function evaluation in the far-right region at n=16.  
- Overall: sound and novel theoretical contribution but limited practical evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Reviewer score: 7/10.
