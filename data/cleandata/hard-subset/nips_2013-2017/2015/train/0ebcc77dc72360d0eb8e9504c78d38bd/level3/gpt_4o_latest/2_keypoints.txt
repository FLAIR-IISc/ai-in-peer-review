- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ‑cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.
- Uses interval/space partitioning with GP priors and UCB for sample selection.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and derives an exponential regret bound, improving over GP‑UCB and BaMSOO.
- Algorithm is clearly described with detailed pseudocode and an illustrative example aiding comprehension.
- Theoretical remarks explain benefits of considering infinitely many possible bounds and show strong theoretical rigor.
- Eliminating auxiliary optimization reduces computational overhead, improving practical applicability.
- Experiments are limited to mainly 1D test functions, raising concerns about empirical validation.
- Scalability is questionable due to interval partitioning and similarity to DIRECT, which struggles in high dimensions.
- Paper lacks concrete discussion or empirical strategies for scaling to higher-dimensional problems.
- Only UCB acquisition is used; it is unclear whether other acquisition functions (e.g., EI) can be integrated.
- Some statements need clearer explanations to improve accessibility for non-expert readers.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, significant exponential regret improvement, and clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental evaluation and unresolved scalability concerns for real-world high-dimensional tasks.
- Suggested improvements: extend experiments to higher dimensions and real applications, discuss scalability strategies (e.g., assumptions or dimensionality reduction), clarify acquisition function generality, and improve clarity of certain statements.
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution worth accepting if authors address scalability concerns and clarify minor points.
