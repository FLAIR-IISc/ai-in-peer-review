- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Addresses a key limitation of prior work, offering a more practical route to exponential regret minimization.  
- Theoretical contribution connects to Lipschitz optimization, A* search, PAC‑MDP, and builds on Munos (2012) and Wang et al. (2013).  
- Novel idea: consider all possible bounds simultaneously and use a GP prior to prune unlikely candidates.  
- Demonstrates clear improvement over existing polynomial regret bounds.  
- Paper is exceptionally well‑written, well‑organized, and includes a lucid algorithm description and illustrative figure.  
- Relationship to prior algorithms (BaMSOO, SOO) is clearly articulated and analysis is rigorous.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, discussing scalability issues and future directions.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: no multiple initial random evaluations (non‑standard BO practice).  
- Lacks comparisons to modern/state‑of‑the‑art BO methods (e.g., information‑theoretic approaches like Entropy Search).  
- Experiments are limited to synthetic functions up to 6 dimensions and include no real‑world problems.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on improving experiments.  
- Requested experimental fixes: include multiple initializations, compare against a broader set of BO baselines, and add at least one real‑world benchmark.  
- Overall score: 6 — Accept with Minor Revisions.
