- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm combining GP UCBs with hierarchical partitioning to consider an infinite family of bounds for global optimization of deterministic black-box functions.
- Claims to solve the open problem of achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary non-convex acquisition optimization or impractical δ-cover sampling.
- Provides theoretical analysis showing an exponential regret bound and explains interaction between GP bounds and infinite-metric search (Remarks 1-3).
- Empirically outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and standard GP methods with EI/PI on a range of benchmark functions.
- Novel and significant: bridges GP-based optimization with infinite-metric hierarchical search (DIRECT/SOO), addressing a well-defined problem.
- Strong technical contribution: first exponential regret bound for a practical GP-based algorithm of this type; insightful theoretical remarks.
- Excellent clarity and presentation: well-written, intuitive algorithm description, strong figures (notably Figure 1) aiding comprehension.
- Minor wording redundancy in the abstract that could be tightened for conciseness.
- Possible typo/error in Theorem 1’s definition of λ that conflicts with the claimed exponential form and needs correction.
- Scalability discussion could be expanded—behavior in moderately high dimensions (D ≈ 20–50) without embeddings remains unclear.
- Overall assessment: technically solid, empirically validated, and clearly presented work that addresses a key open problem; strong recommendation for acceptance.
