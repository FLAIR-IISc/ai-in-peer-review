- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method using an unknown semi-metric and simultaneous consideration of multiple candidate bounds to balance global and local search.
- Core idea: leverage tighter bounds than the GP prior via an unknown semi-metric and maintain multiple bounds to avoid auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Theoretical contribution: proof (Theorem 1) of exponential convergence (regret bound) for the method.
- Remarks analyze how GP prior tightness affects behavior and limitations.
- Algorithm is illustrated with a clear hierarchical partitioning visualization (Figure 1), aiding comprehension.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and didactic, accessible to readers less familiar with BO and GPs.
- Strong empirical validation: IMGPO consistently outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on benchmark functions.
- Demonstrates potential scalability via a 1000-dimensional test combined with REMBO, though further study is needed.
- Authors honestly acknowledge scalability concerns in high dimensions and propose future work directions.
- Minor issues: abstract contains redundant formulation that could be removed for concision.
- Minor typographical error: misplaced parenthesis on line 72.
- Missing quantitative analysis: limited discussion/measurement of computational and memory costs from considering multiple bounds and the impact of Ξmax.
- Recommendation: strongly recommended for acceptance; weaknesses are minor and not grounds for rejection.
