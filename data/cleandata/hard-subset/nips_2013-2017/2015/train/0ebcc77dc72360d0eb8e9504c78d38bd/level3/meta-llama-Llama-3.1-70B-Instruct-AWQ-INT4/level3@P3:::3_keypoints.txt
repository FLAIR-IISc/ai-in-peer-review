- Introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Leverages an unknown bound encoded by continuity at the global optimizer and considers infinitely many candidate bounds for robustness.  
- Main contributions are clearly and coherently presented, with strong high-level intuition in Section 3.2.  
- Provides programmatic details in Section 3.3 that facilitate implementation and replication.  
- Experimental results (Section 5) demonstrate effectiveness and compare favorably to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis shows an exponential regret bound in the number of function evaluations.  
- Use of Gaussian Processes (GP) reduces regret by increasing Ngp and decreasing ρ̄t.  
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and sufficient for an expert reader to reproduce results.  
- Novelty: combines existing Bayesian optimization ideas in a new way; infinite-candidate bounds are a unique aspect.  
- Significance: addresses a difficult problem, handles non-convex black-box functions, and offers improved convergence rates.  
- Quality: technically sound with well-supported claims and honest evaluation of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, efficient approach with strong theoretical and empirical support and clear exposition.  
- Argument against acceptance: assumes deterministic objectives, which may limit real-world applicability.  
- Argument against acceptance: potential degradation in high-dimensional spaces, as acknowledged by the authors.  
- Argument against acceptance: considering infinitely many bound candidates could increase computational complexity.
