- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. and Wang et al., addressing impractical δ‑cover sampling and auxiliary optimization costs in prior work.  
- Leverages a semi‑metric assumption to consider infinitely many possible upper bounds instead of a single GP‑estimated bound.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves exponential regret bound O(λ^{N+Ngp}) with λ<1, improving on BaMSOO’s polynomial bound.  
- Analysis shows how GP‑bound tightness affects performance and that even a “useless” GP bound can be beneficial within the infinite‑metric scheme.  
- Proofs are technically sound and provided in the supplementary material.  
- Experimental evaluation on eight benchmarks shows consistent improvements over SOO, BaMSOO, GP‑PI, and GP‑EI, especially on multimodal problems.  
- Authors acknowledge scalability limits: λ approaches 1 as dimensionality increases, indicating challenges in very high dimensions.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well‑structured, with effective visualizations and well‑documented pseudocode.  
- Original idea is novel (infinite bounds) while drawing inspiration from DIRECT and SOO; clearly distinguished from prior art.  
- Significance: removes two major practical barriers, making exponential convergence more attainable and informing future BO research.  
- Strengths summarized: strong theory, practical algorithm, excellent exposition, comprehensive experiments, thoughtful analysis.  
- Weaknesses summarized: minor technical errors, assumes GP familiarity, limited discussion of very high‑dimensional performance, could use more intuitive explanations.  
- Reviewer recommendation: paper is well‑positioned for acceptance at NIPS given its theoretical and empirical merits.
