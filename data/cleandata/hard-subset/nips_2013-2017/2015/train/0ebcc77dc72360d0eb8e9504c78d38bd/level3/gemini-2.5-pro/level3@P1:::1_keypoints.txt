- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence.
- Removes the need for an auxiliary non-convex optimization step and the impractical δ-cover sampling used in prior exponential-convergence work.
- Combines hierarchical partitioning methods (e.g., SOO) with Gaussian Process probabilistic bounds and prunes an infinite set of semi-metrics using GP-UCB.
- Directly addresses an open problem (Wang et al., 2014) and provides theoretical analysis with improved regret bounds.
- Strength: original and significant theoretical contribution bridging bound-based global optimization and GP methods.
- Strength: paper is very clear, well-organized, and well-written with helpful illustrations and insightful remarks.
- Strength: practical potential — removing the inner optimization loop could make the method faster and broadly useful.
- Weakness: experimental methodology is weak — deterministic methods use single runs and initialization protocol is insufficient for robust comparison.
- Weakness: comparisons omit stronger, contemporary baselines (e.g., Entropy Search, Predictive Entropy Search).
- Weakness: lack of real-world application experiments; synthetic benchmarks only and the 1000-D test doesn’t demonstrate IMGPO’s standalone scalability.
- Overall: compelling theoretical advance but empirical validation is insufficient; acceptance would require more rigorous and comprehensive experiments.
