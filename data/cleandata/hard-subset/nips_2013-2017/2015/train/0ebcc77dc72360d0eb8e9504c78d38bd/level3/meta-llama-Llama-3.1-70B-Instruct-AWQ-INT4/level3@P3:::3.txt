The paper "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence" presents a novel approach to Bayesian optimization that achieves exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors introduce the Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO) algorithm, which leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer. This approach allows for the consideration of infinitely many possible candidates of the bounds, leading to a more robust and efficient optimization method.

The main contributions of the paper are clearly and coherently presented in the main section. The authors provide a strong illustrative example in Section 3.2, which conveys high-level intuition about the algorithm's behavior. The programmatic details in Section 3.3 facilitate implementation and replication of the algorithm. The experimental results in Section 5 demonstrate the method's effectiveness and compare it to other state-of-the-art algorithms.

The paper integrates ideas from the Bayesian optimization literature, both with and without continuity estimation. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's regret bound, which is shown to be exponential in the number of function evaluations. The use of GP is also shown to reduce the regret bound by increasing Ngp and decreasing ρ̄t.
