## Review of “Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence”

This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), achieving exponential convergence without relying on δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization – both significant limitations of existing approaches like those by de Freitas et al. [1]. The core idea is to circumvent the need for a tight Lipschitz constant by simultaneously considering a range of possible bounds, leveraging the GP to prune implausible candidates. 

The paper is well-structured and clearly articulates its contributions. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and positions IMGPO within the broader context of Bayesian optimization and global optimization. Section 3.2 provides an excellent illustrative example, making the somewhat abstract concept of infinite-metric optimization surprisingly intuitive. The detailed algorithmic description in 3.3 is a significant strength, facilitating both understanding and potential replication. The experimental results, presented in Section 5, are compelling, demonstrating IMGPO’s effectiveness across a variety of benchmark functions and comparing favorably to established methods like SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-EI. 

The authors skillfully integrate ideas from both sides of the Bayesian optimization literature – those focused on continuity estimation and those relying on explicit Lipschitz bounds. The theoretical analysis, while dense, establishes an exponential convergence rate and provides insights into the interplay between the GP prior, the unknown semi-metric, and the algorithm’s performance. The discussion of limitations and potential future work, particularly regarding scalability to higher dimensions, demonstrates intellectual honesty and a thoughtful consideration of the broader research landscape.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Addresses a significant open problem in Bayesian optimization – achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.
*   **Clarity:** Exceptionally well-written and organized, with a strong pedagogical approach.
*   **Technical Soundness:** Rigorous theoretical analysis supports the claimed convergence rate.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Compelling experimental results demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.
*   **Reproducibility:** Publicly available code enhances the paper’s impact.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Scalability:** The analysis highlights potential challenges in scaling to very high dimensions, though the authors acknowledge this.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The impact of Ξmax, while explored, could benefit from a more in-depth sensitivity analysis.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   IMGPO represents a significant advancement in Bayesian optimization, addressing a key limitation of existing methods.
*   The paper is technically sound, clearly written, and well-supported by both theoretical analysis and experimental results.
*   The code release promotes reproducibility and further research.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The scalability concerns, while acknowledged, may limit the immediate applicability of the method to certain problem domains.



**Overall:** This is a strong contribution to the NIPS community. The paper is of high quality, addresses an important problem, and presents a novel and effective solution. I recommend acceptance.
