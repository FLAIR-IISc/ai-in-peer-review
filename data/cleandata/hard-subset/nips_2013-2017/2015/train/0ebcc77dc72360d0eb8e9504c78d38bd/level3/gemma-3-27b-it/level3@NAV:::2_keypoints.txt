- Proposes IMGPO, a BO method claiming exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Core idea: hierarchical partitioning combined with UCB to implicitly consider many Lipschitz constants via interval division.
- Algorithm is clearly described and accompanied by an illustrative example.
- Builds on prior BO work and addresses practical implementation issues noted by Wang et al.
- Avoids potentially intractable δ-cover sampling, a notable practical advantage.
- Provides theoretical analysis demonstrating exponential regret improvements.
- Acknowledges relation to DIRECT but does not explore this connection in depth.
- Strengths: novel approach, clear presentation, solid theoretical grounding, and elimination of auxiliary optimization.
- Main weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to 1D tests only.
- Scalability concerns: interval partitioning may incur exponential resolution growth in higher dimensions, similar to DIRECT.
- Recommendation: authors should discuss scalability and consider dimensionality reduction or adaptive partitioning strategies.
- Unclear whether other acquisition functions (e.g., Expected Improvement) can be integrated; "global and local searches" statement is ambiguous.
- Needs clarification on why no function evaluation occurred in the far-right region at n=16.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical guarantee and practical contribution with clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical validation and inadequate discussion of scalability and related-method limitations.
- Overall score: 7/10 — solid theoretical contribution but limited impact without higher-dimensional experiments.
