- Presents IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: use an unknown semi-metric for smoothness and consider all possible bounds via hierarchical partitioning.  
- Builds on BO literature, bridging continuity-estimation and no-continuity approaches, and improves over prior work like BaMSOO.  
- Main contribution: exponential convergence rate with a practical algorithm.  
- Section 3.2 provides a strong illustrative example clarifying the multiple-bounds intuition.  
- Section 3.3 gives sufficient implementation details for replication.  
- Experimental results show IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and standard GP methods on benchmarks.  
- Theoretical analysis is dense but well-structured and supports the exponential convergence claim.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and discuss scalability to higher dimensions as future work.  
- Minor weakness: assumption of a square search domain (mitigable by scaling).  
- Minor weakness: limited discussion of parameter sensitivity (e.g., Ξmax) and practical guidance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel solution to key BO limitations, rigorous theory, strong empirical validation, clear exposition, and significant contribution to BO.  
- Arguments against acceptance: square-domain assumption may limit applicability; needs deeper analysis of parameter sensitivity.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper and recommended for acceptance.
