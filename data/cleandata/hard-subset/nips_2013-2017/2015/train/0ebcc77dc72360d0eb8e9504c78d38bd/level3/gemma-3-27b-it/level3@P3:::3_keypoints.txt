- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.
- Core idea: consider an infinite set of potential bounds encoded by an unknown semi-metric, blending global and local search.
- Builds on and integrates prior GP continuity estimation and relaxed Lipschitz methods, situating well within existing BO literature.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous: Theorem 1 proves exponential convergence; assumptions and detailed remarks are clear and justified.
- Paper is very clear and well-organized; Figure 1, Section 3.2, and Algorithm 1 aid intuition and reproducibility.
- Originality: novel simultaneous exploration of multiple bounds without requiring a specific Lipschitz constant.
- Significance: removes the δ-cover sampling bottleneck, offers improved regret bounds, and shows compelling empirical performance.
- Scalability concern: potential issues in high dimensions as parameter λ approaches 1, limiting practical applicability.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance depends on Ξmax; lacks extensive sensitivity analysis or adaptive selection strategies.
- Computational cost: slower than SOO per Table 1, though authors claim it is faster than traditional GP methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a key limitation, strong theory, clear algorithm, and convincing experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: high-dimensional scalability, higher computational cost, and insufficient hyperparameter analysis.
- Overall: high-quality, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address hyperparameter sensitivity and discuss scalability further.
