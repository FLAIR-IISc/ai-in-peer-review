Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes Infinite-Metric Gaussian Process Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian global optimization algorithm that attains an exponential convergence (simple regret) rate without requiring δ-cover sampling and without auxiliary optimization of an acquisition function. The method blends hierarchical partition-based optimistic search (in the spirit of SOO/Munos and DIRECT/Jones) with GP-derived UCBs, but explicitly avoids committing to a single smoothness metric. Instead, it assumes there exists some unknown semi-metric controlling local variability near a global optimizer and explores over an infinite family of candidate bounds, while using the GP posterior to prune candidates that are provably looser.

This work directly builds on and contrasts with: (i) GP-UCB (Srinivas et al., ICML’10) and practical BO with GP-EI/PI (Snoek et al., NIPS’12), which require solving an auxiliary nonconvex acquisition optimization; (ii) de Freitas et al. (ICML’12) who derive exponential rates under deterministic observations but rely on δ-cover sampling; and (iii) SOO (Munos, NIPS’11) and BaMSOO (Wang et al., AISTATS’14), which avoid δ-covers but only achieve polynomial rates. The paper also connects to high-dimensional BO via REMBO (Wang et al., IJCAI’13). IMGPO is positioned as resolving the open problem identified by Wang et al.: achieving exponential regret in GP-based BO without δ-covers.

Main ideas:
- Replace reliance on a single (possibly loose) bound with simultaneous consideration of infinitely many candidate semi-metrics around the optimum (Assumption 1), formalized via a hierarchical partition scheme.
- Use GP-UCB only to screen out inferior candidates/intervals, not as a sole driver; avoid auxiliary acquisition optimization by operating directly on partition centers with a tri-section rule.
- Analyze the resulting algorithm via the “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt, yielding a regret bound rN = O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1 under mild Hölder-type assumptions (Assumption 2), elucidating how GP usage reduces ρ̄t and increases Ngp to sharpen the bound.

Strengths:
- Conceptual novelty: principled “infinite-metric” optimism that leverages GP information without committing to a potentially mis-specified smoothness metric; a clear unification of bound-based global optimization and BO.
- Theoretical contribution: an exponential simple regret bound without δ-covers and without needing known Lipschitz/Hölder constants; analysis clarifies roles of L, α, p, dimension D, and ρ̄t, and provides insight through well-articulated remarks.
- Algorithmic clarity: Section 3.2 provides strong intuition; Section 3.3 offers pseudocode and sufficient detail to reimplement; the UCB choice with a union bound is clearly motivated.
- Empirical support: Broad comparisons (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI/EI) across multiple benchmarks, including a REMBO setting for high ambient dimension; IMGPO is generally superior or competitive, and avoids the expensive inner optimization typical of BO.
- Quality of writing: Clear, coherent, and well organized.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- Assumptions and constants: Theorems depend on a Hölder-type condition around the optimum; while weaker than known-Lipschitz, this is still a smoothness assumption. The form of λ in Theorem 1 appears typographically ambiguous (e.g., “λ = 3 − α2CDρ̄t”); please clarify the exact exponent/log factor and constants.
- Practical validity of UCB: Guarantees rely on f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with high probability while hyperparameters are re-estimated online via empirical Bayes. This can break standard confidence bounds under mis-specification. Please discuss how UCB validity is maintained with hyperparameter learning, and consider reporting robustness to mis-specified kernels (beyond the Rosenbrock example) and to misspecified lengthscales.
- Computational complexity: Step (iii) may compute up to O(3Ξmax) UCBs; while Ξmax caps this, practical guidance/ablation on Ξmax is limited. Include runtime scaling with D, N, and Ξmax, and sensitivity plots for Ξmax across several problems.
- Fairness of baselines: GP-PI/EI are solved by SOO plus a local optimizer; state-of-the-art practice often uses multi-start L-BFGS or DIRECT/CMA-ES for acquisition optimization. Consider including a stronger acquisition optimizer and/or GP-UCB with multi-start to ensure fair comparison, and include DIRECT as a non-GP baseline given the algorithmic kinship.
- Noise: The current theory and experiments consider deterministic objectives. Many BO applications are noisy. Please discuss and, if possible, provide a variant or analysis for sub-Gaussian noise, or at least an empirical test under moderate noise.
- High-dimensionality: The analysis notes λ→1 as D grows. The REMBO experiment is encouraging, but IMGPO alone remains limited in high D. It would help to articulate when IMGPO should be paired with embedding/additive structure and whether the theory can extend to those cases.
- Minor issues: Some notational inconsistencies and typos in Algorithm 1 and theorem statements (e.g., indexation in line 23; “Ξ← Ξ + 22”; missing exponents; grammar). Also, the code URL appears non-anonymized, which may compromise double-blind review.

Quality:
Technically sound overall, with a meaningful proof strategy and careful discussion of constants/assumptions. Some clarifications of constants, UCB validity under hyperparameter learning, and computational overhead would further strengthen the work.

Clarity:
Well written and organized. The illustrative 1D example, algorithm description, and remarks significantly aid understanding. A notation table and a brief complexity section would improve readability.

Originality:
High. The infinite-metric perspective that systematically blends GP-derived pruning with partition-based optimism to avoid δ-covers and auxiliary optimization is novel and addresses a recognized gap.

Significance:
High. Removing δ-cover and auxiliary optimization while achieving exponential regret is theoretically impactful and practically relevant. The method should influence both BO and deterministic global optimization communities.

Pros for acceptance:
- Resolves an open problem: exponential-rate GP-based BO without δ-covers or acquisition optimization.
- Strong theory with clear insights; broadly useful analysis framework.
- Compelling empirical performance and practical algorithmic details.

Cons for acceptance:
- Ambiguities/typos in key theorem constants; limited discussion on UCB validity with hyperparameter learning.
- Limited treatment of noisy objectives and high-D scaling without embeddings.
- Baseline optimization for PI/EI may be weaker than standard practice; runtime analysis could be expanded.
- Potential anonymity issue with code URL.

Questions for authors:
1) Please clarify the exact expression for λ in Theorem 1 and all constants C, β, and their dependence on D and partitioning.
2) How do you preserve valid GP-UCB confidence bounds while re-estimating hyperparameters each iteration? Any empirical evaluation under kernel mis-specification?
3) What is the worst-case and typical computational cost of step (iii) as a function of Ξmax and D? Can you provide wall-clock comparisons versus BaMSOO and well-optimized GP-UCB/PI/EI?
4) Can the analysis extend to sub-Gaussian noise? If not, what breaks?
5) Could you include ablations: IMGPO without GP (to illustrate Remark 2) and with alternative division schemes discussed in the supplement?

Overall recommendation:
Strong accept. The paper presents a principled and practical algorithm with an exponential convergence guarantee that addresses a recognized gap in BO, is clearly written, and is validated empirically. Addressing the clarifications and minor issues above would further enhance the final version.
