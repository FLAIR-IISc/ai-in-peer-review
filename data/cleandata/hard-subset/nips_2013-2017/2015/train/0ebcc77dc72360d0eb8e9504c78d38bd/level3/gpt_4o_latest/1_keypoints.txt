- Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. (2012) and generalizes bound-based search methods with a theoretically grounded approach.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including exponential regret bounds that improve on BaMSOO’s polynomial bounds.  
- Novel approach: considers infinitely many possible bounds and leverages GP priors to reduce the search space.  
- Well-written and clearly organized, with effective positioning within global and Bayesian optimization literature.  
- Offers insights into trade-offs between using a specific bound versus multiple bounds, with practical implications for future work.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: tests only on synthetic functions up to 6 dimensions, limiting generalizability.  
- Lacks comparisons with state-of-the-art Bayesian Optimization methods (e.g., Entropy Search, advanced GP-UCB variants).  
- Experiments do not include multiple initial evaluations of f, preventing meaningful statistical analysis of variability.  
- Scalability concerns for higher dimensions are noted but not empirically addressed or mitigated.  
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution but needs improved experimental rigor, real-world validation, and robust statistical analysis.  
- Final score 5/10; suitable for a theory-focused audience but requires significant experimental improvement for broader impact.
