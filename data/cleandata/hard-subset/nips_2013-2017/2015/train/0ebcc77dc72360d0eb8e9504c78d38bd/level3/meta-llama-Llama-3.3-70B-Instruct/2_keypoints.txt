- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Uses space partitioning and GP prior information to reduce unknownness of the semi-metric.  
- Provides a clear algorithm description, illustrative example, and source code for reproducibility.  
- Offers a thorough theoretical analysis, including infinite-metric exploration loss and the effect of GP UCB tightness.  
- Experimental results show IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on tested functions.  
- Computational-time comparison: IMGPO is much faster than traditional GP methods but slower than SOO.  
- Major limitation: experiments are limited to 1D functions, raising scalability concerns for higher dimensions.  
- Authors acknowledge scalability issues and suggest additional assumptions (e.g., from prior work) may be needed.  
- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with sufficient details to reproduce results.  
- Could benefit from more discussion of practical applications and limitations.  
- Minor clarifications requested, such as clearer use of UCB and explanation of certain statements.
