This paper introduces IMGPO, a novel global optimization algorithm based on Gaussian Processes (GPs). The central contribution is an algorithm that achieves an exponential convergence rate without relying on the impractical delta-cover sampling procedure required by prior work with similar theoretical guarantees, namely Freitas et al. (2012). The work is well-contextualized, positioning itself as a solution to an open problem identified by Wang et al. (2012), whose BaMSOO algorithm achieved only polynomial regret after removing the delta-cover requirement. IMGPO cleverly combines hierarchical partitioning with GP-based upper confidence bounds, effectively considering an infinite family of potential semi-metrics to guide its search, thereby avoiding the need for auxiliary optimization.

The paper’s strengths are primarily theoretical. The originality and significance of providing the first practical GP-based algorithm with an exponential regret bound are high. This is a solid theoretical advancement in the field of Bayesian optimization. Furthermore, the paper is exceptionally clear and well-written. The authors do an excellent job of explaining the intuition behind their method and placing it within the broader literature on bound-based search algorithms.

However, the paper's significant theoretical novelty is undermined by a weak experimental evaluation, which prevents a stronger recommendation. The empirical validation falls short of the standards typically expected at this conference for the following reasons:
1.  **Lack of Statistical Rigor:** The experiments do not appear to use multiple random initial function evaluations, which is a standard and crucial practice in the Bayesian optimization literature for ensuring robust and statistically meaningful comparisons between algorithms.
2.  **Missing Baselines:** The comparisons are made against older or related methods (SOO, BaMSOO) but omit current state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization techniques. Powerful information-theoretic acquisition functions, such as Entropy Search or Predictive Entropy Search, are considered standard baselines and their absence makes it difficult to assess IMGPO's practical performance.
3.  **Limited Scope:** The evaluation is confined to low-dimensional (D≤6) synthetic functions. There are no experiments on real-world applications, such as machine learning hyperparameter tuning or scientific problems, which are the primary motivation for developing such algorithms.

In summary, while the theoretical contribution is elegant and interesting, the experimental validation is not thorough enough to demonstrate its practical significance or superiority over the current state of the art. The work is a valuable theoretical step, but its empirical claims are insufficiently supported.
