This paper proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian global optimization algorithm that attains exponential convergence without auxiliary acquisition-function optimization and without δ-cover sampling. The method marries hierarchical partitioning (as in SOO/DIRECT) with GP-derived UCBs to prune candidates, while conceptually considering an infinite family of continuity bounds via an unknown semi-metric. The analysis introduces the infinite-metric exploration loss ρt and proves an exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp) under mild regularity assumptions, improving on BaMSOO’s polynomial rates. The approach is positioned carefully with respect to GP-UCB, SOO, DIRECT, and REMBO, and experiments on standard benchmarks (plus a 1000-D REMBO setting) show compelling performance and favorable wall-clock trade-offs relative to GP-based baselines.

Quality: The theoretical development is technically sound and clearly states assumptions (unknown semi-metric continuity, deterministic evaluations). The bound transparently reveals the role of D, ρ̄t, and GP usage (Ngp), and the remarks provide helpful intuition on when GP-UCB bounds help. Section 3.3’s pseudocode and parameterization details increase reproducibility. Experiments are thorough, use fixed kernels/hyperparameters with empirical Bayes updates, and include ablations on Ξmax.

Clarity: The paper is well written and well organized. Section 3.2’s 1-D walkthrough is an excellent aid to intuition; Section 3.3’s programmatic details should enable reimplementation. A minor suggestion is to consolidate the roles of Ξ, Ξmax, and η in one place and add a short complexity accounting for UCB calls in step (iii).

Originality: The key idea—exploiting an infinite set of possible bounds while using GP-UCB only to prune—is novel and addresses a known gap (exponential rates without δ-cover and without auxiliary optimization). The “exploration loss” concept is a useful analytical tool.

Significance: The contribution is important for Bayesian optimization, eliminating two practical pain points, and the rate result represents theoretical progress. The practical performance appears strong.

Weaknesses and suggestions: (i) Scalability: the bound’s dependence on D can make λ approach 1; a discussion of additive/low-dimensional structure beyond the REMBO demo would be valuable. (ii) Deterministic setting: clarify robustness and any extensions to noisy observations. (iii) Kernel misspecification: consider sensitivity analyses to hyperparameter learning and GP prior choices. (iv) Baselines: including BO methods like Thompson sampling or entropy search (even with auxiliary optimization) would further contextualize gains.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Clear, novel method with strong theory (exponential rate) and practical algorithm.
- Eliminates auxiliary optimization and δ-cover assumptions.
- Compelling empirical results and clear exposition.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited treatment of noise and high-dimensional scalability.
- Some missing baselines and sensitivity analyses.
