This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence without the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem of global optimization and the existing approaches, including Bayesian optimization and Lipschitz optimization. They then introduce their new algorithm, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), which leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the algorithm and its theoretical foundations. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the algorithm's regret bound, which is a significant contribution to the field.

However, the experimental section is somewhat disappointing. The authors only evaluate their algorithm on synthetic functions, with a maximum dimension of 6. While these experiments do show that IMGPO outperforms other algorithms, they do not provide a comprehensive evaluation of the algorithm's performance in real-world scenarios. Furthermore, the experiments do not use multiple initial evaluations of f, which is a standard practice in Bayesian optimization. This makes it difficult to draw meaningful statistical comparisons between the algorithms.

Additionally, the authors do not compare their algorithm to state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods, such as information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search. This is a significant omission, as these methods have been shown to be highly effective in a variety of applications.

Overall, while the theoretical contributions of this paper are interesting, the experimental evaluation is below the conference standard. I would recommend that the authors expand their experimental evaluation to include real-world scenarios and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.
