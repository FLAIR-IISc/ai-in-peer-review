- Proposal: Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimizers or δ-cover sampling by combining GP-UCB bounds with hierarchical partitioning and implicit infinite semi-metrics.  
- Novelty/Significance: Addresses a stated open problem and elegantly unifies two previously divergent lines of global optimization research.  
- Theoretical quality: Theorem 1 proves an exponential regret bound, a substantial improvement over polynomial bounds (e.g., BaMSOO); Remarks 1–3 clarify how GP bound tightness and infinite-metric exploration drive performance.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Paper is well-written with clear high-level intuition, an effective illustrative example, and detailed pseudocode adequate for replication.  
- Experimental validation: Thorough benchmarking against SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI on diverse functions (including a high-D case); results consistently favor IMGPO.  
- Scalability concern: Convergence constant λ can approach 1 as dimension D grows, so more explicit discussion of practical limits and conditions for effectiveness is needed despite the REMBO 1000-D example.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Introduces parameters (η, Ξmax) and a heuristic Ξ update; lacks sensitivity analysis or practical guidance for setting them.  
- Implementation complexity: Certain pseudocode steps (e.g., lines 21–23 for finding the best UCB in a subtree) are conceptually dense and would benefit from clearer implementation details.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, theoretically sound, empirically validated contribution that advances efficient global optimization.
