This paper presents a new algorithm for global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, generalizing the work of Freitas et al., 2012, which relies on an impractical sampling process.

The paper is well-written and easy to understand, and the role of the new algorithm in the current literature of bound-based search methods is well explained. In this sense, I think this work deserves some attention from the community.

I give 5 votes, however, this is not a good score because I think the experimental section is below standard compared to what is expected to be seen in BO papers. My main criticisms of this section are:

- The authors do not compare the methods for several initial evaluations of f because this is standard in BO methods. Therefore, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be obtained.
- Although they propose a bound-based search method, the authors should compare their results with the state-of-the-art BO approaches. Information theoretical approaches, such as entropy search, are not used in the experiments.
- All experiments are performed on synthetic functions (whose maximum dimension is 6). No real weightlab usage of parameter tuning experiments is used to demonstrate the performance of the method in real scenarios.

This paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. The paper is good, with some interesting theoretical results, but in my opinion, the experimental section is below NIPS paper standards.
