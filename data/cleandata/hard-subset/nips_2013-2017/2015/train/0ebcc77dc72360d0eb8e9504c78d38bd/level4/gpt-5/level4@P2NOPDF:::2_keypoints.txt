- Proposes a variant of Bayesian global optimization using space/interval partitioning.  
- Uses UCB to avoid the inner global optimization step for selecting the next query.  
- Theoretical analysis claims improved performance, specifically exponentially decreasing regret.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and supported by an illustrative example; contribution appears novel and presentation clear.  
- Main reservation: empirical validation and scalability are weak—experiments appear limited to one-dimensional tests (not stated explicitly).  
- Concern that interval/space partitioning will not scale well to higher dimensions due to exponential growth in hyperrectangle resolution.  
- Method is closely related to DIRECT, which is known to degrade significantly in high dimensions.  
- Paper should discuss this limitation, practical implications, and possible mitigations (adaptive partitioning, dimensionality reduction, or structure-exploiting assumptions).  
- Minor question: Is the method inherently tied to UCB, or can it be adapted to other acquisition functions (e.g., EI or Thompson sampling)?  
- Minor clarification: explain “candidates of the bounds” and how global vs. local search is balanced in practice.  
- Minor clarification: explain why at n=16 no function evaluation occurs in the example.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution and novel perspective, but limited empirical scope and lack of scalability discussion reduce practical impact.
