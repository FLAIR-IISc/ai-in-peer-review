This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence, addressing limitations of existing approaches that rely on auxiliary optimization or impractical δ-cover sampling techniques. The authors propose a Gaussian process (GP)-based algorithm that achieves an exponential convergence rate without these requirements. The core idea involves leveraging an unknown semi-metric to guide the search, combined with a hierarchical partitioning strategy.

This work builds upon a strong foundation in Bayesian optimization, a well-established area within NIPS, particularly with the rise of GP-based methods [13, 14, 15]. Recent work by de Freitas et al. [1] demonstrated exponential convergence for Bayesian optimization but required δ-cover sampling, which was subsequently identified as a significant practical hurdle [2]. This paper directly tackles this challenge, aiming to maintain the theoretical advantages of exponential convergence without the associated computational cost.  The proposed method also connects to broader research in bound-based search methods like DIRECT [19] and SOO [18], drawing inspiration from their principles of utilizing bounds to guide optimization. 

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The paper provides a solid theoretical guarantee of exponential convergence, a significant result in the field of Bayesian optimization.  The analysis appears rigorous, and the authors clearly articulate the assumptions required for the convergence bound.
* **Practical Relevance:** Eliminating the need for δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization addresses significant practical limitations of existing methods. This makes the proposed algorithm more accessible and applicable to real-world problems.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the methodology. The use of Figure 1 is particularly effective in illustrating the hierarchical partitioning approach.  The authors do a good job of explaining the underlying concepts even for readers less familiar with GPs and Bayesian optimization.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm across a variety of benchmark functions, consistently outperforming competing methods. The comparison against established algorithms like SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-EI provides a strong validation of the approach.
* **Insightful Discussion:** The authors provide a thoughtful discussion of the limitations and potential future directions, including the trade-offs between computational cost and convergence rate, and the scalability to higher dimensions.  The remarks at the end of section 4 provide valuable insights into the underlying mechanisms and potential improvements.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Parameter Sensitivity:** While the experiments show strong performance, the discussion of parameter tuning (e.g., Ξmax) is relatively brief.  A more detailed investigation of the sensitivity to different parameter settings would be valuable.
* **Scalability Concerns:** The authors acknowledge scalability issues with increasing dimensionality (Section 4). While they suggest potential avenues for improvement, this remains a significant limitation.
* **Assumption 1:** The assumption of the existence of an unknown semi-metric ` may be strong for some practical applications. While the algorithm is designed to work even if the GP bound is not tight, a more in-depth discussion of the conditions under which this assumption holds would be beneficial.
* **Redundancy in Abstract:** The abstract contains a somewhat redundant statement ("exponential convergence without the need of auxiliary optimization and without the δ-cover sampling") - it could be more concise.  (l.49)
* **Minor Typo:** A misplaced parenthesis appears in line 72.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant challenge in Bayesian optimization – achieving exponential convergence without impractical requirements.
* The theoretical results are strong and well-supported.
* The experimental results demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.
* The contribution is novel and relevant to the NIPS community.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Scalability to high-dimensional problems remains a concern.
* The assumption regarding the existence of a suitable semi-metric needs further justification.
* The discussion of parameter sensitivity could be more detailed.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The authors have successfully developed a novel algorithm with compelling theoretical guarantees and demonstrated its effectiveness through rigorous experimentation. While there are some limitations regarding scalability and parameter sensitivity, these are acknowledged by the authors and do not detract from the overall quality of the work. I believe this paper is well-suited for acceptance to NIPS.

**Confidence:** 9/10
