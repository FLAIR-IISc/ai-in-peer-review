- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian Global Optimization variant that generates samples via interval/space partitioning.  
- Claims theoretical improvement with exponential convergence/regret.  
- Eliminates the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling in BO.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and accompanied by a thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Uses a simple illustrative example that aids understanding of the method.  
- Paper is well-organized and provides a clear discussion of results.  
- Empirical evaluation is largely limited to 1D test problems.  
- Scalability to higher dimensions is a major concern; method is related to DIRECT which is known to perform poorly as dimensionality increases.  
- Authors include some higher-dimensional experiments but should discuss limitations and possible modifications for better high-D performance.  
- Minor clarification needed: whether acquisition functions other than UCB (e.g., Expected Improvement) can be used.  
- Minor clarification needed: the meaning of "simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds."  
- Minor clarification needed: why at n=16 the far-right interval is divided without a function evaluation.  
- Recommendation: accept, but encourage addressing minor clarifications and providing more insight on scalability to higher dimensions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and sound contribution, strong theoretical results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited 1D experiments, unresolved high-dimensional scalability, and minor clarity issues.
