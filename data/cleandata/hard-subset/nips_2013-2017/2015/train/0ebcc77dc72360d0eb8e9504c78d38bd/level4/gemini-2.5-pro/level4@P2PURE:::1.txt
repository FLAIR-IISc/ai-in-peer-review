This paper introduces a new global optimization algorithm that achieves exponential regret while avoiding delta-cover sampling. The proposed method generalizes the work of de Freitas et al. (2012) by replacing their impractical sampling procedure. The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly situates the new algorithm within the existing literature on bound-based search methods. In this respect, the work is a noteworthy contribution that deserves the community's attention.

However, I am recommending a borderline score, as the experimental section falls below the standard expected for a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper. My main criticisms are as follows:

-   The authors do not compare the methods across multiple random initializations, which is standard practice in BO. This omission prevents a meaningful statistical comparison of the algorithms' performance.
-   Despite proposing a bound-based search method, the authors do not compare their results against state-of-the-art BO approaches. Notably, prominent information-theoretic methods, such as Entropy Search, are absent from the experiments.
-   The evaluation is limited to low-dimensional (at most 6D) synthetic functions. The paper lacks experiments on real-world problems, such as hyperparameter tuning, to illustrate the method's performance in practical scenarios.

In summary, while the paper presents some interesting theoretical results for global optimization, its experimental validation is not sufficiently rigorous for a publication at this venue.
