- Introduces a variant of Bayesian Global Optimization using a space/interval partitioning algorithm.  
- Uses UCB to avoid the internal global optimization step for selecting the next sample.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the method achieves exponential regret, improving over prior work.  
- Algorithm is well-described and supported by an illustrative example.  
- Paper is novel, generally clear, and well presented.  
- Major concern: experiments appear to be limited to 1D test functions (not explicitly stated).  
- Scalability concern: interval partitioning likely does not scale to higher dimensions (resolution grows exponentially).  
- This limitation is related to known issues with the DIRECT algorithm and affects practical applicability.  
- Recommendation: authors should address scalability in the paper, even though theory remains valid.  
- Minor: clarify whether UCB is essential or if other acquisition functions (e.g., EI) could be used.  
- Minor: rephrase the unclear sentence about conducting simultaneous global and local searches.  
- Minor: clarify why at n=16 the far right point does not trigger a function evaluation.
