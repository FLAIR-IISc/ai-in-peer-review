The paper presents a novel algorithm for global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, thereby generalizing the work of Freitas et al., 2012, which relies on an impractical sampling procedure. 

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the role of the new algorithm within the existing literature on bounded-based search methods is clearly explained. In this regard, I believe this work warrants attention from the community.

I give it a score of 5, but not a higher one, because I find the experimental section below the standard expected for a paper in Bayesian Optimization (BO). My main criticisms of this section are:

*   The authors do not compare the methods across multiple initial evaluations of the objective function *f*, which is standard practice in BO research. Consequently, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be drawn.
*   Although the authors propose a bounded-based search method, they should compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches. Information-theoretic methods, such as Entropy Search, are not included in the experimental evaluation.
*   All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions (with a maximum dimension of 6). No real-world parameter tuning experiments are used to demonstrate the performance of the method in practical scenarios.

The paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. It is a strong paper with some interesting theoretical results, but in my opinion, the experimental section does not meet the standards expected of a NIPS publication.
