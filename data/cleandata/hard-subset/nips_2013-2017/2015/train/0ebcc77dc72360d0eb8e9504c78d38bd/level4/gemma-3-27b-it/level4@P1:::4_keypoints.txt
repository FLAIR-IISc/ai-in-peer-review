- Addresses global optimization for non-convex black-box f(x) and targets simple regret rn as performance metric.
- Removes need for auxiliary non-convex global optimization common in BO methods.
- Eliminates impractical δ-cover sampling while achieving exponential convergence.
- Uses Gaussian processes (GPs) for modeling, with clear overview of GP predictive equations and kernels.
- Highlights UCB and EI acquisition functions and limitations of GP-UCB due to bound tightness.
- Introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO) that leverages unknown tighter bounds encoded by continuity at the global optimizer.
- Core idea: simultaneously perform global and local search over all candidate bounds, using the GP to prune candidates.
- Algorithm uses hierarchical partitioning of the domain into hyperintervals centered at evaluation points.
- Selection of intervals is based on center values and GP-derived UCB to identify regions likely containing the global optimum.
- Provides full technical details, notation (e.g., depth h, exploration loss ρt), and formal statement of Theorem 1 for exponential convergence.
- Positions work relative to BaMSOO and DIRECT and builds on Munos' multi-bound ideas.
- Analysis discusses factors affecting the regret bound, importance of GP bound tightness, and benefits of considering infinitely many bounds.
- Acknowledges scalability limitations to very high dimensions and suggests potential improvements.
- Experiments on eight functions show IMGPO consistently outperforming SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.
- Notes experimental caveat: fixed kernel hyperparameters were used and may not reflect all real-world scenarios.
- Code is publicly available, aiding reproducibility.
- Strengths: novelty, rigorous theoretical guarantee, empirical validation, and clear exposition.
- Weaknesses: potential scalability issues in high dimensions and reliance on fixed kernel settings in experiments.
- Final recommendation from reviewer: Strongly Accept.
