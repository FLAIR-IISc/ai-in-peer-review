

This paper introduces a novel variant of Bayesian global optimization that generates future samples via space partitioning, eliminating the need for internal global optimization in selecting the next sample. The approach leverages UCB to achieve exponential regret bounds, providing a clear theoretical improvement over prior work. The algorithm is well-described, with a helpful illustrative example, and the contribution is both novel and presented with clarity.

The primary concern lies in the experimental evaluation, which is limited to 1D test functions (a limitation not explicitly acknowledged). The space partitioning strategy appears inherently constrained to low-dimensional settings, as the required resolution would scale exponentially with dimensionality. This is a critical practical limitation, as the method resembles the DIRECT algorithm—well-known to suffer severely from the curse of dimensionality in higher dimensions. The paper should explicitly address this scalability issue, as it significantly impacts the method’s applicability beyond 1D problems, even though it does not affect the theoretical contribution.

Minor points:  
- p.2: The brief reference to UCB as "for brevity" requires justification. Why not consider alternatives like expected improvement?  
- p.3: The statement "we simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds" is unclear.  
- p.4: The note that "no function evaluation occurs" at n=16 merits brief clarification.  

Overall, the theoretical contribution is sound and significant. However, the lack of higher-dimensional experiments and the absence of discussion regarding scalability represent a notable gap. Addressing these would substantially strengthen the paper’s practical relevance. The work is promising but requires explicit acknowledgment of its dimensional limitations.
