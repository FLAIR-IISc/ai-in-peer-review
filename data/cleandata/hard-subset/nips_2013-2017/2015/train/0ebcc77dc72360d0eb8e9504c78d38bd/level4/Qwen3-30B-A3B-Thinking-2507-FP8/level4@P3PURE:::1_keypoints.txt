- Introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling, achieves exponential regret, and generalizes Freitas et al. (2012).  
- Paper is well-written, well-contextualized within bounded-based search literature, and presents theoretically interesting results.  
- Reviewer believes the contribution deserves significant attention and recommends a score of 5.  
- Experimental section is below NeurIPS standards for empirical rigor.  
- Experiments do not perform comparisons across multiple initial evaluations of f, preventing meaningful statistical comparison.  
- Experiments omit comparisons with state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods (e.g., information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search).  
- All experiments use synthetic functions of at most dimension 6 and lack real-world validation (e.g., biological or engineering parameter tuning).  
- Overall: strong theoretical contributions but experimental shortcomings undermine empirical validation required for NeurIPS.
