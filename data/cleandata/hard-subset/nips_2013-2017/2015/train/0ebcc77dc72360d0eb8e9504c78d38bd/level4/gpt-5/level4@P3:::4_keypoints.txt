- Contribution: Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm using infinite-metric optimism that attains exponential simple-regret rates without auxiliary acquisition optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Key idea: Blends hierarchical partitioning with considering a family of smoothness bounds and uses GP-derived UCBs only to prune candidates.
- Theory: Provides clear assumptions (existence of optimizer and Hölder-dominated semi-metric) and a theorem giving an exponential regret bound adapting to unknown (L, α, p).
- Analysis novelties: Introduces infinite-metric exploration loss ρt and counts virtual GP-based evaluations Ngp to quantify cost of multiple bounds.
- Algorithm: Explicit pseudocode with a standard, implementable tri-partition scheme and practical GP UCB-based pruning to avoid unnecessary evaluations.
- Experiments: Benchmarks on 1–6D functions, a custom test, and a 1000D REMBO scenario; IMGPO generally outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, PI/EI and is faster than classic GP BO.
- Positioning: Well-situated relative to GP-UCB, SOO/DOO, DIRECT, and addresses an open problem from prior work (Wang et al.).
- Assumptions/scope concern: Need to state the function class (e.g., Hölder-smooth around x* with parameters (L,α,p)) early and clarify uniformity of the “best unknown” triples versus function class.
- Hyperparameter learning concern: Using f(x) ≤ U(x|D) while re-estimating GP hyperparameters via empirical Bayes can invalidate high-probability UCB guarantees; robustness needs clarification or citation.
- Computational complexity issue: Ambiguous statements (“3Ξmax” vs 3^Ξmax, “Ξ ← Ξ + 22”) must be corrected and a clear per-iteration and total complexity bound in N, D, Ξmax provided.
- Dimensional dependence: λ and convergence degrade with D (partitioning base and constants); authors should quantify empirical degradation and discuss remedies (additive kernels, REMBO, ARD).
- Behavior of ρ̄t: The bound hinges on ρ̄t; empirical traces of ρt across runs/functions should be provided to show when GP pruning reduces exploration loss.
- Experimental fairness: All methods used isotropic Matérn 5/2 without ARD—include sensitivity analyses (ARD, SE, model selection), wall-clock budgets, and true-eval vs GP-skip ratios to ensure fair comparison.
- Noisy objectives: Method and theory are deterministic-only; discuss possible extensions to noisy settings (noise-aware UCBs, replication) even if theory is left for future work.
- Clarity/notation: Algorithm 1 indexing and dense notation need a schematic or glossary (Th, T′h+ξ, Ngp, Ξ, ρt); confirm domain Ω assumptions and division rules.
- Editorial nits: Tighten abstract, fix stray/misplaced phrases and typos (double “the”, unclear increments), and correct formatting in Algorithm 1.
- Originality: The combination of infinite-metric optimism with GP pruning and the ρt notion is novel and distinguishes the work from BaMSOO.
- Significance: Achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover and without auxiliary acquisition optimization is theoretically and practically notable, despite dimensionality caveats.
- Pros: Removes two practical limitations (δ-cover and auxiliary optimization), offers novel analysis (ρt, Ngp), reduces real evaluations via GP screening, and shows strong empirical results with released code.
- Cons: Deterministic-only analysis, theoretical and practical dimensional dependence, unclear UCB guarantees under data-dependent hyperparameters, and several algorithmic/detail clarifications needed.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid advance combining theory and practice; address clarifications and add modest sensitivity experiments to strengthen the submission.
