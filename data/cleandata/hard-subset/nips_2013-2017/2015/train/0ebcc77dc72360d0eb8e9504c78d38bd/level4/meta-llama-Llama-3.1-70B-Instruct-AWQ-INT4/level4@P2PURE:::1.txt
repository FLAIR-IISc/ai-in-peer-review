The paper presents a novel algorithm for global optimization, avoiding delta-cover sampling and achieving exponential regret, thus generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012) that relies on an impractical sampling procedure.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a clear explanation of the new algorithm's role within the current literature on bounded-based search methods. This work deserves attention from the community in this regard.

I give a score of 5, but not higher, because the experimental section falls short of the standard expected in a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper. My primary criticisms of this section are:

- The authors do not compare the methods for multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, which is a standard practice in BO. As a result, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be obtained.
- Although the authors propose a bounded-based search method, they should compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches. Notably, information-theoretic approaches, such as Entropy Search, are not used in the experiments.
- All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions with a maximum dimension of 6. No real-world experiment, such as a wet lab or parameter tuning scenario, is used to illustrate the method's performance in practical settings.

The paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, with some interesting theoretical results. However, in my opinion, the experimental section does not meet the standards of a NIPS paper.
