- Presents a novel Bayesian optimization method combining Gaussian processes with an infinite-metric approach and hierarchical partitioning, claiming exponential convergence.  
- Avoids auxiliary inner optimization loops and impractical δ-cover sampling used by prior methods.  
- Theoretical analysis rigorously proves an exponential convergence rate and examines effects of parameters, infinite-metric exploration loss, and the GP prior.  
- Algorithm is well-structured and clearly explained, with an illustrative example (Figure 1) that aids understanding.  
- Strong connection to related work (Lipschitz optimization, GP-based BO, de Freitas et al., Wang et al.) and to bound-based/DIRECT methods.  
- Main strength is the significant theoretical contribution demonstrating improved convergence over previous results.  
- Primary concern is scalability to high-dimensional problems due to exponential growth of hyperrectangle partitions (similar to DIRECT).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to low-dimensional problems and does not address high-dimensional scalability.  
- Paper should add a discussion acknowledging scalability limits and suggest remedies (e.g., dimensionality reduction or adaptive partitioning).  
- Minor clarification needed: clarify the phrase that UCB is considered “for brevity” and whether other acquisition functions fit the framework.  
- Minor clarification needed: rephrase the sentence about conducting global and local searches from all candidate bounds for clearer interpretation.  
- Minor clarification needed: explain why no function evaluation occurs at n=16 in the illustrative example.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach; proven exponential convergence; clear presentation and strong literature connections.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments focused on low dimensions and lack of discussion on practical scalability; acceptance recommended if scalability concerns and minor clarifications are addressed.
