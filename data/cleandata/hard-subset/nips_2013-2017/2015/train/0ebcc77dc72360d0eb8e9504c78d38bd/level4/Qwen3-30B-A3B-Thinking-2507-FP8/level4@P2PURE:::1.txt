

The paper introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling while achieving exponential regret, thereby generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012), which relies on an impractical sampling procedure. The manuscript is well-written, clearly structured, and effectively situates the new algorithm within the context of bound-based search methods, making a compelling case for its significance to the community. I therefore believe this work merits attention.  

I assign a score of 5 (strong accept), though this is not a higher score due to shortcomings in the experimental section, which falls below the standard expected for a Bayesian optimization (BO) paper. My primary criticisms are as follows:  
- The authors do not compare their method across multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, a standard practice in BO, precluding meaningful statistical comparison.  
- Despite proposing a bound-based search method, the authors omit comparisons with state-of-the-art BO approaches, such as information-theoretic methods (e.g., Entropy Search).  
- All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions (with a maximum dimension of 6), lacking real-world applications or real-world parameter tuning scenarios (e.g., wet-lab experiments) to demonstrate practical utility.  

While the theoretical contributions are interesting and the paper is well-structured, the experimental section fails to meet the rigor expected of a NIPS submission.
