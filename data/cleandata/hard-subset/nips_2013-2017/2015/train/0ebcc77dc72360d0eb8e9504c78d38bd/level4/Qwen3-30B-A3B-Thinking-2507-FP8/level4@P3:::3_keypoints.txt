- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Resolves an open problem from Wang et al. and de Freitas et al. by avoiding impractical δ‑cover sampling.  
- Key insight: simultaneously consider all possible bounds encoded by an unknown semi‑metric rather than a single GP prior bound.  
- Combines bound‑based methods (e.g., SOO) with Bayesian methods (GP‑UCB) into a unified, flexible framework.  
- Provides rigorous theory proving an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, improving on BaMSOO’s polynomial bound.  
- Analysis separates impacts of UCB tightness, GP usage, and infinite‑metric exploration and includes Remarks 1–3 for parameter effects.  
- Clear exposition with a 1D visual example (Section 3.2) and well‑structured pseudocode (Algorithm 1) enabling reproducibility.  
- Empirical results across eight multimodal functions (1–1000 dims) show consistent superiority, especially in harder high‑dimensional cases.  
- Uses a single isotropic Matern (ν=5/2) kernel with default hyperparameters to reflect practical unknown hyperparameter settings.  
- Significant contribution: addresses a fundamental limitation in Bayesian optimization and offers insights potentially useful beyond BO.  
- Limitation: exponential convergence degrades in very high dimensions (D > 10) as λ approaches 1.  
- Practical gap: more systematic guidance on selecting Ξmax would strengthen implementation advice.  
- Overall judgment: technically sound, original, clearly written, well‑validated experimentally; recommendation to accept.  
- Pros: solves a major open problem, strong theoretical analysis, clear presentation, compelling experiments.  
- Cons: scalability challenges in very high dimensions and insufficient guidance on Ξmax selection.
