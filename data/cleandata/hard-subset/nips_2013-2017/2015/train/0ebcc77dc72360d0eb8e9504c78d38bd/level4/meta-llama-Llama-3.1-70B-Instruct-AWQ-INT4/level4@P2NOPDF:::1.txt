The paper presents a novel algorithm for global optimization, which avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, thereby generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012) that relies on an impractical sampling procedure. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a clear explanation of the new algorithm's role within the current literature of bounded-based search methods. This work deserves attention from the community due to its contributions.

I vote 5, but I do not assign a higher score due to concerns regarding the experimental section, which falls short of the standard expected in a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper. My main criticisms of this section are:

- The authors do not compare the methods for multiple initial evaluations of the objective function f, which is a standard practice in BO. Consequently, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be obtained.
- Despite proposing a bounded-based search method, the authors should compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches, including information-theoretic methods such as Entropy Search, which are not used in the experiments.
- All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions with a maximum dimension of 6. The absence of real-world experiments, such as wet-lab or parameter tuning scenarios, limits the illustration of the method's performance in practical situations.

In summary, the paper presents a new global optimization algorithm with some interesting theoretical results. However, in my opinion, the experimental section does not meet the standards expected in a NIPS paper.
