- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian global optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary non-convex optimization by using hierarchical partitioning and GP-UCB pruning.  
- Proves an exponential regret bound O(λ^{N + N_gp}) with λ < 1, markedly better than BaMSOO’s polynomial regret.  
- Theoretical analysis explicitly links performance to GP-UCB tightness and semi-metric exploration loss (Remarks 1–3).  
- Algorithmic design is elegant: evaluates all candidate bounds from an unknown semi-metric and uses GP UCB to filter candidates; pseudocode is clear and executable.  
- Strong novelty and rigorous theoretical contribution, with clear connections to prior work (DIRECT, SOO, BaMSOO).  
- Experimental scope is limited to 1D benchmarks and a 1000D example using REMBO, raising scalability concerns due to partitioning’s curse of dimensionality.  
- Authors should more directly address dimensionality effects (impact on ρ̄_t, computational cost, and runtimes) and include 2D/3D experiments or analysis.  
- Minor issues: clarify UCB wording on Page 2, rephrase ambiguous sentence about simultaneous search on Page 3, and note that the n=16 (no evaluation) choice is deliberate (Page 4).  
- Overall assessment: significant theoretical advance with clear presentation; scalability is a practical limitation but not a fatal flaw.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions to address dimensionality and clarity concerns.
