- Proposes IMGPO: a novel Bayesian optimization combining GP modeling with hierarchical partitioning using an unknown semi-metric, claiming exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.  
- Provides a strong theoretical analysis demonstrating exponential convergence and carefully analyzing the impact of the GP prior and exploration loss (ρt).  
- Well-written and clearly motivated, with good positioning relative to prior work and a clear connection to bound-based methods like DIRECT.  
- Figure 1 is a helpful illustrative example that clarifies the algorithm’s mechanics.  
- Addresses the common challenge of unknown Lipschitz constants by simultaneously considering multiple potential bounds via an unknown semi-metric.  
- Discussion includes limitations and potential improvements, particularly regarding scalability to higher dimensions.  
- Experimental evaluation is a significant weakness: mostly low-dimensional benchmarks and insufficient isolation of IMGPO’s contribution within REMBO for the 1000‑D test.  
- Scalability concerns from interval partitioning (akin to DIRECT) are noted but not empirically addressed or mitigated.  
- Minor clarity issues: limited justification for choosing UCB as the acquisition function, unclear statement about “simultaneously conduct[ing] global and local searches,” and an obscure explanation for the lack of function evaluation at n=16.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, clear presentation, novel approach addressing key limitations, and thorough analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental validation, unresolved scalability concerns, and some unclear textual statements.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing scalability (more high-dimensional experiments or detailed mitigation strategies) and clarifying the minor points.
