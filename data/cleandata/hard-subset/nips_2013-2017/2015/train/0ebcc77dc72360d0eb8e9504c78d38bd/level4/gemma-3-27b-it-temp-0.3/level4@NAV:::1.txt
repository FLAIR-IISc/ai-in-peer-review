This paper introduces a novel algorithm for global optimization, circumventing the limitations of delta-cover sampling and achieving exponential regret – a generalization of the approach presented by Freitas et al. (2012), which relied on a less practical sampling strategy. 

The manuscript is clearly written and well-structured, effectively situating the new algorithm within the existing landscape of bounded-based search methods. I believe this work has the potential to be of significant interest to the research community.

I assign a score of 5, but not higher, due to concerns regarding the experimental evaluation. Specifically, the experimental section falls short of the standards typically expected in Bayesian Optimization (BO) publications. My primary criticisms are:

*   The authors do not evaluate the methods across multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, a standard practice in BO research. Consequently, a robust statistical comparison between the methods is lacking.
*   Despite proposing a bounded-based search method, the results are not benchmarked against state-of-the-art BO approaches. Notably, information-theoretic methods like Entropy Search were not included in the experimental comparison.
*   The experiments are limited to synthetic functions, with a maximum dimensionality of 6. The absence of experiments on real-world parameter tuning problems hinders the demonstration of the method’s performance in practical applications.

In summary, the paper presents a promising new global optimization algorithm with interesting theoretical properties, but the experimental validation requires substantial improvement to meet the expectations for a NIPS publication.
