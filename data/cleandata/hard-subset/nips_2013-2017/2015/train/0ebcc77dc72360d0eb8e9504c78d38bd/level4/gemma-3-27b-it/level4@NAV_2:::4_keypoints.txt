- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Circumvents impractical δ-cover sampling and removes costly auxiliary optimization steps common in prior methods.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis establishing exponential convergence rates for IMGPO.  
- Practical innovation makes Bayesian optimization more accessible and computationally efficient.  
- Algorithm is clearly explained and supported by an illustrative figure and step-by-step rationale.  
- Empirical results show IMGPO consistently outperforms baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) on benchmark functions.  
- Sensitivity analysis on Ξmax offers useful insight into algorithm behavior.  
- Related work is comprehensive and accurately positions the contribution within global and Bayesian optimization literature.  
- Discussion of scalability to higher dimensions is limited and needs deeper exploration and remedies.  
- Hyperparameter choices (σ = 1, l = 0.25) appear arbitrary and warrant a more detailed sensitivity study.  
- Minor typographical/clarity errors (e.g., "formulation is redondant", misplaced parenthesis) should be corrected.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical and practical advance, rigorous proofs, strong experiments, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient scalability discussion, hyperparameter sensitivity concerns, minor typos.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-supported paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
