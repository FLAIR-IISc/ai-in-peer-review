- Introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. but removes impractical δ-cover sampling while addressing an open problem from Wang et al.  
- Observes GP-UCB is standard but auxiliary optimization remains a practical limitation.  
- Key insight: leverage an unknown continuity-based bound at the global optimizer instead of relying only on the GP upper bound.  
- Provides rigorous theory: exponential regret bound O(λ^{N+Ngp}) with λ < 1 under reasonable assumptions; proofs in supplementary material.  
- Shows theoretical improvement over BaMSOO and related work, with compelling analyses in Remarks 1–3 about not committing to a single upper bound.  
- Experimental evaluation compares IMGPO to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on eight benchmarks across dimensions 1–1000.  
- Empirically, IMGPO consistently outperforms competitors, especially on multimodal functions.  
- Experimental setup is practical: single isotropic Matern kernel (ν=5/2) with no handpicked hyperparameters.  
- Paper is clear and well-written with helpful figures (notably Figure 1) and clear pseudocode (Algorithm 1).  
- Effectively explains the tradeoff between GP upper bounds and unknown continuity bounds, and situates work among DIRECT, SOO, GP-UCB.  
- Main strength: resolves the open problem of exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.  
- Theoretical framework (Remarks 1–3) gives broader insights on using loose bounds in optimization.  
- Minor weaknesses: could analyze how Ξmax affects performance in more detail (partly in supplementary), and assumes Ω is a hyperrectangle.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept—significant theoretical contribution with clear practical implications.
