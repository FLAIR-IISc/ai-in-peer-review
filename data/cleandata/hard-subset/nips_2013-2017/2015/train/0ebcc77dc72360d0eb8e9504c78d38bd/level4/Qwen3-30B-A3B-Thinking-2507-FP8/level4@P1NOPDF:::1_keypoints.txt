- Introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and attains exponential regret bounds.  
- Generalizes Freitas et al. (2012) by removing an impractical sampling requirement.  
- Theoretical contributions are well-motivated, clearly written, and well contextualized within bounded-search literature.  
- The authors effectively demonstrate theoretical advantages and the work deserves community attention.  
- Reviewer assigns a top score of 5, noting it could be higher with stronger experiments.  
- Empirical shortcoming: no standard Bayesian optimization benchmarks or multiple initializations, preventing statistically rigorous comparisons.  
- Empirical shortcoming: missing key state-of-the-art BO baselines (e.g., Entropy Search).  
- Empirical shortcoming: experiments rely solely on synthetic functions (dim ≤6) with no real-world or parameter-tuning validations.  
- Conclusion: compelling theory but insufficient empirical rigor; stronger evaluations would substantially strengthen the paper.
