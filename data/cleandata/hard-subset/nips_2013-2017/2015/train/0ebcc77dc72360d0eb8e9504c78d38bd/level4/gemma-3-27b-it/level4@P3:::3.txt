This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method, dubbed Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), designed to achieve exponential convergence without relying on auxiliary optimization or the impractical δ-cover sampling technique required by prior work [1]. Bayesian optimization, particularly with Gaussian Processes (GPs), is a well-established technique for global optimization of non-convex black-box functions, finding applications in areas like machine learning hyperparameter tuning [3] and engineering design [4]. Recent advances by de Freitas et al. [1] demonstrated exponential convergence, but at the cost of δ-cover sampling, which Wang et al. [2] highlighted as a significant practical limitation. This paper directly addresses this limitation, offering a compelling solution.

The paper is clearly structured. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and positions IMGPO within the existing literature. Section 3.2 provides a particularly valuable, illustrative example that aids understanding of the algorithm’s mechanics, and Section 3.3 further clarifies implementation details. The experimental results presented in Section 5 demonstrate promising performance across a variety of benchmark functions.  A key strength is the authors’ recognition of the interplay between different approaches within the Bayesian optimization field – specifically, integrating ideas from methods *with* and *without* continuity estimation.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a Significant Practical Limitation:** The elimination of δ-cover sampling is a major step forward, making exponential convergence achievable in more realistic settings.
* **Clear and Well-Organized Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and logically structured, facilitating comprehension. The illustrative example (Section 3.2) is particularly effective.
* **Detailed Algorithmic Description:** Section 3.3 provides sufficient detail for implementation and replication of the proposed algorithm. The pseudocode is helpful.
* **Promising Experimental Results:**  The empirical evaluation demonstrates that IMGPO generally outperforms existing methods, particularly when a tight GP bound isn't readily available.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The authors provide a theoretical analysis, demonstrating an exponential convergence rate and analyzing the factors influencing performance (Section 4).
* **Thoughtful Discussion:** The remarks at the end of Section 4 offer valuable insights into the algorithm's behavior and potential avenues for improvement.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Scalability Concerns:** While the results are promising, the analysis indicates potential scalability challenges in higher dimensions, with the convergence rate potentially approaching 1 as dimensionality increases. The paper acknowledges this and suggests future directions, but further investigation is needed.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The choice of Ξmax appears to be important for performance, as noted in the experiments.  A more detailed discussion of how to select this parameter in practice would be beneficial.
* **Limited Comparison to Strong Baselines:** While the comparison to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI is reasonable, it would be strengthened by including comparisons to more recent and highly optimized Bayesian optimization packages (e.g., those available in BoTorch or Scikit-Optimize).
* **Assumption 1:** The assumption of an unknown semi-metric `(x, x*)` while reasonable, could benefit from further discussion of its implications and potential limitations in certain problem settings.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper solves a significant practical problem in Bayesian optimization.
* The method achieves a strong theoretical guarantee of exponential convergence.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains a complex algorithm.
* The experimental results are promising and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* The work builds upon and integrates existing research in a novel and insightful way.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Scalability to very high dimensions remains a concern.
* The hyperparameter sensitivity (Ξmax) could make the algorithm more challenging to use in practice.
* The comparison to other Bayesian optimization libraries could be improved.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The authors have addressed a key limitation of prior work and presented a well-justified and thoroughly evaluated solution. While the scalability concerns and hyperparameter sensitivity warrant consideration, the strengths of the paper clearly outweigh its weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should expand the discussion of Ξmax selection and consider including comparisons to more contemporary Bayesian optimization libraries in their experimental evaluation.
