- Proposes a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and attains exponential regret bounds.  
- Generalizes Freitas et al. (2012) by removing their impractical sampling requirement.  
- Paper is well written, easy to follow, and clearly situates the algorithm within bounds-based search literature.  
- Theoretical results are interesting and merit community attention.  
- Reviewer recommends score 5, but not higher, due to experimental shortcomings.  
- Experiments lack repeated runs from multiple initial evaluations of f, preventing meaningful statistical comparisons.  
- No comparison against state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods (e.g., information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search).  
- All experiments are on synthetic functions (max dimension 6); no real-world (wet-lab or hyperparameter tuning) evaluations.  
- Overall: strong theory and presentation, but experimental section is below NIPS standards.
