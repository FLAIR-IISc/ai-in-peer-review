- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that attains exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Eliminates reliance on a single GP-derived bound by considering infinitely many possible bounds (infinite-metric approach).  
- Theoretical contribution: first GP-based method with exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, improving on prior polynomial bounds.  
- Builds on and synthesizes ideas from SOO (Munos, 2011) and BaMSOO (Wang et al., 2013) while overcoming their polynomial-regret limitation.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including a clear definition of infinite-metric exploration loss and parameter effects on regret.  
- Algorithm presentation is clear and well-documented, with helpful pseudocode (Algorithm 1) and an illustrative figure for hierarchical partitioning.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: eight benchmark functions across 1–1000 dimensions, realistic setup using a single isotropic Matern kernel and default hyperparameters.  
- Empirical results show IMGPO consistently outperforms baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI), especially on multimodal problems.  
- Authors analyze the effect of the Ξmax parameter on performance, demonstrating attention to implementation trade-offs.  
- Main weakness: limited discussion and concrete strategies for scalability to truly high-dimensional problems; λ may approach 1 as dimension increases.  
- 1000-D experiment is synthetic (embedding a 2D function), so real high-dimensional scalability is not convincingly addressed.  
- Mentions connection to REMBO for high-dimensional problems but does not elaborate or integrate strategies.  
- Minor weakness: lacks comparison to some more recent high-dimensional BO methods (e.g., Kandasamy et al., 2015; Wang et al., 2013).  
- Overall assessment: substantial theoretical advance, well-executed experiments and presentation; recommended for acceptance despite scalability and comparison limitations.
