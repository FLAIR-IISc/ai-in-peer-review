The paper presents a variant of Bayesian Global Optimization that utilizes a space/interval partitioning algorithm to generate future samples. By employing Upper Confidence Bound (UCB), this approach avoids the internal global optimization problem of selecting the next sample. Theoretical analysis demonstrates that this method achieves exponential regret, thereby improving upon previous work. The algorithm is clearly described, explained, and illustrated with an example, making a novel contribution to the field.

Overall, the paper is well-presented, and the authors have made a significant contribution. However, I have one major reservation regarding the experimental results. Although not explicitly stated, the results appear to be limited to 1D test functions. I suspect that the interval partitioning approach may not scale well to higher dimensions, as the required "resolution" would need to grow exponentially. This concern is exacerbated by the fact that the approach is related to the well-known DIRECT algorithm, which is known to suffer from poor performance as the dimensionality of the problem increases. The authors should address this limitation in the paper, as it significantly impacts the practical applicability of the method, even if it does not affect the theoretical contribution.
