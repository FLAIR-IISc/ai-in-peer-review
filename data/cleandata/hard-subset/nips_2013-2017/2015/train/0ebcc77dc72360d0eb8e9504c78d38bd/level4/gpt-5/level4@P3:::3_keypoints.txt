- Proposes IMGPO: a BO method combining hierarchical partitioning with GP UCBs for pruning, avoiding acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Key idea: be optimistic over an infinite family of semi-metric bounds (infinite-metric) and use GP UCBs to prune/defer evaluations.  
- Introduces infinite-metric exploration loss ρt to quantify cost of not committing to a specific bound.  
- Claims exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp) under Hölder-type regularity, with GP reducing ρ̄t and affecting Ngp.  
- Theoretical scaling with dimension D is explicit: λ → 1 as D grows, limiting high-D gains.  
- Algorithm is clearly specified with deterministic division rules and uses UCBs to skip evaluations efficiently.  
- Main theoretical guarantee assumes deterministic observations and fixed high-probability UCBs; empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates may invalidate these guarantees.  
- Proofs are deferred to the supplement; some technical details and hyperparameter-safety measures are not fully addressed.  
- Writing and organization are good; pseudocode and examples make the method reproducible.  
- Minor presentation issues: notation (n/N/t), typos, and need clearer explanation of step (iii) complexity and Ξmax.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of optimistic partition methods and GP pruning, and the infinite-metric perspective is a useful contribution.  
- Significance: removes two practical BO bottlenecks and may influence future BO designs; empirical results show competitive performance and runtime benefits.  
- Strengths: exponential convergence without δ-covering or auxiliary optimization, clear algorithm and analysis, insightful ρt metric, and strong empirical performance.  
- Weaknesses: analysis limited to deterministic objectives, unclear robustness to noise and online hyperparameter learning, and degradation with increasing D.  
- Experimental concerns: limited high-dimensional evidence, missing GP-UCB and DIRECT baselines, potential unfairness in EI/PI optimization, and lacking ablations (no-GP, bound-committing).  
- Suggested experiments/clarifications: address hyperparameter update safeguards, include no-GP and GP-only baselines, add GP-UCB/DIRECT, detail per-iteration complexity and Ξmax sensitivity, and fix notation/typos.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel/principled algorithm, strong theory and insights, good empirical results, and available code.  
- Cons for acceptance: deterministic-only theory, unclear hyperparameter/hyperprior handling, limited high-D evidence, and missing ablations/baselines.  
- Recommendation: leaning toward accept, contingent on addressing experimental and clarity concerns.
