- Proposes IMGPO, a novel global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization.  
- Leverages continuity at a global optimizer to encode an unknown bound—key conceptual contribution.  
- Achieves exponential convergence rate (exponential regret) and generalizes Freitas et al. (2012).  
- Thorough theoretical analysis, including proof of exponential convergence and discussion of GP UCB tightness.  
- Positions the algorithm clearly within bound-based search literature and compares to prior work.  
- Shows IMGPO has a better regret bound than BaMSOO in the analysis.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited—only compares to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on synthetic functions.  
- Does not perform multiple initial evaluations of f, hindering meaningful statistical comparisons typical in BO.  
- No comparisons with state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search).  
- Experiments are only on synthetic problems with dimensionality up to 6; no real-world benchmarks.  
- Overall contribution is interesting but experimental section is below NIPS standards; acceptance score: 5.  
- Recommended improvements: expand experimental setup (multiple inits), include state-of-the-art and real-world tasks, and analyze GP UCB tightness effects.
