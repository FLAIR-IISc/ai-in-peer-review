- Summary: Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based BO using hierarchical partitioning and an “infinite-metric” family of bounds with GP-UCB pruning, achieving exponential simple-regret without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization.
- Theoretical improvement: Proves a regret bound O(λN + Ngp) with λ < 1, improving on BaMSOO’s polynomial rates.
- Novelty: Removes need for δ-cover sampling and auxiliary global optimization by exploring many possible smoothness bounds while using GP posteriors to prune.
- Relation to prior work: Clearly links to DIRECT, SOO, optimism-based methods (UCB/A*), and BaMSOO, explaining how GP information tightens the search.
- Clarity/organization: Algorithm description, pseudocode, and illustrative figure are clear and well organized.
- Practicality: Avoids solving a nonconvex acquisition at each iteration and provides public code.
- Theoretical depth: Offers nonasymptotic analysis and insights into GP-UCB’s role and the infinite-metric exploration loss.
- Experimental weakness — statistics: No multiple random initializations or reporting of mean/variance across runs, limiting statistical comparisons.
- Experimental weakness — baselines: Missing comparisons to strong BO baselines (Entropy Search, PES, MES), Thompson sampling, and GP-UCB with robust optimizers.
- Experimental weakness — benchmarks: Evaluations are synthetic (mostly ≤6D) with only one REMBO-style high-D synthetic task; no real-world tuning or design problems.
- Ablation missing: Lacks detailed ablations on key parameters (e.g., Ξmax, η), kernel choices, and isolation of GP screening vs. infinite-metric contributions.
- Runtime/scalability: Runtime and wall-clock comparisons are briefly discussed but not deeply analyzed; empirical scalability beyond REMBO is limited.
- Dimension dependence: Analysis notes λ→1 as D increases, but little empirical exploration or mitigation strategies for high-dimensional problems.
- Quality assessment: Theoretical results appear sound and well presented; experimental section lacks breadth and rigor for top BO conferences.
- Clarity issues: Generally well written with minor editorial/formatting typos to fix.
- Originality: Central idea of combining GP pruning with infinite-metric search is original and addresses a known open problem.
- Significance: High theoretical significance; practical impact would be stronger with more comprehensive experiments.
- Suggested improvements: Add multiple-seed statistical evaluations (means/variances), wall-clock times, comparisons to information-theoretic baselines and Thompson sampling, real-world benchmarks, targeted ablations, and complexity/memory discussion.
- Pros for acceptance: Strong novel theoretical contribution, clear extension of prior bound-based methods, accessible presentation, and available code.
- Cons for acceptance: Limited and statistically weak experiments, no real-world applications, and insufficient assessment of scalability and practical performance.
- Overall rating: 5 — solid theoretical/algorithmic contribution but empirical evaluation needs significant strengthening.
