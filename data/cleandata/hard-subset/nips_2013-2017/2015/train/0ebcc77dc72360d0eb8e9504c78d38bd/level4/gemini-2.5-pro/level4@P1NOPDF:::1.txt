This paper presents a new algorithm for global optimization that achieves exponential regret. It generalizes the work of de Freitas et al. (2012) by avoiding the impractical delta-cover sampling procedure upon which that work relies.

The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly explains the role of the new algorithm within the existing literature on bound-based search methods. In this sense, I believe this work is a noteworthy contribution that deserves the community's attention.

However, I am recommending a score of 5; a higher score is not warranted because the experimental section falls below the standard one would expect for a NIPS paper. My main criticisms of this section are as follows:

- The authors do not compare the methods using multiple random initializations for the function f, which is standard practice in Bayesian Optimization. Consequently, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be obtained.
- Although the paper proposes a bound-based search method, it should be compared against state-of-the-art Bayesian Optimization approaches. Key information-theoretic methods, such as Entropy Search, are notably absent from the experiments.
- All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions with a maximum of six dimensions. No real-world applications, such as hyperparameter tuning or a wet-lab experiment, are used to illustrate the method's performance in practical scenarios.

In summary, the paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. While the theoretical results are interesting, the experimental section, in my opinion, is not sufficiently rigorous for a NIPS publication.
