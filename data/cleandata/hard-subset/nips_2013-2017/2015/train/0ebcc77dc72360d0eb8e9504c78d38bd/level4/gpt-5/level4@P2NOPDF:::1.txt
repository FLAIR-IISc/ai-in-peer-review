This paper proposes a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, extending the work of Freitas et al. (2012), whose method relied on an impractical sampling scheme. The manuscript is clearly written, easy to follow, and convincingly situates the contribution within the literature on bound-based search methods. In this respect, the work merits attention from the community.

I assign a score of 5. I do not assign a higher score because the experimental section falls below the standard typically expected in Bayesian optimization (BO) papers. My main concerns are:

- Lack of multiple initializations: The comparisons do not consider multiple initial evaluations of f, which is standard practice in BO to account for variability. Without this, it is difficult to draw meaningful statistical conclusions about performance.

- Missing state-of-the-art baselines: Although the method is a bound-based search approach, it should be compared against strong contemporary BO methods, including information-theoretic approaches such as Entropy Search. These baselines are absent.

- Limited empirical scope: All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions, with maximum dimensionality of six. No real-world tasks—such as wet-lab optimization or hyperparameter tuning—are included to demonstrate practical effectiveness.

In summary, the paper introduces a promising algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, and it offers interesting theoretical contributions. However, the empirical evaluation is not yet at the level expected for an NIPS paper. Strengthening the experiments with multiple-start protocols, comprehensive state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., Entropy Search), and real-world benchmarks would substantially improve the submission.
