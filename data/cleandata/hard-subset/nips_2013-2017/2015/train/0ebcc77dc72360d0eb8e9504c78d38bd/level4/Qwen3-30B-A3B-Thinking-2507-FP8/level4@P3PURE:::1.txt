

The paper introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling while achieving exponential regret, thereby generalizing the approach of Freitas et al. (2012), which relies on an impractical sampling procedure. The work is well-written, clearly contextualized within the literature on bounded-based search methods, and presents theoretically interesting results. I believe this contribution deserves significant attention from the community.  

I recommend a score of 5; however, the experimental section falls below the standard expected for a NeurIPS publication. My primary concerns are:  
- The authors do not compare methods across multiple initial evaluations of $f$, which is standard practice in Bayesian optimization (BO) and prevents meaningful statistical comparison.  
- Despite proposing a bounded-based search method, the experiments omit comparisons with state-of-the-art BO approaches, such as information-theoretic methods (e.g., Entropy Search).  
- All experiments utilize synthetic functions (maximum dimension 6) without real-world validation, such as parameter tuning in biological or engineering contexts, limiting the demonstration of practical utility.  

While the theoretical contributions are strong, the experimental shortcomings prevent the paper from meeting NeurIPS standards for empirical rigor.
