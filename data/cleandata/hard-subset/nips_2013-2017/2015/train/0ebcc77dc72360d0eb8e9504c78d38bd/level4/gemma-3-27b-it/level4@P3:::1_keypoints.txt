- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Builds on and addresses limitations of de Freitas et al.'s δ-cover–based approach.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves exponential convergence rate without impractical δ-cover procedures.  
- Novel algorithmic idea: considers multiple potential bounds and uses the GP prior to prune implausible candidates.  
- Well-written paper with clear motivation, algorithm description, and theoretical analysis.  
- Good positioning within related work and useful connections to bound-based search methods.  
- Publicly available code improves reproducibility.  
- Experimental validation is limited to synthetic functions up to 6 dimensions, reducing generalizability.  
- Lacks evaluations on real-world benchmark tasks to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Incomplete empirical comparisons—does not include information-theoretic baselines like Entropy Search.  
- Experiments omit standard BO practice of multiple random initializations, hurting statistical rigor.  
- Insufficient discussion and analysis of hyperparameter selection and sensitivity.  
- Overall: strong theoretical advance but weakened by limited and incomplete experiments.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions requesting real-world benchmarks, stronger baselines (e.g., Entropy Search), multiple random initializations with statistical reporting, and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
