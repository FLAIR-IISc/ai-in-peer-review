- Novel method: uses interval/space partitioning with UCB to generate samples and avoid internal global optimization.  
- Theoretical strength: proves exponential regret bounds, improving on existing techniques.  
- Presentation: algorithm is clearly described, well-motivated, and illustrated with an example.  
- Major concern: experimental evaluation appears limited to one-dimensional problems, raising scalability doubts.  
- Scalability rationale: partitioning likely requires exponential growth of points in higher dimensions, similar to DIRECT’s degradation.  
- Recommendation: authors should acknowledge dimensionality limitations and discuss mitigation strategies (e.g., adaptive partitioning, dimensionality reduction).  
- Minor point—UCB phrasing: “UCB considered for brevity” is unclear; state whether approach generalizes to other acquisition functions (e.g., EI).  
- Minor point—search description: clarify “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor point—example detail: explain why no function evaluation occurs at n=16 in the example.  
- Overall: significant theoretical contribution and clear presentation, but practical applicability depends on addressing high-dimensional scaling.
