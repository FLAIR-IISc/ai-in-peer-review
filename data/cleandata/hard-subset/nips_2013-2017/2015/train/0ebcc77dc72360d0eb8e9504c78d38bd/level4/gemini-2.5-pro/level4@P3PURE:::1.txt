This paper introduces a novel algorithm for global optimization that generalizes the work of de Freitas et al. (2012). The key contribution is that the proposed method achieves exponential regret without relying on the impractical delta-cover sampling procedure required by the prior work.

The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and effectively situates the new algorithm within the existing literature on bound-based search methods. In this sense, the work is a valuable contribution that deserves the attention of the community.

However, I am recommending a score of 5, as a higher score is precluded by an experimental section that falls below the standard expected for a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper at this conference. My main criticisms of this section are as follows:

-   The authors do not compare the methods across multiple random initializations, which is standard practice for BO methods. Consequently, a meaningful statistical comparison of the methods cannot be obtained from the results provided.
-   Although the paper proposes a bound-based search method, it should be benchmarked against the broader state-of-the-art in Bayesian Optimization. Prominent information-theoretic approaches, such as Entropy Search, are notably absent from the experiments.
-   The evaluation is limited to low-dimensional synthetic functions (with a maximum of six dimensions). The paper would be significantly strengthened by demonstrating the method's performance on real-world scenarios, such as a hyperparameter tuning problem or a wet-lab experiment.

In summary, the paper presents a new global optimization algorithm with interesting and well-supported theoretical results. However, in my opinion, the experimental section is not yet at the level required for a NIPS publication and needs substantial improvement.
