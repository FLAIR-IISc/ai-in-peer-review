This paper introduces a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling – both limitations of existing approaches. The core idea is to circumvent the need for δ-cover sampling, previously considered impractical [1, 2], and eliminate auxiliary optimization, a computationally expensive step common in many Bayesian optimization methods. The authors propose an Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO) algorithm, demonstrating its theoretical convergence properties and empirical performance on several benchmark functions.

The paper effectively summarizes related work, positioning its contribution within the broader context of global optimization, Bayesian optimization, and Gaussian process modeling. It correctly identifies the challenges associated with Lipschitz continuity assumptions and the advancements made by leveraging Gaussian processes to relax these assumptions [3, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15]. The authors accurately represent the recent theoretical progress by de Freitas et al. [1] and highlight the impracticality of their δ-cover sampling requirement, as pointed out by Wang et al. [2]. 

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis, establishing an exponential convergence rate for IMGPO. The proof, outlined and referenced in detail, demonstrates a clear understanding of the underlying mathematical principles.
* **Practical Innovation:** Eliminating both the auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling requirements addresses significant practical barriers in Bayesian optimization, making the method more accessible and efficient.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the algorithm, aided by the illustrative figure (Figure 1). The explanation of the algorithm’s steps and the rationale behind them is particularly commendable.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results consistently demonstrate the superior performance of IMGPO compared to several baseline algorithms (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI) across a diverse set of benchmark functions. The sensitivity analysis regarding Ξmax provides further insight into the algorithm’s behavior.
* **Comprehensive Related Work:** The authors demonstrate a strong grasp of the relevant literature, accurately citing and contextualizing their work within the field. 

**Weaknesses:**

* **Scalability Discussion:** While the authors acknowledge the potential scalability challenges in higher dimensions (Section 4), the discussion is limited. Further exploration of techniques to address this limitation, potentially leveraging ideas from [14, 20], would strengthen the paper.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The choice of hyperparameters (σ = 1, l = 0.25) appears somewhat arbitrary. While the authors justify this by using a standard empirical Bayesian method for updating, a more detailed investigation into the sensitivity of performance to these initial values could be beneficial.
* **Minor Typo/Clarity:** Line 49, the phrase "formulation is redondant" is unclear and likely a typo. Line 72 contains a misplaced parenthesis. These minor issues do not detract significantly from the overall clarity but should be addressed.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a significant theoretical and practical advance in Bayesian optimization.
* The proposed method addresses key limitations of existing approaches, enhancing usability and efficiency.
* The theoretical analysis is rigorous and the experimental results are compelling.
* The paper is well-written, clearly explained, and demonstrates a strong understanding of the related literature.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The discussion of scalability limitations could be more thorough.
* The sensitivity of the algorithm to hyperparameter selection deserves further investigation.
* The minor typographical errors should be corrected.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The proposed method is theoretically sound, practically relevant, and well-supported by experimental evidence. While some minor improvements could be made, the paper meets the criteria for acceptance at NIPS. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
