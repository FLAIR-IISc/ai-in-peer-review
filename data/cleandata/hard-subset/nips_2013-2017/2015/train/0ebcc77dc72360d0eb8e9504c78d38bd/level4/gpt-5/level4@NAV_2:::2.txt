The paper introduces a variant of Bayesian global optimization in which future evaluations are chosen via a space/interval partitioning strategy. By using UCB, it removes the need for an internal nonconvex optimization to select the next query. The theoretical analysis claims exponential simple-regret, representing an improvement over prior approaches. The algorithm is thoroughly described and supported by a clear illustrative example. Overall, the contribution is novel and the presentation is generally clear.

I have one principal concern. The experiments appear to be conducted only on 1D test problems (this is not explicitly stated). I suspect that the hyperrectangle-based partitioning will not scale favorably as dimensionality grows, since the required resolution would increase exponentially. This is reminiscent of the well-known DIRECT method, which is known to degrade significantly in higher dimensions. The paper should at least discuss this issue. While this does not affect the theoretical results, it is highly relevant for practical applicability.

Minor comments:
- p.2: UCB is used “for brevity.” Does the approach extend to, e.g., expected improvement, or is UCB essential here?
- p.3: “...we simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.” This statement was unclear to me; please clarify.
- p.4: “At n=16, the far right... but no function evaluation occurs.” A brief explanation of why no evaluation is performed would improve clarity.

In summary, the paper proposes a Bayesian optimization method that selects points via interval partitioning and claims exponential regret. The work seems sound and original, but the empirical evaluation appears limited to 1D cases.
