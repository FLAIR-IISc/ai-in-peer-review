This paper presents a type of Bayesian global optimization, in which future samples are generated through a space/interval partitioning algorithm.

Using UCB, it avoids the (internal) global optimization problem of selecting the next sample.

Theoretical analysis shows that this approach gives theoretical improvement (exponential regression), which improves previous work.

The algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example.

This paper makes a new contribution and is clearly presented overall.

I have reservations with this paper.

The experimental results seem to be only for 1D testing tasks (not explicitly stated).

I suspect that the interval partitioning approach does not scale well for higher dimensions (hypercubes) because the "resolution" would need to be increased rapidly.

This approach is related to the famous DIRECT algorithm, which is seriously affected when the dimensionality of the problem increases.

I think the paper needs to say at least something about this.

It does not change the theoretical contribution, but is clearly important for any practical purpose.
