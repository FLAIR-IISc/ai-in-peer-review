- Proposes a novel global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, generalizing Freitas et al. (2012).  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and situates the algorithm within the literature on bound-based search methods.  
- Reviewer believes the work deserves attention and interest from the community.  
- Overall score 5, withheld from higher due to experimental weaknesses.  
- Experiments do not evaluate multiple initial function evaluations, preventing meaningful statistical comparisons.  
- No comparisons to state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods (e.g., information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search).  
- All experiments are on synthetic benchmarks of dimensionality ≤6; lack of real-world tasks (e.g., wet-lab or parameter tuning) limits practical validation.  
- Conclusion: strong theoretical contributions but experimental section falls short of NIPS expectations.
