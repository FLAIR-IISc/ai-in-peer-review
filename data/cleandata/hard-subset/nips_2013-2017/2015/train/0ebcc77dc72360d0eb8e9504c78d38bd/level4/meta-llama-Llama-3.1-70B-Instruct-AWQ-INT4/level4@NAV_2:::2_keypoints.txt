- Introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a new Bayesian global optimization algorithm.  
- Claims exponential convergence (theoretical improvement in regret) without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example.  
- Theoretical analysis supports improvements over prior work.  
- Major reservation: experiments are only on low-dimensional test functions.  
- Concern that interval/hyperrectangle partitioning likely does not scale to higher dimensions (resolution grows exponentially).  
- Approach is related to DIRECT, which is known to perform poorly in high dimensions.  
- Paper should discuss practical limitations in high-dimensional spaces and suggest possible modifications or remedies.  
- Minor: clarify whether UCB is used only for brevity or if alternatives (e.g., expected improvement) are possible.  
- Minor: clarify the statement about "simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds."  
- Minor: explain the comment "At n=16, the far right...but no function evaluation occurs."  
- Strength: novel and sound contribution to Bayesian global optimization.  
- Strength: clear algorithm description and example.  
- Strength: solid theoretical improvements demonstrated.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing the scalability discussion for high dimensions and clarifying unclear statements.
