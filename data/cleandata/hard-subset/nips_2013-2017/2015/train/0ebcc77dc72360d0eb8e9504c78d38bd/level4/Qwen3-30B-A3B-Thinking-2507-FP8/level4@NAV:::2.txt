

The paper introduces a variant of Bayesian Global Optimization that generates future samples via a space/interval partitioning algorithm. By employing UCB, it circumvents the internal global optimization challenge of selecting the next sample. Theoretical analysis demonstrates that this approach achieves exponential regret bounds, representing a significant improvement over prior work. The algorithm is clearly described and supported by an illustrative example. The contribution is novel and the presentation is overall clear.  

I have one reservation: the experimental results are limited to 1D test functions (not explicitly stated). Given that interval partitioning requires resolution to grow exponentially with dimensionality, I suspect the approach does not scale well to higher dimensions (hyperrectangles). This is particularly concerning as the method bears resemblance to the DIRECT algorithm, which suffers from poor scalability in higher dimensions. The paper should address this limitation, as it is critical for practical applications despite not affecting the theoretical contribution.  

Minor comments:  
- p.2: UCB is noted as "for brevity." Does this imply the method could accommodate alternatives like expected improvement? The text suggests UCB is mandatory.  
- p.3: The statement "...we simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds" is unclear.  
- p.4: The note "At n=16, the far right...but no function evaluation occurs" requires clarification.  

The work is theoretically sound and novel but appears limited to 1D test problems.
