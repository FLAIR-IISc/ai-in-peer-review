- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian global optimization method removing auxiliary optimization and d-cover sampling; clearly motivated and competitive empirically.
- Clear exposition and strong organization, with contributions stated up front.
- Pedagogical clarity: accessible illustrative example and step-by-step implementation description.
- Practical relevance: addresses usability/robustness pain points in GP-based BO.
- Good literature integration showing relation to methods that do and do not use continuity estimation.
- Empirical results are encouraging and suggest competitiveness in realistic settings.
- Theoretical guarantees are unclear—needs statements on convergence, simple regret, and whether known rates are recovered.
- Computational complexity and scalability are not analyzed—runtime per iteration, memory, wall-clock comparisons, and dimensional scaling should be reported.
- Sensitivity and robustness to kernel choice, hyperparameter priors, noise levels, and hyperparameter learning strategies need evaluation.
- Comparative coverage should be expanded: systematic comparisons across method classes and ablations to identify which IMGPO components drive gains.
- Implementation clarity lacking in parts—add pseudocode annotations for candidate selection, initialization, batch handling, stopping criteria, and public code or minimal reference implementation.
- Discuss applicability and extensions: handling of constraints, multi-fidelity, batch/parallel evaluations, and noisy expensive objectives.
- Minor: define “infinite-metric” early and ensure consistent notation.
- Minor: clearly state any heuristics (e.g., exploration–exploitation) and show their effects via ablations.
- Minor: highlight failure modes or tasks where IMGPO underperforms for balance.
- Overall: well-written, timely contribution that would be stronger with clearer theory, complexity analysis, and more systematic empirical validation.
- Recommendation: Accept (leaning strong accept if the authors incorporate clarifications on theory, complexity, and broader comparisons).
