Summary
This paper introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian global optimization algorithm that eliminates the need for auxiliary optimization and d-cover sampling. The approach is clearly motivated and presented, and the manuscript is well structured. The authors provide both a high-level intuitive explanation and a programmatic description that facilitate understanding and replication. Empirically, the method appears competitive and promising.

Strengths
- Clear exposition and organization: The main contributions are stated up front and supported throughout. The flow from intuition to algorithmic detail is effective.
- Pedagogical clarity: The illustrative example in Section 3.2 offers an accessible, high-level understanding of the algorithm’s behavior. Section 3.3 complements this with a concrete, step-by-step description suitable for implementation.
- Practical relevance: Removing reliance on auxiliary optimization and d-cover sampling addresses two common pain points in Bayesian optimization, improving usability and potentially robustness.
- Literature integration: The paper thoughtfully connects strands of prior work that rely on continuity (or smoothness) estimation with those that do not, helping to position IMGPO within the broader landscape.
- Empirical results: The experiments are encouraging and suggest that IMGPO is competitive in realistic settings.

Weaknesses and Suggestions
- Theoretical guarantees: While the motivation for avoiding d-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization is strong, the paper would benefit from a clearer articulation of theoretical properties. For instance:
  - What convergence or simple regret guarantees (if any) hold under common GP assumptions?
  - Are there conditions under which IMGPO recovers known rates, or does it trade off some guarantees for practicality?
- Computational complexity and scalability: Please discuss runtime per iteration and memory usage relative to standard GP-BO baselines, especially as the number of evaluations grows. Explicit wall-clock comparisons would strengthen the empirical case. Clarifying how IMGPO scales with dimensionality and whether any mechanisms are included for high-dimensional settings (e.g., embeddings, additive structure) would also be valuable.
- Sensitivity and robustness: An analysis of sensitivity to kernel choice, hyperparameter priors, and noise levels would help practitioners. Does IMGPO require different hyperparameter learning strategies than standard GP-BO? Is it robust to mis-specified kernels or heteroscedastic noise?
- Comparative coverage: Since a core claim is to unify or bridge ideas from methods with and without continuity estimation, consider adding:
  - A more systematic comparison across both categories, with careful tuning and identical budgets.
  - An ablation showing which elements of IMGPO drive the gains (e.g., the specific acquisition or selection strategy).
- Clarity on implementation details: Section 3.3 is helpful; still, a few additions could further aid reproducibility:
  - Pseudocode annotations explaining how candidate points are selected without auxiliary optimization.
  - Details on initialization, batch handling (if any), and stopping criteria.
  - Public code or at least a minimal reference implementation would increase impact.
- Applicability and extensions: Briefly discuss how IMGPO would handle common extensions such as constraints, multi-fidelity evaluations, batch/parallel evaluations, and expensive-to-evaluate but noisy objectives.

Minor Comments
- Define the “infinite-metric” terminology early, with an intuitive explanation, and ensure consistent notation throughout.
- If any heuristics are used (e.g., for exploration–exploitation balance), state them clearly and describe their effect in ablations.
- Highlight failure modes or tasks where IMGPO does not outperform baselines to provide a balanced picture.

Overall Assessment
This is a well-written and timely contribution to Bayesian global optimization. By avoiding auxiliary optimization and d-cover sampling, IMGPO addresses practical challenges that often limit the applicability of GP-based methods. The exposition is strong, and the examples and programmatic description significantly lower the barrier to adoption. With clearer theoretical positioning, explicit runtime/scalability analyses, and more systematic empirical comparisons (including sensitivity studies and ablations), the paper would be even stronger.

Recommendation: Accept (leaning strong accept if the authors can incorporate clarifications on theory, complexity, and broader comparisons).
