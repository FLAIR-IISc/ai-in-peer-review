The paper presents a new algorithm for global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, generalizing the work of de Freitas et al. (2012) that relies on an impractical sampling procedure. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the role of the new algorithm within the current literature of bound-based search methods is well-explained. This work deserves attention from the community.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm, including a detailed description of the Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO) method and its relationship to previous algorithms. The theoretical results, including the exponential convergence rate and the regret bound, are well-presented and provide a solid foundation for the algorithm.

However, the experimental section falls short of the standard expected in a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper. The authors do not compare the methods for multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, which is a common practice in BO. This limitation makes it challenging to obtain a meaningful statistical comparison of the methods.

Furthermore, the authors should compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches, including information-theoretic methods like Entropy Search. The experiments are limited to synthetic functions with a maximum dimension of 6, and no real-world experiments, such as parameter tuning in machine learning or wet lab experiments, are presented to illustrate the performance of the method in practical scenarios.

In conclusion, the paper presents an interesting new global optimization algorithm with some valuable theoretical results. However, the experimental section requires more comprehensive comparisons and real-world applications to demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness and robustness.
