- Introduces a Bayesian Global Optimization variant that uses space-partitioning to select samples.
- Leverages the UCB criterion to avoid directly optimizing an acquisition function for the next evaluation.
- Theoretical analysis claims improved performance, demonstrating exponential regret that outperforms prior results.
- Algorithm is well-described, includes a helpful illustrative example, and the paper is clearly presented and novel.
- Main concern: experiments appear to be only on one-dimensional functions (not stated), raising doubts about scalability to higher dimensions.
- Scalability worry: interval/hyperrectangle partitioning likely requires exponentially many partitions with dimension, similar to DIRECT, which may hurt practical applicability.
- Minor: p.2 — clarify whether other acquisition functions (e.g., Expected Improvement) can be used or if UCB is essential.
- Minor: p.3 — the phrase about conducting “global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds” is unclear and needs rephrasing or elaboration.
- Minor: p.4 — in the n=16 example, explain why the far-right location does not receive a function evaluation.
