- Proposes a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and attains exponential regret.
- Extends and improves on Freitas et al. (2012) by removing an impractical sampling scheme.
- Manuscript is clearly written, easy to follow, and well situated within bound-based search literature.
- Work is theoretically interesting and merits community attention.
- Reviewer assigns score 5 due to weaknesses in empirical evaluation.
- Lacks multiple initializations for function evaluations, which is standard in BO and needed for meaningful statistics.
- Missing comparisons to strong contemporary BO baselines, notably information-theoretic methods like Entropy Search.
- Empirical scope is limited to synthetic functions up to 6 dimensions; no real-world benchmarks (e.g., wet-lab optimization or hyperparameter tuning).
- Recommendation: strengthen experiments with multiple-start protocols, comprehensive state-of-the-art baselines, and real-world tasks to meet conference standards.
