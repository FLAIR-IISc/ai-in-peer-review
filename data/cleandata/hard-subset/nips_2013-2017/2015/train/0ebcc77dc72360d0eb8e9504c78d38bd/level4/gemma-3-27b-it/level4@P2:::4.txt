This paper introduces a Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling, both limitations of existing approaches. The core idea is to address the difficulty of obtaining tight bounds in Gaussian Process (GP) optimization by leveraging an unknown semi-metric encoding inherent continuity at a global optimizer. The method, termed Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), combines GP-UCB with a hierarchical partitioning strategy and demonstrates strong performance across several benchmark functions.

The paper builds upon recent work in Bayesian optimization, particularly de Freitas et al. [1], which established exponential convergence with δ-cover sampling – a computationally demanding requirement. Wang et al. [2] attempted to address this with a combination of GP-UCB and a hierarchical partitioning method, but only achieved polynomial convergence. This work directly tackles the open problem highlighted by Wang et al. [2] of achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  The approach relates to broader concepts in bound-based search methods, drawing connections to Lipschitz optimization and A* search, but innovatively incorporates an infinite set of potential bounds.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The IMGPO algorithm presents a novel approach to Bayesian optimization, achieving exponential convergence without the impractical δ-cover sampling. This addresses a significant limitation of previous work [1].
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a theoretical analysis demonstrating the exponential convergence rate (Theorem 1) and carefully outlines the assumptions required for this guarantee. The analysis also offers insight into the interplay between GP-derived bounds and the unknown semi-metric.
* **Clarity & Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains complex concepts, even for readers less familiar with GP optimization. The use of Figure 1 effectively illustrates the algorithm's procedure. The inclusion of pseudocode (Algorithm 1) is very helpful.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results (Figure 2) demonstrate the effectiveness of IMGPO across a variety of benchmark functions, generally outperforming SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI and GP-EI.
* **Insightful Discussion:**  The discussion section provides valuable commentary on the impact of different components, such as the role of the GP bound and the potential for scalability improvements.  Remarks 1, 2 and 3 offer valuable insights.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Scalability Concerns:** While promising results are shown, the analysis acknowledges potential scalability limitations as input dimensionality increases (Section 4). The authors suggest future work leveraging additional assumptions to address this.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:**  The performance appears somewhat sensitive to the choice of Ξmax.  While the effect of this parameter is discussed, further investigation into its optimal setting and robustness would be beneficial.
* **Minor Technicality:** Line 49 contains a redundant formulation. Line 72 has a misplaced parenthesis.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a significant advancement in Bayesian optimization, addressing a key limitation of existing methods.
* The theoretical analysis provides strong justification for the algorithm’s effectiveness.
* The experimental results demonstrate compelling performance improvements.
* The writing is exceptionally clear and well-organized, making the paper accessible to a broad audience.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The scalability limitations raise concerns about the practical applicability of the algorithm to very high-dimensional problems.
* While minor, the technical issues with line 49 and 72 should be addressed.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant contributions to the field of Bayesian optimization. The novelty of the approach, coupled with the rigorous theoretical analysis and compelling empirical results, make it a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings. The acknowledged scalability concerns and minor technical points are not substantial enough to warrant rejection. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the noted technical issues and potentially elaborate on strategies for addressing the scalability limitations in future work.
