This paper introduces a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm, IMGPO, which achieves an exponential convergence rate. The key contribution is that it accomplishes this without relying on auxiliary non-convex optimization to maximize an acquisition function, or the impractical δ-cover sampling procedure required by previous work with similar theoretical guarantees [1]. The proposed method instead uses a hierarchical partitioning scheme informed by a Gaussian Process prior, effectively combining ideas from optimistic optimization and GP-based methods.

**Strengths:**
*   **Originality and Significance:** The work addresses a well-known open problem in the field: how to design a practical GP-based optimization algorithm with an exponential convergence rate [2]. The theoretical result, which removes the need for δ-cover sampling, is a significant and elegant contribution.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow. The authors effectively situate their work within the broader context of bound-based search methods, making the motivation and novelty of their approach clear to the reader.
*   **Theoretical Quality:** The theoretical analysis appears sound and provides a novel framework for optimization that considers an infinite set of possible semi-metrics. This perspective is insightful and could potentially inspire further research combining different families of optimization algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**
The primary weaknesses of this paper are concentrated in the experimental validation, which does not feel comprehensive enough to fully support the claims of the algorithm's practical advantages.
*   **Lack of Statistical Rigor:** The experiments for most algorithms are based on single deterministic runs. Standard practice in the Bayesian optimization literature is to perform multiple runs with different random initializations to report mean performance and a measure of variance (e.g., standard deviation or error bars). Without this, it is difficult to make a statistically meaningful assessment of the algorithm's performance relative to the baselines.
*   **Incomplete Baselines:** While the proposed method is a bound-based search algorithm, it is positioned as a contribution to the Bayesian optimization literature. As such, the empirical comparison should include the current state-of-the-art in this area. The experiments are missing comparisons to powerful information-theoretic methods such as Entropy Search or Predictive Entropy Search, which are standard benchmarks in modern BO papers.
*   **Limited Scope of Evaluation:** The empirical evaluation is conducted exclusively on synthetic functions, with most benchmarks being of low dimensionality. To demonstrate the practical utility and significance of the proposed method, it would be highly beneficial to include results from a real-world application, such as hyperparameter tuning of a machine learning model. This would provide more convincing evidence of the algorithm's performance in realistic scenarios.

**Overall Assessment:**
This paper presents a theoretically interesting and novel algorithm for global optimization. The achievement of an exponential regret bound without δ-cover sampling is a noteworthy theoretical advancement. However, the experimental section is below the standard expected for a top-tier conference like NIPS. The lack of statistical comparisons, state-of-the-art baselines, and real-world application studies weakens the paper's overall impact and makes it difficult to verify the practical claims. While the theoretical contributions are valuable, the paper would be much stronger if the empirical evaluation were substantially improved.
