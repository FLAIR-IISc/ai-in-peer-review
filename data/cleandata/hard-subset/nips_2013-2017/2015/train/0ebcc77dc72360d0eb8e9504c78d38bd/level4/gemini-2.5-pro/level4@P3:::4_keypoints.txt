- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary non-convex optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Combines hierarchical partitioning (like DIRECT/SOO) with GP-UCB used for pruning rather than direct selection.  
- Implicitly considers an infinite family of smoothness assumptions (semi-metrics) around the optimum, leveraging SOO-like ideas.  
- Theorem 1 provides a formal proof of exponential convergence, improving over polynomial bounds of practical alternatives like BaMSOO.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and shows IMGPO outperforming strong baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) across varied benchmarks.  
- Method elegantly synthesizes bound-based GP methods and partitioning-based methods, offering a novel and powerful hybrid approach.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and accessible, with effective algorithm exposition and helpful figures.  
- Addresses a significant open problem in Bayesian optimization with likely high impact for researchers and practitioners.  
- Removes two major practical drawbacks of prior work: avoids costly acquisition maximization and impractical δ-cover sampling.  
- Scalability to high-dimensional inputs is a concern; convergence constant λ may approach 1 as dimension D increases (curse of dimensionality).  
- Suggests embedding IMGPO in REMBO but lacks explicit analysis of infinite-metric exploration loss ρ̄t as dimensionality grows.  
- Insufficient practical guidance on hyperparameters (Ξmax and η); needs sensitivity analysis or heuristics for new problems.  
- Minor presentation issues: redundant abstract phrasing and potential confusion using lowercase `l` for a kernel hyperparameter.  
- Strong arguments for acceptance: solves a long-standing problem, provides the first practical GP-based exponential-rate algorithm, offers rigorous theory and strong experiments, and has clear presentation.  
- Main argument against acceptance: limited discussion/detail on scalability to higher dimensions.  
- Reviewer’s overall recommendation: strong accept — strengths far outweigh minor weaknesses.
