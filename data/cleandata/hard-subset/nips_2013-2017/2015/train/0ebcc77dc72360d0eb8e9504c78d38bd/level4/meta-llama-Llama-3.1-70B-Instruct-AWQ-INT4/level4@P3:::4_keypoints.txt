- Proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with strong balance between theory and practical considerations.  
- Provides theoretical foundations, convergence analysis, and regret bounds for IMGPO.  
- Supports theoretical claims with comprehensive experimental results on various objective functions.  
- Experimental results show IMGPO outperforms state-of-the-art algorithms in most cases.  
- Includes detailed comparisons highlighting IMGPO's advantages and limitations relative to other methods.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and uses effective graphs and figures.  
- Reviewer rates Quality, Clarity, Originality, and Significance each 9/10.  
- Recommends acceptance to NIPS 2015.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective approach, thorough theoretical and experimental analysis, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: may be challenging for readers unfamiliar with Gaussian processes and global optimization; some technical details could be hard to follow.  
- Overall weaknesses are minor and outweighed by the paper's strengths.
