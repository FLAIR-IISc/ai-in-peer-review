- Proposes a hierarchical space-partitioning variant of Bayesian Global Optimization using UCB on intervals to avoid auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves exponential regret, improving on existing BGO guarantees.  
- Algorithm presentation is clear and supported by a helpful step-by-step example.  
- Novel approach and strong theoretical advance in sample-selection for BGO.  
- Effective methodological advantage: avoids the usual inner-loop global optimization of the acquisition function.  
- Primary weakness: scalability to higher dimensions is unclear and likely poor due to interval → hyperrectangle partitioning.  
- Experiments appear to be restricted to one-dimensional test functions, which limits evidence of practical performance.  
- Partitioning strategy is conceptually similar to DIRECT and likely suffers from the curse of dimensionality.  
- Paper should explicitly discuss expected performance and limitations in higher-dimensional settings.  
- Question: Is UCB essential for the theoretical results, or can the framework extend to other acquisition functions (e.g., Expected Improvement)?  
- Request for clarification: explain the phrase about "simultaneously conduct global and local searches" and why at n=16 a partition occurs without a function evaluation.
