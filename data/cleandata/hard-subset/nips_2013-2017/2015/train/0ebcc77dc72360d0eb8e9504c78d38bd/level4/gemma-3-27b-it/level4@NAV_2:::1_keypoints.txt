- Proposes a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm with exponential convergence that avoids auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling, improving on de Freitas et al.

- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and effectively situates the approach among bound-based search methods.

- Motivation and contributions are clearly articulated and technical details are comprehensible.

- Considered a valuable addition to the field deserving attention.

- Reviewer recommends a score of 5 but not higher due to experimental shortcomings.

- Experiments do not use multiple random initial evaluations, preventing statistically rigorous comparisons.

- Lacks comparison to state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods (e.g., information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search).

- Evaluation is limited to synthetic benchmarks up to 6D and omits real-world parameter tuning experiments, weakening practical validation.
