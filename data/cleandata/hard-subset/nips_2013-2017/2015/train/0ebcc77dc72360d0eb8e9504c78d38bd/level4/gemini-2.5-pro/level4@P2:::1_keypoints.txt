- Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based BO algorithm that claims exponential convergence without auxiliary non-convex optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: hybridize GP-UCB with hierarchical partitioning (SOO-like) to consider an infinite family of semi-metric bounds and use GP bounds to prune the search.  
- Aimed to be robust to GP prior misspecification while leveraging GP information.  
- Theoretical contribution: sound analysis with an exponential regret bound (Theorem 1) and insightful trade-off discussion (Remarks 1–3).  
- Originality & significance: novel unification addressing an open problem and offering an elegant approach.  
- Clarity: well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.  
- Main weakness: experimental evaluation is insufficient for top-tier BO standards.  
- Lack of statistical rigor: appears to report single runs/seeds without averages or confidence intervals.  
- Incomplete baselines: omits strong contemporary methods, notably information-theoretic acquisitions (e.g., ES/PES).  
- No real-world evaluation: experiments limited to synthetic, low-dimensional benchmarks; no practical tasks like hyperparameter tuning.  
- Suggested improvements: run multiple random initializations with error bars, include stronger baselines (ES/PES), and add a real-world experiment (e.g., neural network hyperparameter tuning).  
- Overall verdict: strong theoretical contribution but empirical evidence is currently inadequate, placing the paper marginally below acceptance.
