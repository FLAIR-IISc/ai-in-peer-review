- Proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO) that removes the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling while retaining exponential convergence.
- Well-structured paper with clear motivation, concise introduction, and linkage to limitations of Lipschitz assumptions and prior Bayesian optimization methods.
- Section 3.2’s illustrative example clarifies algorithm mechanics; Section 3.3 provides an implementable programmatic description.
- Experiments show promising performance and synergy between continuity-estimating and non-continuity-estimating Bayesian optimization ideas.
- Strength — Technical soundness: addresses δ-cover sampling limitation and proves exponential convergence (Theorem 1).
- Strength — Clarity: exceptionally well-written and organized with reproducibility-focused descriptions.
- Strength — Originality: novel use of an unknown semi-metric and considering infinitely many potential bounds.
- Strength — Significance: removal of δ-cover and auxiliary optimization makes IMGPO practically valuable and competitive with SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.
- Weakness — Scalability: potential issues as input dimension grows; may require additional assumptions for high-dimensional problems.
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: experiments fix Ξmax and hyperparameters; lacks thorough sensitivity analysis or guidance.
- Weakness — Literature connection: could better relate IMGPO to broader robust optimization and explicit model uncertainty approaches.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses practical limitation, strong theoretical results, clear presentation, promising empirical gains, and novel perspective.
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about high-dimensional scalability and limited hyperparameter analysis.
- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically sound, and well-presented contribution; recommended for acceptance despite noted concerns.
