- Novel algorithm: introduces a Bayesian Global Optimization variant using space/interval partitioning and UCB to avoid internal global optimization.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides exponential regret bounds that improve on prior work.  
- Presentation: algorithm is well-described with clear exposition and an illustrative example.  
- Experimental limitation: results are confined to 1D test functions and this is not explicitly stated in the paper.  
- Scalability concern: interval partitioning likely requires exponential resolution growth with dimension, raising serious scalability issues.  
- Practical applicability: method may suffer the same high-dimensional degradation as DIRECT, making real-world use questionable despite theoretical gains.  
- Minor: the claim that UCB is used "for brevity" needs justification and comparison to other acquisitions (e.g., expected improvement).  
- Minor: the phrase on page 3 about "simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds" is ambiguous and needs clarification.  
- Minor: the claim that "no function evaluation occurs at n=16" contradicts the described evaluation process and requires explanation.
