This paper presents a variant of Bayesian Global Optimization that generates future samples through a space/interval partitioning algorithm. By utilizing Upper Confidence Bound (UCB), this approach avoids the internal global optimization problem of choosing the next sample. The authors provide a theoretical analysis, demonstrating that their method achieves exponential regret, thereby improving upon previous work.

The algorithm is clearly described, explained, and illustrated with an example, making it easy to follow. The paper makes a novel contribution to the field and is overall well-presented. However, I have one major reservation regarding the experimental results. It appears that the results are only presented for 1D test functions, although this is not explicitly stated. I suspect that the interval partitioning approach may not scale well to higher dimensions, where hyperrectangles would require an exponentially growing "resolution." This concern is exacerbated by the fact that the approach is related to the well-known DIRECT algorithm, which is known to suffer from poor performance as the dimensionality of the problem increases.

I believe it is essential to address this limitation in the paper, as it significantly impacts the practical applicability of the proposed method. Although it does not affect the theoretical contribution, it is crucial to discuss the potential scalability issues to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the algorithm's strengths and weaknesses.

Minor comments:

On page 2, the authors mention that UCB is used "for brevity." It would be beneficial to clarify whether other acquisition functions, such as expected improvement, could be used instead. The current presentation suggests that UCB is the only viable option.

The statement on page 3, "...we simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds," is unclear and requires further explanation.

On page 4, the authors mention that "At n=16, the far right...but no function evaluation occurs." It would be helpful to provide a brief explanation for this observation to enhance clarity.

In conclusion, while the paper presents a sound and novel contribution to Bayesian Global Optimization, its evaluation appears to be limited to 1D test problems. Addressing the potential scalability issues and providing clearer explanations for certain statements would strengthen the paper and enhance its overall impact.
