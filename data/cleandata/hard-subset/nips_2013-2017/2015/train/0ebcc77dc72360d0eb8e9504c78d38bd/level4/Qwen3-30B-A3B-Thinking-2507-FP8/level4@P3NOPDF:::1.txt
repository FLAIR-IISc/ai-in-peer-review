

The paper introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that eliminates the need for delta-cover sampling while achieving exponential regret, thereby generalizing Freitas et al. (2012) and addressing their reliance on an impractical sampling procedure. The theoretical contribution is significant, and the manuscript is exceptionally well-written, with a clear exposition of the algorithm’s position within existing bounded-based search literature. This work merits substantial attention from the optimization and Bayesian optimization (BO) communities due to its rigorous theoretical foundation and potential to simplify practical implementation.

The paper’s primary strength lies in its theoretical advancement: the avoidance of delta-cover sampling—a computationally prohibitive step in prior methods—while maintaining exponential regret guarantees. The derivation is sound, and the connection to Freitas et al. is well-articulated, demonstrating a meaningful extension of existing theory. The clarity of presentation ensures the contribution is accessible to readers familiar with BO fundamentals.

However, the experimental evaluation falls significantly short of the standards expected in a top-tier NIPS paper, particularly for a work centered on BO. The following limitations undermine the paper’s impact:

1.  **Lack of Statistical Rigor in Comparisons:** The experiments do not account for multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, a standard practice in BO to ensure fair statistical comparison. Without this, the reported performance metrics (e.g., convergence speed, final objective value) lack statistical significance, making it impossible to draw robust conclusions about the algorithm’s relative efficacy.

2.  **Inadequate Benchmarking Against State-of-the-Art:** The authors propose a bounded-based search method but omit critical comparisons with leading BO approaches. Information-theoretic methods like Entropy Search (Hennig & Osborne, 2012) and Knowledge-Gradient (Frazier et al., 2009), which are widely regarded as benchmarks for BO performance, are not included. This omission prevents readers from assessing whether the proposed method offers tangible advantages over established techniques in terms of sample efficiency or solution quality.

3.  **Over-Reliance on Synthetic Functions:** All experiments are conducted on low-dimensional synthetic test functions (maximum dimensionality 6), with no validation on real-world problems. This is a critical gap, as BO is frequently applied to challenging real-world scenarios (e.g., hyperparameter tuning in machine learning, robotics, or experimental design). The absence of a real-world case study or wet-lab experiment (e.g., optimizing parameters for a physical system or a non-trivial machine learning pipeline) severely limits the paper’s practical relevance and fails to demonstrate scalability or utility beyond controlled settings.

While the theoretical novelty is compelling, these experimental shortcomings prevent the work from fully convincing the community of its practical value. The field of BO demands rigorous empirical validation on diverse benchmarks, including real-world applications, to justify new methods. The current experimental section does not meet this standard, particularly given the high bar set by recent NIPS publications in optimization.

In summary, the paper makes a valuable theoretical contribution to global optimization, and its clarity and precision are commendable. However, the experimental evaluation lacks the depth, breadth, and statistical rigor expected for a NIPS publication. The absence of benchmark comparisons against modern BO methods and the exclusive use of synthetic functions are particularly concerning for a method positioned as a practical alternative to existing techniques.

I recommend acceptance (score 5) due to the strong theoretical core, but the experimental flaws prevent a higher score. The authors should address these limitations in a revised version, particularly by incorporating statistical comparisons across multiple initializations, benchmarking against Entropy Search and similar methods, and adding at least one non-trivial real-world application. Without these improvements, the paper’s impact will be constrained to the theoretical community, rather than bridging the gap to practical BO applications.
