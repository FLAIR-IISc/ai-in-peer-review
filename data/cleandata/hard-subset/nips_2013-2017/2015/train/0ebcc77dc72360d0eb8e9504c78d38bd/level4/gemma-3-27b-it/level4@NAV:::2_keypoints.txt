- Introduces a novel Bayesian Global Optimization method using space/interval partitioning to select samples, avoiding internal global optimization for UCB.  
- Provides theoretical analysis with improved exponential regret bounds over existing methods.  
- Algorithm is well-defined and effectively illustrated with a clear example; paper is valuable and clearly written.  
- Experimental evaluation appears limited to one-dimensional test functions (not explicitly stated).  
- Concern that interval partitioning may not scale to higher dimensions; required resolution likely grows exponentially (similar to DIRECT).  
- Theoretical results remain valid, but the paper lacks discussion of practical scalability to higher dimensions.  
- Clarify the justification for using UCB “for brevity” on page 2 and whether other acquisition functions (e.g., Expected Improvement) can be accommodated.  
- Clarify the ambiguous statement on page 3 about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Expand the explanation on page 4 for why no function evaluation occurred at n=16.
