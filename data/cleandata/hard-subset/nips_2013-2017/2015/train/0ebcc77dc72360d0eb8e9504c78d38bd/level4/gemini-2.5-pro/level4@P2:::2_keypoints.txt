- Proposes IMGPO: a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence for deterministic black-box optimization.  
- Claims to avoid costly auxiliary non-convex optimization for next-point selection and removes the impractical δ-cover sampling requirement from prior exponential-convergence work.  
- Core method: combines GP-UCB with hierarchical partitioning, using hyperrectangle center values (like DIRECT/SOO) and GP UCB to prune the search tree.  
- Introduces an “infinite family” of Lipschitz-like bounds implicitly considered by the algorithm to guide exploration.  
- Main theoretical result: a high-probability regret bound that decreases exponentially with the number of evaluations.  
- Strength: addresses an important open problem (fast exponential convergence) and offers a novel hybrid of bound-free partitioning and model-based search.  
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis with sound proofs and the new concept of “infinite-metric exploration loss” to analyze trade-offs.  
- Strength: clear presentation, good pseudocode, and an illustrative 1D figure that aids intuition.  
- Weakness: scalability—hierarchical partitioning likely suffers from the curse of dimensionality; standalone IMGPO’s performance in higher D is unclear.  
- Weakness: experimental high-dimensional result (1000D) relies on REMBO embeddings, so it doesn’t demonstrate standalone IMGPO scalability.  
- Suggestion: clarify the algorithm’s intuitive mechanics for “searching over the infinite set of candidate bounds” and the interplay of the described steps.  
- Suggestion: discuss whether theoretical guarantees depend fundamentally on UCB or if other acquisitions (e.g., EI) can be incorporated.  
- Suggestion: add standalone experiments on moderate dimensions (≈10–20D) to show how IMGPO degrades with D.  
- Suggestion: explicitly explain the Figure 1 step where no evaluation occurs at n=16 (i.e., UCBs were below the current best), to aid reader understanding.
