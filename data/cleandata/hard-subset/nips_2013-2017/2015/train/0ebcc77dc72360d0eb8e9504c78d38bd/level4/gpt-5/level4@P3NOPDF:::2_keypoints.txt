- Proposes a partition-based Bayesian Global Optimization variant that uses UCB to avoid inner acquisition optimization.
- Theoretical analysis claims exponential regret bounds, an improvement over typical sublinear rates.
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example.
- Strength: partitioned UCB eliminates the costly inner global optimization step in BGO.
- Strength: novel and well-situated relative to existing BGO literature.
- Empirical evaluation appears limited to one-dimensional test functions, which is a major limitation.
- Partitioning scales poorly in higher dimensions (exponential growth of hyperrectangles), raising scalability concerns.
- Paper should analyze how candidate region count grows with dimension and budget, and propose pruning/heuristics.
- Unclear whether theoretical guarantees extend to higher dimensions or depend on prohibitive dimension-dependent constants.
- Request: clarify if other acquisitions (EI, PI, Thompson sampling) fit the framework and which parts of analysis rely on UCB properties.
- Request: define precisely what “global” versus “local” search means in the partitioning scheme and how candidates are generated/prioritized.
- Request: explain the example note (“no function evaluation occurs” at n=16)—pruning, dominance, or tie-breaking?
- Request: explicitly state assumptions needed for exponential regret (noise, smoothness/kernel, hyperparameter knowledge).
- Request: provide per-iteration computational complexity for GP updates, candidate subregion maintenance, partitioning/pruning, and memory use.
- Suggestion: include higher-dimensional experiments (d=2–5) on benchmarks, report wall-clock time and compare to DIRECT, GP-UCB, EI.
- If multi-D experiments infeasible, add detailed scalability analysis (candidate growth, pruning effectiveness, dimensional dependence of bounds).
- Suggestion: discuss connections/distinctions with DIRECT and whether GP+UCB avoids DIRECT’s pathological behaviors.
- Suggestion: clarify GP hyperparameter handling (fixed, re-estimated, marginalized) since it affects UCB and theory.
- Suggestion: provide stopping criteria, tie-breaking rules, recommended budgets, and sensitivity/initialization guidance for practitioners.
- Minor edits: explicitly state compatibility of other acquisitions if UCB is “for brevity,” rephrase ambiguous sentences, and explicitly report experiment dimensionality.
