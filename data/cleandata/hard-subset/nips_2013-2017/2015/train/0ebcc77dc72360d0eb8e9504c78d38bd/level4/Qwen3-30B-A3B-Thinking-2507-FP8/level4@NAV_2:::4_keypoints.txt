- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.  
- Resolves the open problem from Wang et al. by exploring infinitely many candidate bounds under a semi-metric assumption.  
- Combines hierarchical partitioning (SOO-like) with GP-based upper confidence bounds; formalized in Algorithm 1 and Theorem 1.  
- Theorem 1 establishes regret O(λ^{N+N_gp}) with λ < 1, proving exponential convergence.  
- Originality: first method to attain exponential convergence without impractical δ-cover sampling.  
- Quality: rigorous theoretical analysis, quantification of GP tightness effects (Remarks 1–3), and clear connections to related work.  
- Experiments: outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI across eight multimodal benchmarks, including a 1000D extension.  
- Clarity: well-structured exposition, intuitive figures, and precise pseudocode.  
- Significance: practical, theoretically grounded solution with potential extensions to other bound-based methods and real-world tasks (e.g., hyperparameter tuning).  
- Weakness: scalability with dimensionality is limited—regret degrades as D increases (λ → 1) without extra assumptions.  
- Weakness: algorithm requires tuning of parameter Ξ_max; sensitivity not fully analyzed across functions.  
- Overall verdict: strong contribution that solves a key open problem but needs further work on high-dimensional scaling and parameter sensitivity.
