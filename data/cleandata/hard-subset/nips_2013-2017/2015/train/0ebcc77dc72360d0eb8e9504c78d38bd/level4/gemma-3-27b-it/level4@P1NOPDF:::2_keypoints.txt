- Proposes a novel Bayesian Global Optimization variant using space/interval partitioning and UCB to avoid internal global optimization for next-sample selection.  
- Provides theoretical analysis claiming improved performance with exponential regret, outperforming existing techniques.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and well-illustrated with an example.  
- Major concern: experimental evaluation appears limited to one-dimensional test functions, which is not explicitly stated.  
- Scalability worry: interval/hyperrectangle partitioning likely suffers exponential resolution growth in higher dimensions (curse of dimensionality), similar to DIRECT.  
- Recommendation: discuss scalability limitations and possible mitigation strategies, or include higher-dimensional experiments.  
- Minor: clarify whether acquisition functions other than UCB (e.g., Expected Improvement) can be used within the framework.  
- Minor: clarify the unclear sentence on p.3 about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor: explain why no function evaluation occurs at n=16 in the p.4 example.  
- Overall: a sound, novel theoretical contribution, but practical applicability is limited by unaddressed higher-dimensional scalability concerns.
