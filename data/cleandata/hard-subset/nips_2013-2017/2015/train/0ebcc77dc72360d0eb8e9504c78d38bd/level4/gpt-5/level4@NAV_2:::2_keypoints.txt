- Uses UCB to avoid an internal nonconvex optimization when choosing the next query.
- Theoretical claim: exponential simple-regret, presented as an improvement over prior methods.
- Algorithm is thoroughly described and illustrated with a clear example.
- Principal concern: experiments appear to be only 1D (not explicitly stated).
- Scalability concern: hyperrectangle-based partitioning likely suffers exponential blow-up with dimension; needs discussion and relation to DIRECT’s high-dimensional degradation.
- Minor: Clarify whether UCB is essential or the approach extends to other acquisition functions (e.g., expected improvement).
- Minor: Clarify the statement about simultaneously conducting global and local searches based on candidate bounds.
- Minor: Explain why at n=16 the far-right candidate receives no function evaluation.
- Overall: Method is novel, sound, and clearly presented, but empirical evaluation seems limited to 1D, affecting practical applicability.
