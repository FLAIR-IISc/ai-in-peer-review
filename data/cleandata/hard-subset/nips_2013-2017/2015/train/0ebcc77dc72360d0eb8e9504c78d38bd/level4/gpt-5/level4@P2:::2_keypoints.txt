- Proposes a GP-based BO algorithm that replaces auxiliary acquisition optimization with hierarchical space-partitioning using UCB and an “infinite-metric” perspective.  
- Proves exponential simple-regret rate O(λN+Ngp) under an unknown semi-metric capturing local smoothness near the optimum.  
- Connects closely to DIRECT, SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-UCB and claims improvement over BaMSOO’s polynomial regret via reduced exploration loss.  

- Conceptual novelty: blends GP posterior information with an infinite family of smoothness bounds in a partitioning framework.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides exponential convergence guarantees without δ-cover sampling or inner acquisition maximization.  
- Practical angle: avoids difficult inner optimization; algorithm well described with 1D illustration and pseudocode.  
- Clarity: motivation and relations to prior work are well framed and easy to follow.  

- Empirical scope/scalability concern: experiments appear low-dimensional (likely 1D); partitioning methods scale poorly with dimension.  
- Computational cost unclear: per-iteration costs (UCB evaluations, Ξ/Ξmax effect, GP hyperparameter updates) not fully quantified.  
- Assumption interpretability: the unknown semi-metric is abstract—needs guidance on when it holds and relation to common kernels.  

- Quality: theoretical analysis seems careful and improved, but empirical validation doesn’t convincingly address higher-dimensional performance.  
- Clarity issues: some statements require rephrasing or clarification for readers (specific lines noted).  
- Originality: novel synthesis of GP information with infinite-metric optimistic partitioning compared to prior methods.  
- Significance: potentially important if scalable or used with dimensionality-reduction, but current practical impact may be limited by dimensionality.  

- Pros for acceptance: strong theoretical advance, clear algorithm/analysis, and a useful pattern for combining GP bounds with partition-based search.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical evidence and insufficient discussion of computational cost and parameter sensitivity in higher dimensions.  

- Suggestion: clarify test problem dimensionality and add higher-dimensional benchmarks or discuss pairing with dimensionality reduction (e.g., random embeddings).  
- Suggestion: provide per-iteration complexity analysis (UCB call count, Ξ/Ξmax role, GP hyperparameter update costs).  
- Suggestion: elaborate when the semi-metric assumption holds and its relation to common kernel smoothness classes.  
- Suggestion: clarify whether the method can use acquisition functions other than UCB (EI/PI) or why UCB is required.  

- Minor: clarify “UCB is considered ‘for brevity’” and whether framework extends to EI/PI.  
- Minor: rephrase the sentence about conducting global and local searches using all candidate bounds for clarity.  
- Minor: explain the n=16 placeholder UCB comment—UCB used as temporary placeholder to defer expensive evaluations.  

- Overall: novel and well-analyzed contribution removing two practical barriers to GP-based BO with exponential regret, but empirical scope and scalability need expansion or deeper discussion.
