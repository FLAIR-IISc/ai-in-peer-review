- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Key insight: consider "infinitely many possible bounds" rather than a single GP prior bound to retain exponential convergence.
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous proof of exponential convergence (Theorem 1) with regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)), λ < 1.
- Advances over prior work (de Freitas et al., Wang et al.) by avoiding impractical δ-cover sampling and improving from polynomial to exponential regret.
- Shows IMGPO can reduce the "infinite-metric exploration loss" using the GP prior, outperforming BaMSOO in analysis.
- Provides insightful remarks (Remarks 1–3) explaining why multiple bounds are advantageous.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and uses figures effectively to illustrate the algorithm.
- Pseudocode (Algorithm 1) is detailed and accessible, with clear explanation of algorithmic intuition.
- Comprehensive experiments comparing IMGPO to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI across eight benchmarks (1–1000 dims).
- Empirical results show IMGPO consistently outperforms competitors in most tested cases.
- Investigates parameter effects (e.g., Ξmax), highlighting trade-offs between performance and computation.
- Technically sound: theoretical claims are well-supported and results appear reproducible.
- Acknowledges limitation that exponential convergence degrades in high dimensions (λ approaches 1).
- Weakness: connection to other bound-based methods could be strengthened with more explicit contrasts.
- Weakness: practical implications of the semi-metric assumption (Assumption 1) need deeper discussion.
- Weakness: lacks concrete strategies for improving high-dimensional scalability beyond suggesting "additional assumptions."
- Originality: novel application of an "infinite-metric" approach to GP-based optimization, while citing related methods like DIRECT and SOO.
- Significance: solves a long-standing open problem in Bayesian optimization with practical and theoretical impact.
- Recommendation: strong accept for NIPS; valuable to both researchers and practitioners.
