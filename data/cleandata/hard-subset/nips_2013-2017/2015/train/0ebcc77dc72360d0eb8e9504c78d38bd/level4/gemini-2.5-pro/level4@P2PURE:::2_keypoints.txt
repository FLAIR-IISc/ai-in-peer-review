- Proposes a novel space-partitioning variant of Bayesian Global Optimization that uses interval partitioning to generate subsequent samples.
- Uses the Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) acquisition to avoid an internal optimization step for selecting the next evaluation point.
- Presents strong theoretical analysis proving an exponential regret bound, improving on prior results.
- Algorithm is clearly described and effectively illustrated with an example; overall presentation is clear.
- Major concern: scalability — experiments appear to be only on one-dimensional functions and the interval-partitioning strategy likely does not scale to higher dimensions.
- Dimensionality issue: number of hyperrectangles required grows exponentially, invoking the curse of dimensionality and similar limitations as the DIRECT algorithm.
- Recommendation: authors should at minimum discuss and address the practical scalability limits of the approach.
- Minor comment: clarify whether UCB is chosen merely for brevity or is fundamental to the algorithm and its theoretical guarantees, and whether other acquisition functions (e.g., Expected Improvement) could be used.
- Minor comment: clarify the sentence on page 3 about "simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds."
- Minor comment: on page 4, explain why the interval on the far right at n=16 does not receive a function evaluation.
