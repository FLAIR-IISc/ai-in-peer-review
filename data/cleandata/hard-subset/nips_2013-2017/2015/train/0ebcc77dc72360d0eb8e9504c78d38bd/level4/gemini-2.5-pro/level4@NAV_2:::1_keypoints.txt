- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary non-convex acquisition optimization.  
- Uses a hierarchical partitioning scheme guided by a Gaussian Process prior, blending optimistic optimization and GP-based methods.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: removes the impractical δ-cover requirement and attains exponential regret bounds.  
- Original and elegant solution to a known open problem in GP-based optimization.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates the work within bound-based search literature.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and offers a novel framework considering an infinite set of semi-metrics.  
- Experimental validation lacks statistical rigor: mostly single deterministic runs without reporting variance or multiple initializations.  
- Incomplete empirical baselines: missing comparisons to state-of-the-art information-theoretic BO methods (e.g., Entropy Search, Predictive Entropy Search).  
- Limited evaluation scope: only synthetic, mostly low-dimensional benchmarks; no real-world applications (e.g., hyperparameter tuning).  
- Overall: strong and novel theoretical results but weakened by insufficient and non-comprehensive experiments, reducing practical credibility for a top-tier venue.
