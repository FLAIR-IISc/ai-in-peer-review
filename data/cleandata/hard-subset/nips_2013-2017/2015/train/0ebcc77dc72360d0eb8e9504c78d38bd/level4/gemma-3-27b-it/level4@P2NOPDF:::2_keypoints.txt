- Introduces a novel Bayesian Global Optimization method using space/interval partitioning to pick sampling locations, avoiding the internal global optimization in UCB-based approaches.
- Demonstrates theoretical improvement with exponential regret bounds over existing methods.
- Algorithm is clearly presented and well-illustrated with an example; represents a genuine and novel contribution.
- Primary concern: experimental evaluation appears limited to one-dimensional test functions (not explicitly stated).
- Scalability concern: interval partitioning likely does not scale to higher dimensions due to exponential growth in partition complexity.
- Method resembles the DIRECT algorithm, which is known to perform poorly as dimensionality increases.
- While theoretical guarantees remain valid, practical applicability in higher dimensions needs discussion and evaluation.
- Minor clarification needed: why UCB was chosen over other acquisition functions (e.g., Expected Improvement) and whether alternatives were considered.
- Clarify the statement about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”
- Explain the behavior noted at n=16 (the far right point where no function evaluation occurs) and why no evaluation happens.
- Overall judgment: sound, innovative work with solid theory; addressing scalability and the minor clarifications would strengthen the paper.
