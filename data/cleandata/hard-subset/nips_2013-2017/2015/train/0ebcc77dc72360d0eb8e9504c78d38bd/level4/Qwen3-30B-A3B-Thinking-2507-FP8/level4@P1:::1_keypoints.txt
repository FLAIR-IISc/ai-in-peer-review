- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without delta-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. (2012) while avoiding the computationally prohibitive delta-cover sampling criticized by Wang et al. (2013).  
- Uses multiple potential bounds instead of a single GP-derived bound to achieve practical exponential convergence.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and situates the method effectively within bound-based search literature.  
- Distinguishes IMGPO from SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-UCB, highlighting greater flexibility in handling unknown bounds.  
- Technical exposition is thorough with careful definitions and rigorous supplementary proofs of exponential convergence.  
- Remarks 1–3 provide valuable theoretical insights into the interaction between GP-derived bounds and unknown semi-metrics.  
- Experimental section is weak: lacks multiple initial evaluations and statistical validation standard in Bayesian optimization.  
- Empirical comparisons omit key state-of-the-art, information-theoretic methods (Entropy Search, Predictive Entropy Search).  
- Experiments rely on synthetic benchmarks of at most 6 dimensions and provide insufficient real-world or high-dimensional evaluation (single REMBO 1000-D test is inadequate).  
- Computational overhead and the trade-off between convergence rate and runtime—particularly in higher dimensions—are not sufficiently clarified.  
- Overall assessment: strong and significant theoretical contribution but experimental validation is insufficient; recommend borderline acceptance pending more comprehensive experiments (statistical comparisons, SOTA baselines, real-world tasks).
