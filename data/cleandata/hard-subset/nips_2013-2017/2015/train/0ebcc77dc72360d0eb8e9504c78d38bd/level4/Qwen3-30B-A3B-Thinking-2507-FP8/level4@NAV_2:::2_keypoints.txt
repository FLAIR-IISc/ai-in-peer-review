- Introduces a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Extends and builds on prior theoretical work (de Freitas et al., SOO) and bridges methods using single vs. multiple upper bounds.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: rigorous proof of exponential regret bounds O(λ^N + N g_p) with λ < 1, improving on BaMSOO’s polynomial bounds.  
- Algorithmic design is clearly explained and supported by an illustrative example (Figure 1).  
- Theoretical analysis yields useful insights on the advantages of considering multiple bounds (Remarks 1–3).  
- Implementation details are thorough and code is publicly available, supporting reproducibility.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to 1D test functions and does not demonstrate practical utility in higher dimensions.  
- Scalability of the interval partitioning approach is not addressed, raising concerns given known high‑dimensional limitations (e.g., DIRECT).  
- The claim in Section 3.1 about “simultaneously conducting global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds” is unclear and needs elaboration.  
- The paper gives an insufficient explanation for skipped function evaluations at certain points (e.g., n=16 example).  
- Pro-acceptance: strong theoretical advance, principled multi‑bound approach, and sound technical proofs.  
- Con-rejection: insufficient experiments and unaddressed scalability concerns limit practical relevance for a conference paper.  
- Suggested revisions: add higher‑dimensional experiments (≥2D, preferably 5D+), discuss scalability and compare to methods like REMBO, clarify Section 3.1, and explain skipped evaluations.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but lack of higher‑dimensional empirical validation weakens practical impact; addressing concerns would make the paper a strong candidate for acceptance.
