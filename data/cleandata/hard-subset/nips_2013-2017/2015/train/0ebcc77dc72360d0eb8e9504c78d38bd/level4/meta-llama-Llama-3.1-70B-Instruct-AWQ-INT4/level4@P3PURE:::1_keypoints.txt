- Proposes a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, generalizing Freitas et al. (2012).  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and effectively places the algorithm within bound-based search literature.  
- Theoretical contribution is noteworthy and deserves community attention.  
- Reviewer assigns a score of 5 (not higher) due to weaknesses in the experimental section.  
- Experiments omit multiple initial evaluations (no repeated runs), preventing meaningful statistical comparison.  
- No comparison to state-of-the-art Bayesian Optimization methods, notably information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search.  
- All experiments are on synthetic functions up to 6 dimensions; no real-world tasks (e.g., wet lab or parameter tuning) are evaluated.  
- Overall: strong theoretical results but experimental evaluation is insufficient for NIPS standards.
