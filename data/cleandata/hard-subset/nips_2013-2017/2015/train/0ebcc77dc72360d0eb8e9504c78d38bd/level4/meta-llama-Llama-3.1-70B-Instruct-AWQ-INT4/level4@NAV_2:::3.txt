This paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence, eliminating the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The authors propose Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), which leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer. The paper is well-organized, and the main contributions are clearly described.

The strengths of the paper include its ability to provide a strong theoretical foundation for the proposed algorithm. The authors provide a good illustrative example of the algorithm in section 3.2, giving the reader a nice high-level understanding. Section 3.3 clearly communicates the workings of the algorithm programmatically for future implementation/replication. The experimental results are also exciting, demonstrating the effectiveness of IMGPO in comparison to other state-of-the-art algorithms.

The paper brings together ideas from the Bayesian global optimization literature that do and do not use continuity estimation, providing a unique perspective on the field. The authors note that their approach illustrates the benefits of combining these two paths, which is a significant contribution to the NIPS community.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the related work and provide a clear comparison with existing methods. The writing is clear, and the paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the algorithm may not be scalable to higher dimensions, as noted by the authors. However, they also provide a possible strategy for addressing this issue by leveraging additional assumptions.

Overall, the paper provides a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization, and the results are well-presented. The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the topic, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence, eliminating the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling.
* The authors provide a strong theoretical foundation for the proposed algorithm.
* The experimental results are exciting, demonstrating the effectiveness of IMGPO in comparison to other state-of-the-art algorithms.
* The paper brings together ideas from the Bayesian global optimization literature that do and do not use continuity estimation, providing a unique perspective on the field.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The algorithm may not be scalable to higher dimensions, which could limit its applicability.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the potential applications and limitations of the proposed method.
