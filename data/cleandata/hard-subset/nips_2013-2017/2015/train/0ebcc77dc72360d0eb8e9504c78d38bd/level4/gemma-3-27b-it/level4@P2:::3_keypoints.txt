- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method that avoids auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling while achieving exponential convergence.
- Strong motivation and contextualization, effectively relating to prior work (de Freitas et al., Wang et al.) and the impracticality of δ-cover sampling.
- Section 3.2’s illustrative example clarifies algorithm mechanics and aids reader intuition.
- Algorithm description and pseudocode (Algorithm 1) are detailed and sufficient for implementation and replication.
- Thorough discussion of relationships to existing methods (BaMSOO, DIRECT), situating IMGPO within bound-based search approaches.
- Experimental results show promising performance on a range of benchmark functions.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and suggest improvements, such as studying the Ξmax parameter and scaling to higher dimensions.
- Practical hyperparameter tuning considerations are discussed, with standard approaches used when ideal kernel/hyperparameters are unknown.
- Technical strength: provides theoretical analysis (Theorem 1) supporting exponential convergence.
- Clarity strength: paper is well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.
- Originality strength: the “infinitely many bounds” concept is creative and addresses limitations of existing BO methods.
- Significance strength: removing auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling enhances practical applicability of Bayesian optimization.
- Weakness: scalability to very high-dimensional problems is limited and requires future work.
- Weakness: potential sensitivity to hyperparameter settings; more extensive tuning strategies would be beneficial.
- Acceptance arguments: addresses a significant practical problem, strong theory, good empirical results, clear presentation, and novel contribution.
- Rejection arguments: scalability and hyperparameter sensitivity could limit practical applicability.
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution deserving enthusiastic acceptance.
