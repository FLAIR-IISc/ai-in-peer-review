This paper introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that eschews delta-cover sampling, thereby achieving exponential regret and generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012) by circumventing an impractical sampling procedure.

The manuscript is well-crafted, with a clear and logical structure, making it easy to follow. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the new algorithm's position within the existing literature on bounded-based search methods, rendering this work deserving of attention from the community.

I assigned a score of 5, but not higher, due to my reservations regarding the experimental section, which falls short of the expected standard for a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper. My primary concerns with this section are:

- The authors do not evaluate the methods with multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, a common practice in BO. Consequently, no statistically meaningful comparison of the methods can be drawn.
- Despite proposing a bounded-based search method, the authors should have compared their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches, including information-theoretic methods like Entropy Search, which are absent from the experiments.
- All experiments were conducted on synthetic functions with a maximum dimension of 6. The authors did not include any real-world wet lab or parameter tuning experiments to demonstrate the method's performance in practical scenarios.

In summary, the paper presents an innovative global optimization algorithm with some intriguing theoretical results. However, in my opinion, the experimental section does not meet the standards expected of a NIPS paper.
