- Proposes a novel Bayesian Global Optimization method using space-partitioning and UCB to avoid solving an internal global optimization subproblem.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong, proving the algorithm attains exponential regret and improves on prior work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with a helpful example.  
- Major reservation: experimental evaluation appears limited to one-dimensional functions (not stated), raising scalability concerns.  
- Concern that interval/hyperrectangle partitioning will scale poorly in higher dimensions (exponential growth), similar to DIRECT's limitations.  
- Authors should explicitly address this practical limitation even if theory remains valid.  
- Minor: Clarify whether other acquisition functions (e.g., Expected Improvement) can be used or if the design fundamentally requires UCB.  
- Minor: Rephrase the unclear sentence about simultaneous global and local searches for clarity.  
- Minor: Explain why no function evaluation occurs at n=16 in the example to improve clarity.
