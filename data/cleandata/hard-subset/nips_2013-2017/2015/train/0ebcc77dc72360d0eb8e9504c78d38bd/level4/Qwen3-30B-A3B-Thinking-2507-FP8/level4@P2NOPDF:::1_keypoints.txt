- Introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling while achieving exponential regret bounds.  
- Extends Freitas et al. (2012) by removing their computationally intractable sampling procedure; theoretical contributions are sound.  
- Clear exposition of the algorithm’s novelty within bounded-search methods; writing is polished and logically structured.  
- Effectively situates the work relative to existing literature, making it accessible and valuable to the optimization community.  
- Experimental evaluation significantly undermines the paper’s impact and does not meet BO standards for NIPS.  
- Only a single initial set of function evaluations is used, preventing robustness assessment and statistical validation.  
- Omits comparisons to key state-of-the-art BO baselines, notably information-theoretic methods like Entropy Search.  
- Relies entirely on synthetic test functions (max dimension 6) with no real-world case studies (e.g., hyperparameter tuning).  
- Recommends adding multi-initial evaluations, comparisons to established BO baselines (Entropy Search, Expected Improvement), and at least one real-world experiment.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution but weak empirical validation; score 5 (weak accept).
