- Introduces a novel Bayesian Global Optimization variant that generates samples via space/interval partitioning.  
- Uses an Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) acquisition to avoid internal global optimization for next-sample selection.  
- Provides theoretical analysis proving exponential regret and improvements over existing methods.  
- Algorithm is well-described and effectively illustrated with an example.  
- Paper is a novel contribution and is presented with commendable clarity.  
- Primary concern: practical scalability—interval partitioning likely does not scale to higher dimensions and may require exponentially increasing resolution.  
- Experimental evaluation appears limited to one-dimensional problems (not explicitly stated), reducing evidence of practical utility in higher dimensions.  
- Scalability limitation is reminiscent of DIRECT, which performs poorly as dimensionality increases and should be addressed.  
- Minor: p.2 — ambiguity whether UCB is used for brevity only or if other acquisitions (e.g., EI) are compatible.  
- Minor: p.3 — unclear phrasing about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor: p.4 — insufficient explanation for why no function evaluation occurs at n=16; rationale should be provided.
