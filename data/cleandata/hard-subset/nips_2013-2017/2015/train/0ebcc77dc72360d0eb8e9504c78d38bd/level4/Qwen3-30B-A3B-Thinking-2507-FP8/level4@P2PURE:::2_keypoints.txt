- Introduces a Bayesian Global Optimization variant that generates future samples by interval subdivision (space partitioning).  
- Uses UCB to avoid an internal global optimization step when selecting the next sample.  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing exponential regret bounds, a significant improvement over prior work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described with a well-explained, illustrated example.  
- Contribution is novel and the paper is generally well-presented.  
- Primary concern: experimental evaluation appears limited to 1D problems (not explicitly stated).  
- Scalability concern: interval partitioning likely does not scale to higher dimensions due to exponential growth in required resolution.  
- Practical limitation amplified by similarity to the DIRECT algorithm, which is known to perform poorly in high dimensions.  
- Recommendation: paper should address this practical high-dimensional limitation despite the strong theoretical results.  
- Minor: p.2 — UCB is introduced without justification; clarification on whether other acquisitions (e.g., EI) are possible is needed.  
- Minor: p.3 — the phrase about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds” is ambiguous and needs clarification.  
- Minor: p.4 — explain why at n=16 the far right point has no function evaluation and clarify that behavior.
