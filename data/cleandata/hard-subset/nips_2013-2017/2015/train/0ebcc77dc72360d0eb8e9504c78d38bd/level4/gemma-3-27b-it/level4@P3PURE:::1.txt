The paper presents a novel algorithm for global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012) which relies on an impractical sampling procedure. 

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the role of the new algorithm within the current literature of bounded-based search methods is clearly explained. In this regard, I believe this work deserves attention from the community.

I give this paper a score of 5, but not a higher score, because the experimental section falls below the standard expected of a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper. My primary criticisms of this section are:

*   The authors do not compare the methods across multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, which is standard practice in BO research. Consequently, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be drawn.
*   Despite proposing a bounded-based search method, the authors do not compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches. Information-theoretic approaches, such as Entropy Search, are not included in the experiments.
*   All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions (with a maximum dimension of 6). No real-world parameter tuning experiments are used to demonstrate the method’s performance in practical scenarios.

The paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. The paper is solid, with some interesting theoretical results, but in my opinion, the experimental section is below the standards typically expected for a NIPS publication.
