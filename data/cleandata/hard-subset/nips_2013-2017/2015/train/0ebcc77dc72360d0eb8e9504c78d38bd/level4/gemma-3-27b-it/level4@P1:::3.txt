The paper describes a Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence that avoids the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling, both of which pose practical challenges. The central idea is to leverage an unknown semi-metric encoding the continuity at a global optimizer, and to simultaneously consider multiple candidate bounds, refining the search based on the information provided by a Gaussian process.

The paper is well-organized and clearly presents its contributions. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and situates the work within the existing literature on Bayesian optimization, particularly highlighting the limitations of previous approaches that rely on δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization. Section 3.2 provides a helpful illustrative example that clarifies the algorithm’s mechanics, and Section 3.3 offers a detailed programmatic description, facilitating potential implementation and replication. The experimental results presented are promising. Importantly, the authors acknowledge the connection between their work and previous approaches, effectively synthesizing ideas from both continuity-estimation-based and non-continuity-estimation-based methods in Bayesian global optimization.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses a Significant Practical Limitation:** The elimination of δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization addresses a key bottleneck in existing Bayesian optimization methods, making the approach more widely applicable.
*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a theoretical analysis demonstrating an exponential convergence rate, establishing a solid foundation for the proposed method.
*   **Clear and Thorough Explanation:** The algorithm is described with commendable clarity, enhanced by the illustrative example and programmatic details.
*   **Empirically Validated:** The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to existing approaches.
*   **Well-Positioned within the Literature:** The paper effectively relates the work to prior research, clearly articulating its novelty and contributions.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Scalability Concerns:** While the theoretical analysis is strong, the discussion acknowledges potential scalability challenges in higher dimensions, suggesting a need for further research to address this limitation.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The reliance on parameters like Ξmax introduces a potential for sensitivity, requiring careful tuning. While the paper discusses the impact of Ξmax, a more in-depth analysis of its influence would be beneficial.
*   **Assumptions:** The method relies on the assumption of an unknown semi-metric. While reasonable, the impact of violations of this assumption on performance is not fully explored. 



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant practical problem in Bayesian optimization.
*   The theoretical results are strong and well-supported.
*   The algorithm is clearly explained and well-motivated.
*   The experimental results are promising and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
*   The work is a significant contribution to the NIPS community.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The scalability to very high dimensions remains a concern.
*   The parameter sensitivity requires careful consideration.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The authors have successfully addressed a significant practical limitation and provided a theoretically sound and empirically validated solution. While scalability remains a concern, the strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses. I recommend acceptance.
