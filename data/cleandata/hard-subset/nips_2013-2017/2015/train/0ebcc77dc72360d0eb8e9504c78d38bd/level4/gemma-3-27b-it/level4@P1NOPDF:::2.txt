The paper presents a novel variant of Bayesian Global Optimization that generates future samples using a space/interval partitioning algorithm. By leveraging Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) acquisition, this approach avoids the internal global optimization problem inherent in selecting the next sample point. The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating that their method achieves improved performance, specifically exhibiting exponential regret, and thus surpasses existing techniques. The algorithm is clearly described and well-illustrated with an example. Overall, the paper makes a valuable and novel contribution, and is presented in a clear and accessible manner. 

I do have one significant reservation regarding the scope of the experimental evaluation. The results appear to be limited to one-dimensional test functions – this is not explicitly stated, but is implied by the presented figures. I suspect that the interval partitioning approach may not scale effectively to higher dimensions (hyperrectangles), as the required resolution would likely grow exponentially with dimensionality. This is a concern because the approach shares similarities with the well-known DIRECT algorithm, which is known to struggle with the curse of dimensionality. While this limitation does not diminish the theoretical contribution, it is a critical consideration for practical applications and should be addressed in the paper. At a minimum, a discussion of the potential scalability issues and possible mitigation strategies would be beneficial.

Minor comments:

*   p.2: The authors state that UCB is considered “for brevity.” This raises the question of whether other acquisition functions, such as Expected Improvement, could also be employed within this framework. While the paper focuses on UCB, clarifying this point would be helpful.
*   p.3: The sentence “…we simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds” is unclear. I found it difficult to understand the meaning of this statement.
*   p.4: “At n=16, the far right…but no function evaluation occurs.” Please provide a brief explanation for *why* no function evaluation occurs at this point, to improve clarity.

In summary, the paper presents a sound and novel approach to Bayesian Global Optimization with promising theoretical properties. However, the lack of evaluation in higher dimensions and the potential scalability issues warrant further discussion.
