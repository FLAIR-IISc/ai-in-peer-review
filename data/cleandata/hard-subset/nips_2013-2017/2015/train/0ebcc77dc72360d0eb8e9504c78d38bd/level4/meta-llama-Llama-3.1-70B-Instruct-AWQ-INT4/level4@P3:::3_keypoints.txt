- Presents IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Formalizes assumption of an unknown semi-metric bound at the global optimizer (Assumption 1).  
- Algorithm conducts simultaneous global and local searches over all candidate bounds, using GP-estimated bounds to prune candidates.  
- Clear technical presentation and concise pseudocode (Algorithm 1) make the method easy to follow.  
- Theoretical regret bound shown to be O(λN + Ngp) with λ < 1, supporting exponential convergence.  
- Empirical results show IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI in most tested cases.  
- Strength: significant and novel contribution to Bayesian optimization theory and practice.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and clear exposition.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with Bayesian optimization and Gaussian processes, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability to high-dimensional problems are not thoroughly addressed.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of hyperparameter choices and their impact on performance.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested revisions to discuss hyperparameter selection and scalability considerations.
