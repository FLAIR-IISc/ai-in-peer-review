The paper presents a novel method for Bayesian global optimization that eliminates the need for d-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization. The authors have organized the paper effectively, clearly outlining the main contributions in the primary section.

A notable strength of the paper is its ability to balance theoretical depth with intuitive explanations. For instance, the illustrative example in section 3.2 provides a high-level understanding of the algorithm, making it accessible to a broad audience. Furthermore, section 3.3 offers a clear and programmatically-oriented explanation of the algorithm, facilitating future implementation and replication.

The experimental results are also noteworthy, demonstrating the potential of the proposed approach. The authors have successfully integrated ideas from various strands of the Bayesian global optimization literature, including those that utilize continuity estimation and those that do not.

The paper introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization algorithm that operates without auxiliary optimization or d-cover sampling. The writing is clear, and the contribution to the NIPS community is substantial, making this a valuable addition to the field.
