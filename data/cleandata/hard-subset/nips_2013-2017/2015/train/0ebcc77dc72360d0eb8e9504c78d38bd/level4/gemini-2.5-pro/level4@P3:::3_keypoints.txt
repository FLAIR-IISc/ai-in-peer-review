- Introduces IMGPO, a practical Bayesian optimization algorithm that attains provable exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary non-convex acquisition optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Core idea: combine hierarchical partitioning (SOO-style unknown semi-metric exploration) with GP-UCB pruning to explore a family of bounds while efficiently discarding regions.
- Major contribution: first practical GP-based method with a theoretical exponential regret bound (Theorem 1).
- Theoretical analysis (Remarks 1–3) clarifies why combining GP-based bounds and infinite-metric exploration yields strong performance.
- Paper is very well written, with clear motivation, intuitive 1D example, and complete pseudocode enabling reimplementation.
- Empirical results across benchmarks and baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) show consistent superiority in simple regret.
- Runtime comparisons and a 1000-D REMBO experiment demonstrate practical efficiency and potential for integration into high-dimensional pipelines.
- Limitation: exponential-rate constant λ approaches 1 with increasing input dimension D, so the core algorithm suffers from the curse of dimensionality.
- Sensitivity concern: introduces parameter Ξmax and a heuristic update for Ξ without thorough sensitivity or ablation analysis.
- Practical cost concern: GP hyperparameter updates every iteration may add non-trivial wall-clock time not reflected in function-evaluation-centric runtime tables.
- Strong case for acceptance: solves a stated open problem, provides novel unification of paradigms, and is backed by rigorous theory and comprehensive experiments.
- Counterpoints against acceptance: scalability to high dimensions and limited analysis of new algorithm parameters warrant further attention.
