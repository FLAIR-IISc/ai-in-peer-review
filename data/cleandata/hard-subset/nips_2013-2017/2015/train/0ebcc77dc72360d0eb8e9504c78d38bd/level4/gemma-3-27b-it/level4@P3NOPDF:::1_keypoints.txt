- Proposes a novel global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret.  
- Generalizes Freitas et al. (2012) while removing their computationally impractical sampling requirement.  
- Paper is clearly written and well situated within the literature on bound-based search methods.  
- Reviewer rates the paper a 5 but lowers enthusiasm due to weak experimental validation.  
- Experiments fail to analyze sensitivity to the number of initial function evaluations, a standard BO diagnostic.  
- No comparisons to state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization baselines (e.g., Entropy Search) are provided.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to synthetic functions only, with dimensionality capped at 6.  
- No real-world or “wetlab” experiments are included, undermining claims of practical utility and generalizability.  
- Conclusion: theoretically solid but requires stronger empirical comparisons and realistic experiments to be convincing for NIPS.
