This paper introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel algorithm for Bayesian global optimization. The primary contribution is a method that elegantly sidesteps two common and often problematic requirements in this domain: the need for an auxiliary optimization subroutine to maximize the acquisition function, and the reliance on d-cover sampling. The work is distinguished by its synthesis of ideas from distinct lines of research within Bayesian optimization, particularly those that do and do not leverage continuity estimation of the objective function.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, making the core concepts accessible. The main contributions are clearly articulated, and the structure guides the reader logically through the development of the algorithm. Several aspects of the presentation are particularly commendable:

*   **Clarity and Intuition:** The illustrative example in Section 3.2 is highly effective. It provides readers with a strong, high-level, and intuitive grasp of the algorithm's mechanics, which is invaluable for understanding the subsequent formalisms.

*   **Reproducibility:** The authors provide clear pseudocode in Section 3.3, which details the algorithm's workings. This significantly enhances the paper's practical value, facilitating reproducibility and future implementation by other researchers in the field.

*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental evaluation provides compelling evidence for the efficacy of the proposed method. The results are strong, demonstrating IMGPO's competitive performance against established baseline methods. This empirical validation lends significant weight to the paper's theoretical contributions.

*   **Novelty and Significance:** The technical contribution is significant. By avoiding the standard practice of auxiliary optimization, IMGPO circumvents a potential source of considerable computational overhead and complexity, representing a notable advancement. Furthermore, by bridging the gap between methods that rely on continuity estimation and those that do not, the authors offer a more unified perspective and a potentially more robust algorithm.

In summary, this paper presents a well-executed and important contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The proposed IMGPO algorithm is novel, addresses a key limitation of existing methods, and is supported by both clear exposition and strong empirical results. The work is of high quality and will undoubtedly be of great interest to the NIPS community. I strongly recommend its acceptance.
