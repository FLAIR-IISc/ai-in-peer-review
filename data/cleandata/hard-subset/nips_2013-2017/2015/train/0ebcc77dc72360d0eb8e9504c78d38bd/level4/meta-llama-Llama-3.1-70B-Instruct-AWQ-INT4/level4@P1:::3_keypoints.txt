- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Presentation is clear and well-organized.
- Provides a detailed algorithm description and useful pseudocode for implementation.
- Uses illustrative examples (Sections 3.2 and 3.3) to clarify algorithm behavior.
- Experimental results show IMGPO often outperforms GP-UCB, BaMSOO, and SOO.
- Theoretical contribution includes a proof of exponential convergence and detailed analysis.
- Paper is accessible to many readers thanks to examples and pseudocode.
- Assumes substantial background in Bayesian optimization and Gaussian processes.
- Some notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to non-experts.
- Lacks a detailed discussion of the method's limitations and directions for future work.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, empirical performance, theoretical significance, and clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: steep background requirements, notation issues, and limited coverage of limitations.
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication.
