This review evaluates the paper "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence."

**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), for global optimization of deterministic black-box functions. The primary contribution is an algorithm that achieves an exponential convergence rate for simple regret. Unlike previous work that demonstrated similar theoretical guarantees [1], IMGPO does not require an impractical δ-cover sampling procedure. Furthermore, it circumvents the need for an auxiliary non-convex optimization step to maximize an acquisition function, a common and often computationally intensive component of many standard Gaussian Process (GP) based methods like GP-UCB.

The core idea is to integrate a GP-UCB framework with a hierarchical partitioning search strategy, inspired by algorithms like SOO [18]. The algorithm maintains partitions of the search space and uses the GP's upper confidence bound (UCB) to prune branches that are unlikely to contain the global optimum. Crucially, it does not rely solely on the GP-derived bound. Instead, it operates under the assumption of an unknown, but well-behaved, semi-metric that bounds the function near its optimum (Assumption 1). By implicitly considering an infinite family of such bounds, the algorithm robustly balances exploration and exploitation. The authors provide a formal proof of the exponential regret bound and conduct experiments comparing IMGPO to several established methods (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) on a range of benchmark functions, demonstrating its superior performance.

**Strengths**

1.  **Significance and Originality:** The paper addresses a clearly stated and important open problem in the field: achieving an exponential convergence rate in GP-based optimization without impractical sampling schemes [2]. The proposed method, which synthesizes hierarchical partitioning with GP bounds in a novel way, is a significant step forward. The concept of leveraging the GP to prune options within an "infinite-metric" search framework is both clever and effective.

2.  **Technical Quality:** The theoretical analysis appears sound and provides a strong foundation for the algorithm. Theorem 1 formally establishes the exponential convergence rate, a marked improvement over the polynomial rates of related practical algorithms like BaMSOO. The analysis in Remarks 1-3 is particularly insightful, offering a clear discussion of how the GP-based bound and the infinite-metric search contribute to the final performance, and why their combination is advantageous.

3.  **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and clear. The authors have made a commendable effort to make complex ideas accessible. The introduction effectively situates the work within the existing literature. The step-by-step description of the algorithm in Section 3.2, complemented by the excellent visual aid in Figure 1, is didactic and greatly helps in understanding the mechanics of IMGPO.

4.  **Experimental Validation:** The empirical results provide compelling evidence for the algorithm's effectiveness. The comparison against strong and relevant baselines on a diverse set of up to 1000-dimensional benchmark functions is thorough. IMGPO consistently outperforms other methods, often by a significant margin. The choice to use a standard kernel and a blind initialization of hyperparameters reflects a realistic use-case and strengthens the claim of the method's practical utility.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

The paper is very strong, and the points below are minor suggestions for further polishing the manuscript.

1.  **Abstract Phrasing:** The abstract is clear but contains some minor redundancy. The first sentence introduces a method that avoids auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling. The second sentence then restates that most methods require auxiliary optimization and that existing methods with exponential convergence require δ-cover sampling. This could be rephrased to be more concise and impactful.

2.  **Presentation:** A thorough proofreading of the final manuscript would be beneficial to catch minor typos and grammatical points, ensuring the highest level of presentation quality.

**Overall Assessment**

This is an excellent paper presenting a significant contribution to the Bayesian optimization literature. It successfully bridges two lines of research in global optimization to solve a recognized open problem. The approach is novel, the theoretical guarantees are strong, and the empirical validation is convincing. The clarity of the presentation makes the work a pleasure to read. I strongly recommend acceptance.
