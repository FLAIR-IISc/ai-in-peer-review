

This paper introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization method that achieves exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling—addressing key limitations in prior work. The authors build upon the foundational GP-UCB framework [15] and the open problem identified by Wang et al. [2], who noted that existing exponential-convergence methods [1] rely on impractical δ-cover sampling. IMGPO resolves this by leveraging a semi-metric assumption (Assumption 1) to simultaneously explore infinitely many candidate bounds, eliminating the need for δ-cover sampling while maintaining exponential regret bounds. The approach integrates hierarchical partitioning (inspired by SOO [18]) with GP-based upper confidence bounds, as formalized in Algorithm 1 and Theorem 1, which establishes a regret bound of $O(\lambda^{N+N_{gp}})$ with $\lambda < 1$.  

**Strengths:**  
- **Originality:** First to achieve exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization, directly addressing Wang et al.’s [2] open problem. The infinite-metric exploration concept (Section 3.1) significantly advances the field beyond prior bound-based methods (e.g., BaMSOO [2] achieves polynomial regret).  
- **Quality:** Theoretical analysis (Section 4) rigorously proves the exponential bound and quantifies the role of GP tightness (Remarks 1–3), with clear connections to related work (e.g., DIRECT [19], SOO [18]). Experiments (Section 5) conclusively demonstrate superior performance over SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI across eight multimodal benchmarks, including high-dimensional extensions (e.g., Sin2 in 1000D).  
- **Clarity:** Well-structured exposition with intuitive figures (e.g., Figure 1 illustrating hierarchical partitioning) and precise pseudocode (Algorithm 1). The paper effectively explains the trade-offs between GP-based bounds and unknown semi-metrics.  
- **Significance:** Provides a practical, theoretically grounded solution to a long-standing limitation in GP optimization, with clear implications for real-world applications (e.g., hyperparameter tuning [3]). The framework’s generality (Remarks 2–3) suggests potential extensions to other bound-based methods (e.g., A* search, PAC-MDP).  

**Weaknesses:**  
- The dimensionality scalability challenge (Remark 1) is acknowledged but not resolved; the regret bound degrades as $D$ increases ($\lambda \to 1$), requiring additional assumptions (e.g., [14]) for higher dimensions.  
- The parameter $\Xi_{\text{max}}$ requires tuning (Section 5), and while Figure 2 shows minimal impact for some functions, its sensitivity for others is not thoroughly analyzed.  

**Pro/Con Acceptance Summary:**  
*Pro:* Solves a critical open problem (Wang et al. [2]), delivers exponential convergence without impractical assumptions, provides rigorous theory with clear experimental validation, and offers a framework for future work.  
*Con:* Dimensionality limitations and $\Xi_{\text{max}}$ dependency require further investigation for broader applicability.  

The paper meets all NIPS quality criteria: technically sound (Theorem 1, experiments), clearly written (Figures, pseudocode), highly original (first exponential-convergence method without δ-cover), and significant (resolves a key practical barrier in Bayesian optimization). It is well-positioned to influence both theoretical and applied work in the field.
