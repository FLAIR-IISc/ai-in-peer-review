This paper proposes a variant of Bayesian Global Optimization that leverages a space/interval partitioning algorithm to generate future samples, utilizing the Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) to circumvent the internal global optimization problem of selecting the next sample. 
Theoretical analysis demonstrates that this approach achieves exponential regret, thus enhancing previous work. The algorithm is well-described and accompanied by an illustrative example. 
The paper offers a novel contribution and is generally well-presented. 
However, I have one major concern regarding this paper. 
It appears that the experimental results are limited to 1D test functions, which is not explicitly stated. I suspect that the interval partitioning approach may not scale effectively to higher dimensions (hyperrectangles), as the required "resolution" would need to grow exponentially. 
This concern is echoed in the well-known DIRECT algorithm, which is known to suffer from poor performance as problem dimensionality increases. 
I believe it is essential to address this limitation in the paper, as it significantly impacts the practical applicability of the approach, even if it does not affect the theoretical contribution. 
Additionally, I have a few minor comments: 
On page 2, the authors mention that UCB is used "for brevity." I would like to know if alternative acquisition functions, such as expected improvement, could be employed. 
The statement on page 3, "...we simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds," is unclear. 
Lastly, on page 4, it would be beneficial to provide a clear explanation for why "At n=16, the far right...but no function evaluation occurs."
