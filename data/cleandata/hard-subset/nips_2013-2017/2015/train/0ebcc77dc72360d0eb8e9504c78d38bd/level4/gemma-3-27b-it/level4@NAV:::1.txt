This paper introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that circumvents the limitations of delta-cover sampling, achieving exponential regret and extending the results of Freitas et al. (2012) by removing the reliance on an impractical sampling procedure. 

The manuscript is clearly written and well-structured, effectively situating the algorithm within the existing landscape of bounded-based search methods. I believe this work has the potential to be of interest to the research community.

I assign a score of 5, though not higher, due to concerns regarding the experimental evaluation. Specifically, the experimental section falls short of the standards typically expected for publications in Bayesian Optimization. My key criticisms are:

*   The authors do not evaluate the methods across multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, a common practice in Bayesian Optimization, thus preventing a robust statistical comparison.
*   Despite proposing a bounded-based search method, the results are not benchmarked against current state-of-the-art Bayesian Optimization techniques, such as information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search.
*   The experiments are limited to synthetic functions with a maximum dimensionality of 6; no experiments on real-world parameter tuning problems are included to demonstrate performance in practical applications. 

In summary, the paper presents a promising new algorithm with interesting theoretical properties, but the experimental validation requires significant improvement.
