- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential simple regret without δ-cover sampling or inner-loop acquisition optimization.
- IMGPO explores an infinite family of unknown semi-metric bounds around the optimum and uses GP posterior UCBs to prune candidates.
- Uses hierarchical partitioning and a skip-evaluation mechanism to avoid auxiliary nonconvex optimization per iteration.
- Provides a regret bound of the form O(λN + Ngp) with λ < 1, explaining how GP-UCB reduces infinite-metric exploration loss.
- Generalizes de Freitas et al. (2012) by removing the impractical δ-cover assumption for deterministic GP bandits.
- Improves over BaMSOO by attaining exponential rather than polynomial rates via GP-informed reduction of uncertainty about the semi-metric.
- Connects conceptually to SOO, DIRECT, and BaMSOO while offering a principled way to use GP bounds without committing to a single bound.
- Theoretical framework is clear with Assumptions 1–2 and a high-probability exponential regret theorem; remarks aid intuition about D, α, p and constants.
- Main theoretical limitation: dependence on dimension D (λ → 1 as D increases), raising scalability concerns unless additional structure is exploited (e.g., REMBO).
- Algorithmic description (partitioning and skip-eval) is well presented; using GP-UCB to avoid evaluations is practically attractive.
- Limited empirical discussion of the trade-off between Ξmax, runtime, and regret; need more runtime vs. evaluation analysis.
- Unknown semi-metric assumption is reasonable but requires discussion of real-world scope and sensitivity to kernel misspecification.
- Paper is well written and clearly positions the bound-based perspective relative to prior algorithms.
- Clarity suggestions: fix terminology/notation inconsistencies, summarize constants near the main theorem, and clarify UCB recomputation frequency and cost.
- Originality: novel combination of infinite-metric exploration, GP-based pruning without auxiliary optimization, and exponential regret proof without δ-cover.
- Potential significance is high if empirical validation supports the theory, but current experiments are insufficient.
- Experimental weaknesses: no multiple random initializations or seeds, preventing statistical comparisons.
- Missing strong BO baselines such as ES, PES, and Thompson sampling, and unclear strength of auxiliary optimizers used for baselines.
- Experiments are mainly synthetic and low-dimensional (≤6D) with only one REMBO 1000D example; no real-world tuning/calibration tasks.
- Reporting gaps: lack of wall-clock time, number of GP hyperparameter updates, and ablations on η, Ξmax, kernel choice, and hyperparameter learning.
- Pros: strong theoretical contribution, elimination of acquisition optimization, insightful analysis, clear literature positioning, and open-source code.
- Cons: empirical evaluation below BO standards, unclear high-dimensional scalability, and limited analysis of kernel/hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Suggested improvements: add multi-seed experiments with confidence intervals and tests; include ES, PES, Thompson sampling, and strong GP-UCB baselines with wall-clock budgets.
- Also recommend real-world tasks, ablations on Ξmax and η, time breakdown for step (iii) and GP fitting, sensitivity analyses for kernels/hyperparameter learning, and exploration of higher-dimensional structure assumptions.
- Reviewer score: 5/10 (borderline) — strong theoretical and algorithmic contribution but empirical validation needs substantial strengthening.
