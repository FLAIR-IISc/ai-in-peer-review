- Proposes a modified Bayesian Global Optimization using space partitioning to generate samples and UCB to avoid internal global optimization.  
- Provides theoretical analysis claiming exponential regret, improving on prior work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example; offers novel insights.  
- Experiments are limited to one-dimensional test functions, raising scalability concerns.  
- Interval partitioning may require exponentially growing resolution in higher dimensions.  
- Similarity to the DIRECT algorithm, which performs poorly in high dimensions, exacerbates practical concerns.  
- Theoretical contributions remain valuable but practical viability is questioned.  
- Minor clarifications needed: why UCB was chosen over alternatives like expected improvement.  
- Additional explanations required for claims about simultaneous global/local search and omission of evaluation at a specific point.
