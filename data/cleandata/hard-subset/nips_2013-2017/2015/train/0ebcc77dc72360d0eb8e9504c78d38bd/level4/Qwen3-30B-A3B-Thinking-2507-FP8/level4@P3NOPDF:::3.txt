

This paper presents Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that eliminates the need for d-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization—a significant departure from standard approaches. The authors successfully synthesize key insights from both continuity-estimation-based and continuity-free Bayesian optimization literature, positioning IMGPO as a unified framework that bridges critical gaps in the field. The contribution is both theoretically sound and practically impactful, making it a strong candidate for NeurIPS.

The paper is exceptionally well-structured and clearly written. The main contributions are articulated with precision in Section 2, where the motivation and technical novelty are established without unnecessary jargon. Section 3.2 provides an excellent illustrative example that conveys the algorithm’s core intuition effectively, enabling readers to grasp its operational principles at a high level. This example avoids excessive detail while clearly demonstrating how IMGPO navigates complex optimization landscapes without relying on d-cover constraints. Section 3.3 further strengthens the work by offering a concise, pseudocode-driven implementation guide, ensuring reproducibility and easing adoption for future researchers. The clarity here is commendable—it transforms abstract methodology into actionable steps.

Empirical results (Section 4) compellingly validate IMGPO’s advantages. The experiments demonstrate consistent performance gains over state-of-the-art baselines across diverse synthetic and real-world benchmarks, particularly in high-dimensional or discontinuous settings where traditional methods struggle. The authors carefully contextualize these results, highlighting scenarios where IMGPO’s elimination of d-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization yields tangible efficiency improvements. Crucially, the ablation studies isolate the impact of each design choice, reinforcing the paper’s core thesis that continuity estimation is not a prerequisite for effective Bayesian optimization.

A notable strength is the paper’s ability to unify two previously divergent strands of research: methods leveraging continuity assumptions (e.g., Lipschitz optimization) and those avoiding them (e.g., random search or non-parametric approaches). By framing IMGPO as a synthesis that retains the robustness of the latter while incorporating the efficiency gains of the former, the authors provide a cohesive theoretical narrative that elevates the work beyond incremental improvement. This conceptual integration is a major contribution to the field.

The writing maintains a professional tone throughout, with minimal redundancy and logical flow between sections. Figures and tables are well-designed, complementing the text without overwhelming it. The related work section (Section 1.2) is particularly effective in situating IMGPO within the broader literature, explicitly addressing how it differs from prior art in terms of assumptions and computational requirements.

Minor suggestions for refinement: 
- The discussion of computational complexity (Section 3.1) could briefly compare runtime against d-cover-based methods to emphasize scalability benefits.
- Clarifying the exact role of the "infinite-metric" in the algorithm’s design (beyond the name) would further strengthen the theoretical foundation.

Overall, this paper delivers a substantial advance in Bayesian optimization. IMGPO’s simplicity, theoretical grounding, and empirical efficacy address a persistent limitation in the field while offering a versatile tool for practitioners. The clarity of presentation, robust experimental validation, and insightful synthesis of existing literature make this a standout contribution. It is well-suited for publication at NeurIPS and will likely influence both theoretical and applied research in optimization. I recommend acceptance without reservation.
