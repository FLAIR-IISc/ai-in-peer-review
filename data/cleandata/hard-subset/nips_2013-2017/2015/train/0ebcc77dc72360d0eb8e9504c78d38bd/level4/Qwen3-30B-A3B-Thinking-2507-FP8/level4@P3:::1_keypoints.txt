- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that attains exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Uses a bound-based search that considers infinitely many candidate semi-metric bounds instead of relying on a single GP-provided upper bound.  
- Generalizes and improves upon BaMSOO by achieving an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, versus BaMSOO’s polynomial bound.  
- Provides a rigorous regret bound (Theorem 1) linking GP-bound tightness to performance and explaining when GP information helps.  
- Analytical remarks (1–3) dissect trade-offs among using GP information, multiple bounds, and the cost of infinite‑metric exploration.  
- Theoretical framework offers a principled perspective on not committing to a single bound, applicable beyond Bayesian optimization.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates the work appropriately relative to SOO, DIRECT, BaMSOO, and other prior art.  
- Theoretical results are mathematically sound with supplementary proofs and honest discussion of limitations (e.g., scalability in higher dimensions).  
- Algorithm description, figure, and pseudocode are clear and sufficient for an expert to understand and potentially reproduce.  
- Originality is strong: the key insight is leveraging infinitely many candidate bounds while using GP to prune the search.  
- Significance is high: addresses a long-standing open problem by achieving exponential convergence without impractical sampling, with potential practical impact.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: lacks multiple initializations, preventing assessment of statistical significance.  
- Experiments omit comparisons with state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., Entropy Search, Information‑Directed Sampling).  
- Empirical tests are limited to synthetic functions up to 6D and include no real-world applications to validate practical utility.  
- Suggested revisions: add multiple initializations, compare with recent BO methods, include at least one real-world application, and analyze the Ξmax parameter’s effect.  
- Recommendation: accept (score 5) based on substantial theoretical contribution, contingent on strengthening experimental validation in a revision.
