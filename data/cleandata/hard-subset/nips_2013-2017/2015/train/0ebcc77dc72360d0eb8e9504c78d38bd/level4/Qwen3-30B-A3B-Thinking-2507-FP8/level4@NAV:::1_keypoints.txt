- Introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling while achieving exponential regret, generalizing Freitas et al. (2012) without their impractical sampling procedure.  
- Manuscript is well-written, logically structured, and situates the algorithm effectively within bound-based search literature.  
- Recommends a strong accept (5) based on the contribution and presentation.  
- Experimental section is below NIPS standards despite strong theory.  
- Does not perform multiple initial function evaluations, preventing statistically rigorous comparisons common in BO literature.  
- Fails to benchmark against state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods (e.g., information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search).  
- Experiments use only synthetic functions (max dimension 6) and lack real-world validation such as wet-lab experiments or parameter tuning scenarios.  
- Theoretical contributions are solid, but experimental shortcomings limit the paper’s impact and preclude a higher score.
