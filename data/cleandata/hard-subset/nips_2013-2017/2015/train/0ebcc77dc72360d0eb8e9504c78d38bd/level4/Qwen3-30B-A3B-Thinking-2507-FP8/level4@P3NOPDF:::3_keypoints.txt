- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that removes the need for d-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization.  
- Synthesizes continuity-estimation-based and continuity-free BO literature, presenting a unified framework.  
- Theoretical foundations are sound and the approach is positioned as a significant advance in Bayesian optimization.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-structured and clearly written, with precise articulation of contributions in Section 2.  
- Section 3.2 offers a clear illustrative example that conveys the algorithm’s core intuition without excessive detail.  
- Section 3.3 provides concise pseudocode, aiding reproducibility and adoption.  
- Empirical results show consistent performance gains over state-of-the-art baselines on synthetic and real benchmarks.  
- Particularly effective in high-dimensional or discontinuous settings where traditional methods struggle.  
- Ablation studies isolate the impact of design choices and support the claim that continuity estimation is not required.  
- Strong conceptual contribution by unifying Lipschitz/continuity-based and continuity-free methods into a cohesive narrative.  
- Figures, tables, and related work section are well-designed and effectively contextualize the contribution.  
- Minor suggestions: include a runtime comparison against d-cover methods in the complexity discussion and clarify the specific role of the “infinite-metric.”  
- Overall recommendation: substantial advance, well-suited for NeurIPS acceptance without reservation.
