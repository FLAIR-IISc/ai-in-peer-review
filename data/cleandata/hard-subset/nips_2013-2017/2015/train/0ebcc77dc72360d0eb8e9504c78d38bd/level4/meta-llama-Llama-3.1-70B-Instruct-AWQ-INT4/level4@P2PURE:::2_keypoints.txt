- Proposes a Bayesian Global Optimization variant that uses space/interval partitioning to generate future samples.  
- Uses Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) to avoid the internal global optimization step for selecting the next sample.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the method achieves exponential regret, improving upon prior work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described, explained, and illustrated with an example; considered a novel contribution.  
- Major reservation: experimental results appear limited to 1D test functions.  
- Concern that the interval partitioning approach may not scale to higher dimensions due to exponentially growing resolution requirements.  
- Method’s relation to the DIRECT algorithm raises additional concern because DIRECT is known to perform poorly in higher dimensions.  
- Authors should explicitly address the scalability/practical applicability limitation in the paper.  
- Minor: UCB is used "for brevity"—clarify whether alternative acquisition functions (e.g., expected improvement) could be used.  
- Minor: Clarify the page 3 statement about "simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds."  
- Minor: Clarify the page 4 sentence about "At n=16, the far right...but no function evaluation occurs" and explain why no evaluation occurs.
