- Introduces a novel Bayesian optimization variant that replaces internal global optimization with a space-partitioning algorithm using UCB to select samples.  
- Key innovation: using UCB avoids a separate global optimization step and yields a theoretically sound method.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: rigorous analysis with exponential regret bounds and clear novelty.  
- Algorithm is clearly described, well-motivated, and illustrated with a concrete example.  
- Major limitation: experiments are only on 1D test functions (e.g., Branin) with no justification for this restriction.  
- Scalability concern: interval/partitioning approach likely requires exponentially many partitions in higher dimensions, mirroring the curse of dimensionality (e.g., DIRECT).  
- Authors should explicitly discuss dimensionality limits or provide higher-dimensional (even small 2D) experiments or references to high-dimensional BO approaches.  
- Minor clarification: justify why UCB is used "for brevity" rather than alternatives like Expected Improvement and how that choice affects algorithm behavior and guarantees.  
- Clarify the phrase about "simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds" and how partitioning enables this.  
- Explain the claim that "no function evaluation occurs" at n=16 in the Branin example—why partitioning did not trigger a sample.  
- Overall: technically sound and novel with strong theory, but practical impact depends on addressing scalability concerns; minor revisions could make it a strong submission.
