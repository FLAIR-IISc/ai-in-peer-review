The paper introduces a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, thereby generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012), which relied on an impractical sampling procedure. The paper is well written and easy to follow, and the role of the new algorithm within the literature on bound-based search methods is clearly explained. In this sense, the work merits attention from the community.

I recommend a score of 5. I am not assigning a higher score because the experimental section falls below the standard expected in Bayesian optimization (BO) papers. My main criticisms are:
- The authors do not compare the methods under multiple initial evaluations of f, as is standard in BO. Consequently, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be drawn.
- Although they propose a bound-based search method, the authors do not compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches. In particular, information-theoretic methods, such as Entropy Search, are not included in the experiments.
- All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions (with a maximum dimension of 6). No real wet-lab or parameter-tuning experiment is provided to illustrate performance in practical scenarios.

Overall, the paper presents a promising global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, and it contains interesting theoretical results. However, in my opinion, the experimental section is below the standards expected for a NIPS paper.
