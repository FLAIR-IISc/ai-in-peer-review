- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential regret convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization by considering infinitely many upper bounds.  
- Directly addresses Wang et al. (2013)’s critique of de Freitas et al. (2012) regarding impractical δ-cover sampling.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: proves exponential regret O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1; supplementary proofs appear sound.  
- Eliminates two major computational barriers (δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization), improving practical feasibility.  
- Novel idea: simultaneously considering infinitely many bounds, distinct from GP-UCB and BaMSOO.  
- Provides rigorous analysis linking bound tightness to regret and explains when IMGPO outperforms alternatives.  
- Exceptionally well-written with clear context, consistent notation, detailed pseudocode, and helpful visualizations of hierarchical partitioning.  
- Significant theoretical implications and a useful framework for analyzing bound tightness in future BO work.  
- Major experimental flaw: no multiple initial evaluations—lacks statistical validation of reported performance.  
- Major experimental flaw: omits important baselines, notably information-theoretic methods (Entropy Search, Predictive Entropy Search).  
- Major experimental flaw: experiments only on synthetic functions up to 6D, with no real-world applications or parameter-tuning studies.  
- Recommendation: score 4 (Weak Accept); accept only if experiments are substantially improved (multiple runs, key baselines, and at least one real-world demonstration).
