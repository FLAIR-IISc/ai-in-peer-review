- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition-function optimization.  
- Addresses two prior limitations: impractical δ-cover sampling (de Freitas et al.) and the need for auxiliary non-convex optimization in GP-UCB.  
- Uses an infinite-metric exploration framework that considers all possible upper bounds from continuity rather than a single GP-derived bound.  
- Integrates GP priors with ideas from SOO and DIRECT to obtain exponential regret bounds, unlike BaMSOO which achieves only polynomial regret.  
- Theoretical contribution (Theorem 1) proves exponential regret O(λ^{N+N_gp}) with λ<1 and analyzes how bound tightness affects performance (Remarks 1–3).  
- Algorithm description (Sections 3.2–3.3) and pseudocode (Algorithm 1) are exceptionally clear and enable replication.  
- Experimental results on eight multimodal benchmarks show consistent superiority over SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, especially in low-dimensional problems.  
- Runtime results (Table 1) confirm improved efficiency by eliminating auxiliary optimization.  
- Strength: original solution to a known open problem by combining infinite-metric exploration with GP, bridging prior literatures.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis linking Assumption 1 to the regret bound and quantifying GP-vs-function-bound trade-offs.  
- Weakness: scalability—exponential convergence deteriorates with dimensionality and experiments lack thorough high-dimensional benchmarks beyond a REMBO-based 1000D embedding.  
- Weakness: parameter sensitivity—Ξmax needs tuning (default 22 works often) and no guidance for automatic selection is provided.  
- Weakness: comparison scope omits some recent BO methods (e.g., adaptive-kernel GP-UCB, other high-dimensional approaches) that could contextualize advantages.  
- Acceptance summary: Pro—resolves δ-cover issue with theoretical and practical gains; Con—limited high-dimensional analysis and a tunable parameter; overall recommendation: accept.
