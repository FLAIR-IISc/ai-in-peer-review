- Introduces a novel Bayesian Global Optimization variant that generates samples via space/interval partitioning.  
- Leverages a UCB acquisition to avoid internal global optimization for next-point selection.  
- Theoretical analysis claims improved performance with exponential regret, outperforming existing methods.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and well-illustrated with a helpful example.  
- Major reservation: experiments appear limited to one-dimensional test functions, raising doubts about scalability.  
- Concern that interval partitioning will require exponentially more partitions in higher dimensions, similar to DIRECT’s degradation.  
- Suggest authors address scalability (discussion, mitigation strategies, or acknowledgement) to clarify practical applicability.  
- Minor: clarify whether UCB was chosen only “for brevity” and if other acquisitions (e.g., EI) are compatible.  
- Minor: rephrase the unclear sentence about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor: explain the behavior at n=16 where “the far right…but no function evaluation occurs.”  
- Overall: novel and clear theoretical contribution, but practical utility is uncertain without higher-dimensional evaluation.
