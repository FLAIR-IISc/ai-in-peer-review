The paper presents a novel algorithm for global optimization that eschews delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, thereby generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012) that relies on an impractical sampling procedure. 

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the authors effectively situate their new algorithm within the current literature on bounded-based search methods. In this regard, I believe this work merits attention from the community.

I have assigned a score of 5, but not a higher score, due to my reservations regarding the experimental section, which I feel falls short of the standards expected in a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper. My primary criticisms of this section are as follows:

- The authors do not compare the methods using multiple initial evaluations of the function f, a standard practice in BO methods. Consequently, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be obtained.
- Although the authors propose a bounded-based search method, they should compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches. Notably, information-theoretic approaches, such as Entropy Search, are absent from the experiments.
- All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions with a maximum dimension of 6. Unfortunately, no real-world wet lab or parameter tuning experiment is used to illustrate the method's performance in practical scenarios.

The paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. While the paper is well-crafted, with some interesting theoretical results, I believe the experimental section falls short of the standards expected in a NIPS paper.
