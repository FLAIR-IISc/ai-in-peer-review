The paper presents a new algorithm for global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, thereby generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012) that relies on an impractical sampling procedure. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the authors effectively explain the role of the new algorithm within the current literature of bounded-based search methods. This work deserves attention from the community in this regard.

I vote a score of 5, but not higher, because I believe the experimental section falls short of the standard expected in a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper. My main criticisms of this section are:

- The authors do not compare the methods for multiple initial evaluations of the objective function f, which is a standard practice in BO methods. As a result, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be obtained.
- Although the authors propose a bounded-based search method, they should compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches. Notably, information-theoretic approaches, such as Entropy Search, are not used in the experiments.
- All experiments are carried out on synthetic functions with a maximum dimension of 6. No real-world experiment, such as a wet lab or parameter tuning, is used to illustrate the performance of the method in realistic scenarios.

The paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. While the paper is well-structured and presents some interesting theoretical results, in my opinion, the experimental section falls short of the standards expected in a NIPS paper.
