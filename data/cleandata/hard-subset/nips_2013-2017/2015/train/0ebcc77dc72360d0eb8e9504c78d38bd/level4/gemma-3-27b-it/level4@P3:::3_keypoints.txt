- Proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization method that achieves exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.
- Directly addresses the practical limitation of δ-cover sampling in prior exponential-convergence work.
- Paper is clearly structured and well-written, with effective motivation and literature positioning.
- Section 3.2’s illustrative example greatly aids understanding of the algorithm’s mechanics.
- Section 3.3 provides detailed implementation guidance and helpful pseudocode for replication.
- Experimental results show IMGPO generally outperforms existing methods, especially when tight GP bounds are unavailable.
- Theoretical analysis demonstrates exponential convergence and analyzes factors affecting performance (Section 4).
- The paper offers thoughtful remarks on algorithm behavior and potential improvements.
- Strength: eliminates a major practical barrier, making exponential convergence more realistic.
- Strength: clear presentation, detailed algorithm description, and convincing empirical evaluation.
- Weakness: potential scalability issues in high dimensions, with convergence rate degrading as dimensionality increases.
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameter Ξmax; more guidance on its selection is needed.
- Weakness: comparisons lack more recent, highly optimized BO baselines (e.g., BoTorch, Scikit-Optimize).
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (unknown semi-metric) needs deeper discussion of implications and limits.
- For acceptance: solves a significant practical problem, has strong theoretical guarantees, and is well-evaluated and written.
- Against acceptance: concerns about high-dimensional scalability, hyperparameter sensitivity, and limited baseline comparisons.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand discussion of Ξmax selection and include comparisons to contemporary BO libraries.
