## Review of Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO)

This paper presents a novel approach to Bayesian global optimization, termed Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), which notably avoids the complexities of d-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization procedures commonly found in existing methods. The authors successfully articulate the core contributions of IMGPO within a well-structured manuscript.

The paper’s strength lies in its clarity and accessibility. The main contributions are effectively highlighted, providing a clear understanding of the method’s novelty and potential impact. Section 3.2 offers a particularly valuable illustrative example, offering readers a high-level conceptual grasp of the algorithm’s operation. Furthermore, Section 3.3 details the algorithmic steps with sufficient precision to facilitate straightforward implementation and replication – a significant benefit for researchers in the field. 

The experimental results presented are compelling and demonstrate the promise of IMGPO. The authors effectively showcase the algorithm’s performance, suggesting a tangible advancement in the state-of-the-art. Beyond the direct algorithmic contribution, the paper’s merit is further enhanced by its successful integration of ideas from distinct branches of the Bayesian global optimization literature, specifically those leveraging and eschewing continuity estimation techniques. This synthesis offers a valuable unifying perspective.

IMGPO offers a significant advantage over existing Bayesian optimization methods by eliminating the need for computationally expensive d-cover sampling. This simplification is particularly noteworthy, as d-cover sampling can become a bottleneck in high-dimensional problems. Similarly, the avoidance of auxiliary optimization reduces the overall complexity and potential for error accumulation. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** IMGPO presents a genuinely novel approach to Bayesian optimization, circumventing common limitations of existing methods.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the method, making it accessible to a broad audience.
* **Reproducibility:** The detailed algorithmic description in Section 3.3 significantly enhances the paper’s reproducibility.
* **Strong Experimental Results:** The experiments demonstrate promising performance, supporting the effectiveness of IMGPO.
* **Unifying Perspective:** The paper provides a valuable synthesis of different approaches within Bayesian optimization.

**Potential Weaknesses (and suggestions for improvement):**

* **Theoretical Guarantees:** While the experimental results are encouraging, the paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of theoretical guarantees regarding the algorithm’s convergence or performance bounds.  Establishing some theoretical foundations would strengthen the contribution.
* **Scalability Discussion:** The paper briefly mentions computational cost, but a more detailed analysis of the algorithm’s scalability with respect to both the dimensionality of the input space and the number of function evaluations would be valuable.  Specifically, how does the computational cost compare to methods *with* d-cover sampling, considering the potential for the infinite metric calculations?
* **Comparison to Recent Alternatives:**  The related work section could be strengthened by a more detailed comparison to very recent advances in Bayesian optimization, particularly those focused on addressing scalability or improving sample efficiency.  For instance, exploring the relationship to methods leveraging deep Gaussian processes or other recent kernel learning techniques would be beneficial.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a strong contribution to the NIPS community. The proposed IMGPO algorithm offers a promising alternative to existing Bayesian optimization methods, addressing key limitations and demonstrating impressive performance. The clarity of the presentation, coupled with the emphasis on reproducibility, makes this work highly valuable.  While further investigation into theoretical guarantees and scalability would be beneficial, the existing manuscript represents a substantial and well-executed piece of research.  I recommend acceptance.
