- Proposes a Bayesian global optimization variant that generates future samples via space/interval partitioning.  
- Uses a UCB acquisition to avoid the usual inner global optimization when selecting the next sample.  
- Theoretical analysis shows exponential regret, an improvement over prior work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and well illustrated with an example.  
- Contribution is novel and presentation is clear.  
- Main reservation: experiments appear restricted to 1D test functions (not explicitly stated).  
- Concern that interval-partitioning will not scale to higher dimensions due to exponential growth in required resolution.  
- Method is closely related to DIRECT, which is known to perform poorly in higher dimensions; the paper should discuss this.  
- Minor: Clarify whether UCB is essential or if other acquisition functions (e.g., expected improvement) are supported.  
- Minor: Rephrase the unclear sentence about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor: Explain why no function evaluation occurs at n=16 in the example.  
- Overall: Strong theoretical contribution but limited practical validation and scalability discussion.
