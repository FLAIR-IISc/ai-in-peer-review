

The paper introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that circumvents delta-cover sampling while achieving exponential regret, thereby generalizing Freitas et al. (2012) by avoiding their impractical sampling procedure. The manuscript is well-written, logically structured, and effectively contextualizes the new algorithm within existing bound-based search literature, warranting significant community attention. I recommend a strong accept (5), though the experimental section falls below NIPS standards. Key limitations include:  
- Absence of multiple initial function evaluations, precluding statistically rigorous comparisons standard in Bayesian optimization (BO) literature.  
- Failure to benchmark against state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search).  
- Exclusive use of synthetic functions (max dimension 6) without real-world validation via wet-lab experiments or parameter tuning scenarios.  
While the theoretical contributions are sound, the experimental shortcomings prevent a higher score.
