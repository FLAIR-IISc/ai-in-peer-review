- Proposes a novel global optimization algorithm that removes the need for delta-cover sampling while achieving exponential regret, generalizing Freitas et al. (2012).
- Significant theoretical contribution with a rigorous foundation and clear connection to existing bounded-based search literature.
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written and accessible to readers familiar with Bayesian optimization.
- Main strength: avoids computationally prohibitive delta-cover sampling yet retains exponential regret guarantees.
- Derivations are sound and the extension of Freitas et al. is well-articulated.
- Experimental evaluation lacks statistical rigor—no multiple initial evaluations to enable fair comparisons.
- Inadequate benchmarking—omits comparisons to state-of-the-art BO methods like Entropy Search and Knowledge-Gradient.
- Over-reliance on low-dimensional synthetic functions (≤6D) with no real-world case studies, limiting demonstrated practical relevance and scalability.
- Experimental shortcomings undermine the paper’s practical impact despite strong theory.
- Recommendation: accept (score 5) for theoretical merit, but experimental flaws prevent a higher score.
- Requested revisions: add statistical comparisons across multiple initializations, benchmark against modern BO methods, and include at least one non-trivial real-world application.
