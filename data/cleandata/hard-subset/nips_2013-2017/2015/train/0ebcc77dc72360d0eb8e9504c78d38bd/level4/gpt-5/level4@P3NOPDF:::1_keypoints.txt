- Proposes a global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling while achieving exponential regret, generalizing Freitas et al. (2012).  
- Clear exposition with well-motivated theoretical contribution and strong connections to bound-based search literature.  
- Technically solid and likely of interest to the community.  

- Experimental protocol is nonstandard: no multiple random seeds/initializations or aggregated statistics, undermining robustness claims.  
- Baselines are limited/incomplete; missing strong contemporary BO methods (Entropy Search/PES, EI, GP-UCB, Thompson sampling).  
- Evaluation is synthetic-only and low-dimensional (≤6D), with no real-world benchmarks to demonstrate practical relevance.  
- No analysis of computational overhead, timing, or scalability per iteration.  

- Use standard BO protocol: multiple seeds, varying initial designs, and report means/variances/error bars.  
- Add stronger/diverse baselines (Entropy Search/PES, EI, GP-UCB, Thompson sampling, random/quasi-random) with fair tuning.  
- Include real-world benchmarks (hyperparameter tuning, robotics/control, wet-lab/materials) to demonstrate impact.  
- Evaluate sensitivity to model misspecification and provide ablations isolating the bound-based contribution.  
- Report per-iteration runtime and wall-clock time to convergence; compare computational cost to baselines.  
- Test higher dimensions (e.g., 10–20) to probe practical limits.  

- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful theoretical advance, but empirical evaluation needs substantial strengthening.  
- Rating: 5 — promising theory and presentation, but current empirics too limited for a stronger recommendation.
