- Proposes a Bayesian global optimization variant that generates future samples via space/interval partitioning.
- Uses UCB to avoid solving an internal global optimization problem for selecting the next sample.
- Theoretical analysis claims improvement over prior work, achieving exponential regret.
- Algorithm is clearly described and effectively illustrated with an example.
- Contribution is novel and the presentation is clear.
- Major reservation: experiments appear to be conducted only on 1D test functions (not explicitly stated).
- Concern that interval-partitioning may not scale to higher dimensions (hyperrectangles) due to exponential resolution growth.
- Notes relation to the DIRECT algorithm, which degrades severely in higher dimensions; paper should discuss scalability.
- Clarifies that scalability concerns do not diminish the theoretical contribution but matter for practical applications.
- Minor comment: unclear whether UCB is essential or other acquisition functions (e.g., EI) could be used despite the “for brevity” remark.
- Minor comment: unclear phrasing about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”
- Minor comment: unclear explanation why at n=16 “the far right... but no function evaluation occurs”; needs clarification.
