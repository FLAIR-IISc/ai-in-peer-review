- Proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel BO method that attains exponential convergence without auxiliary non-convex optimization or δ‑cover sampling.
- Avoids reliance on a single GP-derived bound by using a "semi-metric" assumption to consider infinitely many possible bounds simultaneously.
- Strong theoretical contribution: proves exponential regret O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, improving over polynomial bounds of prior methods (e.g., BaMSOO).
- Provides a comprehensive analysis of interactions between GP prior use and infinite-metric exploration, with insights on bound tightness and regret trade-offs.
- Algorithm presentation is clear, with detailed pseudocode and an effective illustrative example of interval partitioning (Fig.1).
- Supplementary material contains necessary theoretical proofs and well-articulated relations to prior work (DIRECT, SOO).
- Experimental evaluation is weak: only 1D test functions (Sin1, Sin2, Branin, Hartmann3), insufficient to demonstrate practical utility.
- Does not address the curse of dimensionality inherent to partitioning methods (e.g., DIRECT, SOO), nor practical scalability strategies.
- Claims of being "practically oriented" are unsupported due to lack of experiments in 2D+ settings and practical scaling analysis.
- Theoretical analysis hints at scalability problems (λ → 1 as dimension D increases), but the paper offers no practical remedies or experiments exploring this.
- Recommended improvements: add higher-dimensional (2D, 3D+) experiments and discuss potential scaling approaches (e.g., leveraging low-dimensional embeddings like REMBO).
- Minor issues: misleading claim that UCB is used "for brevity" despite being central; unclear phrasing about "candidates of the bounds" and how global/local search is conducted; insufficient explanation for the GP-based UCB use instead of function evaluation at n=16.
- Overall: valuable and novel theoretical advance, but practical impact is limited until higher-dimensional scalability and empirical validation are demonstrated.
