- Introduces IMGPO: hierarchical partitioning plus GP-UCB to select evaluation points, avoiding auxiliary non-convex optimization.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proves exponential convergence rate without requiring δ-cover sampling, improving on BaMSOO’s polynomial bound.  
- Originality/significance: first GP-based method achieving exponential regret bound without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover, resolving an open problem.  
- Clarity: algorithm presentation is clear, with an effective 1D illustrative example and well-explained relations to SOO, BaMSOO, and DIRECT.  
- Technical quality: rigorous theoretical analysis; Remarks 1–3 effectively explain trade-offs between GP bounds and the infinite-metric approach.  
- Primary weakness: potential scalability issues in higher dimensions due to hierarchical partitioning and the curse of dimensionality.  
- Empirical scope: experiments include higher-D problems (Hartmann6, Rosenbrock10, 1000D via REMBO) but lack a direct discussion of performance degradation with increasing dimension.  
- Recommendation: paper should explicitly discuss practical implications of dimensionality and when standard GP-UCB with an auxiliary optimizer might be preferable.  
- Minor question 1: Clarify whether the theoretical framework can extend to other acquisition functions (EI, PI) or is specific to UCB.  
- Minor comment 2: Rephrase or expand the sentence about "simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds" for clarity.  
- Minor comment 3: In the t=9, n=16 example, explicitly state why the interval is divided without a function evaluation (presumably UCB below current best; reference step (v)).
