This paper introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel algorithm for Bayesian global optimization. The primary contribution of this work is a method that circumvents the need for auxiliary optimization or d-cover sampling, which are common requirements in existing approaches. The paper is exceptionally clear, well-structured, and presents a significant contribution to the field.

The authors have organized the paper effectively, ensuring the main contributions are highlighted and easy to follow. The clarity of the presentation is a key strength. For instance, the illustrative example in Section 3.2 is particularly effective, providing readers with an intuitive grasp of the algorithm's core mechanics. Furthermore, the procedural description in Section 3.3 is laid out with precision, which will undoubtedly facilitate straightforward implementation and replication of the work by other researchers.

Beyond its practical advantages, the paper makes a valuable conceptual contribution by synthesizing ideas from disparate lines of research in Bayesian global optimization—specifically, those that rely on continuity estimation and those that do not. This unification offers a new perspective and enriches the theoretical landscape of the field.

The empirical evaluation is thorough, and the experimental results are compelling, demonstrating the practical efficacy of the proposed algorithm.

In summary, this is a well-executed paper that is both clearly presented and technically sound. It offers a strong and valuable contribution to the NIPS community by introducing a novel, practical, and well-motivated algorithm for Bayesian optimization.
