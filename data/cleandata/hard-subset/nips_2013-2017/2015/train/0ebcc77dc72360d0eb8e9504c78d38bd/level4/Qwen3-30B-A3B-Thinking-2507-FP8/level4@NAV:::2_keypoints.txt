- Introduces a variant of Bayesian Global Optimization using space/interval partitioning.  
- Employs UCB to avoid the internal global optimization step for selecting the next sample.  
- Theoretical analysis shows exponential regret bounds, a significant improvement over prior work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and accompanied by an illustrative example.  
- Contribution is novel and the presentation is overall clear.  
- Major reservation: experiments are limited to 1D test functions (not explicitly stated).  
- Concern that interval partitioning requires exponentially growing resolution and likely does not scale to higher dimensions.  
- Method resembles DIRECT and may inherit poor scalability in higher dimensions.  
- Paper should address the scalability limitation for practical applications despite the theoretical results.  
- Minor comment p.2: unclear if UCB is mandatory or if alternatives like expected improvement are possible.  
- Minor comment p.3: unclear statement about conducting simultaneous global and local searches based on all candidates.  
- Minor comment p.4: clarify the remark about n=16 and the far right region where no function evaluation occurs.
