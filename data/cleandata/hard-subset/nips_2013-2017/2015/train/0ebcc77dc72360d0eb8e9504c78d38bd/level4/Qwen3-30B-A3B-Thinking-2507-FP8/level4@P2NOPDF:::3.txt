

The paper introduces IMGPO (Infinite-Metric GP Optimization), a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that eliminates the need for d-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization—a significant advancement over existing methods. The core contribution is clearly articulated early and consistently reinforced throughout the paper.  

The structure effectively highlights the methodological innovation: Section 3.2 provides a well-chosen illustrative example that conveys the algorithm’s intuitive operation without excessive technical detail, enabling immediate comprehension. Section 3.3 complements this with precise pseudocode and implementation guidance, ensuring reproducibility and facilitating future adoption.  

Experimental results demonstrate compelling empirical performance, particularly in scenarios where traditional approaches struggle due to their reliance on d-cover sampling. The authors convincingly show that IMGPO achieves competitive or superior optimization efficiency while avoiding computational overheads inherent in prior methods.  

Crucially, the paper synthesizes key threads from the Bayesian optimization literature by unifying approaches that employ continuity estimation with those that explicitly avoid it. This conceptual bridge clarifies the trade-offs between different design choices and positions IMGPO as a versatile framework applicable across diverse problem classes.  

The writing is exceptionally clear and concise, with minimal jargon and logical flow from problem statement to solution. The contributions are not merely incremental but represent a meaningful step toward more practical and broadly applicable Bayesian optimization. The absence of unnecessary technical baggage (e.g., auxiliary optimization steps) significantly enhances the method’s accessibility and scalability.  

This work delivers a substantial methodological advance for the optimization community, with clear implications for real-world applications where sampling efficiency is critical. The paper’s clarity, technical soundness, and empirical validation make it a strong candidate for publication at NeurIPS.
