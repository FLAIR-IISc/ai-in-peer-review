- Introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence.  
- Eliminates the need for solving a non-convex auxiliary optimization at each step.  
- Removes reliance on impractical δ-cover sampling procedures.  
- Synergistically combines GP probabilistic bounds with the infinite-metric (SOO-like) optimization philosophy.  
- Implicitly considers an infinite family of semi-metrics for function smoothness and uses the GP to prune that search space.  
- Bridges two research lines (GP-based bounds and bound-free infinite-metric methods) and addresses an open problem by providing a practical GP method with exponential guarantees.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized presentation, with an instructive example and reproducible pseudocode.  
- Original and significant conceptual contribution unifying GP-UCB-style bounds with SOO’s infinite-metric approach.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: Theorem 1 gives a rigorous exponential regret bound and insightful analysis of trade-offs.  
- Comprehensive and convincing experiments showing IMGPO outperforms strong baselines; REMBO experiment suggests high-dimensional potential.  
- Limitation: regret bound depends on input dimension D, so scalability to very high dimensions remains challenging.  
- Limitation: introduces parameter Ξmax without detailed guidance; practical advice or analysis on setting it would be helpful.
