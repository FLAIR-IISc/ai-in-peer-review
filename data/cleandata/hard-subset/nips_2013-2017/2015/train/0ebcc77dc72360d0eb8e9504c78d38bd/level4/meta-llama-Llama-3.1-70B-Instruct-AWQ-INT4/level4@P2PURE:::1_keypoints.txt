- Introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret.
- Generalizes Freitas et al. (2012) by removing reliance on an impractical sampling procedure.
- Provides interesting theoretical results and situates the algorithm clearly within bounded-based search literature.
- Well-written and easy to follow; the contribution deserves community attention.
- Overall score 5 due to shortcomings in the experimental section.
- Experiments lack multiple initial evaluations/replicates, preventing meaningful statistical comparisons.
- Does not compare against state-of-the-art Bayesian Optimization methods, notably information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search.
- All experiments are synthetic and limited to dimensions ≤6, with no real-world (e.g., wet lab or parameter tuning) validations.
- Conclusion: strong theory but experimental evaluation falls short of expected NIPS standards.
