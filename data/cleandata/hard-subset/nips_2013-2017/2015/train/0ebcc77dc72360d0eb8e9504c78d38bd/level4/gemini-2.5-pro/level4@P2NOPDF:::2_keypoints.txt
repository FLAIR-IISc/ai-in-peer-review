- Introduces a novel Bayesian Global Optimization method that avoids acquisition-function maximization by using hierarchical space-partitioning guided by UCB.  
- Primary theoretical contribution: proof of exponential regret, a significant improvement over prior bounds.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with a helpful step-by-step example.  
- Major concern: scalability is unclear and experiments appear limited to one-dimensional test functions.  
- Interval-partitioning likely won’t scale to higher dimensions due to exponential growth in hyperrectangles (curse of dimensionality).  
- Method’s similarity to DIRECT reinforces concerns about poor high-dimensional performance.  
- Recommendation: authors should explicitly discuss scalability and provide theoretical arguments or preliminary higher-dimensional experiments.  
- Minor comment 1: Clarify whether UCB is chosen for brevity or if the theoretical analysis extends to other acquisitions (e.g., Expected Improvement).  
- Minor comment 2: Rephrase/clarify the sentence about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor comment 3: Explain why, in the n=16 example, a region is partitioned without a function evaluation (why the evaluation is skipped).
