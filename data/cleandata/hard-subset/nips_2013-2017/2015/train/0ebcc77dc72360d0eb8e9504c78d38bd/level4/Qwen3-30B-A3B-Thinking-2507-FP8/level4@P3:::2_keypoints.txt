- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Builds on and improves prior work (de Freitas et al. 2013; Wang et al. 2013) by removing impractical sampling and improving regret rates.
- Key innovation: simultaneously considers infinitely many candidate bounds instead of relying on a single GP-derived bound.
- Theoretical analysis is strong: proves an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1 under reasonable assumptions and provides full proofs in the supplement.
- Analyzes how GP, infinite-metric exploration, and bound tightness each affect the regret bound.
- Experimental results show consistent improvement over baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) on 1D test functions with a fair hyperparameter setup.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear conceptual framing, useful examples, and precise pseudocode.
- Connects algorithm operation to theoretical analysis throughout, maintaining clarity and mathematical rigor.
- Original contribution: novel infinite-bounds insight that addresses a fundamental limitation in GP-based optimization.
- Clearly differentiates from related methods (BaMSOO, DIRECT, SOO, GP-UCB) and highlights when multiple bounds are beneficial (Remarks 1–3).
- Significant advance: exponential convergence is a meaningful theoretical and practical improvement, removing costly auxiliary steps.
- Acknowledges dimensionality challenges (Remark 1) and suggests promising directions (e.g., REMBO) for higher-dimensional scaling.
- Strengths include first GP-based exponential convergence without auxiliary steps, rigorous theory, clear algorithm description, public code, and thoughtful theoretical discussion.
- Weaknesses: experiments limited to 1D, dimensionality scaling needs fuller discussion in the main text, minor clarifications on "infinitely many candidates," and lack of discussion on compatibility with other acquisition functions like EI.
- Recommendation: accept — substantial theoretical contribution with practical implementability and clear value to the NIPS community.
