## Review of NIPS 2015 Submission

This paper presents a novel algorithm for global optimization that circumvents the limitations of delta-cover sampling, achieving exponential regret and generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012), which relied on a computationally impractical sampling procedure. The paper is clearly written and easy to follow, effectively situating the proposed algorithm within the broader landscape of bounded-based search methods. I believe this work makes a valuable contribution and warrants attention from the research community.

My overall assessment is a score of 5. However, this score is tempered by concerns regarding the experimental evaluation, which falls below the standard typically expected of papers in Bayesian Optimization (BO) and related fields presented at NIPS. While the theoretical results are interesting, the lack of a comprehensive empirical analysis limits the impact of the work.

My primary criticisms of the experimental section are outlined below:

* **Insufficient Evaluation of Initial Samples:** The authors do not explore the sensitivity of their method, or competing methods, to varying numbers of initial function evaluations. This is a standard practice in BO literature, as the initial exploration phase significantly impacts performance. Without this analysis, a meaningful statistical comparison between the proposed algorithm and others is difficult to achieve. The performance gains (or lack thereof) stemming from the algorithm’s specific characteristics remain unclear.

* **Lack of Comparison to State-of-the-Art BO Methods:** Despite proposing a bounded-based search method, the authors do not compare their results against established, state-of-the-art Bayesian Optimization techniques. Specifically, information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search, which represent a strong baseline in this area, are absent from the experimental comparisons. Including such baselines would provide a more robust assessment of the proposed algorithm’s strengths and weaknesses.

* **Limited Scope of Experiments: Synthetic Functions Only:** All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions, and these functions are limited to a maximum dimension of 6. While synthetic benchmarks are useful for initial validation, the absence of experiments on real-world parameter tuning problems is a significant drawback. Demonstrating performance on practical, real-world scenarios is crucial to establishing the utility and generalizability of the algorithm. A “wetlab” experiment, even a relatively simple one, would substantially strengthen the paper.

In summary, this paper presents a theoretically sound and well-written algorithm with a clear connection to existing literature. However, the experimental section requires significant improvement to convincingly demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed approach and justify its presentation at a conference like NIPS. Addressing the concerns outlined above – particularly by including comparisons to strong BO baselines and evaluating performance on more realistic problems – would substantially enhance the value and impact of this work.
