- Proposes IMGPO, a novel Bayesian global optimization method that avoids d-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents the core contributions and algorithm.
- Section 3.2 provides a helpful illustrative example for conceptual understanding.
- Section 3.3 gives detailed algorithmic steps that facilitate implementation and reproducibility.
- Experimental results are strong and demonstrate the promise of IMGPO.
- The method synthesizes ideas from different Bayesian optimization approaches, offering a unifying perspective.
- Eliminates computationally expensive d-cover sampling, improving practicality for higher dimensions.
- Also avoids auxiliary optimization, reducing complexity and potential error sources.
- Lacks sufficient theoretical guarantees on convergence or performance bounds.
- Needs a more detailed scalability analysis with respect to dimensionality and evaluation count.
- Related work comparison should better address very recent scalable or sample-efficient Bayesian optimization methods (e.g., deep GPs or advanced kernel learning).
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed contribution with recommendation for acceptance, contingent on further theoretical and scalability analysis.
