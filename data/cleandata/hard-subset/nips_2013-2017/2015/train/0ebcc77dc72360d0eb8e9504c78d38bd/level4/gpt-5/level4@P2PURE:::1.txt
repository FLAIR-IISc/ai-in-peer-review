The paper introduces a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, thereby generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012), which relies on an impractical sampling procedure. The paper is well written and easy to follow, and the role of the algorithm within the existing literature on bound-based search methods is clearly articulated. In this sense, I think the work deserves attention from the community.

I vote 5; however, I do not give a higher score because the experimental section falls below the standard one expects in a BO paper. My main criticisms are:
- The authors do not compare the methods under multiple initial evaluations of f, as is standard in BO. Consequently, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be made.
- Although they propose a bound-based search method, the authors do not compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches. Information-theoretic methods, such as Entropy Search, are not included in the experiments.
- All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions (with maximum dimension 6). No real wet-lab or parameter-tuning experiment is used to demonstrate performance in realistic scenarios.

In summary, the paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. It is a solid paper with interesting theoretical results, but in my view the experimental section is below the standards expected for a NIPS paper.
