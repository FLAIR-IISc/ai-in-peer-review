This paper describes a method of Bayesian global optimization that does not require de-covering sampling or auxiliary optimization.

The paper is structured in such a way that its main contributions are well-described in the main section.

The authors provide a good illustrative example of the algorithm in section 3.2, which gives the reader a nice high-level idea.

Additionally, section 3.3 clearly states the algorithm's functionality programmatically for future implementations/iterations.

The experimental results have also proven to be quite exciting.

Furthermore, the authors note that this paper combines ideas from the Bayesian global optimization literature that use consistency assumptions and those that do not.

The paper describes infinite-metric GP optimization (IMGPO), an algorithm for Bayesian optimization without the need for auxiliary optimization or de-covering sampling. This paper is not only clearly written but also makes a strong contribution to the NIPS community.
