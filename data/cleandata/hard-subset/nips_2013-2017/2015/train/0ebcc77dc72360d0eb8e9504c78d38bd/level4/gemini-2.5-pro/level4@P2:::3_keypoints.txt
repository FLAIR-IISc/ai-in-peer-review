- Introduces IMGPO, a novel GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Key insight: combine hierarchical partitioning (SOO-like) with GP-UCB pruning to avoid needing a known smoothness constant.  
- Theoretical contribution: first practical GP-based method with a proven exponential regret bound (Theorem 1).  
- Removes impractical components of prior work (auxiliary nonconvex optimization and δ-cover sampling).  
- Analysis clearly explains how GP posterior and infinite-metric search jointly enable performance gains.  
- Empirical evaluation on eight benchmarks (1 to 1000 dims) shows IMGPO generally outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI/EI.  
- Honest reporting: identifies one case where SOO competed due to a misleading GP prior and explains it.  
- Paper quality: technically strong, well-written, and reproducible with detailed pseudocode and illustrative examples.  
- Originality: elegant unification of two research lines that solves an open problem from prior work.  
- Significance: practically appealing and likely influential for hyperparameter tuning and expensive black-box optimization.  
- Minor weakness: scalability degrades with dimensionality (convergence rate parameter λ depends on dimension D).  
- Promising mitigation: successful use of IMGPO within REMBO demonstrates a potential path for high-dimensional problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves an important open problem, novel and effective method, strong theory and experiments, high clarity.  
- Argument against acceptance (minor): concern about high-dimensional scalability, though addressed and common across global optimization methods.
