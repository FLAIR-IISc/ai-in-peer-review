The paper presents a new algorithm for global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012) that relies on an impractical sampling procedure. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the role of the new algorithm within the current literature of bound-based search methods is well-explained.

The authors propose a novel approach that leverages the existence of an unknown bound encoded in the continuity at a global optimizer, which is a key contribution of the paper. The algorithm, called Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), is shown to achieve an exponential convergence rate without the need for auxiliary optimization and delta-cover sampling.

The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm, including a proof of the exponential convergence rate and a discussion of the effects of the tightness of the upper confidence bound (UCB) by GP. The authors also provide a comparison with previous algorithms, such as the BaMSOO algorithm, and show that IMGPO achieves a better regret bound.

However, the experimental section is somewhat limited. The authors only compare the performance of IMGPO with a few other algorithms, including SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, on a set of synthetic functions. While the results show that IMGPO outperforms the other algorithms in general, the experimental setup is not comprehensive enough to fully demonstrate the strengths and weaknesses of the algorithm.

In particular, the authors do not compare the methods for multiple initial evaluations of f, which is a standard practice in Bayesian optimization (BO) methods. This makes it difficult to obtain a meaningful statistical comparison of the methods. Additionally, the authors do not compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches, such as information-theoretic approaches like Entropy Search.

Furthermore, all the experiments are carried out on synthetic functions, with a maximum dimension of 6. It would be beneficial to include real-world experiments, such as parameter tuning in machine learning or engineering design problems, to demonstrate the performance of the method in real scenarios.

Overall, while the paper presents some interesting theoretical results and a novel algorithm, the experimental section is below the standards of a NIPS paper. With a more comprehensive experimental setup and a comparison with state-of-the-art BO approaches, the paper could be even stronger.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel approach to global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret.
* The algorithm, IMGPO, is shown to achieve an exponential convergence rate without the need for auxiliary optimization and delta-cover sampling.
* The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm.

Weaknesses:

* The experimental section is limited and does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other algorithms.
* The authors do not compare the methods for multiple initial evaluations of f, which is a standard practice in BO methods.
* The experiments are only carried out on synthetic functions, with a maximum dimension of 6.
* The paper does not compare the results with state-of-the-art BO approaches.

Acceptance: 5

Recommendations for improvement:

* Include a more comprehensive experimental setup, including multiple initial evaluations of f and a comparison with state-of-the-art BO approaches.
* Include real-world experiments to demonstrate the performance of the method in real scenarios.
* Provide a more detailed analysis of the effects of the tightness of the UCB by GP on the performance of the algorithm.
