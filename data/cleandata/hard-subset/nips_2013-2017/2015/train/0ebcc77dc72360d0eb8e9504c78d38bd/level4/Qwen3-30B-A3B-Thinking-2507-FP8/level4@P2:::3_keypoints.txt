- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization.  
- Resolves Wang et al.'s open problem by combining GP estimates with infinite-metric hierarchical partitioning.  
- Unifies "known-bound" (e.g., GP-UCB) and "unknown-bound" (e.g., SOO, DIRECT) approaches via an infinite-metric exploration framework.  
- Theoretical advance: proves exponential regret bound r_N = O(λ^{N+N_gp}) with λ < 1 (Theorem 1) and tight characterization of infinite-metric loss ρ_t.  
- Uses GP-estimated bounds over infinitely many candidate semi-metrics to dynamically tighten search and outperform single-bound methods.  
- Empirically outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on 8 multimodal benchmarks (including 1000D Sin2), except Rosenbrock2 where GP mismodeling hurt performance.  
- Experimental setup and presentation are rigorous and transparent (50 runs, standardized kernels/hyperparameters, clear figures/tables).  
- Algorithm description, pseudocode, and intuitive figures are clear and reproducible in principle.  
- Acknowledged scalability limitation: λ → 1 as dimension D increases, reducing practical exponential gains in high dimensions.  
- 1000D experiment relies on REMBO as a subroutine, which does not fully address core high-dimensional scalability concerns.  
- Implementation nuance: GP-based label/skip logic (e.g., skipping evaluations when UCB < f^+) adds practical complexity not fully discussed.  
- Computational overhead and management of GP labels for large N need clearer discussion to improve reproducibility.  
- Strong arguments for acceptance: resolves key open problem, solid theory and experiments, new analytical framework with broader implications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved high-dimensional scalability and insufficient detail on GP label management.  
- Overall assessment: significant, well-executed contribution with strong theory, experiments, and clarity; recommended for acceptance despite noted scalability/implementation caveats.
