The paper introduces a global optimization algorithm that dispenses with δ-cover sampling while achieving exponential convergence in simple regret, thereby extending the results of de Freitas et al. (2012), which relied on an impractical sampling scheme.

The manuscript is clearly written and easy to follow, and the positioning of the proposed algorithm within the landscape of bound-based search methods is well articulated. In this regard, I believe the work merits attention from the community.

I assign a score of 5, but not higher, because the experimental section falls below what is typically expected in BO papers. My main concerns are:
- The comparisons do not consider multiple random initializations of f, as is standard in BO; consequently, no meaningful statistical comparisons are provided.
- Although the approach is bound-based, the experiments omit comparisons with state-of-the-art BO methods, particularly information-theoretic approaches such as Entropy Search.
- All experiments are on synthetic benchmarks (with maximum dimension 6). No real-world tasks (e.g., wet-lab or hyperparameter tuning) are included to demonstrate practical performance.

In summary, the paper proposes a new global optimization algorithm that avoids δ-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. The theory is appealing and the presentation is good, but in my view the experimental evaluation is below NIPS standards.
