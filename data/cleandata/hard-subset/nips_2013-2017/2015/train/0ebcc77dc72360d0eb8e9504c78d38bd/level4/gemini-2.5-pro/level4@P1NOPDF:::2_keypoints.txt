- Proposes a variant of Bayesian Global Optimization using space-partitioning to select evaluation points.  
- Uses an Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) criterion on partitions to avoid globally optimizing an acquisition function.  
- Strong theoretical analysis proving an exponential regret bound, a significant improvement over prior work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with a running example.  
- Overall well-written and presents a novel, clear contribution.  
- Primary reservation: scalability and limited experimental scope (experiments appear restricted to 1D).  
- Concern that interval-partitioning may not scale to higher dimensions due to exponential growth in hyperrectangles (similar to DIRECT).  
- Recommendation: authors should discuss expected performance in higher dimensions or include experiments on low-dimensional (e.g., 2D/3D) problems.  
- Minor comment: clarify whether the use of UCB is fundamental to the theory or if other acquisition functions (e.g., Expected Improvement) could be used.  
- Minor comment: clarify the meaning of "simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds."  
- Minor comment: in the example, explain why no function evaluation occurs in the far-right interval at n=16.
