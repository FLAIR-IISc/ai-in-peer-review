This paper describes a method for Bayesian global optimization that avoids the need for d-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization procedures. The main contributions are clearly articulated in the initial sections of the paper, providing a strong foundation for understanding the proposed approach. 

The authors effectively illustrate the algorithm with an example in section 3.2, offering readers a valuable high-level overview. Furthermore, section 3.3 provides a clear and programmatic description of the algorithm’s implementation, facilitating potential replication and future work. The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the promise of the method. 

Notably, the authors highlight the paper’s contribution in synthesizing ideas from both continuity estimation-based and non-continuity estimation-based approaches within the Bayesian global optimization literature. The paper introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), an algorithm for Bayesian optimization that successfully circumvents the limitations of auxiliary optimization and d-cover sampling. Overall, this is a clearly written and significant contribution to the NIPS community.
