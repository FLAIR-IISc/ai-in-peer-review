- Introduces a Bayesian global optimization variant using space/interval partitioning to select future evaluations.
- Employs UCB to avoid the internal global optimization step when choosing the next sample.
- Theoretical analysis shows improved guarantees with exponentially fast regret, outperforming prior work.
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example.
- Contribution is novel and the paper is overall well written.
- Major reservation: experiments appear limited to 1D functions, which is not stated explicitly.
- Concern that interval/hyperrectangle partitioning will not scale to higher dimensions due to exponential resolution growth.
- Related to the DIRECT algorithm, which also degrades substantially with increasing dimensionality; the paper should discuss this.
- Minor: clarify whether UCB is essential or if other acquisition functions (e.g., expected improvement) could be used.
- Minor: rephrase the unclear sentence on p.3 about conducting global and local searches based on candidate bounds.
- Minor: explain on p.4 why no function evaluation occurs at n=16 for clarity.
