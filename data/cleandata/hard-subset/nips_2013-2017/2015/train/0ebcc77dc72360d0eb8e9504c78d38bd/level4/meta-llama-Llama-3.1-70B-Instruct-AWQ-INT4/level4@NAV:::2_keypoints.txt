- Proposes a Bayesian Global Optimization variant using interval/space partitioning to generate samples and UCB to avoid internal global optimization.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the method achieves exponential regret, improving prior results.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and includes an illustrative example.  
- Overall a novel contribution and generally well-presented.  
- Major concern: experiments appear limited to 1D (not stated) and may not scale to higher dimensions.  
- Scalability worry: interval/hyperrectangle partitioning likely requires exponentially increasing resolution in higher dimensions (curse of dimensionality).  
- Echoes limitations of the DIRECT algorithm, which performs poorly as dimensionality grows.  
- Recommendation: authors should address this practical scalability limitation in the paper, even if theory remains valid.  
- Minor: clarify whether other acquisition functions (e.g., expected improvement) can be used instead of UCB.  
- Minor: clarify the sentence about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor: explain the statement on page 4 about “At n=16, the far right...but no function evaluation occurs.”
