- Proposes a novel GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.
- Core idea: use an unknown semi-metric to guide search combined with hierarchical partitioning.
- Builds on prior work (de Freitas et al.) and addresses the practical hurdle of δ-cover sampling.
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous proof of exponential convergence with clearly stated assumptions.
- Practical relevance: removes impractical requirements, making the method more applicable to real problems.
- Clear presentation and exposition; Figure 1 effectively illustrates the hierarchical partitioning.
- Empirical validation: consistently outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-EI on benchmark functions.
- Insightful discussion of limitations, trade-offs between cost and convergence, and potential future improvements.
- Weakness: limited analysis of parameter sensitivity (e.g., Ξmax) and tuning.
- Weakness: scalability to higher-dimensional problems remains a significant concern.
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (existence of an unknown semi-metric) may be strong and needs more justification.
- Weakness: redundant phrasing in the abstract that could be tightened (line 49).
- Weakness: minor typo—a misplaced parenthesis in line 72.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a key challenge, strong theory, solid experiments, clear writing, and novel contribution.
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved scalability, need for justification of the semi-metric assumption, and limited parameter sensitivity analysis.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution and suitable for acceptance to NIPS.
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
