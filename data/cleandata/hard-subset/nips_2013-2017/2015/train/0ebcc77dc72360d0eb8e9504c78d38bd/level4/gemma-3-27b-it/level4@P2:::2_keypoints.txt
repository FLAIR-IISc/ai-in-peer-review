- Proposes a novel Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence that avoids auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling.
- Core idea: infinite-metric GP optimization with hierarchical partitioning to explore the search space efficiently.
- Balances global and local search by considering a range of potential bounds on the objective function.
- Clear, well-written exposition with a helpful illustrative example and public code repository.
- Significant theoretical contribution: rigorous analysis proving exponential regret convergence.
- Builds on and appropriately cites prior work in Bayesian and Lipschitz optimization, and notes connections to DIRECT.
- Strength: explicitly addresses practical limitations of prior methods (e.g., unknown Lipschitz constants, impractical sampling).
- Empirical evaluation is limited to 1D or low-dimensional problems, raising scalability concerns.
- Partitioning-based approach may suffer from the curse of dimensionality similar to DIRECT; scalability discussion is needed.
- Recommend including discussion or preliminary experiments on higher-dimensional benchmarks to strengthen the paper.
- Minor revisions: expand justification for UCB choice and note other acquisition functions (e.g., EI).
- Minor clarifications needed on the statement about simultaneous global/local searches and the missing evaluation at n=16.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, clarity, and practical significance.
- Argument against acceptance: unresolved scalability concerns in higher dimensions.
- Overall: strong paper recommended for acceptance if authors address scalability concerns and add discussion or preliminary high-dimensional results.
