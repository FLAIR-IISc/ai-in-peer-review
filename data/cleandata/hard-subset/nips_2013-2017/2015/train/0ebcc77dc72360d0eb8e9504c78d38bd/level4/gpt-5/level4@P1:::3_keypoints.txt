- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization method that uses a GP prior and hierarchical partitioning to reason over infinitely many candidate bounds.  
- Avoids auxiliary nonconvex optimization and the δ-cover sampling procedure.  
- Positions IMGPO between GP-UCB and SOO/BaMSOO to retain GP simplicity while achieving fast convergence.  
- Paper is clearly organized with a well-motivated introduction and related-work positioning.  
- Section 3.2 provides an intuitive 1D example that aids understanding.  
- Section 3.3 gives precise, implementation-ready pseudocode supporting reproducibility.  
- Algorithmic description clearly separates global/local search roles and the use of GP-derived UCBs for candidate screening.  
- Empirical results are strong: IMGPO is competitive or superior to baselines across diverse benchmarks with realistic kernel/hyperparameter treatment.  
- Analysis is conceptually informative, showing benefits of leveraging multiple implicit bounds versus a single estimated bound and connecting to prior bound-based search paradigms.  
- Originality: the “infinite-metric” combination of GP modeling with partitioning and avoidance of explicit continuity bounds is novel.  
- Significance: addresses practical BO pain points and offers insights likely valuable to both theory and practice communities.  
- Key strengths: clear exposition, illustrative example and detailed pseudocode, promising experiments, conceptual integration of BO approaches, and practical relevance (no auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling).
