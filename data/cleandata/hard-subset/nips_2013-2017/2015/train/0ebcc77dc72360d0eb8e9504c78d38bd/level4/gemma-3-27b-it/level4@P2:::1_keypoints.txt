- Introduces a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without delta-cover sampling from de Freitas et al. (2012).
- Core contribution: exponential convergence rate attained without auxiliary optimization or delta-cover sampling.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, making the approach easy to understand.
- Authors effectively contextualize the work relative to Lipschitz optimization and Gaussian process optimization.
- Reviewer score: 5 — strong theory but experiments do not meet NIPS standards.
- Experiments omit multiple random initializations, weakening statistical robustness of comparisons.
- Comparisons do not include recent state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., information-theoretic acquisition functions like Entropy Search).
- Evaluation is limited to synthetic benchmark functions (up to 6D) with no real-world parameter tuning experiments.
- Experimental results lack statistical significance testing, reducing confidence in observed differences.
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretical novelty, clear presentation, and solid contextualization.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak experimental validation, limited practical application evidence, and missing statistical analysis.
