- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary non-convex optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Combines GP-UCB modelling with optimistic hierarchical partitioning (inspired by SOO/DIRECT), using an implicit infinite family of upper bounds from an unknown semi-metric.  
- Uses GP-UCB not to drive search directly but to prune provably suboptimal branches, speeding identification of intervals with the global optimizer.  
- Theorem 1 proves an exponential regret bound, a significant improvement over polynomial bounds (e.g., BaMSOO).  
- Proposes the "infinite-metric exploration loss" (ρt) to quantify the cost of not committing to a single known bound.  
- Analysis clarifies how the GP's tightness influences convergence and why the infinite-metric approach can outperform known Lipschitz-like bounds.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on benchmarks from 1 to 1000 dimensions.  
- Experiments realistically assume unknown kernel/hyperparameters and include timing results showing practical speedup from avoiding auxiliary optimization.  
- Paper is technically sound, exceptionally well-written, and clear, with effective figures and implementable pseudocode.  
- Minor clarity issues: abstract repetition and a few typographical/phrasing errors needing proofreading.  
- Work is highly original, novelly synthesizing GP-UCB pruning with optimistic partitioning to solve a known open problem.  
- Significant impact: resolves a theoretical open problem and offers a practical, easier-to-implement algorithm for expensive black-box optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty/significance, strong theoretical results, convincing empirical evidence, and exceptional clarity.  
- Argument against acceptance: potential scalability degradation in very high dimensions (λ → 1); more discussion of limitations would help despite the REMBO experiment.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
