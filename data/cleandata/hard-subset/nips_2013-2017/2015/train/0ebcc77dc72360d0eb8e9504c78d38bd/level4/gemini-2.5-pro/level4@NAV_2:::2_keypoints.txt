- Introduces IMGPO: combines hierarchical partitioning with GP UCB to avoid auxiliary acquisition optimization and δ-cover sampling, achieving exponential convergence without those impractical requirements.
- Significance/originality: solves a known open theoretical problem by integrating GP bounds with infinite-metric partitioning (SOO/DIRECT style).
- Technical quality: rigorous analysis with Theorem 1 giving improved regret bounds; Remarks 1–3 clarify the GP–partitioning interplay.
- Clarity: well-written, formal algorithm presentation plus an intuitive 1D example and solid literature positioning.
- Weakness: scalability—hierarchical partitioning is subject to the curse of dimensionality and convergence degrades with higher D.
- Weakness: experiments are mostly low-dimensional (D ≤ 6); the 1000-D REMBO result shows use as a subroutine rather than core scalability; lacks moderate (10–20D) benchmarks.
- Question: clarify whether the theory critically depends on UCB’s high-probability upper bound and if it can extend to other acquisitions like Expected Improvement.
- Suggestion: explain that “global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds” means implicit exploration over a family of function smoothnesses (semi-metrics l).
- Suggestion: in Figure 1 walkthrough, explicitly link the n=16 no-evaluation step to algorithm step (v) to show how GP UCB avoids a costly function evaluation.
