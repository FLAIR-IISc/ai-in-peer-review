This paper presents a new algorithm for global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. Thus, Freitas L. Al, 2012's work, which relies on an unrealistic sampling method, has been generalized.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The role of the new algorithm in the current literature of constraint-based search methods is well explained. In this sense, I think this work deserves some attention from the community.

I voted 5, but it's not a good score because I think the experimental section is below the standard of a BO paper that would be seen. My main criticisms of this section are:

- The authors did not compare the methods of multiple initial estimates of f, as this is a standard of BO methods. Therefore, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods is found.
- Although they proposed a constraint-based search method, the authors should have compared their results with the state-of-the-art methods of BO methods. Information theoretical methods, such as entropy search, were not used in the experiment.
- All experiments were done on synthetic functions (up to 6). No real weight lab was used to test the effectiveness of the method in real-world situations.

The paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. The paper is beautiful, with some interesting theoretical results, but in my opinion, the experimental section is below the standard of a NIPS paper.
