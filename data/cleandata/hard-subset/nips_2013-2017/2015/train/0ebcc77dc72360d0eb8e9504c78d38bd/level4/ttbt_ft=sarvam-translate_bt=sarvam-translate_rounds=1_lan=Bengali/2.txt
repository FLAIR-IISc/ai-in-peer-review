This paper presents a variation of Bayesian global optimization, where a future sample is generated through a space/partition algorithm.

Using UCB, it can avoid the (internal) global optimization problem of how to select the next sample.

Theoretical analysis shows that this method provides theoretical improvement (exponential regret), which is better than previous work.

The algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example.

The paper makes a new contribution and is presented clearly overall.

I have a doubt about this paper.

The experimental results seem to be only for 1D test functions (not explicitly mentioned).

I suspect that the space/partition method does not work well for higher dimensions (hyper rectangles) because the "resolution" must increase exponentially.

This method is related to the well-known DIRECT algorithm, which is known to be badly affected when the problem dimension increases.

I think at least something should be said about this in the paper.

It does not change the theoretical contribution, but it is clearly important for any practical purpose.
