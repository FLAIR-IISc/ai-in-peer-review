- Proposes a Bayesian global optimization variant that generates future samples via interval/space partitioning and uses UCB to avoid inner global optimization.
- Theoretical analysis yields improved guarantees, notably exponentially decaying regret.
- Algorithm is clearly described and well illustrated with an example; contribution appears novel and presentation is clear.
- Main reservation: experiments appear to be conducted only on 1D test functions (not stated explicitly).
- Concern that the interval-partitioning strategy will scale poorly in higher dimensions (partitions become hyperrectangles and required resolution grows exponentially).
- Method is closely related to DIRECT, which is known to degrade severely with dimensionality; this connection should be discussed.
- Paper should either provide higher-dimensional experiments or give a clear rationale for focusing on 1D.
- Minor: Clarify whether UCB is used merely “for brevity” or if the method requires UCB; can it extend to other acquisition functions (e.g., expected improvement)?
- Minor: Rephrase the unclear sentence on p.3 about “candidates of the bounds” and precisely explain the concurrent global vs. local search.
- Minor: Explain why no function evaluation occurs at n=16 as stated on p.4.
- Overall: sound, novel BO variant with strong theoretical results, but empirical evaluation and scalability concerns need to be addressed.
