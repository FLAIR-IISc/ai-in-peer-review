- Introduces a novel Bayesian optimization method that replaces inner optimization with a space-partitioning algorithm.  
- Leverages UCB acquisition to avoid the computationally intensive global optimization step.  
- Theoretical analysis yields exponential regret bounds, improving on prior work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and supported by a well-illustrated example.  
- Contribution is novel, paper is well-structured and accessible.  
- Major concern: experiments are limited to 1D functions with no justification.  
- Scalability concern: interval partitioning likely fails in higher dimensions due to exponentially growing partition resolution (similar to DIRECT).  
- Paper should explicitly discuss this limitation and propose extensions or alternatives for higher-dimensional problems.  
- Minor: unclear why UCB was chosen “for brevity” and why alternatives (e.g., EI) were excluded.  
- Minor: ambiguous phrasing about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds” needs rewording.  
- Minor: clarify why the partitioning algorithm does not trigger a function evaluation at n=16 in the example.  
- Overall: technically sound and theoretically significant; suitable for publication after addressing scalability concerns and the minor clarifications.
