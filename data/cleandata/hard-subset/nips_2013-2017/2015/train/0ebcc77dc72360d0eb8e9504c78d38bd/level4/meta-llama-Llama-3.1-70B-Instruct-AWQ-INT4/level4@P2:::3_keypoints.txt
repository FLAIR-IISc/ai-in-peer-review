- Proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence.
- IMGPO does not require auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling, addressing key limitations of prior methods.
- Manuscript is clear and well-organized, effectively conveying main contributions.
- Introduction gives a thorough background and motivation, highlighting limitations of existing approaches.
- Uses the concept of an unknown semi-metric to guide optimization.
- Section 3.2 provides a helpful illustrative example for high-level understanding.
- Section 3.3 presents clear technical details, facilitating implementation and replication.
- Experimental results are strong, showing IMGPO's effectiveness across tasks and vs. SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI.
- Paper unifies ideas from Bayesian global optimization that both use and do not use continuity estimation.
- Provides a practical analysis framework explaining why non-tight bounds can still be useful.
- Well-written and accessible to an informed audience.
- Assumes familiarity with Bayesian optimization and GPs, which may hinder non-expert readers.
- Lacks sufficient discussion of limitations and potential future research directions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective method, strong experiments, meaningful contribution to NIPS.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility for non-experts and insufficient discussion of limitations.
