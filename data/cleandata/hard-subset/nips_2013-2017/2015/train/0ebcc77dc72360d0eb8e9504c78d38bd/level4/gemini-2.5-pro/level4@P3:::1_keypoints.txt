- Paper introduces IMGPO, a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that combines hierarchical partitioning with GP-UCB to achieve exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling, supported by theoretical analysis and synthetic experiments.

- Strength: Strong theoretical contribution — proves an exponential regret bound (Theorem 1) improving over prior polynomial bounds.

- Strength: Novel algorithmic synthesis — elegantly merges partitioning-based methods with GP-derived bounds to prune search over an infinite family of semi-metrics.

- Strength: Clear presentation — well-written, well-organized, with helpful illustrative examples and insightful analysis.

- Weakness: Limited baselines — experiments omit modern information-theoretic methods (e.g., Entropy Search, PES), preventing assessment against state-of-the-art.

- Weakness: No real-world benchmarks — evaluation is restricted to synthetic functions, so practical utility in expensive real-world tasks is untested.

- Weakness: Insufficient statistical rigor — deterministic methods evaluated from a single run with fixed initialization and no sensitivity analysis to GP hyperparameters.

- Argument for acceptance: Resolves an open problem with a practical GP-based algorithm and a strong theoretical guarantee.

- Argument for acceptance: IMGPO is a novel, elegant combination of two optimization paradigms.

- Argument for acceptance: Paper is clearly written and presents ideas effectively.

- Argument for rejection: Experimental section is below top-tier standards and lacks key comparisons (e.g., Entropy Search).

- Argument for rejection: Lack of real-world application experiments undermines practical significance.

- Argument for rejection: Empirical results lack statistical robustness (single-init runs, no error bars).

- Overall: Borderline paper — strong and significant theoretical advance but weakened by an inadequate experimental evaluation; needs stronger empirical validation for full acceptance.
