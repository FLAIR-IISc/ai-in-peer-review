- Introduces a novel Bayesian Global Optimization variant using interval/space partitioning to generate candidate samples.
- Uses UCB to avoid solving an internal global optimization for the next sample.
- Theoretical analysis shows exponential regret, improving on prior work.
- Algorithm is clearly described, explained, and illustrated with an example.
- Paper is well-presented and makes a novel contribution to the field.
- Major concern: experiments appear to be only on 1D test functions (not explicitly stated).
- Scalability concern: interval partitioning likely does not scale to higher dimensions due to exponential resolution growth (similar limitations as DIRECT).
- Authors should explicitly discuss this practical scalability limitation, even if theory remains valid.
- Minor comment: clarify whether alternative acquisition functions (e.g., expected improvement) can be used instead of UCB.
- Minor comment: clarify the statement about conducting “global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”
- Minor comment: explain why “no function evaluation occurs” at n=16 (page 4).
