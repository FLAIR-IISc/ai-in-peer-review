This paper proposes a novel Bayesian optimization method, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), which achieves exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling. The authors present a clear and well-organized manuscript, effectively conveying the main contributions of their work.

The introduction provides a thorough background on Bayesian optimization, highlighting the limitations of existing methods that rely on δ-cover sampling or auxiliary optimization. The authors then present their approach, which leverages the concept of an unknown semi-metric to guide the optimization process.

Section 3.2 offers a helpful illustrative example of the algorithm, providing a high-level understanding of how IMGPO works. The technical details of the algorithm are also clearly presented in Section 3.3, making it easier for readers to implement and replicate the method.

The experimental results in Section 5 are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of IMGPO in various optimization tasks. The comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, such as SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, highlights the strengths of the proposed algorithm.

One of the notable contributions of this paper is that it brings together ideas from the Bayesian global optimization literature that do and do not use continuity estimation. The authors provide a practically oriented analysis framework, enabling readers to understand why not relying on a particular bound is advantageous and how a non-tight bound can still be useful.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a significant effort to provide a strong contribution to the NIPS community. The manuscript is clear, concise, and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel Bayesian optimization method that achieves exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.
* The authors provide a clear and well-organized manuscript, effectively conveying the main contributions of their work.
* The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of IMGPO in various optimization tasks.
* The paper brings together ideas from the Bayesian global optimization literature that do and do not use continuity estimation.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a certain level of familiarity with Bayesian optimization and Gaussian processes, which may make it challenging for non-experts to follow.
* The authors could provide more discussion on the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective Bayesian optimization method that addresses the limitations of existing approaches.
* The experimental results demonstrate the strengths of the proposed algorithm, and the comparison with other state-of-the-art methods is thorough and convincing.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper assumes a certain level of familiarity with Bayesian optimization and Gaussian processes, which may limit its accessibility to a broader audience.
* The authors could provide more discussion on the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research.

Overall, I believe that this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization and is worthy of acceptance. The authors have presented a clear and well-organized manuscript, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.
