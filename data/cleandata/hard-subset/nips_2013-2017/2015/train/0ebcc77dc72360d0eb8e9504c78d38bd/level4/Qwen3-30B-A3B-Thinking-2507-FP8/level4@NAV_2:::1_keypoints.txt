- Introduces IMGPO, a novel GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential regret convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Addresses limitations of prior works (de Freitas et al. 2012; Wang et al. 2013) by removing restrictive sampling/optimization requirements.  
- Places IMGPO within the bound-based search framework and clearly differentiates it from BaMSOO and SOO via an infinite-metric exploration mechanism.  
- Theoretical analysis (Theorem 1) rigorously establishes the exponential regret bound and links GP prior looseness with the unknown semi-metric bound.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured exposition of the algorithm and its theoretical foundations.  
- Strength: effectively situates the problem within Bayesian optimization literature and contrasts related methods.  
- Strength: insightful analysis of the trade-off between bound tightness and exploration (Remarks 1–3).  
- Strength: implementation details are sufficiently precise for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks statistical rigor—no multiple initializations or significance testing across random seeds.  
- Weakness: omits important state-of-the-art baselines, e.g., information-theoretic methods like Entropy Search.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to synthetic functions up to 6D with no real-world parameter tuning or high-dimensional (e.g., 1000D) validations, limiting practical demonstration.  
- Overall: strong theoretical novelty but experimental section fails to convincingly demonstrate practical advantages over existing methods.
