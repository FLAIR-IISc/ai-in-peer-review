The paper presents a new algorithm for global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012) that relies on an impractical sampling procedure.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the role of the new algorithm within the current literature of bound-based search methods is well-explained. In this sense, I think that this work deserves some attention from the community.

However, I have some reservations about the experimental section, which I believe falls short of the standards expected in a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper. My main criticisms are:

* The authors do not compare the methods for multiple initial evaluations of f, as is standard in BO methods. Therefore, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be obtained.
* Although they propose a bound-based search method, the authors should compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches. Information-theoretic approaches, such as Entropy Search, are not used in the experiments.
* All the experiments are carried out on synthetic functions (with a maximum dimension of 6). No real-world experiment, such as parameter tuning in a wet lab, is used to illustrate the performance of the method in real scenarios.

Overall, the paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. The paper is nice, with some interesting theoretical results, but in my opinion, the experimental section is below the standards of a NIPS paper. I vote 5, but not a higher score due to these concerns.
