The paper introduces a novel algorithm for global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, thereby generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012) which relies on a less practical sampling procedure. 

The paper is well-written, clear, and effectively situates the new algorithm within the existing literature on bounded-based search methods. I believe this work warrants attention from the research community. 

I assign a score of 5, but not higher, due to weaknesses in the experimental evaluation. Specifically, my criticisms are as follows:

- The authors do not evaluate the methods across multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, a standard practice in Bayesian optimization. Consequently, a statistically sound comparison of the methods is lacking.
- Despite proposing a bounded-based search method, the authors do not compare their results to state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization approaches. Information-theoretic methods, such as Entropy Search, are absent from the experimental analysis.
- The experiments are limited to synthetic functions with a maximum dimension of 6. No real-world parameter tuning experiments are included to demonstrate performance in practical scenarios.

In summary, the paper presents a new global optimization algorithm with interesting theoretical properties, but the experimental section falls short of the standards typically expected for a NIPS publication.
