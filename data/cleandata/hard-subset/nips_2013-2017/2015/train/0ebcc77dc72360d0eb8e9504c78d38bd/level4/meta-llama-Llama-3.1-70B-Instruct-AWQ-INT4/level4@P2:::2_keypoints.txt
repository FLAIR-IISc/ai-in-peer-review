- Proposes a Bayesian Global Optimization variant using space/interval partitioning with theoretical exponential regret improvements.  
- Method appears sound and novel but evaluations are limited to 1D test problems.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example.  
- Experimental evaluation seems restricted to low-dimensional functions (not explicitly stated), raising scalability concerns.  
- Interval partitioning may require exponentially increasing resolution, causing potential computational issues.  
- Approach is related to DIRECT, which performs poorly in higher dimensions; the paper should discuss this limitation.  
- Minor: clarify whether acquisition functions other than UCB (e.g., Expected Improvement) can be used.  
- Minor: clarify the statement about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor: explain the observation “At n=16, the far right... but no function evaluation occurs.”  
- Overall: novel contribution but needs more discussion of limitations and extensions to higher-dimensional problems.
