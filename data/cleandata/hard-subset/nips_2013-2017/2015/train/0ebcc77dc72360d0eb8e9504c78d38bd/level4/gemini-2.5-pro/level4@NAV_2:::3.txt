This paper presents Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm based on Gaussian Processes (GP). The central contribution is an algorithm that achieves an exponential convergence rate without relying on either auxiliary optimization subroutines or the impractical δ-cover sampling procedure required by previous theoretical work [1]. The authors achieve this by synergistically combining a GP-UCB-style bound with a hierarchical partitioning search strategy. This method implicitly considers an infinite family of potential semi-metrics governing the function's smoothness, using the GP to efficiently prune the search space. This approach effectively unifies two lines of research in global optimization that diverged from the DIRECT algorithm [19]: those that rely on a known bound and those that do not.

**Strengths:**

*   **Significance and Originality:** The work addresses a significant open problem explicitly stated in prior work [2]: how to design a GP-based optimization algorithm with an exponential convergence rate that avoids δ-cover sampling. The proposed solution is both novel and elegant, bridging a conceptual gap between different families of optimization algorithms.
*   **Technical Quality:** The theoretical claims are well-supported. Theorem 1 establishes an exponential regret bound, which is a substantial improvement over the polynomial bounds of related contemporary methods like BaMSOO [2]. The analysis provided in Remarks 1-3 offers valuable and practical insights into how the tightness of the GP bound and the "infinite-metric" exploration contribute to performance.
*   **Clarity and Reproducibility:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The core idea is introduced with a clear, high-level intuition in Section 3.1. The illustrative example and corresponding figure in Section 3.2 are highly effective at explaining the algorithm's mechanics. The detailed pseudocode appears sufficient for others to replicate the work.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing. IMGPO is benchmarked against strong and relevant baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) on a diverse suite of test functions, including a high-dimensional problem. The results consistently demonstrate the superior performance of the proposed method, validating its practical utility.

**Weaknesses / Suggestions for Improvement:**

*   **Scalability Discussion:** The analysis notes that the convergence rate constant λ can approach 1 as dimensionality `D` increases, suggesting potential scalability challenges. While the paper demonstrates a strong result on a 1000-dimensional problem by using IMGPO within the REMBO framework [14], the paper would be strengthened by a more explicit discussion of the algorithm's practical limitations with respect to dimensionality and the conditions under which it is most effective.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The algorithm introduces new parameters, notably `η` and `Ξmax`, and a heuristic update rule for `Ξ`. While the experimental settings are provided, the paper would be more impactful for practitioners if it included a sensitivity analysis or more detailed guidance on setting these parameters.
*   **Algorithm Complexity:** While the pseudocode is detailed, certain steps (e.g., lines 21-23, which involve finding the best UCB in a potential subtree) are conceptually dense. A slightly more detailed explanation of the implementation logic for this screening step could further improve the paper's clarity and ease of implementation for future researchers.

In summary, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the Bayesian optimization literature. It is theoretically sound, empirically validated, and clearly communicated. The proposed IMGPO algorithm is an important step forward for efficient global optimization.
