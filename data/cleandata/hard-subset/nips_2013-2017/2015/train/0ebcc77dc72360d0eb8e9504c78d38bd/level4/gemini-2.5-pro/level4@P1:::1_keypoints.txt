- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Removes the impractical sampling assumption from de Freitas et al. (2012), addressing a noted open problem.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly situates the method within bound-based search literature.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and represents a significant contribution to Bayesian optimization.  
- Experiments lack statistical rigor: results are based on single runs without means or variance/error bars.  
- Missing comparisons to stronger state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., Entropy Search, Predictive Entropy Search).  
- All evaluations are on synthetic benchmarks; no real-world application (e.g., hyperparameter tuning or robotics) is shown.  
- Suggested fixes: run multiple randomized trials with statistical reporting, include top baselines, and add at least one practical application.
