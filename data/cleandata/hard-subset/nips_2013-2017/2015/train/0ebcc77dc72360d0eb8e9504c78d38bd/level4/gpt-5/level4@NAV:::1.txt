The paper introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that circumvents delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, thereby generalizing the work of Freitas et al., 2012, which depends on an impractical sampling scheme.

The manuscript is clearly written and easy to follow, and the positioning of the algorithm within the current literature on bounded-based search methods is well articulated. In this regard, I believe the work merits attention from the community.

I vote 5, but not higher, because the experimental section falls below what one typically expects in a BO paper. My main concerns are:
- The authors do not compare methods under multiple initial evaluations of f, as is standard in BO. Consequently, no meaningful statistical comparison can be drawn.
- Although they propose a bounded-based search method, the authors should compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches. Information-theoretic methods, such as Entropy Search, are absent from the experiments.
- All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions (with a maximum dimension of 6). No real wet-lab or parameter-tuning task is included to demonstrate performance in practical scenarios.

In summary, the paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. It is a solid piece with interesting theoretical contributions, but in my view the experimental section is below the standards of an NIPS paper.
