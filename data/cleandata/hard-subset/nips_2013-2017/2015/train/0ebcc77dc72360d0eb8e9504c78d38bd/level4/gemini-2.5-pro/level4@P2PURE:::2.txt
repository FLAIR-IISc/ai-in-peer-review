This paper introduces a novel variant of Bayesian Global Optimization that generates subsequent samples using a space-partitioning algorithm. By leveraging the Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) criterion, the method avoids the internal optimization problem typically required to select the next evaluation point. A key strength of the paper is its theoretical analysis, which demonstrates an exponential regret bound, improving upon previous results. The algorithm is well-described and effectively illustrated with an example, and the overall presentation is clear.

My main reservation, however, concerns the scalability of the proposed method. The experimental results appear to be conducted exclusively on one-dimensional test functions, although this is not explicitly stated. I suspect that the interval-partitioning strategy will not scale gracefully to higher dimensions. As the dimensionality increases, the number of hyperrectangles required to maintain a given resolution would likely grow exponentially, a limitation often associated with the curse of dimensionality. This issue is known to severely affect the performance of the related DIRECT algorithm. While this potential weakness does not diminish the paper's theoretical contribution, its practical implications are significant. The authors should, at a minimum, address this potential limitation and discuss the scalability of their approach.

Minor Comments:
*   On page 2, the use of UCB is justified "for brevity." This phrasing is ambiguous. Is UCB merely a choice of convenience, or is it fundamental to the algorithm's mechanics and theoretical guarantees? Could other acquisition functions, such as Expected Improvement, be used instead?
*   On page 3, the statement "...we simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds" was unclear. Please clarify the meaning of this sentence.
*   On page 4, in the discussion of the example at n=16, it is noted that for the interval on "the far right...but no function evaluation occurs." For clarity, please briefly explain why an evaluation is not performed at this location.
