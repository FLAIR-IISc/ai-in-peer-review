- Proposes a Bayesian Global Optimization variant that generates future samples via space/interval partitioning.  
- Uses Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) to avoid the internal global optimization step for choosing the next sample.  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing exponential regret, an improvement over prior work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example.  
- Paper makes a novel contribution and is overall well-presented.  
- Major concern: experiments appear to be limited to 1D test functions (not explicitly stated).  
- Scalability worry: interval/hyperrectangle partitioning may not scale to higher dimensions due to exponential resolution growth.  
- Related to DIRECT, which is known to perform poorly as dimensionality increases, exacerbating scalability concerns.  
- Essential to discuss or address practical scalability limitations in the paper.  
- Minor: clarify whether acquisition functions other than UCB (e.g., expected improvement) can be used.  
- Minor: clarify the unclear statement on page 3 about simultaneously conducting global and local searches.  
- Minor: explain the observation on page 4 where at n=16 the far right shows no function evaluation.  
- Overall: addressing scalability and clarifying the mentioned statements would strengthen the paper.
