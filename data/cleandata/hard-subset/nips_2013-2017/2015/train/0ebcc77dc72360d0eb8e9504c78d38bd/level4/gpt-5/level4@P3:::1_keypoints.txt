- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization method combining hierarchical partitioning with GP posteriors to reason over an infinite family of Hölder-type semi-metrics and avoid auxiliary acquisition optimization.
- Theoretical claim: proves exponential simple-regret O(λN+Ngp) (λ < 1) under mild smoothness, improving on BaMSOO and avoiding δ-cover sampling.

- Theory is a nontrivial contribution: establishes exponential convergence, articulates infinite-metric exploration loss ρt and dimensional dependence, with plausible high-level proofs (details in supplement).
- Dimensionality is a concern: λ approaches 1 as D grows, highlighting scalability limits.

- Algorithm is well specified: hierarchical partitioning, UCB-based deferred evaluations, and periodic hyperparameter updates with clear interplay of parameters η, Ξ, Ξmax.
- Empirics favor IMGPO on low-D benchmarks and one REMBO example, but suffer failures when the GP prior is misleading.

- Writing is clear and well positioned among related methods; notation needs streamlining and some symbols/sets should be defined at first use.
- A schematic of accept/reject decisions in the algorithm would improve intuition.

- The infinite-metric idea is novel, bridging GP BO and optimistic partition search, and achieving exponential regret without δ-cover is a notable theoretical advance.
- Analysis clarifies trade-offs between GP calibration, ρ̄t, and Ξn.

- Significance: important theoretical advance for deterministic BO and practical simplification by removing auxiliary optimization, but practical impact is tempered by weak empirical validation.

- Strength: first GP-based BO with exponential convergence in deterministic setting without δ-cover or auxiliary optimization.
- Strength: clear extension and connection to DIRECT, SOO/DOO, GP-UCB, and BaMSOO with insightful discussion on non-tight GP bounds.
- Strength: practical algorithmic design enabling deferred evaluations and accessible implementation.
- Strength: transparent theoretical dependence on smoothness and dimension.

- Weakness: experimental protocol is limited—no multi-starts, no statistical tests, and missing strong baselines like ES/PES.
- Weakness: benchmarks are synthetic (≤6D except REMBO); lacks real-world tuning tasks to demonstrate practical utility.
- Weakness: restricted to deterministic setting; unclear extension to noisy objectives and lacks noisy benchmarks or robustness discussion.
- Weakness: missing sensitivity/ablation studies on Ξmax, η, kernel choice, hyperparameter learning, and deferral cost-accuracy trade-offs.
- Weakness: scalability concerns not fully addressed beyond a single REMBO example; need discussion of mitigations (additive models, embeddings).
- Weakness: notation and presentation need unification and clearer placement of key definitions.

- Arguments for acceptance: strong novel theory, conceptual bridging of methods, and practical algorithm removing auxiliary optimization.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical evaluation by BO standards, deterministic-only focus, and incomplete exploration of scalability and sensitivity.

- Overall: well-written paper with compelling theory and solid algorithmic ideas, but empirical shortcomings warrant stronger experiments before acceptance; borderline recommendation pending revised experiments.
