This paper introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization method designed to circumvent the limitations of existing approaches that rely on either auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling. The core contribution lies in achieving exponential convergence without these requirements, addressing a significant practical challenge in the field.

The paper is well-structured, beginning with a clear motivation and contextualization within the broader landscape of global optimization and Bayesian optimization. The authors effectively relate their work to previous NIPS publications, notably referencing de Freitas et al. [1] and Wang et al. [2], and highlighting the impracticality of δ-cover sampling as identified by Wang et al. The introduction convincingly argues for the need for a method like IMGPO. 

Section 3.2 provides a helpful illustrative example, enhancing the reader's understanding of the algorithm's mechanics. The algorithmic description in Section 3.3 is detailed and clear, offering sufficient information for potential implementation and replication. The inclusion of pseudocode (Algorithm 1) is particularly valuable. The authors also thoughtfully discuss the relationship of their work to existing algorithms like BaMSOO [2] and DIRECT [19], positioning IMGPO within the broader context of bound-based search methods.

The experimental results presented in Section 5 demonstrate promising performance, showcasing IMGPO’s effectiveness across a range of benchmark functions. The authors acknowledge limitations and discuss potential avenues for improvement, such as exploring the impact of the Ξmax parameter and considering scalability to higher dimensions.  They also address the practical consideration of hyperparameter tuning, noting their use of standard approaches when ideal kernel and hyperparameter knowledge is unavailable.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a technically sound algorithm with a theoretical analysis supporting its exponential convergence rate (Theorem 1).
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the authors’ line of reasoning. The illustrative example and pseudocode are particularly helpful.
*   **Originality:** IMGPO represents a novel combination of ideas, effectively addressing the limitations of existing Bayesian optimization methods. The concept of considering “infinitely many bounds” is a creative and insightful approach.
*   **Significance:**  The elimination of both auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling requirements significantly enhances the practicality of Bayesian optimization. The potential for broader application and building upon this work is substantial.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Scalability:** While promising, the analysis acknowledges limitations in scalability to very high dimensions, indicating a potential area for future research.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance of the algorithm, as with many GP-based methods, may be sensitive to hyperparameter settings. More extensive exploration of hyperparameter tuning strategies could be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant practical limitation in Bayesian optimization.
*   The theoretical analysis provides strong justification for the algorithm’s effectiveness.
*   The experimental results demonstrate promising performance.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.
*   The approach is novel and represents a valuable contribution to the field.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The scalability limitations may restrict the applicability of the method to certain problem domains.
*   The sensitivity to hyperparameters, while acknowledged, could require significant effort for practical application.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The originality of the approach, combined with the clear presentation and solid theoretical foundation, makes it highly deserving of acceptance to NIPS. While the scalability limitations are worth noting, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality and potential impact of the work.  I recommend acceptance with enthusiasm.
