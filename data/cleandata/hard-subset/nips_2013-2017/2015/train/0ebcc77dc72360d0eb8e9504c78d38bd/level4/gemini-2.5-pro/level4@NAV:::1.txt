This paper introduces a novel global optimization algorithm that achieves exponential regret without resorting to delta-cover sampling. The proposed method serves as a generalization of the work by Freitas et al. (2012), notably improving upon their reliance on an impractical sampling scheme.

The manuscript is clearly written and accessible. The authors do an excellent job of situating their algorithm within the broader context of bounded-based search methods. For these reasons, I believe the work is a noteworthy contribution that warrants the community's attention.

I am recommending a score of 5; I cannot justify a higher rating as the experimental validation is not on par with the standards expected for a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper. My primary concerns with this section are as follows:

*   The authors fail to perform comparisons over multiple initial evaluations of the function *f*, which is standard practice in the BO literature. This omission prevents any meaningful statistical analysis of the competing methods' performance.
*   Despite positioning the work as a bounded-based search method, the empirical comparison neglects to include state-of-the-art BO baselines. Specifically, prominent information-theoretic approaches, such as Entropy Search, are absent from the experiments.
*   The empirical evaluation is confined to low-dimensional (up to 6D) synthetic benchmarks. The paper would be significantly strengthened by demonstrating the algorithm's effectiveness on a real-world task, such as a parameter tuning or wet-lab experiment, to validate its performance in practical scenarios.

In summary, this paper introduces a new global optimization algorithm with compelling theoretical properties. While the theoretical contributions are interesting, the experimental section, in its current form, does not meet the rigorous standards expected of a NeurIPS paper.
