This paper presents a new algorithm for global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret bounds, thereby generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012), which relies on an impractical sampling procedure. The paper is well written and easy to follow, and the role of the new algorithm within the literature on bounds-based search methods is clearly explained. In this sense, I believe the work deserves attention from the community.

I recommend a score of 5, but not higher, because the experimental section falls below the standards expected in a Bayesian optimization (BO) paper. My main criticisms are:

- The authors do not compare methods across multiple initial evaluations of f, as is standard in BO. Without repeated runs from different initializations, no meaningful statistical comparison can be made.

- Although they propose a bounds-based search method, the authors should compare their results against state-of-the-art BO approaches. Information-theoretic methods, such as Entropy Search, are not included in the experiments.

- All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions (with maximum dimension 6). No real wet-lab or hyperparameter-tuning experiment is provided to demonstrate performance in realistic scenarios.

In summary, the paper introduces a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret bounds. The theoretical results are interesting and the presentation is clear, but in my view the experimental section is below the standards for a NIPS paper.
