- Proposes a variant of Bayesian Global Optimization using space/interval partitioning to generate future samples and avoid internal global optimization, guided by UCB.  
- Strong theoretical analysis showing the method achieves exponential regret, improving on prior work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example, making it easy to follow.  
- Novel contribution with clear and well-organized presentation.  
- Major concern: experiments appear limited to 1D (not stated), raising significant scalability concerns for higher dimensions.  
- Interval-partitioning approach likely requires exponentially increasing resolution in higher dimensions, suggesting poor scalability.  
- Relation to the DIRECT algorithm (known to perform poorly in high dimensions) exacerbates the scalability concern.  
- Recommendation: authors should explicitly acknowledge and discuss scalability implications and provide more comprehensive evaluations.  
- Minor: clarify whether other acquisition functions (e.g., Expected Improvement) can replace UCB.  
- Minor: clarify the statement about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor: explain why at n=16 the far right interval is divided without a function evaluation.  
- Overall: sound and novel theoretical contribution, but practical applicability depends on addressing scalability and broader empirical evaluation.
