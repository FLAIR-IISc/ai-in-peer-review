- Proposes a Bayesian optimization method with exponential convergence that removes the need for auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling.  
- Leverages an unknown semi-metric encoding continuity at the global optimizer and jointly considers multiple candidate bounds using a Gaussian process.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly presented, with a helpful illustrative example and programmatic description aiding implementation.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: provides analysis demonstrating exponential convergence.  
- Eliminates key practical bottlenecks (δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization), improving applicability.  
- Empirical results are promising and validate the method versus existing approaches.  
- Effectively positions and synthesizes the work within prior continuity- and non-continuity-based Bayesian optimization literature.  
- Scalability to high-dimensional problems is a concern and requires further research.  
- Potential parameter sensitivity (e.g., Ξmax) necessitates careful tuning; deeper analysis of this sensitivity is lacking.  
- Relies on an unknown semi-metric assumption; the effects of violating this assumption are not fully explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a practical problem, strong theory, clear algorithm, promising experiments, and notable contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about high-dimensional scalability and parameter sensitivity.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance despite noted scalability limitations.
