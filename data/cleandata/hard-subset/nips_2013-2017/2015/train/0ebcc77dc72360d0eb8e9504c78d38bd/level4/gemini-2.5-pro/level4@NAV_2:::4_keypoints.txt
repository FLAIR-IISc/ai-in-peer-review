- Main contribution: IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm that attains exponential convergence while avoiding auxiliary non-convex optimization and δ-cover sampling.
- Key idea: combines hierarchical partitioning (SOO-style) with GP-UCB probabilistic bounds to implicitly handle an infinite set of possible smoothness semi-metrics and prune the search tree.
- Theoretical result: rigorous analysis culminating in Theorem 1 proving exponential regret bounds for the best unknown smoothness parameters (L, α, p).
- Novelty/significance: first GP-based method achieving exponential convergence without impractical assumptions or costly subroutines, addressing an open problem from prior work.
- Technical quality: formal, rigorous proofs and a general analysis framework (Remarks 1–3) clarifying trade-offs between known vs unknown bounds.
- Empirical evaluation: thorough comparisons on standard benchmarks (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI/EI) showing consistent sample-efficiency gains for IMGPO.
- Clarity and reproducibility: well-written, well-organized exposition with step-by-step algorithm description, pseudocode, and an illustrative figure.
- Practical concern 1: Ξ_max is fixed in experiments; sensitivity analysis or a heuristic for setting it would improve practical guidance.
- Practical concern 2: lack of an ablation study isolating the GP-based pruning (disable lines 19–24) to empirically validate its contribution.
- Minor issue: small proofreading and tightening of abstract phrasing could improve polish.
- Argument for acceptance: solves an important open problem, offers strong theoretical guarantees, removes impractical assumptions, and shows strong empirical performance.
- Argument against acceptance: scalability to very high-dimensional problems remains limited (noted by authors), though this is a common limitation and mitigable via hybrid approaches.
