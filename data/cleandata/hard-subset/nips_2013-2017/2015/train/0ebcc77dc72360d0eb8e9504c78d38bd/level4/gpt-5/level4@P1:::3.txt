This paper proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization algorithm that avoids both auxiliary nonconvex optimization and the δ-cover sampling procedure. The method leverages a Gaussian process prior while not committing to a single, potentially loose bound; instead, it implicitly reasons over infinitely many candidate bounds via a hierarchical partitioning strategy. The paper positions IMGPO relative to GP-UCB and SOO/BaMSOO, aiming to retain the practical simplicity of GP-based Bayesian optimization while achieving fast convergence without δ-cover sampling.

The paper is clearly organized and easy to follow. The main contributions and algorithmic choices are well-motivated in the introduction and related sections. Section 3.2 provides an intuitive, step-by-step illustration that helps the reader quickly grasp the core idea, and Section 3.3 translates the method into a precise, implementation-ready pseudocode. The empirical section demonstrates strong performance across a range of benchmark functions, and the discussion highlights how the approach draws together ideas from the Bayesian optimization literature that do and do not rely on explicit continuity assumptions.

Quality:
- The algorithmic description is precise, and the pseudocode in Section 3.3 supports reproducibility. The procedural breakdown clarifies the roles of the global/local search components and the use of GP-derived UCBs to screen candidates.
- The experimental results are compelling and suggest that IMGPO is competitive or superior to strong baselines in several settings. The choice to fix a popular kernel (Matern 5/2) and update hyperparameters empirically reflects realistic use.
- The analysis is conceptually informative, explaining how leveraging multiple implicit bounds can be preferable to relying on a single estimated bound. The theoretical framing connects well with prior bound-based search paradigms.

Clarity:
- The paper is well-written overall, with a logical progression from motivation to method, theory, and experiments. The worked 1D example in Section 3.2 substantially aids understanding.
- The presentation of the algorithmic steps is careful and programmatic, which should facilitate independent implementation and replication.

Originality:
- The combination of GP modeling with an “infinite-metric” perspective—eschewing dependence on a single continuity bound while still exploiting GP-derived UCBs—is a noteworthy and novel contribution in the Bayesian optimization literature.
- The positioning relative to SOO/DIRECT-style partitioning and GP-UCB-style optimism is clearly articulated, and the paper emphasizes that IMGPO eliminates δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization.

Significance:
- Avoiding auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling addresses practical pain points for Bayesian optimization in real-world settings.
- The empirical results indicate strong performance, and the analysis offers useful insights into when and why not committing to a single bound can be advantageous. This perspective is likely to be of interest to both theory and practice communities at NIPS.

Strengths:
- Clear exposition and organization.
- Intuitive illustrative example (Section 3.2) and detailed pseudocode (Section 3.3).
- Promising experimental results across diverse benchmarks.
- Conceptual integration of BO approaches with and without explicit continuity estimation.
- Practical relevance: no auxiliary optimization, no δ-cover sampling.

Overall, the paper is clearly written and provides a strong contribution to the NIPS community by proposing a practically motivated Bayesian optimization method that is easy to implement, empirically effective, and conceptually unifies strands of prior work.
