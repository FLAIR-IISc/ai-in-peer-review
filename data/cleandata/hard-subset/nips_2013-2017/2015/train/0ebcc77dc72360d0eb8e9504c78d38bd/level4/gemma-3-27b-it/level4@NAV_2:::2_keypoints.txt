- Proposes a novel Bayesian optimization method achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ‑cover sampling.  
- Core algorithm couples Gaussian processes with an infinite‑metric approach and hierarchical interval partitioning to select samples.  
- Avoids an internal optimization loop for choosing the next evaluation point.  
- Theoretical analysis proves an exponential regret bound, improving over prior work (e.g., BaMSOO).  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly explained, and supported by an illustrative example.  
- Authors situate the work appropriately among GP-based BO methods and compare to BaMSOO and DIRECT.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to relatively low-dimensional benchmarks (Sin1, Sin2, Branin, Hartmann3, etc.).  
- Scalability concern: interval partitioning (similar to DIRECT) may suffer from the curse of dimensionality, requiring exponential resolution growth.  
- Practical applicability is questionable without discussion or mitigation of high‑dimensional performance.  
- Minor: justification for focusing on UCB acquisition should be clarified and possible applicability to other acquisitions (e.g., EI) discussed.  
- Minor: statement about simultaneous global and local searches based on all bound candidates is unclear and needs elaboration.  
- Minor: clarify why no function evaluation occurs at n = 16 in the example/experiment.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong theoretical guarantees, clear presentation, and practical elimination of δ‑cover/auxiliary optimization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns and limited empirical validation in high‑dimensional, realistic settings.
