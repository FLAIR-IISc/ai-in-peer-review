- Introduces IMGPO: combines hierarchical partitioning with a GP to achieve global optimization without auxiliary acquisition optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proves an exponential simple-regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)), improving over polynomial bounds like BaMSOO.  
- Novelty: integrates infinite-metric/DIRECT-SOO style partitioning with GP-UCB to avoid needing a known smoothness constant.  
- Presentation: paper is clear, with a helpful 1D illustrative example and complete pseudocode.  
- Practical advantage: avoids inner non-convex optimization of the acquisition function, simplifying implementation and computation.  
- Primary weakness: scalability to high dimensions is limited by hierarchical partitioning (curse of dimensionality).  
- Experiments insufficiently address scalability—the 1000D test uses REMBO embedding and does not demonstrate IMGPO’s standalone high-D performance.  
- Recommendation to authors: include explicit discussion of practical dimensionality limits and when IMGPO is appropriate.  
- Question on UCB: clarify whether the exponential-convergence proof requires UCB’s high-probability upper bound or extends to other acquisition functions (e.g., EI).  
- Clarify the “infinite candidates” claim by explaining how partitioning implicitly explores the best semi-metric from Assumption 1.  
- Clarify Figure 1: explicitly state why some candidate centers (red asterisks at n=16) are not evaluated (UCB < current best f+).  
- Overall recommendation: accept for a strong theoretical and methodological contribution, with suggestions to better address dimensionality limitations.
