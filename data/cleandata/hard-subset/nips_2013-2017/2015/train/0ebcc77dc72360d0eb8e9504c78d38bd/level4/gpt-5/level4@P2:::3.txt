This paper introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization algorithm that attains exponential convergence without relying on auxiliary optimization of acquisition functions or the impractical δ-cover sampling procedure. The method leverages a GP prior together with a hierarchical partitioning scheme and a principled use of UCBs to balance global and local search, while implicitly considering an unknown continuity-based bound at the optimizer. The approach builds on and contrasts with GP-UCB and BaMSOO, aiming to keep the benefits of GP-based guidance while avoiding computational bottlenecks and unrealistic sampling assumptions.

Strengths:
- Clarity and organization: The paper is clearly written and well structured. The main contributions are stated prominently and connected to prior work in Bayesian optimization and optimistic optimization.
- Exposition and reproducibility: The 1D illustrative walkthrough in Section 3.2 provides an intuitive, high-level understanding of how the algorithm explores and refines intervals. Section 3.3 complements this with precise, programmatic details and pseudocode, making implementation and replication straightforward.
- Experimental evidence: The empirical results are compelling across a range of benchmarks, showing practical gains and supporting the theoretical claims. The setup avoids hand-tuned kernels, which strengthens the case for robustness.
- Positioning within the literature: The paper thoughtfully bridges strands of work that do and do not assume known smoothness or Lipschitz constants, and explains how IMGPO exploits GP-based information without incurring auxiliary optimization.

Evaluation:
- Quality: The technical development appears sound, with a clear path from assumptions to an exponential regret bound and a careful discussion of how GP guidance and infinite-metric exploration interact.
- Clarity: Explanations, figures, and pseudocode are effective; the writing is accessible to the NIPS audience.
- Originality: Eliminating both auxiliary optimization and δ-cover sampling while achieving exponential convergence is a notable contribution that advances the state of the art relative to GP-UCB and BaMSOO.
- Significance: The results are likely to be of interest to researchers and practitioners using GP-based Bayesian optimization, especially in settings where acquisition optimization is costly or unreliable.

Overall, this is a well-presented, substantive contribution. The algorithm is clearly described, empirically validated, and meaningfully advances Bayesian global optimization by delivering exponential convergence without auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling. I recommend acceptance.
