- Proposes IMGPO: a GP-based Bayesian optimization combining hierarchical partitioning and GP UCBs to achieve exponential simple-regret for deterministic black-box functions without auxiliary global optimization or δ-cover sampling.  
- Key conceptual novelty: “infinite-metric” viewpoint — hedge over an infinite family of bounds induced by an unknown semi-metric and use GP posteriors to discard loose candidates.  
- Situates work relative to DIRECT, SOO/DOO, and BaMSOO; improves over BaMSOO by achieving exponential rather than polynomial regret.  
- Theoretical contribution: careful regret analysis separating GP posterior’s role (reducing exploration loss, Ngp term) from partitioning (interval refinement), with explicit dependence on dimension and Hölder parameters.  
- Introduces infinite-metric exploration loss to quantify cost of not committing to a single bound and proves O(λN+Ngp) regret with λ < 1 under mild continuity assumptions.  
- Algorithmic clarity: pseudocode and illustrative 1D figure; roles of steps (i)–(v) and GP placeholders are explained.  
- Empirics are focused on low-dimensional benchmarks; dimensionalities in text/captions need clarification and moderate-D results are lacking.  
- Practical concern: hyperrectangular partitioning likely scales poorly with dimension; need discussion/experiments on D ≥ 5–10 or remedies like REMBO/additive structure.  
- Clarify whether UCB is essential: paper restricts to UCB “for brevity” but must state if UCB is required for proofs or if EI/PI could be used.  
- Clarify Figure walkthrough: explicitly state skipped evaluations (e.g., at n=16) when UCB at new center is below current best and UCB is used as placeholder.  
- Provide guidance on tuning parameters (η, Ξmax) and sensitivity; explain how to set Ξmax relative to dimension and budget.  
- Discuss kernel/hyperparameter handling: empirical Bayes instability early on, safeguards used (priors/regularization), and extension to noisy observations.  
- Add complexity discussion: per-iteration cost for GP updates, number of UCB evaluations, and how Ξ controls computational cost.  
- Strengths: eliminates auxiliary acquisition optimization, strong theoretical guarantees (exponential regret), novel conceptual tools, and available code for reproducibility.  
- Weaknesses: unclear empirical scope, scalability concerns in higher dimensions, and some exposition gaps (role of UCB, skipped evaluations, infinite-metric intuition).  
- Suggestions: clarify dimensionalities in experiments, add moderate-D tests or discuss scalability remedies, improve Sections 3.1–3.2 wording, and state where UCB is required.  
- Minor edits: clarify “for brevity we consider UCB,” rephrase global/local search sentence, and explain n=16 event in Figure walkthrough.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance (exponential regret without δ-cover), clear algorithm design, and conceptual insights on GP bounds.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited/high-dimensional empirical validation and practical scaling not fully addressed.
