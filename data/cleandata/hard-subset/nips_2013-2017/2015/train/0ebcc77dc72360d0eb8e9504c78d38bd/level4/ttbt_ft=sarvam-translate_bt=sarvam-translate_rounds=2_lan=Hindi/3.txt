This paper describes a method of Bayesian global optimization that does not require de-covering sampling or auxiliary optimization.

The paper is structured in such a way that the main contributions of the paper are well-described in the main section.

The authors provide a good example of the algorithm in Section 3.2, which gives the reader a good high-level understanding.

Section 3.3 also clearly conveys the functioning of the algorithm for future implementations/iterations of the program.

The experimental results also prove to be quite exciting.

Furthermore, the authors state that this paper brings together ideas from the Bayesian global optimization literature that use and do not use stability estimates.

This paper describes infinite-metric GP optimization (IMGOPO), which is an algorithm for Bayesian optimization without the need for auxiliary optimization or de-covering sampling. This paper is not only clearly written but also makes a strong contribution to the NIPS community.
