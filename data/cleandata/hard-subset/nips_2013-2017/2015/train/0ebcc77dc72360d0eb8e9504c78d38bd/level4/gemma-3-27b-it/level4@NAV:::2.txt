This paper introduces a novel Bayesian Global Optimization method that utilizes a space/interval partitioning strategy to determine future samples, circumventing the need for an internal global optimization step typically found when using Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) acquisition functions. The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating improved performance with exponential regret bounds, exceeding that of existing methods. The algorithm is well-defined and effectively illustrated with a clear example. Overall, the work presents a valuable and clearly written contribution to the field.

However, I have a concern regarding the practical scalability of the proposed approach. The experimental evaluation appears limited to one-dimensional test functions (though this is not explicitly stated), and I anticipate that the interval partitioning strategy may not scale favorably to higher dimensional problems. The resolution required for effective partitioning would likely grow exponentially with dimensionality, potentially mirroring the limitations observed in related algorithms such as DIRECT. While the theoretical results remain valid, a discussion of this potential scaling issue is crucial for assessing the practical applicability of the method.

Several minor points require clarification: On page 2, the justification for using UCB “for brevity” is unclear. Could the framework accommodate other acquisition functions, such as Expected Improvement? On page 3, the statement “...we simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds” lacks clarity. Finally, on page 4, the explanation for the lack of function evaluation at n=16 could benefit from further detail.

In summary, the paper presents a theoretically sound and novel algorithm, but lacks discussion of its scalability to higher dimensions, and contains some minor points requiring clarification.
