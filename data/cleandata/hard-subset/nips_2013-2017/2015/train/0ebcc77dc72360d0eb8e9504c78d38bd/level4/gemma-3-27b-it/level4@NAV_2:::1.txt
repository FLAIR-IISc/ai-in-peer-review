The paper presents a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without requiring auxiliary optimization or δ-cover sampling, building upon the work of de Freitas et al. [1] which suffered from the impracticality of the latter. 

The paper is clearly written and well-organized, effectively contextualizing the proposed approach within the broader landscape of bound-based search methods. The authors clearly articulate the motivation and contribution of their work, and the technical details are presented in a comprehensible manner. This work presents a valuable addition to the field and warrants attention from the research community.

I recommend a score of 5, but not higher, due to limitations in the experimental evaluation. My primary concerns regarding the experimental section are:

*   The authors do not evaluate the methods across multiple random initial evaluations of the objective function, a standard practice in Bayesian optimization. This hinders a statistically rigorous comparison between the proposed method and existing approaches.
*   Despite presenting a bound-based search method, the study lacks comparison with state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization techniques. Information-theoretic approaches, such as Entropy Search, are not included in the experimental analysis.
*   The evaluation is limited to synthetic benchmark functions, with a maximum dimensionality of 6. The absence of real-world parameter tuning experiments limits the demonstration of the algorithm’s performance in practical scenarios.

In summary, this paper introduces a new global optimization algorithm with strong theoretical guarantees. However, the experimental validation falls short of the standards typically expected for a NIPS publication.
