- Introduces IMGPO: a Bayesian optimization method combining GP-UCB with hierarchical partitioning to achieve exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.
- Addresses the difficulty of tight GP bounds by leveraging an unknown semi-metric encoding continuity at the global optimizer.
- Resolves an open problem from prior work by achieving exponential convergence without computationally demanding δ-cover sampling.
- Theoretical contribution: provides a rigorous analysis and Theorem 1 proving exponential convergence under stated assumptions.
- Novelty: uses an infinite set of potential bounds, connecting ideas from Lipschitz optimization and A* search in a new way.
- Empirical validation: strong performance on benchmark functions, generally outperforming SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.
- Clarity and presentation: paper is exceptionally well-written, with helpful Figure 1 and clear pseudocode (Algorithm 1).
- Insightful discussion: Remarks 1–3 and the discussion section clarify component impacts and potential scalability improvements.
- Scalability concern: potential limitations as input dimensionality increases, requiring further assumptions or methods to scale.
- Parameter sensitivity: performance appears somewhat sensitive to the choice of Ξmax, needing more investigation into robustness and tuning.
- Minor technical issues: redundant formulation at line 49 and a misplaced parenthesis at line 72 should be corrected.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant advancement, strong theoretical justification, compelling experiments, and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: practical applicability may be limited by scalability; technical fixes required.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions to fix technical issues and discuss scalability strategies.
