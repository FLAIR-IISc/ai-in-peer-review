- Proposes a variant of Bayesian Global Optimization using space/interval partitioning to generate future samples.
- Avoids internal global optimization for next-sample selection by using Upper Confidence Bound (UCB).
- Theoretical analysis shows the method achieves exponential regret, claimed to improve on prior work.
- Algorithm is clearly described, explained, and illustrated with an example; considered a novel contribution and well-presented.
- Major concern: experiments appear limited to 1D functions (not explicitly stated), raising scalability questions.
- Interval partitioning likely does not scale to higher dimensions due to exponential growth in required resolution.
- Relation to DIRECT suggests the approach may suffer poor performance in higher dimensions; this limitation must be addressed.
- Theoretical contribution remains valuable, but practical applicability is significantly affected and should be discussed.
- Minor: clarify whether UCB is used “for brevity” or if alternatives (e.g., expected improvement) are possible.
- Minor: clarify the meaning of “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”
- Minor: explain the statement on page 4 about “At n=16, the far right... but no function evaluation occurs.”
