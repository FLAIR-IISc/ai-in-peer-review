- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence for simple regret without δ‑cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- Core idea: combine GP‑UCB bounds with hierarchical partitioning and an infinite family of semi‑metric bounds to balance exploration and exploitation.  
- Theorem 1 provides a formal proof of exponential regret convergence, improving on polynomial rates of practical alternatives.  
- Technical analysis (Remarks 1–3) clarifies how GP bounds and infinite‑metric search jointly yield strong performance.  
- Empirical results show IMGPO consistently outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP‑PI, and GP‑EI on diverse benchmarks up to 1000 dimensions.  
- Experiments used a standard kernel and blind hyperparameter initialization, demonstrating practical robustness.  
- Significance/originality: addresses an important open problem by synthesizing hierarchical search with GP bounds in a novel way.  
- Clarity/presentation: paper is well‑written, well‑organized, and aided by a clear algorithm description and helpful figures.  
- Minor writing issues: abstract contains some redundancy and could be more concise.  
- Minor editing: recommend thorough proofreading to remove typos and grammatical issues.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with solid theory and empirical validation; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
