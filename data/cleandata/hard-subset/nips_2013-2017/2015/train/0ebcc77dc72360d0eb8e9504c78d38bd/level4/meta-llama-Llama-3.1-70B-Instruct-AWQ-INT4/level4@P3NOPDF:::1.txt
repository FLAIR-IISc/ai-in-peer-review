The paper presents a novel algorithm for global optimization that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, thereby generalizing the work of Freitas et al. (2012) that relies on an impractical sampling procedure. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the authors effectively explain the role of their new algorithm within the current literature on bounded-based search methods. This work deserves attention from the community due to its theoretical contributions.

I assign a score of 5, but I do not give a higher score because the experimental section falls short of the standards typically expected in a Bayesian optimization (BO) paper. My primary criticisms of this section are as follows:

- The authors do not compare the methods for multiple initial evaluations of the objective function f, which is a standard practice in BO methods. Consequently, no meaningful statistical comparison of the methods can be obtained.
- Although the authors propose a bounded-based search method, they should compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches. Notably, information-theoretic approaches, such as Entropy Search, are not used in the experiments.
- All the experiments are carried out on synthetic functions with a maximum dimension of 6. The authors do not use real-world experiments, such as wetlab or parameter tuning experiments, to illustrate the performance of the method in practical scenarios.

In summary, the paper presents a new global optimization algorithm with some interesting theoretical results. However, in my opinion, the experimental section does not meet the standards of a NIPS paper.
