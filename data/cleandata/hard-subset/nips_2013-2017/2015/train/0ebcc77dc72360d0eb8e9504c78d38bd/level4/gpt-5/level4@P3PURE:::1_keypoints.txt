- Introduces a new global optimization algorithm that avoids delta-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret.  
- Generalizes Freitas et al. (2012) by removing reliance on an impractical sampling procedure.  
- Paper is well written, easy to follow, and clearly positions the algorithm within bound-based search literature.  
- Theoretical results are interesting and the work merits community attention.  
- Reviewer recommends a score of 5.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks multiple random initial evaluations of f, preventing meaningful statistical comparisons.  
- No comparison with state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods (e.g., information-theoretic methods like Entropy Search).  
- Experiments are limited to synthetic functions (max dimension 6) with no real-world or wet-lab/parameter-tuning case studies.  
- Overall: promising theory but experimental section is below the standard expected for a NIPS paper.
