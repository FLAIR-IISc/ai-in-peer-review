- Introduces a novel Bayesian global optimization variant using space partitioning to select future samples without internal global optimization.  
- Leverages UCB to obtain exponential regret bounds, representing a clear theoretical improvement over prior work.  
- Algorithm is well-described and accompanied by a helpful illustrative example; contribution is novel and clearly presented.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to 1D test functions and this limitation is not explicitly acknowledged.  
- Space partitioning approach appears inherently constrained by the curse of dimensionality—resolution scales exponentially with dimensionality.  
- Method resembles DIRECT and thus likely suffers severely in higher dimensions, a critical practical limitation.  
- Paper should explicitly address scalability and applicability beyond 1D, even if theory remains unaffected.  
- Minor: p.2—referencing UCB “for brevity” needs justification; alternatives (e.g., expected improvement) should be considered.  
- Minor: p.3—the phrase “we simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds” is unclear.  
- Minor: p.4—the note that “no function evaluation occurs” at n=16 requires brief clarification.  
- Overall: theoretical contribution is sound and significant, but lack of higher-dimensional experiments and discussion of scalability is a notable gap; work is promising but should explicitly acknowledge dimensional limitations.
