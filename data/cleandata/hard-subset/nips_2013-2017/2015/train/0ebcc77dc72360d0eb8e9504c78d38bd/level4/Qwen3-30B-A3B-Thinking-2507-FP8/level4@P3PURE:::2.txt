

The paper introduces a novel variant of Bayesian Global Optimization that generates future samples via space/interval partitioning, avoiding the need for internal global optimization to select the next sample by leveraging UCB. Theoretical analysis demonstrates exponential regret bounds, representing a clear improvement over prior work. The algorithm is well-described, with a clear exposition and illustrative example. The contribution is novel and the paper is generally well-structured.

However, a significant reservation arises regarding the experimental evaluation. The results are limited to 1D test functions (not explicitly stated in the paper), and the authors should address the scalability of the interval partitioning approach to higher dimensions. Given that the resolution would require exponential growth with dimensionality, the method likely suffers from the same dimensionality limitations as the DIRECT algorithm, which is known to degrade rapidly in higher dimensions. This concern is critical for practical applicability, even if it does not impact the theoretical contribution.

Minor comments:
- Page 2: The statement that UCB is used "for brevity" requires justification. Why not consider alternative acquisition functions (e.g., expected improvement)?
- Page 3: The phrase "we simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds" is ambiguous and needs clarification.
- Page 4: The claim that "no function evaluation occurs at n=16" requires explanation, as it contradicts the described evaluation process. 

The work is theoretically sound and novel, but the lack of higher-dimensional experiments and discussion of scalability represents a notable gap for practical deployment.
