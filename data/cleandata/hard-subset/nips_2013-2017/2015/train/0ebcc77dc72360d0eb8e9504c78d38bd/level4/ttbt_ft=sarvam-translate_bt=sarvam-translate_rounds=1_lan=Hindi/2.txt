This paper presents a type of Bayesian global optimization where future samples are generated through a space/interval partitioning algorithm.

Using UCB, it avoids the (internal) global optimization problem of selecting the next sample.

Theoretical analysis shows that this approach gives theoretical improvement (exponential regress), which improves previous work.

The algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example.

This paper makes a new contribution and is presented clearly overall.

I have a reservation with this paper.

The experimental results seem to be only for 1D test functions (not explicitly stated).

I suspect that the interval partitioning approach does not scale well for higher dimensions (hyper rectangles) because the "resolution" would need to increase rapidly.

This approach is related to the famous DIRECT algorithm, which is severely affected as the dimensionality of the problem increases.

I think the paper needs to say at least something about this.

It does not change the theoretical contribution but is clearly important for any practical purpose.
