- Proposal: IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without auxiliary acquisition optimization or δ-cover sampling; proves O(λN+Ngp) regret and compares empirically to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI.
- Relation to prior work: Resolves a limitation of de Freitas et al. (δ-cover) and complements BaMSOO by attaining exponential rather than polynomial rates.
- Key idea: Uses GP posteriors to prune an infinite family of implicit bounds from local continuity around the unknown optimum.
- Theoretical strength: Exponential convergence guarantee without δ-cover or inner acquisition optimization; analysis clarifies role of GP tightness and “infinite-metric exploration loss.”
- Empirical strength: Benchmarks generally favor IMGPO, supporting theoretical claims.
- Algorithmic advantage: Avoids costly inner/global optimization of acquisition functions typical in GP-UCB-style methods.
- Presentation: Clear, didactic exposition with helpful figures, pseudocode, and intuitive description.
- Quality: Technical development appears sound; probability guarantees and regret analysis are coherent, though proofs are in the supplement.
- Originality: Novel fusion of GP posteriors with infinite-metric pruning relative to GP-UCB, DIRECT, SOO, and BaMSOO.
- Significance: Potentially practically useful for deterministic black-box optimization where acquisition optimization is costly or brittle.
- Weakness: Some dependence of constants on dimensionality (λ→1 as D increases), indicating scalability challenges without additional structure.
- Weakness: Limited discussion on robustness to kernel misspecification and guidance for choosing Ξmax or handling adverse GP priors.
- Weakness: Proof details and tighter intuition/constants largely relegated to the supplement.
- Minor presentation issues: Tighten repetitive abstract phrasing; rephrase awkward sentence around line 49; fix misplaced parenthesis around line 72.
