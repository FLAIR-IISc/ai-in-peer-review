The paper presents a novel algorithm for global optimization that avoids δ-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret, extending the work of de Freitas et al. (2012) by removing its reliance on an impractical sampling procedure.

The paper is well-written and clear, making it easy to follow the methodology and understand its position within the existing literature on bound-based search methods. This work is a valuable contribution and merits attention from the research community.

I rate this paper a 5, but not higher, due to concerns about the experimental section, which falls short of the standard expected in a Bayesian Optimization (BO) paper. My primary criticisms are:

*   The authors do not compare the methods across multiple initial evaluations of the objective function, a standard practice in BO research. Consequently, meaningful statistical comparisons between the methods are lacking.
*   Despite proposing a bound-based search method, the authors do not compare their results with state-of-the-art BO approaches. Information-theoretic methods, such as Entropy Search, are not included in the experimental evaluation.
*   All experiments are conducted on synthetic functions (with a maximum dimension of 6). No real-world parameter tuning experiments are presented to demonstrate the algorithm's performance in practical scenarios.

The paper presents a new global optimization algorithm that avoids δ-cover sampling and achieves exponential regret. It is a strong paper with interesting theoretical results, but in my opinion, the experimental section does not meet the standards typically expected of a NIPS publication.



References:
