The paper presents a variant of Bayesian Global Optimization, utilizing a space/interval partitioning algorithm to generate future samples. This approach avoids the internal global optimization problem of choosing the next sample by employing Upper Confidence Bound (UCB). The theoretical analysis demonstrates exponential regret, thereby improving upon previous work. The algorithm is clearly described, explained, and illustrated with an example, making a novel contribution to the field.

The paper is well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of their approach. However, I have one major reservation regarding the experimental results. It appears that the results are only presented for 1D test functions, which is not explicitly stated. I suspect that the interval partitioning approach may not scale well to higher dimensions, as the "resolution" would require exponential growth. This concern is supported by the fact that the approach is related to the well-known DIRECT algorithm, which is known to suffer from poor performance as the dimensionality of the problem increases.

I believe it is essential to address this limitation in the paper, as it significantly impacts the practical applicability of the approach. Although it does not affect the theoretical contribution, it is crucial to discuss the potential scalability issues.

Additionally, I have a few minor comments. On page 2, the authors mention that UCB is considered "for brevity." I would like to know if alternative acquisition functions, such as expected improvement, could be used instead. The statement on page 3, "...we simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds," is unclear and requires further explanation. Finally, on page 4, it would be helpful to clarify why "no function evaluation occurs" at n=16.

Overall, the paper presents a sound and novel approach to Bayesian Global Optimization, but its evaluation is limited to 1D test problems. Addressing the potential scalability issues and providing further clarification on the mentioned points would strengthen the paper.
