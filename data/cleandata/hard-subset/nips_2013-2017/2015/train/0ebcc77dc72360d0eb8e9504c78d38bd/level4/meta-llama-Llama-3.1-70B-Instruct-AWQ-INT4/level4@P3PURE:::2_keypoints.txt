- Proposes a Bayesian Global Optimization variant that generates future samples via space/interval partitioning.  
- Leverages the UCB acquisition to avoid solving an internal global optimization for the next sample.  
- Provides theoretical analysis proving exponential regret, improving on prior results.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example, representing a novel contribution.  
- Experiments appear to be limited to 1D test functions, but this limitation is not explicitly stated.  
- Major scalability concern: interval partitioning likely does not scale to higher dimensions due to exponential growth in required resolution (hyperrectangles).  
- Method’s relation to DIRECT suggests it may suffer poor performance in higher dimensions and this practical limitation should be addressed.  
- Minor: clarify whether alternative acquisition functions (e.g., expected improvement) could be used instead of UCB.  
- Minor: clarify the unclear statement about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor: explain the observation on page 4 (“At n=16, the far right...but no function evaluation occurs”).
