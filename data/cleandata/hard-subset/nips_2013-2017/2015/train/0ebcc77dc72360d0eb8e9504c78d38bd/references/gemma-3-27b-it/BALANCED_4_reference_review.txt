This paper proposes a novel Gaussian Process (GP)-based global optimization algorithm (IMGPO) aiming to maintain an exponential convergence rate without the computationally demanding δ-cover sampling procedure required by de Freitas et al. [1]. The core idea is to leverage an unknown semi-metric bound on the objective function, simultaneously conducting global and local searches based on multiple candidate bounds, and using the GP to prune unlikely candidates. The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating exponential convergence and experimental results showcasing superior performance compared to several baselines on a range of benchmark functions.

The paper addresses a significant challenge in Bayesian optimization – achieving theoretical guarantees like exponential convergence without impractical computational costs. The motivation is well-articulated, clearly outlining the limitations of existing methods and the gap that IMGPO aims to fill. The connection to previous work, particularly [1, 2], is well-established. The technical details, including the algorithm description and the analysis, appear sound and are presented with sufficient clarity, albeit dense. The inclusion of pseudocode (Algorithm 1) is helpful. The experiments provide a compelling demonstration of IMGPO's effectiveness, especially its ability to outperform alternatives even with a naive hyperparameter tuning strategy. The inclusion of insights from the experiments (e.g., the impact of Ξmax) adds to the paper’s value. The acknowledgement of limitations and potential future work (scalability, exploration of different division procedures) is commendable.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The approach of simultaneously considering multiple possible bounds and leveraging the GP to refine the search space is a novel contribution.
*   **Theoretical Guarantees:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis demonstrating exponential convergence, a strong result for this problem.
*   **Practical Relevance:** Addressing the issue of δ-cover sampling makes the algorithm more practical for real-world applications.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Extensive experiments on benchmark functions demonstrate the superiority of IMGPO over existing methods.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and organized, making the technical details relatively accessible.
* **Acknowledged Limitations:**  The discussion of scalability issues and future work shows realistic appraisal of the method.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Complexity:** The algorithm and its analysis are quite complex, which might make it challenging for practitioners to implement and understand fully. Algorithm 1, while helpful, is dense and could benefit from more detailed comments.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The parameter Ξmax appears to influence performance, and the optimal setting might be problem-dependent. While the paper discusses this, further investigation into automated parameter tuning could be beneficial.
*   **Scalability Concerns:** The theoretical analysis acknowledges scalability issues with increasing dimensionality. The experimental results, while promising, only go up to 1000 dimensions. More extensive testing in higher dimensions would be valuable.  The use of REMBO as a subroutine partially addresses this, but the limitations of REMBO should also be acknowledged.
* **Limited Comparison:** The comparison to GP-PI and GP-EI seems somewhat limited, particularly regarding the hyperparameter optimization process.  More details about how hyperparameters were chosen for *all* compared methods would increase confidence in the results.
* **Semi-Metric Assumption:** The assumption of an unknown semi-metric (Assumption 1) feels a little strong. While plausible, it is not always clear if this assumption would hold for practical problems. A discussion of how robust the algorithm is to violations of this assumption would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant theoretical advancement in Bayesian optimization, addressing a critical limitation of previous work.
*   The empirical results demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details.
*   The authors acknowledge the limitations of their approach and suggest promising avenues for future research.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The complexity of the algorithm and analysis might limit its adoption by practitioners.
*   The scalability concerns raise questions about its applicability to high-dimensional problems.
*   The semi-metric assumption may not hold for all practical scenarios.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a strong contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The novelty of the approach, the rigorous theoretical analysis, and the compelling experimental results outweigh the identified weaknesses.  The scalability issue is a concern, but the authors acknowledge it and suggest potential solutions. The complexity is manageable for researchers in the field. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.

**Suggested Revisions:**

*   Provide more detailed comments within Algorithm 1 to improve readability.
*   Conduct additional experiments in higher dimensions to further investigate scalability.
*   Discuss the robustness of the algorithm to violations of the semi-metric assumption.
*   Provide a more detailed description of the hyperparameter optimization process for *all* compared methods.
*   Consider adding a section on potential applications of IMGPO to real-world problems.
