Summary
The paper proposes IMGPO, a Gaussian-process-based global optimization algorithm for deterministic black-box maximization that aims to achieve exponential simple-regret convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary inner optimization. The key idea is to combine GP-derived upper confidence bounds (UCB) with a hierarchical partitioning scheme (akin to DIRECT/SOO), while “not committing” to a single Lipschitz-like bound. The authors formalize this via an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt capturing the penalty for exploring across many implicit bounds, and analyze a regret bound that is exponential in N + Ngp, where N is the number of function evaluations and Ngp the number of GP-based pseudo-evaluations. They claim improvements over BaMSOO (polynomial rate) and competitive empirical performance on several benchmarks.

Relation to prior work: This sits squarely between DIRECT/SOO (no explicit metric, partition-based search) and GP-UCB/BaMSOO (GP-based optimism). The conceptual “infinite-metric” viewpoint generalizes SOO’s unknown smoothness and tries to use GP to screen candidates. The main prior most relevant are Munos’ DOO/SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-UCB analyses (Srinivas et al.). The paper distinguishes itself by claiming exponential convergence without δ-cover and without optimizing an acquisition function.

Strengths
- Interesting conceptual synthesis of partition-based search and GP optimism, with a clear algorithmic proposal avoiding acquisition optimization.
- Attempts to formalize the value of not committing to a single bound via ρt and to quantify the role of GP (Ngp and screening).
- Empirically competitive on several deterministic benchmarks; the deterministic division scheme is attractive for budgeted settings.
- Addresses a question explicitly raised by Wang et al. (BaMSOO): exponential rates without δ-cover, aiming to fill a gap.

Major weaknesses and concerns
1) Theoretical correctness and assumptions
- The guarantee f(x) ≤ U(x|D) “during an execution” with probability ≥ 1 − η is used crucially, but no conditions are provided under which this holds. In GP-UCB, such uniform high-probability bounds require strong assumptions (e.g., f in RKHS with bounded norm or f a GP sample) and a carefully designed βt. Here, f is only assumed Hölder-continuous (Assumption 2), and hyperparameters are updated by empirical Bayes online. Under these conditions, the GP-UCB confidence property is generally not valid. This appears to undermine the correctness of the proof sketch.
- The exponential bound rN = O(λN+Ngp) counts Ngp (number of UCB computations) in the exponent. As written, this lets one improve the bound by calling U more often without any new function evaluations, which is not a meaningful measure of optimization progress. Standard practice is to bound regret in terms of N (evaluations), not the number of cheap model queries. This is a major conceptual issue.
- The definition of λ is inconsistent/likely a typesetting error: “λ = 3 − α2CDρ̄t < 1” is nonsensical. Presumably λ = 3^{-α/(2 C D ρ̄t)} or similar. Several exponents/logs throughout Theorem 1 and remarks appear malformed. As stated, the theorem is not checkable.
- ρ̄t is left essentially uncontrolled; the bound becomes vacuous unless one proves that ρ̄t stays O(1) or at least sublinear in N. The remarks handwave about “useful” UCBs but provide no rigorous sufficient condition (in terms of kernel, f, hyperparam adaptation) ensuring N/ρ̄t = Ω(N).
- The analysis mixes Hölder continuity (Assumption 2) with GP-UCB concentration; there is no bridging assumption connecting the GP posterior to the Hölder class. Without this, the claimed reduction in ρ̄t and increase in Ngp induced by GP is unsubstantiated.

2) Algorithmic clarity and correctness
- Pseudocode has multiple inconsistencies: line numbers/indices, undefined variables, and suspicious update rules. Examples:
  - Line 21: definition and computation of ξ and T′h+ξ(ch,i∗h) requires a full recursive expansion; computational burden is not analyzed.
  - Line 23 condition appears reversed relative to the textual description of step (iii).
  - Line 38: “Ξ ← Ξ + 22” and “Ξ ← max(Ξ − 2−1, 1)” are unclear (do you mean Ξ += 2^2 and Ξ −= 2^{-1}?).
  - Overloading of n, N, t, M, Ngp is confusing; N is both evaluations and appears to be incremented in an odd place when resolving GP-dummy values.
- Step (iii) may require up to 3^Ξmax UCB evaluations per candidate; this could be large even if “rarely happens”. No time complexity or practical cost analysis is given; yet the theory critically depends on Ngp.

3) Empirical evaluation
- Important details are missing: evaluation budgets, stopping criteria, number of evals per function, wall-clock time. The text references Table 1 and Figure 1/2, but critical quantitative details are not present here.
- GP-PI/EI baselines: the acquisition optimization strategy (SOO + gradient local search) is unusual and may disadvantage these methods; multi-start L-BFGS or global optimizers are standard.
- Hyperparameter learning uses empirical Bayes online; this can break GP-UCB confidence guarantees and should be discussed.
- The REMBO experiment is anecdotal; it compares IMGPO vs BaMSOO as subroutines only, with no comparison to standard REMBO baselines.
- No ablations to isolate the effect of Ξmax, screening (step iii), or GP versus no-GP variants beyond brief comments.

4) Presentation and formatting
- Many typographical/notation errors: malformed exponents/logs, inconsistent norms (||·||αp), missing terms, undefined constants, footnote markers dangling, inconsistent notation for sets and indices. References are incomplete (missing years/venues), which makes verification difficult.
- The code link is given; but reproducibility claims should specify commit/version and environment.

Minor comments and questions
- Assumption 1: clarify semi-metric properties needed (symmetry/triangle inequality?), and how it relates to Assumption 2.
- Clarify β, C, and their dependence on D and the division scheme; the sentence “if we conduct 2^D or 3^D−1 evaluations per node… C=1” needs derivation.
- Provide a bound that depends only on N (or present both N and model-query bounds), otherwise the claim of exponential convergence in evaluations is misleading.
- Discuss robustness to observation noise; currently limited to deterministic f.

Pros
- Interesting hybrid idea (partition search + GP screening).
- Strives for exponential simple-regret bounds without δ-cover or acquisition optimization.
- Empirical results suggest potential practical benefits.

Cons
- Theoretical guarantees rely on unjustified GP confidence bounds and include Ngp in the exponent.
- Numerous notation/typing errors make the main theorem unverifiable.
- Algorithmic steps and complexity are unclear; pseudocode likely incorrect in places.
- Experimental details insufficient; baseline choices may be suboptimal.

Recommendation
Overall: Weak reject (score: 4/10). The paper has a promising idea but, in its current form, the theoretical claims are not convincingly established, and the presentation contains too many technical and formatting issues. I encourage the authors to (i) fix the theorem/notation, provide a regret bound in terms of N only, and state precise conditions for GP confidence; (ii) clarify/verify pseudocode; (iii) strengthen and fully specify experiments. Confidence: 0.7.
