- Summary: Proposes IMGPO, combining hierarchical optimistic partitioning with GP-UCB pruning to avoid δ-cover sampling and inner acquisition optimization for deterministic global optimization.
- Summary: Introduces an “unknown bound” assumption (existence of semi-metric ℓ around x*) and an infinite-metric exploration loss ρt to account for exploring many candidate bounds.
- Summary: Algorithm maintains a tree of hyperrectangles, selects by center values, uses GP-UCB to screen intervals and skip evaluations; claims exponential simple-regret bound rN = O(λN+Ngp).

- Strength: Novel bridge between GP-based Bayesian optimization and optimistic partitioning that avoids δ-cover sampling and acquisition maximization.
- Strength: Infinite-metric exploration loss ρt is a useful conceptual tool to analyze exploration cost from noncommittal bounds.
- Strength: Theorems decompose how GP (via Ngp and ρ̄t) influences rates and connect to DOO/SOO constants and Hölder smoothness.
- Strength: Broad experiments with consistent kernel/hyperparameter protocol show competitive performance and lower CPU time than standard GP methods.

- Key concern: UCB validity is assumed but not justified—no precise probabilistic assumptions on f or GP/hyperparameters are given, and empirical Bayes updates complicate union bounds.
- Key concern: Exponential convergence claim depends on ρ̄t and Ξn; without bounds on these quantities the rate can degenerate (λ → 1), so the claim must be qualified.
- Key concern: Notational and typographical ambiguities in theorem and algorithm (e.g., λ expression likely has wrong formatting, undefined constants C and CD, odd β definition).
- Key concern: Specific Algorithm 1 issues—unclear steps (lines 21–24), use of T′ that can be exponentially large, and apparent typos (e.g., Ξ update).
- Key concern: Computational complexity unclear—many UCB computations possible, T′ growth exponential in ξ and D; need complexity analysis in evaluations and GP inference calls.
- Key concern: Mismatch between assumptions and practice—analysis relies on GP-UCB validity while hyperparameters are updated; robustness or mitigation (e.g., freezing hyperparameters) is needed.
- Key concern: Empirical baselines could be stronger—GP-PI/EI with naive acquisition optimization may understate GP methods; suggest GP-UCB/ES/EI with better optimization and kernel choices.

- Quality: Theory is promising but underspecified probabilistically and in constants; exponential rate claim should be tempered or supported by bounds on ρ̄t.
- Clarity: Generally readable but several notation/typo issues and dense Algorithm 1/Theorem 1 passages need fixing.
- Originality: Novel combination of infinite-metric optimistic partitioning with GP pruning; extends ideas from DOO/DIRECT with GP information.
- Significance: Potentially notable if theoretical gaps are closed; empirical gains are encouraging but not decisive.

- Question 1: Under what precise assumptions on f and GP/hyperparameters does f(x) ≤ U(x|D) hold uniformly for all algorithm uses?
- Question 2: Can you provide an upper bound on ρ̄t (in expectation or high probability) under Assumption 2 and mild GP posterior conditions to justify unconditional exponential rates?
- Question 3: Please clarify and correct definitions of λ, C, CD, β, and fix Algorithm 1 typos (e.g., line 38).
- Question 4: What is the computational complexity per iteration in terms of UCB evaluations and GP updates, and sensitivity to Ξmax and D?
- Question 5: How robust is IMGPO to GP mis-specification, and can you provide an ablation disabling GP pruning to isolate its effect?

- Minor: Provide derivation or lemma for ζM choice and its high-probability UCB validity under the paper’s GP/hyperparameter protocol.
- Minor: Anonymize or supply the code URL as supplementary material for review.

- Pros: Conceptual novelty using GP to prune infinite-metric optimistic search.
- Pros: Theoretical framework isolates GP and exploration-loss contributions.
- Pros: Avoids auxiliary acquisition optimization, offering a practical algorithmic approach.
- Pros: Empirically strong across benchmarks with consistent hyperparameter handling.

- Cons: Key theoretical assumption (UCB validity) not clearly stated/justified, especially with empirical Bayes.
- Cons: Exponential rate claim hinges on unbounded quantities (ρ̄t, Ξn) and thus may overclaim without additional bounds.
- Cons: Several algorithmic and notation clarity issues plus limited complexity analysis and baseline strength.

- Overall recommendation: Borderline—weak reject in current form due to theoretical gaps, but could be acceptable if UCB assumptions, theorem notation, and ρ̄t/complexity issues are adequately addressed.
