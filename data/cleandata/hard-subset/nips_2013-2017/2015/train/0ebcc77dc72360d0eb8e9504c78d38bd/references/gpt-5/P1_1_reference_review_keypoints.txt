- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimizer combining hierarchical partitioning with GP UCB used as a filter to seek exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- Introduces the “infinite-metric” idea: hedge over infinitely many semi-metrics and use GP UCB to prune candidates.  
- Connects optimistic partitioning (HOO/DOO/SOO) and GP Bayesian optimization, claiming improvement over BaMSOO and de Freitas et al.  
- Empirical results on eight benchmarks show faster convergence for IMGPO in most cases.

- Main theorem claims exponential simple regret under local Hölder-type assumptions, but the formula for λ is unclear/likely mistyped and must be stated precisely.  
- UCB validity is asserted using a fixed prior schedule, but the algorithm updates GP hyperparameters (empirical Bayes), invalidating standard GP-UCB confidence guarantees; Rosenbrock2 shows UCB violations.  
- The regret bound depends on ρ̄t (average infinite-metric exploration loss) but no nontrivial upper bound on ρ̄t is provided; exponential rate may be vacuous if ρ̄t grows with N.  
- The role, growth, and computational impact of Ξn need clearer bounds and statements (e.g., number of UCB queries per iteration).

- Algorithm 1 contains notational/instructional glitches: inconsistent indices and comparisons, unclear ςM context, overlapping variable names (N, n, t, Ngp, Ξ, Ξmax), and odd lines like “Ξ← Ξ + 22” and unusual constant definitions.  
- Reproducibility hampered by ambiguous step (iii) and the construction of T′h+ξ.

- Experiments learn hyperparameters (empirical Bayes), contradicting theoretical fixed-prior assumptions and requiring explicit discussion.  
- Need fair computational budgets and wall-clock time breakdowns showing f-eval vs UCB-eval overhead; current runtime reporting is brief.  
- Inconsistent initialization: deterministic methods use fixed start while PI/EI averaged over 50 restarts—either randomize all or report both fixed and multi-start results.  
- Missing REMBO details: embedding dimension, bounds, and how IMGPO operates with the embedding.

- Paper is generally readable but proof notation typos and ambiguities make verification difficult; symbols should be defined once and notation unified.  
- Theorem 1 should be restated with all constants (C, β, λ) precisely and explicit assumptions on kernel/noise/hyperparameters.

- The hedging-over-infinite-metrics plus GP UCB filter is novel and interesting.  
- The infinite-metric exploration loss ρt framing gives an interpretable decomposition of regret contributions.  
- If exponential rates can be proved under realistic hyperparameter handling, this would be a significant advance over BaMSOO.

- Potentially high significance, but theoretical gaps (hyperparameter learning and ρ̄t dependence) reduce immediate impact.

- Pros: ambitious combination of ideas, new analytic perspective, removal of δ-cover and auxiliary acquisition optimization, open-source code, promising empirical gains.  
- Cons: theoretical guarantees seem to assume fixed GP prior while experiments use empirical Bayes, multiple typos/ambiguities hinder verification, no control on ρ̄t, and experimental fairness/reporting need tightening.

- Questions for authors: please restate Theorem 1 precisely with λ and all constants and specify whether kernel/hyperparameters are fixed.  
- Can you provide confidence guarantees when hyperparameters are learned, or restrict the theorem to fixed hyperparameters?  
- Do you have empirical measurements of ρ̄t and Ξn over time to justify the exponential rate?  
- Please report wall-clock times with breakdowns and ensure comparable budgets across methods.

- Recommendation: Weak reject pending revised submission that fixes theorem/assumptions, clarifies algorithm and proofs, and tightens experimental protocol.
