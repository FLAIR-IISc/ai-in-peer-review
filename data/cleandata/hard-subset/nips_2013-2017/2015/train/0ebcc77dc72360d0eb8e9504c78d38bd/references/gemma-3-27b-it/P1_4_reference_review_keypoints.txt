- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: explore multiple potential function bounds simultaneously, using GP priors to prune implausible bounds and hierarchical partitioning for search.  
- Provides theoretical analysis proving exponential convergence, addressing an open problem from prior work.  
- Empirically outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on diverse benchmarks, including high-dimensional tests.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. (2017) and Wang et al. (2018), aiming to close the gap between practicality and exponential guarantees.  
- Connects to bound-based search literature (Lipschitz optimization, A* search, PAC-MDP) and highlights importance of tight bounds.  
- Novel "infinite-metric optimization" concept is clever and well-motivated by GP guidance.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains the algorithm and related concepts effectively.  
- Offers comprehensive discussion of behavior, limitations, future work, and insightful analytical remarks.  
- Provides open-source code, supporting reproducibility.  
- Scalability concerns: theoretical limits suggest potential issues in very high dimensions (λ → 1), needing deeper mitigation discussion.  
- Parameter sensitivity: choice of Ξmax (max partition depth) is influential, but practical selection guidance is lacking.  
- Implementation complexity may hinder adoption compared to simpler Bayesian optimization methods.  
- Limited empirical comparison to more recent state-of-the-art BO methods (e.g., modern kernel learning, multi-fidelity approaches).  
- Relies on semi-metric assumptions (Assumptions 1 & 2); implications if violated are not fully explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, novel approach, strong empirical support, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability and complexity concerns, and somewhat limited comparisons to current BO techniques.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with substantial contribution; theoretical and empirical results outweigh noted concerns.  
- Recommendations: expand scalability discussion and mitigation strategies, provide guidance on selecting Ξmax, include comparisons to more recent BO methods, and discuss sensitivity to the semi-metric assumption.
