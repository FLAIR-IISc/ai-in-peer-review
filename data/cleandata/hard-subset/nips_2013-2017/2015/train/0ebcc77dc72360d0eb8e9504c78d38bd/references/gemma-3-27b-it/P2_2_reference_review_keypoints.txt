- Presents IMGPO, a novel GP-based global optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: simultaneously consider many Lipschitz-like bounds (a semi-metric) together with GP uncertainty to balance global and local search.  
- Provides a theoretical analysis proving exponential convergence and empirical results showing IMGPO outperforms several baselines.  
- Correctly situates the work relative to BO literature and prior exponential-convergence results (de Freitas et al., 2017; Wang et al., 2017).  
- Clearly connects to bound-based methods like DIRECT and SOO, framing IMGPO as a Bayesian variant that manages multiple potential bounds.  
- References are comprehensive and relevant.  
- Theoretical guarantee of exponential convergence is a significant contribution addressing an open problem.  
- Novel and intuitive approach to balancing global/local exploration by managing multiple candidate bounds.  
- Empirical validation on benchmarks demonstrates consistent outperformance of SOO, BaMSOO, PI, and EI.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; algorithm exposition and Figure 1 aid clarity.  
- Practical discussion of GP bound tightness, Ξmax effects, and code availability are strengths.  
- Convergence rate appears sensitive to input dimension D; scalability to high dimensions is a concern.  
- Performance depends on parameter Ξmax (and likely η and GP kernel choices); more sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Computational overhead from considering many bounds is not fully analyzed; Table 1 shows IMGPO is slower than SOO.  
- Lacks detailed discussion on GP hyperparameter tuning and its impact within IMGPO.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel algorithm, convincing empirical results, clear presentation, and reproducible code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high-dimensional scalability concerns, parameter sensitivity, and missing computational complexity analysis.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution worth acceptance, with concerns addressable via minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, requesting elaboration on scalability, more sensitivity analysis (Ξmax, η, kernel), a computational complexity comparison, and clearer hyperparameter optimization details.
