Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a GP-based global optimization algorithm for deterministic black-box functions. The central idea is to avoid reliance on a single, potentially loose GP-derived upper bound by implicitly considering an infinite family of unknown semi-metric bounds around a global optimizer, while still using the GP’s UCB to prune candidates. The algorithm uses a hierarchical partitioning scheme, selects candidate rectangles across depths using unknown-bound logic (steps (i)–(ii)), and then screens using GP-UCB over a locally expanded finite set (step (iii)). It can “skip” evaluations if the GP-UCB suggests the subregion cannot improve on the incumbent. The main theoretical claim is an exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1, where N is the number of function evaluations and Ngp is the number of skipped-evaluation UCB uses. Empirically, IMGPO is compared against SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on synthetic benchmarks; it generally outperforms baselines.

The work builds on: (i) GP-UCB and its deterministic-observation variant (Srinivas et al.; de Freitas et al.) but avoids δ-cover sampling and auxiliary global optimization; (ii) optimistic partition-based methods (DIRECT, DOO/SOO, BaMSOO); (iii) near-optimality dimension and local Hölder assumptions (Munos). The “infinite-metric” viewpoint is positioned as a principled way to hedge against misspecified bounds.

Strengths
- Ambitious and timely goal: removing δ-cover and auxiliary optimization while claiming exponential convergence is important.
- Interesting algorithmic design that fuses partition-based optimism with GP-driven pruning and evaluation skipping.
- Attempts to quantify the cost of “not committing to a bound” via the infinite-metric exploration loss ρt, with insightful remarks about how GP reduces ρ̄t and contributes via Ngp.
- Experiments broadly favorable to IMGPO; code availability is noted.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical rigor and correctness
- Theorem 1 statement has likely errors. λ is defined as λ = 3 − α/(2 C D ρ̄t) < 1 (as typeset), which is dimensionally and semantically implausible (and can be negative). Given rN ≲ exp(−α[(N+Ngp)/(2CDρ̄t) − Ξn − 2] ln 3), one expects λ = 3−α/(2CDρ̄t), i.e., λ = 3−(α/(2CDρ̄t)), not 3 − α/(2CDρ̄t). The current definition appears to be a serious typographical or mathematical error.
- The UCB validity. The paper sets ςM = sqrt(log(π^2 M^2/(12 η))) to ensure f(x) ≤ U(x|D) “during execution” with probability ≥ 1 − η. This resembles a union bound over a finite set of UCB queries, but standard GP-UCB guarantees require fixed kernel hyperparameters and either an RKHS norm bound or information gain arguments. Here, hyperparameters are re-estimated after each iteration (empirical Bayes), the GP prior may be misspecified, and no RKHS/IG assumptions are stated. The experiments acknowledge that the property f ≤ U often fails (e.g., Rosenbrock2). This undermines the key pruning step (iii) and the theorem’s “with probability 1 − η” statement.
- Dependence on Ngp. The claimed bound is exponential in N+Ngp. But Ngp counts the number of UCB computations (skip decisions), which are computationally cheap and can, in principle, be increased by tuning Ξmax or the internal expansion strategy. It is unusual to state convergence in terms of sum of evaluations plus internal acquisition evaluations; this can make the theoretical improvement partially a function of computational effort rather than sample efficiency. The result would be more meaningful as a function of N alone (or with explicit wall-clock complexity).
- Assumptions and constants are insufficiently specified. Assumption 1/2 reduce to a Hölder-type near-optimality condition, but constants L, α, p, C, β are ambiguously defined or not connected to measurable quantities. The role and definition of C (taken from DOO/SOO) is not self-contained. The analysis also assumes Ω is a square; the generalization is deferred and not presented here.
- The event over which the high-probability bound holds must union-bound over all points where U is computed (including those in T′ expansions). The construction and measurability with data-dependent hyperparameter selection are not addressed.

Algorithmic clarity and reproducibility
- Pseudocode has multiple notation/line inconsistencies: i∗h+ξ reuse; “line 25” reference; variable reuse of n, N, t; unclear increments of Ξ (line 38 shows “Ξ ← Ξ + 22” which is likely a typesetting error for Ξ ← Ξ + 2−2 or similar). The while-true loop with GP-based labels is confusing.
- Step (iii) complexity: up to 3Ξmax UCBs; the behavior and tuning of Ξ/Ξmax are critical but insufficiently analyzed. The impact of Ξ on the bound appears only as a penalty Ξn, but practical guidance and sensitivity are limited.
- Many equations and symbols appear malformed (e.g., fsin1 definition; various missing parentheses; ς vs ξ; superscripts and subscripts). References are incomplete and not in standard format.

Experimental evaluation
- Important baselines omitted or weakly treated: GP-UCB itself is not included; modern BO methods (e.g., entropy search variants, Thompson sampling, predictive entropy search) are not compared. BaMSOO is included but parameterization may not be optimal; GP-PI/EI are run with SOO/local solvers, but details on their optimization are sparse.
- Only isotropic Matérn 5/2 with fixed initialization; while fair for “unknown kernel” scenarios, an ablation exploring kernel mis-specification sensitivity would strengthen claims, especially since IMGPO relies on GP for pruning.
- Variability treatment is inconsistent: deterministic methods shown once; stochastic baselines averaged over 50 runs. Statistical significance is not reported; figures and Table 1 are referenced but not fully available here.
- The 1000D REMBO experiment is promising but under-detailed (embedding dimension, budgets, seeds, and whether IMGPO’s partitioning interacts with REMBO’s subspace are not discussed).

Clarity, organization, and formatting
- Numerous typos, notation clashes, and incomplete references hinder readability and verifiability (e.g., missing years/pages; inconsistent author formatting; malformed equations). The manuscript would benefit from substantial editing.

Questions for the authors
- Please restate Theorem 1 precisely and correct the definition of λ. Provide a clear list of assumptions needed for UCB validity with empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates. Do you assume a fixed true kernel and bounded RKHS norm?
- Can you provide a regret bound purely in terms of N (function evaluations), possibly separating computational complexity for UCB calls?
- How is Ξ updated exactly (line 38)? What is the theoretical effect of Ξmax truncation? Provide a sensitivity study.
- Can you add GP-UCB and a modern acquisition (e.g., Thompson sampling) as baselines? Also include DIRECT to connect with your “infinite-metric” narrative.
- Please provide a detailed proof sketch in the main paper to justify the high-level steps that are most nonstandard (event construction; role of ρ̄t; handling of hyperparameter learning).

Pros
- Addresses an important open gap (δ-cover-free exponential rate claim).
- Creative hybrid of partition-based optimism and GP gating.
- Insightful discussion of the cost of not committing to a bound (ρt) and how GP helps.

Cons
- Theoretical claims hinge on questionable UCB coverage under hyperparameter learning; theorem statement contains apparent errors.
- Bound uses Ngp, conflating computational with sample complexity.
- Clarity issues, notation errors, and incomplete references.
- Limited and somewhat dated baselines; insufficient ablations.

Recommendation: Reject
While the idea is interesting and potentially impactful, the current version has significant issues in theorem correctness/assumptions, clarity, and experimental validation. A revised version fixing the theory (especially UCB coverage and λ), clarifying the algorithm, tightening notation, and expanding experiments could be much stronger. Confidence: high.
