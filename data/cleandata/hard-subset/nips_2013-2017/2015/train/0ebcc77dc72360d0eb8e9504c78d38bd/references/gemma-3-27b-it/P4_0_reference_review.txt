This paper introduces a novel Gaussian Process (GP)-based global optimization algorithm, IMGPO, aiming to achieve exponential convergence without relying on the computationally expensive δ-cover sampling procedure required by previous theoretical results (de Freitas et al. [1]). The authors address the open problem highlighted by Wang et al. [2] of developing a GP-based algorithm with exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling. IMGPO leverages hierarchical partitioning and an “infinite-metric” approach, effectively considering a broader range of potential Lipschitz-like bounds than standard GP-UCB. The core idea revolves around simultaneously conducting global and local searches based on all possible bounds, utilizing the GP prior to prune less promising candidates.

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the motivation, proposed algorithm, theoretical analysis, and experimental evaluation. The connection to existing work in Bayesian optimization, Lipschitz optimization, and bound-based search methods is clearly articulated. The algorithm is explained with a helpful illustrative figure (Figure 1) and pseudocode (Algorithm 1). The theoretical analysis establishes an exponential convergence rate, and the experimental results demonstrate promising performance on a variety of benchmark functions, outperforming several baseline algorithms.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The “infinite-metric” approach, considering a wider range of potential bounds, represents a novel contribution to GP-based optimization.
*   **Theoretical Guarantee:** The paper provides a theoretical analysis demonstrating exponential convergence, a significant improvement over polynomial bounds achieved by some previous methods.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The elimination of the δ-cover sampling procedure addresses a major practical limitation of earlier theoretical results.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the algorithm, its theoretical foundation, and experimental results. The inclusion of pseudocode enhances reproducibility.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on multiple benchmark functions demonstrate the effectiveness of IMGPO compared to established algorithms.
*   **Detailed Analysis:** The analysis section includes a discussion of the impact of the GP prior, hyperparameter selection, and the connection to previous work, providing valuable insights.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Scalability Discussion:** While the paper acknowledges the potential scalability issues with increasing input dimensionality, the discussion remains somewhat limited. The proposed solution of leveraging additional assumptions (e.g., [14, 20]) feels like a direction for future work rather than a fully addressed concern.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance of IMGPO appears sensitive to the choice of Ξmax (maximum depth of the hierarchical partitioning tree), as noted in the experiments. A more in-depth analysis of the impact of this parameter on performance and computational cost would be beneficial.
*   **Comparison with State-of-the-Art:** The comparison with GP-PI and GP-EI, while present, feels somewhat superficial. A more rigorous comparison with more recent and advanced Bayesian optimization algorithms, particularly those utilizing ensemble methods or surrogate model ensembles, would strengthen the paper.
*   **Assumption 1 (Unknown Bound):** The assumption of an unknown semi-metric ` seems rather strong. A discussion on the practical implications of this assumption and its impact on the performance of the algorithm would be valuable. Is this assumption reasonable for many real-world applications?
*   **Limited Ablation Studies:** The impact of individual components of the algorithm, such as the hierarchical partitioning strategy, is not thoroughly investigated through ablation studies.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound algorithm with a clear improvement in convergence rate compared to existing methods.
*   The elimination of the δ-cover sampling procedure makes the algorithm more practical for real-world applications.
*   The experimental results provide strong evidence of the algorithm's effectiveness on a range of benchmark functions.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the algorithm and its theoretical foundations.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The scalability discussion is limited, and the sensitivity to the Ξmax parameter raises concerns about practical applicability in high-dimensional spaces.
*   The comparison with state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization algorithms is not comprehensive.
*   The strong assumption of an unknown semi-metric ` warrants further discussion and justification.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The proposed algorithm, IMGPO, addresses a key limitation of previous theoretical results and demonstrates promising performance on benchmark functions. While some aspects, such as scalability and parameter sensitivity, require further investigation, the novelty, theoretical soundness, and practical relevance of the work justify acceptance. I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions, focusing on expanding the scalability discussion, providing a more in-depth analysis of the impact of Ξmax, and strengthening the comparison with state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization algorithms.
