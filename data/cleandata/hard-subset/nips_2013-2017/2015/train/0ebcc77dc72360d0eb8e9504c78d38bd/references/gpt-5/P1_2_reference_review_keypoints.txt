- Proposes IMGPO: a GP-UCB filter integrated into hierarchical optimistic partitioning to hedge over infinitely many unknown semi-metrics.  
- Algorithm builds a tree of hyperrectangles, divides promising ones, and sometimes uses GP-UCB values in place of true evaluations to reduce queries.  
- Claims an exponential simple-regret bound in N+Ngp and introduces “infinite-metric exploration loss” to account for hedging cost.  
- Builds on GP-UCB, de Freitas’ deterministic analysis, SOO/DOO/DIRECT, and BaMSOO; aims to remove δ-cover sampling and auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- Major theoretical concern: exponential rate under Assumptions 1–2 conflicts with standard lower bounds that imply polynomial regret unless extra structure is assumed.  
- Needs precise relation between t, N, and ρ̄t and explicit regimes where ρ̄t = O(1); reconcile exponential claim with near-optimality dimension.  
- Possible typo in λ definition—likely intended as an exponent (e.g., λ = 3^{−α/(2CDρ̄t)}); authors should correct and clarify.  
- UCB concentration argument is compromised by adaptive hyperparameter re-estimation; empirical violations on Rosenbrock were observed.  
- Must provide a robustness argument for hyperparameter adaptation or restrict theoretical claims to fixed hyperparameters.  
- Assumptions are vague: “semi-metric” is not formalized and Assumption 2 effectively imposes local Hölder smoothness—connect this to near-optimality dimension.  
- The bound’s dependence on N+Ngp requires clarification—if Ngp scales with N, exponential-in-N claim may not hold in practice.  
- Algorithmic complexity unclear: worst-case UCB evaluation in expanded subtrees could be exponential (O(3^{Ξmax})); need per-iteration complexity and guidance on Ξmax.  
- Notation and indexing are heavy/inconsistent (n,N,t,h,M); authors should provide a symbol table and clearly define “iteration” vs “function evaluation.”  
- Pseudocode is dense and contains typos (e.g., Ξ update, reuse of l); include a short worked example to clarify lines 21–24.  
- Role of η and how it propagates to global failure probability should be explicitly stated.  
- Originality: the infinite-metric exploration loss is a novel analytical device; using GP-UCB as a filter within SOO-style partitioning is a new integration/analysis.  
- Significance: could be notable if theoretical guarantees are tightened and UCB validity under hyperparameter updates is ensured; avoiding acquisition optimization is practically appealing.  
- Empirical issues: missing GP-UCB baseline and common optimizers for PI/EI; GP-PI/EI optimized via SOO may be unfair.  
- Recommend plotting x-axis as true evaluations N (excluding Ngp) and ensuring wall-clock comparisons use consistent implementations.  
- Request ablations: IMGPO without GP to isolate infinite-metric effect, variation over Ξmax and η, and sensitivity to kernel mismatch.  
- High-dimensional REMBO result is encouraging but needs more details and comparisons (e.g., REMBO+EI).  
- Specific clarifications requested: conditions for ρ̄t = O(1); corrected λ definition; a valid concentration argument under adaptive hyperparameters or scoped theorem; formal definition of semi-metric and implied near-optimality dimension.  
- Pros: interesting hybrid approach, novel analysis tool, promising empirical performance.  
- Cons: theoretical exponential claim appears inconsistent with lower bounds, UCB validity under hyperparameter updates not justified, experimental comparisons incomplete.  
- Overall: reviewer leans to a weak reject pending clarifications, stronger theory, and more comprehensive/fair experiments.
