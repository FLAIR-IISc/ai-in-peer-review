## Review of "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces a novel Gaussian Process (GP)-based Bayesian optimization algorithm (IMGPO) designed to achieve exponential convergence without relying on the computationally expensive δ-cover sampling procedure required by previous theoretical results (de Freitas et al., 2017). The core idea is to leverage an unknown, potentially tighter semi-metric encoding the continuity at the global optimizer, alongside the GP’s own uncertainty estimates. IMGPO performs a hierarchical partitioning search, guided by both the GP’s UCB and this implicitly considered unknown metric, effectively balancing exploration and exploitation. The authors provide a theoretical analysis guaranteeing exponential convergence and demonstrate empirical performance on a suite of benchmark functions, showing improved results over existing methods, particularly in scenarios where the GP’s inherent uncertainty estimates are less informative.

**Related Work:**

The paper appropriately positions itself within the existing literature on Bayesian Optimization, Lipschitz optimization, and global optimization. It correctly identifies the limitations of earlier approaches requiring known Lipschitz constants or δ-cover sampling. The reference to Wang et al. (2018) and the open problem they highlight is crucial. The connection to bound-based search methods like A* search and SOO is insightful, emphasizing the importance of tight bounds for efficient optimization.  The inclusion of relevant work on random embeddings (Kandasamy et al., 2015) is also valuable, acknowledging alternative approaches to high-dimensional Bayesian Optimization.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Guarantee:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis, proving exponential convergence under reasonable assumptions. This is a significant contribution, as achieving this rate without δ-cover sampling has been a long-standing open problem.
* **Novel Approach:** The concept of implicitly leveraging an unknown semi-metric alongside the GP's uncertainty estimates is a clever and innovative idea. It allows the algorithm to benefit from potentially tighter bounds without requiring their explicit knowledge.
* **Empirical Performance:** The experimental results demonstrate that IMGPO consistently outperforms several competing algorithms across a variety of benchmark functions.  The ablation studies, though limited, offer some insight into the impact of the algorithm's components.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The description of the algorithm is clear, and the motivation for each step is explained.
* **Code Availability:** The public availability of the code is a major strength, fostering reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Scalability Concerns:** While the exponential convergence is theoretically appealing, the analysis highlights potential scalability issues as dimensionality increases.  The λ parameter approaching 1 with increasing D suggests a diminishing convergence rate in higher dimensions. This point, while acknowledged, requires further investigation, particularly concerning practical applications in high-dimensional spaces.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The experiments show that performance can be sensitive to the choice of Ξmax. While the authors provide some guidance, a more thorough sensitivity analysis and a more principled method for setting this parameter would be beneficial.
* **Limited Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies are somewhat limited.  A more detailed analysis of the impact of each component of the algorithm (e.g., the hierarchical partitioning strategy, the interaction between GP and the unknown metric) would be valuable.
* **Assumptions:** Assumption 1, stating the existence of an unknown semi-metric, is reasonable but could be more thoroughly justified.  Discussing the types of functions for which this assumption is likely to hold would strengthen the argument. The reliance on the isotropic Matern kernel in experiments, while acknowledging the lack of a known good kernel, could be a limiting factor.
* **Comparison to Recent Advances:**  The paper doesn’t explicitly compare to very recent advances in Bayesian Optimization, like those incorporating deep Gaussian processes or other advanced kernel learning techniques.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant open problem in Bayesian Optimization, offering a theoretically grounded solution with a proven exponential convergence rate.
* The proposed approach is novel and insightful, effectively combining the strengths of GP-based methods with a broader perspective on bounding the objective function.
* The empirical results are compelling, demonstrating the algorithm's effectiveness across a range of benchmark functions.
* The code availability promotes reproducibility and further research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The scalability concerns related to high dimensionality raise questions about the algorithm’s applicability to real-world problems.
* The parameter sensitivity and limited ablation studies leave some aspects of the algorithm's behavior unclear.
* The analysis could be strengthened by a more thorough justification of the underlying assumptions and a more detailed comparison to recent advances in the field.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The theoretical results are compelling, the proposed algorithm is innovative, and the empirical results are promising. While there are some weaknesses, they are not severe enough to outweigh the strengths. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NIPS.

**Rating:** Accept (Strong Accept)

**Confidence:** 8/10
