- Problem and claim: Proposes IMGPO, combining hierarchical partitioning with GP-UCB to achieve exponential simple-regret convergence for deterministic black-box optimization without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization.
- Key idea: Implicitly consider infinitely many Hölder-type smoothness bounds via partition-and-bound and use GP UCB only to prune overly loose candidates.
- New concept: Introduces infinite-metric exploration loss ρt to quantify extra partitions from not committing to a single smoothness constant.
- Main theorem: Bounds simple regret as O(exp(−c (N+Ngp)/(D ρ̄t) + …)), where Ngp counts skipped evaluations replaced by GP-UCB.
- Empirics: IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-PI/EI on standard test functions and a single REMBO-1000D case under a Matern-5/2 kernel with empirical Bayes hyperparameter learning.
- Strength — importance: Removing δ-cover and auxiliary optimization while keeping fast convergence is practically valuable.
- Strength — design: Elegant, practical integration of hierarchical partitioning with GP-UCB to defer costly function evaluations.
- Strength — analysis: ρt offers an interpretable link between unknown smoothness and search overhead; analysis discusses GP effects on ρt and regret.
- Strength — practical: Code available, simple division scheme, avoids inner nonconvex acquisition optimization.
- Weakness — UCB validity: Theoretical use of UCB relies on uniform high-probability bounds, which are not justified under online empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates or model misspecification.
- Weakness — Ngp inclusion: Counting Ngp like actual evaluations inflates convergence claims since skipped-UCB points do not add posterior information.
- Weakness — dependence on ρ̄t: Exponential rate requires ρ̄t = O(1); if GP bounds are uninformative ρ̄t can grow and the claimed rate weakens substantially.
- Weakness — assumptions: “Infinite-metric” framing is mainly an analytical twist on standard local Hölder smoothness; novelty is more analytical than algorithmic relative to SOO/BaMSOO/DIRECT.
- Weakness — sensitivity to Ξ/Ξmax: Algorithm behavior and the theorem degrade with the lookahead cap Ξmax, but control/selection of this parameter is heuristic and underanalyzed.
- Weakness — experiments: Baseline comparisons (e.g., PI/EI optimization setup) may be atypical, missing DIRECT baseline, lack of ablations on ρ̄t/Ξmax/hyperparameters, and only one high-dimensional REMBO case.
- Weakness — observed UCB violations: Empirical UCB-validity violations are noted but their impact is not analyzed.
- Weakness — clarity: Notational glitches, typos, and unclear dependence of constants on dimension and partitioning hinder readability.
- Questions for authors: Provide explicit assumptions/theorems for UCB validity with fixed hyperparameters and clarify behavior under online hyperparameter updates.
- Questions for authors: Restate regret purely in terms of N (e.g., bound Ngp by N and Ξmax) and give conditions guaranteeing bounded ρ̄t.
- Questions for authors: Report sensitivity/ablation for Ξmax and kernel misspecification, include DIRECT baseline, and quantify wall-clock time and UCB calls versus function evaluations.
- Recommendation: Weak reject — promising ideas but requires rigorous UCB justification under hyperparameter learning, a bound in N only, and more thorough experiments/ablations to support the exponential-convergence claim.
