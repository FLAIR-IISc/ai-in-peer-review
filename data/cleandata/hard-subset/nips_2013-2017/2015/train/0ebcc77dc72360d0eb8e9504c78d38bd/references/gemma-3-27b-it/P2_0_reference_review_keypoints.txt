- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.
- Uses an “infinite-metric” approach: consider many Lipschitz-like bounds simultaneously and use the GP prior to prune candidates.
- Provides theoretical analysis guaranteeing exponential convergence.
- Empirical results show IMGPO outperforms baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, standard GP) on benchmark functions.
- Situates work relative to prior literature (de Freitas et al., Wang et al., Munos) and connects to bound-based search methods.
- Strength: strong theoretical guarantee with detailed analysis of parameter impacts.
- Strength: novel and well-motivated infinite-metric optimization idea addressing tight-bound difficulties.
- Strength: consistent empirical validation across diverse benchmarks and thoughtful baseline comparisons.
- Strength: clear writing, logical organization, and mostly accessible mathematical notation.
- Strength: authors acknowledge limitations and discuss parameter sensitivity and scalability.
- Weakness: algorithm complexity with several tunable parameters (Ξmax, η) and limited exploration of their interactions.
- Weakness: observed performance sensitive to choice of Ξmax, suggesting practical tuning challenges.
- Weakness: scalability to higher dimensions is a concern and no concrete mitigation strategies are provided.
- Weakness: experimental setup reuses standard benchmarks, offering limited demonstration of unique strengths in novel scenarios.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on empirical Bayesian hyperparameter updates, affecting reproducibility.
- Evaluation: technically sound and complete (Quality: Strong); generally clear (Clarity: Good); original (Originality: Strong); significant (Significance: Good).
- Pros: strong theory, novel approach, empirical improvements, addresses an important open problem.
- Cons: complexity, parameter sensitivity, scalability limits, experimental originality lacking.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: expand discussion of parameter sensitivity, address scalability more directly, and provide more hyperparameter-tuning details for reproducibility.
