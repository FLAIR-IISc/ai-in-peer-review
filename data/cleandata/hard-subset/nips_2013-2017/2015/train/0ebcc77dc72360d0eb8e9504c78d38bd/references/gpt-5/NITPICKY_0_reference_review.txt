Summary
The paper proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm for deterministic black-box functions. The core idea is to avoid relying on a single (possibly loose) bound from the GP and instead implicitly consider “infinitely many” candidate bounds via a hierarchical partitioning strategy, while using GP-UCB only to prune/skip evaluations. The theoretical contribution claims an exponential convergence (simple regret) rate O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ<1, without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary nonconvex optimization, under an “unknown semi-metric” assumption equivalent to Hölder continuity around the global optimum. Empirically, IMGPO is compared against SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on several standard benchmarks.

Relation to prior work
The algorithm sits between bound-based partitioning methods (DIRECT, DOO/SOO) and GP-based Bayesian optimization (GP-UCB, EI/PI). The paper aims to improve on BaMSOO (polynomial rates) by combining hierarchical partitioning with GP-based screening, and conceptually resonates with optimistic optimization (HOO/DOO/SOO) under unknown smoothness. The theoretical framing borrows from Munos’ near-optimality dimension framework and UCB-based arguments.

Strengths
- Ambitious and timely goal: a GP-based optimizer with exponential regret decay without δ-cover sampling or auxiliary acquisition optimization.
- Interesting hybrid design that uses GP posterior for pruning while avoiding overreliance on a potentially mis-specified GP bound.
- The analysis attempts to make explicit how the tightness of UCB and the “infinite-metric exploration loss” affect rates, yielding some interpretability.
- Empirical results (as described) suggest favorable performance versus SOO/BaMSOO/GP-EI/PI across several benchmarks.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and clarity
- Theorem 1 statement appears incorrect: λ is written as λ = 3 − α/(2CD ρ̄t) < 1, which is generally >1. Given the following exponential form, it seems λ should be 3^(−α/(2CD ρ̄t)). This is a critical typo in the main claim.
- The regret bound depends on N + Ngp, where Ngp is the number of “GP evaluations” (skipped objective evaluations with GP-based labels). Counting internal GP computations toward sample complexity is nonstandard and problematic; one could inflate Ngp by design without paying evaluation cost. Guarantees should be stated in terms of the actual number of objective evaluations N.
- The high-probability UCB property f(x) ≤ μ(x|D)+ςσ(x|D) is invoked with ςM = sqrt(log(π^2 M^2 / (12η))) but no assumptions are stated to justify it. Standard results require either (i) f is a draw from the GP with fixed hyperparameters, or (ii) f lies in the RKHS with bounded norm and β_t depends on the information gain. Here, hyperparameters are re-estimated every iteration via empirical Bayes, invalidating standard UCB guarantees. The paper even notes that f(x) ≤ U(x|D) “did not hold many times” in experiments. This undermines the probabilistic statements central to the analysis.
- The “unknown semi-metric” assumptions reduce to f(x*) − f(x) ≤ L||x−x*||_p^α (Hölder continuity). Exponential rates under these assumptions typically require always diving along a single path (ρ̄t = O(1)), which the paper does not prove. The main rate depends on ρ̄t but no nontrivial bound on ρ̄t is provided; thus the exponential claim is conditional and not guaranteed by the algorithm as written.
- The definition and use of ρt/ρ̄t are confusing and sometimes inconsistent (e.g., “ρτ can be computed as ρt = …”). The argument “ρ̄t can increase up to O(N/t)” and the resulting bounds contain unclear steps, including a stray “1” footnote marker embedded in the math.
- The update rule for Ξ is odd and possibly erroneous: “Ξ ← Ξ + 22” (do you mean +2^2=+4?) and “Ξ ← max(Ξ − 2−1, 1)”. Ξ is supposed to be an integer, but you subtract 1/2. The role of Ξ in the analysis (Ξn ≤ Ξ) is not clearly tied to these heuristics.
- Pseudocode inconsistencies: in step (iii) lines, z(h,i*h) compares to g(ch+ξ, i*h+ξ), but i*h+ξ is defined as “smallest positive integer s.t. i*_(h+ξ) ≠ ∅,” which need not be a descendant of ch,i*h; the notation T′_{h+ξ}(ch,i*h) vs indices is unclear. The while loop in steps (i)-(ii) “resolves” GP-based dummy values by immediate evaluation, but the logic for when previously assigned GP-based g-values are revisited/updated is under-specified.
- The main rate remark claims “still better than known results” even when UCB is “useless,” but the bound becomes very weak and the comparison is not rigorous.

Experimental concerns
- Anonymity violation: a public code link to a non-anonymized lab URL appears in the main text.
- The GP-UCB baseline is not included; GP-PI/EI are run with SOO-based and gradient-based auxiliary optimization, which is nonstandard and may disadvantage them. Details are insufficient for reproducibility (e.g., restarts, budgets, tolerances).
- Hyperparameters are globally fixed initially and then learned per iteration; this makes the already unproven UCB condition even less credible. The paper acknowledges UCB violations in Rosenbrock2, which contradict the supposed 1−η guarantee used in the theory.
- Missing/unclear reporting: the referenced Figure 1, Figure 2, and Table 1 are not available here; the text refers to specific diagrams and timing results without sufficient detail. Deterministic algorithms are reported as single runs, while stochastic ones are averaged; this is fine, but the setup and seeds for randomness are not fully specified.

Presentation and formatting
- Numerous typos and notation issues: mismatched parentheses, spacing, inconsistent indices (t, τ), inconsistent use of bold/roman, missing equation numbers, stray footnote marker, inconsistent capitalization of acronyms, and nonstandard notation (e.g., ς for UCB parameter).
- Bibliography entries are incomplete (missing years/venues), inconsistent, and in several cases ambiguous; some key related work (HOO/POO/StoSOO variants) could be cited and contrasted more carefully.
- The paper repeatedly asserts “exponential convergence” without sufficiently emphasizing that it hinges on unproven bounds on ρ̄t and on UCB correctness.

Pros
- Interesting hybrid idea: use GP to reduce exploration while not committing to a single bound.
- Potentially practical algorithm that avoids expensive inner optimization of acquisitions.
- Attempts to analyze the benefit of “infinite metrics” explicitly.

Cons
- Central theorem likely misstated and depends on questionable assumptions (UCB validity with learned hyperparameters; dependence on Ngp).
- No bound on ρ̄t; exponential claim is conditional and not established in general.
- Many technical and editorial inconsistencies; pseudocode and parameter updates contain apparent errors.
- Anonymity breach and insufficient experimental detail.

Recommendation: Reject
While the idea is promising, the current version has substantial theoretical and presentation flaws that compromise confidence in the main claims. I encourage the authors to (i) correct Theorem 1 and remove Ngp from the complexity term or justify it rigorously, (ii) clearly state assumptions under which UCB holds (and avoid changing hyperparameters in the proof), (iii) provide a nontrivial bound on ρ̄t or temper claims about exponential rates, (iv) fix pseudocode and parameter update inconsistencies, (v) anonymize code and strengthen/clarify the experimental setup, and (vi) thoroughly revise the writing and references.
