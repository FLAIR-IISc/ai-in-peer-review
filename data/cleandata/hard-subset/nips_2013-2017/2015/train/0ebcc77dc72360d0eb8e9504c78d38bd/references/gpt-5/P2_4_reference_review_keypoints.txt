- Proposal: IMGPO combines hierarchical infinite-metric partitioning with GP-UCB screening to avoid δ-cover sampling and aims for exponential simple-regret rates.
- Core idea: blend SOO/DOO/DIRECT-style search over unknown Hölder bounds with GP UCBs to defer or skip true evaluations via surrogate values.
- New concept: introduces infinite-metric exploration loss ρt to quantify cost of not committing to a specific smoothness bound.
- Main theoretical claim: Theorem 1 states an exponential regret bound O(λ^{N+Ngp}) adapting to best Hölder parameters a posteriori.
- Empirics: IMGPO generally outperforms or matches SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI and is faster on low-dimensional tests and a 1000D REMBO embedding.
- Positive: Algorithm is well-motivated, precisely described with pseudocode, and analysis introduces useful quantities (ρt, Ξ) connecting GP pruning to search cost.
- Concern — UCB validity: Theoretical analysis assumes uniform f(x) ≤ U(x|D) high-probability without stating required assumptions (GP sample, RKHS norm bound), and empirical Bayes updates may invalidate coverage.
- Concern — theorem clarity: Notational/typo issues (e.g., malformed λ expression) and ill-defined constants (C, ρ̄t, Ξn) obscure the result and its dimensional dependence.
- Concern — hyperparameter learning: Using empirical Bayes after each iteration likely breaks UCB concentration and is not addressed in theory.
- Experiments lacking diagnostics: Missing Ngp/N, empirical trajectory of ρ̄t, and sensitivity analyses to η and Ξmax; REMBO results need success rates over random embeddings.
- Clarity issues: Several math typos/ambiguities (λ, ςM, T′h+ξ), unclear practical complexity of “fully expanded tree” in step (iii), and confusing statement of Assumptions 1–2.
- Suggestion: Explicitly interpret Assumption 1 as Hölder continuity at x* and relate p, α to standard smoothness classes.
- Originality: Novel synthesis of infinite-metric search with GP UCB and the ρt concept, but some elements are close to BaMSOO; exponential-rate claim is the main novelty.
- Significance: Potentially notable if exponential guarantee holds under realistic assumptions; current impact limited by unclear UCB assumptions and notation.
- Pros summary: Addresses impractical δ-cover, conceptually clean GP + infinite-metric synthesis, and promising empirical performance with code promised.
- Cons summary: Theoretical reliance on unsupported UCB event, opaque notation/constants, and incomplete experimental diagnostics and failure-mode discussion.
- Requests to authors: (1) State precise conditions guaranteeing f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with η under online hyperparameter learning; (2) fix/clarify λ and define all constants and typical magnitudes of ρ̄t and Ξn; (3) report Ngp/N, empirical ρ̄t, sensitivity to η/Ξmax, and REMBO success rates; (4) detail computational cost and implementation of step (iii) and typical Ξn sizes.
- Minor comments: Fix typos/notation glitches, cite HOO/DOO re near-optimality dimension, and clarify acquisition optimization for baselines.
- Recommendation: Weak reject due to gaps in theoretical assumptions and clarity, with potential for acceptance after revisions and stronger diagnostics.
