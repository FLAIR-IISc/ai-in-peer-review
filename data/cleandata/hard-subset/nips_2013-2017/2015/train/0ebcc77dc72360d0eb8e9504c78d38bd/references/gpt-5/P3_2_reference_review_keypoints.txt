- Paper proposes IMGPO, combining GP-UCB with DIRECT/SOO-style hierarchical partitioning to hedge over an infinite family of semi-metrics for deterministic global optimization.  
- Assumes existence of a global optimizer x* and an unknown semi-metric l satisfying a Hölder-type condition; uses GP-UCB to avoid unnecessary evaluations.  
- Main theoretical claim: exponential simple regret bound O(λ^{N+N_gp}) (λ<1) without δ-cover sampling from de Freitas et al. (2012).  
- Empirical results show IMGPO often outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on standard test functions.  

Strengths:
- Addresses an important gap by aiming to recover exponential rates without impractical δ-cover sampling.  
- Conceptual novelty in “infinite-metric” exploration and formalizing infinite-metric exploration loss ρ_t.  
- Practical algorithmic design: lazy UCB evaluations, adaptive lookahead depth Ξ with cap Ξ_max, no auxiliary acquisition optimization, and code provided.  
- Clear relation to DIRECT/SOO/BaMSOO and alignment with DOO/SOO assumptions.  

Weaknesses / Technical concerns:
- Theorem 1 contains a likely typo/incorrect λ expression and inconsistent constants; many notational slips reduce clarity.  
- Missing explicit modeling assumptions required for GP-UCB validity (GP sample path or bounded RKHS norm); β/ς_t must be set accordingly.  
- Exponential rate depends on unquantified ρ̄_t and Ξ_n; worst-case ρ̄_t can degrade, weakening the headline claim.  
- Step (iii) complexity can be large (evaluating 3^ξ UCBs); no time-complexity analysis or practical guidance for Ξ_max.  
- Pseudocode and variable usage are confusing (Ξ update inconsistencies, overloaded n/N/t, unclear T’ and ξ search).  
- Experimental comparisons omit DIRECT and GP-UCB baselines and stronger acquisition optimization methods; ablations and more high-dim benchmarks are lacking.  

Suggestions for improvement:
- State and justify assumptions ensuring f(x) ≤ μ+βσ (GP sample path or RKHS bounded norm) and adjust ς/β accordingly or condition results on UCB validity.  
- Correct Theorem 1 (λ expression), define all constants, and include a high-level proof sketch in the main text.  
- Provide bounds or empirical characterization of ρ̄_t (e.g., under GP prior or posterior concentration) and guidance/analysis for Ξ_max scheduling and time complexity.  
- Clarify and clean pseudocode (Ξ updates, T’ construction, consistent variable names).  
- Add baselines (DIRECT, GP-UCB), ablations (without GP pruning, known smoothness), and report wall-clock time.  

Pros (summary):
- Novel and principled integration of GP pruning with infinite-metric optimistic optimization; practical and theoretically ambitious.  

Cons (summary):
- Key theoretical and clarity issues (GP-UCB assumptions, λ typo), unquantified dependence on ρ̄_t, and incomplete computational/experimental analysis.  

Overall recommendation:
- Promising and potentially impactful, but acceptance should be conditional on fixing theoretical gaps (GP assumptions, Theorem 1) and improving clarity and experiments.
