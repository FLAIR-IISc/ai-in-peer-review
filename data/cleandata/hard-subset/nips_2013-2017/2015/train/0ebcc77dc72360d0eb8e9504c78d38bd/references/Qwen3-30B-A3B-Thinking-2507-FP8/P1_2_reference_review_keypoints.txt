- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm that attains exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling by considering an infinite family of semi-metrics.  
- Main theoretical result: Theorem 1 gives an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, improving on prior polynomial bounds.  
- Novel conceptual idea: use infinitely many possible bounds instead of relying on a single GP-provided bound.  
- Strong experimental validation: IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on multiple benchmarks (except for one case with a misleading GP prior).  
- Clear discussion of limitations, including scalability and trade-offs between reducing ρ̄t and increasing Ξn.  
- Missing detailed intuition in the main paper about the relationship between the semi-metric and the GP UCB; full proofs are relegated to the supplement.  
- Scalability concern: λ approaches 1 as dimensionality increases, and the paper lacks concrete practical remedies for high-D problems.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Ξmax is fixed to 22 in experiments but its effect on performance isn’t thoroughly quantified.  
- Limited comparisons: paper does not compare against some more recent Bayesian optimization methods (e.g., Kandasamy et al. 2015, Wang et al. 2017).  
- Clarity is generally good; pseudocode is helpful, but technical links (semi-metric ↔ UCB, ρ̄t’s practical role) need clearer exposition for non-experts.  
- Originality: the infinite-bound approach is a significant departure from GP-UCB and BaMSOO and constitutes a novel contribution.  
- Significance: solves an important open problem and has potential to influence future BO research.  
- Suggested improvements: explain semi-metric vs GP UCB relation, propose scalability strategies, quantify Ξmax effects, add comparisons to recent work, and clarify how Ξmax affects ρ̄t and regret.  
- Overall verdict: technically sound, well-supported by theory and experiments, minor issues remain but do not outweigh the paper’s contributions — recommendation to accept.
