- Summary: Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization claiming exponential regret O(λ^(N+Ngp)) without δ‑cover by considering infinitely many bounds and using hierarchical partitioning; shows favorable empirical results.

- Strength — Theoretical contribution: Establishes an exponential regret bound for a GP method without δ‑cover and introduces the infinite-metric exploration loss concept.

- Strength — Novel perspective: Argues and analyzes benefits of not committing to a single (possibly loose) GP bound (Remarks 1–3).

- Strength — Experimental comparison: Compares against SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI using a fixed isotropic Matern kernel and hyperparameters, reflecting practical settings.

- Strength — Practical implementation: Provides motivation and pseudocode enabling reproducibility.

- Weakness — Theoretical soundness: Exponential convergence hinges on λ = 3 − α/(2CDρ̄_t) < 1, but paper doesn’t provide conditions ensuring λ < 1 or characterize ρ̄_t.

- Weakness — Ambiguous regret bound: Unusual definition of ρ̄_t (max of averages) makes the O(λ^(N+Ngp)) bound hard to interpret and its scaling with N unclear.

- Weakness — Experimental limitations: No concrete runtime comparisons and no statistical significance tests for reported performance differences.

- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: Fixed Ξ_max = 22 lacks justification and guidance; effect of Ξ_max on performance is not analyzed.

- Weakness — Scalability concerns: λ may approach 1 as dimension D increases; limited evidence on true high‑dimensional scalability beyond REMBO-wrapped test.

- Weakness — Comparison with baselines: Does not clearly compare regret bound forms (e.g., BaMSOO’s polynomial bound vs IMGPO’s claimed exponential bound).

- Theoretical critique 1: The λ < 1 condition is problematic (e.g., ρ̄_t=1 can yield λ>1 for small D); no sufficient conditions given.

- Theoretical critique 2: Constant C is vaguely defined and not tied to the specific problem/setup.

- Theoretical critique 3: Regret bound depends on unknowns L, α, p with no estimation or practical guidance.

- Algorithm critique 1: Notation and steps unclear (e.g., meaning of ξ and T'_h+ξ).

- Algorithm critique 2: Role and effect of the “GP‑based” label in the pseudocode are not clearly explained.

- Algorithm critique 3: Apparent contradiction between the textual description of skipping evaluations when UCB < best and the pseudocode’s evaluation logic.

- Experimental critique 1: Claim of IMGPO being “much faster” is unsupported by measured runtimes.

- Experimental critique 2: No explanation why Ξ_n did not reach Ξ_max for some benchmark functions.

- Experimental critique 3: 1000‑D result relies on REMBO as a subroutine, so it doesn’t directly validate IMGPO’s native scalability.

- Overall assessment: Novel and promising theoretical idea but exponential convergence claim is not fully substantiated due to gaps around λ and ρ̄_t; experiments are supportive but need stronger validation.

- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on analyses/clarifications of ρ̄_t scaling, λ’s relation to practical convergence, runtime comparisons, and guidance for choosing Ξ_max.

- Final comment: Addresses an important open problem with novel analysis, but requires tighter theoretical justification and more thorough experiments to be a major contribution.
