- Presents IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: combine GP-derived UCB bounds with the possibility of a tighter, unknown semi-metric and hierarchical partitioning.  
- Theoretical result: proves exponential convergence rate under Assumption 1 (unknown semi-metric).  
- Empirical validation: outperforms competing methods on diverse benchmarks with simple hyperparameter settings.  
- Removes impractical δ-cover sampling requirement from prior theoretical work.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. and contrasts with Wang et al.; relates to DIRECT, SOO, and bound-based search literature.  
- Novelty: simultaneous exploration using GP estimates and multiple potential bounds (infinite-metric idea).  
- Practical relevance: more applicable to real problems due to elimination of δ-cover sampling.  
- Clear presentation and helpful figures (e.g., Figure 1) explaining hierarchical partitioning.  
- Provides useful discussion and insights in Remarks 1–3 about algorithm behavior.  
- Scalability concern: convergence rate may degrade in high dimensions (parameter can approach one).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: limited analysis, Ξmax impact only briefly discussed.  
- Strong assumption: unknown semi-metric (Assumption 1) is critical and may not hold broadly.  
- Comparisons: lacks evaluation against more recent Bayesian optimization advances (multi-fidelity, adaptive acquisitions).  
- Implementation: division procedure (Algorithm 1) lacks some implementation detail and clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, practical algorithm, strong empirical performance, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high-dimensional scalability not fully addressed, reliance on semi-metric assumption, incomplete state-of-the-art comparisons.  
- Overall verdict: reviewer recommends "strong accept" pending addressing scalability and comparative evaluation concerns.
