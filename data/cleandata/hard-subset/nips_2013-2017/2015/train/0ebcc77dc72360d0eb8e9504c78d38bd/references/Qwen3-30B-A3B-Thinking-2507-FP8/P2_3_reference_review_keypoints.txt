- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling by using an unknown semi-metric (infinite-metric) assumption.  
- Combines GP UCB with infinitely many possible bounds and hierarchical partitioning to guide search; proves regret O(λ^(N+N_gp)) with λ < 1.  
- Strong technical quality: rigorous theoretical analysis that improves over BaMSOO’s polynomial regret to exponential.  
- Remarks provide insight on the GP’s role, UCB tightness, and why relying on infinitely many bounds helps.  
- Comprehensive experiments on eight functions across dimensions 1–1000, comparing to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.  
- Authors acknowledge cases where the GP prior is misleading (e.g., Rosenbrock2) and include an ablation study on Ξ_max sensitivity.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; algorithm could use a more intuitive explanation of the hierarchical partitioning.  
- Notation is consistent overall, though terms like “infinite-metric exploration loss” could be more intuitive.  
- Original contribution: resolves an open problem from Wang et al. by avoiding δ-cover and not relying on a single GP bound.  
- Connects to broader bound-based search methods (A*, PAC-MDP) and situates work within existing literature.  
- Significant practical implications: exponential convergence can substantially reduce costly function evaluations and shows empirical gains.  
- Limitations: scalability to very high dimensions (λ approaches 1), lack of concrete examples when this is problematic, and Ξ_max tuning required.  
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound, original, significant; suggest adding concrete examples of dimensionality limitations.
