Summary and relation to prior work
The paper introduces IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm for deterministic black-box objectives that aims to achieve exponential simple-regret convergence without the impractical δ-cover sampling of de Freitas et al. and without auxiliary acquisition optimization. IMGPO combines hierarchical partitioning (as in DIRECT/DOO/SOO) with GP UCB-style bounds. The key conceptual move is to “not commit” to a single known smoothness bound: the authors assume the existence of an unknown semi-metric ` that upper-bounds suboptimality near a global optimizer, and then explore across an infinite family of such bounds (“infinite-metric”), using the GP UCB to prune candidates and to insert “GP-based” temporary values at centers to avoid evaluating points that are very likely suboptimal. They define an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt (number of intervals expanded per iteration) and prove a simple regret bound that is exponential in N + Ngp (number of true and GP-based center evaluations), up to factors depending on ρ̄t, a lookahead depth parameter Ξ, dimension D and Hölder parameters L, α, p. Empirically, IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, PI/EI on several synthetic functions.

This work sits at the intersection of optimistic hierarchical partitioning (DIRECT, HOO/DOO/SOO) and GP-based Bayesian optimization (UCB/PI/EI, BaMSOO). It aims to close the gap between the impractical exponential rates requiring δ-covers and practical BO, by using GP information to reduce the exploration overhead of infinite-metric search.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: a clear articulation of “infinite-metric” search (not committing to a single smoothness bound) coupled with GP-based pruning. The definition and use of the infinite-metric exploration loss ρt is insightful.
- Theory: a high-probability simple-regret bound that scales as O(λN+Ngp) (λ < 1), under Hölder-type assumptions around the optimum, and that cleanly exposes how GP affects the bound via Ngp, ρ̄t, and Ξn. The remarks help interpret when GP helps and the role of tightness of UCB.
- Algorithmic practicality: avoids δ-cover sampling and auxiliary global optimization of the acquisition, provides pseudocode, and releases code.
- Empirics: consistent gains over reasonable baselines on a range of deterministic test functions; discussion of computational cost; some sensitivity analysis for Ξmax.

Weaknesses and detailed comments
- Correctness/clarity of the main bound:
  - Theorem 1 presents λ = 3 − α2CDρ̄t, which cannot be < 1 for general values and is dimensionally inconsistent. From the exponential form, one expects λ = 3−α/(2CDρ̄t). Please correct this and check all constants. Several typographical issues appear throughout (e.g., missing brackets/exponents), which obscure the analysis.
  - The role of ρ̄t and Ξn is crucial but left largely implicit. The bound is only informative if ρ̄t is O(1) or at least sublinear in N; otherwise the effective rate degrades. Can you bound ρ̄t (in expectation or high probability) under Assumptions 1–2, at least for typical partitions or under near-optimality dimension assumptions? Absent such control, the exponential claim is fragile.
  - The statement “our bound holds for the best values of the unknown L, α, p” is unusual. The theorem appears to hold for the (unknown) true constants satisfying Assumptions 1–2, not the infimum over all possible triplets. Please clarify what “best” means and whether there is an implicit optimization over constants in the proof.

- Assumptions vs. implementation:
  - The high-probability UCB property f(x) ≤ U(x|D) across all calls typically requires that f lies in the RKHS of the true kernel with a known hyperparameter setting and uses fixed (or confidence-enveloped) hyperparameters. In the algorithm and experiments, hyperparameters are re-estimated by empirical Bayes at every iteration. This can break the coverage guarantee. The paper also acknowledges cases where “the property did not hold many times.” The theory should either assume fixed hyperparameters or explicitly analyze the effect of hyperparameter learning on the UCB validity (e.g., via union bounds over a hyperparameter grid).
  - Assumption 2 imposes global Hölder control of `(x′,x)` by a norm. In effect this implies f(x*) ≤ f(x) + L||x − x*||α globally, a fairly strong growth assumption. Please discuss whether the analysis could be localized (e.g., only in a basin around x*), and how this compares to near-optimality dimension assumptions used in HOO/DOO/SOO.

- Algorithmic details needing clarification:
  - Step (iii) lookahead: computing z(h,i*) as the max U over T′h+ξ requires a virtual full expansion; the complexity is controlled by Ξmax but can still be large in D. Please provide complexity analysis in terms of Ξmax, D, and the partition scheme.
  - The “GP-based” label logic guarantees eventual evaluation when a rectangle becomes competitive, which preserves correctness even if UCB underestimates. It would help to formalize this safeguard in the analysis (e.g., show that miscalibrated UCBs cannot cause infinite deferral).
  - Parameter Ξ updates (line 38) are hard to parse; 22 likely means 2^2. Please fix notation and justify the heuristic.

- Empirical evaluation:
  - Baselines: it would be informative to include DIRECT and CMA-ES (or other strong global optimizers for deterministic settings). For BO, UCB should also be included alongside EI/PI, and the acquisition optimization should use standard continuous optimizers (multistart L-BFGS, DIRECT), not SOO, to avoid biasing the comparison.
  - Robustness: given the theory’s sensitivity to UCB calibration, include ablations showing IMGPO with and without step (iii), and with fixed vs learned hyperparameters. Reporting ρ̄t, Ngp, and Ξn over time would connect the experiments to the theory.
  - High dimension: the 1000-D REMBO experiment is encouraging but outside the scope of the provided theory. Please clarify that this is an illustration and avoid over-claiming scalability.

- Writing/organization:
  - Several equations have typos or missing symbols; Figure/Table references are hard to follow without the supplement (e.g., Table 1). Please proofread carefully and ensure all quantities are defined before use.

Quality
The algorithmic idea is sound and the overall method is carefully engineered; the analysis, while promising, rests on assumptions that are stronger or mismatched to the implementation (hyperparameter learning), and key rate-determining quantities (ρ̄t, Ξn) are not controlled. Experiments are positive but could be more comprehensive and better tied to the theory.

Clarity
Readable at a high level, but the paper suffers from typographical errors in equations, ambiguous constants, and under-specified complexity of step (iii). Pseudocode is helpful; more intuition and a small 2D schematic would further aid understanding.

Originality
The combination of infinite-metric selection with GP-based pruning and the explicit accounting of ρt are novel. The method builds on DIRECT/SOO and GP-UCB/BaMSOO, but the synthesis and analysis constitute a meaningful advance.

Significance
If the exponential-rate claim can be made robust (especially w.r.t. hyperparameter learning and control of ρ̄t), this is a significant contribution for deterministic BO. Even as-is, the ideas are likely to influence practical hybrid BO/partitioning methods.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Clarify and correct Theorem 1 constants (especially λ) and provide any nontrivial upper bounds on ρ̄t and Ξn.
- State explicitly the conditions under which the UCB property holds with learned hyperparameters, or restrict the theorem to fixed hyperparameters and discuss the mismatch with experiments.
- Add baselines (DIRECT, CMA-ES, GP-UCB) and ablations (no GP, no step (iii), fixed hyperparameters). Report ρ̄t, Ngp, Ξn trajectories.
- Provide complexity analysis for step (iii) and guidance for choosing Ξmax.
- Improve notation and fix typos throughout.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel and practically motivated algorithmic idea with insightful analysis.
- First attempt to reconcile exponential simple regret with practical GP-based BO without δ-covers.
- Positive empirical results; code released.

Arguments against acceptance
- Theoretical guarantees hinge on assumptions not met in the implementation (hyperparameter learning) and leave critical quantities uncontrolled.
- Typos/clarity issues in the main theorem; some claims are ambiguous.
- Empirical baselines and ablations are insufficient to fully substantiate the claims.

Overall, this is a promising contribution; with clarified/corrected theory and stronger experiments, it would be a strong paper.
