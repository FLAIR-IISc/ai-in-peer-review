- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: consider many possible Lipschitz-like bounds and use the GP to filter out overly restrictive ones instead of relying solely on GP uncertainty.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis demonstrating exponential convergence.  
- Removes reliance on computationally expensive δ-cover sampling, improving practical applicability.  
- Empirically outperforms baseline algorithms on benchmark functions, with controlled kernel/hyperparameter tuning.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. and contrasts with Wang et al., situating itself among bound-based search methods and connections to DIRECT.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant and advances GP-based Bayesian optimization.  
- Novel approach that shifts focus from finding an optimal bound to leveraging imperfect bounds via the GP.  
- Practical relevance: more applicable to real-world problems by avoiding δ-cover sampling.  
- Clear writing, logical organization, and helpful pseudocode (Algorithm 1).  
- Includes detailed analysis and discussion of GP tightness, infinite-metric benefits, and future directions.  
- Algorithm complexity: multiple parameters (Ξmax, η) and hierarchical partitioning may be hard to implement and tune.  
- Scalability concerns: theoretical analysis indicates potential issues in high dimensions; experiments use REMBO up to 1000 dims but more mitigation strategies are needed.  
- Parameter sensitivity: impact of Ξmax not fully explored; trade-offs between performance and computational cost require deeper analysis.  
- Computational cost: relative running time vs. baselines is not thoroughly discussed.  
- Dependency on GP quality: method reduces but does not eliminate reliance on the GP providing useful information; robustness to poor GPs needs study.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theory, exponential rate, practical removal of δ-cover sampling, supportive empirical results, and insightful analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: algorithm complexity may hinder adoption, unresolved scalability, and insufficient analysis of parameter sensitivity and computational cost.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with compelling theoretical and empirical contributions; weaknesses are addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, including more detail on computational complexity, clearer explanation of GP–partition interplay (with examples), thorough analysis of Ξmax, and discussion of high-dimensional scalability strategies.
