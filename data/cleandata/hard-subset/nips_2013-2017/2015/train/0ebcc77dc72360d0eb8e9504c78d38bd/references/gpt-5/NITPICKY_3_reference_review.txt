Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a hierarchical partitioning algorithm that integrates GP-based UCB with an “infinite set” of candidate bounds encoded by an unknown semi-metric on the domain. The key idea is to avoid relying on a single, potentially loose UCB bound by simultaneously considering all possible upper bounds implied by continuity around the global optimizer and using the GP to prune candidates. The authors define an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt to quantify the extra exploration due to not committing to one bound, and prove a claimed exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp) (λ<1) without δ-cover sampling and without solving an auxiliary continuous optimization for the acquisition. The method is compared empirically to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on several synthetic benchmarks, with IMGPO typically outperforming.

This line of work relates to: (i) GP-UCB and deterministic GP bandits (Srinivas et al.; de Freitas et al. for exponential rates under δ-cover), (ii) partition-based optimistic optimization (DOO/SOO, Munos), and (iii) DIRECT-style schemes that avoid a fixed Lipschitz constant (Jones et al.). The proposed approach sits between GP-UCB (single bound) and SOO/DIRECT (bound-free), attempting to leverage both.

Strengths
- Interesting conceptual blend: using GP-UCB solely for pruning while keeping a partition-based search that hedges over unknown smoothness. The “infinite-metric exploration loss” is a neat lens to analyze this.
- Avoids δ-cover sampling and auxiliary acquisition optimization, addressing practical issues raised by de Freitas et al. and Wang et al.
- Empirical results (as described) suggest favorable performance across several functions and a REMBO-based high-D example.
- Providing a concrete algorithm and pseudo-code is useful to the community.

Major weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and clarity:
- Theorem 1 has inconsistent notation and an apparently incorrect definition of λ. You write rN ≤ const · exp(−α[(N+Ngp)/(2CDρ̄t) − Ξn − 2] ln 3) = O(λN+Ngp) but also define λ = 3 − α2CDρ̄t < 1. The latter is dimensionally and numerically nonsensical (could be negative or >1). Please reconcile; likely λ should be 3−α/(2CDρ̄t) or 3−α/(CDρ̄t) in the exponent.
- Assumption 1 (“unknown semi-metric”) is underspecified. What properties of the semi-metric do you require (symmetry, separation, triangle inequality)? As stated, it risks being vacuous. Assumption 2 (Hölder-type upper bound with L, α, p) effectively provides the smoothness; then Assumption 1 adds little. Please tighten definitions and state exactly what is assumed where.
- The high-probability UCB guarantee is dubious under empirical Bayes hyperparameter learning and possible kernel mis-specification. The analysis claims that with ςM = sqrt(log(π^2 M^2/(12η))), f(x) ≤ μ+ςMσ at all queried points with prob. ≥ 1−η. This is not standard without an RKHS norm bound and fixed hyperparameters; your experiments also show violations (Rosenbrock2). The main theorem depends on this property; the paper must state the precise conditions under which the guarantee holds (e.g., well-specified GP prior, fixed hyperparameters, bounded RKHS norm), or separate the practical algorithm from the theorem’s assumptions.
- Counting Ngp (the number of “virtual” UCB-based placeholders) in the exponent of the regret is unusual. These do not gather information about f; they are optimistic surrogates that you later “resolve.” Why should regret decay depend on Ngp in the same way as on N? The proof sketch suggests pruning efficiency, but this deserves a careful justification; otherwise it incentivizes inflating Ngp to claim better rates.
- The role and update of Ξ, Ξmax are unclear and sometimes nonsensical: “Ξ ← Ξ + 22” and “Ξ ← max(Ξ − 2−1, 1)” while Ξ is declared integer. Step (iii) involves expanding a subtree to depth ξ and computing a max over T′; complexity can blow up to 3ξ (hence the ad-hoc cap). The analysis should reflect this cost; the current discussion minimizes it as “rare.”
- Several definitions are ambiguous or inconsistent: T′h+ξ(ch,i∗h), β = supx,x′ 1/2||x−x′||∞, factors like (3βD1/p)α (is it 3 βD1/p or (3β)D1/p?), and ρ̄t’s dependence on t vs N. Some expressions appear mangled (e.g., Remark 1 footnote, missing parentheses and subscripts).

Experimental methodology:
- Comparisons are not fully fair: IMGPO, SOO, BaMSOO are deterministic single runs; GP-PI/EI are averaged over 50 random initializations. Either average all methods over multiple random seeds (e.g., random tie-breaking or random initial points), or fix a common initial point and report single runs for all.
- You optimize GP-PI/EI acquisitions using SOO or gradient-based local search; these choices can strongly affect baselines. Standard practice uses multi-start L-BFGS or global optimizers; please justify and detail settings for reproducibility.
- Hyperparameters are adapted via empirical Bayes every iteration for all GP-based methods; this invalidates your UCB guarantee in practice. Please either (i) separate theory (fixed, known hyperparameters) and practice, or (ii) provide a robustness discussion/ablation.
- Missing details: number of evaluations per problem, stopping criteria, exact domains, noise level (deterministic?), number of repeats for IMGPO, and full numerical results. The cited Table 1 is not included here.
- The REMBO experiment needs more detail (embedding dimension, bounds, number of random embeddings/trials) to assess robustness.

Formatting, anonymity, and presentation:
- The paper appears to violate double-blind review: the code URL reveals author identity and affiliation.
- Many typos and notation glitches: inconsistent subscripts/superscripts, missing spaces and punctuation, malformed equations (e.g., “3 − α2CDρ̄t”), and inconsistent use of n/N/t. References are incomplete and inconsistently formatted (missing years/venues; “AISTAT”).
- Pseudocode has indexing, set, and typing inconsistencies (e.g., Th updates, integer vs real updates to Ξ).

Originality and significance
- The high-level idea of hedging over unknown smoothness while leveraging GP uncertainty for pruning is interesting, and, if the exponential rate can be established under clear, reasonable assumptions, would be a notable contribution beyond BaMSOO/SOO. However, due to the issues above, the theoretical novelty is not yet convincingly established, and the empirical section, while promising, does not yet meet the bar for rigor and reproducibility.

Questions for the authors
1. Precisely restate Theorem 1 with a corrected λ and all constants, and list all assumptions required for the UCB event to hold.
2. What is the exact definition of “semi-metric” you use? Can you give non-vacuous examples where Assumptions 1–2 hold, and relate them to near-optimality dimension?
3. Justify the inclusion of Ngp in the exponent. Under what circumstances can Ngp grow large without hurting actual information gain?
4. Provide complexity bounds as a function of Ξmax and D for step (iii), and an empirical scaling plot.
5. Re-run baselines with consistent randomization, and include complete numerical tables and standard deviations for all methods.

Pros
- Novel synthesis of GP-based pruning with partition-based optimistic search.
- Attempt at exponential regret without δ-cover or auxiliary optimization.
- Clear algorithmic structure with stepwise explanation.

Cons
- Theoretical statement appears inconsistent; key assumptions unclear or unrealistic as implemented.
- UCB guarantee with empirical Bayes is not justified; experiments contradict the assumption.
- Counting Ngp in the exponent is questionable.
- Experimental comparisons and reporting need tightening.
- Numerous presentation errors and a violation of anonymity.

Recommendation: Reject (until theory and presentation are corrected). With a cleaned-up and correct theorem, clarified assumptions, stronger and fairer experiments, and fixed presentation/anonymity, this could become a strong submission. Confidence: high.
