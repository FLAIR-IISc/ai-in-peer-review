Summary and relation to prior work
The paper tackles global optimization of a deterministic black-box function using Bayesian optimization with Gaussian processes (GPs). It proposes IMGPO, a GP-based algorithm that avoids the impractical δ-cover sampling and the auxiliary continuous argmax in GP-UCB, while claiming an exponential simple-regret rate O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1, where N is the number of function evaluations and Ngp is the number of substituted GP-based “dummy” evaluations.

The key idea is to not rely on a single, potentially loose GP-derived bound. Instead, leverage the existence of an unknown semi-metric ` that upper-bounds suboptimality at the global optimum (Assumption 1), together with a Hölder-type upper bound on ` (Assumption 2). The algorithm uses hierarchical partitioning (DIRECT/SOO-style) and maintains at each depth the most promising cell by center value, further pruned by GP-UCB values on a local lookahead subtree of limited depth Ξ. The “infinite-metric” perspective is formalized via an exploration loss ρt (number of intervals divided per iteration), which quantifies the cost of not committing to any single bound. Theoretical analysis yields an exponential rate in N+Ngp with explicit dependence on dimension D, ρ̄t, and Ξn. The method relates to: DIRECT (infinite set of Lipschitz constants, but only consistency); SOO/DOO/HOO (optimistic hierarchical search with finite-time bounds but polynomial rates); and BaMSOO (SOO + GP-UCB, polynomial rates). The authors claim to resolve an open problem highlighted by Wang et al. (AISTATS’14) by obtaining exponential rates without δ-cover sampling.

On benchmarks (Sin1/2, Branin, Hartmann-3/6, Rosenbrock-2, a function from [22], and a 1000D REMBO embedding), IMGPO generally outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI and GP-EI under a uniform Matern-5/2 kernel and hyperparameter learning.

Quality
- Theory: The main theorem provides a high-probability exponential regret bound under Assumptions 1–2, with constants C, D, β, ρ̄t, Ξn. The proof sketch is not included here, but the structure (hierarchical partitioning with geometric shrinkage per depth and a bound on how often non-optimal branches are expanded via ρ̄t and GP pruning) is plausible. However, there are a few issues that should be clarified:
  - λ definition appears misstated in text (printed as λ = 3 − α/(2CDρ̄t)). From the bound with exp(−α[(N+Ngp)/(2CDρ̄t) − Ξn − 2] ln 3), λ should be 3^{−α/(2CDρ̄t)}.
  - The high-probability guarantee f(x) ≤ U(x|D) relies on a choice of exploration parameter ςM. In practice the algorithm learns GP hyperparameters online; this typically breaks standard uniform-UCB coverage guarantees. The paper even notes cases where the property did not hold. The theorem needs to state the conditions under which coverage is ensured (fixed kernel/hyperparameters; or a robust β schedule that accounts for hyperparameter uncertainty), or else separate the practical variant from the one analyzed.
  - The bound depends on ρ̄t, which is algorithm- and instance-dependent and can be as large as O(N/t). While the remarks give insight, the bound’s interpretability would improve with worst-case upper bounds on ρ̄t under simple conditions, or empirical measurement of ρ̄t vs N to support “useful GP” regimes.
  - Complexity of step (iii): computing z(h,i*) requires up to 3^{Ξ} UCBs per candidate cell; the paper caps Ξ by Ξmax but does not quantify overall computational complexity per iteration in terms of D, Ξmax, and number of depths traversed.

- Experiments: The empirical section is useful but modest. Strengths include: common kernel/hyperparameter initialization across methods; comparison to SOO and BaMSOO; and a high-dimensional REMBO test. However:
  - No comparison to DIRECT (natural baseline given the “infinite metric” positioning).
  - GP-PI/EI optimization is done by SOO + gradient-based local search; consider also standard multi-start L-BFGS or CMA-ES to avoid handicapping these baselines.
  - No ablation studies: IMGPO without GP (to isolate the benefit of GP pruning); effect of Ξmax; effect of mis-specified kernels; and sensitivity to η.
  - The claim of exponential convergence is not empirically illustrated (e.g., linear decrease in log-regret vs N+Ngp with slope consistent with the theory and dependence on D and ρ̄t).
  - In Rosenbrock, the UCB property “did not hold many times.” This undermines the theoretical premise; please either fix β to maintain coverage or report theory-compliant runs separately.

Clarity
- The paper is generally readable but has several notation/typographical issues and dense algorithmic descriptions. Suggestions:
  - Correct λ and ensure all constants (C, β, p, α, ρ̄t, Ξn) are clearly defined where first used.
  - Explicitly state the conditions on the GP prior/hyperparameters for which the UCB coverage property holds. If hyperparameters are learned, specify whether the bound is still valid or provide a separate theorem/assumption.
  - Clarify Assumption 1/2 in terms of Hölder continuity around x*: f(x*) − f(x) ≤ L ||x − x*||_p^α.
  - Provide a succinct complexity analysis in big-O for the per-iteration cost, including UCB calls and updates as a function of D and Ξmax.
  - The pseudocode references and line numbers are hard to follow; consider a more compact high-level algorithm sketch plus a clean appendix version.

Originality
- The combination of hierarchical optimistic partitioning with GP-UCB-derived pruning and the “infinite-metric exploration loss” is novel and well-motivated. Prior work (DIRECT, SOO, BaMSOO) does not provide exponential rates without δ-cover. The analytical framing that explains when not committing to a single bound helps is also interesting.

Significance
- If the theoretical claim holds under realistic GP assumptions, the result is a substantial advance: exponential simple-regret without δ-cover and without inner argmax optimization, addressing a known gap. Practically, the method may become a compelling alternative to GP-UCB/EI in deterministic settings. The dependence on D and ρ̄t suggests scalability limitations; discussing or integrating low-dimensional structure (REMBO/additive models) is promising.

Pros
- Addresses an open problem; exponential regret with GP without δ-cover or inner argmax.
- Clear algorithmic contribution; avoids expensive continuous acquisition optimization.
- Insightful analysis on the role of GP tightness and infinite-metric exploration loss.
- Public code; reasonable experimental comparisons; high-dimensional demonstration via REMBO.

Cons
- Coverage guarantee with learned GP hyperparameters is unclear and appears violated in practice.
- Bound depends on opaque ρ̄t and Ξn; constants and λ typo; clarity issues in notation.
- Limited baselines (no DIRECT), limited ablations and empirical validation of exponential rate.
- Computational complexity of step (iii) and practical tuning of Ξmax not fully discussed.

Questions for the authors
1) Under what precise conditions on GP prior/hyperparameters does f(x) ≤ U(x|D) hold for all UCB calls with probability 1−η? How is this reconciled with hyperparameter learning?
2) Can you report measured ρ̄t and Ngp over iterations to empirically validate Remarks 1–2?
3) How does IMGPO compare to DIRECT on your benchmarks?
4) Can you include ablations (IMGPO without GP, varying Ξmax) and plots of log-regret vs N+Ngp to evidence exponential decay?
5) What is the per-iteration time complexity and memory footprint as a function of D and Ξmax?

Minor comments
- Fix λ definition; define C and β upon first use; ensure consistent norms for p and ∞-norm in β.
- Clean up pseudocode line references and typos (“the the”, “satisfied”).
- Some references are incomplete; ensure correct bibliographic details.

Recommendation
Weak accept. The paper offers a novel and potentially impactful algorithmic idea with a strong theoretical claim addressing a recognized gap, and promising empirical results. However, the theoretical coverage vs hyperparameter learning, clarity/typos, and missing empirical ablations should be addressed for camera-ready. My confidence is moderate.
