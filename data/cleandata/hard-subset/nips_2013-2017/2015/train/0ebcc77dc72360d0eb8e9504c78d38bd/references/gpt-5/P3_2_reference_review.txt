Summary and relation to prior work
The paper tackles deterministic global optimization of a black-box f over a bounded domain using Bayesian optimization with Gaussian processes (GPs). The main contribution is a new algorithm, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), that combines GP-derived upper confidence bounds (UCBs) with a DIRECT/SOO-style hierarchical partitioning and selection rule that effectively hedges over an infinite family of possible “semi-metric” bounds around the global optimum. The authors assume the existence of a global optimizer x* and a (unknown) semi-metric l such that f(x*) ≤ f(x) + l(x, x*) and that l satisfies a Hölder-type condition l(x’, x) ≤ L||x’−x||_p^α (the same weak Lipschitz smoothness as in HOO/DOO/SOO). IMGPO maintains, for each depth in a partition tree, a best center, screens candidates across scales using both the unknown-bound logic (like SOO) and GP-UCB, and uses GP-UCB to avoid unnecessary evaluations by temporarily assigning UCBs as “dummy” center values. Theoretical analysis claims an exponential simple regret bound O(λ^{N+N_gp}) with λ<1, without the δ-cover sampling procedure required by de Freitas et al. (2012). Empirically, IMGPO is compared to SOO, BaMSOO (Wang et al., 2014), GP-PI, and GP-EI on standard test functions; it often outperforms baselines.

Strengths
- Addresses an open gap: de Freitas et al. showed exponential rates with δ-cover sampling, which is impractical; BaMSOO removed δ-cover but achieved only polynomial rates. IMGPO proposes a principled way to use GP information to prune expansions within a partition tree and claims exponential rates under standard weak-smoothness assumptions.
- Conceptual novelty: “infinite-metric” exploration explicitly avoids committing to a single smoothness bound and formalizes the additional cost via an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρ_t. The remarks illuminate how GP-UCB helps by reducing ρ̄_t and increasing N_gp, and when relying on a single bound can be worse.
- Practical algorithmic touches: lazy evaluation via UCBs, adaptive lookahead depth Ξ with a cap Ξ_max, and no auxiliary global optimization of the acquisition. Code is provided.
- Clear relation to DIRECT/SOO/BaMSOO and the bound-based search literature; assumptions are aligned with the DOO/SOO framework.

Weaknesses and technical concerns
- Key theorem and notational issues: The λ definition in Theorem 1 appears wrong (“λ = 3 − α/(2 C D ρ̄_t) < 1” is dimensionally inconsistent and contradicts the subsequent bound). From the stated regret bound, λ should be 3^{−α/(2 C D ρ̄_t)}. Please fix the statement and ensure constants are correct. Several other notational slips (e.g., “2D or 3D−1 function evaluations” without clear context, “square” instead of hypercube) impede readability.
- Assumptions needed for GP-UCB validity: The paper uses ς_M = sqrt(log(π^2 M^2/(12 η))) and claims f(x) ≤ μ(x|D)+ς_M σ(x|D) with probability ≥1−η over the entire run. Standard results require either (a) f is a GP sample path under the specified kernel or (b) f lies in an RKHS with bounded norm and β_t depends on the information capacity γ_t and B (Srinivas et al., 2010). This paper does not state such an assumption, yet builds crucial pruning and the union bound over M on it. The practical experiments also acknowledge GP misspecification (Rosenbrock2), where the guarantee would fail. The theory must clearly state the modeling assumption (GP sample path or bounded RKHS norm) and adjust ς_M accordingly, or make theorems conditional on the event that the UCBs are valid.
- Dependence on ρ̄_t and Ξ_n: The “exponential” rate is in terms of N+N_gp but scaled by ρ̄_t and offset by Ξ_n. No bound or control of ρ̄_t is provided under the stated assumptions; in the worst case, the authors concede it can degrade to O(N/t). As a result, the exponential rate is conditional on GP-UCB being “useful” so that ρ̄_t is effectively constant. This is insightful but weakens the headline claim. It would strengthen the paper to provide explicit upper bounds on ρ̄_t (e.g., in expectation) under the GP prior or under weak Hölder conditions plus mild posterior concentration assumptions.
- Complexity of step (iii): Computing z(h,i*_h) requires a hypothetical full expansion to depth ξ and evaluating UCBs on a 3^ξ set; even with Ξ_max, worst-case cost can be large. There is no time-complexity analysis or practical guidance for Ξ_max beyond a small empirical study. A tighter analysis or heuristic with guarantees would improve the practical relevance.
- Algorithm details need clarification: The update rule for Ξ in line 38 looks inconsistent (“Ξ←Ξ+22” vs “Ξ←max(Ξ−2−1,1)”). Please clarify intended schedules. The notion of T’ and the search over ξ can be described more crisply. Some steps use overloaded variables (n, N, t) that are easy to confuse.
- Experimental evaluation: While the benchmark suite is reasonable and the protocol avoids hand-picking kernels, the comparisons to GP-PI/EI depend on how acquisition optimization is performed (SOO + gradient method); standard multistart L-BFGS or CMA-ES might be stronger. Including DIRECT and GP-UCB would be informative, as well as ablations (IMGPO without GP pruning; IMGPO with idealized known smoothness). The REMBO+IMGPO result is interesting but limited; a few more high-dimensional structured benchmarks would help.

Quality
The paper is technically ambitious and mostly sound, but the GP-UCB assumption gap, λ typo, and clarity issues need attention. Proofs are in the supplement; given the complexity of the bound, a higher-level proof sketch in the main text would aid verification.

Clarity
The paper is generally readable but hampered by typos, inconsistent notation, and a dense algorithmic description. Pseudocode could be streamlined; variables and parameters should be defined once and used consistently. The remarks section is helpful.

Originality
Combining infinite-metric optimistic optimization with GP-derived pruning to recover exponential rates without δ-cover is original to my knowledge, given the DOO/SOO/BaMSOO lineage.

Significance
If the theoretical issues are rectified, the contribution is significant: it advances the theory of GP-based global optimization in the deterministic setting, offers a practical algorithm without auxiliary acquisition optimization, and empirically improves over strong baselines.

Suggestions for improvement
- State and justify the modeling assumptions under which f(x) ≤ U(x|D) holds; adjust ς (or switch to the RKHS β_t form).
- Correct Theorem 1 (λ expression), define all constants, and add a brief proof sketch.
- Provide bounds or empirical characterization of ρ̄_t and guidance for Ξ_max/Ξ scheduling; include time-complexity analysis of step (iii).
- Clarify pseudocode (Ξ updates, T’ construction, use of M, N, n, t).
- Add baselines (DIRECT, GP-UCB) and ablations; report wall-clock time alongside regret.

Pros
- Addresses an important open issue; conceptually neat integration of GP and infinite-metric optimism.
- Theoretical exponential regret under standard weak smoothness.
- Practical algorithm without auxiliary acquisition optimization; code provided.
- Strong empirical results on diverse benchmarks.

Cons
- Missing/unclear assumptions for GP-UCB validity; λ typo; clarity issues.
- Exponential rate hinges on unquantified ρ̄_t; limited analysis of computational cost.
- Experimental baselines and ablations could be stronger.

Overall: promising and potentially impactful, but I recommend acceptance only if the theoretical and clarity issues (notably GP assumptions and Theorem 1 statement) are satisfactorily addressed.
