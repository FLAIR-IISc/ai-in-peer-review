- Proposes IMGPO: a hierarchical-partitioning Bayesian optimization that prunes candidates using GP-UCB and considers an implicit infinite family of unknown bounds.  
- Key idea: avoid committing to one possibly loose bound by using GP posterior to eliminate inferior candidates and skip evaluations via “dummy” GP-based center values.  
- Theoretical claim: exponential simple regret bound O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1 under a local semi-metric/Hölder-type bound around the optimizer.  
- Introduces “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt to quantify overhead from not fixing a single bound and shows GP predictions reduce this overhead.  
- Experiments: synthetic benchmarks (1–1000D, REMBO for 1000D) show IMGPO typically attains lower regret faster than SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI; code available.  
- Contrasts to prior work: achieves exponential rates without δ-cover sampling and without auxiliary acquisition optimization (unlike some prior exponential-rate results).  
- Builds on SOO/DOO/DIRECT partitioning ideas; differs from BaMSOO by using GP-UCB only for pruning and saving evaluations.  
- Analysis combines near-optimality assumptions with GP confidence and the new ρt notion.  
- Strength: conceptually interesting bridge between bound-based GP-UCB and unknown-smoothness partitioning paradigms.  
- Strength: provides an interpretable exponential regret bound that decomposes the role of GP (Ngp, ρ̄t, Ξn) and shows dependence on L, α, p.  
- Strength: practical—simple to implement on partitioning frameworks, evaluation skipping is a useful speedup, code release is positive.  
- Strength: empirically promising across benchmarks and scalable to very high dimensions via REMBO.  
- Weakness: unclear probabilistic assumptions guaranteeing the UCB event (is f a GP sample or in an RKHS ball?), and reliance on union bounds without reconciling γT/information gain.  
- Weakness: exponential rate constants depend on D, C, α, ρ̄t, Ξn; λ→1 as D grows so practical advantage over polynomial rates is not conclusively demonstrated.  
- Weakness: pseudocode has notational inconsistencies and typos; reproduction is hindered and complexity of maximizing over T′ up to depth ξ can blow up.  
- Weakness: Ξmax and Line 38 adaptation are heuristic; need clearer accounting of per-iteration computational complexity, memory, and how Ξ affects bounds.  
- Weakness: experimental baselines (GP-EI/PI) may be implemented unfairly (using SOO+gradient); acquisition optimizer choice can bias comparisons.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of GP hyperparameter sensitivity and kernel misspecification; empirical UCB violations (e.g., Rosenbrock2) need reporting.  
- Weakness: missing experimental details—exact evaluation budgets, stopping criteria, how “dummy” skips are counted, wall-clock times, and ablation studies.  
- Weakness: related-work positioning could be sharpened with explicit comparison to GP-UCB deterministic bounds and other BO methods that avoid heavy auxiliary optimization.  
- Questions for authors: precisely state probabilistic assumptions on f for uniform UCB validity and justify choice of ςM; relate to γT-based analyses.  
- Questions: provide tighter bounds or empirical estimates for ρ̄t and Ξn on benchmarks and report their practical sizes.  
- Questions: give per-iteration computational complexity as a function of D, depth(T), Ξmax and sensitivity of performance to Ξmax and η.  
- Questions: clarify how function evaluation budgets are matched across methods when IMGPO skips evaluations via UCB.  
- Pros summary: novel principled algorithm, first practical GP-based optimizer with exponential guarantee without δ-cover/auxiliary optimization, clear insights via ρ̄t, promising results and code.  
- Cons summary: needs stronger clarity/rigor on UCB assumptions and proofs, better explanation of practical complexity/Ξ, fairer baseline/ablation experiments, and caution on exponential constants in high D.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on clarifications, improved exposition, and stronger experimental methodology.
