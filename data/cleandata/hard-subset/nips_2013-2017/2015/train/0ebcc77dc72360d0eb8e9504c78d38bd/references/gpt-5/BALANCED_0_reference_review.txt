Summary and relation to prior work
The paper tackles deterministic global optimization of a black-box function f over a bounded domain Ω, measuring performance by simple regret. It proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a Bayesian optimization algorithm that combines hierarchical partitioning (à la SOO/DOO) with GP-based upper confidence bounds (UCB) to screen expansions, while conceptually considering “infinitely many” candidate semi-metric bounds around the global optimizer. The core idea is to reduce reliance on a single (possibly loose) bound by leveraging an unknown semi-metric bound near the optimum (Assumption 1) and to use GP-UCB to eliminate candidate intervals that are certainly suboptimal. The algorithm avoids two impractical aspects previously required to achieve exponential convergence with GPs: δ-cover sampling (de Freitas et al., ICML’12) and auxiliary inner optimization to maximize acquisition functions.

The analysis introduces the infinite-metric exploration loss ρt, quantifying extra expansions incurred by not committing to a single bound, and proves a regret bound rN = O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1 under Assumptions 1–2 (a Hölder-type continuity assumption). The paper argues how GP-UCB affects the bound (via Ngp, ρ̄t, and Ξn), and compares theoretically and conceptually to SOO/DOO (Munos, NIPS’11), DIRECT (Jones et al., JOTA’93), GP-UCB (Srinivas et al., ICML’10), and BaMSOO (Wang et al., AISTATS’14). Experiments on standard benchmarks indicate favorable performance versus SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-EI/PI; a high-dimensional example uses REMBO (Wang et al., IJCAI’13) with IMGPO as a subroutine.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: a principled mechanism to hedge over unknown local smoothness by “infinite-metric” selection, combined with GP-UCB to prune; clear connection to SOO/DOO and DIRECT.
- Theoretical ambition: an exponential simple-regret rate without δ-cover and without inner acquisition optimization is a strong claim. The role of ρ̄t gives insight into when GP information is helpful.
- Practical algorithmic ideas: hierarchical partitioning, GP-based skipping of costly evaluations, and adaptive depth control (Ξ) are well-motivated; code is promised.
- Empirical results suggest consistent gains over prior deterministic optimistic optimization (SOO/BaMSOO) on a range of benchmarks.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Correctness/clarity of the main bound: Theorem 1 states λ = 3 − α/(2CDρ̄t) < 1, which appears dimensionally incorrect; from the displayed inequality, λ should be 3−α/(2CDρ̄t). This typo casts doubt and needs correction and careful restatement of constants.
- Confidence bounds under hyperparameter learning: The property f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with probability ≥ 1−η is invoked while GP hyperparameters are re-estimated at every iteration. Standard GP-UCB guarantees assume fixed kernel/hyperparameters (or require additional uniform-over-hyperparameters arguments). The paper does not justify that the stated choice of ςM retains valid coverage with empirical Bayes updates, which is central to both screening and the proof.
- Dependence on ρ̄t: Exponential decay in N holds if ρ̄t is O(1). The paper provides heuristic remarks, but no formal bound on ρ̄t in terms of objective properties or algorithm parameters. Worst-case growth can erode the “exponential in N” claim. Making the conditions under which ρ̄t stays bounded explicit would strengthen significance.
- Algorithmic complexity: Step (iii) requires computing maxima of U over fully expanded depth-ξ subtrees, potentially expensive in high D unless Ξmax is small. Complexity analysis and practical costs are not fully quantified.
- Experimental rigor and fairness: GP-EI/PI are optimized using SOO and a local gradient method rather than standard multi-start quasi-Newton; no DIRECT baseline is included despite strong relevance. Deterministic competitors are run once; only GP-PI/EI have 50 runs with error bars. Ablations (e.g., IMGPO without GP screening; sensitivity to kernel mis-specification; varying Ξmax) would clarify what drives gains.
- Double-blind violation: The code URL appears to reveal author identity and affiliation, which is not appropriate for a double-blind review process.

Quality
- The high-level proof strategy is plausible and aligns with DOO/SOO styles, but typos in Theorem 1 and the absence of a justification for UCB validity under hyperparameter learning are significant technical gaps.
- The introduction of ρ̄t is insightful, yet the lack of concrete bounds makes the claimed exponential rate contingent on unverified behavior.
- Empirical results are promising but could be more comprehensive and better controlled.

Clarity
- Generally readable, with a detailed algorithmic walk-through. However, there are several typographical/notation issues (definition of λ; “Ξ← Ξ + 22”; inconsistent indices; overloading of n/N/Ngp; missing constants C definition). A careful pass to fix these would help.
- The interpretation of Assumption 1 would benefit from context (e.g., equivalence to local Hölder continuity at x*).

Originality
- The combination of infinite-metric optimistic partitioning with GP-based pruning to obtain exponential regret without δ-cover is, to my knowledge, novel and addresses an open problem stated by Wang et al. (AISTATS’14).

Significance
- If the proof gaps are resolved and conditions for bounded ρ̄t are clarified, the result would be a meaningful advance for deterministic BO. The method could influence practice in settings where function evaluations dominate computation and auxiliary inner optimization is undesirable.

Questions and suggestions for improvement
- Please correct Theorem 1’s expression for λ and provide a consistent statement of all constants (C, β) and their roles.
- Justify the high-probability UCB coverage when GP hyperparameters are re-estimated. If needed, state the theorem under fixed hyperparameters and discuss extensions or provide empirical coverage diagnostics.
- Provide conditions (e.g., kernel alignment, near-optimality dimension) under which ρ̄t is bounded, or at least a high-probability bound in terms of problem parameters.
- Add DIRECT and GP-UCB baselines, and an ablation of IMGPO without GP screening to isolate contributions.
- Report wall-clock times per method and iteration; quantify the overhead from step (iii) and the effect of Ξmax.
- Improve reproducibility and double-blind compliance by removing identifying URLs from the submission; provide anonymized supplementary material.
- Clarify the update rule for Ξ (the “+ 22” looks like a typo), and the complexity of computing T′h+ξ.
- Discuss sensitivity to kernel choice/mis-specification and to the η parameter.

Pros for acceptance
- Addresses a recognized open problem; potentially important theoretical and practical contribution.
- Clear algorithmic idea with encouraging empirical results.
- Insightful analysis framework (ρ̄t) connecting GP informativeness to regret.

Cons for acceptance
- Technical correctness concerns (typos in theorem; lack of coverage justification with learned hyperparameters).
- Dependence on an unquantified ρ̄t for the main claim.
- Experimental comparisons and ablations could be stronger.
- Double-blind violation via code link.

Recommendation
Borderline reject. The paper is promising and could merit acceptance after fixing the theorem statement, addressing UCB validity under hyperparameter learning (or restricting the claim), and strengthening empirical comparisons. As it stands, the combination of theoretical and experimental gaps and the double-blind issue prevents me from recommending acceptance at NeurIPS.
