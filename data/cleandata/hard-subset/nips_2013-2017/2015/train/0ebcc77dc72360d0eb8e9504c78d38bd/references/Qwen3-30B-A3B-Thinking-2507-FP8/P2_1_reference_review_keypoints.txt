- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm using an "infinite-metric" idea to consider infinitely many continuity bounds rather than a single GP-provided bound.  
- Introduces an unknown semi-metric to encode local continuity at the global optimizer and uses GP upper bounds to prune candidates.  
- Proves exponential convergence with regret O(λ^(N+Ngp)) (λ < 1) and provides a probability guarantee (1-η).  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound, built on clear Assumptions 1 and 2 and a well-structured main theorem.  
- Experiments on eight functions (1–1000 dims) show IMGPO generally outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.  
- Uses realistic experimental setup (isotropic Matern kernel, blind initialization) and discusses exceptions where GP prior misled performance.  
- Acknowledges practical trade-offs: algorithm is slower than SOO and performance depends on GP bound tightness (Remark 1).  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with helpful figure and detailed pseudocode; theoretical sections are precise for informed readers.  
- Suggests improving intuition around the "infinite-metric exploration loss" ρt and its effect on performance.  
- Novelty: first GP-based method achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling, and provides a new framework explaining benefits of not relying on a single bound.  
- Effectively positions against related work (BaMSOO, GP-UCB, DIRECT) and bridges two lines of global optimization research.  
- Significant contribution with potential broad impact; makes exponential-convergence results more practically applicable and extensible to other bound-based methods.  
- Limitations: theoretical complexity may be hard for some readers, missing comparisons to some recent methods (e.g., sparse GPs), and scalability to very high dimensions not fully addressed.  
- Minor reproducibility concern: claims public source code but no URL provided in the text.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strengths in theory, novelty, and empirical performance outweigh minor weaknesses.
