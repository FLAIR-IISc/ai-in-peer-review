1. Paper proposes IMGPO, a GP-based algorithm claiming exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling, addressing an open problem from Wang et al. (2013).

2. Novelty: combines GP-based methods and bound-based search by considering all possible bounds via an unknown semi-metric.

3. Theoretical strength: proves an exponential regret bound (Theorem 1) stronger than prior polynomial bounds like BaMSOO.

4. Conceptual contribution: introduces "infinite-metric exploration loss" and argues why considering infinitely many bounds can be beneficial.

5. Empirical support: IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on eight benchmark functions, with public code and clear pseudocode.

6. Major theoretical concern: condition for λ < 1 (λ = 3 - α/(2CDρ̄t) < 1) may be unrealistic since it requires very small ρ̄t, otherwise λ > 1 makes the bound meaningless.

7. Practicality issue: λ approaches 1 as dimensionality D increases, raising scalability concerns not sufficiently quantified.

8. Notational/terminology issues: definitions like ρ̄t are confusing and counterintuitive, making analysis hard to follow.

9. Misleading claim: "exponential convergence" is stated as O(λ^(N+Ngp)) even though Ngp ≪ N, so convergence may not be exponential in function evaluations.

10. Unclear link: relationship between the GP prior and the unknown semi-metric ` is not clearly operationalized or explained.

11. Experimental limitations: single isotropic Matern kernel and fixed hyperparameters for all functions may bias results and disadvantage comparative methods.

12. Comparison concerns: using SOO as auxiliary optimizer for GP-PI/EI may be suboptimal and comparisons with BaMSOO are not fully aligned.

13. Missing runtime analysis: claims of IMGPO being faster lack concrete runtime comparisons or complexity analysis.

14. Insufficient scalability discussion: authors do not estimate for which dimensions λ exceeds 1 or how severe dimensionality effects are.

15. Recommendation: clarify theoretical conditions for λ < 1 and show empirical behavior of ρ̄t on concrete problems.

16. Recommendation: improve notation and explicitly state that exponential rate is in N+Ngp, discussing practical implications.

17. Recommendation: expand experiments to test different kernels, include runtime comparisons, and study regret vs. dimensionality.

18. Recommendation: better situate work among related literature, e.g., Wang et al. (2013), and contrast theoretical guarantees.

19. Overall assessment: promising conceptual and theoretical contribution but critical theoretical and experimental issues remain; reviewer recommends weak acceptance conditional on revisions.

20. Final comment: infinite-metric exploration is a valuable framework with potential impact if theoretical concerns and empirical validation are adequately addressed.
