Summary and relation to prior work
The paper addresses deterministic global optimization of a black-box function using Bayesian optimization with Gaussian processes. It proposes IMGPO, a hybrid algorithm that combines hierarchical partitioning (SOO-style) with GP-based upper confidence bounds, while not committing to any single smoothness/Lipschitz bound. The key idea is to assume only the existence of an unknown semi-metric bound at the global maximizer (a Hölder-type condition), and to “hedge” over infinitely many candidate bounds by selecting and refining rectangles across depths, using GP-UCB only to filter out clearly suboptimal regions and to avoid unnecessary evaluations. The paper introduces the “infinite-metric exploration loss” to quantify the extra cost of not committing to a single bound. The main theoretical claim is an exponential simple-regret bound O(λN+Ngp) (λ<1) without the impractical δ-cover sampling of de Freitas et al. and without the auxiliary inner optimization for UCB. The algorithm relates to SOO/DOO and DIRECT, and to BaMSOO (SOO + GP-UCB) which provided only polynomial rates. Empirically, the method is compared to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on standard synthetic benchmarks, plus a high-dimensional setting via REMBO.

Quality (soundness, substantiation)
- Theory: The framework is carefully motivated. The exponential bound hinges on Assumptions 1–2, which amount to a Hölder-type condition around x*. The regret bound is stated in terms of the average infinite-metric exploration loss and a depth parameter Ξn. This is informative and clarifies when GP information helps (by reducing ρ̄t and increasing Ngp), but it also pushes part of the difficulty into hard-to-control terms (ρ̄t, Ξn). A major concern is the use of empirical Bayes to re-fit GP hyperparameters while appealing to a GP-UCB-type high-probability envelope f(x) ≤ U(x|D). Such concentration guarantees typically require fixed kernel/hyperparameters (or careful union bounds over a restricted class). With hyperparameters adapted from data, the UCB validity is not automatic; yet step (v) relies on this validity to safely skip evaluations. The experiments themselves note cases where GP was “misleading,” which undermines the claimed high-probability guarantee in practice.
- Algorithmic specification: The core procedure is described clearly and pseudocode is provided. However, step (iii) requires computing a max-UCB over a fully expanded subtree up to depth ξ, controlled by Ξmax; computational cost and how ξ interacts with guarantees and runtime are only partially addressed. The update rule for Ξ is heuristic.
- Proofs are not included here (in supplement). Given the novelty of the bound, having a short proof sketch or key lemma in the main text would help.

Clarity
The paper is generally readable and well organized. The motivation for “not committing to a single bound” and how GP reduces the set of candidates is clear. Some notation and constants (e.g., C, definition of T′ sets) could be clarified earlier, and there are minor typos. The role and effect of Ξ, Ξmax, and ςM would benefit from more intuition and a brief complexity discussion. The assumption that Ω is a hyperrectangle is standard but the generalization note could be expanded.

Originality
The “infinite-metric” idea—hedging over all potential smoothness/semi-metrics and using GP to prune—is novel relative to SOO/DOO and GP-UCB/BaMSOO. Achieving an exponential simple-regret rate for deterministic BO without δ-covers and without an inner acquisition optimizer is a nontrivial theoretical contribution. The explicit notion of exploration loss ρt is also a useful analytical device.

Significance
From a theory perspective, improving known rates for deterministic BO and clarifying when GP information helps is valuable. Practically, eliminating the inner nonconvex optimization and exploiting cheap UCB evaluations can matter when function evaluations are expensive. However, the dependence of the bound on ρ̄t and Ξn, and sensitivity to GP hyperparameter learning, may limit robustness. The deterministic setting is narrower than noisy BO, and it is unclear how the approach would extend to noise. The authors acknowledge scaling issues with D and suggest structure-based remedies.

Empirical evaluation
The benchmarks are standard and show consistent gains for IMGPO over baselines, with one case where mis-specified GP hurts. Using a fixed isotropic Matérn kernel and EB updates for all methods is fair in spirit, but:
- For GP-EI/PI, performance can depend strongly on acquisition optimization; the paper uses SOO and a gradient-based local method, but details (multi-starts, budgets) matter.
- GP baselines are stochastic (random initializations) while IMGPO/SOO/BaMSOO are deterministic divisions; reporting means and variances is appropriate, but care about fairness is needed.
- No ablation to isolate the effect of the GP filter vs. the infinite-metric selection is shown.
- The computational overhead from step (iii) and U-evaluations is addressed qualitatively; a more detailed wall-clock breakdown would help.

Pros
- Novel algorithmic idea combining SOO-style partitioning with GP-based pruning, avoiding acquisition optimization and δ-covers.
- Exponential simple-regret bound under Hölder assumptions, improving over BaMSOO’s polynomial rate.
- Clear analysis of how GP affects regret via Ngp, ρ̄t, and Ξn, offering insight into when GP helps.
- Solid empirical gains on standard functions; code availability.

Cons
- Theoretical guarantee relies on UCB validity while hyperparameters are adapted via EB; this is a known gap in GP-UCB-style analyses and is critical here because UCB is used to skip evaluations.
- Key terms in the bound (ρ̄t, Ξn) are not controlled a priori; practical guidance is limited.
- Computational cost of step (iii) and choice/update of Ξ are heuristic; more analysis desirable.
- Experiments are limited to deterministic synthetic problems; no robustness study under noise or model mis-specification; limited ablations.

Questions for authors
- Can you formalize conditions under which EB hyperparameter updates preserve the UCB guarantee (e.g., via discretization of hyperparam space and union bounds or PAC-Bayes arguments)?
- How sensitive is performance to Ξmax and the Ξ update rule? Any adaptive stopping criterion for step (iii) with guarantees?
- Can you report wall-clock time broken down into f-evals, U-evals, and GP hyperparameter optimization?
- How does IMGPO behave under small observation noise?

Recommendation
Borderline accept (leaning accept). The paper addresses an important problem with a novel and well-motivated algorithm, advances the theoretical state-of-the-art for deterministic BO, and shows promising empirical results. The main weakness is the gap between the theoretical UCB guarantee and the practical use of EB hyperparameters (acknowledged by the authors), and the somewhat heuristic control of ξ/Ξ. With clarifications and a more cautious statement of the conditions under which the guarantees hold (or an experiment demonstrating robustness to hyperparameter learning), this would make a strong NIPS contribution.
