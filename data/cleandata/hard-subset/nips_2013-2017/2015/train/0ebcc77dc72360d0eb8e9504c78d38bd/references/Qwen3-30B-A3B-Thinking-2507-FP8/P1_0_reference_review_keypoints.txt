- Proposal: Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.
- Key insight: Considers infinitely many candidates for an unknown continuity bound (semi-metric) rather than relying solely on the GP-provided bound.
- Theoretical contribution: Theorem 1 shows regret O(λ^{N+Ngp}) with λ < 1, proving exponential convergence.
- Builds on: Extends ideas from SOO and DIRECT while removing the impractical δ-cover requirement from prior work.
- Strength — theoretical rigor: Provides careful definitions, assumptions, and a well-justified formal relationship between the unknown bound and the GP bound.
- Strength — addressing prior limits: Explicitly overcomes limitations of de Freitas et al. (δ-cover) and Wang et al. (polynomial regret).
- Strength — experiments: Comprehensive empirical evaluation on eight benchmarks across dimensions 1–1000 with appropriate baselines.
- Strength — practical performance: IMGPO outperforms competing methods in most tested cases.
- Originality: The infinite-metric approach is novel and bridges single-bound and multi-bound optimization frameworks.
- Significance — theory: Exponential convergence is a major improvement over prior polynomial GP-based methods.
- Significance — broader impact: Provides a framework explaining when avoiding a single bound is advantageous beyond Bayesian optimization.
- Weakness — Assumption 2: Reliance on semi-metric bounded by L||x'-x||^α_p may be restrictive and lacks empirical justification.
- Weakness — high-dimensional scalability: λ approaches 1 as dimensionality increases and no concrete high-dimensional strategy is provided.
- Weakness — computational complexity: Growth of the infinite-metric exploration loss ρ̄_t and overall complexity are not fully analyzed.
- Clarity — concept explanation: "Infinite-metric exploration loss" is introduced without sufficient intuition.
- Clarity — pseudocode: Algorithm 1 is complex and would benefit from more explanatory comments.
- Clarity — bounds relationship: The interplay between the unknown bound and GP-based bound is not fully explained for practical conditions.
- Significance — applicability: No clear roadmap or experiments for applying IMGPO to very high-dimensional problems (e.g., via REMBO).
- Specific comment 1: Theorem 1 depends on Ξ_n but the paper doesn't clarify how Ξ_n grows with N in practice.
- Specific comment 2: Unclear when the GP-based bound helps versus when it might hurt (effect of GP).
- Specific comment 3: Experimental results lack deeper analysis on why IMGPO excels on some functions (e.g., Sin1/Sin2) but not on others (e.g., Rosenbrock2).
- Specific comment 4: Role and effect of the Ξ_max parameter on performance and CPU trade-offs need more intuition.
- Overall assessment: Strong, technically sound paper making a significant theoretical and practical contribution to Bayesian optimization.
- Recommendation: Accept — the paper advances the state of the art by achieving exponential convergence without impractical assumptions.
- Final thought: A meaningful advancement that bridges bounded and bound-free search methods and is likely to influence future research, despite some scalability and clarity gaps.
