- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm claiming exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling using an "infinite-metric exploration loss" idea.  
- Algorithm combines hierarchical partitioning with consideration of multiple candidate bounds rather than a single GP-UCB.  
- Provides a theoretical regret bound (Theorem 1) claiming exponential convergence and analyzes GP-UCB's effect on regret.  
- Experimental results on eight benchmarks show IMGPO outperforming SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI in most cases.  
- Clear problem statement situating the work within Bayesian optimization and addressing an open problem from prior work.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant: a concrete exponential-rate regret bound and useful remarks on GP-UCB.  
- Experiments consistently demonstrate empirical improvements over baselines.  
- Paper discusses practical concerns about GP-UCB tightness and when to rely on GP information versus multiple bounds.  
- The "exponential convergence" claim is misleading because the rate parameter λ depends on dimension D and approaches 1 as D increases, limiting practicality in high dimensions.  
- Unclear how IMGPO truly avoids δ-cover sampling and how this differs from BaMSOO, which also claims avoidance.  
- Ambiguity in how the assumed "unknown bound" interacts with the GP-UCB filter; relationship lacks precise mathematical formulation.  
- Experiments are limited to low-to-moderate dimensions (1–100) and do not convincingly address high-dimensional optimization or runtime costs.  
- Computational cost and sensitivity to parameters (e.g., Ξmax) are not thoroughly analyzed or reported (no CPU time comparisons).  
- The paper does not clearly articulate why its theoretical bound is fundamentally better than BaMSOO's or how the approaches differ algorithmically.  
- The infinite-metric exploration loss is an interesting and principled idea for balancing GP information and multiple bounds.  
- Practical implementation (Algorithm 1) does not convincingly show exponential convergence in practice given the dimensionality issue.  
- Recommendations: honestly address dimensionality dependence, clarify differences from BaMSOO, provide scalability/runtime experiments, and formalize the unknown-bound vs GP-UCB relationship.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically interesting but overstated claims; scores Quality 6/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 5/10; final recommendation: Weak Reject.
