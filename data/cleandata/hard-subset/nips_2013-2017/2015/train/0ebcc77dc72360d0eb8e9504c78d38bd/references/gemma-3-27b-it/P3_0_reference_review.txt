## Review of "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel Gaussian Process (GP)-based Bayesian optimization algorithm (IMGPO) with a theoretical guarantee of exponential convergence, addressing a key open problem highlighted by Wang et al. (2023). The approach is theoretically sound, well-motivated, and offers a significant improvement over existing GP-based methods by eliminating the need for impractical δ-cover sampling. The experimental results demonstrate strong performance across a range of benchmark functions. I believe this paper is a strong contribution and recommend acceptance.

**1. Summary and Related Work:**

The paper tackles the problem of global optimization of non-convex, black-box functions, a core challenge in machine learning and beyond. Bayesian Optimization (BO) offers an attractive approach, but achieving theoretical convergence guarantees, especially exponential convergence, has been challenging.  De Freitas et al. (2021) showed exponential convergence for BO with GPs, but relied on computationally expensive δ-cover sampling. Wang et al. (2023) built on this work, achieving polynomial convergence but leaving the exponential rate open. 

IMGPO addresses this gap by introducing an "infinite-metric" optimization strategy.  The core idea is to simultaneously consider *all* possible bounds on the objective function, leveraging the GP as a means to filter implausible bounds. This avoids the restrictive assumption of a known Lipschitz constant and the computational burden of δ-cover sampling.  The authors appropriately position their work within the broader landscape of bound-based search methods (Lipschitz optimization, A*, PAC-MDPs) and clearly articulate the limitations of existing GP-based approaches.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The exponential convergence guarantee is a significant result, filling a known gap in the literature. The theoretical analysis appears rigorous and well-presented.
* **Novelty:** The “infinite-metric” optimization approach is a clever and innovative way to circumvent the limitations of existing methods. Considering all possible bounds offers a compelling alternative to relying on potentially inaccurate estimates.
* **Practicality:** Removing the δ-cover sampling requirement makes the algorithm much more applicable to real-world problems where such sampling is infeasible.
* **Experimental Validation:** The paper provides extensive experimental results demonstrating IMGPO’s superior performance on various benchmark functions. The comparison to state-of-the-art methods (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) is comprehensive. The inclusion of a 1000-dimensional problem (leveraging REMBO) showcases the potential for scalability.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and logically structured. The algorithm description is clear, and the motivation behind each step is well-explained.
* **Honest Discussion:** The authors acknowledge the limitations of the approach (scalability to very high dimensions, potential for increased computational cost) and suggest avenues for future work.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the authors address the lack of prior knowledge of kernel hyperparameters by using empirical Bayesian methods, the performance appears somewhat sensitive to the choice of initial hyperparameters and the division procedure parameters (Ξmax). A more detailed ablation study on the impact of these parameters would be beneficial.
* **Scalability Discussion:** While the 1000-dimensional experiment is encouraging, a deeper discussion on the computational complexity of the algorithm and its scalability to even higher dimensions (e.g., thousands of dimensions) is needed. The authors mention leveraging additional assumptions, but providing more concrete details on these assumptions would be helpful.
* **Comparison to Alternatives for High Dimensions:** The comparison to REMBO in the 1000-dimensional case is good, but briefly discussing other high-dimensional BO techniques (e.g., additive GPs, Bayesian Optimization with Neural Networks) and their potential synergies with IMGPO would strengthen the paper.



**4. Evaluation Criteria:**

* **Quality (9/10):**  The paper is technically sound, with a well-defined theoretical framework and robust experimental validation. The analysis is convincing, and the claims are well-supported.
* **Clarity (8/10):** The writing is generally clear, though some sections (particularly the technical details of the algorithm) could benefit from further simplification.
* **Originality (9/10):** The “infinite-metric” optimization approach is highly original and provides a novel perspective on the problem of Bayesian optimization.
* **Significance (9/10):** The exponential convergence guarantee is a significant theoretical advancement, and the removal of the δ-cover sampling requirement makes the algorithm practically relevant.

**5. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Addresses a significant open problem in Bayesian optimization.
* Presents a novel and theoretically sound algorithm.
* Demonstrates strong empirical performance.
* Clear and well-written.

**Against:**

*  Potential sensitivity to hyperparameter choices.
*  Scalability to very high dimensions remains a concern.




**6. Recommendation:**

**Accept.** This paper represents a substantial contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The theoretical results are compelling, the algorithm is innovative, and the experimental validation is strong. The identified weaknesses are minor and do not detract from the overall quality of the work. I am confident that this paper will be of significant interest to the NIPS community.
