Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes IMGPO, a Gaussian-process-based global optimization algorithm for deterministic, black-box functions. The key idea is to combine optimistic, hierarchical partitioning (as in DIRECT/SOO/DOO) with GP-derived upper confidence bounds (UCB) to “filter” which cells to expand, while not committing to a single smoothness/Lipschitz bound. The authors introduce an “unknown bound” assumption expressed via a semi-metric around a global optimizer (a local Hölder-type condition), and define the infinite-metric exploration loss ρt to capture the overhead from considering many possible bounds. They prove a simple-regret bound that decays exponentially in the number of function evaluations plus the number of GP-based “skipped evaluations” (N + Ngp), with a rate constant that depends on dimension D, ρ̄t, and other problem constants; they compare against SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on standard benchmarks, claiming consistent empirical improvements.

This work builds on: (i) GP-UCB and GP-EI/PI for BO; (ii) exponential regret for GP bandits with impractical δ-cover sampling (de Freitas et al.); (iii) optimistic partitioning methods (DIRECT, DOO, SOO) and BaMSOO (SOO + GP-UCB), which attain polynomial rates. The contribution is an exponential simple-regret rate without δ-covers or auxiliary acquisition maximization, plus an analysis clarifying when GP-based UCB is “useful” even if loose.

Quality:
- Theory: The main theorem gives rN ≤ const · exp(−c · (N + Ngp − terms)) and interprets how D, the Hölder parameters (L, α, p), the exploration loss ρ̄t, and a lookahead parameter Ξn affect the constant c. The high-level derivation (exponential diameter shrinkage by depth; linear depth growth in node expansions; filtering reduces wasted expansion) is coherent and aligns with optimistic partitioning analyses.
- However, there are important issues to address for soundness and clarity:
  - The definition of λ appears mistyped: “λ = 3 − α/(2 C D ρ̄t) < 1” seems intended to be λ = 3^{−α/(2 C D ρ̄t)} < 1, consistent with the exponential bound. Please correct.
  - The event f(x) ≤ U(x|D) “with probability ≥ 1−η” is asserted via ςM = sqrt(log(π^2 M^2/(12η))). In standard GP-UCB, such uniform high-probability bounds require fixed kernels and assumptions on function regularity (e.g., RKHS norm constraints), and do not generally hold when hyperparameters are tuned online by empirical Bayes. The paper relies on this event both for safety (skipping evaluations when UCB < best) and for the regret guarantee. The assumptions under which this event truly holds here (deterministic observations; adaptive hyperparameters; misspecified kernels) are not stated or justified. This is the biggest theoretical gap.
  - The bound for rN depends on (N + Ngp). Using Ngp (counts of GP-based “dummy” placements) to strengthen an rN bound can be misleading: rN is evaluated after N function evaluations, so extra GP computations shouldn’t improve the budgeted regret. The authors should either bound rN in terms of N only or clearly justify why decisions influenced by GP-only steps permit dependence on Ngp (and discuss the practical meaning).
  - The “best values of unknown L, α, p” phrasing is confusing. Typically, the bound holds for any triplet that satisfies Assumption 2; you do not get to minimize over them post hoc. Please clarify whether the theorem is uniform over all admissible (L, α, p), or holds with the true Hölder parameters.
- Experiments: The benchmarks are standard and the comparisons reasonable. The results suggest IMGPO is typically stronger than SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-EI/PI under a fixed Matern-5/2 kernel and empirical Bayes. However:
  - There is no ablation showing the contribution of the GP filter (e.g., IMGPO without step (iii)) or the impact of Ξmax and ρ̄t.
  - Complexity: step (iii) can be heavy; empirical CPU times are reported but a complexity analysis or bounds on GP calls per iteration would help.
  - For Rosenbrock2, the authors note UCB fails often; this underscores the concern above about the theoretical event f ≤ U with learned hyperparameters.

Clarity:
- Generally well written, but there are several notation/typo issues (λ as above; several lines in Algorithm 1 need polishing; some variables like g(·) are introduced informally). Please define all symbols precisely and ensure consistent notation.
- The role and update of Ξ and Ξmax need clearer motivation and complexity implications.
- Provide precise assumptions for the theorem (fixed kernel? hyperparameters fixed vs learned? nature of the randomness for the 1−η probability?).

Originality:
- The combination of “infinite-metric” exploration with GP filtering and a proof of exponential simple-regret decay without δ-cover or auxiliary maximization is novel to my knowledge. The infinite-metric exploration loss is an insightful device to quantify the cost of not committing to a single bound.

Significance:
- If the high-probability UCB condition under adaptive hyperparameters can be justified or the theorem reframed to avoid it, this would be a meaningful advance: a practical GP-based method with strong rates and no auxiliary optimization. The analysis (Remarks 1–3) offers useful intuition. However, the dependence on D is unfavorable (λ→1 as D grows), and the use of Ngp in the rate muddies practical implications. Empirically, gains over BaMSOO/GP-EI/PI are encouraging.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel and conceptually interesting algorithmic idea.
- Claimed exponential simple-regret rate without δ-cover or auxiliary optimization.
- Interpretable analysis framework (ρ̄t, Ξn) and discussion of when GP bounds help.
- Solid empirical results on standard benchmarks; code availability.

Cons for acceptance:
- The key high-probability UCB event under adaptive, misspecified GP hyperparameters is not justified; this threatens the main theorem.
- Typo/notation errors in core statements; some ambiguity about constants and assumptions.
- The rate’s dependence on Ngp is nonstandard for fixed-evaluation budgets.
- Limited ablations; computational complexity of step (iii) and Ξmax not deeply analyzed.
- References are incomplete in formatting; some related optimistic methods could be discussed more (e.g., HOO/DOO bounds, DIRECT variants).

Suggestions for improvement:
- State and prove the UCB containment under the exact modeling/learning assumptions used; or separate the theorem into two parts: (a) deterministic bound that does not rely on GP UCB being valid; (b) an improvement when UCBs are valid under explicit conditions (fixed kernel, RKHS assumptions).
- Correct λ and other typos; formalize all notation; clarify the role of Ngp and provide an rN-only bound.
- Add ablations (no-GP filter; various Ξmax; sensitivity to kernel misspecification) and a complexity analysis of step (iii).
- Tighten and complete references.

Overall recommendation: Borderline accept. The idea and empirical results are compelling and of interest to the NeurIPS BO community, but the theoretical guarantee hinges on an unproven high-probability UCB property under adaptive hyperparameters. If the authors can fix the clarity issues and qualify or justify the central probabilistic assumption, the paper would be a strong contribution.
