- Overall: Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based BO algorithm with exponential convergence, addresses a known open problem, and is recommended for acceptance.  
- Problem/Summary: Targets global optimization of non-convex black‑box functions where BO convergence guarantees are hard to obtain.  
- Prior work gap: Existing GP-based exponential rates relied on δ-cover sampling or achieved only polynomial rates.  
- Core idea: Introduces “infinite-metric” optimization—consider all possible bounds and use the GP to filter implausible ones.  
- Avoids assumptions: Eliminates need for known Lipschitz constant and costly δ-cover sampling.  
- Positioning: Well-situated among bound-based search methods (Lipschitz optimization, A*, PAC-MDPs) and clarifies limitations of prior GP approaches.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides a rigorous exponential convergence guarantee, filling a literature gap.  
- Novelty: The infinite-metric approach is original and conceptually clever.  
- Practicality: More applicable to real problems by removing δ‑cover sampling requirement.  
- Experimental validation: Strong empirical performance across benchmarks with comprehensive comparisons (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI).  
- High-dimensional test: Demonstrates potential scalability with a 1000‑D experiment using REMBO.  
- Writing and presentation: Clear, well-structured paper with understandable algorithm description.  
- Honest limitations: Authors acknowledge scalability and computational-cost limitations and suggest future work.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Performance depends on initial kernel hyperparameters and division procedure parameters (Ξmax); needs ablation.  
- Weakness — scalability discussion: Lacks deeper analysis and concrete assumptions for scaling to thousands of dimensions.  
- Weakness — alternatives for high-D: Should discuss or compare to other high-dimensional BO methods (additive GPs, BO with neural nets).  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: Solves a significant open problem, is theoretically sound, novel, empirically strong, and well-written.  
- Rejection arguments: Concerns about hyperparameter sensitivity and scalability to very high dimensions.  
- Final recommendation: Accept — substantial contribution with minor addressable weaknesses.
