- Presents IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling by combining multiple Lipschitz-like bounds with GP uncertainty for global/local search.
- Positions the work within global optimization and Bayesian optimization literature, building on and addressing limitations of de Freitas et al. and Wang et al.
- Theoretical contribution: proves exponential convergence rate without δ-cover sampling, addressing a key open problem.
- Novel idea: simultaneously exploring many potential smoothness bounds via a semi-metric to handle unknown function smoothness.
- Empirical validation: outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and standard GP methods on benchmark functions using fixed hyperparameters for fair comparison.
- Clear presentation: well-written, with helpful Figure 1 and a detailed (if dense) Algorithm 1.
- Insightful analysis of GP bound tightness (Remarks 1–3) clarifying algorithm behavior.
- Reproducibility: authors provide open-source code.
- Algorithm complexity: implementation appears complex, which may hinder adoption and further development.
- Scalability concerns: potential curse of dimensionality and ad-hoc workaround suggestions; more mitigation strategies needed.
- Parameter sensitivity: choice of Ξmax is crucial, experiments show its impact, and the selected value (22) lacks clear justification.
- Implicit assumptions: restricting domain to a hyperrectangle is limiting; generalization mentioned only in a footnote.
- Limited comparative analysis: needs deeper investigation into when IMGPO excels or fails (e.g., why SOO beat it on Rosenbrock2).
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical result, novel approach, strong empirical support, and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: practical concerns over complexity, scalability, and parameter sensitivity.
- Overall assessment: strong and compelling contribution with legitimate but addressable practical concerns.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing justification for Ξmax, generalization beyond hyperrectangles, clearer limitations and failure cases, and future work on scalability.
