- Presents IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm claiming exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.
- Core idea: consider multiple Lipschitz-like bounds implicitly and use the GP to prune candidates rather than rely solely on GP uncertainty.
- Provides theoretical analysis proving an exponential convergence rate for the proposed algorithm.
- Empirical results show IMGPO generally outperforms several baselines, especially when the GP prior is weak.
- Paper is clearly written with helpful figures and a clear algorithm description.
- Addresses a significant problem in Bayesian optimization by removing impractical sampling requirements.
- Theoretical contribution is significant and advances prior work (e.g., de Freitas et al., 2017).
- Novel concept of refining a set of potential bounds via the GP is insightful.
- Convergence rate depends on a parameter (λ) that can approach 1 as dimensionality increases, indicating scalability issues.
- Performance is sensitive to Ξmax (hierarchical partitioning depth), requiring careful tuning.
- Experiments used a single kernel and hyperparameter initialization, limiting empirical robustness.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous but complex and relies on several assumptions; more intuition would help.
- Comparisons lack some more recent and sophisticated Bayesian optimization methods.
- Highly relevant to NIPS given its theoretical and algorithmic contributions to global optimization.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel algorithm, clear presentation, empirical gains, and significance.
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability in high dimensions, hyperparameter sensitivity, and limited comparisons.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
- Recommendations: discuss high-dimensional implications and mitigations, provide guidance or sensitivity analysis for Ξmax, compare to newer methods, and add more intuitive explanations of theory.
