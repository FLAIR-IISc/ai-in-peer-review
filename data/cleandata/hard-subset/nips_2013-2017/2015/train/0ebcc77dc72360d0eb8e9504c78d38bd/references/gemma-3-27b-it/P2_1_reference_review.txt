## Review of "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel Gaussian Process (GP)-based global optimization algorithm (IMGPO) that aims to achieve exponential convergence without relying on the computationally demanding δ-cover sampling procedure required by previous theoretical results (de Freitas et al., 2017). The core idea is to implicitly consider a wider range of possible Lipschitz-like bounds on the objective function, leveraging the GP to prune unpromising candidates, rather than relying solely on the GP's own uncertainty estimates. The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating an exponential convergence rate and empirical results showcasing IMGPO's performance against several baseline methods.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Contribution:** The paper successfully derives an exponential convergence rate for a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm, a significant advancement over previous work that required impractical sampling methods. This is a strong theoretical result.
* **Novel Idea:** The concept of considering multiple potential bounds and using the GP to refine this set is innovative and potentially insightful.  It addresses a key limitation of traditional GP-UCB approaches by acknowledging the difficulty of obtaining a tight bound directly from the GP.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results demonstrate that IMGPO generally outperforms other methods, particularly in scenarios where the GP prior is less informative.  The inclusion of standard benchmark functions provides a reasonable evaluation.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and the algorithm is described clearly. The inclusion of a figure illustrating the algorithm's hierarchical partitioning is helpful.
* **Addresses a Significant Problem:** The paper tackles a long-standing challenge in Bayesian optimization - achieving strong theoretical guarantees without computationally prohibitive requirements.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Scalability Concerns:** While achieving exponential convergence is promising, the analysis reveals that the convergence rate depends on a parameter (λ) that can approach 1 as the dimensionality increases. This suggests potential scalability limitations for high-dimensional problems, though the authors acknowledge this and suggest possible mitigations.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance of IMGPO seems sensitive to the choice of Ξmax, the parameter controlling the depth of the hierarchical partitioning. Finding an appropriate value for Ξmax may require experimentation.
* **Experimental Setup:**  The authors acknowledge using a single kernel and hyperparameter initialization across all experiments. While justified by the aim of simulating a practical scenario, this limits the robustness of the empirical evaluation.  A more thorough hyperparameter tuning process or a sensitivity analysis would be valuable.
* **Complexity of the Analysis:** The theoretical analysis, while rigorous, is quite complex and relies on several assumptions.  A more intuitive explanation of the key mathematical concepts could improve accessibility.
* **Limited Comparison with State-of-the-Art:** While the comparison to several baselines is good, the paper could benefit from a comparison to more recent and sophisticated Bayesian optimization methods beyond those listed.



**Relevance to NIPS:**

The paper is highly relevant to NIPS. It addresses a core problem in machine learning (global optimization) with a novel theoretical and algorithmic contribution. The work builds on existing NIPS papers (e.g., de Freitas et al., 2017; Snoek et al., 2012; Wang et al., 2018) and pushes the state-of-the-art in Bayesian optimization.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical result demonstrating exponential convergence.
* Novel and insightful algorithm design.
* Clear and well-written presentation.
* Demonstrates empirical improvements over established baselines.
* Addresses a significant problem in the field.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Scalability concerns in high dimensions.
* Hyperparameter sensitivity requiring careful tuning.
* Limited comparison to the very latest methods in the field.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The theoretical result is impressive, and the proposed algorithm appears promising. While there are some limitations regarding scalability and hyperparameter sensitivity, these are acknowledged by the authors.  The strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses, and I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.

**Recommendations for Improvement:**

*  Discuss the implications of the convergence rate approaching 1 in high dimensions and elaborate on potential solutions.
*  Provide guidance on selecting an appropriate value for Ξmax, potentially through a sensitivity analysis.
* Consider including a comparison to more recent and sophisticated Bayesian optimization methods.
*  Offer a more intuitive explanation of the core theoretical concepts to improve accessibility.
