## NIPS Review: Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel Gaussian Process (GP)-based global optimization algorithm, dubbed Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), designed to achieve exponential convergence without relying on the computationally expensive δ-cover sampling procedure required by previous theoretical results (de Freitas et al. [1]). The core idea is to leverage the assumption of an unknown, potentially tighter, semi-metric governing the function’s continuity around its optimum, alongside the information provided by the GP. IMGPO maintains a hierarchical partitioning structure, intelligently exploring the search space based on both GP-derived bounds (UCB) and the potential for tighter bounds implied by the unknown semi-metric. The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating an exponential convergence rate and empirical validation showing performance gains over existing methods.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds directly on the theoretical framework of de Freitas et al. [1], addressing the practical limitation of δ-cover sampling. It also acknowledges and contrasts itself with Wang et al. [2], which achieved polynomial regret bounds using GP-UCB with the SOO algorithm. IMGPO aims to surpass these results by regaining the exponential convergence rate without relying on complex sampling schemes or auxiliary optimization procedures. The paper appropriately positions itself within the broader context of bound-based search methods (Lipschitz optimization, A*, PAC-MDP) and acknowledges connections to DIRECT [19] and SOO [18], highlighting the benefit of considering multiple possible bounds.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Guarantee:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis, proving an exponential convergence rate. This is a significant contribution as it establishes a strong theoretical foundation for the proposed algorithm.
* **Novel Approach:** The central idea of simultaneously exploring based on both GP-estimated bounds and the potential for a tighter, unknown semi-metric is innovative and addresses a key limitation of existing GP-based optimization methods.
* **Practical Relevance:** Eliminating the δ-cover sampling requirement makes the algorithm significantly more applicable to real-world problems where such sampling is impractical.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on a diverse set of benchmark functions demonstrate IMGPO's effectiveness and often outperform competing algorithms, even with a simple hyperparameter setting.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the algorithm's mechanics and underlying principles. The inclusion of Figure 1 is particularly helpful for understanding the hierarchical partitioning process.
* **Comprehensive Analysis:** The discussion sections (Remark 1, 2, 3) offer valuable insights into the algorithm's behaviour and the impact of different components.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Scalability Concerns:** The analysis acknowledges potential scalability issues in high dimensions, noting that the convergence rate depends on a parameter that can approach one as dimensionality increases. While the authors suggest leveraging additional assumptions to address this, the discussion remains somewhat limited.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Although the experiments use relatively simple, fixed hyperparameters, the impact of varying Ξmax is briefly mentioned. A more detailed investigation of hyperparameter sensitivity would be beneficial.
* **Assumptions:** The assumption of an unknown semi-metric (Assumption 1) is critical to the theoretical guarantee but may not hold for all functions. Further discussion of the types of functions where this assumption is likely to be valid would be valuable.
* **Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** While the comparison to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI and GP-EI is helpful, a comparison to more recent and advanced Bayesian optimization methods incorporating techniques like multi-fidelity optimization or adaptive acquisition functions would strengthen the results.
* **Implementation Details:** The description of the division procedure in Algorithm 1 could be more detailed and potentially clarify some of the implementation nuances.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution by achieving exponential convergence for GP-based optimization without relying on impractical sampling methods.
* The proposed algorithm, IMGPO, offers a practical and innovative approach to global optimization.
* The empirical results demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness and competitiveness.
* The clear writing and thorough analysis make the paper accessible and insightful.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The scalability concerns in high dimensions are not fully addressed.
* The reliance on the assumption of an unknown semi-metric might limit the applicability of the algorithm.
* The comparison to current state-of-the-art Bayesian optimization methods is incomplete.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept**. Despite the minor weaknesses, the theoretical novelty, practical relevance, and empirical validation make it a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The authors successfully address a significant limitation of existing theoretical results and propose an algorithm with a promising combination of theoretical guarantees and practical performance. Addressing the scalability concerns and providing a more comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods in a revised version would further strengthen the paper.

**Confidence:** 8/10
