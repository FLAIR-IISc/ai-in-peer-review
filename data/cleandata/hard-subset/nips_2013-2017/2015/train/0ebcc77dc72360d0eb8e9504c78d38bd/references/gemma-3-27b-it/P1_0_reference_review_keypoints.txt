- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm that attains exponential convergence without δ‑cover sampling.  
- IMGPO explores many potential bounds simultaneously using GP information and a hierarchical partitioning scheme.  
- Provides theoretical analysis proving exponential convergence and empirical results showing competitive or superior performance.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. (2017) by removing δ‑cover sampling and improves on Wang et al. (2018) by combining GP-UCB with SOO.  
- Connects to bound-based search methods (Lipschitz optimization, A*, PAC-MDPs) and algorithms like DIRECT, SOO, and REMBO.  
- Theoretical guarantee: rigorous proof of exponential convergence is a major contribution.  
- Practicality: avoids expensive δ‑cover sampling, making the method more applicable in practice.  
- Novel idea: simultaneous exploration of multiple possible bounds addresses limitations of single GP-derived bounds.  
- Empirical validation: strong benchmark results, often outperforming SOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, especially in higher dimensions.  
- Detailed analysis: thorough discussion of parameter effects (e.g., Ξmax) and GP/infinite-metric interactions; useful “Remarks” section.  
- Code availability: source code is public, aiding reproducibility.  
- Complexity: algorithm description is dense and the interaction of components can be hard to follow; pseudocode needs clearer annotations.  
- Parameter sensitivity: insufficient investigation of sensitivity to GP hyperparameters and other parameters (e.g., η); initialization concerns.  
- Scalability concerns: theoretical analysis suggests potential issues in very high dimensions (λ term), requiring more mitigation discussion.  
- Limited high‑dimensional baselines: reliance on REMBO makes direct comparison to other state-of-the-art high‑dimensional BO methods difficult.  
- Acceptance pros: strong theory, novel approach, compelling experiments, literature connections, and public code.  
- Acceptance cons: algorithm complexity, limited parameter-sensitivity analysis, theoretical scalability worries, and indirect high‑dimensional evaluation.  
- Overall: strong contribution worth acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommended minor revisions: improve algorithm clarity and visuals; perform parameter sensitivity analysis (especially GP hyperparameters); expand scalability discussion and mitigation strategies; include more direct high‑dimensional baselines if feasible.
