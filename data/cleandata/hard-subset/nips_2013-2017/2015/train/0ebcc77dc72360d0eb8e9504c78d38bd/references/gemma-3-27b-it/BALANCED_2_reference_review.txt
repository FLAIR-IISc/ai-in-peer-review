This paper proposes a novel Gaussian Process (GP)-based global optimization algorithm (IMGPO) that aims to achieve exponential convergence without relying on the computationally expensive δ-cover sampling procedure required by de Freitas et al. [1]. The core idea is to leverage an unknown semi-metric ` that potentially provides a tighter bound on the function than the GP prior alone, and to simultaneously search based on multiple candidate bounds, filtering those looser than the GP-estimated bound. The paper provides a theoretical analysis demonstrating exponential convergence and experimental results showing improved performance over several baseline algorithms.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** The removal of the δ-cover sampling requirement from GP-based Bayesian optimization is a significant step forward, as it addresses a major practical limitation of the theoretical results in [1].  This makes the proposed approach more applicable to real-world problems.
* **Theoretical Guarantees:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis guaranteeing exponential convergence, a strong result for global optimization. The analysis is clearly presented and the assumptions are stated explicitly.
* **Novel Approach:** The idea of considering an infinite set of possible bounds and filtering based on the GP prior is innovative and well-motivated. The connection to bound-based search methods like A* and Lipschitz optimization is insightful.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The experimental evaluation is thorough, covering a range of test functions, including both standard benchmarks and those used in previous work.  The comparison with several relevant baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) is appropriate. The inclusion of a high-dimensional test case using REMBO with IMGPO/BaMSOO is valuable.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The algorithm is explained clearly, and the notation is consistent.
* **Code Availability:** Providing publicly available code is excellent and encourages reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Algorithm:** The algorithm description, particularly Section 3.2, is quite dense and may be challenging to fully grasp without careful reading.  A more visual explanation or a simplified example walkthrough could improve clarity. The interplay between hierarchical partitioning, UCB, and the unknown semi-metric is not immediately obvious.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The influence of parameters like Ξmax is mentioned, but the guidance on setting these parameters in practice is limited. Further investigation into parameter sensitivity and potentially adaptive parameter selection strategies would be beneficial.
* **Scalability Concerns:** While the exponential convergence is a strong theoretical result, the analysis highlights potential scalability issues in high dimensions (λ approaching 1).  The paper acknowledges this and suggests future work to address it, but a more detailed discussion of the practical limitations in high-dimensional settings would be helpful.
* **Limited Discussion of GP Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper states that GP hyperparameters are updated using an empirical Bayesian method, but provides little detail about the specific method used or its impact on performance.  A more in-depth discussion of the GP hyperparameter tuning process would strengthen the paper.
* **Connection to Existing Literature:** While related work is cited, the paper could more explicitly connect the proposed approach to other relevant research areas, such as multi-fidelity optimization or exploration-exploitation trade-offs in Bayesian optimization.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Novel and theoretically sound approach to addressing a practical limitation in Bayesian optimization.
* Strong theoretical guarantees of exponential convergence.
* Thorough experimental evaluation demonstrating improved performance over existing methods.
* Clear and well-organized presentation (despite some density in algorithm description).
* Publicly available code.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* Algorithm complexity may hinder understanding and implementation.
* Potential scalability issues in high dimensions.
* Limited discussion of parameter sensitivity and GP hyperparameter tuning.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The proposed algorithm addresses a critical practical limitation of existing theoretical results, provides strong theoretical guarantees, and demonstrates promising empirical performance. While there are some minor weaknesses related to algorithm complexity and scalability, these are outweighed by the strengths of the work.  I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NeurIPS.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Simplify Algorithm Explanation:**  Provide a more intuitive explanation of the algorithm, perhaps with a step-by-step example.  Consider adding a diagram to illustrate the hierarchical partitioning and the selection process.
* **Parameter Tuning Guidance:** Include more detailed guidance on setting key parameters like Ξmax. Explore adaptive parameter selection strategies.
* **Scalability Analysis:**  Investigate the performance of IMGPO in higher dimensions and discuss the limitations.
* **GP Hyperparameter Tuning Details:** Provide more information about the GP hyperparameter tuning process.
* **Broader Literature Connection:**  Explicitly connect the work to other relevant research areas in Bayesian optimization and related fields.
