- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Key idea: consider infinitely many possible upper bounds instead of a single GP-derived bound.  
- Theoretical result: proves exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, improving over prior polynomial bounds (e.g., BaMSOO).  
- Connects to and extends bound-based search methods and algorithms like DIRECT and SOO using GP information.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous proof techniques and sound analysis.  
- Novel conceptual contribution: infinite-bound approach combining bound-based guarantees and GP flexibility.  
- Experimental results on benchmarks show consistent improvement over SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.  
- Paper is generally well-written, with clear algorithm description, helpful figure, and structured pseudocode.  
- Assumes a strong Lipschitz-like semi-metric (Assumption 2), which may be restrictive.  
- Scalability concerns: λ approaches 1 as dimension increases, suggesting degraded performance in high dimensions.  
- Limited empirical evidence for high-dimensional performance beyond a REMBO-based 1000-D Sin2 example.  
- Explanation of the infinite-metric exploration loss (ρt) and handling of temporary dummy values is unclear and needs strengthening.  
- Computational cost/wall-clock time comparisons are lacking despite claims of faster performance.  
- Connection to DIRECT could be articulated more precisely, especially regarding finite regret improvements.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested additions of wall-clock runtime discussion and more concrete high-dimensional evaluations.
