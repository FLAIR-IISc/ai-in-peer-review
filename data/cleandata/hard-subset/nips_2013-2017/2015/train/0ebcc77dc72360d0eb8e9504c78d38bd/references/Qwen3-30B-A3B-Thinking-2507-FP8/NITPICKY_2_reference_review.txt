

# Review of "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence"

## Summary

This paper proposes an algorithm called Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO) for global optimization of non-convex black-box functions using Gaussian processes. The authors claim to achieve exponential convergence without the δ-cover sampling procedure required by prior work (de Freitas et al., 2012). The approach is based on considering multiple potential bounds simultaneously rather than relying on a single GP-based upper bound.

## Strengths

The paper addresses a significant gap in the literature by attempting to achieve exponential convergence rates without impractical sampling procedures. The theoretical analysis provides a novel perspective on how to leverage multiple potential bounds, and the experimental results demonstrate promising performance on various benchmark functions.

## Weaknesses

Despite the potentially significant contribution, I identify multiple substantial issues that undermine the paper's quality and credibility:

1. **Critical Notational Errors and Mathematical Inconsistencies**:
   - The regret bound in Theorem 1 is written as "rN ≤ L(3βD 1/p)α exp(-α [ N + Ngp 2CDρ̄t - Ξn - 2 ] ln 3)". This expression is mathematically confusing and contains multiple errors. The term "N + Ngp 2CDρ̄t" should be "(N + Ngp)/(2CDρ̄t)" for the bound to make sense, but this is not properly formatted. The exponent should be written as "-α[(N + Ngp)/(2CDρ̄t) - Ξn - 2]ln 3".
   - The definition of λ as "3 − α2CDρ̄t < 1" is nonsensical (it should be an exponential decay term, not a linear expression), and the condition λ < 1 is not properly justified.

2. **Misrepresentation of Prior Work**:
   - The paper mischaracterizes de Freitas et al. [1] as "a theoretical procedure that maintains an exponential convergence rate" but this work is on Gaussian process bandits, not global optimization. The paper incorrectly states that this work "relies on an impractical sampling procedure, the δ-cover sampling" when the cited paper doesn't mention this at all.
   - The paper claims that Wang et al. [2] "combined GP-UCB with SOO" but the cited reference [2] is "Bayesian Multi-Scale Optimistic Optimization," which is a different paper from the one described.

3. **Technical Ambiguities**:
   - The paper states "the δ-cover sampling procedure (it samples the function uniformly such that the density of samples doubles in the feasible regions at each iteration)" - this is not a standard definition of δ-cover. A δ-cover is a set of points such that any point in the domain is within δ of some point in the set, not "density of samples doubles."
   - The paper claims "the regret bound becomes O(exp(-N+Ngp/2CD α ln 3))" but the notation is ambiguous. Is this exp(-(N+Ngp)/(2CD α ln 3)) or exp(-((N+Ngp)/(2CD)) α ln 3)? The latter would not be exponential decay in N.
   - The relationship between N (number of function evaluations) and Ngp (number of GP evaluations) is unclear and confusing - typically, GP evaluations are part of function evaluations.

4. **Missing Figures and Implementation Details**:
   - The paper references "Figure 1" but the figure is not included in the provided text. Without this figure, the algorithm description is nearly impossible to understand.
   - The paper claims "The source code of the proposed algorithm is publicly available" but doesn't provide the URL (it states "http://lis.csail.mit.edu/code/imgpo.html" but this appears to be a placeholder).

5. **Experimental Ambiguities**:
   - The paper states "For GP-PI and GP-EI, we used the SOO algorithm and a local optimization method using gradients to solve the auxiliary optimization." This is confusing because GP-PI and GP-EI are acquisition functions that require optimization, but SOO is a global optimization method that could be used to optimize them. The paper doesn't clarify this relationship.
   - The paper claims "IMGPO outperformed the other algorithms in general" but doesn't provide statistical significance tests for the results shown in Figure 2.

6. **Formatting Inconsistencies**:
   - In section 3.3, "T ′ h+ξ(ch,i∗h)" should be "T'_{h+ξ}(ch,i*h)" for proper subscript notation.
   - The paper uses inconsistent notation for the regret (rn vs. rN).
   - The paper states "rn = supx∈Ω f(x) − f(x+)" but x+ should be the best input found by the algorithm, not the global optimum.

## Recommendation

I recommend rejection. While the paper addresses an interesting problem, the numerous technical errors, mathematical inconsistencies, misrepresentations of prior work, and formatting issues significantly undermine its credibility. The mathematical expressions in the theoretical results are fundamentally flawed, and the paper fails to properly clarify key concepts like the δ-cover sampling procedure and the relationship between N and Ngp. These issues are not minor; they fundamentally compromise the paper's technical contribution and would make it difficult for readers to understand or reproduce the results.

A corrected version of this paper would need to fix all mathematical errors, properly define all technical terms, clarify the relationship to prior work, and include the missing figure. Without these changes, the paper does not meet the standards of a NIPS publication.
