- Proposes IMGPO, combining hierarchical optimistic partitioning with GP-UCB used as a pruning filter to avoid δ-cover sampling and auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- IMGPO “hedges” over an infinite family of semi-metrics instead of committing to a single smoothness bound.  
- Introduces infinite-metric exploration loss ρt and derives a regret bound rN = O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1 and Ngp counting GP-filtered (virtual) evaluations.  
- Authors argue GP affects constants via ρ̄t and Ngp; empirical results show IMGPO often outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-PI/EI.  
- Builds on optimistic partition methods (DIRECT/SOO/DOO) but avoids assuming a known smoothness constant.  
- Positions IMGPO between bound-based BO (GP-UCB) and unknown-metric optimistic partitioning (SOO/DOO), addressing exponential rates without δ-cover.  
- Uses REMBO for a 1000-D experiment, plugging IMGPO or BaMSOO as the BO subroutine.  
- Novel conceptual contribution: infinite-metric view plus GP-based pruning and the ρt lens to analyze exploratory overhead.  
- Ambitious theoretical claim: exponential regret without δ-cover or auxiliary acquisition optimization.  
- Practical algorithm design: deterministic partitioning, parsimonious GP use, empirical Bayes for hyperparameters, and code release.  
- Empirics are promising across benchmarks and include discussion of cases where GP misleads (e.g., Rosenbrock2).  
- Major theoretical gap: analysis requires a high-probability global UCB bound f(x) ≤ U(x|D), but the paper does not state the necessary modeling assumptions.  
- Using empirical Bayes for hyperparameters undermines guarantees; authors acknowledge UCB failures in experiments.  
- Regret bound mixes true evaluations N with internal GP-filter count Ngp, conflating sample complexity with computational work.  
- Dependence on data/algorithm-dependent ρ̄t yields weak control of the exponent; worst-case guarantees may be loose and non-explicit in N, D, α.  
- Notational and clarity problems in theory: λ expression appears inconsistent/wrong and many constants/assumptions are hard to track; several proofs deferred.  
- Computational complexity of the GP-based pruning step (computing z(h,i*)) can be combinatorially expensive; time/memory costs are unanalyzed.  
- Empirical comparisons may be disadvantaged by nonstandard optimization for baselines; budgets, stopping criteria, and evaluation counts lack detail.  
- More ablations needed (e.g., without GP pruning, varying Ξmax) and tests with misspecified kernels.  
- Novelty rated moderately high, but the significance depends on making theoretical guarantees rigorous under clear assumptions and separating Ngp from N.  
- Recommendations: state explicit UCB validity conditions, provide bounds purely in N (or separate Ngp), correct and unify λ/constants, bound ρ̄t, analyze step (iii) complexity, and strengthen experimental rigor.  
- Overall recommendation: weak reject pending clarified assumptions, corrected theoretical claims, and stronger/clearer empirical validation.
