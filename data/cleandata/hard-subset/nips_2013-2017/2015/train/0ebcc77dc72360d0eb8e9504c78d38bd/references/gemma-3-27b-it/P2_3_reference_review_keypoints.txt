- Presents IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm claiming exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: consider multiple Lipschitz-like bounds simultaneously and use the GP to filter overly conservative bounds.  
- Combines hierarchical partitioning with GP-UCB and provides a theoretical analysis showing exponential convergence.  
- Empirical results show IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and standard GP-based optimization on benchmark functions.  
- Theoretical guarantee of exponential convergence is a major strength and appears rigorous.  
- The multi-bound approach guided by GP uncertainty is novel and impactful.  
- Experiments provide good evidence of effectiveness, though not extensive.  
- Writing and motivation are generally clear; core ideas are well-organized.  
- Algorithm description and pseudocode (Sections 3.2–3.3, Alg.1) are dense and hard to follow.  
- Several parameters (e.g., Ξmax, η) lack sensitivity analysis and chosen values seem arbitrary.  
- Scalability to high dimensions is a concern; theoretical bounds depend unfavorably on dimension D.  
- GP hyperparameter tuning is handled heuristically (fixed initialization then empirical Bayes), which is unsatisfying.  
- Comparisons to other methods may be unfair due to limited hyperparameter tuning for baselines.  
- Paper is technically sound with convincing analysis, but clarity in complex sections could improve.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; request clearer algorithm exposition, parameter sensitivity analysis, scalability exploration, and clarified hyperparameter tuning for comparisons.
