- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm using "infinite-metric" exploration to consider all possible semi-metric upper bounds and achieve exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.
- Addresses the practical limitation of unknown Lipschitz constants by not relying on a single GP upper bound.
- Builds on and contrasts with prior work: removes δ-cover requirement from De Freitas et al., improves over BaMSOO’s polynomial bounds, and links to SOO/DIRECT ideas while achieving finite exponential regret.
- Theoretical contribution: proves an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) (λ < 1) without δ-cover sampling.
- Conceptual novelty: introduces the "infinite-metric" framework that integrates GP priors with consideration of infinitely many bounds.
- Experimental validation: outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on multiple benchmark functions, with caveats where GP priors are misleading (e.g., Rosenbrock2).
- Practical implementation: addresses settings like Ξmax and uses isotropic Matern kernel defaults to make the approach practitioner-friendly.
- Honest limitations: acknowledges scalability/dimensionality challenges and trade-offs when using GP (Remarks 1–3).
- Algorithm clarity: pseudocode and Section 3.2 are complex and need more intuitive explanation, especially for "infinite-metric exploration loss."
- Computational complexity: lacks a thorough runtime/complexity analysis comparing IMGPO’s overhead to baselines.
- Parameter sensitivity: insufficient guidance on selecting Ξmax and incomplete exploration of its effect on regret bounds.
- Related work connection: could better relate to other "infinite" or high-dimensional BO approaches (e.g., Kandasamy et al.).
- Implementation detail: provided source code URL appears to be a placeholder and needs fixing.
- Recommended improvements: add intuitive examples for infinite-metric loss, clarify Ξmax-versus-regret, include complexity analysis, fix code link, and discuss constrained optimization extensions.
- Pro-acceptance: major theoretical advance (exponential convergence without δ-cover), novel framework, strong empirical performance, and solid writing.
- Con-acceptance: clarity issues for non-experts, incomplete complexity and parameter guidance, and broken code link.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant, and well-supported — reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
