- Proposes IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm that attains exponential convergence without requiring impractical δ-cover sampling.  
- Key idea: consider infinitely many possible semi-metric bounds (beyond the GP-estimated bound) to balance global and local search.  
- Proves an exponential regret bound O(λ^N + N g_p) with λ < 1, improving on prior polynomial bounds (e.g., BaMSOO).  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on eight benchmark functions.  
- Strong theoretical quality: rigorous analysis with Theorem 1 and complete proofs in supplementary material.  
- Experimental design is sound (diverse benchmarks, consistent kernel use); results are convincing.  
- Main theoretical limitations: analysis assumes Ω is a hyperrectangle, restricting general-domain applicability.  
- Practical limitation not addressed: algorithm behavior with noisy function evaluations is not studied.  
- Presentation is clear and well-structured; Algorithm 1 and Figure 1 aid understanding.  
- The role of Ξmax and its effect on performance could be explained more clearly.  
- Related work is adequately cited, but connections to more recent BO literature (e.g., Gardner et al. 2017) could be improved.  
- Original and significant: resolves an open problem by achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling and introduces a novel multi-bound framework.  
- Remarks 1–3 deepen theoretical insight into when multiple bounds help, offering a framework beyond the algorithm itself.  
- Major practical caveat: dimensionality scaling (λ → 1 as D increases) remains unresolved and limits high-dimensional applicability.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant theoretical contribution with convincing experiments; recommendation: accept.
