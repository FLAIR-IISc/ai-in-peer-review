- Paper proposes IMGPO: a GP-based Bayesian optimization using hierarchical partitioning and local GP-UCB pruning, claiming exponential simple-regret O(λ^{N+Ngp}) without δ-cover or inner argmax.  
- Key idea: avoid relying on a single GP bound by using an unknown semi-metric and Hölder-type bounds plus an “infinite-metric” exploration loss ρt to guide partitioning.  
- Theoretical claim: high-probability exponential regret bound under Assumptions 1–2, with constants C, D, β, ρ̄t, and lookahead depth Ξn.  
- Typo/error: λ is misstated in the text and should be 3^{−α/(2CDρ̄t)} per the provided bound.  
- UCB coverage issue: the guarantee f(x) ≤ U(x|D) depends on exploration parameter and fixed GP hyperparameters; online hyperparameter learning typically breaks uniform-UCB coverage.  
- Theorem needs clarification/separation between analyzed (fixed hyperparameters) and practical (learned hyperparameters) variants or a robust β schedule that accounts for hyperparameter uncertainty.  
- Dependence on ρ̄t weakens interpretability: ρ̄t is algorithm- and instance-dependent and can be as large as O(N/t); worst-case bounds or empirical measurements are needed.  
- Computational complexity concern: step (iii) can require up to 3^{Ξ} UCB evaluations per candidate and the paper lacks a big-O per-iteration cost analysis in terms of D, Ξmax, and depths traversed.  
- Experiments use common kernel/hyperparameter init and compare to relevant baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI) including a 1000D REMBO test.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to DIRECT despite the “infinite metric” relation.  
- Baseline optimization concern: GP-PI/EI were optimized by SOO+local search; should also try multi-start L-BFGS or CMA-ES to avoid handicapping baselines.  
- Missing ablations: no IMGPO-without-GP, no study of Ξmax effects, no analysis under mis-specified kernels, and no sensitivity analysis for η.  
- Exponential convergence claim not empirically validated: no log-regret vs N+Ngp plots showing linear decay with theoretical slope or dependence on D and ρ̄t.  
- Practical inconsistency: UCB property failed often on Rosenbrock, undermining theoretical premises; recommend either fixing β or reporting theory-compliant runs separately.  
- Clarity issues: numerous notation/typo problems; constants (C, β, p, α, ρ̄t, Ξn) should be defined where first used and λ corrected.  
- Need explicit statement of GP prior/hyperparameter conditions for which UCB coverage holds, and whether learned hyperparameters preserve bounds.  
- Assumptions 1–2 should be clarified as Hölder continuity around x*: f(x*)−f(x) ≤ L ||x−x*||_p^α.  
- Provide a succinct per-iteration time/memory complexity analysis including UCB call counts as function of D and Ξmax.  
- Pseudocode is dense and has line-reference issues; suggest a compact high-level sketch plus a clean appendix version.  
- Originality: novel combination of hierarchical optimistic partitioning with GP-UCB pruning and the infinite-metric exploration-loss framing.  
- Significance: if theoretical claims hold under realistic GP assumptions, this is a substantial advance offering exponential regret without δ-cover or inner argmax; scalability depends on D and ρ̄t.  
- Pros summary: addresses open problem, avoids expensive continuous acquisition optimization, provides insightful analysis, public code, and high-dimensional demo.  
- Cons summary: unclear coverage with learned hyperparameters, dependence on opaque ρ̄t and Ξn, notation/typos, missing DIRECT baseline and ablations, and incomplete complexity/tuning discussion.  
- Key questions for authors: conditions for UCB coverage with learned hyperparameters; empirical ρ̄t and Ngp over iterations; comparisons to DIRECT; ablations (no-GP, varying Ξmax) and log-regret plots; and per-iteration time/memory complexity.  
- Minor fixes requested: correct λ, define constants at first use, consistent norms, clean up pseudocode/typos, and complete references.  
- Recommendation: weak accept — promising and novel but needs clarification on hyperparameter coverage, notation fixes, ablations, and empirical validation for camera-ready.
