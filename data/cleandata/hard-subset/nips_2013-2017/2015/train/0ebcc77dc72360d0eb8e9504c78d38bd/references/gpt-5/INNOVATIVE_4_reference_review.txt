Summary
This paper proposes IMGPO, a new Bayesian optimization algorithm that combines Gaussian-process-based upper confidence bounds with hierarchical partitioning while explicitly not committing to a single smoothness/Lipschitz bound. The core idea is to leverage (i) the probabilistic UCB bound from a GP to prune, and (ii) an “infinite-metric” search across all candidate semi-metrics that could upper bound the regret around a global maximizer. The authors prove an exponential simple regret rate O(λ^{N+Ngp}) without relying on the δ-cover sampling procedure used by de Freitas et al., and without auxiliary optimization of the acquisition function. They introduce the notion of infinite-metric exploration loss ρ_t to capture the cost of not committing to one bound, and show how the GP reduces this cost and the number of true evaluations. Empirically, IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-EI, and GP-PI on several benchmark functions, and can be used as a subroutine within REMBO for very high dimensions.

Relation to prior work
The work sits at the intersection of SOO/DOO-style optimistic optimization (Munos) and GP-based BO (e.g., GP-UCB, BaMSOO). The key novelty relative to BaMSOO is an exponential convergence rate without δ-covers and without auxiliary acquisition optimization, while retaining the practical pruning enabled by GP posteriors. Relative to DIRECT/DOO/SOO, the paper departs from committing to a single smoothness, and formalizes the trade-off via ρ_t. The authors position the result against prior polynomial regret results and provide an analysis that clarifies when the GP helps.

Strengths
- Novel conceptual contribution: the “infinite-metric” principle that decouples correctness from any specific smoothness bound, yet uses the GP to prune the space of candidate bounds. This is a bold and interesting direction.
- Theoretical advance: an exponential simple regret rate without δ-covers, a long-standing pain point in GP-based BO theory for deterministic settings.
- Clear articulation of how dimensionality, smoothness (α, p), and the exploration loss ρ̄_t enter the bound, with insightful remarks on the role of GP (Ngp and ρ̄_t) and when not committing to a bound helps.
- Practical algorithm: avoids auxiliary optimization of acquisition functions; code is provided; empirical results are broadly favorable across standard benchmarks.
- Honest discussion of limitations (e.g., λ approaches 1 as D grows).

Weaknesses and concerns
- Assumptions and UCB validity: The high-probability property f(x) ≤ U(x|D) is central, but the conditions under which it holds are not stated precisely. Standard GP-UCB guarantees require either f drawn from the specified GP prior or f in an RKHS with a bounded norm, with fixed kernel/hyperparameters and specific β_t schedules. Here the algorithm updates hyperparameters via empirical Bayes, yet still claims the property with probability ≥ 1−η by setting ς_M = sqrt(log(π^2 M^2 / (12η))). This mismatch needs clarification or a robustness argument (e.g., confidence inflation to account for hyperparameter learning).
- Implicit bound via ρ̄_t: The main rate depends on the averaged exploration loss ρ̄_t, but there is no nontrivial upper bound provided for ρ̄_t under the algorithm’s dynamics. While the remarks are illuminating, the theorem is thus partly implicit. Even a coarse worst-case bound (or conditions that ensure N/ρ̄_t = Ω(N)) would strengthen the result.
- Notation/typos: The expression for λ appears garbled (“Let λ = 3 − α2CDρ̄t < 1”) and should read something like λ = 3^{−α/(2 C D ρ̄_t)}. A few other notational slips make the main theorem harder to parse.
- Experimental methodology details: GP-PI/EI baselines are optimized via SOO+local gradients, which is unusual and may disadvantage them relative to standard multi-start L-BFGS or CMA-ES pipelines for acquisition optimization. Reporting results with a more conventional acquisition optimizer would make comparisons less debatable. An ablation that disables the GP pruning (to isolate the effect of the infinite-metric idea) would be very informative and would empirically validate Remarks 1–3.
- Scalability: While the paper acknowledges the D-dependence, complexity per iteration (due to step (iii) and Ξ_max) and memory growth are not analyzed. A brief computational complexity discussion would help practitioners.

Quality
The theoretical development is interesting and, if the UCB validity under empirical Bayes is addressed, appears sound. The algorithm description is fairly detailed, with pseudocode. Experiments are reasonable and generally supportive, though some baselines and optimizer choices could be improved. Proofs are deferred to the supplement.

Clarity
Mostly clear, with a few dense sections (the step (iii) screening and the definition of T′) and some typos/notation issues. The remarks are helpful. I recommend tightening the theorem statement (explicit λ), explicitly listing assumptions for the UCB guarantee, and adding a short complexity section.

Originality
High. Combining infinite-metric optimism with GP pruning to obtain exponential rates without δ-covers is a fresh and bold idea. The “exploration loss” lens is also novel and insightful.

Significance
High for the BO community working on deterministic global optimization. The result reopens the path to exponential rates in a practical GP-based algorithm and could influence design choices in bound-based search more broadly.

Questions and suggestions for the authors
- Precisely state the probabilistic assumptions under which f(x) ≤ U(x|D) holds with empirical Bayes updates. If needed, propose a conservative β_t schedule that restores validity.
- Provide an explicit expression for λ in Theorem 1 and fix notation typos.
- Give any nontrivial bound or heuristic characterization of ρ̄_t under IMGPO (e.g., in terms of near-optimality dimension or kernel length-scale).
- Add an ablation: IMGPO without GP (or with randomized GP pruning) to empirically validate the effect on ρ̄_t/Ngp and regret.
- Use a standard acquisition optimizer for GP-PI/EI (e.g., multi-start L-BFGS or CMA-ES) and report wall-clock including acquisition optimization to strengthen fairness claims.
- Briefly analyze per-iteration and total computational complexity vs. SOO/BaMSOO.

Recommendation
Accept (leaning strong). The paper introduces a compelling new direction with a theoretically meaningful and practically motivated algorithm, achieving an exponential rate without δ-covers. While some clarity and methodological details need tightening, the novelty and potential impact outweigh the issues.

Confidence
High.
