- Summary: Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm that jointly explores GP UCB and a spectrum of Lipschitz-like bounds via hierarchical partitioning to avoid δ-cover sampling.  
- Theoretical claim: Proves exponential convergence rate without requiring the impractical δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: “Infinite-metric” approach—consider many possible semi-metrics (bounds) alongside GP uncertainty to guide search.  
- Algorithm design: Uses hierarchical partitioning and a mechanism to skip evaluations in promising regions for efficiency.  
- Empirical results: Outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on multiple benchmarks, with caveats about hyperparameter tuning.  
- Related work: Properly positions itself relative to Lipschitz-based methods, de Freitas et al., SOO, REMBO, and bound-based search literature.  
- Strength — theory: Significant theoretical advance by removing δ-cover requirement while retaining exponential convergence.  
- Strength — novelty: Innovative infinite-metric concept that mitigates overreliance on GP uncertainty.  
- Strength — practicality & clarity: Incorporates practical algorithmic choices and is generally well-written.  
- Weakness — complexity: Algorithm and its exposition (Algorithm 1, hierarchical partitioning) are complex and require careful reading.  
- Weakness — parameter sensitivity: Performance depends on several tunable parameters (Ξmax, η, kernel hyperparams) with limited sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness — scalability: Potentially poor scaling with input dimension; experiments don’t directly test IMGPO at very high D.  
- Weakness — comparisons: Lacks comparison to some recent BO advances (e.g., deep GPs, more high-dimensional methods).  
- Weakness — assumptions: Relies on a strong Assumption 1 about an unknown semi-metric and bounds (L, α, p), whose violation may affect guarantees.  
- For acceptance: Significant theoretical advance, novel approach, and strong empirical performance argue for acceptance.  
- Against acceptance: Algorithmic complexity, scalability concerns, and parameter sensitivity argue against broad applicability.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with compelling theory and experiments; drawbacks (complexity/scalability) do not outweigh contribution — recommend acceptance.  
- Rating: Accept (Strongly Consider).  
- Recommendations: Simplify algorithm description (diagram/flowchart), add sensitivity analysis, run higher-dimensional scalability tests, discuss practical impact of Assumption 1, and compare to newer BO methods.
