Summary
The paper proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm for deterministic black-box functions. The main claimed contribution is an exponential simple regret bound O(λN+Ngp) (λ < 1) without relying on δ-cover sampling or an auxiliary inner optimization. The algorithm blends hierarchical partitioning (à la DIRECT/SOO/DOO) with GP-UCB-style upper bounds to prune evaluations, while introducing the notion of “infinite-metric exploration,” i.e., not committing to a single (unknown) smoothness bound but screening intervals using all candidate bounds, filtered by GP-based UCBs. The analysis assumes there exists an unknown semi-metric ℓ such that f(x*) ≤ f(x) + ℓ(x, x*), together with a Hölder-type upper bound ℓ(x, x′) ≤ L‖x − x′‖αp. Experiments on eight deterministic benchmarks compare IMGPO to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI, reporting superior performance overall.

Strengths
- Ambitious goal: a GP-based optimizer with exponential simple regret without δ-cover. If correct and practically realizable, this would be impactful.
- Interesting integration of hierarchical optimistic search (SOO/DOO/DIRECT lineage) with GP-derived UCBs for pruning and “lazy” evaluations.
- Attempts to make precise how GP information can reduce exploration cost (ρt) and accelerate convergence.
- Practical implementation details (e.g., skipping evaluations when UCB is below current best) that could reduce wall-clock time in some settings.

Weaknesses
- Core theoretical guarantee is largely conditional and data-dependent. The exponential rate in Theorem 1 depends critically on the “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρ̄t and the lookahead depth Ξn. The paper does not provide a priori bounds on these terms under verifiable conditions, nor characterize when GP-UCB will make ρ̄t = O(1). As a result, the claimed exponential rate is not an unconditional property of the algorithm.
- Use of empirical Bayes to update GP hyperparameters breaks standard GP-UCB concentration guarantees. The coverage claim f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with probability 1 − η relies on specific β/ς schedules and fixed kernels; adapting hyperparameters online generally invalidates these guarantees, yet the analysis assumes them.
- Assumptions are close to prior optimistic optimization work (Munos’s DOO/SOO): the “unknown semi-metric” with Hölder bound is essentially the standard smoothness assumption used to derive near-optimality dimension results. The “infinite-metric” framing reads more as a rebranding than a substantial conceptual shift.
- Experimental validation is limited and arguably unfair to GP baselines:
  - Only deterministic test functions, no noise robustness.
  - GP baselines use a single isotropic Matern kernel with fixed initialization; no model selection or multiple restarts as is standard in BO practice.
  - Missing strong baselines that are natural here (e.g., GP-UCB with standard optimizer, DIRECT, LIPO/DOO variants), and ablations (IMGPO without GP; SOO with identical splitting; sensitivity to Ξmax).
  - The REMBO experiment is under-specified and lacks comparisons to REMBO+standard acquisition (EI/UCB).
- Clarity/rigor issues:
  - The definition and role of constants C, ρ̄t, Ξn are not made precise enough to let the reader assess when λ < 1 holds.
  - Several steps in Algorithm 1 (e.g., line 38 “Ξ ← Ξ + 2^2”) and the construction of T′h+ξ are difficult to parse; computational complexity of step (iii) is not analyzed beyond a heuristic cap Ξmax.
  - The union bound for UCB uses M (number of U calls), but with hyperparameter updates and data-dependent branching, it is unclear that the finite-time bound is valid.
- The claimed novelty “first GP-based method with exponential convergence without δ-cover” needs careful qualification, given the dependence on unbounded, algorithm-specific quantities and close ties to existing optimistic optimization frameworks.

Quality
The theoretical contribution is intriguing but not fully convincing as written: the bound’s key quantities are uncontrolled, and the probabilistic UCB guarantee is assumed despite hyperparameter learning. The experiments, while suggestive, do not constitute thorough validation against strong, properly tuned baselines, and omit noisy settings. Overall, this reads as promising but not yet rigorous.

Clarity
The paper is generally readable but has several notational/organizational issues. Important constants and assumptions should be introduced more cleanly; the algorithmic steps and their computational cost need clearer exposition. There are minor typos and some overloaded notation.

Originality
The combination of GP pruning with hierarchical optimistic partitioning is close to BaMSOO; the “infinite-metric exploration” idea echoes DIRECT/DOO/SOO where a family of possible smoothness models is implicitly considered. The framing is somewhat novel, but the conceptual delta over prior work appears incremental.

Significance
If one could provide unconditional exponential rates under standard Hölder assumptions, this would be significant. As is, the result hinges on unverified properties (ρ̄t, Ξn, valid UCBs under EB hyperparameters), and the empirical evidence is not yet compelling enough to shift practice.

Pros for acceptance
- Ambitious attempt to close a known theoretical gap (exponential rate without δ-cover).
- Potentially useful practical heuristic (lazy evaluations via UCB in a partitioning framework).
- Connects GP-based BO with optimistic partitioning in an interesting way.

Cons for acceptance
- Exponential bound is conditional on quantities not bounded a priori; reliance on UCB coverage with empirical Bayes is not justified.
- Experimental evaluation is limited, lacks strong/tuned baselines and noisy problems.
- Incremental relative to BaMSOO/SOO/DOO; novelty primarily in analysis framing.
- Clarity and rigor need work; key constants and algorithmic costs are under-specified.

Questions and suggestions
- Provide conditions under which ρ̄t and Ξn are bounded independently of N (e.g., assumptions on kernel/length-scale relative to true Hölder constants), or at least high-probability bounds.
- Either fix GP hyperparameters or use a theoretically justified adaptation scheme; otherwise, relax the UCB-based claims or provide new concentration results under EB updates.
- Add thorough baselines: DIRECT, GP-UCB with standard optimizers and multiple restarts, ablations (no-GP IMGPO), sensitivity to Ξmax and η, and noisy settings.
- Clarify constants (C, β, ρ̄t), algorithmic complexity (especially step (iii)), and the precise definition of semi-metric used.
- Improve presentation and correct typos.

Recommendation
Reject (borderline). The direction is interesting, but the current version does not provide a convincing, unconditional theoretical advance nor sufficiently rigorous and comprehensive empirical validation to support the strong claims.
