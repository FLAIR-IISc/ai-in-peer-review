Summary and relation to prior work
The paper tackles deterministic global optimization of a black-box function over a compact domain using Bayesian optimization. It proposes Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), which combines hierarchical partitioning (as in SOO/DOO/DIRECT) with Gaussian process (GP) upper confidence bounds (UCB) to screen candidate regions. The key modeling assumption is that there exists an unknown semi-metric ` such that f(x*) − f(x) ≤ `(x, x*), and further that `(x, x*) ≤ L∥x − x*∥p^α (unknown Hölder parameters L, α, p). Unlike de Freitas et al. (ICML’12), which achieved exponential regret under an impractical δ-cover sampling, IMGPO aims for exponential convergence without δ-cover and without auxiliary global optimization of an acquisition function. The algorithm maintains a hierarchy of hyperrectangles, selects candidates across depths using empirical center values and GP-UCB screening, and adaptively decides whether to evaluate f or leave a GP-based “placeholder” value. The analysis introduces the “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρt to quantify the overhead of not committing to a single bound, and proves a regret bound of the form rN = O(λ^{N+Ngp}) with λ < 1, where Ngp counts outstanding GP-based placeholders and the rate depends inversely on dimension and on ρ̄t. The paper relates IMGPO to SOO/DOO (Munos, NIPS’11) and BaMSOO (AISTATS’14), claiming an improvement from polynomial to exponential regret rates. Experiments on several benchmark functions compare IMGPO to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI and GP-EI (Snoek et al., NIPS’12), and show favorable performance; a 1000-D REMBO setting (Wang et al., IJCAI’13) illustrates compatibility with random embeddings.

Quality
- Theory: The main theorem presents an exponential regret bound under Assumptions 1–2 and a high-probability UCB property. The dependence on ρ̄t and D is explicit and informative. However, the guarantee critically relies on the event f(x) ≤ U(x|D) holding uniformly over all UCB calls. In the paper and experiments, GP hyperparameters are learned via empirical Bayes and the kernel is potentially misspecified; in these settings, the asserted probability control is not established. The paper does not formalize conditions (e.g., f drawn from the GP prior with fixed hyperparameters, or RKHS-norm bounded functions as in Srinivas et al., ICML’10) under which the UCB property and the union bound with ςM are valid. This is a significant gap between the stated theorem and practical use.
- Role of ρ̄t: The exponential rate depends on ρ̄t in the denominator of the exponent. There is no a priori bound relating ρ̄t to N without further assumptions on the GP’s adequacy; the remarks give qualitative cases (useful vs. useless GP bounds) but no formal conditions guaranteeing ρ̄t = O(1) or even o(N). Consequently, the “exponential” rate can be vacuous if ρ̄t grows with N. It would strengthen the result to state explicit conditions (e.g., kernel smoothness and hyperparameter set containing the truth) under which ρ̄t remains bounded with high probability.
- Counting and complexity: Ngp in the theorem/code denotes the number of live GP-based surrogate values (placeholders), not the number of UCB computations. Step (iii) evaluates many UCBs (up to 3Ξmax per depth) but these are not reflected in Ngp. As the empirical cost typically scales with the number of GP predictions and posterior updates, the complexity-vs-regret tradeoff is not fully captured. Clarifying the computational complexity per iteration and how it scales with D, t, and Ξmax would help.
- Algorithmic details: The pseudocode is helpful, but some notation is hard to parse (e.g., T′h+ξ(ch,i∗h), and the selection of ξ). The choice and adaptation of Ξ and Ξmax are heuristic; their effect on guarantees is acknowledged but not analyzed beyond appearing as −Ξn in the exponent.

Clarity
The paper is generally readable and includes illustrative figures/pseudocode. However:
- Assumption 1 could be stated more directly as a Hölder-type local smoothness at x*, followed by Assumption 2; as written, the semi-metric formulation is more abstract than necessary.
- The derivation/justification of ςM = sqrt(log(π^2 M^2/(12η))) and the precise event over which the union bound is taken should be made explicit, along with the modeling assumptions (fixed kernel/hyperparameters or prior over them).
- Define precisely Ngp, M, ρt, ρ̄t, Ξn in the main text and keep these consistent with code.
- Some formatting issues (e.g., a footnote fragment inside Remark 1) hinder comprehension.

Originality
Combining infinite-metric optimistic partitioning with GP-UCB as a screening device is a neat idea, building on DIRECT/SOO/DOO and BaMSOO. The introduction of ρ̄t to quantify the “cost of not committing to a bound” is insightful. The claimed exponential regret without δ-cover appears novel in the GP-based BO literature, though its strength hinges on unverified conditions. Related work on convergence rates for GP-based BO such as Bull (2011, Ann. Stat.) could be discussed for context on achievable rates under smoothness assumptions.

Significance
If the exponential rate holds under realistic and checkable conditions, this would be a meaningful theoretical advance and the algorithm is practically relevant (no auxiliary optimization, good empirical performance). However, the lack of rigorous conditions ensuring the UCB property with learned hyperparameters and the absence of a bound on ρ̄t limit the immediate theoretical impact. Empirically, IMGPO performs well across benchmarks and integrates with REMBO, supporting practical significance.

Pros
- Clear problem focus: deterministic BO without δ-cover or auxiliary acquisition optimization.
- Conceptual novelty: infinite-metric selection augmented by GP-UCB pruning.
- Theoretical framework exposes dependence on smoothness, dimension, and exploration loss.
- Competitive to strong empirical baselines; code availability.

Cons
- The high-probability UCB assumption is not justified under hyperparameter learning/misspecification; theory-practice mismatch.
- No formal control of ρ̄t; “exponential” rate can degrade without conditions on GP adequacy.
- Computational complexity of UCB screening not analyzed; Ngp does not reflect actual GP call cost.
- Clarity issues in notation and some presentation/formatting; missing discussion of related rate results (e.g., Bull, 2011).

Suggestions/questions for authors
- State explicit assumptions under which f(x) ≤ U(x|D) holds across all calls (e.g., fixed true kernel/hyperparameters, or RKHS norms) and revise the theorem accordingly. Discuss the implications of empirical Bayes.
- Provide any upper bounds or typical behavior for ρ̄t under reasonable GP assumptions; can it be bounded by a constant with high probability?
- Clarify the computational cost per iteration and how many GP posterior predictions are made; relate regret to total wall-clock cost.
- Unify and clarify notation for Ngp, M, ρt, Ξn; give a self-contained definition of T′h+ξ.
- Include DIRECT as an additional baseline, and consider reporting sensitivity to Ξmax more broadly.
- Discuss related convergence-rate results for GP-based BO (e.g., Bull, 2011) to position your contributions.
