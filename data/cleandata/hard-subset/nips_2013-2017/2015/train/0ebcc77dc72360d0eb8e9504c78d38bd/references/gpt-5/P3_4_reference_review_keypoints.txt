- Paper introduces IMGPO: a GP-augmented hierarchical partitioning optimizer that avoids δ-cover sampling by exploring an infinite family of smoothness bounds and using GP-UCB pruning.
- Key idea: “infinite-metric” search (not committing to one smoothness) plus GP-based temporary center values to avoid evaluating likely-suboptimal points.
- Theoretical claim: high-probability exponential simple-regret bound depending on N+Ngp, infinite-metric exploration loss ρ̄t, lookahead Ξ, dimension D and Hölder parameters.
- Empirically, IMGPO outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, PI/EI on several deterministic benchmarks and code is released.
- Strength: conceptual novelty in combining infinite-metric selection with GP pruning and defining the exploration loss ρt.
- Strength: provides a clean, interpretable theoretical bound showing how GP affects rates via Ngp, ρ̄t, and Ξn.
- Strength: algorithm is practical—avoids δ-covers and acquisition optimization—pseudocode and experiments provided.
- Strength: positive empirical gains and some sensitivity analysis for Ξmax; computational cost discussed.
- Weakness: Theorem 1 has apparent errors (λ expression and dimensional inconsistency) and multiple typographical/math issues that obscure analysis.
- Weakness: role and control of ρ̄t and Ξn are left implicit; without bounds on ρ̄t the exponential-rate claim is fragile.
- Weakness: ambiguous claim that bound holds for “best values” of unknown L, α, p — needs clarification whether this means true constants or an optimization over triplets.
- Weakness: UCB high-probability coverage assumption conflicts with empirical hyperparameter re-estimation (empirical Bayes), which can break guarantees.
- Weakness: Assumption 2’s global Hölder growth is strong; analysis should consider localization or relate to near-optimality dimension.
- Weakness: Step (iii) lookahead complexity and Ξmax scaling in D are under-specified; complexity analysis needed.
- Weakness: need formalization that GP-based deferral/safeguard cannot cause infinite deferral when UCB is miscalibrated.
- Weakness: Ξ update notation/heuristic unclear (typos like “22”); notation and heuristics need fixing and justification.
- Empirical weaknesses: missing strong baselines (DIRECT, CMA-ES, GP-UCB), biased acquisition optimization comparisons, lack of ablations (no GP, no step (iii), fixed vs learned hyperparams).
- Empirical suggestion: report trajectories of ρ̄t, Ngp, and Ξn to connect experiments to theory; clarify REMBO/1000-D as illustrative only.
- Writing/organization: multiple typos, missing symbols, and unclear figure/table references; needs careful proofreading and clearer definitions.
- Recommendation: promising and novel but requires corrected constants/proofs, control of ρ̄t/Ξn, clear handling of hyperparameter learning, stronger baselines and ablations before acceptance.
- Suggested fixes: correct Theorem 1 constants, bound ρ̄t (or state conditions), state UCB validity with learned hyperparameters or restrict to fixed hyperparams, add baselines/ablations, provide complexity for step (iii), and fix notation/typos.
