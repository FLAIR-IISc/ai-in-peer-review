- Proposes IMGPO combining optimistic hierarchical partitioning with GP-UCB filtering to avoid committing to a single smoothness bound.
- Introduces an “unknown bound” semi-metric (local Hölder-type) and infinite-metric exploration loss ρt to quantify overhead from multiple bounds.
- Claims an exponential simple-regret bound decaying in N + Ngp with a rate depending on D, Hölder parameters, ρ̄t, and a lookahead Ξn.
- Builds on GP-UCB/PI/EI, optimistic partitioning methods (DIRECT/DOO/SOO), and BaMSOO, while claiming exponential rates without δ-covers or auxiliary acquisition maximization.
- Main theorem and high-level analysis are coherent and align with optimistic partitioning intuitions.
- Typo in definition of λ; likely intended λ = 3^{−α/(2 C D ρ̄t)} < 1 and must be corrected.
- The high-probability event f(x) ≤ U(x|D) is asserted using ςM but lacks justification under adaptive/empirical Bayes hyperparameter tuning and misspecified kernels.
- The unproven UCB containment under learned hyperparameters is the biggest theoretical gap threatening the main theorem.
- Using Ngp in the rN bound is misleading since rN is measured after N evaluations; dependence on GP-only steps needs justification or removal.
- The phrase about “best values of unknown L, α, p” is confusing; clarify whether the theorem is uniform over admissible triplets or depends on true parameters.
- Experiments use standard benchmarks and show IMGPO typically outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, and GP-EI/PI under a fixed Matern-5/2 kernel with empirical Bayes.
- No ablation studies isolating the GP filter, and no sensitivity analysis for Ξmax or ρ̄t are provided.
- Step (iii) (GP filtering) can be computationally heavy; empirical CPU times are reported but theoretical complexity or GP-call bounds per iteration are missing.
- Rosenbrock2 results show UCB often fails empirically, underscoring concerns about the UCB assumption with learned hyperparameters.
- Notation and presentation issues throughout (λ typo, informal introductions like g(·), Algorithm 1 lines) reduce clarity and must be fixed.
- The role, update, and complexity implications of Ξ and Ξmax need clearer motivation and explanation.
- Theorem assumptions must be stated precisely (fixed vs learned kernel hyperparameters, randomness behind 1−η probability).
- The infinite-metric exploration loss and combining GP filtering with infinite-metric exploration is novel and insightful.
- Dependence on dimension D is unfavorable and causes λ→1 as D grows, limiting practical significance in high dimensions.
- If the UCB condition under adaptive hyperparameters can be justified or the theorem reframed, the work would be a meaningful advance.
- Pros: novel idea, exponential regret claim without δ-covers, interpretable analysis, useful discussion of when GP helps, solid empirical results and code.
- Cons: unjustified UCB event under adaptive hyperparameters, notation/typo errors, nonstandard Ngp dependence, limited ablations, and incomplete complexity analysis.
- Suggested fixes: prove UCB containment under stated modeling assumptions or separate deterministic vs probabilistic parts of the theorem; correct typos and formalize notation.
- Suggested experiments: add ablations (no-GP filter, varying Ξmax), sensitivity to kernel misspecification, and complexity analysis of GP filtering step.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept pending fixes to the UCB assumption, clarity and notation, and stronger empirical/complexity analyses.
