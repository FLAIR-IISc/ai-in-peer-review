- Introduces IMGPO, a Bayesian optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: consider infinitely many possible upper bounds (semi-metrics) instead of a single GP-derived bound.  
- Provides theoretical analysis proving exponential regret O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1.  
- Claims a significant improvement over prior polynomial-regret GP methods (e.g., BaMSOO).  
- Avoids impractical δ-cover sampling and addresses an open problem from Wang et al.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on benchmarks.  
- Theoretical contribution is rigorous and technically sound, with a detailed proof of Theorem 1.  
- Paper should more explicitly compare the exponents in regret bounds and quantify practical improvements.  
- Clarely written and well-organized, supported by pseudocode and illustrative figures.  
- Explanation of the infinite-metric exploration loss ρ_t is brief; practical implications and trade-offs need more emphasis.  
- Original and well-positioned relative to prior work (first GP-based method to combine multi-bound idea with exponential convergence).  
- Significant practical advance but limited by scalability in higher dimensions (acknowledged but not fully addressed).  
- Empirical evaluation is convincing but the link between theoretical rates and log-scale experimental plots could be clearer.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
