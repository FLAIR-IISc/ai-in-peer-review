- Proposes IMGPO, a GP-based global optimization algorithm achieving exponential convergence without δ‑cover sampling by considering all possible unknown bounds and using GP UCB to prune them.

- Proves an exponential regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1, improving over prior polynomial bounds like BaMSOO.

- Provides clear theoretical analysis linking the unknown semi-metric bound, GP UCB, and infinite‑metric exploration loss to the regret.

- Introduces the novel "infinite-metric" idea that simultaneously considers all candidate bounds, bridging bound-based and GP-based optimization approaches.

- Empirically outperforms baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI, GP-UCB) across eight benchmark functions and multiple dimensions.

- Experimental setup uses a single kernel with randomly initialized hyperparameters and examines the effect of the Ξmax parameter.

- Main theoretical concern: Theorem 1’s λ < 1 requirement is not justified—α/(2CDρ̄t) > 2 may not hold in practice and needs clarification.

- Experimental rigor issue: Lack of statistical significance testing and incomplete reporting of means/variances across methods weakens empirical claims.

- Scalability concern: Exponential convergence degrades with increasing dimension (λ→1), and high-dimensional performance is insufficiently analyzed beyond one REMBO-based test.

- Algorithm clarity: Pseudocode (Algorithm 1) is complex and would benefit from a clearer, stepwise walkthrough of the main operations.

- Overall assessment: Significant and novel theoretical contribution with supporting experiments, but requires clarification on λ and stronger empirical validation.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Arguments for acceptance: First GP-based method with exponential convergence w/o δ-cover sampling; novel theoretical framework; strong experimental results; clear distinction from prior work; thoughtful limitations analysis.

- Arguments against acceptance: Potential theoretical gap on λ < 1; missing statistical tests; limited high-dimensional evaluation.
