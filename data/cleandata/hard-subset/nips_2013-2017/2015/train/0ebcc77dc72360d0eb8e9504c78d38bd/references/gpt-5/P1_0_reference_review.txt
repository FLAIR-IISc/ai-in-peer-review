Summary and relation to prior work
The paper tackles deterministic global optimization of a nonconvex black-box function f over a bounded domain. It proposes IMGPO, a Gaussian-process-based algorithm that aims to achieve exponential convergence in simple regret without relying on the impractical δ-cover sampling of de Freitas et al. The key idea is to combine hierarchical optimistic partitioning (as in DOO/SOO) with a GP-based UCB to prune the search. The authors introduce an “unknown bound” assumption: there exists an unknown semi-metric ℓ around a global optimum x* such that f(x*) ≤ f(x) + ℓ(x, x*), with ℓ Hölder-like (Assumption 2). Because the tight bound is unknown, the algorithm effectively explores over infinitely many candidate bounds (“infinite-metric”), while using GP-UCB to discard candidates looser than the GP-derived bound. They define the infinite-metric exploration loss ρt to quantify the extra divisions from not committing to a single bound.

Algorithmically, IMGPO maintains a tree of hyperrectangles, selects intervals by center values at each depth, and uses GP-UCB to screen intervals (and to skip function evaluations when the UCB is already below the current best). Unlike GP-UCB, IMGPO does not solve a separate acquisition maximization problem.

Theoretically, under Assumptions 1–2 and a high-probability validity of GP-UCB (parameterized via ζM), the paper states an exponential regret bound of the form rN = O(λN+Ngp) with λ < 1, where Ngp counts “virtual” evaluations deferred via UCB, and the rate constant depends on dimension D, constants from SOO/DOO-style analyses, as well as ρ̄t and Ξn (algorithm-dependent quantities). Remarks discuss how GP affects ρ̄t and Ngp. Empirically, the authors compare to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI and GP-EI on several benchmarks, showing generally faster convergence and lower CPU time than standard GP methods (though slower than SOO), with a high-D REMBO wrapper experiment.

Strengths
- Interesting and timely attempt to bridge GP-based BO and optimistic partitioning, avoiding δ-cover sampling and auxiliary optimization of the acquisition.
- The “infinite-metric exploration loss” is a useful lens to explain when not committing to a single bound can help, and how GP information can reduce exploration cost.
- Theorems provide a readable decomposition of how GP (via Ngp and ρ̄t) can influence rates; the analysis connects to DOO/SOO constants and Hölder smoothness.
- Experiments are broad across multiple benchmark functions, with a consistent kernel/hyperparameter protocol to avoid hand-tuning; IMGPO is competitive to strong tree-based baselines.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Validity of UCB: The analysis assumes that f(x) ≤ U(x|D) holds with high probability across all uses M, but the paper does not state assumptions ensuring this (e.g., f is a GP sample path with fixed hyperparameters, or f lies in the RKHS with bounded norm with βt as in Srinivas et al., 2010). Empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates further complicate any union bound. This is crucial since pruning and the regret bound depend on UCB validity; the Rosenbrock2 experiment suggests violations in practice.
- Exponential rate qualification: The stated bound depends on ρ̄t and Ξn. Without a nontrivial upper bound on ρ̄t (and control of Ξn), one cannot conclude an unconditional exponential rate in N; λ can approach 1 as D or ρ̄t increase. The claim of “exponential convergence” should be qualified by conditions under which ρ̄t = O(1) (or at least sublinear), and by clarifying the role of Ξn.
- Notational/typographical ambiguities in the theorem and algorithm:
  - The definition “λ = 3 − α/(2 C D ρ̄t) < 1” reads as 3 minus a fraction; likely intended as λ = 3^(−α/(2 C D ρ̄t)). Please fix.
  - Constants C and CD are referenced but not clearly defined in the main text.
  - β = supx,x′ 1/2 ||x − x′||∞ is oddly defined; explain its role/necessity.
  - Algorithm 1 lines contain several unclear parts (e.g., lines 21–24: how i*_{h+ξ} is obtained; z(h,i*_h) uses T′ which can be exponentially large; lines 38 “Ξ ← Ξ + 2^2” likely a typo).
- Computational complexity: Step (iii) may compute many UCBs (limited by Ξmax), and T′ grows exponentially with ξ and D. A complexity analysis (in function evaluations and GP inference calls) would help position IMGPO vs. BaMSOO/SOO.
- Assumptions vs. practice: Assumption 2 (Hölder continuity w.r.t. ℓ) is reasonable, but the analysis also relies on GP-UCB validity with changing hyperparameters; this mismatch should be discussed and, if possible, mitigated (e.g., by freezing hyperparameters or proving robustness).
- Empirical baselines: While BaMSOO and SOO are appropriate, GP-PI/EI with naive acquisition optimization and a single kernel may understate strong GP baselines. Consider GP-UCB/ES/EI with multi-start DIRECT/CMA-ES and alternative kernels, or ablate IMGPO with/without GP to quantify the contribution of GP pruning.

Quality
- Theory is promising but currently underspecified in its probabilistic assumptions and precise constants; exponential rate claim should be tempered or further justified by bounding ρ̄t and clarifying UCB validity.
- Experiments are solid breadth-wise; more rigorous baselines and complexity measurements would strengthen claims.

Clarity
- Generally readable, but several notation/typo issues and some dense passages in Algorithm 1 and Theorem 1. Clarify definitions of all constants, fix exponents, and tighten pseudocode.

Originality
- The combination of infinite-metric optimistic partitioning with GP pruning and the analysis framing via ρt is novel relative to SOO/BaMSOO. The high-level idea of trading on multiple bounds traces to DIRECT/DOO but is extended here with GP information.

Significance
- If the theoretical gaps are addressed, this could be a notable step toward practical BO with strong rates without δ-covering or inner acquisition optimization. Current empirical gains are encouraging.

Questions for the authors
1. Under what precise assumptions on f and the GP/hyperparameters does f(x) ≤ U(x|D) hold uniformly over the algorithm’s uses? Can you adapt the RKHS-based analysis of Srinivas et al. (2010) to your setting with deterministic observations and changing hyperparameters?
2. Can you provide an upper bound on ρ̄t (in expectation or high probability) under Assumption 2 and some mild conditions on the GP posterior (e.g., variance decay), to make the “exponential” rate unconditional?
3. Please clarify the definition of λ, C, CD, β, and fix the typos in Algorithm 1 (e.g., line 38).
4. What is the computational complexity per iteration (number of UCB evaluations, GP updates)? How sensitive is performance to Ξmax and D?
5. How robust is IMGPO to GP mis-specification? Can you add an ablation where GP pruning is disabled to isolate the gain from GP?

Minor comments
- Provide the full derivation or a lemma ensuring the choice ζM = sqrt(log(π^2 M^2/(12 η))) yields the stated high-probability UCB property with your GP/hyperparameter protocol.
- The code URL should be anonymized for review or provided as supplementary material.

Pros
- Conceptual novelty in using GP to prune an infinite-metric optimistic search.
- Theoretical framework that isolates contributions of GP and exploration loss.
- Avoids auxiliary optimization of acquisitions; practical algorithmic contribution.
- Empirically strong on a range of benchmarks.

Cons
- Key theoretical assumption (UCB validity) is not clearly stated/justified, especially with empirical Bayes.
- Exponential rate claim hinges on unbounded quantities (ρ̄t, Ξn); needs qualification or bounds.
- Some algorithmic and notation clarity issues; limited complexity analysis and baseline strength.

Overall recommendation
Borderline accept if the authors can clarify the UCB assumptions, fix the theorem notation, and better characterize ρ̄t/complexity; otherwise, the contribution risks overclaiming. My inclination is weak reject in current form due to the theoretical gaps, but I view the direction as promising and encourage revision. Confidence: medium.
