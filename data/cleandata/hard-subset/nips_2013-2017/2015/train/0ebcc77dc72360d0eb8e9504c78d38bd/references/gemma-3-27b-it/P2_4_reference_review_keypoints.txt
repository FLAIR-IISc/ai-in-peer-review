- Presents IMGPO: a novel GP-based global optimization algorithm claiming exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Core idea: consider all possible Lipschitz-like bounds simultaneously, perform global and local searches, and use the GP to prune unlikely candidates.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis and an explicit exponential regret/convergence bound.  
- Empirical results show superior performance versus several baselines on diverse benchmark functions; code is publicly released.  
- Builds on de Freitas et al. (2017) and connects to broader Lipschitz and GP-based Bayesian optimization literature.  
- Strength: removes the impractical δ-cover sampling requirement, making theory more practical.  
- Strength: novel and clever use of simultaneous bounds guided by the GP to balance exploration and exploitation.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments and generally clear, well-organized writing.  
- Weakness: potential scalability issues in higher dimensions (λ approaches 1) with limited mitigation discussion.  
- Weakness: algorithm has several sensitive parameters (e.g., Ξmax, η) with insufficient sensitivity analysis or practical tuning guidance.  
- Weakness: sparse details on GP hyperparameter tuning; reliance on fixed initial hyperparameters may limit performance.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to more modern BO methods (e.g., multi-fidelity, meta-learning approaches).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, removes key practical limitation, convincing experiments, and code release.  
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about high-dimensional scalability and parameter sensitivity require further investigation.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with compelling theory and experiments; minor weaknesses do not outweigh merits.  
- Recommendation: Accept (Strongly).  
- Grades: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
