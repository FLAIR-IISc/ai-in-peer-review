

# Review of "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence"

## Summary
This paper introduces Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a novel Bayesian optimization algorithm that achieves exponential convergence without requiring the δ-cover sampling procedure previously used in de Freitas et al.'s work [1]. The authors address a key limitation in Bayesian optimization literature: while GP-UCB [15] provides a theoretically sound approach, its exponential convergence rate relies on impractical sampling procedures. IMGPO instead leverages the concept of "infinite-metric exploration" by simultaneously considering all possible semi-metric bounds rather than relying on a single bound from the GP prior. The approach combines elements from DIRECT [19] and SOO [18] algorithms, while maintaining the theoretical guarantees of exponential convergence. The paper provides a rigorous regret bound and demonstrates empirical superiority over existing methods including SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI.

## Strengths
1. **Theoretical Contribution**: The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by proving an exponential regret bound (O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1) without requiring δ-cover sampling, which is a major advancement over Wang et al.'s BaMSOO [2] that achieves only polynomial regret.

2. **Novel Conceptual Framework**: The "infinite-metric exploration" concept provides a new perspective on bound-based optimization methods. The authors clearly demonstrate how considering infinitely many possible bounds can outperform relying on a single bound, which is a valuable theoretical insight for the optimization community.

3. **Rigorous Analysis**: The paper provides detailed theoretical analysis with clear assumptions and a well-structured proof of the regret bound. The authors also include insightful remarks (Remarks 1-3) that explain the practical implications of their theoretical results.

4. **Empirical Validation**: The experimental results on eight benchmark functions demonstrate consistent improvements over baselines, particularly on multimodal functions. The authors properly address the practical concern of not knowing optimal kernel parameters, using a standard approach (isotropic Matern kernel) with blind initialization.

5. **Clear Relation to Prior Work**: The paper effectively situates its contribution within the Bayesian optimization literature, contrasting with GP-UCB [15], BaMSOO [2], DIRECT [19], and SOO [18], and clearly articulating the theoretical and practical advantages of their approach.

## Weaknesses
1. **Algorithm Clarity**: The description of the algorithm (particularly steps (i)-(iii) in Section 3.2) is somewhat difficult to follow without referring to the pseudocode. The explanation of how the "infinite-metric exploration" translates to the algorithm's mechanics would benefit from a more intuitive description.

2. **Parameter Sensitivity**: The paper mentions that increasing Ξmax can improve performance (Section 5), but doesn't provide guidance on selecting an optimal value for this parameter. The relationship between Ξmax and the algorithm's performance could be better explained.

3. **Domain Restriction**: The algorithm assumes Ω is a hyperrectangle, which is a limitation for many practical applications. While the authors mention generalizing this in Section 4, they don't provide details on how this could be accomplished.

4. **Scalability Discussion**: The paper correctly notes that the exponential rate degrades with higher dimensionality (Theorem 1), but doesn't provide concrete suggestions for addressing this challenge in high-dimensional settings, which is a significant practical limitation.

5. **Theoretical Assumptions**: The analysis relies on Assumption 2 (a specific bound on the semi-metric), which might not hold for all functions. The paper could better discuss the practical implications of this assumption.

## Recommendation
This paper presents a theoretically sound and practically valuable contribution to Bayesian optimization. The main strength lies in its novel theoretical framework for bound-based optimization that achieves exponential convergence without impractical sampling procedures. The paper is well-written, thoroughly analyzed, and empirically validated.

The main weaknesses are related to clarity of the algorithm description and the limited discussion of parameter sensitivity and domain generalization. However, these are not fundamental flaws in the technical contribution.

The paper meets all NIPS criteria: it is technically sound (quality), well-structured (clarity), presents a genuinely novel approach (originality), and provides both theoretical and empirical evidence of significant improvements over prior work (significance).

## Final Assessment
This paper makes a substantial contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization by resolving a long-standing open problem: achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling. The theoretical framework is novel and the experimental results support the claims. While there are minor clarity issues in the algorithm description and some limitations in domain restrictions, these do not undermine the paper's core contribution.

**Recommendation**: Accept
