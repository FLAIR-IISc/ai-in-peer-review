- Proposes IMGPO (Infinite-Metric GP Optimization) that claims exponential convergence without δ‑cover sampling by considering infinitely many candidate bounds and using the GP bound for pruning.  
- Provides a theoretical regret bound O(λ^(N+Ngp)) with λ < 1 and formalizes the infinite‑metric exploration loss ρt.  
- Theorem 1 and associated analysis convincingly establish exponential regret under stated assumptions.  
- Empirical results show IMGPO outperforming baselines (SOO, BaMSOO, GP‑PI, GP‑EI) on standard benchmarks.  
- Notes a key limitation: λ approaches 1 as dimension D increases, weakening exponential convergence in higher dimensions.  
- The paper acknowledges the dimensionality limitation but fails to thoroughly explore when IMGPO is preferable in high‑D settings.  
- Experimental evaluation is reasonable but lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability.  
- The claim that IMGPO is "much faster" than traditional GP methods needs stronger justification given potential costs of hierarchical partitioning.  
- Paper is well‑organized and clearly written; Algorithm 1 pseudocode is sufficiently detailed despite complexity.  
- Relationship to prior work (BaMSOO, SOO, DIRECT) is clearly articulated and distinctions are well explained.  
- Remarks 1–3 provide valuable theoretical insight, especially on benefits of multiple bounds even with a good known bound.  
- Original idea of integrating multiple bounds with GP to achieve exponential convergence without δ‑cover sampling is novel and well motivated.  
- Theoretical contributions go beyond the algorithm, offering useful conceptual understanding of bound selection trade‑offs.  
- Practical significance is promising but tempered by computational cost concerns and the dimensionality issue.  
- Strengths: first GP‑based exponential method without δ‑cover, clear theory, insightful discussion, and solid empirical comparisons.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient computational complexity discussion, limited exploration of dimensionality effects, lack of practical guidance for users, and shallow experimental cost analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to significant theoretical contribution.  
- Required revisions: expand discussion of computational complexity/scalability, provide guidance on when to use IMGPO vs alternatives, and include detailed computational cost analysis in experiments.
