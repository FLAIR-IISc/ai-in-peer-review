**Summary**
This paper addresses the problem of achieving exponential convergence rates for Bayesian optimization with Gaussian processes (GP-BO) in the deterministic setting. Previous work had either achieved this with the impractical assumption of a δ-cover sampling procedure or settled for polynomial rates with practical algorithms. The authors propose a novel algorithm, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), which is the first practical GP-BO method to achieve a provable exponential regret bound. The core idea is a conceptual departure from standard GP-UCB. Instead of relying solely on the upper confidence bound provided by the GP, IMGPO assumes the existence of an unknown, potentially tighter semi-metric that bounds the function's smoothness. The algorithm then uses a hierarchical partitioning scheme, akin to SOO, to implicitly search over an infinite family of these potential bounds, while cleverly using the GP-UCB to prune branches of the search tree that are provably suboptimal. This synthesis of the DIRECT/SOO line of work with modern GP-BO is the paper's key contribution. The authors provide a theoretical analysis supporting their exponential convergence claim and demonstrate strong empirical performance against several baselines, including SOO and BaMSOO, on a suite of benchmark problems.

**Strengths**

*   **Originality & Significance:** The paper's primary strength lies in its outstanding novelty. The idea to not fully trust the GP-derived bound and instead use it as a tool to accelerate a search over an infinite family of implicit bounds is both bold and powerful. This work elegantly bridges two historically separate approaches to global optimization: the statistical approach of Bayesian optimization and the deterministic partitioning approach of algorithms like DIRECT and SOO. By doing so, it solves a well-recognized open problem in the field: achieving exponential convergence for GP-BO without impractical sampling schemes. This is a significant theoretical advancement that pushes the frontier of our understanding of efficient global optimization.

*   **Strong Theoretical Contribution:** The main theoretical result, Theorem 1, formally establishes the exponential regret bound, a substantial improvement over the polynomial bounds of comparable practical algorithms like BaMSOO. The analysis framework is also insightful, particularly the introduction of the "infinite-metric exploration loss" (ρt) and the subsequent remarks. These remarks clearly articulate how the GP component and the infinite-metric search component contribute to the final bound, offering a clear rationale for why this hybrid approach is beneficial.

*   **Practical Advantages and Strong Empirical Results:** Beyond its theoretical appeal, the proposed IMGPO algorithm has practical benefits. Notably, it avoids the need for an auxiliary optimization of the acquisition function at each step, which is often a computational bottleneck in standard GP-BO. The experiments are well-designed, comparing against the most relevant baselines on a diverse set of functions. The results in Figure 2 are compelling, showing that IMGPO consistently and significantly outperforms other methods across various dimensionalities and function complexities.

**Weaknesses**

*   **Algorithmic Complexity:** While the high-level intuition is explained well, the algorithm itself (Algorithm 1) is quite complex. It involves several interacting loops, conditions, and parameter updates (e.g., the adaptive rule for Ξ) that feel somewhat ad-hoc. While the empirical results are strong, the justification for some of these specific design choices could be stronger. This complexity may pose a barrier to easy adoption and implementation for practitioners.

*   **Scalability:** The authors are transparent about the fact that the constant λ in their exponential bound approaches 1 as dimensionality increases, indicating that scalability remains a challenge. While the 1000-dimensional experiment using REMBO is a nice proof of concept for embedding IMGPO in other frameworks, it doesn't address the inherent scalability limitations of the core algorithm.

*   **Clarity of Pseudocode:** The pseudocode in Algorithm 1 is dense. A few steps, such as the check at Line 21-23 involving the computation of `z(h, i*h)` over a projected subtree `T'`, are difficult to parse without careful study. A more detailed walkthrough of this specific mechanism in the main text could have improved clarity.

**Arguments for Acceptance**
1.  **High Novelty:** The core idea of combining infinite-metric optimization with GP-based pruning is a fundamentally new and exciting direction for Bayesian optimization.
2.  **Major Theoretical Advance:** The paper solves an important open problem by providing the first practical GP-BO algorithm with a proven exponential convergence rate.
3.  **Strong Empirical Performance:** The algorithm demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on a range of challenging benchmark problems.
4.  **Conceptual Insight:** The analysis provides a valuable new perspective on how to combine different sources of information (statistical GP bounds and deterministic assumptions on smoothness) for efficient optimization.

**Arguments for Rejection**
1.  **Complexity:** The final algorithm is complex and contains some heuristic components, which may detract from its elegance.
2.  **Scalability:** The known theoretical and practical challenges with high-dimensional inputs are not fully resolved by the core method.

**Recommendation:** Accept

This paper presents a truly novel and significant contribution. The conceptual leap—to use the GP not as the sole source of the optimization bound, but as an accelerator for a more robust search strategy—is the kind of bold idea that can open up new avenues of research. The fact that this idea is backed by both a landmark theoretical result (exponential convergence) and strong empirical evidence makes it a standout submission. The weaknesses related to algorithmic complexity and scalability are minor in comparison to the magnitude of the contribution and are common challenges in this field. This is precisely the type of work that NIPS should champion: a novel, ambitious, and impactful paper that fundamentally advances the state of the art.
