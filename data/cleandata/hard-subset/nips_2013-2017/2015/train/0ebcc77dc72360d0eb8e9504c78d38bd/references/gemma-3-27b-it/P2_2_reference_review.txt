## Review of "Bayesian Optimization with Exponential Convergence"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel Gaussian Process (GP)-based global optimization algorithm (IMGPO) that achieves exponential convergence without relying on the computationally expensive δ-cover sampling procedure required by prior work (de Freitas et al., 2017).  The core idea is to simultaneously consider a large set of potential Lipschitz-like bounds (represented by a semi-metric) alongside the GP’s uncertainty estimates, effectively conducting both global and local searches. The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating an exponential convergence rate and empirical results showing IMGPO outperforms several baseline methods.

**Relationship to Prior Work:**

The paper correctly situates itself within the Bayesian Optimization (BO) literature, particularly addressing the limitations of existing exponential convergence results which hinged on impractical sampling strategies. It builds on the work of de Freitas et al. (2017) and Wang et al. (2017) by removing the δ-cover requirement.  The connection to bound-based search methods like DIRECT (Jones et al., 1993) and SOO (Munos, 2013) is clearly articulated, positioning IMGPO as a Bayesian approach leveraging the benefits of considering multiple potential bounds.  The references are comprehensive and relevant.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Guarantee:** The exponential convergence rate is a significant theoretical contribution, addressing a key open problem in the field.  The proof, while deferred to the supplementary material, is clearly stated in terms of key parameters.
* **Novel Approach:**  The idea of explicitly managing and balancing global and local exploration via simultaneous consideration of multiple potential bounds is clever and intuitive.  It offers a practical way to mitigate the limitations of relying solely on GP-derived uncertainty estimates.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on a variety of benchmark functions demonstrate the effectiveness of IMGPO in practice, consistently outperforming baselines.  The comparison to SOO, BaMSOO, and standard GP-based methods (PI, EI) is well-chosen.
* **Clear Writing and Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The algorithm is described clearly, aided by the illustrative Figure 1.
* **Practical Considerations:** The discussion regarding the effect of the GP bound tightness and the impact of parameters like Ξmax demonstrate a good understanding of the algorithm's behavior in practice.  The availability of the code is also a strong positive.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Scalability Concerns:** While the theoretical analysis is strong, the analysis suggests the convergence rate is sensitive to the input dimension (D).  The authors acknowledge this limitation, but further discussion on how to mitigate it in high-dimensional settings would be beneficial. The mention of leveraging assumptions from related work is promising, but could be expanded.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:**  The parameter Ξmax plays a crucial role in performance. The experiments provide some insight, but a more detailed sensitivity analysis would be valuable. How does the performance change with different values of η and the GP kernel parameters?
* **Computational Cost:**  While removing δ-cover sampling is a major improvement, the paper doesn’t provide a detailed computational complexity analysis comparing IMGPO to other methods. The simultaneous consideration of many bounds likely introduces additional overhead. Table 1 shows that it is slower than SOO, which is important to acknowledge.
* **Limited Discussion of Hyperparameter Optimization:** The authors use a standard empirical Bayesian method for hyperparameter optimization but lack detailed discussion about tuning of the hyperparameters. A discussion of the challenges/benefits of tuning hyperparameters within IMGPO would strengthen the paper.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical foundation with an exponential convergence guarantee.
* Novel and intuitive algorithm addressing a significant open problem in Bayesian Optimization.
* Compelling empirical results demonstrating superior performance compared to strong baselines.
* Clear presentation and well-motivated approach.
* Code availability promotes reproducibility.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Scalability to very high dimensions remains a concern.
* Parameter sensitivity requires further investigation.
* Lack of a detailed computational complexity analysis.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization.  The theoretical results are strong, the proposed algorithm is novel and well-motivated, and the empirical results are convincing. While the scalability and parameter sensitivity concerns warrant further investigation, they do not fundamentally undermine the value of the work.  The paper meets all four review criteria (Quality, Clarity, Originality, Significance) to a high degree.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**  I recommend accepting this paper, contingent on the authors addressing the following:

1.  **Elaborate on scalability:** Discuss potential strategies for mitigating the dimensionality issues highlighted in the analysis.
2.  **Provide a more detailed sensitivity analysis:** Investigate the impact of key parameters (Ξmax, η, GP kernel parameters) on performance.
3.  **Include a computational complexity analysis:** Compare the computational cost of IMGPO to other algorithms, providing a clearer understanding of its practical overhead.
4.  **Clarify hyperparameter optimization strategy**: Provide additional details of hyperparameter optimization (especially the GP parameters) and the effect of tuning within the IMGPO framework.
