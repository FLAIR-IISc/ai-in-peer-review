- Proposal: IMGPO combines SOO/DOO-style infinite-metric hierarchical partitioning with GP-UCB screening to perform deterministic global optimization of black-box nonconvex functions.  
- Main theoretical claim: IMGPO achieves exponential simple regret O(λ^{N+N_gp}) without δ-cover sampling by leveraging an unknown Hölder-type bound plus GP screening.  
- New analytic concepts: introduces “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρ_t and explicates the roles of N_gp (skipped evaluations) and Ξ_n in the bound.  
- Key theoretical caveat: the proof requires the UCB property f(x) ≤ μ+ςσ to hold uniformly with high probability, which typically needs fixed/known GP hyperparameters or RKHS assumptions—conditions not clearly stated.  
- Empirical Bayes mismatch: the algorithm updates hyperparameters online, which can invalidate UCB coverage and thus the stated theoretical guarantee; experiments even show UCB failures (“misleading prior”).  
- Typo/notation issues: suspected incorrect expression for λ (should likely be 3^{−α/(2 C D ρ̄_t)}), overloaded notation (n, N, t), and some constants (e.g., C) not defined when introduced.  
- Ξ_n concerns: Ξ_n appears in the bound but no upper bound or empirical scaling is provided; the update rule is heuristic and may increase computation.  
- Complexity concerns: step (iii) expands local subtrees and triggers many U calls; wall-clock and complexity scaling versus D, Ξ_max, tree depth and number of U calls M are not analyzed beyond a small table.  
- Experimental strengths: compares favorably to SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, GP-EI on benchmarks, includes REMBO+IMGPO in 1000D, and provides public code.  
- Experimental weaknesses: GP-PI/EI optimization setup is nonstandard (SOO + local gradient search), no ablation isolating GP screening vs infinite-metric exploration, no robustness tests to kernel/hyperparameter misspecification, and no noisy-observation experiments.  
- Clarity: generally readable with helpful 1D example and pseudocode, but some constructions (sets T′, line 21) and notation need clearer presentation.  
- Originality: the hybrid of infinite-metric partitioning with GP-UCB and the infinite-metric exploration loss are novel and distinct from BaMSOO and δ-cover GP bandits.  
- Significance: potentially notable advance if exponential guarantees can be solidified under realistic GP assumptions or if algorithmic robustness to hyperparameter learning is demonstrated.  
- Pros summary: conceptually clean hybrid method, exponential-rate bound without δ-cover/auxiliary optimization, useful analytic constructs, strong empirical results, public code.  
- Cons summary: theoretical guarantees rely on strong GP assumptions not stated, dependence on ρ̄_t and Ξ_n is heuristic and unbounded, computational overhead and many U calls unexplored, and experimental fairness/robustness ablations missing.  
- Requests/suggestions: state precise UCB assumptions (fixed kernel or RKHS bounds), fix λ and define constants, provide empirical traces of ρ̄_t/N_gp/Ξ_n, clarify GP-PI/EI optimization baseline, add ablations (no-GP and misspecified kernels), and discuss/extend to noisy observations.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and innovative; lean to accept if theoretical assumptions are clarified and empirical evaluation (ablation, robustness, complexity) is strengthened, otherwise address the gap between guaranteed theory and the implemented hyperparameter-learning algorithm.
