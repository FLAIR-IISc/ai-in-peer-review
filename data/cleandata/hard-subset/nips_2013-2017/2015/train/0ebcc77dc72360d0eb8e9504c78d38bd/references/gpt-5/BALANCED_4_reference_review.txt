Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper tackles deterministic global optimization of a black-box function f over a hyperrectangle Ω using Bayesian optimization with Gaussian processes (GPs). The authors propose Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), a hierarchical partitioning algorithm that blends optimism from GP-UCB with an “infinite-metric” exploration strategy inspired by DIRECT/SOO: rather than commit to a single smoothness bound, IMGPO maintains candidates across all possible (unknown) semi-metric bounds and uses the GP’s UCB to prune candidates deemed inferior. The main theoretical claim is an exponential simple-regret bound O(λ^{N+Ngp}) (λ<1), without the impractical δ-cover sampling used by de Freitas et al. (ICML’12), and without auxiliary global optimization of acquisition functions. The analysis introduces an “infinite-metric exploration loss” ρ_t quantifying the extra search due to not committing to a single bound. IMGPO is compared empirically against SOO (Munos’ optimistic optimization), BaMSOO (Wang et al., AISTATS’14), and GP-PI/EI. The authors argue IMGPO generally outperforms, while being much faster than standard GP BO that relies on solving auxiliary nonconvex acquisitions.

Relative to prior NIPS/ML work: the algorithm builds on DIRECT (Jones et al.), DOO/SOO (Munos), GP-UCB (Srinivas et al.), and BaMSOO. The key claim of novelty is achieving exponential convergence for a GP-based method without δ-cover, by combining infinite-metric search with GP-based pruning.

Strengths:
- Interesting and timely idea: leveraging a GP to prune within an optimistic infinite-metric search, thus reducing dependence on a single (often misspecified) Lipschitz/Hölder constant.
- Attempts to bridge two successful lines: DIRECT/SOO-type partitioning and GP-based BO, and provides intuition (Remarks 1–3) about how a non-tight bound can still be useful.
- Avoids auxiliary acquisition optimization, which can be costly/unreliable, and provides a practical algorithm with released code.
- Empirical results on several multimodal benchmarks suggest IMGPO is competitive or superior to strong baselines, including BaMSOO and SOO; use in REMBO shows potential in high-D with structure.

Weaknesses and concerns:

Quality (soundness and support):
- The key theorem and assumptions need clarification. Assumption 1 posits existence of an unknown semi-metric bound at the optimizer; Assumption 2 then specializes it to Hölder-type continuity with unknown constants. In parallel, the algorithm and its guarantees rely on the event f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with probability ≥ 1−η across all calls. This latter event is only well-justified under a Bayesian assumption (f is a GP sample with the specified kernel/hyperparameters), or under a frequentist RKHS-style assumption à la GP-UCB. The paper does not state such an assumption. Moreover, the experiments acknowledge that this UCB property often fails (Rosenbrock2), which undermines the stated high-probability guarantees as written.
- The main bound as stated has notational errors and ambiguity. For example, λ is written as “3 − α/(2CDρ̄_t) < 1,” which appears dimensionally incorrect; likely λ = 3^{−α/(2CDρ̄_t)}. Constants C, ρ̄_t, Ξ_n, and their dependence on dimension and algorithmic choices should be precisely defined in the theorem statement. It is unclear whether λ is a fixed constant independent of N or effectively shrinks with N via ρ̄_t, in which case “exponential” may be misleading in the worst case.
- The dependence on hyperparameter learning is not treated. The guarantee that f ≤ U with probability 1−η typically assumes fixed kernel/hyperparameters; online re-estimation breaks the Bayesian calibration and calls into question the validity of the union-bound choice of ς_M.
- The role and update rule for Ξ/Ξ_max and the complexity of step (iii) are underspecified. The text inconsistently references 3Ξ_max vs. 3^Ξ. A clearer complexity analysis per iteration is needed.

Clarity:
- The presentation suffers from typos, missing definitions, and notation inconsistencies (e.g., the expression for λ, uses of ς, updates for Ξ at line 38, and references to lines/steps). The algorithm description is difficult to follow without the figure. More compact, self-contained pseudocode with clear definitions of T′_{h+ξ}, ρ_t computation, and the GP-based “dummy” labels would help.
- The probabilistic model (Bayesian vs frequentist), and how it coexists with Hölder continuity assumptions, should be explicitly stated.

Originality:
- The “infinite-metric exploration loss” concept and the particular way GP-UCB is used to prune an SOO-style search are novel and interesting. While related in spirit to BaMSOO, the no-auxiliary-optimization design and the analysis angle appear new. However, the claimed exponential rate under broad Hölder continuity seems very strong; some discussion of lower bounds or conditions under which exponential is possible (e.g., near-optimality dimension) is warranted.

Significance:
- If the theory were correct and clearly scoped, a GP-based BO algorithm with exponential convergence without δ-cover would be highly impactful. The empirical results are promising. However, given the theoretical ambiguities and clarity issues, it is hard to assess the true generality and tightness of the guarantees.

Specific suggestions for improvement:
- Clarify the probabilistic assumptions. Either assume f is a sample from the GP prior, or adopt a frequentist framework (e.g., f in the RKHS of κ with bounded norm) and derive high-probability UCBs accordingly, citing Srinivas et al. (ICML’10). Explain how hyperparameter learning affects guarantees, or freeze hyperparameters for the theory.
- Fix the theorem statement: correct λ, define all constants precisely, state conditions under which λ is a constant independent of N, and provide a proof sketch in the main text.
- Reconcile the Hölder assumption with the GP-based bounds. If both are needed, justify consistency, or state that the probability is with respect to the GP prior regardless of reality (Bayesian regret).
- Provide a clear complexity analysis of step (iii) and the impact of Ξ_max on both runtime and regret. The current text contains conflicting bounds.
- Strengthen experiments: include ablations (without GP pruning; with varying Ξ_max), and fairness checks for GP-PI/EI (e.g., standard acquisition optimizers used in practice), sensitivity to kernel choice, and more runs for deterministic methods if any internal randomness exists (e.g., tie-breaking).
- Compare to additional relevant baselines when appropriate (e.g., DIRECT, HOO for stochastic analogs), and discuss near-optimality dimension to relate to DOO/SOO theory.
- Improve clarity and fix typos throughout; define all variables at first use and ensure the pseudocode compiles conceptually.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel and potentially impactful idea combining GP information with infinite-metric optimistic search; avoids auxiliary optimization.
- Promising empirical performance across diverse benchmarks.
- Insightful remarks on when GP UCB helps and how not committing to a single bound can be advantageous.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Theoretical guarantees hinge on unclear/inconsistent assumptions; UCB validity under hyperparameter learning and model misspecification is not addressed.
- The main theorem statement has errors and missing definitions; the “exponential” claim may not hold in the worst case as presented.
- Clarity issues and numerous typos make the method and analysis hard to parse and reproduce.
- Experimental setup for GP-PI/EI may disadvantage those methods; additional baselines/ablations are needed.

Recommendation: Reject (borderline). The paper introduces an interesting algorithmic idea with promising empirical results, but the theoretical foundation and clarity need significant revision. Addressing the above points could make this a strong submission in a future iteration.
