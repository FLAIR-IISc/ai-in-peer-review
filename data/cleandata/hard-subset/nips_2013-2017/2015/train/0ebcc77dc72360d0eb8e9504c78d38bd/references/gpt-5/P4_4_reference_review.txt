Summary and relation to prior work
The paper addresses deterministic global optimization of a black-box function over a bounded domain using Bayesian optimization with Gaussian processes (GPs). The main goal is to achieve an exponential convergence rate of simple regret without relying on the impractical δ-cover sampling procedure used in prior work. The proposed algorithm, Infinite-Metric GP Optimization (IMGPO), combines hierarchical partitioning (as in DIRECT and SOO/HOO/DOO) with GP-based upper confidence bounds (UCB) to prune candidate regions. A key conceptual contribution is to avoid committing to a single known smoothness bound; instead, the method implicitly considers infinitely many possible “unknown” semi-metric bounds around a global optimizer and uses the GP posterior to reduce this set.

Relative to de Freitas et al. (ICML’12) who obtained exponential regret using δ-cover sampling for GP bandits with deterministic observations, and BaMSOO (AISTATS’14) which obtained polynomial regret by combining SOO with GP-UCB, this paper claims exponential regret O(λN+Ngp) with λ<1, where N is the number of function evaluations and Ngp counts GP-based “virtual” evaluations. The paper relates the approach to DIRECT (Jones et al.) and SOO/DOO (Munos, NIPS’11), and discusses high-dimensional extensions via REMBO (Wang et al., IJCAI’13) and additive models.

Quality (technical soundness)
- Assumptions and guarantees: The analysis assumes (i) an unknown semi-metric upper bound at the global optimizer (Assumption 1) and (ii) Hölder continuity of this bound with parameters L>0, α>0, p≥1 (Assumption 2). These are standard-type smoothness conditions and the algorithm does not require the constants to be known. The main theorem gives an exponential regret bound that depends on quantities introduced to capture the cost of “not committing” to a specific bound (the infinite-metric exploration loss ρt), the lookahead depth Ξn used in GP pruning, and constants C, β that depend on the domain and division scheme.
- Use of GP-UCB: The method relies on f(x) ≤ U(x|D) holding at the finitely many UCB queries with probability ≥1−η via a schedule ςM = sqrt(log(π^2 M^2/(12η))). This is a union-bound-style schedule, but its validity depends on modeling assumptions (e.g., f being a sample from the chosen GP prior, or f in an RKHS with bounded norm) and on fixed kernel hyperparameters. The paper updates GP hyperparameters by empirical Bayes; without additional assumptions, this can invalidate the high-probability UCB property. The paper should clarify the probabilistic assumption under which f(x) ∼ N(μ,σ^2) conditionally holds for all queried points and whether the guarantee survives hyperparameter adaptation.
- Main bound: Theorem 1 states rN ≤ const · exp(−α[(N+Ngp)/(2CDρ̄t) − Ξn − 2] ln 3). The definition of λ in the text appears to contain a typographical error (“λ = 3 − α2CDρ̄t < 1”); presumably λ = 3−α/(2CDρ̄t). The bound is informative when ρ̄t is O(1) or grows slowly; in the worst case, remarks show ρ̄t can increase and the exponent weakens. No explicit upper bound on ρ̄t is provided in terms of problem parameters, leaving some looseness in the final guarantee.
- Algorithmic details: The hierarchical splitting into three subrectangles along the longest side aligns with DIRECT/SOO traditions. The pruning step (iii) uses GP-UCB over a finite set T′ determined by a lookahead depth ξ capped by Ξmax; the bound worsens with Ξn, reflecting computational effort. The pseudocode and the notion of GP-based “temporary” values are sensible, but some notation is imprecise (e.g., the definition of β and norms, see clarity below).

Overall, while the high-level proof strategy is plausible—exponential decrease in cell diameter plus finite “waste” due to infinite-metric exploration—the correctness of the high-probability GP-UCB filtering under hyperparameter learning requires clearer assumptions.

Clarity
- The paper is generally understandable, but several equations and definitions contain typographical or notational issues: the definition of λ, the expression “||(x−x′)||αp” (likely ||x−x′||p^α), and β = supx,x′ 1/2||x−x′||∞ could be clarified. Symbols N, n, and t are used for counts (evaluations, nodes, iterations) and should be clearly distinguished early.
- The role and bounds on ρt, ρ̄t, C, and Ξn would benefit from a concise summary. A short proof sketch for Theorem 1 (main ideas and where GP-UCB enters) would help.
- The union-bound rationale for ςM and the modeling assumptions under which f(x) ≤ U(x|D) holds with probability ≥1−η at all queried points should be stated explicitly.
- The experimental section references figures and a table; ensure legends, axes, and computational budgets are fully specified. The code link is helpful.

Originality
- The “infinite-metric” lens—explicitly accounting for the cost of not committing to a single smoothness bound and using GP posterior to reduce that cost—is novel. Achieving an exponential rate without δ-covers in a GP-based method appears new, contingent on the above assumptions. The analysis that decomposes the effect of GP via Ngp, ρ̄t, and Ξn is also original and insightful.

Significance
- If the exponential guarantee under realistic GP assumptions (including hyperparameter learning) holds, this is a meaningful theoretical advance over BaMSOO’s polynomial rate and bridges bound-based and GP-based optimization traditions. Empirically, IMGPO outperforms several baselines on standard benchmarks and interfaces with REMBO for high-dimensional tasks, indicating practical potential. The dimension dependence in the exponent and the role of ρ̄t limit scalability; nevertheless, the contribution is relevant to the NIPS community on Bayesian optimization and optimistic search.

Pros for acceptance
- Novel algorithmic framework combining infinite-metric optimistic search with GP-based pruning.
- Claimed exponential regret without δ-cover sampling; clear contrast with prior bounds.
- Useful analytical decomposition (remarks on Ngp, ρ̄t, Ξn) offering intuition.
- Empirical gains on multiple benchmarks; public code.

Cons for acceptance
- GP-UCB high-probability condition under hyperparameter updates and model misspecification is not rigorously justified; the main theorem’s probabilistic assumptions need to be explicit.
- Key notation and definitions (λ, norms, constants) contain typos/ambiguities; some parts are hard to parse.
- No explicit bound or behavior characterization for ρ̄t; the main rate can be loose without it.
- Experimental baselines and acquisition optimization choices (e.g., using SOO to optimize EI/PI) may not reflect best practices; missing comparisons to GP-UCB with strong optimizers.

Questions and suggestions for the authors
1. Precisely state the stochastic assumption underpinning f(x) ≤ U(x|D) with prob ≥1−η at all UCB query points, and discuss whether this holds under empirical Bayes hyperparameter updates. If needed, specify conditions (e.g., f in RKHS ball and fixed kernel) under which the theorem is valid.
2. Correct the definition of λ and clarify all constants (C, β) and norms in Assumption 2. Provide a self-contained summary table of symbols N, n, t, Ngp, ρt, ρ̄t, Ξn, Ξmax.
3. Provide bounds or typical behavior for ρ̄t (e.g., in terms of near-optimality dimension), and discuss scenarios where ρ̄t remains O(1).
4. Include an ablation: IMGPO without GP pruning (to isolate the infinite-metric effect) and with different Ξmax to show the trade-off. Also compare against GP-UCB with a strong global optimizer (e.g., multistart L-BFGS, DIRECT) and tuned exploration schedules.
5. Clarify computational complexity of step (iii) and provide wall-clock budgets for all methods. Ensure acquisition optimization baselines are representative.
6. Tighten notation and fix typographical issues throughout, and add a brief proof sketch highlighting the key steps that yield the exponential term.
