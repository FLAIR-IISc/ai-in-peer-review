- Proposal: IMGPO combines hierarchical partitioning (DIRECT/SOO style) with GP-UCB pruning and an “infinite-metric” view to avoid committing to a single Lipschitz bound.  
- Claim: Achieves exponential simple-regret decay under local Hölder assumptions without δ-cover sampling, with regret rN = O(λN+Ngp) (λ < 1).  
- Empirics: Outperforms SOO, BaMSOO, GP-PI, and GP-EI on standard multimodal benchmarks and a high-dimensional REMBO-style example.  

Strengths:
- Conceptual: Infinite-metric viewpoint is a neat, insightful explanation for combining optimistic partitioning with GP pruning.  
- Theoretical: If correct, the exponential regret result is a nontrivial improvement over prior GP-based methods (e.g., BaMSOO’s polynomial rates).  
- Practicality: Deterministic hierarchical splitting avoids auxiliary global acquisition optimization; UCB-based skip-evaluation is an intuitive efficiency trick.  
- Empirical: Strong performance under blind initialization and a common kernel class is a fair stress test.

Weaknesses / Concerns:
- Ngp dependence: The bound counts Ngp (GP-based dummy placements) with N, making the theoretical rate less clean; need relation Ngp = O(N) or a pure N-only bound.  
- λ typo/clarity: Theorem 1’s λ definition appears wrong (likely meant 3^(−α/(2CDρ̄t))) and must be corrected.  
- Adaptive hyperparameters: Re-estimating GP hyperparameters each iteration may invalidate the high-probability UCB envelope; this needs formal justification or revised assumptions.  
- Scalability/constants: Bound degrades with dimension; constants C, L, α, p, and effect of Ξn are not quantified, hindering practical assessment.  
- Assumptions: Assumption 1 is vacuous without Assumption 2; connection to near-optimality dimension should be made explicit.  
- Clarity/notation: Multiple typos and inconsistent notation (ρt vs ρτ, Ξ update, T′h+ξ construction) complicate comprehension and reproducibility.  
- Experimental limitations: Only deterministic tests; baselines for GP-PI/EI may be weak due to optimizer choice; missing wall-clock/runtime comparisons and ablations on Ξmax and kernel misspecification.

Quality:
- Theoretical claims hinge on correctness of UCB under adaptive hyperparameters and on interpreting Ngp; proofs are in the supplement but need stronger discussion and explicit probabilistic model.  
- Empirical results are promising but require more comprehensive evaluations and runtime analysis.

Clarity:
- Overall readable but marred by notational inconsistencies, typos, and dense algorithm description; needs clearer pseudocode and a small worked example.

Originality:
- The hybrid of infinite-metric optimistic partitioning with GP pruning and the claimed exponential rate (without δ-cover) appears novel relative to BaMSOO/SOO/GP-UCB.

Significance:
- If the adaptive-hyperparameter gap and Ngp interpretation are resolved, the result is a meaningful theoretical and practical advance for deterministic BO.

Questions / Suggestions:
- Fix the λ definition and ensure theorem matches exponential bound.  
- Precisely state probabilistic assumptions for UCB when hyperparameters are re-estimated; under what conditions does the envelope hold?  
- Relate Ngp to N or provide a regret bound solely in terms of N, or justify counting Ngp as a resource.  
- Provide complexity/runtime bounds for step (iii), clarify Ξ update, and show runtime vs. Ξmax trade-offs.  
- Discuss extension to noisy observations and whether guarantees can be retained.  
- Add ablations on kernel mis-specification, stronger acquisition optimizers for GP baselines, and report wall-clock time.

Verdict:
- Borderline accept: compelling idea and potential impact, but require clarifications/fixes on adaptive hyperparameters, Ngp interpretation, notation, and experiments.
