- Introduces IMGPO, a GP-based Bayesian optimization algorithm claiming exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling.  
- Uses an unknown semi-metric plus GP uncertainty and hierarchical partitioning to balance exploration and exploitation.

- Positions work within Bayesian optimization, Lipschitz/global optimization, and cites relevant open problems and related methods (de Freitas et al., Wang et al., Kandasamy et al.).  
- Connects to bound-based search methods (A*, SOO) and discusses alternatives for high-dimensional BO.

- Provides a rigorous theoretical proof of exponential convergence under reasonable assumptions.  
- Novel idea: implicitly leveraging a potentially tighter unknown semi-metric together with GP uncertainty.  
- Strong empirical results: IMGPO outperforms competing algorithms on benchmark functions.  
- Clear writing and algorithm description with code publicly available for reproducibility.  
- Ablation studies included, offering some insight into component impacts.

- Scalability concerns: convergence rate may diminish as dimensionality increases (λ→1 with larger D).  
- Parameter sensitivity: performance can depend on choice of Ξmax and lacks a principled tuning method.  
- Ablation studies are limited; more detailed component analysis is needed.  
- Assumption 1 (existence of unknown semi-metric) needs stronger justification and discussion of applicable function classes.  
- Experiments rely on isotropic Matern kernel; limited exploration of advanced kernel choices or kernel learning.

- For acceptance: solves an important open problem with theoretical guarantees, novel method, strong empirical results, and reproducible code.  
- Against acceptance: concerns about high-dimensional scalability, parameter sensitivity, limited ablations, and insufficient justification of assumptions and comparisons to recent BO advances.

- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; merits acceptance.  
- Reviewer rating: Accept (Strong Accept), Confidence 8/10.
