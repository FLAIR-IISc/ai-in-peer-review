This paper proposes a novel Gaussian Process (GP)-based global optimization algorithm (IMGPO) that achieves an exponential convergence rate without relying on the computationally demanding δ-cover sampling procedure required by de Freitas et al. [1]. The core idea is to simultaneously consider all possible bounds on the objective function, leveraging the GP prior to prune unlikely candidates and combine global and local search strategies via hierarchical partitioning. The method is theoretically grounded, with a regret bound analysis proving exponential convergence under certain assumptions.

The paper clearly builds upon existing work in Bayesian Optimization, particularly addressing the limitations of previous approaches like [1, 2]. It positions itself well within the context of bound-based search methods and highlights the importance of bound tightness. The connection to SOO [18] and the comparison with other GP-based methods (GP-PI, GP-EI) provide a valuable frame of reference. The authors carefully address the practical concern of unknown kernel hyperparameters by using a standard empirical Bayesian method.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The proposed IMGPO algorithm introduces a unique approach by considering all possible bounds on the objective function, offering a potentially significant improvement over existing methods that rely on a single bound.
*   **Theoretical Guarantees:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis, proving an exponential convergence rate, which is a strong result in the field of global optimization.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The algorithm description is relatively clear, aided by the illustrative figure. The connection to existing concepts like bound-based search and hierarchical partitioning helps readers understand the underlying principles.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of IMGPO on a range of benchmark functions, outperforming several competing algorithms. The comparisons with SOO, BaMSOO, and standard GP methods (GP-PI, GP-EI) are valuable.
*   **Practical Considerations:** The authors acknowledge and address practical concerns, such as the unknown kernel hyperparameters and scalability issues.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Complexity:** The algorithm appears quite complex, with several parameters (Ξmax, η) and intricate steps in the hierarchical partitioning process. This complexity might make it difficult to implement and tune in practice.
*   **Scalability Discussion:** While the paper touches upon scalability limitations in higher dimensions, the discussion feels somewhat brief. The reliance on REMBO [14] for the 1000-dimensional problem suggests that IMGPO may not scale effectively without additional assumptions or modifications.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The impact of parameters like Ξmax isn't fully explored. The statement that increasing it doesn't always improve performance is acknowledged, but a more detailed sensitivity analysis would be beneficial.
*   **Assumptions:** The analysis relies on certain assumptions (Assumption 1 and 2) about the objective function, which may not hold in all real-world scenarios. The authors acknowledge this, but a discussion of the robustness of the algorithm to violations of these assumptions would be valuable.
*   **Figure 1 detail:** Figure 1 is quite busy and hard to decipher, and doesn't contribute to understanding the method.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound algorithm with strong performance on benchmark functions.
*   It addresses a significant limitation of previous Bayesian Optimization approaches by eliminating the need for δ-cover sampling.
*   The theoretical analysis provides valuable insights into the convergence properties of the algorithm.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the practical effectiveness of IMGPO.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The algorithm's complexity and parameter sensitivity might hinder its adoption in practice.
*   The scalability limitations in higher dimensions need further investigation.
*   The assumptions underlying the theoretical analysis may not always hold in real-world applications.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The proposed IMGPO algorithm is novel, theoretically well-grounded, and experimentally validated. While the algorithm’s complexity and scalability limitations are concerns, the benefits of achieving exponential convergence without δ-cover sampling outweigh these drawbacks. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to improve clarity regarding parameter tuning and scalability. Specifically, the authors could benefit from providing more guidance on selecting appropriate values for Ξmax and discussing potential strategies for addressing scalability issues in high-dimensional spaces. Improving Figure 1 would also aid clarity.
