- Proposes a Bayesian Global Optimization variant that generates future samples via space/interval partitioning.  
- Uses UCB to avoid the internal global optimization problem of selecting the next sample.  
- Theoretical analysis shows exponential-regret improvements over previous work.  
- Algorithm is clearly described and illustrated with an example.  
- Paper makes a novel contribution and is overall clearly presented.  
- Major reservation: experimental results appear to be only on 1D test functions (not explicitly stated).  
- Concern that the interval-partitioning approach won’t scale to higher dimensions because required resolution grows exponentially.  
- Method is related to DIRECT, which is known to perform poorly in high dimensions—this issue should be discussed.  
- Scalability concerns don’t affect the theoretical contribution but are significant for practical use.  
- Minor: unclear whether UCB is required or if other criteria (e.g., expected improvement) could be used despite “for brevity.”  
- Minor: unclear sentence about “simultaneously conduct global and local searches based on all the candidates of the bounds.”  
- Minor: clarify why at n=16 the far-right region has no function evaluation.  
- Overall: sound and novel work but evaluation appears limited to 1D problems.
