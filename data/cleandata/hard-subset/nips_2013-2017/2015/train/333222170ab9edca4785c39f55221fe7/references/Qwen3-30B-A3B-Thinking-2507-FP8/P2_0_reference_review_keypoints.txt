- Summary: Proposes "on-the-job learning" using Bayesian decision theory and a stochastic-game model, approximated by MCTS, to query crowds selectively and balance accuracy, latency, and cost; evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image tasks with large cost and accuracy gains.

- Strength (Quality): Technically sound framework with strong theoretical grounding, comprehensive experiments, open-source implementation, and careful acknowledgment of limitations.

- Strength (Clarity): Exceptionally well-written and organized with clear formulation, helpful figures, and reproducible experimental detail.

- Strength (Originality): Novel on-the-job learning concept that dynamically improves during test time and applies Bayesian decision theory in a principled way, distinct from prior active/online methods.

- Strength (Significance): Highly relevant to real-world deployment with scarce labels, demonstrating substantial practical impact (e.g., order-of-magnitude cost reduction on NER) and broad applicability.

- Weakness (Quality): Lacks thorough discussion of failure cases such as persistent crowd errors, systematic biases, generalization beyond frozen worker pools, and computational scalability/MCTS overhead.

- Weakness (Significance): Method’s effectiveness depends on rapid crowd responses; paper insufficiently examines scenarios with long response times (e.g., sentiment analysis).

- Overall Assessment: High-quality, well-executed, and significant contribution whose strengths outweigh moderate weaknesses, with compelling experimental evidence.

- Recommendation: Accept — valuable advance for active learning and practical AI deployment, with minor improvements suggested around robustness and scalability.
