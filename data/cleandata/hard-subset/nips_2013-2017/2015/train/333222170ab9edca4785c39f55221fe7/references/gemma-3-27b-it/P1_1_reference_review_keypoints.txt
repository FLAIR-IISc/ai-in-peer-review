- Presents a novel on-the-job learning framework that queries crowd labels selectively to balance accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Models the problem as a stochastic game and applies Bayesian decision theory, approximated via Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS).  
- Demonstrates effectiveness of LENSE on NER, sentiment, and image classification with significant cost reductions and accuracy gains.  
- Positions work relative to online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing, emphasizing active querying before prediction.  
- Distinguishes from prior work (e.g., Legion:AR) by providing a principled machine learning foundation for on-the-job learning.  
- Novelty and significance: practical paradigm with a principled Bayesian decision-theoretic formulation.  
- Technical soundness: well-motivated stochastic-game formulation and MCTS with progressive widening.  
- Empirical validation: convincing experiments and a useful ablation study demonstrating Bayesian benefits.  
- Implementation & reproducibility: public code and CodaLab setup are strong positives.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, with helpful figures (e.g., Figure 2 illustrating the game tree).  
- Complexity: framework has many parameters and design choices, making tuning and generalization challenging.  
- Crowd modeling: response model (70% accuracy) is simplistic and may not reflect real worker variability and bias.  
- Task specificity: experiments cover clear prediction tasks; generalization to open-ended or more complex tasks is unclear.  
- Limited baselines: comparisons lack recent, sophisticated active learning algorithms.  
- Section 3: needs more intuitive explanation of how progressive widening handles continuous time.  
- Section 4: Algorithm 1 should include more implementation details (e.g., UCT exploration constant, successor selection).  
- Section 5: should provide per-task breakdown of cost savings and further discussion of worker quality.  
- Related work: could better reference recent reinforcement learning advances for sequential decision-making.  
- Acceptance pro: addresses an important practical problem with a novel, technically sound approach and reproducible results.  
- Acceptance con: complexity, simplistic crowd model, and limited comparisons to state-of-the-art active learning methods.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution that warrants acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to detail MCTS implementation, improve crowd modeling, and add stronger active learning comparisons.
