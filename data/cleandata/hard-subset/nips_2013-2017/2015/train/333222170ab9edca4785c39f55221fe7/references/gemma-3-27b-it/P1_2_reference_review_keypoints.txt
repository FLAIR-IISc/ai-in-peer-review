Summary: Proposes "on-the-job learning" that queries crowds at test time, formulated as a stochastic game solved with Bayesian decision theory and MCTS; demonstrates LENSE on NER, sentiment, and image tasks with cost/accuracy gains.

Related work: Clearly situates work among online/active learning and crowdsourcing, distinguishing test-time learning and connecting to Legion:AR while filling theoretical gaps.

Novelty: Introduces a practical, novel on-the-job learning paradigm and a Bayesian decision-theoretic formulation for query optimization.

Technical soundness: Rigorous mathematical formulation and reasonable approximation via MCTS with progressive widening; frozen worker responses used to control variability.

Empirical results: Strong results across tasks, e.g., 8% F1 over single human and 28% over online learning on NER; shows cost reductions and accuracy improvements versus baselines.

Clarity: Generally well-written and clear, though some dense sections (game tree) need careful reading; Figure 2 aids understanding.

Reproducibility: Open-source LENSE and CodaLab experiments enable reproducibility.

Complexity: Framework complexity (stochastic game, Bayesian theory, MCTS) may hinder adoption by non-experts.

Scalability: MCTS computational cost may not scale to large/high-dimensional problems; scalability not fully addressed.

Crowd model assumptions: Assumes optimistic/homogeneous crowd accuracy (70%); real-world heterogeneity and biases underexplored.

Limited ablations: Lacks detailed ablation studies on key components (utility weights, progressive widening) to isolate contributions.

Arguments for acceptance: Novel, practical approach with sound technical contributions, strong empirical evidence, and open-source release.

Arguments against acceptance: Complexity, potential scalability issues, and simplistic crowd modeling.

Overall assessment: Strong paper with high potential impact; benefits outweigh drawbacks but minor revisions needed.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add scalability discussion, test crowd sensitivity and improved modeling, include ablation study, and clarify the game tree explanation.
