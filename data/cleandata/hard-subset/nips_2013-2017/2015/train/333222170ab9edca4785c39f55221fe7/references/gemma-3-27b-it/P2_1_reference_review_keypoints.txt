- Introduces “on-the-job learning”: query crowds for uncertain parts, improve model online while minimizing cost and latency.
- Models problem as a stochastic game and applies Bayesian decision theory with MCTS for approximate policy optimization.
- Demonstrates LENSE on NER, sentiment, and image classification with cost reductions and accuracy gains versus baselines.
- Positions work between online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing; distinguishes proactive test-time querying and adaptive policies.
- Correctly cites and differentiates from Legion:AR; related work section is comprehensive.
- Novel and significant paradigm addressing limited labeled data with practical, cost-effective crowdsourcing.
- Solid theoretical formulation using stochastic games and Bayesian decision theory.
- Use of MCTS with progressive widening is a reasonable approximation for policy search.
- Empirical results are convincing across diverse tasks and baselines; ablation shows cost reduction over time.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides publicly available code and worksheets for reproducibility.
- Models real-world factors like worker response times and accuracy, increasing realism.
- Technical complexity of MCTS and stochastic game details may be hard to follow; a simpler walkthrough would help.
- Experiments use pre-collected worker statistics, which may bias results and not reflect live crowdsourcing dynamics.
- Scalability concerns: MCTS computational cost may be prohibitive for large-scale or high-dimensional problems.
- Limited error analysis; more detailed failure-mode investigation would be beneficial.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel practical approach, solid technical contributions, strong empirical evidence, and available code/data.
- Arguments against acceptance: system complexity may hinder adoption, crowdsourcing setup may lack realism, and scalability issues remain.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contribution; strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommended for acceptance (Strong Accept, 8/10).
