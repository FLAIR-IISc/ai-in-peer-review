- Introduces "on-the-job learning": combine active querying of crowd workers with simultaneous model improvement to avoid large upfront labeled data.
- Novel stochastic game formulation models trade-offs between accuracy, latency, and cost.
- Bayesian decision-theoretic approach with MCTS and progressive widening is technically sound and appropriate for continuous-time decisions.
- Strong empirical results across NER, sentiment, and image tasks, showing notable cost reductions and improved accuracy (e.g., 88.4% F1 on NER with >10x cost reduction).
- Practical implementation with open-source code and careful crowdsourcing design (worker training, payments, response-time modeling).
- Ambiguous cost model: unclear whether reported cost reductions are monetary, time-based, or a weighted combination, and how parallel vs. sequential queries factor in.
- Insufficient comparison to Legion:AR; claim of novelty over prior work is questionable without deeper contrast.
- Lacks failure analysis: no exploration of highly ambiguous inputs, systematic worker biases, or long-term effects of noisy worker labels.
- Misleading baseline comparison to online learning, since online learning typically assumes ground-truth labels unavailable in this setting.
- Scalability concerns: no discussion of scaling to large input streams or realistic, dynamic worker pools (evaluation used fixed 5 responses per label).
- Overall assessment: meaningful, well-executed contribution at the intersection of active learning and crowdsourcing with solid theory and practice.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify cost model, better differentiate from Legion:AR, and expand limitations discussion.
