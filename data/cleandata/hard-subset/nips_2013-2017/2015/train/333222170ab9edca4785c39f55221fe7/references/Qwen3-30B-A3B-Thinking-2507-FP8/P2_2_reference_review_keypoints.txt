- Introduces "on-the-job learning": system queries crowd during inference, uses responses to update model, and becomes self-sufficient without large labeled datasets.  
- Models the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Approximates the optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and progressive widening.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification with meaningful baselines.  
- Shows large cost reductions and accuracy improvements (e.g., > an order of magnitude cost reduction on NER and ~8% F1 gain).  
- Technical approach is sound with a thorough theoretical foundation (game tree, utility, environment model).  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and statistically supported.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and reproducible (open-source code and CodaLab workspace).  
- Strongly differentiates from related work (online learning, active classification, Legion:AR).  
- Novel contribution: dynamic, inference-time querying and Bayesian/stochastic-game formulation not addressed by prior work.  
- Significant practical impact: reduces deployment cost while maintaining/improving accuracy; queries per example decline as model learns.  
- Minor limitations: scalability of MCTS to larger inputs/complex models and limited discussion on why Bayesian approach is necessary versus simpler heuristics.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — meets criteria of quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
