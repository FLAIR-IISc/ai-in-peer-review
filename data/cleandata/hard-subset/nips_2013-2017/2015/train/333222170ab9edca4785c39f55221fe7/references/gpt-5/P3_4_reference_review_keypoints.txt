- Summary: Introduces "on-the-job learning," a decision-theoretic framework that issues asynchronous crowd queries at test time to maintain initial accuracy and reduce crowd reliance over time, using a Bayesian utility and an MCTS planner (LENSE) for CRF-based NER and other tasks.
- Relation to prior work: Positions against online/active learning and links to crowd-POMDPs, real-time crowd systems, and budgeted/adaptive feature acquisition literature.
- Strength: Clear, timely problem formulation with a principled decision-theoretic game model that accounts for cost and latency.
- Strength: Uses MCTS with progressive widening to handle mixed discrete/continuous planning, a sensible approximate planner choice.
- Strength: Emphasizes structured prediction (token-level NER queries) and demonstrates qualitative behaviors like parallel vs sequential querying.
- Strength: Provides open-source implementation and reproducibility materials.
- Weakness: Response-noise model is simplistic (fixed 70% accuracy, uniform errors), ignoring worker heterogeneity, item difficulty, and established crowd models (Dawid–Skene/GLAD).
- Weakness: Response-time model is underspecified and relies on pre-collected times without clear parametric form or covariates.
- Weakness: Policy evaluation uses a frozen pool of 5 worker responses per label, risking underestimated variance and limited external validity; no live end-to-end trials.
- Weakness: Utility/metric mismatch—utility optimizes expected MAP accuracy but NER evaluation uses span F1; "Accuracy" in Eq.2 is undefined and calibration issues are unaddressed.
- Weakness: CRF is trained directly on noisy crowd labels without explicit noise modeling or EM-style marginalization of true labels.
- Weakness: Baselines are weak or mismatched (unrealistic online learning, strawman threshold); lacks comparisons to myopic VOI or TurKontrol-style POMDPs.
- Weakness: Missing sensitivity analyses and ablations (weights wM/wT, MCTS parameters, planner latency, parallel vs sequential queries).
- Weakness: Clarity gaps—undefined accuracy metric, thin response-time/noise specifications, and unexamined impact of waiting constraints.
- Significance: Problem is important and the framework is promising, but empirical evidence is insufficient due to modeling and evaluation limitations.
- Key questions: How are wM and wT chosen and how sensitive are results? What exactly is "Accuracy" in Eq.2 for structured outputs? Can worker-reliability models be incorporated and compared? What is planner runtime scaling? Are there live experiment results?
- Pros: Novel integration of decision-theoretic planning with real-time crowd interaction, clear formalization, and open-source release.
- Cons: Oversimplified environment/noise models, limited baselines and simulated evaluation, and utility/metric misalignment without calibration or ablations.
- Recommendation: Weak reject; urges stronger environment modeling (worker reliabilities), live experiments, better baselines, and sensitivity/ablation studies to strengthen claims.
