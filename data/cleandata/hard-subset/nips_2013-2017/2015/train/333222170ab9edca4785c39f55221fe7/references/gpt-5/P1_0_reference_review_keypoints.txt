- Proposes "on-the-job learning": interleave ML predictions with real-time crowd queries at test time to maintain high quality from day one.  
- System issues token-level queries, waits for asynchronous retainer responses, and decides when to stop and predict.  
- Models decision problem as a stochastic game between system and crowd using CRF pθ(y|x), crowd response model pR, and latency model pT.  
- Utility trades off expected MAP-prediction accuracy against monetary and latency costs.  
- Uses MCTS with UCT and progressive widening to approximate intractable optimal policies over mixed discrete–continuous spaces.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face classification with improved accuracy–cost tradeoffs versus pure human or machine baselines.  
- Open-source implementation (LENSE) and frozen response pools provided for reproducibility.  
- Sits at intersection of online learning, active learning, crowdsourcing control, and structured prediction; handles asynchronous responses and structured outputs.  
- Clear motivation and practical relevance for rapid deployment with scarce labeled data.  
- Principled decision-theoretic formulation explicitly balancing accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Algorithmic novelty: applying MCTS with progressive widening yields intuitive querying behaviors (parallel vs sequential).  
- Empirical breadth across tasks and reported substantial cost savings (notably on NER).  
- Environment modeling is simplistic: pR fixed at 0.7 ignores per-worker reliability, item difficulty, and class confusions; pT unspecified.  
- CRF training with noisy/repeated labels is underspecified (no detail on handling multiple noisy responses).  
- Utility definition (ExpAcc) and its efficient computation/calibration for structured y are unclear and may bias decisions.  
- Real-time feasibility not evaluated: no runtime/compute budget, simulation count, or planning overhead reported.  
- Evaluation lacks statistical rigor: no error bars, significance tests, dataset splits, or sensitivity analyses for key parameters.  
- Simulation protocol (freezing small response pools) may distort real-world variance, correlations, and nonstationarity.  
- Design simplification forbidding new queries while waiting is unablated and its impact unquantified.  
- Clarity issues: missing modeling/implementation details, hyperparameters, rollout policies, typos, and incomplete references.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of structured prediction and real-time crowd querying via MCTS, but limited comparison to VOI/POMDP baselines.  
- Significance: potentially impactful if made robust, but simplified crowd models and weak baselines limit the strength of claims.  
- Suggested improvements: learn richer pR/pT models, clarify pθ learning from noisy labels, report latency and planning costs, add stronger baselines and ablations, and provide trade-off curves with error bars.  
- Pros for acceptance: important setting, principled formulation, elegant integration, open-source release, and encouraging empirical gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: oversimplified environment/learning models, limited evaluation rigor, and unvalidated design choices.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept pending clarifications and additional experiments.
