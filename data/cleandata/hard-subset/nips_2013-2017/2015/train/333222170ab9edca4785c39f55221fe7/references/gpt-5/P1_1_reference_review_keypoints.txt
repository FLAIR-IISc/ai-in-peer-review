- Proposes LENSE: an on-the-job learning framework that interleaves model inference with real-time crowd queries at test time.  
- Formalizes the interaction as a stochastic game with actions to query tokens, wait for asynchronous responses, or return a prediction.  
- Uses a Bayesian decision-theoretic utility that trades off expected prediction accuracy against monetary and latency costs.  
- Approximates the intractable optimal policy via Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with progressive widening for continuous response times.  
- Models the environment with a CRF pθ(y|x), a simple fixed-response noise model pR, and a latency model pT.  
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and face classification show cost reduction versus pure crowdsourcing and improved accuracy over pure ML baselines.  
- Provides a retainer-based real-time crowd setup and open-source code and data.  
- Positions the work relative to online/active learning and test-time feature acquisition by emphasizing pre-decision information acquisition and crowd costs/delays.  
- Closest to Legion:AR but adds a principled Bayesian decision-theoretic formulation with structured prediction and MCTS planning.  
- Relates to POMDP-based crowdsourcing optimization and crowd reliability models, with novelty in structured prediction and per-instance accuracy–cost–latency optimization.  
- Strength: clear, principled formulation linking expected utility to accuracy, time, and money.  
- Strength: reasonable CRF-based environment model capturing partial observations and redundancy value.  
- Strength: pragmatic planner (MCTS + progressive widening) for a challenging continuous-time game.  
- Strength: solid experimental setup across tasks with reproducible code.  
- Weakness: utility–metric mismatch—planning uses ExpAcc of MAP label while evaluation reports F1 (especially for NER).  
- Weakness: simplistic fixed pR (70% correct, uniform otherwise) ignores class, context, and worker variability and may bias the planner.  
- Weakness: evaluation simulates worker responses from a pre-collected pool which may distort adaptivity benefits and ignores feedback loops or worker learning.  
- Weakness: missing computational performance details—planning time per decision, number of simulations, and scalability with sequence length and outstanding queries.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter choices for wM, wT, and latency model pT are not fully specified and need sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: no ablations to isolate contributions (sequential vs parallel querying, progressive widening effect, structured vs independent labels).  
- Clarity suggestion: precisely define the “Accuracy” used in ExpAcc and reconcile with reported F1 or optimize a surrogate aligned to F1.  
- Clarity suggestion: detail how wM and wT are chosen and provide sensitivity curves.  
- Clarity suggestion: provide algorithmic details (simulations, branching, rollout policies) and wall-clock overhead.  
- Originality: novel integration of structured prediction, real-time crowd querying, and Bayesian MCTS planning for latency–cost–accuracy tradeoffs.  
- Significance: timely and impactful for deployable ML under scarce labels and latency constraints, with promising practical gains.  
- Questions: How are wM and wT chosen and how sensitive are results?  
- Questions: What are planning compute times per decision and number of simulations used?  
- Questions: How does performance change if pR is learned (per-class or per-worker) instead of fixed at 0.7?  
- Questions: Can you report results when optimizing a utility aligned to F1 or a calibrated approximation?  
- Questions: In simulation, what happens if more than five redundant queries are issued for a label?  
- Suggestions: learn and adapt pR online with worker- and class-specific reliability and compare to fixed pR.  
- Suggestions: calibrate model probabilities and align planning utility with evaluation metric (F1) or empirically validate ExpAcc as a proxy.  
- Suggestions: add ablations (no structuring, no progressive widening, batch vs sequential) and detailed runtime/sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggestion: include a small-scale exact or near-exact planner on toy problems as a sanity check.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept—principled and practical with encouraging results but needs stronger modeling and evaluation details.
