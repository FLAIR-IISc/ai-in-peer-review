- Proposes "on-the-job learning": maintain high accuracy at deployment while reducing human crowd reliance over time using a stochastic game and Bayesian decision theory.  
- Introduces LENSE and an MCTS with progressive widening approximation to compute policies balancing accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing large cost reductions and F1 improvements over baselines.  
- Strong practical motivation: addresses deployment without large labeled datasets with compelling real-world examples.  
- Technically sound formulation: Bayesian decision-theoretic stochastic game is well-motivated and consistent.  
- Appropriate approximation: MCTS with progressive widening is a reasonable approach for the intractable optimal policy.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: diverse tasks and baselines, with useful cost–accuracy tradeoff analysis.  
- Reproducible: open-source implementation and CodaLab repository provided.  
- Clearly differentiated from related areas (online learning, active learning, active classification).  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees: no rigorous analysis of MCTS approximation performance or convergence.  
- Questionable crowd model: assumes 70% crowd accuracy without justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Evaluation setup may not reflect true deployment: uses labeled datasets rather than a true "no data initially" scenario.  
- Missing comparisons to relevant recent work on noisy active learning and real-time crowdsourcing.  
- Insufficient analysis of task-dependent performance variability and guidance on when the approach is most effective.  
- Suggested fixes: add theoretical discussion of MCTS, justify crowd accuracy with empirical data, and test a true no-labels-start scenario.  
- Suggested fixes: expand related work to include noisy-label active learning and real-time crowdsourcing literature.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strong, practical contribution with convincing empirical results but needs minor revisions to address theory and crowd-model assumptions.
