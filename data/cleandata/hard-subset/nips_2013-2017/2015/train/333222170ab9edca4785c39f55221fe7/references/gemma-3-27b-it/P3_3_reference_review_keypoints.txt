- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated framework for on-the-job learning using Bayesian decision theory; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem addressed: Enable accurate AI deployment without large labeled datasets by querying crowd labels during prediction.  
- Key contribution: Formalizing on-the-job learning as a stochastic game to balance accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Method: Uses Bayesian decision theory and MCTS with progressive widening to optimize querying strategies.  
- Relation to prior work: Distinct from online/active learning and active classification; builds on but generalizes ideas from Legion:AR with stronger theory.  
- Connections: Situates work in structured prediction and crowdsourcing literature with clear context.  
- Novelty: Stochastic game formulation is a significant, principled contribution.  
- Technical soundness: MCTS + progressive widening adaptation is well-justified for continuous time and large state spaces.  
- Empirical evidence: Strong results on NER, sentiment, and image tasks showing cost reductions and accuracy gains over baselines.  
- Reproducibility: Well-written paper with open-source LENSE implementation and datasets on CodaLab.  
- Practical relevance: Addresses a real-world problem of deploying models with limited upfront resources.  
- Weakness — complexity: Stochastic game formulation and MCTS details are complex and may hinder accessibility.  
- Weakness — crowd modeling: Assumes a simplistic 70% accuracy crowd model; more realistic worker models could improve performance.  
- Weakness — scalability: MCTS computational cost may limit applicability to very large-scale problems despite progressive widening.  
- Weakness — limited analysis: Time–cost/latency vs. accuracy tradeoff needs deeper ablation or sensitivity studies.  
- For acceptance: Novel, technically sound, practically relevant, strong empirical results, and reproducible code.  
- Against acceptance: Technical complexity, simplistic crowd model, and potential scalability concerns.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and address scalability and crowd-model questions.  
- Suggested improvements: Add an intuitive MCTS example, analyze sensitivity to crowd response models, discuss MCTS computational cost and optimizations, expand time–cost tradeoff analysis, and include a visual of the state/game tree.
