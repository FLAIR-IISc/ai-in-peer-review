- Introduces on-the-job learning: query crowd only when uncertain, update model iteratively, formulated as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory.  
- Proposes an MCTS-based approximation with progressive widening to solve the optimization balancing accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification showing large cost reductions and accuracy gains versus strong baselines.  
- Positions work relative to online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing, highlighting novelty in querying before prediction and model improvement at test time.  
- Connects to Legion:AR while providing formal machine learning foundations and comprehensive related work.  
- Novelty: conceptually sound, practically motivated framework and stochastic-game formulation.  
- Technical soundness: MCTS and progressive widening are well-justified and the game tree, utility, and environment model are clearly described.  
- Empirical results: compelling improvements across tasks, especially NER, with strong baselines and informative ablation study.  
- Clarity & reproducibility: well-written, organized, and open-source code/data (LENSE) provided.  
- Significance: addresses a practical problem with broad impact for resource-constrained, real-time applications.  
- Crowdsourcing setup may be artificial due to reliance on a fixed pool of sampled responses, possibly underestimating variable worker quality.  
- Framework introduces many tunable parameters (utility weights, MCTS settings) with limited sensitivity analysis.  
- Limited error analysis; lacks detailed insight into remaining system failures after iterative learning.  
- MCTS approximation lacks strong theoretical performance guarantees and convergence discussion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, solid technical contributions, strong empirical evidence, and excellent reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: artificial crowdsourcing setup, unclear parameter sensitivity, and lack of theoretical guarantees for the approximation.  
- Overall assessment: strong accept (8/10) — valuable contribution with minor weaknesses that do not preclude acceptance.
