- Proposes on-the-job learning (OTJ) framework that combines crowd queries and iterative model refinement for structured prediction with scarce labels.  
- Models OTJ as a stochastic game and uses Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with progressive widening to approximate an optimal querying policy.  
- Implements LENSE and shows cost reductions and performance gains on NER, sentiment, and image classification compared to baselines.  
- Differentiates OTJ from traditional active learning by emphasizing continuous model refinement during test time without a predefined training phase.  
- Positions work relative to prior art (including Legion:AR) and provides a thorough related work discussion.  
- Novel framing of OTJ via Bayesian decision theory to balance accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Strong practical motivation targeting scenarios where large labeled datasets are infeasible.  
- Sound methodology with a detailed game-tree, utility function, and environment model.  
- Robust empirical evaluation across three tasks with comparisons to relevant baselines and an insightful ablation study.  
- Open-source code and data released for reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-written and figures (e.g., game tree) aid clarity.  
- The theoretical framework and MCTS notation are complex and may be hard for non-experts.  
- Crowdsourcing experiments freeze worker responses, which may not reflect varying real-world worker quality.  
- Lacks detailed discussion on scalability of MCTS to very large state spaces and real-world computational constraints.  
- System performance may be sensitive to utility-function weight choices; sensitivity analysis is missing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, strong empirical results, and good reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: framework complexity, experimental crowdsourcing limitations, and limited scalability/hyperparameter sensitivity discussion.  
- Overall assessment: reviewer recommends strong accept, citing novelty, soundness, relevance, and strong results.  
- Qualitative evaluation: quality, originality, and significance rated excellent; clarity rated good with room for simplification.
