- Proposes "on-the-job learning": a decision-theoretic framework that interleaves model inference with asynchronous crowd queries, optimizing a utility trading accuracy, latency, and cost using MCTS with progressive widening for continuous time.  
- Connects to online/active learning, test-time feature acquisition, crowd-powered interfaces, and POMDP-style crowd control; novelty lies in structured outputs and continuous-time MCTS planning.  
- Strength: Tackles a practical deployment bottleneck by integrating crowd and model at test time to deliver high accuracy without large upfront training.  
- Strength: Principled Bayesian decision-theoretic formulation with an explicit utility that yields intuitive query behaviors (parallelism, redundancy).  
- Strength: Technically interesting handling of continuous-time/asynchronous responses via progressive widening in MCTS.  
- Strength: Empirical results show substantial human cost reductions (notably on NER) and accuracy gains over automated baselines; cost per example decreases as the model learns.  
- Strength: Reproducibility aided by released code, data, and retainer implementation details.  
- Weakness: Simplified crowd response model (fixed 0.7 accuracy, uniform errors, no per-worker reliability); not learned from data or tested for sensitivity.  
- Weakness: Utility optimized (MAP-sequence accuracy) mismatches evaluation metrics (token-level F1 for NER), risking biased planning.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on simulation using five cached responses per label limits realism and constrains policy space (artificial diminishing returns).  
- Weakness: Baselines are weak—missing myopic value-of-information and empirical comparisons to POMDP-based crowd-control or structured-query heuristics.  
- Weakness: Key implementation details missing or brief (pT parameterization, pθ calibration, wM/wT sensitivity, MCTS budget, runtime/scalability, ablations).  
- Weakness: Cost accounting omits retainer amortization and platform overheads, which can dominate real-world costs.  
- Weakness: Generality unclear—strongest gains mainly on NER (dominant NONE class); benefits on balanced/harder tasks need more evidence.  
- Evaluation: Quality moderate (sound formulation but weakened by assumptions and simulation); clarity generally good but needs more methodological detail; originality moderate–high; significance moderate.  
- Suggestions: Learn and report pR (possibly per-worker) and sensitivity to noise; consider online worker reliability estimation.  
- Suggestions: Align utility with evaluation metric (token/span-F1) or analyze implications of current choice.  
- Suggestions: Add myopic VoI and POMDP baselines, plus ablations (progressive widening, parallel vs sequential queries).  
- Suggestions: Report MCTS simulation budgets, wall-clock decision times, scalability, and Pareto curves for latency-accuracy-cost under varying weights.  
- Suggestions: Include small-scale live deployments and amortize retainer costs; clarify pT estimation and pθ calibration diagnostics.  
- Pros for acceptance: Timely, practical problem; principled decision-theoretic approach with novel continuous-time planning; open-source release and promising cost/accuracy results.  
- Cons for acceptance: Unrealistic crowd noise model, utility/metric mismatch, heavy simulation reliance, missing stronger baselines and cost/accounting details.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept—promising and substantive contribution but needs stronger empirical rigor and sensitivity analyses.
