- Introduces "on-the-job learning": a decision-theoretic framework that issues selective asynchronous crowd queries at test time while training a model from noisy responses.
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game with actions: query, wait, or return, and a utility trading accuracy, monetary query cost, and latency.
- Environment modeled by a structured prediction model pθ(y|x), a response-noise model pR(r|yq), and a response-time model pT.
- Exact planning is intractable; proposes an approximate planner using MCTS with UCT and progressive widening to handle continuous-time responses.
- Implements system LENSE and evaluates on NER, sentiment, and image classification, claiming higher accuracy at lower cost than baselines and cost reduction over time.
- Positions work at the intersection of online learning, active learning, crowdsourcing control, test-time feature acquisition, and structured prediction.
- Novelty: integrates noisy, delayed crowd responses as test-time features with continuous-time VOI-style planning; MCTS+progressive widening appears new in this context.
- Related work section is generally good but misses deeper discussion of some budgeted/VOI planning and dynamic feature acquisition literature.
- Strength: clear, principled stochastic-game formulation that yields intuitive policies (redundancy, parallelization, adaptivity).
- Strength: methodological contribution of MCTS with progressive widening for continuous-time stochastic responses.
- Strength: natural structured-prediction conditioning on crowd responses via CRF posterior to exploit label correlations.
- Strength: practical realism (retainer pool, real-time response modeling) and targeting a real need for high accuracy before full training.
- Strength: empirical evidence shows consistent cost reductions and adaptation over time relative to simple baselines.
- Weakness: overly simplistic noise model pR(r|yq)=0.7 uniform, ignoring worker heterogeneity, instance difficulty, and worker correlations.
- Weakness: under-specified training/inference details—unclear how CRF is trained from noisy, partial responses (EM, marginalization, soft labels?).
- Weakness: planner objective uses expected MAP accuracy but evaluation uses F1 for sequence tasks; objective/evaluation mismatch not justified or quantified.
- Weakness: evaluation uses a frozen pool of five pre-collected responses per item, creating an offline simulation that may distort concurrency, availability, and long-tail noise effects.
- Weakness: baselines are weak or mismatched (e.g., online-learning baseline uses gold labels); missing stronger myopic VOI or POMDP/TurKontrol comparisons.
- Weakness: lacks discussion of planning computational overhead, MCTS budget, UCT constants, rollout policy, and scaling with sequence length and concurrency.
- Weakness: sensitivity of utility hyperparameters (wM, wT) not clearly documented or analyzed.
- Weakness: design constraint disallowing new queries while waiting may harm responsiveness; impact not quantified.
- Key questions for authors: exact CRF training method from noisy/partial responses; exact accuracy functional used in ExpAcc for NER; calibration and updating of pT and pR; MCTS budget, UCT constants, rollout policy, and average planning time; possibility of live end-to-end experiments or larger replay pools.
- Suggested improvements: incorporate richer noise models (per-worker reliabilities, instance difficulty), add ablations (MCTS vs greedy VOI, parallel vs sequential, progressive widening ablation).
- Suggested improvements: report planning latency and sensitivity to wM/wT and show Pareto frontiers (accuracy vs cost vs latency).
- Suggested improvements: align planning objective with evaluation metric (e.g., expected token F1) or empirically justify surrogate, and include live experiments where possible.
- Pros summary: principled formulation, practical value, novel MCTS use, meaningful cost reductions, and open-source release.
- Cons summary: simplistic noise model, unclear training details, small simulated replay pool, limited baselines, missing analysis of planning overhead and utility sensitivity, and objective/evaluation mismatch.
- Recommendation: weak accept — important and compelling work but needs clearer training details, stronger evaluation/baselines, and more analysis to be fully convincing.
