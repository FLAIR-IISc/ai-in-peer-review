- Presents "on-the-job learning": query crowd only when uncertain, framed as a stochastic game solved with Bayesian decision theory and MCTS.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on NER, sentiment, and image classification, reducing labeling cost while maintaining or improving accuracy.  
- Provides an open-source implementation (LENSE) to promote reproducibility.  
- Positions work clearly relative to online learning, active learning, crowdsourcing, and Legion:AR, emphasizing test-time adaptation.  
- Novel combination of Bayesian decision theory and MCTS for principled, sequential decision-making under label cost and latency tradeoffs.  
- Formalizes the problem with a utility function that balances accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Detailed experimental and crowdsourcing setup with controls for worker quality.  
- Complexity and dense mathematical notation may hinder accessibility for some readers.  
- Limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (utility coefficients, MCTS settings) and its effect on performance.  
- Discussion of generalizability is limited; unclear which task characteristics favor or hinder the approach.  
- Simplified crowd response model (fixed 70% accuracy) may not capture real worker variability; more sophisticated modeling could help.  
- Lacks comparison to recent state-of-the-art active learning methods using neural uncertainty estimation.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions addressing hyperparameter sensitivity, generalizability, and additional comparisons.  
- Review confidence: 8/10.
