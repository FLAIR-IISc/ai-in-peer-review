- Introduces "on-the-job learning": systems use crowd-sourcing initially and reduce dependence as they learn.
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game between system and crowd.
- Uses Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, cost, and latency.
- Approximates optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and progressive widening.
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification tasks.
- Reports substantial cost reductions (over an order of magnitude on NER) and higher accuracy than baselines.
- Technically sound approach with a well-motivated problem statement.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive with multiple baselines.
- Crowd response model pR(r|yq) claimed 70% accuracy without sufficient measurement or validation details.
- Response time model pT(ti|si) lacks statistical justification and validation despite anecdotal timing ranges.
- Comparison to "online learning" is imprecise and potentially misleading given the system’s incremental updates.
- Implementation details (progressive widening, handling multiple in-flight queries) need clearer explanation.
- Novel combination of active learning, crowd-sourcing, and Bayesian decision theory for structured prediction.
- Insufficient differentiation and detailed comparison from Legion:AR and related online active learning work.
- Practical significance is high for low-data, resource-constrained deployments; immediate accuracy from start is valuable.
- Scalability to large deployments and handling of concept drift or varying crowd quality are not addressed.
- Strengths: well-motivated, principled stochastic-game formalism, comprehensive experiments, strong Bayesian foundation, publicly available code/data.
- Weaknesses: weak justification of crowd/time models, problematic online learning comparison, inadequate related-work differentiation, limited scalability discussion, no analysis of MCTS approximation error.
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions.
- Required revisions: justify crowd response and response-time models rigorously.
- Required revisions: clarify and correct the comparison to online learning.
- Required revisions: strengthen differentiation from Legion:AR and related work.
- Required revisions: include discussion/analysis of MCTS approximation error.
- Required revisions: address scalability and long-term deployment issues (concept drift, worker quality).
