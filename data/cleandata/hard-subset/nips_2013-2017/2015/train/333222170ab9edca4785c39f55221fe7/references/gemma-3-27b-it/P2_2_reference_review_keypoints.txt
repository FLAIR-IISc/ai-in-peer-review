- Summary: Proposes "on-the-job learning" that queries crowd labels at test time using a Bayesian decision-theoretic stochastic game and MCTS to balance cost, latency, and accuracy, evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image tasks (LENSE).  
- Relation to prior work: Positions work between online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing, emphasizing test-time querying and deeper theoretical framing than related Legion:AR.  
- Novelty & significance: Introduces a practical paradigm for limited-data scenarios, formalized as a stochastic game to trade off cost/latency/accuracy.  
- Technical soundness: MCTS-based approximation and the utility function are well-motivated; experimental setup (retained crowd, frozen responses) is rigorous.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and organized with clear problem formulation and helpful figures.  
- Reproducibility: Provides open-source implementation and CodaLab workspace for code, data, and experiments.  
- Experimental scope (weakness): Tasks are standard; would benefit from tests on more complex, real-world scenarios.  
- Sensitivity analysis (weakness): Lacks detailed analysis of hyperparameters like cost/latency weights and MCTS parameters.  
- Response model simplification (weakness): Assumes 70% uniform crowd accuracy, which oversimplifies real worker behavior.  
- Limited failure-case discussion (weakness): Insufficient exploration of scenarios where on-the-job learning fails (e.g., systematically incorrect crowd).  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, practically relevant framework with sound technical approach, strong empirical improvements, and reproducible artifacts.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited experimental breadth and oversimplified response modeling reduce confidence in generality.  
- Overall assessment & recommendation: Strong contribution worth accepting with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: Add sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters, discuss failure cases/limitations, and broaden experimental evaluation to harder real-world scenarios.
