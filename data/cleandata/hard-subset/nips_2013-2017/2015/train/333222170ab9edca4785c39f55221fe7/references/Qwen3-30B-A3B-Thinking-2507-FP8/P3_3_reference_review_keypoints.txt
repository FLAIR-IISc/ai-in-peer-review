- Introduces "on-the-job learning": combine active learning and crowdsourcing as a stochastic game, formalized with Bayesian decision theory and approximated via MCTS with progressive widening (LENS), evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image tasks.  
- Shows strong technical formulation and distinguishes from online learning and active classification.  
- Claims large cost reductions (over an order of magnitude) on NER and improved accuracy over baselines.  
- Misleading comparison: "full human annotation" is not clearly defined and baseline (human n-query majority vote) is not equivalent.  
- Methodological limitation: using a frozen pool of 5 worker responses sampled without replacement underestimates crowd stochasticity and diversity.  
- Reproducibility issue: insufficient details on the CRF (feature set and training procedure).  
- Utility function (Equation 1) is confusing; ExpAcc definition appears mismatched to action-dependent utility.  
- Reporting issue: claims like "28% F1 improvement" lack baseline F1 scores for context.  
- Need clearer, concrete distinction between on-the-job learning and active classification in practice.  
- Originality: novel application of Bayesian decision theory for real-time active querying and model improvement during test time.  
- Prior work overlap: use of MCTS with progressive widening and links to active learning with noisy labels are not sufficiently differentiated or connected to related literature.  
- Significance: addresses an important deployment problem and shows promising NER results, but smaller gains on sentiment and image tasks raise generalization concerns.  
- Claim that query rate and cost decrease over time is plausible but under-analyzed; Figure 4 needs deeper analysis of query-rate trends.  
- Strengths: novel practical framework, principled game-theoretic model, strong NER experiments, and open-source implementation.  
- Weaknesses: unclear/misleading comparisons, missing CRF/training details, limited task-specific analysis, and crowd-sampling methodological issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: define "full human annotation," report baseline F1 scores, provide CRF feature/training details, analyze why NER benefits more, and clarify distinction from active classification.
