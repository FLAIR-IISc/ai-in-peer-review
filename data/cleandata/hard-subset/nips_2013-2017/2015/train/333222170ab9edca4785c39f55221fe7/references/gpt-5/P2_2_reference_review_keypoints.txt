- Proposes "on-the-job learning": a decision-theoretic framework that uses real-time crowdsourcing at test time to get high accuracy immediately and reduce crowd reliance over time.
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game between system and crowd with a utility trading off accuracy, cost, and latency.
- Uses MCTS with progressive widening to approximate optimal policies and handle continuous response times.
- Environment model combines a structured predictor pθ(y|x), a simple noisy-response worker model pR, and a response-time model pT.
- Implements the system as LENSE and evaluates on NER, sentiment, and image classification with a retainer-based crowd.
- Strength: principled sequential decision formulation with an explicit Bayesian expected-utility objective and asynchronous responses.
- Strength: algorithmic novelty in combining MCTS + progressive widening and using UCT for adaptive search under uncertainty.
- Strength: empirical gains, notably large cost reduction and improved F1 on NER compared to 5-vote majority and other baselines.
- Weakness: utility/metric mismatch—utility optimizes expected accuracy (MAP) while results are reported in F1; sensitivity to the chosen accuracy function is unclear.
- Weakness: simplistic, fixed worker-noise model (pR=0.7) that ignores worker heterogeneity, item difficulty, and context.
- Weakness: simulation protocol pre-collects 5 responses and samples without replacement, which may bias latency/error correlations and under-represent real-world variance.
- Weakness: baselines are limited; missing adaptive voting (POMDP/Dawid–Skene), cost-aware active classification, and ablation studies of LENSE components.
- Weakness: unclear hyperparameter selection and calibration for utility weights wM and wT and the model pθ; sensitivity analyses are missing.
- Weakness: no theoretical/regret bounds or analysis of MCTS/progressive widening convergence properties.
- Weakness: some reproducibility details (dataset splits, CRF/RNN configs, update frequency) are underspecified despite code release.
- Clarity: overall clear but underspecified details on the utility accuracy function, wM/wT setting, and formal definitions relating utility to structured F1.
- Originality: novel combination of decision-theoretic asynchronous crowd planning, structured prediction with online learning, and MCTS with progressive widening.
- Significance: important and timely problem with generic approach; strongest empirical impact on NER but practical adoption depends on robustness to worker variability and retainer practicality.
- Pros summary: principled formulation, novel planning approach, strong NER results, and open-source release.
- Cons summary: fixed worker noise model, metric/calibration issues, limited live evaluation, missing stronger baselines and theoretical analysis.
- Suggested clarifications/experiments: define Accuracy in utility vs. F1, provide sensitivity plots for wM/wT, learn/adapt pR and pT online, run ablations (greedy vs MCTS, batch sizes, progressive widening), add adaptive worker-reliability baselines, and report planning overhead.
- Recommendation: weak accept pending stronger modeling of worker variability, metric alignment, additional baselines/ablations, and more realistic evaluations.
