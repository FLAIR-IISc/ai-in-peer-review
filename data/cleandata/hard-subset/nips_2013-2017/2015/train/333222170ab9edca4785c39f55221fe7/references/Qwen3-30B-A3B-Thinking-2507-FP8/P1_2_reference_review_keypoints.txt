- Introduces "on-the-job learning": a framework that uses crowd labels initially and gradually becomes self-sufficient via a Bayesian decision-theoretic stochastic game.  
- Distinguishes this approach from online learning, active learning, and active classification, and builds on prior work like Legion:AR with a formal ML foundation.  
- Strength: Strong practical motivation for building accurate systems without large labeled datasets in resource-constrained, real-world settings.  
- Strength: Principled theoretical formulation using Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, latency, and cost (utility function captures objectives).  
- Strength: Rigorous experiments on NER, sentiment, and image classification showing large cost reductions and improved accuracy over pure crowd methods.  
- Strength: Provides open-source implementation (LENS) and detailed experimental setup for reproducibility.  
- Strength: Clearly distinguishes related work, arguing for the novelty of the on-the-job learning formulation.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical guarantees or error/convergence bounds for the Monte Carlo Tree Search approximation.  
- Weakness: Simplified crowd model assumptions (e.g., 70% accuracy, response time distributions) without sensitivity analysis or worker detail.  
- Weakness: Task-specific performance varies (best on NER, worse on Sentiment/Faces) with only speculative analysis of causes.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns not addressed—MCTS complexity may be prohibitive for large/high-traffic streams.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of failure modes (e.g., consistently inaccurate crowd, insufficient signal for the model to learn).  
- Recommendation: Strong paper, significant contribution, meets NIPS standards; empirical results justify acceptance.  
- Pro/Con summary: Pros — novel, practical framework, solid Bayesian foundation, comprehensive experiments, large cost reductions, reproducible code; Cons — limited theoretical guarantees, questionable crowd-model assumptions, need deeper task-specific analysis.
