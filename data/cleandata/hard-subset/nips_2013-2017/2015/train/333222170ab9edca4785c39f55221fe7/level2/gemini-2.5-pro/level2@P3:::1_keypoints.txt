- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy systems from a cold start by querying a crowd in real time and learning from their labels.  
- Formulates the problem as a stochastic game grounded in Bayesian decision theory, trading off accuracy, monetary cost, and latency.  
- Exact optimal policy is intractable; proposes MCTS with progressive widening to approximate the value function and handle continuous time.  
- Distinguishes from online/active learning by actively querying before committing to a prediction and learning online without a pre-existing labeled dataset.  
- Evaluated on three tasks (NER, sentiment, image classification) with strong baselines; shows significant cost reduction and accuracy gains (notably NER).  
- Uses a fixed pool of pre-collected crowd responses for consistent, reproducible experiments; open-source code and CodaLab worksheet released.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-motivated, clearly written, and includes helpful figures (e.g., game tree visualization).  
- Highly original synthesis of active learning, crowdsourcing, online learning, and game theory to address cold-start interactive learning.  
- Results are practically significant, offering a path to deploy accurate systems immediately while reducing human labeling costs over time.  
- Suggestion: crowd response model assumes fixed uniform worker accuracy (70%); discuss impact of variable worker reliability and item difficulty or richer crowd models.  
- Suggestion: response-time model pT is underspecified; specify distribution used and its effects.  
- Suggestion: analyze scalability and computational cost of running MCTS per instance, including typical simulation counts and runtime scaling.  
- Suggestion: discuss selection and sensitivity of utility hyperparameters (wM, wT) that balance cost and latency against accuracy.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem setting, principled Bayesian/stochastic-game approach, strong multi-task empirical validation, and full reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance (minor): simplifying environment model assumptions and limited analysis of practical computational scalability.  
- Overall: strengths substantially outweigh weaknesses; paper is an excellent, impactful contribution likely to inspire further interactive ML research.
