- Proposes an on-the-job learning framework that combines real-time crowdsourcing and automated predictions to deploy high-accuracy systems from zero training data.  
- Casts the problem as a stochastic game under Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Uses a Monte Carlo Tree Search approximation to compute policies because the optimal policy is intractable.  
- Evaluated on three datasets, showing substantial cost reductions and accuracy improvements over pure crowd and pure ML baselines.  
- Novel framework that integrates crowdsourcing with Bayesian decision-theoretic modeling for on-the-job learning.  
- Strong theoretical foundation using stochastic games and Bayesian decision theory.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation across three datasets demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Provides an open-source implementation for reproducibility and reuse.  
- Computing the optimal policy is intractable; the MCTS approximation may not always be optimal.  
- Relies on an assumed crowd worker accuracy (70%), which may not hold in real settings.  
- Evaluation is limited to three datasets, leaving generalizability to other domains unclear.  
- Further evaluation is needed to fully characterize strengths and limitations.  
- Presents a significant and novel contribution to deploying accurate systems with limited training data.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and useful for researchers and practitioners in crowdsourcing and Bayesian decision theory.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance despite limitations.
