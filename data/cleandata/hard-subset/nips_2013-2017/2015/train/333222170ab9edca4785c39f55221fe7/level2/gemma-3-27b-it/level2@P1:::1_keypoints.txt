- Proposes an on-the-job learning framework that uses real-time crowdsourcing plus Bayesian decision theory and MCTS to trade off querying, accuracy, and latency.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing significant cost reductions and improvements over online-learning baselines.  
- Well-motivated practical problem and strong contribution in formalizing interactive querying as a stochastic game.  
- Using Bayesian decision theory for optimal policy selection is a solid theoretical idea.  
- Applying MCTS to approximate the intractable optimal policy is sensible in principle.  
- Open-source release of the LENSE system is a valuable contribution.  
- Description of the MCTS approximation is hand-wavy and Algorithm 1 lacks implementation detail.  
- Missing specifics on progressive widening and how the UCT rule is applied in continuous-time settings.  
- No sensitivity analysis of key MCTS hyperparameters (e.g., exploration constant c).  
- Baseline comparisons are limited; a stronger active learning/static query policy baseline is absent.  
- Worker-quality analysis relies on a frozen pool of responses and may not reflect dynamic crowd behavior.  
- Large performance gap between NER and other tasks is unexplained; more error analysis is needed.  
- Simplifying assumption that queries cannot be issued mid-wait is restrictive and its impact isn’t thoroughly evaluated.  
- Relationship to related work (Legion:AR) could be explored in more depth with comparisons of modeling choices.  
- Utility function is conceptually clear but wM and wT weights are unspecified and their effect on policy is not discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, strong empirical results, open-source code, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient MCTS/detailing, limited baselines, lack of hyperparameter/worker analysis, query-simplification concerns, and missing utility-weight discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing MCTS details, expanded baselines, hyperparameter/utility analyses, and deeper experimental discussion.
