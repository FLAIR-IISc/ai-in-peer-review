- Proposes LENSE, a framework for on-the-job learning that learns from noisy crowds while reducing cost over time.
- Models on-the-job learning as a stochastic game and approximates the optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and progressive widening.
- Evaluated on three tasks: named-entity recognition (NER), sentiment classification, and image classification using real-world datasets and crowdsourcing.
- Main claims: LENSE achieves high accuracy while reducing annotation cost by over an order of magnitude vs. full human annotation.
- Experimental results support claims, e.g., 88.4% F1 on the NER task with significant cost reduction.
- Use of MCTS with progressive widening is presented as a novel and effective contribution for policy approximation.
- Paper is well-written, clear, well-organized, and provides thorough experimental presentation and analysis.
- Strengths: novelty, clarity, thorough experiments, and use of real-world crowd data increasing credibility.
- Weakness: assumes a noisy crowd model but lacks detailed analysis of the noise model.
- Weakness: lacks clear comparisons to other state-of-the-art approaches in the field.
- Weakness: does not discuss potential limitations such as required crowd size or bias in crowd responses.
- Recommendation: overall positive—reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty, motivation, and experimental support.
