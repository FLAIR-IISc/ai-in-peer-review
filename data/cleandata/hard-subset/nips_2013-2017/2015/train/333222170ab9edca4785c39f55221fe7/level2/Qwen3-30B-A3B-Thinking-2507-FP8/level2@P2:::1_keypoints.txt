- Proposes "on-the-job learning" using real-time crowdsourcing to enable accurate AI starting from zero training examples.
- Models the problem as a stochastic game under Bayesian decision theory balancing accuracy, latency, and cost.
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy.
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification; reports substantial cost reductions and accuracy gains.
- Strongest empirical result on NER: 88.4% F1 and more than an order of magnitude cost reduction versus full human annotation.
- Clear problem formulation and well-motivated, principled Bayesian framework.
- MCTS with progressive widening is a reasonable approximation for the optimization problem.
- Claims about "more than an order of magnitude" cost reduction lack a clear definition of "full human annotation" and exact cost numbers.
- Baseline definitions (Human n-query, “expert provided labels,” Human 1-query) are unclear and absolute metric values are often omitted.
- Reported relative improvements (8% F1, 28% over online learning) lack absolute scores, making practical significance unclear.
- Crowd response model assumed at 70% accuracy is overly simplistic and not justified or sensitivity-analyzed.
- Results may be vulnerable to real-world variations in crowd behavior due to the simplistic response model.
- Insufficient differentiation from related work (Legion:AR); claims of test-time self-improvement are potentially misleading.
- Presentation of experimental results needs clearer absolute metrics and more precise baseline definitions.
- Paper should discuss scalability to larger datasets and more complex tasks; current limitations not fully addressed.
- Originality: distinguishes itself from online/active learning by starting predictions immediately and updating models during prediction while balancing multiple objectives.
- Significance: potentially high practical impact for rapid deployment without large labeled datasets, especially for NER.
- Recommendation: promising but requires significant revisions; current unclear presentation of results warrants rejection pending stronger evidence and clarifications.
