- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy AI from zero training examples by using real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty during prediction.
- Models the problem as a stochastic game grounded in Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, latency, and cost.
- Proposes an approximation using Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening.
- Implements LENSE (Learning from Expensive Noisy Slow Experts) and provides code/data on CodaLab for reproducibility.
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing large cost reductions and accuracy gains.
- On NER: over an order of magnitude cost reduction and +8% F1 over single-human labeling, +28% over online learning.
- Clear, well-motivated problem formulation that differentiates from online/active learning and active classification.
- Strong technical quality: careful game-theoretic model, reasonable crowd response/time assumptions, and thoughtful experimental controls (frozen worker pool).
- Authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., fixed 70% crowd accuracy assumption) and explain differences from related work.
- Analysis of query pattern evolution provides useful insight into learning dynamics (Figure 3).
- Minor suggestions: strengthen analysis of why threshold baseline underperforms and discuss scalability to higher-throughput systems.
- High practical significance for real-world, zero-data deployment scenarios due to flexibility in balancing accuracy, latency, and cost.
- Well-written, well-organized paper with clear figures and sufficient implementation details; recommendation: accept.
