**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a framework for on-the-job learning, which enables a high-accuracy system to be deployed starting with zero training examples. The framework uses real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty where needed and outputs predictions when confident. The approach is cast as a stochastic game based on Bayesian decision theory, allowing for the balancing of latency, cost, and accuracy objectives. The authors develop an approximation based on Monte Carlo Tree Search to compute the optimal policy. The approach is evaluated on three datasets, demonstrating significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements over pure crowd and pure machine learning approaches.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper introduces a new framework for on-the-job learning, which combines real-time crowdsourcing and Bayesian decision theory to achieve high accuracy and low cost.
2. **Theoretical foundation**: The authors provide a solid theoretical foundation for their approach, using stochastic games and Bayesian decision theory to model the on-the-job learning setting.
3. **Empirical evaluation**: The paper presents a thorough empirical evaluation of the approach on three datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing cost and improving accuracy.
4. **Practical implementation**: The authors provide an open-source implementation of their system, making it easy for others to use and build upon their work.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational complexity**: The authors acknowledge that computing the optimal policy is intractable, and their approximation based on Monte Carlo Tree Search may not always find the optimal solution.
2. **Assumptions about crowd behavior**: The paper assumes that crowd workers are 70% accurate, which may not always be the case in practice.
3. **Limited generalizability**: The approach is evaluated on three specific datasets, and it is unclear how well it will generalize to other domains and tasks.
4. **Need for further evaluation**: While the paper presents promising results, further evaluation is needed to fully understand the strengths and limitations of the approach.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper presents a novel approach to on-the-job learning, which combines real-time crowdsourcing and Bayesian decision theory. The approach is significant because it addresses the challenge of deploying high-accuracy systems with limited training data. The paper's contribution is substantial, as it provides a new framework for thinking about on-the-job learning and demonstrates its effectiveness in reducing cost and improving accuracy.

**Quality, Clarity, and Usefulness**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the theoretical foundation and empirical evaluation. The authors provide sufficient details about their approach and implementation, making it easy for others to understand and build upon their work. The paper is useful for researchers and practitioners interested in on-the-job learning, crowdsourcing, and Bayesian decision theory.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting the paper, as it presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of on-the-job learning. The authors provide a solid theoretical foundation, thorough empirical evaluation, and practical implementation of their approach. While there are some limitations and areas for further evaluation, the paper's strengths outweigh its weaknesses, and it has the potential to make a substantial impact in the field.
