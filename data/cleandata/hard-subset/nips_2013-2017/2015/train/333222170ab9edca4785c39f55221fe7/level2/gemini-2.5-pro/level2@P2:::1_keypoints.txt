- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy a high-accuracy system from scratch by querying a crowd in real time and learning from responses.  
- Processes input streams, querying specific parts, waiting for responses, or returning a final prediction per example.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game between system and crowd with actions to query, wait, or predict.  
- Defines a Bayesian decision-theoretic utility that trades off accuracy, monetary cost, and latency.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy in continuous time.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements over strong baselines.  
- Novel and significant: addresses deployment without pre-labeled data, bridging human-powered and ML systems.  
- Principled and elegant modeling: stochastic game + Bayesian framework is preferable to simple heuristics.  
- Sensible approximation: MCTS + progressive widening is well-justified for this setting.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation: diverse tasks, strong baselines, controlled crowdsourcing, and impressive NER results.  
- Clear and reproducible: well-written paper with open-source code and a CodaLab worksheet.  
- Weakness — static crowd model: assumes fixed worker accuracy and ignores per-worker reliability.  
- Weakness — computational cost: MCTS planner incurs overhead that may hurt latency or scale for large action spaces.  
- Weakness — sensitivity to utility weights: limited discussion on how monetary/latency weights were chosen or affect behavior.  
- Weakness — limited model details: paper omits specifics of CRF features for NER (though code likely contains them).  
- Overall: strong, impactful contribution with minor practical extensions and clarifications suggested.
