This paper introduces a new framework called on-the-job learning, which aims to deploy a high-accuracy system starting with zero training examples. The authors propose a stochastic game based on Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy objectives in a principled way. They develop an approximation based on Monte Carlo Tree Search to compute the optimal policy, which is intractable. The approach is evaluated on three datasets: named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel approach to on-the-job learning, which is a challenging problem in machine learning.
* The authors provide a thorough formulation of the problem and a well-motivated solution based on Bayesian decision theory.
* The experimental results show significant improvements in cost and accuracy compared to baseline methods.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the crowd workers are reliable and accurate, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their approach, which could be a concern for large-scale applications.
* The paper could benefit from more comparisons with existing active learning and online learning methods.

Originality:

* The paper presents a new framework for on-the-job learning, which is distinct from existing approaches in active learning and online learning.
* The use of Bayesian decision theory and Monte Carlo Tree Search is a novel combination of techniques for this problem.

Significance:
