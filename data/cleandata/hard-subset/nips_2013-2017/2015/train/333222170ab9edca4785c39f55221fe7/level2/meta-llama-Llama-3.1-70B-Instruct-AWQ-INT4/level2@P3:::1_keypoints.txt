- Novel framework for on-the-job learning using Bayesian decision theory and modeling the setting as a stochastic game.  
- Proposes an approximation algorithm based on Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to find the optimal policy.  
- Empirical evaluation on NER, sentiment classification, and image classification shows significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements vs baselines.  
- Paper is technically sound with correct derivations, clear presentation, and comprehensive experimental comparisons.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of limitations, notably the unrealistic assumption that crowd workers are i.i.d.  
- Does not provide a detailed analysis of the algorithm’s computational complexity for large-scale use.  
- Well-written with consistent notation and logical flow, though the introduction could be more concise.  
- Related work section is incomplete and needs clearer differentiation from active learning and crowdsourcing literature.  
- Original contribution is meaningful (Bayesian/stochastic-game framing + MCTS approximation) but builds on existing active learning/crowdsourcing work.  
- Potentially significant for applications requiring high accuracy and low cost (NLP, vision, robotics).  
- Needs more detailed discussion of potential applications, limitations, and cost–benefit tradeoffs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, new algorithmic approach, strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient limitation analysis (i.i.d. workers), missing complexity analysis, and shallow related work comparison.  
- Recommended revisions: discuss limitations (worker assumptions), analyze computational complexity, expand related work, and include application/cost-benefit discussion.
