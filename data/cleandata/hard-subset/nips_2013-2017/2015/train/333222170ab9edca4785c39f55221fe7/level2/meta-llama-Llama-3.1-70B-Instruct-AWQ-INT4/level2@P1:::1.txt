The paper "On-the-Job Learning with Bayesian Decision Theory" proposes a novel framework for learning from noisy crowds in real-time, while maintaining high accuracy and reducing cost over time. The authors model the on-the-job learning setting as a stochastic game and use ideas from game playing to approximate the optimal policy. The technical core of the approach is sound, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their system, LENSE, on three tasks: named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of explaining the problem formulation, model, and game playing algorithm. The related work section is comprehensive, and the authors clearly position their work within the broader context of online learning, active learning, active classification, crowdsourcing, and structured prediction.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors propose a novel framework for on-the-job learning that combines ideas from game playing and Bayesian decision theory.
* The technical core of the approach is sound, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their system on three tasks.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of explaining the problem formulation, model, and game playing algorithm.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The evaluation could be more comprehensive, with additional baselines and experiments to further demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.
* The authors could provide more insight into the behavior of the model and the game playing algorithm, particularly in terms of how the system adapts to changing worker quality and response times.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the approach and potential avenues for future work.

Overall, the paper is a significant contribution to the field of machine learning and crowdsourcing, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach in a range of tasks. The paper is well-suited for publication in a top-tier conference like NIPS.
