- Summary: Proposes an on-the-job learning framework that interleaves model prediction with real-time crowd micro-queries, formalized as a stochastic game trading off accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Summary: Uses MCTS with progressive widening to approximate the optimal policy and combines a CRF structured-prediction model with simple worker accuracy and latency models.  
- Summary: Empirical results on NER, sentiment, and face classification show cost savings vs full human annotation and accuracy gains vs single-vote crowds and an “online learning with gold” baseline.

- Strength: Clear, principled utility that explicitly trades off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Strength: Technically interesting use of MCTS with progressive widening for continuous-time, partially observed planning.  
- Strength: Integration with structured prediction (CRF) and intuitive behavior (redundancy, parallel vs sequential querying).  
- Strength: Strong empirical gains on NER and open-source implementation for reproducibility.

- Weakness: Environment model is simplistic (fixed 70% worker accuracy, independence assumptions, no per-worker reliabilities or adversarial errors).  
- Weakness: Missing sensitivity/robustness analyses to model mis-specification and worker heterogeneity.  
- Weakness: Baselines omit strong decision-theoretic alternatives (myopic VOI, POMDP solvers, TurKontrol-like policies).  
- Weakness: Online-learning baseline uses unavailable oracle gold labels and cannot query at test time, limiting its usefulness.  
- Weakness: Planning computational costs (MCTS budget, wall-clock overhead) and latency impact are not quantified.  
- Weakness: Experimental protocol using a fixed response pool may understate real-world variability and decouples timing context.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous claims (e.g., outperforming expert labels) and lack of statistical significance reporting.  
- Weakness: Some practical design choices (no mid-wait queries, loss/weight settings) lack ablation.

- Quality: Formulation and MCTS approximation are reasonable but expected-accuracy computation and decision-rule discussion are thin.  
- Quality: Empirics are promising but need stronger baselines and robustness checks; reproducibility aided by code but missing some implementation details.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well written and organized; some minor typos and clarifications needed (ExpAcc, pR/pT calibration, pθ update details).

- Originality: Novel combination of decision-theoretic planning with structured prediction and real-time crowd responses using MCTS in continuous time.  
- Significance: Potentially high practical impact for rapid-deployment ML systems, with strongest evidence on structured tasks like NER.

- Questions: How sensitive is performance to mis-specified worker accuracy and can per-worker reliabilities be learned online?  
- Questions: What are the MCTS computational budgets and resulting wall-clock latencies?  
- Questions: How were wM and wT chosen; can Pareto curves be reported?  
- Questions: Can the authors compare to myopic VOI and TurKontrol-style controllers and clarify the “boosting vs expert labels” claim?

- Suggestions: Add ablations on worker accuracy priors, heterogeneity, and response-time modeling and report robustness curves.  
- Suggestions: Include decision-theoretic baselines (myopic VOI, POMDP/heuristic controllers) and statistical significance tests.  
- Suggestions: Report MCTS computational costs, analyze the “no mid-wait query” constraint, and learn/incorporate worker reliabilities.  
- Suggestions: Clarify data protocol, CRF/RNN details, hyperparameters, and provide full metric breakdowns.

- Pros for acceptance: Principled, novel formulation with solid approximate planning, strong NER gains, practical relevance, and open-source code.  
- Cons for acceptance: Simplified/mis-specified environment model, missing strong baselines, unclear computational overhead, and some ambiguous claims.

- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept, leaning to accept contingent on addressing experimental and modeling robustness gaps.
