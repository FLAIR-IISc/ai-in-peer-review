**Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces a novel framework for on-the-job learning, which enables a system to learn from noisy crowds in real-time while maintaining high accuracy and reducing cost over time. The framework is based on Bayesian decision theory and models the on-the-job setting as a stochastic game. The authors propose an approximation algorithm based on Monte Carlo Tree Search and progressive widening to find the optimal policy. They evaluate their approach on three tasks: named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification, and demonstrate significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements compared to baselines.

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, and the authors provide a detailed comparison with baselines.

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of their approach.
