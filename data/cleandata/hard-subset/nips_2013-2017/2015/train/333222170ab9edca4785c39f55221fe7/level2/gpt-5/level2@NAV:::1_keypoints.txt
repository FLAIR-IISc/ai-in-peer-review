- Summary: Proposes “on-the-job learning,” a decision-theoretic framework that deploys a model with zero initial training data by selectively querying crowd workers and learning from noisy, delayed labels; formalized as a stochastic game optimizing accuracy, latency, and cost and approximated via MCTS with progressive widening, evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image tasks.  
- Formulation quality: Sound formalization and appropriate MCTS approximation; utility and environment models are clearly specified.  
- Model misspecification risk: Uses a fixed response model (pR=0.7, uniform noise) and does not model worker heterogeneity, raising robustness concerns.  
- Evaluation design concerns: Mixes live retainer collection with simulations from a frozen 5-response pool, which controls variance but may bias results; needs more end-to-end live trials and sensitivity analyses.  
- Missing baselines: Does not compare to POMDP/crowd-planning baselines (e.g., TurKontrol-style policies).  
- Clarity: Paper is well organized and clear overall, but some parameters (wM, wT), the response-time model, and ablation details (progressive widening, parallel vs sequential querying) need more explanation.  
- Originality: Novel integration of decision-theoretic planning with structured prediction and real-time crowdsourcing; using MCTS for continuous-time query planning appears new.  
- Significance: Demonstrates practical impact across three tasks with reduced cost and comparable or improved accuracy, useful for rapid deployment without labeled data.  
- Pros: Principled formulation, effective approximate planner, demonstrated cost/accuracy gains, open-source release, tackles a timely practical problem.  
- Cons: Simplistic crowd model lacking worker-specific reliability, limited live evaluation and sensitivity testing, and missing POMDP-based comparisons.  
- Suggested experiments: Learn worker reliabilities (e.g., Dawid–Skene), perform sensitivity analyses for pR/pT/wM/wT, ablate MCTS components (progressive widening, parallel vs sequential), and compare against TurKontrol-style policies.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept.
