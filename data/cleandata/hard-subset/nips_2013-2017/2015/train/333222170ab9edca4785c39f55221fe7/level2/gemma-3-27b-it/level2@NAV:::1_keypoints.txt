- Proposes “on-the-job learning”: deploy AI with zero initial training data by querying a crowd at runtime.
- Core idea: strategically query crowd to resolve uncertainty, trading off latency, cost, and accuracy via Bayesian decision theory.
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search to approximate the optimal policy for the intractable decision problem.
- Well-motivated framing as a stochastic game, providing a principled way to balance competing objectives.
- Empirical results on NER, sentiment, and image classification are promising, with notable >10x cost reduction on NER.
- Open-source implementation (LENSE) enhances reproducibility and impact.
- Weakness: evaluation uses pre-existing labeled datasets, which somewhat contradicts the “zero training examples” premise.
- Suggestion: evaluate system performance during the initial bootstrap phase to better demonstrate learning from scratch.
- Weakness: crowd modeling is simplistic and could benefit from richer models of worker reliability and bias.
- Related-work discussion is thorough but needs stronger differentiation from active learning beyond “operates at test time.”
- Presentation could be streamlined; some sections are dense and would benefit from more illustrative examples.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address dynamic learning evaluation and clearer positioning within active learning literature.
