- Novelty: Introduces an innovative "on-the-job learning" framework that models ML+crowdsourcing as a stochastic game and uses MCTS to approximate optimal policies.  
- Empirical performance: Demonstrates strong results—large cost reductions (e.g., order-of-magnitude for NER) and improved accuracy (28% F1 gain on NER vs. online learning).  
- Practical utility: Well-suited to real-world low-data scenarios (e.g., disaster relief) and reduces dependence on crowdsourcing over time.  
- Reproducibility: Authors provide open-source code and datasets enabling replication and extension.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized and clearly explains methodology, utility function, and experiments.  
- Limited task diversity: Evaluation is confined to three tasks; broader domains (speech, medical, etc.) are needed to show generality.  
- Worker variability: Does not fully analyze robustness to noisy or inconsistent crowd workers in uncontrolled settings.  
- Scalability concerns: Real-time crowdsourcing approach raises questions about throughput and system bottlenecks for large-scale applications.  
- Theoretical justification: Lacks detailed theoretical analysis or guarantees for the MCTS approximation in this context.  
- Suggestion — more tasks: Include experiments on more diverse domains to validate generalizability.  
- Suggestion — robustness: Explore and propose strategies to handle varying crowd-worker quality.  
- Suggestion — scalability: Provide deeper analysis of scalability and latency/bottleneck trade-offs for high-throughput use cases.  
- Suggestion — theory: Add theoretical insights or approximation guarantees for the proposed policy optimization.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — significant, practical, and impactful contribution; Rating: 8/10.
