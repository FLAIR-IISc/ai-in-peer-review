- Proposes a theoretically grounded framework for on-the-job learning modeling crowd-to-self transition as a stochastic game under Bayesian decision theory.  
- Formalizes the tradeoff between latency, cost, and accuracy using a CRF predictor and Bayesian utility maximization, with MCTS for query selection.  
- Implements the method as LENSE and evaluates on NER, sentiment, and image classification, reporting large cost reductions and F1 improvements (e.g., 88.4% F1 on NER with >10× lower cost).  
- Strength: Provides a principled theoretical framing that advances prior on-the-job learning work and handles intractable policies via MCTS.  
- Strength: Empirical NER results are compelling and the open-source release plus frozen response pools improve reproducibility.  
- Strength: Clearly situates the approach relative to online learning and active classification, highlighting differences.  
- Weakness: Baseline threshold choices (0.3 and 0.88) lack justification and may have been tuned on test data, weakening comparative claims.  
- Weakness: Claimed 8% F1 improvement over a single human label conflates human effort with algorithmic benefit; gains may reflect more labeling rather than better selection.  
- Weakness: Online learning baseline is unrealistic because it was trained on perfect gold labels, not the noisy labels available in the proposed setting.  
- Weakness: Performance gains are task-dependent (strong on NER, marginal on sentiment/image), so generalizability is unclear.  
- Technical issues: Equation (2) omits conditioning on s, Equation (3) drops y in p_R(r_i | y_{q_i}), Algorithm 1’s N(σ) is undefined, and the assumed 70% crowd accuracy is unvalidated.  
- Weakness: Lack of error analysis—no breakdown of when sequential vs. parallel queries are optimal or why LENSE corrects specific errors.  
- Suggested fixes: Justify threshold values via ablation, compare to realistic/state-of-the-art online learning baselines, and provide detailed error analysis to separate algorithmic gains from human annotation effects.  
- Overall: Novel and well-motivated theoretical contribution but empirical claims are overstated due to weak baselines and technical gaps; recommendation: weak accept pending minor revisions.
