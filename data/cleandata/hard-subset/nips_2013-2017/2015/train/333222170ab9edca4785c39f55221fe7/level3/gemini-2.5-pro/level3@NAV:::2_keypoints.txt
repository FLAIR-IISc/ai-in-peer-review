- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy classifiers with zero labeled data using real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty while balancing accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Models the setting as a stochastic game grounded in Bayesian decision theory and uses Monte Carlo Tree Search for an approximate policy.  
- Distinguishes itself from online active learning/active classification by actively acquiring information before making predictions to achieve immediate high accuracy.  
- Novel and significant problem formulation addressing the cold-start challenge for classifiers.  
- LENSE system effectively manages noisy, slow, and expensive human experts.  
- Integrates latency and cost optimization into the learning framework, a compelling contribution.  
- Strong experimental results: ~10x cost reduction on the NER task versus a 5-vote human baseline while improving performance.  
- Evaluation thorough, comparing classifier accuracy against multiple human-vote baselines.  
- Main weakness: lack of clarity and opaque explanations; behavioral example too abstract and needs a concrete text/crowd/timing example.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent labeling (e.g., "entropic" vs "threshold"), missing references/figure numbers.  
- Related Work placed at the end hinders early contextualization and reads like an afterthought.  
- Underperforms 5-vote human baseline in some cases, highlighting trade-offs between model de-noising and acquiring more human consensus.  
- Recommendation: accept pending major revisions to improve clarity, add concrete examples, and fix manuscript organization and errors.
