- Proposes "on-the-job learning": a hybrid system that balances crowdsourced labels and online model training to maintain accuracy while reducing cost over time.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game between system and crowd, using a CRF-based Bayesian decision process with a continuous-time query timing component.  
- Develops an approximate solver using Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening (Algorithm 1) to optimize expected utility trading off accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Implements system LENSE and evaluates on NER, sentiment, and face-ID tasks against baselines (majority voting with 1/3/5 votes, online learning, threshold baseline).  
- Novelty: extends active classification to on-the-job deployment without initial labeled data and formalizes a utility-optimization stochastic-game perspective.  
- Core idea of reducing crowd reliance as model confidence increases is well-motivated and practically relevant.  
- Experiments show promising results: significant cost reductions (over an order of magnitude for NER) and accuracy improvements, with decreasing crowd dependence over time.  
- Major clarity issues hinder presentation, especially abrupt and poorly justified continuous-time extension (Section 3).  
- Continuous-time complexity appears unnecessary given the threshold baseline achieves comparable performance with simpler implementation.  
- Multiple reporting errors: incorrect claim of "beating human performance" on 5-vote NER (5-vote outperforms LENSE), missing 5-vote Face-ID and Sentiment results in some tables, and misstatements about threshold baseline performance.  
- Notation and readability problems (e.g., equation 2 missing s, equation 5 uses q inconsistently) reduce clarity.  
- Recommendation: present core method without continuous time first, correct reporting and notation errors, and clarify relationship to the threshold baseline.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution to on-the-job learning but requires major revisions for exposition and experimental reporting; recommend acceptance with major revisions.
