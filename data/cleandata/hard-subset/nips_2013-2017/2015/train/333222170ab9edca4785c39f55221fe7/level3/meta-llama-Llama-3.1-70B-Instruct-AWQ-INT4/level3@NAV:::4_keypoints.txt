- Presents a novel on-the-job learning framework using Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.
- Formalizes the setting as a stochastic game between system and crowd, enabling high-quality results from the start without a trained model.
- Main ideas are well-motivated and clearly explained with a thorough related-work review (online/active learning, crowdsourcing, structured prediction).
- Technical core is sound; game tree, utility function, and environment model are clearly described.
- Uses Monte Carlo tree search and other familiar techniques in a novel combination.
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and image classification are well-designed and show the method outperforms machine-only and human-only baselines on quality and cost.
- Paper is well-written overall, with clear technical detail, but some sections are dense and may be hard for non-experts.
- Application to face recognition is unclear—uncertain whether full instances or parts are queried.
- Threshold-baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters; origins and sensitivity analyses are not reported.
- Distinction from (active) online learning is weak; unclear how the approach meaningfully differs from active learning.
- Results are significant with broad applicability (NLP, vision, HCI) and advance the state of the art.
- Overall: sound and promising work that requires revisions to clarify application details, baseline choices, and distinctions from related methods.
