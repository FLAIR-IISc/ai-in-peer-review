- Introduces "on-the-job learning": interleave prediction with crowd queries to deploy AI without pre-labeled data.  
- Formalizes the setting as a stochastic game and approximates the policy using Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS).  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment classification, and image classification, showing large cost reductions vs full annotation and gains over online learning and a threshold baseline.  
- Builds on online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing literature but claims novelty in combining them for dynamic test-time learning.  
- Differentiates from prior real-time crowd labeling and active classification with an adaptive, game-theoretic formulation.  
- Strength — Novelty: the stochastic-game framing and MCTS-based policy approximation are genuinely novel and compelling.  
- Strength — Empirics: strong practical benefits on NER and positive results on sentiment and image tasks.  
- Strength — Clear formulation: well-defined tradeoffs (latency, cost, accuracy) and a principled utility function.  
- Strength — Reproducibility: open-source implementation (LENSE) provided.  
- Weakness — Face recognition experiment lacks detail on query granularity (whole image vs regions).  
- Weakness — Utility function may be less appropriate for independent, non-structured tasks where queries don't generalize across examples.  
- Weakness — Threshold baseline parameters (0.3, 0.98) are arbitrary; sensitivity analysis is missing.  
- Weakness — Missing a standard uncertainty-sampling active learning baseline for direct comparison.  
- Weakness — Distinction from active online learning is subtle; authors need a stronger argument for fundamental differences.  
- Weakness — Some implementation details (e.g., progressive widening) and environment model explanations are glossed over.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, well-motivated framework with compelling NER results and open-source code.  
- Argument against acceptance: unclear implementation details, incomplete baselines (no uncertainty sampling), and questionable utility for non-structured settings.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify experiments, discuss utility limitations, add stronger baseline comparisons, and expand implementation details and applicability discussion.
