- Proposes a novel on-the-job learning framework that uses real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty and emit predictions when confident.  
- Models the problem as a stochastic game based on Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Introduces a hybrid system that relies on crowdsourced labels early and transitions to an online-trained model as confidence increases.  
- Incorporates continuous time and a deadline-driven expected-utility objective to control query timing and rate.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and Temporal Difference (TD) learning to approximate the intractable optimization.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment classification, and image classification with comprehensive experiments.  
- LENSE outperforms most baselines but does not beat the 5-vote majority baseline.  
- A simple threshold baseline performs nearly as well and sometimes with lower latency.  
- Shows the model requires fewer crowd labels over time and attains high initial accuracy via crowd expertise.  
- Strengths: originality, significance, thorough multi-task evaluation, and generally clear presentation.  
- Weakness: continuous-time formulation complicates exposition and algorithmic details, hurting clarity.  
- Notation issues (e.g., use of q in eq. 5) and minor presentation errors need fixing.  
- Empirical reporting errors/omissions: incorrect "beating human" claim for 5-vote NER and missing 5-vote results for Face-ID and Sentiment in some tables/figures.  
- Related work coverage is broad but the paper should clarify distinctions from existing online/active learning and crowdsourcing methods.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10; recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
