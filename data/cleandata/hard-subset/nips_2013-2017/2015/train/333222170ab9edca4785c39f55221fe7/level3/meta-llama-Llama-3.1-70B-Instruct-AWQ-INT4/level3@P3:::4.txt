**Paper Review: On-the-Job Learning with Bayesian Decision Theory**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel framework for on-the-job learning, which enables a high-accuracy system to be deployed without requiring a trained model. The approach leverages real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty and output predictions when confident. The authors model on-the-job learning as a stochastic game based on Bayesian decision theory, which allows them to balance latency, cost, and accuracy objectives in a principled way. They develop an approximation based on Monte Carlo Tree Search and progressive widening to reason about continuous time. The authors evaluate their approach on three datasets: named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification, demonstrating significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements.

**Strengths**

* The paper introduces a novel framework for on-the-job learning, which addresses the challenge of deploying high-accuracy systems without requiring a trained model.
* The authors provide a rigorous mathematical formulation of the problem, using Bayesian decision theory and stochastic games.
* The proposed approach is evaluated on three datasets, demonstrating significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements.
* The authors provide an open-source implementation of their system, LENSE, which is available for further development and testing.

**Weaknesses**

* The application to face recognition is unclear, and it is not evident how the entire instance is queried or if something else is used.
* In non-structured settings, the model does not propagate information across examples, which may limit the applicability of the proposed utility function.
* The threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters, and their origin and sensitivity are not reported.
* The authors do not provide an uncertainty-sampling baseline, which is a common approach in active learning.
* The distinction from active online learning is weak, and it is not entirely clear how the proposed approach differs from existing active learning methods.

**Quality**

* The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a rigorous mathematical formulation of the problem.
* The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.
* The authors are careful about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

**Clarity**

* The paper is clearly written, and the authors provide a well-organized presentation of their work.
* The paper adequately informs the reader, and the expert reader can reproduce the results with sufficient information.

**Originality**

* The paper proposes a novel framework for on-the-job learning, which addresses the challenge of deploying high-accuracy systems without requiring a trained model.
* The authors provide a new combination of familiar techniques, using Bayesian decision theory and stochastic games to model on-the-job learning.
* The paper is significantly different from previous contributions, and the authors adequately reference related work.

**Significance**

* The results are important, and other people (practitioners or researchers) are likely to use these ideas or build on them.
* The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research, and it advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.
