- Proposes on-the-job learning: hybrid system using real-time crowd queries plus an online-trained model that reduces crowd dependence as confidence grows.
- Frames decision problem as a stochastic game over a CRF, optimizing a Bayesian utility trading accuracy (ExpAcc), monetary cost, and latency.
- Explicitly models continuous time (query send times and response times) in the environment P(y,r,t|x,q,s).
- Uses MCTS with UCT and progressive widening to approximate intractable planning in continuous time (Algorithm 1).
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face-ID against majority-vote baselines, an online-learning oracle, and a threshold heuristic; implemented as LENSE.
- Connects to online learning, active learning, POMDP crowd control, and structured prediction with partial measurements.
- Formulation is principled; environment factorization is reasonable but assuming constant worker accuracy (e.g., 0.7) is simplistic.
- MCTS + progressive widening is appropriate but the empirical benefit of continuous time over simpler discrete/batch models is not isolated.
- Empirically outperforms 1- and 3-vote baselines and the online oracle, but often fails to beat 5-vote majority; threshold baseline is competitive and sometimes faster.
- Reporting issues and omissions reduce credibility and need correction.
- Major clarity problems around mid-Section-3’s continuous-time notation and the terse description of Algorithm 1.
- Recommendation: present core method in discrete/event-driven form first, then add continuous-time as an extension with focused ablations.
- Request clarification on return policy: whether return is driven by entropy/confidence threshold, explicit deadline t_deadline, or purely optimized utility with wT.
- Novelty: framing as a stochastic game over a CRF with continuous-time modeling and MCTS planning is original.
- Significance: important and practical problem with potential impact, but limited empirical gains over simpler heuristics temper impact.
- Pros for acceptance: timely problem, principled decision-theoretic formulation, novel continuous-time treatment, multi-domain evaluation, open-source release, shows decreasing crowd usage.
- Cons against acceptance: clarity and notation problems, modest gains over threshold baseline, failure to consistently beat 5-vote majority, reporting errors, limited analysis of when continuous-time helps.
- Specific corrections: remove incorrect “beats human” claim for 5-vote NER and include 5-vote results consistently for Face-ID and Sentiment.
- Specific corrections: add threshold baseline to missing Sentiment experiments, bold best results and latency values consistently.
- Specific corrections: clearly define and justify t_deadline, monetary/time weights (wM, wT), query costs, and concurrency limits.
- Algorithm fixes: avoid claiming TD learning if not used; provide pseudocode for ExpAcc estimation and the rollout policy.
- Notation fixes: include s in conditioning of p(y,r,t|x,q,s) and avoid reusing q as both action index and function/distribution.
- Threshold baseline correction: fix the uncertainty-reduction inequality and justify the 0.3 factor used in the model.
- Worker modeling: discuss sensitivity to pR and consider modeling or learning worker reliabilities rather than assuming uniform accuracy.
- Ablations requested: compare discrete-time/planner-without-latency to continuous-time planner to quantify latency/quality gains from continuous time.
- Minor presentation fixes: ensure consistent bolding, state query costs and deadlines, and report allowed parallel queries.
- Overall recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing clarity, reporting, and ablation issues to strengthen the paper’s credibility and impact.
