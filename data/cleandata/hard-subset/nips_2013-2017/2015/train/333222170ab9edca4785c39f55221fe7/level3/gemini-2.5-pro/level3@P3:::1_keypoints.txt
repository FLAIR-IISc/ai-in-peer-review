- Proposes on-the-job learning that starts with real-time crowdsourcing and transitions to ML, formalized as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory and approximated with MCTS; evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image tasks.  
- Strength: Addresses a significant, practical "cold start" problem with a novel, principled stochastic-game/Bayesian formulation.  
- Strength: Theoretical formulation is sound and modeling querying as sequential decision-making is appropriate; MCTS is a reasonable approximation.  
- Strength: Paper is generally well-written and clearly motivated.  
- Weakness: Empirical gains over the simple Threshold baseline are modest on some tasks; baseline parameters (0.3 and 0.88) are unexplained and may be untuned.  
- Weakness: Large cost reduction on NER may be partly explained by the model quickly learning a dominant "none" class, needing more discussion.  
- Weakness: Equation 2 (ExpAcc) should be conditioned on the current state/posterior; posterior dependence is not clearly stated.  
- Weakness: Response model pR(ri | y_qi) assumes worker answers depend only on the queried label—this simplification should be explicitly stated and justified.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 has a likely typo (N(s) vs. N(σ)) and lacks a formal definition of N(σ) in the caption/text.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether underlying ML models/baselines (e.g., CRF, Online learning) are competitive; reported benefits may come from extra human labels rather than a superior querying strategy.  
- Weakness: Lacks qualitative/error analysis and example cases illustrating when the MCTS policy outperforms heuristics or when it fails, and lacks analysis of over/under-querying behaviors.  
- Overall: Important and promising contribution but incomplete—needs stronger baselines, deeper empirical and qualitative analysis, and clearer technical details for reproducibility; recommend significant revision.
