- Summary: Proposes a Bayesian decision-theory framework for on-the-job learning using real-time crowdsourcing, modeled as a stochastic game and approximated with MCTS/progressive widening; evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image tasks with cost and accuracy gains.

- Strength: Novel framework enabling high-accuracy deployment without a pre-trained model.

- Strength: Rigorous mathematical formulation using Bayesian decision theory and stochastic games.

- Strength: Empirical evaluation on three datasets shows significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements.

- Strength: Open-source implementation (LENSE) provided for reproducibility and further work.

- Weakness: Face-recognition application is unclear—how instances are queried and processed is not explained.

- Weakness: In non-structured settings the model fails to propagate information across examples, limiting utility of the proposed objective.

- Weakness: Threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters without reporting their origin or sensitivity.

- Weakness: Missing uncertainty-sampling baseline, a common and relevant active-learning comparison.

- Weakness: Distinction from active online learning is weak; differences from existing active learning methods are not clearly articulated.

- Quality: Technically sound with theoretical support and experiments that back the claims.

- Clarity: Paper is clearly written and organized, with sufficient detail for expert reproduction.

- Originality: Offers a novel combination of Bayesian decision theory and stochastic games for on-the-job learning and differs meaningfully from prior work.

- Significance: Results are likely to be useful to practitioners and researchers and advance the state of the art.

- Pro arguments: Novel framework, rigorous formulation, and strong empirical results across multiple tasks.

- Con arguments: Unclear face-recognition setup, arbitrary baseline parameters, and absence of uncertainty-sampling baseline.

- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing weaknesses (clarify face-recognition use and add uncertainty-sampling baseline/sensitivity analyses).
