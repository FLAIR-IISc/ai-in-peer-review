- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy system from zero examples using real-time crowdsourcing and only predict when confident.  
- Model improves online, reducing human queries over time.  
- Formulates problem as a stochastic game with Bayesian decision theory balancing accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search to approximate the optimal querying/prediction policy.  
- Novel focus on maintaining high accuracy from the very first instance, linking online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing.  
- Strength: original, practically significant paradigm that reduces upfront data collection needs.  
- Strength: principled formulation and compelling empirical results (over order-of-magnitude cost reduction on NER).  
- Strength: public release of code and data improves reproducibility.  
- Weakness: clarity issues—application to face recognition/image classification is unclear and not explained.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation weaknesses—threshold baseline uses arbitrary parameters with no sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: missing standard uncertainty-sampling active learning baseline for comparison.  
- Weakness/applicability: utility function seems tailored to structured tasks; benefits for non-structured tasks are unclear.  
- Weakness/originality: distinction from online active learning is not clearly articulated; active learning could be adapted to this setting.  
- Conclusion: promising and sound core idea, but needs clearer experiments, stronger baselines, and better positioning relative to existing work.
