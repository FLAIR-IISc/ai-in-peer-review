- Paper introduces "on-the-job learning": real-time crowd queries plus online model updates to deliver high accuracy from the first input while reducing crowd reliance over time.
- Formalizes the problem as a Bayesian decision-theoretic stochastic game trading off accuracy, latency, and monetary cost.
- Uses MCTS with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy over asynchronous, continuous-time crowd responses.
- System LENSE evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face/image classification and compared to multiple human and machine baselines.
- Strength: tackles an important, time-sensitive deployment problem requiring zero initial labels.
- Strength: principled Bayesian objective that explicitly balances cost, time, and accuracy.
- Strength: novel technical contribution combining MCTS and progressive widening for continuous-time crowd responses.
- Strength: practical design (retainer pool, asynchronous querying) matching deployment constraints.
- Strength: empirical improvements over machine-only and single-vote human baselines, and cost reductions vs multi-vote consensus.
- Weakness: exposition opaque in places; key example is abstract and needs a concrete end-to-end micro-trace.
- Weakness: inconsistent baseline naming (“entropic” vs “threshold”) across tables and text.
- Weakness: minor errors and missing references (e.g., “Figure ??”) and other proofreading issues.
- Weakness: related work is placed late; recommend moving a concise related-work section earlier for context.
- Weakness: simplistic worker/noise model (pR fixed at 0.7) ignores worker heterogeneity and drift; needs per-worker or per-task modeling and comparison to aggregation methods (Dawid–Skene, GLAD, MACE).
- Weakness: evaluation relies heavily on pre-collected response/latency pools rather than live experiments; needs clarification and sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: potential calibration issues—ExpAcc with MAP may overweight overconfident models; request sensitivity analyses for wM and wT and ablations isolating time reasoning.
- Weakness: LENSE underperforms a 5-vote baseline in accuracy; recommend explicit cost–accuracy frontier plots with confidence intervals.
- Quality: generally sound formulation and appropriate approximation methods, but reproducibility is hindered by missing hyperparameter and feature details.
- Clarity: paper is readable but occasional opacity and notation inconsistencies; a walk-through example and notation table would help.
- Originality: novel integration of Bayesian decision theory, real-time crowdsourcing, and MCTS for continuous-time responses.
- Significance: addresses a relevant deployment problem likely to influence crowd-in-the-loop and test-time acquisition research.
- Pros for acceptance: principled framing, novel algorithmic idea, demonstrated trade-offs, and open-source release.
- Cons for acceptance: exposition issues, simplistic noise model, reliance on pre-collected pools, and accuracy trade-offs vs strong multi-vote baselines.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to mis-specification of pR and pT and whether these can be learned online.
- Question: can the policy incorporate worker selection/routing to historically fast/accurate workers?
- Question: how do wM and wT map to SLAs and can the framework support constrained optimization instead of a scalarized utility?
- Minor requests: standardize “threshold” vs “entropic” naming, fix placeholder figure refs, and consider moving related work earlier.
