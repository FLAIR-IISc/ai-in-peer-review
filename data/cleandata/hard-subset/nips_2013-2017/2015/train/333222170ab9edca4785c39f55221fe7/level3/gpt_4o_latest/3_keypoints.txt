- Proposes a novel hybrid on-the-job learning system that transitions from crowd labels to an incrementally trained model to reduce crowd reliance over time.
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game using a CRF and balances latency, cost, and accuracy via Bayesian decision theory.
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search and Temporal Difference learning to approximate the intractable optimal policy.
- Implements LENSE and evaluates across NER, sentiment, and image classification tasks.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance: large cost reductions (e.g., order-of-magnitude in NER) and improved precision, recall, and F1 versus most baselines.
- Open-source implementation improves reproducibility and practical adoption.
- Theoretical grounding is solid through Bayesian decision theory and MCTS-based optimization.
- Continuous-time dynamics add realism but complicate the model and provide little empirical benefit; should be treated as an extension.
- Simpler baselines (the "threshold" baseline) perform nearly as well in some cases, questioning the need for full model complexity.
- Paper presentation is occasionally dense; the stochastic game and utility sections need simplification for accessibility.
- Experimental reporting is incomplete for some baselines (e.g., 5-vote and threshold) and should highlight latency and cost metrics more clearly.
- Some parameters (e.g., t_deadline) and variables are not clearly defined, hindering reproducibility.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, complete experiments, and justify model complexity.
- For acceptance: novel, impactful approach with strong empirical/theoretical support and open-source code; Against acceptance: clarity/reporting gaps and insufficient justification for added complexity (continuous time).
