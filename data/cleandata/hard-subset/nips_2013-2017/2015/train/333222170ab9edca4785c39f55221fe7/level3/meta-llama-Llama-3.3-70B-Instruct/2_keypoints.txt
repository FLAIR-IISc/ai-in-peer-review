- Proposes LENSE, a novel on-the-job learning framework that learns from noisy crowd responses by modeling the problem as a stochastic game.  
- Uses Monte Carlo tree search with progressive widening to approximate the optimal policy.  
- Evaluated on three tasks: named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification.  
- Strength: principled handling of noisy crowd responses and ability to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Strength: demonstrates cost reductions and accuracy improvements over baselines with thorough behavioral analysis.  
- Weakness: example in the paper is abstract and hard to follow; needs a specific query with crowd responses.  
- Weakness: inconsistent terminology in Tables 2 and 3 causing reader confusion.  
- Weakness: related work placement is unclear and should be moved earlier for context.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and future directions.  
- Paper quality: clear, well-organized, with sufficient implementation and evaluation details and well-presented results.  
- Originality/significance: novel combination of game theory, machine learning, and crowdsourcing addressing zero-labeled-example classifiers with high potential impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
