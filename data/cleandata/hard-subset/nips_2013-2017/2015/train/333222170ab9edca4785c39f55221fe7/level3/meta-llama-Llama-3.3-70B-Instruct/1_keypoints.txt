- Uses Bayesian decision theory and develops an MCTS-based approximation algorithm to find querying policies.
- Problem formulation is clear, well-motivated, and technically sound.
- Thorough theoretical analysis of the game tree and utility function.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: three tasks and multiple baselines.
- Presentation omits important details (e.g., undefined N(s) in Algorithm 1, missing derivation of baseline thresholds).
- Empirical gains are small—the method only slightly outperforms the threshold-based heuristic.
- Technical issues noted (questions about Equation (2)) that require addressing.
- Lacks sufficient discussion/comparison to state-of-the-art ML systems, and limited description of the tasks used.
- Needs error analysis and a detailed study of how varying worker quality affects results.
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality; writing and organization are good.
