- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy systems from scratch by querying human workers in real time and training an online model (CRF) from responses.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game between system and crowd using Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Proposes an MCTS-based approximation with progressive widening to handle intractable optimal policy computation in continuous time.  
- Demonstrates generality with experiments on NER, sentiment analysis, and image classification, showing learning over time and reduced query cost.  
- Includes multiple baselines (human-only, online learning, heuristic) to contextualize results.  
- Strength: original and significant—practical paradigm for bootstrapping ML without labeled data and likely to inspire follow-up work.  
- Strength: principled formulation—elegant Bayesian decision-theoretic model and stochastic-game perspective.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation across tasks showing reduced reliance on crowd while maintaining accuracy.  
- Weakness: clarity and organization suffer—continuous-time model introduced immediately makes exposition hard to follow; suggest presenting discrete-time first.  
- Weakness: limited justification for full complexity—simple threshold baseline often performs comparably, so trade-offs between LENSE and the heuristic need clearer discussion.  
- Weakness: inaccuracies in experimental reporting undermine confidence (misleading abstract claim about outperforming expert labels).  
- Weakness: missing baselines/results—5-vote baseline absent for Sentiment and Faces; threshold baseline missing for Sentiment and cost-over-time plot.  
- Weakness: minor formatting/reporting errors (e.g., not bolding best F1/lowest latency in tables).  
- Question: threshold baseline formula appears to have a typo—uncertainty should not increase with more queries; please clarify.  
- Question: unclear termination condition—how does the system decide to stop querying (deadline vs. confidence threshold)?  
- Notational issue: utility conditioning in Eq (1) and environment model in Eq (3) seem inconsistent; check conditioning sets.  
- Notational/algorithm issue: UCT updates reference V(sigma')/N(sigma') for unvisited nodes (N=0); clarify initialization or exploration rule.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—address clarity and experimental reporting to increase impact and adoption.
