- Introduces a novel on-the-job learning framework that queries crowdsourced labels during inference, modeled as a Bayesian stochastic game and approximated with MCTS, evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification.  
- Addresses a compelling real-world problem: building accurate systems without initial labeled data by trading off latency, cost, and accuracy.  

Strengths:
- Novel combination of Bayesian decision theory, stochastic game modeling, and MCTS for real-time on-the-job learning.  
- Demonstrates large practical impact (e.g., order-of-magnitude cost reduction on NER) and accuracy improvements over baselines.  
- Thorough evaluation across three diverse tasks indicating initial generalizability.  
- Open-source LENSE release promotes reproducibility and follow-up work.  
- Clear motivation framing the challenge of deployment without pre-labeled data.

Weaknesses:
- Explanations can be opaque; illustrative example (Line 154) lacks concrete details about queries, responses, and timings.  
- Minor inconsistencies and errors (e.g., threshold baseline labels, missing figure reference) reduce polish.  
- Related work placement (Section 5) feels disjointed and reads as a list rather than integrated analysis.  
- Insufficient analysis of baselines: LENSE underperforms a 5-vote human baseline in Table 2 and needs deeper discussion of tradeoffs.  
- Sparse details on the crowdsourcing setup, worker selection, quality control, and potential response biases.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Tackles an important problem with a principled, novel approach and strong empirical gains; open-source release adds value.

Arguments for rejection:
- Presentation opacity, minor errors, and inadequate discussion of limitations versus stronger human baselines raise concerns.

Overall assessment:
- Solid and promising work; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, fix inconsistencies, add a concrete illustrative example, and better analyze the 5-vote tradeoffs.

Rating: Accept (with minor revisions).
