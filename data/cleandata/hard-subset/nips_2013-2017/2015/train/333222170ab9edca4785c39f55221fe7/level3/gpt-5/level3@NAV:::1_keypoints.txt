- Paper formalizes on-the-job learning as a Bayesian decision-theoretic stochastic game trading accuracy, latency, and cost, approximated with MCTS/UCT and progressive widening.  
- System queries a real-time crowd initially and transitions to self-prediction as the CRF improves, deciding which labels to query and when to stop.  
- Framing builds on value-of-information and POMDP-style crowd control, extending prior real-time crowd-machine loops with a principled objective and search-based policy.  
- Modeling choices are sensible and MCTS approximation is reasonable for the large state/action space.  
- Key technical details are missing or unclear: noise model assumes uniform 70% worker accuracy and independence, omitting heterogeneity and calibration.  
- Latency model and its priors are under-specified.  
- Important hyperparameters (UCT c, progressive widening schedule, wM/wT scaling) are not reported or analyzed.  
- Empirical gains over the threshold heuristic are modest; stronger baselines (myopic VOI, greedy expected-utility with exact marginals, POMDP approximations, stronger learners) are needed.  
- Eq. (2) needs clarification on conditioning; ExpAcc should be under p(y | x, q, s, r, t).  
- Eq. (3) must explain why pR(ri | y, qi) drops dependence on full y and qi (locality/stationarity assumptions).  
- Algorithm 1: N(·) should be defined consistently and UCT constant c and progressive widening rule must be specified.  
- Baseline threshold choices (0.3 uncertainty reduction, 0.88/0.98 target confidences) need justification—were they tuned or derived from the response model?  
- Decision-theoretic formulation and MCTS planning combination is a novel and timely synthesis, though components are known.  
- Potentially impactful for rapid deployment with limited data, but conclusions are limited by evaluation design and choice of baselines.  
- Recommend error analysis and ablations (remove progressive widening; vary pR accuracy; model worker heterogeneity) to show where MCTS helps vs. heuristics.  
- Pros: clear principled formulation, plausible algorithm, open-source implementation.  
- Cons: underwhelming empirical comparisons vs. weak baselines and missing technical/sensitivity analyses.  
- Overall: promising approach that requires clarifications and stronger empirical support before stronger acceptance recommendation.
