- Proposes a novel on-the-job learning framework using a stochastic game and Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Clearly differentiates from online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing by building accuracy during test time.  
- Experimental results on NER, sentiment, and image classification show promise, especially in reducing annotation cost versus full labeling.  
- Publicly available code enhances reproducibility and follow-up research.  
- Explanations and technical details are often opaque; Section 3 (stochastic game and utility) is dense and needs clearer, more intuitive exposition.  
- The example at Line 154 is too abstract; a concrete example with realistic crowd responses and timings is needed.  
- Inconsistent labeling of the threshold baseline as “entropic” and “threshold”; labels should be standardized.  
- Related work placed in Section 5 feels disjointed and should be more integrated throughout the paper.  
- Table 2 shows LENSE beats a 3-vote consensus but loses to a 5-vote consensus; this trade-off and the role of additional votes for denoising needs deeper discussion.  
- Minor issue: missing figure reference on Line 086 ("Figure ??").  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution but requires major revisions for clarity and organization; recommend acceptance with major revisions.
