- Addresses on-the-job learning: start with crowd queries and transition to ML as labels accrue.  
- Formalizes interaction with crowd as a stochastic game within Bayesian decision theory.  
- Policy optimizes a utility trading off accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Uses MCTS with progressive widening to approximate intractable optimal policy.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing cost savings vs pure crowdsourcing and improvements over an online baseline.  
- Quality: technically sound formulation and reasonable choice of MCTS for large state spaces.  
- Experiments cover three diverse tasks and an open-source implementation aids reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Marginal gains over the simple threshold baseline on some tasks question practical necessity of MCTS.  
- Weakness: Lack of deeper analysis explaining when and why LENSE outperforms heuristics.  
- Weakness: Online learning baseline strength unclear; base models may not be state-of-the-art, confounding conclusions.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written with clear problem setup and algorithm overview.  
- Missing detail: Utility definition (Eq. 1) needs clearer conditioning in the expectation.  
- Missing detail: Response model (Eq. 3) should justify dependence only on local label rather than full label sequence.  
- Missing detail: N(σ) in Algorithm 1 is poorly defined beyond a comment.  
- Missing detail: Threshold baseline hyperparameters (0.3, 0.98) lack justification or validation procedure.  
- Originality: Novel, principled casting of on-the-job learning as a stochastic game and use of MCTS in this context.  
- Significance: Problem is practically important and the decision-theoretic framework is valuable, but empirical evidence is limited.  
- For acceptance: principled Bayesian formulation, novel stochastic-game + MCTS approach, practical importance, and open-source code.  
- For rejection: insufficient empirical advantage over simple heuristics, inadequate baseline/hyperparameter justification, and lack of qualitative/error analysis to justify complexity.
