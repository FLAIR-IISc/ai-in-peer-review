- Proposes a novel on-the-job learning framework casting prediction as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off cost, latency, and accuracy.  
- Demonstrates promising empirical results on NER, sentiment, and image classification with significant cost reductions versus full annotation.  
- Claims improvement on face recognition but fails to specify whether whole images or specific regions were queried and how that affects performance.  
- Utility function does not propagate information across examples, limiting adaptability in dynamic, non-structured environments.  
- Threshold baseline uses parameters (0.3 and 0.98) that appear arbitrary and lacks any sensitivity analysis.  
- Critical omission of an uncertainty-sampling (standard active learning) baseline, making comparative gains unclear.  
- Weak distinction from active online learning since active learning could be adapted to update models at test time similarly.  
- Some experimental details are unclear, reducing reproducibility and interpretability of results.  
- Clear presentation and available code improve reproducibility and understanding.  
- Overall concerns about generalizability, baseline selection, and evaluation lead to a recommendation of weak reject.
