- Introduces an on-the-job learning framework that uses real-time crowdsourcing and gradually shifts to self-prediction, formalized as a stochastic game under Bayesian decision theory balancing latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Proposes an approximation using Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with progressive widening to handle the intractability of computing the optimal policy.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment classification, and image classification; shows significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements on NER; system LENSE released as open source.  
- Strength: Theoretical soundness — rigorous Bayesian/stochastic-game formulation and a thoughtful MCTS solution.  
- Strength: Practical relevance — tackles high-accuracy deployment without pre-existing labeled data, reducing labeling cost.  
- Strength: Empirical results are promising, particularly large gains on the NER task versus baselines.  
- Strength: Open-source code and datasets improve reproducibility and encourage follow-up work.  
- Weakness: Clarity issues — Equation (2) needs clarification about conditioning and the disappearance of y; definition of N(s) in Algorithm 1 is unclear.  
- Weakness: Empirical limitations — gains are much weaker on sentiment and image tasks, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: Baseline details missing — derivation of threshold baseline values (0.3 and 0.88) is not provided, making comparisons hard to judge.  
- Weakness: Role of human annotation not sufficiently analyzed — lacks error analysis or case studies showing where human labels drive improvements.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether the “online” baselines are best-in-class machine-learned systems, which could undermine fairness of comparisons.  
- Suggestion: Provide detailed clarifications for Equation (2) and the definition of N(s) in Algorithm 1.  
- Suggestion: Explain how threshold baseline values were derived and justify baseline selection.  
- Suggestion: Add error analysis and case studies to quantify the impact of human annotations and identify failure modes.  
- Suggestion: Compare LENSE to state-of-the-art machine learning systems and expand discussion of limitations and future improvements.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — novel and theoretically sound with potential, but needs clearer exposition and stronger empirical/baseline analysis.
