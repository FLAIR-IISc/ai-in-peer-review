- Proposes "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy systems from zero labels by using real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty per incoming instance.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game and applies Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Optimal policy is intractable; authors propose an MCTS approximation with progressive widening.  
- Implements LENSE and evaluates on NER, sentiment, and image classification; shows large cost reduction and improved accuracy vs standard online baselines.  
- Differentiates from online/active learning by focusing on querying before making a prediction on the current instance rather than only for model improvement.  
- Strength: novel, principled game-theoretic formulation for bootstrapping models without labeled data.  
- Strength: strong empirical NER results—order-of-magnitude cost reduction and ~8% F1 gain over a single annotator.  
- Strength: full system, code, data, and experiments released for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: method appears most effective for structured prediction where partial labels propagate information.  
- Weakness: limited applicability to non-structured tasks (Sentiment, Faces) where partial queries are less meaningful.  
- Weakness: unclear experimental details—e.g., what constitutes a "query" on the Faces dataset.  
- Weakness: threshold baseline uses unexplained hyperparameters (0.3 and 0.98); needs sensitivity analysis or justification.  
- Weakness: missing comparison to standard active-learning baselines (e.g., uncertainty sampling adapted to this setting).  
- Weakness: distinction from related online active learning work is somewhat brittle; novelty mainly in within-instance sequential policy.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a practical problem with principled theory, sound MCTS solution, strong NER impact, and reproducibility.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited generality to non-structured tasks, weaker baselines, and missing experimental clarity.  
- Requested revisions: clarify query mechanisms for Faces/Sentiment, justify or analyze threshold baseline parameters, add an uncertainty-sampling baseline, and discuss limitations for non-structured tasks.
