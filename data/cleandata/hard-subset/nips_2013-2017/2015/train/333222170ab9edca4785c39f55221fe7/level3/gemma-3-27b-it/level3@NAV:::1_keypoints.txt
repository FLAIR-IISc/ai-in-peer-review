- Paper proposes an on-the-job learning framework using Bayesian decision theory, modeled as a stochastic game and approximated with MCTS.  
- Framework aims to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy while transitioning from crowd reliance to model self-sufficiency.  
- Builds on prior online/active learning and crowdsourcing work, extending [6] with a stronger theoretical foundation.  
- Sensible sequential decision-making formulation that fits the on-the-job learning setting.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, theoretical justification, and a reasonably described methodology.  
- Empirical evaluation covers three tasks (NER, sentiment, image classification) showing potential.  
- Weakness: empirical gains over a threshold-based heuristic are small and not clearly convincing.  
- Missing clarifications: Eq. (2) should condition on state s; unexplained disappearance of y from p_R(r_i | y, q_i).  
- Missing definitions: Algorithm 1 does not specify N(s).  
- Baseline choices unclear: no justification for threshold values (0.3 and 0.88) and whether online baselines are optimal.  
- Crucial analysis lacking: need to determine if gains stem from the framework or annotation quality, plus an error analysis with specific examples.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to address clarifications, strengthen baselines, and provide thorough empirical/error analysis.
