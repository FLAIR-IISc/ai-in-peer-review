- Proposes "on-the-job learning" to deploy high-accuracy systems with zero initial training examples.  
- Models the setting as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Uses real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty and increasingly relies on the model as it improves.  
- Computes policies via a Monte Carlo Tree Search approximation because the optimal policy is intractable.  
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment classification, and image classification datasets.  
- Reports LENSE outperforms machine-only and human-only baselines in quality and cost.  
- On NER, LENSE achieves 88.4% F1 with over an order-of-magnitude cost reduction versus full human annotation.
