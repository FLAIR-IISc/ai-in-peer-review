- Presents a novel on-the-job learning framework that enables high-quality outputs from the start without a pre-trained model.  
- Models the problem as a stochastic game based on Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Uses real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty and outputs predictions when confident, reducing queries as the model improves.  
- Related to online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing but differs by maintaining high initial accuracy rather than only asymptotic guarantees.  
- Differs from active classification because it does not rely on a fully labeled dataset at test time.  
- Technical core: adapts Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with progressive widening to approximate the optimal policy in the stochastic game.  
- Evaluated on named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification tasks.  
- LENSE outperforms baselines (threshold heuristic, online learning) in both quality and cost.  
- Reported NER result: F1 88.4% at a cost over an order of magnitude lower than a 5-vote approach.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-organized, and the theoretical framing of crowd→model transition is useful.  
- Missing technical details: definition of N(s) in Algorithm 1 is not provided.  
- Baseline choices lack justification (e.g., thresholds 0.3 and 0.98 are unexplained).  
- Lacks sufficient analysis of competing “online” systems, including error analysis and illustrative example cases.  
- Unclear whether gains are due to the on-the-job learning framework itself or simply from added human annotations.  
- Paper could benefit from more figures and tables to better illustrate results and trade-offs.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and original with promising empirical results, but needs additional empirical/explanatory work.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing empirical gaps, missing technical details, and providing more analysis and examples.
