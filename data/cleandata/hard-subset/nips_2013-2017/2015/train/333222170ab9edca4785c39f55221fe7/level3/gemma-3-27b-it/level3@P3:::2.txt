This paper proposes a novel framework, “on-the-job learning,” for deploying accurate systems without initial labeled data, leveraging real-time crowdsourcing and Bayesian decision theory to balance accuracy, latency, and cost. The core idea of strategically querying a crowd to resolve uncertainty and improve a model iteratively is compelling, particularly for scenarios where pre-labeling is impractical. The approach is formalized as a stochastic game and approximated using Monte Carlo Tree Search.

The work builds upon existing research in online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing, but distinguishes itself by actively seeking information *during* prediction and simultaneously optimizing for multiple objectives. The authors appropriately cite related work, though its placement in Section 5 feels somewhat disjointed (discussed below). This paper notably addresses a significant challenge – building useful AI systems with minimal upfront investment – and offers a technically sophisticated solution.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty and Significance:** The on-the-job learning paradigm is a valuable contribution, addressing a practical problem often overlooked in the machine learning literature. The combination of Bayesian decision theory, stochastic game formulation, and MCTS for solving it is a creative and potentially impactful approach.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The results across three datasets (NER, sentiment classification, and image classification) demonstrate the effectiveness of LENSE, consistently outperforming baselines in terms of cost and/or accuracy. The 8% F1 improvement over single human labeling and 28% over online learning on NER are particularly impressive.
* **Practical Implementation:** The open-source implementation (LENSE) and detailed description of the crowdsourcing setup enhance the reproducibility and impact of the work.
* **Addressing Noisy Crowd Responses:** Explicitly modeling and handling noisy crowd responses through the response model (pR) is a crucial aspect of the framework.
* **Latency Optimization:** The consideration of latency as an optimization objective, alongside accuracy and cost, is a novel and valuable contribution.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity and Opaqueness:** While technically sound, the explanations are often dense and difficult to follow. The stochastic game formulation, while theoretically elegant, could benefit from more intuitive explanations and visualizations.
* **Example at Line 154:** The example provided at Line 154 regarding the system’s behavior is overly abstract. A concrete query with realistic crowd responses and timings would significantly improve understanding. For instance, demonstrating a scenario with ambiguous NER tags and varying crowd response times would be highly illustrative.
* **Inconsistent Baseline Labeling:** Tables 2 and 3 inconsistently label the threshold baseline as “entropic” vs “threshold,” which is confusing and should be standardized.
* **Related Work Placement:** Placing related work in Section 5 feels unnatural. It disrupts the flow of the paper and suggests a lack of integration of prior work into the core narrative. Related work should be interwoven throughout the introduction and method sections, highlighting how LENSE builds upon and differs from existing approaches.
* **Missing Figure Reference:** Line 086 contains a missing figure reference ("Figure ??"). This should be corrected.
* **Limited Error Analysis:** A more in-depth analysis of error cases and the types of queries where LENSE struggles would provide valuable insights.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a compelling and practically relevant problem.
* The proposed approach is novel and technically sound.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods.
* The open-source implementation promotes reproducibility and wider adoption.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The paper suffers from clarity issues that hinder understanding.
* Minor inconsistencies (baseline labeling, missing figure reference) detract from the overall quality.
* The placement of related work is questionable.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant contribution to the field. Despite the clarity issues and minor inconsistencies, the novelty, technical depth, and strong empirical results outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the clarity concerns, standardizing the baseline labeling, correcting the figure reference, and integrating related work more effectively throughout the paper.  Specifically, adding a concrete example to illustrate the query process and providing more intuition behind the stochastic game formulation would substantially improve the paper's accessibility.
