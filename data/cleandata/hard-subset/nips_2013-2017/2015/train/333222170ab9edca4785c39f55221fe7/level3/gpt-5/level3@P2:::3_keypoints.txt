- Proposes "on-the-job learning": interleave real-time crowdsourced queries with model inference and continual updates to achieve high accuracy from zero labeled data.  
- Frames decision-making as a Bayesian stochastic game between system and crowd with actions {query, wait, return} optimizing expected utility (accuracy vs cost vs latency).  
- Environment model couples structured prediction pθ(y|x) (linear-chain CRF), crowd-response noise pR(r|yq), and response-time pT(t|s) to handle asynchronous continuous-time responses.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search (UCT) with progressive widening to plan in continuous time because exact optimization is intractable.  
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and face attributes show LENSE improves accuracy at lower cost and decreasing latency over time, especially for NER.  
- Differentiates from online/active learning by actively querying before prediction and continuously updating at test time; extends decision-theoretic crowdsourcing with structured outputs and continuous-time planning.  
- Strength: well-motivated real-world problem addressing immediate accuracy under budget and latency constraints.  
- Strength: principled Bayesian decision-theoretic formulation coupling prediction, noisy responses, and response times.  
- Strength: sensible practical planner (MCTS + progressive widening) for continuous-time decisions.  
- Strength: empirical gains over single-vote and online-learning baselines, with cost reductions and fewer queries over time.  
- Strength: open-source code and reproducible experiments.  
- Weakness: exposition suffers when continuous-time modeling is introduced mid-section; notation issues and typos hinder readability.  
- Weakness: limited evidence that continuous-time modeling provides significant benefits over simpler baselines; threshold baseline often competitive and sometimes faster.  
- Weakness: inconsistent reporting (missing 5-vote baselines for some tasks, inconsistent inclusion of threshold baseline in tables/figures).  
- Weakness: experimental realism concerns—simulations reuse a frozen pool of worker responses/latencies, raising robustness questions to distribution shifts and prior misspecification.  
- Quality concern: limited sensitivity analyses (worker accuracy, latency distributions, penalty weights) and no robustness checks for pθ miscalibration.  
- Quality concern: missing ablations isolating continuous-time planning vs discrete-time/batched variants.  
- Quality concern: frozen worker pool may understate variance and overstate stability; needs discussion.  
- Clarity suggestions: present a discrete-time/no-time version first, then extend to continuous time for accessibility.  
- Clarity suggestions: clarify return decision criterion (entropy/confidence vs time/deadline) and how wT or deadlines were applied in experiments.  
- Clarity suggestions: state how many queries are launched in parallel vs sequentially and interaction with wT.  
- Clarity suggestions: fix notation/typos (consistent use of s in conditionals, avoid reusing q, correct threshold baseline inequality, ensure consistent bolding and complete baseline reporting).  
- Originality: novel combination of continuous-time crowd queries, structured prediction, and MCTS planning; game-theoretic formalization for structured outputs is a strong contribution.  
- Significance: high practical potential for real-time human-in-the-loop systems; NER results compelling, other tasks show room for improved modeling.  
- Key author questions: sensitivity to assumed worker accuracy and pR/pT misspecification; discrete- vs continuous-time benefit; selection/calibration of wM and wT; online learning of heterogeneous worker reliabilities; behavior under bursty arrivals and variable pool sizes.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on improved presentation, consistent reporting, and ablations clarifying continuous-time value.  
- Reviewer confidence: high.
