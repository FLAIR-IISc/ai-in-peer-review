- Introduces an on-the-job learning framework that uses crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty and reduce human input as the model improves.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game grounded in Bayesian decision theory.  
- Proposes an approximation algorithm using Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) to handle intractable optimal policy computation.  
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment, and image classification tasks, reporting cost reductions and performance improvements over baselines.  
- Novel contribution: formalizing real-time model–crowd interaction as a stochastic game for on-the-job learning.  
- Technical soundness: solid theoretical basis combining Bayesian decision theory with MCTS and careful trade-off consideration (latency, cost, accuracy).  
- Significance: potential to enable rapid deployment in resource-constrained, time-sensitive applications (e.g., disaster relief, monitoring).  
- Clarity and documentation: core concepts, implementation details, and experiments are generally well-explained; code (LENSE) and CodaLab artifacts provided for reproducibility.  
- Continuous-time extension detracts from clarity and adds complexity with limited empirical benefit compared to simpler baselines.  
- Experimental reporting errors/omissions: incorrect claim about outperforming human 5-vote majority in NER and missing 5-vote results for Face-ID and Sentiment in several tables/figures.  
- Presentation issues: inconsistent highlighting of best results and latencies; threshold baseline missing from some sentiment experiments.  
- Minor technical issues: equation (2) missing variable 's', confusing use of 'q' in equation (5), and a likely typo in the threshold-baseline inequality.  
- Ambiguity about experimental protocol: unclear role of t_deadline and whether final answers are reported by entropy threshold or time limit.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with promising empirical gains and public code for reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: added continuous-time complexity with little benefit, reporting errors/omissions, and presentation issues needing correction.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix clarity, correct reporting errors, improve presentation, and clarify experimental setup (suggest presenting core method first and treating continuous-time as an extension).
