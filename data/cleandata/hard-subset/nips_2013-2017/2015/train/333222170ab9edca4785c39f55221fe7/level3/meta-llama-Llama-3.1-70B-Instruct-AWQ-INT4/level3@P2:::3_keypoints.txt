- Proposes a novel on-the-job learning framework that combines crowdsourcing and online learning to achieve high accuracy at low cost.  
- Models the on-the-job learning setting as a stochastic game based on Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Provides a detailed problem formulation, model, and algorithmic description.  
- Evaluates the method on three tasks (NER, sentiment, image classification) with experimental results.  
- Thoroughly situates the work within related literature (online learning, active learning/classification, crowdsourcing, structured prediction).  
- Claims significant contributions: learning from noisy crowds on-the-job to maintain accuracy and reduce cost over time.  
- Demonstrates empirical strengths: originality, significance, potential impact, and outperforming most baselines.  
- Clarity suffers from complex concepts and notation, particularly the mid-Section-3 introduction of continuous time.  
- Continuous-time extension adds complexity but shows limited experimental benefit, questioning its necessity.  
- Contains minor errors and omissions: incorrect "beats human performance" claim for 5-vote NER and missing 5-vote results for Face-ID and Sentiment.  
- Presentation and formatting issues: best results and some latency values not bolded; threshold baseline missing from sentiment experiments.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to improve clarity, remove or relegate continuous-time details, fix errors/missing results, and improve formatting.
