- Proposes "on-the-job learning": deploy models with zero initial training by using real-time crowdsourcing, modeled as a stochastic game balancing latency, cost, and accuracy, approximated with MCTS; evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image tasks.  
- Strength: Addresses a well-motivated real-world need for immediate deployment without large labeled datasets.  
- Strength: Uses Bayesian decision theory to provide a principled trade-off among latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Strength: Thorough implementation with public code/data and strong empirical results on NER.  
- Weakness: Face-recognition/image-classification setup is unclear — unclear whether queries target whole images or specific features.  
- Weakness: Utility function appears tailored to structured prediction (e.g., NER) and its applicability to non-structured tasks is not discussed.  
- Weakness: Threshold baseline uses arbitrary parameters (0.3m ≥ 0.98) with no justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: Omits a standard uncertainty-sampling active learning baseline, making it hard to judge novelty of the query strategy.  
- Weakness: Fails to clearly distinguish from active online learning; no explanation why standard active learning cannot be applied by treating the input stream as the active set.  
- Overall: Promising for structured prediction but limited by unclear details, methodological gaps, and limited applicability.  
- Suggested fixes: Clarify face-recognition details, justify baseline parameters, add a standard active-learning baseline, and better differentiate from existing active learning methods.
