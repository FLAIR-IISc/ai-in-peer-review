- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy classifiers from zero labeled examples by querying crowd at prediction time and learning online.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Optimal policy is intractable; authors approximate it with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) plus progressive widening for continuous time.  
- Evaluated on three tasks (NER, sentiment, image classification) demonstrating generality.  
- Novel synthesis across online learning, active learning, and active classification; distinct because it queries before prediction, learns from noisy crowd oracles, and adapts online.  
- Significant and original problem framing with real-time latency and monetary cost integrated into a decision-theoretic model.  
- Technically sound: principled Bayesian foundation and well-justified MCTS approximation for policy learning.  
- Strong empirical results: large cost reductions (order-of-magnitude on NER) and accuracy gains over online-learning baselines.  
- Good empirical setup: diverse tasks, appropriate baselines (human-only, ML-only, heuristics), and noteworthy result of outperforming 3-vote human majority cost-effectively.  
- Reproducible work: code, data, and experiments released on CodaLab.  
- Presentation clarity needs improvement; some explanations are opaque and a concrete step-by-step example is recommended.  
- Terminology inconsistency ("Threshold" vs "Entropic") and a missing figure reference reduce readability.  
- Related Work placed at the end is unconventional and disrupts flow; should be moved earlier.  
- Experimental nuance: LENSE is more cost-effective but slightly below 5-vote baseline F1 on NER, highlighting a cost–accuracy tradeoff that merits discussion.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel, and practical work with addressable presentation issues; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
