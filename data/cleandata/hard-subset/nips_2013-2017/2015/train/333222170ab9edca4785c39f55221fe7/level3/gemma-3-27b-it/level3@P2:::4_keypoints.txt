- Proposes "on-the-job learning": combine real-time crowdsourcing with a Bayesian decision-theoretic stochastic-game formulation to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Uses a Monte Carlo Tree Search approximation to find policies for the intractable optimal solution.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing large cost reductions and performance gains versus full human labeling and simple baselines.  
- Builds on online/active learning, crowdsourcing, and structured prediction literature but emphasizes mandatory per-time-step predictions and test-time adaptation.  
- Open-source implementation (LENSE) and detailed crowdsourcing setup provided, aiding reproducibility.  
- Well-written paper with clear motivation, formulation, and solution description.  
- Thorough experimentation with multiple baselines enabling reasonable comparisons.  
- Main novelty: principled on-the-job learning formulation and Bayesian game-theoretic approach.  
- Strength is strongest for structured prediction (e.g., NER) where uncertainty localizes and yields large benefits.  
- Limited generalizability: approach seems less convincing for non-structured tasks where information doesn't propagate between examples.  
- Baseline sensitivity: threshold baseline uses seemingly arbitrary parameters (m and 0.98) without justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Missing an uncertainty-sampling active-learning baseline to isolate benefits of the proposed method.  
- Weak distinction from active online learning; paper needs clearer articulation of advantages over adapted online active learning.  
- Face recognition experiment lacks detail (what was queried—whole image vs. facial features), impeding assessment of applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to novelty, strong NER results, and reproducibility, but revise on comparisons and details.  
- Requested revisions: expand face-recognition details, add uncertainty-sampling baseline, justify/tune threshold parameters, clarify advantages over active online learning, and discuss non-structured task limitations.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10; overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
