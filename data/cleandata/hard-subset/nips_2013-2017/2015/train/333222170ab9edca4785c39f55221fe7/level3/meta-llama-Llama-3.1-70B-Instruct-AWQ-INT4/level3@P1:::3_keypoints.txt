- Proposes "on-the-job learning": combining crowdsourced labels with an online-trained model for structured prediction.
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory with system and crowd as players.
- Develops an approximation algorithm using Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and progressive widening to optimize expected utility.
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, reporting cost reductions and accuracy improvements over baselines.
- Strength: novel and original framework that integrates crowd labeling with online learning.
- Strength: well-formulated and theoretically grounded approach.
- Strength: efficient approximation algorithm (MCTS + progressive widening).
- Strength: empirical gains across multiple tasks.
- Weakness: major clarity problems, especially the mid-Section-3 continuous-time introduction that complicates exposition and algorithmic details.
- Weakness: continuous-time extension yields limited experimental benefit and may be unjustified relative to simpler baselines.
- Weakness: reporting errors and omissions (incorrect "beats human" claim for 5-vote NER; missing Face-ID and Sentiment results).
- Weakness: presentation/formatting issues (missing bolding for best results, missing latency values, omitted baseline in sentiment experiments).
- Suggestion: present core method without continuous time first and treat continuous time as an extension; fix notation issues (e.g., eq.2 and eq.5).
- Overall: original and significant work likely to stimulate adoption and discussion.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix clarity, reporting errors, and experimental details.
