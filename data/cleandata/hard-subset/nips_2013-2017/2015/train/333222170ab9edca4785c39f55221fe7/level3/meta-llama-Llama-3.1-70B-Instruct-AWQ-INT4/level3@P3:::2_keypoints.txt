- Proposes an on-the-job learning framework that learns from noisy crowd responses in real time, modeled as a stochastic game and approximated with game-playing techniques.
- System starts with zero training data, queries crowd only for uncertainty, and reduces reliance on crowd as model improves.
- Casts the problem in Bayesian decision-theoretic terms to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search and progressive widening to approximate the optimal policy.
- Evaluated on three tasks, showing significant cost reductions vs. pure crowd and accuracy gains vs. pure ML.
- Provides an open-source implementation of the system.
