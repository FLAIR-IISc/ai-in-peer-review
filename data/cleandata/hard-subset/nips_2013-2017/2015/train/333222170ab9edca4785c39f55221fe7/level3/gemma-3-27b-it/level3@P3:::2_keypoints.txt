- Proposes "on-the-job learning": deploy accurate systems without initial labels by querying crowds during prediction using Bayesian decision theory.
- Core idea: strategically query crowd to resolve uncertainty and iteratively improve the model, useful when pre-labeling is impractical.
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game and approximates solutions with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS).
- Distinguishes itself from prior work by actively seeking information during prediction and optimizing accuracy, latency, and cost simultaneously.
- Related work is cited but its placement in Section 5 feels disjointed and should be better integrated.
- Strong empirical results on NER, sentiment, and image classification, with notable F1 improvements over baselines.
- Provides an open-source implementation (LENSE) and detailed crowdsourcing setup, aiding reproducibility.
- Explicitly models and handles noisy crowd responses via a response model (pR).
- Includes latency as an explicit optimization objective, which is novel and practically valuable.
- Explanations are dense and sometimes hard to follow; more intuition and visualizations are needed for accessibility.
- The example at Line 154 is too abstract; a concrete scenario with realistic crowd responses and timings would help.
- Inconsistent labeling of the threshold baseline in Tables 2 and 3 ("entropic" vs "threshold") creates confusion.
