- Paper proposes on-the-job learning that interleaves real-time crowdsourcing with model prediction using a Bayesian utility trading off accuracy, cost, and latency, planned via MCTS with progressive widening.  
- Formalizes and extends prior real-time crowd systems (e.g., Legion:AR) and decision-theoretic crowd control, targeting structured outputs and continuous time.  

Strengths
- Principled formulation: stochastic-game view and explicit utility unify query scheduling choices (parallel vs sequential, redundancy).  
- Explicit modeling of asynchronous, noisy crowds by separating pθ(y|x), pR (response), and pT (response time).  
- Approximate planning: MCTS + UCT with progressive widening is a reasonable approach for continuous-time decision-making.  
- Empirical promise: reduced cost vs. multi-vote human baselines and improved F1 vs. single-vote and online-learning baselines; open-source release.  

Weaknesses
- Missing technical details limit reproducibility and interpretation (algorithm/hyperparameters, environment model fitting, learning updates).  
- Baselines and analysis are underpowered; gains may largely come from access to human labels at prediction time rather than superior policy.  
- Simplistic modeling assumptions: fixed worker accuracy (0.7), independence across queries/workers, underspecified label integration into pθ.  
- Limited ablations: unclear contribution of MCTS/UCT, progressive widening, utility weights, and response-time modeling.  

Quality concerns / required clarifications
- Threshold baseline formula appears inconsistent/incorrect and needs correction and justification.  
- Fixed pR (0.7 accuracy) is strong; sensitivity to worker accuracy distributions and noise models is missing.  
- UCT hyperparameter c, progressive widening schedule, and utility weights are not reported or justified.  
- Computational cost and wall-clock latency of MCTS at test time are not presented.  

Clarity issues
- Eq. (2) should explicitly condition ExpAcc on (x,q,s,r,t) to match decision dependence.  
- Transition from pR(ri|yqi) to p(r′|x,r,q) needs explicit marginalization explanation and justification.  
- Algorithm 1 uses symbols N(σ), V(σ), C(σ), c without definition; progressive widening schedule is not specified.  
- Environment model pT parametric form and estimation procedure are unspecified; independence/stationarity assumptions unclear.  
- Learning/update procedure for pθ during on-the-job operation (how noisy labels are used) is underspecified.  

Originality
- Novel decision-theoretic integration of MCTS/progressive widening for structured prediction with crowds and continuous-time reasoning, extending prior POMDP-style work.  

Significance
- Potentially impactful for rapid-deployment ML needing accuracy SLAs before training data exists, but stronger validation is needed to establish generality beyond NER.  

Specific requests for experiments/analysis
- Condition ExpAcc on context in Eq. (2) and clarify marginalization in response model.  
- Report sensitivity analyses to worker accuracy, class/instance-dependent noise, and redundancy strategies.  
- Define and report UCT c, progressive widening schedule, and computational overhead/latency.  
- Compare to stronger baselines: offline supervised with equal label budget, budgeted pool-based active learning, and POMDP policy like TurKontrol.  
- Provide diagnostic error analysis and qualitative examples of success/failure, and quantify gains from pre-prediction human input vs. policy/model.  

Pros for acceptance
- Principled decision-theoretic framing with concrete algorithm and open-source code.  
- Clear motivation for real-time deployment settings and promising NER results.  

Cons for acceptance
- Missing/under-specified technical details and experimental reporting.  
- Baselines and analyses insufficient to rule out simple explanations for gains (human labels at prediction time).  
- Simplistic noise model and limited ablations reduce confidence in claims.  

Suggested revisions
- Tighten math and algorithm specification; define all symbols and report hyperparameters.  
- Add ablations and sensitivity studies (pR accuracy, wM/wT, UCT c, progressive widening).  
- Strengthen baselines with budget-controlled comparisons and present cost–accuracy–latency Pareto curves.  
- Explore richer worker modeling (per-worker reliability, redundancy allocation) and detail label integration into pθ.  

Overall assessment
- A timely, principled contribution with promising results but currently limited by missing details, weak baselines, and insufficient analyses; warrants further experiments and clarifications.
