- Paper formalizes on-the-job learning as a Bayesian stochastic game trading off accuracy, latency, and cost with an MCTS+progressive widening approximation.
- Prediction model: CRF updated online; environment model factors crowd response accuracy and response-time distributions.
- Empirical claims: cost reductions vs pure crowd, accuracy improvements vs online learner, and comparison to a simple threshold heuristic across NER, sentiment, and face tasks.
- Main contribution: principled decision-theoretic framing of crowd→model transition that generalizes prior on-the-job systems.
- Strength: clear, principled formulation with an explicit utility balancing accuracy, time, and money that yields intuitive behaviors.
- Strength: reasonable, generalizable MCTS+progressive widening approximation for continuous-time decision trees.
- Strength: empirical consistency across tasks and public code/data for reproducibility.
- Weakness: notation and conditioning underspecified—ExpAcc should be conditioned on (x, q, s, r, t) but is not explicit.
- Weakness: environment model unclear—discrepancy between pR(ri | yqi) and earlier sampling r′ ~ p(r′ | x, r) and missing explanation of marginalization/integration over y.
- Weakness: unjustified fixed worker accuracy (0.7) and unclear whether pR is learned or adapts over time.
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 notation inconsistent—N(s) vs N(σ) undefined; tie-breaking, initialization, and rollout policy unspecified.
- Weakness: MCTS hyperparameters and stopping criteria (UCT c, simulation depth, rollout policy) are not reported or analyzed for sensitivity.
- Weakness: ExpAcc computation for structured outputs and efficient expectation approximation are not described.
- Baselines weak: threshold heuristic is hand-tuned and lacks principled tuning or validation justification (0.3 factor and threshold confusion 0.98 vs 0.88).
- Baselines weak: the online ML baselines may be too simple—stronger contemporaneous models (richer CRFs, BiLSTM-CRF, deep sentiment models, deep face features) are not compared.
- Evaluation: main comparison to a single heuristic is underwhelming; need stronger baselines like myopic VoI, greedy uncertainty sampling, or small POMDP solvers.
- Evaluation: frozen pool of 5 workers and sampling without replacement may bias results and under-represent real-time worker variance.
- Evaluation: missing error analysis and examples to show when LENSE helps or harms and to attribute gains to policy vs extra annotations.
- Clarity: generally well-written but missing reproducibility details such as conditioning, environment model, MCTS params, priors for pR and pT, feature sets, and regularization.
- Clarity: report chosen weights wM and wT and provide sensitivity analysis.
- Originality: novel combination of stochastic-game Bayesian utility with MCTS+progressive widening in human-in-the-loop structured prediction.
- Significance: promising and likely influential, but empirical evaluations currently insufficient to claim clear superiority.
- Specific requests: explicitly condition Eq. (2); reconcile pR notation and sampling; clarify if pR/pT are learned or fixed and whether pθ and pR are calibrated.
- Specific requests: define N(s)/N(σ), c, rollout policy, termination, progressive widening constants, and MCTS hyperparameters.
- Specific requests: justify/tune baseline thresholds on validation and either strengthen ML baselines or frame results as using a simple ML backend.
- Pros summary: principled objective, practical MCTS approximation, code/data release, and consistent cost reductions with reasonable accuracy.
- Cons summary: missing technical details, weak heuristics/baselines, limited ablations/error analysis, and reliance on a small frozen worker pool.
- Overall assessment: reasonable and timely with a valuable decision-theoretic framing but currently borderline due to empirical and explanatory gaps; needs clarifications, stronger baselines, and additional analyses.
