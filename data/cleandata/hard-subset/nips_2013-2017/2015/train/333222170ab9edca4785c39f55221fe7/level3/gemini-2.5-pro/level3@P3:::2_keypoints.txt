- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy a high-accuracy classifier from zero labeled examples by querying real-time crowdsourcing only when uncertain.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game balancing accuracy, cost, and latency using Bayesian decision theory.  
- Optimal policy is intractable; proposes an MCTS approximation with progressive widening.  
- Distinguishes itself from online/active learning by actively querying before making test-time predictions.  
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and image classification compare LENSE to reasonable baselines and use a "frozen pool" of worker responses for fair reproducibility.  
- Significance/originality: addresses a practical cold-start problem and integrates latency/cost into query decisions at the token/part level.  
- Technical quality: sound formulation as a stochastic game and reasonable use of MCTS; experiments are well-designed.  
- Impactful results: strong performance, notably >10x cost reduction on NER versus a 5-vote baseline while improving accuracy over expert labels.  
- Clarity issues: explanations are sometimes opaque; core concepts need clearer, concrete walkthrough examples (e.g., step-by-step query timelines).  
- Presentation inconsistency: baseline naming varies between tables ("entropic" vs "threshold") and should be standardized.  
- Results discussion needs depth: LENSE outperforms 3-vote but underperforms 5-vote baselines—investigate whether this is due to MCTS approximation, noise model limits, or human error complexity.  
- Paper organization: "Related Work" should appear earlier (after the introduction) to better contextualize contributions.  
- Minor issues: manuscript needs proofreading (e.g., missing figure reference "Figure ??").  
- Recommendation: original, technically solid, and influential work recommended for acceptance contingent on clarity improvements and expanded discussion.
