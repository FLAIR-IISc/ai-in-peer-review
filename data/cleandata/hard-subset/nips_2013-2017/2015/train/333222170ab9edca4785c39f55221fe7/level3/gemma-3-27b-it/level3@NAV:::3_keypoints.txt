- Presents LENSE, a hybrid on-the-job learning framework that uses crowdsourcing initially and transitions to a self-improving model.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game with a utility function balancing latency, cost, and accuracy — a strong theoretical contribution.  
- Uses MCTS + TD learning to approximate the optimal policy due to intractability of exact optimization.  
- Experimental evaluation on NER, sentiment, and face-ID shows cost reduction vs full annotation and promising performance.  
- Key empirical finding: the model requires fewer crowd labels over time as confidence increases; outperforms online learning baselines.  
- Open-source implementation increases reproducibility and practical value.  
- Overall novelty and potential impact justify acceptance, subject to fixes.  
- Major clarity issues, especially the introduction of continuous time, which adds complexity without clear experimental benefit.  
- Recommendation to present the foundational discrete-time method first and treat continuous time as a secondary extension.  
- Reporting errors: incorrect claim of "beating human performance" on NER (5-vote) and missing 5-vote results for Face-ID and Sentiment.  
- Missing threshold baseline in sentiment experiments (Table 3 and Figure 4).  
- Notational problems: equation (2) should include s in the distribution; equation (5) uses q ambiguously for a distribution.  
- Likely typo in the threshold baseline uncertainty expression that needs correction.  
- Need clearer explanation of practical use of t_deadline and the criteria for reporting answers (entropic threshold vs. time-based).  
- Acceptance recommended conditional on addressing clarity, presentation, reporting omissions, and notational fixes.
