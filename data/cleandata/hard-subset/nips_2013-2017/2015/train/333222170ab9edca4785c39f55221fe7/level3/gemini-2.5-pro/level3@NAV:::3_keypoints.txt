- Introduces "on-the-job learning": combine real-time crowdsourcing with online model training, formalized as a stochastic game with Bayesian decision-theoretic policy and continuous-time query/response modeling, approximated via MCTS.  
- Novel and significant formulation addressing rapid ML deployment with a human "crutch," presented in a principled way likely to inspire hybrid human-AI work.  
- Core ideas are obscured by complex presentation; continuous-time introduction in Section 3 and its role in MCTS make the method hard to follow.  
- Experimental results do not convincingly justify model complexity: LENSE yields limited gains over a simpler threshold baseline and fails to beat a 5-vote majority baseline on NER.  
- Paper contains reporting and presentation errors: unsubstantiated "beating human performance" claim, missing baseline results in tables/figures, formatting inconsistencies, and notational mistakes (e.g., Eq. 2 and threshold description).  
- Recommendation: accept but require major revision—restructure to present the discrete-time model first, introduce continuous time as an extension, and correct experimental reporting, notation, and formatting issues.
