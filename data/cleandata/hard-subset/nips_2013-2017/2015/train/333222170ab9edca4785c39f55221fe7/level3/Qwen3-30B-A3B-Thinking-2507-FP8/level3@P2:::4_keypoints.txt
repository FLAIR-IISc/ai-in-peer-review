- Presents "on-the-job learning": a stochastic game between system and crowd using Bayesian decision theory, approximated by MCTS (LENSE), showing large cost reductions and F1 gains.  
- Strength: Principled, novel framing for structured prediction with a clear utility function and MCTS-based policy approximation.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results on NER (88.4% F1, >10× cost reduction) and an open-source implementation (LENSE) for reproducibility.  
- Strength: Useful qualitative analysis of system behavior and adaptation to crowd responses.  
- Weakness: Limited scope — unclear how the framework handles unstructured tasks like image classification or partial queries on images.  
- Weakness: Weak baseline comparisons — threshold baseline parameters are arbitrary and there's no uncertainty-sampling baseline for isolation of contributions.  
- Weakness: Blurred distinction from active learning — the claimed novelty over active/online learning is not well justified.  
- Weakness: Incomplete analysis — weaker face recognition results and the claim of model saturation with UNIGRAMS lack explanation or evidence.  
- Pro for acceptance: Novel stochastic-game formulation, solid NER results, principled Bayesian approach, open-source code.  
- Con for acceptance: Ambiguous face-recognition implementation, missing key baselines, arbitrary parameter choices, and overreaching applicability claims.  
- Overall: Interesting and technically sound for structured prediction but overreaches in generality and lacks crucial baselines and analyses.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; revise by clarifying scope, adding uncertainty-sampling baselines and sensitivity analyses, and explaining image/face-recognition experiments.
