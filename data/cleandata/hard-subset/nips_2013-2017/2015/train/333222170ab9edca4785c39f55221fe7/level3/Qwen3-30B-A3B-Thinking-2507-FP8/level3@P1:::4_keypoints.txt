- Introduces "on-the-job learning": systems start with zero training data and shift from crowd queries to autonomous prediction over time.  
- Models the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- System queries the crowd on uncertain input parts, updates its model with responses, and eventually stops querying.  
- Implements LENSE with Monte Carlo Tree Search and progressive widening to approximate the decision process.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing strong empirical gains and large cost reductions (especially NER).  
- Provides a clear problem formulation and a principled, theoretically grounded query-selection method versus simple heuristics.  
- NER results are particularly compelling (88.4% F1 at >10x lower cost than 5-vote human annotation).  
- Ambiguity in the image/face-recognition setup (whole image vs. parts queried) undermines the claim of broad applicability.  
- Framework appears best suited to structured prediction with label correlations; unclear performance in non-structured settings.  
- Technical details of how the crowd response model integrates with the CRF are opaque and need clarification.  
- Threshold baseline uses arbitrary parameters (0.3 and 0.98) without justification or sensitivity analysis, weakening that comparison.  
- Distinction from active online learning is weak; authors don’t convincingly show why active adaptations wouldn’t work.  
- Missing a standard uncertainty-sampling baseline from active learning, leaving comparisons incomplete.  
- Recommendation: weak reject — interesting core idea and solid NER results, but limited explanations, questionable baselines, and incomplete comparisons reduce the paper’s impact.
