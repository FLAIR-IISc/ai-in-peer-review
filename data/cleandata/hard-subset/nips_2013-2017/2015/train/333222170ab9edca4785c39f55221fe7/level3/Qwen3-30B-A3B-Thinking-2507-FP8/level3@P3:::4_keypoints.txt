- Proposes "on-the-job learning": a Bayesian decision-theoretic framework modeling a stochastic game between system and crowd to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy; approximated via MCTS and tested on NER, sentiment, and image classification with reported cost reductions.

- Clear, principled formulation addressing deployment without pre-existing labeled data.

- Thorough experiments across multiple domains showing significant cost reduction (notably >10x for NER).

- Open-source implementation (LENS) and reproducibility via CodaLab.

- Unclear description of the image/face recognition setup—how crowd queries were posed (whole image vs. parts) is unspecified.

- Method may not generalize well to non-structured data since the utility relies on label propagation across structured outputs.

- Threshold baseline uses arbitrary parameters (0.3 and 0.98) without justification or sensitivity analysis, weakening the baseline comparison.

- Missing a standard uncertainty-sampling (active learning) baseline for direct comparison.

- Weakly argued distinction from active learning; unclear why active learning adaptations couldn’t address the same setting.

- Limited experimental scope and reliance on dataset specifics (e.g., NER’s dominant "none" class) raise concerns about generalizability.

- Overall: novel and well-motivated but undermined by experimental and clarity issues.

- Recommendation: Reject; authors should clarify implementation details, justify baselines/parameters, include stronger baselines, and demonstrate broader applicability before resubmission.
