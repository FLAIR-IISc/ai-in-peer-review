- Introduces a novel, theoretically grounded framework for on-the-job learning enabling high-accuracy deployment from scratch with little labeled data.  
- Models on-the-job learning as a stochastic game and uses Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search to approximate the optimal policy since exact computation is intractable.  
- Empirically evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing cost reductions over crowd-only and accuracy gains over pure ML.  
- Main contribution is a more principled formulation of an "on-the-job learner" compared to prior work.  
- Presentation is generally good, but some important technical details are missing.  
- Empirical results are underwhelming, especially compared to a threshold-based heuristic baseline.  
- Authors must justify choice of threshold values (0.3 and 0.88) used in the baseline.  
- Unclear whether the online ML systems used are the best baselines and how much improvement stems from human annotation vs model.  
- Request for error analysis and example cases to support empirical claims.  
- Need clarification of technical elements: definition of N(s) in Algorithm 1 and why y disappears from p_R(r_i | y, q_i) in Eq. 2.  
- Ask for more analysis on model behavior and the impact of different parameters on results.  
- Overall promising approach but requires improved technical detail and stronger empirical support to be convincing.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
