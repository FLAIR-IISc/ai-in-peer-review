- Presents a novel "on-the-job learning" framework to deploy high-accuracy systems without pre-existing labeled data.  
- Formulates the problem as a stochastic game and uses Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Approximates the intractable optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS).  
- Evaluates LENSE on NER, sentiment classification, and image classification tasks.  
- Empirical results show significant cost reductions vs human-only approaches and accuracy improvements over ML baselines, especially for NER.  
- Strength: Innovative unification of online learning, active learning, and structured prediction targeting immediate high accuracy.  
- Strength: Technically sound formulation (stochastic game) and principled use of Bayesian decision theory.  
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence and open-source implementation for reproducibility.  
- Strength: Clear and well-supported application to structured prediction tasks like NER.  
- Weakness: Limited applicability to non-structured tasks where information doesn’t propagate between examples.  
- Weakness: Baseline issues—threshold baseline uses arbitrary parameters without sensitivity analysis; omitted uncertainty sampling baseline.  
- Weakness: Some framework details and distinction from active/online learning are under-explained.  
- Weakness: Practical crowdsourcing challenges (worker quality, response variability) are not fully addressed.  
- For acceptance: Novel problem framing, sound technical contributions (stochastic game + MCTS), and strong empirical gains justify publication.  
- Against acceptance: Limited generalizability, insufficient baseline/sensitivity analysis, and inadequate discussion of related work and distinctions.  
- Conclusion: Promising and technically solid work that needs clearer explanations and additional baselines; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
