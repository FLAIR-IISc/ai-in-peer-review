- Addresses deploying high-accuracy prediction with zero initial training data via "on-the-job learning" that starts with crowdsourcing and transitions to a model.  
- Formulates the decision of when/what to query as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Provides a theoretical grounding that extends prior heuristic systems (e.g., Lasecki et al.).  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) as an approximation because computing the optimal policy is intractable.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment analysis, and image classification tasks.  
- Strength: novel, principled formulation that elegantly frames the dynamic trade-off and addresses a relevant deployment problem.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear motivation.  
- Weakness: missing technical details hurt clarity and reproducibility (e.g., N(σ) in Algorithm 1 undefined; expected accuracy conditioning in Eq. 2 unclear; response model pR drops conditioning on full output without justification).  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is underwhelming—MCTS yields only modest gains over threshold-based heuristics.  
- Weakness: baseline choices lack justification (threshold values unexplained) and it's unclear if the online learning baseline is strong, so gains may stem from added human labels rather than the decision framework.  
- Weakness: lacks error analysis and concrete examples to illustrate policy behavior.  
- Overall: promising and valuable theoretical contribution but limited impact due to missing details and shallow experiments; needs stronger empirical substantiation before top-conference acceptance.
