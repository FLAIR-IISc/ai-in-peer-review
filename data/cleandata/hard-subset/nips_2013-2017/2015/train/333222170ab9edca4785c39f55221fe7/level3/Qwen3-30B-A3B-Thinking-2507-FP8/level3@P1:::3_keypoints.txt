- Proposes "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy systems with zero initial training data by balancing crowdsourcing and model learning.  
- Models system–crowd interaction as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory, querying crowd on uncertain input segments while updating the model.  
- Defines a utility function trading off accuracy, latency, and cost; approximates optimal policy via MCTS with progressive widening to handle continuous response times.  
- Empirically evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification; reports >10× cost reduction (NER), +8% F1 over single-human, and +28% F1 over online learning.  
- Claims to extend Legion:AR with a rigorous ML foundation and differs from static active or pure online learning.  
- Strength: original framing of deployment as a dynamic decision problem addressing scarce-label, time-sensitive real-world needs.  
- Strength: elegant integration of structured prediction (CRF), crowd response modeling, and time-sensitive utility optimization beyond prior static approaches.  
- Strength: compelling empirical gains and open-source reproducibility (LENSE, CodaLab).  
- Weakness: continuous-time extension and game-state notation are introduced abruptly and are hard to follow; MCTS/progressive widening justification is unclear.  
- Suggested fix: present discrete-time core method first, then introduce continuous time as an extension to improve clarity.  
- Weakness: experimental claims inconsistent—paper asserts beating humans while table shows 5-vote human > LENSE; threshold baseline outperforms LENSE on Sentiment but is downplayed.  
- Suggested fix: correct claims and explicitly compare LENSE vs. threshold baseline including trade-offs (latency vs. simplicity).  
- Weakness: notational and reporting errors (time-dependent terms missing in equations, overloaded symbol q, missing baseline and Face-ID results, unclear latency/deadline reporting).  
- Weakness: unclear practical benefit of continuous-time complexity given threshold baseline’s comparable or better latency/accuracy in places.  
- Recommendation: major revisions required—restructure Section 3, correct experimental claims and missing results, fix notation, and clarify necessity of continuous time; otherwise weakly acceptable.
