- Introduces an “on-the-job learning” framework that starts without training data and queries a crowd to learn while operating.  
- Formulates the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off cost, latency, and accuracy.  
- Approximates the intractable optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) using progressive widening.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing large cost reductions versus full human annotation.  
- Shows performance improvements over online learning and a single-human labeling baseline.  
- Distinguishes itself from traditional active learning by focusing on continuous learning during test time.  
- Builds on prior work (Legion:AR) but provides deeper theoretical foundations and a more sophisticated MCTS-based solution.  
- Related-work comparison is generally thorough, covering crowdsourcing, structured prediction, and cost-sensitive learning.  
- Strength: principled approach with practical impact, notably strong NER results demonstrating cost savings.  
- Strength: open-source implementation (LENSE) enhances reproducibility and follow-up research.  
- Strength: explicit modeling of crowd response times and reasoning about latency is a valuable contribution.  
- Weakness: missing technical details impede full understanding (unclear conditioning in Equation 2).  
- Weakness: definition of N(σ) in Algorithm 1 needs clarification.  
- Weakness: threshold baseline uses unexplained parameter values (0.3 and 0.88) and lacks justification.  
- Weakness: comparison to the threshold baseline is underwhelming and should better highlight MCTS advantages.  
- Weakness: online learning baseline is not fully characterized, leaving uncertainty if it represents the best possible baseline.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper error analysis and illustrative examples showing where LENSE outperforms baselines.  
- Weakness: insufficient investigation of worker quality and potential annotation biases; worker-accuracy discussion feels tacked on.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address technical clarifications, justify baselines, and strengthen experimental analysis.
