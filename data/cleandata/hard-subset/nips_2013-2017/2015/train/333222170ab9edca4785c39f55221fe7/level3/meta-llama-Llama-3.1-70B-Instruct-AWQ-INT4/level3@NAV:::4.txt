The paper "On-the-Job Learning with Bayesian Decision Theory" presents a novel framework for on-the-job learning that leverages Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy objectives in a principled way. The authors propose a stochastic game model to formalize the on-the-job learning setting, where the system and the crowd interact to produce high-quality results from the start without requiring a trained model.

The main ideas of the paper are well-motivated and clearly explained. The authors provide a thorough review of related work in online learning, active learning, active classification, crowdsourcing, and structured prediction. The technical core of the approach is sound, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the game tree, utility function, and environment model.

The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on three tasks: named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification. The results show that the proposed approach outperforms machine and human-only comparisons on both quality and cost.

However, there are some limitations and unclear aspects of the paper. For example, the application to face recognition is unclear, and it is not clear whether the entire instance is queried or something else. Additionally, the threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters, and their origin and sensitivity are not reported. Furthermore, the distinction from active online learning is weak, and it is not clear how the proposed approach differs from active learning.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the technical details. However, some of the sections, such as the problem formulation and model sections, are dense and may be difficult to follow for non-experts.

In terms of significance, the results are important, and the approach has the potential to be used in a variety of applications, including natural language processing, computer vision, and human-computer interaction.
