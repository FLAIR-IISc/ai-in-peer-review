- Proposes "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy systems from zero examples by querying crowds during inference using Bayesian decision theory.  
- Models the problem as a stochastic game between system and noisy crowd responses, balancing accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Develops an approximation algorithm using Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to handle continuous-time aspects.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification against baselines (majority vote with 1/3/5 votes, online learning, threshold baseline).  
- Technically sound with well-motivated theoretical foundations.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across three diverse tasks with appropriate baselines.  
- Carefully considers tradeoffs between accuracy, latency, and cost and reports significant cost reductions (notably on NER).  
- Continuous-time extension appears to provide limited practical benefit compared to the simpler threshold baseline.  
- Claim of "boosting performance relative to expert labels" is misleading: 5-vote majority (expert labels) outperforms their method on NER.  
- Continuous-time model description is complex, poorly motivated, and hard to understand/adopt.  
- Significant clarity issues: abrupt continuous-time introduction, inconsistent notation, and unclear implementation details for the threshold baseline.  
- Missing experimental details: exact cost values not reported despite claiming order-of-magnitude reductions; threshold baseline omitted from some plots/tables for sentiment.  
- Originality: novel on-the-job learning concept and stochastic game formulation, extending prior work (e.g., Legion:AR).  
- Significance: addresses an important real-world problem and could help rapid deployment without large labeled datasets, though marginal gains vs. simpler baselines reduce impact.  
- Recommendation: major revisions — clarify core method, remove or better justify continuous-time extension, correct misleading performance claims.  
- Positive practical contributions: open-source implementation (LENSE) and potential practitioner value.  
- Negative practical concerns: overstates improvement over simpler baselines and lacks clarity/complete experimental reporting.
