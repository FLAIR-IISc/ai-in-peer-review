- Proposes "on-the-job learning": test-time interactive framework that queries a real-time crowd when uncertain and updates a model to reduce crowd dependence.  
- Casts decision process as a stochastic game optimized with MCTS and progressive widening under a Bayesian utility trading accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Uses CRF for structured outputs (NER) and standard classifiers for unstructured tasks (sentiment, faces); environment uses fixed crowd noise and empirical latency models.  
- Evaluated LENSE against human n-vote, an online learning baseline, and a handcrafted threshold baseline on NER, sentiment, and face classification.  
- Sits at intersection of online learning, active learning, crowdsourcing, and structured prediction; closest to decision-theoretic active classification and crowd-powered POMDPs but continuously updates online.  

- Strength: Clear and timely problem for rapid deployment with zero pretraining via real-time crowd interaction.  
- Strength: Principled unified utility over accuracy/latency/cost and coherent stochastic game formulation.  
- Strength: Practical approximation algorithm (MCTS + progressive widening) suitable for continuous-time stochastic actions.  
- Strength: Real-time implementation with retainer pool and released code/data improves reproducibility.  
- Strength: Strong empirical gains on NER with large cost reductions and improvements over baselines.  

- Weakness: Utility is myopic — does not account for value of information for future instances, possibly underinvesting early in learning.  
- Weakness: Weak distinction from active online learning; myopic EVI or uncertainty-sampling baselines are not included.  
- Weakness: Threshold baseline uses arbitrary parameters (0.3, 0.98) with no origin or sensitivity analysis, undermining comparisons.  
- Weakness: Missing uncertainty-sampling/entropy and structured EVI/lookahead-1 baselines that would be important comparators.  
- Weakness: Crowd model is crude (fixed pR=0.7) and mismatched with recorded responses, ignoring worker heterogeneity and potential bias.  
- Weakness: Unclear query granularity and integration for face recognition (whole-label vs attributes/regions) and for sentiment.  
- Weakness: Decision loss uses MAP accuracy which may be misaligned for skewed structured metrics (e.g., F1 in NER); should consider expected F1 or token-level utility.  
- Weakness: No sensitivity analyses for monetary/time weights (wM, wT), calibration of predictive probabilities, or MCTS hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: Ambiguity about CRF updates — whether noisy responses are used directly, latency handling during updates, and update frequency (per response vs per instance).  

- Quality: Technically sound with reasonable approximations and a working system, but evaluation limited by missing strong baselines and sensitivity analyses.  
- Clarity: Main idea is clear but important details are ambiguous (query space for faces/sentiment, response integration, threshold rationale, MCTS hyperparams, termination and rollout policies).  
- Originality: Novel combination of real-time crowd querying, online updating, and decision-theoretic MCTS solution, though relation to active online learning could be clearer.  
- Significance: Promising for rapid deployment with convincing NER gains, but limited applicability to non-structured tasks and ignores cross-instance VOI.  

- Requested experiment: Add uncertainty-sampling and myopic EVI baselines (sequential and parallel querying) with online updates after each response.  
- Requested experiment: Report sensitivity to threshold-baseline parameters (0.3, 0.98), wM, wT, and the crowd accuracy assumption pR=0.7.  
- Requested experiment: Evaluate worker-modeling variants (e.g., Dawid–Skene, per-worker reliabilities) and their effect on policy/outcomes.  
- Requested experiment: Specify face query granularity and compare attribute-level querying to whole-label querying.  
- Requested experiment: For structured outputs, compare sequence-level vs token-level utilities and expected F1 decision rules.  
- Requested experiment: Provide ablations on MCTS hyperparameters, time budgets, and compute latency to validate on-the-job feasibility.  

- Pros for acceptance: Novel, principled, practical framework with real-time implementation and significant NER improvements.  
- Cons against acceptance: Missing key baselines and sensitivity analyses, ad hoc threshold baseline, limited gains on non-structured tasks, and weak empirical distinction from active online learning.
