- Proposes a novel on-the-job learning framework that starts from zero labeled examples by using real-time crowdsourcing and outputs predictions when confident.  
- Models the problem as a stochastic game based on Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- System improves over time, reducing dependence on crowdsourced queries.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; figures and tables effectively illustrate concepts and results.  
- Provides a thorough empirical evaluation on three datasets showing reduced cost and improved performance over baselines.  
- Claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results; the work is technically sound.  
- Original contribution: applying Bayesian decision theory and stochastic-game formulation to on-the-job learning.  
- Significant potential impact and wide applicability for deploying high-accuracy systems under cost/latency constraints.  
- Weakness: insufficient implementation details and lack of specifics on algorithms/approximations for the optimal policy.  
- Weakness: related work discussion is limited and lacks direct comparisons to other on-the-job learning methods.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept.
