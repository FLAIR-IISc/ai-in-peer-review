- Introduces "on-the-job learning": building high-accuracy classifiers from zero labeled examples by querying crowds during deployment.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search to approximate the intractable optimal policy.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification tasks.  
- Shows large cost reductions (over an order of magnitude for NER) and accuracy gains versus baselines.  
- Reports concrete gains: LENSE achieves +8% F1 over single-human labeling and +28% over online learning on NER.  
- Compelling and meaningful problem with novel timing/latency optimization contributions.  
- Strong technical quality and well-motivated experiments.  
- Clarity issues: abstract/example at Line 154 is too abstract and needs a concrete, realistic illustration (including timings).  
- Inconsistent terminology: Tables 2 and 3 label the threshold baseline as "entropic" vs "threshold," causing confusion.  
- Related work section (Section 5) is poorly organized and fails to clearly distinguish from prior work like Legion:AR.  
- Missing figure reference at Line 086 and insufficient explanation of tradeoffs between LENSE and multi-vote approaches (Table 2).  
- Overall: valuable contribution but requires substantial revision to fix presentation, examples, labeling inconsistencies, and related-work placement before acceptance.
