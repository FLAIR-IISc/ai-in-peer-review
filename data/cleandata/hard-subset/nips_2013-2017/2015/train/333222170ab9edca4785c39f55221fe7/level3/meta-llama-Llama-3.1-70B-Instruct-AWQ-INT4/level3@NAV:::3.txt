This paper proposes a novel framework for on-the-job learning, which combines crowdsourcing and machine learning to achieve high accuracy from the start without requiring a trained model. The authors model the on-the-job setting as a stochastic game and use ideas from game playing to approximate the optimal policy. The system, called LENSE, is evaluated on three tasks: named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification.

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and the proposed solution. The use of Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy is a significant contribution. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing that it can achieve high accuracy at a lower cost than traditional machine learning approaches.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, although some sections could be improved for clarity. The introduction provides a clear motivation for the work, and the problem formulation is well-defined. The model and algorithm sections are detailed and provide a good understanding of the proposed approach. The experiments section is thorough, and the results are well-presented.

The paper is original and significant, as it proposes a new framework for on-the-job learning that combines crowdsourcing and machine learning. The use of Bayesian decision theory and stochastic games is a novel contribution, and the experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear summary of the contributions and limitations of the work. The related work section is thorough, and the authors provide a good overview of the existing literature on online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing.

However, there are some minor issues with the paper. The notation could be improved, and some of the equations are not well-formatted. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm.

Overall, I recommend accepting this paper, as it makes a significant contribution to the field of machine learning and provides a novel framework for on-the-job learning.
