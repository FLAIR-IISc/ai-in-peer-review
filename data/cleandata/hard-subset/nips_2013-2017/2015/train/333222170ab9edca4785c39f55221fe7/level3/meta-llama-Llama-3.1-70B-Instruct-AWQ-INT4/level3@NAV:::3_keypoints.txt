- Proposes a novel on-the-job learning framework combining crowdsourcing and machine learning to achieve high initial accuracy without a trained model.  
- Models the on-the-job setting as a stochastic game and leverages game-playing ideas to approximate the optimal policy.  
- Introduces the system LENSE and evaluates it on named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification.  
- Technically sound with a thorough analysis of the problem and proposed solution.  
- Uses Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy—highlighted as a significant contribution.  
- Experiments show LENSE can achieve high accuracy at lower cost than traditional ML approaches.  
- Paper is well-written, with a clear introduction, well-defined problem formulation, and detailed model and algorithm sections.  
- Original and significant contribution by combining Bayesian decision theory and stochastic games for on-the-job learning.  
- Well-organized with a thorough related work section covering online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing.  
- Minor issues: notation could be improved and some equations are poorly formatted.  
- Minor issues: lacks detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm.  
- Minor issues: some sections could be clarified further.
