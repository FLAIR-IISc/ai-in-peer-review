- Proposes a hybrid system combining crowdsourced labels with an online-trained model, framed as a stochastic game using a CRF and continuous-time queries/responses.  
- Core idea: rely on crowd early, learn from labels, and reduce crowd dependence as model confidence increases.  
- Incorporates continuous time (query issue time s, response time t) and optimizes expected utility before a deadline t_deadline.  
- Uses a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s) and approximates intractable optimization with Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD-learning (Algorithm 1).  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face-ID tasks against baselines including 1/3/5-judge majority votes, online learning with true labels, and a "threshold" baseline.  
- LENSE outperforms most baselines on precision/recall/F1 but is beaten by the 5-judge majority vote on NER.  
- The simpler "threshold" baseline achieves nearly identical performance to LENSE and lower latency on NER, and slightly better on face-ID.  
- Model dependence on crowd labels decreases over time as the underlying model improves (Figure 3).  
- Unlike standard online learning, the system achieves high accuracy from the first example by leveraging the crowd when uncertainty is high (Figure 4).  
- Reviewer views the work as original, significant, and recommendable for acceptance.  
- Major weakness: paper clarity, especially the complex continuous-time formulation that yields little experimental benefit over the threshold baseline.  
- Suggest restructuring: present the threshold model as the core method and introduce continuous time as an extension to improve readability and adoption.  
- Missing or inconsistent baselines: claim of "beats human performance" unsupported; 5-judge baseline absent for Face-ID and Sentiment in several places; Threshold baseline missing from sentiment experiments.  
- Presentation issues: Table 2 should bold best results; experimental t_deadline/stopping criterion is unclear.  
- Minor methodological issues: Equation (2) likely omits conditioning on s; F(q) in Equation (5) uses confusing notation reusing q; Threshold baseline inequality in description appears incorrect and should be revised.
