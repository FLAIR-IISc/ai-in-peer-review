- Proposes a hybrid approach that uses crowdsourced labels early and an incrementally trained model to reduce crowd reliance over time.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game between the system and the crowd using a CRF prediction model.  
- System issues token queries and maximizes expected utility accounting for query/response timing and a deadline.  
- Expected utility is computed with a simulation dynamics model approximated via Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face identification tasks.  
- Method outperforms all baselines except a 5-vote crowdsourced version; the "threshold" baseline performs nearly as well.  
- "Threshold" baseline achieves lower latency for NER and remains strong even with sequence-dependent tasks.  
- Authors demonstrate the model needs fewer crowdsourced labels over time given a sufficiently strong model.  
- Major clarity concern: introduction of continuous time complicates the paper without clear benefits to results.  
- Recommendation: present the core method without continuous time and treat continuous time as an extension.  
- Reporting issues: omitted 5-vote case for Face-ID and Sentiment, Table 2 lacks bolding for best results, and Threshold baseline missing for sentiment experiment.  
- Method-description issues: unclear use of t_deadline, unspecified distribution in Eq. (2), confusing use of q in Eq. (5), and an incorrect expression describing the Threshold baseline.  
- Overall assessment: highly original, significant contribution with impressive results and strong acceptance candidate despite clarity/reporting issues.
