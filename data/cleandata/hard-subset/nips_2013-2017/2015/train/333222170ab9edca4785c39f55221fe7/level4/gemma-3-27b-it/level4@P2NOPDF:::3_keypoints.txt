1. Introduces a hybrid approach that combines crowdsourced labels with an incrementally trained model, framed as a stochastic game based on a CRF prediction model.  
2. System and crowd are modeled as players issuing queries q for tokens x (true label y) to elicit responses r, optimizing expected utility U(q,r) under a deadline t_deadline.  
3. Expected utility computation requires a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s) (Eq. 4); continuous-time formulation makes optimal action selection intractable.  
4. Authors approximate action selection using Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning (Algorithm 1).  
5. Evaluated on simplified NER, sentiment, and face ID tasks against baselines: majority vote (1/3/5 votes), online learning, a “threshold” baseline, and full LENSE model.  
6. Results show the full model outperforms all methods except the 5-vote crowdsourced approach; the 5-vote case yields best performance.  
7. The Threshold baseline performs nearly as well as LENSE and has notably lower latency on NER, even outperforming LENSE on face ID.  
8. Model reliance on crowdsourced labels decreases over time with training data, and it attains high initial accuracy by leveraging crowd expertise during high-uncertainty periods.  
9. Paper is original, significant, and potentially impactful—first principled proposal for simultaneous crowd learning and system training, deserving acceptance.  
10. Main clarity concern: continuous-time extension adds substantial complexity but provides limited experimental benefit; the threshold variant might suffice for many applications.  
11. Recommendation to refocus the paper on the core method without continuous time and present continuous time as a natural extension to improve clarity and accessibility.  
12. Results presentation issues: incorrect claim of “beats human performance” (5-vote beats model in NER), and 5-vote results are omitted from several tables/figures and should be included and bolded where best.  
13. The Threshold baseline is missing from the sentiment experiment and must be added to Table 3 and Figure 4.  
14. Minor methodological/notation issues: clarify use of t_deadline and response readiness criteria; Eq. (2) should include s; rename q in Eq. (5) to avoid confusion.  
15. Correct likely typo in Threshold baseline inequality (inequality direction and interpretation of m reducing uncertainty).
