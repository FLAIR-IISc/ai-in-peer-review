- Primary contribution: provides a more rigorous theoretical foundation for the on-the-job learner introduced in [22].  
- Method summary: learner starts with crowdsourced outputs and progressively builds its own predictor, reducing crowd queries; query decisions solved via Monte Carlo Tree Search.  
- Presentation: generally clear but missing crucial details in places.  
- Empirical results: not particularly compelling when compared to a threshold-based heuristic.  
- Overall assessment: approach appears reasonable and warrants further exploration.  
- Clarification needed (Eq. 2): left-hand side should be conditioned on s, and the role/disappearance of y in p_R(r_i | y, q_i) is unclear.  
- Clarification needed (Alg. 1): definition of N(s) is unspecified.  
- Clarification needed (thresholds): how the baseline threshold values 0.3 and 0.88 were determined.  
- Clarification needed (baselines/tasks): are the “online” baseline systems state-of-the-art for the tasks; if so, reported gains may be from human annotation—examples and error analysis are requested.  
- Final note: theoretical framework is promising despite mixed empirical evidence; more analysis (e.g., error analysis, baseline justification) is needed.
