The main contribution of this paper is a more theoretically grounded formulation of the on-the-job learner proposed in [22]. The system initially queries the crowd to produce outputs, then progressively makes its own predictions and queries the crowd only infrequently. The decision of whether to query the crowd for a given input is modeled as a decision problem and solved using Monte Carlo Tree Search.

The presentation is generally clear but omits several important details. The empirical results, compared to a threshold-based heuristic, are somewhat underwhelming. Nevertheless, the approach is reasonable and merits further investigation.

Please address/clarify the following:
1. Equation (2) is unclear. The left-hand side should also be conditioned on s. Also, how does y in p{R}(ri | y, q_i) disappear?
2. What is N(s) in Algorithm 1?
3. How were the 0.3 and 0.88 values in the threshold baseline obtained?
4. I am not familiar with the exact tasks used here. Are the “online” systems the best machine-learned systems for this task? If not, why not? If so, then any additional gains must be purely due to human annotation. What are some such cases? Some error analysis would be helpful.

In sum, the paper presents a theoretical formulation of an on-the-job learner that begins as pure crowdsourcing and gradually transitions to a learned model that only queries the crowd on low-confidence inputs. The empirical results are somewhat mixed, but this is a promising direction that deserves further discussion.
