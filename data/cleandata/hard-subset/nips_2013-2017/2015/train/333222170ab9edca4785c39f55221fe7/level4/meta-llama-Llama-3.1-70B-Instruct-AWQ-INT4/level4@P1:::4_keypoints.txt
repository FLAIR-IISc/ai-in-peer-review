- Introduces a novel "on the job learning" setting where annotator queries occur between receiving an example and producing a prediction, enabling trade-offs between latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Formalizes the setting as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to principledly trade off latency, cost, and accuracy objectives.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search to approximate the optimal policy because exact computation is intractable.  
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment, and image classification datasets and compares to human-only, online learning, and threshold-based baselines.  
- Reports that the proposed approach outperforms baselines on both quality and cost.  
- Strength: method can balance latency, cost, and accuracy and adapt to changing circumstances for real-time decisions.  
- Weakness: unclear how the method was applied to the face recognition task (e.g., how instances were queried).  
- Weakness: threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters with no explanation of their origin or sensitivity.  
- Suggestion: include an uncertainty-sampling (active learning style) baseline for a more established comparison.  
- Critique: the distinction from active online learning is not fully convincing since active learning could be adapted to this domain and has theory for query selection.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written, well-organized, and technically sound but omits some methodological details.  
- Significance: novel and useful approach with demonstrated effectiveness, but applicability may be limited to certain task types.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, clear method, empirical effectiveness, and practical relevance.  
- Rejection arguments: limited task applicability, unclear baseline parameter choices, and insufficient detail on the face recognition experiment.
