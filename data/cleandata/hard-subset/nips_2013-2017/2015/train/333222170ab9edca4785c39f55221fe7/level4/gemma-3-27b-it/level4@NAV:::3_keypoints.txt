- Proposes a hybrid prediction method that uses crowdsourced labels early and an incrementally trained model later, formalized as a stochastic game with a CRF.

- System issues token queries q, receives responses r with utility U(q,r), and optimizes expected utility under a deadline t_deadline considering response times t.

- Dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s) (Eq. 4) is needed to compute expected utility and guide decisions.

- Exact optimization is intractable; action selection is approximated with Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning (Algorithm 1).

- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face-ID against baselines (1/3/5-vote humans, online learning, Threshold, LENSE); LENSE outperforms all but the 5-vote human baseline.

- The Threshold baseline (no continuous time) achieves comparable performance and lower latency on NER and even outperforms LENSE on face-ID in some cases.

- Empirically shows decreasing reliance on crowds over time and faster early accuracy than online learning by leveraging crowd expertise under high model uncertainty.

- Paper is original, significant, and likely to stimulate research and practical adoption; reviewer recommends acceptance.

- Major clarity concern: introducing continuous time mid-Section 3 greatly increases complexity while providing limited experimental benefit.

- Recommendation: present the core method without continuous time first, then introduce continuous-time as an extension, and more clearly discuss when Threshold suffices.

- Reporting issues: inaccurate claim of “beats human performance” for NER with 5 votes; 5-vote results missing for face-ID and sentiment in tables/figures; some results/latencies should be bolded.

- Missing baselines and fixes: include Threshold in the sentiment experiments and add the 5-vote case across reported results.

- Minor methodological/notation fixes: clarify response readiness under t_deadline; include s in Eq. (2); replace confusing use of q in Eq. (5); correct the Threshold expression (l.246-252).
