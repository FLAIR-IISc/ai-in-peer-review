- Proposes a hybrid system that combines crowdsourced labels and an incrementally trained model, leaning on the crowd early and relying more on the model as it gains confidence.  
- Formulates the problem as a stochastic game using a CRF prediction model where system and crowd are players issuing queries and receiving timed responses.  
- Incorporates continuous time and deadlines (t_deadline) to decide when and how many queries to send, optimizing expected utility U(q,r) under response-time dynamics.  
- Computing expected utility requires a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s) and is intractable due to continuous time, so authors use MCTS + TD learning (Algorithm 1) as an approximation.  
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and face-ID compare majority vote (1/3/5), online learning (oracle labels), a “threshold” baseline (no continuous time), and the full model LENSE.  
- LENSE outperforms all baselines except the 5-vote human majority; the threshold baseline performs nearly as well and often with lower latency (notably on NER).  
- The model reduces reliance on crowd labels over time with a strong online learner and achieves high initial accuracy versus online-only learning by leveraging crowd expertise early.  
- Continuous-time formulation adds substantial complexity but provides minimal empirical benefit over the simpler threshold baseline, raising clarity and practicality concerns.  
- Reviewer suggests presenting the core method without continuous time and treating continuous time as an extension to improve clarity and adoption.  
- Reporting issues: claims of “beats human performance” are inaccurate for 5-vote NER; 5-vote results are missing for Face-ID and Sentiment in several tables/figures and should be included and bolded where best.  
- The threshold baseline is missing from the sentiment experiment’s results (Table 3 and Figure 4) and should be added.  
- Method-description issues: unclear whether t_deadline was used in experiments or how answer-ready criteria were determined (entropy vs. deadline).  
- Technical corrections: equation (2) should condition on s (p(y,r,t|x,q,s)); change confusing use of q in F(q) in Eq. 5 to a different symbol; and fix the Threshold baseline inequality (RHS should be 1-0.88).  
- Overall assessment: highly original and significant work deserving acceptance, but clarity (especially around continuous time) and several presentation/factual fixes are needed.
