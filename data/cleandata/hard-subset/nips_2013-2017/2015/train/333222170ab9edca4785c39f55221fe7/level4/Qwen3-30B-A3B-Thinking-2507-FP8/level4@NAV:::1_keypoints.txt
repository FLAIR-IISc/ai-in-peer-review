- Primary contribution: a theoretically rigorous formulation of an "on-the-job learner" from [22] that queues crowd responses and uses Monte Carlo Tree Search to minimize queries while shifting to autonomous predictions.  
- Presentation: generally clear but omits critical technical details.  
- Empirical results: modest improvements over a threshold-based heuristic.  
- Overall judgment: approach is logically sound and warrants further investigation.  
- Issue 1: Equation (2) unclear — LHS should be conditioned on state s and the role of y in p^{(R)}(r_i | y, q_i) is unaccounted for.  
- Issue 2: Algorithm 1 missing definition of N(s).  
- Issue 3: Need justification for chosen threshold-baseline values (0.3 and 0.88).  
- Issue 4: Clarify whether the referenced "online" systems are state-of-the-art; if not, explain why; if yes, show how gains come from human annotation with illustrative examples and error analysis.  
- Conclusion: formalizes the crowd→autonomy transition, but mixed empirical results require more discussion.
