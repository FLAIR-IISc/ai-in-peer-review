- Proposes an “on-the-job learning” setting allowing queries between input receipt and prediction; clear and well-motivated for structured prediction where specific sequence elements can be queried.  
- Application to the face recognition (unstructured) task is unclear—did the model query entire instances, and how does the proposed utility function help when information isn’t propagated across examples?  
- The model does not propagate information across adjacent positions in the unstructured setting, so the utility function’s applicability and benefit are questionable.  
- The threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters; the paper lacks explanation of their origin and a sensitivity analysis to show robustness.  
- An uncertainty-sampling baseline (common in active learning) is missing and would be an informative comparison.  
- The paper’s distinction from online active learning is incomplete—active learning could be adapted (treating the sequence as the active set), and its theory could inform query selection.  
- Overall, the framework is novel but some methodological details remain unclear and the presented applicability seems fairly restricted.
