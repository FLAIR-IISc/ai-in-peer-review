The main contribution of this paper is to provide a more theoretically sound formulation of the 'on-the-job learner' concept proposed in [22]. The learner starts by lining up in a crowd to give their input, but gradually begins to make predictions and occasionally inquire from the crowd. The choice of whether or not to inquire from the crowd for any given input is made as a decision problem solved through Monte Carlo tree search.

The presentation is mostly good, although it lacks important details. The empirical results are somewhat disappointing compared to the threshold-based heuristic. But it certainly seems like a reasonable approach and deserves further investigation.
