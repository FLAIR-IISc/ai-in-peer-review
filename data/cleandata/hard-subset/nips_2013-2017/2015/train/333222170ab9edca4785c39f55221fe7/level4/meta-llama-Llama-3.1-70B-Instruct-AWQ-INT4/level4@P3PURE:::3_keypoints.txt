- Proposes a hybrid system that combines crowdsourced labels and an online-trained model, gradually shifting from crowd to model as confidence grows.
- Formulates the problem as a stochastic game using a CRF prediction model where system and crowd are players issuing queries and receiving timed responses.
- Incorporates query timing and response deadlines (t_deadline) to decide how many queries to send and at what rate.
- Expected utility computation requires a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s) and is intractable in continuous time.
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning (Algorithm 1) as an approximation for optimizing in continuous time.
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment, and face identification tasks against majority-vote humans, online learning (oracle), a "threshold" baseline, and the full LENSE model.
- LENSE outperforms most baselines in precision/recall/F1 but is beaten by 5-vote crowdsourcing when available.
- The "threshold" baseline often matches LENSE's accuracy while yielding lower latency (notably on NER) and even better performance on face ID.
- Demonstrates that the model needs fewer crowdsourced labels over time and achieves high initial accuracy by leveraging crowd expertise early.
- Primary concern: introducing continuous time complicates the formulation and optimization, yet gives minimal empirical benefit over the simpler threshold approach.
- Suggests restructuring: present the core method without continuous time for clarity and practical adoption, with continuous time as an optional extension.
- Reporting issues: claim of "beats human performance" is inaccurate for 5-vote NER; 5-vote results missing for some tasks and should be included and bolded where best.
- Experimental omissions: Threshold baseline is missing from the sentiment experiments and should be included in tables/figures.
- Method description issues: unclear what t_deadline was used experimentally and what criterion signaled answer readiness.
- Notational/typo issues: equation (2) should condition on s; use a different letter than q in F(q) to avoid confusion; threshold expression appears to have a sign/inequality error.
- Overall judgment: highly original and significant work deserving acceptance but needs clearer exposition and fixes to presentation and experimental reporting.
