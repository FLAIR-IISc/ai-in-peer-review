The authors introduce a scenario called "on-the-job learning," in which a model can query an annotator after receiving an input but before making its final prediction.

While the algorithm's application to structured prediction tasks—where elements of a sequence are queried—is straightforward, its implementation for the face recognition task is unclear. Was the entire instance queried for a label? In a non-structured setting, the model would not propagate information between adjacent examples, which calls into question the validity of the proposed utility function, as it appears to depend on this mechanism.

The thresholding baseline relies on two parameters whose values appear arbitrary. The paper should clarify how these values were chosen and analyze the model's sensitivity to them. Furthermore, the experiments would be strengthened by including an uncertainty sampling baseline, a standard approach in active learning.

Finally, the authors take care to distinguish their setting from active online learning. However, standard active learning could be straightforwardly adapted to this domain by treating the sequence as the active set. For instance, a model could be updated with the responses to its queries before making a final prediction. While such an approach might not fully capture the temporal aspects of the task, it would allow the work to draw upon the extensive theory from active learning for choosing the query set.

In summary, while the proposed framework appears novel, its contribution is diminished by unclear details regarding its application and a seemingly narrow scope of applicability.
