The authors introduce a concept called "on the job learning", which involves querying annotators for information between receiving an example and generating a prediction. 
While the algorithm's functionality is apparent in structured prediction tasks, where sequence elements are queried, its application to the face recognition task is unclear - specifically, whether the instance being queried was the only consideration. 
In non-structured settings, the model does not propagate information between adjacent positions (examples), which raises questions about the utility function's applicability. 
The threshold baseline relies on two parameters with seemingly arbitrary values; the authors should provide the origin of these values and an analysis of their sensitivity. 
A baseline comparison with uncertainty sampling, similar to active learning, would be a valuable addition. 
Although the authors emphasize the distinction between this setting and active online learning, active learning methods could be adapted to this domain by treating the active set as a sequence; for instance, the model could be updated upon receiving responses and then output a prediction. 
While this approach would not capture the task's temporal nature, existing theory on selecting the query set could be leveraged. 
Overall, the framework appears novel, but some aspects require clarification, and its applicability seems limited.
