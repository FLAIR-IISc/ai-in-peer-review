- Proposes a hybrid system combining crowdsourced labels with an incrementally trained model that relies on the crowd early and defers to the model as confidence grows.  
- Frames the task as a stochastic game using a CRF where the system issues queries, receives responses, and maximizes expected utility under a time deadline.  
- Computes expected utility via a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t | x,q,s); continuous-time formulation makes exact solution intractable, so they use MCTS + TD learning (Alg. 1).  
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment, and face-ID tasks comparing majority-vote (1/3/5), online learning, a “threshold” baseline, and the full LENSE model.  
- LENSE outperforms most baselines in F1 but is beaten by the 5-vote crowd; the threshold baseline performs nearly as well and has lower latency on NER.  
- Empirically shows the method uses fewer crowd labels as the model strengthens and attains high accuracy from the first examples by leveraging crowd expertise.  
- Reviewer views the work as strong, original, principled, and likely to spur follow-on work; recommends acceptance.  
- Main clarity concern: continuous-time component is introduced mid-section, adds conceptual/algorithmic cost, and provides limited empirical benefit.  
- Suggests presenting the non-time-based core method first and treating continuous time as an extension to improve clarity and adoption.  
- Notes that the threshold baseline may be sufficient for many practical tasks and already represents a significant practical advance.  
- Reporting issues: claim of “beats human performance” is incorrect for 5-vote NER; 5-vote results are missing for Face-ID and Sentiment in multiple tables/figures and should be included and bolded when best.  
- The threshold baseline is missing from the sentiment experiment (Table 3 and Figure 4) and should be added.  
- Requests methodological clarifications: specify t_deadline usage and reporting criterion, include s in Equation (2)’s distribution, fix confusing F(q) notation, and correct the threshold baseline inequality.  
- Minor presentation fixes: bold best results/latencies in tables and correct notation/expression errors.
