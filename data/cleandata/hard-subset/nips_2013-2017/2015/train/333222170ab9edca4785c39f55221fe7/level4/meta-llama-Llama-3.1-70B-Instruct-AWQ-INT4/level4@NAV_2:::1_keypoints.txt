- Main contribution: a more theoretically sound formulation of an on-the-job learner that transitions from crowd queries to autonomous predictions.  
- Query decision modeled as a decision problem solved with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS).  
- Presentation is mostly good but omits important technical details.  
- Empirical results versus a threshold-based heuristic are somewhat underwhelming.  
- Paper is technically sound with a clear, well-organized structure and thorough model/algorithm description.  
- Notable strength: use of Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Demonstrated ability to balance latency, cost, and accuracy, achieving significant cost reductions while maintaining high accuracy.  
- Improvements needed: expand empirical results and provide more detailed performance analysis.  
- Improvements needed: provide more context about the evaluation tasks and how they compare to prior work.  
- Clarity issues: some technical details are not well-explained and need clarification.
