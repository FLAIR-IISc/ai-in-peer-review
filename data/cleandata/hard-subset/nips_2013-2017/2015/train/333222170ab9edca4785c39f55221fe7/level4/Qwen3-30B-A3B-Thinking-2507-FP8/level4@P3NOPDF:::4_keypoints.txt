- Framework is well-motivated for structured prediction but its extension to face recognition (non‑structured) is unclear.
- In non‑structured settings the lack of propagation between examples makes the proposed utility function meaningless; if queries only concern the instance, the method reduces to single‑annotation prediction.
- Authors must clarify how queries are operationalized for non‑structured tasks and justify the utility function’s applicability there.
- The threshold baseline introduces two unexplained parameters with seemingly arbitrary values and lacks any sensitivity analysis.
- A baseline based on uncertainty sampling (standard active learning) should be included and adapted to the sequential setting for a more meaningful comparison.
- The claimed distinction from active online learning is weak, since active learning can be adapted to treat the sequence as a pool; authors should explain why existing theory is inadequate beyond temporal aspects.
- The paper’s core novelty (mid‑prediction queries) is interesting but key implementation details—especially for face recognition and query interactions with non‑structured data—are missing.
- Lack of parameter justification and missing active‑learning baselines weaken the empirical evaluation and claims of advantage.
- Concrete recommended fixes: clarify face recognition implementation and utility validity, justify and analyze threshold parameters, add uncertainty‑sampling baseline, and rigorously differentiate from active learning.
- Overall: promising idea but currently limited by unclear applicability to non‑structured tasks and insufficient baselines, so scope/novelty appear overstated.
