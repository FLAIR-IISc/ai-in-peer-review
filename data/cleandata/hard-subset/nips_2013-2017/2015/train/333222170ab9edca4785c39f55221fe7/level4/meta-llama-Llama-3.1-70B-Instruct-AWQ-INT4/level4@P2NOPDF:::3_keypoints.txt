- Proposes LENSE: combine crowdsourced labels and an incrementally trained CRF model, relying more on the crowd early and reducing reliance as model confidence grows.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game between system and crowd with queries, responses, and utility values under time constraints.  
- Uses a simulation dynamics model to compute expected utility accounting for true labels, crowd responses, and response times.  
- Introducing continuous time makes optimization intractable; authors approximate with Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning.  
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and face identification compare LENSE to multiple baselines (1/3/5-vote, online learning, and a “threshold” baseline).  
- Results: LENSE outperforms most baselines but is beaten by 5-vote crowdsourcing; the threshold baseline matches LENSE in many cases and has lower latency for NER.  
- Continuous-time modeling complicates presentation and algorithmic mechanics while offering minimal empirical benefit over the simpler threshold approach.  
- Recommendation: present core method without continuous time and add continuous-time as an extension to improve clarity and impact.  
- Reporting and presentation issues: incorrect “beats human performance” claim for 5-vote NER, missing 5-vote results for Face-ID and Sentiment, and incorrect bolding of table entries and latency.  
- Missing/incorrect experimental detail: threshold baseline omitted for sentiment experiments and should be included in Table 3 and Figure 4.  
- Method description fixes needed: clarify t_deadline and answer-ready criteria, denote distribution variable s in Eq.(2), replace confusing q in Eq.(5), and correct the threshold expression to (1 - p_theta(yi|x))*0.3^m <= (1-0.88).
