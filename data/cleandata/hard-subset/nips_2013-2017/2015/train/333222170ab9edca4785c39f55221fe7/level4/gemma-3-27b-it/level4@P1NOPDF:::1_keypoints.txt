- Main contribution: a more theoretically grounded formulation of an on-the-job learner that shifts from crowd-sourced outputs to model predictions using Monte Carlo Tree Search to decide queries.
- Decision model: querying the crowd for an input is treated as a decision problem solved via MCTS.
- Presentation: generally good but missing important details.
- Empirical results: improvements over a threshold-based heuristic are modest/underwhelming.
- Clarification needed (Eq. 2): left-hand side should be conditioned on s and explain why y disappears in p_R(r_i | y, q_i).
- Clarification needed (Algorithm 1): define what N(s) denotes.
- Clarification needed (baseline thresholds): explain how 0.3 and 0.88 were chosen for the threshold baseline.
- Clarification needed (comparative systems): specify whether the “online” comparison systems are state-of-the-art, and provide error analysis/examples to show when gains stem from human annotation versus model improvements.
- Summary: theoretically interesting approach that transitions from crowd to model, mixed empirical evidence, and deserving of further investigation with the requested clarifications.
