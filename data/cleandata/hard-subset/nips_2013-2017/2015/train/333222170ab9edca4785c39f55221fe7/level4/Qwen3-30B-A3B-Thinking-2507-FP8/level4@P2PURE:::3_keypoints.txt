- Proposes a hybrid method that combines crowdsourced labels with online-trained models, relying on the crowd early and reducing reliance as model confidence grows.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game with a CRF, where the system issues queries q for tokens x, receives responses r, and maximizes expected utility U(q,r).  
- Incorporates time by modeling query issue times s, response times t, and a deadline t_deadline that affects query volume and rate.  
- Expected utility computation requires simulating dynamics P(y,r,t | x,q,s) (Eq.4); continuous-time dynamics make exact optimization intractable.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning to approximate optimal action selection (Algorithm 1).  
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment, and face identification, comparing majority-vote (1,3,5), online learning, a threshold baseline (no continuous time), and the full model LENSE.  
- LENSE achieves superior precision/recall/F1 versus most baselines, but is outperformed by 5-vote in some cases; threshold baseline performs nearly equivalently and has lower NER latency.  
- The method reduces crowd label usage over time with a strong training model and maintains high initial accuracy by leveraging crowd expertise when uncertain.  
- Paper is original and significant—the first principled complete proposal for hybrid learning from crowds during model training, meriting acceptance.  
- Major clarity concern: introducing continuous time mid-Section 3 complicates presentation and yields minimal empirical gains; recommend presenting discrete-time/core method first and continuous time as an optional extension.  
- Need to clarify that the threshold baseline remains strong even for label-interaction tasks (e.g., NER), not only single-label tasks.  
- Incorrect/incomplete reporting: “beats human performance” claim is inaccurate for NER (5-vote), and 5-vote results are missing for Face ID/Sentiment in Tables 2,3 and Figure 4—these should be included and highlighted.  
- The threshold baseline is missing from sentiment results (Table 3, Figure 4) and must be added.  
- Section 3.1 does not state whether t_deadline was used in experiments or how answer readiness was determined (entropy threshold vs deadline)—this must be clarified.  
- Equation (2) should include s as a parameter: p(y,r,t | x,q,s).  
- Equation (5) uses confusing notation F(q) for a distribution; use a different symbol (e.g., d) to avoid clash with query q.  
- The threshold baseline description likely has an inequality error: increasing m should reduce uncertainty, so the formula should use ≤ rather than ≥.  
- Overall: elegant, compelling approach with strong results, but needs clarity fixes and more complete reporting, and the continuous-time complexity should be justified or demoted to an extension.
