- Proposes a theoretically sound "on the job learner" building on [22].
- Learner starts by using crowd outputs and gradually makes its own predictions, querying the crowd only when necessary.
- Query decision is posed as a decision problem and solved with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS).
- Presentation is generally clear but omits important details in some areas.
- Empirical results, compared to a threshold-based heuristic, are underwhelming.
- Approach is reasonable and warrants further investigation.
- Equation (2) needs clarification: LHS should be conditioned on s and how y is eliminated from p(R|y,q_i) must be explained.
- Algorithm 1 uses N(s) without definition; authors should define this term.
- Threshold baseline values (0.3 and 0.88) seem arbitrary and require justification and explanation of how they were chosen.
- Unclear whether the "online" systems are the best ML baselines; authors should justify their choice or provide examples and error analysis showing improvements due to human annotation.
- Addressing these issues will strengthen the paper and clarify the proposed method despite mixed empirical results.
