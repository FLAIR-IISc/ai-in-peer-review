- Introduces a hybrid system that combines crowdsourced labels with an incrementally trained online model, shifting from humans to automation over time.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game between the system and the crowd using a CRF prediction model with queries (q) and responses (r).  
- Incorporates continuous time (queries at s, responses at t) and optimization under a final deadline (t_deadline).  
- Continuous-time expected-utility optimization is intractable; authors approximate with Monte Carlo Tree Search and Temporal Difference learning.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face identification against baselines: majority vote (n=1,3,5), standard online learning, and a Threshold baseline.  
- Full model (LENSE) outperforms most baselines on precision/recall/F1 but does not beat 5-voter human consensus.  
- The Threshold baseline performs nearly as well and sometimes better (e.g., lower latency on NER, higher F1 on Face ID), questioning the empirical benefit of continuous time.  
- Demonstrates reduced reliance on crowdsourcing over time and strong initial accuracy by using crowd input when model uncertainty is high.  
- Strength: novel, principled stochastic-game formulation that is significant and likely to inspire follow-up work.  
- Weakness: major clarity problems, especially from the continuous-time formulation, which complicate adoption for limited empirical gain.  
- Recommendation to restructure: present the Threshold method first and treat the continuous-time model as an extension to improve clarity.  
- Experimental reporting issues: unsupported claim of beating human performance; missing baseline results (5-vote missing for Face ID/Sentiment; Threshold missing for Sentiment); formatting inconsistencies (best results not bolded).  
- Minor methodological questions: unclear t_deadline/decision criteria; Eq.(2) missing conditioning on s; confusing reuse of q in F(q) in Eq.(5); likely reversed inequality in Threshold baseline description.  
- Conclusion: original and significant work worthy of acceptance if clarity and reporting issues are addressed.
