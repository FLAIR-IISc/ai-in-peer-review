- Presents a hybrid approach that combines crowdsourced labels and an incrementally trained model, relying on crowd early and reducing reliance as the model gains confidence.
- Formulates the problem as a stochastic game based on a CRF, with system and crowd as players issuing queries and receiving responses with associated utilities.
- Incorporates continuous time and a t_deadline to decide when queries are sent and when answers must be reported, making utility maximization time-sensitive.
- Computing expected utility uses a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s) and becomes intractable with continuous time, so the authors approximate with Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning (Algorithm 1).
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and face-ID compare majority-vote humans (1/3/5), online learning, a “threshold” baseline (no continuous time), and the full model (LENSE).
- LENSE outperforms most methods in precision/recall/F1 but is beaten by 5-vote human labels; the threshold baseline performs nearly as well and has lower latency on NER.
- Shows that the model uses progressively fewer crowdsourced labels as it learns and achieves high early accuracy compared to pure online learning by leveraging crowd expertise.
- Paper is original, significant, and likely to stimulate research and adoption; merits acceptance.
- Major clarity concern: introducing continuous time (Section 3) substantially increases complexity while offering minimal experimental benefit.
- Empirical point: threshold baseline often suffices in practice and sometimes outperforms LENSE (e.g., face ID), so continuous-time complexity may be unnecessary for many tasks.
- Suggestion: emphasize or develop the core method without continuous time and present continuous time as an optional extension to improve accessibility and impact.
- Reporting inaccuracies: claim that the model “beats human performance” is incorrect given the 5-vote results for NER.
- Missing/incomplete results: 5-vote baseline omitted from Face-ID and Sentiment tables/figures and should be included and highlighted where best.
- Formatting/annotation fixes: bold best-performing entries (5-vote in Table 2, threshold latency in NER, LENSE value for Face ID) to reflect true winners.
- Experimental omission: Threshold baseline is missing from the sentiment experiment (Table 3 and Figure 4) and must be added.
- Methodological clarifications needed: unclear what t_deadline was used and what criteria determined when an answer was reported.
- Technical fixes: equation (2) should include s in the distribution; equation 5’s use of q is confusing (choose a different symbol); Threshold baseline expression likely has the inequality sign reversed.
