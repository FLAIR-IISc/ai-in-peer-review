- Proposes a hybrid system that combines crowdsourced labels with an incrementally trained model, relying on the crowd early and reducing queries as model confidence grows.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game on a CRF between the system and the crowd, with queries q, responses r, response times t, schedules s, and optimization of expected utility under a t_deadline.  
- Computes expected utility using a simulation dynamics model P(y, r, t | x, q, s); direct optimization is intractable.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with temporal-difference learning (Algorithm 1) as a practical approximation.  
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment, and face identification, comparing majority vote (1/3/5), online learning with oracle labels, a non-temporal “Threshold” baseline, and the full model (LENSE).  
- LENSE outperforms most baselines on precision/recall/F1 but is beaten by 5-vote crowd on NER.  
- The Threshold baseline is nearly as good as LENSE and achieves substantially lower latency on NER; it even outperforms LENSE on face identification.  
- LENSE uses fewer crowd labels over time as the model strengthens and attains higher initial accuracy than pure online learning by leveraging crowd when uncertain.  
- Reviewer judges the paper original and significant and recommends acceptance.  
- Major clarity concern: introducing continuous time midway through Section 3 complicates exposition and yields limited empirical benefits.  
- Practical recommendation: present the non-temporal core method first and treat continuous-time as an optional extension, since the Threshold baseline is often sufficient.  
- Reporting issues: the claim that the model “beats human performance” is incorrect against 5-vote on NER; 5-vote results are missing for face ID and sentiment in several places.  
- Presentation fixes: consistently highlight best results (e.g., 5-vote top accuracy, Threshold NER latency, LENSE face ID latency) and add the Threshold baseline to the sentiment experiments.  
- Methodological clarifications requested: specify t_deadline used and the stopping rule (entropy-based or fixed).  
- Notation and equation fixes: include s in Eq. (2) p(y,r,t | x,q,s); rename the argument in Eq. (5) to avoid using q twice; correct the reversed inequality in the Threshold baseline description.  
- Overall: accept but streamline exposition, foreground the simpler non-temporal method, position continuous time as an extension, and fix reporting/notation issues.
