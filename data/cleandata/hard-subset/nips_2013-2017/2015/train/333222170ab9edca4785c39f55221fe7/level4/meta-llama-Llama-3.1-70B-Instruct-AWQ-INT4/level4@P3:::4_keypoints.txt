- Proposes a novel on-the-job learning framework that actively queries a crowd in real-time.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Develops an approximation algorithm based on Monte Carlo tree search to compute the optimal policy.  
- Well-motivated and relevant to many real-world applications.  
- Clear and thorough explanation of the framework and algorithm.  
- Experiments are well-designed and show significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements over baselines.  
- Unclear how the algorithm is applied to non-structured prediction tasks (e.g., face recognition); utility-function adaptation needs detail.  
- Threshold baseline has two seemingly arbitrary parameters; authors should explain parameter choices and sensitivity.  
- Missing comparison to an uncertainty-sampling baseline similar to active learning.  
- Insufficient discussion on why active learning cannot be adapted and why the proposed framework is preferable for on-the-job learning.  
- Novel combination of Bayesian decision theory and stochastic games; MCTS approximation is original.  
- Significant potential impact: framework can be applied across tasks and shows strong empirical gains.  
- Overall quality is high but some application details and clarifications are needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: lack of clarity on non-structured tasks, arbitrary baseline parameters, and weak comparison/discussion with active learning.
