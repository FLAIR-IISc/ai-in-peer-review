- Tackles building high-accuracy classifiers from zero labeled examples, with focus on noisy crowd respondents and novel timing/latency optimization.  
- Proposes "on-the-job learning" framework using Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Models the problem as a stochastic game with a CRF prediction model and uses Monte Carlo Tree Search to approximate optimal policy.  
- Evaluated on three datasets: named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification.  
- Empirical results show LENSE outperforms baselines in accuracy and cost.  
- On NER, LENSE achieves 88.4% F1 at over an order-of-magnitude lower cost than a 5-vote baseline.  
- Analysis shows system cost steadily decreases over time as the model improves.  
- Strengths: novel problem setting, thorough evaluation, and compelling baseline comparisons.  
- Explanations can be opaque; some derivations and descriptions are hard to follow.  
- The example at Line 154 is too abstract and should be replaced with a concrete query/crowd-response/time example.  
- Minor presentation issues: inconsistent terminology in Tables 2 and 3 ("entropic" vs. "threshold").  
- Related work coverage is thorough but its placement in Section 5 is odd and could be moved or integrated earlier.  
- Overall: well-written and persuasive with minor presentation and clarity issues that should be fixed.
