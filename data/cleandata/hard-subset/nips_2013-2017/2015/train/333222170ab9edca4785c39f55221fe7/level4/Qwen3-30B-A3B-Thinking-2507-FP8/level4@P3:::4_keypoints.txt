- Introduces "on-the-job learning": build accurate systems from zero labeled examples by using real-time crowdsourcing, modeled as a stochastic game with Bayesian decision theory and approximated via Monte Carlo Tree Search to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.

- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification with strong empirical gains: >10x cost reduction for NER, +8% F1 over single-human labeling, +28% over online learning baselines.

- Formalizes a setting distinct from standard active learning and pure crowdsourcing, extending active classification by enabling model improvement during test time.

- The stochastic-game/Bayesian foundation is principled and differentiates the work from prior systems like Legion:AR.

- LENSE implementation shows practical utility; open-sourced code increases reproducibility.

- Evaluation design is thoughtful, including worker quality control via pre-sampling of responses and delays.

- Strength: elegant formalization that jointly optimizes accuracy, latency, and cost.

- Weakness: unclear application to non-structured tasks (face recognition); need clarification whether queries were instance-level or how framework was adapted.

- Weakness: threshold baseline is under-justified—parameters (e.g., 0.3m and 0.98) lack explanation and no sensitivity analysis is provided.

- Weakness: inadequate comparison to active learning/uncertainty sampling; authors should show why standard uncertainty sampling fails or include it as a baseline.

- Weakness: paper does not explain how system handles many simultaneously uncertain positions or the prioritization of queries.

- Requested revisions: clarify face-recognition setup, justify/analytically test threshold parameters, add uncertainty-sampling comparison, and explain multi-uncertainty querying strategy.

- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revision—novel, impactful framework with important but addressable concerns.
