- Unclear how on-the-job learning was applied to face recognition — is each instance just queried, and how does utility function apply in non-structured settings?  
- In non-structured tasks the model doesn't propagate information across examples, making the rationale for the utility function less clear.  
- The threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters without explanation of their origin.  
- Paper lacks sensitivity analysis showing how performance varies with threshold baseline parameters.  
- Missing a standard uncertainty sampling baseline (active learning style) for comparison.  
- The authors downplay but do not fully address how active learning could be adapted to this domain (e.g., updating on responses then predicting).  
- Adapting active learning could leverage established query-selection theory and should be compared.  
- The framework is novel and addresses a practical problem of limited initial labeled data.  
- Formulating the problem as a stochastic game with Bayesian decision theory is principled for balancing latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Empirical results show strong cost reductions and performance gains, especially on the NER task.  
- Public implementation (LENSE) aids reproducibility and further research.  
- Application to non-structured tasks (sentiment, face recognition) shows weaker performance, suggesting limited generalizability.  
- Overall contribution is promising but requires clarification of details and broader baseline comparisons.
