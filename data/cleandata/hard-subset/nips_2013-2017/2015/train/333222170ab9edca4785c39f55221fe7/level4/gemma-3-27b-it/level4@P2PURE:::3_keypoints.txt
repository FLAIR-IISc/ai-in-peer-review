- Proposes a hybrid system that uses heavy crowdsourcing during ramp-up and incrementally reduces crowd reliance as an online model gains confidence.  
- Casts the problem as a stochastic game built on a CRF prediction model where system and crowd are players issuing queries, receiving responses, and accruing utility.  
- Incorporates continuous time and response latencies, maximizing utility under a t_deadline constraint that limits query timing and rate.  
- Computing expected utility requires a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s); continuous time makes optimization intractable, so authors use Monte Carlo Tree Search plus TD learning (Alg. 1).  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face-ID tasks against baselines: majority vote (1/3/5), online learning (oracle labels), a “threshold” baseline (no continuous time), and the full LENSE model.  
- LENSE outperforms most baselines in precision/recall/F1 but is beaten by 5-vote crowdsourcing; the Threshold baseline performs nearly as well and with lower latency on NER.  
- Shows that LENSE requires fewer crowdsourced labels over time with a strong training signal and outperforms online learning from the first example by leveraging crowd expertise when uncertainty is high.  
- Paper is original, significant, and likely to stimulate research and adoption; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Major clarity concern: introducing continuous time substantially increases complexity while yielding minimal experimental benefit; suggest presenting the core (non-continuous) method first and treating continuous time as an extension.  
- Emphasize the strong performance of the Threshold baseline— for many practical applications it may suffice despite sequence dependencies.  
- Results/reporting issues: incorrect “beats human performance” claim for NER; missing 5-vote data for Face-ID and Sentiment in multiple tables/figures; table bolding inconsistencies; Threshold baseline omitted from the sentiment experiment.  
- Minor methodological/notation issues: unclear use of t_deadline in experiments, equation (2) should condition on s, confusing use of q in F(q) in eq. (5), and a sign/inequality error in the Threshold baseline expression.  
- Final judgment: accept with revisions to improve clarity, fix experimental reporting, and better justify or de-emphasize the continuous-time contribution.
