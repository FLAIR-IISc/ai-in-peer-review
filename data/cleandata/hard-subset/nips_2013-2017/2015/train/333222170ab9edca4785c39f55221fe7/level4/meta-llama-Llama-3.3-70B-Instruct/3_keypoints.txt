- Proposes a hybrid approach that combines crowdsourced labels with an incrementally trained model, relying on the crowd early and reducing queries as the model gains confidence.  
- Formalizes the interaction as a stochastic game where the system issues queries to the crowd to maximize expected utility.  
- Uses a simulation dynamics model to compute utility by predicting true labels, crowd responses, and response times given input, query, and time.  
- Introduces continuous time into the model, which makes the optimization problem intractable.  
- Employs Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning as approximations to handle the intractability.  
- Evaluates on named entity recognition, sentiment recognition, and face identification tasks.  
- Compares against baselines including majority vote, online learning, and a "threshold" baseline that omits continuous time.  
- Shows the proposed model generally outperforms most baselines on precision, recall, and F1.  
- Notes the "threshold" baseline often achieves comparable performance with lower latency in some cases.  
- Demonstrates the model reduces reliance on crowdsourced labels over time and leverages crowd expertise better than online learning when the model is strong.  
- Praises the paper as original, significant, and well-motivated, recommending acceptance.  
- Criticizes the continuous-time formulation as complicating the presentation and unclear in benefit; suggests presenting the core method without continuous time first and adding continuous time as an extension.  
- Requests clearer reporting and analysis of the strong "threshold" baseline.  
- Points out minor presentation issues: missing 5-vote data in tables/figures, inconsistent latency reporting, and need for clarification on the experiment deadline, the distribution in Eq.(2), and notation in Eq.(5).
