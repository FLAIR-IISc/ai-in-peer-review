- Proposes a hybrid method that integrates crowdsourced labels with an incrementally trained online model, relying more on crowd early and less as model confidence grows.  
- Formulates the problem as a stochastic game using a CRF where system and crowd are players, issuing timed queries and receiving responses.  
- Defines a utility U(q,r) and optimizes expected utility under query timing and a deadline constraint t_deadline.  
- Requires a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s) to compute expected utility; continuous time makes exact optimization intractable.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search and Temporal Difference learning (Algorithm 1) as an approximation for the continuous-time optimization.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face identification tasks versus majority vote baselines, an oracle online model, a non-temporal "threshold" baseline, and the full LENSE model.  
- LENSE outperforms most baselines in precision/recall/F1 except the 5-annotator crowdsourced approach.  
- The threshold baseline achieves nearly identical performance to LENSE (and lower latency on NER), and slightly outperforms LENSE on Face ID.  
- Demonstrates that LENSE reduces reliance on crowdsourced labels over time and attains high accuracy from the first example by leveraging the crowd when uncertain.  
- Reviewer finds the work original, significant, and likely impactful, and recommends acceptance despite concerns.  
- Major clarity weakness: continuous-time formulation is introduced midpaper and adds complexity without clear experimental payoff.  
- Suggests restructuring: present core method without time first, then introduce continuous-time as an extension to improve accessibility and adoption.  
- Experimental/reporting issues: claim of "beats human performance" contradicted by 5-vote NER; 5-vote results missing for Face ID and Sentiment in several places and should be included/bolded where best.  
- Reporting issues: threshold baseline missing entirely from sentiment experiments (Table 3, Fig. 4) and should be included; latency bests should be bolded appropriately.  
- Method-description issues: t_deadline usage and stopping criteria in experiments not specified; Equation (2) should condition on s; notation F(q) is confusing; threshold uncertainty inequality appears incorrect.
