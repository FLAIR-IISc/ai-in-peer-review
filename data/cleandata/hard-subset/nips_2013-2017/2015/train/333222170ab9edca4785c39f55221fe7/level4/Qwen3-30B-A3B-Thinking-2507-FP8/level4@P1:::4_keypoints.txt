- Proposes an on-the-job learning framework that queries humans at prediction time to maintain accuracy without initial training data.  
- Framework is clearly articulated and technically sound for structured prediction tasks (e.g., NER).  
- Application to unstructured tasks (face recognition) is unclear—uncertainty propagation across sequence elements used in utility is absent for images.  
- This ambiguity undermines the claimed generality of the method beyond structured domains.  
- The threshold baseline uses two unexplained parameters (uncertainty threshold and query multiplier) with no justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Arbitrary parameter choices weaken the baseline’s credibility since they directly control query frequency and cost.  
- A stronger baseline would be uncertainty sampling/standard active learning adapted to a stream, to better isolate the Bayesian framework’s contribution.  
- The authors’ distinction from online active learning is insufficient; they should empirically show why active learning cannot maintain high accuracy from the first prediction.  
- Active learning could be adapted (treat input as a batch of queryable regions with updates after responses); the paper should explain why that adaptation fails in accuracy-latency-cost trade-offs.  
- Empirical results on NER are strong (88.4% F1, >10× cost reduction), demonstrating practical value in structured settings.  
- Overall, stochastic game formulation and MCTS approximation are novel, but unclear unstructured application, ungrounded baseline parameters, and limited comparison reduce the paper’s broader impact; more face recognition details and rigorous baselines are needed.
