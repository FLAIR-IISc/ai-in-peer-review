- Presents a theoretically grounded “on-the-job” learner that moves from full crowdsourced labels to selective self-reliance using Monte Carlo Tree Search.  
- Clear overall presentation but missing specific mathematical and implementation details in places.  
- Empirical gains over a threshold-based baseline are modest, though the approach is promising.  
- Equation (2) appears incorrect: LHS likely needs conditioning on state s and y disappears from p_R(ri | y, q_i); needs mathematical clarification.  
- Algorithm 1 references N(s) without definition; the function and its role must be specified.  
- Threshold baseline uses values 0.3 and 0.88—need explanation of how these thresholds were chosen (tuned on validation set or prior knowledge).  
- Unclear whether compared “online” systems are state-of-the-art; this affects how significant human annotation improvements are.  
- Request for concrete examples and detailed error analysis showing cases where crowd annotation improves performance and where it fails.  
- Overall, a valuable theoretical contribution that would be strengthened by addressing the above clarifications and deeper empirical analysis.
