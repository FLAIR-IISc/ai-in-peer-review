- Proposes a theoretically-grounded formulation of the "on-the-job learner" that transitions from crowd-sourced predictions to an autonomous model and uses Monte Carlo Tree Search to decide when to query the crowd.

- Paper is generally well-written but lacks clarity on several important technical details.

- Approach is theoretically sound and promising, but empirical results are not fully convincing.

- Performance improvement over a simple threshold-based heuristic is underwhelming.

- Requests clarification on Eq. (2): LHS should likely be conditioned on state s, and explain how y is marginalized or removed from p_R(r_i | y, q_i).

- Requests definition of N(s) used in Algorithm 1.

- Asks how thresholds 0.3 and 0.88 for the baseline were selected and whether they were tuned on a validation set.

- Requests justification for choice of "online" comparison systems: confirm whether they are state-of-the-art or, if not, provide error analysis showing when human intervention is most critical.
