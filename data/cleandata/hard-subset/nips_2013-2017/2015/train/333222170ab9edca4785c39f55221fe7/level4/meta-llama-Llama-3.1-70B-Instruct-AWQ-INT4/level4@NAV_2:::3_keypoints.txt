- Proposes deploying high-accuracy systems with zero training examples using real-time crowdsourcing plus Bayesian decision theory.  
- Frames on-the-job learning as a stochastic game to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- System adaptively queries crowd for uncertain inputs and updates its prediction model online.  
- Introduces continuous-time stochastic game formulation as a novel twist on online/active learning and crowdsourcing.  
- Presents LENSE and evaluates it on named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification.  
- Reports significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements for LENSE compared to traditional approaches.  
- Major technical contributions: stochastic game framework and an approximation algorithm based on Monte Carlo Tree Search.  
- Provides thorough evaluation with multiple baselines and performance-over-time analysis.  
- Clarity and organization issues: continuous-time introduction (Section 3) is not well-motivated.  
- Experimental results do not clearly demonstrate the benefits of continuous time; the threshold baseline often performs comparably.  
- Suggests the paper should better justify continuous time and highlight scenarios where it offers clear advantages.  
- Minor presentation issues: unclear notation in equations (2) and (5), unclear description of the threshold baseline, and missing 5-vote results for all datasets.  
- Overall assessment: technically substantial and potentially high-impact with revisions to clarity and presentation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: originality, significant technical contributions, and thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity/organization problems, unproven benefits of continuous time, and minor notation/presentation issues.
