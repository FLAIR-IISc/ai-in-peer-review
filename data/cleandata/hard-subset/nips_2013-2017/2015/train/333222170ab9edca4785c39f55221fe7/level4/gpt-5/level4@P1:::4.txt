The paper introduces an “on-the-job learning” setting, in which the system can query annotators between receiving an input and committing to a prediction. This is clear for structured prediction tasks, where specific elements of a sequence are queried before producing the final output.

However, the application to the face recognition task is not clear. Was the entire instance simply queried? In the unstructured setting, the model does not propagate information across adjacent positions (i.e., across examples), so it is not obvious how the proposed utility function applies or provides benefit in this case.

Regarding baselines, the threshold method uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters. Where do these values come from, and how sensitive are the results to them? A sensitivity analysis would help establish robustness. In addition, an uncertainty-sampling baseline, akin to that commonly used in active learning, would be informative for comparison.

The paper distinguishes this setting from online active learning, but active learning could be adapted quite directly to this domain by treating the sequence as the active set. For example, the model could be updated as responses arrive and then output a prediction at the end. While this would not capture the temporal aspects of decision making, there is substantial theory in active learning to guide query selection that seems applicable here.

Overall, the framework appears novel, but some details remain unclear, and the applicability seems fairly restricted as currently presented.
