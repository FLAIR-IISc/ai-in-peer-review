- Introduces “on-the-job learning” where the system can query annotators before making a prediction; approach is clear for structured prediction (querying sequence elements).
- Application to face recognition/non-structured setting is unclear—what is being queried (the whole instance?), and how do query responses affect subsequent predictions when information doesn't propagate across examples?
- The proposed utility function’s validity in non-structured scenarios is questionable and needs detailed explanation.
- The threshold baseline depends on two seemingly arbitrary parameters—authors must clarify how they were chosen and provide sensitivity analysis.
- Add a baseline using uncertainty sampling (active-learning-style) for a useful comparison.
- Active learning could be adapted to this domain (treat active set as a sequence and update), so the paper should more carefully position itself relative to adapted active learning approaches and leverage existing theory.
- Overall: framework is novel but several details are unclear and applicability seems limited; clarify non-structured setting, strengthen baselines, and better situate work relative to active learning.
