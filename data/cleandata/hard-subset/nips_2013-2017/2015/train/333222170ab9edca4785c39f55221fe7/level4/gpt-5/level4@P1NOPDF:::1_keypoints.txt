- Main contribution: a more theoretically grounded formulation of the on-the-job learner from [22], with querying decisions cast as a decision problem solved via MCTS.  
- Learner behavior: initially relies on crowd queries, then transitions to making its own predictions and queries only for low-confidence inputs.  
- Decision method: Monte Carlo Tree Search is used to decide whether to query the crowd for a given input.  
- Presentation: generally clear but omits several important technical details.  
- Empirical results: improvements over a simple threshold-based heuristic are modest/underwhelming.  
- Overall assessment: approach is reasonable and worth further investigation despite mixed results.  
- Clarification needed (Eq. 2): left-hand side should be conditioned on s; unclear how y in p_R(r_i | y, q_i) disappears.  
- Clarification needed (Alg. 1): define what N(s) denotes.  
- Clarification needed (baseline): explain how the 0.3 and 0.88 thresholds were determined.  
- Clarification needed (datasets/systems): specify whether the “online” systems are the best machine-learned systems for these tasks; if not, justify choices; if yes, show examples and error analysis to attribute gains to human annotation.
