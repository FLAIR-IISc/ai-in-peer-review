The authors describe a situation called "on-the-job learning," where commenters are asked to make a prediction after receiving an example.

Although it is clear how the algorithm works for a structured prediction task, where the elements of the sequence are asked, it is not at all clear how it was applied to a face recognition task – was it just an example? In a non-structured setting, the model will not propagate information between adjacent positions (instances), so it seems the utility function would have no meaning.

The threshold baseline has two parameters that are seemingly arbitrary. Where did these values come from, and what is their sensitivity?

Another baseline for uncertainty sampling used in active learning would be interesting.

Finally, the authors are interested in distinguishing between this setting and active online learning, but active learning can easily be adapted to work in this domain, where the active set in question is now a sequence. For example, there is no reason why the model cannot be updated in the same way after receiving a response and then generate a prediction output at the end. Although this clearly does not capture the transient nature of the task, there are many theories for selecting the query set. Apparently, a new structure, although some details are not clear, seems quite limited in application.
