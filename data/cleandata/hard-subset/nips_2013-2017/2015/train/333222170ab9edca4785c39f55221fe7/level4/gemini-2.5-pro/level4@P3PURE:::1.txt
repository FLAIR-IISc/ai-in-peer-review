This paper provides a more theoretically sound formulation for an "on the job learner," a system initially proposed in [22]. The proposed learner begins by querying a crowd for all predictions and gradually transitions to an automated model that queries the crowd selectively on low-confidence inputs. The decision of when to query is framed as a sequential decision problem and solved using Monte Carlo Tree Search.

The presentation is generally clear, though it lacks some important details. While the proposed approach is well-motivated and reasonable, the empirical results are not entirely compelling, showing only modest gains over a simple threshold-based heuristic. Nevertheless, the framework is interesting and merits further investigation.

Please address or clarify the following points:

1.  Could the authors please clarify Equation (2)? The left-hand side should presumably also be conditioned on the state *s*. Furthermore, it is unclear how the variable *y* is marginalized out from the term p{R}(ri | y, q_i).

2.  The notation N(s) used in Algorithm 1 is not defined in the text.

3.  The paper should provide a justification for the specific values (0.3 and 0.88) chosen for the threshold-based baseline. How were these values selected?

4.  It is unclear whether the "online" systems used for comparison represent the state-of-the-art for these tasks. If stronger machine learning models exist, the reported gains might be less significant. Conversely, if these baselines are indeed state-of-the-art, then the performance improvement comes solely from the strategic use of human annotation. In this case, an error analysis would be valuable to characterize the types of instances where the proposed querying strategy is most beneficial.
