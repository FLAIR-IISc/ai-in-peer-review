- Main contribution: a principled theoretical framework for an "on the job learner" that shifts from crowd workers to autonomous predictions using MCTS for sequential decision-making.
- Paper is generally well-written and clear but omits several important technical details.
- Empirical gains over a simple threshold-based baseline are modest.
- Method is conceptually sound and a promising direction for future work.
- Clarification needed for Equation (2): LHS should likely be conditioned on state s and explain marginalization of y from p_R(r_i | y, q_i).
- Algorithm 1 uses N(s) without definition—please specify what N(s) denotes.
- Clarify how the threshold baseline values 0.3 and 0.88 were selected to ensure reproducibility.
- Justify choice of "online" baselines: are they state-of-the-art? If not, strengthen justification; if yes, provide error analysis to show where human-in-the-loop helps most.
- Summary: formalizes a useful on-the-job learning idea that reduces crowd reliance, but empirical results are mixed and more detail/analysis is required.
