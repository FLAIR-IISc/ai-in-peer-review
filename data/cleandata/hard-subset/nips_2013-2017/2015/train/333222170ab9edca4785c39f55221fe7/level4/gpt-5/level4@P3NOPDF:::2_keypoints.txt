- Paper introduces LENSE, a system for denoising and scheduling crowd responses to train classifiers with zero labeled examples, emphasizing latency-aware decision-making.
- Novel focus on timing and latency optimizations in crowd-sourced labeling, despite existing work on noise handling.
- Strength: Direct human-vs-model comparisons ground practical value and performance claims.
- Strength: Informative baselines show LENSE outperforms 3-vote consensus but underperforms 5-vote, highlighting a trade-off.
- Strength: Problem is significant—building classifiers from zero labels with crowd noise and latency constraints is a relevant, underexplored setting.
- Weakness: Exposition is opaque; abstract example at Line 154 fails to clarify how LENSE operates in realistic scenarios.
- Weakness: Inconsistent terminology—“threshold” and “entropic” appear interchangeably in Tables 2 and 3, causing confusion.
- Weakness: Related work is placed too late (Section 5), hindering readers’ ability to contextualize contributions early.
- Weakness: Unresolved reference “Figure ??” at Line 086 indicates broken or missing figure references.
- Weakness: Paper lacks a clear cost–accuracy–latency analysis to explain when LENSE is preferable to increasing consensus (e.g., 5-vote).
- Suggestion: Include a concrete end-to-end walkthrough (with numbers, timestamps, conflicting answers) to demonstrate LENSE’s decision and belief-update process.
- Suggestion: Standardize baseline names and definitions across text, tables, and captions, or clearly distinguish different baselines.
- Suggestion: Move or summarize related work earlier to clarify differences from prior crowdsourcing, aggregation, and active learning methods.
- Suggestion: Reframe evaluation to report costs/latencies (votes solicited, time) and provide Pareto curves or matched-budget comparisons versus consensus methods.
- Suggestion: Fix figure/reference placeholders and check caption/numbering consistency.
- Suggestion: Tighten narrative and add an algorithm box or pseudocode for the decision policy (when to query, wait, or stop).
- Minor: Define all baselines once and reference consistently.
- Minor: Ensure table captions are self-contained (dataset, metric, tasks, budgets).
