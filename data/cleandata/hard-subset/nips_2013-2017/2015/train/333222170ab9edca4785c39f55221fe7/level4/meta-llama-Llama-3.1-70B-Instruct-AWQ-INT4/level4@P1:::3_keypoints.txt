- Presents a hybrid system that uses crowdsourced labels plus an online-trained model, leaning on crowd early and reducing queries as model confidence grows.  
- Formulates the problem as a stochastic game based on a CRF where system and crowd are players issuing queries and receiving responses.  
- System issues queries q for tokens x with true labels y, receives responses r, and maximizes expected utility U(q,r) under a time deadline.  
- Continuous-time response modeling (queries at times s, responses at t) is included to optimize actions given a t_deadline.  
- Computing expected utility requires a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s) and is intractable, so authors use Monte Carlo Tree Search plus TD learning (Algorithm 1).  
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and face-ID compare majority-vote (1,3,5), online oracle learning, a "threshold" baseline, and the full continuous-time model (LENSE).  
- LENSE outperforms all baselines except the 5-vote crowd in precision/recall/F1; the threshold baseline performs nearly as well and sometimes with lower latency.  
- Shows the model needs fewer crowdsourced labels over time and attains high early accuracy by leveraging crowd when uncertainty is high.  
- Work is highly original, principled, and significant; reviewer recommends acceptance and expects impact and further work.  
- Major clarity issues: introduction of continuous time midway (Section 3) greatly increases complexity and reduces readability.  
- Empirically continuous-time appears to provide little benefit over the simpler threshold baseline, raising doubts about its necessity.  
- Suggest restructuring: present core method without continuous time, then add continuous-time as an extension to improve accessibility and adoption.  
- Reporting issues: claims of "beats human performance" are misleading (5-vote wins NER); 5-vote results missing from some tables/figures and bolding is inconsistent.  
- Missing threshold baseline in the sentiment experiment (Table 3, Figure 4) should be included for fair comparison.  
- Unclear experimental details: what t_deadline was used and how answers were declared ready (entropic threshold or t_deadline?).  
- Minor technical fixes: equation (2) should condition on s (p(y,r,t|x,q,s)); equation (5) uses confusing notation F(q)—rename; threshold baseline inequality appears incorrect.  
- Overall: strong, novel work worthy of inclusion but needs clarity, experiment reporting fixes, and clearer positioning of the continuous-time contribution.
