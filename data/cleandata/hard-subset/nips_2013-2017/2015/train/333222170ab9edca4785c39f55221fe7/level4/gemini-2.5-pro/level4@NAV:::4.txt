The paper introduces a framework referred to as "on the job learning," where a model can make requests to an annotator between the time an example is received and a prediction is made.

While the algorithm's application to structured prediction tasks, involving queries on sequential elements, is well-explained, its implementation for the face recognition task is ambiguous. It is unclear what specific information is being queried—is it the entire instance? In a non-structured setting, information would not be propagated between adjacent positions, which seems to undermine the rationale for the proposed utility function.

The threshold-based baseline utilizes two parameters that seem to be chosen arbitrarily. The manuscript should provide justification for these values and an analysis of the model's sensitivity to them.

An additional baseline comparison against uncertainty sampling, a common technique in active learning, would strengthen the experimental evaluation.

Lastly, the authors strongly differentiate their setting from active online learning. However, active learning could be straightforwardly adapted for this problem by defining the sequence as the active set under consideration. For instance, the model could be updated upon receiving query responses before generating its final prediction. Although this might not entirely capture the task's temporal nature, the extensive literature on query set selection in active learning provides a rich theoretical foundation.

The proposed framework is intriguing, but several key details are unclear, and its applicability seems to be fairly limited.
