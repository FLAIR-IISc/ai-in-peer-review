- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy a high-accuracy predictor from zero labeled data by querying crowdsourced labels for uncertain parts of each input and improving the model over time.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game and derives a Bayesian utility balancing accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing substantial gains over human-only, machine-only, and heuristic baselines.  
- Novel and significant: addresses cold-start deployment where high accuracy is required immediately.  
- Strong technical formulation: principled, elegant game-theoretic/Bayesian setup and sensible use of MCTS.  
- Empirical strength: especially strong results on structured prediction (NER), reducing annotation cost while improving F1 versus baselines.  
- Good reproducibility: authors provide open-source code, data, and experiments.  
- Applicability unclear for non-structured tasks (e.g., face classification): unclear whether queries are whole-instance labels and how the structured model or utility offers advantages there.  
- Experimental baselines need strengthening: the Threshold baseline uses unexplained parameter choices (0.3 and 0.98) without sensitivity analysis.  
- Missing active-learning comparisons: should include standard uncertainty-sampling or adapted active-learning baselines to better contextualize gains.  
- Positioning vs. active learning is underdeveloped: an empirical comparison to a pre-prediction active-learning adaptation would clarify the specific benefits of the game-theoretic approach.  
- Overall: novel and valuable contribution with strong core ideas and results, but clarity on non-structured applicability and more rigorous baselines are needed.
