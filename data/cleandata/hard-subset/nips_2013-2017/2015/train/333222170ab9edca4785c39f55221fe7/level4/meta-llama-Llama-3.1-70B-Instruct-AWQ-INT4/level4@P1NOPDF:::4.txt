The authors describe a scenario called "on the job learning", where annotators are queried for additional information between receiving an example and outputting a prediction. While the algorithm's functionality is clear for structured prediction tasks, where elements of a sequence are queried, its application to the face recognition task is less evident. Specifically, it is unclear whether the instance being queried is the only aspect of the task that is relevant. In the non-structured setting, the model does not propagate information between adjacent positions (examples), which raises questions about the utility function's applicability.

The threshold baseline has two parameters that appear to be arbitrarily chosen. The authors should provide more information about the origin of these values and the sensitivity of the results to these parameters. An additional baseline of uncertainty sampling, similar to that used in active learning, would be a valuable comparison.

The authors emphasize the distinction between this setting and active online learning, but it is worth noting that active learning could be adapted to work in this domain with relative ease. For instance, the model could be updated upon receiving responses and then output a prediction at the end. Although this approach would not capture the temporal nature of the task, there is a substantial body of theory that could be leveraged to inform the selection of the query set. Overall, the framework presented appears to be novel, but some details are unclear, and its applicability seems to be fairly limited.
