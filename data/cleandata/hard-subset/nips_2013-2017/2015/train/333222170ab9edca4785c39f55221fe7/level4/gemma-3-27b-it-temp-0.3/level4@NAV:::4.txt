The authors introduce a method termed “on the job learning,” wherein annotators are prompted for information between example presentation and prediction generation. While the algorithmic process is well-defined for structured prediction tasks involving queries to sequence elements, its application to the face recognition task remains unclear – specifically, what constitutes the queried instance in this context? In the unstructured setting, information propagation between examples is absent, raising questions about the relevance of the proposed utility function.

The threshold baseline employs two parameters whose selection appears ad hoc. The justification for these values, and an analysis of their impact on performance, are needed. Including a baseline based on uncertainty sampling, similar to techniques used in active learning, would strengthen the evaluation. 

The authors emphasize the distinction between this framework and active online learning; however, active learning principles could be readily adapted to this sequential domain. For instance, the model could be updated incrementally upon receiving responses, culminating in a final prediction. Although this adaptation wouldn't fully leverage the task’s temporal dynamics, established theory exists for query set selection. This work presents a potentially novel framework, though certain details require clarification, and its generalizability appears limited.
