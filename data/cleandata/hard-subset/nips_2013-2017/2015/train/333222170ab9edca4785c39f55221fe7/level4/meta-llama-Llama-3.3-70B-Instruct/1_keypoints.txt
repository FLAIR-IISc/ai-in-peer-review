- Presents a significant theoretical improvement to the on-the-job learner from [22], formalizing a transition from crowd-sourced outputs to self-prediction with selective querying.  
- Frames the crowd-query decision as a decision problem and solves it using Monte Carlo Tree Search.  
- Overall presentation is clear but omits important technical details in places.  
- Empirical comparison to a threshold-based heuristic shows only modest gains, warranting further exploration.  
- Eq. (2) appears incomplete: the LHS should be conditioned on s, and the disappearance of y in p_R(r_i | y, q_i) needs explanation.  
- Notation N(s) in Algorithm 1 is undefined and must be clarified.  
- The origin/derivation of threshold baseline values (0.3 and 0.88) is not provided and should be explained.  
- Must confirm whether the "online" systems are the best-performing models; if so, observed improvements may stem from human annotation and require error analysis and example scenarios.
