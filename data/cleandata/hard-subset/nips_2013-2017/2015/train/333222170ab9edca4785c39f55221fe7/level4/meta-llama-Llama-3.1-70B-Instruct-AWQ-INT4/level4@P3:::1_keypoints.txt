- Paper proposes a theoretical formulation of an "on-the-job learner" that transitions from crowd queries to autonomous predictions.  
- Main contribution: a more theoretically sound formulation and algorithm using Bayesian decision theory and Monte Carlo Tree Search.  
- Presentation is mostly good but omits important details and clarity in places.  
- Empirical results are underwhelming compared to a threshold-based heuristic.  
- Paper situates well within online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing literature.  
- Technically sound with a clear, well-motivated problem formulation and justified methods.  
- Clarity issues: Equation (2) is unclear and Algorithm 1 omits definitions (e.g., N(s)).  
- More intuition is needed about algorithm behavior and relationship to the threshold heuristic.  
- Originality: novel formulation and algorithm distinct from prior work, leveraging Bayesian decision theory and MCTS.  
- Significance: addresses learning from noisy and expensive data with potential broad applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated formulation; clear explanation; tackles a challenging, widely relevant problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical gains versus baseline; missing derivations and explanations; limited experimental/task detail.  
- Reviewer asks authors to condition Equation (2) on s and clarify p_R(ri | y, q_i) and its derivation.  
- Reviewer requests definition and explanation of N(s) in Algorithm 1.  
- Reviewer requests derivation/explanation of threshold baseline values (0.3 and 0.88) and the heuristic.  
- Reviewer requests more details about experimental tasks, datasets, and state-of-the-art baselines and performance.
