The authors present an “on-the-job learning” scenario in which annotators are queried between receiving an input and producing a prediction. While the algorithm is clear for structured prediction, where elements of a sequence are queried, it is not at all clear how this was applied to face recognition—was the query simply the instance itself? In the non-structured setting, the model would not propagate information between adjacent positions (examples), so the utility function wouldn’t make sense.

The threshold baseline uses two apparently arbitrary parameters. How were these values chosen, and how sensitive are the results to them? Including an uncertainty-sampling baseline, akin to that used in active learning, would also be informative.

Finally, although the authors emphasize a distinction from active online learning, active learning could be adapted quite straightforwardly to this domain by treating the active set as a sequence. For example, the model could be updated upon receiving responses and then output a prediction at the end. While this would not capture the temporal nature of the task, there is substantial theory to draw upon for selecting the query set.

Overall, the framework appears novel, but several details are unclear, and its applicability seems fairly restricted.
