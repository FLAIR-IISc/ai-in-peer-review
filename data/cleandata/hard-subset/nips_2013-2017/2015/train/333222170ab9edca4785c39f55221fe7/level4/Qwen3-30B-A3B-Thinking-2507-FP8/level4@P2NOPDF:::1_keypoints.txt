1. Proposes an MCTS-based, theoretically grounded on-the-job learner that shifts from crowd queries to autonomous predictions based on confidence.  
2. Core innovation: modeling the query decision as an MCTS problem to enable principled transition from crowd reliance to model prediction.  
3. Presentation is generally clear but omits important implementation details needed for reproducibility.  
4. Empirical results show modest gains over a threshold-based heuristic, indicating the approach is reasonable but not transformative yet.  
5. Eq. (2) needs correction: value function must be conditioned on state s (e.g., V^π(s)), and the role of observation y in p^(R)(r_i | y, q_i) must be clarified since y should not appear in the query reward distribution.  
6. Algorithm 1: explicitly define N(s) (likely the MCTS visit count for state s) to ensure reproducibility.  
7. Threshold baseline: justify chosen values (0.3, 0.88); state whether they were tuned on a validation set and describe the tuning procedure and dataset.  
8. Baseline comparison & task context: clarify whether baseline online systems are SOTA; if not, justify their selection; if they are SOTA, show concrete examples where human annotation, not model improvements, yields gains and provide error analysis.  
9. Theoretical contribution is sound and fills a gap in adaptive learning, but empirical evaluation lacks depth and stronger baselines.  
10. Addressing the formalism clarifications, baseline justification, and task-specific insights would significantly strengthen the paper and motivate further exploration.
