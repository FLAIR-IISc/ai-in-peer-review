- Proposes a hybrid strategy combining crowdsourced annotations with an incrementally trained online model, formalized as a stochastic game over a CRF that issues timed queries and accounts for response delays and deadlines.  
- Uses a simulation model P(y,r,t|x,q,s) to compute expected utility and approximates intractable continuous-time optimization with Monte Carlo Tree Search plus TD learning (Algorithm 1).  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face-ID tasks against baselines: majority vote (1,3,5), oracle online learning, a “threshold” baseline, and the full LENSE method.  
- LENSE outperforms all baselines except the 5-vote crowd condition; threshold baseline performs nearly as well and has lower latency on NER.  
- Paper is original, significant, practically relevant, and likely to spark follow-on work; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Main clarity concern: introduction of continuous time mid-Section 3 substantially complicates exposition while providing limited empirical benefit.  
- Suggestion: present the simpler non-time-based core method first and treat continuous-time as an extension; explicitly acknowledge the strength of the threshold baseline.  
- Results/presentation errors: incorrect claim that the model “beats human performance” (not true vs. 5-vote); 5-vote condition is missing in Face-ID and Sentiment in several tables/figures.  
- Formatting issues: 5-vote results and certain latency entries should be bolded (e.g., Threshold latency for NER, LENSE for Face-ID).  
- Missing baseline: Threshold baseline is absent for the sentiment task and should be added to Table 3 and Figure 4.  
- Unclear experimental details: which t_deadline (if any) was used and what stopping rule determined when to output a prediction.  
- Technical/notation fixes: equation (2) should include s in the distribution; rename the argument in F(q) to avoid overloading q; correct the inequality in the Threshold baseline description.  
- Overall: accept with revisions to improve clarity around continuous-time modeling and to fix empirical presentation and notation issues for the camera-ready.
