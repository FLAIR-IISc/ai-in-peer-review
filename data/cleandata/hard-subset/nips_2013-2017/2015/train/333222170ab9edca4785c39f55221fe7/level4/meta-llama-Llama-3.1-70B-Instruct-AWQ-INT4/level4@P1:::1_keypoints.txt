- Main contribution: proposes a more theoretically sound formulation of an on-the-job learner that transitions from crowd labels to model predictions, using Monte Carlo Tree Search to decide queries.
- Presentation: mostly clear but omits important technical details.
- Empirical results: somewhat underwhelming, particularly compared to a threshold-based heuristic.
- Overall merit: approach is reasonable and worth further investigation.
- Equation (2): needs clarification—LHS should be conditioned on s and the handling of y in pR(ri | y, qi) is unclear.
- Algorithm 1: N(s) is not defined; include its definition for reproducibility.
- Threshold baseline: uses thresholds 0.3 and 0.98 with no justification; explain how these values were chosen.
- Online systems: clarify whether the used “online” systems are the best for the tasks; if so, reported gains may mainly come from human annotation—include error analysis.
- Recommendation: address the above points to strengthen the paper’s clarity and empirical claims.
