- Proposes a novel on-the-job learning framework enabling deployment from zero training examples, modeled as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Main contribution is an MCTS-based approximation algorithm to compute the otherwise intractable optimal policy.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear overview of problem and approach.  
- Technical core is sound and the model/algorithm are thoroughly explained.  
- Empirical results are promising, showing significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements over baselines.  
- Presentation needs more details on experimental setup and hyperparameter choices (e.g., how the threshold-based heuristic was chosen).  
- Notation errors/inconsistencies: Eq. (2) should be conditioned on s, disappearance of y in p_R(ri | y, qi) unclear, and N(s) in Algorithm 1 undefined.  
- Empirical gains are somewhat underwhelming relative to the threshold-based heuristic.  
- Interesting empirical observation: system cost decreases over time as the model improves.  
- Recommend more error analysis and comparisons to state-of-the-art ML systems on the evaluated tasks.  
- Overall assessment: promising and potentially significant contribution with minor revisions.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, thorough, and empirically promising.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient experimental/hyperparameter detail, notation issues, underwhelming comparison to heuristic, and lacking deeper error/SOTA analysis.
