- Proposes a hybrid crowd + online-trained model cast as a stochastic game over a CRF, using crowd queries and model predictions to maximize expected utility under a time budget.  
- System issues queries q for tokens x, receives responses r with utility U(q,r), and models dynamics P(y,r,t|x,q,s); optimization approximated via Monte Carlo Tree Search + TD learning (Algorithm 1).  
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and face identification using baselines: 1/3/5-vote humans, online learning (oracle), a “threshold” baseline, and the full LENSE model.  
- Results: LENSE outperforms all baselines except the 5-vote human ensemble; the threshold baseline performs nearly as well and has lower latency on NER, and sometimes better on face ID.  
- Shows that LENSE requires fewer crowdsourced labels over time and achieves high accuracy from the first examples by leveraging crowd labels while model uncertainty is high.  
- Strengths: highly original, principled approach with impressive results and strong potential for follow-up work and practical adoption.  
- Main weakness: poor clarity—introduction of continuous time mid-section complicates exposition and optimization while delivering minimal empirical benefit.  
- Recommendation to authors: present core method without continuous time as primary exposition and treat continuous-time as an extension to improve understandability and adoption.  
- Reporting issues: claim of “beats human performance” is incorrect for 5-vote NER; the 5-vote condition is missing from several tables/figures and should be included.  
- Formatting/data presentation: best-performing entries (e.g., 5-vote, Threshold latency for NER, LENSE for Face ID) should be bolded in tables.  
- Missing baseline: the Threshold baseline is absent from the sentiment experiment (Table 3/Fig 4) and should be added.  
- Experimental detail missing: unclear whether and how t_deadline was used in experiments and what stopping criteria determined when to report an answer.  
- Equation/notation problems: Eq (2) should include s in the distribution p(y,r,t|x,q,s); Eq (5)’s F(q) uses confusing notation (rename to avoid clash with query q).  
- Threshold baseline description likely contains a typographical error in the inequality for the stopping condition (sign/direction appears wrong).  
- Overall recommendation: accept the paper but request clarity fixes and reporting corrections (many fixable at camera-ready).
