This paper tackles the problem of creating high-accuracy classifiers beginning with zero labeled examples. A component problem in this paper is handling noisy crowd respondents, and while this topic has been considered from many angles in the literature, the timing and latency optimization factors of this paper are novel and interesting.

The authors propose a framework called on-the-job learning, which produces high-quality results from the start without requiring a trained model. When a new input arrives, the system can choose to asynchronously query the crowd on parts of the input it is uncertain about. After collecting enough evidence, the system makes a prediction. The goal is to maintain high accuracy by initially using the crowd as a crutch, but gradually becoming more self-sufficient as the model improves.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem it addresses is relevant to the NIPS community. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and propose a novel solution that combines ideas from Bayesian decision theory, stochastic games, and Monte Carlo tree search. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, and the results are promising.

However, I found some parts of the paper to be somewhat opaque. For example, the explanation of the game tree and the utility function could be improved. The example given in the paragraph starting at Line 154 is rather abstract and difficult to follow. It might be useful to use a specific query example with crowd responses and times similar to what they would be in a real case.

I also found it confusing that the threshold baseline is sometimes referred to as "entropic" and sometimes as "threshold" in Tables 2 and 3. It would be helpful to use consistent terminology throughout the paper.

Additionally, I am not sure why the related work is in Section 6. It would be more conventional to have it as a separate section, perhaps after the introduction.

Overall, I think this is a compelling problem, and the authors have made a good effort to address it. However, some parts of the paper could be improved for clarity and readability.
