This paper tackles the problem of creating high-accuracy classifiers starting with zero labeled examples. A key component of this work is handling noisy crowd respondents, and while this topic has been considered extensively in the literature, the timing and latency optimization factors explored here are novel and interesting (Line 086: Figure ??).

I appreciate the paper’s comparison of classifier accuracy to human performance. Table 2 reveals that the LENSE system outperforms a 3-vote baseline but underperforms a 5-vote baseline, which presents a significant comparison regarding the relative efficiencies of denoising crowd responses via additional consensus versus the LENSE system’s approach. The example provided in the paragraph starting at Line 154 is rather abstract and difficult to follow. To improve readability, I recommend illustrating the concept with a specific query example, including representative crowd responses and associated times from a realistic scenario. In Tables 2 and 3, the terminology used for the threshold baseline is inconsistent – it is sometimes referred to as “entropic” and sometimes as “threshold,” which is confusing. Finally, the placement of the related work in Section 5 is unusual. 

Overall, the paper addresses a compelling problem, but the explanations are somewhat opaque.
