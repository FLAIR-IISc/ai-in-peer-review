- Proposes a principled hybrid framework framing crowd + model interaction as a stochastic game, using a continuous-time CRF to formalize decreasing crowd reliance as model confidence grows.  
- Core insight: use heavy crowdsourcing early in training and reduce queries over time to balance accuracy and cost under a deadline constraint.  
- System optimizes query timing and quantity to maximize expected utility given costs and deadlines.  
- Tackles intractability with Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning (Algorithm 1).  
- Experiments (NER, sentiment, Face ID) show LENSE attains F1 near the 5-vote majority baseline while significantly reducing crowd dependence.  
- LENSE achieves high accuracy from the first example, unlike pure online learning.  
- A "threshold" baseline (no continuous time) performs nearly identically to LENSE on NER and sentiment and has notably lower latency, questioning the practical benefit of continuous time.  
- Continuous-time extension adds complexity with marginal empirical gains and should be presented as an optional refinement, not the core method.  
- Missing critical baselines/results: 5-vote human performance absent for Face ID and Sentiment; threshold baseline missing for Sentiment—these omissions hinder fair comparison.  
- Presentation issues: best-performing methods and latencies should be clearly highlighted in tables/figures for readability.  
- Minor technical fixes needed: define t_deadline; condition distributions on query timing `s` in Eq.(2); clarify notation in Eq.(5); correct the threshold uncertainty inequality.  
- Overall assessment: highly original and significant work that merits acceptance, but requires clarity improvements and inclusion/correction of omitted results before camera-ready.
