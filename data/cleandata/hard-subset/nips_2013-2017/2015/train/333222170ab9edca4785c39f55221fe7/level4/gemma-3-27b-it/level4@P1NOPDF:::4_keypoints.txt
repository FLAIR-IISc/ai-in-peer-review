- The "on-the-job learning" setting is clear for structured prediction (querying individual sequence elements) but its application to face recognition/non-structured tasks is unclear — it may simply query whole instances.  
- Because the model does not propagate information between examples in non-structured settings, the utility function’s rationale is questionable.  
- The threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters with no justification; their origin and sensitivity need investigation.  
- An additional baseline based on uncertainty sampling (active learning) should be included for comparison.  
- The paper overstates differences from active online learning — active learning can be adapted by treating sequences as active sets and updating incrementally, with existing theory to guide queries.  
- Overall, the framework appears novel but some details are unclear and its general applicability seems limited.
