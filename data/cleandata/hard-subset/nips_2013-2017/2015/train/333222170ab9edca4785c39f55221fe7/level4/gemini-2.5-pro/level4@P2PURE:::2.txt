This paper tackles the important problem of creating high-accuracy classifiers from scratch, without any initial labeled examples. A central component of this work involves handling noisy responses from crowd workers. While this topic has been considered from many angles, the authors' focus on incorporating timing and latency as optimization factors is a novel and interesting contribution.

The comparison of classifier accuracy to human performance is a valuable aspect of the paper. Table 2 presents an insightful result: the LENSE system outperforms the 3-vote baseline but underperforms the 5-vote baseline. This finding highlights a significant trade-off between the efficiency of the LENSE system's de-noising method versus the simpler approach of achieving consensus through additional crowd responses.

However, the clarity of the explanations could be improved. The example beginning on Line 154 is rather abstract and difficult to follow. To enhance readability, it would be beneficial to use a concrete example, illustrating a specific query with realistic crowd responses and corresponding latency data.

A few minor points:
*   In Tables 2 and 3, the baseline is inconsistently referred to as both "entropic" and "threshold," which may confuse the reader.
*   The placement of the related work in Section 5 is unconventional. Moving it earlier in the paper could provide readers with better context.
*   There appears to be a missing figure reference on Line 086.

Overall, the paper addresses a compelling problem, but its impact is diminished by explanations that are somewhat opaque. Improving the clarity of the examples and presentation would significantly strengthen the work.
