- Proposes a decision-theoretic framework for on-the-job learning that shifts from crowd labels to model predictions over time.  
- Core contribution: casting selective crowd-querying as a sequential decision problem solved with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS).  
- Strength: novel, principled formulation and generally clear presentation.  
- Weakness: notational/technical clarity issues, notably in Equation (2) regarding conditioning and marginalization.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 uses N(s) without definition—needs clarification.  
- Empirical concern: performance gains over a simple threshold baseline are modest, limiting practical impact.  
- Question about baseline thresholds (0.3 and 0.88): need rationale or validation procedure for those values.  
- Question about base learners: clarify whether the online models are state-of-the-art, since this affects interpretation of gains.  
- Suggestion: include error analysis showing when the MCTS querying strategy correctly queries humans versus the baseline.  
- Overall: promising and theoretically sound approach that warrants further investigation after addressing clarifications and experimental concerns.
