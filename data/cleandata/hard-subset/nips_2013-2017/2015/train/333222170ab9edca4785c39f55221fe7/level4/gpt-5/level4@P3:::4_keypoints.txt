- Proposes on-the-job learning that interleaves real-time crowd queries with prediction to achieve high initial accuracy without pre-training.  
- Frames decision making as a stochastic game under Bayesian decision theory trading accuracy, latency, and monetary cost.  
- Uses MCTS with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy for continuous-time decisions.  
- Demonstrates empirical cost reductions vs. full human annotation and accuracy gains over pure machine/human baselines on NER, sentiment, and face tasks; open-sourced as LENSE.  
- Integrates ideas from online learning, active learning, active classification, and crowdsourcing, emphasizing pre-prediction querying and latency-aware actions.  
- Differentiates from prior systems (Legion:AR, TurKontrol) by providing a structured decision-theoretic treatment for structured outputs and real-time latency.  
- Strength: Clear, principled formulation (stochastic game + Bayesian utility) and reasonable MCTS approximation for continuous-time.  
- Strength: Practical impact for rapid deployment with decreasing reliance on crowd over time.  
- Strength: Strong empirical cost-accuracy tradeoffs on NER and reproducibility via open-source code and worksheets.  
- Strength: Qualitative insights about parallel vs. sequential querying, redundancy for noise, and decreasing query rates as models improve.  
- Weakness: Unclear formalization for unstructured tasks (sentiment/faces) — need explicit instance-level action framing and utility behavior.  
- Weakness: Lack of sensitivity analysis and unclear choice/tuning of utility weights (wM, wT) and their effect on policies.  
- Weakness: Simplified response model (fixed 70% accuracy, uniform errors) — needs analysis of worker heterogeneity, class imbalance, and item difficulty.  
- Weakness: Time/response model details missing — need parametric form, fitted parameters, goodness-of-fit, and ablations (e.g., log-normal vs empirical).  
- Weakness: Baselines are weak/ad hoc (threshold baseline); must add stronger baselines like uncertainty sampling, greedy value-of-information, and offline-trained structured policies.  
- Weakness: MCTS implementation details omitted — report UCT constant, simulation budget, runtime, and ablations for progressive widening and query strategies.  
- Weakness: Missing dataset/model specifics — label sets, handling of “misc”, model architectures/features for pθ, and online update protocol.  
- Quality: Core idea is sound and plausible, but important implementation and experimental details need fuller documentation.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written but needs clearer formalization for non-structured cases, symbol definitions, and relocation of the 70% accuracy assumption into main text.  
- Originality: Novel framing combining real-time, latency- and cost-aware pre-prediction querying with decision-theoretic game and MCTS use.  
- Significance: High potential impact for human-in-the-loop rapid deployment if strengthened with baselines and analyses.  
- Suggestions: Formalize unstructured case, add active learning/classification baselines, run sensitivity analyses for wM/wT/response/time models, report MCTS hyperparameters and runtime, and model worker heterogeneity.  
- For acceptance: Principled formulation, empirical improvements, open-source code, and insightful qualitative behaviors.  
- Against acceptance: Incomplete comparisons to strong baselines, limited sensitivity analyses, unclear unstructured formalization, and missing experimental details.  
- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept — promising work that requires stronger baselines and more thorough analyses.
