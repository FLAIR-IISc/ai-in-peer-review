- Proposes an on-the-job learning framework that achieves high accuracy from the start by selectively querying crowd workers without initial labeled data.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to jointly optimize accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Introduces an approximate solver based on Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening.  
- Empirically evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, reporting large cost reductions (over an order of magnitude for NER) and accuracy gains (8% F1 vs. single-human, 28% vs. online learning).  
- Novel integration of Bayesian decision-theoretic stochastic games distinguishes it from prior online active learning and active classification work.  
- Addresses a significant practical gap for deploying AI with scarce labeled data, offering real-world impact (e.g., disaster response).  
- Experiments are well-designed with controlled crowdsourcing and the implementation is reproducible (CodaLab link provided).  
- Clarity issue: the example in Section 3 is overly abstract and should include a concrete instance with query times, responses, and label marginals.  
- Terminology inconsistency: the "threshold baseline" is labeled as "entropic" across tables, leading to reader confusion.  
- Structural issue: related work is misplaced (Section 6) and should be moved earlier to better contextualize contributions against recent NIPS work.  
- Overall recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to clarify the example and standardize terminology.
