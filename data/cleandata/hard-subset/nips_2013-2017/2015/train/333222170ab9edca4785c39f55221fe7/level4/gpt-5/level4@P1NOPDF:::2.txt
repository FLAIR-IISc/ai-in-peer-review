I appreciate that this paper directly compares classifier accuracy to human performance. In particular, Table 2 is informative: LENSE outperforms the 3-vote baseline but underperforms the 5-vote baseline. This highlights an important trade-off between denoising crowd responses by increasing consensus versus using the LENSE approach, and suggests a meaningful comparison of their relative efficiencies.

However, parts of the exposition are hard to follow. The example beginning at Line 154 is quite abstract; a concrete, end-to-end query example—with representative crowd responses and realistic response times—would greatly improve readability and make the method’s mechanics clearer.

There are also terminology inconsistencies that cause confusion. In Tables 2 and 3, the threshold baseline is referred to as “entropic” in some places and “threshold” in others; please standardize the naming and clearly define each baseline once. Additionally, at Line 086 there is an unresolved reference (“Figure ??”) that should be fixed.

I am also unsure why the related work appears in Section 5. Given how central prior work on crowd noise modeling and active/real-time decision-making is to your contribution, placing the related work earlier (e.g., after the introduction) would better situate your approach and clarify how your timing and latency optimization choices differ from or build upon existing methods.

Overall, this is a compelling problem: creating high-accuracy classifiers starting from zero labeled examples, while handling noisy crowd respondents. Although crowd noise has been widely studied, the paper’s focus on timing and latency optimization is novel and interesting. With clearer explanations, a concrete running example, consistent baseline terminology, and earlier positioning of related work, the paper would be much easier to follow and its contributions more evident.
