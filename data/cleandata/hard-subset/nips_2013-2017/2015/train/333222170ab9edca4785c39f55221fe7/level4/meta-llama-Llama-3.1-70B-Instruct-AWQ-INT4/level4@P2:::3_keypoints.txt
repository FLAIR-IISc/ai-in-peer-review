- Paper proposes a novel hybrid system that combines crowdsourced labels and an online-trained model, relying on the crowd early and the model more as confidence grows.
- Formulates the problem as a stochastic game with system and crowd as players, using a CRF prediction model and utility U(q,r) for query outcomes.
- Queries are issued over time with response latencies, and utility is optimized with respect to a deadline t_deadline, deciding query count and timing.
- Computing expected utility requires a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s); continuous-time makes optimization intractable so MCTS + TD learning (Algorithm 1) is used as an approximation.
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and face identification compare majority-vote humans, online oracle, a "threshold" baseline (no continuous time), and full LENSE model.
- Results show the method is original and effective, and reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Major clarity issue: introduction of continuous time mid-paper complicates exposition and algorithms while offering minimal empirical benefit.
- The threshold baseline often matches or beats LENSE in latency and has roughly equivalent performance, especially notable in NER and face-ID tasks.
- For many tasks the simpler threshold approach may be sufficient; paper would be more impactful if core method were presented without continuous time and continuous-time treated as an extension.
- Reporting issues: claim that model "beats human performance" is false for 5-vote NER; 5-vote cases are missing for Face-ID and Sentiment in several tables/figures.
- Presentation errors: best results (5-vote) and certain latency values should be bolded in tables; Threshold baseline is missing from the sentiment experiment results.
- Method details unclear: unclear whether and how t_deadline was used in experiments and what criterion reported answers met (entropy threshold or deadline).
- Equation issues: eq.(2) should include s in the conditioning (p(y,r,t|x,q,s)); eq.(5) uses q as a confusing label for a distribution function.
- Threshold baseline description likely has a sign/error in the inequality expressing uncertainty reduction.
- Strengths summarized: novel hybrid approach, strong empirical results versus multiple baselines, and high originality.
- Weaknesses summarized: continuous time adds complexity with little benefit, threshold baseline often competitive or better, and multiple reporting/clarity issues.
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
- Overall recommendation: accept, but revise to clarify/remove/justify continuous-time component, fix experimental reporting, and address minor notation/description errors.
