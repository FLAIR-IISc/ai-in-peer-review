The authors present a setting termed "on the job learning," in which annotator queries are issued between receiving an example and producing a prediction.

While the algorithm is straightforward for structured prediction tasks, where elements of a sequence are queried, it is not at all clear how this was instantiated for the face recognition task—was the entire instance simply queried? In the non-structured case, the model would not propagate information across neighboring positions (examples), so the utility function seems ill-specified.

The threshold baseline includes two parameters that appear arbitrary. How were these values selected, and how sensitive are the results to them? An additional baseline using uncertainty sampling, as in active learning, would be informative.

Finally, although the authors emphasize the distinction between this setting and active online learning, active learning could be adapted quite directly to this domain by treating the active set as a sequence. For example, the model could be updated as responses arrive and then produce a prediction at the end. While this would not capture the temporal nature of the task, there is ample theory to guide query selection. In sum, the framework appears novel, but some details are unclear and its applicability seems fairly limited.
