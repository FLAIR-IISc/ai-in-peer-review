- Introduces an “on-the-job learning” framework where a model can query an annotator between input receipt and prediction; demonstrated on structured prediction and face recognition.  
- Core idea of strategic querying during inference is appealing but needs stronger empirical validation.  
- Algorithm is clearly described for structured prediction (queries on output elements).  
- Adaptation to non-structured face recognition is unclear: unclear what the annotator is asked and how queries/answers are defined.  
- Utility function for face recognition feels ill-defined because information isn’t propagated between examples as in the structured case; a concrete query/response example is needed.  
- Baseline uses a threshold-based querying strategy with two parameters whose selection appears arbitrary and unjustified.  
- Paper lacks sensitivity analysis for those threshold parameters to show robustness of results.  
- Missing comparison to standard uncertainty sampling / active learning baselines; such baselines would provide important context.  
- Authors should discuss how traditional active/online learning could be adapted to this setting and clarify advantages of the proposed method over those alternatives.  
- Overall: potentially novel contribution but limited by lack of clarity on face recognition application and insufficient baseline/comparative experiments; needs more explanation and stronger evaluation.
