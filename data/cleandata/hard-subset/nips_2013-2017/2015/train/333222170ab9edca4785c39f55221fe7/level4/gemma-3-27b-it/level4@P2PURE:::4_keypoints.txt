- The “on the job learning” algorithm is well-defined for structured sequence tasks but its application to face recognition is unclear—what is the queried instance in this non-structured setting?

- In non-structured tasks information does not propagate between examples, calling into question the validity and utility of the proposed query/utility function.

- The threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters; the authors must justify their origin and report sensitivity analyses.

- Add a baseline based on uncertainty sampling/active learning-style query selection for a stronger comparison.

- The authors overstate differences from active online learning: active learning can be adapted by treating the sequence as the active set and updating the model after responses.

- The framework may be novel but lacks clarity in important details and appears limited in applicability.
