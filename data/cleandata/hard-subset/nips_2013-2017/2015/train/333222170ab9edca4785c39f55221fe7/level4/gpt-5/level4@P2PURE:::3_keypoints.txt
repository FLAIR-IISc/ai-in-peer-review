- Proposes a hybrid system that combines crowdsourced labels with an incrementally (online) trained model, relying on the crowd during warm-up and reducing crowd involvement as model confidence increases.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game over a CRF where the system issues timed queries, receives responses with latencies, and maximizes expected utility subject to a deadline.  
- Computes expected utility via a simulation dynamics model P(y, r, t | x, q, s) and approximates optimization using Monte Carlo Tree Search plus TD learning (Algorithm 1).  
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment, and face-ID tasks, comparing majority-vote humans (n=1,3,5), online learning with oracle labels, a “Threshold” (no continuous time) baseline, and the full continuous-time model (LENSE).  
- Empirical results: LENSE outperforms most methods but not the 5-vote human condition; the Threshold baseline is close and has lower latency on NER and better performance on face-ID.  
- Strength: original, principled, and significant contribution—first complete proposal for hybrid crowd+online learning and merits acceptance.  
- Main weakness: clarity—introducing continuous time mid-Section 3 complicates exposition and optimization while delivering only marginal empirical gains.  
- Suggestion: present the non-continuous Threshold approach as the core method and treat continuous time as an extension to simplify understanding and encourage adoption.  
- Reporting issues: incorrect claim of “beats human performance” (fails vs 5-vote); 5-vote condition missing in several tables/figures for Face-ID and Sentiment; bolding errors in Table 2; Threshold results missing from sentiment experiments.  
- Minor methodological/notation issues: unclear whether and how t_deadline was used in experiments; equation (2) should include s; equation (5) uses confusing notation q for a distribution; Threshold baseline inequality sign/formula is incorrect.  
- Overall: principled and compelling method with strong results, but continuous-time complexity, clarity, and reporting errors should be fixed.
