- Summary: Proposes "on-the-job learning" to deploy high-accuracy systems without labeled data by querying a crowd at test time, formalized as a stochastic game with a Bayesian utility balancing accuracy, cost, and latency, approximated via MCTS.

- Strength: Significant/original — novel, practical formulation for bootstrapping ML systems with no initial data.

- Strength: Quality of approach — principled Bayesian decision-theoretic framing and suitable use of MCTS for the sequential decision problem.

- Strength: Empirical results — strong NER performance with >10x cost reduction vs. 5-vote baseline and improved F1 over a single expert.

- Strength: Reproducibility — code and data released on CodaLab.

- Weakness: Clarity — several technical/modeling and algorithmic details are insufficiently explained, hindering understanding and reproducibility.

- Weakness: Comparison to heuristic baseline — modest gains over a threshold-based heuristic on some tasks raise questions about the practical benefit of the MCTS complexity.

- Weakness: Baseline strength — choice and power of the online-learning baseline (e.g., CRF) may not represent state-of-the-art, complicating attribution of gains to the framework.

- Weakness: Lack of error analysis — missing deeper analysis of failure modes and instances where the system continues to rely on crowd input.

- Suggestion: Clarify the marginalization used to compute p(r' | x, r, q) from the joint model (Equation 3).

- Suggestion: Explicitly confirm UCT/UCT-style notation and visit-count definitions in Algorithm 1 (line 5).

- Suggestion: Justify threshold-baseline hyperparameters (e.g., 0.3 reduction, 0.98 confidence) and describe tuning procedure.

- Suggestion: Compare the online baseline to established results and add qualitative error examples showing when LENSE queries the crowd successfully versus baseline failures.

- Overall recommendation: Novel and well-motivated contribution with strong NER results; recommend acceptance pending clarifications of technical details and stronger baseline/error analyses.
