The authors propose a scenario called "on the job learning", where annotators are queried between receiving an example and outputting a prediction. While the algorithm's functionality is clear for structured prediction tasks, where elements of a sequence are queried, its application to the face recognition task is not well explained - specifically, whether the instance itself was being queried. In non-structured settings, the model does not propagate information between adjacent positions (examples), which raises questions about the utility function's applicability. 

The threshold baseline has two parameters with seemingly arbitrary values; it would be beneficial to know their origin and the sensitivity of the results to these parameters. An additional baseline of uncertainty sampling, similar to that used in active learning, could provide further insights. The authors emphasize the distinction between this setting and active online learning, but it appears that active learning could be adapted to this domain by treating the active set as a sequence. For instance, the model could be updated upon receiving responses and then output a prediction at the end. Although this approach would not capture the task's temporal nature, it could leverage existing theory for choosing the query set. Overall, the framework seems novel, but some details are unclear, and its applicability appears limited.
