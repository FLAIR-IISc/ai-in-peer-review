- Presents an "on-the-job learning" framework that queries crowd annotators at prediction time to enable deployment without initial labeled data.  
- Balances latency, cost, and accuracy using Bayesian decision theory and approximates the optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search.  
- Formalizes and builds on prior heuristic work ([6]) to provide a principled human-in-the-loop prediction method.  
- Differentiates from related online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing work by actively seeking information during prediction and adapting from observed data.  
- Models the problem as a stochastic game to clearly trade off competing objectives in a principled way.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on three tasks (NER, sentiment, image classification), showing cost reductions versus full annotation and improvements over online learning and single-label baselines.  
- Releases an open-source implementation (LENSE), supporting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: application to image classification is under-explained; unclear how the query mechanism and utility apply to non-structured prediction.  
- Weakness: threshold-baseline parameters appear arbitrarily chosen and lack sensitivity analysis or justification.  
- Weakness: lacks a direct comparison to an adapted active learning baseline that incrementally updates on the same input sequence.  
- Weakness: choice of AdaGrad for learning the CRF is noted but not justified.  
- Acceptance recommendation contingent on clarifying image-task application and justifying or analyzing threshold baseline parameters.
