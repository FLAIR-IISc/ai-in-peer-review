- Presents a hybrid method that combines crowdsourced labels with an incrementally trained online model, querying humans less as model confidence grows, framed as a stochastic game using a CRF and optimized via MCTS + TD learning due to continuous-time intractability.

- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face-ID; compared LENSE to baselines (majority vote with 1/3/5 votes, online learning with true labels, and a “threshold” baseline).

- LENSE outperforms most baselines but is beaten by the 5-vote crowdsourcing method.

- The simpler threshold baseline achieves nearly identical performance to LENSE and often has lower latency; on face-ID it slightly outperforms LENSE.

- Strength: highly original, principled formulation integrating ML and crowdsourcing; likely to stimulate research and practical adoption and is a strong accept candidate.

- Weakness: poor clarity, especially the continuous-time formulation (Section 3) which adds complexity but gives minimal empirical benefit.

- Recommendation to restructure: present the simpler threshold model first and introduce the continuous-time model as an extension for clarity and adoption.

- Authors should be more transparent in results discussion about the strong performance of the threshold baseline, even on sequence-dependent tasks.

- Claim that the model “beats human performance” is unsupported since the 5-vote baseline outperforms it on NER.

- The 5-vote baseline is missing from Face-ID and Sentiment experiments/tables/figures and should be included.

- Table 2 formatting: bold best-performing result per column (including 5-vote for accuracy and Threshold for NER latency).

- Threshold baseline is missing entirely from the sentiment analysis results (Table 3 and Figure 4) and should be added.

- Unclear experimental detail: what t_deadline was used and whether reporting used a fixed deadline or an entropy threshold stopping rule.

- Equation issue: Eq.(2) likely omits conditioning on query time s — should be p(y,r,t | x,q,s).

- Notation conflict: Eq.(5) uses q in F(q) while q already denotes a query — change the letter for the nominal distribution.

- Threshold description bug: the stopping-condition inequality appears wrong; it should probably be (1 - p_theta(y_i|x)) * 0.3^m <= (1 - 0.88) rather than >= 0.88.
