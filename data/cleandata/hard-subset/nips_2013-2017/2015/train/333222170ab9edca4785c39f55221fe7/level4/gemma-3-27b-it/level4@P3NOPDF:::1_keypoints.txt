- Proposes a theoretically grounded on-the-job learning method that transitions from crowd labels to a selective learned model using MCTS.  
- Empirical gains are weak and not convincingly better than a simple threshold-based baseline.  
- Presentation is generally clear but omits crucial technical details that must be clarified.  
- Equation (2) is unclear: it should be conditioned on state s, and the role or elimination of y in p_R(ri | y, qi) needs explanation.  
- Algorithm 1 uses N(s) without definition—clarify whether this denotes MCTS visit counts or something else.  
- Threshold baseline uses values (0.3, 0.88) with no justification—explain how these were chosen (e.g., cross-validation).  
- Need stronger comparison to state-of-the-art online systems and deeper error analysis to show where the method truly helps (e.g., ambiguous or edge cases).  
- Overall: promising and theoretically solid, but requires clearer technical details and more convincing empirical evaluation.
