- Paper proposes "on-the-job learning" (queries during prediction) well-motivated for structured prediction but application to face recognition is unclear.  
- Ambiguity whether queries in the non-structured face task target whole instances (labels) or individual features.  
- In non-structured settings there are no temporal dependencies, so the model cannot propagate information between examples, undermining the utility function.  
- Without propagation, the rationale for querying specific sequence positions in non-structured tasks collapses, indicating a framework–task mismatch.  
- Threshold-based baseline is unjustified: its two parameters seem arbitrary with no explanation of selection or sensitivity analysis.  
- Lack of an uncertainty sampling baseline (standard in active learning) weakens empirical comparisons.  
- Claimed distinction from active online learning is overstated—active learning can be adapted to query sequence positions, calling novelty into question.  
- Framework is conceptually interesting but hampered by implementation ambiguities and limited scope; unclear handling of non-structured tasks undermines generality.  
- Narrow focus on interleaving queries with predictions restricts applicability despite the temporal novelty.  
- Paper needs clearer technical justification and broader validation (clarify face recognition setup and stronger baselines) to support its significance and novelty.
