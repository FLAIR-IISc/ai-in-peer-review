- Main contribution: a more theoretically grounded formulation of the on-the-job learner from [22], using Monte Carlo Tree Search to decide when to query the crowd.  
- Method: system starts by querying the crowd for outputs, then progressively predicts itself and queries infrequently based on a decision model.  
- Presentation: generally clear but omits several important implementation/details.  
- Empirical results: improvements over a threshold-based heuristic are modest and somewhat underwhelming.  
- Overall assessment: approach is reasonable and merits further investigation despite mixed results.  
- Clarification needed (Eq. 2): left-hand side should be conditioned on s, and it’s unclear how y in p_R(r_i | y, q_i) disappears.  
- Clarification needed (Alg. 1): what is N(s)?  
- Clarification needed (baseline thresholds): how were the 0.3 and 0.88 values in the threshold baseline obtained?  
- Clarification/analysis needed (tasks and baselines): are the “online” systems the best machine-learned models for these tasks; if so gains may be due to human annotation—provide error analysis and examples.
