- Tackles creating high-accuracy classifiers from zero labeled examples, focusing on noisy crowd respondents with novel timing/latency optimization.  
- Proposes "on-the-job learning": asynchronously query crowd on uncertain parts and gradually rely on the model.  
- Frames the task as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search approximation to compute the optimal policy due to intractability of exact solution.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification datasets against baselines including human, online learning, and threshold methods.  
- Empirical results show the proposed approach outperforms baselines in accuracy and cost.  
- Strength: principled multi-objective balancing via Bayesian decision theory and MCTS.  
- Strength: multi-dataset evaluation enhances credibility and generality of results.  
- Weakness: illustrative example (Line 154) is abstract and hard to follow; needs a concrete query/response/time example.  
- Weakness: related work section lacks sufficient context and detailed explanations of compared approaches.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Clarity is generally good but some explanations could be improved for readability.  
- Originality: introduces novel timing and latency optimization in crowd-handling despite prior work on noisy respondents.  
- Significance: addresses an important ML problem with potentially wide applicability.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, technical soundness, clear presentation, and strong evaluation.  
- Rejection arguments: readability issues, weak related work discussion, and limited discussion of limitations and future work.
