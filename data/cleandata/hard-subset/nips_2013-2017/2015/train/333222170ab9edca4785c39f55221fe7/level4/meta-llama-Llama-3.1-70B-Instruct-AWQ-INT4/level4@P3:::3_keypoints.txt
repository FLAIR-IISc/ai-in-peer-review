- Proposes a hybrid system that combines crowdsourced labels with an incrementally (online) trained model, relying on the crowd early and the model more as it learns.  
- Core technical framing casts the problem as a stochastic game between the system and crowd using a CRF prediction model.  
- Authors situate the work clearly relative to prior NIPS and other literature; reviewer demonstrates solid understanding.  
- Technical approach is sound and “elegantly” models data, true labels, and crowd responses.  
- Method is original, novel, and shows promising, practically relevant results.  
- Main clarity concern: introduction of continuous time is confusing and likely unnecessary—the core method could be presented without it.  
- Reviewer observes continuous time provides minimal empirical benefit.  
- Minor methodological issues: some notation and equations lack clarity and could be improved.  
- Presentation suggestions: include the 5-vote case for Face-ID and Sentiment tasks and bold best results in tables.  
- Overall evaluation: strong paper with balanced strengths and weaknesses (Quality 9/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10).  
- Recommendation: accept, provided authors address clarity (especially continuous time) and the minor method/presentation comments.
