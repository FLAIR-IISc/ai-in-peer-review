- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy with zero training data, use real-time crowdsourcing and learn from interactions to become autonomous.
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game and applies Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, monetary cost, and latency.
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy.
- Distinguishes itself from standard online/active learning by allowing queries before making a prediction, maintaining high performance during deployment.

- Strength: Addresses a practical, novel problem of accurate deployment with no initial data.
- Strength: Provides an elegant, principled theoretical formulation (stochastic game + Bayesian utility).
- Strength: MCTS is a well-motivated approximation given intractability of the exact solution.
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments on NER, sentiment, and image classification show cost reductions and accuracy improvements.
- Strength: Code, data, and experiments are publicly available, aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness: Paper omits detailed derivation for computing p(r′ | x, r, q) by marginalizing y, harming clarity and reproducibility.
- Weakness: Baselines are weakly justified—threshold baseline uses unexplained heuristic constants.
- Weakness: Online learning baseline is not directly comparable because it cannot query at test time, so gains may reflect setting differences.
- Weakness: Empirical analysis lacks depth—modest gains over simple heuristics (e.g., sentiment) question the necessity of MCTS complexity.
- Weakness: No error analysis to show which examples LENSE helps or when it fails to query and errs.
