- Main contribution: a more theoretically grounded formulation of the on-the-job learner from [22], moving from crowd outputs to model predictions over time.
- Approach: casts the crowd-query decision as a decision problem solved with Monte Carlo Tree Search.
- Presentation is generally good but omits important implementation/details.
- Empirical results are somewhat underwhelming compared to a threshold-based heuristic.
- Overall assessment: approach is reasonable and promising, merits further study and discussion.
- Clarification needed: Equation (2) is unclear — LHS should be conditioned on s, and how does y in p_R(r_i | y, q_i) disappear?
- Clarification needed: what does N(s) represent in Algorithm 1?
- Clarification needed: how were the threshold baseline values 0.3 and 0.88 chosen?
- Clarification/analysis needed: what are the “online” systems used — are they state-of-the-art for these tasks; if not, why; if yes, then gains may come purely from human annotation — provide examples and error analysis.
