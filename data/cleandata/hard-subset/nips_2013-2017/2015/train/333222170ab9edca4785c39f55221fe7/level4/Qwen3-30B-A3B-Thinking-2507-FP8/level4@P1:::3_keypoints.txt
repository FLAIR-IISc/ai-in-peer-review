- Introduces "on-the-job learning": combining crowdsourced labels with incrementally trained models to operate without initial labeled data.  
- Core idea: strategically query the crowd for uncertain predictions early, then reduce reliance as the model improves.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game between system and crowd using Bayesian decision theory to balance accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- System issues targeted queries for uncertain input components and updates its model with crowd responses.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy and handle continuous time.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face classification against baselines (majority vote n=1,3,5; online oracle; threshold; full model).  
- Results: LENSE outperforms most baselines (except 5-vote on NER), achieves 88.4% F1 on NER, and reduces costs >10× on NER and ~2× on sentiment/face for similar accuracy.  
- Demonstrates decreasing query rate over time and faster reduction in crowd dependency with richer features (RNN).  
- Significant, practical contribution with principled framing and open-source implementation to aid adoption.  
- Clarity issues: continuous-time formulation (Section 3) adds complexity without clear benefit to results.  
- The threshold baseline is a strong, simpler alternative and is under-emphasized despite comparable/better performance on some tasks.  
- Presentation problems: missing 5-vote and threshold baselines in several tables/figures; latency values not consistently highlighted.  
- Minor technical clarifications needed: unspecified t_deadline; Equation (2) should include time variables; confusing notation in Equation (5); possible sign/error in threshold baseline condition.  
- Overall assessment: original, significant, technically sound work deserving acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and comparisons.
