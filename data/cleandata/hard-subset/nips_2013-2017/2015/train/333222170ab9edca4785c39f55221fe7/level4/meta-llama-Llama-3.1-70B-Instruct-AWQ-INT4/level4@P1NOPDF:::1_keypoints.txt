- Main contribution: provides a more theoretically sound formulation of an "on-the-job" learner that shifts from crowd answers to model predictions using a Monte Carlo Tree Search decision policy.  
- Learner behavior: starts by relying on crowd outputs and gradually queries the crowd only for inputs deemed necessary (low-confidence cases).  
- Presentation: generally clear but omits important details in some areas.  
- Empirical results: improvements over a threshold-based heuristic are modest/underwhelming.  
- Recommendation: approach is reasonable and merits further investigation despite mixed results.  
- Eq. (2) issue: left-hand side should be conditioned on s and the elimination of y from p(R|y, q_i) is unclear.  
- Algorithm clarity: N(s) in Algorithm 1 is undefined and needs specification.  
- Baseline explanation: the threshold baseline uses values 0.3 and 0.88 without justification—need to explain how these were chosen.  
- Online-system validity: unclear whether the "online" systems are the best possible models for the tasks; if not, gains may stem from human annotation, so provide examples and error analysis.
