- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy accurate AI from zero examples by querying crowds during prediction, framed as a stochastic game balancing latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing substantial cost reductions and accuracy gains versus baselines.  
- Related to online learning, active learning, and active classification; closest prior work is Legion:AR but without the paper's theoretical foundation.  
- Strength: Addresses a real-world need for deploying models with minimal initial data (e.g., disaster relief).  
- Strength: Provides a principled Bayesian decision-theoretic framework for tradeoffs among objectives.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results (over an order of magnitude cost reduction on NER; +8% F1 vs single-human; +28% vs online learning).  
- Strength: Open-source implementation (LENS) improves reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Application to face recognition is unclear—face classification is non-structured, so the structured prediction formulation and utility rationale need clarification.  
- Weakness: Threshold baseline uses arbitrary parameters (0.3 and 0.98) without justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: Distinction from active learning is not fully convincing; paper needs a rigorous comparison (e.g., uncertainty sampling adapted to streaming).  
- Weakness: Applicability may be limited to structured prediction; paper should explicitly discuss limitations for non-structured tasks.  
- Overall: Novel, well-motivated framework with solid theory and strong empirical support, but needs clarification on non-structured tasks, baseline choices, and active-learning comparisons.  
- Quality: Technical approach is sound and experiments are well-supported; main technical concern is non-structured task applicability.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written but requires clarification on face recognition and threshold parameters.  
- Originality: Novel relative to online learning and active classification.  
- Significance: Addresses an important practical problem with potential for large cost savings.  
- Pro: Novel framework, strong results, theoretical foundation, reproducible implementation.  
- Con: Unclear handling of non-structured tasks, arbitrary baseline parameters, incomplete active learning comparison.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify face recognition application, justify threshold parameters, and include stronger active-learning comparisons.
