- Introduces "on the job learning": querying annotators between receiving an example and making a prediction.
- Method is clearly applicable to structured prediction (querying sequence elements), but its use in face recognition is unclear—was the queried instance the sole consideration?
- In non-structured settings the model does not propagate information between adjacent positions, casting doubt on the utility function's applicability.
- The threshold baseline depends on two seemingly arbitrary parameters; the source of these values and a sensitivity analysis are needed.
- A baseline comparison with uncertainty sampling (active learning) would strengthen the evaluation.
- Active learning methods could be adapted by treating the active set as a sequence and updating the model after receiving responses.
- Such an adaptation would not capture temporal aspects of the task, but existing theory for query-set selection could be leveraged.
- Overall, the framework appears novel but requires clarifications and its applicability seems limited.
