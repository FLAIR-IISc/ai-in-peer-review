The main contribution of this paper is a more theoretically sound formulation of an “on the job” learner, building upon the work proposed in [22]. This learner begins by relying on crowdsourced outputs, but progressively develops its own predictive capabilities and reduces its reliance on querying the crowd. The decision of when to query the crowd for a given input is elegantly modeled as a decision problem solved via Monte Carlo Tree Search.

The presentation is generally good, though lacking in crucial details. The empirical results, when compared to a threshold-based heuristic, are somewhat underwhelming. However, the approach appears reasonable and warrants further investigation.

Please address/clarify the following points:

1. Equation (2) is unclear. The left-hand side should be conditioned on the state *s*. Additionally, the variable *y* in *p{R}(ri | y, qi)* appears to be unused; please explain its purpose or remove it.
2. What is the definition of *N(s)* in Algorithm 1?
3. How were the values of 0.3 and 0.88 determined for the threshold baseline?
4. I am not familiar with the specific tasks employed in this evaluation. Are the “online” systems used as baselines the state-of-the-art machine learned systems for these tasks? If not, please justify this choice. If they *are* state-of-the-art, any performance gain achieved through the addition of human annotation should be clearly attributable to the selective querying strategy. Providing examples of such cases, alongside an error analysis, would be beneficial.

This paper presents a theoretical formulation of an “on the job” learner that transitions from a purely crowdsourced system to a learned model that selectively queries the crowd only for low-confidence inputs. The empirical results are mixed, but this work has the potential to stimulate valuable discussion in this area.
