- Proposes a novel hybrid approach (LENSE) that combines crowdsourced labels and an incrementally trained model, relying on the crowd early and reducing reliance as model confidence grows.  
- Formalizes the interaction as a stochastic game using a CRF prediction model with the system and crowd as players.  
- System issues token queries and selects actions to maximize expected utility, accounting for query timing and response latency.  
- Expected utility is computed via a simulation dynamics model with approximations using Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face identification, compared against majority-vote baselines (1/3/5), online learning with true labels, a "threshold" baseline, and LENSE.  
- LENSE outperforms most baselines but is beaten by the 5-vote crowdsourced version; the "threshold" baseline performs nearly as well and often yields better latency for NER.  
- Shows LENSE uses fewer crowd labels over time and attains high initial accuracy by leveraging crowd expertise when model uncertainty is high.  
- Paper is highly original and significant with impressive results and potential for practical adoption.  
- Major clarity concern: introducing continuous time (Section 3) complicates formulation and optimization while providing minimal experimental benefit; recommend presenting core method without continuous time and treating continuous time as an extension.  
- Results reporting issues: incorrect claim of beating human performance (not true for 5-vote NER); 5-vote results missing for Face-ID and Sentiment in tables/figures; Threshold baseline missing for sentiment; recommend bolding best (5-vote) results in Table 2.  
- Minor methodological/reporting fixes needed: clarify t_deadline and its experimental value/criteria; include variable s in eq.(2); replace confusing use of q in eq.(5); correct the Threshold baseline expression to (1 - p_theta(yi|x))*0.3^m <= (1-0.88).  
- Overall recommendation: improve clarity, emphasize the strong Threshold baseline, and fix reporting/notation issues to increase impact and accessibility.
