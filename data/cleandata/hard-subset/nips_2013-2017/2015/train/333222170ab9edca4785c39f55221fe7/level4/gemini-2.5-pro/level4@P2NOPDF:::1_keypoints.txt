- Presents a theoretically grounded "on-the-job" learning formulation that begins with crowd labels and transitions to model predictions, querying the crowd when confidence is low.  
- Models the query decision as a decision-theoretic planning problem solved with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS).  
- Strength: novel and principled approach; framing query decisions as planning with MCTS is elegant.  
- Weakness: experimental gains are marginal over a simpler threshold-based heuristic, calling practical benefits of added complexity into question.  
- Weakness: presentation omits key details needed for full understanding and reproducibility.  
- Request: clarify derivation in Equation (2), including conditioning on state s and how y is marginalized in p_R(r_i | y, q_i).  
- Request: define the variable N(s) used in Algorithm 1.  
- Request: justify choice of threshold baseline parameters (0.3 and 0.88) and state whether they were tuned on validation data.  
- Request: provide more context on the online base learners (are they state-of-the-art?) and add error analysis showing which examples trigger human queries and why.  
- Overall: a reasonable, valuable starting formulation that merits further investigation and clarification.
