- Paper proposes LENSE, a hybrid system combining crowdsourced labels with an incrementally trained online model, reducing human reliance as model confidence improves.
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game between system and crowd using a CRF prediction model, where queries q receive responses r with utility U(q,r).
- Incorporates continuous time: queries issued at time s receive responses at time t, and the system optimizes strategies to meet a deadline t_deadline.
- Exact expected-utility optimization is intractable; authors approximate using Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and Temporal-Difference (TD) learning.
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment analysis, and face identification, compared to baselines including 1/3/5-vote human consensus, an online model trained with true labels, and a "threshold" baseline.
- Results: full LENSE outperforms all baselines except 5-vote consensus; the Threshold baseline is comparable and has notably lower latency on NER.
- Demonstrates progressive reduction in crowd labels as the model improves and high initial accuracy by leveraging crowd when uncertainty is high.
- Reviewer finds the paper interesting and significant, recommending acceptance for the conference.
- Major weakness: clarity—introduction of continuous time complicates formulation and optimization without clear empirical benefit.
- Empirical concern: the Threshold (time-agnostic) baseline achieves nearly identical or better performance in some tasks, questioning the value of continuous time.
- Suggest restructuring: present the simpler Threshold model first, then treat continuous-time LENSE as an extension to improve accessibility and adoption.
- Experimental/reporting issues: the claim that the model "beats human performance" is not supported by NER results where 5-vote wins.
- Missing baseline results: 5-vote baseline absent for Face-ID and Sentiment in some tables/figures; Threshold baseline missing entirely from sentiment experiments.
- Presentation issues: Table 2 boldfacing inconsistent; role and usage of t_deadline in experiments unclear (how final answer is triggered).
- Technical/notation issues: Equation (2) likely missing conditioning on s; Equation (5) uses confusing F(q) notation; Threshold baseline description likely contains a typo in the inequality involving m and uncertainty.
