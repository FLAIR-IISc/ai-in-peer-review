- Proposes "on-the-job learning" with clear algorithm for structured prediction tasks.  
- Application to face recognition is unclear — ambiguous whether the instance itself was queried.  
- In non-structured settings the model cannot propagate information between adjacent examples, making the utility function ill-defined.  
- The threshold baseline uses two arbitrary parameters without justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- A baseline using uncertainty sampling (active learning) would be a stronger, missing comparison.  
- Authors downplay active online learning, but active learning could be adapted by treating the active set as a sequence and updating on responses, leveraging existing query-selection theory.  
- The framework seems novel but lacks clarity on key implementation details and has limited applicability.
