- Main contribution: a theoretically grounded formulation of an “on-the-job learner” that transitions from crowd annotations to autonomous predictions.  
- Method: casts when-to-query decisions as a sequential decision problem solved with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS).  
- Presentation: generally clear but omits important details needed to fully evaluate the approach.  
- Empirical result: performance vs a simple threshold-based heuristic is somewhat underwhelming.  
- Overall assessment: framework is sensible and worth further exploration despite mixed empirical gains.  
- Request 1 (Eq. 2): clarify conditioning—LHS appears unconditioned on s and p_R(r_i | y, q_i) seems to drop dependence on y without shown steps/assumptions.  
- Request 2 (Alg. 1): define N(s) explicitly (visit count, rollout budget, or other), and specify initialization and update rules.  
- Request 3 (threshold baseline): describe how thresholds 0.3 and 0.88 were chosen (validation tuning, grid search, heuristic) and confirm fair tuning under same constraints (cost/latency).  
- Request 4a (online systems): state whether compared “online” systems are the strongest available models or justify selection if not.  
- Request 4b (error analysis): provide examples where the hybrid system improves over the standalone model and where it fails or introduces errors.  
- Request 4c (policy comparison): give a brief error analysis showing when crowd queries help and whether the MCTS policy outperforms confidence-thresholding.  
- Recommended fixes: address missing derivation details, define N(s), report baseline selection procedure, and include focused error analysis to strengthen the paper.
