- Paper proposes a theoretically grounded on-the-job learner that transitions from crowd labels to model predictions, using Monte Carlo Tree Search to decide when to query the crowd.
- Presentation is generally clear but omits important technical details.
- Empirical comparison against a threshold-based heuristic yields underwhelming results.
- Recommendation: Equation (2) is unclear — LHS should be conditioned on s and the elimination of y in p(R|y, q_i) is not explained.
- Recommendation: Definition of N(s) in Algorithm 1 is missing.
- Recommendation: Origin and justification of the 0.3 and 0.88 values used in the threshold baseline are not provided.
- Recommendation: Unclear whether the “online” systems in evaluation are the best available for the task; authors should justify selection and provide examples/error analysis to show where improvements come from.
- Overall assessment: Approach is reasonable and contributes a useful theoretical formulation, but empirical evidence is mixed and further investigation is warranted.
