- Problem & approach: Proposes on-the-job learning using real-time crowdsourcing, formulating it as a Bayesian stochastic game balancing latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Solution method: Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) approximation to decide when to query the crowd and update the model.  
- Datasets & results: Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing strong empirical performance and large cost savings.  
- Novelty: Distinguishes itself from supervised, pure crowdsourcing, online, and active learning by adapting during test time and using a game-theoretic framework.  
- Strength: Novel and principled stochastic-game formulation for balancing objectives.  
- Strength: Reports >10x cost reduction on NER versus full human annotation.  
- Strength: Consistently outperforms baselines across tasks.  
- Strength: Open-source implementation (LENSE) and public data improve reproducibility.  
- Strength: Addresses a practical real-world scenario of limited initial labeled data.  
- Weakness: Some explanations are opaque; example timeline (Line 154) is hard to follow and needs concreteness.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent terminology (e.g., "threshold baseline" vs "entropic baseline") causes confusion.  
- Weakness: Unconventional placement of related work after the conclusion disrupts paper flow.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of worker quality impact and robustness to low-quality workers.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, novel, and practically valuable work; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and address weaknesses.  
- Requested revisions: Add a concrete query/timing example, standardize terminology, move related work earlier, and expand discussion on worker quality.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles an important problem with a novel framework, strong empirical gains, and public code/data.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Needs clearer explanations, conventional related work placement, and deeper treatment of worker reliability.
