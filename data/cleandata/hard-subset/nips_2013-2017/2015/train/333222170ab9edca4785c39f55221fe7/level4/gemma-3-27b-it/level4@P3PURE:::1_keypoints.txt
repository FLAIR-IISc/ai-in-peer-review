- Main contribution: a more theoretically sound “on the job” learner that shifts from crowdsourced outputs to its own predictions over time.  
- Builds on prior work [22] and models the decision of when to query the crowd as a decision problem solved via Monte Carlo Tree Search.  
- Presentation is generally good but omits crucial implementation/details.  
- Empirical gains versus a threshold-based heuristic are underwhelming.  
- Clarify Equation (2): LHS should be conditioned on state s and explain or remove the apparently unused variable y in p_R(ri | y, qi).  
- Define N(s) in Algorithm 1.  
- Explain how threshold baseline values 0.3 and 0.88 were chosen.  
- State whether the “online” baseline systems are state-of-the-art for the tasks; if not, justify their use.  
- If baselines are state-of-the-art, demonstrate that any performance gain from added human annotation is attributable to selective querying, with examples and error analysis.  
- Overall assessment: approach is reasonable, results are mixed, but the idea merits further investigation and discussion.
