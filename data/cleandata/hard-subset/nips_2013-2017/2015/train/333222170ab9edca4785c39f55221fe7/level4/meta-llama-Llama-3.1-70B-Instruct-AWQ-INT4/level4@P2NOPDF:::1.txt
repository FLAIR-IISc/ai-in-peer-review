This paper presents a theoretically sound formulation of an "on the job learner" initially proposed in [22]. The learner begins by relying on crowdsourced output but gradually shifts to making its own predictions, only querying the crowd sporadically. The decision to query the crowd for a given input is framed as a decision problem, solved using Monte Carlo Tree Search.

The presentation is generally clear, although it lacks crucial details in some areas. The empirical results, particularly in comparison to a threshold-based heuristic, are somewhat underwhelming. Nevertheless, the approach appears reasonable and warrants further investigation.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should address the following concerns:

1. Equation (2) requires clarification. The left-hand side should also be conditioned on s, and it is unclear how the variable y in p(R|y, q_i) is eliminated. A revised explanation or correction of this equation would be beneficial.

2. The notation N(s) in Algorithm 1 is not defined. The authors should provide a clear explanation of this term to ensure readers can follow the algorithm.

3. The threshold baseline values of 0.3 and 0.88 seem arbitrary. The authors should provide a detailed explanation of how these values were obtained to facilitate a more informed comparison with the proposed method.

4. The authors should clarify whether the "online" systems used in the experiments represent the best machine-learned systems for this task. If not, they should justify why these systems were chosen. If they are the best systems, any improvement achieved by the proposed method would be solely due to human annotation. Providing error analysis and examples of such cases would be helpful in understanding the method's performance.

Overall, this paper contributes to the development of a theoretical framework for an "on the job learner" that transitions from a crowdsourcing system to a learned model. While the empirical results are mixed, they do spark interesting discussions and merit further exploration.
