The authors propose a novel framework for "on the job learning," where annotators are queried between receiving an example and outputting a prediction. While the algorithm is well-defined for structured prediction tasks, where elements of a sequence are queried, its application to the face recognition task is unclear. Specifically, it is uncertain whether the instance being queried is the only aspect being considered. Furthermore, in non-structured settings, the model does not propagate information between adjacent positions, which raises questions about the utility function's applicability.

The threshold baseline has two parameters with seemingly arbitrary values. It would be beneficial to provide information on how these values were determined and to analyze the sensitivity of the results to these parameters. Additionally, comparing the proposed method to an uncertainty sampling baseline, similar to those used in active learning, would provide a more comprehensive evaluation.

The authors emphasize the distinction between their setting and active online learning, but it is possible to adapt active learning methods to this domain. For instance, the model could be updated upon receiving responses and output a prediction at the end. Although this approach would not capture the temporal nature of the task, it could leverage existing theory on selecting the query set. Overall, the framework appears novel, but some details require clarification, and its applicability seems limited.
