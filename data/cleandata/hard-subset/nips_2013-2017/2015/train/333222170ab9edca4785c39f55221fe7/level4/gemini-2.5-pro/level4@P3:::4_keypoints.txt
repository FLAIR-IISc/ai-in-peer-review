- Introduces "on-the-job learning": a framework that starts with zero training examples and uses real-time crowdsourcing per input while updating the model to reduce future reliance on humans.
- Formalizes the problem as a Bayesian stochastic game balancing accuracy, latency, and monetary cost.
- Proposes an MCTS with progressive widening approximation (system LENSE) to handle intractable optimal policy computation.
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing large cost reductions versus pure crowdsourcing and accuracy gains over standard online learning.
- Novel and practically significant problem setting bridging supervised learning and crowdsourcing.
- Principled technical formulation grounded in Bayesian decision theory; MCTS is a well-motivated approximation.
- Strong empirical results, notably order-of-magnitude cost reduction and improved F1 on NER; multi-task evaluation strengthens claims.
- Paper is clear, well-written, and the authors released code and data for reproducibility.
- Unclear applicability and benefits for non-structured tasks (e.g., image classification); need to explain how the framework adapts and why sequential decisions help.
- Comparisons to active learning baselines are insufficient; uncertainty-sampling and adapted active-learning strategies should be included or discussed.
- Threshold-baseline uses seemingly arbitrary constants (0.3 and 0.98); paper should justify or provide sensitivity analysis.
- Overall: strong, high-quality contribution with addressable weaknesses; worth acceptance for the community.
