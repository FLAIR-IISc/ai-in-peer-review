This paper tackles the problem of training high-accuracy classifiers from scratch, using only unlabeled data and noisy crowd-sourced labels. A key component of this work is its method for handling these noisy responses. While the general problem of learning from crowds has been studied extensively, the paper's focus on incorporating timing and latency as factors for optimization is a novel and interesting contribution.

A major strength of the paper is its direct comparison of the proposed system's accuracy to human performance. The results in Table 2 are particularly insightful, showing that the LENSE system outperforms a 3-vote majority baseline but underperforms a 5-vote baseline. This result provides a valuable comparison of the relative efficiencies of de-noising crowd responses via the proposed method versus simply collecting additional labels to build consensus.

However, the paper's contributions are somewhat obscured by a lack of clarity in its explanations.
*   The example provided in the paragraph beginning on Line 154 is quite abstract and difficult to follow. The explanation would benefit from a concrete example using a specific query with realistic crowd responses and timings to ground the discussion.
*   There is some inconsistent terminology that creates confusion for the reader. In Tables 2 and 3, for example, the same baseline method appears to be referred to as both "entropic" and "threshold."
*   The paper's structure is unconventional, with the Related Work placed late in the manuscript (Section 5). The authors should consider moving this section to a more standard position or providing a clear justification for its current placement.
*   Please note the missing figure reference on Line 86 ("Figure ??").

In summary, the paper addresses a compelling and important problem. However, the significance of its contributions is currently undermined by explanations that are at times opaque. Improving the clarity of the writing and presentation would significantly strengthen the manuscript.
