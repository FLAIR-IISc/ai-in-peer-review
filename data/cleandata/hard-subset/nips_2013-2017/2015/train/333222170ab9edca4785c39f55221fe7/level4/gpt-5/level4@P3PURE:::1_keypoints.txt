- Main contribution: Provides a more theoretically grounded formulation of an “on-the-job learner” that starts with crowd responses and gradually shifts to self-prediction, using MCTS to decide when to query the crowd.  
- Presentation: Mostly clear but several important details are missing.  
- Empirical results: Improvements over a threshold-based heuristic baseline are somewhat underwhelming.  
- Overall approach: Reasonable and merits further investigation.  
- Clarification needed (Eq. 2): Left-hand side seems to miss conditioning on s and it’s unclear how y disappears from p_R(r_i | y, q_i).  
- Clarification needed (Alg. 1): The meaning of N(s) is not defined.  
- Clarification needed (threshold baseline): Rationale for choosing threshold values 0.3 and 0.88 is not given.  
- Clarification needed (tasks/baselines): Unclear if the “online” systems are the strongest ML baselines; need error analysis and examples where gains are due solely to human annotation.  
- Overall assessment: Useful theoretical contribution with mixed empirical evidence but a promising research direction.
