- Primary contribution: a more theoretically grounded formulation of an on-the-job learner that shifts from crowd predictions to self-predictions, using Monte Carlo Tree Search to decide when to query the crowd.
- Method summary: learner queries crowd for low-confidence inputs early and reduces queries over time as its own predictions improve.
- Presentation: generally clear but omits critical details in places.
- Empirical results: improvements over a threshold-based heuristic are modest and somewhat underwhelming.
- Overall assessment: approach is promising and merits further exploration despite mixed empirical performance.
