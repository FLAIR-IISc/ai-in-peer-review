- Method is clear for structured prediction but unclear for face recognition/non-structured tasks — specify whether whole instances were queried and the query granularity.  
- In non-structured settings there is no notion of adjacent positions to propagate information — define and justify the proposed utility function for non-structured tasks.  
- Clarify how queries and utility are handled for non-structured tasks (mechanics and formal definition).  
- The threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters — state how they were chosen (validation/heuristics) and report sensitivity to those values.  
- Include a sensitivity analysis for baseline hyperparameters to show robustness of results.  
- Add an uncertainty-sampling (margin/entropy) active-learning baseline for stronger comparison.  
- Discuss and/or empirically compare to an adapted standard active-learning approach (treating the active set as a sequence and updating as responses arrive).  
- Overall: novelty is promising but many details are underspecified; clarifying non-structured applicability, justifying/analyzing baselines, and adding principled baselines would substantially improve the paper.
