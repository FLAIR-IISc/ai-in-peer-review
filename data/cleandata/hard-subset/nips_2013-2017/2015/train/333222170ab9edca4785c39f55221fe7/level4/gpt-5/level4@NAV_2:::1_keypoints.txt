- Primary contribution: a decision-theoretic formulation of an on-the-job learner building on [22].  
- System design: initially uses real-time crowd responses, then shifts to model predictions and queries crowd only for low-confidence parts.  
- Method: decision of whether/what to query is posed as a sequential decision problem and approximately solved with Monte Carlo Tree Search.  
- Writing: generally clear but several important technical details are missing.  
- Empirics: improvements over a simple threshold-based heuristic are not particularly strong.  
- Overall assessment: approach is sensible and worth further exploration despite mixed results.  
- Clarification needed (Eq.2): left-hand side should be conditioned on s and explanation required for how y disappears in pR(ri | y, qi).  
- Clarification needed (Alg.1): definition/meaning of N(s) is unspecified.  
- Clarification needed (baseline): how threshold values 0.3 and 0.88 were determined.  
- Clarification/analysis requested: confirm whether the “online” systems are the strongest ML baselines; if gains stem from human annotation, provide example cases and error analysis.
