- Proposes a hybrid system that combines crowdsourced labels and an incrementally trained CRF model, formulated as a stochastic game where the system issues time-stamped queries to maximize expected utility.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with TD learning to approximate the intractable continuous-time optimization.  
- Empirical evaluation on NER, sentiment, and face-ID tasks compares majority vote (1/3/5), online learning, a "threshold" baseline, and the full LENSE model.  
- LENSE outperforms all baselines except the 5-vote human majority; the threshold baseline performs nearly as well and has lower latency on some tasks.  
- Demonstrates that the system requires fewer crowdsourced labels over time and achieves high initial accuracy compared to online learning by relying on the crowd early.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, calling the work highly original and significant.  
- Major concern: paper clarity is poor, especially the continuous-time formulation introduced in Section 3, which adds complexity with little empirical benefit.  
- Suggestion: present the core method without continuous time (making it easier to understand and implement) and treat continuous time as an extension.  
- The threshold baseline is strong and should be explicitly acknowledged as often sufficient; tone down claims favoring continuous-time over threshold.  
- Reporting issues: the claim that the model "beats human performance" is incorrect for the 5-vote NER case.  
- Missing results: 5-vote baselines for Face-ID and Sentiment are absent from multiple tables/figures and should be included.  
- Formatting/visibility: best results and relevant latency values (e.g., Threshold latency in NER, LENSE in Face ID) should be bolded in tables.  
- The Threshold baseline is missing from the sentiment experiments (Table 3 and Figure 4) and should be added.  
- Method clarifications requested: specify the t_deadline used in experiments and the criterion for reporting an answer (entropic threshold or deadline).  
- Equation and notation fixes: include s in p(y,r,t|x,q,s) for Eq. (2); rename the confusing use of q in F(q) in Eq. (5); correct the inequality/threshold expression in the Threshold baseline description.  
- Minor point: many clarity issues can be fixed in the camera-ready version, which would improve the paper’s impact and adoption.
