- Paper introduces a hybrid system that combines crowdsourced labels with an online-trained model, relying on the crowd early and reducing reliance as the model gains confidence.
- Approach is formulated as a stochastic game using a CRF-based prediction model where system and crowd act as players issuing queries and receiving responses.
- System issues token queries over time, assigns utility U(q,r) to outcomes, and aims to maximize expected utility under a deadline t_deadline.
- Expected utility is computed via a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t | x,q,s); continuous-time formulation makes direct optimization intractable.
- Authors approximate optimization using Monte Carlo Tree Search combined with TD learning (Algorithm 1).
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face identification tasks against baselines: majority vote (1/3/5), online oracle learning, a “threshold” baseline, and the full LENSE model.
- Results: LENSE outperforms most baselines but is beaten by the 5-vote crowdsourced baseline; the threshold baseline achieves comparable performance with lower latency on NER and sometimes outperforms LENSE on face ID.
- Paper shows the model uses fewer crowdsourced labels over time and attains high early accuracy by leveraging crowd expertise during high-uncertainty phases.
- Strengths: original, principled framework for hybrid crowd-model learning, compelling results vs active/online learning, likely impactful and worthy of acceptance.
- Main weakness: clarity—continuous-time formulation adds complexity and is not clearly justified by experimental gains.
- Recommendation: consider presenting the non-continuous “threshold” approach as the core method and treat continuous time as an extension to improve accessibility and adoption.
- Presentation/results issues: claim of “beats human performance” is inaccurate for 5-vote NER; 5-vote results are missing for face ID and sentiment in several tables/figures and should be included and bolded where best.
- Specific table/figure fixes: bold best results (5-vote in Table 2), bold threshold latency for NER and LENSE value for face ID, and include the threshold baseline in sentiment experiments.
- Minor methodological clarifications requested: clarify use of t_deadline in experiments and decision criterion for reporting answers; include s in Equation (2); rename variable in Equation (5) to avoid confusion with query q; correct inequality in threshold baseline description (likely (1-0.88)).
- Overall: strong, innovative paper with addressable clarity and presentation issues; recommended for acceptance.
