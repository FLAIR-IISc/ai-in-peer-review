- Framework for "on the job learning" is novel and well-defined for structured prediction but its application to face recognition is unclear.  
- Unclear whether the queried item in face recognition is the only aspect considered or how queries are defined.  
- In non-structured settings the model does not propagate information between adjacent positions, questioning the utility function's applicability.  
- The threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters; the paper lacks explanation of how they were chosen and sensitivity analysis.  
- The evaluation should include a comparison to an uncertainty-sampling (active learning) baseline for completeness.  
- The authors’ distinction from active online learning is overstated — active learning methods could be adapted (e.g., update on responses and select queries using existing theory).  
- Overall: framework is novel but requires clarification of details and has limited/unclear applicability.
