This paper proposes a theoretically sound formulation of an "on the job learner" that builds upon the work presented in [22]. The learner begins by relying on the crowd to produce its output, but over time, it starts making its own predictions and querying the crowd only when necessary. The decision to query the crowd for a given input is framed as a decision problem, which is solved using a Monte Carlo Tree Search.

The presentation is generally clear, although it lacks important details in some areas. The empirical results, which are compared to a threshold-based heuristic, are somewhat underwhelming. Nevertheless, the approach appears reasonable and warrants further investigation.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should address the following issues:

1. Equation (2) requires clarification. The left-hand side of the equation should also be conditioned on s, and it is unclear how the variable y in p(R|y, q_i) is eliminated. A more detailed explanation of this equation would be helpful.

2. In Algorithm 1, the notation N(s) is not defined. The authors should provide a clear explanation of this term to avoid confusion.

3. The threshold baseline values of 0.3 and 0.88 seem arbitrary. The authors should provide a justification for these specific values and explain how they were obtained.

4. It is unclear whether the "online" systems used in this study represent the best machine-learned systems for this task. If not, the authors should explain why they were chosen. If they do represent the best systems, then any improvement achieved by the proposed method is likely due to human annotation. Providing some examples of such cases, along with an error analysis, would be beneficial in understanding the strengths and limitations of the approach.

Overall, this paper presents a theoretical formulation of an "on the job learner" that gradually transitions from a pure crowdsourcing system to a learned model. While the empirical results are mixed, they contribute to the ongoing discussion in this area. Addressing the above issues will help to strengthen the paper and provide a clearer understanding of the proposed method.
