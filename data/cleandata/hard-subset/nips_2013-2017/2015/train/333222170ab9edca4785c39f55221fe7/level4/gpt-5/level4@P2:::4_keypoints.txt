- Proposes "on-the-job learning": start with no training data, query crowd at prediction time, and update model online to reduce crowd reliance.
- Casts the problem as a Bayesian stochastic game balancing accuracy, latency, and monetary cost.
- Uses MCTS with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy in continuous time.
- Experiments (NER, sentiment, images) show LENSE reduces cost and matches/improves accuracy, particularly strong on NER; implementation released.
- Relates to online learning, active learning, test-time feature acquisition, and crowd-POMDP methods.
- Differs from prior work by combining structured prediction with real-time crowd queries under a unified decision-theoretic objective.
- Strength: principled objective jointly trading off accuracy, latency, and cost using Bayesian decision theory.
- Strength: novel stochastic-game formulation and MCTS-based planner for test-time querying.
- Strength: empirical cost reductions and accuracy gains, open-source code, and thoughtful modeling of asynchronous crowd latency.
- Weakness: unclear applicability to unstructured tasks (Faces) — what is queried and how inference/utility operate without structure.
- Weakness: baseline choices may be weak or ad hoc (threshold params unexplained); sensitivity analysis needed.
- Weakness: missing baselines such as uncertainty sampling, VOI/expected-error reduction, and a simple active-learning-style update-then-predict baseline.
- Weakness: environment assumptions questionable — fixed pR=0.7 across tasks/workers; need per-task/worker reliability estimates and robustness checks.
- Weakness: latency model pT priors/hyperparameters not specified; robustness to latency distribution changes not shown.
- Weakness: comparisons to decision-theoretic crowd-control (e.g., TurKontrol) and test-time feature acquisition methods are missing.
- Weakness: computational cost and scalability (planning latency, MCTS budget, scaling with sequence length/classes) not reported.
- Weakness: unclear claim about outperforming "expert" labels on NER — define expert and clarify how quality was measured.
- Clarity: notation, footnotes, and some definitions (e.g., ExpAcc, query rules) need tightening; Faces/Sentiment pipelines should be schematized.
- Originality: novel integration of stochastic-game modeling and MCTS for structured prediction with time/cost-aware querying.
- Significance: could impact real-time ML with no labeled data if generalized and supported by stronger comparisons; current evidence strongest for NER.
- Requested additions: precise query/action descriptions for Faces/Sentiment and how information propagates without structure.
- Requested additions: add uncertainty sampling and simple active-learning baselines and discuss time-awareness differences.
- Requested additions: sensitivity analyses for threshold parameters, pR, pT, and utility weights (wM, wT).
- Requested additions: report planning overhead, MCTS budget, and scaling behavior; consider per-worker reliability or hierarchical pR models.
- Recommendation: promising and borderline-accept, contingent on clarifying non-structured tasks, strengthening baselines, and adding sensitivity and computational analyses.
