- Introduces "on-the-job learning": systems start with no training data and reduce crowdsourcing as models improve.
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, latency, and cost.
- Approximates the optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and progressive widening.
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification; reports large cost reductions (over an order of magnitude on NER) and improved performance versus pure crowd and pure ML.
- Builds on prior work (especially Lasecki et al. 2013) and relates to active learning, active classification, and crowd-sourced workflows.
- Distinction from active learning: maintains high accuracy from the start rather than only asymptotically.
- Distinction from active classification: system improves during test time instead of using a static policy.
- Strength: principled Bayesian decision-theoretic formulation improves over ad-hoc prior approaches.
- Strength: strong empirical results on NER, including an 8% F1 gain over single-human labeling and 28% over online learning, plus steady query reduction.
- Strength: open-source implementation (LENSE) and detailed experimental setup enhance reproducibility.
- Weakness: clarity issues in Equation (2) and undefined/unclear notation (e.g., p_R(r | y_q) and elimination of y).
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 references N(s) without definition, harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: threshold baseline uses parameters (0.3, 0.88) with no justification or tuning details.
- Weakness: unclear whether the "online learning" baseline represents state-of-the-art ML for the tasks, so gains may reflect baseline limitations.
- Originality: theoretical framing as a stochastic game/Bayesian decision process is novel, though practical system builds on prior work.
- Significance: potential for large crowdsourcing cost savings and useful steady reduction in queries, but needs more analysis on task-specific effectiveness (e.g., why NER benefits most).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions pending clarifications.
- Requested revisions: clarify Eq. (2) and ExpAcc(p); define all symbols in Algorithm 1 including N(s); justify threshold baseline parameters; contextualize and justify ML baselines versus state-of-the-art; detail differences from [6] and [22]; analyze why the method works better on NER.
