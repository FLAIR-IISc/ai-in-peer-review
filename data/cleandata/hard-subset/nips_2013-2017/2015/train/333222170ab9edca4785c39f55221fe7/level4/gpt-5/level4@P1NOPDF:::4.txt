The paper introduces “on-the-job learning,” in which the system can query annotators between receiving an instance and issuing a prediction. For structured prediction tasks, where elements of a sequence can be queried, the algorithmic setup is clear. However, it is not at all clear how this was applied to the face recognition task—were you simply querying the instance label? In the unstructured setting, the model does not propagate information between adjacent examples, so the proposed utility function seems ill-defined or at least not meaningful. Please clarify how the utility is computed and used in this case.

The threshold baseline includes two parameters that appear arbitrary. How were these values chosen, and how sensitive are the results to them? Some discussion or analysis of parameter selection and robustness would be helpful. In addition, a baseline using uncertainty sampling, analogous to that used in active learning, would be an informative comparison.

The paper emphasizes a distinction between this setting and active online learning. However, active learning could be adapted straightforwardly to the current domain by treating the active set as a sequence. For example, the model could be updated as responses arrive and then produce a final prediction at the end. While such a method would not capture the temporal aspect of mid-trajectory predictions, there is substantial theory to guide query selection in that framework. It would strengthen the paper either to compare against such an adapted active-learning baseline or to more clearly articulate why the proposed setting leads to fundamentally different decisions and outcomes.

Overall, the framework appears novel, but several details are unclear, and its applicability seems fairly restricted. Clarifying how the method functions in unstructured tasks like face recognition, justifying baseline choices and parameters, and providing stronger comparisons to adapted active-learning approaches would significantly improve the paper.
