- Introduces on-the-job learning with real-time crowd annotations; procedure is clear for structured prediction but not for face recognition/unstructured tasks.

- Unclear what the query unit was for face recognition and how information is propagated without adjacency, so the proposed utility function’s appropriateness is questionable in unstructured cases.

- The threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters; the paper doesn’t explain how they were chosen or report sensitivity to their values.

- The evaluation omits a standard active learning baseline (e.g., uncertainty sampling), which would be an informative comparison.

- Online active learning could be adapted by treating the sequence as the active pool and updating the model as responses arrive; existing theory could guide principled query selection even if temporal dynamics aren’t modeled.

- Overall assessment: framework is novel but several details are unclear and the scope of applicability appears limited.
