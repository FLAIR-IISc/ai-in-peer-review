

The authors introduce a scenario termed "on-the-job learning," wherein annotator queries occur between receiving an example and outputting a prediction. While the algorithm's operation for structured prediction tasks (e.g., querying elements within a sequence) is clear, its application to the face recognition task remains ambiguous. Specifically, it is unclear whether the query pertained solely to the instance itself. In the non-structured setting, the model lacks propagation of information between adjacent examples, rendering the utility function ill-defined.  

The threshold baseline employs two parameters whose origin and sensitivity are not justified. The authors should specify the rationale for these values and demonstrate sensitivity analysis. Additionally, a baseline employing uncertainty sampling—common in active learning—would provide a more meaningful comparison.  

The authors emphasize the distinction between this setting and active online learning. However, standard active learning could be readily adapted to this domain by treating the sequence as the active set: the model could update upon receiving responses and output a final prediction, leveraging established theoretical frameworks for query selection. While this adaptation would not fully capture the temporal dynamics, it undermines the novelty claim. Overall, the framework appears seemingly novel but suffers from unclear implementation details and restricted applicability.
