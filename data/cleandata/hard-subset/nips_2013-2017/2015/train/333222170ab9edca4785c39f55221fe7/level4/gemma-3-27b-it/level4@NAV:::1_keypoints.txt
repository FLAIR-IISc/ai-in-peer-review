- Primary contribution: develops a more rigorous theoretical framework for an on-the-job learning algorithm building on prior work [22].  
- Method: learner transitions from crowd responses to its own predictions using Monte Carlo Tree Search to decide when to query the crowd.  
- Writing: paper is generally well-written but omits important details.  
- Empirical results: show only limited improvement over a threshold-based heuristic.  
- Clarification needed for Equation (2): LHS should be conditioned on state s and the role/disappearance of y in pR(ri | y, q_i) must be explained.  
- Algorithm detail missing: meaning and definition of N(s) in Algorithm 1 is unclear.  
- Baseline details: explain how threshold values 0.3 and 0.88 were chosen.  
- Model comparison: clarify whether the "online" systems are state-of-the-art and, if not, why performance differs; if they are, attribute gains appropriately to human annotation and provide examples.  
- Recommend: include a more thorough error analysis and examples to support empirical claims despite the valuable theoretical contribution.
