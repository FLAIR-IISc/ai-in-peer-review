- Proposes an “on-the-job learning” framework that queries annotators between receiving an input and making a prediction, motivated for time- and cost-constrained settings.  
- Novel and timely formulation emphasizing temporal decision-making under cost and latency constraints.  
- Natural and clear instantiation for structured sequence labeling where querying sequence elements and propagating information is intuitive.  

- Unclear how the framework is instantiated for unstructured tasks (e.g., face recognition): query unit and decomposition are not specified.  
- Utility definition and justification for non-structured settings are missing—propagation-based utilities may not transfer to independent examples.  
- Need explicit specification of the utility/decision policy (whether it’s propagation-based or per-instance value-of-information and any approximations used).  
- Threshold baseline uses ad-hoc parameters without explanation; require how values were chosen and a sensitivity analysis.  
- Missing simple baselines: add uncertainty sampling (margin/entropy) and cost-sensitive/budgeted active-learning adaptations for streaming/online settings.  
- The distinction from (online) active learning is under-explained; adaptions of active learning could be strong comparators and should be discussed or evaluated.  
- Scope/applicability unclear: state assumptions needed for unstructured tasks (decomposability, localized queries, factorized representation) and limitations.  

- Request precise description of the face-recognition instantiation: query space, response format, timing model, belief updates, and utility computation details.  
- Add sensitivity analyses for threshold parameters and key policy hyperparameters (e.g., delay cost, query cost).  
- Include an uncertainty-sampling baseline and an online active-learning variant with model updates and a single final prediction under comparable time/budget constraints.  
- Discuss computational complexity and real-time latency of utility evaluation and query selection, and possible approximations/caching.  
- Clarify annotation model assumptions: response-time distributions, annotator noise, and how these are learned or estimated.  

- Overall: promising and elegant for structured prediction but currently lacks clarity and empirical comparison for unstructured tasks and relies on insufficiently principled baselines; needs the above clarifications and experiments to assess generality and impact.
