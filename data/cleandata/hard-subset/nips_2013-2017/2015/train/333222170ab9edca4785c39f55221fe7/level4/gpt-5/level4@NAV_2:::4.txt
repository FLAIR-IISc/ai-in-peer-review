The paper introduces an “on-the-job learning” setting in which the system solicits real-time crowd annotations between receiving an input and committing to a prediction.

While the procedure is clear for structured prediction—where the system queries specific positions in a sequence—its application to the face recognition task is not explained: was the entire instance the query unit? In the unstructured case, there is no adjacency to propagate information across examples, so it is unclear how the proposed utility function remains appropriate.

The threshold baseline relies on two seemingly arbitrary parameters. How were these chosen, and how sensitive are the results to their values?

An additional baseline based on uncertainty sampling, in line with standard active learning, would be informative.

Finally, although the paper highlights distinctions from online active learning, that paradigm could be adapted to this setting by treating the sequence as the active pool. For instance, the model could be updated as responses arrive and then produce a final prediction; while this would not capture temporal dynamics, there is substantial theory for principled query selection. In sum, the framework seems novel, but several details are unclear and the scope of applicability appears limited.
