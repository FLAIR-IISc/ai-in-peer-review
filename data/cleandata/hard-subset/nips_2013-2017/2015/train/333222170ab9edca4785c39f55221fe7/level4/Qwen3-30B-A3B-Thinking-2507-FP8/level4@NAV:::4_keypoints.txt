- Introduces "on the job learning" where annotator requests occur between receiving an example and making a prediction.  
- Algorithm is well-defined for structured prediction (querying sequence elements) but its application to face recognition is ambiguous — unclear whether whole instances or parts were queried.  
- In non-structured settings the model does not propagate information between adjacent examples, making the proposed utility function inapplicable.  
- The threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters; their origin and sensitivity need justification.  
- A baseline using uncertainty sampling (common in active learning) is missing and would be a more meaningful comparison.  
- Authors claim a distinction from active online learning, but standard active learning can be adapted by treating the active set as a sequence and updating after each response.  
- That adaptation would not capture temporal dynamics fully but benefits from established theoretical foundations for query selection.  
- The framework offers a novel perspective but leaves key implementation details unclear and appears limited in applicability.
