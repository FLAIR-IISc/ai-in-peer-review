- Introduces "on the job learning" where an algorithm can query an annotator between receiving an example and making a prediction.  
- Application to structured prediction is clear, but implementation for face recognition (non-structured) lacks detail on how querying and utility functions are adapted.  
- Unclear whether entire instances are queried in non-sequential tasks and how utility propagation works without sequence structure.  
- Experimental baselines are weak: the "threshold" baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters without justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Missing comparison to a standard uncertainty sampling active learning baseline, which would better contextualize results.  
- The paper overstates distinction from active learning; active methods could be adapted to the proposed setting and should be discussed and compared.  
- Claims of novelty are weakened by lack of thorough comparison to related work and limited demonstration of applicability beyond sequential data.
