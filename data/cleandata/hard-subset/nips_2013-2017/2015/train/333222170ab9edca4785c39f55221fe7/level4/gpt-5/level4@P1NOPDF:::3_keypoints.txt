- Proposes a hybrid system that combines crowdsourced labels with an incrementally (online) trained model, relying on the crowd during warm-up and reducing crowd involvement as model confidence grows.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game using a CRF prediction model where system and crowd are players issuing queries and receiving responses with utilities.  
- Queries have timing and response dynamics; utility is maximized subject to a deadline, determining query count and timing.  
- Computing expected utility requires a dynamics model P(y, r, t | x, q, s); continuous time makes exact optimization intractable.  
- Uses an approximation via Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning (Algorithm 1).  
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment classification, and face identification, comparing majority vote (1,3,5), online learning with oracle labels, a “threshold” baseline (no continuous time), and the full model (LENSE).  
- LENSE outperforms most baselines in precision/recall/F1 but is beaten by 5-vote crowdsourcing in some cases.  
- The “threshold” baseline performs nearly as well as LENSE and often has lower latency (notably on NER), sometimes matching or exceeding LENSE on face ID.  
- LENSE demonstrates reduced crowd label requests over time and strong early accuracy by leveraging crowd labels when uncertain.  
- Reviewer praises originality, significance, and practical potential, recommending acceptance.  
- Main concern: continuous-time formulation complicates exposition and offers minimal empirical benefit; recommend presenting the threshold (discrete-time) core method first and continuous time as an extension.  
- Results/presentation issues: incorrect “beats human” claim for NER, missing 5-vote entries in several tables/figures, and bolding/latency emphasis errors in Table 2.  
- Missing baseline: “Threshold” is absent from the sentiment experiment (Table 3 and Figure 4) and should be included.  
- Method clarifications needed: specify what t_deadline (or reporting criterion) was used; equation (2) should include s in the distribution; change confusing notation q in F(q); fix the inequality/sign in the Threshold baseline expression.
