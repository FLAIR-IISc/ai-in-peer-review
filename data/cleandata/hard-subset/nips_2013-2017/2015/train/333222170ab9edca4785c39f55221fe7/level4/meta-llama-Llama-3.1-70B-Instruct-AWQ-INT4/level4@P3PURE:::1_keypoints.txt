- Main contribution: a more theoretically sound formulation of an on-the-job learner that transitions from crowd outputs to autonomous predictions.
- Query decision modeled as a decision problem solved with Monte Carlo Tree Search.
- Presentation is generally clear but omits important technical details in places.
- Empirical results are underwhelming, especially compared to a threshold-based heuristic.
- Clarify Equation (2): left-hand side should be conditioned on s and explain how y is marginalized in p(R|ri, y, qi).
- Provide an explicit definition of N(s) used in Algorithm 1.
- Justify the choice of 0.3 and 0.88 in the threshold baseline.
- Clarify whether the "online" systems used are the best for the task; if not, justify choices, otherwise analyze gains due to human annotation and include error analysis.
- Overall: approach is reasonable and merits further investigation despite mixed empirical results.
