This paper tackles the problem of building high-accuracy classifiers starting from zero labeled examples. A key component is handling noisy crowd responses; while this has been studied extensively, the paper’s focus on timing and latency optimization is novel and interesting.

I appreciate the direct comparison of classifier accuracy to human performance. In Table 2, it is particularly interesting that LENSE outperforms the 3-vote baseline but underperforms the 5-vote baseline. This highlights a meaningful comparison between denoising crowd responses via increased consensus and the LENSE approach, and it suggests an important trade-off between additional crowd votes and algorithmic denoising.

That said, several presentation issues limit readability:
- The example in the paragraph beginning at Line 154 is abstract and difficult to follow. For clarity, please provide a concrete query example with realistic crowd responses and associated response times that reflect a plausible real-world scenario.
- In Tables 2 and 3, the threshold baseline is referred to inconsistently as “entropic” and “threshold,” which is confusing. Please standardize the terminology.
- I do not understand why the related work appears in Section 5; its placement is unconventional and makes it harder to situate the contributions early. Consider moving or clearly justifying this structure.
- Line 086 contains “Figure ??”—please resolve this reference.
- More generally, some explanations are opaque; tightening the exposition and adding illustrative examples would improve accessibility.

Overall, this is a compelling problem with an interesting and novel treatment of timing and latency, but the paper would benefit from clearer examples, consistent baseline naming, a more conventional organization of related work, and resolution of the noted presentation issues.
