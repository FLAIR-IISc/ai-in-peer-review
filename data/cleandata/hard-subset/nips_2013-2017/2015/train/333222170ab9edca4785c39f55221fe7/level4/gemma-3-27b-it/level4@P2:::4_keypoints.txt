- Core idea: Combine model predictions with real-time crowdsourcing to query humans only on uncertain parts and reduce crowd reliance as the model learns.
- Formalization: Modeled as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory with MCTS approximation to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.
- Empirical evaluation: Demonstrated on NER, sentiment, and image classification with large cost reductions and performance gains versus full annotation and online learning.
- Novelty: Presents a new, principled framework ("on-the-job learning") bridging supervised learning and crowdsourcing.
- Theoretical foundation: Bayesian decision-theoretic formalization provides a sound approach to trade-offs in deployment.
- Practical contribution: Open-source implementation (LENSE) and detailed experiments improve reproducibility and adoption potential.
- Motivation: Clearly motivated for scenarios needing immediate deployment with scarce labeled data.
- Strength on NER: Particularly strong empirical results for NER, showing an order-of-magnitude cost reduction and improved performance.
- Clarity issue for non-structured tasks: Application to face recognition (non-structured) is unclear—how querying and utility function were applied needs explanation.
- Parameter sensitivity: Threshold baseline depends on two parameters without sufficient justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Missing baseline: Lacks comparison to a standard uncertainty sampling baseline adapted for online query selection.
- Relation to prior work: Needs a more thorough discussion of how this differs from or relates to active online learning and simplified active learning adaptations.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance contingent on clarifying the face recognition setup and justifying threshold baseline parameter choices.
