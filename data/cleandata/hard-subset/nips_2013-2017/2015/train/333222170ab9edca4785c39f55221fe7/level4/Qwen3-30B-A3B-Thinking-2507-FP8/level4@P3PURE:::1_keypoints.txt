- Main contribution: provides a theoretically sound formulation of an on-the-job learner that shifts from crowd-sourced outputs to autonomous predictions while querying the crowd infrequently.  
- Decision model: models the decision to query the crowd as a sequential decision problem solved via Monte Carlo Tree Search.  
- Presentation: generally clear but omits important technical details.  
- Empirical results: comparisons to a threshold-based heuristic are modest/underwhelming.  
- Overall assessment: approach is reasonable and merits further investigation.  

Specific concerns:
- Eq. (2) unclear: left-hand side should be conditioned on state s, and it is unclear how variable y disappears in p^R(r_i | y, q_i).  
- Algorithm 1: meaning of N(s) is not defined.  
- Baseline thresholds: derivation/justification of threshold values 0.3 and 0.88 used in the baseline comparison is not explained.  
- Task and baselines: tasks lack detailed specification; unclear whether “online” baselines are state-of-the-art—if not, rationale for choice is missing; if yes, any gains may be solely from human annotation and require concrete examples and error analysis.  
- Conclusion: theoretically grounded formulation that stimulates valuable discussion despite modest empirical gains.
