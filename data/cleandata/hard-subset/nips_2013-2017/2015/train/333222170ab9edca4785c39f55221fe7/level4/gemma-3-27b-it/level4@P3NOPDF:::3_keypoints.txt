- Paper proposes a hybrid method (LENSE) that combines crowdsourced labels and an online-trained model, relying on the crowd initially and reducing queries as the model gains confidence.  
- Formulation: cast as a stochastic game between system and crowd using a CRF prediction model; system issues queries q for tokens x (true label y) and receives responses r.  
- Incorporates continuous time and a deadline t_deadline to model query timing and response delays, controlling number and rate of queries.  
- Expected utility requires a simulation dynamics model P(y, r, t | x, q, s); exact optimization is intractable.  
- Optimization uses an approximation based on Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning (Algorithm 1).  
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and face-ID compare majority vote (1/3/5), online learning, a “threshold” baseline (no continuous time), and LENSE.  
- Results: LENSE outperforms all methods except the 5-vote human baseline on most metrics; the 5-vote baseline is best in Table 2.  
- The “threshold” baseline performs nearly as well to LENSE and has lower latency on NER; it even outperforms LENSE on face-ID.  
- Figures show LENSE uses progressively fewer crowdsourced labels as the model learns and achieves high accuracy from the first example by leveraging crowd expertise.  
- Reviewer judges the work original, significant, and worthy of acceptance, noting it’s the first principled proposal for simultaneous crowd learning and system training.  
- Major clarity concern: continuous-time formulation adds complexity but yields limited empirical benefit, potentially hindering accessibility and impact.  
- Recommendation: present the core method without continuous time and treat continuous time as an extension; emphasize the strong performance of the simpler “threshold” baseline.  
- Reporting issues: claim of “beating human performance” is inaccurate for 5-vote NER; 5-vote results missing for face-ID and sentiment in several tables/figures and should be included and bolded where best.  
- Missing baseline: “Threshold” is absent from the sentiment experiment and must be added for completeness.  
- Minor technical fixes requested: clarify t_deadline usage and response-readiness criteria; include s in Equation (2)’s distribution; change the letter q in F(q) to avoid confusion; correct the threshold baseline expression (inequality/term ordering).  
- Overall: promising and original work deserving acceptance, but clarity (especially on continuous time) and several reporting/method corrections are needed for broader adoption.
