- Significant theoretical contribution: presents a more sound formulation of an "on the job learner" building on [22].
- Learner workflow: initially uses crowdsourcing, progressively learns its own predictor, and queries crowd only when necessary.
- Decision framing: crowd-querying is posed as a decision problem solved via Monte Carlo Tree Search.
- Presentation: generally clear but missing crucial details in some areas.
- Empirical results: underwhelming performance compared to a threshold-based heuristic.
- Equation (2) issue: LHS should be conditioned on s and the disappearance of y in p_R(ri | y, q_i) needs explanation.
- Algorithm 1 ambiguity: definition and purpose of N(s) must be clarified.
- Baseline parameters: authors should justify the choice of thresholds 0.3 and 0.88.
- Experimental setup clarity: state whether the "online" systems are state-of-the-art; if not, justify choice; if yes, provide error analysis and examples to show improvements stem from human annotation.
- Overall: approach is reasonable and worth further exploration despite mixed empirical findings.
