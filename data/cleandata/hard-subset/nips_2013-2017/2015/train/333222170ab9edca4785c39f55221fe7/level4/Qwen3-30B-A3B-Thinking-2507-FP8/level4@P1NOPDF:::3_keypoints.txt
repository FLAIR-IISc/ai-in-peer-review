- Proposes a hybrid framework (LENSE) combining crowdsourced labels with incrementally trained models, formalized as a stochastic game optimizing expected utility under a deadline.  
- Core method models dynamics P(y,r,t|x,q,s) in continuous time and approximates intractable optimization via MCTS and TD learning (Algorithm 1).  
- Evaluated against baselines (majority vote 1/3/5, online learning, threshold baseline) on NER, sentiment, and face identification tasks.  
- LENSE achieves near-peak precision/recall/F1, outperforming baselines except full 5-vote crowdsourcing.  
- The threshold baseline matches LENSE on NER with lower latency and outperforms LENSE on face ID, questioning continuous-time benefits.  
- LENSE effectively reduces crowd reliance over time and attains faster early accuracy gains than online learning by using crowd when model uncertainty is high.  
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance, calling the paper novel and practically impactful as the first principled, complete hybrid framework.  
- Main clarity issue: introducing continuous time mid-paper complicates presentation without clear experimental advantage.  
- Recommendation to present the core (discrete-time) method first and treat continuous time as an optional extension.  
- Paper should explicitly emphasize the threshold baseline’s strong empirical performance and practical sufficiency.  
- Missing data: 5-vote results are absent for Face ID and sentiment—these must be included and highlighted alongside threshold NER latency.  
- Correction needed for threshold baseline formula: uncertainty condition should be (1−p_θ(y_i|x))·0.3^m ≤ (1−0.88), not ≥ 0.88.  
- Additional methodological clarifications required: specify how t_deadline is used in experiments; include s in Equation (2)’s P(y,r,t|x,q,s); fix confusing notation in Equation (5) (replace q in F(q)).  
- Conclusion: continuous-time extension is technically sound but adds complexity without clear gains; streamlining and consistent reporting will maximize impact.
