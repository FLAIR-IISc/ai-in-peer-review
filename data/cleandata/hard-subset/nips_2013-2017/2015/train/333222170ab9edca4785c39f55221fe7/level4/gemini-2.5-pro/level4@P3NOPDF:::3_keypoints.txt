- Proposes a hybrid system combining crowdsourced labels with an incrementally trained online model, framed as a stochastic game using a CRF and optimized with Monte Carlo Tree Search + TD learning, accounting for response times and deadlines.

- Novel and significant: one of the first principled frameworks for combining human and machine intelligence with an elegant stochastic-game formulation.

- Strong empirical results: LENSE outperforms multiple baselines across NER, sentiment, and face-ID, bootstrapping from the crowd early and reducing dependence over time.

- Main clarity/impact issue: modeling continuous time substantially complicates the approach but yields little empirical benefit versus simpler methods.

- Evidence against continuous-time complexity: the simpler "threshold" baseline achieves comparable or better performance and much lower latency on NER (Table 2).

- Restructuring recommendation: present the core hybrid model without temporal dynamics first, then introduce the continuous-time extension as optional.

- Emphasize the threshold baseline: authors should acknowledge and perhaps present it as a core contribution given its simplicity and strong performance.

- Missing results to include: 5-vote baseline for Face-ID and Sentiment (Tables 2,3, Figure 4) and the Threshold baseline for sentiment (Table 3, Figure 4).

- Presentation fixes: bold best results in Table 2 (including 5-vote F1 and Threshold latency) and qualify the claim that the model "beats human performance" with respect to the 5-vote baseline.

- Experimental detail clarification: specify how t_deadline was chosen and the practical stopping criterion for LENSE (fixed deadline vs entropy threshold).

- Equation clarifications: Eq. (2) should likely condition on query time s (P(y,r,t|x,q,s)), and Eq. (5) should avoid reusing q for the nominal distribution to prevent confusion.

- Threshold-baseline typo: inequality likely incorrect—should ensure uncertainty decreases with m (proposed correction: (1 - p_theta(y_i|x)) * 0.3^m <= (1 - 0.88)).

- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance but request addressing clarity, restructuring, and the listed minor issues to increase the paper's impact.
