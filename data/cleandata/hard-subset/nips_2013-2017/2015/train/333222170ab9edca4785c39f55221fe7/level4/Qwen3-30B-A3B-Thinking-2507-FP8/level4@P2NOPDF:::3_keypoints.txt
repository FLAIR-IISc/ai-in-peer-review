- Proposes a principled hybrid framework (LENSE) that integrates crowdsourced labels with online model training, reducing crowd reliance as model confidence grows.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game between the system (queries) and the crowd (responses), optimizing expected utility under time constraints.  
- System selects token-level queries q over time s to maximize utility U(q,r) subject to a deadline t_deadline that limits query volume/rate.  
- Exact expected-utility optimization requires P(y,r,t|x,q,s) and is intractable in continuous time, so authors approximate with Monte Carlo Tree Search and TD learning (Alg. 1).  
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and face ID compare majority vote baselines (1/3/5 judges), online oracle learning, a non-time “threshold” baseline, and LENSE.  
- LENSE achieves F1 nearly matching the 5-vote baseline, shows reduced crowd dependence over time, and higher initial accuracy versus online learning.  
- Primary concern: introducing continuous time adds complexity with negligible empirical benefit—threshold baseline performs comparably or better with lower latency.  
- Recommendation to present the core (non-time) method first and treat continuous time as an optional extension to improve clarity and adoptability.  
- Missing data: 5-vote baseline is omitted from Face ID and Sentiment tables/figures and should be included and highlighted.  
- Inaccurate performance claim: the paper’s “beats human performance” statement for NER is unsupported and inconsistent with table values.  
- Unclear technical details: how t_deadline is enforced (hard deadline vs. entropic threshold) needs clarification.  
- Notational errors: Eq. (2) should include s in p(y,r,t|x,q,s); Eq. (5) overloads F(q) and needs a different symbol.  
- Threshold-baseline description contains a sign/error in the uncertainty condition; the inequality should reflect that increasing m reduces uncertainty.  
- Overall assessment: highly original and practically promising, but presentation and clarity issues (esp. continuous time) must be fixed; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
