The main contribution of this paper is to provide a more theoretically sound construction of the 'on-the-job learner' concept proposed in [22]. The learner starts by queuing in the crowd to give their input, but gradually begins to make predictions and sometimes asks the crowd for input. The choice of whether or not to ask the crowd for any input is made as a solved decision problem through Monte Carlo tree search.

The presentation is mostly good, although it lacks important details. The empirical results are somewhat disappointing compared to threshold-based heuristics. But it certainly seems like a reasonable approach and deserves further investigation.
