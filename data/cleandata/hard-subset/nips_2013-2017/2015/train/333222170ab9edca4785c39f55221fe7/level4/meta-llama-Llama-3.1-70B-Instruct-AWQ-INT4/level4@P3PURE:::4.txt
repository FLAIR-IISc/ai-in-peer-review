The authors describe a scenario called "on the job learning", where requests are made to annotators between receiving an example and outputting a prediction. While it is clear how the algorithm works for a structured prediction task, where elements of the sequence are queried, it is unclear how this was applied to the face recognition task - was it simply the instance being queried? In the non-structured setting, the model would not propagate information between adjacent positions (examples), so the utility function appears to lose its meaning.

The threshold baseline has two parameters that seem to be arbitrarily chosen. The authors should provide more information on the origin of these values and the sensitivity of the results to these parameters. A further baseline of uncertainty sampling, similar to that used in active learning, would be an interesting addition.

The authors emphasize the distinction between this setting and active online learning, but active learning could be adapted to work in this domain with relative ease. For instance, the model could be updated in the same manner upon receiving responses and then output a prediction at the end. Although this approach would not capture the temporal nature of the task, there is a substantial body of theory that could be drawn upon to inform the selection of the query set. Overall, the framework appears to be novel, but some details are unclear, and its applicability seems to be fairly restricted.
