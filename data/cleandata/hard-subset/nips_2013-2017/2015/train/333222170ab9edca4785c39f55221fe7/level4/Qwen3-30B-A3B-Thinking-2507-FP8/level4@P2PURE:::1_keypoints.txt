1. Primary contribution: a theoretically rigorous formulation of an "on-the-job learner" that shifts from crowd-sourced outputs to autonomous predictions, using Monte Carlo Tree Search to decide when to query the crowd.  
2. Presentation: generally clear but omits key technical details.  
3. Empirical results: modest improvements over a threshold-based heuristic.  
4. Clarification needed for Eq. (2): left-hand side should condition on s and the variable y in p^{(R)}(r_i | y, q_i) is unaccounted for.  
5. Clarification needed: define N(s) in Algorithm 1.  
6. Clarification needed: specify how baseline threshold values 0.3 and 0.88 were chosen.  
7. Clarification needed for baselines: state whether the "online" systems are state-of-the-art; if not, justify baseline choice; if yes, acknowledge gains arise from human annotation and provide error analysis/examples.  
8. Overall assessment: theoretically grounded and reasonable approach, but limited empirical gains; addressing the above points would strengthen the paper.
