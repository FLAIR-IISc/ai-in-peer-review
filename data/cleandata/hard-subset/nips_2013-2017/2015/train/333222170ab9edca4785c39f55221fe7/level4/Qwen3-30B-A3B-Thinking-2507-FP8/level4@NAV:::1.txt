

The primary contribution of this paper is to establish a theoretically rigorous formulation for an "on-the-job learner" introduced in [22]. The learner initially relies on crowd-sourced outputs by queuing worker responses but progressively shifts toward autonomous predictions while minimizing crowd queries through a decision process framed as a Monte Carlo Tree Search.  

The presentation is generally clear but omits critical details. Empirical results compared to a threshold-based heuristic are modest. Nevertheless, the approach is logically sound and warrants further investigation.

This work formalizes a transition from crowd-sourcing to autonomous learning, with mixed empirical results that merit discussion.
