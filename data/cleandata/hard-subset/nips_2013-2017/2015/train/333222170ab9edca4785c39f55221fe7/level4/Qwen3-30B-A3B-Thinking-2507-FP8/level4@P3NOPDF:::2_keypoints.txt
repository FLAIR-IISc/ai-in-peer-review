- Presents a compelling method (LENSE) for building high-accuracy classifiers from zero labeled examples with a novel focus on latency and noisy crowd responses.  
- Table 2 shows LENSE outperforms a 3-vote consensus baseline but underperforms a 5-vote baseline, highlighting a trade-off between efficiency and accuracy.  
- The paper needs deeper discussion on when LENSE vs vote-based de-noising is preferable given their different latency–accuracy profiles.  
- The example at Line 154 is overly abstract and should be replaced with a concrete, real-world illustration showing a query, timestamped crowd responses, and LENSE’s processing.  
- Terminology is inconsistent in Tables 2–3: “threshold baseline” vs “entropic threshold” — standardize the name (e.g., “entropic threshold baseline”) and define it clearly.  
- Related work is placed awkwardly in Section 5; it should precede the methodology (e.g., Section 2 or merged with the introduction) to better contextualize contributions.  
- The Line 86 claim about zero-shot classifiers is slightly misleading; the core contribution is latency-aware optimization of crowd labeling and should be clarified early.  
- Experimental results (Tables 2–3) support that LENSE reduces latency without sacrificing accuracy relative to some baselines, though the 5-vote baseline achieves higher accuracy.  
- Emphasizing real-world latency constraints is a key strength of the paper and distinguishes it from prior work.  
- Presentation issues (inconsistent terminology, abstract examples, misplaced related work) obscure the paper’s contributions and reduce impact.  
- Recommendations: replace the abstract example with a detailed case study; standardize and define the entropic-threshold baseline; move related work earlier; refine the abstract to emphasize latency-optimized crowd labeling.  
- Overall: technical content is sound and promising, but exposition must be improved to match the quality of the results.
