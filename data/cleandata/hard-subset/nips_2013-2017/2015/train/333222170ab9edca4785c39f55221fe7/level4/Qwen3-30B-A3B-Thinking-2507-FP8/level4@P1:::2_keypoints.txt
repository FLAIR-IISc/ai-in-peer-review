- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy AI from zero labeled examples by using real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty and reduce human input over time.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to balance accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Approximates the intractable optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search and progressive widening.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing strong empirical gains.  
- Reports over 10× cost reduction for NER compared to full human annotation.  
- Shows accuracy improvements: +8% F1 over single-human labeling and +28% over online learning baselines.  
- Open-source implementation (LENSE) provided for reproducibility.  
- Strength: addresses a practical zero-shot deployment problem with a principled, well-motivated framework.  
- Strength: Bayesian formulation gives a rigorous way to trade off accuracy, cost, and latency absent in prior work.  
- Strength: Experimental results are significant, well-contextualized, and demonstrate scalable cost reductions.  
- Weakness: Exposition clarity suffers—method example (Paragraph 3.3) is overly abstract and needs a concrete step-by-step illustration.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent terminology for the threshold baseline ("entropic" vs "threshold")—should be standardized.  
- Weakness: Related work section is misplaced and should follow problem formulation to better contextualize novelty.  
- Weakness: Paper underexplains the technical reasons why the Bayesian approach outperforms the threshold baseline (e.g., adaptive sequencing vs static thresholds).  
- Recommendation: High-quality contribution; accept with minor revisions to clarify methodology, standardize terms, and reposition related work.
