- Novel framework for on-the-job learning that uses Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.
- Clear organization and explanation of problem formulation, model, and algorithm.
- Key contribution: modeling on-the-job learning as a stochastic game to capture system–crowd interactions and derive optimal querying policies.
- Significant algorithmic contribution: approximation combining Monte Carlo tree search with progressive widening.
- Strong experimental results on NER, sentiment, and image classification showing better quality and lower cost than human-only and machine-only baselines.
- Lack of clarity on applying the approach to the face recognition (non-structured) task, including utility adaptation and information propagation across positions.
- Threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters without explanation of selection or sensitivity analysis.
- Missing comparison to an uncertainty-sampling baseline similar to active learning.
- Unclear why the approach cannot be adapted to active online learning; model updates and final prediction could be handled similarly.
- Paper could draw on existing theory for query-set selection in active/online learning settings.
- Overall: novel, well-motivated, and potentially widely applicable contributions.
- Acceptance concerns: need clearer exposition for the face task, detailed justification and sensitivity for threshold baseline, and inclusion of uncertainty-sampling/active-learning comparisons.
