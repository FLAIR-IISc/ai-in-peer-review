The main contribution of this paper is to provide a more theoretically sound formulation of "on the job learner" proposed in [22]. The student initially starts by giving the crowd a line to create their output, but gradually starts making predictions and occasionally asking the crowd questions. Whether to ask the crowd for a specific input is modeled as a decision problem solved by Monte Carlo tree search.

The presentation is generally good, although there is a lack of important information. The empirical results are somewhat disappointing compared to a threshold-based heuristic. However, it certainly seems like a reasonable approach and deserves further investigation.
