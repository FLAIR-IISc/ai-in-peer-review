- Proposes "on-the-job learning": algorithm may query an annotator after receiving an input but before producing a prediction.
- Application to structured prediction (querying sequence elements) is clear; face recognition application is not well-explained.
- Unclear whether face recognition queries were entire instances; in non-structured settings information won't propagate between examples, calling the utility function's applicability into question.
- Threshold baseline depends on two seemingly arbitrary parameters without justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Missing comparison to an uncertainty sampling baseline commonly used in active learning.
- Claimed distinction from active online learning may be overstated; active learning could be adapted by treating the sequence as the active set and updating the model after query responses but before final prediction.
- Adapting active learning would allow leveraging existing theoretical work for query selection, though temporal aspects may differ.
- Overall: framework appears novel but applicability seems limited and key implementation/experimental details are unclear.
