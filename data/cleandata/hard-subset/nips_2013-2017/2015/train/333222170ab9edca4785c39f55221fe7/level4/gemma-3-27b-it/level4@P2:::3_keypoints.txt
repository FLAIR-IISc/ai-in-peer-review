- Proposes a hybrid on-the-job learning method that combines crowdsourced labels and an online-trained model, reducing crowd reliance as the model improves.  
- Frames the interaction as a stochastic game using a CRF prediction model where the system issues timed queries and maximizes expected utility.  
- Incorporates response times by issuing queries at times s with response delays t to optimize under a time/cost budget.  
- Computes expected utility via a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s); continuous time makes exact optimization intractable.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search plus TD learning (Algorithm 1) as an approximation for decision-making under continuous time.  
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment, and face ID against majority voting (1/3/5 votes), online learning, a “threshold” baseline, and LENSE.  
- LENSE generally outperforms baselines except the 5-vote crowdsourced version; the threshold baseline performs nearly as well and has lower latency on NER.  
- Shows the model needs fewer crowd labels over time with sufficient capacity and achieves high accuracy from the first example vs. online learning.  
- Paper is original, principled, and significant, likely to generate discussion and adoption, but clarity issues could reduce impact.  
- Primary clarity concern: continuous-time formulation adds complexity with limited empirical benefit; recommend presenting it as an extension and focusing core method without continuous time.  
- Authors should highlight the strong performance of the threshold baseline, even with sequence label dependencies, to aid adoption.  
- Results/presentation issues: incorrect “beats human performance” claim for NER (5-vote), missing 5-vote results for Face-ID and Sentiment, and missing/bolding inconsistencies for best results and latency values.  
- Threshold baseline is absent from the sentiment experiment and should be included in Table 3 and Figure 4.  
- Minor methodological fixes needed: clarify t_deadline and reporting criteria; include s in p(y,r,t|x,q,s) in Eq.2; rename F(q) in Eq.5 to avoid confusion; correct the threshold baseline expression.
