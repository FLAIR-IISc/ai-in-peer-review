1. Main contribution: provides a theoretically rigorous formulation of an "on-the-job learner" that transitions from crowd-sourced labels to self-prediction, using MCTS for query decisions.  
2. Presentation is generally clear but omits several critical implementation details.  
3. Empirical gains over a threshold-based heuristic are modest, offering limited evidence of superiority.  
4. Overall approach is logically sound and worth exploring further, but current evaluation does not establish clear practical advantages.  
5. Equation (2) is problematic: LHS seems to lack conditioning on state s, and the role/disappearance of y in p^{(R)}(r_i | y, q_i) is unexplained.  
6. Algorithm 1 uses N(s) without definition—unclear whether this denotes visit count, state frequency, or something else.  
7. Threshold baseline parameters (0.3 and 0.88) are unjustified—no explanation of how they were chosen.  
8. Baseline comparison is unclear: are the "online" systems state-of-the-art? If not, why use non-competitive baselines; if yes, clarify that gains may be due to human annotation and provide examples.  
9. Recommend a brief error analysis and specific examples showing where human input improves over the best learned model.  
10. Empirical validation must be strengthened to show tangible benefits over simpler alternatives and to determine whether improvements come from the adaptation mechanism or simply the human-in-the-loop setup.
