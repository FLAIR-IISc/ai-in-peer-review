This paper tackles the problem of creating high-accuracy classifiers starting with zero labeled examples. A key component of the proposed approach is handling noisy crowd respondents; while this topic has been considered from many angles in the literature, the focus on timing and latency optimization factors is a novel and interesting contribution.

A strength of this paper is its comparison of classifier accuracy to human performance. The results in Table 2 are particularly interesting, showing that the LENSE system outperforms the 3-vote baseline but underperforms the 5-vote baseline. This highlights a significant trade-off concerning the relative efficiencies of de-noising crowd responses via additional consensus versus using the LENSE system's approach.

However, the clarity of the paper could be substantially improved.
*   The example given in the paragraph starting at Line 154 is abstract and difficult to follow. For better readability, it would be beneficial to use a concrete example involving a specific query with realistic crowd responses and timing data.
*   In Tables 2 and 3, the inconsistent naming for the threshold-based baseline, which is referred to as both "entropic" and "threshold," creates unnecessary confusion.
*   The placement of the related work in Section 5 is unconventional. Presenting this context earlier in the paper would likely be more helpful for the reader.
*   There appears to be a missing figure reference on Line 086 ("Figure ??").

Overall, the paper addresses a compelling problem, but the explanations are somewhat opaque, which detracts from the impact of the work.
