- Introduces "on the job learning" with well-defined sequential query process for structured tasks, but its application to face recognition is unclear (what constitutes a query; was the whole instance queried?).
- Because examples don't propagate information between each other in the face-recognition setup, the proposed utility function's rationale is questionable.
- The threshold baseline uses two seemingly arbitrary parameters without justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Evaluation omits a standard uncertainty-sampling (active learning) baseline that would strengthen comparisons.
- Authors distinguish from active online learning, yet active-learning methods could be adapted to this sequential domain and leverage established query-selection theory.
- Overall, the framework is novel but lacks clarity and appears limited in generalizability.
