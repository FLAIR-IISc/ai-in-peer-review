The main contribution of this paper is to provide a more theoretically sound formulation of an "on-the-job" learner, building upon the work proposed in [22]. This learner initially relies on the crowd to produce its output but gradually begins making its own predictions and querying the crowd only when necessary. The decision of whether to query the crowd for any given input is modeled as a decision problem, solved using a Monte Carlo Tree Search.

The presentation is generally clear, although it lacks important details in some areas. The empirical results, compared to a threshold-based heuristic, are somewhat underwhelming. Nevertheless, the approach seems reasonable and warrants further investigation.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should address the following points:

1. Equation (2) appears to be incorrect. The left-hand side should also be conditioned on s. Furthermore, it is unclear how the y in p(R|y, q_i) is eliminated. A clarification on this would be helpful.
2. The definition of N(s) in Algorithm 1 is not provided. Please specify what this notation represents.
3. The threshold baseline uses the values 0.3 and 0.88. Could the authors explain how these specific values were obtained?
4. As someone unfamiliar with the exact tasks used in this study, it is unclear whether the "online" systems represent the best machine-learned systems for this task. If not, why not? If they do, then any gain achieved by the proposed method is likely due to human annotation. It would be helpful to provide examples of such cases and include some error analysis to support the findings.

This paper presents a theoretical formulation of an "on-the-job" learner that transitions from a pure crowdsourcing system to a learned model, querying the crowd only for low-confidence inputs. While the empirical results are mixed, the work is a good starting point for further discussion and exploration in this area.
