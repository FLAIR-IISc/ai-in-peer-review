- Core idea of “on-the-job learning” is sound and well-explained for structured prediction, where queries can target specific sequence elements.  
- Face recognition application is unclear—uncertain what is being queried and without sequential propagation the utility function seems less meaningful.  
- The threshold baseline depends on two seemingly arbitrary parameters; the paper lacks discussion of how they were chosen and sensitivity analysis.  
- Missing comparison to uncertainty sampling (common active learning baseline); adding this would strengthen evaluation.  
- Active learning methods could be adapted to the sequential setting and might provide useful theoretical/query-selection insights despite not capturing full temporal aspects.  
- Overall framework is potentially novel but implementation details and broad applicability are unclear, limiting impact.  
- Recommended fixes: clarify the face-recognition query setup, justify baseline parameter choices, and compare against relevant active-learning strategies.
