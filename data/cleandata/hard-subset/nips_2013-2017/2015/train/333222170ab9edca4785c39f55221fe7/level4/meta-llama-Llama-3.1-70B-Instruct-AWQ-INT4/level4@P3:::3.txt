This paper presents a hybrid approach for using both crowdsourced labels and an incrementally (online) trained model to address prediction problems. The core idea is to lean heavily on the crowd as the system is ramping up, learn from the labels thus acquired, and then use the crowd less and less often as the model becomes more confident. This is done via a sophisticated framing of the problem as a stochastic game based on a CRF prediction model in which the system and the crowd are both players.

The authors provide a clear summary of the main ideas in the paper, relating them to previous work at NIPS and elsewhere. They also demonstrate a good understanding of the paper's content, highlighting its strengths and weaknesses. The reviewer's comments are detailed, specific, and polite, providing constructive feedback to the authors.

The paper's technical soundness is well-evaluated, with the reviewer noting that the approach is "elegantly cast as a stochastic game that models many aspects of the data, true labels, and crowd worker responses." The reviewer also appreciates the paper's originality, novelty, and interesting results, which show the method's potential for practical applications.

However, the reviewer raises some concerns about the paper's clarity, particularly with respect to the introduction of continuous time. They suggest that the core method could be developed fully without the introduction of time, making it easier to understand and implement. The reviewer also notes that the addition of continuous time seems to yield minimal benefits in performance.

The reviewer provides some minor comments on the method description, including the use of notation and the clarity of certain equations. They also suggest some improvements to the presentation of the results, such as including the 5-vote case for the Face-ID and Sentiment tasks and bolding the best performance in the tables.

Overall, the review is well-structured, clear, and provides a balanced evaluation of the paper's strengths and weaknesses. The reviewer's comments are constructive and specific, providing the authors with useful feedback to improve their work.

**Rating:**

* Quality: 9/10
* Clarity: 7/10
* Originality: 9/10
* Significance: 9/10

**Recommendation:**

The paper is recommended for acceptance, but the authors should address the concerns raised by the reviewer regarding clarity, particularly with respect to the introduction of continuous time. The authors should also consider the minor comments on the method description and the presentation of the results.
