- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy from zero training data using real-time crowdsourcing that decreases as the system learns.  
- Formulates the problem as a stochastic game between system and crowd under Bayesian decision theory to balance accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Policy decides which input parts to query, whether to wait for responses, and when to return a prediction.  
- Optimal policy is intractable; authors propose an approximation using Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with progressive widening for continuous time.  
- System LENSE evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, compared to baselines including majority vote, online learning with oracle labels, and a Threshold ablation.  
- Results: LENSE matches ~5-person human accuracy at much lower cost and outperforms standard online learning, especially early in deployment.  
- Strength: novel, practical framework addressing cold-start prediction without large labeled datasets.  
- Strength: principled, decision-theoretic formulation that moves beyond heuristic hybrid systems.  
- Strength: solid technical contribution (MCTS approximation) and comprehensive experiments across tasks.  
- Strength: authors release code and data, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: clarity of presentation is weak, which could limit impact and adoption.  
- Weakness: introducing continuous time complicates formulation and algorithm without convincing experimental benefit.  
- Suggestion: present a simpler (non-temporal) core method first, then introduce continuous-time as an extension.  
- Reporting issue: 5-vote human baseline missing for Sentiment and Faces in several tables/figures and should be included.  
- Reporting issue: Threshold baseline (key ablation) is absent from sentiment experiments and must be reported.  
- Reporting issue: best-performing values are not consistently highlighted in results tables, reducing readability.  
- Detailed question: clarify the t_deadline or decision criterion used to determine sufficient confidence to return predictions.  
- Technical correction: Equation (3) should likely condition on s (query issue times): p(y, r, t | x, q, s).  
- Technical correction: the Threshold baseline inequality appears incorrect; it should reflect that increasing m reduces uncertainty (example provided).  
- Overall: high-quality, significant work worthy of acceptance pending clearer presentation and more complete experimental reporting.
