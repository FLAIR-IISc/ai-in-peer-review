This paper addresses the challenging and important problem of training high-accuracy classifiers starting with zero labeled examples. The proposed system, LENSE, uses crowd-sourcing to acquire labels and incorporates novel factors such as the timing and latency of crowd responses to handle noisy data. While the problem is compelling and the core ideas are promising, the overall presentation could be significantly improved for clarity.

**Strengths:**

*   The paper's focus on optimizing for timing and latency when de-noising crowd responses is a novel and interesting contribution to the literature on learning from crowds.
*   A key strength of the evaluation is the direct comparison of the classifier's accuracy to human performance baselines. This provides a clear and valuable benchmark for the system's effectiveness.
*   The results presented in Table 2 are particularly insightful. The finding that LENSE outperforms a 3-vote majority baseline but is surpassed by a 5-vote baseline offers a significant insight into the relative efficiencies of different de-noising strategies. This comparison effectively frames the value of the LENSE approach against the simpler, albeit potentially more costly, method of collecting additional consensus votes.

**Areas for Improvement:**

*   The paper's clarity is a major concern. The explanations are often opaque, making it difficult to fully grasp the methodology. For instance, the illustrative example beginning on Line 154 is highly abstract and hard to follow. To enhance readability and comprehension, the authors should consider replacing this with a concrete, step-by-step example using a specific query with plausible crowd responses and associated timings. This would provide a much clearer demonstration of the proposed method in action.
*   The placement of the "Related Work" section as late as Section 5 is unconventional and detracts from the paper's flow. This section should ideally appear earlier to contextualize the work and establish its novelty with respect to the existing literature. Moving it closer to the introduction would help readers better situate the paper's contributions from the outset.
*   There is an inconsistency in the terminology used for one of the baselines in Tables 2 and 3. The same baseline appears to be referred to as both "entropic" and "threshold" at different points. This should be standardized throughout the paper to avoid confusion.

**Minor Issues:**

*   On Line 086, there appears to be a missing reference to a figure ("Figure ??"). Please ensure all cross-references in the manuscript are correct.

In summary, this paper tackles a compelling problem with a novel approach. However, its potential impact is currently hindered by a lack of clarity in the explanations and an unconventional structure. Addressing the issues of clarity, particularly with a more concrete example, and reorganizing the paper would substantially strengthen the submission.
