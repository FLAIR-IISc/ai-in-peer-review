- Paper tackles on-the-job learning: deciding which parts to crowdsource per input, waiting for responses, and predicting when confident, reducing crowd reliance over time.  
- Formalizes setting as a Bayesian decision-theoretic stochastic game optimizing accuracy, latency, and monetary cost.  
- Uses MCTS with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy for continuous-time responses.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification against human n-vote, online learning, and a threshold heuristic; includes real-time crowdsourcing and open-source code.  
- Differs from prior online/active learning by actively querying before committing and optimizing query timing/parallelism; model continues learning at test time.  
- Strength: addresses an important, practical setting where labeled data is scarce but immediate accurate decisions are needed.  
- Strength: principled Bayesian framework that captures trade-offs and intuitive behaviors like redundant labels and parallel queries.  
- Strength: MCTS with progressive widening is a reasonable algorithmic approximation.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation across multiple datasets, baselines, and a real-time crowd setup increases credibility and reproducibility.  
- Insight: LENSE outperforms 3-vote but underperforms 5-vote, highlighting trade-offs between consensus de-noising and adaptive querying.  
- Weakness: example at Line 154 is abstract and hard to follow; needs a concrete, fully worked query sequence with timestamps and posterior updates.  
- Weakness: inconsistent baseline naming (“threshold” vs “entropic”) across tables and text; standardization needed.  
- Weakness: related work placed after experiments disrupts flow; should be moved earlier for better context.  
- Weakness: broken figure reference at Line 086 (“Figure ??”) must be fixed.  
- Weakness: some modeling choices (batch vs sequence queries, utility weight selection) lack clear exposition and motivational narrative.  
- Quality: formulation and MCTS approximation are technically sound, but presentation issues reduce transparency.  
- Clarity: generally structured but hampered by the opaque example, naming inconsistency, and broken reference.  
- Originality: novel framing of on-the-job learning as a continuous-time stochastic game balancing latency and cost with accuracy.  
- Significance: practical and broadly relevant; shows cost reductions over full human annotation and improvements over single-vote and online baselines.  
- Specific fix: correct the Figure ?? reference at Line 086.  
- Specific fix: replace abstract Line 154 example with step-by-step example including token labels, crowd responses, timestamps, actions, and utility.  
- Specific fix: unify baseline name in Tables 2 and 3 and clarify the heuristic’s operation in captions.  
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, principled formulation, reasonable approximation, strong empirical comparisons, reproducible code.  
- Cons for acceptance: opaque explanations, inconsistent terminology, broken reference, and unclear guidance when human consensus (e.g., 5-vote) may outperform the method.  
- Overall: compelling and principled work with solid evidence, but needs clearer exposition, consistent terminology, and corrected references.
