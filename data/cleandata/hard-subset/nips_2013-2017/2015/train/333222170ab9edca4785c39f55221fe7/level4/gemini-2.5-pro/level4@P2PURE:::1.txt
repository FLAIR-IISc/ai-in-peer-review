This paper presents a theoretically-grounded formulation of the "on-the-job learner" introduced in [22]. The proposed system initially relies on crowdsourcing for all predictions but gradually transitions to an autonomous model that selectively queries the crowd for inputs on which it has low confidence. The decision of whether to query the crowd for a given input is framed as a sequential decision problem, which the authors solve using Monte Carlo Tree Search.

The paper is generally well-written but lacks clarity on several important details. While the proposed approach is theoretically sound and merits further investigation, the empirical results are not entirely convincing. Specifically, the performance improvement over a simple threshold-based heuristic is underwhelming. Despite these weaknesses, the paper introduces a promising research direction that could stimulate valuable discussion.

Please address the following points for clarification:

1.  Equation (2) is unclear. It appears the left-hand side (LHS) should also be conditioned on the state `s`. Furthermore, please clarify how the term `y` is marginalized out or otherwise vanishes from the expression `p{R}(ri | y, q_i)`.

2.  In Algorithm 1, the term `N(s)` is used without a prior definition. Please define this variable.

3.  The paper should explain how the specific values of 0.3 and 0.88 were selected for the threshold-based baseline. Were these parameters tuned on a validation set?

4.  The choice of the "online" systems used for comparison needs justification. Are these systems representative of the state-of-the-art for the given tasks? If they are not, the reported gains might be misleading. If they are, then any improvement over this baseline is attributable solely to the human annotation component. In this case, it would be insightful to provide an error analysis that characterizes the instances where human intervention is most critical, which would better highlight the practical utility of the proposed framework.
