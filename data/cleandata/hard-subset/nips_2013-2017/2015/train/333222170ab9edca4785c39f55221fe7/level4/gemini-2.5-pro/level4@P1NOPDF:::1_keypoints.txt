- Proposes a theoretically grounded formulation of the "on the job learner" that uses Monte Carlo Tree Search to decide when to query the crowd versus predict autonomously.
- Presentation is generally clear but omits important details.
- Empirical results are underwhelming compared to a simple threshold-based heuristic.
- Equation (2) is unclear: the left-hand side should likely be conditioned on s, and it's unclear how y drops out of p_R(r_i | y, q_i) in the derivation.
- Algorithm 1 is ambiguous because N(s) is not defined.
- The choice of threshold baseline values (0.3 and 0.88) is unexplained.
- Need clarification whether the "online" comparison systems are state-of-the-art; if not, justify their selection; if they are, provide error analysis showing where human annotation provides the benefit.
- Overall: a principled framework that transitions from crowdsourcing to semi-automation, worthy of further investigation despite weaker empirical evidence.
