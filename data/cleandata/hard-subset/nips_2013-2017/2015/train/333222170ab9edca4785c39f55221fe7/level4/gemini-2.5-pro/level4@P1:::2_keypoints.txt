- Introduces "on-the-job learning" to deploy high-accuracy classifiers from zero labeled examples by querying a crowd per input and updating the model over time.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to balance accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Proposes a Monte Carlo Tree Search approximation to find policies in the intractable state space.  
- Novel approach: actively queries before predicting on each instance, unlike standard online or active learning.  
- Problem formulation is compelling and practically relevant, addressing systems that must be accurate from day one.  
- Optimization of query timing and latency within a decision-theoretic framework is a strong, novel contribution.  
- Experiments compare the system to human annotation baselines, showing LENSE outperforms 3-vote but underperforms 5-vote consensus.  
- Strong empirical results: large cost reduction on NER while boosting performance compared to full human annotation.  
- Explanations of system behavior are opaque and would benefit from clearer detail.  
- Needs concrete, realistic walkthrough examples (query times, crowd responses) to illustrate belief updates and actions.  
- Inconsistent terminology in experiments ("entropic" vs. "Threshold baseline") creates confusion.  
- Paper organization is suboptimal (Related Work placed after experiments); moving it would improve flow.
