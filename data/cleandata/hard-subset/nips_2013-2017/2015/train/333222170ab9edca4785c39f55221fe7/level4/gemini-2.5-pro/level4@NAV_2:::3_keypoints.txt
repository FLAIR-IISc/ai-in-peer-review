- Paper introduces "on-the-job learning": hybrid real-time crowdsourcing + online-trained model that relies on crowd labels initially and reduces queries as model gains confidence.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game under Bayesian decision theory, with actions to maximize a utility balancing accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Continuous-time modeling of queries/responses is used, making optimal policy intractable; authors approximate with Monte Carlo Tree Search and progressive widening.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification tasks against baselines: human majority vote (n=1,3,5), online learning with true labels, and a simpler "threshold" decision-theoretic baseline.  
- Results: LENSE achieves strong cost–performance tradeoffs, outperforms online learning early, reduces cost vs. full human annotation, and is comparable to threshold and 5-vote baselines.  
- Strength — originality/significance: first complete decision-theoretic framework for combining real-time crowdsourcing and online model improvement; elegant formulation with practical impact.  
- Strength — quality: strong experimental results and good comparison to a strong ablation (threshold baseline).  
- Weakness — clarity: continuous-time model complicates formulation, utility, and algorithm; added complexity seems unjustified by modest empirical gains over the simpler threshold variant.  
- Suggestion: present the core framework without time first (i.e., the threshold baseline) and then introduce continuous-time as an extension to improve clarity and adoption.  
- Suggestion: be transparent about the strong performance of the threshold baseline, since it’s a significant empirical finding.  
- Reporting issues: claim that model “boosts performance relative to expert labels” is unsupported—5-vote baseline outperforms LENSE on NER.  
- Reporting issues: 5-vote baseline results missing for Sentiment and Faces in Tables/Figures; Threshold baseline missing from some experiments—these should be added and best results properly highlighted.  
- Method clarity issues: unclear stopping criterion—whether experiments used a hard deadline or uncertainty-based termination despite including a time penalty wT.  
- Notation inconsistency: Equation (3) conditions on query issue times s, but Equation (1) omits s from the conditioning set.  
- Possible typo in Threshold baseline description: inequality for deciding number of queries appears reversed and should likely be ≤ rather than ≥.
