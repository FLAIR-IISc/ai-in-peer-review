- Introduces "on-the-job learning" enabling deployment from zero training examples by integrating real-time crowdsourcing.
- Formalizes the problem as a Bayesian stochastic game that trades off latency, cost, and accuracy when querying the crowd versus predicting.
- Proposes a Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) approximation to compute the policy and implements it in a system called LENSE.
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment classification, and image classification, showing large cost reductions for NER and smaller reductions for the other tasks.
- Demonstrates higher accuracy than pure online learning and single-human-label baselines while reducing annotation cost.
- Strong theoretical foundation via Bayesian decision theory and a novel stochastic-game formulation beyond prior active learning work.
- Practical contribution: end-to-end implementation and multi-domain empirical evaluation.
- Presentation lacks technical detail—Equation (2) should condition on query history s since accuracy depends on responses.
- Threshold baseline parameters (0.3 and 0.88) are unexplained; need justification for their selection and task-specific effectiveness.
- Algorithm 1 is unclear—meaning of N(s) in the MCTS implementation must be specified.
- Insufficient justification that the online learning baseline is the best possible ML comparator; gains may come from extra human labels rather than the framework itself.
- Empirical results are mixed: dramatic gains on NER but only modest (~2x) cost reductions on sentiment and image tasks.
- Needs error analysis to explain task-dependent performance differences (e.g., NER may benefit from a dominant "none" class).
- Crowd response model is under-specified; reporting only "roughly 70% accurate" is inadequate and the assumed pR(r|yq)=0.7 if r=yq is overly simplistic.
- Claims of "more than an order of magnitude" improvement may be overstated without clearer baseline cost context.
- Overall promising and potentially significant, but requires clearer technical details, parameter justification, algorithmic clarification, and deeper empirical analysis.
