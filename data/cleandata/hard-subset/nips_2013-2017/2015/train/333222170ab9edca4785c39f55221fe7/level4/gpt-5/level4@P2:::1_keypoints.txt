- Paper formalizes on-the-job learning with no labeled data, using real-time crowdsourcing at test time and gradually shifting to a learned model.  
- Decision problem cast as a stochastic game under Bayesian decision theory trading accuracy, latency, and monetary cost.  
- Exact optimization is intractable; policy approximated with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and progressive widening for continuous time.  
- Prediction model is a CRF; crowd responses modeled as noisy labels with a response-time distribution.  
- System evaluated on NER, sentiment, and face/image classification using a retainer pool for real-time responses.  
- Reported results: substantial cost reductions on NER, improvements over expert labels in that setting, and gains vs single-vote human and online learning baselines.  
- Open-source implementation (LENSE) and code/data released.  
- Extends prior crowd-in-the-loop and on-the-job systems with a principled decision-theoretic formulation and practical search procedure.  
- Contrasts with traditional online/active learning by querying at test time and handling structured outputs.  
- Strength: principled Bayesian utility explicitly balancing accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Strength: practical planning via MCTS with progressive widening for large continuous-time state spaces.  
- Strength: structured prediction benefits (CRF) and alignment with asynchronous real-time crowdsourcing.  
- Strength: empirical promise on NER and public code for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: several modeling details underspecified or confusing (utility, conditioning, environment model).  
- Weakness: empirical gains over simple threshold heuristics are modest in places and lack error analysis.  
- Weakness: online machine baselines not clearly positioned against strong task-specific baselines, obscuring source of improvements.  
- Weakness: simplified crowd model (fixed 70% accuracy) with limited robustness analysis for worker heterogeneity or calibration errors.  
- Weakness: limited ablations and sensitivity analysis on cost/time weights, assumed worker accuracy, and parallel vs sequential querying.  
- Request for clarification: Eq. (2)/(3) conditioning and step-by-step derivation of p(r′ | x, r, q).  
- Request: standardize notation in Algorithm 1 and define variables/counts for UCT and progressive widening.  
- Request: explain how threshold baseline constants were chosen and report sensitivity.  
- Request: justify strength of machine baselines or include stronger baselines and quantify where crowd supervision helps.  
- Request: report environment model calibration, per-task/worker reliabilities, sensitivity to miscalibration, and adaptive learning of rater reliabilities.  
- Request: show policy changes with wM and wT, analyze parallel vs sequential querying, and compare MCTS to a greedy myopic policy.  
- Request: detail online updating of pθ (frequency, optimizer, regularization), use of gold labels vs crowd responses, and measures against feedback loops.  
- Request: clarify evaluation protocol for simulated responses, variance vs live conditions, and NER dataset/expert label details.  
- Overall: principled and promising work with useful implementation, but needs clearer modeling details, stronger baselines, ablations, and error analysis to substantiate claims beyond NER.
