- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy a high-accuracy system from scratch by querying a crowd in real time and updating the model to reduce crowd reliance over time.
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game between the system and the crowd.
- Uses Bayesian decision theory to define a utility balancing accuracy, monetary cost, and latency.
- Approximates the intractable optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) plus progressive widening to handle continuous time.
- Implements LENSE and evaluates on NER, sentiment, and image classification; reports cost reduction vs pure crowdsourcing and accuracy gains over an online learning baseline.
- Strength: addresses a novel, practical problem bridging supervised learning and crowdsourcing.
- Strength: principled, theoretically sound formulation using a stochastic-game/Bayesian framework.
- Strength: technically well-motivated MCTS-based solution for complex decision-making.
- Strength: reproducible work with open-source code and datasets.
- Weakness: clarity issues—needs explicit derivation of conditional probabilities (marginalizing y and t) and clearer notation/definitions in pseudocode (e.g., N(σ)).
- Weakness: threshold baseline hyperparameters appear arbitrary; require justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: online learning baseline is insufficiently characterized; must confirm it is a strong representative model.
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis showing which instances benefit from on-the-job querying.
- Weakness: modest gains on some tasks raise questions about the practical benefit of the computationally expensive MCTS vs simpler heuristics; needs complexity–performance discussion.
- Summary: solid, theoretically grounded contribution that would be strengthened by clearer model exposition and a deeper, better-justified experimental evaluation.
