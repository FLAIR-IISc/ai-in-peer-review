- Tackles building high-accuracy classifiers from zero labeled examples, focusing on noisy crowd responses with timing/latency optimization.  
- Frames on-the-job learning as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Approximates the optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search.  
- Evaluated on named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification datasets.  
- Novel framing and application of Bayesian decision theory + MCTS to this problem.  
- Shows significant cost reductions versus full human annotation, especially >10x on the NER task.  
- Achieves an 8% F1 gain over a single human label and 28% F1 gain over online learning.  
- Publicly available implementation (LENSE) enhances reproducibility.  
- Comparison to human-vote baselines is insightful: outperforms 3-vote but not 5-vote consensus.  
- Illustrative example (line 154) is abstract and hard to follow; a concrete query/timing example would help.  
- Inconsistent naming of the threshold baseline (“entropic” vs “threshold”) creates confusion.  
- Related work is placed unconventionally (after experiments) rather than before evaluation.  
- Mathematical formulation is rigorous but could use more intuition for non-experts in stochastic games.  
- Overall recommendation: strong candidate for acceptance pending minor clarity and presentation revisions.
