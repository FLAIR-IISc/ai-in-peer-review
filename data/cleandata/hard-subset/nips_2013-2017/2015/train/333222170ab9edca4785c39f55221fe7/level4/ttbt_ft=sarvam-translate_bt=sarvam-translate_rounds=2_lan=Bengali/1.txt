The main contribution of this paper is to provide a more theoretically sound formulation of "On the Job Learner" as proposed in [22]. The student initially starts the crowd with a line to create their output, but gradually begins to predict and occasionally asks the crowd questions. Whether to ask the crowd a specific input is modeled as a decision problem solved by Monte Carlo trees.

The presentation is generally good, although there is a lack of important information. The empirical results are somewhat disappointing compared to threshold-based heuristics. However, it certainly seems like a reasonable approach and deserves further investigation.

This paper presents a theoretical formulation of "On the Job Learner" that starts as a pure crowdsourcing system and gradually transforms into a learning model that only asks the crowd for low-confidence inputs. The empirical results are somewhat mixed, but it's a good place to start a discussion on this path.
