- Proposes LENSE, a hybrid system that uses crowdsourced labels initially and an online-trained model increasingly as confidence grows.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game with the system and crowd as players, using a CRF prediction model.  
- System issues token queries over time to maximize expected utility under a deadline, balancing cost and latency.  
- Continuous-time model makes exact optimization intractable, so Monte Carlo Tree Search is used as an approximation.  
- Experiments on NER, sentiment, and face identification compare majority vote (5-vote), online learning, a “threshold” baseline, and LENSE.  
- LENSE often outperforms baselines in precision/recall/F1, but the 5-vote crowd frequently achieves the best performance.  
- Shows LENSE requires fewer crowdsourced labels over time and provides high accuracy from the first example by leveraging crowd expertise.  
- Paper is original, significant, and potentially impactful—first principled complete proposal for hybrid crowd+model learning.  
- Main clarity issue: introduction of continuous time adds complexity while offering limited empirical benefit.  
- Threshold baseline often matches or beats LENSE on latency and sometimes performance (notably on face-ID and marginally on NER).  
- Recommendation: emphasize the threshold method as a simpler core contribution and present continuous time as an extension.  
- Reporting issues: claim of "beats human performance" is incorrect when compared to 5-vote NER; 5-vote results missing for Face-ID and Sentiment in several tables/figures.  
- Presentation fixes: bold best (5-vote) and relevant latency values; include the Threshold baseline in the sentiment experiment tables/figures.  
- Method clarity comments: unclear what deadline/stop criterion was used in experiments (entropy threshold or fixed deadline).  
- Technical corrections requested: equation (2) likely needs s included, change notation in equation (5) to avoid q confusion, and fix Threshold baseline expression.  
- Overall verdict: strong, novel work deserving acceptance, but needs improved clarity and clearer treatment/justification of continuous time.
