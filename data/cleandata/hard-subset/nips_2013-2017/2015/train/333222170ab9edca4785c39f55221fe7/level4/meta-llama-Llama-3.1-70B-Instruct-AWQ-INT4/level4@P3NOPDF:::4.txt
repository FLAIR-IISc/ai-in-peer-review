The authors present a novel framework for "on the job learning," where annotators are queried for information between receiving an input example and outputting a prediction. While the algorithm's functionality is clear in the context of structured prediction tasks, where elements of a sequence are queried, its application to the face recognition task is less apparent. Specifically, it is unclear whether the instance being queried is the sole point of application. In non-structured settings, the model does not propagate information between adjacent positions (examples), which raises questions about the utility function's relevance.

The threshold baseline introduced in the paper has two parameters with seemingly arbitrary values. The authors should provide more information on the origin of these values and the sensitivity of the results to these parameters. Additionally, incorporating a baseline of uncertainty sampling, similar to those used in active learning, would be a valuable comparison.

The authors emphasize the distinction between their setting and active online learning, but it is worth noting that active learning techniques can be adapted to this domain with relative ease. For instance, the model could be updated upon receiving responses and output a prediction at the end. Although this approach would not capture the temporal nature of the task, it would allow the authors to draw upon existing theory for selecting the query set. Overall, the proposed framework appears novel, but some details remain unclear, and its applicability seems somewhat restricted.
