- Main contribution: a more theoretically grounded formulation of an "on the job learner" from [22].  
- Method: system starts by queuing the crowd and gradually transitions to self-prediction, deciding queries via Monte Carlo Tree Search.  
- Presentation: generally good but missing important technical details.  
- Empirical results: comparison to a threshold-based heuristic is underwhelming.  
- Overall assessment: approach is reasonable and merits further investigation.  
- Clarification needed (Eq. 2): LHS should be conditioned on s and explain why y disappears from p_R(r_i | y, q_i).  
- Clarification needed (Alg. 1): define N(s).  
- Clarification needed (baseline): justify where the 0.3 and 0.88 threshold values come from.  
- Clarification/analysis needed: are the "online" systems the best ML systems for these tasks; if so, gains may be from human annotation—provide error analysis and example cases.  
- Summary takeaway: theoretical framing of crowd→model transition is useful, but empirical and explanatory gaps limit conclusions.
