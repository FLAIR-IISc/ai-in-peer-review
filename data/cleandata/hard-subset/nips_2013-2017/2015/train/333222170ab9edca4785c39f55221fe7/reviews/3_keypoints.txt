- Proposes a hybrid system that uses crowdsourced labels early and gradually relies on an online-trained model as confidence increases.  
- Frames the problem as a stochastic game based on a CRF where the system and crowd are players issuing queries and receiving responses with utility U(q,r).  
- Incorporates continuous time (query send times s and response times t) and optimizes expected utility w.r.t. a t_deadline that controls query timing and rate.  
- Computes expected utility via a simulation dynamics model P(y,r,t|x,q,s); continuous time makes exact optimization intractable, so MCTS + TD learning (Alg.1) are used as approximations.  
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment, and face-ID tasks against baselines: majority votes (1,3,5), online learning (oracle), and a non-time “threshold” baseline.  
- The full model (LENSE) outperforms most baselines but not the 5-vote majority; the threshold baseline performs nearly as well and sometimes with lower latency.  
- Shows the model requires decreasing numbers of crowd labels over time and attains high initial accuracy by leveraging crowd expertise when uncertainty is high.  
- Work is original, significant, and likely to spur discussion and adoption; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Major clarity problems, especially the mid-Section-3 introduction of continuous time, which complicates exposition and algorithmic details.  
- Continuous-time extension yields limited experimental benefit, so its added complexity may not be justified given the threshold baseline’s strong performance.  
- Recommends presenting the core method without continuous time first and treating continuous time as an extension to improve clarity and adoption.  
- Reporting errors/omissions: claim of “beats human performance” is incorrect for 5-vote NER; 5-vote results are missing for Face-ID and Sentiment in several tables/figures.  
- Presentation/formatting issues: best results and certain latency values are not bolded; threshold baseline is missing from the sentiment experiments in Table 3 and Figure 4.  
- Clarify experimental use of t_deadline and criteria for reporting answers (entropic threshold vs. time).  
- Fix notation: equation (2) should include s in the distribution p(y,r,t|x,q,s).  
- Improve notation/notation clash: equation (5) uses q for a distribution function which is confusing—choose a different letter.  
- Possible typo in Threshold baseline description: the uncertainty expression/inequality appears incorrect (should reflect decreasing uncertainty with increasing m).
