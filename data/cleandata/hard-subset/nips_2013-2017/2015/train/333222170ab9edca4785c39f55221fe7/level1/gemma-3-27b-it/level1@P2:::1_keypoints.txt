- Presents on-the-job learning as a stochastic game, using Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Core idea: strategically query crowd in real-time to resolve uncertainty and reduce human reliance as the model improves.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy.  
- Open-source implementation (LENSE) provided, enhancing reproducibility and follow-up research.  
- Experimental results show substantial cost reductions and accuracy gains versus baselines, notably on NER.  
- Ablation study (Figure 3) clarifies how model capacity affects reliance on human supervision.  
- Authors handle worker quality by freezing a pool of responses, a practical mitigation strategy.  
- Technical foundation is solid; Bayesian decision framework is principled and well-motivated.  
- Connection to online/active learning is well-articulated; formulation emphasizes simultaneous learning and prediction.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized; Figure 2 and experiment descriptions aid understanding.  
- Areas needing improvement: more detailed description of MCTS implementation and progressive widening.  
- Needs deeper analysis of the MCTS search space and its impact on performance.  
- Some mathematical notation could be simplified for better readability; Algorithm 1 needs clearer comments.  
- Novel integration of Bayesian decision theory, MCTS, and on-the-job learning; stochastic-game formalization is a fresh perspective.  
- Significant real-world impact potential for cold-start deployment scenarios (e.g., disaster relief, emerging markets).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address MCTS/detail and clarity concerns.
