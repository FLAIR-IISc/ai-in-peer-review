- Proposes "on-the-job learning": a stochastic game between system and crowd that queries parts of inputs and learns during test time using Bayesian decision theory.  
- Balances latency, cost, and accuracy with an approximate solution using MCTS and progressive widening.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing large cost reductions (order-of-magnitude on NER) and accuracy improvements over baselines.  
- Technical soundness: clear theoretical framework, well-defined utility, and a complete algorithmic solution addressing policy intractability.  
- Experiments are thorough with diverse tasks, meaningful baselines, and statistically supported improvements (e.g., +8% F1 over single-human labeling on NER).  
- Clarity: well-organized, clearly written, good figures, and reproducible implementation with code and data released.  
- Novelty: formalizes a new on-the-job learning setting and applies MCTS/progressive widening in a novel way that differs from prior active learning/classification work.  
- Significance: addresses practical scenarios needing immediate high accuracy (e.g., disaster response) and demonstrates real-world impact via LENSE.  
- Limited scalability discussion: experiments use relatively small datasets and do not fully address larger-scale or more complex tasks.  
- Simplifying crowd model: assumes fixed 70% worker accuracy and does not explore varying or heterogeneous crowd quality or adaptation mechanisms.  
- Related work comparison could be strengthened by explicitly contrasting with more recent NIPS papers on Bayesian active learning and noisy observations.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, significant, and well-presented work that advances the state of the art.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong contribution with minor weaknesses.  
- Pro acceptance: novel practical framework, strong theory and experiments, clear writing, reproducible.  
- Con acceptance: limited scalability analysis, simplifying crowd assumptions, slight gap in contrasting very recent related work.
