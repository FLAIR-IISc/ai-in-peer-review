- Proposes "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy predictors from zero labeled data by issuing real-time crowd queries and updating a model online.  
- Frames query-selection as a stochastic game and optimizes a Bayesian utility balancing accuracy, monetary cost, and latency.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy in continuous-time decision space.  
- Implements LENSE and evaluates on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing large cost reductions and strong early-stage accuracy.  
- Technical formulation is principled and well-matched to the trade-offs; MCTS+progressive widening is an appropriate approximation.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive with sensible baselines and transparent reproducibility (code/data released).  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and supported by helpful figures and visualizations.  
- The on-the-job learning problem setting and the game/MCTS framing are novel and advance beyond traditional active/online learning.  
- Addresses a significant practical problem (cold-start deployment) with broad potential impact for hybrid human-AI systems.  
- Weakness: assumes a fixed 70% worker accuracy model, ignoring per-worker and per-task variability.  
- Weakness: relies on a retainer crowd model for real-time responses, which may not be generally available or cost-effective.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of computational overhead and scalability of running MCTS per incoming example.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, principled decision-theoretic approach, strong empirical results, high impact, and reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical dependence on retainer crowds, simplifying worker-noise assumptions, and unquantified computational costs.
