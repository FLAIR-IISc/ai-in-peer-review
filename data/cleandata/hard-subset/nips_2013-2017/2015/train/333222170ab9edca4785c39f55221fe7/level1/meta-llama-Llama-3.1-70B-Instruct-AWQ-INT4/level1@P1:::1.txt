The paper "On-the-Job Learning with Bayesian Decision Theory" presents a novel approach to building high-accuracy systems with zero initial training examples. The authors propose an on-the-job learning framework that uses real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty and output predictions when confident. The framework is cast as a stochastic game based on Bayesian decision theory, which allows for the principled balancing of latency, cost, and accuracy objectives.

The authors relate their work to previous research in online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing. They highlight the key differences between their approach and existing methods, such as the ability to maintain high accuracy from the start and the use of a dynamic policy that improves over time.

The technical core of the paper is the modeling of the on-the-job setting as a stochastic game and the use of Monte Carlo Tree Search to approximate the optimal policy. The authors also present a detailed description of their environment model, which includes a prediction model, a response model, and a latency model.

The experimental evaluation of the proposed approach is thorough and well-executed. The authors test their method on three datasets (named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification) and compare it to several baselines, including human-only and machine-only approaches. The results show that the proposed method outperforms the baselines in terms of both quality and cost.
