- Novel framing: Treats on-the-job learning as a stochastic game and applies MCTS for decision-making.
- Core idea: Balances latency, cost, and accuracy by selectively querying a crowd, useful when pre-training data is scarce.
- Empirical gains: Experiments on NER, sentiment, and image classification show substantial cost reductions and performance improvements.
- Writing and structure: Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and effectively introduces the problem.
- Modeling: Problem formulation and model are detailed, mathematically sound, and grounded in Bayesian decision theory.
- Experimental setup: Comprehensive experiments with multiple baselines and clear crowdsourcing procedure.
- Reproducibility: Public implementation (LENSE) is provided, aiding reproducibility and follow-up work.
- MCTS clarity: Implementation (Algorithm 1) is dense and lacks sufficient detail for easy reproduction, especially progressive widening.
- Progressive widening ambiguity: Unclear what constitutes a “visited” state and how states are “grown”; needs a clearer explanation or example.
- Utility weights: Choice and justification of wM and wT are insufficient; parameter selection and sensitivity are not analyzed.
- Baseline comparisons: Comparisons feel uneven; missing stronger online active learning baselines like uncertainty sampling.
- Legion:AR critique: Paper downplays Legion:AR’s contributions inaccurately—Legion:AR includes related learning-based components.
- Task imbalance: Experiments lean heavily on NER; weaker gains on sentiment and image tasks are not well-explained.
- Task-specific analysis: Lacks investigation into why the method excels on NER (task structure, noise, CRF effectiveness).
- Related work: Discussion could be more critical and should better connect to active learning and reinforcement learning perspectives.
- Acceptance arguments: Novelty, significant empirical gains, technical soundness, and reproducibility support acceptance.
- Rejection concerns: Issues with implementation detail, parameter sensitivity, baseline strength, and limited task analysis argue against acceptance.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing MCTS clarity, sensitivity analysis, stronger baselines, and deeper task-specific discussion.
