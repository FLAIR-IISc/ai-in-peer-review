- Introduces "on-the-job learning": use real-time crowdsourcing to achieve high accuracy from the first prediction and improve the model as crowd feedback accumulates.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game and uses Bayesian decision theory to trade off accuracy, latency, and monetary cost.  
- Approximates the intractable optimal query policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and progressive widening.  
- Novel emphasis on maintaining high accuracy on every prediction from the outset, distinct from prior online active learning/active classification work.  
- Significance: addresses the cold-start deployment problem with a practical, practitioner-relevant framework.  
- Quality: technically sound formulation, sensible MCTS approximation, strong experiments on three tasks, and reproducible code/data.  
- Originality & clarity: novel integrated formulation and exceptionally clear writing.  
- Weakness — simplified crowd model: assumes fixed worker accuracy and does not model instance- or worker-specific reliability or online learning of worker profiles.  
- Weakness — scalability: per-instance MCTS planning may be computationally expensive; lacks runtime complexity and practical impact analysis.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: utility weights for cost/latency are manually set and sensitivity is not evaluated.  
- Overall: strong, impactful paper with minor weaknesses; strongly recommended for acceptance.
