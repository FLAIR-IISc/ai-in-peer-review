- Proposes LENSE, a novel on-the-job learning framework that learns from noisy crowds while reducing cost over time.  
- Models the on-the-job setting as a stochastic game and uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to approximate the optimal policy.  
- Evaluated on named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification, showing significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements over baselines.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and well-organized technical sections.  
- Experiments are thorough and well-designed, with impressive empirical results.  
- Strength: novel, broadly applicable approach to on-the-job learning.  
- Strength: technical exposition is clear and easy to follow.  
- Strength: strong experimental design and results.  
- Weakness: assumption that crowd workers are noisy/unreliable may not hold in all settings.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale applications.  
- Weakness: related work discussion could be expanded, especially in online and active learning.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution with impressive results.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Requested revisions: address assumptions about worker reliability and provide complexity analysis.
