- Proposes an on-the-job learning framework that deploys high-accuracy systems from zero examples by querying crowdsourcing in real time.  
- Formulates the problem as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to balance accuracy, cost, and latency.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening as a practical approximation (implementation: LENS).  
- Evaluated on named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification with comprehensive baselines.  
- Empirical results are strong—e.g., >10x cost reduction on NER versus 5-vote human labeling while improving accuracy.  
- Builds on active learning, active classification, and crowdsourcing literature but is novel in dynamic, test-time learning.  
- Technically sound: well-motivated formulation, appropriate utility function, and robust environment model.  
- Implementation and experimental methodology are thorough, enabling reproducibility (open-source LENS).  
- Clarity is generally good, though the state representation and the “frozen pool of worker responses” need clearer explanation.  
- Original contribution: first formal ML foundation for on-the-job learning in a setting similar to Legion:AR.  
- Significant practical impact for deployment in data-scarce settings and clear cost-accuracy tradeoff benefits.  
- Weakness: lacks detail on how the CRF is updated with crowd responses beyond “AdaGrad.”  
- Weakness: claim of workers being “70% accurate” needs empirical justification.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations, failure modes, and a more explicit comparison to Legion:AR.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address CRF update details, worker accuracy evidence, and clarifications.
