- Proposes on-the-job learning: systems without pre-training query crowd at test time, formulated as a stochastic game balancing latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Uses Bayesian decision theory and approximates optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and progressive widening.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing substantial cost reductions and performance gains.  
- Claims novelty in combining on-the-job learning, Bayesian decision theory, and MCTS for real-time querying.  
- Technically sound formulation; MCTS and progressive widening are appropriate for the intractable policy optimization.  
- Empirical results are convincing: notable improvements (e.g., +8% F1 over a single human labeler, +28% over online learning).  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, with a well-motivated introduction and readable model/algorithm descriptions.  
- Open-source code and datasets on CodaLab enhance reproducibility and facilitate follow-up work.  
- Framework complexity: multiple interacting components (Bayesian game, MCTS, response/latency models) are hard to follow.  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (UCT constant, cost/latency weights) is likely and under-documented.  
- Simplified crowd model (fixed 70% accuracy, frozen response pool) may not reflect real, evolving worker behavior and biases.  
- Baseline comparisons could include more sophisticated cost- and latency-aware active learning methods.  
- MCTS approximation may not converge to optimal policies in complex scenarios; limitations and improvement avenues are not fully discussed.  
- Overall: strong, novel, and practically relevant work with solid empirical support; recommended for acceptance despite complexity and modeling caveats.
