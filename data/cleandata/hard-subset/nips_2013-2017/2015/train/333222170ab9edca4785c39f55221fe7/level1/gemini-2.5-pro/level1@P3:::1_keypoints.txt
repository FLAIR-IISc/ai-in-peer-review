- Introduces and formalizes "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy ML with no initial training data by combining a model with real-time crowdsourcing.

- Core idea: query crowd workers on uncertain input parts per instance and update an online CRF as labels arrive to reduce crowd reliance.

- Differentiates from online and active learning; closest to active classification but learns a dynamic policy and updates at test time.

- Formal framework: models system-crowd interaction as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to balance accuracy, query cost, and latency.

- Approximates the intractable optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) adapted with progressive widening for continuous time.

- Strength — Significance/Originality: addresses the cold-start problem with a novel, practical human-in-the-loop formulation likely to inspire follow-up work.

- Strength — Technical quality: principled stochastic-game/Bayesian formulation, MCTS+progressive widening, and integration with an online-updatable CRF.

- Strength — Clarity/Organization: exceptionally well-written, clear motivation, helpful figures, and good positioning relative to related fields.

- Strength — Empirical evaluation: thorough experiments on NER, sentiment, and image classification with strong baselines, showing cost reductions and improved F1 (notably NER).

- Strength — Reproducibility: all code, data, and experiments provided on CodaLab.

- Weakness — Computational cost of planning: no analysis of MCTS planning latency or scalability per instance, which may be a real-time bottleneck.

- Weakness — Simplifying assumptions in crowd model: assumes uniform 70% worker accuracy and simplistic latency model, without testing robustness to heterogeneous workers or response times.

- Weakness — Relationship to POMDPs: needs a more detailed comparison to POMDP-based approaches (e.g., TurKontrol) to clarify advantages of the stochastic-game formulation.

- Pro-acceptance summary: novel and practical problem, principled solution, strong empirical results, clear presentation, and reproducible work.

- Con-acceptance summary: unanswered concerns about MCTS computational feasibility and simplistic crowd modeling.

- Conclusion: outstanding paper with strengths far outweighing minor weaknesses; recommended for acceptance.
