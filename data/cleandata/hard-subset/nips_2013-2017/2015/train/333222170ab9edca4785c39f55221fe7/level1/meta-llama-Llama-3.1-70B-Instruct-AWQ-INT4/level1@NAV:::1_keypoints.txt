- Models on-the-job learning as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy.
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment, and image classification, showing substantial cost reductions and accuracy gains over baselines.
- Technically sound with clear theoretical analysis, including game tree, utility function, and environment model.
- Experimental design is thorough with clear dataset, baseline, and result descriptions.
- Lacks a detailed analysis of framework limitations and potential biases.
- Well-written and accessible with sufficient background, though some mathematical details may be hard for non-experts.
- Novel combination of stochastic games, Bayesian decision theory, and crowdsourcing applied to on-the-job learning.
- Addresses an important problem—deploying accurate systems with limited training data—and suggests new research directions.
- Pro-accept: novel framework, strong empirical results across three tasks, addresses a significant problem, and solid theoretical support.
