- Introduces "on-the-job learning," a framework for deploying high-accuracy AI systems with zero initial training examples.  
- Models the problem as a stochastic game grounded in Bayesian decision theory.  
- System strategically queries crowdsourcing for uncertain predictions while simultaneously improving its model.  
- Balances latency, cost, and accuracy objectives using a Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) approximation.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment classification, and image classification tasks.  
- Achieves over an order of magnitude cost reduction versus full human annotation.  
- Delivers an 8% F1 improvement over single-human labeling and 28% over online learning.  
- Clearly distinguishes from prior work: extends active classification by enabling model improvement at test time.  
- Differs from online learning by maintaining high accuracy from the start rather than only asymptotically.  
- Acknowledges relation to Legion:AR but claims deeper ML foundations for the on-the-job setting.  
- Strengths: strong Bayesian theoretical grounding, comprehensive cross-domain experiments, and open-source implementation.  
- Paper is technically sound with well-justified trade-offs and excellent clarity and presentation.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of failure modes, especially handling systematic crowd errors beyond the assumed 70% accuracy.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of computational complexity and scalability of the MCTS approximation.  
- Weakness: inadequate explanation for why the method performs particularly well on NER compared to other tasks.  
- Significance: high practical impact for real-world deployment with scarce labeled data; recommendation to accept.
