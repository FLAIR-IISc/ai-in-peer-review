- Introduces "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy AI with zero initial training by querying crowd labels during test time.  
- Models the problem as a stochastic game between system and crowd, using Bayesian decision theory to balance accuracy, latency, and cost.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to approximate the intractable optimal policy for continuous-time decisions.  
- Clearly differentiates from online learning, active learning, and active classification, and builds on prior Bayesian decision-theory and structured prediction work.  
- Technically sound with a principled theoretical foundation and appropriate algorithmic implementation for the continuous-time setting.  
- Comprehensive experiments on NER, sentiment, and image classification with suitable baselines; strong empirical gains reported.  
- Achieves >10x cost reduction on NER and improved F1 (8% vs single-human, 28% vs online learning).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; mathematical formulation and algorithm description are precise and readable.  
- Minor clarity issue: game tree transitions (Figure 2b) could use a more intuitive explanation.  
- Original contribution: formalizing on-the-job learning as a stochastic game and enabling model improvement during test time.  
- Significant practical relevance for scenarios with scarce labeled data; open-source implementation (LENSE) aids adoption.  
- Strengths: novel framework, strong empirical results, solid Bayesian foundation, comprehensive evaluation, good discussion of limitations.  
- Weaknesses: formulation can be complex to grasp, limited treatment of concept drift, assumes constant worker accuracy, and scalability to very large datasets not fully explored.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with validated practical impact; recommended for acceptance.
