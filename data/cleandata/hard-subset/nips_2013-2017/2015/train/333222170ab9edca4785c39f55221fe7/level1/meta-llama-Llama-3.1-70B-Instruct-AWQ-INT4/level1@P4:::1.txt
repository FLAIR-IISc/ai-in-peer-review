This paper introduces a novel framework for on-the-job learning, which leverages Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy objectives in a principled way. The authors model the on-the-job setting as a stochastic game and develop an approximation based on Monte Carlo Tree Search to find the optimal policy. The paper presents an extensive evaluation of the proposed approach on three tasks, including named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification.

The main idea of the paper is to deploy a high-accuracy system starting with zero training examples, using real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty where needed and outputting predictions when confident. The authors argue that their approach is more effective than traditional supervised learning and crowdsourcing methods, as it can maintain high accuracy from the beginning and reduce the reliance on crowdsourcing queries over time.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their framework and its components. The evaluation section is comprehensive, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in reducing cost and improving accuracy.
