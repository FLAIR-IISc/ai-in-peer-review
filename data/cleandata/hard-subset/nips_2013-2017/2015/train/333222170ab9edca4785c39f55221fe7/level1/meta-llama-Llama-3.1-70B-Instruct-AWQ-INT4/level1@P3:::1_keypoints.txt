- Proposes an on-the-job learning framework using Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy when starting with no training data and learning from noisy crowds in real time.
- Models on-the-job learning as a stochastic game between the system and crowd, where the system chooses queries to optimize objectives.
- Presents an approximation algorithm using Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to handle continuous time.
- Evaluates on NER, sentiment, and image classification tasks, showing significant cost reductions and accuracy gains over baselines.
- Connects to online/active learning, active classification, crowdsourcing, and structured prediction, distinguishing itself from Legion:AR, Flock, and TurKontrol.
