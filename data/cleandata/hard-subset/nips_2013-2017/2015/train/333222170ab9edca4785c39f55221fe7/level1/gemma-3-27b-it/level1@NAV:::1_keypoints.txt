- Proposes an on-the-job learning framework that learns from interactions and crowd feedback without pre-trained models.  
- Frames learning as a stochastic game using Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Distinguishes itself from online/active learning and crowdsourcing by improving during test time and maintaining high initial accuracy.  
- Provides comprehensive related-work coverage situating the contribution across relevant fields.  
- Principled approach addressing a practical problem and balancing important operational objectives.  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) to approximate the intractable Bayesian decision policy.  
- Experiments show significant cost reduction on NER and improvements over single-label and standard online baselines.  
- Public code release enhances reproducibility and future research.  
- Limited experimental scope (primarily NER), raising concerns about generalization to other domains.  
- Utility function (Equation 1) and choice of coefficients (wM, wT) lack intuitive justification and need clarification.  
- Insufficient in-depth analysis of the impact of noisy crowd labels and worker quality variation.  
- Progressive widening in the MCTS algorithm is under-explained and feels like a black box.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strong and novel contribution despite the noted minor weaknesses.
