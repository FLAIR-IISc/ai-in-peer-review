**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes an on-the-job learning framework that uses Bayesian decision theory to balance latency, cost, and accuracy objectives in a principled way. The framework is designed for scenarios where there is no initial training data, and the system must learn from noisy crowds in real-time. The authors model on-the-job learning as a stochastic game between the system and the crowd, where the system chooses queries to maximize accuracy, minimize latency, and cost. The paper presents an approximation algorithm based on Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and progressive widening to reason about continuous time. The authors evaluate their approach on three tasks: named-entity recognition, sentiment classification, and image classification, and demonstrate significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements over baseline methods.
