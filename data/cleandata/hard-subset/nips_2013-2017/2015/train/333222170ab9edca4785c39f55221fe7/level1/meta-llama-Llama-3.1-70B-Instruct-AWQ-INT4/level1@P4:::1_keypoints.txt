- Novel on-the-job learning framework using Bayesian decision theory to trade off latency, cost, and accuracy.  
- Models the on-the-job setting as a stochastic game and approximates the optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS).  
- Core idea: start with zero training examples and use real-time crowdsourcing to resolve uncertainty, outputting predictions when confident.  
- Evaluated on three tasks (NER, sentiment classification, image classification) with extensive experiments.  
- Claims the approach maintains high accuracy from the start and reduces dependence on crowd queries over time.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the framework and components.  
- Comprehensive evaluation shows reduced cost and improved accuracy in experiments.  
- Strength: principled Bayesian formulation balancing multiple objectives.  
- Strength: MCTS-based approximation for finding policies is a significant methodological contribution.  
- Strength: thorough empirical results across multiple tasks.  
- Weakness: assumes crowd workers are reliable and accurate, which may not hold in real-world settings.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale applications.  
- Weakness: needs more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Significance: potential impact on NLP, computer vision, and human–computer interaction by improving cost/accuracy trade-offs.  
- Quality/clarity: well-organized presentation, clear results, and discussion of limitations.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel framework, notable MCTS approximation, and comprehensive empirical validation.  
- Argument for rejection: unrealistic crowd assumptions, missing complexity analysis, and insufficient SOTA comparisons.
