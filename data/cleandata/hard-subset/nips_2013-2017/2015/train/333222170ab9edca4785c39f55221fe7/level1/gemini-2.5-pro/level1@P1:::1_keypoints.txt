- Proposes "on-the-job learning": deploy high-accuracy predictors from zero labeled data by querying crowd at test time and learning online.  
- Formalizes the problem as a stochastic game where actions are query, wait, or predict, optimizing a utility that trades off accuracy, monetary query cost, and latency.  
- Uses a CRF as the structured prediction model that is updated online from crowd labels.  
- Approximates the intractable optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search (UCT) and progressive widening to handle continuous time.  
- Integrates ideas from active learning and decision-theoretic crowdsourcing while novelly coupling structured model learning with dynamic test-time querying.  
- Strong experimental evaluation across NER, sentiment, and image classification showing large cost reductions and accuracy gains versus sensible baselines.  
- Exceptional clarity and reproducibility: well-written paper with code/data/experiments provided on CodaLab.  
- Strength: significant, practical problem framing with a principled Bayesian decision-theoretic foundation.  
- Weakness: simplistic crowd model (fixed 70% accuracy, uniform errors) that ignores varying or evolving worker reliability.  
- Weakness: no analysis of computational overhead or wall-clock latency for the MCTS decision-making required for real-time use.  
- Weakness: missing discussion/ablation on sensitivity and selection of key utility hyperparameters (wM and wT).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel, and well-executed work whose limitations point to natural extensions rather than fatal flaws.
