## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces “highway networks,” a novel neural network architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward networks. The core idea, inspired by LSTM networks, is to incorporate “information highways” via adaptive gating mechanisms that allow for unimpeded information flow across many layers. The authors claim that this architecture enables the successful training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard stochastic gradient descent, overcoming the difficulties typically encountered with increasing depth.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the challenges of training deep networks and positioning highway networks as a solution. The authors effectively connect their work to prior research on optimization techniques, initialization strategies, and skip connections, as well as recurrent networks like LSTMs. The proposed architecture is presented clearly, with a concise mathematical formulation. The experimental results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 are compelling, demonstrating that highway networks can be trained to significantly greater depths than plain networks while maintaining or improving performance. The analysis of transform gate behavior and layer importance provides valuable insights into how these networks function.

A key strength is the demonstration that very deep networks can be trained *directly* with SGD, without resorting to more complex two-stage training procedures. This simplifies the training process and opens up possibilities for exploring even deeper architectures. The ablation studies and visualizations (Figures 2 & 4) are particularly insightful, revealing how information flows through the network and which layers are most crucial.

However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough comparison to other recent advancements in deep network training, such as residual networks (ResNets). While the authors mention skip connections, a direct comparison to ResNets in terms of performance and training stability would strengthen the contribution. Furthermore, the hyperparameter search process is not fully detailed, and a more systematic exploration of the transform gate bias initialization could be valuable. The discussion of limitations is brief; a more in-depth analysis of potential drawbacks, such as increased memory requirements due to the gating mechanisms, would be appreciated.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel architecture effectively addresses the vanishing gradient problem in deep networks.
*   Strong experimental results demonstrate successful training of very deep networks.
*   Insightful analysis of network behavior provides valuable understanding.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited comparison to recent state-of-the-art architectures like ResNets.
*   Hyperparameter search details are lacking.
*   Brief discussion of limitations.

**Overall:**

This is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is a promising approach for training very deep networks, and the experimental results are convincing. Despite some minor weaknesses, the paper's strengths outweigh its shortcomings. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above.
