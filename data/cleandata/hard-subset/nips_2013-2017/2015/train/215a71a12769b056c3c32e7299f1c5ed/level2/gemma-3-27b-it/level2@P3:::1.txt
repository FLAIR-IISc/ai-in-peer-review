This paper introduces highway networks, a novel neural network architecture designed to facilitate training of very deep networks. Inspired by LSTM networks, highway networks employ adaptive gating mechanisms to allow for unimpeded information flow across many layers. The core idea is to enable direct training via stochastic gradient descent even for networks with hundreds of layers, a significant challenge for traditional feedforward architectures.

The paper effectively positions the work within the context of recent advances in deep learning, highlighting the crucial role of depth and the difficulties encountered during training. The introduction provides a comprehensive overview of related work, including techniques like better optimizers, initialization strategies, skip connections, and knowledge distillation. The authors clearly articulate the limitations of these existing approaches and motivate the need for a new architecture like highway networks.

The strength of the work lies in its simplicity and effectiveness. The highway network architecture is elegantly presented and the mathematical formulation is clear and concise. The authors demonstrate, through extensive experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 datasets, that highway networks can be successfully trained to significant depths without suffering from the vanishing gradient problem that plagues plain networks.  The comparisons to FitNets and state-of-the-art methods are well-executed and convincingly demonstrate the benefits of the proposed approach. The analysis section, particularly the visualization of transform gate activity and layer importance, provides valuable insights into the inner workings of highway networks. The authors also thoughtfully discuss limitations and potential avenues for future research.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. While the initializations are discussed, a more detailed justification for the specific choices (e.g., the negative bias initialization) would be beneficial. A discussion of computational costs, especially compared to other deep learning architectures, is missing. While the paper compares to FitNets, a more thorough comparison in terms of parameter efficiency, given the additional parameters introduced by the gating mechanisms, would strengthen the results.  The analysis section presents interesting visualizations, but further quantitative analysis of these observations (e.g., quantifying the sparsity of transform gate activity) would be valuable.  The references are generally good, but some citations (e.g., [31], [32]) are mentioned almost in passing and could benefit from a slightly more detailed discussion of their relationship to the presented work.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel architecture that demonstrably improves training of very deep networks.
*   Clear and concise mathematical formulation.
*   Comprehensive experimental validation on multiple datasets.
*   Insightful analysis of network behavior.
*   Addresses a significant challenge in deep learning.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of computational costs.
*   Parameter efficiency could be explored in more detail.
*   Some aspects of the analysis could be strengthened with quantitative results.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway networks offer a practical and effective solution to the problem of training very deep networks. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and supported by compelling experimental results. While some minor improvements could be made, the strengths of the work far outweigh its weaknesses.  A clear accept recommendation is warranted.
