- Introduces "highway networks," a novel feedforward architecture using adaptive gating to enable very deep models.  
- Each layer outputs a gated combination of a transformed input and the input itself via a transform gate.  
- Key claim: highway networks can be trained directly with SGD even at hundreds of layers, unlike plain networks.  
- Strong empirical evidence on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 showing high accuracy and fast convergence as depth increases.  
- 100-layer highway network dramatically outperforms similarly-sized plain networks in training/optimization.  
- Section 4 analysis shows gates learn to dynamically route information based on input, not just static skip connections.  
- Experiments are well-designed with thorough ablation studies (e.g., layer importance analysis in Fig. 4).  
- Theoretical grounding via Jacobian analysis explains smooth transitions between identity and transformation behaviors.  
- Clear, well-organized writing that supports the main claims.  
- Original idea: applying LSTM-style gating to feedforward networks is novel and properly contextualized against prior work.  
- Missing citation/acknowledgement of contemporaneous ResNet work, though understandable given timing.  
- High significance: enables exploration of much deeper architectures with simple training and provides insights into information flow.  
- Matches or exceeds state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10 while using fewer parameters than some competitors.  
- Limited comparisons to other strong 2015 architectures (e.g., VGG, GoogLeNet), weakening the significance claim.  
- Computational overhead from additional gate parameters and training cost is not quantified.  
- Insufficient results on CIFAR-100, limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (bias init), and depth analysis restricted to a few datasets.
