- Introduces Highway Networks: gating-inspired architecture (from LSTM) enabling training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Adaptive "highway" gates allow information to flow across many layers without attenuation.  
- Demonstrates direct SGD training of depths previously intractable and good generalization on benchmarks.  
- Well-written paper with clear motivation, concise mathematical formulation, and articulated LSTM connection.  
- Experiments on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 are comprehensive and convincingly show benefits.  
- Ablation studies and gate-activity visualizations provide useful insights into network behavior.  
- Authors position work within related literature and provide reproducible code and hyperparameter results.  
- Novel contribution addressing vanishing gradients in very deep networks by adapting LSTM ideas to feedforward nets.  
- Technically sound architecture with strong empirical support.  
- Clear notation and explanations enhance readability and understanding.  
- Reproducibility is strong due to publicly released code and hyperparameter searches.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to transform-gate bias initialization with limited guidance for tuning across settings.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational cost and training-time comparisons to alternatives.  
- Weakness: analysis could deeper explain why certain layers are active/inactive (e.g., link gate activity to learned feature maps).  
- Minor clarity/typo issue in Section 3.3.1 that could be rephrased for readability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a core deep-learning problem, is novel, empirically validated, well-written, and reproducible.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs more on hyperparameter optimization, computational cost analysis, and deeper internal analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution with convincing experiments; recommended for acceptance.
