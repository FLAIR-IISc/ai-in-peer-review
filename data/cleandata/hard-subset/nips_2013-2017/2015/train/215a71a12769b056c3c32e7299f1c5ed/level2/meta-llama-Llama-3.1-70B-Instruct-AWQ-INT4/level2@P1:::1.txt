This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep neural networks. The authors draw inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks and propose the use of adaptive gating units to regulate information flow across layers. The paper presents a thorough analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of highway networks, including their ability to be trained directly using stochastic gradient descent (SGD) and their capacity for generalization.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their proposed architecture. The notation is well-defined, and the equations are easy to follow. The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, demonstrating the effectiveness of highway networks on several benchmark datasets.

One of the main strengths of the paper is its ability to tackle the problem of training very deep networks, which has been a long-standing challenge in the field. The authors provide a convincing argument that their proposed architecture can overcome this challenge, and the experimental results support this claim.

Another strength of the paper is its thorough analysis of the behavior of highway networks. The authors provide a detailed examination of the transform gate biases, the mean activity of the transform gates, and the block outputs, which helps to shed light on the inner workings of the network.

The paper also raises some interesting questions about the behavior of deep networks. For example, the authors note that the transform gate biases tend to decrease during training, which suggests that the network is learning to be more selective in its routing of information. This is an interesting observation that warrants further investigation.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it does not provide a comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art architectures. While the authors do compare their results to those of FitNets, they do not provide a detailed analysis of how their architecture compares to other popular architectures, such as ResNets or DenseNets.

Another potential weakness is that the paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the adaptive gating units are learned during training. While the authors provide a detailed explanation of the transform gate biases, they do not provide a similar explanation for the learning of the gating units.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a convincing argument for the effectiveness of their proposed architecture. The experimental results are thorough, and the analysis of the behavior of highway networks is detailed and insightful.
