- Introduces highway networks, a novel architecture enabling training of very deep neural networks.  
- Architecture is inspired by LSTM and uses adaptive gating units to regulate inter-layer information flow.  
- Paper shows highway networks can be trained directly with SGD and exhibit good generalization.  
- Thorough experimental results on multiple benchmark datasets demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Well-written with clear notation and easy-to-follow equations.  
- Detailed analysis of transform gate biases, mean gate activity, and block outputs illuminates network behavior.  
- Main strength: effectively tackles the long-standing problem of training very deep networks.  
- Interesting observation: transform gate biases tend to decrease during training, suggesting increased selectivity.  
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art architectures (e.g., ResNets, DenseNets).  
- Weakness: does not clearly explain how adaptive gating units are learned during training.  
- Quality score: 9/10 — technically sound with thorough experiments and insightful analysis.  
- Clarity score: 9/10 — well-written, well-presented experimental results.  
- Originality score: 8/10 — novel application of adaptive gating inspired by LSTM, though gating idea itself is not new.  
- Significance score: 9/10 — addresses a major challenge in deep learning with convincing evidence.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, strong experiments, convincing demonstration of effectiveness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient SOTA comparisons and unclear learning mechanism for gating units.
