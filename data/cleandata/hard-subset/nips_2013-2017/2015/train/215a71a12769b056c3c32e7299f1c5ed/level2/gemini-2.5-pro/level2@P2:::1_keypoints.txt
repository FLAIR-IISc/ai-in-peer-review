- Proposes "Highway Networks": feedforward layers with adaptive transform and carry gates (LSTM-inspired) that create information highways enabling stable training of very deep nets (up to 100–1000 layers).

- Significance: simple, general, and effective solution to training extremely deep networks; applicable to fully-connected and convolutional layers and likely highly influential.

- Originality: novel adaptation of LSTM-style gating to feedforward nets; learned, data-dependent gates offer more flexibility than prior fixed skip connections.

- Novel initialization: negative bias for transform gates to encourage carry behavior at start of training is a clever, practical contribution.

- Technical quality: strong experimental evidence—MNIST optimization shows plain nets fail with depth while highway nets remain stable up to 100 layers.

- Technical quality: comparison with FitNets on CIFAR-10 demonstrates single-stage SGD training of deep, thin networks without complex hint-based pretraining.

- Technical quality: competitive CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 results indicate good generalization, not just trainability.

- Clarity and analysis: paper is well-written; gate-activity visualizations and lesioning study provide insights, including adaptive use of depth for harder tasks.

- Weakness: lacks ablation vs fixed identity skip connections to isolate the advantage of adaptive gating.

- Weakness: should analyze sensitivity to gate-bias initialization (e.g., zero or positive biases) to show robustness.

- Weakness: dimensionality changes are handled with plain layers; integrating projection into highway blocks could be discussed as an improvement.

- Overall: excellent, well-supported contribution with minor addressable weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance and oral presentation.
