- Introduces highway networks, a novel architecture using adaptive gating (inspired by LSTM) to enable training of very deep networks via SGD.  
- Enables unimpeded information flow across many layers, addressing vanishing gradients common in plain feedforward nets.  
- Positions the work well within deep learning literature, reviewing optimizers, initialization, skip connections, and knowledge distillation.  
- Clearly motivates the need for a new architecture by outlining limitations of existing approaches.  
- Architecture is simple and effective, with a clear and concise mathematical formulation.  
- Extensive experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 show successful training at significant depths.  
- Comparisons to FitNets and state-of-the-art methods are convincing and demonstrate benefits of highway networks.  
- Analysis includes insightful visualizations of transform gate activity and layer importance, shedding light on network behavior.  
- Authors discuss limitations and propose avenues for future research.  
- Initialization choices (e.g., negative bias) lack detailed justification and could be better explained.  
- Missing discussion of computational costs and runtime compared to other architectures.  
- Parameter efficiency concerns because gating introduces extra parameters; more comparison needed.  
- Analysis would benefit from additional quantitative measures (e.g., sparsity metrics for gate activity).  
- Some references are cited only briefly and need a clearer discussion of their relevance.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound paper with compelling results and a clear accept recommendation.
