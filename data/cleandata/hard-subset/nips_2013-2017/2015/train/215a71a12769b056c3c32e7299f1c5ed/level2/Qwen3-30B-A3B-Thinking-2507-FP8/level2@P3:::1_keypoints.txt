- Introduces highway networks that enable direct training of extremely deep feedforward nets via adaptive gating inspired by LSTMs; core Eq. (3) combines transformed input (H) and carry (T) to control information flow.  
- Novelty: learns dynamic routing mechanisms rather than relying on fixed skip connections or teacher-student distillation.  
- Empirical strength: MNIST results show highway nets maintain performance across 10–100 layers while plain nets degrade drastically at 100 layers.  
- CIFAR-10 results: highway nets match or exceed Fitnet performance in single-stage training, avoiding two-stage procedures required by plain nets.  
- Analysis insight: transform gates become selective during training (Fig. 2), not static pass-throughs.  
- Analysis insight: layer importance varies by dataset (Fig. 4), with more complex tasks (e.g., CIFAR-100) requiring more layers.  
- Experiments are rigorous: include hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and consistent results across five runs.  
- Design avoids reliance on problem-specific initialization schemes used by prior work.  
- Clarity is high: equations, figures, and notation are well explained; formulation is logically derived from LSTM concepts.  
- Significance: substantially addresses the core challenge of training very deep networks and advances exploration of deeper architectures.  
- Weakness: inadequate contextualization of related work, especially unclear relationship to a similar LSTM-inspired model [32], weakening the novelty claim.  
- Weakness: underdeveloped discussion of how highway networks relate to or influenced later skip-connection methods (e.g., ResNets).  
- Weakness: computational overhead and parameter efficiency from gating are not quantified; lacks direct parameter-count vs accuracy comparisons.  
- Limitation: absence of ImageNet-scale experiments limits demonstration of scalability to larger tasks.  
- Trade-offs: claims about "extremely deep" nets are supported by MNIST, but trade-offs between depth and parameter efficiency are not fully explored.  
- Opportunity: layer importance analysis suggests unused layers could be pruned to improve efficiency, but pruning is not investigated.  
- Overall judgment: high-quality, original contribution with robust evidence and practical impact; paper meets NIPS criteria and should be accepted.
