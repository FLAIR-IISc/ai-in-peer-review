This paper introduces Highway Networks, a novel neural network architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep networks. The core idea is inspired by LSTM recurrent networks, incorporating "highway" connections – adaptive gating units – that allow information to flow across many layers without attenuation. The authors demonstrate that these networks can be trained directly with SGD to depths previously intractable for standard feedforward networks, and generalize well.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation, the proposed architecture, and the experimental results. The connection to LSTM networks is well-articulated, and the mathematical formulation of the highway layer is concise and easy to follow. The experiments on MNIST and CIFAR datasets are comprehensive and convincingly demonstrate the benefits of the proposed architecture. The ablation studies (layer importance analysis) and the visualization of gate activity provide valuable insights into the internal workings of the network. The authors also clearly acknowledge related work and position their contribution within the existing literature.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The highway network architecture represents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning, offering a novel solution to the vanishing gradient problem in very deep networks. While inspired by LSTM, the application to feedforward networks, and the resulting ability to train *extremely* deep networks with standard SGD, is a significant advance.
* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed architecture is mathematically well-defined and intuitively motivated. The experiments presented provide strong empirical evidence supporting the claims made by the authors.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand. The notation is clear, and the explanations are concise and precise.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The authors conduct thorough experiments on multiple datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100) and compare their approach to existing methods, including FitNets. The analysis of layer importance and gate activity further strengthens the empirical validation.
* **Reproducibility:** The authors have made their code and hyperparameter search results publicly available, which greatly enhances the reproducibility of their work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions the importance of the transform gate bias initialization, but doesn't provide extensive guidance on how to tune this parameter for different architectures or datasets.  A more detailed discussion of hyperparameter optimization strategies would be beneficial.
* **Limited Discussion of Computational Cost:** While the paper demonstrates that highway networks can be trained, it doesn’t provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost compared to other deep learning architectures. This information would be valuable for practitioners considering using this approach. The paper notes similar parameter counts to Fitnets, but doesn’t quantify training time differences.
* **Analysis Depth:** While the visualization of gate activity and layer importance is insightful, a more in-depth analysis of *why* certain layers become important or inactive could provide further understanding of the network's behavior. For instance, relating gate activity to feature maps learned in each layer.
* **Minor Typo/Clarity Issue:** In Section 3.3.1, the sentence "However, Romero et. al. [25] recently reported that training on CIFAR-10 through plain backpropogation was only possible for maxout networks with a depth up to 5 layers when the number of parameters was limited to∼250K and the number of multiplications to ∼30M."  seems awkwardly worded.  Perhaps rephrasing it to better convey the meaning would improve clarity.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning: training very deep networks.
* The proposed highway network architecture is novel, technically sound, and empirically validated.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate significant improvements over existing approaches.
* The paper is well-written, clear, and provides valuable insights into the behavior of deep networks.
* The code availability promotes reproducibility and further research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of hyperparameter optimization and computational cost.
* While the analysis is insightful, a deeper understanding of the network's internal workings could be achieved. 

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is a promising approach to training very deep networks, and the experimental results are convincing. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract from the overall quality of the work. I strongly recommend accepting this paper to NIPS 2015.
