- Introduces "highway networks": a feedforward architecture with adaptive transform and carry gates to enable very deep models.  
- Gates are inspired by LSTM mechanisms and create "information highways" that carry inputs across many layers.  
- Directly addresses the degradation problem where plain networks’ training error increases with more layers.  
- Empirical validation: highway networks train reliably at depths of 50–100 layers while plain networks fail.  
- Competitive results on CIFAR-10/CIFAR-100, matching or outperforming state-of-the-art (e.g., FitNets) without complex multi-stage training.  
- High originality: first to introduce trainable, data-dependent gating for feedforward networks.  
- Section 4 analysis visualizes learned gate biases/activities, showing sparse, data-dependent routing.  
- Lesion study demonstrates the network utilizes added depth for complex tasks rather than ignoring layers.  
- Minor weakness: lacks formal theoretical analysis of gradient flow despite strong empirical evidence.  
- Additional minor note: CIFAR performance might improve with more hyperparameter tuning.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a critical problem, elegant biologically/theoretically motivated design, extensive convincing experiments, and high potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: none of significance; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
