- Introduces Highway Networks with adaptive gating (inspired by LSTM) to enable training of very deep networks via SGD.
- Demonstrates theoretical motivation and empirical success on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100, showing improved optimization and generalization versus plain networks.
- Novelty: adaptive gating to enable extremely deep networks is a significant and well-motivated contribution.
- Experimental rigor: extensive experiments and comparisons support the claims and show scalability with depth.
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with clear explanations, figures, and available source code.
- Practical usefulness: addresses a key bottleneck in deep learning, making deep architectures more trainable and accessible.
- Limited benchmarking: lacks comparisons with more recent depth-focused architectures (e.g., ResNets).
- Analysis depth: needs deeper investigation into why some layers remain unused and the effects of hyperparameter choices (e.g., gate bias initialization).
- Scalability concerns: unclear performance and computational efficiency on larger, more complex datasets like ImageNet.
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a fundamental problem, presents robust results, and has strong practical impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete comparisons to newer methods and unproven scalability to large-scale tasks.
- Recommendation: accept—significant contribution with some areas for further exploration to strengthen impact.
