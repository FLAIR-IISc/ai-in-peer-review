- Proposes highway networks: layers with input-dependent transform gate T and carry gate C=1−T, y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)), inspired by LSTM gating.
- Uses negative bias initialization for T to favor carry initially, enabling training of very deep networks with vanilla SGD.
- Shows optimization studies on thin fully connected nets up to 100 layers on MNIST where highway nets avoid degradation seen in plain nets.
- Demonstrates competitive results on MNIST with 10-layer convolutional highway networks.
- Trains thin, relatively deep highway nets on CIFAR-10/100 in a single stage without hints, matching or exceeding FitNets under similar budgets.
- Presents analyses of gate behavior and layer lesioning, showing input-dependent routing and varied layer importance with depth.
- Clearly motivated architecture linking RNN/LSTM gating to feedforward networks, filling a conceptual gap.
- Simple, practical recipe (bias T negative) that is architecture-agnostic and easy to apply.
- Code release and reuse of hyperparameters across datasets improve transparency and reproducibility.
- Missing ablation comparing input-dependent gating to fixed identity skips (residual connections) to isolate gating's contribution.
- The constraint C = 1 − T is not evaluated; an ablation with independent carry and transform gates is suggested.
- Claim of training “hundreds/thousands of layers” is not fully substantiated—convolutional experiments mostly ≤32 layers.
- Fairness of comparisons could be improved by including modern techniques (e.g., batch normalization) and stronger baselines.
- CIFAR architectural/training details are under-specified in the main text, hurting reproducibility without supplemental code.
- Computational overhead of gating (extra parameters and multiplies) is not quantified; parameter counts and FLOPs should be reported.
- Methodology and optimization study are sound and well-controlled; CIFAR experiments show practical trainability though not state-of-the-art.
- Paper is clearly written and organized, with helpful figures and intuition; main-text hyperparameters could be expanded.
- The idea of input-dependent gated skips is novel and distinct from prior fixed-skip or deep-supervision approaches.
- If validated more broadly, highway nets offer a simple mechanism to train very deep models and study depth/width trade-offs.
- Main shortcomings: lack of critical ablations, limited deep convolutional results, and omission of some strong contemporary baselines.
- Overall recommendation: lean accept — promising, well-motivated work that would be stronger with additional ablations and baseline comparisons.
