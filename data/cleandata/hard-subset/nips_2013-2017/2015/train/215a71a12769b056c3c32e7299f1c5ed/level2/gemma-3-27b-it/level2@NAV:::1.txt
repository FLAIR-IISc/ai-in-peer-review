## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to incorporate “information highways” inspired by LSTM gating mechanisms, enabling unimpeded information flow across many layers via adaptive gating units. This addresses the well-known vanishing gradient problem that hinders the training of deep networks. The authors demonstrate that these highway networks can be effectively trained with standard stochastic gradient descent, even with hundreds of layers, and generalize well to unseen data.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the challenges of training deep networks and the limitations of existing approaches like better optimizers and initialization strategies. The proposed architecture is elegantly presented, building upon the established LSTM framework, and the mathematical formulation (Eq. 3) is concise and understandable. The experimental results are convincing, showing that highway networks outperform plain networks significantly as depth increases on MNIST, and achieve competitive or superior performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 compared to FitNets and other state-of-the-art methods. The analysis of gate activity and layer importance (Figures 2 & 4) provides valuable insight into the network’s internal workings. 

A strength lies in the thorough comparison to existing techniques, demonstrating not just improved training but also comparable or better performance. The authors also acknowledge limitations, particularly that the optimal transform gate bias is a hyperparameter requiring tuning. However, the paper would benefit from a more detailed ablation study investigating the impact of different gate functions or highway configurations. While they mention a similar model [32] was proposed concurrently, a more comprehensive discussion comparing the two would be valuable. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks.
*   Strong empirical results demonstrating improved performance compared to existing methods.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.
*   Insightful analysis of network behavior.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited ablation study to understand the impact of architectural choices.
*   Brief discussion of related concurrent work [32].

**Overall:** This is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field. The proposed Highway Networks offer a promising solution to the challenge of training very deep networks, opening up new possibilities for exploring deeper and more efficient architectures. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points mentioned above.
