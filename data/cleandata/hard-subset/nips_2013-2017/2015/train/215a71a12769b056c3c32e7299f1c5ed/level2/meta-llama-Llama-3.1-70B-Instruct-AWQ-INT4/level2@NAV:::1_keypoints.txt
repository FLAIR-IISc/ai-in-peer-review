- Proposes Highway Networks that use adaptive LSTM-inspired gates to allow unimpeded information flow across very deep feedforward nets.  
- Shows Highway Networks can be trained directly with SGD on networks with hundreds of layers and achieve competitive benchmark performance.  
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical analysis and experimental results that support the claims.  
- Architecture and advantages over traditional feedforward networks are clearly explained with consistent notation and straightforward derivations.  
- Novel application of adaptive gating from LSTM to feedforward networks — an innovative and significant contribution.  
- Demonstrates important insights into network behavior, including information routing and the role of individual layers.  
- Pros: novel architecture, rigorous experiments, and valuable behavioral analysis.  
- Con: could include additional experiments on more challenging datasets or tasks.  
- Con: lacks deeper analysis of computational cost and memory requirements.  
- Con: assumes reader familiarity with deep learning, reducing accessibility for non-experts.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication.
