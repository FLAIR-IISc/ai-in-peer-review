- Introduces highway networks with adaptive transform and carry gates (LSTM-inspired) to enable information highways across very deep layers.  
- Key innovation (Eq. 3): output is a gated weighted mix of transformed input and the input itself.  
- Can be trained directly with SGD without special initialization or two-stage training, achieving strong results on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100.  
- Gating mechanism is novel and distinct from fixed skip connections or knowledge distillation, offering continuous learnable shortcuts.  
- Theoretical analysis (Eqs. 4–5) shows how layers interpolate between identity and transformation to aid gradient flow.  
- Empirical rigor: comprehensive comparisons to plain networks and FitNets; 100-layer highways vastly outperform plain nets in optimization.  
- Ablation studies and visualizations provide insight into information routing and layer importance.  
- Clear, reproducible description with implementation details, negative-bias init, and shared code/hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: Insufficient contrast with contemporaneous ResNets, which weakens the novelty claim.  
- Weakness: Claims of "hundreds of layers" are not fully supported—experiments max out at 100 layers and need clearer contextualization.  
- Weakness: Gating dynamics (e.g., increasing transform gate biases) are observed but lack deeper interpretation and implications for generalization.  
- Pro: Strong novelty, solid empirical validation, clear theoretical grounding, and practical impact enabling deeper networks without complex training.  
- Con: Minor contextual and analytical gaps (ResNet comparison and deeper gating analysis).  
- Overall recommendation: Strong contribution—recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Minor revision suggestions: add explicit ResNet comparison, report parameter counts for Highway models in Table 2, and clarify the bias gradient in Figure 2 caption.
