This paper proposes a novel architecture for training very deep neural networks, called Highway Networks, which allows unimpeded information flow across many layers. The authors draw inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks and use adaptive gating units to regulate the information flow. The paper demonstrates that Highway Networks can be trained directly using stochastic gradient descent (SGD), even with hundreds of layers, and achieve competitive performance on several benchmark datasets.

The authors provide a clear explanation of the Highway Network architecture and its advantages over traditional feedforward networks. The experiments are rigorous and well-designed, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.
