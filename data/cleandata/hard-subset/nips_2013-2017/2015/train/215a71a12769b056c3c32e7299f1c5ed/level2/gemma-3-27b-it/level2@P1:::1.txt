This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward neural networks. The key idea is to incorporate adaptive gating mechanisms, inspired by LSTMs, which allow for direct information flow across many layers, alleviating the vanishing gradient problem and enabling the training of networks with hundreds of layers. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks through experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, showing that they can be trained effectively with standard SGD and achieve competitive or superior performance compared to existing methods, particularly in scenarios where depth is a limiting factor.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture is a creative and well-motivated approach to address the challenges of training deep networks. The LSTM-inspired gating mechanism is a clever adaptation of a successful recurrent network concept to the feedforward setting.
*   **Empirical Results:** The experimental results are compelling. The authors demonstrate that highway networks can indeed be trained to significantly greater depths than plain networks, and achieve good performance on standard datasets. The comparison to FitNets is particularly valuable.
*   **Clarity of Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and explains the highway network architecture clearly. The mathematical notation is concise and easy to follow. The figures (especially Figure 1) effectively illustrate the benefits of the proposed approach.
*   **Thorough Analysis:** The ablation study and analysis of layer importance (Figure 4) provide valuable insights into the behavior of highway networks and demonstrate that the increased depth is genuinely utilized for complex problems. The discussion of data-dependent routing (Figure 3) is also insightful.
*   **Availability of Code:** The authors have made their code publicly available, which is a significant contribution to reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Exploration of Gating Mechanisms:** The paper focuses primarily on a specific implementation of the gating mechanism (T(x) = σ(WTTx + bT) and C = 1-T). While effective, the authors don't explore alternative gating functions or configurations. A brief discussion of potential variations and their impact would be beneficial.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the authors mention a random search for hyperparameters, the details of the search space and the sensitivity of the results to hyperparameter choices could be elaborated upon. It's unclear how robust the performance is to changes in these settings.
*   **Comparison to More Recent Methods:** The paper references work up to 2013-2015. Since then, there have been significant advancements in network architectures (e.g., ResNets, DenseNets) and optimization techniques (e.g., Adam, batch normalization). A comparison to these more recent methods, even if brief, would help contextualize the contribution of highway networks.  ResNets, in particular, offer a related approach to address vanishing gradients.
*   **Scaling to Very Large Datasets:** The experiments are primarily conducted on relatively small datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10/100). It would be interesting to see how highway networks perform on larger, more complex datasets like ImageNet.
*   **Explanation of Negative Bias:** While the authors explain the *use* of a negative bias for the transform gates, the *reason* why it is effective beyond simply enabling initial carry behavior is not fully elaborated. A deeper theoretical understanding of this initialization strategy would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and well-motivated architecture for training very deep networks.
*   The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks.
*   The analysis provides valuable insights into the behavior of the proposed architecture.
*   The code availability promotes reproducibility and further research.
*   The paper addresses a significant challenge in deep learning: training very deep networks.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The exploration of gating mechanisms is limited.
*   The comparison to more recent state-of-the-art methods is lacking.
*   The scalability to very large datasets remains an open question.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is a clever and effective approach to address the challenges of training very deep networks. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NIPS.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically addressing the points raised in the weaknesses section (particularly a discussion of recent advancements like ResNets and scaling to larger datasets).
