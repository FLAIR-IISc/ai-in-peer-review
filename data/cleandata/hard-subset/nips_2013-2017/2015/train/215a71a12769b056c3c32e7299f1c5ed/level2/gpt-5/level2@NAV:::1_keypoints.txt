- Proposes highway networks with LSTM-like transform gates enabling data-dependent routing and improved gradient flow in very deep feedforward nets.
- Main claims: SGD can train networks with hundreds–>1000 layers using adaptive gating and simple initialization; competitive generalization on MNIST/CIFAR with analyzable depth usage.
- Builds on skip connections and deep supervision but differs by learning per-layer, per-unit gates rather than fixed shortcuts or auxiliary losses.
- MNIST optimization study convincingly shows plain nets degrade with depth while highway nets do not and converge faster.
- CIFAR-10/100 results are competitive with FitNets at similar parameter/compute budgets without multi-stage training.
- Analysis of gate statistics and layer lesioning provides insightful information about routing and layer importance.
- Strongest “hundreds/1000 layers” claim is supported mainly by MNIST curves and anecdotal pilots; lacks large-scale/challenging benchmarks.
- Compute and memory overhead of gates and fair wall-clock runtime comparisons are not quantified.
- Some references in the paper are incomplete.
- Paper is generally clear with precise equations and intuitive interpretation; training details are described and code is promised.
- Reproducibility would benefit from compact pseudocode and a clearer architectural diagram.
- The gating formulation y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)) is a novel, learnable alternative to fixed skips and teacher hints.
- If validated broadly, the mechanism could significantly stabilize and accelerate training of very deep nets and inspire conditional computation work.
- Arguments for acceptance: simple principled mechanism with strong optimization evidence, competitive CIFAR performance without distillation, and insightful analyses with code availability.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical scope (no large-scale tasks, limited baselines), efficiency/overhead not rigorously evaluated, and missing ablations.
- Constructive suggestions: add comparisons to fixed identity skips and deep supervision under matched budgets.
- Constructive suggestions: report wall-clock time, memory, parameter overhead, and gradient-norm diagnostics across depth.
- Constructive suggestions: ablate C=1−T vs independent carry gate and study sensitivity to gate-bias initialization.
- Constructive suggestions: evaluate on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet or SVHN) and provide more runs with confidence intervals.
