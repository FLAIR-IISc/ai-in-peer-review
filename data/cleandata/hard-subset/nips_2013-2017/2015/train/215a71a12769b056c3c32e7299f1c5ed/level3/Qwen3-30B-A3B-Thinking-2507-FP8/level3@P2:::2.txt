

This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to enable direct training of extremely deep feedforward networks (up to 100+ layers) through LSTM-inspired adaptive gating mechanisms. The core innovation lies in Equation 3, where a transform gate *T* and carry gate *C* (*C* = 1 − *T*) regulate information flow: *y* = *H*(*x*, *W*<sub>H</sub>)·*T* + *x*·(1 − *T*). This allows information to propagate through "highways" with minimal attenuation, contrasting sharply with plain networks whose gradients vanish with depth. The work builds on and significantly extends prior NIPS contributions: it addresses limitations of skip connections (e.g., [22, 23]) by making gating input-dependent, differs from FitNets’ two-stage training [25], and adapts LSTM concepts [29, 30] for feedforward networks rather than recurrent ones. Crucially, it demonstrates that highway networks achieve comparable or superior accuracy to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., FitNets, maxout networks) while training directly via SGD—a key advancement over prior depth-limited approaches.

The paper excels in **quality** and **clarity**. The theoretical foundation (Section 2) is sound, with clear derivation of the layer Jacobian (Equation 5) and intuitive interpretation of gating behavior. Experiments (Section 3) rigorously validate the core claim: on MNIST, 100-layer highway networks outperform similarly sized plain networks by >100× in loss (Figure 1). The CIFAR-10/100 results (Table 2–3) show highway networks matching or exceeding FitNets and maxout networks while using fewer parameters and avoiding two-stage training. The analysis (Section 4) is particularly compelling: Figure 2–4 reveal how gates dynamically route information (e.g., sparse activity per input in Figure 2, class-dependent routing in Figure 3, and layer importance via lesioning in Figure 4). This provides unprecedented insight into deep network behavior, directly addressing the "black box" criticism of depth. The writing is exceptionally clear, with well-structured figures and concise explanations—e.g., the highway layer’s flexibility (Equation 4) is immediately graspable.

**Originality** is strong. While acknowledging LSTM [29, 30] and FitNets [25] as precursors, the paper explicitly differentiates its contribution: (1) highway networks train *directly* (no teacher networks), (2) gating is input-dependent (unlike fixed skip connections), and (3) the architecture is agnostic to *H* (e.g., convolutional, recurrent). The comparison to prior NIPS work (Section 1) is thorough, citing relevant papers like [10, 11, 12, 18, 20, 25, 32] and clarifying distinctions. No prior NIPS paper has enabled training of networks with 100+ layers *without* specialized initialization or multi-stage procedures.

**Significance** is high. By solving the fundamental depth barrier, highway networks open the door to studying architectures where depth is a true hyperparameter (e.g., "how many layers are *actually* needed?," as shown in Figure 4). This advances the field beyond empirical "deeper is better" trends toward principled depth exploration. The results on CIFAR-10/100 (Table 3) demonstrate practical utility: highway networks achieve competitive accuracy with minimal computational overhead, and the architecture is broadly applicable (e.g., convolutional variants in Section 2.1). Crucially, the paper shows that depth *enhances* performance without sacrificing generalization (Table 1, 2, 3), directly countering the common belief that very deep networks overfit.

Minor weaknesses are limited to a typo ("Tables 3 and 3" on p.5) and the paper’s focus on simplified highway blocks (Section 2.2). However, the core contribution remains robust, and the pilot experiments on complex blocks (e.g., LSTM-unrolled variants) suggest room for future extensions without undermining the main results.

**Pro/Con Summary for Acceptance**:  
*Pro*: Solves a core deep learning challenge (training depth) with a simple, generalizable architecture; rigorous experiments and novel analysis; clear, high-impact contribution to NIPS’s mission of advancing deep learning.  
*Con*: None substantive; the minor typo is easily corrected.  

This is a high-quality, original paper that significantly advances the state of the art in training deep networks. It is clearly written, thoroughly evaluated, and addresses a fundamental problem with clear implications for future research. The work is a worthy addition to NIPS, providing both theoretical insight and practical utility. I strongly recommend acceptance.
