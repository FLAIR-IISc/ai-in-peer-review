- Introduces highway networks: a feedforward architecture with learnable gates inspired by LSTM to ease training of very deep networks.
- Gates regulate information flow, allowing gradients to propagate through many layers and overcoming optimization challenges of plain deep nets.
- Contrasts this approach with previous methods like careful initialization or fixed skip connections.
- Primary strengths: originality and simplicity—first successful adaptation of LSTM-like gating to deep feedforward networks.
- Implementation is straightforward and results are compelling, showing no performance degradation with increased depth.
- Analyses (gate activations and layer lesioning) are thorough and provide valuable behavioral insights.
- Suggests alternative framing: view highway layers as more powerful gated computational blocks (similar to VGG/Inception) rather than merely skip mechanisms.
- Proposes an experiment: train a single thin-and-tall highway network jointly on MNIST and CIFAR with a shared trunk to study task-specific gating reuse.
- Recommends validating the approach on a larger benchmark like ImageNet to demonstrate broader potential.
- Overall judgment: technically sound, clearly presented, no major weaknesses, and strongly recommended for acceptance.
