- Proposes highway networks with adaptive gating (inspired by LSTM) to enable direct information flow and mitigate vanishing gradients in very deep feedforward nets.  
- Core idea is well-explained and builds logically on prior work (initialization techniques, skip connections, FitNets).  
- Experiments convincingly show highway networks can be trained with hundreds of layers using standard SGD.  
- Analysis of learned gate output patterns is strong and provides insights into information routing (Figures 2 & 3).  
- The claim that sigmoid gates “never” output exactly 0/1 and therefore equation 5’s gradients are incorrect is misleading; gradients remain defined and the real issue is saturation.  
- Authors must explicitly detail their backpropagation implementation to clarify gradient handling.  
- The initial negative bias initialization, while effective, appears arbitrary and needs justification (e.g., plot of performance vs. initial bias).  
- Comparative claims to FitNets require clarification on whether experimental settings are identical or differ, with differences explicitly stated.  
- Reporting only maximum accuracies is insufficient; mean and standard deviation should be provided for statistical significance and reproducibility.  
- A theoretical/learning-theory analysis of the gate units (why they aid gradient propagation and act as conditional identities) would substantially strengthen the paper.  
- For: important problem, novel and effective solution, convincing experiments, insightful analysis.  
- Against: unclear backprop details, arbitrary bias choice without analysis, and insufficient result reporting are significant weaknesses.
