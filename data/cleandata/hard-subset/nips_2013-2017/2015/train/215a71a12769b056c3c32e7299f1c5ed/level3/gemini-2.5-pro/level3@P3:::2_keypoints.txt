- Introduces highway networks with LSTM-inspired gating that adaptively controls information flow across layers.  
- Each layer can choose to transform input or carry it unchanged via "information highways," enabling unimpeded gradient flow.  
- Enables direct training of extremely deep feed-forward networks (hundreds of layers) using standard SGD.  
- Positions work relative to prior methods (initialization, optimizers, skip connections) and explicitly parallels LSTM gating in depth dimension.  
- Favorable comparison to FitNets: single-stage training of deep, thin models versus FitNets’ two-stage teacher-student approach.  
- Original and significant idea that allows exploration of architectures much deeper than previous standard feed-forward nets.  
- Strong empirical validation: optimization experiments on MNIST and competitive/outperforming results on CIFAR-10/CIFAR-100.  
- Paper is very clear, well-written, and well-organized, with precise explanation of architecture and experiments.  
- In-depth analysis including gate visualizations, data-dependent routing, and layer lesioning reveals internal dynamics and adaptive use of depth.  
- No significant methodological weaknesses identified; experiments are thorough and conclusions well-supported.  
- Minor comment: typographical error on page 5 referring to "Tables 3 and 3."  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, impactful contribution with strong recommendation for acceptance.
