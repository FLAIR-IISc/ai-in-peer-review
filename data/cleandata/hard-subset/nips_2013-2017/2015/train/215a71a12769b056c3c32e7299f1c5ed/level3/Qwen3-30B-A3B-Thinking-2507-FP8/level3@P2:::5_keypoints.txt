- Introduces highway networks using LSTM-inspired transform and carry gates to enable information flow across many layers.  
- Enables direct SGD training of extremely deep feedforward networks (hundreds of layers), addressing optimization difficulties.  
- Extends skip/residual connection ideas by using adaptive gating instead of fixed skips.  
- Strong empirical results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 showing maintained or improved accuracy with increased depth.  
- Provides analysis of gate behavior and layer importance (Figures 2 and 4) offering insight into network operation.  
- Originality: novel application of adaptive LSTM-like gating to depth-related training issues.  
- Quality: technically sound with thorough experiments and reproducible details.  
- Significance: allows researchers to study very deep architectures without complex training schemes.  
- Clarity: well-written exposition and clear motivation linking to LSTM concepts.  
- Weakness: lacks evaluation on large-scale datasets like ImageNet to demonstrate broader applicability.  
- Weakness: very deep highway networks can still be effectively "shallow" in practice, suggesting further analysis needed.  
- Weakness: relationship to other recent architectures (e.g., residual networks, Inception) could be more thoroughly discussed.  
- Pro for acceptance: simple, elegant, broadly applicable method with compelling empirical evidence and no complex training requirements.  
- Con for acceptance: limited large-scale evaluation and incomplete comparative analysis with other architectures.  
- Recommendation: recommend acceptance to NIPS; significant and influential contribution to deep learning.
