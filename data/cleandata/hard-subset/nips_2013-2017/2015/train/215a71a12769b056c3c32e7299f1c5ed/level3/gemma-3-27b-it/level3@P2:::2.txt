This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward neural networks. The core idea is to allow information to flow unimpeded across many layers via “information highways” implemented using adaptive gating mechanisms inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks. The authors demonstrate that these networks can be trained directly with stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to depths exceeding 100 layers, a significant improvement over the difficulties encountered with plain, deeply stacked networks.

The work builds upon a substantial body of research concerning the challenges of training deep networks. The paper appropriately acknowledges prior efforts to address these issues, including improved optimizers (e.g., [13, 14, 15]), initialization strategies ([16, 17, 18, 19]), alternative activation functions ([20, 21]), skip connections ([22, 23, 24]), and knowledge distillation ([25, 26]). It positions highway networks as a complementary approach, offering a fundamentally different architectural solution rooted in facilitating information propagation. The connection to LSTMs ([29, 30]) is explicitly drawn and justified, and the authors differentiate their work from related recent techniques such as FitNets [25].

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The highway network architecture represents a genuinely novel approach to tackling the training difficulties of deep networks. The introduction of transform and carry gates allows for dynamic control of information flow, offering a flexible alternative to fixed skip connections.
* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed architecture is mathematically well-defined, and the explanations are clear and concise. The analysis of the Jacobian matrix (Equation 5) is a nice touch, highlighting the architectural properties.
* **Empirical Validation:** The paper presents comprehensive experimental results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 datasets. The comparison to plain networks demonstrates the significant advantage of highway networks in terms of trainability and performance with depth. Furthermore, comparisons to FitNets [25] and state-of-the-art methods strengthen the claim of significance.
* **In-Depth Analysis:** The visualizations and analyses presented in Sections 4 and 4.1 are particularly insightful. Examining the transform gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance provides a deeper understanding of how highway networks function internally and highlights the potential for data-dependent routing.
* **Clarity & Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized. The notation is consistent, and the figures are informative. The code and hyperparameters are publicly available, promoting reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

The paper is remarkably strong, and identifying significant weaknesses is challenging. A minor point is that while the authors mention that the choice of activation function (ReLU) is used consistently, a brief exploration of other activation functions in combination with highway networks could have further broadened the scope of the study. However, this is a minor consideration given the substantial contributions already made. There's a typo on page 5 mentioning "Tables 3 and 3".



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway network architecture offers a promising solution to the longstanding problem of training very deep networks, and the authors provide compelling evidence to support their claims. The thorough analysis and clear presentation further enhance the value of this work. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate significant improvements over existing approaches.
* The analysis provides valuable insights into the inner workings of highway networks.
* The paper is well-written, clear, and reproducible.
* The topic is highly relevant to the NIPS community, aligning with the conference’s focus on deep learning and optimization.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* There are no significant arguments against acceptance. The minor suggestions mentioned above do not detract from the overall quality of the work.

**Rating:** Strongly Accept. This paper is of excellent quality and deserves to be presented at NIPS. It’s a well-researched, clearly articulated, and highly impactful contribution.
