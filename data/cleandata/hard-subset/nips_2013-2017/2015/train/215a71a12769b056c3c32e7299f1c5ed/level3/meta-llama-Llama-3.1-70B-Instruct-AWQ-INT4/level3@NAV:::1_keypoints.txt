- Introduces highway networks that enable unimpeded information flow across many layers using adaptive gating inspired by LSTM.  
- Enables training of extremely deep networks directly with SGD, without complex initialization or layer-wise training.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the architecture and relation to prior work.  
- Experimental results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 convincingly demonstrate effectiveness and generalization.  
- Analysis of learned gate-output patterns provides useful insight into how highway networks operate.  
- Thorough analysis and comprehensive experiments addressing the challenge of training very deep networks.  
- Concern: use of sigmoid gates (outputs never exactly 0 or 1) raises questions about correctness of gradients in Equation 5 and backprop implementation—needs clarification.  
- Concern: initialization using a constant negative bias for transform gates is not principled and requires deeper analysis of its effects.  
- Concern: comparative claims vs other approaches lack clarity; need detailed comparison of experimental settings between studies.  
- Concern: reporting only maximum accuracy is insufficient—mean and standard deviation across multiple runs should be provided.  
- Overall: strong, impactful paper that requires minor revisions to address the above concerns.
