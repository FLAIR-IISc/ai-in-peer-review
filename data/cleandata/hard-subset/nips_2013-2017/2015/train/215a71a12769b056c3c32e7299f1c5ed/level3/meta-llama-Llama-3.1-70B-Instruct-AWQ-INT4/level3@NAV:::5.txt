This paper introduces a novel architecture for training very deep neural networks, called highway networks. The authors draw inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks and propose an adaptive gating mechanism that allows for unimpeded information flow across many layers. This approach enables the training of extremely deep networks using simple gradient descent.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach, including experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 datasets. The results demonstrate that highway networks can be trained directly without suffering from the difficulties posed by depth, and they achieve competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods.

One of the key strengths of this paper is its simplicity and elegance. The authors' idea of using an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism to enable the training of very deep networks is straightforward yet effective. The analysis and experiments provided in the paper are solid and well-executed, demonstrating the potential of highway networks for deep learning.

A potential alternative perspective on very deep networks is that they may not be as deep as they seem when layers contain sub-layers or more complex nonlinearities, such as those found in Inception or VGG networks. This perspective raises the question of whether highway networks can be viewed similarly as layers composed of richer substructures.

To further investigate the properties of highway networks, I would suggest an experiment where a thin-and-tall network is trained jointly on MNIST and CIFAR-10, forking at the top. This would allow us to see if the inference patterns match those of separately trained networks and whether parameters underutilized by MNIST are repurposed by CIFAR-10.

Overall, I believe that this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning, and I recommend testing the approach on larger datasets, such as ImageNet, to further demonstrate its potential.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper introduces a novel and simple approach for training very deep neural networks.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis and experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.
* The idea of using an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism is elegant and well-motivated.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper may benefit from a more detailed comparison with other approaches for training deep networks.
* The authors could provide more insight into the interpretability of the learned representations in highway networks.
