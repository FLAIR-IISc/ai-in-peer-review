- Introduces highway networks with adaptive transform and carry gates inspired by LSTM to ease training of very deep feedforward nets.  
- Leverages prior work (initialization schemes, skip-connections) to address optimization difficulties in deep networks.  
- Clear, elegant presentation and an effective architectural idea.  
- Experiments convincingly show highway networks (up to 100 layers) train with SGD while plain networks fail to converge.  
- Strong analysis of learned gate-output patterns and a lesion study demonstrating data-dependent routing and layer importance.  
- Technical error: Equation 5’s Jacobian is incorrect because sigmoid-based transform gates never produce exact 0 or 1; backprop implementation must be clarified.  
- Initialization concern: using constant negative biases for gates appears ad-hoc; authors should show performance versus initial bias to justify this choice.  
- Comparative claims (e.g., versus FitNets) need clarification on whether experimental settings are identical or how they differ.  
- Reporting concern: only maximum accuracies reported; mean and standard deviation across multiple runs are required for robust comparisons.  
- Suggestion: include a learning-theory analysis of how gating (conditional identities) affects optimization and representation capacity.
