- Proposes a novel on-the-job learning framework that learns from noisy crowds to reduce cost and maintain high accuracy.  
- Models the on-the-job setting as a stochastic game and uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with progressive widening to approximate the optimal policy.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment classification, and image classification, showing significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements over baselines.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains technical details, and provides thorough analysis of results.  
- Technical claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments; authors are careful and honest about strengths and weaknesses.  
- Originality stems from a novel combination of familiar techniques and clear differentiation from prior work.  
- Significance is high: addresses a difficult problem better than prior work and is likely to be used or extended by others.  
- Unclear modeling of the gate unit T using a standard sigmoid activation—needs detail on network structure and backpropagation.  
- Initialization effects are unclear—analysis needed on performance sensitivity to initial bias values (e.g., constant negative biases).  
- Comparison to recent studies is unsatisfying due to unclear differences in experimental setups; clarify to enable fair comparison.  
- Reporting "max" accuracies in tables is misleading; recommend reporting mean accuracy and standard deviation instead.  
- From a learning-theory perspective, investigating how gate units propagate inputs through selective layers would deepen understanding and applicability.
