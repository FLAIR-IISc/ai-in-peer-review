- Proposes Highway Networks to enable training of very deep feedforward neural networks.  
- Uses adaptive gating mechanisms ("information highways") inspired by LSTMs to allow unimpeded information flow.  
- Directly addresses the vanishing/exploding gradient problem that limits deep network training.  
- Shows highway networks can be trained with hundreds of layers using standard SGD, unlike traditional feedforward architectures.  
- Builds on prior depth-related work (optimizers, initialization, skip connections, soft targets) but offers dynamic routing rather than fixed connections or pre-training.  
- Notes similarity to LSTMs but emphasizes the difference: layer-wise feedforward operation vs. recurrence.  
- Provides thorough empirical results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, comparing to plain nets and FitNets.  
- Demonstrates easier training and comparable or superior performance to existing methods.  
- Analyzes gate behavior and layer importance, showing dynamic routing and substantial contribution from many layers for complex tasks.  
- Strong case for acceptance: novel, well-motivated, theoretically sound, and empirically validated solution to a fundamental DL problem.  
- Writing and organization are clear and commendable.  
- Minor editorial correction needed: fix "Tables 3 and 3" on page 5; reviewer suggests no further improvements.
