This paper proposes a novel neural network architecture called "highway networks" that enables the training of very deep networks. The authors introduce an adaptive gating mechanism inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks, which allows unimpeded information flow across many layers. The paper provides an in-depth analysis of how the gates select inputs across different layers and presents promising empirical results.

The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and original. The authors acknowledge similarities to previous work, such as FitNets and LSTMs, but clearly explain the differences and include comparisons. The paper is technically sound, with claims well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.

One of the strengths of the paper is its thorough analysis of the highway networks' behavior. The authors provide visualizations of the transform gate activity and block outputs, which help to understand how the network processes information. They also investigate the routing of information and layer importance, showing that the network can learn to dynamically adjust the routing of information based on the current input.

The empirical results presented in the paper are promising, with highway networks outperforming plain networks and achieving state-of-the-art results on several datasets. The authors also demonstrate that highway networks can be trained directly using stochastic gradient descent, without the need for specialized optimizers or initialization schemes.

Overall, this is a very solid paper with a novel technique and thorough analysis. The reviewer has no suggested improvements, and the only minor editorial note is a typo in the paper ("Tables 3 and 3" on page 5).

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and original neural network architecture that enables the training of very deep networks.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the highway networks' behavior, including visualizations and experiments.
* The empirical results presented in the paper are promising, with highway networks outperforming plain networks and achieving state-of-the-art results on several datasets.
* The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and technically sound.

Arguments against acceptance:

* None.

* Accept the paper for publication at NIPS.
