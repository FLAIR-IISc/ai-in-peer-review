- Tackles training very deep feedforward networks by introducing "Highway Networks" with layer-wise adaptive transform and carry gates.
- Gates let each layer smoothly interpolate between a non-linear transform and identity, creating information highways that ease gradient flow.
- Demonstrates direct training of networks up to 100 layers with standard SGD, which plain networks fail to train at similar depths.
- Empirical validation on MNIST and competitive results on CIFAR-10/100; code provided for reproducibility.
- High technical quality with well-motivated LSTM-inspired analogy and strong experimental support.
- Convincing optimization experiments directly compare plain vs. highway networks across depths with thorough hyperparameter search and variance reporting.
- Exceptionally clear and well-written presentation with informative figures and visualizations.
- Original idea: learned, data-dependent gating for depth-wise information flow is novel compared to prior skip connections.
- Significant impact: enables exploration of much deeper architectures and provides methods to analyze effective network depth and information routing.
- Analysis (layer lesioning and visualizations) is insightful, showing networks can adapt their effective complexity per task.
- Minor clarity issue: statement that "most transform gates are active on average" seems to contradict low mean activity in the visualization and should be clarified.
- Suggested additional baseline: plain networks initialized near identity (e.g., identity-like weights) to isolate benefits of learned gating vs. fixed near-identity starts.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective architecture; strong theoretical motivation and experiments; useful analysis tools; excellent presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: none significant; only minor issues that can be fixed in revision.
- Overall assessment: excellent, original, technically sound paper with compelling results and a strong recommendation for acceptance.
