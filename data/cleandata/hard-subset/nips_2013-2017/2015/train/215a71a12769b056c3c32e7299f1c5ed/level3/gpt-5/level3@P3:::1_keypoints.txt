- Paper introduces highway networks: feedforward layers with LSTM-style gates y = H(x)⊙T(x) + x⊙(1−T(x)) and negative biasing of T to create “information highways” enabling very deep training.
- Empirical results show stable optimization up to 100 layers (pilot >1000), competitive MNIST/CIFAR results with small models, and insightful gate analyses.
- Strength: clear formulation and simple negative-gate-bias recipe that reliably enables deep-net training.
- Strength: experiments convincingly demonstrate optimization advantages over plain nets and competitive accuracy under modest augmentation.
- Strength: strong analysis (gate bias dynamics, sample-conditional routing, lesion studies) gives rare visibility into deep-net computation.
- Missing/incorrect gradient expressions: Eq.(5) only holds for T∈{0,1}; provide exact forward/backprop equations implemented, including full Jacobian and any gradient-stopping approximations.
- Initialization concerns: negative gate bias b_T is an ad hoc hyperparameter acting as a structural regularizer; report sensitivity of performance to b_T (plots, per-depth/dataset) and state tuning protocol.
- Comparative claims insufficiently normalized: explicitly state differences vs. prior work (architecture, params, FLOPs, data augmentation, LR schedules, model selection) and avoid claiming direct superiority if settings differ.
- Reporting practice needs improvement: report mean ± std over multiple seeds, number of trials, and distributions for random-search comparisons (not just best runs).
- Recommended ablations to strengthen paper: independent carry gate C(x) vs. C=1−T, gate nonlinearity choices and saturation stats, cost analysis (params/training time/memory), and gated vs. plain resizing when dimensionality changes.
- Reproducibility requests: include explicit forward/backward equations, tensor shapes and parameter counts per block, learning-rate schedules per dataset, and precise model-selection criteria.
- Originality: novel application of LSTM-style adaptive gating to feedforward depth, distinct from fixed skip connections; gate-behavior analyses are original and valuable.
- Significance: method potentially lowers optimization barrier for very deep nets and offers a path-composition view of depth, likely influential despite modest-scale datasets.
- Suggested theory additions: light learning-theory treatment analyzing gates as conditional identities, path-based complexity, generalization bounds tied to open-gate fraction, and conditions for non-vanishing gradients.
- Pros summary: simple, compelling idea; strong empirical evidence SGD can train very deep nets; insightful empirical analyses.
- Cons summary: underspecified/incorrect backprop for gated layer, insufficient analysis of gate-bias initialization, and non-normalized/ best-only comparisons.
- Recommendation: lean accept contingent on (1) fully specifying/fixing gradient/backprop equations and (2) strengthening reporting and comparisons (mean±std, b_T sensitivity, and explicit setting differences).
