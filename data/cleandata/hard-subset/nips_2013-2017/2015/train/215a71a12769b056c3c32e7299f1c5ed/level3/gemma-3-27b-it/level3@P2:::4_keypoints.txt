- Introduces highway networks: gating-based “information highways” inspired by LSTM to enable very deep feedforward networks.
- Shows highway networks can be trained with standard SGD to hundreds of layers, overcoming training difficulties of plain deep nets.
- Positions contribution within prior work on optimization, initialization, activations, and skip connections, and links architecture motivation to LSTM.
- Demonstrates effectiveness via experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, with strong training and generalization results.
- Analysis shows transform gates regulate information flow and identifies which layers are most important.
- Paper is clear, well-organized, with reproducible code and hyperparameters provided.
- Strength: conceptually simple yet effective architecture that enables training of much deeper networks.
- Weakness: analysis section could be more extensive, e.g., deeper exploration of gate values across input classes.
- Weakness: needs broader ablation studies (different activations, layer configs) to fully characterize design choices.
- Weakness: comparison to Fitnets lacks discussion of computational trade-offs.
- Weakness: related-work discussion should more explicitly address residual networks and dense connections.
- Argument for acceptance: novel architecture offering a distinct mechanism for deep network training.
- Argument for acceptance: significant potential impact by enabling deeper models for complex tasks.
- Argument for acceptance: technically sound with convincing experimental support.
- Argument for acceptance: well-written and accessible presentation.
- Argument against acceptance: limited depth of analysis into learned behaviors.
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient explicit connection to recent residual/dense advances.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; recommend acceptance.
