- Introduces highway networks, an architecture inspired by LSTMs that enables direct training of extremely deep networks (up to 100+ layers) with standard SGD.  
- Addresses vanishing gradients and poor initialization via adaptive gating mechanisms that create "information highways" for unimpeded layer-to-layer flow.  
- Builds on skip connections and knowledge distillation but offers a more principled single-stage solution that removes the need for two-stage training.  
- Strong empirical quality: comprehensive experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 show superior performance as depth increases versus plain networks.  
- Solid theoretical foundation with clear mathematical formulation of the highway layer transformation.  
- Excellent clarity: well-written, clear notation, logical flow, and effective visualizations illustrating information routing and layer importance.  
- High originality: highway networks represent a notable departure from prior deep training methods and are broadly applicable.  
- Substantial significance: enabling direct training of very deep networks opens new directions for architecture exploration and future research.  
- Key contributions: demonstration of training 100+ layer networks directly and insightful analysis of information routing and layer importance (Section 4).  
- Experimental results are compelling, showing good generalization while maintaining training efficiency.  
- Minor weaknesses: a wording error in Section 4.1 ("not active on average" vs. "most transform blocks are active on average").  
- Suggestion: better contextualize and compare with identity-initialized layers with noise and clarify how transform gates differ from that simpler scheme.
