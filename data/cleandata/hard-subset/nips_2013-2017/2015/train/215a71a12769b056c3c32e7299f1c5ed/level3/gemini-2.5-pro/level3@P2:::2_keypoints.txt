- Proposes "highway networks" with adaptive transform and carry gates to enable training of very deep feed-forward networks.  
- Gates are LSTM-inspired and allow data-dependent routing of information and gradients across many layers.  
- Enables direct training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard SGD and a simple initialization strategy.  
- Places work in context of prior depth-related methods (initialization, advanced optimizers, FitNets) and contrasts dynamic gating with fixed skip connections.  
- Original and significant idea: adapting LSTM-style gating to solve the degradation problem in deep feed-forward nets.  
- Strong technical quality with well-designed experiments validating optimization advantages as depth increases.  
- MNIST experiments show plain networks degrade beyond ~20 layers while highway networks maintain/improve up to 100 layers.  
- Comparison to FitNets on CIFAR-10 demonstrates similar or better performance without complex two-stage training.  
- CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 results are competitive with contemporary state-of-the-art, showing good generalization.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly motivated, and mathematically precise.  
- In-depth analyses include transform-gate visualizations revealing selective gating behavior.  
- Demonstrates data-dependent, class-specific information routing through gate activity analyses.  
- Layer lesioning study shows networks use depth for complex tasks and can effectively bypass layers for simpler tasks.  
- Reviewer finds no substantive weaknesses; only minor proofreading/typographical issues noted.  
- Strong arguments for acceptance: high impact, novelty, strong empirical results, excellent analysis, and clarity.  
- No significant arguments against acceptance; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
