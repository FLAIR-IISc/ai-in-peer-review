## Review of “Training Very Deep Networks”

This paper introduces “highway networks,” a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward neural networks. Inspired by LSTM networks, highway networks employ adaptive gating mechanisms to enable more direct information flow across layers, mitigating the vanishing/exploding gradient problem that plagues very deep architectures. The core idea – allowing layers to act as identity mappings when beneficial – is a clever and intuitive approach to stabilizing training. The paper effectively positions this work within the existing landscape of deep learning research, referencing relevant efforts in optimization, initialization, skip connections, and knowledge distillation.

The strength of this paper lies in its clear articulation of the problem and its proposed solution. The authors convincingly demonstrate through experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 that highway networks can be trained effectively to depths previously inaccessible with standard backpropagation. The experimental evaluation is solid, with comparisons to established methods like FitNets and state-of-the-art convolutional networks. The visualizations in the analysis section (Figure 2 & 4) offer valuable insights into the network's internal workings, particularly the selective activation of gates and the differing importance of layers. The clarity of the writing is also commendable; the paper is well-organized and explains the concepts in a readily understandable manner. 

However, the analysis section, while insightful, could benefit from more depth. For example, while the visualizations of gate activity are useful, exploring the correlation between gate activity and the data being processed in more detail could be valuable. Further illustration of *how* the network learns to route information – perhaps through visualizations of gradient flow – would strengthen the analysis. Additionally, the authors briefly mention a more complex LSTM-inspired block design; detailing its performance compared to the simplified version presented would be informative.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture represents a genuinely novel approach to training very deep networks, generalizing prior cross-layer techniques.
*   **Significance:** Enabling the training of deeper networks is a significant step towards realizing the full potential of deep learning for complex tasks.
*   **Strong Empirical Evidence:** The experimental results consistently demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Limited Analysis:** While the analysis section provides some insights, it could be more comprehensive.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning. Despite the minor weakness in the analysis, the novelty, significance, and clear presentation of the work outweigh any concerns. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to expand upon the analysis section.
