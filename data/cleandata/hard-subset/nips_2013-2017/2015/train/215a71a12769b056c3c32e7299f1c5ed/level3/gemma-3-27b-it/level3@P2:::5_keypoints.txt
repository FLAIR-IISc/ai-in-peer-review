- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-inspired adaptive gating to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Demonstrates that highway networks can be trained via SGD to depths where plain networks fail.  
- Strong empirical results shown on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Situates the work well within existing literature on optimizers, initialization, activations, and skip connections.  
- Novelty: combining gating and skip-like connections in feedforward networks is a significant and original contribution.  
- Technical soundness: architecture, equations, and transform/carry gate explanations are clear and correct.  
- Empirical validation is compelling, especially the MNIST depth comparison and CIFAR results.  
- Analysis: ablation studies, gate bias analysis, and visualizations provide useful insights into network behavior.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and accessible even to readers unfamiliar with LSTMs.  
- Weakness: needs deeper discussion of relationships to other complex-layer architectures (e.g., Inception, VGG-style stacked convolutions).  
- Weakness: lacks large-scale experiments (e.g., ImageNet) to fully demonstrate practical significance.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant original contribution, overcomes key limitation in training deep feedforward nets, strong experiments and clear presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: absence of ImageNet-scale results and insufficient exploration of connections to other architectures.  
- Suggested future experiment: train a single thin-and-tall highway network jointly on MNIST and CIFAR with forked outputs to study parameter reuse and dynamic routing.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contributions; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
