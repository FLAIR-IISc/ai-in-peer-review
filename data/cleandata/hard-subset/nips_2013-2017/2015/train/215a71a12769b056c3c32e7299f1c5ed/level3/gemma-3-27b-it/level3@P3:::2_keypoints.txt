- Proposes highway networks, a novel architecture to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Core idea: introduce “information highways” with adaptive gating (inspired by LSTMs) to allow information flow across many layers without attenuation.  
- Demonstrates that these networks can be trained with standard SGD to depths previously inaccessible to plain networks.  
- Provides thorough empirical results, including comparisons to plain networks and FitNets, showing successful training of networks with hundreds of layers.  
- Analyzes gate behavior in detail (Figures 2–4), offering insight into how gates control information flow.  
- Builds on prior work in optimization, initialization, and skip connections but clearly differentiates and advances the field.  
- Technically sound: clear mathematical formulation (Equation 3) and robust experimental validation.  
- Includes ablation studies and layer importance analysis that strengthen the empirical claims.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized writing; figures and notation are informative.  
- Minor editorial issue: "Tables 3 and 3" on page 5 should be corrected.  
- Original contribution: novel application of adaptive gating to feedforward networks, effectively addressing vanishing gradients.  
- Significant potential impact: enables exploration of extreme depth and may improve performance across tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, thorough experiments, insightful analysis, clear presentation, addresses a major challenge.  
- Arguments against acceptance: none; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
