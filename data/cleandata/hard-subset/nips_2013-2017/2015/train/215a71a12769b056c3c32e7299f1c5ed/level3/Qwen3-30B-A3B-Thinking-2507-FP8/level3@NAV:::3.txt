

This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture inspired by LSTM units that enables direct training of extremely deep networks (up to 100 layers) using standard SGD. The authors address the long-standing challenges of vanishing gradients and poor initialization in deep networks by introducing adaptive gating mechanisms that allow information to flow unimpeded through "information highways" across layers. This work builds upon and extends previous approaches like skip connections [22, 23] and knowledge distillation [25], but offers a more principled solution that eliminates the need for two-stage training procedures.

The paper excels in all four evaluation criteria. Quality is strong with comprehensive experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 demonstrating that highway networks achieve superior performance with increased depth compared to plain networks. The theoretical foundation is sound, with clear mathematical formulations of the highway layer transformation. Clarity is excellent - the paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear notation, logical flow, and compelling visualizations in Figures 2-4 that illustrate information routing and layer importance. Originality is high, as the highway network concept represents a significant departure from prior work on deep network training, offering a general solution applicable to various network architectures. Significance is substantial, as the ability to directly train very deep networks opens new possibilities for architecture exploration and could influence future deep learning research.

The paper's strongest contributions are the demonstration of direct training of networks with 100+ layers and the insightful analysis of information routing and layer importance (Section 4). The experimental results are particularly compelling, showing that highway networks generalize well while maintaining training efficiency.

Minor weaknesses include a small error in Section 4.1 (should read "not active on average" instead of "most transform blocks are active on average") and an opportunity to better contextualize the approach by comparing with identity-initialized layers with noise (a simpler alternative that the authors suggest addressing). The authors should clarify how their transform gates differ from this simpler scheme.

Overall, this is a high-quality, well-executed paper that makes a significant contribution to deep learning. The work is clearly superior to previous approaches for training very deep networks, and the insights about information routing have broad implications. I strongly recommend acceptance.
