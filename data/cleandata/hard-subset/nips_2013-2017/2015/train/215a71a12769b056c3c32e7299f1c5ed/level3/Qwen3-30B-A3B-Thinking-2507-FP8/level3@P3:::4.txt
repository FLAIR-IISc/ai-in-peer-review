

# Review of "Training Very Deep Networks" (NIPS 2015)

This paper introduces "highway networks," a novel architecture designed to overcome training difficulties in extremely deep neural networks. The authors propose a generalization of previous cross-layer techniques by introducing adaptive gating mechanisms inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks. The key innovation is the use of transform gates (T) and carry gates (C) that regulate information flow across layers, creating "information highways" that allow unimpeded information propagation. This approach enables direct training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard gradient descent, unlike plain networks that become increasingly difficult to optimize with depth.

The paper positions itself within the context of recent advances in deep learning, acknowledging prior work on skip connections [22, 23, 24], knowledge distillation [25, 26], and normalized initialization [16, 17]. It distinguishes itself by introducing a flexible gating mechanism that dynamically routes information based on input rather than using fixed skip connections. The work builds on the observation that very deep networks can represent functions more efficiently than shallow ones [10, 11, 12], while addressing the practical challenge of training such networks.

**Quality**: The paper demonstrates strong technical soundness with comprehensive experimental validation. The authors conduct rigorous optimization experiments on MNIST, showing that highway networks maintain performance as depth increases, while plain networks suffer significantly (100-layer highway networks outperform similarly-sized plain networks by over two orders of magnitude). The CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 experiments demonstrate competitive results with state-of-the-art methods while using fewer parameters. The analysis of transform gate behavior (Figure 2) provides valuable insights into how information flows through the network. The main weakness is that the analysis section could benefit from additional illustrations and more detailed examination of the gating mechanisms across different architectures and datasets.

**Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical formulation of highway networks (Equation 3) is clear and well-motivated, with a helpful discussion of the special cases (Equation 4). The experimental setup is thoroughly described, and the results are presented in an accessible manner with appropriate statistical reporting (Best (mean ± std.dev.)). The main messages are communicated effectively without unnecessary jargon.

**Originality**: The highway network architecture represents a significant conceptual advance over prior work. While skip connections [22, 23, 24] have been used before, this paper generalizes them with adaptive gating mechanisms that allow information to flow through multiple layers without attenuation. The approach is sufficiently original while building on established techniques (LSTMs), and the paper adequately references related work to establish its novelty.

**Significance**: This work addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning: training very deep networks. The ability to train networks with hundreds of layers directly using standard gradient descent has substantial implications for both research and applications. The paper demonstrates that highway networks can match or exceed the performance of state-of-the-art methods with fewer parameters, making them more efficient. The analysis of layer importance (Figure 4) provides a novel way to assess the contribution of depth for specific problems, which is difficult with plain networks.

**Strengths**:
- Clear conceptual innovation with strong experimental validation
- Demonstrates practical benefits for training extremely deep networks
- Provides valuable insights into information flow through deep networks
- Well-executed experiments across multiple datasets
- Clear and effective communication of main ideas

**Weaknesses**:
- Analysis section could include more varied details and additional illustrations
- Limited discussion of computational cost compared to other approaches
- The paper could more thoroughly explore the relationship between highway networks and residual connections (which emerged around the same time)

The paper makes a significant contribution to deep learning by enabling the training of much deeper networks than previously possible. The highway network architecture is sufficiently general to be applied across various network types (fully connected, convolutional) and demonstrates clear advantages over plain networks. The experimental evidence strongly supports the claims, and the approach has proven influential in subsequent deep learning research (with residual networks [36] being a direct descendant of these ideas).

The authors have clearly met the four criteria: the work is technically sound (Quality), well-written (Clarity), conceptually novel (Originality), and addresses a fundamental problem with important implications (Significance). While the analysis section could be strengthened, the paper's main contribution is compelling and well-supported.

**Recommendation**: Accept. This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture has proven to be influential, and the paper's experimental evidence is robust and convincing. The minor weaknesses noted do not detract from the paper's overall impact and quality.
