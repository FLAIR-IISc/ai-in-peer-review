- Introduces highway networks, a novel architecture to overcome difficulties in training very deep neural networks.  
- Draws inspiration from LSTM and uses adaptive gating units to regulate inter-layer information flow.  
- Enables training of extremely deep networks (hundreds of layers) with simple gradient descent.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise overview of related work.  
- Emphasizes importance of network depth and challenges like the vanishing gradient problem.  
- Provides detailed architecture description and training procedure, including transform gate bias initialization.  
- Experimental results show training of highway networks up to 100 layers and strong performance on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100.  
- Includes analysis of internal behavior, information routing, and layer importance.  
- Strengths: originality, high-quality writing, and compelling experimental validation.  
- Suggests minor correction: change “most transform blocks are active on average” to “not active on average” in Section 4.1.  
- Recommends additional comparison to identity-initialized layers (with noise) and clarification of how transform gates differ.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, reproducible, significant contribution, and a pleasure to read.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel idea, strong experiments, clear writing, and better solution to a difficult problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: none.  
- Rating: 9/10.
