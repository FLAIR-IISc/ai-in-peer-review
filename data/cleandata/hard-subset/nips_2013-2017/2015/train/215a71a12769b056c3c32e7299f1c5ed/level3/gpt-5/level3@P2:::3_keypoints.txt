- Proposes highway networks: LSTM-style transform and carry gates (C=1−T) per layer to learn input-dependent routing and enable near-identity behavior when T≈0.  
- Initializes transform-gate biases negative to bias initial carry behavior, allowing gradients/activations to propagate across many layers.  
- Demonstrates training very deep (50–100+) networks end-to-end with vanilla SGD, without multi-stage training or auxiliary losses.  
- Empirical results: highway nets maintain performance and converge faster than plain nets across depths; competitive on MNIST/CIFAR-10/100 with thin models.  
- Provides analyses of gate activity, data-dependent routing, and lesioning to probe layer importance and information flow.  
- Clearly positioned relative to prior work (LSTM gating, initialization/rectifiers, maxout, skip connections, FitNets) and distinguished by learned, conditional routing.  
- Strong experimental quality: controlled comparisons, careful training protocol, multiple runs with stats, and released code for reproducibility.  
- Missing baseline comparisons: identity/orthogonal-initialized deep plain nets (with small noise or identity bypass) to separate gating vs. near-identity initialization.  
- Missing baseline: fixed skip/shortcut connections under matched capacity/compute to contrast with learned gates.  
- Ablation gaps: sensitivity to transform-gate bias, freeing C vs. tying C=1−T, computational/parameter overhead of gates, and gradient-norm/activation statistics across depth.  
- Limited scale: no large-scale dataset experiments; focus is on optimization behavior rather than SOTA performance.  
- Writing and presentation are clear; figures effectively illustrate gating and routing behavior.  
- Correction needed: Section 4.1 sentence should state “most transform blocks are not active on average” for CIFAR-100.  
- Minor clarifications requested: quantify gate parameter/computation overhead, explain dimensionality changes handling, and clarify practical impact of tying C=1−T vs. learning separate C.  
- Originality: novel and elegant extension of LSTM-style gating to feedforward nets to create learnable highways distinct from fixed skips or initialization-only methods.  
- Significance: simple, broadly applicable method that reliably enables very deep network training and offers insights into depth utilization.  
- Pros: easy to implement, stable optimization with increasing depth, competitive benchmarks without hints, insightful analyses, and available code.  
- Cons: lacks specific baseline comparisons, limited gate-design ablations, and no large-scale experiments.  
- Suggested improvements: add identity/orthogonal and fixed-bypass baselines, report gradient/activation statistics, ablate gate bias and separate carry gate, and quantify gate overhead.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, original, well-supported paper; strong recommendation to accept (high confidence).
