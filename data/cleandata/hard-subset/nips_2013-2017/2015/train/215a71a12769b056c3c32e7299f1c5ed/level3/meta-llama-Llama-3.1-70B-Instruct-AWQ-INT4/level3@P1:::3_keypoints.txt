- Proposes "highway networks," LSTM-inspired layers with adaptive gates to regulate information flow across very deep networks.  
- Enables training of extremely deep networks (up to 100 layers) using simple gradient descent.  
- Addresses vanishing/exploding gradient and initialization issues via transform gate biases.  
- Experimental results show state-of-the-art performance on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Information routing and layer importance experiments give insight into how different layers are used.  
- Well-written, well-organized, and concise presentation with engaging exposition.  
- Thorough literature positioning and clear explanation of the method and analyses.  
- Novel and significant contribution with potential major impact on deep learning.  
- Reviewer finds no arguments against acceptance.  
- Suggested correction: change Section 4.1 wording to "not active on average" instead of "most transform blocks are active on average."  
- Suggested future work: compare highway gates to identity-initialized layers (with noise) and clarify differences.  
- Suggested future work: explore highway networks in other domains such as NLP or speech recognition.
