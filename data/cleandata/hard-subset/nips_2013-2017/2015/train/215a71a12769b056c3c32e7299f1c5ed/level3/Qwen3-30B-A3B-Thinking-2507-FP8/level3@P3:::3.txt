

**Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"**

This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to enable direct training of extremely deep neural networks (up to 100+ layers) using standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The authors draw inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks to introduce adaptive gating units that regulate information flow through the network. The key insight is that by allowing information to flow through "highways" (i.e., paths where inputs are directly carried through layers), the network can avoid vanishing gradients and other challenges associated with training very deep architectures. This represents a significant departure from previous approaches that relied on normalized initialization schemes [16, 17], skip connections [22, 23], or two-stage training methods [25].

The paper is exceptionally well-positioned within the deep learning literature. It properly situates its contributions relative to prior work on network depth (citing the dramatic ImageNet accuracy improvements from 84% to 95% [1-3]), recurrent networks [7], and previous attempts to address depth limitations. The highway network architecture builds upon these foundations while introducing a fundamentally new approach to information routing.

**Quality** - The paper is technically sound with rigorous experimental validation. The authors demonstrate training of 100-layer highway networks with superior performance compared to plain networks (more than 2 orders of magnitude better on MNIST), while showing that highway networks generalize well to unseen data. The analysis of information routing (Section 4.1) and layer importance (Section 4.2) provides compelling evidence of the network's internal dynamics. The paper correctly identifies that "most transform blocks are active on average" should read "not active on average" in Section 4.1, and this minor correction would strengthen the analysis. The authors are honest about limitations, noting that for simpler tasks like MNIST, many layers remain unused.

**Clarity** - The paper is exceptionally well-written with clear notation and logical flow. The highway network architecture is explained with sufficient detail, and the figures effectively illustrate key concepts. The distinction between highway networks and plain networks is well-articulated, and the experimental setup is thoroughly described. The only clarity issue is the correction noted above.

**Originality** - This work presents a genuinely novel approach to deep network training. While skip connections have been used previously, the adaptive gating mechanism inspired by LSTMs represents a significant conceptual advance. The authors could further strengthen their originality claim by explicitly comparing with identity-initialized layers (with noise), which is a simpler alternative that has been used to train deeper networks [36]. The authors should explain how transform gates differ from this simple scheme and why adaptive gating provides advantages.

**Significance** - The significance of this work is substantial. Training very deep networks directly with SGD has been a long-standing challenge, and highway networks provide a practical solution. The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100 with simpler architectures that are easier to train. The ability to directly evaluate layer importance (Figure 4) represents a valuable methodological advance that has not been possible with plain networks. The analysis of information routing shows that highway networks learn data-dependent routing mechanisms, which is a significant insight into how deep networks process information.

**Overall Assessment** - This is a high-quality, well-written paper with a novel idea that addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning. The experiments are thorough and convincing, and the results demonstrate clear advantages over previous approaches. The paper is a pleasure to read and makes a significant contribution to the field.

**Recommendation** - Accept. This paper represents a major advancement in deep learning by enabling the direct training of extremely deep networks. The minor correction to Section 4.1 and the suggested comparison with identity-initialized layers would further strengthen the paper, but these are easily addressed.

**Strengths:**
- Novel architecture enabling direct training of extremely deep networks
- Comprehensive experiments across multiple datasets and depths
- Compelling analysis of information routing and layer importance
- Well-written with clear exposition
- Demonstrates practical advantages over previous approaches

**Weaknesses:**
- Section 4.1 contains an error that needs correction ("not active on average" instead of "most transform blocks are active on average")
- Could provide more explicit comparison with identity-initialized layers (with noise) to better highlight the advantages of adaptive gating

This paper makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning by providing a practical solution to a fundamental problem, and it is a pleasure to read. The authors have provided a clear, well-supported argument for the advantages of their highway network architecture.
