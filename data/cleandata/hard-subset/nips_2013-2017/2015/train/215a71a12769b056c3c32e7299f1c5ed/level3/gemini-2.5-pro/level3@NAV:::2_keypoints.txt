- Introduces highway networks, a feed-forward architecture with learnable gates to enable training of very deep networks.  
- Gates, inspired by LSTM, dynamically choose to transform or carry inputs, creating "information highways."  
- Addresses optimization difficulties and degraded information propagation in very deep plain networks.  
- Authors acknowledge similarities to LSTMs and FitNets while clearly differentiating their approach.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and well-designed, including MNIST and CIFAR experiments.  
- Demonstrates ability to train networks hundreds of layers deep directly with SGD.  
- Claims are convincingly supported by experimental results showing superiority over plain networks and competitive performance with contemporary models.  
- Paper is original, significant, and technically sound.  
- Writing and organization are clear and accessible.  
- Offers in-depth analysis of internal dynamics via gate visualizations and lesion studies, providing insights into learned routing.  
- No major criticisms; work is complete, polished, and convincing.  
- Minor editorial issue: incorrect table reference on page 5 ("Tables 3 and 3").  
- Strong recommendation for acceptance; likely to be influential and widely used.
