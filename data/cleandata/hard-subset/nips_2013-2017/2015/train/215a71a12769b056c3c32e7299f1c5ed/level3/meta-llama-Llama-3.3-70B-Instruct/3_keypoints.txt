- Proposes a novel on-the-job learning framework that learns from noisy crowds while reducing cost over time.  
- Models the on-the-job setting as a stochastic game and leverages game-playing ideas to approximate the optimal policy.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- Strong originality: using a stochastic-game formulation for on-the-job learning is novel and interesting.  
- Technical soundness: thorough theoretical analysis supporting the approach.  
- Significant empirical results: demonstrates substantial cost reductions and accuracy improvements over baselines.  
- Experiments show potential applicability across NLP, computer vision, and recommender systems.  
- Main weakness: assumes a specific crowdsourcing setup, limiting generalizability to other scenarios.  
- Needs more discussion distinguishing transform gates from simple initialization schemes.  
- Could benefit from additional experiments and analyses to further validate effectiveness.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, significant contribution with good potential for acceptance.
