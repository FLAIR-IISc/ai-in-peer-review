This paper proposes highway networks, a feed-forward architecture that introduces input-dependent transform and carry gates to regulate information flow, enabling inputs to be copied unchanged across layers. By biasing the transform gates toward carry at initialization, the model creates “information highways” that mitigate vanishing gradients and allow successful optimization of networks with tens to hundreds of layers via standard SGD. The authors position the work clearly relative to LSTM gating (source of the gating idea), skip connections and deep supervision, and FitNets/hint-based training; unlike FitNets, highway networks train deeply in a single stage and use learned gates to create adaptive, input-dependent shortcut paths. The paper includes an in-depth analysis of gate behavior across depth, showing data-dependent routing and selective use of layers, and reports strong empirical results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 with competitive or superior performance at comparable parameter budgets.

Quality: The paper is technically sound and complete. The layer formulation and Jacobian discussion are clear and justify why gradients propagate. Experimental design is careful (controlled parameter counts, hyperparameter search, convergence analysis), and the gate analyses (bias evolution, activity patterns, lesion studies) convincingly support the claims. Open-source code availability is a plus.

Clarity: The manuscript is very well written and organized, with accessible notation and informative figures. Minor editorial issue: on p.5 “Tables 3 and 3” appears to be a typo.

Originality: Adapting LSTM-style gating to deep feed-forward networks with per-layer, input-dependent routing is a novel architectural contribution distinct from prior fixed skip connections and teacher-student methodologies. The paper clearly delineates similarities and differences and includes direct comparisons.

Significance: The ability to reliably train very deep networks with simple optimization is important and likely to influence both practice and subsequent architectures. The analyses provide insight into depth utilization and input-dependent computation, offering value beyond raw accuracy gains.

Pros:
- Novel, principled architecture with strong intuition and analysis
- Clear empirical improvements and stable optimization at extreme depths
- Thorough comparisons to relevant prior work
- High-quality writing and reproducibility

Cons:
- Only a minor editorial typo; no substantive weaknesses identified

Recommendation: Strong accept. This is a solid, original contribution with compelling empirical results and insightful analysis.
