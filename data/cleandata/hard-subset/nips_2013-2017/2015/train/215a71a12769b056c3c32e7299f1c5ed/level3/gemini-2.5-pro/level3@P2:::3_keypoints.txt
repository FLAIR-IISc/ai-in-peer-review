- Proposes "highway networks": layers with learnable transform and carry gates that adaptively combine input and transformed input to enable very deep feedforward training with simple SGD.  
- Empirically demonstrates successful training of up to 100-layer highway networks on MNIST and CIFAR, outperforming plain networks of similar depth.  
- Technical quality is high; optimization experiments (Sec. 3.1) convincingly show the architecture’s advantage as depth increases.  
- Strong analysis (Sec. 4) using gate visualizations and layer lesioning explains how the network uses depth and routes information.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, with transparent experimental setup, illustrative figures, and released code for reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity issue: phrasing "active on average" for CIFAR-100 contradicts figure—should clarify gate activity wording.  
- Highly original: applies learnable, input-dependent gating (inspired by LSTM) to feedforward networks, extending beyond fixed skip connections.  
- Highly significant: addresses critical challenge of training very deep networks, influencing subsequent architectures (e.g., ResNets) and depth understanding.  
- Suggested additional experiment: compare to a non-adaptive baseline (e.g., identity-initialized layers) to isolate benefits of adaptive gating.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, simple, effective solution; enables stable training of very deep nets; thorough experiments and insightful internal analysis; clear and impactful.  
- Argument against acceptance (minor): lacks comparison to an identity-initialized non-adaptive baseline to fully attribute gains to learned gating.
