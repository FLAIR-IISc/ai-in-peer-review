This paper proposes a novel architecture, called highway networks, that enables the training of very deep neural networks. The authors draw inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks and introduce adaptive gating units to regulate the information flow across layers. This allows unimpeded information flow across many layers, which the authors term "information highways." The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed architecture and presents promising empirical results on various datasets.

The authors relate their work to previous contributions in the field, including the use of skip connections, soft targets, and layer-wise training. They also acknowledge similarities to FitNets and LSTMs but clearly explain the differences and include comparisons. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their architecture and the underlying intuition.

The proposed highway networks are shown to be effective in training very deep networks, with the authors demonstrating the training of networks with hundreds of layers. The empirical results are promising, with the authors showing that their approach can match or exceed the performance of state-of-the-art methods on various datasets.

The paper also provides an in-depth analysis of how the gates select inputs across different layers. The authors present visualizations of the gate activity and block outputs, which provide insight into the inner workings of the network. They also investigate the routing of information through the network and show that the network can learn to dynamically adjust the routing based on the input.

The authors also address the potential concern that many layers might remain unused if the transform gates stay closed. They show that this is not the case and that the network can learn to utilize all of its layers, even for complex problems.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their architecture. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The paper is also well-organized, and the authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.
