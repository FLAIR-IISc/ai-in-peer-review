1. Proposes highway networks that use transform and carry gates to create information highways enabling training of extremely deep feedforward models.  
2. Gates, inspired by LSTMs, let layers dynamically transform or copy inputs, improving gradient flow through hundreds of layers.  
3. Highway networks can be trained directly with standard SGD at depths (100+) where plain networks fail to converge.  
4. Experiments on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 show strong generalization, matching or outperforming more complex two-stage methods like FitNets.  
5. Technical strengths include a clear formulation and an important initialization strategy biasing gates toward carry behavior.  
6. Analyses (gate visualizations and lesioning) demonstrate data-dependent routing and selective utilization of depth across tasks.  
7. The idea is novel and significant, connecting LSTM gating to feedforward nets and enabling exploration of much deeper architectures (influencing later ResNets).  
8. Suggests reframing depth as layer complexity; a highway layer could be seen as a more expressive computational block akin to Inception/VGG motifs.  
9. Recommends a joint-task experiment (e.g., shared trunk for MNIST and CIFAR-10) to test whether gates learn task-specific routing and parameter reuse.  
10. Notes the need to validate scalability on large datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to strengthen the architecture's practical impact.  
11. Overall assessment: well-executed, convincing, and impactful work—strongly recommended for acceptance.
