- Proposes "highway networks," a novel neural architecture enabling training of very deep networks via adaptive gating.
- Introduces an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism that allows near-unimpeded information flow across many layers.
- Provides in-depth analysis of gate behavior and how gates select inputs across layers.
- Presents visualizations of transform gate activity and block outputs to clarify network processing.
- Investigates routing of information and layer importance, showing dynamic adjustment based on input.
- Empirical results show highway networks outperform plain networks and achieve state-of-the-art on several datasets.
- Demonstrates highway networks can be trained directly with SGD without special optimizers or initialization.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and original.
- Authors acknowledge and compare to related work (e.g., FitNets, LSTMs) and clarify differences.
- Claims are technically sound and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.
- Reviewer suggests no substantive improvements; only minor editorial typo ("Tables 3 and 3").
- No arguments against acceptance; recommendation: accept for publication at NIPS.
