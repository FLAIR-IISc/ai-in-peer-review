- Proposes "highway networks": novel deep architecture with direct cross‑layer "information highways" and LSTM‑inspired adaptive gating.  
- Motivated by importance of depth and challenges like vanishing gradients and poor activation/gradient propagation.  
- Architecture enables unimpeded information flow across many layers to overcome training difficulties.  
- Demonstrates highway networks can be trained directly with SGD and improve classification on MNIST, CIFAR‑10, CIFAR‑100.  
- Provides analysis of learned representations and information routing that gives insight into network behavior.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares favorably to plain and state‑of‑the‑art networks.  
- Paper is well‑written and communicates main messages clearly.  
- Strength: novel, well‑motivated architecture that generalizes prior cross‑layer techniques.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical results supporting claims.  
- Strength: analysis of representations offers valuable understanding of the method.  
- Weakness: analysis sections could include more varied details and additional illustrations.  
- Weakness: some figures need improved clarity and readability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong experiments, insightful analysis, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need for richer analysis and clearer figures.  
- Overall: significant contribution to deep learning; reviewer recommends acceptance.
