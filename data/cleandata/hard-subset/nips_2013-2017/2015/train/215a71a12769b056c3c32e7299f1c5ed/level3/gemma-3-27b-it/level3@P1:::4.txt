## Review of “Training Very Deep Networks”

This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward neural networks. The core idea builds upon LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms to allow for unimpeded information flow across layers, addressing the well-known difficulties in training very deep networks due to vanishing gradients and poor propagation of activations. The authors demonstrate that these highway networks can be trained directly with stochastic gradient descent (SGD) even with hundreds of layers, a significant advancement over previous methods.

The work is situated within the broader context of recent successes achieved through deep neural networks in supervised machine learning, referencing breakthroughs in image classification (Krizhevsky et al., 2012; Szegedy et al., 2015; Simonyan & Zisserman, 2014) and other areas. The paper acknowledges the established benefits of network depth but also highlights the challenges in effectively training such deep models. It correctly positions itself alongside related work focused on improving optimization (Martens et al., 2012; Sutskever et al., 2013), initialization strategies (Glorot & Bengio, 2010; He et al., 2015), and alternative architectures like skip connections (Ciresan et al., 2012; Romero et al., 2015) and FitNets (Romero et al., 2015). The authors skillfully explain how highway networks differ from and build upon these existing approaches.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The highway network architecture represents a genuinely novel approach to addressing the training difficulties of very deep networks. While building on the concept of skip connections and inspired by LSTM gating mechanisms, the adaptive gating system provides a more flexible and dynamic means of controlling information flow.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation of the highway layer (Equation 3) is clear and concise. The authors provide a logical justification for their design choices and initialization strategies, particularly the negative bias initialization for the transform gates, drawing inspiration from LSTM training (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997).
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results are compelling. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 datasets, consistently outperforming plain networks of comparable size and demonstrating competitive results compared to state-of-the-art methods (Graham, 2014). The ablation studies (Figure 4) and analysis of gate activity (Figure 2 & 3) provide valuable insights into the inner workings of the network.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly articulates the problem, the proposed solution, and the main contributions. The notation is consistent, and the explanations are easy to follow.
* **Significance:** The ability to train significantly deeper networks opens up new possibilities for exploring the benefits of depth in various machine learning applications. This is particularly important as deeper networks often exhibit superior representational capacity and performance.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Analysis Depth:** While the analysis sections provide valuable insights, they could be expanded upon. For instance, exploring the relationship between the transform gate activity and the input data in more detail could reveal further understanding of the network’s learning process. Visualizing the learned weights of the transform gates could also be informative.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions random search for hyperparameters but doesn't provide extensive details on the sensitivity of the performance to these choices. A more thorough hyperparameter analysis would strengthen the results.
* **Limited Architectural Exploration:** The authors primarily focus on a relatively simple highway network architecture. Exploring variations in the gating mechanism or the integration of highway layers with other architectural components could further enhance the method's performance.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a significant problem in deep learning.
* The experimental results are strong and consistently demonstrate the benefits of highway networks.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly articulates its contributions.
* The work has the potential to impact a wide range of machine learning applications by enabling the training of deeper and more powerful networks.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The analysis sections could be more in-depth, providing further insights into the network’s behavior.
* While strong, the hyperparameter sensitivity analysis is limited.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway network architecture is a clever and effective solution to the challenges of training very deep networks. The authors have provided compelling empirical evidence to support their claims, and the paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.  I recommend **acceptance** with a high confidence level. The potential impact of this work is substantial, and it is likely to stimulate further research in this area.
