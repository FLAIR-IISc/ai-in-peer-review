

This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture that enables training of extremely deep feed-forward networks through input-dependent gating mechanisms inspired by LSTMs. The authors propose a layer transformation y = H(x,WH)·T(x,WT) + x·(1-T(x,WT)), where transform gates T control the flow of information between layers. Unlike standard feed-forward networks that suffer from vanishing gradients as depth increases, highway networks maintain effective information flow across hundreds of layers, enabling direct training via simple gradient descent.

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with thorough experimental validation. The authors conduct comprehensive experiments on MNIST and CIFAR datasets, showing highway networks achieve superior performance compared to plain networks and FitNets even at extreme depths (100+ layers). The analysis of gate behavior across layers (Figure 2) provides valuable insights into how information is dynamically routed based on input, with compelling evidence that the gating mechanism adapts to different inputs rather than implementing a static shortcut.

The paper excels in clarity with a well-structured presentation that makes the technical contributions accessible. The writing is precise, and the experimental details are sufficiently detailed for reproducibility. The authors appropriately acknowledge similarities to FitNets and LSTMs while clearly articulating the key differences, particularly regarding the dynamic routing of information versus fixed skip connections.

Originality is strong, as the highway network concept represents a significant departure from standard deep network architectures. The paper clearly differentiates itself from previous work while building upon relevant concepts. The significance is demonstrated through the paper's ability to train networks with unprecedented depth while maintaining generalization performance.

The only minor issue is a typo on page 5 ("Tables 3 and 3"), which should be corrected. No substantive weaknesses were identified. Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to deep learning, providing both a novel technique and thorough analysis of its behavior. The results demonstrate that highway networks can be trained directly through many layers without the need for complex initialization schemes or multi-stage training, opening new possibilities for extremely deep architectures. This work represents a meaningful advance in the field and should be accepted.
