Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes highway networks, a feed-forward architecture that introduces LSTM-inspired transform and carry gates to enable unimpeded information flow across many layers. Each layer computes y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1 − T(x)), where T is a sigmoid gate and H is any standard transform (e.g., affine+nonlinearity or convolution). By biasing T toward zero at initialization, the network behaves near identity initially, which facilitates gradient flow and optimization. The approach generalizes prior skip-connection ideas by replacing fixed shortcuts with learnable, input-dependent gates, akin to LSTM gating for temporal depth.

Relative to prior work on improved optimization (e.g., Hessian-free, careful initialization), local competition/maxout, deep supervision, and hint-based training (FitNets), the contribution is a simple architectural modification that allows direct end-to-end SGD training of very deep networks (hundreds or even >1000 layers in pilots) without special pretraining, teachers, or sophisticated optimizers. The paper empirically demonstrates robustness to depth (MNIST optimization study), competitive to state-of-the-art accuracy on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 under standard augmentation, and favorable comparisons to FitNets at comparable parameter/compute budgets. Analysis sections examine gate behavior, data-dependent routing, and layer lesioning.

Strengths
- Clear, simple, and general architectural idea that is easy to implement and plugs into fully connected and convolutional layers.
- Strong empirical evidence that optimization difficulty does not grow with depth for highway networks; shows large gaps vs plain nets at 50–100 layers.
- Competitive accuracy on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 with modest augmentation, including one-stage training of thin deep models where prior work required hints.
- Insightful analyses: visualization of gate biases/activities, class-conditional routing, and lesion studies to assess layer importance.
- Good clarity and communication; code and scripts promised, increasing reproducibility and impact.
- Conceptually unifies skip connections and LSTM-style gating, offering a general mechanism for depth-wise credit assignment.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Baselines: Results primarily compare to plain networks and FitNets; a stronger empirical case would include a “hard-wired skip” baseline (identity shortcuts without gates) matched for parameters, and batch normalization variants, to isolate the specific benefit of gating vs simple residual connections or normalization.
- Sensitivity/ablations: While gate biasing is motivated and explored qualitatively, a more systematic study (test error vs initial bT, effect of learning rate schedules, different activation functions) would strengthen claims about robustness.
- Scope of datasets: The main results are on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 with limited augmentation. Including at least one larger-scale or more diverse dataset (or stronger augmentation settings) would better support claims about very deep architectures in practice.
- Overhead and efficiency: The parameter and compute cost added by the gates is only implicitly discussed. Reporting FLOPs/parameter counts and inference-time overhead would help practitioners.
- Carry gate tying: The paper fixes C = 1 − T. An ablation where C is learned independently could clarify whether the coupling is necessary or whether additional flexibility helps/hurts.
- Analysis breadth: The analysis is informative but could include statistics over multiple random seeds (for gate sparsity patterns and lesion sensitivity) and quantitative measures of routing diversity across classes.

Quality
The technical contribution is sound and well-motivated. The formulation is straightforward, gradients are standard, and the initialization strategy is consistent with LSTM gating practice. Empirical support is compelling for the optimization claims and adequate for generalization under the tested settings. Some missing baselines (residual/skip without gates, batch norm) and broader datasets temper the strength of the generalization claims but do not undermine the core contribution.

Clarity
The paper is clearly written and well-organized. The derivation and intuition for the gating mechanism are easy to follow. Figures illustrating training curves, gate activity, and lesioning are informative. Minor suggestions: add a concise architectural diagram of a highway block; include a table summarizing parameter/FLOP counts for all models; detail the exact architectures in an appendix (filter sizes, strides, padding, and where dimensionality changes occur).

Originality
The work is original in bringing LSTM-style gating to depth-wise information flow in feed-forward nets. While skip connections and hint-based training are established, the move to adaptive, input-dependent gates that generalize skip connections is novel and well-differentiated.

Significance
High. Enabling direct training of very deep networks is important both scientifically and practically. The method is simple, general, and likely to be adopted. The analyses (especially lesioning and routing) provide new insights into depth utilization.

Questions for the authors
- How do highway networks compare to an identity-skip (residual) baseline with matched parameters and training protocol?
- What is the impact of batch normalization within H and/or on gate inputs? Does BN reduce the need for strong negative bT?
- How sensitive is performance to the initial transform gate bias across datasets and depths? Can you provide a small ablation?
- Have you tried learning C independently from T? Any stability or performance differences?
- What is the computational overhead (parameters, FLOPs, inference latency) introduced by gates relative to plain networks?

Pros for acceptance
- Simple, general architectural idea with strong empirical validation.
- Clear demonstration that very deep nets can be trained via standard SGD.
- Competitive accuracy without complex training pipelines.
- Insightful analyses illuminating how depth is utilized.

Cons for acceptance
- Missing comparisons to identity-skip baselines and batch normalization.
- Limited dataset breadth and augmentation settings.
- Incomplete ablations on gate design and initialization.

Overall recommendation
Accept. The paper presents a compelling, novel architectural mechanism that materially improves the trainability of very deep networks, with solid experiments and insightful analyses. With a few additional baselines and ablations (which could be addressed in a camera-ready), this work is likely to have significant impact.

Reviewer confidence
High.
