This paper proposes a novel architecture for training very deep neural networks, dubbed "highway networks." The key idea is to use adaptive gating units to regulate the information flow across layers, inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks. This approach allows for unimpeded information flow across many layers, enabling the training of extremely deep networks using simple gradient descent.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their proposed architecture. The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating that highway networks can be trained up to 100 layers without significant degradation in performance. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the inner workings of their network, including the routing of information and the importance of individual layers.

Strengths of the paper include:

* Originality: The paper proposes a novel architecture for training very deep neural networks, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* Technical soundness: The authors provide a thorough analysis of their proposed architecture, including a detailed description of the adaptive gating units and the training procedure.
* Experimental results: The authors provide impressive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach on several benchmark datasets.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* Limited comparison to other methods: While the authors compare their approach to plain networks, they do not provide a comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art methods for training deep neural networks.
* Lack of theoretical analysis: While the authors provide a detailed analysis of their proposed architecture, they do not provide a theoretical analysis of the limitations and potential pitfalls of their approach.

Suggestions for improvement:

* Provide a more comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art methods for training deep neural networks.
* Consider providing a theoretical analysis of the limitations and potential pitfalls of the proposed approach.
* Consider exploring the application of highway networks to other domains, such as natural language processing or speech recognition.

Overall, this is a great paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The authors' proposed architecture has the potential to enable the training of extremely deep neural networks, which could lead to significant advances in a variety of applications.
