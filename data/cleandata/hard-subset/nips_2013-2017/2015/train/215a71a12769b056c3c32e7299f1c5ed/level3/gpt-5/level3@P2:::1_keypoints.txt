- Proposal: Highway Networks add input-dependent gates (y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x))) with negative bias init to favor identity maps and enable very deep feed-forward training.
- Strength: Clear, LSTM-inspired gating idea with simple implementation and strong intuition.
- Strength: Convincing optimization evidence that 50–100 layer networks train faster and better than plain nets of similar capacity.
- Strength: Competitive MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 results for thin, deep architectures without multi-stage distillation.
- Strength: Novel, careful analyses (gate visualizations, class-conditional routing, lesion studies) that reveal depth utilization.
- Strength: Practical guidance on negative bias initialization and public code release.
- Weakness: Jacobian/backprop statements are imprecise; need exact Jacobian and empirical gate saturation statistics to justify simplifications.
- Weakness: Initialization sensitivity not quantified; request performance vs initial bT (and depth) plots and discussion of bT selection/regularization effects.
- Weakness: Comparative claims need fairness checks—report matching training protocols, augmentation, parameter/FLOP budgets, and optimizer schedules.
- Weakness: Inconsistent reporting of results—request mean ± std over multiple seeds and number of runs for all methods, including baselines.
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical/learning-theoretic analysis on when gated layers prevent vanishing/exploding gradients and benefit over fixed skips.
- Quality: Architecture and training are sound, analyses strong, but require precise Jacobian treatment and more rigorous ablations (e.g., learn C vs C=1−T, gate parameterizations).
- Empirical asks: Add fairness-controlled CIFAR comparisons, gradient norm vs depth plots (plain vs highway), and training-curve variability across seeds.
- Clarity: Generally well written; fix minor typos, ensure consistent notation, and add pseudo-code for forward/backward passes with gradients.
- Originality: Novel combination of LSTM-style input-dependent gating in feed-forward nets and original diagnostic analyses.
- Significance: Simple, general method enabling training of very deep networks with SGD; analyses likely to inspire follow-up work.
- Questions: Provide exact backprop equations including ∂T/∂x and gate saturation observations.
- Questions: Report sensitivity to bT, weight-init scale, and depth with corresponding plots (performance and gradient norms).
- Questions: Quantify parameter and FLOP overhead for common conv settings and trade-offs with depth/width.
- Questions: Have you tried learning C independently and training at 200–1000 layers on CIFAR-100—what are the outcomes?
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, effective, well-supported empirical evidence, insightful analyses, and released code.
- Cons for acceptance: Incomplete reporting and fairness details, missing precise gradient/Jacobian treatment and theoretical guarantees, limited ablations.
- Minor requests: Unify experimental reporting (seeds, epochs, augmentation, compute), include per-layer gate activity histograms/saturation metrics, and show CIFAR results with/without global average pooling.
- Overall: Strong submission with impactful idea and analyses; addressing Jacobian precision, reporting consistency, and initialization sensitivity would substantially strengthen the paper—reviewer leans toward acceptance.
