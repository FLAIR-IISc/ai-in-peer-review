- Proposes a novel on-the-job learning framework to learn from noisy crowds while maintaining accuracy and reducing cost over time.
- Models the on-the-job setting as a stochastic game and applies game-playing ideas to approximate the optimal policy.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a thorough analysis of the approach.
- Provides detailed description of the stochastic game model, utility function, and approximation algorithm.
- Includes comprehensive experiments on three tasks demonstrating cost reduction and accuracy improvements.
- Offers a clear, concise discussion of related work situating the approach within crowdsourcing and ML.
- Assumes noisy crowd workers but lacks a detailed analysis of the noise model and its impact.
- Does not clearly discuss limitations of the approach or avenues for future work.
- Weaknesses are minor and do not outweigh the paper’s contributions.
- Overall assessment: high-quality work with potential significant impact; recommended for NIPS acceptance.
