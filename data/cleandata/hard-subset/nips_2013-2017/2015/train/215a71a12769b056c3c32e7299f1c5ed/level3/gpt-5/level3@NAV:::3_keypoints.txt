- Proposes Highway Networks: feedforward nets with LSTM-style transform and carry gates to enable unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Initializes transform gates with negative bias so layers default to carrying inputs, mitigating vanishing/exploding gradients and easing plain SGD optimization.  
- Demonstrates successful training up to 100 layers on MNIST and CIFAR with competitive generalization and analyses of information routing and layer importance.  
- Positions work relative to prior efforts (variance-preserving init, improved optimizers, maxout, skip connections, deep supervision, distillation) and claims novelty in data-dependent, learnable gating versus fixed shortcuts.  
- Technically sound with controlled optimization studies across depths and careful analyses (gate activity, class-conditional routing, layer lesioning).  
- Suggests missing direct comparison to plain networks with identity-initialized layers plus small noise (near-identity residual mapping) to separate benefits of adaptive gates from fixed shortcuts.  
- Recommends ablations: test C = 1 − T vs learning C independently and report gradient norms across depth to strengthen claims.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and available code; correction needed in Section 4.1 wording about transform block activity.  
- High originality: novel and well-motivated adaptation of LSTM-style gating to very deep feedforward networks.  
- Strong significance: provides a practical recipe (gate biasing) enabling direct SGD training of very deep nets and insightful analyses likely to influence future architectures.  
- Pros: original architecture enabling 100+ layers, strong empirical evidence and analyses, clear writing and open code.  
- Cons: lacks head-to-head comparison with identity-initialized/residual-like baselines and has limited ablations on gate design and gradient diagnostics.  
- Overall recommendation: excellent paper; recommend acceptance.
