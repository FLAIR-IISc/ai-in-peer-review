- Proposes highway networks: adaptive gating per layer that blends transformed input and identity to enable very deep feedforward training.  
- Gating mechanism inspired by LSTM allows information to flow through “information highways” without attenuation.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results: 100-layer highway nets vastly outperform plain nets on MNIST and scale well with depth.  
- On CIFAR-10, highway nets match or beat Fitnets while being trained in a single stage (no two-stage training).  
- Analysis of gate activations (Fig. 2) shows input-dependent information routing across layers.  
- Layer-importance analysis (Fig. 4) indicates effective utilization of many layers for complex tasks like CIFAR-100.  
- Clear description and strong connection to prior work on skip connections and LSTM; architecture is well-motivated.  
- Main technical concern: Equation 5’s Jacobian claim is incorrect because sigmoid gates never output exact 0/1; backprop implementation must be clarified.  
- Initialization concern: using constant negative biases for transform gates is ad hoc; need plot of performance vs. initial bias and a more principled scheme.  
- Comparative claims need clarification: explicitly state whether experimental settings match prior work (e.g., Fitnets) or explain differences.  
- Reporting practices need improvement: report mean and standard deviation across random seeds instead of only maximum accuracies.  
- Suggestion: provide a learning-theory analysis of gate units to explain how gating yields conditional identities and selective propagation.  
- Overall assessment: significant, original, and well-supported contribution enabling direct training of extremely deep networks.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing gradient clarification, initialization characterization, experimental comparability, and reporting variability.
