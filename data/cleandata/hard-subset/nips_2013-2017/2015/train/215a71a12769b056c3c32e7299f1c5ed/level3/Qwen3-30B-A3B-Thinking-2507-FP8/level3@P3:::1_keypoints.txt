- Introduces highway networks with adaptive gating to enable direct training of extremely deep feedforward networks, building on LSTM, normalized initialization, and skip connections and predating ResNet.  
- Strong theoretical motivation and convincing experiments showing simple gradient descent trains highway networks with hundreds of layers while plain nets fail.  
- Analysis of gate outputs shows gates learn selective behavior rather than simply shutting down layers.  
- Comprehensive empirical comparisons on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 show consistent improvements for deeper highway architectures.  
- Technical error: claim that the Jacobian equals identity when T=0 is invalid since T=σ(·)∈(0,1); authors must clarify how backprop is implemented for gate units.  
- Initialization concern: using constant negative biases for transform gates is not well-justified; bias acts as a hyperparameter and requires evaluation (performance vs initial bias).  
- Comparative claims vs FitNets are unclear — need explicit statement of whether experimental settings, preprocessing, augmentation, and hyperparameters were matched.  
- Reporting practice is inconsistent: paper sometimes reports only maximum accuracies instead of mean ± std over multiple runs as claimed.  
- Suggestion: provide a learning-theoretic analysis of gate units to formalize how layers act as conditional identities and allow selective information flow.  
- Suggestion: explicitly document all experimental differences between highway networks and comparative methods, including training procedures and hyperparameters.  
- Suggestion: fix and explain the gradient/backpropagation implementation for sigmoidal gate units to resolve the mathematical inconsistency.  
- Suggestion: add performance vs initial bias plots to justify bias initialization choices.  
- Overall assessment: significant and influential contribution demonstrating direct training of very deep nets with insightful gate analysis, but marred by technical and reporting issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions contingent on addressing gradient computation, bias analysis, and consistent statistical reporting.
