- Proposal: Highway Networks use transform and carry gates (T and C=1−T) to enable input-dependent routing and near-identity paths in very deep feedforward nets, inspired by LSTM gating.
- Empirical results: Stable training of tens-to-hundreds-layer networks on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 with competitive accuracy and analyses of gate activity, routing, and lesioning.
- Relation to prior work: Related to LSTM gating, skip/shortcut connections, deep supervision, normalized initialization, maxout, and FitNets, but novel in learned multiplicative gates implementing conditional identities.
- Strength: Idea is clearly described, simple to implement, and well-motivated by LSTM principles.
- Strength: Experiments convincingly show optimization stability with depth; gate-output and lesioning analyses are especially insightful.
- Strength: Original contribution of adaptive gating to deep feedforward stacks with potential impact on training very deep models without multi-stage distillation.
- Strength: Paper is generally well organized and readable.
- Weakness: Technical correctness — sigmoid T never exactly 0/1; Eq.5 is misleading; reviewer requests the exact Jacobian used in backprop including T′(x) and clarification of gradient flow (per-dimension vs shared gates).
- Weakness: Initialization — constant negative transform-gate biases by depth may act as a regularizer; request plots of performance vs initial bias and depth and tests of uncoupling C=1−T versus independent carry gates.
- Weakness: Comparative claims — clarify whether baselines (e.g., FitNets, maxout) used identical data augmentation, parameter/compute budgets, and optimization; otherwise detail differences and avoid direct numerical comparisons.
- Weakness: Reporting practice — report mean ± std over multiple seeds and number of runs for all experiments; for prior-work numbers, indicate source and settings.
- Weakness: Broader analysis — suggest adding a learning-theory perspective on gates as conditional identities enabling selective deep paths (e.g., path-wise capacity/regularization).
- Summary judgment: Pros — clear, simple, effective with strong analyses and likely influence; Cons — gradient derivation issue, incomplete initialization ablations, and comparability/reporting weaknesses.
- Overall recommendation: Lean toward acceptance pending fixes to technical derivations and tightened experimental reporting.
