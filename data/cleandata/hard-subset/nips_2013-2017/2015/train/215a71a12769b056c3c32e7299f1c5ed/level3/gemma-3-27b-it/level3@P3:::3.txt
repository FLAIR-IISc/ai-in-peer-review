This paper proposes a novel architecture, Highway Networks, designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward networks – demonstrating successful training up to 100 layers. The core idea is to incorporate LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms that allow for unimpeded information flow across layers, addressing the vanishing/exploding gradient problems that typically plague deep networks. This is a high-quality, well-written paper presenting an original idea with compelling experimental results.

The work builds upon a long line of research addressing the challenges of training deep networks. Early work focused on better optimization algorithms (e.g., [13, 14, 15]) and initialization strategies ([16, 17, 18, 19]). The authors rightly point out the historical issues with gradient propagation in deep networks, referencing related work on skip connections ([22, 23, 2, 24]), knowledge distillation ([25, 26]), and layer-wise training ([26, 27]). The inspiration drawn from LSTM networks ([29, 30]) is clearly articulated and well-motivated, acknowledging prior LSTMinspired models [32]. The paper effectively positions itself within the existing landscape of deep learning research.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The Highway Network architecture offers a fresh approach to tackling the depth problem, going beyond simple initialization or optimization tricks. The introduction of transform and carry gates is a creative solution inspired by recurrent networks.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation of the highway layer (Equation 3) is clear and concise. The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of the underlying principles.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 provide solid evidence of the effectiveness of Highway Networks. The comparison to FitNets and state-of-the-art methods is informative. The ablation study with layer lesioning (Section 4.2) is particularly insightful.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The notation is consistent, and the explanations are clear. The visualizations (Figures 2 and 4) effectively illustrate the inner workings of the network.
* **Significance:** The ability to train significantly deeper networks than previously possible opens up new avenues for exploring the benefits of depth in various applications. The analysis of information routing and layer importance provides valuable insights into the behavior of deep neural networks.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Discussion of Computational Cost:** While the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of Highway Networks, it does not delve deeply into the computational overhead introduced by the gating mechanism. A more detailed analysis of the trade-off between depth and computational cost would be beneficial.
* **Potential Sensitivity to Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper mentions the importance of initializing the transform gate bias but could benefit from a more systematic exploration of the sensitivity of the results to this hyperparameter.
* **Correction Needed:** Section 4.1 incorrectly states "most transform blocks are active on average." This should read "not active on average," reflecting the observed sparsity in the transform gate activity.



**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Comparison to Identity-Initialized Layers:** It would be valuable to compare the performance of Highway Networks to that of similarly deep networks initialized with identity matrices (with added noise). This would help to isolate the specific benefits of the transform gates versus a simple mechanism for preserving information flow.  Specifically, how does the adaptive gating mechanism *differ* from simply allowing gradients to flow unimpeded through identity mappings? Does the learned gating offer any advantage over this simple approach?
* **Further Analysis of Gating Mechanism:** A more in-depth analysis of the gating mechanism would be desirable. For example, exploring the correlation between gate activity and feature importance could provide further insights into how the network learns to route information.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* This paper presents a genuinely novel and well-motivated approach to tackling a fundamental challenge in deep learning – training very deep networks.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of Highway Networks across multiple datasets.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.
* The analysis of information routing and layer importance provides valuable insights into the behavior of deep neural networks.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* While the paper addresses a significant problem, the computational overhead of the gating mechanism is not fully explored.
* There's a minor inaccuracy in Section 4.1 that needs correction.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a great paper and a pleasure to read. The Highway Network architecture is a significant contribution to the field of deep learning, and the authors have presented their work in a clear and convincing manner. The minor weaknesses identified are easily addressable. I strongly recommend acceptance.
