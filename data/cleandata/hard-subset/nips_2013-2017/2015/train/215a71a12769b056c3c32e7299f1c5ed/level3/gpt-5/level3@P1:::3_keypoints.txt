- Proposes Highway Networks: feedforward layers with LSTM-style transform and carry gates to enable unimpeded information and gradient flow.
- Layer computation: y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)), where T is a transform gate and 1−T is the carry gate.
- Negative initialization of gate biases biases layers to carry inputs early, creating identity-like "information highways".
- Enables training of very deep networks (50–100+ layers, pilot >1000) with plain SGD and no special optimization tricks.
- Experiments on fully connected and convolutional models (MNIST, CIFAR-10/100) show depth-robust optimization and competitive generalization.
- Optimization study on MNIST is careful (matched parameter counts, random hyperparameter search, depth sweeps) and shows faster convergence and lower loss for deep highway nets vs plain nets.
- Analyses (gate activity, data-dependent routing, layer lesioning) support the mechanism: early layers transform more, many mid layers act as carries on simple tasks, harder tasks use more depth.
- Related to skip connections, deep supervision, FitNets, and normalized initialization but novel in providing data-dependent multiplicative gating akin to LSTM.
- Technical formulation is sound and well-motivated by gradient/Jacobian considerations.
- Originality: novel and timely idea to bring LSTM-style gating to feedforward depth control.
- Significance: advances practical training of very deep networks and offers tools to analyze depth utilization.
- Weakness: lacks stronger baselines such as identity-initialized or residual-style skips with learned scalars.
- Weakness: missing ablations isolating input-dependent gating benefits vs fixed/learned static skips.
- Weakness: runtime and parameter overhead of gating are not quantified.
- Weakness: some results report only "best run" without full variance reporting.
- Clarity: paper is well written and reproducible, but needs a concise hyperparameter/preprocessing table and a wording correction in Section 4.1.
- Suggestions: compare to identity-initialized and residual baselines, ablate gating (freeze T, independent carry, lower-dim T), measure compute/parameter overhead, report variance, and study sensitivity to gate bias initialization.
- Recommendation: high-quality, original, and significant work; overall positive recommendation for acceptance.
