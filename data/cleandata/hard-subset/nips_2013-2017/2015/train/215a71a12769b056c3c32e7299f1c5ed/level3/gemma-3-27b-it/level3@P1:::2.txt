This paper introduces highway networks, a novel neural network architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep networks. The authors address the well-known challenges of vanishing gradients and optimization difficulties that arise with increasing depth in standard feedforward networks. Inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, highway networks incorporate adaptive gating mechanisms that allow information to flow across many layers with minimal attenuation. The core idea is to introduce "information highways" that allow layers to selectively bypass transformations, effectively acting as identity mappings when appropriate. 

The work builds on a substantial body of previous research in deep learning, including efforts to improve optimization (e.g., [13, 14, 15]), initialization strategies ([16, 17, 18, 19]), and activation functions ([20, 21]). It also acknowledges and relates to existing techniques that attempt to improve information flow, such as skip connections ([22, 23, 24]) and knowledge distillation ([25, 26]).  Crucially, the authors position their work as a simpler and more direct approach than some of these alternatives, particularly those requiring multi-stage training procedures. The paper also references related work on recurrent networks [28, 29, 30] which served as inspiration for the gating mechanisms.

**Strengths:**

The paper’s strengths are numerous. Technically, the proposed highway network architecture is sound and well-motivated. The mathematical formulation (Equation 3) is clear and concise. The authors provide strong empirical evidence, demonstrating that highway networks can be trained effectively with hundreds of layers using standard stochastic gradient descent – a feat difficult to achieve with plain networks.  The experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 are well-designed and demonstrate generalization ability. The ablation study with layer importance evaluation (Figure 4) provides valuable insights into the behavior of the trained networks. The clear explanation of the architecture’s connection to LSTMs, while highlighting key differences, contextualizes the contribution effectively.

The clarity of the paper is exceptional. The writing is precise and the organization logical. The authors present a thorough and detailed analysis of their results, including visualizations (Figures 1, 2, 3, and 4) that aid in understanding the inner workings of the network. The inclusion of hyperparameter settings and links to publicly available code further enhances reproducibility and allows for future research.

The originality of the approach is significant. While inspired by LSTMs, adapting the gating mechanism to feedforward networks in this way represents a novel contribution. The exploration of information routing within the network and the analysis of layer importance are also original aspects of the work.

The significance of this work is considerable.  Overcoming the difficulties of training very deep networks has the potential to unlock even greater performance in various machine learning tasks.  The demonstrated ability to train such networks directly with SGD, without resorting to more complex training procedures, is a substantial advancement.  The analysis of gate behavior and layer importance provides valuable insights into the dynamics of deep learning.

**Weaknesses:**

This is a strong paper and I struggled to identify significant weaknesses. The authors are careful to evaluate the strengths and limitations of their work. There's a minor editorial issue on page 5 ("Tables 3 and 3") but this does not affect the technical content.  

**Arguments for and against acceptance:**

**For acceptance:**

*   Novel and well-motivated architecture for training very deep networks.
*   Strong empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of highway networks.
*   Clear and well-written presentation with thorough analysis.
*   Addresses a significant challenge in deep learning.
*   Potential for significant impact on future research.

**Against acceptance:**

*   No major weaknesses identified. The work is of high quality and represents a valuable contribution to the field.



**Overall:**

This is an excellent paper that presents a novel and effective approach to training very deep neural networks. The architecture is well-motivated, the experiments are thorough, and the analysis is insightful. The paper is clearly written and makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. I strongly recommend acceptance to NIPS 2015.
