- Proposes "highway networks" with adaptive gating inspired by LSTMs to enable training of very deep feedforward networks (up to 100 layers).
- Core idea: create unimpeded "information highways" to mitigate vanishing/exploding gradients and optimization difficulties.
- Novel contribution that advances depth in architectures beyond prior work (Krizhevsky et al., Szegedy et al., Simonyan & Zisserman).
- Demonstrates technical soundness with strong experimental validation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.
- Shows clear benefits of highway networks over plain networks, especially as depth increases.
- Analysis includes insightful visualizations of gate activity and layer importance supporting dynamic routing claims.
- Writing and organization are clear and effective.
- Suggested improvement: add a direct comparison to identity-initialized layers with small noise to clarify the transform gates' unique contribution.
- Minor correction needed in Section 4.1 wording to "not active on average."
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, significance, technical soundness, and clarity.
- Arguments against acceptance: only minor issues (lack of identity-initialized baseline comparison and a typo).
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — significant, original, and high-quality contribution.
