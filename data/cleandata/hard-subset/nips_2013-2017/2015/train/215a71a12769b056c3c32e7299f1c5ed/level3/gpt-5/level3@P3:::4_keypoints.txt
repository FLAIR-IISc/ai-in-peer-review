- Introduces Highway Networks that use elementwise transform and carry gates: y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)), with LSTM-inspired negative bias init to enable very deep nets trained with vanilla SGD.  
- Relates to skip connections, FitNets (teacher–student), improved initializations/activations, and LSTM gating; novelty is input-dependent, per-unit/per-example gating versus fixed skips.  
- Clear, principled design: T/C gating interpolates between identity and non-linear transform with intuitive initialization.  
- Strong empirical evidence: very deep highway nets converge faster and match/surpass comparable-capacity baselines (MNIST, CIFAR-10/100) without distillation.  
- Careful optimization study: MNIST depth sweep shows plain nets degrade with depth while highway nets remain stable up to 100 layers under matched settings.  
- Insightful analyses: gate activity visualizations, class-conditional routing, and lesion experiments show data-dependent gating and adaptive use of depth.  
- Significant impact: enables direct training of much deeper networks with simple SGD and is broadly applicable.  
- Limited ablations vs. alternative cross-layer schemes (e.g., fixed identity shortcuts, deep supervision) to isolate benefits of adaptive gating.  
- Incomplete exploration of design choices: fixed C = 1−T only, no study of separate/tied/shared gates or full sensitivity to transform-gate bias.  
- Limited dataset scope: no large-scale results (e.g., ImageNet) to assess scalability and efficiency.  
- Computational overhead not systematically quantified: extra params, FLOPs, runtime, and memory for T/C are not reported.  
- Technically sound and well executed; claims supported and reproducibility aided by public code and fixed hyperparameters.  
- Clearly written with practical gate-bias guidance; could add more diagnostics (e.g., gradient norms, gate saturation histograms).  
- Original contribution: high — LSTM-style input-dependent gating in feedforward depth is a substantive, novel idea.  
- High significance: advances training of very deep nets without multi-stage procedures or specialized architectures.  
- Suggested ablations: fixed identity shortcuts, separate carry gate C, shared vs. unshared gates, and removing gates after training.  
- Suggested measurements: quantify parameters, FLOPs, runtime, and memory relative to plain nets for matched accuracy.  
- Suggested diagnostics: show gradient propagation across depth and distributions of gate saturation/per-layer histograms.  
- Suggested broader analyses: more qualitative visualizations for convolutional layers and discussion of interaction with normalization layers.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of performance to initial transform-gate bias and principled setting of bT?  
- Questions for authors: did you test separate carry gates (C ≠ 1−T) and what were the effects?  
- Questions for authors: for convolutional gates, are T parameters shared spatially or per-location, and what's the cost/capacity effect?  
- Questions for authors: can you report precise compute and memory overheads for the CIFAR models?  
- Questions for authors: how far can depth be pushed in practice (e.g., 200–1000 layers) before diminishing returns or optimization issues?  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept — novel, well-motivated, empirically supported, and significant contribution with high confidence.
