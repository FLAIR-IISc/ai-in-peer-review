- Proposes highway networks using LSTM-inspired adaptive gates to create “information highways” for training very deep nets.
- Builds on prior work (optimizers, initialization, activations, skip connections) and explicitly relates to LSTM motivation.
- Novelty: adaptive transform/carry gates provide a more flexible mechanism than existing skip connections.
- Technical soundness: clear mathematical formulation (Eq. 3) and explanation of gate functions.
- Experiments: well-designed results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 showing improvements over plain nets and FitNets.
- Ablation study (layer lesioning) offers useful insight into network behavior.
- Clarity and communication: paper is exceptionally well-written and supported by helpful figures.
- Significance: enables direct SGD training of very deep networks, making deeper models more practical and expressive.
- Limited analysis depth: needs more comprehensive quantitative/visual analysis of learned gate parameters and behaviors.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: insufficient detail about hyperparameter search and the impact of initial gate bias.
- Computational cost: lacks analysis of additional parameters and runtime overhead introduced by gates.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical evidence, excellent writing, and high potential impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: analysis is not deep enough and computational overhead is underexplored.
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to expand analysis and discuss computational cost.
