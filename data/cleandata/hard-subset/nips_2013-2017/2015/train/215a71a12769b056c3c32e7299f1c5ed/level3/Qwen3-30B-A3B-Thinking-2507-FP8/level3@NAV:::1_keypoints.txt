- Introduces highway networks: novel architecture using adaptive gating (LSTM-inspired) to enable training of extremely deep nets.
- Provides theoretical motivation that highway networks maintain information flow across many layers.
- Shows empirical evidence that highway networks can be trained directly with SGD for very deep architectures (100+ layers), unlike plain networks.
- Builds on prior work (deep nets, skip connections, LSTMs) but offers a novel approach to information flow.
- Strength: clear description of the highway architecture.
- Strength: convincing experimental results showing superior performance of highway vs. plain networks at extreme depths.
- Strength: strong analysis of learned gate-output patterns and lesioning experiments demonstrating layer routing and multi-layer usage.
- Criticism: gate T uses sigmoid (never exactly 0/1) yet Equation 5 discusses gradients for T=0 or T=1 — clarify practical backprop implementation.
- Criticism: constant negative bias initialization (e.g., -1, -3) is unsatisfying — provide performance vs. initial bias plot since bias acts as a regularizer.
- Criticism: comparative claims need clarification — explicitly state whether experimental settings match recent studies for fair comparison.
- Criticism: only maximum accuracies reported — provide mean and standard deviation for all methods to assess statistical significance.
- Suggestion: include a learning-theory analysis of the gate units to theoretically ground how gates enable layers to act as conditional identities.
- Overall verdict: strong contribution with high impact; suitable for NIPS pending the suggested revisions.
