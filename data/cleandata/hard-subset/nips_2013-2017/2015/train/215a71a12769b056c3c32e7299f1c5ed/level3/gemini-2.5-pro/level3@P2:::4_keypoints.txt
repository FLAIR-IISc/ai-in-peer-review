- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-inspired gated feedforward layers that enable training of very deep networks by adaptively routing information.
- Addresses the core problem of poor information and gradient propagation in very deep plain networks.
- Gates at each layer dynamically interpolate between transforming inputs and carrying them forward, creating “information highways.”
- Generalizes skip connections by making pathways learned and data-dependent rather than fixed shortcuts.
- Significance: enables training of networks with hundreds or thousands of layers via standard SGD, opening new architectural exploration.
- Originality: novel and elegant application of LSTM-like gating to feedforward depth control, more flexible than prior skip connections.
- Quality (experiments): MNIST optimization shows highway networks avoid degradation with depth.
- Quality (experiments): CIFAR-10/100 results demonstrate strong generalization; deep thin highway nets outperform wider, shallow FitNets in one stage.
- Analysis: Section 4 provides useful gate-activity visualizations and a lesion study that indicate data-dependent routing and layer utility.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, clearly motivated, with simple notation and effective experimental presentation.
- Weakness: analysis could be deeper—need aggregated gate statistics across samples, lesioning of layer pairs/groups, and tracking gate evolution during training.
- Acceptance case: novel, significant, practical contribution with strong empirical support and high potential impact.
- Minor con: analysis brevity is improvable but not a fundamental flaw.
- Overall: excellent, clear, and impactful paper that convincingly addresses a major obstacle in deep learning.
