- Proposes highway networks: a novel architecture using LSTM-inspired adaptive gating to allow unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Enables training of extremely deep networks with simple stochastic gradient descent by alleviating optimization difficulties.  
- Thorough analysis of the problems in training deep networks and how the proposed architecture addresses them.  
- Experiments show dynamic information routing and that the network utilizes many layers adaptively (information routing and layer importance).  
- Empirical results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 demonstrate highway networks train directly with SGD while plain deep nets become hard to optimize.  
- Analysis of transform gates and block outputs indicates gating contributes to both easing training and core computation.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproduction by expert readers.  
- Provides a comprehensive review of prior deep-learning work and argues for the importance of depth theoretically and empirically.  
- Strengths: original idea, compelling experiments, technical soundness, and high scores for quality/clarity/originality/significance (9/10).  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to approaches using identity-initialized layers with noise—needs discussion and experiments.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of computational (time and space) complexity and comparison to alternative methods.  
- Overall: strong paper with significant contributions and good support for acceptance.
