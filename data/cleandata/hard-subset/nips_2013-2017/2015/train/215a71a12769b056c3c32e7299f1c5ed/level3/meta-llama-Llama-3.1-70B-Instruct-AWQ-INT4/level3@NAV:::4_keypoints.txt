- Proposes "highway networks": novel architecture with direct cross-layer connections and adaptive gating inspired by LSTM to train very deep nets.  
- Provides theoretical foundations, experimental evaluation, and comparisons to existing methods.  
- Shows highway networks can be trained with SGD and achieve strong results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Novel and well-motivated design building on LSTM and cross-layer connectivity.  
- Experimental evaluation is solid, with thorough comparisons and effective demonstrations.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation that is easy to follow.  
- Analysis sections need more varied details and additional illustrations to better support claims.  
- Lacks an in-depth discussion of limitations and potential future work directions.  
- Represents a significant contribution with potential to enable training of much deeper networks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong experimental support, and potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis detail and limited discussion of limitations/future work.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the analysis and limitations discussion.
