- Problem addressed: training very deep feed-forward networks; propose "highway networks" to enable stable SGD training for hundreds of layers.  
- Core idea: each layer uses transform and carry gates (inspired by LSTM) to balance transformed output vs. carried input, creating data-dependent information highways.  
- Advantage over prior work: offers flexible, learned routing vs. fixed skip-connections or special initializations.  
- MNIST results: plain networks degrade past ~20 layers, highway nets train effectively to 100+ layers and converge faster/better.  
- CIFAR-10 (thin nets): highway nets train in a single stage and match or outperform FitNets that require two-stage training with hints.  
- CIFAR-10/100 (conv nets): very deep convolutional highway networks achieve strong practical performance on standard benchmarks.  
- Analysis/interpretability: gate-activity visualizations and lesioning show data-dependent routing — many layers used for hard tasks, many dormant for easy tasks.  
- Quality: paper is very high quality, technically sound, with comprehensive and well-designed experiments.  
- Clarity: exceptionally clear writing, intuitive formulation, logical organization, and reproducible code/details.  
- Originality: highly original application of adaptive gating to feed-forward depth control, well-situated among related methods.  
- Significance: substantial — enables training of extremely deep networks with standard SGD and likely influences future architectures.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, simple, effective architecture; strong empirical support; excellent internal analysis; high relevance to the community.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reviewer reports no significant criticisms.  
- Minor comment: small typo / incorrect table reference in "Comparison to State-of-the-art Methods" (page 5, right column).
