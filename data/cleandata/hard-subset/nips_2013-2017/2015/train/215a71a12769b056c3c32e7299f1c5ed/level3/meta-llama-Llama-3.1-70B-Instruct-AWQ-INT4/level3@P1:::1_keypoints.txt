- Introduces highway networks, a novel architecture enabling direct SGD training of very deep networks and achieving competitive performance.  
- Clearly motivates the importance of depth and reviews prior work and training challenges for deep networks.  
- Architecture is inspired by LSTM and uses adaptive gating units to regulate layer-wise information flow, with clear mathematical formulation.  
- Experiments convincingly show highway networks avoid degradation with increasing depth and perform well on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Optimization comparisons to plain networks with normalized initialization support the effectiveness of highway networks.  
- Strong analysis of gate-output patterns shows transform gate biases decrease during training and gates dynamically route information per input.  
- Concern: Using a sigmoid for gate T means outputs are not exactly 0/1, so gradients in Equation 5 are incorrect and backpropagation implementation must be clarified.  
- Concern: Initialization of gate biases with depth using constant negative values is unsatisfying; authors should plot performance vs initial bias.  
- Concern: Comparative claims need clarification—state whether recent studies used identical experimental settings for direct comparison.  
- Request: Report mean and standard deviation for every method instead of only max accuracies.  
- Suggestion: Include a learning-theory analysis of gate units to explain how conditional identities enable deep information propagation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, convincing experiments, and insightful gate analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear backpropagation/gradient derivation, unsatisfactory bias initialization choice, and unclear comparative experimental details.  
- Overall: Well-written and well-described idea with fairly convincing experiments, but several methodological clarifications and additional analyses are needed.
