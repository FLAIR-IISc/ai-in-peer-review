- Introduces "highway networks": feedforward layers with LSTM-inspired transform and carry gates to train extremely deep nets with simple SGD.
- Gates let the network carry inputs unchanged or apply non-linear transforms, creating information highways that mitigate vanishing gradients.
- Empirical results show highway networks with hundreds of layers train successfully on MNIST and CIFAR, outperforming plain deep networks.
- Detailed analysis visualizes gate biases/activities, demonstrates data-dependent routing, and uses layer lesioning to assess layer importance.

Strengths:
- Novel and significant contribution: adapts LSTM-style gating to feedforward nets, enabling practical exploration of much greater depth.
- Clear motivation and presentation: idea is well-explained and compactly formulated (Eq. 3).
- Convincing experimental validation: optimization experiments and CIFAR results support claims of easier training and competitive generalization.
- Strong, insightful analysis: gate visualizations, data-dependent routing, and lesioning studies provide meaningful internal understanding.

Weaknesses / Questions:
- Technical clarity on backpropagation: Eq. (5) presents an idealized Jacobian; request precise gradient/backprop implementation details.
- Initialization sensitivity: using a manually chosen negative bias for transform gates feels like a hyperparameter; request performance vs. initial bias plot.
- Ambiguity in comparisons: unclear whether experimental settings match FitNets/others; need apples-to-apples comparisons or clear delineation of differences.
- Reporting practice: “Best (mean ± std.dev.)” is misleading; recommend reporting mean and std across all runs for every method.
- Suggestion for future work: add theoretical discussion on how gating affects optimization landscape, expressivity, or generalization.
