## Review of “Training Very Deep Networks”

This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to incorporate LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms into each layer, allowing for unimpeded information flow across many layers via “information highways.” The authors demonstrate successful training of networks with over 100 layers using standard stochastic gradient descent, a feat previously challenging for plain, deeply stacked networks. This work directly addresses the longstanding problem of vanishing/exploding gradients and initialization difficulties that historically limited the depth of trainable neural networks.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly building upon previous work in deep learning. It correctly identifies the increasing importance of network depth for achieving state-of-the-art results in areas like image classification (citing Krizhevsky et al., 2012; Szegedy et al., 2015; Simonyan & Zisserman, 2014) and highlights the theoretical advantages of deep representations (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997; Håstad, 1991).  The authors meticulously survey existing techniques for training deep networks, including improved optimizers (Martens, 2010), initialization strategies (Glorot & Bengio, 2010; He et al., 2015), activation functions (Srivastava et al., 2015), and skip connections (Ciresan et al., 2012; Romero et al., 2015). The inspiration drawn from Long Short-Term Memory networks (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber, 1997) is explicitly stated and logically connected to the proposed solution.

The strengths of this paper are numerous. The technical contribution is significant. Highway networks represent a clever and effective modification to standard feedforward architectures. The experimental validation is thorough. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 datasets, comparing their performance to established baselines such as FitNets (Romero et al., 2015). Importantly, the analysis of information routing and layer importance (Section 4) provides valuable insights into the behavior of these deep networks, revealing that the gating mechanism allows for dynamic routing and selective layer utilization. The inclusion of visualizations (Figures 2 & 4) greatly enhances understanding of these dynamics. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors’ line of reasoning. The code and hyperparameter settings are publicly available, promoting reproducibility.

However, there are some minor weaknesses. While the paper effectively addresses the problem of training very deep networks, a more direct comparison to identity-initialized layers (with added noise) would be beneficial. Explaining how the transform gates fundamentally differ from this simpler scheme, and why they offer an advantage, could further strengthen the argument.  Additionally, a minor correction is needed in Section 4.1: the phrasing "most transform blocks are active on average" should be revised to "not active on average" to accurately reflect the observed behavior. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture is a novel approach to tackling the challenges of training very deep networks.
*   **Significance:** The ability to train networks with hundreds of layers opens up new possibilities for exploring the benefits of depth in various applications.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper provides a clear theoretical justification for the proposed architecture and demonstrates its effectiveness through rigorous experimentation.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand.
*   **Reproducibility:** The availability of code and hyperparameters promotes reproducibility.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The incremental benefit over simpler techniques like identity initialization isn’t fully explored.
*   The minor error in Section 4.1 (easily corrected).

**Overall Assessment:**

This is an excellent paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway networks offer a promising solution to the long-standing problem of training very deep networks, and the accompanying analysis provides valuable insights into their behavior. Despite the minor weaknesses, the strengths of this paper overwhelmingly support acceptance. I strongly recommend accepting this paper to NIPS 2015. It is a pleasure to read and I anticipate that it will have a substantial impact on future research in deep learning.
