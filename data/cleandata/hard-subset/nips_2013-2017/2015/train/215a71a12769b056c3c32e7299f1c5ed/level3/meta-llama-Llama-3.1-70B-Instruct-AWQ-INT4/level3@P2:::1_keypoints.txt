- Introduces "highway networks," a novel architecture using adaptive gates to enable training of very deep neural networks.
- Draws inspiration from LSTM and uses transform/carry gates to regulate information flow across layers.
- Demonstrates that highway networks can be trained directly with SGD without degradation as depth increases.
- Provides detailed analysis of learned gate-output patterns and their roles in information routing.
- Situates work relative to prior deep-learning efforts (optimizers, initialization, skip connections) and recent NIPS papers.
- Strength: clear description of the architecture and its gating components (gate unit, transform gate, carry gate).
- Strength: fairly convincing experiments showing effective training and generalization for very deep networks.
- Strength: strong empirical analysis of gate behaviors that aids understanding of the model.
- Weakness: use of sigmoid gates means outputs are never exactly 0/1, raising questions about gradients in eq. 5 and backprop implementation.
- Weakness: unsatisfying bias initialization strategy for gates (constant negative by depth); needs performance vs. initial-bias plot to justify.
- Weakness: comparative claims lack clarification—differences in experimental settings versus recent studies must be stated.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and easy to follow, but some notation/terminology may be unfamiliar to non-experts and needs more explanation or references.
- Originality: high—introduces a novel gating-based approach for deep network training.
- Significance: high—addresses a major deep-learning challenge and could influence future research.
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel architecture, effective experimental results, and thorough gate-output analysis.
- Con-acceptance arguments: unclear backprop details for sigmoid gates, unsatisfying gate-bias initialization justification, and unclear experimental comparisons.
