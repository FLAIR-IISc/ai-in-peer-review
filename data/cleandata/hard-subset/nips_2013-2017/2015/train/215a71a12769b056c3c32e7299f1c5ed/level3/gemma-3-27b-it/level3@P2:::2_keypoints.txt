- Proposes highway networks that use LSTM-inspired adaptive gates to enable training of very deep feedforward nets, successfully trained with SGD to depths >100 layers.
- Positions work within existing literature (optimizers, initialization, activations, skip connections, distillation) and explicitly connects to LSTMs while differentiating from FitNets.
- Novelty: introduces transform and carry gates for dynamic control of information flow as a flexible alternative to fixed skip connections.
- Technical soundness: mathematically well-defined architecture with useful Jacobian analysis (Equation 5).
- Empirical validation: comprehensive experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 showing clear advantages over plain networks and comparisons to FitNets/SOTA.
- In-depth analysis: informative visualizations of transform gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance illustrating data-dependent routing.
- Clarity & presentation: exceptionally well written, consistent notation, informative figures, and publicly available code/hyperparameters for reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited exploration of activation functions beyond ReLU.
- Weakness: minor typo on page 5 referencing "Tables 3 and 3".
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution that convincingly addresses training very deep networks.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, strong empirical results, insightful analysis, clear presentation, and high relevance to the community.
- Arguments against acceptance: none significant; only minor suggestions noted.
- Rating: Strongly Accept.
