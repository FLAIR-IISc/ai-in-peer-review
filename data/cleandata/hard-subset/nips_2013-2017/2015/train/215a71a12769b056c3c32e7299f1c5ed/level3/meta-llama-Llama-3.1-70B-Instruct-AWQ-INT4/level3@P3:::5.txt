This paper introduces a novel architecture called "highway networks" designed to overcome the difficulties of training very deep neural networks. The authors draw inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks and propose an adaptive gating mechanism to regulate the information flow across layers. This allows for unimpeded information flow across many layers, enabling the training of extremely deep and efficient architectures.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach. The idea of using an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism to train deep feedforward networks is simple yet elegant. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks in training very deep networks, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the inner workings of their model.

One of the strengths of this paper is its ability to provide a new perspective on the problem of training deep networks. The authors' use of an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism is a novel approach that has not been explored before. The paper also provides a thorough analysis of the benefits and limitations of their approach, including the potential for underutilization of parameters and the importance of careful initialization.

The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the ability of highway networks to train very deep networks with ease. The authors also provide a detailed comparison with existing approaches, including Fitnets and state-of-the-art methods on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100.

One potential weakness of this paper is the limited scope of the experiments.
