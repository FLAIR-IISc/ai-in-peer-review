- Introduces "highway networks," a novel architecture using adaptive gating to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.
- Gates allow layers to act as identity mappings when beneficial, mitigating vanishing/exploding gradients.
- Inspired by LSTMs and positioned relative to optimization, initialization, skip connections, and knowledge distillation literature.
- Clear, intuitive core idea and well-organized, readable exposition.
- Strong empirical results showing highway networks train effectively on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 to depths previously inaccessible.
- Solid experimental comparisons to FitNets and state-of-the-art convolutional networks.
- Visualizations (Figures 2 & 4) provide useful insights into selective gate activation and per-layer importance.
- Analysis section is insightful but could be deeper and more comprehensive.
- Suggested additional analyses: correlate gate activity with input data and visualize gradient flow to show routing behavior.
- Requested comparison of the more complex LSTM-inspired block design versus the simplified version used.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, significance, strong empirical evidence, and clarity.
- Argument against acceptance: limited depth of analysis; overall recommendation is acceptance with minor revisions.
