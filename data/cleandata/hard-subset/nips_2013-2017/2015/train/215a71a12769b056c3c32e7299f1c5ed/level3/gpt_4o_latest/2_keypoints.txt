- Proposes "highway networks": an architecture using input-dependent gates to create information highways across layers.  
- Inspired by LSTMs; gates let information flow unimpeded, mitigating vanishing gradients and poor gradient propagation.  
- Provides theoretical motivation and empirical evidence on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 showing promising performance.  
- Novel gating mechanism enables dynamic, input-dependent routing of information across layers.  
- Allows training of very deep networks (exceeding 100 layers) using simple SGD.  
- Outperforms plain networks of similar depth and converges significantly faster.  
- Includes insightful analysis showing gates selectively route information and contribute to computation, not just training.  
- Paper is high-quality, technically sound, well-written, and clearly distinguishes from related work (FitNets, LSTMs).  
- Offers practical guidance (e.g., gate initialization) making the method accessible to practitioners.  
- Strengths: originality, clarity, significance, robust experiments and analyses.  
- Weaknesses: minor typographical error ("Tables 3 and 3") and limited comparisons to broader state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
