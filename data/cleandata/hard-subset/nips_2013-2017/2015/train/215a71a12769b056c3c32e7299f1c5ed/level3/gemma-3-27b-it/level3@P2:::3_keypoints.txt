- Introduces highway networks: feedforward layers with LSTM-inspired gating to allow unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Demonstrates successful training of networks up to 100 layers using standard SGD, mitigating vanishing/exploding gradients.  
- Builds on prior work (optimizers, initialization, activations, skip connections) and clearly explains LSTM inspiration for feedforward nets.  
- Technically sound with strong theoretical motivation and experiments that support the claims.  
- Experiments cover MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 and include comparisons to relevant baselines (e.g., FitNets).  
- Analysis of information routing and layer importance (Sections 4 & 4.1) offers valuable insight into internal behavior beyond performance metrics.  
- Code availability enhances reproducibility and impact.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well organized, with consistent notation and readable equations.  
- Minor wording correction suggested: change “most transform blocks are active on average” to “most transform gates are not active on average.”  
- Novel contribution: applying LSTM-like gating to feedforward networks to address depth training is original and effective.  
- Acknowledges concurrent similar work [32], but provides more detailed analysis and experiments.  
- Suggests additional comparison to identity-initialized layers with noise to further highlight gating advantages.  
- Significant impact: enables training of extremely deep networks with standard procedures and informs architecture design via layer-importance analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong experiments, clarity, comprehensive analysis, reproducibility, and addresses a fundamental deep-learning challenge.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential computational cost of gating and the need for broader comparisons to other recent architectures.  
- Overall assessment: high quality, significant, and clear work; strong recommendation for acceptance.
