- Introduces highway networks: feed-forward layers with adaptive input-dependent gates enabling very deep networks.  
- Mathematical formulation: output y = H(x) * T(x) + x * (1 - T(x)) with transform gate T biased to carry information initially.  
- Inspired by LSTMs but distinct: feed-forward adaptive gating vs. recurrent LSTMs and static skip connections.  
- Core contribution: simple architecture-based solution that enables direct training of extremely deep networks without complex initialization or multi-stage training.  
- Quality: strong technical contribution with clear formulation, rigorous experiments, and insightful analysis.  
- Optimization results: highway networks train robustly to 100+ layers; plain networks fail beyond ~20 layers (MNIST).  
- Gate analysis: gates route information per-input, layers become class-selective, and deep networks utilize all layers for complex tasks.  
- Empirical performance: CIFAR-10/100 results match or exceed state-of-the-art using single-stage training; code provided for reproducibility.  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written, clear notation, transparent experimental setup; minor editorial typo noted.  
- Originality: novel combination of adaptive gating in feed-forward nets, avoiding limitations of prior methods like FitNets and careful initialization.  
- Significance: resolves a key barrier to scaling depth, enables analysis of deep computation, and is broadly applicable beyond vision.  
- Weaknesses: minor typo and no discussion of computational cost/efficiency.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
