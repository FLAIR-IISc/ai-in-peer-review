- Proposes highway networks: LSTM-inspired gating to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Gating mechanism allows unimpeded information flow and easier optimization with standard SGD.  
- Empirical results show highway networks train to much greater depths and outperform comparable plain networks on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100.  
- Analysis of learned gate biases and activity reveals selective routing and varying layer importance across tasks.  
- Architecture novelty: principled application of recurrent-style gates to feedforward networks.  
- Paper is generally well-written with consistent notation and clear mathematical formulation.  
- Major weakness: backpropagation description is ambiguous—equation for the Jacobian assumes T(x) is exactly 0 or 1, but T uses a sigmoid.  
- Initialization sensitivity: proposed negative bias values for transform gates (e.g., -1, -2, -3) appear ad-hoc and need systematic analysis.  
- Comparative claims (e.g., against FitNets) lack clarity about whether experimental setups and hyperparameters were matched.  
- Results reporting is incomplete in places—must provide mean and standard deviation across runs, not only best accuracy.  
- Overall technically sound, but clarification of gradient implementation and initialization analysis is required.  
- Originality: significant and novel contribution with potential to impact deep learning model design.  
- Significance: enables training much deeper networks and offers insights that could drive further architecture advances.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, strong empirical evidence, insightful gate analysis, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: ambiguity in backpropagation, ad-hoc initialization justification, unclear comparisons, incomplete result reporting.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify backpropagation, analyze initialization effects, ensure consistent result reporting, and specify experimental differences; optionally add a learning-theory analysis of gates.
