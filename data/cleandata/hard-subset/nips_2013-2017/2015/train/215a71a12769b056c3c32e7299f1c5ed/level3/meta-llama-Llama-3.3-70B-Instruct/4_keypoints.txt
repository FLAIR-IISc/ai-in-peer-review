- Proposes a novel framework for on-the-job learning that models the setting as a stochastic game and uses game-playing ideas to approximate optimal policies.
- Paper is well-written with a clear and concise explanation of the approach.
- Strength: original, significant, and technically sound; novel combination of online learning, active learning, and crowdsourcing.
- Strength: thorough problem analysis and a well-motivated solution.
- Strength: experimental results demonstrate effectiveness with detailed comparisons to baselines.
- Weakness: analysis of experimental results is too summary-level and lacks insight into why the approach outperforms baselines.
- Weakness: limited discussion of method limitations and potential avenues for future work.
- Weakness: strong assumption of access to a crowd of workers may limit applicability to other settings.
- Argument for acceptance: significant novel contribution with solid experiments and clear presentation.
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient result interpretation, limited applicability due to crowd assumption, and weak discussion of limitations/future work.
