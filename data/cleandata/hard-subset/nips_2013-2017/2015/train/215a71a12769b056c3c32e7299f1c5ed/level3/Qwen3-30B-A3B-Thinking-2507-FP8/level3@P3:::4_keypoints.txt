- Introduces "highway networks" using adaptive transform and carry gates to create information highways for very deep nets.  
- Gating mechanism is inspired by LSTMs and generalizes skip connections by routing information based on input.  
- Enables direct training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard gradient descent, avoiding optimization collapse in plain nets.  
- Strong technical soundness with rigorous experiments showing 100-layer highway nets vastly outperform plain counterparts on MNIST.  
- Competitive CIFAR-10/100 results while using fewer parameters than many state-of-the-art methods.  
- Analysis of transform gate behavior (Figure 2) provides useful insights into information flow through layers.  
- Positioning and related work discussion properly cites skip connections, knowledge distillation, and normalized initialization.  
- Mathematical formulation and experimental setup are clear, well-motivated, and well-presented.  
- Original contribution: adaptive gating that allows multi-layer unattenuated information flow, extending prior skip-connection ideas.  
- Significant impact: addresses fundamental challenge of training very deep networks and influences later work (e.g., residual networks).  
- Strengths: clear innovation, strong experimental validation, practical benefits for extreme depth, multi-dataset experiments, excellent presentation.  
- Weaknesses: analysis could include more illustrations and deeper gating investigations across architectures/datasets.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of computational cost compared to alternatives.  
- Weaknesses: could more thoroughly explore relationship to residual connections arising contemporaneously.  
- Recommendation: Accept — high-quality, influential paper with robust experimental support; minor issues do not undermine contribution.
