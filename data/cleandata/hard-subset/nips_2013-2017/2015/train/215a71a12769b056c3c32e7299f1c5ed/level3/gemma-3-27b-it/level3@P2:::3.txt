This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward neural networks. The core idea is to incorporate LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms into each layer, allowing for unimpeded information flow across many layers via “information highways.” The authors demonstrate successful training of networks with up to 100 layers using standard stochastic gradient descent, a feat previously challenging due to vanishing/exploding gradient problems. This work builds upon a growing body of research addressing the difficulties of training deep networks, including improvements to optimizers (e.g., [13, 14, 15]), initialization strategies ([16, 17, 18, 19]), activation functions ([20, 21]), and skip connections ([22, 23, 24, 25]). The inspiration from LSTM networks [29, 30] is clearly articulated and effectively translated to the feedforward setting.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and presents a compelling solution to a significant problem in deep learning. The theoretical motivation is well-explained, and the experimental results robustly support the claims. The authors carefully evaluate both the strengths and weaknesses of their approach, acknowledging potential limitations. The experiments are well-designed and cover a range of datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100), including comparisons to relevant baselines like FitNets [25]. The analysis of information routing and layer importance (Sections 4 & 4.1) provides valuable insights into the internal workings of highway networks and goes beyond simply demonstrating improved performance. The code availability is a strong positive aspect.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized. The notation is consistent, and the equations are presented in a readable format. The explanation of highway networks and the gating mechanism is intuitive and easy to follow. The authors provide sufficient detail to allow for reproduction of the results. A minor correction: in Section 4.1, the phrasing "most transform blocks are active on average" should be revised to “most transform gates are not active on average” for better accuracy.

**Originality:** The idea of applying LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms to feedforward networks to address the depth problem is novel and represents a significant contribution. While related work explores various techniques to improve deep network training, this approach offers a unique and effective solution.  It's worth noting that a similar idea was concurrently proposed [32], as acknowledged by the authors, but the detailed analysis and comprehensive experiments presented here significantly strengthen the contribution. Comparing and contrasting highway networks to simple identity-initialized layers with added noise would further highlight the advantages of the proposed gating mechanism. The dynamic routing analysis is also a novel aspect that distinguishes this work.

**Significance:** The results are important because they demonstrate that extremely deep networks can be trained effectively using standard optimization techniques. This opens up new possibilities for exploring the benefits of increased depth in various applications. The ability to train networks with hundreds of layers without specialized training procedures is a major step forward.  The analysis of layer importance provides valuable insights into network architecture design and can guide the development of more efficient and interpretable models. This work is likely to be influential and will inspire further research in deep learning.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and effective solution to the problem of training very deep networks.
*   Strong experimental results on multiple datasets.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.
*   Comprehensive analysis of information routing and layer importance.
*   Code availability facilitates reproducibility and further research.
*   Addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The computational cost of the gating mechanism could be a limitation for certain applications. (However, the benefits of increased depth likely outweigh this cost in many cases.)
*   While the authors compare to FitNets, a more detailed comparison with other recent deep learning architectures could further strengthen the paper.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a great paper and a pleasure to read. It presents a significant advance in the field of deep learning by enabling the training of extremely deep networks. The originality, clarity, quality, and significance of the work are all exceptionally high. I strongly recommend acceptance.
