The paper "Training Very Deep Networks" presents a novel approach to training very deep neural networks, a long-standing problem in the field of deep learning. The authors propose the use of "highway networks," which are inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks and utilize adaptive gating units to regulate the flow of information across layers. This approach allows for the training of extremely deep networks, with up to 100 layers, using simple gradient descent.

The paper is well-written and presents a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problems associated with training deep networks, including the issue of vanishing gradients, and demonstrate how their approach overcomes these difficulties. The experimental results are impressive, showing that highway networks can be trained to achieve state-of-the-art performance on several benchmark datasets, including MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to tackle the initialization and exploding/vanishing gradient problems that have historically limited the depth of neural networks. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the transform gate biases and demonstrate how they can be used to control the flow of information across layers. The experiments on information routing and layer importance are particularly compelling, as they provide insight into how the network is using the different layers to process the input data.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear and concise explanations of the proposed method and experimental results. The writing is engaging, and the authors do a great job of conveying their enthusiasm for the research.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to training very deep networks, which is a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The use of LSTM-style units to regulate the flow of information across layers is a clever idea, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness.

The paper is well-positioned in the context of previous work on deep learning, and the authors provide a thorough review of the relevant literature. The paper is also well-supported by experimental results, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

In terms of significance, the paper has the potential to make a significant impact on the field of deep learning. The ability to train very deep networks using simple gradient descent is a major breakthrough, and the paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method.
