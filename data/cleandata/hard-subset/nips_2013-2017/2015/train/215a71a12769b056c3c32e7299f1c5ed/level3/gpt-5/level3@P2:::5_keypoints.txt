- Proposal: Highway Networks add LSTM-like transform/carry gates so each layer computes H(x)·T(x)+x·(1−T(x)), enabling near-identity initialization and progressive opening of transforms.
- Prior work: Builds on LSTM gating, normalized init/rectifiers, skip/shortcut connections and deep supervision, FitNets/teacher hints, and maxout/competitive mechanisms.
- Strength — conceptual clarity: A minimal, elegant LSTM-style gating adaptation with C=1−T that preserves dimensionality.
- Strength — optimization: Thin but very deep MLPs (up to 100 layers) train readily with SGD and achieve much lower training loss than plain nets.
- Strength — generalization: Competitive MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 results without heavy augmentation; matches FitNets-like performance in a single stage.
- Strength — analysis: Useful visualizations of gate biases/activities, class-conditional routing, and lesion studies showing layer importance.
- Strength — practicality: Gate-bias heuristic is robust, applies to fully connected and conv layers, and code is publicly released.
- Weakness — scale and scope: No large-scale experiments (e.g., ImageNet) to validate significance in realistic settings and very deep regimes.
- Weakness — comparisons: Lacks direct baselines against fixed identity skips and deep supervision to quantify benefits of adaptive gating.
- Weakness — ablations: C=1−T choice and gate-tying across depth are untested; independent carry gates and other designs should be evaluated.
- Weakness — overhead/efficiency: Missing parameter/FLOP overhead, wall-clock training time, and memory footprint comparisons to plain or skip nets.
- Weakness — stability diagnostics: Needs gradient norm statistics, sensitivity to gate-bias init, and robustness to activation choices.
- Weakness — depth claims: Mention of >1000-layer training is anecdotal; at least brief quantitative results are needed.
- Quality: Paper is technically sound with controlled studies and mechanism-revealing analyses, though more ablations/compute accounting would help.
- Clarity: Well-written and organized; minor improvements include consolidating notation, detailing hyperparameter searches/schedules, and clarifying dimensionality-changing transitions.
- Originality: Novel and insightful adaptation of LSTM-style gating to feedforward stacks, distinct from fixed skips; Grid LSTM appropriately cited.
- Significance: High — a simple mechanism addressing deep training could influence architectures and practice if validated at scale.
- Suggested experiments: Train 50–100+ layer conv highway nets on ImageNet; ablate gate designs (independent C, per-channel/unit gates); report params/MACs/training time; analyze path-depth distributions and gradient flow; run multitask sharing experiments; compare representational capacity to stacked-sublayer modules.
- Arguments for acceptance: Simple, general mechanism that eases deep optimization, strong empirical/analytical evidence, reproducible code, and clear relation to influential ideas.
- Arguments against acceptance: No large-scale validation and limited ablations on gate parameterization and initialization sensitivity.
- Overall recommendation: Leans accept; would be stronger with the suggested comparisons and at least one large-scale experiment.
- Reviewer confidence: High on methodology and positioning; moderate regarding large-scale performance implications.
