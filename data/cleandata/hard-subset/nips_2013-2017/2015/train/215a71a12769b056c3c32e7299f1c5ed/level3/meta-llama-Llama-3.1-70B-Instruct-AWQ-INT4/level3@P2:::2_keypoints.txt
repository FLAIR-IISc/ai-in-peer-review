- Proposes highway networks, a novel architecture that enables training of very deep neural networks.  
- Introduces adaptive gating units inspired by LSTM to regulate inter-layer information flow, termed "information highways."  
- Provides thorough theoretical and empirical analysis of the architecture.  
- Demonstrates training of networks with hundreds of layers and competitive or superior performance on multiple datasets.  
- Relates work to skip connections, soft targets, layer-wise training, FitNets, and LSTMs, and includes comparisons while clarifying differences.  
- Presents visualizations and analysis of gate activity and block outputs, offering insight into internal routing.  
- Shows the network learns dynamic input-dependent routing through layers.  
- Addresses concern about unused layers by demonstrating gates do not keep layers inactive and that all layers can be utilized.  
- Paper is technically sound and reproducible, with sufficient detail for expert readers.  
- Writing and organization are clear, with good exposition of architecture and intuition.  
- Work is original in applying adaptive gating for deep feedforward networks.  
- Significant contribution to the challenge of training very deep networks, with strong empirical support.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, thorough analysis, promising results, clear writing, and relevance to a hard problem.  
- No arguments against acceptance noted.  
- Minor editorial note: typo on page 5 referring to "Tables 3 and 3."
