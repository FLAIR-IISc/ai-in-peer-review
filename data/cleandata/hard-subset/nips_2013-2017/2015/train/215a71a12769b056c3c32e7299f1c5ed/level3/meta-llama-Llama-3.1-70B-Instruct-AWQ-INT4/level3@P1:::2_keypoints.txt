- Proposes "highway networks" to overcome difficulties in training very deep neural networks.  
- Introduces adaptive gating units inspired by LSTM to allow unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Enables training of extremely deep networks directly via simple gradient descent.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and accessible, with one minor typo on page 5.  
- Provides in-depth analysis of gate behavior and how gates select inputs across layers.  
- Empirical results show highway networks can be trained to depths not possible with standard feed-forward architectures.  
- Thorough comparisons to state-of-the-art methods, including FitNets and maxout networks.  
- Original contribution that clearly distinguishes itself from related work like FitNets and LSTMs.
