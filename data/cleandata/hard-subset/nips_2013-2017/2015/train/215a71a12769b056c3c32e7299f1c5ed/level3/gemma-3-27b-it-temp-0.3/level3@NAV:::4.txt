## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces “highway networks,” a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward neural networks. The core idea is to incorporate “information highways” – direct cross-layer connections with adaptive gating mechanisms inspired by LSTMs – to alleviate the vanishing gradient problem and enable unimpeded information flow. This builds upon prior work addressing the challenges of training deep networks, such as improved optimizers (e.g., [13, 14, 15]), initialization strategies ([16, 17]), and skip connections ([22, 23, 24]). The authors convincingly demonstrate through experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 that highway networks can be trained effectively with standard SGD, even with hundreds of layers, outperforming traditional networks of similar complexity.

The strengths of this paper are considerable. The proposed method is both novel and well-motivated, offering a compelling solution to a significant problem in deep learning. The experimental evaluation is solid, with thorough comparisons to existing techniques like FitNets [25] and state-of-the-art methods [5, 34]. The clarity of the paper is also commendable; the main ideas are communicated effectively, and the mathematical formulation is concise. The authors also appropriately acknowledge related work, including a similar LSTM-inspired model [32] appearing concurrently. The significance of enabling the training of increasingly deeper networks is substantial, potentially unlocking new levels of performance in various applications.

However, the analysis section, while insightful, could be expanded. While Figures 2 and 4 provide valuable glimpses into the network’s inner workings – transform gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance – more varied details or additional illustrations would strengthen the analysis. For example, visualizing the learned gating mechanisms for specific input examples could provide further intuition. A more detailed ablation study examining the impact of different gate initializations or network widths would also be beneficial. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and effective approach to training very deep networks.
*   Strong experimental validation on multiple datasets.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.
*   Significant potential impact on the field.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Analysis section could be more comprehensive.



Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. Despite the minor weakness in the analysis, the novelty, experimental rigor, and clarity of the work strongly support acceptance. I recommend accepting this paper to NIPS.
