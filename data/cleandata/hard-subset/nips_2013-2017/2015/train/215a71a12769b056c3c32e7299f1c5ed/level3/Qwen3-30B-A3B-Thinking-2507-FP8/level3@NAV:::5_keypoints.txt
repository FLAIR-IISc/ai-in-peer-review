- Introduces "highway networks," an LSTM-inspired gating architecture that enables direct training of extremely deep feedforward networks with simple gradient descent.  
- Adaptive gates create "information highways" allowing unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Differentiates from prior skip connections and two-stage methods by enabling single-stage training of hundreds of layers without special initialization.  
- Strong technical quality demonstrated by rigorous experiments on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100.  
- Highway networks consistently outperform plain networks of similar depth; 100-layer models show orders-of-magnitude improvements over plain counterparts.  
- Compelling analyses, including layer-importance studies showing dynamic layer usage per input and broad layer contribution on complex tasks (e.g., CIFAR-100).  
- Paper is clear and well-structured with effective visualizations of information routing.  
- Original contribution: novel application of LSTM-style gating to feedforward depth, offering a conceptual shift about depth vs. layer complexity.  
- Significant impact: enables systematic exploration of extremely deep architectures previously limited by training difficulties.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: simpler datasets (MNIST) may not utilize full depth while complex tasks benefit more.  
- Suggested improvement: perform joint MNIST+CIFAR training to study parameter-sharing and resource allocation across tasks.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, well-executed contribution; recommendation for acceptance.
