- Introduces highway networks: a novel architecture using LSTM-inspired adaptive gating to allow unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Enables training of extremely deep networks with simple gradient descent.  
- Thorough experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 showing competitive performance and no depth-related optimization issues.  
- Strength: simple, elegant, and effective LSTM-style gating mechanism.  
- Solid analysis and well-executed experimental validation.  
- Raises alternative perspective that apparent depth may come from richer sublayers (e.g., Inception/VGG), questioning the uniqueness of highway layers.  
- Suggested experiment: train a thin-and-tall network jointly on MNIST and CIFAR-10 with a fork at the top to test parameter reuse and shared inference patterns.  
- Recommends evaluation on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to demonstrate scalability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, simplicity, thorough experiments, and well-motivated approach.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs more detailed comparison with other deep-training methods.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited insight into interpretability of learned representations.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10; overall positive recommendation.
