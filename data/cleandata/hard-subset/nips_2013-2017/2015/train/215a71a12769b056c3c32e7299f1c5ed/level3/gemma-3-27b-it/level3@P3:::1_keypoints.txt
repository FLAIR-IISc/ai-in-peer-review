- Introduces highway networks: adaptive gating (LSTM-inspired) to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Core idea is sound and builds on depth benefits; effectively relates to optimizers, initialization, and skip connections.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and presents contributions and equations straightforwardly.  
- Technical quality: experiments are convincing that highway networks train deeper models; analysis of learned gates is insightful.  
- Critical technical flaw: Equation 5 incorrectly treats the Jacobian as discrete; gates output continuous values and backprop implementation must be explained.  
- Missing theoretical backing beyond LSTM inspiration; a learning-theory analysis would strengthen the paper.  
- Notation and organization are good, but Equation 5 is problematic and misleading.  
- Originality: novel application of adaptive gating to feedforward nets; concurrent similar work is acknowledged.  
- Significance: substantial potential impact by enabling investigation of much deeper architectures and per-layer contributions.  
- Specific concern — gradient calculation: authors must correct Eq.5 and detail exact backpropagation through gates.  
- Specific concern — initialization bias: need systematic exploration (e.g., plot of performance vs initial transform bias) to show sensitivity.  
- Specific concern — comparative claims: require explicit matching or disclosure of dataset, augmentation, and architecture differences when comparing to prior work.  
- Specific concern — reporting statistics: report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs for all methods (especially MNIST).  
- Specific concern — learning-theory: discuss how conditional identity behavior helps expressiveness, gradient flow, and generalization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective architecture; convincing experiments; insightful gate analysis; clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: critical Eq.5 error; insufficient bias hyperparameter study; unclear experimental comparison details; limited theory.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions — fix gradient calculation, analyze initialization bias, and improve comparisons, reporting, and theoretical discussion.
