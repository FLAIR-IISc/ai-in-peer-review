- Proposes LENSE, a novel framework for on-the-job learning that learns from noisy crowd labels while reducing cost over time.  
- Models the on-the-job setting as a stochastic game and approximates the optimal policy with Monte Carlo Tree Search and progressive widening.  
- Evaluated on NER, sentiment classification, and image classification, showing significant cost reductions and accuracy improvements over baselines.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanations of methods and experiments.  
- Technical approach is sound and experimentally effective.  
- Provides a thorough related-work discussion and distinguishes the approach from existing methods.  
- Key strength: principled trade-off of latency, cost, and accuracy using Bayesian decision theory.  
- Detailed analysis of system behavior, including effects of crowd worker quality and benefits of richer features to reduce supervision.  
- Suggested improvement: evaluate scalability on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to demonstrate performance in more challenging settings.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more detailed analysis of computational complexity and limitations for scalability and real-time performance.  
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution to ML and crowdsourcing; reviewer recommends acceptance.
