This paper proposes highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate training of very deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to introduce “information highways” – connections that allow information to flow across many layers without attenuation, inspired by gating mechanisms in LSTMs. The authors demonstrate that these networks can be trained with standard stochastic gradient descent to depths previously inaccessible to plain networks, and provide a thorough analysis of how these gates function.

The paper is a strong contribution to the field. It addresses a critical challenge in deep learning – the difficulty of training very deep networks – and offers an elegant and effective solution. The work builds upon existing research in optimization (e.g., [13, 14, 15]), initialization strategies ([16, 17]), and skip connections ([22, 23, 24]), but clearly differentiates itself and offers a significant advancement. The acknowledgment of related work like FitNets [25] and LSTMs [29, 30] is thorough and the distinctions are clearly explained. The empirical results are promising, demonstrating the ability to train networks with hundreds of layers. The analysis of gate behavior (Figures 2-4) provides valuable insight into the workings of the proposed architecture.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The mathematical formulation of highway networks is clear and concise (Equation 3). The experimental validation is robust, with comparisons to plain networks and FitNets. The authors acknowledge limitations and provide a careful evaluation of both the strengths and weaknesses of their approach. The inclusion of ablation studies, such as layer importance analysis (Figure 4), strengthens the credibility of the findings.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The motivation for the work is clearly presented, and the proposed architecture is explained in a straightforward manner. The use of notation is consistent and the figures are informative. The organization follows a logical flow, starting with background, introducing the method, presenting experiments, analyzing results, and concluding with a discussion.  A minor editorial note: on page 5, "Tables 3 and 3" should be corrected.

**Originality:** The concept of using adaptive gating mechanisms to create “information highways” in feedforward networks is novel. While inspired by LSTMs, the application to deep feedforward networks and the detailed analysis of gate behavior represent a significant contribution. The paper demonstrates a unique approach to overcoming the vanishing gradient problem and enabling the training of much deeper networks.

**Significance:** This work has the potential to significantly impact the field of deep learning. The ability to train very deep networks could lead to improved performance on a variety of tasks. The insights gained from the analysis of gate behavior could inform the design of future neural network architectures. The paper addresses a fundamental problem and advances the state-of-the-art in a demonstrable way.  It provides a valuable tool for researchers seeking to explore the benefits of extreme depth in neural networks.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks.
*   Thorough experimental validation and comparison to existing methods.
*   In-depth analysis of gate behavior, providing valuable insights.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.
*   Addresses a significant challenge in deep learning.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   None. This is a very strong paper.



**Overall:** This is a very solid paper with a novel technique and thorough analysis. I have no suggested improvements and strongly recommend acceptance to NIPS.
