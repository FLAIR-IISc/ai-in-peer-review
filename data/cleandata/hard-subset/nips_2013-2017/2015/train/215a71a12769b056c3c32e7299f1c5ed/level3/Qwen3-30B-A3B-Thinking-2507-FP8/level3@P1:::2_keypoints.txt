- Introduces highway networks that enable direct training of extremely deep feedforward nets (100+ layers) with standard SGD.  
- Tackles the depth-related optimization degradation by introducing adaptive gating units inspired by LSTMs.  
- Each highway layer adaptively mixes transformed and carried inputs via a learned transform gate T(x).  
- Empirically, highway networks overcome the depth barrier (e.g., 100-layer highways >> plain nets on MNIST) while maintaining strong generalization.  
- Experiments are comprehensive across MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 with rigorous comparisons to plain networks.  
- Analysis shows transform gates perform data-dependent routing, not static shortcuts, and layer importance varies by task.  
- Ablation and lesion studies validate the contribution and utilization of individual layers.  
- Enables single-stage training without teacher networks or complex initialization required by FitNets.  
- Novelty: adaptive, input-dependent gating for feedforward networks distinguishes it from LSTMs, skip connections, and prior work.  
- Related work and theoretical foundations are thoroughly discussed and grounded (including practical initialization b^T = -1 to -3).  
- Writing quality is high, with clear organization and sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Significance is high: makes extreme depth practical and opens new avenues to study layer utilization and depth requirements.  
- Weaknesses are minimal (only a trivial typo noted).  
- Reviewer’s conclusion: technically rigorous, novel, impactful — strongly recommended for acceptance; no cons identified.
