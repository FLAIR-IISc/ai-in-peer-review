- Proposal: Introduces highway networks with LSTM-like transform gates T to compute y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)), enabling near-identity initialization and unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Relation to prior work: Generalizes skip connections by using learnable, input-dependent gates and enables end-to-end SGD for very deep nets without pretraining or special optimizers.  
- Empirical claims: Demonstrates robustness to depth (hundreds to >1000 layers in pilots), strong optimization vs plain nets, and competitive accuracy on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100.  
- Strength: Simple, general architecture easy to implement for fully connected and convolutional layers.  
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence that optimization difficulty does not grow with depth for highway networks.  
- Strength: Competitive single-stage training of thin deep models, matching or beating prior FitNet results under comparable budgets.  
- Strength: Insightful analyses including gate bias/activity visualizations, class-conditional routing, and lesion studies.  
- Strength: Clear writing and promised code/scripts improving reproducibility and impact.  
- Weakness: Missing stronger baselines—no hard-wired identity-skip (residual) comparison or batch-normalized variants matched for parameters.  
- Weakness: Insufficient systematic ablations on gate bias, learning rates, and activation choices to support robustness claims.  
- Weakness: Limited dataset scope (mostly MNIST and CIFAR) and limited augmentation; lacks larger-scale validation.  
- Weakness: Overhead/efficiency not quantified—no FLOPs/parameter counts or inference-time cost reported.  
- Weakness: Carry gate tied as C=1−T; no ablation testing independent learning of C.  
- Weakness: Analyses lack statistics over multiple seeds and quantitative measures of routing diversity.  
- Quality: Technical formulation and initialization are sound; empirical support compelling for optimization claims but missing some baselines.  
- Clarity: Well-written, organized, and illustrated; minor suggestions to add architecture diagram, parameter/FLOP table, and architecture details in appendix.  
- Originality: Novel application of LSTM-style gating to depth-wise information flow, distinct from existing skip/hint methods.  
- Significance: High — makes training very deep networks practical with a simple, general mechanism likely to be widely adopted.  
- Questions for authors: Compare to identity-skip/residual baseline; effect of batch normalization; sensitivity to initial bT; learn C independently; quantify computational overhead.  
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, general idea with strong empirical validation and insightful analyses demonstrating practical importance.  
- Cons for acceptance: Missing key baselines (residual/BN), limited datasets/augmentations, and incomplete gate/initialization ablations.  
- Recommendation: Accept — compelling contribution with solid experiments and clear impact, pending a few additional baselines/ablations.  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
