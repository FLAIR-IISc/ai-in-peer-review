- Introduces "highway networks," a novel deep architecture using adaptive transform and carry gates inspired by LSTMs to enable information highways.
- Each layer includes transform and carry gates that regulate information flow, allowing unimpeded gradient and activation propagation across many layers.
- Highway networks can be trained directly with standard SGD without complex initialization or two-stage training, even for hundreds of layers.
- Method is original, generalizing skip connections and recurrent gating in a significant way.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and shows consistent accuracy improvements on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 versus plain networks and competitors like Fitnets.
- Demonstrates comparable or better performance than shallower networks while using significantly fewer parameters.
- Technical presentation is sound, clearly communicated, and implementation details support reproducibility.
- Analysis effectively demonstrates information flow and validates dynamic routing via transform gates.
- Primary weakness: analysis could include more varied details and additional visualizations of gate behavior across layers and datasets.
- Overall contribution is significant, practical, and theoretically grounded, addressing a fundamental deep learning challenge.
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance (meets NIPS quality, originality, and significance criteria).
