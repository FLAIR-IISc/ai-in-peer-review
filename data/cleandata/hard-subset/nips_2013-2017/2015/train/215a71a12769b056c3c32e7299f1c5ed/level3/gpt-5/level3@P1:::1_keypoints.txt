- Proposes highway networks using input-dependent sigmoid gates (T and C=1−T) to let layers transform or carry inputs, creating information “highways” across depth.  
- Each layer can learn to transform or pass-through inputs, easing gradient flow and optimization at extreme depths.  
- MNIST experiments: plain nets degrade with depth while highway networks with 50–100 layers remain trainable and converge faster.  
- Convolutional highway results on MNIST/CIFAR-10/100 are competitive with FitNets and contemporaries at comparable parameter/compute budgets, trained in one stage.  
- Analyses (gate activations, data-dependent routing, lesioning) show simple tasks rely on early layers, harder tasks use many layers.  
- Unifies ideas from LSTMs, skip/shortcut connections, deep supervision, and distillation, but uniquely learns multiplicative, input-dependent routing.  
- Technical soundness: training results and analyses are convincing, but the gradient/Jacobian exposition is incorrect or incomplete.  
- Critical gradient issue: Eq.5 ignores T(x)’s dependence on x; reviewer provides the fuller Jacobian and requests exact backprop expressions and numerical-stability discussion.  
- Empirical evaluation: optimization study is careful; CIFAR comparisons are useful but need more rigorous reporting and setup disclosure.  
- Reproducibility: public code is a plus; paper should add explicit training schedules, number of epochs, augmentation details, and exact model specs.  
- Clarity: architecture and figures are clear, but the gradient presentation must be corrected to avoid misleading readers.  
- Initialization: recommending negative gate biases is plausible but heuristic; request sensitivity analysis of initial bT across depths/datasets and discussion of failure modes.  
- Comparative claims: require explicit statements on whether preprocessing, augmentation, epochs, parameter counts, and compute budgets match cited works or note differences.  
- Originality: novel at the time in applying input-dependent multiplicative gating to feedforward depth with simple training and diagnostic analyses.  
- Significance: enables training of very deep nets with plain SGD, potentially influencing architecture design and conditional computation methods.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, strong optimization evidence across depths, single-stage training, insightful gating analyses, and public code.  
- Weaknesses/requests: provide full backprop formulas, bT sensitivity ablations, mean±std reporting over multiple runs, documented baseline reimplementations, and possibly light theory.  
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a central challenge simply and effectively, showing optimization and generalization benefits with interpretable analyses.  
- Cons for acceptance: incorrect/missing gradient exposition, heuristic initialization without sensitivity analysis, and some comparisons/reporting lacking statistical rigor.  
- Overall: strong and timely contribution; reviewer leans toward acceptance pending requested clarifications, corrected gradients, and more rigorous reporting/ablations.
