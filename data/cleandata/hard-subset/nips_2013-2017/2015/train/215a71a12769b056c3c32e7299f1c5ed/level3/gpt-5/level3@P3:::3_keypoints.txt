- Proposes highway networks: feedforward layers with LSTM-style transform/carry gates y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)) and negative-bias initialization to preserve activations/gradients across depth.
- Demonstrates successful direct training (SGD+momentum) of very deep (up to 100+) fully connected and convolutional networks with faster convergence than plain nets.
- Situates work relative to LSTMs, variance-preserving initializations, and depth-assisting strategies (maxout, skip connections, FitNets), emphasizing input-dependent, learnable gating as the novelty.
- Technical formulation and initialization rationale are sound; experiments support claims about trainability and optimization behavior.
- Empirical results include MNIST sweeps, CIFAR-10/100 comparisons, and compelling qualitative analyses (gate statistics, lesioning) showing dynamic routing and layer importance.
- Two empirical omissions: no direct comparison to identity-initialized layers or fixed skip connections, and no compute/parameter overhead analysis at equal accuracy.
- Claims are measured, code and hyperparameters are shared, and limitations are acknowledged.
- Paper is very well written and organized; figures and equations are clear and analyses are exemplary.
- Correction: sentence in Section 4.1 should state that most transform gates are not active on average (sparse mean activity), not active.
- Originality: first application of LSTM-style gating to arbitrarily deep feedforward nets at this scale; learned input-dependent gating and initialization are novel.
- Significance: enables end-to-end training of 50–100+ layer networks and introduces a general architectural principle for depth credit assignment.
- Strengths: elegant architecture addressing vanishing/exploding gradients, trains very deep models with standard SGD, insightful routing/lesion analyses, reproducible code, competitive CIFAR performance.
- Main weaknesses/suggestions: add experiments comparing to identity-initialized layers and fixed additive skips, report compute/parameter/wall-clock trade-offs, and ablate coupling C = 1−T vs. independently learned C and scalar vs. per-unit gates.
- Pro arguments: principled solution to deep training, strong empirical evidence, simple to implement, minimal tuning, high clarity and originality.
- Con arguments: lacks direct identity/skip baselines, limited theoretical analysis, and no detailed compute/parameter trade-off study.
- Minor comments: fix “backpropogation” typo, clarify gate bias schedule selection and sensitivity, and note stability when gates saturate.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — original, carefully executed, and practically significant work.
