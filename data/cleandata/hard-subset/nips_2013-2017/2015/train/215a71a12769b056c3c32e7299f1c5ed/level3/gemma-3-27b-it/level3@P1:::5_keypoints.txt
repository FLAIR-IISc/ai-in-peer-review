- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-inspired gating to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Adaptive gates create “information highways” that alleviate vanishing gradients and optimization issues.  
- Demonstrates successful training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard SGD.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 showing improved trainability and generalization.  
- Builds on prior work in optimization, initialization, activations, and skip connections while offering an architectural solution.  
- Technical contribution is clear, well-motivated, and technically sound.  
- Strong empirical evidence and insightful analysis (e.g., Figures 2 and 4) on information flow and layer contributions.  
- Well-designed experiments and comprehensive literature referencing.  
- Originality could be better contextualized relative to modern architectures (e.g., Inception, stacked 3x3 convolutions) that add internal layer depth.  
- Raises concerns about parameter utilization: some layers become essentially unused on simple datasets (e.g., MNIST).  
- Suggests experiment: train a deep thin network jointly on MNIST and CIFAR-10 with task-specific forks to study parameter reuse.  
- Recommends further evaluation on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to assess scalability and real-world applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, strong empirical results, thorough analysis, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of relation to existing architectural trends and lack of large-scale dataset evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: Strong Accept; significant, elegant contribution with encouragement to evaluate at larger scale.
