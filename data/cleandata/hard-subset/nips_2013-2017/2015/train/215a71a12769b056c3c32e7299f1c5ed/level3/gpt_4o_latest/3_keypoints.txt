- Introduces Highway Networks: an LSTM-inspired gated feedforward architecture enabling training of very deep networks.  
- Addresses initialization and vanishing-gradient problems to allow effective training beyond 100 layers.  
- Uses adaptive transform/carry gates to enable unimpeded information flow across layers.  
- Builds on prior work (skip connections, initialization, optimization) while providing a more flexible dynamic routing mechanism.  
- Technically sound with theoretical insights supporting the design.  
- Extensive experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 demonstrate scalability, generalization, and superiority over plain networks.  
- Achieves direct training of 100-layer networks with stochastic gradient descent, a significant practical result.  
- Clear presentation with useful visualizations (transform gate activity, layer importance).  
- Code and hyperparameter search results are released, supporting reproducibility.  
- Originality highlighted by novel application of LSTM-like gating to feedforward nets beyond existing skip-connection approaches.  
- Minor clarity issue in Section 4.1 that should be revised for precision.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion on how transform gates differ from identity-matrix initialization—needs clarification.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions; strengths clearly outweigh weaknesses.
