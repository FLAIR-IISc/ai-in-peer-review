- Proposes highway networks: a novel neural architecture using LSTM-inspired adaptive gating to train very deep networks.
- Gating mechanism creates "information highways" that allow near-unimpeded information flow and training with simple gradient descent.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides a thorough analysis of learned gate-output patterns.
- Experiments are convincing, showing effective training of networks with hundreds of layers.
- Concern: Gate uses sigmoid (never exactly 0 or 1), raising questions about the correctness of gradients in Equation 5 and requiring backpropagation clarification.
- Concern: Transform gate biases are initialized to a constant negative value, which is unsatisfying and may be suboptimal.
- Suggestion: Provide a plot of performance versus initial bias since bias acts like a regularizer favoring longer highways.
- Suggestion: Clarify comparative claims by explicitly stating differences in experimental settings from recent studies.
- Suggestion: Report mean and standard deviation for results rather than only maximum accuracy to better characterize performance.
- Suggestion: Include a learning-theory analysis of gate units to explain conditional identity behavior and input propagation through selective layers.
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.
- Authors are careful and honest in evaluating strengths and weaknesses; paper is reproducible for expert readers.
- Originality stems from a novel combination of familiar techniques to create highway networks.
- Significance: results could advance deep learning by improving training of very deep networks.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, convincing experiments, and detailed gate-output analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved gradient correctness issue, questionable bias initialization, and unclear experimental comparisons.
