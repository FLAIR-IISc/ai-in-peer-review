## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper presents a compelling approach to training very deep feedforward networks – demonstrating successful training up to 100 layers – by drawing inspiration from LSTM recurrent networks and introducing “highway networks” with adaptive gating mechanisms. The core idea of enabling unimpeded information flow through these “information highways” is novel and effectively addresses the longstanding challenges of vanishing/exploding gradients and optimization difficulties that plague deep network training. This work builds upon prior efforts in network depth, such as the work by Krizhevsky et al. (2012) and subsequent improvements with deeper architectures (Szegedy et al., 2015; Simonyan & Zisserman, 2014), demonstrating a significant step forward in enabling deeper architectures without resorting to techniques like FitNets (Romero et al., 2015) or complex initialization schemes.

The strengths of this paper are numerous. It's a technically sound contribution that tackles a fundamental problem in deep learning. The experimental validation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 is thorough and convincing, clearly showcasing the benefits of highway networks over plain networks, especially as depth increases. The analysis section, particularly the visualization of gate activity (Figure 2) and layer importance (Figure 4), provides valuable insights into the network's internal workings and supports the authors' claims regarding dynamic routing and layer utilization. The clarity of the writing is also commendable – the concepts are explained effectively, and the paper is well-organized.

However, a comparison to identity-initialized layers with added noise would strengthen the work. While the paper motivates the gating mechanism as a solution to gradient flow, briefly discussing its distinction from simply initializing weights as identity matrices (with a small amount of noise), as suggested by the supervisor, would clarify the unique contribution of the transform gates. This comparison would allow readers to better appreciate the advantages of the more complex gating approach. Additionally, while Section 4.1 accurately describes the observation, it should be corrected to state "not active on average," as noted by the supervisor.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** Introduces a genuinely novel architecture (highway networks) inspired by LSTMs.
*   **Significance:** Successfully trains very deep networks, opening up new possibilities for architectural exploration.
*   **Technical Soundness:** Supported by strong experimental results and insightful analysis.
*   **Clarity:** Well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Minor: Lack of direct comparison to a simple baseline of identity-initialized layers with noise.
*   Minor: Typo in Section 4.1.

**Overall:** This is a great paper, a pleasure to read, and a significant contribution to the field. I strongly recommend acceptance to the conference. The originality, significance, and technical quality are all high, and the minor suggestions outlined above would further enhance the paper's impact.
