- Proposes Highway Networks: LSTM-inspired gating (transform/carry) to enable training of very deep feedforward nets (up to 100 layers).
- Addresses vanishing/exploding gradients by allowing unimpeded information flow across layers.
- Situates work well within literature on optimization, initialization, skip connections, knowledge distillation, and LSTM-inspired models.
- Novelty: creative application of gating to depth problem beyond initialization/optimization tricks.
- Technical soundness: clear mathematical formulation (Equation 3) and solid theoretical grounding.
- Empirical validation: strong results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 and useful comparisons to FitNets and SOTA.
- Ablation (layer lesioning) study provides insightful analysis of layer importance and information routing.
- Clarity: well-written, consistent notation, and effective visualizations (Figures 2 and 4).
- Significance: enables training much deeper networks and opens avenues for studying depth benefits.
- Weakness: limited discussion/analysis of computational overhead introduced by gating mechanisms.
- Weakness: potential sensitivity to transform-gate bias and other hyperparameters not systematically explored.
- Correction needed: Section 4.1 misstated transform block activity; should report they are "not active on average."
- Suggestion: compare to identity-initialized deep networks to isolate benefits of learned gating versus identity mappings.
- Suggestion: deeper analysis of gate activity correlations with feature importance to better understand routing.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with compelling experiments, clarity, and insightful analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete exploration of computational cost and a minor factual inaccuracy in Section 4.1.
- Overall assessment: strong paper, minor easily addressable issues, reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
