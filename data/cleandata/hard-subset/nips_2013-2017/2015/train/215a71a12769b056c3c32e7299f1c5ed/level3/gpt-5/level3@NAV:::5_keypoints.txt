- Introduces highway networks: feedforward nets with LSTM-style transform/carry gates to let information flow across hundreds of layers.  
- Shows plain SGD can train very deep networks (100+ layers) with supporting optimization curves.  
- Empirical results include CIFAR and MNIST performance plus analyses of gate activity and layer lesioning.  
- Relates well to prior depth work (VGG, Inception), initialization for rectifiers, skip connections, and FitNets/distillation while offering a distinct LSTM-inspired mechanism.  
- Technically solid and carefully evaluated; optimization and gate/layer analyses are convincing.  
- Minor methodological gaps: need systematic account of parameter/computation overhead from gates.  
- Missing ablations on the C = 1 − T constraint and sensitivity to gate-bias initialization.  
- Clear, well-organized, and reproducible (code and hyperparameters provided); figures effectively illustrate gating dynamics.  
- Original and elegant contribution: first thorough application of LSTM-like gating to deep feedforward nets.  
- High potential significance if adopted broadly; ImageNet-scale validation would strengthen impact.  
- Alternative framing: view highway layers as modules that internally choose between identity and transformation, clarifying effective depth.  
- Suggested experiment: train a thin-and-tall shared highway trunk on MNIST+CIFAR to study parameter reuse and shared inference patterns.  
- Pros: simple, novel, effective; enables very deep nets with plain SGD; strong analyses and released code.  
- Cons: limited large-scale validation and modest discussion of compute/parameter overhead; missing gate-design ablations.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept, with encouragement to test on ImageNet and deepen analyses of parameter sharing and gate design.
