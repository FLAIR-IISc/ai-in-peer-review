- Introduces "highway networks": LSTM-inspired transform and carry gates that enable training very deep feedforward networks by allowing information to flow across many layers.  
- Demonstrates highway networks up to 100 layers can be trained directly with SGD, mitigating vanishing gradients.  
- Shows gates learn data-dependent routing, dynamically adjusting effective network depth per input.  
- Original and significant idea: applies LSTM-like gating to feedforward nets, enabling much greater depth and adaptive routing.  
- Strong experimental evidence: optimization experiments show performance does not degrade with depth versus plain nets.  
- Comparison to FitNets: achieves comparable or better results without complex two-stage training.  
- Insightful analysis and visualizations: gate biases/activities and layer lesioning reveal task-dependent use of depth (MNIST vs CIFAR-100).  
- Very clear writing and presentation; formulation and LSTM connection are well explained.  
- Suggest reframing: highway layers can be seen as richer computational blocks, not just enabling depth, relating to Inception/VGG-style micro-architectures.  
- Suggest multi-task experiment: train one thin-and-tall highway network on MNIST and CIFAR-100 with shared trunk and separate heads to inspect task-specific gating.  
- Suggest scalability test: evaluate on large-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to confirm practical impact and adoption potential.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, and impactful work with strong recommendation for acceptance.
