- Introduces "highway networks," a feedforward architecture using LSTM-style adaptive gating to train extremely deep networks (e.g., 100+ layers) with standard SGD.  
- Adaptive gates create "information highways" that mitigate vanishing/exploding gradients by allowing signals to pass unimpeded.  
- Original and significant idea: adapting LSTM gating to feedforward nets enables new exploration of depth and is likely to be widely adopted.  
- Technically sound with rigorous experiments on MNIST and CIFAR; direct comparison shows plain networks fail at comparable depths.  
- Section 4's analysis (dynamic information routing and layer lesion study) provides valuable insight into learned behavior.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear, well-written, and well-organized.  
- Needs comparison to a simpler baseline (e.g., fixed identity-initialized skip connections) to isolate benefits of learned gating.  
- Minor typo/clarification in Section 4.1 about whether transform gates are active on average.  
- Strong recommendation for acceptance; strengths far outweigh minor weaknesses.
