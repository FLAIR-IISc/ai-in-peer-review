- Addresses training very deep feedforward networks by introducing "highway networks" with learnable layer-wise gates that blend transformed input and direct carry-through.  
- Gates (sigmoids) enable information highways that prevent gradient degradation, allowing standard SGD to train hundreds of layers.  
- Empirical results on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 show highway networks overcome optimization issues of plain deep nets and match/beat contemporary methods.  
- Novel and well-motivated idea: adapts LSTM-style gating to feedforward nets, enabling training of extremely deep architectures.  
- Strong empirical validation: controlled MNIST optimization experiments and competitive CIFAR results, with single-stage end-to-end training.  
- Excellent analysis: visualizations of gate activity, class-conditional routing evidence, and layer-lesioning to probe effective depth.  
- Clarify gradient-flow description: Jacobian discussion is misleading because sigmoid gates yield smooth interpolations, not exact identity or H'.  
- Provide analysis of transform-gate bias initialization (b_T) sensitivity, as current heuristic values appear ad-hoc.  
- Make comparisons to other methods (e.g., FitNets) more rigorous by matching or clearly reporting experimental settings.  
- Report means and standard deviations for all results (including baselines) across multiple runs for fair comparison.  
- Suggest adding brief theoretical discussion on how conditional identity mapping eases optimization and affects functional/learned depth.  
- Overall assessment: significant, well-executed contribution with minor clarity/reporting issues; recommendation to accept.
