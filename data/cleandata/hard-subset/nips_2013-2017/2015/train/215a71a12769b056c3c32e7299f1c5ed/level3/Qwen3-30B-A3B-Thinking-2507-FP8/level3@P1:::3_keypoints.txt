- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-inspired adaptive gating that enables direct training of extremely deep (100+ layer) feedforward nets.  
- Addresses core challenge of degraded optimization with increased depth, enabling exploration of very deep architectures.  
- Builds on LSTM principles using transform gates to regulate inter-layer information flow.  
- Trains via simple SGD with only a negative bias initialization for transform gates, avoiding complex schemes or two-stage training.  
- Contrasts with prior skip connections and layer-wise pretraining by offering an end-to-end unified solution.  
- Technical exposition is precise and clear, with a well-motivated derivation of the highway layer.  
- Experiments are thorough: 100-layer highway nets match shallow plain nets on MNIST and outperform plain nets that degrade with depth.  
- On CIFAR-10/100, highway networks match or exceed FitNet results in a single stage with comparable parameter counts.  
- Analysis of information routing shows transform gates exhibit input-dependent, sparse per-sample activity and dynamic routing (note: reviewer flags correction that most gates are NOT active on average).  
- Layer importance study demonstrates adaptive use of depth: early layers matter more on complex tasks (CIFAR-100) while many layers are idle for simpler tasks (MNIST).  
- Originality is strong: gating is integrated into the core architecture to enable direct training of arbitrary depth.  
- Suggestion: explicitly contrast highway gates with identity-initialized/identity-mapping approaches in Section 2.2 to clarify differences.  
- Minor weaknesses: misstatement in Section 4.1 about gate activity and insufficient empirical contrast with identity-initialized layers to prove gating necessity.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution that makes extremely deep feedforward networks practical and broadly applicable; recommend acceptance.
