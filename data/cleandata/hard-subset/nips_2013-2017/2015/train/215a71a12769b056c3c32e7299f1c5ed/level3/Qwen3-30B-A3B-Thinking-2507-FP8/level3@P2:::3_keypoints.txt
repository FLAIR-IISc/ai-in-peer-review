- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-inspired adaptive gating to enable direct training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Addresses vanishing/exploding gradients and poor information flow with a unified architectural solution (no special initialization or two-stage training).  
- Extends LSTM ideas to feedforward "information highways" that allow unimpeded information flow across layers.  
- Strong theoretical analysis supported by comprehensive experiments across multiple datasets.  
- Shows SGD can directly train 100+ layer highway networks, with dramatic training-loss improvements over plain nets (MNIST).  
- CIFAR-10/100 experiments demonstrate matching or exceeding state-of-the-art without two-stage training.  
- Analysis of information routing and layer importance reveals dynamic routing and effective use of many layers.  
- Clear, precise architecture description, thorough experimental design, and effective illustrative figures.  
- Minor correction: Section 4.1 wording should be "not active on average" rather than "most transform blocks are active on average."  
- Original contribution: adaptive gating is a significant advance over fixed skip connections and is well-differentiated from prior work.  
- Would be strengthened by an explicit comparison to identity-initialized layers (with noise) to justify the gating necessity.  
- Limited discussion/analysis of the computational overhead introduced by the gating mechanism.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a long-standing problem, demonstrates significant improvements, provides new insights, and is well-executed.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis of gating cost and weaker comparison to identity initialization.  
- Overall strong recommendation for acceptance; significant contribution with immediate practical and theoretical impact.
