This paper, "Training Very Deep Networks," proposes a novel architecture designed to overcome the difficulties of training very deep neural networks. The authors introduce "highway networks," which allow unimpeded information flow across many layers using adaptive gating units inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks. This architecture enables the training of extremely deep and efficient networks directly through simple gradient descent.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed architecture, its inspiration from LSTM networks, and its differences from other related work, such as FitNets. The authors provide an in-depth analysis of how the gates select inputs across different layers, which is essential for understanding the behavior of the proposed architecture.

The empirical results presented in the paper are promising, demonstrating that highway networks can be trained to depths that are not possible with standard feed-forward architectures. The authors also provide a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including FitNets and maxout networks.

One of the strengths of this paper is its clarity and organization. The authors do an excellent job of explaining complex concepts in a way that is easy to understand, making the paper accessible to a broad audience. The writing is clear, concise, and free of errors, with the exception of a minor typo on page 5.

The originality of the paper is another significant strength. While the authors acknowledge similarities to other work, such as FitNets and LSTMs, they clearly explain the differences and contributions of their proposed architecture. The paper presents a novel technique that addresses a significant challenge in deep learning, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

The reviewer has no suggested improvements, which is a testament to the quality of the paper. The only minor editorial note is the typo on page 5, which is easily corrected.

In terms of the NIPS conference reviewing guidelines, this paper meets all the criteria for a high-quality paper. It is technically sound, with well-supported claims and a thorough analysis of the proposed architecture. The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results. The originality of the paper is significant, and the results are important and relevant to the field of deep learning.

Overall, this is a very solid paper with a novel technique and thorough analysis. It is a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning and is likely to have a significant impact on future research in this area.
