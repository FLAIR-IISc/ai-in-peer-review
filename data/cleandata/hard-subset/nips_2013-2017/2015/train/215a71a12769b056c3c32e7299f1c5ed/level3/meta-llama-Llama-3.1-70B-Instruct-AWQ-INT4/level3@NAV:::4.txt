This paper proposes a novel architecture for training very deep neural networks, dubbed "highway networks", which utilizes direct cross-layer connections, or "information highways", to facilitate the flow of information across multiple layers. The authors draw inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks and incorporate adaptive gating units to regulate the information flow.

The paper presents a thorough analysis of the proposed architecture, including its theoretical foundations, experimental evaluation, and comparison to existing methods. The experiments demonstrate that highway networks can be trained directly using stochastic gradient descent (SGD) and achieve state-of-the-art performance on various datasets, including MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed architecture is novel and well-motivated, building upon existing work on LSTM networks and cross-layer connections.
* The experimental evaluation is solid, with a thorough comparison to existing methods and a demonstration of the effectiveness of the proposed architecture.
* The paper provides a clear and well-organized presentation of the ideas, making it easy to follow and understand.

However, there are some areas for improvement:

* The analysis sections could benefit from more varied details or additional illustrations to further support the claims made in the paper.
* The paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the limitations of the proposed architecture and potential avenues for future work.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning, enabling the training of increasingly deeper networks in various applications. The proposed architecture is original, well-supported by experimental evidence, and has the potential to advance the state of the art in deep learning.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated architecture for training very deep neural networks.
* The experimental evaluation is solid and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed architecture.
* The paper has the potential to advance the state of the art in deep learning and enable the training of increasingly deeper networks in various applications.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The analysis sections could benefit from more varied details or additional illustrations to further support the claims made in the paper.
* The paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the limitations of the proposed architecture and potential avenues for future work.

Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions to address the areas for improvement mentioned above.
