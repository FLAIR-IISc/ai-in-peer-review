- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-inspired gated layers creating “information highways” for deep feedforward nets.  
- Enables training of networks over 100 layers with standard SGD, addressing vanishing/exploding gradients and initialization issues.  
- Well-motivated and builds logically on prior deep-learning work and theoretical benefits of depth.  
- Thorough survey of related techniques (optimizers, initialization, activations, skip connections) and clear LSTM inspiration.  
- Significant technical contribution: a simple, effective modification to feedforward architectures.  
- Strong experimental validation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 with comparisons to baselines like FitNets.  
- Insightful analysis of information routing and layer importance showing dynamic gating and selective layer use.  
- Useful visualizations that clarify network dynamics (Figures 2 & 4).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.  
- Code and hyperparameters are publicly available, supporting reproducibility.  
- Minor weakness: lacks direct comparison to identity-initialized layers with noise to show gate advantage.  
- Minor correction needed: Section 4.1’s phrase “most transform blocks are active on average” should read “not active on average.”  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, significance for very deep networks, technical soundness, clarity, and reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear incremental benefit over simpler identity-init schemes and the small textual error.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, impactful paper; strong recommendation for acceptance.
