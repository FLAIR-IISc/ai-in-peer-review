- Introduces Highway Networks: gated feed-forward layers y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)) enabling transform vs. carry pathways.  
- Uses negative transform-gate bias to initialize layers near identity so networks learn when to transform vs. carry.  
- Demonstrates SGD can train very deep highway nets (10–100 layers) while plain nets stall.  
- Analyses of gate biases, gate activities, and lesion studies show data-dependent routing and effective depth utilization.  
- Positions work relative to LSTM gating, initialization/rectifier advances, skip/shortcut connections, and FitNets.  
- Distinct from prior work by providing input-dependent, multiplicative routing and single-stage training without teacher hints.  
- Technical formulation is clear and sound; gating design is simple yet expressive and initialization is justified.  
- Empirical results: strong optimization evidence on MNIST, competitive CIFAR-10/100 performance under comparable budgets.  
- Methodology is careful: multiple runs, random hyperparameter search, reused settings, and public code.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized; figures and implementation details are informative.  
- Original contribution: novel application of LSTM-style gating to deep feed-forward nets with principled initialization and routing analysis.  
- Significance: practical approach to train very deep models without auxiliary losses, with small overhead and actionable initialization guidance.  
- Strengths: clear idea, strong depth-scaling evidence, competitive results, thorough analyses, and reproducibility.  
- Weaknesses: limited evaluation scope (no large-scale benchmarks like ImageNet), limited ablations on design choices, and mostly conceptual comparisons to alternative skip designs.  
- Computational cost of gates (extra params/multiplies) is not quantified in detail.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a central challenge, shows empirical benefits, provides insightful analyses, and is reproducible.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing large-scale experiments and additional ablations; partial overlap in spirit with skip connections.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to T bias and schedules; effects of learning C independently or adding gate regularizers; quantification of compute/parameter overhead.  
- Minor comments: fix typo “Tables 3 and 3” and consider reporting gate-induced compute (fraction of active transforms) as proxy for dynamic depth.  
- Overall assessment: very solid, novel, and effective technique with strong evidence and insightful analysis — recommended for acceptance.
