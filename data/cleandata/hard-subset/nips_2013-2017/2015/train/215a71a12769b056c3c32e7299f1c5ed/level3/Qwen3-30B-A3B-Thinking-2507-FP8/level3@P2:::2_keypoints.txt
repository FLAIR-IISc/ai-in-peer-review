- Introduces highway networks: a novel architecture using LSTM-inspired adaptive gating to train extremely deep feedforward nets (100+ layers).  
- Core innovation (Eq.3): transform gate T and carry gate C=1−T control y = H(x,W_H)·T + x·(1−T) to enable information highways.  
- Enables gradients and information to propagate with minimal attenuation, overcoming vanishing gradients in plain deep networks.  
- Builds on prior work but differs by making skip connections input-dependent and adapting LSTM concepts to feedforward nets.  
- Trains directly with SGD (no teacher or multi-stage training), unlike FitNets and some prior depth-limited approaches.  
- Demonstrates comparable or superior accuracy to state-of-the-art (FitNets, maxout) while often using fewer parameters.  
- Strong theoretical foundation and clear derivation (layer Jacobian, Eq.5) with intuitive gating interpretation.  
- Rigorous experiments: e.g., 100-layer highway nets dramatically outperform plain nets on MNIST (>100× loss improvement).  
- CIFAR-10/100 results match or exceed competitors, showing practical utility and generalization without overfitting.  
- Analysis reveals dynamic gating behavior: sparse per-input activity, class-dependent routing, and layer importance via lesioning.  
- Originality: direct training, input-dependent gating, and H-agnostic design distinguish it from prior NIPS and LSTM-related work.  
- High significance: removes depth as a primary obstacle, allowing principled exploration of very deep architectures.  
- Minor weaknesses: small typo and focus on simplified highway blocks, but pilot experiments suggest extensibility.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, original, impactful work—strong accept.
