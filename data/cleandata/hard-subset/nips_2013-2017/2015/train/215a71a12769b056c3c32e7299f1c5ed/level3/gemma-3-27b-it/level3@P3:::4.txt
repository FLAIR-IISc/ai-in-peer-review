This paper proposes highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate training of very deep neural networks by introducing direct cross-layer connections inspired by LSTM recurrent networks. The core idea is to enable unimpeded information flow through “information highways” via adaptive gating mechanisms, allowing for significantly deeper networks to be trained effectively with standard stochastic gradient descent.

The paper clearly builds upon prior work addressing the challenges of training deep networks, such as improved optimizers [13, 14, 15], initialization strategies [16, 17, 18, 19], alternative activation functions [20, 21], and skip connections [22, 23, 24]. It also relates to techniques like FitNets [25] and neural history compression [26], although the authors emphasize that their approach allows for single-stage training even with limited computational budgets. The connection to LSTM networks [29, 30] is explicitly acknowledged and serves as a strong motivational basis for the proposed architecture.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The highway network architecture represents a genuinely novel approach to tackling the vanishing gradient problem in deep networks. While skip connections exist, the adaptive gating mechanism, inspired by LSTMs, offers a more flexible and potentially powerful way to control information flow.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation of highway networks (Equation 3) is clear and concise. The explanation of how the transform and carry gates function is well-presented.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 datasets. The comparison with plain networks and FitNets convincingly shows the benefits of the proposed architecture, particularly in terms of trainability and generalization. The ablation study with layer lesioning (Figure 4) provides valuable insight into network behavior.
* **Clarity and Communication:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The main ideas are clearly articulated, and the explanations are easy to follow. The use of figures (e.g., Figure 1, Figure 2) enhances understanding.
* **Significance:** Enabling the training of very deep networks is a significant step towards building more powerful and expressive models. The ability to train such networks directly with SGD, without relying on complex two-stage procedures, makes the approach highly practical.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Analysis Depth:** While the analysis section (Section 4) provides some interesting insights, it could be more comprehensive. The visualization of gate activity (Figure 2) is helpful, but additional illustrations or quantitative analysis of the learned gate parameters might be beneficial. For example, showing the distribution of transform gate values across layers or for different input classes could reveal further patterns.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, but doesn’t fully detail the sensitivity of the results to these choices. Providing more details about the hyperparameter search process and the range of values explored would strengthen the analysis. While the initial bias is mentioned as important, a more systematic exploration of its impact could be included.
* **Computational Cost:** The paper doesn't explicitly discuss the computational overhead introduced by the gating mechanism. While training is facilitated, the addition of transform gates increases the number of parameters and computations per layer. A more detailed analysis of the computational cost could be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to training very deep networks.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods.
* The clarity and quality of the writing are exceptional.
* The work has the potential to significantly impact the field of deep learning by enabling the development of deeper and more powerful models.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The analysis section could be more in-depth, providing a more nuanced understanding of the learned representations and network behavior.
* The computational cost of the gating mechanism is not fully explored.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway network architecture is a clever and effective solution to the challenges of training very deep networks. While the analysis section could be more comprehensive, the overall quality of the paper is excellent. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above. Specifically, the authors should consider expanding the analysis section with additional visualizations and quantitative results, and providing a more detailed discussion of the computational cost of the proposed approach.
