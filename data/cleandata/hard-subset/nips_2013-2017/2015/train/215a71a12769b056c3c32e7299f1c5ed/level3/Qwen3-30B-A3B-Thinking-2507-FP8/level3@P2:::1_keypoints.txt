- Proposes "highway networks": transform and carry gates (T and C=1−T) enabling training of 100+ layer feedforward nets via information highways.  
- Contrasts with pretraining, distillation, and skip connections; targets vanishing gradient problem directly.  
- Builds on prior work (LSTMs, normalized initialization) but provides a direct single-stage training solution.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated architecture with strong empirical validation on MNIST and CIFAR showing 100-layer nets train effectively and outperform plain nets.  
- Strength: detailed gate-behavior analysis revealing dynamic, sample- and class-dependent routing and layer importance via lesioning.  
- Strength: thorough experiments with ablations and baseline comparisons.  
- Weakness: unclear gradient derivation — Equation 5 treats T as binary though it’s sigmoid-valued; backpropagation clarification required.  
- Weakness: initialization heuristic (constant negative bias) is ad hoc and needs bias vs. performance analysis to justify.  
- Weakness: comparative clarity lacking — Table 2 omits key experimental differences with FitNets (hyperparameters, augmentations).  
- Weakness: reporting only max accuracy; should report mean ± std.dev. to show variability.  
- Weakness: lacks brief theoretical/learning-theory analysis explaining why gates enable selective identity mappings.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, and impactful work with fixable technical issues.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant contribution that enabled deeper architectures and influenced later work (e.g., ResNets).  
- Requested revisions: clarify gradient equations, add bias-sensitivity plot, detail experimental settings vs prior work, and report mean ± std.dev.
