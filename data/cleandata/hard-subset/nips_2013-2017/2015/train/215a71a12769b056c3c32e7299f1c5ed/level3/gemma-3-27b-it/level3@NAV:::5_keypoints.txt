- Introduces highway networks: gating (“transform” and “carry”) inspired by LSTMs to enable unimpeded information flow across very deep feedforward networks.  
- Core idea: layers can adaptively choose to transform input or pass it through, addressing vanishing gradients.  
- Builds on prior depth, optimization, and initialization work and relates to skip connections and hint-based training while offering more dynamic gating.  
- Strength: simple, effective architecture that enables training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard SGD.  
- Empirical analyses (Figures 2 & 4) show how information is routed and which layers contribute most.  
- Competitive performance compared to FitNets and other state-of-the-art methods (Tables 2 & 3).  
- Weakness: relationship to other deep architectures (e.g., Inception, stacked 3x3 convs) is underexplored; may be a generalization of existing techniques.  
- Question whether highway mechanisms are an explicit instantiation of principles already present in richer per-layer substructures.  
- Suggested experiment: train a thin, tall network forked at top layers for MNIST and CIFAR-10 to compare transform-gate activations and parameter sharing vs separately trained nets.  
- Recommendation: extend experiments to larger datasets like ImageNet to demonstrate scalability.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, well-executed contribution with solid experimental support.
