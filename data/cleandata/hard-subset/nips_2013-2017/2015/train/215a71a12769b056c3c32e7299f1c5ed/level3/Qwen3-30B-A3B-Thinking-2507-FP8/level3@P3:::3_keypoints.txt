- Main contribution: Introduces highway networks with adaptive gating (inspired by LSTM) to enable direct training of very deep networks (100+ layers) with standard SGD.  
- Key insight: Allowing information to flow through "highways" avoids vanishing gradients and facilitates training of extremely deep architectures.  
- Positioning: Well-situated within deep learning literature, building on depth, recurrent nets, and prior skip/initialization approaches while proposing a fundamentally new routing mechanism.  
- Quality/experiments: Technically sound with rigorous experiments showing much better training of deep networks (100 layers) and strong generalization; analyses of routing and layer importance are convincing.  
- Minor correction: Section 4.1 contains a wording error — should read "not active on average" rather than "most transform blocks are active on average."  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written with clear notation, figures, and experimental descriptions; only the above minor clarity issue noted.  
- Originality: Novel adaptive-gating approach is a significant conceptual advance over simple skip connections; could be strengthened by explicit comparison to identity-initialized layers with noise.  
- Suggested comparison: Authors should explain and compare transform gates to identity-initialized layers (with noise) to clarify advantages of adaptive gating.  
- Significance: Substantial — provides a practical solution to training very deep nets, achieves strong CIFAR results with simpler architectures, and enables evaluation of layer importance.  
- Interpretation insight: Analysis shows data-dependent routing learned by the network, offering new understanding of deep network information processing.  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, significant, and well-written paper that makes an important contribution to deep learning.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor fixes (Section 4.1 wording and additional comparison with identity initialization).  
- Strengths (condensed): Novel architecture, comprehensive experiments, compelling analyses, clear exposition, practical advantages over prior approaches.  
- Weaknesses (condensed): Minor error in Section 4.1 and lack of explicit comparison to identity-initialized layers with noise.
