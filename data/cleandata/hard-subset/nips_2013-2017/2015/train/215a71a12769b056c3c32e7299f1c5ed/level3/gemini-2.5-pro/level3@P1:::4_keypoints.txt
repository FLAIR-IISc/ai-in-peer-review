- Proposes highway networks: feedforward layers with learned transform and carry gates (LSTM-inspired) that create adaptive information highways.  
- Gates let information bypass nonlinear transforms, enabling smooth gradient flow and mitigating vanishing gradients in very deep networks.  
- Novelty: generalizes fixed skip-connections to learned, instance-dependent gating—treats depth analogously to time in recurrent nets.  
- Technical quality: sound methodology with comprehensive experiments; optimization plots (MNIST) show 50–100 layer highways train where plain nets fail.  
- Generalization: strong results on CIFAR-10/100 competitive with state-of-the-art (e.g., FitNets) without complex multi-stage training.  
- Experimental rigor: reports multiple runs and standard deviations; results convincingly support claims.  
- Clarity: paper is very well-written, clearly motivated, with concise notation and helpful LSTM connection.  
- Originality: adapting LSTM-style gating to depth is an elegant, novel conceptual contribution.  
- Significance: enables training of networks with hundreds of layers, opening new research directions and practical applications.  
- Insight: shows networks can learn to use more layers for complex tasks while leaving layers idle for simpler ones.  
- Weakness: analysis (Sections 4.1–4.2) is limited; needs deeper study of the gated transformations H(x, WH), not just gate activations.  
- Weakness: layer lesioning analysis shows only final training error; lacks class-wise or representation-level effects of lesioning.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: solves a critical problem, introduces a novel elegant idea, has strong empirical support, and high potential impact.  
- Con-acceptance argument: internal analysis is preliminary and could be deepened (minor concern).  
- Overall verdict: excellent, technically sound, novel work that merits acceptance.
