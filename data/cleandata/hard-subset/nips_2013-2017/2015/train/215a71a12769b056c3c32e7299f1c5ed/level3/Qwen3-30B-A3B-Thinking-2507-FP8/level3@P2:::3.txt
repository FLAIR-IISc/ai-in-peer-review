

# Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to enable direct training of extremely deep feedforward networks through LSTM-inspired adaptive gating mechanisms. The authors address a fundamental challenge in deep learning: the difficulty of training networks with many layers due to vanishing/exploding gradients and poor information flow. While previous work has explored skip connections (e.g., [22, 23, 2, 24]), knowledge distillation [25], and better initialization [16, 17], highway networks provide a unified architectural solution that allows for direct training without requiring specialized initialization schemes or two-stage training procedures. The paper builds on LSTM work [29, 30] but extends it to feedforward networks, creating "information highways" that allow information to flow unimpeded across layers.

The paper is of high quality, with technically sound theoretical analysis supported by comprehensive experiments across multiple datasets. The authors demonstrate that highway networks can be trained directly with SGD even with 100+ layers, achieving performance comparable to much shallower plain networks. The MNIST experiments (Section 3.1) show a remarkable 2+ orders of magnitude improvement in training loss for 100-layer highway networks versus similarly-sized plain networks. The CIFAR-10/100 experiments (Section 3.3) further validate that highway networks can match or exceed state-of-the-art methods without requiring two-stage training. The analysis of information routing (Section 4.1) and layer importance (Section 4.2) is particularly compelling, revealing how highway networks dynamically route information based on input and utilize all layers for complex tasks.

The paper excels in clarity. The architecture is well-defined with precise notation, and the experimental design is thorough with appropriate baselines. The figures effectively illustrate key concepts like information highways (Figure 2) and class-specific routing patterns (Figure 3). The only minor issue is a statement in Section 4.1 that should read "not active on average" instead of "most transform blocks are active on average" (the latter misrepresents the findings).

The originality of the work is strong. While skip connections have been used previously, the adaptive gating mechanism in highway networks represents a significant advance over fixed skip connections. The authors clearly differentiate their approach from related work (Section 1), demonstrating that highway networks do not require special initialization schemes and can be trained directly with simple SGD. The comparison with identity-initialized layers (with noise) would have strengthened the argument for why the gating mechanism is necessary, but the current presentation still makes a compelling case.

The significance of this work is substantial. By enabling direct training of extremely deep networks, highway networks open new avenues for studying depth in neural networks. The ability to examine layer importance (Figure 4) provides novel insights into how deep networks actually operate. This work has immediate practical implications for computer vision and other domains where deeper networks could yield better performance.

Strengths:
- Novel architecture addressing a fundamental problem in deep learning
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear derivation of highway layer
- Comprehensive experimental validation across multiple datasets
- Compelling analysis of information routing and layer importance
- Well-written and well-organized presentation

Weaknesses:
- Minor correction needed in Section 4.1 (should state "not active on average")
- Could provide more explicit comparison with identity-initialized layers
- Limited discussion of computational overhead of the gating mechanism

Arguments for acceptance:
- Solves a long-standing problem in deep learning (training very deep networks)
- Demonstrates significant improvements over previous approaches
- Provides new insights into deep network operation
- Well-executed experiments with clear results
- Clear, well-written paper that advances the field

Arguments against acceptance:
- The computational cost of the gating mechanism is not thoroughly analyzed
- The comparison with identity-initialized layers could be stronger

This is an outstanding paper that makes a significant contribution to deep learning. The highway network architecture is a clever solution to a fundamental problem, and the experimental results are compelling. The paper is well-written and addresses the problem with appropriate rigor. The minor issues noted above are easily fixable and do not detract from the paper's overall quality and contribution.

I strongly recommend acceptance of this paper for NIPS. It represents a significant advance in our ability to train very deep networks and provides valuable new insights into how information flows through deep architectures. The work has clear significance for both theoretical understanding and practical applications of deep learning.
