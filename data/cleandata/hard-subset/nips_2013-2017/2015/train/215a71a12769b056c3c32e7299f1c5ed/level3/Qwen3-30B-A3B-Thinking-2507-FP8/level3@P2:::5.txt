

# Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to enable training of extremely deep feedforward neural networks through an LSTM-inspired adaptive gating mechanism. The authors propose a simple modification to the standard feedforward layer, introducing transform and carry gates that allow information to flow across many layers without attenuation. The key insight is that highway networks can be trained directly with SGD even with hundreds of layers, addressing a fundamental challenge in deep learning.

The paper builds on several strands of previous work. It acknowledges the importance of network depth for representational efficiency (citing works on recurrent networks, Boolean circuits, and neural networks), the limitations of standard backpropagation for very deep networks, and the use of skip connections and knowledge distillation for training deeper architectures. The highway network concept extends the idea of residual connections (as seen in [22, 23, 2]) by introducing adaptive gating mechanisms inspired by LSTM networks [29, 30], rather than fixed skip connections.

The main contribution is demonstrating that highway networks can be trained directly through simple gradient descent even with hundreds of layers, unlike plain networks which become increasingly difficult to optimize with depth. The authors provide strong empirical evidence through experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, showing that highway networks maintain or improve accuracy with increasing depth while being easier to train.

**Strengths:**
- **Originality:** The highway network architecture represents a novel approach to addressing the training difficulties of very deep networks. While skip connections have been used before, the adaptive gating mechanism inspired by LSTMs is a fresh perspective that enables direct training of extremely deep networks.
- **Quality:** The paper is technically sound with thorough experimental validation across multiple datasets. The analysis of gate behavior (Figure 2) and layer importance (Figure 4) provides valuable insights into how highway networks operate.
- **Significance:** The work enables researchers to study extremely deep architectures without resorting to complex training procedures. The ability to train networks with hundreds of layers directly with SGD is a significant practical contribution to the field.
- **Clarity:** The paper is well-written, with clear exposition of the highway network architecture and thorough experimental validation. The connection to LSTM networks is well-motivated and the paper provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.

**Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement:**
- While the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of highway networks on MNIST and CIFAR, testing on larger-scale datasets like ImageNet would provide stronger evidence of the approach's broader applicability.
- The authors note that highway networks with many layers can still be "shallow" in terms of effective depth (as shown in Figure 4), suggesting a potential connection to architectures like Inception [3] or VGG's stacked 3x3 convolutions. This is an interesting perspective that could be explored more deeply.
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of how highway networks relate to other recent architectures (e.g., residual networks [4]), particularly since the adaptive gating mechanism differs from the fixed skip connections in residual networks.

**Pro and Con for Acceptance:**

*Pro:*
- Highway networks provide a simple, elegant solution to training extremely deep networks directly with SGD
- The approach is broadly applicable across different network architectures and datasets
- The paper provides compelling empirical evidence and insightful analysis of the gating mechanism
- The method does not require complex initialization schemes or two-stage training procedures

*Con:*
- Limited evaluation on very large-scale datasets like ImageNet
- The relationship to other deep network architectures (e.g., residual networks, Inception) could be more thoroughly discussed
- The paper does not explore how highway networks might integrate with other recent advances in network architecture design

**Recommendation:**

This paper makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning by enabling the direct training of extremely deep feedforward networks. The highway network architecture is both simple and effective, addressing a fundamental challenge in deep learning. The experimental results are robust and the analysis of the gating mechanism provides valuable insights into how these networks operate.

The paper is well-written and technically sound, making a clear contribution to the field. While the authors could further explore the relationship between highway networks and other deep learning architectures (particularly residual networks), the current work stands as a valuable contribution to the literature.

I recommend acceptance for NIPS. This work has significant potential to influence future deep learning research by enabling the study of extremely deep architectures without the need for complex training procedures. The authors' suggestion to test on ImageNet is well-founded and would strengthen the paper further, but the current work is already strong enough for acceptance.

The authors have made an important contribution to the field with this paper, and I believe it will be of interest to the NIPS community. The highway network concept is elegant, well-motivated, and empirically validated, making it a valuable addition to the deep learning toolkit.
