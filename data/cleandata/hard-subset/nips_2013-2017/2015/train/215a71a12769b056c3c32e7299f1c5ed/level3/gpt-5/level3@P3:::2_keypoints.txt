- Introduces Highway Networks: per-layer input-dependent gates y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)) to create information highways and enable training of very deep feed-forward nets with SGD.
- Connects to LSTMs, FitNets, and skip connections while clarifying differences (gating across depth, single-stage training, learned vs fixed shortcuts).
- Uses LSTM-inspired negative gate bias initialization to encourage early carry behavior.
- Empirical: highway MLPs train robustly up to 100 layers on MNIST, while plain MLPs fail as depth increases.
- Empirical: convolutional highway networks achieve competitive results on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 with modest parameters and compute, matching or exceeding FitNet baselines.
- Analysis: visualizations and lesion studies show gates adapt across layers, implement data-dependent routing, and selectively allocate depth per task/sample.
- Technical soundness: simple, well-justified formulation with controlled experiments and extensive hyperparameter search supporting optimization claims.
- Empirical support: thorough gate analyses (bias dynamics, mean activity, per-sample selectivity) and released code/hyperparameters for reproducibility.
- Clarity: clearly written, well-organized, concise math, informative figures, and available code.
- Originality: novel per-layer gated additive pathways in feed-forward nets that go beyond fixed shortcuts and hint-based training.
- Significance: provides a practical recipe for training very deep nets, integrates with conv layers, and introduces dynamic routing across depth that others can build on.
- Strengths: elegant architecture easing optimization, input-dependent gates, principled initialization, strong empirical evidence, and thorough analysis.
- Weaknesses: experimental scope limited to MNIST/CIFAR with conservative augmentation and budgets; lacks large-scale evaluations.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated, demonstrably trainable very deep models, careful comparisons to related work, rich analysis, reproducible.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited large-scale evaluation (noted as minor).

- Recommendation: Strong accept.
