- Overall: idea well described, experiments fairly convincing, and strong analysis of learned networks' gate-output patterns.
- Gate unit T uses a sigmoid so outputs are never exactly 0/1; gradients in eq.5 are therefore incorrect—authors must explain exactly how backpropagation is implemented.
- Initialization concern: using constant negative biases by depth is unsatisfying—provide a plot of performance vs initial bias since bias acts like a regularizer favoring "longer highways."
- Clarify comparative claims: either recent studies use the same experimental setting (allowing direct comparison) or they do not—authors must state the differences.
- Reporting practice: do not report max accuracies only—provide mean and standard deviation for every method.
- Suggestion: include a learning-theory analysis of gate units, which allow layers to act as conditional identities and enable inputs to propagate through many selective layers.
