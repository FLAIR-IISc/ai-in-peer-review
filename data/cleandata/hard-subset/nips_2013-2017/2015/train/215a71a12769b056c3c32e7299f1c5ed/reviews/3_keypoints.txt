- Proposes using LSTM-style units to train very deep networks (demonstrates training up to 100 layers).
- High-quality, well-written paper with an original idea and interesting results.
- Tackles initialization and exploding/vanishing gradient problems that have historically limited deep networks.
- Experiments on information routing and layer importance are especially compelling.
- Specific correction: Section 4.1 should read "not active on average" instead of "most transform blocks are active on average."
- Suggestion: compare/contrast with identity-initialized layers (with noise) and explain how transform gates differ from that simple scheme.
- Overall endorsement: great paper and a pleasure to read.
