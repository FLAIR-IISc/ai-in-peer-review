- Reviewer congratulates the authors for being first to apply an LSTM-like gating trick to grow/train very deep networks.  
- Proposes an alternative perspective: very deep networks may be "not-so-deep" when layers contain sub-layers or more complex nonlinearities (relates to Inception, stacked 3x3 in VGG).  
- Wonders if highway networks can be viewed similarly as layers composed of richer substructures.  
- Suggests an experiment: train one thin-and-tall network jointly on MNIST and CIFAR (forking at the top) to see if inference patterns match separately trained networks.  
- Interested in whether parameters underutilized by MNIST are repurposed by CIFAR and how efficient parameter sharing is across a deep hierarchy.  
- No major complaints; paper’s LSTM-inspired idea is simple, elegant, enables training very deep feedforward networks, and the analyses are solid; recommends testing on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet).
