- Proposes highway networks: feedforward layers with input-dependent transform gates (T) and carry pathways (C = 1 − T) inspired by LSTM to train very deep models with standard SGD.  
- With appropriate transform-gate bias initialization, gradients and activations propagate across hundreds of layers, removing need for pretraining or distillation.  
- Positions work relative to prior art: LSTM gating, skip/shortcut connections, modern initialization/activation strategies, and fitnet/distillation methods.  
- Conceptually treats very deep networks as “not-so-deep” compound layers with learned internal routing, analogous to Inception/VGG but adaptive via gates.  
- Method is technically sound and clearly specified (gating formulation, dimensionality constraints, initialization).  
- Experiments show highway nets (50–100 fully connected layers) train faster and reach much lower loss than plain nets with matched capacity.  
- Convolutional highway nets achieve competitive accuracy on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 without complex augmentation, matching or surpassing FitNet-style models.  
- Analyses show sparse, input-dependent gate activity, selective behavior of negative gate biases, and layer lesion studies revealing dataset-dependent layer usage.  
- Visualization and lesion diagnostics improve interpretability and support claims about routing and layer importance.  
- Lacks large-scale validation (e.g., ImageNet) to test generalization and practical scalability under modern augmentation.  
- Needs ablations comparing C = 1 − T to independent carry/transform gates and systematic study of bias initialization magnitude.  
- Requires more comprehensive comparisons to contemporary skip-connection approaches beyond plain nets and fitnets.  
- Clarity is good overall; paper should explicitly state parameter sharing between H and T and quantify computational overhead of gating.  
- Suggests adding a compact schematic of convolutional highway blocks and downsampling transitions for reproducibility.  
- Originality stems from adapting LSTM-style multiplicative gating to feedforward depth, enabling direct training of very deep nets without auxiliary losses.  
- Significance: offers a practical recipe for scaling depth with standard optimizers and may inspire work on dynamic routing and conditional computation.  
- Recommended additional experiments: multi-task depth sharing, ImageNet-scale evaluation, ablations on gate design and initialization, and robustness to activation/initialization choices.  
- Pros: simple principled architecture, strong optimization gains across depth, insightful gate/layer analyses, clear LSTM-to-depth connection.  
- Cons: limited large-scale validation, some design choices unablated, and computational overhead of gating not fully quantified.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept.
