- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-inspired gating in feedforward nets to enable very deep network training and mitigate vanishing gradients.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation tied to challenges of training deep networks and related work.  
- Originality: applies adaptive gating from LSTMs to feedforward architectures, enabling training of networks with hundreds of layers.  
- Strong empirical results: convincingly trains extremely deep networks and outperforms plain networks as depth increases.  
- Analysis of information routing and layer importance offers valuable insights; ablation study is compelling.  
- Authors provide code and hyperparameters, supporting reproducibility.  
- Lacks detailed comparison to a concurrently proposed similar LSTM-inspired model [32].  
- Missing thorough analysis of computational cost introduced by gating (parameter and runtime implications).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity analysis is limited beyond initial gate bias discussion.  
- Would benefit from outlining potential avenues for further architectural and hyperparameter optimization on CIFAR experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical evidence, clear presentation, reproducibility, and insightful analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited related-work comparison, insufficient computational cost discussion, and limited hyperparameter sensitivity study.  
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful paper that warrants acceptance to NIPS 2015 (minor phrasing edit made in Section 4.1).
