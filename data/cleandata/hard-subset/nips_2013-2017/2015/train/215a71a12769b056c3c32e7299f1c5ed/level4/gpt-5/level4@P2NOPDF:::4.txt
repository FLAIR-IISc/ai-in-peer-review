This paper proposes a generalized approach for training very deep neural networks using “information highways,” i.e., direct connections to higher layers that facilitate signal and gradient flow. The idea is clearly demonstrated through experiments, and the authors report consistent improvements in classification accuracy.

Quality: The core idea is novel and the empirical results are strong. The experimental design convincingly shows the benefits of the proposed architecture across relevant benchmarks. That said, the analysis could be expanded with more diverse diagnostics or illustrative examples (e.g., more ablations, visualizations, or deeper discussion of failure modes and hyperparameter sensitivity) to strengthen the understanding of why and when the method works best.

Clarity: The paper is well written and the main contributions are communicated clearly. The motivation for information highways and the high-level mechanisms are easy to follow. A bit more detail on certain components (e.g., gating behavior, initialization choices, and training stability) would further improve readability, but the presentation is generally clear.

Originality: While the method builds on earlier ideas such as cross-layer connections, it offers a meaningful generalization that enables training substantially deeper models. The framing and implementation of the information highways appear sufficiently original relative to prior techniques.

Significance: Enabling the effective training of very deep networks is an important problem with broad impact across application areas. The proposed approach demonstrates tangible accuracy gains and can influence how deeper architectures are designed and optimized, making the contribution significant to the field.

Overall assessment: This is a well-executed paper that introduces a timely and effective method for training deep networks via information highways. Despite some room for richer analysis and illustrative detail, the empirical validation is solid and the contributions are novel and practically relevant. I view the paper as a valuable addition to the literature on deep neural network optimization.
