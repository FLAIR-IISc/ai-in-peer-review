This paper introduces a novel architecture for training very deep neural networks, featuring gates that allow input signals to propagate through layers unaltered when appropriate. This mechanism demonstrably enables the successful training of networks exceeding the depth achievable with standard feedforward architectures.

A particularly strong aspect of this work is the insightful analysis of trained networks. The authors investigate how these gates selectively process information at different layers, providing valuable understanding of the learned representations—precisely the type of investigation one would expect from a paper of this nature. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and presents a genuinely original contribution. While acknowledging connections to related work such as FitNets and LSTMs, the authors effectively delineate the distinctions and provide relevant comparisons. The experimental results are promising and support the effectiveness of the proposed approach. 

I found the paper to be consistently solid and technically sound. The proposed gating technique is innovative and yields compelling results, further strengthened by the thorough and interesting analysis.

I have no further suggestions for improvement.
