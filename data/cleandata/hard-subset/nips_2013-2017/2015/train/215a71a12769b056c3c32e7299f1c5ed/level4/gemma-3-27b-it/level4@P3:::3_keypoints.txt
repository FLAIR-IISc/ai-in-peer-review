- Proposes highway networks with adaptive gating (inspired by LSTM) to enable training of very deep feedforward networks with SGD.  
- Novel application of LSTM-like gates to feedforward architectures enabling unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Transform and carry gates are well-defined, providing a flexible and technically sound mechanism to control information flow.  
- Empirical results demonstrate successful training of networks with hundreds of layers and strong performance on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Analysis of information routing and layer importance offers valuable insights into internal behavior and data-dependent routing.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent notation.  
- Lacks detailed comparative analysis with other depth-enabling techniques, particularly residual networks (ResNets).  
- Needs more systematic study of hyperparameter sensitivity, especially transform gate bias across depths and datasets.  
- Limited theoretical justification for why highway networks improve gradient propagation or provide bounds on gradient norms.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, compelling experiments, insightful analysis, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and limited theoretical grounding.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution to deep learning; strong recommendation for acceptance.  
- Minor: fix typo in Section 4.1 ("most transform blocks are active on average" → likely "not active on average").  
- Minor: clarify how transform gates differ from simply initializing layers as identity matrices.
