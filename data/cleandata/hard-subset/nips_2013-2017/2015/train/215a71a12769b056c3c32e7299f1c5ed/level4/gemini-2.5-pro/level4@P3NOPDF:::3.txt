This paper proposes a novel architecture, termed "highway networks," which utilizes a gating mechanism inspired by LSTMs to enable the effective training of extremely deep feedforward neural networks. This is a high-quality, well-written paper presenting an original and impactful idea, supported by interesting and convincing results.

The machine learning community has long understood the potential benefits of increased network depth, yet progress has been historically hampered by the challenges of training. The vanishing and exploding gradient problems, in particular, have made it exceedingly difficult for information and gradients to propagate through many layers, arguably limiting the widespread success of deep networks for decades. This paper directly confronts this fundamental problem and presents a simple yet elegant solution that enables the successful training of networks with a hundred or more layers.

The empirical validation is strong, but the experiments that analyze the network's internal dynamics are particularly insightful. The visualizations showing the routing of information and the changing importance of different layers throughout training provide a compelling look into how these networks operate and lend significant support to the architectural design.

**Specific Comments:**

1.  In Section 4.1, the text states that "most transform blocks are active on average." Based on the context and the corresponding analysis, it seems this might be a typo and should perhaps read "not active on average." A clarification from the authors on this point would be helpful.

2.  A simple alternative for training very deep networks is to initialize each layer's weight matrix to be close to the identity matrix (potentially with a small amount of added noise), which would also facilitate information flow at the start of training. The paper would be strengthened by a brief discussion of how the proposed adaptive transform gates provide an advantage over this simpler, static initialization scheme.

In conclusion, this is an excellent paper demonstrating a powerful and practical method for training very deep neural networks. The work is well-motivated, the solution is novel, and the analysis is thorough. It was a pleasure to read, and I strongly support its acceptance.
