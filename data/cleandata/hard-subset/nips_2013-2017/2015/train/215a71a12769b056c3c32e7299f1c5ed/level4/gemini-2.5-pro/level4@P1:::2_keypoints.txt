- Introduces "highway networks," feed-forward layers with adaptive gates (inspired by LSTM) that enable information highways for very deep nets.  
- Gates allow layers to either transform inputs or carry them unchanged, facilitating unimpeded gradient and information flow.  
- Directly addresses optimization difficulties of very deep plain networks, enabling training of hundreds of layers with simple SGD.  
- Elegant and principled architecture adapting LSTM-style gating to feed-forward nets.  
- Strong empirical validation: outperforms/degrades less than plain nets at large depths and matches/surpasses methods like FitNets on CIFAR-10/100 with single-stage SGD.  
- Insightful analysis: gate visualizations, data-dependent routing, and layer lesioning reveal how depth is utilized per task.  
- High quality, clear writing, well-contextualized and novel despite conceptual links to LSTMs and skip connections.  
- No significant weaknesses identified.  
- Minor comment: typo on page 5 referring to "Tables 3 and 3."  
- Overall recommendation: solid, significant contribution; strongly recommend acceptance.
