- Introduces a novel architecture using input-dependent gating to propagate input information unchanged through layers.  
- Effectively addresses the vanishing gradient problem in deep feedforward networks, enabling much deeper architectures.  
- Analysis of learned gating patterns reveals how layers selectively preserve or transform information across depth.  
- Technical contribution is well-motivated and clearly articulated.  
- Carefully distinguishes the approach from FitNets and LSTMs, emphasizing dynamic, input-adaptive gating versus static/recurrent mechanisms.  
- Empirical evaluation achieves state-of-the-art performance on benchmarks while remaining computationally efficient.  
- Ablation studies and layer-wise analysis substantiate the gating mechanism’s efficacy.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear, precise technical exposition.  
- Experimental design is rigorous and results are clearly presented.  
- Minor typo: reference to "Tables 3 and 3" should likely be "Tables 3 and 4" (page 5).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, impactful work that merits publication with no major revisions required.
