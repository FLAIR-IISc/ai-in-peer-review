This paper introduces an architecture for very deep networks that allows the input to be “carried” unchanged through a layer via gates whose activations depend on the input itself. This mechanism enables training much deeper networks than would be feasible with a standard feed-forward architecture.

The authors provide a particularly insightful analysis of the trained models, examining how the gates select their inputs across different layers—exactly the kind of investigation I wanted to see. The paper is high quality, clearly written, and original. Although it naturally resembles some recent work (notably FitNets and LSTMs), the differences are clearly explained and appropriate comparisons are made. The results are promising.

I have no suggested improvements. Note the duplicated reference to “Tables 3 and 3” (p. 5).

Overall, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel and achieves good results. The analysis is interesting and thorough.
