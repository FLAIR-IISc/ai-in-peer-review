- Introduces using LSTM units to train extremely deep neural networks.
- High-quality, well-written paper with a novel idea and compelling results.
- Addresses longstanding initialization and vanishing/exploding gradient problems that limit deep networks.
- Demonstrates ability to train networks with 100 layers.
- Experiments showing information routing and per-layer importance are particularly impressive.
- Section 4.1 claim "most transform blocks are active on average" is likely incorrect; should read "not active on average."
- Suggests considering identity (plus noise) initialization per layer and discussing how transform gates differ from that approach.
- Overall assessment: excellent paper and a pleasure to review.
