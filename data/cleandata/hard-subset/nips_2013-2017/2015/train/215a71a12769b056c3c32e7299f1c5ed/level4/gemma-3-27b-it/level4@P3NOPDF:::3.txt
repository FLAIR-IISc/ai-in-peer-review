This paper proposes an innovative approach to training very deep neural networks utilizing Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) units. It represents a high-quality contribution, well-written and presenting a genuinely original idea with compelling results.

A central challenge in deep learning has been the practical difficulty of training networks with a large number of layers. While increasing depth often correlates with improved performance, issues like vanishing/exploding gradients and poor initialization hinder effective optimization. Indeed, these challenges arguably limited the practical application of deep neural networks for decades. This work directly tackles this problem and successfully demonstrates a method for training networks with 100 layers, a significant achievement.

The core idea leverages the gating mechanisms inherent in LSTM units to control information flow through the network. By treating each layer as a “transform block” within a recurrent framework, the authors effectively regulate gradient propagation and facilitate learning in extremely deep architectures. The experimental results are particularly noteworthy. The visualization of information routing and layer importance provides valuable insight into the learned representations and demonstrates that not all layers contribute equally to the final output – a finding with important implications for network compression and understanding. These visualizations are both compelling and intuitively demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed method.

**Specific Comments:**

In Section 4.1, the statement “most transform blocks are active on average” appears to be inaccurate and should be revised to “not active on average,” based on the presented results. 

The paper would benefit from a brief discussion differentiating the proposed method from related work, specifically Highway Networks. While both approaches aim to mitigate the challenges of training deep networks, Highway Networks rely on learned gating functions to directly scale the activations, whereas this work employs the more complex stateful mechanisms of LSTMs.  It would be interesting to comment on how the transform gates, driven by the LSTM structure, offer advantages over initializing each layer with an identity matrix (potentially with added noise) – a surprisingly effective baseline for deep network initialization. Briefly explaining how the LSTM dynamics move beyond this simpler scheme would further strengthen the contribution.

**Overall:**

This is an excellent paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. It presents a practical and effective method for training very deep neural networks, accompanied by insightful analysis. The clarity of the writing and the thoroughness of the experiments make it a pleasure to read. I recommend acceptance.
