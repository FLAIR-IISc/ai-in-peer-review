- Proposes “information highways”: direct connections to upper layers to train very deep stacked neural networks.
- Demonstrates improved classification accuracy through solid experimentation.
- Idea is novel and is a generalization of prior cross-layer connection techniques.
- Main messages are clearly and effectively communicated.
- Contributions are well-motivated and relevant to advancing deep network training.
- Experimental evidence convincingly supports the method’s effectiveness.
- Analysis of why highways work is limited and could be expanded.
- Recommend visualizing learned highway weights and their usage to shed light on mechanism.
- Suggest ablation studies on highway count and placement to quantify design choices.
- Need more thorough comparisons with vanishing-gradient solutions (e.g., batch norm, ResNets) on performance, cost, and implementation ease.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing the above analysis and comparison points.
