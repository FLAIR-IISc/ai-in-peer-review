- Introduces highway networks, an LSTM-inspired architecture with adaptive gates to ease training of very deep nets.  
- Proposes transform and carry gates to regulate information flow across layers.  
- Clearly explains architecture and components.  
- Positions work relative to prior deep-learning research and emphasizes importance of depth and training challenges (vanishing gradients, information propagation).  
- Shows experiments where highway networks can be trained with SGD while plain deep networks are increasingly hard to optimize.  
- Reports competitive results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of learned networks and convincing experimental evidence.  
- Strength: detailed discussion of potential benefits and limitations.  
- Weakness: using a standard sigmoid for gate T may be suboptimal.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on backpropagation, particularly gradient behavior in Equation 5.  
- Weakness: initialization effects are not fully analyzed; constant negative-bias scheme may be suboptimal and needs performance-vs-bias plots.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons lack clarity and should report mean accuracy and standard deviation, not just maxima.  
- Suggestion: provide a deeper learning-theory analysis of how additional gates affect learning dynamics and representational power.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, clear exposition, and strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable gate choice, incomplete analysis of initialization and gradients, and limited theoretical insight.
