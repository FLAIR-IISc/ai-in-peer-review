This paper proposes highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate training of very deep feedforward neural networks. Inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks, highway networks incorporate adaptive gating mechanisms that allow information to flow across many layers with reduced attenuation. The core idea is to enable layers to either transform the input or “carry” it unchanged, effectively creating information “highways” through the network. The authors demonstrate that these networks can be trained with standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to depths previously unattainable with conventional architectures, and provide a detailed analysis of the learned gating behavior.

This work builds upon a growing body of research addressing the challenges of training deep neural networks. Recent NIPS papers have explored techniques such as improved optimizers (e.g., [13, 14, 15]), initialization strategies ([16, 17]), and novel activation functions ([20, 21]) to improve gradient flow and enable deeper architectures.  Related work also includes skip connections [22, 23, 24] and knowledge distillation techniques [25, 26] aimed at alleviating the vanishing gradient problem. The approach presented here distinguishes itself by offering a layer-wise gating mechanism that dynamically regulates information flow, a concept drawing direct inspiration from recurrent network architectures like LSTMs [29, 30].  The authors appropriately position their work within this context, referencing relevant prior work and highlighting the novelty of their approach.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The highway network architecture is a conceptually simple yet effective solution to the training difficulties of very deep networks. The LSTM-inspired gating mechanism offers a compelling alternative to existing techniques.
* **Technical Soundness:** The paper provides a clear mathematical formulation of the highway network layer (Equation 3) and demonstrates through rigorous experiments that these networks can be effectively trained with SGD.
* **Experimental Validation:** The results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks, particularly in comparison to plain networks and related methods like FitNets [25]. The ablation studies and comparisons to state-of-the-art techniques are convincing.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The analysis of the learned gating behavior (Figures 2 & 4) is a significant strength.  It provides valuable insights into how highway networks utilize their capacity and offers a deeper understanding of the underlying mechanisms. The investigation of layer importance and data-dependent routing is particularly insightful.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized. The notation is consistent, and the explanations are concise and easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the authors acknowledge the importance of the transform gate bias, a more detailed exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly with respect to network depth and dataset complexity, would be beneficial. 
* **Computational Cost:** The introduction of gating mechanisms adds computational overhead. A more detailed discussion of the computational cost relative to other deep learning architectures would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective approach to training very deep neural networks.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate a significant improvement over existing techniques.
* The detailed analysis of the learned gating behavior provides valuable insights into the inner workings of highway networks.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* None. While minor improvements could be made (e.g., more detailed hyperparameter analysis), the overall quality of the paper is very high and justifies acceptance.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong and original paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway network architecture offers a promising solution to the challenges of training very deep networks, and the detailed analysis provides valuable insights into the underlying mechanisms.  I highly recommend accepting this paper for NIPS 2015.
