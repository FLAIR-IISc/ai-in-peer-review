- Introduces highway networks that use learnable transform gates (T) and carry gates (C = 1 − T) to create input-dependent skip connections enabling very deep feedforward nets.
- Each layer computes y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)); negatively biased gate initialization biases toward carrying and mitigates vanishing signals/gradients.
- Shows highway nets with dozens–hundreds of layers can be trained directly with SGD without pretraining.
- Situates work among initialization schemes, skip connections, and teacher-student methods; novelty is multiplicative, input-dependent gating in feedforward nets.
- Experiments compare highway nets to plain nets (with normalized init) and FitNets on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100, with analyses of gate behavior and layer importance.
- Clear, well-motivated architectural idea linking LSTM-style gating to deep feedforward networks.
- Strong optimization results: highway nets remain trainable as depth increases and converge faster/better on MNIST.
- Competitive generalization on CIFAR-10/100 with single-stage training and modest augmentation.
- Provides insightful analyses (gate activations, dynamic routing, lesioning) showing gating aids optimization and contributes to computation.
- Simple, practical initialization heuristic (negative gate bias) that appears robust across depths and architectures.
- Empirical scope is limited to MNIST and CIFAR; lacks large-scale benchmarks (e.g., ImageNet) to support claims about training hundreds of layers.
- No comparisons to alternative stabilization methods (batch normalization, residual/identity skips), making it hard to isolate gating benefits.
- Ablation studies are incomplete: role of C = 1 − T, independent carry gates, gate parameterization, and sensitivity to gate bias are not systematically evaluated.
- Computational costs (parameter counts, FLOPs, inference/training time) relative to baselines are not reported.
- Some training details and hyperparameter search descriptions are insufficient for fair comparison and reproducibility.
- Quality: technically sound and experimentally supported for optimization claims, but needs broader baselines and larger-scale tests.
- Clarity: generally well written; would benefit from explicit dimensionality choices, H nonlinearities clarification, schematic block diagram, and pseudo-code.
- Originality: novel application of LSTM-style multiplicative gating to feedforward depth with distinct initialization strategy.
- Significance: important contribution to training deep networks, though impact would be stronger with head-to-head comparisons and larger datasets.
- Suggest comparing to fixed identity skip connections and batch norm with matched depths/parameter counts.
- Suggest reporting parameter counts, FLOPs, training/inference time, and wall-clock training comparisons.
- Recommend additional ablations: independent carry gates, alternate gate nonlinearities, removing negative bias, shared vs layer-specific gates, and mixed highway/plain layers.
- Encourage evaluation of deeper convolutional highway networks (>50 layers) and preliminary ImageNet experiments to validate scalability.
- Ask for clearer hyperparameter search protocol, regularization and augmentation details, and minor editorial fixes (typos, unified notation, layer diagram).
- Arguments for acceptance: elegant/practical architecture, enables single-stage training of very deep models, provides useful analyses, and is easy to implement.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical scope, missing comparisons to other stabilization techniques, unquantified computational trade-offs, and incomplete ablations.
- Overall assessment: compelling and timely contribution that improves trainability of very deep nets; recommend acceptance conditional on clarifying comparisons and expanding ablations where feasible.
