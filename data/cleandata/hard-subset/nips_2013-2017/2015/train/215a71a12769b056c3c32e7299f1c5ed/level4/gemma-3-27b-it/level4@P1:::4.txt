The paper proposes a method for training very deep neural networks, introducing “highway networks” with direct connections across many layers – information highways – to facilitate information flow. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach through experimentation, showing improved classification accuracy compared to plain networks.

**Quality:** The core idea is novel and well-motivated, drawing inspiration from LSTM networks. The experimental validation is thorough and supports the claims made in the paper. The analysis sections, while present, could benefit from further elaboration and potentially additional visualizations to fully convey the insights gained. The authors carefully evaluate both the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and the main contributions are clearly communicated. The notation is well-defined, and the equations are presented in a straightforward manner. The organization of the paper is logical, progressing smoothly from the introduction of highway networks to the experimental results and analysis.

**Originality:** While building upon existing techniques such as skip connections, the highway network architecture represents a significant and original contribution. The adaptive gating mechanism, inspired by LSTMs, provides a novel way to control information flow and mitigate the vanishing gradient problem in very deep networks. The related work section is comprehensive and appropriately acknowledges prior contributions.

**Significance:** The paper addresses a critical challenge in deep learning – the difficulty of training very deep networks – and offers a promising solution. The ability to train networks with hundreds of layers directly, without resorting to complex multi-stage training procedures, is a significant advance. The potential impact of this work is substantial, as it could enable the development of even more powerful and accurate deep learning models. The demonstrated ability to analyze layer importance and information routing provides unique insights into network behavior.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks.
*   Strong experimental results demonstrating improved performance compared to standard networks.
*   Clear and well-written presentation of the method and results.
*   Addresses a significant challenge in the field of deep learning.
*   Provides insights into the behavior of deep networks through analysis of layer importance and information routing.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The analysis section could be expanded with additional details and visualizations.
*   While thorough, the experimental evaluation primarily focuses on relatively standard datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10/100). Exploring the method's performance on more complex tasks could further strengthen the paper.



Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway network architecture is both novel and effective, and the experimental results are compelling. I recommend acceptance.
