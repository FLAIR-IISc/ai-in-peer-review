- Summary: Proposes "highway networks" with learnable transform and carry gates (LSTM-inspired) to train very deep networks (up to 100 layers) with SGD, showing data-dependent routing and meaningful layer contributions.

- Significance: Tackles a fundamental problem (training very deep nets) and enables practical exploration of extreme depths, demonstrating direct SGD training of 100-layer models.

- Originality and elegance: Introduces a novel, LSTM-inspired dynamic gating mechanism that turns static skip-connections into flexible, data-dependent information routing.

- Technical quality and experimental rigor: Strong experiments comparing to plain nets and SOTA on CIFAR-10/100; insightful gate visualizations and lesioning studies that explain why the method works.

- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, precise method description, and organized experimental presentation.

- Weakness — Gate activity clarification: Request to clarify the apparent contradiction between the text claiming "most transform gates are active on average" and Figure 2 showing low mean activity.

- Weakness — Baseline comparison: Suggests comparing to a simpler baseline (identity-like weight initialization to encourage carry behavior) and discuss advantages of learnable gating over this non-adaptive scheme.

- Recommendation: Strong accept — original, significant, well-validated work likely to have substantial impact.
