The paper proposes a generalization of training neural networks with many stacked layers, leveraging the concept of "information highways" to facilitate effective learning. This idea is well-illustrated through experimentation, demonstrating its potential to improve classification accuracy. 

Quality: The novelty of the idea is a significant strength, and the experimentation is solid. However, the analysis sections could benefit from a more diverse range of details or illustrations to further substantiate the claims.

Clarity: The main messages of the paper are well-delivered and clear, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments.

Originality: Although the proposed method is a generalization of previous neural network techniques, it is sufficiently original to contribute meaningfully to the field.

Significance: The paper effectively illustrates its significance to the field, as the trend towards training deeper neural networks to achieve better results in various application areas continues.

The paper presents an effective method for training very deep neural networks by introducing 'information highways', which establish direct connections to upper network layers. While this approach generalizes prior techniques, such as cross-layer connections, the authors have demonstrated its effectiveness through experimentation. The contributions are novel and well-supported by experimental evidence, making this a valuable addition to the field.
