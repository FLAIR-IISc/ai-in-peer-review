This paper proposes a novel architecture for very deep networks, enabling the input to be "carried" unchanged through a layer using gates that are dependent on the input itself. This innovation facilitates the training of much deeper networks than would be possible with a standard feedforward architecture.

The authors present a thorough analysis of the trained networks, examining how the gates select their inputs in different layers of the network. This analysis is a crucial contribution, providing valuable insights into the inner workings of the proposed architecture.

The paper is of exceptionally high quality, clearly written, and original. Although it naturally shares some similarities with other recent work, such as FitNets and LSTMs, the authors clearly explain the differences and provide relevant comparisons where appropriate.

The results achieved by the proposed technique are promising, demonstrating the potential of this approach. One minor correction is needed: the paper refers to "Tables 3 and 3" on page 5, which appears to be an error.

Overall, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel and achieves good results. The analysis is interesting and thorough, making this a valuable contribution to the field.
