I liked the article; the concept is well-described, and the experiments are reasonably convincing. In my opinion, the best thing is that the authors have tried to analyze the learning networks associated with the gate output pattern.

(1) The gate unit is modeled with a standard sigmoid activation. Therefore, the response value is never exactly 0 or 1 (which the authors also mentioned), and the gradients of equation 5 are not correct. The authors should explain in this article how backpropagation is correctly done in these networks.

(2) Analyzing the effect of initialization is always difficult, but the authors' suggestion to use constant negative biases, depending on the number of layers, is also not satisfactory. I would like to see a plot of performance with the initial bias value. In this case, the initial bias acts as a kind of regulator, while a high negative bias supports the networks with a "long highway" (I liked this comparison).

(3) The sentence "reported in recent research in similar experimental settings" is not satisfactory. Either the methods used the same experimental setting, which would allow for comparison, or they did not, which would directly prohibit comparison. The authors need to clarify the difference in the experimental setup in the article.

(4) Giving "maximum" accuracy in the table is quite unusual and not logical from a statistical point of view. Therefore, the authors should always provide the average accuracy and standard deviation of each method (and when available).

(5) It would be interesting to study the proposed method from the perspective of learning theory. The article describes how additional gate units can be used in neural networks that allow the layers to function only as an identifier. The response of the gate unit depends on the input and therefore allows it to propagate the input through a very large number of electoral layers.
