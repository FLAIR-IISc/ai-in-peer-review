I had also contemplated using LSTM-style gating to preserve/route gradients as depth increases, but had not begun that line of work. Congratulations to the authors for getting there first.

There is another lens that may be useful: a very deep network can be viewed as a not-so-deep network whose layers contain sublayers, making each “layer” a more complex nonlinearity than a standard rectifier/sigmoid layer. This is how I interpret GoogLeNet’s Inception modules and VGG’s stacks of 3×3 convolutions, and I wonder if the same perspective applies to highway networks.

I suggest one additional experiment: train a single thin-and-tall model jointly on MNIST and CIFAR (branching near the top for the two domains). I am interested in whether, at inference, the shared trunk exhibits the same patterns observed in models trained separately, and whether parameters underutilized on MNIST are leveraged by CIFAR. In other words, it would be informative to see how sharing emerges in a very deep hierarchy, and how efficient that sharing is.
