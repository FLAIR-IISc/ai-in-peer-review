This paper proposes a generalization for training neural networks with a large number of stacked layers, introducing an architecture with 'information highways'. The effectiveness of this concept is well-illustrated through solid experimentation, where the authors demonstrate that their method can improve classification accuracy.

**Quality:** The core idea is novel and the experimental evaluation is solid. The paper's claims are well-supported by the results. However, the analysis sections could be strengthened with more detailed illustrations or a greater variety of analytical experiments.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written. Its main contributions and the mechanics of the proposed method are presented with excellent clarity.

**Originality:** While the proposed method builds upon and generalizes previous techniques, it is a sufficiently original and elegant formulation that represents a significant conceptual advance.

**Significance:** This work is highly significant. It provides a practical and effective solution to the critical challenge of training increasingly deep neural networks, which is a key driver for achieving state-of-the-art results in many application areas.

**Summary:**

The paper describes an effective method for training very deep neural networks by creating direct connective paths, or 'information highways', to upper layers of the network. Although this can be viewed as a generalization of prior techniques involving cross-layer connections, the authors convincingly demonstrate its effectiveness through rigorous experimentation. The contributions are novel, well-supported by strong experimental evidence, and address an important and timely problem in the field.
