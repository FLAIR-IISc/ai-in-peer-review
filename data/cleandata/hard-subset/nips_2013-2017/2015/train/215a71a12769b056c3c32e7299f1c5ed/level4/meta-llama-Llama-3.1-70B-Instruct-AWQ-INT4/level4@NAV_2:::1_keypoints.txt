- Introduces highway networks that use adaptive gating (inspired by LSTM) to enable unimpeded information flow and training of hundreds of layers with SGD.  
- Positions work within prior deep-learning efforts, addressing depth, vanishing gradients, and existing remedies (optimizers, initialization, skip connections).  
- Strength: clear, detailed presentation of the highway network architecture and gating mechanism.  
- Strength: convincing experimental results demonstrating effective training of very deep networks.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of learned networks, including informative patterns in gate outputs.  
- Weakness: gate uses standard sigmoid (never exactly 0/1), raising questions about gating behavior and training dynamics.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation of how backpropagation is performed through the gating mechanism.  
- Weakness: effect of initialization is under-analyzed; recommending constant negative biases by depth is unsatisfying.  
- Suggestion: provide plots of performance versus initial bias values to illustrate initialization effects.  
- Weakness: experimental setup and comparisons to other methods are unclear; ambiguous wording about "recent studies" needs clarification.  
- Concern: reporting "max" accuracies in tables is statistically questionable; lacks measures of variance.  
- Recommendation: report mean accuracy and standard deviation for every method instead of max values.  
- Overall: significant and convincing contribution, but requires clearer exposition and deeper analysis on gating, initialization, and experimental comparisons.
