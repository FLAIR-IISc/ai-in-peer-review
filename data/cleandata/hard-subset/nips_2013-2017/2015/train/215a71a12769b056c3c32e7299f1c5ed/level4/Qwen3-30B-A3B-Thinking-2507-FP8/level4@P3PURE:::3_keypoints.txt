- Proposes leveraging LSTM-like units to train extremely deep neural networks, addressing a major challenge in the field.
- High-quality, well-written, and original approach.
- Context: adding more layers usually helps, but initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients have historically limited deep networks.
- Paper directly confronts this problem and demonstrates training networks up to 100 layers.
- Experimental analysis of information routing and layer importance is particularly compelling.
- Correction needed: Section 4.1 should state "most transform blocks are not active on average."
- Suggestion: discuss how transform gates differ from an identity-initialized layer baseline and highlight advantages of the gating mechanism.
- Overall assessment: strong paper and a pleasure to read.
