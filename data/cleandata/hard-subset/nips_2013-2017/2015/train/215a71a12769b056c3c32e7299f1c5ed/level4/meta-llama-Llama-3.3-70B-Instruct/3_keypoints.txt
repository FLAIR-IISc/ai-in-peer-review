- Introduces a novel method using LSTM units to train exceptionally deep networks.  
- Paper is high quality, well-structured, and clearly written with innovative ideas and noteworthy findings.  
- Directly addresses longstanding initialization and vanishing/exploding gradient issues that limit deep networks.  
- Demonstrates successful training of networks up to 100 layers.  
- Experiments on information routing and layer importance are particularly intriguing.  
- Suggest rephrasing Section 4.1: "most transform blocks are not active on average" instead of "are active."  
- Recommend discussing an alternative initialization using identity matrices (with optional noise) and how the paper’s transform gates differ from that approach and from highway networks.  
- Overall assessment: excellent paper demonstrating a viable method for training deep neural networks and engaging to read.
