- Proposes highway networks with adaptive gating (inspired by LSTMs) to enable training of very deep feedforward nets.  
- Claims highways allow unimpeded information flow so networks with hundreds of layers can be trained with standard SGD.  
- Empirical validation provided on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 with comparisons to FitNets and other methods.  
- Builds on prior work (optimizers, initialization, activations, skip connections) and frames highway gating as a distinct, promising solution.  
- Strong analysis of learned gate outputs and layer importance (Figures 2 and 4) offering insight into network behavior.  
- Novelty: presents a new architectural approach to mitigate vanishing gradients in deep nets.  
- Empirical strength: demonstrates effectiveness across multiple datasets and relevant baselines.  
- Analysis strength: examines internal gating patterns, not just performance metrics.  
- Clarity: concept and mathematical formulation are clearly explained.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on backpropagation/gradient calculations when transform gate T (sigmoid) never exactly hits 0 or 1.  
- Weakness: initialization of negative biases (e.g., -1, -2, -3) appears ad-hoc; needs systematic study and discussion of regularization effects.  
- Weakness: vague comparative phrasing (“report by recent studies…”); requires explicit comparisons and experimental-setup clarifications.  
- Weakness: statistical reporting is inadequate—only max accuracies reported; mean and standard deviation are needed.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis on how gated connections affect representational capacity or the optimization landscape.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, effective architecture with convincing experiments and insightful analysis; well-written.  
- Rejection arguments: missing gradient/backprop details, ad-hoc initialization, poor statistical reporting, and lack of theory could limit robustness and impact.  
- Overall: strong paper with high potential; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address gradient explanation, initialization justification, clearer comparisons, and full statistical reporting.
