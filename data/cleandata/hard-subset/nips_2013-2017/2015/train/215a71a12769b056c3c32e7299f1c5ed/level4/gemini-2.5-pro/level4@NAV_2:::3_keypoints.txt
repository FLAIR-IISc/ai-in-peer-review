- Introduces "highway networks," using LSTM-like adaptive transform and carry gates to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Addresses vanishing gradients and optimization difficulties by creating information highways that allow unimpeded flow across many layers.  
- Demonstrates that networks with hundreds of layers can be trained directly with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Empirical results on MNIST show highway networks avoid performance degradation with increased depth, unlike plain networks.  
- Competitive results on CIFAR-10/100, outperforming more complex multi-stage-trained models like FitNets.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear; Section 4 analysis and visualizations (data-dependent routing and layer lesioning) give strong insight into why the method works.  
- Clarify phrasing in Section 4.1 about transform gate activity—whether “active on average” means non-zero or high activation; showing distribution of mean activations would help.  
- Suggest comparing or discussing the method versus simple identity-like weight initialization as an alternative for training deep networks to contextualize benefits of adaptive gating.  
- Overall conclusion: excellent, novel, well-motivated, convincingly validated work likely to be highly influential.
