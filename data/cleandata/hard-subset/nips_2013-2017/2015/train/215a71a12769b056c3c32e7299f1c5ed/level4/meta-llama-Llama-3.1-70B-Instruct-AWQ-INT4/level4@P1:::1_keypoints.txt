- Proposes "highway networks," a novel gating architecture (inspired by LSTM) that enables training of very deep networks and competitive performance on benchmarks.
- Paper is well-written and idea clearly described, with some analysis of learned gate patterns.
- Claims that depth is crucial but training difficulty increases with depth; highway gating enables unimpeded information flow across many layers.
- Demonstrates direct training with SGD and promising experimental results, but experiments are only partially convincing.
- Gate unit T uses a sigmoid (never exactly 0 or 1); authors must clarify how backpropagation is performed because gradients in Equation 5 appear incorrect.
- Initialization scheme (constant negative biases varying with depth) is inadequately analyzed; initial bias likely acts as a regularizer favoring long highways.
- Recommend plotting performance versus initial bias and providing a more detailed study of the initialization effects.
- Authors reference "recent studies" but do not clarify differences in experimental setups, making direct comparison difficult.
- Reporting "max" accuracies in tables is statistically inappropriate; should report mean and standard deviation across runs.
- Theoretical implications and learning-theory analysis of allowing layers to act as identity via gates are not explored and would add value.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, competitive results, clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear backprop derivation, insufficient initialization analysis, unclear experimental comparisons, improper reporting of results, and lack of theoretical analysis.
- Recommended improvements: provide detailed backprop derivation, analyze and plot initialization effects, clarify experimental settings vs prior work, report mean±std for results, and explore theoretical foundations.
