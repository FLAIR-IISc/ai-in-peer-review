I applaud the authors for exploring the concept of using the LSTM trick to train deeper networks, an idea I had also considered but had yet to pursue. 

There is an alternative perspective to consider: a very deep network can be viewed as a not-so-deep network where each layer has sub-layers, allowing for more complex non-linear behavior than traditional rectifier or sigmoid layers. This perspective is reminiscent of the inception of GoogLeNet and the stacked 3x3 convolutional layers in VGG. I wonder if this perspective can also be applied to highway networks.

One experiment I would suggest the authors consider is training a single, thin but tall network on both MNIST and CIFAR datasets, with a fork at the top for the two domains. I am interested in seeing whether, at inference time, the network exhibits the same pattern as separately trained networks for each domain, and whether the model parameters underutilized in MNIST are utilized by CIFAR. In other words, I would like to see how sharing occurs in a very deep hierarchy and whether it is efficient.

I have no major complaints about the paper. I encourage the authors to test their network on larger datasets such as ImageNet.

This paper presents an idea inspired by LSTM that enables the training of very deep feedforward networks. The idea is simple and elegant, and the analysis is solid.
