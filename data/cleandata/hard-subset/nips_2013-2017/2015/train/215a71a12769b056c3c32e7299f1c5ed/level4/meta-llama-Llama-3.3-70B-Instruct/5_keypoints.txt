- Reviewer explored LSTM-like gradient control for deeper networks and commends the authors for pioneering this direction.  
- Suggests reframing very deep networks as shallower networks whose layers contain complex sub-layers (akin to Inception/VGG) and asks if this view applies to highway networks.  
- Proposes an experiment: train a single thin-but-tall shared network on MNIST and CIFAR (with a possible top fork) to study parameter sharing and reuse.  
- Curious whether a shared network’s inference patterns match separately trained networks and whether CIFAR would utilize parameters unused by MNIST.  
- No major criticisms; recommends evaluating the method on larger datasets such as ImageNet to strengthen the results.  
- Overall verdict: the paper’s LSTM-inspired method for training very deep feedforward networks is simple, refined, and well-analyzed.
