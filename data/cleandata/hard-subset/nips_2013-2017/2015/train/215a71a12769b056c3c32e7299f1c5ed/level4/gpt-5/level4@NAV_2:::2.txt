This work introduces an architecture for very deep networks that allows inputs to be “carried” unchanged through layers via input-dependent gating.
This, in turn, enables training of much deeper models than is practical with a standard feedforward design.
The authors include a very informative analysis of trained networks, examining how the gates select and route information across layers—exactly the kind of study I hoped to see.
The paper is high quality, clearly written, and original.
Although there are natural links to recent work (notably FitNets and LSTMs), the differences are clearly articulated and compared where appropriate.
The results are promising.
I have no suggested improvements.
Note “Tables 3 and 3” (p. 5).
  Overall, this is a very strong paper. The proposed method is novel and achieves good results. The analysis is insightful and thorough.
