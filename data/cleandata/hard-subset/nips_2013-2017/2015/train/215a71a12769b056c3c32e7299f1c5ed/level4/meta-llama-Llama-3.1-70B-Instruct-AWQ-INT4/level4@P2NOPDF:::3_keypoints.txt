- Proposes an innovative method for training very deep networks using LSTM/highway-style transform gates.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally high quality, well organized, and engagingly written.  
- The idea is original and the results are intriguing and thought-provoking.  
- Directly addresses the longstanding trade-off between increasing depth and the difficulties of initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients.  
- Successfully demonstrates training of 100-layer networks.  
- Experiments on routing of information and layer importance are noteworthy and provide valuable insights.  
- Correction suggested: Section 4.1 should state that transform blocks are "not active on average" rather than "most transform blocks are active on average."  
- Suggest adding discussion comparing transform gates to simple identity initialization (with small noise) to clarify differences and implications.  
- Overall assessment: outstanding paper with likely significant impact on the field.
