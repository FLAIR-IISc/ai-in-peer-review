I find this paper presents a compelling idea, well-described and supported by convincing, though not conclusive, experimental results. A particularly strong aspect of this work is the authors’ attempt to analyze the learned networks by examining the patterns of gate outputs.

However, several points require clarification and further investigation:

(1) The gate unit *T* is modeled using a standard sigmoid activation function. Consequently, its output will never be precisely 0 or 1, as acknowledged by the authors. This impacts the validity of the gradients presented in equation 5. The paper should detail precisely how backpropagation is implemented in these networks, given this constraint.

(2) While the effect of initialization is often difficult to analyze, the proposed use of constant negative biases dependent on the number of levels feels somewhat ad hoc. I recommend including a plot illustrating performance as a function of the initial bias value. The initial bias acts as a regularizer in this context, with highly negative biases appearing to favor networks with "longer highways"—a comparison I find insightful. 

(3) The phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings” is insufficiently precise. Either the cited methods employ the same experimental setup, enabling direct comparison, or they do not, in which case a direct comparison is inappropriate. The authors must clarify the differences in experimental setups.

(4) Reporting only “max” accuracies in the tables is atypical and statistically questionable. I suggest consistently reporting the mean accuracy and standard deviation for each method, when available.

(5) It would be valuable to explore the theoretical underpinnings of this approach from a learning theory perspective. The paper introduces additional gate units that allow layers to effectively act as identity mappings. Since the gate unit response is input-dependent, this enables the propagation of information through a potentially large number of highly selective layers. Further investigation into the implications of this architecture could yield significant insights.
