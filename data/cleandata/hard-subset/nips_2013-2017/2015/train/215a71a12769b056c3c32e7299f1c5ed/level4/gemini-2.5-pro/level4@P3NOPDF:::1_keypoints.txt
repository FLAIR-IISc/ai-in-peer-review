- Proposes Highway Networks with gating units to enable effective training of extremely deep neural networks.  
- Strength: clear, intuitive concept with valuable analysis of learned gate-output patterns.  
- Major 1: Eq.5 simplification incorrectly treats sigmoid gates as binary—authors must provide full backprop gradient equations for continuous-valued gates.  
- Major 2: Transform-gate bias initialization (negative constant) is an interesting heuristic but needs an ablation study and plot of final accuracy vs initial bias to assess sensitivity.  
- Major 3: Experimental comparisons are insufficiently rigorous—clarify whether baselines were re-run under identical settings or specify all differences in preprocessing, augmentation, architectures, and hyperparameters.  
- Major 4: Reporting "max" accuracies is statistically fragile—results should include mean and standard deviation across multiple runs (different seeds).  
- Minor/Future: Recommend discussion of connections to conditional computation, adaptive depth, and learning-theoretic implications to broaden impact.  
- Summary: Promising, well-written work that requires clearer gradient formulation, bias-initialization analysis, and more rigorous/ statistical experimental evaluation.
