The paper proposes a generalization of training neural networks with many stacked layers, leveraging the concept of "information highways" to facilitate effective training. This approach is well-supported by experimental results, demonstrating its potential to improve classification accuracy. 

In terms of quality, the novelty of the idea is a significant strength, and the experimentation is thorough. However, the analysis sections could benefit from a more diverse range of details or illustrations to further substantiate the findings.

The clarity of the paper is commendable, as the main messages are clearly conveyed. The authors have successfully articulated the core contributions and their implications.

The proposed method exhibits sufficient originality, despite being a generalization of previous neural network techniques. By building upon established concepts, such as cross-layer connections, the authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of their approach through experimentation.

The significance of the paper is evident, as the need to train increasingly deeper neural networks to achieve better results in various application areas is a pressing concern in the field. The authors' method provides a viable solution to this challenge, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

Overall, the paper presents an effective method for training very deep neural networks using "information highways," which involve establishing direct connections to upper network layers. Although this approach is an extension of prior techniques, the authors have provided compelling experimental evidence to support its novelty and efficacy.
