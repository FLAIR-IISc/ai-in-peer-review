This research paper proposes the use of LSTM units as a method for training very deep networks.

It is a high-quality, well-written paper. The idea is original, and the results are quite interesting.

Machine learning researchers often find that they achieve better results by adding more and more layers to their neural networks, but the difficulties of initialization and decay/explosive gradients have been severely limited. In fact, the difficulties of getting information to flow through deep neural networks have arguably kept them out of widespread use for 30 years. This paper directly addresses this problem and demonstrates a method for training a 100-layer net.

In particular, the experiments demonstrating information routing and layer importance are very good.

Specific comments:

It seemed that in Section 4.1, 'most transform blocks are on average active' should be changed to 'are not on average active'.

One can initialize each layer of a very deep network with an identity matrix (possibly with a small amount of noise). It might be worth commenting on how transform gates differ from the highway network from this simple scheme. This is a fantastic paper, demonstrating a method for training very deep neural networks, and it was a pleasure to read.
