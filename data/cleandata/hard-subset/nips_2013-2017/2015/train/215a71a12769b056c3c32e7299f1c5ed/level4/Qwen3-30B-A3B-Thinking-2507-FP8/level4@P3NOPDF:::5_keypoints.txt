- Proposes an elegant LSTM-inspired method ("trapping gradients") enabling training of extremely deep feedforward networks with rigorous, well-supported analysis.  
- Authors successfully implement a previously speculative idea, earning significant credit for practical realization.  
- Reframes "very deep" networks as "not-so-deep" layers with complex sub-structures, aligning with architectures like GoogLeNet and VGG.  
- Suggests viewing highway/residual pathways as layered hierarchical components for better theoretical cohesion.  
- Recommends future work on parameter sharing: train a single "thin and tall" network (shared initial layer) on MNIST and CIFAR-10 to test dynamic repurposing of parameters across domains.  
- Experimental validation is strong on MNIST/CIFAR-10, but should be extended to ImageNet to test scalability and real-world utility.  
- Overall contribution is novel, principled, cleanly implemented, and convincingly demonstrated, bridging LSTM concepts to feedforward design.  
- No major technical or methodological concerns; writing, figures, and claims are clear and well-supported.  
- Enthusiastic recommendation for acceptance.
