This paper presents an innovative approach to training very deep neural networks by leveraging Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) units. It is a strong contribution, clearly written and presenting compelling results.

A longstanding challenge in deep learning has been the difficulty of effectively training networks with a large number of layers. While increasing network depth often leads to improved performance, practical obstacles such as gradient vanishing/exploding and sensitive initialization procedures have historically limited this potential. These challenges arguably hindered the widespread adoption of deep networks for decades. This work directly tackles these issues and successfully demonstrates the training of networks with 100 layers, a significant achievement.

The experiments are particularly insightful. The visualizations illustrating information routing and layer importance are especially noteworthy and provide valuable intuition into the learned representations within these deep networks. These findings contribute to a better understanding of how deep networks process information. 

I have a few specific suggestions for improvement:

In Section 4.1, the phrase “most transform blocks are active on average” appears to be counterintuitive. I believe “not active on average” more accurately reflects the observed behavior and would improve clarity.

Furthermore, it might be beneficial to discuss how the proposed transform gates differentiate this approach from simpler techniques like initializing each layer with an identity matrix (potentially with added noise). While this initialization can allow for some initial gradient flow, the learned gating mechanism in this work offers a more sophisticated and adaptive solution. A brief commentary on this distinction would further strengthen the paper.

Overall, this is an excellent paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning by providing a practical method for training extremely deep neural networks. It was a pleasure to read and I recommend it highly.
