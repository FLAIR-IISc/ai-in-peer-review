

This paper proposes an architecture for very deep networks that enables input to be "carried" unchanged through a layer using gates dependent on the input itself. This approach allows significantly deeper networks to be trained compared to standard feedforward architectures. The authors conduct a compelling analysis of trained networks to determine how gates select inputs across different layers, which aligns precisely with the analysis I would have expected. The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and original. Although some similarities exist with recent work (notably FitNets and LSTMs), the distinctions are clearly articulated and appropriately compared. Promising results are achieved. No improvements are suggested. Note: "Table 3" (p. 5). The proposed technique is novel and achieves strong results, with a thorough and insightful analysis.
