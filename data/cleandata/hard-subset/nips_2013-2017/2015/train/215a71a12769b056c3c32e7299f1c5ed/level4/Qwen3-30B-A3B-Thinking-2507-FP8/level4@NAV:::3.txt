

This paper introduces LSTM units as a solution for training extremely deep neural networks.  
This is a high-quality, well-written manuscript with a novel approach and compelling results.  
While machine learning practitioners often achieve improved performance by increasing network depth, challenges in initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients have historically constrained practical adoption for decades. This work directly tackles this challenge, demonstrating a viable method for training networks with up to 100 layers.  
The experimental analysis of information flow and layer contributions is particularly insightful.  
Specific comments:  
In Section 4.1, the statement "most transform blocks are active on average" likely should read "most transform blocks are inactive on average."  
The authors might consider discussing how their transform gates differentiate this approach from initializing deep networks with identity matrices (plus minor noise), a simpler alternative scheme.  
This represents a significant contribution to the field, and the paper was a pleasure to evaluate.
