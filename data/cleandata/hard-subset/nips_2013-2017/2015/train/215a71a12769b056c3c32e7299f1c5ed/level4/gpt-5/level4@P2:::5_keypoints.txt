- Proposes highway networks: layers use a transform gate and complementary carry gate (C=1−T) to blend transformed input with identity, with negative bias init to enable deep training with SGD.  
- Demonstrates very deep highway networks (fully connected and conv) train reliably, converge faster than plain nets with normalized init, and perform competitively on MNIST, CIFAR-10/100.  
- Releases code and hyperparameters, aiding reproducibility.  
- Analyzes gate activity and lesion studies showing sparse, input-dependent routing and adaptive use of depth.  
- Relates to LSTM, skip connections, deep supervision, and initialization methods, but is novel in providing multiplicative, input-dependent highways in feedforward nets.  
- Strength: simple, targeted architecture that directly addresses deep-net optimization via learnable gating.  
- Strength: strong empirical evidence that 50–100 layer models train as easily as much shallower ones and converge faster.  
- Strength: competitive generalization with relatively few parameters.  
- Strength: insightful visualizations and lesion analyses quantifying per-layer contributions.  
- Weakness: no large-scale experiments (e.g., ImageNet) to validate scalability and practical impact.  
- Weakness: comparisons are limited; need benchmarks against strong contemporary baselines under matched parameter/augmentation budgets.  
- Weakness: the complementary carry choice (C=1−T) is restrictive; ablations with independent carry gates are missing.  
- Weakness: gate bias initialization and scheduling lack systematic ablation.  
- Weakness: computational and memory overhead of gating (parameters, ops) are not quantified; runtime analysis is absent.  
- Weakness: some architectural/implementation specifics (conv configs, padding/stride, normalization/regularization) are under-specified for full reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: run large-scale ImageNet experiments with standard augmentation to assess scalability, parameter efficiency, and training speed.  
- Suggestion: map depth vs. width trade-offs under fixed parameter or FLOP budgets to identify when depth helps or layers remain idle.  
- Suggestion: compare C=1−T to independently learned carry gates and report effects on stability, convergence, and accuracy.  
- Suggestion: report gating dynamics (training curves for gate activity, transform output distributions, bias evolution) to characterize optimization behavior.  
- Suggestion: test cross-domain sharing (single deep network with forked heads) to probe task-dependent layer reuse and routing.  
- Suggestion: evaluate robustness to initialization schemes, optimizers, and inclusion/exclusion of techniques like batch normalization.  
- Assessment: technically sound, well-motivated, clearly presented, original, and potentially high impact, but would benefit from more ablations and large-scale tests.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept; encourage adding larger-scale results and suggested analyses to strengthen the final version.
