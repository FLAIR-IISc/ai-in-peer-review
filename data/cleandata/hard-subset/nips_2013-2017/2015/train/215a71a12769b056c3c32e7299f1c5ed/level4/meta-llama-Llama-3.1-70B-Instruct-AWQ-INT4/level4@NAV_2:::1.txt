The paper "Training Very Deep Networks" presents a novel approach to training very deep neural networks by introducing a new architecture called highway networks. The main idea is to allow unimpeded information flow across many layers using adaptive gating units, inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks. The authors demonstrate that highway networks can be trained directly using stochastic gradient descent (SGD) even with hundreds of layers, which is a significant improvement over traditional feedforward networks.

The paper relates to previous work on deep neural networks, highlighting the importance of depth in achieving state-of-the-art performance in various tasks. The authors discuss the challenges of training deep networks, including the vanishing gradient problem, and propose a solution using highway networks. They also review existing approaches to training deep networks, including better optimizers, initialization strategies, and skip connections.

The strengths of the paper include the clear presentation of the highway network architecture, the thorough analysis of the learned networks, and the convincing experimental results. The authors provide a detailed explanation of the highway network architecture, including the adaptive gating mechanism, and demonstrate its effectiveness in training very deep networks. The analysis of the learned networks, including the pattern of gate outputs, provides valuable insights into how the network learns to route information.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. One concern is the use of a standard sigmoid activation function for the gate unit T, which means that the response value is never exactly 0 or 1. The authors should explain how backpropagation is performed in these networks. Additionally, the effect of the initialization is not thoroughly analyzed, and the proposal to use constant negative biases depending on the number of levels is not satisfying. A plot of the performance with respect to the initial value of the bias would be helpful.

Furthermore, the paper could benefit from more clarity on the experimental setup and the comparison with other methods. The sentence "report by recent studies in similar experimental settings" is unclear, and the authors should provide more details on the differences between the experimental setups. Additionally, providing "max" accuracies in tables is not common and not reasonable from a statistical point of view. The authors should provide mean accuracy and standard deviation for every method.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning, and the idea of highway networks is well-described and convincing. However, there are some areas that require further clarification and analysis.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to training very deep neural networks.
* The highway network architecture is clearly presented and well-explained.
* The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.
* The analysis of the learned networks provides valuable insights into how the network learns to route information.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The use of a standard sigmoid activation function for the gate unit T is not thoroughly explained.
* The effect of the initialization is not thoroughly analyzed.
* The comparison with other methods is not clear, and the experimental setup could be better explained.
* Providing "max" accuracies in tables is not common and not reasonable from a statistical point of view.

Recommendations for improvement:

* Provide a clear explanation of how backpropagation is performed in highway networks.
* Analyze the effect of the initialization more thoroughly and provide a plot of the performance with respect to the initial value of the bias.
* Clarify the experimental setup and the comparison with other methods.
* Provide mean accuracy and standard deviation for every method.
