1) Overall positive: idea well-described and experiments partly convincing.  
2) Notable contribution: authors analyze learned networks with respect to the pattern of gate outputs.  
3) Technical flaw: gate T uses sigmoid (never exactly 0/1), so gradients in Eq.5 are incorrect—need to explain how backprop is implemented.  
4) Initialization concern: using constant negative biases by depth is not fully satisfactory—include a plot of performance vs initial bias since bias acts like a regularizer favoring “longer highways.”  
5) Comparative claim issue: the phrase “as reported by recent studies in similar experimental settings” is misleading—clarify differences in experimental setups to justify comparisons.  
6) Statistical reporting: presenting max accuracies is inappropriate—report mean accuracy and standard deviation for every method.  
7) Suggestion: investigate the approach from a learning-theory perspective, since input-dependent gates let layers act as identity and enable propagation through many selective layers.
