This paper proposes highway networks, a novel architecture inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks, to facilitate training of very deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to introduce adaptive gating mechanisms – transform and carry gates – allowing for unimpeded information flow across many layers. This addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning: the difficulty of optimizing networks as depth increases.

The paper clearly situates itself within the existing literature on deep learning, acknowledging the empirical success of depth in areas like image classification (e.g., [1, 2, 3]) and its theoretical advantages in representing certain function classes (e.g., [7, 8, 9]). It thoroughly reviews prior attempts to tackle the training difficulties of deep networks, including improved optimizers ([13, 14, 15]), initialization strategies ([16, 17, 18, 19]), activation functions ([20, 21]), skip connections ([22, 23, 24]), and multi-stage training procedures ([25, 26]). The authors effectively highlight the limitations of these approaches and position highway networks as a complementary solution.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The introduction of highway networks represents a novel approach to addressing the vanishing/exploding gradient problem in deep networks. The LSTM-inspired gating mechanism is a creative solution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed architecture is well-defined and mathematically sound, with a clear explanation of the transform and carry gates (Equation 3). The analysis of the Jacobian is insightful.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results are compelling. Demonstrating successful training of networks with hundreds of layers, surpassing the performance of plain networks (Section 3.1), is a significant achievement. Comparisons to FitNets [25] and state-of-the-art methods (Tables 2 & 3) further validate the effectiveness of the approach.
*   **Analysis and Insights:** The analysis of information routing and layer importance (Section 4) provides valuable insights into the behavior of highway networks. Figures 2 & 4 are particularly informative.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The notation is clear, and the explanations are concise and easy to follow. The authors provide sufficient detail for reproduction of results.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Initialization Sensitivity:** While the authors propose a reasonable initialization scheme for the transform gates, a more systematic exploration of its impact on performance and convergence speed could be beneficial.
*   **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper mentions random search for hyperparameters, but a more detailed description of the search space and the criteria used for selection would be helpful.
*    **Comparison to Identity Initialization:** The authors do not explicitly address the potential similarity to initializing each layer with an identity matrix. A discussion on how the adaptive gating mechanism differentiates highway networks from this simpler approach would strengthen the paper.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning with a novel and effective solution.
*   The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate a significant advancement in the ability to train very deep networks.
*   The analysis provides valuable insights into the behavior of the proposed architecture.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The reliance on empirical results and limited theoretical analysis. While the motivation is sound, a more formal analysis of the gating mechanism’s impact on gradient flow could be beneficial.
*   The lack of detailed exploration of initialization strategies and hyperparameter tuning.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway networks offer a practical and effective solution to the challenge of training very deep networks. The strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses, and I strongly recommend acceptance. This work has the potential to significantly impact future research in deep learning and enable the development of even more powerful and complex models.
