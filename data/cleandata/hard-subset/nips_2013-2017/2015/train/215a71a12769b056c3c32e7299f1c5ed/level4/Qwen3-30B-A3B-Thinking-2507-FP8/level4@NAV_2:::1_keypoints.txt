- Introduces highway networks enabling direct SGD training of extremely deep (100+ layer) feedforward nets via adaptive information highways.  
- Architecture is LSTM-inspired with dynamic gating, offering a principled alternative to fixed skip/residual connections.  
- Advances prior work by eliminating two-stage training (e.g., FitNets) and enabling rigorous study of extreme depth.  
- Strong empirical results on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 with training stability and performance matching or exceeding shallower nets.  
- Thorough experiments and ablation studies (Figures 2–4) illustrate gate dynamics and layer utilization.  
- Clarity and presentation are good, with Figure 2 convincingly visualizing information highways.  
- Weakness: Sigmoid gate T never attains exact 0/1, which undermines the simplified gradient claim (Equation 5); backprop implications need clarification.  
- Weakness: Bias initialization heuristic (e.g., bias=-1 for depth≈10) lacks empirical validation across values; appears ad hoc.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous phrasing about comparisons to prior studies—must explicitly contrast single-stage SGD with previous two-stage setups.  
- Weakness: Reporting only "max" accuracy for baselines is statistically unsound; should provide mean ± std over multiple runs for all methods.  
- Weakness: Paper would benefit from deeper theoretical analysis relating selective gating to capacity (e.g., circuit complexity or residual network theory).  
- Pro: Enables direct training of very deep networks without two-stage procedures and provides novel insights into layer utilization via gate analysis.  
- Con: Requires technical clarifications (gradient analysis, bias initialization), clearer experimental comparisons, and stronger statistical reporting to meet conference standards.
