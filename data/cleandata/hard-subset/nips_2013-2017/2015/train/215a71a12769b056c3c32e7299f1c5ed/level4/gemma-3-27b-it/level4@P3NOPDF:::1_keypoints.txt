- Core idea: novel highway-style deep networks with gate units is clearly articulated and experimentally promising.  
- Strength: analysis of learned behavior via gate output patterns provides valuable insight into highway utilization.  
- Backpropagation concern: gate T uses sigmoid (continuous) yet Equation 5 assumes discrete behavior—need explicit gradient/backprop derivation for continuous gates.  
- Initialization concern: using constant negative biases scaled by depth seems ad hoc; authors must justify the scaling and its effect on learned highways.  
- Suggestion: include a plot of performance versus initial bias to show the impact and potential optimal range.  
- Comparative reporting issue: vague phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings”—authors must state similarities/differences or avoid broad comparisons.  
- Statistical reporting issue: reporting only max accuracies is insufficient; provide mean and standard deviation for all methods to assess variability and significance.  
- Theoretical gap: paper lacks analysis of representational, generalization, or optimization advantages introduced by input-dependent gate-based highways.  
- Reproducibility/impact: addressing backpropagation details, initialization rationale, clear comparisons, standard statistics, and theoretical analysis would significantly strengthen the work.
