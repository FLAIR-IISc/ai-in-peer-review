This paper describes an idea inspired by LSTM that allows the training of very deep feedforward networks. The idea is simple and elegant, and the analysis is solid.

The authors introduce a new architecture called highway networks, which are designed to overcome the difficulties of training very deep networks. They take inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks and use adaptive gating units to regulate the information flow. This allows for the creation of "information highways" that enable unimpeded information flow across many layers.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed architecture, including a detailed description of the highway network's components and how they work together to facilitate the flow of information. The authors also provide experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach, including training extremely deep networks with hundreds of layers.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity and organization. The authors do an excellent job of explaining complex concepts in a clear and concise manner, making the paper easy to follow. The writing is also well-organized, with each section building logically on the previous one.

The paper also makes a significant contribution to the field by providing a new approach to training very deep networks. The authors demonstrate that their approach can be used to train networks with hundreds of layers, which is a significant improvement over existing methods.

One potential criticism of the paper is that it does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for training deep networks. While the authors do provide some comparisons with other approaches, they are limited in scope and do not provide a complete picture of how their approach stacks up against other methods.

Another potential criticism is that the paper does not provide enough analysis of the limitations of their approach. While the authors do discuss some of the potential limitations of their approach, they do not provide a comprehensive analysis of the potential drawbacks of using highway networks.
