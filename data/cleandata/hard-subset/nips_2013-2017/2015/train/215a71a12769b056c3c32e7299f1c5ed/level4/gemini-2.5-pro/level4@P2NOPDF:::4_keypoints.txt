- Introduces "information highways": direct, unimpeded connections that allow information to flow across many layers.  
- Generalizes prior cross-layer connection techniques with a novel gated, data-dependent formulation.  
- Provides strong empirical evidence showing networks with hundreds of layers can be trained and achieve improved classification accuracy on benchmarks.  
- Technical quality is high: core idea is novel and experimental validation is solid and convincing.  
- Analysis could be strengthened by additional ablation studies and visualizations of information flow to explain why the architecture works.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the method with sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Originality: thoughtful generalization of existing ideas; gated data-dependent flow is a valuable contribution.  
- Significance: addresses the core problem of training very deep networks, enabling deeper architectures and advancing the state of the art.
