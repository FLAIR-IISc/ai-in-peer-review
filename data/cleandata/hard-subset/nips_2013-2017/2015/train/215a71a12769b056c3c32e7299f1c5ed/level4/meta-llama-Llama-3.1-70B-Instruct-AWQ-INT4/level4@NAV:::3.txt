This paper introduces the utilization of LSTM units as a viable approach for training neural networks of considerable depth.

The manuscript is of high quality, showcasing exceptional writing and a novel concept, with the results being particularly intriguing.

A common challenge faced by machine learning researchers is the limitation imposed by initialization difficulties and decaying or exploding gradients when attempting to add layers to neural networks. Notably, the hindrance of information flow through deep neural networks has historically restricted their widespread adoption for several decades. This paper directly tackles this issue and successfully demonstrates a method for training neural networks comprising 100 layers.

The experiments highlighting information routing and layer importance are especially noteworthy.

Specific comments:

In Section 4.1, it appears that the statement 'most transform blocks are active on average' should be revised to 'not active on average.'

An alternative approach to initializing each layer of a deep network could involve utilizing the identity matrix, potentially with a minor addition of noise. It may be beneficial to elaborate on how transform gates differentiate highway networks from this straightforward scheme.

Overall, this paper presents a compelling method for training deep neural networks and is a pleasure to read.
