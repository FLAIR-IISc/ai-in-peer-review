

This paper introduces the use of LSTM units to train extremely deep neural networks. It is a high-quality, well-written contribution with an original idea and compelling results. While machine learning researchers have long observed that increasing network depth improves performance, challenges in initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients have severely constrained progress. Indeed, the fundamental difficulty of enabling information flow through deep architectures arguably delayed widespread adoption of deep networks for decades. This work directly addresses this challenge and demonstrates a method for successfully training networks with 100 layers. The experiments on information routing and layer importance are particularly compelling.  

Specific comments:  
- In Section 4.1, the statement "most transform blocks are active on average" should be corrected to "not active on average."  
- It would be valuable to discuss how transform gates in this approach differ from the simpler alternative of initializing each layer with an identity matrix (potentially with minor noise), especially in relation to highway networks.  

This is an excellent paper that effectively demonstrates a viable method for training very deep neural networks and was a pleasure to review.
