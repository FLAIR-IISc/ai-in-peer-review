- Introduces an innovative method—“highway connections” inspired by LSTM—to train very deep feedforward networks.  
- Core idea enables gradient flow through deep networks; presented as simple, elegant, with sound analysis.  
- Reviewer commends the implementation and notes personal interest/parallel thinking.  
- Proposes viewing very deep nets as fewer layers with complex sub-layered structures, analogous to Inception or stacked VGG blocks.  
- Suggests framing highway networks within that perspective to better explain their effectiveness.  
- Recommends an experiment: train a single highly-parameterized “thin and tall” network forked at input for MNIST and CIFAR-10 to study parameter sharing.  
- Wants to test whether parameters underutilized on MNIST are used when processing CIFAR-10 at inference.  
- Urges evaluation on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to assess scalability and generalizability.  
- Overall judgment: strong contribution, significant step toward much deeper networks, well-supported, worthy of acceptance.
