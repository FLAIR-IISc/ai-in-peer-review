

The paper presents a well-motivated idea with clear exposition and generally convincing experimental results. A notable strength is the authors' analysis of learned networks through the pattern of gate outputs.

(1) The gate unit T employs a standard sigmoid activation, meaning its response never reaches exactly 0 or 1 (as acknowledged by the authors). Consequently, the gradients derived in Equation (5) are incorrect. The authors must clarify the exact backpropagation procedure implemented for these networks.

(2) While initialization effects are inherently challenging to analyze, the proposal to use constant negative biases dependent on the number of levels is unsatisfactory. The authors should include a plot showing performance as a function of the initial bias value. This bias acts as a regularizer, where a more negative initial value encourages networks with "longer highways" (a useful analogy).

(3) The phrase "report by recent studies in similar experimental settings" is ambiguous. If the cited studies use identical experimental settings, a direct comparison is valid; otherwise, such a comparison is invalid. The authors must explicitly clarify the differences in experimental setups between their work and prior studies.

(4) Reporting only "max" accuracies in tables is uncommon and statistically unjustifiable. The authors should consistently report mean accuracy and standard deviation for all methods (and available baselines) to enable proper statistical assessment.

(5) It would be valuable to explore the proposed approach from a learning theory perspective. The paper describes using additional gate units to allow layers to act as identity mappings, with gate responses depending on input to enable propagation through a large number of highly selective layers. A theoretical analysis of this mechanism would strengthen the contribution.
