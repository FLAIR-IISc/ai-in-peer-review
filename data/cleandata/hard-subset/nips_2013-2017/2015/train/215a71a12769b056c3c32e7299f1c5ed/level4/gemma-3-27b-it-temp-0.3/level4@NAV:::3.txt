This paper introduces a novel approach to training very deep neural networks leveraging LSTM units. 

Overall, this is a strong and clearly written contribution. The core idea is original, and the presented results are compelling. 

A persistent challenge in deep learning has been the difficulty of effectively training networks with a large number of layers, primarily due to issues with initialization and gradient instability – problems that historically hindered the adoption of deep networks for decades. This work directly tackles this challenge and successfully demonstrates a method capable of training networks with 100 layers. 

The experiments illustrating information flow and layer significance are particularly insightful.

Specific feedback:

In Section 4.1, the statement “most transform blocks are active on average” appears to be inaccurate and should be revised to “not active on average.”

The authors might consider discussing how their transform gates distinguish this approach from simpler initialization schemes, such as initializing each layer with an identity matrix (potentially with added noise). This would further highlight the contribution. 

In conclusion, this is an excellent paper that successfully demonstrates a viable method for training very deep neural networks and was a pleasure to review.
