This paper proposes a novel generalization of training techniques for deep neural networks, introducing the concept of “information highways” – direct connections bypassing intermediate layers to facilitate information flow. The idea is intuitively appealing and demonstrably effective, as evidenced by the presented experimental results which show improved classification accuracy.

**Quality:** The core idea is genuinely novel, building upon but significantly extending prior work on deep network training. The experimental validation is solid and well-executed. However, the analysis section could benefit from more in-depth investigation. While the results are compelling, further exploration of *why* information highways are effective – perhaps through visualization of learned highway weights or activation patterns – would strengthen the paper considerably. Examining the sensitivity of performance to different highway configurations (e.g., number of highways, placement of connections) would also add valuable insight. A more nuanced discussion of the computational cost associated with these additional connections would be helpful, potentially comparing it to other deep learning acceleration techniques.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and the central message is communicated with clarity. The concept of information highways is easily understood, and the motivation for the approach is clearly articulated. The notation is consistent and the descriptions of the experiments are sufficiently detailed to allow for replication. 

**Originality:** While the method draws inspiration from existing techniques like skip connections and cross-layer connections, the specific implementation and theoretical justification of information highways represent a significant and original contribution. The authors effectively position their work within the existing literature while clearly highlighting its distinct advantages.

**Significance:** The ability to effectively train very deep neural networks is a crucial challenge in modern machine learning. As application areas increasingly demand deeper models for improved performance, techniques like information highways, which address the vanishing gradient problem and facilitate long-range dependency learning, are of considerable importance to the field. This work has the potential to unlock further advances in areas like computer vision, natural language processing, and speech recognition. 

In summary, this is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed method is innovative, well-supported by experimental results, and clearly explained. While the analysis section could be strengthened with more detailed investigation, the paper, as it stands, warrants acceptance. I recommend accepting this paper, and encourage the authors to consider the suggestions for future work to further enhance its impact.
