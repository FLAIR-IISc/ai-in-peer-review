- Proposes a novel architecture using input-dependent gates to carry information unchanged through a layer.  
- This mechanism enables training of networks much deeper than standard feed-forward architectures.  
- Paper is high quality, clearly written, and presents an original contribution.  
- Strong, insightful analysis of trained networks, examining how gates select inputs across layers.  
- Thorough analysis provides an illuminating view of gate function at different depths and adds significant value.  
- Correctly positions work relative to FitNets and LSTMs, clearly articulating differences.  
- Provides appropriate comparisons to related approaches.  
- Empirical results are promising.  
- Overall assessment: a very solid paper with no suggested improvements.  
- Minor typo: page 5 refers to "Tables 3 and 3".
