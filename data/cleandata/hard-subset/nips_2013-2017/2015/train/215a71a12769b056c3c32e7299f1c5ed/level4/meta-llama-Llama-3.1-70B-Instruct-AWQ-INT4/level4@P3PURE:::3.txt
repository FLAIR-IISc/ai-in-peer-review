This paper proposes the use of LSTM units as a method for training very deep networks. The paper is of high quality and well-written, presenting an original idea with interesting results. Machine learning researchers often find that adding more layers to their neural networks yields better results, but the difficulties of initialization and decaying or exploding gradients have long been a significant limitation. The challenges of propagating information through deep neural networks have arguably hindered their widespread adoption for 30 years. This paper directly addresses this problem and demonstrates a method for training 100-layer networks.

The experiments illustrating the routing of information and layer importance are particularly noteworthy. 

Specific comments:
In Section 4.1, it appears that 'most transform blocks are active on average' should be revised to 'not active on average.'
One potential approach to initializing each layer of a very deep network is to use the identity matrix, possibly with a small amount of noise. It would be worth commenting on how the transform gates differentiate highway networks from this simple scheme. Overall, this is a great paper that demonstrates a method for training very deep neural networks, and it was a pleasure to read.
