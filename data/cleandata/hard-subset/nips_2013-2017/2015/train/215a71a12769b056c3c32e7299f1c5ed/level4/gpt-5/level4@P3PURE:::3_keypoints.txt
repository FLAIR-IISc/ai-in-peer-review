- Proposes LSTM-style gated units to enable training of very deep networks; original idea with compelling results.
- High-quality, well-written paper that convincingly addresses long-standing initialization and vanishing/exploding gradient issues.
- Demonstrates practical training of networks on the order of ~100 layers.
- Experiments on information routing and layer importance are especially insightful.
- Typo/correction: Section 4.1 should read “not active on average” instead of “most transform blocks are active on average.”
- Suggestion: clarify how highway networks’ learnable gates differ from simple identity-plus-noise initialization and why gating confers advantages.
- Overall verdict: excellent, practical contribution and a pleasure to read.
