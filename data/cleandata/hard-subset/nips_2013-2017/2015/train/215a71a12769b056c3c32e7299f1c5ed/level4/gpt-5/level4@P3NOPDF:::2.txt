This paper introduces an architecture for very deep feedforward networks that uses input-dependent gates to “carry” activations through layers unchanged when beneficial. By enabling unimpeded information flow, the method allows training substantially deeper networks than standard architectures, addressing optimization challenges associated with depth.

The contribution is well-motivated and clearly presented. The authors provide a careful analysis of the learned gating behavior across layers, illuminating how the network adaptively balances transformation and carry paths. This interpretability component is particularly valuable and is exactly the kind of analysis I hoped to see: it helps demystify why and when depth is useful in practice, and how gating supports gradient flow and representational reuse.

The paper situates itself appropriately relative to prior work. While the gating mechanism is reminiscent of ideas in LSTMs and there is some conceptual overlap with FitNets, the architectural differences and training regimes are clearly explained. Where relevant, the empirical comparisons are fair and informative.

Experimental results are strong and promising. The method enables training of notably deeper models than vanilla feedforward baselines and delivers improved or competitive performance. The breadth of experiments and the diagnostic analyses provide convincing evidence for the approach’s effectiveness.

Overall, the paper is of high quality: it is clearly written, original, and technically sound. The methodology is simple yet powerful, and the empirical study is thorough.

Suggestions and minor comments:
- I have no substantive suggestions for improvement; the paper is already in excellent shape.
- Editorial: there appears to be a duplicate reference to “Tables 3 and 3” on p. 5; please correct.

In summary, this is a solid and impactful contribution. The proposed technique is novel, practical, and well-validated, and the analysis sheds useful light on the behavior of very deep gated networks. I recommend acceptance.
