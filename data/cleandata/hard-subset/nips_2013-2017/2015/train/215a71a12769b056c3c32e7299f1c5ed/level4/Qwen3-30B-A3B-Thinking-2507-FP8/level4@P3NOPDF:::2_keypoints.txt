- Introduces a novel feedforward architecture with input-dependent gating that lets inputs be carried unchanged through layers.  
- Addresses vanishing gradients, enabling training of much deeper networks than standard feedforward models.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on benchmarks while remaining computationally efficient.  
- Thorough analysis of gate behavior across layers reveals meaningful information-flow patterns and offers theoretical/practical insights.  
- Clear articulation of distinctions from related work (FitNets, LSTMs): no recurrence, uniform feedforward gating, no auxiliary tasks.  
- Experiments are robust: comprehensive baselines, effective ablation studies isolating the gating contribution.  
- Figures and tables (e.g., Fig.4, Tab.3) convincingly show how gates evolve to preserve or transform information.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, original, technically sound, and accessible.
