This paper introduces a novel approach to training deep neural networks, leveraging the concept of “information highways” – direct connections to higher layers – to facilitate learning. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this technique through compelling experimental results, showing improvements in classification accuracy. The core idea represents a valuable generalization of existing neural network training methods, such as those employing cross-layer connections. 

In terms of quality, the work presents a solid experimental foundation, though the analysis section could benefit from expanded detail or illustrative examples. The paper is clearly written, effectively conveying its central message. The proposed method demonstrates sufficient originality while building upon established techniques. 

The significance of this work is clear; as the trend towards increasingly deep networks continues to drive progress in many application areas, effective training methods become paramount. This paper offers a promising contribution in that regard, providing an affective method for training very deep architectures. The contributions are both novel and well-validated through experimentation.
