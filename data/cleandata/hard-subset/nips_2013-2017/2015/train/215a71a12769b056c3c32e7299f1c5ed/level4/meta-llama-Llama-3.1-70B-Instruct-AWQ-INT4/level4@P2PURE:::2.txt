This paper proposes a novel architecture for very deep networks, enabling the input to be "carried" unchanged through a layer using input-dependent gates. This design facilitates the training of much deeper networks than would be feasible with a standard feed-forward architecture.

The authors conduct an intriguing analysis of the trained networks to investigate how the gates select their inputs across different layers, which is a crucial aspect of the paper that I appreciate. The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and original.

Although it bears some resemblance to recent works (notably FitNets and LSTMs), the authors clearly explain the differences and provide relevant comparisons where applicable. The results obtained are promising.

I do not have any suggested improvements for the paper. However, I would like to point out a minor error: the paper refers to "Tables 3 and 3" on page 5, which appears to be a typographical mistake.

Overall, this is a very solid paper that presents a novel technique, achieves good results, and provides an interesting and thorough analysis.
