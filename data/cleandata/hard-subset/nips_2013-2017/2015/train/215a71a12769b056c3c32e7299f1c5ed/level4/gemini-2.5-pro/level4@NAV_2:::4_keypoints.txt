- Proposes "highway networks": adaptive gating units (inspired by LSTM) that let signals be carried unchanged or transformed across layers.  
- Aimed at solving optimization issues (poor gradient propagation) in very deep feedforward networks.  
- Positions the approach relative to prior work (skip-connections, initialization, hint-based training) as a more flexible, learnable alternative.  
- High technical quality with well-designed experiments demonstrating optimization improvements up to 50–100 layers.  
- CIFAR-10/100 results show strong generalization, competitive with FitNets without complex multi-stage training.  
- Section 4’s gate visualizations and lesion study offer valuable insights into the network’s learned behavior.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized; highway layer is explained both intuitively and formally (Eq. 3), figures aid understanding.  
- Novelty: makes bypassing dynamic and data-dependent via learnable gates, adapting LSTM gating to feedforward spatial context.  
- Significance: enables direct SGD training of very deep networks and shows the model can modulate effective depth by task complexity.  
- Strong arguments for acceptance: elegant, effective architecture with rigorous analysis and high potential impact.  
- Weakness: analysis of gate behavior is based on post-training statistics; lacks study of gate dynamics during training.
