- Introduces highway networks that enable training very deep feedforward nets using LSTM-inspired gating.  
- Core idea: adaptive transform/carry gates create “information highways” to mitigate vanishing gradients and poor activation propagation.  
- Situates work among prior efforts (optimizers, initialization, activations, skip connections, knowledge distillation) and relates to FitNets.  
- Novelty: applying LSTM-like gating to feedforward architectures is a significant, original contribution.  
- Soundness: theoretical justification is convincing and experiments show successful training to much greater depths with standard SGD.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear motivation, architecture description, and accessible math/notation.  
- Experimental validation: strong empirical results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, competitive with existing methods.  
- Analysis: transform-gate behavior analysis reveals dynamic routing and effective capacity usage.  
- Weakness — limited scope: experiments are on relatively small datasets; lacks large-scale (e.g., ImageNet) evaluation.  
- Weakness — alternative perspectives: could better connect to Inception, VGG, and views of deep nets as complex sub-layers.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: transform gate bias and other hyperparameters not thoroughly investigated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a core deep-learning problem with a novel, effective architecture and convincing results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evaluation scale and insufficient exploration of connections to related architectures.  
- Overall: strong paper; novelty, soundness, and clarity justify acceptance to NIPS 2015 (recommend accept).  
- Suggestion: evaluate a single thin-and-tall network across multiple datasets to probe representations and transfer potential.
