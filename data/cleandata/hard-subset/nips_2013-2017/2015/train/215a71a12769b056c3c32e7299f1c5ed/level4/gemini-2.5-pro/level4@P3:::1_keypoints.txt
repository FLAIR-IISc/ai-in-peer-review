- Introduces highway networks with transform and carry gates that create information "highways" allowing training of very deep feedforward models.  
- Empirical results show highway networks can be trained with SGD to depths up to ~100 layers and outperform plain networks of similar depth.  
- Architecture is positioned as a dynamic, data-dependent gating alternative to initialization tricks, optimizers, and static skip-connections.  
- Strong originality: learnable, input-dependent gating is a significant conceptual advance enabling exploration of greater depth.  
- High-quality analysis: detailed visualizations and lesioning studies show learned gate behavior and adaptive use of depth per task.  
- Strong empirical validation: optimization experiments (MNIST) and competitive CIFAR-10/100 results; single-stage deep thin networks outperform FitNets.  
- Clarity on backpropagation is lacking: paper uses an idealized T∈{0,1} case but should explicitly show gradient/backprop equations for sigmoid gates.  
- Gate bias initialization is ad-hoc (depth-dependent negative biases); needs systematic sensitivity analysis and guidance.  
- Experimental comparisons need clearer alignment with prior work (e.g., data augmentation, optimization schedules) to ensure fairness.  
- Inconsistent result reporting (claimed mean±std but tables show best-of-5); recommend reporting mean and standard deviation for robustness.  
- Overall conclusion: significant and well-supported paper with minor clarity and reporting issues; recommended for acceptance.
