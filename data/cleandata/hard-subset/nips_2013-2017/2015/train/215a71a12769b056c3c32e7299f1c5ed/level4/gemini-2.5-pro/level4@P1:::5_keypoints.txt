- Introduces Highway Networks, a feedforward architecture using adaptive gates to enable training of extremely deep networks.  
- Gates are inspired by LSTM and create "information highways" that regulate/allow unimpeded data flow across many layers.  
- Empirical evidence shows Highway Networks with tens or hundreds of layers can be trained directly with standard SGD.  
- Authors provide solid analysis demonstrating how these networks learn to utilize depth; the layer lesioning study is particularly insightful.  
- Visualizations of data-dependent information routing further support the contribution.  
- Main strength: simple, elegant, and highly effective solution; novel adaptation of LSTM gating to feedforward nets with substantial impact.  
- Direct comparisons with plain networks clearly show optimization advantages as depth increases.  
- Paper is well-written and has no major flaws; reviewer’s comments are constructive suggestions.  
- Suggest discussing an alternative perspective: deep nets as shorter stacks of more complex blocks (e.g., Inception, VGG) and how Highway Networks fit or challenge that view.  
- Recommend a multi-task experiment (shared trunk for MNIST and CIFAR-100) to test dynamic routing and efficient feature sharing.  
- Advise evaluating on a large-scale benchmark (e.g., ImageNet) to fully demonstrate the architecture’s potential for 100+ layer models.  
- Overall assessment: excellent paper with strong empirical support and insightful analysis; recommend acceptance.
