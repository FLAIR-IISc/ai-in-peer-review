- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-style transform gates T(x) blend H(x) and input x allowing layers to interpolate between transform and carry.  
- Simple negative-bias initialization of T enables training of very deep (50–100+) feedforward networks with plain SGD.  
- Experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10/100 show strong optimization, faster convergence, and competitive generalization without special tricks.  
- Analyses include gate-activity visualizations, data-dependent routing, and lesion studies demonstrating layer importance.  
- Architecture is clearly defined and technically sound; claims are supported by experiments and analyses.  
- Empirical comparisons convincingly show depth hurts plain nets but not highway nets; convergence speed improvements are demonstrated.  
- Limited ablations: comparisons focus on plain nets and FitNets; missing fixed shortcuts, varying carry formulations (C ≠ 1 − T), and more extensive gate-bias sensitivity studies.  
- No large-scale (e.g., ImageNet-level) evaluation included to strengthen significance.  
- Paper is well written; figures effectively illustrate routing and layer contributions.  
- Suggest adding discussion of computational overhead from gates and a concise parameter/ops comparison to baselines.  
- Core idea is novel: input-dependent LSTM-style gating applied to deep feedforward networks, going beyond static skip connections.  
- Practical significance: provides a simple recipe (architecture + init) to scale depth without pretraining or teacher signals.  
- Pros: simple elegant change, trains very deep nets with SGD, competitive CIFAR results, insightful analyses, available code.  
- Cons: limited ablations and comparisons, sensitivity to gate-bias initialization underexplored, experiments limited to small/medium datasets.  
- Specific comment: clarify Section 4.1 wording about transform-gate activity (average vs. per-sample sparsity) and quantify average T across layers/classes.  
- Suggest an ablation contrasting identity-initialized static skips with adaptive highway gates to highlight benefits (gradient preservation, per-sample routing).  
- Minor: fix typos (e.g., “backpropogation” → “backpropagation”).  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — original, practically important contribution with convincing empirical and analytical support.
