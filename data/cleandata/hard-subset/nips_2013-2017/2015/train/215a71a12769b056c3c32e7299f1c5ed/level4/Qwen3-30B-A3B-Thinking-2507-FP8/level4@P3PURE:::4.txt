

The paper proposes a generalization of training very deep neural networks through the introduction of "information highways," which establish direct connections to upper network layers. The core idea is effectively illustrated through experimentation, with the authors demonstrating improved classification accuracy using this approach. The method represents a meaningful generalization of prior techniques, such as cross-layer connections, and its effectiveness is well-supported by empirical results.  

The contributions are sufficiently novel, and the experimental evaluation is robust and comprehensive. While the analysis could benefit from additional illustrative examples or deeper technical exploration, the overall methodology and results are clearly presented. The main messages of the paper are effectively communicated, ensuring clarity for the reader.  

The work addresses a significant challenge in the field: the need to train increasingly deeper neural networks to achieve better performance in practical applications. By providing an effective framework for training very deep architectures, the paper demonstrates clear relevance and potential impact for advancing deep learning systems.
