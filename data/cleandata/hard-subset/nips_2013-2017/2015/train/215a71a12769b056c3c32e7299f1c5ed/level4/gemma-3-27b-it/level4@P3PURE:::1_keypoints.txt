- Paper presents a compelling idea, well-described with convincing (but not conclusive) experiments; strong contribution is analysis of learned networks via gate-output patterns.

- (1) Gate T uses a sigmoid and thus never outputs exact 0/1, which undermines the gradients in Eq. 5—authors must explain precisely how backpropagation is implemented given this.

- (2) The constant negative-bias initialization tied to depth seems ad hoc; include a plot of performance vs initial bias since the bias acts as a regularizer favoring "longer highways."

- (3) The phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings” is imprecise—clarify whether cited methods use the same setup or explain the experimental differences.

- (4) Reporting only max accuracies is statistically questionable—report mean accuracy and standard deviation (when available) for each method.

- (5) Recommend deeper theoretical analysis from a learning-theory perspective: input-dependent gates enabling identity mappings and selective deep propagation deserve further investigation.
