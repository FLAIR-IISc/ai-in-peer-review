This paper proposes a novel architecture for training very deep neural networks, inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks. The idea of using adaptive gating units to regulate information flow across layers is original and well-presented.

The authors provide a thorough overview of the challenges associated with training deep neural networks, including the difficulties of initialization and decaying/exploding gradients. They demonstrate how their proposed architecture, called highway networks, can effectively overcome these challenges and enable the training of extremely deep and efficient architectures.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed architecture and its benefits. The experimental results are also well-presented and provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of highway networks. In particular, the experiments showing the routing of information and layer importance are insightful and demonstrate the potential of highway networks for deep learning.

Specific comments:

* In Section 4.1, it seems that the statement "most transform blocks are active on average" should be changed to "most transform blocks are not active on average".
* One potential criticism of highway networks is that they may not be significantly different from a simple scheme where each layer is initialized with the identity matrix (possibly with a small amount of noise). It might be worth commenting on how the transform gates in highway networks differentiate them from this simple scheme.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that demonstrates a method for training very deep neural networks. The idea is original, and the results are interesting and well-presented.

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

**Clarity**: The paper is clearly written and well-organized. The authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

**Originality**: The problems or approaches presented in the paper are new and original. The use of LSTM-inspired adaptive gating units to regulate information flow across layers is a novel contribution to the field.

**Significance**: The results presented in the paper are important and have the potential to impact the field of deep learning. The ability to train extremely deep and efficient architectures is a significant advancement, and the paper demonstrates the potential of highway networks for solving complex problems.

**Arguments Against Acceptance**:
