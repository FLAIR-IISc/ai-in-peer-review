

This paper introduces a novel approach for training extremely deep neural networks using gating mechanisms inspired by LSTMs, addressing the longstanding challenges of vanishing/exploding gradients and poor information flow in deep architectures. The work is exceptionally well-executed: the core idea is both original and highly relevant to the field, the technical presentation is clear and rigorous, and the empirical results provide compelling evidence for the method's effectiveness in training networks exceeding 100 layers.  

The authors effectively contextualize their contribution within the historical difficulty of scaling neural networks, noting that initialization challenges and gradient instability have historically hindered practical deployment for decades. Their solution—leveraging transform gates to selectively route information across layers—directly tackles these barriers. The experimental results, particularly the analysis of layer-wise information routing and the quantification of layer importance, are especially compelling. These insights not only validate the method’s technical soundness but also offer valuable interpretability, making the findings significantly more impactful than a mere performance benchmark.  

The paper is exceptionally well-written, with a logical flow that guides the reader from problem statement through methodological innovation to rigorous evaluation. The writing is precise, concise, and free of unnecessary jargon, enhancing readability without sacrificing technical depth. The results are presented with clarity, and the ablation studies adequately support the claimed advantages of the proposed approach.  

**Specific comments for improvement:**  
1.  In Section 4.1, the statement "most transform blocks are active on average" appears contradictory to the subsequent analysis. Given the paper’s findings on layer importance, it should read: "most transform blocks are *not* active on average," reflecting the observation that many gates predominantly pass information unchanged. This correction is crucial for accuracy.  
2.  The authors could strengthen their discussion by briefly comparing their transform gates to a simpler initialization strategy (e.g., initializing each layer with the identity matrix, possibly with minor noise). A short clarification on how the gating mechanism fundamentally differs from this baseline—particularly in enabling *adaptive* information flow rather than static identity mapping—would further underscore the novelty of their contribution.  

This is a strong, high-impact paper that makes a significant contribution to deep learning. The method is clearly superior to prior approaches for training very deep networks, the experiments are thorough and well-designed, and the writing is exemplary. It represents a substantial step forward in overcoming the practical limitations of deep architectures and is highly recommended for publication. The authors have delivered a clear, original, and technically sound advance that will likely influence future work in deep network design.
