- Reviewer had independently considered LSTM-style gating for preserving/ routing gradients and congratulates the authors for being first to realize it.
- Suggests viewing very deep networks as shallower networks whose layers contain sublayers (more complex nonlinearities), as in Inception/VGG, and asks if this perspective fits highway networks.
- Recommends an experiment: jointly train a thin-and-tall model on MNIST and CIFAR with a branch near the top to study shared-trunk behavior and parameter reuse.
- Wants to know whether the shared trunk at inference shows the same patterns as separately trained models and whether CIFAR leverages parameters underutilized by MNIST.
- No major complaints; encourages evaluation on a larger dataset such as ImageNet.
- Overall assessment: the LSTM-inspired mechanism is simple, elegant, enables training very deep feedforward networks, and the analyses are solid.
