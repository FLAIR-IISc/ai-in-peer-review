

I find the paper well-motivated and clearly presented, with experiments that provide reasonable empirical support. A notable strength is the authors' analysis of learned networks through the pattern of gate outputs.

(1) The gate unit T employs a standard sigmoid activation, meaning its response values never exactly reach 0 or 1 (as acknowledged by the authors). Consequently, the gradients derived in Eq. (5) are mathematically inconsistent. The authors must clarify how backpropagation is implemented in these networks.

(2) While initialization effects are inherently complex to analyze, the proposed strategy of using constant negative biases dependent on the number of levels is unsatisfying. The authors should include a plot showing performance as a function of the initial bias value. This bias acts as a regularizer, where a more negative initial value encourages networks with "longer highways" (a useful analogy).

(3) The phrase "report by recent studies in similar experimental settings" is ambiguous. If experimental setups are identical, direct comparison is possible; otherwise, it is invalid. The authors must explicitly clarify differences in experimental configurations relative to prior work.

(4) Reporting maximum accuracies in tables is statistically inappropriate and uncommon. The authors should consistently report mean accuracy and standard deviation for each method (and available data).

(5) The paper would benefit from a learning theory analysis of the proposed approach. Specifically, the mechanism of using gate units to enable layers to act as identities—where gate responses depend on inputs, allowing propagation through many selective layers—deserves theoretical exploration.
