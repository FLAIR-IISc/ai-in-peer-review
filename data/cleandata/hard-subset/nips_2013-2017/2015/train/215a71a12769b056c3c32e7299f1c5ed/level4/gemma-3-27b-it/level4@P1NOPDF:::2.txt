This paper proposes a novel architecture for training very deep networks, allowing inputs to be carried through layers largely unchanged via gates dependent on the input itself. This approach enables the successful training of significantly deeper networks than is typically possible with standard feedforward architectures.

The authors provide a particularly interesting analysis of the trained networks, investigating how the gates select their inputs at different layers – precisely the kind of analysis I would expect and appreciate. 

This is a high-quality paper; it is clearly written, original, and demonstrates a strong technical contribution. While there are naturally similarities to recent related work, such as FitNets and LSTMs, these are clearly acknowledged and appropriately compared. The results are promising and demonstrate the potential of the proposed approach.

I have no specific suggestions for improvement. Please note a minor typographical error on page 5: “Tables 3 and 3” should be corrected.

Overall, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel and achieves compelling results. The analysis is both interesting and thorough.
