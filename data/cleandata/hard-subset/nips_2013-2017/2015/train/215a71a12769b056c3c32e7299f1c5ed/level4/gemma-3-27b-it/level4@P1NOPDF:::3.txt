This paper proposes leveraging LSTM units to facilitate the training of very deep networks. 

This is a high-quality and well-written paper. The idea is original, and the results are quite interesting. 

Machine learning researchers have consistently observed improved performance with increasing neural network depth. However, challenges related to initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients have severely limited this progress. Indeed, difficulties in enabling effective information flow through deep networks arguably hindered their widespread adoption for three decades. This paper directly addresses this problem and demonstrates a method for training networks with 100 layers.

In particular, the experiments illustrating information routing and layer importance are compelling.

Specific comments:

In Section 4.1, the statement “most transform blocks are active on average” appears to be incorrect and should likely read “not active on average.”

One potential initialization strategy for very deep networks involves initializing each layer with an identity matrix (perhaps with a small amount of noise). It might be worthwhile to discuss how transform gates differentiate this approach from highway networks. 

Overall, this is a strong paper demonstrating a successful method for training very deep neural networks, and it was a pleasure to read.
