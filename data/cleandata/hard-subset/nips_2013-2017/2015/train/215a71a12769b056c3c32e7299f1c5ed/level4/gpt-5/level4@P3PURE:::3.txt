This paper proposes using LSTM-style gated units to enable the training of very deep networks. It is a high-quality, well-written paper. The idea is original, and the results are compelling. Machine learning researchers often observe improved performance by increasing network depth, but initialization challenges and vanishing/exploding gradients have been major obstacles—arguably limiting deep networks for decades. This paper addresses the problem directly and convincingly demonstrates a method for training networks with on the order of 100 layers. The experiments on information routing and layer importance are particularly insightful.

Specific comments:
- In Section 4.1, the phrase “most transform blocks are active on average” appears to be a mistake; it should read “not active on average.”
- It would be helpful to clarify how highway networks, with their learnable transform gates, differ from a simpler scheme that initializes each layer as (approximately) the identity matrix (e.g., identity plus small noise). A brief discussion of why gating confers advantages over identity initialization alone would strengthen the positioning.

Overall, this is an excellent paper that presents a practical method for training very deep neural networks. It was a pleasure to read.
