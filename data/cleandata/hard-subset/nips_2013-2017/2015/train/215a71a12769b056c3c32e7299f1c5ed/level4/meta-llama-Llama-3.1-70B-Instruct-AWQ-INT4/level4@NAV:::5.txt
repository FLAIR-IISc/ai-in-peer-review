I commend the authors for pioneering the use of the LSTM trick to train deeper networks, an approach I had also considered but hadn't yet explored. 

An alternative perspective on this concept is that a very deep network can be viewed as a not-so-deep network with layers that have sub-layers, each exhibiting more complex non-linear behavior than traditional rectifier or sigmoid layers. This perspective resonates with the inception of GoogLeNet and the stacked 3x3 layers in VGG, and I wonder if it can also be applied to highway networks.

One experiment I suggest the authors consider is training a single thin and tall network on both MNIST and CIFAR datasets (with a fork at the top for the two domains). I am curious to see whether, at inference time, the network exhibits the same pattern as separately trained networks for each domain and whether underutilized model parameters in MNIST are leveraged by CIFAR. Essentially, I would like to see how sharing occurs in a very deep hierarchy and whether it is as efficient as possible.

I have no major criticisms of the paper. I encourage the authors to test their network on larger datasets, such as ImageNet.

The paper presents an LSTM-inspired idea for training very deep feedforward networks. The concept is straightforward yet elegant, and the analysis is sound.
