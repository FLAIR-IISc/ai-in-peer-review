This paper proposes Highway Networks, a novel architecture inspired by the gating mechanisms of LSTMs, to enable the effective training of extremely deep neural networks. This is a high-quality, well-written paper that presents an original idea with compelling experimental results.

The machine learning community has long recognized that network depth is a crucial component for performance, yet progress has been severely limited by optimization difficulties such as vanishing and exploding gradients. The fundamental challenge of propagating information and gradients through many layers has arguably hindered the widespread use of very deep architectures for decades. This paper addresses this long-standing problem head-on, demonstrating a straightforward and effective method for training networks with hundreds of layers.

The experiments analyzing the dynamic routing of information and the learned importance of individual layers are particularly insightful.

Specific comments:

*   In Section 4.1, there may be a typo. The text states that ‘most transform blocks are active on average,’ but the surrounding context and figures seem to suggest the opposite should be true (i.e., ‘not active on average’). Please clarify or correct this.

*   The paper would be further strengthened by a brief discussion contrasting the proposed architecture with a simpler baseline, such as a standard deep network where each layer is initialized to be near the identity function (e.g., using an identity matrix with small-scale noise). While such an initialization might also ease optimization, a key advantage of highway networks appears to be their ability to *learn* to route information dynamically. A comment on this distinction would be valuable.

In summary, this is an excellent and timely paper that demonstrates a significant advance in training very deep neural networks. It was a pleasure to read and review.
