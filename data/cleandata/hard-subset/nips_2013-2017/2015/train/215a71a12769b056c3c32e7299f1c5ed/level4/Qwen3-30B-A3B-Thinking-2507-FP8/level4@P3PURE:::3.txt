

This paper proposes leveraging LSTM units to train extremely deep neural networks, addressing a critical challenge in the field. The work is of high quality, well-written, and presents an original approach. Machine learning researchers have long observed that adding more layers typically improves results, yet the persistent difficulties of initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients have severely limited progress. Indeed, the challenge of enabling effective information flow through deep architectures arguably prevented widespread adoption of deep networks for nearly three decades. This paper directly confronts this problem and demonstrates a viable method for training networks with up to 100 layers.

The experimental analysis of information routing and layer importance is particularly compelling. Specific comments:  
1. In Section 4.1, the statement "most transform blocks are active on average" should be corrected to "most transform blocks are not active on average."  
2. The authors might consider discussing how transform gates differentiate their method from a simple initialization scheme where each layer is initialized with the identity matrix (possibly with minor noise), highlighting the advantages of the proposed gating mechanism over this baseline.  

This is a strong paper that successfully demonstrates a practical approach to training very deep neural networks, and it was a pleasure to read.
