- Introduces highway networks with adaptive gating (LSTM-inspired) to enable training of very deep feedforward nets.  
- Shows these networks can be trained with standard gradient descent even for hundreds of layers and analyzes learned gate behavior.  
- Well-written paper with clear motivation and insightful connection to LSTM recurrent networks.  
- Analysis of gate activations (figures) provides valuable intuition about network operation.  
- Thorough empirical validation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, outperforming plain nets and comparing to FitNets.  
- Properly situates work relative to prior research on deep learning, initialization, and skip connections.  
- Technical concern: transform gate uses sigmoid (never exactly 0/1); authors should clarify backprop implementation and accuracy of the Jacobians in Eq. 5.  
- Needs deeper analysis of negative bias initialization for transform gates; suggest plotting performance versus initial bias to show its regularizing role.  
- Vague comparative phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings”—must explicitly detail differences/similarities in experimental setups.  
- Statistical concern: Tables report only maximum accuracies; should report mean and standard deviation consistently.  
- Suggestion: include learning-theoretic analysis of gates enabling identity mappings to study representational capacity and optimization landscapes.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a key deep learning challenge, proposes a novel, well-motivated architecture, strong empirical results, insightful analysis, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minor Jacobian/technical inaccuracies, limited bias-init analysis, vague comparisons, weak statistical reporting, and lack of theoretical analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant impact; accept with minor revisions to address the points above.
