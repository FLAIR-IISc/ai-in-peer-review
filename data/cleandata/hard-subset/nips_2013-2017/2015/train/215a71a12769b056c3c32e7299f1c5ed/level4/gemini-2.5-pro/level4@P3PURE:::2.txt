This paper proposes a novel architecture for very deep networks that uses input-dependent gates to allow information to be "carried" unchanged through a layer. This mechanism enables the training of much deeper networks than would be possible with a standard feed-forward architecture, and the authors demonstrate promising results.

The paper is of high quality, original, and clearly written. The authors effectively situate their method in relation to recent work (notably LSTMs and FitNets), clearly explaining the differences and providing comparisons where appropriate. A particularly strong aspect of this work is the analysis of the trained networks. The authors provide an insightful investigation into how the gates select their inputs across different layers, which is a crucial step in understanding the behavior of the proposed model.

In summary, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel, the analysis is interesting and thorough, and the results are strong. I have no suggested improvements.
