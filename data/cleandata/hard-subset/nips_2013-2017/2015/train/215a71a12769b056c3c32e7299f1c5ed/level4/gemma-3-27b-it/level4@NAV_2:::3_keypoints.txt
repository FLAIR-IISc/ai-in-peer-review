- Proposes highway networks that use LSTM-inspired transform and carry gates to enable training of very deep feedforward networks by preserving information flow.
- Clearly situates the work within prior deep learning literature and frames highway networks as complementary to existing solutions (optimizers, initialization, activations, skip connections, multi-stage training).
- Novelty: introduces a creative gating mechanism to address vanishing/exploding gradients in deep networks.
- Technical soundness: architecture is well-defined, mathematically justified, and includes insightful Jacobian analysis.
- Empirical validation: demonstrates successful training of networks with hundreds of layers and strong comparisons to baselines and state-of-the-art methods.
- Analysis and insights: provides useful investigation of information routing and layer importance with informative figures.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, and reproducible.
- Weakness — initialization sensitivity: proposed initialization for transform gates needs more systematic evaluation of its impact.
- Weakness — hyperparameter tuning: lacks detailed description of hyperparameter search space and selection criteria.
- Weakness — identity initialization comparison: does not explicitly compare or discuss differences with simple identity-initialized layers.
- Argument for acceptance: addresses a fundamental deep learning challenge with a novel, effective solution and strong empirical results.
- Argument for acceptance: analysis and presentation are clear and provide valuable insights for future research.
- Argument against acceptance: relies heavily on empirical results with limited formal theoretical analysis of gating effects on gradient flow.
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient exploration of initialization strategies and hyperparameter tuning.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution that substantially advances training of very deep networks; recommendation to accept.
