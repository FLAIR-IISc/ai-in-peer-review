This paper presents a method for training neural networks with a large number of stacked layers, termed 'information highways,' which create direct connections to upper layers. The authors demonstrate through solid experimentation that this approach is effective and can improve classification accuracy. While the method can be viewed as a generalization of prior techniques, such as cross-layer connections, the contributions are novel and well-supported by the provided evidence.

**Quality:** The core idea is novel and the experimentation is solid. The analysis sections, however, could be strengthened with a greater variety of details or more illustrative examples.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written, and its main contributions are communicated very clearly.

**Originality:** The proposed method is sufficiently original. While it generalizes previous techniques, it presents a novel and effective framework for training deep networks.

**Significance:** This work is significant as it addresses the critical challenge of training increasingly deep neural networks, a key factor for achieving better results in many application areas.
