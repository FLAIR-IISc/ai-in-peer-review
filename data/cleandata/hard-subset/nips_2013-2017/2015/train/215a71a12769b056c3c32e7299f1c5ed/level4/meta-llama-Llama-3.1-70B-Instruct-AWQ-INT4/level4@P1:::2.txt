This paper proposes an architecture for very deep networks, dubbed "highway networks," that enables input to be "carried" unchanged through a layer using gates dependent on the input itself. This innovation allows for much deeper networks to be trained than would be possible with a standard feed-forward architecture.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the trained networks, shedding light on the manner in which the gates select their inputs in different layers of the network. This analysis is particularly insightful and exactly what one would hope to see in a paper of this nature.

The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and original. Although there are some similarities to other recent work (notably FitNets and LSTMs), the authors clearly explain the differences and provide relevant comparisons where appropriate.

The results presented in the paper are promising, demonstrating the effectiveness of highway networks in training very deep architectures. The authors' experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 datasets show that highway networks can be trained directly using stochastic gradient descent (SGD), achieving competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods.

One minor error was noted: the paper references "Tables 3 and 3" on page 5, which appears to be a typographical mistake.

Overall, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel and achieves good results, and the analysis is interesting and thorough. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field, and their work is likely to be of interest to researchers working on deep learning architectures.

Strengths:

* Novel architecture for very deep networks
* Thorough analysis of trained networks
* Promising results on several datasets
* Clearly written and well-organized paper

Weaknesses:

* Minor typographical error ( referencing "Tables 3 and 3")

Arguments for acceptance:

* Original and significant contribution to the field of deep learning
* Well-written and clearly organized paper
* Thorough analysis of trained networks
* Promising results on several datasets

Arguments against acceptance:

* Minor typographical error ( referencing "Tables 3 and 3")

Overall, I strongly recommend accepting this paper for publication.
