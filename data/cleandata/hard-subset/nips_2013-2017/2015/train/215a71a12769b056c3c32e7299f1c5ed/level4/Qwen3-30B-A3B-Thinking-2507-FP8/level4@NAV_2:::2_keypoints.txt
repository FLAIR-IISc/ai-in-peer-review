- Introduces highway networks: an architecture using LSTM-like adaptive gating to enable direct training of extremely deep feedforward nets (100+ layers).  
- Positions work within prior literature, extending depth-limit understanding (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber; Glorot & Bengio) and contrasting with FitNets and fixed skip connections.  
- Core innovation: transform/carry gates that dynamically regulate information flow to mitigate vanishing gradients.  
- Provides rigorous experiments: 100 random hyperparameter searches per depth on MNIST, ablation studies, and comparisons to plain nets and FitNets.  
- Theoretical analysis of Jacobian behavior (Eq. 5) complements empirical validation of gate dynamics (Figs. 2–4).  
- Exceptional clarity: well-organized presentation, precise notation, intuitive gate explanations, and informative visualizations.  
- Originality: adaptive gating as a novel mechanism distinct from teacher-student distillation and static skips.  
- Significance: enables direct training of much deeper networks (100+ layers) with strong MNIST/CIFAR results and advances study of depth effects.  
- Limited analysis of computational efficiency (FLOPs/training time) and comparison to contemporary designs like ResNets.  
- Gate initialization explored only via negative bias values; broader initialization effects are not studied.  
- MNIST experiments use simpler architectures than CIFAR-100, which may underestimate scalability concerns.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — technically sound, novel, and impactful, with minor weaknesses not undermining the contribution.  
- Minor fix: correct typo "Tables 3 and 3" to "Tables 3 and 4."
