- Proposes "information highways," connections that enable unimpeded information flow to train much deeper networks.  
- Frames highways as a novel, significant generalization of prior cross-layer connection techniques.  
- Strong empirical validation: thorough experiments showing stable training of much deeper models and improved benchmark classification accuracy.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and an easy-to-follow technical description.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of why the method works; should include visualizations/statistics of highway gate behavior across depths and training stages.  
- Needs more extensive ablation studies to isolate contributions of individual components and justify design choices.  
- Overall: high-quality, novel, and well-executed work likely of strong interest to the NIPS community.
