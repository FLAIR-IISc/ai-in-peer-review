- Adapts LSTM-style transform and carry gates to feedforward "highway" networks, enabling effective training of very deep (≈100-layer) models.
- Method addresses vanishing/exploding gradients and initialization issues by providing data-dependent shortcut pathways.
- Empirical results convincingly show successful training of very deep networks and meaningful, data-dependent information routing.
- Analysis of layer utilization and pathway visualizations offers valuable intuition and interpretability about how depth is exploited.
- Concept is novel and significant: provides a principled mechanism for adaptive depth and gradient flow beyond ad hoc initialization.
- Demonstrating 100-layer training is a noteworthy practical contribution given historical optimization difficulties.
- Approach is well-motivated with clear connections to LSTM gating and a coherent training recipe that encourages identity-preserving behavior at initialization.
- Experimental analyses of layer importance and routing are a highlight and strengthen the paper’s claims.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and supported by informative figures and analyses.
- Clarify Section 4.1 where wording (“most transform blocks are active on average”) appears inconsistent with presented histograms/metrics.
- Recommend explicitly contrasting highway gating with simple identity initialization (and possibly an ablation) to show gating’s qualitative advantages.
- Suggest adding a brief note on computational overhead (parameters and runtime) of gating versus the optimization benefits for practitioners.
