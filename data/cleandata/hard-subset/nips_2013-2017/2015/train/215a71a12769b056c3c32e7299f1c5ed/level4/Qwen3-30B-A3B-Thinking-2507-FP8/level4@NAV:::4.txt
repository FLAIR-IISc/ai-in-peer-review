

Quality: The proposed approach for generalizing training in deep neural networks demonstrates novelty, and the experimental validation is robust. The analysis would benefit from expanded detail or supplementary visualizations.
Clarity: The paper's core contributions are clearly articulated and well communicated.
Originality: The method presents sufficient novelty despite extending existing neural network techniques, such as cross-layer connections.
Significance: This work addresses a critical need in the field, as increasingly deep architectures require efficient training strategies to achieve improved performance across applications.
The paper introduces an effective method for training very deep neural networks using 'information highways'—direct connections to upper layers. Although an extension of prior techniques like cross-layer connections, the authors demonstrate its efficacy through comprehensive experimentation. The contributions are novel and robustly substantiated by empirical results.
