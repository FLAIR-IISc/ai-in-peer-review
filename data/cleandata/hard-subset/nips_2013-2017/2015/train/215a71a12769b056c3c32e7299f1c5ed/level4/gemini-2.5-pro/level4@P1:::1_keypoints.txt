- Introduces highway networks with transform and carry gates (LSTM-inspired) to enable training of very deep feedforward models.  
- Gates create "information highways" that allow portions of inputs to pass unchanged, facilitating unimpeded gradient flow.  
- Strong empirical evidence: highway networks up to 100 layers train with standard SGD and outperform plain networks in optimization.  
- Competitive generalization: solid performance on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 without sacrificing ease of training.  
- Insightful analysis: gate activity visualizations, class-dependent routing, and layer lesioning reveal learned routing and effective depth.  
- Original and significant contribution that could enable exploration of extreme network depth across applications.  
- Technical quality is high; experiments convincingly support the claims.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized, with intuitive and formal explanations of the highway layer.  
- Weakness: Equation 5 (Jacobian) uses idealized T=0/1 cases; should explicitly note this simplification and show the actual backprop derivative.  
- Weakness: Transform gate bias initialization is treated heuristically; needs systematic sensitivity analysis (plots across depths).  
- Weakness: Experimental reporting lacks statistical rigor—results are often single figures despite intent to report mean ± std over runs.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to prior work need precise disclosure of differences in augmentation, model capacity, and optimization details.  
- Further comment: The architecture suggests interesting theoretical questions (expressivity, optimization landscape); a brief discussion would be valuable.  
- Overall recommendation: Excellent, impactful paper with minor presentation and reporting issues; strongly recommend acceptance.
