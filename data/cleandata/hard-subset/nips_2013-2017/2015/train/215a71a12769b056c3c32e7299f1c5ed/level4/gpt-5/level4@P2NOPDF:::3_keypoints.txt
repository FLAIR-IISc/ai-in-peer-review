- Proposes LSTM-inspired gating to enable training of very deep networks.
- High-quality, well-written, and original manuscript with compelling empirical results.
- Tackles initialization and vanishing/exploding gradient issues to train ~100-layer networks.
- Analyses of information routing and layer-wise importance are insightful and illuminate depth usage.
- Notes a likely typo in Section 4.1: “most transform blocks are active on average” should read “not active on average.”
- Requests clarification comparing transform gates to simple identity-initialization and explaining gates’ benefits (dynamic routing, selective transformation, training stability).
- Overall assessment: excellent paper and a pleasure to read.
