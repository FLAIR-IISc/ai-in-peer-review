- Reviewer noted they considered an LSTM-inspired gradient-trapping approach themselves and praised the authors for realizing it first.  
- Suggests viewing extremely deep networks as shallower architectures with composite sub-layers, relating to inception, VGG, and highway networks.  
- Recommends an experiment training a single thin-and-tall network jointly on MNIST and CIFAR-10 (shared initial layers) to study cross-domain parameter utilization.  
- States no significant concerns and urges validation on larger datasets like ImageNet.  
- Concludes the paper presents a simple, LSTM-inspired idea that enables training very deep feedforward networks with rigorous analysis.
