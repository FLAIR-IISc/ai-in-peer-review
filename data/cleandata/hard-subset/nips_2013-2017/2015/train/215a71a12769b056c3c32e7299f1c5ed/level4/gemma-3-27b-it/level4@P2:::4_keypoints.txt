- Introduces highway networks that enable training very deep nets via “information highways” with LSTM-inspired gating.  
- Shows these networks can be trained with standard SGD even with hundreds of layers.  
- Builds on prior work (improvers in optimization, initialization, skip connections) and explicitly relates to LSTM.  
- Technically sound with a clear mathematical formulation.  
- Strong experimental validation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Ablation studies and transform-gate analyses (Figures 2 & 4) are particularly valuable.  
- Analysis section should include more discussion of failure cases and limitations.  
- Needs comparisons to more recent architectures and training techniques beyond cited related work.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, consistent notation, and helpful diagrams that aid reproducibility.  
- Novelty lies in the adaptive gating mechanism combining skip connections and LSTM-like control.  
- Significant impact: addresses the core challenge of training very deep networks without specialized procedures and generalizes well.  
- Insights into gate behavior could guide future architecture design.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a fundamental problem, introduces an effective new architecture, strong experiments, clear presentation, and insightful analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of limitations/failure modes and missing comparisons to newer methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
