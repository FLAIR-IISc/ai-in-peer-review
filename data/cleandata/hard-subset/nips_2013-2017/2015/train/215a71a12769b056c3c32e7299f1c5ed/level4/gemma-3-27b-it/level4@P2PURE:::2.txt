This paper introduces a novel architecture for training very deep networks, allowing inputs to propagate through layers unchanged via input-dependent gates. This gating mechanism facilitates the training of significantly deeper networks than traditional feedforward architectures permit. The authors present a compelling analysis of the trained networks, investigating how the gates select inputs across different layers – precisely the type of investigation I expected and appreciate. The paper is of high quality, well-written, and presents original work. While acknowledging similarities to recent related research, such as FitNets and LSTMs, the authors clearly articulate the distinctions and provide appropriate comparisons. The results demonstrate the promise of this approach. I have no suggestions for improvement. Please note a minor repetition in the table references ("Tables 3 and 3" on p5). Overall, this is a strong paper; the proposed technique is novel and yields strong results, and the analysis is both interesting and thorough.
