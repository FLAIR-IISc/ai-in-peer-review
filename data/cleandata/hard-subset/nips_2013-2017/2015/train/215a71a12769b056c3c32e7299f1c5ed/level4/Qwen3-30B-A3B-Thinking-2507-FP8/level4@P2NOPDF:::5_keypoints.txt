- Extends LSTM gradient-trapping to train extremely deep feedforward networks, providing a simple, elegant solution to vanishing gradients.
- Well-motivated approach with rigorous analysis and clear empirical advantages over standard deep networks.
- Significant contribution for practical training of very deep architectures.
- Insightful connection to hierarchical network design: "very deep" as blocks of complex sub-layers (e.g., Inception, VGG).
- Suggests exploring whether highway/residual networks can be interpreted as hierarchical sub-layer blocks to unify design philosophies.
- Recommends a single-network multi-domain experiment (train one thin-and-tall network on MNIST + CIFAR-10 with early branching) to probe parameter-sharing and feature reutilization.
- Core idea is robust with thorough ablation studies and no major technical concerns.
- Advises scaling evaluation to larger benchmarks like ImageNet to demonstrate broader scalability and generalization.
- Overall strong support for publication.
