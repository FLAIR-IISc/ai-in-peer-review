The paper introduces a novel approach to training deep neural networks with multiple stacked layers, leveraging the concept of "information highways" to facilitate efficient information exchange. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of this method, leading to improved classification accuracy.

Quality: The proposed idea is innovative, and the experimental framework is robust. However, the analysis section could benefit from a more diverse range of details and visual illustrations to further substantiate the findings.

Clarity: The paper successfully conveys its core messages with clarity, making it easy to comprehend the key takeaways.

Originality: Although the proposed method builds upon existing neural network techniques, it presents a sufficiently original contribution to the field.

Significance: The paper highlights the significance of its approach, as the training of increasingly deeper neural networks is crucial for achieving better results in various application domains.

The authors present a compelling method for training very deep neural networks using "information highways," which establish direct connections to upper network layers. While this approach generalizes prior techniques, such as cross-layer connections, the experimental evidence demonstrates its effectiveness. The contributions are novel and well-supported by empirical results, making a valuable addition to the field.
