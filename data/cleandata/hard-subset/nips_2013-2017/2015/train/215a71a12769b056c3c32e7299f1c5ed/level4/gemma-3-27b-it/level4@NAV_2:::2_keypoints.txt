- Proposes "highway networks," a novel architecture using adaptive gating (LSTM-inspired) to enable unimpeded information flow across very deep feedforward networks.
- Demonstrates that highway networks can be trained directly with SGD, scaling to hundreds of layers and generalizing well to unseen data.
- Clearly situates the contribution within prior work on depth, optimization, initialization, activations, and skip connections.
- Explicitly cites and explains LSTM inspiration and distinguishes highway networks from prior approaches like FitNets.
- Technical formulation is sound and logically motivated.
- Empirical results convincingly show training success where standard architectures fail (Section 3.1).
- Analysis of learned gating, gate biases, activities, and information routing gives valuable insight and addresses concerns about unused layers (Section 4).
- Paper is well-organized, clear, and uses consistent notation.
- Code availability is a significant positive for reproducibility.
- Minor issues include repetitive phrasing (e.g., "Tables 3 and 3").
- Lacks a more detailed discussion of computational cost and parameter efficiency relative to alternative methods.
- The core idea builds on LSTM concepts, so readers familiar with LSTMs may not view it as radically novel despite a significant adaptation to feedforward networks.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, strong empirical evidence, insightful analysis, clarity, and public code.
- Arguments against acceptance: minor editorial issues, limited cost/efficiency analysis, and incremental novelty relative to LSTM ideas.
