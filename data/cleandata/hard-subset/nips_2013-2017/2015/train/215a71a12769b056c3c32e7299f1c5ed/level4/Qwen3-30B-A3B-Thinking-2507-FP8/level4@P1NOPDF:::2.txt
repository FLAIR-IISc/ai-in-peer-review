

This paper introduces a novel architecture for very deep networks that enables inputs to pass through layers unchanged via input-dependent gates. This mechanism facilitates training significantly deeper networks than standard feedforward approaches, addressing a key challenge in deep learning. The authors conduct an insightful analysis of how these gates select inputs across different network layers—a precisely the kind of detailed investigation I hoped to see.  

The paper is well-written, original, and of high quality. While there is notable overlap with recent work (e.g., FitNets and LSTMs), the distinctions are clearly articulated, and relevant comparisons are provided where appropriate. The results are promising, demonstrating the technique’s effectiveness.  

I have no suggestions for improvement.
