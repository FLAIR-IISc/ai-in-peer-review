- Congratulates authors for being first to explore using LSTM-style gradient control to train very deep feedforward networks.  
- Offers alternative perspective: a very deep network can be viewed as a shallower network whose layers contain sub-layers with richer nonlinear behavior (analogy to Inception/VGG).  
- Suggests investigating whether this sub-layer viewpoint applies to highway networks.  
- Proposes experiment: train a single thin, tall network forked at the top on both MNIST and CIFAR to study cross-domain sharing.  
- Wants to see if jointly trained network reproduces inference patterns of separately trained models and whether CIFAR leverages parameters underutilized by MNIST.  
- Encourages scaling experiments to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to further validate the approach.  
- Overall judgment: no major complaints; idea is simple, elegant, and supported by solid analysis.
