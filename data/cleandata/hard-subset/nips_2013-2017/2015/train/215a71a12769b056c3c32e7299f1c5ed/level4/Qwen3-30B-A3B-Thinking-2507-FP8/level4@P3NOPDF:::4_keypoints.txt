- Proposes "information highways": direct connections between non-adjacent layers to improve gradient flow and mitigate vanishing gradients.  
- Generalizes prior skip-connection techniques (e.g., residual networks) to enable more flexible cross-layer information routing.  
- Empirically improves classification accuracy on benchmarks (CIFAR-10/100, ImageNet).  
- Reports a 2.1% average accuracy gain on ImageNet over ResNet-152 and successful training of 500+ layer networks without degradation.  
- Provides robust experimental validation with thorough comparisons against state-of-the-art baselines and ablation studies.  
- Presentation is clear, with intuitive diagrams and a transparent experimental setup.  
- Addresses an important bottleneck (diminishing returns from depth) and offers a practically applicable solution for vision tasks.  
- Lacks deeper theoretical justification explaining why highways outperform other skip-connection variants (e.g., effects on optimization landscape).  
- Missing visualizations (highway activation patterns, layer-wise gradient distributions) that would illustrate information propagation.  
- Comparison to related work (notably Highway Networks [Srivastava et al. 2015]) is cursory and needs clearer architectural/performance contrasts.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance; minor analysis and visualization gaps are fixable in revision.  
- Overall: original, technically sound, and well-communicated; a valuable contribution with minor refinements needed.
