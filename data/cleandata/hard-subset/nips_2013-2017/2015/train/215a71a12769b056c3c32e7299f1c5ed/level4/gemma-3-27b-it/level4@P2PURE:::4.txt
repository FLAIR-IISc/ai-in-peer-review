This paper presents a generalization of training techniques for deep, stacked neural networks, introducing the concept of ‘information highways.’ The effectiveness of this approach is well-demonstrated through experimentation, with the authors showing improvements in classification accuracy. 

Quality: The idea is novel and the experimental validation is strong. The analysis section could benefit from more detailed explanations or illustrative examples.

Clarity: The paper’s core messages are clearly and effectively communicated.

Originality: While building upon previous neural network techniques—such as cross-layer connections—the proposed method is sufficiently original.

Significance: This work is significant to the field, as training increasingly deep neural networks is crucial for achieving state-of-the-art results in many applications.

Specifically, the paper describes an effective method for training very deep neural networks by establishing direct connections to upper layers, termed ‘information highways.’ Though a generalization of prior approaches, the authors demonstrate its efficacy through robust experimentation. The contributions are novel and well-supported by experimental results.
