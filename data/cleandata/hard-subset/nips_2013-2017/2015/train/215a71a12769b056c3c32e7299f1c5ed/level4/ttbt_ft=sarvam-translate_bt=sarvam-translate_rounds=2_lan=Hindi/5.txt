I was also thinking about using the LSTML trick to trap gradients to deepen the network, but I haven't started working on it yet. First of all, congratulations to the authors for getting there!

Perhaps there's a different perspective: a very deep network isn't as deep as a network where there are sub-layers in layers. Each "layer" has more complex nonlinear behavior than a traditional rectifier/sigmoid layer. I personally see the beginning of GoogleNet as being like this, 3 stacked 3x3 VGGs, etc., and I wonder if this is even a valid way to look at a highway network.

There's an experiment I would suggest the authors try: training both MNIST and CIFAR using a thin and long network (you can fork from the top for the two domains). My interest lies in whether, at prediction time, the network displays the same patterns found in the separately trained networks for each domain, whether the model parameters chosen by CIFAR are reduced in MNIST. In other words, I would like to see how sharing occurs in a very deep hierarchy, and whether it is as efficient as possible.

No major complaints. I encourage the authors to try the network on larger datasets like ImageNet, etc.
