This paper introduces a novel approach leveraging LSTM units to facilitate the training of extremely deep neural networks.

Overall, this is a strong and clearly written contribution. The proposed method is original, and the presented results are compelling. 

A persistent challenge in deep learning has been the difficulty of effectively training networks with a large number of layers, stemming from problems with initialization and unstable gradients—issues that historically hindered the adoption of deep networks for decades. This work directly tackles this problem, successfully demonstrating training of networks with 100 layers. 

The experiments illustrating information flow and layer relevance are particularly insightful and well-presented.

Specific feedback:
In Section 4.1, the statement “most transform blocks are active on average” appears to be inaccurate and should be revised to “not active on average.”
The paper might benefit from a discussion of how the transform gates provide an advantage over simply initializing each layer with an identity matrix (potentially with added noise), a common and straightforward initialization technique. This is an excellent paper, offering a promising method for training very deep neural networks, and was a pleasure to review.
