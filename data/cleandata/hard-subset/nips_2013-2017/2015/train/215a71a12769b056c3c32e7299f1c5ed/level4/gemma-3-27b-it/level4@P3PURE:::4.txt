This paper proposes a generalization of training techniques for deep neural networks with many stacked layers. The “information highway” concept is well-motivated and effectively demonstrated through experimentation, leading to improved classification accuracy.

**Quality:** The idea presented is novel, and the experimental validation is solid. While the analysis section is well-executed, it could benefit from a more diverse range of details or illustrative examples.

**Clarity:** The paper’s central messages are clearly articulated and easily understood.

**Originality:** The proposed method is sufficiently original, representing a valuable generalization of existing neural network techniques like cross-layer connections.

**Significance:** The work demonstrates clear significance to the field, as the ability to effectively train increasingly deep neural networks is crucial for achieving improved performance in a wide range of applications.

The paper describes an effective method for training very deep neural networks via ‘information highways’ – direct connections to upper network layers. Although building upon prior techniques such as cross-layer connections, the authors convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of this method through comprehensive experimentation. The contributions are novel and strongly supported by experimental evidence.
