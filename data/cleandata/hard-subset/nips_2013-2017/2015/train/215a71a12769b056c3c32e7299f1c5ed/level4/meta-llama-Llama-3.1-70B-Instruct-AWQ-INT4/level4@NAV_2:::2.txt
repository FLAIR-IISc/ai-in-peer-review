This paper introduces a novel architecture for training very deep neural networks, addressing the long-standing challenge of vanishing gradients and poor propagation of activations in traditional feed-forward networks. The proposed "highway networks" utilize adaptive gating units, inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks, to regulate information flow and enable unimpeded propagation of inputs across many layers.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the trained networks, shedding light on how the gates select their inputs in different layers. This analysis is a significant contribution, as it offers valuable insights into the inner workings of the proposed architecture.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a concerted effort to clearly explain the differences between their approach and other recent work, such as FitNets and LSTMs. The experimental results are promising, demonstrating the effectiveness of highway networks in training very deep architectures.

The paper's strengths include its originality, clarity, and thorough analysis. The authors have successfully demonstrated the potential of their proposed architecture, and the results are likely to be of significant interest to the community.

In terms of weaknesses, there are no major concerns. The paper is well-structured, and the authors have provided sufficient background information to facilitate understanding of the proposed architecture.
