- Commends authors for exploring training very deep feedforward networks and recognizes the LSTM-inspired gradient-control approach.  
- Notes the idea is innovative and deserving of recognition.  
- Offers alternative view: a very deep network can be seen as a shallower network whose layers contain sub-layers, enabling richer non-linear behavior.  
- Draws parallels to GoogLeNet inception modules and VGG stacked 3x3 convolutions, and asks if this perspective applies to highway networks.  
- Proposes an experiment: train one thin, tall network with a top fork for MNIST and CIFAR to compare shared vs. domain-specific inference patterns.  
- Interested in whether parameters underutilized on MNIST are repurposed by CIFAR, revealing sharing efficiency in deep hierarchies.  
- Praises the paper’s simplicity, elegance, and thorough analysis.  
- Suggests testing on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to further validate capabilities.
