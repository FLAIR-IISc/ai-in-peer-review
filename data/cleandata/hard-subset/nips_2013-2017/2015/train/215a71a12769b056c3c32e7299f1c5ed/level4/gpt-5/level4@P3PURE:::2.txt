This paper proposes an architecture for very deep networks that allows the input to be carried unchanged through a layer via input-dependent gates. This mechanism enables training much deeper networks than would be possible with a standard feedforward architecture.

The authors present a very interesting analysis of the trained networks, examining how the gates select their inputs across different layers—exactly the kind of investigation I wanted to see. The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and original. While there is naturally some similarity to other recent work (notably FitNets and LSTMs), the differences are clearly explained, and comparisons are provided where appropriate. The results are promising.

I have no suggested improvements. Note “Tables 3 and 3” (p. 5).

Overall, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel and achieves good results, and the analysis is interesting and thorough.
