This paper proposes an architecture for very deep networks, referred to as highway networks, which enables unimpeded information flow across many layers through adaptive gating units. This approach allows for the training of extremely deep networks using simple gradient descent, overcoming the difficulties associated with training deep networks.

The authors provide a clear and concise background on the importance of network depth in achieving state-of-the-art results in supervised machine learning tasks, as well as the challenges associated with training deep networks. They also discuss related work, including the use of better optimizers, initialization strategies, and techniques such as skip connections and deep supervision.

The proposed highway network architecture is well-motivated and clearly explained, with a detailed description of the adaptive gating mechanism and its inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the trained networks, including a study of the transform gate biases, mean activity, and block outputs, which provides valuable insights into the behavior of the network.

The experimental results are promising, demonstrating the ability to train extremely deep networks with good generalization performance. The comparison to FitNets and other state-of-the-art methods is also useful, highlighting the advantages of the proposed approach.

The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and original, with a thorough analysis of the proposed technique. The authors have done an excellent job of explaining the differences between their approach and related work, and the results are well-supported by the experimental evaluation.
