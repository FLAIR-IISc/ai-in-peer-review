

This paper introduces a novel architectural framework for extremely deep neural networks that enables input information to propagate unchanged through individual layers via input-dependent gating mechanisms. This approach fundamentally addresses the vanishing gradient problem in deep feedforward networks, enabling the training of significantly deeper architectures than previously feasible. The authors' analysis of the learned gating patterns across network layers provides valuable insights into how these mechanisms selectively preserve or transform information at different depths—a critical perspective that aligns precisely with the community's interest in understanding deep network behavior.  

The technical contribution is well-motivated and clearly articulated. While the work shares conceptual parallels with recent methods like FitNets and LSTMs, the authors meticulously distinguish their approach through explicit comparisons and a thorough discussion of key differences, particularly regarding the dynamic, input-adaptive nature of their gating strategy versus static or recurrent mechanisms. The empirical evaluation demonstrates compelling results, achieving state-of-the-art performance on benchmark tasks while maintaining computational efficiency. The ablation studies and layer-wise analysis further substantiate the efficacy of the proposed gating mechanism.  

The paper is exceptionally well-written, with logical flow and precise technical exposition. The experimental design is rigorous, and the results are presented with clarity. The analysis of gating behavior—showing how different layers prioritize input preservation versus transformation—adds significant theoretical depth beyond mere performance gains. This work not only delivers strong empirical outcomes but also advances our understanding of deep network dynamics, making it a meaningful contribution to the field.  

A minor note: The reference to "Tables 3 and 3" (page 5) appears to be a typo; it should likely be "Tables 3 and 4" or another corrected pair.  

Overall, this is a high-quality, original, and impactful paper. The methodology is sound, the analysis is insightful, and the results are robust. The authors have successfully demonstrated both the practical utility and theoretical significance of their approach. No major revisions are required. This work merits publication in NIPS for its clear innovation, thorough validation, and valuable contribution to the understanding of deep network architectures.
