The paper proposes a novel generalization of training neural networks with multiple stacked layers, introducing the concept of "information highways" to facilitate the flow of information across layers. This idea is effectively demonstrated through experimentation, showcasing improved classification accuracy results.

Quality: The idea presented in the paper is novel and well-supported by solid experimentation. However, the analysis sections could benefit from more diverse details and illustrations to further strengthen the authors' claims.

Clarity: The main messages of the paper are clearly conveyed, making it easy for readers to grasp the core concepts and contributions.

Originality: Although the proposed method builds upon previous neural network techniques, it is sufficiently original and offers a distinct approach to training deep neural networks.

Significance: The paper highlights the significance of the proposed method to the field, as the increasing depth of neural networks is crucial for achieving better results in various application areas.

The authors present an effective method for training very deep neural networks by establishing direct connections to upper network layers, which they term "information highways." While this approach is a generalization of prior techniques, such as cross-layer connections, the experimental evidence provided demonstrates its efficacy. The contributions of the paper are novel and well-supported by experimental evidence, making it a valuable addition to the field.
