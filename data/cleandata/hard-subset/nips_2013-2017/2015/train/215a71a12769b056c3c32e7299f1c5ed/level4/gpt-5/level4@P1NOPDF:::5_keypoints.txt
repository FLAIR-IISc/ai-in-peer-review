- Reviewer had independently considered using LSTM-style gradient "trapping" to grow much deeper feedforward networks and congratulates the authors for achieving this first.
- Proposes a perspective that a very deep network can be seen as a not-so-deep network whose layers contain richer sublayers, relating to Inception and VGG module interpretations.
- Asks whether highway networks can be fruitfully understood in the same "layers-as-sublayers" framework.
- Experimental suggestion: train a narrow-and-deep shared trunk jointly on MNIST and CIFAR with task-specific heads to study emergent sharing behavior.
- Specific empirical questions: do shared networks show activation/usage patterns like separately trained nets, are MNIST-underused parameters recruited by CIFAR, and how efficient is the sharing?
- Encourages evaluation on larger datasets such as ImageNet.
- No major complaints from the reviewer.
- Overall assessment: LSTM-inspired mechanism effectively enables training very deep feedforward networks; the idea is simple, elegant, and the analysis is solid.
