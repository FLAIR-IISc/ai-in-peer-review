This paper presents a compelling idea and the experimental results are largely convincing. A particularly strong aspect of the work is the authors’ analysis of the learned networks through the lens of gate output patterns.

(1) The gate unit T employs a standard sigmoid activation, resulting in outputs that are never truly 0 or 1, as acknowledged by the authors. Consequently, the gradients presented in equation 5 appear inaccurate. The paper would benefit from a detailed explanation of the backpropagation process implemented in these networks.

(2) While the influence of initialization is often complex, the proposed use of constant negative biases scaled by the number of levels feels somewhat arbitrary. Including a plot illustrating performance as a function of the initial bias value would strengthen the analysis. The initial bias effectively functions as a regularizer, favoring networks with “longer highways” – a comparison I found insightful. 

(3) The statement “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings” lacks precision. For a meaningful comparison, either the methods should utilize identical experimental setups, or the differences between setups should be clearly delineated. The authors should clarify the experimental conditions being referenced.

(4) Reporting only maximum accuracies in the tables is atypical and statistically questionable. The authors should consistently report mean accuracy alongside the standard deviation for each method presented (where available).

(5) A theoretical investigation from a learning theory perspective would be a valuable addition. The paper introduces gate units that enable layers to function as identity mappings, with gate responses modulating input propagation through potentially numerous, highly selective layers. Further exploration of this mechanism is warranted.
