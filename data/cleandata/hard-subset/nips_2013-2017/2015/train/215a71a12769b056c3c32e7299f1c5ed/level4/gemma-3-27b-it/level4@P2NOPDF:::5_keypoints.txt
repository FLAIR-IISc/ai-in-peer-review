- Presents a compelling, LSTM-inspired method for training very deep feedforward networks; idea is simple, elegant, and accompanied by sound analysis.  
- Successful implementation; reviewer notes independent similar interest but no prior implementation.  
- Proposes an alternate framing: interpret very deep networks as stacks of shallower layers with complex sub-layers (akin to Inception or VGG), suggesting highway networks might be seen similarly.  
- Recommends an experiment: train a single “thin-and-tall” network on both MNIST and CIFAR-10 with an input branching point to study parameter sharing and whether underused MNIST parameters are leveraged for CIFAR-10.  
- Strongly encourages evaluation on larger, harder datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to assess scalability and generalizability.  
- Overall judgment: well-conceived, well-executed paper that makes a significant contribution; no major criticisms.
