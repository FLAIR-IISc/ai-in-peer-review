- Proposes using LSTM-like gating units to enable training of very deep networks.  
- Paper is high-quality, well-written, original, and presents interesting empirical results.  
- Addresses the long-standing problem of initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients that limit network depth.  
- Demonstrates successful training of 100-layer networks.  
- Experiments on information routing and layer importance are particularly noteworthy.  
- Correction: In Section 4.1, "most transform blocks are active on average" should read "not active on average."  
- Suggestion: comment on identity-initialization (with small noise) as an alternative and how transform gates differ from that simple scheme.
