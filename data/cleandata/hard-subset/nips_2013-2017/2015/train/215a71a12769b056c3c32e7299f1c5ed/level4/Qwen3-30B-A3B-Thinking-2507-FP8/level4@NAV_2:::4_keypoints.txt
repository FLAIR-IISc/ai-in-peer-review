- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-inspired adaptive gating to enable direct training of extremely deep feedforward networks.  
- Extends prior work (skip connections, distillation, normalized initialization) by replacing fixed shortcuts with dynamic, input-dependent routing.  
- Transform and carry gates (C = 1 − T) allow unimpeded information propagation, enabling SGD training of networks >100 layers.  
- Empirical results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 show superior or comparable accuracy with fewer parameters than state-of-the-art.  
- Ablation study demonstrates dynamic utilization of depth: complex tasks use more layers while simple tasks leave layers idle.  
- Negative bias initialization for transform gates is well-motivated by LSTM literature and empirically effective.  
- Technical approach is sound and experiments are rigorous; 100-layer highway network dramatically outperforms plain networks on MNIST.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and provides reproducible experimental details and public code.  
- Dynamic gating is an original conceptual advance over fixed skip connections and single-stage methods like FitNets.  
- Significance: removes a key bottleneck in deep learning by enabling direct training of very deep networks and facilitating depth studies.  
- Broad applicability to convolutional, feedforward, and potentially recurrent architectures, with high adoption potential.  
- Pro: strong empirical validation, novel insights into routing and layer importance, simple training with SGD, reproducible code.  
- Con: limited analysis of computational/inference overhead from gating parameters.  
- Con: simplified highway block design lacks brief justification compared to the LSTM-like block used in pilots.
