This paper proposes a novel architecture for very deep networks that uses input-dependent gates to allow information to be carried unchanged through a layer. This mechanism enables the training of networks that are significantly deeper than what is feasible with standard feed-forward architectures.

The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and presents an original contribution. A particularly strong aspect of this work is the insightful analysis of the trained networks. The authors investigate the manner in which the gates select their inputs across different layers, providing an illuminating view of their function at various depths. This analysis is thorough and adds significant value to the paper.

The authors correctly position their work with respect to related approaches (notably FitNets and LSTMs), clearly articulating the key differences and providing appropriate comparisons. The empirical results achieved are promising.

In summary, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel and effective, and the analysis is both interesting and thorough. I have no suggested improvements.
