- Strength: Well-articulated idea, convincing experiments, and strong analysis linking learned networks to gate-output patterns.

- Issue 1: Sigmoid gates never reach exact 0/1, making gradients in eq.5 inaccurate; authors must clarify how backpropagation is implemented.

- Issue 2: Constant negative bias initialization (based on depth) is unsatisfactory; provide a plot of performance vs initial bias since bias acts as a regularizer favoring "longer highways."

- Issue 3: The phrase "report by recent studies in similar experimental settings" is unclear; either compare using identical setups or explicitly state experimental differences.

- Issue 4: Reporting "max" accuracies is statistically unsound; report mean accuracy and standard deviation (and other relevant stats) consistently.

- Suggestion: Explore the approach from a learning-theory perspective—gates enable layers to act as input-dependent identities and selective multi-layer propagation.
