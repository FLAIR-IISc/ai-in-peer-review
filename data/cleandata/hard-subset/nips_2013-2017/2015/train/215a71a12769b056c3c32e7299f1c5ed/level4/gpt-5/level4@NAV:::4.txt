The paper introduces a generalized approach to training neural networks with many stacked layers. The information highway concept is well supported by experiments, and the authors demonstrate that this method improves classification accuracy.

Quality: the core idea appears novel, and the experimental evaluation is strong. The analysis sections could benefit from a greater variety of details or illustrative examples.

Clarity: the main messages are communicated effectively and are quite clear.

Originality: the proposed method is sufficiently original, despite being a generalization of prior neural network techniques.

Significant: the work is significant to the field, as training increasingly deep neural networks is essential for attaining better results in application domains.

The paper presents an effective method for training very deep neural networks via “information highways,” i.e., direct connections to higher layers. Although it generalizes prior techniques such as cross-layer connections, the authors show through experiments that the method is effective. The contributions are notably novel and well substantiated by empirical evidence.
