- Introduces using LSTM units to train extremely deep neural networks (up to 100 layers).  
- High-quality, well-written paper with an original idea and compelling results.  
- Directly addresses initialization and vanishing/exploding gradient challenges that limit depth.  
- Demonstrates successful information flow and routing through very deep architectures.  
- Experiments on information routing and layer importance are particularly compelling.  
- Correction: In Section 4.1, change "most transform blocks are active on average" to "not active on average."  
- Suggestion: Discuss how transform gates differ from simple identity-initialized layers (with noise) and relation to highway networks.  
- Overall assessment: excellent paper and a pleasure to review.
