- Paper proposes an LSTM-inspired mechanism that enables training very deep feedforward networks.
- The idea is simple, elegant, and accompanied by solid analysis and clear presentation.
- Reviewer notes they had independently considered a similar gradient-trapping trick, praising the authors for acting first.
- Proposes conceptual view: very deep networks can be seen as shallower layers with internal sub-layers producing richer nonlinear behavior (relates to GoogLeNet, VGG).
- Suggests exploring whether this viewpoint applies to highway networks.
- Recommends an experiment: train a single thin, tall network jointly on MNIST and CIFAR (forking at the top) to study parameter sharing and whether CIFAR reuses MNIST-underutilized parameters.
- Curious about how efficiently sharing occurs in very deep hierarchies.
- No major complaints; encourages scaling experiments to larger datasets such as ImageNet.
