- Congratulations to the authors for successfully adapting LSTM gradient-stabilization to train very deep feedforward networks.

- Proposes an interpretation that very deep nets can be viewed as shallower architectures whose individual layers contain complex sub-structures, richer than standard rectifier/sigmoid units.

- Notes this perspective aligns with design philosophies behind GoogLeNet inception modules, VGG stacked 3×3 convolutions, and possibly highway networks; asks if this framing helps understand the approach.

- Recommends an additional experiment: train one tall-and-thin network jointly on MNIST and CIFAR-10 with an early split to test parameter sharing vs. domain-specific utilization.

- Wants to know whether CIFAR-10 uses parameters that MNIST underutilizes and how efficiently shared representations are exploited in the deep hierarchy.

- Rates the idea as simple and elegant, theoretically sound, and empirically compelling, with no major concerns.

- Suggests validating the method on larger benchmarks like ImageNet to assess scalability and broader applicability.
