This paper proposes highway networks, a deep architecture that enables information to be carried across many layers via data-dependent gates. Inspired by LSTM, each layer computes a transform H(x) and a transform gate T(x), with a carry pathway weighted by 1 − T(x). This mechanism allows signals and gradients to traverse hundreds of layers without attenuation, making very deep networks trainable with standard SGD. The authors present both fully connected and convolutional variants, describe a simple initialization scheme (negative bias for the transform gates), and provide a thoughtful analysis of learned gating behavior and layer contributions.

Relation to prior work is clear. The approach is reminiscent of LSTM gating but applied to feedforward depth rather than temporal depth. It differs from teacher–student methods such as FitNets, which rely on multi-stage training and soft targets, and from fixed shortcut/skip connections: here the routing is learned and input-dependent. The authors compare against plain networks with variance-preserving initialization and against FitNets, and discuss related work on initialization, activation functions, and deep supervision.

Quality: The paper is technically sound. The optimization study is convincing: as depth increases, plain networks degrade while highway networks maintain performance and converge faster, even at 50–100 layers. Classification results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 are competitive, achieved with modest data augmentation and single-stage training, and are reported with variance across runs where available. The analysis sections are a highlight: visualizations of gate biases and activations, evidence of data-dependent routing, and layer lesion experiments that quantify layer importance. The empirical claims are well supported.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and well organized. The architecture and training setup are described succinctly, and the comparisons are easy to follow. The analysis is exactly the kind of insight one would hope to see for a new architecture. Minor issue: on p. 5 the text refers to “Tables 3 and 3,” which appears to be a typographical error.

Originality: The core idea—adapting LSTM-style gating to enable information highways in deep feedforward networks—is novel and distinct from prior skip-connection and hint-based training approaches. The differences from related work (notably FitNets and LSTMs) are clearly explained and, where appropriate, experimentally compared.

Significance: Enabling direct training of very deep networks is important. The method is simple, broadly applicable (fully connected and convolutional), and likely to influence both research and practice. Results are promising and the analysis provides useful understanding of how depth is utilized.

Pros:
- Simple, elegant architecture that makes very deep networks trainable with standard SGD.
- Strong optimization results demonstrating depth scalability.
- Competitive performance on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 without complex training pipelines.
- Clear comparisons to relevant prior work (FitNets, LSTM-inspired methods).
- Thorough and insightful analysis of gating behavior, data-dependent routing, and layer importance.

Cons:
- Only a minor typographical issue: “Tables 3 and 3” (p. 5).

Recommendation: Accept. This is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel, achieves good results, and includes analysis that is both interesting and thorough. I have no suggested substantive improvements beyond the minor table reference fix.
