I liked this paper; the ideas are well-described, and the experiments are reliable to a certain extent. In my opinion, the best part is that the authors have tried to analyze the learned networks in relation to the pattern of the gate output.

(1) The gate unit T is modeled with a standard sigmoid activation. Therefore, the response value is never exactly 0 or 1 (this was also stated by the authors), and the gradients in equation 5 are not correct. The authors should explain in the paper how the backpropagation is done correctly in these networks.

(2) Analyzing the effect of initialization is always difficult. However, the authors' proposal to use a constant negative bias based on the number of layers is also not satisfactory. I would like to see a plot of the performance with respect to the initial value of the bias. The initial bias acts as a kind of regularizer in this case, with a high negative bias supporting the network with "long highways" (I like this comparison).

(3) The sentence "reported by recent studies in similar experimental settings" is unsatisfactory. Either the methods use the same experimental setting, which would allow for comparison, or they don't, which would restrict direct comparison. The authors need to clarify the difference in experimental setup in the paper.

(4) Giving "maximum" accuracy in the tables is unusual and not appropriate from a statistical point of view. Therefore, the authors should always provide the mean accuracy and standard deviation for each method (and when available).

(5) It would be interesting to study the proposed approach from the perspective of learning theory. The paper explains how to use additional gate units in neural networks that allow layers to function only as an identifier. The gate unit response depends on the input and therefore allows the input to propagate through very selective layers.
