- Proposes "information highways": direct connections to higher layers to improve signal and gradient flow for training very deep networks.
- Demonstrates consistent classification accuracy improvements across relevant benchmarks.
- Core idea is novel and supported by strong empirical results; experimental design convincingly shows benefits.
- Analysis is limited and should include more diagnostics (ablations, visualizations), failure-mode discussion, and hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Paper is well written with clear motivation and high-level explanation of mechanisms.
- Needs more detail on gating behavior, initialization choices, and training stability to improve clarity.
- Builds on prior cross-layer connection ideas but provides a meaningful and sufficiently original generalization.
- Contribution is significant: enables effective training of much deeper networks with tangible accuracy gains and broad impact.
- Overall: well-executed, timely, and practically relevant work, albeit with room for richer analysis.
