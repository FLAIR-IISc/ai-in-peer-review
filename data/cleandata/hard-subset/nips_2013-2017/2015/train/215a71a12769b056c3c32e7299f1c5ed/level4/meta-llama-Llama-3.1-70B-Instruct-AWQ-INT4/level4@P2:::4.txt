The paper "Training Very Deep Networks" proposes a novel architecture for training neural networks with many stacked layers, introducing the concept of "information highways" to facilitate the flow of information across layers. The authors draw inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks and utilize adaptive gating units to regulate the information flow. This approach enables the training of extremely deep and efficient architectures directly through simple gradient descent.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the challenges associated with training deep neural networks, including the difficulties of propagating activations and gradients through multiple layers. The authors argue that their proposed architecture, dubbed "highway networks," can overcome these challenges by allowing information to flow unimpeded across many layers.

The quality of the paper is high, with solid experimentation and a clear presentation of the results. The analysis sections could benefit from a bit more variety in terms of details and illustrations, but overall, the paper provides a compelling case for the effectiveness of highway networks.
