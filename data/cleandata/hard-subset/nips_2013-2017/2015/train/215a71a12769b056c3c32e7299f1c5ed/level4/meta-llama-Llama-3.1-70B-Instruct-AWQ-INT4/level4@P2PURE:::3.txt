This paper proposes the use of LSTM units as a method for training very deep networks, offering a novel approach to addressing the challenges associated with deep neural networks.

The paper is of high quality and well-written, presenting an original idea with intriguing results. In the field of machine learning, researchers often achieve better results by adding more layers to their neural networks, but difficulties with initialization and decaying or exploding gradients have long hindered progress. The struggles of propagating information through deep neural networks have, in fact, limited their widespread adoption for nearly three decades. This paper tackles this issue directly and demonstrates a viable method for training 100-layer networks.

Notably, the experiments illustrating information routing and layer importance are particularly compelling. 

Specific comments:

In Section 4.1, it appears that 'most transform blocks are active on average' should be revised to 'not active on average.'

One potential initialization strategy for very deep networks is to use the identity matrix for each layer, possibly with a small amount of added noise. It would be beneficial to discuss how the transform gates in highway networks differentiate from this simple approach. 

Overall, this is an excellent paper that demonstrates a method for training very deep neural networks, making for an enjoyable read.
