- Proposes "highway networks": adaptive gating units inspired by LSTM to allow unimpeded information flow and mitigate vanishing gradients.  
- Demonstrates training of very deep networks (hundreds of layers) with standard SGD and good generalization.  
- Provides insightful analysis of learned gate outputs to understand information routing.  
- Well-structured paper that situates work among prior techniques (skip connections, FitNets) with convincing MNIST/CIFAR experiments.  
- Critique 1: Backpropagation explanation is incomplete because sigmoid transform gates are not exact 0/1, affecting the validity of Equation 5 gradients.  
- Critique 2: Initialization strategy (negative biases depending on depth) needs further analysis; recommend plotting performance vs initial bias to show sensitivity.  
- Critique 3: Phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings” is vague—clarify which setups are comparable or state differences.  
- Critique 4: Reporting only max accuracies is insufficient; should report mean and standard deviation for all methods.  
- Critique 5: Theoretical underpinnings (representational capacity/generalization bounds) of input-dependent identity mappings are not explored and would strengthen the paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective solution to training very deep nets; strong empirical results across datasets; valuable gate-pattern analysis; clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete gradient/backprop explanation; insufficient analysis of bias initialization; unclear comparisons to related work; lack of statistical rigor.  
- Overall: Strong contribution; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions addressing the listed concerns.
