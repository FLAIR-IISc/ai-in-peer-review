Summary
This paper introduces highway networks, a feedforward architecture that incorporates LSTM-inspired gating to enable information to flow along “information highways” across many layers. Each layer computes y = H(x) · T(x) + x · (1 − T(x)), where T is a learned transform gate (with carry C = 1 − T). This design allows the network to interpolate between transforming the input and passing it through, mitigating vanishing gradients and facilitating optimization at extreme depths. The authors propose initializing the transform gate bias negatively to favor carry behavior at the start of training.

The paper relates this approach to prior work on deep optimization (normalized initialization, Hessian-free and momentum methods), locally competitive activation functions, skip connections, layer-wise pretraining, and teacher-student “FitNets” training. The key difference is the adaptive, multiplicative gating mechanism inspired by LSTMs that enables direct training of very deep feedforward networks with standard SGD.

Empirically, the authors demonstrate:
- Optimization robustness: fully connected highway networks of 50–100 layers train reliably on MNIST, whereas similarly parameterized plain networks degrade markedly with depth.
- Generalization: convolutional highway networks achieve competitive results on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 without heavy augmentation; they match or exceed FitNets under comparable parameter/multiplication budgets while training in a single stage.
- Interpretability: analyses show data-dependent routing through gates and per-layer lesion studies that reveal which layers are functionally important given task complexity.

Quality
The paper is technically sound and carefully executed. The optimization study convincingly supports the claim that highway networks maintain trainability as depth increases, with clear control comparisons to plain networks using normalized initialization. The CIFAR experiments are not tuned for state-of-the-art accuracy, but they appropriately demonstrate that ease of training does not sacrifice generalization. The analysis sections on routing and layer importance are particularly insightful and reinforce that the gates contribute meaningfully beyond easing optimization.

Clarity
The manuscript is clear and well organized. The architectural formulation and training procedure (including gate bias initialization) are easy to follow. Figures on gate activity and lesioning are informative. One minor point in Section 4.1 (see Specific comments) could benefit from clarification.

Originality
The approach is novel in bringing LSTM-style gating into deep feedforward layers to create adaptive skip/carry paths. While related to long-standing ideas like skip connections and more recent techniques such as hint-based training, the contribution is distinct: multiplicative, input-dependent routing that enables training of hundreds of layers with standard SGD.

Significance
Highway networks provide a practical path to training very deep architectures and offer tools to analyze depth utilization. This is a meaningful advance for building deeper, more efficient models and for understanding how depth is used in practice. The method is likely to influence architecture design and further research on trainable depth.

Strengths
- Clear, principled architecture with simple training and initialization.
- Strong optimization evidence across depths, with matched baselines.
- Competitive generalization under modest data augmentation.
- Insightful analyses of gating behavior and per-layer importance.
- Public code and hyperparameter details.

Weaknesses / questions
- While comparisons to FitNets are fair within fixed budgets, broader comparisons to alternative skip-connection schemes would further contextualize the gains.
- A brief discussion contrasting highway gating with simple identity-initialized plain layers or fixed skip connections would clarify when gating is essential versus sufficient.

Specific comments
- Section 4.1: The sentence “most transform blocks are active on average” appears inconsistent with the surrounding discussion/figures; it seems it should read “not active on average.” Please verify and rephrase accordingly.
- It would be useful to comment explicitly on how transform/carry gates differ in practice from identity initialization of each layer (possibly with small noise). In particular, adaptive, input-dependent gates provide multiplicative routing and selective transformation, whereas identity-initialized plain layers or fixed skip connections lack such dynamic control; emphasizing this distinction would strengthen the narrative.

Pros
- Elegant LSTM-inspired gating that enables training of 100+ layer networks with SGD.
- Thorough optimization study with matched-capacity baselines.
- Single-stage training outperforms or matches FitNets under comparable budgets.
- Analyses reveal meaningful, input-dependent routing and task-dependent layer usage.

Cons
- Limited direct comparison to other skip/shortcut strategies beyond teacher-student training.
- Theoretical analysis is light; most support is empirical (albeit strong).

Overall evaluation
This is a high-quality, original, and impactful paper. It directly addresses one of the central obstacles to scaling depth—information and gradient flow—through a simple, effective architectural mechanism. The experiments and analyses are compelling, and the paper is a pleasure to read. I recommend acceptance.
