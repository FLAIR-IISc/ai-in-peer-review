This paper proposes an effective method for training very deep neural networks by introducing a novel architectural element termed 'information highways'. These highways are connections that allow for the unimpeded flow of information across many stacked layers, addressing a key challenge in the optimization of deep architectures. The authors present this as a generalization of prior techniques, such as cross-layer connections, and demonstrate its efficacy through a series of solid experiments, showing improved classification accuracy on benchmark datasets.

**Strengths:**

*   **Significance and Novelty:** The contribution is both significant and timely. As the field increasingly relies on deeper models to achieve state-of-the-art results, methods that facilitate the training of such networks are of paramount importance. The proposed 'information highway' concept is an elegant and powerful generalization of existing ideas, and its formulation is sufficiently original to be a noteworthy contribution.

*   **Empirical Validation:** The paper's claims are well-supported by strong experimental evidence. The experiments are thorough and convincingly illustrate that the proposed architecture enables the stable training of networks that are substantially deeper than what was previously practical. The resulting improvements in classification accuracy clearly validate the method's effectiveness.

*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written, and its core ideas are communicated with excellent clarity. The motivation for the information highways is well-argued, and the technical description of the architecture is easy to follow. The main message is delivered effectively and persuasively.

**Areas for Improvement:**

*   While the experimental results are compelling, the analysis of *why* the method works so well could be deepened. The paper would benefit from a more detailed investigation into the behavior of the highway gates during training. For instance, providing visualizations or statistics on how these gates operate at different depths and at various stages of learning could offer valuable insights into the dynamics of information flow. Such an analysis could help answer questions about whether certain layers learn to primarily carry information while others focus on transformation.

*   A more extensive ablation study could further strengthen the authors' claims. Examining the precise contribution of different components of the proposed architecture would provide a more complete picture of the method's mechanics and justify the specific design choices made.

**Conclusion:**

Overall, this is a high-quality paper presenting a novel, significant, and well-executed contribution to the field of deep learning. It effectively addresses a critical problem and supports its claims with strong empirical results. The work is clearly presented and should be of great interest to the NIPS community.
