This paper introduces a novel architecture, called highway networks, that facilitates the training of very deep neural networks. The authors draw inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks and propose a modification to the traditional feedforward network architecture. By incorporating adaptive gating units, information can flow freely across many layers, allowing for the training of extremely deep networks using simple gradient descent.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed architecture, including a comparison with existing methods for training deep networks. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks on several benchmark datasets, including MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100. The results show that highway networks can be trained to achieve state-of-the-art performance on these datasets, even with a large number of layers.

One of the key contributions of this paper is the demonstration that highway networks can be trained without the need for specialized initialization schemes or optimization techniques. The authors show that the proposed architecture can be trained using standard stochastic gradient descent, making it a more accessible and widely applicable solution for training deep networks.

The paper also provides an insightful analysis of the behavior of the transform gates in the highway networks. The authors show that the gates can learn to dynamically adjust the routing of information based on the input, allowing the network to adapt to different patterns and structures in the data.

A potential concern with the proposed architecture is that many layers may remain unused if the transform gates stay closed. However, the authors provide evidence that this is not the case, and that the network can learn to utilize all of its layers to achieve good performance.

Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning, providing a novel and effective solution for training very deep neural networks. The idea is simple and elegant, and the analysis is solid. The authors are encouraged to try the network on bigger datasets such as ImageNet to further demonstrate its effectiveness.

Strengths:

* Novel and effective solution for training very deep neural networks
* Simple and elegant idea inspired by LSTM
* Solid analysis and experimental results
* Demonstrates the ability to train deep networks without specialized initialization schemes or optimization techniques

Weaknesses:

* Limited analysis of the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed architecture
* No comparison with other state-of-the-art methods for training deep networks

Suggestions for future work:

* Try the network on bigger datasets such as ImageNet
* Investigate the computational cost and efficiency of the proposed architecture
* Compare with other state-of-the-art methods for training deep networks
* Explore the application of highway networks to other domains and tasks.

Arguments for acceptance:

* Novel and effective solution for training very deep neural networks
* Solid analysis and experimental results
* Demonstrates the ability to train deep networks without specialized initialization schemes or optimization techniques

Arguments against acceptance:
