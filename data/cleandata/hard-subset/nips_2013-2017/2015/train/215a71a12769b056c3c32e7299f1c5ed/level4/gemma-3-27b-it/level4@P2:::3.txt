This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep neural networks. The core idea is to incorporate LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms that allow for unimpeded information flow across many layers, mitigating the vanishing gradient problem that often hinders the training of deep feedforward networks.

The paper is well-written and presents a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method. The motivation for addressing the challenges of training deep networks is well-established, referencing the recent empirical successes achieved through increased network depth and the limitations imposed by optimization difficulties. The authors effectively position their work within the existing landscape of techniques aimed at improving deep network training, such as improved optimizers, initialization strategies, and skip connections, highlighting how highway networks offer a distinct advantage. The originality of the approach lies in the application of adaptive gating, inspired by LSTMs, to feedforward architectures, enabling the training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard gradient descent. 

The strengths of this work are substantial. The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the ability to train extremely deep highway networks, surpassing the performance of comparable plain networks, particularly as depth increases. The analysis of information routing and layer importance (Section 4) provides valuable insights into the behavior of these networks, showcasing how they dynamically adjust information flow based on the input and utilize a significant proportion of their layers even for complex tasks. The ablation study showing layer importance is particularly compelling. Furthermore, the authors provide readily available code and hyperparameters, promoting reproducibility and further research.

However, there are a few points for consideration. While the paper mentions a similar LSTM-inspired model proposed concurrently [32], a more detailed comparison highlighting the key differences in architecture and performance would be beneficial. The paper could also benefit from a discussion on the computational cost associated with the increased number of parameters introduced by the gating mechanism. Although the authors demonstrate successful training, a more comprehensive analysis of the hyperparameter sensitivity (beyond the initial bias for the transform gates) would strengthen the findings. Finally, the authors acknowledge that better performance could be achieved with improved architectures and hyperparameters on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, and outlining potential avenues for future optimization would add value.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture represents a novel approach to addressing the training difficulties of very deep networks.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper provides compelling experimental evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of highway networks in training networks with significantly more layers than previously achievable.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written, clearly explaining the proposed method and its advantages.
*   **Reproducibility:** The authors provide code and hyperparameters, facilitating reproducibility.
*   **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis of information routing and layer importance provides valuable insights into the behavior of the networks.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Limited Comparison to Related Work:** A more detailed comparison to the concurrently proposed LSTM-inspired model [32] would be beneficial.
*   **Computational Cost Analysis:** A more thorough discussion of the computational cost associated with the gating mechanism is lacking.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** A more comprehensive analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity would strengthen the results.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway network architecture effectively addresses a long-standing challenge in training very deep networks, and the experimental results are compelling. Despite minor areas for improvement, the originality, clarity, and impact of this work warrant acceptance. I recommend accepting this paper to NIPS 2015.
