- Overall: Well-crafted, timely paper with a clear core idea and convincing empirical results; strongest contribution is analysis of gate-output distributions beyond raw performance.

- Backpropagation through gate T: Gradients in Eq.5 appear inconsistent with a fully differentiable sigmoid gate—provide explicit gradient derivations for gate params, transform params, and inputs, and state/justify any approximations (e.g., straight-through, stopped gradients).

- Initialization and bias: The depth-scaled negative bias needs sensitivity analysis—run sweeps over initial biases, report training dynamics (gradient norms, convergence, stability), and track empirical “effective depth” (T distribution and its evolution).

- Comparisons and experimental settings: Clarify and align or qualify comparisons—provide exact datasets/splits, preprocessing, model capacity, training budget, optimization, and evaluation protocols or reimplement baselines under matched settings.

- Reporting best vs. mean: Replace “max” accuracy with mean ± std (or SE), number of runs, and where appropriate median/IQR or confidence intervals to avoid overstating results.

- Learning-theoretic perspective and selectivity: Discuss how gating affects capacity/regularization, relate to path-based capacity and implicit ensembles, quantify selectivity (sparsity of open gates, path lengths, mutual information), and compare to residuals/dropout/conditional computation.

- Additional reproducibility/analysis suggestions: Provide pseudo-code for forward/backward passes with shapes and parameter counts; include diagnostic plots of gate outputs across depth/time and correlate with performance; report computational overhead (params, FLOPs, wall-clock); clarify whether depth-dependent biases are learned or fixed and whether they are tied across layers.

- Overall recommendation: Promising and impactful work but requires clarifications on gradient formulation, initialization study, controlled comparisons, and statistically sound reporting before acceptance.
