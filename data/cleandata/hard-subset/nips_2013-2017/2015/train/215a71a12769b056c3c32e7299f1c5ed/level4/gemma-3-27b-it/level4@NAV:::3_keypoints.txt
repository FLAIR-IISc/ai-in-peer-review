- Proposes a novel method using LSTM-like units to enable training of extremely deep neural networks.  
- Paper is strong, clearly written, original, and presents compelling results.  
- Directly tackles long-standing issues of initialization and unstable gradients in deep networks.  
- Demonstrates successful training of networks with 100 layers.  
- Experiments on information flow and layer relevance are insightful and well-presented.  
- Correction needed: Section 4.1’s claim “most transform blocks are active on average” is inaccurate; should state they are “not active on average.”  
- Suggestion: discuss how transform gates compare to simple identity initialization (possibly with noise).  
- Overall very positive assessment — an excellent, promising paper.
