- Proposes using LSTM units to train extremely deep neural networks, addressing a long-standing challenge.
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly motivated.
- Tackles critical problems (vanishing/exploding gradients and poor information flow) that hinder very deep architectures.
- Demonstrates successful training of networks exceeding 100 layers, a significant advance.
- Contributions are strong both theoretically and empirically.
- Experimental analysis of information routing and layer-wise importance is particularly compelling.
- Results are convincing and supported by thorough ablation studies.
- Revision needed: Section 4.1 should state that most transform blocks are inactive on average (reflecting observed sparsity), not active.
- Clarification requested: explain how the transform gates differ from simply initializing layers as identity (with minor noise).
