- Proposes an architecture with input-dependent gates that carry activations unchanged through layers, enabling much deeper feedforward networks by preserving information flow.
- Well-motivated and clearly presented contribution that addresses optimization issues of depth.
- Provides a careful, interpretable analysis of learned gating behavior, showing how networks balance transformation and carry paths and aid gradient flow and reuse.
- Positions itself appropriately relative to prior work (e.g., LSTMs, FitNets) with clear architectural distinctions and explanations.
- Empirical results are strong: can train notably deeper models than vanilla baselines and achieves improved or competitive performance.
- Experiments are broad and include useful diagnostic analyses that convincingly support the approach’s effectiveness.
- Paper is clear, original, technically sound, and methodologically simple yet powerful.
- Minor editorial issue: duplicate reference to “Tables 3 and 3” on p.5; no substantive improvements suggested.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
