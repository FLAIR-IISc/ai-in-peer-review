- Reviewer had considered LSTM-inspired gradient-trapping to grow deep nets and congratulates the authors for successfully implementing it.
- Offers perspective: very deep nets can be seen as shallow nets whose "layers" contain sub-layers, producing richer per-layer nonlinearity.
- Notes this perspective aligns with GoogLeNet inception modules, VGG’s stacked 3×3 convolutions, and may explain highway networks.
- Suggests an experiment: train one thin-and-tall network on both MNIST and CIFAR-10 with an early shared segment to test cross-domain parameter reuse.
- Key experimental question: do parameters underutilized for MNIST get recruited by CIFAR-10, showing how sharing operates in deep hierarchies.
- Encourages evaluation on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet).
- No major concerns; praises the paper’s simple, elegant LSTM-inspired idea and solid theoretical analysis.
