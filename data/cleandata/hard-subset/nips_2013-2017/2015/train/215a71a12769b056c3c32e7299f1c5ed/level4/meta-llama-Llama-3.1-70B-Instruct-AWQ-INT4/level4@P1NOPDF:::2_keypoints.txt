- Proposes an innovative architecture for very deep networks that lets inputs be "carried" unchanged through layers via input-dependent gates.  
- Gate design enables training of substantially deeper networks compared to standard feed-forward architectures.  
- Authors provide a thorough, well-executed analysis of how gates select inputs across different layers.  
- Analysis is precisely the kind of investigation appropriate for this work.  
- Paper is high quality, clearly written, original, and achieves promising results.  
- Similarities to FitNets and LSTMs are acknowledged and differences are clearly explained with comparisons.  
- Reviewer has no suggested improvements.  
- Minor error: paper refers to "Tables 3 and 3" (duplication) on page 5.  
- Overall assessment: a very solid paper with a novel technique, good results, and interesting analysis.
