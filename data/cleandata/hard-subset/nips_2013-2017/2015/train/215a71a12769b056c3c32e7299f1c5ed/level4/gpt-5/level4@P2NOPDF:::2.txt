This paper introduces an architecture for very deep networks that uses input-dependent gates to carry inputs unchanged through layers. This mechanism enables the effective training of much deeper models than standard feed-forward architectures.

The authors provide an insightful analysis of the trained networks, examining how gates select and route information across different layers—exactly the kind of diagnostic study I was hoping to see.

The paper is high quality, clearly written, and original. While there are natural connections to recent work (notably FitNets and LSTMs), the distinctions are clearly articulated, and appropriate empirical comparisons are included.

The results are promising. I have no substantive suggestions for improvement. One minor issue: the text references “Tables 3 and 3” (p. 5).

Overall, this is a very strong paper. The proposed technique is novel, achieves good performance, and is supported by thorough and informative analysis.
