This paper proposes a gated architecture for very deep networks that allows inputs to be carried forward unchanged through layers via input-dependent gates. This enables training much deeper networks than would be feasible with a standard feedforward architecture.

The authors conduct an insightful analysis of the trained models, examining how the gates select their inputs across different layers—exactly the kind of investigation I was hoping to see.

The paper is high quality, clearly written, and original. While there are natural similarities to recent work (notably FitNets and LSTMs), the differences are clearly articulated, and comparisons are made where appropriate. The results are promising.

I have no substantive suggestions for improvement. Minor note: on p. 5, the text refers to “Tables 3 and 3.”

Overall, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel, achieves good results, and is analyzed thoroughly.
