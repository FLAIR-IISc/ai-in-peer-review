- Reviewer considered using LSTM-style gradient clipping to train deeper networks (not implemented) and congratulates authors for being first to explore this approach.
- Proposes alternative view: a very deep network can be seen as a shallower network whose layers contain sub-layers, analogous to Inception modules and VGG stacked convolutions, and asks if this applies to highway networks.
- Suggests an experiment: train one tall-and-thin network on both MNIST and CIFAR-10 (possibly with an input branching) to study parameter sharing and whether weights underutilized on MNIST are leveraged for CIFAR-10.
- Recommends evaluating the approach on larger datasets such as ImageNet.
- Overall judgment: idea is LSTM-inspired, simple and elegant, enables training very deep feedforward networks, analysis is thorough, and there are no major criticisms.
