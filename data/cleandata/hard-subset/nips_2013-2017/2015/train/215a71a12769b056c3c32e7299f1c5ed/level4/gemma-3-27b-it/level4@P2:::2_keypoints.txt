- Introduces highway networks: an architecture using adaptive gating (inspired by LSTM) to enable very deep feedforward networks.  
- Gating permits unimpeded information and gradient flow, allowing training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard SGD.  
- Places contribution in context of depth-driven advances (e.g., AlexNet, VGG, Inception) and prior remedies (optimizers, initialization, skip connections).  
- Clearly links the design inspiration to LSTM recurrent networks.  
- Technically sound and well-motivated approach addressing vanishing gradients.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized with consistent notation and clear explanations.  
- High originality by novel combination of LSTM-like gates and skip connections.  
- Convincing experimental results on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 demonstrating effective training and generalization.  
- Detailed analysis of trained networks (Figures 2 & 4) provides insights into gating behavior and information routing.  
- Minor weaknesses: a typographical error ("Tables 3 and 3") noted.  
- Could better discuss limitations of related methods like FitNets (e.g., computational budget) for clearer comparison.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/effective architecture, clear presentation, strong experiments, insightful analysis, significant contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: only minor issues (typo and limited comparative discussion).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant paper; strongly recommended for acceptance.
