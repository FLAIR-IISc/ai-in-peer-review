- Intriguing, well-explained idea with experiments that are convincing to a degree.  
- Strength: authors analyze learned networks with respect to gate output patterns.  
- Concern: Gate T uses a sigmoid so outputs aren’t exactly 0/1, calling into question gradient accuracy in Equation 5; authors must explain how backpropagation is performed.  
- Concern: Initialization effects are complex; constant negative biases by level are unconvincing—include a plot of performance vs initial bias since bias acts as a regularizer favoring “longer highways.”  
- Concern: The phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings” is ambiguous; clarify experimental setting differences to justify direct comparisons.  
- Concern: Reporting “max” accuracies is statistically unsound; provide mean accuracies, standard deviations, and other statistical information for each method.  
- Suggestion: Analyze the approach from a learning-theory perspective to explain how input-dependent gates enable identity-like layers and selective propagation through many layers.  
- Overall: Addressing these clarifications and analyses would substantially strengthen the manuscript.
