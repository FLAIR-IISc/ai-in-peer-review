The paper proposes a generalization for training neural networks with many stacked layers. The “information highway” idea is demonstrated to work well through experiments, and the authors show that it improves classification accuracy.

Quality: The idea appears novel, and the experimental evaluation is solid. The analysis sections could benefit from a broader variety of details or additional illustrative material.

Clarity: The main messages are well delivered and quite clear.

Originality: The method is sufficiently original, even though it generalizes earlier neural network techniques such as cross-layer connections.

Significance: The paper is significant for the field, as training increasingly deep neural networks is important for achieving better results in application areas.

Overall, the paper presents an effective method for training very deep neural networks via “information highways,” i.e., direct connections to upper layers. Although it generalizes prior techniques like cross-layer connections, the authors demonstrate effectiveness through experimentation, and the contributions are novel and well supported by empirical evidence.
