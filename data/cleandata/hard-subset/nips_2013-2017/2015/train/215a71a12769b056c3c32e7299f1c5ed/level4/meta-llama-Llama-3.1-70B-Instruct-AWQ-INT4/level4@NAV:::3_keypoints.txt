- Proposes using LSTM-style units to train very deep neural networks.
- Manuscript is high-quality, well-written, novel, and yields intriguing results.
- Directly addresses initialization and vanishing/exploding gradient issues that limit network depth.
- Successfully demonstrates training of 100-layer networks.
- Experiments on information routing and layer importance are especially noteworthy.
- Correction: Section 4.1 should read 'not active on average' instead of 'most transform blocks are active on average.'
- Suggestion: discuss identity-matrix (plus small noise) initialization as an alternative and clarify how transform gates differ.
- Overall: a compelling method for deep network training and a pleasure to read.
