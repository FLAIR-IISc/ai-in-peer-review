This paper proposes leveraging LSTM-style units as a mechanism for training extremely deep networks.
This is a high-quality, clearly written submission. The idea is novel and the results are quite compelling.
Researchers in machine learning often see gains by stacking more layers, yet issues with initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients have been a major bottleneck. Indeed, the challenge of transmitting information through deep architectures arguably limited their broad adoption for decades. This work confronts the problem directly and demonstrates a way to train networks with roughly 100 layers.
The experiments on information routing and the assessment of layer importance are particularly striking.

Specific comments:
- In Section 4.1, the statement “most transform blocks are active on average” appears to be a slip; it should likely read “not active on average.”
- One could initialize each layer of a very deep model to the identity matrix (perhaps with small noise). It would be helpful to elaborate on how the transform gates distinguish highway networks from this simple identity-initialization scheme.

Overall, this is an excellent paper that presents a method for training very deep neural networks, and it was a pleasure to read.
