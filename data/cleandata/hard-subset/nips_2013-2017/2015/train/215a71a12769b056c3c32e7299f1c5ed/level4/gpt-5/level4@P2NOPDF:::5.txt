This paper presents an LSTM-inspired mechanism that enables training very deep feedforward networks. The idea is simple and elegant, and the analyses are solid.

I had independently considered using LSTM-style gating to preserve gradients and make deeper architectures trainable, but had not pursued it. Congratulations to the authors for executing this approach convincingly.

An alternative perspective that may clarify the contribution is to view a very deep network as a shallower network whose “layers” contain sub-layers, yielding more complex nonlinear transformations than standard ReLU/sigmoid layers. This is how I interpret the Inception modules in GoogLeNet and the stacks of 3×3 convolutions in VGG. It would be interesting to discuss whether Highway Networks can be understood in the same way.

I also suggest an experiment: train a single thin and tall (narrow and deep) network jointly on MNIST and CIFAR, branching only at the top into two task-specific heads. At inference, examine whether the network exhibits patterns similar to those found in models trained separately on each dataset, and whether parameters that are underutilized for MNIST are leveraged by CIFAR. This could shed light on how sharing emerges in a very deep hierarchy and how efficient that sharing is.
