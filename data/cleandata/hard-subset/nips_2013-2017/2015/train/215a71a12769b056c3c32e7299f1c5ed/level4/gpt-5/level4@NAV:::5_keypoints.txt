- Reviewer had independently considered an LSTM-style gradient-preserving trick and praises the authors for implementing it first.
- Offers an alternative viewpoint: very deep networks can be seen as shallower networks whose layers contain complex sublayers (e.g., Inception, stacked VGG blocks); asks if highway networks fit this interpretation.
- Suggests an experiment: train a single thin-and-tall network on both MNIST and CIFAR with a fork at the top to study shared vs task-specific representations.
- Wants to know if the jointly trained network exhibits the same inference patterns as separately trained models and whether underutilized MNIST parameters are repurposed for CIFAR.
- No major complaints; encourages evaluation on larger datasets such as ImageNet.
- Overall judgment: the LSTM-inspired idea is simple, elegant, and supported by solid analyses.
