- Proposes LSTM-inspired units and a new "highway networks" architecture enabling unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Idea is original and the results are interesting and convincing.  
- Addresses long-standing initialization and exploding/vanishing gradient problems limiting deep networks.  
- Demonstrates training of very deep networks, including 100-layer nets.  
- Provides a thorough review of prior work (optimizers, initialization, skip connections, deep supervision).  
- Discusses limitations of previous approaches and how highway networks overcome them.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.  
- Experiments are well-designed and effectively demonstrate the method’s benefits.  
- Routing of information and layer-importance experiments are particularly noteworthy.
