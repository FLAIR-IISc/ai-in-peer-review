- Introduces "highway networks," using learned gating units inspired by LSTMs to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Addresses vanishing/exploding gradient problems by regulating information flow across layers.  
- Concept is intuitive, connects nicely to LSTM gating, and the theoretical analysis is solid.  
- Empirical results on MNIST and CIFAR demonstrate the method's promise.  
- Novel insight: adapts the "gradient trapping" principle from recurrent to feedforward architectures.  
- Suggests situating the work relative to "networks of networks" (e.g., Inception, VGG) and whether stacked highway layers act as a single expressive meta-layer.  
- Proposes a multi-task experiment (joint MNIST+CIFAR) to study task-dependent gating and effective depth/resource sharing.  
- Recommends demonstrating scalability on large datasets like ImageNet to validate practical utility.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated paper with convincing initial results and a strong recommendation for acceptance.
