- Proposes an innovative method to train very deep networks by using LSTM units as layer-wise transform blocks.
- Paper is high-quality, well-written, original, and yields compelling empirical results.
- Tackles core deep-learning issues (vanishing/exploding gradients, poor initialization) and successfully trains networks with 100 layers.
- Key idea: leverage LSTM gating/statefulness to control information flow and regulate gradient propagation across many layers.
- Experiments and visualizations demonstrate effective information routing and show that layers contribute unequally, with implications for compression and interpretability.
- Specific correction: Section 4.1’s claim “most transform blocks are active on average” should be revised to “not active on average” per the results.
- Recommend adding a brief comparison to Highway Networks and to identity-initialization baselines, explaining how LSTM dynamics go beyond those simpler schemes.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution, thorough experiments, clear writing — recommend acceptance.
