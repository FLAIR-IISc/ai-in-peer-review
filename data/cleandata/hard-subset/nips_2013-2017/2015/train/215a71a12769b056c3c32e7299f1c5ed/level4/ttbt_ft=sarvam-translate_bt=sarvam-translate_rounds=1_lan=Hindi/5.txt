I was also thinking about using the LSTML trick to deepen the network by trapping gradients, but I haven't started working on it yet. First of all, congratulations to the authors for getting there!

There's probably a different perspective: a very deep network isn't as deep as a network where there are sub-layers in layers. Each "layer" has more complex non-linear behavior than a traditional rectifier/sigmoid layer. I personally see the beginning of GoogleNet as such, 3 stacked 3x3 in VGG, etc., and I wonder if this is even a valid way to look at highway networks.

There's an experiment I would suggest the authors try: training both MNIST and CIFAR using a thin and long network (you can fork from the top for the two domains). My interest lies in whether, at prediction time, the network exhibits the same pattern found in the network trained separately for each domain, whether the model parameters picked up by CIFAR are used less in MNIST. In other words, I would like to see how sharing occurs in a very deep hierarchy, and whether it is as efficient as possible.

No major complaints. I encourage the authors to try the network on larger datasets like ImageNet, etc.
