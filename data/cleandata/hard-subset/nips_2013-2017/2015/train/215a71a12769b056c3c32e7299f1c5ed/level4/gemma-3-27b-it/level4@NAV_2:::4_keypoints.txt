- Proposal: Introduces highway networks — an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism enabling unimpeded information flow to train very deep feedforward networks.  
- Technical soundness: Method is well-founded and shown to be trainable with SGD even for hundreds of layers.  
- Empirical evaluation: Comprehensive experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 with compelling comparisons to plain nets and FitNets.  
- Analysis strengths: Provides useful analysis of transform gate behavior and layer importance (Figures 2 & 4) and information routing (Figure 3).  
- Suggested additional analysis: Would benefit from sensitivity experiments for different gating functions and regularization strategies.  
- Missing discussion: Lacks a comparison of computational cost relative to alternative deep architectures.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, consistent notation, and helpful visual aids.  
- Originality: Novel combination of skip connections and gating; LSTM-like gating applied to feedforward networks is a distinct contribution.  
- Significance: Enables training much deeper networks, with practical gains on CIFAR tasks and potential to spur further research.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a critical problem, proposes an effective novel architecture, shows strong empirical results, and offers insightful analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Needs broader experimental exploration (hyperparameter sensitivity, compute cost) and deeper theoretical analysis of the gating mechanism.
