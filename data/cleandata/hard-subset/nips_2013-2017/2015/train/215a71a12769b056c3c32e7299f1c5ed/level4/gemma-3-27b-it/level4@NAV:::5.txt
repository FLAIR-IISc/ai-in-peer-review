This work presents a compelling approach to training very deep feedforward networks, drawing inspiration from the LSTM's gradient management techniques. The authors deserve recognition for successfully implementing this concept, an idea I had also considered but not yet pursued.

An alternative, and perhaps complementary, viewpoint is that these very deep networks can be re-interpreted as networks with complex, multi-layered “sub-layers” within each traditional layer – analogous to the inception modules in GoogLeNet or stacked convolutions in VGG. It would be interesting to explore if this perspective applies to highway networks as well.

I suggest an experiment to further investigate the network’s representational capacity: train a single, deep network on both MNIST and CIFAR-10, branching the network at the input for domain-specific processing. Observing whether, at inference time, this shared network exhibits similar patterns to independently trained networks, and if underutilized parameters in one domain are effectively leveraged by the other, would provide valuable insight into hierarchical feature sharing and its efficiency.

Overall, this is a strong paper with no significant weaknesses. I recommend the authors extend their evaluation to larger datasets like ImageNet. The core idea is both simple and elegant, and the accompanying analysis is well-executed.
