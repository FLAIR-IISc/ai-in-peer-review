- Introduces highway networks that enable direct training of extremely deep feedforward nets (100+ layers) via adaptive transform/carry gating.  
- Core innovation: gating mechanism (Eq. 3) with negative bias initialization that biases gates toward "carry" to maintain stable gradient flow.  
- Addresses depth-related optimization degradation without multi-stage pretraining or distillation.  
- Experiments show highway nets outperform plain nets at large depths and match/exceed CIFAR-10/100 SOTA with fewer parameters than maxout/FitNets.  
- Strong analysis of gate dynamics revealing input-dependent routing and per-layer importance, enabling study of depth utilization.  
- Originality: feedforward adaptive gating distinguishes the model from LSTMs and fixed skip connections; negative-bias init is a novel, LSTM-inspired tactic.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: architecture, experiments, and figures are clear; public code released.  
- Weakness: technical imprecision in Eq.5 claiming gradients are “exactly” I or H' when sigmoid gates never reach 0/1 — should state “approach” as T → 0/1.  
- Weakness: recommendation of initial biases (-1/-2/-3) for different depths lacks empirical ablation or supporting plots.  
- Weakness: inconsistent statistical reporting — tables show only best accuracies while text claims mean ± std.dev; tables should include variability.  
- Weakness: insufficient explicit comparison/distinction from similar LSTM-inspired work ([32]) to fully establish novelty.  
- Overall: strong, significant, and well-validated contribution with minor fixable issues; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Pro/Con: Pro — enables >100-layer training, clear gate analysis, outperforms single-stage methods; Con — gradient equation inaccuracy, missing bias ablation, inconsistent stats, need clearer differentiation from related work.
