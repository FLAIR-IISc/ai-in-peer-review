This paper proposes highway networks, a novel architecture inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks, to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to introduce adaptive gating mechanisms that allow for unimpeded information flow across many layers, mitigating the vanishing gradient problem that typically hinders the training of deep networks. The authors demonstrate that these highway networks can be successfully trained with hundreds of layers using standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD), achieving strong performance on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 datasets.

This is a strong paper addressing a fundamental challenge in deep learning: training very deep networks. The motivation is well-articulated, building upon the recent empirical successes achieved through increased network depth and acknowledging the difficulties associated with training such networks. The proposed highway network architecture is a clever and intuitive approach, drawing inspiration from the well-established LSTM framework. The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating that highway networks can be trained effectively to depths previously inaccessible with standard techniques, and achieve competitive performance. The analysis of information routing and layer importance provides valuable insights into the behavior of these networks. The paper is also exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture represents a novel approach to address the challenges of training very deep feedforward networks. While inspired by LSTMs, its application to feedforward networks is a significant contribution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method is well-defined and mathematically sound. The use of transform and carry gates provides a flexible mechanism for controlling information flow.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The authors provide thorough experimental validation on multiple datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100) demonstrating the effectiveness of highway networks. The comparisons with existing methods, such as FitNets, are particularly informative.
*   **Clarity & Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized. The notation is consistent, and the explanations are concise and easy to follow.
*   **Analysis & Insights:** The analysis of information routing and layer importance provides valuable insights into the internal workings of highway networks, demonstrating how they utilize depth and potentially learn data-dependent routing strategies.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Comparison to other Depth-Enabling Techniques:** While the paper mentions several related approaches (e.g., better optimizers, initialization strategies, skip connections), it could benefit from a more detailed comparative analysis. For example, a direct comparison with residual networks (ResNets) [He et al., 2015], which have become a dominant approach for training deep networks, would be valuable.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The influence of the transform gate bias initialization is mentioned, but a more systematic study of hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly with respect to network depth and dataset complexity, would strengthen the analysis.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** While the empirical results are strong, a more detailed theoretical justification for the effectiveness of highway networks would be beneficial. Exploring the connection between highway networks and gradient propagation, or providing bounds on the gradient norm, could offer deeper insights.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks, addressing a longstanding challenge in the field.
*   The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating the practical benefits of highway networks on multiple datasets.
*   The analysis of information routing and layer importance provides valuable insights into the behavior of these networks.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparative analysis with other state-of-the-art techniques for training deep networks, such as ResNets.
*   The theoretical justification for the effectiveness of highway networks is limited.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway network architecture is a novel and effective approach for training very deep networks, and the experimental results are compelling. While a more detailed comparative analysis and theoretical justification would be beneficial, the strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses. I strongly recommend acceptance.

**Minor Comments:**

*   In Section 4.1, the phrase "most transform blocks are active on average" appears to be a typo and should likely be "not active on average."
*   Discussing how the transform gates differentiate highway networks from simply initializing each layer with an identity matrix would provide further clarification on the contribution of this work.
