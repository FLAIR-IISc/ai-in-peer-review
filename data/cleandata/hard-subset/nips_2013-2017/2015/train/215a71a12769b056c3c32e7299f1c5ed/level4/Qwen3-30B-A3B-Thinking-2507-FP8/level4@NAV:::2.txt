

This paper introduces an architecture for very deep networks that allows input to pass through layers unmodified via input-dependent gating mechanisms. This enables significantly deeper networks to be trained compared to standard feedforward approaches. The authors provide a compelling analysis of how gates select inputs across different layers of the trained networks, exactly the kind of investigation I anticipated. The paper is well-written, original, and of high quality. While inherently related to recent work (e.g., FitNets and LSTMs), the distinctions are clearly articulated and appropriate comparisons are included. Promising results are achieved. No revisions are recommended. Note: "Tables 3 and 3" (p. 5). This represents a strong contribution; the method is novel, effective, and the analysis is both insightful and comprehensive.
