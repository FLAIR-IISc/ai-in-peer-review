### Summary of the Paper

This paper addresses the significant challenge of training very deep feedforward neural networks. The authors propose a novel architecture, termed "Highway Networks," which is inspired by the gating mechanisms in Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks. The core idea is to introduce adaptive gating units that regulate information flow through the network. Each layer can dynamically choose to either transform its input, as in a conventional network, or carry it through unchanged. This "information highway" mechanism allows for unimpeded gradient and information flow across many layers, effectively mitigating the optimization difficulties that plague standard deep networks.

The authors provide strong empirical evidence supporting their claims. Through a series of controlled experiments on MNIST, they demonstrate that while the performance of "plain" networks degrades significantly with depth, highway networks with up to 100 layers can be trained effectively using standard SGD. Furthermore, on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, they show that highway networks can match or surpass the performance of architectures like FitNets, which require a more complex, multi-stage training procedure, while using only direct, single-stage training. A key contribution of the paper is a detailed analysis of the trained networks, which provides insights into the learned behavior of the gates, showing that they perform data-dependent routing and can effectively learn the necessary computational depth for a given task.

### Strengths and Arguments for Acceptance

This is an excellent and well-executed paper that makes a significant contribution to the field.

1.  **High Significance and Originality:** The paper introduces a simple, elegant, and effective solution to the very important problem of training extremely deep neural networks. While inspired by LSTMs, the application of learnable gating to control information flow in a deep feedforward architecture is a novel and impactful idea. It provides a principled alternative to other contemporary approaches like complex initializations, layer-wise pre-training, or fixed skip-connections, which were prevalent at the time.

2.  **Strong and Convincing Experiments:** The experimental validation is thorough and compelling.
    *   The optimization experiments on MNIST (Section 3.1) provide a clear and direct comparison against plain networks, convincingly demonstrating that the highway architecture overcomes the degradation problem associated with increasing depth.
    *   The comparison with FitNets is particularly strong. Showing that highway networks can be trained directly to achieve similar or better performance than a method requiring a complex, hint-based training procedure is a powerful result that highlights the practical advantages of the proposed architecture.
    *   The results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 are competitive and demonstrate that the benefits of the architecture translate to challenging, real-world datasets.

3.  **Insightful Analysis:** A major strength of the paper is the analysis presented in Section 4. The authors go beyond simply reporting performance metrics and investigate *how* the trained networks function. The visualizations of gate activities and the lesion study are particularly insightful. They provide strong evidence for two desirable properties:
    *   **Data-Dependent Routing:** The network learns to route information differently for different inputs, suggesting the gates are performing a non-trivial computational role beyond simply easing optimization.
    *   **Automatic Depth Adaptation:** The lesion study suggests that the network learns to use as much depth as is necessary for the complexity of the task (using most layers for CIFAR-100, but leaving many idle for the simpler MNIST). This is a highly valuable property for designing deep architectures.

4.  **Exceptional Clarity:** The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The motivation is well-established, the highway layer formulation is presented concisely, and the experimental setup and results are easy to follow. The connection to LSTMs is explained well, providing excellent context for the work.

### Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement

The paper is very strong, and the weaknesses are minor. They are offered primarily as suggestions for further strengthening the work or for future research.

1.  **Sensitivity to Gate Bias Initialization:** The authors state that initializing the transform gate bias `bT` to a negative value is important for training. While they provide general guidelines, a more detailed analysis of the sensitivity to this hyperparameter would be valuable. For example, what happens if `bT` is initialized to 0 or a positive value? Does training fail completely, or just become slower? An ablation study on this initialization would further clarify its role.

2.  **Comparison to Fixed Skip Connections:** The paper argues that the adaptive gating is a key advantage over fixed "skip" connections. While the data-dependent routing analysis provides indirect support for this claim, a direct empirical comparison to a baseline with a simple, fixed identity mapping (e.g., `y = H(x, WH) + x`) would have been a powerful addition. This would explicitly quantify the benefit of the learnable, adaptive gates over a non-adaptive shortcut.

3.  **Computational Overhead:** The transform gates add extra parameters and computations to each layer. While the number of parameters is likely modest compared to the transform `H`, a brief discussion of the computational overhead (in terms of FLOPs and parameters) compared to a plain network would be helpful for practitioners assessing the trade-offs of the architecture.

### Conclusion

This is a very strong, well-written, and impactful paper that I recommend for acceptance. The proposed highway network architecture is a novel and significant contribution that directly addresses a major roadblock in deep learning research. The method is simple, well-motivated, and supported by a comprehensive set of experiments and an excellent analysis that provides valuable insights into the behavior of very deep models. This work is likely to be highly influential and to inspire a great deal of follow-up research.
