An alternative perspective: extremely deep networks may be conceptualized as shallower architectures where individual layers incorporate sub-layers. Each composite "layer" exhibits more complex nonlinear dynamics than standard rectifier/sigmoid units. This aligns with the design philosophy of GoogLeNet's inception modules, VGG's stacked 3×3 convolutions, and potentially offers a valid lens for interpreting highway networks.  

I recommend the authors conduct an additional experiment using a single thin-and-tall network trained jointly on MNIST and CIFAR-10 (with shared initial layers for both domains). Specifically, I am interested in whether, during inference, the network demonstrates the same parameter utilization patterns observed in separately trained domain-specific models—particularly whether parameters underutilized for MNIST are effectively leveraged by CIFAR-10. This would clarify how parameter sharing operates within deep hierarchical structures and its efficiency.
