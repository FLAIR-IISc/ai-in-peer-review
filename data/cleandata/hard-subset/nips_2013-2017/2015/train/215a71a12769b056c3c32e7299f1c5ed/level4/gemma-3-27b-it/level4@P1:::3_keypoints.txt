- Proposes LSTM-inspired gating (“highway networks”) to enable unimpeded information flow across many feedforward layers.  
- Addresses vanishing/exploding gradients and poor activation propagation in very deep networks.  
- Demonstrates successful training of networks with 100+ layers.  
- Experiments show insightful analysis of information routing and the importance of individual layers.  
- Shows highway networks learn to utilize depth meaningfully, with different layers contributing differently.  
- Paper is high-quality, well-written, and easy to follow.  
- Approach is original and technically sound, supported by experimental results.  
- Significant contribution: enables research and performance gains from much deeper networks.  
- Comment: Section 4.1 contains a potentially misleading phrase about transform block activity and needs clarification.  
- Suggestion: discuss how adaptive gating differs from simpler identity initialization strategies to highlight learned gate behavior.  
- Minor weakness: practical benefits beyond MNIST and CIFAR are not fully demonstrated; larger/complex datasets would strengthen the paper.
