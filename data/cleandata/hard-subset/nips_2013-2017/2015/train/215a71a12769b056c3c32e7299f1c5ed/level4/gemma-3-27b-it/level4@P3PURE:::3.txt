This paper proposes the use of LSTM units as a method for training very deep networks. It is a high-quality, well-written contribution with an original idea and compelling results.

Machine learning researchers have consistently observed performance gains by increasing the depth of neural networks; however, challenges related to initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients have historically limited this approach. Indeed, these difficulties in enabling information flow through deep networks arguably hindered their widespread adoption for three decades. This paper directly addresses this problem and demonstrates a successful method for training networks with 100 layers. 

The experiments illustrating information routing and layer importance are particularly insightful. 

Specific comments:

In Section 4.1, the statement “most transform blocks are active on average” appears to be inaccurate and should be revised to “not active on average.”

Initializing each layer of a very deep network with the identity matrix (potentially with a small amount of noise) provides a baseline for comparison. It might be valuable to comment on how transform gates differentiate this approach from such a simple scheme, and from highway networks in general. 

Overall, this is an excellent paper demonstrating a viable method for training very deep neural networks and was a pleasure to read.
