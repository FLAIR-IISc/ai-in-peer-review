- Introduces highway networks: adaptive gating (transform T and carry C=1−T) to enable training very deep feedforward nets (100+ layers) with standard SGD.
- Gates inspired by LSTM allow dynamic routing of information through "information highways" across layers.
- Highway layers let the network choose to transform inputs or pass them unchanged, maintaining stable gradient flow and mitigating vanishing gradients.
- Empirical results: 100-layer highway networks show >2 orders of magnitude lower loss and faster convergence than plain networks on MNIST.
- Generalization: matches or exceeds state-of-the-art on CIFAR-10/100 in a single training stage, outperforming FitNets’ two-stage approach.
- Detailed analysis of gate behavior shows layer-specific activity, data-dependent routing, and adaptive depth utilization (more depth used for complex tasks).
- Provides ablation studies on gate initialization, layer importance, and comparisons to relevant baselines.
- Technical contributions are rigorously motivated and supported by theoretical analysis (Equations 4–5) and empirical validation.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with effective figures visualizing optimization, gate dynamics, and layer utilization.
- Minor clarity issues: brief ResNet comparison suggested, clarify relation to LSTM “unrolled” block, and fix typo "Tables 3 and 3".
- Originality: dynamic, input-dependent gating is distinct from prior fixed skip connections and FitNets’ static teacher-student method.
- Advantage over prior work: can be trained without specialized initialization required by some earlier methods.
- Limitation: experiments limited to MNIST and CIFAR (no ImageNet results) and depth still constrained by computational budget.
- Minor weakness: limited comparison to ResNets (emerged shortly after) and gate computation overhead not quantified.
- Overall assessment: significant, impactful contribution that advances training of very deep networks; reviewer recommends acceptance.
