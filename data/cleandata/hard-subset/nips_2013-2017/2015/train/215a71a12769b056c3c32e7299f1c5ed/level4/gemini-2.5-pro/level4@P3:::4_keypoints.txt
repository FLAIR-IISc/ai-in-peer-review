- Introduces highway networks with adaptive gating (inspired by LSTM) to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.
- Gates can either apply a layer's nonlinearity or carry input forward, creating "information highways" that mitigate vanishing gradients.
- Demonstrates direct training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard SGD, unlike plain architectures.
- Situates the method relative to initialization schemes, optimizers, skip connections, and FitNets, arguing for an architectural solution.
- Empirical results on MNIST and CIFAR show highway networks overcome depth-related optimization issues and achieve competitive performance.
- High technical quality: well-motivated formulation and strong experiments; MNIST optimization study compellingly supports the core hypothesis.
- Comparison with FitNets indicates simpler direct training can match or outperform complex multi-stage training procedures.
- Analysis (including lesion study) visualizes layer importance and provides insight into how the network uses depth.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and reproducible, with code and hyperparameters provided.
- Original contribution: dynamic, data-dependent gating for feedforward networks adapts LSTM ideas in a novel way.
- Significant impact: enables exploration of extremely deep architectures and shows networks can learn to use or ignore layers based on task complexity.
- Suggestion: perform a more detailed study of the effect of initial transform gate bias on learned gates and performance.
- Suggestion: visualize feature maps H(x, W_H) at various depths to show what transformations active layers learn.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, potential impact, strong empirical support, clarity, and insightful analysis.
- Argument against acceptance: does not establish new state-of-the-art on CIFAR benchmarks (minor concern).
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommended for acceptance.
