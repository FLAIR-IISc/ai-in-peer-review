This paper proposes a generalization for training neural networks with many stacked layers by introducing “information highways”—direct connections to higher layers. The idea is well supported by experiments, which show that it enables the effective training of very deep models and yields improved classification accuracy.

Quality: The idea appears novel, and the experimental evaluation is solid. The paper convincingly demonstrates that information highways improve performance on classification tasks. That said, the analysis sections would benefit from more variety and detail, or additional illustrations, to further illuminate why and when the approach works best.

Clarity: The main messages are clearly communicated, and the presentation is generally easy to follow.

Originality: While the method builds on and generalizes prior techniques such as cross-layer connections, it is sufficiently original in formulation and application. The proposed framework offers a coherent way to construct and train much deeper architectures than conventional approaches.

Significance: The contribution is significant for the field. As applications increasingly rely on deeper networks to achieve better results, methods that enable stable and effective training at depth are of high value.

Overall: The paper presents an effective approach to training very deep neural networks via information highways—i.e., direct connections to upper layers. Although it generalizes earlier ideas on cross-layer connectivity, the contributions are novel and well supported by empirical evidence. With a more varied and in-depth analysis section, the work would be even stronger, but as it stands, it provides a meaningful and practical advance.
