1) Gradient calculation for sigmoid gates: Equation 5 is incorrect under standard sigmoid outputs; authors must clarify how backprop handles non-binary gate outputs (e.g., approximations or straight-through estimators).  
2) Bias initialization justification: The use of constant negative biases tied to depth lacks empirical support and needs a sensitivity analysis across bias values to validate robustness.  
3) Comparison with prior work: Claims of improvement are vague; the authors must detail experimental protocol differences (splits, hyperparameters, metrics) to justify comparisons.  
4) Accuracy reporting: Reporting only max accuracies is inappropriate—provide mean ± standard deviation across runs (including baselines) for proper statistical assessment.  
5) Theoretical perspective: The paper needs theoretical analysis explaining why gate-based selective bypasses prevent vanishing gradients, affect generalization, and relate formally to residual/skip connections.  
6) Overall assessment: Novel and promising idea but technical and presentation gaps (gradient handling, bias choice, comparisons, statistics) require major revision.
