- Introduces highway networks, a feedforward architecture with gating to enable direct training of very deep networks (100+ layers) using standard SGD.
- Adapts LSTM-inspired gating to create "information highways" that mitigate vanishing gradients by allowing information to pass unchanged across layers.
- Clearly positions work relative to prior methods (skip connections, FitNets) and distinguishes single-stage highway training from two-stage distillation or complex initialization.
- Demonstrates strong technical quality with rigorous empirical validation on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 showing deep highway networks outperform plain nets (e.g., >100x loss improvement on MNIST).
- Provides thorough ablation studies and layer lesioning (Figure 4) showing dynamic, task-dependent use of depth rather than redundant layers.
- Supplies theoretical justification via gate behavior (Equations 4–5) and empirical analysis of gate activity patterns (Figures 2–3) illustrating input-dependent routing.
- Writing and presentation are clear, notation is precise, experiments are reproducible with public code and well-documented hyperparameters.
- Minor clarity/typo fixes suggested: clarify the "100-layer" claim wording and correct "Tables 3 and 3" to "Table 3."
- Original contribution is strong: applies gating principles to feedforward nets enabling single-stage training of ultra-deep models distinct from LSTMs and FitNets.
- High significance: addresses a core scaling limitation, is simple to implement (few extra parameters), and compatible with common frameworks for broad applicability.
- Compelling arguments for acceptance: novel solution to depth problem, rigorous empirical and analytical support, clear and reproducible presentation.
