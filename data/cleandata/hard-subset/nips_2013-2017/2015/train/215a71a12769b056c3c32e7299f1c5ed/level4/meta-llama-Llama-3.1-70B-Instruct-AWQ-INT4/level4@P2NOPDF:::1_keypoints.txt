- Strength: Intriguing idea with well-described, mostly convincing experiments and useful analysis of learned networks via gate-output patterns.
- Issue: Gate unit T uses sigmoid so outputs are never exactly 0/1, making gradients in Eq.5 inaccurate; authors must explain how backpropagation is actually performed.
- Issue: Initialization impact is complex; using constant negative biases by depth is insufficient—need more thorough analysis (e.g., plot performance vs initial bias).
- Insight: Initial bias functions as a regularizer—high negative bias favors “longer highways,” an important comparison to highlight.
- Issue: The phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings” is unclear—authors should use identical settings or explicitly state differences for fair comparison.
- Issue: Reporting “max” accuracies is statistically unsound; provide mean accuracy, standard deviation, and other relevant metrics instead.
- Suggestion: Investigate theoretical/learning-theory implications of input-dependent gate units that allow layers to act as identity and enable propagation through many selective layers.
