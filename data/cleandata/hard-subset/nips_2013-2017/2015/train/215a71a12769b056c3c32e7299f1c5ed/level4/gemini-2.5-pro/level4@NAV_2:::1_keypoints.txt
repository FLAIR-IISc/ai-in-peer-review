- Proposes "highway networks": feedforward layers with LSTM-style transform gates that blend transformed output and input to create adaptive information highways.  
- Core contribution: enables direct training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard SGD by improving gradient flow.  
- Distinct from prior skip-connections: gating is dynamic and input-dependent rather than fixed.  
- Empirical evidence: optimization experiments show highway nets avoid degradation seen in plain deep nets.  
- Competitive performance on benchmarks (CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100), showing practical generalization.  
- Strong analysis of internal dynamics: transform gate activities, class-dependent routing, and layer lesioning reveal meaningful, data-dependent computation.  
- Strength/novelty: adapting LSTM gating to feedforward nets is elegant and allows exploring extreme depth.  
- Weakness: Equation 5 uses a simplified Jacobian assuming gates are exactly 0/1, which is misleading since sigmoids never reach those values.  
- Weakness: transform gate bias initialization is heuristic (e.g., -1, -2, -3) and lacks a sensitivity analysis showing performance vs initial bias.  
- Weakness: unconventional reporting "Best (mean ± std.dev.)" emphasizes single best run; should report mean and standard deviation across runs.  
- Weakness: comparisons to prior methods (e.g., FitNets) need clarity on matched experimental settings (parameter counts, compute, augmentation) for fair evaluation.
