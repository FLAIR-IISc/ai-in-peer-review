- Introduces "highway networks": feedforward layers with adaptive transform and carry gates (LSTM-inspired) that allow information to pass unmodified and mitigate vanishing gradients.
- Enables training of extremely deep networks (50–100+ layers) directly with standard SGD, demonstrated empirically.
- Paper addresses a central challenge in deep learning (credit assignment/gradient flow) with an elegant, effective solution.
- Original and significant idea: adapting LSTM-style gating to feedforward nets opens exploration of much greater depth.
- Strong technical quality and thorough empirical evaluation; direct comparisons show highway nets train where plain nets fail.
- Competitive performance on standard benchmarks (CIFAR-10/100) indicating good generalization of very deep models.
- Insightful analysis: gate activation visualizations and layer lesioning reveal dynamic routing and learned "effective depth."
- Clear, well-written, and well-organized presentation.
- Request clarification on gate activity claims for CIFAR-100 vs. figure (suggest adding color bar or activity threshold).
- Suggest discussing/contrasting with a plain-network identity-weight initialization baseline to show advantage of adaptive, data-dependent gating.
- Overall assessment: excellent, original, significant work; strongly recommended for acceptance.
