This paper introduces a novel architecture for extremely deep networks, which employs input-dependent gates that allow information to be passed through a layer without transformation. This mechanism facilitates the training of substantially deeper networks than is feasible with standard feed-forward architectures.

The authors provide an insightful analysis of the trained models, investigating how the gates operate at different depths to select their inputs, which is precisely the kind of evaluation one would hope to see for this method.

The submission is of high quality, presenting an original contribution in a clear and well-written manner. While there are some conceptual similarities to other recent methods (most notably FitNets and LSTMs), the paper clearly explains the key distinctions and offers direct comparisons where relevant. The method achieves promising results.

I have no suggestions for improvement.

Minor typo: on page 5, the text refers to "Tables 3 and 3".

This is a solid contribution. The technique presented is novel, achieves strong results, and is supported by an interesting and thorough analysis.
