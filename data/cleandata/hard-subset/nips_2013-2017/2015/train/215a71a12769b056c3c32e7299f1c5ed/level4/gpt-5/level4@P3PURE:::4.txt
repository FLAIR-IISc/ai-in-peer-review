Summary:
The paper proposes a generalization for training neural networks with many stacked layers by introducing “information highways,” i.e., direct connections to upper layers. Although this approach generalizes prior techniques such as cross-layer connections, the authors demonstrate through experiments that it is an effective method for training very deep networks and that it improves classification accuracy.

Quality:
- The core idea appears novel, and the experimental evaluation is solid.
- The analysis sections would benefit from a broader variety of details or additional illustrations to deepen understanding.

Clarity:
- The main messages are well delivered and generally clear.

Originality:
- The method is sufficiently original, even though it builds on and generalizes previous neural network techniques.

Significance:
- The work is significant for the field, as it addresses the practical need to train increasingly deep neural networks to achieve better performance in applications.

Overall assessment:
- The paper presents an effective method for training very deep networks using information highways (direct connections to upper layers). While it is a generalization of prior cross-layer connection techniques, the contributions are novel and well supported by experimental evidence, and the observed gains in classification accuracy substantiate the approach. The paper would be even stronger with more diverse analysis and visualizations to further illuminate the method’s behavior.
