This paper proposes the use of LSTM units as a method for training very deep networks.

This is a high-quality, well-written paper that presents an original idea and yields quite interesting results. Machine learning researchers often find that adding more layers to their neural networks improves performance, but the challenges of initialization and vanishing or exploding gradients have long been a significant hindrance. In fact, the difficulties of propagating information through deep neural networks have arguably limited their widespread adoption for over 30 years. This paper tackles this problem head-on and demonstrates a viable method for training 100-layer networks.

In particular, the experiments illustrating the routing of information and layer importance are noteworthy. 

Specific comments:

In Section 4.1, it appears that 'most transform blocks are active on average' should be revised to 'most transform blocks are not active on average.'

One potential approach to initializing each layer of a very deep network is to use the identity matrix, possibly with a small amount of noise added. It would be beneficial to comment on how the transform gates differentiate highway networks from this simple initialization scheme.

Overall, this is a great paper that demonstrates a method for training very deep neural networks, and it was a pleasure to read.
