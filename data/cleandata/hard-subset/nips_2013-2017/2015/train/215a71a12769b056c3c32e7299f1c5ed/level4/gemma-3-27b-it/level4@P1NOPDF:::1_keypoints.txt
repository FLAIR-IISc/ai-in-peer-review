- Overall: paper is well described and experiments are somewhat convincing; most valuable is the analysis of learned networks via gate output patterns.

- (1) Gate unit T uses sigmoid so outputs aren’t exactly 0/1; gradients in Eq.5 look incorrect—authors must explain exactly how backpropagation is implemented.

- (2) Initialization choice (constant negative biases dependent on depth) seems ad hoc; provide a plot of performance vs initial bias since bias acts like regularization favoring “longer highways.”

- (3) The phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings” is vague—clarify whether comparisons use the same experimental setup or explicitly state differences.

- (4) Reporting only max accuracies is statistically questionable—report mean accuracy and standard deviation for each method consistently.

- (5) Suggest studying the method from a learning-theory perspective: input-dependent gates enable layers to act as identity mappings and propagate signals through many selective layers.
