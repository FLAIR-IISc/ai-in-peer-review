1. Reviewer had independently considered an LSTM-inspired gradient-trapping technique to deepen networks and congratulates the authors for achieving this first.

2. Offers an alternative view: very deep networks act like not-so-deep networks whose layers contain complex sub-layers, analogous to Inception modules and VGG stacked convolutions, possibly applicable to highway networks.

3. Suggests an experiment: train a single thin-and-tall network on both MNIST and CIFAR-10 (splitting early) to see if parameter utilization at inference matches separately trained models and whether CIFAR reuses MNIST-underutilized parameters.

4. This experiment would reveal how parameter sharing functions in deep hierarchical structures and whether it yields optimal efficiency.

5. No major concerns with the paper.

6. Recommends evaluating the model on larger datasets such as ImageNet.

7. Summarizes the paper as a simple, elegant LSTM-inspired method that enables training very deep feedforward networks with solid analysis.
