

This paper proposes a novel architecture for very deep networks that enables input to be "carried" unchanged through a layer using gates dependent on the input itself. This approach facilitates training significantly deeper networks than is feasible with standard feedforward architectures. The authors present an interesting and thorough analysis of the trained networks to determine how the gates select inputs across different layers, exactly the kind of analysis I would have wanted to see. The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and original. While there is natural similarity to recent work (notably FitNets and LSTMs), the differences are clearly explained and appropriate comparisons are provided. Promising results are reported. No specific improvements are suggested. Note: "Tables 3 and 3" (p. 5) contains a typo (sic). This is a very solid paper, with a novel technique that achieves strong results and a compelling analysis.
