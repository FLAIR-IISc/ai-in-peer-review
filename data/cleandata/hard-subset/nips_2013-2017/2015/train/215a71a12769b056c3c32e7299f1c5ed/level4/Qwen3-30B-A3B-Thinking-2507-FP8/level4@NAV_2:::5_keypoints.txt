- Introduces highway networks with adaptive transform and carry gates to enable direct training of extremely deep (100+ layer) feedforward networks via simple gradient descent.  
- Builds on LSTM, Fitnets, and depth-limitation studies; addresses vanishing gradients by allowing unimpeded information flow across layers.  
- Shows superior optimization and generalization vs. plain networks on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, with >2 orders of magnitude improvement at 100 layers.  
- Analysis of gate biases and layer importance (Figures 2 and 4) supports the gating mechanism’s efficacy.  
- Adaptive gating is a novel architectural contribution, distinct from fixed skip connections and Fitnets’ two-stage training.  
- Enables direct study of depth–accuracy relationships and achieves competitive CIFAR-10 performance with fewer parameters than state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-structured and clear, with concise notation and thorough experimental details; highway formulation generalizes plain networks elegantly.  
- Evaluation is limited to small datasets (MNIST/CIFAR); lacks ImageNet experiments to validate scalability to large, complex tasks.  
- Does not explore sensitivity of negative bias initialization across architectures and datasets.  
- Omits comparison to Residual Networks (ResNets); needs clarification of differences (dynamic gating vs. fixed skip connections).  
- Pro-accept: solves a fundamental deep-learning training problem, provides robust/reproducible experiments, and offers new insights into layer utilization and routing.  
- Con-accept: limited dataset scope and insufficient comparison to the dominant ResNet approach.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions (add ImageNet experiments and ResNet comparison).
