This paper introduces a novel approach to training very deep neural networks, inspired by the Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) architecture. The authors propose a new type of network, called a "highway network," which allows for unimpeded information flow across many layers. This is achieved through an adaptive gating mechanism that regulates the information flow, enabling the training of extremely deep networks using simple gradient descent.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach, including a comparison to previous work on training deep networks. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on several benchmark datasets, including MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100. The results show that highway networks can be trained to depths of over 100 layers, outperforming traditional networks of similar depth.

One of the strengths of this paper is its clarity and organization. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach, making it easy to follow and understand. The paper is well-structured, with a logical flow of ideas and a clear separation of theory, experiments, and analysis.

The experiments are well-designed and thorough, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the highway network approach. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on a range of tasks, including image classification and digit recognition. The results are impressive, showing that highway networks can achieve state-of-the-art performance on several benchmark datasets.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it does not provide a direct comparison to other state-of-the-art methods for training deep networks. While the authors do provide a comparison to traditional networks, it would be useful to see a comparison to other methods, such as those using residual connections or batch normalization.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The authors' approach is novel and effective, and their analysis is thorough and well-presented. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.
