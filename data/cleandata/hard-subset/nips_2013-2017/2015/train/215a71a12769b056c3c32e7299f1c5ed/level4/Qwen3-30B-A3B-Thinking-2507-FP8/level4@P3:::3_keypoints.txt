- Introduces Highway Networks: LSTM-inspired adaptive gating to train extremely deep (100+ layer) feedforward networks with standard SGD.  
- Uses multiplicative transform/carry gates to create "information highways" that allow unimpeded signal propagation across layers.  
- Dynamically routes information via learned gates, avoiding two-stage training or fixed shortcut paths used in prior skip-connection or pretraining work.  
- Empirically maintains training stability and generalization as depth increases, outperforming plain networks by >100× at 100 layers on MNIST.  
- Matches or exceeds state-of-the-art results on CIFAR-10/100 with comparable parameter budgets.  
- Strong technical validation: thorough ablation studies, statistical significance reporting, and relevant baseline comparisons.  
- Theoretical support via Jacobian analysis and empirical demonstrations of information routing strengthen the claims.  
- Effective initialization scheme (negative bias for transform gates) is well-motivated and robust across architectures.  
- Original contribution: learned, adaptive gating distinguishes it from fixed skip connections and teacher-student distillation approaches.  
- Significant impact: addresses vanishing gradients, enables direct study of depth effects and layer importance previously infeasible.  
- Clear presentation: well-structured paper, clear notation, intuitive figures, transparent experimental setup, and shared code for reproducibility.  
- Weakness — minor wording ambiguity: "most transform blocks are active on average" should clarify high average activity vs. sparse per-sample activation.  
- Weakness — comparative context: paper could better contrast Highway Networks with ResNets and explicitly discuss differences with fixed skip connections.  
- Weakness — depth threshold: lacks quantitative definition of "very deep" and explicit comparison to prior depth limits of plain networks.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, impactful work with minor, easily addressable issues; reviewer recommends strong acceptance.
