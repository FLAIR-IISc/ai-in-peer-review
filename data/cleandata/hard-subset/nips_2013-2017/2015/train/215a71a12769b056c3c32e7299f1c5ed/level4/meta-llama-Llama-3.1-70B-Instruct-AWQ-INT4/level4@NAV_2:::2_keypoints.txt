- Proposes a novel "highway networks" architecture enabling training of very deep feed-forward networks.  
- Addresses vanishing gradients and poor activation propagation by allowing unimpeded input flow across layers.  
- Uses adaptive gating units inspired by LSTM to regulate information flow through layers.  
- Provides thorough analysis of trained networks, showing how gates select inputs at different layers and offering insights into inner workings.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly distinguishes the approach from related work (e.g., FitNets, LSTMs).  
- Experimental results are promising, demonstrating the effectiveness of highway networks for very deep architectures.  
- Strengths: originality, clarity, and thorough analytical treatment.  
- Weaknesses: none significant reported.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, insightful analysis, strong experimental support, clear exposition.  
- Overall: solid paper making a valuable contribution, recommended for acceptance.
