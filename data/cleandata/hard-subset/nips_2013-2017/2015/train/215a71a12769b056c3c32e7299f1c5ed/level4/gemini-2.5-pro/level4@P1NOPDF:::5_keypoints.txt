- Introduces an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism that enables effective training of extremely deep feed-forward networks.  
- Core concept is well-motivated and supported by solid analysis.  
- Gating mechanisms effectively manage information and gradient flow in very deep architectures.  
- Reviewer praises the novelty and successful formulation/implementation of the approach.  
- Offers an alternative perspective: view deep networks as shallower compositions of more complex modules rather than monolithic stacks.  
- Notes similarity of this perspective to Inception modules and stacked convolutions in VGG.  
- Requests authors' thoughts on interpreting Highway Networks as composed of more complex module-like layers.  
- Suggests an experiment: train a deep, narrow multi-task network on MNIST and CIFAR-10 with task-specific output forks.  
- Proposes analyzing transform-gate behavior at inference to see if gates bypassed for MNIST activate for CIFAR, illuminating parameter sharing and transfer.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with no major weaknesses.  
- Recommends evaluating scalability on larger benchmarks such as ImageNet.
