- Introduces highway networks: y = H(x) ⊙ T(x) + x ⊙ (1 − T(x)) with negative-bias-initialized gates to create “information highways” enabling very deep networks to train with SGD.
- Positions work relative to LSTM gating, initialization/optimizer advances, skip connections, and FitNets; gates are learnable and input-dependent vs fixed skips.
- Formulation is simple and well motivated; C = 1 − T keeps parameterization compact and stable, with clear initialization rationale.
- Controlled MNIST experiments show plain nets degrade with depth while highway nets train effectively up to 100 layers.
- Convolutional experiments on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 achieve accuracy comparable to or better than FitNets under similar parameter/computation budgets.
- Analyses of gate activity, data-dependent routing, and lesion studies substantiate intended gate behavior and routing dynamics.
- Limitation: evaluations confined to small/medium-scale vision benchmarks; no ImageNet-scale experiments.
- Limitation: ablations isolating gating effects vs. added parameters are limited for convolutional settings.
- Suggestion: additional diagnostics (e.g., gradient norms through depth) would better demonstrate optimization vs capacity benefits.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized; equations and visualizations effectively convey the “information highway” concept.
- Minor clarity issues: typos (“varying varying depths,” “backpropogation”), inconsistent reference formatting, and Section 4.1 wording that can confuse readers.
- Originality: novel application of adaptive, input-dependent transform/carry gates to very deep feedforward networks, with novel initialization and routing analyses.
- Significance: enables stable training of 50–100+ layer networks with vanilla SGD, simple to implement, and offers useful insights on depth utilization and routing.
- Specific comment: clarify Section 4.1 by showing per-unit average activity vs per-sample sparsity (e.g., histogram) to avoid confusion.
- Specific comment: compare highway nets to identity-initialized plain layers to disentangle effects of explicit carry paths vs initialization.
- Specific comment: report parameters and FLOPs for H and T separately for convolutional models and consider shared/reduced T ablations.
- Specific comment: provide sensitivity plots for gate bias bT across depths and explore independent carry gates (C ≠ 1 − T).
- Specific comment: include gradient-norm diagnostics across layers during training for plain vs highway nets to support credit-assignment claims.
- Specific comment: briefly discuss potential extensions to sequence models (RNNs/CNNs for sequences) to broaden impact.
- Pros: simple, well-motivated architecture; strong controlled optimization results; insightful gate analyses; code availability aids reproducibility.
- Cons: limited large-scale evaluation; limited ablations on gate cost/benefit and parameterizations; minor clarity and formatting issues.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, original, and significant contribution; recommend acceptance after minor clarifications and suggested ablations.
