- Introduces highway networks with adaptive LSTM-style gates to ease training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Gates let each layer balance transforming input vs carrying it forward, creating “information highways” for gradients.  
- Directly addresses optimization difficulties encountered by plain deep networks.  
- Empirical evidence: highway networks with up to 100 layers can be trained end-to-end with SGD.  
- Outperforms plain networks on MNIST in optimization performance.  
- Achieves competitive results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, beating FitNet without multi-stage training.  
- Originality/significance: elegant adaptation of gating to feedforward nets enabling exploration of extreme depth.  
- Technical quality: sound methodology with rigorous experiments and a convincing controlled optimization study.  
- Analysis: lesion study provides insightful evidence of how depth is utilized by the network.  
- Clarity: well-written, intuitive presentation, simple math, and thorough experimental reporting.  
- Suggestion: reframe as moderately deep networks with complex per-layer blocks to relate to Inception/VGG-style architectures.  
- Suggestion: validate on a large-scale dataset like ImageNet to strengthen practical impact.  
- Future idea: multi-task experiment (single thin-and-deep highway net for MNIST+CIFAR) to study layer reuse and resource allocation.
