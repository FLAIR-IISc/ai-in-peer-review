- Proposes highway networks: an LSTM-inspired architecture using adaptive gating units to regulate inter-layer information flow.
- Idea is original, well-presented, and clearly motivated.
- Provides thorough overview of deep network training challenges (initialization, vanishing/exploding gradients).
- Demonstrates highway networks can overcome these challenges and enable training of very deep, efficient architectures.
- Experimental results are strong, well-presented, and show informative routing and layer-importance behavior.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and reproducible for expert readers.
- Technically sound: claims supported by theory and experiments; authors are careful in evaluation.
- Originality: adaptive gating across layers is a novel contribution.
- Significance: results are important and could impact deep learning by enabling much deeper models.
- Specific correction: change “most transform blocks are active on average” to “most transform blocks are not active on average” in Section 4.1.
- Potential criticism: highway networks may be similar to simple identity-initialized layers; authors should clarify how transform gates differ.
- Could benefit from more detailed comparisons with other deep-training methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, strong experiments, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: possible lack of distinction from identity initialization schemes and limited comparative evaluation.
