- Proposes highway networks with adaptive gating (inspired by LSTM) to enable training of very deep feedforward nets.  
- Main contribution: shows extremely deep highway networks can be trained directly with SGD and generalize well.  
- Paper is well-written with clear architecture description, theoretical discussion, experiments, and comparisons.  
- Experiments demonstrate effective training of networks with hundreds of layers.  
- Backpropagation through the gate T is unclear; gradients in Eq. 5 are incorrect because sigmoid outputs are never exactly 0 or 1.  
- Initialization effects are under-analyzed; authors should show performance vs. bias initialization (e.g., plot).  
- Experimental comparisons lack clarity about setups; “similar experimental settings” needs specification.  
- Results report maxima only; should provide statistical measures (mean accuracy, standard deviation).  
- Recommend deeper learning-theory analysis of gate behavior and theoretical foundations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, strong empirical evidence, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear backprop derivation, insufficient initialization study, ambiguous experimental comparisons, limited theoretical analysis.  
- Overall: promising and convincing work but requires clarifications and additional analyses to strengthen claims.
