- Introduces highway networks using LSTM-inspired gating y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)) with negative transform-gate bias initialization to favor carry behavior early in training.  
- Positions method relative to prior work (skip connections, initialization, FitNets) but emphasizes adaptive, multiplicative, input-dependent gating as the key difference.  
- Optimization: fully connected highway networks of 50–100 layers train reliably on MNIST, while similarly parameterized plain nets fail as depth increases.  
- Generalization: convolutional highway networks achieve competitive results on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 and match or exceed FitNets under comparable budgets with single-stage training.  
- Interpretability: gate-activity analyses and per-layer lesion studies show data-dependent routing and reveal which layers matter for task complexity.  
- Quality: technically sound, carefully executed experiments, and convincing optimization comparisons with matched baselines.  
- Clarity: manuscript is clear and well organized; figures on gateways and lesioning are informative; minor wording in Section 4.1 needs fixing.  
- Originality: novel application of LSTM-style gating to deep feedforward layers, enabling adaptive skip/carry paths.  
- Significance: provides a practical method for training very deep architectures and tools to analyze depth utilization, likely to influence future design.  
- Strengths: simple principled architecture, strong empirical optimization evidence, competitive generalization, insightful analyses, and public code/hyperparameters.  
- Weaknesses/questions: limited direct comparisons to other skip/shortcut schemes and relatively light theoretical analysis (mostly empirical support).  
- Specific comment: Section 4.1 sentence “most transform blocks are active on average” appears inconsistent and likely should read “not active on average.”  
- Specific comment: Recommend explicitly contrasting transform/carry gating with identity-initialized layers or fixed skips to clarify when dynamic gating is essential.  
- Overall evaluation: high-quality, original, impactful paper; recommendation to accept.
