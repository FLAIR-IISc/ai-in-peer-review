The paper proposes a generalization of training neural networks with many stacked layers, introducing the concept of "information highways" to facilitate the training process. This idea is effectively illustrated through experimentation, demonstrating its potential in improving classification accuracy.

The quality of the paper is strong, with a novel idea supported by solid experimentation. However, the analysis sections could benefit from more varied details or illustrations to further reinforce the findings. The authors present a thorough evaluation of their method, but additional insights or visualizations would enhance the reader's understanding.

The clarity of the paper is commendable, as the main messages are well-delivered and easy to comprehend. The authors successfully convey the key contributions and implications of their work, making the paper accessible to a broad audience.

The originality of the proposed method is sufficient, building upon previous neural network techniques while introducing a distinct innovation. The concept of information highways offers a fresh perspective on training deep neural networks, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how their approach differs from existing methods.

The significance of the paper is evident, as it addresses a pressing issue in the field: training deeper neural networks to achieve better results in various application areas. The authors demonstrate the potential of their method in improving classification accuracy, which is a critical aspect of many machine learning tasks.

In summary, the paper presents an effective method for training very deep neural networks using information highways, which involves building direct connections to upper network layers. Although this approach is a generalization of prior techniques, such as cross-layer connections, the authors provide convincing experimental evidence to support its novelty and effectiveness. The contributions of the paper are well-supported by empirical results, and the authors' ideas have the potential to make a meaningful impact in the field.
