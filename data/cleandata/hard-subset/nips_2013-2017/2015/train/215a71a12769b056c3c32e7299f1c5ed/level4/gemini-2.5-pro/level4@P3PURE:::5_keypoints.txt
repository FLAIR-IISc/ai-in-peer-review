- Paper presents a simple, elegant LSTM-inspired gating idea that enables training very deep feed-forward networks.  
- Method is well-motivated and the analysis is solid.  
- Core idea: use a gating mechanism (like LSTM) to manage gradient flow in very deep networks.  
- Reviewer congratulates authors for first successful implementation and analysis of this approach.  
- Alternative perspective: view very deep nets as shallower stacks of more complex composite layers (e.g., Inception, stacked convolutions).  
- Asks whether highway networks can be interpreted as such composite-layer architectures.  
- Suggests an experiment: train a deep narrow network on MNIST and CIFAR jointly to study layer-wise information flow, parameter reuse, and feature sharing.  
- Overall no major complaints; recommends validating the approach on larger datasets such as ImageNet.
