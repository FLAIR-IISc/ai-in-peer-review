- Introduces Highway Networks: input-dependent gating blends H(x) with input via T(x) to enable unattenuated signal/gradient paths, inspired by LSTM gating.  
- Positions work among prior deep-training strategies (initialization, optimizers, skip connections, deep supervision, FitNets) and contrasts learned routing with fixed shortcuts.  
- Empirical claim: enables successful training of very deep fully connected (up to 100 layers) and deep convolutional nets.  
- Results: competitive or better performance on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 under comparable parameter/computation budgets.  
- Analysis: examines gate behavior, depth utilization, data-dependent routing, and lesion studies quantifying layer contributions.  
- Strength: clear, LSTM-inspired architectural idea well adapted to feedforward depth.  
- Strength: stabilizes optimization and yields much faster convergence using standard ReLUs and SGD.  
- Strength: careful comparisons to FitNets and reasonable MNIST/CIFAR experimental setup.  
- Strength: insightful analyses (gate biases, sparsity, class-conditional routing) and lesion experiments.  
- Strength: provides practical training guidance (gate bias initialization) and released code/resources.  
- Weakness: lacks direct ablation against fixed identity skip connections of comparable depth/width to isolate gating benefit.  
- Weakness: no quantification of parameter, FLOP, runtime, or memory overhead introduced by gates.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to gate bias initialization is discussed but not systematically evaluated across datasets/depths.  
- Weakness: CIFAR experiments use modest augmentation; stronger baselines with richer augmentation could affect competitiveness.  
- Weakness: some experiments are pilot/sanity checks and hyperparameters are sometimes reused; fuller tuning needed to strengthen SOTA claims.  
- Quality: technically sound formulation, adequate training details, and convincing evidence that depth ceases to hinder optimization.  
- Clarity: well written and organized with clear notation and figures; minor reference/numbering issues (e.g., “Tables 3 and 3”).  
- Originality: novel application of input-dependent gating to enable ultra-deep feedforward training, distinct from FitNets and fixed skips.  
- Significance: important contribution enabling very deep nets with standard tools and likely to influence architecture and conditional computation work.  
- Suggestion: provide parameter/FLOP breakdown and wall-clock training time/memory usage versus baselines.  
- Suggestion: include ablation comparing highway layers to identity skip connections with same depth/width.  
- Suggestion: report sensitivity to gate bias initialization across depths/datasets.  
- Suggestion: investigate gate saturation at test time and potential for conditional computation/pruning.  
- Suggestion: clarify handling of dimensionality changes (downsampling) and gate sharing/per-channel behavior in conv stacks.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, well-motivated idea with strong empirical evidence, insightful analyses, and open-source code.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing ablations vs. fixed skips, incomplete computational-cost accounting, and limited hyperparameter tuning in places.  
- Minor issues: fix table numbering and a few reference typographical/formatting inconsistencies.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept.
