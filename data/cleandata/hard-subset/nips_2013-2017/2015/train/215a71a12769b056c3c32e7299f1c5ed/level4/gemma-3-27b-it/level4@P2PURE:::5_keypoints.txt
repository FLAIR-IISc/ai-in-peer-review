- Presents an elegant, simple LSTM-inspired mechanism that enables training very deep feedforward networks, supported by solid analysis.
- Reviewer independently considered a similar approach and congratulates authors for being first to pursue it.
- Proposes an alternative interpretation: very deep networks can be seen as shallower networks whose layers contain internal sub-layers with richer nonlinearities (relates to Inception, VGG, highway nets).
- Suggests an experiment: train a single "thin and tall" network on both MNIST and CIFAR-10 with a fork at the input to study cross-domain knowledge transfer.
- Wants to observe whether the shared network at inference resembles separately trained networks and whether parameters underutilized on MNIST are leveraged for CIFAR-10, to assess sharing efficiency.
- No major criticisms; recommends evaluating the method on larger datasets such as ImageNet.
