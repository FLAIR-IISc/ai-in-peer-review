- Presents a simple, elegant LSTM-inspired gating idea that enables training of very deep feedforward networks.
- Concept is well-motivated and supported by solid accompanying analysis.
- Using LSTM-like gates to manage gradient flow in deep architectures is insightful; authors are first to implement and validate this approach.
- Suggests an alternative framing: view the model as moderately deep networks of richer "meta-layers" or blocks (akin to Inception or stacked convolutions).
- Recommends the authors discuss whether highway networks can be interpreted as such meta-layer/block architectures.
- Proposes a multi-task experiment (shared trunk, separate heads for MNIST and CIFAR-10) to study parameter utilization and feature sharing across tasks.
- Notes no major weaknesses but urges evaluation on larger-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to demonstrate scalability and practical impact.
