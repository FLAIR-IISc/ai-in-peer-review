- Commends authors for pioneering the use of the LSTM trick to train deeper feedforward networks.  
- Proposes alternative view: very deep networks can be seen as shallower networks with complex sub-layers, akin to GoogLeNet/VGG designs.  
- Asks whether this perspective/generalization could apply to highway networks.  
- Suggests experiment: train a single thin tall network shared by MNIST and CIFAR (with a fork at the top) to study parameter sharing and utilization.  
- Wants to see if the shared network reproduces domain-specific inference patterns and whether CIFAR leverages MNIST’s underutilized parameters.  
- Has no major criticisms, recommends testing on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet).  
- Praises the paper: LSTM-inspired idea is straightforward, elegant, and well-analyzed.
