- Introduces highway networks: adaptive gating (LSTM-inspired) enabling direct training of extremely deep (100+ layer) feedforward networks.  
- Claims highway networks overcome vanishing gradients, allowing standard SGD training without specialized initialization or staged procedures.  
- Builds on prior work (LSTM, skip connections, knowledge distillation) but offers a more general solution that avoids teacher networks or layer-wise pretraining.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results: 100-layer highway nets vastly outperform plain nets in optimization error and achieve competitive CIFAR-10 accuracy (92.7% vs 85.5%).  
- Method is general (applies to convolutional, fully connected, recurrent networks) and trained with minimal overhead (one gating layer per highway layer).  
- Technical exposition is clear with solid mathematical formalism, intuitive "information highways" explanation, and publicly released code/hyperparameters.  
- Experimental design is rigorous: ablation studies, gate-dynamics analysis, and controlled comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Analysis shows data-dependent gating that dynamically routes information, with sparse per-sample gate activation despite high average activity.  
- Weakness: insufficient explicit comparison to identity (or near-identity) initialization; needs clearer argument why identity init alone is insufficient.  
- Minor wording issue: "most transform blocks are active on average" should read "most transform gates are active on average."  
- Recommendation: high-quality, technically sound, impactful contribution that merits acceptance.  
- Pro: solves training-depth problem simply, shows empirical superiority, offers novel insights into routing/layer use, and is reproducible.  
- Con: minor clarifications needed on distinction from identity initialization and brief acknowledgement of later related work (residual networks).
