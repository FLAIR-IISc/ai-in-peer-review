- Proposes "highway networks" with adaptive transform and carry gates (LSTM-inspired) to enable training of extremely deep feedforward models.  
- Gates allow parts of the input to be carried unchanged across layers, mitigating vanishing gradients and easing optimization.  
- Empirically shows highway networks up to 100 layers can be trained with SGD and outperform plain networks on MNIST, with competitive results on CIFAR-10/100.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and provides an intuitive connection to LSTMs.  
- Experiments are rigorous and convincingly demonstrate the optimization advantage over plain networks (notably Section 3.1).  
- Analysis (Section 4) — including gate bias/activity visualizations and layer lesioning — gives strong, interpretable insight into model behavior.  
- Novelty: adaptive, data-dependent gating in feedforward networks is a meaningful advance beyond fixed skip connections.  
- Significance: enables training of very deep networks and allows task-dependent utilization of depth, with high potential impact.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to small datasets; lacks large-scale experiments (e.g., ImageNet) for broader validation and comparison to VGG/GoogLeNet.  
- Weakness: would benefit from discussion relating highway networks to block-based architectures (Inception, stacked 3x3 convs in VGG).  
- Suggested future experiment: multi-task training (e.g., MNIST+CIFAR-10) to study gating-mediated feature sharing and specialization.  
- Overall: excellent, influential work with minor weaknesses that can be addressed in future extensions.
