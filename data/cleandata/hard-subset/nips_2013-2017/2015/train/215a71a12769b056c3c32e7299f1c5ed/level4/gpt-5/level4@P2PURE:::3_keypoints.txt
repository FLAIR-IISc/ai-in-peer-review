- Proposes using LSTM-like transform/gate units to train very deep networks; original idea with interesting results.  
- Well-written, high-quality paper that addresses longstanding vanishing/exploding gradient and information-flow problems.  
- Demonstrates practical training of extremely deep (100-layer) networks.  
- Experiments on information routing and layer importance are particularly compelling.  
- Specific correction: Section 4.1 wording is reversed—“most transform blocks are active on average” should read “not active on average.”  
- Suggestion: compare or comment on how transform gates differ from simple identity initialization (with small noise) per layer.  
- Overall positive recommendation: a great, enjoyable-to-read paper demonstrating a practical method for deep networks.
