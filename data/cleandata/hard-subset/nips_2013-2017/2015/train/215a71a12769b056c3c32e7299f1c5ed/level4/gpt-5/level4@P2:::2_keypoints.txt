- Summary & relation to prior work: Proposes highway networks that use input-dependent transform/carry gates (inspired by LSTM) to blend transformed and identity pathways, enabling very deep feedforward/convolutional nets and relating to skip connections, deep supervision, and FitNets.  
- Quality: Methodology and experiments are sound and well-controlled, showing much better optimization for deep nets; some ablations (gate variants, overhead) are missing.  
- Clarity: Clearly written and well-structured with effective figures; minor typo noted (“Tables 3 and 3”).  
- Originality: Novel application of LSTM-style gating to feedforward depth, distinct from fixed skip-connections and distillation approaches.  
- Significance: Important practical contribution that lowers the barrier to training very deep networks with standard SGD, likely to influence follow-up work.  
- Strengths: Simple principled architecture and initialization, robust optimization across depth, competitive MNIST/CIFAR results with modest models, and insightful analyses (gate behavior, routing, lesioning).  
- Weaknesses & suggestions: Needs additional ablations (independent carry gates, gate-bias sensitivity, computation/parameter overhead), broader baseline comparisons, and hyperparameter sensitivity studies.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a central challenge with a simple, general mechanism and provides convincing empirical and analytical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Some missing ablations and comparisons limit understanding of trade-offs; not state-of-the-art with heavy augmentation (not the primary goal).  
- Overall assessment: Strong, original, and technically sound paper with persuasive experiments and analyses; recommended for acceptance.
