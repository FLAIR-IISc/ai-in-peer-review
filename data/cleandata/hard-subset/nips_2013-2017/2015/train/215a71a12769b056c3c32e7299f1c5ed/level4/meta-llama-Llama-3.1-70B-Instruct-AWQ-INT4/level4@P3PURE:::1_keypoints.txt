- Positive: Idea is well described, experiments are reasonably convincing, and the analysis of learned networks via gate-output patterns is a notable strength.
- Concern: Gate unit T uses a sigmoid (never exactly 0 or 1), so the gradients in Equation 5 are incorrect — authors must explain how backpropagation is actually performed.
- Concern: Initialization handling is unsatisfying — using constant negative biases tied to depth needs justification and a plot showing performance vs. initial bias (bias acts like a regularizer encouraging “longer highways”).
- Concern: The phrase "report by recent studies in similar experimental settings" is unclear — authors must clarify whether compared methods share identical experimental settings or differ, and specify those differences.
- Concern: Reporting only "max" accuracies is statistically inappropriate — provide mean accuracy and standard deviation for every method instead.
- Suggestion: It would be valuable to analyze the proposed gating approach from a learning-theory perspective, given gates enable layers to act as input-dependent identities and propagate inputs through many selective layers.
