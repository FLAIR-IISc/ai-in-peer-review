This paper introduces a novel architecture for training very deep neural networks, addressing the vanishing gradient problem that often hinders the successful optimization of such networks. The core idea is to allow the input to be propagated, unchanged, through a layer via gates that are dynamically controlled by the input itself. This mechanism facilitates the training of significantly deeper networks compared to standard feedforward architectures.

The strength of this work lies not only in the proposed architecture but also in the insightful analysis of trained networks. The authors meticulously investigate how the gating mechanisms select inputs at different layers, providing a valuable understanding of the learned representations and the flow of information within the network – precisely the type of analysis one would hope to see for such a novel approach.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented. The motivation is strong, the technical details are articulated with precision, and the experimental setup is thoroughly described. The originality of the work is readily apparent. While acknowledging the related work – specifically mentioning FitNets and LSTMs – the authors effectively delineate the key differences and appropriately contextualize their contribution within the existing literature. This careful positioning demonstrates a strong understanding of the field. 

The experimental results are promising, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed architecture in training deeper networks and achieving strong performance. The results presented provide convincing evidence for the efficacy of the approach. 

I found no significant weaknesses in this submission and have no specific suggestions for improvement. However, there is a minor typographical error on page 5 where “Tables 3 and 3” are referenced; this should be corrected.

Overall, this is a solid and valuable contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed technique is both novel and effective, and the accompanying analysis is insightful and thorough. I recommend acceptance.
