This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate training of very deep neural networks. The core idea—inspired by LSTM recurrent networks—is to incorporate “highway” connections with adaptive gating mechanisms that allow for unimpeded information flow across many layers. The authors demonstrate that these networks can be trained effectively using standard stochastic gradient descent, even with hundreds of layers, and provide experimental validation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 datasets.

The paper addresses a crucial challenge in deep learning: the difficulty of training very deep feedforward networks. Previous work has explored various approaches, including improved optimizers (e.g., [13, 14, 15]), initialization strategies ([16, 17, 18, 19]), specialized activation functions ([20, 21]), and skip connections ([22, 23, 24, 25, 26]). The authors build upon these ideas by drawing inspiration from the gating mechanisms in LSTMs [29, 30], offering a distinct and promising solution. The analysis of the learned gate outputs (Figure 2 and Section 4) is a particularly strong aspect of this work, providing insight into how the network leverages these highway connections.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture presents a novel approach to address the vanishing gradient problem in deep networks, offering a different perspective compared to existing methods.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on multiple datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100) demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks in enabling the training of very deep architectures.  The comparisons to FitNets [25] and other state-of-the-art methods are valuable.
*   **Analysis of Learned Networks:** The analysis of the gate outputs and layer importance (Figures 2 and 4) provides valuable insights into the network’s internal workings, going beyond simply demonstrating performance gains. This investigation into *how* the network learns is a significant contribution.
*   **Clarity of Explanation:** The concept of highway networks and the mathematical formulation are clearly explained and easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Gradient Calculation:** The authors acknowledge that the transform gate `T` uses a sigmoid activation, preventing it from reaching exactly 0 or 1. However, the paper lacks a detailed explanation of how backpropagation is performed in these networks, particularly how gradients are handled when `T` is not precisely 0 or 1. Clarification is needed on the precise gradient calculations.
*   **Initialization Sensitivity:** While the proposed negative bias initialization for the transform gates is effective, the reasoning for selecting specific values (e.g., -1, -2, -3) based on network depth feels somewhat ad-hoc. A more systematic investigation, potentially with a plot of performance versus initial bias value, would strengthen the argument. The regularization effect of the bias should be discussed in more detail.
*   **Comparative Language:** The phrase "report by recent studies in similar experimental settings" (Section 3.3.1) is vague. The authors should either provide a direct comparison with specific studies and clearly outline the similarities and differences in experimental setup, or rephrase the statement.
*   **Statistical Reporting:** Reporting only “max” accuracies in the tables is not standard practice. It’s crucial to provide mean accuracy and standard deviation to assess the statistical significance and reliability of the results.
*   **Theoretical Underpinnings:**  The paper focuses primarily on empirical results.  A discussion of the theoretical implications of adding these gated connections – for example, how it affects the representational capacity or optimization landscape – would be valuable. Investigating the connection to learning theory would strengthen the work.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks, addressing a significant challenge in the field.
*   The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate the benefits of highway networks across multiple datasets.
*   The analysis of the learned network behavior provides valuable insights into the functionality of the proposed architecture.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key concepts.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The lack of detailed explanation regarding gradient calculations and the ad-hoc nature of the initialization strategy could raise concerns about the robustness and generalizability of the approach.
*   The statistical reporting needs to be improved by including mean and standard deviation values.
*   The absence of a theoretical analysis limits the broader impact and understanding of the contribution.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a strong paper with the potential to significantly impact the field of deep learning. The proposed highway network architecture is a valuable contribution, and the experimental results are compelling. However, addressing the weaknesses related to gradient calculation, initialization sensitivity, statistical reporting, and theoretical analysis would greatly strengthen the paper. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions** addressing these points. Specifically, the authors should: (1) provide a detailed explanation of the backpropagation process in highway networks, (2) justify the choice of initial bias values with a more systematic investigation, (3) clarify the comparison to related work, and (4) report mean accuracy and standard deviation for all results.
