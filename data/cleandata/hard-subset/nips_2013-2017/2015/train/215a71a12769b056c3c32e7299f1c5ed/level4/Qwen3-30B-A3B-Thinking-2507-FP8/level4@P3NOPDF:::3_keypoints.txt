- Proposes using LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms to train extremely deep networks, addressing vanishing/exploding gradients and poor information flow.
- Enables stable training of networks exceeding 100 layers, significantly beyond prior practical limits.
- Core innovation: explicit, learned control of inter-layer information flow via transform gates rather than fixed mechanisms.
- Experimental analysis includes visualization of information routing, layer activation patterns, and quantification of "layer importance."
- Robust empirical validation with ablation studies and comparisons to relevant baselines showing consistent performance gains.
- Technical exposition and figures are clear, precise, and effective at conveying complex concepts.
- Paper offers valuable theoretical intuition about how deep networks process information, not merely an incremental improvement.
- Minor suggestion 1: fix typo in Section 4.1—should read "most transform blocks are not active on average."
- Minor suggestion 2: add a brief discussion contrasting transform gates with identity (near-identity) initialization strategies.
- High-impact, generalizable contribution with strong recommendation for acceptance at NIPS.
