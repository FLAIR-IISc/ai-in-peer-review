An alternative perspective: a very deep network can be viewed as a not-so-deep network where each "layer" contains sub-layers. Each such "layer" exhibits more complex non-linear behavior than a standard rectifier or sigmoid layer. This aligns with my understanding of inception modules in GoogLeNet, the stacked 3x3 convolutions in VGG, and I wonder if it similarly applies to highway networks.

I suggest the authors conduct an experiment using a single thin-and-tall network to train on both MNIST and CIFAR-10 (splitting the network early for the two domains). Specifically, I am interested in whether, at inference time, the network exhibits the same parameter utilization patterns as networks trained separately for each domain—particularly whether model parameters underutilized in MNIST are effectively leveraged by CIFAR-10. This would reveal how parameter sharing operates within a deep hierarchical structure and whether it achieves optimal efficiency.
