This paper proposes using LSTM units as a method for training very deep networks. It is a high-quality, well-written paper; the idea is original, and the results are quite interesting. Machine learning researchers frequently observe that adding more layers improves performance, but difficulties with initialization and decaying/exploding gradients have been a severe limitation. Indeed, the challenge of maintaining information flow in deep neural networks arguably prevented their widespread use for about 30 years. This paper addresses the issue head-on and demonstrates a method for training 100-layer networks. The experiments on information routing and layer importance are particularly compelling.

Specific comments:
- In Section 4.1, “most transform blocks are active on average” seems reversed and should read “not active on average.”
- One could initialize each layer of a very deep network with the identity matrix (possibly with small noise). It would be helpful to comment on how the transform gates distinguish highway networks from this simple scheme.

Overall, this is a great paper that demonstrates a practical method for training very deep neural networks, and it was a pleasure to read.
