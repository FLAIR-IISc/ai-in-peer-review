- Proposes highway networks, an LSTM-inspired architecture using adaptive gates to enable unimpeded information flow through very deep feedforward networks.
- Idea is simple, elegant, and the analysis of the architecture is solid.
- Provides detailed description of components and how they create "information highways."
- Demonstrates experimentally that extremely deep networks (hundreds of layers) can be trained effectively.
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and explains complex concepts concisely.
- Makes a significant and novel contribution by adapting LSTM ideas to feedforward deep networks.
- Experimental results support the effectiveness of the approach.
- Does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art deep training methods.
- Lacks thorough analysis of the limitations and potential drawbacks of highway networks.
- Recommendation: accept, but request more comprehensive comparisons and deeper analysis of limitations.
- Overall rating: 8/10.
