This paper proposes an architecture for very deep networks that enables input to be "carried" unchanged by a layer using input-dependent gates.

It is capable of training much deeper networks than a standard feed forward architecture.

The authors provide a very interesting analysis of trained networks to determine how the gates at different layers of the network select their inputs, which I wanted to see.

The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and original.

Although there are some similarities with other recent works (especially FitNet and LSTM), the differences are clearly explained and compared where appropriate.

Promising results have been achieved.

I have no proposed improvements.

Note "Tables 3 and 3" (page 5).

This is a very powerful paper. The proposed technique is new and achieves good results. The analysis is interesting and thorough.
