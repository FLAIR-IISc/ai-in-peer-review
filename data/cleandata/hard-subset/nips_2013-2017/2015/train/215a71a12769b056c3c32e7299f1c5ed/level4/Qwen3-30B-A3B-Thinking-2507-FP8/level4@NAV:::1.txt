

The paper presents a well-articulated idea with convincing experimental results. I particularly appreciate the authors' effort to analyze learned networks through the lens of gate output patterns.

(1) The gate unit T employs a standard sigmoid activation, which inherently produces values strictly between 0 and 1 (as acknowledged by the authors). Consequently, the gradients in Equation 5 are mathematically incorrect. The authors must clarify the exact backpropagation mechanism implemented for these networks.  
(2) While initialization effects are inherently complex, the proposed constant negative bias strategy—dependent on the number of levels—is insufficiently justified. A performance plot varying the initial bias value would strengthen this claim. The initial bias functions as a regularizer, where more negative values encourage networks with "longer highways," a comparison I appreciate.  
(3) The phrase "report by recent studies in similar experimental settings" is ambiguous. Either the experimental setups are directly comparable for meaningful benchmarking, or they are not; the authors must explicitly clarify the differences in experimental configurations.  
(4) Reporting only "max" accuracies in tables is statistically inappropriate and non-standard. The authors should consistently provide mean accuracy and standard deviation for all methods evaluated.  
(5) Exploring the proposed approach through a learning theory perspective would be valuable. The paper details gate units enabling identity propagation through selective layers, yet the theoretical implications of input-dependent gate responses warrant deeper analysis.
