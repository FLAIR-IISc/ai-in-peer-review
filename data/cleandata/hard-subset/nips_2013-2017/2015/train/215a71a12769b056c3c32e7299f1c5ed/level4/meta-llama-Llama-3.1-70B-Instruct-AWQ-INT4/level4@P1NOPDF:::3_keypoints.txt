- Proposes using LSTM-like (highway) units to enable training of very deep networks.  
- Paper is high-quality, well-written, original, and produces interesting results.  
- Tackles long-standing issues of initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients in deep nets.  
- Demonstrates a viable method for training 100-layer networks.  
- Experiments on routing of information and layer importance are particularly noteworthy.  
- Specific correction: in Sec. 4.1, change “most transform blocks are active on average” to “most transform blocks are not active on average.”  
- Suggestion: comment on how transform gates differ from simple identity (with noise) initialization per layer.  
- Overall endorsement: great paper and a pleasure to read.
