This paper proposes Highway Networks, a novel architecture that employs gating units inspired by LSTMs to facilitate the training of extremely deep neural networks. This is a high-quality, well-written paper that presents an original idea with compelling results.

It is widely recognized in the machine learning community that increasing network depth can improve performance, yet progress has long been hampered by optimization difficulties such as vanishing and exploding gradients. These challenges have historically limited the effective training of very deep architectures. This paper directly confronts this fundamental problem, presenting a practical method for training networks with up to one hundred layers. The experiments demonstrating dynamic information routing and analyzing the functional importance of different layers are particularly insightful.

Specific Comments:

1.  In Section 4.1, the phrase "most transform blocks are active on average" appears to be a typo. Based on the context and results, it seems this should read "are *not* active on average."

2.  A potential baseline for enabling deep network training is to initialize each layer's weight matrix to be near the identity transformation (e.g., the identity matrix with added noise). It would strengthen the paper to include a discussion on how the proposed transform gates offer advantages over this simpler initialization scheme.

Overall, this is an excellent paper that demonstrates a compelling method for training very deep neural networks. It was a pleasure to read.
