- Overall: Paper is promising with a clear core idea, persuasive empirical results, and useful probing of gating patterns.

1. The transform gate uses a sigmoid (never exactly 0/1), so Eq. 5’s Jacobian is technically incorrect—clarify exact backpropagation and derivatives through the gating pathway.

2. Recommending fixed negative gate biases by depth is insufficient—include a plot of performance vs. initial transform-gate bias to show initialization effects and regularization behavior.

3. The phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings” is too vague—explicitly state differences in experimental protocols or ensure comparisons are fair.

4. Reporting “max” accuracies is statistically uninformative—report mean accuracy and standard deviation for each method instead.

5. Add a learning-theoretic analysis: the gating enables approximate identity mappings and input-dependent routing across layers; a theoretical treatment would strengthen the contribution.
