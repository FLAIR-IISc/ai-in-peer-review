- Proposes highway networks: deep feedforward architecture with data-dependent transform and carry gates to route information across layers.
- Inspired by LSTM gating: each layer computes H(x) and T(x) with carry pathway weighted by 1 − T(x).
- Enables signals and gradients to traverse hundreds of layers, making very deep networks trainable with standard SGD.
- Presents both fully connected and convolutional variants and a simple initialization (negative bias for transform gates).
- Provides thorough analysis: gate biases/activations, data-dependent routing, and layer lesion experiments quantifying layer importance.
- Relation to prior work is clear: reminiscent of LSTM but distinct from FitNets and fixed skip connections due to learned, input-dependent routing.
- Compares against plain networks (variance-preserving init) and FitNets; discusses related work on initialization, activations, and deep supervision.
- Technical quality is strong: convincing optimization study showing plain nets degrade with depth while highway nets scale to 50–100 layers and converge faster.
- Classification results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 are competitive using modest augmentation and single-stage training; variances reported where available.
- Empirical claims are well supported by experiments and analyses.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized; architecture and training setup are described succinctly.
- Minor clarity/typo issue: reference “Tables 3 and 3” on p. 5.
- Originality: novel adaptation of LSTM-style gating to feedforward depth, distinct from skip-connection and hint-based approaches.
- Significance: simple, broadly applicable method likely to impact research and practice by enabling direct training of very deep networks.
- Pros: elegant/simple architecture, strong depth scalability, competitive results without complex pipelines, clear comparisons, insightful analysis.
- Con: only a minor typographical issue noted.
- Recommendation: Accept — solid, novel technique with good results and thorough analysis.
