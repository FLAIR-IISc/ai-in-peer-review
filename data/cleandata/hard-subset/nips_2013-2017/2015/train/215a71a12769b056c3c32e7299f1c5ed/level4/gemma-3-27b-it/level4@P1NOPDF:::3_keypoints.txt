- Proposes using LSTM units to enable training of very deep (100-layer) networks.  
- Paper is high-quality, well-written, and presents an original idea.  
- Results are interesting and demonstrate successful deep-network training.  
- Addresses core issues of depth: initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients.  
- Method empirically enables effective information flow through very deep networks.  
- Experiments on information routing and layer importance are compelling.  
- Typo/correction: “most transform blocks are active on average” should likely be “not active on average.”  
- Suggestion: discuss identity-initialization strategies and how transform gates differ from highway networks.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper and a pleasure to read.
