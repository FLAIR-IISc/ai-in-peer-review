- Introduces a novel "highway network" architecture, using LSTM-like gating, to enable effective training of extremely deep networks.  
- Tackles the vanishing/exploding gradient problem by allowing unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Empirically demonstrates successful training of networks up to 100 layers.  
- Experiments are thoughtfully designed; analyses and visualizations of internal dynamics and gating behavior are especially insightful.  
- Requests clarification: the claim "most transform blocks are active on average" seems counterintuitive and may be a typo or needs verification/clarification.  
- Suggests adding a brief comparison to a simple baseline (e.g., identity-like weight initialization for deep plain networks) to highlight advantages of dynamic gates over static initialization.  
- Overall verdict: excellent, well-written, original, and a significant contribution to training very deep neural networks.
