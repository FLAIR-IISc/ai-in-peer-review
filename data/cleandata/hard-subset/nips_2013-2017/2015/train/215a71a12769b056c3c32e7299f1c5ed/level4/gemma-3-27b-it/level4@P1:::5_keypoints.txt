- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-inspired adaptive gating to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.
- Idea is simple, elegant, and backed by solid theoretical and empirical analysis.
- Clearly frames the vanishing gradient problem and situates work among optimizers, initialization, activations, and skip-connection methods.
- Mathematical formulation is concise and clear (Equation 3).
- Empirical results demonstrate training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard gradient descent (Figure 1).
- Relevant comparisons to Fitnets and state-of-the-art methods support the authors’ claims (Tables 2 & 3).
- Analysis of learned gate biases and layer importance yields valuable insights into network behavior (Figures 2 & 4).
- Code and hyperparameters are publicly available.
- Transform gate bias initialization is treated as a hyperparameter and needs more systematic exploration.
- Discussion of relationships to other depth-increasing techniques (e.g., Inception, stacked 3x3 convs) is limited and could be expanded; gating may generalize these methods.
- Suggests an experiment: train a single deep “thin and tall” network forked for MNIST and CIFAR-10 to study parameter sharing and utilization.
- Experiments are limited to small datasets; evaluation on larger datasets like ImageNet would strengthen the paper.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/elegant solution, strong validation, clear writing, insightful analysis, public code.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited large-scale experiments, need further study of gate bias and deeper comparison to other methods.
- Overall recommendation: accept.
