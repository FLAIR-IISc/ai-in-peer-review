- Main contribution: Introduces highway networks that use LSTM-style gating to enable unimpeded information flow and train very deep feedforward networks via SGD.  
- Empirical result: Shows highway networks can be trained directly with SGD even with hundreds of layers, outperforming plain networks in optimization and generalization.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on and differentiates from prior techniques (better optimizers, initialization, skip connections) and explicitly links to LSTM recurrence.  
- Technical quality: Method is technically sound with convincing theoretical motivation and supportive experiments, including ablation studies.  
- Weakness in analysis: Transform gate behavior analysis is interesting but limited and would benefit from more quantitative measures or visualizations.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, notation is consistent, equations concise, and experiments reproducible.  
- Related-work integration: Related work is discussed but could be better integrated throughout the paper rather than concentrated in the introduction.  
- Originality: Novel combination of gating and skip connections for feedforward networks and exploration of data-dependent routing increases originality.  
- Significance: Enables exploration of much deeper architectures, with potential broad impact across vision, NLP, and speech, and offers insights into layer importance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a fundamental training challenge, presents a novel effective architecture, provides strong experimental validation, and is clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Needs deeper gate analysis, improved related-work integration, and further evidence of practical benefits beyond the shown experiments.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable contribution that outweighs minor weaknesses—recommend accept.
