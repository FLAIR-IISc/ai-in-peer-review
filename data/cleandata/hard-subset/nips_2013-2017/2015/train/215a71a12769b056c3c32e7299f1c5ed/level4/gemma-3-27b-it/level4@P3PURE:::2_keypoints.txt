- Proposes a novel architecture enabling very deep networks by carrying input signals unchanged through layers via input-dependent gates.  
- Gating mechanism allows training of significantly deeper networks than standard feedforward architectures.  
- Provides compelling, expected analysis of trained networks, examining how gates select inputs across layers.  
- Paper is high quality, clearly written, and presents original work.  
- Authors acknowledge similarities to FitNets and LSTMs but clearly articulate differences and provide appropriate comparisons.  
- Experimental results are promising and demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Reviewer has no suggested improvements.  
- Minor issue: repeated table reference on page 5 ("Tables 3 and 3").  
- Overall assessment: solid paper with a novel, effective technique and thorough, interesting analysis.
