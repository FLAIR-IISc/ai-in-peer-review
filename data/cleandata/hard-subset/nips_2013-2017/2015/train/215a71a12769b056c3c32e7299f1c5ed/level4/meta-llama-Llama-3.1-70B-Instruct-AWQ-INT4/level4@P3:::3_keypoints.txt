- Proposes LSTM-inspired adaptive gating ("highway networks") for training very deep networks.
- Paper is high-quality, well-written, and the idea is original and interesting.
- Addresses core problems of initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients to enable training of 100-layer nets.
- Provides a thorough literature review and identifies key challenges in training very deep networks.
- Introduces a novel, well-explained architecture (highway networks) designed to overcome these challenges.
- Experiments are well-designed and show highway networks can be trained directly with SGD.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on several benchmark datasets.
- Offers detailed analyses of network behavior, including information routing and layer importance.
- Specific comment: Section 4.1 wording should change "most transform blocks are active on average" to "not active on average."
- Specific suggestion: discuss how transform gates differ from simple identity initialization of layers.
- Strengths summarized: original idea, clear writing, thorough literature analysis, strong experiments, SOTA results.
- No weaknesses were noted by the reviewer.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective method, strong experimental validation, and good presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: none noted.
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful paper demonstrating an effective method for training very deep neural networks.
