- Proposal: Highway networks use LSTM-inspired adaptive gates to let layers transform or carry inputs, creating information “highways” for very deep feedforward nets.
- Training: These networks can be trained with standard SGD to depths previously unattainable, with detailed analysis of learned gating behavior.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on work improving gradient flow (optimizers, initialization, activations, skip connections) but uniquely offers layer-wise, LSTM-style gating.
- Novelty: Conceptually simple yet effective gating mechanism provides a compelling alternative for training deep networks.
- Technical soundness: Clear mathematical formulation (Equation 3) and rigorous experiments support the approach.
- Experimental validation: Strong results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100; favorable comparisons to plain networks and FitNets, with ablation studies.
- Analysis: Detailed investigation of gates, layer importance, and data-dependent routing gives insight into network behavior.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with consistent notation.
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Needs more exploration of transform gate bias and sensitivity across depths and datasets.
- Weakness — computational cost: Gating introduces computational overhead; more cost comparisons are warranted.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, effective method with compelling experiments, insightful analysis, and excellent presentation.
- Arguments for rejection: None significant; only minor additional analyses suggested.
- Overall assessment: Strong, original contribution that justifies acceptance to NIPS 2015.
- Minor note: Typo on page 5: "Tables 3 and 3" should likely be "Table 3."
