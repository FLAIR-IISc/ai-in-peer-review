- Proposes a simple, elegant LSTM-inspired gating mechanism that enables training of extremely deep feedforward networks; method is well-motivated with solid analysis.  
- Extends the gradient-trapping gate trick from recurrent to feedforward architectures—natural, insightful, and a valuable contribution.  
- Offers an alternative perspective: very deep networks can be seen as stacks of complex computational blocks/macro-layers rather than many simple layers, analogous to Inception/VGG motifs.  
- Suggests a multi-task experiment (train one deep narrow network on MNIST and CIFAR-10 with separate heads) to probe layer utilization, feature sharing, and task-specific depth allocation.  
- Recommends testing the architecture on a larger, more complex dataset (e.g., ImageNet) to demonstrate scalability and strengthen empirical results.  
- Reviewer reports no major weaknesses and congratulates the authors.
