I had also considered using an LSTM-style gradient-preserving trick to scale networks to greater depths, but hadn’t started the work. Kudos to the authors for getting there first!

There is a possible alternative viewpoint: a very deep network can be seen as a not-so-deep network whose layers contain sublayers. Each “layer” thus implements a more complex nonlinearity than a conventional rectifier/sigmoid layer. This is how I think about GoogLeNet’s Inception, the three stacked 3x3 blocks in VGG, etc., and I wonder if this is also a valid way to view the highway network.

I suggest one experiment: use a single thin-and-tall network to train on both MNIST and CIFAR (with a fork at the top for the two domains). I’m interested in whether, at inference, the network exhibits the same patterns as models trained separately for each domain, and whether parameters underutilized on MNIST are taken up by CIFAR. In other words, I’d like to see how sharing emerges in a very deep hierarchy and how efficient that sharing is.

No major complaints. I encourage the authors to evaluate the network on larger datasets such as ImageNet.
