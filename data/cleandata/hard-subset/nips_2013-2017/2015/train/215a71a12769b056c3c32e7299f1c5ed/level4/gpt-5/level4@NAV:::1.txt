I find the paper compelling: the central idea is clearly articulated, and the experiments are convincing to a reasonable extent. What I appreciate most is the authors’ effort to probe the learned networks by examining the patterns of gate outputs.
(1) The gate unit T uses a standard sigmoid activation. Consequently, its output never attains exact 0 or 1 values (as the authors also note), and the gradients in Eq. 5 are therefore not correct. The paper should explicitly detail how backpropagation is actually performed in these networks.
(2) While initialization effects are notoriously difficult to analyze, the proposal to use constant negative biases as a function of the number of levels is not fully satisfactory. I would like to see a plot of performance versus the initial bias value. In this setting, the initial bias acts as a kind of regularizer, with more negative biases favoring networks with “longer highways” (a comparison I like).
(3) The phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings” is unsatisfactory. Either the methods use the same experimental setup, permitting direct comparison, or they do not, which precludes it. The authors should clarify the differences between experimental setups in the paper.
(4) Reporting “max” accuracies in tables is uncommon and not statistically sound. The authors should instead report mean accuracy and standard deviation for each method (when available).
(5) It would be interesting to analyze the proposed approach from a learning-theoretic perspective. The paper introduces additional gate units that can let layers act as an identity. Because the gate responses are input-dependent, they allow inputs to be propagated through many highly selective layers.
