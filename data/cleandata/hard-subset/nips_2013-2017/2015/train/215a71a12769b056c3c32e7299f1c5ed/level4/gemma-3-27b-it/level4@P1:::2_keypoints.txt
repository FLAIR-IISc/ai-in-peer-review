- Proposes a novel highway network architecture using input-dependent gates to carry information unchanged through layers.  
- Enables training of significantly deeper feedforward networks than typical architectures.  
- Provides detailed analysis of trained networks showing how gates select inputs across layers.  
- Paper is high quality, clearly written, and presents an original contribution.  
- Differences from related work (FitNets, LSTMs) are clearly explained and appropriately compared.  
- Experimental results are promising and demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- LSTM-inspired gating facilitates information flow and gradient propagation, allowing training of hundreds of layers with SGD.  
- Analysis reveals patterns in information routing and layer importance in highway networks.  
- Experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 demonstrate practical applicability and generalization.  
- Minor issue: repeated mention of “Tables 3 and 3” on page 5.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, significance, clarity, and originality.  
- Arguments against acceptance: none significant; LSTM influence noted but contribution remains substantial.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
