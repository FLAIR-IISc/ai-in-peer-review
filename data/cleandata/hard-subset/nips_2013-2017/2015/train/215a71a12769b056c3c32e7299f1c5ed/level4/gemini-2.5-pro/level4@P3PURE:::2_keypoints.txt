- Proposes a novel architecture for very deep networks using input-dependent gates that allow information to be carried unchanged through layers.  
- This gating mechanism enables training of much deeper networks than standard feed-forward architectures, with promising empirical results.  
- Paper is high quality, original, and clearly written.  
- Effectively situates method relative to prior work (notably LSTMs and FitNets), explaining differences and providing comparisons.  
- Provides strong, insightful analysis of trained networks, investigating how gates select inputs across layers.  
- Overall assessment: solid paper—novel technique, thorough analysis, strong results; reviewer recommends no changes.  
- Minor issue: typo on page 5 referencing "Tables 3 and 3."
