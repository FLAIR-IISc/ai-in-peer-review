- Proposes highway networks with LSTM-inspired adaptive gates (information highways) to allow unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Demonstrates training of very deep feedforward networks (hundreds of layers) directly with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Properly situates work among prior research on optimization, initialization, activations, skip connections, and knowledge distillation.  
- Distinguishes contribution by using per-layer adaptive gating rather than focusing on optimization or initialization tricks.  
- Novelty: elegant, LSTM-like gating mechanism that is a fresh approach to training deep networks.  
- Technical soundness: clear mathematical formulation (Eq. 3) and justified initialization bias for transform gates.  
- Empirical validation: strong results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100; convincing comparisons to plain nets and FitNets.  
- Valuable analyses: ablation study and layer-importance analysis (Figure 4) enhance understanding.  
- Clarity: well-written with consistent notation and informative figures.  
- Significance: enables exploration of much deeper models, with potential for substantial impact.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of architectural variations, especially transform/carry gate configurations beyond C = 1 − T.  
- Weakness: added gating increases parameters and computational cost; lacks detailed overhead analysis.  
- Weakness: missing experiments on large-scale dataset (ImageNet) to demonstrate scalability.  
- For acceptance: novel, technically sound, compelling experiments, clear presentation, and high potential impact.  
- Against acceptance: absence of ImageNet results and limited architectural exploration.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance; suggestion to try a single network on both MNIST and CIFAR is useful but not essential.
