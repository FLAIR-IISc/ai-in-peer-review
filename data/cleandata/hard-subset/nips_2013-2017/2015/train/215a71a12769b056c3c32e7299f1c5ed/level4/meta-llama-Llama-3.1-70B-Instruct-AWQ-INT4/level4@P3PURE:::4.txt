The paper proposes a generalization of training neural networks with many stacked layers, leveraging the concept of "information highways." This idea is effectively demonstrated through experimentation, and the authors have shown that classification accuracy results can be improved using this method.

Quality: The idea presented appears to be novel, and the experimentation is solid. However, the analysis sections could benefit from a bit more variety in terms of details or illustrations to further support the claims.

Clarity: The main messages of the paper are well-delivered and quite clear, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments.

Originality: Although the proposed method is a generalization of previous neural network techniques, it is sufficiently original and contributes to the existing body of knowledge.

Significance: The paper illustrates its significance to the field, as the ability to train deeper neural networks is crucial for achieving better results in various application areas.

The paper describes an effective method for training very deep neural networks by means of "information highways," which involve building direct connections to upper network layers. Although this approach is a generalization of prior techniques, such as cross-layer connections, the authors have demonstrated its effectiveness through experimentation. The contributions are novel and well-supported by experimental evidence.
