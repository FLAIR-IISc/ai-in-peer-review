This paper presents an LSTM-inspired mechanism that enables the training of very deep feedforward networks. The idea is simple and elegant, and the analyses are solid. I had also considered using an LSTM-style “gradient-trapping” mechanism to grow deeper networks, so congratulations to the authors for being the first to get there.

A potentially useful perspective is to view a very deep network as a not-so-deep network whose layers contain sub-layers, so that each “layer” implements a richer nonlinearity than a traditional rectifier/sigmoid layer. This is how I interpret Inception modules in GoogLeNet and the three stacked 3×3 convolutions in VGG. I wonder whether highway networks can also be productively viewed this way.

I suggest an experiment: train a single thin-and-tall network jointly on MNIST and CIFAR, branching only at the top into two task-specific heads. At inference time, examine whether the network exhibits patterns similar to those found in separately trained models for each domain, and whether parameters that are underutilized for MNIST are picked up by CIFAR. In other words, I would like to see how sharing emerges in a very deep hierarchy and whether that sharing is as efficient as possible.

I have no major complaints. I encourage the authors to evaluate the network on larger datasets such as ImageNet.
