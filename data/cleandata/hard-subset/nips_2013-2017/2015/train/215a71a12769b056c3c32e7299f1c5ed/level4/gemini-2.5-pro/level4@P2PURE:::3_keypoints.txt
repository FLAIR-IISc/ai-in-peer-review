- High-quality, well-written paper presenting an original, effective method for training extremely deep neural networks.
- Proposed architecture is inspired by LSTM-style gating and offers a compelling solution to a longstanding optimization challenge.
- Addresses vanishing/exploding gradients and demonstrates successful training of networks up to ~100 layers.
- Experiments that visualize information routing and analyze dynamic layer importance are particularly insightful.
- Typo in Section 4.1: "most transform blocks are active on average" likely should read "not active on average."
- Suggestion: compare transform gates to a simple near-identity initialization baseline and clarify advantages of dynamic gating over static initialization.
- Overall assessment: significant advance in training very deep networks and a pleasure to read.
