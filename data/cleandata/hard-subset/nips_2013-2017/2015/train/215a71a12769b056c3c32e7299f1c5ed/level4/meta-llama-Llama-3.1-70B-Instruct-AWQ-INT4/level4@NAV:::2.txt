This paper introduces a novel architecture for extremely deep networks, incorporating self-gated layers that allow input data to bypass transformation, contingent upon the input itself.
This innovation facilitates the training of substantially deeper networks than would be feasible with traditional feedforward architectures.
The authors conduct a compelling analysis of the trained networks, elucidating the gate selection mechanisms at various network layers, which aligns with my expectations for such a study.
The manuscript is of high caliber, boasting clarity, originality, and technical merit.
Although it shares some similarities with recent works (notably FitNets and LSTMs), the authors succinctly articulate the distinctions and provide pertinent comparisons where applicable.
The results reported are encouraging and demonstrate the technique's potential.
I do not have any suggested improvements for the manuscript.
However, I would like to point out a minor error: the repetition of "Tables 3 and 3" on page 5.
Overall, this is a well-crafted paper that presents a groundbreaking technique, yields favorable results, and features an engaging and thorough analysis.
