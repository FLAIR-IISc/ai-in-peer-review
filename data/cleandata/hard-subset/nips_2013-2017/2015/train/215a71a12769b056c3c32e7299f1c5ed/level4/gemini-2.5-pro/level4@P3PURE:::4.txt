This paper proposes a generalization for training neural networks with many stacked layers by introducing 'information highways,' which create direct connections to upper layers. The authors demonstrate through solid experimentation that this method is effective and can improve classification accuracy.

The quality of the work is strong; the core idea seems novel and the experimental support is solid. The proposed method is sufficiently original, although it is presented as a generalization of previous techniques such as cross-layer connections. Furthermore, the paper's main messages are delivered clearly. The work is significant to the field, as the ability to effectively train increasingly deep neural networks is crucial for achieving better results in many application areas.

The primary area for improvement is in the analysis sections, which could be strengthened by including a greater variety of details or illustrations.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective method for training very deep neural networks, and its contributions are well-supported by experimental evidence.
