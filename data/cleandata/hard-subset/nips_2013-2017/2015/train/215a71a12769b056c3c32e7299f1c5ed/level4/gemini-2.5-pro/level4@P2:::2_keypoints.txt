- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-inspired transform and carry gates that let feedforward networks train at extreme depths and be optimized with SGD on tasks like MNIST and CIFAR.  
- Novel and significant idea: adapts temporal LSTM gating to depth, addressing vanishing gradients and enabling exploration of very deep architectures.  
- Technical quality is high with rigorous experiments showing highway networks overcome degradation as depth increases (10→100+ layers).  
- Strong comparison to FitNets: matches/surpasses a recent two-stage method using single-stage training.  
- In-depth analysis: visualizations of gate activations, data-dependent routing, and layer lesioning demonstrate meaningful use of depth.  
- Empirical finding: network uses depth for complex tasks (CIFAR-100) and ignores unnecessary layers for simpler tasks (MNIST).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and situates itself appropriately in related work.  
- Weakness: gate coupling C = 1 − T limits flexibility; independent carry/transform gates could enable richer behaviors (future work).  
- Weakness: sensitivity to transform-gate bias initialization should be discussed—how critical are the small negative biases?  
- Weakness: routing analysis is largely qualitative; a quantitative metric (e.g., average effective path length per class) would strengthen claims.  
- Minor comment: possible table reference typo on page 5 (should likely refer to Tables 2 and 3).  
- Conclusion: solid, original, and effective contribution with strong empirical support; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
