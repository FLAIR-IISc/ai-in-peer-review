- Proposes Highway Networks: a new, LSTM-inspired gated architecture enabling effective training of extremely deep neural networks.  
- Paper is high-quality, well-written, original, and shows compelling experimental results.  
- Tackles the long-standing optimization problems (vanishing/exploding gradients) that limit very deep architectures, demonstrating training of networks with hundreds of layers.  
- Experiments analyzing dynamic routing and the learned importance of individual layers are particularly insightful.  
- Specific issue: Possible typo in Section 4.1 — text says “most transform blocks are active on average” but figures/context suggest it should say “not active on average”; needs clarification.  
- Suggested improvement: Include a brief comparison to a simpler baseline (e.g., standard deep network initialized near identity) and discuss how highway networks’ ability to learn dynamic routing differs.  
- Overall verdict: Excellent, timely paper that represents a significant advance and was a pleasure to review.
