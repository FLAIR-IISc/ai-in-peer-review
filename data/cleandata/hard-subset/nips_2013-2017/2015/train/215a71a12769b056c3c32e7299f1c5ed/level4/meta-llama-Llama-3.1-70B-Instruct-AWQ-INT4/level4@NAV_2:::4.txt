This paper introduces a novel architecture for training very deep neural networks, which the authors term "highway networks." The central idea is to create "information highways" that allow for unimpeded information flow across many layers, inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks. The proposed architecture uses adaptive gating units to regulate the information flow, enabling the training of extremely deep and efficient networks.

The authors provide a thorough review of previous work on training deep neural networks, highlighting the challenges and limitations of existing approaches. They also clearly explain the motivation behind their proposed architecture and how it addresses these challenges. The paper is well-organized, and the main ideas are easy to follow.

The strengths of the paper include the novelty of the proposed architecture, the solid experimentation, and the clear delivery of the main messages. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through rigorous optimization experiments and classification accuracy results on various datasets. The analysis sections provide valuable insights into the behavior of the proposed architecture, including the routing of information and the importance of individual layers.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the analysis sections could benefit from more variety in terms of details or illustrations. Additionally, while the authors provide a clear explanation of their proposed architecture, some readers may find the notation and mathematical formulations challenging to follow.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning, as it provides a novel and effective method for training very deep neural networks. The proposed architecture has the potential to improve the performance of deep neural networks in various application areas, and the authors provide sufficient evidence to support their claims.
