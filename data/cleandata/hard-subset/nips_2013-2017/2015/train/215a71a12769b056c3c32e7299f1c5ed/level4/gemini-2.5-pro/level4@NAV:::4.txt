This paper presents a generalized framework for training deep neural networks composed of many stacked layers. The central idea of 'information highways' is shown to be effective through a series of experiments. Moreover, the authors demonstrate that this method can enhance classification accuracy.

Quality: The proposed concept is novel, and the empirical validation is sound. The analysis could be strengthened with a greater variety of details or illustrative examples.
Clarity: The paper is well-written, and its core messages are communicated with a high degree of clarity.
Originality: While a generalization of previous neural network techniques, the proposed method possesses sufficient originality.
Significance: The paper holds considerable significance for the field, given the ongoing need to effectively train increasingly deep neural networks to improve results in key application areas.

The paper details an effective technique for training very deep neural networks through the use of 'information highways,' which establish direct connections to upper layers of the network. Although it extends prior techniques like cross-layer connections, the authors have empirically proven the method's effectiveness. The contributions are novel and are convincingly supported by the experimental evidence presented.
