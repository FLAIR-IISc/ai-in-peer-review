A potentially insightful perspective is that very deep networks can be viewed as shallow networks where each "layer" incorporates sub-layers, resulting in more complex non-linear behavior per layer compared to traditional rectifier or sigmoid units. This aligns with my interpretation of GoogLeNet's inception modules, the three stacked 3×3 convolutions in VGG, and may also provide a valid framework for understanding highway networks.

I suggest the authors conduct an additional experiment: train a single thin-and-tall network on both MNIST and CIFAR-10, with a shared initial segment (forking early for domain-specific processing). The key question is whether, at inference time, the network exhibits similar parameter utilization patterns as networks trained separately for each domain—specifically, whether parameters underutilized in MNIST are effectively recruited by the CIFAR-10 task. This would illuminate how parameter sharing operates within a deep hierarchical structure and whether it achieves optimal efficiency.
