- Introduces "highway networks," a novel architecture inspired by LSTM that enables unimpeded information flow across many layers.  
- Uses adaptive gating units to regulate information flow, allowing training of extremely deep networks via SGD.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis with consistent notation and sound mathematical derivations.  
- Demonstrates dynamic routing of information and evaluates the contribution of individual layers.  
- Extensive and well-presented experiments across datasets and architectures showing effective training of very deep networks.  
- Results are competitive with state-of-the-art on several benchmarks.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the approach easy to follow.  
- Key contribution: enabling direct training of very deep networks (hundreds of layers).  
- Strengths and weaknesses are discussed and related work is reviewed.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparisons to other deep-training approaches.  
- Would benefit from additional experiments or analyses to further validate effectiveness.  
- Suggestion to evaluate on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to strengthen claims.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with impressive results; recommended for acceptance (rating 9/10, confidence 8/10).
