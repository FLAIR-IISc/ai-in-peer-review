This is a high-quality, well-written paper that presents an original and effective method for training extremely deep neural networks. The proposed architecture, which draws inspiration from the gating mechanisms in LSTMs, offers a compelling solution to a long-standing challenge in the field.

It is well-established that increasing network depth can improve performance, but progress has been severely limited by optimization difficulties such as vanishing and exploding gradients. Indeed, the challenge of propagating information through many layers arguably hindered the widespread use of deep networks for decades. This paper confronts this problem directly, successfully demonstrating a method for training networks with as many as 100 layers. The experiments that visualize information routing and analyze the dynamic importance of individual layers are particularly insightful.

Specific Comments:

*   In Section 4.1, the phrase "most transform blocks are active on average" appears to be a typo; it should likely read "not active on average."
*   It would be beneficial for the authors to comment on how the proposed transform gates differentiate highway networks from a simpler baseline, such as initializing each layer of a very deep network with a near-identity transformation (e.g., an identity matrix with a small amount of noise). Clarifying the advantages of the dynamic gating mechanism over this static initialization scheme would strengthen the paper.

Overall, this is an excellent paper that demonstrates a significant advance in training very deep neural networks. It was a pleasure to read.
