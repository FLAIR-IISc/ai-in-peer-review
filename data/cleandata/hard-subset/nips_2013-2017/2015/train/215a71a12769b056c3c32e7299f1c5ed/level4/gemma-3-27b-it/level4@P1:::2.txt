This paper proposes a novel architecture for very deep networks that enables information to be “carried” unchanged through layers using input-dependent gates. This allows for the training of significantly deeper networks than is typically possible with standard feedforward architectures. The authors perform a particularly interesting analysis of the trained networks to determine how the gates select inputs across different layers – precisely the kind of investigation one would expect and appreciate.

The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and presents an original contribution. While there is naturally some similarity to recent work, such as FitNets [25] and LSTMs [29, 30], the differences are clearly explained, and appropriate comparisons are made. The results are promising and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The main strength of this work lies in its ability to address the longstanding challenge of training very deep networks. By drawing inspiration from LSTM recurrent networks, the authors have devised a mechanism that facilitates information flow and gradient propagation, enabling successful training with hundreds of layers using standard stochastic gradient descent. This is a significant achievement, especially considering the difficulties encountered with plain networks. The detailed analysis of the learned gate behavior provides valuable insights into how these highway networks operate, revealing patterns in information routing and layer importance.  The experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 demonstrate the practical applicability and generalization ability of the proposed architecture. 

A minor point to address is the repeated mention of "Tables 3 and 3" on page 5.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture is a novel approach to address the difficulties of training very deep networks.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method is theoretically well-motivated and supported by extensive experimental results.
*   **Significance:** The ability to train extremely deep networks opens up possibilities for exploring more complex models and achieving improved performance on challenging tasks.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method and experimental results.
*   **Originality:** The approach is demonstrably different from existing techniques and offers a unique perspective on the problem of deep network training.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   None significant. While the technique builds on LSTM ideas, the application to feedforward networks and the detailed analysis presented represent a substantial contribution.



Overall, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is innovative and achieves compelling results. The analysis is insightful and thorough. I recommend acceptance.
