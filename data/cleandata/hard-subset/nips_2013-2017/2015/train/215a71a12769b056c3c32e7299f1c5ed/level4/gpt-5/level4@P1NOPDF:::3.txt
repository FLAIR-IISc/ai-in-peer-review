This paper proposes using LSTM units to train very deep networks. It is a high-quality, well-written paper. The idea is original, and the results are quite interesting. Machine learning researchers often obtain better results by adding more layers to their neural networks, but issues with initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients have been severe bottlenecks. Indeed, the difficulty of propagating information through deep networks arguably kept them from widespread use for decades. This paper tackles the problem directly and demonstrates a method for training 100-layer networks. The experiments on information routing and layer importance are particularly compelling.

Specific comments:
- In Section 4.1, “most transform blocks are active on average” appears to be a mistake and should be “not active on average.”
- One could initialize each layer of a very deep network with the identity matrix (perhaps plus small noise). It would be useful to comment on how transform gates distinguish highway networks from this simple identity-initialization scheme.

Overall, this is a great paper that clearly demonstrates a practical method for training very deep neural networks. It was a pleasure to read.
