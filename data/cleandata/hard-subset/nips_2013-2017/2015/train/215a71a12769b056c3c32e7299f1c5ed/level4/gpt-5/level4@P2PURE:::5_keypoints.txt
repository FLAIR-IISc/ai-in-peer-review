- Reviewer had considered a similar LSTM-style trick to preserve/trap gradients for deep networks and congratulates the authors for getting there first.
- Proposes viewing very deep networks as shallower networks with richer sub-layers, likening this to Inception and VGG, and wonders if highway networks can be understood similarly.
- Suggests an experiment: train a single thin-and-tall network jointly on MNIST and CIFAR with a shared trunk and dataset-specific top branches to inspect shared representations and parameter reuse.
- Encourages testing the method on larger datasets such as ImageNet.
- Overall positive: the LSTM-inspired idea enables training very deep feedforward networks; the idea is simple, elegant, and the analyses are solid.
