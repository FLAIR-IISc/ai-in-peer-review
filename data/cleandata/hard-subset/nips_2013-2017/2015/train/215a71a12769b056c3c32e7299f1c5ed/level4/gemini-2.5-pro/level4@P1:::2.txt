This paper introduces "highway networks," a novel feed-forward architecture designed to facilitate the training of extremely deep neural networks. The core idea, inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, is to incorporate adaptive gating units that regulate information flow through the network. These gates can learn to either transform the input at a given layer or carry it forward unchanged, creating "information highways" that allow unimpeded gradient and information flow across many layers. This architecture directly addresses the well-known difficulty of training standard "plain" networks as their depth increases, a problem that typically leads to degraded performance and optimization challenges. The authors demonstrate that highway networks with hundreds of layers can be trained directly and efficiently using simple stochastic gradient descent.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Elegant and Effective Architecture:** The proposed highway network is an elegant and principled solution to the significant challenge of training very deep networks. By adapting the gating mechanism from LSTMs for a feed-forward context, the authors provide a powerful yet simple method to combat the optimization difficulties that have historically limited network depth.

2.  **Strong Empirical Validation:** The paper's claims are convincingly supported by a thorough set of experiments. The optimization experiments in Section 3.1 provide a direct and clear comparison against plain networks, demonstrating that while plain networks degrade significantly beyond 20 layers, highway networks maintain strong performance at depths of 50 and 100 layers. Furthermore, the results on CIFAR-10/100 show that highway networks can match or surpass the performance of more complex methods like FitNets, which require a multi-stage training process, using only a single stage of standard SGD.

3.  **Insightful Analysis:** A particularly strong aspect of this work is the detailed analysis of the trained networks' internal dynamics. The authors go beyond simply reporting accuracy scores and provide valuable insights into *how* the architecture works. The visualizations of gate activities (Figure 2) and the analysis of data-dependent information routing (Figure 3) are excellent, showing that the network learns to selectively engage different layers for different inputs and classes. The layer lesioning study (Figure 4) is also highly informative, revealing how the network learns to utilize its depth according to task complexity—using fewer layers for MNIST and nearly all available layers for the more complex CIFAR-100 dataset. This kind of analysis is exactly what is needed to understand these models and is a major contribution in its own right.

4.  **High Quality, Clarity, and Originality:** The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and well-organized. The core concepts are explained effectively, and the work is well-contextualized within the existing literature. While the idea is inspired by LSTMs and shares some conceptual similarity with concurrent work, the specific application and the thorough investigation are novel and constitute a significant contribution. The authors clearly distinguish their approach from related techniques like skip connections and FitNets.

**Weaknesses:**

The paper is a very complete and compelling piece of work, and it is difficult to identify any significant weaknesses. The proposed method is well-motivated, the experiments are thorough, and the analysis is insightful.

**Minor Comments:**

*   On page 5, the text refers to "Tables 3 and 3." This appears to be a minor typo.

**Summary:**

This is a very solid paper that presents a novel, simple, and effective technique for training very deep neural networks. The proposed highway network architecture achieves excellent results, and the accompanying analysis provides a deep and interesting look into the model's behavior. The work is high-quality, original, and significant, as it opens the door to routinely training and studying networks of a depth that was previously considered impractical. I strongly recommend acceptance.
