- Proposes using LSTM-style units (highway networks) to train very deep networks.  
- Paper is high quality, well-written, original, and presents intriguing results.  
- Addresses long-standing issues of initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients in deep nets.  
- Demonstrates a viable method for training 100-layer networks.  
- Experiments on information routing and layer importance are particularly compelling.  
- Correction: Section 4.1 should state that transform blocks are “not active on average,” not “most transform blocks are active on average.”  
- Suggestion: discuss identity-initialization (with small noise) as an alternative and contrast how transform gates differ from that simple approach.  
- Overall assessment: excellent paper and an enjoyable read.
