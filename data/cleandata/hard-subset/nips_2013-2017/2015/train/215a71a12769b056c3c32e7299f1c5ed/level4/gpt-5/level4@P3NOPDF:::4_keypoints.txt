- Summary: Proposes "information highways"—direct cross-layer connections—to train much deeper networks, generalizing skip connections and showing improved accuracy and optimization stability.

- Novelty/Relevance: Timely, sufficiently original generalization of cross-layer connections addressing deep-network degradation.

- Empirical Support: Strong experiments showing consistent accuracy gains and successful optimization of substantially deeper models.

- Clarity: Well-written paper with clear motivation, method, and findings.

- Ablation Needs: Requires richer ablation studies (which layers get highways, connection density/patterns).

- Depth Sensitivity: Needs analysis of at what depths highways help and how benefits scale with added layers.

- Information-Flow Diagnostics: Should visualize/measure gate activations or effective path lengths to support intuition.

- Comparisons to Related Work: Needs direct comparisons with other skip-connection variants, auxiliary classifiers, and deep supervision to clarify novelty and trade-offs.

- Computational Costs: Must quantify overhead (parameters, FLOPs, memory) and provide parameter- or compute-matched baselines to separate capacity vs. optimization effects.

- Reproducibility: Provide detailed training protocols (initialization, gating, learning-rate schedules, regularization, hyperparameters) and any special highway initializations.

- Limitations/Scope: Discuss regimes where highways may not help and whether performance saturates or degrades at extreme depths.

- Questions for Authors: Sensitivity to highway initialization/gating; runtime/memory overhead; generality across architectures/datasets; optimal placement/fraction of highways; whether highways act as identity mappings early and how representations evolve.

- Suggested Improvements: Add ablations/sensitivity analyses, controlled parameter-matched comparisons, gate/path visualizations, expanded related work, and full training details for replication.

- Overall Evaluation: Strong, well-motivated, and empirically validated contribution with high significance; recommend acceptance pending deeper analysis and clearer positioning.
