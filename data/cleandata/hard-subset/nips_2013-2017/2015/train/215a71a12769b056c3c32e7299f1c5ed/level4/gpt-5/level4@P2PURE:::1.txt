I like the paper: the idea is clearly described and the experiments are convincing to a certain degree. What I appreciate most is the authors’ effort to analyze the learned networks by examining the patterns of gate outputs.

(1) The gate unit T uses a standard sigmoid activation, so its output is never exactly 0 or 1 (as the authors also state). Consequently, the gradients in eq. 5 are not correct. The paper should explain precisely how backpropagation is actually performed in these networks.

(2) While the effect of initialization is always tricky to analyze, proposing constant negative biases that depend on the number of levels is also not satisfactory. Please include a plot of performance as a function of the initial bias. Here, the initial bias acts as a kind of regularizer, with a highly negative bias favoring networks with “longer highways” (I like this comparison).

(3) The phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings” is unsatisfying. Either the methods use the same experimental setting, which permits comparison, or they do not, which precludes a direct comparison. The paper should clarify the differences among the experimental setups.

(4) Reporting “max” accuracies in tables is uncommon and not sound from a statistical perspective. The authors should report mean accuracy and standard deviation for every method (when available).

(5) It would be interesting to study the proposed approach from a learning-theoretic perspective. The paper presents additional gate units in neural networks that allow layers to act as the identity. Because the gate response depends on the input, it enables inputs to propagate through a large number of very selective layers.
