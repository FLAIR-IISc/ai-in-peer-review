- Introduces highway networks using LSTM-like transform and carry gates to enable direct training of very deep feedforward networks with standard SGD.  
- Shows deep highway nets (tens–hundreds of layers) optimize effectively and avoid the degradation seen in plain nets, with experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Compares favorably to FitNets without requiring multi-stage distillation.  
- Analyzes gate biases/activities, data-dependent routing, and layer importance via lesion studies to explain how depth is used.  
- Positioning: connects to LSTM gating, improved initialization, skip connections, maxout, and hint-based training; gates provide learned, input-dependent routing unlike fixed skips.  
- Reviewer notes independent prior consideration of the idea and congratulates authors for the first convincing execution.  
- Conceptual perspective: highway layers can be viewed as composite, input-adaptive sub-layer structures analogous to Inception/VGG-style “complex layers.”  
- Suggested experiment: train a single thin-and-tall highway jointly on MNIST and CIFAR with a top fork to probe shared gating, parameter reuse, and gate activity patterns.  
- Strengths: simple elegant idea, strong optimization across depth, solid empirical comparisons, and insightful diagnostic analyses.  
- Weaknesses: evaluation limited to small datasets with modest augmentation; lacks large-scale (ImageNet) experiments to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion on relation to composite-layer architectures and explore large-scale or multi-task evaluations.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—recommend acceptance with encouragement to extend evaluation to ImageNet-scale data and to run the proposed multi-task experiment.
