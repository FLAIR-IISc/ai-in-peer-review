Summary: Proposes highway networks with adaptive transform and carry gates (LSTM-inspired) that create information highways enabling training of very deep feedforward models, validated on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 with layer-behavior analysis.

Strength — Significance & Novelty: Adapts gating to feedforward nets to address a central challenge (training unprecedented depth) in an elegant and original way.

Strength — Empirical Validation: Controlled experiments show highway networks scale to hundreds of layers and outperform plain networks on image tasks without sacrificing generalization.

Strength — Analysis & Insight: Layer lesioning and gate-activation visualizations provide compelling evidence of data-dependent routing and differing effective depths for tasks of varying complexity.

Strength — Presentation: Paper is clearly written, well organized, and accessible.

Weakness/Question — Baseline Comparison: Lacks discussion/comparison to a simpler identity-like weight initialization baseline that could mimic carry behavior; needs clarification of advantages of learned gating.

Weakness/Question — Gate Activity Interpretation: Requests precise clarification of what “active on average” means for transform gates in Figure 2 (thresholds or quantitative interpretation).

Conclusion: High-quality, impactful, and well-supported work with minor clarifications requested; strongly recommended for acceptance.
