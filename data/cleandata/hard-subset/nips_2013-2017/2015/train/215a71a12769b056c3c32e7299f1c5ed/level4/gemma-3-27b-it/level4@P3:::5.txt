This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward neural networks. Inspired by the gating mechanisms of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks, highway networks employ “information highways” – adaptive gating units – to allow for unimpeded information flow across many layers. The authors demonstrate that these networks can be trained directly with stochastic gradient descent, even with hundreds of layers, a significant achievement given the difficulties encountered when training very deep “plain” networks.

The work builds upon a strong foundation of prior research addressing the challenges of training deep networks. The authors appropriately cite key papers on optimization techniques (e.g., [13-15]), initialization strategies ([16, 17]), and activation functions ([20, 21]) designed to mitigate vanishing gradients. They also acknowledge related work utilizing skip connections ([22-24]) and knowledge distillation ([25, 26]). The connection to LSTM ([29, 30]) is clearly articulated, and the inspiration is well-motivated. This paper distinguishes itself from prior efforts by proposing a fundamentally different architectural approach – adaptive gating within each layer – rather than focusing solely on optimization or initialization schemes. The authors also correctly position their work in relation to recent advances like FitNets [25] and address the limitations of those approaches.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture presents a fresh and compelling approach to overcoming the training difficulties associated with very deep networks. The LSTM-inspired gating mechanism is an elegant solution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation of the highway network (Equation 3) is clear and concise. The initialization scheme proposed, biasing the transform gates towards carrying information initially, is insightful and well-justified by the LSTM analogy.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks. The comparison to plain networks and FitNets convincingly shows the benefits of the proposed architecture. The ablation study and analysis of layer importance (Figure 4) are particularly valuable.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The notation is consistent, and the figures are informative.
*   **Significance:** The ability to train extremely deep networks opens up new possibilities for exploring more complex models and potentially achieving improved performance on challenging tasks.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Exploration of Architectural Variations:** While the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the basic highway network structure, further investigation into different configurations of the transform and carry gates (beyond setting C = 1-T) might be beneficial.
*   **Computational Cost:** The addition of the gating mechanism introduces extra parameters, potentially increasing the computational cost compared to plain networks. While the paper demonstrates that highway networks can be trained efficiently, a more detailed analysis of the computational overhead would be useful.
*   **ImageNet Scale:** While experiments on CIFAR datasets are comprehensive, the results on the larger and more challenging ImageNet dataset are missing. Demonstrating the scalability of highway networks to ImageNet would further strengthen the paper.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to training very deep networks.
*   The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods.
*   The work has the potential to significantly impact the field of deep learning by enabling the exploration of more complex models.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed architecture and experimental results.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of results on ImageNet limits the generalizability of the findings.
*   The paper could benefit from a more thorough investigation of different architectural variations.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is a clever and effective solution to the challenges of training very deep networks. While the lack of ImageNet results and limited exploration of architectural variations are minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns. I recommend accepting this paper. The reviewers suggestion of training a single network on both MNIST and CIFAR is insightful and would be a valuable addition, but not essential for acceptance.
