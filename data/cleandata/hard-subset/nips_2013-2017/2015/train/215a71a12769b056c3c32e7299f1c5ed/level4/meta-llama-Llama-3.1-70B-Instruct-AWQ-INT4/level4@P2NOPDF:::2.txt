This paper presents a novel architecture for very deep networks, enabling the input to be propagated unchanged through a layer using input-dependent gates. This innovation facilitates the training of significantly deeper networks than would be feasible with a standard feed-forward architecture.

The authors conduct a thorough and insightful analysis of the trained networks, examining how the gates select inputs across different layers. This investigation is particularly noteworthy, as it sheds light on the inner workings of the proposed architecture.

The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and original. Although it shares some similarities with recent works, such as FitNets and LSTMs, the authors effectively highlight the differences and provide relevant comparisons where applicable. The results achieved are promising, demonstrating the potential of the proposed technique.

I have no suggested improvements for the paper. However, I did notice a minor error: the text on page 5 refers to "Tables 3 and 3," which appears to be a typo.

Overall, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel and achieves good results. The analysis is interesting and thorough, providing valuable insights into the behavior of the deep networks.
