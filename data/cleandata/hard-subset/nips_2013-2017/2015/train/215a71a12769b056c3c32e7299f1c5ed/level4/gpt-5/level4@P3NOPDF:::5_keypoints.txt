- Proposes simple LSTM-inspired transform and carry gates enabling successful training of very deep feed-forward “highway” networks.  
- Clear motivation, solid analysis, and convincing initial experiments showing mitigation of depth-related optimization problems.  
- Conceptual contribution: gates let the network dynamically allocate effective depth without brittle initialization or complex optimization.  
- Exposition is clear and empirical results on MNIST and CIFAR support the main claims.  
- Alternative perspective: view deep networks as “macro-layers” (sub-layer stacks) similar to Inception/VGG; highway layers interpolate between identity and learned transformations.  
- Suggested experiment: train a thin-and-tall shared trunk with task-specific heads for MNIST and CIFAR to analyze task-dependent gate activations, depth usage, and parameter repurposing.  
- Specific analysis questions for the suggested experiment: do gate activations/effective depths differ by task, are MNIST-underutilized parameters used for CIFAR, and how efficient is sharing vs separate models?  
- Recommend evaluation on larger-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to test scalability and training advantages with more data and capacity.  
- Minor suggestions: provide more intuition/evidence on gate behavior across depth, initialization, training evolution, and regularization sensitivity.  
- Run ablations separating transform and carry gate roles and compare to deep rectifier networks with careful initialization.  
- Report qualitative diagnostics (e.g., per-layer and per-input gate value distributions) to support claims about gradient flow, adaptive computation, and effective depth.  
- Overall assessment: timely, impactful, clearly presented idea with solid analysis and promising experiments; additional experiments would strengthen generality and practical value.
