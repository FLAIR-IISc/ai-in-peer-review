This paper introduces a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep neural networks. The core mechanism involves input-dependent gates that can allow information to be "carried" through a layer unchanged, effectively creating an adaptive identity path. This approach directly addresses the challenges associated with training extremely deep networks using standard feed-forward architectures, enabling models of significantly greater depth to be trained successfully.

The quality of the paper is high. The exposition is clear, the motivation is well-established, and the proposed method is original. While there are conceptual similarities to other recent work, notably LSTMs and FitNets, the authors do an excellent job of situating their contribution within the existing literature. They clearly articulate the key differences from these related methods and provide appropriate empirical comparisons, strengthening the case for their approach.

A particularly strong aspect of this work is the insightful analysis of the trained networks' behavior. The authors investigate how the gates operate at different network depths, revealing the manner in which they selectively transform or pass through their inputs. This analysis moves beyond simply reporting performance metrics and offers a valuable understanding of the underlying mechanics of the proposed architecture, which is a commendable and crucial component of the paper.

The empirical results are promising and effectively demonstrate the benefits of the proposed technique. The architecture achieves strong performance, validating its capability to train deeper models than was previously feasible.

In summary, this is a very solid paper that presents a novel, well-motivated, and effective technique. The analysis is thorough and insightful, and the results are compelling. The work represents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning.

I have no suggested improvements. The paper is ready for publication.
