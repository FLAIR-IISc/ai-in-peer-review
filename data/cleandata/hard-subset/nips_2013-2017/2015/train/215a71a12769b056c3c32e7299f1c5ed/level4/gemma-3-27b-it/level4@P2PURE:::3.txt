This paper introduces a novel approach to training very deep neural networks using Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) units.
This is a strong, well-written contribution. The core idea is original, and the experimental results are compelling.
A common challenge in machine learning is that performance often improves with network depth, but this is hindered by difficulties in initialization and the problems of vanishing or exploding gradients. These issues arguably limited the practical application of deep neural networks for decades. This work directly tackles this problem, demonstrating a method capable of successfully training networks with 100 layers.
The experiments illustrating information routing and layer importance are particularly insightful.
Specific comments:
In Section 4.1, the statement that “most transform blocks are active on average” appears to be inaccurate and should be revised to “not active on average.”
Initializing each layer of a very deep network with an identity matrix (potentially with added noise) represents a simple baseline. It would be beneficial to discuss how the transform gates in this architecture differentiate it from such a scheme and contribute to its success, especially in relation to highway networks. Overall, this is an excellent paper that convincingly demonstrates a method for training very deep neural networks, and it was a pleasure to review.
