- Proposes "highway networks," a gated architecture that lets inputs be carried unchanged, enabling much deeper networks to be trained.  
- Gates are input-dependent, allowing layers to selectively carry or transform information.  
- Thorough analysis of trained networks revealing how gates select inputs across layers.  
- Clear, well-written, and original presentation with explanations of differences from FitNets and LSTMs.  
- Experimental results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 show highway networks train directly with SGD and achieve competitive performance.  
- Promising technique that significantly contributes to deep learning architectures.
