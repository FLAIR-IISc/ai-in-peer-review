- Introduces a novel gating mechanism inspired by LSTMs to train extremely deep networks, addressing vanishing/exploding gradients and poor information flow.  
- Core idea is original, relevant, and technically well-executed.  
- Presentation is clear, rigorous, and logically organized from problem to evaluation.  
- Empirical results convincingly show the method can train networks exceeding 100 layers.  
- Authors contextualize the work against historical initialization and gradient-instability challenges.  
- Transform gates selectively route information across layers, directly tackling scaling barriers.  
- Layer-wise routing analysis and quantification of layer importance provide strong interpretability and validation.  
- Ablation studies adequately support the claimed advantages of the approach.  
- Writing is precise, concise, and free of unnecessary jargon, enhancing readability.  
- Improvement 1: Correct Section 4.1 to state that “most transform blocks are not active on average,” reflecting that many gates pass information unchanged.  
- Improvement 2: Briefly compare transform gates to simple identity initialization (with noise) and clarify how gating enables adaptive rather than static information flow.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, high-impact paper that substantially advances training of very deep architectures and is highly recommended for publication.
