- Proposes a novel architecture that lets the input be "carried" unchanged through a layer using input-dependent gates.  
- Enables training of much deeper networks than standard feedforward architectures.  
- Presents a thorough analysis of trained networks, showing how gates select inputs across different layers.  
- The analysis provides valuable insights into the architecture's inner workings.  
- Paper is exceptionally high quality, clearly written, and original.  
- Notes similarities to FitNets and LSTMs but clearly explains differences and includes relevant comparisons.  
- Experimental results are promising and demonstrate the approach's potential.  
- Minor correction needed: paper refers to "Tables 3 and 3" on page 5.  
- Overall assessment: a solid, novel technique that achieves good results and is a valuable contribution.
