This paper introduces a novel architecture, called highway networks, designed to facilitate the training of very deep neural networks. The authors draw inspiration from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks and propose the use of adaptive gating units to regulate information flow across layers. This approach enables the creation of "information highways" that allow for unimpeded information flow, making it possible to train extremely deep networks directly using stochastic gradient descent (SGD).

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed architecture, including a detailed description of the highway network layers, the training process, and the experimental results. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by training highway networks with hundreds of layers and achieving competitive performance on several benchmark datasets, including MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-supported argument for the effectiveness of their approach. The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the benefits and limitations of their method. One potential area for improvement is the analysis section, which could benefit from a more varied set of illustrations and details to further support the authors' claims.

Clarity: The paper is well-written, and the main messages are clearly conveyed. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of training deep neural networks and effectively motivate the need for their proposed solution. The technical details are well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of their architecture and training process.

Originality: The proposed method is sufficiently original, although it builds upon previous neural network techniques. The authors effectively demonstrate how their approach addresses the limitations of existing methods and provides a novel solution to the problem of training very deep neural networks.

Significance: The paper is significant, as it addresses a critical challenge in the field of deep learning. The ability to train very deep neural networks has important implications for a wide range of applications, and the authors' proposed solution has the potential to make a substantial impact.
