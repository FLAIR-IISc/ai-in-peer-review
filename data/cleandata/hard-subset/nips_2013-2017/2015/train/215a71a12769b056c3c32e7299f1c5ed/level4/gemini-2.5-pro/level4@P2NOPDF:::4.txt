This paper introduces an effective method for training very deep neural networks by means of 'information highways'—direct, unimpeded connections that allow information to flow across many layers. This approach is presented as a generalization of prior techniques, such as cross-layer connections, but with a novel formulation that proves highly effective. The authors provide strong empirical evidence to support their claims, demonstrating that this method enables the training of networks with hundreds of layers and leads to improved classification accuracy on standard benchmarks. Overall, the contributions are novel, well-motivated, and convincingly supported by the experimental results.

**Quality:** The technical quality of the paper is high. The core idea is novel, and the experimental validation is solid and convincing. A potential area for improvement is the analysis section, which could be further strengthened by including a greater variety of details or illustrations. For instance, further ablation studies or visualizations of the information flow could offer deeper insights into why the proposed architecture is so effective.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The main contributions are articulated with excellent clarity, and the proposed method is explained with sufficient detail for the work to be understood and reproduced.

**Originality:** While the concept of connecting non-adjacent layers builds on previous work, the proposed method is a thoughtful generalization of these techniques. The specific formulation of gated, data-dependent information flow represents a sufficiently original and valuable contribution to the field.

**Significance:** This work is highly significant as it addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning: the difficulty of training increasingly deep neural networks. By providing a practical and effective solution to this problem, the paper paves the way for developing and applying much deeper architectures, which is crucial for advancing the state-of-the-art in various application domains.
