This paper presents an elegant and simple idea, inspired by the LSTM gradient trapping mechanism, that enables the training of very deep feedforward networks, accompanied by solid analysis. I had independently considered a similar approach but had not yet begun implementation; I congratulate the authors on being the first to pursue this direction.

An alternative perspective worth considering is that a very deep network can be viewed as a shallower network comprised of layers containing internal sub-layers. These sub-layers exhibit more complex non-linear behavior than traditional rectified linear or sigmoid units. This interpretation aligns with architectures like GoogLeNet’s inception modules and the stacked 3x3 convolutions in VGG, and may also be a valid way to conceptualize highway networks.

I suggest an experiment to further investigate the network’s capacity for knowledge transfer. The authors could train a single, “thin and tall” network on both MNIST and CIFAR-10, forking the network at the input layer to handle the two domains. I am particularly interested in observing whether, at inference time, this shared network exhibits similar patterns to networks trained separately for each domain, and whether model parameters underutilized during MNIST training are leveraged for CIFAR-10. Understanding how sharing occurs within such a deep hierarchy, and its efficiency, would be valuable.

I have no major criticisms. I encourage the authors to evaluate the network’s performance on larger datasets such as ImageNet.
