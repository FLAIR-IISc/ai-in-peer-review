- Proposes a novel "highway networks" architecture using "information highways" to train very deep stacked layers.
- Draws inspiration from LSTM networks and employs adaptive gating units to regulate information flow.
- Enables training extremely deep, efficient architectures directly with simple gradient descent.
- Thoroughly analyzes challenges of propagating activations and gradients through many layers.
- Argues highway networks overcome information/gradient propagation difficulties by allowing unimpeded flow across layers.
- Experimental evaluation is solid and results are clearly presented.
- Paper quality is high, with clear, concise, and accessible writing.
- Analysis sections could use more variety in details and additional illustrations.
- Method is sufficiently novel but builds upon existing neural network techniques.
- Contribution is significant with potential lasting impact on deep learning research.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, strong experiments, clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited variety in analysis and reliance on prior techniques.
