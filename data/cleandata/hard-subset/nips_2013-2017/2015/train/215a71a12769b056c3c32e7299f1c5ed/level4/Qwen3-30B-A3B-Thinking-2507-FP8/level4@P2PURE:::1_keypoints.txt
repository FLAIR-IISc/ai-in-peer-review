- Strength: Paper is well-motivated, clearly presented, with reasonable experiments; analysis of learned networks via gate output patterns is a notable strength.

- Concern 1: Gate T uses a sigmoid so outputs never reach 0 or 1, making the gradients in Eq. (5) mathematically inconsistent; authors must clarify how backpropagation is implemented.

- Concern 2: Initialization strategy of using constant negative biases tied to depth is unsatisfying; include a plot of performance vs initial bias since the bias acts as a regularizer encouraging "longer highways."

- Concern 3: The phrase "report by recent studies in similar experimental settings" is ambiguous; authors must explicitly state experimental differences to justify direct comparisons.

- Concern 4: Reporting maximum accuracies is statistically inappropriate; report mean accuracy and standard deviation for each method and dataset.

- Suggestion 5: Include a learning-theory analysis explaining how gate units enable layers to act as identities and allow selective propagation through many layers.
