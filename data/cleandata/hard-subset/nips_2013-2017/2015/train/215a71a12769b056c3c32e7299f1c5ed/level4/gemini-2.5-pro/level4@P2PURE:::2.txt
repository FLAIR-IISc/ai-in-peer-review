This is a high-quality, clearly written, and original paper. The authors propose a novel architecture for very deep networks that utilizes input-dependent gates, enabling information to be carried unchanged through a layer. This mechanism makes it possible to train significantly deeper networks than is feasible with standard feed-forward architectures.

A particularly strong component of the paper is the analysis of the trained networks. The authors investigate how the gates select their inputs across different layers, providing valuable insight into the model's behavior. The paper also does an excellent job of positioning the proposed method relative to related work, such as LSTMs and FitNets, by clearly explaining the key differences and providing appropriate empirical comparisons.

Overall, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel, the analysis is interesting and thorough, and the results are promising. I have no suggested improvements.
