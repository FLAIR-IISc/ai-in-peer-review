This paper proposes a novel architecture for training very deep networks, enabling the input to be "carried" unchanged through a layer using gates dependent on the input itself. This architecture, called highway networks, allows for much deeper networks to be trained than would be possible with a standard feed-forward architecture.

The authors provide a clear and thorough analysis of the trained networks, determining the manner in which the gates select their inputs in different layers of the network. This analysis is particularly interesting and provides valuable insights into the behavior of the proposed architecture.

The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and original. Although there is some similarity to other recent work, such as FitNets and LSTMs, the differences are clearly explained, and comparisons are made where appropriate. The authors achieve promising results, demonstrating the effectiveness of their proposed architecture.

The paper is well-organized, and the writing is concise and easy to follow. The authors provide sufficient background information and relate their work to previous research in the field, making it easy for readers to understand the context and significance of their contributions.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity. The authors explain complex concepts in a clear and concise manner, making it easy for readers to follow their arguments. The paper is also well-structured, with each section building on the previous one to provide a comprehensive understanding of the proposed architecture.

In terms of originality, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed architecture is novel and addresses a significant problem in training very deep networks. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of their proposed architecture, which is not typically seen in other papers.

The results presented in the paper are promising and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed architecture. The authors compare their results to other state-of-the-art methods and show that their architecture can achieve competitive performance.

One potential criticism of the paper is the lack of a more detailed comparison to other methods. While the authors do compare their results to FitNets and LSTMs, a more detailed analysis of the differences between these methods and the proposed architecture would be beneficial.

However, overall, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel and achieves good results. The analysis is interesting and thorough, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel architecture for training very deep networks.
* The authors provide a clear and thorough analysis of the trained networks.
* The paper is well-organized, and the writing is concise and easy to follow.
* The authors achieve promising results, demonstrating the effectiveness of their proposed architecture.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison to other methods.
* Some readers may find the analysis of the trained networks to be too detailed or technical.

Rating: 9/10

Recommendation: Accept
