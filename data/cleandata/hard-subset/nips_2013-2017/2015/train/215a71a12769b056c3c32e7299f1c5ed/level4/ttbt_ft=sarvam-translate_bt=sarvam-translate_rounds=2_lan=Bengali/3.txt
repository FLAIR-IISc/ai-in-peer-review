This paper proposes using LSTM units as a method for deep network training.

It's a high-quality, well-written paper. The idea is fundamental, and the results are quite interesting.

Machine learning researchers often observe that they get better results by adding more layers to their neural networks, but the difficulty of getting started and decay/blown gradients were severely limited. In fact, the difficulties of feeding information through deep neural networks probably kept them away from widespread use for 30 years. This paper directly solves this problem and demonstrates a method for 100-layer net training.

In particular, the tests showing the importance of routing information and layers are excellent.

Specific comments:

It seemed that in section 4.1, the phrase "most transform blocks are active on average" should be changed to "are not active on average."

Each layer of a very deep network can be initialized with an identity matrix (possibly with a slight noise). It might be worth commenting on how transform gates distinguish highway networks from this simple scheme. It's a great paper, demonstrating a method for training very deep neural networks, and it was a pleasure to read.
