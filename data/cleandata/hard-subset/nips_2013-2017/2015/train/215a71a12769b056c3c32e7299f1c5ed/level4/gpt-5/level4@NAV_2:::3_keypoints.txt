- Introduces an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism to enable training of very deep networks.  
- Strong, clearly written paper with an original central idea and compelling empirical results.  
- Directly tackles initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients to train networks with ~100 layers.  
- Experiments on data-dependent routing of information and on layer importance are particularly striking.  
- Correction needed in Sec. 4.1: phrase “most transform blocks are active on average” appears reversed—should be “not active on average.”  
- Suggestion: comment on how transform gates differ from or improve upon simple identity-plus-noise layer initialization.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, practical method and a pleasure to read.
