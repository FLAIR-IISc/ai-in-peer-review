1) Overall: idea is clear, experiments convincing, and the analysis of gate output patterns is a strength.

2) Sigmoid gates never produce exact 0/1, so Equation 5’s gradients are incorrect — the paper must detail how backpropagation is actually implemented.

3) Initialization: using constant negative biases based on depth is insufficiently justified — include a plot of performance vs initial bias and note that bias acts as a regularizer favoring “longer highways.”

4) Comparison wording: clarify whether cited recent studies use the same experimental setup; explicitly state any differences that prevent direct comparison.

5) Reporting metrics: reporting “max” accuracies is statistically questionable — report mean and standard deviation for each method instead.

6) Theory: investigate the approach from a learning-theory perspective — gate units enable layers to act as input-dependent identities, allowing propagation through many selective layers.
