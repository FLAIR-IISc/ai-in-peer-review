- Proposes "highway networks" with direct across-layer connections (information highways) to facilitate information flow in very deep nets.  
- Introduces adaptive gating, inspired by LSTMs, to control information routing and mitigate vanishing gradients.  
- Experimental results show improved classification accuracy over plain networks.  
- Experimental validation is thorough and supports the paper’s claims.  
- Analysis sections exist but require more elaboration and additional visualizations to better communicate insights.  
- Paper is well-written with clear notation, equations, and logical organization.  
- Though related to skip connections, the gating mechanism is a significant and original contribution.  
- Addresses the important problem of training very deep networks directly, enabling networks with hundreds of layers.  
- Provides useful insights into layer importance and information routing behavior.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, strong empirical results, clear presentation, and significant potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: analysis could be expanded and experiments are mostly on standard datasets; needs evaluation on more complex tasks.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; recommend acceptance.
