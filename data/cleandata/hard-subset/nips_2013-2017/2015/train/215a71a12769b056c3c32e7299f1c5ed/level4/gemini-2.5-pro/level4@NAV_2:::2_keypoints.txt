- Proposes "highway networks," a novel feed-forward architecture using LSTM-like adaptive gates to create information highways across many layers.
- Gates dynamically regulate information flow, addressing optimization issues like poor credit assignment and vanishing gradients in very deep plain networks.
- Empirical validation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 shows highway networks avoid performance degradation as depth increases versus plain networks.
- Section 3.1 optimization experiments provide strong controlled evidence that depth scaling is feasible with highway networks.
- Section 4 analysis visualizes transform gate biases/activities and includes a lesion study, offering deep insight into layer utility and internal dynamics.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with a concise formal description and effective figures that aid understanding.
- Original contribution: learns data-dependent bypassing via gates for feed-forward vision models, distinct from LSTM gating and skip connections.
- Significant impact: enables direct SGD training of networks with hundreds of layers, opening new avenues for deep representation learning.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel elegant architecture, thorough empirical evidence, insightful internal analysis, and high clarity.
- Arguments against acceptance: reviewer lists no strong objections.
- Minor correction: caption/labeling typo on page 5 where two tables are both labeled "Table 3."
