- Proposes "highway networks" inspired by LSTM that use adaptive gating to enable unimpeded information flow and train very deep nets with simple gradient descent.
- Provides a thorough analysis and situates the approach relative to prior work on training deep networks.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, training networks over 100 layers and outperforming traditional deep nets.
- Experiments are well-designed and comprehensive, covering image classification and digit recognition tasks.
- Results are impressive, showing state-of-the-art performance on several benchmarks.
- Paper is clear, well-structured, and easy to follow, with good separation of theory, experiments, and analysis.
- Does not provide direct comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods (e.g., residual connections, batch normalization).
- Lacks a clear explanation or evidence of how the approach generalizes to other tasks or domains.
- Strong contribution overall: novel, effective method with thorough analysis—argument for acceptance.
- Main arguments against acceptance: missing comparisons to competing modern methods and limited cross-domain validation.
- Recommended improvements: add direct comparisons to state-of-the-art alternatives, include more cross-domain experiments, and clarify applicability to other tasks.
