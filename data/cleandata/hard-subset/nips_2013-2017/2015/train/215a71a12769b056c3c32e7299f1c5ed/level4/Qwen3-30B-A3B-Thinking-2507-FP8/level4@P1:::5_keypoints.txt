- Introduces highway networks with transform and carry gates enabling direct training of extremely deep feedforward networks.  
- Gates allow smooth transition between identity mappings and transformed representations, inspired by LSTM.  
- Enables training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard SGD without special initialization or two-stage training.  
- Highway networks maintain strong performance as depth increases, unlike plain networks whose performance degrades.  
- Thorough experimental validation on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 shows improved training stability and final performance, including 100-layer models.  
- Core idea is elegant, simple, and accessible while addressing a major deep learning challenge.  
- Analysis of learned gating mechanisms gives insight into dynamic input-dependent information routing.  
- Clear connection to prior work (LSTM, Fitnets), with compelling comparison showing simpler single-stage training.  
- Main weakness: limited exploration of relationships to other architectures improving information flow (e.g., Inception, VGG).  
- Reviewer suggests investigating parameter sharing across tasks (e.g., joint MNIST and CIFAR-10 training) to study depth-wise routing and reuse.  
- Paper is well-written, reproducible, and the reviewer recommends acceptance, encouraging experiments on larger benchmarks like ImageNet.
