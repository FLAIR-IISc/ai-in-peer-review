- Proposes "Highway Networks": feedforward layers with learned gates (inspired by LSTM) that let layers transform or carry inputs, enabling very deep training.  
- Strong empirical evidence: highway networks train with SGD to depths up to 100 and outperform/deploy competitively on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Direct comparison shows highway nets match or surpass FitNets without multi-stage hint-based training.  
- Analysis shows gates perform data-dependent routing, indicating non-trivial computational role.  
- Lesion study indicates automatic depth adaptation: networks use more layers for harder tasks (CIFAR-100) and leave layers idle for simpler tasks (MNIST).  
- High significance and originality: simple, principled solution to deep network optimization distinct from pre-training or fixed skip connections.  
- Experiments are thorough and convincing, with clear optimization and dataset evaluations.  
- Insightful analyses and visualizations (gate activities, lesion study) provide understanding beyond performance metrics.  
- Exceptional clarity and well-organized presentation with a clear connection to LSTMs.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to transform-gate bias initialization (bT) — needs more detailed ablation on initialization effects.  
- Weakness: lacks a direct empirical comparison to fixed skip/identity connections to quantify the benefit of adaptive gating.  
- Weakness: no detailed discussion of computational overhead (FLOPs/parameters) introduced by gates.  
- Minor: typo in Section 3.3.2 heading referencing "Table 3".  
- Conclusion: strong, impactful paper recommended for acceptance; likely to inspire follow-up research.
