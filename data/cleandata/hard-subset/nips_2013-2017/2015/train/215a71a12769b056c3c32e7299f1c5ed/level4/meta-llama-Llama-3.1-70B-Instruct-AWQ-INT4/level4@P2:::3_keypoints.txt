- Proposes LSTM-inspired highway networks using adaptive gating to train very deep feedforward networks.
- Targets the long-standing difficulties of initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients in deep nets.
- Original idea: novel combination of LSTM-style gates with feedforward architectures.
- Demonstrates direct training of 100-layer networks using SGD.
- Well-designed experiments that illustrate information routing and layer importance.
- Shows transform gates route information in an input-dependent way.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible to expert readers.
- Results are technically sound and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.
- Provides sufficient detail for reproducibility for expert readers.
- Lacks detailed comparison with other methods for training very deep networks.
- Does not present a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of the approach.
- Specific correction: Section 4.1 should state transform blocks are "not active on average."
- Suggests commenting on how transform gates differ from simple identity-initialized layers with noise.
- Overall recommendation for acceptance based on originality, experimental results, and clarity.
