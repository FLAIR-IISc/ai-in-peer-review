2. Gate activation/gradients: T uses sigmoid so outputs are never exactly 0/1, making the gradients in Eq.5 incorrect; the paper must state how backpropagation is actually performed.

3. Initialization concerns: using constant negative biases dependent on depth is unsatisfactory—include a plot of performance vs initial bias, since bias acts like a regularizer favoring “longer highways.”

4. Comparison clarity: the phrase “report by recent studies in similar experimental settings” is vague—clarify differences in experimental setups or ensure direct comparability.

5. Reporting statistics: reporting “max” accuracies is unsound—report mean accuracy and standard deviation for every method.

6. Theory direction: recommend a learning-theoretic study, as input-dependent gates enable selective propagation through many layers and merit theoretical analysis.
