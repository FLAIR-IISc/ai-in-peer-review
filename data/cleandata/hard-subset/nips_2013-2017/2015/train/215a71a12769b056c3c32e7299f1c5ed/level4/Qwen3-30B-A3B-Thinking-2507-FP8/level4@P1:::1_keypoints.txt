- Introduces highway networks with transform and carry gates (inspired by LSTM) to enable direct training of extremely deep feedforward networks.  
- Gates create adaptive "information highways" that allow activations to propagate without attenuation.  
- Empirically show highway networks trainable at 100+ layers and achieve strong results on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100.  
- Highway networks outperform or match shallower plain networks without requiring two-stage distillation.  
- Analysis of gate behavior reveals input-dependent routing and interpretable layer-wise contributions.  
- Architecture description and experimental design are clear, with attention to hyperparameter robustness.  
- Weakness: Equation (5) gradient calculation is incorrect or misleading; backpropagation behavior must be clarified and corrected.  
- Weakness: Bias initialization strategy (constant negative biases) is arbitrary and needs empirical justification (sensitivity analysis).  
- Weakness: Comparisons to recent studies (e.g., FitNets) are ambiguous; methodological differences must be explicitly stated.  
- Weakness: Reporting only max accuracy in tables is inappropriate; should report mean ± std. dev. across runs for reliability.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks theoretical grounding on how gating affects capacity or generalization.  
- Pros: Simple, effective solution to training very deep networks that enables >100-layer training without distillation.  
- Pros: Provides novel, interpretable insights into layer utilization and input-dependent routing with strong benchmark performance.  
- Cons: Technical oversight in gradient derivation, heuristic initialization, incomplete statistical reporting, and unclear baseline comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: Significant, original, and influential contribution with fixable issues; aligns with conference criteria.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
