This paper proposes a novel architecture for training very deep networks, allowing input signals to be carried unchanged through layers via gates dependent on the input itself. This mechanism facilitates the training of significantly deeper networks than would be feasible with standard feedforward architectures. The authors provide a compelling analysis of the trained networks, investigating how the gates select inputs at different layers – precisely the type of investigation I would expect and appreciate. 

The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and presents original work. While natural similarities exist to recent related work, such as FitNets and LSTMs, the authors clearly articulate the differences and provide appropriate comparisons. The results achieved are promising. 

I have no suggested improvements. A minor note: there appears to be a repetition in the table references on page 5 ("Tables 3 and 3").

Overall, this is a solid paper. The proposed technique is novel and demonstrably effective, and the accompanying analysis is both interesting and thorough.
