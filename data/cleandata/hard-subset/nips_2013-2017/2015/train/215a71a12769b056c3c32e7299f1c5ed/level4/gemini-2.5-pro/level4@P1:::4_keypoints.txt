- Proposes "highway networks," using adaptive, LSTM-like gates to route information and ease training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Gates allow layers to either transform inputs or carry them through unchanged, enabling data-dependent routing.  
- Demonstrates direct training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard SGD, overcoming plain-network optimization issues.  
- Strong empirical results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 showing stable training and competitive or superior performance to methods like FitNets.  
- Technical approach is sound and claims are well-supported by thorough optimization and performance experiments.  
- Section 4 analysis (gate visualizations and lesioning) provides useful insights into layer utilization and behavior.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized, with effective motivation, precise formulation, and informative figures.  
- Original contribution in translating LSTM gating to feedforward networks, introducing learned, dynamic skip connections.  
- Highly significant: enables practical training of extremely deep networks and likely to influence future architecture design.  
- Strong case for acceptance: addresses a central deep learning problem with a simple, effective, and well-validated method.  
- Minor critique: analysis of learned routing behavior could be deeper and include more varied illustrations.
