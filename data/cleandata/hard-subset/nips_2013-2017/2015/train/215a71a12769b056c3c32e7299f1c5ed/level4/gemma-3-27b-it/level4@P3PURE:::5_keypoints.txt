- Proposes an LSTM-inspired method that enables training of very deep feedforward networks; idea is simple, elegant, and backed by solid analysis.  
- Reviewer congratulates authors for being first to successfully explore leveraging LSTM gradient mechanisms in this setting.  
- Alternative perspective: view very deep networks as sequences of not-so-deep modules (sub-layers), akin to Inception or stacked 3x3 VGG blocks, which might also explain highway networks.  
- Suggests an experiment: train a single thin-tall network forked at the input to handle both MNIST and CIFAR to study cross-domain parameter utilization.  
- Specifically interested in whether parameters underutilized for MNIST become effectively used for CIFAR and how sharing occurs in a deep hierarchical structure.  
- No major complaints; recommends evaluation on larger datasets such as ImageNet.
