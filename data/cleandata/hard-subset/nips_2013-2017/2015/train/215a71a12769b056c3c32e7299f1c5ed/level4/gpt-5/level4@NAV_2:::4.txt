The paper introduces a method for training neural networks with many stacked layers. The “information highway” concept is supported by experiments, and the authors demonstrate that this approach can improve classification accuracy.

Quality: the core idea appears novel, and the experimental evaluation is solid. The analysis sections could benefit from a wider variety of details or illustrations.

Clarity: the paper communicates its main points effectively and is generally clear.

Originality: the proposed approach is sufficiently original, though it extends prior neural network techniques.

Significance: the work is impactful for the field, as training increasingly deep networks is key to achieving superior results in practical applications.

The paper presents an effective technique for training very deep networks using “information highways,” i.e., direct pathways to higher layers. Although it generalizes earlier ideas such as cross-layer connections, the authors show its effectiveness through experiments. The contributions are quite novel and well supported by empirical evidence.
