This research paper proposes a generalization of training multi-layer neural networks. The concept of information highways is well illustrated through experiments. Furthermore, the authors demonstrate that this method can improve classification accuracy results.

**Quality:** The idea seems novel, and the experiments are solid. The analysis sections could use a little more variety in details or illustrations.

**Clarity:** The main messages of the research paper are well-stated and quite clear.

**Originality:** The proposed method is sufficiently original, although it is a generalization of previous neural network techniques.

**Importance:** The research paper demonstrates importance for the field as deeper and deeper neural networks need to be trained to achieve better results in application areas.

This research paper describes an effective method for training very deep neural networks by creating direct connections from 'information highways' or upper network layers. Although it is a generalization of previous techniques like cross-layer connections, the authors have shown this method to be effective through experiments. The contributions are quite new and well-supported by experimental evidence.
