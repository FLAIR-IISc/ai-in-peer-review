This paper proposes an innovative architecture for very deep networks, allowing input to be "carried" unchanged through a layer using gates that depend on the input itself. This design enables the training of much deeper networks than would be possible with a standard feed-forward architecture.

The authors conduct a fascinating analysis of the trained networks to determine how the gates select their inputs in different layers of the network. This analysis is precisely what one would hope to see in a paper of this nature.

The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and original. Although it shares some similarities with other recent work (notably FitNets and LSTMs), the authors clearly explain the differences and provide comparisons where appropriate. The results achieved are promising.

I have no suggested improvements. However, I did notice a minor error: the paper refers to "Tables 3 and 3" on page 5, which appears to be a duplication.

Overall, this is a very solid paper. The proposed technique is novel and achieves good results. The analysis is interesting and thorough.
