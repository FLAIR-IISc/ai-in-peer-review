This paper introduces a novel architecture for training very deep neural networks, utilizing input-dependent gates to allow information to propagate directly through layers. 
This gating mechanism facilitates the successful training of networks with significantly greater depth than is achievable with traditional feedforward approaches.
A particularly strong aspect of this work is the insightful analysis of the trained network’s gating behavior across different layers – precisely the kind of investigation needed to understand the mechanism.
The paper is of a high standard, exhibiting clarity of presentation and originality.
While acknowledging related work, such as FitNets and LSTMs, the authors clearly delineate the differences and provide appropriate comparisons.
The results demonstrate the promise of the proposed technique.
I have no suggestions for improvement.
Please note a minor typographical issue: "Tables 3 and 3" (p5).
Overall, this is a strong and well-executed paper presenting a novel technique with compelling results and a thorough analysis.
