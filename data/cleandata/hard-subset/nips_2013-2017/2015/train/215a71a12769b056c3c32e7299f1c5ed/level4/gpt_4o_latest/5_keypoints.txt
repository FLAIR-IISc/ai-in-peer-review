- Reviewer considered using LSTM-inspired gradient trapping to build deeper networks but did not pursue it; praises authors for successfully implementing this approach.
- Proposes an alternative perspective: very deep networks can be seen as shallower networks whose layers contain sub-layers, producing richer non-linearities (similar to GoogLeNet, VGG, highway nets).
- Suggests an additional experiment: train a single thin tall network on both MNIST and CIFAR with a fork at the top to compare learned representations.
- Interested in whether parameters underutilized by MNIST become leveraged by CIFAR, shedding light on parameter sharing and efficiency in very deep hierarchical networks.
- Has no major criticisms of the paper and recommends testing on larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) to validate scalability and performance.
- Concludes the paper presents a simple, elegant LSTM-inspired method enabling training of very deep feedforward networks, with thorough and well-executed analysis.
