This paper proposes a novel architecture for very deep networks, enabling the input to be "carried" unchanged through a layer using gates that are dependent on the input itself. This innovation allows for the training of much deeper networks than would be possible with a standard feedforward architecture. The authors conduct a thorough and intriguing analysis of the trained networks, examining how the gates select their inputs across different layers, which is precisely the kind of insight that adds significant value to the paper. The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and original. Although it naturally shares some similarities with other recent work, notably FitNets and LSTMs, the authors clearly explain the differences and provide comparisons where appropriate. The results achieved are promising. I do not have any suggested improvements. However, I did notice a minor error: the text references "Tables 3 and 3" on page 5, which appears to be a typographical mistake. Overall, this is a very solid paper that presents a novel technique and achieves good results, accompanied by an interesting and thorough analysis.
