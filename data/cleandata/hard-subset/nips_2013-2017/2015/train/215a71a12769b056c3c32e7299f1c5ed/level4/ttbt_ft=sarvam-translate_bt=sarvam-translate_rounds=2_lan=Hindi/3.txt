This research paper proposes the use of LSTM units as a method for training very deep networks.

It is a high-quality, well-written paper. The idea is original, and the results are quite interesting.

Machine learning researchers often find that they achieve better results by adding more and more layers to their neural networks, but the difficulties of initialization and decay/explosive gradients have severely limited them. In fact, the difficulties of obtaining the flow of information through deep neural networks have kept them out of widespread use for 30 years. This paper directly addresses this problem and demonstrates a method for training 100-layer nets.

In particular, the experiments demonstrating information routing and layer importance are very good.

Specific comments:

It seems that in section 4.1, 'most transform blocks are average active' should be changed to 'are not average active'.

One can initialize each layer of a very deep network with an identity matrix (possibly with a small amount of noise). It may be worth noting how transform gates differ from highway networks from this simple scheme. This is a fantastic paper, demonstrating a method for training very deep neural networks, and it was a pleasure to read.
