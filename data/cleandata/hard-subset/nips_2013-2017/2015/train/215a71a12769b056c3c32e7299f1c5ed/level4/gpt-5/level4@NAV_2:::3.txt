This paper introduces an LSTM-inspired mechanism to enable the training of very deep networks.

This is a strong, clearly written paper. The central idea is original, and the empirical results are compelling.

Practitioners often observe improved performance by increasing network depth, but issues with initialization and vanishing/exploding gradients have been a major obstacle. Indeed, difficulty in propagating information through deep stacks likely limited their widespread use for decades. This work tackles the problem directly and demonstrates a practical approach to training networks with on the order of 100 layers.

The experiments on data-dependent routing of information and on layer importance are particularly striking.

Specific comments:
- In Section 4.1, “most transform blocks are active on average” appears to be the opposite of what the figures suggest; it seems it should read “not active on average.”
- One could initialize each layer in a very deep network to (near) identity (e.g., I plus small noise). It would be useful to comment on how the transform gates in highway networks differ from and improve upon this simple scheme.

Overall, this is an excellent paper that presents a practical method for training very deep neural networks, and it was a pleasure to read.
