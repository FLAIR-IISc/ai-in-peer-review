This paper introduces “Highway Networks,” a surprisingly simple yet effective architectural modification to deep feedforward networks inspired by LSTM gating mechanisms. The core idea – allowing information to flow more easily across layers via adaptive gating – is elegant and addresses a critical bottleneck in training very deep networks. The authors demonstrate, convincingly, that highway networks can be trained with standard SGD to depths far exceeding those achievable with plain networks, and without resorting to complex two-stage training procedures. This is a significant contribution, particularly given the recent emphasis on depth as a key factor in achieving state-of-the-art performance in many machine learning tasks.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation, method, and experimental results. The notation is consistent and the mathematical formulation of highway layers is concise. The experimental validation is comprehensive, demonstrating the effectiveness of highway networks on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 datasets. The ablation studies, specifically the layer importance analysis (Figure 4), are particularly insightful, revealing how different layers contribute to the final performance and demonstrating that the gating mechanism isn’t simply learning to pass information through unchanged. The inclusion of readily available code and hyperparameters further strengthens the paper.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture is a genuinely novel approach to addressing the training difficulties of deep feedforward networks. While inspired by LSTMs, the application to feedforward networks is non-trivial and insightful.
*   **Simplicity:** Despite its effectiveness, the highway network concept is remarkably simple to understand and implement. This ease of adoption is a significant advantage.
*   **Empirical Results:** The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate a clear advantage over plain networks, particularly at increasing depths. The performance on CIFAR datasets is competitive with more complex architectures.
*   **Analysis:** The analysis of layer importance and the visualization of gate activity provide valuable insights into the behavior of highway networks.
*   **Reproducibility:** Publicly available code and hyperparameters promote reproducibility and encourage further research.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Theoretical Justification:** While the empirical results are strong, the paper lacks a more in-depth theoretical analysis of *why* highway networks work so well. A deeper understanding of the gradient flow and optimization landscape would be beneficial.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions that the transform gate bias is a hyperparameter, but doesn’t thoroughly explore its sensitivity. A more systematic analysis of this parameter would be helpful.
*   **Comparison to other recent approaches:** While acknowledging FitNets, the comparison to other recent depth-related works (e.g., residual networks – though admittedly post-NIPS submission) is somewhat limited. A more thorough discussion of the advantages and disadvantages compared to these alternatives would be valuable.
*   **Limited Exploration of Gate Dynamics**: While the visualizations in Figure 2 provide some insight, a more extensive study of the gating mechanisms, potentially using information-theoretic measures, could reveal more about how information is routed.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a genuinely novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks.
*   The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods.
*   The simplicity of the approach makes it easily accessible and adaptable.
*   The provided analysis offers valuable insights into the behavior of highway networks.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The theoretical justification is somewhat lacking.
*   The hyperparameter sensitivity analysis is limited.
*   The comparison to other recent depth-related approaches could be more comprehensive.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that introduces a significant new contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is a clever and effective solution to a challenging problem. While some theoretical and comparative analysis could be strengthened, the novelty, simplicity, and compelling empirical results outweigh these minor shortcomings. I strongly recommend acceptance. The paper's potential impact on future research in deep learning is substantial.
