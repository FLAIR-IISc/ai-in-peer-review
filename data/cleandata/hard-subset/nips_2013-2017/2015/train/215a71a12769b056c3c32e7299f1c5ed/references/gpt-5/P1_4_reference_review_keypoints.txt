- Proposes Highway Networks: layers compute y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)) with learned transform gates and negative-bias initialization to create “information highways.”  
- Demonstrates easy optimization of very deep (up to 100-layer) fully connected highway nets on MNIST, unlike plain nets.  
- Shows convolutional highway nets achieve competitive or better results on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 compared to FitNets using single-stage SGD.  
- Analyses (gate activities, lesioning) indicate data-dependent routing and selective use of depth; early layers are critical and depth usage grows with dataset complexity.  
- Situates work relative to LSTM-inspired gating and prior deep-net techniques (initialization, optimization, skip connections, deep supervision, FitNets), arguing gates give dynamic input-dependent routing.  
- Empirical evidence is strong for optimization benefits and competitive generalization; analyses support main claims.  
- Fairness: MNIST FC comparisons control per-layer parameter count; CIFAR comparisons to FitNets are plausible but need clearer parameter/FLOP accounting since gates add cost.  
- Missing ablations: independent carry gate vs C = 1−T, bias magnitude and depth interaction, parameter-cost vs optimization effects, H replaced by identity, and comparisons to fixed shortcuts.  
- Paper mentions pilot results beyond 1000 layers but lacks quantitative evidence in main experiments.  
- Theoretical discussion is intuitive but limited; more analytical insight into gradient/Jacobian behavior would strengthen claims.  
- Writing and organization are generally good; figures are informative.  
- Notational consistency needs improvement (element-wise ops), and convolutional gating parameterization should be specified (spatial vs channel, parameter counts).  
- Experimental details mostly adequate but require exact parameter/FLOP counts, LR schedule, momentum, weight decay, and augmentation details for reproducibility.  
- Core idea (LSTM-like depth gating and negative-bias init) is novel, nontrivial, and well-situated among prior work.  
- Significance: simple, general mechanism addressing optimization barriers for very deep nets; likely impactful even if not SOTA with heavy augmentation; public code increases impact.  
- Pros: simple modification enabling very deep training with SGD; faster convergence; competitive CIFAR results without distillation; insightful analyses and code release.  
- Cons: lack of ablations disentangling capacity vs optimization, no justification for C = 1−T, missing baselines (fixed skips, deep supervision) and precise compute matching, limited theory, unsubstantiated >1000-layer claim.  
- Questions for authors include effects of independent carry gate, sensitivity to gate bias and learning rates, exact parameter/FLOP overhead of T, gate saturation/pruning potential, and interactions with regularization/normalization.  
- Minor comments: unify notation, fix typos, clarify conv gating granularity, and add pseudocode for highway layer.  
- Recommendation: Accept (strong) — novel, practical mechanism with clear empirical benefits and high reviewer confidence.
