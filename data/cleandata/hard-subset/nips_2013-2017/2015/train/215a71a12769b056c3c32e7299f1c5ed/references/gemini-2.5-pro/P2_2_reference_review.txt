### **Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"**

**Summary**

This paper introduces a novel neural network architecture, dubbed "Highway Networks," designed to facilitate the training of extremely deep feedforward models. The core problem the authors address is the difficulty of optimizing very deep "plain" networks, where the propagation of signals and gradients becomes problematic as the number of layers increases. The proposed solution is inspired by the gating mechanisms in Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks. Highway Networks introduce adaptive gating units that dynamically regulate the flow of information through the network. Specifically, each layer can learn to either transform its input (as in a conventional layer) or carry it forward unchanged. This creates "information highways" where data can flow unimpeded across many layers, effectively mitigating the optimization challenges associated with depth. The authors demonstrate empirically that Highway Networks with tens or even hundreds of layers can be trained directly with SGD, a feat not achievable with plain networks of similar depth. They validate their approach on MNIST and CIFAR datasets, showing that not only can these deep networks be optimized, but they also generalize well, achieving competitive performance without complex multi-stage training procedures like those required by prior work (e.g., FitNets).

---

### **Evaluation**

This is an excellent paper that presents a simple, elegant, and highly effective solution to a significant and well-known problem in deep learning. The work is well-motivated, clearly explained, and supported by a strong set of experiments and analysis.

**Quality: High**

The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by rigorous experimentation.

*   **Experimental Design:** The experiments are thoughtfully designed to directly test the central hypothesis. The optimization comparison in Section 3.1, where Highway and plain networks of increasing depth are trained on MNIST, provides a clear and convincing demonstration of the architecture's primary benefit. The degradation of the plain network's performance with depth, contrasted with the stable performance of the Highway Network, is compelling evidence.
*   **Analysis:** The analysis in Section 4 is a major strength. The visualization of the transform gate activations (Figure 2) provides valuable insight into the network's learned behavior, suggesting a sparse, data-dependent routing mechanism. The lesion study (Figure 4) is particularly insightful, demonstrating that the network can learn to utilize its depth according to task complexity—using many layers for CIFAR-100 but leaving many idle for the simpler MNIST task. This is a powerful property and the authors do a good job of investigating and presenting it.
*   **Completeness:** The work feels complete. It introduces a new architecture, provides an effective initialization strategy (biasing carry gates), demonstrates its optimization advantages, shows its practical utility on standard benchmarks, and provides an analysis of its internal workings.

**Clarity: High**

The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.

*   **Explanation of Core Idea:** The core mechanism of the Highway Network is introduced with clear notation and a simple, intuitive equation (Equation 3). The analogy to LSTM gates is helpful context, and the explanation of how the transform and carry gates function is straightforward.
*   **Organization:** The paper is logically structured, moving from motivation and related work to the proposed method, experimental validation, in-depth analysis, and discussion. The figures are clear and effectively support the paper's claims.
*   **Reproducibility:** The authors state that source code and hyperparameters are publicly available, which is a significant plus for reproducibility and further research. The description of the experimental setup seems sufficient for an expert to replicate the main results.

**Originality: High**

The work is highly original and presents a significant conceptual advance.

*   **Novelty of the Approach:** While the idea of skip connections is not entirely new, and the gating mechanism is borrowed from LSTMs, the application of a learned, dynamic gating mechanism to control information flow in very deep feedforward networks is a novel and powerful contribution. It elegantly reformulates skip connections from a fixed architectural choice into a learned, data-dependent function of the layer itself. This is a substantial conceptual leap over previous methods like deep supervision or static shortcut connections.
*   **Relation to Prior Work:** The paper does an excellent job of situating itself within the existing literature. It clearly acknowledges its inspiration from LSTMs and differentiates its approach from other methods for training deep nets, such as improved optimizers, initializations, and teacher-student training (FitNets). The direct comparison to FitNets highlights the originality and practical advantage of their simpler, end-to-end training paradigm.

**Significance: Very High**

The potential impact of this work is enormous. It directly addresses a fundamental barrier to progress in deep learning—the difficulty of effectively utilizing depth.

*   **Enabling Deeper Models:** This work provides a practical and general recipe for successfully training networks that are an order of magnitude deeper than what was previously considered feasible with standard methods. This opens the door for exploring the benefits of extreme depth on more complex and challenging problems.
*   **Foundational Idea:** The concept of creating unimpeded information paths through gating is a powerful primitive that is likely to be widely adopted and built upon. It provides a new way to think about network architecture, where the effective depth and computational path can be dynamically determined by the network itself based on the input. This is a profound idea that could influence architectural design for years to come.
*   **Advancing State-of-the-Art:** While the results on CIFAR-10/100 are competitive rather than record-shattering, the true significance lies not in a marginal benchmark improvement but in the fundamental capability it unlocks. The paper convincingly demonstrates that deep, thin networks can outperform shallower, wider ones, providing a practical tool to explore this trade-off.

---

### **Arguments for Acceptance**

1.  **Novelty & Impact:** Introduces a simple, novel, and highly effective architecture that overcomes a major obstacle in training very deep neural networks. The significance for the field is potentially huge.
2.  **Strong Empirical Evidence:** The paper provides a series of well-designed experiments that clearly and convincingly support its central claims. The head-to-head comparison with plain networks is particularly effective.
3.  **Excellent Analysis:** The authors go beyond simply showing that their method works; they provide insightful analysis (gate visualizations, lesion studies) that helps explain *why* it works and reveals interesting learned behaviors like adaptive depth.
4.  **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, making a powerful idea easy to understand and appreciate.

### **Arguments for Rejection**

1.  **Lack of Large-Scale Experiments:** The experiments are confined to MNIST and CIFAR. While convincing, validation on a larger-scale dataset like ImageNet would have made the claims about scalability indisputable.
2.  **Modest SOTA Improvement:** The performance gains on CIFAR benchmarks are solid but not groundbreaking. A reviewer focused solely on state-of-the-art numbers might see this as a weakness, though this would miss the paper's primary contribution, which is architectural and methodological.

---

### **Recommendation: Strong Accept**

This is a foundational paper that introduces a significant and elegant architectural innovation. It convincingly solves the problem of training very deep networks with a simple, LSTM-inspired gating mechanism. The work is of high quality, clearly presented, and highly original. Its significance lies in opening up a new frontier of research into extremely deep architectures. The minor weakness regarding the scale of experiments does not detract from the importance of the core contribution. This paper is an excellent fit for NIPS and is likely to be highly influential.
