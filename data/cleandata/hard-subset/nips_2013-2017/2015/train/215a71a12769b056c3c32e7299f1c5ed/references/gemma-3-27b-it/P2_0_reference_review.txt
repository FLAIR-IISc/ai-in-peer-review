## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel neural network architecture designed to facilitate the training of extremely deep feedforward networks. Inspired by the gating mechanisms of LSTMs, Highway Networks incorporate "transform" and "carry" gates that allow information to flow across many layers without attenuation, mitigating the vanishing gradient problem that plagues traditional deep networks. The authors demonstrate through extensive experimentation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 that Highway Networks can be trained effectively with standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to depths exceeding 100 layers, outperforming conventional networks and competitive with state-of-the-art approaches.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The idea of applying LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms to feedforward networks to address the depth problem is a significant contribution.  While the concept of skip connections exists (see [22, 2, 24]), the adaptive, data-dependent gating provided by Highway Networks is a distinct and clever innovation.
* **Empirical Results:** The paper presents a compelling set of experiments demonstrating the effectiveness of Highway Networks. The results on MNIST and CIFAR datasets consistently show that Highway Networks can be trained to greater depths and achieve comparable or better performance than existing methods. The comparison to FitNets [25] is particularly strong, demonstrating the ability to train in a single stage.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The authors provide a clear and concise mathematical formulation of Highway Networks (Equation 3) and offer insightful analysis of the Jacobian (Equation 5), demonstrating the flexibility of the architecture.  The discussion in Section 4 regarding the layer importance and routing of information is particularly valuable.
* **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is well-written and organized. The notation is clear, and the explanations are easy to follow. The inclusion of visualizations (Figures 1, 2, 3, and 4) enhances understanding and supports the claims made in the paper.  The authors also make their code and hyperparameter search results publicly available, which is commendable.
* **Addressing a Core Problem:** The paper tackles a central challenge in deep learning – the difficulty of training very deep networks – and proposes a practical and effective solution.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Depth:** While the Jacobian analysis is insightful, the paper lacks a more rigorous theoretical understanding of *why* Highway Networks work so well. A deeper investigation into the gradient flow dynamics within these networks would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Although the paper acknowledges the importance of the transform gate bias initialization, a more thorough exploration of the sensitivity to other hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, momentum) and their interactions would strengthen the results.
* **Comparison to Recent Advances:** The paper was written before the advent of ResNets [36], which also address the vanishing gradient problem using skip connections. A discussion of how Highway Networks compare to and potentially complement ResNets would be valuable. Although skip connections are mentioned in the introduction, a more in-depth comparison is lacking.
* **Computational Cost:** While the paper shows Highway Networks can be trained, it doesn’t deeply investigate the computational cost associated with the gating mechanisms themselves compared to traditional networks of similar performance.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed Highway Network architecture provides a compelling solution to the problem of training very deep networks, and the experimental results are convincing. While there are some areas where the paper could be strengthened (e.g., theoretical analysis, comparison to ResNets), the overall quality and originality of the work are high.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning.
* Introduces a novel and effective architecture (Highway Networks).
* Demonstrates strong empirical results on multiple datasets.
* Well-written and clearly presented.
* Code and hyperparameters are publicly available.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Lack of deeper theoretical analysis.
* Limited discussion of comparison with very recent architectures like ResNets.
* Could benefit from a more thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.



**Rating:** **Strong Accept (8/10)**

I believe this paper is well-deserving of acceptance to NIPS. It presents a novel, well-supported, and potentially impactful contribution to the field.  The strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses, and the work is likely to be influential in future research on deep learning architectures.
