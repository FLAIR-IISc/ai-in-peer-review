## NIPS Review: Training Very Deep Networks

**Summary:**

This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel neural network architecture inspired by LSTMs, designed to facilitate the training of extremely deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to introduce "information highways" through adaptive gating mechanisms that allow information to flow across many layers without attenuation, addressing the vanishing/exploding gradient problem often encountered in deep networks. The authors demonstrate, through empirical results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, that highway networks can be trained effectively with standard SGD, even at depths exceeding 100 layers, outperforming or matching the performance of shallower networks and existing techniques like FitNets. The paper further analyzes the internal workings of these networks, providing insights into the routing of information and layer importance.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon a substantial body of work addressing the challenges of training deep networks. It acknowledges previous efforts focused on better optimizers (e.g., [13, 14, 15]), initialization strategies ([16, 17, 18, 19]), activation functions ([20, 21]), skip connections ([22, 23, 2, 24]), and knowledge distillation ([25, 26]).  However, it distinguishes itself by proposing a novel architectural modification – the highway layer – rather than focusing solely on optimization or initialization. The inspiration from LSTM networks ([29, 30]) is clearly stated and well-motivated, providing a strong link to established methods for handling long-range dependencies.  The comparison with FitNets ([25]) and state-of-the-art convolutional networks ([5, 34]) provides valuable context and demonstrates the competitive performance of highway networks. The paper also builds on their prior short report [31] and acknowledges a concurrent work [32] pursuing a similar LSTM-inspired model.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The highway network architecture is a genuinely new approach to training deep networks, offering a conceptually simple yet effective solution to the vanishing gradient problem.
* **Empirical Results:** The authors provide convincing empirical evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of highway networks. The consistent performance improvements across multiple datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100) and depths is compelling. The comparisons to FitNets and existing methods are well-presented and highlight the advantages of the proposed approach.
* **Ease of Training:** A key strength is the simplicity of training.  Highway networks are trained with standard SGD, eliminating the need for complex two-stage procedures or specialized initialization schemes often required for deep networks.
* **Analysis:** The analysis section, particularly the visualizations of gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance (Figures 2 & 4), provides valuable insights into the internal workings of highway networks and supports the claims made in the paper.  The observed data-dependent routing is particularly intriguing.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the highway network architecture and its motivation. The notation is well-defined and the mathematical formulation is concise.
* **Code Availability:** Publicly available code and hyperparameters greatly enhance reproducibility and allow for further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Theoretical Justification:** While the empirical results are strong, the paper lacks a rigorous theoretical analysis of *why* highway networks work so well. A deeper understanding of the gradient flow and the role of the gating mechanisms would strengthen the contribution.  Why does this specific gating mechanism prove so effective?
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions a random search for hyperparameters, but doesn't provide a detailed discussion of the sensitivity of the results to these hyperparameters.  A more thorough sensitivity analysis would be beneficial.
* **Limited Exploration of Gate Variants:** The paper focuses on a specific form of the carry gate (C = 1-T). Exploring alternative gate functions or more complex gating mechanisms could potentially further improve performance.
* **Convolutional Layer Details:** While convolutional highway layers are mentioned, the details are somewhat limited. More exploration of different convolutional architectures and their interaction with highway layers would be interesting.
* **Comparison to more recent architectures:** The comparisons are mostly to methods available at the time of submission. It would be useful to include a brief comparison to more recent deep learning architectures and techniques.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods.
* The analysis provides valuable insights into the behavior of highway networks.
* The code availability promotes reproducibility and further research.
* The work addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning – the difficulty of training very deep networks.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of a rigorous theoretical analysis weakens the contribution.
* The paper could benefit from a more thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and exploration of gate variants.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is a novel and effective solution to the challenges of training very deep networks. While a more rigorous theoretical analysis and a more detailed exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity would strengthen the paper, the strong empirical results and insightful analysis are sufficient to warrant acceptance.

**Recommendation:** **Accept** (with minor revisions – encourage the authors to address the weaknesses mentioned above, especially regarding theoretical justification).
