

# Peer Review: "Training Very Deep Networks"

## Summary
This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel architecture for deep feedforward neural networks that incorporates an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism to facilitate information flow across layers. The key innovation is the transform gate (T) and carry gate (C), allowing the network to learn how much to transform input versus simply carry it forward: y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1-T(x)). The authors demonstrate that highway networks can be trained directly with SGD even at extreme depths (100+ layers), while plain networks struggle significantly with depth. Through comprehensive experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, they show highway networks achieve competitive or superior performance with fewer parameters compared to state-of-the-art methods. The paper also provides valuable analysis of gating behavior and layer importance.

The work builds on previous efforts to train deep networks, including better initialization schemes [16,17], skip connections [22,23,2], distillation methods [25,26], and layer-wise training [26,27]. It specifically extends LSTM's gating mechanism [29,30] to feedforward networks, rather than recurrent ones.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with well-designed experiments across multiple standard benchmarks. The authors provide thorough ablation studies (e.g., the lesioning experiments in Section 4.2) and statistical analysis (mean ± std.dev.) across multiple runs. The results clearly demonstrate that highway networks maintain performance as depth increases, while plain networks degrade. However, the paper lacks theoretical analysis to explain why highway networks enable better training of deep networks—only empirical evidence is provided. The experimental design is robust, but the paper would be strengthened by more detailed implementation specifics (e.g., exact highway layer implementation in Caffe/Brainstorm) to aid reproducibility.

## Clarity
The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and uses precise mathematical notation. Key concepts are thoroughly explained with helpful figures (Figures 1-4). The experimental setup is well-described, including hyperparameters and training procedures. The main weakness is the lack of comparison to ResNets (He et al., 2015), which was published around the same time and shares a similar skip connection approach. This omission makes it difficult to assess the paper's relative contribution. The authors should clarify how their approach differs from and improves upon ResNets.

## Originality
The highway network architecture represents a novel application of gating mechanisms to feedforward networks. While it builds on LSTM and skip connection ideas, it offers a distinct approach to enabling training of extremely deep networks. However, the paper's originality is significantly diminished by the lack of comparison to ResNets, which were presented at NIPS 2015 and CVPR 2016. The authors should thoroughly address how their work differs from and improves upon ResNets, as this is a critical comparison for this topic.

## Significance
The problem of training very deep networks is fundamental to deep learning, and the paper provides a practical solution that enables direct training of networks with 100+ layers. The results demonstrate that highway networks can match or exceed state-of-the-art performance with fewer parameters, which has significant practical implications. The analysis of layer importance (Section 4.2) also provides new insights into how deep networks operate. However, the significance is somewhat reduced by the concurrent development of ResNets, which achieved similar or better results with a simpler architecture (just identity mappings with addition rather than gating).

## Overall Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Solves a fundamental problem in deep learning (training very deep networks)
- Demonstrates clear empirical advantages over plain networks
- Provides valuable insights into layer importance and information routing
- Well-executed experiments with multiple datasets and baselines
- Clear and accessible writing

**Weaknesses:**
- Lack of theoretical analysis for the approach
- Fails to adequately distinguish from ResNets (published around the same time)
- Limited discussion of computational cost of the gating mechanism
- Missing comparison to ResNets in the experimental section

## Recommendation
The paper makes a valuable contribution to deep learning by demonstrating a practical approach to training extremely deep networks. While the work is not entirely novel compared to ResNets (which were published around the same time), it still represents a significant advance in understanding how to train very deep networks. The empirical results are strong, and the analysis provides meaningful insights.

I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. The authors should address the ResNet comparison thoroughly, explaining how their approach differs from and improves upon ResNets. This is essential for establishing the paper's contribution in the context of recent literature.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Solves an important problem in deep learning
- Demonstrates clear empirical advantages
- Provides valuable analysis of network behavior
- Well-executed experiments across multiple benchmarks

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
- Limited theoretical foundation
- Inadequate comparison to ResNets
- Missing computational cost analysis

The paper is technically sound, well-written, and addresses an important problem in deep learning. With minor revisions addressing the ResNet comparison, it would make a strong contribution to the field.
