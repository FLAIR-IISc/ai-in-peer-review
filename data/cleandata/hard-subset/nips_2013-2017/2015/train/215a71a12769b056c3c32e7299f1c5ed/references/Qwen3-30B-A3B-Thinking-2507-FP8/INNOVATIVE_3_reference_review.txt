

# Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces Highway Networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate training of extremely deep feedforward neural networks. The authors propose an LSTM-inspired adaptive gating mechanism that allows information to flow across layers with minimal attenuation, creating "information highways" through the network. The key innovation is the use of transform gates (T) and carry gates (C) that control how much of the input is transformed versus carried through, enabling the network to smoothly transition between identity mapping (T=0) and standard transformation (T=1). The authors demonstrate that Highway Networks can be trained directly with SGD for depths exceeding 100 layers, whereas plain networks become increasingly difficult to optimize as depth increases.

The paper makes a compelling case for the novelty of this approach. While skip connections have been used in neural networks before (e.g., [22, 23, 2, 24]), Highway Networks introduce adaptive gating that allows the network to learn how to route information based on input, rather than using fixed skip connections. This differs significantly from Residual Networks (ResNets), which were published after this work but represent a similar but distinct approach. The authors clearly differentiate their contribution from previous work on skip connections and residual networks, noting that Highway Networks allow for dynamic routing of information, which is not possible with fixed skip connections.

The experimental validation is thorough and well-executed. The authors demonstrate that Highway Networks achieve similar performance to much shallower plain networks at extreme depths (100+ layers) on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100. Notably, they show that Highway Networks can be trained in a single stage without the need for two-stage training procedures (e.g., FitNets [25]), which was previously required for deep networks with limited computational budgets. The analysis of information routing (Figure 2-4) provides valuable insights into how Highway Networks actually function, revealing that they learn to route information differently based on input data.

**Strengths:**
- **High novelty:** The concept of adaptive information highways represents a significant conceptual advance beyond traditional skip connections
- **Strong experimental validation:** Demonstrates training of networks with >100 layers, a significant achievement
- **Insightful analysis:** The investigation of information routing and layer importance provides new understanding of deep network behavior
- **Clear differentiation from prior work:** Properly situates the contribution relative to existing skip connection approaches
- **Practical significance:** Provides a simple, effective method for training extremely deep networks with standard SGD

**Weaknesses:**
- The theoretical justification for why Highway Networks work is somewhat limited (primarily analogies to LSTM)
- The paper acknowledges using a simplified form of the LSTM-inspired block, not the full LSTM architecture
- The comparison to ResNets is missing (as they were published after this work), though this is understandable
- The paper doesn't fully explore the relationship between highway networks and other recent approaches to deep network training

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pro:*
- The highway network concept is genuinely novel and represents a significant step beyond fixed skip connections
- The ability to train networks with >100 layers using standard SGD is a major practical contribution
- The paper provides valuable insights into how information flows through deep networks
- The experimental results are robust across multiple benchmarks and architectures
- The approach is general and can be applied to various network types (convolutional, fully connected)

*Con:*
- The theoretical foundation could be strengthened
- The simplified LSTM-inspired block design may limit the potential of the approach
- The paper doesn't fully address how highway networks compare to the recently proposed ResNets (though this is understandable given timing)
- The paper could better explore the relationship between highway networks and other recent approaches to deep network training

As a reviewer who prioritizes novelty and bold approaches over methodological perfection, I find this paper to be highly significant. The highway network concept represents a genuinely new direction in deep learning architecture design, with the potential to influence future research on deep network training. The paper's ability to train networks with over 100 layers using standard SGD is a notable achievement that demonstrates the practical value of the approach.

While the theoretical justification could be stronger, the experimental evidence is compelling and the paper provides valuable new insights into information flow in deep networks. The authors have clearly demonstrated that their approach is not just another incremental improvement, but a fundamentally different way of thinking about deep network architecture.

The significance of this work is substantial. It opens up new possibilities for training extremely deep networks without special training techniques, potentially enabling more complex models for a wide range of applications. The paper also provides a new framework for analyzing how much depth is actually needed for a given problem, which is a valuable contribution to the understanding of deep learning.

I believe this paper makes a strong contribution to the field that merits acceptance at NIPS. The novelty of the highway network concept, combined with the compelling experimental results, outweighs the minor theoretical limitations. This work represents a significant step forward in our ability to train very deep networks, and it has the potential to influence future research in deep learning architecture design.
