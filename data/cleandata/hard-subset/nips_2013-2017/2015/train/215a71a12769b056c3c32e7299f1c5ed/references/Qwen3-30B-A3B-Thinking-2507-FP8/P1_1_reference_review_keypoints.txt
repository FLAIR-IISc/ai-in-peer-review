- Proposes Highway Networks using LSTM-like transform and carry gates (y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1-T(x))) to enable training of very deep feedforward nets.
- Empirically shows Highway Networks train with SGD to 100+ layers and outperform plain networks on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 with fewer parameters.

- Strength: Simple, practical solution addressing vanishing gradients that scales to very deep networks with strong empirical gains.
- Strength: Well-motivated by LSTM intuition and supported by Jacobian analysis showing near-identity mappings via gates.
- Strength: Strong experimental validation across datasets and depths, including comparisons to plain nets and FitNets.
- Strength: Insightful analyses of transform gate behavior and layer importance revealing dynamic routing of information.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison/discussion relative to ResNet-style residual connections appearing around the same time.
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of limitations and failure modes (e.g., noisy or low-data regimes and gate-learning data requirements).
- Weakness: Sparse implementation/hyperparameter details, particularly on transform gate bias interaction with activations and architectures.
- Weakness: Limited theoretical analysis of why gradients propagate through highway layers beyond empirical evidence.

- Quality: Technically sound experiments and claims, minor omission being ResNet comparison.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and reproducible from provided math and figures.
- Originality: Novel adaptation of LSTM gates to feedforward networks, distinct from prior skip/teacher-student methods.
- Significance: Enables direct training of 100+ layer networks, offering substantial practical impact for architecture design.

- Overall: Significant, elegant, and well-validated contribution that should be accepted after addressing minor comparisons and gate-detail clarifications.
- Recommendation: Accept.
