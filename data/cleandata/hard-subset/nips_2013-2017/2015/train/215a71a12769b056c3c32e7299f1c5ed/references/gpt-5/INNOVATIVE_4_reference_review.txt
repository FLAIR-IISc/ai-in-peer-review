Summary
The paper proposes Highway Networks: feedforward architectures that introduce learnable, LSTM-inspired gates between successive layers to facilitate information and gradient flow across very deep networks. Each layer computes y = H(x) · T(x) + x · (1 − T(x)), where H is a standard transform (e.g., affine + nonlinearity or convolution) and T is a sigmoid “transform gate” (with the “carry” gate tied as 1 − T). The central idea is to provide data-dependent paths of near-identity mappings (“information highways”) that reduce attenuation of signals through depth. A simple but effective initialization biases T toward carry (negative bias), analogous to LSTM forget-gate initialization, enabling optimization at depths where plain nets stall.

Empirically, the authors show: (i) on MNIST, thin fully connected highway nets of 50–100 hidden layers optimize reliably and much faster than capacity-matched plain nets, which degrade with depth; (ii) competitive results on MNIST with thin convolutional highways; (iii) on CIFAR-10/100, they can train deeper/thinner models in a single stage with SGD, matching or exceeding FitNets trained with teacher hints at similar parameter/compute budgets; (iv) analyses of gate activations, data-dependent routing, and lesion studies indicating that deeper layers are used adaptively (especially on CIFAR-100).

Relation to prior work: The approach is clearly inspired by LSTM gating for long-range temporal credit assignment and by prior skip/shortcut connections (including error injection). It contrasts with normalized initialization, improved optimizers, locally competitive activations, and distillation. The work is contemporaneous with increasing depth in CNNs (VGG, Inception) and connects conceptually to fixed skip connections, but introduces learned, input-dependent gating as a general mechanism for depth.

Strengths
- Novelty: A bold, simple, and general architectural idea—LSTM-like gating across depth—that directly targets the optimization barrier for very deep feedforward nets.
- Practicality: Minimal changes to existing layers; negative-bias gate initialization is simple and effective; code availability is a plus.
- Empirical evidence on optimization: Clear degradation of plain nets with depth vs stable optimization for 50–100 layer highways; faster convergence.
- Single-stage training: Demonstrates that deep, thin models can be trained without teacher hints, matching or surpassing FitNet baselines at similar budgets.
- Insightful analyses: Gate activity patterns, class-conditional routing, and lesion studies provide rare interpretability of depth utilization.
- Generality: Formulation applies to fully connected and convolutional layers; not tied to a specific nonlinearity.

Weaknesses
- Scale of evaluation: Results are limited to MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 with modest augmentation; no large-scale validation (e.g., ImageNet).
- Comparisons: Missing a head-to-head against fixed identity skip connections (e.g., residual-style), batch normalization, or other modern training aids at similar depth/width; unclear how much benefit is from gating vs any skip path.
- Ablations: Limited exploration of design choices (free carry gate vs C=1−T, effect of removing gates at test, sensitivity to gate bias, alternative gate parameterizations, impact on compute/memory).
- Fairness/metrics: Parameter and FLOP matching are asserted but not exhaustively documented; gate overhead and runtime are not quantified; variance across runs is partially reported.
- Clarity nits: Some typos and places where architectural details (e.g., conv gate sharing, exact layer configurations) could be more explicit for full reproducibility.

Quality
Technically sound and carefully executed for the stated scope. Claims about optimization ease are well supported; generalization claims are reasonable given the datasets but would benefit from larger-scale benchmarks and stronger baselines. Theory is minimal, but the core mechanism is well motivated by LSTM analogies.

Clarity
Generally clear and well organized. The gating formulation, initialization, and training protocols are described plainly. Figures provide useful diagnostics. Please tighten notation (minor inconsistencies), and provide full architectural/compute details in an appendix for exact reproducibility.

Originality
High. Bringing LSTM-style gating to depth in feedforward nets with input-dependent, learnable routing is a fresh and impactful idea, distinct from prior fixed skip/shortcut connections and from distillation.

Significance
High. Enabling reliable training of very deep nets with a simple, general mechanism is likely to influence architectures and practice. Even if subsequent work explores fixed skips or normalization, the learned gating perspective is valuable and opens new directions (adaptive routing, conditional computation).

Questions and suggestions
- Provide ablations: (a) free carry gate vs tying C=1−T; (b) removing gates after training; (c) varying gate bias and showing training curves; (d) histograms of T activations (saturation) across depth.
- Compare against plain nets with identity shortcuts (fixed additive or concatenative) and batch normalization at matched budgets.
- Quantify parameter/FLOP overhead and wall-clock training time for highways vs plain nets.
- Explore whether highway depth helps at fixed parameter count (depth/width trade-offs).
- Clarify whether T is per-unit/channel or shared across spatial locations; discuss memory implications.

Pros for acceptance
- Introduces a significant new architectural concept with strong empirical support.
- Simple, general, and readily adoptable; code provided.
- Compelling optimization evidence and insightful analyses.

Cons against acceptance
- Limited scale and baseline breadth; no ImageNet-level validation.
- Missing direct comparison to fixed skip/residual-style connections and batch normalization.
- Incomplete ablations on gating design choices and overhead.

Recommendation
Accept (strong). The paper presents a genuinely novel and promising mechanism that materially advances our ability to train very deep networks. While large-scale validation and additional comparisons would strengthen the case, the conceptual contribution is substantial and timely.

Confidence
High.
