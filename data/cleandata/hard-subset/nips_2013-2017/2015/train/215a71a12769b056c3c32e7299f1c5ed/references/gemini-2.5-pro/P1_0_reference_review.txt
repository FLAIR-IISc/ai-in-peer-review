### Summary

This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel neural network architecture designed to enable the training of extremely deep feedforward networks. The core problem the authors address is the difficulty of training traditional ("plain") deep networks due to poor information propagation and vanishing gradients, which persists even with modern initialization schemes. The proposed solution is inspired by the gating mechanism of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks. Each layer in a Highway Network has a "transform gate" and a "carry gate" that dynamically control the flow of information. The layer's output is a weighted sum of the transformed input (as in a plain layer) and the untransformed input itself. This allows information to flow unimpeded across many layers, creating "information highways."

The authors relate this idea to previous work on training deep networks, including improved optimizers, initialization techniques (e.g., Glorot & Bengio, He et al.), skip connections, and multi-stage training procedures like FitNets. The key distinction of Highway Networks is the adaptive, data-dependent gating mechanism, which is more flexible than fixed skip connections. The paper provides strong empirical evidence that Highway Networks with hundreds of layers can be trained directly and easily using SGD with a simple initialization strategy (biasing the gates towards "carry" behavior initially).

### Strengths

1.  **Novel and Elegant Solution to a Fundamental Problem:** The paper tackles one of the most significant challenges in deep learning at the time: the degradation of performance as network depth increases. The proposed solution is both intuitive and powerful, elegantly adapting a proven concept from recurrent networks (LSTM gating) to the feedforward domain. This provides a principled way to build and train networks of unprecedented depth.

2.  **Strong and Convincing Experimental Validation:** The empirical evaluation is thorough and well-designed.
    *   The optimization experiment on MNIST (Section 3.1, Figure 1) is a standout. It provides a direct and compelling comparison against plain networks, clearly demonstrating that as depth increases to 50 or 100 layers, plain networks fail to train effectively while highway networks maintain strong performance and faster convergence.
    *   The comparison to FitNets (Section 3.3.1) is also very effective. It shows that Highway Networks can match or exceed the performance of a state-of-the-art method that requires a complex, two-stage training procedure, while using only simple, single-stage SGD. This highlights the practical utility of the architecture.
    *   The results on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 demonstrate that the approach is not just an optimization trick but also leads to models that generalize well and achieve competitive performance.

3.  **Insightful Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply reporting performance metrics. Section 4 provides a valuable analysis of the trained networks' internal workings.
    *   The visualizations of gate activities (Figure 2) and the investigation of data-dependent routing (Figure 3) offer compelling evidence that the gating mechanism is not just a static "on/off" switch but learns complex, input-specific behaviors.
    *   The layer lesioning study (Figure 4) is an excellent contribution. It demonstrates that the network can learn to utilize its depth effectively for complex tasks (CIFAR-100) while effectively ignoring unneeded layers for simpler tasks (MNIST). This suggests the architecture inherently learns an "effective depth," a highly desirable property.

### Weaknesses

1.  **Limited Discussion on Computational Overhead:** The gating mechanism introduces additional parameters (`WT`, `bT`) and computations (a matrix multiplication and element-wise operations) per layer. While the benefits are clear, the paper does not explicitly discuss the trade-offs in terms of computational cost or memory usage compared to a plain network or a network with fixed skip connections. A brief analysis of this overhead would make the evaluation more complete.

2.  **Architectural Details for Reproducibility:** While the paper is generally clear, some of the finer architectural details for the CIFAR experiments (Section 3.3.2) are slightly sparse. For instance, the exact configuration of the "all convolutional" network with global average pooling could be described more precisely to facilitate exact reproduction of the state-of-the-art comparison.

3.  **Relationship to Fixed Skip Connections:** The paper contrasts its approach with fixed skip connections. However, a deeper discussion would be beneficial. Since a highway layer can learn to set its transform gate to a constant `T(x) = 0`, it can effectively learn a fixed identity skip connection. It would be interesting to analyze if the gates in a trained network converge to static values or if the data-dependent variability is truly critical for performance. This could further strengthen the argument for the necessity of the gating mechanism over a simpler, non-gated residual connection.

### Detailed Comments on Review Criteria

*   **Quality:** The paper is of very high technical quality. The claims are well-substantiated by rigorous and well-controlled experiments. The analysis section adds significant depth and credibility to the work. It is a complete and well-executed piece of research.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The core idea is introduced with clear notation and motivation. The figures are informative and effectively support the main arguments. The manuscript is a pleasure to read.
*   **Originality:** The work is highly original. While it draws inspiration from LSTMs and skip connections, the application of a learned, dynamic gating mechanism to enable training of very deep feedforward networks is a novel and significant contribution. It clearly distinguishes itself from prior work on deep network training.
*   **Significance:** The significance of this work is extremely high. It provides a practical and effective solution to a major bottleneck in deep learning research, directly enabling the community to explore the benefits of network depth in a way that was previously infeasible. The ideas presented are likely to be widely used and built upon by other researchers, advancing the state-of-the-art in numerous application domains.

### Recommendation

This is an excellent paper that presents a novel, elegant, and highly effective architecture for training very deep neural networks. The claims are backed by strong empirical evidence and insightful analysis. The work addresses a fundamental problem in the field with a solution that is both principled and practical. The weaknesses are minor and do not detract from the overall contribution. I strongly recommend acceptance, and I believe this paper is a candidate for an oral presentation.
