- Introduces highway networks with an LSTM-inspired adaptive gating: y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)), creating “information highways.”  
- Enables direct training of very deep (100+ layer) feedforward networks with standard SGD; plain networks degrade with depth.  
- Strong empirical performance on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, matching or exceeding shallower nets with fewer parameters.  
- Builds on prior work (initialization, skip connections, distillation) but differs by using adaptive, data-dependent gating rather than fixed skips.  
- Provides analysis of transform-gate behavior and layer importance, showing dynamic routing and some unused layers.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, capacity-controlled, include ablations, hyperparameter searches, and multiple runs for significance.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations (unused layers) and give implementation/hyperparameter details for reproducibility.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear notation, effective figures, and reproducible experimental protocol.  
- Highway networks are an original contribution distinct from LSTMs and residual connections due to adaptive gating.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to ResNets (contemporaneous work) and insufficient exploration of the relationship to residual connections.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis explaining why highway networks succeed at extreme depths.  
- Weakness: minimal discussion of interactions with other advances (e.g., batch normalization, weight/weight normalization).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address comparisons to ResNets and clarify relation to residual connections.
