- Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed paper that convincingly shows highway networks enable training of very deep feedforward networks; recommend acceptance.

- Problem addressed: Training very deep feedforward networks is hard due to vanishing/exploding gradients; paper targets this long-standing challenge.

- Main idea: Introduces highway networks with adaptive transform and carry gates, inspired by LSTM, to allow information to flow across many layers.

- Empirical claim: Highway networks can be trained to depths of hundreds of layers where plain networks fail.

- Related work: Situates work among optimizers, initialization, activations, skip connections, distillation, and layer-wise training; acknowledges very recent similar papers.

- Novelty & significance: Presents a novel, impactful architecture that expands practical depth of feedforward models.

- Strong empirical results: Convincing experiments on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 with comparisons to FitNets and SOTA methods.

- Clear explanation & design: Architecture and mathematical formulation are well-presented and intuitively motivated.

- Detailed analysis: Visualizations of gate biases, activities, and ablation studies provide insight into layer usage and internal behavior.

- Reproducibility: Code and hyperparameters are publicly available, facilitating replication.

- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: More guidance needed for choosing transform gate bias, which appears problem-dependent.

- Weakness — computational cost: Training extremely deep networks remains computationally expensive despite easier optimization.

- Weakness — theoretical justification: Lacks deeper theoretical analysis of gradient flow dynamics to explain why highway networks work.

- Quality & clarity: Paper is technically sound, well-written, and organized with clear notation.

- Originality & significance: Creative extension of existing ideas with potential to significantly impact deep learning practice.

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a core problem, offers an effective novel solution, strong empirical evidence, insightful analysis, and promotes reproducibility.

- Arguments against acceptance: Hyperparameter tuning difficulty, high computational cost, and limited theoretical understanding.

- Final recommendation: Strong accept — contribution is significant and practical weaknesses are minor.
