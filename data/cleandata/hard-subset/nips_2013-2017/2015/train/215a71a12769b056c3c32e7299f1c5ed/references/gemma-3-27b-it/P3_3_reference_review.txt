## NIPS Review: Training Very Deep Networks

**Summary:**

This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel neural network architecture inspired by LSTM gating mechanisms, designed to facilitate the training of extremely deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to introduce "transform" and "carry" gates at each layer, allowing for direct information flow across many layers, mitigating the vanishing/exploding gradient problem that plagues deep plain networks. The authors demonstrate, through extensive experimentation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, that Highway Networks can be trained effectively with standard SGD to depths far exceeding those achievable with traditional architectures, and achieve competitive performance.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly identifies the growing importance of depth in achieving state-of-the-art results in supervised learning (citing Krizhevsky et al., Szegedy et al., Simonyan & Zisserman). It positions its work within the broader context of tackling the difficulties of training deep networks, referencing techniques like improved optimizers (Martens et al.), initialization strategies (Glorot & Bengio), activation functions (Srivastava et al.), skip connections (Ciresan et al., He et al.), and knowledge distillation (Romero et al.).  The connection to LSTM (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber) as a key inspiration is well-articulated, and the authors acknowledge related work proposing LSTM-inspired models (e.g., [32]). The comparison to FitNets (Romero et al.) is particularly relevant, demonstrating a competitive alternative.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The Highway Network architecture represents a genuinely novel approach to addressing the depth problem.  While inspired by LSTMs, the application of gating mechanisms to feedforward networks in this way is a significant contribution.
* **Empirical Validation:** The authors provide a comprehensive set of experiments across multiple datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100) demonstrating the effectiveness of Highway Networks. The results consistently show superior performance compared to plain networks of similar complexity, especially at increasing depths.
* **Thorough Analysis:** The paper goes beyond simply demonstrating performance. The analyses of transform gate biases, activity patterns (Figure 2), and layer importance (Figure 4) provide valuable insights into *how* Highway Networks function, and why they are successful.  The observation about data-dependent routing is particularly interesting.
* **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is well-written and the concepts are explained clearly, even for someone unfamiliar with the specifics of LSTM networks. The mathematical notation is concise and well-defined.
* **Reproducibility:** The authors provide links to source code and hyperparameter search results, significantly enhancing the reproducibility of their work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the authors conduct a random search, the paper doesn't deeply explore the sensitivity of performance to the transform gate bias initialization.  A more systematic investigation of this hyperparameter, and its interaction with other training parameters, would strengthen the claims.
* **Computational Cost:** Although the paper demonstrates that Highway Networks can be trained, it doesn’t directly address the *computational cost* of training.  The increased number of parameters in the gating mechanism may offset some of the benefits, particularly for very large datasets and models. This aspect needs more discussion.
* **Limited Exploration of Gate Function:** The paper simplifies the gating mechanism by setting C = 1-T. While this simplification is reasonable, it's unclear if more sophisticated gate functions could further improve performance or stability.  Exploring alternatives might be a fruitful direction for future work.
* **Comparison to Modern Architectures:** The comparisons to FitNets and older architectures are useful, but a comparison to more recent state-of-the-art architectures (e.g., ResNets, DenseNets) would provide a stronger benchmark for evaluating the significance of the work.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning – training very deep networks.
* Presents a novel and well-motivated architectural solution (Highway Networks).
* Provides strong empirical evidence of its effectiveness across multiple datasets.
* Includes insightful analysis of the learned behavior of the networks.
* The code release promotes reproducibility.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The computational cost of the gating mechanism is not thoroughly addressed.
* A more systematic hyperparameter study (especially regarding the transform gate bias) is needed.
*  Comparison to more recent SOTA architectures would strengthen the claims.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The Highway Network architecture is a clever and effective solution to the problem of training very deep feedforward networks. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper significantly outweigh them.  I believe this work is highly relevant to the NIPS community and warrants acceptance.

**Rating:** **Strong Accept** (8/10)
