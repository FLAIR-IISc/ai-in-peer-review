## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel neural network architecture inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, designed to facilitate the training of extremely deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to incorporate adaptive gating mechanisms (transform and carry gates) within each layer, allowing information to flow more easily across many layers without attenuation – effectively creating “information highways.” The authors demonstrate, through experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, that highway networks can be trained with standard SGD to depths exceeding 100 layers, outperforming traditional ("plain") networks which suffer from optimization difficulties as depth increases.  The paper also provides analysis into the learned gating behavior and layer importance.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a Core Problem:** The difficulty of training very deep networks is a fundamental challenge in deep learning. This paper tackles this problem head-on with a conceptually simple, yet effective solution.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the benefit of highway networks.  The comparisons to plain networks, particularly in Figure 1, clearly illustrate the improved optimization capabilities and final performance.  The CIFAR results (Tables 2 & 3) further showcase the practical applicability of the approach.
* **Novelty:** While inspired by LSTMs, adapting the gating mechanism to feedforward networks is a novel contribution. The idea of dynamically controlling information flow through gating is insightful.
* **Analysis of Learned Behavior:**  The analysis of transform gate biases and activity (Figures 2-4) provides valuable insights into *how* highway networks function, moving beyond simply demonstrating *that* they work.  The visualization of information flow is particularly compelling.
* **Clear Writing and Organization:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The mathematical notation is clear, and the explanations are concise.
* **Code Availability:**  The availability of code and hyperparameters fosters reproducibility and encourages further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Justification:** While the LSTM inspiration is mentioned, the paper lacks a deeper theoretical analysis of *why* highway networks work so well. A more rigorous understanding of the gradient flow properties compared to plain networks would strengthen the contribution.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The initialization of the transform gate bias (mentioned in Section 2.2) appears crucial for success. While the authors provide guidelines, a more comprehensive investigation of the sensitivity to this and other hyperparameters would be beneficial.
* **Comparison to More Recent Methods:** The paper was written in 2015.  Since then, other techniques like residual connections (ResNets) have become extremely popular and effective for training deep networks. A discussion of how highway networks compare to ResNets and other modern architectures would add significant value.  (This is not a fatal flaw given the publication date, but a relevant point for assessing significance.)
* **Potential for Redundancy:** The analysis in Figure 4 suggests that many layers in the highway network can be “lesioned” without significant performance degradation. This raises the question of whether the depth is truly necessary or if a shallower network with the same gating mechanisms could achieve comparable performance.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound.  The experimental methodology is rigorous, and the results are well-supported. The work feels complete for its time, though as noted above, could benefit from further theoretical analysis.
* **Clarity:** Excellent. The paper is very well-written and easy to follow, even for readers unfamiliar with the specifics of LSTM networks.
* **Originality:**  The approach is novel and represents a creative adaptation of LSTM principles to feedforward networks. The concept of highway layers is a distinct contribution.
* **Significance:** At the time of publication, this paper was a significant contribution to the field, demonstrating a viable path to training very deep networks. While subsequent advancements like ResNets have become more prevalent, the insights gained from highway networks remain valuable.  The ability to analyze layer importance is particularly noteworthy.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Successfully demonstrates a method for training significantly deeper networks than previously possible.
* Provides strong empirical evidence supporting the efficacy of the proposed architecture.
* Offers a novel and insightful approach to addressing a fundamental challenge in deep learning.
* Includes valuable analysis of learned behavior.
* Well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Lacks a strong theoretical foundation.
* The impact has been somewhat diminished by the emergence of more popular techniques like ResNets.
* Hyperparameter sensitivity is a potential concern.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.** 

This is a valuable and well-executed paper that made a significant contribution to the field at the time of its publication. While the landscape of deep learning has evolved, the core ideas and experimental results remain relevant. The authors should be encouraged to add a brief discussion of how highway networks compare to more recent architectures like ResNets, and potentially explore the redundancy of layers further.  A slightly more detailed theoretical justification would also be welcome. However, these are minor points and do not detract from the overall quality and significance of the work.
