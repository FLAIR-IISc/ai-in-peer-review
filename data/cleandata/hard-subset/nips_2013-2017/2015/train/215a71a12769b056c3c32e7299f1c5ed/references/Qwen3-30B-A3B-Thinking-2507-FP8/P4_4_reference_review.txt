

# Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces Highway Networks, a novel architecture designed to enable training of extremely deep feedforward neural networks using standard stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The authors propose a gating mechanism inspired by Long Short Term Memory (LSTM) networks, where each layer contains a transform gate T and a carry gate C = 1-T. This allows information to flow through layers with minimal attenuation, creating "information highways" that overcome the vanishing gradient problem that typically hinders training of very deep networks. The authors demonstrate that Highway Networks can be trained to depths of 100+ layers without significant performance degradation, whereas plain networks show substantial performance drops at similar depths.

The paper relates to previous work on deep learning by building upon several key concepts: normalized initialization [16,17], skip connections [22,23,2], distillation techniques [25,26], and the theoretical advantage of deep networks [10,11,12]. While skip connections and distillation approaches have been used previously to address depth-related challenges, the Highway Network architecture provides a more unified solution that allows direct training without requiring multiple stages or teacher networks.

**Strengths:**
1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a theoretically grounded architecture with clear mathematical formulation. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the information flow through highway layers, demonstrating how the gating mechanism enables information to propagate without attenuation. The experimental results are thorough and well-designed, with multiple datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100) and comprehensive comparisons to plain networks and other approaches.

2. **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The highway network architecture is clearly explained with consistent notation, and the figures effectively illustrate the information flow and gating mechanisms. The experimental methodology is detailed enough for reproducibility.

3. **Originality:** The highway network architecture represents a novel approach to addressing the depth problem in neural networks. While inspired by LSTM, the application to feedforward networks with an adaptive gating mechanism is distinct from previous work on skip connections and distillation. The paper clearly differentiates its contribution from related work.

4. **Significance:** The ability to train networks with 100+ layers directly with SGD represents a significant advance in deep learning. The authors demonstrate that highway networks can match or exceed the performance of shallower networks with fewer parameters, and they provide valuable insights into how information flows through deep networks. The paper also introduces a novel way to assess layer importance through lesioning experiments.

**Weaknesses:**
1. **Limited Comparison to Contemporary Work:** The paper predates the ResNet architecture (which was published in 2015), but does not acknowledge that ResNet, which uses similar skip connections, was being developed concurrently. A more complete comparison to ResNet would strengthen the paper's position.

2. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper discusses the importance of initializing the transform gate bias to negative values, the optimal values appear to be dataset and architecture-dependent. The paper does not provide a systematic analysis of how sensitive the performance is to this hyperparameter.

3. **Theoretical Limitations:** The paper demonstrates empirical success but does not provide theoretical guarantees about the convergence properties of highway networks compared to plain networks. A more rigorous theoretical analysis would strengthen the contribution.

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pro:*
- The highway network architecture is a significant contribution that enables training of extremely deep networks directly with SGD.
- The experimental results are comprehensive, demonstrating consistent improvements over plain networks across multiple datasets.
- The paper provides valuable insights into information flow through deep networks and introduces a novel method for assessing layer importance.
- The architecture is general and can be applied to various network types (convolutional, fully connected).

*Con:*
- The paper does not compare to ResNet, which was published shortly after this work and represents a similar approach to the depth problem.
- The theoretical analysis, while sound, does not fully address why highway networks overcome the depth problem more effectively than other approaches.
- The paper claims that highway networks can be trained to "1000+ layers" but the experiments only go up to 100 layers, making this claim somewhat speculative.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is novel, well-motivated, and effectively addresses a major challenge in training deep networks. The experimental results are thorough and provide strong evidence for the claims made. The paper is clearly written and well-organized, making it accessible to the NIPS community.

The paper advances the state of the art in training very deep networks by demonstrating that networks with 100+ layers can be trained directly with standard SGD, whereas previous approaches required specialized techniques or two-stage training. The insights into information flow and layer importance provide valuable theoretical understanding beyond just the empirical results.

While the paper would benefit from a more complete comparison to ResNet (which emerged shortly after this submission), this does not diminish the paper's significant contribution to the field. The highway network concept has influenced subsequent work in deep learning, including the development of residual networks.

The paper meets all four criteria: it is technically sound with well-supported claims, clearly written, novel in its approach to the depth problem, and significant in its impact on the field of deep learning. I recommend acceptance.
