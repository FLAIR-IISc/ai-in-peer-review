- Proposes Highway Networks: layers compute y = T(x)·H(x) + (1−T(x))·x with learned LSTM-like gates (T) initialized with negative bias to enable very deep feedforward training.  
- Connects to prior work: skip/shortcut connections, LSTM gating repurposed across depth, initialization (Glorot/He), FitNets/student–teacher methods, and locally competitive activations.  
- Soundness: idea and training recipe are well-motivated; controlled MNIST experiments show clear depth and convergence advantages over plain nets.  
- Empirics: strong optimization evidence up to 100 layers, CIFAR results competitive with FitNets without hints but not state-of-the-art under standard augmentation.  
- Missing ablations: no comparison to fixed skip/residual connections, no test of enforcing C=1−T vs learning both gates, unclear gate granularity (per-unit vs per-channel), sensitivity to gate-bias initialization unquantified, and no gradient-norm diagnostics.  
- Claims of training >1000 layers are not backed by quantitative results (reported results stop at 100 layers).  
- Analysis: gate activity, class-conditional routing, and lesioning provide useful insights that effective depth adapts to input/task complexity.  
- Technical caveat: Jacobian discussion omits T(x) dependence; extremes are heuristic and need a more precise derivation or appendix.  
- Reproducibility: code release is positive, but key experimental details (exact CIFAR architectures, schedules, compute overhead) are sparse.  
- Clarity: paper is mostly clear and well-structured, but contains typos, inconsistent references, and ambiguity about gating shapes in convolutional layers.  
- Originality: novel and elegant use of learned, input-dependent interpolation between identity and transform, distinct from fixed shortcuts and auxiliary-loss methods.  
- Significance: addresses training of very deep networks with a simple general mechanism likely to influence architectures and practice, though broader benchmarks would strengthen impact.  
- Strengths: simple LSTM-grounded idea, strong optimization evidence, single-stage training matching FitNets without hints, insightful analyses, and released code.  
- Weaknesses: limited benchmarks (no ImageNet), no direct comparison to residual shortcuts, sparse ablations, unsubstantiated extreme-depth claims, and minor clarity issues.  
- Key questions for authors: comparisons to residual networks of matched capacity, compute/parameter cost of gates in conv layers, sensitivity to gate-bias and gradient norms, experiments with per-channel/spatial gates, and effect of relaxing C=1−T.  
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, novel general mechanism, strong optimization results, insightful analyses, and public code.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical scope, missing critical ablations and direct residual comparisons, moderate benchmark performance, and minor presentation issues.  
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance provided the authors add direct comparisons to identity-skip/residual connections, basic ablations (C free vs 1−T, gate granularity), and clearer architectural/training details.
