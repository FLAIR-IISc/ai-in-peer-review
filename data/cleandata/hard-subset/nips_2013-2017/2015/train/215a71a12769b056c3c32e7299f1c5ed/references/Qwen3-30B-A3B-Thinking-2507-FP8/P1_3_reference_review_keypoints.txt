- Introduces Highway Networks with LSTM-inspired adaptive gating (transform T, carry C=1−T) to create "information highways" enabling deep feedforward networks to train directly with SGD.  
- Shows highway networks can be trained at 100+ layers without performance degradation and generalize well on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Novel contribution: learnable, adaptive gating differs fundamentally from fixed skip connections and builds on LSTM/skip ideas.  
- Experiments are well-designed and thorough, comparing to plain networks with normalized initialization.  
- Analysis of gate behavior and layer importance offers useful insights; unused layers do not harm performance.  
- Significance: enables extremely deep networks to match/exceed shallower models with fewer parameters, useful for resource-constrained settings.  
- Weakness: lacks rigorous theoretical analysis explaining why gating overcomes vanishing gradients despite referencing Jacobian analysis.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to contemporary and later skip-connection approaches (e.g., ResNets) beyond FitNets.  
- Weakness: does not quantify computational overhead of the gating mechanism or its impact on inference time.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of failure cases or scenarios where plain networks might outperform highway networks.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant—strong NIPS submission candidate.  
- Recommendation: accept; substantial contribution with convincing experiments and insights.  
- Suggestion: add discussion comparing highway networks to ResNets to contextualize contribution.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed analysis of computational cost and inference overhead of gating.  
- Suggestion: include experiments demonstrating cases where highway networks may be less effective.  
- Suggestion: strengthen theoretical analysis with additional mathematical insight into gradient preservation.
