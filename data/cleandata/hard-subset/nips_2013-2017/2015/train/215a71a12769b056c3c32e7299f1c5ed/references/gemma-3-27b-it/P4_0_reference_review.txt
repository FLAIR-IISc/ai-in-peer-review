This paper proposes “Highway Networks,” a novel architecture modification for feedforward networks designed to ease training of very deep networks. The core idea is to introduce “highway” connections with gating mechanisms inspired by LSTMs, allowing information to flow across many layers without attenuation. The authors demonstrate, through extensive experimentation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, that these networks can be trained to significantly greater depths than plain networks using standard SGD, and achieve competitive performance.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and addresses a significant challenge in deep learning – the difficulty of training very deep feedforward networks. The authors establish a clear connection to previous work on recurrent networks (LSTMs) and discuss relevant approaches like improved optimizers, initialization strategies, skip connections, and knowledge distillation. The mathematical formulation of the highway layer is concise and easy to understand. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering different datasets and comparing highway networks to both plain networks and existing state-of-the-art methods like FitNets. The analysis of the transform gate biases and layer importance provides valuable insights into the behavior of these networks.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture is a novel approach to addressing the vanishing gradient problem in deep feedforward networks. While inspired by LSTMs, applying this gating mechanism to feedforward networks is a significant contribution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation is sound and the experimental results strongly support the claims made by the authors. The comparisons to plain networks and other existing techniques are well-designed and convincing.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The authors clearly explain the motivation behind their work, the technical details of the highway network architecture, and the results of their experiments.
*   **Significance:** The ability to train significantly deeper networks opens up possibilities for improved performance on a variety of tasks. The analysis of layer importance is also a valuable contribution to understanding how these networks function.
*   **Thoroughness:** Extensive experiments were conducted on a range of datasets and architectures, and ablation studies analyzing gate biases and layer contribution were performed.
*   **Availability:** The authors publicly released their code and hyperparameter search results, promoting reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Although the paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, the sensitivity of the results to the initial bias of the transform gates could be further explored. A more systematic analysis of the effect of this parameter on performance would be beneficial.
*   **Computational Cost:** While highway networks improve trainability, the added parameters in the transform gates increase the computational cost compared to plain networks. A more detailed analysis of this trade-off would be valuable.
*   **Limited Exploration of Gate Function:** The paper focuses on the sigmoid activation function for the transform gates. Exploring other activation functions or more complex gating mechanisms could potentially lead to further improvements.
*   **CIFAR-100 comparison**: The comparison to state-of-the-art CIFAR-100 methods seems limited and doesn't offer much depth. It relies on a relatively basic comparison.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks.
*   The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate a significant improvement over existing approaches.
*   The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to understand.
*   The code and hyperparameters are publicly available, promoting reproducibility.
*   The analysis of layer importance provides valuable insights into the behavior of these networks.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The hyperparameter sensitivity of the results could be a concern.
*   The computational cost of highway networks is potentially higher than that of plain networks.
*   The limited exploration of alternative gate functions might leave room for further improvement.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway network architecture is a novel and effective solution to the problem of training very deep feedforward networks. The experimental results are compelling and the paper is well-written and clear. The weaknesses identified are relatively minor and do not detract from the overall quality of the work. I strongly recommend accepting this paper to NIPS.
