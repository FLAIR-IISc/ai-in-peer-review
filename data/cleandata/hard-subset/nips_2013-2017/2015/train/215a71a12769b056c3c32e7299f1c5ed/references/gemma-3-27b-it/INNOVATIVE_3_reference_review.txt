This paper introduces Highway Networks, a novel architectural modification to deep feedforward networks inspired by LSTM gating mechanisms, aimed at easing the training of very deep networks. The core idea – allowing information to flow more easily through layers via adaptive gating – is compelling and represents a significant departure from standard network architectures and recent trends like FitNets. The authors demonstrate successful training of networks with hundreds of layers, a feat previously difficult to achieve with standard techniques. The paper is well-structured and the experiments, while not exhaustive, provide convincing evidence of the approach's effectiveness.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The concept of highway networks is genuinely novel. While inspired by LSTMs, the adaptation to feedforward networks is non-trivial and offers a potentially powerful solution to the vanishing gradient problem in deep networks.
*   **Significance:** Successfully training networks with 100+ layers opens up exciting possibilities for exploring the benefits of extreme depth. This addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning and has the potential to impact various applications.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The results on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 are promising, demonstrating that highway networks can be trained effectively and achieve competitive performance. The comparison to FitNets is particularly insightful.
*   **Analysis:** The ablation studies examining layer importance (Figure 4) and information routing (Figure 3) provide valuable insights into the behavior of highway networks.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The mathematical notation is clear and the explanations are concise.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the authors draw an analogy to LSTMs, a deeper theoretical understanding of *why* highway networks work so well would be beneficial. A more rigorous analysis of the gradient flow through these networks, beyond the Jacobian observation, would strengthen the paper.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The initialization scheme for the transform gates seems important, but the discussion of hyperparameter tuning is brief. A more detailed analysis of sensitivity to different initialization values and learning rates would be valuable.
*   **Comparison to Baselines:** The comparison to plain networks is strong, but comparing to more recent optimization techniques (e.g., Adam, RMSprop) with appropriate tuning could further highlight the advantages of highway networks.
*   **Computational Cost:** While the paper demonstrates successful training, the computational cost of training very deep networks, even with highway connections, remains a concern. A discussion of the practical limitations of scaling this approach to even larger networks would be helpful.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a genuinely novel and potentially impactful idea.
*   The experimental results provide strong evidence that highway networks can overcome the challenges of training very deep networks.
*   The analysis provides valuable insights into the behavior of these networks.
*   The paper addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical justification for the success of highway networks is somewhat lacking.
*   The hyperparameter sensitivity of the approach requires further investigation.
*   The computational cost of training very deep networks remains a practical concern.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of deep learning. While some theoretical and practical questions remain, the authors have demonstrated a compelling proof-of-concept. The potential impact of this work is substantial, and I believe it warrants acceptance despite minor weaknesses. The novelty and boldness of the approach outweigh the lack of complete theoretical justification. I recommend acceptance.
