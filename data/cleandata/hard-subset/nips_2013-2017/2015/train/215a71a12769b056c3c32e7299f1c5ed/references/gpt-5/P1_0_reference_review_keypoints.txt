- Proposal: Highway Networks add elementwise, input-dependent gates (y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x))) to feedforward layers to ease information and gradient flow across many layers.  
- Key practical trick: initialize transform-gate bias negatively so networks start biased toward carry behavior, enabling deep nets to train with plain SGD.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on LSTM gating ideas and addresses vanishing/exploding gradients, complementing skip connections, deep supervision, and advanced optimizers.  
- Differentiation: contrasts with FitNets by training thin, deep networks from scratch in one stage; emphasizes learnable routing versus fixed shortcuts.  
- Technical soundness: layer is simple, differentiable, well defined; Jacobian analysis and negative-bias training guidance are sensible and validated.  
- Empirical support: optimization study on MNIST (10–100 layers) shows highway nets maintain performance and converge faster than plain nets.  
- Empirical support (cont.): CIFAR-10/100 results are competitive at similar parameter/compute budgets without teacher guidance; analyses (gate evolution, lesioning) illuminate depth usage.  
- Evaluation weaknesses: lacks direct comparison to identity (ungated) skip connections and no ablation of C=1−T versus a free carry gate.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: gate bias sensitivity only partially explored; activation choices and gate-init sensitivity need more analysis.  
- Computational cost: parameter/FLOP/memory/runtime overhead from gates is not thoroughly quantified.  
- Clarity: well written and organized; training recipe and experiments are clear, visualizations informative; some architectural details should be added.  
- Originality: novel application of LSTM-like gating to feedforward depth and a practical initialization strategy enabling very deep training.  
- Significance: important practical advance—allows very deep networks to be trained with off-the-shelf optimizers, likely to influence future architectures.  
- Strengths: simple, general modification; effective negative-bias initialization; robust optimization results; competitive CIFAR performance; insightful analyses and released code.  
- Weaknesses: missing key ablations/comparisons, limited sensitivity analysis, unquantified computational overhead, and no large-scale (ImageNet) experiments.  
- Suggested experiments: compare to identity skips/deep supervision, ablate C=1−T vs learned C, report params/FLOPs/latency, expand gate-bias sensitivity study, and add preliminary ImageNet tests.  
- Requested clarifications: sensitivity of performance to initial gate bias; experiments with independent carry gate or constraints; comparison to ungated identity shortcuts; exact computational overhead; potential structured gating (channel-wise vs element-wise).  
- Recommendation: Accept (strong) — clear, novel, practically impactful work with solid evidence and useful analyses.
