## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel neural network architecture inspired by LSTM gating mechanisms, designed to facilitate the training of extremely deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to introduce "transform" and "carry" gates at each layer, allowing information to flow more easily across many layers without attenuation – creating “information highways.” The authors demonstrate through experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 that highway networks can be trained with standard SGD to significantly greater depths than traditional ("plain") networks, achieving competitive performance, and demonstrating better generalization.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a Core Challenge:** The paper tackles a fundamental problem in deep learning: the difficulty of training very deep feedforward networks. Vanishing/exploding gradients are a well-known issue, and this work proposes a compelling architectural solution.
* **Novelty:** The introduction of highway networks, with their dynamically adjustable information flow, represents a novel approach. While inspired by LSTMs, the application to feedforward networks and the resulting architectural modifications are significant.  The adaptive gating mechanism is a clever idea.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experiments presented convincingly demonstrate the benefits of highway networks. The results on MNIST, particularly the comparison with plain networks at increasing depths (Figure 1), are compelling.  The performance on CIFAR-10/100 is competitive, showcasing the practical applicability of the approach.
* **Thorough Analysis:** The analysis section (Section 4) goes beyond simply presenting results. The investigation of gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance provides valuable insights into how highway networks function and why they are effective.  This is a major strength.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The notation is well-defined, and the explanations are easy to follow.  The motivation and contributions are clearly stated.
* **Reproducibility:** The authors make their code and hyperparameter search results publicly available, fostering reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the paper provides some intuition for why highway networks work, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the gradient flow and information propagation would be beneficial.  The explanation regarding Jacobian is a good start, but doesn't fully explain *why* this architecture avoids gradient problems as effectively as it does.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper acknowledges that the initial bias for the transform gates is a hyperparameter, but a more detailed discussion of the sensitivity of performance to this and other hyperparameters would be valuable. How generalizable are the suggested initialization values?
* **Comparison to Recent Advances:** While the authors reference relevant work, the field of deep learning is moving rapidly. A discussion of how highway networks compare to more recent advances in architectural design (e.g., ResNets, DenseNets) would strengthen the paper.  ResNets, in particular, offer a similar solution to the vanishing gradient problem through skip connections; a detailed comparison would be insightful.
* **Figure Resolution:** Some figures (particularly Figure 2) have low resolution and are difficult to interpret.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. It addresses a fundamental problem with a novel and effective solution, supported by strong empirical results and thoughtful analysis.  While a more rigorous theoretical treatment and a more detailed discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity would be beneficial, these weaknesses are relatively minor.  The originality, clarity, and significance of this work clearly place it within the scope of NIPS.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Solves a major problem in deep learning: training very deep networks.
* Introduces a novel and effective architecture (highway networks).
* Provides strong empirical evidence of the benefits of highway networks.
* Offers valuable insights into the inner workings of these networks.
* Well-written, clear, and reproducible.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Lacks a comprehensive theoretical analysis.
* Could benefit from a more thorough comparison to recent architectural advances like ResNets.
* Limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity.




**Rating:**  **Strong Accept (8/10)**. This paper is a valuable contribution to the field and is highly likely to be influential.
