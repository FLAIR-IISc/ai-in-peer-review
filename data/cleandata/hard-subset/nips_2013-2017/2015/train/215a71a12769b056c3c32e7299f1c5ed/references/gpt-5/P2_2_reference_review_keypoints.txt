- Proposes Highway Networks: per-layer input-dependent gates blending transformed H(x) and carry x via y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)), inspired by LSTM gating to preserve activations/gradients across depth.  
- Experiments: controlled MNIST optimization (plain vs highway up to 100 layers), convolutional highway MNIST models, CIFAR-10/100 comparisons (single-stage training vs FitNets), and gate behavior + lesioning analyses.  
- Novelty: input-dependent, learnable gating across depth (adaptive per input/unit), distinguishing it from fixed skip-connections and multi-stage distillation approaches.  

- Technical soundness: architecture and gate biasing (negative b) are well specified and plausibly explain trainability via Jacobian ≈ identity when T→0.  
- Empirical support: careful MNIST controlled studies show convergence advantages for highway nets as depth increases; CIFAR results are competitive under limited parameter budgets and single-stage training.  
- Analysis: gate visualizations and lesioning demonstrate data-dependent routing and depth utilization differences across tasks.  
- Missing empirical/theoretical work: no formal gradient-preservation theory, limited ablations (gate bias, tying C=1−T), absent key baselines (batch norm, fixed skips), and no large-scale datasets (e.g., ImageNet).  

- Clarity: paper is generally clear with actionable initialization guidance and informative figures, but some reproduction details are missing (exact conv architectures, data augmentation, compute/FLOPs, parameter counts).  

- Originality: novel application of LSTM-style gating across depth to enable adaptive routing; clearly positioned relative to distillation and hard-wired skips.  

- Significance: offers a simple, practical method to reliably train very deep networks without staged supervision or heavy engineering; gating enables dynamic computation and layer-importance analysis, likely to influence future architectures despite not being SOTA.  

- Strengths: simple intuitive architecture, strong optimization evidence, practical gate-bias training recipe, insightful analyses beyond accuracy, and single-stage training of deep thin models.  

- Weaknesses/suggestions: add baselines (batch norm, plain skip connections), more ablations (bias values, independent carry gate, parameter overhead), report FLOPs/params/train time for fair comparison, evaluate larger/deeper convolutional models, and include theoretical analysis of gradient dynamics.  

- Questions for authors: how do highway nets compare to plain skip connections under identical budgets; what's the quantitative effect of negative gate bias and does batch norm obviate it; have you tried independent carry gate C vs tying; what are runtime and memory overheads of gating?  

- Recommendation: leaning accept.
