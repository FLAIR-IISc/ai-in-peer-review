- Paper introduces Highway Networks, using LSTM-like transform and carry gates to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.
- Demonstrates effective training of >100-layer networks on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 with strong empirical results.
- Novelty: applies adaptive, data-dependent gating to feedforward nets, distinct from existing skip connections.
- Empirical results: consistently better or comparable performance and single-stage training advantage over FitNets.
- Theoretical justification: clear mathematical formulation and Jacobian analysis; insightful discussion on layer importance and information routing.
- Clarity and presentation: well-written, organized, with helpful visualizations and public code/hyperparameters.
- Addresses a core deep learning challenge by providing a practical solution to vanishing gradients in deep networks.
- Weakness: lacks deeper theoretical analysis of why Highway Networks work, especially gradient dynamics.
- Weakness: limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity beyond transform gate bias initialization.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to later skip-connection architectures like ResNets.
- Weakness: does not thoroughly analyze the computational cost of gating mechanisms relative to alternatives.
- Overall assessment: strong, original contribution with convincing experiments; weaknesses do not outweigh strengths.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles fundamental problem, novel effective architecture, strong experiments, clear presentation, reproducible code.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient theoretical depth, limited comparison to recent architectures, incomplete hyperparameter sensitivity study.
- Rating: Strong Accept (8/10).
