## Peer Review: Training Very Deep Networks

**Summary:**

This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel neural network architecture inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to incorporate "information highways" – adaptive gating mechanisms – that allow for easier information flow across many layers, mitigating the vanishing/exploding gradient problems that plague traditional deep networks. The authors demonstrate through experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 that Highway Networks can be trained to significantly greater depths (over 100 layers) than plain networks, achieving competitive or superior performance, even without relying on techniques like FitNets' two-stage training.  The paper also includes an analysis of the learned gate behavior and layer importance, offering insights into how these networks function.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly identifies the increasing importance of network depth in achieving state-of-the-art results in supervised learning (citing Krizhevsky et al., Szegedy et al., Simonyan & Zisserman). It acknowledges prior work addressing the training difficulties of deep networks, including improved optimizers (Martens et al., Dauphin et al.), initialization strategies (Glorot & Bengio, He et al., Sussillo & Abbott), activation functions (Srivastava et al., Raiko et al.), skip connections (Ciresan et al., Graham), and knowledge distillation (Romero et al., Hochreiter & Schmidhuber).  The inspiration from LSTM networks (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber) is explicitly stated, framing Highway Networks as an adaptation of LSTM's gating mechanisms to the feedforward setting. It also appropriately positions the work in relation to FitNets (Romero et al.) demonstrating comparable performance with a potentially simpler approach.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The Highway Network architecture is a relatively simple yet effective modification to standard feedforward networks that directly addresses the problem of training very deep networks. The adaptation of LSTM-style gating to feedforward networks is a creative approach.
* **Empirical Results:** The experimental results are compelling.  The paper convincingly demonstrates that Highway Networks can be trained to depths where plain networks fail, achieving strong performance on multiple datasets. The comparisons to FitNets and state-of-the-art methods are well-executed.
* **Analysis:** The analysis of the learned gate biases and layer importance (Figures 2 & 4) provides valuable insights into the network's internal workings and strengthens the argument that these layers are indeed contributing to the computation, not just being bypassed.
* **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the Highway Network architecture and experimental setup. The mathematical notation is clear and concise.
* **Code Availability:** Making the code and hyperparameters publicly available is a significant strength, fostering reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Justification:** While the LSTM inspiration is mentioned, there isn’t a strong theoretical justification for *why* these gates work so well in the feedforward context.  A deeper exploration of the gradient flow properties of Highway Networks compared to plain networks would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper acknowledges that the initial bias for the transform gates is a hyperparameter. While guidelines are provided, a more systematic investigation of its impact on performance would be helpful.
* **Simplified Implementation:** The authors acknowledge using a simplified block design. While understandable for initial exploration, it's unclear how well the results generalize to more complex gating mechanisms resembling a full LSTM block as mentioned in the introduction.
* **Comparison Depth:** The comparison to plain networks seems somewhat limited in depth.  While the paper demonstrates a clear performance difference, testing a broader range of depths for both architectures, and perhaps tuning the plain network architectures more aggressively, could further solidify the advantages of Highway Networks.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks.
* The experimental results are strong and demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods.
* The analysis provides valuable insights into the network's behavior.
* The code availability promotes reproducibility and further research.
* The work addresses a significant challenge in deep learning – training very deep networks – and offers a promising solution.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of a strong theoretical justification for the success of Highway Networks.
* The limited investigation of hyperparameter sensitivity.
* The relatively simplified implementation of the gating mechanism.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning. The Highway Network architecture is a promising approach for training very deep networks, and the experimental results are compelling.  While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I believe this paper is a worthy addition to NIPS and recommend **acceptance**.

**Rating:** Strong Accept.
