This paper introduces Highway Networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate training of very deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to incorporate gating mechanisms, inspired by LSTMs, that allow for easier information flow across layers, mitigating the vanishing/exploding gradient problem. The authors demonstrate through experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 that these networks can be trained to depths previously unattainable with standard backpropagation, achieving competitive or superior results compared to existing methods.

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the motivation, methodology, and experimental results. The introduction effectively highlights the challenges of training deep networks and positions Highway Networks as a potential solution. The mathematical formulation of the highway layer is concise and easy to understand. The experimental section is comprehensive, comparing Highway Networks to plain networks, FitNets, and state-of-the-art methods. The analysis section provides valuable insights into the inner workings of these networks, including the learned gate biases and layer importance.  The inclusion of publicly available code and hyperparameters further strengthens the reproducibility of the work.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The introduction of highway layers, incorporating LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms into feedforward networks, is a genuinely novel contribution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation is clear and consistent. The experiments demonstrate that the proposed architecture addresses the challenges of training very deep networks. The analysis section supports the claims with empirical evidence.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The concepts are explained in a clear and accessible manner.
*   **Significance:** The ability to train significantly deeper networks opens up possibilities for improved performance on complex tasks and provides a new tool for exploring the benefits of depth in neural networks.
*   **Reproducibility:** Publicly available code and hyperparameters enhance the reproducibility of the research.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper addresses initialization and bias, the sensitivity of performance to other hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, momentum) is not thoroughly investigated.  The search space for these parameters is mentioned, but a detailed analysis of their impact is lacking.
*   **Limited Comparison to Recent Architectures:** While comparisons to FitNets and previous methods are included, the landscape of deep learning architectures is rapidly evolving. A more comprehensive comparison to recent advances, such as ResNets [He et al., 2016], would further strengthen the paper’s impact.  The authors acknowledge this to an extent in the discussion, but a direct empirical comparison would be more convincing.
*   **Gating Mechanism Interpretation:**  The analysis of the gating mechanism is insightful, but further investigation into *why* certain layers are deemed more important than others could be valuable.  Is this specific to the dataset or a general property of deep highway networks?
*   **Computational Cost:** While the paper demonstrates that highway networks can be trained, it doesn’t provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost associated with the increased number of parameters and operations.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to training very deep networks.
*   The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed architecture.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.
*   The availability of code and hyperparameters promotes reproducibility.
*   The work has the potential to significantly impact the field of deep learning.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The hyperparameter sensitivity analysis is limited.
*   The comparison to recent architectures could be more comprehensive.
*   The interpretation of the gating mechanism could be further explored.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed Highway Networks offer a promising approach to overcoming the challenges of training very deep feedforward networks. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper far outweigh them.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above, specifically a more detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and a comparison to ResNets.
