### **Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"**

#### **Summary**

This paper addresses the significant challenge of training very deep feedforward neural networks. The authors identify that increasing network depth in conventional ("plain") architectures leads to optimization difficulties, primarily due to poor propagation of signals and gradients. To overcome this, they propose a new architecture called "Highway Networks," inspired by the gating mechanisms in Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks. The core idea is to introduce adaptive "transform" and "carry" gates at each layer. These gates dynamically control the flow of information, allowing a layer to either transform its input, as in a plain network, or pass it through unchanged. This creates unimpeded "information highways" that allow gradients to flow across many layers. The authors demonstrate empirically that Highway Networks with up to 100 layers can be trained effectively using standard SGD, whereas plain networks of similar depth fail to converge. They show that this architecture achieves competitive or state-of-the-art results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, and provide an insightful analysis of the learned gate behaviors.

#### **Strengths**

1.  **Significance and Impact:** The paper tackles a fundamental and timely problem. At the time of writing, effectively training networks beyond a few dozen layers was a major bottleneck for the field. By providing a simple and effective architectural solution, this work opens the door to exploring the benefits of much greater network depth, which has proven to be a critical factor in the success of modern deep learning. The proposed idea is likely to be highly influential and widely adopted.

2.  **Novelty and Elegance of the Approach:** The core contribution—applying an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism to a feedforward architecture—is novel, elegant, and well-motivated. While skip connections existed, making them adaptive and data-dependent via a learned gate is a powerful extension. The formulation `y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1 - T(x))` is clean and provides a smooth interpolation between a standard transformation and an identity mapping, directly addressing the information flow problem.

3.  **Strong and Convincing Empirical Evidence:** The experimental section is thorough and provides compelling support for the paper's claims.
    *   The optimization experiment in Section 3.1 is the paper's centerpiece. The direct comparison of training curves for plain vs. highway networks of increasing depth (up to 100 layers) on MNIST is a clear and dramatic demonstration of the architecture's effectiveness. It leaves no doubt that highway networks solve the optimization problem that plagues their plain counterparts.
    *   The experiments on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 show that the benefits are not limited to a simple dataset or fully-connected architectures. The ability to train deep convolutional highway networks in a single stage, outperforming the more complex two-stage "FitNets" approach, underscores the practical utility of the method.
    *   The performance is competitive with state-of-the-art methods, proving that the ease of training does not come at the cost of generalization.

4.  **Insightful Analysis:** Section 4 goes beyond simply reporting performance metrics and attempts to understand *how* the highway networks function. The visualizations of gate activations and the analysis of data-dependent routing are particularly insightful. The lesion study in Figure 4 provides a novel way to quantify the contribution of each layer, demonstrating that for complex tasks like CIFAR-100, the network learns to utilize its depth, while for simpler tasks like MNIST, it effectively learns to "switch off" unnecessary layers. This adds significant depth and understanding to the contribution.

#### **Weaknesses**

1.  **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper introduces a key initialization trick: setting the transform gate biases to negative values (e.g., -1 to -3) to encourage "carry" behavior at the start of training. While the authors acknowledge this is a hyperparameter and provide a general guideline, the work would be strengthened by a more detailed sensitivity analysis. It is unclear how critical the exact choice of this bias is and whether the training is robust to different initialization schemes.

2.  **Relationship to Fixed Skip Connections:** The paper mentions skip connections in the introduction but could benefit from a more explicit discussion of the relationship between its learnable gates and hard-wired residual connections (as in the concurrent work on Residual Networks). While the flexibility of adaptive gates is an advantage, a direct empirical comparison to a network with fixed identity skip connections would have been a valuable addition to contextualize the benefits of the gating mechanism itself.

3.  **Computational Overhead:** The highway layers introduce additional parameters and computations for the transform gates (`WT`, `bT`). While this overhead seems modest, the paper does not explicitly quantify it in terms of parameter count or FLOPs relative to a plain network of the same size. A brief discussion of this trade-off would make the evaluation more complete.

#### **Evaluation against NIPS Criteria**

*   **Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality. The claims are well-supported by rigorous and well-designed experiments. The inclusion of an analysis section to interpret the model's behavior demonstrates a commitment to understanding beyond just achieving a high score. The work is complete and scientifically sound.

*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The core concept is introduced clearly with simple equations and strong motivation. The experimental setup and results are presented in a way that is easy to follow. The public release of code further enhances clarity and reproducibility.

*   **Originality:** The work is highly original. While it draws inspiration from LSTMs, its application to deep feedforward architectures to solve the gradient flow problem is a novel and significant conceptual leap. It clearly distinguishes itself from prior work on initialization, optimization, and fixed skip connections.

*   **Significance:** The significance of this work is extremely high. It provides a practical and powerful solution to one of the most important problems in deep learning at the time. The ability to train networks of hundreds of layers directly with SGD is a breakthrough that paves the way for a new generation of deeper, more powerful models. The ideas presented are foundational and are very likely to be built upon by many other researchers.

#### **Recommendation**

This is an outstanding paper that makes a clear, novel, and highly significant contribution to the field. The proposed Highway Network architecture elegantly solves a critical optimization problem, is backed by strong empirical evidence, and is presented with exceptional clarity. The strengths of the paper far outweigh its minor weaknesses. I strongly recommend acceptance.
