

# Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

## Summary
This paper introduces "highway networks," a novel architecture designed to enable the direct training of extremely deep feedforward networks using stochastic gradient descent. The authors propose an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism that allows information to flow across layers with minimal attenuation, creating "information highways." The approach is distinct from previous methods like skip connections (which are fixed) and teacher-student distillation (which requires multi-stage training). The paper demonstrates that highway networks can be trained to depths of 100+ layers without performance degradation, outperforming plain networks by orders of magnitude on MNIST and CIFAR datasets.

## Strengths

**Quality:** The paper provides rigorous experimental validation across multiple datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100) with careful hyperparameter tuning. The ablation studies (e.g., lesioning experiments in Section 4.2) convincingly demonstrate that highway networks effectively utilize their depth, unlike plain networks. The analysis of transform gate behavior (Figures 2-4) provides valuable insights into the information routing mechanism.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The notation is consistent, the highway network architecture is clearly explained, and the experimental setup is thoroughly described. The figures effectively illustrate key concepts and results, making the paper accessible to readers familiar with deep learning.

**Significance:** The paper addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning—training very deep networks—which has significant implications for the field. The ability to train networks with hundreds of layers directly with SGD opens new possibilities for architectural exploration and could impact both research and applications.

## Weaknesses

**Theoretical Justification:** While the paper provides strong empirical evidence, it lacks a compelling theoretical explanation for why highway networks facilitate training. The Jacobian analysis (Equation 5) is insufficient to explain the observed optimization benefits. A more rigorous theoretical discussion of the optimization landscape would strengthen the paper.

**Comparison to Recent Work:** The paper misses a critical comparison to ResNets (He et al., 2015), which was published around the same time and also uses skip connections to train very deep networks. The paper mentions "similar LSTM-inspired model" [32] but fails to acknowledge that ResNets were published at the same time as this work (2015). This omission weakens the novelty claim.

**Computational Cost Analysis:** The paper claims highway networks have similar parameter counts to plain networks but doesn't analyze the computational cost. How much additional computation does the gating mechanism require? The paper should provide a detailed comparison of training time and memory requirements between highway and plain networks.

**Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the authors suggest specific bias values for transform gates (-1, -2, -3), they don't systematically explore how sensitive performance is to this choice. A sensitivity analysis would help readers understand the practical requirements of the approach.

## Recommendations for Improvement

1. **Add comparison to ResNets:** The paper should directly compare highway networks to ResNets, which were published in the same year (2015). This would clarify the relationship between the two approaches and strengthen the novelty claim. The authors should acknowledge ResNets' similar contribution while highlighting differences in architecture and behavior.

2. **Expand theoretical discussion:** Include a more thorough theoretical analysis of why highway networks improve optimization. Consider analyzing the condition number of the Jacobian or providing convergence guarantees under specific conditions.

3. **Provide computational cost analysis:** Include a detailed comparison of training time, memory usage, and FLOPs between highway and plain networks. This would help readers understand the practical trade-offs of the approach.

4. **Conduct hyperparameter sensitivity analysis:** Systematically test how performance varies with different initial bias values for the transform gates. This would help establish practical guidelines for using highway networks.

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper with significant experimental contributions to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is novel, well-motivated, and empirically validated across multiple benchmarks. However, the paper's novelty is somewhat diminished by the omission of comparison to ResNets, which was published concurrently and addresses the same problem with a different approach. The theoretical justification for the method is also underdeveloped.

The paper would be significantly strengthened by addressing these issues. With these improvements, the paper would make a valuable contribution to the field by providing a clear, well-justified, and practically useful approach to training extremely deep networks.

## Recommendation
I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper has strong experimental evidence and addresses an important problem in deep learning. The main issue is the lack of comparison to ResNets, which should be added to clarify the relationship between these two approaches. The theoretical justification could also be strengthened. These revisions would significantly enhance the paper's impact and novelty.
