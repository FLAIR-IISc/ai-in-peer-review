- Paper introduces "highway networks" with transform/carry gates to enable training very deep (100+) feedforward networks by preserving information flow.  
- Strength: Well-designed architecture with strong empirical validation across MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 and informative ablation studies.  
- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with helpful figures and reproducible experimental details.  
- Strength: Original idea at the time—LSTM-inspired gating applied to feedforward depth is novel.  
- Strength: Demonstrates practical significance by training much deeper networks and matching/exceeding shallower baselines.  
- Weakness: Fails to acknowledge or compare with contemporaneous residual networks (He et al., 2015), which address the same problem with simpler, more effective skip connections.  
- Weakness: Limited theoretical analysis—insufficient justification of why highway networks work beyond basic Jacobian discussion.  
- Weakness: Significance is reduced by residual networks; paper does not show advantage over them.  
- Recommendation: Reject in current form due to critical omission of comparison to residual networks.  
- Suggestion: Add acknowledgment and thorough empirical/theoretical comparison with residual networks.  
- Suggestion: Provide stronger theoretical analysis of information flow and convergence properties.  
- Suggestion: Expand experiments to directly compare highway and residual networks on standard benchmarks.  
- Suggestion: Clarify relationship to other recent deep learning advances and position the work accordingly.  
- Conclusion: Technically sound and well-executed but critically undermined by omission of residual-network comparison; needs substantial revision to be publishable.
