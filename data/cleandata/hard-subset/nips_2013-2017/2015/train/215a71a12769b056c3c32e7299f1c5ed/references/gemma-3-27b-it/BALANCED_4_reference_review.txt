This paper proposes “highway networks,” a novel architecture for training very deep feedforward networks, inspired by the gating mechanisms of LSTMs. The core idea is to introduce “information highways” through adaptive gating, allowing gradients and activations to flow more easily across many layers. The authors demonstrate through experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 that highway networks can be trained effectively to depths exceeding 100 layers using standard SGD, a feat challenging for traditional “plain” networks. The paper is well-written and addresses a significant problem in deep learning: the difficulty of training very deep feedforward networks.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture represents a conceptually simple yet effective modification to standard feedforward networks. The LSTM-inspired gating mechanism is a clever way to address vanishing gradient problems and improve information flow.
*   **Empirical Results:** The experimental results are compelling. The paper convincingly demonstrates that highway networks can be trained to much greater depths than plain networks without significant performance degradation. The comparisons to FitNets and state-of-the-art methods are insightful. The ablation studies, particularly Figure 4 illustrating layer importance, are valuable.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to understand. The notation is clear, and the explanation of the highway network concept is intuitive.
*   **Thoroughness:** The authors conduct a thorough series of experiments, exploring different datasets, architectures, and hyperparameters.  The analysis of the gating mechanism’s behavior (Figures 2 & 3) adds depth to the understanding of how highway networks function.
*   **Reproducibility:** Providing the source code and hyperparameter search results greatly enhances reproducibility, which is commendable.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the paper provides intuition for why highway networks work, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the gradient flow or expressivity of these networks would be beneficial.  The connection to LSTM's success, beyond the inspiration, is not deeply explored.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The initial bias for the transform gate appears to be a crucial hyperparameter. More discussion on how to effectively tune this parameter in different settings would be helpful. While guidelines are provided, a more systematic investigation might reveal optimal ranges.
*   **Comparison to Residual Networks:** The paper does not thoroughly discuss or compare to more recent architectures like Residual Networks (ResNets) [He et al., 2016]. ResNets, published concurrently and independently, also address the vanishing gradient problem and enable the training of very deep networks via skip connections. Highlighting the distinctions and potential tradeoffs between highway networks and ResNets would strengthen the paper. (Though the authors couldn't have known about ResNets at submission time, it's a valid point for consideration in a revised version.)
*   **Limited Ablation Studies:** While Figure 4 is a great start, further ablation studies exploring the impact of different gating mechanisms or architectural choices (e.g., different placement of highway layers) could provide more insights.
*   **CIFAR-100 Results:** The CIFAR-100 results are presented, but lack a detailed comparison to established, strong baselines beyond FitNets.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper proposes a novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks, addressing a core challenge in deep learning.
*   The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods for training deep feedforward networks.
*   The paper is well-written, clear, and provides sufficient details for reproducibility.
*   The analysis of the gating mechanism offers valuable insights into the network's behavior.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of theoretical analysis limits the understanding of *why* highway networks work so well.
*   The comparison to ResNets (a concurrent work) is missing, hindering a full assessment of the contribution.
*   The hyperparameter sensitivity regarding the transform gate bias requires further investigation.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. While the lack of extensive theoretical analysis and comparison to ResNets are weaknesses, the novelty of the approach, the compelling experimental results, and the clarity of the presentation outweigh these concerns. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NeurIPS, potentially with a request for minor revisions to address the points mentioned above. The authors should address the relation of their work to ResNets and expand the discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity.



**Recommendation:** Accept (with minor revisions)
