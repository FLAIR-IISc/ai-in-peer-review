

# Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces "highway networks," a novel architectural modification to deep feedforward neural networks that enables training of extremely deep networks (100+ layers) directly with stochastic gradient descent. The key innovation is an LSTM-inspired adaptive gating mechanism that creates "information highways" allowing information to flow across many layers without attenuation. The authors demonstrate that highway networks can be trained more effectively than plain networks, with 100-layer highway networks performing orders of magnitude better than similarly-sized plain networks on MNIST. They also show that highway networks can match or exceed the performance of state-of-the-art methods on CIFAR-10/CIFAR-100 with fewer parameters.

The paper builds upon previous work on skip connections [22, 23, 24, 25] and LSTM networks [29, 30], but makes a significant conceptual leap by introducing a continuous gating mechanism that allows layers to dynamically control information flow rather than using fixed skip connections. This is distinct from the later ResNet approach, which uses identity mappings as a special case of the highway network's gating mechanism (though ResNets were published after this work).

**Strengths:**
- **High novelty:** The highway network concept is genuinely novel and bold, addressing a fundamental challenge in deep learning (training very deep networks) with a clean architectural innovation. This represents a significant conceptual advance rather than an incremental improvement.
- **Strong empirical validation:** The experiments clearly demonstrate the benefits of highway networks, especially at extreme depths. The 100-layer highway network outperforming plain networks by orders of magnitude is a compelling result.
- **Clear exposition:** The paper is well-written, with consistent notation and helpful figures that illustrate the "information highways" concept.
- **Practical significance:** The approach enables direct training of extremely deep networks without two-stage procedures, potentially simplifying deep learning workflows.

**Weaknesses:**
- **Limited scope:** The experiments are restricted to MNIST and CIFAR datasets; more diverse experiments (e.g., on ImageNet or other challenging tasks) would strengthen the claims.
- **Lack of theoretical analysis:** While the empirical results are convincing, the paper doesn't provide theoretical justification for why the highway networks work so well.
- **Comparison to ResNets:** The paper predates ResNets (2015), so it doesn't discuss the relationship to this influential work. However, the highway network concept is essentially the generalization from which ResNets emerged (with ResNets using the special case where the transform gate is always 0).

**Originality:** The highway network architecture represents a significant conceptual advance over existing approaches to deep network training. While inspired by skip connections and LSTMs, the specific gating mechanism with continuous transform gates is novel. The paper clearly differentiates itself from previous work and presents a new direction for deep learning research.

**Significance:** This paper addresses a fundamental problem in deep learning with a simple yet powerful solution. The ability to train extremely deep networks directly with SGD has major implications for the field, as it removes a significant barrier to exploring deeper architectures. The highway network concept has directly influenced subsequent work, including ResNets, which became one of the most influential deep learning architectures. The paper makes a clear contribution to understanding how to train very deep networks.

**Quality:** The methodology is sound, the experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincing. The authors are careful to acknowledge limitations (e.g., the need for negative bias initialization) and discuss the behavior of the transform gates.

**Recommendation:** I recommend acceptance. This paper introduces a bold, novel approach that addresses a significant problem in deep learning with clear empirical validation. The highway network concept represents a genuine conceptual advance that has influenced subsequent work (including ResNets) and enables new research directions. While the experimental scope is somewhat limited, the core idea is sufficiently novel and impactful to warrant acceptance at NIPS. The paper's primary contribution is the conceptual framework of highway networks, which stands on its own merits even if the specific implementation has been superseded by ResNets.

The paper's significance is particularly high because it tackles a fundamental challenge in deep learning (training very deep networks) with a simple yet powerful architectural insight. This is exactly the type of bold, novel contribution that NIPS values most highly. The fact that the highway network concept directly inspired ResNets, which became one of the most influential deep learning architectures, further underscores the paper's importance. While the paper could benefit from more diverse experiments, the core idea is sufficiently strong to justify acceptance.
