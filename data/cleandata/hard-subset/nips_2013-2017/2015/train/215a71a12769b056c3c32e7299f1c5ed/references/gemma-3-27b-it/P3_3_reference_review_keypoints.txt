- Introduces Highway Networks using transform and carry gates to train very deep feedforward networks.  
- LSTM-inspired gating allows direct information flow, mitigating vanishing/exploding gradients.  
- Demonstrates effective training with standard SGD on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, achieving competitive performance.  
- Situates work within depth-focused literature and cites relevant prior techniques (optimizers, initialization, activations, skip connections).  
- Explicitly compares to FitNets and acknowledges related LSTM-inspired models.  
- Novel application of gating mechanisms to feedforward networks.  
- Comprehensive experiments show superior performance over plain networks as depth increases.  
- Analyses of transform gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance provide insight into network behavior and data-dependent routing.  
- Clear presentation and concise mathematical notation.  
- Code and hyperparameter search results released, enhancing reproducibility.  
- Lacks systematic investigation of sensitivity to transform gate bias and its interactions with other hyperparameters.  
- Does not thoroughly address computational cost and parameter overhead introduced by gating.  
- Simplifies gating with C = 1 - T; alternative gate formulations are not explored.  
- Limited comparison to more recent state-of-the-art architectures (e.g., ResNets, DenseNets).  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a core challenge, proposes a novel solution, strong empirical evidence, insightful analysis, and released code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient computational-cost analysis, inadequate hyperparameter study, and need for stronger SOTA comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; minor weaknesses do not outweigh strengths — Strong Accept (8/10).
