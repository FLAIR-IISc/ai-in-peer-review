

# Review: "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel architectural modification for deep neural networks that enables direct training of extremely deep networks (100+ layers) using standard SGD. The key innovation is an LSTM-inspired adaptive gating mechanism that allows information to flow across layers without attenuation, creating "information highways." The authors show that highway networks can train significantly deeper than plain networks while maintaining or improving performance on standard benchmarks.

The paper builds upon several strands of prior work: normalized initialization for shallow networks [16,17], skip connections [22,23,24], and LSTM networks [29,30]. However, it differs from previous approaches by introducing *adaptive* gating rather than fixed skip connections. The authors clearly position their work as addressing the fundamental problem of information flow in very deep networks, distinct from prior work on better optimizers or initialization strategies.

**Quality (4/5):** The paper is technically sound with well-designed experiments across multiple datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100). The comparison between highway and plain networks at varying depths (Figures 1, 3, 4) provides strong empirical evidence for the main claim. The Jacobian analysis (Equations 4-5) offers theoretical justification for the architecture's properties. However, the paper would benefit from a more thorough theoretical analysis of why highway networks train better than plain networks, particularly regarding the dynamics of gradient flow. The authors acknowledge the need for negative bias initialization as a hyperparameter, but don't explore how this interacts with different activation functions or network architectures.

**Clarity (5/5):** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The notation is consistent, the core idea is clearly explained in Section 2, and the experiments are well-structured. The figures (particularly Figures 2-4) effectively illustrate the behavior of highway networks and provide valuable insights into information routing. The writing style is precise and accessible to the target audience.

**Originality (4/5):** The highway network concept is genuinely novel at the time of submission, though it predates ResNets (which were published in 2015). The adaptive gating mechanism represents a significant conceptual advance over fixed skip connections. The paper clearly differentiates itself from prior work on skip connections (which are hard-wired) and other approaches to depth. While the paper doesn't explicitly cite ResNets (which would have been submitted around the same time), the highway network approach is distinct in its adaptive gating mechanism.

**Significance (4/5):** The problem of training very deep networks is highly significant in deep learning, as depth is a key factor in performance improvements. Highway networks provide a practical solution that enables direct training of extremely deep networks without complex two-stage procedures. The paper demonstrates that highway networks can match or exceed the performance of shallower networks with fewer parameters (Table 2), and the analysis of layer importance (Figure 4) offers valuable insights for understanding deep network computation. The approach has had lasting influence, as highway networks prefigure the adaptive gating mechanisms later used in ResNets and other architectures.

**Strengths:**
- Clear, well-executed experimental validation across multiple datasets
- Theoretical justification through Jacobian analysis
- Insightful analysis of transform gate behavior and information routing
- Demonstrated ability to train networks with 100+ layers directly
- Practical implementation details (e.g., initialization strategy)

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited theoretical analysis of why highway networks train better
- Doesn't compare to ResNets (which were published around the same time)
- The analysis of transform gate behavior (Figure 2) could be more detailed
- The paper doesn't fully explore the relationship between highway networks and residual connections

**Recommendation:** I recommend accepting this paper. It makes a significant contribution to the problem of training very deep networks, with clear experimental validation and a novel architectural insight. The highway network concept has proven to be influential, as it directly inspired subsequent work on residual networks. While the paper has some limitations in theoretical depth and comparison to ResNets (which weren't known at submission time), these don't undermine the core contribution. The work is of high quality, clearly written, and addresses a fundamental problem in deep learning with practical solutions.

This paper is a strong contribution to the field, and I believe it would have significant impact on both theoretical understanding and practical applications of deep learning. It advances the state of the art in training deep networks by providing a simple yet effective architectural solution that enables direct training of extremely deep networks without complex training procedures. The insights about information flow and layer importance are particularly valuable for understanding deep network behavior.

The paper meets all the NeurIPS review criteria well, with particular strength in clarity and significance. It's a solid, well-executed piece of work that would be valuable to the machine learning community.
