- Overall assessment: Highway networks provide a simple, effective solution to training very deep feedforward nets; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem addressed: Training very deep feedforward networks remains difficult despite optimizers and initializations.  
- Main idea: Introduce "transform" and "carry" gates, inspired by LSTMs, to dynamically route information and mitigate vanishing gradients.  
- Novelty: Adapting LSTM-like gating to feedforward networks is an insightful and practically useful contribution.  
- Empirical strength: Successfully trains networks over 1000 layers with strong MNIST and CIFAR results, outperforming plain nets.  
- Comparisons: Competitive with FitNets and other methods, often with fewer parameters.  
- Experimental validation: Thorough experiments, optimization studies, ablations, and provided code/hyperparameters support reproducibility.  
- Interpretability: Analysis of gate biases and layer importance offers insight into how highway networks work.  
- Theoretical gap: Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis connecting gating to optimization landscape improvements.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Transform gate bias initialization needs further systematic investigation.  
- Computational cost: Additional transform gates add parameters; FLOPs comparisons are missing.  
- Overfitting risk: Some layers appear unused, raising potential overfitting concerns on simple datasets.  
- Pro-acceptance points: Advances state-of-the-art, simple and effective, strong empirical evidence, clear writing, code available.  
- Con-acceptance points: Limited theory, possible hyperparameter sensitivity, increased computational cost.  
- Minor revisions suggested: Add FLOPs comparisons, expand analysis of transform gate bias, discuss overfitting/regularization, and cite related differentiable routing work.
