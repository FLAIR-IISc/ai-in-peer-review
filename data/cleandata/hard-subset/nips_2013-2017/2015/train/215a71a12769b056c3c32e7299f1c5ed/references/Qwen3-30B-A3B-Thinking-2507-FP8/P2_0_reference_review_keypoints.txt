- Introduces Highway Networks with gating y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)) to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Demonstrates effective training of 100+ layer networks; competitive results on MNIST, CIFAR-10/100 with fewer parameters.  
- Strong experimental design: proper hyperparameter tuning, multiple runs, clear baselines, and replication details.  
- Analysis reveals "information highways" and data-dependent routing, offering insight into layer importance and dynamics.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, notation, visualizations, and organization.  
- Significant contribution: shows deep networks can be trained directly with SGD without complex initialization or two-stage training.  
- Main originality issue: fails to contextualize or compare adequately to ResNets (skip connections y = F(x)+x), a contemporaneous and influential approach.  
- Theoretical depth is limited: Jacobian analysis is brief and does not fully explain optimization behavior or connections to other gating mechanisms.  
- Comparative analysis is incomplete—lacks direct comparison to ResNets and other top architectures (e.g., ImageNet results).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: add comparison/discussion of ResNets, clarify relationship to LSTM/other gates, and explain claimed advantages of highway gating.  
- Pros summary: strong empirical evidence, clarity, valuable insights into information routing, addresses a core deep-learning challenge.  
- Cons summary: poor contextualization relative to ResNets, limited theory, and incomplete comparative analysis.
