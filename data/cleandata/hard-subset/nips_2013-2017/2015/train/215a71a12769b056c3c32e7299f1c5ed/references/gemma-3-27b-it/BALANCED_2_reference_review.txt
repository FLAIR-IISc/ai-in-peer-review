This paper introduces Highway Networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward neural networks. The core idea is to incorporate “information highways” – LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms – within each layer, allowing for easier propagation of activations and gradients through a large number of layers. The authors demonstrate that highway networks can be trained effectively with standard SGD, even with depths exceeding 100 layers, outperforming plain networks which struggle with optimization at comparable depths. The work is well-motivated, addressing a crucial challenge in deep learning – training very deep networks – and offers a compelling solution.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important problem:** Training very deep networks remains a significant challenge in deep learning. The vanishing/exploding gradient problem continues to hinder the effective utilization of depth despite theoretical benefits.
* **Novelty and Insight:** The introduction of highway networks, inspired by LSTM gating mechanisms, is a clever and effective approach. This represents a novel architectural modification to standard feedforward networks.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results are convincing. The authors demonstrate the ability to train significantly deeper networks than previously possible with standard techniques, as shown in Figure 1 and Tables 1-3. The comparison to FitNets is particularly strong.
* **Thorough Evaluation:** The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, ranging from MNIST to CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100. The ablation studies (Figure 4) and analysis of gate behavior (Figure 2 & 3) provide valuable insights into the network's functioning.
* **Clear and Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the highway network architecture and the rationale behind the design choices. The notation is consistent and the figures are informative.
* **Code Availability:** The authors have made their code publicly available, which is a strong positive aspect for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the authors demonstrate successful training, the paper doesn't extensively analyze the sensitivity of performance to the initialization of the transform gate bias (bT). Further investigation of this hyperparameter and its impact on training dynamics would be valuable.
* **Limited Comparison to Other Recent Approaches:** The paper focuses primarily on comparison with FitNets and plain networks. A more comprehensive comparison with other recent advancements in deep network training, such as residual networks (ResNets) [He et al., 2015], might be warranted, even if only briefly to position the work within the broader context. ResNets also address vanishing gradient problems, albeit with a different mechanism.
* **Computational Cost:** Although the paper demonstrates successful training, the computational cost of training these very deep networks may be significant, particularly on large datasets. A discussion of the computational resources required and potential optimizations would be helpful.
* **Theoretical Analysis:**  While the paper provides an intuitive explanation of why highway networks should work, a more formal theoretical analysis of the gradient flow within these networks would strengthen the contribution.  Equation 5 touches on this, but a more in-depth analysis would be beneficial.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks, addressing a fundamental challenge in the field.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a significant improvement over existing approaches.
* The analysis provides valuable insights into the network's behavior and the importance of the gating mechanism.
* The code is publicly available, fostering reproducibility and further research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The hyperparameter sensitivity and computational cost aspects warrant further investigation.
* A more thorough comparison to other state-of-the-art techniques, such as ResNets, would strengthen the paper's positioning.
* A more rigorous theoretical analysis would add further support for the proposed approach.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is a clever and effective solution to the problem of training very deep networks. Despite minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh its limitations. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should consider including a more detailed discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity, a brief comparison to ResNets, and potentially exploring a theoretical analysis of gradient flow.
