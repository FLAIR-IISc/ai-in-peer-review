## Peer Review: Training Very Deep Networks

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling and well-executed investigation into training very deep feedforward networks. The proposed “highway network” architecture, inspired by LSTM gating mechanisms, demonstrably overcomes the optimization difficulties that plague training very deep plain networks. The results are strong, the paper is clearly written, and the contribution is significant. I recommend acceptance.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper addresses the longstanding challenge of training very deep feedforward networks, which are theoretically powerful but practically difficult to optimize due to vanishing/exploding gradients.  The authors observe that deep recurrent networks (LSTMs) successfully address this issue through gating mechanisms. They propose highway networks, which introduce adaptive gating mechanisms into feedforward networks, enabling information to flow more easily across layers. This allows for training networks with hundreds of layers, a feat previously unattainable with standard techniques.

The authors effectively situate their work within the existing literature. They discuss prior efforts to improve training of deep networks, including better optimizers (e.g., Adam, momentum), initialization strategies (e.g., Glorot/He initialization), activation functions (e.g., ReLU, competitive activation functions), and skip connections. They also properly acknowledge related work on distillation and layer-wise training, highlighting the limitations of these approaches. The acknowledgement of recent, similar work (references [31, 32]) demonstrates honesty and awareness of the broader research landscape.

**2. Strengths:**

*   **Novelty & Significance:** The highway network architecture is a novel and impactful contribution. Enabling the training of extremely deep feedforward networks opens up new possibilities for modeling complex functions and achieving improved performance on challenging tasks.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results are convincing. The paper clearly demonstrates that highway networks can be trained successfully to depths where plain networks fail. The MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 experiments provide strong evidence of the efficacy of the proposed approach. The comparisons with FitNets and state-of-the-art methods are well-executed.
*   **Clear Explanation & Design:** The highway network architecture is presented clearly and concisely, with a well-defined mathematical formulation. The intuition behind the transform and carry gates is easy to grasp.
*   **Detailed Analysis:** The analysis section (Section 4) goes beyond simply presenting results.  The visualizations of gate biases, activities, and layer importance provide valuable insights into the inner workings of highway networks.  The ablation study demonstrating selective layer utilization is particularly insightful.
*   **Reproducibility:**  The authors make their code and hyperparameters publicly available, which is commendable and facilitates reproducibility.

**3. Weaknesses:**

*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper does a good job of describing the hyperparameter search, it would be beneficial to provide more guidance on selecting appropriate values for the transform gate bias, especially as it seems problem dependent (different values for MNIST vs. CIFAR).
*   **Computational Cost:** Although highway networks train *more easily* than plain networks of comparable depth, the computational cost of training very deep networks remains substantial. This isn't a flaw of the method, but it’s an important practical consideration.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** While the LSTM inspiration is a good starting point, a more thorough theoretical justification for why highway networks work could further strengthen the paper.  A deeper understanding of the gradient flow dynamics in highway networks would be valuable.




**4. Qualitative Evaluation:**

*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by experimental results. The experimental design appears rigorous, and the analysis is insightful.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is clear, and the explanations are concise and easy to follow.
*   **Originality:** The highway network architecture is a novel contribution that builds upon existing ideas in a creative and effective way. The related work section is thorough and demonstrates an awareness of the existing literature.
*   **Significance:** The results are important and have the potential to impact the field of deep learning.  The ability to train very deep feedforward networks could lead to significant improvements in performance on a wide range of tasks.

**5. Arguments For & Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning – training very deep networks.
*   Presents a novel and effective architectural solution (highway networks).
*   Demonstrates strong empirical results on multiple datasets.
*   Provides insightful analysis of the learned representations.
*   Promotes reproducibility by making code and hyperparameters publicly available.

**Against Acceptance:**

*   Hyperparameter tuning can be challenging.
*   Computational cost of training very deep networks remains high.
*   Lacks a deeper theoretical understanding of why highway networks work.




**6. Recommendation:**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper.  The contribution is significant, the results are compelling, and the paper is exceptionally well-written. While there are minor weaknesses, they do not detract from the overall quality of the work. The highway network architecture represents a valuable addition to the toolbox of techniques for training deep learning models and has the potential to unlock further advances in the field.
