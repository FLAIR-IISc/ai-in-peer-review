- Proposes Highway Networks: feedforward layers with learned input-dependent transform gates T(x) and carry behavior y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)), inspired by LSTM gating.
- Practical trick: initialize transform-gate biases bT negative to bias early training toward carry behavior, enabling very deep training.
- Claims and evidence that highway nets can be trained with vanilla SGD to depths of 50–100+ and even >1000 in pilots, unlike plain nets.
- Empirical results show faster/better optimization and competitive test accuracy on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, matching or surpassing FitNets without teacher hints.
- Conceptual novelty: learns input-dependent routing/skip connections, going beyond fixed skips and deep supervision.
- Good empirical/analytic support: optimization studies, gate-activity visualizations, lesioning, hyperparameter detail and code for reproducibility.
- Missing comparisons to contemporaneous strong techniques (e.g., batch normalization, pre-activation/residual variants), limiting comparative claims.
- Limited experimental scale: no ImageNet or large-scale benchmarks, and no wall-clock/throughput comparisons.
- Incomplete ablations: tying C = 1 − T is assumed without evaluating uncoupled carry gates or sensitivity to bT across depths.
- No formal theoretical guarantees on gradient flow or conditions for stability at extreme depths.
- Lack of reported computational overhead: parameters, FLOPs, and runtime/throughput impact of gating not analyzed.
- Paper is clearly written with intuitive equations and helpful figures; some implementation specifics could be expanded despite code availability.
- Overall assessment: methodologically significant and likely impactful; recommendation to accept despite missing baselines and ablations.
