

# Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces "Highway Networks" as a novel architecture designed to enable direct training of extremely deep neural networks using standard stochastic gradient descent. The key innovation is an LSTM-inspired adaptive gating mechanism that creates "information highways" through the network, allowing information to flow across many layers without attenuation. The authors demonstrate that highway networks can be trained to 100+ layers while maintaining good generalization performance, whereas plain networks suffer from optimization difficulties as depth increases.

The paper makes a compelling case for the novelty of their approach by clearly differentiating it from prior work. While skip connections and distillation methods have been used to address deep network training (e.g., [22, 23, 25, 26]), highway networks introduce a dynamic gating mechanism that learns how to route information rather than using fixed shortcuts. This represents a significant conceptual advance over previous approaches. The authors properly cite related work, including the LSTM architecture [29, 30] and other gating mechanisms [20, 21], while clearly establishing the novelty of their highway network concept.

The paper's strengths are substantial. The core idea is bold and novel, addressing a fundamental challenge in deep learning that has limited the practical depth of neural networks. The experimental results are compelling: highway networks achieve significantly better performance than plain networks at extreme depths (e.g., 100-layer networks outperform similarly-sized plain networks by more than two orders of magnitude), and they match or exceed state-of-the-art methods on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 with single-stage training. The analysis of transform gate behavior (Figure 2) provides valuable insights into how information flows through the network, and the layer importance analysis (Figure 4) offers a new way to evaluate the contribution of individual layers in deep networks.

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with well-designed experiments that systematically compare highway networks to plain networks and other approaches. The training methodology is sound, using standard SGD with momentum and carefully tuned hyperparameters. The authors provide clear ablation studies showing how the highway architecture enables direct training of extremely deep networks without needing two-stage procedures. The paper is well-organized, clearly written, and the figures effectively illustrate key concepts.

Minor weaknesses include the limited exploration of highway networks on more complex datasets beyond CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100, and the paper could have provided a more thorough analysis of the computational overhead introduced by the gating mechanism. The theoretical justification for why highway networks work so well is somewhat limited, though the experimental evidence is strong.

Despite these minor limitations, this paper makes a significant contribution to deep learning. The highway network concept is not just an incremental improvement but a fundamentally new direction for designing deep architectures. The paper successfully demonstrates that extremely deep networks can be trained directly without resorting to complex multi-stage procedures, which opens up new possibilities for network design and optimization.

The significance of this work is substantial. It addresses a core problem in deep learning that has limited the practical application of very deep networks. The highway network architecture has the potential to influence future deep learning research and applications by enabling deeper and more powerful networks. The authors provide a clear path for future work, including exploring more complex block designs and applying highway networks to other domains.

In summary, this paper introduces a novel and bold approach to training very deep networks that successfully overcomes a fundamental limitation in the field. While the methodological details could be expanded, the core idea is sufficiently novel and impactful to warrant acceptance at NIPS. The authors have made a significant contribution that will likely influence the direction of future deep learning research.

**Strengths:**
- Highly novel concept (highway networks with adaptive gating)
- Addresses a fundamental problem in deep learning
- Demonstrates clear empirical advantages over plain networks
- Provides valuable analysis of network behavior
- Well-written and well-organized

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited exploration on more complex datasets
- Minor theoretical justification for why highway networks work
- Computational overhead of gating mechanism not fully analyzed

**Recommendation:** Accept. This paper introduces a significant new concept with strong empirical validation that addresses a core challenge in deep learning. The novelty and potential impact outweigh the minor methodological limitations.
