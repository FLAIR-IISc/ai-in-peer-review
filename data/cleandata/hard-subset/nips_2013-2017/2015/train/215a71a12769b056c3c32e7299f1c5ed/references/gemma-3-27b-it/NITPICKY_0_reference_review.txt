## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper proposes “highway networks,” a novel architectural modification to feedforward neural networks inspired by LSTM, designed to facilitate the training of very deep networks. The core idea is to introduce “information highways” – adaptive gating mechanisms – that allow for easier information flow across layers, mitigating the vanishing/exploding gradient problem. The authors demonstrate that highway networks can be trained effectively to significantly greater depths than standard (“plain”) networks using stochastic gradient descent, and achieve competitive results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.

**Summary of Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The highway network concept is a genuinely interesting and potentially impactful modification to standard feedforward architectures. The LSTM-inspired gating mechanism is a clever way to address the challenges of training deep networks.
* **Empirical Results:** The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments demonstrating the benefits of highway networks. The results on MNIST and CIFAR datasets showcase their ability to train significantly deeper networks than plain networks, and achieve competitive performance. The ablation studies (Section 3.1, Figure 1) are particularly convincing.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The analysis section (Section 4) provides insightful visualizations of the learned gating mechanisms. The examination of layer importance (Figure 4) and routing of information (Figure 3) offers valuable understanding into how highway networks operate.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a logical flow of ideas. The notation is clear and consistent.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

* **Lack of Theoretical Justification:** While the LSTM inspiration is mentioned, the paper lacks a rigorous theoretical analysis of *why* highway networks work so well. Why does the gating mechanism specifically alleviate the gradient vanishing problem?  A more detailed examination of the Jacobian behavior during training would be valuable. The authors state in Section 5 that derivation of an initialization scheme is “not essential,” but given the initial bias plays a role (Section 2.2), a more thorough investigation of this hyperparameter and its impact on convergence would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The success of highway networks appears sensitive to the initial bias of the transform gates. Although the authors provide guidelines, a more systematic hyperparameter study is needed, especially considering the impact on different datasets and architectures.
* **Minor Implementation Details & Reproducibility:** The description of the convolutional highway layers (Section 2.1) is slightly vague. Specifically, the exact zero-padding strategy is not explicitly stated. Additionally, the reliance on both Caffe and Brainstorm frameworks raises concerns about reproducibility. Providing a unified codebase would greatly improve the transparency and accessibility of the research. The URL provided leads to a defunct webpage.
* **Presentation of Results:** Table 3 lacks sufficient context. What specific modifications were made to the networks beyond replacing the fully connected layer? The comparison to state-of-the-art methods feels cursory.  The phrase "we reuse the hyperparameters..." is insufficient; a complete list should be provided or referenced.
* **Formatting & Editorial Issues:** Throughout the paper, there are inconsistencies in the use of boldface and italics. Equation 4, “Similarly, for the Jacobian…”, is awkwardly phrased and unclear.  The sentence beginning “Our pilot experiments…” in Section 2.2 is abrupt. There’s a grammatical error in the last sentence of the abstract ("…generalize well to unseen data.” should be “…generalization ability.").



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The highway network architecture represents a genuinely novel and potentially important contribution to the field of deep learning.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the practical benefits of highway networks.
* The detailed analysis provides valuable insights into the inner workings of these networks.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of theoretical justification weakens the overall contribution.
* The sensitivity to hyperparameters and limited reproducibility raise concerns.
* The minor formatting and editorial issues detract from the clarity and polish of the paper.




**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a promising new approach to training very deep neural networks. While the empirical results are strong and the ideas are innovative, the lack of theoretical grounding and some implementation/reproducibility concerns prevent a strong recommendation for acceptance.  The paper is a significant step forward, but requires substantial revision before it reaches the standard expected for NIPS.  Specifically, the authors need to address the theoretical justification, provide a more comprehensive hyperparameter analysis, ensure reproducibility, and address the minor formatting issues.

**Recommendation:** **Reject with strong encouragement to revise and resubmit.** If the authors can address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly providing more theoretical insight and increasing reproducibility, the paper has the potential to be a significant contribution to the field.
