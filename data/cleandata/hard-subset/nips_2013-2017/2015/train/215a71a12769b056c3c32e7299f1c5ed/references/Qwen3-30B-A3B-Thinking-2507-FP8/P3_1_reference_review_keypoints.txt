- Introduces "highway networks" with LSTM-style transform/carry gates allowing y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1-T(x)) to enable very deep feedforward training.  
- Demonstrates SGD can train extremely deep networks (100+ layers) where plain networks fail, with strong experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100.  
- Provides clear problem statement and practical impact by avoiding two-stage procedures like distillation.  
- Empirically analyzes transform gate behavior, offering insights into learned data-dependent routing and information flow.  
- Architecture is simple and easy to implement, with practical initialization guidance (negative bias for transform gates).  
- Lacks citation and comparison to ResNet (He et al., 2015), undermining novelty claims given similar identity-skip ideas.  
- No theoretical analysis explaining why gating specifically mitigates vanishing gradients or provides guarantees on depth.  
- Fails to compare against contemporary architectures like ResNet and DenseNet, limiting perspective on relative performance.  
- Omits analysis of computational cost and scaling overhead introduced by the gating mechanism.  
- Claims depth robustness empirically but does not provide theoretical bounds on maximum trainable depth.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept — valuable empirical contribution but novelty weakened by omission of ResNet and lack of stronger theory.  
- Suggested revisions: acknowledge and compare to ResNet, add theoretical justification for gating, analyze computational cost, and situate work among recent architectures.
