## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling and impactful contribution to the field of deep learning, addressing a fundamental challenge – training very deep networks. The introduction of "Highway Networks" offers a surprisingly simple yet effective solution to the vanishing/exploding gradient problem, enabling the training of networks with hundreds of layers. The experimental validation, particularly demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods with similar computational budgets, is strong. I believe this work is a significant step forward and is highly suitable for acceptance to NIPS.

**1. Summary and Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper tackles the difficulty of training very deep feedforward networks, a problem identified as a key obstacle to achieving further progress in many machine learning tasks. The authors observe that depth is crucial for representational power, citing examples from recurrent networks and theoretical work on circuit complexity. They acknowledge prior work attempting to address this issue, broadly categorizing them as optimizer improvements (e.g., Adam, SGD with momentum), initialization strategies (e.g., Xavier/Glorot initialization), activation function design (e.g., ReLU variants), and skip connections/distillation techniques (e.g., FitNets).  However, they argue these methods often have limitations or require significant hyperparameter tuning. 

The core idea of Highway Networks is inspired by LSTM, introducing “transform gates” and “carry gates” that dynamically control information flow through the layers, enabling gradients to propagate more effectively. This is a novel architectural approach that provides a trainable mechanism for adapting the effective depth of the network based on the input data. The authors correctly position their work within the broader context of addressing the difficulties associated with training deep networks, highlighting the advantages of their approach over existing solutions.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Insight:** The highway network architecture is a clever and surprisingly simple solution to a longstanding problem. The analogy to LSTM is insightful, and the proposed gating mechanism is well-motivated.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper provides compelling experimental evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of Highway Networks.  The results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 show substantial improvement over plain networks and competitive performance compared to state-of-the-art methods, especially considering the simplicity of the proposed architecture.  The ability to train networks with 100+ layers is remarkable.
* **Thorough Analysis:** The analysis section (Section 4) is a significant strength. Investigating the transform gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance provides valuable insights into how Highway Networks function and why they are effective.  The visualization of information flow is particularly insightful.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The notation is consistent and easy to follow.  The equations are clearly explained and the architectural diagrams are helpful.
* **Publicly Available Code:**  The authors’ commitment to reproducibility by providing publicly available code and hyperparameters is commendable.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper demonstrates good performance, the sensitivity to the initial transform gate bias is acknowledged but not explored in depth. A more systematic investigation of this hyperparameter's influence across different datasets and architectures would be valuable.  (Though, it's understandable that a fully exhaustive search is beyond the scope of a single paper.)
* **Computational Cost:** The added parameters of the gating mechanism introduce a slight computational overhead. While this is often outweighed by the benefits of training deeper networks, a more detailed analysis of the trade-off between performance and computational cost would be helpful.
* **Limited Exploration of Architectures:** The paper primarily focuses on convolutional highway networks. Investigating the performance of highway networks with other architectures (e.g., fully connected layers with different connectivity patterns) could further demonstrate the generality of the approach.
* **Explanation of Sparsity:** While the authors observe sparse activity in the transform gates, the underlying reasons for this sparsity aren't fully explored.  A deeper understanding of why the network learns to selectively activate certain pathways would be beneficial.



**4. Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (9/10):**  The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported by experimental results.  It presents a complete and well-executed piece of work.
* **Clarity (9/10):** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand, even for readers unfamiliar with the specifics of LSTM or deep network training.
* **Originality (9/10):** The Highway Network architecture is a novel and insightful contribution to the field.  While inspired by LSTM, the application to feedforward networks and the resulting benefits are original.
* **Significance (10/10):** The results are highly significant. The ability to train very deep networks opens up new possibilities for achieving state-of-the-art performance on a wide range of machine learning tasks.  The insights gained from the analysis of Highway Networks could also inform the design of future deep learning architectures.

**5. Arguments for and Against Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Solves a fundamental problem in deep learning – training very deep networks.
* Provides a novel and effective architectural solution (Highway Networks).
* Demonstrates strong empirical results on multiple datasets.
* Offers valuable insights into the behavior of deep networks.
* Publicly available code promotes reproducibility.

**Con:**

* Hyperparameter sensitivity requires careful tuning.
* Introduces a slight computational overhead. (However, the benefits likely outweigh this cost.)



**Overall Recommendation:**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper to NIPS. It represents a significant advance in the field of deep learning, offering a practical and effective solution to a challenging problem. The paper is well-written, thoroughly analyzed, and supported by compelling experimental results.  The insights gained from this work will undoubtedly influence future research in deep learning.
