## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel neural network architecture designed to facilitate the training of extremely deep feedforward networks. Inspired by LSTM networks, Highway Networks incorporate adaptive gating mechanisms allowing information to flow more easily across many layers, mitigating the vanishing/exploding gradient problem that typically hinders the training of deep plain networks. The authors demonstrate, through experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, that highway networks can be trained effectively with standard SGD, achieving competitive or superior performance compared to existing methods, even with limited computational resources. They also provide an analysis of the learned gating mechanisms and layer importance.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a critical problem:** The difficulty of training very deep networks is a long-standing challenge in deep learning. This paper offers a compelling architectural solution.
* **Novelty:** The introduction of highway networks, with their adaptive gating mechanisms, is a genuinely novel approach. While inspired by LSTMs, the application to feedforward networks and the resulting theoretical insights are significant.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper presents thorough experimental validation across multiple datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100). Results consistently demonstrate the superiority of highway networks over plain networks, particularly at increasing depths, and are competitive with state-of-the-art methods. The comparison to FitNets is particularly insightful.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The analysis of the learned gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance provides valuable insights into how highway networks function internally. These analyses strengthen the claims and demonstrate that the architecture isn’t just working, but doing so in a plausible and interpretable manner.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and the proposed solution. The mathematical notation is clear and concise.
* **Code Availability:**  Publicly available code is a major plus, encouraging reproducibility and follow-up work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper addresses hyperparameter tuning, the optimal settings (especially the initial transform gate bias) appear somewhat sensitive and may require careful adjustment for different tasks/architectures. This could present a barrier to adoption.
* **Computational Overhead:**  The addition of transform and carry gates introduces extra parameters. While the paper focuses on demonstrating trainability, a more thorough analysis of the computational cost compared to other approaches (e.g., ResNets) would be beneficial.
* **Limited Comparison to ResNets:** The paper briefly mentions skip connections but lacks a direct comparative analysis with Residual Networks (ResNets), which have become the standard approach for training very deep networks. A direct comparison is crucial to establish the relative merits of highway networks.  Given ResNets were published around the same time, this is a noticeable omission.
* **Analysis Depth:** While the analysis is good, it could be expanded.  For example, investigating the correlation between gate activity and feature relevance could provide further insights.




**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:**  The paper is technically sound and presents a complete piece of work. The claims are well-supported by both theoretical reasoning (regarding gradient flow) and extensive experimental results. The authors are careful to acknowledge the limitations of their study.  (Strong)
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the highway network architecture and its benefits. The notation is consistent and accessible.  (Strong)
* **Originality:** The concept of applying LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms to feedforward networks is novel and represents a significant contribution.  (Strong)
* **Significance:**  This work is highly significant.  The ability to train extremely deep networks efficiently is a major advance. Highway networks have the potential to unlock new levels of performance in a wide range of applications. The insights into layer importance and information routing are also valuable. (Strong)

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning.
* Presents a novel and effective architecture.
* Backed by strong empirical results.
* Provides insightful analysis.
* Clearly written and well-organized.
* Code is publicly available.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* Lacks a direct comparison to ResNets.
* Hyperparameter sensitivity could be a practical limitation.
* Limited discussion of computational overhead.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is an excellent paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The proposed highway networks offer a promising solution to the problem of training very deep feedforward networks. While a direct comparison to ResNets is missing, the strong empirical results and insightful analysis outweigh this limitation.  I **strongly recommend acceptance** to NIPS.



**Confidence:** 9/10
