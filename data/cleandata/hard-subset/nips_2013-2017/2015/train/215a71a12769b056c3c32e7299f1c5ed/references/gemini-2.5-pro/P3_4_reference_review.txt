**Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"**

**Summary and Relation to Previous Work**

This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel neural network architecture designed to enable the training of extremely deep feedforward models. The central problem addressed is the well-known optimization difficulty that arises in "plain" deep networks, where performance degrades as depth increases beyond a certain point due to issues with information and gradient propagation. The core idea is inspired by the gating mechanisms in Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks. The authors propose augmenting standard feedforward layers with a "transform gate" and a "carry gate." These gates dynamically control how much of the input is transformed by the layer's non-linearity versus how much is passed through unchanged. This creates unimpeded "information highways," allowing gradients to flow directly across many layers, thus mitigating the vanishing gradient problem.

This work is situated within a clear line of research aiming to push the boundaries of network depth. It effectively contrasts its approach with several alternatives: 1) improved optimization methods (e.g., Hessian-Free, momentum methods), 2) sophisticated initialization schemes (e.g., Glorot & Bengio, He et al.), 3) hard-wired skip connections (as seen in GoogLeNet), and 4) complex multi-stage training procedures like hint-based training in FitNets. The key distinction is that Highway Networks learn a *dynamic, data-dependent* routing mechanism, rather than relying on static architectural choices or complex training protocols. The direct conceptual link to LSTM is a primary source of originality, elegantly repurposing a solution for temporal credit assignment to solve a problem of spatial (i.e., depth-wise) credit assignment.

**Strengths**

1.  **Elegant and Effective Solution to a Major Problem:** The paper tackles one of the most significant challenges in deep learning at the time: the degradation problem in very deep networks. The proposed gating mechanism is a simple yet powerful modification to a standard layer, providing an elegant solution that is both intuitive and highly effective.

2.  **Compelling Empirical Validation:** The experiments are thorough and convincingly demonstrate the paper's central claims.
    *   The optimization experiment on MNIST (Figure 1) is a clear and powerful illustration of the core issue. It shows plain networks failing catastrophically as depth increases, while highway networks maintain stable and effective training even at 100 layers.
    *   The direct comparison to FitNets is particularly strong. The authors show that a highway network can achieve similar or better performance than a FitNet of comparable size, but with a standard, single-stage SGD training process. This underscores the practical advantage of the proposed architecture over more complex training schemes.
    *   The competitive results on CIFAR-10/100 demonstrate that the benefits are not confined to a toy problem and that the architecture generalizes well.

3.  **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis in Section 4 is a major strength and elevates the paper beyond a simple presentation of a new architecture.
    *   The visualization of gate activities and block outputs provides valuable insight into the network's learned behavior, showing sparse, data-dependent transformations.
    *   The analysis of class-conditional gate activity (Figure 3) provides strong evidence for the claim that the network learns dynamic information routing, a key advantage over static skip connections.
    *   The lesion study (Figure 4) is an excellent and novel method for probing the "effective depth" of the network. The finding that the network utilizes more layers for a complex task (CIFAR-100) than a simple one (MNIST) is a profound result, suggesting that highway networks can automatically adapt their functional complexity to the problem at hand.

4.  **Practicality and Simplicity:** The proposed method is easy to implement and integrates seamlessly into existing deep learning frameworks. The initialization strategy for the gate biases (initializing with a negative value) is a simple and effective heuristic that facilitates training from the start.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

1.  **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** The paper is primarily empirical. While the intuition drawn from LSTMs and the Jacobian analysis (Equation 5) is compelling, the work would be further strengthened by a more formal analysis of the optimization landscape. For example, an analysis of the gradient norms during backpropagation could provide a more quantitative explanation for why training is so stable.

2.  **Sensitivity to Gate Bias Initialization:** The initial transform gate bias is introduced as a new and important hyperparameter. The authors provide a helpful rule of thumb (e.g., -1 for depth 10, -3 for depth 30), but a more systematic study of the sensitivity to this hyperparameter would be beneficial. It is unclear how critical the exact value is, or how one should select it for much deeper networks (e.g., 500 layers).

3.  **Clarity on Architectural Details:** While generally well-written, some architectural details could be clarified. For instance, when dimensionality changes are required, the paper states that a "plain layer" is used. It would be helpful to specify precisely how this interacts with the highway structure (e.g., is it a single pre-processing layer, or are plain layers interspersed?). A small architectural diagram would enhance clarity.

4.  **Simplification of Gates (`C = 1 - T`):** The paper simplifies the gates by setting `C = 1 - T`. While this is practical and reduces parameters, it couples the decision to carry information with the decision to transform it. A brief discussion on the potential implications of this design choice, perhaps referencing the pilot experiments with more complex LSTM-like blocks, would be welcome. Could a decoupled gate offer additional flexibility or performance gains?

**Assessment**

*   **Quality:** High. The paper is technically sound. The claims are exceptionally well-supported by rigorous and well-designed experiments. The analysis is insightful and goes well beyond simply reporting performance metrics.
*   **Clarity:** Very good. The paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow. The core idea is explained with admirable simplicity. Minor improvements, as noted above, are possible but do not detract from the overall clarity.
*   **Originality:** High. The application of LSTM-style gating to deep feedforward networks is a highly novel and creative idea. It clearly distinguishes itself from prior work and opens up a new and fruitful direction for architectural design.
*   **Significance:** Very high. This work represents a fundamental breakthrough in training very deep neural networks. It provides a practical and robust solution to the degradation problem, directly enabling the exploration of architectures that are an order of magnitude deeper than what was previously feasible with standard methods. This work has had, and will continue to have, a major impact on the field, influencing the design of subsequent deep learning models (e.g., its clear conceptual relationship to residual networks).

**Recommendation: Strong Accept**

This is an outstanding paper that introduces a novel, elegant, and highly effective architecture. It addresses a critical problem with compelling experiments and insightful analysis. The strengths far outweigh the minor weaknesses. This is a landmark contribution to the field of deep learning and a clear candidate for acceptance at NIPS.
