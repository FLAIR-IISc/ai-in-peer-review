- Summary: Proposes Highway Networks with LSTM-like transform gates to route information, enabling SGD training of very deep (100+) feedforward nets and showing improved training/generalization on MNIST/CIFAR.  
- Strength: Clear, important contribution with compelling experiments demonstrating training at extreme depth where plain nets fail.  
- Strength: Analysis of transform-gate behavior reveals dynamic, input-dependent routing and provides insight beyond fixed skip connections.  
- Strength: Rigorous experimental design with hyperparameter search, multiple datasets, ablation/lesioning studies, and effective negative-bias gate initialization.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to ResNets—fails to contextualize against simpler identity-skip methods that achieved similar/ better results.  
- Weakness: Lacks strong theoretical justification—Jacobian analysis is insufficient to explain why gating prevents vanishing gradients or differs fundamentally from skip connections.  
- Weakness: Overstated claims about SGD training and generalization given concurrent ResNet results and lack of evidence showing superiority to ResNets.  
- Weakness: Increased implementation complexity due to gate parameters not justified relative to simpler, faster ResNets.  
- Related work: Cites prior work but neglects proper engagement with concurrent ResNet literature (He et al., 2015).  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound and interesting, but significance is limited by ResNets; impact would be much greater with a proper ResNet comparison.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; revise to include thorough ResNet comparisons, clarify scenarios where highway nets are preferable, and better articulate relation to skip connections.
