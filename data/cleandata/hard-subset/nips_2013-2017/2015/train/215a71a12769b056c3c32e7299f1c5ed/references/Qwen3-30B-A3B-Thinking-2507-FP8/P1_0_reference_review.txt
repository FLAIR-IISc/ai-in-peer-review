

# Peer Review: "Training Very Deep Networks"

## Summary
This paper introduces "highway networks" as a novel architecture for training extremely deep feedforward neural networks. The authors propose an LSTM-inspired adaptive gating mechanism that allows information to flow across many layers without attenuation, creating "information highways." The core innovation is the highway layer formulation: y = H(x,WH)·T(x,WT) + x·(1-T(x,WT)), where T is the transform gate and (1-T) is the carry gate. This architecture enables direct training of networks with 100+ layers using standard SGD, whereas plain networks suffer from severe performance degradation as depth increases. The paper demonstrates strong empirical results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, showing that highway networks match or exceed the performance of shallower networks with fewer parameters.

The work builds upon several prior approaches: normalized initialization strategies [16,17], skip connections [22,23], and distillation techniques [25,26]. However, it differs fundamentally by introducing an adaptive gating mechanism that dynamically routes information rather than using fixed skip connections. The paper also provides valuable analysis of how the transform gates operate in trained networks, revealing data-dependent routing behavior.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with well-designed experiments that thoroughly evaluate the proposed approach. The authors provide a comprehensive comparison between highway and plain networks across multiple datasets and depths, controlling for network capacity. The ablation studies (Figures 2-4) offer valuable insights into how the highway network operates, particularly regarding the behavior of transform gates and the importance of individual layers.

The authors are careful to acknowledge limitations, such as the potential for unused layers, and demonstrate that this doesn't adversely affect performance. They provide detailed experimental protocols, including hyperparameter search procedures and multiple runs for statistical significance. The paper is a complete piece of work with clear claims supported by evidence.

## Clarity
The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical formulation is clear and consistent, with well-chosen notation. The figures effectively illustrate key concepts (e.g., information highways in Figure 2), and the experimental results are presented in a logical, easy-to-follow manner. The paper provides sufficient detail for experts to reproduce the results, including implementation details and hyperparameter settings.

## Originality
The highway network architecture represents a significant departure from previous approaches to training deep networks. While the paper acknowledges inspiration from LSTMs [29,30] and residual connections [22,23], it offers a novel mechanism for information flow that is distinct from both. The key innovation is the adaptive gating mechanism that allows the network to dynamically route information rather than using fixed skip connections.

However, the paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison to ResNets, which were published around the same time (He et al., "Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition," CVPR 2016) and have since become the dominant approach for training very deep networks. The paper's focus on highway networks as a solution to the same problem that ResNets address (training very deep networks) makes this comparison essential for contextualizing the contribution.

## Significance
This work addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning: training very deep networks. The paper demonstrates that highway networks can be trained directly with standard SGD even at extreme depths (100+ layers), which is a significant practical contribution. The results show that highway networks can match or exceed the performance of shallower networks with fewer parameters, making them more efficient.

The insights into information routing and layer importance (Figure 4) provide valuable theoretical understanding of how deep networks operate. This work has the potential to influence future deep learning architectures by providing a simple yet effective mechanism for enabling training of extremely deep networks.

## Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
- Clear, well-motivated technical contribution addressing a fundamental problem in deep learning
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple datasets and network depths
- Valuable analysis of transform gate behavior and layer importance
- Simple yet effective architecture that can be applied to various network types
- Well-written and well-organized paper with clear figures and explanations

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited comparison to ResNets (published around the same time), which have since become the dominant approach for training very deep networks
- Lack of theoretical analysis explaining why highway networks work better than plain networks for extreme depths
- Minimal discussion of how highway networks might interact with other recent advances (e.g., batch normalization, weight normalization)
- The paper doesn't fully explore the relationship between highway networks and residual connections, which are conceptually related

## Recommendation
This paper makes a valuable contribution to deep learning by introducing highway networks as an effective solution for training extremely deep feedforward networks. The empirical results are strong, the architecture is simple yet effective, and the analysis provides important insights into how deep networks operate.

While the paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison to ResNets (which were published around the same time), the core contribution remains significant and novel. The highway network concept has influenced subsequent work in deep learning, including the development of more sophisticated gating mechanisms.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the limited comparison to ResNets and to clarify the relationship between highway networks and residual connections. The paper is technically sound, well-written, and addresses an important problem in deep learning.

**Overall Assessment:** The paper is a strong contribution to the field that advances our understanding of how to train very deep networks. It is technically sound, well-executed, and has significant practical implications. While the comparison to ResNets is limited, this does not undermine the core contribution of the paper. The work is highly relevant to NIPS and represents a meaningful advance in deep learning research.
