- Overall: Highway Networks present a simple, effective solution that enables training of very deep networks and merit acceptance.
- Problem & context: Addresses the fundamental difficulty of training very deep feedforward networks and situates the work among optimizer, initialization, activation, and skip-connection approaches.
- Core idea: Introduces transform and carry gates (inspired by LSTM) to dynamically control information flow and enable gradient propagation through hundreds of layers.
- Novelty & insight: The gating architecture is a clever, well-motivated adaptation of LSTM ideas to feedforward networks.
- Empirical results: Strong performance on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and successful training of 100+ layer networks with competitive accuracy.
- Analysis: Thorough investigation of gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance with useful visualizations explaining how Highway Networks work.
- Presentation & reproducibility: Well-written paper with clear notation, helpful diagrams, and publicly available code and hyperparameters.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Performance depends on initial transform gate bias, but this sensitivity is acknowledged without exhaustive study.
- Computational cost: Gating adds parameters and slight computational overhead; trade-offs between cost and depth benefits need more analysis.
- Architectural generality: Evaluation focuses mainly on convolutional highway networks; broader architectural exploration is limited.
- Sparsity explanation: Observed sparse gate activity is reported but not fully explained or analyzed.
- Evaluation metrics: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 10/10.
- Acceptance arguments (pro): Solves a core problem, offers a novel effective architecture, strong empirical support, insightful analysis, and reproducible code.
- Acceptance arguments (con): Requires careful hyperparameter tuning and incurs some computational overhead.
- Recommendation: Strongly recommended for acceptance at NIPS.
