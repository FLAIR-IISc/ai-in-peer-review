This paper introduces "highway networks," a modification to feedforward neural networks inspired by LSTM, aiming to facilitate training of very deep networks. The core idea is to introduce "transform" and "carry" gates that allow information to flow across layers with less attenuation, addressing the vanishing gradient problem. The paper presents experimental results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 demonstrating the effectiveness of highway networks in training deeper models compared to plain networks.

While the idea is conceptually sound and the results appear promising, the paper suffers from a concerning number of minor, yet pervasive, flaws. These flaws, while individually small, accumulate to create a sense of carelessness that undermines confidence in the presented work. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture is a novel approach to training very deep feedforward networks, drawing inspiration from recurrent networks in a creative way.
*   **Empirical Results:** The paper provides experimental evidence suggesting that highway networks can be trained to greater depths than plain networks, particularly on CIFAR datasets.
*   **Clear Motivation:** The problem of training deep networks is well-motivated, and the paper clearly articulates the limitations of existing approaches.
*   **Code Availability:** The public availability of code and hyperparameters is commendable.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Notational Inconsistency:** The notation is inconsistent. For example, the use of boldface for vectors *and* matrices is confusing. Furthermore, the capitalization of *italicized* capital letters feels arbitrary and inconsistent with standard mathematical notation. Equation 1 omits biases for clarity, yet biases *are* used in subsequent equations (e.g., bT in Section 2.2).
*   **Mathematical Imprecision:** Equation (5) states "dy/dx = {I, if T(x,WT) = 0, H'(x,WH), if T(x,WT) = 1}". This is a simplification. It holds *almost* everywhere but fails at points where T(x,WT) transitions between 0 and 1, resulting in a non-differentiable point. This is a fundamental flaw not addressed.
*   **Insufficient Justification for C = 1-T:** The choice to set the carry gate C to 1-T is presented without justification. While it simplifies the equations, the authors fail to discuss potential alternative formulations or the impact of this specific choice.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper acknowledges the importance of the transform gate bias initialization as a hyperparameter, but provides only vague guidelines ("-1, -2 and -3"). A more systematic analysis of this parameter’s impact would be valuable.
*   **Figure Clarity:** Figure 2 lacks sufficient labeling and explanation. The connection between transform gate activity and “information highways” is not clearly demonstrated visually. The choice of color schemes hinders interpretation.
*   **Redundant/Weak Analysis:** The lesioning experiment in Section 4.2, while interesting, feels somewhat contrived. Simply removing a layer is not a particularly informative analysis of the learned representations.
*   **Minor Typographical Errors:** Numerous minor typos and grammatical errors are present throughout the paper (e.g., "varying varying depths" in Section 3.1).
*   **Citation Issues:** The citation style is inconsistent. Some citations are fully formatted while others are not. Reference [31] is to a shorter report, suggesting the authors may be aware of limitations in the earlier work that aren't fully addressed here.
*   **Lack of Ablation Studies:** The impact of each component of the highway network (transform gate, carry gate) is not isolated. Ablation studies would help in understanding the contribution of each element.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   Novel architecture with potential to address a significant problem in deep learning.
*   Empirical results demonstrate improved training of deeper networks.
*   Code availability promotes reproducibility.

**Con:**

*   Numerous minor technical flaws and inconsistencies raise concerns about the rigor of the work.
*   Mathematical imprecision in Equation 5.
*   Lack of thorough analysis and justification for key design choices.
*   The overall presentation lacks polish.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the novelty and potential of highway networks, the significant number of flaws – both technical and presentational – leads me to recommend **rejection**. The paper requires substantial revision to address the identified inconsistencies, imprecisions, and lack of thorough analysis. While the idea is promising, the current presentation does not meet the quality standards expected of a NIPS publication. The accumulation of minor errors suggests a lack of carefulness that undermines confidence in the results and the authors' understanding of the underlying principles. A more rigorous treatment of the mathematical details and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation are essential before this work could be considered for acceptance.
