- Proposal: Introduces Highway Networks—layers y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x))—using learned sigmoid gates to interpolate between identity and transform.  
- Initialization: Negative bias for T biases layers to carry inputs initially, easing optimization.  
- Empirical claim: Very deep networks (50–100 FC layers; 10–30 conv layers) train directly with SGD and converge faster than plain nets.  
- Results: Competitive generalization on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 without teacher-student distillation.  
- Analyses: Visualize gate activity, show class-conditional routing, and use lesioning to quantify layer importance.  
- Relation to prior work: Positions method among optimizers, activations, skip connections, FitNets, and LSTM gating—connects to skip connections but adds input-dependent multiplicative gating.  
- Strength — architecture: Simple, general, and easy-to-implement gating mechanism that makes depth beneficial.  
- Strength — optimization: Convincing experiments showing trainability and faster convergence with matched parameter budgets.  
- Strength — recipe: Gate parameterization and negative bias initialization are simple and architecture-agnostic.  
- Strength — generalization: Matches or surpasses FitNet baselines under similar parameter budgets and modest augmentation.  
- Strength — insight: Routing/lesion analyses provide useful understanding of depth usage.  
- Weakness — missing baselines: No comparison to batch normalization or to ungated residual/skip connections, leaving causal attribution unclear.  
- Weakness — limited scale: Experiments limited to MNIST/CIFAR; no ImageNet-scale validation and >1000-layer claim anecdotal.  
- Weakness — ablations: Few quantitative studies on independent carry gate vs C=1−T, sensitivity to T bias, gating cost, or 1×1 bottlenecks.  
- Weakness — clarity/details: Some architecture/training details (shapes, counts, schedules, unpublished pilot blocks) are terse or missing.  
- Weakness — fairness: Comparisons could include strong fully convolutional baselines (e.g., All-CNN) under same augmentation/compute.  
- Quality: Technically sound and carefully executed; optimization study is convincing but causal attribution and gate overhead are under-quantified.  
- Clarity: Generally well written; suggests consistent terminology, tables of architectures/parameters, and reporting of training schedules and T bias distributions.  
- Originality: Novel application of LSTM-like gating to depth-wise routing in feedforward nets; per-unit input-dependent gating is conceptually new.  
- Significance: Architecturally significant—enables training very deep networks without teacher supervision and likely to influence future designs.  
- Suggestions: Compare to plain skip (H(x)+x) and BN, ablate independent C vs 1−T and T bias sensitivity, report compute/memory overhead, clarify conv gate sharing and 1×1 usage, and include larger-scale/deeper conv experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept—original and important idea with compelling empirical evidence; stronger baselines/ablations urged for final version.  
- Confidence: Reviewer confidence reported as high.
