- Proposes Highway Networks: layers compute y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)) with negative gate-bias initialization to carry inputs and ease training of very deep nets.
- Strength: simple, well-motivated formulation with helpful interpolation and Jacobian discussion.
- Strength: optimization experiments show highway nets train reliably and converge faster at depths (50–100 layers) where plain nets fail.
- Strength: convolutional experiments are fair under budget constraints and demonstrate single-stage SGD training advantage over two-stage FitNet training.
- Strength: analyses (gate bias/activity visualizations, per-class routing, lesion studies) support that gates enable both optimization and meaningful, input-dependent computation.
- Strength: reproducibility aided by released code, hyperparameters, and multiple-run statistics.
- Weakness: no direct baseline comparing to deep networks with fixed identity/shortcut connections, leaving the benefit of learned gates unclear.
- Weakness: limited ablation studies—C constrained to 1−T, gate-bias initialization not systematically explored, no post-training gate removal/pruning experiments.
- Weakness: lacks formal theoretical analysis of gradient propagation (e.g., Jacobian spectra or gradient norms) despite central claims about vanishing gradients.
- Weakness: extreme-depth claim (>1000 layers) is only pilot-level and not supported by main-text results beyond 100 layers.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to other training aids (e.g., batch normalization, additional regularization) to show complementarity or redundancy.
- Weakness: CIFAR results are not state-of-the-art and stronger baselines under the same augmentation regime would strengthen significance.
- Clarity request: specify convolutional gating granularity (per spatial location, per channel, receptive field, sharing).
- Clarity request: report parameter/computation overhead and training/inference time introduced by gates.
- Clarity request: clarify how dimensionality changes are handled in deep conv stacks and whether plain layers limit highway depth.
- Clarity request: provide training curves and gradient-norm statistics for conv experiments analogous to Fig. 1.
- Originality: novel extension of LSTM-style gating to depth, offering learned continuous routing vs fixed skips and an alternative to teacher-student distillation.
- Significance: simple, broadly applicable method that enables reliable training of very deep networks with standard SGD and is likely to influence future architectures.
- Suggestions: add identity-skip baseline, explore independent carry gates C(x), ablate gate-bias choices and learned distributions, report gradient norms, quantify gate overhead, and discuss compatibility with dropout/batch norm.
- Recommendation: lean accept (high confidence).
