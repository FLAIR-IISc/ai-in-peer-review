Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes “highway networks,” a feedforward architecture that introduces learnable, LSTM-inspired gates to ease information flow across many layers. Each layer combines a transformed path H(x) and a carry path x, weighted by an elementwise transform gate T(x) with C(x)=1−T(x), allowing paths along which signals and gradients propagate with little attenuation (“information highways”). A simple but important initialization—negative biases for T—biases the network toward carry behavior at the start of training, analogous to LSTM forget-gate initialization, and enables SGD to optimize networks with tens to hundreds of layers.

The work directly addresses the optimization difficulties of very deep networks. Prior approaches include careful initialization [Glorot & Bengio; He et al.], improved optimizers [Martens; Sutskever], local competition/maxout [Goodfellow et al.; Srivastava et al.], skip connections and deep supervision [Lee et al.], and teacher-student distillation for thin deep nets (FitNets). Compared to these, the contribution here is an architectural mechanism that adaptively gates information at each layer, yielding depth-robust optimization without layerwise pretraining or teacher hints.

Empirically, the authors show that thin fully connected highway networks scale in depth on MNIST where plain nets fail; convolutional highway networks train easily on CIFAR-10/100 without multi-stage hint-based training and achieve accuracy competitive with fitnets under similar parameter/compute budgets. Analyses of gate activity and “lesioning” layers suggest learned, data-dependent routing and that deeper tasks utilize more layers.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The layer formulation is clear, the gating mechanism is well motivated by LSTM, and the suggested initialization is sensible. The experiments substantiate the central claim: highway networks train directly at depths where plain architectures struggle, and they converge faster.
- Empirics: Results cover MNIST and CIFAR-10/100, include comparisons to plain nets and fitnets, and provide insightful internal analyses (gate biases/activities, class-conditional routing, and lesion studies). Code availability is a plus.
- Limitations: Benchmarks are not pushed to SOTA; comparisons omit strong contemporaneous baselines (e.g., batch normalization, fixed skip/residual connections). Ablations (e.g., C≠1−T, removing gates, fixed skip-only) are limited. Sensitivity to the transform gate bias and overhead from gating are not comprehensively characterized. Analyses are largely qualitative.

Clarity
The paper is generally well written and organized. The core idea and equations are easy to follow; implementation and training details are provided, and figures effectively illustrate gating behavior. Minor issues: a few typos (“varying varying”), and some architectural/training specifics could be made more explicit (exact layer configs, schedules, augmentation details) to fully enable reproduction from the text alone, though code availability mitigates this.

Originality
High. The introduction of LSTM-style gating into deep feedforward nets to create learnable, per-unit, depth-wise routing is a bold and novel idea at the time of submission. While skip connections have precedent, the adaptive, input-dependent gating and simple bias initialization combine to offer a new way to make very deep nets trainable without teachers or special pretraining.

Significance
High. If adopted, this mechanism can change how we design and train deep and thin networks across modalities, lowering the barrier to depth without complicated training curricula. The concept of data-dependent routing across depth is compelling and likely to stimulate substantial follow-up research. Even if not yet SOTA, the architectural idea is impactful.

Strengths
- Clear, principled architectural innovation: learnable gates enabling robust information/gradient flow across depth.
- Simple, effective initialization trick; works with standard SGD and various activations/transforms.
- Strong evidence that depth-robust optimization is achieved; faster convergence than plain nets.
- Insightful analyses (gate activity, class-conditional routing, layer lesioning) that support the routing interpretation.
- Practical: applies to fully connected and convolutional layers; code released.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Missing baselines: compare against fixed skip connections without gates (i.e., residual summation) and batch normalization; report parameter/compute-parity results.
- Ablations:
  - C=1−T vs learning both T and C; channel-shared vs elementwise gates; replacing sigmoid gates with ReLU/HardSigmoid.
  - Effect of transform-gate bias bT: sensitivity curves, per-dataset optimal range, training stability.
  - Quantify gradient norms across depth to directly show improved signal propagation.
- Broader benchmarks: deeper conv highway nets (>50 layers) on CIFAR with standard augmentation; perhaps SVHN or ImageNet-scale pilots to test scalability.
- Cost/benefit: report parameter and FLOP overhead from gates and measured wall-clock impacts.
- Some reported results reuse hyperparams across datasets; a modest per-dataset tuning would make comparisons fairer.

Questions for the authors
- How critical is the constraint C=1−T? Did learning C independently help or hurt?
- Can you provide a direct comparison to an identical architecture with identity skip connections and no gates?
- How sensitive is training to bT and to the choice of σ? Would hard-saturating or piecewise-linear gates work as well?
- Have you explored combining highway layers with batch normalization, and if so, how do the effects interact?

Decision
Accept.

Rationale: The paper introduces a genuinely new architectural concept—adaptive, per-unit gated skip pathways—that materially advances our ability to train very deep networks with standard optimization. The empirical evidence convincingly demonstrates improved trainability and offers thoughtful analyses of the learned routing. While additional baselines and ablations would strengthen the case, the novelty and potential impact are high, and the identified weaknesses are addressable and do not undermine the core contribution.

Confidence
High.
