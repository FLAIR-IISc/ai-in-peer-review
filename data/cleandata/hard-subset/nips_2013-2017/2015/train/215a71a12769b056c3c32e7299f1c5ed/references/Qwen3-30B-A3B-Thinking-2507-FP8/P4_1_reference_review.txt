

# Review: Training Very Deep Networks

This paper proposes "highway networks" as a novel architecture for training extremely deep feedforward neural networks. The authors address the well-known challenge of vanishing gradients in deep networks by introducing an adaptive gating mechanism inspired by Long Short Term Memory (LSTM) networks. The core innovation is the highway layer defined as y = H(x,WH)·T(x,WT) + x·(1-T(x,WT)), where T is a transform gate (and C=1-T is the carry gate). The key insight is that with negative bias initialization for T (e.g., -1, -2, -3), networks can be trained directly with SGD even at extreme depths (over 100 layers), whereas plain networks become increasingly difficult to optimize with depth. The paper demonstrates that highway networks achieve comparable or better performance than shallower networks while being significantly deeper, with experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.

The paper builds on previous work in deep learning, particularly the use of skip connections (e.g., [22, 23, 24, 25]) and the LSTM architecture [29, 30]. It clearly differentiates from related work by proposing a dynamic gating mechanism rather than fixed skip connections, and by showing that highway networks can be trained in a single stage without teacher networks or layer-wise pretraining. The authors also relate their work to the theoretical understanding of depth efficiency in neural networks [10, 11, 12].

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a clear mathematical formulation of highway networks and provides strong experimental validation. The negative bias initialization scheme for transform gates is well-motivated and consistently effective across multiple datasets and network architectures. The experiments on MNIST (Section 3.1) provide compelling evidence that highway networks overcome the depth limitation faced by plain networks.

2. **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear notation, logical flow, and helpful figures (particularly Figures 1-4). The analysis of transform gate behavior (Section 4.1) and layer importance (Section 4.2) provides valuable insights into how highway networks operate, which is rare in architectural proposals.

3. **Novelty:** The highway network architecture represents a meaningful advance over existing approaches. The dynamic gating mechanism differs fundamentally from fixed skip connections [22, 23, 24] and distillation methods [25]. The paper clearly demonstrates how this architecture enables training networks with over 100 layers using standard SGD, which is a significant contribution to the field.

4. **Significance:** The problem of training very deep networks is central to deep learning. The paper demonstrates that highway networks can match or exceed the performance of shallower networks (like FitNets [25]) while being deeper and often using fewer parameters. The ability to directly train extremely deep networks without special training procedures represents a substantial practical advance.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Comparison to Recent Work:** The paper does not compare to Residual Networks (ResNets), which were published around the same time (2015) and achieved similar goals of enabling very deep network training. While ResNets were not yet known at the time of this submission, the paper should acknowledge this work and explain how highway networks differ from ResNets.

2. **Theoretical Justification:** While the paper provides empirical evidence that negative bias initialization works, it lacks a theoretical explanation for why this specific initialization scheme is effective for highway networks. The connection to LSTM initialization [30] is mentioned, but the paper could better explain why this initialization helps with the specific challenges of very deep feedforward networks.

3. **Depth Limits:** The paper demonstrates training networks up to 100 layers, but doesn't explore whether there are limits to this approach or how performance might degrade with even greater depth. This would strengthen the claim that highway networks solve the depth problem.

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pro:*
- The paper presents a clear, novel architectural solution to a fundamental problem in deep learning
- The experimental evidence is thorough and compelling across multiple datasets
- The paper provides valuable insights into how information flows through deep networks
- The approach enables training networks with over 100 layers using standard SGD

*Con:*
- Limited comparison to ResNets (which emerged around the same time)
- Lack of theoretical justification for the negative bias initialization
- Does not fully explore the limits of the approach (e.g., maximum trainable depth)

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong technical contribution that makes a significant advance in the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture addresses a critical problem in training very deep networks with a simple yet effective modification to the standard feedforward architecture. The paper is well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and provides valuable insights into how these networks operate. The novelty is clear, the technical content is sound, and the significance of the contribution is substantial.

While the paper could be strengthened by addressing the comparison to ResNets (which was published in the same year) and providing more theoretical justification for the initialization scheme, these do not detract from the core contribution. The paper represents a meaningful advance that will likely be influential in the deep learning community.

I recommend acceptance to NIPS. The paper makes a clear, significant contribution to the field that is well-supported by both theoretical analysis and extensive experimental validation.
