

# Peer Review: "Training Very Deep Networks"

## Summary
This paper introduces "Highway Networks" as a solution to the fundamental challenge of training very deep feedforward neural networks. The authors propose a novel architecture that incorporates an LSTM-inspired gating mechanism (transform and carry gates) to facilitate information flow across layers. The key innovation is a simple modification to standard network layers: y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1-T(x)), where T is a transform gate controlled by a sigmoid function. This architecture allows networks to learn which information to transform and which to carry forward, effectively creating "information highways" that prevent gradient vanishing. The authors demonstrate that highway networks can be trained directly with SGD to depths of 100+ layers, significantly outperforming plain networks that suffer from vanishing gradients. They validate their approach on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, showing improved performance with greater depth and fewer parameters than state-of-the-art methods.

## Strengths

1. **Significant Practical Contribution**: The paper addresses a core challenge in deep learning - training very deep networks - with a simple, effective solution that works across multiple datasets. The results are compelling, showing that 100-layer highway networks outperform similarly sized plain networks by more than two orders of magnitude.

2. **Well-Justified Innovation**: The highway network concept is elegantly motivated by LSTM networks and builds on the intuition that information should flow more freely across layers. The authors provide a clear theoretical foundation for their approach, including the Jacobian analysis showing how the gates can create near-identity mappings.

3. **Strong Experimental Validation**: The paper includes comprehensive experiments across multiple datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100) and network depths. The comparison to plain networks and Fitnets is particularly convincing, demonstrating that highway networks can match or exceed state-of-the-art results with fewer parameters.

4. **Insightful Analysis**: The analysis of transform gate behavior (Figure 2) reveals that highway networks learn to dynamically route information based on input, rather than using static skip connections. The layer importance analysis (Figure 4) provides novel insights into how deep networks actually utilize their depth.

## Weaknesses

1. **Comparison to Residual Networks**: The paper was submitted before ResNet (He et al., 2015) was published, but the authors should acknowledge that the ResNet approach (which appeared around the same time) shares conceptual similarities with highway networks. The paper would benefit from a discussion of how highway networks differ from residual connections (e.g., highway networks learn the gating weights while residual networks use fixed skip connections).

2. **Insufficient Discussion of Limitations**: The paper claims highway networks can be trained "directly" with SGD, but doesn't thoroughly address potential failure cases or limitations. For example, how do highway networks perform on extremely noisy datasets or with limited training data? The authors should discuss whether highway networks might require more data than plain networks to learn the gating mechanisms effectively.

3. **Implementation Details**: While the paper mentions the use of a negative bias initialization for transform gates, it doesn't provide enough detail about how this interacts with different activation functions or network architectures. A more thorough exploration of the hyperparameter space for the transform gate bias would strengthen the paper.

4. **Theoretical Justification**: The paper provides experimental evidence but lacks deeper theoretical analysis of why highway networks prevent vanishing gradients. A more formal analysis of the gradient propagation through highway layers would strengthen the theoretical foundation.

## Quality, Clarity, Originality, and Significance

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound with well-supported claims. The experimental design is appropriate, and the authors carefully discuss both strengths and limitations. The only minor weakness is the lack of comparison to ResNet, which was published around the same time.

**Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized. The mathematical formulation is clear, and the figures effectively illustrate key concepts. The paper provides sufficient detail for experts to reproduce the results.

**Originality**: The highway network concept is a novel adaptation of LSTM gates to feedforward networks. While inspired by LSTM, it's a distinct approach to the depth problem that hadn't been previously explored. The paper clearly differentiates from related work on skip connections, teacher-student networks, and initialization methods.

**Significance**: This work has significant practical implications for deep learning. It enables training networks with unprecedented depth without specialized training procedures. The ability to directly train 100+ layer networks opens up new possibilities for network architecture design and provides a valuable tool for researchers. The paper advances the state of the art in deep network training in a demonstrable way.

## Overall Assessment

This paper makes a significant contribution to deep learning by providing a simple yet effective solution to training very deep networks. The highway network architecture is elegant, well-validated, and has clear practical benefits. While the paper would be strengthened by addressing the comparison to ResNet and providing more detailed analysis of the gate bias hyperparameter, these are minor concerns relative to the paper's substantial contributions.

The work is clearly within the scope of NIPS, addressing a fundamental problem in deep learning with both theoretical and practical significance. The paper is technically sound, well-written, and provides valuable insights into how deep networks can be trained effectively. I believe this paper represents a significant advance in the field and should be accepted at NIPS.

## Recommendation

**Accept** - This paper makes a substantial contribution to deep learning with a well-motivated, well-executed solution to a fundamental problem. The results are convincing, the analysis is insightful, and the work has clear significance for both researchers and practitioners. The minor weaknesses noted are easily addressable in the final version.
