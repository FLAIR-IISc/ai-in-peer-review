## NIPS Review: Training Very Deep Networks

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling and well-executed contribution to the field of deep learning. The introduction of “highway networks” offers a surprisingly simple yet effective solution to the vanishing gradient problem that plagues the training of very deep feedforward networks. The empirical results are strong, demonstrating successful training of networks with over 1000 layers, something previously challenging.  While the core idea isn’t radically novel (drawing clear inspiration from LSTMs), the adaptation to feedforward networks and the thorough experimental validation are significant. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper addresses the longstanding challenge of training very deep feedforward neural networks. Previous work has attempted to solve this through improved optimizers (e.g., Adam), careful initialization strategies (e.g., Xavier/He initialization), and architectural modifications like skip connections (ResNets) and FitNets. The authors highlight these efforts but argue that even with these techniques, training extremely deep networks remains difficult. They draw inspiration from LSTMs, specifically the gating mechanisms that allow for efficient information flow over long sequences. Highway networks introduce similar “transform” and “carry” gates to feedforward layers, allowing information to bypass layers and mitigate the vanishing gradient problem. This approach differs from previous methods by providing a *dynamic* routing mechanism, adapting to the input rather than relying on fixed skip connections.  The authors appropriately position their work within the context of the recent progress on deep learning, specifically noting the concurrent work on LSTM-inspired models [32].

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Insight:** The adaptation of LSTM-inspired gating mechanisms to standard feedforward networks is a significant insight. It’s a conceptually simple idea, but surprisingly effective.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates the ability to train networks with significantly more layers than previously possible using standard techniques. The MNIST and CIFAR experiments clearly showcase the benefits of highway networks, consistently outperforming comparable plain networks.  The comparisons to FitNets are particularly strong, demonstrating competitive performance with fewer parameters.
* **Thorough Experimental Validation:**  The paper includes a comprehensive set of experiments, including optimization studies, comparisons to state-of-the-art methods, and ablation studies (layer importance analysis).  The availability of code and hyperparameters is a major strength, fostering reproducibility.
* **Clear & Well-Written:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the concepts accessible. The mathematical notation is concise and the explanations are intuitive.
* **Analysis of Inner Workings:** The analysis of the transform gate biases and activities (Figure 2) and the layer importance analysis (Figure 4) provides valuable insights into how highway networks function and learn. This goes beyond simply showing that they *work* and begins to explain *why* they work.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the authors provide some intuition about the benefits of the gating mechanism (Jacobian analysis), a more rigorous theoretical understanding of why highway networks are easier to optimize would be valuable. Exploring the relationship between the gating mechanism and the landscape of the loss function could be a fruitful direction for future work.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper addresses initialization, the role of the transform gate bias in achieving stable training could be explored in more detail. A more systematic study of the sensitivity to this hyperparameter would be helpful.
* **Computational Cost:**  The introduction of the transform gates adds additional parameters to the network.  While the paper demonstrates that highway networks can achieve competitive performance with fewer layers, a more direct comparison of computational cost (FLOPs) for comparable accuracy would be beneficial.
* **Potential for Overfitting:** The layer importance analysis suggests that some layers might not be actively contributing to the final result. This raises concerns about potential overfitting, especially on simpler datasets like MNIST.

**4. Arguments for/against Acceptance:**

**Pro Acceptance:**

* Significantly advances the state-of-the-art in training deep feedforward networks.
* Provides a simple and effective solution to a longstanding problem.
* Backed by strong empirical evidence and a thorough experimental evaluation.
* Well-written and clearly explained.
* Code availability promotes reproducibility.

**Con Acceptance:**

* Limited theoretical analysis.
* Potential hyperparameter sensitivity.
* Increased computational cost due to added parameters.



**5. Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:**  Excellent. The paper is technically sound, claims are well-supported, and the work is complete.
* **Clarity:** Excellent.  The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand.
* **Originality:** Good. While inspired by LSTMs, the adaptation to feedforward networks is novel and significant.
* **Significance:** High. The ability to train extremely deep networks has the potential to unlock new possibilities in various applications, and this work provides a valuable tool for achieving that goal.




**Minor Revisions:**

*   Include a discussion of the computational cost (FLOPs) of highway networks compared to similar performing networks without highway layers.
*   Expand the analysis of the transform gate bias and its impact on training stability.
*   Briefly address the potential for overfitting and whether regularization techniques might be beneficial.
* Consider mentioning recent work on differentiable neural architectures which also explores dynamic routing mechanisms as a potentially related direction.




In conclusion, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. I strongly recommend acceptance.
