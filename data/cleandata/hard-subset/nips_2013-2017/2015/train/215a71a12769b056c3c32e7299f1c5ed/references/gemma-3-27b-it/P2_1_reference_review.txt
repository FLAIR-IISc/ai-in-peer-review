## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces “Highway Networks,” a novel neural network architecture inspired by LSTM, designed to facilitate the training of extremely deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to introduce “highway” connections, dynamically controlled by gating mechanisms, which allow information to flow across many layers with reduced attenuation. The authors demonstrate through extensive experimentation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 that highway networks can be trained to significantly greater depths than traditional "plain" networks using standard SGD, and achieve competitive or superior performance.  The paper also provides insightful analysis of the learned gating mechanisms and layer importance.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a Core Problem:** The difficulty of training very deep networks is a fundamental challenge in deep learning. This paper offers a compelling and novel architectural solution.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experiments convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks. The results on MNIST, and particularly the comparisons to FitNets on CIFAR-10/100, are significant.  The ability to train much deeper networks *without* resorting to multi-stage training is a key advantage.
* **Thorough Analysis:**  The paper goes beyond simply presenting results. The analysis of the learned gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance provides valuable insights into *how* highway networks function, and why they are effective. This is a major strength.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The notation is clear, and the architectural concepts are explained intuitively.
* **Reproducibility:** The authors provide links to code and hyperparameters, promoting reproducibility, which is commendable.
* **Novelty:** While inspired by LSTM, the application of adaptive gating to feedforward networks to address the vanishing gradient problem is a novel and impactful idea.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the authors mention hyperparameter tuning, the details are somewhat limited. It would be beneficial to have a more detailed discussion of the sensitivity of performance to different hyperparameters, especially the transform gate bias.  How robust are the results to different initializations beyond the explored range?
* **Computational Cost:** The introduction of gating mechanisms adds parameters to the model.  While the authors demonstrate good performance with comparable parameter counts to other methods, the additional computational cost of the gates themselves is not explicitly discussed. 
* **Limited Comparison to Modern Architectures:** The comparisons to FitNets are valuable, but the paper could be strengthened by a brief comparison to more recent architectures that also address the depth problem, such as residual networks (ResNets).  Acknowledging ResNets and explicitly contrasting the highway network approach would be beneficial. (This was published concurrently with ResNets, so it’s understandable it wasn’t available during submission, but it's worth acknowledging in a later version.)
* **Figure 4 Interpretation:** The layer importance analysis (Figure 4) is intriguing, but the explanation regarding unused layers is somewhat speculative. More analysis is needed to solidify this claim.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The claims are well-supported by experimental evidence and the analysis is insightful. The work is complete and presents a significant contribution. The authors are careful in evaluating both strengths and weaknesses.
* **Clarity:** The paper is very clear and well-organized. The notation is well-defined, and the concepts are explained in an accessible manner.
* **Originality:** The approach is novel and represents a significant departure from traditional feedforward network architectures. The authors adequately reference related work, although adding a discussion of concurrent work (like ResNets, if a later version is considered) would be useful.
* **Significance:** The results are highly significant. The ability to train extremely deep networks directly with SGD has the potential to impact a wide range of applications. The insights into information flow and layer importance are also valuable contributions.


**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Solves a crucial problem in deep learning (training very deep networks).
* Demonstrates strong empirical results across multiple datasets.
* Provides insightful analysis that goes beyond simply reporting performance numbers.
* Clear writing and good organization.
* Promotes reproducibility with publicly available code.

**Against:**

* Hyperparameter sensitivity needs more detailed discussion.
* Computational cost of gating mechanisms could be a concern.
* Limited comparison to more recent advanced architectures.




**Overall Recommendation:**

I **strongly recommend acceptance** of this paper. It presents a novel, technically sound, and empirically validated solution to a fundamental problem in deep learning.  The thorough analysis and clear writing make it a valuable contribution to the field. While there are minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality and significance of the work.  This paper has the potential to influence future research on deep neural network architectures.
