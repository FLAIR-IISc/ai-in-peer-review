- Introduces Highway Networks: LSTM-inspired gating to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Demonstrates effective training with standard SGD on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, achieving competitive or superior performance.  
- Addresses the long-standing problem of training very deep networks with a compelling architectural solution.  
- Novel application of adaptive gating mechanisms from recurrent to feedforward networks.  
- Strong empirical validation across multiple datasets and depth regimes, with insightful comparison to FitNets.  
- Detailed analysis of learned gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance offering interpretability.  
- Well-written, clearly organized paper with clear mathematical notation.  
- Publicly available code promotes reproducibility and follow-up work.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (notably initial transform gate bias) may require careful tuning per task.  
- Additional parameters from transform and carry gates introduce computational overhead that is not fully analyzed.  
- Lacks direct comparative analysis with Residual Networks (ResNets), a key contemporary baseline.  
- Analysis could be deeper, e.g., correlating gate activity with feature relevance.  
- Overall judged technically sound, original, and significant; recommended for acceptance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fundamental problem addressed, novel effective architecture, strong results and analysis, clear writing, and code release.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing ResNet comparison, hyperparameter sensitivity, and limited computational cost discussion.  
- Final recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance; reviewer confidence 9/10.
