- Introduces Highway Networks: feedforward architecture with transform and carry gates (y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x))) to control information flow.  
- Enables direct SGD training of very deep networks (100+ layers) where plain networks fail.  
- Empirical results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 show competitive or superior performance with fewer parameters.  
- Provides analysis of gating behavior and layer importance (including lesioning experiments).  
- Experiments are well-designed, with ablation studies and statistical reporting across runs.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis explaining why highway networks improve trainability.  
- Reproducibility could be improved with more implementation details (e.g., exact layer code/params).  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and uses helpful figures and precise notation.  
- Major omission: no comparison to ResNets, which use related skip connections and emerged concurrently.  
- Novel application of LSTM-like gating to feedforward networks, but originality is reduced by ResNets' concurrent work.  
- Significant practical contribution to training very deep networks and insights into information routing.  
- Significance tempered by ResNets achieving similar/better results with simpler identity additions.  
- Strengths: solves core deep-learning training problem, strong empirical evidence, insightful analysis, clear writing.  
- Weaknesses: no theoretical backing, insufficient ResNet comparison, limited discussion of computational cost of gating.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—authors should thoroughly address and contrast with ResNets.
