- Introduces "highway networks": feedforward layers with LSTM-inspired transform and carry gates enabling y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)).
- Demonstrates highway networks can be trained to extreme depths (100+ layers) with standard SGD, outperforming plain networks.
- Experiments are rigorous and reproducible across MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 with multiple random runs and thorough comparisons.
- Provides insightful empirical analysis of transform gate behavior and information routing (Figures 2–4).
- Lacks theoretical justification for why highway networks mitigate vanishing gradients and no formal gradient-flow analysis.
- Relies on negative-bias initialization as a hyperparameter but doesn’t fully explore its necessity or effect on generalization.
- Paper is well-written with clear notation and illustrative figures, but CIFAR architecture details are insufficient for full reproducibility.
- Fails to discuss comparison to ResNets and does not clearly differentiate from a related LSTM-inspired model [32].
- Original contribution: dynamic gating mechanism (vs. fixed skip connections) enabling direct training of very deep nets without distillation or staged training.
- Significant practical impact: can match or exceed performance of wider, shallower networks with fewer parameters and informs network design.
- Strength: compelling empirical evidence of scalability to extreme depths using standard training methods.
- Strength: thorough experimental validation across datasets and architectures.
- Strength: insightful analysis of gating and information flow.
- Weakness: missing comparison to contemporaneous ResNets and limited theoretical grounding.
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions to address ResNet comparison and clarify relation to [32].
