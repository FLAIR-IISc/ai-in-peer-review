- Overall assessment: Strong, significant contribution introducing Highway Networks that enable training extremely deep feedforward nets with standard SGD; recommend acceptance.  
- Problem addressed: Training very deep feedforward networks is difficult due to vanishing gradients and optimization challenges.  
- Proposed method: Highway Networks use LSTM-inspired transform and carry gates to allow direct information flow and easier gradient propagation.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on LSTM gating ideas and acknowledges related work on optimizers, initialization, skip connections, and FitNets/Srivastava et al.  
- Novelty & significance: Applying gating to feedforward nets to train hundreds of layers is an important and practical advance.  
- Empirical results: Convincing experiments on MNIST and CIFAR-10/100 show clear advantages over plain nets and competitive performance with state-of-the-art.  
- Ablation & analysis: Layer importance and gate-bias/activity studies give useful insight into network behavior and data-dependent routing.  
- Clarity & presentation: Paper is well-written with clear motivation and concise mathematical formulation.  
- Reproducibility: Code and hyperparameter search results are publicly available.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Initial transform gate bias selection appears heuristic and needs more systematic study.  
- Limited gate exploration: The paper focuses on a simple T = 1 − C gating; more complex or adaptive gates were not explored.  
- Computational cost: Training very deep highway networks may be computationally expensive; more discussion/comparison of resources is needed.  
- Theoretical understanding: Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis explaining why highway networks are easier to train.  
- Pros for acceptance: Significant improvement in deep training, strong empirical validation, clarity, code release, and enabling extreme depth.  
- Cons for acceptance: Hyperparameter sensitivity, limited gating variants explored, and absence of comprehensive theory.  
- Questions for authors: Request systematic exploration of initial transform bias and its impact on speed/performance.  
- Questions for authors: Ask whether more complex or adaptive gating functions were tried and what results were observed.  
- Questions for authors: Request estimates of computational cost (training time, memory) for 100+ layer highway nets vs comparable deep conv nets.  
- Questions for authors: Seek intuition or preliminary theory on why negative initialization of transform gates works well.
