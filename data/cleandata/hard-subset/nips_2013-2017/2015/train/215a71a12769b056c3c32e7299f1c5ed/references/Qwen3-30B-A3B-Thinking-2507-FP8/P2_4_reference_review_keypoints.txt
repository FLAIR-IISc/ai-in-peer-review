- Introduces Highway Networks with transform and carry gates (inspired by LSTMs) enabling training of very deep feedforward nets (>100 layers) via information highways.
- Demonstrates through experiments on MNIST and CIFAR that highway networks maintain performance as depth increases while plain networks degrade.
- Paper is technically sound with clear math (Eqns 2–3) and well-designed experiments and baselines.
- Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis explaining why highway networks improve optimization or gradient flow.
- Clear writing, consistent notation, and effective figures illustrating gate behavior and information flow.
- Insufficient explanation of why negative bias initialization works and how it differs from standard initializations.
- Novel adaptation of LSTM-style gating to feedforward nets, but originality is weakened by failing to engage with concurrent ResNet work.
- Fails to acknowledge or compare to ResNets, a significant oversight given ResNets’ similar skip-connection solution and later dominance.
- Does not convincingly show superiority over simpler skip-connection approaches (ResNets) or clear performance gains on major benchmarks.
- Limited comparisons to other modern techniques (e.g., batch normalization) and unclear computational efficiency relative to alternatives.
- Strengths: enables extreme-depth training, detailed analysis of transform gates, practical training guidelines, and solid experimental design.
- Weaknesses: omission of ResNet comparison, weak theoretical justification, limited modern baselines, and only marginal CIFAR gains.
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions—must address ResNet omission, add theoretical analysis, benchmark comparisons, and efficiency discussion.
