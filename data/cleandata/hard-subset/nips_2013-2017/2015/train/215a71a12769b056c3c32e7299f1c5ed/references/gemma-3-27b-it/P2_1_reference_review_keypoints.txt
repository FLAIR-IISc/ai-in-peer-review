- Introduces Highway Networks: LSTM-inspired gated feedforward architecture enabling information flow across very deep networks.  
- Demonstrates that highway networks can be trained to much greater depths with standard SGD on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Shows competitive or superior performance compared to baselines and FitNets, without multi-stage training.  
- Provides thorough analysis of learned gates, gate biases, activity patterns, and layer importance.  
- Clear writing, intuitive notation, and well-organized presentation.  
- Promotes reproducibility by providing code and hyperparameters.  
- Novel application of adaptive gating to address vanishing gradients in feedforward nets.  
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter sensitivity, especially transform gate bias and initialization robustness.  
- Computational cost of added gating parameters and runtime is not explicitly analyzed.  
- Limited comparison/acknowledgement of concurrent depth-focused architectures (e.g., ResNets).  
- Interpretation of layer importance (Figure 4) is somewhat speculative and needs more analysis.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant, and insightful work with strong empirical support.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
