This paper introduces "Highway Networks," a novel architectural modification to deep feedforward networks designed to ease training by enabling more direct information flow across layers, inspired by LSTM gating mechanisms. The central idea—allowing layers to dynamically choose between transforming inputs or simply carrying them forward—is a clever and potentially impactful contribution. The paper demonstrates that these networks can be trained to depths previously inaccessible to standard feedforward architectures, maintaining competitive performance on MNIST and CIFAR datasets.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network concept is genuinely novel. While inspired by LSTMs, the application of adaptive gating to feedforward networks in this manner hasn't been explored before (and the authors acknowledge related, more recent work). The dynamic routing capability is a significant departure from standard architectures and skip connections.
*   **Impactful Results:** Demonstrating successful training of networks with 100+ layers using standard SGD is a noteworthy achievement. The improved optimization compared to plain networks, particularly at depth, is compelling evidence for the effectiveness of the approach. The single-stage training success in the face of prior two-stage requirements for deep networks is also a strong point.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and the highway network formulation is clearly presented with accompanying equations. The motivation and intuition behind the gating mechanism are also well-explained.
*   **Analysis:** The analysis attempting to understand *how* these networks function, including the visualization of gate activity and layer importance, goes beyond simply demonstrating performance and offers valuable insights. The visualization in Figure 2 and the layer ablation study (Figure 4) are especially helpful.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the CIFAR results are promising, the comparison to state-of-the-art is somewhat superficial. The authors acknowledge that better results are possible with more extensive hyperparameter tuning and architecture exploration. A more thorough comparison with recent, high-performing architectures on CIFAR-10/100 would strengthen the impact.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The initialization of the transform gate bias appears crucial. The paper suggests guidelines, but a more systematic investigation of the sensitivity to this parameter would be valuable.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The paper lacks a strong theoretical justification for *why* highway networks work. While the LSTM analogy is presented, a deeper understanding of the optimization landscape and the role of the gates in facilitating gradient flow would be beneficial.
*   **Minor Clarity Issues:**  The description of how dimensionality changes are handled (using sub-sampling/zero-padding or plain layers) could be more detailed.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper introduces a truly novel architectural component with the potential to significantly impact the training of very deep networks.
*   The empirical results convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks in overcoming the optimization challenges associated with depth.
*   The analysis provides valuable insights into the internal workings of these networks.
*   The paper is clearly written and well-organized.
*   The potential for future research and application is high.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive.
*   The paper lacks a strong theoretical grounding.
*   The reliance on a specific initialization scheme raises concerns about robustness.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a very strong paper that presents a significant and novel contribution to the field. While there are some weaknesses, the core idea and the empirical results are compelling enough to outweigh these concerns. The paper’s bold approach to addressing the challenges of training deep networks is highly commendable. I am willing to overlook the incomplete validation and minor flaws given the novelty and potential impact of the work. This paper represents an important step forward in our ability to train very deep neural networks and has the potential to inspire further research in this area. 

**Recommendation:** Accept.
