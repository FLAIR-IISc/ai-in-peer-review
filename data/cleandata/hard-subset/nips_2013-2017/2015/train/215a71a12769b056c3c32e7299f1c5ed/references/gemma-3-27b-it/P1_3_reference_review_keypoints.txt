- Introduces "Highway Networks," LSTM-inspired gating layers that create information highways enabling very deep feedforward networks.  
- Gates (transform and carry) allow information to flow across many layers, mitigating vanishing/exploding gradients.  
- Empirically demonstrates successful training with standard SGD on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 at depths >100 layers.  
- Shows competitive or superior performance compared to shallower networks and FitNets.  
- Provides analysis and visualizations (gate biases, activity patterns, layer importance) revealing data-dependent routing and layer roles.  
- Novel architectural contribution distinct from prior work on optimizers, initialization, activations, and skip connections.  
- Clear motivation and explicit connection to LSTM literature.  
- Comparisons to FitNets and state-of-the-art conv nets offer helpful context.  
- Paper is well-written with clear notation and concise mathematical formulation.  
- Public code and hyperparameters improve reproducibility.  
- Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis explaining why the specific gating mechanism is so effective.  
- Insufficient discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and reliance on random search.  
- Limited exploration of alternative gate formulations beyond C = 1 − T.  
- Limited details and exploration of convolutional highway layer variants and interactions.  
- Comparisons mostly to contemporaneous methods; missing discussion of more recent architectures.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, strong empirical results, insightful analysis, and reproducible code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical grounding and inadequate hyperparameter and gate-variant exploration.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions, especially to address theoretical justification.
