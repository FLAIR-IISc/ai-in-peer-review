

# Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to enable direct training of extremely deep neural networks through adaptive gating mechanisms inspired by Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks. The authors propose a layer transformation that combines a transformation component with a carry component, controlled by a transform gate (T) and carry gate (1-T). This architecture allows information to flow through the network with minimal attenuation, addressing the core challenge of training very deep networks where gradient propagation becomes increasingly difficult. The work builds on previous efforts to overcome training difficulties in deep networks, including better initialization strategies [16,17], skip connections [22,23], and knowledge distillation [25], but differs by introducing an adaptive gating mechanism rather than fixed skip connections.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper provides a clear theoretical foundation for highway networks, demonstrating how the architecture enables information flow through the network. The experimental results are robust, with thorough comparisons between highway networks and plain networks across multiple datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100) and varying depths. The 100-layer highway network's performance significantly outperforms similarly-sized plain networks, supporting the core claim.

2. **Novel Insights:** The paper offers valuable analysis of information routing in highway networks (Figures 2-4), revealing that the gating mechanism enables data-dependent routing where different blocks are activated for different inputs. The lesioning experiments (Figure 4) provide unique insights into layer importance, showing that highway networks can effectively utilize all layers for complex tasks like CIFAR-100, while simpler tasks like MNIST may not require full depth.

3. **Practical Impact:** The work demonstrates that highway networks can be trained with simple SGD without complex initialization schemes or two-stage training procedures, making them accessible to practitioners. The results on CIFAR-10 show that highway networks can match or exceed the performance of more complex FitNet architectures with a single training stage.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Comparison to ResNets:** The paper was submitted to NIPS 2015, but fails to acknowledge or compare with the concurrent ResNet work [36] (which was accepted to NIPS 2015), which also uses skip connections to enable training of very deep networks. While highway networks and ResNets are conceptually distinct (adaptive gating vs. fixed skip connections), the paper should explicitly discuss this relationship to situate its contribution within the field.

2. **Ad-hoc Initialization Strategy:** The negative bias initialization for transform gates is presented as a key enabler, but the paper doesn't sufficiently justify why this specific initialization is necessary or whether it could be eliminated with better training techniques. This makes the approach somewhat dependent on a hyperparameter that may not generalize across all architectures.

3. **Computational Cost:** The paper doesn't analyze the additional computational cost of the gating mechanism (transform gate parameters), which could be significant for very large networks. While the authors mention that highway networks can use fewer parameters than state-of-the-art methods, they don't provide a detailed cost-benefit analysis.

4. **Theoretical Limitations:** The paper doesn't provide a theoretical analysis of the conditions under which highway networks can be trained, beyond the empirical evidence. A more rigorous analysis of the stability of gradient flow through highway networks would strengthen the theoretical foundation.

**Recommendation:**

The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning by introducing a practical and effective architecture for training extremely deep networks. The highway network design is clearly novel, the experiments are comprehensive, and the insights into information routing are valuable. While the paper has some weaknesses in its comparison to concurrent work and justification of the initialization strategy, these do not undermine the core contribution.

The significance of this work is high, as it directly addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning that has implications for both theory and practice. The ability to train networks with hundreds of layers directly through SGD opens up new possibilities for architecture exploration and could lead to more powerful models for a variety of tasks.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Novel architecture that solves a fundamental problem in deep learning
- Comprehensive experimental validation across multiple datasets and depths
- Unique insights into information flow and layer importance
- Practical approach that can be easily implemented by practitioners

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
- Limited comparison to concurrent ResNet work
- Ad-hoc initialization strategy that requires careful tuning
- No theoretical analysis of gradient flow properties

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. The authors should address the comparison to ResNets and provide more justification for the initialization strategy in any revision, but the core contribution is significant and well-supported. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
