This paper introduces “highway networks,” a novel architecture designed to facilitate training of very deep feedforward networks by incorporating gating mechanisms inspired by LSTMs to allow for unimpeded information flow. The core idea—allowing layers to selectively “carry” information, bypassing transformation—is both intuitive and elegantly implemented. The authors demonstrate convincingly that highway networks can be trained with standard SGD to depths previously inaccessible to plain networks, and that they generalize well.

The paper is well-motivated, acknowledging the increasing importance of depth in neural networks while pinpointing the training difficulties that arise with it. The related work is comprehensive, covering a wide range of techniques attempting to address the vanishing gradient problem and improve training dynamics, including better optimizers, initialization schemes, skip connections, and knowledge distillation. The authors correctly position highway networks within this landscape, highlighting their distinct advantage: enabling direct training of extremely deep networks without relying on more complex procedures or specialized activations.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty and Insight:** The highway network architecture is a genuinely novel contribution. The analogy to LSTMs is insightful and the resulting gating mechanism is a clever way to address the challenges of training deep feedforward networks.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results are compelling. The paper demonstrates, through a series of well-designed experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, that highway networks can be trained significantly deeper than plain networks without sacrificing performance.  The comparison to FitNets is particularly strong.
* **Theoretical Justification (Partial):** While not a full theoretical analysis, the paper provides some intuition as to *why* highway networks work, through the Jacobian analysis and the discussion of information highways.
* **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The mathematical notation is clear and the explanations are concise. The analysis section, including the visualizations of gate activations and layer importance, adds significant value.
* **Code Availability:** The authors provide publicly available code, which is crucial for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** The Jacobian analysis, while a good start, feels somewhat superficial. A more thorough theoretical understanding of why highway networks avoid the vanishing/exploding gradient problem would be highly valuable.  The paper hints at a connection to LSTMs, but doesn’t fully formalize it.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper acknowledges the importance of initializing the transform gate bias, but doesn’t provide extensive guidance on how to tune this parameter.  A more systematic exploration of the hyperparameter space, and perhaps a discussion of how the optimal bias value changes with network depth or dataset complexity, would be beneficial.
* **Gate Sparsity Interpretation:**  The analysis suggests that many gates are closed (sparse activation), but the implications of this are not fully explored. Is this a desirable property? Does it contribute to regularization?  More discussion is needed.
* **Comparison to other recent deep network architectures:** While the FitNet comparison is good, a more thorough comparison with other very recent architectures designed for depth (e.g., ResNets, DenseNets, which came *after* this NIPS submission but are now dominant) would strengthen the paper’s position.  Acknowledging these developments, even briefly, is important.



**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks.
* The experimental results are strong and demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key concepts.
* The code is publicly available, facilitating reproducibility and further research.
* The work represents a significant step forward in our ability to train and understand deep neural networks.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

* The theoretical analysis is limited and doesn't fully explain why highway networks work.
* Hyperparameter tuning, specifically the transform gate bias, could be better explored.
* The paper would benefit from a discussion of more recent architectures addressing the same problem.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is a clever and effective solution to the problem of training very deep networks. While the theoretical analysis could be more thorough and some aspects of the architecture could be explored in more detail, the strong experimental results and the novelty of the approach justify acceptance.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.  Specifically, I’d suggest a slightly expanded discussion of the theoretical underpinnings and a brief acknowledgement of more recent deep network architectures.



**Rating:** Accept (with minor revisions)
