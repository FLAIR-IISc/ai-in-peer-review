- Proposes highway networks with adaptive gating (inspired by LSTMs) to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Shows these networks can be trained with SGD to depths previously unattainable and perform competitively on MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100.  
- Clearly motivated problem and thorough related-work review (vanishing gradients, initialization, skip connections, knowledge distillation).  
- Sensible conceptual adaptation of LSTM ideas to feedforward architectures.  
- Experimental evaluation compares to plain nets, FitNets, and state-of-the-art methods; includes useful ablation studies (e.g., layer importance).  
- Code and hyperparameters are publicly released, supporting reproducibility.  
- Novel architecture offering a meaningful advance for training very deep networks.  
- Empirical results convincingly demonstrate improved trainability and generalization at much greater depths, notably versus FitNets.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear explanations, consistent notation, and well-defined equations.  
- Lacks rigorous theoretical justification for why highway networks improve gradient flow or prevent vanishing/exploding gradients.  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (notably transform gate bias bT) is a concern and not systematically explored.  
- Adds computational overhead via transform gates, but trade-offs between depth and computational cost are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Limited analysis of gate behavior and how gates route information during training and inference.  
- Overall: strong, valuable contribution with compelling empirical results; recommended for acceptance despite theoretical and sensitivity shortcomings.
