- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-inspired gating to enable training of very deep feedforward networks via “information highways.”
- Demonstrates training with SGD of networks over 100 layers on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100, outperforming plain networks.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly explains motivation, architecture, and experiments with clear notation and helpful diagrams.
- Relates work appropriately to prior research (optimization, initialization, skip connections, FitNets, recurrent networks) with comprehensive references.
- Ablation studies and analyses (layer importance, information routing) provide useful insight into network behavior.
- Novelty: applies LSTM-style gating to feedforward nets, offering a new solution to vanishing gradients in deep networks.
- Technical soundness: empirical results and comparisons are convincing and well-benchmarked.
- Significance: enables training of much deeper models and allows examination of layer importance and routing.
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters (especially initial transform gate bias) not fully explored.
- Weakness: increased parameters from gating raise unanswered questions about inference speed and memory cost.
- Weakness: experiments focus mainly on convolutional variants; broader architectural exploration is limited.
- Weakness: lacks deeper theoretical analysis of why highway networks improve gradient flow and learning.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a key challenge, proposes an effective novel architecture, and provides strong empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: needs more thorough hyperparameter study, inference cost analysis, broader architectural tests, and stronger theoretical justification.
- Overall recommendation: strong, technically sound paper with significant contribution; recommend acceptance despite minor shortcomings.
