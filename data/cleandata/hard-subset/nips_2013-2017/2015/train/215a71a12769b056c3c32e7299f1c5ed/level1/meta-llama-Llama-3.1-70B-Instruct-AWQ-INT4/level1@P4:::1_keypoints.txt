- Introduces Highway Networks to enable training very deep nets by allowing unimpeded information flow, inspired by LSTM gating.
- Shows Highway Networks can be trained directly with SGD and generalize well on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.
- Provides a thorough review of previous deep-training techniques and their limitations.
- Technically sound with clear notation, equations, and well-organized presentation.
- Architecture components (transform and carry gates) are clearly described and motivated.
- Empirical and theoretical claims are well-supported by analysis and experiments.
- Paper is clearly written; figures and tables effectively illustrate results.
- Novel contribution: adaptive gating units for feedforward networks to handle very deep architectures.
- Significant because it offers a practical tool for training much deeper networks and improves performance potential.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clarity, technical soundness, and strong empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: paper may be too specialized/technical for some readers.
- Possible omission of some relevant prior work on training deep networks.
- Insufficient exploration of the limitations of the Highway Network architecture.
- Suggestions: expand discussion of limitations, better relate to other approaches, and add broader experimental validation.
