- Proposes Highway Networks using LSTM-inspired adaptive gating to create information highways that mitigate vanishing/exploding gradients.  
- Shows that very deep feedforward networks (hundreds of layers) can be trained with standard SGD on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 with competitive or superior performance.  
- Presents highway gating as a more general/adaptive alternative to prior fixes (better optimizers, initialization schemes, skip connections).  
- Analysis of transform gate behavior (Figures 2 & 4) offers insight and support that multiple layers are effectively utilized.  
- Provides publicly available code and detailed hyperparameter search information.  
- Main strength: elegant, simple, and effective solution enabling direct training of very deep networks.  
- Weakness: insufficient ablation studies, especially on different gating functions and transform gate initialization.  
- Weakness: comparisons to more recent or advanced state-of-the-art architectures are limited.  
- Weakness: explanation of improved gradient flow lacks strong theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: layer importance analysis relies on lesioning, which may be an unreliable indicator.  
- For acceptance: novelty, strong experimental results, clear analysis of gating, and available code.  
- Against acceptance: limited ablations, incomplete SOTA comparisons, and need for stronger theoretical support.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant contribution with strengths outweighing limitations.
