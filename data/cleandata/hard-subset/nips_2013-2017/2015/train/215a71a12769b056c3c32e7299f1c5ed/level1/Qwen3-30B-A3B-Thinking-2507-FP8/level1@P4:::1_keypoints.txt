- Introduces highway networks, an LSTM-inspired gating architecture (transform and carry gates) to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Claims highway networks address poor propagation of activations/gradients in deep "plain" networks by providing information highways.  
- Empirical results show highway networks can be trained directly with SGD at extreme depths and outperform plain networks on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Experimental design is rigorous: normalized initialization, thorough hyperparameter search, multiple runs, and statistical significance reporting.  
- Lesioning experiments demonstrate highway networks utilize many layers for complex tasks, unlike plain networks.  
- Paper lacks theoretical analysis explaining why highway networks are easier to train than plain networks.  
- Writing, notation, and figures are clear and the paper provides sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Highway networks are presented as a novel and distinct contribution relative to skip connections and LSTM, offering dynamic routing of information.  
- The work is significant: enables direct training of extremely deep networks, matches/exceeds performance with fewer parameters, and opens new research directions.  
- Strengths: clear problem statement, strong empirical validation, insightful analysis of information flow, practical impact, and high-quality presentation.  
- Weaknesses: missing theoretical justification, limited demonstration of claimed "hundreds" of layers (max 100 shown), unclear computational overhead of gating, and few additional example architectures.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, empirically compelling, and significant — recommend acceptance.
