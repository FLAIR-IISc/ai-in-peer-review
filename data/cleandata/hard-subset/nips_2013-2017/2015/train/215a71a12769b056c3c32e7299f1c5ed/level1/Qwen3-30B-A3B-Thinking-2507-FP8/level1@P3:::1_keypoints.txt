- Introduces highway networks: adaptive gating (transform T and carry 1-T) to enable direct training of very deep networks.  
- Combines a transformation and carry component per layer to allow information flow with minimal attenuation.  
- Inspired by LSTM gating; differs from prior work by using adaptive gates instead of fixed skip connections.  
- Technical soundness: clear theoretical foundation and robust experiments showing 100-layer highway nets outperform plain nets.  
- Empirical validation across MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 and varying depths supports core claims.  
- Novel insights: analysis of data-dependent routing via gates and lesioning experiments revealing layer importance variations by task.  
- Practical impact: can be trained with simple SGD without complex initialization or multi-stage training; matches/exceeds FitNet on CIFAR-10.  
- Weakness — limited comparison to concurrent ResNet work and no explicit discussion of their relationship.  
- Weakness — reliance on ad-hoc negative-bias initialization for transform gates without sufficient justification.  
- Weakness — no detailed analysis of additional computational cost or parameter overhead introduced by gating.  
- Weakness — lacks rigorous theoretical analysis of conditions or stability for gradient flow through highway networks.  
- Recommendation: significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address comparisons and initialization rationale.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture solving a fundamental problem, comprehensive experiments, insightful analysis, and practical applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient ResNet comparison, ad-hoc initialization tuning, and missing theoretical gradient-flow analysis.
