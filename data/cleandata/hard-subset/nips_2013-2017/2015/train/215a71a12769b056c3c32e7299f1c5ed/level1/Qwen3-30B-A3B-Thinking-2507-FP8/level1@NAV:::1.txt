

This paper introduces "highway networks," a novel architecture designed to overcome the training difficulties of extremely deep neural networks. The authors propose a gating mechanism inspired by LSTMs that allows information to flow across many layers without attenuation, enabling direct training of networks with hundreds of layers using simple SGD. The paper builds on previous work addressing depth challenges, including normalized initialization [16,17], skip connections [22,23], and knowledge distillation [25], but provides a more unified solution by integrating adaptive gating into the network architecture itself.

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality through extensive experiments across MNIST and CIFAR datasets. Highway networks consistently outperform plain networks at increasing depths (e.g., 100-layer highway networks outperform similarly-sized plain networks by over two orders of magnitude on MNIST), with thorough ablation studies showing how information flows through the network. The layer importance analysis (Figure 4) provides valuable insights into how deep networks actually utilize their depth.

Clarity is excellent, with clear notation, well-organized figures, and sufficient detail for reproducibility. The highway network formulation (Equation 3) is elegant and well-explained, with the gating mechanism intuitively described as "information highways."

Originality is strong for its time (2015), as the adaptive gating approach was novel compared to fixed skip connections. However, the paper doesn't sufficiently distinguish itself from later ResNet work (which emerged shortly after), though it's clear the highway concept predates ResNets.

Significance is high, as the paper directly addresses a critical bottleneck in deep learning: the difficulty of training very deep networks. By enabling direct training of extremely deep architectures, it paved the way for subsequent advances. The analysis of information routing (Figure 2-4) provides valuable theoretical insights beyond just empirical results.

Pro: Novel architecture enabling direct training of very deep networks, thorough experimental validation, clear theoretical insights into information flow.
Con: Limited comparison to ResNets (published around same time), minimal discussion of computational overhead of gating mechanisms.

Overall, this is a high-quality, significant contribution that advances the state of the art in deep learning. The paper meets all NIPS quality criteria and makes a lasting impact on the field. I recommend acceptance.
