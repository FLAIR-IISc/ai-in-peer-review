- Addresses the key challenge of training very deep feedforward networks plagued by vanishing/exploding gradients.  
- Proposes "highway networks" that gate layer outputs as y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)), enabling learned skip connections.  
- Gates (T(x)) are sigmoid functions; initializing transform-biases negative encourages identity paths and stable SGD training for hundreds of layers.  
- Architecture lets the network dynamically regulate information flow and choose between identity or nonlinear transforms per layer.  
- Strong empirical results: highway nets maintain performance up to hundreds of layers on MNIST where plain nets fail.  
- On CIFAR-10, deep thin highway nets match or outperform FitNets without complex teacher-student training.  
- Analysis shows data-dependent routing by gates and that networks learn an appropriate "effective depth" for tasks.  
- Lesioning study provides novel visualization of how depth is utilized per task complexity.  
- High technical quality: method is sound, well-motivated, and experimentally validated with controlled optimization comparisons.  
- Clarity is excellent: clear motivation, precise formulation, informative figures, and released source code for reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel application of LSTM-style gating to feedforward depth and a new perspective on learned effective depth.  
- Significance: enables practical training of orders-of-magnitude deeper feedforward models and opens new architectural directions.  
- Main criticism: gating idea is inspired by LSTMs, so the core mechanism is not entirely novel, but its application here is impactful.
