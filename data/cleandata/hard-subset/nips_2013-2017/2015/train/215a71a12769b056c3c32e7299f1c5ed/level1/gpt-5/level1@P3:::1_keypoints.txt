- Proposes Highway Networks: per-unit gating y = H(x)·T(x) + x·(1−T(x)) with negative-bias init to create trainable identity paths.
- Shows very deep fully-connected networks (50–100 layers) train reliably on MNIST, unlike plain nets of equal size.
- Shows convolutional highway nets train in one stage on CIFAR-10/100 and match/exceed FitNets without teacher hints.
- Demonstrates gates learn meaningful, input-dependent routing via gate-activity visualizations and lesion studies.
- Builds on LSTM gating, normalized inits/rectifiers, skip connections, deep supervision, and distillation; novelty is LSTM-style gates for feedforward identity paths.
- Layer formulation is simple and well-motivated; near-identity Jacobian when T≈0 mitigates gradient attenuation.
- Training protocol (SGD+momentum, ReLU, biased T) and controlled hyperparameter search are reasonable and reproducible.
- Empirical evidence convincingly supports improved optimization with depth, meaningful routing, and thin-deep models matching FitNets.
- Missing ablation comparing to simple fixed skip/additive identity shortcuts, leaving unique value of adaptive gating unclear.
- No comparison to batch normalization, a strong baseline for easing deep-net optimization.
- Carry gate tied as C=1−T; effects of allowing independent carry gate C not studied.
- Parameter and compute overhead from gates, and training/inference speed, are not quantified.
- Optimization results emphasize best runs rather than reporting distributions (medians/IQRs) to show robustness.
- Claim of training >1000 layers is anecdotal and lacks quantitative results.
- Paper is clearly written with helpful intuition and reproducible architecture details, but omits some training specifics and per-layer FLOP/parameter counts.
- Core idea and routing/layer-importance analyses are original and valuable.
- Method is significant for enabling reliable training of very deep nets and likely to have high impact.
- Strengths: simple effective architecture, strong depth-optimization evidence, competitive CIFAR results without hints, insightful analyses, public code.
- Weaknesses: missing critical comparisons (fixed skips, BN), limited variant exploration, no overhead quantification, emphasizes best-case runs, no large-scale (ImageNet) evaluation.
- Suggested improvements: add ablations (fixed shortcuts, scalar T, independent C, BN), report distributions and gradient diagnostics, quantify parameters/FLOPs and runtimes, explore projection shortcuts and gate-bias effects, and add larger-scale results.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, general, simple mechanism addressing a central challenge with strong empirical and analytical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks critical ablations and BN comparisons, limited scale of evaluation, incomplete computational/robustness reporting.
- Overall assessment: strong, timely contribution that leans toward acceptance pending additional comparisons and reporting.
