- Introduces highway networks: gating mechanisms (like LSTMs) that let layers selectively carry information, enabling very deep feedforward networks.
- Core idea is intuitive and well-implemented: layers can bypass transformations to maintain information flow.
- Demonstrates highway networks can be trained with standard SGD to depths previously inaccessible to plain networks and generalize well.
- Well-motivated paper that situates depth challenges and training difficulties in context.
- Comprehensive related work and correct positioning among methods for vanishing gradients and improved training dynamics.
- Novelty and insight are strong: the LSTM analogy and gating mechanism are genuine contributions.
- Empirical results are compelling across MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, with strong comparison to FitNets.
- Partial theoretical justification via Jacobian analysis and “information highways,” but not a full formal treatment.
- Clear presentation, concise notation, useful visualizations of gate activations and layer importance.
- Publicly available code enhances reproducibility.
- Weakness: theoretical analysis is limited and could be deeper/formalized.
- Weakness: sensitivity to transform gate bias and lack of systematic hyperparameter guidance.
- Weakness: gate sparsity observed but its implications (regularization, desirability) are not fully explored.
- Weakness: limited comparison to subsequent deep architectures (e.g., ResNets, DenseNets) that address depth.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand theoretical discussion and acknowledge recent architectures; overall a significant contribution.
