This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate training of very deep feedforward neural networks. The core idea is to incorporate “information highways” – adaptive gating mechanisms inspired by LSTMs – that allow information to flow across many layers without attenuation. The authors demonstrate that these networks can be trained directly with SGD to depths previously unattainable with standard architectures, and achieve competitive performance on several benchmark datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100).

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a critical challenge in deep learning: the difficulty of training very deep networks. The problem is clearly articulated, and the paper provides a concise yet thorough review of related work, including techniques addressing vanishing gradients, initialization strategies, skip connections, and knowledge distillation.  The authors correctly identify the limitations of existing approaches and position highway networks as a potential solution. The connection to LSTM networks is a sensible starting point, and the adaptation to feedforward networks appears conceptually sound.  The experimental evaluation is reasonably comprehensive, comparing highway networks to plain networks, FitNets, and state-of-the-art methods. The ablation studies, like examining layer importance (Figure 4), are insightful. The availability of code and hyperparameters is a strong positive.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The highway network architecture is a genuinely novel contribution, offering a potentially effective approach to training very deep networks.
* **Empirical Results:** The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the ability to train networks with significantly more layers than previously possible with standard techniques, with good generalization performance. The comparison to FitNets is particularly strong.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the core concepts and the proposed architecture. The equations are well-defined and the notation is consistent.
* **Thoroughness:** The paper includes a comprehensive review of related work and addresses potential limitations. The ablation studies add further insight.
* **Reproducibility:** The authors have made their code and hyperparameters publicly available, fostering reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Theoretical Justification:** While the LSTM inspiration is reasonable, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of *why* highway networks improve gradient flow or prevent vanishing/exploding gradients would be beneficial. The paper relies heavily on empirical evidence.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The optimal initialization of the transform gate bias (bT) appears crucial. While the authors provide guidance, a more systematic exploration of the sensitivity to this and other hyperparameters would strengthen the findings.
* **Computational Cost:** The introduction of the transform gates adds computational overhead.  The paper doesn’t extensively analyze the trade-off between depth and computational cost compared to other deep learning architectures. While depth is emphasized, the potential efficiency gains from reducing depth using alternative methods are not directly addressed.
* **Limited Exploration of Gate Behavior:** While Figures 2 and 3 provide some insight into the transform gate behavior, a more detailed analysis of how the gates route information during learning and inference would be valuable. What types of features or inputs activate different pathways?



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks, addressing a long-standing challenge in deep learning.
* The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods, especially in terms of achievable depth.
* The paper is well-written, clearly explains the proposed approach, and provides sufficient details for reproducibility.
* The availability of code and hyperparameters is a significant benefit.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The lack of a strong theoretical justification weakens the paper. While empirical results are strong, understanding *why* highway networks work is important.
* The potential hyperparameter sensitivity could limit the practical applicability of the approach.
*  The computational overhead of the gating mechanism is not thoroughly investigated.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. While the lack of a rigorous theoretical analysis and the potential hyperparameter sensitivity are weaknesses, the empirical results are compelling and demonstrate the practical value of highway networks. The paper is well-written, clearly presented, and reproducible.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept**.  I believe this paper is a valuable addition to NIPS and has the potential to influence future research in deep learning. The authors have addressed a significant problem with a novel and effective solution. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above in future work could further strengthen the contribution.
