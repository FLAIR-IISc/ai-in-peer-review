- Introduces highway networks, a novel architecture enabling training of very deep networks.  
- Architecture is LSTM-inspired, using adaptive gating to allow unimpeded information flow across layers.  
- Can be trained directly with SGD even for networks with hundreds of layers.  
- Paper provides detailed model description and thorough analysis of the architecture.  
- Experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 show strong performance and sometimes outperform FitNets and deep supervision.  
- Well-written, clear, concise, and well-organized presentation.  
- Technical contribution is significant and original, with potential high impact on deep learning fields.  
- Experimental design is strong and provides convincing evidence of effectiveness.  
- Lacks a thorough analysis of computational complexity and scalability.  
- Insufficient analysis of robustness to noise and perturbations.  
- Reviewer recommendation: strong accept (rated 9/10), with acceptance arguments emphasizing novelty and experiments and rejection arguments focusing on missing complexity and robustness analyses.
