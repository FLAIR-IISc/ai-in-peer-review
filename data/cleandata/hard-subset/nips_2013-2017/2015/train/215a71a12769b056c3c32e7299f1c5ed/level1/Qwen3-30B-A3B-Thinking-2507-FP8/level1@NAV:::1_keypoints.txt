Introduces "highway networks" with LSTM-like gating to enable training of extremely deep networks via unhindered information flow.  
Integrates adaptive gating into architecture, building on prior ideas (normalized init, skip connections, distillation) for a more unified solution.  
Demonstrates strong empirical results on MNIST and CIFAR; highway nets vastly outperform plain nets at large depths (e.g., 100-layer MNIST).  
Provides thorough ablation studies and layer-importance analysis (Figures 2–4) revealing how information is routed through depth.  
Presentation is clear and reproducible; formulation (Eq. 3) and gating mechanism are well explained and intuitive.  
Original and timely work (2015) introducing adaptive gating ahead of ResNets, though not extensively contrasted with subsequent ResNet work.  
High significance: addresses a core bottleneck in deep learning and enables direct training of extremely deep architectures.  
Pros: novel architecture, thorough experimental validation, and insightful analysis of information flow.  
Cons: limited comparison to ResNets and little discussion of the computational overhead introduced by gates.  
Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution meeting NIPS criteria; recommendation to accept.
