- Proposes "highway networks," a new architecture to enable training of very deep networks using adaptive gating inspired by LSTM.
- Core idea: adaptive gating units regulate information flow, allowing near-unimpeded signal propagation across many layers.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and motivation.
- Makes a significant technical contribution with a thorough analysis of highway network properties.
- Shows highway networks can be trained to extreme depths directly with stochastic gradient descent.
- Claims computational efficiency for the proposed architecture.
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness on multiple benchmark datasets.
- Paper assumes substantial background in deep learning, which may hinder non-expert readers.
- Some experimental results come from a limited number of runs, raising concerns about statistical significance.
- Lacks a detailed comparison with alternative architectures and deep-training techniques.
- Reviewer judges the paper as meeting conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/significant architecture, solid technical analysis, and supportive experimental evidence.
- Arguments against acceptance: steep background assumptions, limited experimental runs, and insufficient comparative analysis.
