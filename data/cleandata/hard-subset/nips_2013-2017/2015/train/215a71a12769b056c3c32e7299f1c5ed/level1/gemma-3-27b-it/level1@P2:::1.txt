This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to facilitate training of very deep feedforward neural networks. The core idea is to incorporate gating mechanisms, inspired by LSTMs, that allow information to flow across many layers without attenuation. The authors demonstrate that these networks can be trained directly with SGD to depths previously inaccessible to standard feedforward networks, and achieve competitive performance on several datasets.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly explaining the challenges of training deep networks and positioning highway networks as a solution. The connection to LSTMs is clearly articulated, and the mathematical formulation of the highway layer is concise and understandable. The experiments are comprehensive, including ablation studies (optimization experiments comparing highway vs. plain networks), and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100. The visualizations (Figure 2 & 4) provide valuable insights into the internal workings of the network and the importance of different layers. The related work section is thorough, citing relevant papers on deep learning, optimization, and recurrent networks. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The highway network architecture is a novel and effective approach to addressing the vanishing gradient problem in deep networks. The use of gating mechanisms, inspired by LSTMs, to enable information flow is a clever idea.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation is clear and correct. The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of highway networks in training very deep networks.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand. The concepts are explained clearly, and the figures are informative.
* **Significance:** The ability to train significantly deeper networks than previously possible has the potential to advance the state-of-the-art in various machine learning tasks. The paper addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The paper includes a wide range of experiments that support the claims made by the authors. These experiments are carefully designed and analyzed.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the authors mention the importance of the transform gate bias, a more detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity would be beneficial.  A systematic exploration of different bias values and other hyperparameters could provide further insights into the behavior of highway networks.
* **Computational Cost:** The introduction of gating mechanisms does increase the number of parameters in the network, potentially increasing computational cost.  A discussion on the trade-off between depth and parameter count would be valuable.  While the paper addresses this point implicitly through comparisons with FitNets, a more explicit analysis of computational cost could strengthen the work.
* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the empirical results are strong, a more formal theoretical analysis of why highway networks work so well would be desirable.  For example, analyzing the gradient flow in highway networks could provide a deeper understanding of their behavior.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* This paper presents a novel and effective architecture for training very deep neural networks.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods.
* The paper is well-written, clear, and concise.
* The work addresses a fundamental challenge in deep learning with significant potential impact.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity.
* A more formal theoretical analysis of the underlying mechanisms would strengthen the work.


**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is a novel and effective approach to addressing the challenges of training very deep networks. The experimental results are compelling, and the paper is exceptionally well-written. Despite some minor weaknesses, the strengths of this work clearly outweigh its limitations.

**Recommendation:**

Strong Accept. This paper is of high quality and suitable for publication at NIPS.
