- Introduces highway networks that use LSTM-style gating to enable training of very deep feedforward networks.  
- Core idea: transform and carry gates allow information to flow across many layers without attenuation.  
- Demonstrates training deep networks with SGD to depths previously inaccessible to standard feedforward architectures.  
- Clear motivation and concise mathematical formulation of the highway layer, with an explicit link to LSTMs.  
- Comprehensive experiments including ablation/optimization studies and comparisons on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100.  
- Visualizations (Figures 2 & 4) give insight into internal behavior and layer importance.  
- Related work section is thorough and cites relevant literature in deep learning and recurrent networks.  
- Strength: novel, effective architecture addressing vanishing gradients via gating mechanisms.  
- Strength: technically sound math and convincing empirical results showing effectiveness for very deep networks.  
- Strength: exceptionally clear writing and informative figures.  
- Strength: significant potential impact by enabling much deeper networks.  
- Strength: extensive experimental evaluation supporting the claims.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, especially transform gate bias.  
- Weakness: increased parameter count and potential computational cost; lacks explicit depth-vs-parameter trade-off analysis.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis of why highway networks work, e.g., formal gradient-flow explanation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective method with strong empirical results and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs deeper hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and more formal theoretical justification.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept (suitable for NIPS).
