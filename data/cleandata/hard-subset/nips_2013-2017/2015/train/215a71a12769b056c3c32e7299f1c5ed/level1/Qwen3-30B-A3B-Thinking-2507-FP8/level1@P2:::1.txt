

# Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces highway networks, a novel architecture designed to enable direct training of extremely deep feedforward neural networks. The authors address the well-known challenge of training very deep networks by borrowing the gating mechanism from Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent networks. The highway network architecture introduces transform gates (T) and carry gates (C) that allow information to flow across many layers without attenuation, creating "information highways" through the network. This approach enables training networks with hundreds of layers using standard stochastic gradient descent without requiring complex initialization schemes or multi-stage training procedures.

The paper relates its work to several previous approaches: normalized initialization strategies [16,17], skip connections [22,23,24], and distillation techniques [25,26]. It clearly differentiates highway networks from these approaches by demonstrating that the adaptive gating mechanism enables dynamic information routing based on input, rather than fixed shortcut connections.

**Quality**: The paper demonstrates strong technical soundness. The authors provide a clear theoretical foundation for their approach (equations 2-5), showing how the highway network architecture facilitates information flow. The empirical evaluation is comprehensive and well-designed, with experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 datasets. The comparison to plain networks and FitNets is particularly convincing, showing that highway networks outperform these baselines even when using comparable parameter counts. The analysis of gate activity (Figure 2) and layer importance (Figure 4) provides valuable insights into how highway networks operate. The paper is careful to acknowledge limitations, noting that for simpler tasks like MNIST, many layers remain unused.

**Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The notation is consistent and clearly defined, and the highway network architecture is explained with sufficient mathematical precision while remaining accessible. The figures effectively illustrate key concepts, particularly Figures 2 and 4 which demonstrate the information highway phenomenon and layer importance. The paper provides enough detail for an expert to reproduce the results, including implementation details (e.g., negative bias initialization for transform gates) and experimental protocols.

**Originality**: This work represents a significant architectural innovation that builds upon prior work in a novel way. While skip connections and distillation techniques have been used before, the highway network's adaptive gating mechanism is a distinct approach that allows the network to learn how to route information. The paper clearly differentiates from recent related work [31,32] and demonstrates that this is not merely a minor variation of previous approaches. The authors cite the relevant NIPS proceedings (e.g., [25] from NIPS 2014) and position their work appropriately within the field.

**Significance**: The problem of training very deep networks is one of the most important challenges in deep learning, and this paper makes a substantial contribution to solving it. The authors demonstrate that highway networks can be trained directly with SGD, even with hundreds of layers, which was previously difficult. The results show state-of-the-art performance on standard benchmarks while using fewer parameters than competing approaches. Most significantly, the paper enables new types of analysis (e.g., layer importance, information routing) that were not possible with plain networks. This work directly influenced subsequent architectures like ResNets (which appeared shortly after NIPS 2015) and has had a lasting impact on deep learning research.

**Pro and Con for Acceptance**:

*Pro:*
- Novel architectural approach that solves a fundamental problem in deep learning
- Strong theoretical and empirical validation across multiple datasets
- Clear, well-organized writing that makes complex ideas accessible
- Demonstrates significant improvements over previous approaches
- Enables new types of analysis on deep network structure

*Con:*
- The paper does not directly compare with other recent approaches like ResNets (which emerged after this paper's submission)
- The analysis of information routing, while insightful, could be expanded to include more detailed ablation studies
- The theoretical analysis of convergence properties is somewhat limited compared to the empirical results

Overall, this paper makes a strong contribution to the field. It addresses a fundamental problem with a novel solution that is both theoretically sound and empirically effective. The work has significant impact potential, as evidenced by its influence on subsequent deep learning architectures. The paper meets all NIPS quality criteria and is well-positioned to advance the state of the art in deep learning. The authors have clearly demonstrated the significance of their contribution through thorough experimentation and insightful analysis.

The paper is technically sound, well-written, and makes a significant contribution to deep learning. It should be accepted at NIPS 2015.
