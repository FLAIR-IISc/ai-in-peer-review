- Proposes highway networks using transform and carry gates (inspired by LSTM) to enable training of extremely deep feedforward networks.  
- Gates create "information highways" allowing information to flow across many layers without attenuation.  
- Enables training of networks with hundreds of layers using standard SGD without complex initialization or multi-stage training.  
- Differentiates from prior work (normalized initialization, skip connections, distillation) by using adaptive, input-dependent gating rather than fixed shortcuts.  
- Strong theoretical foundation (equations 2–5) explaining how architecture facilitates information flow.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 showing superiority to plain networks and FitNets with comparable parameters.  
- Analysis of gate activity and layer importance provides insights into information routing and layer usage.  
- Acknowledges limitations: many layers unused on simple tasks (e.g., MNIST).  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized writing with reproducible implementation and training details (e.g., negative bias initialization).  
- Novel architectural contribution that meaningfully advances prior approaches and is distinct from contemporary related work.  
- Significant impact: addresses a core problem in deep learning, achieves strong benchmarks with fewer parameters, and enabled later architectures (e.g., ResNets).  
- Pros: novel solution, solid theory and experiments, clear presentation, enables new analyses.  
- Cons: lacks direct comparison to later approaches like ResNets, could expand information-routing ablations, and theoretical convergence analysis is limited.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, significant, and should be accepted at NIPS 2015.
