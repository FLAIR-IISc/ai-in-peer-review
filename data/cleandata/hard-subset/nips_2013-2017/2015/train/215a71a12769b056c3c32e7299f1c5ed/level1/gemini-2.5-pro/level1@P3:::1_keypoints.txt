- Proposes "highway networks": feedforward layers with learnable transform and carry gates (inspired by LSTM) to allow unimpeded information flow and ease optimization in very deep nets.  
- Uses transform-gate bias initialization (negative) so networks initially behave like identity mappings, facilitating gradient flow from the start.  
- Empirically shows highway nets train up to 100 layers on MNIST and match/exceed FitNets on CIFAR-10 without complex multi-stage training.  
- Analyzes learned gate activities and lesioning to show data-dependent routing and automatic adjustment of effective depth.

- Strength: Novel, simple, and powerful idea—learnable gating for skip connections is an insightful solution to deep-network optimization.  
- Strength: Strong experimental validation and ablation analyses (optimization comparisons, lesion studies, CIFAR results) supporting claims.  
- Strength: Clear presentation, precise formulation, and informative figures linking ideas to results.

- Weakness: Missing baseline comparison to simple additive skip connection (y = H(x) + x) to isolate the benefit of gating versus just having an identity path.  
- Weakness: No discussion or experiments justifying the simplification C = 1 − T rather than using independent transform and carry gates.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to the transform-gate bias initialization (new hyperparameter) is not thoroughly analyzed—effects of b_T = 0 or positive unclear.  
- Weakness: Interpretation of gate dynamics could be expanded—gates becoming more negative might indicate sparse selective transformations or default-to-identity behavior.

- Final assessment: Excellent, high-impact paper—clear accept with potential to become foundational in deep architecture research.
