## Review of "Training Very Deep Networks"

This paper introduces “highway networks,” a novel neural network architecture designed to facilitate the training of very deep feedforward networks. The core idea is to incorporate “information highways” inspired by LSTM gating mechanisms, allowing for unimpeded information flow across many layers via adaptive gating units. The authors demonstrate that these networks can be trained directly with SGD to depths exceeding 100 layers, overcoming the optimization difficulties typically encountered with plain deep networks.

This work builds upon a substantial body of research addressing the challenges of training deep networks. Previous efforts have focused on improved optimizers (e.g., [13, 14, 15]), initialization strategies ([16, 17]), and architectural modifications like skip connections ([22, 23, 24]). The authors appropriately position their work within this context, highlighting how highway networks offer a distinct approach by enabling direct training of extremely deep networks without relying on techniques like two-stage training or specialized activation functions. The connection to LSTM networks [29, 30] is clearly articulated and provides a strong motivation for the proposed architecture.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The highway network architecture presents a fresh perspective on addressing the vanishing gradient problem in deep networks. The LSTM-inspired gating mechanism is a clever and effective approach.
*   **Empirical Results:** The experiments convincingly demonstrate the ability to train significantly deeper networks than previously possible with standard techniques. The results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and CIFAR-100 are compelling.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the highway network architecture and its underlying principles. The mathematical notation is concise and easy to follow.
*   **Analysis:** The analysis of transform gate behavior (Figure 2) and layer importance (Figure 4) provides valuable insights into how highway networks function and learn.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Theoretical Justification:** While the empirical results are strong, the paper lacks a rigorous theoretical analysis of why highway networks are more trainable. A deeper understanding of the gradient flow dynamics would be beneficial.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning but doesn’t provide extensive details on the search process. The sensitivity of performance to the initial bias of the transform gates could be further investigated.
*   **Comparison to Recent Work:** The paper references FitNets [25] but could benefit from a more detailed comparison to other recent advances in deep network training, such as residual networks [36] which also address the vanishing gradient problem.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for training very deep networks.
*   The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a significant advance in the field.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key concepts.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of theoretical justification limits the understanding of the underlying mechanisms.
*   The hyperparameter sensitivity and limited comparison to recent work could be addressed with further experimentation.

**Overall:**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of deep learning. The highway network architecture is a promising approach to overcoming the challenges of training very deep networks, and the empirical results are compelling. While some theoretical and experimental refinements would be beneficial, the paper is of high quality and deserves to be accepted.
