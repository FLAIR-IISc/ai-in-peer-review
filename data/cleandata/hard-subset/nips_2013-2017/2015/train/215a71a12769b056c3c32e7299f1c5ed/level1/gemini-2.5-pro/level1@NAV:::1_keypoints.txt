- Introduces highway networks: LSTM-style adaptive gating enabling training of extremely deep feedforward networks by blending transform and carry behavior.  
- Targets optimization problems (e.g., vanishing gradients) and offers an alternative to careful initialization, specialized optimizers, or multi-stage training like FitNets.  
- Primary contribution is original and significant—the application of LSTM-style gating to feedforward nets is elegant and timely.  
- Strong experimental validation: controlled MNIST experiments show highway nets overcome optimization barriers as depth increases.  
- CIFAR-10/100 results show strong generalization and competitive performance using a simpler single-stage training process.  
- Analysis is thorough: gate activation visualizations and lesion studies indicate data-dependent routing and adaptive effective depth.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured.  
- Weakness: gating introduces additional parameters and model complexity.  
- Weakness: lacks exploration of whether simple fixed identity skip connections (C=1) could provide similar benefits.  
- Weakness: transform gate bias initialization (e.g., -1 to -3) appears sensitive and may require careful tuning across settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and effective solution, strong empirical support, and insightful analysis.  
- Argument against acceptance: necessity of learned gating versus simpler skip connections is not fully justified.  
- Overall: high-quality paper making an important contribution; strengths outweigh the minor weaknesses.
