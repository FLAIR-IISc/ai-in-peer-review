- Proposes Principal Differences Analysis (PDA) that finds projections maximizing Wasserstein divergence between high-dimensional distributions.
- Introduces SPARDA, a sparse variant to identify features responsible for distributional differences.
- Methodology is clearly written and includes a convex approximation of the top-k multiclass SVM.
- Uses Wasserstein distance as a natural divergence measure between populations.
- Provides theoretical analysis with concentration results and sparsistency guarantees.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on experiments distinguishing cell populations in somatosensory cortex and hippocampus.
- Compares to related methods (sparse LDA, logistic lasso) and discusses related work thoroughly.
- Strength: handles high-dimensional data and identifies contributing features.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to some prior works due to implementation availability.
- Weakness: code is not currently available; authors plan to release upon acceptance.
- Recommendation: additional experiments and method comparisons would strengthen the paper.
- Overall: well-written, convincing experiments and solid theory; could make a significant contribution with more comparisons and public code.
