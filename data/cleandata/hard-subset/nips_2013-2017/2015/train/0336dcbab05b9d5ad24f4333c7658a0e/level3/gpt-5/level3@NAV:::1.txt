This paper tackles top-k multiclass classification by proposing a top-k hinge loss that directly optimizes top-k accuracy. The key idea is to replace the nonconvex k-th order statistic with the convex average of the k largest score differences, yielding a tight convex upper bound to the top-k error that strictly improves on the standard multiclass SVM hinge except in degenerate cases. The formulation recovers Crammer–Singer when k=1 and leads naturally to a new “top-k simplex,” for which the authors derive the conjugate and an efficient projection procedure. Building on this, they present a Prox-SDCA optimizer with batch-wise dual updates and a principled stopping criterion. The paper carefully situates the method vis-à-vis ranking-based losses (Usunier et al.) and structured SVM for multivariate measures (Joachims), clarifying when their surrogate is tighter and how it differs from reductions to binary ranking.

Quality: The technical development is sound, with clear convex analysis, duality, and an efficient projection algorithm that addresses the coupling induced by the top-k cap. The optimization is well-motivated and practical. Experiments across five vision datasets indicate consistent improvements in top-k metrics over strong baselines, sometimes improving top-1 as well. Missing, however, are direct comparisons to [25, 8] (the authors cite unavailability); these are important and should be added if at all possible (reimplementations or faithful surrogates), along with more detailed runtime comparisons and sensitivity to k and λ.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and well organized; proofs are indicated and technical choices are justified. The relationship to prior work is thoughtfully discussed.

Originality: The top-k hinge with its dual/top-k simplex and associated projection procedure is a neat and nontrivial extension of multiclass SVMs. The connection and contrast to existing ranking losses are well articulated.

Significance: Optimizing top-k is practically important in large-label problems. The proposed surrogate and efficient solver are likely to be adopted and extended.

Pros:
- Clear, principled surrogate that generalizes Crammer–Singer
- Elegant convex-analytic treatment and efficient projection
- Strong, scalable optimizer with stopping criterion
- Consistent empirical gains on multiple datasets
- Good discussion of related work

Cons:
- Missing comparisons to [25, 8]
- Limited analysis of computational trade-offs and sensitivity
- Theory could discuss consistency to top-k error

Requests: Please add comparisons to [25, 8] if feasible, report detailed wall-clock/runtime results, and release code upon acceptance to ensure reproducibility. Overall: a neat and effective formulation of top-k multiclass SVM.
