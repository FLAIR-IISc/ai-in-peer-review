- Targets multiclass problems with many similar classes and proposes a convex top-k hinge loss that upper-bounds the nonconvex kth-score loss (reduces to standard hinge at k=1).
- Derives the conjugate loss leading to a novel “top-k simplex” in the dual and optimizes via Prox-SDCA with per-example block updates.
- Develops an efficient projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex by analyzing two regimes (knapsack vs. cone) with near-sorting complexity guarantees.
- Generalizes Crammer–Singer (k=1), relates to ordered-weighted hinge losses (tighter bound), and contrasts with structured SVM and ranking reductions.
- Motivation and contribution are clear: a principled convex surrogate that tightens the gap to top-k zero-one loss.
- Primal–dual derivation is clean and corrects previous SDCA derivation issues; analysis of projection regimes is technically solid.
- Optimization uses closed-form dual improvements, rank-1 primal updates, and a duality-gap stopping rule; projection handles bias term.
- Demonstrates scalability on large image datasets (Places205, ImageNet) with millions of examples and hundreds–thousands of classes.
- Empirically improves top-k accuracy across five datasets versus Crammer–Singer and ranking baselines; sometimes improves top-1 as well.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, provides pseudocode and released code, aiding reproducibility.
- Lacks study of statistical properties such as top-k calibration or Bayes consistency; theoretical analysis would strengthen the work.
- Missing comparison to linear softmax (multinomial logistic) baselines on the same features; inclusion would contextualize gains versus deep softmax.
- Needs more detailed wall-clock runtime and memory comparisons with strong linear baselines (e.g., LibLinear, multinomial logistic) for m≈1000.
- Should study training vs. evaluation k mismatch systematically and provide guidance for choosing training k′.
- Does not address class imbalance or cost-sensitive extensions; discussion of such extensions would broaden applicability.
- Cone-projection worst-case search is mitigated empirically but needs more analysis of runtime distribution (frequency and iterations).
- Overall technically sound and complete; derivations careful and experiments supportive, but some theoretical and runtime aspects are underexplored.
- Clarity is high; connections to prior work are well-explained and implementation details are reproducible.
- Originality: novel top-k hinge, top-k simplex conjugate, and efficient projection; meaningful integration with Prox-SDCA.
- Significance: high practical relevance for large-label problems; likely to be adopted by practitioners using linear models.
- Questions for authors: provide linear softmax results; theory on top-k calibration; sensitivity to bias ρ; statistics on cone vs. knapsack projections; guidance on choosing k′.
- Pros: principled tighter surrogate, efficient scalable optimization, clear exposition, released code.
- Cons: missing calibration/consistency theory, absent linear softmax comparisons, limited runtime breakdowns, little guidance on k′ and imbalanced/cost-sensitive settings.
- Recommendation: Strong accept — technically solid, well-motivated, and practically impactful contribution.
