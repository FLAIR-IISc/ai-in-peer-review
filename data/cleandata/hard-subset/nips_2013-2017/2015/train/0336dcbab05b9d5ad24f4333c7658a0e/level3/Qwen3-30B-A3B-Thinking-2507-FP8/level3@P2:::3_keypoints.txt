- Introduces optimizing for top-k performance with a loss that is zero when the true class is among top-k predictions.
- Proposes a convex upper bound called the top-k hinge loss and a novel "top-k simplex" projection.
- Extends the Crammer & Singer multiclass SVM and relates to Usunier et al.'s ranking-based losses.
- Presents a rigorous derivation of the convex conjugate and shows the method recovers standard multiclass SVM when k=1.
- Develops an efficient Prox-SDCA optimization scheme and a detailed, non-trivial projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex.
- Experimental evaluation on five image datasets (including ImageNet) shows consistent improvements in top-k accuracy versus baselines.
- Reports top-5 accuracy gains of 1.2–2.6% over standard multiclass SVM; improvements consistent across different k and datasets.
- Implementation released as a C++ library with Matlab interface, enhancing reproducibility.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient mathematical detail.
- Main weakness: limited theoretical motivation for choosing the specific convex upper bound φk(a) over alternatives.
- Suggestion: provide stronger theoretical justification and discussion of how the chosen bound relates to top-k error properties and optimization.
- Minor typo noted on page 3 ("globally optimal leads to" — remove "optimal").
- Originality: top-k convex formulation and top-k simplex/projection are novel and meaningful technical contributions.
- Significance: addresses a practically important metric, scales to large problems, and advances the state of the art.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper, recommend acceptance.
