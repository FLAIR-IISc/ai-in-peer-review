- Proposes a novel top-k hinge loss that generalizes multiclass SVM to directly optimize top-k performance.  
- Uses the sum of the k largest score components as a convex approximation to the top-k error.  
- Derives the convex conjugate of the top-k hinge loss and introduces a new "top-k simplex".  
- Develops an efficient optimization method via Prox-SDCA enabled by the top-k simplex and projection algorithm.  
- Establishes connections to ranking-based losses (Usunier et al.) and claims a tighter convex upper bound than prior methods.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical derivations grounded in Fenchel duality and detailed analysis of projections.  
- Evaluates on five image classification datasets, including Places205 and ImageNet, showing consistent top-k accuracy improvements.  
- Reports notable gains on large-class datasets (e.g., +2.6% top-5 accuracy on Caltech 101).  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and math derivations are accessible.  
- Technical contributions (top-k simplex and projection algorithm) are original and practically relevant.  
- Significant for real-world large-scale classification where class ambiguity and top-k metrics matter.  
- Main weaknesses: missing empirical comparisons with Wsabie and Wsabie++ and code not yet public.  
- Limited discussion of the computational complexity of the projection algorithm.  
- Recommendation: accept conditionally, asking authors to include Wsabie comparisons in the camera-ready version if possible.
