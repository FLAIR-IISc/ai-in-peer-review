This paper introduces the top-k multiclass SVM, a direct method to optimize for top-k performance in image classification problems with a large number of classes. The authors propose a convex upper bound on the top-k error, called the top-k hinge loss, and develop an efficient optimization scheme based on an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex. The top-k hinge loss is a novel combination of familiar techniques, and its relation to ranking-based losses is clearly explained. The paper also presents an extensive evaluation of the proposed method on five image classification datasets, showing consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines.

The paper is well-written, and the material is non-trivial. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the top-k hinge loss and its optimization scheme. The experimental results are good, and the comparison with other methods is thorough. However, it would be beneficial to further motivate the choice of the loss function and provide more insights into its properties.
