## Review of "Top-k Multiclass SVM"

This paper proposes a novel formulation for top-k multiclass classification based on Support Vector Machines (SVMs). The authors generalize the standard multiclass SVM by introducing a top-k hinge loss, cleverly approximating the top-k error with a convex function – the sum of the k largest score components. A key contribution is the efficient optimization algorithm developed for this new loss, leveraging a projection onto a novel “top-k simplex”. The paper is well-written and clearly presents the motivation, formulation, and experimental evaluation. It effectively builds upon prior work in multiclass SVMs [5, 24] and ranking-based losses [27], outlining clear relationships and distinctions.

A significant strength of this work lies in its theoretical grounding. The derivation of the primal-conjugate pair for the top-k hinge loss (Proposition 2) and the detailed analysis of the top-k simplex are valuable contributions. Furthermore, the authors demonstrate awareness of existing optimization techniques, adapting Prox-SDCA [24] for efficient training. The experimental results on five datasets consistently show improvements in top-k accuracy compared to baselines, showcasing the practical effectiveness of the proposed method. 

However, a crucial weakness is the lack of comparison with methods [8, 25] due to implementation unavailability. These comparisons are important, as acknowledged by the authors, and would strengthen the claim of improved performance. While the authors provide a detailed analysis of existing approaches and justify their choices, the absence of these comparisons leaves a gap in the evaluation. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed projection algorithm, especially in comparison to existing simplex projection methods.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound formulation of top-k multiclass SVM.
*   Efficient optimization algorithm with well-defined convergence properties.
*   Consistent experimental improvements across multiple datasets.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Missing comparisons with [8, 25].
*   Limited discussion of the computational complexity of the projection algorithm.

**Overall:**

This is a neat and well-executed formulation of top-k multiclass SVM that demonstrably works well. The theoretical contributions are solid, and the experimental results are promising. I recommend acceptance contingent upon the authors making the code publicly available and addressing the comparison to [8, 25] if implementations become available. I strongly encourage the authors to prioritize making the code public upon acceptance, as requested.
