- Proposes a novel top-k SVM that directly optimizes top-k classification error using a convex "top-k hinge loss," generalizing Crammer and Singer's multiclass SVM (k=1).  
- Defines the top-k hinge as the average of the top k margin-violating scores, offering a tighter convex bound than prior ranking-loss-based formulations.  
- Derives a dual that leads to a new convex feasible set, the "top-k simplex," paralleling the standard simplex in multiclass SVMs.  
- Integrates the formulation into Proximal SDCA for scalability, with the key optimization step reduced to a biased Euclidean projection onto the top-k simplex.  
- Develops a novel, efficient algorithm for projection onto the top-k simplex to make training practical for large datasets.  
- Validates the method on five image classification datasets (including ImageNet and Places205), showing consistent and significant top-k accuracy improvements over strong baselines.  
- Motivation and problem significance are strong: aligns training objective with widely used top-k evaluation in large-scale, ambiguous-class settings.  
- Technical contributions are original and rigorous: elegant loss, theoretical connection to prior work, and an algorithmic projection contribution of independent interest.  
- Demonstrates scalability and practicality via Prox-SDCA and experiments on millions of examples; code release planned for reproducibility.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and situates itself well relative to prior art.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing; in some cases optimizing top-k also improves top-1 accuracy.  
- Minor theoretical weakness: lacks deeper formal analysis of the gap between φk and alternative bounds and when the tighter bound yields better generalization.  
- Experimental question left open: effect of mismatching training k and evaluation k (e.g., training with k=10 for top-5 evaluation) and the regularization role of k.  
- Overall assessment: a complete, high-quality, and impactful contribution to large-scale multiclass classification.
