This paper proposes a novel extension to the traditional multiclass Support Vector Machine (SVM) algorithm, which the authors term top-k multiclass SVM. The motivation behind this work is the common occurrence of semantically similar classes in many multiclass problems, where the cost of misclassification is only incurred if the ground truth is outside the top k predictions.

The authors begin by reviewing the traditional multiclass SVM of Crammer and Singer, which they then extend to the top-k setting. They propose a convex upper bound on the top-k error, which they term the top-k hinge loss. This loss function is designed to relax the penalty for making an error in the top-k predictions, and is shown to be a tighter upper bound on the actual top-k zero-one loss than existing methods.

The authors then derive an efficient optimization procedure for the top-k hinge loss, which they term Prox-SDCA. This procedure is based on the proximal stochastic dual coordinate ascent framework, and is shown to have strong convergence guarantees.

One of the main technical contributions of this work is the derivation of an efficient algorithm for projecting onto the top-k simplex, which is a crucial step in the optimization procedure. The authors show that this algorithm is scalable and comparable to existing algorithms for the standard simplex projection.

The authors evaluate their method on five large-scale image classification datasets, and demonstrate consistent improvements in top-k accuracy over existing methods. They also show that their method is scalable to very large datasets, with millions of training examples.

Overall, this paper presents a well-motivated and clearly written contribution to the field of multiclass classification. The authors provide a thorough review of the relevant literature, and their proposed method is shown to be effective and efficient through extensive experimentation.
