This paper proposes a novel extension of the multiclass Support Vector Machine (SVM) to optimize for top-k performance, which is particularly relevant for image classification problems with a large number of classes. The authors introduce the top-k multiclass SVM, which is based on a tight convex upper bound of the top-k error. They also propose a fast optimization scheme using an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex.

The paper is well-written, and the material is non-trivial. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach, including the derivation of the top-k hinge loss and the efficient projection onto the top-k simplex. The experimental results are also good, showing consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to tackle a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The top-k error is a more relevant metric for image classification problems with a large number of classes, and the authors' approach provides a direct method to optimize for this metric. The paper also provides a nice connection to ranking-based losses, showing that the top-k hinge loss is a special case of the family of convex losses for ranking and multiclass classification.

However, there are a few minor issues that could be improved. One suggestion is to further motivate the choice of the loss function. While the authors provide a clear explanation of the top-k hinge loss, it would be helpful to discuss the intuition behind this choice and why it is a good upper bound on the top-k error. Additionally, there is a typo on page 3: "globally optimal leads to" should be "globally optimal".

Overall, this is a nice paper that describes an interesting problem and provides a novel solution. The core idea of learning with respect to the top-k loss is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The experimental results are also good, showing consistent improvements in top-k accuracy.
