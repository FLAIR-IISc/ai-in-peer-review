- Proposes Top-k Multiclass SVM to directly optimize top-k classification error for many-class problems.  
- Introduces a novel convex "top-k hinge loss" as a tight upper bound on the non-convex top-k zero-one loss.  
- Recovers the Crammer & Singer multiclass SVM as the k=1 special case.  
- Defines the loss as the average of the k largest scores among incorrect classes plus a margin term.  
- Demonstrates the proposed loss is a tighter surrogate than formulations derived from Usunier et al.'s ranking losses.  
- Derives the Fenchel dual of the top-k hinge loss, revealing dual variables constrained to a new "top-k simplex."  
- Develops an efficient Euclidean projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex (technical contribution of independent interest).  
- Implements scalable optimization via Proximal Stochastic Dual Coordinate Ascent (Prox-SDCA).  
- Evaluates on five image classification datasets (including Places205 and ImageNet) showing consistent top-k accuracy improvements.  
- Strength: addresses a practical and important problem for large-scale classification tasks.  
- Strength: original loss formulation and identification of the top-k simplex with a novel projection algorithm.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical derivations and sound technical treatment.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments and strong empirical performance against baselines.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with useful figures and released implementation.  
- Weakness: motivation for using the average of top-k scores could be elaborated; deeper discussion of loss properties and error weighting would help.  
- Weakness: minor typo noted; a final proofread is recommended.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel well-motivated convex loss, independent projection algorithm, scalable and empirically superior method, high-quality presentation.  
- Argument against acceptance: only minor concerns about motivation depth; not a serious flaw.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with convincing theory and experiments; recommend acceptance.
