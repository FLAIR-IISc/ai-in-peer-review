The paper "Top-k Multiclass SVM" proposes a novel approach to multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes. The main idea is to relax the penalty for making an error in the top-k predictions by introducing a top-k hinge loss. This loss function is a convex upper bound of the top-k error, which is the standard zero-one loss.

The authors begin by reviewing the multiclass SVM of Crammer and Singer, which is then extended to the top-k multiclass SVM. The top-k hinge loss is defined as the sum of the k largest score components, which is a convex approximation to the target top-k loss. The authors also derive the conjugate of the proposed loss function and show that it is a primal-conjugate pair for the top-k hinge loss.

The paper then discusses the relation of the top-k hinge loss to ranking-based losses. The authors show that the top-k hinge loss is a special case of a more general family of convex losses for ranking and multiclass classification. They also compare their approach to previous work, including the multiclass SVM of Crammer and Singer and the ranking-based methods of Usunier et al.

The authors propose an efficient optimization algorithm for the top-k multiclass SVM, which is based on the proximal stochastic dual coordinate ascent (Prox-SDCA) framework. They show that the algorithm has strong convergence guarantees and is easy to adapt to their problem. The authors also discuss the advantages of their method, including the fact that it can be kernelized and has a well-defined stopping criterion.

The experimental results show that the proposed method is effective compared to baselines. The authors evaluate their method on five image classification datasets of different scale and complexity, including Caltech 101 Silhouettes, MIT Indoor 67, SUN 397, Places 205, and ImageNet 2012. They show that optimizing the top-k hinge loss yields consistently better top-k performance, and that their method is scalable to large datasets with millions of training examples.
