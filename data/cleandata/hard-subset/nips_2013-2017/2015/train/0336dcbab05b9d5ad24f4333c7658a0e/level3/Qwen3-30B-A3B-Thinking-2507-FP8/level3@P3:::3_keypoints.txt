- Paper proposes a top-k extension of multiclass SVM with a convex top-k hinge loss and an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex, optimized via Prox-SDCA.  
- Two main contributions: a theoretically sound convex top-k hinge loss and an efficient algorithm for projection onto the top-k simplex.  
- The loss is proven to be a tighter upper bound on top-k error than alternative ranking-based formulations.  
- Empirical evaluation on five image datasets shows consistent top-k accuracy improvements across ks, especially on large datasets (SUN397, Places205).  
- Quality: technically solid with complete proofs (conjugate duality, top-k simplex properties) and thorough experiments against appropriate baselines.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, with clear mathematical exposition and helpful illustrations aiding reproducibility.  
- Originality: while optimizing top-k is not entirely new, the specific convex formulation and projection algorithm are significant technical contributions differentiating it from prior ranking losses.  
- Significance: addresses a practical problem (class ambiguity in large-scale image classification) and demonstrates useful, consistent performance gains.  
- Weakness 1: paper could better motivate why the chosen top-k hinge (φk) yields practical gains over the alternative (φ̃k), since experiments show similar performance.  
- Weakness 2: minor typo on page 3 ("globally optimal leads to" → remove "optimal").  
- Weakness 3: the connection between the top-k loss and real-world class ambiguity could be articulated more clearly with concrete examples.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — satisfies NIPS criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance) and the projection algorithm is of independent interest.
