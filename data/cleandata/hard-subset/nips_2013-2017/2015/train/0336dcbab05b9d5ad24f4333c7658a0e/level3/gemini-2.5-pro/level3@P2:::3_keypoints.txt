- Proposes the top-k multiclass SVM to directly optimize top-k classification error by penalizing when the true class is not in the top k predictions.  
- Introduces a convex upper bound called the "top-k hinge loss", generalizing the Crammer–Singer multiclass SVM (k=1).  
- Shows the convex conjugate of the loss is defined on a novel "top-k simplex", extending the standard simplex geometry of multiclass SVMs.  
- Adopts Proximal Stochastic Dual Coordinate Ascent (Prox-SDCA) to optimize the objective.  
- Develops a novel, efficient algorithm to project onto the top-k simplex, a non-trivial technical contribution.  
- Empirical evaluation on five datasets (including Places205 and ImageNet) shows consistent top-k accuracy improvements over baselines.  
- Strength: Addresses a practical, widely relevant problem for large-scale, ambiguous-class classification.  
- Strength: Presents novel, theoretically grounded formulation with solid derivations and proofs that the loss is a convex upper bound.  
- Strength: Provides a non-trivial algorithmic contribution (top-k simplex projection) and scalable optimization.  
- Strength: Well-written, clearly organized, and connects appropriately to related work (e.g., Usunier et al.).  
- Weakness: Needs stronger intuition and motivation for the specific top-k hinge form (averaging the top-k incorrect scores) and discussion of alternatives.  
- Minor issue: Typo on page 3, section 2.2 ("finding a globally optimal leads to" should be corrected).  
- Arguments for acceptance: practical importance, elegant convex formulation, significant theory and algorithmic contributions, strong empirical results, and clarity.  
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient elaboration on the choice of the specific loss form (minor concern).  
- Overall recommendation: High-quality paper; recommends acceptance.
