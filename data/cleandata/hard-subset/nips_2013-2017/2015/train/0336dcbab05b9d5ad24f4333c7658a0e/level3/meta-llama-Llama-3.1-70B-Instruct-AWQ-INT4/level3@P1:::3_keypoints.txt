- Proposes a novel top-k multiclass SVM that directly optimizes top-k performance for problems with many ambiguous classes.
- Introduces the top-k hinge loss, a convex upper bound on top-k error that generalizes Crammer and Singer’s multiclass SVM (recovers it when k=1).
- Presents an efficient optimization scheme using projection onto the top-k simplex, the convex domain of the conjugate loss.
- Shows the top-k hinge loss is related to ranking-based convex losses (special case of Usunier et al.’s family).
- Experimental evaluation on five image recognition datasets demonstrates consistent top-k accuracy improvements and good scalability.
- Paper is well-written, mathematically sound, and the derivations and notation are clear.
- The contribution is original and addresses an important, widely applicable problem in multiclass classification.
- Significance is supported by convincing empirical results and clear discussion of implications.
- Main weakness: the choice of the top-k hinge loss could be better motivated theoretically or empirically.
- Minor issue: typo on page 3 (“globally optimal leads to” should be “leads to”).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, new loss, solid experiments, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient motivation for the specific loss choice and limited discussion of broader implications and future work.
