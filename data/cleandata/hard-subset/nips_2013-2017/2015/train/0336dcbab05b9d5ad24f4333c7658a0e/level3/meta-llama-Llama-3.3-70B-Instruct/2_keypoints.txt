- Proposes Principal Differences Analysis (PDA) to find projections maximizing Wasserstein divergence between high-dimensional distributions.
- Introduces SPARDA, a sparse variant to identify features driving distributional differences.
- Provides an efficient optimization procedure for the top-k loss, enabling scalability to large datasets.
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise methodological explanations and applications.
- Experimental results show PDA/SPARDA outperform sparse PCA, logistic regression, and LDA in identifying differences.
- Strength: novel approach addressing differences between high-dimensional distributions, relevant across fields.
- Strength: sparse variant (SPARDA) facilitates feature identification.
- Strength: scalable optimization for the top-k objective.
- Weakness: assumes compactly supported distributions, which may not hold in many real-world settings.
- Weakness: lacks a thorough analysis of computational complexity for large-scale datasets.
- Weakness: does not clearly explain how to choose the regularization parameter (important hyperparameter).
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add computational complexity analysis, guidance on regularization selection, and consider relaxing compact-support assumptions; rating 8/10.
