## Review of "Top-k Multiclass SVM"

This paper introduces a novel approach to multiclass SVM learning, directly optimizing for top-k accuracy instead of the standard zero-one loss. The core idea – learning with respect to a top-k loss, defined as incurring zero loss if the true class is within the top-k predictions – is a sensible relaxation for tasks with inherent class ambiguity, particularly in image classification with many classes. The authors present a convex upper bound on the top-k loss, termed the top-k hinge loss, and develop an efficient optimization scheme based on projection onto a novel “top-k simplex”. 

The work is well-written and technically sound. The derivation of the primal-conjugate pair for the top-k hinge loss is clearly presented, and the connection to existing ranking-based losses (specifically, Usunier et al., 2016) is appropriately highlighted. The experimental results on five datasets are compelling, consistently demonstrating improvements in top-k accuracy compared to baseline methods, including Crammer and Singer's multiclass SVM and state-of-the-art ranking-based methods. The scalability to large datasets like Places 205 and ImageNet is a significant strength.

However, while the motivation for considering top-k accuracy is intuitive, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion *why* this particular loss function, with its specific convex relaxation, was chosen.  A more thorough comparison with alternative convex relaxations, or a more rigorous justification of the properties of the proposed upper bound, would strengthen the contribution. 

I noted a minor typo on page 3: “globally optimal leads to” – the word "optimal" should be removed. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The direct optimization of top-k accuracy with a convex formulation is a significant contribution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The derivations and optimization scheme appear mathematically rigorous.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The consistent performance improvements across multiple datasets provide strong empirical evidence.
*   **Scalability:** The method scales well to large-scale datasets.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Loss Function Motivation:** While reasonable, the justification for the specific choice of the top-k hinge loss could be strengthened.

**Overall:** This is a nice paper describing an interesting problem and presenting a well-executed solution. Despite the minor suggestion for improvement, I recommend acceptance. The work addresses a practical issue in multiclass classification and provides a valuable new tool for the machine learning community.
