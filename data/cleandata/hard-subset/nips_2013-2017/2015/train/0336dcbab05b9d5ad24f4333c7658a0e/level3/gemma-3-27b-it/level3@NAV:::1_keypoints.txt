- Proposes a novel top-k multiclass SVM using a convex top-k hinge loss approximated by the sum of the k largest score components.  
- Introduces an efficient optimization algorithm that leverages projection onto a new “top-k simplex.”  
- Strong theoretical contributions including the primal–conjugate derivation for the top-k hinge loss (Proposition 2).  
- Detailed analysis of the top-k simplex structure.  
- Adapts Prox-SDCA for efficient training with well-defined convergence properties.  
- Experimental results show consistent top-k accuracy improvements on five datasets.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly situates itself relative to prior multiclass SVM and ranking-loss work.  
- Main weakness: missing empirical comparisons with methods [8, 25] due to unavailable implementations.  
- Additional weakness: limited discussion of the computational complexity of the projection algorithm versus existing simplex projection methods.  
- Recommendation: accept contingent on making code public and addressing comparisons to [8, 25] if implementations become available.
