## Review of "Top-k Multiclass SVM"

This paper introduces a novel approach to multiclass SVM classification by directly optimizing for top-k accuracy. The core idea – learning with respect to a top-k loss, defined as incurring zero loss if the true class is within the top-k predictions – is well-motivated given the challenges of class ambiguity in large-scale classification tasks. The authors propose a convex upper bound on the top-k loss and develop an efficient optimization scheme based on projection onto a novel “top-k simplex.” 

The work builds upon the established foundation of multiclass SVMs (Crammer & Singer, 2005) and relates to recent work in ranking-based losses (Usunier et al., 2014).  The contribution of defining and efficiently projecting onto the top-k simplex is particularly noteworthy, as it enables scalable optimization. The paper clearly articulates the connection to existing ranking methods and provides a compelling argument for the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The paper is technically rigorous, with clear derivations and a well-defined optimization framework. The theoretical justification for the proposed loss function and its convex upper bound is convincing.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experimental results on five datasets demonstrate consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines, including standard multiclass SVM and ranking-based methods. The scalability to large datasets like Places 205 and ImageNet is a significant positive.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the technical details. The notation is consistent, and the explanations are clear.
* **Originality:** The direct optimization of top-k accuracy with a convex formulation is a novel contribution. The introduction of the top-k simplex and its efficient projection algorithm further enhances the originality of the work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Loss Function Motivation:** While the rationale for using a top-k loss is intuitive, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of *why* this specific convex upper bound was chosen. A more thorough exploration of alternative upper bounds and their properties would strengthen the argument. 



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper describing an interesting and well-executed research project. The technical contributions are significant, and the experimental results are compelling. The minor weakness regarding the loss function motivation does not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Novel and technically sound approach to multiclass classification.
* Strong experimental results demonstrating improved performance.
* Clear and well-written presentation.
* Potential for impact in areas with high class ambiguity.
