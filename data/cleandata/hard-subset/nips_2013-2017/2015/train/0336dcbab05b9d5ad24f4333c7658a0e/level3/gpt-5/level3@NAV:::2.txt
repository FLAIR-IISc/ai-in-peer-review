Summary: The paper addresses multiclass settings where many classes are semantically similar and evaluation often uses top-k error (no cost if the true label is within the top k predictions). The authors propose a top-k multiclass SVM that directly optimizes a convex surrogate tailored to this metric. Specifically, they introduce the top-k hinge loss, a tight convex upper bound on the nonconvex top-k zero-one loss, derive its convex conjugate, and show it recovers the Crammer–Singer SVM when k=1. A key technical contribution is the “top-k simplex” and an efficient projection algorithm onto this set (and the associated cone), enabling a Prox-SDCA optimization scheme with principled stopping via duality gaps. Empirically, on five image classification datasets (including large-scale Places205 and ImageNet), the method improves top-k accuracy over strong baselines such as Crammer–Singer, SVMPerf for multivariate measures, TopPush, and Wsabie/LaRank-style approaches, demonstrating scalability.

Relation to prior work: The formulation is connected to ordered weighted ranking losses of Usunier et al.; the paper shows their variant (φ̃k) and argues the proposed φk is a tighter bound. Optimization builds on Prox-SDCA (Shalev-Shwartz & Zhang) but requires new projections; comparisons include Joachims’ SVMPerf and TopPush (Li et al.). The contribution is clearly differentiated.

Quality: Technical development is careful and sound: convexity, conjugates, dual derivation with yi-compatibility, and efficient block updates are well justified. The projection algorithm is a genuine contribution beyond a straightforward simplex projection. Experiments cover multiple scales and show consistent gains; however, statistical significance, sensitivity analyses (k, λ), and more detailed wall-clock comparisons vs. baselines would strengthen claims. A discussion of surrogate consistency with respect to top-k risk would be valuable.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written and easy to follow. The top-k simplex/cone and the role of the bias term are well motivated. Consider moving some key projection details from the supplement and summarizing worst-case vs. typical runtime in the main text.

Originality: Modeling top-k via a bespoke convex hinge and introducing the top-k simplex with efficient projection is novel relative to multiclass SVMs and prior ranking surrogates.

Significance: Top-k metrics are ubiquitous in large-scale vision; a scalable, principled method is likely to be useful to researchers and practitioners. The code release enhances impact.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, well-motivated problem; principled convex surrogate tailored to top-k
- Novel top-k simplex and efficient projection; solid optimization via Prox-SDCA
- Strong empirical results at scale; practical relevance; code provided
- Good positioning relative to Crammer–Singer and ranking-based losses

Cons against acceptance:
- No theoretical calibration/consistency results for top-k risk
- Limited statistical/significance analyses and ablations (k choice, α vs. β variants)
- Some scalability comparisons rely on one-vs-all fallbacks for competitors, potentially disadvantaging them
- More detailed runtime and memory profiling on large datasets would help quantify scalability further
