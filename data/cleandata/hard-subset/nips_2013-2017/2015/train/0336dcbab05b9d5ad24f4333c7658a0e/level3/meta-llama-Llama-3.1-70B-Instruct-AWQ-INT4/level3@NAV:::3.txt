This paper proposes a novel approach to multiclass classification problems by introducing the concept of top-k multiclass SVM. The authors argue that in problems with a large number of classes, it is more practical to allow k guesses and evaluate classifiers based on the top-k error instead of the standard zero-one loss. The paper is well-written, and the material is non-trivial.

The authors provide a thorough derivation of the top-k hinge loss, which is a convex upper bound of the top-k error. They also propose an efficient optimization scheme based on an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex. The experimental results on five datasets show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity and organization. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, followed by a detailed derivation of the top-k hinge loss and its optimization scheme. The experimental results are also well-presented and easy to follow.

However, one potential weakness of the paper is the lack of motivation for the choice of the loss function. While the authors provide a clear derivation of the top-k hinge loss, they do not provide a detailed explanation of why this loss function is more suitable for multiclass classification problems than other loss functions.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel approach to multiclass classification problems, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the top-k hinge loss and its optimization scheme, which is a valuable contribution to the understanding of this problem.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses a common problem in multiclass classification, which is the difficulty of evaluating classifiers based on the standard zero-one loss. The authors' approach provides a more practical and effective way of evaluating classifiers, which is a significant contribution to the field.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper for publication. However, I would suggest that the authors provide a more detailed explanation of the motivation for the choice of the loss function, and also provide more comparisons with other loss functions to demonstrate the superiority of their approach.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to multiclass classification problems.
* The authors provide a thorough derivation of the top-k hinge loss and its optimization scheme.
* The experimental results show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines.
* The paper is well-written and clearly organized.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper lacks a detailed explanation of the motivation for the choice of the loss function.
* The authors do not provide a thorough comparison with other loss functions to demonstrate the superiority of their approach.
* The paper assumes a certain level of background knowledge in convex optimization and multiclass classification, which may not be familiar to all readers.
