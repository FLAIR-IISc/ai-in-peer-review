- Proposal: Introduces a Top-k Multiclass SVM to directly optimize top-k classification performance for high-ambiguity, large-scale tasks.  
- Core idea: Defines a novel top-k hinge loss that is a tight, convex upper bound on the non-convex top-k zero-one loss.  
- Relation to prior work: Elegant generalization of Crammer & Singer multiclass SVM and positioned as a tighter surrogate within Usunier et al.’s ranking-loss family.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Develops an efficient projection algorithm onto the novel “top-k simplex,” enabling scalable optimization via Prox-SDCA.  
- Quality: Theoretical derivations (primal, convex conjugate, dual) are sound and non-trivial, with strong empirical support.  
- Experiments: Demonstrates consistent top-k accuracy improvements across five image classification datasets compared to multiple baselines.  
- Originality: Both the top-k hinge loss formulation and the projection algorithm are novel and of independent interest.  
- Significance: Addresses a relevant practical problem and provides a scalable tool likely to be adopted and extended by practitioners.  
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- Minor weaknesses: Motivation for summing the top-k scores could be expanded; minor typo on page 3 (“optimal” redundant).  
- Recommendation: Strong accept.
