- Proposes a novel top-k multiclass SVM that incurs cost only when the true label is outside the top-k predictions.  
- Targets common large-class image classification scenarios where top-k errors matter.  
- Introduces a top-k hinge loss as a natural, convex upper bound extension of the standard loss.  
- Derives an efficient optimization procedure using Fenchel duality and a primal-dual formulation.  
- Adopts the Prox-SDCA framework, with clear justification of its advantages.  
- Experiments on five image classification datasets show scalable performance and consistent top-k accuracy improvements over baselines (e.g., Crammer-Singer SVM).  
- Strength: well-motivated problem and a novel, principled loss formulation.  
- Strength: efficient, well-explained optimization derivation and use of Prox-SDCA.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrate scalability and effectiveness.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of the algorithm’s time complexity.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of hyperparameter choices and their impact.  
- Weakness: comparisons could be more comprehensive, especially including other ranking-based losses.  
- Quality: technically sound with clear explanations and consistent notation.  
- Clarity: well-written paper with well-designed figures.  
- Originality: presents a novel approach to top-k multiclass classification.  
- Significance: addresses a common problem and offers a scalable, effective solution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
