- Proposes Top-k Multiclass SVM that directly optimizes top-k classification error using a novel "top-k hinge loss" as a tight convex upper bound on the top-k 0-1 loss.  
- Top-k hinge loss formulated as the sum of the k largest values of the margin-augmented score vector, generalizing Crammer and Singer's multiclass SVM (k=1).  
- Derives the convex conjugate and dual problem for the proposed loss, enabling efficient optimization.  
- Develops a scalable Prox-SDCA-based optimization scheme tailored to the top-k loss with convergence guarantees.  
- Identifies the "top-k simplex" in the dual and provides a novel, efficient projection algorithm onto this set.  
- Relates the proposed loss to prior ranking losses (Usunier et al.) and argues it gives a tighter bound.  
- Extensive empirical evaluation on five image datasets, including Places205 and ImageNet, shows consistent top-k accuracy improvements over baselines.  
- Strength: Addresses a practically important metric (top-k) with a theoretically sound, convex surrogate and novel optimization tools.  
- Strength: Technical derivations and algorithmic development are thorough and appear correct with strong scalability.  
- Strength: Paper is very clear, well-organized, and provides detailed theoretical and algorithmic exposition.  
- Weakness: Experimental comparisons lack broader state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., feature-learning or pooling methods like Sun et al., Gong et al.).  
- Weakness: Reproducibility depends on code quality; authors should ensure well-documented code and scripts to reproduce results.  
- Acceptance argument: Original, theoretically grounded, scalable method with empirical gains and potentially broadly useful optimization contributions.  
- Rejection argument: Experimental scope could be expanded to compare against a wider set of competitive methods on the benchmarks.
