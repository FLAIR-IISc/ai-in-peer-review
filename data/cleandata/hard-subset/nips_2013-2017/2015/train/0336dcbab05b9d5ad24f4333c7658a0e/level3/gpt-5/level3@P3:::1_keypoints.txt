- Proposes Top-k Multiclass SVM: a convex “top-k hinge” surrogate using the average of the k largest score residuals, generalizing Crammer–Singer (k=1) and tied to Ky-Fan norms.
- Derives the convex conjugate and introduces the “top-k simplex,” a constrained polytope that defines the dual feasible set.
- Develops an efficient Prox-SDCA solver whose per-example dual update reduces to a (biased) Euclidean projection onto the top-k simplex.
- Presents and analyzes a dedicated algorithm for projection onto the top-k simplex (sorting-based, O(d log d + k d) with a worst-case O(k d) partition search).
- Positions work relative to ordered weighted hinge losses, truncated top-k hinge variants, structured SVMs, and ranking reductions (TopPush), clarifying when recall@k aligns with top-k error.
- Experiments on five image datasets (including Places205 and ILSVRC12 with CNN features) show consistent top-k accuracy gains over strong baselines and scalability to millions of examples.
- Strength: neat, tight, convex, and interpretable surrogate that smoothly generalizes multiclass SVM.
- Strength: solid theoretical contributions—Fenchel-duality, yi-compatibility, conjugate pairs, and characterization of the top-k simplex.
- Strength: efficient, principled optimization with convergence guarantees and a verifiable duality-gap stopping criterion.
- Strength: clear writing, good organization, and helpful geometric/optimization lemmas.
- Weakness: missing empirical comparisons with key prior works [25, 8] due to unavailable implementations—requested re-implementations or matched comparisons.
- Weakness: potential trade-off reducing top-1 accuracy on some datasets; more systematic analysis of top-1 vs top-k (and varying k at train vs eval) needed.
- Weakness: per-update complexity scales with number of classes m via the projection dimension—add runtime/memory analysis versus Crammer–Singer and OVA.
- Weakness: projection algorithm has worst-case O(k d) exhaustive partition search—summarize timing comparisons in main text and describe heuristics to prune partitions.
- Weakness: need more ablations on λ, training k, and the two loss variants (φk vs φ̃k) to assess hyperparameter sensitivity and robustness.
- Requested additions: include comparisons/reproductions for [25, 8] or a careful cross-paper comparison; commit to releasing full training/testing code and projection library with reproduction scripts.
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance contingent on adding the missing comparisons and committing to public code release.
