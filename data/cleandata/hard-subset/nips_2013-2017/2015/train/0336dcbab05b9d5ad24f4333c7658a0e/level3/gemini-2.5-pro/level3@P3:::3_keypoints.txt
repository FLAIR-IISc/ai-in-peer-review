- Introduces Top-k Multiclass SVM to directly optimize top-k classification accuracy rather than top-1.  
- Motivates top-k error as more practical for problems with many ambiguous classes.  
- Replaces standard multiclass hinge loss with a novel convex "top-k hinge loss" as an upper bound on non-convex top-k zero-one loss.  
- Constructs the convex bound by averaging the k largest scores of incorrect classes.  
- Derives the convex conjugate of the new loss, leading to the definition of a "top-k simplex."  
- Proposes a novel, efficient algorithm for projection onto the top-k simplex, a key optimization component.  
- Integrates the formulation into the scalable Prox-SDCA optimization framework.  
- Generalizes the Crammer and Singer multiclass SVM and relates thoughtfully to ranking losses by Usunier et al.  
- Paper is technically sound; derivations and optimization treatment are non-trivial and well-executed.  
- Strong empirical validation across five diverse datasets (including Places205 and ImageNet) showing consistent top-k improvements.  
- The projection algorithm is an independent algorithmic contribution with potential broader use.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent notation.  
- Shows that the proposed φk is a tighter bound than a related prior formulation φ̃k.  
- Practical significance: directly optimizes a commonly used metric and scales to large datasets.  
- Suggestion: strengthen intuition/motivation for averaging top-k scores as the chosen convex relaxation.  
- Minor typo: remove redundant word "optimal" on page 3, Section 2.2 last paragraph.  
- Acceptance arguments: important problem, novel and sound solution, algorithmic contribution, strong empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Rejection concern: incremental novelty relative to general ranking-loss frameworks [27] could be argued, but mitigated by tighter bound and new optimization machinery.
