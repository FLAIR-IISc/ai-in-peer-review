- Introduces a novel multiclass SVM approach that directly optimizes for top-k accuracy.
- Proposes a convex upper bound called the top-k hinge loss and an optimization scheme using projections onto a new “top-k simplex.”
- Develops an efficient optimization framework based on Prox-SDCA.
- Derivation of the top-k hinge loss and its conjugate is clear and logically presented.
- Connects well to prior work (Crammer & Singer, Usunier et al.) and demonstrates good literature understanding.
- Experimental results on five datasets show consistent top-k accuracy improvements over baselines.
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets, including Places205 and ImageNet.
- Limited motivation/justification for choosing the specific form of the top-k hinge loss; alternatives and tightness vs tractability discussion would help.
- Minor typographical error on page 3: phrasing “globally optimal leads to.”
- Strengths: novelty, technical soundness, experimental validation, scalability, and clear writing.
- Weaknesses: insufficient justification for loss form choice and a minor typo.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid, empirically effective, significant contribution, and well-presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited deeper justification of the loss form and potentially incremental improvements.
- Overall recommendation: accept—strengths outweigh minor weaknesses, with suggestion to better motivate the loss choice.
