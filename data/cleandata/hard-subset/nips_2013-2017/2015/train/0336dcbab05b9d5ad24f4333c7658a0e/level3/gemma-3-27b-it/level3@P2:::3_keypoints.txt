- Proposes optimizing multiclass SVMs directly for top-k performance by relaxing penalties outside the top-k and introducing a convex upper bound with projection onto a novel "top-k simplex."
- Builds on Crammer & Singer formulation and relates clearly to ranking-based losses and other prior work (e.g., Usunier, Joachims, Li).
- Top-k hinge loss gives a principled convex upper bound on top-k zero-one loss enabling efficient optimization.
- Introduces the top-k simplex (Δk) and derives its convex conjugate as a significant theoretical contribution.
- Uses Prox-SDCA for optimization; framework is well-justified and appears scalable.
- Experiments on five datasets (Caltech101, MIT Indoor67, SUN397, Places205, ImageNet2012) show consistent top-k accuracy improvements over baselines.
- Releases C++ implementation, aiding reproducibility and adoption.
- Motivation for directly optimizing top-k could be expanded beyond matching evaluation metrics, especially for large-class datasets.
- Needs clearer linkage between the theoretical extra slack of the top-k hinge loss and the observed empirical improvements.
- Minor typo on page 3 ("globally optimal leads to" should be corrected).
- Experimental analysis could be deeper — e.g., explore top-k vs top-1 trade-offs and performance across different k values.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, convincing experiments, practical relevance, and code release.
- Arguments against acceptance: improvements are incremental, theoretical depth in convex optimization is limited, and motivation could be stronger.
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed paper recommended for acceptance despite not being a paradigm shift.
