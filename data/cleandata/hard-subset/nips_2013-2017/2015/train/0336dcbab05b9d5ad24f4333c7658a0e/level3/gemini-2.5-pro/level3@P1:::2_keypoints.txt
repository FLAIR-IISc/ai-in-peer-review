- Addresses directly optimizing top-k performance in multiclass classification, important for large-class, ambiguous tasks (e.g., ImageNet).  
- Observes standard multiclass SVMs optimize a top-1 surrogate and can be suboptimal for top-k evaluation.  
- Proposes the "top-k multiclass SVM" with a novel top-k hinge loss defined as the average of the k largest incorrect-class scores offset by the correct-class score.  
- Shows the top-k hinge is a tight, convex upper bound on the non-convex top-k error and reduces to the standard multiclass SVM when k=1.  
- Relates the loss to Usunier et al.’s ranking losses, arguing their formulation gives a tighter bound and comparing empirically.  
- Optimizes the l2-regularized objective using Prox-SDCA, requiring derivation of the Fenchel dual.  
- Identifies and characterizes the dual constraint set as a novel "top-k simplex" (a generalization of the simplex).  
- Develops a novel, efficient algorithm for projection onto the top-k simplex, a non-trivial contribution of independent interest.  
- Empirical evaluation on five image datasets (including SUN397, Places205, ImageNet) shows consistent improvements in top-k accuracy over strong baselines and scalability to >1M examples.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and organized, with a good comparison to prior work.  
- Strength: technically original—new loss, dual characterization, and projection algorithm make the approach practical and theoretically sound.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis of the trade-off between improving top-k and potentially degrading top-1 accuracy, and when this trade-off occurs.  
- Weakness: projection algorithm has a worst-case runtime of O(d log d + k d); discussion of behavior for very large d (e.g., tens of thousands) is limited.  
- Weakness: two loss variants (φk vs. φ̃k) perform similarly in experiments; more intuition or guidance on when the tighter bound helps is needed.
