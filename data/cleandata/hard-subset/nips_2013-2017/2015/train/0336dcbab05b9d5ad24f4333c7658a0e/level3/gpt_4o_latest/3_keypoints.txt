- Paper proposes a top-k multiclass SVM optimizing top-k error via a convex top-k hinge loss and Prox-SDCA, showing empirical gains on five image datasets.  
- Tackles an important, non-trivial problem relevant for large-class, ambiguous settings.  
- Demonstrates consistent top-k accuracy improvements and scalability on large datasets (e.g., ImageNet, Places205).  
- Introduces a novel top-k hinge loss, efficient Prox-SDCA optimization, and a projection algorithm for the top-k simplex.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- Needs stronger intuition and practical motivation for the specific top-k hinge loss choice over alternatives.  
- Contains a minor typographical error ("globally optimal leads to" → "globally leads to").  
- Should provide deeper comparison to ranking-based losses (e.g., Usunier et al. 2010) to better situate the contribution.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, offers a novel theoretically sound method with strong empirical and scalable results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient motivation for the loss choice and limited contextualization within related ranking-based literature.  
- Recommendation: Accept, pending addressing the motivation discussion, related-work comparisons, and the minor typo.
