- Proposes a top-k multiclass SVM that optimizes top-k classification performance.  
- Introduces the top-k hinge loss using the sum of the k largest scores as a tight convex upper bound.  
- Recovers standard multiclass SVM as a special case when k = 1.  
- Derives the convex conjugate and defines the top-k simplex as a natural extension of the simplex.  
- Develops an efficient Prox-SDCA optimization algorithm with a novel projection onto the top-k simplex.  
- Establishes strong theoretical connections to ranking-based losses.  
- Empirical results show consistent top-k accuracy improvements across five image classification datasets versus standard SVM and baselines.  
- Paper is very clear and well written, with helpful illustrations and structured derivations.  
- Original and significant contribution to multiclass classification and large-scale image recognition.  
- Main weakness: missing empirical comparisons with prior works [25, 8]; reviewer requests these be addressed before acceptance.
