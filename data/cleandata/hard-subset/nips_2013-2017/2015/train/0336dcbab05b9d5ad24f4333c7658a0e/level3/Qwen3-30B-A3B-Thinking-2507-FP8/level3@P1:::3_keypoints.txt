- Proposes an extension of multiclass SVM to optimize top-k classification by defining a top-k loss where a prediction is correct if the true class is among the top k.  
- Introduces a tight convex upper bound (top-k hinge loss) that extends the Crammer and Singer multiclass SVM to the top-k setting.  
- Derives the convex conjugate of the top-k hinge loss and provides a novel, efficient projection onto the top-k simplex.  
- Optimizes the proposed loss efficiently using Prox-SDCA, leveraging the new projection operator.  
- Carefully relates the method to prior work (notably Usunier et al. [27]) and shows their formulation gives a tighter convex upper bound than alternative formulations.  
- Theoretical analysis is complete with clear propositions and proofs supporting the optimization framework.  
- Empirical evaluation on five datasets (Caltech 101 Silhouettes, MIT Indoor 67, SUN 397, Places 205, ImageNet 2012) shows consistent top-k improvements, e.g., 1.2–2.6% in top-5 accuracy.  
- Demonstrates practical scalability to large datasets with millions of examples, an important applied contribution.  
- Strengths: elegant, theoretically grounded extension of multiclass SVM; useful top-k simplex projection; strong empirical results across diverse datasets.  
- Weaknesses: motivation for selecting the specific convex upper bound could be stronger and more intuitive.  
- Minor issues: a typo on page 3 and limited discussion of trade-offs / practical implications for top-1 accuracy when optimizing for top-k.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address motivation and the small typo.
