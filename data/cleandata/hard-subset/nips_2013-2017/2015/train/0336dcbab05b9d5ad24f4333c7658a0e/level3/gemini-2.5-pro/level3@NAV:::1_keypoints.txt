- Proposes a top-k multiclass SVM with a novel top-k hinge loss that is a tight convex upper bound using the sum of the k largest scores.  
- Derives the convex conjugate of the loss, introducing a "top-k simplex" (a contraction of the standard simplex).  
- Presents a fast, scalable optimization scheme based on Prox-SDCA.  
- Develops an efficient algorithm for projection onto the top-k simplex (technical result of independent interest).  
- Situates the work within existing literature and discusses relations/advantages over Usunier et al.'s ranking losses.  
- Theoretical and technical formulation is elegant, clear, and a principled extension of multiclass SVMs.  
- Empirically shows consistent, notable improvements in top-k accuracy on five image classification datasets versus several baselines.  
- Main weakness: missing comparisons to relevant baselines (Sun & Ponce, Gong et al.) due to unavailable implementations—should be added.  
- Reproducibility concern: authors should publicly release their code upon acceptance.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-written, original, and significant contribution with strong theoretical and empirical support.
