- Proposes top-k multiclass SVM: allow k guesses and evaluate with top-k error instead of zero-one loss.
- Paper is well-written, non-trivial, and clearly organized.
- Derives a convex top-k hinge loss as an upper bound on top-k error.
- Introduces an efficient optimization scheme using projection onto the top-k simplex.
- Experiments on five datasets show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy versus baselines.
- Novel approach with significant practical value for large-scale multiclass problems.
- Lacks clear motivation for why the chosen top-k hinge loss is preferable to alternatives.
- Does not provide thorough comparisons with other loss functions to demonstrate superiority.
- Assumes reader familiarity with convex optimization and multiclass classification.
- Overall recommendation: accept, but request stronger motivation and additional comparative experiments.
