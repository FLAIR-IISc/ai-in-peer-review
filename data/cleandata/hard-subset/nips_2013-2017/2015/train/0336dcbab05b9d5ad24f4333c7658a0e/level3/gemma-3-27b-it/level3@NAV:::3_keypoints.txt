- Proposes directly optimizing top-k accuracy for multiclass SVMs via a convex top-k hinge loss.  
- Introduces an efficient optimization scheme using projection onto a novel "top-k simplex."  
- Derives a clear primal-conjugate pair for the top-k hinge loss and connects to existing ranking-based losses.  
- Experimental results on five datasets show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy over baselines (Crammer–Singer and ranking methods).  
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets like Places205 and ImageNet.  
- Paper is well-written and technically rigorous.  
- Core motivation for choosing this specific convex relaxation (top-k hinge) is under-discussed.  
- Lacks thorough comparison with alternative convex relaxations or deeper justification of the proposed upper bound's properties.  
- Minor typo: page 3 phrase “globally optimal leads to” — remove "optimal."  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, empirical validation, and scalability.  
- Main argument against acceptance: insufficient motivation/justification for the specific loss choice.
