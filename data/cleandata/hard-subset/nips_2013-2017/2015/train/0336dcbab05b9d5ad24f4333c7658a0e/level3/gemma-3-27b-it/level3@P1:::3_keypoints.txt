- Introduces a novel multiclass SVM that directly optimizes top-k performance to address class ambiguity in high-class-count image classification.  
- Proposes a top-k multiclass SVM based on a tight convex upper bound of top-k error and an efficient optimization scheme using projection onto a new “top-k simplex.”  
- Builds on Crammer and Singer and ranking-loss literature, offering, to the authors’ knowledge, the first direct optimization of top-k error.  
- Experimental results show consistent top-k accuracy improvements across five datasets, including large-scale Places205 and ImageNet2012 (supplement).  
- Derivation of the top-k hinge loss and its convex conjugate is clear; the top-k simplex is a significant technical contribution.  
- Optimization framework using Prox-SDCA and efficient projection is appropriate and well-presented.  
- Release of a C++ library enhances reproducibility and practical applicability.  
- Comprehensive mathematical treatment and discussion of relationships between different top-k loss formulations strengthen the work.  
- Authors acknowledge and demonstrate trade-offs between top-k gains and potential top-1 accuracy loss.  
- Weakness: Rationale for choosing the specific convex upper bound φk over the alternative φ̃k is underdeveloped and needs stronger theoretical justification.  
- Suggestion: Provide ablation studies or deeper theoretical comparison between upper bounds and a more detailed comparison to Usunier et al. [27].  
- Minor issue: Typo on page 3 ("globally optimal leads to") should be corrected.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, strong empirical results, practicality (code release), and clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient motivation/analysis for upper-bound choice and a minor typo.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong, high-quality paper worthy of acceptance to NIPS 2015.
