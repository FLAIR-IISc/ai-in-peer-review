- Proposes a novel extension of multiclass SVM to directly optimize top-k performance for large-class image classification.
- Introduces the top-k multiclass SVM based on a tight convex upper bound of the top-k error and derives a top-k hinge loss.
- Proposes a fast optimization scheme using an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex.
- Paper is well-written, notation is clear, and derivations are concise and non-trivial.
- Experimental results show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy over various baselines.
- Provides a useful connection to ranking-based losses, showing the top-k hinge loss is a special case of convex ranking/multiclass losses.
- Theoretical analysis and empirical claims are technically sound and well-supported.
- Main weakness: the paper could better motivate the choice of the top-k hinge loss and provide more intuition for why it is a good upper bound.
- Minor issue: typo on page 3 ("globally optimal leads to" should be "globally optimal").
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clear derivation, efficient algorithm, and consistent empirical gains.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited motivation for the loss choice and a minor editorial error.
- Overall rating: 8/10.
