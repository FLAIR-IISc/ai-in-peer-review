- Introduces Principal Differences Analysis (PDA) to find projections that maximize Wasserstein divergence between high-dimensional distributions.  
- Well-written, clear, and covers non-trivial material.  
- Use of the Wasserstein distance is a sensible and effective choice for the divergence measure.  
- Experimental results are strong and demonstrate PDA’s effectiveness.  
- Comparisons to sparse PCA and logistic regression are useful and favorable.  
- Novel and significant contribution for characterizing differences between populations.  
- Needs more motivation and discussion for the choice of loss function and comparisons to other divergences.  
- Typo on page 3: remove "optimal" from "globally optimal leads to".  
- Paper can be dense/technical, potentially hard to follow for readers without a strong statistical background.
