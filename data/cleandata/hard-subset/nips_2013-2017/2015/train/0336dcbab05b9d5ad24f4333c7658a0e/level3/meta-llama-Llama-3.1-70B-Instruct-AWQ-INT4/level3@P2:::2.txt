The paper proposes a novel approach to multiclass classification problems where the cost occurs only if the ground truth is outside the top k predictions. This is a common scenario in many image classification problems with a large number of classes. The authors introduce the top-k multiclass SVM algorithm, which explicitly models this top-k cost scenario.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors clearly explain the limitations of existing methods. They provide a thorough review of related work, including multiclass SVM and ranking-based losses. The top-k hinge loss formulation is a natural extension of the standard loss, and the authors provide a convex upper bound on the top-k error.

The optimization procedure for the top-k loss is efficiently derived using Fenchel duality, and the authors provide a primal-dual formulation of the problem. The use of proximal stochastic dual coordinate ascent (Prox-SDCA) framework is well-justified, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the advantages of their approach.

The paper also presents experimental results on five image classification datasets, demonstrating the scalability and effectiveness of the proposed top-k multiclass SVM algorithm. The results show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy over compared methods, including the multiclass SVM of Crammer and Singer.

* The paper addresses a common problem in multiclass classification and provides a novel solution.

* The optimization procedure is efficiently derived, and the use of Prox-SDCA framework is well-justified.
* The experimental results demonstrate the scalability and effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
