- Proposes a top-k multiclass SVM optimizing top-k accuracy via a convex top-k hinge loss and its conjugate.  
- Introduces the top-k simplex and an efficient projection algorithm, optimized with Prox-SDCA and evaluated on five image datasets.  
- Theoretical derivations (Propositions 1–4, Theorem 1) are sound and logically presented.  
- Experiments show consistent top-k accuracy improvements and scalability to large datasets (Places205, ImageNet).  
- Lacks a theoretical proof that optimizing the top-k hinge loss necessarily improves top-k performance—current argument is heuristic.  
- Claims superiority over Usunier et al. [27] (φk ≤ φ̃k) but offers no concrete examples or experiments showing practical impact.  
- Projection algorithm runtime stated as O(d log d + kd) but lacks comparison to standard simplex projections (O(d log d)) and detailed complexity analysis.  
- Choice of the specific convex relaxation φk(a) is not well justified compared to other possible relaxations.  
- The connection to ranking-based losses (Usunier et al.) is discussed but not made explicit enough.  
- Practical implementation details are sparse, particularly how the exhaustive search in Lemma 3 is implemented.  
- Novel contributions include the top-k hinge loss, its convex conjugate, the top-k simplex, and the projection algorithm.  
- The top-k simplex is an elegant and novel extension of the standard simplex.  
- The paper should better distinguish itself from related/top-k work in deep learning and prior ranking losses.  
- High significance for applications where top-k accuracy matters, especially large-scale image classification.  
- Demonstrated scalability is a strong practical contribution.  
- Could expand discussion of real-world scenarios prioritizing top-k and integrate comparisons to recent deep-learning top-k methods.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with strong empirical validation; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Strengths: clear theory, novel top-k simplex, efficient projection algorithm, comprehensive experiments, scalability.  
- Weaknesses: missing theoretical justification for top-k improvement, unconvincing comparison to [27], insufficient complexity analysis, limited relation to deep-learning approaches.  
- Requested revisions: provide theoretical justification for top-k improvement, stronger comparison to Usunier et al., more detailed complexity analysis, and broaden related-work discussion to include deep-learning methods.  
- Specific comment: clarify why φk(a) < φ(a) is beneficial beyond “extra slack.”  
- Specific comment: emphasize that ψk’s nonconvexity arises from the k-th largest operator, not the sum.  
- Specific comment: highlight that ∆k is a contraction of the simplex (∆k ⊂ ∆) and its implications.  
- Specific comment: justify why top-k is more appropriate than top-1 for very large-class problems.  
- Specific comment: substantiate the claim that [11] does not scale to large datasets or explain why.
