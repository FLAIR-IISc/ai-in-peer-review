- Overall: Solid, well-motivated extension of multiclass SVM for top-k with convex relaxation and Prox-SDCA optimization; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Summary: Proposes a top-k hinge loss as a convex upper bound to a non-convex top-k zero-one loss, derives its Fenchel conjugate, and optimizes with Prox-SDCA; shows consistent top-k accuracy gains on image datasets.
- Strength — Motivation: Relaxing penalties for top-k predictions addresses practical ranking and retrieval needs.
- Strength — Theory: Rigorous derivation of convex relaxation and conjugate with clear links to prior multiclass and ranking work.
- Strength — Optimization: Uses Prox-SDCA for scalability and provides a detailed treatment of projection onto the top-k simplex.
- Strength — Experiments: Evaluated across multiple datasets with comparisons to SVMPerf and TopPush showing consistent improvements.
- Strength — Presentation: Clear writing, logical organization, and public C++ code release for reproducibility.
- Weakness — Incremental gains: Improvements in top-k accuracy are consistent but modest; more analysis of where gains matter is needed.
- Weakness — Novelty: Core idea parallels existing ranking literature; novelty mainly in SVM formulation and analysis.
- Weakness — Projection complexity: Top-k simplex projection adds significant algorithmic complexity; computational cost needs deeper analysis.
- Weakness — Missing ablations: No ablation studies to isolate contributions of components (e.g., bias in projection).
- Detail — Section 2.2: Request clearer, intuitive example showing non-convexity of ψk.
- Detail — Section 3.2.1: Ask justification for choosing algorithm [13] for continuous quadratic knapsack and whether alternatives were considered.
- Detail — Section 5: Request quantitative summary (e.g., average improvement) to support claim of systematic top-k gains.
- Experiments question: Request results for varying k and identification of optimal k values.
- Related work: Suggest deeper discussion of learning-to-rank methods that optimize top-k metrics (e.g., ListNet, LambdaMART).
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a practical problem, solid theory, scalable implementation, consistent empirical gains, and released code.
- Arguments against acceptance: Incremental contribution, reliance on prior ranking ideas, and potential practical limitations from projection complexity.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: (1) strengthen analysis of when gains are largest, (2) justify and compare the chosen projection algorithm, (3) add ablation studies, (4) expand related work on learning-to-rank.
