Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes a convex surrogate for top-k multiclass classification, the top-k hinge loss φk(a) = max{0, (1/k) ∑j=1..k (a+c)[j]}, which upper-bounds the nonconvex top-k zero-one loss ψk(a) and reduces to the Crammer–Singer multiclass hinge loss at k=1. A key technical contribution is the characterization of the convex conjugate of φk as the indicator of a novel “top-k simplex” Δk, a contraction of the standard simplex with per-coordinate coupling constraints xi ≤ (1/k)⟨1,x⟩. This enables a clean Fenchel-dual derivation for an ℓ2-regularized formulation and yields a Prox-SDCA optimization scheme where each dual block update reduces to a (biased) Euclidean projection onto Δk(r). The authors develop efficient algorithms for this projection by reducing to either a continuous quadratic knapsack problem (when the sum constraint is active) or to a projection onto a “top-k cone” (otherwise), with runtime O(d log d + kd). They also position their loss relative to the ranking-based family Lβ of Usunier et al. (ICML’09): the alternative φ̃k that averages the positive parts of the top-k margins is shown to be a looser bound than φk except in edge cases. Empirically, on five image datasets (Caltech101 silhouettes, MIT Indoor67, SUN397, Places205, ImageNet12) using fixed CNN features, the proposed top-k SVM variants improve top-k accuracy consistently over Crammer–Singer and over several ranking baselines (SVMPerf for recall@k, TopPush, Wsabie++), with code released. The optimization builds on Shalev-Shwartz & Zhang’s Prox-SDCA; the dual derivation corrects a minor typo from that work.

Strengths:
- Clear and principled surrogate: φk is a natural convex upper bound to top-k error and strictly tighter than φ̃k in common regimes; reduces to well-known multiclass SVM at k=1.
- Convex-analytic contribution: identification of the top-k simplex Δk as the conjugate domain and a variational representation φk(a)=max⟨a+c,λ⟩ over Δk. This is insightful and likely reusable.
- Optimization contribution: careful dual derivation and an efficient projection algorithm onto Δk(r), handling both the knapsack-active and cone cases; complexity and implementation details are provided.
- Practicality and scalability: Prox-SDCA with a per-example block update and a computable duality gap; evidence of scalability to millions of examples and 1000 classes; released C++/Matlab code.
- Empirical gains: consistent top-k accuracy improvements across datasets; competitive against ranking-based methods and one-vs-all SVMs.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- Theoretical guarantees beyond convexity are limited. There is no analysis of calibration/consistency of φk with respect to top-k error, nor generalization bounds tailored to top-k. Adding even partial theory (e.g., Bayes-optimal margin conditions or Fisher consistency for top-k) would strengthen the contribution.
- Tightness discussion is qualitative. While φk ≤ φ̃k is established, quantitative bounds on the gap to ψk, or conditions under which optimization of φk preferentially increases top-k accuracy without hurting top-1, are not provided. A small synthetic study illustrating regimes of improvement vs. trade-off would be useful.
- Experimental scope: evaluation is on linear models over fixed CNN features; comparisons to standard cross-entropy softmax (trained or fine-tuned, which is commonly used and often optimizes top-k well in practice) are missing. Even with frozen features, an OVA logistic baseline and a multinomial logistic regression baseline would be informative.
- Fairness of baselines: for some ranking methods, the reduction to binary is infeasible at scale and the paper falls back to OVA variants, which may disadvantage those baselines. Where possible, report both reduction-based and OVA numbers on smaller datasets, and clearly state where fallbacks were used to avoid bias.
- Projection algorithm details: while the worst-case complexity is acceptable, the exhaustive search over partitions in the cone case could be costly for large d and k. More extensive timing comparisons (beyond the supplement) and ablations on the effect of the bias term ρ would increase confidence.
- Clarity: some notation is dense and several key proofs and the full projection algorithm are deferred to the supplement. Including a concise pseudocode for the projection and a brief worked example would help.

Quality:
- Technically sound overall; derivations are careful, with a correct use of Fenchel duality and j-compatibility. The projection method is well motivated. Experiments are thorough for the chosen setting, with cross-validation and multiple datasets. Claims are generally supported, though additional theoretical insight would be beneficial.

Clarity:
- Generally well organized. The introduction of Δk and its geometric intuition are clear (figure helps). Some sections (projection onto the top-k cone) are terse; adding a short algorithm box in the main paper would improve readability.

Originality:
- The top-k hinge φk and its conjugate/top-k simplex are novel to my knowledge, beyond the Lβ family. The projection algorithm for Δk(r) appears new and nontrivial due to the coupling constraint. The connection to Prox-SDCA is natural but executed well.

Significance:
- Addressing top-k metrics is important in large-class problems. The method is scalable and shows consistent empirical gains, making it likely to be adopted. The convex-analytic tools (Δk, projection) may spur follow-up work.

Pros for acceptance:
- Conceptually clean, convex surrogate tailored to top-k with a principled dual.
- Solid optimization and an implementable, scalable algorithm with stopping criteria.
- Consistent empirical improvements on relevant benchmarks; code release.

Cons for acceptance:
- Lack of calibration/consistency theory for top-k.
- Missing comparison to multinomial logistic/softmax baselines and limited analysis of trade-offs between top-1 and top-k.
- Some projection details relegated to the supplement; potential runtime concerns in worst cases.

Recommendation: Weak accept. The paper offers a meaningful, technically solid contribution with practical impact. Strengthening the theoretical understanding of φk and broadening baselines (especially softmax/logistic) would elevate it further.

Constructive suggestions:
- Add a brief theoretical discussion on when φk is top-k calibrated or at least conditions guaranteeing improvement in top-k error.
- Include multinomial logistic regression and OVA logistic baselines; on at least one smaller dataset, compare to fine-tuned softmax to contextualize gains.
- Expand runtime and convergence plots for the projection and SDCA (iterations vs. duality gap; effect of k and d).
- Provide a small ablation varying ρ in the biased projection to show its effect.
- Clarify how training k should be chosen relative to evaluation k, and report sensitivity to mismatch.
