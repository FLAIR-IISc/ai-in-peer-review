- Introduces a convex relaxation of the top-k zero-one loss for multiclass SVMs, called the top-k hinge loss.
- Derives the Fenchel conjugate and defines a novel “top-k simplex” for the loss.
- Uses Prox-SDCA for scalable optimization and provides implementation details plus released code.
- Empirical results on image classification datasets show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy over standard multiclass SVMs.
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous and the primal–conjugate connections and ranking-loss relations are insightful.
- Optimization approach is appropriate and well-explained; experiments are reasonably thorough.
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation; Figure 2 effectively illustrates the top-k simplex.
- The distinction between the two loss variants (φk and φ̃k) is subtle and needs clearer, more intuitive explanation.
- Originality is good: applying convex relaxation to top-k SVMs is novel, though it builds on established convex/SDCA techniques.
- Significance is practical—useful for applications needing ranked outputs—but performance gains are modest.
- Downsides: gains are not large and theoretical novelty is incremental relative to prior work.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify φk vs φ̃k and discuss broader applications beyond image classification.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 7/10; final verdict: Accept.
