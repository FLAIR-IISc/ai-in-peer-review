- Proposes a top-k hinge loss and convex relaxation for optimizing top-k accuracy in multiclass SVMs.  
- Derives the dual formulation and introduces the “top-k simplex” as the conjugate domain.  
- Provides an efficient projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex and uses Prox-SDCA for optimization.  
- Empirically shows consistent top-k accuracy improvements on multiple image classification datasets.  
- Commits to releasing code for reproducibility.  

- Clear motivation: optimizing top-k accuracy is relevant when approximate correctness suffices.  
- Rigorous mathematical treatment with sound derivations and a novel constrained polytope.  
- Efficient optimization approach and a corrected SDCA update typo from prior work.  
- Comprehensive empirical validation with comparisons to SVMPerf and TopPush.  

- Incremental novelty: core idea of loss relaxation for top-k is not entirely new.  
- Projection algorithm complexity needs more detailed runtime analysis, especially for exhaustive search cases and scaling with k and d.  
- Limited ablation studies—more analysis of φk vs φ̃k and SDCA/projection parameters is needed.  
- Discussion of the “bias” in the top-k hinge loss is insufficiently explored.  

- Suggest expanding related work to include broader ranking losses (e.g., listwise methods).  
- Recommend adding a visual aid and clearer explanation for the projection onto ∆k(r), especially non-equality constraint cases.  
- Ask for justification of choosing algorithm [13] for the continuous quadratic knapsack step.  
- Request error bars/confidence intervals and training time comparisons to strengthen experimental claims.  
- Advise discussing reasons for dataset-dependent variability in improvement magnitudes.  

- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, valuable dual/top-k simplex derivation, consistent empirical gains, and released implementation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: relatively incremental, insufficient runtime analysis for projection, and limited empirical ablations.  

- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: expand related work, provide detailed runtime scaling of the projection algorithm, add ablation studies for parameter impacts, and address clarity/experimental completeness comments.
