- Proposes a novel top-k extension of multiclass SVM with a convex top-k hinge loss and corresponding top-k simplex.  
- Derives the convex conjugate of the top-k hinge loss and introduces a contracted "top-k simplex."  
- Presents an efficient projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex enabling practical optimization with Prox-SDCA.  
- Empirical results show consistent top-k accuracy improvements across five image datasets, including a +2.5% top-5 gain on SUN397 and scalability to ImageNet.  
- Originality: clear and novel theoretical contribution extending multiclass SVM to optimize top-k performance.  
- Quality: rigorous theoretical derivations (Propositions 1–4, Theorem 1) and a novel projection algorithm.  
- Clarity: well-organized writing, consistent notation, and clear relation to prior work (Crammer & Singer, Usunier et al.).  
- Significance: addresses a practically relevant problem with demonstrated improvements and scalability to large datasets.  
- Weakness: insufficient experimental detail and key numbers for the largest datasets (Places205, ImageNet) in the main text.  
- Weakness: unclear motivation and empirical justification for presenting two top-k hinge variants (φk and φ̃k).  
- Weakness: lacks systematic analysis of the top-1 vs. top-k accuracy tradeoff when optimizing for top-k.  
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS; main contribution is strong and weaknesses can be addressed in a camera-ready version.
