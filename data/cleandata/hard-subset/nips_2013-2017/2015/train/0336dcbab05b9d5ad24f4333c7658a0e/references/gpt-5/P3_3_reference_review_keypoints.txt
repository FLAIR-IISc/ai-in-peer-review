- Proposes a convex top-k surrogate φk (average of k largest margins) and an efficient large-scale Prox-SDCA solver.  
- Derives the convex conjugate and identifies a novel “top-k simplex” Δk with coupling constraints.  
- Each dual block update reduces to a biased Euclidean projection onto Δk(r); projection solved by two-regime algorithm (tight sum → quadratic knapsack; otherwise → top-k cone via thresholding).  
- Experiments on multiple image datasets with CNN features show consistent top-k accuracy gains and released code.

Strengths:
- Principled convex surrogate directly targeting top-k error, often tighter than prior ordered-hinge surrogates.  
- Elegant dual derivation: Δk provides a clean conjugate characterization and reusable geometry.  
- Solid optimization: Prox-SDCA with exact block updates, computable duality gap, and practical projection routine with runtime comparable to simplex projection.  
- Empirical evidence across datasets, including large-scale, showing improved top-k accuracy and scalability.  
- Reasonable discussion of relation to ranking-based losses and structured SVM/recall@k tradeoffs.

Weaknesses:
- No statistical consistency or calibration results for φk; theoretical calibration discussion is missing.  
- Main paper experimental reporting is thin; large-scale results and ablations (train vs test k, λ sensitivity) deferred to supplement.  
- Missing strong baselines like multinomial logistic regression (softmax) on fixed features.  
- Projection algorithm’s correctness and edge cases rely on supplement; complexity O(d log d + k d) may be costly for very large m and needs empirical profiling.  
- Notational/typo issues, incomplete references, and limited discussion on numerical stability, warm-starts across k, and kernelization practicality.

Quality:
- Technically sound overall; convex analysis and dual derivation coherent, projection algorithm nontrivial but appearing correct, Prox-SDCA adaptation carefully executed.  
- Tightness claims for φk vs. φ̃k supported analytically and empirically.

Clarity:
- Generally well-written and organized, but projection section is dense and would benefit from a high-level roadmap, a 1D/2D illustrative example, and a brief proof sketch.  
- Hyperparameter search and compute budget description is lacking for reproducibility.

Originality:
- The top-k hinge as average of top-k margins and the Δk conjugate characterization appear novel; projection algorithm and Δk geometry are original and practically important.  
- Use of Prox-SDCA itself is standard but integration with the new loss is novel.

Significance:
- Relevant and impactful for multiclass vision tasks where top-k metrics matter; Δk geometry and projection method may generalize to other structured surrogates.  
- Empirical gains are moderate but consistent, indicating practical utility.

Questions / Suggestions:
- Provide calibration/consistency results for φk or discuss known barriers/conditions.  
- Analyze how to choose train k when evaluating at a different k′ (grid of train k vs test k).  
- Add softmax (multinomial logistic) baseline on fixed features.  
- Include per-epoch timing and projections/sec as m grows; compare projection cost empirically to simplex projection and LibLinear.  
- Clarify Proposition 4 notation/scaling and include a compact derivation sketch.  
- Cite Ky Fan literature and connect Δk to known polytopes (e.g., uniform matroid base polytopes).

Minor comments:
- Fix missing years/details in references, section numbering typos, and other minor typos.  
- State the Lipschitz constant of φk and whether SDCA tuning uses it.  
- Discuss numerical stability when top-k scores are tied.

Recommendation:
- Weak accept: valuable and technically solid contribution; strengthening theoretical calibration and expanding baselines would increase impact.
