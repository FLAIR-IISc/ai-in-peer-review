Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes a convex surrogate for top-k multiclass classification, extending the Crammer–Singer multiclass SVM by introducing a “top-k hinge” loss φk(a) = max{0, (1/k)∑j=1..k (a+c)[j]}, which upper-bounds the nonconvex top-k zero-one loss ψk. The authors show φk is tighter than (and bounded by) a Usunier-style ordered-weighted hinge loss φ̃k(a) = (1/k)∑j=1..k max{0, (a+c)[j]}. They derive the convex conjugate of φk, introducing the “top-k simplex” ∆k as its effective domain, and position their approach as a natural generalization of the Crammer–Singer loss (recovered at k=1). Optimization is done via Prox-SDCA, requiring a novel (biased) projection onto ∆k(r); they give an efficient algorithm by reducing to a continuous quadratic knapsack problem in certain cases and otherwise solving a top-k cone projection via thresholds and a tractable enumeration over partitions. Experiments on five image classification benchmarks (including large-scale Places205 and ImageNet) with fixed CNN features show consistent gains in top-k accuracy compared to standard multiclass SVM and ranking-based baselines (SVMPerf Recall@k, TopPush, Wsabie++). Code is released.

This work relates to multiclass SVMs (Crammer & Singer), ranking surrogates and LaRank/OWPC (Usunier et al.), structured SVM optimization of multivariate measures (Joachims), and convex functions based on sums of k largest elements (Ogryczak & Tamir), as well as SDCA optimization (Shalev-Shwartz & Zhang). The top-k simplex and its projection algorithm appear novel in this context.

Strengths:
- A principled, convex surrogate for top-k multiclass error with a clear relationship to both Crammer–Singer and ranking-based losses; φk is a tighter upper bound than φ̃k for intermediate regimes.
- Clean convex-analytic development: conjugate derivation and the introduction of the top-k simplex ∆k; φk(a) as support function over ∆k is elegant.
- Optimization framework using Prox-SDCA with a well-defined duality gap stopping criterion; kernelization is straightforward.
- A technically solid and useful projection algorithm onto ∆k(r), including handling of the “biased” case; complexity comparable to standard simplex projection.
- Empirical evidence across multiple datasets (including very large ones) showing consistent improvements in top-k metrics; open-source implementation.

Weaknesses:
- Limited statistical guarantees: no calibration/regret or consistency analysis linking φk to top-k error, nor margin bounds beyond convexity/tightness arguments.
- Empirical scope: comparisons largely against SVM and ranking baselines on fixed CNN features; missing comparisons to multinomial logistic regression (softmax) or deep finetuning with top-k-aware objectives, which are common baselines for top-k accuracy.
- Some algorithmic details are deferred to the supplement; the partition-enumeration step for cone projection is worst-case O(kd) after sorting, but practical behavior and wall-clock costs on large m are not deeply analyzed.
- Clarity/notation: occasional typos and section numbering issues; the role of the bias parameter ρ (set to 1 in practice) and its empirical impact are not explored.

Quality:
- The optimization and conjugate derivations appear sound and well-grounded in convex analysis. The top-k cone/simplex projection development is a substantive technical contribution, with proofs referenced in the supplement. The empirical results are plausible and consistent with the stated goal, though the statistical analysis is limited. Runtime/memory results for the projection and SDCA on large m,n would strengthen the quality claims.

Clarity:
- Generally well-written, but the manuscript has minor typographical issues (e.g., section numbering, inconsistent dot prefixes) and heavy notation that could be streamlined. Including a concise pseudocode for the projection routine and a figure clarifying the thresholding/partitioning mechanism would aid readability. A more explicit comparison between φk and φ̃k with plots illustrating the gap regimes would help.

Originality:
- The top-k hinge φk and the associated top-k simplex conjugate/domain are, to my knowledge, novel for multiclass SVMs. The projection algorithm onto ∆k(r) with a bias term is also original. The relation to Usunier’s weighted losses is clearly drawn, and the tighter bound is a meaningful conceptual contribution.

Significance:
- Top-k metrics are important in high-class-count settings. Providing a convex, scalable surrogate and an efficient optimizer is practically useful. The impact would be higher with stronger connections to or improvements over softmax/logistic baselines or deep finetuning, but the contributions are still valuable, especially for linear or kernel multiclass settings and for optimization toolkits.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel and principled convex surrogate with clear theoretical framing.
- New projection algorithm that may be useful beyond this task.
- Scalable optimization with convergence diagnostics; code release.
- Consistent empirical gains on top-k metrics across several datasets.

Cons for acceptance:
- Lack of statistical consistency/calibration results for top-k error.
- Missing strong baselines (multinomial logistic regression, deep finetuning) in experiments.
- Some clarity and presentation issues; practical complexity characterization could be more thorough.

Questions/suggestions for the authors:
- Can you provide calibration/regret bounds for φk with respect to top-k error, or at least discuss conditions under which φk is top-k calibrated?
- Include comparisons to multinomial logistic regression (softmax) trained on the same features, reporting top-k accuracy and training time.
- Report wall-clock times and iteration counts for the projection step vs. standard simplex projection across a range of m,k,d, as well as overall SDCA training time and memory footprint on large datasets.
- Explore sensitivity to k during training vs. evaluation (e.g., train with different k and evaluate top-k’ for multiple k’).
- Clarify the effect of the bias term ρ; is ρ=1 critical, or could ρ be tuned?
- Add pseudocode for the projection algorithm and a brief geometric interpretation of ∆k (e.g., relation to Ky Fan norms/sum of top-k).
- Fix typos/section numbering and specify tie-handling in sorting-based steps.

Recommendation: Weak accept. The paper presents a solid convex surrogate and an optimization method with a nontrivial projection algorithm, and demonstrates consistent empirical benefits on top-k accuracy. Addressing the experimental and clarity points would strengthen the contribution further.
