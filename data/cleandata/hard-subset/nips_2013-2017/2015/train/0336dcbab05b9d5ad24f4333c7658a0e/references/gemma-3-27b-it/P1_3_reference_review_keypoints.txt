- Proposes a Top-k Multiclass SVM that relaxes penalties for predictions within the top-k to improve top-k accuracy.
- Introduces a convex relaxation of the ideal top-k loss and derives its Fenchel conjugate for optimization.
- Implements optimization with Prox-SDCA and demonstrates scalability on large image classification datasets.
- Builds on Crammer and Singer (2005) and connects to ranking-based loss literature (Usunier et al., Joachims).
- Novel top-k hinge loss addresses a practical need not covered by standard SVMs.
- Theoretical derivations (primal-conjugate pair) are rigorous with detailed proofs in the supplement.
- Practicality shown via an efficient implementation and scalability using Prox-SDCA.
- Empirical results consistently improve top-k accuracy across multiple datasets compared to baselines.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear notation and good connection to prior work.
- Open-source code release improves reproducibility and usability.
- Limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, especially regularization (lambda) and choice of k.
- Some datasets show decreased top-1 accuracy, posing trade-off concerns for certain applications.
- Would benefit from comparisons to more recent, highly optimized large-scale multiclass methods.
- Projection algorithm description is dense and relies heavily on references, hindering accessibility.
- For acceptance: novel, sound, scalable, empirically validated, and reproducible work.
- Against acceptance: modest gains, potential top-1 trade-offs, and projection complexity may limit impact.
- Overall recommendation: accept, with minor revisions to address hyperparameter analysis and clarify projection steps.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
