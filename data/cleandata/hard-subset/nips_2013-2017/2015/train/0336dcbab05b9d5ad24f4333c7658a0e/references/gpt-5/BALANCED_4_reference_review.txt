Summary
The paper targets top-k multiclass classification, where a prediction is counted correct if the true label appears among the top k scores. Starting from the Crammer–Singer multiclass SVM, the authors propose a convex top-k hinge loss φk(a) = max{0, (1/k) sum of the k largest coordinates of (a+c)}, which upper-bounds the nonconvex top-k zero-one loss ψk and is tighter than the weighted-ordered hinge losses Lβ of Usunier et al. when βj = 1/k for j ≤ k. They derive the convex conjugate of φk, showing its effective domain is a “top-k simplex” ∆k = {x: ⟨1,x⟩ ≤ 1, 0 ≤ xi ≤ (1/k)⟨1,x⟩}, which reduces to the standard simplex at k=1. Using Fenchel duality, they give a dual for `2-regularized multiclass learning and show how to optimize it via Prox-SDCA with exact per-example multi-coordinate updates. The inner update is a (biased) Euclidean projection onto ∆k(r), for which they develop an algorithm: it reduces to a continuous quadratic knapsack when the sum constraint is active; otherwise it becomes a projection onto a “top-k cone,” handled by analyzing optimality conditions and solving for two thresholds with at most O(kd) iterations after sorting. Empirically, on five image classification datasets (Caltech101 Silhouettes, MIT Indoor67, SUN397, Places205, ImageNet12) with CNN features, their top-k SVM variants consistently improve top-k accuracy over Crammer–Singer and several ranking baselines (SVMPerf recall@k, TopPush, WSABIE), while remaining scalable via SDCA. Code is released.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The convexity of φk and its conjugate domain are well motivated; the dual derivation under a “j-compatibility” condition is careful, and plugging φk into the Prox-SDCA framework is appropriate. The projection algorithm onto ∆k(r) is a substantial technical component, with clear ties to CQKP and novel handling of the x_i ≤ (1/k)⟨1,x⟩ coupling and bias term.
- Empirical support: The experiments indicate consistent gains on top-k metrics across datasets and k; however, details about large-scale results, runtimes, convergence, and memory are mainly deferred to the supplement. Error bars and statistical significance are not reported.
- Missing analysis: No statistical consistency/calibration results are provided for φk with respect to top-k error; beyond being an upper bound tighter than φ̃k, there is no theoretical guarantee that minimizing φk is preferable to existing surrogates.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well organized, with a helpful connection to Usunier’s Lβ and a clear algorithmic pipeline. However, several sections rely heavily on the supplement (proofs, algorithmic details), and there are formatting/numbering glitches and minor typos. The “biased projection” intuition could be improved, and some notation (e.g., repeated use of c = 1 − e_yi) is terse.
- Several references are incomplete (years/pages missing). The correction note regarding [24] would benefit from a precise statement.

Originality
- The top-k hinge φk as the average of the top-k margins, its conjugate and the associated “top-k simplex,” appear novel and elegant; the formulation strictly tightens the Usunier-style φ̃k for intermediate regimes. The projection algorithm onto ∆k(r) under coupling and bias constraints is also new and likely of independent interest.
- While ranking-based surrogates and SVMPerf for recall@k exist, the specific convex surrogate, its dual, and exact efficient optimization via SDCA represent a nontrivial contribution.

Significance
- Top-k metrics are widely used in vision and retrieval; a scalable, convex, tighter surrogate is practically relevant. The proposed projections and the SDCA instantiation could be reused in other structured multiclass problems.
- Using fixed CNN features, the method shows consistent empirical benefits; the practical impact would be higher with stronger baselines (e.g., cross-entropy fine-tuned models) or end-to-end variants, but for linear classifiers on deep features this is still meaningful.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear, principled surrogate tailored to top-k; tighter than prior convex upper bounds.
- Solid dual derivation and integration with Prox-SDCA with a certified stopping rule.
- Novel projection algorithm onto a nonstandard simplex with coupling constraints; potentially reusable.
- Consistent empirical gains and demonstrated scalability; code available.

Cons for acceptance
- No consistency/calibration analysis for top-k; theory stops at convexity/duality.
- Experimental section in main text lacks runtime/convergence comparisons and error bars; large-scale results relegated to supplement.
- Some clarity and citation issues; several proofs/algorithms only sketched.
- Baselines could be strengthened and standardized; fairness of hyperparameter tuning and computational budgets is under-documented.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide (or at least discuss) calibration/consistency of φk for top-k error, or conditions under which minimizing φk is statistically sound; alternatively, give risk bounds or margin-based generalization tailored to top-k.
- Report comprehensive runtime/memory and convergence comparisons (per-epoch cost, epochs to target duality gap) versus Crammer–Singer, SVMPerf recall@k, and standard simplex projections.
- Add ablations: effect of training k versus evaluation k; comparison of φk (α) vs φ̃k (β) across regimes; impact of the biased projection (ρ) term; sensitivity to m and k.
- Clarify the projection algorithm with full pseudocode and a correctness sketch; discuss worst-case vs typical-case complexity empirically.
- Improve clarity (fix numbering/typos), expand intuition for ∆k and the bias, and complete citations (years, venues). Make the correction to [24] precise with a minimal reproducible derivation.
- Consider including softmax-based linear baselines (cross-entropy) and structured surrogates beyond SVMPerf where feasible; if not scalable, explain constraints and provide OVA variants consistently.

Recommendation
Weak accept. The paper presents a well-motivated, technically novel convex surrogate for top-k multiclass classification, a nontrivial optimization component (projection onto the top-k simplex), and a scalable SDCA-based solver with promising empirical results. Strengthening the theoretical guarantees and experimental reporting would further solidify the contribution.
