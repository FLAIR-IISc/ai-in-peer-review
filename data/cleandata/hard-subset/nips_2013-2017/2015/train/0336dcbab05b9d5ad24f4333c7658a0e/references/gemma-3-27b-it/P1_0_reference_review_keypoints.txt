- Proposes a convex relaxation (top-k hinge loss) for top-k multiclass SVMs and optimizes it via Prox-SDCA.
- Introduces and analyzes the dual form and convex conjugate of the top-k hinge loss.
- Provides efficient algorithms for projection onto a new top-k simplex geometric object.
- Demonstrates scalability on large image datasets (Places205, ImageNet) and implements Prox-SDCA with the new projection routines.
- Empirically shows consistent top-k accuracy gains over standard multiclass SVMs and several ranking-based methods.
- Builds on Crammer & Singer (2001) and optimization framework of Shalev-Shwartz & Zhang (2014), situating work among ranking-based losses (e.g., Usunier et al.).
- Correctly acknowledges and compares to relevant existing tools (LibLinear, SVMPerf, TopPush).
- Novelty: explicitly optimizes for top-k performance and introduces the top-k simplex.
- Theoretical soundness: provides rigorous derivations and proofs (in supplement) validating the approach.
- Scalability: combined optimization and projection methods scale to large datasets.
- Empirical strength: includes large-scale experiments with consistent improvements; implementation released for reproducibility.
- Limited discussion on hyperparameter sensitivity, especially impact of k and regularization λ.
- Lacks nuanced theoretical justification for focusing on top-k vs. strict accuracy and when each is preferable.
- Projection algorithm description (top-k cone handling) is complex and relies heavily on lemmas—needs clearer, more intuitive presentation.
- Comparisons omit some more recent state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., Wsabie++ may be outdated).
- Pro-acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically effective, scalable, and reproducible contribution.
- Con-acceptance: needs more hyperparameter analysis, clearer motivation for top-k focus, and simplified projection exposition.
- Overall: solid contribution with strengths outweighing minor weaknesses.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: add detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, expand discussion on when top-k is preferable, clarify projection algorithm (e.g., visual aid), and compare against recent state-of-the-art baselines.
