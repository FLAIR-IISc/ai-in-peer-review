

# Review of "Top-k Multiclass SVM"

## Summary
This paper introduces a top-k multiclass SVM framework that extends the Crammer and Singer multiclass SVM to optimize for top-k classification accuracy rather than top-1 accuracy. The authors propose a convex top-k hinge loss function φ_k(a) that serves as an upper bound on the non-convex top-k zero-one loss. They derive the convex conjugate of this loss, define a top-k simplex, and develop an efficient projection algorithm. The paper also demonstrates the scalability of the approach on several image classification datasets, showing consistent improvements in top-k accuracy.

## Strengths
The paper presents a clear extension of existing multiclass SVM work, with well-structured theoretical derivations. The top-k hinge loss and its convex conjugate are well-motivated, and the connection to ranking-based losses (Usunier et al., 2009) is insightful. The experimental results are promising, showing consistent top-k accuracy improvements across multiple datasets, and the implementation is made available to the community. The paper is well-written overall, with clear mathematical formulations and a logical flow from problem definition to solution.

## Weaknesses

### 1. Notational and Formatting Inconsistencies
- The paper uses inconsistent notation for the top-k simplex. Definition 1 defines it as ∆_k(r), but Proposition 2 refers to it as ∆_k. This should be standardized throughout.
- In Proposition 1, the notation "1y ≠ yi" is used without clear definition. It should be explicitly defined as the indicator function for y ≠ yi.
- The phrase "max{0, (a+ c)[1]}" appears in Proposition 1, but the paper later states "thresholding with 0 is redundant." This is confusing since the threshold is necessary for the definition to be meaningful for all inputs (not just those where (a+c)[1] ≥ 0). The paper should clarify this point.

### 2. Technical Errors
- In Proposition 4, the expression "b = 1‖xi,xi‖ (q\yi + (1− qyi)1)" contains unclear notation. The term "q\yi" is not defined and appears to be a typo. It should be "q without the yi component" or properly defined.
- In Lemma 3, the expression "x*i = 1k+ρk2 ∑k i=1 a[i]" contains a notational error. It should be "x*i = (1/(k + ρk²)) ∑_{j=1}^k a[j]" for i ∈ U.
- The paper claims "φk(a) = max{〈a+ c, λ〉 |λ ∈ ∆k}" in Proposition 2, but the proof is deferred to the supplement. This is acceptable, but the paper should at least provide a brief sketch of the proof in the main text to establish credibility.
- In Section 5.1, the paper states "For the scene recognition datasets, we use the Places 205 CNN [30] and for ILSVRC 2012 we use the Caffe reference model [10]." However, the reference [10] is listed as "Caffe: Convolutional architecture for fast feature embedding, Y. Jia, E. Shelhamer, J. Donahue, S. Karayev, J. Long, R. Girshick, S. Guadarrama, T. Darrell, arXiv preprint arXiv:1408.5093, ," which is an arXiv preprint, not a conference paper. This should be corrected to cite the official Caffe publication if available.

### 3. Missing Clarifications
- The paper claims "ψk(a) ≤ φk(a) ≤ φ1(a) = φ(a)" without proof. While this inequality is likely true, the paper should either provide a brief proof or explicitly state that the proof is in the supplement.
- In Section 2.2, the paper states "the function fk(x) = x[k] returning the k-th largest coordinate is nonconvex for k ≥ 2." This is true, but the paper should cite a reference for this standard result.
- The paper claims "We have repeated the derivation from Section 5.7 in [24] as there is a typo in the optimization problem (20) leading to the conclusion that ayi,i must be 0 at the optimum." However, the paper does not specify what the typo is or why it necessitates the modification. This should be clarified.

### 4. Experimental Section Issues
- The paper reports "systematic increase in top-k accuracy" but does not provide statistical significance tests for these improvements. In ML, such tests are expected to demonstrate that the improvements are not due to random chance.
- The paper references the supplement for scalability results but does not include the necessary details in the main text. This makes it difficult to assess the claims without accessing the supplement.
- The GitHub link "https://github.com/mlapin/libsdca" appears to be a generic library name, not specific to this paper. The authors should provide a more precise reference to their implementation.

### 5. Minor Citation Issues
- The reference for [27] (Usunier et al.) lacks the year of publication. This is a minor formatting issue but should be corrected.
- The ImageNet reference is missing the year of the challenge, which is a standard citation detail.
- The citation [3] for "the sum of the k largest components is known to be convex" refers to "Convex Optimization" by Boyd and Vandenberghe, a book, not a paper. This should be clarified or replaced with a specific paper that proves the result.

## Conclusion
While the paper presents a theoretically sound extension of multiclass SVM to the top-k setting with promising experimental results, it contains several minor technical errors and notational inconsistencies that undermine the paper's precision. The paper's technical claims are not fully verified in the main text, and several key details are deferred to the supplement. The minor but numerous technical issues (particularly in Proposition 4 and Lemma 3) suggest a lack of careful proofreading, which is unacceptable for a top-tier conference like NIPS.

The significance of the work is clear, but the paper does not meet the high standards of technical precision expected at NIPS. The minor flaws are not so severe as to invalidate the main contribution, but they collectively demonstrate a lack of attention to detail that is particularly concerning for a paper that claims to be a "perfect" extension of existing work.

**Recommendation: Reject**
