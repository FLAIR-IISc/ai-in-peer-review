- Proposes a convex, scalable top-k multiclass loss φk = max{0, (1/k) sum of k largest (a+c)} and shows direct top-k surrogate is nonconvex for k ≥ 2.  
- Derives conjugate and identifies the “top-k simplex” ∆k, giving a compact dual: φk*(b) = −⟨c,b⟩ if b ∈ ∆k, +∞ otherwise.  
- Shows φk is a strictly tighter convex upper bound to top-k error than ordered-weighted/average top-k surrogates except at extremes.  
- Optimizes with Prox-SDCA, reducing each dual block update to a biased projection onto ∆k(r).  
- Develops new projection algorithms: a knapsack case when sum constraint is tight and a top-k cone case with thresholds (t,u) and index sets (U,M,L), with an O(d log d + kd) solver.  
- Provides correctness proofs (lemmas, case analysis); main proofs in supplement but main ideas are clearly conveyed.  
- Empirical evaluation on five image datasets using CNN features and linear classifiers shows consistent top-k accuracy gains; code is released.  
- Theoretical development is sound, carefully handles conjugates and j-compatibility, and corrects a subtlety in prior work.  
- Projection algorithm is the central technical contribution and is well-justified; split into cases and conditions are clearly stated.  
- Paper is dense but organized; ∆k geometry and SDCA derivation are clear, though minor notational/formatting issues remain.  
- Recommends adding concise pseudo-code for the projection and more intuition for the top-k cone thresholds and bias ρ for non-experts.  
- Originality: novel top-k hinge surrogate, identification of ∆k, and integration with exact Prox-SDCA updates.  
- Significance: valuable for high-class-count problems; projection routine may have broader use; empirical gains are modest but consistent.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: compare to multinomial logistic (softmax), include at least one large-scale main-text result, report wall-clock and projection runtime, analyze effect of training k vs evaluation k, and discuss calibration/generalization guarantees.  
- Questions for authors: softmax comparisons, sensitivity to λ and training k, whether projection is a bottleneck and possible accelerations, and kernel extension practicality.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance.  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
