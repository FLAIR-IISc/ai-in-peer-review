## Peer Review for "Top-k Multiclass SVM"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel convex relaxation of a top-k multiclass SVM loss function. The core idea is to replace the direct top-k zero-one loss with a convex upper bound – the top-k hinge loss – which is then analyzed for its dual form and optimized using Prox-SDCA. The authors demonstrate scalability and empirical improvements in top-k accuracy on several image classification datasets, showing consistent gains over standard multiclass SVM and comparisons to ranking-based methods. The paper also provides efficient algorithms for projecting onto the top-k simplex, a novel geometric object introduced in this work.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon the multiclass SVM formulation of Crammer and Singer (2001) and leverages the optimization framework of Shalev-Shwartz and Zhang (2014). It positions itself within the broader context of ranking-based loss functions, specifically relating to the work of Usunier et al. (2016).  The authors correctly acknowledge and compare to relevant existing methods such as LibLinear, SVMPerf, and TopPush.  The contribution lies in providing a new convex relaxation tailored for top-k performance, along with a corresponding optimization strategy.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The top-k hinge loss offers a fresh perspective on multiclass classification, explicitly optimizing for performance in retrieving the top-k predictions, which is often more relevant than strict accuracy in applications like information retrieval or recommendation systems. The introduction of the top-k simplex is also a valuable theoretical contribution.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides a rigorous derivation of the dual form of the proposed loss function, along with the necessary convex conjugate. The proofs (available in the supplement) are crucial for establishing the validity of the approach.
* **Scalability:** The use of Prox-SDCA, combined with efficient projection algorithms onto the top-k simplex, demonstrates the scalability of the method to large datasets (Places 205 and ImageNet).
* **Empirical Results:** The experimental results across multiple datasets consistently show improvements in top-k accuracy compared to standard multiclass SVM and related methods. The inclusion of results on large-scale datasets is particularly compelling.
* **Implementation & Release:**  The authors have released their implementation, which facilitates reproducibility and further research.
* **Clear Connection to Existing Work**: The paper explicitly connects to and differentiates itself from related work, providing a comprehensive context.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Discussion of Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper mentions cross-validation for hyperparameters, the details are sparse. A more in-depth analysis of how the choice of `k` and the regularization parameter `λ` impacts performance would be beneficial.  How does performance degrade as `k` increases?
* **Theoretical Justification for Top-k Focus:**  The paper implicitly assumes that optimizing for top-k is inherently desirable. While this is often true, a more nuanced discussion on when and why this is the case would strengthen the paper.  Are there scenarios where strict accuracy is preferable?
* **Projection Algorithm Complexity:** The description of the projection algorithm, particularly the handling of the top-k cone case, is somewhat complex and relies heavily on lemmas. A more intuitive explanation, perhaps with a flowchart, would improve readability.
* **Comparison to Stronger Baselines:** While comparisons to relevant methods are included, some of the baselines (e.g., Wsabie++) appear to be somewhat outdated.  Comparing against more recent state-of-the-art methods, where feasible, would further solidify the contribution.



**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to multiclass classification that addresses a practically important problem (top-k accuracy).
* The empirical results are consistently positive, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The scalability of the approach is demonstrated on large-scale datasets.
* The implementation is publicly available, fostering reproducibility.

**Con:**

* The discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity is limited.
* The theoretical justification for the top-k focus could be more nuanced.
* The projection algorithm description is complex and could benefit from simplification.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of multiclass learning. The proposed top-k hinge loss and the associated optimization framework offer a valuable alternative to traditional approaches, particularly in scenarios where top-k accuracy is paramount.  The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the empirical results are convincing. While the paper has a few minor weaknesses, the strengths significantly outweigh them.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  Provide a more detailed discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and the impact of `k` on performance.
2.  Expand the discussion on the benefits and limitations of optimizing for top-k accuracy, considering scenarios where strict accuracy might be preferred.
3.  Clarify the description of the projection algorithm, potentially with a visual aid or flowchart.
4. Consider adding a comparison with a more recent state-of-the-art baseline.
