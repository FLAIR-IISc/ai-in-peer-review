- Presents a technically sound extension of multiclass SVM to the top-k setting with clear mathematical formulation.
- Derives the Fenchel conjugate for the new top-k hinge loss and proposes a convex relaxation for a non-convex objective.
- Establishes a primal-conjugate pair enabling efficient optimization via Prox-SDCA.
- Provides algorithmic details including projection onto the top-k simplex and complexity discussion.
- Empirical results show consistent top-k accuracy improvements across diverse datasets (e.g., Places205, ImageNet).
- Writing is clear and the presentation makes complex math accessible.
- Builds on prior multiclass SVM and ranking literature, positioning the contribution within existing work.
- Practical relevance for applications where top-k ranking matters (recommendation, retrieval).
- Novelty is somewhat incremental—relaxing loss for top-k is related to existing ranking losses.
- Performance likely sensitive to choice of k and regularization λ; more analysis of parameter sensitivity is needed.
- Projection complexity (O(d log d + k d)) needs clearer practical implications and comparison to existing methods.
- Comparisons to recent state-of-the-art ranking algorithms, especially deep-learning-based methods, are limited.
- Specific clarifications suggested: redundancy comment in Proposition 1, intuition/examples for non-convexity of ψk(a), and sufficiency/necessity in Lemma 4.
- Suggests visualizing the top-k simplex (Definition 1) and providing standard deviations/confidence intervals and computation times for experiments.
- Overall assessment: solid, practically useful contribution; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
