

# Review of "Top-k Multiclass SVM"

## Summary
This paper proposes a top-k extension to the multiclass SVM of Crammer and Singer, introducing a convex top-k hinge loss function that generalizes the standard multiclass SVM. The authors derive a convex conjugate for this loss and show it can be optimized efficiently using Prox-SDCA. The approach is theoretically grounded, with novel contributions including the top-k simplex and efficient projection algorithms. The experimental results demonstrate consistent improvements in top-k accuracy across multiple image classification datasets.

## Strengths
The paper makes a theoretically sound extension to the multiclass SVM framework, with a clear connection to existing work on ranking losses. The top-k hinge loss is well-motivated and the derivation of the conjugate is rigorous. The optimization framework is well-justified, and the experimental results demonstrate clear improvements in top-k accuracy compared to standard multiclass SVM. The implementation details and scalability to large datasets are particularly impressive.

## Weaknesses

### Formatting and Presentation Issues
The paper contains numerous formatting inconsistencies that undermine its professionalism. In Equation (1), the notation "1y 6=yi" should be properly typeset as "1_{y \neq y_i}". Equation (3) has a critical formatting error: "1 k k∑ j=1" should be "1/k \sum_{j=1}^k". In Lemma 3, the expression "k+ρk2" is ambiguous and should be "k + ρk^2" or "k(1 + ρk)". Equation (6) has similar issues with "(k − |U |)2" which should be "(k - |U|)^2".

The paper contains several unexplained references to "the supplement" for proofs that aren't provided in the main text. For instance, "All proofs can be found in the supplement" appears without indication of what the supplement contains. The paper claims to "show in Proposition 3" that the top-k hinge loss is yi-compatible, but Proposition 3 is not included in the provided text.

### Technical Inconsistencies
The paper states that "ψk(a) is unfortunately nonconvex since the function fk(x) = x[k] returning the k-th largest coordinate is nonconvex for k ≥ 2" without providing a proof or reference. This is a critical claim that requires justification. The inequality ψk(a) ≤ φk(a) ≤ φ1(a) is stated without proof, despite being central to the paper's argument.

In Proposition 2, the authors claim "φk(a) = max{〈a+ c, λ〉 |λ ∈ ∆k}", but this representation is not consistent with the earlier definition of φk(a) as a maximum over the k largest components. This disconnect between the primal and dual representations needs clarification.

The paper states "we have a well-defined stopping criterion as we can compute the duality gap (see discussion in [2])" but [2] is not properly cited (it's likely Boyd & Vandenberghe's "Convex Optimization" but this should be explicitly referenced). The exact formula for the duality gap is not provided, which is crucial for reproducibility.

### Missing Information
The paper claims to have "extensive evaluation and comparison of both versions of the top-k hinge loss" but does not present the actual results in the main text. The reference to "Table 1" is made without including the table, making it impossible to assess the claimed improvements.

The implementation details are incomplete. The paper mentions "we release our implementation of the projection procedures and both SDCA solvers as a C++ library" but provides no link to the implementation, making it impossible for others to reproduce the results.

### Minor but Significant Errors
In Section 5.1, the number of training examples for ImageNet 2012 is listed as "n = 1281167" which should be "n = 1,281,167". This is a critical error that affects the credibility of the experimental results.

The paper refers to "Wsabie++ (denoted W++,Q/m)" without explaining these abbreviations, which is confusing for readers unfamiliar with this method.

The paper states "the ground truth score (a+ c)[yi] = 0" but fails to clarify why this is true. The definition of c as "1−eyi" is ambiguous - is this a vector where c_j = 1 - δ_{j,yi}?

## Recommendation
While the paper presents an interesting theoretical extension to multiclass SVMs with potentially useful practical applications, the numerous technical inconsistencies, formatting errors, and missing information significantly undermine its credibility. The paper is technically sound in its core contributions but fails to present them with the rigor expected at NIPS.

The paper would benefit from a complete revision that:
1. Corrects all mathematical formatting errors
2. Provides complete proofs for key claims
3. Includes the missing experimental results
4. Clarifies ambiguous notation
5. Properly cites all references

Given these significant issues that prevent proper evaluation of the paper's contributions, I recommend rejection. The authors have a promising idea, but the current presentation lacks the rigor and completeness required for publication at NIPS.
