Summary
The paper proposes a convex surrogate for top-k multiclass error by replacing the multiclass hinge with a “top-k hinge” φk(a) = max{0, (1/k) sum of the k largest components of a+c}, where a_j = ⟨w_j, x⟩ − ⟨w_y, x⟩ and c = 1 − e_y. The key theoretical device is the “top-k simplex” Δk(r) = {x: ⟨1,x⟩ ≤ r, 0 ≤ x_i ≤ (1/k)⟨1,x⟩}, which arises as the effective domain of the Fenchel conjugate. This yields a primal–dual formulation enabling optimization via Prox-SDCA. A central technical contribution is an algorithm for (biased) Euclidean projection onto Δk(r) (and onto the associated “top-k cone”), which is needed for the dual update. Experiments on several image classification datasets (from small to large scale) show improved top-k accuracy compared to the Crammer-Singer SVM and several ranking-based baselines.

Relation to prior work: The work builds on multiclass SVMs (Crammer & Singer) and on ranking losses (Usunier et al.) and relates to CQKP (continuous quadratic knapsack) literature for projection. The top-k surrogate is connected to Ky Fan norms, and the conjugate is an indicator over Δk. Optimization uses Prox-SDCA (Shalev-Shwartz & Zhang).

Strengths
- Clear motivation: directly targeting top-k accuracy is useful when m is large.
- Elegant convex formulation: φk is a natural and tighter upper bound than averaging hinge losses on the top-k (φ̃k), with a neat support-function view φk(a) = max_{λ∈Δk} ⟨a+c, λ⟩.
- Dual derivation and reduction of updates to structured projections are principled.
- The projection problem is interesting and nontrivial (upper bounds depend on the sum).
- Empirical results suggest consistent top-k gains on several benchmarks; scalability claims are plausible with SDCA.

Weaknesses and concerns
Quality/technical soundness
- The projection algorithm is not fully convincing:
  - Lemma 4 provides only a sufficient condition for zero projection when ρ>0, but the algorithm states “we leave x=0 as the fallback case.” Returning x=0 without necessity breaks optimality; this undermines correctness of the dual updates and consequently convergence guarantees.
  - The exhaustive search over partitions U, M, L with O(kd) iterations requires precomputed partial sums to be O(1) per iteration; this is not stated. Handling of degeneracies and numerical stability is not discussed.
  - The decision rule for whether the sum constraint is active (via λ = t + p/k − ρr ≥ 0) needs a more transparent derivation and a correctness argument (only sketched).
- Convergence guarantees are asserted via Prox-SDCA, but conditions for linear rate or even standard sublinear rates are not established for φk:
  - Lipschitz/strong convexity constants of the loss are not provided; no bound on duality gap decay is stated for this specific loss.
  - No empirical convergence plots or iteration complexities are given.
- The “top-k cone” solution (Lemma 3) gives a system of equations with feasibility constraints; existence/uniqueness and how one avoids cycling across partitions is not established.
- The dual update (Proposition 4) has notational inconsistencies (e.g., q = W^T x_i − ||x_i||^2 a_i; then b uses q\y_i and q_{y_i}); the mapping a\y_i = −x, a_{y_i,i} = ⟨1,x⟩ is unusual and poorly motivated. A step-by-step derivation is needed in the main text (not only in the supplement).
- The claim “φk(a) < φ(a) unless all k largest scores are the same” is asserted without proof; strict inequality can fail in other scenarios if the top-k average equals the max.
- No generalization analysis/top-k calibration is provided, nor any discussion of consistency for top-k risk.

Clarity and presentation
- The manuscript has many formatting and typographical issues that impede readability:
  - Section numbering is broken (.2, .2.1, .3, .1, etc.); figure/caption text is garbled (“/3 1/2 ...”).
  - Footnotes and side remarks are misplaced (“A convex function f : X → R ∪ {±∞}...” appears mid-paragraph).
  - Notation: [1] denotes the largest order statistic, but this is not always restated where used; [m] is defined oddly; bold vs. non-bold vectors are inconsistent; some vectors are undefined at first use.
  - The reference list is incomplete and inconsistent (missing years, truncated author lists, missing venues).
- Important proofs and algorithmic details are deferred to the supplement; the main text lacks enough to reproduce results.

Originality
- The φk surrogate as a Ky Fan-style convex upper bound is natural; relating its conjugate to the top-k simplex is neat. However, connections to existing convex surrogates for top-k (e.g., ordered weighted hinge losses, Lovász-type surrogates, or Ky Fan-based losses) are not deeply compared. The reduction to structured projections is incremental over known SDCA approaches.

Significance
- If correct and stably implemented, the approach could be useful in large-scale settings. However, correctness concerns in the projection step and the lack of rigorous convergence/computational analysis limit immediate impact. Empirical comparisons raise fairness questions:
  - Some baselines are run via one-vs-all due to scalability issues introduced by the authors’ reduction, which may disadvantage them.
  - Hyperparameter tuning details (grid per method, per-k) are sparse; top-10 training evaluated on top-5 should be compared to baselines also optimized for top-5.
  - No variance/error bars; large datasets are relegated to the supplement.

Serious policy issue
- The paper includes a non-anonymized code link (github.com/mlapin/libsdca), which reveals author identity and violates the double-blind policy. This alone is grounds for rejection.

Questions/suggestions for authors
- Provide a correct, complete projection algorithm without heuristic fallbacks, with proofs of optimality and complexity, and clear pseudocode.
- State Lipschitz/strong convexity properties of φk; provide convergence rates or at least empirical convergence plots.
- Clarify the dual update derivation; fix notational inconsistencies and dimension checks (e.g., Hj definition).
- Report complete experimental details, ensure fair baselines (including tuning for the evaluated k), and provide variance estimates.
- Fix sectioning, figure captions, and references; remove non-anonymized links.

Pros
- Well-motivated objective targeting top-k error.
- Clean convex surrogate and conjugate characterization via the top-k simplex.
- Potentially scalable SDCA-based implementation with promising empirical gains.

Cons
- Non-anonymized code link violates policy.
- Projection algorithm contains a correctness-breaking fallback; proofs and details are insufficient.
- Poor writing/formatting; many missing or inconsistent references.
- Limited theoretical assurances and incomplete experimental reporting.

Recommendation: Reject
