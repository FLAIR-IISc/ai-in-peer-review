- Overall: The paper proposes a theoretically sound extension of multiclass SVM to top-k prediction with efficient optimization and shows consistent empirical gains; recommend Accept with minor revisions.  
- Main idea: Introduces a top-k hinge loss as a convex surrogate for a non-convex top-k multiclass loss and optimizes it via Fenchel duality and Prox-SDCA.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Crammer & Singer’s multiclass SVM and Shalev-Shwartz & Zhang’s Prox-SDCA, and connects to ranking losses (Usunier et al.).  
- Theoretical contribution: Rigorous derivation of the Fenchel conjugate addressing non-convexity issues in extending multiclass SVM to top-k.  
- Technical contribution: Defines the top-k simplex (∆k) and provides an efficient projection algorithm onto it, filling a gap where standard simplex projections don’t apply.  
- Practical relevance: Top-k classification is important for applications like image retrieval and recommendation systems.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on multiple datasets show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy, especially on larger datasets.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Paper is well-written and organized, and authors provide a C++ library with Matlab interface.  
- Novelty concern: Core idea is intuitive and straightforward; the work is more an efficient, rigorous implementation than a fundamental new principle.  
- Baselines: Comparisons to strong ranking or multi-label baselines could be improved; some chosen baselines may be suboptimal.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Lacks detailed analysis of sensitivity to k and regularization λ; cross-validation is mentioned but not deeply explored.  
- Minor issues: A few typographical and stylistic errors throughout the manuscript.  
- Section 2.2 comment: More intuitive explanation for why the chosen convex upper bound φk(a) works well is needed.  
- Section 3.2.1 comment: Notation in Proposition 4 is dense; clarify relationship between b and training data xi for readability.  
- Section 4.1 comment: Provide a reference or implementation for the continuous quadratic knapsack algorithm used.  
- Experiments comment: Include Places205 and ImageNet 2012 results in the main paper and analyze trade-offs between top-1 and top-k accuracy (e.g., when top-1 degrades).  
- Acceptance arguments: For — strong theory, consistent empirical gains, good writing, and available implementation; Against — limited core novelty and need for stronger baselines/parameter analysis.  
- Recommendation and required revisions: Accept with minor revisions to (1) better motivate φk, (2) expand parameter sensitivity discussion especially for k, (3) move key supplement results into main text, and (4) proofread for typographical errors.
