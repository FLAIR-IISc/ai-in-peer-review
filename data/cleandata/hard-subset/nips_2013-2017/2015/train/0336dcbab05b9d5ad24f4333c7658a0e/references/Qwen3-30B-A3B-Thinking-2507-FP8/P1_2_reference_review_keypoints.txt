- Summary: Proposes a top-k multiclass SVM optimizing top-k accuracy via a convex top-k hinge loss, derives its conjugate, defines a top-k simplex with an efficient projection algorithm, and demonstrates empirical gains using Prox-SDCA on five image datasets.

- Theoretical contributions: convex conjugate of top-k hinge, introduction of top-k simplex, and projection algorithm with complexity analysis.

- Implementation and experiments: efficient C++/Matlab implementation; scalable to large datasets (including ImageNet) with consistent top-k accuracy improvements.

- Strength: Theoretical soundness with clear, well-supported proofs for lemmas and propositions.

- Strength: Novel technical contribution in the top-k simplex and its efficient projection algorithm.

- Strength: Comprehensive experiments across multiple datasets and comparisons with related methods.

- Strength: Practical implementation and demonstrated scalability for large-scale problems.

- Strength: Clear positioning relative to prior multiclass SVM and ranking-loss literature.

- Weakness: Limited empirical comparison between the two proposed top-k hinge variants (α vs β); experiments only use α.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of trade-offs between top-1 and top-k performance and unexplored reasons for occasional top-1 improvements.

- Weakness: Limited discussion of related recent top-k optimization work (e.g., Li et al. 2013, TopRank/TopPush).

- Weakness: Missing specific theoretical guarantees (e.g., convergence rates) for the proposed optimization beyond applying Prox-SDCA.

- Weakness: No ablation study to quantify the contribution of the loss, simplex, and projection algorithm individually.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-written paper with clear theoretical and practical contributions, suitable for applications prioritizing top-k accuracy.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Specific comment: Clarify and empirically compare the choice between top-k SVMα and top-k SVMβ.

- Specific comment: Expand analysis and explanations for when and why top-k optimization affects top-1 accuracy.

- Specific comment: Strengthen related-work discussion to include recent top-k optimization methods and distinctions from TopPush.

- Specific comment: Provide convergence-rate analysis or theoretical guarantees for the optimization algorithm in this context.

- Specific comment: Discuss practical implications and complexity of kernelizing the top-k SVM for large-scale use.

- Pro (accept): Clear theoretical extension of multiclass SVM to top-k, novel simplex/projection, comprehensive experiments, practical scalable implementation, and strong writing.

- Con (accept): Limited loss-formulation comparison, insufficient trade-off analysis, incomplete related-work coverage, and lack of ablation study.
