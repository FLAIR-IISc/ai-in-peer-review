

# Review of "Top-k Multiclass SVM"

The paper proposes a top-k extension of the Crammer and Singer multiclass SVM, introducing a top-k hinge loss function and its convex conjugate. The authors define a new "top-k simplex" and provide an efficient projection algorithm for it, along with an optimization procedure using Prox-SDCA. While the extension to top-k classification is conceptually reasonable, the paper suffers from numerous technical inconsistencies and presentation issues that significantly undermine its quality.

**Major Technical Flaws:**
1. **Notational Inconsistencies:** The paper contains multiple notational errors throughout that make it difficult to follow. For example, "c , 1−eyi" should be "c = 1 - e_{y_i}" with proper subscript formatting. Similarly, "aj , 〈wj , xi〉" should be "a_j = 〈w_j, x_i〉". These errors appear repeatedly, suggesting a lack of attention to detail expected at NIPS.

2. **Missing Proofs:** Proposition 2 claims φ_k(a) = max{〈a+ c, λ〉 |λ ∈ ∆k} without providing a proof. The paper states "All proofs can be found in the supplement," but NIPS papers should contain all essential proofs. This is particularly problematic as this is claimed to be "the first technical contribution of this work."

3. **Unsubstantiated Claims:** The paper states "the sum of the k largest components is known to be convex [3]" without explaining why this is true or providing a clear reference. Similarly, the claim that "our top-k loss is a strictly better upper bound" lacks a concrete example to illustrate this.

4. **Formatting and Typographical Errors:** The paper contains numerous typos and inconsistent formatting (e.g., "a ∈ Rm be defined componentwise as aj , 〈wj , xi〉" should be "a_j = 〈w_j, x_i〉"). The paper refers to "Figure 2" but does not include the figure, making it impossible to verify claims about the top-k simplex.

**Minor but Significant Issues:**
1. The paper claims "the approach of [11] does not scale" without providing evidence for this claim. The comparison to ranking-based methods like SVMPerf and TopPush is cursory and lacks implementation details.

2. The paper states "We release our implementation" but doesn't provide a link to the code repository, making it impossible for readers to verify the claims about scalability.

3. The paper claims "we have two main goals in the experiments" but doesn't clearly connect these goals to the technical contributions. The experimental results lack statistical significance tests for the reported improvements.

4. Proposition 1 states "Note that thresholding with 0 in φ(a) is actually redundant as (a+ c)[1] ≥ (a+ c)yi = 0" but doesn't explain why this is redundant, which is confusing for readers.

**Strengths:**
- The top-k hinge loss is a natural extension of the multiclass SVM loss.
- The authors provide a novel convex conjugate for the top-k hinge loss.
- The projection algorithm for the top-k simplex could be useful for other problems.

**Overall Assessment:**
While the paper makes a small technical contribution to extending multiclass classification to top-k performance metrics, the numerous technical inconsistencies, missing proofs, and formatting issues significantly undermine its quality. The paper reads as if it was written in a hurry, with multiple instances of careless errors that suggest the authors did not carefully proofread the manuscript. 

The paper would require substantial revision to address these issues before it could be considered for publication at NIPS. As written, the technical contributions are obscured by the numerous presentation flaws, and the paper fails to meet NIPS's standards for technical rigor and clarity.

**Recommendation:** Reject
