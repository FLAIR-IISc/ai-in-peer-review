- Proposes extending multiclass SVM to optimize top-k performance via a novel convex top-k hinge loss.  
- Derives the convex conjugate of the loss rigorously and shows the method recovers Crammer & Singer when k=1.  
- Develops an optimization framework based on Prox-SDCA.  
- Introduces the top-k simplex, a novel convex set, and an efficient projection algorithm of independent interest.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, spanning five datasets from 4.1k to 2.4M examples.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements in top-k accuracy across datasets (e.g., +2.6% top-5 on Caltech 101).  
- Shows the method scales to very large datasets (Places205, ImageNet), making it practically applicable.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear notation, logical derivations, and helpful figures.  
- Supplementary material provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- The top-k hinge loss is a tighter convex upper bound than prior ranking-based approaches (e.g., Usunier et al.).  
- The contribution is both original and significant for large-scale classification where top-k matters.  
- Strengths: solid theoretical foundation, efficient optimization, thorough experiments, and practical relevance.  
- Weaknesses: limited analysis of why optimizing for top-k sometimes improves top-1; comparisons to ranking methods (Usunier et al.) could be deeper; Wsabie++ comparison is restricted on large datasets.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, practically relevant work that merits acceptance (recommend accept).
