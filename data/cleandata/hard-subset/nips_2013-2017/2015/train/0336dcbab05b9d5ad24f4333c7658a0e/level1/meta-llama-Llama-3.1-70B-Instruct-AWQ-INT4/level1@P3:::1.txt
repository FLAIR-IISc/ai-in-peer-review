This paper introduces a novel approach to multiclass classification problems, particularly in image recognition tasks with a large number of classes. The authors propose a top-k multiclass SVM that directly optimizes for top-k performance by using a tight convex upper bound of the top-k error. The method is based on a generalization of the well-known multiclass SVM of Crammer and Singer.

The paper starts by reviewing the multiclass SVM, and then it introduces the top-k hinge loss, a convex upper bound on the top-k error. The authors show that this loss function is a natural extension of the multiclass SVM and that it can be optimized efficiently using the proximal stochastic dual coordinate ascent (Prox-SDCA) framework. The paper also discusses the relation of the top-k hinge loss to ranking-based losses and shows that it is a better upper bound on the actual top-k zero-one loss.

One of the main technical contributions of the paper is the derivation of the conjugate of the proposed loss function, which is used to compute the dual variables update in the Prox-SDCA framework. The authors also propose an efficient algorithm for projecting onto the top-k simplex, which is a crucial step in the optimization process.

The experimental results show that the top-k multiclass SVM consistently outperforms various baselines, including the multiclass SVM of Crammer and Singer, on five image classification datasets. The results also demonstrate the scalability of the method to large datasets with millions of training examples.

* The paper introduces a novel approach to multiclass classification problems that directly optimizes for top-k performance.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the reader is familiar with the multiclass SVM and the Prox-SDCA framework, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
