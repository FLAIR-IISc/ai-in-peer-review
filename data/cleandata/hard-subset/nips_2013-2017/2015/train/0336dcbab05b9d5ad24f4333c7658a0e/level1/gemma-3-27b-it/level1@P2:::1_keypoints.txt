- Proposes a top-k multiclass SVM that directly optimizes top-k accuracy via a convex upper bound and Prox-SDCA optimization.  
- Introduces the “top-k simplex,” derives its convex conjugate, and provides an efficient projection algorithm.  
- Builds on Crammer–Singer multiclass SVM and relates to ranking-based losses; main novelty is the top-k hinge loss and projection method.  
- Experiments on five datasets show consistent top-k accuracy improvements and scalability to large datasets (Places205, ImageNet).  

- Technically rigorous: correct derivations using Fenchel duality and Prox-SDCA.  
- Original: novel top-k simplex and efficient projection algorithm.  
- Clear presentation: well-written, consistent notation, good connections to prior work.  
- Relevant and significant: addresses class ambiguity in large-scale image classification with practical gains.  

- Improvements are modest (often ~1–2%), questioning practical significance given added complexity.  
- Projection algorithm description is terse and runtime O(d log d + k d) may be high; needs deeper analysis and empirical timing.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., λ) not thoroughly explored; lacks comprehensive ablation studies.  
- Comparison to stronger, modern baselines (e.g., ResNet, Inception) is missing.  

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, sound method; consistent gains; scalable; well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest gains; projection efficiency concerns; limited hyperparameter and baseline analysis.  

- Overall: solid contribution with technical novelty but modest empirical gains and methodological questions; reviewer recommends weak accept contingent on addressing weaknesses.
