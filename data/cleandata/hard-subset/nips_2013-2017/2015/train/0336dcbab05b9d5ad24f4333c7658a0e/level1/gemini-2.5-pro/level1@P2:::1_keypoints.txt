- Introduces Top-k Multiclass SVM to directly optimize a surrogate for top-k classification error.  
- Notes direct generalization using the k-th largest score is non-convex and intractable.  
- Proposes a convex top-k hinge loss based on the average of the k largest scores.  
- Derives the Fenchel dual and identifies the dual domain as a new "top-k simplex."  
- Adopts Proximal SDCA for optimization, requiring projection onto the top-k simplex.  
- Develops a novel, efficient projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex (non-trivial).  
- Evaluates against multiclass SVM, one-vs-all, SVMPerf, TopPush on five image datasets.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements in top-k accuracy and scalability to large datasets (Places205, ImageNet).  
- Recovers Crammer–Singer multiclass SVM as the k=1 special case.  
- Relates to Usunier et al.’s ranking framework and shows a tighter surrogate than their variant (top-k SVMβ).  
- Distinguishes from structured-output SVMs by being instance-decomposable and more scalable.  
- Theoretical contributions (Fenchel conjugate, top-k simplex characterization) are sound.  
- The projection algorithm section (Section 4) is very dense and hard to follow without supplements.  
- Main text would benefit from more intuition and a schematic for the partitioning/search strategy in the projection.  
- Original contributions include the top-k hinge loss, the top-k simplex, and the projection method.  
- Significant practical impact by aligning training objective with commonly used top-k evaluation metrics; public implementation provided.  
- Reported empirical gains are consistent but relatively small (≈1–3%).  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with important contributions; reviewer recommends acceptance despite readability issues in the projection section.
