This paper proposes a top-k multiclass SVM, motivated by the ambiguity often present in high-class image classification problems. The core idea is to directly optimize for top-k accuracy using a convex upper bound on the top-k error. The authors also present an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex and demonstrate improvements on five datasets.

This work builds upon the well-established multiclass SVM framework [5] and extends it to handle the top-k setting. The related work section appropriately cites Crammer and Singer’s work as a foundational element. The paper also references and contrasts its approach with ranking-based losses [27], specifically noting connections to LaRank and TopPush [16, 11]. The introduction of the top-k simplex is novel, and the authors draw parallels to the standard simplex. The connection to Usunier et al.’s work on general ranking losses is a valuable inclusion.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The direct optimization of top-k accuracy within an SVM framework is a valuable contribution, particularly the convex relaxation and efficient projection. The top-k simplex is a genuinely interesting theoretical result.
* **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the convex upper bound (the top-k hinge loss) appears mathematically sound, and the subsequent analysis regarding its conjugate function and the top-k simplex is rigorous. The optimization framework utilizing Prox-SDCA is well-established and appropriate. The correction of the typo in [24] is also a positive aspect.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experiments across five datasets demonstrate consistent improvements in top-k accuracy, suggesting the method's effectiveness.  The inclusion of comparison to established baselines (SVMOVA, SVMPerf, TopPush) is crucial.
* **Scalability:** The claim of scalability to large datasets (Places 205, ImageNet) is significant, though the detailed results are relegated to the supplement.
* **Implementation Availability:**  Releasing the implementation is a major plus and will facilitate further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity – Top-k Simplex:** While mathematically defined, the geometric intuition behind the top-k simplex (Figure 2) could benefit from a more detailed explanation. The impact of the upper bound constraint on xi values isn't immediately obvious. A more visual representation might help.
* **Clarity – Projection Algorithm:** The description of the projection algorithm in section 4 is dense and relies heavily on referenced lemmas. The flow of how the algorithm decides between knapsack and cone projection could be clearer.
* **Significance – Marginal Gains:** While the improvements in top-k accuracy are consistent, the magnitude of the gains in some cases (e.g., +1.2% on MIT Indoor 67) seems relatively small. The paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the scenarios where this method provides the most significant advantage.
* **Experimental Details:** Some experimental details are sparse. For example, the exact hyperparameter search space and the specific CNN architecture used for feature extraction could be elaborated upon.
* **Limited Ablation Studies:** The paper could benefit from an ablation study to understand the contribution of each component, particularly the convex relaxation. For example, what happens if the non-convex ψk(a) is used directly (even if optimization is harder)?



**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to top-k multiclass classification.
* The theoretical contributions (top-k hinge loss, top-k simplex) are valuable.
* The experimental results demonstrate consistent improvements over strong baselines.
* The implementation is publicly available, which promotes reproducibility and further research.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* The magnitude of performance gains in some experiments is relatively small.
* The clarity of some sections (projection algorithm, top-k simplex intuition) could be improved.
* The paper lacks a detailed ablation study to justify the design choices.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a solid paper that makes a worthwhile contribution to the field. The technical novelty and experimental validation are strong. While there are some areas where clarity could be improved, these are relatively minor. The consistent performance gains and the availability of the implementation outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend acceptance, with minor revisions to improve clarity and provide more detailed experimental analysis. Specifically, the authors should: 

1.  Expand the explanation of the top-k simplex.
2.  Clarify the flow of the projection algorithm.
3.  Discuss the practical significance of the observed performance gains.
4.  Consider including an ablation study.
5.  Provide more complete experimental details.
