- Proposes a "top-k hinge" surrogate that averages the k largest margin violations to directly upper-bound discrete top-k error and tightens Crammer-Singer for k>1.
- Derives the conjugate and identifies a new feasible set, the "top-k simplex," coupling per-coordinate upper bounds to the sum.
- Develops a Prox-SDCA solver whose dual update reduces to a (biased) Euclidean projection onto the top-k simplex.
- Provides algorithms and structural lemmas for projection (including top-k cone), with worst-case O(d log d + k d) complexity, comparable to classic simplex projection.
- Empirical results on five vision datasets (including Places205 and ILSVRC) show consistent top-k accuracy gains over multiclass SVM and ranking baselines.
- Connects to prior work: Crammer-Singer multiclass SVMs, ranking surrogates (Usunier, Joachims), and SDCA optimization (Shalev-Shwartz & Zhang).
- The surrogate is convex, reduces to Crammer-Singer at k=1, upper-bounds top-k error, and the primal-dual derivation is coherent and corrects a prior SDCA subtlety.
- Prox-SDCA framework is appropriate, provides convergence guarantees and a principled stopping criterion via the duality gap.
- Projection algorithm is a substantive, possibly independent algorithmic contribution with careful case analysis (knapsack vs top-k cone).
- Experiments include implementation details and released code, supporting reproducibility, but lack statistical significance tests and ablations.
- Missing comparisons to multinomial logistic regression (softmax), a common and relevant top-k baseline.
- Paper is well-organized with dense but consistent notation; key proofs and large-scale details are relegated to the supplement.
- The idea of summing the k largest components is known, but integrating it into a multiclass convex surrogate with full primal-dual derivation and solver appears novel.
- The top-k simplex projection is a reusable primitive with practical significance for large-vocabulary recognition.
- Empirical gains are modest (~1–3% absolute top-5) but consistent and scalable to million-example datasets.
- Lacks theoretical statistical guarantees (calibration/consistency) relating the surrogate to Bayes optimal top-k risk.
- Additional experiments suggested: softmax baseline, confidence intervals/significance, ablation on k-mismatch and biased vs unbiased projection, and kernel/nonlinear variants.
- Some key results should be moved from supplement to main text (compact main-table for large datasets, runtime comparisons).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel surrogate and projection primitive, solid duality-based optimization with guarantees, consistent scalable gains, and released code.
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of statistical guarantees, missing softmax baseline and limited ablation/significance analysis, and only moderate improvements with occasional top-1 degradation.
- Overall judgment: strong, principled, and practically relevant paper with novel optimization contributions and solid empirical evidence, deserving acceptance despite some missing analyses.
