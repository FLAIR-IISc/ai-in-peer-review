## Review of Top-k Multiclass SVM

This paper proposes a novel approach to multiclass SVM, directly optimizing for top-k accuracy instead of the standard zero-one loss. The core idea is to formulate a convex upper bound on the top-k error, leading to a tractable optimization problem. A key contribution is the definition and efficient projection onto the “top-k simplex,” a novel geometric object arising in the formulation. The authors demonstrate improvements in top-k accuracy on five datasets compared to baseline methods.

The paper builds upon the well-established multiclass SVM framework [5, 24] and addresses the limitations of standard SVMs in scenarios with high class ambiguity. The motivation for optimizing top-k accuracy is sound, particularly in applications like image classification with numerous classes. The proposed top-k hinge loss provides a convex relaxation of the true top-k error, enabling efficient optimization via Prox-SDCA. The derivation of the dual problem and the efficient projection algorithm are significant technical contributions. The connection to ranking-based losses [27] is also well-articulated.

A strength of the paper is the thorough experimental evaluation. The authors test their method on a diverse set of datasets, including large-scale ones like Places 205 and ImageNet. The comparisons to existing methods, including SVMPerf [11] and TopPush [16], are comprehensive. The release of the implementation as a C++ library is a valuable contribution to the community. 

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity of the projection algorithm, particularly its scaling with the number of classes. While the authors mention a runtime of O(d log(d) + kd), a more precise analysis and comparison to existing simplex projection algorithms would be helpful. Additionally, the impact of the regularization parameter λ on the performance of the top-k SVM could be explored further. The discussion of why top-k optimization sometimes *increases* top-1 accuracy is intriguing but could be expanded.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound approach to top-k multiclass classification.
*   Efficient optimization algorithm with a well-defined projection onto a novel geometric object.
*   Strong empirical results on diverse datasets.
*   Released implementation for reproducibility.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Computational complexity analysis of the projection algorithm could be more detailed.
*   Further exploration of the impact of regularization and the observed top-1 accuracy improvements.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field. The proposed method is well-motivated, technically sound, and empirically validated. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points mentioned above.
