- Introduces the top-k multiclass SVM that directly optimizes top-k accuracy, generalizing Crammer & Singer.
- Proposes the convex "top-k hinge loss" as a tight upper bound on the non-convex top-k zero-one loss by averaging top-k violating scores.
- Derives the Fenchel dual of the regularized objective and characterizes a novel "top-k simplex" (a contraction of the standard simplex).
- Develops a Prox-SDCA optimization framework whose updates require projection onto the top-k simplex.
- Designs a novel, efficient projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex; algorithm is of independent interest.
- Provides rigorous theoretical development (convex conjugate, dual characterization) and a complete formulation.
- Empirically validates the method on five image classification datasets (including ImageNet, Places205), showing consistent top-k accuracy improvements.
- Publicly releases code to support reproducibility and future research.
- Strength: addresses a practical, important problem—direct top-k optimization is more appropriate for ambiguous, large-scale tasks.
- Strength: technically solid and self-contained with significant theoretical and algorithmic contributions.
- Strength: clear writing and logical organization, with a well-explained relation to prior ranking losses (e.g., Usunier et al.).
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation across varied dataset scales and strong baselines.
- Suggestion: Section 4 (projection algorithm) is dense and would benefit from additional geometric intuition or low-D examples illustrating sets U, M, L.
- Suggestion: discuss the top-1 vs top-k trade-off (e.g., slight top-1 drop on MIT Indoor67) and dataset/ k factors that influence it.
- Suggestion: clearly indicate which baseline variants (OVA vs full multiclass reduction) were used per dataset in the results tables.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated formulation; key contributions (top-k simplex, projection algorithm); complete theory+algorithm+experiments; high practical relevance.
- Arguments against acceptance: projection section needs clearer presentation; empirical gains are incremental though expected for mature tasks.
