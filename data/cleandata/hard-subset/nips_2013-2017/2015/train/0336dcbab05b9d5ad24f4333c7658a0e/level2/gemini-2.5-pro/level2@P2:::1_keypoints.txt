- Addresses optimizing classifiers directly for top-k accuracy, important for large-scale multiclass problems with many similar classes.
- Proposes a top-k multiclass SVM that generalizes the Crammer and Singer multiclass SVM.
- Introduces the top-k hinge loss, a novel convex loss that tightly upper-bounds the non-convex top-k zero-one loss.
- Derives the convex conjugate of the loss and identifies a new convex domain called the "top-k simplex."
- Embeds the formulation into the Proximal Stochastic Dual Coordinate Ascent (Prox-SDCA) framework for efficient optimization.
- Develops a novel, efficient algorithm for projection onto the top-k simplex, addressing constraints not solvable by existing CQKP solvers.
- Evaluates the method on five image classification datasets, including Places205 and ImageNet, showing consistent top-k improvements over multiple baselines.
- Shows scalability to large datasets and releases code to benefit the community.
- Strength: Significant and original direction—direct convex optimization for top-k is well-motivated and theoretically elegant.
- Strength: Technical rigor—clear primal-conjugate derivation and a crucial practical projection algorithm; Prox-SDCA is an appropriate choice.
- Strength: Clarity and organization—well-written, logically structured, and clear relation to prior ranking losses.
- Weakness: Needs deeper analysis of the trade-off between optimizing for top-k and potential decreases in top-1 performance (suggests plotting top-1/top-5 vs training k).
- Weakness: Limited discussion on practical differences between the two loss variants (φ_k vs \tilde{φ}_k), since empirical results are often similar.
- Weakness: Projection algorithm has worst-case O(kd) behavior due to exhaustive partition search; paper should comment on practical runtime and possible pruning.
- Overall conclusion: Strong, well-executed contribution across theory, algorithms, and experiments; minor analyses could be deepened—strongly recommended for acceptance.
