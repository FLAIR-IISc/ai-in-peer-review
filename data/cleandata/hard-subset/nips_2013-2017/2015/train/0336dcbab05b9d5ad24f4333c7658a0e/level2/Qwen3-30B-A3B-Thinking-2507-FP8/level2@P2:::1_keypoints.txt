- Paper proposes a top-k multiclass SVM optimizing top-k performance via a convex top-k hinge loss, derives its convex conjugate, and provides an efficient projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex; evaluated on five image datasets with consistent top-k accuracy improvements.

- Strength: Meaningful and relevant extension of multiclass SVM to top-k settings for large-scale image classification.

- Strength: Sound theoretical development with well-justified proofs for the top-k hinge loss and its conjugate.

- Strength: Efficient projection algorithm for the top-k simplex is a valuable technical contribution with broader applicability.

- Strength: Comprehensive experiments showing consistent improvements across multiple datasets, including large-scale ones (Places205, ImageNet2012).

- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly organized.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison with existing top-k methods (e.g., SVMPerf, TopPush) and lack of detailed analysis of differences/improvements.

- Weakness: No statistical significance tests reported, making it hard to judge the meaningfulness of improvements.

- Weakness: No systematic analysis of trade-offs between top-k and top-1 performance despite noting occasional top-1 gains.

- Weakness: Computational cost and runtime/memory trade-offs versus baselines are not adequately discussed.

- Weakness: Sensitivity of performance to choice of k (important hyperparameter) is not explored.

- Suggestion: Provide a more detailed comparison with related top-k methods and clarify distinctions/improvements.

- Suggestion: Include statistical significance tests for reported results.

- Suggestion: Systematically analyze performance variation with k to show practical implications.

- Suggestion: Discuss computational trade-offs, runtime, and memory requirements compared to baselines.

- Suggestion: Investigate why top-k optimization sometimes improves top-1 accuracy.

- Overall: Solid, theoretically grounded contribution that scales to large datasets and is well-suited for NIPS.

- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10; Recommendation: Accept.
