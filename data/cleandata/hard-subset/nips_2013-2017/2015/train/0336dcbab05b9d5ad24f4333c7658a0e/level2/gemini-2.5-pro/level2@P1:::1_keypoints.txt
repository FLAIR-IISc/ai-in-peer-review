- Proposes a novel multiclass SVM that directly optimizes top-k performance by replacing the Crammer–Singer hinge with a tight convex "top-k hinge loss."  
- Derives the Fenchel dual and introduces a new convex domain called the "top-k simplex."  
- Embeds the formulation in Prox-SDCA, requiring efficient coordinate updates for dual variables.  
- Develops a novel, efficient algorithm to project onto the top-k simplex, a non-trivial optimization contribution.  
- Evaluated on five image classification datasets (including Places and ImageNet) and shows consistent top-k accuracy gains over strong baselines.  
- Theoretical derivations and the top-k hinge loss are sound, well-motivated, and presented with mathematical rigor.  
- The custom projection algorithm is complete, efficient, and of independent interest to optimization researchers.  
- Experiments are extensive and support scalability and empirical effectiveness; comparisons include related loss formulations (SVMα vs SVMβ).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and situates contributions relative to prior work.  
- Original contributions include the tighter top-k surrogate, the top-k simplex, and the projection algorithm not addressed by prior methods.  
- Highly significant for real-world large-class problems where top-k accuracy matters; practical and likely to be built upon.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, strong theoretical and algorithmic contributions, strong empirical results, and high clarity.  
- Minor criticisms: experiments use fixed pre-trained CNN features (limited discussion of end-to-end integration) and a deeper empirical analysis of SVMα vs SVMβ would be useful.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
