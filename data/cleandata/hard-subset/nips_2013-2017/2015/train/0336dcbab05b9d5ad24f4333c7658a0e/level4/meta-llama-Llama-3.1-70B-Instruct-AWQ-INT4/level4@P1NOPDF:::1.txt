The paper proposes a novel top-k hinge loss for top-k classification, generalizing the multiclass SVM by utilizing the sum of the k largest components as a convex approximation to the desired top-k loss. An efficient optimization algorithm is also proposed, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of the relationships with previous work. The paper is clearly written, making it easy to follow.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to baseline approaches. However, there are a few areas that would benefit from further attention. Firstly, the authors did not report comparisons with [25, 8] due to the unavailability of implementations. Nevertheless, I am particularly interested in seeing these comparisons, as they would provide valuable insights into the proposed method's performance relative to existing approaches.

Furthermore, I would like to suggest that the authors consider making the code publicly available upon acceptance. This would facilitate the reproduction of the results and enable other researchers to build upon the proposed method.

Overall, the paper presents a neat formulation of top-k multiclass SVM that works well. With a few minor additions, such as the requested comparisons and public code release, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
