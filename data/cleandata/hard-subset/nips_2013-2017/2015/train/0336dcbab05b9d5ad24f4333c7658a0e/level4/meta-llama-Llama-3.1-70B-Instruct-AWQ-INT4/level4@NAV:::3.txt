This paper presents non-trivial material, and its overall writing quality is commendable. The experimental results are also noteworthy.

One potential improvement could be to provide additional motivation for the chosen loss function, offering further insight into its selection.

Additionally, a minor correction is needed on page 3, where "globally optimal leads to" should be revised to simply "globally leads to" by removing the word "optimal." 

Overall, this is a well-crafted paper that tackles an intriguing problem. A key contribution of the paper is its exploration of learning with respect to the top-k loss, where the model generates k predictions, and the loss becomes zero if the true class is among these k predictions.
