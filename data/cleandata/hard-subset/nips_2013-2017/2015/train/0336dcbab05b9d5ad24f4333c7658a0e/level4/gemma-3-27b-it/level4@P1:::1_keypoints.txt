- Proposes a top-k hinge loss for top-k classification that generalizes the multiclass SVM.  
- Uses the sum of the k largest components as a convex approximation to the target top-k loss.  
- Presents an efficient optimization algorithm, including projection onto the top-k simplex.  
- Discusses relationships to prior ranking-based losses and structured SVMs.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-motivated.  
- Experiments on five datasets show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy versus several baselines.  
- Missing comparisons with prior works [25, 8] due to unavailable implementations; reviewer requests their inclusion if possible.  
- Reviewer urges public release of code upon acceptance to ensure reproducibility.  
- Novelty: offers a new convex relaxation for optimizing top-k performance.  
- Technical soundness: solid derivation of primal–conjugate pair and efficient projection methods.  
- Empirical validation: practical effectiveness demonstrated across datasets.  
- Concern: scalability — needs more detailed analysis of computational complexity and memory for very large datasets.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommendation to accept.
