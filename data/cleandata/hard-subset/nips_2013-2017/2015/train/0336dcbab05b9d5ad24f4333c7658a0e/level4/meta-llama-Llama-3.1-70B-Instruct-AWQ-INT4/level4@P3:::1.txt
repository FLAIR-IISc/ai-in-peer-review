This paper proposes a top-k multiclass SVM that generalizes the multiclass SVM of Crammer and Singer. The authors introduce a top-k hinge loss, which is a convex upper bound on the top-k error. This loss function is based on the sum of the k largest components, making it a suitable approximation for the desired top-k loss. The authors also derive an efficient optimization algorithm using the proximal stochastic dual coordinate ascent (Prox-SDCA) framework.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their proposed method. They also discuss relationships with previous work, including the multiclass SVM and ranking-based losses. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to various baselines.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel formulation of top-k multiclass SVM that works well in practice.
* The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method, making it easy to follow.
* The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

Weaknesses:

* The authors do not report comparisons with [25, 8] due to the unavailability of the implementations. It would be interesting to see these comparisons to further validate the proposed method.
* The code is not publicly available, which makes it difficult for others to reproduce the results.

Suggestions for improvement:

* The authors should consider making the code public upon acceptance to facilitate reproducibility and further research.
* It would be beneficial to include comparisons with [25, 8] in the experimental results to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

Overall, the paper presents a neat formulation of top-k multiclass SVM that works well in practice. With some minor improvements, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective formulation of top-k multiclass SVM.

* The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method.
