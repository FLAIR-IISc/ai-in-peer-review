- Proposes a convex top-k hinge loss by summing the k largest hinge losses, extending multiclass SVMs to top-k classification.  
- Convex formulation offers a tractable approximation to the non-convex top-k loss and enables efficient optimization with standard solvers.  
- Establishes theoretical connections to prior top-k learning work (e.g., refs [25, 8]).  
- Empirical results show consistent, strong improvements in top-k accuracy across multiple benchmarks versus baselines.  
- Optimization is implemented efficiently and avoids combinatorial complexity of exact top-k minimization.  
- Ablation studies confirm the convex relaxation’s contribution to stable training and overall performance.  
- Omission of direct comparisons with closely related works [25, 8] weakens the empirical evaluation; add them if feasible.  
- Request that authors commit to releasing code upon publication to ensure reproducibility.  
- Minor clarification needed: motivate the choice of summing top-k losses versus other convex approximations (e.g., log-sum-exp).  
- Minor clarification needed: report computational overhead relative to standard SVMs (per-iteration cost).  
- Minor clarification needed: explicitly state the k values used in experiments (e.g., top-1, top-5).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, experimentally thorough — recommended for acceptance.
