- Addresses the important problem of semantically similar classes in large-scale multiclass classification where standard metrics are suboptimal.  
- Proposes "top-k SVM," a novel loss that incurs cost only if the true class is outside the top-k predictions.  
- Provides a precise mathematical extension of the SVM loss to the top-k setting.  
- Derives an efficient dual formulation that leverages top-k structure for scalable optimization.  
- Optimization complexity is linear in the number of classes, making it feasible for tens of thousands of classes.  
- Empirical results on ImageNet, CIFAR-100, and MNIST show consistent top-k accuracy improvements over strong baselines (e.g., +2.1% top-10 on ImageNet).  
- Maintains competitive training times and demonstrates linear scaling of training time with dataset size.  
- Ablation studies show the necessity of the top-k loss and that standard losses fail to capture semantic similarities.  
- Experimental design is thorough, with multiple datasets, varied k-values, and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Results include statistical details (e.g., standard deviations), improving reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly and rigorously written, and bridges theory and practical implementation.  
- Minor suggestion: more explicit discussion of how the top-k loss captures class confusion or semantic proximity via loss gradients.  
- Overall assessment: principled, scalable, and impactful contribution; strong candidate for acceptance.
