This paper introduces an interesting approach for learning with a top-k loss function. In this framework, the model makes k predictions, and the loss is zero if the true class is present within this set of predictions.

The technical material is non-trivial and the paper is well-written. The proposed method is supported by strong experimental results.

My main suggestion is for the authors to provide a more thorough motivation for the choice of the loss function, as this would further strengthen the paper.

I also spotted a minor typo on page 3: in the phrase "globally optimal leads to," the word "optimal" appears to be redundant and should be removed.

Overall, this is a well-executed paper on an interesting problem.
