The content of this paper is substantive and the writing is clear. The experimental results are strong as well.
My only suggestion is to further justify the choice of the loss function.
Also, I noticed a typo on page 3: "globally optimal leads to" (remove "optimal"). This is a solid paper that tackles an interesting problem. The main idea is to train with a top-k loss, where k predictions are produced and the loss is zero if the true class appears among the k predictions.
