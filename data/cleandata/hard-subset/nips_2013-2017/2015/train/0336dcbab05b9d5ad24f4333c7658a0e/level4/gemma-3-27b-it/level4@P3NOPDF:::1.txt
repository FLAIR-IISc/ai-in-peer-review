## Review of NIPS 2015 Submission [Paper ID]

This paper introduces a novel hinge loss function specifically designed for top-k classification, generalizing the multiclass Support Vector Machine (SVM) framework. The core idea is to approximate the desired top-k loss by utilizing the sum of the k largest components of the standard multiclass hinge loss, resulting in a convex and optimizable objective. The authors present an efficient optimization algorithm for this loss function and provide a thorough discussion of its relationship to existing work. Overall, the paper is clearly written and presents a well-motivated and technically sound contribution. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to a range of established baselines.

**Strengths:**

The primary strength of this work lies in its elegant formulation of a top-k multiclass SVM. The proposed loss function offers a principled approach to addressing the challenges of ranking a subset of classes, and the convex approximation strategy is both clever and practical. The optimization algorithm appears well-designed and contributes to the feasibility of applying this method to real-world datasets. The clarity of the paper is also commendable; the mathematical notation is generally accessible, and the explanations are concise and intuitive. The experimental validation provides convincing evidence of the method’s performance improvements over competitive baselines.

**Weaknesses & Questions:**

While the paper is strong overall, the lack of comparison to specific methods is a notable limitation. Specifically, the authors acknowledge the absence of comparisons with [25] and [8] due to implementation availability. However, these two papers represent relevant and potentially strong baselines for top-k classification, and their omission weakens the claims of superiority. I strongly encourage the authors to address this point. Even if a direct reproduction is not feasible, exploring alternative methods for obtaining results – perhaps through contacting the authors of those papers or exploring publicly available, related implementations – would significantly strengthen the evaluation. A discussion of *why* these methods might perform differently from the proposed approach, even without direct empirical comparison, would also be helpful.

Furthermore, the reproducibility of these results is crucial. To facilitate wider adoption and further research in this area, I request that the authors make their code publicly available upon acceptance of the paper. Providing a readily accessible implementation would be a significant service to the community.

**Minor Points:**

*   Consider briefly discussing the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, especially with respect to the value of *k*. Understanding the scalability of the method to larger values of *k* is important.
*   While the related work section is adequate, expanding it to include more recent advances in ranking losses (beyond those explicitly mentioned) might provide valuable context.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid contribution that proposes a neat and effective formulation for top-k multiclass SVM. The proposed method addresses a relevant problem, offers a sound technical solution, and is supported by promising experimental results. Addressing the concerns regarding the missing baseline comparisons and making the code publicly available would significantly enhance the impact of this work.

**Recommendation:**

Strong Accept (pending resolution of the baseline comparison concern and commitment to code release).
