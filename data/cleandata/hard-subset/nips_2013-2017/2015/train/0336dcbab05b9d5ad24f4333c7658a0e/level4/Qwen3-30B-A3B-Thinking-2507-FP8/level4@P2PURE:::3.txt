

The material in this paper is non-trivial and the paper is well-written, with strong experimental results. The only suggestion I have is to provide further motivation for the choice of the loss function. Additionally, a typo was spotted on page 3: "globally optimal leads to" should be corrected to "globally leads to" (remove "optimal"). This is a nice paper that describes an interesting problem, presenting the idea of learning with respect to the top-k loss, where k predictions are made and the loss is zero if the true class is included in those k predictions.
