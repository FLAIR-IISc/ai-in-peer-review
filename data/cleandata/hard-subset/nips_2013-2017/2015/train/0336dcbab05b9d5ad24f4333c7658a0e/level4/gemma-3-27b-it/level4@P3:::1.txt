This paper introduces a novel approach to multiclass classification by directly optimizing for top-k accuracy, a setting particularly relevant when class discrimination is challenging. The authors propose a top-k hinge loss, a convex relaxation of the ideal top-k loss function, and derive an efficient optimization scheme based on projections onto a novel “top-k simplex.” The work builds upon the well-established multiclass SVM framework [5] and connects to recent advances in ranking-based losses [27].

The paper is generally well-written and clearly organized. The motivation for considering top-k classification is well-articulated, and the mathematical derivations are presented in a concise and understandable manner. The connection to existing work is reasonable, and the authors acknowledge limitations regarding comparisons with certain methods ([25, 8]) due to implementation availability. The experimental results on five datasets demonstrate consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines, including the standard multiclass SVM and related ranking methods. The authors also highlight the scalability of their approach to large datasets like Places 205 and ImageNet 2012 (detailed in the supplement).

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The direct optimization of top-k accuracy is a valuable contribution, especially given the increasing prevalence of large-scale classification problems where obtaining the single best prediction is less crucial than having a reasonably accurate short list of candidates.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed top-k hinge loss is a well-motivated convex relaxation, and the derivation of its conjugate function and the associated optimization algorithm appear sound. The introduction of the top-k simplex is also a clever mathematical construct.
*   **Efficiency:** The authors emphasize the efficient implementation of their algorithm, leveraging existing BLAS libraries and the Prox-SDCA framework [24].
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results across five datasets provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The consistent improvements in top-k accuracy are encouraging.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** While comparisons are made to several relevant baselines, the acknowledged lack of comparison to [25, 8] is a drawback. These methods represent important related work, and including a comparison, even with a caveat regarding implementation effort, would strengthen the paper.
*   **Practical Significance of Improvements:** While statistically significant improvements are reported, the magnitude of the improvements in top-k accuracy, especially on some datasets (e.g., MIT Indoor 67), is relatively small. A more thorough discussion of the practical implications of these improvements would be beneficial. Is the increase in accuracy worth the additional computational cost?
*   **Detailed Ablation Studies:** The paper could benefit from more detailed ablation studies to understand the contribution of different components of the proposed approach. For example, a comparison between the two versions of the top-k hinge loss (3) and (5) across all datasets would be valuable.
*   **Code Availability:** The authors mention releasing the code upon acceptance, which is commendable. Making the code publicly available at the time of publication would significantly enhance the reproducibility and impact of the work.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to top-k multiclass classification.
*   The proposed method demonstrates consistent improvements in top-k accuracy across multiple datasets.
*   The optimization algorithm is efficient and scalable.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of comparison to certain state-of-the-art methods ([25, 8]) is a limitation.
*   The practical significance of the reported improvements is not fully discussed.
*   More detailed ablation studies could provide further insights into the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of multiclass classification. The proposed approach is novel, technically sound, and empirically validated. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not outweigh the strengths of the work. I recommend **acceptance** with the encouragement that the authors address the limitations related to comparisons with [25, 8] and provide a more detailed discussion of the practical significance of their results. Releasing the code publicly upon publication would further enhance the impact of this work.
