In many multiclass classification problems with numerous classes, semantic similarity between classes is common. This paper proposes a top-k SVM that explicitly targets scenarios where loss is incurred only when the ground-truth label falls outside the top k predicted classes (e.g., the ImageNet challenge). The authors extend the loss to a top-k loss and develop an efficient optimization procedure. Experiments on large-scale datasets demonstrate the method’s scalability and show clear improvements over competing algorithms.

The paper is well motivated and easy to follow. It addresses an important practical issue—high similarity among classes in multiclass settings—that arises frequently in real applications. The proposed top-k SVM is designed for such cases, scales to very large datasets, and outperforms compared methods.
