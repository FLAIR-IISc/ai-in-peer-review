This paper presents non-trivial material and is well-written, with strong experimental results. One potential improvement would be to provide additional motivation for the chosen loss function, offering further insight into its selection. A minor correction is also needed on page 3, where the phrase "globally optimal leads to" should be revised to simply "globally leads to" by removing the word "optimal". Overall, this is a nice paper that tackles an interesting problem, introducing the idea of learning with respect to the top-k loss, where the model makes k predictions and incurs zero loss if the true class is among these k predictions.
