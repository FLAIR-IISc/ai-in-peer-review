This paper proposes a novel top-k hinge loss for the task of top-k classification. The method generalizes the standard multiclass SVM by using the sum of the k largest components of the score vector as a convex approximation of the true top-k loss. The authors also present an efficient optimization algorithm for this formulation and thoroughly discuss its relationship to previous work.

The paper is clearly written, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method when compared to the chosen baselines. Overall, this is a neat formulation of a top-k multiclass SVM that performs well.

I have two minor points for the authors:

1.  The authors state that comparisons with the methods in [25, 8] were omitted due to the unavailability of their implementations. However, a comparison with these closely related works would significantly strengthen the paper's experimental evaluation. I would be very interested to see these results if they can be included.

2.  To facilitate reproducibility and encourage future work in this area, will the authors make their code publicly available upon acceptance?
