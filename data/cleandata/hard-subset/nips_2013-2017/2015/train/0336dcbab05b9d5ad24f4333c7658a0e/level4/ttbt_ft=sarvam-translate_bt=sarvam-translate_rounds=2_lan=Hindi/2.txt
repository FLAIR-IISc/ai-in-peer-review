In many multi-class classification problems where the number of classes is very large, semantic similarity between classes can often be observed. This paper proposes an algorithm called Top-K SVM, which is explicitly designed to deal with learning scenarios where you incur a cost only when the ground truth falls outside the proposed top k classes (for example, the ImageNet challenge). After increasing the loss function to a top-k loss, an efficient optimization process is devised. Experiments conducted on large datasets showed the algorithm's scalability and demonstrated clear improvements compared to comparable algorithms.

The paper is clearly motivated and easy to understand. And the problem (homogeneous classes in multi-class learning) they work on is an important issue that frequently occurs in many practical applications. This paper has developed the Top-K SVM algorithm to handle situations in multi-class classification when some classes may be very similar to each other. The algorithm scales to very large datasets and shows improvements over comparable methods.
