This paper proposes a novel top-k hinge loss for the task of top-k classification. The proposed formulation is an elegant generalization of the standard multiclass SVM, utilizing the sum of the k largest score components as a tight convex upper bound on the true (non-convex) top-k loss. The authors develop an efficient optimization algorithm for their objective and provide a clear discussion of the method's relationship to previous work. The paper is well-written, and the experimental results effectively demonstrate the advantages of the proposed approach over the selected baselines.

**Strengths:**

*   The formulation of the top-k multiclass SVM is theoretically sound, intuitive, and represents a valuable contribution to the field.
*   The paper is written with exceptional clarity, making the technical contributions and their motivation easy to understand.
*   The empirical evaluation provides convincing evidence that the proposed method is effective for its stated purpose.

**Points for Clarification and Improvement:**

1.  The authors mention that they did not compare their method against the approaches in [8, 25] due to the unavailability of public implementations. While this is understandable, these references represent significant and highly relevant prior work. A direct empirical comparison would be crucial for comprehensively situating the proposed method within the literature and would substantially strengthen the paper's claims. Could the authors elaborate on the challenges of re-implementing these baselines? Providing such a comparison, even on a subset of the experiments, would be highly beneficial.

2.  To promote reproducibility and facilitate future research that builds upon this work, I strongly encourage the authors to make their implementation publicly available. Would the authors be willing to release their code upon acceptance of the paper?

**Summary:**

This paper introduces a neat, well-motivated, and effective formulation of a top-k multiclass SVM. The work is a solid contribution, and I am leaning towards acceptance. My enthusiasm would be higher if the authors could provide the requested comparisons to key prior work.
