This paper presents non-trivial material and is well-written. The experimental results are also of high quality. 

One potential improvement is to provide additional motivation for the choice of the loss function. A more in-depth discussion on this aspect could enhance the paper's clarity and justify the authors' decisions.

I also noticed a minor typo on page 3, where "globally optimal leads to" should be revised to "globally leads to" by removing the word "optimal".

Overall, this is a nice paper that tackles an interesting problem. The central idea is to learn with respect to the top-k loss, where k predictions are made, and the loss is zero if the true class is included in the k predictions. This concept is well-explored, and the authors' approach is clearly explained.
