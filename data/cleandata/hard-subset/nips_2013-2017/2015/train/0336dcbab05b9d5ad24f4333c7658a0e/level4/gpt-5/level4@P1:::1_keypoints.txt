- Proposes a top-k multiclass SVM that directly optimizes a convex surrogate of the top-k error via a top-k hinge averaging the k largest margins.
- Derives the convex conjugate of the top-k hinge, introducing a “top-k simplex” that generalizes the standard simplex (recovered when k=1).
- Formulates optimization with Prox-SDCA where the main step is a (biased) projection onto the top-k simplex.
- Provides an algorithm for projecting onto the top-k simplex, analyzes its complexity, and shows scalability to large datasets.
- Empirically demonstrates consistent top-k accuracy gains on five image classification benchmarks versus strong baselines.
- Extends Crammer–Singer by replacing the top-1 hinge with a top-k hinge and compares/relates to ranking losses and structured SVM recall@k methods.
- Connects to continuous quadratic knapsack problems and explains why prior simplex projection algorithms are not directly applicable.
- Strength: Offers a clean, well-motivated convex surrogate that directly targets the top-k evaluation metric.
- Strength: Strong theoretical development (conjugate derivations, top-k simplex properties, Fenchel duality compatibility).
- Strength: Efficient, practically useful projection routine that is of independent interest.
- Strength: Clear writing and thorough experimental evaluation, including large-scale scenarios.
- Weakness: Missing empirical comparisons with prior works [25] and [8]; reviewer asks for partial reproductions or a careful discussion.
- Weakness/Question: Requests authors commit to releasing code upon acceptance to improve reproducibility.
- Evaluation summary: Technically sound, clearly presented, original contributions, and likely significant for high-cardinality classification.
- Pros for acceptance: Principled formulation, practical optimization with convergence guarantees, empirical improvements, and clarity.
- Cons for acceptance: Lacks specific comparisons and clarity on code release.
- Overall assessment: Neat, well-executed, and impactful work recommended for acceptance, pending inclusion of requested comparisons and code release.
