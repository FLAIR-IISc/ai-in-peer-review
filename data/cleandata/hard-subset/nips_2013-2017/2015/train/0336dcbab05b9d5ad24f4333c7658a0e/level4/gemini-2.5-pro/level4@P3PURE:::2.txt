This paper addresses multiclass classification problems where a large number of classes exhibit semantic similarity. The authors propose an algorithm, top-k SVM, designed specifically for learning scenarios where no cost is incurred if the ground truth class is among the top-k predictions, a common metric in challenges like ImageNet. The paper extends the standard SVM loss to a top-k loss and formulates an efficient optimization procedure for the new objective.

The paper is clearly motivated and easy to follow. The problem of managing similarity between classes in large-scale multiclass learning is important and arises in many practical applications. By formulating the top-k SVM, the authors provide a principled way to handle this scenario. A key strength of the proposed algorithm is its scalability to large datasets, and the experiments demonstrate clear improvements over the compared methods.
