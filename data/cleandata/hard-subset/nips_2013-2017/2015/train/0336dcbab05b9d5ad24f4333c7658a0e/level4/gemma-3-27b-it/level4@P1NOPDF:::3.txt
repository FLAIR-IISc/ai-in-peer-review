The material presented in this paper is non-trivial, and the paper is well-written overall. The experimental results are also promising. 

My primary suggestion is to provide further motivation for the chosen loss function. While the approach is sound, a more detailed explanation of *why* this particular loss was selected—compared to other potential alternatives—would strengthen the paper. 

I also noted a minor typo on page 3: “globally optimal leads to” should be revised to “globally leads to.”

This is a strong paper that addresses an interesting problem. The core idea – learning with respect to a top-k loss, where k predictions are made and the loss is zero if the true class is included within those k predictions – is compelling. This approach effectively shifts the focus from precise ranking to ensuring the correct class is represented within a reasonable set of predictions. I believe this is a valuable contribution and appreciate the clarity with which it is presented.
