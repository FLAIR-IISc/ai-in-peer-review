

The paper introduces a top-k hinge loss for top-k classification, generalizing multiclass SVM by employing the sum of the k largest losses as a convex approximation to the desired top-k objective. The authors present an efficient optimization algorithm and thoughtfully contextualize their approach relative to prior work. The paper is well-written and the experimental results demonstrate the proposed method's effectiveness against established baselines.  

1. The authors omit comparisons with [25, 8] due to unavailability of implementations. I would value seeing these results to better assess the method's relative performance.  
2. I request that the authors make the code publicly available upon acceptance to facilitate reproducibility and further research.  

This presents a clean formulation of top-k multiclass SVM that delivers strong empirical results.
