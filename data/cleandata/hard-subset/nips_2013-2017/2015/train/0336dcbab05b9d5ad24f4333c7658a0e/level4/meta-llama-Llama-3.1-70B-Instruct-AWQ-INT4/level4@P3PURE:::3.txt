The material in this paper is non-trivial, and the paper is well-written. The experimental results are also of good quality.

The only suggestion I have is to provide further motivation for the choice of the loss function, as this would strengthen the paper's contributions.

Additionally, I noticed a typo on page 3: "globally optimal leads to" should be corrected to "globally leads to" by removing "optimal".

Overall, this is a nice paper that describes an interesting problem. The key idea is to learn with respect to the top-k loss, where k predictions are made and the loss is zero if the true class is included in the k predictions.
