This paper presents a compelling approach to an interesting and non-trivial problem. The writing is clear and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. 

My primary suggestion for improvement concerns the motivation behind the chosen loss function. While the concept of optimizing for top-k accuracy is intuitively appealing, a more detailed justification for this specific loss formulation – perhaps contrasting it with alternative ranking or set-based loss functions – would strengthen the paper. Exploring the theoretical properties or potential advantages of this loss, beyond empirical performance, could be beneficial.

I did identify a minor typographical error on page 3: “globally optimal leads to” should be revised to “globally leads to.”

In summary, this is a well-executed paper that introduces a novel learning framework. The core idea – learning with respect to a top-k loss, where the loss is zero if the true class is present within the top-k predictions – is well-articulated and shows promise. Addressing the motivation for the loss function would further enhance the contribution of this work.
