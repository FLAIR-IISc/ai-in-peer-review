In many multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes, semantic similarity between classes is often present. This paper proposes a novel algorithm, top-k SVM, specifically designed to address learning scenarios where a cost is incurred only when the ground truth label is not within the top *k* predicted classes – a situation common in challenges like ImageNet. The authors extend the standard loss function to a top-k loss and then develop an efficient optimization procedure to solve the resulting problem. Experiments on large-scale datasets demonstrate the algorithm’s scalability and show clear performance improvements over existing methods.

The paper is well-motivated and easy to understand. The addressed problem – leveraging semantic class similarity in multiclass learning – is important and frequently encountered in practical applications. This work formulates the top-k SVM algorithm to effectively handle multiclass classification tasks where similar classes may lead to acceptable, near-miss predictions. The algorithm scales well to very large datasets and exhibits improved performance compared to the baselines.
