The paper introduces a top-k hinge loss for top-k classification. It extends the multiclass SVM by using a convex surrogate based on the sum of the k largest components, providing a principled approximation to the desired top-k error. The authors develop an efficient optimization algorithm and clearly position their method relative to prior work. The paper is well written, and the experimental results show consistent improvements over strong baselines.

Comments:
1. The paper omits comparisons with [25, 8] due to the lack of available implementations. I would still be very interested in seeing these comparisons, even if via reasonable reimplementations or partial evaluations.
2. Could the authors commit to releasing their code upon acceptance? Public code would greatly aid reproducibility and adoption.

Overall, this is a neat and well-motivated formulation of a top-k multiclass SVM that performs well empirically.
