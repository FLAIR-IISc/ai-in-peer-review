- Summary: Proposes a top-k hinge loss summing the k largest score differences as a convex surrogate for top-k multiclass error, generalizing multiclass SVM (k=1) with an efficient optimizer and consistent empirical gains.

- Strength: Clear and well-motivated formulation directly targeting top-k performance and smoothly generalizing multiclass SVM via k.

- Strength: Convex, tractable objective from summing k largest components, appealing both theoretically and practically.

- Strength: Provides an efficient, scalable training algorithm suitable for practical use.

- Strength: Situates the method within prior multiclass SVM surrogates and related top-k objectives with good literature positioning.

- Strength: Empirical results show meaningful improvements over strong baselines across datasets.

- Weakness: Missing direct comparisons with closely related methods [25, 8] due to unavailable implementations, weakening empirical validation.

- Weakness: Unclear whether code and scripts will be released, raising reproducibility concerns.

- Question: If original implementations for [25, 8] are unavailable, can authors reimplement key components for at least one dataset, provide proxy comparisons using reported numbers or controlled ablations, and discuss expected performance differences?

- Question: Will authors commit to releasing code and experiment scripts upon acceptance, specifying platform, dependencies, and implementations for the top-k hinge, gradients/subgradients, and optimizer?

- Minor: Report per-iteration computational complexity scaling with number of classes and k, and include basic timing results.

- Minor: Provide guidance on choosing k and sensitivity to hyperparameters, especially regularization.

- Minor: Clarify how score ties are handled in top-k evaluation and whether ties affect surrogate alignment with the metric.

- Overall: A neat, principled, convex top-k multiclass SVM with efficient optimization and positive empirical results; addressing missing comparisons and committing to code release would substantially strengthen the paper.
