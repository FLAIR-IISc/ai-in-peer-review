This paper tackles an interesting problem: learning with a top-k loss, where the model produces k predictions and incurs zero loss if the true class is among them. The material is non-trivial, the paper is well written, and the experimental results are strong.

My main suggestion is to further motivate the specific choice of loss function. Please explain why this particular loss is preferred over plausible alternatives and provide more intuition for its behavior and benefits.

Minor issue: on page 3, “globally optimal leads to” should read “globally leads to” (remove “optimal”).

Overall, this is a solid and engaging paper.
