- Proposes directly optimizing top-k performance for multiclass SVMs by allowing k predictions and measuring top-k error, motivated for large-class image classification.  
- Introduces a convex upper bound of the top-k error to obtain a tractable optimization problem.  
- Develops an efficient projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex, a standalone technical contribution.  
- Mathematical derivations appear correct and build logically on prior multiclass SVM work (Crammer & Singer; Shalev-Shwartz & Zhang).  
- Connects to ranking-based losses (Usunier et al.), situating the contribution within existing literature.  
- Experimental validation on five datasets shows consistent improvements in top-k accuracy over several baselines.  
- Paper is generally well-written and technically sound.  
- Weakness: insufficient motivation/rationale for the specific top-k hinge loss formulation and lack of comparison to alternative convex relaxations.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational complexity analysis and comparison to existing methods beyond scalability remarks.  
- Minor typo noted: “globally optimal leads to” should read “globally optimal solution.”  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, strong experimental results, and practical contribution of the projection scheme.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommend acceptance.
