This paper addresses multiclass classification problems characterized by a large number of classes that often exhibit semantic similarity. The authors introduce an algorithm, termed top-k SVM, specifically designed for learning scenarios where a penalty is incurred only if the ground-truth class is absent from the top-k predictions (e.g., the ImageNet challenge). This is achieved by extending the conventional SVM loss to a top-k loss, for which an efficient optimization procedure is also developed. Experimental evaluation on large-scale datasets demonstrates the algorithm's scalability and reveals clear performance improvements over the baseline methods.

The motivation for this work is well-articulated, and the paper is presented with clarity. The problem of managing class similarity in large-scale multiclass learning is of significant practical importance and is prevalent in numerous real-world applications. To this end, the paper formulates the top-k SVM algorithm to effectively handle classification settings where a high degree of inter-class similarity exists. The method proves to be scalable to very large datasets and shows superior performance compared to the other algorithms in the evaluation.
