This paper addresses an interesting and important problem: learning with respect to the top-k loss, where the model makes k predictions and incurs zero loss if the true class is among the k predicted labels. The material is non-trivial, the exposition is clear, and the experimental results are strong.

My main suggestion is to further motivate the choice of the loss function. While the proposed objective is reasonable for top-k evaluation, the paper would benefit from a clearer rationale for this specific loss, including a more explicit connection to the problem setting and why this choice is particularly appropriate.
