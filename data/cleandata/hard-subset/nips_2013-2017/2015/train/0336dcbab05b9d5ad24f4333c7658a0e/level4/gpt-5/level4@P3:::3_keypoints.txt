- Introduces a top-k multiclass SVM optimizing a convex "top-k hinge" loss that averages the k largest margin violations and is a tighter convex upper bound than standard multiclass hinge and many Usunier surrogates.  
- Derives the convex conjugate of the loss, defining a novel "top-k simplex" with data-dependent upper bounds.  
- Adapts Prox-SDCA to the top-k loss; dual updates reduce to a (biased) projection onto the top-k simplex.  
- Develops an efficient projection algorithm handling coupled upper bounds, distinguishing knapsack vs cone cases, with complexity comparable to sorting-based simplex projections.  
- Proves convexity and inequalities relating the surrogate to the top-k zero-one loss and prior surrogates; j-compatibility simplifies the dual derivation.  
- Implements rank-1 primal updates for efficiency and provides convergence guarantees via Prox-SDCA.  
- Empirical evaluation on five image datasets (including Places and ImageNet) shows consistent top-k accuracy gains over Crammer–Singer, SVMPerf, TopPush, and Wsabie variants; code released.  
- Experiments lack runtime/memory comparisons, ablations (effect of bias ρ), and systematic guidance on training k vs evaluation k; recommend reporting standard deviations on small datasets.  
- Paper is generally clear; geometric intuition for the top-k simplex is helpful.  
- Suggests better motivation and a small synthetic example comparing φk (average top-k scores) vs φ̃k (average top-k positive parts).  
- Requests a brief explanation of the practical impact and frequency of the biased projection term ρ.  
- Minor typo: "globally optimal leads to" should be fixed; remove stray footnote marker.  
- Originality: top-k hinge, its conjugate/top-k simplex, the coupled-upper-bound projection algorithm, and SDCA adaptation appear novel.  
- Significance: directly optimizing top-k is important for large-class recognition; method is scalable and offers a reusable optimization primitive.  
- Pros: principled tighter surrogate, elegant dual formulation, practical SDCA updates, novel projection algorithm with analysis, strong empirical results and released code.  
- Cons: limited guidance on selecting training k for multiple evaluation ks, incomplete empirical analysis (runtime/memory, cone vs knapsack frequency, ρ ablation), and no softmax baseline comparison.  
- Questions for authors requested: guidance on training k for multiple evaluation ks; sensitivity to λ and ρ and whether ρ>0 changed convergence/accuracy; empirical distribution of knapsack vs cone cases and any worst-case behavior; training time and peak memory vs baselines; softmax baseline results on same features.  
- Recommendation: solid, well-executed paper with theoretical and practical contributions; reviewer leans toward acceptance pending suggested additions.
