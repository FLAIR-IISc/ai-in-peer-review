This paper introduces a top-k hinge loss function designed for top-k classification problems. The proposed approach generalizes the multiclass Support Vector Machine (SVM) by approximating the desired top-k loss using the sum of the k largest components, resulting in a convex and optimizable function. The authors also present an efficient optimization algorithm and provide a thorough discussion of related work. Overall, the paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach when compared to several baseline methods. 

However, a significant omission is the lack of comparison to methods presented in [25, 8]. The authors state this is due to the unavailability of implementations, but comparative results against these works would substantially strengthen the evaluation. I strongly encourage the authors to address this if possible, perhaps by attempting to implement these methods themselves or exploring whether implementations become available prior to the camera-ready deadline.

Furthermore, to facilitate reproducibility and encourage wider adoption, I request that the authors make their code publicly available upon acceptance of the paper. 

In summary, this is a well-formulated and effective approach to top-k multiclass SVM. The clarity of the presentation and positive empirical results make this a worthwhile contribution.
