- Proposes a Top-k Multiclass SVM with a new convex loss that directly optimizes top-k accuracy and an efficient Prox-SDCA-based optimization using a fast projection onto a “top-k simplex.”
- Starts from Crammer–Singer SVM and introduces a tight convex upper bound on the top-k zero-one loss, deriving the Fenchel conjugate and defining the top-k simplex.
- Develops a (biased) projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex/top-k cone that handles nontrivial coupling constraints absent in standard simplex projection.
- Scales to large datasets and shows consistent top-k accuracy gains across five image classification benchmarks; code is released.
- Provides solid dual derivations, yi-compatibility condition, and characterizes the top-k simplex as the conjugate’s effective domain.
- The proposed convex surrogate is claimed tighter than ordered-hinge variants and is instance-decomposable, allowing exact Prox-SDCA optimization.
- Empirical evaluation is strong with reasonable baselines (Crammer–Singer, ranking-based reductions, Wsabie variants).
- Motivation for the specific top-k hinge choice needs strengthening; explain why averaging top k scores is preferable to alternatives and when φk outperforms φ̃k.
- Recommend a small synthetic study to illustrate differences among ψk, φk, and φ̃k to build intuition.
- Need analysis of k-mismatch: how training at one k (e.g., k=10) affects performance at smaller k (e.g., top-5), and guidance on selecting k.
- Clarify computational costs with quantitative runtime plots for the projection (varying d, k) and end-to-end training times versus strong baselines (e.g., LibLinear CS-SVM, one-vs-all).
- Expand discussion of the biased projection (ρ>0): provide intuition for the bias, its impact on solutions, and whether other regularizers change behavior or complexity.
- Fix incomplete/malformed references (years, venues, author lists), including ImageNet and others with missing fields.
- Address minor clarity issues: define the stopping tolerance (relative duality gap left blank) and make explicit how the duality gap is computed in practice.
- Suggest reporting variance over multiple splits and including ablations on the projection routine’s runtime to strengthen empirical claims.
- Clarify hyperparameter choices (regularization λ, selection of k) and the role of averaging over top k scores in the loss design.
- Correct typographical errors (e.g., “globally optimal leads to”) and improve a few presentation points.
- Novelty: the top-k hinge as a tight convex upper bound and the top-k simplex/projection algorithm are novel and technically nontrivial.
- Significance: directly optimizing top-k metrics at scale is valuable for vision tasks; projection algorithm may have broader use.
- Pros summary: principled tighter surrogate, new top-k simplex and efficient projection, exact scalable optimization via Prox-SDCA, consistent empirical gains and code release.
- Cons summary: limited intuition/motivation for surrogate choice, missing analysis of training vs. evaluation k, insufficient quantitative runtime details, and minor presentation issues.
- Overall recommendation: strong, well-executed paper recommended for acceptance after addressing clarity, motivation, and runtime/analysis points.
