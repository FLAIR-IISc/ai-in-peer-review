The paper presents a novel method for multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes, where class ambiguity is common. The authors propose a top-k multiclass SVM, which directly optimizes for top-k performance by generalizing the well-known multiclass SVM. The method is based on a tight convex upper bound of the top-k error, and the authors also propose a fast optimization scheme using an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex.

The paper is well-written and provides a thorough explanation of the proposed method, including the mathematical formulation and the optimization framework. The experimental results on five image classification datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines.

The strengths of the paper include the novelty of the proposed method, the thorough mathematical formulation, and the good experimental results. The authors also provide a clear and concise explanation of the method, making it easy to follow.

However, there are a few weaknesses and suggestions for improvement. One suggestion is to provide more motivation for the choice of the loss function. While the authors explain the reasoning behind the top-k hinge loss, it would be helpful to provide more context and justification for this choice.

Additionally, there is a minor typo on page 3, where it says "globally optimal leads to" - the word "optimal" should be removed.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents an interesting and novel method for multiclass classification problems. With some minor revisions to address the suggestions above, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
