This paper presents a well-written and technically sound approach to an interesting problem: learning with respect to a top-k classification loss. The core idea is to train a model that makes *k* predictions, where the loss is zero if the true class is included within that set of predictions. The contributions are non-trivial, and the claims are supported by strong experimental results.

My primary suggestion is to provide a more detailed motivation for the specific choice of the loss function. While the top-k objective is intuitive, a deeper discussion on why this particular formulation was chosen over other potential surrogate losses or relaxations would further strengthen the paper and provide valuable context for the reader.

On a minor note, I spotted a typo on page 3 in the phrase "globally optimal leads to," where the word "optimal" appears to be redundant.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a valuable contribution. The method is interesting, the execution is solid, and the results are convincing.
