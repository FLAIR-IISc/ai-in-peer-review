- Proposes a novel approach to multi-class classification by training directly with a top-k loss.  
- Core idea: train the model to output k predictions such that the true class is among them.  
- Problem formulation is practically relevant where a small set of plausible options is preferable to a single answer.  
- Technical contributions are non-trivial and sound.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly structured, making methods and theory easy to follow.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and shows convincing improvements over relevant baselines.  
- Main suggestion: strengthen motivation for the specific loss choice by explaining why this functional form was chosen over alternatives.  
- Recommend elaborating on theoretical properties (e.g., convexity, smoothness) and comparing conceptually to other structured-prediction losses.  
- Minor typo: on page 3 the phrase "globally optimal leads to" has a redundant "optimal."  
- Overall assessment: strong paper addressing a relevant problem with a well-executed solution and compelling empirical support.
