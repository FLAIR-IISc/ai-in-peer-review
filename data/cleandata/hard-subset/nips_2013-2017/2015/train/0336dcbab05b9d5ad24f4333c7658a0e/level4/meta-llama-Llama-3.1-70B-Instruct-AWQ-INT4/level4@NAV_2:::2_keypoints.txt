- Proposes top-k SVM to handle scenarios where cost occurs only if ground truth is outside the top k predictions.  
- Extends the loss to a top-k loss and formulates an efficient optimization procedure.  
- Top-k SVM is based on a tight convex upper bound of the top-k error.  
- Optimization relies on an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex (of independent interest).  
- Authors derive the conjugate of the proposed loss and present a primal-dual formulation.  
- Experiments on large-scale datasets (millions of examples) show scalability.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvement in top-k performance over compared methods.  
- Improving top-k may sometimes decrease top-1 accuracy, though top-1 can increase on larger datasets.  
- Paper is clearly motivated, well-written, and easy to follow.  
- Algorithm addresses an important practical multiclass problem and is significant for large-scale settings.  
- Originality is solid though top-k loss ideas are not entirely new.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed time-complexity analysis of the algorithm.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art algorithms.  
- Reported scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
