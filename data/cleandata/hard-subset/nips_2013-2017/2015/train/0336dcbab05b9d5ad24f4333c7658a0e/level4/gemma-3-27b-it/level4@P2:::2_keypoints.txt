- Addresses class ambiguity in multiclass classification by optimizing top-k accuracy rather than zero-one loss.  
- Proposes a top-k Multiclass SVM with a tight convex upper bound on the top-k error.  
- Introduces an efficient optimization scheme including a novel projection onto the top-k simplex.  
- Builds on Crammer and Singer’s multiclass SVM framework, providing a natural extension.  
- Demonstrates scalability and consistent top-k accuracy improvements on five datasets, including Places205 and ImageNet.  
- Well-motivated, clearly written, and technically sound presentation.  
- Top-k hinge loss and top-k simplex projection are original and substantial technical contributions.  
- Needs a more detailed comparison to Usunier et al. [27] to clarify practical advantages.  
- Lacks thorough empirical benchmarking of the projection runtime versus standard simplex projection methods.  
- Does not analyze sensitivity of results to hyperparameters (e.g., λ) beyond cross-validation.  
- Ablation study is limited; impact of individual components and loss variants not fully explored.  
- Strengths (novelty, theory, scalability, empirical gains) outweigh the noted weaknesses.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS 2015.
