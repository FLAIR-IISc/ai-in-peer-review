This paper proposes a novel top-k hinge loss for top-k classification tasks. The method generalizes the standard multiclass SVM by employing the sum of the k largest hinge loss components as a convex surrogate for the ideal, non-convex top-k loss. The authors also present an efficient optimization algorithm for their formulation and clearly situate their contribution by discussing its relationship to previous work. The paper is well-written, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method against the chosen baselines.

My main comments are as follows:

1.  The authors state that they did not compare their method against the approaches in [25, 8] due to the lack of publicly available implementations. While this is understandable, a direct empirical comparison with these highly relevant methods would significantly strengthen the paper's evaluation and provide a more complete picture of its performance.

2.  To promote reproducibility and encourage further research in this area, I would strongly encourage the authors to release their code publicly upon the paper's acceptance.

Overall, this is a solid contribution that presents a principled and elegant formulation of a top-k multiclass SVM, supported by strong empirical results.
