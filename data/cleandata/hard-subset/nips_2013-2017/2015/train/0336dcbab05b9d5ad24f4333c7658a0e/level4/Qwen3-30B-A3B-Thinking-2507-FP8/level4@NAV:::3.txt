

The paper presents non-trivial contributions with excellent exposition. The experimental validation is robust.  
The primary suggestion is to provide deeper justification for the choice of loss function.  
Additionally, a typo appears on page 3: "globally optimal leads to" should omit "optimal".  
This is a strong contribution describing an interesting problem, particularly the use of top-k loss where the loss is zero if the true class is included in the top-k predictions.
