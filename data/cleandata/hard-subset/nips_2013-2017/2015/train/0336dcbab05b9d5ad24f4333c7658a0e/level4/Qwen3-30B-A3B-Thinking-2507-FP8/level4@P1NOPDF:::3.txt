

The paper presents a non-trivial contribution with clear exposition and comprehensive experimental results. The core idea—learning via a top-k loss where the loss is zero if the true class appears among the top-k predictions—is well-motivated and addresses an interesting problem. The overall writing is strong and accessible.

The only minor suggestion is to provide deeper justification for the choice of loss function. While the top-k approach is compelling, elaborating on why this specific loss formulation is preferable to alternatives (e.g., cross-entropy with top-k selection, or other ranking losses) would strengthen the theoretical foundation. A brief discussion of the loss’s properties—such as its relationship to ranking metrics or its behavior under different data distributions—would help contextualize the method’s advantages.

Additionally, a typo appears on page 3: "globally optimal leads to" should read "globally leads to" (remove "optimal"). This correction is straightforward but important for precision. 

Overall, this is a solid submission that advances the understanding of top-k classification. The technical execution is sound, and the experimental validation is thorough. With the minor elaboration on the loss function, the paper would be even more impactful.
