This paper introduces a novel approach to multiclass Support Vector Machine (SVM) learning, directly optimizing for top-k performance instead of the standard zero-one loss. The core idea – allowing k predictions and evaluating based on top-k error – is well-motivated, particularly for image classification tasks with a large number of classes where class ambiguity is common. The authors propose a convex upper bound of the top-k error, leading to a tractable optimization problem, and present an efficient projection scheme onto the top-k simplex, which is a contribution in its own right. Experimental results on five datasets demonstrate consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to several baselines.

The paper is generally well-written and technically sound. The mathematical derivations appear correct, and the proposed optimization framework builds logically upon existing work in multiclass SVMs, specifically referencing Crammer and Singer [5] and Shalev-Shwartz & Zhang [24]. The connection to ranking-based losses, highlighted through the work of Usunier et al. [27], provides helpful context and positions the contribution within a broader research landscape. The extensive experimental validation across diverse datasets strengthens the claims of the paper.

A potential area for strengthening is further motivation for the specific form of the proposed top-k hinge loss (Equation 3). While the authors explain its convexity and its relation to the true top-k error, elaborating on the rationale behind choosing this particular upper bound—perhaps comparing it more directly to alternative convex relaxations—could enhance understanding. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm compared to existing methods, beyond mentioning its scalability.

I did identify a minor typo on page 3: “globally optimal leads to” should be revised to “globally optimal solution”. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The direct optimization of top-k performance in multiclass SVMs is a novel approach.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method is based on a solid theoretical foundation, leveraging convex optimization techniques.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The consistent improvements in top-k accuracy across multiple datasets provide strong empirical support.
*   **Practical Contribution:** The efficient projection scheme onto the top-k simplex is a valuable technical contribution.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Limited Discussion of Loss Function Choice:** The rationale behind the specific formulation of the top-k hinge loss could be further elaborated.
*   **Missing Complexity Analysis:** A more detailed comparison of computational complexity with existing methods would be beneficial.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that addresses an interesting and practically relevant problem. The proposed method is well-motivated, technically sound, and empirically validated. The minor weaknesses mentioned above do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I recommend acceptance.
