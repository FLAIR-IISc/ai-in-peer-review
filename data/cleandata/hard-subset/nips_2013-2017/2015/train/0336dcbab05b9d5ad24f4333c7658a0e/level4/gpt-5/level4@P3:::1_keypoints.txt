- Proposes a top-k multiclass SVM using a top-k hinge loss that averages the k largest margins, a convex surrogate reducing to Crammer–Singer when k=1.
- Derives the convex conjugate and introduces the “top-k simplex” as the effective domain of the conjugate.
- Builds an optimization framework via Fenchel duality and Prox-SDCA requiring projections onto the top-k simplex/cone.
- Presents an efficient, runtime-guaranteed algorithm for biased projections, relating to the continuous quadratic knapsack problem.
- Positions the method against ranking-based surrogates and structured SVMs, claiming a tighter bound than ordered hinge losses.
- Empirical evaluation on five image datasets shows consistent top-k accuracy gains and scalability to millions of examples.
- Clear motivation: top-k metrics are appropriate when class ambiguity is high.
- Sound convex formulation: principled surrogate tighter than prior ordered hinge losses.
- Solid dual derivation: j-compatibility and the corrected dual form are clearly presented.
- Novel projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex/cone with practical runtime guarantees.
- Scalable optimization: Prox-SDCA with verifiable duality-gap stopping and kernelizability.
- Comprehensive empirical study supports claims across diverse scales.
- Missing comparisons to probabilistic top-k models and some recent scalable baselines ([25], [8]).
- No theoretical analysis of calibration/Fisher-consistency for top-k risk.
- Limited guidance on choosing training k relative to evaluation k; practitioners need validation studies.
- Some runtime and projection vs. standard simplex comparisons are deferred to the supplement instead of the main text.
- Reduction-to-binary baselines can be unfavorable at large scale; paper should report one-vs-all variants and ensure tuning parity.
- Minor clarity issues: need an intuitive explanation of top-k simplex geometry and edge cases (k=1, k=m).
- Requests: publicly release code and reproduction scripts, including projection routines and both top-k loss variants.
- Requests: add comparisons/discussion for [25] and [8], provide k-sensitivity validation, discuss calibration/consistency, and move a concise runtime comparison into the main paper.
- Overall assessment: technically solid, original, and practically effective; recommended for acceptance with minor empirical and clarity additions.
