In many multiclass classification problems involving a large number of classes, semantic similarities between classes are commonly observed. This paper proposes an algorithm called top-k SVM, which explicitly addresses the learning scenario where a cost is incurred only when the ground truth is outside the top k proposed classes, such as in the ImageNet challenge. By extending the loss function to top-k loss, the authors formulate an efficient optimization procedure. Experiments on large-scale datasets demonstrate the scalability of the algorithm, with clear improvements observed over the compared algorithms.

The paper is clearly motivated and easy to follow, tackling an important problem that frequently arises in many practical applications: the presence of similar classes in multiclass learning. The authors formulate the top-k SVM algorithm to handle the case in multiclass classification where some classes may be very similar to each other. Notably, the algorithm scales to very large datasets and shows improvements over the compared methods.
