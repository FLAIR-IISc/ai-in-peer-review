

The paper addresses an important practical challenge in multiclass classification with large numbers of classes: the presence of semantically similar classes. It proposes top-k SVM, an algorithm designed for scenarios where a cost is incurred only if the ground truth class is not among the top-k predicted classes (e.g., as in the ImageNet challenge). The authors extend the standard SVM loss to a top-k loss formulation and derive an efficient optimization procedure. Experiments on large-scale datasets demonstrate the algorithm's scalability and clear improvements over competing methods.  

The problem is well-motivated and the paper is clearly written. The focus on handling semantically similar classes—a common issue in real-world applications—is highly relevant. The top-k SVM formulation effectively addresses this challenge, and the experimental results convincingly show its advantages in scalability and performance compared to existing approaches.
