- Novel method: extends multiclass SVM to optimize top-k performance via a convex top-k hinge loss and projection onto a new top-k simplex.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides a tight convex relaxation (φk), derives its convex conjugate, and naturally extends Crammer & Singer’s SVM.  
- Optimization: proposes an efficient Prox-SDCA-based algorithm with clear convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical results: demonstrates consistent top-k accuracy improvements across five image datasets and scales to large datasets (e.g., Places205).  
- Clarity and reproducibility: well-written, clear definitions and visualizations, with implementation (C++/Matlab) and thorough experimental setup.  
- Originality: addresses an important practical problem; the top-k simplex and its projection algorithm are notable technical contributions.  
- Weakness — loss choice: motivation and comparison for choosing φk over alternative bounds (e.g., φ̃k) are insufficiently analyzed.  
- Weakness — trade-offs: lacks a systematic analysis of when top-k optimization helps or harms top-1 accuracy.  
- Weakness — baselines: comparisons to ranking-based methods (SVMPerf, TopPush) are not comprehensive in terms of efficiency, scalability, or theory.  
- Recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to justify loss choice and expand the trade-off discussion.  
- Minor issue: fix the typo on page 3 ("globally optimal leads to" should remove "optimal").
