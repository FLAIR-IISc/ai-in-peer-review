In many multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes, semantic similarity between classes is common. This paper proposes an algorithm called top-k SVM to address learning scenarios where a loss is incurred only when the ground-truth label falls outside the top k predicted classes (e.g., the ImageNet challenge). The authors extend the loss function to a top-k loss and develop an efficient optimization procedure. Experiments on large-scale datasets demonstrate the algorithm’s scalability and show clear improvements over the compared algorithms.

The paper is clearly motivated and easy to follow. The problem of similar classes in multiclass learning is important and frequently encountered in practical applications. The proposed top-k SVM directly handles cases where classes are very similar, scales to very large datasets, and exhibits improvements over competing methods.
