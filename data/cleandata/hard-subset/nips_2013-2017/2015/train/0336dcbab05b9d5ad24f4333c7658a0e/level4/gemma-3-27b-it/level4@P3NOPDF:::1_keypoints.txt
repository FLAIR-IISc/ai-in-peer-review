- Proposes a novel hinge loss for top-k classification that generalizes multiclass SVM and is convex/optimizable.
- Approximates the top-k loss by summing the k largest components of the standard multiclass hinge loss.
- Introduces an efficient optimization algorithm and situates the method relative to existing work.
- Paper is clearly written, mathematically sound, and well-motivated.
- Experimental results show the proposed method outperforms several established baselines.
- Major weakness: lacks comparison to relevant prior methods ([25] and [8]), weakening claims of superiority.
- Recommendation to contact authors or find implementations to include or discuss those omitted baselines.
- Requests authors to release code upon acceptance to ensure reproducibility and adoption.
- Minor: include computational complexity analysis, particularly dependence on k, to clarify scalability.
- Minor: expand related work to cover more recent advances in ranking losses.
- Overall assessment: solid and effective contribution; addressing missing baselines and code release would increase impact.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, pending resolution of baseline comparisons and commitment to code release.
