

The paper presents non-trivial contributions and is well-written. The experimental results are strong and convincingly support the claims.  

The primary suggestion is to provide deeper motivation for the choice of loss function. Additionally, a typo appears on page 3: "globally optimal leads to" should be corrected to "globally leads to" (remove "optimal").  

This is a strong paper that addresses an interesting problem. The core idea—learning with respect to a top-k loss, where k predictions are made and the loss is zero if the true class is included in the top-k predictions—provides a clear and meaningful approach.
