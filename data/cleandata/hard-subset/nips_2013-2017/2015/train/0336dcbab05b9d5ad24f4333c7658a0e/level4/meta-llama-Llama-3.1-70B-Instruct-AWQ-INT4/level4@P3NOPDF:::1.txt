This paper proposes a novel top-k hinge loss for top-k classification, generalizing the multiclass SVM framework. By utilizing the sum of the k largest components as a convex approximation to the desired top-k loss, the authors provide a theoretically sound approach to this problem. The manuscript is clearly written, and the proposed optimization algorithm is efficiently designed. Additionally, the authors engage in a thorough discussion of the relationships between their work and previous studies in the field.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, outperforming baseline approaches in various scenarios. However, there are a few aspects that would further enhance the paper's completeness and impact.

One notable omission is the comparison with the methods proposed in [25] and [8]. Although the authors mention that these comparisons were not feasible due to the unavailability of implementations, it would be highly beneficial to include these comparisons in future work. The inclusion of such results would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's strengths and weaknesses relative to existing approaches.

Furthermore, making the code publicly available upon acceptance would significantly contribute to the reproducibility and accessibility of the research. This would enable other researchers to build upon and extend the work presented in the paper, fostering a more collaborative and progressive research environment.

Overall, the paper presents a neat formulation of top-k multiclass SVM that yields promising results. With a few additional comparisons and the release of the implementation, the work has the potential to make a meaningful impact in the field of machine learning.
