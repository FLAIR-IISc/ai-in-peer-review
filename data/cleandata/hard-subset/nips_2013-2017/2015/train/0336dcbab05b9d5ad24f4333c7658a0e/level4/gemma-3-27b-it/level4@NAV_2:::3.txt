The paper introduces a novel approach to multiclass SVMs by directly optimizing for top-k performance, motivated by the common issue of class ambiguity in large-scale classification problems. This is a well-defined problem and a natural extension of standard multiclass SVMs. The authors propose a tight convex upper bound of the top-k error and a fast optimization scheme leveraging an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex – a contribution of independent interest. The work builds upon existing literature in multiclass SVMs [5, 24] and ranking-based losses [1, 9, 27, 28], and clearly positions itself within this landscape.

The paper’s strengths lie in its technical soundness and the clarity of its presentation. The derivation of the primal-conjugate pair for the top-k hinge loss (Proposition 2) is well-explained, and the connection to existing ranking losses is insightful. The proposed optimization framework, based on Prox-SDCA [24], is appropriate and leverages efficient algorithms. The experimental results on five datasets demonstrate consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines, showcasing the practical effectiveness of the proposed method. The scalability to large datasets like Places 205 and ImageNet (reported in the supplement) is also a notable strength. 

A potential weakness is the limited additional motivation beyond the observation of class ambiguity for using top-k optimization. While the motivation is reasonable, a more detailed discussion of scenarios where top-k performance is particularly crucial (e.g., applications with high cost of false negatives) could strengthen the paper. Additionally, while the authors acknowledge the trade-off between top-k and top-1 accuracy, a more in-depth analysis of this trade-off, perhaps relating it to dataset characteristics, would be valuable. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Technically sound and well-motivated extension of multiclass SVMs.
*   Novel convex upper bound for top-k error and efficient optimization scheme.
*   Demonstrated empirical improvements on multiple datasets, including large-scale ones.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation.
*   Contribution of the top-k simplex and its projection algorithm.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited in-depth discussion of scenarios where top-k optimization is particularly advantageous.
*   Could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the trade-off between top-k and top-1 accuracy.

I noted a minor typo on page 3: "globally optimal leads to" should be "globally optimal". 

Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of multiclass classification. The technical contributions are sound, the experimental results are compelling, and the paper is clearly written. I believe it is a suitable submission for NIPS.
