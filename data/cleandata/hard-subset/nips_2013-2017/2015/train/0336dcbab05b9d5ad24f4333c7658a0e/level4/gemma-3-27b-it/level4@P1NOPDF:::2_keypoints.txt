- Proposes top-k SVM to handle multiclass problems where cost occurs only if the true label is outside the top k predictions.  
- Introduces a top-k loss function and an efficient optimization procedure for scalable training.  
- Experiments on large-scale benchmarks show scalability and consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Problem motivation is strong and the paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- Main strengths: practical relevance, elegant extension of SVM loss, and well-designed optimization.  
- Improvements over baselines are not always large; magnitude of gains can be modest.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of when/top-k SVM performs best (e.g., relation between gains and class semantic similarity).  
- Related work discussion is insufficient—needs deeper comparison to class-hierarchy and label-embedding methods.  
- The choice of k is under-discussed; more guidance and strategies for selecting k in practice are needed.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions.
