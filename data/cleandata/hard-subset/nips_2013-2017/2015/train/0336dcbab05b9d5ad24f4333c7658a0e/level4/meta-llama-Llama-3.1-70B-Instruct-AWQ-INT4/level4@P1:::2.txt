This paper proposes an algorithm called top-k SVM to explicitly deal with the learning scenario where a cost is incurred only when the ground truth is outside the top k proposed classes, which is particularly relevant in applications such as the ImageNet challenge. The authors extend the loss function to top-k loss and formulate an efficient optimization procedure. Experiments on large-scale datasets demonstrate the scalability of the algorithm and show clear improvements over compared algorithms.

The paper is clearly motivated and easy to follow. The problem it addresses, namely similar classes in multiclass learning, is an important one that frequently arises in many practical applications. The authors have formulated the top-k SVM algorithm to handle the case in multiclass classification when some classes might be very similar to each other.

The technical contribution of the paper is sound. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the top-k hinge loss and its relation to ranking-based losses. The optimization framework, based on Fenchel duality and proximal stochastic dual coordinate ascent (Prox-SDCA), is well-established and efficient. The projection onto the top-k simplex is also well-formulated and solved efficiently.

The experimental results are convincing, showing consistent improvements in top-k accuracy over various baselines on multiple datasets. The authors have also demonstrated the scalability of their algorithm to large datasets with millions of training examples.

However, there are a few minor issues that could be improved. The paper could benefit from more discussions on the limitations of the proposed algorithm and potential future work. Additionally, some of the notations and symbols used in the paper could be better explained or defined.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a significant contribution to the field of multiclass classification. The top-k SVM algorithm is a valuable addition to the existing toolbox of machine learning algorithms, and its scalability and effectiveness make it a promising approach for large-scale applications.

Strengths:
