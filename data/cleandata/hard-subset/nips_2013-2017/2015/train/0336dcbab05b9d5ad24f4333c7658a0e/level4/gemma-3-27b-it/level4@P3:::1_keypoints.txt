- Introduces a novel method that directly optimizes top-k accuracy for multiclass classification, addressing scenarios where a short candidate list matters more than a single prediction.  
- Proposes a top-k hinge loss as a convex relaxation of the ideal top-k loss and derives its conjugate and optimization algorithm.  
- Introduces the “top-k simplex” and an efficient projection-based optimization scheme.  
- Builds on multiclass SVM literature and relates to recent ranking-based losses.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and mathematically concise.  
- Empirical results on five datasets show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy, including scalability to large datasets (Places205, ImageNet) detailed in the supplement.  
- Efficient implementation leveraging BLAS and Prox-SDCA is demonstrated.  
- Lacks comparison to important state-of-the-art methods ([25, 8]) due to implementation availability, which weakens empirical claims.  
- Magnitude of improvements is sometimes small (e.g., MIT Indoor 67); practical significance and cost-benefit discussion are limited.  
- More detailed ablation studies are needed (e.g., comparing the two top-k hinge loss variants across datasets).  
- Authors plan to release code upon acceptance, but immediate public release would improve reproducibility and impact.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, novel, and technically sound work with empirical validation; recommend acceptance if authors address missing comparisons, practical significance discussion, and release code.
