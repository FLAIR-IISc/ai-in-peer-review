- Proposes a novel top-k multiclass SVM that directly optimizes top-k performance using a convex upper bound of the top-k error.  
- Introduces the top-k hinge loss based on the sum of the k largest score components as a convex surrogate for top-k error.  
- Provides a primal–conjugate pair showing the top-k hinge loss is a natural extension of multiclass SVM.  
- Develops a fast optimization scheme relying on an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex.  
- Experiments on five image classification datasets show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy over multiple baselines.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Suggestion: further motivate the choice of the top-k hinge loss and discuss its advantages and disadvantages versus other possible losses.  
- Minor issue: typo on page 3 — "globally optimal leads to" should read "globally optimal solution is computationally intractable."  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective approach with strong experimental support and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient motivation for the loss choice and the noted typo; otherwise minor revisions recommended.
