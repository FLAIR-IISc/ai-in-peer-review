

The paper addresses a common challenge in multiclass classification with large numbers of classes: the presence of semantic similarity between classes. It proposes top-k SVM, an algorithm designed for scenarios where a cost is incurred only when the ground truth class lies outside the top-k predicted classes (e.g., as in the ImageNet challenge). The authors extend the standard loss function to a top-k loss and develop an efficient optimization procedure. Experiments on large-scale datasets demonstrate the algorithm's scalability and significant improvements over competing methods. The paper is clearly motivated and well-structured, with a compelling focus on an important practical problem—handling semantically similar classes in multiclass settings. The top-k SVM formulation effectively addresses this challenge, scales to very large datasets, and consistently outperforms baseline approaches.
