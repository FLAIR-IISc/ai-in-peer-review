The material in this paper is non-trivial and the paper is well-written. The experimental results are also promising.

This paper addresses the problem of class ambiguity in image classification by proposing a top-k multiclass SVM, directly optimizing for top-k accuracy instead of the standard zero-one loss. The core idea – allowing k guesses and evaluating based on the top-k error – is a sensible relaxation for challenging classification tasks. The authors build upon the existing multiclass SVM framework of Crammer and Singer [5] and develop a convex upper bound for the top-k error, which is crucial for efficient optimization. A notable contribution is the derivation of an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex, a geometric object of independent interest. The experiments on five datasets demonstrate consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to several baselines.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a technically sound approach, building upon established SVM theory and providing a rigorous derivation of the top-k hinge loss and its conjugate. The proposed optimization scheme appears well-motivated and efficient.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written, making the technical details accessible. The notation is consistent and the key ideas are explained thoroughly.
*   **Originality:** The problem of directly optimizing for top-k accuracy in multiclass SVM is novel. While related work exists on ranking-based losses [27], the proposed approach offers a unique formulation and efficient optimization strategy. The introduction of the top-k simplex is also a noteworthy contribution.
*   **Significance:** The results are significant, demonstrating consistent improvements in top-k accuracy on multiple datasets. This work has the potential to be valuable for applications where obtaining a single, highly confident prediction is less important than having a set of plausible candidates.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Motivation of Loss Function:** While the intuition behind the top-k loss is clear, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of why this specific convex upper bound (equation 3) is chosen over other potential alternatives, such as the one presented in [27] (equation 5). A more thorough comparison of the properties of these different loss functions would strengthen the paper.
*   **Typo:** A minor typo exists on page 3: "globally optimal leads to" should be "globally optimal".

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a relevant problem.
*   The proposed method demonstrates consistent improvements in performance on multiple datasets.
*   The derivations and optimization techniques are well-motivated and clearly explained.
*   The introduction of the top-k simplex is a valuable theoretical contribution.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The motivation for choosing the specific convex upper bound for the top-k loss could be strengthened.
*   The experimental evaluation, while thorough, could be expanded to include comparisons with more recent state-of-the-art methods.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that addresses an interesting and important problem with a technically sound and well-motivated approach. The experimental results are promising and the contributions are significant. Despite the minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh the concerns. I recommend acceptance.
