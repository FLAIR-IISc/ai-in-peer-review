The material in this paper is non-trivial, and the paper is well-written. The experimental results are also good.

The only suggestion I have is to further motivate the choice of the loss function. Providing a more in-depth explanation for this choice would strengthen the paper.

Additionally, I spotted a typo on page 3: "globally optimal leads to" should be revised to simply "globally leads to" by removing "optimal." 

This is a nice paper that describes an interesting problem. The idea of learning with respect to the top-k loss is intriguing, where k predictions are made and the loss is zero if the true class is included in the k predictions.
