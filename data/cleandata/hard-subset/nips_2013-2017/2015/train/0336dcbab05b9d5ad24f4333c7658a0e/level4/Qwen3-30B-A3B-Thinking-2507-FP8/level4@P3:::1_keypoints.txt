- Proposes a top-k multiclass SVM that directly optimizes top-k classification via a new convex top-k hinge loss.  
- Top-k hinge loss uses the sum of the k largest score components as a convex approximation to top-k error.  
- Derives the convex conjugate of the loss (Proposition 2) and shows it reduces to standard multiclass SVM when k=1.  
- Develops an efficient Prox-SDCA optimization algorithm with a novel projection onto the top-k simplex.  
- Projection algorithm has runtime O(d log d + k d), comparable to standard simplex projection and scalable to large datasets.  
- Empirical results show consistent top-k accuracy improvements across five image-classification datasets, including Places205 and ImageNet 2012.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- The top-k hinge loss and projection algorithm are original contributions and of independent interest.  
- The work is technically sound with a clear theoretical foundation and practical significance for ambiguous large-scale classification.  
- Major weakness: missing comparisons with Wsabie [25] and related methods [8], which are standard baselines for top-k evaluation.  
- Minor weakness: limited theoretical analysis of generalization properties for the top-k loss.  
- Recommendation: accept (strong paper) but add comparisons with Wsabie/related methods in revision to strengthen evaluation.
