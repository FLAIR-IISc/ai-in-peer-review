This work introduces a top-k hinge loss for the task of top-k classification, which serves as a generalization of the multiclass Support Vector Machine. The formulation leverages the sum of the k largest components as a convex surrogate for the desired top-k loss. The paper contributes an efficient optimization algorithm and provides a clear discussion of its relationship to prior art. The manuscript is well-written, and the experimental evaluation validates the proposed method's effectiveness against established baselines.

1.  The paper omits a comparison to the methods in [25, 8], citing the unavailability of their implementations. An empirical comparison against these closely related works would significantly strengthen the evaluation, and I would be very interested in seeing it.
2.  To facilitate reproducibility, will the authors commit to releasing their code and models publicly upon acceptance?

This is a solid contribution that presents an elegant and effective formulation for a top-k multiclass SVM.
