- Proposes a direct top-k hinge loss: a tight, convex surrogate for top-k error that generalizes Crammer & Singer and derives its Fenchel dual introducing the "top-k simplex".
- Develops a practical Prox-SDCA optimization scheme that requires and uses a novel, efficient projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex.
- The projection onto the top-k simplex is non-trivial, not a standard CQKP, and the proposed algorithm is a significant independent contribution.
- Empirical validation on five image datasets shows consistent and scalable improvements in top-k accuracy versus baselines.
- Reported gains are meaningful (e.g., +2.5% top-5 on SUN397), and optimizing top-k can sometimes also improve top-1 accuracy.
- Paper is well-written, technically detailed, situates itself relative to prior work, and promises to release a C++/Matlab implementation for reproducibility.
- Theoretical contribution: φk is proven to be a strictly tighter bound on top-k error than the related φ̃k from prior ranking-loss frameworks.
- Optimization contribution: choice of Prox-SDCA is well-justified and the dual update requiring top-k simplex projection is handled efficiently.
- Empirical contribution: method scales to large datasets (Places205, ImageNet) and shows consistent gains across settings.
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis of the top-1 vs top-k trade-off; a visualization of performance as k varies would be informative.
- Weakness: despite φk being theoretically tighter, SVMα and SVMβ often perform similarly empirically—needs discussion on why.
- Weakness: experiments use fixed pre-trained CNN features; paper should discuss comparison to end-to-end deep training and whether the loss can be adapted for neural nets.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — high-quality, novel, theoretically sound, and practically effective work.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel top-k SVM generalization, top-k simplex theory, new projection algorithm, strong scalable results, clear writing and code release.
- Arguments against acceptance: no major objections; only minor suggested analyses and discussions.
