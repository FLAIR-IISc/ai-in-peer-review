

This paper proposes a novel extension of multiclass SVM to optimize directly for top-k classification performance, which is particularly relevant for image classification problems with large numbers of classes where class ambiguity is common. The authors build upon Crammer and Singer's multiclass SVM [5] and extend the framework through a tight convex upper bound of the top-k error, introducing the top-k hinge loss. This work relates to Usunier et al.'s general framework for ranking-based losses [27], but provides a more direct approach to the top-k problem by designing a convex upper bound specifically for the multiclass setting. The paper also makes a significant technical contribution by deriving an efficient projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex, which is a novel extension of the standard simplex.

Quality: The paper is technically sound with a clear theoretical derivation of the top-k hinge loss and its convex conjugate. The optimization scheme based on Prox-SDCA is well-motivated, and the experimental results on five diverse image classification datasets consistently demonstrate improvements in top-k accuracy compared to multiple baselines. The authors carefully evaluate both strengths and weaknesses, noting that top-k optimization might slightly decrease top-1 accuracy on some datasets while improving it on others. The only minor technical weakness is the insufficient motivation for choosing φk(a) over φ̃k(a), the alternative top-k hinge loss formulation.

Clarity: The paper is exceptionally well-written with clear notation, logical flow, and helpful visualizations (e.g., Figure 2 illustrating the top-k simplex). The experimental setup and results are presented with sufficient detail to allow reproducibility. The only minor issue is a typo on page 3: "globally optimal leads to" should be "globally leads to" (remove 'optimal').

Originality: The paper makes a significant original contribution by directly optimizing for top-k performance rather than using indirect approaches like ranking-based methods. While related to previous work on ranking losses [27], the authors provide a more direct convex upper bound for the top-k error that is specifically designed for the multiclass classification setting. The extension of the standard simplex to the top-k simplex represents a novel technical contribution.

Significance: The top-k loss is highly significant for large-scale image classification where class ambiguity is prevalent. The consistent improvements across five diverse datasets (Caltech 101 Silhouettes, MIT Indoor 67, SUN 397, Places 205, and ImageNet 2012) demonstrate the practical value of this approach. The results suggest that optimizing for top-k performance is often more appropriate for large-class problems than standard zero-one loss, making this work particularly relevant for real-world applications.

Pro:
- Novel extension of multiclass SVM to top-k setting with direct optimization
- Theoretical justification for the top-k hinge loss and its convex conjugate
- Efficient optimization scheme with strong empirical results across multiple datasets
- Clear and well-structured presentation of technical contributions
- Practical significance for large-scale image classification

Con:
- Insufficient motivation for choosing φk(a) over φ̃k(a) (the alternative top-k hinge loss)
- Minor typo in manuscript (page 3, "globally optimal leads to" should be "globally leads to")

This is a strong, well-executed paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the field. The technical content is sound, the writing is clear, and the results are significant for large-scale classification tasks. The minor weakness in motivation for the loss function choice is easily addressable and does not detract from the overall quality of the work. I recommend acceptance.
