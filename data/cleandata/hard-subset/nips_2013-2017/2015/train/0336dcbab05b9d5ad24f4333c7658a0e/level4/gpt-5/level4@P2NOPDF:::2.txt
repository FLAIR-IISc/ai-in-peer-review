Many multiclass classification tasks with large label spaces exhibit semantic overlap among classes. This paper introduces top-k SVM, an algorithm tailored to scenarios where a prediction incurs loss only if the true label is not among the top k predicted classes (e.g., the ImageNet challenge). The authors extend the conventional multiclass loss to a top-k loss and develop an efficient optimization procedure. Experiments on large-scale datasets demonstrate strong scalability and consistent improvements over competitive baselines.

The paper is well motivated and easy to follow. The addressed problem—similar or confusable classes in multiclass learning—is important and common in practice. By directly optimizing a top-k objective, the method effectively accounts for near-miss predictions among similar classes and better aligns training with evaluation metrics used in real-world applications. The algorithm scales to very large datasets and yields clear, measurable gains compared with alternative approaches.
