- Proposes a novel top-k hinge loss for multiclass classification that directly optimizes top-k performance by convexly upper-bounding the non-convex top-k error.  
- Generalizes the Crammer–Singer multiclass SVM by averaging the k largest scores of incorrect classes to form the loss.  
- Develops a fast, scalable optimizer using the Proximal Stochastic Dual Coordinate Ascent (Prox-SDCA) framework.  
- Introduces an efficient algorithm for Euclidean projection onto the "top-k simplex," a key technical contribution for the dual formulation.  
- Argues the proposed loss gives a tighter bound on top-k error compared to prior ranking-based losses.  
- Presents thorough empirical validation on five image classification datasets (including Places205 and ImageNet) showing consistent top-k accuracy improvements over baselines.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with technical details and related-work connections well explained.  
- Missing empirical comparisons to methods in [25] and [8]; reviewer requests a conceptual discussion of differences if implementations are unavailable.  
- Asks whether the authors will release their implementation to aid reproducibility and adoption.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, theoretically sound, and effective work that advances multiclass SVMs for top-k metrics.
