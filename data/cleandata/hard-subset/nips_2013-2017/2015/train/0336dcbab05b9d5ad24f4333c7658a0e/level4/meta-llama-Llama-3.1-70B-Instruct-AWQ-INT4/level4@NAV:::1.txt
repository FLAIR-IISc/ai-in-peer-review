The paper introduces a novel top-k hinge loss formulation for top-k classification tasks, effectively generalizing the multiclass Support Vector Machine (SVM) framework. By leveraging the sum of the k largest components as a convex surrogate for the desired top-k loss, the authors devise a computationally efficient optimization algorithm. The manuscript is well-organized and clearly written, facilitating easy comprehension. The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the proposed method's superiority over existing baselines.

However, there are a couple of aspects that warrant further attention. Firstly, it would be beneficial to include a comparison with the approaches outlined in [25, 8], as this would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's performance. Although the authors mention that the implementations were not readily available, attempting to replicate or acquire these implementations could significantly enhance the paper's impact.

Furthermore, making the code publicly available upon acceptance would greatly facilitate the reproducibility of the results and enable the community to build upon this work more effectively.

Overall, the paper presents a concise and elegant formulation of top-k multiclass SVM, yielding promising results.
