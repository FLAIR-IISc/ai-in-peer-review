- Addresses large-scale multiclass problems where top-k error is a more appropriate metric than top-1, proposing a method to directly optimize top-k performance.
- Main contribution: a novel convex "top-k hinge loss" that tightly upper-bounds the non-convex top-k 0-1 loss and generalizes Crammer and Singer SVM (k=1).
- Optimization via Proximal Stochastic Dual Coordinate Ascent (Prox-SDCA), yielding a scalable training scheme.
- Technical contribution: an efficient algorithm for projection onto the "top-k simplex" required by the dual updates.
- Strong empirical validation on five image datasets, including Places205 and ImageNet, showing consistent top-k accuracy improvements and scalability.
- Well-motivated, technically solid, and clearly written paper that situates itself properly within prior work (Crammer & Singer, ranking losses).
- Experimental results convincingly support claims across diverse scales and tasks.
- Minor weakness: limited analysis comparing the tighter top-k hinge (φk) to the simpler ranking-derived variant (φ̃k); more characterization of when φk yields significant practical gains would help.
- Overall recommendation: excellent work with significant theoretical and practical contributions; strongly recommended for acceptance.
