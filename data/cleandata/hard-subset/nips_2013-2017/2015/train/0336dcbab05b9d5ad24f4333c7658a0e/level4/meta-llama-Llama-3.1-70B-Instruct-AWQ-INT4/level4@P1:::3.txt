The paper proposes a novel approach to multiclass classification by directly optimizing for the top-k performance using a convex upper bound of the top-k error. The authors extend the well-known multiclass SVM to the top-k multiclass SVM and provide a fast optimization scheme based on an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex. The experimental results show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines on five image classification datasets.

The main idea of the paper is to relax the penalty for making an error in the top-k predictions by introducing a new loss function, called the top-k hinge loss. This loss function is a convex upper bound on the top-k error and is based on the sum of the k largest components of the score vector. The authors also provide a primal-conjugate pair for the top-k hinge loss and show that it is a natural extension of the multiclass SVM.

The paper is well-written, and the experimental results are good. However, I have a few suggestions to improve the paper. Firstly, I think it would be helpful to motivate the choice of the loss function further. While the authors provide some intuition about why the top-k hinge loss is a good choice, it would be useful to have a more detailed discussion about the advantages and disadvantages of this loss function compared to other possible choices.

Additionally, I spotted a typo on page 3 - "globally optimal leads to" should be "globally optimal solution is computationally intractable".

Overall, this is a nice paper that describes an interesting problem and proposes a novel solution. The idea of learning with respect to the top-k loss is a good one, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to multiclass classification that directly optimizes for the top-k performance.
* The authors provide a fast optimization scheme based on an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex.
* The experimental results show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines.

Weaknesses:

* The choice of the loss function could be motivated further.
* There is a typo on page 3.
