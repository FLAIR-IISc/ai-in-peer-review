

This paper presents a meaningful contribution with a well-written exposition and strong experimental results. The core idea of learning with a top-k loss—where the model predicts k classes and incurs zero loss if the true class is among these predictions—is both novel and well-motivated by the problem context. The approach effectively addresses challenges in scenarios where partial correctness is acceptable or preferable to strict single-class prediction.

The primary suggestion for improvement concerns the motivation for the chosen loss function. While the top-k loss is clearly defined, the authors could strengthen their argument by more explicitly connecting the loss formulation to the specific characteristics of the problem domain (e.g., why top-k is superior to alternatives like cross-entropy or hinge loss for this task, or how the value of k is derived from empirical observations). A brief discussion of the theoretical or practical rationale behind the loss choice would significantly enhance the paper’s clarity and impact.

Additionally, a minor typo requires correction: on page 3, "globally optimal leads to" should read "globally leads to" (the word "optimal" is redundant and should be removed).

Overall, this is a solid submission that tackles an interesting problem with a clean methodology and convincing results. The clarity of the writing and the quality of the experiments make it a strong candidate for acceptance. Addressing the minor points above would further elevate the paper.
