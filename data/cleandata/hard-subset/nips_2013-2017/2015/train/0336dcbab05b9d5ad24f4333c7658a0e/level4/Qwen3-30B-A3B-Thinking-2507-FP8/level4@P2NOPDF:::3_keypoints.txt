- Paper presents a meaningful contribution with clear exposition and strong experimental results.  
- Core idea—learning with a top-k loss (zero loss if true class is among k predictions)—is novel and well-motivated.  
- Approach effectively addresses scenarios where partial correctness is acceptable or preferable to strict single-class prediction.  
- Primary suggestion: strengthen motivation for the chosen loss by explicitly connecting it to domain characteristics and explaining why top-k is superior to alternatives (e.g., cross-entropy, hinge).  
- Explain how the value of k is determined (empirically or otherwise) and provide theoretical/practical rationale for the loss choice to improve clarity and impact.  
- Minor typo: page 3 “globally optimal leads to” should read “globally leads to” (remove “optimal”).  
- Overall assessment: solid submission with a clean methodology and convincing results; strong candidate for acceptance if minor points are addressed.
