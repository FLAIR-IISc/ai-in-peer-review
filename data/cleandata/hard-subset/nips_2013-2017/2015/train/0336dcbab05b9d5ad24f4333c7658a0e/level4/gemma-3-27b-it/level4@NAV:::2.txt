In numerous multiclass classification tasks involving a substantial number of classes, inherent semantic similarities often exist between them. This paper introduces top-k SVM, an algorithm specifically designed to address learning scenarios where cost is incurred only when the ground truth label falls outside the top *k* predicted classes – a common characteristic of challenges like ImageNet. The authors extend the standard loss function to a top-k loss formulation and subsequently develop an efficient optimization procedure for this modified objective. Empirical evaluation on large-scale datasets demonstrates the algorithm’s scalability and reveals performance gains compared to existing methods.

The paper presents a well-defined motivation and is readily comprehensible. The addressed problem – the impact of class similarity in multiclass learning – is significant and frequently encountered in real-world applications. Specifically, this work proposes top-k SVM to effectively manage multiclass classification problems where similar classes may lead to acceptable, near-miss predictions. The algorithm exhibits favorable scalability to large datasets and achieves performance improvements over the considered baselines.
