The paper proposes a top-k hinge loss for top-k classification. It generalizes the multiclass SVM and uses the sum of k largest components as a convex approximation to the desired top-k loss. An efficient optimization algorithm is proposed and relationships with previous work are discussed. The paper is clearly written and the experiment results show the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to baselines.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of their method, including its relation to ranking based losses and its optimization via Prox-SDCA. The paper is well-organized and the writing is clear and concise. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, particularly in terms of top-k accuracy.

The strengths of the paper include its novel formulation of the top-k multiclass SVM, its efficient optimization algorithm, and its thorough experimental evaluation. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of their method and its relation to previous work.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it does not provide comparisons with all relevant previous work. Specifically, the authors mention that they did not compare their method to [25, 8] due to the availability of the implementations. However, it would be interesting to see these comparisons in the future.

Another potential area for improvement is in making the code public upon acceptance. This would allow other researchers to build upon the authors' work and to reproduce their results.

Overall, the paper presents a neat formulation of top-k multiclass SVM that works well. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their method and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments.

Arguments pro acceptance:
- The paper proposes a novel formulation of top-k multiclass SVM.
- The authors provide an efficient optimization algorithm for their method.
- The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
- The paper is well-written and well-organized.

Arguments con acceptance:
- The paper does not provide comparisons with all relevant previous work.
- The code is not currently public.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
