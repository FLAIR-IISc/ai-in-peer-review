This paper addresses the common scenario in multiclass classification problems where a large number of classes exhibit semantic similarity. The authors propose an algorithm, termed top-k SVM, to tackle the learning scenario where a cost is incurred only when the ground truth label falls outside the top k predicted classes, such as in the ImageNet challenge. By extending the loss function to incorporate top-k loss, the authors devise an efficient optimization procedure. Experimental results on large-scale datasets demonstrate the algorithm's scalability and show clear improvements over competing methods.

The paper is well-motivated and easy to follow, tackling an important problem that frequently arises in practical applications. The top-k SVM algorithm is specifically designed to handle multiclass classification scenarios where classes may be very similar to one another. Notably, the algorithm is capable of scaling to extremely large datasets and achieves improvements over existing methods.
