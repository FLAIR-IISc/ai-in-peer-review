This paper proposes a novel algorithm, top-k SVM, to address multiclass classification problems where semantic similarity between classes is prevalent. In such scenarios, the cost is typically incurred only when the ground truth label falls outside the top k predicted classes, a common setting in challenges like ImageNet. The authors extend the traditional loss function to accommodate top-k loss and develop an efficient optimization procedure. Experimental results on large-scale datasets demonstrate the algorithm's scalability and show noticeable improvements over competing methods.

The paper is well-motivated and easy to follow, tackling an important problem that frequently arises in practical applications. The top-k SVM algorithm effectively handles multiclass classification cases where certain classes exhibit high similarity. Notably, the algorithm exhibits excellent scalability to large datasets and outperforms compared methods.
