Overall assessment:
- The paper addresses a non-trivial problem and is clearly written. The empirical results are strong and support the claims.

Main suggestion:
- Please provide a stronger motivation for the specific loss function. In particular:
  - Explain why this top-k loss is preferable to standard alternatives (e.g., cross-entropy, hinge, or listwise losses) for the target applications.
  - Clarify its theoretical properties (e.g., consistency, calibration with respect to top-k accuracy) and any advantages for optimization (smoothness, convexity, stability).
  - Discuss how k should be chosen in practice and include a sensitivity analysis showing performance as k varies.
  - If applicable, articulate how the loss aligns with the evaluation metric and whether it trades off precision and recall within the top-k set.

Minor issues:
- Typo on page 3: “globally optimal leads to” should be “globally leads to” (remove “optimal”).

Summary of the contribution:
- The paper studies learning with a top-k loss, where the model produces k predictions and the loss is zero when the true class is included among those k predictions, and positive otherwise. This is an interesting and practically relevant problem setup, and the paper presents it well.

Overall, this is a solid and well-executed paper. With a clearer justification for the choice of loss and guidance on setting k, the contribution would be even stronger.
