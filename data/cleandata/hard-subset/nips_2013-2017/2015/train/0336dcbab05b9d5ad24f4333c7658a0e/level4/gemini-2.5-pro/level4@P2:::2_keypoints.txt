- Addresses multiclass classification with many classes where zero-one loss is too punitive and proposes optimizing top-k error instead.  
- Main contribution: "top-k multiclass SVM," a formulation that directly optimizes a convex surrogate for top-k error.  
- Introduces a novel "top-k hinge loss" defined as the average of the top k incorrect-class scores, providing a tight convex upper bound on top-k zero-one loss.  
- Derives the convex conjugate of the top-k hinge loss and defines a new convex polytope called the "top-k simplex" generalizing the standard simplex.  
- Proposes a practical optimization scheme based on Proximal Stochastic Dual Coordinate Ascent (Prox-SDCA).  
- Develops an efficient algorithm for Euclidean projection onto the top-k simplex — a significant technical contribution of independent interest.  
- Situates the loss relative to Usunier et al.'s ranking losses and argues their formulation gives a tighter upper bound on top-k error.  
- Empirical validation on five image datasets (including Places205 and ImageNet) shows consistent improvements in top-k accuracy over strong baselines.  
- Paper is technically strong, well-written, and clearly organized, with thorough theoretical analysis and comprehensive experiments.  
- Releases a high-quality C++ implementation, enhancing reproducibility and practical impact.  
- Weakness: optimizing for top-k can sometimes reduce top-1 accuracy (trade-off not fully explored; more guidance on when to prefer the method would help).
