- Addresses multiclass classification with many classes and class ambiguity, arguing top-k error is a more appropriate metric than zero-one loss.
- Main contribution: "Top-k Multiclass SVM," a novel convex surrogate (top-k hinge loss) that directly optimizes top-k error.
- Builds on Crammer and Singer multiclass SVM; direct k-th margin extension is non-convex, so authors propose a convex upper bound.
- Defines top-k hinge loss as the average of the k largest values of (a + c), providing a tighter convex upper bound than a Usunier et al. alternative.
- Derives the Fenchel dual and shows optimization requires projection onto a new convex set, the "top-k simplex" (∆k).
- Develops an efficient algorithm for projection onto ∆k, reducing cases to a continuous quadratic knapsack problem or a projection onto a "top-k cone."
- Integrates the method into Proximal Stochastic Dual Coordinate Ascent (Prox-SDCA) for optimization.
- Extensive experiments on five image datasets, including Places205 and ImageNet, show consistent top-k accuracy improvements over baselines (multiclass SVM, one-vs-all, SVMPerf, TopPush).
- Method scales to large datasets and sometimes yields improvements in top-1 accuracy as well.
- Technical strengths: well-motivated convex surrogate, sound dual derivation, novel projection algorithm of independent interest.
- Presentation strengths: clear writing, consistent notation, formal propositions/lemmas, and an open-source implementation for reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited intuition/motivation for choosing the average of the top-k scores—more geometric or probabilistic interpretation would help.
- Minor note: paper is well-edited and the Section 4.1 discussion linking to CQKP is particularly clear and useful.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with significant practical and technical merit; strongly recommended for acceptance.
