In high-cardinality multiclass settings, semantic overlap among categories is often observed. This paper introduced an algorithm called top-k SVM to explicitly handle the learning scenario where a cost is incurred only when the true label falls outside the top k predicted classes (e.g., the ImageNet challenge). By extending the loss function to a top-k loss, the authors derived an efficient optimization procedure. Experiments on large-scale datasets demonstrated the algorithm’s scalability and showed clear improvements over competing methods.

The paper is clearly motivated and easy to follow. The problem they address—similar classes in multiclass learning—is important and arises frequently in real-world applications. This work formulates the top-k SVM to manage multiclass cases where some categories are very similar to one another. The algorithm scales to very large datasets and showed improvements over the compared methods.
