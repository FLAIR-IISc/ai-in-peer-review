- Introduces multiclass SVMs that directly optimize top-k performance to address class ambiguity in large-scale classification.  
- Proposes a tight convex upper bound for the top-k error.  
- Presents a fast optimization scheme using an efficient projection onto the top-k simplex; the projection algorithm is an independent contribution.  
- Clearly positions the work within existing multiclass SVM and ranking-loss literature.  
- Derives the primal–conjugate pair for the top-k hinge loss (Proposition 2) with clear exposition.  
- Uses Prox-SDCA as the optimization framework and leverages efficient algorithms.  
- Experimental results on five datasets show consistent improvements in top-k accuracy over baselines.  
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets (Places205, ImageNet) in the supplement.  
- Strengths: technical soundness, clarity of presentation, and well-organized manuscript.  
- Weakness: limited additional motivation beyond class ambiguity; could discuss specific applications where top-k matters.  
- Weakness: needs more in-depth analysis of the trade-off between top-k and top-1 accuracy and how it relates to dataset characteristics.  
- Minor typo noted on page 3 ("globally optimal leads to" → "globally optimal").  
- Overall: strong, valuable contribution to multiclass classification and suitable for NIPS.
