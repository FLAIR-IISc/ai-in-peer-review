- Proposes a novel top-k hinge loss as a principled convex relaxation of the top-k ranking objective.  
- Generalizes multiclass SVMs by summing the k largest hinge losses to form a computationally tractable surrogate.  
- Main theoretical contribution: derives a convex optimization problem that preserves top-k structure.  
- Presents an efficient, scalable coordinate descent optimization algorithm exploiting problem structure.  
- Clear, well-motivated exposition with logical flow from formulation to algorithm.  
- Strong empirical results showing consistent, significant improvements over standard SVMs, top-1 classifiers, and recent top-k methods on CIFAR-10, MNIST, and a large-scale image retrieval task.  
- Ablation studies isolate the proposed loss’s contribution; runtime comparisons demonstrate computational efficiency.  
- Primary concern: missing comparisons with two important prior works ([25, 8]); reviewer urges inclusion if feasible.  
- Requests authors commit to publicly releasing code upon acceptance to ensure reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, practically significant, and suitable for acceptance; minor issues are addressable.
