This paper addresses multiclass classification problems that feature a large number of semantically similar classes. The work is specifically motivated by learning scenarios where performance is measured by top-k accuracy, meaning a cost is incurred only if the ground truth class is not among the top-k predictions. To tackle this, the authors propose the top-k SVM, an algorithm that extends the standard SVM framework with a novel top-k loss function, and they formulate an efficient optimization procedure for the resulting objective.

The paper is well-motivated and clearly written. It addresses an important and practical challenge that arises frequently in large-scale classification. The proposed algorithm is shown to be highly scalable, and experiments conducted on large datasets demonstrate a clear improvement over compared methods.
