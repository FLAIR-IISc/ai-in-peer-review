- Proposes a top-k multiclass SVM that directly optimizes top-k performance via a convex top-k hinge φk that reduces to Crammer–Singer when k=1.  
- Derives the convex conjugate of φk and introduces the top-k simplex ∆k with adaptive upper bounds xi ≤ (1/k)〈1,x〉 as the conjugate domain.  
- Relates φk to ranking-based losses (Usunier et al.’s Lβ) and contrasts it with an alternative averaging loss φ̃k.  
- Uses Prox-SDCA with block dual updates and incremental primal maintenance for optimization.  
- Provides an efficient algorithm for (biased) projection onto ∆k(r) and the top-k cone with complexity comparable to classical simplex projection methods.  
- Experiments on five image datasets (Caltech 101 Silhouettes, MIT Indoor 67, SUN 397, Places 205, ImageNet 2012) show consistent top-k accuracy gains over strong baselines and scalability to millions of examples; code is released.  
- Strengths: convex tight surrogate, elegant dual derivation and top-k simplex, practical projection algorithm, extensive scalable experiments, and clear positioning relative to prior ranking methods.  
- Weaknesses: motivation and intuition for choosing φk over alternatives (e.g., φ̃k) could be strengthened, including when optimizing top-k may reduce top-1 performance.  
- Quality: technical development appears sound, proofs provided, projection algorithm well specified, and Prox-SDCA offers principled stopping via duality gap.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and clearly written; main clarity suggestion is more intuition on φk vs alternatives.  
- Originality: novel combination of top-k hinge, top-k simplex, and efficient projection enabling exact SDCA updates; appealing geometric perspective.  
- Significance: practically important for large-scale multiclass problems (especially vision), with likely adoption due to consistent gains, scalability, and public code.
