In many multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes, semantically similar categories are common. This paper proposes top-k SVM, an algorithm that explicitly addresses settings in which a loss is incurred only when the true label is not among the top k predicted classes (e.g., the ImageNet challenge). The authors extend the standard multiclass loss to a top-k loss and develop an efficient optimization procedure. Experiments on large-scale datasets demonstrate good scalability and consistent improvements over competing methods.

The paper is well motivated and easy to follow. The problem of similar classes in multiclass learning is important and arises frequently in practice. By formulating an objective that matches the top-k evaluation criterion, the proposed approach directly handles cases in which some classes are very similar to one another. The algorithm scales to very large datasets and shows clear gains over the compared approaches.
