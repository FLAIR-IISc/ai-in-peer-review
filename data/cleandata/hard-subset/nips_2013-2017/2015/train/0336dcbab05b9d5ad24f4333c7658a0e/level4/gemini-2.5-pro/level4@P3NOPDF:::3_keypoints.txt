- Proposes a novel approach to learning with a top-k classification loss by training models to output a set of k predictions with zero loss if the true class is included.  
- Addresses a practical and important setting, especially for large-scale classification where a small accurate candidate set is more useful than a single guess.  
- Methodology is technically non-trivial, clearly written, and easy to follow.  
- Problem motivation is strong and the paper makes a compelling case for relevance.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and shows strong, convincing results for directly optimizing the top-k objective.  
- Main suggestion: better motivate and justify the specific loss choice, including theoretical properties and comparisons with alternative surrogates.  
- Request for clarification: is the proposed loss a convex surrogate for the 0-1 top-k loss, and how does it relate to hinge/logistic losses?  
- Minor issue: typo on page 3 (“globally optimal leads to” — remove redundant “optimal”).  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed paper that would be excellent with an expanded discussion of the loss function.
