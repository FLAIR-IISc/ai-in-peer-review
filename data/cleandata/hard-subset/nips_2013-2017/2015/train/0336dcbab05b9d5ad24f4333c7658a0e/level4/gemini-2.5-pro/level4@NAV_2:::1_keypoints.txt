- Introduces a novel top-k hinge loss that directly optimizes top-k classification accuracy.  
- Generalizes Crammer and Singer multiclass SVM to the top-k setting.  
- Proves the top-k hinge is a tight, convex upper bound on the non-convex top-k zero-one loss.  
- Derives the Fenchel dual, leading to the new convex “top-k simplex” polytope.  
- Presents a scalable Prox-SDCA optimization algorithm tailored to the top-k loss.  
- Contributes an efficient projection algorithm onto the top-k simplex.  
- Clarifies relationship to Usunier et al.’s family of ranking losses.  
- Empirically validated on five datasets (including Places205 and ILSVRC2012) with consistent top-k accuracy gains.  
- Notes occasional small decreases in top-1 accuracy (e.g., MIT Indoor 67), suggesting a trade-off that needs further discussion.  
- Suggests adding comparisons to other large-scale multiclass methods (e.g., output codes, classifier trees) for broader context.  
- Manuscript is technically strong, well-written, and likely impactful; overall recommendation favors acceptance.
