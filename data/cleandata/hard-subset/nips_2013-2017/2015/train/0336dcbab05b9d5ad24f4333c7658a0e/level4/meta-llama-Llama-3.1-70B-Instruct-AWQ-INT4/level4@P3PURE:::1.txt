This paper proposes a top-k hinge loss for top-k classification, generalizing the multiclass SVM by utilizing the sum of the k largest components as a convex approximation to the desired top-k loss. An efficient optimization algorithm is also proposed, and the authors discuss relationships with previous work. The paper is clearly written, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to the baselines.

There are a couple of aspects that could further enhance the paper. Firstly, the authors did not report comparisons with [25, 8] due to the unavailability of the implementations. However, comparisons with these methods would be highly valuable, and I would be particularly interested in seeing these results.

Additionally, it would be beneficial if the authors could make the code publicly available upon acceptance. This would facilitate the reproducibility of the results and allow other researchers to build upon this work.

Overall, the paper presents a neat formulation of top-k multiclass SVM that works well, and with a few additional details, it has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
