In many multiclass classification problems with a large number of classes, semantic similarity between classes is commonly observed. This paper proposes an algorithm, termed top-k SVM, to explicitly address learning scenarios where a cost is incurred only when the ground truth label is outside the top *k* predicted classes – a situation frequently encountered in challenges such as ImageNet. After extending the loss function to a top-k loss, the authors formulate an efficient optimization procedure. Experiments on large-scale datasets demonstrate the algorithm’s scalability and a clear performance improvement over existing methods.

The paper is clearly motivated and easy to follow. The problem it addresses – leveraging semantic similarity in multiclass learning – is an important one with relevance to many practical applications. Specifically, the authors formulate the top-k SVM algorithm to effectively handle multiclass classification cases where classes may exhibit significant similarity. The algorithm scales well to very large datasets and achieves improvements over comparable methods.
