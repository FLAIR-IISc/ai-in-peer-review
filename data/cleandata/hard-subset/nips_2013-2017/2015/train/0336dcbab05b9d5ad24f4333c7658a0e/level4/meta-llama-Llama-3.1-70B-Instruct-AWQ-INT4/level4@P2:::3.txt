The paper "Top-k Multiclass SVM" proposes a direct method to optimize for top-k performance in multiclass classification problems, particularly when the number of classes is large and class ambiguity is common. The authors extend the well-known multiclass SVM by introducing a tight convex upper bound of the top-k error, which they call the top-k hinge loss.

The main idea is to relax the penalty for making an error in the top-k predictions. The authors show that their proposed loss function is a convex upper bound on the actual top-k zero-one loss and can be optimized efficiently using a proximal stochastic dual coordinate ascent (Prox-SDCA) framework.

The paper is well-written, and the experimental results are good, demonstrating consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines on five image classification datasets. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the relation between their top-k hinge loss and ranking-based losses.

One suggestion for improvement is to further motivate the choice of the loss function. While the authors provide a clear explanation of the top-k hinge loss, it would be helpful to discuss the reasoning behind choosing this specific loss function and how it compares to other possible choices.

Additionally, there is a minor typo on page 3, where "globally optimal leads to" should be corrected to simply "leads to".

Overall, this is a nice paper that describes an interesting problem and proposes an efficient solution. The idea of learning with respect to the top-k loss is well-motivated, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and efficient method for optimizing top-k performance in multiclass classification problems.
* The experimental results are good, demonstrating consistent improvements in top-k accuracy compared to various baselines.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the relation between the top-k hinge loss and ranking-based losses.

Weaknesses:

* There is a minor typo on page 3.
