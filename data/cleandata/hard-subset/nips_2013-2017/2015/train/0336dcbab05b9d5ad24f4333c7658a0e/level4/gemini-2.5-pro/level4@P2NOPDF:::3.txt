This paper presents a novel and interesting approach to multi-class classification by learning directly with a top-k loss function. The core idea is to train a model to produce *k* predictions, with the objective that the true class is included within this set. This is a practically relevant problem formulation for many applications where providing a small set of plausible options is more valuable than insisting on a single correct answer. The material is non-trivial, and the technical contributions are sound.

The paper is very well-written and clearly structured, making the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings easy to follow. The experimental evaluation is thorough, providing convincing results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach when compared to relevant baselines.

My primary suggestion for improvement relates to the motivation for the specific choice of the loss function. While the top-k objective is intuitively appealing, a more detailed discussion of why this particular functional form was chosen over other potential alternatives would strengthen the paper. For example, elaborating on the theoretical properties of this loss (e.g., convexity, smoothness) or providing a conceptual comparison to other losses used in structured prediction could offer valuable context. Further motivating the design choices would help the reader better appreciate the nuances and broader implications of this contribution.

Additionally, I noticed a minor typo on page 3 in the phrase "globally optimal leads to"; the word "optimal" appears to be redundant and should be removed.

Overall, this is a strong paper that addresses a relevant problem with a well-executed solution and compelling empirical support.
