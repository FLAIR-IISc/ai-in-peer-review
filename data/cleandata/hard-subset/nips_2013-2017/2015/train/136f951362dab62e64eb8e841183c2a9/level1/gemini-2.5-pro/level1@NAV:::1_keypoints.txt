- Problem: Tackles time-sensitive recommendation and predicting user return times by recommending the right item at the right moment.  
- Method: Proposes modeling each user-item interaction as a Hawkes process with base intensity and excitation matrices captured under a low-rank assumption.  
- Novelty: Integrates point-process (Hawkes) dynamics with low-rank matrix factorization for collaborative temporal recommendation.  
- Generalization: Low-rank parameterization enables transfer to unobserved user-item pairs.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on matrix/tensor factorization literature but uniquely introduces event-driven point-process dynamics.  
- Optimization: Develops a scalable algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods to avoid costly SVDs.  
- Theory: Provides convergence rate analysis (Theorem 2) giving theoretical backing for the optimization method.  
- Empirical strength: Thorough evaluation on synthetic and three real-world datasets, outperforming strong baselines on recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Model validation: Uses the time-change theorem to validate the Hawkes process assumption empirically.  
- Missing details: Hyperparameter tuning (ρ, λ, β) and sensitivity analysis are not discussed, limiting reproducibility.  
- Limited scope: Experiments only use a simple exponential kernel; no empirical comparison with flexible/nonparametric kernels.  
- Missing demonstrations: Claimed ability to incorporate contextual features is not empirically shown.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, principled approach; significant scalable technical contribution with theoretical guarantees; strong empirical results.  
- Rejection argument: Empirical validation omits key claimed extensions (flexible kernels, contextual features).  
- Overall: Strengths (novelty, technical depth, empirical performance) outweigh minor limitations; solid advance in temporal recommendation.
