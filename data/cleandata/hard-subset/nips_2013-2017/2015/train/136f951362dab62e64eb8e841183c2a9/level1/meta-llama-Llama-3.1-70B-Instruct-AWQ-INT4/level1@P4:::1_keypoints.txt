- Summary: Proposes a framework combining self-exciting Hawkes processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction with an efficient O(1/t) convergent algorithm that scales to millions of pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Strength — Novelty: Novel combination of self-exciting point processes and low-rank models for this task.
- Strength — Technical soundness: Thorough mathematical formulation and an efficient parameter-learning algorithm.
- Strength — Scalability: Algorithm scales to very large user-item and event volumes.
- Strength — Empirical evaluation: Extensive experiments on synthetic and real data showing superior performance versus baselines.
- Weakness — Assumptions: Assumes user-item interactions follow a self-exciting Hawkes process, which may not hold in all real-world cases.
- Weakness — Simplifications: Uses an exponential triggering kernel assumption that may not capture complex real-world dynamics.
- Weakness — Hyperparameter tuning: Hyperparameters (ρ, λ, β) are tuned for best performance without clear explanation of the selection procedure.
- Weakness — Limited interpretability: Lacks clear interpretation of learned model parameters and underlying interaction dynamics.
- Originality: Combination of known components is novel and advances recommender-system methods.
- Significance: Addresses an important problem (time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction) with potential real-world impact.
- Clarity: Well-written and accessible with clear explanations of methods and background.
- Acceptance arguments — Pro: Novel, technically solid, scalable, and empirically strong.
- Acceptance arguments — Con: Questionable modeling assumptions, simplified kernel choice, and unclear hyperparameter selection.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance pending revisions to address the listed weaknesses.
