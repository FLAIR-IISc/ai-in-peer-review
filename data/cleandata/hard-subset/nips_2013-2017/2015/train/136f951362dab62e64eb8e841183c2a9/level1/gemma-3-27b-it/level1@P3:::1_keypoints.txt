- Proposes a novel framework combining Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and user return-time prediction.
- Core idea is compelling: models temporal dynamics of user-item interactions beyond traditional rating prediction.
- Provides a convex optimization formulation with a proven O(1/t) convergence rate.
- Presents a scalable algorithm claimed to handle millions of user-item pairs.
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real-world datasets supports the method’s effectiveness, including useful ablation studies.
- Novelty: explicit connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank recommendation is a significant contribution.
- Theoretical rigor: convexity and convergence guarantees are strong points.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, motivated, and mathematically clear.
- Practical relevance: addresses both recommendation timing and return-time prediction with potential applications (e.g., healthcare).
- Weakness: limited exploration of kernel choices (only exponential) and lack of analysis on kernel bandwidth sensitivity.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on selection/tuning of nuclear-norm regularization parameters (λ, β) and their impact on performance.
- Weakness: scalability claims need more concrete runtime/resource details and deeper complexity analysis.
- Weakness: brief descriptions of real-world datasets—missing sparsity, event rates, and preprocessing details.
- Weakness: comparisons to state-of-the-art temporal models (e.g., STiC and newer point-process methods) are incomplete or superficial.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, experimentally validated, scalable, and well-presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete kernel/regularization exploration, limited scalability/dataset details, and insufficient comparisons to recent temporal baselines.
- Overall assessment: strong paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions addressing kernel selection, regularization tuning, scalability/runtime details, dataset descriptions, and stronger comparisons.
