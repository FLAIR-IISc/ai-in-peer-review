## Review of "Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities"

This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation by connecting self-exciting point processes and low-rank models. The core idea is to model user-item interactions as a Hawkes process, leveraging its ability to capture temporal dependencies and recurrent patterns. A low-rank structure is then imposed to facilitate generalization to unseen user-item pairs. The authors present a convex formulation of the problem and develop an efficient optimization algorithm with a provable O(1/t) convergence rate, demonstrating scalability to large datasets.

The work builds upon recent advancements in temporal recommendation, particularly the work of Kapoor et al. [12, 11] which utilizes survival analysis and hidden semi-Markov models. However, the proposed approach distinguishes itself by offering a more general framework capable of recommending *any* new item, unlike the limitations of survival analysis focused on single terminal events. The connection to low-rank models is a key contribution, enabling knowledge transfer and scalability. The paper also correctly identifies the limitations of traditional rating-based recommendation systems and highlights the importance of modeling user engagement through temporal patterns, echoing concerns raised in [26].

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation is a significant contribution.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The convex formulation and convergence analysis provide a strong theoretical underpinning for the proposed method.
* **Scalability:** The algorithm’s O(1/t) convergence rate and demonstrated scalability to millions of user-item pairs are impressive.
* **Empirical Validation:**  The results on both synthetic and real datasets demonstrate superior performance compared to several baselines, including STiC [11] and tensor factorization methods [3].
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the technical details.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Kernel Choice:** The paper primarily focuses on the exponential kernel. While justified, exploring other kernel options and their impact on performance could strengthen the work.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The sensitivity of the algorithm to the choice of hyperparameters (ρ, λ, β) isn’t thoroughly discussed.
* **Limited Baselines:** While several baselines are included, a comparison with more recent and sophisticated temporal recommendation models (e.g., those based on recurrent neural networks) would be beneficial.
* **Real-World Data:** The real-world datasets used (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) are somewhat standard. Exploring more complex and diverse datasets could further validate the approach.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to a challenging problem in recommendation systems.
* The demonstrated scalability and empirical performance are compelling.
* The work has the potential to significantly impact the field of time-sensitive recommendation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The limited exploration of kernel choices and parameter sensitivity.
* The lack of comparison with more recent state-of-the-art temporal recommendation models.



**Overall:**

I believe this paper represents a strong contribution to the field. The novelty of the approach, combined with the theoretical guarantees and empirical results, makes it worthy of acceptance. While there are some areas for improvement, the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
