- Problem: tackles time-sensitive recommendation—predicting the most relevant item at the right moment and a user’s next return time.  
- Method: novel framework combining self-exciting point processes with low-rank models.  
- Formulation & algorithm: convex formulation with an efficient optimization algorithm and provable O(1/t) convergence rate.  
- Novelty vs prior work: extends beyond survival-analysis approaches (e.g., Kapoor et al.) by enabling recommendation of new items.  
- Scalability: low-rank modeling supports large datasets.  
- Theoretical contribution: clear mathematical formulation and rigorous convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical results: strong performance on synthetic and real datasets, outperforming several baselines including state-of-the-art.  
- Strengths highlighted: technical soundness, detailed algorithm description, convergence analysis, and clear exposition of the method.  
- Weaknesses highlighted: background on temporal point processes is overly lengthy; writing could be more concise.  
- Weaknesses highlighted: experiments lack deeper analysis of why the method outperforms (e.g., inspection of learned parameters/triggering kernel).  
- Weaknesses highlighted: related work discussion could be more nuanced (e.g., mention inhomogeneous Hawkes extensions).  
- Weaknesses highlighted: practical implications of the O(1/t) convergence rate are not clearly connected to experiments.
