- Introduces Low Rank Hawkes Process (LRHP) framework for time-sensitive recommendation and predicting user return times.  
- Models each user-item event stream with a Hawkes process: baseline (long-term) + self-excitation (short-term), with low-rank Λ0 and A to handle sparsity.  
- Casts parameter estimation as convex optimization with nuclear-norm regularization and non-negativity to enforce low-rank structure.  
- Proposes a hybrid optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient (for non-negativity/smooth loss) and conditional gradient/Frank-Wolfe (for nuclear norm) to avoid full SVDs.  
- Provides theoretical convergence analysis with an O(1/t) rate for the proposed algorithm.  
- Evaluates on synthetic and three real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II), outperforming baselines on time-sensitive ranking and return-time prediction.  
- Uses time-change theorem analysis to validate Hawkes modeling assumption on real data.  
- Strength: Novel and principled synthesis of collaborative filtering and continuous-time Hawkes processes, avoiding time discretization.  
- Strength: Strong technical formulation and a scalable, theoretically-grounded optimization method of independent interest.  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation with explicit links between learned intensities and target tasks.  
- Strength: Comprehensive empirical validation demonstrating convergence, scalability, parameter recovery, and real-data improvements.  
- Weakness: Uses a shared exponential kernel bandwidth σ for all interactions, which may be too restrictive and sensitivity is unexplored.  
- Weakness: Multiple hyperparameters (λ, β, ρ, σ) require tuning; practical selection (and the theoretical threshold ρ*) is not well addressed.  
- Weakness: LMO step needs top singular vector computations which can be costly at very large scales; practical implementation/complexity details are sparse.  
- Conclusion: Strong, original contribution with minor implementation/extension caveats — recommendation: accept.
