- Addresses time-sensitive recommendation by modeling continuous-time user-item interactions and predicting both what to recommend and when a user will return.
- Proposes "Low Rank Hawkes Processes": model each user-item event stream as a Hawkes process with baseline intensity and self-excitation.
- Uses low-rank structure on baseline and excitation matrices (Λ0 and A) to transfer patterns across sparse user-item pairs.
- Formulates parameter estimation as a convex optimization: negative log-likelihood plus nuclear-norm regularization and non-negativity constraints.
- Develops a custom algorithm combining proximal gradient (for smooth loss/non-negativity) and conditional gradient (for nuclear-norm), with O(1/t) convergence guarantee.
- Theoretical analysis is strong, guaranteeing a unique global optimum for the convex formulation.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: synthetic experiments validate convergence and recovery; three diverse real-world datasets show superior performance to baselines.
- Clear, well-organized writing and helpful background on temporal point processes; figures and pseudocode support comprehension.
- Novel contribution arises from fusing Hawkes processes with low-rank collaborative filtering and a new hybrid optimization approach.
- Practical significance is high: addresses important problems in engagement-driven recommendation and is extensible to include contextual features.
- Weakness: equivalence between formulations (Eq.4 and Eq.5) depends on hyperparameter ρ exceeding a threshold ρ* that depends on the unknown optimum, so ρ must be tuned in practice.
- Weakness: sensitivity to the ρ hyperparameter is not explored, leaving theoretical/practical gap in parameter selection.
- Weakness: experiments use a simple exponential triggering kernel, so robustness to more complex or nonparametric kernels is unclear.
- Minor clarity issue: optimization section is dense and block-wise matrix notation could be more conventional for non-specialist readers.
- Overall recommendation: strengths (novelty, technical rigor, empirical gains, clarity) outweigh weaknesses, supporting acceptance.
