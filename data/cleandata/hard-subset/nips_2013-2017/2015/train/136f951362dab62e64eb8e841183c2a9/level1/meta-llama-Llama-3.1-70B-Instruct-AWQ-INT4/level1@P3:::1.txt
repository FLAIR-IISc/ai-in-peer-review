The paper "Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities" proposes a novel framework that connects self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture the recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs. The authors address two related questions: how to recommend the most relevant item at the right moment and how to accurately predict the next returning time of users to existing services.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem, related work, and the proposed framework. The technical details are well-explained, and the experiments are thorough and convincing.

The proposed framework is novel and has the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in time-sensitive recommendation systems. The use of self-exciting point processes and low-rank models is innovative and allows for the capture of complex temporal patterns in user behavior. The authors also provide an efficient algorithm for learning the model parameters, which is essential for large-scale applications.

The experiments are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework. The authors evaluate their method on both synthetic and real datasets, including last.fm, tmall.com, and MIMIC II medical dataset. The results show that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art competitors, including Poisson process, STiC, SVD, and tensor factorization.

The paper has several strengths:

1. **Novelty**: The proposed framework is novel and has the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in time-sensitive recommendation systems.
2. **Technical soundness**: The technical details are well-explained, and the experiments are thorough and convincing.
3. **Scalability**: The authors provide an efficient algorithm for learning the model parameters, which is essential for large-scale applications.
4. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The experiments are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework on various datasets.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses:

1. **Limited interpretability**: The proposed framework is complex, and it may be challenging to interpret the results and understand the underlying dynamics of the system.
2. **Sensitivity to hyperparameters**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the sensitivity of the proposed framework to hyperparameters, which may affect the results.
3. **Assumptions**: The authors assume that the user behavior follows a self-exciting point process, which may not always be the case in real-world applications.

In conclusion, the paper "Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities" proposes a novel and effective framework for time-sensitive recommendation systems. The authors provide a thorough and convincing evaluation of their method, and the results demonstrate the potential of the proposed framework to improve the state-of-the-art in this area.
