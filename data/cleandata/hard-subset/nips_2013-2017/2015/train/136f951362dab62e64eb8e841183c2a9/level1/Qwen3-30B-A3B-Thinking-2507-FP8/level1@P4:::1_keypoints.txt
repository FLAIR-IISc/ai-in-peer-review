- Proposes a novel framework connecting Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation.
- Targets two problems: recommending the most relevant item at the right time and predicting users’ next return time.
- Formulates a convex optimization with nuclear-norm regularization to enable knowledge transfer across user-item pairs.
- Introduces an algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods to solve the problem.
- Provides theoretical convergence analysis with an O(1/t) rate and full proofs, including equivalence of formulations.
- Generalizes beyond prior survival/HSMM approaches by enabling recommendation of new (unseen) items.
- Contrasts with SVD and tensor methods by modeling continuous temporal dynamics without time discretization.
- Evaluates comprehensively on synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) for ranking and return-time tasks.
- Consistently outperforms strong baselines (Poisson, STiC, SVD, tensor factorization) on both tasks.
- Shows linear scalability with dataset size, indicating suitability for large-scale problems.
- Strengths: technically sound, well-justified theory, thorough empirical evaluation, clear exposition, and reproducibility.
- Weaknesses: assumption of exponential triggering kernel—needs exploration of other kernels; related work could include more recent dynamic MF; lacks practical integration details with existing systems.
- Suited for NIPS given novelty, theoretical rigor, empirical support, and clear real-world relevance.
- Overall: strong contribution bridging temporal point processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation.
