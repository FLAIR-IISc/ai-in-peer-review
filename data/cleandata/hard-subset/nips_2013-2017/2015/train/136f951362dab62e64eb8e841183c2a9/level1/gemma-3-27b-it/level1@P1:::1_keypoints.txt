- Proposes a novel framework combining Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Models user-item interactions as a Hawkes process and leverages low-rank structure to generalize to unobserved pairs.  
- Presents a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm with O(1/t) convergence guarantee.  
- Algorithm is designed to scale to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real-world datasets shows strong performance versus STiC, tensor factorization, and SVD baselines.  
- Paper is clearly written with a good explanation of point process connections for non-experts.  
- Authors note easy extensibility to incorporate contextual information (user profiles, text, etc.).  
- Builds on and distinguishes itself from prior temporal recommendation and survival-analysis work, enabling recommendation of new items.  
- Parameter tuning details (ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth, rank) and sensitivity analysis are insufficiently described.  
- Experiments rely solely on an exponential kernel; impact of other kernels (Weibull, log-logistic) is unexplored.  
- The conditional gradient step and linear minimization oracle may be complex to implement; practical implementation issues aren’t discussed.  
- Real-world dataset preprocessing and statistics (e.g., avg events per user-item pair) are underreported.  
- Comparisons omit more contemporary/high-performing recommender systems (e.g., deep RNNs with attention).  
- Overall: strong, novel, and technically solid paper with minor weaknesses; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.  
- Rating: 7/10 (Strong Accept with Minor Revisions).
