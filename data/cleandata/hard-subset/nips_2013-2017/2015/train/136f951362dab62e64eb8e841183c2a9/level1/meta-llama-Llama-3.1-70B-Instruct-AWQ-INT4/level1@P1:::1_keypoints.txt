- Proposes a novel framework linking self-exciting point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Develops a convex, scalable optimization algorithm with O(1/t) convergence, claimed to handle millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Reports superior predictive performance on synthetic and real datasets versus state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Model is flexible and can incorporate additional contextual features (user profiles, text, spatial info).  
- Assumes user-item interactions follow a Hawkes self-exciting process, which may not hold in all real-world settings.  
- The model is more complex than some competitors, potentially reducing interpretability and ease of use.  
- Requires hyperparameter tuning, which can be time-consuming and expertise-dependent.  
- Evaluation is limited to item recommendation and return-time prediction; other tasks (e.g., rating prediction, user clustering) are not studied.  
- Original contribution: unique connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank models and an efficient learning algorithm.  
- Significant potential impact for improving temporal recommender systems and advances in scalable optimization.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation and clear figures, though some technical derivations may be hard for non-experts.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, potential impact, and technical soundness of model and algorithm.  
- Arguments for rejection: strong modeling assumptions, limited empirical evaluation, and increased model complexity.
