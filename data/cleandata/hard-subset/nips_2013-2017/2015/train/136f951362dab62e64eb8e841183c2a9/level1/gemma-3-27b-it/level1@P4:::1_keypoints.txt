- Proposes a novel framework combining Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation using a convex formulation and efficient algorithm with convergence guarantees.
- Models user-item consumption as a Hawkes process with a low-rank structure to generalize to unseen user-item pairs.
- Claims broader generality than prior temporal-recommendation work (e.g., Kapoor et al.) by enabling recommendation of new items.
- Grounds temporal dependency modeling in established Hawkes/process literature.
- Develops an optimization algorithm mixing proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with a formal convergence result (Theorem 2).
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs, hundreds of millions of events).
- Shows strong empirical performance on synthetic and real-world datasets, outperforming baselines like STiC, SVD, and tensor factorization.
- Paper is generally clear, well-organized, and presents technical details accessibly.
- Novelty: the Hawkes + low-rank combination is a new and theoretically sound contribution to temporal recommendation.
- Technical soundness: convex formulation and algorithmic design are well-motivated and justified.
- Scalability: explicit attention to and evidence for handling large-scale data.
- Empirical validation: comprehensive experiments support the method’s effectiveness.
- Weakness: parameter tuning (ρ, λ, β) is under-detailed and lacks sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: primary focus on exponential triggering kernel; no experimental exploration of other kernels.
- Weakness: real-world dataset descriptions lack useful statistics (e.g., sparsity, event distributions).
- Weakness: comparisons could include deeper analysis of scenarios where the low-rank assumption is most beneficial.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, scalable, empirically strong, and addresses an important problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient parameter-tuning details, limited kernel exploration, and sparse dataset reporting.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor weaknesses that do not undermine its contributions.
- Recommendation: Accept.
