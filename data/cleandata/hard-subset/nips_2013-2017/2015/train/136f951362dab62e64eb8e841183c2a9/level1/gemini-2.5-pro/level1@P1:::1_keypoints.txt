- Models two problems (what to recommend now and when a user will return) by treating user-item interactions as multivariate Hawkes processes.  
- Uses low-rank baseline intensity and self-excitation matrices (Λ0 and A) to handle sparsity and generalize to unobserved user-item pairs.  
- Learns parameters by maximizing log-likelihood with nuclear norm regularization and non-negativity constraints, yielding a convex optimization problem.  
- Introduces a hybrid optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient (for non-negativity) and conditional gradient/Frank-Wolfe (for nuclear norm), avoiding full SVDs and with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (convergence rate) and validates modeling choices via the time-change theorem.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic data and three real-world datasets shows superior recommendation and return-time prediction versus strong baselines.  
- Novelty/significance: first principled continuous-time framework that unifies low-rank collaborative filtering with self-exciting point processes and can recommend unseen items.  
- Technical quality: elegant modeling, clever split optimization approach, and solid theoretical analysis.  
- Experimental rigor: diverse datasets, strong baselines, held-out user-item sequences, and synthetic experiments for parameter recovery.  
- Weakness: Optimization section is dense and needs more intuition and detail (LMO/top singular vectors, line search) for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Practical sensitivity to hyperparameter ρ is not analyzed; guidance or heuristics for setting ρ are missing.  
- Weakness: Kernel choice limited to exponential; no ablation or sensitivity study on kernel shape/bandwidth.  
- Weakness: Scalability concern—top singular vector computations in the conditional gradient step may be expensive at industrial (million-scale) sizes.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, offers a novel, theoretically-grounded model and efficient algorithm, with strong empirical support.  
- Against acceptance: clarity of optimization details and insufficient exploration of key hyperparameters/kernels may hinder reproducibility and practical adoption.  
- Conclusion: High-quality, impactful paper; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses — strong recommend for acceptance.
