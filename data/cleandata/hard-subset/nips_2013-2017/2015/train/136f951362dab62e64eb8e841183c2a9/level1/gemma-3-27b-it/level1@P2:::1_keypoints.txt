- Proposes a novel framework combining Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank factorization for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Models user-item interactions as a Hawkes process to capture temporal dependencies and uses low-rank factors to generalize across users/items.  
- Targets both item recommendation at the right time and prediction of users’ next return time.  
- Introduces a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm with a provable convergence rate.  
- Well-motivated: addresses the common limitation of ignoring temporal dynamics in recommender systems.  
- Novel conceptual contribution: principled connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank models.  
- Strong theoretical analysis, including convergence guarantees for the optimization method.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real-world datasets show improved performance over state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Authors acknowledge potential extensions (e.g., adding contextual information) to broaden applicability.  
- Related work section is reasonably thorough and cites relevant prior work.  
- Exposition is dense: mathematical notation and algorithm explanation are hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Synthetic experiments lack detailed description of the data generation process and how ground-truth low-rank structure was enforced.  
- Real-data experiments need more detail on parameter tuning strategies and the specific baselines used.  
- Comparisons to baselines would benefit from deeper discussion of each baseline’s strengths and weaknesses in the given datasets.  
- Presentation of results could be improved (e.g., missing error bars, less interpretable figures).  
- Discussion of model limitations is brief; more thorough analysis of failure cases and future research directions is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, empirical effectiveness, and practical significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues, insufficient experimental detail, and limited discussion of limitations.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand experimental details, and elaborate limitations/future work.
