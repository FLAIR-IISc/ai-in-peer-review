- Proposes a novel framework integrating Hawkes self-exciting point processes with low-rank matrix models for time-sensitive recommendation.
- Targets two tasks: recommending relevant items at the right moment and predicting users' next return times.
- Establishes a unified connection between temporal point processes and low-rank collaborative filtering to capture recurrent temporal patterns.
- Differentiates from Kapoor et al. by generalizing to recommend new items (overcoming their limitation).
- Addresses tensor-factorization limitations by modeling full history dependence of events.
- Introduces a convex relaxation (Eq. 4) and provides rigorous convergence analysis (Theorem 2) with O(1/t) rate.
- Proposes a novel optimization algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods for this problem.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: synthetic data with ground truth and three real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II).
- Demonstrates clear improvements over strong baselines (Poisson, STiC, SVD, tensor factorization), especially on held-out sequences.
- Shows scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and reproducible with consistent notation and well-described algorithms/experiments.
- Applicable to practical domains (music streaming, e-commerce, healthcare) and can incorporate additional contextual features.
- Strengths: novel integration, rigorous theory, comprehensive experiments, scalability, and practical applicability.
- Weakness: limited discussion of cold-start handling for new users/items.
- Weakness: lacks statistical significance tests to confirm claimed performance gains.
- Weakness: assumes exponential triggering kernel with only brief mention of alternatives.
- Weakness: intensity-to-recommendation mapping could be made more concrete with examples.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance—meets NIPS criteria with high originality and significance.
