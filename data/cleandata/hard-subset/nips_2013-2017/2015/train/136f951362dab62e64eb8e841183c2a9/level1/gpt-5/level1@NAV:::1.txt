This paper addresses two coupled problems in recommender systems that are often treated separately: ranking items at the right moment and predicting user return times. The authors model each user–item interaction stream as a self-exciting Hawkes process with an exponential triggering kernel, and couple streams through low-rank structure on two nonnegative matrices: baseline intensities Λ0 and self-excitation magnitudes A. Estimation is cast as a convex program with nuclear-norm regularization and nonnegativity constraints. To scale, they introduce a penalty reformulation with auxiliary variables and propose a blended proximal/conditional-gradient algorithm that projects onto the nonnegative orthant for the smooth part and uses a linear oracle (top singular vectors) for the nuclear norms. They prove an O(1/t) convergence rate (with an additional accelerated component for the smooth part) and show strong performance on synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC-II), outperforming Poisson, survival/HMM (STiC), SVD, and Poisson-tensor baselines. The work relates to point process modeling [4–6,10], multidimensional/low-rank Hawkes learning (e.g., Zhou, Zha, Song, AISTATS/ICML), and rank minimization/conditional gradients (e.g., Harchaoui et al.).

Quality: The formulation is technically sound: the parametric Hawkes likelihood yields a concave log-likelihood in nonnegative parameters, enabling convex estimation with nuclear norms. The penalty reformulation is justified by a threshold ρ*, and the algorithmic design is sensible. However, ρ* is problem-dependent and unknown; in practice ρ is tuned without certificates that the penalized optimum matches the constrained one. Comparisons omit stronger temporal models (e.g., mutually-exciting across items/users, nonparametric kernels), and calibration of time predictions is not reported.

Clarity: Overall clear and well organized, with helpful background. Some notation is dense (e.g., φ, ψ vectors) and algorithmic details (L, step sizes, cost per iteration, memory footprint, stopping criteria) could be expanded. A brief discussion on identifiability between Λ0 and A would help.

Originality: The connection of time-sensitive recommendation to a low-rank Hawkes framework with a convex estimator is novel, and the proximal–CG blend under nonnegativity plus nuclear norms is an interesting algorithmic contribution compared to ADMM-based approaches.

Significance: The ability to jointly produce time-aware rankings and return-time predictions at scale is valuable. Results suggest improved accuracy and good scalability, making the approach likely to influence subsequent temporal recommendation work.

Pros for acceptance:
- Principled convex formulation linking Hawkes processes and low-rank transfer.
- Scalable algorithm with theoretical rate and practical efficiency.
- Strong empirical results on varied domains; handles both ranking and return time.
- Clear path to incorporating context features.

Cons against acceptance:
- Reliance on penalty parameter ρ without optimality certificates in practice.
- Limited baselines (no mutually-exciting or nonparametric Hawkes, fewer modern temporal recommenders).
- Assumes per-pair self-excitation and exponential kernels; seasonality/context evaluated only minimally.
- Some clarity gaps in complexity analysis and reproducibility details.
