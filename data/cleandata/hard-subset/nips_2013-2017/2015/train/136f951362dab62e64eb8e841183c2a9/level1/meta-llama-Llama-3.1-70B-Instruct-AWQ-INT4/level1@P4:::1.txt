**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction using self-exciting point processes and low-rank models. The authors model user-item interactions as a self-exciting Hawkes process, which captures the temporal dependencies between events. They then transfer the learned knowledge to unobserved user-item pairs using low-rank models. The authors also develop an efficient algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods to learn the model parameters. The algorithm achieves the optimal O(1/t) convergence rate and scales up to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events.

**Strengths**

1. **Novelty**: The paper proposes a novel framework that combines self-exciting point processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction.
2. **Technical soundness**: The authors provide a thorough mathematical formulation of the problem and develop an efficient algorithm to learn the model parameters.
3. **Scalability**: The algorithm scales up to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events, making it suitable for large-scale applications.
4. **Empirical evaluation**: The authors conduct extensive experiments on both synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating the superior performance of their algorithm compared to state-of-the-art competitors.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Assumptions**: The paper assumes that the user-item interactions can be modeled as a self-exciting Hawkes process, which may not always be the case in real-world applications.
2. **Simplifications**: The authors simplify the problem by assuming that the triggering kernel has an exponential form, which may not capture the complexity of real-world user-item interactions.
3. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The authors tune the hyperparameters (ρ, λ, β) to give the best performance, but do not provide a clear explanation of how these hyperparameters were chosen.
4. **Limited interpretability**: The paper does not provide a clear interpretation of the learned model parameters, making it difficult to understand the underlying dynamics of the user-item interactions.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel framework that combines self-exciting point processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction. While the individual components of the framework are not new, the authors' combination of these components is novel and contributes to the state-of-the-art in recommender systems.

**Significance**

The paper addresses a significant problem in recommender systems, namely, time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction. The authors' framework has the potential to improve the performance of recommender systems in real-world applications, particularly in scenarios where user-item interactions exhibit temporal dependencies.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the mathematical formulation and algorithm. The authors provide sufficient background information on self-exciting point processes and low-rank models, making the paper accessible to readers without prior knowledge of these topics.

**Arguments pro and con acceptance**

* The paper proposes a novel framework that addresses a significant problem in recommender systems.
