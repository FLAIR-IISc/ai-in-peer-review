- Proposes a novel framework connecting self-exciting (Hawkes) point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal user-item patterns.  
- Addresses two tasks: recommending the most relevant item at the right moment and predicting users' next return time.  
- Formulates a convex optimization problem combining Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix constraints.  
- Develops an efficient algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Differs from prior work (e.g., Kapoor et al. STiC) by enabling generalization to new items and incorporating temporal dynamics beyond standard matrix factorization.  
- Strong theoretical analysis with convergence proofs and clear presentation.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets (last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC II).  
- Demonstrates superior predictive performance for recommendation and return-time prediction versus Poisson, SVD, and tensor baselines.  
- Scales to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events.  
- Highly original contribution that fills an important literature gap; references relevant prior work appropriately.  
- Well-written, clearly organized, and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.  
- Minor weaknesses: cold-start for new users/items not explicitly addressed; some technical details could be made more accessible.  
- Meets NIPS criteria (Quality, Clarity, Originality, Significance); strong recommendation for acceptance.
