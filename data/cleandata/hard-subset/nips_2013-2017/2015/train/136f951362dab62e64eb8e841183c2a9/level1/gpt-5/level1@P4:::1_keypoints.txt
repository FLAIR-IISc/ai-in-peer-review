- Addresses ranking and next-return-time prediction by modeling each user–item sequence as an exponential-kernel univariate Hawkes process.  
- Transfers information to unseen pairs via low-rank base-intensity and self-excitation matrices learned with nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Shows the Hawkes log-likelihood is concave in nonnegative parameters, yielding a convex optimization with nuclear-norm penalties.  
- Proposes a scalable auxiliary-variable algorithm combining proximal gradient (nonnegativity-constrained smooth part) and conditional gradient (Frank–Wolfe) for the nuclear norms.  
- Algorithm requires only top singular vectors per iteration, avoiding full SVDs and offering computational advantages for large matrices.  
- Provides convergence analysis for the penalized objective with an O(1/t) rate.  
- Empirical results show improved predictive performance and scalability (up to 100M events) on synthetic data and three real datasets (Last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC II).  
- Novelty: couples Hawkes processes with low-rank nuclear-norm regularization for time-sensitive recommendation, distinct from prior low-rank Hawkes and time-aware MF work.  
- Significance: could influence temporal recommendation modeling if convexity and scalability claims hold and empirical gains generalize.  
- Main theoretical concern: Theorem 1’s exact-penalty claim (finite ρ equivalence) is questionable—quadratic penalties are typically not exact and the threshold depends on unknown optima.  
- Convergence guarantees apply to the penalized problem; equivalence to the original constrained problem is not convincingly established.  
- Stability concern: Hawkes-process stability (branching ratio <1) is not discussed or enforced, risking supercritical processes and interpretation/estimation issues.  
- Modeling limitation: captures only self-excitation per pair and omits cross-excitation between items or intra-user activity dependencies.  
- Baselines: missing stronger time-aware recommenders (e.g., timeSVD++, time-varying MF) and point-process-based recommenders; tensor baselines use discretization and stronger temporal baselines are absent.  
- Return-time prediction: uses Ogata’s thinning for sampling at test time without analysis of test-time cost or broader calibration metrics (e.g., CRPS, QQ plots across sequences).  
- Practical details lacking: step-size/Lipschitz selection, hyperparameter tuning/validation protocol, and held-out protocol limited to unseen pairs (not true cold-start).  
- Clarity issues: typos, notational inconsistencies, and stray references detract from exposition.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled convex formulation, scalable solver with nontrivial convergence analysis, strong empirical scale and performance, and a potentially general optimization template.  
- Cons for acceptance: questionable penalty theorem, missing stability enforcement, limited comparisons and modeling of cross-excitation, and clarity/methodological detail gaps.  
- Suggested fixes: either prove Theorem 1 or reframe as a smoothed surrogate; consider augmented Lagrangian/ADMM variants avoiding full SVDs.  
- Suggested fixes: enforce or discuss Hawkes stability constraints and their practical implications.  
- Suggested fixes: include stronger temporal baselines and a mutually exciting Hawkes variant to evaluate cross-item excitation benefits.  
- Suggested fixes: add ablations on kernel choice and hyperparameters (ρ, λ, β), report test-time cost and calibration metrics, and improve notation/proofreading.
