This paper presents a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation by combining self-exciting point processes and low-rank models. The core idea is to model user-item interactions as a Hawkes process, leveraging its ability to capture temporal dependencies and then employ low-rank factorization to generalize across users and items. The authors address both the problem of recommending the most relevant item at the right moment and predicting the next returning time of a user. They propose a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm with a provable convergence rate.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a significant limitation of traditional recommendation systems that often ignore temporal dynamics. The connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank models is a novel contribution, offering a principled way to model and predict user behavior. The theoretical analysis of the optimization algorithm, including the convergence rate, is a significant strength. The experimental results on both synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach compared to state-of-the-art methods. The authors also acknowledge potential extensions, such as incorporating additional contextual information, further enhancing the model's applicability.  The related work section is reasonably thorough, citing relevant papers including Kapoor et al. [12, 11] and recent work on tensor factorization.

However, several aspects could be improved. While the theoretical analysis is a strong point, the explanation of the algorithm and the mathematical notation can be dense and difficult to follow for readers not already familiar with point processes and optimization techniques. A more intuitive explanation of the algorithm steps would be beneficial. The synthetic data experiments, while providing a controlled environment, lack detailed explanation of the data generation process.  More specifics regarding how the ground truth low-rank structure was enforced would improve clarity. Furthermore, the experimental setup on real datasets could benefit from a more detailed description of parameter tuning strategies and the baselines used. While comparisons are made to several baselines, a deeper discussion of the strengths and weaknesses of each baseline in the context of the specific datasets would be helpful. The presentation of results could be more visually appealing and easier to interpret; for example, error bars are missing in some plots. Finally, the discussion of the limitations of the model is brief. A more thorough analysis of potential failure cases and areas for future research would be valuable.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation is a new and promising approach.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The convex formulation and the provable convergence rate of the optimization algorithm are strong theoretical contributions.
*   **Empirical Performance:** The experimental results demonstrate superior performance compared to state-of-the-art methods on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
*   **Significance:** Addressing temporal dynamics in recommendation systems is an important problem with practical applications.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Clarity:** The mathematical notation and algorithmic explanation can be dense and challenging to follow.
*   **Experimental Detail:** The synthetic data generation process and the experimental setup on real datasets lack sufficient detail.
*   **Limited Discussion of Limitations:** A more thorough analysis of potential failure cases and future research directions is needed.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some areas for improvement in clarity and experimental detail, this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of recommender systems. The novelty, theoretical soundness, and empirical performance justify acceptance. The paper addresses an important problem with a principled and effective approach. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to improve clarity and provide more details regarding the experimental setup. The authors should consider elaborating on the limitations of their model and potential avenues for future research.
