- Proposes time-sensitive recommendation by modeling each user–item pair as a self-exciting Hawkes process with exponential kernel and low-rank structure on baseline and excitation matrices.  
- Enforces nuclear-norm regularization and nonnegativity to generalize to unobserved user–item pairs, yielding a convex estimation problem.  
- Introduces an auxiliary-variable quadratic-penalty reformulation and a block algorithm using proximal updates for nonnegativity and conditional gradient for nuclear-norm, claiming O(1/t) convergence.  
- Addresses two tasks: time-sensitive item ranking by instantaneous intensity and return-time prediction via sampling the next event time.  
- Empirical results on synthetic, last.fm, tmall, and MIMIC II datasets show improved ranking and MAE versus Poisson, SVD, tensor factorization, and STiC baselines.  
- Connects collaborative filtering/tensor recommendation literature with point-process/Hawkes modeling and related low-rank Hawkes work.  
- Modeling and convexity are technically sound given exponential kernels and linear parameterization; nonnegativity is appropriate for intensities.  
- The hybrid proximal/conditional-gradient optimization is reasonable and leverages efficient top-singular-vector oracles; overall O(1/k) rate is consistent.  
- Theorem 1 claiming exact equivalence for a finite quadratic penalty ρ* is delicate and needs either a softened claim or an approximation bound since ρ* is problem-dependent and impractical to verify.  
- Paper does not discuss enforcing Hawkes stability/branching ratio (α ∫γ < 1) or the impact of violations on prediction and sampling.  
- Algorithm appears scalable (only top singular vectors and simple projections), but wall-clock times and memory footprints vs. baselines are not reported.  
- Evaluation design (cold-start at pair level and within-sequence splits) is sensible, but ranking metrics like Recall@k/NDCG@k and statistical significance are missing.  
- Paper is mostly clear and well organized; derivations and pseudocode are helpful.  
- Clarify stability enforcement, efficient gradient computation for long histories, kernel bandwidth choice and sensitivity, and temper the penalty-equivalence claim in the main text.  
- The idea of low-rank Hawkes for user–item recommendation is novel relative to prior Hawkes network work.  
- Significance is high: addresses important time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction problems with a general, scalable approach.  
- Pros: principled Hawkes modeling, convex estimation, scalable algorithm with convergence guarantee, strong empirical gains, and applicability to multiple tasks.  
- Cons: questionable finite-ρ equivalence claim in Theorem 1, no explicit stability constraints, missing standard ranking metrics and significance reporting, limited comparisons to other continuous-time recommenders.  
- Suggestions/questions: report whether learned α satisfy stability, provide ablations for low-rank vs per-pair and self-excitation effects, add Recall@k/NDCG@k and variance estimates, analyze sensitivity to ρ and kernel σ and give guidance for choosing ρ, compare to additional survival/time-aware baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance pending clarification of the penalty equivalence, stability treatment, and expanded evaluation details.
