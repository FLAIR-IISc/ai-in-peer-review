- Jointly addresses ranking at the right moment and user return-time prediction using a unified model.  
- Models each user–item stream as a self-exciting Hawkes process with exponential kernel, coupling streams via low-rank nonnegative matrices Λ0 and A.  
- Estimation formulated as a convex program with nuclear-norm regularization and nonnegativity constraints.  
- Scalable solver: penalty reformulation with auxiliary variables and a blended proximal/conditional-gradient algorithm (nonnegative projection for smooth part, linear oracle/top singular vectors for nuclear norms).  
- Proven convergence: O(1/t) rate with an accelerated component for the smooth part.  
- Strong empirical performance on synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC-II), outperforming Poisson, STiC, SVD, and Poisson-tensor baselines.  
- Situates work among point-process modeling, multidimensional/low-rank Hawkes learning, and rank-minimization/conditional-gradient literature.  
- Strength: technically sound convex formulation since the parametric Hawkes log-likelihood is concave in nonnegative parameters.  
- Strength: algorithmic design is sensible and justified by a threshold ρ* in the penalty reformulation.  
- Weakness: threshold ρ* is problem-dependent and unknown; in practice ρ is tuned without certificates that the penalized and constrained optima coincide.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons omit stronger temporal models (mutual excitation across items/users, nonparametric kernels) and do not report time-prediction calibration.  
- Clarity: overall clear and well organized, but some notation is dense and algorithmic details (L, step sizes, per-iteration cost, memory, stopping criteria) need expansion.  
- Suggestion: add a brief discussion on identifiability between baseline Λ0 and excitation A.  
- Originality: novel connection of time-sensitive recommendation to low-rank Hawkes with a convex estimator and a notable proximal–CG algorithmic contribution versus ADMM.  
- Significance: valuable capability to jointly produce time-aware rankings and return-time predictions at scale with promising accuracy and scalability.  
- Pros: principled convex formulation, scalable algorithm with theoretical rate, strong empirical results across domains, and extensibility to context features.  
- Cons: reliance on tunable ρ without guarantees, limited baselines, restrictive modeling assumptions (per-pair self-excitation, exponential kernels), and gaps in complexity/reproducibility details.
