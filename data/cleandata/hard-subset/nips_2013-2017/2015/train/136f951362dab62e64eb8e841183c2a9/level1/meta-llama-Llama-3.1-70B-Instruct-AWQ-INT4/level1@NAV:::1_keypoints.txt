- Proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation and next-return-time prediction.  
- Introduces a convex formulation linking self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent user-item temporal patterns.  
- Paper is well written with clear background on temporal point processes and the low-rank Hawkes process model.  
- Provides a detailed optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods for efficient learning.  
- Strength: Novel contribution to time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Strength: Rigorous mathematical formulation and well-designed optimization (technical soundness).  
- Strength: Extensive empirical evaluation on synthetic and real data showing superior scalability and predictive performance.  
- Weakness: Related work review is not comprehensive enough for time-sensitive recommendation and temporal point processes.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion on interpretability (physical meaning of parameters and implications).  
- Weakness: Comparisons to state-of-the-art methods are incomplete.  
- Pro-accept: Novel, technically sound method with strong empirical results and potential impact.  
- Con-accept: Needs a more thorough literature review, improved interpretability discussion, and broader empirical comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the weaknesses.
