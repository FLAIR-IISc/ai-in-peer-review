- Novelty: Introduces a framework combining self-exciting (Hawkes) point processes with low-rank models to recommend the right item at the right time and predict user return times.
- Method: Proposes a convex optimization approach that leverages low-rank structure for scalable parameter estimation.
- Temporal modeling: Uses Hawkes processes to capture recurrent user activity and time-sensitive behavior.
- Scalability: Optimization blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods, achieving O(1/t) convergence and scaling to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Empirical performance: Demonstrates superior predictive accuracy on synthetic and real-world datasets (music streaming, e-commerce, medical records) versus baselines.
- Flexibility: Framework can incorporate contextual information (user profiles, spatial features) and generalizes to nonnegative matrix rank minimization problems.
- Strength — Technical novelty: Establishes a significant new connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models.
- Strength — Scalability: Convincing large-scale experiments show practical applicability.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Extensive experiments across diverse datasets support claims.
- Weakness — Clarity: Optimization algorithm and theoretical proofs are dense and need clearer exposition or visual aids.
- Weakness — Baseline comparisons: Lacks comparisons with some relevant methods (e.g., tensor factorization, recent temporal recommendation advances).
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (ρ, λ, β) and their impact on performance.
- Weakness — Limitations: Limited discussion of model assumptions (low-rank, exponential triggering kernels) and potential failure cases.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a timely problem, is theoretically sound, empirically strong, and practically scalable/flexible.
- Arguments against acceptance: Needs improved clarity and more comprehensive sensitivity/limitations analysis before publication.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance, with revisions to improve clarity and add parameter sensitivity/limitations discussion.
