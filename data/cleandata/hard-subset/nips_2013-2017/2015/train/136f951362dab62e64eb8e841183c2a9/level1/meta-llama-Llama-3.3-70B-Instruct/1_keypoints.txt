- Proposes a novel framework linking self-exciting Hawkes point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Models user-item consumption events as a Hawkes process and estimates parameters via convex optimization.  
- Develops an algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Scales to problems with millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and thorough model analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive on synthetic and real datasets and shows superior predictive performance versus baselines.  
- Provides detailed analysis of algorithm convergence and scalability.  
- Strengths: novel framework, efficient algorithm with optimal convergence, strong scalability, comprehensive experiments, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: assumes a specific triggering-kernel form that may not fit all applications.  
- Weaknesses: lacks detailed sensitivity analysis to hyperparameters.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of limitations and potential extensions.  
- Rating: 8/10; Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
