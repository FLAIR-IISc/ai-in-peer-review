- Proposes a novel framework combining self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal user-item patterns.
- Addresses two tasks: recommending the most relevant item at the right time and predicting users' next return time.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with a good problem framing and related work discussion.
- Technical derivation and model details are well-explained.
- Authors provide an efficient learning algorithm suitable for large-scale applications.
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets (Last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC II) support the claims.
- Method outperforms state-of-the-art baselines including Poisson process, STiC, SVD, and tensor factorization.
- Strength: clear novelty with potential to advance time-sensitive recommendation systems.
- Strength: technical soundness and thorough, convincing experimental evaluation.
- Strength: scalability due to an efficient parameter-learning algorithm.
- Weakness: model complexity limits interpretability of results and underlying dynamics.
- Weakness: no detailed analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters.
- Weakness: relies on the assumption that user behavior follows a self-exciting point process, which may not hold universally.
- Minor concern: the work may be too technical for some readers despite overall clarity.
- Overall scores high (9/10) for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; arguments for acceptance emphasize novelty and evaluation, while arguments for rejection note complexity, interpretability, and missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
