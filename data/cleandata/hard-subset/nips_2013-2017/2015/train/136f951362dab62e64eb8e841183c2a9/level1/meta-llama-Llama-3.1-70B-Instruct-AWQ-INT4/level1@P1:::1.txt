**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction of users to existing services. The authors connect self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture the recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs. They develop a convex optimization algorithm to learn the model parameters efficiently, achieving an optimal O(1/t) convergence rate. The algorithm is scalable to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events. Experimental results on synthetic and real datasets demonstrate the superior predictive performance of the proposed model compared to state-of-the-art competitors.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel framework**: The authors propose a novel connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture the recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs.
2. **Scalable algorithm**: The developed algorithm is efficient and scalable to large datasets, achieving an optimal O(1/t) convergence rate.
3. **Superior predictive performance**: Experimental results demonstrate the superior predictive performance of the proposed model compared to state-of-the-art competitors.
4. **Flexibility**: The model can be readily generalized to incorporate other contextual information, such as user profiles, textual features, and spatial information.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Assumptions**: The authors assume that the user-item consumption pairs follow a self-exciting Hawkes process, which might not always be the case in real-world scenarios.
2. **Model complexity**: The proposed model is more complex than some of the competing models, which might make it more challenging to interpret and understand.
3. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The authors mention that hyperparameter tuning is necessary to achieve good performance, which can be time-consuming and require significant expertise.
4. **Limited evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their model on two tasks: item recommendation and returning-time prediction. It would be interesting to see how the model performs on other tasks, such as rating prediction or user clustering.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models, which is a unique contribution to the field of recommender systems. The authors also develop an efficient and scalable algorithm to learn the model parameters, which is an important contribution to the field of optimization.

**Significance**

The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of recommender systems, which is the prediction of user behavior over time. The proposed model has the potential to improve the accuracy of recommender systems and provide more personalized recommendations to users. The paper also contributes to the field of optimization by proposing an efficient and scalable algorithm for learning the model parameters.
