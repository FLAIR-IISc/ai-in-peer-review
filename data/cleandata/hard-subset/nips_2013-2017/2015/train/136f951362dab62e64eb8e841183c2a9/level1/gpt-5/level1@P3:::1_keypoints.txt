- Proposes time-sensitive recommendation by modeling each user–item interaction stream as a self-exciting Hawkes process with intensity λu,i(t)=Λ0(u,i)+A(u,i)Σγ(t−tu,ij).  
- Imposes low-rank structure on Λ0 and A via nuclear-norm regularization to generalize to unseen pairs and times, yielding a convex estimation problem.  
- Introduces a hybrid optimization method combining proximal gradient for nonnegativity and conditional gradient (Frank–Wolfe) for nuclear norms via an auxiliary-variable penalty.  
- Proves an O(1/t) convergence rate and demonstrates scalability on large synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II).  
- Evaluates two tasks: item ranking at a given time using intensities and return-time prediction from the conditional time-to-event density.  
- Connects temporal point process (Hawkes) modeling with collaborative filtering and positions the formulation as a novel convex low-rank Hawkes estimation.  
- Strength: Clear modeling rationale—Λ0 for long-term preference and A for recency/self-excitation—with convex likelihood under nonnegativity.  
- Strength: Elegant, practical optimization scheme with an oracle using top singular vectors and a carefully stated convergence bound.  
- Strength: Large-scale synthetic evaluation and appropriate Q–Q time-change diagnostics on real data to justify Hawkes modeling.  
- Weakness: Model assumes independence across items—λu,i(t) uses only (u,i) history—ignoring cross-excitation, competition, and item-item influences.  
- Weakness: Stability and identifiability issues not addressed—no branching-ratio constraint, potential supercritical fits, and tradeoffs among A, Λ0, and kernel σ.  
- Weakness: Return-time evaluation uses MAE (not a proper scoring rule) and costly Monte Carlo (Ogata thinning) for expectations without runtime/variance reporting.  
- Weakness: Missing stronger baselines—dynamic Poisson/gamma-Poisson, timeSVD++, multivariate Hawkes—and missing standard ranking metrics like Recall@k/NDCG@k.  
- Weakness: Theoretical equivalence between constrained and penalized formulations depends on unknown ρ*; practical guidance on choosing ρ is lacking.  
- Weakness: Numerical and practical issues—Lipschitz gradient assumption may fail near zero intensities, need for ε-floor, and per-iteration top singular vector cost may be a bottleneck.  
- Clarity: Generally well written but contains notation/typo glitches and insufficient experimental protocol details (negatives, ranking computation, hyperparameters, kernel selection).  
- Originality: Combining Hawkes processes with low-rank recommendation in a convex formulation is novel; optimization relates to known proximal/conditional gradient variants.  
- Significance: Promising direction that could impact time-sensitive recommendation and retention modeling if empirical evaluation and modeling limitations are strengthened.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel principled formulation, scalable algorithm with theoretical guarantees, and addressing both ranking and return-time tasks.  
- Cons for acceptance: Modeling limitations (no cross-excitation, global kernel, no stability constraints), incomplete empirical comparisons and metrics, and missing practical/theoretical guidance on ρ.  
- Suggested improvements: Extend to low-rank multivariate Hawkes or analyze sufficiency of per-pair history; enforce stability constraints and discuss identifiability.  
- Suggested improvements: Use proper scoring rules (likelihood/CRPS) for return-time, reduce MC overhead (median/mode or direct likelihood), and report runtime/variance for MC estimates.  
- Suggested improvements: Add strong time-aware and point-process baselines, report standard ranking metrics, provide sensitivity analyses for ρ/λ/β and a continuation strategy for ρ.  
- Suggested improvements: Address numerical stability (ε-floor), give wall-clock times and hardware, fix notation/typos, and describe experimental protocol and hyperparameter choices explicitly.
