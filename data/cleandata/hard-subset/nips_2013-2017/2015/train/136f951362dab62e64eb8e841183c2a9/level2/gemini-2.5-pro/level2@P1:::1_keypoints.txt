- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework modeling user-item event streams as low-rank coupled Hawkes processes (baseline + self-excitation).  
- Connects Hawkes processes with collaborative filtering by assuming low-rank structure on baseline and excitation parameter matrices.  
- Formulates parameter estimation as a convex log-likelihood maximization with nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Introduces a hybrid optimization algorithm (proximal + conditional gradient) with theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Uses the model for two tasks: ranking items by current intensity (recommendation) and predicting users' next return times.  
- Novel and significant: moves beyond “what” to “when” in recommendations and could inspire new research directions.  
- Elegant modeling: Hawkes processes capture long-term preference and short-term bursty behavior more realistically than Poisson or independent-event models.  
- Strong technical contribution: well-posed convex formulation and a new solver that handles non-negativity and nuclear-norm constraints.  
- Thorough experiments: synthetic + three real datasets, comparisons to baselines, and goodness-of-fit checks via the time-change theorem.  
- Scalability concern: conditional gradient step requires top singular vectors of an m×n gradient, potentially prohibitive for very large user/item counts.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: multiple tuning parameters (λ, β, ρ, σ) with limited guidance; practical setting of ρ vs. theoretical ρ* is unclear.  
- Kernel limitation: experiments use only an exponential kernel; more complex temporal patterns (e.g., periodicity) are not explored.  
- Learning limitation: trained only on observed positive events, unclear performance when discriminating positives from many negatives (standard ranking/negative sampling scenarios).  
- Overall: high-quality, original work with solid theory and empirical support; recommended for acceptance.
