- Proposes a novel framework connecting Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Targets two problems: recommending the right item at the right moment and predicting a user’s next return time.  
- Models user activity as a Hawkes process with low-rank parameters to enable knowledge transfer across user-item pairs.  
- Presents a convex optimization formulation and an efficient algorithm with provable O(1/t) convergence.  
- Designed to scale to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs, hundreds of millions of events).  
- Shows superior empirical performance on synthetic and real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II).  
- Includes useful ablation studies (e.g., Tensor-#-Last vs Tensor-#-Avg) to analyze component impacts.  
- Novel relative to prior work (e.g., Kapoor et al.) because it can generalize to recommending new items.  
- Differs from prior temporal recommendation work by directly modeling event sequences with point processes.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presented.  
- Adequately situates and differentiates itself from related literature.  
- Weakness: exponential kernel choice is not fully justified; alternatives (Weibull, log‑logistic) not discussed.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of parameter sensitivity (ρ, λ, β) and practical guidelines for tuning.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons to recent state-of-the-art temporal/attention-based recommendation methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient dataset details (sparsity, average events per user) for result interpretation.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of practical deployment challenges (cold-start, online computation costs).  
- Minor typo: “The unit time for both dataset is hour” → “datasets.”  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong theoretical guarantees, and compelling experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited SOTA comparisons, missing sensitivity/practical discussions, and weak kernel justification.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper worth accepting if authors address parameter sensitivity, broader comparisons, practical considerations, and kernel justification.
