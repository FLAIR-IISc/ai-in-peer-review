This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction by connecting self-exciting point processes and low-rank models. The authors address two key questions: how to recommend the most relevant item at the right moment and how to accurately predict the next returning time of users to existing services. They develop a convex formulation and an efficient learning algorithm to solve the low-rank point process estimation problem efficiently.

**Claims:**

1. The proposed framework can effectively capture the recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs.
2. The authors' algorithm can efficiently learn the model parameters and achieve a good estimation performance with a modest number of observed entries.
3. The proposed model can outperform state-of-the-art competitors on both synthetic and real datasets in terms of predictive performance.

**Support for Claims:**

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their framework and algorithm. They also conduct extensive experiments on both synthetic and real datasets to evaluate the performance of their model. The results show that their algorithm converges fast and achieves a good estimation performance with a modest number of observed entries. Moreover, their model outperforms state-of-the-art competitors on both synthetic and real datasets in terms of predictive performance.

**Usefulness:**

The proposed framework and algorithm have practical applications in various domains, such as e-commerce, music streaming, and healthcare. The ability to recommend relevant items at the right moment and predict the next returning time of users can improve user engagement and satisfaction.

**Field Knowledge:**

The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the relevant literature in the field, including point processes, Hawkes processes, and low-rank models. They also provide a clear and concise explanation of the technical details of their framework and algorithm.

**Novelty:**

The authors propose a novel connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models, which is a significant improvement over existing approaches. Their algorithm is also efficient and scalable, making it suitable for large-scale applications.

**Completeness:**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility, including the formulation of the problem, the algorithm, and the experimental setup.

**Limitations:**

The authors acknowledge that their model assumes a simple exponential form for the triggering kernel, which may not be suitable for all applications. They also mention that their algorithm requires a modest number of observed entries to achieve a good estimation performance.

**Constructive Criticism:**

One potential limitation of the paper is that the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their algorithm. They also do not compare their model with other state-of-the-art models that use different techniques, such as deep learning.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

Pro:

* The paper proposes a novel and efficient framework for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis and experimental evaluation of their model.
* The proposed algorithm is scalable and suitable for large-scale applications.

Con:

* The paper assumes a simple exponential form for the triggering kernel, which may not be suitable for all applications.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their algorithm.
* The paper does not compare their model with other state-of-the-art models that use different techniques.

Overall, I believe that the paper is well-written and provides a significant contribution to the field of recommendation systems. The authors' proposed framework and algorithm are novel, efficient, and scalable, making them suitable for large-scale applications. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the algorithm and a comparison with other state-of-the-art models.
