- Paper models user–item interaction streams as per-pair self-exciting Hawkes processes with intensity λu,i(t)=Λ0(u,i)+A(u,i)∑jγ(t−tu,ij) to support time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Λ0 and A are given low-rank structure via nuclear-norm regularization, yielding a convex negative log-likelihood estimation under nonnegativity.  
- Authors propose a scalable algorithm combining proximal gradient (nonnegativity) and conditional gradient (nuclear norms) via penalty splitting and prove O(1/t) convergence.  
- Empirical evaluation on large synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) shows strong gains on ranking-at-time and return-time prediction versus Poisson, tensor factorization, and survival baselines.  
- Time-change diagnostic indicates Hawkes better fits real sequences than survival-model baselines.  
- Connects temporal point processes (Hawkes) with low-rank recommendation and contrasts to tensor/Poisson and survival/HMM methods.  
- Clear problem motivation and unified framework for both item ranking and return-time prediction.  
- Convex formulation with principled, scalable algorithm and practical convergence guarantee.  
- Large-scale, multi-domain empirical validation with meaningful diagnostics.  
- Demonstrates improvements over strong baselines and discusses flexible extensions (context, tensorization, nonparametric kernels).  
- Does not formally address Hawkes stability/regularity (risk of explosion); should enforce or monitor A(u,i)∫γ<1 or similar constraints.  
- Potential identifiability issues between Λ0 and A (trade-off between base rate and excitation) lacking diagnostic analysis.  
- Equivalence theorem for penalty formulation requires ρ>ρ* but ρ* is unknown; practical guidance or continuation schedules are missing.  
- LMO needs top singular vectors of m×n gradient matrices; runtime/memory breakdown and comparisons to ADMM or proximal-SVD are absent.  
- Model omits cross-item and cross-user mutual excitation; this may limit realism for music/shopping domains.  
- Recommendation evaluation limited to rank of true item; should report recall@k, NDCG@k and calibration/coverage for return-time predictions.  
- Cold-start performance not evaluated despite claiming context incorporation potential.  
- Formulation and optimization appear technically sound, but some implementation/complexity details (Lipschitz constant, step sizes, Ogata sampling cost, memory footprint) are missing.  
- Writing and organization are generally clear; suggest clarifying stability constraints, per-iteration costs, kernel ψ derivation, and hyperparameter tuning.  
- The coupling of per-pair Hawkes with low-rank transfer and the specific proximal/conditional-gradient blend is a novel contribution.  
- Addressing an important problem with likely impact on continuous-time recommendation and temporal modeling.  
- Reviewer questions: how is Hawkes stability ensured/checked, how is L chosen, runtime vs ADMM/proximal-SVD and GPU use, Ogata sampling cost, recall@k/cold-start results, and experiments with alternative kernels?  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, convex, scalable model with strong empirical gains and theoretical optimization guarantees.  
- Cons against acceptance: insufficient treatment of stability/identifiability, incomplete evaluation (metrics, cross-excitation, cold-start), and weak practical guidance on penalty and runtime.  
- Overall: technically solid and well-motivated paper that merits acceptance pending addressing stability and expanded evaluation concerns.
