- Proposes a novel framework linking Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank matrix models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Formulates the problem as a convex optimization and develops an efficient algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees (Theorem 2) for the proposed optimization algorithm.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic and real-world datasets (Last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC II).  
- Outperforms several strong baselines (Poisson, STiC, SVD, tensor factorization) on ranking and return-time tasks.  
- Shows originality by bridging temporal point processes and low-rank matrix factorization for recommendations.  
- Situates the work well in related literature and distinguishes it from survival-analysis approaches like Kapoor et al.  
- Highlights practical significance for applications (music streaming, e-commerce, healthcare) and ability to incorporate contextual features.  
- Strengths: solid theoretical foundation, comprehensive experiments, clear formulation, well-motivated novelty, and practical relevance.  
- Weakness: lacks explicit discussion of model limitations and sensitivity to triggering kernel parameter choices.  
- Weakness: could better explain fundamental differences from Poisson/traditional temporal recommendation methods.  
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability trade-offs require more detailed analysis.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient technical detail for reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution that meets NIPS standards and is a compelling candidate for acceptance; authors should address noted limitations in revision.
