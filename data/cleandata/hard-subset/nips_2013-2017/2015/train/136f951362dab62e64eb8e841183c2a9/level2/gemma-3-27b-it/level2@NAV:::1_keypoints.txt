- Proposes a novel framework combining Hawkes (self-exciting point) processes with low-rank factorization for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Leverages Hawkes processes to model user activity recurrence and low-rank models for scalability, addressing temporal dynamics and data sparsity.  
- Clearly states limitations of existing methods in handling temporal behavior and sparsity, motivating the work.  
- Presents a convex problem formulation and an optimization algorithm with an O(1/t) convergence rate and claimed scalability.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) show superior performance versus baselines like STiC and tensor factorization.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and suggest extensions such as incorporating additional contextual information.  
- Baseline comparisons (e.g., tensor factorization) are described only cursory and need more implementation/detail reporting.  
- Real-world dataset descriptions lack depth on preprocessing, parameter tuning, and dataset-specific characteristics affecting results.  
- Convergence analysis is mathematically sound but needs more intuitive explanation of constants and their practical impact.  
- Connection to broader Hawkes-process literature is limited to citations and brief differences; recent scalable Hawkes estimation work is under-discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model combination, strong empirical results, scalable algorithm with theoretical guarantees, and clear motivation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient baseline and dataset detail, less accessible convergence discussion, and limited contextualization in Hawkes literature.  
- Overall recommendation: solid paper meriting acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen baselines, experimental details, and literature/contextual discussion.
