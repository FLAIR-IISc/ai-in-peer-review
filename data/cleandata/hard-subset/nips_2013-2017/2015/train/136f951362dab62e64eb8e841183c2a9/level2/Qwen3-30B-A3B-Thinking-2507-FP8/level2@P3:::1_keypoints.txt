- Proposes a novel framework connecting Hawkes self-exciting point processes with low-rank matrix models for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Targets two tasks: recommending the most relevant item at the right time and predicting users' next return time.  
- Formulates the problem as a convex optimization with nuclear norm regularization.  
- Develops an efficient algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.  
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs, hundreds of millions of events).  
- Evaluates on synthetic and real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) and outperforms baselines including Poisson, SVD, tensor factorization, and STiC.  
- Novelty: meaningful, previously overlooked integration of Hawkes processes with low-rank recommendation models.  
- Differentiates from prior work (e.g., Kapoor et al.) by enabling generalization to new items.  
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous convergence analysis (Theorem 2) proving optimal convergence rates.  
- Empirical contribution: comprehensive experiments, multiple metrics (item rank, return-time error), and informative ablation studies.  
- Clarity: well-structured, clear background on temporal point processes, and precise mathematical exposition.  
- Discusses potential to incorporate contextual information (user profiles, spatial features) as a basis for extensions.  
- Technical innovation: convex formulation and optimization algorithm advance beyond previous approaches.  
- Significance: applicable to e-commerce, music streaming, and healthcare; return-time prediction aids marketing and proactive interventions.  
- Practical relevance: scalability claims make the approach suitable for real-world deployment.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and empirical analysis of computational runtime and complexity.  
- Weakness: did not empirically demonstrate incorporation of contextual information.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, high-quality, significant contributions, and suitable for publication at NIPS.
