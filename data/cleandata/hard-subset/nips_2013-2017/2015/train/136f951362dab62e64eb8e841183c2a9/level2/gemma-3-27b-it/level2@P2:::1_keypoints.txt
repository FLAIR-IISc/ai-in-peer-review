- Proposes combining Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank factorization for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Models user-item interactions as a Hawkes process to capture temporal dependencies and uses low-rank structure to generalize across users/items.  
- Presents a convex problem formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm that mixes proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.  
- Provides a provable convergence rate (O(1/t)) for the algorithm, though derivation details are sparse.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic and real datasets, scaling to millions of user-item pairs.  
- Well-motivated contribution addressing temporal dynamics overlooked by traditional recommenders.  
- Novel connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank models is a significant methodological contribution.  
- Experimental comparisons against relevant baselines are thorough but lack deeper behavioral analysis.  
- Missing visualizations or ablation studies to isolate contributions of the self-exciting vs. low-rank components.  
- Theoretical convergence analysis references prior work and omits full derivation details, reducing clarity.  
- Related work coverage is adequate but should include dynamic graph embeddings and sequential recommenders (e.g., GRU4Rec, SASRec) and broader temporal point process literature.  
- Notation is dense and some algorithmic parameter choices (ρ, λ, β) lack transparent explanation, hindering reproducibility.  
- Writing is generally good but contains some overly complex sentences; clarity could be improved with a glossary and simpler exposition.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with acceptance suggested, contingent on improving theoretical clarity, experimental ablations, and related-work positioning.
