- Tackles time-sensitive recommendation: ranking items at the right moment and predicting user return times via Hawkes processes with low-rank user–item structure.  
- Proposes a convex formulation using nuclear-norm regularization on base intensities and self-excitation parameters.  
- Designs a scalable solver combining proximal gradient for nonnegativity and conditional gradient for nuclear norms, with O(1/t) convergence and per-iteration costs avoiding full SVDs.  
- Empirical evaluation on large synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC-II) shows improvements over Poisson, SVD, STiC, and Poisson tensor factorization.  
- Demonstrates gains on time-sensitive ranking of ground-truth items and on return-time MAE.  
- Situates work relative to prior temporal point process and matrix/tensor factorization literature; novelty is convex low-rank Hawkes modeling with a tailored optimizer.  
- Modeling choices are technically sound; likelihood and convex relaxation are standard and correctly applied.  
- Theoretical claims (equivalence under penalty and convergence) are plausible, but the penalty threshold ρ* is not made operational.  
- Experiments are extensive and suggest scalability and accuracy gains, but lack statistical significance tests and uncertainty estimates.  
- Missing ablations and sensitivity analyses (kernel choices, rank sensitivity, σ, ρ) to justify design decisions.  
- Some clarity issues: dense notation and inconsistent claims switching between O(1/t) and O(1/t^2) — needs clarification.  
- Practical guidance for hyperparameter selection (ρ, λ, β, step sizes, bandwidth) is limited.  
- Suggest extending model beyond per user–item self-excitation to mutual excitation or shared kernels.  
- Recommend reporting variance/confidence, calibration of return-time predictions, and formal statistical tests.  
- Ask for sensitivity analyses and ablations comparing nuclear-norm vs factorization and kernel choices.  
- Suggest comparisons to stronger contemporary TPP baselines (nonparametric Hawkes, learned kernels).  
- Request more details on memory/throughput and sharing code for reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear, relevant problem, convincing scalable formulation, and solid empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited analysis of design choices and statistical rigor, missing comparisons to recent TPP methods, and sparse reproducibility/hyperparameter guidance.
