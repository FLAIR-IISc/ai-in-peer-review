This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction by connecting self-exciting point processes and low-rank models. The authors provide a convex formulation of the problems and develop an efficient algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods to solve the low-rank point process estimation problem efficiently. The algorithm achieves the optimal O(1/t) convergence rate and scales up to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their framework, including the underlying theory and mathematical formulations. The experimental evaluation on both synthetic and real datasets demonstrates the superior scalability and predictive performance of the proposed method compared to state-of-the-art competitors.

The strengths of the paper include:

* A novel and well-motivated framework for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction.
* A convex formulation of the problems that allows for efficient optimization.
* An efficient algorithm that achieves the optimal O(1/t) convergence rate and scales up to large datasets.
* A thorough experimental evaluation on both synthetic and real datasets.

However, there are some weaknesses and potential areas for improvement:

* The paper assumes that the triggering kernel has an exponential form, which might not be the case in all scenarios. It would be beneficial to explore other forms of the triggering kernel and their impact on the performance of the algorithm.
* The authors mention that the model can be generalized to incorporate other contextual information, such as user-profiles and item-contents. However, they do not provide any experimental evaluation of this claim.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including a discussion of their strengths and weaknesses.

Overall, I believe that this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of recommender systems and time-sensitive recommendation. The proposed framework and algorithm are well-motivated, and the experimental evaluation demonstrates their effectiveness.

Arguments pro acceptance:
