**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction using self-exciting point processes and low-rank models. The framework captures the recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs and addresses two central questions: (1) how to recommend the most relevant item at the right moment, and (2) how to accurately predict the next returning time of users to existing services. The authors develop an efficient algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods to solve the low-rank point process estimation problem efficiently. The algorithm achieves the optimal O(1/t) convergence rate and scales up to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events. The framework is evaluated on both synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating its superior scalability and predictive performance.

**Strengths**

2. **Efficient algorithm**: The authors develop an efficient algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods to solve the low-rank point process estimation problem efficiently.
3. **Scalability**: The algorithm scales up to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events, making it suitable for large-scale applications.
4. **Superior predictive performance**: The framework demonstrates superior predictive performance on both synthetic and real datasets, outperforming state-of-the-art competitors.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity**: The framework and algorithm may be complex to implement and understand, requiring a strong background in point processes and optimization techniques.
2. **Assumptions**: The framework assumes that the user-item consumption pairs follow a self-exciting point process, which may not always be the case in real-world applications.
3. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The algorithm requires tuning of hyperparameters, such as the penalty parameter ρ, which can be time-consuming and may require expertise.
