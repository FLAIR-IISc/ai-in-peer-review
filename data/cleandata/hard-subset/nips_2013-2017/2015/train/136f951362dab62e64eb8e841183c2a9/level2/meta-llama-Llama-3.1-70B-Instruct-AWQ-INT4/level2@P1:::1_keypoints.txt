- Proposes a novel framework linking self-exciting point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Provides a convex formulation enabling efficient optimization.  
- Develops an efficient algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.  
- Algorithm achieves optimal O(1/t) convergence and scales to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Paper is well-written with clear theoretical and mathematical exposition.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data show superior scalability and predictive performance over competitors.  
- Strength: well-motivated framework addressing time sensitivity in recommendations.  
- Strength: convexity and algorithmic efficiency with provable convergence.  
- Weakness: assumes an exponential triggering kernel, which may not hold universally.  
- Weakness: claims about generalizing to incorporate contextual information are not empirically validated.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison and discussion against other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; overall significant contribution to recommender systems.  
- Minor revisions: discuss triggering-kernel assumptions and their impact, include more detailed comparisons to other methods, and evaluate incorporation of contextual information.  
- Rating: 8/10.
