- Proposes a novel framework that connects self-exciting (Hawkes) point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption data.
- Targets two key problems: recommending the most relevant item at the right moment and predicting users' next returning time.
- Main technical contributions: a convex optimization formulation combining Hawkes processes with low-rank constraints.
- Introduces an efficient algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.
- Provides theoretical convergence analysis proving O(1/t) convergence rates (Theorem 2).
- Paper is well-organized and gives a clear background on temporal point processes for non-expert readers.
- The connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models is novel, well-motivated, and addresses limitations of prior work (e.g., Kapoor et al.).
- Convex formulation (Equation 4) is theoretically sound.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, using synthetic and real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) and multiple baselines.
- Results show clear improvements over baselines for item recommendation and return-time prediction; Hawkes model better captures temporal dynamics.
- Framework is flexible and can incorporate additional contextual information (user profiles, spatial features), increasing applicability.
- Limited discussion on how to implement or quantify benefits of incorporating additional contextual information; needs a concrete example.
- Does not adequately address cold-start scenarios for new users or items, relevant for next-return prediction.
- Evaluation metrics need clearer explanation (e.g., how "predicted rank" is aggregated across items/users).
- Comparison with related temporal recommendation work could be more detailed (missing discussion of papers like Xiong et al.).
- Minor suggestion: include explicit computational complexity comparisons for large-scale datasets despite claims of scalability.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, theoretically grounded paper with thorough experiments; recommendation for acceptance.
