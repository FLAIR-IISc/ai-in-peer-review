- Proposes a novel framework linking Hawkes self-exciting point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation, modeling user-item interactions to enable knowledge transfer.  
- Models interactions as a Hawkes process with low-rank parameterization for efficient optimization and generalization to new items.  
- Develops a convex formulation and an optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees with an established O(1/t) rate.  
- Empirically outperforms several baselines on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Demonstrates scalability to large-scale datasets and the ability to incorporate contextual information.  
- Addresses an important gap by explicitly modeling temporal dynamics and recurrent user behavior patterns.  
- Technically sound with significant contributions in formulation, algorithm design, and theory.  
- Needs deeper comparison with Kapoor et al. and clearer explanation of how this method overcomes their limitations.  
- Should compare more thoroughly with RNN-based sequence modeling approaches common in recommendation.  
- Justification for choosing the exponential kernel is limited; sensitivity to other kernels should be explored.  
- Experimental details on hyperparameter tuning and model selection (ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth, rank) are brief and need expansion for reproducibility.  
- Results presentation should include statistical significance tests (e.g., p-values) to strengthen claims.  
- Dataset descriptions lack detail on characteristics such as sparsity and event-time distributions.  
- Some sections are dense with mathematical notation; more intuitive explanations and consistent notation are needed.  
- Originality: novel combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank models plus original optimization and theoretical analysis.  
- Significance: likely to improve recommendation performance in applications like mobile services, ad bidding, and EHRs.  
- Overall recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address related-work comparisons, kernel justification, experimental details, and readability.
