- Proposes a novel framework connecting self-exciting point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Targets two problems: recommending the most relevant item at the right moment and predicting users' next returning time.
- Develops a convex formulation and an efficient learning algorithm for low-rank point process estimation.
- Claim: framework effectively captures recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs.
- Claim: algorithm can efficiently learn parameters and perform well with a modest number of observed entries.
- Claim: model outperforms state-of-the-art competitors on synthetic and real datasets for predictive performance.
- Support: provides thorough theoretical analysis of the framework and algorithm.
- Support: extensive experiments show fast convergence and good estimation with modest data.
- Usefulness: applicable to domains like e-commerce, music streaming, and healthcare to improve engagement.
- Field knowledge: authors demonstrate strong familiarity with literature (point processes, Hawkes, low-rank) and clear technical exposition.
- Novelty: introduces a new connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models and presents an efficient, scalable algorithm.
- Completeness: sufficient methodological and experimental details for reproducibility.
- Limitation: assumes a simple exponential triggering kernel that may not fit all applications.
- Limitation: requires a modest number of observed entries to achieve good estimation performance.
- Criticism: lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the algorithm.
- Criticism: does not compare against other state-of-the-art methods using different techniques (e.g., deep learning).
- Pro acceptance: novel, efficient, theoretically sound, experimentally validated, and scalable.
- Con acceptance: kernel assumption, missing complexity analysis, and limited comparative baselines.
- Overall: well-written, significant contribution but would benefit from complexity analysis and broader comparisons.
