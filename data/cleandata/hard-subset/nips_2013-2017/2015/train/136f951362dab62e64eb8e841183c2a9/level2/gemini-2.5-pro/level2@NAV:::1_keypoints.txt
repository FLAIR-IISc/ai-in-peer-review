- Proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendations that also predicts user return times.  
- Models user-item interaction sequences as self-exciting Hawkes processes.  
- Assumes low-rank baseline intensity and self-excitation matrices to generalize across sparse user-item data.  
- Connects point-process modeling with collaborative filtering and extends temporal recommendation literature by enabling recommendations of new items.  
- Original and technically strong: combines Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix factorization.  
- Presents a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm with formal convergence analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: model outperforms tensor and classic matrix factorization baselines on three real-world datasets.  
- Uses the time-change theorem to validate the Hawkes process assumption on real data.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of inference-time computational complexity and scalability for large item catalogs.  
- Suggests adding candidate-generation or approximation strategies to address scalability.  
- Weakness: experiments use a parametric exponential triggering kernel; discussion of non-parametric kernels would be beneficial.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, theoretically sound, empirically effective paper; strong accept recommendation.
