- Addresses time-sensitive recommendation: what to recommend now and when a user will return, using continuous-time modeling.
- Proposes Low Rank Hawkes Process: per user-item Hawkes processes with intensities from baseline preferences plus self-excitation.
- Enforces low-rank structure on baseline and excitation matrices to generalize across sparse user-item pairs and predict unobserved entries.
- Learns parameters via convex maximum likelihood with nuclear-norm regularization and non-negativity constraints.
- Introduces a custom hybrid proximal–conditional-gradient optimization algorithm with theoretical O(1/t) convergence.
- Empirically validated on one synthetic and three large real-world datasets for item ranking and return-time prediction.
- Demonstrates superior performance versus baselines (static MF, tensor factorization, other temporal models) and goodness-of-fit checks using time-change theorem.
- Strength: novel and significant unification of collaborative filtering and temporal point processes addressing both “what” and “when.”
- Strength: technically rigorous formulation, tailored scalable algorithm, and formal convergence analysis (Theorem 2).
- Strength: clear writing, logical development, and comprehensive experimental setup.
- Weakness: limited reproducibility details—hyperparameter tuning ranges, validation strategy, and sensitivity not fully reported.
- Weakness: reliance on penalty parameter ρ (equivalence result needs ρ sufficiently large); sensitivity analysis and more intuition on accelerated updates would help.
- Weakness: lacks explicit comparison of computational cost/training time versus baselines to assess practical trade-offs.
- Weakness: extensions to include contextual features are only briefly mentioned; a concrete formulation sketch would strengthen claims.
- Recommendation rationale: strong novelty, solid theory, and compelling empirical gains argue for acceptance; minor issues around hyperparameter/compute transparency are addressable.
