This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction of users to existing services. The authors connect self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture the recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs. The proposed model is estimated via a convex optimization problem, and an efficient algorithm is developed to solve the low-rank point process estimation problem.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem, related work, and the proposed model. The mathematical formulation is well-presented, and the optimization algorithm is clearly explained.

The paper has several strengths:

1. The authors address a relevant and timely problem in the field of recommender systems. The proposed model is novel and has the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction.
2. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed model, including its mathematical formulation, optimization algorithm, and convergence analysis.
3. The authors evaluate the proposed model on both synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating its superior scalability and predictive performance compared to state-of-the-art competitors.

However, there are some minor issues that need to be addressed:

1. The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the related work, particularly the differences between the proposed model and existing approaches.
2. The authors could provide more insights into the interpretation of the results, particularly the implications of the proposed model for real-world applications.
3. Some of the figures and tables could be improved for better clarity and readability.

Overall, this is a well-written and well-structured paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of recommender systems. The proposed model has the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the model and its evaluation on both synthetic and real datasets.
