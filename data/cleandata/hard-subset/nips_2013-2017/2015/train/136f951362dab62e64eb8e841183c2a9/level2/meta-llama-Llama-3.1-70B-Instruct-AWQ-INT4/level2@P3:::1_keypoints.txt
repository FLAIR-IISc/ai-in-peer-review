- Proposes a novel framework combining self-exciting point processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Develops an efficient algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Scales to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events.  
- Demonstrates superior predictive performance on synthetic and real datasets, outperforming state-of-the-art methods.  
- Framework and algorithm may be complex to implement and require strong background in point processes and optimization.  
- Assumes user-item consumption follows a self-exciting point process, which may not hold in all real-world scenarios.  
- Requires time-consuming hyperparameter tuning (e.g., penalty parameter ρ).  
- Originality: novel combination of self-exciting point processes with low-rank modeling.  
- Significance: addresses timing and return-time prediction, with potential to improve recommender systems.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear mathematical and optimization explanations.  
- Related work: situates contribution relative to survival analysis, hidden semi-Markov models, and tensor factorization, and highlights their limitations.  
- Future work: incorporate additional context information (user profiles, item attributes).  
- Future work: extend the framework to other applications (e.g., next purchase-time prediction).  
- Future work: further improve algorithm scalability for even larger datasets.  
- Conclusion: method shows strong empirical performance but may be complex and sensitive to hyperparameter tuning.
