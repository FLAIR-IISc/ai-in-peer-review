- Models time-sensitive recommendation with per (user,item) Hawkes processes and shares strength via low-rank (nuclear-norm) constraints on base intensities Λ0 and excitation A.
- Uses exponential Hawkes kernel with global decay σ and formulates convex maximum-likelihood estimation under nonnegativity and nuclear-norm regularization.
- Introduces auxiliary variables Z1,Z2 with a quadratic penalty to avoid full SVDs and ties them to (Λ0,A).
- Proposes a hybrid optimization: projected (accelerated) proximal gradient on (Λ0,A) and conditional gradient (Frank–Wolfe with rank-1 LMOs) on (Z1,Z2).
- Provides a global O(1/t) convergence rate and per-iteration cost proportional to number of events plus two top singular-vector computations.
- Empirical evaluation on large synthetic data (~100M events) and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) shows improved item ranking and return-time prediction vs several baselines.
- Includes time-rescaling (QQ) diagnostics indicating Hawkes fits better than survival models in their experiments.
- Relates to classical Hawkes and recent kernel-learning work, applying these to recommendation with low-rank parameter sharing.
- Optimization builds on proximal and conditional-gradient literature, adapting methods to a block-structured composite objective with nuclear norms and nonnegativity.
- Strength: clear unified formulation linking continuous-time point processes with collaborative low-rank modeling and a convex estimator.
- Strength: scalable algorithmic design that avoids full SVDs and empirically scales to millions of pairs and 100M events.
- Strength: solid empirical setup including held-out-item setting and QQ diagnostics for model fit.
- Strength: theoretical convergence analysis for the hybrid scheme with practical per-iteration complexity.
- Weakness: Theorem 1’s claim of exact equivalence for finite penalty ρ is questionable and lacks precise conditions; ρ* depends on unknowns.
- Weakness: model only captures self-excitation within pairs, omitting cross-item excitation and exogenous periodicities (time-varying Λ0).
- Weakness: global σ may be restrictive; sensitivity or learned decay parameters are not explored.
- Weakness: baseline comparisons lack detail (e.g., tensor discretization tuning) and additional temporal baselines are missing.
- Weakness: Hawkes stability/branching-ratio conditions are not discussed or enforced.
- Weakness: practical details for return-time Monte Carlo (Ogata) — sample counts, runtimes, variance — and reproducibility (code, hyperparameters, stopping) are sparse.
- Quality: technically sound overall, but the exact-penalty theoretical claim is the weakest point and should be revised or qualified.
- Clarity: paper is readable but has minor notation inconsistencies/typos; complexity per iteration and Monte Carlo sample counts should be specified.
- Originality: novel combination of Hawkes continuous-time modeling with low-rank sharing for recommendation; optimization synthesis is reasonable but not radically new.
- Significance: addresses an important problem with a likely useful, scalable approach; broader impact could increase by modeling exogenous rhythms and cross-item effects.
- Suggestions: clarify or reframe Theorem 1 (finite-ρ conditions), discuss/ensure Hawkes stability, add σ and rank ablations and a time-of-day baseline feature.
- Suggestions: report quantitative metrics with variances, wall-clock runtimes, number of Ogata samples; expand baseline tuning/details and consider renewal/Cox/Hawkes-grouped baselines.
- Pros for acceptance: novel, convex framework; practical scalable algorithm with theoretical rate; strong empirical performance at scale.
- Cons for acceptance: questionable exact-penalty claim, limited exogenous/cross-item modeling, and sparse baseline/tuning/reproducibility details.
