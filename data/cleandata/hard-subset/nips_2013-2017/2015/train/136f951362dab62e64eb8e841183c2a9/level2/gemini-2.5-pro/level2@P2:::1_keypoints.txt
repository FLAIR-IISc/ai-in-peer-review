- Introduces a time-sensitive recommendation framework modeling user-item interactions as self-exciting Hawkes processes with base intensity and self-excitation strength.  
- Organizes Hawkes parameters into low-rank user/item matrices to handle sparsity and enable generalization.  
- Formulates parameter estimation as a convex optimization problem with nuclear norm regularization.  
- Proposes a novel hybrid optimization algorithm (proximal + conditional gradient) to handle non-negativity and non-smooth nuclear norm, with a proven convergence guarantee.  
- Evaluates on synthetic and three large real-world datasets (music, e-commerce, healthcare), showing superior recommendation and return-time prediction performance.  
- Empirical validation includes appropriate strong baselines, time-change theorem tests showing Hawkes fits better than Poisson/survival models, and scalability experiments.  
- Strength: novel and significant synthesis of temporal point processes and collaborative filtering addressing dynamic user engagement.  
- Strength: strong technical contribution with convex formulation, clever alternative problem, efficient algorithm, and theoretical convergence (Theorem 2).  
- Strength: thorough, convincing empirical evaluation across datasets and tasks.  
- Weakness: clarity/reproducibility — algorithmic details (e.g., explicit gradients) and sensitivity to the tuned threshold ρ* are under-specified.  
- Weakness: model assumptions — experiments use only an exponential kernel and only self-excitation (no mutual-excitation); impact of more flexible kernels or mutual effects is not explored.  
- Weakness: comparison details — tensor baseline discretization choices and tuning are not fully described, which could affect baseline fairness.  
- Overall: excellent, technically sound paper with minor issues; strongly recommended for acceptance.
