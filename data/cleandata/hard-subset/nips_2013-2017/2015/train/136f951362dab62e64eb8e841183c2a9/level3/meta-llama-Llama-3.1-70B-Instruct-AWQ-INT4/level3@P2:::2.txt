This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction by combining self-exciting point processes and low-rank models. The authors address two central questions: how to recommend the most desirable item at the right moment, and how to predict the next returning time of a user to a service. The proposed model is based on Hawkes processes, which are a type of point process that can capture the temporal dependencies between events. The authors also develop an efficient algorithm for learning the model parameters, which blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.

The paper is well-motivated, and the proposed approach is novel and interesting. The authors provide a clear explanation of the background and related work, and the paper is well-organized. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, and the results show that the proposed method outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines.

However, there are some areas that need improvement. The paper could benefit from more details on the selection and tuning of hyperparameters, such as the latent dimension and the bandwidth parameter σ. Additionally, the authors could provide more characterization of the datasets used in the experiments, such as the number of recurring events per user/item pair and temporal irregularity.

The writing could also be improved. There are several language issues, typos, and grammar errors throughout the paper. The authors could also provide more clarity on the optimization algorithm and the convergence analysis.

Overall, the paper is reasonably good but not great. The proposed approach is novel and interesting, and the experimental evaluation is comprehensive. However, the paper could benefit from more details on hyperparameter tuning and dataset characterization, and the writing could be improved.
