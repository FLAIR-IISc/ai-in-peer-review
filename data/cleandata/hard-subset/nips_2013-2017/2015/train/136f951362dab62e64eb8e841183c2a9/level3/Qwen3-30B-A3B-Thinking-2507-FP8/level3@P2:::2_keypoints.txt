- Proposes combining self-exciting (Hawkes) point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Models user-item consumption as low-rank Hawkes processes with baseline preferences (Λ₀) and self-excitation (A).  
- Main technical contribution: convex optimization formulation and an efficient algorithm (proximal + conditional gradient) with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Addresses recommending the right item at the right time and predicting next user return time.  
- Novel extension of Hawkes processes to recommendation, distinct from survival-analysis-based methods.  
- Theoretical foundation and convergence analysis (Theorem 2) are sound.  
- Algorithm reportedly scales to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs).  
- Empirical evaluation lacks key details: no explanation or sensitivity analysis for bandwidth σ of the triggering kernel.  
- Hyperparameter tuning details for the proposed method and baselines are missing, harming robustness assessment.  
- Missing sensible baseline that combines temporal dynamics (STiC) and collaborative filtering (SVD).  
- Paper clarity is poor: numerous language issues, typos, and unclear sections (notably low-rank modeling in Sec. 3.2).  
- Dataset characterization relevant to temporal modeling (e.g., recurring events per pair, temporal irregularity) is omitted.  
- Significance is high in application, but impact is limited by incomplete experiments and unclear hyperparameter settings.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage revision addressing hyperparameter/sensitivity analysis, stronger baselines, dataset details, and language/clarity improvements.
