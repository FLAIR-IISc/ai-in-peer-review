- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation model combining low-rank user-item factors with a self-exciting Hawkes process.  
- Targets two problems: recommending the right item at the right time and predicting users' next return times.  
- Extends prior temporal recommendation work by using a continuous-time event model rather than discrete-time approaches.  
- Leads to a convex optimization formulation with an efficient, scalable distributed algorithm.  
- Original and significant: novel combination of collaborative filtering and temporal point processes with strong empirical performance.  
- Advantageous for modeling recurrent user activities in continuous time versus static/discrete models.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and technically accessible.  
- Theoretical issues: ambiguity in the definition of ρ* (appears circular) and unclear boundedness of ρ*.  
- Theory–practice gap: convergence analysis depends on ρ but experiments tune ρ empirically, reducing theoretical relevance.  
- Experimental gap: solid evaluation but missing comparison to SVD++, a strong temporal baseline that should be included.  
- Overall assessment: strong, elegant, and potentially impactful work that requires clearer theoretical justification and broader experimental comparisons.
