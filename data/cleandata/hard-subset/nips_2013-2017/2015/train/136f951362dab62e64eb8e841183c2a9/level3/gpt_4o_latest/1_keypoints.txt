- Introduces a novel framework combining self-exciting Hawkes processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendations and return-time prediction.
- Proposes a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm mixing proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence.
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs, hundreds of millions of events).
- Evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) against baselines like SVD, tensor factorization, and Poisson processes.
- Strength: novel methodology that meaningfully models recurrent user-item interactions.
- Strength: well-designed optimization with theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Strength: empirical scalability and promising predictive performance.
- Weakness: motivation and practical relevance of predicting user return times are not clearly articulated with concrete real-world use cases.
- Weakness: assumption of widespread time-varying preferences is not sufficiently validated on real data.
- Weakness: missing comparisons with simpler/sensible baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., delay categorization).
- Weakness: reported improvements over baselines (e.g., SVD) are not consistently significant or clearly quantified; lacks comparisons to popular recommendation methods.
- Weakness: clarity issues and minor typos (confusing examples and a typo on page 3) reduce polish.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and promising but has gaps in motivation, baselines, and clarity; marginally acceptable pending revisions.
- Recommendation: Marginal/OK with revisions — accept if weaknesses are addressed.
