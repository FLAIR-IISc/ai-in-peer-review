This paper proposes a novel point-process collaborative filtering model, combining constant and self-exciting intensities through user/item latent vectors to capture temporal patterns in user-item interactions. The authors address the problems of time-sensitive recommendation and predicting user return times. This builds upon recent work leveraging point processes for temporal modeling, particularly Kapoor et al. [12, 11], but aims to overcome their limitation of not being able to generalize to new items. The approach involves a convex optimization formulation and a new algorithm with an O(1/t) convergence rate, demonstrated on both synthetic and real datasets.

The core idea – integrating point processes with latent factor models – is interesting and novel. Connecting Hawkes processes to low-rank approximations offers a potentially powerful way to model both the temporal dynamics and underlying structure of user-item interactions. The problem itself is significant; accurate time-sensitive recommendations and return-time prediction have clear practical value for web services and other applications, as highlighted by examples in electronic health records and ad bidding.

However, while promising, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. The most glaring is the lack of detail regarding hyperparameter selection. The bandwidth parameter σ for the triggering kernel γ(t, ti) is used extensively, yet the paper provides no explanation for how it’s set or any sensitivity analysis to its value. This is a critical omission, as the choice of σ significantly impacts the model's ability to capture temporal dependencies. Similarly, the latent dimension for the low-rank approximations is not discussed in terms of selection or tuning, for both the proposed model and the baselines. A clear justification for the chosen dimensions is necessary.

The experimental evaluation, while comprehensive in terms of datasets, could be improved. While the inclusion of STiC [12] and SVD as a simple ensemble is sensible given their complementary strengths, the paper doesn’t thoroughly analyze *why* their ensemble performs as it does. A more in-depth comparison would strengthen the results. Furthermore, the paper lacks adequate dataset characterization relevant to temporal modeling. Providing statistics like the number of recurring events per user/item pair or measures of temporal irregularity (e.g., standard deviation of inter-event times) would offer valuable context and insight into the challenges posed by each dataset.

The paper's clarity is also a significant issue. Frequent language errors, typos, and grammatical mistakes impede understanding and detract from the overall presentation. While not insurmountable, these issues require careful attention during revision. The notation, while generally standard, could benefit from more consistent use and explanation.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel combination of point processes and latent factor models.
*   Addresses a practically relevant problem with a potentially effective solution.
*   Demonstrates scalability to large datasets.
*   Provides a theoretical convergence guarantee for the proposed optimization algorithm.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of detail regarding hyperparameter tuning, particularly for σ.
*   Insufficient dataset characterization for temporal modeling.
*   Clarity issues due to language errors and typos.
*   Limited analysis of baseline performance and comparative results.
*   Optimization algorithm described in high level. Missing implementation details.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper is reasonably good but falls short of being great. The core idea is strong, and the initial results are encouraging, but the weaknesses regarding hyperparameter selection, dataset characterization, clarity, and analysis hold it back. A major revision addressing these concerns is necessary before this paper could be considered for acceptance. Specifically, a thorough sensitivity analysis of σ, a detailed explanation of hyperparameter tuning for all models, additional dataset statistics, and careful proofreading are essential. 

**Recommendation:** Reject with request for major revisions.
