- Proposes a novel point-process collaborative filtering model combining constant and self-exciting intensities via user/item latent vectors to capture temporal patterns.  
- Builds on prior point-process work (Kapoor et al.) but aims to generalize to new items using low-rank latent factors.  
- Uses a convex optimization formulation and a new algorithm with an O(1/t) convergence guarantee.  
- Addresses practical problems of time-sensitive recommendation and user return-time prediction.  
- Core idea (integrating Hawkes-like processes with latent factor models) is interesting and novel.  
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets and shows results on synthetic and real data.  
- Major omission: no detail or sensitivity analysis for the bandwidth parameter σ of the triggering kernel, which critically affects temporal modeling.  
- Missing discussion of latent dimension selection/tuning for the low-rank approximations (both proposed model and baselines).  
- Experimental analysis could be deeper—insufficient explanation for ensemble performance (STiC + SVD) and limited baseline comparison.  
- Dataset characterization is inadequate for temporal tasks (e.g., recurring-event counts, inter-event time statistics not provided).  
- Clarity problems: frequent language errors, typos, and inconsistent/under-explained notation impede understanding.  
- Optimization algorithm described at a high level with missing implementation details.  
- Overall: promising and reasonably good but falls short due to methodological and presentation gaps; needs major revision.  
- Recommendation: Reject with request for major revisions (address σ sensitivity, hyperparameter tuning, dataset stats, deeper analysis, and careful proofreading).
