- Summary: Proposes a novel framework combining self-exciting point processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and next-return time prediction with an efficient scalable optimizer and strong empirical results.

- Strength 1: Tackles two important problems—time-sensitive recommendation and next-return time prediction.

- Strength 2: Novel combination of self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture recurrent user activities.

- Strength 3: Develops an efficient optimization algorithm that scales to large datasets and achieves strong performance.

- Strength 4: Experimental results on synthetic and real datasets demonstrate effectiveness.

- Weakness 1: Motivation for predicting next-return time is unclear; practical use cases for web companies are not well explained.

- Weakness 2: Lacks simple baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last time delay, most frequent delay).

- Weakness 3: Experimental comparisons lack clarity on statistical significance and practical impact versus SVD and popular-item baselines.

- Weakness 4: Presentation issues include typos and some confusing examples.

- Originality: The components are known but their combination and application to these problems is original.

- Significance: Addresses important problems and has potential to improve user engagement with state-of-the-art results.

- Quality: Technically sound with theoretical and empirical support, but needs more detailed analysis and comparisons.

- Clarity: Generally well-written but introduction, related work, and experimental presentation could be clearer and more concise.

- Conclusion: Overall a good contribution that, with revisions addressing weaknesses, could be a strong paper.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify motivation, add simple baselines, and improve experimental presentation.
