**Paper Review: Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction using a combination of self-exciting point processes and low-rank models. The authors introduce a convex formulation for the problem and develop an efficient optimization algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods. The proposed method is evaluated on both synthetic and real datasets, demonstrating its superior scalability and predictive performance.

**Strengths**

1. **Originality**: The paper proposes a novel combination of point processes and latent factor models, which is an interesting and original approach to time-sensitive recommendation.
2. **Significance**: The problem and method have practical value, and the paper demonstrates its effectiveness in real-world applications.
3. **Technical soundness**: The paper provides a thorough technical analysis, including a convex formulation, optimization algorithm, and convergence analysis.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Clarity**: The paper contains language issues, typos, and grammar errors, which can make it difficult to follow.
2. **Lack of details**: The paper is missing details on the selection and tuning of key hyperparameters, such as the latent dimension and the bandwidth parameter σ.
3. **Sensitivity analysis**: The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the sensitivity of the results to the choice of σ.
4. **Baseline comparison**: The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, including a simple ensemble of STiC and SVD.

**Suggestions for improvement**

1. **Improve clarity**: Revise the paper to address language issues, typos, and grammar errors.
2. **Provide more details**: Include more information on the selection and tuning of key hyperparameters.
3. **Conduct sensitivity analysis**: Perform a thorough analysis of the sensitivity of the results to the choice of σ.
4. **Enhance baseline comparison**: Include a more comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Rating**

Overall, I would rate this paper as reasonably good but not great. While it proposes an original and significant approach to time-sensitive recommendation, it requires improvement in terms of clarity, detail, and thoroughness of analysis.

**Arguments for acceptance**

1. Originality and significance of the approach.
2. Technical soundness and thorough analysis.
3. Potential impact on real-world applications.

**Arguments against acceptance**

1. Lack of clarity and detail in the paper.
2. Limited comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.
3. Need for more thorough sensitivity analysis.
