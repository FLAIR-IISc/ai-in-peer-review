- Proposes a novel framework combining Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank latent factor models for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Aims to solve recommending the most relevant item at the right moment and predicting users' next return times.  
- Extends prior point-process approaches by incorporating latent factors to generalize to new items (addressing a limitation of survival-analysis methods).  
- Theoretical contribution: convex optimization formulation with O(1/t) convergence and a hybrid proximal + conditional gradient algorithm.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) shows scalability and promising predictive performance.  
- Major experimental flaw: lacks a sensible baseline—should include an ensemble of STiC and SVD given their complementary strengths.  
- Missing explanation and sensitivity analysis for the γ function bandwidth parameter σ.  
- Omits critical hyperparameter tuning details for the proposed method and baselines, harming reproducibility.  
- Dataset characterization is insufficient for temporal modeling (e.g., missing recurring-events-per-user-item and temporal irregularity metrics).  
- Presentation problems: poor clarity, language issues, typos, and grammatical errors that hinder readability.  
- Overall: interesting and practically relevant idea, but experimental shortcomings and presentation issues significantly undermine the contribution.
