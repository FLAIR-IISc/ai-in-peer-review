- Proposes a time-sensitive recommendation framework modeling each user–item interaction stream as a low-rank Hawkes process combining base intensity Λ0 and excitation A with nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Derives a convex surrogate, an auxiliary-variable exact-penalty variant, and a hybrid solver mixing proximal gradient (nonnegativity) and conditional gradient (nuclear norm), with an O(1/t) convergence bound.  
- Goes beyond static/time-discretized recommendation and sequence models by modeling continuous-time self-excitation and tying parameters across sparse user–item pairs via low-rank structure.  

Strengths:
- Principled stochastic-process modeling: continuous-time Hawkes likelihood with convincing time-change diagnostics.  
- Novel modeling + algorithmic combo: low-rank Hawkes enables transfer across sparse pairs and an SVD-avoiding scalable solver.  
- Strong empirical performance and scalability: outperforms baselines on synthetic and real data and scales to hundreds of millions of events.  
- Clear, readable presentation with sensible experiments and ablations.  

Weaknesses / Questions:
- Eq. (8)/ρ* is unclear: the definition depends on unknown optima, may be ill-posed/unbounded, and needs clarification of variables and domain.  
- Theory–practice gap: Theorem 2’s ρ is not used to set hyperparameters in experiments; bridge or empirical validation is missing.  
- Incomplete baselines: missing strong time-aware comparators (TimeSVD++/SVD++, FPMC, dynamic Poisson factorization).  
- Limited evaluation metrics: recommend reporting Recall@k/NDCG@k and probabilistic/calibration metrics (NLL, CRPS, quantile coverage) with confidence intervals.  
- Modeling scope: captures only within user–item self-excitation, not cross-excitation between items/artists.  
- Minor fixes: reconcile abstract rate notation with Theorem 2, clarify per-iteration complexity and distributed/parallel details.  

Quality:
- Technically sound overall but needs clarity on exact-penalty parameter and theory–hyperparameter handling; empirical evidence is strong.  

Completeness:
- Complete study (model, algorithm, theory, experiments) but would benefit from stronger baselines and more diagnostic metrics.  

Honesty:
- Generally careful; needs explicit practical handling of ρ and conditions for theoretical claims.  

Clarity:
- Very clear and organized; suggest adding an intuitive explanation of exact-penalty equivalence, a symbol table, and details on scalable top singular vector computation.  

Originality:
- High: novel combination of Hawkes processes with low-rank user–item structure for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  

Significance:
- High potential impact: addresses an important problem with scalable methods applicable to other continuous-time recommendation and EHR tasks.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Principled continuous-time formulation with empirical gains.  
- Scalable, elegant algorithm with convergence analysis.  
- Clear writing and extensible, interpretable model.  

Cons against acceptance:
- Theoretical clarity issues around Eq. (8)/ρ* and mismatch between theory and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Missing strong time-aware and sequential baselines.  
- Limited evaluation metrics for ranking and return-time calibration.  

Suggestions for improvement:
- Clarify Eq. (8), provide a computable bound or continuation scheme for ρ, and include sensitivity experiments or at least one experiment with theoretically valid ρ.  
- Add TimeSVD++/SVD++ and, if possible, FPMC/dynamic Poisson factorization baselines.  
- Report Recall@k/NDCG@k and probabilistic time-forecast metrics (NLL, CRPS) with confidence intervals.  
- Discuss limitations and extensions for cross-excitation/multidimensional Hawkes modeling.  
- Provide per-iteration complexity, memory footprint, and details on top singular vector computations and any distributed implementation.  

Overall recommendation:
- Leaning accept, contingent on addressing theoretical clarity about the penalty parameter and strengthening baselines and evaluations.
