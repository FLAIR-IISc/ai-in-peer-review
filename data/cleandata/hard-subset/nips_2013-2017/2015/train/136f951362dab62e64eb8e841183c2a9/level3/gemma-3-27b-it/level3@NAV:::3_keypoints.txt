- Novel approach: combines low-rank models with Hawkes processes to model user-item interactions as recurrent event processes.  
- Principled contribution: offers a convex formulation and an efficient estimation algorithm.  
- Empirical results: shows improved performance on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Scalability: algorithm is scalable, distributed-solvable, and reports O(1/t) convergence.  
- Novelty vs prior work: more generalizable than earlier temporal point-process methods, able to recommend new items rather than only predict return times.  
- Presentation: paper is technically sound and well-written with clear motivations and contributions.  
- Clarity issue: explanation around Equation (8) is unclear—must specify which parameter (ρ) is being maximized and address potential unboundedness of ρ*.  
- Theorem concern: Theorem 2’s practical relevance is limited because the prescribed ρ value was not used in experiments.  
- Baseline gap: experimental evaluation lacks a strong time-aware baseline—SVD++ should be included for a more robust comparison.  
- Complexity analysis: needs a more thorough comparison of computational complexity against existing methods.  
- Overall recommendation: reviewer favors acceptance, contingent on addressing the clarity and experimental completeness issues.
