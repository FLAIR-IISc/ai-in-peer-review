- Proposes a novel framework linking self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption.  
- Develops an efficient optimization algorithm with O(1/t) convergence for parameter learning.  
- Evaluated on synthetic data and two real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com) for recommending at the right moment and predicting next-return time.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: clear convex formulation and convergence analysis; optimization appears well-designed.  
- Model claims good scalability to large datasets.  
- Motivation for predicting next-return time is weak and practical applications are underdeveloped beyond vague “marketing strategies.”  
- Claim of time-varying preferences lacks empirical justification; no dataset analysis to show prevalence or causes of such patterns.  
- Experimental design omits simple baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last delay, most frequent delay) and ignores coarser, practical delay categories.  
- Results unclear on practical significance: unclear if improvements over SVD and popular-item baselines are substantial or user-perceivable.  
- Presentation issues: typo ("abel"→"able") and unsupported examples (retweets/link-creation) without references.  
- Connection to practical recommendation systems remains tenuous; marginal gains on modest datasets don’t justify method complexity.  
- Overall: technically novel and solid but needs stronger real-world motivation, deeper empirical analysis, and more comprehensive baselines.
