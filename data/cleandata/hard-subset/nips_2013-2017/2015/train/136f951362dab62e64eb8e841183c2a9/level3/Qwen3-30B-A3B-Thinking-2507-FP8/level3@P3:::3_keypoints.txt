- Proposes a novel low-rank Hawkes-process framework combining self-exciting point processes with matrix models to capture recurrent temporal user-item patterns.  
- Models intensity as base long-term preferences plus self-exciting short-term effects; formulates a convex optimization solved by a distributed algorithm with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Targets two tasks: recommending the most relevant item at the right time and predicting users' next return time.  
- Builds on prior survival-analysis and matrix-factorization work, addressing limitations of methods that cannot generalize to new-item recommendation.  
- Strength: Principled, theoretically sound modeling with a well-motivated convex formulation and convergence analysis (Theorem 2).  
- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with structured algorithmic details and pseudocode.  
- Strength: Novel integration of low-rank matrix models and Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Strength: Scalable distributed algorithm and strong empirical performance on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) versus several baselines.  
- Weakness: Equation (8) is unclear about which parameter is maximized and whether the threshold ρ* is bounded or scales with problem size.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2’s prescribed ρ is not used in experiments; empirical tuning of ρ weakens the theoretical claim.  
- Weakness: Experimental baselines omit SVD++, a relevant time-aware recommendation method, weakening comparative evaluation.  
- Weakness: Claims about easy incorporation of additional contextual information are not demonstrated experimentally.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — novel approach with strong results; theoretical issues are minor and can be fixed, and adding SVD++ would strengthen the paper.
