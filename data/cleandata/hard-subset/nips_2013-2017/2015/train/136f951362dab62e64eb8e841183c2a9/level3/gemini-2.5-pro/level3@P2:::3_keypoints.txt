- Problem: time-sensitive recommendation—recommend right item at right moment and predict user's next return time.
- Proposed model: combines low-rank matrix factorization with multi-dimensional Hawkes point processes.
- Per user-item pair: intensity = baseline + self-exciting term dependent on event history.
- Low-rank assumption: baseline intensities (Λ0) and self-excitation coefficients (A) are low-rank matrices.
- Learning: maximize event log-likelihood with nuclear-norm regularization to enforce low-rank structure.
- Optimization: novel algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods, O(1/t) convergence and linear scalability claimed.
- Empirical evaluation: outperforms baselines (Poisson, STiC, SVD, tensor factorization) on synthetic and real datasets for recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Relation to prior work: extends Kapoor et al. by enabling cross-user/item transfer and recommending unseen items; finer-grained temporal modeling than tensor factorization.
- Strength — originality/significance: principled continuous-time framework capturing long-term preferences and short-term dynamics; unifies recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Strength — technical quality: convex formulation and scalable custom optimization that handles non-negativity and nuclear-norm constraints; thorough experiments.
- Strength — clarity: well-written, logically presented model and experiments.
- Weakness — theoretical clarity: Theorem 1 uses a threshold ρ* but the maximization set and finiteness of ρ* are not clearly specified.
- Weakness — theory vs practice gap: convergence guarantee requires a specific ρ, but experiments treat ρ as a tuned hyperparameter; sensitivity not reported.
- Weakness — experimental baselines: missing comparisons to strong time-aware recommendation methods (e.g., SVD++, Koren’s temporal dynamics).
- Acceptance arguments: novel and practical modeling approach, strong scalable algorithm with theoretical guarantees, and solid empirical gains.
- Rejection arguments: unclear theoretical details (Theorem 1) and incomplete baseline comparisons make it hard to fully assess contributions.
- Overall judgment: high-quality, significant contribution with addressable weaknesses; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
