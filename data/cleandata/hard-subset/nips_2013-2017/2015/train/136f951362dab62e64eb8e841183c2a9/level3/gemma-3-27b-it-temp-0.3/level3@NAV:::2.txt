## Review of "Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities"

This paper proposes a novel point-process collaborative filtering model that combines constant and self-exciting intensities via user/item latent vectors. The authors aim to address the limitations of existing recommendation systems by explicitly modeling the temporal behavior and recurrent activities of users, tackling both item recommendation and next-visit prediction. The approach leverages Hawkes processes and low-rank models, coupled with a convex optimization algorithm designed for scalability. 

The core idea – integrating point processes with latent factor models – is interesting and novel, building upon recent work in temporal recommendation (e.g., [12]) but extending it with a more flexible and generalizable framework. The problem addressed is practically relevant, as understanding and predicting user engagement timing is crucial for many web services. The potential to incorporate additional contextual information, as mentioned by the authors, further enhances the significance of the work.

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. While the optimization algorithm is claimed to be efficient, the setting of the crucial bandwidth parameter σ in the triggering kernel (γ function) is not adequately explained. A sensitivity analysis to σ is entirely missing, leaving a significant question about the robustness of the results. Furthermore, the paper lacks detail regarding the selection and tuning of other key hyperparameters, such as the latent dimension, for both the proposed method and the baseline comparisons. This makes it difficult to assess the fairness of the experimental results. 

The experimental evaluation, while including synthetic data, would benefit from more thorough dataset characterization relevant to temporal modeling. Reporting metrics like the number of recurring events per user/item pair or the temporal irregularity of events would provide valuable context. Critically, the authors acknowledge the relevance of STiC [12] and SVD, but the proposed ensemble baseline (STiC + SVD) is not included, hindering a comprehensive comparison. 

Clarity is also a concern. The paper contains numerous language issues, typos, and grammatical errors, which impede understanding. While the technical ideas are promising, the presentation needs significant improvement.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel combination of point processes and latent factor models.
*   Potentially significant impact on time-sensitive recommendation.
*   Scalable optimization algorithm.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of sensitivity analysis for key parameters (σ).
*   Missing hyperparameter tuning details.
*   Insufficient dataset characterization.
*   Missing crucial baseline (STiC + SVD).
*   Poor clarity and numerous language errors.

**Overall Assessment:** Reasonably good, but not great. The paper presents a promising approach, but the lack of thorough experimentation and clarity issues prevent a strong recommendation for acceptance in its current form. I recommend rejection with encouragement to revise and address the identified weaknesses.
