- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework that models time-varying user preferences and predicts users' next-return time.  
- Connects self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns in user-item interactions.  
- Provides a convex formulation for recommendation and return-time prediction problems.  
- Develops a new optimization algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Claims scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events.  
- Evaluated on one synthetic and two modest real-world datasets.  
- Shows strong predictive performance, outperforming baselines like Poisson process, STiC, SVD, and tensor factorization.  
- Demonstrates that incorporating user activity history improves predictive accuracy and captures temporal dynamics.  
- Lacks sufficient real-world motivation and clear practical use cases for predicting next-return time.  
- Uses limited baselines for return-time prediction, omitting stronger options like survival analysis or ML-based methods.  
- Presentation issues: contains a typo and a confusing, unreferenced switch to retweet/link-creation examples in Section 2.  
- Raises questions about how widespread time-varying preferences are in real applications and the paper’s practical relevance.  
- Overall technically sound, well-written, and original but would benefit from more motivation, additional baselines, and deeper analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, new optimization algorithm, and strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak real-world motivation, insufficient baselines, and presentation flaws.
