- Proposes a novel framework combining self-exciting (Hawkes) point processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction.  
- Targets two central problems: recommending the most desirable item at the right moment and predicting a user’s next return time.  
- Uses Hawkes processes to capture temporal dependencies between events.  
- Develops an efficient parameter-learning algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.  
- Paper is well-motivated, novel, and interesting with clear background and organization.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and shows the method outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Lacks sufficient details on hyperparameter selection and tuning (e.g., latent dimension, bandwidth σ).  
- Needs more dataset characterization (e.g., number of recurring events per user/item pair, temporal irregularity).  
- Writing has language issues, typos, and grammar errors that hinder clarity.  
- Optimization algorithm and convergence analysis require clearer explanation.  
- Significance is practical but presentation and experimental details need improvement.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, clear motivation and related work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient hyperparameter/dataset details, poor writing, and unclear optimization/convergence.  
- Suggested improvements: add hyperparameter tuning and dataset details, fix language errors, clarify optimization/convergence, and consider adding an STiC+SVD ensemble baseline.
