This paper proposes a time-sensitive recommender that models each user–item pair as a self-exciting point process with a constant baseline and a history-dependent component, coupled across pairs through low-rank user–item matrices for the base intensity (Λ0) and self-excitation (A). The authors cast maximum likelihood estimation with nuclear norms as a convex program and develop an efficient blended proximal/conditional-gradient algorithm with an O(1/t) rate and cheap updates (projection for nonnegativity; rank-1 LMOs for nuclear norms). Empirical results on large synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) show improvements on time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction. The work is well motivated and relates to temporal CF (e.g., Koren; tensor PF), point processes and Hawkes learning (e.g., Hawkes; Zhou et al.; Du et al.), and survival/STiC-style baselines.

Quality: The modeling and convexity are sound; the likelihood is concave in the nonnegative parameters and the optimization strategy is reasonable. However, key experimental and methodological details are missing. The bandwidth σ of the triggering kernel is not specified, nor is any sensitivity analysis to σ. Other important hyperparameters (latent dimensionality for baselines; λ, β, ρ for the proposed method) lack selection/tuning details, hindering reproducibility and fair comparison. The experimental suite omits a sensible baseline that would likely be strong here: a simple ensemble combining STiC (for return-time) with SVD (for cross-item generalization), e.g., by calibrated score fusion.

Clarity: The paper is difficult to follow in places and contains language issues, typos, and grammar errors. Notation around γ and σ needs clarification; algorithmic pseudocode would benefit from more explicit definitions of variables and step-size/L constants.

Originality: The combination of Hawkes-style self-excitation with low-rank collaborative structure in a convex formulation is novel and interesting, beyond prior temporal MF or per-pair Hawkes models.

Significance: The problem is important and the approach is potentially useful to practitioners, but the empirical section needs strengthening. Please: (1) add the STiC+SVD ensemble baseline; (2) report how σ is set and include sensitivity/ablation (e.g., α=0, Λ0-only, kernel family); (3) document hyperparameter search spaces/criteria for all methods; (4) characterize datasets w.r.t. temporal recurrence (repeats per pair, inter-arrival irregularity, share of singletons), and add standard top-k metrics (Recall/NDCG) and time-calibration diagnostics.

Pros: clear motivation; principled convex model; scalable algorithm; promising results; novel combination. Cons: missing key baseline; incomplete hyperparameter reporting and sensitivity; limited dataset characterization; clarity issues.

Overall: Reasonably good but not great; I lean toward rejection this round, with concrete paths to a strong revision.
