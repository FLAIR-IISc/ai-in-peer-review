- Proposes low-rank Hawkes-process model for joint item-timing recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Models each user–item event sequence as a self-exciting Hawkes process with baseline + triggering kernel.
- Shares strength across pairs via nuclear-norm low-rank constraints, keeping estimation convex.
- Derives convex negative log-likelihood objective with non-negativity and nuclear-norm penalties.
- Proposes hybrid optimization blending proximal gradient (non-negativity) and conditional gradient/LMO (nuclear norm).
- Provides convergence analysis with O(1/t) rate and O(1/t^2) recovery in smooth/no-nuclear-norm case.
- Scales to millions of pairs and hundreds of millions of events in experiments.
- Outperforms Poisson, survival-analysis, and tensor factorization baselines on synthetic and three real datasets.
- Advances continuous-time modeling over discretized time-aware factorization methods.
- Relates to survival/hazard models but relaxes single-event and i.i.d. gap assumptions.
- Connects to low-rank Hawkes/network estimation but ties univariate processes via user–item structure.
- The convex parameterization, low-rank sharing, and optimization method are well-motivated and technically sound.
- Theorem/penalized-vs-constrained equivalence depends on ρ > ρ*; definition, domain, and boundedness of ρ* are unclear.
- Convergence guarantees assume fixed ρ above threshold, but experiments tune ρ without checking the condition.
- Paper omits discussion on Hawkes stability (subcritical condition ασ < 1) and how supercritical parameters are avoided/enforced.
- Empirical baseline set is missing strong time-aware and implicit-feedback models (SVD++/TimeSVD++, Hu–Koren–Volinsky).
- Could compare to additional continuous-time baselines (renewal/ACD) and nonparametric Hawkes kernel learning.
- Need to report computational overhead of Ogata’s thinning sampling and explore faster return-time approximations.
- Clarity is generally good, but Equation (8)/ρ*, stability conditions, and LMO implementation details need precise clarification.
- Novel combination of low-rank user–item structure with Hawkes-process modeling and hybrid optimization appears original.
- Significant practical impact for recommendation and healthcare event modeling, and method is extensible to additional context.
- Pros: principled continuous-time model, convex estimation, efficient algorithm with guarantees, strong empirical performance.
- Cons: ambiguity around ρ*/convergence, lack of stability discussion, missing strong recommender baselines, limited cost/sensitivity analysis.
- Suggested improvements: clarify ρ*/Eq.(8), enforce/analyze Hawkes stability and identifiability, add stronger baselines, report runtimes and scalability.
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
- Reviewer confidence: high on modeling/optimization, moderate on recommender-baseline breadth.
