- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework combining low-rank models with self-exciting Hawkes processes to model recurrent user activity and predict next-return times.  
- Targets two underexplored problems: recommending the most relevant item at the optimal time and predicting user return intervals.  
- Connects temporal point processes (intensity functions) with low-rank user-item structures, formulating a convex optimization with nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Presents an algorithm mixing proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence and support for non-negativity and low-rank constraints.  
- Demonstrates empirical scalability to 100M+ events and linear scaling with data size.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets (last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC II), outperforming Poisson baselines, STiC, and tensor factorization for item ranking and return-time prediction.  
- Strength: strong technical quality and well-motivated convex formulation with rigorous convergence analysis.  
- Strength: empirical validation of scalability far beyond prior work and clear, intuitive exposition with helpful figures.  
- Strength: novel application of Hawkes processes to low-rank time-sensitive recommendation with compelling real-world use cases (ads, healthcare).  
- Weakness: theoretical gap regarding the practical value/estimation of Theorem 1’s threshold ρ*; experiments tune ρ empirically instead.  
- Weakness: important baseline SVD++ (time-aware CF) is omitted, weakening empirical comparisons.  
- Weakness: no ablation/testing of the exponential kernel choice versus nonparametric alternatives.  
- Pro acceptance arguments: novelty, scalability, technical soundness, clear impact and applications.  
- Con acceptance arguments: unresolved theory for ρ*, missing SVD++ baseline, and lack of kernel ablation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to (1) clarify practical estimation of ρ*, (2) include SVD++ in experiments, and (3) add a short ablation on kernel choices.
