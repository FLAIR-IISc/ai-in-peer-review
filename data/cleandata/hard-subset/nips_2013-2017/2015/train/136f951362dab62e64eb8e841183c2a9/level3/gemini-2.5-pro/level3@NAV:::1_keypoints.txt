- Proposes a unified framework for time-sensitive recommendation: recommend items at the right moment and predict user's next return time.
- Core contribution: novel model linking low-rank factorization with self-exciting Hawkes processes to capture static preferences and history-dependent temporal dynamics.
- Presents an efficient optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with formal convergence analysis.
- Integrates prior temporal collaborative filtering and point process modeling into a single convex formulation.
- Strength: original and creative integration of low-rank models with Hawkes processes for joint item and return-time modeling.
- Strength: solid technical contribution—scalable optimization algorithm that may be of independent interest.
- Weakness: motivation is weak—no data analysis demonstrating prevalence of time-varying preferences or separating them from simple popularity effects.
- Weakness: practical utility of predicting precise next-return time is not well justified with concrete web-service use cases.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation omits crucial baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last inter-event time, most frequent delay), limiting assessment of model value.
- Weakness: recommendation gains are not contextualized—unclear if improvements over baselines like SVD or popularity are practically meaningful.
- Weakness: could consider more practical evaluation targets (e.g., coarse time categories short/medium/long).
- Weakness: minor clarity issues (abrupt switch to retweet examples in Section 2, typo “abel”).
- Overall: interesting and promising idea with solid methods, but undermined by weak motivation, insufficient baselines, and lack of practical-impact analysis; requires substantial revision.
