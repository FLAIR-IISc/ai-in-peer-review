- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework combining Hawkes self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal user-item patterns.
- Models user activity as a Hawkes process and uses low-rank approximations for scalability and generalization to unseen items.
- Develops a convex optimization formulation and an efficient algorithm with provable O(1/t) convergence.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic and real-world datasets for item recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Builds on and contextualizes prior work in temporal point processes and recommender systems, highlighting limitations of survival analysis and traditional MF/tensor methods.
- Novelty: the Hawkes + low-rank combination is a genuine and principled contribution to modeling temporal dependencies.
- Theoretical soundness: provides a convex formulation and formal convergence guarantees.
- Scalability: algorithm is designed to handle millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, with clear motivation and contributions.
- Potential impact: predicting user return times has broad applications (ad bidding, healthcare interventions).
- Weakness — parameter tuning: practical handling of theoretical parameter ρ is unresolved and relies on empirical tuning, weakening relevance of theoretical results.
- Weakness — baselines: omission of SVD++ as a baseline weakens empirical evaluation.
- Weakness — kernel choice: focusing on the exponential triggering kernel may limit model flexibility; learning or alternative kernels could help.
- Weakness — equation clarity: some equations (e.g., Eq. 2) need clearer definitions of terms.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, scalable, empirically superior, and well-presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical use of theoretical parameter selection, missing SVD++ comparison, and simplified triggering kernel.
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful paper that merits acceptance despite noted concerns.
- Rating: Accept (Strongly consider).
