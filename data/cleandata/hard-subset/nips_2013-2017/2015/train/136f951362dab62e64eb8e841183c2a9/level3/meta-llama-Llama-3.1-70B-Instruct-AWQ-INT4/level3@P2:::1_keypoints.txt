- Proposes a time-sensitive recommendation method that models time-varying user preferences and predicts users' next-return time.  
- Main contribution: a novel convex formulation linking self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal user-item patterns.  
- Develops an efficient convex optimization algorithm with O(1/t) convergence rate.  
- Strength: models time-varying preferences and next-return time prediction effectively.  
- Strength: clear, concise explanation of model and algorithm.  
- Strength: evaluated on one synthetic and two real datasets, showing method effectiveness.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification that time-varying preferences are widespread—needs dataset analysis to identify causes.  
- Weakness: unclear practical motivation for predicting next-return time; real-world use cases are not explained.  
- Weakness: lacks simple baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last delay, most frequent delay, coarse delay categories).  
- Weakness: experimental results lack clarity on statistical significance and practical impact versus SVD and popular-item baselines.  
- Weakness: presentation issues—typo ("abel"→"able") and confusing, unsupported example switch to retweets/link-creation.  
- Originality: novel and interesting connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models.  
- Originality caveat: modeling time-varying user preferences itself is not new and needs contextual comparison to prior work.  
- Significance: results are modest and may have limited impact without deeper analysis and stronger motivation.  
- Overall verdict: OK/marginal; tackles interesting problems but needs better motivation, baselines, and analysis to be stronger.
