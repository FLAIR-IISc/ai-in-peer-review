This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation that combines low-rank user-item structure with a Hawkes process. The authors address two central questions in recommendation systems: how to recommend the most desirable item at the right moment and how to predict the next returning time of a user to a service. The proposed model connects self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture the recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs.

The authors provide a thorough background on temporal point processes and introduce their model in terms of low-rank self-exciting Hawkes processes. They also develop a new optimization algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods to learn the model efficiently. The algorithm achieves the optimal O(1/t) convergence rate and scales up to problems with millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events.

The authors evaluate their algorithm by comparing it with state-of-the-art competitors on both synthetic and real datasets. The results show that their algorithm achieves superb predictive performance in the two time-sensitive recommendation tasks. They also demonstrate that their model can better explain the observed data compared to other survival analysis models.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The approach appears novel for the relatively new time-sensitive recommendation problem. The work is potentially impactful due to the modeling choice, distributed solvability, and improved empirical performance.
