- Proposes a novel, well-motivated framework for time-sensitive recommendation combining low-rank user–item structure with a Hawkes process.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Proposed algorithm is novel and appears effective for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including convergence rate analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering both synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Lacks a detailed empirical comparison with existing methods, particularly SVD++.  
- Some theoretical equations (e.g., Eq. (8)) are unclear and need better explanation.  
- The prescribed value of ρ in Theorem 2 is not used in experiments, reducing the theorem’s experimental relevance.  
- Paper would benefit from a deeper discussion of result implications and potential applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, solid theoretical analysis, and comprehensive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparisons to baselines, unclear theoretical exposition, and mismatch between theory (ρ) and experiments.
