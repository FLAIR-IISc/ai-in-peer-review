This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation by combining low-rank user-item structure with a Hawkes process. The authors present a principled stochastic-process modeling of observations (ratings) and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on both synthetic and real datasets.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it a pleasure to read. The authors do a great job of explaining the technical details of their approach, including the low-rank Hawkes process and the convex formulation of the problem. The experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed approach over existing methods.

One of the strengths of the paper is its originality. The authors propose a novel approach to time-sensitive recommendation, which is a relatively new and challenging problem. The combination of low-rank user-item structure with a Hawkes process is a unique and innovative idea that sets the paper apart from existing work in the field.

The significance of the paper is also high. The proposed approach has the potential to impact the field of recommender systems, particularly in applications where time-sensitive recommendations are crucial. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on real-world datasets, including music streaming logs and shopping events, which further highlights the significance of the work.

However, there are some theoretical concerns that need to be addressed. Specifically, Equation (8) is unclear about which parameter is maximized (is it over ρ?), and ρ* may be unbounded. Additionally, Theorem 2's relevance is weakened because its prescribed ρ is not used in experiments. The authors should clarify these points to strengthen the theoretical foundations of their approach.

Furthermore, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened by including additional baselines, such as SVD++ (a strong, time-aware baseline). This would provide a more comprehensive comparison of the proposed approach with existing methods.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper for publication. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field of recommender systems, and their work has the potential to impact the development of time-sensitive recommendation algorithms.
