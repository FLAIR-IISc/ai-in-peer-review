- Paper proposes combining Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix factorization to model time-sensitive item recommendation and next-return-time prediction, solved via a convex formulation and an efficient proximal+conditional gradient algorithm, evaluated on synthetic and three real datasets.  
- Strength: Novel integration of Hawkes processes and low-rank models to capture long-term preferences and short-term, history-dependent effects.  
- Strength: Presents a technically sound, efficient optimization algorithm handling non-negativity and nuclear-norm regularization, with convergence analysis and optimal rates.  
- Strength: Ambitious, comprehensive scope addressing both time-sensitive item ranking and return-time prediction in a unified framework.  
- Weakness: Insufficient empirical justification that user preferences are widely self-exciting; lacks exploratory analysis distinguishing user-specific recurrence from item-side trends.  
- Weakness: Weak motivation and unclear practical use cases for predicting precise next-return times (e.g., how a web company would use a 3.7-hour prediction).  
- Weakness: Experimental baselines are inadequate—missing simple but important baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last delay, mean/median/most frequent inter-event delays).  
- Weakness: No comparison to coarser, possibly more practical formulations (e.g., predicting time categories like short/medium/long).  
- Weakness: Practical impact of numerical improvements is unclear—lack of user-facing ranking metrics (NDCG@k, Precision@k) and analysis of where improvements occur across users/items.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues include typos and abrupt, confusing examples (e.g., retweets/link creation) that don't align with the main application focus.  
- Overall: Interesting and technically solid work with promising ideas, but weakened by limited real-world motivation, missing simple baselines, and insufficient analysis of practical significance, making it a borderline paper.
