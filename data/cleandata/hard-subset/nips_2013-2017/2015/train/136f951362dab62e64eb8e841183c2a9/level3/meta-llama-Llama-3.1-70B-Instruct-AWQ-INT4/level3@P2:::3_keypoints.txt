- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework combining low-rank user–item structure with a Hawkes (self-exciting) process.  
- Addresses recommending the right item at the right time and predicting users’ next return times.  
- Connects self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent user–item temporal patterns.  
- Provides thorough background on temporal point processes and clearly introduces the low-rank Hawkes model.  
- Develops a new optimization algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.  
- Algorithm attains optimal O(1/t) convergence and scales to millions of user–item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets shows strong predictive performance for both recommendation and return-time tasks.  
- Demonstrates better data explanation compared to other survival analysis models.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Strength: novel, impactful modeling choice with distributed solvability and strong empirical results.  
- Weakness: theoretical concern about Eq. (8) and boundedness of ρ* needs more discussion.  
- Weakness: relevance of Theorem 2 is weakened because its prescribed ρ is not used in experiments.  
- Weakness: experimental baselines should include stronger time-aware methods like SVD++.  
- Quality: 8/10; Clarity: 9/10; Originality: 8/10; Significance: 9/10.
