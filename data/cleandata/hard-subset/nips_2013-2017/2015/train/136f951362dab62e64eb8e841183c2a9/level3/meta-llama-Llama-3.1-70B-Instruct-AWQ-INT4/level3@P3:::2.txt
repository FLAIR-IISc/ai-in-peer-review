This paper proposes a novel framework that combines self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture the recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs. The authors aim to address two related questions: recommending the most relevant item at the right moment and predicting the next returning time of users to existing services. The proposed model is technically sound, and the authors provide a convex formulation of the problem and an efficient optimization algorithm to solve it.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their model and the underlying concepts from the theory of temporal point processes. However, the writing could be improved, as there are some language issues, typos, and grammar errors throughout the paper. Additionally, the authors could provide more details on the selection and tuning of key hyperparameters, such as the latent dimension, for both the proposed method and baselines.

The experiments are comprehensive, and the authors evaluate their algorithm on both synthetic and real datasets. The results show that the proposed model achieves superior predictive performance compared to other state-of-the-art competitors. However, the authors could provide more dataset characterization relevant to temporal modeling, such as the number of recurring events per user-item pair and temporal irregularity.

One of the strengths of the paper is its originality. The combination of point processes and latent factor models is novel and interesting. The authors also demonstrate the practical value of their approach, as it can be applied to various domains, such as music streaming, e-commerce, and healthcare.

However, the significance of the paper could be improved by providing more analysis of the sensitivity of the γ function bandwidth parameter σ and its impact on the results. Additionally, the authors could discuss the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research.

In terms of the conference reviewing guidelines, the paper meets the criteria for quality, originality, and significance. However, the clarity of the paper could be improved, and the authors should address the issues mentioned above.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and interesting combination of point processes and latent factor models.
* The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of their algorithm on both synthetic and real datasets.
* The results show that the proposed model achieves superior predictive performance compared to other state-of-the-art competitors.
* The approach has practical value and can be applied to various domains.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The writing could be improved, as there are some language issues, typos, and grammar errors throughout the paper.
* The authors could provide more details on the selection and tuning of key hyperparameters.
* The paper lacks analysis of the sensitivity of the γ function bandwidth parameter σ and its impact on the results.
* The authors could discuss the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research.

Overall, I would recommend accepting the paper, but the authors should address the issues mentioned above to improve the clarity and significance of their work.
