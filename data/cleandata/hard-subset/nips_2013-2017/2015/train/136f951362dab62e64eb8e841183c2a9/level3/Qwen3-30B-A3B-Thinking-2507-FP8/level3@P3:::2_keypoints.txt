- Proposal: Combines self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to form a low-rank Hawkes process for time-sensitive recommendation.
- Model detail: Intensity mixes a constant baseline (long-term preference) and a self-exciting component (short-term influence).
- Optimization & algorithm: Presents a convex formulation with an efficient proximal+conditional gradient algorithm and O(1/t) convergence.
- Evaluation scope: Tested on synthetic and real datasets for item recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Strength — novelty: Novel connection between point processes and low-rank collaborative filtering.
- Strength — theory: Sound theoretical analysis with clear convergence-rate results.
- Strength — scalability: Claims to scale to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Strength — relevance: Addresses a timely problem in temporal recommendation systems.
- Weakness — baselines: Lacks a sensible baseline comparison (e.g., ensemble of STiC for return-time and SVD for static recommendation).
- Weakness — hyperparameters: Missing details and sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (e.g., triggering kernel bandwidth σ, latent dimensions).
- Weakness — data characterization: Insufficient dataset statistics for temporal modeling (e.g., recurring-event counts, inter-event time distributions, temporal irregularity).
- Weakness — clarity: Significant language, typo, and exposition issues that make the paper hard to follow (Algorithm 1 and key math unclear).
- Quality assessment: Technical approach is sound but experimental validation, especially on real data, is incomplete and undermines claimed performance.
- Clarity assessment: Paper suffers from organization and explanation gaps; key parameters and functions are not properly explained.
- Originality assessment: The method is original and well-positioned relative to related work (e.g., STiC).
- Significance assessment: Potential practical impact is high but limited by inadequate experimental support.
- Recommendation: Recommend rejection in current form; encourage revisions to improve experiments and clarity.
- For acceptance: Novel idea, strong theoretical contribution, practical relevance.
- Against acceptance: Missing sensible baseline, unclear parameter choices/no sensitivity analysis, poor dataset characterization, and clarity problems.
