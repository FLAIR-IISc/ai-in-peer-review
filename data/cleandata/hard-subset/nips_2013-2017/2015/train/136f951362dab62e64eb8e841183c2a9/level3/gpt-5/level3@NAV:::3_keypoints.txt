- Proposes a time-sensitive recommender combining low-rank user–item structure with a Hawkes process for recurrent user activities.  
- Casts parameter learning as a convex problem with nuclear-norm regularization and nonnegativity, and uses a proximal + conditional-gradient/LMO algorithm with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Tackles two tasks: ranking items at the right moment (via intensities) and predicting users’ return times (via the conditional density).  
- Reports consistent improvements over Poisson, tensor factorization, SVD, and STiC on synthetic and three real datasets; scalable implementation.  
- Well grounded in temporal point processes and related work, but novel in explicitly modelling self-excitation with shared low-rank structure.  
- Strengths: principled modeling, thoughtful optimization engineering, provable convergence component, strong empirical performance at scale, clear writing.  
- Eq. (8) (penalty-equivalence condition) is unclear about what variable(s) are maximized and the feasible set; needs explicit notation.  
- Potential unboundedness of ρ* if the denominator approaches zero—provide conditions ensuring finiteness (e.g., exact-penalty assumptions, uniqueness, bounded sublevel sets) or use augmented Lagrangian.  
- Theorem 2 proves convergence for fixed ρ, but experiments tune ρ differently, creating a theory–experiment mismatch that weakens practical relevance.  
- Recommend a sensitivity study showing solution invariance beyond a computable lower bound on ρ* to strengthen theoretical claims.  
- Experimental baselines should include strong time-aware methods (SVD++/TimeSVD++) to solidify empirical comparisons.  
- Clarify and define all constants in Theorem 2 for transparency.  
- Provide more details on the distributed/parallel implementation and wall-clock comparisons versus ADMM-based solvers.  
- Limited statistical reporting: include confidence intervals and further sensitivity analyses.  
- Overall originality: combining Hawkes processes with low-rank sharing in a convex, scalable formulation appears novel; significance is high.
