This paper proposes a novel model for time-sensitive recommendation by combining collaborative filtering with temporal point processes. The core idea is to model the event stream for each user-item pair as a Hawkes process, where the constant base intensity and the self-exciting component are derived from low-rank user and item latent factor matrices. This elegantly unifies static user preferences (à la SVD) with dynamic, history-dependent behavior. The authors propose a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm to learn the model parameters. The work is positioned as an improvement over prior methods like survival analysis models [12], which cannot generalize to unobserved items.

The main strength of this paper lies in its originality. The fusion of low-rank models and self-exciting point processes is a novel and principled approach to a significant practical problem. The method has the potential to advance the state of the art in time-aware recommendation.

However, the paper has several weaknesses that temper its contribution. The experimental validation is not sufficiently rigorous. A crucial baseline is missing: a simple ensemble of a static model like SVD and a temporal model like STiC [12] would have been a more sensible competitor, given their complementary strengths. Furthermore, the paper lacks critical details regarding hyperparameter selection for both the proposed model and the baselines (e.g., latent dimension, regularization terms), which undermines the reproducibility and fairness of the comparison. For a paper focused on temporal dynamics, the datasets should be better characterized with statistics relevant to the problem, such as the distribution of recurring events per user/item pair or measures of temporal irregularity.

In terms of clarity, the manuscript is difficult to follow. It suffers from numerous language issues, typos, and grammatical errors. Key methodological details are omitted, most notably how the triggering kernel bandwidth parameter σ is set, and no sensitivity analysis is provided for this crucial parameter.

In summary:
**Pros:**
*   Novel and elegant combination of latent factor models and point processes.
*   Addresses a significant and practical problem in recommendation systems.

**Cons:**
*   Experimental evaluation lacks a key baseline and sufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning.
*   Missing methodological details (e.g., setting σ) hinder reproducibility.
*   Poorly written, with many language errors that obscure the technical content.

While the core idea is promising, the weaknesses in the experimental validation and presentation are significant. The paper requires substantial revision before it can be considered for publication.
