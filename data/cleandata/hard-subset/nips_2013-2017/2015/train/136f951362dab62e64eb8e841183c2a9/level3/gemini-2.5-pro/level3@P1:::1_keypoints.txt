- Introduces Low Rank Hawkes Process: combines Hawkes self-exciting point processes with low-rank matrix models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Models intensity λ_ui(t) as a sum of static baseline and dynamic history-dependent component triggered by past events.  
- Uses low-rank matrices (Λ0 and A) with nuclear norm regularization to handle sparsity and generalize to unobserved user-item pairs.  
- Proposes an efficient convex optimization algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods, with O(1/t) convergence analysis.  
- Evaluated on one synthetic and three real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) for item recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Strength — Originality/Significance: novel, principled unification of temporal Hawkes dynamics with collaborative low-rank structure addressing both long- and short-term preferences.  
- Strength — Technical Quality: sound convex formulation, nontrivial optimization approach handling nuclear norm and nonnegativity, with convergence proof.  
- Strength — Clarity: generally well-written; background and model development are clear.  
- Weakness — Motivation: lacks data-driven evidence from datasets showing necessity of the sophisticated temporal model versus simpler trend effects or recurring patterns.  
- Weakness — Practicality of return-time task: unclear why predicting exact next return time is useful in practice and how it would be concretely applied.  
- Weakness — Experimental baselines: missing simple heuristic baselines (e.g., last delay, median delay) for return-time prediction, making value added unclear.  
- Weakness — Evaluation framing: focuses on exact time prediction rather than more practical/coarse categories (hour/day/week) and lacks analysis of practical significance of recommendation gains over strong popularity baselines.  
- Weakness — Presentation: minor organizational issues, e.g., not clearly positioning differences with related work (Kapoor et al.) early on.  
- Overall: technically solid and novel contribution but borderline due to weak motivation and insufficient experimental comparisons; stronger motivation and baseline analyses would make it a much stronger paper.
