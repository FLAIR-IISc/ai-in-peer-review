This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation, combining low-rank user-item structure with a Hawkes process. The authors present a principled stochastic-process modeling of observations, which is a significant improvement over existing methods. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it a pleasure to read.

The proposed approach is novel and appears to be a significant contribution to the field of time-sensitive recommendation. The use of a Hawkes process to model the temporal dynamics of user behavior is a clever choice, as it allows for the capture of complex patterns in user activity. The combination of this process with a low-rank user-item structure is also a good idea, as it enables the model to generalize to new items and users.

The experimental results are impressive, with the proposed method outperforming several state-of-the-art baselines on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of their method, including an analysis of the impact of different parameters and a comparison with other methods.

One theoretical concern is the unclear maximization in Equation (8). It is not specified which parameter is being maximized, and the value of ρ* may be unbounded. This could potentially lead to issues with the convergence of the algorithm.

Additionally, the prescribed value of ρ in Theorem 2 is not used in the experiments. This weakens the relevance of the theorem, as it is not clear how the chosen value of ρ affects the performance of the algorithm.

In terms of experimental baselines, it would be beneficial to include SVD++ as a strong, time-aware baseline to strengthen the comparisons. This would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method and help to establish its superiority over other state-of-the-art methods.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of time-sensitive recommendation. The proposed approach is novel, and the experimental results are impressive. With some minor revisions to address the theoretical concerns and experimental baselines, this paper has the potential to be highly impactful.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of time-sensitive recommendation.
* The proposed approach is well-motivated and cleverly combines low-rank user-item structure with a Hawkes process.
* The experimental results are impressive, with the proposed method outperforming several state-of-the-art baselines.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
