- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation model combining collaborative filtering with temporal point processes (Hawkes process per user-item pair).  
- Models base intensity and self-excitation via low-rank user/item latent factor matrices, unifying static preferences with dynamic history-dependent behavior.  
- Presents a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm to learn model parameters.  
- Claims improvement over prior survival-analysis methods by enabling generalization to unobserved items.  
- Main strength: original and principled fusion of latent-factor models and self-exciting point processes.  
- Potential to advance state-of-the-art in time-aware recommendation systems.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently rigorous.  
- Missing a crucial baseline: an ensemble of a static model (e.g., SVD) and a temporal model (e.g., STiC).  
- Lacks critical hyperparameter selection details for both the proposed model and baselines, weakening reproducibility and fairness.  
- Datasets are poorly characterized for temporal dynamics (e.g., distributions of recurring events, temporal irregularity measures).  
- Manuscript clarity is poor with language errors and omitted methodological details (notably how kernel bandwidth σ is set and absence of sensitivity analysis).  
- Overall: promising idea but requires substantial revision in experiments, methodological detail, and presentation before publication.
