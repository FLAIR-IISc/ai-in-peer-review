- Proposes a novel point-process collaborative filtering model combining constant and self-exciting intensities via user/item latent vectors to capture temporal dynamics.  
- Models user-item occurrences as a Hawkes (self-exciting) process with history-dependent intensity, more expressive than Poisson models.  
- Introduces a low-rank latent-factor component to transfer knowledge from observed to unobserved user-item pairs.  
- Develops an efficient optimization algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.  
- Algorithm is shown to converge and is evaluated on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Empirical results reportedly outperform state-of-the-art baselines in predictive performance.  
- Lacks clear explanation and sensitivity analysis for the γ function bandwidth parameter σ.  
- Does not sufficiently explain selection and tuning of key hyperparameters (e.g., latent dimension).  
- Missing dataset characterization important for temporal modeling (e.g., counts of recurring events per pair, temporal irregularity).  
- Paper suffers from language issues, typos, and grammar errors that reduce clarity.  
- Method is original and practically significant, but presentation and experiments need strengthening.  
- Recommendation: revise to clarify σ and hyperparameter choices, add dataset temporal stats, and fix language/presentation.
