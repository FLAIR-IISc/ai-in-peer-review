- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework combining self-exciting point processes (Hawkes) and low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal user-item patterns.  
- Tackles two problems: recommending the most relevant item at the right moment and predicting users' next return time.  
- Models intensity per user-item as baseline (long-term preference) plus self-exciting component (past-event influence), both with low-rank structure.  
- Formulates a convex optimization with nuclear-norm regularization and develops an efficient algorithm mixing proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.  
- Provides convergence analysis (Theorem 2) showing O(1/t) rates and rigorous theoretical grounding.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) show scalability to millions of pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Demonstrates superior performance to baselines on recommendation and return-time prediction and argues Hawkes better explains temporal patterns than i.i.d. models.  
- Novelty is strong by bridging temporal point processes and collaborative filtering, extending prior temporal recommendation and low-rank factorization work.  
- Main technical contribution (convex formulation and optimization) is sound and potentially applicable beyond recommendation tasks.  
- Experimental weaknesses: lacks a sensible baseline ensemble (e.g., STiC + SVD) to fairly assess joint modeling benefits.  
- Missing analysis/sensitivity for key hyperparameter σ (triggering kernel bandwidth) and insufficient hyperparameter tuning details for method and baselines.  
- Dataset characterization is inadequate for temporal modeling—missing stats like recurring-event counts, temporal irregularity, and event density.  
- Paper clarity suffers from language issues, typos, and confusing notation (e.g., triggering kernel parametrization), hindering comprehension.  
- Overall assessment: good theoretical quality and originality, moderate significance, but presentation and incomplete experiments weaken the empirical case.  
- Recommendation: reject pending fixes—address baselines, hyperparameter analysis (especially σ), clarity/language, and dataset characterization before resubmission.
