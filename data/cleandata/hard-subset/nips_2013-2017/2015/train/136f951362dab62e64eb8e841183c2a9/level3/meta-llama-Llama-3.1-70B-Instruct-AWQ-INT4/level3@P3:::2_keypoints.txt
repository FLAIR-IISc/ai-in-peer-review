- Novel framework that combines self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns in user-item pairs.
- Targets two tasks: recommending the most relevant item at the right moment and predicting users’ next return time.
- Technically sound with a convex problem formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm.
- Well-organized paper with clear explanations of the model and temporal point process concepts.
- Writing quality needs improvement due to language issues, typos, and grammar errors.
- Insufficient detail on selection and tuning of key hyperparameters (e.g., latent dimension) for the method and baselines.
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets showing superior predictive performance versus state-of-the-art.
- Lacks dataset characterization relevant to temporal modeling (e.g., counts of recurring events per user-item pair, temporal irregularity).
- Original and interesting approach with practical applicability across domains (music, e-commerce, healthcare).
- Missing sensitivity analysis for the γ function bandwidth parameter σ and its effect on results.
- Should discuss method limitations and outline potential future research directions.
- Meets conference criteria for quality, originality, and significance but clarity must be improved.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, contingent on addressing the noted issues.
