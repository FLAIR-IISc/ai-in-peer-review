- Addresses time-sensitive recommendation: recommending the right item at the right time and predicting users' next interaction times.  
- Models user-item interaction sequences with a multivariate Hawkes process capturing long-term baseline preference and short-term self-excitation.  
- Generalizes to unobserved user-item pairs by assuming low-rank baseline (Λ0) and excitation (A) matrices, linking point processes to collaborative filtering.  
- Poses learning as a convex optimization minimizing negative log-likelihood with non-negativity and nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Proposes a scalable algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods to solve the optimization.  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) for item ranking and return-time prediction, showing superior performance over several baselines.  
- Originality: novel combination of low-rank matrix factorization with Hawkes point processes for recommendation.  
- Significance: addresses practical need for temporal/re-engagement modeling and contributes an optimization method of independent interest.  
- Modeling & empirical quality: Hawkes choice is well-justified; experiments show strong results and goodness-of-fit analysis (time-change theorem).  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and easy to follow.  
- Theoretical concern 1: Theorem 1’s threshold ρ* (Equation 8) is ambiguously defined and may be unbounded if certain norms are zero.  
- Theoretical concern 2: Theorem 2’s convergence condition (ρ > ρ*) is not connected to experiments since ρ is treated as a tuned hyperparameter.  
- Experimental weakness: Missing comparison to strong time-aware baselines like SVD++ adapted for temporal dynamics.  
- Overall summary: Strong, novel, and well-executed contribution to time-sensitive recommendation with minor theoretical clarifications and a missing baseline comparison.
