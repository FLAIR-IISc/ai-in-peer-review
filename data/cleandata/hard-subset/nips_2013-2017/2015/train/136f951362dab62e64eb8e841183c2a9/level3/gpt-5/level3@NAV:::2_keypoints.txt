- Contribution: Proposes a time-sensitive recommender that models each user–item pair as a self-exciting point process with low-rank coupling via matrices for baseline (Λ0) and excitation (A).  
- Method: Casts maximum likelihood estimation with nuclear-norm regularization as a convex program and develops a blended proximal/conditional-gradient algorithm with O(1/t) rate and cheap updates.  
- Empirics: Shows improvements on synthetic and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Soundness: Modeling and convexity are sound; likelihood is concave in nonnegative parameters and the optimization approach is reasonable.  
- Missing kernel details: The triggering-kernel bandwidth σ is unspecified and no sensitivity analysis to σ is provided.  
- Missing hyperparameter details: Latent dimensionality and regularization/tuning parameters (λ, β, ρ) lack selection and tuning details, hindering reproducibility.  
- Missing baseline: A sensible baseline (e.g., an ensemble combining STiC for return-time with SVD for cross-item generalization) is omitted.  
- Clarity issues: Paper is difficult to follow in places, contains typos/grammar errors, and needs clearer notation around γ and σ.  
- Algorithmic clarity: Pseudocode should more explicitly define variables and step-size/L constants.  
- Originality: The convex combination of Hawkes-style self-excitation and low-rank collaborative structure is novel and interesting.  
- Significance: Problem is important and approach is potentially useful, but empirical evaluation needs strengthening.  
- Requested experiments: Reviewer asks to add the STiC+SVD baseline, report and ablate σ (and α=0, Λ0-only, kernel family), document hyperparameter search spaces/criteria, and characterize datasets (repeats per pair, inter-arrival irregularity, singletons) plus standard top-k and time-calibration metrics.  
- Pros: Clear motivation, principled convex model, scalable algorithm, promising results, and novelty.  
- Cons: Missing key baseline, incomplete hyperparameter reporting and sensitivity analysis, limited dataset characterization, and clarity problems.  
- Recommendation: Lean toward rejection this round with concrete revision paths to make the paper strong.
