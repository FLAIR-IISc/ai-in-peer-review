- Introduces a novel framework for analyzing high-dimensional structured estimation with sub-exponential design matrices and noise.
- Establishes a relationship between Gaussian and exponential widths, allowing transfer of Gaussian-width results to exponential-width bounds.
- Enables analysis of commonly encountered sub-exponential designs, which is a significant practical contribution.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear problem setup, contributions, and technical exposition.
- Thorough background and related-work discussion that contextualizes the contributions.
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness and show slower error decay for sub-exponential versus Gaussian designs.
- Provides a unified framework for analyzing various norms, including the l1 norm and group-sparse norms.
- Proves a stronger RE condition result for Lasso and group-lasso problems, which is notable.
- Theoretical concerns: Eq. (8) and Theorem 2 need clarification on the maximization parameter and boundedness of ρ*.
- Experimental suggestion: include SVD++ as an additional baseline to improve evaluation quality.
- Paper could benefit from more discussion of the implications and potential applications of the framework.
- Overall high impact potential, with novel, well-motivated contributions likely to be widely adopted.
