- Paper proposes a low-rank Hawkes-based time-sensitive recommendation framework that recommends items at the right moment and predicts next return time.  
- Core model: per user–item Hawkes processes with exponential kernel, base intensity and excitation tied by two low-rank nonnegative matrices (Λ0, A).  
- Estimation cast as a convex problem with nuclear-norm regularization and an equivalent penalty to avoid SVDs; optimization mixes proximal gradient (nonnegativity) and conditional gradient/LMO (nuclear norm) with a convergence guarantee.  
- Evaluated on large-scale synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) for time-aware top-k recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Relates to temporal recommendation, count/tensor factorization, and point-process/Hawkes literature, contrasting with survival-analysis return-time methods.  
- Strength: principled connection of Hawkes processes with low-rank transfer to handle sparse user–item pairs.  
- Strength: convex objective and novel hybrid optimizer separating nonnegativity and nuclear-norm handling.  
- Strength: scalable implementation demonstrated on millions of pairs and 100M synthetic events with informative convergence plots.  
- Strength: unifies two practical tasks (ranking and return-time) within one framework.  
- Strength: well-motivated extensions (side information, nonparametric kernels, spatiotemporal tensors).  
- Weakness: practical motivation for return-time prediction is underdeveloped and lacks concrete use cases or offline–online impact proxies.  
- Weakness: missing simple, strong baselines for return-time (last-gap, median/mode gaps, per-user/item, seasonal Poisson, Weibull) and for recommendation (popularity/recency, seasonality-aware popularity).  
- Weakness: claim of widespread time-varying preferences is unsupported by exploratory data analysis or variance decomposition.  
- Weakness: experimental reporting under-emphasizes practical effect sizes and lacks statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: modeling choices need more discussion (Hawkes stability/branching ratio, identifiability between α and kernel bandwidth, sensitivity to hyperparameters, cross-excitation).  
- Weakness: presentation issues and minor typos; abrupt or unclear example transitions (Section 2).  
- Quality: technically sound formulation and carefully designed optimization, but empirical validation lacks critical baselines and significance testing.  
- Clarity: generally well organized and clear optimization section, but needs stability discussion, per-iteration complexity, task motivation, and typo fixes.  
- Originality: novel low-rank Hawkes formulation for dyadic transfer and a useful penalty reformulation enabling separate proximal/LMO steps.  
- Significance: potentially significant if temporal modeling yields meaningful gains, but current real-data experiments and analyses are insufficiently persuasive.  
- Suggestions: strengthen concrete use-case motivation (notification/ad timing, bidding, reminders) and consider categorical delay prediction for actionability.  
- Suggestions: add simple temporal baselines (last-gap, median-gap, seasonal Poisson, Weibull), recency-weighted/time-windowed popularity, and ablations (Λ0-only, Hawkes without low-rank, low-rank Poisson with seasonality).  
- Suggestions: quantify temporal nonstationarity and recurrence (ACF, seasonality strength, recurrence proportion) and disentangle Λ0 vs. A contributions.  
- Suggestions: report absolute effect sizes, perform statistical tests, show bucketed return-time accuracy, and provide runtime/memory and hyperparameter sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggestions: discuss Hawkes stability enforcement, identifiability, cross-item excitation, and cold-start handling with side features.  
- Arguments for acceptance: elegant convex scalable formulation, novel hybrid optimizer, unified treatment of two time-sensitive tasks with promising results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient practical motivation, missing baselines and ablations, limited analysis of when self-excitation matters, and presentation issues.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak reject; technically solid but requires stronger empirical grounding, baselines, ablations, and clearer motivation to be impactful.  
- Confidence: High (reviewer familiar with temporal recommendation, point processes, and large-scale convex optimization).
