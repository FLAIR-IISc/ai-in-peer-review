- Proposes a novel framework linking low-rank recommendation models with Hawkes self-exciting point processes to model recurrent temporal user behavior.
- Develops a convex optimization formulation and an efficient algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence.
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets (millions of pairs, hundreds of millions of events).
- Evaluated on recommending items at the right time and predicting next-return time.
- Technical foundations and convergence analysis are rigorous and well-motivated.
- The low-rank Hawkes model is an original contribution addressing limitations of prior work (e.g., Kapoor et al.).
- Paper fails to convincingly motivate the practical need for next-return time prediction or provide concrete use cases.
- Experimental baselines are insufficient—omits simple, interpretable return-time baselines (e.g., last delay, most frequent delay).
- Dataset analysis is limited; no evidence that time-varying preferences are widespread or what drives observed temporal patterns.
- Unclear practical impact: improvements shown are modest and not contextualized in terms of user or business metrics.
- Presentation issues (typo, unsupported examples) undermine clarity and credibility.
- Acceptance arguments: novel model connection, rigorous optimization with guarantees, and demonstrated scalability.
- Rejection arguments: weak practical motivation, missing simple baselines, insufficient dataset analysis, and unclear impact.
- Overall assessment: technically solid but lacks justification and empirical validation of practical significance.
- Recommendation: Marginal—approve only after stronger motivation, added baselines, and deeper dataset analysis.
