- Proposes a novel point-process collaborative filtering model combining constant and self-exciting intensities via user/item latent vectors.  
- Targets time-sensitive recommendation and next-visit prediction, emphasizing temporal dynamics of interactions.  
- Theoretical grounding with a convex optimization formulation and a scalable algorithm.  
- Empirical evaluation performed on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Novel and interesting combination of Hawkes processes with low-rank latent factor models.  
- Addresses a significant, practically relevant problem by modeling temporal aspects beyond static preferences.  
- Claim of scalability demonstrated on large datasets.  
- Missing a crucial baseline: a sensible ensemble of STiC and SVD for fair comparison.  
- Unclear justification and missing sensitivity analysis for the γ function bandwidth parameter σ.  
- Insufficient details on selection and tuning of key hyperparameters (e.g., latent dimension) for the method and baselines, harming reproducibility.  
- Dataset characterization lacks temporal properties (e.g., recurring events per user/item, event timing irregularity).  
- Paper clarity is poor with numerous language issues, typos, and grammatical errors.  
- Overall: promising and theoretically sound but experimental gaps and clarity issues warrant rejection and resubmission after substantial revision.
