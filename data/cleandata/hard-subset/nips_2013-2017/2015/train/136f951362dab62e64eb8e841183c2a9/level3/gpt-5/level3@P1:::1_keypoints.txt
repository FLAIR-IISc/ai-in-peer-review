- Proposes time-sensitive recommendation using Hawkes processes with low-rank (nuclear-norm) parameterization on base intensity Λ0 and self-excitation A.  
- Addresses two tasks: item ranking by instantaneous intensity λu,i(t) and next-return-time prediction via the point-process conditional density (approximated by thinning).  
- Derives a convex surrogate and an efficient algorithm combining proximal steps for nonnegativity with conditional gradient for nuclear norms.  
- Provides convergence analysis with an O(1/t) rate and demonstrates scalability on large synthetic data.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) versus Poisson, SVD, Poisson tensor factorization, and STiC survival/HMM baseline.  
- Reports improved ranking metrics and lower return-time MAE compared to baselines.  
- Relates to prior Hawkes and survival/time-to-event literature, with novelty in low-rank Hawkes parameterization and a scalable solver.  
- Strength: clear problem framing linking ranking and return-time tasks and mathematical motivation via Hawkes processes.  
- Strength: technically solid convex formulation with a clean separation of nonnegativity and rank penalties using auxiliary variables.  
- Strength: practical algorithmic contributions (proximal for nonnegativity, LMO via top singular vectors) and an accelerated scheme.  
- Strength: demonstrated scalability and diverse real-world datasets covering media, commerce, and clinical domains.  
- Strength: uses time-rescaling as a diagnostic to compare Hawkes vs survival assumptions.  
- Weakness: practical utility of next-return-time prediction is under-motivated; needs concrete use cases and mapping of prediction error to business/clinical gains.  
- Weakness: return-time baselines are incomplete; add simple strong heuristics (last inter-arrival, median/frequent inter-arrival, seasonality-aware, coarse delay buckets).  
- Weakness: missing statistical significance, practical effect sizes, and common recommendation metrics (Recall@k/NDCG) plus absolute baselines like popular-item.  
- Weakness: lacks ablation studies (Λ0 only, A only, different kernels including nonparametric) to identify source of gains and kernel robustness.  
- Weakness: unclear practical guidance for choosing penalty ρ; needs sensitivity analysis and heuristics for selection.  
- Clarity issues: add citations for examples, define symbols on first use, clarify per-iteration computational cost, memory, and test-time ranking computation/caching.  
- Presentation fixes: typos (e.g., “abel”), correct O(1/t) in abstract, and ensure legible labeled figures/axes.  
- Originality caveat: model only allows self-excitation per user–item pair; many domains have cross-excitation—discuss or experiment with low-rank mutually-exciting extensions.  
- Significance concern: unclear whether reported gains are user-perceivable; provide absolute metrics, CIs, and analyses of when time variation matters (cold-start, seasonality, bursts).  
- Additional suggestions: include cold-start performance vs history length, aggregate calibration/PIT/time-rescaling across sequences, and be cautious about clinical claims without prospective validation.  
- Pros for acceptance: solid, general methodology with scalable solver and convergence guarantees; meaningful advance over survival-only and static recommenders.  
- Cons for acceptance: underspecified practical impact for return-time tasks, missing simple baselines and ablations, and some clarity/presentation issues.  
- Overall assessment: technically competent and promising, but needs stronger empirical grounding and clearer motivation for return-time prediction; currently marginal.
