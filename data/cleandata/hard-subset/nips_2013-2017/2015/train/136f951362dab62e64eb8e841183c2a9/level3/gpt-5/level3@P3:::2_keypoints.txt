- Paper models time-sensitive recommendation with Hawkes-like self-exciting point processes and low-rank baseline/excitation matrices estimated via convex nuclear-norm penalties.
- Connects temporal point-process/Hawkes literature with low-rank collaborative filtering and survival/return-time models, enabling generalization across dyads.
- Novel combination: decomposes long-term affinity and short-term excitation in a low-rank, convex framework.
- Scalable optimization: prox–Frank–Wolfe blend avoids full SVDs and comes with an O(1/t) convergence guarantee.
- Evaluated on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) for time-aware ranking and return-time prediction with a time-change QQ diagnostic.
- Empirical results indicate improvements over Poisson, SVD, tensor baselines, and STiC on applicable tasks.
- Missing a strong ensemble baseline (e.g., STiC + SVD re-ranking) to test whether the model adds beyond combining existing components.
- Kernel bandwidth σ selection is not described and no sensitivity/ablation is provided, raising robustness concerns.
- Hyperparameter tuning details (λ, β, ρ, latent ranks for baselines, step sizes, stopping criteria) are insufficient, hurting reproducibility and fairness.
- Dataset statistics relevant to temporal modeling (events per dyad, inter-event CV/burstiness, seasonality, cold-start rates) are not reported.
- Theoretical guidance on choosing ρ (penalty vs constrained equivalence) is missing and ρ* is not practically estimable.
- Limited ablations: no baseline-only vs excitation-only comparisons, alternative kernels, or nuclear-norm strength studies.
- Writing and notation quality are problematic with typos, inconsistent symbols (γ0/Λ0, α/A), and unclear expressions (e.g., missing t in O(1/ )).
- Technically sound overall, but empirical evaluation and reporting fall short of best practices.
- High-level ideas are understandable but the paper needs substantial editing, unified notation, and explicit hyperparameter/experimental protocols.
- The modeling and optimization contributions are original and practically useful.
- Significance is moderate pending stronger empirical validation and clearer presentation.
- Key questions for authors: how σ is chosen and its sensitivity; how λ, β, ρ are tuned and sensitivity to ρ; baseline latent ranks/tuning; full dataset temporal statistics; stopping criteria, runtimes, and step-size/L estimation.
- Recommended improvements: add STiC+SVD and Poisson+STiC baselines, comprehensive ablations (baseline vs excitation, σ, kernel family, rank strength), expanded metrics and calibration plots, full dataset characterization, and major copyediting.
- Acceptance arguments: novel, principled, scalable approach with promising results; rejection arguments: incomplete experiments, missing baselines, limited sensitivity analyses, and clarity issues.
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger empirical validation and clearer presentation.
