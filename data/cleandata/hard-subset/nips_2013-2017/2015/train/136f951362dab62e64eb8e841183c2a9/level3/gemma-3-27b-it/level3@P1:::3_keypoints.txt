- Introduces a novel framework combining Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal user-item interaction patterns.  
- Models user activity as a Hawkes process and uses low-rank factorization to generalize across users and items, enabling recommendations for unseen items.  
- Develops a convex optimization formulation and an efficient algorithm with provable convergence guarantees.  
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance on synthetic and real-world datasets compared to several state-of-the-art methods.  
- Builds on prior recommender and temporal point process literature, addressing limitations of static preference models and existing temporal approaches.  
- Argues the approach is more suitable than matrix/tensor factorization for modeling time-varying user behavior.  
- Novelty: the Hawkes + low-rank combination is original and theoretically justified.  
- Technical soundness: strong theoretical grounding and a convergence result (Theorem 2).  
- Scalability: efficient optimization algorithm designed to handle large datasets (millions of pairs, hundreds of millions of events).  
- Empirical strength: consistently outperforms baselines including STiC and tensor factorization methods.  
- Clarity: the paper is well-written and organized, making technical contributions easy to follow.  
- Generalizability: framework can incorporate additional contextual features (user profiles, text, etc.).  
- Weakness: parameter tuning process for ρ, λ, and β is unclear; practical relevance of estimating ρ* is questionable.  
- Weakness: omission of a strong baseline (SVD++) weakens the empirical evaluation.  
- Weakness: theoretical conditions (e.g., specific ρ for Theorem 2) are not empirically validated, creating a theory–experiment disconnect.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations (sensitivity to kernel choice, low-rank assumption, failure scenarios).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically solid, strong empirical gains, clear writing, and potential field impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical difficulty selecting ρ may limit applicability, missing SVD++ comparison, and limited limitations discussion.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address parameter tuning, baseline inclusion, and limitations discussion.
