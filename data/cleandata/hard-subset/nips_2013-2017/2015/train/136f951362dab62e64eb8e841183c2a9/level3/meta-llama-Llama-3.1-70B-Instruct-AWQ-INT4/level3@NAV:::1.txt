**Paper Review: Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation and next-return time prediction by modeling recurrent user activities using self-exciting point processes and low-rank models. The authors develop an efficient optimization algorithm to learn the model parameters and demonstrate its superior scalability and predictive performance on both synthetic and real datasets.

**Strengths**

1. The paper tackles two important problems in recommendation systems: time-sensitive recommendation and next-return time prediction.
2. The proposed framework is novel and combines self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture recurrent user activities.
3. The authors develop an efficient optimization algorithm that scales up to large datasets and achieves state-of-the-art performance.
4. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework on both synthetic and real datasets.

**Weaknesses**

1. The motivation for predicting next-return time is unclear, and the paper needs to explain practical use cases for web companies.
2. The paper lacks simple baselines for return-time prediction, such as last time delay and most frequent delay.
3. The experimental results lack clarity on significance and practical impact compared to SVD and simple popular-item baselines.
4. The presentation has some issues, including typos and confusing examples.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel framework that combines self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture recurrent user activities. While the individual components are not new, the combination and application to time-sensitive recommendation and next-return time prediction are original.

**Significance**

The paper addresses two important problems in recommendation systems and demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on both synthetic and real datasets. The proposed framework has the potential to improve the engagement of users in modern web-services.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the paper could benefit from more detailed analysis and comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, but some sections, such as the introduction and related work, could be improved for clarity and concision. The presentation of the experimental results could also be improved for better understanding.

**Conclusion**

Overall, the paper is a good scientific contribution to the field of recommendation systems. While it has some weaknesses, the strengths and originality of the proposed framework make it a valuable contribution. With some revisions to address the weaknesses, the paper has the potential to be a strong contribution to the field.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting the paper with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above. The authors should provide more clarity on the motivation for predicting next-return time, include simple baselines for return-time prediction, and improve the presentation of the experimental results.
