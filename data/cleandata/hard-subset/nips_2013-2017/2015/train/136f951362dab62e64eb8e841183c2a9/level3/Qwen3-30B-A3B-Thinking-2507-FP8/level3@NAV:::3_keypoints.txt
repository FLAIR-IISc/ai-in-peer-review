- Proposes a novel framework combining Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank matrix models to capture recurrent temporal user-item interaction patterns.
- Develops an efficient distributed optimization algorithm with O(1/t) convergence that scales to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Targets two time-sensitive recommendation tasks: recommending items at optimal moments and predicting user return times.
- Demonstrates clear novelty and principled theoretical foundation using stochastic processes in an underexplored area.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) outperform existing methods.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and explains model and algorithm clearly.
- Practical significance is high due to distributed solvability and scalability to large datasets.
- Theoretical concerns: Equation (8) is ambiguous about which parameter is being maximized and suggests ρ* might be unbounded.
- Theorem 2 prescribes a ρ not used in experiments, reducing the theorem’s empirical relevance.
- Experimental comparison omits SVD++, a strong time-aware baseline that should be included.
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify theoretical points and add SVD++ in experiments.
