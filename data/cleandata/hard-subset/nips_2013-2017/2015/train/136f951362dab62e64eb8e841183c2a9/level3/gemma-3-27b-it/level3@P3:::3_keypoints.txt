- Proposes a novel distributed algorithm combining low-rank matrix factorization with a Hawkes process for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Technically sound, clearly written, and original in applying Hawkes processes to recommendation.
- Effectively combines temporal point processes and matrix factorization, enabling a generative model that can recommend new items.
- Low-rank structure and distributed solvability make the method scalable to large datasets.
- Explanation of Equation (8) and the parameter ρ is unclear, raising concerns about unboundedness and algorithm stability.
- Theorem 2 provides theoretical grounding but its relevance is weakened because the prescribed ρ is not used in experiments.
- Experiments omit comparison to SVD++, a strong time-aware baseline, weakening the empirical evaluation.
- Choice of tensor factorization variants as baselines is not well-justified.
- Clarity and organization are major strengths; figures and convergence analysis are well-presented.
- Originality is notable: integrating Hawkes processes with low-rank models and accommodating contextual information.
- Significance is high due to principled stochastic modeling, scalability, and promising empirical results across domains.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify Equation (8) and ρ, explain experimental ρ choice, and include SVD++ as a baseline.
