- Proposes a novel framework combining low-rank user-item matrices with self-exciting Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation.
- Addresses two tasks: recommending the right item at the right time and predicting users' next returning times.
- Introduces a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm mixing proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.
- Algorithm achieves an O(1/t) convergence rate and is theoretically grounded with convergence guarantees.
- Demonstrates scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events via a distributed solution.
- Shows superior predictive performance on synthetic and selected real-world datasets.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and clear in presenting technical details and experiments.
- Theoretical ambiguity: Equation (8) is unclear about the maximization parameter and possible unboundedness of ρ*.
- Theorem 2’s convergence guarantees are not exploited in experiments, reducing practical validation of theory.
- Experimental evaluation omits SVD++, a notable time-sensitive recommendation baseline.
- Generalizability is not fully explored—performance on datasets with different temporal dynamics is uncertain.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled approach, scalable and efficient algorithm, clear writing, and strong empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: theoretical ambiguities, limited experimental baselines, and unclear generality across domains.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to resolve theoretical ambiguities and expand experimental baselines.
