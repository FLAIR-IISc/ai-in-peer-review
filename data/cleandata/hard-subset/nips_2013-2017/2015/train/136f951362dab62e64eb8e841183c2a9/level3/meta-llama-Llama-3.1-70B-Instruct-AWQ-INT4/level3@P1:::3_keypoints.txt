- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework combining low-rank user-item structure with a Hawkes process.  
- Presents a principled stochastic-process modeling of observations, improving on existing methods.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Using a Hawkes process to model temporal dynamics is a clever choice that captures complex user activity patterns.  
- Combining Hawkes with low-rank structure helps generalize to new users and items.  
- Experimental results are strong: method outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Evaluation is thorough, including parameter-impact analysis and method comparisons.  
- Theoretical concern: Equation (8) has an unclear maximization (unspecified parameter) and ρ* may be unbounded, risking convergence issues.  
- Theorem 2 prescribes a value of ρ that is not used in experiments, weakening the theorem’s practical relevance.  
- Suggestion: include SVD++ as a strong, time-aware baseline to strengthen empirical comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Rating: 8/10.
