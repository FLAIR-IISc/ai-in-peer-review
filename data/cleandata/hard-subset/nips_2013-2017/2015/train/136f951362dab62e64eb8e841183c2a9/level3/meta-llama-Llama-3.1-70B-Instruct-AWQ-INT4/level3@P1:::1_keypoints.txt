- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation method using a low-rank self-exciting Hawkes process to model time-varying user preferences and predict next-return time.
- Develops an efficient optimization algorithm to train the model.
- Evaluated on one synthetic and two modest real-world datasets, showing effectiveness in item recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides a thorough related-work review.
- Reviewer asks for dataset analysis to justify how widespread time-varying preferences are and to identify underlying causes (e.g., new popular songs).
- Motivation for predicting next-return time is unclear; paper should explain practical use cases and benefits for web companies and users.
- Experimental comparisons lack clarity on statistical significance and practical impact versus SVD and simple popular-item baselines.
- Needs more detailed analysis and stronger baselines to quantify the magnitude of improvements.
- Presentation issues: typo ("abel"→"able") and a confusing, unsupported switch to retweets/link-creation examples in Section 2 without references.
- Scores well on Quality and Clarity but weaker on Originality and Significance due to limited real-world motivation and insights.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound method, well-evaluated, and clearly presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient real-world motivation, weak baselines/comparisons, and unclear practical impact.
- Overall verdict: OK/marginal with recommendation for minor revisions to address dataset analysis, motivation, baselines, significance, and presentation.
