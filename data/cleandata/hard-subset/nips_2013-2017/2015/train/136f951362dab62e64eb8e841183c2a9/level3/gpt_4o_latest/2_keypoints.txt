- Novelty: Introduces a new combination of self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to jointly model temporal and recurrent user-item interactions.  
- Practical significance: Addresses time-sensitive recommendation challenges and can incorporate contextual information (spatial, textual, profiles).  
- Optimization algorithm: Proposes an efficient algorithm combining proximal and conditional gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence and scalability to large datasets.  
- Empirical validation: Demonstrates superior performance on synthetic and real-world datasets (Last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC II) for item recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Baseline limitations: Lacks comparison to an ensemble baseline combining STiC and SVD, which could be a stronger benchmark.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient details on hyperparameter selection (e.g., σ, latent dimensions) and sensitivity analysis, hindering reproducibility.  
- Dataset characterization: Datasets are not fully described in terms of recurring events and irregular temporal patterns, limiting context for results.  
- Clarity and presentation: Paper contains language issues, typos, and dense technical derivations that need clearer explanations and visual aids.  
- Argument for acceptance: Presents a novel, practical, and technically sound approach with strong empirical gains and scalable optimization.  
- Argument against acceptance: Reproducibility and interpretability are limited by missing sensitivity analyses and dataset statistics, and presentation needs improvement.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing baseline comparisons, hyperparameter analysis, dataset details, and presentation quality.
