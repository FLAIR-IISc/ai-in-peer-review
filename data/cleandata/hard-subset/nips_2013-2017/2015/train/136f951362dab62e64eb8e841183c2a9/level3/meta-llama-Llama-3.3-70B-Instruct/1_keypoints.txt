- Proposes a method for high-dimensional structured estimation with norm-regularized estimators (e.g., Lasso) under sub-exponential design and noise.  
- Establishes theoretical results for norm-regularized estimation for any norm and for distributions heavier-tailed than sub-Gaussian.  
- Main technical contribution: relates Gaussian and exponential widths of sets, enabling extension of Gaussian-width-based results to the sub-exponential case.  
- Provides a unified framework for non-asymptotic error analysis and structured recovery in norm-regularized regression.  
- Paper is well-written, with a clear introduction and well-organized technical sections.  
- Detailed proofs are provided for the theoretical results.  
- Experimental section is limited but gives some preliminary insight into method performance.  
- Strength: generalizes existing sub-Gaussian results to the sub-exponential setting.  
- Strength: offers novel insights into the relationship between Gaussian and exponential widths with potential broader impact.  
- Weakness: experimental validation is sparse and needs expansion.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed empirical comparison with existing methods, especially for the sub-Gaussian case.  
- Weakness: assumption that design matrix and noise are sub-exponential may not hold in all practical settings.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality; reviewer recommends acceptance with suggested improvements to experiments and comparisons.
