- Novelty: Combines self-exciting (Hawkes) point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation, a unique and promising approach.  
- Theoretical foundation: Presents a convex optimization formulation and proves an O(1/t) convergence rate.  
- Scalability: Algorithm scales to large datasets (millions of pairs, hundreds of millions of events).  
- Empirical performance: Outperforms several baselines on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Generality: Framework can incorporate additional contextual information (user profiles, spatial features).  

- Baseline comparison weak: Lacks stronger baselines or ensembles (e.g., STiC + SVD) that combine temporal and affinity strengths.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient discussion and analysis of hyperparameter tuning, especially the triggering kernel bandwidth (σ) and latent dimension.  
- Dataset characterization: Missing detailed dataset stats (e.g., recurring events per pair, temporal irregularity measures) to contextualize results.  
- Clarity and presentation: Writing contains language errors and typos that hinder understanding; needs careful revision.  
- Limited error analysis: No breakdown of error types (e.g., early/late/inappropriate recommendations) to guide improvements.  

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound, empirically effective, scalable, and extensible to context.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Empirical evaluation weakened by baseline/hyperparameter issues, unclear presentation, and limited dataset characterization.  

- Overall assessment: Valuable contribution that is theoretically and empirically promising but requires revisions; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions suggested: Strengthen baseline comparisons, provide hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, improve dataset descriptions, and revise writing for clarity.
