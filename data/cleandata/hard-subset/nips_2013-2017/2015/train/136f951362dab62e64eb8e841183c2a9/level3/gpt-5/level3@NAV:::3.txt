This paper proposes a time-sensitive recommender that couples low-rank user–item structure with a Hawkes process for recurrent user activities. The authors cast parameter learning as a convex problem with nuclear-norm regularization and nonnegativity constraints, and design an efficient algorithm that blends proximal gradient (for constrained smooth parts) with conditional gradient/LMO (for nuclear norms), with a provable O(1/t) convergence component. The method addresses two tasks—ranking items at the right moment via intensities and predicting users’ return times via the conditional density—and reports consistent improvements over Poisson, tensor factorization, SVD, and STiC on synthetic and three real datasets. The approach is well grounded in temporal point processes and related to prior work on survival analysis and tensor factorization, but differs by explicitly modeling self-excitation and sharing via low-rank structure.

Quality: The modeling choice is principled and the optimization is thoughtfully engineered. Empirical results are strong and at scale. However, there are theoretical clarity issues: (i) Eq. (8) (the penalty-equivalence condition) is unclear about what quantity is maximized—does the maximization range over ρ? Please specify the variables and feasible set. As written, ρ* can be unbounded if the denominator approaches zero; provide conditions ensuring finiteness (e.g., exact-penalty assumptions, uniqueness, or bounded sublevel sets) or switch to an augmented Lagrangian treatment. (ii) Theorem 2’s convergence guarantee is stated for a fixed ρ, but the experiments tune ρ without using the theorem’s prescribed threshold, weakening its practical relevance. A sensitivity study that shows invariant solutions beyond a computable lower bound on ρ* would strengthen the claims. Experimental baselines should include SVD++/TimeSVD++ (Koren, 2008/2009) as strong time-aware comparators; these are standard for temporal recommendation and would solidify the empirical case.

Clarity: The paper is well written, organized, and easy to follow. Please clarify Eq. (8), define all constants in Theorem 2, and provide more details on the distributed/parallel implementation and wall-clock comparisons to ADMM-based solvers.

Originality: Combining Hawkes processes with low-rank sharing in a convex, scalable formulation appears novel for time-sensitive recommendation.

Significance: High. The modeling framework, scalability, and performance gains make this likely to influence both research and practice.

Pros for acceptance:
- Principled stochastic-process modeling with shared low-rank structure
- Convex formulation and efficient algorithm with convergence guarantees
- Strong results on synthetic and real data; scalable implementation
- Clear and well written

Cons against acceptance:
- Ambiguities around Eq. (8)/ρ* and its boundedness
- Theorem–experiment mismatch regarding ρ
- Missing strong time-aware baselines (SVD++/TimeSVD++)
- Limited statistical reporting (e.g., CIs) and sensitivity analyses
