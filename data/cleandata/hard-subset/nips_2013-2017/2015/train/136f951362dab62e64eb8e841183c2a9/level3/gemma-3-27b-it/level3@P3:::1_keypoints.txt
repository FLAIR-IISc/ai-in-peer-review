- Proposal: time-sensitive recommendation using Hawkes processes + low-rank models to capture user dynamics and predict return times, with a convex formulation, an algorithm claiming O(1/t) convergence, evaluated on synthetic and two real datasets.
- Strength: attempts to model temporal aspects of user behavior, an under-explored area in recommendation.
- Strength: connecting Hawkes processes with low-rank models is potentially valuable and novel.
- Strength: efficient optimization algorithm with a favorable claimed convergence rate suggesting scalability.
- Originality concern: needs broader contextualization beyond Kapoor et al. (2014, 2015); insufficient discussion of related point-process and temporal methods.
- Motivation weak: justifications (ad bidding, healthcare) are superficial and feel disconnected from real industry problems.
- Missing baselines: simple return-time predictors (last observed delay, most frequent delay) and coarser delay-category approaches are omitted.
- Experimental clarity lacking: no statistical significance tests, confidence intervals, or effect sizes; MAE-only reporting is insufficient.
- Practical impact unclear: reported improvements over SVD and simple baselines are not shown to be substantial or user-perceptible.
- Presentation problems: typos (e.g., “abel”), and jarring, unsupported digressions (retweets/link-creation) that confuse focus.
- Overall assessment: technically competent but marginal — significant revisions needed on motivation, baselines, experiments, and presentation.
- Acceptance arguments: novel modeling combination, efficient optimizer, empirical validation; Rejection arguments: weak motivation, missing comparisons, unclear significance, presentation and contextualization issues.
