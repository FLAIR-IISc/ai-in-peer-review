This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation, combining low-rank user-item structure with a Hawkes process. The approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

The key strengths of the paper are:

* The proposed algorithm is novel and appears to be effective in addressing the time-sensitive recommendation problem.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm, including a convergence rate analysis.
* The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including both synthetic and real-world datasets.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, particularly SVD++.
* The theoretical analysis is thorough, but some of the equations, such as Eq. (8), are unclear and could be better explained.
* The prescribed value of ρ in Theorem 2 is not used in the experiments, which weakens the relevance of the theorem.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results and the potential applications of the proposed framework.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a novel and effective approach to time-sensitive recommendation. However, there are some areas for improvement, particularly in terms of comparison with existing methods and clarity of the theoretical analysis.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and effective approach to time-sensitive recommendation.
* The theoretical analysis is thorough and provides a good understanding of the algorithm's behavior.
* The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed framework.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods.
