

This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation by connecting self-exciting point processes (Hawkes processes) with low-rank matrix models to capture recurrent temporal patterns in user-item interactions. The authors develop an efficient distributed optimization algorithm with O(1/t) convergence rate that scales to millions of user-item pairs. The work addresses two key time-sensitive recommendation tasks: item recommendation at optimal moments and predicting user returning times. 

The paper's strengths are significant. It demonstrates clear novelty in combining Hawkes processes with low-rank structure for time-sensitive recommendations, a relatively underexplored area. The theoretical foundation using stochastic processes is principled, and the empirical results on both synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) show superior performance over existing methods. The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the model and algorithm. The distributed solvability and scalability to large datasets (hundreds of millions of events) represent substantial practical significance.

However, several theoretical concerns weaken the paper's contribution. Equation (8) is ambiguous about which parameter is being maximized, and the analysis suggests ρ* might be unbounded, raising questions about the theoretical foundation. Theorem 2's prescribed ρ is not used in experiments, diminishing its relevance to the empirical results. Additionally, the experimental comparison lacks SVD++, a strong time-aware baseline that would better validate the approach against state-of-the-art methods.

The paper excels in clarity and originality, and the significance is high due to the modeling choice and practical applicability. While the theoretical concerns are serious, they don't completely undermine the work, and the empirical results remain compelling. The missing baseline (SVD++) is a notable omission that weakens the comparative claims.

Overall, this is a strong paper with significant contributions to time-sensitive recommendation. The theoretical concerns should be addressed in revision, but the core contribution is valuable and well-executed. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify the theoretical aspects and include SVD++ in the experiments.
