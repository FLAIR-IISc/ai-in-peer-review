- Proposes a novel framework linking self-exciting point processes (Hawkes) with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns in user-item interactions.  
- Targets two tasks: recommending the most desirable item at the right moment and predicting a user’s next return time.  
- Presents a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm with O(1/t) convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical results show performance gains on synthetic and real datasets (Last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II).  
- Advances beyond prior temporal recommender work by enabling generalization to new items via low-rank modeling.  
- The Hawkes–low-rank connection and theoretical convergence analysis are novel and technically solid contributions.  
- Model can incorporate additional contextual information, enhancing flexibility.  
- Weak motivation for focusing on time-varying preferences; lacks dataset analysis to show prevalence and cause of temporal variation.  
- Practical utility of predicting exact return times is unclear; coarser time categories might be more realistic and useful.  
- Experimental evaluation misses simple but important baselines (e.g., last-observed delay, most frequent delay, popularity-based recommendation).  
- Results lack statistical significance testing, effect-size discussion, and assessment of practical impact on user/business metrics.  
- Dataset descriptions are cursory; more detail on dataset characteristics is needed.  
- Minor presentation issues: typo (“abel”→“able”) and abrupt, under-cited example transitions (retweets/link creation).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel Hawkes–low-rank link, solid theory and convergence, demonstrated empirical gains, contextual extensibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak problem justification, unclear utility of precise return-time prediction, missing baselines, insufficient experimental analysis.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and novel but borderline; recommend acceptance only after major revisions addressing motivation, baselines, and experimental rigor.
