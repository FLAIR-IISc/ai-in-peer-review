- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework combining Hawkes self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to predict both what to recommend and when a user will return.  
- Models event intensity per user-item as a sum of a static baseline and a history-dependent self-exciting component, with both matrices assumed low-rank to generalize to unobserved pairs.  
- Formulates parameter estimation as a convex problem with nuclear norm regularization and proposes an efficient algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of the optimization algorithm, claiming an O(1/t) convergence rate.  
- Evaluates the model on synthetic and real-world datasets for item recommendation and return-time prediction tasks.  
- Strength: original and significant combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank collaborative filtering to jointly address “what” and “when.”  
- Strength: intuitive model decomposition capturing long-term preferences (baseline) and short-term self-excitation (history-dependent component).  
- Strength: solid technical contribution in optimization algorithm design and convergence analysis.  
- Weakness: manuscript clarity suffers from grammatical errors and awkward phrasing, hindering readability.  
- Weakness: missing crucial experimental details, notably the selection and sensitivity of the triggering kernel bandwidth σ.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning methodology (search space, validation procedure) is not described, harming reproducibility and fairness of comparisons.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks a stronger, realistic baseline (e.g., ensemble of matrix factorization for ranking + STiC for return-time) for comparison.  
- Weakness: datasets are insufficiently characterized temporally (e.g., events per pair, inter-event time stats, sequence lengths) to justify the point-process model.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem with a novel, well-motivated model and valuable optimization theory.  
- Against acceptance: poor writing and missing experimental/hyperparameter details (especially σ) and weaker empirical benchmarking reduce reproducibility and empirical confidence.  
- Overall: interesting and promising approach but needs substantial revision to improve clarity and provide missing experimental details and stronger baselines.
