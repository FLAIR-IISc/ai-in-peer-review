- Paper addresses time-sensitive recommendation: item recommendation at the right moment and predicting users' next return time.  
- Proposes a novel framework linking self‑exciting Hawkes processes with low‑rank matrix models to capture long‑ and short‑term effects per user‑item pair.  
- Uses low‑rank assumptions on baseline intensity and self‑excitation matrices to generalize across sparse user‑item pairs.  
- Formulates parameter estimation as a convex optimization with nuclear‑norm regularization.  
- Develops an efficient algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with fast convergence and scalability.  
- Evaluated on one synthetic and three real datasets, showing improvements over static MF and recent temporal baselines and ability to recommend new items.  
- Strength: novel and original combination of point processes and low‑rank models for temporal recommendation.  
- Strength: technically sound convex formulation and a scalable optimization algorithm with theoretical guarantees.  
- Strength: addresses a significant, practical problem of when users will return and what they will want.  
- Weakness: insufficient empirical motivation that user preferences are continuously time‑varying; lacks exploratory analysis of temporal patterns (seasonality, trends, etc.).  
- Weakness: unclear practical utility of predicting exact next return time and no concrete use cases or suggestion to predict coarser intervals.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation omits simple but crucial baselines for return‑time (e.g., last observed delay, mean/most frequent delay).  
- Weakness: unclear whether reported performance gains are practically significant (e.g., effect on top‑k recommendations vs simple popularity).  
- Weakness: presentation issues—examples sometimes feel disconnected from core task and there are minor typos.  
- Summary: promising, novel, and technically strong work but currently undermined by weak motivation and experimental validation; reviewer leans toward rejection unless motivation and baselines/analysis are strengthened.
