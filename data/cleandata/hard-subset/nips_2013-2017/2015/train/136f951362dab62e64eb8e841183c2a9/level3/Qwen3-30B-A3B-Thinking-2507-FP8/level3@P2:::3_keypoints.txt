- Proposes a novel framework linking Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Formulates a convex optimization with nuclear-norm regularization and develops an algorithm combining proximal-gradient and conditional-gradient methods.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves an O(1/t) convergence rate (Theorem 2) for the proposed algorithm.  
- Empirical results show superior performance on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II), handling millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Addresses the practical problem of recommending the right item at the right time and predicting user return times, with applications in marketing and healthcare.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- Theoretical concern: Equation (8) is unclear about which parameter is maximized and the definition of the threshold ρ* may be unbounded.  
- Weak linkage between theory and practice: the prescribed ρ in Theorem 2 is not used in experiments.  
- Missing an important experimental baseline: SVD++ (a strong time-aware method) is not included.  
- Insufficient experimental details on runtime and memory requirements for the largest datasets, limiting scalability claims.  
- Pro acceptance arguments: novel modeling connection, strong theoretical guarantees, superior empirical performance, practical significance, clear exposition.  
- Con acceptance arguments: unresolved ρ* issue, omission of SVD++ baseline, unclear ρ in Equation (8), and insufficient scalability metrics.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution that merits acceptance pending minor revisions to address ρ* clarity, include SVD++ comparison, and provide more scalability details.
