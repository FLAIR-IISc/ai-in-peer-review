- Paper proposes a time-sensitive recommendation method that models time-varying user preferences and predicts users’ next-return time, trained with an efficient optimization algorithm and evaluated on one synthetic and two modest real datasets.  
- Reviewer questions whether time-varying preferences are widespread and asks for dataset analysis to justify the claim and identify causes (e.g., new popular songs vs. other patterns).  
- Motivation for predicting next-return time is unclear; the paper needs to explain practical use cases for web companies.  
- Missing simple baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last time delay, most frequent delay) and recommendation to consider coarser, easier-to-predict delay categories (short/medium/long/very long).  
- Experimental results lack clarity on significance and practical impact compared to SVD and simple popular-item baselines—unclear if improvements are modest or substantial and user-perceivable.  
- Presentation issues: typo “abel”→“able” and confusing, unsupported switch to retweets/link-creation examples in section 2 without references.  
- Overall: tackles two problems but lacks real-world motivation, sufficient baselines, and deeper analysis; paper judged OK/marginal.
