- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework combining Hawkes self-exciting processes with low-rank matrix models to handle recurrence and unobserved user-item pairs.
- Models long-term static preferences via a base intensity matrix and short-term history-dependent dynamics via a self-exciting component.
- Assumes low-rank parameter matrices and casts estimation as a convex optimization problem regularized by the nuclear norm.
- Develops an efficient, scalable optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with strong convergence properties.
- The convex formulation and principled stochastic-process modeling are technically sound and provide theoretical guarantees.
- Theorem 1’s definition of the threshold ρ* is unclear (unspecified max operator) and may be ill-defined if denominators vanish, raising concerns about boundedness.
- Practical utility of Theorem 1 is limited because ρ* is hard to compute and ρ is tuned empirically, weakening the theory–practice connection.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and shows strong scalability and performance on large synthetic and real datasets.
- Experimental comparison should include stronger baselines such as SVD++ (and temporal variants) to better validate against state-of-the-art time-aware collaborative filtering.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.
- The integration of Hawkes processes with low-rank models is original and distinguishes the work from prior art.
- Work is significant for recommender systems by addressing when users will engage as well as what they will engage with, with practical applications (ads, engagement, planning).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/principled model, solid convex formulation, efficient algorithm, strong empirical results, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear theoretical treatment of ρ* and limited practical relevance of that theory, and missing stronger experimental baselines.
