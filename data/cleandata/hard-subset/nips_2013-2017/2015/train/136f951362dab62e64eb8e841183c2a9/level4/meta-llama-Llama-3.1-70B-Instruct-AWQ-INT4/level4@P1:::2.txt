**Summary**

This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. Specifically, for each pair of user and item, the event density is the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity depending on the past events. Two sets of intensity are computed by two sets of user and item latent vectors respectively. The corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation, and empirical studies have been conducted to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed methods over other commonly used alternatives.

**Quality**

The overall quality of the paper is reasonably good but not great. While the authors have made a good effort to present their ideas, there are some areas that require improvement. For instance, the experimental studies can be enhanced by including additional baselines, such as a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD, as suggested in the review. Furthermore, the authors should provide more details on how some important hyperparameters are chosen for both baseline methods and the proposed method, e.g., the dimension of the latent vectors.

**Clarity**

The paper presentation can be improved in terms of language, typos, and grammar errors. Some sentences are wordy or awkwardly phrased, which may hinder understanding. For example, the abstract could be rephrased for better clarity and concision.

**Originality**

The proposed method is an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models. The authors have successfully integrated these two concepts to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items, which is a novel contribution to the field of recommender systems.

**Significance**

Both the problem and the method have good practical values. The proposed approach has the potential to improve the performance of recommender systems by incorporating temporal and recurrent patterns in user behavior. The authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of their method through empirical studies on synthetic and real-world datasets.

**Suggestions for Improvement**

1. Include additional baselines, such as a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD, to further demonstrate the advantage of the proposed method.
2. Provide more details on how some important hyperparameters are chosen for both baseline methods and the proposed method, e.g., the dimension of the latent vectors.
3. Improve the presentation by rephrasing wordy or awkwardly phrased sentences and correcting typos and grammar errors.
4. Provide more details about the characteristics of the two real-world datasets, such as the number of recurring events per user/item pair and the irregularity of the temporal pattern.
5. Discuss the sensitivity of the results to the bandwidth parameter σ in the self-exciting process.
