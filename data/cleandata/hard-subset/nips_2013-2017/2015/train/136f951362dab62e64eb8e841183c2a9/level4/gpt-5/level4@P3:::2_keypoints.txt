- Proposes a time-sensitive recommendation framework modeling recurrent user behavior with low-rank Hawkes processes combining a nonnegative base rate and self-excitation per user–item pair.  
- Shares information across user–item pairs by learning base-rate and excitation matrices under nuclear-norm (low-rank) regularization with nonnegativity, yielding a convex estimation problem.  
- Introduces an efficient solver that mixes proximal gradient (for nonnegativity) and conditional gradient (for nuclear norm) updates with provable O(1/t) convergence and cheap iterations.  
- Evaluates on large synthetic data and three real datasets (Last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC II) for ranking by intensity and return-time prediction, outperforming Poisson, STiC, SVD, and tensor factorization baselines.  
- Builds on prior Hawkes/process and temporal recommendation work, extending parameter learning by adding low-rank structure for recommendation and contrasting with survival-analysis and CF approaches.  
- Strength: clear unified formulation for time-sensitive ranking and return-time prediction with interpretable nonnegative low-rank inductive bias.  
- Strength: convex, scalable learning with a novel blended optimization algorithm and empirical scalability.  
- Strength: qualitative validation via time-rescaling supports Hawkes assumptions.  
- Weakness: unclear protocol and sensitivity for kernel bandwidth σ and other hyperparameters (λ, β, ρ) — needs selection protocol and sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: stability/branching-ratio concerns for exponential kernels are not enforced or analyzed; need discussion/checks of stability across pairs.  
- Weakness: baselines could be stronger—add hybrid (SVD + timing), renewal-process, and Hawkes without low-rank sharing to test benefits of joint modeling.  
- Weakness: evaluation incomplete — report standard ranking metrics (MRR, Recall@k, NDCG@k) and stronger return-time calibration (time-rescaling KS) beyond a QQ plot.  
- Weakness: dataset characterization missing — include per-user/item event counts, recurrence rates, inter-event distributions, seasonality, and sparsity patterns.  
- Weakness: practical/reproducibility details lacking — initialization, power-method/SVD parameters, stopping criteria, wall-clock time, memory, and handling/tuning of ρ should be reported (and possibly an adaptive/continuation scheme).  
- Weakness: identifiability concerns between Λ0 and A (baseline vs. absorbed low-frequency history) need discussion and regularization implications.  
- Assessment: modeling and estimation are technically sound and original; clarity can be improved (unify notation, define symbols, algorithm pseudocode, complexity table).  
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually clean model, convex scalable learning with theoretical algorithmic contribution, and strong empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing hyperparameter/stability details, weaker baselines, and limited reporting of ranking metrics and dataset temporal stats.  
- Suggested revisions: add sensitivity/stability analyses, stronger hybrid/renewal baselines, standard ranking metrics, dataset temporal statistics, more implementation details, and clearer notation/proofs.
