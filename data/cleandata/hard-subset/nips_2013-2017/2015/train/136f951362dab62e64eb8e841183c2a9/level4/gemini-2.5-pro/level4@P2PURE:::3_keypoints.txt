- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation algorithm combining Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix factorization, optimized in a distributed manner and showing superior empirical performance.
- Approach is well-founded by modeling rating observations with stochastic processes.
- Theoretical issue: Equation (8) and Theorem 2 are ambiguous about which parameter the maximum is over; if over ρ, ρ* might be unbounded.
- Practical/theoretical disconnect: experiments do not use Theorem 2 to select ρ, weakening the theorem’s practical utility.
- Experimental baseline omission: should include SVD++ as a strong comparative benchmark.
- Clarity: the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Originality: the Hawkes + low-rank combination appears to be a novel contribution.
- Significance: tackles an important real-world problem and the distributed, high-performing model has strong practical potential.
