- Introduces a novel framework linking self-exciting point processes (Hawkes) with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and user return-time prediction.  
- Core idea: capture recurrent temporal patterns in user–item interactions beyond static preference models.  
- Presents a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm with provable O(1/t) convergence.  
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real-world data show superior performance to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Addresses an important problem by incorporating temporal dynamics that many existing methods overlook.  
- Key novelty: combining Hawkes processes with low-rank structure to enable transfer across user–item pairs and efficient parameter estimation.  
- Theoretical analysis of the optimization algorithm strengthens the technical contribution.  
- Framework can potentially incorporate additional contextual features (profiles, text, spatial data).  
- Weak motivation: needs stronger evidence for the prevalence and causes of time-varying user preferences.  
- Motivation for predicting exact return time is weak—should include concrete practical use cases (e.g., ad bidding).  
- Experimental evaluation lacks simple but important baselines (last inter-event, most frequent delay, popularity-based recommendations).  
- Suggests framing return-time prediction as a classification task (short/medium/long delays) for realism and interpretability.  
- Needs more detailed analysis and quantification of performance gains over SVD and practical significance.  
- Minor issues: typo ("abel" → "able") and abrupt, under-contextualized introduction of social network examples in Section 2.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, strong theory, scalability, empirical gains, and extensibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak motivation for exact return-time focus, missing baselines, insufficient experimental analysis, and some clarity issues.  
- Overall recommendation: marginal accept, contingent on addressing the raised concerns.
