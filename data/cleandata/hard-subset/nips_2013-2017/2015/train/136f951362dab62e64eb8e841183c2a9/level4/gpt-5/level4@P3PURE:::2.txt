-- Summary --
The paper proposes a point-process collaborative filtering model to capture temporal and recurrent structures between users and items. For each user–item pair, the event intensity is modeled as the sum of a constant base rate and a self-exciting component that depends on past events. These two components are parameterized by two separate sets of user and item latent vectors. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and present empirical studies showing advantages over commonly used alternatives.

While promising, the experimental evaluation can be strengthened. A particularly sensible additional baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Since the authors argue that [12, 13] cannot recommend completely new items—whereas SVD can—comparing against this ensemble would be informative.

In addition, for the γ function used in the self-exciting process, please clarify how the bandwidth parameter σ is set and provide a sensitivity analysis with respect to σ. More generally, the paper lacks details on how important hyperparameters are chosen for both the baselines and the proposed method, such as the dimensionality of the latent vectors.

It would also help to include more details about the two real-world datasets. Because the focus is on temporal structure, please report simple statistics such as the number of recurring events per user–item pair and measures of temporal irregularity.

-- Quality --
Overall quality is reasonably good but not outstanding. The methodological contribution is clear and the optimization is well motivated, but the empirical section would benefit from stronger baselines, clearer hyperparameter selection protocols, and additional analysis of temporal characteristics.

-- Clarity --
The presentation can be improved. There are language issues, typos, and grammatical errors that hinder readability. Clarifying the model description (especially the roles of the two latent vector sets), detailing hyperparameter choices, and adding dataset statistics would improve clarity.

-- Originality --
The method is an interesting and novel combination of point processes with latent factor models.

-- Significance --
Both the problem and the proposed method have practical relevance. The approach addresses temporal and recurrent behavior in recommendation, and the empirical results suggest benefits over standard baselines. However, the experimental study and the presentation need improvement, particularly with respect to baselines (e.g., STiC+SVD ensemble), hyperparameter selection and sensitivity (e.g., σ in the γ function), and dataset characterization.
