- Paper proposes a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations and next-return-time prediction, with an efficient optimization algorithm and experiments on one synthetic and two modest real datasets.  
- Unclear whether time-varying user preferences are common; authors should analyze the real datasets and investigate causes (e.g., new popular songs vs. other patterns).  
- Motivation for predicting next returning time is weak; explain practical use cases and benefits for companies.  
- Missing simple baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last time delay, most frequent delay).  
- Predicting exact return time may be too ambitious; suggest predicting delay buckets (short/medium/long/very long) instead.  
- Experimental results need clearer quantification of the performance lift over SVD: magnitude, user-perceptible impact, and comparison to recommending popular items (with daily recalculation).  
- Small fixes: correct typo "abel" → "able"; rephrase Section 2 which abruptly mentions retweets/link creation without references.  
- Overall assessment: addresses two problems but lacks clear motivation, missing baselines and analyses; paper is acceptable but marginal.
