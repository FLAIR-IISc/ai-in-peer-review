- Proposal: Combines Hawkes self-exciting processes with low-rank matrix factorization to model time-sensitive recommendations and recurrent user-item events.  
- Methodology: Represents baseline intensities and self-excitation via low-rank matrices and formulates parameter estimation as a convex problem solved with a proximal + conditional gradient algorithm.  
- Evaluation: Tested on item recommendation at a given time and next-return-time prediction, showing improvements on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Strength — Originality: Novel integration of temporal point processes with collaborative filtering for recurrent activity modeling.  
- Strength — Significance: Addresses an important practical problem (when users engage) and proposes an optimization approach of independent interest.  
- Weakness — Baselines: Missing comparison to natural strong baselines like an ensemble of STiC and standard matrix factorization (e.g., SVD).  
- Weakness — Experimental details: Lacks hyperparameter selection and sensitivity analysis (e.g., bandwidth σ for triggering kernel, latent dimension tuning), hurting reproducibility.  
- Weakness — Dataset analysis: Insufficient temporal dataset statistics (recurrence counts, inter-event time distributions, burstiness) to justify temporal modeling claims.  
- Weakness — Clarity: Manuscript needs proofreading to fix grammar, typos, and awkward phrasing.  
- Overall: Interesting and valuable approach but would be stronger with more competitive baselines, detailed hyperparameter tuning, deeper dataset characterization, and improved clarity.
