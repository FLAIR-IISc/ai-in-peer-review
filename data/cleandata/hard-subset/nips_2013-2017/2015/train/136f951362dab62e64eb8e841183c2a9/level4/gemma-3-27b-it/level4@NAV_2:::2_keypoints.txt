- Proposes a point-process collaborative filtering model with event density = baseline + self-exciting intensity, estimated by a convex optimization algorithm.  
- Connects self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture temporal and recurrent user–item interactions.  
- Offers a unified framework for item recommendation and returning-time prediction, and can generalize to recommend new items.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: provable O(1/t) convergence and claimed scalability to millions of user–item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Empirical results show improvements over several state-of-the-art methods on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Strengths: technically sound formulation, clear problem setup, strong theoretical results, and demonstrable scalability.  
- Authors acknowledge possible extensions, such as adding contextual information (user profiles, text, spatial features).  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently comprehensive; missing comparisons such as an STiC + SVD ensemble baseline.  
- Lacks hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, weakening confidence in robustness of results.  
- Choice of bandwidth σ in the exponential triggering kernel is not fully justified and should be explored.  
- Hyperparameter tuning procedures (e.g., latent dimensions) are under-specified, hurting reproducibility and fairness of comparisons.  
- Dataset reporting is lacking—need descriptive statistics (recurrence distribution, temporal irregularity) to justify point-process modeling.  
- Minor clarity and grammar issues that could be improved for readability.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem with clear benefits for personalization and predicting return times.  
- Recommendation: promising paper with solid foundations; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
