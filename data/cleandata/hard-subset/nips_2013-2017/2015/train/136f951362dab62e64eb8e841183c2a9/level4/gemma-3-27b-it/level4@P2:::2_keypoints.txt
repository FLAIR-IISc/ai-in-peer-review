- Introduces a collaborative filtering model using temporal point processes to capture recurrent user-item interactions.
- Connects self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to predict both recommended items and users’ next return times.
- Uses convex optimization for parameter estimation with an algorithm that achieves O(1/t) convergence.
- Demonstrates scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Shows improved empirical performance over state-of-the-art methods on synthetic and real datasets.
- Novelty: first to combine point processes and low-rank modeling enabling generalization to new items.
- Theoretical strength: convex formulation and formal convergence guarantees.
- Generality: framework can incorporate additional contextual information (profiles, text, location).
- Weakness: missing comparison to a potentially strong baseline (ensemble of STiC + SVD).
- Weakness: lacks hyperparameter details and sensitivity analysis (e.g., latent dimensions, kernel bandwidth σ).
- Weakness: real-world datasets are insufficiently characterized (recurrence counts, temporal irregularity).
- Weakness: presentation contains awkward phrasing and needs clearer editing.
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, scalable, and empirically effective.
- Against acceptance: evaluation needs stronger baselines and hyperparameter analysis; reproducibility/presentation concerns.
- Overall: recommend acceptance conditional on addressing baselines, hyperparameter sensitivity, and clarity.
