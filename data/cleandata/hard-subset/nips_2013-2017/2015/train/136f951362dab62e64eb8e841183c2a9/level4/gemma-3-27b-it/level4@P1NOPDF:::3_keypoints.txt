- Presents a novel, distributed algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation combining low-rank user-item structure with a Hawkes process, showing improved performance over existing methods.
- Uses a principled stochastic-process-based modeling of observations (ratings).
- Primary theoretical concern: Eq. (8) is ambiguous about which parameter is maximized; if maximization is over ρ, ρ* may be unbounded.
- Theorem 2 appears not to be used in choosing ρ in experiments, reducing its practical importance.
- Experimental baseline omission: SVD++ (a strong, Netflix-challenge-winning method) is not included and should be considered.
- Current baseline selection is not wholly inadequate but would be strengthened by adding SVD++.
- Clarity: the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Originality: the approach is novel given that time-sensitive recommendation is relatively unexplored.
- Significance: the problem is impactful and the proposed method’s low-rank + Hawkes modeling and distributed solution are promising.
