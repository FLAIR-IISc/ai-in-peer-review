- Proposes a new method for time-sensitive recommendations and next-return-time prediction, optimized with an efficient algorithm.
- Experiments use one synthetic dataset (for scalability) and two modest-sized real-world datasets.
- Central claim that user preferences are time-varying is not substantiated and needs empirical analysis on the real datasets.
- If preferences vary, the paper should investigate causes (e.g., emergence of new popular items) to provide insight.
- Motivation for predicting a user’s next returning time is unclear; the paper must explain practical uses for web companies.
- Missing simple baselines for return-time prediction, e.g., last-observed delay and most-frequent delay.
- Predicting exact returning times is hard; suggest reformulating as discrete delay categories (short/medium/long/very long).
- Improvement over SVD is reported but the magnitude and practical significance of the lift are not clearly assessed.
- Experiments should include a strong dynamic popularity baseline (daily-updated popular items) for context.
- Paper lacks sufficient motivation for relevance and potential impact of the problems addressed.
- Several important analyses and baselines are missing, weakening the empirical evaluation.
- Minor issues: typo on page 3 ("abel" → "able") and a jarring, under-referenced paragraph introducing retweets/link creation that needs rewriting.
- Overall assessment: paper is OK/marginal due to motivation, analysis, and baseline shortcomings.
