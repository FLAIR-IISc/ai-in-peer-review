- Proposes a novel, scalable algorithm combining low-rank user–item structure with Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Uses a principled stochastic process framework as a strong theoretical foundation.  
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance and supports distributed implementation for scalability.  
- Theoretical clarity issue: Eq. (8) maximization variable is unspecified and it’s unclear whether the optimizer ρ* is bounded under stated conditions.  
- Theorem 2’s practical relevance is unclear: it isn’t referenced or used when selecting ρ in experiments, reducing its perceived value.  
- Baseline comparison omission: SVD++ (a key time-aware baseline) is missing, weakening empirical validation.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with effective exposition of methods and contributions.  
- Originality: integrating low-rank matrix modeling with Hawkes processes for this problem is novel and timely.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical gap—modeling temporal dynamics in recommender systems with potential real-world impact.  
- Minor fixes recommended: specify optimization variable in Eq. (8) and boundedness of ρ*, include or acknowledge omission of SVD++ in experiments, and clarify how Theorem 2 informs practical choice of ρ.  
- Overall: promising and compelling approach with strong results; addressing the theoretical and experimental concerns would substantially improve rigor and impact.
