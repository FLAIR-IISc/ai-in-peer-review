- Motivation for time-varying preferences is weak—authors should analyze real datasets to show that user preferences vary and characterize the nature of that variation (global trends vs. user-specific patterns).
- Motivation for predicting return time is insufficient—paper needs concrete use cases and explanations of practical impact for online services.
- Missing baselines for return-time prediction—compare against simple predictors like last observed delay or most frequent delay per user.
- Task formulation for return-time prediction may be impractical as regression—consider reframing as classification into short/medium/long return windows.
- Experimental results lack context—unclear whether improvements over SVD are practically meaningful; include a strong time-aware "most popular" baseline recalculated daily.
- Minor typo: "and is abel to capture" should be "and is able to capture."
- Section 2 abruptly mentions retweets and link creation without references or clear connection; rephrase for clarity and flow.
- Overall: promising idea but weakened by insufficient motivation and incomplete experimental evaluation (missing baselines and analyses).
