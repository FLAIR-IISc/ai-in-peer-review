- Proposes a time-aware recommendation combining low-rank user–item modeling with Hawkes-process temporal dynamics and supports distributed optimization.
- Method is principled, modeling ratings with stochastic processes, and shows superior empirical performance on reported datasets.
- Theoretical concern: Equation (8) is ambiguous about which parameter is maximized (e.g., ρ) and whether the optimal ρ* can be unbounded; feasible set, assumptions, and regularization need clarification.
- Theoretical concern: Theorem 2 does not guide the experimental choice of ρ, reducing its practical relevance; authors should either use it to select ρ or explain why it’s inapplicable and provide an alternative selection procedure.
- Baselines: Recommend adding SVD++ as a strong, time-aware baseline to make comparisons more comprehensive and convincing.
- Clarity: Paper is well written, notation and algorithmic details are clear, and the distributed optimization is described accessibly.
- Originality: Combining low-rank modeling with Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation appears novel.
- Significance: Time-aware recommendation is practically important; distributed implementation and empirical gains suggest meaningful impact.
