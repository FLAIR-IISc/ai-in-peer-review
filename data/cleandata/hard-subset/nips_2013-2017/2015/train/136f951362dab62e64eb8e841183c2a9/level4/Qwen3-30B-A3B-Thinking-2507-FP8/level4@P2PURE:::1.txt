

The paper introduces a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations that accounts for time-varying user preferences, distinguishing it from existing approaches. It also proposes predicting a user's next return time. Learning employs an efficient optimization algorithm developed by the authors. Experiments demonstrate efficiency on large-scale synthetic data and results on two modest-sized real-world datasets.

First, the prevalence of time-varying user preferences requires stronger justification. Please analyze the real datasets used in experiments to substantiate this claim. If observed, is the variation primarily driven by new popular items (e.g., songs), or are there other underlying patterns?

Second, the motivation for predicting next return time must be clarified before claiming it as a contribution. Specifically, how would a web company utilize this prediction, and for what practical purpose?

Regarding baselines for return time prediction: simple alternatives like the last observed time delay or the most frequent delay were not tested. Moreover, predicting exact return times appears overly challenging; a more feasible task would be categorizing delays (e.g., short: 1-5 days, medium: 5-14 days, long: 14-33 days, very long: >33 days).

Experimental section: The significance of performance gains over SVD is unclear—should this be considered groundbreaking (order-of-magnitude improvement) or modest? Would users perceive a meaningful difference in recommendations? Additionally, how do the results compare to recommending daily-updated popular items, a strong baseline that is difficult to surpass, particularly in practice?

2) Section 2, first paragraph: The abrupt shift to discussing retweets and social network link creation without references is confusing; rephrase to maintain context.  

The paper addresses two problems (time-sensitive recommendations and user return time prediction) but lacks clear motivation for their relevance or impact. Several key baselines and analyses are missing (detailed above). Overall, the paper is acceptable but marginal.
