- Proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation using low-rank user-item structure and Hawkes processes.  
- Method is amenable to distributed computation and reports superior performance to compared methods.  
- Strength: adopts a principled stochastic process framework for modeling rating observations.  
- Theoretical clarity concern: maximization in Eq. (8) over parameter ρ is unclear and ρ* may be unbounded.  
- Practical relevance concern: Theorem 2 is not used in experiments—ρ is chosen heuristically, reducing the theorem’s practical impact.  
- Baseline concern: SVD++ (a leading time-sensitive recommendation method) is not included in comparisons.  
- Clarity: paper is well-structured and easy to follow.  
- Originality: novel integration of low-rank matrix modeling with Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with substantial real-world potential.
