- Proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation using Hawkes self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal user-item patterns.
- Develops an efficient algorithm with O(1/t) convergence that scales to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.
- Using Hawkes processes to model temporal dynamics is a novel contribution compared to existing methods.
- Thorough evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets showing superior scalability and predictive performance versus state-of-the-art competitors.
- Balances theoretical rigor (convergence analysis) with practical considerations (scalability, context incorporation, real-world applicability).
- Provides a comprehensive related-work discussion (survival analysis, hidden semi-Markov models, tensor factorization) and explains how the method addresses their limitations.
- Experimental baselines are incomplete—SVD++ is not included and should be added for a stronger comparison.
- Presentation of Theorem 2 is unclear: the empirical choice of ρ is not clearly related to the theoretical result and needs more explanation.
- Overall assessment: novel, impactful, and practically compelling for time-sensitive recommendation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, thorough evaluation, and strong theory-practice balance.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing important baseline (SVD++) and unclear practical use of Theorem 2.
- Reviewer rating: 8/10 with confidence 8/10.
