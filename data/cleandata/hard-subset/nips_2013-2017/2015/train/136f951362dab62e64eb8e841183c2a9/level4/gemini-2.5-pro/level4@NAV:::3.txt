This work introduces a novel approach for time-aware recommendation that models the low-rank properties of the user-item matrix while leveraging a Hawkes process to capture temporal dynamics. The proposed algorithm is amenable to distributed computation and is shown to outperform existing baselines.

**Quality:** The authors have taken a principled, stochastic process-based approach to model the rating data, which is commendable. However, I have a few suggestions for improvement.

A question arises regarding the theoretical analysis, specifically concerning Equation (8) and Theorem 2. It is unclear which parameter is being maximized over in Eq. (8); if it is ρ, the potential for an unbounded ρ* should be addressed. Furthermore, the practical significance of Theorem 2 is diminished, as it does not appear to guide the selection of ρ in the experimental section.

Regarding the experimental evaluation, the comparisons could be strengthened by including SVD++ [17] as a baseline. Given its strong performance in time-sensitive settings, such as the Netflix challenge, its inclusion would provide a more comprehensive benchmark. Nevertheless, the current selection of baseline methods is reasonable.

**Clarity:** The manuscript is clearly written and the presentation is easy to follow.

**Originality:** The work appears novel, addressing the relatively new and important problem of time-sensitive recommendation with a unique combination of techniques.

**Significance:** The research has the potential for significant impact, as time-aware recommendation is a critical problem area. By providing a new, high-performing, and distributable algorithm based on low-rank matrix properties and Hawkes processes, this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field.
