- Novel method linking low-rank matrix factorization with Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Proposes an efficiently solvable algorithm with demonstrated scalability.  
- Shows improved performance over existing methods on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Theoretical concern: Eq. (8) is ambiguous about the parameter over which the maximum is taken.  
- Theoretical concern: If the maximization in Eq. (8) is over ρ, ρ* might be unbounded.  
- Practical concern: Theorem 2 is not used to select ρ in experiments, reducing its practical relevance.  
- Experimental suggestion: Include SVD++ as a strong baseline for more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Reviewer does not consider the absence of SVD++ a critical flaw.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and easy to follow with sufficient implementation details.  
- Originality: The approach appears novel and represents a new direction for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Significance: The method has potential impact by capturing temporal dynamics to improve recommendations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/principled approach, strong empirical results, efficient scalable algorithm, clear presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: minor theoretical issues around Eq. (8)/Theorem 2 and lack of comparison to SVD++.
