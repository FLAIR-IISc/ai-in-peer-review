## Review: Point Process Collaborative Filtering

**Summary:** This paper introduces a novel collaborative filtering model based on point processes, designed to capture the temporal and recurrent interactions between users and items. The model defines the event density for each user-item pair as the sum of a constant baseline intensity and a self-exciting intensity derived from past events. This self-excitation is governed by latent vectors representing both users and items. The authors provide an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and demonstrate, through empirical evaluation, that their approach outperforms commonly used alternatives.

However, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened. A logical baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. The authors correctly identify that STiC and similar methods struggle with recommending entirely new items, a limitation SVD can address; comparing against this ensemble would provide a valuable point of reference. Additionally, the paper lacks detail regarding the selection of the bandwidth parameter, σ, used within the γ function governing the self-exciting process. An analysis of the model’s sensitivity to variations in σ would be beneficial. Furthermore, the methodology lacks specifics on hyperparameter selection for both the proposed model and the baselines, such as the dimensionality of the latent vectors. Providing this information would increase reproducibility and understanding. 

Finally, more detailed characteristics of the real-world datasets used would be helpful. Given the focus on temporal structure, reporting basic statistics – such as the average number of recurring events per user/item pair and a measure of the irregularity of the temporal patterns – would provide valuable context.

**Quality:** The overall quality of the paper is reasonably good, though it falls short of being excellent.

**Clarity:** The presentation would benefit from careful revision. The language is sometimes imprecise, and the manuscript contains typos and grammatical errors that detract from its readability.

**Originality:** The proposed method represents an interesting and potentially fruitful combination of point process modeling and latent factor approaches to collaborative filtering.

**Significance:** Both the problem addressed – modeling temporal dynamics in user-item interactions – and the proposed method have considerable practical value. The model provides a potentially powerful approach to capturing and leveraging the temporal and recurrent nature of recommendation data. Addressing the points raised regarding experimental rigor and presentation would further enhance the impact of this work.
