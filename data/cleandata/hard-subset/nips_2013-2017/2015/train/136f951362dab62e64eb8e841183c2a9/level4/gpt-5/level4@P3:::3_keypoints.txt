- Paper models time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction with per-pair Hawkes processes and shares strength via low-rank, nonnegative baseline and excitation matrices estimated with nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Authors propose an equivalent-penalty formulation with auxiliary variables and a hybrid proximal/conditional-gradient optimizer with O(1/t) convergence and strong empirical performance on large synthetic and real datasets.  
- Novelty: couples Hawkes point processes with low-rank convex estimation for recommendation, differing from survival-analysis and multivariate Hawkes literature.  
- Theory concern 1: The equivalence threshold ρ* is defined using unknown optima; the reviewer asks for the maximization set, boundedness argument, and a computable/practical rule or bound for ρ.  
- Theory concern 2: Theorem 2 requires a Lipschitz constant L to set step-sizes; the paper should say how L is obtained in practice (backtracking/bounds) and sensitivity to this choice.  
- Modeling concern 1: Need discussion/constraints ensuring Hawkes stability (branching ratio < 1) and identifiability between baseline and excitation parameters.  
- Modeling concern 2: Return-time estimation via Ogata’s thinning Monte Carlo can be costly/high-variance; for exponential kernels use tractable survival/mean computations or report median/mode as alternatives.  
- Experiments: add stronger temporal baselines (e.g., TimeSVD++/temporal MF), renewal or seasonally modulated Poisson models, and a multivariate Hawkes baseline if feasible.  
- Metrics: report top-k ranking metrics (HR@k, NDCG@k), calibration/NLL for return times, confidence intervals, and variability to assess significance.  
- Scalability: provide wall-clock runtime, peak memory, and details on SVD linear-oracle computations (power/Lanczos iterations, tolerances) and any parallelization used.  
- Clarity: define all quantities at first use (e.g., the set in ρ*), fix minor typos, and trim redundant introduction text.  
- Originality: method is a novel and principled combination of Hawkes processes, low-rank nonnegativity, and a hybrid convex optimization approach.  
- Significance: approach is practically important and extensible to contextual features, with potential impact in recommendation and event modeling.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled continuous-time recurrent modeling, convex low-rank nonnegative formulation, efficient hybrid optimization with guarantees, and strong large-scale empirical results including cold-start-style evaluation.  
- Cons against acceptance: ρ* is defined via intractable quantities with limited practical guidance, missing key temporal baselines, limited discussion on Hawkes stability/identifiability and step-size choice, and costly return-time estimation alternatives not explored.  
- Overall: strong, well-motivated paper recommended for acceptance pending clarifications on ρ*, step-size/Lipschitz choice, stability, and addition of stronger temporal baselines and standard evaluation metrics.
