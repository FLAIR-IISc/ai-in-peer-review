This paper proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation tasks by leveraging the low-rank structure in user-item matrices and incorporating the Hawkes process. Notably, the algorithm is amenable to distributed solutions and demonstrates superior performance compared to existing methods.

Quality: Overall, the paper adopts a principled approach to modeling observations (ratings) using stochastic processes. However, several points warrant attention.

Firstly, in the theoretical section, Eq. (8) and Theorem 2 merit clarification. Specifically, it is unclear over which parameter the maximum is taken in Eq. (8). If it is indeed over \rho, then it is possible for \rho* to become unbounded. Furthermore, the choice of \rho in the experiments does not utilize Theorem 2, diminishing its significance.

Another concern lies in the experimental baselines. Considering SVD++ [17], a top-performing algorithm in the Netflix challenge, as an additional baseline could enhance the paper's quality. Although the current baselines are satisfactory, incorporating SVD++ would provide a more comprehensive comparison.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

Originality: The paper appears to be novel, particularly given the relatively recent emergence of time-sensitive recommendation as a distinct problem.

Significance: The proposed algorithm has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of time-sensitive recommendation. By effectively capturing the low-rank structure in user-item matrices and leveraging the Hawkes process, the algorithm offers a promising solution for real-world applications.
