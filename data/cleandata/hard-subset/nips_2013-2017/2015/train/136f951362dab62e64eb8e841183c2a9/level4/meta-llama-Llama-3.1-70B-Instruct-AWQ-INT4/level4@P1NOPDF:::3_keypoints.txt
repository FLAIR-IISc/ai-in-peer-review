- Proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation combining low-rank user-item structure with Hawkes processes; distributed and empirically outperforms existing methods.  
- Strength: takes a principled approach by modeling ratings with stochastic processes.  
- Theoretical issue: Eq. (8) is unclear about which parameter the maximum is taken over (is it over ρ?), and whether ρ* can be unbounded.  
- Theorem 2 issue: the theorem is not used to choose ρ in experiments, reducing its practical importance.  
- Experimental baselines: missing strong comparators such as SVD++; should be included for fair benchmarking.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Originality: appears novel given the relative novelty of time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Significance: the scalable/distributed algorithm and improved results are noteworthy for an important application area.
