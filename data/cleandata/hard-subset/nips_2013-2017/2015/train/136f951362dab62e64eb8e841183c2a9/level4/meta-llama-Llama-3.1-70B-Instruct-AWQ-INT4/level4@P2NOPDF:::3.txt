This paper proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation by leveraging the low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and incorporating the Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed manner and demonstrates superior performance compared to existing methods.

Quality: Overall, I appreciate the principled approach taken by the authors to model the observations (ratings) using stochastic processes. However, I have a few concerns that require clarification.

Firstly, I would like to seek clarification on the theoretical aspect presented in Eq. (8) and Theorem 2. Specifically, it is unclear over which parameter the maximum is taken in Eq. (8). Is it over \(\rho\)? If so, can \(\rho\)* be unbounded? Furthermore, I noticed that Theorem 2 is not utilized when selecting \(\rho\) in the experiments, which diminishes its significance.

Additionally, I suggest considering the inclusion of SVD++ [17], a top-performing algorithm in the Netflix challenge, as a baseline in the experiments. Although the current baselines are satisfactory, incorporating SVD++ would further enhance the quality of the evaluation.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it a pleasure to read.

Originality: In my opinion, the paper presents a novel contribution, as time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively new and emerging problem.

Significance: I believe that time-sensitive recommendation has the potential to make a significant impact. The proposed algorithm, which combines low-rank structure and Hawkes process, offers a promising solution to this problem. Its ability to be solved in a distributed fashion and achieve better results than existing methods further underscores its significance.
