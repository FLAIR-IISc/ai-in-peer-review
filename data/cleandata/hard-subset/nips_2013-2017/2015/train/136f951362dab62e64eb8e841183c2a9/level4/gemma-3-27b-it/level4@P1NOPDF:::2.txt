-- Summary --
This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model designed to capture the temporal and recurrent structure of user-item interactions. Specifically, the event density for each user-item pair is modeled as the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity, the latter of which depends on past events. These intensities are computed using two sets of latent vectors – one for users and one for items. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and demonstrate, through empirical studies, the advantages of their proposed method compared to commonly used alternatives.

However, the experimental studies could be improved. A sensible baseline to include would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. The authors argue that STiC [12, 13] struggles to recommend completely new items, a limitation that SVD could potentially address; therefore, comparing against this ensemble would be valuable. In addition, regarding the γ function used for the self-exciting process, the bandwidth parameter σ is not clearly defined. It would be useful to understand how σ is set and how sensitive the results are to variations in its value. Furthermore, the paper lacks details regarding the selection of other important hyperparameters for both the baseline methods and the proposed method – for example, the dimensionality of the latent vectors.

Finally, providing more detailed characteristics of the two real-world datasets would be helpful. Given that the paper focuses on temporal structure, discussing simple statistics like the number of recurring events per user/item pair, and the irregularity of the temporal patterns, would strengthen the analysis.

-- Quality --
The overall quality is reasonably good, but not exceptional.

-- Clarity --
The paper’s presentation could be improved regarding language, typos, and grammatical errors.

-- Originality --
The proposed method represents an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models.

-- Significance --
Both the problem addressed and the proposed method have good practical value. This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. The corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation and empirical studies have been done to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed methods to other commonly used alternatives. However, the experimental studies and the presentation could be improved.
