This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation by connecting self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture the recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs. The authors present a convex formulation and develop an efficient algorithm that scales up to problems with millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed model through experiments on synthetic and real datasets, achieving superb predictive performance compared to state-of-the-art competitors.

The paper relates to previous work in the field, particularly the recent work of Kapoor et al. [12, 11], which also addresses the problem of predicting returning time for music streaming services. However, the proposed model differs in its ability to recommend new items in future time, making it more versatile and practical.

The strengths of the paper include its novel combination of point processes and low-rank models, its efficient optimization algorithm, and its demonstration of effectiveness through experiments. The authors also provide a clear and concise presentation of their model and algorithm, making it easy to follow and understand.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement. The experimental studies can be enhanced by including additional baselines, such as a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. The authors should also provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the choice of the bandwidth parameter σ for the self-exciting process. Additionally, the paper would benefit from more information on the characteristics of the real-world datasets, such as the number of recurring events per user/item pair and the irregularity of the temporal patterns.

In terms of quality, the paper is reasonably good but not great. The presentation can be improved in terms of language, typos, and grammar errors. The originality of the proposed method is high, as it combines point processes and low-rank models in a novel way. The significance of the paper is also high, as it addresses a practical problem with good practical values.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective framework for time-sensitive recommendation.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model through experiments on synthetic and real datasets.
* The paper addresses a practical problem with good practical values.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The experimental studies can be improved by including additional baselines and providing more details on hyperparameter tuning.
* The presentation can be improved in terms of language, typos, and grammar errors.
* The paper may benefit from more information on the characteristics of the real-world datasets.
