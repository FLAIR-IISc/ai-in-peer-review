- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework combining self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent user-item temporal patterns.  
- Models event intensity as baseline + self-exciting component with low-rank parameter constraints for efficient estimation.  
- Targets two tasks: recommending the right item at the right time and predicting next user return time.  
- Presents a convex optimization formulation and an algorithm mixing proximal gradient and conditional gradient with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Demonstrates scalability to millions of user-item pairs and applicability to include contextual features.  
- Theoretical analysis (Theorem 2) is rigorous and establishes a novel convergence result for the combined algorithm.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) shows superior performance over multiple baselines and strong scalability.  
- Originality: low-rank formulation of self-exciting point processes enables generalization to new items, unlike prior work.  
- Significance: addresses practically important problems for web services (music, e-commerce, healthcare) with large-scale applicability.  
- Clarity: generally well-organized with clear math and thorough experimental design, including useful ablation studies.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed hyperparameter selection and sensitivity analysis (e.g., σ, latent rank, regularization parameters).  
- Weakness: comparison misses a meaningful baseline ensemble (e.g., STiC + SVD) that could handle cold-start items.  
- Weakness: manuscript contains grammatical issues, unclear phrasing, and imprecise description of the triggering kernel and algorithmic complexity.  
- Weakness: insufficient dataset details (e.g., events per user-item pair, temporal irregularity) to contextualize results.  
- Recommendation: promising, technically sound paper that would be acceptable after minor revisions to improve clarity, hyperparameter analysis, baseline comparisons, and dataset reporting.
