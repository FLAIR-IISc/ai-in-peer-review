This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. Specifically, for each pair of user and item, the event density is the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity depending on the past events. Two sets of intensity are computed by two sets of user and item latent vectors respectively. The corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation and empirical studies have been done to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed methods to other commonly used alternatives.

The paper is well-motivated, and the proposed method is an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models. The authors provide a clear explanation of the model and its components, including the use of self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture the recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs. The optimization algorithm is also well-explained, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of its convergence properties.

However, the experimental studies can be improved. For example, another sensible baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Since the authors argued that [12, 13] are not able to recommend completely new items, which can be covered by SVD, and it would be valuable to compare the proposed method to this simple ensemble. Additionally, the authors could provide more details about how the hyperparameters are chosen for both baseline methods and the proposed method, e.g., the dimension of the latent vectors.

The paper presentation can also be improved in terms of language, typos, and grammar errors. There are several places where the text is unclear or awkwardly phrased, which can make it difficult to follow. Furthermore, the authors could provide more details about the characteristics of the two real-world datasets, such as the number of recurring events per user/item pair and how irregular the temporal pattern is.

Overall, the paper has good practical values, and the proposed method is a valuable contribution to the field of recommender systems. However, the experimental studies and the presentation can be improved to make the paper more compelling.
