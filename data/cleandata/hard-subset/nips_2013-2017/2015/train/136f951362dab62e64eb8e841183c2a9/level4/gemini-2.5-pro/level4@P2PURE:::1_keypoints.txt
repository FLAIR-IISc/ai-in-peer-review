- Proposes a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations and a model for predicting users' next return time, with an efficient optimization algorithm.  
- Experiments use one large synthetic dataset for efficiency and two modest real-world datasets for evaluation.  
- The claim that time-varying user preferences are widespread needs stronger empirical justification via analysis of the real datasets.  
- If preferences vary, the paper should identify underlying patterns and whether variation is due to external trends or user-specific behavioral shifts.  
- Motivation for predicting next return time is weak; the paper should clarify practical applications and how companies could use such predictions.  
- Return-time prediction lacks simple baselines (e.g., most recent return interval, most frequent return interval) for comparison.  
- Predicting exact return time may be impractical; consider reframing as a classification task over coarse time buckets (short/medium/long/very long).  
- Improvement over the SVD recommendation baseline is unclear; the practical significance and perceptual impact of the gains should be quantified.  
- Missing comparison to a strong dynamic popularity baseline (e.g., daily-updated popular items), which is often hard to beat in practice.  
- Minor issues: typo on page 3 ("abel" → "able") and a confusing, unreferenced paragraph in Section 2 about retweets/link creation.  
- Overall: addresses interesting problems but lacks sufficient motivation, baselines, and analyses, making the contribution marginal in current form.
