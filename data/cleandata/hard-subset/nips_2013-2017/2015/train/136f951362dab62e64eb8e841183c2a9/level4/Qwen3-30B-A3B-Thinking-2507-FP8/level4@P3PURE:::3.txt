

This paper presents a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation by leveraging low-rank structure in the user-item matrix and modeling temporal dynamics via Hawkes processes. The approach is amenable to distributed computation and demonstrates superior performance compared to existing methods.  

**Quality:** The paper adopts a principled stochastic process framework for modeling rating observations, which is a strong aspect. However, two concerns arise:  
1. **Theoretical Clarity (Eq. (8) and Theorem 2):** The maximum in Eq. (8) is taken over parameter ρ, but it is unclear whether ρ* could be unbounded. Furthermore, Theorem 2 is not utilized in the experimental parameter selection (ρ is chosen heuristically), diminishing its practical significance.  
2. **Baseline Comparison:** SVD++ [17], the Netflix Prize winner and a leading method for time-sensitive recommendation, should be included as a baseline. Its absence weakens the comparative evaluation, though the current baselines are adequate.  

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it easy to follow.  

**Originality:** The work addresses time-sensitive recommendation—a relatively novel problem domain—with a novel integration of low-rank matrix structure and Hawkes processes.  

**Significance:** Time-sensitive recommendation holds substantial potential for real-world applications, and this paper offers a meaningful contribution to advancing the field.
