- Proposes a novel framework linking self-exciting point processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendations.  
- Captures recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption to model time-varying preferences.  
- Addresses two tasks: recommending the best item at the right moment and predicting a user’s next return time.  
- Significant departure from one-time-preference recommenders by incorporating temporal dynamics and recurrent activities.  
- Develops an efficient optimization algorithm with fast convergence and scalability to millions of pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets show the model outperforms Poisson process, STiC, SVD, and tensor factorization baselines.  
- Provides analysis of the optimization algorithm’s convergence rate and demonstrates scalability.  
- Strength: well-motivated model with clear assumptions and mathematical formulation.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of real datasets (e.g., user preference distributions, item popularity).  
- Weakness: unclear practical motivation and use-cases for predicting next return time for web companies.  
- Weakness: omitted simple baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last time delay, most common delay).  
- Weakness: limited discussion of model limitations and future research directions.  
- Overall: well-written paper with potential for significant contribution if dataset analysis, motivation for return-time prediction, and additional baselines are addressed.
