- Proposes a novel point-process collaborative filtering model combining constant intensity and self-exciting components parameterized by user/item latent vectors.  
- Derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and reports empirical improvements over several baselines.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: missing a strong baseline ensemble (STiC + SVD) that could better assess performance.  
- Does not analyze sensitivity to the Gaussian kernel bandwidth (σ) used in the self-exciting component.  
- Lacks justification and analysis of hyperparameter choices, particularly latent vector dimensionality.  
- Provides insufficient descriptive statistics for datasets; should report recurring-event counts and temporal irregularity measures.  
- Technical approach appears sound and derivations are correct, but experimental shortcomings reduce confidence in results.  
- Presentation quality needs improvement due to awkward phrasing, grammatical errors, and typos.  
- The idea of combining point processes with latent-factor collaborative filtering is original and interesting.  
- The problem and method are practically significant for temporal recommendation, but stronger experimental validation is needed.
