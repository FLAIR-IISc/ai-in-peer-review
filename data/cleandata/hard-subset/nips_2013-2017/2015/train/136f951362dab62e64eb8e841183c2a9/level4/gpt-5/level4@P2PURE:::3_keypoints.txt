- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation algorithm combining low-rank user–item modeling with a Hawkes process; supports distributed computation and outperforms existing methods.
- Principled approach: models ratings as stochastic processes.
- Theoretical ambiguity in Eq. (8): unclear which parameter is maximized (is it over ρ?) and whether ρ* can be unbounded.
- Theorem 2’s practical relevance is limited because it is not used to select ρ in experiments.
- Experimental evaluation misses SVD++ (a strong, time-aware baseline); including it would strengthen comparisons.
- Clarity: paper is well written and easy to follow.
- Originality: addresses a relatively new problem and appears novel.
- Significance: time-sensitive recommendation is impactful; the method’s distributed solution and improved results are valuable.
