This paper presents a novel algorithm for tackling time-sensitive recommendation by capturing the low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and utilizing the Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed fashion and achieves better results than existing methods.

Quality: Overall, I think the paper takes a principled approach to modeling the observations (ratings) using stochastic processes. However, I have a few comments.

One concern in the theoretical part is related to Eq. (8) and Theorem 2. Specifically, in Eq. (8), it is unclear over which parameter the maximum is taken. Is it over \(\rho\)? If so, can \(\rho^{*}\) be unbounded? Furthermore, Theorem 2 is not utilized when choosing \(\rho\) in the experiments, which diminishes the importance of the theorem.

Another comment pertains to the baselines in the experiments. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17], the winner of the Netflix challenge, is one of the best algorithms for time-sensitive recommendation. Incorporating SVD++ into the baseline would further enhance the quality of the paper. Although I am not dissatisfied with the current baselines, adding SVD++ would provide a more comprehensive comparison.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Originality: In my opinion, the paper appears to be novel, as time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively novel problem.

Significance: I believe that time-sensitive recommendation can have a significant impact.
