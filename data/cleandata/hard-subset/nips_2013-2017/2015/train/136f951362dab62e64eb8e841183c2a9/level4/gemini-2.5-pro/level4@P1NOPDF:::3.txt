This paper presents a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation that models user-item interactions using a low-rank matrix factorization framework combined with a Hawkes process. The authors show that the resulting optimization problem is amenable to a distributed implementation and that their method achieves superior performance compared to existing approaches.

**Quality**

Overall, the paper takes a principled approach by modeling user ratings with a stochastic process. However, I have a few comments regarding the theoretical and experimental aspects of the work.

My primary theoretical concern relates to Eq. (8) and the accompanying Theorem 2. In Eq. (8), it is unclear over which parameter the maximum is taken. If the maximization is over ρ, it is not obvious whether the resulting optimal value, ρ*, would be bounded. Furthermore, the selection of ρ in the experiments does not appear to be guided by Theorem 2, which diminishes the practical significance of this theoretical result.

My second comment concerns the choice of experimental baselines. While the current set of baselines is reasonable, the evaluation could be strengthened significantly by including a comparison to SVD++ [17]. As the winning algorithm of the Netflix Challenge, SVD++ is a widely recognized and powerful baseline for recommendation tasks that incorporate temporal dynamics. Adding it would provide a more comprehensive and convincing assessment of the proposed model's performance.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The motivation is clear, and the proposed model is explained coherently.

**Originality**

The work appears to be novel. The core contribution—applying a Hawkes process to model the intensity of user-item interactions within a low-rank factorization framework—is, to my knowledge, a new and interesting idea in the context of time-sensitive recommendation.

**Significance**

The problem of time-sensitive recommendation is of high practical importance, with numerous applications. This paper makes a significant contribution by providing a novel, principled, and high-performing method for this task. The fact that the algorithm can be implemented in a distributed fashion further enhances its potential impact for large-scale industrial systems.
