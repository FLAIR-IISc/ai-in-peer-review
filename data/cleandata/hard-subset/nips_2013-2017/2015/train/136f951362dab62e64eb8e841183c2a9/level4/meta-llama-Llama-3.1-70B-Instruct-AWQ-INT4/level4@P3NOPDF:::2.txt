This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. Specifically, for each pair of user and item, the event density is the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity depending on the past events. Two sets of intensity are computed by two sets of user and item latent vectors respectively. The corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation, and empirical studies have been conducted to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed method over other commonly used alternatives.

However, the experimental studies can be improved in several aspects. For instance, a sensible baseline to consider would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Given that the authors argue that [12, 13] are unable to recommend completely new items, which can be addressed by SVD, it would be valuable to compare the proposed method to this simple ensemble. Additionally, the authors should provide more details on how the bandwidth parameter σ is set for the γ function used in the self-exciting process. It would also be beneficial to analyze the sensitivity of the results to variations in σ. Furthermore, the paper lacks details on how important hyperparameters, such as the dimension of the latent vectors, are chosen for both the baseline methods and the proposed method.

It would also be helpful to provide more information about the characteristics of the two real-world datasets used in the study. Since the paper focuses on temporal structure, discussing simple statistics such as the number of recurring events per user/item pair and the irregularity of the temporal pattern would be useful.

The overall quality of the paper is reasonably good but not outstanding. The presentation can be improved in terms of language, with several typos and grammatical errors noted throughout the text. 

The proposed method is an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models, demonstrating good originality.

The problem and method proposed in the paper have good practical value. The paper's focus on capturing temporal and recurrent structure between users and items is relevant, and the corresponding optimization algorithm and empirical studies demonstrate the advantage of the proposed method over other alternatives. However, the experimental studies and presentation can be improved to strengthen the paper's significance.

Recommendations for improvement:

1. Consider additional baselines, such as an ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD.
2. Provide more details on hyperparameter selection and sensitivity analysis.
3. Offer more information about the characteristics of the real-world datasets.
4. Improve the presentation by addressing language, typos, and grammatical errors.

By addressing these areas, the authors can strengthen the paper and improve its overall quality and significance.
