- Paper proposes a time-aware recommendation model that explicitly handles time-varying user preferences and predicts users’ next return time, trained with a proposed efficient optimization algorithm.
- Experiments include one synthetic dataset for scalability and two modest-sized real-world datasets.
- Reviewer asks for analysis showing how widespread time-varying user preferences are in the real datasets and what drives the variation (e.g., new popular songs vs other patterns).
- Reviewer requests clear motivation for predicting next return time and practical use-cases for how a web company would use such predictions.
- Missing simple baselines for return-time prediction: most recent inter-arrival time and most frequent inter-arrival time.
- Predicting exact return time may be unrealistic; suggest using categorical delay bins (short/medium/long/very long) as a more practical target.
- Experimental results unclear: need to quantify how substantial the improvement over SVD is and whether users would notice the difference.
- Compare against a popularity baseline (especially frequently refreshed popularity) since it can be hard to beat in practice.
- Small comments: typo on page 3 ("abel" → "able"); unclear references/context in Section 2 when mentioning retweets and link creation.
- Overall assessment: paper lacks clear motivation and several necessary baselines/analyses, making it an OK/marginal contribution.
