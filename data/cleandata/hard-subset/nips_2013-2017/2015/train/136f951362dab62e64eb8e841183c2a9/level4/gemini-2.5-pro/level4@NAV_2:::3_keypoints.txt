- Tackles two problems: recommending the right item at the right time and predicting a user's next return time.
- Proposes a novel framework connecting low-rank matrix models with self-exciting Hawkes processes.
- Models static user preferences with a low-rank base intensity matrix and dynamic, history-dependent behavior with a low-rank self-excitation matrix.
- Extends prior survival-analysis-style approaches and can generalize to recommend new items.
- Provides a convex formulation and an efficient, scalable optimization algorithm combining proximal-gradient and conditional-gradient methods with convergence guarantees.
- High-quality, principled work supported by theoretical analysis and extensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets.
- Minor concern: theoretical condition on penalty parameter ρ is not used directly in experiments; sensitivity analysis or discussion of ρ is recommended.
- Experimental comparisons could be strengthened by adding strong time-aware baselines (e.g., Time-SVD++).
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with lucid presentation of motivation, methods, and experiments.
- Highly original idea: modeling Hawkes process parameters as low-rank to transfer temporal patterns across user-item pairs.
- Significant and practically valuable results; model is effective, scalable, and the optimization method may be of independent interest.
