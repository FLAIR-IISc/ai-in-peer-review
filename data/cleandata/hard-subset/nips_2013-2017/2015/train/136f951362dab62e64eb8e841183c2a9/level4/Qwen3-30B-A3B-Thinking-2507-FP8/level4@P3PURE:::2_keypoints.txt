- Proposes a point process-based collaborative filtering model capturing temporal and recurrent user–item interactions via constant plus self-exciting intensities.  
- Uses two sets of user and item latent vectors to compute intensities and derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation.  
- Empirical results claim advantages over common alternatives but the experimental evaluation needs strengthening.  
- Missing a more appropriate baseline: recommend comparing to a simple ensemble of STiC and SVD, since STiC alone cannot recommend new items.  
- Lacks details on the bandwidth parameter σ for the γ function: no method for selecting σ or sensitivity analysis provided.  
- Critical hyperparameters (e.g., latent vector dimensionality) are not justified for the proposed method or baselines.  
- Dataset characterization is insufficient; need statistics like frequency of recurring events per user–item pair and temporal irregularity.  
- Overall quality is reasonable but not outstanding.  
- Presentation suffers from language issues, typos, and grammatical errors that require correction.  
- Method is original as a novel combination of point process modeling and latent factor approaches.  
- Problem and method have practical significance, but experimental limitations reduce the strength of empirical claims.
