- Paper lacks empirical motivation showing how user preferences actually change over time in the real datasets used.  
- No justification for predicting exact return times or how such predictions would be actionable for platforms.  
- Fails to evaluate simple, effective baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last observed delay, most frequent delay).  
- Should consider predicting return-time buckets (short/medium/long) instead of exact times as a more practical objective.  
- Experimental analysis is weak: synthetic data shows scalability but not why the method is efficient or realistic.  
- Real-dataset results are reported qualitatively; no statistical significance testing or quantification of practical impact (e.g., CTR lift, churn reduction).  
- Critical recommendation baseline—daily-updated popularity—is missing from comparisons.  
- Improvement over SVD is not shown to be meaningful or significant relative to simpler methods.  
- Real datasets are modest in size, limiting the generalizability of scalability claims based on synthetic data.  
- Section 2 introduces social network examples abruptly and without clear relevance, harming clarity.  
- Typographical errors (e.g., "is abel to capture") indicate need for proofreading.  
- Overall: idea of time-aware recommendations is promising but unsubstantiated; contributions seem incremental without stronger validation.  
- Recommendation: paper is weak and should be rejected pending major revisions.  
- Required revisions: analyze real data to justify time-varying preferences, justify or reframe return-time prediction (or use buckets), add baselines (last delay, most frequent, popularity), run rigorous experiments with significance testing and quantify business impact, and clarify practical utility.
