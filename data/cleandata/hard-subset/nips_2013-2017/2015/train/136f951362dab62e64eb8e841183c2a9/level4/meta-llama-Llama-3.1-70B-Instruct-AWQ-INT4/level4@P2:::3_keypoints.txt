- Proposes a novel, distributed algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation using low-rank user-item structure and Hawkes processes, showing improved performance.
- Modeling observations via stochastic processes is principled and well-executed.
- Theoretical ambiguity in Equation (8) and Theorem 2: unclear over which parameter the maximum is taken (ρ?), and whether ρ* can be unbounded.
- Theorem 2 is not used to select ρ in experiments, which weakens its practical relevance.
- Experiments use adequate baselines but should include SVD++ (Netflix challenge winner) for a stronger comparison.
- Paper is well-written and clear.
- Originality stems from applying Hawkes processes and low-rank structure to the relatively new problem of time-sensitive recommendation.
- Significance is substantial due to practical impact and the algorithm’s distributed solvability and empirical gains.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, principled approach, empirical improvements, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: theoretical gaps (Eq.8/Theorem 2), missing key baseline (SVD++), and possibly limited incremental contribution.
