- Introduces a novel time-sensitive recommendation algorithm combining low-rank matrix factorization with a Hawkes process and a distributed optimization scheme, reporting superior performance.
- Method is principled by modeling user ratings as a stochastic process.
- Primary theoretical concern: Eq. (8) and Theorem 2 are unclear about which parameter is maximized, particularly whether maximization is over ρ.
- The boundedness of the optimal ρ* is not established, raising questions about the theoretical result’s validity.
- Experimental selection of ρ appears not to follow Theorem 2, reducing the practical relevance of the theoretical claim.
- Experimental baselines are reasonable but incomplete; missing comparison to SVD++, a strong Netflix-Challenge baseline.
- Paper is well written, with clear motivation and coherent explanation of the model.
- Contribution appears novel by applying a Hawkes process within a low-rank factorization framework for time-sensitive recommendation.
- Problem is significant and the distributed implementation enhances potential large-scale impact.
