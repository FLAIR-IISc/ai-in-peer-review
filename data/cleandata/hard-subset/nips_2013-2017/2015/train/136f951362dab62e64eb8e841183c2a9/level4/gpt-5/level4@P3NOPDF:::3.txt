Summary:
The paper proposes a principled algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation that combines low-rank modeling of the user–item interaction matrix with a Hawkes process to capture temporal dynamics. The method admits a distributed implementation and demonstrates improved performance over existing baselines.

Strengths:
- Sound modeling: The approach leverages stochastic process modeling (Hawkes processes) to capture intensity and excitation effects in user behavior, integrated with a low-rank structure for collaborative filtering.
- Practicality: The algorithm is amenable to distributed optimization, which is important for large-scale recommendation tasks.
- Empirical results: The method outperforms several baselines on relevant datasets, indicating strong practical value.

Concerns and questions:
- Theoretical clarity (Eq. 8 and Theorem 2):
  - In Eq. (8), it is unclear over which parameter the maximization is taken. If the maximization is over ρ, please state this explicitly and specify the feasible domain for ρ.
  - If ρ is indeed optimized, can ρ* become unbounded? What constraints, regularization, or assumptions ensure boundedness and well-posedness of the maximization?
  - The practical role of Theorem 2 is unclear. The experiments do not appear to rely on Theorem 2 for choosing ρ, which diminishes the theorem’s perceived importance. Please clarify whether Theorem 2 guides hyperparameter selection (e.g., providing a principled range for ρ) or offers other actionable insights. If not, consider discussing why it remains useful (e.g., guarantees under certain regimes) or aligning the experimental protocol with the theorem’s guidance.
- Baselines:
  - Including SVD++ [17], or more precisely its time-aware variant (e.g., timeSVD++), would significantly strengthen the empirical evaluation. SVD++-style models are widely regarded as strong baselines for recommendation systems with temporal effects, and their omission makes it harder to contextualize gains. If inclusion is infeasible, a discussion justifying its absence and, if possible, a comparison to published results would be helpful.
  - That said, the current baselines are reasonable; adding SVD++/timeSVD++ would simply make the case more compelling.

Clarity:
- The paper is well written and easy to follow. The modeling assumptions, optimization strategy, and experimental setup are presented clearly overall.
- The main area where clarity should be improved is the precise definition and role of ρ in Eq. (8), the constraints on its value, and how Theorem 2 connects to the implemented algorithm and hyperparameter selection.

Originality:
- The integration of Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix modeling for recommendation is, to my knowledge, novel, particularly in the context of time-sensitive recommendation where capturing self- and cross-excitation is important. The framing and algorithmic treatment represent a meaningful contribution.

Significance:
- Time-sensitive recommendation is an impactful and timely problem with practical relevance in many domains (e-commerce, media, ads). The proposed approach is likely to influence subsequent work by providing a principled stochastic-process-based framework that also scales to large datasets. The empirical improvements and distributed solution increase the method’s applicability.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Theory–practice alignment:
  - Explicitly state the optimization domain for ρ in Eq. (8), provide conditions ensuring boundedness, and, if applicable, include a short proof sketch or citation.
  - Explain how Theorem 2 should be used in practice. If the experiments selected ρ via cross-validation, discuss how this choice relates to Theorem 2. Consider adding an ablation or sensitivity study to illustrate whether the theorem’s guidance leads to comparable or better performance than naive tuning.
- Baseline enhancement:
  - Add SVD++/timeSVD++ as a baseline, or provide a detailed rationale for excluding it. If possible, include results on at least one dataset where such a baseline is available.
- Empirical details (optional but helpful):
  - Report sensitivity to key Hawkes parameters (e.g., kernel choice, decay rates) and regularization strengths, to demonstrate robustness.
  - Provide more details on the distributed solver: communication cost, convergence behavior, and strong/weak scaling on increasing numbers of workers.

Overall assessment:
This is a solid and well-motivated paper that advances time-sensitive recommendation by uniting low-rank collaborative filtering with Hawkes process-based temporal modeling, delivering both theoretical insight and practical performance. The main weaknesses concern theoretical clarity around Eq. (8)/Theorem 2 and the absence of a very strong time-aware baseline (SVD++/timeSVD++). Addressing these points would substantially strengthen the contribution. I view the paper positively and believe it is a valuable addition to the literature on temporally aware recommender systems.
