- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation algorithm combining low-rank matrix factorization with Hawkes processes to model temporal dynamics.
- Algorithm is scalable and amenable to distributed optimization, which is a significant practical contribution.
- Experimental results show the proposed method outperforms existing techniques on several datasets.
- Main strength is the principled synthesis of collaborative filtering and self-exciting temporal point processes.
- Concern: Equation (8) is ambiguous—it's unclear over which parameter(s) the maximization defining ρ* is taken and whether ρ* is guaranteed to be bounded.
- Concern: Disconnect between Theorem 2 and experiments—theorem guidance for choosing ρ does not appear to be used in practice, weakening its practical relevance.
- Experimental evaluation is solid but could be more comprehensive by adding stronger baselines.
- Suggestion: Include SVD++ as a competitive baseline since it handles temporal dynamics and implicit feedback and is a high-performing benchmark.
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and uses consistent notation.
- The work is original in combining low-rank factorization with Hawkes processes for dynamic recommendations.
- Problem significance is high with practical impact; the approach is likely to be influential.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with promising results; address theoretical clarifications and add stronger baselines to solidify contributions—leaning toward acceptance.
