-- Summary --
This work introduces a point process collaborative filtering model designed to model the temporal and recurrent interactions between users and items. The model defines the event density for each user-item pair as the sum of a constant component and a self-exciting component driven by past events, with these intensities parameterized by user and item latent vectors. An optimization algorithm is presented for parameter estimation, and empirical results demonstrate performance gains over existing methods. 

However, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened. A relevant baseline comparison would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD, given the authors’ assertion that existing methods struggle with new item recommendation (a limitation SVD could address).  Further clarification is needed regarding the bandwidth parameter σ used within the γ function governing the self-exciting process – specifically, how it is determined and the sensitivity of the results to its variation. Additionally, the paper lacks details regarding hyperparameter selection for both the proposed model and baseline methods, such as the dimensionality of the latent vectors. 

Providing more descriptive statistics of the two real-world datasets used would also be beneficial. Considering the focus on temporal structure, reporting metrics like the average number of recurring events per user/item pair and a measure of temporal irregularity would provide valuable context.

-- Quality --
The overall quality of the work is good, but there is room for improvement.

-- Clarity --
The paper’s presentation would benefit from enhanced language clarity and careful attention to typos and grammatical errors.

-- Originality --
The proposed approach represents a novel integration of point process models with latent factor models, offering an interesting contribution to the field.

-- Significance --
The addressed problem and the proposed method hold considerable practical value. This paper proposed a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. The corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation and empirical studies have been done to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed methods to other commonly used alternatives. However, the experimental studies and the presentation can be improved.
