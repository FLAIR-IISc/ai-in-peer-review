This paper presents a new algorithm that incorporates a low-order structure in the user-item matrix and addresses the problem of time-sensitive recommendations using the Hawkes process. This algorithm can be solved in a distributed manner and delivers better results than existing methods.

Quality: Overall, I believe the paper adopts a principled approach to modeling observations (rating) using stochastic processes. However, I have some comments.

A concern in the theoretical section is equation (8) and theorem 2. In equation 8, what parameter do we take the maximum value on? Is it on \rho? In that case, can \rho* be infinite? Also, theorem 2 was not used when the paper selected \rho in the experiment, which diminishes the importance of the theorem.

Another comment is about the experiment's baseline. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17] (winner of the Netflix Challenge) is one of the best algorithms for time-sensitive algorithms. Adding SVD++ to the baseline would improve the quality further. Although I am not dissatisfied with the current baseline.

Clarity: The paper was well-written and easy to follow.

Originality: The paper seemed new to me because time-sensitive recommendations is a relatively new problem.

Significance: I believe time-sensitive recommendations can be impactful. This paper presents a new algorithm to address the problem of time-sensitive recommendations using a low-order structure in the user-item matrix and the Hawkes process. This algorithm can be solved in a distributed manner and delivers better results than existing methods.
