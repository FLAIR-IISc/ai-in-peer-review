-- Abstract -- This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture spatial and repetitive structure between users and items. Specifically, for each pair of user and item, the event density is a sum of a constant intensity and a self-stimulated intensity depending on past events. Two sets of intensities are calculated through two sets of user and item latent vectors. The corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation is developed, and empirical research is conducted to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method compared to other commonly used alternatives.

However, the empirical research can be improved. For example, another realistic basis could be a common ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Since the authors argue that [12, 13] is not capable of recommending completely new items, which could be covered by SVD, and it would be worthwhile to compare the proposed method with this common ensemble. Furthermore, how is the bandwidth parameter \sigma set for the \gamma function used for the self-stimulated process? How sensitive will the results be if we change \sigma? Moreover, there are no details on how other important hyperparameters are chosen for both the baseline method and the proposed method, such as the latent vector dimension.

Providing more detailed descriptions of the characteristics of two real-world datasets would also be helpful. Since this paper is about spatial structure, it is necessary to discuss general statistics such as the number of repetitive events per user/item pair and how irregular the spatial pattern is.

-- Quality -- Overall quality is reasonably good but not very good.
-- Clarity -- The presentation of the paper could be improved in terms of language, typography, and grammatical errors.
-- Originality --
The proposed method is an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models.
-- Significance --
Both the problem and the method have good practical value. This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture spatial and repetitive structure between users and items. The corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation is developed, and empirical research is conducted to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method compared to other commonly used alternatives. However, the empirical research and presentation can be improved.
