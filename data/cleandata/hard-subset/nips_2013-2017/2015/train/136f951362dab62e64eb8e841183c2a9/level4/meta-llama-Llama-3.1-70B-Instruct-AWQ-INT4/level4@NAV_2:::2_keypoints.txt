- Proposes a novel framework combining self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns in user-item pairs.  
- Presents a convex formulation and a scalable algorithm that handles millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events.  
- Demonstrates superior predictive performance on synthetic and real datasets versus state-of-the-art competitors.  
- Differs from prior work (Kapoor et al.) by enabling recommendation of new items in future time, increasing versatility and practicality.  
- Strength: novel combination of point processes and low-rank models.  
- Strength: efficient, scalable optimization algorithm.  
- Strength: clear and concise presentation of model and algorithm.  
- Weakness: experimental studies lack additional baselines (e.g., ensemble of STiC and SVD).  
- Weakness: insufficient details on hyperparameter tuning, particularly the bandwidth parameter σ.  
- Weakness: missing descriptive information on real datasets (number of recurring events per pair, temporal irregularity).  
- Presentation quality needs improvement (language, typos, grammar).  
- Overall assessment: high originality and significance; paper quality is reasonable but not outstanding.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective framework, strong empirical results, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete experiments/comparisons, insufficient tuning and dataset details, and presentation issues.
