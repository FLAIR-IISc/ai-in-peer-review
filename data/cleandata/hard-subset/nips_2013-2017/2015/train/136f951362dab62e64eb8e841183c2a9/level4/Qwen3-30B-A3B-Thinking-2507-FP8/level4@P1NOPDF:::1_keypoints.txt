- Introduces method for time-sensitive recommendations and return-interval prediction but lacks sufficient motivation for both contributions.  
- Claim that time-varying preferences are widespread is unsupported; needs empirical analysis of datasets to show preference evolution.  
- Motivation for predicting exact return times is unclear; paper must justify how this improves user experience or business metrics.  
- Overlooks simple baselines for return-time forecasting (e.g., last observed interval, global most frequent interval).  
- Predicting exact return times is likely unrealistic; recommend framing as coarse-class classification of interval bins.  
- Experimental gains over SVD and other baselines are described as “modest” without quantitative context or statistical significance.  
- Fails to compare against the strong, common baseline of daily recalculated popular items—critical to assess practical gains.  
- Results use two modest-sized real datasets and one synthetic set—insufficient evidence of scalability or practical utility.  
- Optimization algorithm seems efficient, but overall contribution impact is weak due to missing justification and baselines.  
- Paper needs deeper real-data analysis, stronger baselines, and explicit discussion of business/user benefit to be convincing.  
- Minor issues: typo on page 3 (“abel” → “able”) and abrupt, unreferenced shift to retweets/link creation in Section 2.
