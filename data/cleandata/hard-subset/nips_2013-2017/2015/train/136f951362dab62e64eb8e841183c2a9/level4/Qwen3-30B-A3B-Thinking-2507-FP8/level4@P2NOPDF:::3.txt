

This paper proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation that leverages low-rank structure in user-item matrices combined with Hawkes processes to model temporal dynamics. The approach is designed for scalability, supporting distributed implementation, and demonstrates superior performance over existing baselines in empirical evaluations.  

**Quality:** The paper adopts a principled stochastic process framework to model rating observations, which is a strong theoretical foundation. However, two key concerns arise:  
1. **Theoretical Clarity (Eq. 8 & Theorem 2):** Equation (8) states a maximization over an unspecified parameter. If the maximization is over $\rho$, it is unclear whether $\rho^*$ remains bounded under the proposed conditions. Furthermore, Theorem 2—which establishes a key theoretical guarantee—is not referenced or utilized in the experimental section when selecting $\rho$. This disconnect diminishes the perceived value of the theorem and warrants clarification on its practical relevance.  
2. **Baseline Comparison:** The experimental baselines exclude SVD++ [17], the Netflix Prize-winning algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation. Given SVD++’s established dominance in this domain, its omission weakens the comparative strength of the results. While the current baselines are acceptable, including SVD++ would provide a more rigorous validation of the proposed method’s novelty and efficacy.  

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with logical flow and accessible explanations of technical concepts. The contributions and methodology are effectively communicated.  

**Originality:** The problem of time-sensitive recommendation is indeed a relatively underexplored niche, and the integration of low-rank matrix structure with Hawkes processes represents a novel technical approach. The paper successfully positions its method within this emerging context.  

**Significance:** Time-aware recommendation systems have substantial practical value for applications like e-commerce and content platforms, where temporal dynamics critically influence user preferences. This work addresses a meaningful gap in modeling such dynamics, with potential for real-world impact through improved personalization.  

**Minor Suggestions for Improvement:**  
- Explicitly define the optimization variable in Eq. (8) and discuss boundedness of $\rho^*$ to resolve theoretical ambiguity.  
- Include SVD++ in experiments to strengthen baseline comparison; if computational constraints prevent this, acknowledge the limitation.  
- Briefly clarify how Theorem 2 informs the choice of $\rho$ in practice, or justify its exclusion from experiments.  

**Overall Assessment:**  
The paper presents a promising and novel approach to time-sensitive recommendation with strong empirical results and a scalable design. Addressing the theoretical and experimental concerns raised would significantly enhance the contribution’s rigor and impact. The core idea is compelling, and the method shows clear potential for adoption in time-aware recommendation systems. With minor revisions, this work would be a valuable addition to the literature.
