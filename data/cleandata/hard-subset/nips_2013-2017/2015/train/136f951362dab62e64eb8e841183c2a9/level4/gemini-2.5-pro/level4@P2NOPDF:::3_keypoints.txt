- Introduces a novel time-sensitive recommendation algorithm combining low-rank matrix factorization with a Hawkes process to model self-exciting user activity.  
- Presents a scalable, distributed algorithm for model estimation, enabling applicability to large-scale datasets.  
- Empirical results show the proposed method outperforms several existing approaches on real-world datasets.  
- Strength: principled, well-motivated stochastic-process formulation that captures temporal dependencies in recommendations.  
- Strength: practical contribution via distributed optimization with clear potential for real-world impact.  
- Concern: Theoretical analysis around Equation (8) is unclear—uncertainty whether the maximization over ρ yields a bounded optimal ρ*.  
- Concern: Disconnect between Theorem 2 and experiments—the theorem’s bound on ρ is not used for hyperparameter selection or experimental guidance.  
- Concern: Experimental baselines are incomplete; missing comparison to SVD++ (a temporal, strong baseline) weakens empirical claims.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and explanations of the model and learning algorithm are clear and easy to follow.  
- Originality: Novel and valuable synthesis of low-rank matrix models with point processes for time-sensitive recommendation.
