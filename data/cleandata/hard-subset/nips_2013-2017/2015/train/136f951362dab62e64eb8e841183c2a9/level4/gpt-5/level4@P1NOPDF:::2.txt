-- Summary --
This paper proposes a point-process collaborative filtering model to capture temporal and recurrent interactions between users and items. For each user–item pair, the event intensity is the sum of a constant base rate and a self-exciting component that depends on past events. These two components are parameterized using two sets of user and item latent vectors. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and present empirical studies showing advantages over commonly used alternatives.

However, the experimental evaluation can be strengthened. A sensible additional baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Since the authors argue that [12, 13] cannot recommend entirely new items—something SVD can address—it would be informative to compare against this straightforward ensemble. In addition, for the γ function used in the self-exciting process, how is the bandwidth parameter σ set, and how sensitive are the results to σ? More generally, key details are missing on how important hyperparameters are chosen for both the baselines and the proposed method (e.g., the dimensionality of the latent vectors), as well as any validation protocol or sensitivity analysis.

It would also help to provide more details on the characteristics of the two real-world datasets. Because the paper focuses on temporal structure, it would be useful to report simple statistics such as the number of recurring events per user–item pair and the irregularity of temporal patterns.

-- Quality --
The overall quality is reasonably good but not great. The modeling idea and optimization are sound, but the evaluation lacks certain baselines, hyperparameter-selection details, sensitivity analyses, and richer dataset characterization.

-- Clarity --
The presentation could be improved. There are issues with language, typos, and grammar that occasionally obscure the main ideas. Clearer notation and more careful writing would help readability.

-- Originality --
The method is an interesting combination of point processes and latent factor models.

-- Significance --
Both the problem and the proposed approach are practically valuable. The paper introduces a point-process collaborative filtering model for temporal and recurrent behavior, derives an optimization procedure, and provides empirical evidence of benefits over standard alternatives. That said, the significance would be stronger with a more comprehensive experimental study (including the STiC+SVD ensemble), clearer reporting of hyperparameter choices and sensitivity, and a richer description of dataset temporal properties.
