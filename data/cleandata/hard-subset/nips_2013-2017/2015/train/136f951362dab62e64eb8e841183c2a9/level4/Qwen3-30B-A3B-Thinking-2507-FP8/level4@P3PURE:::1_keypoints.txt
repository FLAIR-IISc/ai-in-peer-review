1. Proposes a new method for time-sensitive recommendations addressing time-varying user preferences.  
2. Introduces predicting the next user return time as a novel contribution.  
3. Learning uses an efficient optimization algorithm developed by the authors.  
4. Experiments: one synthetic dataset (for scalability) and two modest-sized real datasets.  
5. Major concern: prevalence of time-varying user preferences is not substantiated with real-data analysis.  
6. Datasets lack demonstration of how preferences evolve (e.g., new hits, seasonality, trend shifts).  
7. Motivation for predicting next return time is weak—paper doesn’t explain real-world utility or use cases.  
8. Unclear how a company would leverage return-time predictions for notifications, curation, or retention.  
9. Exact return-time prediction seems overly ambitious; recommend simpler, actionable baselines.  
10. Suggested baselines: last observed delay and most frequent time delay in the dataset.  
11. Suggested alternative task: coarser-grained delay classification (short/medium/long/very long).  
12. Experimental gains over SVD/other baselines are not clearly quantified or contextualized.  
13. Missing comparison to strong baseline of dynamically updated popular-item recommendations.  
14. Minor issues: typo “abel”→“able” and abrupt, uncited shift to social network examples in Section 2.  
15. Overall: technically sound but marginal due to weak motivation, missing baselines, and unclear practical impact; needs real-data analysis, clearer applications, and feasible prediction targets.
