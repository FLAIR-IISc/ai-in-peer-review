- Proposes a point-process collaborative filtering model combining constant and self-exciting intensities for user-item event densities.
- Uses two sets of user/item latent vectors to compute intensities and derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation.
- Clear explanation of model components, self-exciting point processes, and low-rank latent factor structure.
- Optimization algorithm is well-explained with a detailed convergence analysis.
- Empirical studies show advantages over common alternatives but are judged insufficiently thorough.
- Missing baseline: should compare to a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD, since that could handle new-item recommendations.
- Hyperparameter selection (e.g., latent dimension) is not detailed for the proposed method or baselines.
- Dataset characterization is lacking—need statistics like number of recurring events per user/item pair and temporal irregularity.
- Presentation issues: language, typos, and awkward phrasing make parts of the text unclear.
- Overall assessment: method is well-motivated, practically valuable, and a valuable contribution to recommender systems.
- Quality: 7/10; Clarity: 6/10; Originality: 8/10; Significance: 8/10.
- For acceptance: novel combination of point processes and latent factors, practical value, and clear model exposition.
- Against acceptance: experiments need improvement and additional details; presentation requires revision and polishing.
