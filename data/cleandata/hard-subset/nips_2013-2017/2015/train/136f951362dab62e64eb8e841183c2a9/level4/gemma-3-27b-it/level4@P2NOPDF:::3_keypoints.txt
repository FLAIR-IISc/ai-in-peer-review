- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation algorithm combining low-rank matrix factorization with a Hawkes process.  
- Algorithm is well-suited for distributed computation and shows empirical improvements over existing methods.  
- Quality: offers a principled stochastic-process model for user ratings but needs clarification on Equation (8)/Theorem 2—unclear maximization over ρ and possibility of an unbounded ρ*.  
- Theorem 2 appears unused in experiments, reducing its practical relevance.  
- Experimental evaluation should include SVD++ (Netflix challenge winner) to provide a stronger baseline.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and easy to follow; motivation and approach are clearly articulated.  
- Originality: presents a novel contribution to a growing area of time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Significance: approach has high potential impact by capturing temporal dynamics and offering scalable distributed implementation.
