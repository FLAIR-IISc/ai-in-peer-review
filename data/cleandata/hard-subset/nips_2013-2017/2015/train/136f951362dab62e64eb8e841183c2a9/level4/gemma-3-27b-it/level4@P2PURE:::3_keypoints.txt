- Introduces a novel time-sensitive recommendation algorithm combining low-rank user-item structure with a Hawkes process, amenable to distributed computation and showing improved performance.  
- Modeling of observations (ratings) via stochastic processes is principled and well-motivated.  
- Theoretical development (Eq. (8) and Theorem 2) is unclear about which parameter is being maximized; if maximized over ρ, ρ* might be unbounded.  
- Theorem 2 appears unused in experimental selection of ρ, reducing its practical relevance.  
- Baseline choices should include strong contenders like SVD++ (Netflix challenge winner) to strengthen comparisons, though current baselines are not wholly inadequate.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to understand.  
- Work seems novel given the relative infancy of time-sensitive recommendation research.  
- The problem and proposed solution are potentially significant and impactful.
