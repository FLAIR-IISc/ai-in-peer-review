- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework linking Hawkes self-exciting point processes with low-rank collaborative models to recommend items and predict return times.  
- Models each user-item event sequence with Hawkes intensity composed of a baseline and history-dependent self-excitation, assuming low-rank baseline (Λ0) and excitation (A) matrices for generalization.  
- Derives a convex optimization objective with nuclear-norm regularization and develops an efficient proximal/conditional-gradient algorithm with formal convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and large-scale real data show the model outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines in item recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Strength: Original and significant idea—elegant unification of Hawkes processes and low-rank collaborative filtering to capture recurrent temporal dynamics.  
- Strength: Strong technical quality—well-justified convex formulation, non-negativity handling, and a novel algorithm with theoretical convergence that has independent interest.  
- Strength: Convincing empirical evaluation, including better fit of Hawkes versus other survival models.  
- Weakness: Missing baseline—no comparison to a simple ensemble of STiC for known items and SVD for new-item ranking, which could be a strong competitor.  
- Weakness: Lacks experimental details needed for reproducibility (e.g., kernel bandwidth σ selection and sensitivity, latent dimension, regularization weights, and baseline hyperparameter tuning).  
- Weakness: Insufficient dataset characterization—needs statistics on recurring event distributions, inter-event times, and temporal irregularity to justify the model choice.  
- Weakness: Presentation could be improved with proofreading to fix minor typos and grammatical errors for clarity.
