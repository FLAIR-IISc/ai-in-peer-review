- Proposes a novel, distributed-friendly algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation that leverages low-rank user–item structure and the Hawkes process, showing superior empirical performance.
- Paper uses a principled stochastic-process-based approach to model observations (ratings).
- Theoretical section needs clarification: Eq. (8) and Theorem 2 are ambiguous about which parameter the maximum is taken over.
- If the maximum in Eq. (8) is over ρ, ρ* could become unbounded; experiments do not use Theorem 2 to choose ρ, reducing the theorem’s practical significance.
- Experimental baselines are reasonable but should include SVD++ [17] to provide a stronger comparison (given its strong performance in Netflix challenge).
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Work appears novel given the recent focus on time-sensitive recommendation.
- Proposed algorithm could have significant real-world impact by combining low-rank structure with temporal Hawkes modeling.
