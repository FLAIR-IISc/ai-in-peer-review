-- Summary -- This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure in user-item interactions. Specifically, the event density for each user-item pair is modeled as the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity, the latter depending on past events. These intensities are computed using two sets of latent vectors – one for users and one for items. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and present empirical studies demonstrating the advantages of their method compared to commonly used alternatives.

However, the experimental studies could be strengthened. A sensible baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. The authors argue that [12, 13] are unable to recommend completely new items, a limitation that SVD could address; comparing against this ensemble would be valuable. In addition, regarding the Gaussian kernel (\gamma function) used in the self-exciting process, the bandwidth parameter \sigma is not specified. It would be important to investigate the sensitivity of the results to variations in \sigma. Furthermore, the paper lacks detail regarding the selection of other important hyperparameters for both the proposed method and the baselines, such as the dimensionality of the latent vectors. 

Providing more details about the characteristics of the two real-world datasets would also be helpful. Given the paper’s focus on temporal structure, discussing simple statistics like the number of recurring events per user/item pair and the irregularity of the temporal patterns would provide valuable context.

-- Quality -- The overall quality is reasonably good, but could be improved.

-- Clarity -- The paper’s presentation could benefit from attention to language, typos, and grammatical errors.

-- Originality -- The proposed method represents an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models.

-- Significance -- Both the problem addressed and the proposed method have good practical value. The paper introduces a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. An optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation, and empirical studies demonstrate advantages over existing methods. However, the experimental studies and presentation could be further refined.
