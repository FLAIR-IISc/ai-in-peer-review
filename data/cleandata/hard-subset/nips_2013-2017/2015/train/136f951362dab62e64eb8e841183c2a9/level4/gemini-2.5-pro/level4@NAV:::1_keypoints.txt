- Introduces a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations that explicitly models temporally dynamic user preferences.  
- Secondary contribution: a model to predict users' next return time to the service.  
- Proposes a custom, efficient optimization algorithm for training the model.  
- Experiments use a large-scale synthetic dataset for scalability and two real-world datasets for practical performance.  
- Lacks data-driven analysis demonstrating prevalence and magnitude of time-varying preferences in the datasets.  
- Needs analysis of the nature of preference shifts (external trends vs. user-specific temporal patterns).  
- Motivation and practical applications for predicting next return time are not clearly articulated.  
- Missing simple but crucial baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last-delay heuristic, modal inter-session time).  
- Suggests reframing return-time prediction as a classification over time-delay bins to improve tractability and usefulness.  
- Practical significance of reported improvements over baselines (e.g., SVD) is unclear and needs contextualization.  
- Missing critical baseline: a temporally-aware non-personalized "most popular" recommender.  
- Minor: typo on page 3 ("abel" → "able").  
- Minor: Section 2 introduces retweets and social links abruptly without references or clear connection; needs rephrasing/integration.  
- Overall: tackles interesting problems but lacks clear practical motivation and key experimental analyses; paper is borderline.
