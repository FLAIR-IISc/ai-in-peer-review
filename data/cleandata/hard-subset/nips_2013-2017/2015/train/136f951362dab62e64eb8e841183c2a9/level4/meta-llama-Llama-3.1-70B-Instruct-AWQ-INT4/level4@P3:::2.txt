This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation by connecting self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption pairs. The authors present a convex formulation and an efficient learning algorithm that scales up to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events.

Summary:
The paper addresses two central questions: how to recommend the most desirable item at the right moment and how to predict the next returning time of a user to a service. The proposed model, based on low-rank self-exciting Hawkes processes, captures the temporal dependencies between past events and future occurrences. The authors develop an efficient optimization algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods, achieving an optimal O(1/t) convergence rate. The model can be generalized to incorporate additional context information, such as user profiles and item contents.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel combination of point processes and low-rank models, which is an interesting and original approach to time-sensitive recommendation.
* The authors provide a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over state-of-the-art competitors on both synthetic and real datasets.

Weaknesses:

* The experimental studies can be improved by including additional baselines, such as a simple ensemble of STiC and SVD, to provide a more comprehensive comparison.
* The paper lacks details on how the bandwidth parameter σ is set for the self-exciting process and how sensitive the results are to variations in σ.
* The authors do not provide sufficient information on the characteristics of the real-world datasets, such as the number of recurring events per user/item pair and the irregularity of the temporal patterns.

Quality: 7/10
The overall quality of the paper is reasonably good, but there are some areas for improvement. The authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their framework and algorithm, but the experimental studies and presentation can be improved.
