- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation method that models evolving user preferences and predicts user return times.
- Distinguishes itself from prior work by explicitly accounting for temporal shifts and adding a return-time prediction component.
- Uses a new optimization algorithm and shows scalability on a synthetic dataset, with additional evaluation on two small real-world datasets.
- The claim that user preferences vary over time needs stronger justification and quantitative evidence.
- Requires deeper analysis of experimental datasets to validate temporal variability and identify its drivers (e.g., new items vs. other patterns).
- The motivation and practical utility of predicting user return times are not clearly articulated; potential applications should be described.
- Return-time evaluation is incomplete and omits simple baselines like previous-delay or most-frequent-delay predictors.
- Predicting exact return times may be unrealistic; consider coarser delay categories (short/medium/long) as a more tractable target.
- Reported improvements over SVD lack clarity on practical significance and perceptibility to users.
- A comparison to a regularly updated popularity-based recommender is missing and would provide important context.
- Minor issues: typographical error (“abel” → “able”) and an abrupt, uncited introduction of retweets/link creation in Section 2.
- Overall assessment: paper lacks compelling motivation, key baselines, and analyses; currently rated OK/marginal.
