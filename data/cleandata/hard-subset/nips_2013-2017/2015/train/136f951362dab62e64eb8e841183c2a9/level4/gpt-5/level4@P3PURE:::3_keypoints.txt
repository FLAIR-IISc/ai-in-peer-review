- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation algorithm using low-rank structure plus a Hawkes process, supports distributed optimization, and outperforms existing methods empirically.  
- Uses a principled stochastic-process model for observations (ratings), which is compelling.  
- Theory concern: Eq. (8) is ambiguous about which parameter is being maximized (is it ρ?), raising the question whether the optimal ρ* could be unbounded.  
- Theorem 2 does not appear to inform the experimental choice of ρ, undermining its practical significance.  
- Baselines: omits SVD++ (Netflix Prize winner), which should be included as a strong time-sensitive recommendation baseline.  
- Clarity: paper is well written and easy to follow.  
- Originality: work appears novel in the relatively new area of time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Significance: method has potential substantial impact due to its modeling approach and distributed solution.
