This paper presents a novel algorithm to tackle time-sensitive recommendation by capturing low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and by using Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed fashion and achieves better results than existing methods.

Quality: The paper presents a principled approach to modeling user behavior using stochastic processes. However, there are a few concerns regarding the theoretical part. Specifically, in Equation (8) and Theorem 2, it is unclear over which parameter the maximum is taken. If it is taken over ρ, then ρ* could potentially be unbounded, which may not be desirable. Furthermore, Theorem 2 is not used in the experiments to choose ρ, which diminishes its importance.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithm. For instance, comparing the performance of the proposed algorithm with other state-of-the-art methods, such as SVD++ [17], which was the winner of the Netflix challenge, would strengthen the paper.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation.
* The algorithm achieves better results than existing methods.
* The paper provides a principled approach to modeling user behavior using stochastic processes.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The theoretical part of the paper could be improved, particularly with regards to Equation (8) and Theorem 2.
* The evaluation of the proposed algorithm could be more comprehensive.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors have done a good job of explaining the proposed algorithm and its components.

Originality: The paper presents a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation, which is a relatively new problem in the field.

Significance: Time-sensitive recommendation has the potential to be impactful, particularly in applications where user behavior is time-dependent. The proposed algorithm could be useful in such scenarios.

Recommendation: I would recommend accepting the paper, but with some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above. Specifically, the authors should clarify the theoretical part of the paper, particularly with regards to Equation (8) and Theorem 2. Additionally, the authors should consider comparing the performance of the proposed algorithm with other state-of-the-art methods, such as SVD++.
