- Proposes a new method for time-sensitive recommendations that explicitly models time-varying user preferences and predicts users’ next return time, trained with an efficient optimization algorithm and evaluated on a large synthetic and two modest real datasets.  
- Lacks evidence that time-varying preferences are prevalent—requires analysis of the real datasets to show whether preferences change and what drives variation (e.g., new popular songs vs other patterns).  
- Motivation for predicting next return time is weak—authors must justify its practical value and give concrete use cases for web companies.  
- Missing simple baselines for return-time prediction, specifically: using the most recent inter-arrival time and the user’s most frequent inter-arrival time.  
- Suggests predicting coarse return-time categories (short/medium/long/very long) as a simpler, more actionable alternative to exact time prediction.  
- Experimental results are hard to interpret: unclear if improvement over SVD is substantial or modest and whether users would notice the difference.  
- Requests comparison to a popularity-based recommender (e.g., daily top-N) because it is often a strong baseline.  
- Minor: Typo on page 3 (“is abel” → “is able”).  
- Minor: Section 2 introduces retweets and link creation abruptly and without citations—rephrase, add references, and clarify relevance to the problem.  
- Overall: Addresses two relevant problems but provides limited motivation, omits important baselines and analyses, and is thus judged OK/marginal.
