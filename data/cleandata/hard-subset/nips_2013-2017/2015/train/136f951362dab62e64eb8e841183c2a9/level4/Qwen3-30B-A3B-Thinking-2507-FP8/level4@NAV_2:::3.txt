

This paper introduces a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation by integrating self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns in user-item interactions. The authors present a convex optimization formulation with a new algorithm that achieves O(1/t) convergence rate, scales to large datasets, and demonstrates superior performance in both item recommendation and returning-time prediction tasks.

Quality: The paper adopts a principled approach to modeling user temporal dynamics using Hawkes processes. However, two theoretical aspects warrant clarification: (1) In Eq. (8) and Theorem 2, it's unclear over which parameter the maximum is taken (likely ρ), and whether ρ* could be unbounded. (2) The theoretical result in Theorem 2 appears not to be utilized in the experimental implementation, diminishing its practical relevance. Regarding baselines, including SVD++—a state-of-the-art time-sensitive recommendation method that won the Netflix challenge—would strengthen the comparative evaluation.

Clarity: The manuscript is well-organized and clearly written, with sufficient technical details to allow reproduction of results.

Originality: The work presents a novel approach to time-sensitive recommendation by connecting self-exciting point processes with low-rank models, addressing a relatively underexplored problem in recommendation systems that differs significantly from prior NIPS publications.

Significance: Time-sensitive recommendation has substantial potential impact for real-world applications, from improving user engagement in mobile services to enabling proactive healthcare interventions. The paper demonstrates how their framework advances the state-of-the-art in capturing temporal dynamics of user behavior, with promising scalability to large-scale datasets.
