This paper introduces a new approach to time-sensitive recommendation that couples low-rank structure in user–item interactions with Hawkes processes to model recurrent user activities. The authors cast learning as a convex problem and propose an efficient algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient steps, claiming O(1/t) convergence and scalability to very large datasets. The method targets two tasks: recommending the right item at the right time and predicting user return times, and it reports superior performance to existing methods. The learning procedure appears amenable to distributed implementation.

Quality: The paper takes a principled stance by modeling observations (ratings) via stochastic point processes, which is appealing. I do have a few concerns. First, in Eq. (8) and Theorem 2: in Eq. (8), over which variable is the maximum taken? Is it over ρ? If so, can ρ* become unbounded? Clarifying the domain and conditions would help. Second, Theorem 2 does not seem to guide the choice of ρ in the experiments; this weakens the practical impact of the theorem if its guidance is not used in model selection.

Regarding baselines, SVD++ [17] (the Netflix Prize winner) is often considered a very strong approach for time-aware recommendation. Including SVD++ as a baseline would further strengthen the empirical comparison, though I am not dissatisfied with the current set.

Clarity: The manuscript is clearly written and well organized, and it is easy to follow.

Originality: The contribution appears novel, as time-sensitive recommendation remains relatively underexplored, and the connection between low-rank modeling and Hawkes processes is interesting.

Significance: Time-sensitive recommendation is potentially impactful. This work presents a new algorithm that leverages low-rank structure with Hawkes processes, admits distributed training, and empirically outperforms existing approaches.
