- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework modeling user-item interaction sequences as low-rank Hawkes processes.  
- Connects self-exciting point processes with low-rank matrix models to capture recurrent temporal user activity patterns.  
- Targets two problems: recommend the most relevant item at the right moment and predict users' next return time.  
- Combines Hawkes-process temporal expressiveness with low-rank structure for scalability across millions of user-item pairs.  
- Uses a convex optimization formulation (Eq. 4) and provides theoretical convergence (Theorem 2: O(1/t) rate).  
- Theorem 1 is unclear in practice because the threshold ρ* depends on unknown optimal parameters, making ρ hard to set.  
- Authors tune ρ empirically but do not link that tuning to the theoretical guarantees.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and transparent, using synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II).  
- Experiments demonstrate superior performance over several baselines (Poisson, STiC, SVD, tensor factorization).  
- Claims strong scalability to hundreds of millions of events and millions of user-item pairs, making it practical for deployment.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and explains the Hawkes–recommendation connection clearly.  
- Novelty: first to combine Hawkes processes with low-rank factorization to enable generalization to unseen items.  
- Significance: addresses an important gap—temporal dynamics in recommendations—with clear applications in e-commerce, streaming, and healthcare.  
- Suggested improvements: compare against SVD++ and clarify ρ selection; better discuss handling of cold-start users/items.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worthy of acceptance given sound technical approach, solid experiments, and practical relevance.
