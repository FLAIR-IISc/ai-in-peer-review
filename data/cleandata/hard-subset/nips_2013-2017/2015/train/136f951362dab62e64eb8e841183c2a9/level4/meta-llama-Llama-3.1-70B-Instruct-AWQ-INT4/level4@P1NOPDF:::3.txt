This paper presents a novel algorithm for tackling time-sensitive recommendation by capturing the low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and utilizing the Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed fashion and achieves better results than existing methods.

Quality: Overall, I think the paper takes a principled approach to modeling the observations (ratings) using stochastic processes. However, I have a few comments. 

One concern in the theoretical part is with Eq. (8) and Theorem 2. Specifically, in Eq. (8), over which parameter do we take the maximum? Is it over ρ? If so, can ρ* be unbounded? Additionally, Theorem 2 is not used when the paper chooses ρ in experiments, which diminishes the importance of the theorem.

Another comment is regarding the baselines in the experiments. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17], the winner of the Netflix challenge, is one of the best algorithms for time-sensitive recommendation. Including SVD++ in the baseline comparisons would further improve the quality of the paper, although I am not dissatisfied with the current baselines.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Originality: In my opinion, the paper appears to be novel, as time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively new problem.

Significance: I believe time-sensitive recommendation can have a significant impact. The paper's contribution to this area, presenting a novel algorithm that captures the low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and utilizes the Hawkes process, is noteworthy. The algorithm's ability to be solved in a distributed fashion and achieve better results than existing methods further adds to its significance.
