- Introduces a novel framework combining self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption.
- Models user-item interactions with event density composed of a constant baseline intensity plus a history-dependent self-exciting component.
- Uses low-rank factorization for user and item latent vectors to compute intensities.
- Develops an efficient optimization algorithm with O(1/t) convergence rate.
- Empirical results on synthetic and real-world data show superior performance on time-sensitive recommendation tasks versus state-of-the-art methods.
- Experimental evaluation needs stronger baselines (e.g., ensemble of STiC and SVD) to better demonstrate incremental value.
- Paper omits how the bandwidth parameter σ for the exponential triggering kernel is selected and lacks sensitivity analysis for it.
- Lacks details on selection of other critical hyperparameters, including latent dimension across methods.
- Real-world dataset descriptions are insufficient—missing stats on recurring events per user-item pair and temporal irregularity.
- Technical quality is acceptable but empirical validation is not comprehensive.
- Clarity suffers from language, grammar, and typographical issues that reduce presentation quality.
- The integration of self-exciting point processes with low-rank factorization is an interesting, novel combination.
- The problem is practically significant; the method’s scalability and ability to incorporate contextual information increase its real-world relevance.
