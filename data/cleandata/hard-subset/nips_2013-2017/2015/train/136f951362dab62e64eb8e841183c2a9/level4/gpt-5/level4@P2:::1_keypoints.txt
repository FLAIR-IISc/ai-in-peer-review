- Summary: Proposes a convex, low-rank Hawkes-based time-sensitive recommendation framework combining baseline preferences and self-excitation, with a scalable proximal–conditional gradient algorithm and O(1/t) convergence; shows improved ranking and return-time prediction on synthetic and three real datasets.

- Relation to prior work: Positions itself between survival/semi-Markov models and temporal factorization, contributing a novel convex low-rank Hawkes formulation and hybrid optimization distinct from discretized/time-independent approaches.

- Strength: Unified model capturing long-term preferences and short-term recency via Hawkes processes.

- Strength: Convex objective with a scalable, practical algorithm and solid convergence analysis.

- Strength: Empirical performance gains on time-aware ranking and return-time prediction across datasets, with time-change calibration checks.

- Strength: Model extensible to include contextual features (profiles, text, location).

- Weakness: Introduction underdevelops motivation for return-time prediction and lacks concrete application scenarios and measurable impact claims.

- Weakness: Missing simple return-time baselines (e.g., last-gap, median/mode gap, per-pair exponential/Rayleigh MLE, Hawkes without low-rank) to contextualize gains.

- Weakness: No direct popularity baselines for ranking (time-varying/global item intensity or decayed counts), which are often strong comparators.

- Weakness: Practical lift unclear — needs absolute and relative top-k metrics (Recall@k/NDCG@k) vs. SVD and popularity to assess user-visible impact.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of when temporal variation matters — quantify self-excitation prevalence/strength (α vs γ0, kernel half-life) and whether gains concentrate on certain users/items.

- Weakness: Return-time evaluation may be high-variance — needs calibration (PIT), robust metrics (median absolute error, quantile errors), sensitivity to thinning samples, and binned-delay classification/AUC.

- Weakness: Missing ablations and sensitivity analyses for self-excitation removal, low-rank regularization, kernel families, and penalty parameter ρ.

- Weakness: Cold-start handling unclear — model cannot score brand-new items/users without context; suggest context-augmented variant or held-out new-item experiments.

- Weakness: Computational details lacking — report wall-clock comparisons, memory footprint, and per-iteration costs versus ADMM/proximal SVD approaches.

- Quality: Formulation and algorithm appear technically sound and convex with convincing convergence, but empirical evidence needs strengthening via additional baselines and ablations.

- Clarity: Generally well written but intro and Section 2 need better motivation/transitions; notation should be defined early and kept consistent; explain Z1/Z2 penalty and choice of ρ.

- Originality: Novel link between low-rank user–item coupling and convex Hawkes intensities with a proximal–conditional gradient hybrid and convergence analysis.

- Significance: Potentially impactful for real-time recommendation, marketing timing, and healthcare monitoring if validated against stronger baselines and application-driven metrics.

- Suggestions: Add simple return-time and time-varying popularity baselines, report top-k absolute gains, provide calibration/variance analyses, perform ablations and sensitivity tests, clarify cold-start, and include computational comparisons.

- Pros: Convex, scalable framework with convergence guarantees, strong empirical results, and extensibility to contextual features.

- Cons: Weak motivation/practical impact for return-time, missing simple baselines, limited ablation/sensitivity analysis, and unclear magnitude of user-visible improvements.

- Minor comments: Fix typo “abel”→“able” (p.3); clarify/remove abrupt retweet/link-creation example in Section 2 or add citations.

- Overall: Promising and potentially significant work but borderline acceptability until stronger baselines, deeper analyses, and clearer application-driven metrics are provided.
