- Proposes a collaborative filtering model using point processes: intensity = constant baseline + self-exciting term, parameterized by two sets of latent user/item vectors.
- Derives an optimization algorithm and reports empirical gains on real-world datasets versus several baselines.
- Strength: Novel, principled synthesis of point process theory and latent factor models to capture temporal dynamics.
- Strength: Tackles an important practical problem—time-sensitive and recurring user preferences—with clear real-world relevance.
- Weakness: Experimental baselines are insufficient; should compare to ensembles (e.g., STiC + SVD) to test whether integration outperforms modular combinations.
- Weakness: Lacks hyperparameter details and sensitivity analysis (e.g., kernel bandwidth σ, latent vector dimensions), hurting reproducibility and fairness.
- Weakness: Dataset characterization is too brief; needs temporal statistics (recurrence counts, inter-event time distributions, irregularity) to justify the model choice.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—grammatical errors and typos—reduce clarity and readability.
- Overall: Promising and well-motivated contribution, but experimental rigor and presentation must be improved to convincingly demonstrate the model's advantages.
