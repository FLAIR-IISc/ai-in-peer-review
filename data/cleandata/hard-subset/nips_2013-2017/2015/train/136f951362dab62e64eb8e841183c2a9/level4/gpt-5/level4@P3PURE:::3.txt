Summary:
The paper proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation that models user–item interactions with a low-rank structure and incorporates a Hawkes process to capture temporal dynamics. The method supports distributed optimization and outperforms existing approaches empirically.

Quality:
The paper takes a principled approach by modeling observations (ratings) using stochastic processes, which I find compelling. I do, however, have several concerns:

- Theory (Eq. 8 and Theorem 2): In Eq. (8), over which parameter is the maximum taken? Is the maximization over ρ? If so, can the optimal ρ* be unbounded? Additionally, Theorem 2 does not appear to inform the choice of ρ in the experiments, which undermines the practical significance of the theorem.

- Baselines: To my knowledge, SVD++ [17] (the winner of the Netflix Prize) is among the strongest algorithms for time-sensitive recommendation. Including SVD++ as a baseline would further strengthen the empirical evaluation. That said, I am not dissatisfied with the current set of baselines.

Clarity:
The paper is well written and easy to follow.

Originality:
The work appears novel, particularly because time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively new problem area.

Significance:
Time-sensitive recommendation has the potential for substantial impact. This paper contributes a new algorithm that leverages low-rank structure and a Hawkes process, can be solved in a distributed manner, and empirically improves upon existing methods.
