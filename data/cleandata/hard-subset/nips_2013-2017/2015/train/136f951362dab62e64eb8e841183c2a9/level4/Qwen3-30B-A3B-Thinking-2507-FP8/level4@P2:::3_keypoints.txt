- Problem addressed: time-sensitive recommendation via a novel combination of low-rank models and Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes.
- Tasks solved: (1) recommend the most relevant item at the right moment, and (2) predict users' next return time.
- Main contributions: principled connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank models; a convex optimization framework capturing recurrent temporal patterns; an efficient O(1/t) convergent algorithm.
- Experiments and scalability: comprehensive evaluation on synthetic and real datasets, outperforming strong baselines and scaling to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Relation to prior work: extends survival-analysis and matrix-factorization approaches by enabling generalization to new items and explicitly modeling temporal dynamics.
- Theoretical strength: solid foundations and a notable convergence result (Theorem 2).
- Theoretical concern: Theorem 1’s use of ρ* is underspecified—unclear what the max is taken over and whether ρ* could be unbounded; experiment choice of fixed ρ is unexplained.
- Clarity and reproducibility: well-written, logically presented, with sufficient algorithmic detail and pseudocode for reproduction.
- Originality: innovative application of Hawkes processes within a low-rank recommendation framework, addressing a gap in temporal recommendation.
- Significance: addresses a highly relevant problem for web services; method is practically applicable due to performance and scalability.
- Pros summarized: novel method, strong theory, thorough experiments, demonstrated scalability, clear presentation.
- Cons summarized: ambiguity in theoretical analysis of ρ*, omission of SVD++ as a baseline, and the related-work comparisons could be strengthened.
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance.
