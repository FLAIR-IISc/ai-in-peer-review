This paper presents a novel point process collaborative filtering model that captures the temporal and recurrent relationships between users and items by modeling the event density as the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity dependent on past events. The model utilizes two sets of user and item latent vectors to compute two sets of intensity, and the corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation. Empirical studies demonstrate the proposed method's advantage over commonly used alternatives.

However, the experimental studies can be enhanced by incorporating additional baselines, such as a simple ensemble of STiC and SVD, which would provide a more comprehensive comparison. Furthermore, the authors should clarify the selection process for the bandwidth parameter σ in the γ function used for the self-exciting process and assess the sensitivity of the results to variations in σ. Additionally, the authors should provide more details on the hyperparameter selection process for both the baseline methods and the proposed method, including the dimension of the latent vectors.

Providing more information about the characteristics of the two real-world datasets, such as the number of recurring events per user/item pair and the irregularity of the temporal patterns, would be beneficial. This would enable a deeper understanding of the temporal structure and the model's performance.

The overall quality of the paper is satisfactory, but there is room for improvement. The presentation can be enhanced by refining the language, correcting typos, and addressing grammar errors.

The proposed method is an innovative combination of point process and latent factor models, showcasing good originality. The problem and method have practical significance, making this a valuable contribution to the field.
