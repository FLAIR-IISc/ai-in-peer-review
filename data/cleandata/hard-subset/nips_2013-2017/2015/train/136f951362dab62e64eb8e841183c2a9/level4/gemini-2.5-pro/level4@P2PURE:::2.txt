-- Summary --
This paper introduces a point process collaborative filtering model designed to capture the temporal and recurrent structure of user-item interactions. For each user-item pair, the model defines an event intensity as the sum of a constant base intensity and a self-exciting intensity that depends on past events. These two components are parameterized by two distinct sets of latent vectors for users and items. The paper derives the corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and provides empirical studies to demonstrate the proposed model's advantages over common alternatives.

However, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened in several ways. For instance, a valuable baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Since the authors argue that models like [12, 13] cannot recommend entirely new items—a weakness SVD addresses—it would be informative to compare the proposed method against such an ensemble. Additionally, the paper lacks crucial details regarding hyperparameter selection. For the γ function used in the self-exciting process, it is unclear how the bandwidth parameter σ was set, and a sensitivity analysis of the results to variations in σ would be beneficial. Furthermore, the paper provides no details on how other important hyperparameters, such as the dimension of the latent vectors, were chosen for the proposed model or the baselines.

Finally, to better contextualize the model's contributions, the paper would benefit from a more detailed characterization of the two real-world datasets. Given the focus on temporal structure, providing descriptive statistics, such as the number of recurring events per user-item pair and measures of the irregularity of the temporal patterns, would be highly relevant.

-- Quality --
The overall quality is reasonably good, though the paper would be significantly improved by addressing the weaknesses in the experimental evaluation.

-- Clarity --
The presentation could be improved. The manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading to correct grammatical errors and typos, which would enhance its overall readability.

-- Originality --
The proposed method represents an interesting combination of point process theory and latent factor models for collaborative filtering.

-- Significance --
The problem of modeling temporal dynamics is of high practical importance in recommender systems, and the proposed method is well-motivated. While the paper introduces a novel model with a corresponding optimization algorithm and demonstrates its potential, its overall impact could be enhanced by strengthening the experimental validation and improving the clarity of the presentation.
