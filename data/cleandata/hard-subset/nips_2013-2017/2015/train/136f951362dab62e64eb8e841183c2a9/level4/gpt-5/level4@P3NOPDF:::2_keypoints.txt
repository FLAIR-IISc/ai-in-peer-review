- Summary: Proposes a collaborative filtering model using temporal point processes with separate background and self-exciting latent factors per user–item pair; provides an optimization algorithm and reports empirical gains.

- Strength: Addresses a clear, relevant problem by combining point processes with latent factors to model long-term preferences and recurrent interactions.

- Strength: Intuitive, interpretable separation between background and self-exciting intensities.

- Strength: Provides a concrete training/optimization procedure rather than only a conceptual model.

- Strength: Empirical results show promising improvements over common baselines.

- Weakness: Baselines and cold-start handling are insufficient; should compare to an ensemble (e.g., STiC + SVD) or fallback strategy for unseen items and report tuning details.

- Weakness: Kernel specification and sensitivity unclear — need explanation of how the gamma kernel bandwidth σ is chosen (global vs. per-entity) and a sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness: Missing hyperparameter details (latent dimensionalities for each component, regularization, learning rates, initialization, early stopping) and validation protocol for fair comparisons.

- Weakness: Dataset characterization lacking — provide statistics on recurring events per pair, inter-event time distributions, temporal coverage, event density, cold-start prevalence, and train/test temporal split.

- Weakness: Evaluation details need clarification — precise metrics, prediction task definition (next-event vs. time-to-event), and reporting of variability/statistical tests.

- Weakness: Writing and presentation need polishing — fix typos, define notation early, and add intuition/figures showing how past events modulate intensity.

- Originality: Meaningful integration of known ingredients; separating background preference from self-excitation at the user–item level is interesting and practically motivated.

- Significance: Relevant for recommendation systems; framework has practical value but requires stronger empirical evidence and baselines.

- Quality: Technically sound with a reasonable optimization approach, but experiments are under-documented and need additional analyses.

- Clarity: Readable but requires tighter notation, improved language, and more implementation detail for accessibility.

- Questions for authors: How is σ selected and how sensitive are results to it (global vs. parameterized)? Are latent dimensions shared or distinct between components? How are completely new items handled at test time and how are their background factors initialized? What is the computational complexity per event and how does training scale?

- Overall assessment: Solid and timely contribution recommended for acceptance contingent on stronger empirical evaluation (especially ensemble baseline), full hyperparameter/dataset details, and improved writing.
