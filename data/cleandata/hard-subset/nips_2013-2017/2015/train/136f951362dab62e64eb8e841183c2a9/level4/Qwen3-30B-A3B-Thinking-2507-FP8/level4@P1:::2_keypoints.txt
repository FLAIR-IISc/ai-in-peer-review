- Proposes a novel framework combining self-exciting (Hawkes) point processes with low-rank models and nuclear-norm convex optimization for time-sensitive recommendations.  
- Models intensity as baseline user preferences plus self-exciting influence from past events and scales to large datasets via convex formulation.  
- Evaluated on next-item recommendation and return-time forecasting against baselines (Poisson, STiC, MF, tensor methods) on synthetic and real data.  
- Strength: sound technical approach with clear convex formulation, convergence guarantees, and demonstrated scalability and empirical effectiveness.  
- Novelty: new integration of point processes with low-rank convex optimization enabling recommendations for new items.  
- Significance: addresses important practical problems (right-item-at-right-moment and return-time prediction) with broader applicability to nonnegative low-rank problems.  
- Major experimental concern: comparison baseline incomplete—STiC’s inability to recommend new items should be contrasted with an ensemble of STiC+SVD.  
- Missing sensitivity analysis for the exponential kernel bandwidth σ and lack of details on its selection.  
- Insufficient hyperparameter selection details for the proposed method and baselines, especially latent dimension choice.  
- Dataset description lacking: needs statistics like distribution of recurring events per user-item and temporal irregularity.  
- Clarity issues: writing has grammatical errors, awkward phrasing, and inconsistent terminology (e.g., misleading description of latent vectors vs. matrix factorization).  
- Overall: promising and impactful contribution but needs stronger experimental validation and careful revision for clarity and precision.
