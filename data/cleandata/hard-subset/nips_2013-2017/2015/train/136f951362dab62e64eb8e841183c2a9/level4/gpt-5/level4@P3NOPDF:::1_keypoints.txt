- Paper proposes time-sensitive recommendation that models time-varying user preferences and predicts next return time, with an optimization approach claimed to scale; evaluated on one large synthetic and two modest real datasets.
- Strength: Addresses temporal dynamics in user preferences, a relevant and challenging problem.
- Strength: Jointly models recommendation and return-time prediction, which could be valuable if well-motivated and actionable.
- Strength: Claims an efficient optimization approach and demonstrates scalability on synthetic data.
- Weakness: Does not demonstrate prevalence, frequency, magnitude, or structure of temporal drift in the real datasets; motivation is speculative.
- Weakness: Motivation and concrete applications for return-time prediction are unclear and not operationalized for product use.
- Weakness: Missing simple and necessary baselines for return-time prediction (last inter-arrival, modal inter-arrival global/user/segment, recency/seasonality-aware baseline).
- Weakness: Return-time regression may be unnecessarily hard; bucketed classification could be more stable and actionable.
- Weakness: Recommendation experiments lack sufficient baselines and clarity—only SVD compared, no popularity/time-aware baselines, and no clear reporting of absolute/relative gains or significance.
- Weakness: Real datasets are modest and may not test scalability or robustness to real-world nonstationarity; synthetic scale experiment is not a full substitute.
- Weakness: Positioning and related work are weak—discussion jumps to retweets/link creation without context or citations and needs clearer situating among time-aware recommendation and survival/hazard models.
- Requested: Provide empirical evidence of temporal drift (per-user/global drift measures, top-k changes, time-varying NDCG, decomposition into global vs user-specific changes).
- Requested: Justify return-time use cases and metrics, define operational thresholds, evaluate regression and bucketed classification, report calibration, and include simple baselines.
- Requested: Add recommendation baselines (popularity, time-decayed MF), report absolute metrics (Recall@k, NDCG@k, MAP), relative improvements, significance, and ablations isolating temporal vs static effects and coupling between modules.
- Requested: Evaluate robustness/practicality—sensitivity to time granularity/window size, cold-start behavior, training/inference time and memory on real datasets, and learning curves.
- Clarity: Tighten motivation by showing why time-varying preferences matter in the target domains and how much they change in practice.
- Clarity: Motivate return-time prediction with product relevance and define success criteria before listing it as a contribution.
- Clarity: Improve flow in Section 2 and avoid abrupt context switches; add relevant references.
- Minor: Fix typo “is abel to capture” → “is able to capture” and add references when mentioning retweets and link creation.
- Overall assessment: Promising direction with a scalable method, but currently under-substantiated due to lack of drift evidence, unclear return-time motivation, missing simple baselines/analyses; recommend marginal/weak reject unless revised.
