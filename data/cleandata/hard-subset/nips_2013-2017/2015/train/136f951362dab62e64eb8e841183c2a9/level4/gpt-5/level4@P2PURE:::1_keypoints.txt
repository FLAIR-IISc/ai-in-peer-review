1. Paper proposes a new method for time-sensitive recommendations that explicitly models time-varying user preferences and predicts users’ next return time, learned via an efficient optimization algorithm and evaluated on one synthetic and two modest real-world datasets.

2. Reviewer asks how prevalent time-varying user preferences are in practice and requests analysis of the real datasets to substantiate this claim.

3. Reviewer asks whether observed preference changes are driven mainly by new popular songs or by other recurring patterns, and requests deeper analysis.

4. Reviewer requests concrete motivation and justification for predicting a user’s next return time, including specific use cases for web companies.

5. Reviewer notes omitted simple baselines for return-time prediction: last observed inter-arrival time and the modal inter-arrival time.

6. Reviewer suggests predicting delay categories (e.g., short/medium/long/very long) as a simpler and more reliable alternative to exact return-time prediction.

7. Experimental results: reviewer finds it hard to assess the magnitude of improvement over SVD and asks whether the gain is substantial or perceptible to users.

8. Reviewer asks for comparison against a strong popularity baseline (e.g., globally popular items refreshed daily), which is often hard to beat.

9. Small comments: typo correction (“is abel” → “is able”) and need to rephrase and add citations for the retweets/link-creation examples in Section 2 due to abrupt context shift.

10. Overall assessment: tackles two problems without adequate motivation or necessary baselines/analyses; overall rating OK/marginal.
