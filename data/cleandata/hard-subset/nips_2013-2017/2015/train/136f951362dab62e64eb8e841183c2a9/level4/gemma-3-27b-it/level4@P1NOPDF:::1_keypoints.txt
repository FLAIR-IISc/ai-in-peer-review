- Proposes a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations and next-return-time prediction with a custom efficient optimization algorithm.  
- Experiments use one synthetic dataset (scalability) and two modest real-world datasets.  
- Need to verify prevalence of rapidly time-varying user preferences in the real datasets and analyze root causes (e.g., new popular items vs. other patterns).  
- Motivation for predicting next return time is unclear; paper should explain concrete applications and business value.  
- Overlooks simple baselines for return-time prediction: last observed delay and most frequent delay.  
- Predicting precise return time is hard; recommend reframing as classification into short/medium/long/very-long return windows.  
- Experimental gains over SVD are not clearly characterized—unclear if improvements are substantial or perceptible to users.  
- Missing comparison to a dynamic popularity baseline, which is often a strong benchmark.  
- Abrupt, unsupported introduction of retweets and link creation in section 2; needs references and smoother context.  
- Several baselines and analyses are missing, and the two posed problems lack adequate motivation and discussion of impact.  
- Minor typo: "and is abel to capture" → "and is able to capture."  
- Overall assessment: OK/marginal paper.
