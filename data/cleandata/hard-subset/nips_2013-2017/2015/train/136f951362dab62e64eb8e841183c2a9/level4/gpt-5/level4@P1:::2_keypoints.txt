- Proposes a time-sensitive recommendation framework using Hawkes processes with low-rank nonnegative matrices tying user–item intensities.  
- Models each user–item intensity as baseline plus history-dependent self-excitation and learns shared structure via two low-rank factors.  
- Formulates convex parameter estimation with nuclear-norm regularization and nonnegativity, and develops a proximal+conditional-gradient algorithm with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Addresses two tasks: time-sensitive item ranking using λu,i(t) and return-time prediction via the conditional density approximated by Ogata’s thinning.  
- Empirical evaluation on large synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) shows strong accuracy and scalability versus Poisson, STiC, SVD, and tensor baselines.  
- Claims extensibility to incorporate contextual features (profiles, text, location) into the intensity function.  
- Contrasts with prior survival-analysis return-time models and tensor-factorization approaches, and situates work relative to prior Hawkes and low-rank methods.  
- Strength: unified principled model for ranking and return-time prediction within a Hawkes framework.  
- Strength: convex formulation with nuclear-norm and nonnegativity plus clear equivalence and convergence analysis.  
- Strength: demonstrated scalability to millions of pairs and hundreds of millions of events with empirical linear scaling.  
- Strength: competitive or superior cross-domain performance (music, e-commerce, clinical).  
- Weakness: missing an important baseline ensemble (STiC+SVD) that could cover unseen-item recommendation and should be compared.  
- Weakness: unclear selection and sensitivity analysis for the triggering-kernel bandwidth σ (needs validation procedure and robustness checks).  
- Weakness: insufficient hyperparameter details for proposed method and baselines (λ, β, ρ, latent dims, m for thinning, stopping criteria, step-sizes).  
- Weakness: lack of dataset temporal characterization (events per pair distribution, fraction with multiple events, inter-event variability, seasonality, train/test split details).  
- Weakness: unclear treatment of truly new items/users with zero history and how cold-starts were handled in experiments.  
- Weakness: return-time estimation uses sampling without reporting sample counts, overhead, variance reduction, or calibration diagnostics.  
- Quality: technically sound with a solid convex approach and practical algorithm, but empirical rigor needs strengthening.  
- Clarity: generally understandable but marred by typos; needs improved notation, expanded pseudocode, input/output definitions, per-iteration complexity, and clearer experimental protocol.  
- Originality: novel combination of Hawkes processes with low-rank nonnegative matrices and a blended proximal/conditional-gradient algorithm with convergence guarantees.  
- Significance: practically valuable and scalable approach to time-sensitive recommendation; relevance would increase with stronger experiments and clearer presentation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled unified model, convex formulation with efficient algorithm and guarantees, and demonstrated scalability and performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing key ensemble baseline and sensitivity analyses, insufficient hyperparameter and dataset details, and presentation/language issues.  
- Overall: solid contribution that would be stronger with improved experimental rigor, additional baselines, sensitivity analyses, and clearer exposition.
