-- Summary -- This paper introduces a point process collaborative filtering model designed to capture the temporal and recurrent patterns in user-item interactions. Specifically, the event density for each user-item pair is modeled as the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity that depends on past events. These intensities are computed using two sets of latent vectors, one for users and one for items. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and present empirical results demonstrating the advantages of their approach over existing methods.

However, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened. A sensible baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Given the authors’ argument that [12, 13] struggle to recommend completely new items – a limitation SVD can address – comparing against this ensemble would be valuable. Furthermore, regarding the gamma function used for the self-exciting process, the method for setting the bandwidth parameter σ is not detailed. An analysis of the sensitivity of the results to variations in σ would be beneficial. Additionally, the paper lacks details concerning the selection of hyperparameters for both the proposed method and the baselines, such as the dimensionality of the latent vectors. 

Providing further characterization of the two real-world datasets would also be helpful. Given the paper's focus on temporal structure, discussing simple statistics like the number of recurring events per user/item pair and the irregularity of the temporal patterns would provide valuable context.

-- Quality -- The overall quality is reasonably good, but could be improved.

-- Clarity -- The paper's presentation suffers from issues with language, typos, and grammatical errors.

-- Originality -- The proposed method represents an interesting combination of point process models and latent factor models.

-- Significance -- Both the problem addressed and the proposed method have practical value. This paper introduces a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. The corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation and empirical studies have been done to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed methods to other commonly used alternatives. However, the experimental studies and the presentation can be improved.
