This paper proposes a new algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation that exploits the low-rank structure of a user–item matrix and models temporal dynamics with a Hawkes process. The method can be solved in a distributed manner and attains better performance than existing approaches.

Quality: Overall, the paper takes a principled approach by modeling the observations (ratings) using stochastic processes. However, I have a few comments.
One concern in the theoretical section involves Eq. (8) and Theorem 2. In Eq. (8), over which parameter is the maximization taken? Is it over rho? If so, could the optimal rho be unbounded? Additionally, Theorem 2 does not appear to be used when selecting rho in the experiments, which diminishes the theorem’s practical significance.
Another comment concerns the experimental baselines. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17] (the Netflix Prize winner) is among the strongest time-sensitive methods. Including SVD++ as a baseline would further strengthen the evaluation, though I am not dissatisfied with the current set.

Clarity: The paper is well written and easy to follow.

Originality: The work appears novel, as time-sensitive recommendation remains a relatively new problem.

Significance: Time-sensitive recommendation can be impactful. This paper introduces a new algorithm for this setting by capturing low-rank structure in a user–item matrix and employing a Hawkes process. The algorithm admits a distributed solution and achieves better results than existing methods.
