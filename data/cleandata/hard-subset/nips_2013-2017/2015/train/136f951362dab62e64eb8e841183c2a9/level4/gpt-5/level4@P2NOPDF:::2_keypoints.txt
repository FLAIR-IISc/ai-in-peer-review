- Introduces a point-process collaborative filtering model with event intensity = baseline + self-exciting term, both parameterized by user and item latent vectors; provides an optimization algorithm and empirical comparisons.

- Experimental evaluation is weak: missing a natural hybrid baseline (ensemble of STiC and SVD) to test handling of new items vs. recurrence.

- Kernel bandwidth σ for the self-exciting term is not specified; selection method and sensitivity analysis are missing.

- Important hyperparameters (e.g., latent dimensionality) and tuning protocol are not reported; sensitivity analyses for these are absent.

- Dataset descriptions lack temporal statistics (e.g., repeat events per user–item pair, inter-event time distributions) to justify modeling recurrence and timing.

- Technical derivations and optimization appear sound, but incomplete evaluation design undermines empirical claims.

- Presentation issues: grammar and typos need fixing, and some definitions (how intensity terms map to latent vectors) are unclear.

- A concise algorithmic summary (step-by-step) would improve readability and reproducibility.

- Originality: offers a meaningful synthesis of temporal point processes with latent factor recommendation models.

- Significance: addresses an important practical problem, but impact depends on strengthening experiments, hyperparameter reporting, and dataset characterization.
