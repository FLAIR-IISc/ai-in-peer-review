- Paper proposes a time-sensitive recommender using low-rank Hawkes processes modeling each user–item sequence with baseline + history-dependent triggering and nuclear-norm convex estimation.
- Authors present a hybrid proximal + conditional-gradient algorithm handling nonnegativity and nuclear norms, prove O(1/t) convergence, and show penalty-based equivalence to the constrained form.
- Model enables time-sensitive top-k recommendation via instantaneous intensities and next-return-time prediction via conditional sampling.
- Positions work relative to point-process and Hawkes literature and contrasts with survival-analysis, timeSVD++, tensor factorization, and Poisson baselines.
- Technical novelty: clear connection between low-rank structure and Hawkes processes with a well-motivated convex surrogate under nonnegativity.
- Algorithmic contribution: scalable proximal/conditional-gradient hybrid with provable convergence and per-iteration operations relying on projections and top singular vectors.
- Scope: unified framework for ranking and return-time prediction with principled incorporation of side information.
- Empirics: large-scale synthetic experiments (hundreds of millions of events) and real-data tests in music, e-commerce, and healthcare demonstrating scalability and promising accuracy.
- Weak motivation: paper claims widespread time-varying preferences and return-time importance but does not quantify these phenomena on the real datasets.
- Missing simple/strong return-time baselines (e.g., last inter-event time, median/mode per pair, user/item Poisson/renewal models, time-varying popularity).
- Limited evaluation metrics: reporting only average rank; needs Recall@k, NDCG@k, MAP and stronger popularity baselines for recommendation, plus RMSE/relative error/calibration for return-time.
- Empirical depth concerns: moderate real dataset sizes, unclear effect sizes/confidence intervals, and lack of ablations (Λ0-only, A-only), kernel/sensitivity analyses (σ, ρ, rank).
- Clarity/details lacking: heavy notation, missing algorithmic specifics (how top singular vectors computed, stopping criteria, step-size/Lipschitz estimation, memory footprint), and rationale for sampling-based return-time estimation.
- Quality assessment: modeling and optimization are technically sound and the convergence analysis is a valuable contribution; empirical results are promising but need stronger evaluation to fully support claims.
- Readability: generally good but needs tightened sections, explicit evaluation protocols/metrics, dataset statistics, and more algorithmic detail.
- Originality: novel application of low-rank Hawkes with convex estimation and a hybrid optimization scheme appears original for the venue.
- Significance: could influence time-sensitive recommendation and user modeling if validated with stronger baselines and metrics; optimization approach may generalize to other nonnegative low-rank problems.
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually compelling integration, efficient algorithm with guarantees, broad applicability, and cross-domain promising results.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient empirical motivation, missing simple baselines and unclear practical improvement over SVD/popularity, limited metrics and ablations, and presentation/clarity issues.
- Suggested improvements: quantify temporal drift/recurrence statistics; add LIET/median/mode/user-item Poisson/popularity baselines and timeSVD++ if possible; report Recall@k/NDCG@k, significance tests, RMSE/calibration for return-time; perform ablations and sensitivity studies; provide algorithmic and implementation details; discuss deployment use cases.
- Minor comments: fix typo “abel”→“able”, support example shifts with citations, and include clear definitions or a notation table.
