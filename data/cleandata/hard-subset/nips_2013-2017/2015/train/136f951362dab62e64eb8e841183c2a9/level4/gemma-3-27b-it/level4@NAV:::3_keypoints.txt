- Introduces a new time-sensitive recommendation algorithm combining low-rank user-item matrix factorization with a Hawkes process.  
- Algorithm is suitable for distributed computation and empirically outperforms current state-of-the-art methods.  
- Presents a sound probabilistic modeling approach treating user ratings as a stochastic process.  
- Clarification needed for Equation (8): if maximization is over ρ, address possibility of an unbounded ρ*.  
- Theorem 2’s practical relevance is limited because it was not used for parameter selection in experiments.  
- Experimental evaluation would be strengthened by adding SVD++ (Netflix-challenge winner) as a baseline.  
- Manuscript is well-written and easy to understand.  
- Work appears novel given the emerging focus on time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Time-sensitive recommendation is significant; the paper’s approach (low-rank + Hawkes, distributed solvability) has potential impact.
