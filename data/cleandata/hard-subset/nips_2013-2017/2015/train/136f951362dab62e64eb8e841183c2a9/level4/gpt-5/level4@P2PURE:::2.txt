-- Summary --
This paper proposes a point process–based collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. For each user-item pair, the event intensity is modeled as the sum of a constant baseline intensity and a self-exciting component that depends on past events. These two components are parameterized via two sets of user and item latent vectors. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and present empirical studies showing advantages over commonly used alternatives.

However, the experimental evaluation can be improved. A sensible additional baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Since the authors argue that [12, 13] cannot recommend completely new items while SVD can, comparing the proposed method to this ensemble would be informative. In addition, for the gamma function used in the self-exciting process, how is the bandwidth parameter sigma chosen, and how sensitive are the results to variations in sigma? Furthermore, there are no details on how other important hyperparameters are selected for either the baselines or the proposed method, such as the dimensionality of the latent vectors.

It would also be helpful to provide more details about the characteristics of the two real-world datasets. Because the paper focuses on temporal structure, it would be useful to report simple statistics such as the number of recurring events per user-item pair and the degree of temporal irregularity.

-- Quality --
Overall quality is reasonably good but not outstanding.

-- Clarity --
The presentation could be improved; there are issues with language, typos, and grammar.

-- Originality --
The method is an interesting combination of point processes and latent factor models.

-- Significance --
Both the problem and the method have practical value. The paper introduces a point process collaborative filtering model to capture temporal and recurrent user-item interactions, derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation, and provides empirical evidence of advantages over common alternatives; however, the experimental study and the presentation can be strengthened.
