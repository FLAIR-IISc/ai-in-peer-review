- Proposes a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations and next-returning-time prediction with an efficient optimizer, evaluated on one synthetic and two modest real datasets.
- Unclear how widespread time-varying user preferences are; needs dataset analysis and investigation of underlying causes (e.g., new releases vs other patterns).
- Motivation for predicting next returning time is weak; paper should justify business utility and how companies would use/integrate this prediction.
- Missing baseline comparisons: last time delay and most-occurring time delay should be included.
- Predicting exact return time may be unrealistic; recommend predicting probability of return within categorical timeframes (short/medium/long/very long).
- Experiments need clearer quantification of performance lift vs SVD—whether improvements are substantial or marginal and noticeable to end-users.
- Should compare to a simple popularity-based baseline (daily recalculated popular items).
- Minor issues: typo ("abel" → "able") and Section 2’s abrupt/context-shifting mention of retweets/link creation without references; rephrase for clarity.
- Overall: addresses two distinct problems but lacks concrete motivation, missing analyses and baselines; acceptable but marginal.
