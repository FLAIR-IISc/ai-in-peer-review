- Paper proposes a point-process collaborative filtering model with base and self-exciting intensity components parameterized by user/item latent vectors.  
- Empirical results claim superiority over several baselines but experimental evaluation needs strengthening.  
- Authors omit a simple ensemble baseline (STiC + SVD) that would test claims about recommending new items.  
- The bandwidth parameter σ for the self-exciting γ function is not explained or sensitivity-tested.  
- Key hyperparameters (e.g., latent vector dimensions) for the proposed method and baselines are not justified or reported, harming reproducibility.  
- Datasets lack temporal characterization (e.g., average recurrence rate per user-item pair, inter-event time distributions) to contextualize applicability.  
- Technical foundation is sound, but experimental rigor is only moderate.  
- Writing has grammatical errors, inconsistent terminology (e.g., "event density" vs. "intensity"), and phrasing issues that reduce clarity.  
- Integration of point processes with latent factor models is an original contribution.  
- The problem is significant for recommender systems, but contribution may be incremental without stronger empirical validation against ensembles.  
- Overall: promising idea but needs improved empirical validation, parameter reporting, dataset characterization, and presentation.
