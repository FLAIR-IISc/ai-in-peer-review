This paper introduces a new algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation, effectively integrating a low-rank user-item matrix factorization with a Hawkes process model. A key strength is the algorithm’s suitability for distributed computation and its demonstrated superior performance compared to current state-of-the-art methods.

Quality: The paper demonstrates a sound, probabilistic modeling approach to representing user ratings as a stochastic process. However, I have a couple of points for consideration. 

Regarding the theoretical contribution, specifically Equation (8) and Theorem 2, clarification is needed concerning the maximization parameter in Equation (8). If the maximum is taken with respect to ρ, the potential for an unbounded ρ* should be addressed. Furthermore, the practical relevance of Theorem 2 is diminished as it wasn’t utilized in the parameter selection process during experimentation.

Concerning the experimental evaluation, including SVD++ [17] – a highly successful algorithm and the winner of the Netflix challenge – as a baseline would strengthen the comparison. While the current baseline selection is adequate, incorporating SVD++ would provide a more comprehensive assessment.

Clarity: The manuscript is well-written and readily understandable.

Originality: The work appears novel, particularly given the relatively recent emergence of time-sensitive recommendation as a focused research area.

Significance: Time-sensitive recommendation holds considerable potential for impact, and this paper presents a novel algorithm to tackle time-sensitive recommendation by capturing low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and by using Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed fashion and achieves better results than existing methods.
