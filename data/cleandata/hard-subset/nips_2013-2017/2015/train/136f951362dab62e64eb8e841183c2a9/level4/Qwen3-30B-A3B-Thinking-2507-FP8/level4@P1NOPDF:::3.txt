

This paper addresses time-sensitive recommendation by modeling user-item interactions through a low-rank matrix structure combined with Hawkes processes. The proposed algorithm is distributed-friendly and demonstrates superior performance over existing baselines.  

**Quality.** The paper adopts a principled approach to modeling ratings via stochastic processes, though two concerns merit attention. First, Equation (8) specifies a maximum over parameter ρ, but it is unclear whether ρ* could become unbounded under this optimization. Second, Theorem 2—which provides theoretical guarantees for convergence—appears unused in the experimental setup, reducing its perceived practical significance.  

Regarding baselines, the current selection lacks SVD++ [17], the Netflix Challenge winner and a strong benchmark for time-sensitive recommendation. Including SVD++ would better contextualize the proposed method’s improvements, though the existing baselines are adequate.  

**Clarity.** The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making the methodology accessible.  

**Originality.** Time-sensitive recommendation remains a relatively underexplored problem, and the integration of low-rank matrix decomposition with Hawkes processes presents a novel approach.  

**Significance.** Time-sensitive recommendation holds meaningful potential for real-world applications, and this work contributes a technically sound solution that advances the field.
