This paper introduces a new algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation that leverages low-rank structure within the user-item matrix and incorporates a Hawkes process. The proposed algorithm is amenable to distributed computation and demonstrates performance improvements over existing approaches.

Quality: I found the paper’s approach to modeling observations (ratings) using stochastic processes to be principled and well-motivated. However, I have a couple of points for consideration. 

Regarding the theoretical development, specifically Equation (8) and Theorem 2, it is unclear over which parameter the maximization is performed. If the maximization is with respect to ρ, is it possible for ρ* to be unbounded? Furthermore, Theorem 2 does not appear to be utilized in the paper's experimental selection of ρ, which diminishes its practical relevance.

Another suggestion concerns the choice of baselines. Given that SVD++ [17] – a highly successful algorithm and the winner of the Netflix challenge – is a strong contender for time-sensitive recommendation tasks, including it in the baseline comparison would strengthen the evaluation. That said, I do not find the current baseline selection wholly inadequate.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to understand.

Originality: The work appears novel, particularly given that time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively recent and underdeveloped research area. 

Significance: Time-sensitive recommendation has the potential to be highly impactful. This paper presents a novel algorithm to tackle time-sensitive recommendation by capturing low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and by using Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed fashion and achieves better results than existing methods.
