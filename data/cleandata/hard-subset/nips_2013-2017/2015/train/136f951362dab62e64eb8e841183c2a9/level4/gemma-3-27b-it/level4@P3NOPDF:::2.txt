## Review of NIPS 2015 Submission

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel point process collaborative filtering model designed to capture the temporal and recurrent interactions between users and items. The core idea is to model user-item interactions as a point process, where the event density for each user-item pair is composed of a constant intensity component and a self-exciting component based on past interaction history. These intensity components are parameterized by user and item latent vectors, allowing the model to learn representations that capture both collaborative and temporal dynamics. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and demonstrate empirically that their method outperforms several established alternatives.

While the proposed approach is promising, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened. A strong baseline comparison would be an ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. The authors correctly identify the limitations of STiC and similar models in recommending new items – a weakness that SVD could potentially address – and a comparison to this simple ensemble would provide a more comprehensive evaluation. Furthermore, the method employs a Gaussian kernel (represented by the \gamma function) within the self-exciting process. The sensitivity of the results to the bandwidth parameter, \sigma, is not explored; a discussion of how \sigma was selected and an analysis of its impact on performance would be valuable. Finally, details regarding hyperparameter selection for both the proposed method and baseline methods are lacking. Specifically, the choice of latent vector dimensionality should be justified and its influence assessed. 

Providing additional descriptive statistics for the real-world datasets used would also enhance the paper. Given the focus on temporal structure, reporting metrics such as the average number of recurring events per user/item pair, and quantifying the irregularity of the temporal patterns would provide valuable context.

**Quality:**

The overall quality of the work is good, but falls short of being excellent. The technical approach is sound, and the derivation of the optimization algorithm appears correct. However, the aforementioned limitations in the experimental evaluation and presentation impact the overall confidence in the results.

**Clarity:**

The paper’s presentation requires improvement. The writing contains instances of awkward phrasing, grammatical errors, and typos which detract from readability. While the core ideas are generally understandable, a careful revision to improve clarity and polish the language is recommended.

**Originality:**

The combination of point process modeling with latent factor models for collaborative filtering represents a novel and interesting contribution. The application of point processes to capture the temporal dependencies in user-item interactions is a significant aspect of the work.

**Significance:**

Both the problem addressed – modeling temporal dynamics in recommendation systems – and the proposed method have considerable practical relevance. Understanding and exploiting the temporal patterns in user behavior can lead to more accurate and timely recommendations. The ability to capture recurrent interactions and the derived optimization algorithm contribute meaningfully to this area. However, strengthening the experimental validation will be crucial to fully demonstrate the significance of the approach.
