Review- The paper introduces a new approach for time-aware recommendations based on user activity. It differs from prior work by explicitly handling time-varying user preferences. It also considers predicting a user’s next return time. Training is carried out with an efficient optimization algorithm proposed by the authors. The experiments include one synthetic dataset to demonstrate scalability and two real-world datasets of modest size.

First, how widespread is the issue of time-varying user preferences? It would help to analyze the real datasets used in the experiments to substantiate this claim. If present, is the variation mainly due to the release of new popular songs, or are there other dominant patterns?

What is the motivation for predicting a user’s next return time? Please motivate this before presenting it as a contribution. How would a web company use such a prediction, and for what purposes?

I believe some simple baselines for return-time prediction were not evaluated: 1) the most recent inter-arrival time; 2) the most frequent inter-arrival time. Also, predicting the exact return time may be overly difficult. A simpler, more practical target might be a categorical delay: short (1–5 days), medium (5–14 days), long (14–33 days), or very long (>33 days) before the next use.

Experimental section: It was difficult to assess how substantial the improvement over SVD is. Is it order-of-magnitude or modest? Would users notice the difference? How does this compare to recommending popular items? In many scenarios, a popularity baseline—especially if refreshed daily—is hard to beat.

Small comments:
1) Typo in the last paragraph of page 3: "and is abel to capture" -> "able".
2) In the first paragraph of Section 2, you mention retweets and link creation in social networks without references. The abrupt context shift was confusing; consider rephrasing. The paper tackles two problems—time-sensitive recommendations and user return-time prediction—without clear motivation for their importance or impact. Several baselines and analyses are missing (details above). Otherwise, overall an OK/marginal paper.
