This paper proposes a new method for time-sensitive recommendations based on user activity. It differs from prior work by explicitly modeling time-varying user preferences, and it also addresses prediction of a user’s next return time. Learning is performed via an efficient optimization algorithm proposed by the authors. The experiments include a large-scale synthetic dataset to demonstrate training efficiency and two real datasets of modest size.

Questions and concerns:
- How prevalent is the issue of time-varying user preferences? Please analyze the real datasets used in your experiments to substantiate this claim. If preferences do vary, is the variation primarily driven by the emergence of new popular songs, or do other patterns dominate?
- What is the motivation for predicting a user’s next return time? This needs to be justified before presenting it as a key contribution. How would a web company use such a prediction, and for what concrete purposes?
- There appear to be simple baselines for return-time prediction that were not evaluated: (1) using the most recent inter-arrival time, and (2) using the most frequent inter-arrival time for the user. Moreover, predicting the exact next return time may be unnecessarily challenging. A simpler and potentially more actionable task would be to predict a coarse category, such as short (1–5 days), medium (5–14 days), long (14–33 days), or very long (>33 days) before the user returns.
- Experimental section: It is hard to gauge the practical significance of the lift over SVD. Is the improvement an order of magnitude, or modest? Would users notice the difference? Please also compare against a popularity-based recommender (e.g., top-N recomputed daily), which is often a strong and surprisingly hard-to-beat baseline.

Minor comments:
1) Typo in the last paragraph of page 3: “and is abel to capture” → “and is able to capture.”
2) In the first paragraph of Section 2, you introduce retweets and link creation in social networks without references and with an abrupt shift in context. Please rephrase, add citations, and make the connection to your problem setting explicit.

Overall assessment:
The paper tackles two problems—time-sensitive recommendations and prediction of user return time—but offers limited motivation for their relevance and impact. Important baselines and analyses are missing, making it difficult to assess the contribution. Aside from these issues, the work is overall OK/marginal.
