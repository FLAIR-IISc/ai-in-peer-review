This paper introduces a new algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation, effectively leveraging low-rank matrix factorization and a Hawkes process. A key strength is the algorithm’s suitability for distributed computation and its demonstrated performance improvements over current state-of-the-art methods.

Quality: The paper demonstrates a sound, probabilistic modeling approach to representing user behavior (ratings) through stochastic processes. However, I have a couple of points for consideration. 

Regarding the theoretical development, specifically Equation (8) and Theorem 2, clarification is needed on the parameter with respect to which the maximization is performed. If the maximization is over ρ, the potential for an unbounded ρ* should be addressed. Furthermore, the practical application of Theorem 2 appears limited, as the paper’s experimental choice of ρ does not utilize the theorem’s guidance, diminishing its relevance.

Concerning the experimental evaluation, the inclusion of SVD++ [17] – a highly successful algorithm from the Netflix challenge and a strong performer in recommendation tasks – as a baseline would strengthen the comparative analysis. While the current baseline selection is adequate, incorporating SVD++ would provide a more comprehensive benchmark.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and readily understandable.

Originality: The work appears novel, particularly given the relatively recent emergence of time-sensitive recommendation as a research area.

Significance: Time-sensitive recommendation holds considerable potential impact, and this paper presents a novel algorithm to tackle time-sensitive recommendation by capturing low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and by using Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed fashion and achieves better results than existing methods.
