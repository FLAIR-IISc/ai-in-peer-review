## Review

**Summary:** This paper introduces a point process collaborative filtering model designed to model the temporal and recurrent interactions between users and items. The model defines the event density for each user-item pair as the sum of a constant and a self-exciting component, with the latter driven by past events and parameterized by user and item latent vectors. An optimization algorithm is presented for parameter estimation, and empirical results demonstrate performance gains over existing methods.

However, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened. A compelling baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD, given the authors’ observation regarding the limitations of [12, 13] in recommending novel items – a weakness potentially addressed by SVD.  Further clarification is needed regarding the bandwidth parameter σ used in the γ function for the self-exciting process; an analysis of the results’ sensitivity to variations in σ would be valuable.  Additionally, the paper lacks detail on the selection of key hyperparameters for both the proposed method and the baselines, such as the dimensionality of the latent vectors. 

Providing more descriptive statistics for the real-world datasets would also enhance the paper. Given the focus on temporal structure, reporting metrics like the number of recurring events per user/item pair and the irregularity of the temporal patterns would be beneficial.

**Quality:** The overall quality of the work is reasonable, though not exceptional.

**Clarity:** The paper’s presentation could be improved through more careful attention to language, and correction of typos and grammatical errors.

**Originality:** The proposed method represents an interesting and novel combination of point process modeling and latent factor techniques.

**Significance:** Both the problem addressed and the proposed method hold practical value. This paper introduces a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. The corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation and empirical studies have been done to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed methods to other commonly used alternatives. However, the experimental studies and the presentation can be improved.
