- Paper proposes a new method for time-sensitive recommendations and a model to predict users' next return time, trained with an efficient optimization algorithm and tested on one large synthetic and two modest real-world datasets.
- Central claim that time-varying user preferences are widespread is insufficiently substantiated; authors should analyze real datasets to show the nature and causes of temporal preference shifts.
- Motivation for predicting next return time is weakly articulated; paper needs concrete practical applications and explanations of business/user value.
- Return time prediction evaluation omits simple but important baselines: "last time delay" and "most occurred time delay" per user.
- Predicting exact return time as regression is overly difficult; reframing as classification into intervals (short/medium/long/very long) would be more practical and actionable.
- Reported improvement over the SVD baseline lacks context on magnitude and practical significance for end-users.
- Experiments do not include a "most popular" baseline, which is a strong and necessary real-world comparator.
- Typographical error: "and is abel to capture" should be "and is able to capture".
- Section 2 abruptly introduces retweets and link creation without context or references, which is confusing and needs better integration.
- Overall: interesting problems but insufficient motivation, missing baselines, and weak experimental analysis leave the paper marginal in its current form.
