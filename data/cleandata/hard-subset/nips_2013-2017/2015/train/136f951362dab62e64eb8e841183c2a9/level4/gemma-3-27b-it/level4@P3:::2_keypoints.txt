- Introduces a novel framework combining Hawkes self-exciting point processes with low-rank matrix factorization to capture recurrent temporal user-item interaction patterns for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Models user activity intensity via a Hawkes process learned through a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm with provable O(1/t) convergence and demonstrated scalability.
- Overcomes prior temporal-method limitations (e.g., survival analysis, hidden semi-Markov models) by enabling generalization to recommend new items via low-rank structure.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets shows competitive performance against multiple baselines, supporting practical relevance for personalization and marketing.
- Novelty: the integration of Hawkes processes and low-rank models is a promising and original contribution.
- Theoretical foundation: convex problem formulation and convergence-guaranteed optimization are significant strengths.
- Practical significance: addresses directly relevant problems of timing recommendations and predicting user return times.
- Comprehensive experiments: inclusion of several baselines on varied datasets provides evidence of effectiveness.
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: lacks detailed discussion and sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (ρ, λ, β, σ) and latent dimension choices.
- Weakness — baseline comparison: missing a potentially strong baseline (ensemble of STiC and SVD) that could offer insightful comparison.
- Weakness — dataset characterization: real-world datasets need more statistics (e.g., average recurring events per user/item, temporal irregularity) to contextualize results.
- Weakness — presentation: writing contains awkward phrasing, typos, and grammatical errors that reduce clarity.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, scalable, empirically validated method with notable practical impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete hyperparameter/sensitivity analysis, missing strong baseline, limited dataset detail, and subpar writing quality.
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address hyperparameter tuning, baseline inclusion, dataset characterization, and proofreading.
