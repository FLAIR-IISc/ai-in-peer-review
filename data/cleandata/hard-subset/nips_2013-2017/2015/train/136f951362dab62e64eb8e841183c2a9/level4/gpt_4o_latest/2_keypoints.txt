- Paper proposes a point process-based collaborative filtering model combining a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity per user-item pair.
- Uses separate latent vectors for users and items to compute two sets of intensities and derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation.
- Empirical results claim advantages over common alternatives, but experimental evaluation needs improvement.
- Missing a meaningful baseline: a simple ensemble of STiC and SVD should be compared to address new-item recommendations.
- Unclear how the bandwidth parameter σ in the γ self-excitation function is selected and how sensitive results are to σ variations.
- Paper lacks details on hyperparameter selection (e.g., latent vector dimensions) for both baselines and the proposed model.
- Dataset descriptions are insufficient; basic statistics on recurring events per user-item pair and temporal irregularity are needed.
- Overall quality is decent but not excellent.
- Presentation and clarity suffer from language issues, typos, and grammatical errors.
- Approach is original in integrating point process modeling with latent factor models.
- Problem and method are practically relevant, but significance is weakened by limited evaluation and presentation issues.
