- Proposes a point-process collaborative filtering model where event density per user-item pair is a constant plus a self-exciting term.  
- Uses two sets of user and item latent vectors to compute distinct intensities and derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation.  
- Empirical studies on synthetic and real-world datasets show advantages over common alternatives.  
- Overall quality is reasonable but has room for improvement.  
- Experiments should include additional baselines (e.g., ensemble of STiC and SVD).  
- Important hyperparameter choices (e.g., latent dimension) are under-specified for both baselines and the proposed method.  
- Paper contains language, typo, and grammar issues; several sentences are wordy or awkward (abstract needs rephrasing).  
- Method is an interesting and novel integration of point processes with latent factor models.  
- Problem and method have practical value and potential to improve recommender systems by modeling temporal and recurrent user behavior.  
- Effectiveness is demonstrated empirically on both synthetic and real datasets.  
- Suggestion: add baseline ensemble of STiC [12] + SVD to strengthen comparisons.  
- Suggestion: report how hyperparameters (e.g., latent dimensions) are chosen for all methods.  
- Suggestion: improve writing clarity and correct typos/grammar.  
- Suggestion: provide dataset characteristics (e.g., number of recurring events per user/item pair, temporal irregularity).  
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to the bandwidth parameter σ in the self-exciting process.  
- Pro: novel method, practical value, and empirical validation.  
- Con: experimental comparisons and hyperparameter tuning are insufficient.  
- Con: presentation quality needs improvement (language and typos).  
- Con: lacks sufficient detail about real-world dataset characteristics.
