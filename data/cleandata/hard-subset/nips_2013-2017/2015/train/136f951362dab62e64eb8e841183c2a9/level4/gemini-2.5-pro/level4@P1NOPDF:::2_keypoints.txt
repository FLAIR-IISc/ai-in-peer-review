- Proposes a point-process collaborative filtering model with baseline and self-exciting terms parameterized by separate latent user/item vectors to capture temporal and recurring interaction patterns.  
- Derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and reports empirical advantages over common alternatives.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: a crucial baseline (an ensemble of STiC and SVD) is missing despite authors' claim that temporal models alone cannot recommend new items.  
- Hyperparameter details are incomplete: the kernel bandwidth (σ) for the self-exciting term is unspecified and lacks sensitivity analysis.  
- Other key hyperparameters (e.g., latent dimensionality) and their selection procedures for both the proposed model and baselines are not reported.  
- Dataset descriptions lack temporal-characteristic statistics (e.g., distribution of recurring events per user-item pair, measures of temporal irregularity).  
- Paper quality is generally good but would be strengthened by addressing experimental and hyperparameter concerns.  
- Presentation suffers from language issues, typos, and grammatical errors that reduce clarity.  
- The method is original and an interesting combination of point processes with latent-factor collaborative filtering.  
- The problem is significant and the proposed approach has practical value, contingent on a more thorough experimental evaluation and improved presentation.
