- Summary: Proposes a point process-based collaborative filtering model with base-rate plus self-exciting intensity using user/item latent vectors, optimized and validated on real datasets.  
- Strength: Conceptually sound fusion of point processes and latent factors that target temporal/recurrent user-item interactions, showing promising empirical performance.  
- Major concern 1 (Baseline): Missing critical baseline—an ensemble of STiC and SVD—to test whether the model outperforms a naive combination that handles recurrence and cold-start.  
- Major concern 2 (Hyperparameters): Paper omits how key hyperparameters (e.g., gamma kernel bandwidth σ and latent dimensionality) are chosen and lacks sensitivity/ablation analyses.  
- Major concern 3 (Dataset characterization): Lacks temporal statistics (mean/median recurrence intervals, coefficient of variation of inter-event times) to show the datasets exhibit the temporal patterns the model claims to capture.  
- Clarity/Presentation: Writing has grammatical errors and ambiguous phrasing; figures/tables lack details (error bars, hyperparameter settings); optimization convergence exposition is weak.  
- Originality/Significance: Interesting, practically relevant incremental contribution combining point processes with latent vectors, addressing temporal dynamics in CF but building on prior work.  
- Recommendation (requested revisions): Add STiC+SVD baseline, conduct σ and latent-dimension sensitivity analyses, provide temporal dataset statistics, and improve language and exposition.  
- Overall assessment: Quality moderate (sound technical foundation but weak experiments), clarity needs major improvement, originality moderate, significance high due to temporal relevance.  
- Final recommendation: Weak reject pending major revisions to strengthen experimental rigor and presentation.
