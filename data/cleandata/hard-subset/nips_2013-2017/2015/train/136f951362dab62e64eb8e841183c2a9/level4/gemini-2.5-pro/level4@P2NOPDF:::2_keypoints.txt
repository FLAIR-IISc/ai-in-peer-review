- Proposes a collaborative filtering model that combines temporal point processes with latent factor models to capture baseline and self-exciting user-item interaction intensities.  
- Strength: Novel synthesis of temporal point processes and latent factors to model rich temporal interaction structures.  
- Strength: Addresses a significant, practical problem of time-sensitive user preferences with a principled approach.  
- Weakness: Missing stronger, pragmatic baselines—should compare against an ensemble of a temporal model (e.g., STiC) and standard latent-factor models (e.g., SVD/matrix factorization).  
- Weakness: Lacks hyperparameter details and sensitivity analysis—especially selection and sensitivity of the self-exciting kernel bandwidth σ and latent vector dimensionality.  
- Weakness: Insufficient dataset characterization—needs statistics on recurrence per user-item pair and inter-event time distributions (mean, median, variance) to contextualize temporal challenges.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues—multiple typos and grammatical errors; needs thorough proofreading to improve clarity.  
- Overall: Interesting and valuable idea but experimental evaluation and methodological/data details must be strengthened to convincingly demonstrate the model’s advantages.
