- Paper proposes a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations and predicting users' next returning time, with an efficient optimization algorithm and experiments on one synthetic and two real datasets.  
- Reviewer questions whether time-varying user preferences are truly widespread and asks for dataset analysis to substantiate this claim and identify underlying drivers (e.g., trends vs other patterns).  
- Reviewer asks for clear motivation and practical use cases for predicting next returning time, explaining how web companies would use such predictions.  
- Missing simple baselines for return-time prediction (most recent delay, most frequent delay) and suggestion to predict delay categories (short/medium/long/very long) instead of exact times.  
- Experimental results unclear: significance of improvement over SVD is not established; reviewer asks if improvement is substantial or noticeable to end users.  
- Request to compare recommendations against a popular-items baseline (e.g., daily-updated popular list), which can be hard to beat.  
- Minor: typo on page 3 ("abel" → "able").  
- Minor: Section 2’s introduction of retweets and link creation lacks references and reads as an abrupt/contextual shift needing rephrasing.  
- Overall: Addresses two relevant problems but lacks strong justification, missing baselines and analyses; overall assessment is OK/marginal.
