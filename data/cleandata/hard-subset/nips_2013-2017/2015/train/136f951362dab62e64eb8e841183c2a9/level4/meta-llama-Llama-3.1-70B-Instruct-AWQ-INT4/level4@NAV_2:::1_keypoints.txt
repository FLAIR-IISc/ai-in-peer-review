- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation method based on user activity and also predicts users' next returning time.
- Introduces an efficient optimization algorithm for learning and demonstrates scalability on synthetic data.
- Experiments include two modest real datasets and comparisons to baselines (Poisson, STiC, SVD, tensor factorization).
- Detailed model description (low-rank Hawkes processes, triggering-kernel parametrization) and thorough related-work discussion.
- Optimization algorithm is well-explained and convergence analysis is rigorous.
- Results claim improved predictive performance over several baselines.
- Needs deeper analysis of real datasets to better characterize time-varying user preferences and behavioral patterns.
- Motivation and practical use-cases for predicting next returning time are insufficiently explained.
- Missing simple baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last time delay, most frequent delay).
- Predicting exact return times may be unrealistic; suggest focusing on coarse categories (short/medium/long delays).
- Experimental section should clarify the practical significance of improvements over SVD and compare to popular-item baselines.
- Minor issues: typo ("abel" → "able") and rephrase first paragraph of Section 2 to avoid unsupported social-network examples.
- Overall well-written and novel, but addressing the above concerns would strengthen the paper.
