This paper proposes a time-aware recommendation algorithm that models user–item interactions via a low-rank structure and temporal dynamics using a Hawkes process. The method supports distributed optimization and demonstrates superior performance to existing methods on the reported datasets.

Quality: The approach is principled and thoughtfully models observations (ratings) with stochastic processes. I have two main concerns about the theoretical component:
- Equation (8): Over which parameter is the maximization taken? Is it over ρ? If so, can the optimal ρ* be unbounded? Please clarify the feasible set, assumptions, and any regularization that ensures boundedness and well-posedness.
- Theorem 2: The theorem does not appear to guide the choice of ρ in the experiments. This disconnect reduces the theorem’s practical relevance. It would strengthen the paper to either (a) use Theorem 2 to select ρ and show the resulting performance, or (b) explain why it is not applicable in practice and provide an alternative principled selection procedure.

Baselines: I recommend adding SVD++ [17], a strong time-aware collaborative filtering method and a key component in the Netflix Prize winner, as a baseline. While I am not dissatisfied with the current comparisons, including SVD++ would provide a more comprehensive and convincing evaluation.

Clarity: The paper is well written and easy to follow. The notation and algorithmic details are generally clear, and the distributed optimization aspects are described in a digestible manner.

Originality: The problem of time-sensitive recommendation remains relatively new, and combining low-rank modeling with Hawkes processes in this context appears novel.

Significance: Time-aware recommendation is practically important. The proposed method’s distributed implementation and empirical improvements over existing approaches suggest meaningful potential impact.
