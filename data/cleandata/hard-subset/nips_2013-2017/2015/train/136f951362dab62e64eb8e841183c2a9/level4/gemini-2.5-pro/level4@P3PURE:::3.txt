This paper presents a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation that models the user-item rating matrix with a low-rank structure and captures temporal dynamics using a Hawkes process. The proposed method can be solved in a distributed manner and is shown to achieve superior results compared to existing methods.

**Quality:**
The paper takes a principled approach, modeling user ratings as observations from a stochastic process. However, I have a few concerns:

1.  Regarding the theoretical analysis, it is unclear in Equation (8) over which parameter the maximum is being taken. If it is over ρ, it is not specified whether ρ* could be unbounded. Furthermore, Theorem 2 is not utilized in the experimental section for the selection of ρ, which diminishes the practical significance of the theorem.

2.  The choice of experimental baselines could be strengthened. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17], the winner of the Netflix challenge, is one of the strongest baselines for time-sensitive recommendation. While the current baselines are reasonable, including SVD++ would make the experimental evaluation more comprehensive and convincing.

**Clarity:**
The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

**Originality:**
The paper addresses the relatively new problem of time-sensitive recommendation and proposes a novel approach. The combination of low-rank matrix modeling with Hawkes processes appears to be an original contribution to this area.

**Significance:**
The problem of time-sensitive recommendation is highly impactful. This paper offers a significant contribution by presenting a novel, distributed algorithm that effectively models temporal dynamics and demonstrates strong empirical performance.
