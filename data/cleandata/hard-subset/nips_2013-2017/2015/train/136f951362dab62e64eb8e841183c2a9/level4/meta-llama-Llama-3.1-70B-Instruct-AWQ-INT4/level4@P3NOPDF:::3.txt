This paper proposes a novel algorithm for tackling time-sensitive recommendation tasks by capturing the low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and leveraging the Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed manner, yielding improved results compared to existing methods.

**Quality:** Overall, the paper adopts a principled approach to modeling observations (ratings) using stochastic processes. However, I have a few concerns and suggestions for improvement.

One concern lies in the theoretical part, specifically in Eq. (8) and Theorem 2. In Eq. (8), it is unclear over which parameter the maximum is taken. Is it over ρ? If so, can ρ* be unbounded? Furthermore, Theorem 2 is not utilized when selecting ρ in the experiments, which diminishes its significance.

Another comment pertains to the baselines used in the experiments. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17], the winner of the Netflix challenge, is one of the best algorithms for time-sensitive recommendations. Incorporating SVD++ into the baselines would further enhance the quality of the evaluation. Although I am not dissatisfied with the current baselines, the addition of SVD++ would provide a more comprehensive comparison.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Originality:** The paper appears to be novel, as time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively new problem. The proposed algorithm's unique combination of low-rank structure and Hawkes process contributes to its originality.

**Significance:** I believe that time-sensitive recommendation has the potential to make a significant impact. The proposed algorithm's ability to capture temporal dynamics and achieve better results than existing methods in a distributed manner makes it a valuable contribution to the field.
