**Summary**

This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model that captures the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. Specifically, for each user-item pair, the event density is represented as the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity that depends on past events. Two sets of intensity are computed using two sets of user and item latent vectors, respectively. The authors derive a corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and provide empirical studies that demonstrate the advantages of their proposed method over commonly used alternatives.

However, the experimental studies can be improved in several ways. For instance, a sensible baseline for comparison would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD, as the authors argue that [12, 13] are unable to recommend completely new items, which can be addressed by SVD. It would be valuable to compare the proposed method to this ensemble. Additionally, the authors should provide more details about the γ function used for the self-exciting process, specifically how the bandwidth parameter σ is set and how sensitive the results are to variations in σ. Furthermore, there is a lack of information about how important hyperparameters, such as the dimension of the latent vectors, are chosen for both the baseline methods and the proposed method.

It would also be helpful to provide more details about the characteristics of the two real-world datasets used in the study. Given the paper's focus on temporal structure, discussing simple statistics like the number of recurring events per user-item pair and the irregularity of the temporal pattern would be useful.

**Quality**

The overall quality of the paper is reasonably good but not outstanding.

**Clarity**

The paper's presentation can be improved in terms of language, typos, and grammar errors. Enhancing the writing quality would make the paper more readable and accessible to a broader audience.

**Originality**

The proposed method is an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models, offering a unique approach to collaborative filtering.

**Significance**

Both the problem and the method have good practical value. The paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items, and the corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation. Empirical studies demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method over other commonly used alternatives. However, the experimental studies and presentation can be improved to enhance the paper's overall impact.
