

This paper introduces a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation by modeling low-rank structure in the user-item matrix and incorporating Hawkes processes. The approach is amenable to distributed computation and achieves superior performance compared to existing methods.  

Quality: The paper adopts a principled stochastic process framework for modeling ratings. However, two concerns arise. First, regarding Equation (8) and Theorem 2: the maximization in Equation (8) is unclear—over which parameter is it taken (e.g., $\rho$)? If over $\rho$, could $\rho^*$ be unbounded? Furthermore, Theorem 2 is not utilized in the experimental parameter selection, undermining its significance. Second, the baseline comparison lacks SVD++ [17], a top-performing method for time-sensitive recommendation (notably the Netflix Prize winner). Including SVD++ would substantially strengthen the empirical evaluation, though the current baselines are acceptable.  

Clarity: The paper is well-written and clearly structured.  

Originality: The work demonstrates novelty in addressing time-sensitive recommendation, a relatively underexplored problem domain.  

Significance: Time-sensitive recommendation holds substantial potential impact, and this paper offers a meaningful contribution toward solving it.
