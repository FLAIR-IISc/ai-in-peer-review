- Proposes a novel algorithm combining low-rank matrix structure with Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation, supporting distributed optimization and showing improved performance over baselines.  
- Theoretical foundation is generally sound but Equation (8) omits the domain/constraints for ρ, risking an ill-posed optimization if ρ is unbounded.  
- Theorem 2 offers a key guarantee, but experiments do not use it to inform ρ selection, reducing the theorem’s practical relevance.  
- Authors should explicitly define the feasible set for ρ and justify its boundedness.  
- Experiments should demonstrate how Theorem 2 guides hyperparameter selection (e.g., via ablation or using derived ρ*), or clarify the theorem’s role.  
- Baseline comparisons lack strong benchmarks like SVD++ (and ideally T-ALS or ST-ALS); adding these would contextualize time-sensitive improvements.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with clear problem formulation, algorithmic steps, and experimental setup.  
- The fusion of low-rank models with Hawkes processes is original and significant for scalable, dynamic recommendation systems.  
- Recommendation: major revision to address theoretical clarifications and strengthen baselines before acceptance.
