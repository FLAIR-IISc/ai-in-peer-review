This paper presents a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation, leveraging low-rank structure in the user-item matrix and utilizing a Hawkes process. The algorithm is designed for distributed computation and demonstrates improved performance compared to existing methods.

Quality: Overall, the paper takes a principled approach to modeling observations (ratings) using stochastic processes. However, I have a few points to raise. 

My primary concern lies in the theoretical section, specifically Eq. (8) and Theorem 2. In Eq. (8), it’s unclear over which parameter the maximization is performed. If it’s over ρ, could ρ* be unbounded? Furthermore, Theorem 2 doesn't appear to be utilized when choosing ρ in the experiments, which diminishes its practical importance. 

Another comment concerns the baselines used in the experimental evaluation. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17], a highly successful algorithm and the winner of the Netflix challenge, is a strong contender for time-sensitive recommendation. Including SVD++ in the baseline comparison would further strengthen the results. That said, I don’t find the current selection of baselines wholly inadequate.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Originality: The paper appears novel, largely because time-sensitive recommendation itself is a relatively new and unexplored problem.

Significance: I believe time-sensitive recommendation has the potential to be impactful. This paper presents a novel algorithm to tackle time-sensitive recommendation by capturing low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and by using Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed fashion and achieves better results than existing methods.
