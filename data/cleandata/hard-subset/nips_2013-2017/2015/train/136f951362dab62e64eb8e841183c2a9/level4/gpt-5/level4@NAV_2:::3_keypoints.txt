- Introduces a method coupling low-rank user–item structure with Hawkes processes to model recurrent user activity.
- Casts learning as a convex problem and proposes an efficient algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient with claimed O(1/t) convergence and scalability.
- Targets two tasks: recommending the right item at the right time and predicting user return times; reports superior performance to existing methods.
- Learning procedure appears amenable to distributed implementation.
- Quality: principled modeling via stochastic point processes is appealing.
- Concern: Eq. (8) and Theorem 2 are ambiguous about the variable over which the maximum is taken (ρ?) and whether ρ* can become unbounded; domain/conditions need clarification.
- Concern: Theorem 2 does not appear to guide the choice of ρ in experiments, reducing its practical usefulness.
- Empirical suggestion: include SVD++ (a strong time-aware baseline) to strengthen experimental comparisons.
- Clarity: manuscript is clearly written, well organized, and easy to follow.
- Originality: the connection between low-rank modeling and Hawkes processes is novel and interesting.
- Significance: time-sensitive recommendation is impactful; the work enables distributed training and shows empirical improvements.
