This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. Specifically, for each pair of user and item, the event density is the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity depending on the past events. Two sets of intensity are computed by two sets of user and item latent vectors, respectively. The corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation, and empirical studies have been conducted to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed method over other commonly used alternatives.

However, the experimental studies can be improved in several ways. For instance, a sensible baseline to consider would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD, as the authors argue that [12, 13] are unable to recommend completely new items, which can be addressed by SVD. A comparison between the proposed method and this ensemble would be valuable. Additionally, it is unclear how the bandwidth parameter σ is set for the γ function used in the self-exciting process, and a sensitivity analysis of the results with varying σ would be beneficial. Furthermore, the paper lacks details on how important hyperparameters, such as the dimension of the latent vectors, are chosen for both the baseline methods and the proposed method.

It would also be helpful to provide more information about the characteristics of the two real-world datasets, particularly since the paper focuses on temporal structure. Simple statistics, such as the number of recurring events per user/item pair and the irregularity of the temporal patterns, would be useful to discuss.

The overall quality of the paper is reasonably good but not outstanding. The presentation can be improved in terms of language, with several typos and grammatical errors noted throughout the text.

The proposed method represents an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models, demonstrating a good level of originality.

The problem and method proposed in the paper have good practical value. The paper demonstrates the advantage of the proposed method over other commonly used alternatives, and the corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation. However, the experimental studies and presentation can be improved to strengthen the paper's overall impact.
