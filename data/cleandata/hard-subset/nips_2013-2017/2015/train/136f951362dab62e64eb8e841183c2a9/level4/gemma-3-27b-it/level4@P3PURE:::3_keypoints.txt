- Presents a novel time-sensitive recommendation algorithm using low-rank user-item structure and a Hawkes process, with distributed solvability and improved empirical performance.
- Quality: principled stochastic-process-based modeling of observed ratings, but some methodological concerns remain.
- Theoretical issue: Eq. (8) is ambiguous about which parameter is maximized; if maximization is over ρ, ρ* may be unbounded.
- Practical relevance concern: Theorem 2 is not used in selecting ρ in experiments, reducing its practical usefulness.
- Experimental suggestion: add SVD++ (a strong, Netflix-challenge-winning baseline) to strengthen empirical comparisons, especially for temporal dynamics.
- Clarity: the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Originality: work appears novel given the relative novelty of time-sensitive recommendation.
- Significance: addresses an impactful problem by combining low-rank structure and Hawkes processes, offering distributed solutions and better results than existing methods.
