This paper presents a novel algorithm to tackle time-sensitive recommendation by capturing low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and by using Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed fashion and achieves better results than existing methods.

Quality: Overall, I think the paper took a principled approach to model the observations using stochastic processes. However, I have a few comments. One concern in the theoretical part is in Eq. (8) and Theorem 2. In Eq. (8), over what parameter do we take the maximum? Is it over ρ? In such a case, can ρ* be unbounded? Additionally, Theorem 2 is not used when the paper chose ρ in experiments, which degrades the importance of the theorem.

Another comment is with the baselines in the experiments. To my knowledge, SVD++ (the winner of the Netflix challenge) is one of the best algorithms for time-sensitive recommendation. Adding SVD++ into the baseline would improve the quality even better. Although I am not dissatisfied with the current baselines, including SVD++ would strengthen the evaluation.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Originality: To me, the paper seems to be novel because time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively novel problem.

Significance: I think time-sensitive recommendation can be impactful. This paper presents a novel algorithm to tackle time-sensitive recommendation by capturing low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and by using Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed fashion and achieves better results than existing methods.

Arguments For Acceptance:
- The paper proposes a novel algorithm that tackles time-sensitive recommendation by capturing low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and by using Hawkes process.
- The algorithm can be solved in a distributed fashion and achieves better results than existing methods.
- Time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively novel problem, and this paper presents a novel solution.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
- The theoretical part of the paper has some unclear points, such as the parameter over which the maximum is taken in Eq. (8) and the boundedness of ρ*.
- The importance of Theorem 2 is degraded since it is not used in the experiments.
- The evaluation could be strengthened by including additional baselines, such as SVD++.
