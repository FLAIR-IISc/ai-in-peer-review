-- Summary --
This work introduces a collaborative filtering model based on point processes to model the temporal and recurring patterns in user-item interactions. For any given user-item pair, the model defines an event density composed of a base intensity and a self-exciting component that is influenced by historical events. This is achieved using two distinct sets of latent vectors for users and items to compute the respective intensity components. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for model parameter estimation and present empirical results suggesting the proposed model outperforms several common alternatives.

However, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened. A notable omission is a comparison against a straightforward ensemble baseline, such as combining STiC [12] with SVD. Given the authors' argument that models like [12, 13] cannot handle new items—a limitation SVD addresses—this ensemble would serve as a highly relevant and competitive benchmark. Furthermore, the paper lacks details regarding the selection of the bandwidth parameter σ within the γ function for the self-exciting process. A sensitivity analysis demonstrating the model's performance with varying σ values would be beneficial. Similarly, the manuscript does not specify how other critical hyperparameters, such as the dimensionality of the latent vectors, were selected for either the proposed model or the baseline methods.

Finally, the paper would be improved by a more detailed characterization of the two real-world datasets. Since the model's primary contribution relates to temporal dynamics, providing statistics such as the frequency of recurring events per user-item pair and measures of temporal irregularity would offer valuable context.

-- Quality --
The technical quality of the paper is solid, though there are clear areas for improvement, particularly in the experimental evaluation.

-- Clarity --
The clarity of the manuscript is hindered by several language, grammatical, and typographical errors. A thorough proofread would significantly improve its readability.

-- Originality --
The work presents a novel synthesis of point process theory and latent factor models for collaborative filtering.

-- Significance --
The problem addressed is of significant practical importance, and the proposed methodology offers a valuable contribution. The paper introduces a point process-based collaborative filtering approach to model temporal user-item dynamics, complete with a derived optimization algorithm and empirical validation. While the results appear promising, the paper's impact could be substantially increased by addressing the weaknesses in the experimental design and improving the overall presentation.
