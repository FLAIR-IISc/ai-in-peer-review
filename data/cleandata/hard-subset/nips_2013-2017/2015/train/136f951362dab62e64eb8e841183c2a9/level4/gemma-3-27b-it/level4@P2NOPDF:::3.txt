This paper introduces a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation, leveraging low-rank matrix factorization and a Hawkes process to model user behavior. A key strength is the algorithm’s suitability for distributed computation and its demonstrated performance improvements over existing approaches.

**Quality:** The paper demonstrates a principled approach to modeling user ratings as a stochastic process. However, I have a few points for consideration.

Regarding the theoretical contribution, specifically Equation (8) and Theorem 2, the maximization parameter requires clarification. Is the maximization performed over ρ? If so, the potential for an unbounded ρ* raises a concern. Furthermore, Theorem 2 appears unused in the experimental selection of ρ, diminishing its practical relevance and impact within the paper.

The experimental evaluation would be strengthened by the inclusion of SVD++ [17], a highly successful algorithm—and the winner of the Netflix challenge—known for its strong performance in collaborative filtering and, potentially, time-sensitive contexts. While the current baseline selection is reasonable, incorporating SVD++ would provide a more comprehensive benchmark.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and generally easy to follow. The authors clearly articulate the motivation and approach.

**Originality:** The work appears novel. Time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively new and actively developing area, and this paper presents a fresh perspective on addressing its challenges.

**Significance:** Time-sensitive recommendation has the potential to be highly impactful, and this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field. The proposed algorithm offers a promising approach to capturing the temporal dynamics of user preferences and improving recommendation accuracy in dynamic environments. The ability to scale the algorithm through distributed computation is also a significant practical advantage.
