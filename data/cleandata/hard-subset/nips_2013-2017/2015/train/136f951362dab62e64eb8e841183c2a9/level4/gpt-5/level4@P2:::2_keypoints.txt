- Proposes a time-sensitive recommendation framework using low-rank Hawkes processes with baseline + history-driven intensity per user–item pair.  
- Uses nonnegative low-rank matrices with nuclear-norm regularization to enable generalization to unseen pairs and a convex estimation problem.  
- Introduces an efficient solver combining proximal updates (nonnegativity) and conditional gradient (nuclear norm) with O(1/t) convergence and large-scale scalability.  
- Evaluates two tasks: instantaneous item ranking by intensity and next-return-time prediction via sampling from the conditional density.  
- Empirical results on synthetic, last.fm, tmall, and MIMIC-II datasets show improvements over Poisson baselines, STiC, SVD, and tensor factorization.  
- Novelty: couples Hawkes-process modeling of self-excitation with low-rank recommendation in a convex estimator and scalable optimization.  
- Theoretical contribution: convex formulation with convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithm.  
- Demonstrates applicability across diverse domains (music, e-commerce, clinical) and uses time-rescaling diagnostics to support Hawkes assumptions.  
- Strength: unified, principled approach jointly modeling “what” and “when.”  
- Strength: practical algorithm avoiding full SVDs and scaling to millions of pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Weakness: missing pragmatic baseline ensemble (e.g., STiC for seen items + SVD for new items) to test advantages over a two-model pipeline.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter selection (latent dims, λ, β, ρ) and lack of sensitivity analyses or guidance for ρ relative to ρ*.  
- Weakness: unclear kernel bandwidth choice (σ) — whether global or per-entity and how it was tuned; needs sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: limited dataset characterization (events per pair, recurrence rates, inter-event dispersion, cold-start proportions) to contextualize gains.  
- Weakness: missing ablations isolating Hawkes vs Poisson and low-rank vs unconstrained/diagonal structures to attribute improvements.  
- Weakness: evaluation metrics incomplete — recommend reporting top-k metrics (Recall@k/Precision@k/NDCG@k) and additional calibration/censoring checks for return-time prediction.  
- Presentation issues: typos, inconsistent notation, and unclear sampling-based return-time estimator details (number of samples); suggest clearer pseudocode and line-search description.  
- Questions for authors: how were σ, λ, β, ρ and baseline latent dims chosen and tuned; robustness to disabling self-excitation or replacing nuclear norms with fixed-rank factorization; top-k metrics; runtime/ADMM comparisons; and cold-start handling/effects.
