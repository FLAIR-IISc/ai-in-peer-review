- Paper proposes a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations that models time-varying user preferences and predicts next return time, with an efficient optimization algorithm.  
- Experiments show efficiency on large-scale synthetic data and results on two modest real-world datasets.  
- The prevalence of time-varying user preferences needs stronger justification; analyze real datasets to determine if variation is due to new popular items or other patterns.  
- Clarify the motivation and practical utility of predicting next return time for web companies (how would they use it?).  
- Missing simple baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last observed delay, most frequent delay).  
- Predicting exact return times may be unrealistic; consider predicting delay categories (short/medium/long/very long).  
- The significance of performance gains over SVD is unclear—need to state whether gains are substantial or modest and whether users would notice.  
- Compare results to a strong practical baseline: recommending daily-updated popular items.  
- Paper addresses two problems but lacks clear motivation for their relevance and impact.  
- Several key baselines and analyses are missing, weakening the experimental evaluation.  
- Minor issues: fix typo ("abel" → "able") and rephrase abrupt, unreferenced shift to retweets/social link creation in Section 2.  
- Overall assessment: acceptable but marginal.
