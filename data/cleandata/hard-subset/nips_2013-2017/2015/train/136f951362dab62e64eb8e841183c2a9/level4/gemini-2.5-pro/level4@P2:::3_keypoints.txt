- Problem: tackles time-sensitive recommendation and predicting users' next-return times.
- Core contribution: novel framework connecting self-exciting Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix models for recurrent user activities.
- Modeling: each user–item interaction sequence is a Hawkes process whose parameters form low-rank user/item matrices to handle sparsity and generalize to unobserved pairs.
- Estimation: convex formulation with nuclear-norm regularization to enforce the low-rank assumption.
- Optimization: custom algorithm combining proximal and conditional gradient methods that avoids full SVDs and achieves O(1/t) convergence, with theoretical analysis.
- Evaluation: tested on synthetic data and three real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) for item ranking and return-time prediction.
- Empirical result: outperforms several relevant baselines and shows superior fit via time-change theorem analysis.
- Strength — originality/significance: elegant, principled combination of temporal point processes and collaborative filtering addressing a practical problem.
- Strength — technical quality: sound convex formulation and a novel, potentially broadly useful optimization method with convergence guarantees.
- Strength — clarity: well-written, clearly structured paper with helpful background and motivation.
- Strength — empirical thoroughness: comprehensive experiments and convincing comparisons against Poisson factorization and tensor methods.
- Weakness — theory vs practice: Theorem 1’s threshold ρ* is non-constructive (depends on optimal solution), so ρ must be tuned in practice; sensitivity discussion would help.
- Weakness — baselines: would be stronger with comparisons to additional time-aware latent-factor methods (e.g., timeSVD++).
