- Problem and contribution: Proposes Hawkes-Recommender, combining self-exciting temporal point processes (Hawkes) with low-rank collaborative filtering to predict what item a user will choose and when they will return.  
- Modeling choice: Models user-item interaction sequences as Hawkes processes with base intensity from a user-item preference matrix and excitation from past interactions.  
- Low-rank assumption: Uses low-rank structure for base intensity and self-excitation parameter matrices to handle sparsity and generalize to unobserved pairs.  
- Estimation and optimization: Frames parameter learning as a convex problem with nuclear norm regularization.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Develops a scalable hybrid proximal-gradient / conditional-gradient algorithm that handles nonnegativity and nuclear norm separately and has a proven convergence rate.  
- Theoretical analysis: Provides convergence analysis (Theorem 2) and an equivalence result (Theorem 1) that requires a hyperparameter ρ to exceed a problem-dependent threshold ρ*.  
- Empirical evaluation: Evaluated on synthetic and three real-world datasets across item recommendation and return-time prediction tasks, outperforming baselines like Poisson models, survival analysis, and tensor factorization.  
- Diagnostic analysis: Uses time-change theorem analysis to show Hawkes assumptions fit data better than alternatives.  
- Strength — originality/significance: Novel and principled integration of Hawkes processes with low-rank collaborative filtering addressing dynamic, time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Strength — technical quality: Elegant convex formulation and a tailored optimization algorithm with theoretical guarantees.  
- Strength — clarity: Well-written, well-structured paper with accessible background and clear explanations of model, optimization, and experiments.  
- Strength — empirical thoroughness: Comprehensive experiments (synthetic + diverse real datasets) that convincingly demonstrate performance gains.  
- Weakness — theoretical-practical gap: Theorem 1 depends on ρ > ρ*, but ρ* depends on the optimal solution, making practical selection of ρ unclear; more discussion or bounds/estimation for ρ* is needed.  
- Weakness — experimental baselines: Would be stronger with comparisons to established time-aware factorization models (e.g., timeSVD++) to better position the method.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept — significant, well-executed contribution with minor weaknesses that can be addressed in revision.
