- Summary: Proposes a point-process-based collaborative filtering model with base and self-exciting intensity terms parameterized by separate user/item latent vectors and shows empirical improvements.  
- Originality: Novel combination of point process theory with latent-factor collaborative filtering.  
- Significance: Well-motivated approach addressing temporal dynamics in recommender systems.  
- Baselines: Comparison set is weak—should include a strong ensemble baseline (e.g., STiC + matrix factorization) to handle cold-start and better demonstrate advantages.  
- Hyperparameter details: Crucial hyperparameter selection info is missing, hindering reproducibility and fairness of comparisons.  
- Kernel bandwidth (σ): Unclear how σ for the self-exciting kernel was chosen; a sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Latent dimension and other hyperparameters: No details on selection procedures for latent vector dimensionality for the proposed model or baselines.  
- Dataset characterization: Datasets lack temporal statistics (e.g., distribution of recurring events per user-item pair, irregularity metrics) to justify the temporal modeling claims.  
- Clarity: Paper contains typos and grammatical errors that reduce readability.  
- Overall assessment: Interesting and important idea but limited by weak experimental validation and missing methodological/dataset details; addressing these would substantially improve the work.
