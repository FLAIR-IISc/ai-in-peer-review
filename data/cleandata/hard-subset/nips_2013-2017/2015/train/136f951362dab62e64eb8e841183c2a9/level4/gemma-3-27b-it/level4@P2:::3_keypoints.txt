- Introduces a novel framework combining Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank factorization for time-sensitive recommendation.
- Models user activity as a Hawkes process and uses low-rank factors to scale to large user-item datasets.
- Provides an efficient optimization algorithm with provable convergence rate and a convex problem formulation.
- Tackles the important problem of predicting when users will interact (timing), not just what they prefer.
- Builds on prior temporal point process and matrix factorization work, extending methods to recommend new items.
- Demonstrates scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Shows strong empirical performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets, outperforming several baselines.
- Framework is general and can incorporate additional contextual information (profiles, text, etc.).
- Theoretical clarification needed around Equation (8) and Theorem 2 (which parameter ρ is maximized and practical relevance).
- Baseline comparison is incomplete; should include strong collaborative-filtering baselines like SVD++.
- Lacks analysis of parameter sensitivity (rank choice, triggering-kernel bandwidth) and its effect on performance.
- More implementation details (hardware, tuning strategies) are needed to improve reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: significant, well-motivated contribution with solid theory and experiments; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the above weaknesses.
