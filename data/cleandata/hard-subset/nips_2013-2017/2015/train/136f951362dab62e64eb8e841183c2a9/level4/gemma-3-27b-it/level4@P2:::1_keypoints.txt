- Novel framework combining self-exciting point processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Convex formulation with an efficient, scalable optimization algorithm.  
- Theoretical soundness with convergence analysis (Theorem 2).  
- Demonstrated scalability to datasets with millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Unified model addressing both recommendation and return-time prediction tasks.  
- Model is extensible to incorporate additional contextual information.  
- Weak initial motivation and lack of concrete examples for the importance of modeling time-varying preferences and return-time prediction.  
- Experimental baselines are insufficient—missing simple baselines for return-time prediction and unclear practical significance of improvements over SVD.  
- Predicting exact return times is overly ambitious; recommending discretized interval classification (short/medium/long) as a more tractable evaluation.  
- No data analysis provided to substantiate the claim of time-varying user preferences on real datasets.  
- Clarity issue: sudden, unrelated reference to “retweets and link creation” in Section 2 that needs removal or context.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically novel, scalable, and flexible approach with solid theoretical foundation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak motivation, inadequate baselines, overly difficult task formulation, and missing empirical support for key claims.  
- Overall assessment: promising work but currently a marginal accept; requires stronger motivation, clearer evaluation, and better data analysis.  
- Minor comments: fix typo ("abel" → "able") and clarify/remove confusing social-network example.
