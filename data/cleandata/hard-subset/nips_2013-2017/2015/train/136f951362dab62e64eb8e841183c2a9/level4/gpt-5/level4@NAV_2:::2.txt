-- Summary --
This paper introduces a point-process-based collaborative filtering approach to model temporal and recurrent user–item interactions. For each user–item pair, the event intensity is decomposed into a constant baseline component and a self-exciting term driven by past events. These two components are parameterized via user and item latent representations, and the authors derive an associated optimization procedure for parameter estimation. Empirical evaluations indicate advantages over commonly used alternatives. That said, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened. A particularly relevant additional baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD: the paper argues that methods like [12, 13] cannot recommend entirely new items, while SVD can, so comparing against this straightforward ensemble would be instructive. Moreover, for the triggering kernel gamma used in the self-exciting component, how is the bandwidth sigma selected, and how sensitive are the results to this choice? It would also help to detail how other important hyperparameters are chosen for both baselines and the proposed method (e.g., the dimensionality of the latent factors). Finally, since temporal structure is central here, adding more characterization of the two real-world datasets—such as the distribution of repeat events per user–item pair and indicators of temporal irregularity—would provide useful context.

-- Quality --
Technical quality is solid but not exceptional.

-- Clarity --
The presentation would benefit from language polishing; there are noticeable typos and grammatical issues.

-- Originality --
The method offers an appealing synthesis of temporal point processes and latent factor models.

-- Significance --
Both the problem setting and the proposed approach are practically meaningful. The paper presents a point-process collaborative filtering model with an accompanying optimization algorithm and empirical results showing improvements over several baselines. However, the experimental design and the clarity of exposition leave room for improvement.
