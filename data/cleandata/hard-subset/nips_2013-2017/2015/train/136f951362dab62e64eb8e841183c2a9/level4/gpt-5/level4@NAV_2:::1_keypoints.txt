- Paper proposes a time-sensitive recommendation framework that models temporally varying user preferences and predicts next return time using Hawkes processes linked with low-rank models and a convex nuclear-norm objective with nonnegativity.  
- Authors present an optimization algorithm combining proximal-gradient and conditional-gradient steps with O(1/t) convergence and show scalability on a large synthetic and two modest real datasets.  
- Reviewer asks for empirical substantiation that time-varying preferences are widespread by analyzing deployed datasets to quantify magnitude and nature of temporal variation (e.g., new popular items vs. recurrent patterns).  
- Reviewer requests practical motivation for next-return-time prediction: explain concrete web-service uses and which operational decisions would rely on these predictions.  
- Missing simple baselines for return-time prediction: last observed inter-event time and most frequent inter-event time per user/item should be included.  
- Suggests considering coarser, actionable prediction tasks (e.g., classifying next gap into short/medium/long/very-long) instead of exact times.  
- Experimental significance unclear: quantify whether improvement over SVD is large or modest and whether end users would notice; compare to strong baselines like frequently updated global-popularity recommendations.  
- Small comments: fix typo “abel” → “able”; rephrase Section 2’s abrupt shift to retweets/link-creation and add references.  
- Overall assessment: tackles two problems without fully motivating relevance/impact and omits several baselines and analyses; verdict OK/marginal.
