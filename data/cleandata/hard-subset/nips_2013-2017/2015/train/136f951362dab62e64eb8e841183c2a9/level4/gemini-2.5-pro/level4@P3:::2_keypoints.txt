- Proposes a point process-based collaborative filtering model that models each user-item event stream as a self-exciting Hawkes process.  
- Intensity decomposed into a constant baseline (long-term preference) plus a self-exciting term (short-term, history-dependent influence).  
- Assumes low-rank structure for baseline intensities (Λ0) and self-excitation magnitudes (A) to enable generalization to unobserved user-item pairs.  
- Learns latent factor representations for users and items for both long-term and short-term components.  
- Frames parameter estimation as a convex optimization problem with nuclear norm regularization to enforce low-rankness.  
- Introduces a novel optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods, with an O(1/t) convergence rate.  
- Demonstrates scalability—training time scales linearly with the size of the training set.  
- Evaluated on time-sensitive item recommendation and returning-time prediction, with superior empirical results on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Strength: novel and elegant synthesis of Hawkes processes and low-rank collaborative filtering for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Strength: valuable technical contribution in the optimization method with theoretical convergence analysis.  
- Strength: modeling power to capture bursty, self-reinforcing user activity while balancing long-term preferences.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons miss practical baselines—e.g., a hybrid ensemble of a temporal model (STiC) and SVD CF is not evaluated.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter selection (e.g., kernel bandwidth σ and latent rank) and no sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: dataset characterization is insufficient—missing stats on recurrence rates, inter-event times, and temporal regularity.  
- Weakness: presentation issues—grammatical errors and typos hinder readability and require proofreading.  
- Detailed comments: authors should discuss alternative kernels (e.g., power-law) and dataset-dependent kernel choice; also explain differing goodness-of-fit across datasets (e.g., last.fm vs MIMIC II).  
- Overall: technically solid, original, and significant work with addressable experimental and presentation weaknesses; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
