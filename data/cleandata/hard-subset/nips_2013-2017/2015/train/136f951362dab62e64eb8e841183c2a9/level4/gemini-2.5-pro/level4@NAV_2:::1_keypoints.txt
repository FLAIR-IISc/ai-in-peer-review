- Proposes modeling user-item interaction sequences as self-exciting Hawkes processes with low-rank base intensity and excitation matrices to handle sparsity and generalize across pairs.  
- Casts parameter estimation as a convex optimization problem with nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Introduces a scalable algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods to handle non-negativity and nuclear-norm constraints.  
- Provides theoretical convergence analysis for the algorithm, showing an O(1/t) rate.  
- Evaluates the method on synthetic and real-world datasets for time-aware item recommendation and next-interaction time prediction.  
- Strength: novel connection between temporal point processes and low-rank models that captures long-term preferences and short-term excitation.  
- Strength: technically sound formulation grounded in temporal point process theory and convex optimization.  
- Strength: solid algorithmic contribution with practical scalability and rigorous convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: paper is generally well-written and clearly presents motivation, model, and algorithm.  
- Weakness: motivation for predicting exact return times is weak; practical use cases and alternatives (e.g., interval prediction) are not well justified.  
- Weakness: limited baselines for return-time prediction—missing simple heuristics like recent or modal inter-event delay.  
- Weakness: recommendation evaluation lacks practical baselines (e.g., recent popularity) and it’s unclear if reported rank improvements matter to end users.  
- Weakness: missing dataset analysis to empirically substantiate the claim that user preferences are time-varying (e.g., time-of-day or day-of-week patterns).  
- Overall: novel and technically strong approach, but needs stronger practical motivation and broader experimental comparisons to better demonstrate impact.
