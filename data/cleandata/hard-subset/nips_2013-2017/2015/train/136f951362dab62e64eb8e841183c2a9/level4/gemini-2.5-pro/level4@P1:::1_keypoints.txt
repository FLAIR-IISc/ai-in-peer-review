- Proposes a novel unified model connecting Hawkes self-exciting point processes with low-rank matrix models to capture long-term preferences and short-term temporal dynamics.  
- Targets two tasks: recommend the most relevant item at a given moment and predict a user's next return time.  
- Casts parameter estimation as a convex optimization problem, enabling global-optimum guarantees.  
- Develops an efficient optimization algorithm blending proximal and conditional gradient methods with theoretical convergence rates.  
- Demonstrates scalability on a large synthetic dataset and predictive performance on three real-world datasets.  
- Strength: the model is a principled and original fusion of temporal point processes and low-rank recommendation models.  
- Strength: convex formulation is a clear advantage for reliable learning.  
- Strength: algorithm is technically solid, efficient, and supported by convergence analysis.  
- Weakness: motivation and problem framing are weak — lacks data-driven analysis showing prevalence and nature of time-varying preferences in the used datasets.  
- Weakness: practical value of predicting exact continuous return times is not well justified or linked to concrete applications.  
- Weakness: return-time evaluation omits simple but important baselines (e.g., last-delay or modal-delay heuristics).  
- Weakness: could improve by offering a simpler, more practical task formulation (e.g., classifying return intervals: short/medium/long).  
- Weakness: recommendation improvements lack contextualization of practical significance and user-facing impact.  
- Weakness: missing comparison to a strong time-aware dynamic popularity baseline (e.g., daily/weekly recalculated popularity).  
- Overall conclusion: technically interesting and borderline publishable, but needs stronger motivation, simpler and stronger baselines, and clearer discussion of practical impact.
