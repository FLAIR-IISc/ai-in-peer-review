- Proposes a novel approach for time-sensitive recommendations and predicting users' next returning time using an efficient optimization algorithm.  
- Experiments include one synthetic large-scale dataset and two modest real-world datasets.  
- Needs verification that time-varying user preferences are prevalent in the real datasets; analyze causes (e.g., new popular content).  
- Provide clear motivation and practical use-cases for predicting next returning time and its benefits to web companies.  
- Missing simple baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last time delay, most common delay).  
- Predicting exact return time may be unrealistic; suggest predicting delay categories (short/medium/long/very long).  
- Experimental improvements over SVD lack statistical/significance analysis and clarity on practical impact.  
- Should compare recommendations to a daily-updated popular-items baseline for a more complete evaluation.  
- Minor issues: typo “abel”→“able” on page 3; abrupt, reference-less introduction of retweets/link creation in section 2—rephrase and add citations.  
- Overall assessment: tackles two important problems but lacks motivation, impact analysis, and necessary baselines; acceptable but marginal.
