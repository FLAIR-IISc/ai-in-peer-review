- Introduces a novel time-sensitive recommendation algorithm combining low-rank matrix factorization with a Hawkes process.  
- Principled, theoretically grounded approach for modeling temporal dynamics in recommendations.  
- Concern: Equation (8) maximization is not clearly specified with respect to which parameter(s).  
- Risk that ρ* could be unbounded if maximization is over ρ, with unclear implications.  
- Theorem 2 justifies a choice of ρ, but that choice is not used in the experiments, creating a theory–experiment disconnect.  
- Experimental evaluation is strong and scalable/distributed, but could be strengthened with stronger baselines (notably SVD++).  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and mathematically well-explained.  
- Work appears novel by integrating Hawkes processes with low-rank factorization for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- High practical significance due to applicability to e-commerce, news, social media, and scalable distributed computation.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper worthy of acceptance if theoretical clarifications (Eq. 8 and Theorem 2) and broader baseline comparisons (e.g., SVD++) are addressed.
