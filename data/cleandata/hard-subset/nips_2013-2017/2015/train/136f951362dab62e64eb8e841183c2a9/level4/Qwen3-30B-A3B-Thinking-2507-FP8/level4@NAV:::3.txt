

Quality: The paper proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation by leveraging low-rank structure in user-item matrices and Hawkes processes. The approach is amenable to distributed computation and outperforms existing methods. The work demonstrates a theoretically sound approach to modeling ratings via stochastic processes. However, two points warrant clarification:  
(1) In Equation (8) and Theorem 2, the parameter over which maximization occurs is unclear—specifically, is it over $\rho$? If so, could $\rho^*$ be unbounded? Additionally, Theorem 2 is not applied in the experimental setup, diminishing its perceived relevance.  
(2) The baseline comparisons omit SVD++ [17], a top-performing method for time-sensitive recommendation (notably the Netflix Challenge winner). Including SVD++ would substantially strengthen the experimental evaluation, though the current baselines are acceptable.  

Clarity: The paper is well-written and logically structured.  
Originality: The problem of time-sensitive recommendation is sufficiently novel, and the proposed methodology represents a meaningful contribution.  
Significance: Time-sensitive recommendation holds substantial potential impact, and this work advances the field through its algorithmic innovation.
