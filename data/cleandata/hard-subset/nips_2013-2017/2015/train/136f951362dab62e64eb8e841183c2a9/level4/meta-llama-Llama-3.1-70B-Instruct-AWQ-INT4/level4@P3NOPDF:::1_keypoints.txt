- Proposes a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations that models time-varying user preferences and predicts users' next returning time.
- Uses an efficient optimization algorithm and shows effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Claim that time-varying preferences are widespread needs stronger support via analysis of the real datasets used.
- Recommend investigating causes of time-varying preferences (e.g., new popular items or other temporal patterns).
- Motivation for predicting next returning time is unclear; authors should justify the task and its applications in a web-company context.
- Suggest demonstrating how returning-time predictions would be used and what business benefits they bring.
- Missing baselines for returning-time prediction; add simple baselines like last time delay and most frequent time delay.
- Predicting exact returning time may be too hard; consider predicting delay categories (short/medium/long/very long).
- Experimental results lack context on significance of gains over SVD—clarify magnitude and practical impact for users.
- Recommend adding comparison to popular item–based recommendation baseline, which is often hard to beat.
- Minor issues: typo "abel"→"able" and confusing, reference-less introduction of retweets/link creation in section 2 that needs rephrasing.
- Overall: addresses relevant problems but lacks clear motivation, necessary analyses, and baselines; paper is acceptable but marginal.
