- Proposes a novel framework connecting self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Addresses two tasks: recommending the most relevant item at the right moment and predicting users' next returning time.
- Builds on Kapoor et al. but extends to item recommendation for new items, formulating a convex optimization with theoretical guarantees.
- Presents an efficient algorithm combining proximal-gradient and conditional-gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence.
- Strong technical foundation in temporal point processes and low-rank matrix optimization.
- Experiments show scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events on synthetic data, plus evaluations on Last.fm and Tmall.
- Outperforms baselines (Poisson processes, STiC, SVD, tensor factorization) on recommendation and return-time tasks.
- Strength: theoretically sound approach with convergence guarantees.
- Strength: demonstrated scalability to large datasets.
- Strength: clear connection between temporal point processes and recommendation systems.
- Strength: good experimental design with appropriate baselines for recommendation.
- Weakness: significance not well established—no analysis showing real datasets exhibit meaningful time-varying preferences.
- Weakness: unclear practical motivation for predicting exact return times and how such fine-grained predictions would be used in practice.
- Weakness: lacks simple but important baselines for return-time prediction (last interval, most frequent interval, median).
- Weakness: predicting exact return time may be less practical than classification into actionable time buckets.
- Weakness: unclear magnitude and practical impact of improvements over SVD; should compare to popular-item (recomputed daily) baseline.
- Minor issues: a typo ("abel") and an abrupt, unreferenced mention of "retweets and link creation."
- NIPS criteria: Quality—strong theory but missing real-world temporal analysis and proper baselines; Clarity—well written but some motivation gaps; Originality—novel connection but could compare to more relevant baselines; Significance—limited by unclear practical motivation and lack of evidence.
- Overall: technically solid and scalable but needs stronger empirical justification of temporal dynamics, clearer practical use cases for return-time prediction, and additional baselines to establish significance.
