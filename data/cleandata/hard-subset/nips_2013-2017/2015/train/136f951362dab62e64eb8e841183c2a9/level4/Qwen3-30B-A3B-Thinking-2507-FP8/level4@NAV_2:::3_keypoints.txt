- Introduces a novel time-sensitive recommendation framework combining self-exciting (Hawkes) point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent user-item temporal patterns.  
- Presents a convex optimization formulation and a new algorithm with O(1/t) convergence that scales to large datasets.  
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance in item recommendation and return-time prediction tasks.  
- Theoretical clarity issue: Eq. (8) and Theorem 2 do not state over which parameter the maximum is taken (likely ρ) and whether ρ* can be unbounded.  
- Practical relevance concern: Theorem 2’s result appears unused in the experimental implementation, reducing its practical impact.  
- Baselines: Evaluation should include SVD++, a strong time-sensitive recommendation method, to strengthen comparisons.  
- Clarity: Manuscript is well-organized and clearly written with sufficient technical detail for reproduction.  
- Originality: Novel connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank recommendation models, addressing an underexplored problem.  
- Significance: Potentially high real-world impact for improving engagement and enabling proactive interventions, with promising scalability.
