- Proposes a novel model for time-sensitive recommendations that captures time-varying user preferences and jointly predicts next-interaction time, with an efficient optimization algorithm and evaluations on one large synthetic and two real datasets.
- Core claim that user preferences are significantly time-varying is asserted but not empirically validated on the real datasets.
- Unclear motivation and practical relevance for predicting exact user return time; applications (e.g., load management, marketing, churn detection) are not explicitly discussed.
- Experimental evaluation omits crucial baselines for return-time prediction, such as last-observed delay or per-user most frequent delay.
- Recommendation experiments lack a strong time-aware popularity baseline (e.g., most popular items in recent window), which is often hard to beat in practice.
- Reported performance gains over SVD are not contextualized; practical significance and user-perceived improvement are unclear.
- Suggests reframing return-time prediction as a classification problem into delay bins (short/medium/long/very long) as a more practical alternative.
- Manuscript has a typo (“abel” → “able”) and contains abrupt, uncited mentions of retweets/link creation that need rephrasing or context.
- Overall assessment: interesting technical approach but insufficiently motivated and experimentally weak; paper feels preliminary and is marginal for publication without addressing motivation, baselines, and evaluation.
