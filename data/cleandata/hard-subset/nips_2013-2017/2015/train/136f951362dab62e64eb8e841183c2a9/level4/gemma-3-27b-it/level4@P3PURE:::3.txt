This paper presents a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation, leveraging low-rank structure in the user-item matrix and employing a Hawkes process. The algorithm is amenable to distributed solutions and demonstrates improved performance compared to existing methods.

**Quality:** Overall, the paper takes a principled approach to modeling observed ratings using stochastic processes. However, I have a few points for consideration.

Regarding the theoretical contribution, specifically Equation (8) and Theorem 2, it is unclear over which parameter the maximization is performed in Eq. (8). If the maximization is over ρ, can ρ* be unbounded? Furthermore, Theorem 2 appears unused in the paper’s experimental selection of ρ, which diminishes its practical relevance.

A suggestion for improving the experimental evaluation is to include SVD++ [17], a strong and well-established algorithm (and winner of the Netflix challenge), as a baseline. Given its performance in collaborative filtering tasks, particularly those involving temporal dynamics, its inclusion would strengthen the comparison. While I do not find the current baseline selection inadequate, adding SVD++ would further enhance the evaluation.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

**Originality:** The work appears novel, motivated by the relative novelty of the time-sensitive recommendation problem itself.

**Significance:** Time-sensitive recommendation is a potentially impactful area. This paper presents a novel algorithm to tackle time-sensitive recommendation by capturing low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and by using Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed fashion and achieves better results than existing methods.
