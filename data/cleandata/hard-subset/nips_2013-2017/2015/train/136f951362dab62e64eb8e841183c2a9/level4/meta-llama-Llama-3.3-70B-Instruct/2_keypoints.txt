- Proposes a novel point-process collaborative filtering model combining a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity to capture temporal and recurrent user–item interactions.  
- Computes two sets of intensities via separate user and item latent vectors and derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation.  
- Empirical results show the proposed method outperforms commonly used alternatives.  
- Experiments should include a simple STiC+SVD ensemble baseline, since SVD can recommend new items that some methods cannot.  
- Investigate sensitivity to the bandwidth parameter σ in the γ function used for the self-exciting component.  
- The process for selecting key hyperparameters (e.g., latent dimension) for both baselines and the proposed method is not clearly described.  
- Provide more detailed dataset characteristics (e.g., number of recurring events per user/item pair, temporal irregularity) given the temporal focus.  
- Overall quality is satisfactory but could be improved.  
- Presentation suffers from language, typo, and grammar issues that reduce clarity.  
- Originality is good: interesting combination of point process and latent-factor models.  
- The problem and method are significant with practical value, and empirical gains indicate potential impact with further improvements.
