- Introduces a point-process-based collaborative filtering model decomposing intensity into baseline and self-exciting terms parameterized by user/item latent factors.  
- Authors derive an optimization procedure for parameter estimation.  
- Empirical results show advantages over common baselines but the experimental evaluation is weak.  
- Missing baseline: a simple ensemble of STiC and SVD should be compared since it could recommend new items while capturing recurrence.  
- Unclear how the triggering kernel bandwidth sigma is selected and how sensitive results are to this choice.  
- Need clearer description of how key hyperparameters (e.g., latent dimensionality) are chosen for both proposed method and baselines.  
- Datasets require more characterization (e.g., distribution of repeat events per user–item pair and measures of temporal irregularity).  
- Technical quality is solid but not exceptional.  
- Clarity needs improvement: language has typos and grammatical issues.  
- Originality: appealing synthesis of temporal point processes and latent factor models.  
- Significance: practically meaningful problem and approach, but experimental design and exposition need strengthening.
