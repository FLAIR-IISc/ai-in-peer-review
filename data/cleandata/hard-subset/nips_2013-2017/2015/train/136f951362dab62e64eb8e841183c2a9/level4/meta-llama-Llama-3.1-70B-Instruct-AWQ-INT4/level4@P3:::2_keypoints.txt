- Proposes a novel low-rank self-exciting (Hawkes) point-process framework for time-sensitive recommendation capturing recurrent temporal patterns.
- Provides a convex formulation and an efficient learning algorithm (proximal + conditional gradient) that scales to millions of pairs and hundreds of millions of events with O(1/t) convergence.
- Model generalizes to include contextual information (user profiles, item content) and predicts both next item and next return time.
- Experimental results claim superiority over state-of-the-art on synthetic and real datasets.
- Strength: original combination of point processes and low-rank models for recommendation.
- Strength: convex formulation and scalable, theoretically grounded optimization algorithm.
- Strength: empirical performance improvements demonstrated.
- Weakness: experimental comparisons lack additional baselines (e.g., ensemble of STiC and SVD) for completeness.
- Weakness: missing details on selection and sensitivity of the bandwidth parameter σ for the self-exciting process.
- Weakness: insufficient description of real-world dataset characteristics (recurrence counts, temporal irregularity).
- Presentation issues: some typos, grammar, and language errors to fix.
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing experimental gaps, σ details, dataset descriptions, and presentation fixes.
