-- Summary --
This paper introduces a point-process-based collaborative filtering model to capture temporal and recurrent interactions between users and items. For each user–item pair, the event intensity is modeled as the sum of a constant baseline rate and a self-exciting component that depends on past events. These components are parameterized via separate user and item latent vectors. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and present empirical studies suggesting that the method outperforms commonly used alternatives.

The experimental evaluation could be strengthened. A natural additional baseline is a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Since the authors argue that [12,13] cannot recommend entirely new items (whereas SVD can), comparing against this straightforward hybrid would clarify whether the proposed method offers advantages beyond such a combination. Moreover, for the kernel γ used in the self-exciting term, please specify how the bandwidth σ is selected and report the sensitivity of performance to σ. More generally, important hyperparameters for both the baselines and the proposed method (e.g., the latent dimensionality) are not described; details on the tuning protocol and chosen values, along with a sensitivity analysis, would improve credibility and reproducibility.

Given the temporal focus, more information about the two real-world datasets would be helpful. Simple statistics such as the number of repeat events per user–item pair and measures of temporal irregularity (e.g., distributions of inter-event times) would contextualize the benefits of modeling recurrence and timing.

-- Quality --
The technical contribution appears sound and the optimization is reasonably derived, but the evaluation design and reporting of hyperparameters are incomplete. Addressing these issues would substantially improve the paper.

-- Clarity --
The presentation can be improved. There are noticeable language issues (grammar, typos), and some definitions (e.g., how the two intensity terms relate to the latent vectors) could be stated more clearly. A concise description of the algorithmic steps would also aid readability.

-- Originality --
The work offers an interesting synthesis of temporal point processes with latent factor models for recommendation.

-- Significance --
The problem is important and the approach has practical value. With a stronger experimental setup, clearer hyperparameter reporting, and richer dataset characterization, the paper could make a meaningful contribution.
