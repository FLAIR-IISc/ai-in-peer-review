- Strength: Addresses temporal dynamics in user preferences, an important challenge for recommendation systems.
- Strength: Includes next-return-time prediction component that could be operationally useful.
- Strength: Proposes an efficient learning algorithm and demonstrates scalability on synthetic data.

- Major: Does not quantify prevalence or magnitude of temporal drift in real datasets; unclear if drift is global or user-specific.
- Major: Insufficient motivation for return-time prediction—needs concrete use cases (e.g., notifications, resource allocation, session ranking).
- Major: Missing simple baselines for return-time (e.g., last inter-arrival time, modal inter-arrival) to contextualize gains.
- Major: Exact return-time prediction may be brittle; suggest coarser classification into delay bins (short/medium/long/very long).
- Major: Recommendation results lack absolute metrics, relative improvements, statistical significance, and a strong popularity baseline (recomputed daily/session-aware).

- Scope: Paper mixes two problems (time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction) without a unifying motivation or clear interaction; consider tightening focus or explaining synergy.

- Experimental setup: Real datasets are modest; discuss transferability to larger/noisier settings and provide runtime/complexity analysis on real data.

- Clarity: Abrupt context shift introducing retweets and link creation without citations or motivation; please add references and relevance.
- Clarity: Minor typo: “and is abel to capture” → “and is able to capture.”

- Overall: Interesting direction with efficient algorithm, but lacks clear motivation for return-time, omits straightforward baselines, and needs deeper analysis of temporal drift; currently borderline/OK.
