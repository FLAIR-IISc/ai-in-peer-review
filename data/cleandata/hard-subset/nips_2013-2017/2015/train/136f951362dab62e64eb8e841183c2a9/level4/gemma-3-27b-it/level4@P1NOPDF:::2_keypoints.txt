- Proposes a point-process collaborative filtering model combining constant and self-exciting intensities to capture temporal and recurrent user–item interactions.
- Computes intensities using separate latent user and item vectors and derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation.
- Empirical results claim advantages over common alternatives but the experimental evaluation is insufficiently thorough.
- Recommend including a baseline ensemble of STiC and SVD, since STiC struggles with new items and SVD could complement it.
- Unclear specification of the γ function’s bandwidth σ for the self-exciting term; need to state how σ is set and report sensitivity to it.
- Missing details on selection and tuning of important hyperparameters (e.g., latent vector dimensionality) for both proposed and baseline methods.
- Paper should report more dataset statistics relevant to temporal structure (e.g., number of recurring events per user/item pair, temporal irregularity).
- Overall quality is reasonably good but not exceptional.
- Presentation needs improvement—language, typos, and grammatical errors should be fixed.
- The approach is an interesting and original combination of point processes and latent factor models with practical significance, though experiments and presentation require strengthening.
