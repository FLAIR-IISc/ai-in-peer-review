- Proposes a novel framework linking self-exciting point processes with low-rank matrix models for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Targets two problems: recommending the most relevant item at the optimal moment and predicting next user return time.  
- Extends prior temporal modeling (e.g., Kapoor et al.) to generalize return-time models for item recommendation.  
- Introduces a convex formulation (Eq.4) and an algorithm combining proximal and conditional gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence (Theorem 2).  
- Theorem 1 shows the approximate formulation (Eq.5) is equivalent when ρ > ρ*, but ρ* depends on unknown optimal parameters, making selection ad hoc.  
- Theorem 2’s guidance for ρ is not used in experiments, weakening the connection between theory and empirical work.  
- Algorithm is scalable and experimentally shown to handle millions of user-item pairs and large event volumes.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) demonstrate superior predictive performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and provides precise, implementable algorithm descriptions.  
- Original contribution: unified framework that jointly addresses item recommendation and return-time prediction and can incorporate contextual features.  
- Baseline comparisons are reasonable but incomplete—missing important methods like SVD++.  
- Strengths: novelty, rigorous theoretical analysis, scalable algorithm, comprehensive empirical evaluation, clear writing.  
- Weaknesses: reliance on an unknown ρ*, lack of experimental use of theoretical guidance, and incomplete baseline set.  
- Overall judgment: strong contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
