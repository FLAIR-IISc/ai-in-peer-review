This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. Specifically, for each pair of user and item, the event density is the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity depending on the past events. Two sets of intensity are computed by two sets of user and item latent vectors respectively. The corresponding optimization algorithm is derived for parameter estimation, and empirical studies have been conducted to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed method over other commonly used alternatives.

However, the experimental studies can be improved in several aspects. For instance, a sensible baseline to consider would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD, as the authors argue that [12, 13] are unable to recommend completely new items, a limitation that can be addressed by SVD. A comparison between the proposed method and this ensemble would be valuable. Additionally, the paper lacks details on how the bandwidth parameter σ for the γ function used in the self-exciting process is set, as well as an analysis of the sensitivity of the results to variations in σ. Furthermore, the authors do not provide information on how important hyperparameters, such as the dimension of the latent vectors, are chosen for both the baseline methods and the proposed method.

Providing more details about the characteristics of the two real-world datasets would also be helpful. Given the focus of the paper on temporal structure, it would be useful to discuss simple statistics, such as the number of recurring events per user/item pair and the irregularity of the temporal pattern.

The overall quality of the paper is reasonably good but not outstanding. The presentation can be improved in terms of language, with several typos and grammatical errors noted.

The proposed method represents an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models, demonstrating good originality.

The problem and method have good practical values, making the paper significant in its field. However, to fully realize its potential, improvements to the experimental studies and presentation are necessary.
