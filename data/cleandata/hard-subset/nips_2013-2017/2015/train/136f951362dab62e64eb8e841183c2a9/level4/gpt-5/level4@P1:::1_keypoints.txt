- Proposes time-sensitive recommendation using low-rank Hawkes processes modeling baseline preference + self-excitation per user–item pair.  
- Ties parameters across sparse pairs via low-rank structure enforced with nuclear-norm penalties.  
- Formulates convex parameter estimation with nonnegativity and nuclear-norm constraints.  
- Introduces an algorithm combining proximal gradient (nonnegativity block) and conditional gradient (nuclear-norm block) with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Proves equivalence between a penalized auxiliary-variable formulation and the original problem above a penalty threshold.  
- Supports two tasks: time-aware item ranking via intensity and next-return-time prediction via Ogata’s thinning sampling.  
- Empirical evaluation on one large synthetic dataset (scalability) and two modest real datasets; compares to Poisson, survival/HMM, SVD, and Poisson tensor factorization.  
- Reported superior performance on ranking and return-time prediction relative to the chosen baselines.  

Strengths:
- Hawkes processes well-motivated for capturing recency/self-excitation alongside persistent preference.  
- Principled low-rank transfer across pairs using convex nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Efficient optimization that cleanly separates constraints with theoretical convergence and practical subroutines.  
- Joint framework addressing both ranking and return-time prediction tasks.  
- Demonstrated scalability on a large synthetic setup.  

Weaknesses / Questions:
- Does not quantify prevalence of time-varying preferences in real datasets (seasonality, drift, self-excitation).  
- Needs ablation (e.g., α=0) to quantify benefit over Poisson baseline.  
- Weak motivation for practical uses of return-time prediction and lack of concrete operational objectives.  
- Missing simple, strong baselines for return-time (last inter-event, mode) and a coarser classification alternative.  
- Missing popularity-based recommendation baseline (e.g., daily refreshed popularity).  
- Real-data experiments are modest; larger real logs or detailed runtime/memory profiling are needed.  
- Occasional abrupt context shifts (e.g., social network examples without references) harm clarity.  

Quality:
- Modeling and optimization are technically sound with clear convexity and convergence derivations.  
- Empirical section needs stronger baselines, ablations, and clearer effect-size reporting.  

Clarity:
- Generally readable but needs tighter motivation for return-time prediction, smoother transitions, and reporting of effect sizes/confidence intervals.  

Originality:
- Novel linkage of low-rank recommendation with Hawkes processes in a convex framework and a useful proximal+conditional gradient algorithm.  

Significance:
- Potentially impactful if empirical gains over practical baselines (dynamic popularity, last/mode delay) are substantial and clearly reported.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel, principled framework combining Hawkes with low-rank structure.  
- Efficient algorithm with theoretical guarantees.  
- Addresses important time-sensitive recommendation tasks.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Insufficient analysis of temporal effects and motivation for return-time use cases.  
- Missing simple but strong baselines and ablation studies.  
- Unclear practical significance due to modest real-data experiments and limited effect-size reporting.
