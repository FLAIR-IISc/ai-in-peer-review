- Proposes a novel framework combining self-exciting (Hawkes) point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation.
- Targets two tasks: recommending the most desirable item at the right time and predicting users’ next return times.
- Introduces a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm with provable convergence guarantees.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic and large-scale real datasets.
- Overcomes limitations of prior work (e.g., Kapoor et al.) by enabling recommendations for new items.
- Theoretical contribution: links Hawkes processes and low-rank models and provides convergence analysis (Theorem 2).
- Concerns about theory: ambiguous definition of the parameter space for ρ* in Eq. (8) and whether ρ* can be unbounded.
- Practical-theory gap: authors did not appear to use Theorem 2’s guidance when selecting ρ in experiments.
- Modeling choice critique: uses an exponential kernel for triggering function without discussion of alternative kernels.
- Writing and organization are generally clear; motivation and notation are consistent.
- Presentation issues: Algorithm 1 needs more inline comments and clearer explanations of parameters and steps.
- Readability could be improved by adding pseudocode for the proximal operator and LMOψ.
- Originality: novel and generalizable approach that can incorporate contextual information (user profiles, text features).
- Significance: addresses an important, widely applicable problem and scales to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Potential impact: can improve engagement and support marketing/resource allocation via return-time predictions.
- Acceptance strengths: principled novelty, strong empirical results, scalability, provable guarantees, and practical relevance.
- Acceptance weaknesses: weak theoretical discussion around Theorem 2, insufficient kernel justification, missing stronger baseline comparisons (e.g., SVD++), and under-documented algorithm. 
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing theoretical clarifications and improving algorithmic exposition (score suggested 7/10).
