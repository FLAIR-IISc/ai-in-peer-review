- Introduces a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations using user activity and also tackles prediction of a user’s next returning time.  
- Proposes an efficient optimization algorithm for learning the model.  
- Experimental evaluation uses a large-scale synthetic dataset and two moderate real-world datasets.  
- Needs analysis to confirm prevalence of significant time-varying user preferences in the experimental datasets.  
- Should investigate causes of temporal variation (e.g., new popular items vs. other patterns).  
- Motivation for predicting next returning time is unclear; provide rationale and practical web-company use cases.  
- Overlooked simple baselines for returning-time prediction: predict next delay from last observed delay, and from the most frequent observed delay.  
- Predicting exact returning time may be overly ambitious; recommend classifying delays into categories (short/medium/long/very long).  
- Experimental results lack clarity on the magnitude of improvement over SVD—unclear if improvements are substantial or perceptible to users.  
- Must benchmark against a popular-item recommendation baseline (which can be strong with daily updates).  
- Typo: "and is abel to capture" should be "and is able to capture."  
- Section 2’s introduction of retweets and link creation is confusing and lacks references; rephrase and clarify context.  
- Overall: introduces two problems but insufficiently motivates them and misses several important baselines and analyses; paper is borderline acceptable.
