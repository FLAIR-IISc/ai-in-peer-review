This paper proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation by leveraging the low-rank structure in a user-item matrix and incorporating the Hawkes process. The algorithm can be solved in a distributed manner and outperforms existing methods.

Quality: Overall, I appreciate the principled approach taken by the authors to model the observations (ratings) using stochastic processes. However, I have a few concerns and suggestions for improvement.

One theoretical concern arises from Eq. (8) and Theorem 2. Specifically, it is unclear over which parameter the maximum is taken in Eq. (8). Is it over \rho? If so, can \rho* be unbounded? Furthermore, Theorem 2 is not utilized when selecting \rho in the experiments, which diminishes its significance.

Another comment pertains to the choice of baselines in the experiments. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17], the winner of the Netflix challenge, is one of the best algorithms for time-sensitive recommendation. Including SVD++ as a baseline would further enhance the quality of the experiments. Although I am generally satisfied with the current baselines, adding SVD++ would provide a more comprehensive comparison.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Originality: The paper appears to be novel, as time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively new problem.

Significance: I believe that time-sensitive recommendation has the potential to be impactful. The proposed algorithm, which captures the low-rank structure in a user-item matrix using the Hawkes process and can be solved in a distributed fashion, demonstrates better performance than existing methods.
