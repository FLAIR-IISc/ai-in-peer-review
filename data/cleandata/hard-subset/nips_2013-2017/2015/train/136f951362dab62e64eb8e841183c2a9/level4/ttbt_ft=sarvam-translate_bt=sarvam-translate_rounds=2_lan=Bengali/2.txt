-- Abstract -- This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture spatial and recurrent structure between users and items. Specifically, for each pair of users and items, the event density is a self-चलित intensity sum depending on a constant intensity and past events. Two sets of intensities are calculated through two sets of user and item latent vectors. The corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation has been developed, and empirical research has been conducted to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method compared to other commonly used alternatives.

However, empirical research can be improved. For example, another realistic basis could be a common ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Since the authors argue that [12, 13] are not able to recommend completely new items, which could be covered by SVD, and it would be worthwhile to compare the proposed method with this common ensemble. Furthermore, how is the \sigma bandwidth parameter \sigma set for the \gamma function used for the self-चलित process? How sensitive will the results be if we change \sigma? Moreover, there is no description of how other important hyperparameters are chosen for both the baseline method and the proposed method, such as latent vector dimensions.

Providing a more detailed description of the characteristics of two real-world datasets would also be helpful. Since this paper is about spatial structure, it is necessary to discuss general statistics such as the number of recurrent events per user/item pair and how irregular the spatial pattern is.

-- Quality -- The overall quality is reasonably good but not very good.
-- Clarity -- The presentation of the paper could be improved in terms of language, typography, and grammatical errors.
-- Originality --
The proposed method is an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models.
-- Significance --
Both the problem and the method have good practical value. This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture spatial and recurrent structure between users and items. The corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation has been developed, and empirical research has been conducted to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method compared to other commonly used alternatives. However, empirical research and presentation can be improved.
