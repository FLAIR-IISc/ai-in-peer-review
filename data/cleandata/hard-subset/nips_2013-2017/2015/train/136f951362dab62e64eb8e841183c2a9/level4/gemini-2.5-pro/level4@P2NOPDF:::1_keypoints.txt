- Proposes a new method for time-sensitive recommendations that models time-varying user preferences and predicts next user return time, with an efficient optimization algorithm and experiments on one synthetic and two modest real-world datasets.

- Key contribution claim: modeling time-varying preferences distinguishes the work from many existing approaches.

- Claims next-return-time prediction as an additional contribution but lacks clear practical motivation and application scenarios.

- Missing simple but important baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., predict last time delay or modal time delay) and for recommendation (popularity-based recommender recalculated frequently).

- Suggests reframing return-time prediction as a classification problem (short/medium/long windows) which may be more practical and actionable.

- Insufficient analysis of datasets to justify the central premise—no evidence that user preference variance is due to temporal dynamics vs. other factors (e.g., new popular items).

- Reported improvements over baselines (e.g., SVD) are not contextualized for practical significance or end-user impact; qualitative/user-centric metrics are needed.

- Experimental evaluation limited by dataset size and missing crucial comparisons, weakening claims about real-world value and scalability.

- Technical approach appears sound, but omissions in motivation, baselines, and data analysis make the paper marginal in its current form.

- Minor issues: typo ("abel" → "able") and a confusing, unreferenced transition in Section 2 discussing retweets and link creation.
