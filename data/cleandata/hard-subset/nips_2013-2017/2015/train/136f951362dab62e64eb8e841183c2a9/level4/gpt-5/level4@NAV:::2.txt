Review-
-- Summary --
This paper presents a point-process collaborative filtering model to capture temporal and recurrent interactions between users and items. Concretely, for each user–item pair, the event intensity is modeled as the sum of a constant base intensity and a self-exciting component that depends on past events. Each component is computed using its own set of user and item latent vectors. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and provide empirical results indicating advantages over commonly used baselines.
However, the experimental evaluation can be strengthened. A natural additional baseline is a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Since the authors claim that [12, 13] cannot recommend entirely new items—whereas SVD can—comparing against this ensemble would be informative. Moreover, for the gamma function used in the self-exciting process, how is the bandwidth parameter sigma chosen, and how sensitive are the results to this choice? In addition, there are no details on how other key hyperparameters are selected for both the baselines and the proposed approach (e.g., the dimensionality of the latent vectors).
It would also be useful to include more characteristics of the two real-world datasets. Given the focus on temporal structure, simple statistics such as the number of repeat events per user–item pair and the irregularity of temporal patterns would be informative.

-- Quality --
The overall quality is solid but not outstanding.

-- Clarity --
The presentation needs polishing; there are issues with wording, typos, and grammatical errors.

-- Originality --
The method is an appealing synthesis of point processes and latent factor models.

-- Significance --
Both the problem and the approach are practically relevant. The paper introduces a point-process collaborative filtering model for temporal and recurrent user–item dynamics, derives an optimization algorithm for parameter learning, and reports empirical gains over standard alternatives. Nonetheless, the experiments and the presentation could be improved.
