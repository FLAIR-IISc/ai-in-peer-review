- Novel method for time-sensitive recommendations and next-returning-time prediction, using an efficient optimization algorithm and evaluated on one synthetic and two real datasets.
- Real datasets should be analyzed to substantiate the claim that time-varying user preferences are widespread.
- If time-variation is prevalent, investigate whether it is driven by new popular content or other underlying patterns.
- Motivation for predicting a user's next returning time is unclear and needs stronger justification.
- Explain how web companies could use returning-time predictions and what benefits they provide.
- Simple baselines for returning-time (e.g., last time delay, most frequent delay) were not evaluated.
- Predicting exact returning times may be overly ambitious; consider predicting delay categories (short/medium/long/very long).
- Experimental results lack clarity on the magnitude of improvement over SVD and whether gains are user-noticeable.
- Include comparison to a popular-item recommendation baseline, since popular items are a strong and evolving baseline.
- Minor issues: typo on page 3 ("abel" → "able") and section 2 abruptly introduces retweets/link creation without context or references.
- Overall assessment: addresses two relevant problems but lacks clear motivation, impact analysis, and several important baselines; generally acceptable but marginal.
