

Summary: This paper introduces a point process-based collaborative filtering model designed to capture temporal and recurrent interaction patterns between users and items. The event density for each user-item pair combines a constant intensity with a self-exciting intensity derived from historical events, computed via separate user and item latent vector sets. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and present empirical results demonstrating the method's superiority over common alternatives. However, the experimental evaluation requires enhancement. Specifically, a more appropriate baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD, as the authors note STiC [12, 13] cannot recommend entirely new items (a capability covered by SVD). Comparing against this ensemble would be valuable. Additionally, the paper does not clarify how the bandwidth parameter σ for the γ function is selected, nor does it address the sensitivity of results to σ variations. Crucially, details on hyperparameter selection for both baselines and the proposed method—such as latent vector dimensions—are missing. Furthermore, the description of the real-world datasets lacks key temporal characteristics, including recurrence frequency per user-item pair and measures of temporal irregularity, which are essential given the paper's focus on temporal dynamics.  
Quality: The overall quality is acceptable but not outstanding.  
Clarity: The paper requires improvement in language, grammar, and typographical accuracy.  
Originality: The integration of point processes with latent factor models represents a novel and interesting approach.  
Significance: The problem addressed and the proposed methodology hold practical relevance. The experimental section, however, requires substantial revision to strengthen the claims.
