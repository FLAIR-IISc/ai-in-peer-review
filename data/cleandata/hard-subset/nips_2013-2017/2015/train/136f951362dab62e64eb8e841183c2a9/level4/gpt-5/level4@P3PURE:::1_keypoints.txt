- Proposes a new method for time-sensitive recommendations that explicitly models time-varying user preferences and predicts next user return time using an efficient optimization algorithm.  
- Experiments use one synthetic dataset (to show scalability) and two modest-sized real datasets.  
- Reviewer questions whether time-varying user preferences are sufficiently widespread and asks for dataset analyses to substantiate this claim and identify dominant variation patterns (e.g., new popular songs).  
- Requests clear motivation and practical use cases for return-time prediction (how a web company would use it concretely).  
- Notes missing simple baselines for return-time prediction: last inter-visit time and most frequent inter-visit time.  
- Suggests reframing return-time prediction as an easier classification task (short/medium/long/very long delays) rather than predicting exact times.  
- Finds it hard to judge experimental significance versus SVD—unclear whether improvements are modest or substantial and whether users would perceive the difference.  
- Flags missing popular-items baseline (especially recalculated daily popularity), which is often hard to beat.  
- Points out presentation issues: abrupt context shift mentioning retweets/link creation without citations and needs rephrasing.  
- Notes a typo: “and is abel to capture” → “and is able to capture.”  
- Overall assessment: tackles two problems without clear motivation or demonstrated practical impact, missing key baselines and analyses; paper is OK/marginal.
