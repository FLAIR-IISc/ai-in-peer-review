- Paper proposes a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations, a model for predicting users’ next return time, and an efficient optimization algorithm, evaluated on one synthetic and two small real-world datasets.  
- The central claim that user preferences vary substantially over time lacks empirical justification and needs dataset analysis to quantify preference change.  
- The paper should analyze why preferences change (e.g., new popular items vs. other patterns) rather than assuming time-variation.  
- The practical motivation for predicting a user’s return time is unclear; the paper must articulate concrete applications and business value.  
- Predicting exact return times is overly ambitious; recommend reframing as classification into discrete return-time intervals (e.g., 1–5, 5–14, 14–33, >33 days).  
- Simple baselines for return-time prediction are missing, specifically using the user’s last observed delay and the user’s most frequent delay.  
- Experimental improvements over SVD are not quantified for practical significance; clarify whether gains are substantial or marginal and perceptible to users.  
- A “recommend popular items” baseline (e.g., daily-updated popularity) is essential and currently missing, as it’s often a strong practical benchmark.  
- Minor issues: fix the typo “abel”→“able,” add citations or remove the retweet/link-creation discussion, and better motivate why solving both recommendation timing and return prediction matters.  
- Overall assessment: paper is marginally acceptable but needs stronger motivation, additional baselines, clearer task framing, dataset analyses, and clearer result interpretation.
