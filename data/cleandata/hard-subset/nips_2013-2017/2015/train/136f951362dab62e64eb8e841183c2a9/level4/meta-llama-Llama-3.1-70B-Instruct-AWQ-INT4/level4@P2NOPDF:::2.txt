**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel point process collaborative filtering model that captures the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. Specifically, the event density for each user-item pair is modeled as the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity, which depends on past events. Two sets of intensity are computed using two sets of user and item latent vectors, respectively. The authors derive a corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and conduct empirical studies to demonstrate the advantages of their proposed method over commonly used alternatives.

However, the experimental studies can be improved in several ways. For instance, a sensible baseline for comparison would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD, as the authors argue that [12, 13] are unable to recommend completely new items, which can be addressed by SVD. It would be valuable to compare the proposed method to this ensemble. Additionally, the authors should provide more details on the choice of the bandwidth parameter σ for the γ function used in the self-exciting process. Specifically, they should discuss how the results would be affected by varying σ. Furthermore, the authors should provide more information on the choice of important hyperparameters, such as the dimension of the latent vectors, for both the baseline methods and the proposed method.

It would also be helpful to provide more details about the characteristics of the two real-world datasets used in the study. Given the focus on temporal structure, simple statistics such as the number of recurring events per user-item pair and the irregularity of the temporal pattern would be useful to discuss.

**Quality**

The overall quality of the paper is reasonably good but not outstanding. While the authors present a novel and interesting approach, the experimental studies and presentation can be improved.

**Clarity**

The paper's presentation can be improved in terms of language, typos, and grammar errors. The authors should proofread the paper carefully to ensure that it is free of errors and easy to follow.

**Originality**

The proposed method is an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field by proposing a novel approach that captures the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items.

**Significance**

Both the problem and the method have good practical values. The proposed point process collaborative filtering model has the potential to improve recommendation systems by capturing the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. The corresponding optimization algorithm and empirical studies demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method over commonly used alternatives. However, the experimental studies and presentation can be improved to make the paper more impactful.
