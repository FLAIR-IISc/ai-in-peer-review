- Proposes a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations that models time-varying user preferences and predicts next return time, trained with an efficient optimization algorithm and evaluated on one synthetic and two real datasets.
- Doubts whether time-varying user preferences are common; recommends analyzing the real datasets to demonstrate the prevalence and drivers (e.g., new popular content) of preference shifts.
- Finds the motivation for predicting a user's next return time unclear and asks for a concrete rationale and practical use cases for web companies.
- Notes missing simple baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last delay, most frequent delay) and suggests classifying delays into coarse buckets (short/medium/long/very long) instead of exact times.
- Questions the practical significance of improvements over SVD—unclear whether gains are substantial or perceptible to users—and requests comparison to a popular-items baseline.
- Minor issues: fix typo (“is abel” → “is able”) and rephrase the abrupt, unreferenced introduction of retweets and link creation in Section 2.
- Overall judgment: tackles two problems but lacks sufficient motivation, baselines, and analyses; paper is acceptable but marginal.
