This paper proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation by modeling user–item interactions with a low-rank structure and incorporating Hawkes processes. The method supports distributed optimization and empirically outperforms existing approaches.

Quality: Overall, the paper takes a principled approach to modeling the observed ratings using stochastic processes. However, I have a few concerns about the theory. In Eq. (8), over which parameter is the maximum taken? Is it over ρ? If so, can ρ* be unbounded? In addition, Theorem 2 does not appear to be used to choose ρ in the experiments, which reduces the practical importance of the theorem.

Regarding the experimental evaluation, the baselines could be stronger. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17] (the winner of the Netflix Prize) is among the best algorithms for time-sensitive recommendation. Including SVD++ as a baseline would further strengthen the empirical comparison. That said, I am not dissatisfied with the current set of baselines.

Clarity: The paper is well written and easy to follow.

Originality: The work appears novel to me, as time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively new problem and the combination of low-rank modeling with Hawkes processes is interesting.

Significance: Time-sensitive recommendation is potentially impactful, and the proposed algorithm is promising. It leverages low-rank structure and Hawkes processes, can be solved in a distributed manner, and demonstrates improved performance over existing methods.
