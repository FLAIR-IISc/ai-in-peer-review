- Proposes a novel, distributed algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation using low-rank user-item structure and Hawkes processes, reporting better results than existing methods.  
- Presents a principled stochastic-process-based model of user behavior.  
- Theoretical concerns: Equation (8) and Theorem 2 are ambiguous about which parameter is maximized; if over ρ, ρ* may be unbounded.  
- Theorem 2 is not used to select ρ in experiments, reducing its practical relevance.  
- Evaluation is limited and should include comparisons to strong baselines like SVD++.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, improved empirical results, and principled modeling.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear theoretical treatment (Eq. 8/Theorem 2) and insufficient empirical comparison.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Originality: addresses a relatively new and important problem with a novel approach.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for applications with time-dependent user behavior.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify theory (Eq. 8/Theorem 2) and expand empirical comparisons (e.g., SVD++).
