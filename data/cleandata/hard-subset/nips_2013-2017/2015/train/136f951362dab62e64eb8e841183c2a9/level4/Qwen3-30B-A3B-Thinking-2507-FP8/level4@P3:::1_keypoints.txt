- Proposes a novel framework linking self-exciting point processes (Hawkes) with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Formulates a convex optimization problem and provides an efficient algorithm with O(1/t) convergence and scalability to large datasets.
- Main novelty: combining Hawkes processes with matrix factorization to enable both recommending items at the right time and predicting user return times.
- Differs from prior work (e.g., Kapoor et al.) by generalizing to new item recommendations; needs broader comparison to recent temporal recommendation papers.
- Strength — Technical quality: convex formulation, rigorous convergence analysis, and clear distinction from Poisson/Hawkes baselines.
- Strength — Scalability: experiments show the method scales to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Strength — Clarity: well-organized paper with clear math and appropriate experimental design.
- Weakness — Significance/motivation: lacks empirical evidence that time-varying preferences (beyond new-item effects) are a significant practical problem.
- Weakness — Returning-time baselines: misses simple but important baselines (e.g., last time delay, most common delay) and interval-based predictions.
- Weakness — SVD comparison: insufficient quantification of gains over strong baselines like SVD and unclear user-impact of reported improvements.
- Weakness — Practical impact: claims about marketing usefulness are vague and lack concrete use-case examples.
- Specific comments: minor typos and unclear references (e.g., "abel" → "able"; unclear mention of retweets/link creation); unclear justification for linking the two addressed tasks.
- Revision requests: provide empirical evidence of time-varying preferences, include simpler returning-time baselines, quantify improvements over SVD/popular-item baselines, and present concrete practical use cases.
- Overall: technically interesting and scalable but needs stronger motivation and baseline comparisons to demonstrate significance.
- Final recommendation: Weak Accept (major revisions required).
