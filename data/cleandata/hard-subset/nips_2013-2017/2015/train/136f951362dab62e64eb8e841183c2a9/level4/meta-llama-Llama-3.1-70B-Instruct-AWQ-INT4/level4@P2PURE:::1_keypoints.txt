- Proposes a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations that models time-varying user preferences and predicts users' next returning time with an efficient optimization algorithm; evaluated on one synthetic and two modest real datasets.  
- Unclear how widespread time-varying user preferences are; the real datasets should be analyzed to validate this claim and identify drivers (e.g., new popular content).  
- The practical motivation and benefits of predicting a user's next returning time are not explained; need clear use-cases for web companies.  
- Simple baselines for return-time prediction (last delay, most frequent delay) were not evaluated; exact-time prediction may be too hard.  
- Suggests reframing return prediction as a coarser classification task (short/medium/long/very long delays).  
- Experimental significance is unclear—uncertain whether improvements over SVD are substantial or user-noticeable.  
- Missing baseline comparison to popular-item recommendation (often updated daily).  
- Minor issues: typo on page 3 ("abel" → "able") and section 2 abruptly introduces retweets/link creation without references—needs rephrasing and citations.  
- Overall: tackles two relevant problems but lacks sufficient motivation, baselines, and analyses; paper is marginally acceptable.
