This research paper presents a new algorithm for dealing with time-sensitive recommendations, which is achieved by capturing the low-rank structure in the user-item matrix and using the Hawkes process. This algorithm can be solved in a distributed manner and achieves better results compared to existing methods.

Quality: Overall, I feel the research paper has adopted a theoretical approach to modeling observations (ratings) using stochastic processes. However, I have some comments.

A concern in the theoretical part lies in equation (8) and theorem 2. In equation 8, what parameter do we take the maximum value on? Is it on \rho? In such a case, can \rho* be unbounded? Furthermore, theorem 2 is not used when the research paper chose \rho in the experiments, which diminishes the importance of the theorem.

Another comment is with the baselines in the experiments. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17] (winner of the Netflix challenge) is one of the best algorithms for time-sensitive algorithms. Adding SVD++ to the baseline would further improve the quality. However, I am not dissatisfied with the current baselines.

Clarity: The research paper was well-written and easy to understand.

Novelty: I find this research paper new because time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively new problem.

Significance: I think time-sensitive recommendation can be impactful.
