

-- Summary -- This paper proposes a point process-based collaborative filtering model to capture temporal and recurrent structures between users and items. For each user-item pair, the event density combines a constant intensity with a self-exciting intensity derived from past events. Two distinct intensity functions are computed using separate sets of user and item latent vectors. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and provide empirical validation demonstrating the method's advantages over common alternatives. However, the experimental evaluation requires improvement. Specifically, a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD should be considered as a baseline, as the authors argue [12, 13] cannot recommend entirely new items (which SVD handles), making this comparison valuable. Additionally, the paper does not specify how the bandwidth parameter σ for the γ function is determined, nor does it analyze the sensitivity of results to σ variations. Crucially, details on hyperparameter selection (e.g., latent vector dimensions) for both the proposed method and baselines are missing. Further, the paper lacks sufficient characterization of the real-world datasets; given its focus on temporal structure, providing statistics such as recurrence frequency per user-item pair and temporal irregularity would strengthen the analysis.  
-- Quality -- The quality is acceptable but not strong.  
-- Clarity -- The presentation contains language issues, typos, and grammatical errors that require revision.  
-- Originality -- The method represents an interesting fusion of point processes and latent factor models.  
-- Significance -- The problem and approach hold practical value.
