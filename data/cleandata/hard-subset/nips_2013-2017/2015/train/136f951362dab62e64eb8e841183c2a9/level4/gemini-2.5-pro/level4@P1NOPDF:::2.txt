-- Summary --
This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal dynamics and recurring patterns in user-item interactions. Specifically, the model defines the event intensity for each user-item pair as the sum of a constant baseline intensity and a self-exciting term that depends on past interactions. The model parameterizes both the constant and self-exciting components using two distinct sets of latent vectors for users and items. The authors derive a corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and conduct empirical studies to demonstrate the advantages of their proposed method over several common alternatives.

However, the experimental evaluation could be strengthened. For instance, a crucial baseline is missing: a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. The authors argue that models like [12, 13] cannot recommend entirely new items. Since SVD can handle this scenario, it would be valuable to see how the proposed method compares against a straightforward ensemble that combines SVD's coverage with a temporal model like STiC.

Additionally, crucial details about hyperparameter selection are missing. For the kernel function ($\gamma$) in the self-exciting process, the authors do not specify how the bandwidth parameter $\sigma$ was set. A sensitivity analysis showing the impact of $\sigma$ on the results would also be beneficial. Furthermore, the paper lacks details on how other key hyperparameters, such as the latent vector dimensionality, were chosen for both the proposed model and the baselines.

Finally, it would be helpful to provide more details on the characteristics of the two real-world datasets. Given the paper's focus on temporal structure, it would be informative to include basic statistics that characterize these dynamics, such as the distribution of recurring events per user-item pair or metrics describing the irregularity of the temporal patterns.

-- Quality --
The overall quality of the paper is good, but it could be significantly strengthened by addressing the issues raised regarding the experimental setup and hyperparameter selection.

-- Clarity --
The presentation of the paper could be improved. There are several language issues, typos, and grammatical errors that detract from its clarity and readability.

-- Originality --
The proposed method, which combines point processes with latent factor models for collaborative filtering, is a novel and interesting contribution.

-- Significance --
The problem addressed is significant, and the proposed method has clear practical value for modern recommendation systems. This paper introduces a point process-based collaborative filtering model designed to capture temporal and recurrent patterns in user-item interactions. The authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and provide empirical results that demonstrate the model's advantages over common alternatives. However, as noted above, the paper would be stronger with a more thorough experimental evaluation and improved presentation.
