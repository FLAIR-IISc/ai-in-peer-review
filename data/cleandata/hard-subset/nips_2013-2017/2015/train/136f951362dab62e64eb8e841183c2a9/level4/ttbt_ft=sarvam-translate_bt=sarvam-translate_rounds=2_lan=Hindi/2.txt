-- Abstract -- This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and objects. Specifically, for each pair of user and object, the event density is the sum of a constant intensity and a self-stimulated intensity based on past events. Two sets of intensity are calculated by two sets of latent vectors of the user and object, respectively. A corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation has been obtained, and empirical studies have been conducted to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed methods over other commonly used alternatives. However, experimental studies can be improved. For example, a more sensible baseline would be a simple set of STiC [12] and SVD. Since the authors argue that [12, 13] are not fully capable of recommending new objects that can be covered by SVD, it would be valuable to compare the proposed method with this simpler set. Furthermore, how is the bandwidth parameter \sigma set for the \gamma function used for the self-stimulated process? How sensitive will the results be if we change \sigma? Additionally, there is no detail on how some other important hyperparameters are chosen for both the baseline methods and the proposed method, for example, the dimension of the latent vector. Providing more details on the characteristics of two real-world datasets would also be helpful. Since this paper is about the temporal structure, it is useful to discuss simple statistics such as the number of recurrent events per user/object pair and how irregular the temporal pattern is.
-- Quality -- The overall quality is reasonably good but not very good.
-- Clarity -- The paper presentation could be improved in terms of languages, typos, and grammatical errors.
-- Originality --
The proposed method is an interesting combination of point process and latent factor model.
-- Importance --
Both the problem and the method have good practical value. This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and objects. A corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation has been obtained, and empirical studies have been conducted to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed methods over other commonly used alternatives. However, experimental studies and presentation can be improved.
