- Summary: Models recommendation and next-return-time by treating each user–item pair as a Hawkes process with low-rank baseline/excitation matrices learned via convex nuclear-norm + nonnegativity regularization.  
- Estimation: Imposes low-rank structure to generalize to unseen pairs, yielding a convex estimation problem.  
- Optimization: Proposes a proximal-gradient + conditional-gradient algorithm using an auxiliary-variable penalty, with an O(1/t) convergence rate and large-scale practicality.  
- Empirical results: Strong performance on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) for time-aware ranking and return-time prediction, outperforming Poisson, tensor-factorization variants, SVD, and survival baselines (e.g., STiC).  
- Relation to prior work: Contrasts continuous-time Hawkes approach with survival models and discretized/time-ignorant matrix/tensor factorization; novelty claimed in combining Hawkes with low-rank transfer.  
- Suggested baseline gap: Recommends explicitly comparing to strong time-aware matrix factorization methods (e.g., Koren’s temporal dynamics, SVD++) for ranking.  
- Strengths: Principled modeling of recurrent/recency effects, transferable low-rank structure, convex formulation, carefully designed scalable algorithm, clear convergence guarantee, and thorough experiments.  
- Main theoretical concern: Ambiguity in the definition and boundedness of the penalty threshold rho* (domain of maximization and finiteness unclear).  
- Practical concern: Hyperparameters (rho, lambda, beta) are tuned empirically without clear link to the theoretical rho* or guidance from Theorem 2.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written; asks for explicit domain for rho* maximization, practical guidance for choosing rho, and a per-iteration complexity summary.  
- Originality: Novel integration of low-rank transfer with Hawkes processes and the proximal–conditional gradient approach for nonnegative nuclear-norm problems.  
- Significance: Relevant and potentially impactful for large-scale recommender systems and other event modeling domains (e.g., health) due to temporal modeling and scalability.  
- Pros (condensed): Principled continuous-time Hawkes model, convex formulation, efficient algorithm with convergence, scalable, strong empirical gains, extensible to context.  
- Cons (condensed): Unclear rho* definition and practical use of theory, missing strong time-aware MF baselines, minor clarity/complexity-summary issues.  
- Recommendation: Solid and timely paper; address rho* clarification and add a strong time-aware MF baseline (e.g., SVD++) to strengthen empirical claims, but overall compelling.
