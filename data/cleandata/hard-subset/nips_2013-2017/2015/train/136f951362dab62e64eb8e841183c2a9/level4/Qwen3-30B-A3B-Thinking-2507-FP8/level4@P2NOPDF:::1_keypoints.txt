- Claims of time-varying user preferences are unsubstantiated; authors must analyze real datasets to demonstrate prevalence and drivers of preference shifts.  
- Return time prediction is weakly motivated; paper lacks concrete practitioner use-cases showing business value.  
- Missing simple baselines for return-time (e.g., last interaction delay, mode-based delay); predicting exact times is unrealistic—use coarse-grained buckets instead.  
- Improvements over SVD are not quantified for statistical or practical significance (e.g., relative RMSE reduction).  
- Paper omits the critical popularity-based recommendation baseline, weakening empirical claims.  
- Scalability shown only on synthetic data; real-world/industry-scale applicability is unproven given modest real datasets.  
- Optimization efficiency alone does not justify the contribution without stronger empirical and practical validation.  
- Typos present (e.g., "abel" → "able") and need correction.  
- Abrupt, unjustified introduction of social network examples disrupts flow and lacks references.  
- Dual-focus wording is redundant; reframe as a unified time-aware recommendation and return-time prediction framework.  
- Overall: identifies a relevant problem but fails to demonstrate prevalence, motivation, or practical impact.  
- Recommendation: Reject; require major revisions including dataset analysis, concrete business motivation, simpler baselines/coarse tasks, popularity comparisons, and clearer objective integration.
