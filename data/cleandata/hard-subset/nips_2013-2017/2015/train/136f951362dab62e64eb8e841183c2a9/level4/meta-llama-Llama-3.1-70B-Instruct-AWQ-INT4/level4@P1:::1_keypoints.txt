- Proposes a novel time-sensitive recommendation approach that explicitly models time-varying user preferences and recurrent activities.  
- Also addresses the less-studied problem of predicting a user's next return time.  
- Well-motivated with clear critique of existing recommendation methods' limitations.  
- Argues temporal activity patterns are a more relevant metric of engagement than ratings or click-through rates.  
- Presents a framework combining self-exciting point processes and low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns.  
- Develops an efficient, scalable algorithm for millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on both synthetic and real datasets.  
- Model can incorporate additional context (user profiles, text, spatial features) for personalized recommendations.  
- Lacks deeper analysis on real datasets to substantiate the prevalence of time-varying preferences.  
- Motivation and practical utility of predicting next returning time for web companies is not clearly explained.  
- Experimental section could include more baselines (e.g., last time delay, most frequent delay) for comprehensive comparison.  
- Predicting exact return time may be overly challenging; predicting delay categories could be a simpler alternative.  
- Overall positive evaluation: promising contribution with need for additional analysis and experimental improvements.  
- Reviewer rating: 7/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
