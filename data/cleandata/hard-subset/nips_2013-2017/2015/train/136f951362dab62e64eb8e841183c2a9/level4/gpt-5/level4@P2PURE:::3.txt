This paper proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation by capturing the low-rank structure of the user–item matrix and leveraging a Hawkes process. The algorithm supports distributed computation and outperforms existing methods.

Quality: Overall, the paper takes a principled approach by modeling the observations (ratings) using stochastic processes. I do, however, have a few comments.
My main theoretical concern is with Eq. (8) and Theorem 2. In Eq. (8), with respect to which parameter is the maximum taken? Is it over ρ? If so, can ρ* be unbounded? Furthermore, Theorem 2 is not used when selecting ρ in the experiments, which diminishes the practical importance of the theorem.
Another comment pertains to the experimental baselines. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17] (the Netflix Prize winner) is among the strongest algorithms for time-sensitive recommendation. Including SVD++ as a baseline would further strengthen the evaluation, though I am not dissatisfied with the current baselines.

Clarity: The paper is well written and easy to follow.

Originality: The paper appears novel, as time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively new problem.

Significance: Time-sensitive recommendation can be impactful. This paper presents a new algorithm that captures the low-rank structure of a user–item matrix and employs a Hawkes process. The method can be solved in a distributed manner and achieves better results than existing methods.
