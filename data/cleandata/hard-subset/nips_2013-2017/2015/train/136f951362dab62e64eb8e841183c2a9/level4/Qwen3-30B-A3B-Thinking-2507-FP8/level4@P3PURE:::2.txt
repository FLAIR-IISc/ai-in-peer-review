

-- Summary -- This paper proposes a point process-based collaborative filtering model designed to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and items. For each user-item pair, the event density is modeled as the sum of a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity dependent on past events. Two sets of user and item latent vectors compute the corresponding intensities, and the authors derive an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation. Empirical studies demonstrate the proposed method's advantages over commonly used alternatives. However, the experimental evaluation requires enhancement. Specifically, a more appropriate baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. The authors argue that STiC [12, 13] cannot recommend completely new items—addressed by SVD—making this ensemble a meaningful comparison. Additionally, the paper lacks details on the bandwidth parameter σ for the γ function used in the self-exciting process: its selection methodology and sensitivity to variations in σ are unclear. Furthermore, critical hyperparameters (including the dimension of latent vectors) are not justified for either the proposed method or baseline approaches. Finally, the characteristics of the two real-world datasets warrant deeper analysis. Given the focus on temporal structure, providing basic statistics—such as the frequency of recurring events per user-item pair and the irregularity of temporal patterns—would strengthen the empirical foundation.

-- Quality -- The overall quality is reasonable but not outstanding.

-- Clarity -- The paper's presentation contains language issues, typos, and grammatical errors that require correction.

-- Originality -- The method represents a novel combination of point process modeling and latent factor approaches.

-- Significance -- The problem addressed and the proposed method hold practical value, though the experimental limitations noted above reduce the impact of the empirical claims.
