

-- Summary -- This paper introduces a novel framework that integrates self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal patterns in user-item consumption data. The authors model user-item interactions as a point process where the event density combines a constant baseline intensity and a self-exciting component dependent on historical events. The model employs low-rank factorization to represent user and item latent vectors for computing these intensities, and develops an efficient optimization algorithm with O(1/t) convergence rate. Empirical evaluations on both synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate the proposed method's superior performance on time-sensitive recommendation tasks compared to state-of-the-art alternatives.

However, several aspects of the experimental evaluation could be strengthened. The authors could include a more comprehensive baseline comparison, such as a simple ensemble of STiC [12] (which handles temporal dynamics but cannot recommend new items) and SVD (which handles item recommendation but not temporal dynamics), to better demonstrate the incremental value of their approach. Additionally, the paper does not specify how the bandwidth parameter σ for the exponential triggering kernel is selected or how sensitive the results are to variations in this parameter. The manuscript also lacks details about the selection of other critical hyperparameters, including the latent dimension for all methods. Furthermore, the characteristics of the real-world datasets could be better described, particularly regarding the number of recurring events per user-item pair and the irregularity of temporal patterns, which would strengthen the contextual understanding of the temporal dynamics being modeled.

-- Quality -- The technical quality is acceptable but not exceptional. The theoretical foundation is sound and the optimization algorithm is well-motivated, but the empirical evaluation could be more comprehensive.

-- Clarity -- The paper would benefit from improved language, grammar, and technical writing. Several typographical errors and grammatical issues reduce the clarity of the presentation.

-- Originality -- The integration of self-exciting point processes with low-rank factorization represents an interesting and novel combination of established techniques that effectively addresses time-sensitive recommendation problems.

-- Significance -- The problem addressed has clear practical significance for modern web services, as the ability to recommend relevant items at optimal times and predict user return times can substantially improve user engagement and marketing efficiency. The method's scalability to large datasets and its ability to incorporate contextual information further enhance its practical value.
