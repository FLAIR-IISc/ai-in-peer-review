- Introduces a collaborative filtering model based on point processes to capture temporal and recurring user-item interactions.  
- Models each user-item event density as a base intensity plus a self-exciting component driven by historical events.  
- Implements two distinct sets of latent vectors for users and items to compute the respective intensity components.  
- Derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and reports empirical results claiming superiority over common alternatives.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks a key baseline: an ensemble like STiC + SVD that would address new-item handling and be a relevant competitor.  
- Paper omits details on selection of the bandwidth parameter σ in the γ function; no sensitivity analysis over σ is provided.  
- Does not specify how critical hyperparameters (e.g., latent dimensionality) were chosen for the proposed model or baselines.  
- Dataset descriptions are insufficient; missing statistics on recurring-event frequency per user-item and measures of temporal irregularity.  
- Technical quality is solid overall but experimental design and evaluation need strengthening.  
- Clarity is hindered by language, grammatical, and typographical errors; requires thorough proofreading.  
- Originality: presents a novel synthesis of point process theory with latent factor collaborative filtering.  
- Significance: tackles an important practical problem and offers a valuable method, but impact is reduced by evaluation and presentation weaknesses.
