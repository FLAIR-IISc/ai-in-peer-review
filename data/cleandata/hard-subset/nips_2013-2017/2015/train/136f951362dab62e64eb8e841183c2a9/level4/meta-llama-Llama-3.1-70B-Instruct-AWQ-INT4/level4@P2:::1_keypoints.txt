- Proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendations and prediction of users' next returning time.
- Introduces an efficient optimization algorithm for learning the model.
- Combines self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to capture recurrent temporal user-item patterns, distinguishing it from one-time preference methods.
- Motivation for modeling time-varying preferences is well-argued, but needs empirical analysis of real datasets to validate causes (e.g., new popular items vs other patterns).
- Motivation and practical utility for predicting next returning time are not clearly explained; more application context (e.g., marketing, retention) is needed.
- Experiments show algorithmic efficiency and improved predictive performance on synthetic and real data.
- The practical significance of the performance gain over the SVD baseline is unclear and should be quantified.
- Missing simple baselines for return-time prediction (e.g., last time delay, most frequent delay).
- Predicting exact return times may be overly ambitious; consider predicting coarse delay categories (short/medium/long).
- Paper is generally well-written but contains minor issues (typo on page 3) and a confusing example in Section 2 (retweets/link creation).
- Strength: novel, time-sensitive recommendation framework addressing recurrent temporal patterns.
- Strength: development of an efficient learning algorithm.
- Weakness: unclear motivation/usefulness of return-time prediction and insufficient baseline comparisons.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of real datasets to support claims about time-varying preferences.
