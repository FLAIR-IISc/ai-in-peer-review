This paper proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation that models user-item interactions by combining a low-rank matrix structure with a Hawkes process. The resulting model can be optimized in a distributed manner and demonstrates superior performance compared to existing methods in the experiments.

**Quality:** The paper's overall approach of using stochastic processes to model rating observations is well-founded and principled. However, I have some concerns regarding the theoretical analysis and the experimental setup.

My primary theoretical concern relates to Equation (8) and Theorem 2. It is unclear over which parameter the maximum is taken in Equation (8). If it is over ρ, a key question arises: can the resulting optimal value, ρ*, be unbounded? Furthermore, the selection of ρ in the experiments does not appear to leverage Theorem 2. This disconnect raises questions about the theorem's practical utility and weakens its contribution to the paper.

A second point concerns the choice of experimental baselines. While the current baselines are reasonable, the evaluation would be significantly strengthened by including a comparison against SVD++ [17]. As a top-performing algorithm from the Netflix challenge, SVD++ is a highly competitive benchmark for collaborative filtering and would provide a more robust assessment of the proposed method's performance.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

**Originality:** The proposed method, which combines Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix factorization for this task, appears to be a novel contribution to the field of time-sensitive recommendation.

**Significance:** The problem of time-sensitive recommendation is of significant practical importance. This paper makes a valuable contribution by proposing a novel, high-performing algorithm based on Hawkes processes and low-rank assumptions. The model's distributed nature further enhances its potential for real-world impact.
