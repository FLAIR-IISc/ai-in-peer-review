- Proposes a point-process collaborative filtering model with intensity = base rate + self-exciting component parameterized by two separate sets of user/item latent vectors.  
- Derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation and shows empirical advantages over common baselines.  
- Experimental evaluation is promising but can be strengthened with a sensible baseline: an ensemble of STiC and SVD.  
- Need clarification and sensitivity analysis for the γ function bandwidth σ used in the self-exciting term.  
- Lacks details on hyperparameter selection (e.g., latent dimensionality) for both the proposed method and baselines.  
- Should report dataset statistics relevant to temporal focus (e.g., counts of recurring events per user–item pair, measures of temporal irregularity).  
- Overall quality: method is solid but empirical section and presentation are not outstanding.  
- Clarity issues: language/typos, unclear explanation of the two latent vector sets, and missing hyperparameter/dataset details.  
- Originality: novel and interesting combination of point processes with latent factor models.  
- Significance: practically relevant for temporal/recurrent recommendation, but needs better baselines, hyperparameter sensitivity, and dataset characterization.
