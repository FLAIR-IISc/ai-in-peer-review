This paper presents a new algorithm that incorporates a low-order structure of the user-item matrix and solves the problem of time-sensitive recommendations using the Hoax process. This algorithm can be solved using a distributed method and provides better results than existing methods.

**Quality:** Overall, I believe the paper adopts a principled approach to modeling observations (rating) using the stochastic process. However, I have some comments.

One concern in the theoretical section is equation (8) and theorem 2. In equation 8, what parameter do we take the maximum value over? Is it over rho? In that case, can rho* be infinite? Also, theorem 2 was not used when selecting rho in the experiment, which diminishes the importance of the theorem.

Another comment is about the experiment's baseline. To my knowledge, SVD++ [17] (winner of the Netflix challenge) is one of the best algorithms for time-sensitive algorithms. Adding SVD++ to the baseline would improve the quality further. Although I am not dissatisfied with the current baseline.

**Clarity:** The paper was well-written and easy to follow.

**Originality:** The paper seemed new to me because time-sensitive recommendations is a relatively new problem.

**Significance:** I believe time-sensitive recommendations can be impactful. This paper presents a new algorithm for solving the problem of time-sensitive recommendations using the user-item matrix and the Hoax process. This algorithm can be solved using a distributed method and provides better results than existing methods.
