This paper introduces a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation, leveraging a low-rank user-item matrix factorization combined with a Hawkes process model. The approach is theoretically grounded and offers a scalable, distributed solution, demonstrating improvements over existing methods in experimental evaluations.

**Quality:** The paper presents a principled approach to modeling user behavior and item interactions as a stochastic process. The combination of low-rank factorization and a Hawkes process is a compelling strategy for capturing the temporal dynamics inherent in recommendation tasks. However, I have a couple of concerns regarding the theoretical aspects. Specifically, in Equation (8), the maximization is not clearly defined with respect to which parameter(s). If the maximization is over ρ, it’s unclear if ρ* could be unbounded, and the implications of this. Furthermore, while Theorem 2 provides theoretical justification for a particular choice of ρ, this choice is not utilized in the experimental section. This disconnect diminishes the practical impact and relevance of the theorem within the context of this work.

The experimental evaluation is well-executed, but could be strengthened by including stronger baseline comparisons. While the chosen baselines provide a reasonable benchmark, the inclusion of SVD++ [17], a highly successful algorithm – and the winner of the Netflix challenge – known for its performance in collaborative filtering tasks, would provide a more rigorous assessment of the proposed method's advantages, particularly given its relevance to time-sensitive scenarios.  However, I do not find the absence of SVD++ to be a critical flaw.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand. The authors clearly articulate the motivation, methodology, and results. The mathematical notation is generally well-defined and the explanations are concise without sacrificing clarity.

**Originality:** The work appears novel. Time-sensitive recommendation is a relatively new and important area of research, and the proposed approach offers a unique combination of techniques not previously explored in this context. The integration of Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix factorization is a noteworthy contribution.

**Significance:** Time-sensitive recommendation has the potential to be highly impactful across a range of applications, including e-commerce, news aggregation, and social media. This paper addresses a crucial challenge within this domain by providing a scalable and effective algorithm for capturing the temporal aspects of user preferences. The ability to efficiently handle large-scale data through distributed computation further enhances the practical significance of this work.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a strong paper with a novel and well-motivated approach to a significant problem. Addressing the questions regarding Equation (8) and the usage of Theorem 2, and considering the inclusion of SVD++ in the experimental evaluation, would further strengthen the contribution. I believe this work is worthy of acceptance.
