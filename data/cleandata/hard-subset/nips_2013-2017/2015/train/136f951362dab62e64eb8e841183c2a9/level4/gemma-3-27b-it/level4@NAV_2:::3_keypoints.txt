- Proposes a novel framework combining Hawkes (self-exciting) point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Uses low-rank approximation to enable scalability and generalization in modeling recurrent temporal user-item interactions.  
- Presents a convex optimization formulation and an efficient algorithm with O(1/t) convergence rate.  
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets and superior performance on synthetic and real-world data versus state-of-the-art methods.  
- Model is extensible to incorporate additional contextual information.  
- Addresses an important and often-overlooked problem: temporal dynamics and recurrent user activities in recommender systems.  
- Hawkes processes provide a principled way to model self-exciting user interactions; low-rank helps computational feasibility.  
- Theoretical analysis, including convergence guarantees, strengthens the paper’s rigor.  
- Technical approach appears sound and experimental results are compelling.  
- Clarification needed on Equation (8)/Theorem 1: explicitly state the maximization variable (ρ) and whether ρ* can be unbounded.  
- Practical relevance of Theorem 2 is limited because the derived ρ* is not used in experiments; tie convergence analysis more closely to experiments.  
- Recommend comparing to stronger baselines (e.g., SVD++) to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and math/experimental setup are generally clear.  
- Ablation studies (e.g., low-rank component, kernel choice) are missing and would clarify component contributions.  
- Originality stems from the novel combination of Hawkes processes, low-rank modeling, and an efficient optimization algorithm.  
- Significance is high due to potential real-world impact on user engagement and applicability to large-scale services.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, theoretical foundation, strong empirical results, scalability, and practical impact.  
- Rejection concerns: need clarification on ρ*/Theorem 2, stronger baseline comparisons, and additional ablation analyses.
