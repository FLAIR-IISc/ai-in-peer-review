-- Abstract -- This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and objects. Specifically, for each pair of user and object, the event density is a sum of a constant intensity and a self-excited intensity based on past events. Two sets of intensity are computed by two sets of latent vectors of the user and object, respectively. A corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation is derived, and empirical studies are conducted to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed methods over other commonly used alternatives.

However, improvements can be made in the experimental studies. For example, a more sensible baseline would be a simple ensemble of STiC [12] and SVD. Since the authors argue that [12, 13] are not entirely capable of recommending new objects that can be covered by SVD, and it would be valuable to compare the proposed method with this simple ensemble. Furthermore, how is the bandwidth parameter \sigma set for the \gamma function used for the self-excited process? How sensitive will the results be if we change \sigma? Additionally, there is no detail on how some other important hyperparameters are chosen for both the baseline methods and the proposed method, for example, the dimension of the latent vector.

Providing more details on the characteristics of two real-world datasets would also be helpful. Since this paper is about the temporal structure, it is useful to discuss simple statistics such as the number of recurrent events per user/object pair and how irregular the temporal pattern is.

-- Quality -- The overall quality is reasonably good but not very good.
-- Clarity -- The paper presentation could be improved in terms of languages, typos, and grammatical errors.
-- Originality --
The proposed method is an interesting combination of point process and latent factor models.
-- Significance --
Both the problem and the method have good practical value. This paper proposes a point process collaborative filtering model to capture the temporal and recurrent structure between users and objects. A corresponding optimization algorithm for parameter estimation is derived, and empirical studies are conducted to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed methods over other commonly used alternatives. However, improvements can be made in the experimental studies and presentation.
