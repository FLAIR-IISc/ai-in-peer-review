

This paper proposes a novel algorithm for time-sensitive recommendation by integrating low-rank matrix structure with Hawkes processes, enabling distributed optimization and demonstrating superior performance over existing baselines. The core idea is compelling, addressing a timely challenge in dynamic recommendation systems. Below, I provide specific feedback to strengthen the submission.

**Quality**  
The theoretical foundation is generally sound, but two aspects require clarification. First, Equation (8) states a maximization over a parameter ρ, yet the paper does not specify the domain or constraints for ρ. If ρ is unbounded (e.g., ρ → ∞), the optimization may become ill-posed, potentially undermining the algorithm’s stability. The authors should explicitly define the feasible set for ρ and justify its boundedness. Second, Theorem 2 establishes a key theoretical guarantee, yet the experimental section does not leverage this result to inform the choice of ρ (e.g., via the derived optimal ρ*). This disconnect weakens the theorem’s relevance and reduces the perceived theoretical contribution. The authors should either demonstrate how Theorem 2 guides hyperparameter selection or clarify its role in the proposed framework.

Regarding baselines, the current comparison lacks a strong time-sensitive benchmark. SVD++ [17], a top-performing method for standard recommendation (including Netflix Prize winner), is widely recognized as a robust baseline for matrix-factorization approaches. While SVD++ does not inherently model temporal dynamics, its inclusion would provide a meaningful reference point for the *standard* recommendation performance, against which the time-sensitive improvements can be contextualized. The absence of SVD++ (or similar state-of-the-art baselines like T-ALS or ST-ALS) limits the paper’s ability to demonstrate the *relative* value of incorporating temporal structure. Adding SVD++ would substantially strengthen the experimental evaluation, even if the authors acknowledge its limitations for time-sensitive settings.

**Clarity**  
The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The problem formulation, algorithmic steps, and experimental setup are easy to follow, with effective use of figures and concise mathematical notation.

**Originality and Significance**  
The problem of time-sensitive recommendation is increasingly relevant for platforms like streaming services and e-commerce, where user preferences evolve rapidly. The fusion of low-rank matrix modeling with Hawkes processes represents a novel approach to capturing temporal dependencies, distinct from prior work that often relies on simple timestamp-based weighting. This combination is sufficiently original to warrant attention, particularly given the demonstrated scalability via distributed optimization. The significance is further amplified by the practical applicability of the method to large-scale systems, where real-time responsiveness is critical.

**Recommendation**  
The paper presents a promising direction with strong potential impact. However, the theoretical gaps and baseline limitations require addressing before acceptance. Specifically:  
1. Clarify the optimization domain for ρ in Equation (8) and validate its boundedness.  
2. Explicitly connect Theorem 2 to the experimental ρ selection (e.g., via ablation studies on ρ’s impact).  
3. Include SVD++ (and ideally one additional time-aware baseline) to establish a stronger performance benchmark.  

These revisions would significantly bolster the paper’s technical rigor and experimental credibility. As currently written, the work is novel and impactful, but the unresolved theoretical details and baseline omissions prevent it from fully realizing its potential. I recommend **major revision** to address these points. The authors have the opportunity to deliver a substantially stronger contribution with these updates.
