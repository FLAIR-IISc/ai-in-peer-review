- Proposes a point-process collaborative filtering model combining a constant intensity and a self-exciting intensity per user–item pair using latent user/item vectors.  
- Derives an optimization algorithm for parameter estimation with theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical results show advantages over common alternatives on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Experimental evaluation is not comprehensive; lacks comparison to a sensible baseline such as an ensemble of STiC and SVD.  
- Paper does not report how the triggering kernel bandwidth σ was set or analyze sensitivity to σ.  
- Important hyperparameter selection (e.g., latent dimensionality) for both baselines and the proposed method is under-detailed.  
- Lacks descriptive statistics of the real-world datasets (e.g., counts of recurring events, temporal irregularity) despite focusing on temporal structure.  
- Technical approach appears sound, but experimental and tuning omissions weaken the claims.  
- Writing quality needs improvement—typos and grammatical errors reduce clarity.  
- Originality is strong: novel combination of point process modeling with low-rank latent factor models.  
- Significance is high: modeling temporal user behavior has practical value for recommender systems and related applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel modeling approach, addresses important problem, strong empirical performance, and efficient algorithm with convergence guarantees.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete experimental comparisons, insufficient hyperparameter details, limited dataset analysis, and subpar writing quality.
