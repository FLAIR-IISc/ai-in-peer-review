

The paper introduces a novel method for time-sensitive recommendations that accounts for time-varying user preferences, distinguishing itself from existing approaches. It further proposes predicting user return intervals. The method employs an efficient optimization algorithm for learning, with experiments demonstrating scalability on a synthetic dataset and efficacy on two modest-sized real-world datasets.  

Key concerns require clarification:  
1. **Motivation for time-varying preferences**: The prevalence of this phenomenon must be substantiated using analysis of the experimental datasets (e.g., is variation driven by new content, behavioral shifts, or other patterns?). Without this, the core problem statement lacks empirical grounding.  
2. **Justification for return-time prediction**: The paper must explicitly articulate the practical value of predicting exact return times for industry applications (e.g., how would a platform leverage this for engagement, retention, or personalized scheduling?).  
3. **Baseline evaluation**: Critical simple baselines for return-time prediction are omitted: (i) last observed interval, (ii) most frequent interval. Additionally, predicting exact return times is overly ambitious; a coarser-grained task (e.g., classifying delays into short/medium/long categories) would be more feasible and practically useful.  
4. **Experimental significance**: The performance gains over SVD (and popular-item baselines) are unclear. Are improvements substantial (order-of-magnitude) or marginal? How would these recommendations impact user experience compared to dynamic popular-item recs?  

Minor issues:  
1. Page 3, last paragraph: "and is abel to capture" → "able".  
2. Section 2, first paragraph: References to retweets/link creation in social networks appear abruptly without context or citations; rephrase to maintain focus.  

The paper addresses two problems (time-sensitive recommendations and return-time prediction) but lacks compelling motivation for their relevance and real-world impact. Critical baselines and analysis (as noted above) are missing, rendering the contribution’s novelty and utility ambiguous. Overall, the work is borderline acceptable pending substantial revisions.
