- Proposes a unified framework for time-sensitive recommendation that models both the right item and right moment using low-rank Hawkes processes.
- Leverages the connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank matrix models to enable time-aware recommendations.
- Introduces a novel convex formulation with nuclear-norm regularization and proves an optimal O(1/t) convergence rate.
- Presents Algorithm 1 that mixes proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods to handle non-negativity and nuclear-norm constraints efficiently.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, using synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) and comparisons to multiple baselines, showing superior performance and scalability.
- Highlights practical relevance to applications like ad bidding and healthcare through next-return-time prediction.
- Assumes a homogeneous exponential triggering kernel without adequate discussion of limitations or extensions to more flexible kernels (e.g., periodic patterns).
- Insufficient comparison and explanation versus related temporal recommendation work (e.g., Kapoor et al., Du et al.), especially how the model generalizes to new items.
- Does not address cold-start scenarios for new users or items, a key limitation for real-world deployment.
- Lacks analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (ρ, λ, β), despite theoretical guarantees depending on proper choices.
- Missing ablation study to isolate contributions of low-rank assumption, Hawkes formulation, and the optimization algorithm.
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound paper with significant contributions and compelling experiments; recommendation: accept.
