- Proposes time-sensitive recommendation by modeling user-item interactions as low-rank Hawkes processes.  
- Tackles recommending the right item at the right moment and predicting users' next return time.  
- Connects self-exciting point processes with low-rank models and formulates a convex optimization with nuclear norm regularization.  
- Develops an efficient optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Demonstrates scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events; strong results on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II).  

- Strength: Technically sound with a novel, well-motivated connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank models.  
- Strength: Thorough theoretical analysis including convergence proof (Theorem 2).  
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and three real-world datasets across domains.  
- Strength: Authors acknowledge limitations and evaluate scalability and predictive performance carefully.  

- Weakness: Needs clearer explanation why prior methods couldn't achieve the combined convergence rate and stronger ties to related optimization work.  
- Weakness: Experiments lack thorough parameter sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (ρ, λ, β).  
- Weakness: Theoretical justification for the low-rank assumption is weak and lacks empirical support on real datasets.  

- Strength: Paper is well-organized with consistent, clear mathematical notation and reproducible pseudocode.  
- Weakness: Some optimization derivations (e.g., reformulation from (4) to (5)) are abrupt and need more intuition.  
- Weakness: Triggering kernel parametrization discussion is vague; choice of exponential kernels not well justified.  

- Strength: Novel contribution connecting low-rank models and Hawkes processes; distinguishes from prior work like STiC.  
- Strength: Convex nuclear-norm formulation advances beyond non-convex approaches in related work.  
- Weakness: Related work section misses several recent temporal recommendation works from KDD/WWW/RecSys.  
- Weakness: Claim of uniqueness on convergence rate needs stronger comparisons to similar optimization techniques.  

- Strength: Addresses an important practical problem (timely personalization) with compelling application examples.  
- Strength: Scalability to large datasets is a major practical contribution.  
- Strength: Unified framework for both recommendation and time prediction is valuable.  
- Weakness: Lacks demonstration of practical impact (e.g., A/B tests) and uses somewhat standard real-world datasets.  

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel, and rigorous paper that advances temporal recommendation and merits acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Accept to NIPS.  

- Pro: Novel Hawkes–low-rank connection; strong theory; comprehensive empirical validation; scalable; distinct from prior work.  
- Con: Incomplete related work coverage; insufficient parameter sensitivity analysis; limited practical impact demonstration.  

- Suggestions: Expand related work to include recent KDD/WWW/RecSys papers; add parameter sensitivity analysis; include a more challenging dataset; provide intuition for alternative optimization formulation.
