- Overall: Novel connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation; reviewer recommends strong accept.  
- Problem/Summary: Predicts both return time and item recommendation using a Hawkes process with low-rank approximation to scale and to recommend new items; clearly relates to prior work (Kapoor et al.).  
- Novelty: Combines Hawkes processes and low-rank modeling in a novel, well-justified way.  
- Technical contribution: Convex formulation with an efficient optimization algorithm (proximal + conditional gradient) achieving O(1/t) convergence.  
- Scalability: Algorithm scales to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Experimental validation: Strong empirical results on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) outperforming several baselines; analyses of convergence and scale are insightful.  
- Generality: Model can incorporate contextual information (spatial, textual, categorical, user profile).  
- Writing: Paper could be more concise; introduction and some algorithm explanations are repetitive.  
- Hyperparameter tuning: Insufficient detail on how hyperparameters (ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth, latent rank) were chosen, affecting reproducibility.  
- Theoretical depth: Needs deeper discussion of assumptions and implications of the low-rank approximation.  
- Quality (NIPS criteria): Technically sound with supporting theory and experiments.  
- Clarity (NIPS criteria): Mostly clear but could be streamlined.  
- Originality (NIPS criteria): Highly original approach.  
- Significance (NIPS criteria): Results are important with significant potential impact across domains.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept; address minor writing, hyperparameter, and theoretical-discussion suggestions.
