- Proposes low-rank Hawkes processes for time-sensitive item recommendation and per user–item return-time prediction.
- Models each user–item pair as a univariate Hawkes process with exponential kernel and imposes nuclear-norm low-rank structure on base intensities and excitation matrices.
- Formulates a convex MLE with nonnegativity constraints, uses variable-splitting quadratic-penalty to decouple norms, and develops a hybrid proximal + conditional gradient optimizer.
- Proves an O(1/t) convergence rate and demonstrates scalability on synthetic data and three large real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II).
- Empirically outperforms Poisson, tensor-factorization, and survival/HsMM baselines; uses time-change Q–Q plots to justify Hawkes modeling.
- Builds on prior temporal point-process, Hawkes-learning, and low-rank recommendation literature and on conditional-gradient/proximal hybrid methods.
- Strength: unified probabilistic framework linking intensity, ranking, and return-time prediction.
- Strength: convex formulation with global optimality under linear intensity and practical nonnegativity enforcement.
- Strength: simple, implementable optimization steps (nonnegative projection + top singular vectors) with stated convergence guarantee.
- Strength: demonstrated scalability to very large event counts and user–item matrices.
- Strength: consistent empirical performance across datasets and a useful Hawkes diagnostic via Q–Q plots.
- Strength: natural prediction for unseen user–item pairs through low-rank sharing.
- Weakness: Claim of exactness for the quadratic variable-splitting penalty (Theorem 1) is questionable and depends on unknown quantities.
- Weakness: No discussion of Hawkes stability/identifiability conditions (e.g., branching ratio < 1) to prevent explosive solutions.
- Weakness: Modeling restricts to self-excitation per user–item pair and omits cross-excitation across items for the same user.
- Weakness: Baselines and metrics are limited; missing stronger time-aware recommenders, top-k metrics (Recall@k, NDCG), and significance testing.
- Weakness: Optimization details unclear — per-iteration cost, number of power iterations, choice/estimation of Lipschitz constant L not specified.
- Weakness: Return-time estimation relies on Monte Carlo without discussing closed-form or more efficient sampling/estimation options and runtime/variance trade-offs.
- Quality: Convexity and algorithmic analysis are generally sound, but Theorem 1 needs stronger justification.
- Quality: Empirical evaluation is large-scale and thorough but would benefit from broader baselines, ablations, and statistical tests.
- Clarity: Paper is mostly clear and well-structured, but some notation and optimization motivation/details could be streamlined and clarified.
- Originality: Novel combination of low-rank structure with Hawkes modeling for recommendation and return-time prediction.
- Significance: Important and practically useful problem; convexity and scalability increase practical impact.
- Questions for authors: clarify Theorem 1/finite-ρ validity or adopt augmented-Lagrangian/ADMM; report stability constraints on A and empirical explosiveness.
- Questions for authors: provide per-iteration costs, number of power iterations, and how L is chosen/estimated.
- Questions for authors: show sensitivity analyses for kernel bandwidth and hyperparameters (λ, β, ρ).
- Questions for authors: report standard ranking metrics (Recall@k, NDCG@k) and statistical significance.
- Pros for acceptance: convex, scalable method with convergence guarantees; strong empirical results; clear link between ranking and return-time tasks; practical impact.
- Cons against acceptance: questionable penalty exactness; limited baselines/metrics and statistical rigor; no Hawkes stability discussion or cross-excitation modeling; missing implementation details for reproducibility.
- Overall recommendation: leaning toward accept conditional on clarifying penalty equivalence, adding standard ranking metrics/significance, and discussing stability/identifiability.
