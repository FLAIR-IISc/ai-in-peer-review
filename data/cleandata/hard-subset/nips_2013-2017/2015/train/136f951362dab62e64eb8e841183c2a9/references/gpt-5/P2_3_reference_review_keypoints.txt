- Tackles time-sensitive item recommendation and return-time prediction using per user–item Hawkes processes with exponential kernels and low-rank nuclear-norm structure on baseline Λ0 and excitation A.  
- Proposes a convex, nonnegativity-constrained formulation and an auxiliary quadratic-penalty reformulation to decouple nuclear-norm and positivity terms.  
- Introduces a hybrid optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient (for nonnegativity) and conditional gradient/Frank–Wolfe (for nuclear norms) with acceleration and proves O(1/t) convergence (and O(1/t^2) when nuclear term is absent).  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) shows improved ranking and return-time prediction versus Poisson, SVD, tensor methods, and a survival/HMM baseline; includes a time-change diagnostic.  
- Relates to prior low-rank/sparse Hawkes and temporal factorization work; novelty is the low-rank convex Hawkes parameterization over user–item grids and the hybrid proximal–FW solver.  
- Strength: Hawkes model fits recurrent/bursty interactions (short-term excitation vs long-term preference) and the convex formulation is well-motivated and computationally efficient via LMOs and simple projections.  
- Strength: Convergence analysis is provided and experiments are broad, including scaling tests and a useful time-change goodness-of-fit check.  
- Weakness: Model allows only self-excitation per user–item and omits cross-item excitation, limiting applicability in domains with inter-item dependencies (e.g., comorbidities).  
- Weakness: Theorem 1’s claim of finite exactness for the quadratic-penalty reformulation is nonstandard and depends on unknown optima; theory lacks a constructive ρ selection or detailed proof.  
- Weakness: Evaluation details missing—train/validation/test splitting for hyperparameter tuning not specified, and Monte Carlo details for return-time prediction (samples, runtime, stability) are omitted.  
- Weakness: Baselines are insufficient—missing stronger temporal point-process baselines (mutually-exciting Hawkes, nonparametric Hawkes, continuous-time factorization) and clearer distinctions from coarse time-binned tensor methods.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns—missing implementation details (initialization, step sizes/Lipschitz estimates, stopping criteria, top singular vector computation, numerical stability) and no code release.  
- Clarity suggestions: unify notation, state assumptions ensuring positivity and gradient Lipschitzness, clarify held-out sequence protocol, and provide per-iteration complexity and SVD computation details.  
- Originality: Moderately original—natural use of Hawkes for recommendation but a useful contribution in the convex low-rank user–item parameterization and hybrid solver.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful as a scalable convex estimator for time-sensitive recommendation, but limited by lack of cross-excitation and gaps in empirical/theoretical rigor.  
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses an important problem with a principled continuous-time model, convex scalable algorithm with guarantees, solid empirical gains, and clear exposition.  
- Cons for acceptance: Questionable quadratic-penalty exactness, limited modeling capacity (no cross-item excitation), insufficient baseline/evaluation detail, and reproducibility/computational reporting shortcomings.  
- Suggested improvements: adopt augmented Lagrangian/ADMM or rigorously justify Theorem 1 with practical ρ selection; add cross-item excitation experiments; clarify validation protocol and include ablations/sensitivity analyses; report runtime/memory and SVD details; release code.  
- Overall assessment: Good ideas and promising experiments but requires stronger theoretical justification, broader baselines, clearer evaluation/reproducibility, and extensions to cross-excitation to increase impact.
