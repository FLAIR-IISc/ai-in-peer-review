- Summary: Proposes modeling user-item interactions as low-rank Hawkes processes to recommend the right item at the right time and predict user return times via a convex formulation and a novel optimization algorithm.

- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation connecting temporal point processes and low-rank matrix models, with provable O(1/t) convergence.

- Strength: Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) showing superior performance versus strong baselines.

- Strength: Practical significance and scalability to millions of pairs and hundreds of millions of events, applicable to domains like music, e-commerce, and healthcare.

- Strength: Novel optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient to handle non-negativity and nuclear-norm regularization.

- Weakness: Limited comparison with recent tensor-based temporal recommendation work (e.g., newer Bayesian/probabilistic tensor factorization methods).

- Weakness: Missing ablation studies to separate contributions of low-rank structure versus Hawkes temporal modeling.

- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of failure modes (e.g., when low-rank assumption breaks or for very sparse users) despite claims about new-item generalization.

- Weakness: Lack of detailed parameter sensitivity analysis and practical guidance for hyperparameter tuning.

- Quality Assessment: Technically sound with rigorous convex formulation, convergence analysis, and convincing experimental validation.

- Clarity Assessment: Well-written and organized with clear math and algorithms, but the Hawkes–low-rank connection and parameter implications need clearer visualization/explanation.

- Originality Assessment: Novel linkage of self-exciting point processes with low-rank recommendation and a distinct optimization contribution, though comparison to recent temporal methods could be stronger.

- Significance Assessment: Addresses an important practical problem by jointly predicting item relevance and return times, with real-world scalability and broader optimization applicability.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance at NIPS based on novel theory, strong experiments, and practical relevance.

- Pros: Novel Hawkes + low-rank approach; rigorous theory with optimal convergence; strong empirical gains; scalable to large datasets; new optimization method.

- Cons: Limited recent-tensor comparisons; no ablation studies; limited failure-case discussion; insufficient parameter-sensitivity analysis.
