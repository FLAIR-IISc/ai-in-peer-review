- Proposes low-rank self-exciting Hawkes process framework for time-sensitive item recommendation and user return-time prediction.  
- Establishes a convex low-rank formulation linking Hawkes processes to low-rank models with theoretical guarantees.  
- Introduces an optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Demonstrates scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic data and real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) with strong results.  
- Positions correctly relative to Kapoor et al.; distinguishes from static matrix/tensor factorization approaches.  
- Should better contextualize with other temporal recommendation work (e.g., Xiong et al., Yi et al.).  
- The convex formulation (Eq.4) and theoretical analysis (Theorems 1–2) are novel and rigorous.  
- Algorithmic contribution effectively handles non-negativity plus nuclear-norm regularization and is reproducible.  
- Experimental comparisons with multiple baselines and appropriate metrics are thorough.  
- Omits comparison to recent deep-learning time-sensitive methods (RNNs, Transformers), a notable gap.  
- Lacks ablation studies to quantify contributions of low-rank structure, Hawkes modeling, kernel choice, and rank.  
- Relies on simplified assumptions (exponential kernel, low-rank, self-exciting behavior) without extensive justification.  
- Limited discussion of model interpretability, especially for healthcare (MIMIC II) insights.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-supported by theory and experiments, representing a complete research contribution.  
- Well-written and organized, though optimization details may be hard for non-experts.  
- Application of Hawkes processes with low-rank structure to recommendation is an original contribution.  
- Work is significant: fills an important gap in time-sensitive recommendation and has clear practical value.  
- Recommend adding comparisons to RNN/Transformer baselines for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Recommend performing ablation studies on model components, kernel types, and factorization rank.  
- Recommend detailing hyperparameter tuning (ρ, λ, β) and exploring alternative triggering kernels (Weibull, log-logistic).  
- Recommend deeper discussion of low-rank assumption limitations and potential extensions.  
- Recommend exploring interpretability and clinical insights for healthcare applications.  
- Final recommendation: accept — strengths substantially outweigh weaknesses.  
- Pro: novel model-theory-algorithm trio, strong empirical validation, scalability, practical relevance.  
- Con: limited deep-learning comparisons, missing ablations, simplified assumptions, limited interpretability discussion.
