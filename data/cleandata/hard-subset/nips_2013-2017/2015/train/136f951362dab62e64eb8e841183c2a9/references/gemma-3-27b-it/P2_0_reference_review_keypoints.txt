- Overall: Solid contribution combining temporal point processes and low-rank MF; lean Accept (7/10).
- Core idea: Model user activity as a Hawkes process with low-rank structure to recommend items at the right time and predict return times.
- Optimization: Convex formulation solved with a proximal-gradient + conditional-gradient algorithm; proven O(1/t) convergence.
- Relation to prior work: Distinguishes from survival/HSMM methods (Kapoor et al.) and standard CF; novel unification of Hawkes processes and low-rank models.
- Strength — Novelty: Unified, expressive, and generalizable framework for time-sensitive recommendation.
- Strength — Theory: Convex formulation and convergence analysis are solid contributions.
- Strength — Scalability: Demonstrated on large datasets (millions of pairs, hundreds of millions of events).
- Strength — Experiments: Comprehensive evaluation on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) with strong baselines.
- Strength — Motivation & writing: Clear application scenarios and mostly well-written exposition.
- Weakness — Accessibility: High technical density and heavy notation may deter non-experts.
- Weakness — Hyperparameters: Insufficient detail on tuning (ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth, rank).
- Weakness — Kernel choice: Limited justification/discussion of using the exponential kernel and alternatives.
- Weakness — Datasets: Need deeper discussion of dataset limitations/biases (e.g., sparsity, representativeness).
- Criteria assessment: High quality, original, and significant; clarity suffers somewhat from density.
- Acceptance arguments: Pro — strong theory, scalability, performance, and novelty; Con — accessibility, limited tuning/kernel/dataset details.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, hyperparameter transparency, and dataset discussion.
