## NIPS Review: Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel approach to time-sensitive recommendation by framing the problem as a low-rank Hawkes process. The authors aim to simultaneously predict both the relevance of an item at a given time *and* the next returning time of a user to a service. They present a convex formulation of this problem, a novel optimization algorithm blending proximal and conditional gradients with a theoretical convergence guarantee, and demonstrate strong performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The core idea of connecting Hawkes processes with low-rank models to address temporal dynamics in recommendation is interesting and potentially impactful.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank matrix factorization for time-sensitive recommendation is a genuinely novel contribution. Existing work often focuses on either predicting ratings or returning times in isolation, or uses methods that don’t generalize to new items.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The convex formulation of the problem and the convergence analysis of the proposed algorithm (O(1/t)) are significant strengths. Demonstrating theoretical guarantees is crucial for establishing the robustness of the approach.
* **Scalability:** The authors claim and demonstrate scalability to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs, hundreds of millions of events), which is a practical necessity for real-world applications.
* **Empirical Performance:** The experimental results on both synthetic and real datasets show that the proposed method outperforms several strong baselines, including Poisson processes, STiC, and tensor factorization methods.
* **Generality:** The framework is flexible and can incorporate additional contextual information (spatial, textual, etc.), making it adaptable to diverse recommendation scenarios.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the core concepts and methodology.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Implementation:** While the algorithm is theoretically sound, its implementation appears complex, involving multiple steps and parameter tuning. The practical hurdles to adoption might be significant for researchers without strong optimization expertise.  The authors could benefit from releasing code to facilitate reproducibility and encourage further research.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance seems heavily reliant on proper tuning of hyperparameters (ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth, rank). The sensitivity to these parameters is not thoroughly discussed, and the practical guidelines for setting them could be improved.
* **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** The comparison to recent, highly sophisticated deep learning-based recommendation systems is missing. While the baselines chosen are reasonable, it's important to contextualize the performance against the current state-of-the-art in recommendation.  Specifically, models leveraging attention mechanisms and sequential modeling are prevalent and could pose a stronger challenge.
* **Dataset Details:** More detailed information about the real-world datasets would be beneficial.  Specifically, the sparsity of the data, the distribution of events, and the characteristics of users and items are not fully described.
* **Justification of Nuclear Norm:** The use of the nuclear norm as a proxy for rank is standard, but a more detailed justification specific to this application would strengthen the paper.  Why is a low-rank structure a reasonable assumption in this context?

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a strong and original contribution to the field of time-sensitive recommendation. The technical soundness, theoretical guarantees, and empirical results demonstrate the potential of the proposed approach. While the complexity of implementation and limited comparison to more recent deep learning techniques are concerns, the strengths of the paper significantly outweigh the weaknesses.  This work is a valuable addition to the NIPS program and has the potential to influence future research in this area.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and well-motivated problem formulation.
* Strong theoretical foundation with convergence guarantees.
* Compelling empirical results demonstrating superior performance.
* Scalability to large datasets.
* Potential for broader impact in various recommendation scenarios.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Implementation complexity might limit adoption.
* Limited comparison to state-of-the-art deep learning methods.
* Hyperparameter sensitivity requires careful tuning.



**Rating:**  **8 / 10 (Strong Accept)**

I recommend accepting this paper to NIPS. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field, and the paper is of high quality and relevance to the NIPS audience. Addressing the minor weaknesses identified above (e.g., releasing code, discussing hyperparameter sensitivity, and providing more detailed dataset information) would further enhance the paper's impact.
