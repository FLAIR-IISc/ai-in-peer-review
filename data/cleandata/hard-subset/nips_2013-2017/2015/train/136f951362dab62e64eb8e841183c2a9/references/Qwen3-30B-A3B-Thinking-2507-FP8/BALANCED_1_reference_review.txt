

# Review of "Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities"

This paper proposes a novel approach to time-sensitive recommendations by modeling user-item interactions as low-rank Hawkes processes. The authors establish a connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models, developing a convex optimization framework that enables both item recommendation and returning-time prediction. The paper builds upon temporal point process theory (particularly Hawkes processes) and extends it to the recommendation setting, where most prior work focuses on static user-item preferences rather than temporal dynamics.

The paper makes several significant contributions. First, it introduces a principled low-rank formulation that captures both short-term (via triggering kernels) and long-term (via baseline intensity) user preferences. Second, it develops a novel optimization algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods, achieving the optimal O(1/t) convergence rate. Third, it demonstrates scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events, with strong empirical performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, and MIMIC II).

**Strengths:**
1. **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical foundation is solid, with clear mathematical formulations and rigorous convergence analysis (Theorem 2). The convex optimization approach is well-justified and addresses a key limitation in prior work (non-convex formulations).
2. **Novelty:** The connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendations represents a meaningful advance beyond existing work (e.g., [11, 12] which focus on returning time prediction but cannot generalize to new item recommendations).
3. **Empirical Rigor:** The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including synthetic data to validate model assumptions, real-world datasets across different domains, and thorough comparison with multiple strong baselines (Poisson, STiC, SVD, tensor factorization).
4. **Practical Impact:** The model's ability to scale to large datasets and provide both item recommendations and returning-time predictions addresses important practical needs in applications like personalized marketing and healthcare.

**Weaknesses:**
1. **Limited Discussion of Limitations:** While the paper acknowledges the need to tune parameters (ρ, λ, β), it doesn't thoroughly discuss the practical challenges of parameter selection in real-world settings. The cold-start problem for new users/items is also not addressed.
2. **Related Work Comparison:** The comparison with Kapoor et al. [11, 12] is somewhat superficial; a more detailed analysis of how their approach differs from the proposed method in terms of both modeling assumptions and practical applicability would strengthen the contribution claim.
3. **Ablation Studies:** The experimental section would benefit from more ablation studies to quantify the contribution of different components (e.g., the low-rank assumption versus the Hawkes process structure).

**Evaluation:**

*Quality:* The paper is technically sound with strong theoretical contributions and thorough empirical validation. The convex formulation and convergence analysis are particularly impressive. The main weakness is the lack of deeper analysis of model limitations.

*Clarity:* The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with sufficient background on temporal point processes. Some technical details (e.g., the exact implementation of the optimization algorithm) could be clarified for reproducibility.

*Originality:* The paper makes a clear and meaningful contribution by connecting Hawkes processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendations. The approach differs significantly from prior work that focuses on static preference prediction.

*Significance:* The problem addressed is highly relevant for modern recommendation systems, and the proposed approach advances the state-of-the-art in capturing temporal dynamics. The scalability to large datasets makes it practically valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Novel connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendations
- Theoretical convergence analysis with optimal rates
- Comprehensive empirical validation across multiple domains
- Practical scalability to large-scale real-world datasets

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
- Limited discussion of practical challenges in parameter tuning
- Insufficient ablation studies to isolate the contribution of different model components
- Cold-start problem for new users/items not addressed

**Recommendation:**
I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of time-sensitive recommendations with strong theoretical and empirical results. The main areas for improvement are adding more detailed discussion of limitations, particularly regarding cold-start scenarios, and including more ablation studies to better understand the contribution of different components. The authors should also provide a more thorough comparison with related work, especially in terms of practical applicability beyond just returning time prediction.

This work represents a strong contribution to the NIPS community, with clear potential for both theoretical impact (in point process modeling) and practical applications in recommendation systems. The authors have provided a compelling case for the importance of capturing temporal dynamics in recommendation systems, and their methodological advances are well-justified and rigorously evaluated.
