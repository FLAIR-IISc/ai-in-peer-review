- Tackles time-sensitive recommendation and user return-time prediction using a unified model.
- Models each user–item interaction as a self-exciting Hawkes process with exponential kernel and low-rank shared Λ0 and A matrices.
- Proposes a convex formulation minimizing negative log-likelihood with nuclear-norm penalties and non-negativity constraints.
- Introduces a penalized splitting formulation and a hybrid optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient (non-negativity) and Frank–Wolfe (nuclear norms).
- Proves convergence (O(1/t)) for the mixed algorithm and shows penalized problem is equivalent to constrained problem when ρ > ρ*.
- Empirical results show strong ranking and return-time performance on synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC II).
- Builds on Hawkes process and low-rank recommendation literature; novel integration of convex low-rank Hawkes with hybrid optimization.
- The equivalence theorem is limited in practice because ρ* depends on unknown optima and ρ is tuned empirically.
- Paper does not enforce or discuss Hawkes stability (branching ratio α·σ < 1), which is simple and convex to add.
- Model assumes independence across user–item pairs, missing cross-item/disease mutual excitation contrary to some motivations.
- Baselines for time-aware recommendation are limited (no TimeSVD++, FPMC, temporal Poisson factorization); return-time baselines could be broader.
- Runtime/memory and Monte Carlo thinning costs are underreported; scalability claims need concrete wall-clock/resource numbers.
- Paper is generally clear with good organization and pseudocode, but some notation, implementation details, and test-time history construction need clarification for reproducibility.
- Contributions are original and significant: interpretable convex model, scalable optimization, and joint scoring plus return-time densities.
- Pros: principled convex model, efficient hybrid algorithm with theory, strong empirical gains, scalable to many events.
- Cons: reliance on tuned ρ for equivalence, missing stability constraints, lack of cross-excitation modeling, weaker baselines and sparse runtime/reproducibility details.
- Suggested improvements: add stability constraints, give practical ρ selection/continuation strategy or bound, include stronger baselines and runtime/memory tables, run ablations (no excitation/low-rank/kernels), and release code.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — technically sound, novel, impactful but would benefit from addressing stability, ρ selection, baseline breadth, and reproducibility.
