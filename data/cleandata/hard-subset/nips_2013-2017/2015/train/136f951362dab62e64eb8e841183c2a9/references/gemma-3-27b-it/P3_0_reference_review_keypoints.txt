- Overall: Novel, technically sound approach bridging Hawkes point processes and low-rank models; recommend Accept with minor revisions.  
- Problem: Tackles time-sensitive recommendation by modeling recurrent user-item interactions and predicting next item and time-to-next interaction.  
- Novelty: New combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank modeling enabling generalization to new items.  
- Theoretical: Convex formulation with a well-defined optimization algorithm and O(1/t) convergence analysis.  
- Scalability: Algorithm designed for large-scale data; demonstrated on millions of user-item pairs.  
- Empirical: Strong results on synthetic and real datasets; ablation studies show benefits over Poisson, STiC, and tensor methods.  
- Generality: Model can potentially incorporate additional contextual signals (spatial, textual, etc.).  
- Writing: Paper is clear and well organized.  
- Related work: Correctly positions relative to Kapoor et al. and other point-process, tensor, and CF literature.  
- Kernel choice: Uses exponential kernel but lacks justification and exploration of alternatives (Weibull, log-logistic).  
- Hyperparameters: Insufficient analysis of sensitivity and guidance for tuning (ρ, λ, β, σ, rank).  
- Computational cost: Missing detailed runtime/complexity analysis and comparisons to existing methods.  
- Deployment: Lacks discussion of real-world challenges (cold-start, noisy data, online learning).  
- Interpretability: Does not explore interpretability of learned low-rank latent factors.  
- Pros: Technically solid, novel, scalable, empirically validated, and well-written.  
- Cons: Weak treatment of kernel choice, hyperparameter sensitivity, computational cost, deployment issues, and interpretability.  
- Revision suggestions: Justify/experiment with other kernels, add hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, include computational cost comparisons, discuss deployment challenges, and examine latent-factor interpretability.
