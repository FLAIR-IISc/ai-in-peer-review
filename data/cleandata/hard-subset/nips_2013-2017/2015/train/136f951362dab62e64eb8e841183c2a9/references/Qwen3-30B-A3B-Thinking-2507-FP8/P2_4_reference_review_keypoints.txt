- Problem: Tackles time-sensitive recommendation — recommending the right item at the right moment and predicting user return times.  
- Method: Models user activity with low-rank Hawkes processes, linking self-exciting point processes and low-rank matrix models.  
- Optimization: Proposes an efficient algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods to handle nuclear-norm and non-negativity constraints.  
- Theory: Provides a convex formulation with rigorous convergence analysis (Theorem 2) achieving optimal rates.  
- Experiments: Comprehensive evaluation on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) for item recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Baselines & Results: Compares to Poisson, STiC, SVD, tensor factorization and consistently outperforms them with statistical evidence.  
- Scalability: Claims scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Limitations acknowledged: Requires hyperparameter tuning (ρ, λ, β) and assumes specific triggering kernel form (exponential).  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written, with clear notation, background, pseudocode, and visualized results.  
- Originality: Novel connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank recommendation models; optimization approach is claimed first-of-its-kind.  
- Practical significance: High applicability across domains (music, e-commerce, healthcare) and ability to incorporate contextual features.  
- Strengths (summary): Novel modeling, strong theoretical foundations, well-designed experiments, clear relation to prior work, practical relevance, and scalability.  
- Weaknesses (summary): Weak justification for low-rank assumption, limited exploration of triggering kernels, insufficient hyperparameter-tuning details, and no explicit computational-complexity comparison.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-supported contribution meeting NIPS criteria in quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Recommendation: Accept — meaningful advance with solid theory, experiments, and practical implications.
