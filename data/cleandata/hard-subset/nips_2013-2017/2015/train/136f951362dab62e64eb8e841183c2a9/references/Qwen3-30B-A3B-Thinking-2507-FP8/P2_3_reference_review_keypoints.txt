- Proposes modeling user activities as low-rank Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation and next-return-time prediction.
- Establishes a novel connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models.
- Introduces a convex formulation and a new optimization algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.
- Provides a rigorous convergence analysis proving an O(1/t) convergence rate (Theorem 2).
- Demonstrates scalability and superior empirical performance on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II).
- Empirical comparisons include strong baselines (Poisson, STiC, SVD, tensor factorization).
- Theoretical analysis is solid but omits discussion of approximation error from the low-rank assumption.
- Lacks detailed computational complexity analysis beyond convergence rate.
- Ablation studies are insufficient to isolate the contributions of the low-rank structure versus the Hawkes process.
- Some technical explanations (Hawkes–low-rank link, penalty method, jargon) need clearer, more intuitive exposition for broader audiences.
- Novelty: first to combine Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix factorization and to use this convex/proximal+conditional gradient approach for the problem.
- Significant practical impact for personalized services, targeted advertising, and healthcare, and applicable to large-scale industrial systems.
- Optimization algorithm could be useful beyond recommendation tasks.
- Recommendation: accept — paper is technically sound, original, significant, and well-validated.
- Suggested improvements: add ablation study, discuss low-rank approximation error, clarify Hawkes–low-rank connection, and compare to recent Hawkes-based recommendation work.
