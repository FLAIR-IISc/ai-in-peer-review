- Tackles time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction by formulating a low-rank Hawkes process.
- Proposes a convex formulation and a novel optimization algorithm combining proximal and conditional gradient methods.
- Connects self-exciting point processes with low-rank models to enable transfer between observed and unobserved user-item pairs.
- Correctly positions the work in temporal recommendation literature and differentiates from Kapoor et al. by supporting new-item recommendation.
- Acknowledges relevant work in point processes and tensor factorization.
- Novelty: first to combine Hawkes processes with low-rank modeling for scalable time-sensitive recommendation.
- Theoretical foundation: convexity and O(1/t) convergence rate for the optimization algorithm.
- Scalability: algorithm shown to handle millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.
- Empirical validation: strong results on synthetic and real datasets with ablation studies supporting the Hawkes model benefit.
- Generality & extensibility: framework can incorporate additional contextual information (spatial, textual, categorical).
- Writing: generally clear and well organized.
- Complexity: dense mathematical presentation may be hard for non-experts to follow.
- Parameter sensitivity: performance likely depends on careful tuning of ρ, λ, and β, with limited discussion of selection.
- Real-world interpretation: lacks deeper explanation of practical recommendations produced for specific datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II).
- Limited SOTA comparison: STiC comparison restricted to return-time prediction rather than full recommendation quality.
- Kernel choice: reliance on exponential kernel without extensive exploration of alternatives.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, well-validated, scalable, and clearly written.
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to math complexity and insufficient parameter-tuning discussion.
- Overall assessment: strong accept; solid contribution with suggested clarifications; rating 8/10.
