- Overall assessment: Recommend Accept with minor revisions for a novel, technically sound low-rank Hawkes approach to time-sensitive recommendation.

- Summary: Models user activity as a self-exciting Hawkes process combined with low-rank matrix factorization to capture temporal dynamics and generalize to new items.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on temporal point processes and Hawkes literature and distinguishes itself by enabling recommendations for new items compared to Kapoor et al.

- Strength — Novelty: Novel combination of Hawkes processes with low-rank models to capture temporal dynamics and generalize across items.

- Strength — Technical soundness: Clear mathematical formulation with an efficient optimization algorithm (proximal + conditional gradient) and convergence analysis (Theorem 2).

- Strength — Experimental validation: Thorough experiments on synthetic and real datasets (Last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) showing strong performance and informative ablation studies.

- Strength — Clarity: Well-written and organized, making methodology and results accessible.

- Strength — Practical relevance: Direct applicability to recommendation and return-time prediction in web services and healthcare.

- Strength — Related work connection: Comprehensive literature review and clear positioning relative to existing approaches.

- Weakness — Kernel choice: Weak justification for using an exponential kernel; lacks discussion of alternatives (Weibull, log-logistic) and their impact.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Sparse details on hyperparameter tuning strategy and no sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness — Scalability analysis: Limited discussion of computational complexity and how runtime scales with users, items, and events.

- Weakness — Real data description: Insufficient details on preprocessing, dataset characteristics, and potential biases.

- Weakness — Baselines: Missing comparisons to more recent SOTA temporal/dynamic recommendation methods, especially deep learning approaches.

- Weakness — Practical deployment: No discussion of implementation complexity or challenges for real-world deployment and maintenance.

- For acceptance: Novel and sound approach, strong empirical results, solid theoretical foundation, clear writing, and high practical relevance.

- Against acceptance: Insufficient kernel justification, limited hyperparameter and complexity analysis, and could compare to newer SOTA methods.

- Question: Please justify the exponential kernel choice and discuss effects of alternative kernels.

- Question: Please detail hyperparameter tuning procedure and provide a sensitivity analysis.

- Question: Please provide computational complexity analysis including runtime vs. users/items/events.

- Question: Are there dataset biases or preprocessing choices that could affect results?

- Question: Can you compare against more recent SOTA temporal recommendation algorithms on the real datasets?

- Overall conclusion: High-quality contribution worthy of acceptance pending minor revisions addressing kernel choice, hyperparameter tuning, scalability, dataset details, and additional SOTA comparisons.
