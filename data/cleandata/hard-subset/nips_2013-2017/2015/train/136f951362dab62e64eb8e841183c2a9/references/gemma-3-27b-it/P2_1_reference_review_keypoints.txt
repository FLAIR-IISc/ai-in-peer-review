- Proposes framing time-sensitive recommendation as a low-rank Hawkes process to predict item relevance and user return time simultaneously.  
- Presents a convex formulation and a novel optimization algorithm combining proximal and conditional gradients with O(1/t) convergence.  
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs, hundreds of millions of events).  
- Empirically outperforms baselines (Poisson, STiC, tensor factorization) on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Framework is flexible and can incorporate contextual information (spatial, textual), aiding generality and new-item generalization.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains concepts and methodology.  
- Novelty: unique combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank matrix factorization for temporal recommendation.  
- Implementation complexity: multi-step algorithm and heavy parameter tuning may hinder practical adoption; releasing code recommended.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance depends on tuning (ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth, rank) with limited guidance provided.  
- Limited comparisons: missing evaluation against recent deep learning sequential/attention-based recommendation models.  
- Dataset description lacking: insufficient detail on sparsity, event distributions, and user/item characteristics.  
- Nuclear-norm use: needs stronger, application-specific justification for the low-rank assumption.  
- Overall: strong, original contribution with solid theory and empirical results; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- For acceptance: novel formulation, theoretical guarantees, strong empirical performance, scalability, and potential broad impact.  
- Against acceptance: implementation difficulty, limited SOTA comparisons, and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Rating: 8/10 (Strong Accept); recommends acceptance with minor revisions (release code, discuss hyperparameter sensitivity, provide dataset details).
