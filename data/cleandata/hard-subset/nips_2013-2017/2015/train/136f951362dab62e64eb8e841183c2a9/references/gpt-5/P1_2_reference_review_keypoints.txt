- Paper addresses joint time-sensitive item ranking and return-time prediction using per-dyad Hawkes processes with low-rank sharing via nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Introduces a convex formulation learning base intensity Λ0 and excitation A under nonnegativity and low-rank constraints.  
- Proposes a hybrid solver: proximal gradient for nonnegative block and conditional gradient (top singular vector LMO) for nuclear-norm block with acceleration.  
- Theoretical results: equivalence between penalized and constrained problems for sufficiently large ρ, and convergence rate O(1/t) (with O(1/t^2) term for the proximal block).  
- Empirical results: synthetic and three real datasets (last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC-II) show improved ranking and return-time prediction versus Poisson, tensor baselines, and STiC; QQ plots support Hawkes fit.  
- Novelty: interesting combination of Hawkes point processes and convex low-rank dyadic modeling; hybrid optimization is an effective instantiation of existing CG/prox ideas.  
- Strength: clear, unified problem formulation for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Strength: convex, identifiable linear-in-parameters Hawkes model with nuclear-norm sharing across dyads.  
- Strength: practical algorithm with cheap per-iteration operations (projections, top-SV) and supporting convergence analysis.  
- Weakness: Stability/branching ratio not discussed or enforced—no constraint ensuring Aσ < 1 to prevent Hawkes explosion.  
- Weakness: Equivalence theorem depends on unknown ρ*; no practical continuation or verifiable scheme provided.  
- Weakness: Missing important baselines—per-dyad Hawkes, factorized dyadic Hawkes, and mutually-exciting cross-item Hawkes—to isolate gains from self-excitation vs. low-rank sharing.  
- Weakness: Evaluation protocol does not test true cold-start (entirely new items/users); claims about future new-item generalization should be tempered or additional splits added.  
- Weakness: Return-time estimator via Ogata Monte Carlo may be expensive/variance-prone; alternatives (numerical quadrature, median) and cost/variance reporting are missing.  
- Clarity issues: tighten notation (α vs A), define vectors on first use, fix typos, and state per-iteration time/memory complexity and top-SV method/tolerances.  
- Reproducibility gaps: hyperparameter tuning details (λ, β, ρ, σ) and sensitivity analyses are not fully reported.  
- Originality caveat: modeling is less explored and valuable, but optimization approach is an effective application of related hybrid CG/prox methods rather than a fundamentally new algorithmic principle.  
- Suggested experiments/improvements: enforce/report stability constraints, add per-pair and factorized Hawkes baselines, ablation studies (only Λ0 low-rank, only A low-rank, A=0), test mutual excitation and side-information for cold-start, and provide a ρ-continuation recipe.  
- Recommendation: solid contribution with scalability and theory; leans toward acceptance pending revisions addressing stability, baselines, cold-start evaluation, and clarity.
