- Problem: Frames time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction as a low-rank Hawkes process to capture temporal dynamics beyond static preferences.  
- Method: Proposes a convex formulation connecting self-exciting point processes with low-rank models and an optimization algorithm mixing proximal and conditional gradient methods.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides solid theoretical foundation and O(1/t) convergence guarantee for the optimization algorithm.  
- Scalability: Algorithm scales to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs, hundreds of millions of events).  
- Empirical validation: Strong results on synthetic and real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) with ablation and held-out performance improvements over baselines.  
- Novelty: Novel combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank modeling for recommendation; addresses an important temporal aspect.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written with helpful background on temporal point processes, but some sections need clearer explanations.  
- Complexity: Model and algorithm are complex, potentially hindering reproducibility and adoption.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient detail on tuning (ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth, rank) and lack of analysis of parameter impacts.  
- Baseline comparisons: Some baselines are dated; missing comparisons to recent deep learning sequence-based recommendation models.  
- Real-world impact: Lacks concrete evidence of practical gains (e.g., CTR or engagement improvements) and deeper discussion of deployment implications.  
- Missing clarity in math: Certain equations (e.g., Eq. 6) lack contextual explanation of variables and derivation steps.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 4/5, Clarity 3.5/5, Originality 4/5, Significance 4/5.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—novel and technically sound but needs clearer exposition and more empirical/contextual analysis.  
- Suggested revisions: Provide detailed hyperparameter tuning procedures, compare to modern deep-learning baselines, expand discussion of real-world metrics and limitations, clarify equations/derivations, and add future work/limitations section.
