- Proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction using low-rank Hawkes processes.  
- Addresses recommending the most relevant item at the right moment and predicting users' next return time.  
- Establishes a connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models enabling efficient transfer learning across user-item pairs.  
- Introduces a new optimization algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with convergence guarantees.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II), outperforming state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Extends prior point-process recommendation work by enabling recommendations of new/unseen items via low-rank structure.  
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous convex formulation and proven convergence rates (Theorem 2).  
- Comprehensive experiments: compares to multiple baselines and evaluates both recommendation and returning-time prediction tasks.  
- Scalability: shows applicability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Adequate baseline comparison, including relevant recent works by Kapoor et al.  
- Novelty: first to connect self-exciting point processes with low-rank recommendation models.  
- Novel optimization method addressing nonnegativity in low-rank matrix estimation.  
- Problem formulation fills a gap by focusing on time-sensitive recommendations rather than static preferences.  
- Practical impact: applicable to music, e-commerce, and healthcare for timely personalized recommendations.  
- Broader applicability: optimization algorithm can generalize to other nonnegative low-rank problems with convex losses.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on both tasks across datasets, indicating significant advancement.  
- Weakness: limited discussion and exploration of the exponential triggering kernel assumption and alternatives.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for reproducibility and robustness.  
- Weakness: low-rank assumption lacks empirical justification on the real datasets used.  
- Clarity issue: connection between formulations (4) and (5) and practical determination of ρ* is under-explained.  
- Clarity issue: implementation details missing for Ogata's thinning algorithm (number of samples m).  
- Clarity issue: could more clearly differentiate the approach from recent NIPS temporal recommendation papers.  
- Recommendation: clarify relationship between formulations and how to estimate ρ* or implications of its choice.  
- Recommendation: expand discussion on kernel limitations and analyze alternatives’ impact on performance.  
- Recommendation: include hyperparameter sensitivity study and illustrate integration into real-world services.  
- Suggestion: add a comparison table contrasting key features (scalability, new-item recommendation, temporal handling) with baselines.  
- Overall assessment: strong technical contribution with solid theory, algorithm, and empirical results; recommend acceptance despite minor presentation and analysis gaps.
