- Overall assessment: Novel, well-motivated method combining self-exciting point processes and low-rank models with theoretical guarantees and strong empirical results; leans Strong Accept pending minor revisions.  
- Summary: Tackles time-sensitive recommendation by modeling event times with Hawkes processes and a low-rank formulation to generalize across user-item pairs; proposes an efficient optimization algorithm with provable O(1/t) convergence.  
- Related work: Properly contrasts with Kapoor et al. (ability to recommend new items), notes survival analysis limits for repeated events, and situates work relative to tensor factorization.  
- Strength — Novelty: Innovative combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank modeling to jointly predict items and return times.  
- Strength — Theoretical foundation: Provides a convex formulation and convergence guarantees for the optimization algorithm.  
- Strength — Scalability: Algorithm designed to scale; experiments show handling millions of pairs and hundreds of millions of events.  
- Strength — Empirical performance: Strong results on synthetic and real-world datasets versus solid baselines.  
- Strength — Practical relevance: Applicable to personalized services, marketing, and healthcare.  
- Strength — Extension potential: Framework can incorporate additional contextual information (spatial, textual, profiles).  
- Weakness — Readability: Paper is dense and notation-heavy, challenging for readers unfamiliar with temporal point processes.  
- Weakness — Algorithm complexity: Algorithm 1 and subroutines are compactly presented and could be more accessible; needs clearer pseudocode.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter tuning: Limited detail on tuning (ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth, rank) and sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness — Failure analysis: Lacks in-depth examination of cases where the model fails or performs poorly.  
- Qualitative — Quality: Technical contributions and experimental setup are excellent with strong scalability and convergence results.  
- Qualitative — Clarity: Logically structured but technical details are often compact and hard for non-experts.  
- Qualitative — Originality: High—novel methodological combination.  
- Qualitative — Significance: High—addresses an important, growing problem with broad applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Bridges two powerful techniques, strong theoretical guarantees, excellent empirical results, and addresses a relevant problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: High technical barrier, under-detailed algorithm description, and limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and failure modes.  
- Recommendations for authors: Improve readability with intuitive explanations and visuals, provide clearer/simplified pseudocode for Algorithm 1, detail hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity, analyze failure cases, and run ablation studies on the low-rank constraint and kernel choices.  
- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 8/10.  
- Final recommendation: Strong Accept (pending minor revisions on readability, algorithmic detail, and hyperparameter/failure analysis).
