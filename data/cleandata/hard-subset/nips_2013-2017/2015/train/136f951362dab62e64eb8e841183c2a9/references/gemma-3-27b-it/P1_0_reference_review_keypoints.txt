- Paper addresses time-sensitive recommendation by modeling recurrent user activities and links self-exciting Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix factorization.
- Authors propose an efficient optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with a provable convergence rate.
- Evaluations on synthetic and real datasets show superior performance in item recommendation and return-time prediction versus several baselines.
- Builds on temporal recommendation and Hawkes process literature, distinguishing itself by enabling recommendations of new items.
- The Hawkes–low-rank connection appears novel and provides a fresh perspective on modeling user behavior.
- Theoretical contributions include a convex formulation and rigorous convergence analysis, strengthening credibility.
- Method scales to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs, hundreds of millions of events).
- Empirical results outperform baselines such as Poisson processes, STiC, SVD, and tensor factorization.
- Paper is generally well-written and well-structured, with helpful figures illustrating the model.
- Practical relevance is strong (applications in ad bidding and proactive healthcare).
- The optimization algorithm and associated theory are dense and would benefit from a more intuitive explanation.
- The paper focuses mainly on the exponential kernel; more exploration of kernel choices and their impact is needed.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (ρ, λ, β) is not discussed; guidance for selecting them in practice is missing.
- More details about real-world datasets (size, sparsity, interaction definitions, e.g., Tmall) are needed for context.
- Comparison set omits recent state-of-the-art recommendation methods, especially deep learning approaches.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with convincing empirical results and practical impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: algorithmic complexity may hinder adoption, limited kernel exploration, and insufficient hyperparameter discussion.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor weaknesses that do not outweigh contributions.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: add an intuitive explanation of the optimization algorithm, discuss kernel choice impacts, provide hyperparameter selection guidance, include more dataset details, and compare to newer state-of-the-art methods.
