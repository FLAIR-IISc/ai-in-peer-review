## Review of "Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction by framing it as a low-rank Hawkes process. The authors aim to move beyond traditional rating-based recommendation systems and leverage the temporal dynamics of user interactions. They propose a convex formulation for this problem, develop a novel optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods, and demonstrate strong performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets.  The key idea is to connect self-exciting point processes with low-rank models, enabling knowledge transfer between observed and unobserved user-item pairs.

**Related Work:**

The authors correctly position their work within the emerging field of temporal recommendation systems. They accurately identify Kapoor et al.'s work [11, 12] as most closely related, and importantly highlight a crucial difference: their method can recommend *new* items, unlike the survival analysis and hidden semi-Markov model approach of Kapoor et al.  The paper also acknowledges relevant work in point processes and tensor factorization, providing a reasonable overview of the landscape.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank modeling for time-sensitive recommendation is a novel approach.  Connecting these two concepts allows for scalable and generalizable recommendations, addressing a key limitation of existing temporal models.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The convex formulation provides a strong theoretical underpinning for the proposed model. The analysis of the optimization algorithm, including the convergence rate, adds to the rigor of the work. The O(1/t) convergence rate is a strong result.
* **Scalability:** The algorithm is designed to scale to large datasets, demonstrated by experiments involving millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events. This is a crucial aspect for practical applicability.
* **Empirical Validation:** The extensive experiments on both synthetic and real datasets convincingly demonstrate the superior performance of the proposed method compared to several strong baselines (Poisson Process, STiC, SVD, Tensor Factorization). The ablation studies showing the benefits of the Hawkes model are particularly compelling.
* **Generality & Extensibility:** The authors highlight the potential for incorporating additional contextual information (spatial, textual, categorical) into the model, enhancing its flexibility.
* **Clear Writing (mostly):** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, with a clear explanation of the core concepts.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** While the theoretical foundation is strong, the paper is densely mathematical and could be difficult for readers unfamiliar with point processes and optimization techniques to fully grasp.  More intuitive explanations of certain concepts would be helpful.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance is likely sensitive to parameters such as ρ, λ, and β, which need careful tuning. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of how these parameters were chosen and the impact of different parameter settings.
* **Real-World Data Interpretation:** While the real-world experiments demonstrate good performance, the practical interpretation of the results in the context of each dataset (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) could be further elaborated. What specific recommendations would the model generate in each scenario?  
* **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** The comparison to STiC is limited to returning time prediction. While this is a fair comparison given the limitations of STiC, a more thorough evaluation of recommendation quality would be beneficial.
* **Kernel Choice:** While the authors justify the exponential kernel, exploring other kernel options and their impact on performance could strengthen the work.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem in recommendation systems.
* The strong theoretical foundation and empirical validation demonstrate the potential of the proposed method.
* The scalability of the algorithm makes it suitable for real-world applications.
* The clear writing and thorough related work section contribute to the overall quality of the paper.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The mathematical complexity might limit the accessibility of the paper to a broader audience.
* The parameter sensitivity and limited discussion of parameter tuning could be a concern.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept**. It presents a significant contribution to the field of time-sensitive recommendation systems. While the mathematical complexity and parameter sensitivity are valid concerns, the novelty, theoretical rigor, scalability, and strong empirical performance outweigh these weaknesses.  Addressing the suggestions regarding clarity and parameter tuning would further enhance the paper's impact.  This work has the potential to influence future research in this area and provide practical benefits for a wide range of applications.



**Rating:** 8/10 (Strong Accept)
