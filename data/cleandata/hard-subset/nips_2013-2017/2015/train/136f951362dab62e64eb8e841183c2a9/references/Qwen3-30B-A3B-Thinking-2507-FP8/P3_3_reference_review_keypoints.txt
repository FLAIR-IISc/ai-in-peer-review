- Proposes a novel framework modeling user-item interactions as low-rank Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation and returning-time prediction.  
- Establishes a convex optimization formulation linking self-exciting point processes to low-rank matrix models.  
- Introduces a new optimization algorithm (proximal + conditional gradient) with theoretical convergence guarantees (O(1/t)).  
- Theoretical contributions include proofs of equivalence between formulations and convergence analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II) shows strong performance on recommendation and return-time tasks.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate algorithm convergence and scalability characteristics.  
- Practical relevance is clear for domains like music streaming, e-commerce, and healthcare.  
- Limited justification for using an exponential triggering kernel over other parametric families (Weibull, log-logistic).  
- Scalability claims are overstated given modest real-world dataset sizes and limited synthetic-event scale.  
- Insufficient comparison with more recent Hawkes-based recommendation works (e.g., Zhou et al., Du et al. follow-ups).  
- Limited discussion of the low-rank assumption’s limitations for capturing complex, non-linear temporal patterns.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel, and practically relevant contribution.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested improvements on kernel justification, broader comparisons, and discussion of low-rank limitations.
