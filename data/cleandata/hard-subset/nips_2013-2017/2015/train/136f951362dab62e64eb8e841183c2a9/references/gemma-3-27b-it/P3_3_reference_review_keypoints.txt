- Overall assessment: Novel, technically sound approach connecting Hawkes processes with low-rank models; recommend acceptance.  
- Problem addressed: Models time-sensitive recommendations by capturing recurring user-item interaction patterns.  
- Core idea: Use Hawkes processes for self-exciting temporal dynamics with a low-rank intensity factorization for scalability.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Convex formulation solved by a scalable algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with convergence guarantees.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on prior temporal point process and recommendation work (e.g., Kapoor et al.) but extends to generalize to new items via low-rank modeling.  
- Novelty strength: Combining Hawkes processes and low-rank structure is a significant and principled contribution.  
- Technical soundness: Rigorous mathematical formulation, approximation for non-negativity constraints, and theoretical results (Theorems 1 and 2).  
- Algorithm efficiency: Designed for scalability and shown to handle large datasets (millions of pairs, hundreds of millions of events).  
- Empirical validation: Strong results on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) outperforming several baselines.  
- Clarity: Well-written, organized paper with clear motivations and illustrative figures.  
- Kernel choice weakness: Reliance on exponential kernel; limited exploration of alternative kernels (Weibull, log-logistic).  
- Hyperparameter tuning weakness: Limited detail on tuning process and sensitivity for ρ, λ, and β.  
- Computational cost weakness: Lacks detailed per-step computational complexity analysis of the proposed algorithm.  
- Baselines weakness: Limited comparison to recent deep learning–based recommender models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant technical contribution, rigorous analysis, strong empirical results, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited kernel exploration, sparse hyperparameter details, and lack of deep-learning baselines.  
- Final recommendation: Strong accept—weaknesses are minor and do not outweigh contributions.
