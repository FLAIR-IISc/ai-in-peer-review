- Proposal: Convex time-sensitive recommendation via univariate Hawkes per user–item with low-rank Λ0 and A, auxiliary variables + quadratic penalty, and a mixed proximal/conditional-gradient solver evaluated on synthetic and three real datasets.  
- Novelty/Relation: Combines Hawkes processes with nuclear-norm low-rank estimation and a mixed proximal/conditional-gradient optimizer — a novel convex low-rank Hawkes formulation.  
- Strength: Clear unified formulation linking intensity ranking and return-time prediction with convexity and global optimality (subject to penalty approximation).  
- Strength: Algorithmic contribution avoids repeated full SVDs using LMOs and top singular vectors, with an O(1/t) convergence proof for the composite scheme.  
- Strength: Sensible experimental protocol (held-out pairs, QQ plots) and scalability demonstrated; real-data results show consistent gains over several baselines.  
- Weakness: Independence assumption — no cross-excitation or competition among items, so substitution/co-consumption patterns are not modeled.  
- Weakness: Hawkes stability (branching ratio ασ < 1) is neither discussed nor enforced; potential for supercritical/exploding processes.  
- Weakness: Single scalar α per pair is simplistic; richer history features, marks, periodicities, or multiple kernels are not modeled.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1’s exact-penalty threshold ρ* depends on unknown optima, so it’s unusable in practice — sensitivity to ρ needs discussion.  
- Weakness: Convergence claims risk overstatement — O(1/t) proven for composite scheme; O(1/t^2) only in special (non-nuclear) cases and should not be implied for the mixed case.  
- Weakness: Missing computational details — no wall-clock comparisons to alternatives (factorized low-rank, randomized SVD), and memory/growth of atom representation for Z1,Z2 is not reported.  
- Weakness: Return-time prediction via Ogata thinning + Monte Carlo may be expensive; number of samples, per-query latency, and potential closed-form/efficient alternatives are not reported.  
- Weakness: Evaluation gaps — lacks standard ranking metrics (Recall@k, NDCG@k), omits strong sequential baselines and a critical low-rank Poisson-only ablation, and doesn’t report significance/variance.  
- Weakness: The claim of generalization to “any new item” is unclear — method cannot score globally unseen items without content/context features; clarify “new.”  
- Quality/Clarity: Generally well written but algorithms’ notation is terse, minor typos and inconsistent numbering remain, and per-iteration computational complexity/memory should be stated.  
- Originality/Significance: Novel and potentially impactful combination, but will be stronger with additional baselines, ablations, and stability/latency analysis.  
- Questions for authors: How is Hawkes stability enforced; any exploding intensities observed? What are runtime per iteration, total training time, rank growth and truncation policy? How many Monte Carlo samples and per-query latency for return-time prediction? Can you report Recall@k/NDCG and add a Poisson-only ablation and periodic/multi-kernel features?  
- Recommendation: Weak accept if major concerns (stability, ablations, computational/latency analysis) are addressed; otherwise borderline.
