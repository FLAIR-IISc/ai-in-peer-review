- Models time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction using per user-item Hawkes processes with low-rank baseline and excitation matrices.  
- Assumes intensities λu,i(t)=Λ0(u,i)+A(u,i)∑jγ(t,tu,ij) with nonnegative parameters and exponential kernel.  
- Enforces low-rank structure via convex nuclear-norm penalties and nonnegativity constraints to share information across unobserved pairs.  
- Introduces an auxiliary-variable formulation decoupling nuclear-norm regularization from nonnegativity.  
- Proposes a hybrid optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient (for nonnegativity) and conditional gradient/Frank–Wolfe (for nuclear norms).  
- Provides convergence analysis: O(1/t) for composite problem, O(1/t^2) when smooth/nonnegative only, and O(1/t) when only nuclear-norm regularization remains.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic data and three real datasets (last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC-II) shows improved item ranking and return-time MAE over Poisson, STiC, and factorization baselines.  
- Uses time-change theorem diagnostics to validate Hawkes vs. survival modeling assumptions on real sequences.  
- Demonstrates scalability to 100M events and millions of pairs, using only top singular vectors in the LMO.  
- Relates to prior Hawkes network learning and temporal factorization work but is distinct in convex low-rank, nonnegative per-pair estimation.  
- Strength: clear continuous-time formulation capturing short- and long-term effects with a principled convex approach.  
- Strength: efficient, scalable hybrid algorithm with nontrivial analysis and practical LMO usage.  
- Strength: solid experimental protocol with entirely held-out user-item sequences and strong baselines.  
- Weakness: stability/identifiability not addressed — no explicit constraint ensuring branching ratio A·∫γ < 1 to prevent explosion.  
- Weakness: equivalence theorem depends on ρ>ρ*, but ρ* depends on unknown optima; practical guidance on choosing ρ is lacking.  
- Weakness: model ignores cross-excitation between items, limiting applicability where substitution/complementarity matters.  
- Weakness: missing continuous-time Hawkes baselines (e.g., factorized nonconvex MF or nonparametric kernels) to isolate gains from Hawkes vs. convex estimation.  
- Weakness: return-time MC sampling details (number of Ogata samples m) and potential variance not reported.  
- Weakness: evaluation metrics limited to rank; missing precision@k/recall@k, time-weighted metrics, and calibration metrics like CRPS or interval coverage.  
- Weakness: notational inconsistencies, missing kernel parameter details, and unclear definitions (D1, D2, L); per-iteration complexity and memory not quantified.  
- Weakness: suggested extensions (context, tensors) are only sketched and not empirically tested.  
- Quality: technically sound optimization and convexity arguments but lacks stability discussion and more ablations/sensitivity analyses (ρ, λ, β, rank).  
- Clarity: readable overall but needs tightened notation, explicit complexity subsection, pseudocode with hyperparameters, and clearer data-split and sampling details.  
- Originality: novel combination of convex low-rank Hawkes modeling with nonnegativity and hybrid proximal/Frank–Wolfe optimization.  
- Significance: relevant to time-sensitive recommendation and healthcare; method and optimization approach likely useful in practice.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled continuous-time model, convex low-rank estimation with guarantees, scalable algorithm, and strong empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing stability constraints, equivalence theorem reliance on uncheckable ρ, limited continuous-time baselines, and reproducibility/clarity gaps.  
- Suggestions: add explicit stability constraints or empirical checks, sensitivity analyses for ρ/λ/β/m, include Hawkes MF or nonparametric baselines, report complexity/memory, and release code.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept (confidence high).
