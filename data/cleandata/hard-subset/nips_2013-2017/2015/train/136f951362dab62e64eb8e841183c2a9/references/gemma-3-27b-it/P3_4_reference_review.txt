## NIPS Review: Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling and technically sound approach to time-sensitive recommendation using a low-rank Hawkes process model. The core idea of connecting Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix factorization is novel and well-motivated. The proposed algorithm appears efficient and the experimental results on both synthetic and real datasets demonstrate its effectiveness. While the paper is well-written and thorough, some aspects could benefit from further clarification and a more critical discussion of limitations. I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper addresses the crucial problem of modeling temporally evolving user preferences for personalized recommendations and predicting user return times. It tackles this by framing user activity as a self-exciting Hawkes process, capturing the influence of past interactions on future ones. Crucially, the authors address the scalability issue by leveraging low-rank matrix factorization, allowing generalization to new items and efficient parameter estimation. This builds upon prior work in temporal point processes (Cox & Isham, Daley & Vere-Jones) and Hawkes processes (Hawkes, 1957), but importantly, distinguishes itself from recent work by Kapoor et al. (2014, 2015) by explicitly enabling recommendations for *new* items. The authors position their work effectively within the broader context of collaborative filtering (Koren, 2010), tensor factorization (Karatzoglou et al., 2010), and time-aware recommendation systems. 

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank models is a key contribution, providing a principled framework for capturing temporal dynamics and enabling generalization.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation is clear and well-motivated. The proposed optimization algorithm, blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods, appears sophisticated and efficient (demonstrated by the scalability results). The convergence analysis (Theorem 2) provides a solid theoretical foundation.
* **Experimental Validation:**  The thorough experimental evaluation on both synthetic and real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) convincingly demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method against several strong baselines. The ablation studies (Figures 2 & 3) are particularly insightful.
* **Clarity of Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the methodology and understand the results. The explanations of the theoretical concepts are accessible.
* **Practical Relevance:** The application to both recommendation and return-time prediction has direct practical implications for various web services, including personalized marketing and healthcare. 
* **Strong Connection to Related Work:** The paper provides a comprehensive overview of relevant literature and clearly articulates its contribution in relation to existing approaches.



**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Kernel Selection & Justification:** While the exponential kernel is used, the justification for this choice is relatively weak.  A more detailed discussion about alternative kernel choices (e.g., Weibull, Log-logistic) and their potential impact on performance would be beneficial.  Why is the exponential kernel most appropriate for these datasets?
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The authors mention tuning the hyperparameters (ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth, latent rank), but the details of the tuning process are sparse.  A more detailed explanation of the hyperparameter search strategy, and a sensitivity analysis, would strengthen the results.
* **Scalability Discussion:** While the results demonstrate good scalability, a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the algorithm (e.g., runtime as a function of number of users, items, and events) would be useful.  How does the performance scale with increasing data volume?
* **Real Data Limitations:**  The description of the real datasets is somewhat limited. More details about the data preprocessing steps, potential biases, and the characteristics of the users and items would be valuable.
* **Comparison with State-of-the-Art (SOTA) on Real Datasets:** While the comparisons are good, the paper could benefit from comparisons to more recent SOTA recommendation algorithms tailored for temporal dynamics, if available, on the real datasets.  Specifically, considering more recent deep learning-based approaches.
* **Practical Considerations:**  The implementation complexity of the proposed method is significant.  A discussion about the practical challenges of deploying and maintaining such a system in a real-world setting would be beneficial.



**4. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The convergence analysis provides a strong theoretical foundation.
* The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
* The problem is relevant and has significant practical implications.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The justification for the choice of the exponential kernel is weak.
* The details of the hyperparameter tuning process are limited.
* A more detailed analysis of the algorithm's computational complexity would be beneficial.




**5. Questions for the Authors:**

* Could you provide a more detailed justification for choosing the exponential kernel, and discuss the potential impact of alternative kernel choices on performance?
* Could you elaborate on the hyperparameter tuning process, and provide a sensitivity analysis demonstrating the impact of different hyperparameter settings on the results?
* Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the algorithm, including runtime as a function of the number of users, items, and events?
* Are there any potential biases in the real datasets that could affect the results?
* Could you compare your method against more recent SOTA recommendation algorithms tailored for temporal dynamics on the real datasets?

**Overall:**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of time-sensitive recommendation. The strengths outweigh the weaknesses, and I believe that the paper is worthy of acceptance at NIPS, pending minor revisions addressing the points raised above. The authors have presented a compelling and well-supported approach that is likely to have a lasting impact on the field.
