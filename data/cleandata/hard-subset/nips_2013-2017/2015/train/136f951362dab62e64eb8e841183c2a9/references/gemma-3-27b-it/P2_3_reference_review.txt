## Review of "Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of time-sensitive recommendation and predicting user returning times by framing it as a low-rank Hawkes process. The authors argue that traditional recommendation systems focus on static preferences and neglect the temporal dynamics of user behavior. They propose a convex formulation leveraging connections between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models, and develop an optimization algorithm blending proximal and conditional gradient methods.  The paper demonstrates the efficacy of their approach on both synthetic and real-world datasets, showcasing improvements over existing methods.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:**  The combination of Hawkes processes with low-rank models for recommendation is a novel and promising approach.  Specifically, addressing the time-sensitive aspect of recommendations with a probabilistic temporal model is a valuable contribution.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the proposed model, including a convex formulation and convergence analysis of the optimization algorithm. The O(1/t) convergence rate is a strong theoretical result.
* **Scalability:** The algorithm demonstrates scalability to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events), which is crucial for practical applications.
* **Empirical Validation:**  The experiments on both synthetic and real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to several baselines. The ablation study and analysis showing improved performance on held-out data are particularly convincing.
* **Clarity (Generally):**  The paper is generally well-written and organized. The background on temporal point processes is helpful for readers unfamiliar with the field.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** While theoretically sound, the proposed model and algorithm are complex.  This complexity makes it potentially difficult for others to implement and adapt.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions tuning parameters (ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth, rank) but doesn't provide sufficient detail on how this tuning was performed. The impact of these parameters on performance is not thoroughly analyzed.
* **Comparison to Strong Baselines:** While comparisons are made, some baselines (e.g., STiC, Tensor Factorization) feel somewhat dated or not state-of-the-art in their respective areas. A comparison against more recent and powerful recommendation algorithms would strengthen the paper.  Specifically, it's not clear how this compares to deep learning-based sequence modeling approaches which are now common in recommendation.
* **Limited Insight into Real-World Performance:** While the results on real-world datasets are positive, the paper lacks a deeper discussion of the practical implications. For example, how much improvement in click-through rate or user engagement does this model achieve in a real-world setting? A more concrete demonstration of real-world utility would be valuable.
* **Some Equations lack Context:** Certain equations, like (6), are presented without sufficient explanation of the variables involved, making it difficult to follow the derivation.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:**  The paper is technically sound and the claims are generally well-supported. The theoretical analysis is strong. However, the complexity of the approach and the limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity raise some concerns. **Score: 4/5**
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally clear, but some sections (particularly around the optimization algorithm) could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples. **Score: 3.5/5**
* **Originality:** The combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank models is a novel contribution. The paper adequately references related work, though potentially missing some recent advancements in sequence-based recommendation. **Score: 4/5**
* **Significance:** The problem addressed is important, and the proposed approach has the potential to significantly improve time-sensitive recommendation systems. The scalability and empirical results suggest that this work could have a real-world impact. **Score: 4/5**

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and theoretically sound approach to a challenging problem.
* Strong empirical results on both synthetic and real-world datasets.
* Demonstrated scalability to large-scale data.
* Solid theoretical guarantees on convergence.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Complexity of the model and algorithm.
* Limited discussion of hyperparameter tuning.
* Comparison to somewhat dated baselines.
* Lack of concrete demonstration of real-world impact.



**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions.  The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to time-sensitive recommendation.  While the complexity and lack of detailed analysis of hyperparameters are concerns, the strengths of the paper, particularly its originality, theoretical foundation, and empirical validation, outweigh these weaknesses. 

**Suggested Revisions:**

* Provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process.
* Compare against more recent and state-of-the-art recommendation algorithms, particularly deep learning-based sequence models.
*  Expand the discussion of real-world implications and potential applications.  Ideally, provide some evidence of how this model translates to improvements in practical metrics (e.g., click-through rate, user engagement).
* Clarify some of the equations and derivations, providing more context and explanation.
* Consider adding a section discussing the limitations of the proposed approach and potential directions for future research.
