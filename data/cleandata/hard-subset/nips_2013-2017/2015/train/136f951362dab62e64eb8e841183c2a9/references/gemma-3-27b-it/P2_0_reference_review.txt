## NIPS Review: Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities

**Overall Assessment:** I believe this paper presents a solid contribution to the field of time-sensitive recommendation, effectively bridging concepts from temporal point processes and low-rank matrix factorization. While the technical details are dense, the paper is generally well-written and supported by comprehensive experiments. I lean towards **Accept**, with a score of **7/10**.  The novelty lies in the unified framework and the efficient optimization algorithm for the specific problem.

**1. Summary & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper tackles the problem of time-sensitive recommendation by modeling user activity as a Hawkes process.  The core idea is to leverage the temporal dynamics of user-item interactions to both recommend items *at the right time* and predict *when* a user will return to a service. They introduce a low-rank constraint to facilitate generalization to new items, and develop an optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.  

The paper correctly positions itself within the existing literature. It acknowledges recent work by Kapoor et al. (2014, 2015) using survival analysis and hidden semi-Markov models, highlighting the limitations of those approaches regarding generalization to new items. It also distinguishes itself from traditional collaborative filtering techniques that don’t explicitly model temporal dynamics. The connection to Hawkes processes and low-rank models is a novel combination.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The unification of Hawkes processes and low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation is a strong point.  This provides a more expressive and generalizable framework than existing approaches.
* **Theoretical Soundness:**  The convex formulation of the problem and the convergence analysis of the proposed optimization algorithm are significant contributions.  The O(1/t) convergence rate is a desirable property.
* **Scalability:**  The authors demonstrate the algorithm’s ability to scale to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs, hundreds of millions of events), a crucial aspect for practical applications.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The experiments on both synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) provide convincing evidence of the effectiveness of the proposed method.  The comparisons with strong baselines (Poisson process, STiC, SVD, Tensor Factorization) are well-conducted.
* **Clear Motivation and Application Scenarios:** The introduction clearly articulates the importance of time-sensitive recommendation with compelling examples (personal assistants, electronic health records, ad bidding).
* **Well-Written (mostly):** Despite the technical complexity, the paper is mostly clearly written and logically organized.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Technical Density:** The paper is quite dense with mathematical notation, which could make it difficult for readers unfamiliar with temporal point processes to fully grasp the details. While understandable given the topic, improved exposition could broaden accessibility.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper mentions tuning parameters (ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth, rank), but the specific details of the tuning process are not fully explained.  More transparency here would be beneficial.
* **Limited Discussion of Kernel Choice:** While the exponential kernel is used, the justification for this choice, and a discussion of the impact of other kernel functions, could be expanded.
* **Real-World Datasets:** The choice of datasets, while varied, could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of their limitations and potential biases.  For example, a discussion of the sparsity of the interaction data in last.fm or the representativeness of the MIMIC II dataset would be valuable.



**4. Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical analysis (convex formulation, convergence proof) and experimental results strongly support the claims. It feels like a complete piece of work, rather than a preliminary study.
* **Clarity:**  While generally well-written, the technical density occasionally hinders clarity.  More intuitive explanations of the mathematical concepts would be helpful.
* **Originality:** The combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank models, along with the tailored optimization algorithm, demonstrates originality. The paper adequately references related work, placing itself within the existing literature.
* **Significance:**  The results are important. Time-sensitive recommendation is a crucial aspect of modern web services, and the proposed approach offers a significant improvement over existing methods.  The scalability of the algorithm further enhances its practical relevance. The framework could be adapted to various domains.




**5. Arguments For & Against Acceptance:**

**Pro-Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical foundations and a well-defined convex optimization problem.
* Demonstrated scalability and excellent performance on both synthetic and real datasets.
* Novel combination of Hawkes processes and low-rank models provides a powerful and generalizable framework.
* Clear motivation and relevance to real-world applications.

**Con-Acceptance:**

* The technical density might limit accessibility for some readers.
* Limited details on hyperparameter tuning and kernel choice.
* A more thorough discussion of the datasets' limitations would be beneficial.



**6. Recommendation:**

I recommend **Accepting** this paper for NIPS 2023. The strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses.  Addressing the minor suggestions regarding clarity, hyperparameter tuning, and dataset discussion would further strengthen the contribution.  This is a valuable addition to the literature on time-sensitive recommendation and has the potential to influence future research in this area.
