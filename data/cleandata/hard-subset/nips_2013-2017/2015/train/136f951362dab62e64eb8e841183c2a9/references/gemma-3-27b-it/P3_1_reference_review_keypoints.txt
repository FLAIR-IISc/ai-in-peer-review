- Novelty: First to combine Hawkes processes with low-rank formulation to generalize to unseen items.  
- Technical soundness: Rigorous formulation with well-explained derivations and convergence guarantees for the optimization algorithm.  
- Scalability: Demonstrated ability to scale to large datasets (millions of user-item pairs).  
- Empirical evaluation: Thorough experiments on synthetic and real datasets (Last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC II) outperforming several baselines.  
- Clarity: Well-written, clear organization and consistent mathematical notation.  
- Practical relevance: Addresses an important, applicable problem for web services, advertising, and healthcare.  
- Implementation complexity: Algorithm likely requires significant engineering effort; code release would aid reproducibility.  
- Kernel selection: Focuses on exponential kernel; needs more discussion and automated selection options.  
- Hyperparameter tuning: Insufficient detail on tuning strategies and sensitivity analysis.  
- Limited comparisons: Lacks benchmarks against recent deep learning sequential/recommendation models (e.g., RNNs with attention).  
- Originality assessment: Integrating existing components yields a fresh, meaningful contribution.  
- Significance assessment: Results are impactful due to predictive performance, scalability, and theoretical backing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach, strong theory, compelling empirical results, scalability, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Implementation difficulty, limited kernel/hyperparameter analysis, and missing recent deep-learning baselines (minor).
