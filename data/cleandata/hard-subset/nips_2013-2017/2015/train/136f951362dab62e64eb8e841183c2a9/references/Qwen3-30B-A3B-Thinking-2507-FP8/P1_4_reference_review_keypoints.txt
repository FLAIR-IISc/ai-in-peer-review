- Proposes modeling user-item interactions as low-rank self-exciting Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Connects temporal point processes with low-rank matrix models and formulates a convex optimization with nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Develops an efficient algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods with proven O(1/t) convergence (Theorem 2).  
- Provides rigorous convergence analysis and strong theoretical foundations.  
- Thorough experiments on synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II) showing superior performance to strong baselines.  
- Demonstrates scalability to large datasets (millions of pairs, hundreds of millions of events), indicating practical relevance.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; mathematical formulations are precise.  
- Could more intuitively explain why Hawkes processes suit recommendation and better motivate low-rank structure.  
- Original contribution: novel linkage of self-exciting point processes and low-rank models enabling generalization to new items and unified recommendation + return-time prediction.  
- Novel convex formulation and optimization algorithm that may interest other rank-minimization problems.  
- Strengths: theoretical rigor, novel modeling, practical scalability, unified task formulation, comprehensive experiments.  
- Weaknesses: does not address cold-start for new users/items.  
- Weaknesses: comparison with recent tensor-based recommendation methods could be more thorough.  
- Weaknesses: unclear how rank-based recommendations translate to real-world scenarios.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution with sound theory, novel efficient algorithm, and strong empirical results.  
- Reservations against acceptance: needs clearer intuitive explanation of Hawkes relevance, cold-start discussion, and broader comparisons.
