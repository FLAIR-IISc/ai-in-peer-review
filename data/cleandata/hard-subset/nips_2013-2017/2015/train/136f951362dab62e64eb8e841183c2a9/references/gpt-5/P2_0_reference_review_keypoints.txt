- Tackles time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction using per user–item Hawkes processes with exponential kernels and low-rank sharing via nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Frames estimation as a convex optimization: negative log-likelihood plus nuclear norms under nonnegativity constraints.  
- Proposes a penalty-based reformulation separating nonnegativity and nuclear-norm terms.  
- Designs an algorithm that mixes proximal gradient (nonnegativity) with conditional gradient/LMO (nuclear norms) to avoid full SVDs.  
- Provides convergence analysis combining O(1/k^2) and O(1/k) components, yielding overall O(1/k) rate.  
- Evaluates on synthetic and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC II), showing improvements over Poisson, SVD, STiC, and Poisson tensor baselines.  
- Uses time-change diagnostics to argue Hawkes dynamics better fit the data than i.i.d. gap models.  
- Novelty is moderate: combines known Hawkes and low-rank ideas with a useful proximal–Frank–Wolfe optimization twist in a recommendation setting.  
- Strength: clear motivation linking temporally varying preferences and return-time prediction, addressing an under-served problem.  
- Strength: sound, convex modeling where intensity is an affine nonnegative function of parameters.  
- Strength: practical algorithm avoiding full SVDs, using top singular vectors and simple projections, with interpretable convergence rates.  
- Strength: empirical evidence of scalability, convergence, and improved accuracy; framework can incorporate features/kernels (discussed).  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to stronger time-aware/sequential recommenders (timeSVD++, dynamic MF, RNNs) and Hawkes/multivariate baselines.  
- Weakness: limited modeling capacity — only dyadic self-excitation with a single exponential bandwidth; no cross-item/user excitation or heterogeneous kernels.  
- Weakness: penalty equivalence (Theorem 1) relies on unknown ρ*; practical guidance for choosing ρ or continuation strategies is lacking.  
- Weakness: key constants (Lipschitz/data dependence) and computational costs (top singular vectors, Ogata sampling) are not explicitly profiled or compared to ADMM/partial-SVD alternatives.  
- Weakness: evaluation uses nonstandard metrics (average rank) and omits Recall@k/NDCG, statistical tests, and sensitivity analyses (σ, sample size, hyperparameters).  
- Quality: technically sound and consistent with known rates; proofs deferred but claims plausible if Lipschitz/LMO conditions hold.  
- Clarity: generally clear and organized, though some notation is terse and algorithmic/implementation details and costs could be expanded.  
- Originality: moderate — useful application/combination but builds on established methods and related low-rank Hawkes work.  
- Significance: practically relevant and attractive to practitioners for convex, scalable time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction.  
- Suggestions: add stronger baselines (timeSVD++, dynamic MF, RNNs, multivariate Hawkes), report standard ranking metrics and statistical significance, provide runtime comparisons to ADMM/partial-SVD, perform ablations/sensitivity studies (σ, samples, ρ), and discuss identifiability/separability of Λ0 and A.  
- Acceptance pro: principled, convex, scalable method with theoretical guarantees and consistent empirical gains.  
- Acceptance con: lacks stronger baselines, limited modeling scope, and insufficient practical guidance on ρ and runtime/metric analyses.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — technically solid and relevant but needs stronger baselines and deeper empirical analysis to be a clear accept.
