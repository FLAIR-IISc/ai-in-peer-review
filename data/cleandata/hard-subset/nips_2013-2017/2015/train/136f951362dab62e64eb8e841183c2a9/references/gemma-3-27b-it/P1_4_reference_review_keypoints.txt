- Summary: Proposes a time-sensitive recommendation framework combining Hawkes processes with low-rank models, plus a proximal-conditional gradient algorithm for scalable parameter estimation and strong empirical results.

- Relation to prior work: Correctly positions itself against Kapoor et al., highlighting the ability to recommend new items and bridging temporal point processes with collaborative filtering and survival analysis.

- Novelty: Introduces a significant contribution by integrating Hawkes temporal dynamics with low-rank recommendation models.

- Theoretical foundation: Provides a convex formulation and proves O(1/t) convergence for the learning algorithm.

- Scalability: Demonstrates scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events, supporting real-world applicability.

- Empirical validation: Shows consistent outperformance of baselines on synthetic and real datasets, with informative ablation studies.

- Generality: Model can incorporate contextual information (spatial, textual, user profiles), increasing versatility.

- Clear writing: Paper is well-written and organized, making core ideas accessible.

- Kernel selection weakness: Insufficient justification for choosing the exponential kernel and limited discussion of kernel impact on performance.

- Parameter tuning weakness: Lacks systematic analysis of sensitivity to regularization parameters (λ, β, ρ).

- Dataset detail weakness: Real-world dataset descriptions and preprocessing details are brief, limiting reproducibility and understanding of dataset characteristics.

- Comparison depth weakness: Comparison to state-of-the-art temporal/sequence recommendation methods (e.g., RNNs, attention models) is limited.

- Practical challenges weakness: Little discussion of deployment issues like cold-start or computational costs for real-time prediction.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, technically sound approach with convincing theory, experiments, scalability, and high potential impact in domains like personalization and healthcare.

- Arguments against acceptance: Needs deeper exploration of parameter/kernal sensitivity, stronger comparisons to sequential recommendation SOTA, and discussion of deployment challenges.

- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable contribution; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing parameter sensitivity, additional SOTA comparisons, and deployment considerations.
