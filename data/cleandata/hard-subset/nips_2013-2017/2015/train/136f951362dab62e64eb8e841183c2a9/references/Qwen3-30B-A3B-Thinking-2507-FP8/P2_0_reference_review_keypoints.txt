- Proposes modeling user-item interactions as low-rank Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation.  
- Develops a convex formulation to predict next relevant item and next user return time using low-rank base intensity and self-exciting matrices.  
- Introduces a novel optimization algorithm combining proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: convexity (Theorem 1) and O(1/k) convergence rate (Theorem 2).  
- Evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets (Last.fm, Tmall, MIMIC II), showing consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Validates the Hawkes model assumption using the time-change theorem, showing better fit than survival analysis approaches.  
- Experimental design is comprehensive and results convincingly demonstrate parameter recovery and practical utility.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear mathematical exposition, good figures, and reproducible algorithm descriptions.  
- Suggests briefly justifying the choice of an exponential triggering kernel versus alternatives for trade-offs in efficiency and flexibility.  
- Original contribution: novel connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank matrix models with nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Differentiates from prior work by enabling generalization to new items and offering a convex low-rank Hawkes formulation.  
- High practical significance for recommendations, advertising, and healthcare, with demonstrated scalability to large user-item spaces.  
- Strengths: theoretical foundation, efficient scalable algorithm, comprehensive experiments, and clear writing.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparison to recent deep learning temporal recommendation methods.  
- Weaknesses: real-world dataset scales are modest (e.g., ~1,000 users on Last.fm).  
- Weaknesses: could include more analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for NeurIPS based on strong technical quality, novelty, and practical relevance.
