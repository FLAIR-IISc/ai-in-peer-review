- Summary: Tackles time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction by modeling each user–item pair as a low-rank, nonnegative Hawkes process estimated via convex nuclear-norm regularization.
- Summary: Introduces a hybrid projected proximal-gradient + conditional-gradient (Frank–Wolfe) algorithm with a quadratic-penalty reformulation to avoid full SVDs, proves O(1/k) convergence, and shows scalability to ~100M events with improved ranking and MAE on real datasets.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on Hawkes processes, convex MLE, nuclear-norm low-rank surrogates, and Frank–Wolfe methods; differs from survival and prior low-rank Hawkes/network work by focusing on per-pair self-excitation and generalization to unseen pairs.

- Strength: Principled convex formulation (for exponential kernels) with nonnegativity and nuclear-norm regularization.
- Strength: Practical algorithm avoiding repeated full SVDs, dominated by top singular vectors and gradients, with an O(1/k) convergence guarantee.
- Strength: Unified framework for both intensity-based ranking and return-time prediction.
- Strength: Demonstrated scalability (synthetic 100M events) and reasonable real-dataset sizes.
- Strength: Diagnostic checks (time-rescaling) indicate Hawkes fits better than simple survival models.
- Strength: Clear extension paths (contextual features, nonparametric kernels, tensorization).

- Weakness: Theorem 1’s quadratic-penalty exactness is questionable since exact penalty thresholds depend on unknown optima; needs clarification or alternative (e.g., augmented Lagrangian/ADMM) with guarantees.
- Weakness: No stability/branching constraints (A σ < 1) are enforced or discussed, risking explosive/ill-posed intensities.
- Weakness: Model only captures self-excitation per pair and ignores cross-excitation (e.g., item-to-item or user-level interactions).
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to recent dynamic Poisson factorization/dynamic MF and Hawkes-based recommender baselines.
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics are limited (only average rank); lacks Hit@k/Recall@k/MRR and held-out log-likelihood/KS tests.
- Weakness: Return-time prediction via Ogata thinning at test time may be computationally expensive; alternatives or cost analysis needed.
- Weakness: Hyperparameter tuning, search ranges, sensitivity, stopping criteria, and wall-clock times are insufficiently detailed for reproducibility.

- Quality: Technically solid and practical, but exact-penalty theory needs clarification and experiments could use stronger baselines and stability analysis.
- Clarity: Well-written and organized with clear optimization section and pseudocode; notation and dataset/run-time details could be tightened.
- Originality: Novel combination of low-rank per-pair Hawkes modeling with convex estimation and a hybrid prox/Frank–Wolfe solver; algorithmic blend is incremental but useful.
- Significance: Valuable for time-sensitive recommendation and return-time prediction if theory and empirical comparisons are strengthened.

- Key question: Please clarify Theorem 1’s exactness conditions, practicality of choosing ρ, and whether ADMM/augmented Lagrangian was considered.
- Key question: Do you enforce/monitor stability (A σ < 1); what fraction of pairs violate it at solution?
- Key question: Report per-iteration costs, typical iterations, and wall-clock times on real datasets.
- Key question: How sensitive are results to σ and to choice of exponential vs. other kernels?
- Key question: Can you report Hit@k and held-out log-likelihood and add dynamic Poisson factorization baselines?

- Pros for acceptance: Convex, scalable method with convergence guarantees; unified treatment of recommendation and return-time; strong multi-dataset performance; practical algorithm avoiding full SVDs.
- Cons for acceptance: Unconvincing penalty exactness claim; missing stability constraints/analysis; weaker baselines and metrics; limited modeling of cross-excitation.

- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept pending clarification of penalty theory, stability enforcement, and stronger empirical comparisons/metrics.
