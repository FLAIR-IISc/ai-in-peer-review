

# Review of "Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities"

This paper proposes a novel approach to time-sensitive recommendation by modeling user-item interactions as low-rank Hawkes processes. The authors address two key problems: recommending relevant items at the right moment and predicting user returning times. They establish a connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models, formulating the problem as a convex optimization task with nuclear norm regularization. The authors develop an efficient optimization algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods, achieving an optimal O(1/t) convergence rate. The approach is evaluated on synthetic data and three real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II medical dataset), demonstrating superior performance over baselines including Poisson processes, STiC, SVD, and tensor factorization methods.

The paper builds upon recent work on temporal recommendation, particularly the STiC approach by Kapoor et al. [11, 12], which focuses on returning time prediction but cannot generalize to recommend new items. The authors correctly identify this limitation and address it through their low-rank Hawkes process formulation, which allows for generalization to unseen user-item pairs while modeling temporal dynamics.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach with strong theoretical foundation. The convex formulation (Equation 4) properly handles the low-rank constraint using nuclear norm regularization. The convergence analysis (Theorem 2) is rigorous and demonstrates optimal convergence rates, which is a significant contribution. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering both synthetic and real-world datasets with appropriate baselines. The authors provide sufficient detail for reproducibility, including parameter settings and implementation details.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The background on temporal point processes (Section 2) is concise and sufficient for readers unfamiliar with the topic. The mathematical formulations (Equations 2-5) are well-explained, and the algorithm descriptions (Algorithms 1-3) are precise. The experimental setup and results are presented logically, with Figures 2-3 effectively illustrating key findings. The paper would benefit from slightly more detailed discussion of the triggering kernel choice (Section 3.3) and the rationale for the low-rank assumption.

**Originality:** The paper makes a clear contribution by connecting self-exciting point processes with low-rank models for time-sensitive recommendation. The convex formulation with nuclear norm regularization for low-rank Hawkes processes appears novel. The optimization algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods (Algorithm 1) is a significant technical contribution. The authors properly contextualize their work against related literature, particularly highlighting the limitations of Kapoor et al.'s approach [11, 12] in the context of recommendation to new items. The paper is sufficiently distinct from previous NIPS work, including recent papers on temporal recommendation.

**Significance:** The paper addresses an important gap in recommendation systems literature: moving beyond static preference prediction to time-sensitive recommendations. The applications to personalized services, health monitoring, and marketing are compelling and well-motivated. The results demonstrate clear improvements over baselines on both synthetic and real-world datasets. The scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events makes the approach practically relevant for modern web services. The model's ability to incorporate contextual information (Section 3.3) further enhances its applicability.

**Strengths:**
- Novel convex formulation connecting Hawkes processes with low-rank models
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with convergence rate guarantees
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation on multiple datasets
- Efficient optimization algorithm with optimal convergence rate
- Clear practical motivation and applications

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited discussion of the theoretical justification for the low-rank assumption
- Comparison with some baselines (e.g., SVD) is somewhat superficial as SVD is not designed for time-sensitive prediction
- The paper doesn't thoroughly explore potential limitations of the approach (e.g., sensitivity to kernel bandwidth)
- The connection to recent NIPS work on temporal recommendation could be more explicitly contrasted

**Arguments for acceptance:**
- The paper presents a technically sound and novel approach to time-sensitive recommendation
- The theoretical contributions (convex formulation, convergence analysis) are strong
- The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the approach's effectiveness
- The work has clear potential for impact in both academia and industry

**Arguments against acceptance:**
- The low-rank assumption justification could be stronger
- Some baseline comparisons seem less relevant for the specific problem
- The paper could better address potential limitations of the approach

Overall, this paper makes a strong contribution to the field of recommendation systems by addressing an important gap in time-sensitive recommendations through a novel convex formulation and efficient optimization algorithm. The technical quality is high, the evaluation is thorough, and the significance of the work is clear. While there are minor areas for improvement in the justification of assumptions and baseline comparisons, these do not undermine the paper's overall contribution. The work is well-suited for NIPS as it presents a novel technical approach to an important problem with strong theoretical and empirical results.
