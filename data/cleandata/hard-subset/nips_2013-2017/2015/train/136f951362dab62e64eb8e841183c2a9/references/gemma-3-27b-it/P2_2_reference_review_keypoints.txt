- Overall: Novel, technically sound approach combining Hawkes processes and low-rank factorization for time-sensitive recommendation; recommend Accept with minor revisions.  
- Summary: Models user actions as a Hawkes process to predict next item and return time, using low-rank approximations for scalability and contrasting with prior survival-analysis work.  
- Novelty: The coupling of Hawkes processes with low-rank matrix factorization and a convex formulation is a novel contribution.  
- Technical soundness: Convex formulation and hybrid optimization (proximal + conditional gradient) are well-motivated with convergence analysis.  
- Scalability: Low-rank approximation addresses scalability of Hawkes processes and experiments demonstrate effective scaling.  
- Empirical validation: Strong results on synthetic and real datasets, consistently outperforming baselines including tensor factorization.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and figures (e.g., Fig. 1a) aid motivation and explanation.  
- Generalizability: Authors note potential to incorporate contextual information, indicating extensibility.  
- Practical-significance weakness: The real-world impact of the measured improvements (e.g., ad bidding or engagement gains) is not clearly quantified.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: No analysis of sensitivity to tuning parameters (ρ, λ, β, bandwidth, rank) is provided.  
- Algorithm complexity: Paper lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis beyond empirical scaling and convergence rate.  
- Kernel choice: Focus is primarily on the exponential kernel without justification or discussion of alternative kernels’ tradeoffs.  
- Evaluation summary: Quality strong, clarity good, originality and significance good but could emphasize practical implications more.  
- Pros: Novel, scalable, well-evaluated, clearly presented, and theoretically rigorous.  
- Cons: Missing concrete quantification of practical benefits, limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and insufficient complexity discussion.  
- Recommendation/actions: Accept with minor revisions—expand discussion of practical implications, address hyperparameter sensitivity, and add a more detailed complexity analysis.
