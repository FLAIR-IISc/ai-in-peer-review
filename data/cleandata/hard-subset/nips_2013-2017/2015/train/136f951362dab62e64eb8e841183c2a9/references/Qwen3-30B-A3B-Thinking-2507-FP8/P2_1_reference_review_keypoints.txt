- Proposes low-rank Hawkes-process framework to make time-sensitive recommendations by modeling self-exciting user behavior.  
- Uses low-rank intensity matrices to enable efficient generalization across user-item pairs and recommend new items.  
- Introduces an optimization algorithm that combines proximal and conditional gradient methods with optimal O(1/t) convergence.  
- Provides a convex formulation and proves equivalence to the original model (Theorem 1).  
- Delivers strong convergence analysis and theoretical guarantees (Theorem 2).  
- Demonstrates superior performance on item recommendation and return-time prediction in synthetic and real datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II).  
- Shows empirical scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of temporal events.  
- Clearly differentiates from related work (e.g., Kapoor et al.) by enabling generalization to new items.  
- Technical quality is high with rigorous justification and solid theoretical foundations.  
- Novelty is high by connecting low-rank matrix models with Hawkes processes for temporal recommendations.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, robust to sparsity, and compares to multiple relevant baselines.  
- Practical significance is high with clear applications in personalization, marketing, and healthcare.  
- Weakness: limited discussion on handling extremely sparse users and consequences when low-rank assumption fails.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to choice of triggering kernel (exponential vs. others) is not fully explored.  
- Weakness: related work coverage could include more recent multidimensional Hawkes literature (e.g., Zhou et al.).  
- Weakness: lacks concrete implementation details—training time, memory requirements, and runtime comparisons on largest datasets.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-written contribution with minor addressable weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Justification: excels in quality, clarity, originality, and significance; minor limitations do not outweigh contributions.
