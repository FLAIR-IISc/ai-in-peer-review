

# Review of "Time-Sensitive Recommendation From Recurrent User Activities"

This paper proposes a novel framework for time-sensitive recommendation by modeling user activities as low-rank Hawkes processes. The authors address the gap in existing recommendation systems that primarily focus on one-time user preferences rather than time-varying preferences. They establish a connection between self-exciting point processes and low-rank models, developing a convex formulation with nuclear norm regularization to capture temporal dynamics. The paper introduces an efficient optimization algorithm that blends proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods, achieving optimal O(1/t) convergence. The approach enables both item recommendation (by ranking items based on intensity functions) and returning-time prediction (via sampling from predicted intensity functions). The work differs from related approaches like Kapoor et al. [12,11], which use survival analysis but cannot generalize to new items, and from standard matrix factorization methods that ignore temporal dynamics.

**Quality:** The paper presents technically sound work with a clear theoretical foundation. The convex formulation with nuclear norm regularization is well-motivated for handling the large-scale nature of the problem. The optimization algorithm effectively combines proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods, with rigorous convergence analysis proving the optimal O(1/t) rate. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, featuring both synthetic data (with controlled ground-truth) and three real-world datasets (last.fm, tmall.com, MIMIC II). The authors carefully compare against multiple relevant baselines (Poisson process, STiC, SVD, tensor factorization) and provide thorough ablation studies on convergence and scalability. The paper acknowledges limitations, such as the need for hyperparameter tuning, and demonstrates robust performance across different settings.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-explained, with sufficient detail for reproducibility. Key concepts (temporal point processes, Hawkes processes, low-rank modeling) are introduced gradually with appropriate examples. The figures (particularly Figure 1, 2, and 3) effectively illustrate the model, algorithm convergence, and experimental results. The writing maintains a good balance between technical depth and accessibility for the NIPS audience.

**Originality:** The paper makes a significant contribution by connecting low-rank matrix factorization with Hawkes processes for time-sensitive recommendation. While Hawkes processes have been used in other contexts (e.g., Du et al. [7,8]), their application to recommendation systems with low-rank constraints appears novel. The authors clearly differentiate their approach from related work, particularly noting that Kapoor et al. [12,11] cannot generalize to new items, which is a critical limitation for recommendation systems. The convex formulation with nuclear norm regularization and the combined optimization algorithm represent novel technical contributions. The paper demonstrates how their approach differs from previous methods through both theoretical analysis and empirical results.

**Significance:** The work addresses an important practical problem in recommendation systems: delivering personalized experiences at the right moment. The ability to predict returning times has clear applications in marketing (e.g., optimizing ad bidding) and healthcare (e.g., predicting disease progression). The model's scalability to millions of user-item pairs and hundreds of millions of events demonstrates practical relevance. The paper shows that temporal dynamics significantly improve performance over static baselines, with consistent gains across multiple datasets and tasks. The framework's flexibility to incorporate contextual information (spatial, textual, etc.) further enhances its practical utility.

**Strengths:**
- Novel connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank matrix factorization for recommendation
- Rigorous theoretical foundation with convex formulation and convergence analysis
- Effective optimization algorithm achieving optimal convergence rates
- Comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets
- Clear demonstration of practical significance across multiple domains

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited discussion of why low-rank assumption is particularly appropriate beyond computational convenience
- Comparison with recent deep learning approaches to temporal recommendation (e.g., RNN-based methods) would strengthen the evaluation
- Hyperparameter tuning details could be more explicit
- The theoretical analysis focuses on exponential kernels but doesn't explore generalization to other kernel forms

**Recommendation:** The paper makes a strong contribution to the field of recommendation systems by addressing an important gap in modeling time-sensitive user behavior. The technical approach is sound, the experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results demonstrate clear improvements over relevant baselines. The connection between Hawkes processes and low-rank matrix factorization is novel and well-motivated. While the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with recent deep learning approaches to temporal recommendation, the current evaluation is sufficiently comprehensive. The work has significant potential for both theoretical impact and practical application in recommendation systems. I recommend acceptance.
