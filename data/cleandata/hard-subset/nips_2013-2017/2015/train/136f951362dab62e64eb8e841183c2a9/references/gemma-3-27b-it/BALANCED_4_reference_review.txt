This paper proposes a novel approach to time-sensitive recommendation by framing the problem as a low-rank Hawkes process. The core idea is to model user activity as a self-exciting point process and leverage low-rank approximations to generalize across user-item pairs. The authors address both item recommendation and returning-time prediction, providing a convex formulation and an efficient optimization algorithm blending proximal gradient and conditional gradient methods. This builds upon and significantly extends recent work on temporal point processes for recommendation, specifically addressing the limitations of existing methods like those by Kapoor et al. which cannot generalize to new items. The paper is well-motivated, addressing the limitations of traditional recommendation systems that often ignore the temporal dynamics of user preferences.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The connection between low-rank models and self-exciting point processes is a novel and promising approach to time-sensitive recommendation. This allows for generalization to unseen user-item pairs, a key limitation of prior work.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The convex formulation and convergence analysis of the proposed algorithm provide a solid theoretical foundation for the method. The O(1/t) convergence rate is a strong result.
* **Scalability:** The algorithm is designed to scale to large datasets with millions of user-item pairs and events, as demonstrated in the experimental results.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The paper presents a thorough experimental evaluation on both synthetic and real datasets, comparing the proposed method to several state-of-the-art baselines.  The analysis regarding the appropriateness of the Hawkes model compared to simpler survival analysis is particularly insightful.
* **Clear Writing:**  The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the core ideas and technical details. The motivation is well presented.
* **Practical Relevance:** The problem addressed is practically relevant, with applications in various domains like mobile recommendations and healthcare.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Implementation:** While the algorithm is theoretically efficient, the implementation appears complex, involving multiple steps and parameter tuning. This could be a barrier to adoption. The description of the algorithms 1-3 and the associated theorems, while rigorous, contribute to this complexity.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance of the algorithm may be sensitive to the choice of parameters (e.g., ρ, λ, β, kernel bandwidth). The paper does not provide extensive guidance on how to tune these parameters effectively. While stated they are tuned for best performance, the process is not detailed.
* **Kernel Choice:** The paper primarily focuses on the exponential kernel. Exploring other kernel functions (e.g., Weibull, Log-logistic) and their impact on performance would strengthen the analysis.  While mentioned as a possibility, it isn’t explored empirically.
* **Limited Baselines:** While several baselines are included, a more comprehensive comparison with recent deep learning-based approaches for sequential recommendation (e.g., RNNs, Transformers) could provide a more nuanced understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed method.
* **Real-World Dataset Size:** While the real-world datasets are used, the size seems relatively modest (e.g., 100K shopping events). Evaluating the algorithm on even larger, more challenging datasets would increase confidence in its scalability and performance.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to time-sensitive recommendation.
* The experimental results demonstrate the superior performance and scalability of the proposed method.
* The paper addresses a practically relevant problem with potential applications in various domains.
* The work significantly extends existing research on temporal point processes for recommendation.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The implementation complexity and parameter sensitivity could limit the practical applicability of the method.
* The comparison with more recent deep learning-based approaches is missing.
* The real-world dataset size might be insufficient to fully demonstrate the scalability and robustness of the algorithm.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of time-sensitive recommendation. The novelty of the approach, the solid theoretical foundation, and the comprehensive experimental evaluation make it a valuable addition to the NIPS conference. While there are some weaknesses related to implementation complexity and the comparison with more recent deep learning models, these do not outweigh the strengths of the paper. 

**Recommendation:**

Accept.  I would encourage the authors to consider including a section in the appendix that provides more detailed guidance on parameter tuning and potentially exploring the impact of different kernel functions. A brief discussion of how the proposed method could be integrated with or compared to recent deep learning-based sequential recommendation models would also be beneficial.
