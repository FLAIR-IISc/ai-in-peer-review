- Proposes a time-sensitive recommendation model using per-pair Hawkes processes with shared low-rank base-intensity and self-excitation matrices (Λ0 and A) regularized by nuclear norms.  
- Casts learning as a convex optimization: minimize negative Hawkes log-likelihood plus nuclear-norm penalties under nonnegativity constraints.  
- Introduces a penalty reformulation with auxiliary variables that decouples nonnegativity and nuclear norms for scalable optimization.  
- Develops an accelerated mixed algorithm combining proximal gradient (nonnegativity block) and conditional gradient/Frank-Wolfe (nuclear-norm block) with closed-form line search.  
- Proves equivalence of the reformulation to the original problem for sufficiently large ρ and establishes an O(1/t) convergence rate (recovering O(1/t^2) when nuclear term vanishes).  
- Empirical results on synthetic and three real datasets (last.fm, tmall, MIMIC-II) show improved item ranking and next-return-time prediction versus several baselines.  
- Situates work at intersection of temporal point processes and low-rank recommendation, differing by a convex low-rank Hawkes matrix approach and mixed proximal/Frank-Wolfe solver.  
- Strength: principled convex formulation enabling transfer to unobserved user–item pairs via low-rank structure.  
- Strength: sensible penalty reformulation and efficient algorithmic components (simple projections, low-cost LMOs, closed-form scaling).  
- Strength: convergence results align with known optimal rates and synthetic experiments demonstrate recovery and scalability.  
- Weakness: equivalence theorem depends on unknown ρ* with no constructive guidance, sensitivity analysis, or continuation scheme provided.  
- Weakness: no discussion or enforcement of Hawkes stability/subcriticality (branching ratio), risking supercritical/explosive dynamics.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited—only rank of ground-truth item reported; missing top-k metrics, stronger baselines, runtime comparisons, and extensive ablations.  
- Weakness: return-time estimation uses Ogata sampling without discussion of variance or computational cost; analytical alternatives for exponential kernels not explored.  
- Clarity issues: notational slips, missing per-iteration complexity and gradient-computation details, and sparse hyperparameter/data-split descriptions hindering reproducibility.  
- Originality: moderately high—novel application of low-rank Hawkes to recommendation with a clean convex formulation and mixed solver.  
- Significance: addresses important problem of timely recommendations with a scalable convex approach, though empirical claims would be stronger with broader metrics and runtime baselines.  
- Pros summary: principled convex model, efficient algorithm with theory, good empirical performance, and extensible framework.  
- Cons summary: non-constructive penalty requirement, missing stability constraints, limited evaluation/baselines, and scant cold-start discussion.  
- Key questions for authors: how is ρ chosen or scheduled; is subcriticality enforced or observed; per-iteration/wall-clock costs vs ADMM or other solvers; sensitivity to kernel bandwidth and regularizers; cold-start performance?  
- Minor suggestions: report top-k metrics and calibration, include ablations on ρ and rank, and release code or detailed implementation notes.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — technically sound and promising but needs stronger evaluation, stability discussion, and practical guidance on ρ.
