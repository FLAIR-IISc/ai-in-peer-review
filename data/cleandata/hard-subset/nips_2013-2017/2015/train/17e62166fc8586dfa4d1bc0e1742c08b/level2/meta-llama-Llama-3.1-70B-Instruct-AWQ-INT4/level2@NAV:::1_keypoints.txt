- Proposes CRCN, a multimodal model combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model to retrieve sentence sequences for image streams.  
- Extends single-image-to-sentence generation to sequence-to-sequence retrieval for image streams.  
- Trained on a large blog-post-with-images dataset and reportedly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Evaluation uses BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall, median rank, and AMT user studies, showing CRCN’s superiority.  
- Provides qualitative examples that illustrate improved sentence-sequence retrieval.  
- Demonstrates practical applications in image captioning, visual storytelling, and HCI.  
- Shows strong knowledge of related multimodal, NLP, and vision literature and positions the work relative to prior methods.  
- The CRCN architecture is novel in its integration of CNNs, BiRNNs, and entity-based coherence.  
- Paper gives sufficient implementation, training, and evaluation details and discusses results and limitations.  
- Limitations include reliance on a large blog-image dataset and potential difficulty generalizing to other domains.  
- Suggests exploring CRCN on other domains (e.g., video captioning, multimodal dialogue) and additional metrics like ROUGE or F1.  
- Pros: novel integrated approach, strong quantitative and user-study results, and practical applicability; Cons: dataset dependence, uncertain generalization, and possibly incomplete evaluation metrics.
