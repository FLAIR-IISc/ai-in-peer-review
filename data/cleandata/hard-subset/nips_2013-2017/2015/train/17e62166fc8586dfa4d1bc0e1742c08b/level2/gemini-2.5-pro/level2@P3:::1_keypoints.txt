- Introduces the novel task of captioning image streams with a sequence of sentences, extending single-image captioning to a sequence-to-sequence setting.  
- Proposes a retrieval-based Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that ranks entire candidate sentence sequences for an image stream.  
- CRCN integrates a CNN for image features, a Bidirectional RNN to model sentence sequence flow, and an entity-based local coherence model for smooth transitions.  
- Uses paragraph vectors to represent individual sentences and a max-margin objective to train the ranking model.  
- Builds a large dataset of image-text streams from blog posts, acknowledging noise from heuristic alignment and summarization.  
- High technical quality: sensible architecture that targets both image relevance and paragraph-level coherence.  
- Training methodology (max-margin) and experimental setup are appropriate and rigorous.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: extensive baselines, ablation study, automatic metrics, and human evaluation supporting the claims.  
- Clarity is generally good, but the local coherence model’s description needs more intuition and detail.  
- The use of paragraph vectors for sentence representation is non-obvious and requires brief justification.  
- Paper should more clearly distinguish retrieval-based composition from word-by-word sentence generation.  
- Highly original and significant: first strong baseline for image-stream captioning and a valuable dataset contribution.  
- Strong arguments for acceptance: novel problem formulation, well-designed technical contribution, convincing evaluation, and high potential impact.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze the beam-search inference approximation (sensitivity to beam size), discuss data pre-processing noise/limitations, and comment on generalizability beyond tourism datasets.
