- Proposes CRCN, a multimodal model (CNN + BiRNN + entity-based local coherence) to generate sentence sequences for image streams, trained on a large blog-image dataset and evaluated quantitatively and via user studies.  
- Novel problem: extends image captioning to generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams.  
- Novel architecture: combines convolutional networks, bidirectional recurrent networks, and entity-based local coherence in a new way.  
- Large-scale evaluation: trained and tested on a big dataset of blog posts with images and assessed with automatic metrics and user studies.  
- Improved performance: Reports outperforming several state-of-the-art methods on quantitative measures and user evaluations.  
- High model complexity: CRCN is computationally heavy and requires large data and resources to train.  
- Limited generalizability: Trained on a specific blog-image dataset, raising questions about cross-domain transfer.  
- Lack of interpretability: No detailed analysis of the individual contributions of CRCN components to overall performance.  
- Limited comparisons: Evaluation contrasts the model with only a few baselines, leaving broader relative performance unclear.  
- Originality/significance: Presents a creative, significant extension of captioning work with a new multimodal model.  
- Clarity and organization: Paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with helpful figures and tables.  
- Writing quality: Clear, concise writing with good grammar and spelling.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel problem and model, and demonstrated improvements in performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Practical adoption concerns due to complexity, uncertain generalizability, and limited interpretability.  
- Revision recommendations: Provide ablation/analysis of component contributions, compare to more state-of-the-art methods, and discuss real-world deployment limitations.
