- Problem: Generates coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams (e.g., trip photo streams).  
- Core contribution: Proposes CRCN combining CNN image features, bidirectional RNN language modeling, and a local coherence model.  
- Training data: Leverages blog posts with images as a large, relevant training corpus.  
- Evaluation: Shows improvements over baselines using BLEU/Recall metrics and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.  
- Motivation: Well-motivated extension of image captioning to maintain inter-sentence coherence across image sequences.  
- Novelty: Integration of a local coherence model into a recurrent convolutional architecture is a key innovation.  
- Experimental setup: Commended for combining automatic metrics and human evaluation and for clearly articulating task challenges.  
- Data preprocessing concern: Description of text segmentation, summarization, and image-to-text block assignment is cursory and needs more detail.  
- Summarization choice: Use of LSA for text summarization is stated without justification versus newer neural methods.  
- Architecture detail missing: Rationale for separating forward/backward RNN input activations (xft and xbt) lacks theoretical or intuitive explanation.  
- Coherence model detail missing: Entity grid construction and the 64-dimensional coherence representation are vaguely described and need illustrative examples.  
- Ablation study: Evaluation lacks a comprehensive ablation analysis removing components (e.g., BRNN) to quantify individual contributions.  
- Metric concerns: Some metric improvements (e.g., BLEU) are small; discussion of BLEU’s limitations for this task could be expanded.  
- Related work: Could cite recent discourse-aware neural generation papers relevant to coherence modeling.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-designed contribution with clear strengths; suggested revisions (more detail and ablations) would strengthen the paper.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel problem, strong architecture with coherence modeling, empirical improvements, and large dataset.  
- Rejection arguments: Insufficient explanations of preprocessing/architecture, limited ablation study, and modest metric gains in places.
