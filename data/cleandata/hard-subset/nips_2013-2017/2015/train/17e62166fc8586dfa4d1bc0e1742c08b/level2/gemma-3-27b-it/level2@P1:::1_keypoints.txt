- Paper extends image captioning to generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams (CRCN).
- Motivation is clear and relatable: describing sequences of event photos rather than single images.
- CRCN architecture combines CNNs, BRNNs, and a local coherence model to produce fluent multi-sentence outputs.
- Use of a local coherence/entity-grid model is a valuable addition to address cross-sentence coherence.
- Training data comes from blog posts (140K images, 20K posts), a clever way to obtain image-sequence pairs.
- Evaluation uses both automatic metrics (BLEU, Top-K recall) and human AMT studies for comprehensive assessment.
- Writing is clear and explanations of methods and experiments are easy to follow.
- Novel direction: focusing on image streams is a meaningful and underexplored contribution.
- Integration of existing components into this new context is original and practically useful.
- Results demonstrate strong performance and potential for applications like travelogue generation and accessibility.
- Weak baseline comparisons: lacks direct comparison to sequential/video-captioning methods.
- Coherence model description is brief; more detail on entity-grid construction and the 64-dim choice is needed.
- Data augmentation strategy (top-κ sentences, κ=3) appears ad-hoc and insufficiently justified.
- Score function (Eq. 3) lacks intuition and discussion of weighting between CNN and RNN outputs.
- Paper under-discusses failure cases and limitations; more analysis of common errors is needed.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—strengths and significance outweigh the listed weaknesses.
