- Proposes sequence-to-sequence generation from image streams via a multimodal Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN).  
- CRCN integrates CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Uses blog posts (image sequences with text) as large-scale, parallel training data.  
- Addresses a novel problem beyond single-image captioning and distinguishes itself from video captioning.  
- Demonstrates empirical gains over state-of-the-art baselines, especially on retrieval metrics and user preference for longer sequences.  
- Evaluation is thorough: quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall, median rank) plus AMT user studies.  
- Provides detailed ablation studies (CRCN vs. RCN) validating the coherence model’s contribution.  
- Acknowledges limitations of standard language metrics for paragraph-level coherence.  
- Paper is generally clear, well-motivated (good examples) and supported by helpful figures and experimental details.  
- Clarity could improve: the integration between the coherence model and the BRNN needs more explicit explanation.  
- Methodological detail needed on the 64-dimensional transition descriptor and why it effectively captures coherence.  
- Suggests more comprehensive evaluation on longer sequences (beyond primarily 5-image user studies).  
- Needs justification for hyperparameter choice (data augmentation κ=3) with more insight into selection.  
- Should include discussion of computational complexity and training/inference time vs. baselines (VGGNet features used).  
- Overall assessment: well-designed, significant, novel, thoroughly evaluated work that should be accepted for presentation.
