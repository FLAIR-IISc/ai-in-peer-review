Summary
The paper tackles the problem of generating a coherent sequence of sentences for an input stream of images, motivated by real-world photo streams in blogs and travelogues. The proposed coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) integrates: (i) CNN image features (VGG fc7), (ii) a bidirectional RNN over paragraph-vector (doc2vec) embeddings of candidate sentences to capture content flow, and (iii) an entity-grid coherence model (Barzilay & Lapata) to encourage locally coherent sentence transitions. Training uses a max-margin objective on aligned image- and sentence-sequences mined from blogs via paragraph-based segmentation and LSA-based summarization (top-κ sentences per image). At test time, the system retrieves a small set of candidate sentences per image via nearest-neighbor retrieval, and performs an approximate divide-and-conquer beam search to select a best sequence under the learned compatibility score. Experiments on two domains (NYC and Disneyland blogs; ~20K posts, ~140K images) show improvements over adapted single-image captioning baselines in automatic metrics (BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, recall@K/median rank) and user studies (AMT), with ablations indicating benefits from both BRNN and coherence components, especially for longer sequences.

Relation to prior work
The work extends image captioning and multimodal matching/embedding (e.g., Karpathy & Fei-Fei; Kiros et al.; Mao et al.; Vinyals et al.) to sequence inputs/outputs. It is also related to video-to-sentence models (e.g., Donahue et al.) but differs by explicitly modeling sentence-level coherence and by handling unordered/abrupt changes across images typical of photo streams. It is complementary to image-sequence retrieval from paragraph queries (Kim et al. CVPR’15) but addresses the reverse mapping. The integration of an entity-grid coherence model with a multimodal embedding for retrieval is, to my knowledge, novel in this setting.

Strengths
- Problem formulation is timely and practically relevant; moving beyond single-image/single-sentence is a meaningful step.
- CRCN is a simple, modular combination of known components; the entity-grid based coherence term is a clear, interpretable addition.
- Sensible training objective that handles sequence alignment with negative sampling by replacing either images or text sequences.
- Scalable candidate generation via nearest neighbors and a pragmatic approximate search; ablations (1NN vs RCN vs CRCN) are informative.
- Both automatic metrics and AMT user studies are provided; AMT analysis on longer sequences underscores the value of coherence modeling.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Evaluation clarity: It is unclear how retrieval metrics (R@K, median rank) are computed when the ground-truth test sentences are not present in the training pool. Please clarify the retrieval protocol (candidate pool composition, what constitutes “ground truth,” and whether permutations of the gold sequence are used to probe coherence sensitivity).
- Fairness of comparison: The proposed method is retrieval-based, whereas some baselines (CNN+RNN/LSTM) are generative. Retrieval can enjoy an advantage in n-gram metrics when training and test domains match. A stronger retrieval baseline that uses only visual similarity + simple sentence bigram/lexical cohesion (or skip-thought/sentence2vec smoothing) would better isolate the effect of the coherence model.
- “End-to-end”/“unsupervised” claims are overstated. The pipeline relies on heavy preprocessing (LSA summarization, doc2vec training, coreference parsing), and the learning is supervised on image–(summarized) sentence pairs induced by heuristics. Please temper the claims and discuss the noise introduced by these steps.
- Coherence modeling details and robustness: The entity grid requires reliable coreference; automatic coref on noisy blog text is error-prone. There is no analysis of error rates or an ablation showing the impact of disabling coreference (using surface noun phrase matching) or varying the history parameter.
- Candidate selection is decoupled from the learned joint model (K-NN in visual space only). This can prune away good text candidates that the learned score would favor. Using the learned similarity to select neighbors (or a two-stage retrieval: broad visual NN followed by cross-modal reranking per position) would be more faithful to the model.
- Potential data leakage: doc2vec representations appear to be trained on the full corpus per domain (including test posts). Even if unsupervised, this can bias evaluation. Similarly, near-duplicate images/posts across splits could inflate retrieval performance. Please ensure and report deduplication and train-only fitting of doc2vec.
- Limited analysis of when coherence helps: Automatic gains are small; the paper asserts larger AMT gains for longer sequences but does not provide statistical significance or failure cases.

Quality
Technically sound overall; the objective and model components are standard and appropriately combined. However, some derivation/notation is confusing: g is a global text coherence vector but is dot-multiplied with every image v_t and summed over t, effectively repeating the same term across positions—please justify this design and discuss alternatives (e.g., modeling adjacency potentials explicitly). Training/optimization details are adequate; search is plausible but its approximation quality is not quantified.

Clarity
The paper is generally readable and well-structured. Important ambiguities remain in evaluation protocol, treatment of doc2vec/coreference, and the exact dimensionalities/mappings (e.g., zero-padding coherence to 300 dims, then to 4096). Figures and tables (not included here) presumably help; please strengthen the methodology and evaluation sections with precise definitions and ablations.

Originality
Moderately high. The problem setting (image stream → sentence sequence) and the integration of entity-grid coherence with multimodal sequence scoring are novel in the captioning literature circa 2015.

Significance
Potentially significant for applications like travelogue summarization and story-like captioning, and for pushing the community toward discourse-aware multimodal models. The empirical gains are encouraging, but stronger baselines and clearer evaluation would increase confidence.

Suggestions for improvement
- Precisely define the retrieval metrics protocol and report statistical significance for AMT studies.
- Add retrieval baselines with simple sentence-level coherence (e.g., adjacent sentence cosine similarity, entity overlap) and reranking without the entity grid, to isolate the contribution of your coherence component.
- Use the learned cross-modal score to guide candidate selection, or expand the candidate set beyond strict visual K-NN.
- Provide ablations: with/without coreference; varying history length; replacing doc2vec with learned sentence encoders; sensitivity to κ for summarization.
- Ensure no train–test leakage in doc2vec and deduplicate images/posts across splits; report these controls.
- Release data/preprocessing code and clarify whether VGG is fine-tuned.

Pros for acceptance
- Novel, relevant problem and a coherent model that explicitly addresses discourse.
- Solid ablations and user studies that suggest real gains for longer sequences.
- Scalable and practical approach using widely available blog data.

Cons for acceptance
- Ambiguities in evaluation protocol and potential fairness issues.
- Overstated “end-to-end/unsupervised” claims and limited analysis of coherence robustness.
- Decoupled candidate generation may limit the model’s potential.

Overall, a promising direction; with clarified and strengthened evaluation and analysis, the paper would make a solid contribution.
