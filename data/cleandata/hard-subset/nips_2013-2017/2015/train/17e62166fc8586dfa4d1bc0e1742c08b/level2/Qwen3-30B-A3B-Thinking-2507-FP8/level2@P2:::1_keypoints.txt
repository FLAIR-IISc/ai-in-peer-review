- Proposes CRCN: a model combining CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model to generate sentence sequences from image streams.  
- Trains on blog posts (NYC & Disneyland datasets; ~20K posts, ~140K images) using LSA-based summarization to extract image–sentence pairs.  
- Well-motivated problem extending single-image captioning and video captioning to multi-image, multi-sentence generation.  
- CRCN architecture is well-motivated, integrating existing techniques with a novel coherence component.  
- Experiments use quantitative metrics (BLEU, METEOR, CIDEr, top-K recall) and human evaluation via AMT.  
- Results show clear improvements over baselines, especially in retrieval metrics and human judgments for longer sequences; ablations confirm coherence model’s contribution.  
- Claim of being “the first” is not fully substantiated; related prior work (e.g., multi-image description, video captioning) needs clearer differentiation.  
- Blog preprocessing and summarization heuristics are ad-hoc and under-justified; potential biases from LSA summarization vs. manual annotation are not explored.  
- Common sentence-level metrics are limited for paragraph/coherence evaluation; human studies focus on short sequences where coherence is less critical.  
- Insufficient detail and analysis for longer-sequence AMT evaluations to fully validate claimed coherence benefits.  
- Lacks examples and discussion of failure cases, limiting insight into model limitations and future directions.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound; training/evaluation follow standard practices.  
- Significance is reasonable (practical scenario, unsupervised blog training), but advancement over simple extensions of existing work should be more clearly argued.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify novelty, justify preprocessing, and add failure-case analysis for camera-ready.
