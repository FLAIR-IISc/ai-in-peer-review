- Proposes CRCN, a novel model that generates coherent sentence sequences from image streams by extending captioning to sequence-to-sequence.
- CRCN integrates CNNs for images, bidirectional RNNs for sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model for smooth transitions.
- Key contribution: extending both input and output from single images/sentences to streams, motivated by real-world multi-photo behavior.
- Extensive experiments on two large-scale blog datasets (NYC and Disneyland; ~20K posts, 140K images).
- Outperforms strong baselines (Karpathy, Kiros, Vinyals) on BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall, and human evaluations.
- Ablation studies confirm the coherence module’s value; CRCN beats RCN (no coherence), especially in retrieval metrics and longer sequences.
- User studies (AMT) show coherence importance increases with sequence length.
- Paper demonstrates strong literature positioning, clear method description, and reproducibility.
- Main limitation: insufficient analysis of blog-data biases and limited evidence for generalizability beyond tourism domains despite unsupervised claims.
- Overall assessment: well-motivated, novel, thoroughly evaluated work; recommendation for acceptance.
