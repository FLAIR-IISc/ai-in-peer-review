- Summary: Introduces the task of describing an image stream with coherent sentence sequences and proposes a retrieval-based Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN, BRNN, and an entity-based coherence model, evaluated on a large weakly-supervised blog dataset.

- Originality & significance: Tackles a novel, well-motivated extension from single-image captioning to image streams, framing a new research direction with strong baseline potential.

- Technical approach: CRCN is well-designed, combining content modeling (BRNN) with an entity-based coherence model to enforce narrative transitions across sentences.

- Empirical evaluation: Thorough experiments, ablations (RCN, 1NN), and human studies show clear improvements and that coherence gains increase with sequence length.

- Resource contribution: Presents a practical pipeline for collecting and processing a large-scale weakly-supervised dataset from blog posts for this new task.

- Clarify "generation" vs retrieval: The paper is retrieval-based but uses generative wording; the authors should explicitly state this distinction (and consider a clearer title).

- Inference scalability concerns: KNN retrieval across the full dataset and combinatorial search may not scale; discuss complexity and propose approximate/efficient alternatives.

- Data preprocessing noise: Heuristic-based alignment and summarization may introduce noisy labels; assess or discuss the impact of label noise on learning.

- Model combination clarity: The description of combining BRNN outputs and coherence features is terse; expand the formulation and justify shared projection parameters.

- Conclusion & recommendation: Solid, well-executed contribution with minor clarity and scalability issues; recommended for acceptance.
