- Proposes CRCN, a multimodal model to retrieve sequences of natural sentences for image streams, extending single-image captioning to a realistic sequential setting.
- CRCN integrates CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model to enforce discourse-level consistency.
- Model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with images collected for the task.
- Evaluated via quantitative metrics and user studies on Amazon Mechanical Turk.
- Main claims: CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art baselines and coherence model integration with BRNN yields significant gains.
- Authors support claims with extensive experiments and human evaluation results.
- Paper is well-written with clear architecture description, training procedure, and comprehensive related work discussion.
- Strength: novel and effective approach for generating sentence sequences over image streams.
- Strength: coherence model + BRNN integration notably improves performance.
- Strength: leverages a large real-world dataset of image-blog posts for training and evaluation.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation combining quantitative and user-study methods.
- Weakness: CRCN’s complexity may make training and optimization difficult.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on a large blog-image dataset may limit reproducibility and applicability.
- Weakness: the coherence model may not capture all aspects of sentence-level coherence.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations and discuss broader applicability.
