- Paper proposes CRCN: retrieve per-image candidate sentences, model sentence flow with BRNN over paragraph vectors, and add entity-grid coherence modeling for image-stream to multi-sentence description.  
- Training data auto-built from blogs by segmenting text around images and summarizing segments to one (+κ) sentences using LSA; trained with max-margin ranking loss.  
- At test time, KNN in image space builds candidate set and approximate beam/divide-and-conquer search uses learned compatibility score.  
- Uses VGG fc7 for images, doc2vec (300d) for sentences, BRNN with ReLU, and a 64-d entity-grid coherence vector zero-padded to 300d then projected to 4096d.  
- Scoring sums per-position sentence–image dot products plus a global coherence vector–image dot product.  
- Training uses structured max-margin with random negatives and BPTT/RMSprop; candidate search approach is well motivated.  
- Plausible approach with experimental support, but several modeling choices lack justification or ablation.  
- Possible length bias: summing scores over positions without length normalization could favor longer sequences; ablation needed.  
- Global coherence term interaction with per-image features seems ad hoc and may double-count; alternative formulations suggested.  
- Zero-padding coherence vector before FC is unusual; a learned linear projection would be more principled.  
- BRNN uses simple ReLU RNNs; LSTM/GRU could improve optimization and should be ablated.  
- Blog-based data pipeline is practical but noisy (heuristic segmentation, LSA summarization, coreference/parsing errors); robustness analysis required.  
- Dataset size (~20k posts, ~140k images) is substantial and realistic for tourism domains (NYC, Disneyland).  
- Evaluation uses BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR against summarized ground truth, sequence retrieval metrics (R@K, median rank), and AMT pairwise preferences.  
- Ablations (1NN vs RCN vs CRCN) show coherence helps, especially for longer sequences (8–10 images) in AMT.  
- Clarify sequence-level retrieval setup (candidate pool size, handling length mismatch) and report AMT significance and absolute scores.  
- Discuss generalization beyond tourism; add runtime/complexity of candidate search and inference.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, but needs clearer entity-grid construction, coreference details, data splits, and search parameter settings.  
- Novelty: first to integrate an entity-based coherence model into a multimodal BRNN for image streams and to train from blogs in this context.  
- Significance: advances toward coherent multi-sentence multimodal descriptions despite being retrieval-based and reliant on noisy preprocessing.  
- Strengths: clear problem, novel coherence integration, large realistic data, solid empirical results, thoughtful ablations.  
- Weaknesses: ad hoc modeling choices, potential length bias, limited domains evaluated, noisy preprocessing, retrieval limits linguistic diversity.  
- Key questions: how are R@K/median rank computed, did authors try LSTM/GRU, sensitivity to K/M/κ, effect of length normalization, and will datasets be released?  
- Suggestions: ablate coherence pathway choices, try hierarchical/LSTM models, provide error analysis and failure cases, test a non-tourism domain, and add AMT protocol/significance tests.  
- Overall: meaningful and timely contribution toward coherent multi-sentence image-stream description; design/evaluation details need tightening.  
- Recommendation: weak accept (confidence: medium).
