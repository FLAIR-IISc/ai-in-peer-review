- Introduces the new task of generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams, extending single-image captioning to sequences.  
- Proposes CRCN: a multimodal model combining a CNN for images, BRNN for text flow, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Trains on weakly-aligned image–text pairs extracted from blog posts, using a practical preprocessing pipeline.  
- Evaluated on two blog datasets (NYC, Disneyland) and outperforms adapted state-of-the-art baselines by automatic metrics and human studies.  
- Strength: tackles a significant, understudied problem with high potential impact (album summarization, storytelling, assistive tech).  
- Strength: technically sound architecture that injects linguistic coherence (entity grid) into a deep model.  
- Strength: resourceful data collection enabling training despite lack of curated paired datasets.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation including ablation studies, multiple baselines, automatic metrics, and AMT human judgments.  
- Weakness: retrieval-based approach limits expressive power versus generative models and cannot describe novel scenes; trade-offs need discussion.  
- Weakness: scoring function formulation (Equation 3) is unclear—why the global coherence vector g_k is dotted with each individual image feature v_lt.  
- Weakness: inference scales poorly (K^N search); beam/divide-and-conquer approximations are used but their accuracy and runtime vs. sequence length are not analyzed.  
- Weakness: dataset preprocessing relies on heuristics that can be noisy; the paper lacks analysis or examples of alignment quality and failure cases.  
- Minor correction: calling the approach “completely unsupervised” is inaccurate—training uses weak supervision from aligned blog data.
