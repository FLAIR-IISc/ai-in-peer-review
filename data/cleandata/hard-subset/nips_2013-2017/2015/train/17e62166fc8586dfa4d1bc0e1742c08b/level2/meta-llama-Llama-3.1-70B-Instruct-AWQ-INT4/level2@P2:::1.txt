The paper "Expressing an Image Stream with a Sequence of Natural Sentences" proposes a novel approach for generating natural language descriptions for image streams. The authors extend the traditional image captioning problem to a sequence-to-sequence problem, where the input is a sequence of images and the output is a sequence of sentences. The proposed approach, called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN), integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and a local coherence model to generate fluent and coherent sentence sequences.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and its components. The use of blog posts as text-image parallel training data is a good idea, as it allows for the learning of semantic relations between long streams of images and text. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, outperforming other state-of-the-art methods in both quantitative and qualitative evaluations.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposal of a novel approach for generating natural language descriptions for image streams, which is a challenging problem that has not been well addressed in previous research.
* The use of a multimodal architecture that integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and a local coherence model, which allows for the generation of fluent and coherent sentence sequences.
* The use of blog posts as text-image parallel training data, which provides a large and diverse dataset for training the model.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, outperforming other state-of-the-art methods in both quantitative and qualitative evaluations.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the proposed approach and its components, which makes it difficult to understand the underlying mechanisms and limitations of the approach.
* The experimental results are not comprehensive, and the authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.
* The paper does not discuss the potential applications and limitations of the proposed approach, which is important for understanding the practical impact of the research.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed approach and its components. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, but more analysis and comparison with other state-of-the-art methods are needed to fully understand the strengths and limitations of the approach.
