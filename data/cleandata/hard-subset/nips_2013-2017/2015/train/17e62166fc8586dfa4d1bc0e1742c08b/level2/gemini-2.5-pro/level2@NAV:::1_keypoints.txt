- Tackles a novel problem: generating a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream, extending single-image captioning to sequence-to-sequence.
- Proposes CRCN: a multimodal architecture combining a CNN for images, a bidirectional RNN for sentence flow, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Novelty lies in integrating a classic NLP coherence model into a deep learning framework to improve narrative flow across sentences.
- Trained on a large-scale dataset of blog posts and evaluated against strong baselines.
- Evaluation is thorough: automatic metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, retrieval) and human studies; ablation shows BRNN and coherence model contribute positively.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.
- Limitation: retrieval-based composition uses sentences from the training set, limiting expressiveness; a hybrid generative approach is suggested.
- Limitation: test-time divide-and-conquer retrieval is approximate; discussion of its computational cost and closeness to optimal search is needed.
- Overall assessment: strong, significant work that advances multi-image understanding and visual storytelling; recommend acceptance.
