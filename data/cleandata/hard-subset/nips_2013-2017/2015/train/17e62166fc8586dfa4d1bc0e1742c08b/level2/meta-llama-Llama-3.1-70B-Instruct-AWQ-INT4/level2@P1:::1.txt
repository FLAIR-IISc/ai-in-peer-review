The paper "Expressing an Image Stream with a Sequence of Natural Sentences" proposes a novel approach for generating natural language descriptions for an image stream. The authors extend the traditional image captioning task to a sequence of images and sentences, which is a more realistic and challenging problem. They design a multimodal architecture called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The authors evaluate their approach on two large datasets of blog posts and demonstrate its superior performance compared to other state-of-the-art methods.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and highlights the contributions of the paper. The related work section is comprehensive and provides a good overview of the existing methods. The architecture of the CRCN model is well-explained, and the authors provide sufficient details about the training and testing procedures.

The experimental results are impressive, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach using both quantitative and qualitative evaluations. The comparison with other state-of-the-art methods is thorough, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the results.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The authors propose a novel and challenging problem of generating natural language descriptions for an image stream.
* The CRCN model is well-designed and effectively integrates multiple components to capture the coherence and fluency of the generated sentences.
* The experimental results are impressive, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach using both quantitative and qualitative evaluations.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The paper assumes that the input image stream is a sequence of images with a clear narrative structure. However, in real-world scenarios, the image stream may not always have a clear narrative structure, and the model may not perform well in such cases.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the CRCN model. This is an important aspect, especially when dealing with large image streams.
* The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the entity-based local coherence model is integrated with the bidirectional recurrent neural networks. This is an important aspect, and the authors should provide more details about this integration.
