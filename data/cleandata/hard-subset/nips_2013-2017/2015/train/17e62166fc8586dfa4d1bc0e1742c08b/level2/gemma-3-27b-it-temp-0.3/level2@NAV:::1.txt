## Review of "Expressing an Image Stream with a Sequence of Natural Sentences"

This paper tackles the novel problem of generating coherent sentence sequences from image streams, moving beyond the single image-single sentence paradigm. The authors propose a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) integrating CNNs, BRNNs, and a local coherence model, trained on a large corpus of blog posts. The core idea of leveraging blog data as a natural source of image-text streams is insightful, and the proposed architecture appears well-motivated to address the challenges of sequential description and coherence.

The paper is generally well-structured and clearly explains the motivation, approach, and experimental setup. The authors convincingly argue for the need to model coherence in image stream description, and the inclusion of a local coherence model is a key contribution. The quantitative results, using BLEU, top-K recall, and user studies via AMT, demonstrate the superiority of CRCN over several baselines, including adaptations of existing image captioning models. The ablation study comparing CRCN to a BRNN-only model further validates the benefit of the coherence component.  The experimental setup is thorough, utilizing a sizable dataset and appropriate evaluation metrics.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. While the quantitative results are promising, the improvements over some baselines are not always substantial, particularly in the language metrics. The qualitative examples, while helpful, are limited and don’t fully showcase the model’s ability to handle complex or nuanced image streams.  The description of the local coherence model, while conceptually sound, lacks sufficient detail regarding the specific implementation and the impact of different parameter choices.  Furthermore, the paper doesn’t extensively discuss potential failure cases or limitations of the approach, such as handling ambiguous or abstract imagery. The reliance on blog data, while clever, introduces potential biases inherent in that data source (e.g., stylistic preferences of bloggers).

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a novel and important problem in multimodal understanding.
*   Proposes a well-motivated and technically sound architecture (CRCN).
*   Demonstrates strong performance through quantitative and qualitative evaluations.
*   Leverages a large and readily available dataset (blog posts).

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Improvements over baselines are not always dramatic.
*   Limited discussion of failure cases and limitations.
*   Insufficient detail regarding the implementation of the coherence model.



**Overall:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of image stream understanding and description. While some aspects could be strengthened with further detail and analysis, the novelty of the problem, the soundness of the approach, and the positive experimental results warrant acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting more detailed explanation of the coherence model and a more thorough discussion of limitations.
