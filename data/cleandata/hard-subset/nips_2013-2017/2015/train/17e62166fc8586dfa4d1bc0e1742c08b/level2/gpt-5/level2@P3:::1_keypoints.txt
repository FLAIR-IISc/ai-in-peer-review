- Tackles paragraph-level description of image streams using a retrieval-based multimodal model (CRCN) combining CNN features, a BRNN over sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid coherence model.  
- Training uses max-margin ranking on automatically paired image/sentence streams harvested from blogs, with κ-best summarization augmentations.  
- At test time K-NN in CNN space yields candidate sentences per image and a beam-style approximate search selects coherent sequences.

- Positions work relative to retrieval-based captioning, multimodal RNN/LSTM captioning, and video description; novelty is integrating entity-grid coherence into multimodal sequence retrieval.

- Strength: Clear and timely problem formulation moving beyond single-image captions to coherent paragraph outputs.  
- Strength: Sensible architecture that jointly models image-sentence compatibility and local entity-based coherence via BRNN and entity-grid.  
- Strength: Pragmatic large-scale data harvesting and segmentation/summarization pipeline from blogs.  
- Strength: Diverse evaluation including automatic metrics, retrieval metrics, and AMT user studies showing coherence benefits for longer sequences.  
- Strength: Ablation studies (1NN, BRNN-only, full CRCN) demonstrate coherence component contributions.

- Weakness: Retrieval-based approach limits generalization to novel content and domains; “unsupervised” claim is overstated due to automatic paired supervision.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to two in-house tourism datasets (NYC, Disneyland); unclear public release and cross-domain generalization.  
- Weakness: Baseline training/details may be unfair or under-specified (tokenization, vocab, stopping criteria), possibly underestimating competing methods.  
- Weakness: Entity-grid coherence feature is coarse, zero-padded, not end-to-end trainable, and its integration with visual features is heuristic.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis on runtime/complexity, sensitivity to hyperparameters (K, M, κ), and preprocessing ablations; no detailed error analysis.

- Quality: Technical components are sound and appropriate (max-margin training, pragmatic search), but several design choices are heuristic and need deeper justification or alternatives.  
- Clarity: Generally well written with model equations and dimensions, but some notation and scoring function aspects need clarification; baseline and cost reporting lacking.  
- Originality: Novel combination of CNN/BRNN with an entity-grid coherence model for image-stream paragraph retrieval and use of blog-derived paired streams.  
- Significance: Offers a step toward story-like photo-stream descriptions with demonstrated gains, but retrieval paradigm and domain-limited evaluation constrain broader impact.

- Essential improvement: Provide fuller baseline training details and include a sequence-aware baseline (e.g., sentence-transition model) to better isolate entity-grid value.  
- Essential improvement: Report runtime/complexity of candidate search and perform sensitivity analyses for K and beam size M.  
- Essential improvement: Clarify “unsupervised” claim and quantify segmentation/summarization noise effects (e.g., ablation varying κ and summarizer).

- Nice-to-have: Ablate coherence model choices (history length, role schema) and compare to simpler coherence proxies like cosine similarity between adjacent sentence embeddings.  
- Nice-to-have: Provide qualitative error analysis and typical failure modes (e.g., discontinuous streams, sparse entities).  
- Nice-to-have: Release datasets and preprocessing code to facilitate replication.

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses meaningful extension to paragraph-level captioning, integrates discourse coherence into multimodal framework with measurable benefits, and shows user-study supported gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Retrieval approach limits generalization, coherence integration is heuristic and not end-to-end, and analysis/baselines and datasets are incomplete or domain-limited.

- Overall recommendation: Leaning toward acceptance, contingent on addressing essential suggestions to strengthen baselines, analysis, and clarity.
