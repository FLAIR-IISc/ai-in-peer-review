- Introduces the task of generating natural-language descriptions for an image stream (sequence of images → sequence of sentences), a more realistic and challenging extension of image captioning.  
- Proposes the Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that combines CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Evaluated on two large blog-post datasets and shows superior quantitative and qualitative performance over state-of-the-art methods.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, comprehensive related work, and detailed description of architecture and training/testing.  
- Strength: novel and challenging problem formulation.  
- Strength: CRCN effectively integrates components to capture coherence and fluency across sentences.  
- Strength: strong experimental results with thorough comparisons and analysis.  
- Weakness: assumes input image streams have clear narrative structure, which may not hold in real-world data.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability for large image streams.  
- Weakness: unclear explanation of how the entity-based local coherence model is integrated with the bidirectional RNNs.  
- Argument for acceptance: novelty of task, well-designed CRCN, and impressive empirical evidence.  
- Argument for rejection: unrealistic assumptions about stream structure and missing complexity analysis.  
- Overall: promising and well-executed work that needs to address noted weaknesses (Rating: 8/10, Confidence: 8/10).
