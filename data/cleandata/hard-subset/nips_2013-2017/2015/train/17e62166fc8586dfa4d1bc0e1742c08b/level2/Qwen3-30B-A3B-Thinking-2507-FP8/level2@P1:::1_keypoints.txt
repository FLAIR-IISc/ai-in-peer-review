- Proposes generating sequences of natural sentences for image streams, not single-image captions.  
- Introduces CRCN: CNN for images, BRNN for sentence modeling, and a local coherence model for smooth transitions.  
- Motivated by users capturing multiple photos during memorable experiences; descriptions should consider the whole image stream.  
- Fills a gap by extending both input and output dimensions from single image/sentence to sequences.  
- Demonstrates CRCN outperforms strong baselines on BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall and AMT user studies on two large blog datasets.  
- Strength: well-motivated problem formulation with compelling real-world example.  
- Strength: well-designed architecture integrating CNN, BRNN, and a coherence model.  
- Strength: creative use of blog posts as training data leveraging user-generated image–text sequences.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation using standard metrics and user studies.  
- Strength: ablation study (RCN and 1NN) effectively shows the coherence model’s contribution.  
- Weakness: insufficient contrast with related work on image-sequence/sequence-to-sequence approaches and recent papers.  
- Weakness: training data collection is under-specified—handling of non-image text and image–text misalignments is unclear.  
- Weakness: claim of being the first to address the problem lacks thorough comparison to prior sequence generation work.  
- Weakness: quantitative improvements are modest (e.g., 1–2 BLEU points) and need explanation of practical significance.  
- Weakness: user study limited—only 100 test streams and queries restricted to 5 images may not reflect longer-sequence coherence.  
- Writing and figures are clear and well-organized; architecture details are well explained though training/hyperparameter choices need more detail.  
- Evaluation ratings: quality—technically sound with thorough evaluation; clarity—well written; originality—novel extension and coherence model; significance—meaningful application with demonstrated improvements.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, technically sound and significant; accept with minor revisions.  
- Actionable requests: clarify distinction from prior sequence-based work, detail blog data collection/alignment, and better justify practical importance of modest metric gains.
