**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes an approach for generating a sequence of natural language sentences for an image stream. The authors introduce a multimodal architecture called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with images and is evaluated using quantitative measures and user studies.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novelty of the problem**: The paper addresses a new problem of generating sentence sequences for image streams, which is a natural extension of the existing work on image captioning.
2. **Multimodal architecture**: The proposed CRCN model is a novel combination of convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model.
3. **Large-scale evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach on a large dataset of blog posts with images and use quantitative measures and user studies to assess the performance of their model.
4. **Improved performance**: The results show that the proposed approach outperforms other state-of-the-art methods in terms of quantitative measures and user studies.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Complexity of the model**: The proposed CRCN model is a complex architecture that requires a large amount of computational resources and data to train.
2. **Limited generalizability**: The model is trained on a specific dataset of blog posts with images, and it is unclear how well it will generalize to other datasets or domains.
3. **Lack of interpretability**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of how the different components of the CRCN model contribute to its performance.
4. **Limited comparison to other methods**: The paper only compares the proposed approach to a few other state-of-the-art methods, and it is unclear how it would perform compared to other approaches.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper proposes a novel approach to generating sentence sequences for image streams, which is a significant extension of the existing work on image captioning. The proposed CRCN model is a new combination of convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model, which demonstrates originality and creativity.
