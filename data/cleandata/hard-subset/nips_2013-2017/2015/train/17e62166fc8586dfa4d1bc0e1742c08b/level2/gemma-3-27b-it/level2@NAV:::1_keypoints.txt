- Tackles generating coherent natural-language descriptions for image sequences, extending beyond single-image captioning.  
- Proposes CRCN combining CNNs for visuals, BRNNs for language, and a local coherence model to ensure fluency.  
- Leverages a large corpus of blog posts with associated images as a novel training resource.  
- Architecture is well-motivated; components collectively provide holistic visual and linguistic understanding.  
- Quantitative results (BLEU, Recall) and AMT user studies show clear improvement over baselines (e.g., Karpathy et al., Vinyals et al.).  
- Ablation (CRCN vs BRNN-only) highlights the benefit of the coherence model.  
- Baselines used are somewhat limited and slightly dated; newer seq2seq/attention models should be compared.  
- Description of the local coherence model (entity grid construction and use) is sparse and needs more clarity.  
- Reliance on BLEU is concerning given its limitations for fluency and coherence evaluation.  
- Ablation studies should be expanded to isolate contributions of CNN and BRNN components more thoroughly.  
- Paper is a novel, valuable contribution and merits acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add recent baselines, provide more coherence-model details, acknowledge BLEU limitations, and consider releasing code.
