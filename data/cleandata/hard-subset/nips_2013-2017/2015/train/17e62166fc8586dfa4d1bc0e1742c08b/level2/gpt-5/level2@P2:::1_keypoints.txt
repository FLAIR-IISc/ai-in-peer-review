- Paper addresses generating coherent sentence sequences for photo streams using a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining VGG features, BRNN over doc2vec sentence candidates, and an entity-grid coherence term.
- Training uses max-margin losses on image–sentence-sequence pairs mined from blogs via paragraph segmentation and LSA summarization; testing retrieves sentence candidates by visual K-NN and uses beam search to assemble sequences.
- Experiments on NYC and Disneyland blog datasets (~20K posts, ~140K images) show automatic metric and AMT user-study improvements, with ablations indicating BRNN and coherence help, especially for longer sequences.
- Extends prior image-captioning and multimodal embedding work to sequence outputs and explicitly models sentence-level coherence, novelly integrating an entity-grid with cross-modal retrieval.
- Strength: timely, practically relevant problem moving beyond single-image captioning.
- Strength: simple, modular model design with an interpretable entity-grid coherence term.
- Strength: sensible negative-sampling training objective and scalable candidate generation; ablations are informative.
- Strength: includes both automatic metrics and AMT studies, showing coherence benefits for longer sequences.
- Weakness: unclear retrieval evaluation protocol—candidate pool composition, definition of ground truth, and handling of permutations need clarification.
- Weakness: fairness of comparisons—retrieval-based method may have n-gram metric advantages over generative baselines; stronger retrieval baselines are needed.
- Weakness: claims of “end-to-end”/“unsupervised” are overstated given heavy preprocessing (LSA, doc2vec, coref) and heuristic supervision.
- Weakness: entity-grid relies on noisy coreference in blogs; no analysis of coref errors or ablation replacing coref with simpler matching.
- Weakness: candidate selection decoupled from learned model (visual K-NN) may prune candidates the model would prefer; suggest learned or two-stage reranking.
- Weakness: potential data leakage—doc2vec trained on full corpus and possible near-duplicates across splits could inflate results; deduplication and train-only fitting should be confirmed.
- Weakness: limited analysis of when coherence helps; automatic gains are small and AMT claims lack statistical significance and failure-case analysis.
- Technical note: notation/design issue—global text coherence vector g is dot-multiplied with every image vector, effectively repeated across positions; justify or consider alternatives.
- Clarity: generally readable but ambiguous on evaluation protocol, doc2vec/coref treatment, and some dimensionality/mapping details; methodology needs precision.
- Originality: moderately high—novel problem setting and integration of entity-grid with multimodal scoring.
- Significance: potentially important for travelogue/story captioning, but stronger baselines and clearer evaluation needed to increase confidence.
- Suggested improvements: clarify retrieval metrics and report AMT significance; add simple coherence baselines and reranking experiments; ablate coref, history length, doc2vec vs learned encoders, and summarization κ sensitivity.
- Suggested improvements: ensure no train–test leakage in doc2vec and deduplicate data; release preprocessing code and clarify VGG fine-tuning.
- Pros for acceptance: novel, relevant problem, interpretable coherence modeling, scalable approach, and user studies indicating benefits for longer sequences.
- Cons for acceptance: ambiguous evaluation and fairness concerns, overstated unsupervised claims, limited coherence robustness analysis, and decoupled candidate generation limiting potential.
