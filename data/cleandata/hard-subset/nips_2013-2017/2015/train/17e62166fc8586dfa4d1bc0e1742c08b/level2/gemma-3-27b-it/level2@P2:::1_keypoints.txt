- Novelty: Introduces image-stream captioning, a natural and important extension of single-image captioning, well-motivated by real-world scenarios.
- Technical soundness: Proposes CRCN combining CNNs, BRNNs, and a coherence model; uses VGGNet for image features.
- Dataset: Leverages blog posts as weakly-supervised text-image parallel corpus (≈140K images, 20K blogs), a clever way to scale data.
- Evaluation: Thorough evaluation with automatic metrics (BLEU, top-K recall) and human AMT studies, comparing multiple baselines and varying stream lengths.
- Ablation study: Compares CRCN to variants (1NN, RCN) to show contributions of each component, especially the coherence model.
- Coherence model clarity: Explanation of the local coherence model (entity grids, transition frequencies) is superficial and lacks detail/visualization.
- Baseline selection: Baselines are mostly single-image captioning extensions; missing comparisons to recent seq2seq/video-captioning models with attention.
- Metric limitations: Reliance on BLEU and similar metrics is problematic for evaluating passage-level fluency/coherence; discourse/coherence metrics should be explored.
- Data augmentation: The top-κ summary sentence selection is ad-hoc; choice of κ=3 lacks justification.
- Writing clarity: Some descriptions (e.g., BRNN in Sec. 3.1) are dense and could be simplified for readability.
- Argument for acceptance: Novel problem setup, technically sound architecture, compelling experimental results, and clever use of blog data support acceptance.
- Argument against acceptance: Inadequate coherence model description, incomplete baseline comparisons, and overreliance on standard language-similarity metrics argue for revisions.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address coherence model detail, baseline comparisons, and evaluation metrics.
