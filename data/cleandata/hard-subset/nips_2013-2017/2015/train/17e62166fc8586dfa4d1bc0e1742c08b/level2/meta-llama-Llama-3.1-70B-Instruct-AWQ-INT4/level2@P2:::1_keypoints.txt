- Proposes CRCN to generate a sequence of natural sentences from an image stream (sequence-to-sequence formulation).
- CRCN integrates CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and a local coherence model to produce fluent, coherent sentence sequences.
- Uses blog posts as large, diverse image–text parallel training data to learn long-stream semantic relations.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of approach and components.
- Experimental results show CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art methods in quantitative and qualitative evaluations.
- Strength: addresses a novel, challenging problem of describing image streams.
- Strength: multimodal architecture enables coherent multi-sentence generation.
- Strength: blog-post data provides substantial training diversity and scale.
- Weakness: insufficient detailed analysis of model components and underlying mechanisms.
- Weakness: experimental comparisons are not comprehensive or detailed enough.
- Weakness: lacks discussion of practical applications and limitations of the method.
- Overall ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong empirical performance, potential applicability to image/video captioning and HCI.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited analysis, incomplete experimental comparison, and missing discussion of applications/limitations.
