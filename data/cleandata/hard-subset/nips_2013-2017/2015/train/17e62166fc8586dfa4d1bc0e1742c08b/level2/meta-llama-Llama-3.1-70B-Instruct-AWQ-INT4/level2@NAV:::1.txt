**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream. The authors extend the traditional single-image-to-single-sentence generation problem to a sequence of images and a sequence of sentences. They design a multimodal architecture called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with images and outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in both quantitative evaluation and user studies.

**Evaluation of Support for Claims**

The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach using both quantitative measures (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall, and median rank) and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk (AMT). The results demonstrate that the CRCN approach outperforms other state-of-the-art methods, including log-bilinear multimodal models and recurrent convolutional models. The authors also provide qualitative examples of sentence sequence retrieval, which illustrate the effectiveness of their approach.

**Assessment of Usefulness**

The proposed approach has practical applications in various fields, such as image captioning, visual storytelling, and human-computer interaction. The ability to generate coherent sentence sequences for image streams can facilitate more effective communication and understanding between humans and machines.

**Check of Field Knowledge**

The paper demonstrates a thorough understanding of the relevant literature in the field of multimodal learning, natural language processing, and computer vision. The authors provide a comprehensive review of previous work and clearly explain how their approach differs from existing methods.

**Evaluation of Novelty**

The proposed CRCN architecture is a novel contribution to the field of multimodal learning. The integration of convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model is a unique approach that has not been explored before.

**Assessment of Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details about the proposed approach, including the architecture, training procedure, and evaluation metrics. The authors also provide a comprehensive discussion of the results and limitations of their approach.

**Discussion of Limitations**

The authors acknowledge the limitations of their approach, including the requirement of a large dataset of blog posts with images and the potential difficulty of extending the approach to other domains.

**Constructive Suggestions**

One potential area for improvement is to explore the application of the CRCN approach to other domains, such as video captioning or multimodal dialogue systems. Additionally, the authors could investigate the use of other evaluation metrics, such as ROUGE or F1-score, to provide a more comprehensive assessment of their approach.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper proposes a novel approach that integrates multiple modalities and provides a comprehensive evaluation of its effectiveness.
* The results demonstrate a significant improvement over state-of-the-art baselines in both quantitative evaluation and user studies.
* The approach has practical applications in various fields, such as image captioning and visual storytelling.

Con:

* The paper relies on a large dataset of blog posts with images, which may not be readily available in other domains.
* The approach may not generalize well to other domains or tasks, such as video captioning or multimodal dialogue systems.
* The evaluation metrics used in the paper may not provide a comprehensive assessment of the approach's effectiveness.
