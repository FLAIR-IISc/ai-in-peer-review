- Presents CRCN, a novel multimodal architecture that generates coherent sentence sequences for image streams (extends single-image captioning to sequences).  
- Integrates CNNs for images, BRNNs for sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model to ensure smooth transitions.  
- Trains on blog-post image-text pairs, leveraging a large natural dataset for text-image parallel data.  
- Demonstrates superiority over baselines using quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, retrieval accuracy) and AMT user studies.  
- Novelty and scope: addresses an underexplored, natural extension of image captioning and adds coherence modeling.  
- Technical rigor: well-designed unified architecture combining CNNs, BRNNs, and coherence modeling; creative dataset choice.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: extensive quantitative and qualitative experiments, including user validation.  
- Practical utility: applicable to tourism, storytelling, and other image-stream domains.  
- Dataset bias: training on NYC and Disneyland blogs may limit generalizability to other domains or unstructured datasets.  
- Scalability concerns: CRCN complexity and approximate divide-and-conquer retrieval may hinder scaling and real-time use.  
- Limited discussion of limitations: paper lacks thorough analysis of noisy/poorly aligned blog text-image pairs and other failure modes.  
- Evaluation metrics: reliance on BLEU/CIDEr may not fully capture paragraph-level fluency/coherence; alternative metrics could be explored.
