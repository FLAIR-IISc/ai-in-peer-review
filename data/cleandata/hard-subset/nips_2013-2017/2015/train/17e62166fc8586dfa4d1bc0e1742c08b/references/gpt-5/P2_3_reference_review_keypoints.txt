- Paper addresses generating multi-sentence descriptions for image streams using a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN image features, a BRNN over sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid coherence model with max-margin training and retrieval-based decoding.

- Data: scalable unsupervised pipeline mines image–sentence sequences from blogs (two travel domains: NYC, Disneyland); evaluation uses BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr, retrieval metrics, and AMT studies.

- Strength: extends captioning to image streams and multi-sentence outputs, an important and under-explored task.

- Strength: principled architecture combining sentence-level sequence modeling and explicit local coherence via entity-grid, with end-to-end learned compatibility between text and images.

- Strength: large-scale data contribution via an unsupervised mining pipeline.

- Strength: empirical gains over single-sentence baselines and AMT user preferences; ablations indicate coherence helps, especially for longer sequences.

- Strength: clear exposition of objective and retrieval/search strategy; sensible engineering choices reported.

- Weakness: retrieval metric definitions (R@K, median rank) are unclear/impossible as stated because ground-truth sentences are not in the training pool; candidate pools need specification.

- Weakness: baselines are adapted single-image captioners concatenated at test time, which may unfairly disadvantage sequence-aware comparisons; stronger sequence-aware baselines are missing.

- Weakness: no comparison to pure generative paragraph models (e.g., hierarchical RNNs) to test limits of retrieval-based approach.

- Weakness: scoring ties a global coherence vector to each image feature repeatedly, which is unjustified and may conflate image compatibility with text-only coherence and scale with sequence length.

- Weakness: coherence representation padding (64→300) is ad hoc; no sensitivity analysis on history length or alternative coherence models.

- Weakness: BRNN runs over paragraph/doc2vec embeddings rather than words, trading tractability for granularity and limiting rephrasing/flexibility.

- Weakness: segmentation/summarization pipeline is noisy with no human validation or error analysis; alignment quality is unverified.

- Weakness: domain bias—evaluation limited to two travel domains; generalization to other topics not shown.

- Weakness: many implementation details deferred and dataset/code release not stated; impact of approximate search vs. exact search not quantified, hurting reproducibility.

- Quality: technically sound with coherent objective and useful ablations, but coherence scoring design and metric ambiguities weaken empirical support.

- Clarity: generally well written and organized, but some notation/design choices lack justification and evaluation definitions need clarification.

- Originality: moderate—novel combination of entity-grid coherence with multimodal CNN-RNN for image streams, but components are mostly known.

- Significance: moderate—important task and promising results, but retrieval limitation and narrow evaluation reduce impact.

- Key questions for authors: define retrieval metrics/candidate sets; justify multiplying coherence vector with each image feature and report alternatives; provide manual evaluation of segmentation accuracy; sensitivity to κ/history/sentence embedding; and code/dataset release plans.

- Pros for acceptance: timely under-explored task, interpretable discourse-aware model, large-scale dataset, and meaningful user study evidence.

- Cons for acceptance: ambiguous retrieval metrics, potentially weak baselines, ad hoc design choices, limited analysis, and domain-limited evaluation.

- Recommendation: weak accept pending clarifications on evaluation metrics; stronger baselines and clearer metric definitions would strengthen the paper.
