## NIPS Review: Expressing an Image Stream with a Sequence of Natural Sentences

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel and ambitious problem – generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams – and proposes a reasonably well-motivated and technically sound approach (CRCN). The paper is generally well-written and clearly presents its contributions. However, the quantitative gains over strong baselines are modest, and the user study results, while positive, aren't overwhelmingly convincing. I believe the paper represents a solid contribution to the field and warrants acceptance, but with minor revisions to strengthen the presentation of results and discussion of limitations.

**Summary:**

The paper tackles the problem of automatically generating a sequence of natural language sentences to describe a sequence of images, motivated by scenarios like travel photo collections.  This extends the single image-single sentence captioning problem.  The proposed CRCN architecture integrates CNNs for image representation, BRNNs for language modeling, and a local coherence model to improve sentence-to-sentence flow. The authors leverage a large, albeit weakly supervised, dataset constructed from travel blogs. They demonstrate improvements over several baselines using both automated metrics (BLEU, Top-K recall) and a user study on Amazon Mechanical Turk.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Problem Formulation:** Extending image captioning to image *streams* is a natural and important next step. The motivation is clear and relatable.
* **Technical Soundness:** The CRCN architecture is well-designed, combining established components (CNNs, RNNs) with a specific focus on coherence. The use of a local coherence model is a key contribution.
* **Large-Scale Dataset:**  Leveraging blog data is a clever way to obtain a large dataset for this task, overcoming the scarcity of parallel image-sentence stream data. The pre-processing pipeline described is reasonable.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors provide a good evaluation strategy including quantitative metrics and a user study. Comparing against multiple baselines strengthens their claims.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is well-organized and generally easy to follow. The figures are helpful in understanding the architecture.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Modest Quantitative Gains:** While the authors demonstrate statistically significant improvements over baselines, the gains in metrics like BLEU and Top-K recall are relatively small. This raises questions about the practical significance of the improvements.  A deeper analysis of *where* CRCN excels (e.g., longer streams, specific image content) would be helpful.
* **Dataset Limitations:** While using blog data is clever, it's also a source of noise and potential bias. Blog authors aren’t writing captions *for* the images; the text is more loosely associated. This inherent weakness of the dataset isn't fully addressed.  The authors acknowledge this but don't fully explore its impact.
* **Coherence Model Complexity:** While the coherence model is a key component, its implementation (parse tree concatenation, entity grids) seems somewhat complex and potentially brittle. A more streamlined or theoretically grounded approach might be preferable.  The authors don't provide much intuition for *why* this specific coherence model works well.
* **Ablation Study could be more granular:** While they have an ablation for the coherence model, exploring the impact of different CNN architectures, RNN variants, or data augmentation strategies would strengthen the analysis.
* **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper lacks a thorough error analysis.  Showing example failures of the CRCN and comparing them to the baseline errors would provide valuable insights.



**Originality:** The problem formulation and the integration of a dedicated coherence model are novel. The use of blogs as a data source is a creative solution to the data scarcity problem.  However, the individual components (CNNs, RNNs) are not new.  The originality lies in the specific combination and application to this new problem.

**Significance:** This work has the potential to be significant. Automatically describing image streams could be valuable for various applications like photo organization, travel storytelling, and assistive technologies. However, the modest gains over baselines temper the immediate impact. Future work building on this foundation could further unlock the potential.

**Quality:**  The paper is technically sound. The methodology is clearly described, and the experimental setup is well-defined. The results are presented clearly, but, as noted above, the effect sizes are relatively small.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized.  However, the detailed explanation of the coherence model (section 3.2) could be more concise and intuitive.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Novel and important problem.
* Technically sound approach with a well-motivated architecture.
* Comprehensive evaluation with quantitative metrics and user studies.
* Demonstrates improvements over strong baselines.
* Large-scale dataset built using a creative approach.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* Modest quantitative gains.
* Dataset limitations and potential biases.
* Complexity of the coherence model.
* Lack of a detailed error analysis.



**Recommendation:** **Accept with minor revisions.**

**Specific Suggestions for Revision:**

* **Expand Error Analysis:** Include a qualitative analysis of failure cases, comparing the errors made by CRCN and the baselines.
* **Address Dataset Limitations:** Discuss the potential biases in the blog data more thoroughly and explore ways to mitigate their impact.
* **Streamline Coherence Model:** Consider simplifying the coherence model or providing a stronger theoretical justification for its design.
* **Granular Ablation Study:**  Perform a more detailed ablation study to investigate the contribution of different components of the CRCN.
* **Emphasize Qualitative Results:** Supplement the quantitative results with more compelling examples of generated sentence sequences that showcase the strengths of the CRCN.
