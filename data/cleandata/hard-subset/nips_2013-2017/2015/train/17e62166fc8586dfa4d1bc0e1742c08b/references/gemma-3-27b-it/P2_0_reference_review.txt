## Review of "Expressing an Image Stream with a Sequence of Natural Sentences"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the novel problem of generating a sequence of natural language sentences to describe a sequence of images (an “image stream”), motivated by real-world scenarios like describing a series of vacation photos. The authors propose a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that combines CNNs for image representation, BRNNs for language modeling, and a local coherence model to ensure fluent transitions between sentences.  They leverage blog posts with accompanying images as a large-scale, weakly supervised dataset and demonstrate strong performance via quantitative metrics (BLEU, Recall) and user studies (AMT).

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper addresses a compelling and largely unexplored problem. The approach is well-motivated, technically sound, and empirically validated. While the gains over strong baselines aren’t *massive*, the consistent improvements across metrics and the strong performance in user studies suggest a meaningful contribution.  The paper is generally well-written and organized, although some sections could benefit from more concise explanation. I believe this paper is a **strong accept** for NIPS, though minor revisions are recommended.

**Detailed Evaluation based on NIPS criteria:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The technical approach is solid. The combination of CNNs, RNNs, and a coherence model is a logical progression given existing work in image captioning and sequence modeling. The use of a max-margin loss function is appropriate for this ranking/retrieval style task. The experimental setup is thorough, including comparisons to several baselines and analysis on two datasets.  The data collection methodology (using blog posts) is clever and addresses the difficulty of obtaining parallel image-sentence stream data.  A minor weakness is the relative complexity of the CRCN, making full reproducibility potentially challenging without significant effort. Further analysis on the impact of the data augmentation (κ=3) would also strengthen the work.

* **Clarity (7/10):** The paper is generally clear, but occasionally suffers from dense writing.  Section 3, describing the CRCN architecture, could be more streamlined. The notation is consistent, but the flow between equations and descriptive text could be smoother.  Figure 2 is helpful, but a diagram illustrating the data flow during training and inference would further enhance understanding. The terminology ("coherent recurrent convolutional networks") feels slightly clunky; a more concise name might be considered.

* **Originality (8/10):** While the individual components (CNNs, RNNs, coherence models) are not novel, the *combination* of these elements to address the specific problem of image stream description is a significant contribution.  Extending single-image captioning to sequential images and sequential sentences is a natural, but previously unaddressed, step. The use of blog data as a large-scale, weakly-supervised training resource is also a creative solution.  The paper appropriately cites and contextualizes related work.

* **Significance (8/10):** The problem addressed is significant. The ability to automatically generate coherent descriptions of image streams has applications in areas like personal photo organization, robotics (describing visual sequences to humans), and accessibility (generating descriptions for visually impaired users).  The results demonstrate a tangible improvement over existing approaches. The fact that the approach is unsupervised (relying on blog data) enhances its potential applicability.  The results suggest this work could inspire further research into more sophisticated coherence models and sequence-to-sequence learning techniques for multimodal data.



**Strengths:**

* **Novel Problem:** Addresses an important and previously under-explored problem.
* **Well-Motivated Approach:** The CRCN architecture is a logical combination of existing techniques, tailored to the task.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** Demonstrates consistent improvements over baselines via quantitative and qualitative evaluation.
* **Creative Data Collection:**  Effectively leverages blog data as a large-scale training resource.
* **Unsupervised Learning:** The unsupervised nature of the approach increases its generality.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The CRCN architecture is relatively complex, potentially hindering reproducibility.
* **Clarity in Section 3:** Description of the architecture could be more concise and easier to follow.
* **Limited Analysis of Data Augmentation:** More investigation into the optimal value of κ would be beneficial.
* **Modest Gains:** While statistically significant, the improvements over strong baselines are not dramatic.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a novel and important problem.
* Presents a technically sound and well-motivated approach.
* Demonstrates strong empirical results on relevant datasets.
* Offers a creative solution to the data scarcity problem.
* Has the potential to impact multiple research areas.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The complexity of the approach might make full reproducibility challenging.
* The gains over strong baselines, while significant, could be larger.




**Recommendation:**

**Strong Accept.** I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity (specifically in Section 3) and potentially include further analysis of the data augmentation strategy. I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the fields of computer vision, natural language processing, and multimodal learning.
