This paper addresses the problem of generating a sequence of natural language sentences to describe a stream of images, extending the traditional image captioning task from single images and sentences to sequential data. The authors propose a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) integrating CNNs, BRNNs, and a local coherence model. They leverage blog posts as a novel source of weakly-supervised training data and demonstrate improvements over several baselines on both quantitative metrics (BLEU, Recall) and user studies.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The problem of generating sequential descriptions for image streams is a natural extension of existing work and relatively unexplored. The proposed approach directly addresses the coherence challenge inherent in multi-sentence generation.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The CRCN architecture appears well-motivated and integrates established components (CNNs, RNNs) in a reasonable manner. The inclusion of a local coherence model is a significant contribution. The use of blog posts as a training dataset is clever and practical, given the lack of parallel image-sentence stream corpora.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors provide a thorough evaluation, including quantitative metrics and user studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach. The comparison to several baselines strengthens the claim of improved performance.
*   **Clear Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and provides sufficient detail to understand the proposed method. The figures are helpful in illustrating the architecture and data flow.
*   **Large Dataset:** Utilizing a dataset of 140K images and 20K blogs demonstrates a scale rarely seen in this task.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Baselines:** While several baselines are included, they are mostly extensions of single-image captioning models, potentially underestimating the difficulty of the sequential generation task. More recent and specifically designed sequence-to-sequence models should be considered.
*   **Coherence Model Details:** The description of the local coherence model, while present, lacks sufficient detail to fully appreciate its implementation. The specific transition descriptions and how they are used in the score function could be clarified.
*   **Data Augmentation:** The description of the data augmentation (using κ=3 sentences) feels somewhat arbitrary. A more principled justification or ablation study evaluating different values of κ would be beneficial.
*   **Evaluation Metric Limitations:** The authors acknowledge the limitations of BLEU and other traditional metrics, but the reliance on these metrics remains. More sophisticated evaluation measures designed for evaluating coherence and relevance in multi-sentence generation could enhance the analysis.
*   **Lack of Error Analysis:** A deeper analysis of the types of errors the model makes would provide valuable insights for future improvements.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper tackles a novel and important problem in multimodal learning.
*   The proposed approach is technically sound and demonstrates significant improvements over existing methods.
*   The evaluation is comprehensive and supports the authors' claims.
*   The work has the potential to impact areas such as visual storytelling and human-computer interaction.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The baselines considered are relatively weak and may not fully capture the complexity of the task.
*   Some aspects of the method, particularly the coherence model and data augmentation, lack sufficient detail and justification.
*   The reliance on traditional evaluation metrics limits the ability to fully assess the quality of the generated sequences.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of multimodal learning by addressing the challenging problem of generating sequential descriptions for image streams. While there are some minor weaknesses, the novelty, technical soundness, and comprehensive evaluation outweigh these concerns. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the clarity of the coherence model and justification for κ value in data augmentation and considering stronger baselines in future work.
