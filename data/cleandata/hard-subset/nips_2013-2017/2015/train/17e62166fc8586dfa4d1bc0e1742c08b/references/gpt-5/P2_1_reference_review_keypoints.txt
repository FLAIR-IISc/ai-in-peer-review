- Proposes the task of captioning an image stream with a sequence of natural sentences (multi-image → multi-sentence).
- Introduces CRCN: a retrieval-and-ranking system that retrieves K candidate sentences per image and ranks full sequences.
- Scoring combines BRNN over doc2vec sentence embeddings, an entity-grid coherence model, and image–text dot-product terms using VGG features.
- Training uses a max-margin loss on aligned image–sentence pairs mined from blogs via paragraph segmentation and LSA-based summarization.
- Test-time search uses a divide-and-conquer, beam-like procedure to assemble high-scoring sentence sequences.
- Evaluated on two large blog datasets (NYC, Disneyland) with BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, and AMT pairwise human studies.
- Ablation experiments show BRNN and coherence components improve retrieval metrics, especially for longer sequences.
- Strength: technically sound integration of known components with a well-specified scoring function and sensible training details.
- Strength: human evaluations corroborate quantitative improvements and are designed as pairwise comparisons.
- Weakness: Unclear definition and computation of retrieval metrics (R@K, median rank) given retrieval-based candidate sets versus test ground truth.
- Weakness: Potentially unfair comparisons—retrieval-based system benefits from human-authored sentences while generative baselines produce novel text, biasing BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR.
- Weakness: Characterizing the method as “unsupervised” is misleading; training uses noisy, heuristic alignments (weak/self-supervised).
- Weakness: Model design concern about how the global coherence vector g is combined (g·v_t summed over t) and lack of justification or alternatives.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on noisy preprocessing (segmentation, summarization, coreference) without robustness analysis or error analysis.
- Weakness: Reproducibility issues—missing details on CNN fine-tuning, doc2vec settings, beam/search parameters, and dataset/code release.
- Clarity issues: confusing notation, unexplained dimensional padding, unclear final score formulation, and missing schematic of search.
- Originality: Task formulation and explicit use of entity-grid coherence for multimodal multi-sentence ranking are novel and meaningful.
- Significance: Practical and valuable insight on coherence for photo-album narration, but retrieval approach limits out-of-domain generalization.
- Recommendation: Weak accept—worthwhile task and sensible approach with demonstrated benefits, contingent on clarifying evaluation and improving reproducibility.
- Questions for authors: define R@K/median rank precisely, state whether CNN is fine-tuned and sensitivity to K/M, robustness to preprocessing errors, and ablation using simpler coherence proxies.
