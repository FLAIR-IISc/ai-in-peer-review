- Summary: Proposes CRCN to rank paragraph-level sentence sequences for image streams using VGG fc7, BRNN over doc2vec sentence vectors, and an entity-grid coherence feature, with KNN candidate retrieval and beam-like assembly.
- Novelty/positioning: Extends single-image captioning and video captioning to image-stream-to-paragraph retrieval with an explicit coherence component.

Strengths
- Problem formulation: Tackles stream-to-paragraph description, moving beyond single-image/sentence tasks.
- Architectural idea: Integrates BRNN over sentences with an entity-based coherence signal and a sequence-level ranking objective.
- Data: Uses large weakly aligned blog corpora (~20K posts, 140K images) with segmentation/summarization pipeline.
- Evaluation: Combines automatic metrics (BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr, retrieval R@K/MedR) with AMT user studies and ablations.
- Practicality: Scalable KNN-based candidate assembly and approximate beam search avoid training long-sequence generators.

Weaknesses / Detailed comments
- Scoring function: Coherence term g is dot-producted with image features per-position, which semantically misaligns text-only coherence with image compatibility.
- “End-to-end” claim: Many components (doc2vec, VGG, entity grids) are precomputed/fixed, so the system is not truly end-to-end.
- Candidate generation: Per-image KNN limits output to training sentences and may be brittle; analysis of K, κ, and beam size M is missing.
- Coherence modeling: Unclear coreference pipeline and how entity grids are computed; need report of entity grid quality and entity counts.
- Evaluation reporting: Main text omits concrete numeric results, effect sizes, confidence intervals, and significance testing.
- Baseline breadth: Lacks hierarchical or paragraph-level generative baselines and comparisons to adapted video-to-paragraph methods.
- Objective/negatives: Uses random negatives; lacks harder negatives (shuffled/same-content) to test ordering/coherence explicitly.
- Generalization: Experiments confined to two tourism domains; cross-domain generalization is untested.

Clarity
- Define coherence computation precisely, including coreference resolution and the “concatenate parse trees” step.
- Justify zero-padding 64-d coherence histograms to 300-d instead of learned projections.
- Explain rationale for “shared parameters for O and q” and why g is compared with image features.
- Provide algorithmic details of the divide-and-conquer beam search (depth Q, complexity, pruning).
- Include full numeric results, hyperparameters (doc2vec training, BRNN size), and whether CNN is fine-tuned.

Originality
- Novel combination of known components (BRNN, entity-grid, ranking) for a new stream-to-paragraph retrieval task; integration is straightforward but distinctive.

Significance
- Addresses an important underexplored task with scalable methods and sizable data, but real-world impact is limited by KNN retrieval and domain restrictions.

Suggestions for improvement
- Redesign sequence-level term so coherence influences ordering directly (e.g., CRF/LSTM transitions or separate sequence score).
- Evaluate with harder negatives (shuffled orders, permuted content) to test order-sensitivity.
- Ablate K (neighbors), κ (augmentation), entity-grid history length, and test CNN fine-tuning effects.
- Add hierarchical sequence baselines (LSTM over sentences) and a generative “no-KNN” model for comparison.
- Report full metrics, effect sizes, statistical significance, AMT agreement; consider releasing code/data.
- Clarify and justify the coreference pipeline and coherence feature projection.

Arguments for acceptance
- Tackles an important, under-addressed problem: coherent paragraph description of image streams.
- Reasonable and novel integration of coherence modeling with multimodal sequence ranking.
- Uses large real-world weakly supervised data and provides scalable inference.
- Reported improvements (automatic and human) and ablations indicate coherence helps, especially for longer streams.

Arguments against acceptance
- Integration lacks deep technical novelty; several heuristic design choices are insufficiently justified.
- Evaluation omits full numeric detail, stronger baselines, and significance tests.
- Method constrained by KNN candidate retrieval and untested beyond tourism domains.
- “End-to-end” and “unsupervised” claims are overstated given reliance on precomputed features and supervised ranking.
