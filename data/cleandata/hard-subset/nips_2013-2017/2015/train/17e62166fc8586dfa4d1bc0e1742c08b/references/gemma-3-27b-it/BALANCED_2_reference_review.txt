This paper addresses the novel problem of generating a sequence of natural language sentences to describe an image stream, motivated by scenarios like travel photo collections. The authors propose a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) integrating CNNs for image representation, RNNs for sentence modeling, and a local coherence model for fluent transitions. They leverage blog posts as a large-scale, weakly supervised dataset and demonstrate improved performance over baselines through quantitative metrics (BLEU, Recall) and user studies.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The problem of generating sequential descriptions for image streams is a natural and important extension of single-image captioning, and this paper is among the first to tackle it.
* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed CRCN architecture appears well-motivated and combines established techniques (CNNs, RNNs) with a crucial coherence model tailored to the sequential nature of the task. The integration of these components seems reasonable and theoretically sound.
* **Dataset and Experimental Setup:** Utilizing blog posts as a data source is a clever way to create a large-scale, weakly supervised dataset for this task. The experimental setup, including comparisons with relevant baselines and user studies, is thorough.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The quantitative and qualitative results presented demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, particularly the benefit of incorporating the coherence model. The user studies provide additional validation.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, approach, and experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Baseline Limitations:** While the baselines are reasonable starting points, they primarily consist of extending single-image captioning models. A stronger baseline specifically designed for *sequential* description generation would provide a more rigorous comparison.  For example, exploring autoregressive models tailored for sequence generation, rather than simply concatenating individual image captions, could be more competitive.
* **Coherence Model Details:** The description of the local coherence model, while conceptually sound, lacks sufficient detail. The implementation of the entity grid and transition frequency calculation could benefit from more clarity. It’s not entirely clear *how* effectively this model captures long-range dependencies beyond local transitions.
* **Data Augmentation:** The data augmentation strategy (using top-κ summarized sentences) feels somewhat ad-hoc. A more principled exploration of data augmentation techniques specifically suited for this task might yield further improvements.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The paper does not discuss the sensitivity of the model to hyperparameters, such as the dropout rates, the dimensions of hidden layers, or the choice of κ in data augmentation.  Understanding this sensitivity is crucial for reproducibility and generalizability.
* **Limited Error Analysis:** While examples are provided, a more detailed error analysis would be beneficial. Understanding *where* the model fails (e.g., handling abrupt scene changes, maintaining long-term context) would provide valuable insights for future work.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a novel and important problem with a well-motivated approach.
* Demonstrates strong empirical results with both quantitative and qualitative evaluation.
* Utilizes a creative data source and comprehensive experimental setup.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The baseline comparisons could be stronger.
* The description of the coherence model could be more detailed.
*  A lack of discussion about hyperparameter sensitivity and a detailed error analysis limits the paper's impact.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that tackles a challenging and novel problem. The proposed CRCN architecture appears well-designed, and the experimental results are compelling. While the baselines could be more competitive and the details of the coherence model could be clarified, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of vision and language processing. I believe that with some revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above, this paper would be a valuable addition to NIPS.

**Recommendation:**

Weak Accept. I recommend acceptance pending minor revisions addressing the points raised above, particularly providing a more competitive baseline and clarifying the implementation details of the coherence model.  A brief discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity would also be beneficial.
