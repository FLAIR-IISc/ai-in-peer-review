- Overall assessment: Novel problem and CRCN approach are interesting, but paper is borderline reject due to weak baselines, limited evaluation, and presentation issues.  
- Problem & contribution: Extends single-image captioning to image streams with a coherence-aware CRCN combining CNNs, RNNs, and a local coherence model.  
- Relation to prior work: Positions work well relative to multimodal RNNs and coherence modeling, citing relevant literature.  
- Strength — Novel formulation: Addressing image streams instead of single images is a valuable and timely extension.  
- Strength — Coherence modeling: Explicit coherence component distinguishes the work from naïvely concatenating single-image captions.  
- Strength — Data source: Using blog posts as weakly supervised data is a clever, scalable approach with described preprocessing.  
- Strength — Evaluation breadth: Includes both quantitative metrics (BLEU, Recall) and human (AMT) studies.  
- Strength — Architecture detail: CRCN is described with sufficient mathematical and architectural detail.  
- Weakness — Weak baselines: Baselines are inadequate and do not include strong sequential/coherence-aware competitors or hierarchical models.  
- Weakness — Limited ablation: Ablation is insufficient; need per-component analyses and comparisons across coherence model choices and paragraph representations.  
- Weakness — Evaluation concerns: AMT setup lacks detail (stream lengths, ordering, statistical rigor) and qualitative examples are limited.  
- Weakness — Presentation: Paper is overly long and repetitive; some sections should be streamlined for clarity.  
- Weakness — Data augmentation choice: The κ=3 augmentation choice is arbitrary and unexplored.  
- Weakness — Coherence claim: Claim that the model captures entity-based coherence is weak and not convincingly supported.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel problem, well-motivated CRCN, and promising large-scale results with mixed evaluations.  
- Rejection arguments: Insufficient baselines, incomplete ablation/evaluation, and unclear impact justify rejection pending major revision.  
- Recommendation: Reject; require stronger baselines, more thorough ablation, clearer AMT/evaluation details, and improved presentation in a revision.
