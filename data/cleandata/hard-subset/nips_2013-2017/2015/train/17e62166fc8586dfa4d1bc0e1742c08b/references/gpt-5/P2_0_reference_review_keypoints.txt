- Problem & approach: CRCN generates sentence sequences for image streams using VGG features, a BRNN over doc2vec sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid coherence signal, trained with max-margin ranking and negative sampling.

- Data: Training data come from heuristic-aligned blog posts (NYC, Disneyland); text is summarized via LSA into κ key sentences and sentences represented by paragraph vectors.

- Inference: Retrieval-based test-time: K-NN images per position followed by beam/divide-and-conquer search over sentence concatenations.

- Empirical claim: CRCN outperforms several baselines (MLBL variants, CNN+RNN/LSTM, global matching) and a BRNN-only ablation; AMT shows stronger human preference for longer sequences.

- Length-bias concern: Scoring sums dot products over positions with no normalization, so longer sequences may get systematically higher scores and bias the margin objective.

- Coherence modeling concern: The global entity-grid vector g is added identically at each time step (g·v_t), which may not capture position-sensitive local sentence-to-sentence coherence.

- Architecture issue: Two stacked FC layers without nonlinearity are effectively linear at test time; claiming they differ “thanks to dropout” is incorrect or misleading.

- Noisy data pipeline: Heuristic alignment, LSA summarization, and coreference are noisy, yet the paper lacks error analysis or robustness checks on segmentation/summarization quality.

- Evaluation fairness: Unclear whether baselines (e.g., CNN+LSTM) were trained on the same preprocessed blog data and vocabulary; some structured sequence baselines are missing.

- Metric limitations: Paragraph-level BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR are weak; although AMT helps, more coherence-specific evaluations (e.g., entity-grid-based) are needed.

- Clarity issues: “End-to-end” and “unsupervised” claims are overstated given fixed doc2vec, external parsers/coref, and non-differentiable entity-grid components.

- Missing training details: Need clarification on handling sequence length in the loss, whether negatives are length-matched, and any score normalization used.

- Parameter/design rationale: The paper should justify sharing parameters for O and q, and explain the choice to omit nonlinearities between FC layers.

- Sensitivity/runtime: Paper lacks analyses for hyperparameters (K, M, κ) and runtime/performance tradeoffs.

- Originality: The task formulation and use of entity-grid in multimodal ranking are novel in this area, but the method is a combination of established components rather than a new learning principle.

- Significance: Task is important and datasets realistic; retrieval-based approach shows promise but may not generalize beyond training corpus and has weakly grounded coherence-image interactions.

- Pros: Addresses paragraph-level description, uses large-scale real data, provides human and automatic evaluation, and presents a practical search strategy.

- Cons: Potential length bias, weakly formulated coherence-image interaction, overstated claims, incorrect architectural claims, suboptimal/missing baselines, limited error/sensitivity analysis, and unclear reproducibility (no released data/code).

- Questions for authors: Do you normalize scores by sequence length or sample length-matched negatives? How were generative baselines trained on your corpus? Why no nonlinearity or position-aware coherence term? Sensitivity to K, M, κ and parser/coref errors?

- Recommendation: Borderline reject — promising direction and human-validated gains, but technical issues (coherence term, length bias), overstated claims, and incomplete baselines/evaluation reduce confidence.
