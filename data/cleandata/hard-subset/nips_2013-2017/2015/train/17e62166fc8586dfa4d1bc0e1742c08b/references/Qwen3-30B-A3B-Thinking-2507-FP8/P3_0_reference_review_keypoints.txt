- Proposes CRCN to generate coherent sentence sequences for image streams by combining CNN image features, BRNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Uses blog posts (NYC & Disneyland datasets, ~20K posts/140K images) as practical image-text training data with quantitative (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and AMT human evaluations.  
- Strength: Clearly motivated extension from single-image captioning to image streams, addressing real user needs for sequential descriptions.  
- Strength: CRCN architecture effectively integrates VGGNet, BRNN, and entity-transition coherence modeling.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation including ablation studies showing coherence model improves retrieval metrics, especially on longer sequences.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty — approach largely combines existing techniques and insufficiently differentiates from recent video captioning work.  
- Weakness: Coherence model is based on prior work (Barzilay & Lapata 2008) and the paper does not justify why this implementation is especially effective for image captioning.  
- Weakness: Modest gains in language metrics (BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR) and only small improvements over the RCN baseline.  
- Weakness: Human evaluation differences are smaller than quantitative metrics suggest and were limited to short (5-image) sequences.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of potential biases and generalization issues from using blog posts as training data (style/topic limitations).  
- Technical: Methodology and experiments are sound and well explained, with valuable ablation studies, but lack deeper theoretical analysis of why coherence helps.  
- Significance: Natural and useful extension to captioning but contribution is incremental and closely related to video captioning literature.  
- Recommendation: Clarify distinctions from video captioning work and justify suitability for image streams.  
- Recommendation: Provide deeper analysis of why the coherence model improves retrieval more than language metrics.  
- Recommendation: Discuss limitations and potential biases of blog-post training data and test generalization to other domains.  
- Recommendation: Extend human evaluations to longer sequences (8–10 images) to better demonstrate coherence benefits.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions / borderline acceptance — practical, well-evaluated work but incrementally innovative for a top-tier venue.
