- Introduces generating a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream (vs. single-image captioning) using blog posts as data.
- Proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN) combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and a local coherence model.
- Evaluated on NYC and Disneyland blog datasets (~20K posts, 140K images) and outperforms Karpathy et al., Kiros et al., and Vinyals et al.

- Novel problem formulation extending captioning to image-stream/sentence-sequence with practical relevance (e.g., photo album summarization).
- Strong experimental validation using BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall, AMT user studies, and ablation analyses showing the coherence model’s contribution.
- Clever, practical data source: blog posts naturally provide image-text sequences with justified preprocessing steps.
- Technically sound architecture integrating established components; appropriate max-margin training.

- Lacks theoretical analysis explaining why the approach, especially the coherence model, improves performance.
- Overstates novelty; related work (e.g., ranking/retrieval of image sequences) addresses similar problems and needs clearer differentiation.
- Evaluation limited to tourism blogs (NYC, Disneyland), raising generalization concerns and lacking cross-domain experiments.
- Missing implementation details (hyperparameters for coherence model, search complexity, and specifics of the divide-and-conquer retrieval) hindering reproducibility.

- Quality: technically solid methods and thorough experiments, but limited by missing theory and implementation specifics.
- Clarity: generally well-written and organized though coherence model description could be clearer.
- Originality: novel problem formulation; architecture combines known components with coherence modeling as the main contribution.
- Significance: meaningful real-world applications and promising empirical improvements, with potential for broader multimodal learning impact.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Suggest clarifying relationship with related work [11] to better distinguish contributions.
- Recommend adding a brief theoretical discussion on the necessity of the coherence model.
- Advise noting limitations/biases of blog-based (tourism) data.
- Request more details on the divide-and-conquer candidate retrieval (exact recurrence structure).
- Suggest adding a small cross-domain experiment to demonstrate generalizability.
