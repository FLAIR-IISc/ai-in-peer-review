- Task & contribution: Proposes CRCN to generate sequences of sentences for image streams, combining CNNs, BRNNs, and a local coherence model; evaluated on a blog-post dataset with BLEU/Recall and AMT.

- Novelty: Tackles the relatively unexplored problem of multi-image, multi-sentence description, extending beyond single-image captioning.

- Technical approach: CRCN architecture is well-motivated; integrating a dedicated coherence model addresses sequential fluency.

- Dataset & experiments: Uses blog posts as weakly-supervised data; shows improvements over several baselines and includes human (AMT) evaluation.

- Related work & writing: Thorough literature review and clear, well-structured presentation.

- Baselines limitation: Comparisons mostly adapt single-sentence models; missing a sequence-to-sequence baseline tailored to image streams.

- Coherence model concerns: Model based on parsing/entity grids is complex and potentially brittle; more analysis needed on robustness and why it helps.

- Dataset limitations: Blog-post data has biases (author curation, tourism focus) that may limit generalizability.

- Evaluation metrics: BLEU and Recall may not capture multi-sentence coherence or narrative flow; better coherence-focused metrics are needed.

- Quality: Rated 8/10 — technically sound with solid experiments.

- Clarity: Rated 8/10 — generally clear though coherence model description could be simplified.

- Originality: Rated 7/10 — component combination not wholly novel but applied interestingly to this task.

- Significance: Rated 7/10 — promising application areas, but broader impact and generalizability require more work.

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a novel problem, technically sound approach, promising results, strong literature review and experiments.

- Arguments against acceptance: Limited baselines, potentially brittle coherence model, and dataset biases.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — request stronger baselines, deeper analysis of the coherence model, and discussion of dataset limitations.
