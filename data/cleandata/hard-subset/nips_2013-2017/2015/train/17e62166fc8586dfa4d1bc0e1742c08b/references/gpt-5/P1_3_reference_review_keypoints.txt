- Tackles generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams using a retrieval-based CRCN combining CNN features, BRNN over paragraph vectors, and an entity-grid coherence model.  
- Retrieval pipeline: retrieve sentences from visually nearest training images per query image, form sequence candidates, and score with CRCN via a max-margin objective aligning ordered sentence and image streams plus coherence.  
- Training data mined from travel blogs with image–text alignment by proximity, LSA summarization per image, and top-κ (κ=3) sentence augmentation.  
- Evaluation: BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics (R@K, median rank), and AMT studies; CRCN outperforms single-image captioning/retrieval baselines, with larger gains on longer sequences.  
- Novelty: combines BRNN sequence-level content modeling with classical entity-grid discourse coherence in a sequence retrieval framework.  
- Methodology: overall architecture and training objective are reasonable; entity grids are a principled way to inject discourse signals.  
- Key technical concern: Eq.3 ties a single global coherence vector g_k to image vectors summed over time—this design is non-standard and the coherence–image interaction is unclear.  
- Potential length bias: scores sum over time so longer sequences may be favored; unclear whether length normalization is applied.  
- Data alignment noise: image–text alignment via nearest text block and LSA is noisy and its impact on training is not quantified.  
- Negative sampling: using 100 negatives per positive may be insufficient for sequence retrieval; no sensitivity analysis provided.  
- Entity-grid integration appears ad hoc: 64-D histograms zero-padded to 300-D then mapped to 4096-D; grid is non-differentiable so gradients likely do not flow—clarification needed.  
- Evaluation baseline issue: comparisons to single-image generative captioners may be unfair; missing stronger sequence-retrieval baselines (e.g., n-gram/LM, CRF/HMM over candidates).  
- Retrieval metric details missing: unclear what constitutes ground-truth retrieval for sequences and whether lexical overlap is required; AMT statistical significance not reported.  
- Clarity/reproducibility: generally understandable but some overloaded notation and insufficient motivation for modeling choices; request release of datasets, preprocessing scripts, doc2vec details, parser/settings, and runtime information.  
- Significance: task is impactful and approach is practical, but dependency on domain-specific corpora and heuristics limits broader generalizability; cross-domain tests would help.  
- Pros: addresses an underexplored task, principled discourse inclusion, large-scale weak supervision, consistent improvements, and clear ablations.  
- Cons: unintuitive coherence–image interaction, possible length bias, noisy alignment, skewed baselines, and reproducibility/evaluation under-specified.  
- Suggestions: clarify Eq.3 and ablate pairwise transition-based coherence, address length normalization, add stronger retrieval baselines (KNN+LM, CRF/HMM), analyze robustness to alignment/κ, specify doc2vec split and runtime, and report automatic coherence metrics.  
- Overall: solid, well-motivated paper with novel integration and promising results; needs clarifications and stronger baselines—reviewer leans toward acceptance.
