- Novel problem: generating a sequence of natural-language sentences to describe an image stream, addressed by the paper.
- Proposed model: Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN) combining CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and a local coherence model.
- Motivation: users take photo sequences (e.g., travel) and need a coherent narrative rather than isolated captions; abrupt visual changes make coherence challenging.
- Relation to prior work: extends image captioning and video description to sequence-to-sequence image-stream narration and emphasizes sentence coherence.
- Dataset: practical collection from blog posts provides natural image-text sequences for training.
- Evaluation: comprehensive comparison to strong baselines (LBL, CNN+RNN, CNN+LSTM) using BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and retrieval metrics.
- Ablation: CRCN vs RCN shows the coherence model meaningfully improves performance.
- Experimental design: sound train/val/test splits and MTurk human studies; paper acknowledges BLEU limitations for paragraph evaluation.
- Reproducibility: sufficient methodological detail provided, though some implementation specifics are in supplementary material.
- Clarity: well-written and organized with helpful figures; BRNN description could use more intuitive explanation.
- Related work coverage: thorough discussion that clearly situates the contribution relative to existing literature.
- Originality: novel problem formulation and novel integration of an existing coherence model into a multimodal architecture for image streams.
- Significance: practically important for social media, travel blogging, and multimedia search; unsupervised blog-based training increases applicability.
- Results: strong empirical improvements over baselines in automatic metrics and human evaluations; coherence model helps especially for longer texts.
- Strengths: novel task formulation, comprehensive evaluation, practical dataset, strong empirical gains, and well-integrated architecture.
- Weaknesses: coherence model itself not novel; limited analysis of computational complexity and scalability; potential selection bias in blog data; insufficient failure-case analysis.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, particularly addressing computational complexity and deeper analysis of failure cases.
