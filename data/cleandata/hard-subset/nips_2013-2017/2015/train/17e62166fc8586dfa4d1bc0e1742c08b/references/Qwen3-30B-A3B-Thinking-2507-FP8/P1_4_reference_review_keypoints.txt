- Paper extends image captioning to generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams using a novel CRCN architecture.
- CRCN combines CNN for images, bidirectional RNN for sentences, and a local coherence model to ensure smooth transitions.
- Authors collect a substantial dataset (20K blog posts, 140K images) from tourism blogs and preprocess it into text-image parallel sequences.
- Evaluation uses BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies, with ablation comparing CRCN vs RCN.
- Strength: Clear problem motivation with real-world use cases and an illustrative figure.
- Strength: Well-designed, unified architecture integrating CNN, BRNN, and coherence modeling.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation including quantitative metrics, user studies, and ablation analysis.
- Strength: Practical and substantial dataset collection from blogs with justified preprocessing.
- Strength: Thorough baseline comparison against state-of-the-art single-sentence captioning models.
- Weakness: Incremental novelty—components largely adapted from prior work and coherence model borrowed from Barzilay & Lapata.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of performance on very long image sequences (e.g., >10 images).
- Weakness: Insufficient evidence that multi-sentence sequences outperform concatenated single-sentence descriptions.
- Weakness: Coherence model implementation (entity grid, 64-dim transition descriptor) under-explained.
- Weakness: Evaluation confined to tourism domains (NYC, Disneyland), limiting claims of domain-agnosticism.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance—well-motivated, sound architecture, comprehensive evaluation despite incremental novelty.
- Suggested improvements: Clarify technical novelty vs prior multimodal networks.
- Suggested improvements: Provide deeper analysis across different sequence lengths.
- Suggested improvements: Offer stronger evidence that sentence sequences are necessary over single-sentence alternatives.
- Suggested improvements: Add more implementation detail for the coherence model.
- Pro: Addresses a clear gap with practical dataset and demonstrates coherence model importance via ablation.
- Con: Novelty incremental, limited long-sequence analysis, insufficient justification for sequences, and under-detailed coherence model.
