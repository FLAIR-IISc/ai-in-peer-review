- Problem: Introduces generation of a sequence of natural sentences describing an image stream, distinct from single-image captioning and video captioning.  
- Method: Proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Data: Leverages human-authored blog posts as natural image-text sequences and releases a sizable dataset (20K posts, 140K images) focused on tourism.  
- Novelty: Formalizes a new multimodal task and highlights the importance of coherence for image streams with sharp visual changes.  
- Technical quality: Methodology is sound, integrates established techniques effectively, and includes comprehensive ablation studies showing each component’s value.  
- Evaluation: Uses quantitative metrics and user preference studies (AMT), showing significant improvements, especially for longer passages.  
- Significance: Addresses a practically relevant problem (e.g., travel/photo albums) and opens new directions in multimodal sequence generation.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, well-organized, reproducible, and supported by clear figures and examples.  
- Dataset generalizability concern: Evaluation is limited to tourism blogs (NYC, Disneyland); generalization to other domains is not analyzed.  
- Coherence model limitation: Coherence relies solely on entity/coreference transitions and may miss thematic or pragmatic aspects of discourse.  
- User study limitation: Human evaluation uses only 100 test streams (max 5 images) and a small additional 8–10 image test, limiting evidence for longer sequences.  
- Overall assessment & recommendation: Strong, novel contribution with thorough evaluation; recommend acceptance, with minor suggestions to discuss coherence model limits and domain-specific challenges.
