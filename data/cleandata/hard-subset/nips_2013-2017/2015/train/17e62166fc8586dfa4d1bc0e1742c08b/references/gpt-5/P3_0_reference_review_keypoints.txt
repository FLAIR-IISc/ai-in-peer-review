- Problem & approach: Proposes CRCN to describe image streams with sentence sequences, combining VGG features, doc2vec BRNN for context-aware sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid coherence model, trained with max-margin retrieval on blog-mined image–sentence pairs.

- Inference & data: At test time retrieves per-image candidate sentences from K nearest training images and uses divide-and-conquer beam search to select the best sequence; dataset assembled from travel blogs (NYC, Disneyland).

- Experimental claim: Reports improvements over single-image captioning baselines on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, and AMT user preferences.

- Strength — problem novelty: Moves beyond single-image captioning to multi-sentence image-stream description, an underexplored and timely problem.

- Strength — coherence modeling: Novel use of entity-grid coherence modeling in a vision–language setting.

- Strength — architecture: Integrates context-aware BRNN text embeddings with a coherence signal and image features; per-sentence vectors use past and future context.

- Strength — practical dataset & human eval: Constructs large-scale domain datasets from blogs and includes human studies; ablations support BRNN and coherence utility.

- Weakness — retrieval vs generation: Method retrieves and stitches sentences rather than generating, making comparisons to generative baselines potentially unfair and advantaging n-gram metrics.

- Weakness — scoring design: Coherence term g · v_t is text-only and only interacts with image features via a repeated dot product, which poorly models sentence-to-sentence visual transitions and lacks principled justification.

- Weakness — evaluation clarity: Retrieval metric computation and "ground truth" definition are unclear given train/test disjointness and absent test-paragraph candidates; procedures for generative baselines are unspecified.

- Weakness — dataset noise: Blog segmentation and LSA summarization likely introduce alignment noise; no quality checks, inter-annotator verification, or analysis of sensitivity to κ are reported.

- Weakness — limited generalization: Evaluation restricted to two tourism domains with domain-specific doc2vec and unfine-tuned VGG, undermining claims of domain-agnostic applicability.

- Weakness — missing details: Key training and implementation details (learning rates, epochs, CNN fine-tuning, sequence stats, beam-search runtime) and the ad hoc padding of the coherence vector are not reported.

- Quality assessment: Components are individually standard and sound, but the scoring integration (especially coherence vector role) is questionable; search optimality and sensitivity analyses are missing; baseline suite lacks strong retrieval+coherence reranker and AMT stats are incomplete.

- Clarity assessment: Paper is readable and organized but needs clarification on retrieval metrics, training details, CNN fine-tuning, test-time complexity, and rationale/illustration for the g · v_t term.

- Originality assessment: Combination of BRNN and entity-grid is novel for this task, but solution is largely a known-components assembly and remains retrieval-based rather than generative.

- Significance assessment: Addresses an important problem with promising results, but limited grounding of coherence, evaluation issues, and baseline fairness reduce overall impact to moderate.

- Key suggestions: Redesign scoring to model adjacent transitions (pairwise/CRF terms), add a retrieval+coherence reranker baseline and BRNN-only ablation, clarify/revise retrieval metrics and report AMT agreement/significance, provide ablations on K/M/κ and runtime, fine-tune CNN and learn a projection for coherence features.

- Recommendation: Borderline reject due to concerns about coherence–vision coupling, evaluation clarity, and baseline fairness, though the work could be much stronger with revisions.
