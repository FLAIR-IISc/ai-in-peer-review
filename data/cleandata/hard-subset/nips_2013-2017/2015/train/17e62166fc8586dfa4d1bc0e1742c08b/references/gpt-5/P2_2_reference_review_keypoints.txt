- Paper tackles multi-sentence "image stream to sentence sequence" generation using a retrieval-based coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN).
- CRCN combines VGG image features, doc2vec sentence embeddings with a BRNN for context, and an entity-grid coherence model for discourse transitions.
- System retrieves candidate sentences per image from a large blog corpus and uses beam search with a max-margin compatibility score to rank concatenations.
- Training data are weakly supervised, automatically aligned from blogs using paragraph tokenization, distance heuristics, and LSA summarization.
- Reported improvements over single-image captioning baselines in BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, and AMT human preferences, especially for longer sequences.
- Technical components are standard and reasonable, and the ranking objective with negative sampling and BPTT is appropriate.
- Key technical concern: coherence score formulation uses a single g_k applied to every image timestep, effectively g_k·sum(v), which may not model local temporal alignment as claimed.
- Coherence descriptor handling is ad hoc: 64-d transition frequencies are zero-padded to 300-d before FC layers instead of using a learned projection or normalization.
- Retrieval search (divide-and-conquer beam search) is heuristic and needs empirical comparison to exhaustive search to support "almost optimal" claims.
- Claim of "completely unsupervised" is overstated given reliance on automatic alignment heuristics and external preprocessing; alignment noise should be analyzed.
- Empirical protocol includes human evaluation and ablations, but stronger diagnostics (sequence length effects, diversity, ablations removing g or replacing entity-grid) are missing.
- Paper is generally well organized and reproducible with reported implementation details, but has minor notational/typographical issues and unclear dimensions/normalization.
- The claim of "end-to-end" training is misleading since doc2vec, parsing, and coreference are external and trained separately.
- Problem formulation (multi-sentence coherent descriptions) is timely and distinct from single-image or short-video captioning.
- Methodologically, CRCN is a thoughtful integration of known components rather than a fundamentally new paradigm; entity-grid integration into multimodal ranking is a novel design choice.
- The large-scale weakly aligned blog corpus is a valuable resource if released, but retrieval-based generation limits generalization beyond the training domain.
- Strengths: clear motivation, explicit discourse coherence modeling, large-scale weak supervision, and comprehensive evaluation including human studies.
- Weaknesses: overstated unsupervised/end-to-end claims, limited analysis of alignment/parser errors, heuristic search without justification, ad hoc coherence-image interaction, and retrieval-only diversity limits.
- Suggested ablations: different coherence formulations (g·sum, per-timestep, learned attention), learned projection for entity-grid, and comparisons to paragraph-level retrieval baselines.
- Suggested analyses: effect of sequence length and domain shift, diagnostics on alignment noise and parser/coref accuracy, and exhaustive search comparisons on short sequences.
- Overall assessment: quality good with caveats, clarity good, originality moderate-to-high, and significance moderate due to retrieval limitations.
- Pro-accept: addresses an important problem, integrates discourse coherence into multimodal modeling, and scales with weak supervision with human-evaluated gains.
- Con-accept: incremental novelty, under-analyzed technical choices (coherence scoring, search), and limited transferability without released data/code or deeper analysis.
