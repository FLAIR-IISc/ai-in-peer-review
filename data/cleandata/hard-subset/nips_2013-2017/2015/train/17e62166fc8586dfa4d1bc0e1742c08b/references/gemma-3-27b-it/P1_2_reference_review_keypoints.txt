- Problem: Generate coherent sequences of sentences describing image streams, moving beyond single-image captioning.  
- Proposed method: Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN) combining CNNs for images, BRNNs for sentences, and a local coherence model.  
- Data contribution: Use of blog posts as a large, weakly supervised image–text corpus for training.  
- Empirical results: CRCN shows strong quantitative performance (BLEU, Recall) and user-preferred outputs in AMT studies.  
- Evaluation: Comprehensive evaluation using both automatic metrics and human studies.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written and clearly explains methods and experiments.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends image captioning and video description literature, emphasizing textual coherence for abrupt visual changes in image streams.  
- Novelty: Combines retrieval-based and generative approaches with an explicit focus on multi-sentence fluency.  
- Strength — problem formulation: Tackles an important, under-explored real-world task (summarizing travel photos, etc.).  
- Strength — architecture: Coherence-aware CRCN is well-motivated and addresses sequence-level challenges.  
- Strength — dataset creativity: Clever use of blogs to overcome annotation scarcity.  
- Strength — empirical credibility: Strong results backed by both metrics and human preference.  
- Weakness — scalability: Retrieval-based approach may not scale well to much larger image databases; divide-and-conquer limitations not fully explored.  
- Weakness — parsing dependency: Local coherence model relies on accurate parse trees, risking degraded performance from parsing errors.  
- Weakness — data bias: Blog-derived training data may bias outputs toward narrative/storytelling styles and limit generalization.  
- Weakness — baselines: Lacks comparison to more recent sequence-to-sequence models (e.g., Transformers).  
- Weakness — ablation depth: Ablation studies exist but need deeper analysis of the coherence model and alternative coherence metrics.  
- For acceptance: Novel problem, effective architecture, strong results, creative dataset, clear presentation.  
- Against acceptance: Concerns about scalability, parsing dependency, and dataset bias.  
- Overall: Strong paper with significant contribution and compelling results; recommended for acceptance.  
- Rating: Accept (7/10).  
- Suggestions: Discuss retrieval scalability and optimizations; test coherence robustness to parsing errors; acknowledge blog-data bias; compare to modern seq2seq/Transformer models; provide deeper ablation of the coherence model.
