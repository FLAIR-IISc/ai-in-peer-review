- Novel problem: generate a sequence of natural sentences describing an image stream (multiple photos) rather than one caption per image, motivated by real-world photo albums.

- Proposed method: Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN) combining CNNs for images, bidirectional RNNs for sentences, and a local coherence model for smooth sentence transitions.

- Data: trained on blog posts (NYC and Disneyland datasets; ~20K posts, 140K+ images) extracted as image-sentence sequences.

- Evaluation: thorough—BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics (top-K recall, median rank), AMT human evaluations, and ablation studies comparing CRCN to RCN and 1NN.

- Strength: well-motivated problem formulation extending image captioning to sequences with practical relevance.

- Strength: technically sound CRCN architecture that explicitly models sentence-to-sentence coherence.

- Strength: strong empirical evaluation and ablations demonstrating component contributions.

- Strength: innovative use of blog posts as natural image-text sequence training data.

- Weakness: limited scope—experiments confined to tourism blogs, unclear generalization to other domains or image-stream types.

- Weakness: insufficient analysis/justification of blog data quality and potential biases (e.g., topical, cultural, gender).

- Weakness: inadequate comparison with existing video-captioning approaches that also handle image sequences.

- Weakness: lacks discussion of computational complexity and scalability for longer image sequences and retrieval.

- Weakness: coherence model description (Section 3.2) is dense and needs clearer explanation.

- Suggested improvements: analyze dataset characteristics/biases, compare more fully to video captioning, clarify coherence model, and discuss computational requirements.

- Clarity: generally well-written and reproducible, but clarify the coherence model and explicitly define "image stream" (typical length, temporal relations).

- Originality: clear and novel extension of captioning to sequence generation with a novel coherence-oriented integration.

- Significance: relevant for photo-album captioning and related applications, but perceived impact limited by narrow evaluation scope.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing dataset analysis, video-captioning comparison, coherence-model clarity, and scalability. 

- Final ratings: Quality—Strong; Clarity—Good; Originality—Strong; Significance—Moderate.
