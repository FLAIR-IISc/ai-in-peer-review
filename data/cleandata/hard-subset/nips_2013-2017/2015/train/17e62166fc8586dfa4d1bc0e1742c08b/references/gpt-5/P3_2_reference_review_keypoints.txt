- Paper tackles describing image streams with sentence sequences using a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN features, BRNN over Doc2Vec, and an entity-grid coherence model.
- Training data built weakly-supervised from travel blogs by aligning images to nearby paragraphs and summarizing segments; model trained with max-margin sequence-level ranking loss and beam-style retrieval.
- Evaluation on NYC and Disneyland corpora uses BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, and AMT user studies, showing CRCN outperforms several baselines.

Strengths
- Moves beyond single-image captioning to sequence-level descriptions for photo streams, addressing a timely and relevant problem.
- Architecture is simple and interpretable, combining sentence-sequence BRNN and entity-grid coherence signals with mostly end-to-end multimodal scoring.
- Data construction is scalable and weakly supervised, producing reasonably large corpora from blogs.
- Evaluation is multi-faceted (automatic metrics, retrieval, AMT) and includes ablation showing coherence helps for longer sequences.
- Paper is overall readable and reproducible at a high level.

Weaknesses and technical concerns
- Scoring function is problematic: global coherence vector gk is dot-multiplied with every image vector, effectively acting as a repeated bias and lacking clear justification.
- Paper omits how sequence score Skl handles variable-length mismatches during training and evaluation (normalization, alignment, truncation unspecified).
- Text modeling relies on fixed Doc2Vec and an ungated BRNN, weaker than contemporary word-level LSTMs/GRUs and unjustified given existing baselines.
- Entity-grid features depend on noisy parsing/coreference with no error analysis; 64-d padding to 300-d and no backprop through parsers weakens the “end-to-end” claim.
- Retrieval pipeline restricts candidates via KNN images, risking exclusion of correct sentences and lacks beam-sensitivity analysis or exact-search comparison.
- Dataset alignment and LSA summarization are noisy with no human validation; automatic metrics and single ground-truth comparisons are potentially misleading.
- Reproducibility suffers from missing critical details (Doc2Vec training/fine-tuning, normalization, negative sampling, beam params) and unclear dataset/code availability.

Questions for the authors
- How are length mismatches handled in Skl during training/evaluation: normalization, truncation, or alignment?
- Why apply the global coherence vector to every image independently instead of aggregating image features or modeling transitions separately?
- Are Doc2Vec embeddings fixed or fine-tuned during CRCN training, and what is the impact?
- Can you report sensitivity to K (KNN), M (beam size), κ (augmentation), and entity-grid history length?
- Is there any human validation of image–text alignment and summarization quality on a subset?
- Will the NYC dataset and preprocessing scripts be released?

Suggestions for improvement
- Redesign scoring to separate global coherence from per-image compatibility (e.g., hierarchical model, CRF transitions, or gk·mean(vlt)).
- Use stronger text encoders (BiLSTM over words with attention) and learn sentence embeddings jointly.
- Provide ablations and sensitivity analyses for K, M, κ, and entity-grid parameters; include exact-search comparisons for small N.
- Add human checks for alignment quality and targeted coherence evaluations (ordering tests, entity transition plausibility).
- Report statistical significance and inter-annotator agreement for AMT and automatic metrics.
- Clarify all training and preprocessing details and consider releasing code and data.

Assessment
- Originality: Moderate — interesting problem and explicit coherence integration, but technical integration is straightforward and scoring questionable.
- Quality: Mixed — plausible and promising but missing details, weaker text modeling, and limited evaluation validation.
- Clarity: Generally clear but with important ambiguities (score definition, length handling, training specifics).
- Significance: Potentially impactful for visual storytelling but currently constrained by retrieval and dataset preprocessing issues.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel, timely problem formulation with an interpretable model that demonstrates coherence improves results.
- Scalable weakly supervised dataset construction and promising user study outcomes.

Arguments against acceptance
- Questionable scoring design and unclear handling of variable-length sequences.
- Shallow text modeling, limited coherence integration, insufficient validation and reproducibility.

Recommendation
- Weak reject: interesting direction but needs clearer/more principled modeling, detailed experimental validation, and reproducibility improvements.
