- Novel task: generating coherent sequences of sentences for image streams rather than single-image captions.  
- Proposed model: Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN image features, BRNN language modeling, and a local coherence module.  
- Key idea: leverage large, unsupervised blog-post data as a proxy parallel corpus of image-sentence streams.  
- Empirical claims: CRCN outperforms several strong baselines on BLEU/Recall and human evaluations (AMT).  
- Relation to prior work: extends image captioning and video-description literature, combining retrieval and coherence modeling ideas.  
- Uses established tools (paragraph vectors, parse trees) in a novel CRCN integration.  
- Strength — coherence modeling: explicit local coherence component to ensure fluent transitions between sentences.  
- Strength — data strategy: using blog data is a practical way to obtain large-scale pseudo-parallel stream data.  
- Strength — evaluation: thorough combination of automatic metrics and user studies, including tests across varying stream lengths.  
- Strength — baselines and clarity: compares to strong recent models and presents the architecture clearly.  
- Weakness — data bias: blog-authored text introduces stylistic/intention biases that may limit generalization.  
- Weakness — complexity: many components and hyperparameters make reproduction and ablation of contributions difficult.  
- Weakness — limited qualitative analysis: more failure cases and deeper qualitative insights are needed.  
- Weakness — LSA summarization: using LSA feels dated; modern seq2seq summarizers might improve results.  
- Weakness — beam search: choice of beam size M=50 is not justified; sensitivity analysis missing.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel problem, sound architecture, strong empirical results, clever data usage, and clear presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: reproducibility concerns from complexity, insufficient handling of data biases, and limited qualitative failure analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address bias discussion, provide deeper ablations and failure analyses, consider newer summarization methods, and justify beam-search parameter.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
