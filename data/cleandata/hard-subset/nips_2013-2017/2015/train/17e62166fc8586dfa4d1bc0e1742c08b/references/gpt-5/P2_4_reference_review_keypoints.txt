- Paper proposes CRCN: combines VGG fc7 image features, a bidirectional RNN over doc2vec sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid coherence model to describe image streams with coherent sentence sequences.
- Training data mined from travel blogs: posts segmented and summarized (LSA) into image–sentence sequences; top-κ sentences per segment augment training.
- Learning uses a max-margin ranking to score compatibility between image sequences and sentence sequences; test-time retrieval uses nearest-neighbor image candidates plus an approximate beam/divide-and-conquer search.
- Experiments on two large datasets (NYC, Disneyland; ~20k posts, 140k images) show improvements over single-image captioning baselines on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, and AMT user studies.
- Technical soundness: pipeline and ranking objective are coherent; BRNN design and entity-grid coherence model are appropriate choices.
- Evidence: multiple metrics, two sizable datasets, and ablations (1NN, BRNN-only vs CRCN) support component contributions, especially coherence for longer sequences.
- Concern: scoring function uses a global coherence vector g_k dotted with every image feature, duplicating a global term and coupling text coherence to image features in an unclear way.
- Concern: summed scores over timesteps risk length bias; negatives often differ in length and no explicit normalization is applied.
- Concern: training data alignment heuristics (distance-based assignment, LSA summarization) may introduce noise; no analysis of alignment quality or its effect is presented.
- Concern: baselines are mostly single-image captioners concatenated per position; missing comparisons to text-only paragraph coherence models or neural paragraph LM re-rankers.
- Concern: reproducibility gaps—doc2vec training specifics, vocabulary handling, candidate generation complexity/timing, dataset/code release—and no statistical significance tests reported.
- Clarity issue: notation and architectural details need tightening (e.g., Eqn.2 padding of coherence vector, shared FC parameters rationale).
- Clarity issue: scoring equation should justify g term and address length normalization; retrieval search lacks complexity estimates and failure-case discussion.
- Clarity issue: “unsupervised” overstates the method; it is weakly supervised via automatically aligned text–image streams; minor notation and language issues persist.
- Originality: task is a natural extension beyond single-image captioning; combining entity-grid coherence with multimodal ranking is a novel combination though components are standard.
- Significance: shows consistent improvements and coherence benefit increases with stream length; mining blog data is potentially useful.
- Limitation: retrieval-based approach cannot generate novel sentences and generalization beyond travel blogs is untested.
- Pros: clear problem formulation, sensible multimodal/coherence architecture, comprehensive evaluation with human studies, and demonstrated benefit for longer sequences.
- Cons: questionable global coherence scoring and length normalization, heuristic noisy data construction, limited baselines for paragraph-level coherence, and reproducibility/clarity issues.
- Key questions for authors: justify g_k · v_l,t and discuss length normalization; report sensitivity to κ, K, and beam size M (ablation); provide alignment quality or robustness analysis; compare entity-grid to a neural paragraph LM re-ranker.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — timely and relevant work with encouraging results, but needs fixes to scoring design, clarity, baselines, and reproducibility to be stronger.
