- Tackles the novel and important problem of generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams.
- Proposes CRCN: a CNN+RNN architecture augmented with a local coherence model to improve inter-sentence flow.
- Positions the work well within related literature but could better connect to broader NLP discourse modeling.
- Uses a large weakly supervised dataset (140K images, 20K blogs) mined from online blogs to address data scarcity.
- Evaluation combines quantitative metrics (BLEU, Recall) with user studies for a more rounded assessment.
- Thorough experimental setup including multiple baselines and ablation studies.
- Novelty: moving beyond single-image captioning to coherent descriptions of image streams.
- Strength: integrating an explicit coherence model into visual captioning is a valuable contribution.
- Clarity issues: the coherence model description (entity grid, 64-dim rep) is dense and hard to follow.
- Modest quantitative gains: CRCN often only slightly outperforms baselines, questioning practical effectiveness.
- Data quality concerns: blog-derived captions introduce noise and potential alignment/bias issues needing deeper analysis.
- Baselines are sometimes weak; comparisons to stronger, attention-based captioning models are missing.
- Over-reliance on BLEU despite its limitations for longer text; should explore better metrics for paragraph-level evaluation.
- Missing experimental details: sensitivity to coherence/history parameter, choice of κ=3 for augmentation, and absent standard deviations in Table 1.
- User study details lacking: unclear AMT instructions and whether annotators saw full sequences or paired comparisons.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on improving clarity, stronger baselines, and deeper dataset/evaluation analysis.
