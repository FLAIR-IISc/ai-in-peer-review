This paper tackles the problem of generating a sequence of natural language sentences to describe an image stream, a natural extension of single-image captioning. The authors propose a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) integrating CNNs, BRNNs, and a local coherence model. They leverage blog posts as a weakly supervised training dataset and demonstrate improved performance compared to baselines on both quantitative metrics and user studies.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty and Problem Setting:** Extending image captioning to image *streams* is a logical and interesting progression. The motivation of describing experiences captured as photo streams is relatable and provides a clear use case.
* **Technical Approach:** The CRCN architecture appears well-motivated. Integrating a coherence model is a key contribution, recognizing the need for fluent transitions between sentences in a sequence. The combination of CNNs, RNNs, and the coherence model appears thoughtfully designed.
* **Dataset Creation:** Leveraging blog posts for training data is a clever way to overcome the scarcity of parallel image-sentence stream data. The pre-processing steps described (text segmentation, summarization, data augmentation) seem reasonable.
* **Empirical Evaluation:** The paper includes both quantitative evaluations (BLEU, Recall) and user studies (AMT), providing a comprehensive assessment of the approach.  The AMT experiments with varying image stream lengths strengthen the conclusions about the importance of the coherence model.
* **Thorough Related Work:** The authors provide a relatively extensive overview of related work, covering both single-image captioning and video description.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Baselines:** While the authors compare to several single-image captioning methods adapted for streams, the baselines are not specifically designed for sequential description.  A stronger baseline tailored to sequential generation would better demonstrate the contribution of the CRCN.  For example, extending a state-of-the-art video captioning model might be beneficial.
* **Coherence Model Detail:** While the concept of the local coherence model is clear, the description feels somewhat superficial. The process of creating the "entity grid" and encoding transitions needs more explanation. The effectiveness of this component heavily relies on the quality of the parse trees and how well the transitions capture semantic relationships – this needs more justification.
* **Data Augmentation:** The data augmentation strategy using top-κ summary sentences is not fully explained in terms of its impact. Is κ=3 a sensitive hyperparameter? Is there a risk of introducing noise or redundancy? A more detailed analysis would be helpful.
* **Loss Function:** The max-margin loss function (Equation 4) is standard, but the selection of 100 negative examples feels somewhat arbitrary.  A discussion of the sensitivity of the results to the number of negative examples would be valuable.
* **Presentation:** The paper is dense. Some sections (e.g., 3.3, 3.4) could be restructured for improved clarity. The equations, while correct, could benefit from more concise and clear notation.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a novel and relevant problem.
* The proposed CRCN architecture is well-motivated and integrates several key components effectively.
* The empirical evaluation is thorough and supports the claims made in the paper.
* The use of blog posts as a training dataset is a creative solution.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The lack of strong baselines specifically designed for sequential description weakens the claim of significant improvement.
* The coherence model, a central contribution, is not described in sufficient detail.
* The paper is somewhat dense and could benefit from improved clarity and organization.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that addresses an interesting problem with a well-designed approach.  The novelty of the problem setting and the integration of a coherence model are significant strengths. However, the relatively weak baselines and lack of detail regarding the coherence model’s implementation are weaknesses that prevent a strong recommendation for acceptance. I recommend **rejection** with encouragement to revise and resubmit. 

**Recommendations for Revision:**

1. **Strengthen Baselines:** Implement a more competitive baseline specifically designed for sequential description, such as adapting a state-of-the-art video captioning model.
2. **Expand Coherence Model Description:** Provide a more detailed explanation of the entity grid creation, transition encoding, and justification for the choice of history parameter.  Consider showing examples of the transitions and how they contribute to coherence.
3. **Analyze Data Augmentation:**  Provide a more thorough analysis of the data augmentation strategy, including sensitivity to the value of κ.
4. **Clarify Loss Function:** Discuss the sensitivity of the results to the number of negative examples in the loss function.
5. **Improve Clarity:**  Restructure sections for improved clarity and conciseness.  Consider using more descriptive variable names and simplifying equations.
6. **Error Analysis:** Include some qualitative error analysis to illustrate cases where the model fails and areas for improvement.
