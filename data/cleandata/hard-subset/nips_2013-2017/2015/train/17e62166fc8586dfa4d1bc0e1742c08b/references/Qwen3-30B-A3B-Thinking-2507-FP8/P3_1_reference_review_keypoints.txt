- Introduces a novel task: generate a sequence of natural sentences describing an image stream rather than single-image captions.  
- Proposes CRCN: integrates CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-transition-based local coherence model.  
- Uses blog posts (NYC/Disneyland) as scalable training data, segmenting and summarizing text with LSA and augmenting with top-K sentences.  
- Evaluation is comprehensive: BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR/top-K recall plus AMT user studies that consider query length effects.  
- Strong motivation and real-world relevance (e.g., travel photo streams) with well-justified examples.  
- Task definition is ambiguous relative to video description; distinction between image streams and videos is not clearly established.  
- Baseline comparisons are weak or outdated (e.g., GloMatch using GIST/Tiny images) and omit recent multi-sentence methods like Image Paragraph Generation.  
- The entity-based coherence model is novel but limited—it only models grammatical roles (S,O,X,-) and ignores deeper semantic relations.  
- Coherence benefits are mainly observed for long sequences (8–10 images), raising concerns about effectiveness for typical use cases.  
- Training data is domain-biased (tourism); claims of domain generality are unsupported by diverse evaluations.  
- Missing technical implementation details (e.g., exact K-nearest selection procedure, feature normalization/fine-tuning, rationale for 64-D transition descriptor and 3-history steps).  
- Contributions are incremental: task definition is the main novelty, while methodological advances combine existing components.  
- Recommendation: weak accept (4/10); paper would improve with stronger baselines, clearer task positioning vs. video, deeper analysis of the coherence model, and evaluations on more diverse datasets.
