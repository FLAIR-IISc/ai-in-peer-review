- Novel problem: tackles multi-sentence descriptions for image streams, a natural and largely unexplored extension of image captioning.  
- Proposed method (CRCN): multimodal architecture combining CNNs, BRNNs, and a local coherence model to generate coherent sentence sequences.  
- Dataset innovation: leverages online blog posts as a practical weakly supervised image–text stream dataset.  
- Coherence model: uses parse-tree-based grammatical-role transitions to enforce fluency between sentences.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms several baselines on NYC and Disneyland datasets by BLEU/recall and in AMT user studies.  
- Presentation: paper is generally well-written with clear architecture illustrations.  
- Limited ablation study: insufficient analysis of each component’s contribution and missing tests like removing the coherence model or varying κ.  
- Shallow failure analysis: lacks detailed examination of why the model fails and which image sequences are problematic.  
- Weak baselines: compared methods are not strongly tailored to sequence generation; stronger baselines (e.g., seq2seq with attention) are needed.  
- Coherence model depth: justification for using grammatical-role transitions is thin and robustness to parsing errors is not addressed.  
- Data bias concerns: blog-derived data may bias narratives and harm generalization to non-blog image streams, not fully explored.  
- Minor clarity issues: some sections (e.g., integration of CNN/RNN/coherence) need clearer explanations and notation.  
- Suggested improvements: conduct extensive ablations, analyze failure cases, include stronger sequence-aware baselines, justify/explore alternative coherence metrics, and discuss dataset bias mitigation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions; overall score 7.5/10.
