This paper addresses the problem of generating a sequence of natural language sentences to describe a stream of images, a natural extension of single-image captioning. The authors propose a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that combines CNNs for image representation, RNNs for sentence modeling, and a local coherence model to ensure fluency. They leverage a novel dataset built from blog posts and demonstrate strong performance through quantitative metrics (BLEU, Recall) and user studies.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Problem Setting:** The task of describing image *streams* rather than single images is a valuable and under-explored direction. The motivation stemming from real-world scenarios like photo collections is clear and relatable.
* **Technical Approach:** The CRCN architecture is well-motivated. Integrating a coherence model is a particularly strong aspect, as it directly addresses a key challenge of multi-sentence generation - maintaining flow and consistency. The combination of existing successful components (CNNs, RNNs) with a novel coherence component feels sensible and provides a good foundation.
* **Dataset Creation:** Utilizing online blog posts as a source of image-text parallel data is clever, especially given the difficulty of creating large, high-quality datasets for this type of task. The preprocessing steps (segmentation, summarization) are clearly described.
* **Experimental Validation:**  The authors provide a thorough experimental evaluation with both quantitative metrics and user studies, which is crucial for assessing the quality of generated text. The comparison to several baselines, including adaptations of existing single-image captioning models, is appropriate.
* **Ablation Studies:** Comparing the full CRCN model with variants (1NN and RCN) helps demonstrate the contribution of each component.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the proposed method and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Coherence Model Details:** While the authors mention the use of parse trees and entity grids for the coherence model, the explanation remains relatively high-level.  More details about how the transition frequencies are used and integrated into the overall score function would be beneficial. It's difficult to assess the true impact of this component without a deeper understanding of its implementation.
* **Data Augmentation:** The data augmentation technique (selecting top-κ summary sentences) seems somewhat ad-hoc. A more principled justification for choosing κ=3 would strengthen this aspect.  How sensitive are the results to this parameter?
* **Baseline Limitations:** The chosen baselines are mostly extensions of *single-image* captioning methods. While this provides a starting point, they may not be the strongest possible comparisons for a *sequence* generation task. Existing video captioning models (e.g., [23, 32] mentioned in the related work) could offer more challenging baselines.
* **Quantitative Metrics:** BLEU and other standard metrics have known limitations for evaluating generated text, especially regarding fluency and coherence. While user studies are included, a more detailed analysis of the types of errors made by the different methods would be valuable.  Are there specific types of coherence issues the CRCN still struggles with?
* **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper lacks a deeper error analysis. Showing examples of failure cases, along with a discussion of potential causes, would strengthen the paper.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The paper would benefit from a discussion of the sensitivity of the results to key hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, dropout rates, RNN hidden size).



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses an important and novel problem.
* Proposes a technically sound and well-motivated architecture.
* Demonstrates strong performance with both quantitative metrics and user studies.
* Uses a creative approach to dataset creation.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The coherence model, a key component, is not explained in sufficient detail.
* The baselines could be stronger, particularly considering the sequence generation nature of the task.
* The evaluation relies heavily on automatic metrics, which are known to be imperfect for this type of task.  A deeper error analysis is needed.


**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that tackles a challenging and interesting problem. The proposed CRCN architecture is a promising approach, and the experimental results are encouraging. However, the lack of detail regarding the coherence model and the limitations of the baselines prevent me from strongly recommending acceptance.  I believe the paper has the potential to be a significant contribution to the field, but it would benefit from addressing the weaknesses outlined above.

**Recommendation:**  Reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit.  Specifically, the authors should:

1.  Provide a more detailed explanation of the coherence model, including the implementation details and how the transition frequencies are used.
2.  Include stronger baselines, such as existing video captioning models.
3.  Conduct a more thorough error analysis, identifying specific failure cases and discussing potential causes.
4.  Investigate the sensitivity of the results to key hyperparameters.
5. Consider adding examples that show how the model handles difficult cases (e.g. sharp changes in visual content).
