Summary
The paper tackles the underexplored problem of translating a stream of images into a coherent sequence of sentences. The proposed coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) integrates: (i) CNN image features (VGG fc7), (ii) a bidirectional RNN over sentence representations (doc2vec paragraph vectors) to capture content flow, and (iii) an entity-grid–based local coherence model (Barzilay & Lapata) to encourage fluent sentence-to-sentence transitions. Training uses max-margin ranking on blog-derived image–text streams created via paragraph-level alignment and LSA-based summarization; at test time, sentences are retrieved from training posts by first selecting K nearest images for each query image and then scoring candidate sequences with CRCN using a beam-like divide-and-conquer search. Evaluation on two blog datasets (NYC, Disneyland) reports higher BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and retrieval metrics than extended single-image caption baselines, and AMT user studies indicate preference for CRCN outputs, especially on longer streams.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on cross-modal retrieval and captioning (e.g., Karpathy & Fei-Fei; Vinyals et al.; Kiros et al.) and coherence modeling (Barzilay & Lapata). It differs from video description (e.g., Donahue et al.) by focusing on photo streams with potentially abrupt content changes and by explicitly including a local coherence module. It is also complementary to [Kim et al. 2014, 2015], which study sequence retrieval rather than sentence generation. The main novelty is the integration of entity-grid coherence with a multimodal sequence model for paragraph-level output.

Strengths
- Problem relevance: Moves beyond single-image/single-sentence captioning to sequence-to-sequence “storytelling,” a natural and important next step.
- Architectural idea: Simple, modular integration of BRNN content flow and entity-grid coherence, trained jointly and combined with CNN image features in a ranking objective.
- Data scale and realism: Curated large blog corpora; design acknowledges noisy, diverse real-world data.
- Human evaluation: AMT studies show that coherence becomes more valuable with longer sequences.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Evaluation validity:
  - Retrieval metrics (R@K, median rank) are ill-specified: if test ground-truth sentences are held out, how can ground-truth sequences appear in the candidate set? The paper needs to clarify exactly what is being retrieved and against which pool; as written, metrics may be inappropriate or misleading.
  - Language metrics (BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR) are weak indicators of paragraph-level fluency/coherence; no sentence-order discrimination or coherence-focused evaluations (e.g., entity-grid discrimination of original vs shuffled paragraphs) are reported.
- Supervision claim: The method is not “completely unsupervised”—it uses paired image–text sequences harvested from blogs and trains with a supervised ranking loss. Please revise this claim.
- Preprocessing reliability: Image–text alignment relies on simple proximity heuristics; summarization via LSA reduces segments to a single sentence. There is no analysis of alignment/summarization quality, error rates, or their effect on training.
- Modeling choices:
  - Coherence vector g is projected to the same 4096-D space and scored by a dot product with every image feature v_t (summed over t). This implies per-image alignment of a global coherence descriptor, which is hard to justify theoretically; it conflates sequence-level coherence with per-image compatibility.
  - The entity-grid is collapsed to a 64-D histogram, zero-padded to 300-D before FC layers—ad hoc and potentially lossy; no ablation on history length, zero-padding, or feature variants.
  - Text modeling uses doc2vec per sentence; no word-level modeling, attention, or explicit grounding of entities in images, which limits the capacity to resolve coreference visually.
- Baselines: Comparisons mostly concatenate per-image captions. Missing stronger sequence-aware alternatives (e.g., hierarchical RNNs for paragraph generation conditioned on image sequences; video-to-paragraph extensions) and retrieval-based paragraph models that incorporate global constraints.
- Search/efficiency: The approximate candidate search (K^N reduced via divide-and-conquer and beam M=50) lacks complexity/runtimes and sensitivity analysis (K, M, N).
- Reproducibility: Dataset, preprocessing code (segmentation, summarization), and trained models are not stated as released; doc2vec training details are minimal. Hard for others to build on.

Quality
Technically plausible and mostly sound, but several design choices (the g·v_t term, padding, shared FC mapping) are insufficiently justified, and key evaluation aspects are ambiguous. More thorough ablations, diagnostic analyses, and clarity about metrics are needed.

Clarity
Generally well written and organized, with clear component descriptions. However, important ambiguities remain: precise candidate set and retrieval metric definition; preprocessing quality; and rationale for the coherence–image interaction.

Originality
Moderate. The problem setting and integration of an entity-grid coherence module into a vision–language sequence model are novel within this context. Many components are standard.

Significance
Moderate. The direction (image streams → coherent paragraphs) is important, and the results suggest benefits from coherence modeling. However, limitations in evaluation, modeling depth, and baselines temper the impact.

Constructive suggestions and questions
- Clarify retrieval metrics: Define the candidate pool, how ground-truth is included/excluded, and how R@K/median rank are computed. If ground-truth is not in the pool, drop these metrics or use appropriate ones.
- Add coherence-focused evaluations: Original vs shuffled paragraph discrimination; entity-grid likelihoods; human judgments focused on coherence/fluency vs relevance separately.
- Provide ablations: Remove g·v_t term; vary history length (2/3/4), κ for data augmentation, K and M in search; separate vs shared FC parameters; remove zero-padding by learning a small MLP from 64-D to 300-D.
- Strengthen baselines: A hierarchical LSTM that generates sentences conditioned on previous sentences and the image sequence; a retrieval baseline that optimizes a global CRF/ILP with pairwise coherence terms.
- Analyze preprocessing: Report alignment/summarization accuracy on a small manually annotated subset; quantify sensitivity to noise.
- Report efficiency: Time/memory vs sequence length; success vs beam size; near-duplicate filtering to avoid leakage.
- Consider grounding entities: Simple coreference resolution tied to detected visual entities could better couple coherence with images.

Pros
- Addresses an important next-step task; coherent multi-sentence output for image streams
- Integrates a classic coherence model into a multimodal sequence framework
- Solid human study showing benefits on longer sequences
- Scales to real, noisy data

Cons
- Ambiguous/inappropriate retrieval metrics; paragraph-level metrics insufficient
- Questionable supervision claim; preprocessing not validated
- Coherence–image interaction term lacks justification; shallow text modeling
- Missing stronger sequence-aware baselines; limited ablations and runtime analysis

Recommendation: Borderline reject. The problem and high-level idea are compelling, but evaluation clarity and methodological justification need significant strengthening for a NeurIPS acceptance.

Confidence: High in evaluation/method concerns; medium in novelty assessment relative to all contemporaneous work.
