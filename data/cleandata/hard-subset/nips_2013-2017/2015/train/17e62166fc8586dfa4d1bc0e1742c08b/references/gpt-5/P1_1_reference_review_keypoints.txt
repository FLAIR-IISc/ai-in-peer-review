- Task: Describe image streams with a sequence of natural sentences using a retrieval-based multimodal model (CRCN).
- Model: CRCN combines VGG fc7 image features, BRNN over doc2vec sentence vectors, and an entity-grid local coherence model.
- Training: Max-margin ranking loss over image-sequence/text-sequence pairs mined from blogs.
- Inference: KNN per image position + beam-like divide-and-conquer search to assemble and score candidate sentence sequences.
- Datasets: Large-scale blog corpora (NYC and Disneyland) with heuristic preprocessing (segmentation + LSA summarization).
- Results: Improves over single-image captioning baselines, a global matching baseline, and ablations; AMT studies favor CRCN for longer streams.
- Novelty: Focus on discrete photo streams and explicit integration of entity-grid coherence into multimodal scoring is novel.
- Strength: Timely, novel problem formulation (sequence-to-sequence for image streams).
- Strength: Clear architecture integrating sentence-level content flow with entity-based local coherence and good motivation.
- Strength: Large-scale noisy-data pipeline and practical weak supervision approach.
- Strength: Comparative evaluation with ablations and human studies supporting coherence benefits.
- Weakness: Ambiguous evaluation protocol—unclear how R@K and median rank are computed relative to KNN-based inference.
- Weakness: Potential length bias—score sums over positions without reported length normalization.
- Weakness: Comparisons may be apples-to-oranges since CRCN retrieves human text while baselines generate novel text.
- Weakness: Coherence term formulation is ad hoc (64-d feature mapped to g then dotted with v_t at every time step) and lacks clear justification.
- Weakness: Heuristic preprocessing (segmentation/alignment/LSA) and noisy entity parsing lack quantitative assessment or ablations.
- Weakness: Claim of “completely unsupervised” is misleading since training uses mined aligned pairs (supervised signal).
- Reproducibility issues: Missing details on CNN fine-tuning, doc2vec training split, learning rates, runtime, convergence, and significance tests.
- Clarity needs: Underspecified retrieval metric protocol, length normalization, training data splits for features, and search algorithm; request algorithm box and diagram.
- Suggestions: Normalize scores by length, rethink coherence–image interaction, add stronger retrieval baselines, more ablations (K, beam M, LSA κ, history), and report significance.
- Acceptance pros: Novel task and coherence integration, large datasets and user studies, empirical gains for longer sequences.
- Acceptance cons: Evaluation ambiguities, length bias, ad hoc technical choices, uneven baseline comparisons, and missing reproducibility details.
- Questions for authors: How are R@K/median rank computed? Are CNN/doc2vec trained only on training data? Is S normalized by length? Why apply coherence vector per time step? Can you quantify segmentation/summarization quality?
