- Overall assessment: Novel, well-written paper proposing CRCN for multi-sentence descriptions of image streams; merits acceptance with minor revisions due to modest gains and limited analysis.

- Problem & approach: Tackles sequence-level image captioning using a CRCN that combines CNNs, BRNNs, and a local coherence model, trained on a large weakly supervised blog-derived dataset.

- Strength — Novel problem formulation: Extending single-image captioning to coherent image streams is natural, important, and well-motivated.

- Strength — Technical soundness: CRCN is well-designed, integrating established components with an explicit coherence module.

- Strength — Large-scale dataset: Clever use of travel blogs provides scale to overcome data scarcity for this task.

- Strength — Comprehensive evaluation: Uses multiple quantitative metrics and an AMT user study, and compares to several baselines.

- Strength — Clear writing: Paper is well-organized and figures help explain the architecture.

- Weakness — Modest quantitative gains: Improvements over strong baselines are statistically significant but small, questioning practical impact.

- Weakness — Dataset limitations: Blog text is noisy and loosely associated with images, introducing bias and weak supervision that's not fully explored.

- Weakness — Coherence model complexity: Parse-tree concatenation and entity-grid approach is complex, brittle, and lacks strong intuition or theoretical justification.

- Weakness — Ablation study insufficient: Needs more granular analysis of CNN variants, RNN choices, and data augmentation effects.

- Weakness — Limited error analysis: Paper lacks thorough qualitative failure cases and comparisons to baseline mistakes.

- Originality: Novel integration of a coherence model for image streams and creative use of blogs; individual components are standard.

- Significance: Potentially valuable for photo organization and storytelling, but immediate impact tempered by modest gains.

- Quality: Methodology and experiments are sound and clearly presented, though effect sizes are small.

- Clarity: Generally clear, but section on the coherence model could be more concise and intuitive.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel important problem, technically solid model, comprehensive evaluation, dataset scale, and improvements over baselines.

- Arguments against acceptance: Small metric gains, dataset noise/bias, coherence model complexity, and lack of detailed error analysis.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Revision suggestions: Add granular error analysis and failure examples; discuss and mitigate blog-data biases; simplify or better justify coherence model; run more detailed ablations; and emphasize qualitative generated examples.
