- Introduces the problem of generating a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream and proposes CRCN to address it.
- CRCN combines CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and a local coherence model to ensure fluent transitions.
- Authors build large-scale text-image parallel datasets from blog posts (NYC and Disneyland; 20K+ posts, 140K images).
- Evaluation uses quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and human studies on AMT.
- Paper is technically sound, with logical architecture design and thorough ablation studies showing component contributions.
- Dataset construction and preprocessing (segmentation, summarization, augmentation) are well-motivated and carefully described.
- Comparison against multiple baselines demonstrates CRCN’s superiority, especially on coherence metrics.
- Insufficient detail on the divide-and-conquer approximate retrieval strategy and analysis of search complexity and trade-offs.
- Clarity could be improved regarding the retrieval method, distinctions among baseline models, and AMT query-shortening procedure.
- Original contribution: shifts focus from single-image captioning/video captioning to image-stream sentence sequence generation, emphasizing coherence.
- Coherence model integration is a notable novel aspect tailored to abrupt visual changes in image streams.
- Significance: practical applications in tourism, social media, and photo management; scalable use of blog posts as data.
- Weaknesses: retrieval strategy details lacking, limited discussion of scalability to longer sequences, baseline justifications could be stronger, and AMT study size may be small for statistical significance.
- Recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance to NIPS given solid methodology, evaluation, and practical impact.
