Summary
This paper tackles the under-explored problem of translating an image stream into a coherent sequence of sentences. The proposed coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) integrates: (i) CNN image features (VGG fc7), (ii) a bidirectional RNN over paragraph vectors of candidate sentences to capture content flow, and (iii) an entity-grid local coherence model to encourage fluent sentence-to-sentence transitions. Training uses a max-margin sequence-level ranking loss over paired image/sentence sequences obtained from large, unstructured blog posts (NYC and Disneyland). At test time, the method retrieves candidate sentences by nearest-neighbor images and performs a constrained beam/divide-and-conquer search to select a fluent, coherent sequence. On two sizable blog datasets, CRCN improves BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and retrieval metrics over strong single-image captioning baselines and is preferred by AMT users, with coherence gains increasing with longer sequences.

Relation to prior work
The paper is positioned beyond single-image single-sentence captioning and beyond short-video single-sentence descriptions. It leverages CNN+RNN multimodal alignment work (e.g., Karpathy & Fei-Fei; Vinyals et al.) but extends to sequence-to-sequence retrieval with explicit coherence modeling, drawing on entity-grid local coherence (Barzilay & Lapata). Compared to video-to-text (e.g., Donahue et al.; Rohrbach et al.), the key novelty is handling sharp semantic changes across photo streams and explicitly modeling sentence-level coherence. It also differs from work retrieving image sequences from paragraphs (Kim et al.) by solving the reverse mapping.

Strengths
- Novel problem setting: image-stream to multi-sentence description, bridging a real use case (photo albums/blogs).
- Bold integration of an entity-based coherence model into a multimodal neural pipeline; this is a refreshing departure from purely word-level decoders.
- Pragmatic, scalable data construction from blogs with segmentation/summarization; unsupervised and domain-agnostic in principle.
- Solid empirical evidence: two large datasets, multiple metrics and AMT studies, ablations showing the value of coherence (CRCN > RCN), with effects increasing with sequence length.
- Clear test-time search strategy balancing local and global coherence.

Weaknesses
- Evaluation metrics for paragraph-level quality (BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR) are known to be brittle; while AMT helps, more paragraph-level metrics or discourse-focused evaluations could strengthen claims.
- The scoring function for coherence is not fully convincing: the sequence-level coherence vector g is dotted with each image vector v_t and summed over t (Eq. 3), which seems mismatched in granularity. A sequence-level image embedding would be more principled; please clarify or justify.
- The retrieval pipeline relies on nearest-image candidates; this could cap expressivity and may bias to visually redundant sentences. A comparison to a generative paragraph model (e.g., hierarchical RNN/LSTM) would be informative.
- Dataset construction relies on heuristics (paragraph tokenization + proximity assignment; LSA summarization). The paper lacks a quantitative sanity check of alignment quality/error rates or robustness to coreference/parser noise.
- Some reproducibility gaps: candidate set size/search hyperparameters, whether doc2vec is trained only on training text, and more detailed ablations (history length in entity grid, effect of separate forward/backward input transforms, K and M sensitivity).

Quality
Technically sound overall, with reasonable design choices and an effective learning objective. The model is largely a retrieval/ranking framework with a thoughtful coherence term. Claims are supported by experiments and user studies, though the coherence-scoring formulation and the absence of stronger sequence-generation baselines leave room for strengthening.

Clarity
Generally well-written and organized. Figures (not provided here) are referenced appropriately. Some notation could be clarified:
- Eq. 3: justify g · v_t repeated over t; consider an explicit sequence-level image representation.
- Explain precisely how retrieval metrics are computed given that training/test are disjoint (what constitutes “ground truth” for R@K and median rank in this setup?).
- Detail whether doc2vec and parsers are trained/fitted on training-only text.

Originality
High. The explicit marriage of an entity-based coherence model with multimodal CNN+BRNN for image-stream-to-paragraph mapping is novel, and the problem formulation itself is a meaningful step beyond single-image captioning and short-video sentence generation.

Significance
Promising. The approach opens a direction for discourse-aware vision-to-language systems and provides a scalable training recipe via blogs. The demonstrated gains and user preference suggest impact for album narration and story-like captioning. This is the kind of bold idea that can seed follow-up work.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide an ablation and diagnostic on the coherence term: remove entity grid, vary history length, compare to simpler bigram sentence-transition models.
- Include a stronger multi-sentence baseline (e.g., hierarchical decoder over sentences) or a CRF/DP-based global sequence model; alternatively, a re-ranker that uses coherence on top of per-image captions.
- Validate data preprocessing: report alignment accuracy on a small manually labeled subset; show robustness to coreference errors.
- Consider a sequence-level image embedding (e.g., mean/temporal encoder over v_t) to pair with g.
- Release code/data and more hyperparameter details (K, M, Q sensitivity).

Pros for acceptance
- Novel and important problem definition.
- Innovative integration of entity-based coherence into multimodal ranking.
- Strong empirical results and user studies; coherence benefit scales with sequence length.
- Scalable, unsupervised data construction.

Cons for acceptance
- Coherence scoring formulation may be misaligned with image sequence granularity.
- Limited comparisons to multi-sentence generative baselines.
- Heuristic dataset construction without alignment validation.
- Paragraph-level metrics are limited; more discourse-aware evaluation desirable.

Overall recommendation
Weak accept leaning to accept. The paper introduces a compelling new direction with a creative, coherence-aware architecture and convincing evidence. Despite some methodological rough edges and missing baselines, the novelty and potential impact justify acceptance.
