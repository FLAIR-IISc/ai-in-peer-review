- Tackles generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams rather than single-image captions.  
- Proposes a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs for images, BRNNs for language, and a local coherence model.  
- Uses blog posts as a parallel image–text corpus to address data scarcity.  
- Shows improved performance over baselines on BLEU/Recall and via user studies.  
- Overall assessment: reasonable, well-motivated extension with sound technical execution; merits acceptance.  
- Novelty is incremental: components are established, main contribution is their combination for this task.  
- Quality strong (8/10): good experiments and acknowledgement of limitations, but limited theoretical analysis.  
- Clarity generally good (7/10): architecture illustrated well, but some sections and notation could be clearer.  
- Originality moderate (6/10): problem is less explored but methods are not novel individually.  
- Significance good (7/10): useful applications, but impact depends on generalizability beyond blog data.  
- Recommend more ablation studies to isolate the coherence model’s contribution.  
- Parameter choices (e.g., κ=3) need better justification.  
- Coherence model (entity grid) explanation should include a small illustrative example.  
- Generalizability to other domains (social media, robotics, video) is unclear and should be discussed.  
- Strengths: addresses under-explored problem, reasonable architecture, effective dataset use, solid empirical evaluation, clear writing.  
- Weaknesses: limited theory, incremental novelty, weak parameter justification, unclear generalizability, coherence model description needs clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: meaningful problem, practical approach, strong empirical results, useful baseline for future work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, limited theoretical grounding, reliance on a single data source.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, with specific suggestions to strengthen novelty claims, ablations, parameter justification, generalizability discussion, and coherence model clarity.
