- Tackles the novel problem of generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams rather than single-image captions.  
- Proposes CRCN: integrates CNNs for images, BRNNs for sentences, and a local coherence model to ensure inter-sentence fluency.  
- Trains on weakly-supervised blog post data (travel blogs) and describes preprocessing steps.  
- Demonstrates improved performance over several adapted baselines using BLEU, Recall, and AMT user studies.  
- Strength: problem novelty and clear motivation for sequential image description.  
- Strength: coherence model is an insightful addition that addresses sequence fluency.  
- Strength: creative use of large-scale blog data to mitigate data scarcity.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation including user studies; architecture and figures are clear.  
- Weakness: baselines are all single-image captioning adaptations; lacks stronger sequence-to-sequence competitors (e.g., video captioning).  
- Weakness: local coherence model description is dense and needs clearer explanation and intuition.  
- Weakness: dataset likely biased toward travel-blog writing style; domain generality not empirically shown.  
- Weakness: limited ablation—more component-wise comparisons (e.g., BRNN vs RNN) are needed.  
- Weakness: qualitative analysis is sparse; more examples and failure cases would strengthen the paper.  
- Overall: technically sound, novel, and significant; recommended as a strong accept pending minor revisions to address baselines, coherence explanation, and ablations.
