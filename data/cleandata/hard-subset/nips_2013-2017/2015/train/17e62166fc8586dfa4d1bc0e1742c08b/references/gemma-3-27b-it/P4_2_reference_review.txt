This paper addresses the problem of generating a sequence of natural language sentences to describe a sequence of images, a natural extension of single-image captioning. The authors propose a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that combines CNNs for image representation, RNNs for sentence modeling, and a local coherence model to ensure fluent transitions between sentences. The approach is evaluated on blog datasets from NYC and Disneyland, demonstrating improvements over several baselines through quantitative metrics (BLEU, Top-K recall) and user studies.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The problem of generating descriptions for image *streams* rather than individual images is a logical and valuable extension of existing work. The integration of a coherence model is a key contribution, as it directly addresses the challenges of maintaining fluency across multiple sentences.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed architecture (CRCN) appears well-motivated and leverages established techniques (CNNs, RNNs) in a sensible way. The use of pre-trained VGGNet features is standard practice. The combination of the BRNN and coherence model, while complex, is clearly explained.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The paper presents a thorough evaluation, including both quantitative metrics and a user study via Amazon Mechanical Turk. The comparison with several baselines is helpful in establishing the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The ablation studies (RCN vs CRCN) convincingly demonstrate the benefit of the coherence model.
*   **Dataset Creation:** Leveraging blog posts as a source of image-text pairs is a clever solution to the data scarcity problem inherent in this task. The preprocessing steps (segmentation, summarization, data augmentation) are described in detail.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The figures effectively illustrate the problem setup and the proposed architecture. The mathematical notation is clear and consistent.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Baseline Selection:** While several baselines are included, they primarily consist of single-image captioning methods adapted to a sequence. A stronger baseline explicitly designed for sequence-to-sequence image description would provide a more rigorous comparison. The chosen baselines' performance is not particularly strong, making it difficult to assess the relative improvement of the CRCN.
*   **Coherence Model Detail:** While the paper explains the high-level idea of the local coherence model, the specific implementation details (e.g., how entity grids are constructed and transitions are encoded) could be elaborated further. The impact of different history parameters (beyond the chosen value of three) could be explored.
*   **Data Dependency:** The reliance on blog post data introduces a potential bias. Blog posts often follow a storytelling narrative, which might not generalize to other types of image streams. The authors acknowledge this limitation, but it’s a significant factor to consider. The results specifically highlight success in the *tourism* domain.
*   **Quantitative Metric Limitations:** The authors acknowledge that metrics like BLEU may not fully capture the quality of generated text. Exploring alternative metrics, or focusing more on qualitative analysis, could provide a more nuanced evaluation.
*   **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper lacks a detailed error analysis of the generated sequences. Identifying common failure modes could provide valuable insights for future improvements.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper tackles a novel and important problem in the intersection of computer vision and natural language processing.
*   The proposed CRCN architecture is technically sound and integrates existing techniques in a creative manner.
*   The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach.
*   The use of blog posts as a data source is a practical and innovative solution to the data scarcity problem.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The baseline comparison could be strengthened with more relevant methods designed for sequence-to-sequence image description.
*   The coherence model's implementation details could be more thoroughly explained.
*   The reliance on blog post data introduces a potential bias that limits generalizability.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of image and language understanding. The proposed CRCN architecture is well-motivated and the experimental results are compelling. While some aspects of the evaluation and implementation could be strengthened, the overall quality of the work warrants acceptance. The novelty of the problem and the technical sophistication of the proposed solution are significant.
