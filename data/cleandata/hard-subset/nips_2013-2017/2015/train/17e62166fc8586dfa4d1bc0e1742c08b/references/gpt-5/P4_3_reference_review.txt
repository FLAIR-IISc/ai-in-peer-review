Summary and relation to prior work
The paper addresses the task of describing an image stream with a sequence of natural sentences. Unlike most prior work that maps a single image to a single sentence, the method targets sequence-to-sequence alignment: a sequence of images to a multi-sentence paragraph. The proposed coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) combines: (i) CNN features (VGGNet) for images, (ii) a bidirectional RNN over sentence embeddings (doc2vec paragraph vectors) to model content flow across sentences, and (iii) an entity-grid local coherence model (Barzilay & Lapata) to capture discourse-level transitions of entities. Training uses a max-margin ranking loss that encourages aligned image–sentence-sequence pairs to score higher than mismatched pairs. At test time, candidate sentences are retrieved for each query image from the nearest neighbors in a large blog-derived training set; a beam/divide-and-conquer search composes candidate sequences, which are ranked by the learned compatibility function.

The work is related to image captioning via retrieval/embedding models [6,8,9,21,26], multimodal RNN/LSTM captioning [3,5,9,19,30], and video-to-sentence description [5,23,32]. It is also close to image-sequence retrieval from paragraph queries [11] and photo/blog summarization [10,11]. The novelty lies in explicitly modeling local coherence of multi-sentence paragraphs and integrating it with multimodal sequence matching for image streams.

Strengths
- Problem formulation: Sequence-to-sequence (image-stream to multi-sentence paragraph) is a natural and underexplored extension of captioning; the focus on coherence across sentences is well motivated.
- Architecture: The integration of a BRNN over sentence embeddings with an entity-grid coherence model is novel in the vision-language setting and appropriate for multi-sentence output.
- Data: Construction of large-scale, weakly supervised parallel streams from blogs (NYC and Disneyland; >20K posts, 140K images) is valuable and scalable.
- Evaluation: Uses both automatic metrics (BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and retrieval metrics) and AMT user studies; ablations (1NN, RCN vs CRCN) support the contribution of coherence modeling, especially for longer sequences.
- Fairness: Strong baselines included (LBL variants, CNN+RNN, CNN+LSTM), and consistent visual features across methods.

Weaknesses
- Ambiguity in scoring function: Equation (3) adds gk · vlt for every time step, effectively multiplying the global coherence term by sequence length. It is unclear if this is intended or if gk should interact with an aggregate of image features (e.g., sum or mean over t). This affects interpretability and potentially training stability.
- Coherence representation: Zero-padding a 64-d entity-grid vector to 300-d before FC layers is ad hoc; a learned projection is more principled. Details on coreference resolution quality and robustness to parsing errors are limited.
- Retrieval-centric approach: The method does not generate novel sentences; it concatenates retrieved sentences. This caps expressivity to the training corpus and may limit generalization beyond the domain/style of the blogs.
- Search procedure: The divide-and-conquer beam search is heuristic with no analysis of approximation quality; no comparison to simpler structured decoding (e.g., dynamic programming with local transition models) is provided.
- “Unsupervised” claim: The approach relies on aligned image–text segments derived via heuristics and LSA summarization; while not manually labeled, it is not fully unsupervised. This should be clarified.
- Baselines: Sequence-aware generation baselines that explicitly condition across time (e.g., LSTM that maintains context across images when generating multiple sentences) are not included. The comparison to video captioning adapted to multi-sentence outputs would strengthen the case.
- Evaluation details: Exact metric values and statistical significance for AMT preferences are not reported here; inter-annotator agreement and significance tests would improve rigor. Language metrics on concatenated paragraphs can be unreliable; acknowledging this limitation is good, but additional discourse metrics or human evaluations focused on coherence would help.

Quality
Overall technically sound, with a clear training objective, reasonable optimization (RMSprop, BPTT), and ablations. Some design choices (Eq. 3, zero-padding for coherence, search) need clarification/justification. The retrieval framing and constraints (K, M) lack sensitivity analyses. Reproducibility would benefit from dataset release details and code availability.

Clarity
The paper is generally well-organized and readable. The dataset pipeline (segmentation, summarization, augmentation) is described clearly. However, Eq. (3) needs clarification; the role of gk and its repeated interaction per time step is unclear. More detail on the entity extraction/coreference pipeline, and on doc2vec training settings, would help.

Originality
Moderate. The core components (CNNs, BRNNs, entity-grid coherence) are known; the novelty is in their combination for image-stream-to-paragraph retrieval and in leveraging blog streams. To the best of my knowledge, explicit coherence modeling for this task is new relative to contemporaneous work.

Significance
Moderate. The problem is important and the approach yields improvements over strong baselines on sizable data. However, reliance on retrieval limits downstream impact compared to generative models, and the domain is restricted to travel blogs. The blog-stream dataset could be a useful resource if released.

Suggestions for improvement
- Clarify/adjust Eq. (3). Consider gk · (aggregate over images) or normalize by sequence length; report sensitivity to sequence length.
- Replace zero-padding of coherence features with a learned projection; ablate different coherence representations (e.g., RST-style features, sentence-level LM).
- Include a sequence-aware generative baseline that conditions on prior sentences/images (e.g., hierarchical LSTM) and/or an adapted video captioning model producing multiple sentences.
- Provide sensitivity analyses for K (nearest neighbors) and M (beam size), and robustness to blog segmentation/summarization noise.
- Report statistical significance and inter-rater agreement in AMT; include human judgments specifically targeting coherence.
- Discuss dataset release and preprocessing scripts to facilitate reproducibility.

Arguments for acceptance
- Addresses a timely, underexplored task and introduces explicit coherence modeling.
- Demonstrates consistent gains over strong baselines and via user studies.
- Presents a scalable data construction pipeline from real-world blogs.

Arguments against acceptance
- Key technical components are largely compositional; novelty is incremental.
- Scoring function and coherence integration contain ambiguities and ad hoc choices.
- Retrieval-only approach limits generalization; stronger sequence-aware baselines are missing.
- Limited analysis of search approximation and parameter sensitivity.

Overall recommendation
A solid contribution on a relevant problem, with clear empirical benefits from coherence modeling. With clarifications to the scoring function and stronger baselines/analyses, the work would be stronger. As is, it is a reasonable but not definitive advance.
