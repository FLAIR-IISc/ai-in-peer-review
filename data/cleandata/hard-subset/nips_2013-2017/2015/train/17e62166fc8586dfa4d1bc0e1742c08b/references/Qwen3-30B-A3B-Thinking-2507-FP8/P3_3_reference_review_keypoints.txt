- Introduces a novel task: generate a sequence of natural sentences describing an image stream and proposes CRCN (CNN + bidirectional RNN + local coherence model).  
- Motivates task as distinct from single-image captioning and video captioning due to possible discontinuities between images.  
- Constructs a dataset from blog posts with a careful preprocessing pipeline (text segmentation, summarization, augmentation).  
- CRCN explicitly integrates a coherence model to handle sharp visual changes between consecutive images.  
- Quantitative results (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and user studies show CRCN outperforms baselines; ablation (CRCN vs RCN) highlights coherence model contribution.  
- Main critique: no evidence shown that “sharp changes” are prevalent in their dataset, so the necessity of a coherence model is questionable.  
- Insufficient comparison to relevant sequential captioning work (e.g., Xu et al. 2016, Kim et al. 2015); missing as baselines or proper discussion.  
- Evaluation metrics misaligned: n-gram based metrics do not capture sequence coherence, and user studies focus on short (5-image) sequences where coherence matters less.  
- Dataset may be biased (blogger style/audience) and the paper does not analyze how these biases affect generalization.  
- Method description is ambiguous: the entity-grid/coherence model is not clearly explained and its learning mechanism/effectiveness is unclear.  
- Overall: technically sound and interesting but novelty is overstated and evaluation does not convincingly validate the coherence contribution.  
- Recommendation: weak rejection; suggests adding dataset examples showing discontinuities, stronger comparisons to sequential captioning, coherence-focused evaluation (human studies), and deeper ablation on longer sequences.
