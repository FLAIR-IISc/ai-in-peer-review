- Tackles image-stream description with coherent multi-sentence paragraphs using CRCN (Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks).
- CRCN combines VGG fc7 image features, a bidirectional RNN over doc2vec sentence vectors, and an entity-grid local coherence model.
- Training uses a max-margin alignment objective between image-sequence and sentence-sequence representations.
- At test time, sentences are retrieved by nearest-neighbor over images and a beam/divide-and-conquer strategy assembles coherent paragraphs.
- Two large blog-derived datasets (NYC and Disneyland) are constructed by segmenting posts and summarizing text via LSA; doc2vec used for sentence embeddings.
- CRCN and an ablated RCN outperform single-image captioning baselines on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, and AMT user studies, especially for longer sequences.
- Novelty claim: paragraph-level modeling for image streams and explicit local coherence integration into multimodal learning.
- Strength: timely and original problem extension from single-image captioning to streams with explicit coherence modeling.
- Strength: sizable datasets with explained segmentation/summarization pipeline.
- Strength: clear modeling details (BRNN, entity-grid transitions, joint mapping) and sensible training choices (max-margin, RMSProp, dropout).
- Strength: consistent empirical gains and ablations showing coherence benefits.
- Weakness: combining a global coherence vector g via g·v_t at every time step seems ad hoc and may act as a global bias; needs justification or alternative designs.
- Weakness: variable-length sequence handling in the max-margin objective is underspecified (truncation/padding/min-length unclear).
- Weakness: baselines are mostly single-image methods; lacks stronger sequence-aware baselines (e.g., LSTM/BRNN over image sequences) for fair comparison.
- Weakness: retrieval vs generation mismatch not fully addressed; retrieval methods can inflate n-gram metrics and a retrieval baseline with same candidate set is needed.
- Weakness: entity-grid depends on noisy coreference/parsing on blogs with no analysis of parser/coref quality or robustness.
- Weakness: ad hoc design choices like zero-padding the coherence vector to 300-D without exploring projection alternatives.
- Weakness: missing implementation/process details (doc2vec training regime, sensitivity to K/M/κ, LSA summary quality, duplicate-sentence handling, runtime); no code/data release stated.
- Weakness: claim of being “unsupervised” is misleading given reliance on heuristically aligned image-sentence pairs and supervised alignment learning.
- Quality: technically plausible but key elements need clarification (length handling, coherence-image dot product, candidate search, doc2vec regime); baselines limit claim strength.
- Clarity: generally readable, but several important details are underspecified and some choices appear ad hoc.
- Originality: moderate—novel problem framing and combination of coherence with multimodal models, but components are standard.
- Significance: potentially useful for visual storytelling and multi-image summarization if datasets and stronger baselines are provided.
- Questions for authors: how are differing sequence lengths handled during training/testing; why combine coherence via g·v_t at each step; is doc2vec trained only on training text; is sentence diversity enforced; sensitivity to K/M/κ and entity-grid history; analysis of parser/coref errors?
- Recommendation: borderline accept—promising direction with positive evidence but needs clarifications, stronger baselines, and more rigorous ablations to strengthen the contribution.
