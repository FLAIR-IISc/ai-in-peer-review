- Novel task: Introduces generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams and proposes CRCN combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and a local coherence model.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends multimodal captioning beyond single images and distinguishes image streams from continuous video captioning due to abrupt visual changes.  
- Strength — Problem formulation: Clearly identifies and motivates a meaningful extension of image captioning based on real-world photo-stream behavior.  
- Strength — Evaluation: Provides thorough evaluation using BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, AMT user studies, and ablation comparisons (CRCN, RCN, 1NN).  
- Strength — Data collection: Uses blog posts for natural image-text pairs with a justified preprocessing pipeline (segmentation, summarization, augmentation).  
- Strength — Empirical results: CRCN significantly outperforms baselines, especially on retrieval metrics, and user studies align with quantitative gains.  
- Strength — Clarity: Clearly states contributions and situates them relative to related work.  
- Weakness — Coherence model detail: Implementation and choice of the 64-D grammatical-role transition representation are underexplained and lack justification.  
- Weakness — Scalability: No analysis of scaling to longer image streams or computational complexity as stream length increases.  
- Weakness — Video captioning comparison: Insufficiently contrasts or compares with multi-sentence video captioning methods and their potential applicability.  
- Weakness — Dataset transparency: Lacks detail on dataset curation (duplicate handling, blog quality filtering, image-text alignment, selection criteria).  
- Weakness — Limitations discussion: Missing discussion on handling non-linear visual narratives or applicability beyond tourism domains.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, novel contribution with sound methodology and compelling results, meriting interest and follow-up work.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Revision 1: Expand and justify the coherence model design and its handling of abrupt visual changes.  
- Revision 2: Include analysis or experiments on scalability to longer image streams.  
- Revision 3: Provide detailed dataset collection and selection criteria.  
- Revision 4: Strengthen comparisons and distinctions with video captioning literature.  
- Revision 5: Add a limitations section addressing non-linear narratives and cross-domain performance.
