- Problem: Introduces the task of generating multi-sentence descriptions for image streams, beyond single-image captioning.
- Method: Proposes the Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN image features, BRNN language modeling, and a local coherence model.
- Dataset: Constructs a large blog-derived dataset (≈140K images, 20K posts) to train and evaluate image-stream captioning.
- Results: Demonstrates improved performance over baselines using BLEU, Top‑K recall, and AMT user studies across varying stream lengths.
- Novelty: Task formulation and explicit incorporation of a coherence model into sequence generation are valuable and under-explored contributions.
- Technical strength: CRCN design is well-motivated; integrating coherence modeling with BRNNs addresses multi-sentence fluency.
- Evaluation strength: Comprehensive evaluation including automatic metrics and human studies provides convincing evidence.
- Clarity: Paper is well written and figures help explain the approach.
- Limited baselines: Comparisons focus on single-image captioning adaptations; lacks direct comparison to video/temporal sequence description methods.
- Coherence model detail: Description of the local coherence model (entity grids, transition encoding, history=3) is under-specified and needs clearer justification.
- Data bias: Blog-sourced data may bias the model toward curated storytelling styles; potential generalization issues are under-discussed.
- Retrieval limitation: Reliance on retrieving sentences from training data restricts novelty and handling of unseen situations.
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-motivated contribution with thorough evaluation; recommended with minor revisions.
- For acceptance: Novel problem, sound architecture, strong empirical results, and large dataset.
- Against acceptance: Insufficient comparison to sequential/video models, dataset bias concerns, and limited generative originality.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address comparisons to video-description methods, discuss dataset biases, and clarify coherence-model details.
