- Overall: novel, ambitious extension of image captioning to image streams; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- CRCN model: integrates CNNs, BRNNs, and a local coherence model to generate coherent sequences of sentences.  
- Data: leverages large weakly supervised blog dataset (≈140K images, 20K blogs) to address parallel data scarcity.  
- Evaluation: solid, comprehensive experiments using BLEU/Recall and AMT user studies; compares against several relevant baselines.  
- Presentation: generally clear and well-structured with helpful figures (e.g., Fig. 2).  
- Strength: problem definition and inclusion of a coherence model are novel and practically significant.  
- Weakness: the local coherence model’s implementation (entity grid, parse roles, transition frequencies) is under-explained and potentially sensitive to parsing errors.  
- Weakness: data augmentation strategy (selecting top-κ sentences) is justified empirically but lacks sensitivity analysis to κ.  
- Weakness: baselines are mostly adapted single-sentence captioning models; lacks comparisons to discourse/coherence-focused text generation models.  
- Weakness: limited error analysis—paper misses systematic examples of common failure cases and qualitative failures.  
- Weakness: the approximate divide-and-conquer retrieval/search strategy is used but its impact on generated sequence quality isn’t sufficiently discussed.  
- Revision suggestions: expand coherence model details, analyze augmentation sensitivity to κ, include coherence-focused baselines, add qualitative error analysis, and discuss retrieval approximation effects.
