- Introduces a novel task: generate a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream rather than single-image captions.  
- Proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN): CNN for images, BRNN for sentences, plus a local coherence model for transitions.  
- Trains on blog posts (20K posts, 140K images) to leverage natural image-text sequences.  
- Evaluates on NYC and Disneyland datasets using BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, retrieval metrics, and user studies.  
- Shows CRCN consistently outperforms baselines, especially on retrieval metrics and human judgments.  
- Main technical contribution is integrating an entity-based coherence model to maintain sentence-to-sentence coherence.  
- Empirically validated with ablation studies showing the coherence component’s impact.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and includes clear architecture diagrams and mathematical formulation.  
- Minor clarity issues: limited discussion of how the coherence model differs from prior coherence work.  
- Minor data transparency issue: more details needed on blog crawling and potential dataset biases.  
- Novelty: genuinely novel focus on sequence generation for image streams and meaningful extension of multimodal networks.  
- Properly differentiates from video captioning and image-sequence retrieval prior work.  
- Significance: practical applications (tourism, social media, photo organization) and release of a valuable blog-based dataset.  
- Weaknesses: extension is incremental over existing captioning, adaptation of coherence model underexplained, limited user study lengths, and baselines could be broader.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept (final score 8) — solid quality, significance, and comprehensive evaluation.
