## Peer Review for "Expressing an Image Stream with a Sequence of Natural Sentences"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel and ambitious approach to extending image captioning from single images to image streams, generating coherent sequences of sentences. The idea is well-motivated by real-world scenarios like travel photo collections. The proposed CRCN architecture, integrating CNNs, RNNs, and a local coherence model, is a significant step towards addressing this challenging problem. While the experimental evaluation is solid and demonstrates promising results, some aspects of the methodology and presentation could be improved. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**.

**1. Summary of Main Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper tackles the problem of generating a coherent narrative (a sequence of sentences) describing a sequence of images, moving beyond the standard single-image-single-sentence captioning paradigm.  The core idea is to leverage readily available blog data as a weakly supervised source of image-text pairs, and to build a model (CRCN) capable of both image understanding (CNN) and fluent, context-aware sentence generation (BRNN + coherence model). 

The work builds upon a substantial body of prior work in image captioning (e.g., [5, 8, 9, 12, 14, 26, 30]) and video description ([23, 32]). It differentiates itself through a specific focus on *streams* of images and the explicit inclusion of a *coherence model* to address the challenges of generating fluent transitions between sentences, which is particularly crucial when visual content changes rapidly between images. The authors correctly position their work relative to retrieval-based approaches ([6, 8, 21, 26]) and multimodal RNNs ([3, 5, 9, 13, 19, 20, 27, 30]).  The extension of [5] is clearly articulated - they address single sentences per video clip, not coherent sequences. 

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The problem definition itself is a valuable contribution, extending a core task in vision and language to a more realistic and complex setting.
* **Technical Soundness:** The CRCN architecture appears well-designed and addresses key challenges of the problem. The integration of CNNs, BRNNs, and the local coherence model is logical and theoretically plausible.
* **Large-Scale Dataset:** Leveraging blog data for training is a clever solution to the scarcity of parallel image-sentence stream datasets. The size of the dataset (140K images, 20K blogs) is commendable.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:**  The paper provides a thorough experimental evaluation with both quantitative metrics (BLEU, Recall) and user studies (AMT).  Comparing against several relevant baselines strengthens the validity of the results.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and structured, with a clear explanation of the methodology and experiments.  Fig. 2 is particularly helpful in visualizing the CRCN architecture.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Coherence Model Detail:** While the concept of a local coherence model is strong, the explanation of its implementation (based on parse trees and grammatical roles) feels somewhat hand-wavy. A more detailed explanation of how the entity grid is constructed and how transition frequencies are used would be beneficial. It's not immediately clear how robust this approach is to parsing errors.
* **Data Augmentation Impact:** The data augmentation strategy (selecting top-κ sentences) is empirically justified, but further analysis of its impact would strengthen the paper.  How sensitive are the results to the value of κ? 
* **Baseline Comparisons:** The chosen baselines, while reasonable, are largely single-sentence captioning models adapted to sequence generation.  It would be valuable to include baselines specifically designed for coherent text generation (e.g., models that explicitly address discourse coherence).
* **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper lacks a detailed error analysis of the generated sequences.  What types of errors does the model make? Are there common failure cases?  Providing examples of both successful and unsuccessful generations would give a better understanding of the model’s limitations.
* **Retrieval Strategy:** While the approximate divide-and-conquer search is understandable, the potential impact of this approximation on the quality of the generated sequences isn’t thoroughly discussed.



**4. Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The technical approach appears sound, and the reported results suggest that the model is learning meaningful relationships between images and text. The use of a max-margin loss function is appropriate for this task.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally clear, but as noted above, the explanation of the coherence model could be more detailed. The notation is consistent, and the figures are helpful.
* **Originality:** The problem definition and the integration of a coherence model are novel contributions.  The use of blog data for training is also a creative solution.
* **Significance:** The ability to generate coherent narratives from image streams has the potential for numerous applications, including automated travelogues, image-based storytelling, and assistive technologies for visually impaired individuals. The results suggest a significant advance over existing approaches.



**5. Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Addresses an important and novel problem.
* Presents a well-designed and technically sound architecture.
* Demonstrates promising results on a large-scale dataset.
* Includes a comprehensive evaluation with both quantitative metrics and user studies.

**Con:**

*  The coherence model explanation lacks detail.
*  Baseline comparisons could be stronger.
*  Limited error analysis.
* Retrieval approximations need further discussion.




**6. Recommendation & Suggestions for Revision:**

I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. 

Specifically, I suggest the authors:

* **Expand the explanation of the local coherence model**, providing more detail on the entity grid construction and transition frequency calculation.
* **Provide a more thorough analysis of the data augmentation strategy**, exploring the sensitivity of results to the value of κ.
* **Consider including baselines specifically designed for coherent text generation.**
* **Include a qualitative error analysis**, providing examples of both successful and unsuccessful generations.
* **Discuss the potential impact of the approximate search strategy** on the quality of generated sequences.
