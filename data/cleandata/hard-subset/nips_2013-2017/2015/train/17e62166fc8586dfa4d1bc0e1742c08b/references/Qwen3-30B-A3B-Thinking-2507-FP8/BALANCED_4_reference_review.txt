

# Review of "Expressing an Image Stream with a Sequence of Natural Sentences"

## Summary and Context
This paper addresses the problem of generating a sequence of natural language sentences to describe a stream of images (as opposed to single-image captioning), which the authors argue is an under-explored but important extension of existing work. They propose Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN), which integrates convolutional networks for image representation, bidirectional RNNs for sentence modeling, and a local coherence model for fluent transitions between sentences. The authors leverage blog posts as a source of image-text parallel data, collecting 20K+ blog posts with 140K images for NYC and Disneyland. Their approach is evaluated with quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and user studies via AMT.

## Strengths
1. **Novel Problem Formulation**: The paper identifies a meaningful gap in the literature by extending the single-image captioning task to multi-image sequence description, which aligns with real-world use cases like travel blogging.

2. **Comprehensive Dataset**: The authors collected a substantial dataset from blog posts (11,863 NYC and 7,717 Disneyland posts with 78K and 60K images respectively), providing a rich resource for this problem.

3. **Thorough Evaluation**: The paper provides multiple evaluation metrics (language and retrieval) and conducts user studies via AMT, which complements the quantitative results.

4. **Well-Structured Methodology**: The CRCN architecture is clearly presented, with detailed descriptions of the BRNN and coherence model components. The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the technical challenges in this problem space.

## Weaknesses
1. **Incremental Novelty**: While the problem is novel, the approach largely combines existing techniques (CNN for image features, BRNN for sentence modeling, and coherence modeling) rather than introducing fundamentally new methods. The contribution is primarily in the integration and application to this specific problem, which is less significant than a novel technical contribution.

2. **Limited Abstraction of Coherence Model**: The improvement from RCN (BRNN-only) to CRCN (with coherence model) is marginal in most metrics (Table 1), with the coherence model's impact being most pronounced in longer sequences (Table 2). The paper doesn't sufficiently isolate the coherence model's contribution from the integration of other components.

3. **Dataset Limitations**: The reliance on blog posts as training data limits generalizability. Blog posts have specific characteristics (e.g., tourism focus, narrative structure) that may not generalize to other domains. The paper doesn't discuss how well the blog text describes the images (e.g., whether all images are actually described by the text).

4. **Lack of Failure Analysis**: The paper doesn't provide examples where their approach fails, nor does it discuss limitations in more detail (e.g., how it handles abrupt scene changes in image streams).

5. **Methodological Ambiguity**: The paper doesn't clearly explain how the coherence model is trained or integrated end-to-end. The training procedure (Section 3.4) focuses on the overall model but not specifically on the coherence component.

## Suggestions for Improvement
1. **More Rigorous Ablation Study**: Conduct a deeper ablation to isolate the contribution of the coherence model. For example, compare CRCN with and without coherence on the same dataset, using identical model configurations otherwise.

2. **Expand Dataset Analysis**: Include a more detailed analysis of the blog dataset quality - how well does the text describe the images? Are there cases where the text doesn't match the images? This would strengthen the validity of their data source.

3. **Discuss Generalization**: Discuss how the approach might be extended to domains without blog-like data structures. Consider including a brief analysis of how the model would perform on a more diverse dataset.

4. **Add Failure Cases**: Include examples where the approach fails, particularly cases with abrupt scene changes or poor image-text alignment in the training data.

5. **Clarify Training of Coherence Model**: Provide more details on how the coherence model is trained and integrated into the end-to-end framework.

## Overall Assessment
The paper addresses a meaningful problem extension from single-image captioning to multi-image sequence description, which is under-explored in the literature. The proposed CRCN architecture is well-structured and the evaluation is comprehensive. However, the novelty is somewhat incremental, as it primarily combines existing techniques rather than introducing fundamentally new methods. The improvements over baselines are consistent but modest, and the reliance on blog data limits the approach's generalizability.

The paper meets the technical standards for NIPS, but its contribution is not sufficiently transformative to warrant acceptance at this level. The work would be strengthened by more rigorous ablation studies, a deeper analysis of the dataset limitations, and clearer demonstration of the coherence model's specific contribution.

**Recommendation: Reject** (with the understanding that this is a borderline case that could be resubmitted after addressing the concerns above). The paper has merit but falls short of the novelty and technical contribution expected for NIPS.
