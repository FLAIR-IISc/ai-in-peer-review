- Tackles translating image streams into coherent sentence sequences via a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN).  
- CRCN integrates VGG fc7 image features, a bidirectional RNN over sentence doc2vec vectors, and an entity-grid local coherence model.  
- Trains with a max-margin ranking loss on blog-derived image–text streams created by paragraph alignment and LSA summarization.  
- At test time, retrieves sentences by nearest-image candidates and scores sequences with CRCN using a divide-and-conquer beam-like search.  
- Evaluated on two blog datasets (NYC, Disneyland) with higher BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and retrieval metrics than single-image caption baselines and preferred in AMT studies, especially for longer streams.  
- Builds on prior cross-modal retrieval, captioning, and coherence modeling work; novelty is integrating entity-grid coherence into a multimodal sequence model.  
- Strength: addresses a relevant next-step task beyond single-image captioning toward multi-sentence “storytelling.”  
- Strength: simple, modular architecture combining BRNN content flow and entity-grid coherence with CNN features.  
- Strength: uses large, realistic, noisy blog corpora and includes human evaluation showing coherence gains for longer sequences.  
- Major concern: Retrieval metrics (R@K, median rank) are ill-specified and may be inappropriate or misleading without clarifying candidate pools.  
- Concern: BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR are weak for paragraph-level fluency; no coherence-focused evaluations (e.g., shuffled vs original) are reported.  
- Claim of being “completely unsupervised” is incorrect—training uses paired image–text sequences and a supervised ranking loss.  
- Preprocessing (image–text alignment via proximity heuristics and LSA summarization) lacks analysis of accuracy or its effect on training.  
- Modeling issue: Global coherence vector g projected and dotted with per-image features v_t conflates sequence-level coherence with per-image compatibility and is theoretically unjustified.  
- Entity-grid feature design (64-D histogram zero-padded to 300-D) is ad hoc and possibly lossy; no ablations on history length or padding.  
- Text modeling is shallow (doc2vec per sentence) with no word-level modeling, attention, or visual grounding for coreference resolution.  
- Baselines are weak—missing stronger sequence-aware models (hierarchical RNNs, video-to-paragraph methods) and retrieval baselines with global constraints.  
- Search/efficiency concerns: candidate search approximation (divide-and-conquer, beam M=50) lacks runtime/complexity analysis and sensitivity studies over K, M, N.  
- Reproducibility: dataset, preprocessing code, and trained models are not confirmed released; doc2vec and preprocessing details are minimal.  
- Quality: technically plausible but several design choices lack justification; evaluation ambiguities weaken claims.  
- Clarity: writing is generally clear, but ambiguous about candidate sets, retrieval metrics, preprocessing quality, and coherence–image interaction rationale.  
- Originality: moderate—novel within context to include entity-grid coherence in vision–language paragraph modeling, but many components are standard.  
- Significance: moderate—interesting direction with promising results but limited by evaluation, modeling depth, and baselines.  
- Key suggestions: clarify retrieval metrics, add coherence-focused evaluations, run ablations (g·v_t, history length, padding), strengthen baselines, analyze preprocessing accuracy, and report efficiency/runtime.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject pending stronger evaluation clarity, methodological justification, ablations, and reproducibility materials.
