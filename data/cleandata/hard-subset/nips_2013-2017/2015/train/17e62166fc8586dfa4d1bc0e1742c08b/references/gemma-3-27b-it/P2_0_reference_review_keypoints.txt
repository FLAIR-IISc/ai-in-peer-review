- Problem: Introduces generating a sequence of natural sentences describing an image stream (e.g., vacation photos).  
- Proposal: Presents CRCN — combines CNN image features, BRNN language modeling, and a local coherence model.  
- Training/data: Leverages blog posts with images as a large-scale, weakly supervised dataset.  
- Loss: Uses a max-margin loss appropriate for ranking/retrieval-style training.  
- Empirical results: Consistent improvements on BLEU, Recall and AMT user studies over strong baselines.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, well-motivated work; reviewer recommends strong accept with minor revisions.  
- Quality: Solid experiments and setup, but CRCN complexity may hinder reproducibility and needs more analysis of data augmentation (κ=3).  
- Clarity: Generally clear but occasionally dense; Section 3 and equation/text flow should be streamlined and a training/inference data-flow diagram added.  
- Originality: Novel application of known components to the image-stream captioning problem; creative use of blog data.  
- Significance: Addresses an important problem with applications in photo organization, robotics, and accessibility; unsupervised training broadens applicability.  
- Strengths: Novel problem, well-motivated model, strong empirical validation, creative large-scale data collection, unsupervised approach.  
- Weaknesses: Model complexity impacting reproducibility, clarity issues in architecture description, limited analysis of augmentation parameter, modest performance gains.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel and important problem, sound method, strong results, creative data solution, potential to inspire further work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Reproducibility concerns due to complexity and relatively modest gains over baselines.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity (Section 3) and further analyze the data augmentation strategy.
