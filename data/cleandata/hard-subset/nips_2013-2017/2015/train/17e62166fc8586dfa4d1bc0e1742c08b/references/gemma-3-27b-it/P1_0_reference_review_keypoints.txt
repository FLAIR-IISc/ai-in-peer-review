- Overall assessment: interesting, novel problem and reasonable approach (CRCN) but lacks technical depth and rigorous analysis; recommendation: weak reject with encouragement to revise.  
- Novelty: extending image captioning to coherent sentence sequences for image streams is a valuable and timely problem.  
- Model contribution: integrating a local coherence model with CNNs and RNNs is a useful idea to improve sentence-to-sentence transitions.  
- Dataset: large-scale blog-derived dataset (140K images, 20K blogs) is a practical and commendable contribution.  
- Evaluation breadth: authors present quantitative (BLEU, Recall) and qualitative (AMT) evaluations, showing effort toward rigorous assessment.  
- Missing recent baselines: absence of transformer-based language models (e.g., GPT-2/BERT) as baselines is a major oversight.  
- Technical clarity: key components (BRNN, local coherence model) and equations lack sufficient explanation and justification.  
- Limited baselines: comparisons rely on older methods, weakening claims of improvement.  
- Modest gains: quantitative improvements are small, especially BLEU, and may not convincingly demonstrate the approach’s value.  
- Evaluation design concerns: testing on only 5 images per stream and incomplete AMT details (questions, participant counts, significance) limit interpretability.  
- Data augmentation choice (κ=3) appears arbitrary and requires ablation to justify.  
- Lack of ablation studies: missing systematic analysis of each CRCN component’s contribution (coherence model, BRNN vs simpler RNN).  
- Retrieval strategy unclear: divide-and-conquer retrieval and beam search details, trade-offs, and implementation are insufficiently described.  
- Overclaimed originality: claiming to be the first is overstated given related work in video captioning; novelty is more in adaptation and explicit coherence modeling.  
- Specific recommendations: expand related work to include transformers, provide detailed algorithmic descriptions and justifications, include stronger baselines, run ablation studies, report full AMT protocol and statistics, and clarify retrieval and hyperparameter choices.
