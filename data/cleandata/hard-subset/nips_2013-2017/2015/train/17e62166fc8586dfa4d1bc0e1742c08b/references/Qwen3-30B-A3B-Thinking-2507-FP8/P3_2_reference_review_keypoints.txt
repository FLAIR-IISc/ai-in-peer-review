- Novel problem: Extends single-image captioning to generate coherent sentence sequences for image streams, addressing an under-explored task.  
- Model: Proposes CRCN that combines CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and a local coherence model for smooth inter-sentence transitions.  
- Dataset: Collects a large blog-based dataset (≈20K posts, 140K images) with a clear preprocessing pipeline to support the new task.  
- Evaluation: Uses multiple quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies to assess quality and coherence.  
- Empirical results: Demonstrates superior performance over baselines and shows coherence importance increases with sequence length.  
- Originality concern: Approach is somewhat incremental—main novelty is applying an existing coherence model to a new multimodal task rather than a fundamentally new method.  
- Coherence-model justification: Insufficient explanation of how the Barzilay & Lapata entity-grid coherence model is adapted to the image–text domain and whether its assumptions hold.  
- Metrics limitation: Relies heavily on BLEU despite its known weaknesses for multi-sentence/paragraph-level evaluation.  
- User study limitation: User evaluations use short sequences (5 images), which may underestimate coherence benefits for longer streams.  
- Data quality/bias: Blog-sourced captions may introduce subjective/emotional language and preprocessing (summarization) could bias or remove context, with limited discussion of these issues.  
- Scalability: The K-nearest retrieval with divide-and-conquer is computationally expensive and the paper lacks discussion of scaling to longer streams or larger datasets.  
- Comparative analysis: Lacks explicit comparisons or discussion of how other sequential modeling approaches (e.g., LRCN) could be adapted to this task.  
- Overall verdict: Solid, well-executed contribution that warrants acceptance, though not revolutionary; minor revisions recommended.  
- Revision recommendations: Add comparisons to other sequential models, deeper justification/adaptation of the coherence model, longer-sequence user studies, and analysis of dataset biases and scalability.
