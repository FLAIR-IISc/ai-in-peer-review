- Proposes a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream by extending both input and output to sequences.  
- Introduces CRCN, a multimodal architecture combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow with a clear problem statement and solution.  
- CRCN is technically sound and models discourse relationships via entity-based coherence.  
- Equation (2) appears incorrect, implying st depends only on ot and suggesting a not fully connected network.  
- Method for generating sentence sequences is reasonable but primitive and ad-hoc (retrieve per image + simple concatenation).  
- Dataset description is insufficient and may breach anonymity/confidentiality (reused blog data and undisclosed crawling details).  
- Important prior work (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014 and related multi-sentence/video description papers) is not cited.  
- Experiments demonstrate superior performance against state-of-the-art using quantitative measures and AMT user studies.  
- Evaluation is limited to the tourism domain, leaving generalization to other domains unclear.  
- Overall contribution is significant but closely mirrors prior video captioning work.  
- Recommendation: Acceptable as a poster paper.
