- Contribution: Introduces CRCN, a novel model for generating coherent sentence sequences from image streams by extending from single image-sentence pairs to sequences.  
- Architecture: Combines CNNs for images, BRNNs for sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model to ensure fluent inter-sentence transitions.  
- Evaluation results: Demonstrates improved performance over state-of-the-art baselines on quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr) and AMT user studies.  
- Dataset: Uses large, scalable blog post image–text corpora to validate real-world applicability on unstructured data.  
- Novelty strength: Tackles an underexplored multimodal problem and meaningfully advances sequence-level image-to-text generation.  
- Design strength: The coherence component is well-motivated and addresses a common shortcoming in prior work.  
- Evaluation strength: Extensive experiments and human studies support the model’s superiority, especially for longer sequences.  
- Missing related work: Omits important video-to-sentence literature that should be cited to better situate the contribution.  
- Statistical significance: Lacks significance testing, weakening confidence in some small metric improvements.  
- User study limitation: Benefits over baselines are marginal for shorter image streams, questioning practical impact in those cases.  
- Clarity/organization: Experimental and training descriptions are overly detailed and could be more concise and better organized.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel problem, well-designed architecture with strong empirical results, and scalable real-world dataset.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Missing citations, no significance tests, and limited practical gains for short sequences.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add related work, include significance testing, and improve clarity.
