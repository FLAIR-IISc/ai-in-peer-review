- Proposes CRCN to generate sequences of sentences from image streams by integrating CNNs, BRNNs, and an entity-based coherence model.
- Extends input and output dimensions to handle sequences rather than single elements, using blog posts as parallel image-text training data.
- Novel problem formulation motivated by real-world multi-photo behavior; targets coherence between sentences unlike typical image captioning work.
- Architecture is well-designed and described with sufficient technical detail for reproducibility.
- Quantitative results: CRCN significantly outperforms baselines on retrieval metrics (R@K, median rank) on NYC and Disneyland datasets.
- Uses blog posts as a practical, scalable source of text–image parallel data, avoiding expensive manual annotation.
- Demonstrates meaningful improvement over prior image-captioning and multimodal methods by explicitly modeling sentence coherence.
- Literature review misses important related work (notably Xu et al., AAAI 2015), weakening novelty claims.
- User study shows only a weak advantage of CRCN over RCN for short sequences (5 images); gap widens for longer sequences (8–10 images) but remains limited.
- No statistical significance testing reported for improvements, making it unclear whether gains are reliable.
- Paper does not adequately justify why coherence modeling is necessary for image streams but not videos; the continuity argument is not rigorously supported.
- Technical quality is solid and experiments are thorough, though evaluation metrics (e.g., BLEU) have limitations for paragraph coherence.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized; figures and supplementary material aid understanding.
- Originality: meaningful contribution in integrating coherence modeling for sentence sequences, though combination of CNNs/RNNs is incremental.
- Significance: addresses an important, practical problem (e.g., travel blogging, social media), with clear quantitative gains but limited practical advantage per user study.
- Overall assessment: solid, accept-worthy work that should add value, contingent on addressing missing related work and statistical testing.
- Recommendation: Accept, with revisions to include omitted related work and add statistical significance analysis.
