This paper proposes a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream. The authors extend both input and output dimensions to a sequence of images and a sequence of sentences, which is a significant contribution to the field of computer vision and natural language processing. The proposed multimodal architecture, called coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN), integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The authors demonstrate the superiority of their approach through extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations, showing its performance over several state-of-the-art baselines.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, including the architecture, training, and evaluation. The use of blog posts as text-image parallel training data is a clever idea, and the authors provide a detailed description of the pre-processing steps involved. The evaluation metrics used, including BLEU, CIDEr, and METEOR scores, are appropriate for the task.

The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the results, including a comparison with several baselines and an ablation study to validate the contributions of key components of their method. The user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk (AMT) provide additional evidence of the effectiveness of their approach.

One potential weakness of the paper is the claim that [5] has "no mechanism for coherence," which is inaccurate. [5] models an intermediate semantic/topic representation that yields coherence. Additionally, Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3 / Eq. (2), and the authors should clarify this discrepancy.

Overall, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of computer vision and natural language processing. The authors' approach is novel, well-motivated, and thoroughly evaluated. With some minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above, this paper is suitable for publication.

Strengths:

* Novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream
* Extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations
* Clear explanation of the approach and architecture
* Use of blog posts as text-image parallel training data
* Thorough analysis of the results, including a comparison with several baselines and an ablation study

Weaknesses:

* Inaccurate claim about [5]
* Inconsistency between Figure 2b and Section 3.3 / Eq. (2)
* Minor revisions needed to address these weaknesses

Recommendation:

* Accept the paper with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
