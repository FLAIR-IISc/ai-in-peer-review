- Problem significance: extending single-image captioning to generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams is logical and valuable.  
- Model: proposes CRCN combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model; technically sound and well-motivated.  
- Coherence component: local coherence model addresses abrupt transitions and improves text fluency.  
- Experiments: trained on a large blog corpus and evaluated on NYC and Disneyland streams with quantitative metrics and AMT user studies.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written and clearly explains problem, method, and experiments.  
- Prior work gap: fails to engage key video-description and multi-sentence generation literature (e.g., Rohrbach et al., Xu et al.), weakening novelty claims.  
- Technical concern: Equation (2) implies st depends only on RNN output ot, suggesting a disconnect between image representation and the coherence model that needs clarification.  
- Generation strategy: retrieving sentences per image and concatenating is primitive; a more holistic planning/generation approach is warranted.  
- Dataset issues: insufficient detail on crawling, inclusion/exclusion criteria, biases, and privacy/anonymization for reused blog data—ethical concerns need addressing.  
- Recommendation: acceptable as a poster; should improve literature review, clarify model architecture, and strengthen dataset/ethical details for stronger acceptance.
