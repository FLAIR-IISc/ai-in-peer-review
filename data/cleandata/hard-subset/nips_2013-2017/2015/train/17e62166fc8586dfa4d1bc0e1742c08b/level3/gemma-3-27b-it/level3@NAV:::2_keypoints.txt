- Problem: Generates coherent sentence sequences for an image stream, extending single-image captioning to multi-image paragraphs.  
- Method: Proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs, BRNNs, and a local coherence model.  
- Core idea: Explicitly modeling inter-sentence coherence within generated sequences.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation with quantitative metrics (BLEU, Top-K recall) and AMT user studies.  
- Strength: Ablation study shows coherence model improves performance.  
- Strength: Promise to release code and dataset to support reproducibility.  
- Issue: Incorrect claim that prior work [5] lacks a coherence mechanism; [5]’s intermediate semantic representations promote coherence.  
- Issue: Inconsistency between Fig. 2b and Eq. (2): figure implies fully connected layers on all sentences, equation implies per-sentence shared parameters.  
- Issue: Sequential linear layers (W_f2, W_f1) in Eq. (2) appear redundant since their composition is a single linear transform; needs justification or ablation.  
- Issue: Unclear rationale for parameter sharing between BRNN outputs and the local coherence model q in Eq. (2); requires clarification.  
- Issue: Unspecified whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.  
- Suggestion: Use METEOR in addition to BLEU, as METEOR is more robust with single references.  
- Decision: Recommendation for acceptance contingent on addressing the above clarifications and justifications.  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance after authors corrected the related-work error and pending fixes on parameter-sharing and linear-layer design.
