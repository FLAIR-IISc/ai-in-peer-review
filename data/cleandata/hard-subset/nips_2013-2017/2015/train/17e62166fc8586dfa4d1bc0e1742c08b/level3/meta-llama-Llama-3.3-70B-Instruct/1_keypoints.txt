- Proposes a novel task: retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream, extending single-image single-sentence retrieval.  
- Introduces CRCN: a multimodal architecture combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- CRCN is designed to learn semantic relations between long streams of images and sequences of sentences.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and provides a solid problem statement.  
- Thorough related-work review highlighting prior limitations and the novelty of this approach.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive (BLEU, top-K recall, AMT user studies) and shows CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art.  
- Sentence-sequence generation is primitive, relying on direct association with training images and simple concatenation.  
- Paper lacks anonymity and the dataset is not disclosed or described in sufficient detail.  
- Possible technical issue: Equation (2) may be incorrect, implying a connectivity problem in the proposed network.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, high clarity, originality, and significance; reviewer recommends acceptance but urges fixes for noted limitations.
