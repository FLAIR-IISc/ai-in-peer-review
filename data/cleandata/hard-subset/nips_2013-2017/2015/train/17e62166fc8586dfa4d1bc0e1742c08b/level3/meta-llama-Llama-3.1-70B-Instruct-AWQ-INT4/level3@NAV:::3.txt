This paper proposes a novel approach for generating natural language descriptions from an image stream, extending the traditional single-image-to-sentence generation problem to a sequence-of-images-to-sequence-of-sentences problem. The authors introduce a multimodal architecture called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), which combines convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with associated images, and its performance is evaluated using quantitative measures (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, and top-K recall) and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed approach, its components, and the experimental setup. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the results, highlighting the strengths and weaknesses of their approach compared to state-of-the-art baselines. The paper's main contributions are the introduction of the CRCN model and its application to the image-stream-to-sentence-sequence generation problem.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to generating natural language descriptions from image streams, which is a more realistic and challenging problem than traditional single-image-to-sentence generation.
* The CRCN model is well-designed, and its components are carefully chosen to address the challenges of the problem.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach using both quantitative measures and user studies.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Weaknesses:

* The literature review is not exhaustive, and some relevant works are missing (e.g., "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text...").
* The user study results show that the proposed approach performs similarly to the RCN baseline, indicating only a weak advantage.
* A statistical significance test is needed to determine whether the reported improvements are reliable.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to a challenging problem, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The CRCN model is well-designed, and its components are carefully chosen to address the challenges of the problem.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach using both quantitative measures and user studies.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The literature review is not exhaustive, and some relevant works are missing.
* The user study results show that the proposed approach performs similarly to the RCN baseline, indicating only a weak advantage.
* A statistical significance test is needed to determine whether the reported improvements are reliable.

Overall, I believe that this paper is a good contribution to the field, and its strengths outweigh its weaknesses. However, I would like to see a more comprehensive literature review and a statistical significance test to confirm the reported improvements.
