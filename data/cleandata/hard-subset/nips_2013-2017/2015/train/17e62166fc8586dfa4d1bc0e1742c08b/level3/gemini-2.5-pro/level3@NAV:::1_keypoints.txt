- Paper proposes CRCN: CNN image encoder + bidirectional RNN for sentence sequence + entity-based coherence model for discourse transitions.
- Trained on a large blog-post dataset and evaluated with automatic metrics and human studies, showing improvements over adapted single-image captioning baselines.
- Tackles an important extension from single-image captioning to multi-image, multi-sentence description.
- Clear writing and well-organized presentation with a technically coherent high-level architecture.
- Novel and well-motivated integration of an explicit entity-based coherence model into a neural framework.
- Major omission of closely related video/multi-sentence description work (e.g., Rohrbach ICCV 2013; Venugopalan CVPR 2015), overstating novelty.
- Sentence generation approach is simplistic—retrieving per-image sentences, concatenating, and re-ranking—rather than end-to-end generation.
- Potential technical error in Equation (2): it implies st depends only on ot, conflicting with the text about fully connected layers and needs clarification.
- Dataset description is insufficient for reproducibility and may breach anonymity by reusing data/methods from prior work without detail.
- Given the coherence-model novelty but notable deficiencies in related work discussion and methodological detail, reviewer recommends acceptance as a poster.
