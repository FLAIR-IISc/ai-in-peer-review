- Proposes the novel task of retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream by extending both input and output to sequences.  
- Introduces the Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Trains CRCN on a large blog-post dataset providing rich text-image parallel data.  
- Evaluates using quantitative metrics (BLEU, top-K recall) and human studies on MTurk.  
- Reports CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art baselines including log-bilinear multimodal and recurrent convolutional models.  
- Main contribution: architecture specifically designed to select coherent sentences for image sequences, with extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations.  
- Promises to release source code and dataset, benefiting the research community.  
- Incorrectly claims prior long-term recurrent convolutional net [5] has “no mechanism for coherence”; prior work models intermediate semantic/topic representations that yield coherence.  
- Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Eq. (2), creating potential confusion.  
- Use of BLEU (especially with a single reference) is suboptimal; METEOR or other metrics may be more appropriate.  
- Model design/implementation questions: sequential use of two linear functions (W_f2, W_f1) in Eq. (2) seems unnecessary and needs ablation justification.  
- Questionable parameter sharing: same FC layer parameters used for BRNN outputs and the local coherence model q in Eq. (2) needs justification.  
- Paper is overall well-written and clear, with minor clarity and correctness issues.  
- Recommendation: accept after authors address minor issues and clarify model design and implementation details.
