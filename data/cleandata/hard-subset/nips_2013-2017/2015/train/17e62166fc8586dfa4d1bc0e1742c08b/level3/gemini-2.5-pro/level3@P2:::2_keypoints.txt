1. Introduces a novel task of generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams and proposes CRCN, a retrieval-based model combining CNN, bidirectional RNN, and entity-based coherence.

2. Significance/originality: Addresses an important next step from single-image captioning to paragraph-level description and is among the first formal treatments of this task.

3. Technical quality: CRCN is well-designed, integrating visual representation, sequence modeling, and explicit discourse coherence, trained with a max-margin ranking objective.

4. Thorough evaluation: Strong empirical comparison to baselines on two large blog-derived datasets using BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr, retrieval metrics, qualitative examples, and AMT user studies.

5. Ablation study: Removal of the coherence module (RCN) shows the explicit coherence model significantly improves results, especially for longer sequences per user study.

6. Reproducibility: Authors commit to releasing code and dataset, aiding future work.

7. Related-work comparison: Claim that prior long-term recurrent models lack coherence is debatable; recommend contrasting implicit topic continuity with this work’s explicit entity-based coherence.

8. Architecture clarity: Figure 2b and Equation (2) appear inconsistent about whether FC layers are applied per timestep or after concatenation; clarification requested.

9. Model design choices: Question the rationale for using the same FC parameters for per-sentence BRNN outputs and the global coherence vector, and why two sequential linear layers were chosen over a single wider layer.

10. Implementation detail: Unclear whether paragraph vectors are pre-trained on the blog corpus and fixed or fine-tuned during CRCN training—important for reproducibility.

11. Evaluation metrics caveat: Suggest acknowledging that n-gram metrics like BLEU correlate less well with human judgments at paragraph level, which may explain modest metric gains versus stronger retrieval/human results.

12. Overall assessment: High-quality, novel, and well-evaluated paper with mainly minor clarification questions; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
