- Proposes CRCN, a multimodal architecture combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model to retrieve sentence sequences for image streams.  
- Extends both input and output to sequences (image stream → sentence sequence), a significant conceptual contribution.  
- Uses blog posts as large-scale text-image parallel training data with detailed preprocessing.  
- Demonstrates superiority over state-of-the-art via extensive quantitative (BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR), qualitative, and human evaluations.  
- Evaluation metrics chosen are appropriate for the task.  
- Provides clear paper writing and thorough explanation of architecture, training, and evaluation.  
- Includes comprehensive result analysis: comparisons to baselines and an ablation study validating key components.  
- Conducts user studies on Amazon Mechanical Turk to corroborate effectiveness.  
- Incorrectly claims prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence”; [5] models an intermediate semantic/topic representation that yields coherence.  
- Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3 / Eq. (2); authors should clarify this discrepancy.  
- Overall assessment: novel, well-motivated, thoroughly evaluated work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to fix the two noted issues.
