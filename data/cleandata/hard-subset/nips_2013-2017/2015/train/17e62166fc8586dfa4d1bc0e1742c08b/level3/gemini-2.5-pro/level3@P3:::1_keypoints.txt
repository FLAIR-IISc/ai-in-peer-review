- Problem: generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams using a multimodal model (CRCN) combining CNN, BRNN, and an entity-based coherence model in a retrieval framework.
- Approach: retrieve candidate sentences by nearest-neighbor image matching, concatenate them into sequences, and rank with CRCN to select the best-compatible sequence.
- Data & Evaluation: trained on large blog-derived datasets; evaluated with BLEU, R@K and user studies showing improvements over baselines.
- Technical strength: CRCN sensibly integrates CNNs, recurrent modeling, and explicit entity-based discourse coherence to improve paragraph-level fluency.
- Experimental strength: thorough evaluation with both automatic metrics and human judgments demonstrating benefit of coherence modeling.
- Clarity: paper is very well-written with clear motivation, helpful diagrams, and an accessible narrative.
- Main technical weakness: Equation (2) appears incorrect or unclear—implies final outputs depend only on aligned inputs, contradicting recurrent modeling and needs clarification/correction.
- Method weakness: candidate sequence generation by independent retrieval and concatenation is primitive and limits outputs to existing training sentences; lacks generative flexibility.
- Originality strength: novel combination of entity-grid discourse modeling with deep vision-and-language architectures.
- Originality weakness: overstates novelty by claiming to be first; fails to cite and engage key video-description literature (e.g., Rohrbach, Guadarrama, Venugopalan).
- Reproducibility concern: dataset collection/crawling details are insufficient and the paper omits discussion of privacy/copyright issues for blog data.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, introduces a well-motivated coherence component, is clearly presented, and shows strong empirical results.
- Arguments for rejection: poor literature positioning (missing citations), unclear/possibly incorrect key equation, and insufficient dataset/method details.
- Recommendation: accept as a poster; strengths outweigh weaknesses but authors should revise to cite related work, fix/clarify Equation (2), and provide dataset collection details.
