- Proposes CRCN: combines CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model to generate sequences of sentences for image streams.  
- Motivation is clear and well-argued: model how humans describe a sequence of photos from an event.  
- Builds on existing image-captioning and video-description work, leveraging CNN+RNN multimodal architectures.  
- Uses blog posts as a clever training corpus for parallel image-text data; preprocessing is reasonably described.  
- Quantitative results (Table 1) show CRCN outperforms several baselines on standard metrics.  
- User study (Table 2) shows only modest gains over the RCN baseline, indicating limited practical impact of the coherence model.  
- Critical methodological gap: no statistical significance testing reported for the user study results.  
- Missing related work: fails to cite closely related recent work (e.g., Xu et al., AAAI 2017).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension to image-stream captioning, logical architecture combining CNN/RNN/coherence, strong quantitative metrics.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest user-study improvements without significance tests, missing related work, and the coherence contribution appears incremental.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions — add the missing related work and perform statistical significance tests on user study results.
