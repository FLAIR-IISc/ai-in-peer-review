- Introduces a novel task: generating a coherent narrative (sequence of sentences) for an image stream rather than independent captions for each image.  
- Proposes CRCN: a multimodal model combining a CNN for visuals, a bidirectional RNN for sentence flow, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Trains the model end-to-end on a large, scalable dataset mined from blog posts that pair image streams with text.  
- Reports quantitative improvements (BLEU, R@K, etc.) and a user study showing CRCN outperforms several adapted image-captioning baselines.  
- Novelty: extends single-image captioning research to sequences and integrates a classic computational-linguistics coherence model into a deep architecture.  
- Strength — originality/significance: addresses an important, underexplored problem with clear applications (e.g., photo album summarization) and clever data sourcing.  
- Strength — technical quality: well-motivated architecture that thoughtfully combines CNN, BRNN, and entity-grid coherence modeling.  
- Strength — evaluation: thorough quantitative evaluation across multiple metrics and retrieval tasks.  
- Strength — results: CRCN (and RCN variant) show large margins over strong baselines on retrieval and language metrics.  
- Weakness — related work: literature review misses relevant video-description and sequence-to-text work (e.g., Xu et al. AAAI 2015) that should be discussed.  
- Weakness — coherence contribution: empirical evidence for the coherence model is weak—only small gains over RCN on language metrics and marginal user-study preference for short sequences.  
- Weakness — statistical testing: paper lacks significance tests to verify whether reported improvements (especially CRCN vs RCN) are statistically meaningful.  
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and organized, with helpful figures explaining model structure and preprocessing.  
- Overall: valuable and interesting contribution, but needs stronger empirical support for the coherence component, a more complete literature context, and significance testing.
