- Tackles generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams, extending single-image captioning to paragraphs.  
- Proposes the Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN): CNN image features + bidirectional RNN + entity-based local coherence model.  
- Uses a retrieval-based pipeline: retrieve candidate sentences per image, score and rank full sequences with CRCN.  
- Trained on large-scale blog-post data and evaluated on two blog datasets with BLEU, R@K, and human MTurk studies showing improvements.  
- Strength: Addresses a significant, practical problem (photo-album/narrative generation) and is a logical next step from single-image captioning.  
- Strength: Paper is well written, clearly structured, and easy to follow.  
- Strength/Novelty: Integrates an explicit entity-based coherence model to score sentence-to-sentence transitions—useful for discourse-level coherence.  
- Weakness: Overstates novelty by ignoring related work in video and multi-sentence description (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014) and lacks positioning against that literature.  
- Weakness: Equation (2) appears misleading or incorrect, suggesting per-image scores independent of global coherence—needs clarification.  
- Weakness: Retrieval-and-ranking generation is ad-hoc compared to end-to-end generative models and relies on an approximate divide-and-conquer search to handle combinatorics.  
- Weakness: Dataset description is insufficient, new NYC dataset collection details are sparse, and reusing a named dataset may compromise double-blind reviewing.  
- Argument for acceptance: Important problem, sensible novel model, clear presentation, and supportive experimental/human results.  
- Argument against acceptance: Missing related-work discussion, unclear technical notation/equation, limited methodological sophistication, and reproducibility concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept as a poster contingent on addressing related work, clarifying Eq. (2), and expanding dataset/reproducibility details.
