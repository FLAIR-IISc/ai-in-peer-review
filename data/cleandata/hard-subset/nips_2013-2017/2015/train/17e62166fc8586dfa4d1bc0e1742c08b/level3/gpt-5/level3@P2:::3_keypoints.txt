- Paper goal: describe an image stream with a coherent sequence of sentences using a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN).
- Method: CRCN combines VGG image encoding, a bidirectional RNN over paragraph vectors, and an entity-grid local coherence model.
- Training/data: max-margin ranking on image–sentence sequences mined from blogs (paragraph segmentation + LSA summarization).
- Inference: retrieve candidate sentences per image via K-NN in CNN space and use divide-and-conquer beam search to select a coherent sequence.
- Datasets/results: evaluated on two tourism blog datasets (~20k posts, ~140k images); shows improvements over single-image captioning baselines on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, and an AMT study.
- Novelty/positioning: extends single-image captioning to multi-image multi-sentence outputs and explicitly models inter-sentence coherence for discontinuous image streams.
- Relation to prior work: should better contrast with contemporaneous sequential visual-to-language work (Venugopalan et al., Yao et al., Xu et al.) and clarify coherence vs. temporal smoothness.
- Soundness: architecture and max-margin objective are reasonable and well-motivated.
- BRNN design concern: using ReLU units instead of biLSTM/GRU is questionable for long sequences—biLSTM baseline needed.
- Coherence feature concern: hand-engineered 64-d entity-grid vector zero-padded to 300-d and linearly fused; learned alternatives or comparisons are needed.
- Preprocessing reliance: heuristic segmentation, LSA summarization, and κ=3 augmentation require robustness analysis and ablations (e.g., κ=1, alternative segmenters).
- Search hyperparameters: sensitivity analyses for K (neighbors) and M (beam size) are missing.
- Evaluation: automatic metric gains are promising but AMT advantage over RCN is small; statistical significance tests are required.
- Baselines: missing stronger sequence-capable baselines (biLSTM sequence model, sentence-level Markov/CRF, adapted video-to-text, simple coherence baselines).
- Analysis: lacks error analysis and qualitative discussion about when coherence helps (e.g., longer sequences, entity continuity).
- Clarity issues: clarify handling of variable-length sequences, justify zero-padding of coherence features, and provide more AMT setup and significance details.
- Reproducibility: state release plans for code and processed blog datasets.
- Originality: novel formulation combining known components (CNNs/RNNs/entity grid) and mined blog data; retrieval-based choice is pragmatic but limits generative novelty.
- Significance: problem is important and coherence modeling is promising, but practical impact is tempered by modest AMT gains, lack of significance testing, and preprocessing dependence.
- Reasons to accept: addresses underexplored problem, sensible modular architecture, consistent quantitative improvements, and principled coherence modeling that helps longer sequences.
- Reasons to reject: missing comparisons to sequence baselines, small/no statistically validated user gains, heavy reliance on heuristics, and ad-hoc coherence feature/fusion choices.
- Questions for authors: report statistical significance (automatic & AMT), compare to biLSTM/GRU BRNN, analyze sensitivity to K/M/κ and noisy coreference, include non-entity coherence baselines, and commit to releasing code/data.
- Overall verdict: interesting and well-motivated contribution that would be stronger with additional baselines, significance testing, and ablations around coherence and preprocessing.
