- Paper proposes CRCN to generate sentence sequences describing image streams, combining CNN, BRNN, and an entity-based coherence model.  
- Method retrieves candidate sentences per image and ranks sequences with CRCN; evaluated with BLEU, R@K, and a Mechanical Turk user study.  
- Problem is a natural extension of single-image captioning and closely related to video description literature.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and experiments are thorough with both automatic and human evaluations.  
- Equation (2) appears flawed: it implies column-wise independence (st depends only on ot), undermining joint sequence modeling and requiring clarification.  
- Dataset collection and preprocessing are under-described; blog crawling heuristics and text segmentation details are missing, harming reproducibility.  
- Reuse of a Disneyland blog dataset may violate double-blind review if not a public benchmark, risking author identification.  
- Main contributions: formulating image-stream-to-sentence-sequence, proposing CRCN with explicit coherence modeling, and evaluation on a large blog dataset.  
- Strength: explicit, entity-based coherence model is novel and improves discourse-level fluency beyond implicit RNN modeling.  
- Weakness: insufficient engagement with prior work in video captioning and multi-sentence video description, overstating novelty.  
- Weakness: approach is retrieval-based rather than fully generative, limiting novelty and adaptability to new content.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and of interest but diminished by related-work gaps, a potential technical flaw, and the retrieval-based design.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant problem, novel coherence component, strong writing and empirical evaluation.  
- Rejection arguments / required revisions: address Equation (2) clarification, expand related work on video description, justify or improve dataset handling and generative capacity; acceptable as a poster contingent on these fixes.
