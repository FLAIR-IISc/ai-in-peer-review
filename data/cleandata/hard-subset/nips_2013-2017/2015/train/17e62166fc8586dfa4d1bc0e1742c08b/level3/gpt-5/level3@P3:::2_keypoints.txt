- Task summary: Retrieve coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams by selecting sentences from blog posts using KNN candidates, paragraph-vector embeddings, a BRNN for sentential sequence, and an entity-grid coherence model.
- Architecture detail: BRNN outputs and an entity-grid coherence vector pass through ReLU and two shared FC layers, combined with CNN image features to score image–sentence compatibility.
- Search strategy: Uses K=5 nearest-image candidates per query and an approximate divide-and-conquer beam search to handle combinatorial sequence space.
- Novelty claim: Differs from single-image captioning and video-to-sentence work by explicitly selecting coherent multi-sentence sequences and integrating explicit sentence-to-sentence coherence.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on retrieval-based captioning, sequence modeling (BRNN/LSTM), and coherence modeling (Barzilay & Lapata); should clarify comparisons to LRCN’s temporal modeling.
- Strength — task clarity: Clear problem formulation and practical dataset construction from blogs (NYC, Disneyland).
- Strength — architecture: Novel combination of BRNN content flow with an entity-grid local coherence model for sentence transitions.
- Strength — evaluation breadth: Extensive metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, recall@K, median rank), qualitative examples, AMT user studies, and ablations isolating coherence effects.
- Strength — empirical findings: Human studies show larger gains for longer streams, supporting the coherence hypothesis.
- Strength — practicality and impact: Scalable retrieval/search strategy and planned dataset/code release increase potential impact.
- Weakness — related-work claim: Overstates that LRCN has “no mechanism for coherence”; should soften and clarify that novelty is multi-sentence coherence modeling, not absence of temporal modeling in prior work.
- Weakness — figure vs equation mismatch: Figure 2b implies joint FC connections while Eq. (2) and notation indicate shared per-sentence FCs; figure or text needs correction.
- Weakness — two linear layers: Two stacked linear layers without nonlinearity reduce to one linear map at inference (dropout aside); request ablation comparing one vs two FCs and clarification about inter-layer nonlinearity.
- Weakness — parameter sharing: Question the inductive bias of sharing FC parameters between BRNN outputs and the coherence vector; request ablation with separate FCs and justification.
- Weakness — evaluation metrics: With single references, BLEU correlates poorly with humans; de-emphasize BLEU and prioritize METEOR and CIDEr, and discuss correlations with AMT results.
- Weakness — paragraph-vector training: Clarify whether doc2vec sentence embeddings are fine-tuned during CRCN training and include ablation (frozen vs fine-tuned vs learned encoders) if feasible.
- Weakness — beam search approximation: Report search completeness measures (oracle upper bound vs found top-1/top-5) to quantify approximation quality.
- Quality assessment: Technically sound overall (max-margin objective, BRNN, entity-grid), but key technical questions remain on linear-layer rationale, parameter sharing, and figure/equation clarity.
- Clarity assessment: Generally well written; needs fixes for Figure 2b/Eq. (2) mismatch and expanded explanation of FC layers, dropout, parameter sharing, and paragraph-vector fine-tuning.
- Originality assessment: Novel for the era—explicit coherence modeling for image-stream-to-multi-sentence retrieval is a thoughtful combination of known components applied to a new task.
- Significance assessment: Relevant and practical problem with demonstrated gains and promising dataset/code release for follow-on work.
- Pros for acceptance: Novel task/architecture, strong quantitative/qualitative/human evaluations, ablation validating coherence, and commitment to release data/code.
- Cons against acceptance: Overstated related-work claim, architectural clarity issues, unanswered questions about two-FC design and parameter sharing, and missing paragraph-vector clarification.
- Post-rebuttal recommendation: Accept if authors (i) correct the LRCN claim, (ii) fix Figure 2b/Eq. (2) and FC explanations, (iii) justify or ablate the two-FC design and parameter sharing, (iv) clarify paragraph-vector fine-tuning, and (v) prioritize METEOR/CIDEr and release resources.
