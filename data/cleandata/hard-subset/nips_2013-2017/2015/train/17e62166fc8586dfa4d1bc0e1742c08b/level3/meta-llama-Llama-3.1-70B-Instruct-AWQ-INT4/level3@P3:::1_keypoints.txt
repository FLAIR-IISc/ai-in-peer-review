- Problem is significant (video-to-text/multi-sentence generation) but closely mirrors prior video captioning work.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Proposes CRCN model that is technically sound and models discourse via entity-based coherence.  
- Equation (2) appears incorrect or implausible, suggesting outputs depend only on previous output.  
- Sentence-sequence generation method is primitive and ad-hoc (retrieve one sentence per image + simple concatenation).  
- Dataset description is insufficient and may breach anonymity/confidentiality due to reused blog data and undisclosed crawling details.  
- Evaluation uses quantitative metrics (BLEU, top-K recall) and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.  
- Results indicate CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art candidate methods.  
- Related work section misses important prior papers (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014) and lacks discussion of differences from existing research.  
- Claims need stronger theoretical analysis or additional experimental support.  
- Paper should include a deeper discussion of limitations and future directions.  
- Originality is moderate: novel combination of existing techniques (multimodal architecture + entity-based coherence) but not a wholly new problem.  
- Overall recommendation: accept as a poster paper given technical soundness and contribution despite limitations.
