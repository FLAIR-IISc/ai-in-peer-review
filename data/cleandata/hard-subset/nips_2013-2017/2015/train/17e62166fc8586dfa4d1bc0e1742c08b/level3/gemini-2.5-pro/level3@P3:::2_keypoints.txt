- Problem & approach: Introduces generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams by retrieving K candidate sentences per image and ranking sequences with a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining a BiLSTM and entity-based coherence model.
- Novelty & significance: Novel, important extension from single-image captioning to multi-image paragraph/story generation with a well-motivated CRCN that explicitly integrates BRNN and a classic coherence model.
- Thorough evaluation: Comprehensive quantitative (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, recall), qualitative examples, AMT human evaluation, and an ablation (CRCN vs. RCN) showing benefits for longer sequences.
- Reproducibility: Authors will release source code and a new blog-post dataset, supporting future work.
- Related work concern: Claim that Donahue et al. has “no mechanism for coherence” is misleading—their recurrent model implicitly enforces coherence and this distinction should be clarified.
- Model clarity: Discrepancy between Figure 2b and Equation (2) about whether FC outputs s_t and g derive from all BRNN outputs or are computed per o_t—figure or equation needs fixing.
- Sequential linear layers: Using two sequential linear transforms (W_f2 then W_f1) is redundant since their composition is linear; authors should justify or ablate against a single wider layer.
- Parameter sharing: Sharing the same FC parameters for per-sentence BRNN outputs and the global coherence vector q is questionable; motivate this choice or test separate transformations.
- Evaluation metric: BLEU is unreliable at sentence/paragraph level with single references; emphasize METEOR (better for synonymy/paraphrase and human correlation).
- Implementation detail: Clarify whether paragraph vectors are fixed after pretraining or fine-tuned during end-to-end CRCN training for reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with addressable clarity/positioning issues; recommendation to revise accordingly.
- Post-rebuttal: Authors satisfactorily addressed main concerns (figure/equation clarification, design justification, related work), reviewer now strongly recommends acceptance and requests explicit paragraph-vector details in the final manuscript.
