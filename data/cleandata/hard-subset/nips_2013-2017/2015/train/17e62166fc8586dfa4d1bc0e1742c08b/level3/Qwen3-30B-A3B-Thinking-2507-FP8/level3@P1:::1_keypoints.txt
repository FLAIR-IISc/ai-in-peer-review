- Proposes CRCN for generating natural-language descriptions from image streams (sequence-level captioning) as an extension of image captioning.  
- CRCN combines CNNs for images, bidirectional RNNs for sentences, and an entity-based local coherence model to ensure fluent transitions.  
- Motivation: users take multiple photos of events, requiring sequence-level descriptions rather than single-image captions.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly structured, with good relation to prior image captioning work.  
- Fails to cite important related work in multi-sentence/video description (notably Rohrbach et al., ICCV 2014), a significant omission.  
- Architecture is technically sound; the entity-based coherence model is a valuable contribution and ablation studies show its importance.  
- Use of blog data as parallel text-image training is innovative but dataset description is insufficient and may breach anonymity/ethical norms.  
- Strong quantitative results on NYC and Disneyland blog datasets; CRCN outperforms baselines on BLEU, CIDEr, and retrieval metrics.  
- AMT user studies further validate the quality of generated sequences.  
- Sentence generation method is ad-hoc (retrieves and concatenates candidate sentences) rather than truly generating novel sequences, limiting novelty.  
- Technical concern: Equation (2) appears incorrect/implausible and needs clarification about sequence dependencies.  
- Evaluation summary: Quality good but dataset issues; Clarity high; Originality moderate due to existing CNN+RNN core and missing video-caption comparisons; Significance meaningful but incremental.  
- Main strengths: clear problem statement, coherence model, comprehensive evaluation.  
- Main weaknesses: insufficient related-work citations, problematic dataset description, ad-hoc retrieval-based generation, and a technical error in Equation (2).  
- Recommendation: accept as a poster; revise to include related work discussion, clarify Equation (2), and provide detailed/ethical dataset description to strengthen the paper.
