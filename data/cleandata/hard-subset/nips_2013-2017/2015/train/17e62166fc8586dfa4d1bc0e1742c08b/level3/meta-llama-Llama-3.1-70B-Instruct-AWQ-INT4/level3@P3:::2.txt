This paper proposes an approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream. The authors develop a multimodal architecture called coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with associated images, and the authors demonstrate its superior performance over other state-of-the-art candidate methods using both quantitative measures and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk.

The main contribution of this paper is the novel architecture for selecting coherent sentences for image sequences. The authors' approach is one level higher than existing sentence generation models, which combine words or phrases from training data to generate a sentence for a novel image. Instead, the authors use sentences from the training database to author a sequence of sentences for a novel image stream. This approach is more suitable for generating fluent and coherent passages.

The strengths of this paper include the extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations, which demonstrate the superiority of the CRCN approach over baselines and an ablation removing the coherence model. The authors also promise to release the source code and dataset, which will be beneficial for the research community.

However, there are some weaknesses and related-work issues. For example, the authors claim that [5] has "no mechanism for coherence," which is inaccurate. [5] models an intermediate semantic/topic representation that yields coherence. Additionally, Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3 / Eq. (2), as the diagram implies FC layers connected to all sentences, but Eq. (2) suggests shared parameters applied per sentence.

In terms of evaluation metrics, the authors use METEOR rather than BLEU, especially with a single reference, for better automatic evaluation. However, the authors could have provided more justification for this choice.

The model design raises some questions. For example, why are two linear functions (W_f2, W_f1) applied sequentially in Eq. (2) when their composition is linear? An ablation study would be helpful to justify this design choice. Additionally, why use the same fully connected layer parameters for BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence model q in Eq. (2)?

The implementation details could be clarified further. For example, whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed during training.

Overall, this paper presents a novel approach for retrieving coherent sentences for image sequences. The authors provide extensive evaluations and demonstrate the superiority of their approach over baselines. However, there are some weaknesses and related-work issues that need to be addressed. With some revisions to address these issues, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel architecture for selecting coherent sentences for image sequences.
* The authors provide extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations, which demonstrate the superiority of their approach.
* The paper addresses a challenging problem in the field of computer vision and natural language processing.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper has some weaknesses and related-work issues that need to be addressed.
* The model design raises some questions that need to be justified through ablation studies.
* The implementation details could be clarified further.

Recommendation:

I recommend acceptance of this paper after the authors address the weaknesses and related-work issues mentioned above. The authors should also provide more justification for their design choices and clarify the implementation details. With these revisions, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
