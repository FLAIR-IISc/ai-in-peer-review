- Task: Paragraph-level description of image streams by retrieving sentence sequences that match visuals and maintain discourse flow.
- Proposed model (CRCN): combines VGG fc7 image features, a bidirectional RNN over paragraph vectors of candidate sentences, and an entity-grid–based local coherence model.
- Training: max-margin objective on aligned blog image–text streams (NYC and Disneyland) harvested via segmentation and summarization.
- Inference: per-image candidate sentences retrieved by KNN in CNN space; sequence assembled with approximate beam/divide-and-conquer search.
- Results: CRCN outperforms single-sentence captioners and simple concatenation baselines on BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr, retrieval recalls, and AMT preferences.
- Relation to prior work: Extends single-image captioning and relates to video description; entity-grid integration is sensible and novel for multimodal captioning but related multi-sentence/video-to-paragraph work coverage is incomplete.
- Strength: Clear problem statement and well-written; architecture and training objective are easy to follow.
- Strength: Sensible BRNN content modeling combined with a novel entity-based local coherence component for multimodal data.
- Strength: Large, realistic blog-derived dataset and pragmatic pipeline; ablations (1NN vs RCN vs CRCN) isolate contributions.
- Strength: Empirical gains on automatic metrics and human studies; analysis shows coherence becomes more valuable for longer sequences.
- Weakness: Fusion formulation (Eq.2) appears to not properly inject global coherence q into each per-sentence representation, limiting cross-sentence interaction.
- Weakness: Scoring (Eq.3) sums coherence contributions over t, risking length bias; unclear whether sequences are length-normalized.
- Weakness: Retrieval-as-KNN plus heuristic beam search is primitive and ad hoc; lacks mechanisms for anti-redundancy, coverage/diversity, or higher-order discourse modeling.
- Weakness: Dataset construction and preprocessing details (queries, filtering, summarization hyperparameters, de-duplication) are underspecified, hampering reproducibility and raising anonymity/licensing/ethics questions.
- Weakness: Evaluation concerns—automatic paragraph-level metrics are noisy, AMT study limited to short sequences (≤5), lack of significance tests, and ground-truth are LSA summaries rather than full human-authored paragraphs.
- Weakness: Related work coverage incomplete—missing contemporaneous multi-sentence/video-to-paragraph and hierarchical/seq2seq approaches.
- Weakness: Minor technical clarities missing (CNN fine-tuning, paragraph-vector training regime, handling duplicate candidate sentences, beam/divide-and-conquer pruning guarantees).
- Quality: Generally sound approach but fusion and length-related scoring issues need clarification or correction; retrieval/search strategy is heuristic.
- Clarity: Well written overall; needs added implementation specifics for data creation, training, and search, and fixes/explanations for Eq.(2)/(3).
- Originality: Moderate—novel application of entity-grid coherence to multimodal retrieval, but builds on established CNN+RNN and parallels video-description work.
- Significance: Reasonable—addresses an important underexplored task with solid gains; broader impact depends on stronger generation models and better datasets.
- Pros for acceptance: Timely problem, integrates discourse coherence, clear empirical gains with ablations and human studies, and well written.
- Cons for acceptance: Fusion/scoring equations seem flawed/insufficiently justified, heuristic retrieval pipeline limits novelty/scalability, dataset details/ethics lacking, and related work coverage incomplete.
- Recommendation: Accept as a poster, contingent on fixes to fusion/scoring formulation, expanded related work, and clearer dataset/evaluation details.
