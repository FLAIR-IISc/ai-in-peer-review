This paper proposes an approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream, which is a novel and challenging task. The authors extend both input and output dimensions to a sequence of images and a sequence of sentences, respectively, and design a multimodal architecture called coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) to tackle this problem.

The CRCN model integrates convolutional neural networks for image representation, bidirectional recurrent neural networks for sentence sequence modeling, and a local coherence model for fluent transitions of sentences. The authors also leverage online natural blog posts as text-image parallel training data, which is a creative and effective way to address the issue of obtaining appropriate text-image parallel corpus.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach using both quantitative measures and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk. The results demonstrate the superiority of their approach over other state-of-the-art candidate methods.

One of the main strengths of this paper is its extensive evaluation, which includes not only quantitative metrics but also qualitative and human evaluations. The authors also promise to release their source code and dataset, which is a significant contribution to the research community.

However, there are a few weaknesses and issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, the authors' claim that [5] has "no mechanism for coherence" is inaccurate, as [5] models an intermediate semantic/topic representation that yields coherence. This mistake suggests that the authors may not have thoroughly reviewed the related work.

Secondly, there is a discrepancy between Figure 2b and Section 3.3 / Eq. (2). The diagram implies that the fully connected layers are connected to all sentences, but Eq. (2) suggests that the shared parameters are applied per sentence. This inconsistency needs to be clarified.

Thirdly, the authors use BLEU as the evaluation metric, which may not be the best choice, especially when there is only a single reference. METEOR is a more suitable metric for automatic evaluation.

Finally, there are a few model design questions that need to be addressed. For example, why are two linear functions (W_f2, W_f1) applied sequentially in Eq. (2) when their composition is linear? The authors should provide an ablation study to justify this design choice. Additionally, why use the same fully connected layer parameters for BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence model q in Eq. (2)? The authors should provide a clear explanation for this parameter-sharing decision.

In terms of implementation details, the authors should clarify whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed.

Overall, this paper is well-written and makes a significant contribution to the field of image captioning. However, the authors need to address the issues mentioned above to further improve the quality of their work.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The paper proposes a novel and challenging task of retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream.
* The authors design a multimodal architecture called coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) to tackle this problem.
* The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the approach using both quantitative measures and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk.
* The authors promise to release their source code and dataset, which is a significant contribution to the research community.

* The authors use BLEU as the evaluation metric, which may not be the best choice.
