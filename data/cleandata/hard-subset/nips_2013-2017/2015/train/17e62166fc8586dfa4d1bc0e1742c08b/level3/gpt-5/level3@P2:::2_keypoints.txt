- Paper tackles generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams using retrieval and sequence selection (CRCN) combining VGG features, BRNN over paragraph vectors, and an entity-grid coherence model.
- CRCN scores image–sentence compatibility with paired mappings and trains with a max-margin ranking loss; test-time uses KNN retrieval (K=5) and a divide-and-conquer beam search.
- Experiments on two large blog-derived datasets (~20k posts, ~140k images) show improvements over single-image captioning/retrieval baselines using BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr, retrieval metrics, ablations, and AMT studies.
- Strength: Addresses an important gap (single-image captions → coherent multi-sentence descriptions) with a timely problem setting.
- Strength: Solid architecture design integrating BRNN for content flow and an entity-grid coherence model, trained end-to-end with structured ranking loss.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation including multiple metrics, ablations, retrieval metrics, and human studies supporting coherence benefits for longer sequences.
- Strength: Uses large weakly supervised blog data with practical preprocessing and promises code/dataset release for reproducibility.
- Weakness: Overstates prior work — claim that LRCN has “no mechanism for coherence” should be softened to “no explicit discourse-level coherence modeling.”
- Weakness: Figure 2b and Eq. (2) appear inconsistent about connectivity; clarify whether FC layers aggregate across sentence positions or act per-sentence.
- Weakness: Two-layer FC mapping question — at test time composition is linear; request ablation comparing single vs two FC layers to separate depth vs regularization effects.
- Weakness: Question parameter sharing between per-sentence BRNN outputs (o_t) and global coherence vector (q); request ablation or justification for shared vs separate projections.
- Weakness: Clarify paragraph vector (doc2vec) training — trained only on blog corpus? fine-tuned during CRCN training? Recommend testing joint fine-tuning.
- Weakness: BLEU can be misleading for retrieval outputs with few references — de-emphasize BLEU and consider SPICE or human judgments as primary evidence.
- Weakness: Consider using LSTM/GRU instead of ReLU-RNN for BRNN to address vanishing gradients on longer streams; a small comparison would strengthen choice.
- Quality: Technically sound with appropriate objective and ablations; coherence contribution well supported though some architectural choices need minor ablations and sensitivity analysis for preprocessing.
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear but needs to fix Fig. 2b vs Eq. (2), detail doc2vec training/fine-tuning, and add a high-level algorithm box for the test-time beam search.
- Originality: Novel move to sequence-level retrieval-based descriptions with explicit discourse coherence; meaningful integration of entity-grid into multimodal model.
- Significance: Likely to influence visual storytelling and retrieval-based captioning; datasets and code release would add practical value.
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, clear algorithmic contribution, strong empirical and human-study results, scalable weakly supervised data, promised release.
- Cons against acceptance: some overstatements in related work, a few architectural choices need clarification/ablation, reliance on heuristic preprocessing not deeply analyzed.
- Requested camera-ready clarifications: (1) revise LRCN comparison wording, (2) clarify Fig. 2b vs Eq. (2) connectivity, (3) ablate single vs two FC layers and shared vs separate mappings, (4) state/fine-tune doc2vec details and sensitivity, (5) de-emphasize BLEU.
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
- Reviewer confidence: High.
