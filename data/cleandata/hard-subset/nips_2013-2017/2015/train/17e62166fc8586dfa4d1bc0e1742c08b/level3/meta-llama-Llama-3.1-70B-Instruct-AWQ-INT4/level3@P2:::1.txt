This paper proposes a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream. The authors extend the input and output dimensions of previous work on image captioning, which typically focuses on generating a single sentence for a single image. The proposed method, called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model to capture the semantic relationships between image streams and text sequences.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear problem statement and solution. The proposed CRCN model is technically sound and effectively captures discourse relationships using entity-based coherence. The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of their approach using both quantitative measures and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk.

However, there are some limitations and areas for improvement. Firstly, the paper does not cite important prior work on video-to-text and multi-sentence generation, such as Rohrbach et al. (2014) and related papers. This omission may indicate a lack of thoroughness in the literature review.

Additionally, the method for generating sentence sequences is reasonable but primitive and ad-hoc. The authors retrieve a sentence per image and simply concatenate them, which may not be the most effective way to generate coherent and fluent sentence sequences.

Furthermore, the dataset description is insufficient and may breach anonymity/confidentiality. The authors reuse blog data from [11] and do not provide sufficient details about the crawling process, which may raise concerns about data privacy and ownership.

In terms of technical soundness, Equation (2) appears incorrect/implausible because it suggests that st depends only on ot, implying a not fully connected network. This may be a typo or a misunderstanding, but it requires clarification.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of image captioning and sentence generation. However, it requires further refinement and attention to detail to address the limitations mentioned above.
