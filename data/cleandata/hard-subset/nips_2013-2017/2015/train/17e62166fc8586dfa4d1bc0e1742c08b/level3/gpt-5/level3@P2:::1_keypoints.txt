- Paper proposes CRCN: combines VGG image features, a bidirectional RNN over sentence vectors, and an entity-grid coherence model to produce multi-sentence descriptions for photo streams, trained with a max-margin objective on blog-derived image–sentence pairs and decoded via retrieval+beam search.  
- Experiments on NYC and Disneyland blog corpora show improvements over strong captioning baselines on BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr, retrieval metrics, and AMT user studies; ablations indicate coherence helps for longer sequences.  
- Related work: closely tied to image captioning, multimodal embedding, and video-to-paragraph literature; needs clearer discussion of prior multi-sentence/video description papers.  
- Strength: clear and timely motivation to move from single-image captions to stream/paragraph generation.  
- Strength: technically sound integration of BRNN sentence representations with an entity-grid coherence model; ablations show tangible benefits.  
- Strength: leverages large, naturalistic blog data and a scalable retrieval+reranking pipeline.  
- Strength: evaluations include automatic metrics and human studies; writing and presentation are clear.  
- Major technical concern: Eq. (2) notation implies per-sentence features s_t do not depend on the coherence vector q—need clarification on whether there is temporal cross-talk or a notational mistake.  
- Major technical concern: Eq. (3) repeats the same g·v_lt term across positions; the semantic validity of dotting a text-only coherence vector with per-image CNN features is unclear and needs justification/ablations (e.g., without g or with time-dependent g_t).  
- Decoding critique: retrieval-based KNN candidate pools and divide-and-conquer beam search are ad hoc, restrict outputs to training sentences, and limit modeling of global paragraph-wide constraints; a structured decoder could be stronger.  
- Experimental details under-specified: dataset splits, average sequence lengths, AMT confidence intervals and inter-annotator agreement, and significance testing are missing; calling the method “unsupervised” is misleading.  
- Clarity/data concerns: dataset crawling/filtering criteria, languages, licenses, code/data release plans, and potential double-blind anonymity breaches are not addressed.  
- Notational clarity: dimensions around Eq. (2) and the role of dropout need more precise explanation.  
- Originality: useful extension combining known components (retrieval-and-rerank + entity-grid), but novelty is incremental relative to prior multi-sentence/video description methods.  
- Significance: important problem with promising results, but practical uptake is limited without public data/code and broader-domain validation; retrieval approach constrains generalization.  
- Questions for authors requested: clarify dependence of s_t on q, justify/ablate g·v_lt and try g_t, provide dataset/anonymization/licenses and release plans, report sensitivity to K/M/sequence length and AMT stats, and include failure-case analysis.  
- Pros summary: well-motivated, readable paper with a sound architecture, meaningful coherence modeling, and strong empirical and human-evaluation results.  
- Cons summary: questionable scoring equations needing clarification/ablations, primitive retrieval-only decoding, incomplete related work coverage, and insufficient dataset/reproducibility and anonymity details.  
- Recommendation: Accept as a poster, contingent on addressing Eq. (2)/(3) concerns, expanding related work, and improving dataset/reproducibility information.
