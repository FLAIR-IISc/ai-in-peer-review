- Task & approach: Retrieves K=5 image–sentence pairs per image, embeds sentences (doc2vec), models sequence with a BRNN plus an entity-grid local coherence module, and scores sentence/image compatibility with a max-margin objective.

- Architecture: Technically sound and well-motivated—combines content compatibility with explicit local coherence to handle abrupt visual changes.

- Experiments: Extensive evaluation on large blog datasets (NYC, Disneyland) with automatic metrics, retrieval measures, AMT human studies, and an ablation without the coherence module.

- Ablation result: Coherence module provides measurable improvements, especially for longer sequences.

- Issue 1 (clarity): Inconsistency between Fig. 2b and Eq. (2)—figure implies FC layers connect jointly to all sentences, while Eq. (2) implies a per-sentence shared mapping plus q; needs reconciliation.

- Issue 2 (model design): Eq. (2) composes two linear layers (Wf2, Wf1), which is linear overall—need an ablation to justify the extra layer beyond dropout and explanation for sharing FC parameters between BRNN outputs ot and coherence vector q.

- Clarity request: Clarify whether paragraph/doc2vec vectors are fixed or fine-tuned during training.

- Originality note: Integration of entity-grid coherence with multimodal sequence scoring is novel, but the claim that [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is inaccurate—LRCN can yield coherent outputs via temporal semantic modeling; revise related-work claim.

- Evaluation metric recommendation: Emphasize METEOR (and CIDEr) over BLEU given single-reference settings.

- Pros summary: Clear, novel architecture; strong empirical gains; meaningful ablation; human studies; large-scale data and promised code/dataset release.

- Cons summary: Minor technical clarity issues (Fig. 2b/Eq.2 discrepancy, linear layers, parameter sharing) and an overstated related-work claim about [5].
