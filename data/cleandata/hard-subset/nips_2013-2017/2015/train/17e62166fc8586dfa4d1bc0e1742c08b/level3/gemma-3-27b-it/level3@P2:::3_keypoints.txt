- Proposes CRCN: integrates CNN image features, bidirectional RNN language model, and a local coherence model to generate coherent sentence sequences from image streams.  
- Leverages a large corpus of blog posts with images as a natural large-scale training dataset.  
- Shows strong quantitative performance (BLEU, top-K recall) against baselines.  
- Supports results with user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk.  
- Addresses a relevant and timely problem: extending single-image captioning to image streams.  
- Coherence model is a notable contribution for ensuring fluency and logical flow across sentences.  
- Technical approach is well-motivated and builds appropriately on CNN/RNN foundations.  
- Paper provides clear architecture, training details, and experimental setup aiding reproducibility.  
- Quantitative results (Table 1) demonstrate a clear advantage over baselines.  
- User study results (Table 2) show a smaller but positive preference for CRCN.  
- Related work is incomplete and omits recent relevant papers (e.g., Xu et al., 2015).  
- User study improvements over some baselines are marginal and need stronger validation.  
- No statistical significance testing reported for user study results; such tests are necessary.  
- Data augmentation technique lacks thorough analysis and ablation (e.g., impact of κ).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address related work and statistical testing.  
- Overall: well-executed, promising work that could advance image/video description with the suggested fixes.
