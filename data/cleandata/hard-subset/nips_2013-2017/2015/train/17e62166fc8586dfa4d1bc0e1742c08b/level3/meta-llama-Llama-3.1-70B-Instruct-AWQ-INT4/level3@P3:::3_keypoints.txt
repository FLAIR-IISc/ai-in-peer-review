- Proposes CRCN, a multimodal model combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model to generate sentence sequences from image streams.  
- Trains on a large blog-post dataset where posts are converted into image–sentence sequences.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of architecture and training procedure.  
- Experimental results show CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art baselines on BLEU and top-K recall metrics.  
- User study on Amazon Mechanical Turk indicates human-preference improvements.  
- Novelty: addresses a less-studied problem and introduces an interesting local coherence component for fluent multi-sentence generation.  
- Strength: innovative combination of multimodal and coherence modeling with potential broad applications (captioning, video description, HCI).  
- Weakness: literature review is not thorough and omits relevant prior work (e.g., "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text...").  
- Weakness: evaluation methodology limited — small MTurk participant pool may not generalize.  
- Weakness: lacks statistical significance testing to validate reported improvements.  
- Originality: meaningful contribution but builds on existing techniques rather than being wholly novel.  
- Recommendation: overall positive — accept with revisions to expand related work coverage and strengthen evaluation (larger user study and significance tests).
