- Proposes CRCN to generate coherent sentence sequences for image streams, extending image captioning beyond single-image pairs.  
- CRCN integrates CNN image features, bidirectional RNN language model, and a local coherence model.  
- Trains on a large blog-image corpus and promises to release code and data for reproducibility.  
- Demonstrates superior performance to baselines using quantitative metrics (BLEU, Top-K Recall) and Amazon MTurk user studies.  
- Well-motivated and structured problem with extensive experimental evaluation.  
- Incorrectly claims related work [5] lacks coherence modeling, showing a gap in familiarity with closely related literature.  
- Diagram in Figure 2b conflicts with Section 3.3 and Eq. (2): architecture suggests full-sequence FC layers while equation implies per-sentence shared parameters.  
- Heavy reliance on BLEU for sequence generation evaluation; metrics like METEOR may better assess fluency and adequacy.  
- Design questions: two sequential linear functions in Eq. (2) appear redundant and need ablation-based justification.  
- Unclear rationale for sharing FC layer parameters between BRNN outputs and local coherence representation; requires explanation.  
- Missing clarity on whether paragraph vectors are fine-tuned during training, affecting reproducibility and result interpretation.  
- Initial recommendation: weak reject pending corrections to related-work claims, architectural consistency, design justifications, and implementation details.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed concerns, clarified mistakes, promised ablation and confirmed paragraph vectors are not fine-tuned.  
- Final revised recommendation: accept, contingent on incorporating agreed-upon changes into the final manuscript.
