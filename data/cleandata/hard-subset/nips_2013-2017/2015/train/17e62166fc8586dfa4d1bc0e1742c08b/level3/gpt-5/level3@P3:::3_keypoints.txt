- Tackles image-stream-to-paragraph generation with a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs, bidirectional RNN over paragraph vectors, and an entity-grid coherence model.  
- Trains with a max-margin structured loss on image–sentence-sequence pairs harvested from blogs; retrieves candidate sentences by KNN and assembles sequences with divide-and-conquer beam search.  
- Demonstrates improvements on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and retrieval metrics on two tourism blog corpora, with modest AMT user-study gains.  
- Novelty: lifts input/output to sequences and explicitly integrates an entity-grid coherence model into a multimodal framework.  
- Technical soundness: architecture and loss are reasonable and ablation (RCN vs. CRCN) is informative.  
- End-to-end claim is overstated: CNN is fixed (VGG fc7) and coherence module depends on external parsing/coreference — need clarification on fine-tuning and gradient flow.  
- The scoring formulation mixes per-sentence embeddings with a single coherence vector g dotted with each image vector — need justification for per-time-step use of sequence-level g.  
- Retrieval evaluation protocol is unclear: explain how R@K and median rank are computed given disjoint train/test and what constitutes a correct match.  
- Preprocessing via segmentation and LSA summarization likely introduces noise; analyze its quality and effect on performance.  
- Missing statistical testing: report significance for automatic metrics (paired bootstrap/permutation) and AMT preferences (binomial test, confidence intervals) plus inter-annotator agreement.  
- Experiments show strong quantitative scores and CRCN gains on retrieval metrics, but AMT advantage is weak for short sequences and needs statistical backing.  
- Provide more detail on user study settings (number of queries, M, K, Q) and effect of KNN candidate quality.  
- Clarity issues: define all symbols (e.g., g) at first use and justify dimensionality padding for the coherence vector.  
- Expand candidate generation and divide-and-conquer beam search description (complexity, pruning, effect of M).  
- Specify whether paragraph vectors are trained jointly or fixed and justify using sentence-level doc2vec over word-level RNN/LSTM encoders.  
- Related work: incomplete coverage of video-to-text literature (e.g., S2VT Venugopalan et al. ICCV 2015, Xu et al. AAAI 2015, temporal-attention models); discuss how coherence modeling differs from temporal modeling.  
- Significance: relevant and likely influential for paragraph-level captioning, but practical gains are limited for short sequences and retrieval-based sentence selection may limit generalization.  
- Pros: timely problem, well-motivated architecture, strong metrics, clear ablation, pragmatic use of large weakly supervised blog data.  
- Cons: no statistical significance analysis, incomplete related work, ambiguous retrieval protocol, noisy preprocessing, not fully end-to-end, coherence–image interaction needs better justification.  
- Suggestions: add statistical tests and AMT agreement, clarify/justify g scoring or use sequence-level term, compare paragraph-vector BRNN to word-level LSTM/GRU encoders, ablate preprocessing, expand related work, and consider fine-tuning CNN and releasing data/splits.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept contingent on strengthened evaluation, clarified protocols, and more complete related-work discussion.
