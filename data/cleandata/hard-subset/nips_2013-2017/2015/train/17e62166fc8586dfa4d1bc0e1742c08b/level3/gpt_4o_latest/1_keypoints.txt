- Addresses generating natural-language descriptions for image sequences, extending single-image captioning to multimodal sequence-level description.
- Proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs for images, BRNNs for sentence modeling, and an entity-based coherence model for sentence transitions.
- Evaluated on a crawled blog-post dataset with quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr) and AMT user studies, showing superior performance to prior methods.
- Well-written and clearly explains the CRCN architecture and its components.
- Integration of an entity-based coherence model into multimodal captioning is a notable and novel contribution.
- Uses real-world blog posts as training data, which is an innovative and challenging dataset choice.
- Dataset crawling and preprocessing lack transparency and the dataset is not disclosed, harming reproducibility and anonymity.
- Sentence generation is somewhat primitive (direct associations and simple concatenation), potentially limiting handling of complex scenarios.
- Equation (2) appears incorrect, indicating a possible flaw in network connectivity that needs clarification or correction.
- Insufficient citation and discussion of related work on video content description weakens positioning and originality claims.
- Overall technically sound with promising results; suitable for acceptance as a poster but requires dataset disclosure, improved sentence generation, and better related-work coverage.
