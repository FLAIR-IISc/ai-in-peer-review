- Problem & approach: Retrieves and composes sentences from blog corpora to describe image streams using a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) with CNN, BRNN over sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid coherence model, trained with a max-margin objective and decoded via KNN + beam search.

- Relation to prior work: Extends retrieval-based image captioning to multi-sentence streams, complements image-paragraph retrieval, and differs from video description by explicitly modeling discourse coherence.

- Strength — novelty: Moves beyond single-image captions to coherent multi-sentence descriptions with an interpretable local coherence component.

- Strength — methodological integration: Combines VGG visual features, BRNN sentence-flow modeling, and entity-grid coherence in a unified scoring function.

- Strength — data & evaluation: Uses large blog-derived datasets (~20k posts, ~140k images) with comprehensive automatic metrics and AMT human studies.

- Strength — empirical results: Shows consistent gains over strong baselines and ablations, with coherence particularly beneficial for longer sequences.

- Strength — reproducibility: Authors commit to releasing code and data.

- Weakness — related-work wording: Claim that prior video model [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is too strong and should be softened to contrast discourse coherence vs. temporal smoothness.

- Weakness — architectural clarity: Fig. 2(b) and Sec. 3.3/Eq. (2) are ambiguous about parameter sharing and per-time vs. sequence-level pathways.

- Weakness — FC layers/dropout: The claim that two FC layers matter because of dropout needs validation via a small ablation comparing one vs. two FC layers (with/without dropout).

- Weakness — parameter sharing: Sharing FC parameters between BRNN outputs o_t and coherence vector q is a strong inductive bias that requires empirical (separate-parameter ablation) and conceptual justification.

- Weakness — scoring design: Adding g_k·v_lt for every t scales coherence alignment by sequence length; consider normalization or a sequence-level visual summary and clarify whether normalization is used.

- Weakness — sentence/CNN fine-tuning: Unclear whether doc2vec paragraph vectors and VGG features are fixed or fine-tuned; authors should clarify and report fine-tuning effects.

- Weakness — metrics emphasis: BLEU with single references is brittle; METEOR and CIDEr should be foregrounded and multi-reference evaluation considered.

- Weakness — preprocessing noise: Paragraph-assignment heuristics and LSA summarization can introduce noise; include error analysis and robustness discussion, especially for parser/coreference errors affecting coherence.

- Weakness — decoding/scalability: Divide-and-conquer beam search is reasonable but a DP formulation under Markov assumptions might be more efficient or exact; discuss trade-offs.

- Quality: Overall technically sound with clear objective and controlled comparisons; clarifications would strengthen the contribution.

- Clarity: Generally well-written; improve Fig. 2(b)/Eq. (2) consistency and expand on key design choices.

- Originality: Novel integration of entity-based coherence into multimodal retrieval for image-stream-to-paragraph generation.

- Significance: Moderate–high practical relevance; released resources would boost impact.

- Questions for authors: 1) Are doc2vec and VGG features frozen or fine-tuned? 2) Why share FC parameters between o_t and q; results for separate projections? 3) Ablation for two FC layers vs one? 4) Effect of normalizing the coherence term or using sequence-level visual summaries? 5) Sensitivity to K (neighbors) and κ (augmented sentences)? 6) Please soften the claim about [5].

- Pros for acceptance: Tackles underexplored, important problem with effective architecture, strong empirical and human-evaluation results, clear coherence benefits for long sequences, and commitment to release data/code.

- Cons against acceptance: Overstatements about prior work, unclear architectural choices and missing ablations, reliance on retrieval/generative limits, and noisy preprocessing.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance after rebuttal clarifications and inclusion of requested ablations and clarifications (paragraph vectors treatment, parameter-sharing/FC-layer ablations, and code/data release).
