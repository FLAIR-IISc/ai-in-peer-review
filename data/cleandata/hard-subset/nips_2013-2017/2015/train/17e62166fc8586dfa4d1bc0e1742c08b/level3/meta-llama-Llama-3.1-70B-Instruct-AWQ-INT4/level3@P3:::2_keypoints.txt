- Proposes CRCN: a multimodal model combining CNNs, BiRNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model to retrieve sentence sequences for image streams.
- Trained on a large dataset of blog posts with images and shown to outperform baselines via quantitative metrics and AMT user studies.
- Main contribution: selects full sentences from a database to produce coherent passages, a higher-level alternative to word/phrase-based generation.
- Strength: extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations; ablation shows coherence model benefit; authors plan to release code and data.
- Related-work issue: incorrect claim that [5] has “no mechanism for coherence”—[5] uses an intermediate semantic/topic representation that yields coherence.
- Presentation issue: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Eq.(2)—diagram implies FC layers connected to all sentences while Eq.(2) implies shared per-sentence parameters.
- Metric choice: uses METEOR instead of BLEU for single-reference evaluation but provides limited justification for this choice.
- Model-design question: sequential application of two linear functions (W_f2, W_f1) in Eq.(2) seems redundant; needs ablation to justify.
- Model-design question: unclear why the same fully connected layer parameters are used for BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence model q in Eq.(2).
- Implementation detail missing: whether paragraph vectors [16] are fine-tuned or kept fixed during training.
- Overall assessment: novel and promising with strong results but requires revisions to address related-work inaccuracies, design justifications, and implementation clarifications.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing weaknesses, providing design justifications (ablation), and clarifying implementation details.
