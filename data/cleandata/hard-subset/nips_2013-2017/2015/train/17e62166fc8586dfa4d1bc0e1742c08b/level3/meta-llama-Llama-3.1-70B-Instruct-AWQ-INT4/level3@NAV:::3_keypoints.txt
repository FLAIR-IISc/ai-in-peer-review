- Proposes extending single-image captioning to sequence-of-images-to-sequence-of-sentences generation.
- Introduces Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Trains CRCN on a large dataset of blog posts with associated image streams.
- Evaluates performance with BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall, and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the approach, components, and experiments.
- Provides thorough analysis comparing CRCN to state-of-the-art baselines, noting strengths and weaknesses.
- Main contributions: the CRCN model and its application to the image-stream-to-sentence-sequence problem.
- Strength: tackles a more realistic and challenging problem than single-image captioning.
- Strength: CRCN architecture is well-designed with carefully chosen components.
- Strength: Evaluation is comprehensive, using both quantitative metrics and user studies.
- Weakness: Literature review is incomplete; relevant works are missing (e.g., “Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text...”).
- Weakness: User study results show CRCN performs similarly to the RCN baseline, indicating only a weak advantage.
- Weakness: Lacks statistical significance testing to confirm reported improvements.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem formulation, solid model design, and thorough evaluation.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete related work, marginal gains over baseline, and missing significance tests.
- Overall: Positive contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses, but needs a more comprehensive literature review and statistical significance analysis.
