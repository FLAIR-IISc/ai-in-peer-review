## Review of "Expressing an Image Stream with a Sequence of Natural Sentences"

This paper proposes a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) to generate sequences of sentences from image streams, extending the traditional image-to-sentence generation task. The authors address the realistic scenario where users capture a series of images, necessitating a model that considers the entire stream for coherent descriptions. The CRCN integrates CNNs for image representation, bidirectional RNNs for language modeling, and a novel entity-based local coherence model. The approach is trained on a large corpus of blog posts with associated images, and evaluated through quantitative metrics (BLEU, top-K recall) and a user study on Amazon Mechanical Turk.

The core idea of extending image captioning to image *streams* is a natural and valuable progression, and the proposed CRCN architecture is well-motivated. The inclusion of a local coherence model is particularly insightful, as maintaining consistency across multiple sentences is crucial for generating natural-sounding descriptions. The quantitative results demonstrate strong performance compared to several baselines, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed approach. 

However, the literature review feels incomplete. While the authors mention video-sentence work, they omit several relevant papers, notably “Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text…” by Xu et al. (AAAI 2017), which directly tackles the problem of sequence-to-sequence generation in a visual context and would be a crucial comparison point. 

The user study results (Table 2) show only a marginal advantage over the RCN baseline, suggesting the coherence model’s impact might be less pronounced than anticipated. Critically, the paper lacks a statistical significance test to determine if the reported improvements in both quantitative and qualitative evaluations are statistically reliable. Without this, it’s difficult to confidently claim the CRCN’s superiority.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a novel and relevant problem – generating descriptions for image streams.
*   The proposed CRCN architecture is well-designed and integrates key components for coherence.
*   Strong quantitative results compared to several baselines.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Incomplete literature review.
*   Lack of statistical significance testing for reported improvements.
*   Modest gains in the user study, raising questions about the practical impact of the coherence model.



**Overall:** This is a solid piece of work with a clear contribution. However, addressing the missing literature and adding statistical significance testing are essential for strengthening the paper’s claims and justifying acceptance. I recommend acceptance contingent on these revisions.
