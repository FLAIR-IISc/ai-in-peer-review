- Problem: Retrieving a coherent sequence of sentences to describe a stream of images.  
- Approach: Retrieval-based method that finds candidate sentences per image from nearest-neighbor blog-post examples.  
- Core contribution: Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that scores candidate sentence sequences by modeling image–sentence compatibility and sequence-level coherence.  
- Architecture details: Combines CNN image features with paragraph-vector sentence features, processed by a bidirectional LSTM.  
- Novelty: Explicit integration of an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Scope: Extends single image→sentence tasks to a sequence-to-sequence visual storytelling setting.  
- Strength: Novel architecture for an under-explored problem.  
- Strength: Strong empirical support via quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, and AMT human evaluations.  
- Strength: Ablation study validates the positive impact of the coherence component.  
- Strength: Authors plan to release code and dataset, benefiting the community.  
- Issue: Incorrect claim that prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” — its intermediate topic representation serves a similar role.  
- Issue: Inconsistency between Figure 2b and Equation (2) regarding whether fully connected layers use separate or shared parameters.  
- Issue: Design choices need justification—why two sequential linear layers in Eq. (2) when their composition is linear; an ablation is requested.  
- Issue: Need rationale for sharing parameters between the BRNN output and the coherence model.  
- Issue: Evaluation should include METEOR instead of BLEU, and clarify whether the paragraph vector model is fine-tuned or fixed.  
- Outcome: Rebuttal addressed concerns; recommendation to accept conditional on incorporating clarifications/corrections and specifying paragraph-vector fine-tuning.
