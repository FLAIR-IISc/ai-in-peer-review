- Proposes CRCN to generate coherent sentence sequences for image streams, extending single-image captioning to multimodal sequence generation.  
- CRCN integrates CNNs for images, bidirectional RNNs for sentences, and a local coherence model to smooth transitions.  
- Key insight: using blog posts as training data where image sequences map to coherent text descriptions.  
- Clearly identifies an underexplored, practically relevant problem (social media/personal storytelling).  
- Strong empirical evaluation: quantitative metrics (BLEU, top-K recall), qualitative examples, and AMT human evaluations.  
- Ablation study shows the coherence model is necessary, especially for longer sequences.  
- Commitment to release code and dataset improves reproducibility and impact.  
- Incorrect claim: paper states [5] lacks coherence mechanisms, but [5] models intermediate semantic representations that provide coherence.  
- Methodological inconsistency: Figure 2b conflicts with Equation (2) about whether FC layers are per-sentence or share parameters.  
- Evaluation concern: METEOR is preferable to BLEU given a single-reference test setup.  
- Model clarity issues: sequential W_f1 and W_f2 are redundant (linear composition) and need ablation; parameter sharing between BRNN outputs and coherence model (q) requires justification.  
- Implementation detail missing: clarify whether the paragraph vector is fine-tuned or fixed.  
- Reviewer notes rebuttal addressed concerns and recommends acceptance based on strong contributions and empirical results.
