- Paper proposes CRCN: combines CNN image features, BRNN over sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid coherence model to score/retrieve multi-sentence image-stream descriptions.  
- Uses weakly aligned blog post image–text streams, summarizes segments to sentences, retrieves per-image candidate sentences and ranks concatenations with a learned compatibility score.  
- Key novelty is integrating an explicit discourse coherence term (entity-grid) with a BRNN for multi-sentence visual description.  
- CRCN formulation is generally sound and the entity-based coherence signal is interesting and useful.  
- Eq. (2) is problematic/misleading: notation implies each st depends only on its corresponding ot (and q → g) rather than on all {ot}, so the claimed “fully connected” cross-sentence coupling needs clarification.  
- Decoding (K-NN per image then concatenate with beam/divide-and-conquer) is reasonable but primitive and ad hoc compared to structured sequence decoding.  
- Manuscript is well written and clear at a high level, but implementation/data details (crawling, anonymization, licensing) are insufficiently documented.  
- Framing and many components mirror prior captioning and video-description work; claims of being “first” should be tempered and key prior work must be cited and contrasted.  
- Results show consistent automatic and human-evaluated gains and the coherence component helps, especially for longer sequences; evaluation is limited to two travel domains.  
- Pros: clear problem and solid model, coherent integration of discourse features, demonstrable empirical improvements, and generally reproducible model components (except data issues).  
- Cons: missing key citations and overstated novelty, problematic Eq. (2) and limited cross-time interaction, primitive decoding and incomplete dataset description/anonymity concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept as a poster contingent on fixing Eq. (2)/clarifying architecture, adding related work, cleaning/clarifying dataset protocol, and discussing more principled decoding.
