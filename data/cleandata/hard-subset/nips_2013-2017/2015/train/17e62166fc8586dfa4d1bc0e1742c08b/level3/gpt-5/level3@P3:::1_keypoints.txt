- Paper proposes CRCN: VGG image features + doc2vec sentence BRNN + entity-grid coherence model, trained with max-margin ranking and beam-like assembly from K candidates.
- Main novelty is integrating an entity-based coherence model into a multimodal ranking framework for image-stream to paragraph generation.
- Experiments on two blog-derived datasets (NYC, Disneyland) show improvements over single-sentence baselines and are supported by AMT user studies.
- Related work discussion is insufficient; several multi-sentence/video description papers (e.g., Rohrbach 2013/2014, S2VT, video paragraph works) are missing or under-discussed.
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow; problem statement and training procedure are well explained.
- CRCN is technically sound and well-motivated; ablations (RCN vs CRCN) demonstrate the value of coherence modeling.
- Evaluation is sensible with automatic metrics and user studies, and training scales with a reasonable ranking loss.
- Equation (2) is ambiguous: formulation implies no temporal mixing in FC layers; needs clarification or correction to match intent.
- Scoring has a potential length bias: the summed global coherence term scales with sequence length unless normalized.
- Decoding is primitive and ad-hoc: nearest-neighbor retrieval + concatenation lacks redundancy avoidance, coverage, length control, and global constraints.
- Dataset description is insufficient and raises reproducibility and ethics/anonymity concerns (crawl details, selection criteria, IRB/ethics, de-identification).
- Manual selection of travelogue posts is under-specified (criteria and annotator agreement missing).
- Reuse statement and acknowledgments may compromise double-blind anonymity; compliance with conference policy should be ensured.
- Baselines are weak: need stronger comparisons like retrieval+language-model reordering, sequence-level re-ranking with redundancy penalties, or adapted video-to-text systems.
- Clarity requests: confirm whether s_t depends only on o_t and how q interacts with s_t, plus details on doc2vec training and preprocessing (κ, LSA, parsing, coreference).
- Originality is moderate: task extension is meaningful but close to existing video captioning and retrieval+coherence work; novelty is the specific BRNN + entity-grid combination.
- Significance is limited by ad-hoc decoding and dataset transparency; improvements shown but broader impact tempered.
- Missing related work: additional multi-sentence/video description papers and neural discourse coherence models should be discussed.
- Key methodological questions for authors: are candidate lengths matched, is Eq. (2) intentionally non-temporal, how are duplicate/near-duplicate sentences avoided in decoding, and will datasets/crawl scripts and an ethics statement be released?
- Pros: clear exposition, well-motivated integration of discourse features, solid empirical gains, and scalable training/inference.
- Cons: ambiguous Equation (2), ad-hoc decoding without global constraints, incomplete related work coverage, and insufficient dataset/ethics transparency.
- Recommendation: Accept as a poster, contingent on clarifying Eq. (2), improving decoding, expanding related work, and improving dataset transparency and anonymity compliance.
