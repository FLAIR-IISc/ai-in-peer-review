This paper proposes a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) for expressing an image stream as a sequence of sentences. The architecture couples CNN image features (VGG), a BRNN over sentence embeddings to capture bidirectional discourse flow, and an entity-grid–based local coherence model (Barzilay & Lapata) to encourage fluent transitions. The system is retrieval-based: for each image position it gathers candidate sentences from visually similar training images; CRCN then scores full sequences with a max-margin objective combining image-text compatibility and coherence. Trained on large blog corpora (NYC, Disneyland), the method is evaluated with BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr and retrieval metrics, plus AMT user studies.

Relation to prior work: The paper situates itself against single-image captioning and video description, and cites LRCN and other multimodal RNNs. However, several relevant video-to-text works are missing, e.g., Xu et al., Jointly Modeling Deep Video and Compositional Text (AAAI’15), Venugopalan et al., Sequence to Sequence – Video to Text (CVPR’15), and Yao et al., Describing Videos by Exploiting Temporal Structure (ICCV’15). These should be discussed, especially regarding discourse modeling vs. coherence.

Strengths
- Clear problem formulation of image-stream to multi-sentence description; fits NeurIPS.
- Sensible integration of BRNNs with an entity-based coherence prior; the coherence component is well-motivated for non-continuous image streams.
- Strong quantitative results across multiple metrics; careful engineering of training and approximate search.
- Useful ablations (1NN, RCN vs. CRCN) and a large-scale dataset.

Weaknesses
- User study indicates only a weak advantage over RCN; statistical significance is not reported. Please add appropriate tests (e.g., paired bootstrap or permutation for AMT preferences; bootstrap for BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr with CIs).
- Claims of “end-to-end” learning are somewhat overstated given the retrieval pipeline and heavy preprocessing (LSA summarization, doc2vec, zero-padded coherence vector). Clarify what is learned jointly vs. fixed.
- Limited analysis of sensitivity to κ (data augmentation), beam size M, K-nearest candidates, and the coherence history length; add robustness studies.
- Reproducibility would benefit from releasing code/data splits and more architectural/training details (optimizer settings per module, BPTT truncation length, runtime).

Quality: Technically sound overall; more statistics and error analysis needed.
Clarity: Generally well-written; tighten the description of the scoring function and training vs. inference pipeline.
Originality: Novel combination and application to image streams; coherence integration is a nice contribution, though components are known.
Significance: Potentially useful to practitioners; impact depends on demonstrated human-perceived gains.

Arguments
Pro: Interesting task; coherent integration; good quantitative gains; large dataset.
Con: Marginal human-study advantage over RCN; missing related work; limited statistical rigor and sensitivity analysis.

Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on adding significance tests and expanding related work.
