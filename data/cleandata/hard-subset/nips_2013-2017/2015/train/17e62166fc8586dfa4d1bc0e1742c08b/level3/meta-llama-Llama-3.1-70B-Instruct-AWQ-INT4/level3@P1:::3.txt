This paper proposes a novel approach for generating natural language descriptions from an image stream, extending previous work that focused on single image-sentence pairs. The authors introduce a multimodal architecture called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), which combines convolutional neural networks (CNNs), bidirectional recurrent neural networks (RNNs), and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with associated images, and its performance is evaluated using both quantitative measures and user studies.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The literature review is thorough, covering related work in image-sentence generation, multimodal neural networks, and coherence models. However, the reviewer notes that several related video-to-sentence works are missing from the literature review.

The quantitative results reported for the proposed method look quite good, with the CRCN model outperforming several state-of-the-art baselines in terms of language similarity metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR) and retrieval accuracies (top-K recall and median rank). However, the user study results (Table 2) show that the method performs similarly to the RCN baseline, indicating only a weak advantage. This raises questions about the significance of the reported improvements and the need for a statistical significance test to determine whether the results are reliable.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting and well-motivated approach to generating natural language descriptions from image streams. However, the reviewer would like to see more rigorous evaluation and analysis of the results, including a statistical significance test, to confirm the effectiveness of the proposed method.
