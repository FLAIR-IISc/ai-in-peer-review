- Task & contribution: Introduces CRCN, a multimodal retrieval-and-ranking model that generates coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams by combining CNN image features, a BRNN for sentence context, and an entity-based coherence model.  
- Novelty: Moves beyond single-image captioning to coherent paragraph generation and explicitly integrates a symbolic coherence model into a deep architecture.  
- Training/data: Trained on large-scale blog post image-text streams and selects sentence sequences by retrieving candidates per image and ranking by CRCN scores.  
- Significance: Addresses a practical, challenging problem with clear applications (automatic content creation, summarization, HCI).  
- Strength — Evaluation: Thorough experiments against strong baselines using BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr/recall@K/median rank plus qualitative examples and an AMT user study favoring CRCN.  
- Strength — Ablation: Ablation (RCN vs CRCN) demonstrates the added value of the explicit coherence model, especially on longer sequences per AMT results.  
- Strength — Quality & data: Clever use of large-scale blog data and solid empirical advances over prior work.  
- Strength — Reproducibility: Authors plan to release code and the NYC dataset, aiding reproducibility and follow-up work.  
- Weakness — Related work: Overstates prior work's lack of coherence modeling; LSTM-based models capture implicit coherence and the distinction should be clarified as “explicit” vs “implicit.”  
- Weakness — Architecture clarity: Figure 2b and Equation (2) appear inconsistent about whether BRNN outputs are concatenated or transformed per-sentence; clarify exact architecture.  
- Weakness — Design justification: Two consecutive linear layers in Eq.(2) collapse to one linear map unless dropout/nonlinearity justifies them—please state this and consider an ablation vs single FC layer.  
- Weakness — Parameter sharing: The same FC parameters project per-sentence BRNN outputs and the global coherence vector; motivate or justify this choice.  
- Weakness — Implementation detail: Specify whether 300-d paragraph vectors are fixed after pre-training or fine-tuned during end-to-end CRCN training.  
- Weakness — Evaluation emphasis: Given BLEU’s limits for sentence-level single-reference evaluation, emphasize METEOR and CIDEr more as they align better with human judgments.  
- Overall verdict: High-quality, significant work with minor clarifications needed; recommend acceptance.
