

# Review for "Expressing an Image Stream with a Sequence of Natural Sentences"

## Summary
This paper introduces a novel task of generating coherent sentence sequences to describe image streams, extending the traditional single-image captioning problem. The authors propose a multimodal architecture called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks for image representation, bidirectional recurrent neural networks for sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model for fluent transitions between sentences. The paper leverages user-generated blog posts as training data, transforming them into text-image parallel sequences through text segmentation and summarization. The authors evaluate their approach on NYC and Disneyland blog datasets (20K+ posts, 140K images), demonstrating superior performance over state-of-the-art baselines in both quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and human evaluations via Amazon Mechanical Turk.

The paper addresses a meaningful extension of the image captioning problem, as users often capture sequences of images during memorable experiences rather than single images. While related work has explored single-image captioning (e.g., [9, 12, 14, 26]) and video captioning (e.g., [23, 32]), this paper is the first to specifically address the problem of generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams. The authors correctly note that existing approaches (e.g., [5]) do not address the generation of multiple sequential sentences with explicit coherence modeling, though their characterization of [5] as having "no mechanism for coherence" is inaccurate—[5] models intermediate semantic/topic representations that inherently yield coherence.

## Strengths
1. **Novel Task**: The paper identifies and addresses a meaningful extension of image captioning that better matches real-world user behavior of capturing image streams during experiences.

2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The authors provide extensive quantitative results across multiple metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and conduct thorough human evaluations via Amazon Mechanical Turk, demonstrating consistent superiority of their approach over baselines.

3. **Ablation Studies**: The ablation of the coherence model (comparing CRCN to RCN) clearly demonstrates the importance of coherence for longer sentence sequences, with human evaluations showing significantly larger performance gaps for longer passages.

4. **Data Collection**: The use of blog posts as natural text-image parallel data is innovative and provides a large-scale, real-world dataset that avoids the need for expensive manual annotation.

5. **Promised Release**: The authors commit to releasing code and dataset, which will greatly facilitate future research in this area.

## Weaknesses
1. **Inaccurate Related Work Claim**: The paper incorrectly states that [5] has "no mechanism for coherence." [5] explicitly models semantic topics across frames, which inherently creates coherent captions. This needs correction to accurately position the contribution.

2. **Figure-Text Inconsistency**: Figure 2(b) appears inconsistent with Section 3.3 and Equation (2), where the diagram suggests FC layers connected to all sentences, but Equation (2) indicates shared parameters applied per sentence. This needs clarification.

3. **Evaluation Metric Choice**: While BLEU is commonly used, METEOR would be more appropriate for this task, especially with a single reference sentence, as it better accounts for semantic similarity. The authors should justify their metric choice or include METEOR results.

4. **Model Design Questions**: The sequential application of two linear layers (W_f2 and W_f1 in Equation 2) appears redundant since their composition is linear. An ablation study would help justify this design choice. Similarly, the rationale for using the same fully connected parameters for BRNN outputs and the coherence model needs clarification.

5. **Implementation Detail**: The paper should clarify whether the paragraph vectors (used for text representation) are fine-tuned or kept fixed during training, as this impacts the model's flexibility and training dynamics.

## Arguments for and against Acceptance

**Arguments for Acceptance**:
- The paper addresses a meaningful extension of image captioning that better matches real-world usage scenarios.
- The CRCN architecture provides a coherent solution to the problem of generating sentence sequences from image streams.
- Extensive quantitative and qualitative evaluations demonstrate clear advantages over baselines.
- The approach is general and applicable to any domain with blog posts containing images.

**Arguments against Acceptance**:
- The incorrect characterization of prior work undermines the paper's positioning.
- Several technical inconsistencies in the model description require clarification.
- The justification for certain architectural choices (two linear layers, shared parameters) is insufficient.

## Recommendation
This paper presents a novel and valuable extension of image captioning to handle image streams with coherent sentence sequences. The core contribution is well-motivated and the evaluation is comprehensive. While there are several technical concerns that need to be addressed (particularly regarding the related work characterization and model design), the authors have already acknowledged these in their rebuttal and committed to making the necessary clarifications.

The paper makes a significant contribution to the field by addressing a meaningful gap in the literature and demonstrating a practical approach with strong empirical results. The proposed CRCN architecture provides a clear framework for generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions from image sequences, and the human evaluations convincingly show the value of the coherence model.

I recommend acceptance with the condition that the authors address the specific technical clarifications mentioned above in the final version. The paper is scientifically sound, well-motivated, and makes a clear contribution to the field that aligns well with NIPS' focus on high-quality, impactful research.
