Summary and relation to prior work
The paper tackles the underexplored problem of expressing an image stream with a sequence of natural sentences. The proposed Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) integrates: (i) CNNs (VGG fc7) for images; (ii) a bidirectional RNN over sentence-level paragraph vectors to model content flow across sentences; and (iii) an entity-based local coherence model (entity grid à la Barzilay & Lapata) to encourage coherent sentence-to-sentence transitions. The model is trained with a max-margin structured loss over paired image- and sentence-sequences harvested from blog posts, which are preprocessed via segmentation and LSA-based summarization to obtain per-image sentences. At test time, CRCN retrieves candidate sentences by KNN over images and uses a divide-and-conquer beam search to assemble high-scoring sequences. Experiments on two tourism blog corpora (NYC, Disneyland) show improvements over several captioning/retrieval baselines on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and retrieval metrics, plus modest gains in AMT user studies.

The work connects to (and extends) single-image captioning and multimodal embedding approaches (e.g., Karpathy & Fei-Fei; Kiros et al.; Vinyals et al.) by lifting both input and output to sequences and explicitly modeling textual coherence. It is also related to video-to-text models (e.g., Donahue et al. LRCN; Rohrbach et al. 2013). The explicit integration of an entity-grid coherence model into a multimodal framework is novel in this space.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The architecture and loss are reasonable for sequence-level retrieval. The entity-grid coherence signal is a thoughtful addition and the ablation (RCN vs. CRCN) is informative. However:
  - The “end-to-end” claim is somewhat overstated: the CNN appears fixed (VGG fc7), and the coherence module depends on external parsing/coreference; please clarify whether the CNN is fine-tuned and how gradients flow to the BRNN and FC layers.
  - The scoring mixes per-sentence text embeddings st with a single coherence-derived vector g that is dotted with each image vector vlt. Conceptually, g is a property of the candidate text sequence; explaining why g·vlt is meaningful per time step (vs. a sequence-level term) would help.
  - Retrieval metrics: It is unclear how R@K and median rank are computed given training/test disjointness; if test sentences never appear in training, what is the “ground truth” being retrieved? Please clarify the protocol and what constitutes a correct match.
  - Preprocessing (segmentation, LSA summarization) likely contributes noise. An analysis of segmentation/summarization quality and its effect on performance would strengthen the empirical claims.
  - Statistical testing is missing. Key differences (especially CRCN vs. RCN in Table 2) are small. Please report significance for:
    - Automatic metrics (paired bootstrap or permutation test for BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR).
    - AMT preferences (two-sided binomial/sign test with confidence intervals) and inter-annotator agreement (e.g., Fleiss’ kappa).
- Experiments: Quantitative scores are strong overall, and the retrieval metrics show clear CRCN gains over RCN. The user study suggests only a weak advantage over RCN for short sequences (≤5 images), with larger gaps for longer sequences; this supports the coherence motivation, but needs statistical backing and more detail (number of queries, M, K, Q settings; effect of KNN candidate quality).

Clarity
The paper is generally well-written and organized. Suggestions:
- Clearly define all symbols (e.g., g) at first use, and justify the dimensionality padding for the coherence vector.
- Expand the description of candidate generation and the divide-and-conquer beam search (complexity, pruning, effect of M).
- Specify whether paragraph vectors are trained jointly or fixed, and why sentence-level doc2vec was preferred over word-level RNN/LSTM encoders.
- Provide dataset splits and release code/data if possible to aid reproducibility.

Originality
- Novel overall task setup (image-stream to paragraph) and the integration of an entity-grid coherence model with CNN/RNN is original and interesting.
- Related work coverage on video-to-sentence is incomplete. In addition to Donahue et al. (LRCN) and Rohrbach et al. (2013), please discuss:
  - Venugopalan et al., Sequence to Sequence – Video to Text (S2VT), ICCV 2015.
  - Xu et al., Jointly Modeling Deep Video and Compositional Text, AAAI 2015 (cited in refs but not discussed).
  - Other contemporaneous video captioning efforts (e.g., Yao et al., temporal attention; if applicable).
Explain how your coherence modeling differs from and complements temporal modeling in video-based methods.

Significance
- The problem is relevant and practically useful (photo streams). The architecture is likely to influence follow-on work on paragraph-level captioning and multimodal discourse modeling.
- That said, the method performs similarly to the RCN baseline in AMT for short sequences, indicating only a weak practical advantage in common settings unless sequences are long. The reliance on sentence retrieval (vs. true generation) may limit generalization to novel content and domains.
- Releasing the blog-stream dataset/splits would increase impact.

Pros
- Timely problem and a coherent, well-motivated architecture.
- Strong results on multiple metrics; clear ablation demonstrating the role of coherence.
- Use of large, weakly supervised blog data is pragmatic.

Cons
- Missing statistical significance analysis; AMT gains over RCN are small for short sequences.
- Incomplete related work on video-to-text.
- Ambiguities in retrieval evaluation protocol; reliance on noisy preprocessing; limited discussion of errors and failure cases.
- Not truly end-to-end; modeling choice for coherence-image interaction could be better justified.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add statistical tests and confidence intervals for all reported improvements; report AMT agreement.
- Clarify and justify the scoring term involving g; consider a sequence-level coherence compatibility term.
- Compare paragraph-vector BRNN to a word-level LSTM/GRU sentence encoder within CRCN.
- Provide analysis of preprocessing quality and an ablation quantifying its effect.
- Expand related work discussion as noted and include S2VT and Xu et al. in the narrative.
- If feasible, fine-tune the CNN and report impact; release data/splits.

Recommendation
Borderline accept. This is a nice, interesting piece of work with a novel integration of coherence modeling and solid quantitative performance. With strengthened evaluation (statistical testing), clarified protocols, and a more complete discussion of related video-to-sentence work, it would make a valuable NIPS contribution.
