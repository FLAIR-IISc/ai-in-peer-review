- Novel task: generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams, extending single-image captioning.  
- Proposed model: Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Data: leverage user-generated blog posts transformed into text-image parallel sequences (NYC and Disneyland datasets: 20K+ posts, ~140K images).  
- Evaluation: extensive quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and AMT human evaluations showing CRCN outperforms baselines.  
- Ablation: removing the coherence model (RCN) degrades performance noticeably, especially for longer passages per human evals.  
- Reproducibility: authors commit to releasing code and dataset.  
- Weakness — related work: incorrect claim that [5] has “no mechanism for coherence”; [5] models semantic/topics across frames.  
- Weakness — figure/text mismatch: Figure 2(b) conflicts with Section 3.3/Equation (2) about FC layer connectivity vs shared per-sentence parameters.  
- Weakness — metrics: BLEU is used but METEOR may be more appropriate for single-reference sentence evaluation; justification or METEOR results needed.  
- Weakness — model design: sequential linear layers (W_f2, W_f1) appear redundant and shared FC parameter use between BRNN outputs and coherence model needs justification or ablation.  
- Weakness — implementation detail: unclear whether paragraph vectors are fine-tuned or kept fixed during training.  
- Arguments for acceptance: meaningful problem, coherent architectural solution, strong empirical results, and general applicability to blog-image domains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: mischaracterization of prior work, technical inconsistencies in model description, and insufficient justification for certain architectural choices.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing the related-work correction and technical clarifications; authors have pledged to make these fixes.
