- Problem: Generates coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams, extending single-image captioning to paragraph-level narration.  
- Method: Proposes CRCN combining CNN image features, a bidirectional RNN for sentence flow, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Training/inference: Trained on large blog-post dataset with a max-margin objective; testing framed as retrieval over candidate sentence sequences.  
- Novelty/significance: Tackles a timely, important extension of image captioning to photo streams and paragraph generation.  
- Technical approach: Well-motivated architecture integrating BRNN and an explicit coherence model to capture semantic flow and referential consistency.  
- Quantitative results: CRCN outperforms adapted state-of-the-art baselines on BLEU, CIDEr, R@K, and Median Rank metrics.  
- Dataset: Uses a large-scale, real-world blog-post dataset, enhancing practical relevance.  
- Missing related work: Fails to discuss several relevant visual-sequence-to-language works (e.g., video-description literature such as Xu et al., AAAI 2015).  
- Weak empirical support for coherence model: User study shows only marginal gains over the RCN baseline for short sequences and modest improvements for longer ones.  
- No statistical significance tests: Results lack significance analysis, making it unclear if reported improvements are reliable.  
- Overall assessment: Interesting and well-executed paper with valuable contributions, but needs better related-work coverage and statistical validation to strengthen claims about the coherence component.
