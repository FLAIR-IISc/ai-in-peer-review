- Tackles multi-sentence description for image streams, extending single-image captioning to sequences.  
- Proposes a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and a local coherence model.  
- Writing is clear and the problem statement is well-defined.  
- Model is technically sound and aims to capture discourse-level coherence.  
- Contribution feels incremental, largely reusing established building blocks (CNNs, RNNs, coherence models).  
- Related work omissions: important prior video-description and multi-sentence captioning papers (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014) are not cited.  
- Equation (2) appears flawed, implying sentence-level state depends only on image feature ot and may break recurrent connectivity.  
- Sentence sequence generation (retrieving per-image sentences and concatenating) is primitive and lacks modeling of the whole stream.  
- Demonstrates performance gains over simple baselines.  
- Dataset description is insufficient: unclear crawling procedures and potential anonymity/privacy issues.  
- Acceptance arguments: clear motivation, readable paper, coherent architecture, empirical improvements.  
- Rejection arguments: incremental novelty, missing prior work, possibly incorrect equation, naïve generation method, and dataset/ethical concerns.  
- Recommendation: Acceptable as a poster; needs fixes to related work, equation, and dataset/ethical clarifications for a full paper.
