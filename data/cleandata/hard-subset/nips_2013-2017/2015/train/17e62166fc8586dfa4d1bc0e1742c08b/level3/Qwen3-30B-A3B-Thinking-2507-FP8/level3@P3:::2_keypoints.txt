- Proposes CRCN to generate coherent sentence sequences for image streams, combining CNNs for images, BRNNs for sentence modeling, and a local coherence model based on entity transitions.
- Trains on blog posts and evaluates on NYC and Disneyland datasets (~20K posts, 140K images).
- Addresses an important gap beyond single-image captioning by producing narrative-like descriptions for image sequences.
- CRCN is a thoughtful integration of existing techniques into a unified sequence-to-sequence image description framework.
- Strong empirical results (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and human evaluation; ablations show BRNN and coherence model contribute meaningfully.
- Authors promise to release code and dataset, improving reproducibility and community impact.
- Incorrectly claims prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence,” despite its semantic-topic modeling that provides coherence.
- Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Eq.(2): diagram suggests FC layers connected to all sentences while equation implies shared per-sentence parameters.
- Evaluation choice critique: METEOR should be emphasized over BLEU for single-reference longer texts due to better correlation with human judgment.
- Architectural question: using two sequential linear functions (W_f2, W_f1) is redundant unless justified—requires ablation or explanation.
- Clarify why the same fully connected layer parameters are used for BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence model q in Eq.(2).
- Implementation detail missing: whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed during training.
- Reviewers judge issues as minor and fixable; authors addressed some concerns in rebuttal, clarifying coherence in [5].
- Final recommendation: Accept — novel problem formulation, effective approach, solid evaluation; minor technical clarifications required.
