- Proposes a novel task: retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream and introduces the CRCN model to handle sequence-to-sequence multimodal retrieval.  
- CRCN combines CNNs for images, bidirectional RNNs for sentence sequence modeling, and a local coherence model for fluent sentence transitions.  
- Leverages online natural blog posts as text-image parallel training data — a creative, effective way to address dataset scarcity.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and authors promise to release source code and dataset.  
- Evaluation is extensive: quantitative metrics, qualitative results, and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies; CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation including human judgments and qualitative analysis.  
- Weakness: Incorrect claim that prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” — [5] uses an intermediate semantic/topic representation that yields coherence.  
- Weakness: Discrepancy between Figure 2b and Section 3.3/Eq.(2) about whether fully connected layers are shared across sentences or applied per sentence; needs clarification.  
- Weakness: BLEU is a poor choice with single references; METEOR (or other metrics) would be more appropriate.  
- Weakness: Model design question — applying two sequential linear functions (W_f2, W_f1) is redundant unless justified; an ablation study is needed.  
- Weakness: Parameter sharing between the FC layer for BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence model q in Eq.(2) requires a clear rationale.  
- Implementation detail unclear: specify whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10); authors should address the listed issues and clarify design/implementation choices.
