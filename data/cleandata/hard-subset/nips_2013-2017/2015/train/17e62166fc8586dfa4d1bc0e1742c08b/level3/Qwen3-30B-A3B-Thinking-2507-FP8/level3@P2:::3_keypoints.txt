- Task: Extends image captioning to generate coherent sequences of sentences from image streams rather than single images.  
- Model: Proposes CRCN combining CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Novelty: Key contribution is extending both input and output to sequences and explicitly addressing image-stream-to-text generation.  
- Data: Leverages blog posts as text-image parallel data and evaluates on a large dataset (≈20K posts, 140K images).  
- Strength: Original and meaningful problem formulation that better matches how people document events.  
- Strength: Well-motivated technical design integrating image representation, sentence modeling, and coherence components.  
- Strength: Interesting use of paragraph vectors and entity-based coherence to encourage fluent transitions.  
- Evaluation: Uses multiple automatic metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and AMT user studies.  
- Weakness: Improvements over the RCN baseline are marginal, especially in user studies, raising doubts about the coherence model’s impact.  
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported for user studies or other comparisons.  
- Weakness: Related work is incomplete—several video-to-sentence papers are omitted, weakening novelty claims.  
- Weakness: Coherence model implementation (entity grid, transition patterns) lacks sufficient technical detail and clarity.  
- Quality: Technically sound and well-organized overall, but experimental claims need stronger analysis and validation.  
- Significance: Problem is important and approach is broadly applicable (unsupervised), but limited empirical gains reduce perceived impact.  
- Decision rationale: Recommend rejection as-is, but the paper could be strengthened with additional analysis, clearer coherence-model description, significance testing, and fuller literature coverage.
