- Paper tackles generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams using a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN).
- CRCN combines VGG fc7 image features, a bidirectional RNN over doc2vec sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid coherence model.
- Candidate sentences are retrieved via KNN in image space and sequences are scored by a max-margin multimodal compatibility function blending per-sentence content and global coherence.
- Training uses weakly aligned text–image sequences from blog posts (paragraph segmentation + LSA summarization); decoding uses a divide-and-conquer beam-like search.
- Experiments on two travel blog corpora (NYC, Disneyland) show improvements on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and retrieval metrics over single-sentence baselines.
- AMT pairwise preferences generally favor CRCN over baselines, with larger gains on longer streams.
- Paper situates itself relative to image-captioning and video-to-text work but should discuss more explicitly related video-to-text models and hierarchical decoders.
- Main novelty: integrating entity-grid coherence modeling into a sequence-level retrieval-and-ranking framework for image streams.
- Technical concern: adding the same gk·vlt coherence term at each timestep may introduce length bias; suggest normalizing by sequence length or using a single aggregated term.
- Technical concern: 64-d entity-grid features zero-padded to 300-d before fusion is ad hoc; a learned projection/MLP would be more principled.
- Evaluation issues: lacks statistical significance testing for automatic metrics and AMT; missing inter-annotator agreement and confidence intervals.
- Baselines are weak for sequence modeling—concatenating per-image captions handicaps coherence comparisons; need at least one sequence-aware neural baseline.
- Analysis lacking sensitivity studies for K (nearest neighbors), M (beam size), Q (divide-and-conquer depth), κ (summary sentences), doc2vec dimensionality, and BRNN mappings.
- Request runtime/complexity analysis and report on length normalization effects for retrieval metrics.
- Clarify notation around coherence vectors (q vs g) and the two FC layers (state nonlinearity), and fix minor reference inconsistencies.
- Preprocessing (paragraph tokenizer, alignment, LSA) is noisy; include qualitative error analysis of summarization and alignment quality.
- Recommend providing oracle upper bounds and analysis of how approximate search compares to exact search; report sensitivity to beam size M.
- Address potential data leakage from near-duplicate images/authors and consider cross-domain generalization (train on one domain, test on another).
- Clarify "unsupervised" wording—approach is better described as weakly supervised using noisy aligned pairs.
- Encourage releasing data splits and code for reproducibility.
- Pros: addresses underexplored problem, novel coherence integration, scalable weakly supervised data, solid gains especially on longer sequences.
- Cons: missing statistical tests, modest AMT margins over RCN, lack of strong sequence-aware baselines, some ad hoc design choices, limited analysis of preprocessing and generalization.
- Overall: interesting and promising work that would be stronger with significance testing, stronger baselines, clearer modeling choices, and expanded analysis.
