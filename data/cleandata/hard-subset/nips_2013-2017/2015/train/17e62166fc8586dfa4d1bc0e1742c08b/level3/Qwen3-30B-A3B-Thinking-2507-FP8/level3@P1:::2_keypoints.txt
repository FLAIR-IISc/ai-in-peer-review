- Proposes generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams using a novel Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN).
- CRCN integrates CNNs for images, bidirectional RNNs for sentences, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Motivated by real-world image sequences (e.g., travel blogs) and uses blog posts preprocessed into image-sentence sequence pairs for training.
- Addresses an underexplored problem: sequence-level captioning rather than single-image captioning.
- Explicitly models coherence between sentences, a departure from prior image-sentence pair approaches.
- Provides extensive evaluation (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and human studies showing consistent improvement over strong baselines.
- Quantitative results and retrieval metrics are thorough and convincingly favor CRCN.
- Ablation study shows the coherence component is critical, especially for longer sequences, corroborated by human evaluation.
- Demonstrates practical applicability on two tourism datasets (NYC, Disneyland) with 20k+ blog posts and promises to release code/dataset.
- Incorrectly claims prior work [5] lacks coherence mechanisms; comparison is misleading because [5] models intermediate semantics that promote coherence.
- Inconsistency between Figure 2b and Equation (2) regarding how fully connected layers connect to sentence representations needs correction.
- Questionable use of two sequential linear transformations (Wf1 and Wf2) in Equation (2) since their composition is linear and could be simplified.
- Relies on BLEU as a primary metric despite its limitations for sequence evaluation; METEOR or other measures may be more appropriate with single references.
- Unclear whether paragraph vectors [16] are fine-tuned or kept fixed, which affects understanding of training dynamics.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant contribution with some clarity and comparison issues; authors addressed some concerns in rebuttal and recommendation is to accept.
