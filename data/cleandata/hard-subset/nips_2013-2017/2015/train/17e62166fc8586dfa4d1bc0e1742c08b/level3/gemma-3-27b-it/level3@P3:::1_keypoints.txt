- Addresses multi-sentence captioning for image streams; problem is significant and well-motivated.  
- Novelty is incremental and closely mirrors video and single-image captioning work.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Proposed Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) is clearly articulated and technically sound.  
- Architecture combines CNNs, BRNNs, and a local coherence model to capture discourse relationships.  
- Equation (2) appears incorrect or oversimplified, implying sentence representation depends only on the image and not previous sentences.  
- Clarification needed on how the BRNN hidden state is integrated into the scoring function.  
- Sentence sequence generation (retrieve candidates per image and concatenate) is ad-hoc and primitive; k-NN retrieval limits diversity.  
- More sophisticated generation/ranking (e.g., beam search or nuanced ranking) is recommended.  
- Dataset description is insufficient, lacking crawling/selection details and risking bias.  
- Privacy/anonymity concerns: insufficient explanation of how identifying user information was handled.  
- Important related work (e.g., Rohrbach et al., Xu et al.) and multi-sentence captioning/discourse papers are omitted; claim of being “first” is inaccurate.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear problem statement, sound architecture with discourse modeling, quantitative/qualitative improvements, and strong writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, missing related work, questionable Equation (2), ad-hoc generation method, and dataset/privacy issues.  
- Overall recommendation: acceptable as a poster if related work, dataset details, and equation clarification are addressed.
