- Proposes CRCN to generate coherent sentence sequences from image streams, extending beyond single-image captioning.  
- CRCN integrates CNNs for images, bidirectional RNNs for sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model for smooth sentence transitions.  
- Leverages unstructured blog posts converted into text–image sequence pairs via segmentation and summarization for training.  
- Shows strong quantitative results on NYC and Disneyland datasets using BLEU, CIDEr, and retrieval metrics.  
- Outperforms strong baselines (e.g., CNN+RNN, MLBL-F), with notable gains in retrieval metrics (R@K) per Table 1.  
- Well-motivated and technically sound architecture that meaningfully advances multimodal generation for image streams.  
- Literature review omits important related work, notably Xu et al. “Jointly Modeling Deep Video and Compositional Text” (AAAI 2016).  
- User study (Table 2) shows only modest improvements over RCN and lacks statistical significance testing.  
- Weak user-study gains, especially on shorter streams, conflict with quantitative claims and may overstate the coherence model’s benefit.  
- Training data is domain-limited (tourism blogs), constraining broader applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions—add the missing reference, perform statistical tests on user results, and clarify/strengthen validation of the coherence component.
