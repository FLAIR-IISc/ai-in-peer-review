- Proposes CRCN: integrates CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model for generating descriptions of image sequences.  
- Tackles a more realistic problem than single-image captioning by focusing on photo streams captured during events.  
- Leverages blog posts as scalable, weakly supervised text-image parallel data for training.  
- Strong quantitative results on two large datasets (NYC, Disneyland, 140K images), outperforming baselines substantially in retrieval metrics (e.g., large R@1 gains).  
- Ablation studies show each component contributes, with the coherence model especially important for retrieval performance.  
- Novel and meaningful extension of image captioning to multi-image sequence coherence.  
- Literature review omits relevant video-to-text works (e.g., Xu et al., 2015; Rohrbach et al., 2015) that address similar sequence problems.  
- User study shows only modest preference for CRCN over RCN overall, with clearer advantage only on longer sequences (8–10 images).  
- Raises concern that the coherence model offers limited improvement for shorter, typical user sequences.  
- Lacks statistical significance testing for reported quantitative and user-study improvements, weakening confidence in claims.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and includes a justified blog preprocessing pipeline and sound paragraph-vector text representation.  
- Coherence model description is unclear; how the entity grid improves fluency beyond BRNN is not fully demonstrated.  
- Significance is moderate: technically solid and practical for domains with abundant blog data, but applicability limited where such data are scarce.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and interesting contribution, but would be strengthened by adding missing related work, significance tests, and clearer justification of the coherence model’s practical necessity.
