- Proposes CRCN: combines CNN image representations, BRNN language modeling, and a local coherence model to generate multi-sentence descriptions for image streams.  
- Trained on a large dataset crawled from blog posts and evaluated on NYC and Disneyland streams using BLEU, top-K recall, and AMT user studies.  
- Tackles a relevant extension of image captioning to multi-image, multi-sentence scenarios; motivation and exposition are clear.  
- CRCN architecture is technically sound; coherence model is a key strength and the method outperforms several baselines with supportive user studies.  
- Related work review is insufficient—lacks discussion of closely related video-description and multi-sentence generation literature (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014, Xu et al. 2017) and broader discourse/coherence work.  
- Technical ambiguity in Equation (2): unclear whether sentence-level representation st depends only on BRNN output ot or also directly on CNN feature vector vlt.  
- Sentence sequence generation is primitive—retrieving per-image candidate sentences and concatenating them lacks global optimization for overall narrative coherence (e.g., beam search with coherence penalties).  
- Dataset description is incomplete: crawling methodology, filtering criteria, and reproducibility details are missing.  
- Ethical/data-privacy concerns from reusing Kim et al. (2011) data—no clarification on anonymization or consent.  
- Strengths: clear problem statement, novel coherence-integrated CRCN, strong empirical results, and readable presentation.  
- Weaknesses: incomplete literature review, minor technical clarification needed, simplistic decoding strategy, and dataset/ethical omissions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a challenging multimodal problem, presents a promising CRCN approach, and shows convincing results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak literature contextualization undermines novelty, dataset and ethical issues need resolution, and generation method could be more sophisticated.  
- Overall recommendation: accept as a poster conditional on addressing related-work gaps, clarifying Equation (2), and improving dataset/ethical documentation.
