The paper "Expressing an Image Stream with a Sequence of Natural Sentences" proposes a novel approach for generating natural language descriptions from an image stream. The authors extend the traditional single-image-to-single-sentence generation problem to a more complex and realistic scenario where the input is a sequence of images and the output is a sequence of sentences. This is a significant contribution to the field of computer vision and natural language processing.

The authors' approach, called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), combines the strengths of convolutional neural networks (CNNs), bidirectional recurrent neural networks (RNNs), and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with images, which is a more challenging and realistic setting than the traditional single-image-to-single-sentence generation problem.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, including the architecture of the CRCN model, the training procedure, and the evaluation metrics. The quantitative results reported in the paper are impressive, showing that the proposed method outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines.

However, the user study results (Table 2) show that the method performs similarly to the RCN baseline, indicating only a weak advantage. This raises questions about the significance of the improvements reported in the paper. A statistical significance test is needed to determine whether the reported improvements are reliable.

Furthermore, the literature review is not exhaustive, and several related video-to-sentence works are missing (e.g., "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text..."). This omission may indicate that the authors are not aware of the latest developments in the field or may have overlooked some relevant studies.

Overall, this is a nice and interesting piece of work that contributes to the field of computer vision and natural language processing. However, the authors need to address the limitations mentioned above to strengthen their claims and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of their approach.
