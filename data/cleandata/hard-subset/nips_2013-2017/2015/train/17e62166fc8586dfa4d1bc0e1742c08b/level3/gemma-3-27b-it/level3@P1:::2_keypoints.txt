- Proposes CRCN to generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams by combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and a local coherence model.  
- Motivated by real-world multi-image scenarios and extends single-image captioning to image streams.  
- Trained on a large corpus of 140K images and 20K blog posts to address dataset scarcity.  
- Demonstrates stronger performance than baselines using BLEU, Top-K recall, and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.  
- Novelty: tackles the relatively unexplored problem of coherent descriptions for image streams.  
- Strong empirical results with comprehensive experiments and an ablation study showing the coherence model's importance.  
- Promised code and data release and a well-motivated practical use case.  
- Related work oversight: incorrect claim that [5] lacks coherence modeling; [5] uses intermediate semantics that capture coherence.  
- Clarity issue: Figure 2(b) appears inconsistent with Section 3.3 and Eq. (2) regarding fully connected layers vs. shared per-sentence parameters.  
- Evaluation metric concern: over-reliance on BLEU with single reference; METEOR or other metrics may be more appropriate.  
- Model design question: sequential fully connected layers (Wf2, Wf1) may be redundant; needs ablation to justify.  
- Parameter sharing concern: unclear rationale for sharing FC parameters between BRNN outputs and the coherence model; could limit capacity.  
- Implementation question: clarify whether the paragraph vector is fixed or fine-tuned during training.  
- Requests: clarify Fig.2(b)/Eq.(2) consistency, perform ablation on the FC layer configuration, and justify parameter sharing.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution despite weaknesses; initial recommendation Borderline Accept pending revisions.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed concerns, clarified architecture, justified design choices, and confirmed paragraph vector is not fine-tuned.  
- Final recommendation: Accept — concerns satisfactorily resolved and paper strengthened by revisions.
