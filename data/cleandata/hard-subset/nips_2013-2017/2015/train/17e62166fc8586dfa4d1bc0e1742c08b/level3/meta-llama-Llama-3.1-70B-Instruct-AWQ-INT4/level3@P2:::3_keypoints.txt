- Extends single-image captioning to generating a sequence of sentences from an image stream — a significant, more realistic task.  
- Proposes CRCN: combines CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Trains on a large dataset of blog posts with images, a more challenging setting than single-image datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of architecture, training, and evaluation.  
- Quantitative results claim CRCN outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines.  
- User study (Table 2) shows CRCN performs similarly to the RCN baseline, indicating only a weak advantage.  
- No statistical significance tests reported to verify reliability of the improvements.  
- Literature review is incomplete; several relevant video-to-sentence works are missing.  
- Technically sound overall but empirical claims need stronger validation.  
- Originality: novel task formulation and combination of model components.  
- Significance: potentially important contribution, but impact is questionable without rigorous evaluation.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel approach, architectural novelty, reported strong quantitative gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: weak user-study improvement, missing significance testing, and incomplete related work.
