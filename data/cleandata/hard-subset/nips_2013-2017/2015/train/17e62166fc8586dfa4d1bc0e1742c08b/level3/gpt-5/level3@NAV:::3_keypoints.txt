- Paper proposes CRCN: a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network to express an image stream as a sequence of sentences.
- Architecture couples VGG image features, a BRNN over sentence embeddings for bidirectional discourse, and an entity-grid coherence model to encourage fluent transitions.
- Retrieval-based pipeline: gather candidate sentences from visually similar training images per position and score full sequences with a max-margin objective combining image-text compatibility and coherence.
- Trained on large blog corpora (NYC, Disneyland) and evaluated with BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr, retrieval metrics, and AMT user studies.
- Related-work coverage misses several video-to-text papers (e.g., Xu et al. AAAI’15; Venugopalan et al. CVPR’15; Yao et al. ICCV’15) and should compare discourse vs. coherence modeling.
- Strength: Clear problem formulation for image-stream to multi-sentence description, suitable for NeurIPS.
- Strength: Sensible integration of BRNNs with an entity-based coherence prior, well-motivated for non-continuous streams.
- Strength: Strong quantitative results across multiple metrics and careful engineering of training and approximate search.
- Strength: Useful ablations (1NN, RCN vs. CRCN) and release of a large-scale dataset.
- Weakness: User study shows only a weak advantage over RCN and lacks reported statistical significance tests.
- Weakness: “End-to-end” learning claim is overstated given the retrieval pipeline and heavy preprocessing; needs clarification of what is learned vs. fixed.
- Weakness: Limited sensitivity analysis — missing robustness studies for κ (data augmentation), beam size M, K-nearest candidates, and coherence history length.
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns — should release code/data splits and more training/architectural details (optimizers, BPTT truncation, runtime).
- Quality: Technically sound overall but requires more statistics and error analysis.
- Clarity: Generally well-written; scoring function and training vs. inference pipeline descriptions should be tightened.
- Originality: Novel combination and application to image streams; coherence integration is a useful contribution though components are known.
- Significance: Potentially useful to practitioners, but impact depends on demonstrated human-perceived gains.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on adding statistical significance tests and expanding related work.
