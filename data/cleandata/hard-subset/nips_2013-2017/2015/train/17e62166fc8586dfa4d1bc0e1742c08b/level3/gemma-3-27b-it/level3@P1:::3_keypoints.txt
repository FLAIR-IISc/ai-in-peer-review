- Proposes CRCN to generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences, combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and a local coherence model, framed as a retrieval-plus-refinement task using a blog-derived dataset.

- Properly situates work within image captioning literature and video description, but misses key video-to-sentence papers (e.g., Xu et al. [47]) and needs a more thorough comparison.

- Strength: tackles a challenging, under-explored problem with a well-designed architecture that leverages narrative blog data.

- Strength: Strong quantitative results—CRCN outperforms baselines on BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, and retrieval metrics; ablation study shows the coherence component helps.

- Weakness: User study results are weak and lack statistical significance testing, making claimed improvements uncertain.

- Weakness: AMT/user study methodology is under-specified (instructions, validation procedures not detailed).

- Weakness: Some sections (notably Section 3.4 on training) are dense and need clearer, more intuitive explanation.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, solid architecture, strong empirical performance, clever dataset construction.

- Arguments against acceptance: small, unverified user-study improvements, incomplete related work coverage, and clarity issues.

- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.

- Required revisions: add statistical significance testing for user studies; expand related work to include missing video-to-sentence papers (e.g., [47]); provide detailed AMT/user study methodology; clarify the training description in Section 3.4.
