This paper proposes a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream, extending both input and output dimensions to a sequence of images and a sequence of sentences. The authors design a multimodal architecture called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear problem statement and solution.

The proposed CRCN model is technically sound and captures discourse relationships using entity-based coherence. However, the equation (2) appears incorrect, suggesting that st depends only on ot, implying a not fully connected network. The method for generating sentence sequences is reasonable but primitive and ad-hoc, retrieving sentence per image and simple concatenation.

The paper lacks sufficient description of the dataset, which may breach anonymity/confidentiality, particularly with the reuse of blog data from [11] and undisclosed crawling details. Important prior work, such as Rohrbach et al. (2014) and related multi-sentence/video description papers, is not cited.

The experimental results demonstrate the superior performance of the proposed approach compared to other state-of-the-art alternatives, using both quantitative measures and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk. However, the evaluation is limited to a specific domain (tourism), and it is unclear how the approach would perform in other domains.

In conclusion, while the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of video-to-text/multi-sentence generation, it closely mirrors prior video captioning work. The proposed CRCN model is technically sound, but the paper lacks sufficient description of the dataset and prior work. The experimental results are promising, but limited to a specific domain.

Recommendation: Acceptable as a poster paper.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream.
* The proposed CRCN model is technically sound and captures discourse relationships using entity-based coherence.
* The experimental results demonstrate the superior performance of the proposed approach compared to other state-of-the-art alternatives.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper lacks sufficient description of the dataset, which may breach anonymity/confidentiality.
* Important prior work is not cited.
* The method for generating sentence sequences is primitive and ad-hoc.
* The evaluation is limited to a specific domain (tourism).
