- Proposes a novel Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) to generate coherent natural-language descriptions from image streams.  
- CRCN combines CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with associated images.  
- Evaluation uses quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, retrieval top-K/median rank) and user studies.  
- Quantitative results show CRCN outperforming several state-of-the-art baselines on language and retrieval metrics.  
- User study results show CRCN performs similarly to the RCN baseline, indicating only a weak advantage.  
- Reviewer requests statistical significance testing to confirm whether reported improvements are reliable.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the approach.  
- Literature review is thorough for image-sentence and coherence work but omits several related video-to-sentence papers.  
- Strengths: novel approach, comprehensive literature coverage (mostly), strong quantitative performance.  
- Weaknesses: missing video-to-sentence related work, weak user-study advantage, and lack of statistical significance analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing weaknesses—add missing related work and perform significance tests.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.
