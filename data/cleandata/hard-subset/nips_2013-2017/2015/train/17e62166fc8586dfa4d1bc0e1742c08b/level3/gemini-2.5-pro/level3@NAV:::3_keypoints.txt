- Introduces CRCN to generate sequences of sentences from image streams, combining CNN, bidirectional RNN, and an entity-based coherence model.  
- Focuses on a novel, practical problem distinct from single-image captioning and video description due to potentially disjointed image streams.  
- CRCN architecture is sensible and thoughtfully integrates strong components.  
- Quantitative results (Table 1) show CRCN outperforms several strong baselines across multiple metrics.  
- Literature review is incomplete; missing related video-to-text works (e.g., Xu et al., AAAI 2015).  
- Coherence model validation is weak: user study (Table 2) shows CRCN performs similarly to the RCN baseline, implying limited human-perceived benefit.  
- The claim that coherence gains increase for longer sequences is interesting but requires more thorough validation.  
- Missing statistical significance tests, so reported improvements may not be reliable.  
- Overall a valuable contribution, but the novel coherence component needs more rigorous analysis to support the claims.
