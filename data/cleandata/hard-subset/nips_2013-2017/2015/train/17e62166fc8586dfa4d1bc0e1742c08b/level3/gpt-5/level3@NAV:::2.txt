This paper tackles the problem of narrating an image stream by retrieving coherent sentences from training blogs. Given a test sequence, the method retrieves K=5 nearest image–sentence pairs per image, embeds candidate sentences (doc2vec), models the sentence sequence with a bidirectional RNN over sentence vectors, and adds an entity-grid-based local coherence model. Outputs from the BRNN and coherence module are passed through ReLU and shared fully connected layers to produce per-sentence and global coherence vectors that are scored against CNN image features with a max-margin objective. The approach is evaluated on large blog datasets (NYC, Disneyland) with quantitative metrics and AMT user studies, including an ablation without the coherence module.

Quality: The architecture is technically sound and well-motivated: coupling content compatibility with an explicit local coherence signal is appropriate for image streams where visual content can change abruptly. The experiments are extensive (automatic metrics, retrieval measures, human evals), and the ablation supports the claimed contribution of the coherence model, particularly for longer sequences. Two issues remain: (1) Figure 2b vs. Eq. (2) inconsistency—Figure suggests FC layers connect to all sentences jointly, whereas Eq. (2) implies a shared mapping applied per-sentence plus q; please reconcile. (2) Eq. (2) uses two linear layers (Wf2, Wf1). Because their composition is linear, an ablation is needed to justify the extra layer beyond the stated role of dropout. Also, why share FC parameters between BRNN outputs ot and coherence vector q? A brief justification or comparison would help.

Clarity: The paper is generally clear, but please clarify whether the paragraph vectors are fixed or fine-tuned during training.

Originality: The explicit integration of entity-grid local coherence with a multimodal sequence scoring model for image streams is novel. However, the claim that [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is inaccurate; LRCN’s temporal semantic modeling can yield coherent outputs via its intermediate representation, even if not entity-based. Please revise.

Significance: Results are strong across metrics and humans, and code/dataset release increases impact. Given the scarcity of benchmarks for image-stream narration, this work is likely to be influential.

Evaluation metric: With single references, METEOR typically correlates better than BLEU; emphasize METEOR (and CIDEr) in the main analysis.

Pros:
- Clear, novel architecture; strong empirical gains; meaningful ablation
- Human studies; large-scale data; promised release

Cons:
- Minor technical clarity issues (Fig. 2b/Eq. 2, linear layers, parameter sharing)
- Overstated related-work claim about [5]

Post-rebuttal decision: Accept. Please incorporate the promised clarifications/edits and explicitly address the paragraph-vector fine-tuning detail.
