- Task: generates a sequence of sentences describing an image stream — a novel extension of single-image captioning.  
- Model: proposes CRCN combining CNN visual features, a BRNN for narrative flow, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Data: trained end-to-end on a large blog-post dataset pairing image streams with text sequences.  
- Evaluation: compared to adapted single-sentence baselines using BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, R@K and an Amazon Mechanical Turk user study.  

Strengths:
- Novel/significant problem formulation with clear applications (photo albums, storytelling).  
- Technically sound CRCN design that explicitly models referential coherence in addition to sequence dependencies.  
- Quantitative results show CRCN/RCN outperform adapted baselines across language and retrieval metrics.  

Weaknesses:
- Related work is incomplete; lacks deeper comparison to video-to-text/paragraph description literature.  
- Empirical support for the entity-based coherence model is weak: AMT results show only marginal preference over the RCN baseline, especially for short sequences.  
- Language-metric improvements of CRCN over RCN are small; gains are mainly in retrieval metrics.  
- No statistical significance testing reported to validate the claimed improvements.  

For/Against acceptance (summary):
- For: novel task, well-motivated architecture, strong quantitative performance on a real dataset.  
- Against: coherence-model contribution not convincingly validated, missing significance tests, and incomplete related-work discussion.  

Key revision suggestion:
- Add statistical significance tests and a more thorough analysis of the coherence model’s impact and comparisons to video-to-text work.
