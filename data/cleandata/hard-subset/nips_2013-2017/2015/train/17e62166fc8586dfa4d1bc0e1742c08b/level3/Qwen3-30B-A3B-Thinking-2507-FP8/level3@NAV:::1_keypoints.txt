- Proposes CRCN: integrates CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based coherence model to generate multi-sentence descriptions for image streams.
- Trains on blog posts by extracting text-image parallel sequences using text segmentation and summarization.
- Demonstrates technical soundness and competitive results (BLEU, top-K recall, user studies).
- Presentation is clear and well organized with effective figures.
- Originality is limited — problem and approach closely parallel prior video captioning work (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014) which is not properly cited.
- Dataset description is insufficient: reuses blog data from [11] without disclosing crawling/collection details, raising anonymity and reproducibility concerns.
- Equation (2) appears implausible, implying st depends only on ot and suggesting a not-fully-connected network or an error.
- Sentence generation is primitive, relying on K-nearest neighbor retrieval and concatenation rather than a learned sequence generation method.
- Evaluation lacks depth despite strong quantitative numbers; methodological shortcomings and limited contextualization weaken the contribution.
- Contribution is marginal — a modest extension/implementation of existing concepts rather than a significant advance.
- Recommendation: accept as a poster paper for clear presentation and reasonable results, but not suitable for spotlight/oral due to limited innovation.
