- Proposes a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream.  
- Introduces CRCN: combines CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Extends traditional image captioning from single image/sentence to image streams/multi-sentence outputs.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow with a clear problem statement and solution.  
- CRCN is technically sound and effectively models discourse relationships via entity-based coherence.  
- Evaluation is thorough, including quantitative metrics and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.  
- Fails to cite important prior work on video-to-text and multi-sentence generation (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014).  
- Sentence-sequence generation is primitive and ad-hoc: retrieves one sentence per image and concatenates them.  
- Dataset description is insufficient; reusing blog data and lack of crawling details raise anonymity/privacy and ownership concerns.  
- Potential technical error: Equation (2) implies st depends only on ot (not fully connected), which needs clarification.  
- Overall contribution is significant but impact may be limited by the simplistic sentence-sequencing method.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept as a poster conditional on addressing missing citations, improving sequence-generation, and clarifying dataset details.
