- Tackles the novel task of generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences, extending single-image captioning to multi-image streams.  
- Proposes the Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs for images, BRNNs for sentences, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Evaluated on a large blog-post dataset, showing improvements over baselines via BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr and user studies.  
- Novel and timely problem with practical applications (e.g., travelogues, storytelling).  
- Innovative model design integrating CNNs, BRNNs, and a local coherence component; creative use of blog posts as training data.  
- Comprehensive evaluation including automatic metrics and human AMT studies.  
- Commendable reproducibility commitment to release code and dataset.  
- Incorrectly claims prior work [5] does not model coherence despite [5] using topic-based semantic coherence.  
- Inconsistency between Figure 2b and Equation (2) about fully connected layer connections that can confuse readers.  
- Overreliance on BLEU for single-reference sentences; METEOR should be emphasized as more appropriate.  
- Lacks ablation study to justify the use of two linear functions in Equation (2).  
- Unclear rationale for parameter sharing between BRNN output and the local coherence model.  
- Unspecified whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or fixed, affecting reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on addressing related-work inaccuracy, clarifying model descriptions and design choices, adding ablations, and emphasizing METEOR.
