## Review of “Expressing an Image Stream with a Sequence of Natural Sentences”

This paper tackles the problem of generating coherent sentence sequences to describe a stream of images, extending beyond the typical single image-single sentence pairing. The authors propose a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) integrating CNNs for image representation, BRNNs for sequence modeling, and a local coherence model. The core idea of explicitly modeling coherence within the generated sequence is a logical and valuable extension to existing image captioning work. 

The paper is strong in its comprehensive evaluation. Extensive quantitative results (BLEU, Top-K recall), coupled with insightful user studies via AMT, convincingly demonstrate the superiority of CRCN over baselines, including a critical ablation study removing the coherence model. The promise to release both code and the dataset is a significant contribution to the community, fostering reproducibility and further research. 

However, several points require attention. The claim that [5] lacks a coherence mechanism is inaccurate; its intermediate semantic representation inherently promotes coherence. This oversight demonstrates a lack of thorough engagement with related work. Further, Figure 2b is inconsistent with the mathematical formulation in Section 3.3/Eq. (2). The figure suggests fully connected layers applied to *all* sentence representations, whereas the equation describes shared parameters applied *per* sentence. This discrepancy needs clarification.

Regarding model design, the sequential application of two linear functions (W_f2, W_f1) in Eq. (2) seems redundant, as their composition is simply another linear function. The authors should justify this design choice, ideally with an ablation study exploring the impact of collapsing them into a single layer. Similarly, the parameter sharing between the BRNN outputs and the local coherence model (q) in Eq. (2) is unclear and could benefit from justification. Finally, the paper should explicitly state whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned during training or remains fixed.

While BLEU is reported, given the single reference, METEOR would likely offer a more robust and informative automatic evaluation metric.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to a challenging problem (sequence-to-sequence image description).
*   Strong empirical validation through extensive quantitative and qualitative evaluations.
*   Availability of code and dataset.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Inaccurate claim regarding related work ([5]).
*   Inconsistency between figure and equation (Fig. 2b vs. Eq. 2).
*   Unjustified model design choices (sequential linear functions, parameter sharing).



**Post-Rebuttal Recommendation:** Acceptance, pending the authors address all the aforementioned points, particularly clarifying the parameter-sharing and linear function choices, and explicitly stating whether the paragraph vector is fine-tuned. The authors have addressed the error in related work.
