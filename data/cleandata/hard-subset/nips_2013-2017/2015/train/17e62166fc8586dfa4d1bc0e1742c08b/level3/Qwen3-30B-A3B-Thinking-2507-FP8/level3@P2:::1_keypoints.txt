- Proposes CRCN to generate multi-sentence descriptions for image streams, integrating CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based coherence model.
- Extends both input (image stream) and output (sentence sequence) dimensions, filling a gap beyond single-image captioning.
- Motivation is sound and well-articulated: users often capture event streams and blog posts provide natural image-text sequences for training.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized.
- Architecture and coherence model are technically reasonable and thoughtfully designed.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and includes AMT user studies showing improvements over baselines.
- Novelty is overstated; closely related video-captioning work (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014) is not cited.
- Equation (2) appears incorrect or implausible (st seemingly depends only on ot), raising technical concerns.
- Sentence-sequence generation is primitive/ad-hoc (retrieve-and-concatenate one sentence per image) rather than elegant end-to-end generation.
- Dataset description and data-collection methodology are insufficient and vague, with possible anonymity/confidentiality or permission issues.
- Contribution is modest and incremental; results are promising but not sufficiently differentiated from prior work.
- Recommendation: suitable for poster presentation but not main conference until novelty, equation, generation method, and dataset issues are addressed.
