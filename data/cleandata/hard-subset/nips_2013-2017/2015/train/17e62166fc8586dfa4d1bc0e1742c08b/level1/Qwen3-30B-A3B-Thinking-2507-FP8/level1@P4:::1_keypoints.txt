- Problem: Extends single-image captioning to generating sentence sequences for image streams (image stream → sentence sequence).  
- Method: Proposes CRCN, combining CNN image features, BRNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Data: Trains on blog posts (NYC and Disneyland datasets: ~20K posts, 140K images) as naturally aligned image-text sequences.  
- Evaluation: Uses BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.  
- Contributions: (1) novel problem formulation, (2) CRCN architecture, (3) superior performance on large-scale blog datasets.  
- Strength: Well-motivated task reflecting real user photo streams and integrating existing techniques in a novel multimodal way.  
- Strength: Clever use of user-generated blog posts to obtain large-scale image-text sequence data without manual annotation.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments and ablation study showing BRNN and coherence model improve performance, especially for longer sequences.  
- Weakness: Insufficient evidence that blog posts provide high-quality alignment between images and associated text.  
- Weakness: Coherence model is a relatively simple extension of prior discourse work and its unique contribution vs BRNN is not clearly quantified.  
- Weakness: User study only evaluates short sequences (5 images), conflicting with the paper’s motivation for longer sequences.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks discussion of failure cases or broader limitations.  
- Technical soundness: Mathematically clear BRNN and coherence formulations, appropriate max-margin training, and reproducible implementation details.  
- Novelty: Credibly first to address image-stream → sentence-sequence, and notable for explicitly integrating coherence into multimodal networks.  
- Significance: Practically relevant and broadly applicable; shows that modeling sequence coherence improves multimodal representation for longer passages.  
- Verdict: Solid, well-written contribution that meets quality/clarity/originality/significance criteria and should be accepted despite noted weaknesses.
