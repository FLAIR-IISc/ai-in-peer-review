- Proposes CRCN: integrates CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based coherence model to generate sentence sequences for image streams.  
- Motivates sequence-level description from real user behavior where multiple photos capture events better than isolated captions.  
- Trains on blog posts (NYC and Disneyland datasets; 20K+ posts, 140K images) to leverage natural image-text storytelling.  
- Evaluates with BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall, and AMT user studies, including varying sequence lengths (5 vs 8–10 images).  
- Strength: well-motivated problem and an innovative, practical blog-based dataset collection without manual annotation.  
- Strength: CRCN architecture coherently combines components and experiments include both quantitative metrics and human evaluation.  
- Strength: User studies effectively show the importance of coherence for longer image sequences.  
- Weakness: Novelty is overstated — multi-sentence/multi-image captioning has prior work (e.g., Kuznetsova 2014, Rohrbach 2015) that is not adequately cited or contrasted.  
- Weakness: Coherence model choice (entity-based transitions) is not sufficiently justified versus alternatives like lexical chains or topic models.  
- Weakness: Blog dataset may be biased by stylistic conventions of blogging, limiting generality to other domains.  
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons are unfair or mismatched (e.g., concatenated single-sentence models, weak image features like GIST vs VGGNet).  
- Weakness: Missing discussion of computational complexity and scalability to longer image streams.  
- Quality: Technical approach is sound and reproducible, but lacks detailed limitations and comprehensive ablation (beyond RCN vs CRCN).  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides sufficient technical detail and figures.  
- Originality: Integrating coherence into image-to-sequence generation is novel, but the overarching multi-sentence idea is not entirely new.  
- Significance: Relevant for social media and photo management, but impact limited by domain-specific dataset and lack of broader evaluation.  
- For acceptance: Strong motivation, innovative dataset, thorough evaluation, and demonstrated value of coherence for longer sequences.  
- Against acceptance: Overstated novelty, unfair baseline comparisons, and inadequate treatment of related work reduce credibility.
