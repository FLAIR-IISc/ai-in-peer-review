- Problem: Extends image captioning from single images to image streams, generating multi-sentence descriptions.  
- Method: Proposes CRCN combining CNN image features, BRNN language modeling, and a local coherence model.  
- Data: Leverages blog posts as a practical, large-scale but noisy text–image stream corpus.  
- Motivation: Well-motivated extension of captioning to sequential data given prevalence of image streams.  
- Architecture: CRCN is reasonable and technically sound, building on established CNN/RNN techniques with a coherence component.  
- Coherence model: Inclusion of a local coherence model is a key contribution for multi-sentence fluency.  
- Experiments: Quantitative (BLEU, Recall), qualitative results, AMT user studies, and ablation comparisons show clear improvements over baselines.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and figures effectively illustrate architecture and data flow.  
- Weakness — Coherence detail: Explanation of how the 64-D coherence representation affects the final score (Eq. 3) is insufficiently detailed.  
- Weakness — Preprocessing: LSA-based text summarization may cause information loss and bias; impact not fully explored or compared to alternatives.  
- Weakness — Baselines: Baselines are somewhat dated; stronger, recent seq-to-seq models (e.g., transformers) should be included.  
- Weakness — Error analysis: Limited analysis of failure modes prevents understanding whether errors stem from images, coherence modeling, or data noise.  
- Weakness — Scalability: Impact of approximate search/beam search on performance and trade-offs between efficiency and accuracy is not deeply analyzed.  
- Accept arguments: Novel and relevant problem, sound CRCN design, convincing experimental gains, creative dataset choice, and clear presentation.  
- Reject arguments: Insufficient coherence-model detail, preprocessing biases, weak baseline comparisons, and limited error analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify coherence implementation, address summarization bias, add stronger baselines, and expand error analysis.
