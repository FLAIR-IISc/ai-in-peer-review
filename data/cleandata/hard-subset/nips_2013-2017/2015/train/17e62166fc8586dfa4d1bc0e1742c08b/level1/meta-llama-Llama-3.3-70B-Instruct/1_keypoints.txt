- Proposes a new task: retrieve/generate a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream (multi-image, multi-sentence) rather than single-image captioning.
- Introduces Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), a multimodal architecture combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Trains CRCN on a large dataset of blog posts with associated images (tourism-focused data).
- Evaluates with quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and qualitative user studies on Amazon Mechanical Turk.
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation of approach, related work, and experiments.
- CRCN model design is technically sound and components are well-motivated and explained.
- Experimental results show CRCN outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines in both quantitative and qualitative evaluations.
- Strength: novel and challenging task that expands prior work.
- Strength: careful technical design and motivation of model components.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation supporting claims.
- Weakness: CRCN is complex and requires large amounts of paired image–text data to train.
- Weakness: high computational cost, potentially limiting real-time applicability.
- Weakness: dataset and experiments focus on tourism topics, raising concerns about generalizability to other domains.
- Arguments for acceptance mirror strengths: novelty, solid model design, strong empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance mirror weaknesses: model complexity and data requirements, computational expense, and unclear cross-domain generalization.
- Overall: promising and well-executed work with potential impact, but needs revisions/addressing of complexity, cost, and generalizability concerns.
