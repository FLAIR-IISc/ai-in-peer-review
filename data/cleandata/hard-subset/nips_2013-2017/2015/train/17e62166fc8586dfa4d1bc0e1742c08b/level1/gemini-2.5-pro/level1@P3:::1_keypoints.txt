- Problem: Introduces the novel task of generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for a stream of images, extending single-image captioning.

- Method: Proposes CRCN, a retrieval-based pipeline that retrieves candidate sentences per image and ranks sentence sequences to produce a coherent paragraph.

- Model components: Uses VGGNet for image representation, a Bidirectional RNN over Paragraph Vectors to model content flow, and an entity-based local coherence model to enforce referential/grammatical transitions.

- Training/data: Trains end-to-end with a max-margin loss on a large dataset extracted from blog posts.

- Novelty: Combines a classic NLP coherence model with modern deep sequence models to handle visual discontinuities in image streams.

- Significance: Addresses an important, well-motivated problem with practical applications (e.g., photo album narration).

- Technical quality: Sound model design integrating semantic flow and structural coherence; appropriate structured loss for ranking.

- Evaluation: Thorough experimental validation with a new large-scale dataset, strong baselines, ablation study (CRCN vs RCN), and AMT human evaluation.

- Weakness — retrieval limitation: Retrieval-based formulation ensures fluent sentences but prevents generating novel, image-specific descriptions.

- Weakness — inference approximation: Search is exponential (K^N); uses divide-and-conquer beam search but lacks analysis of beam-size sensitivity or approximation quality.

- Weakness — data preprocessing noise: Heuristic text-image alignment and LSA summarization may introduce noisy training pairs; robustness not assessed.

- Weakness — clarity: The combination of BRNN outputs and the coherence vector is described compactly and unclearly; needs a clearer walkthrough or diagram.

- Acceptance arguments: Novel task, clever model combining coherence and deep learning, extensive evaluation, and clear contribution.

- Rejection arguments: Retrieval constraints, unanalysed approximate inference, and potentially noisy preprocessing are notable limitations.

- Recommendation: Overall high-quality work with strengths outweighing weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
