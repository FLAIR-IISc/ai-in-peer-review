- Proposes CRCN to generate coherent sequences of sentences for image streams, extending single-image captioning.
- CRCN integrates CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Motivated by real-world image sequences (e.g., travel) where single-image captions are insufficient.
- Trains on blog posts, extracting image-sentence pairs via a preprocessing pipeline.
- Builds on prior image/video captioning work but focuses on image streams with abrupt visual changes.
- Technical implementation is sound with clear architecture descriptions and training details.
- Uses appropriate evaluation metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and AMT user studies.
- Ablation studies demonstrate each component’s contribution, especially the coherence model.
- Results show CRCN improves retrieval and user preference, particularly for longer sequences.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and includes illustrative figures and examples.
- Novel contribution in extending captioning to image streams and introducing a coherence component.
- Practical advantage in using unstructured blog posts for (unsupervised) training data.
- Limited discussion of blog dataset biases and potential lack of generalization across domains.
- Coherence model implementation (entity grid, 64 transition types) needs clearer explanation.
- Language metric gains (BLEU, CIDEr) are modest, suggesting impact may be more on ranking/coherence than sentence quality.
- Core building blocks (CNNs, BRNNs) are standard; novelty lies mainly in their integration with the coherence model.
- Could better discuss computational complexity and concrete practical applications/integration scenarios.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed contribution with convincing results; recommendation to accept.
