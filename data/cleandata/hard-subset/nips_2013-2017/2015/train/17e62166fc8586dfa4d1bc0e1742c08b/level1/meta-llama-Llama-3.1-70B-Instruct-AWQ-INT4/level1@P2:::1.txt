**Summary**

This paper proposes an approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream. The authors extend the traditional single-image-to-single-sentence generation problem to a sequence-of-images-to-sequence-of-sentences problem. They introduce a multimodal architecture called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with images, and the authors demonstrate its superior performance compared to other state-of-the-art methods using quantitative measures and user studies.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel problem setup**: The authors address a new and challenging problem of generating sentence sequences for image streams, which is a natural extension of the traditional single-image-to-single-sentence generation problem.
2. **Multimodal architecture**: The proposed CRCN architecture is well-designed, integrating multiple components to capture the relationships between images and sentences.
3. **Large-scale dataset**: The authors collect a large dataset of blog posts with images, which is essential for training and evaluating the CRCN model.
4. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The authors conduct a thorough evaluation of their approach using both quantitative measures and user studies.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity of the model**: The CRCN architecture is complex, with multiple components and hyperparameters to tune. This may make it challenging to train and optimize the model.
2. **Limited interpretability**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the learned representations and the decision-making process of the CRCN model.
3. **Dependence on pre-trained models**: The authors rely on pre-trained models, such as VGGNet, which may not be optimal for the specific task at hand.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel approach to generating sentence sequences for image streams, which is a significant extension of the traditional single-image-to-single-sentence generation problem. The authors also introduce a new multimodal architecture, CRCN, which integrates multiple components to capture the relationships between images and sentences.

**Significance**

The paper has significant implications for various applications, such as image captioning, video description, and human-computer interaction. The proposed approach can be used to generate more natural and coherent descriptions of image streams, which can improve the user experience in various applications.

**Quality**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise description of their approach. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the results. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the learned representations and the decision-making process of the CRCN model.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise description of their approach. The figures and tables are well-designed and help to illustrate the key concepts and results.
