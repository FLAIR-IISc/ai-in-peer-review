Strengths:
- Novelty and scope: Extends image captioning to image streams and integrates coherence modeling, filling an underexplored gap.
- Technical soundness: CRCN combines CNNs, BRNNs, and an entity-based coherence model, trained creatively on blog post data.
- Comprehensive evaluation: Uses quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, Top-K) and AMT user studies, showing superior performance to baselines.
- Practical relevance: Useful for travelogue generation, digital storytelling, and visual content summarization.

Weaknesses:
- Dataset limitations: Training and evaluation confined to tourism topics (NYC, Disneyland), limiting demonstrated generalizability.
- Scalability concerns: Divide-and-conquer sentence sequence retrieval may not scale to longer image streams or much larger datasets.
- Evaluation metrics: Relies on standard language metrics with known coherence limitations; coherence-specific automatic metrics are lacking.
- Clarity: Paper is dense with technical detail and could benefit from a more concise, accessible explanation of the architecture and training.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Tackles a novel, important problem with a well-designed, technically sound approach.
- Integration of coherence modeling into multimodal networks is innovative and advances the state of the art.
- Thorough experiments and user studies provide convincing evidence of improvement.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Unclear generalizability to other domains/datasets beyond tourism.
- Scalability of the retrieval mechanism for longer sequences is not demonstrated.

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance: significant and well-supported contribution whose strengths outweigh the noted limitations.
