- Proposes CRCN: a multimodal model (CNNs + BRNNs + entity-based coherence) to generate sentence sequences for image streams, trained on blog posts and evaluated on NYC/Disneyland datasets.
- Extends captioning from single-image single-sentence to multi-image multi-sentence generation, addressing cross-sentence coherence.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains over baselines on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR/top-K recall and in Amazon Mechanical Turk human studies.
- Technical approach is sound and reproducible, with well-documented training, optimization, and informative ablation studies.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with effective illustrations and rigorous methodological exposition.
- Original contribution lies in integrating coherence modeling with multimodal networks and using blog posts as large-scale paired data.
- Significant practical impact for photo albums, travel blogging, and social media, with domain-independent and unsupervised advantages.
- Strengths: novel problem formulation, coherence integration, practical data source, comprehensive quantitative and human evaluations, strong ablations, clear presentation.
- Weaknesses: unclear scalability to long image streams, coherence model limited to entity transitions (may miss thematic/emotional coherence), user studies restricted to 5 images, no concrete failure-case examples, potential domain bias in blog data.
- Recommendation: Accept for NIPS — significant, well-executed contribution with minor limitations.
