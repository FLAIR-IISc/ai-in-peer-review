- Summary: Proposes CRCN to map image streams to sentence sequences, trained on a large blog-image dataset and shown to outperform baselines in metrics and user studies.

- Novel problem setup: Extends single-image captioning to the sequence-of-images → sequence-of-sentences task.

- Multimodal architecture: CRCN combines CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.

- Large-scale dataset: Authors collect and use a substantial blog post image–text dataset for training and evaluation.

- Comprehensive evaluation: Uses quantitative measures and user studies to validate performance.

- Model complexity: CRCN is complex with many components and hyperparameters, making training and optimization challenging.

- Limited interpretability: Paper lacks detailed analysis of learned representations and model decision processes.

- Dependence on pre-trained models: Relies on pre-trained networks (e.g., VGGNet) that may not be optimal for this specific task.

- Originality: Novel extension of image captioning and a new multimodal CRCN architecture.

- Significance: Potential impact on image captioning, video description, and HCI by producing more coherent multi-image descriptions.

- Quality: Well-written and experimentally thorough, but would benefit from deeper analysis of internal representations.

- Clarity: Well-organized presentation with helpful figures and tables.

- Pro acceptance: Novel task, new CRCN architecture, and strong empirical/user-study results.

- Con acceptance: High model complexity, reliance on pre-trained models, and insufficient interpretability analysis.

- Recommendation: Accept, with suggestions to address model complexity and interpretability in future work.
