**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream. The authors extend the traditional image-to-sentence generation problem to a sequence-to-sequence problem, where the input is a stream of images and the output is a sequence of sentences. They introduce a multimodal architecture called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks for image representation, bidirectional recurrent neural networks for sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model for fluent transitions of sentences. The authors evaluate their approach using large datasets of unstructured blog posts and demonstrate its superior performance compared to state-of-the-art alternatives.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel Problem Formulation**: The paper addresses a new problem of generating sentence sequences for image streams, which is a natural extension of the traditional image-to-sentence generation problem.
2. **Well-Designed Architecture**: The proposed CRCN architecture is well-designed, and the authors provide a clear explanation of each component and how they interact with each other.
3. **Strong Experimental Results**: The authors provide comprehensive experimental results, including quantitative evaluations and user studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.
4. **Good Use of Related Work**: The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain how their approach differs from previous methods.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Complexity of the Model**: The proposed CRCN architecture is complex, and the authors could provide more details on how to implement and train the model.
2. **Limited Discussion of Limitations**: The authors could provide a more detailed discussion of the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research.
3. **Some Sections are Overly Technical**: Some sections of the paper, such as the description of the BRNN model, may be difficult for non-experts to follow.

**Arguments for Acceptance**

1. **Originality and Impact**: The paper addresses a new problem and proposes a novel approach that has the potential to impact the field of computer vision and natural language processing.
2. **Technical Soundness**: The paper provides a thorough description of the proposed architecture and experimental results, demonstrating the technical soundness of the approach.
3. **Clarity and Organization**: The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and results.

**Arguments against Acceptance**

1. **Complexity of the Model**: The proposed architecture may be too complex for some readers to follow, which could limit the paper's impact.
2. **Limited Discussion of Limitations**: The authors could provide a more detailed discussion of the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research.
