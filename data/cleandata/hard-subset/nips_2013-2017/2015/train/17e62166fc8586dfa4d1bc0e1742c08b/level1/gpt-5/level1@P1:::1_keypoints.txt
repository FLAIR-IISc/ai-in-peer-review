- Task: Generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams (image-to-paragraph retrieval).

- Model: Proposes CRCN combining VGG fc7 image features, a sentence-level bidirectional RNN over doc2vec paragraph vectors, and an entity-grid coherence model.

- Generation: Retrieval-based—selects per-image candidate sentences from nearest-neighbor training images and scores sequences with a learned compatibility function and approximate beam-style search.

- Training/Data: Trains with max-margin ranking loss on weakly aligned blog image–text pairs mined from large corpora (NYC & Disneyland datasets).

- Evaluation: Uses BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics (R@K, median rank), and AMT user studies; coherence gains stronger for longer sequences.

- Strength: Addresses an important, underexplored task beyond single-image captioning and video captioning.

- Strength: Novel integration of sentence-level BRNN with an explicit entity-grid coherence model, showing empirical benefits over ablations.

- Strength: Practical, scalable pipeline using weakly supervised blog data without manual sentence annotations.

- Strength: Multi-faceted evaluation including human preferences supports the paper’s claims.

- Weakness: Scoring function ties a single sequence-level coherence vector g to image features via dot products, potentially introducing length bias and spurious interactions.

- Weakness: Retrieval-only approach cannot generate novel sentences, limiting expressivity and possibly biasing results.

- Weakness: Preprocessing/alignment (index-distance heuristics, LSA summarization, doc2vec) is noisy and unvalidated; risks affecting training and evaluation.

- Weakness: Baselines are limited—missing stronger retrieval and sequence models (hierarchical RNNs, LSTM/GRU BRNNs, sequence-level retrieval with smoothness).

- Weakness: Key implementation details and search suboptimality/sensitivity analyses are deferred or missing; code/data release not discussed.

- Weakness: Claim of being “unsupervised” is overstated given reliance on weak supervision and supervised NLP components.

- Quality: Technically sound overall but needs ablations to verify scoring/length effects aren’t driving gains.

- Clarity: Well organized, but the score function, length effects, and mixing of g with image features need clearer explanation and main-text details.

- Originality: Novel application of entity-grid coherence to multimodal sequence selection; components are known but integration is new.

- Significance: Practically and scientifically relevant step toward coherent multi-sentence multimodal descriptions; impact would increase with stronger baselines and domains.

- For acceptance: Tackles a meaningful task, introduces explicit coherence modeling, and leverages large-scale weak supervision with positive empirical and human-evaluation results.

- Against acceptance: Conceptual issues in scoring function, limited sequence-level baselines, preprocessing/validation and reproducibility concerns.

- Key suggestions: Normalize scores by sequence length and/or separate coherence scoring; ablate the coherence term.

- Key suggestions: Replace/augment BRNN with LSTM/GRU or hierarchical paragraph RNNs and compare to stronger retrieval baselines enforcing temporal smoothness.

- Key suggestions: Provide quantitative validation of segmentation/summarization, error analyses, sensitivity of search (K/M), and release data/code.
