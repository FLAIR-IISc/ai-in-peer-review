- Problem: Extends image captioning from single images to sequences, generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams.  
- Method: Proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN image features, BRNN language modeling, and a local coherence model.  
- Training/eval: Trained and evaluated on blog post data (NYC, Disneyland) using BLEU, Top-K recall, and AMT user studies.  
- Strength — Novelty: Natural and valuable extension to sequential image description, addressing realistic multi-image scenarios.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: CRCN is well-motivated, leverages established components, and uses a max-margin ranking loss appropriate for retrieval-based setup.  
- Strength — Coherence: Inclusion of a local coherence model is a key contribution targeting fluency across sentences.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments and user studies show consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Strength — Dataset: Creative use of blog posts with reasonable preprocessing for creating parallel image–text sequences.  
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written with helpful figures explaining architecture and experiments.  
- Weakness — Baselines: Comparisons lack direct competitors for sequential description; many baselines are single-image models concatenated post-hoc.  
- Weakness — Data augmentation: Use of κ=3 is under-justified and lacks sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness — Coherence model: Reliance on parse trees/entity grid adds complexity and its mechanism could be explained more intuitively.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameters: Insufficient analysis of sensitivity to learning rate, dropout, RNN hidden size, etc.  
- Weakness — Error analysis: Lacks detailed qualitative examination of failure cases to reveal limitations.  
- For acceptance: Addresses a novel problem, proposes a sound model, provides strong empirical evidence, and contributes a dataset.  
- Against acceptance: Incomplete baseline comparison and insufficient methodological detail/analysis in some areas.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable contribution with minor shortcomings that do not outweigh merits.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
