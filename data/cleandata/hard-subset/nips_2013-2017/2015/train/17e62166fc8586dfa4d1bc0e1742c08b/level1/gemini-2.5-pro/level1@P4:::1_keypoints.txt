- Novel task: generating a sequence of natural sentences to describe a stream of images, extending single-image captioning to visual narratives.  
- Model (CRCN): retrieval-based multimodal architecture combining VGG image features, a bidirectional RNN for sentence-level context, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Retrieval/composition pipeline: retrieve candidate sentences per image from training set, then select and arrange them to form a paragraph; trained end-to-end with a max-margin loss.  
- Data collection: practical large-scale parallel corpus created by processing blog posts to pair image streams with narrative text.  
- Evaluation: extensive experiments on NYC and Disneyland datasets using automatic metrics (BLEU, CIDEr) and Amazon Mechanical Turk human studies showing CRCN outperforms baselines.  
- Strength: tackles a significant, novel problem moving vision-and-language toward holistic visual storytelling.  
- Strength: well-motivated architecture that explicitly models both semantic content flow and discourse coherence.  
- Strength: innovative, in-the-wild data collection pipeline that supplies a valuable resource for the task.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation and ablation studies convincingly validate model components (BRNN and coherence model).  
- Weakness: retrieval-based design restricts generative capacity and cannot produce novel sentences/compositions unseen in training.  
- Weakness: scoring function (Eq. 3) is not well-justified—unclear why a global coherence vector should be dotted with each local image feature.  
- Weakness: test-time search uses a divide-and-conquer beam search heuristic without analysis of how often it fails to find the global optimum or what errors it introduces.  
- Quality/clarity: paper is high-quality and clearly written, with useful figures; minor clarifications on scoring rationale and retrieval complexity are needed.  
- Originality/significance: first formalization of the task; the CRCN and blog-derived dataset are novel and likely to have substantial impact.
