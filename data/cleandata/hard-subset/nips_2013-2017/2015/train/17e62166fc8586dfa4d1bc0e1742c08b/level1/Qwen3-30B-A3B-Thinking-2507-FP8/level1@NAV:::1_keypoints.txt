- Proposes a new task: generate a sequence of natural-language sentences describing an image stream (instead of single-image captions).  
- Introduces CRCN: integrates CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Leverages blog posts as scalable training data (NYC & Disneyland datasets: ~20K posts, ~140K images).  
- Evaluation uses BLEU, top-K recall, and user studies; includes thorough ablation experiments.  
- Main contribution: extends image captioning to sequence-to-sequence generation with explicit coherence modeling.  
- Positions work relative to prior image/video captioning and targets abrupt transitions in image streams.  
- Strength: technically sound with ablation studies demonstrating the coherence model’s value.  
- Strength: clever, scalable dataset collection from naturally aligned blog text and images.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation; user studies show coherence matters more for longer sequences.  
- Weakness: unclear generalization beyond tourism/blog domains or when text-image alignment is weak.  
- Weakness: AMT evaluations used short (5-image) sequences, possibly underrepresenting coherence gains for longer streams.  
- Weakness: model architecture is complex and not all design choices are fully justified.  
- Quality: High—well-supported experiments and analyses.  
- Clarity: Good—well-organized, though some technical details could be more accessible.  
- Originality: High—addresses a novel and meaningful extension of image captioning.  
- Significance: Moderate—practical relevance limited by unsupervised setup, contribution is mainly methodological.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
