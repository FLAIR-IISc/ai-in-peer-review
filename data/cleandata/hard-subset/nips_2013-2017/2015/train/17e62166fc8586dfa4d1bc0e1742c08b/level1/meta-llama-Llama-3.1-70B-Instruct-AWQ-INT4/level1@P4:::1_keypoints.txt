- Proposes retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream, extending inputs/outputs to image and sentence sequences.  
- Introduces Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Trained on a large dataset of blog posts with images and evaluated with quantitative metrics and user studies.  
- Key novelty: explicit coherence model to capture flow and passage-level coherence in multimodal sequence generation.  
- Positions work relative to image-sentence, multimodal, and video-sentence literature; notes prior sequence-retrieval models don’t generate natural sentences.  
- Strength: novel task formulation and departure from single image–sentence pair approaches.  
- Strength: comprehensive multimodal architecture integrating visual, sequential, and coherence components.  
- Strength: large-scale evaluation including human studies provides thorough performance assessment.  
- Strength: CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art baselines (log-bilinear multimodal and recurrent convolutional models).  
- Weakness: model complexity demands large training data and significant computational resources.  
- Weakness: trained on a specific blog-post dataset, raising concerns about cross-domain generalization.  
- Weakness: some automated metrics used (e.g., BLEU, CIDEr) may not adequately measure sequence coherence and fluency.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address noted weaknesses.  
- Suggested revisions: detail computational/resource requirements, discuss/augment evaluation metrics for coherence, and provide analysis of generalizability to other domains.
