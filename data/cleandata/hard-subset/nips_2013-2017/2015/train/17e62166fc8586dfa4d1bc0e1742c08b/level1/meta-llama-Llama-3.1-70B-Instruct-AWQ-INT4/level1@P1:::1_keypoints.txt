- Problem: retrieve a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream, extending captioning to sequential input/output.  
- Model: proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Dataset: trained on a large-scale, unstructured blog-post dataset (20K posts, 140K images).  
- Evaluation: quantitative metrics (BLEU, Top-K recall) and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk.  
- Organization/clarity: paper is well-organized and clearly explains architecture and training.  
- Empirical results: CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art alternatives in experiments.  

Strengths:
- Novelty: addresses a new, natural extension of image captioning to image streams.  
- Multimodal architecture: integrates visual, sequential, and coherence components for richer modeling.  
- Large-scale realistic data: uses challenging unstructured blog data for training/evaluation.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: combines automatic metrics with human studies.

Weaknesses:
- Model complexity: CRCN is computationally heavy and may not suit small datasets or limited hardware.  
- Reliance on pre-trained models: uses models like VGGNet that may not be optimal for this specific task.  
- Limited interpretability: lacks detailed analysis of learned representations and decision processes.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel and challenging problem formulation with clear contribution.  
- Demonstrated superior empirical performance over baselines.  
- Clear exposition of method and training.

Arguments against acceptance:
- High model complexity may limit practical applicability.  
- Dependence on pre-trained networks could reduce task-specific optimality.  
- Limited analysis/interpretability of model internals.

Overall: strong, novel contribution with convincing results but concerns about complexity, dependence on pretraining, and interpretability.
