- Extends single-image-to-sentence generation to sequence-to-sequence retrieval for image streams, a more realistic and challenging task.
- Proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Trains CRCN on a large dataset of blog posts with images, which is a notable dataset contribution.
- Paper is well-written with clear descriptions of architecture, training, and evaluation metrics.
- Experiments are thorough and show CRCN outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines.
- Strength: Addresses a novel, under-explored task with practical implications.
- Strength: CRCN architecture and integration of coherence modelling is an important innovation.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation including quantitative metrics and user studies.
- Strength: Large blog-image dataset adds value to the community.
- Weakness: CRCN is complex and requires significant computational resources to train.
- Weakness: Approach may have limited generalizability beyond image-stream sentence generation.
- Weakness: Model performance heavily depends on the quality and representativeness of training data.
- For acceptance: Novel task, strong architectural contribution, and comprehensive empirical validation.
- Against acceptance: High complexity may hinder replication, limited applicability, and data-dependence concerns.
- Overall: Significant contribution but needs to address complexity and data-quality issues; Rating 8/10; Recommend accept with minor revisions requesting more training and evaluation details.
