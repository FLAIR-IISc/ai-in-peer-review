- Introduces the novel task of generating coherent sequences of sentences describing a stream of images, moving beyond single-image captioning.  
- Proposes CRCN: a multimodal architecture combining a CNN for images, a bidirectional RNN for sentence sequence flow, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Uses a large new dataset constructed from blog posts for training and evaluation.  
- Technically sound with comprehensive experimental evaluation using multiple automatic metrics (BLEU, R@K, etc.) and an Amazon Mechanical Turk user study.  
- Ablation study (CRCN vs. RCN) demonstrates the concrete contribution of the coherence model.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes helpful diagrams and dataset/experimental details to aid reproducibility.  
- Originality: formulates an important new problem and novel integration of the Barzilay & Lapata coherence model into a deep learning framework.  
- Significance: opens promising research directions in vision-and-language with applications in storytelling and photo-album summarization; shows clear improvement over adapted state-of-the-art.  
- Minor weakness: inference is retrieval-and-ranking based rather than fully generative, limiting production of entirely novel sentences and should be framed as a limitation/future work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a novel, well-motivated problem; proposes an original, effective architecture; presents a thorough, large-scale evaluation demonstrating SOTA performance.  
- Argument against acceptance: reliance on retrieval-based inference narrows the methodological contribution compared to pure generation approaches.  
- Overall: strengths substantially outweigh minor weaknesses; high-quality, compelling contribution likely of strong interest to the community.
