- Tackles underexplored problem of generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams rather than single-image captions.
- Proposes CRCN combining VGG fc7 image features, a bidirectional RNN over sentence embeddings (doc2vec) for content flow, and an entity-grid coherence model.
- Trains with a max-margin retrieval objective on weakly aligned travel-blog image/text pairs, summarized with LSA.
- At test time retrieves per-image candidate sentences via K-NN and assembles sequences with a divide-and-conquer beam search scored by the learned compatibility function.
- Compares to adapted single-image captioning/retrieval baselines and performs ablation studies; evaluation uses BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, and AMT preferences.
- Novelty: extends captioning to image-stream → sentence-sequence and integrates classical entity-grid coherence with neural sequence modeling.
- Architectural strength: principled integration of BRNN content flow and entity-grid coherence learned end-to-end for sequence-level scoring.
- Data approach strength: leverages large weakly aligned web corpora and is in principle domain-agnostic.
- Empirical strength: consistent improvements on retrieval metrics and AMT user preference, especially for longer sequences; ablations attribute gains to coherence component.
- Major weakness: quality of text-to-image alignment and LSA summarization is unvalidated and potentially noisy.
- Evaluation weakness: protocol unclear—how positives/negatives are defined at test time and applicability of BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR for paragraph-level paraphrase is questionable.
- Modeling concern: coherence term dots the same vector with every image feature (counted N times) without adequate justification; alternative formulations may be preferable.
- Baseline concern: comparisons may be weak—stronger sequence-level baselines (LSTM/GRU ranking or CRF pairwise models) are missing.
- Generalization weakness: experiments limited to two tourism datasets; no evidence on other domains like event albums or instructional sets.
- Quality: training objective and optimization are generally sound; beam search plausible but lacks analysis of suboptimality and runtime.
- Metric concerns: paragraph-level BLEU/CIDEr have limited interpretability; AMT lacks reported significance testing and inter-annotator agreement.
- Clarity: paper is mostly well organized with clear descriptions of pipeline and models.
- Clarity issues: ambiguities around evaluation protocol, rationale for coherence-image interaction (Eq.3), and candidate search parameters and cost.
- Minor presentation: some notational reuse and shape-description issues need tightening.
- Originality: novel problem framing and hybrid neural + classical coherence integration; pragmatic retrieval-based paragraph construction distinct from word-level generation.
- Significance: meaningful step toward narrative generation for photo streams; user preference gains suggest practical utility but broader impact limited by evaluation and domain scope.
- Pros for acceptance: novel task and architecture, principled discourse coherence integration, empirical and user-study-backed improvements, scalable training from web data, comprehensive ablations.
- Cons for acceptance: fragile/unclear evaluation, unvalidated alignment/summarization, under-justified coherence-image interaction, missing stronger sequence baselines, limited domain evidence and runtime analysis.
- Suggested improvements: clarify test-time retrieval setup, report AMT statistics and agreement, and provide significance testing.
- Suggested improvements: quantify alignment/summarization accuracy on an annotated subset.
- Suggested improvements: add stronger sequence baselines (LSTM/GRU ranking, CRF pairwise) to isolate entity-grid contribution.
- Suggested improvements: explore alternative sequence-level coherence scoring (not repeated per image), ablate coherence history length, and test sensitivity to K, M, Q and runtime.
- Suggested improvements: demonstrate results on at least one additional domain to support generality.
- Overall assessment: promising and timely contribution with thoughtful discourse integration but requires clearer evaluation and stronger baselines; reviewer leans borderline accept pending revisions.
