- Introduces a novel task: generating coherent paragraph-length descriptions for image streams as an extension of single-image captioning.  
- Proposes the Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN visual features, a bidirectional RNN for paragraph context, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Uses VGGNet for per-image feature extraction and represents sentences via paragraph vectors.  
- Leverages a large corpus of blog posts to create weakly-aligned image–sentence sequence training data.  
- Frames the task as retrieval at test time: construct candidate sentence sequences from training data and rank them with a learned compatibility score.  
- Trains the model with a max-margin loss to prefer ground-truth sequences over mismatches.  
- Evaluates on two new blog datasets (NYC and Disneyland) with comparisons to adapted single-image captioning baselines.  
- Provides thorough ablation studies showing benefits of the BRNN and explicit coherence model, and reports AMT human evaluation favoring CRCN.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated problem likely to inspire follow-up research in multimodal narrative generation.  
- Strength: sound methodology that sensibly integrates classic entity-based coherence into a deep-learning framework.  
- Strength: comprehensive and convincing empirical validation, including human preference studies; coherence benefits grow with sequence length.  
- Strength: resourceful data collection by mining blog posts to address dataset scarcity.  
- Weakness: fundamentally retrieval-based rather than generative, limiting novelty of produced sentences and needing clearer framing.  
- Weakness: inference requires searching a K^N space and uses a heuristic divide-and-conquer beam search whose approximation quality and cost are uncharacterized.  
- Weakness: training data pre-processing (text segmentation and LSA summarization) may introduce noisy alignments that propagate into learning.  
- Weakness: Section 3.3’s description of fusing CNN, RNN, and coherence outputs is dense and needs clearer explanation or a diagram for reproducibility.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel problem, technically sound model combining deep learning and NLP coherence, and strong empirical/human evaluation.  
- Rejection arguments: retrieval-only approach, uncharacterized heuristic inference, and potentially noisy data pipeline.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommended for acceptance due to novelty, solid method, and thorough evaluation.
