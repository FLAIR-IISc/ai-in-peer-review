- Tackles describing an image stream with a sequence of natural sentences and proposes the CRCN multimodal retrieval architecture.  
- CRCN encodes images with VGGNet and represents candidate sentences with paragraph vectors plus a bidirectional RNN for sentence-to-sentence context.  
- Injects discourse coherence via an entity-grid model aggregated over the whole paragraph.  
- Training uses a max-margin objective aligning image and sentence sequences; inference performs approximate beam-style search over sentences from K-nearest images.  
- Constructs a large text–image parallel corpus from blogs (NYC and Disneyland) via paragraph-proximity alignment and LSA summarization.  
- Empirical gains over strong single-image captioning baselines on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and retrieval metrics; AMT users prefer CRCN outputs, especially for longer sequences.  
- Distinct from prior captioning/video-to-text work by explicitly modeling local coherence to improve multi-sentence fluency.  
- Dataset construction is practical but introduces alignment noise; lacks analysis of segmentation/summarization accuracy or robustness.  
- Scoring function Skl duplicates the coherence contribution N times—needs clarification on normalization or justification via ablations.  
- Baselines are mostly single-sentence systems; missing hierarchical/paragraph baselines (e.g., LSTM/GRU paragraph models or simple bigram sentence-level LM) to isolate coherence gains.  
- The “unsupervised” claim is overstated: the method is weakly supervised using mined image–text pairs.  
- Paper is technically sound, generally clear and well-organized; requests include reporting preprocessing errors, κ/K/M sensitivity, runtime, and code/data release plans.  
- Integration of an entity-grid coherence model into multimodal retrieval is novel and timely.  
- If datasets/code are released, the work is likely impactful for storytelling and album summarization.
