- Problem & Contribution: Extends single-image captioning to generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams using a proposed CRCN model.

- Model: CRCN combines CNN image features, BRNN sentence modeling, and a local coherence model to enforce fluency across sentences.

- Data & Training: Trains on blog posts (image-text sequences) to overcome data scarcity for stream-level captioning.

- Evaluation: Tested on NYC and Disneyland datasets with BLEU/top-K recall metrics and AMT user studies showing human preference.

- Novelty: The extension to image streams is a meaningful and under-addressed problem in captioning.

- Technical Soundness: Architecture is well-motivated, integrating established CNN/RNN components with a coherence model.

- Coherence Model Strength: Inclusion of a local coherence/entity-grid component helps produce fluent sentence sequences.

- Dataset Choice Strength: Using blog posts as sequential image-text data is a clever and practical solution.

- Evaluation Strength: Comprehensive quantitative and human evaluations support the method’s effectiveness.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written with helpful figures illustrating architecture and experiments.

- Lack of Video Comparison: Limited comparison to video captioning literature and video-based methods (e.g., 3D CNNs, LSTM video models).

- Coherence Model Detail Missing: The paper provides cursory description of the entity-grid/coherence model and lacks justification for the 64-dim representation.

- Data Augmentation Justification: κ=3 for augmentation is empirically chosen without sensitivity analysis or rationale.

- Retrieval Approximation Concern: Divide-and-conquer search and beam size (M=50) introduce an approximation whose impact on performance isn’t fully analyzed.

- Baseline Limitations: Baselines are mainly single-image methods adapted to sequences; more specialized sequence-generation baselines could strengthen comparisons.

- Recommendation: Overall a significant contribution with convincing results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to detail the coherence model and better connect/differentiate from video captioning.
