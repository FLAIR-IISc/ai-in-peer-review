**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream. The authors extend both input and output dimensions to a sequence of images and a sequence of sentences, which is a departure from previous work that focuses on single image-sentence pairs. They introduce a multimodal architecture called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with images and is evaluated using both quantitative measures and user studies.

**Related Work**

The authors discuss related work in the areas of image-sentence generation, multimodal neural networks, and video-sentence works. They highlight the key novelty of their approach, which is the explicit inclusion of a coherence model to capture the flow of text content and the coherence of passage. They also compare their work with previous studies that have addressed the retrieval of image sequences for a query paragraph, but note that these models are not applicable to natural sentence generation.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream, which is a departure from previous work that focuses on single image-sentence pairs.
2. **Multimodal architecture**: The CRCN model integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model, which is a comprehensive approach to modeling the relationships between images and text.
3. **Large-scale evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach using a large dataset of blog posts with images and both quantitative measures and user studies, which provides a thorough assessment of the model's performance.
4. **Significant improvement**: The results show that the CRCN model outperforms other state-of-the-art candidate methods, including the log-bilinear multimodal models and recurrent convolutional models.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity**: The CRCN model is a complex architecture that requires a large amount of training data and computational resources.
2. **Limited generalizability**: The model is trained on a specific dataset of blog posts with images, and it is unclear how well it will generalize to other domains or datasets.
3. **Evaluation metrics**: The authors use a range of evaluation metrics, but some of these metrics, such as BLEU and CIDEr, may not be well-suited for evaluating the coherence and fluency of sentence sequences.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream, which is a departure from previous work that focuses on single image-sentence pairs. The authors also introduce a new multimodal architecture that integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model.

**Significance**

The paper addresses a significant problem in the field of computer vision and natural language processing, which is the generation of natural language descriptions for image streams. The authors' approach has the potential to be applied in a range of applications, such as image and video captioning, and human-computer interaction.
