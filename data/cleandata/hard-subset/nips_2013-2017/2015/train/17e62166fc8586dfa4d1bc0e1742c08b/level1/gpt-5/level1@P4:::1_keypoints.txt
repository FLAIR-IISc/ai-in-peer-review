- Paper tackles multi-image-to-multi-sentence description, modeling sentence-to-sentence coherence via a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN).
- CRCN combines VGG fc7 image features, BRNN over doc2vec sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid coherence model, trained with a max-margin ranking loss on weakly aligned blog data.
- At test time, candidate sentences are retrieved from K-NN images per position and ranked with an approximate divide-and-conquer beam search.
- Evaluation uses BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr, retrieval metrics, and AMT pairwise preferences, showing CRCN outperforms single-sentence baselines and ablations.
- Scoring function is questionable: the global coherence vector g_k is applied to every time step, tying a text-only signal to visual features and scaling with sequence length.
- No length normalization: scores scale with sequence length, potentially biasing toward longer candidates.
- Search procedure is only sketched; reproducibility needs pseudocode, complexity analysis, and sensitivity to K and beam size M.
- Claiming “completely unsupervised” is misleading; training uses heuristically aligned image–sentence pairs (weak supervision).
- Preprocessing (paragraph-to-image assignment, LSA summarization) is heuristic and noisy; misalignment rates and robustness are unreported.
- Entity-grid implementation choices (transition history length 3, zero-padding to 300 dims) are ad hoc and need ablation.
- Empirical results lack exact numeric values, confidence intervals, and statistical tests (including AMT inter-annotator agreement).
- Releasing blog datasets and code is necessary for reproducibility given departure from standard benchmarks.
- Paper is generally well-organized with clear equations, but needs clarification on g·v term, length normalization, search algorithm, and doc2vec details.
- Doc2vec training specifics, OOV handling, and whether embeddings are fine-tuned are not reported.
- Main novelty is integrating discourse coherence into multimodal sequence-level ranking for image streams, which appears novel for the timeframe.
- Problem is relevant and underexplored; approach could influence visual storytelling research but is limited by retrieval-based generation and domain-specific datasets.
- Strengths: well-motivated problem, sensible BRNN+entity-grid architecture, comprehensive evaluation protocol, and use of large-scale weakly aligned data.
- Weaknesses: potential length bias, unclear global coherence role, insufficient search details, noisy preprocessing, misleading supervision claim, and limited domain generalization.
- Suggested fixes: normalize scores by length; ablate/remove/apply g once; vary coherence history length and κ augmentation; provide search pseudocode and sensitivity analysis.
- Suggested reporting: quantify preprocessing alignment accuracy, report full metrics with confidence intervals and significance tests, detail doc2vec training, and release code/datasets.
- Arguments for acceptance: timely, novel integration of discourse coherence, principled approach, and demonstrated user-preferred improvements.
- Arguments against acceptance: unjustified technical choices (scoring/length bias), reproducibility concerns, and limited evaluation to custom domains.
- Overall: promising and novel work but requires clearer justification of scoring, stronger analysis of preprocessing and search, and more thorough reporting for acceptance.
