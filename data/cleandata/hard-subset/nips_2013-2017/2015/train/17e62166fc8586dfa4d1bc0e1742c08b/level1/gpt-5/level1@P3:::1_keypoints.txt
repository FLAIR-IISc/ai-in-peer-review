- Summary: Proposes CRCN to generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams by combining CNN features, BRNN over doc2vec sentence reps, and an entity-grid coherence model, trained on weakly aligned blog data and using retrieval + beam search for sequence selection.
- Data/training: Uses paragraph-based image–text alignment with LSA summarization (κ=3) on travel blogs, and a max-margin sequence-level ranking loss for training.
- Evaluation: Tests on NYC and Disneyland travel blog corpora showing improvements over concatenated single-image captioning baselines on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, and AMT preferences.

Strengths
- Timely and relevant: extends captioning from single images/sentences to image streams/multi-sentence paragraphs.
- Novelty: explicitly integrates an entity-based coherence model with a neural sequence model for vision–language.
- Practical data pipeline: uses scalable, readily available blog data and clearly describes the weak alignment process.
- Reasonable training/decoding: sequence-level loss and beam search are sensible and yield consistent empirical gains.
- Human eval: AMT results corroborate automatic metrics and isolate coherence benefits for longer sequences.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Scoring/length bias: Sequence score double-counts a global coherence term across timesteps, causing linear scaling with sequence length and potential training bias.
- Negative sampling confound: Random-length negatives may confound margin with sequence length effects.
- Conceptual mismatch: Dotting a text-only coherence vector with image features is semantically questionable; coherence should be a separate sequence-level prior or language-only transition model.
- Overstated claims: “End-to-end” and “unsupervised” are misleading due to non-differentiable components and supervised pretraining; alignment relies on heuristics without noise analysis.
- Weak baselines: Baselines are not fully comparable—retrieval approach advantages vs. generative baselines; stronger retrieval/structured baselines are missing.
- Narrow/noisy dataset: Domains are limited to travel blogs with stylistic bias and noisy LSA-based alignments without quantitative validation.
- Evaluation limitations: Paragraph-level BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR correlate poorly with multi-sentence quality; AMT reporting lacks details on worker qualifications, agreement, and significance.
- Missing analyses: No sensitivity analysis for beam/search hyperparameters (K, M) or evaluation of beam approximation quality.

Quality
- Technically sound overall but notable issues in scoring objective and coherence–vision interaction; training/optimization are standard and experiments are broadly reasonable.

Clarity
- Generally clear and organized, but contains notational ambiguities and missing details (doc2vec training, CNN fine-tuning, beam settings); “end-to-end” phrasing is misleading.

Originality
- Moderate: novel in integrating entity-grid coherence with neural sequence modeling for image streams, but many components reuse established methods.

Significance
- Moderate: important problem and promising gains, but retrieval reliance, domain-specific data, and methodological concerns limit immediate impact.

Suggestions for improvement
- Redefine sequence score to separate image–sentence compatibility from a length-normalized sequence-level coherence prior and provide ablations.
- Add stronger retrieval baselines with pairwise coherence potentials or adapt latent structured methods for sentence selection.
- Validate alignment: report alignment accuracy on an annotated subset and analyze coreference/parse noise impact.
- Improve AMT reporting: provide worker qualifications, inter-annotator agreement, and statistical significance tests.
- Analyze sensitivity to K and M and compare beam search to exhaustive search on shorter sequences.
- Consider LSTM/GRU sequence models, CNN fine-tuning, and learned sentence encoders rather than doc2vec, or empirically justify choices.

Pro arguments for acceptance
- Tackles an important extension of image captioning to coherent multi-sentence descriptions.
- Sensible and novel integration of a coherence model with consistent metric and human-evaluated gains.
- Practical approach using large-scale weakly aligned data.

Con arguments for acceptance
- Questionable scoring formulation with potential length bias and semantic mismatch between coherence and vision.
- Incomplete and potentially unfair baseline comparisons; lack of stronger retrieval-structured baselines.
- Limited domains and noisy supervision without validation; overstated claims about end-to-end/unsupervised learning.
- Evaluation lacks depth in analysis, sensitivity, and significance reporting.
