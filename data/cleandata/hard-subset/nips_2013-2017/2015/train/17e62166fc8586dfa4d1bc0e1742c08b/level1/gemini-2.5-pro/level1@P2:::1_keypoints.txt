- Tackles the novel task of generating coherent sentence sequences describing an image stream, a natural extension beyond single-image captioning.  
- Motivation: users capture photo sequences; coherent narratives are more useful than disjoint captions.  
- Proposes CRCN: combines CNN (VGG) for visual features, BRNN with paragraph vectors for sentence sequencing, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Incorporates entity-grid coherence (Barzilay & Lapata) to model smooth discourse-entity transitions and improve fluency.  
- Trains on large datasets harvested from blog posts using a max-margin objective to learn image–sentence-sequence compatibility.  
- Treats test-time generation as a retrieval problem and uses a divide-and-conquer beam search to find near-optimal sentence sequences from an exponentially large space.  
- Builds on CNN+RNN captioning paradigms but extends to sequence-to-sequence inputs/outputs with an explicit coherence component.  
- High technical quality: sound architecture, well-motivated integration of learned and structured coherence components.  
- Comprehensive experiments: two new large-scale datasets, multiple automatic metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR), retrieval metrics, and AMT human evaluation.  
- Ablation study shows each CRCN component (coherence model, sequence model) contributes to performance gains.  
- Weakness: inference relies on heuristic divide-and-conquer beam search; the claim of near-optimality lacks rigorous justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: data preprocessing uses heuristics (e.g., assign text to nearest image) that may introduce noisy supervision; impact not fully analyzed.  
- Limitation: retrieval-based approach cannot generate truly novel sentences, constrained by the training corpus.  
- Originality: novel problem formulation and practical use of blog posts for weak supervision; constituent model parts are from prior work but combined originally.  
- Significance: advances toward holistic visual narrative generation with applications in albums, travelogues, and assistive tech; datasets likely to spur follow-up work.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel problem, effective CRCN combining deep and coherence models, thorough evaluation and clear improvements.  
- Rejection arguments: heuristic, unproven inference optimality; retrieval-based generative limitation; incremental novelty in model components.  
- Overall conclusion: strong, well-executed paper with minor weaknesses; recommendation for acceptance.
