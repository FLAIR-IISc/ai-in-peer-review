This paper proposes a novel approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream. The authors extend the traditional single-image-to-single-sentence generation task to a sequence-to-sequence task, which is more challenging and closer to real-world applications. They design a multimodal architecture called Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with images, which is a unique and valuable contribution to the field.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, including the architecture of the CRCN model, the training process, and the evaluation metrics. The experiments are thorough, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach compared to several state-of-the-art baselines.

The strengths of the paper are:

1. Novelty: The paper addresses a new and challenging task that has not been well-explored in the literature.
2. Architecture: The CRCN model is well-designed, and the integration of the coherence model is a key innovation that sets it apart from previous approaches.
3. Evaluation: The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of their approach, including quantitative metrics and user studies.
4. Dataset: The use of a large dataset of blog posts with images is a significant contribution to the field.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

1. Complexity: The CRCN model is complex, and the training process requires a significant amount of computational resources.
2. Limited generalizability: The approach is specifically designed for the task of generating sentence sequences for image streams, and it may not be directly applicable to other tasks.
3. Dependence on data quality: The performance of the CRCN model relies heavily on the quality of the training data, which may not be representative of all possible scenarios.

Arguments for acceptance:

1. The paper addresses a new and challenging task that has significant implications for real-world applications.
2. The CRCN model is well-designed, and the integration of the coherence model is a key innovation that sets it apart from previous approaches.
3. The evaluation is comprehensive, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Arguments against acceptance:

1. The complexity of the CRCN model may be a barrier for some researchers who want to replicate the results.
2. The approach may not be directly applicable to other tasks, which may limit its generalizability.
3. The dependence on data quality may be a concern, especially if the training data is not representative of all possible scenarios.

Overall, I believe that the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing and computer vision, and it has the potential to inspire new research directions. However, the complexity of the CRCN model and the dependence on data quality are concerns that need to be addressed in future work.

Rating: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the concerns mentioned above and provide more details about the training process and the evaluation metrics.
