- Novel task: extends single-image captioning to generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams, motivated by real user photo sequences.  
- Model: proposes CRCN combining a CNN for visual features, a BRNN for cross-sentence flow, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Data: creatively trains on large-scale blog posts as paired image-stream and text-sequence data.  
- Technical strength: principled integration of state-of-the-art components into a sensible architecture.  
- Empirical strength: thorough quantitative evaluation (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, recall) showing significant gains over strong baselines (Table 1).  
- Related-work gap: insufficient discussion/comparison with video-description literature (e.g., Xu et al. AAAI 2015) to better situate the contribution.  
- Coherence-model validation: user study shows only marginal preference for CRCN over RCN (53.8% at sequence length 5); supplementary longer-sequence results help but main evidence is weak and needs statistical significance testing.  
- Overall assessment: well-executed and meaningful contribution with strong quantitative results, but limited user-study evidence for the key coherence component.
