This paper proposes CRCN, a model that integrates CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model for sentence generation from image streams.  
Approach is well-motivated and produces strong quantitative results.  
Related work misses key prior video-to-sentence research (e.g., "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework").  
User study (Table 2) shows performance comparable to the RCN baseline, questioning practical significance of gains.  
Statistical significance testing is required to determine whether observed improvements are reliable.  
Overall: algorithm generates coherent sentences effectively, but the advantage over baselines appears marginal in human evaluation.
