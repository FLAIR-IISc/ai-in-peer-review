This paper criticizes the problem of describing a sequence of images obtained from a blog post with a sequence of continuous sentences. To do this, the paper proposes retrieving K=5 most similar images and corresponding sentences from the training set for each query image. The main contribution of this paper is determining a way to select the most relevant sentences for the query image sequence, which provides a coherent description. For this, the sentences are first embedded in a vector, and then the sequence of sentences is modeled with a bidirectional LSTLM. The output of the bidirectional LSTLM is first fed through a ReLU and fully connected layer, and then evaluated with a score of consistency between the image and the sentence. Also, a local coherent model [1] is included to maintain consistency between the sentences.

Strengths/Positives: - This paper proposes a new and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for an image sequence. - This paper provides a comprehensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluation, which shows the superiority of their method over several baselines without using coherence. They also provide an ablation test by removing the coherence model [1]. - The authors have promised to publish the source code and dataset.
