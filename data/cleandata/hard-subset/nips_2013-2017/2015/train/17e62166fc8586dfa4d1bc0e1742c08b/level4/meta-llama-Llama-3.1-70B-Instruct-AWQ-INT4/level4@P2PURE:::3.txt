This work explores the task of generating sentences from an image stream by designing a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN). The proposed CRCN architecture combines convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. Overall, this is a well-conceived study, although there are several areas that can be improved.

One notable omission is the lack of discussion on several related works that focus on generating sentences from videos, such as jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language within a unified framework. Incorporating these references would strengthen the paper's contribution and contextualize the proposed approach within the broader research landscape.

While the quantitative results of the proposed method appear promising, the user study presented in Table 2 reveals that the performance of the proposed method is comparable to one of the baselines, RCN. To verify the reliability of the improvement, it is essential to conduct significance tests. The algorithm for generating sentences from an image stream is nicely designed, and the quantitative results are encouraging; however, the user study only demonstrates a marginal advantage over the baselines.
