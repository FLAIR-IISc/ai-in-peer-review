- Tackles generating sequences of sentences for image sequences, extending single-image captioning to a meaningful sequential setting.  
- Fails to cite and discuss closely related video captioning work (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014), which is directly relevant.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem statement and sound methodology.  
- Proposes a CRCN architecture combining CNNs for images, bidirectional RNNs for language, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- The entity-based coherence component is a key strength, addressing fluency and logical connections across sentences.  
- Leverages a large blog-post image-text dataset, demonstrating a practical approach to gathering training data.  
- Sentence generation is simplistic—retrieving and concatenating sentences from similar training images—limiting creativity and expressiveness.  
- Potential anonymity breach by reusing data/methods from Kim et al. (2011) and insufficient dataset acquisition details, raising bias and fairness questions.  
- Dataset is not publicly available, hindering reproducibility.  
- Equation (2) appears erroneous and needs correction to clarify network connections.  
- Empirical results show quantitative and qualitative gains over baseline methods.  
- For acceptance: technically sound model, novel coherence integration, demonstrated performance improvements, and practical data strategy.  
- Against acceptance: omission of related work, simplistic retrieval-based generation, dataset/anonymity concerns, possible equation error, and lack of public dataset.  
- Overall: promising contribution but concerns about related work, dataset access, and the equation error justify a weaker recommendation (poster) until addressed.
