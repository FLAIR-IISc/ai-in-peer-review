This paper addresses the problem of generating a sequence of natural language sentences from an image stream, a natural extension of single image captioning. The authors propose a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) integrating convolutional neural networks for image representation, bidirectional recurrent neural networks for language modeling, and a novel entity-based local coherence model to ensure fluency across sentences. The approach is trained on a large corpus of blog posts and demonstrates strong performance compared to several baselines, using both automated metrics (BLEU, Top-K recall) and a user study via Amazon Mechanical Turk.

The core idea of extending image captioning to streams and explicitly modeling inter-sentence coherence is a logical and valuable contribution. Previous work in image captioning (e.g., [8, 9, 12, 14, 15, 26, 21, 30]) primarily focuses on single image-single sentence pairs. The motivation stemming from how users naturally capture and share experiences through image streams is well articulated. The CRCN architecture appears thoughtfully designed, and the use of blog posts as a training resource is clever, addressing the difficulty of obtaining paired image-sentence stream data. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The work tackles a relatively unexplored problem – generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed CRCN architecture, integrating CNNs, RNNs, and a coherence model, appears technically sound and well-motivated.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The authors provide quantitative results on two datasets (NYC and Disneyland) and support these with a user study on AMT.
*   **Resourcefulness:** Utilizing blog posts as a text-image parallel corpus demonstrates a pragmatic approach to data acquisition.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Related Work:** While the authors cite relevant work, the discussion of video-to-sentence generation could be more comprehensive. Specifically, [32] (Xu et al., AAAI 2017) which directly tackles sequential description generation, should be discussed.
*   **User Study Analysis:** The user study results (Table 2) show only marginal improvements over the RCN baseline, particularly for shorter streams. The lack of statistical significance testing weakens the claim of a demonstrable user preference. Reporting confidence intervals or p-values would be crucial to validate the observed differences.
*   **Ablation Study Detail:** While the ablation study comparing CRCN, RCN, and 1NN is valuable, providing more granular analysis of the coherence model’s contribution (e.g., impact on different sentence lengths, types of coherence transitions) would strengthen the findings.
*   **Clarity (Minor):** The description of the coherence model (Section 3.2) is somewhat dense and could benefit from a clarifying example. Visualizing the “entity grid” would be helpful.

**Originality:** The problem statement is original, and while the individual components (CNNs, RNNs) are well-established, their integration within the CRCN architecture for this specific task demonstrates novelty. The entity-based coherence model is a particularly interesting contribution.

**Significance:** This work has the potential to be impactful. Generating coherent descriptions of image streams has applications in areas like automated storytelling, event summarization, and accessibility. If the user study results can be convincingly validated with statistical significance, this work could be highly influential. The ability to leverage readily available blog data also makes the approach widely applicable.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a novel and important problem.
*   Presents a well-designed and technically sound architecture.
*   Demonstrates promising quantitative and qualitative results.
*   Potential for significant impact in various applications.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   User study results are not statistically significant.
*   Related work discussion could be more comprehensive.
*   Ablation study could provide more detailed insights.



**Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  Perform statistical significance testing on the user study data and report the results.
2.  Expand the related work section to include more recent and relevant video-to-sentence generation papers, such as [32].
3.  Provide a more detailed analysis of the coherence model’s contribution through a more granular ablation study.
4.  Consider adding a clarifying example or visualization to improve the explanation of the coherence model.
