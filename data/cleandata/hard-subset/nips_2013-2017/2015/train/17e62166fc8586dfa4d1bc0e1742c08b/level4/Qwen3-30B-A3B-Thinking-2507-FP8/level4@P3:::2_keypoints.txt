- Problem: extends image captioning from single-image-single-sentence to sequence-to-sequence narration of image streams.  
- Model: proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN) combining CNN image features, BRNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Novelty: first explicit treatment of sequence-to-sequence image description with sentence-to-sentence coherence.  
- Data: leverages blog posts as natural text-image parallel data for narrative supervision.  
- Quantitative results: significantly outperforms baselines on BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, and top-K recall.  
- Human evaluation: Amazon Mechanical Turk studies show clearer improvements in perceived quality.  
- Evaluation quality: comprehensive evaluation including multiple metrics and ablation studies demonstrating the coherence model's contribution.  
- Technical soundness: method is well-motivated and technically justified.  
- Practical significance: relevant to real-world applications (photo collections) and extensible to other sequential image domains; code/dataset promised.  
- Clarity issues: relationship between BRNN output and coherence model in Equation (2) needs clarification.  
- Paragraph vector: unclear whether paragraph vector is trained end-to-end or used as a fixed pre-trained representation.  
- Figure/equation mismatch: discrepancy between Figure 2b and Equation (2) regarding connections of fully connected layers to sentence vectors.  
- Architecture detail: request justification for using two linear layers (Wf2 Wf1) instead of a single linear layer.  
- Prior work claim: challenge the statement that [5] lacks a coherence mechanism since it models topic coherence via intermediate representations.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance; authors addressed concerns in rebuttal and strengthened the paper.
