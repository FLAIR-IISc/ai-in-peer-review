- Novel task: sequence-to-sequence generation of coherent sentence sequences from image streams (photo sequences) rather than single-image captions.  
- Method: proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN) combining CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Key innovation: treats both input (image sequence) and output (sentence sequence) as structured temporal data to model inter-sentence coherence.  
- Training data: leverages user-generated blog posts (natural image–text alignment) to create a large, realistic dataset without manual annotation.  
- Dataset scale: NYC/Disneyland collection with ~20K blogs and ~140K images.  
- Evaluation: compares to multimodal RNN and retrieval baselines using BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall, and human studies.  
- Empirical results: CRCN outperforms baselines on automatic metrics and user studies, with an ablation (CRCN vs. RCN) isolating coherence benefits.  
- Strength — originality/significance: addresses an underexplored, practical problem and introduces coherence modeling missing from prior video/sentence work.  
- Strength — quality: method is technically sound, dataset curation is replicable, experiments are thorough including ablation and user studies.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-organized with clear diagrams and a detailed preprocessing pipeline (minor equation clarity issues).  
- Weakness — significance: reported improvements are sometimes marginal (e.g., small user-study gains for 5-image sequences) and lack statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness — clarity: coherence effects and AMT task design need clearer quantification and justification (e.g., why use 5-image queries).  
- Weakness — related work: misses relevant video-to-sentence literature (e.g., Xu et al. ICCV 2015), weakening novelty claims.  
- Acceptance summary: recommend accept — valuable contribution with strong dataset and coherent modeling, though needs statistical testing and a related-work revision.
