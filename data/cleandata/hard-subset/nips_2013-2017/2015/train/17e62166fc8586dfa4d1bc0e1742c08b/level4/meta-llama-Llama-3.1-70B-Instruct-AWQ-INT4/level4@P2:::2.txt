The paper proposes a novel approach to describe a sequence of images from blog-posts with a sequence of consistent sentences. The authors aim to retrieve the most relevant sentences for the query image sequence, providing a coherent description. To achieve this, they define a way to select the most relevant sentences by embedding sentences in a vector and modeling the sequence of sentences with a bidirectional LSTM. The output of the bi-directional LSTM is then scored with a compatibility score between image and sentence. Additionally, a local coherence model is included to enforce the compatibility between sentences.

The paper's main contribution lies in its effective architecture to retrieve coherent sentences for an image sequence. The authors provide an extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluation, showing the superiority of their approach against several baselines that do not use coherence. They also provide an ablation experiment, removing the coherence model, which further demonstrates the effectiveness of their approach.

The authors' evaluation is thorough, including both automatic metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, and top-K recall) and human evaluation via Amazon Mechanical Turk (AMT). The results show that their approach outperforms other state-of-the-art methods, and the human evaluation confirms that their generated sentences are more coherent and preferred by users.

However, there are a few points that need clarification. Firstly, the authors claim that there is "no mechanism for the coherence between sentences" in [5]. Although not the contribution of [5], [5] predicts an intermediate semantic representation of videos, which is coherent across sentences by modeling the topic of the multi-sentence description. This point should be clarified to provide a more accurate comparison with existing work.

Secondly, the description of the model in Section 3.3 and Equation (2) seems to be inconsistent with Figure 2b. While Figure 2b implies that the fully connected layers are connected to all sentences, Equation (2) implies that the parameters are shared across sentences but only connected to the vector representing a single sentence. This inconsistency should be addressed to provide a clear understanding of the model.

Thirdly, the authors may consider using a more suitable metric for evaluating the generated sentences, such as METEOR, especially when there is only a single reference sentence. BLEU is a widely used metric, but it may not be the most effective metric for evaluating sentence coherence.

Lastly, the authors should consider providing more insights into their design choices, such as the use of two linear functions in Equation (2) and the sharing of parameters between the BRNN output and the local coherence model. An ablation study showing the benefit of these design choices would be interesting and provide more depth to the paper.

After the rebuttal, the authors have addressed the issues with the formulation, evaluation, and related work. The paper proposes an interesting new model to retrieve coherent sentences for an image stream, which is convincingly evaluated. However, to be a convincing paper, several clarifications have to be made, including the fine-tuning or fixing of the paragraph vector.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective architecture to retrieve coherent sentences for an image sequence.
* The authors provide an extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluation, showing the superiority of their approach against several baselines.
* The authors promise to release source code and dataset.

Weaknesses:

* The paper needs clarification on several points, including the comparison with existing work, the model description, and the design choices.
* The authors should consider using a more suitable metric for evaluating the generated sentences.
* The fine-tuning or fixing of the paragraph vector should be clarified.

Recommendation:

I recommend the paper for acceptance after the authors address the issues mentioned above. The paper proposes an interesting new model to retrieve coherent sentences for an image stream, which is convincingly evaluated. However, to be a convincing paper, several clarifications have to be made.
