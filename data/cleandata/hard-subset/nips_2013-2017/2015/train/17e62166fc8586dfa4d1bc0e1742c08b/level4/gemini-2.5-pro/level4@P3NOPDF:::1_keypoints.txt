- Problem significance: Generating narrative descriptions for image sequences is an important, well-motivated task beyond single-image captioning.  
- Technical soundness: The proposed Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) is novel and effectively models image-sequence-to-sentence-sequence relationships.  
- Discourse and coherence: Integration of an entity-based coherence model strengthens narrative quality and yields more natural stories.  
- Missing related work: The paper omits comparison to video description literature (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014), a significant and relevant prior line of work.  
- Simplistic generation method: Story generation uses retrieval-and-concatenation of sentences from visually similar images, an ad-hoc approach that limits novelty and diversity.  
- Reproducibility and dataset: Datasets were crawled via an unspecified method and not released, preventing result verification or follow-up research.  
- Potential anonymity issue: Heavy reuse of an undescribed dataset from prior work suggests the authors may be the same, risking double-blind review integrity.  
- Clarity of Equation (2): Eq. (2) appears erroneous or unclear, implying no recurrent connection (s_t derived only from o_t); needs correction.  
- Recommendation: Accept as a poster—core ideas are novel but authors should release data/code, discuss video-description literature, and clarify technical details.
