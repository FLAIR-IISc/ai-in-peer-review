- Task is relevant to computer vision but closely parallels video multi-sentence generation work (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014) which is not cited.
- Paper is well-written and accessible with a clearly stated problem.
- Solution uses solid technical methods and a technically sound architecture.
- Proposed CRCN model effectively captures relationships between image sequences and natural sentences.
- Model incorporates discourse relationships via entity-based coherence modeling.
- Sentence generation method is simplistic and ad-hoc: each sentence is matched to similar training images and concatenated in image order without refinement.
- Dataset disclosure is lacking: Disneyland blog data reused and NYC data newly collected, but dataset not shared and collection method in [11] is unspecified.
- Possible double-blind violation: authors may be the same as those in [11], based on dataset description.
- Equation (2) appears incorrect, implying s_t depends only on o_t and suggesting the network may not be fully connected.
- Overall assessment: model is technically sound but sentence generation is weak; paper is suitable for a poster.
