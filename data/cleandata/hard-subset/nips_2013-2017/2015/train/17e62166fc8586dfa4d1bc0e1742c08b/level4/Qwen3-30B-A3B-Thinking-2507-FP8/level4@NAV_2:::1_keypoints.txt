- Problem: Extends single-image captioning to retrieving sequences of natural sentences describing an image stream.
- Model: Proposes CRCN combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Motivation: Grounded in real-world user behavior of capturing image sequences during memorable experiences.
- Contribution: Addresses an under-explored, practically relevant problem in image captioning.
- Novelty: Coherence model provides a novel mechanism to ensure fluent transitions between sentences.
- Technical soundness: CRCN architecture is well-designed and integrates multiple relevant techniques.
- Evaluation: Uses BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall and AMT user studies showing clear improvements over strong baselines.
- Strength: Paper is well-motivated, comprehensively evaluated, and clearly written.
- Weakness: Related work insufficiently cites and distinguishes from video description research (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014).
- Weakness: Sentence generation is primitive, relying on nearest neighbor search and simple concatenation.
- Weakness: NYC dataset collection lacks sufficient detail and transparency about crawling/data provenance.
- Weakness: Possible issue in Equation (2) suggesting st may be derived solely from ot, implying potential connectivity flaws.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance as a poster, contingent on addressing related work linkage and data transparency.
- Pro arguments: Meaningful problem extension, well-integrated CRCN, and strong empirical improvements.
- Con arguments: Need better video-description comparison, more sophisticated sentence generation, and clearer data collection details.
