- Problem: Describe an image stream with a coherent sequence of natural sentences using a multimodal architecture (CRCN).
- Model: CRCN combines CNN image features, a bidirectional RNN over sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid local coherence model.
- Retrieval strategy: Retrieves candidate sentences from visually similar blog images and ranks sequences by learned image–text compatibility with a max-margin objective.
- Evaluation: Uses BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval R@K/median rank, and AMT user studies; ablations show gains, especially for longer sequences.
- Novelty: Explicitly models local coherence (entity grid) within a CNN–BRNN multimodal framework for multi-sentence description.
- Relation to prior work: Sits between image captioning and video description; should better compare to prior video-to-language and paragraph captioning (e.g., Xu et al., AAAI 2015).
- Strength — task importance: Moving from single images/sentences to image streams/paragraphs is timely and significant.
- Strength — architecture: Well-motivated integration of visual features, BRNN language modeling, and entity-based coherence.
- Strength — data: Practical, scalable use of blog posts as weakly supervised image–text data.
- Strength — results: Strong quantitative improvements over baselines and informative ablation studies showing BRNN and coherence contributions.
- Strength — implementation: Clear training details (BPTT, RMSprop, dropout), negative sampling, and approximate sequence search.
- Weakness — related work: Needs fuller discussion of video-to-text and paragraph-captioning literature and clearer differentiation.
- Weakness — user study stats: Human preference claims require statistical significance tests, inter-annotator agreement, and effect sizes.
- Weakness — evaluation focus: Automatic metrics weakly capture discourse coherence; add coherence-specific evaluations and stratified readability/fluency ratings.
- Weakness — retrieval vs generation: Retrieving sentences limits novelty, may cause stylistic mismatch, redundancy, and coverage issues; discuss diversity mechanisms.
- Weakness — preprocessing/alignment: Paragraph proximity and LSA summarization may introduce noise; validate segmentation, sensitivity to κ, and provide failure analysis.
- Weakness — coherence fusion: Zero-padding 64-d entity vector to 300-d is ad hoc; consider learned projection/MLP and analyze history length and coreference errors.
- Weakness — search & scalability: Clarify computational cost of K-NN retrieval and beam search (M=50), and runtime/memory scaling with sequence length.
- Weakness — reproducibility: State if code, models, and processed datasets or scripts will be released; provide more hyperparameter and negative-sampling details.
- Clarity: Paper is mostly clear but could streamline notation, fix minor typos, and add an example illustrating the entity grid.
- Originality: Meaningful contribution by integrating entity-based coherence with multimodal sequence learning beyond standard captioning.
- Significance: Moderate to high; task important and approach promising but would benefit from stronger human-evaluation and related-work coverage.
- Acceptance pros: Addresses underexplored problem, novel coherence integration, solid quantitative gains and supportive ablations.
- Acceptance cons: Limited demonstrated user-study advantage over BRNN-only, incomplete related work coverage, ad hoc design choices and limited sensitivity analyses.
- Overall: Strong, well-executed submission that would be more compelling with improved related work, rigorous human evaluation, and additional analysis.
