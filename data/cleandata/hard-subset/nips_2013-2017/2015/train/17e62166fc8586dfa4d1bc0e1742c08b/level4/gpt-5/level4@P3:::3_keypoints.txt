- Introduces task of generating multi-sentence descriptions for image streams and proposes the Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN).
- CRCN integrates VGG CNN image features, a bidirectional RNN over doc2vec sentence vectors, and an entity-grid coherence model.
- Retrieval-based approach: retrieve candidate sentences from blog corpus and rank sequences by a learned compatibility score using a max-margin loss.
- Experiments on NYC and Disneyland blog datasets (~20K posts, ~140K images) show improvements over strong baselines on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and retrieval metrics, plus AMT user studies.
- Well grounded in retrieval-based captioning and multimodal RNN literature; novelty in adding an entity-grid coherence model for multi-sentence outputs.
- Suggestion to expand related work to include temporally structured video-to-text models (e.g., Venugopalan et al., CVPR 2015) and recent video-captioning frameworks.
- Methodology is sensible: per-image candidate retrieval plus global sequence scoring with a linguistically motivated coherence signal.
- Use of paragraph vectors (doc2vec) for sentence-level modeling is pragmatic and appropriate.
- Technical clarity needed: justify Eq.(3) where one sequence-level coherence vector interacts with each image independently vs. aggregated image representations or an image RNN.
- Ablation needed for BRNN input projections (shared vs. separate Wfi/Wbi) to support the design choice.
- Preprocessing concerns: segmentation heuristic (assigning paragraphs to nearest image) and LSA summarization may introduce noise; report sensitivity to κ and segmentation quality.
- Training details missing: sensitivity to number and mining strategy of negatives, and whether length normalization is applied to avoid bias toward sequence length.
- Evaluation limitations: BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR may not capture paragraph-level fluency/coherence; recommend coherence-specific evaluations (entity-grid discrimination) and paragraph-level metrics (e.g., ROUGE-L).
- Empirical results: strong retrieval gains, smaller language-metric improvements; AMT advantage over BRNN-only (RCN) is modest—statistical significance tests and full AMT stats required.
- Clarity improvements requested: pseudocode/complexity of divide-and-conquer + beam search, doc2vec training details (corpus, window, DM vs. DBOW, epochs), and explicit statement of which components are trained jointly.
- Originality: meaningful extension to multi-sentence image-stream captioning and novel integration of an entity-grid coherence model into a deep multimodal pipeline.
- Significance could be increased by cross-domain evaluation, error analysis (content mismatch vs. incoherent transitions), and demonstrating consistent benefits on longer narratives.
- Pros: timely problem, thoughtful integration of components, solid retrieval performance, useful blog-derived dataset, scalable retrieval-plus-ranking pipeline.
- Cons: limited empirical advantage on language metrics and short-sequence AMT, heavy reliance on heuristic preprocessing, Eq.(3) design untested, domain restricted to tourism.
- Suggested improvements: add statistical significance tests and CIs, explicit coherence evaluations, ablation studies (coherence term, alternative image–coherence interactions, BRNN projections, κ/segmentation sensitivity), and cross-domain/generalization tests.
- Overall assessment: well-motivated and promising contribution; recommend acceptance conditional on additional significance analyses, coherence-focused evaluation, and clarification/ablations of key modeling choices.
