## Review of NIPS 2015 Submission: [Paper Title – Replace with Actual Title]

This paper addresses the challenging problem of generating coherent textual descriptions for sequences of images, such as those found in blog posts. The authors propose a novel architecture that leverages retrieval of similar image-sentence pairs from a training set, followed by a sequence modeling approach to select and order the most relevant sentences for the given image sequence. The core contribution lies in effectively combining image-sentence compatibility scoring with a local coherence model to ensure the generated description maintains semantic consistency.

**Strengths / Positive Aspects:**

*   **Novel and Effective Architecture:** The proposed architecture is a compelling approach to generating coherent descriptions from image sequences. It effectively integrates retrieval-based and generation-based techniques.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The paper presents a thorough evaluation, including quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, and a human evaluation, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed method over several baselines, particularly those that do not explicitly model coherence. The ablation study effectively highlights the importance of the local coherence model.
*   **Reproducibility:** The authors’ commitment to releasing source code and the dataset is a significant positive aspect, facilitating reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses / Questions / Unclarities:**

1.  **Related Work – Claim Regarding [5]:** The paper states in Line 94 that "[5] has no mechanism for coherence between sentences." While not the primary focus of [5], it’s important to acknowledge that their prediction of an intermediate semantic representation for videos inherently models topic coherence across sentences in a multi-sentence description. A more nuanced discussion of related work would be beneficial.
2.  **Figure 2b Discrepancy:** Figure 2b appears inconsistent with the description in Section 3.3 and Equation (2). The figure seems to suggest a fully connected layer connecting to all sentences, while Equation (2) indicates parameter sharing across sentences and connection to a single sentence vector. Clarification is needed.
3.  **Evaluation Metric – BLEU vs. METEOR:** Given the use of a single reference sentence, the BLEU score may not be the most appropriate evaluation metric. The METEOR metric (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) often performs better in such scenarios and should be considered.
4.  **Redundant Linear Functions (Equation 2):** The application of two consecutive linear functions (W<sub>f2</sub> and W<sub>f1</sub>) in Equation (2) is unclear. Since two linear transformations can be collapsed into a single linear transformation, the benefit of this specific design choice is not immediately apparent. An ablation study exploring the impact of removing one of these layers would be informative.
5.  **Parameter Sharing in Fully Connected Layers and Coherence Model:** Why are the same parameters from the fully connected layers used for both the bidirectional RNN output (o<sub>t</sub>) and the local coherence model q (Equation 2)? The rationale behind this design choice should be explained.
6.  **Paragraph Vector Fine-tuning:** Was the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned during training, or was it kept fixed? This detail is important for understanding the overall training process and the contribution of the pre-trained embedding.

**Post-Rebuttal:**

After reviewing the rebuttal, I recommend this paper for acceptance. The authors have effectively addressed the concerns raised regarding the formulation, evaluation, and related work. The clarifications provided have significantly strengthened the paper and increased my confidence in the validity of the results. 

**Specifically, please clarify in the final version whether the paragraph vector [16] was fine-tuned during training or remained fixed.**

Overall, this paper proposes an interesting and well-evaluated model for retrieving coherent sentences for image streams. While several clarifications were necessary, the authors have demonstrated a willingness to engage with the feedback and improve the manuscript, resulting in a convincing and valuable contribution to the field.
