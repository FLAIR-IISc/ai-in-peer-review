- Summary: Proposes CRCN combining CNN visual features, bi-RNN temporal modeling, and an entity-based local coherence module for sentence generation from image streams.
- Importance: Tackles an important task (narrating image sequences/videos) with a sensible architecture for visual dynamics and discourse coherence.
- Strength: Well-motivated task with clear practical value.
- Strength: Thoughtful integration of temporal modeling (bi-RNN) with CNN visual features.
- Strength: Novel entity-based local coherence module addressing discourse-level consistency.
- Strength: Strong quantitative results on standard automatic metrics, outperforming baselines.
- Weakness: Related work coverage on video-to-text and sequence-to-sequence/LSTM captioning is incomplete and should be expanded.
- Weakness: Human evaluation shows only marginal gains over baseline and lacks statistical significance testing and inter-annotator agreement.
- Weakness: Contribution of the coherence module is not isolated; ablation studies are missing.
- Weakness: Unclear how entities are defined, tracked, and how coreference errors are handled; supervision and feature design need more detail.
- Weakness: Evaluation lacks breadth—additional temporal baselines and fair comparisons (features/parameter counts) are needed.
- Weakness: Analysis depth is limited; needs qualitative examples, failure cases, performance vs. stream length, and metric comparisons.
- Weakness: Experimental clarity lacking—provide optimization, regularization, preprocessing, frame sampling, variable-length handling, and runtime/complexity.
- Question: How are entity representations obtained/updated and is the coherence signal trained end-to-end or separately?
- Question: How is bidirectionality used at inference—offline full-context or an online/causal variant?
- Question: How robust is CRCN to noisy or long image streams and irregular sampling?
- Question: Do improvements transfer across domains/datasets and different visual or language features?
- Suggestion: Expand related work and explicitly compare to closely related video-to-text models.
- Suggestion: Strengthen human evaluation with more evaluators, blind pairwise tests, Likert scales, inter-annotator agreement, and significance tests.
- Suggestion: Add ablations/sensitivity analyses for the coherence module, temporal choices, and visual features.
- Suggestion: Provide qualitative examples with entity chains to demonstrate coherence effects.
- Suggestion: Include full implementation/training details and release code/models for reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: Solid and timely contribution targeting discourse coherence but needs stronger related work, statistical validation of human studies, and ablations.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept, leaning accept if empirical analysis, significance testing, and ablations are strengthened.
