This research paper attacks the problem of describing a sequence of images from a blog post with a sequence of sentences. To do this, the paper proposes obtaining K=5 most similar images and corresponding sentences from the training set for each query image. The paper's main contribution is defining a method for selecting the most relevant sentences for the query image sequence, which provides a coherent description. To do this, the sentences are first embedded into a vector, and then the sequence of sentences is modeled with a bidirectional LSTM. The output of the bidirectional LSTM is first fed through relu and fully connected layers and then scored with a compatibility score between the image and the sentence. In addition, a local consistency model [1] is included to apply the consistency between sentences.

Strengths/Positives: - The paper proposes a novel and effective architecture for obtaining coherent sentences for an image sequence. - The paper provides a comprehensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluation, demonstrating the superiority of their approach against several baselines that did not use consistency. They also provide an ablation study, in which the consistency model [1] is removed. - The authors promise to release the source code and dataset.

Weaknesses/Questions/Ambiguities: 1. Line 94: The paper claims that [5] "does not have a mechanism for consistency between sentences." Although [5] does not contribute, [5] predicts an intermediate semantic representation of the video, which models the subject of the multi-sentence description, making the sentences consistent. 2. Accuracy/Clarity: Figure 2b does not match description 3.3/Equation (2). While Figure 2b implies that the fully connected layers are connected to all the sentences, this is not the case in Equation 2, which implies that the parameters are shared across sentences, but connected to a vector representing only one sentence. 3. Meteor (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) is a better metric for automatically evaluating generated sentences, especially if there is only one reference sentence. 4. Why are two linear functions applied back-to-back in Equation 2 (W{f2}, W{f1})? Given that the two linear functions are again a linear function, the benefit is unclear. An ablation study showing the benefit of these functions would be interesting. 5. Why are the same parameters used for the BRNN output (o_t) and the local consistency model q (Equation 2) as for the fully connected layers? 6. Is the paragraph vector [16] properly tuned or kept stable?
 === Post Debriefing === After reading the debriefing, I recommend the paper for acceptance. The authors have successfully addressed the issues with the formulation, evaluation, and related tasks.
Please make the changes promised at the end and also clarify the following point at the end. 6. Is the paragraph vector [16] properly tuned or kept stable? The paper proposes an interesting new model for obtaining coherent sentences for an image stream, which has been satisfactorily evaluated. However, to be a solid research paper, several clarifications need to be made.
