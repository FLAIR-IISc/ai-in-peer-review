- Fails to cite and engage with relevant video-captioning prior work (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014), which conceptually underpins image-sequence description.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and presents a technically sound solution.  
- Proposed CRCN model effectively captures image-sequence to sentence-sequence relationships using entity-based coherence modeling.  
- Sentence generation is simplistic: each sentence is generated independently from a nearest-neighbor training image and then concatenated in image order.  
- Generation strategy neglects sequence-level dependencies and is weaker than sequence-to-sequence approaches.  
- Data collection lacks transparency: newly collected NYC dataset is not publicly available, the cited crawling method is undocumented, and replication is impossible.  
- Possible breach of anonymity—authors may be identical to cited work [11], suggested by dataset description.  
- Equation (2) is questionable: it suggests s_t depends only on o_t, which would preclude using prior hidden states and contradict the claimed architecture.  
- Technical contributions are valid but the combination of literature omission, simplistic generation, and data transparency issues make the paper suitable only for a poster, not a spotlight/oral.
