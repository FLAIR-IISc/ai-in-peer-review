- Tackles retrieving and generating natural-language sentences for image sequences; significant CV/PR problem.
- Work is closely related to video captioning (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014) but fails to cite that literature.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and uses solid techniques.
- CRCN model effectively learns relationships between sentence sequences and image sequences.
- Model architecture is technically sound and leverages entity-based coherence to capture discourse relationships.
- Sentence-sequence generation is reasonable but simplistic: each sentence is tied to similar training images and concatenated by image order.
- Dataset details are inadequately disclosed (reuse of Disneyland blog data from [11] and newly collected NYC data), raising anonymity and reproducibility concerns.
- Equation (2) appears incorrect, implying st depends only on ot and suggesting a lack of full connectivity.
- Overall: CRCN models discourse well but generation is somewhat ad-hoc; suitable for acceptance as a poster.
