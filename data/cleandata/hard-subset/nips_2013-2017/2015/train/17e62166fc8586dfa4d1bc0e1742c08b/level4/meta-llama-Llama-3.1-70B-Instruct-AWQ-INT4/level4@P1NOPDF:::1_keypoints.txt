- Tackles retrieval of natural sentences for image sequences, a significant problem related to video description literature (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014), but fails to cite related work.  
- Paper is well written, easy to follow, with a clearly stated problem and solution.  
- Proposed CRCN model effectively captures relationships between sentence sequences and image sequences; architecture is technically sound and models discourse via entity-based coherence.  
- Sentence-generation method is reasonable but somewhat primitive/ad-hoc: each sentence is matched to similar training images and sequences are simply concatenated in image order.  
- Dataset issues: Disneyland blog data reused from [11] and newly collected NYC data are not disclosed; [11] lacks crawl details, raising anonymity concerns that authors may be the same as [11].  
- Equation (2) seems incorrect or misleading, implying st depends only on ot and suggesting the network may not be fully connected.  
- Despite limitations, reviewer recommends acceptance as a poster paper.
