- Problem: Generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions from image streams, extending single-image captioning.  
- Proposed method: Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN image features, bidirectional RNN language model, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Training data: Trained on a large corpus of blog posts to obtain image–sentence stream supervision.  
- Evaluation: Quantitative metrics (BLEU, Top-K recall) on NYC and Disneyland datasets and a user study on Amazon Mechanical Turk.  
- Strength — Novelty: Addresses a relatively unexplored problem of multi-sentence image-stream description.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: CRCN architecture is well-motivated and integrates components effectively.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: Promising quantitative results and user study comparisons to baselines.  
- Strength — Resourcefulness: Clever use of blog posts as a pragmatic data source.  
- Weakness — Related work: Related work discussion is incomplete; needs coverage of video-to-sentence generation (e.g., Xu et al., AAAI 2017 [32]).  
- Weakness — User study: Reported improvements are marginal for short streams and lack statistical significance testing (no p-values/confidence intervals).  
- Weakness — Ablation study: Needs more granular analysis of the coherence model’s contribution (e.g., by sentence length and coherence transition types).  
- Weakness — Clarity (minor): Coherence model description is dense; would benefit from an example or entity-grid visualization.  
- Originality: Problem formulation is original; integrating CNNs/RNNs with an entity-based coherence model is novel.  
- Significance: Work has potential impact for storytelling, event summarization, and accessibility if user-study claims are validated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel and important problem, well-designed architecture, promising results, and practical applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: User study not statistically validated, incomplete related work, and insufficiently detailed ablation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: (1) perform statistical significance testing on user study results, (2) expand related work to include relevant video-to-sentence papers (e.g., [32]), (3) provide a more granular ablation analysis of the coherence model, (4) add a clarifying example or visualization of the entity-grid.
