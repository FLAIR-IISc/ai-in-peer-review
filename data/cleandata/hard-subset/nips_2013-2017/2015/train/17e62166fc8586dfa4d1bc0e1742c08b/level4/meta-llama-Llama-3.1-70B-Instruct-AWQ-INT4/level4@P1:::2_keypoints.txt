- Paper addresses generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences by retrieving K=5 nearest training images/sentences and selecting relevant sentences for coherence.
- Main technical contribution: sentence embedding + bidirectional LSTM sequence model, followed by ReLU+fully connected layers and an image–sentence compatibility score, augmented with a local coherence model.
- Strength: Novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for image streams.
- Strength: Extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations showing superiority over non-coherence baselines, with an ablation removing the coherence model.
- Strength: Authors promise to release source code and dataset.
- Weakness: Claim that prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is inaccurate since [5] models coherent intermediate semantic representations/topics.
- Weakness/clarity: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2); figure suggests FC layers connect to all sentences while Eq(2) implies parameter sharing per sentence.
- Weakness: Automatic evaluation should include METEOR rather than relying mainly on BLEU, especially with single references.
- Weakness/question: Eq(2) uses two linear layers (Wf2, Wf1) in succession, which is redundant unless there is a nonlinearity—an ablation is needed to justify them.
- Weakness/question: Why the same FC parameters are used for both the BRNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model q in Eq(2)?
- Weakness/question: It is unclear whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed—reviewer asks for clarification in the final paper.
- Post-rebuttal recommendation: Recommend acceptance; authors addressed formulation, evaluation, and related-work issues.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel model, convincing evaluations, and issues addressed in rebuttal.
- Rejection concerns: Remaining clarity issues (Fig2b vs Eq2), missing ablation for the two linear functions, and unclear paragraph-vector fine-tuning.
