1. Addresses retrieval of a sequence of natural sentences for a sequence of images — a significant CV/PR problem.  
2. Fails to cite related work on multi-sentence video description (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014).  
3. Paper is well-written and easy to follow with a clear problem statement.  
4. Proposes CRCN model that is technically sound and captures sentence–image sequence relationships.  
5. Effectively models discourse relationships using entity-based coherence models.  
6. Sentence generation method is primitive/ad-hoc: associate sentences to similar training images and concatenate.  
7. Dataset issues: reuses Disneyland blog data and crawls NYC data but dataset is not publicly available and crawling details are missing.  
8. Anonymity concern: authors may be the same as those of the cited dataset paper [11].  
9. Potential flaw in Equation (2) implying st depends only on ot, suggesting incomplete network connectivity.  
10. Overall judgment: solid contribution but has issues; recommend acceptance as a poster (rating 6.5/10).
