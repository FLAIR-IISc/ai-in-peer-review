

This work proposes a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) for generating sentences from image streams, integrating convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The approach is well-motivated and presents a technically sound framework for the task. However, the paper requires significant revisions to strengthen its contribution and evaluation.  

First, the related work section omits key prior efforts in video-to-sentence generation, notably the work on "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework." Including this would better contextualize the novelty of CRCN.  

Second, while the quantitative results appear promising, the user study in Table 2 shows performance comparable to the RCN baseline, with only a modest advantage. Statistical significance testing is essential to confirm whether the observed improvements are reliable and not attributable to random variation. Without this, the claimed superiority of the proposed method remains unsubstantiated.  

The algorithm itself is well-designed, but the evaluation must be strengthened to support the paper's claims.
