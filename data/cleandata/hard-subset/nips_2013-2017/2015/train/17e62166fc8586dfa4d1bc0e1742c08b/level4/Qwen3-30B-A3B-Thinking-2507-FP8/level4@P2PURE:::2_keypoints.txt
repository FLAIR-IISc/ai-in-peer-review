- Proposes a novel, effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentence sequences describing image sequences from blog posts.  
- Provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations showing clear superiority over non-coherence baselines; ablation removing the coherence model validates the approach.  
- Authors commit to releasing source code and the dataset.  
- Claim that prior work [5] lacks a coherence mechanism is questionable since [5] models topic across multi-sentence descriptions via an intermediate semantic representation.  
- Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2): the figure implies the fully connected layer connects to all sentences, while Eq. (2) shows shared parameters but a single-sentence input.  
- BLEU is a suboptimal metric here; METEOR is more appropriate for single-reference sentence evaluation.  
- Using two sequential linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) is redundant because they compose to one linear transform; an ablation is needed to justify this design.  
- The same fully connected layer parameters are shared between the BRNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model (q); the rationale for this parameter sharing is unclear.  
- It is unclear whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.  
- After rebuttal, the reviewer recommends acceptance, noting authors addressed formulation, evaluation, and related-work concerns; final clarification on paragraph-vector fine-tuning is required.
