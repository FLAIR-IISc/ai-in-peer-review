- Addresses retrieval and sequencing of sentences for image sequences, but fails to cite related video-description work (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014).  
- Paper is clearly written with a well-defined problem and an understandable solution.  
- CRCN model effectively learns relationships between sentence sequences and images.  
- Architecture is well-designed and successfully incorporates discourse relationships via entity-based coherence modeling.  
- Sentence generation is simplistic: sentences copied from similar training images and concatenated in image order.  
- Data collection issues: reused/collected blog data not publicly available; [11] lacks crawling details and shared authorship may breach anonymity.  
- Equation (2) seems flawed, implying s_t depends only on o_t and suggesting incomplete network connectivity.  
- Overall sentence-generation approach feels ad hoc despite a technically sound model.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance as a poster.
