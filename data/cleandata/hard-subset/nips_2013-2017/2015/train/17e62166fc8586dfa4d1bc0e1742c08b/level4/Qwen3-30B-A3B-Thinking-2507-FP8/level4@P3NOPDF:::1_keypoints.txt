- Task is important (generating multi-sentence descriptions from image sequences) and relevant to vision-language understanding.
- Paper fails to contextualize within related work, especially video captioning and sequential image description (missing key citations).
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem formulation and logical structure.
- CRCN model appropriately integrates visual and linguistic features and leverages entity-based coherence; architecture is technically sound for the matching task.
- Sentence generation is naive: it selects the most similar training image per frame and concatenates sentences, failing to model inter-sentence dependencies (temporal coherence, pronoun resolution).
- Dataset disclosure is inadequate: NYC crawling methodology is not described, dataset not released, and potential undisclosed reuse/overlap with prior work undermines reproducibility.
- Equation (2) appears erroneous—hidden state s_t is defined only from observation o_t, contradicting intended recurrent dependence on s_{t-1}; needs correction/clarification.
- Strengths are clear problem statement, motivated model, and solid execution for core matching task.
- Major weaknesses—missing citations, simplistic generation approach, undisclosed dataset methods, and a likely equation error—limit the paper’s contribution and novelty.
- Recommended improvements: situate work in prior literature, replace ad-hoc generation with true sequence modeling (e.g., LSTM/Transformer), and fully disclose dataset/methods.
- Overall judgment: contribution is incremental and flawed; reviewer leans toward rejection and advises substantial revision before acceptance.
