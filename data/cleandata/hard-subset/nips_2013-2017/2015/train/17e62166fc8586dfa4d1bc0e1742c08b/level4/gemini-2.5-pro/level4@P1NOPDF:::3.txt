This paper addresses the task of generating descriptive sentences from an image stream. The authors propose a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks, a bidirectional recurrent neural network, and an entity-based local coherence model.

Overall, this is a solid paper that presents an interesting and well-motivated approach. The algorithm is novel and the quantitative results are strong. However, the work could be improved in the following areas:

1.  **Missing Related Work:** The literature review could be more comprehensive. The authors appear to have missed several relevant papers on the closely related task of video-to-sentence generation. For example, the paper "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework" should be cited and discussed.

2.  **Statistical Significance of User Study:** While the automatic evaluation metrics are promising, the human evaluation presented in Table 2 is less conclusive. The proposed CRCN model performs similarly to the RCN baseline in the user study, suggesting only a marginal improvement in perceptual quality. To strengthen the claims of superiority, the authors should conduct statistical significance tests on these results to verify whether the observed advantage over the baseline is reliable.

In summary, the paper introduces a well-designed algorithm for generating sentences from image streams with strong quantitative results. The contribution would be more convincing if the claims were supported by a more rigorous human evaluation, including significance testing, and a more thorough discussion of the relevant literature.
