- Task & contribution: Proposes retrieving K=5 nearest train images/sentences per query image and selecting a coherent sentence sequence via sentence embeddings, a bidirectional LSTM, ReLU+FC scoring, and a local coherence model.  
- Main architecture claim: Novel, effective model for retrieving coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences.  
- Evaluation: Comprehensive quantitative, qualitative, human evaluations and an ablation study; outperforms baselines that lack coherence modeling.  
- Reproducibility: Authors will release code and dataset.  
- Weakness 1: Claim that prior work [5] lacks coherence is questionable because [5] predicts intermediate semantic representations that can enforce topical coherence.  
- Weakness 2: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2)—unclear whether FC layers connect to all sentences or share parameters per sentence.  
- Weakness 3: Automatic evaluation metric choice: METEOR would be more appropriate than BLEU when only a single reference sentence is available.  
- Weakness 4: Questionable design: two sequential linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) are redundant unless nonlinearity or other justification is provided; an ablation is needed.  
- Weakness 5: Unclear why the same FC parameters are used for both BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence model q—needs clarification.  
- Weakness 6 / Open question: Is the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned during training or kept fixed?  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance after authors addressed major concerns, but asks that promised revisions be made and the paragraph-vector fine-tuning question be clarified.
