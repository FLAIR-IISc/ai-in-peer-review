This paper presents a method for generating sentences from a stream of images by introducing a novel architecture, the Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN). This model integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model.

Overall, this is a solid contribution, but the work could be significantly improved in the following areas:

*   The discussion of related work is not comprehensive, as it omits several key studies in video-to-sentence generation. For instance, the authors should include and discuss "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework" to better situate their contribution.
*   There is a discrepancy between the automated metrics and the human evaluation. While the quantitative results for the proposed method appear strong, the user study in Table 2 indicates that its performance is comparable to the RCN baseline. To validate the claim of improvement, a statistical significance test is necessary to confirm that the observed gains are reliable.

In summary, the paper introduces an interesting algorithm for sentence generation from an image stream with impressive quantitative results. However, the claims of superiority would be more convincing if the user study demonstrated a clearer advantage over baselines, supported by statistical analysis.
