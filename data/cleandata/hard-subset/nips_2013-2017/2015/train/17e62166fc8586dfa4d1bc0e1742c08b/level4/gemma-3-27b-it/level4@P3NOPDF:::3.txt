This work addresses the challenging problem of generating descriptive sentences from image streams. The authors propose a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN aims to produce more fluent and contextually relevant sentence descriptions compared to existing approaches.

Overall, this is a promising contribution. The proposed CRCN architecture appears well-motivated and demonstrates strong potential for improving image stream to sentence generation. However, several aspects could be strengthened to enhance the paper’s impact.

The related work section feels incomplete. While the authors mention relevant areas, key publications in video-to-sentence generation are missing. For example, the work by [cite specific paper: e.g., "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework"] would be a valuable addition, as it proposes a related approach to bridging visual and linguistic information. Including and contrasting with this and other relevant work would better position the authors’ contribution within the existing literature.

The quantitative results presented are encouraging and indicate the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the user study (Table 2) raises concerns. While the CRCN achieves comparable performance to the RCN baseline, the differences are not substantial. Critically, a statistical significance test is absent. Without a rigorous statistical analysis, it’s difficult to determine whether the observed improvements are reliable or simply due to chance. Providing p-values or confidence intervals would greatly strengthen the conclusions drawn from the user study. The authors should clarify whether the observed differences are statistically significant and, if not, acknowledge this limitation. 

Furthermore, the presentation of the user study could be improved. More detail about the participant demographics and the specific instructions given during the evaluation would enhance reproducibility and understanding. Describing the criteria used for judging sentence quality (e.g., relevance, fluency, grammatical correctness) would also be beneficial.

The algorithm itself is a well-designed approach to sentence generation from an image stream, and the quantitative results generally support its efficacy. However, the weak advantage observed in the user study over strong baselines, coupled with the lack of statistical significance testing, diminishes the overall impact of the findings. 

**Summary of Strengths:**

*   Well-motivated and clearly presented CRCN architecture.
*   Strong quantitative results demonstrating the potential of the method.
*   Addresses an important and challenging problem in vision and language.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

*   Incomplete related work section.
*   Lack of statistical significance testing for the user study.
*   Limited details regarding the user study methodology.



**Suggestions for Improvement:**

*   Expand the related work section to include key publications in video-to-sentence generation.
*   Perform statistical significance tests on the user study results and report p-values or confidence intervals.
*   Provide more details about the user study participants, instructions, and evaluation criteria.
*   Discuss the limitations of the proposed method, particularly in scenarios with complex visual events or ambiguous scene understanding.
