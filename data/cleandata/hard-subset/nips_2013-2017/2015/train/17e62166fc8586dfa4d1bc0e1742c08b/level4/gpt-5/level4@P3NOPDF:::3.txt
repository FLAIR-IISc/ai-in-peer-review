Summary
The paper tackles sentence generation from an image stream by proposing a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN). The model combines CNNs for visual feature extraction, bidirectional RNNs for temporal modeling, and an entity-based local coherence component to improve discourse-level consistency across generated sentences. The problem is important, and the architecture is sensible for capturing both visual dynamics and linguistic coherence.

Strengths
- Well-motivated task with clear practical value (narrating image sequences or videos).
- Thoughtful integration of temporal modeling (bi-RNN) with visual features (CNN).
- Explicit attention to local coherence via an entity-based module is novel and addresses a common weakness in sequence captioning.
- Quantitative results are strong on standard automatic metrics and suggest gains over baselines.

Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement
- Coverage of related work on video-to-text is incomplete. At minimum, the paper should discuss prior models that jointly model video and language (e.g., “Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework”) and contemporary video captioning approaches (e.g., sequence-to-sequence or LSTM-based methods for video description). Positioning CRCN relative to such work will clarify its novelty and contributions.
- Human evaluation in Table 2 appears to show only a marginal advantage over the RCN baseline. A statistical significance analysis (e.g., bootstrap resampling for pairwise preferences, Wilcoxon signed-rank tests, or permutation tests) is needed to validate the reliability of the gains. Please also report inter-annotator agreement.
- The contribution of the entity-based coherence module is not fully isolated. An ablation study that removes or varies the coherence component (and its features) would help quantify its impact on both automatic metrics and human judgments.
- It is unclear how entities are defined and tracked. Are entities derived via coreference resolution, named-entity recognition, or noun-phrase extraction? How are coreference errors handled, and how does this affect training and inference? More detail on supervision and feature design for the coherence model is needed.
- Evaluation breadth: It would be helpful to include additional baselines that capture temporal structure (e.g., RNN/LSTM variants without bidirectionality or without coherence modeling) and to ensure fair comparisons (same features, comparable parameter counts).
- Analysis depth: The paper would benefit from qualitative analysis (success/failure cases, coherence errors, temporal drift), performance as a function of stream length, and comparison on both automatically computed metrics (e.g., BLEU, METEOR, CIDEr) and human-labeled coherence/naturalness.
- Experimental clarity: Please provide more training details (optimization, regularization, data preprocessing, frame sampling strategy, handling of variable-length streams), and report runtime/complexity to assess scalability.

Questions for the Authors
- How are entity representations obtained and updated across time? Is the coherence signal differentiable and trained end-to-end with the rest of the network, or trained separately and combined at inference?
- What is the role of bidirectionality at inference time if sentences are generated left-to-right? Do you use full-sequence context at test time (making the approach offline), or is there an online/causal variant?
- How robust is CRCN to noisy or long image streams? Have you tested on longer sequences or with irregular sampling intervals?
- Do improvements hold across different domains/datasets, and do they transfer when visual features or language corpora change?

Suggestions
- Expand the related work section and explicitly compare to closely related video-to-text models, clarifying architectural differences and advantages.
- Strengthen human evaluation: increase the number of evaluators, use blind pairwise comparisons and Likert scales for coherence/fluency/relevance, report inter-annotator agreement, and conduct statistical significance tests.
- Add ablations and sensitivity analyses for the coherence module, temporal modeling choices (uni- vs. bidirectional), and visual features.
- Provide qualitative examples with entity chains to illustrate how the coherence model influences generation.
- Include implementation details and, if possible, release code/models to facilitate reproducibility.

Overall Assessment
This is a solid and timely contribution that explicitly targets discourse coherence in sentence generation from image streams. The automatic metrics are promising, and the architectural choices are reasonable. However, the paper currently falls short in related work coverage and in the strength and analysis of human evaluations. With a more comprehensive comparison to prior video-to-text methods, statistical validation of user studies, and ablations isolating the coherence component, the paper would be significantly stronger.

Recommendation: Borderline accept (leaning accept if the empirical analysis and significance testing are strengthened).
