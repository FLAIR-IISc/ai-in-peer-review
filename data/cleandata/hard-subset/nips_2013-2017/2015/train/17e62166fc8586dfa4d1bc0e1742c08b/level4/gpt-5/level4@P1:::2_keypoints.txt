- Paper: Proposes CRCN to generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams using CNN features, a bidirectional RNN over paragraph vectors, and an entity-grid coherence model, trained on web blog data with retrieval-based decoding.

- Strength: Tackles a novel, well-motivated task of multi-sentence image-stream description.

- Strength: Effective multimodal architecture integrating CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based coherence model.

- Strength: Thorough evaluation (BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, AMT studies, qualitative examples) and useful ablations.

- Strength: Promises to release code and datasets, aiding reproducibility and follow-up work.

- Weakness/Question 1: Overstates prior work [5] — should acknowledge it captures topic-level coherence even if not entity-based local coherence.

- Weakness/Question 2: Fig. 2(b) appears inconsistent with Sec.3.3/Eq.(2); clarify whether FC layers operate per-sentence with shared weights or jointly and revise figure/text.

- Weakness/Question 3: Emphasize METEOR results and discuss discrepancies among BLEU, CIDEr, and METEOR, especially with single references.

- Weakness/Question 4: Two stacked linear layers without nonlinearity are redundant unless dropout justifies them — run ablations for (a) one vs two FC layers and (b) dropout rates.

- Weakness/Question 5: Sharing FC parameters between BRNN outputs O and coherence vector q needs justification or an ablation comparing shared vs separate projections.

- Weakness/Question 6: Clarify whether paragraph vectors are fine-tuned end-to-end or kept fixed, discuss stability vs adaptability, and report results for both.

- Assessment — Quality: Model and experiments are solid but need additional ablations for some architectural choices.

- Assessment — Clarity: Generally clear, but fix figure/text mismatch and soften the claim about [5].

- Assessment — Originality: Novel problem setting and novel integration of entity-based coherence with multimodal RNN/CNN.

- Assessment — Significance: Strong empirical results and dataset likely to spur follow-up work.

- Pros for acceptance: Addresses an important underexplored problem, presents a coherent architecture with demonstrated gains, and provides strong empirical validation + resources.

- Cons for acceptance: Overstated related-work claim and figure inconsistency; some architectural choices lack ablation justification.

- Post-rebuttal: Recommends acceptance contingent on incorporating clarifications and explicitly stating whether paragraph vectors are fine-tuned and why.
