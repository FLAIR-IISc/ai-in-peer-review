- Tackles retrieving natural-language sentences for image sequences and assembling them into a sentence sequence; important task related to video multi-sentence description but missing citation to prior video work (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014).  
- Manuscript is clearly written, easy to follow, and the problem is well defined and addressed with solid techniques.  
- Proposed CRCN model learns relationships between image sequences and sentence sequences; architecture appears technically sound and the entity-based coherence component effectively captures discourse relations.  
- Sentence-sequence generation is sensible but somewhat basic/ad hoc: each sentence is retrieved from visually similar training images and concatenated in image order.  
- Dataset and anonymity concern: NYC data newly collected, dataset not released, and referenced paper lacks crawling details—this suggests author overlap with [11] and undermines anonymity.  
- Potential technical error: Equation (2) may be incorrect as written, implying st depends only on ot and questioning network connectivity/formulation.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound approach with an ad hoc assembly step; reviewer would support acceptance as a poster.
