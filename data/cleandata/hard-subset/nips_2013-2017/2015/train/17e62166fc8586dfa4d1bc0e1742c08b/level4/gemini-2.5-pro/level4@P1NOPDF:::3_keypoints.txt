- Task and proposal: Paper addresses generating descriptive sentences from image streams and proposes a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs, a bidirectional RNN, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Strength: The algorithm is novel, well-motivated, and yields strong quantitative results.
- Missing related work: Literature review is incomplete and omits relevant video-to-sentence papers (e.g., "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework").
- Human evaluation weakness: The user study results (Table 2) show CRCN performing similarly to the RCN baseline, indicating only marginal perceptual improvement.
- Statistical testing needed: Authors should perform statistical significance tests on the human evaluation to confirm whether CRCN’s advantage over the baseline is reliable.
- Overall recommendation: Solid contribution but would be more convincing with a more thorough literature discussion and a more rigorous, significance-tested human evaluation.
