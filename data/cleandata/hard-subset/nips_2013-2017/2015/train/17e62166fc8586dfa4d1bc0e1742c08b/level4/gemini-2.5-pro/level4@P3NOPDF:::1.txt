This paper addresses the problem of generating a coherent sequence of natural sentences, or a "story," to describe a given sequence of images. This is a significant and challenging problem at the intersection of computer vision and natural language processing. The paper is well-written, and the proposed approach is explained clearly.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Problem Significance:** The task of generating narrative descriptions for image sequences is an important step beyond single-image captioning and has numerous potential applications. The paper motivates this problem well.

2.  **Technically Sound Model:** The proposed Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) is a novel and technically sound architecture. It effectively models the relationships between an image sequence and a corresponding sentence sequence.

3.  **Discourse and Coherence:** A key strength of the paper is its explicit modeling of discourse relationships. The integration of an entity-based coherence model to improve the narrative quality of the generated text is a strong contribution and helps produce more natural-sounding stories.

**Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement:**

1.  **Missing Related Work:** The paper fails to cite or compare its work to the highly relevant field of video description (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014, "A Dataset for Movie Description"). A video can be conceptualized as a sequence of images (frames or keyframes from shots), making the task fundamentally similar. The omission of this entire line of research is a significant weakness, as it prevents a clear understanding of how this work advances the state of the art in sequential visual description.

2.  **Simplistic Generation Method:** While the CRCN model itself is well-designed for scoring the quality of a story, the method used for generating new stories is rather simplistic. The approach retrieves sentences associated with visually similar training images and concatenates them in the order of the input images. This retrieval-and-concatenation method seems ad-hoc and may limit the model's ability to generate novel and diverse descriptions that are not closely tied to the training data.

3.  **Reproducibility and Dataset:** There are serious concerns regarding the reproducibility of the experiments. The authors reuse a dataset from their previous work [11] and create a new one using the same crawling method. However, the details of this crawling method are not provided in either paper, and more importantly, the datasets have not been made publicly available. This lack of transparency makes it impossible for other researchers to verify the reported results or build upon this work.

4.  **Potential Anonymity Issue:** The heavy reliance on an undescribed data collection method from a single prior publication [11] strongly suggests that the authors of both papers are the same. While not a direct violation, this makes it difficult to maintain the integrity of the double-blind review process.

5.  **Clarity of Equation (2):** There appears to be an error or typo in Equation (2). As written, it implies that the state `s_t` is derived only from the output `o_t`, which would mean there is no recurrent connection from the previous state `s_{t-1}`. This would undermine the "recurrent" nature of the network. The authors should clarify this formulation.

**Recommendation:**

Overall, this paper presents an interesting approach to a challenging problem. The core model (CRCN) and the use of a coherence model are valuable contributions. However, the work is let down by a simplistic generation strategy, a significant gap in the related work, and major concerns about reproducibility due to the private dataset.

Despite these weaknesses, the core ideas are novel enough to be of interest to the community. Therefore, I recommend accepting this paper as a poster. The authors should be strongly encouraged to address the issues raised above in the final version, particularly by releasing their dataset and code, adding a thorough discussion of the video description literature, and clarifying the technical details of their model.
