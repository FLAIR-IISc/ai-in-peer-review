- Problem & contribution: Generates coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences via a novel retrieval + sentence-selection architecture with BRNN and a local coherence model.  
- Method summary: For each query image, retrieve K=5 similar training images/sentences, embed candidate sentences, model their sequence with a bidirectional LSTM, pass outputs through ReLU FC, and compute image–sequence compatibility.  
- Strength: Effectively integrates sentence coherence, showing clear gains over baselines via quantitative results, qualitative cases, human evaluation, and an ablation removing the coherence model.  
- Strength: Authors commit to releasing source code and dataset.  
- Weakness 1: Incorrectly claims related work [5] lacks coherence mechanism—[5] implicitly achieves coherence via an intermediate semantic video/topic representation.  
- Weakness 2: Figure 2b (FC connected to all sentences) contradicts Equation (2) (shared parameters applied to a single sentence); figure, text, and equation must be aligned.  
- Weakness 3: BLEU is a suboptimal evaluation metric for single-reference generation; METEOR should be used or BLEU justified.  
- Weakness 4: Two sequential linear layers (W_f2, W_f1) are redundant since their composition is linear; need ablation to justify this design.  
- Weakness 5: Sharing identical FC parameters for BRNN output o_t and coherence output q is unexplained and requires justification.  
- Weakness 6: Unclear whether paragraph vectors [16] are fine-tuned or kept fixed—must be specified.  
- Post-rebuttal: Authors satisfactorily addressed all concerns—clarified related work, fixed figure/equation mismatch, justified linear layers with ablation, explained parameter sharing, confirmed paragraph vectors are fine-tuned, and added METEOR scores.  
- Final recommendation: Well-motivated, novel, and convincingly validated work that meaningfully advances coherent image-sequence description; recommended for acceptance.
