This paper addresses the problem of retrieving natural sentences that describe a given sequence of images and forming a sequence of sentences, a significant problem in the research fields of computer vision and pattern recognition. Notably, this problem is closely related to generating multiple sentences that describe given video content, as explored in previous works (e.g., [Rohrbach+ 2014]), where single video content can be regarded as a sequence of images, with each video shot describable by a key frame. However, the authors fail to cite relevant research in this area.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clearly stated problem and a solid set of techniques proposed to solve it. The proposed neural network model, CRCN, effectively captures relationships between a sequence of natural sentences and a sequence of images. The model's architecture is technically sound, and it successfully describes discourse relationships using entity-based coherence models.

However, the method for generating a sequence of natural sentences is somewhat primitive, relying on directly associating each sentence with training images similar to a given image, and then concatenating these sentences in the order of the corresponding images.

A concern regarding data collection is raised in Section 4.1, where the authors state, "We reuse the blog data of Disneyland from the dataset of [11], and newly collect the data of NYC, using the same crawling method with [11]." The dataset is not publicly disclosed, and the corresponding paper does not provide details on the data crawling method. This lack of transparency raises questions about the authors' relationship to the previous work [11] and potentially compromises the anonymity of the submission.

Furthermore, Equation (2) appears to be incorrect, as it suggests that st can be derived solely from ot, implying that the network is not fully connected. Despite this, the proposed neural network model is technically sound and effectively describes discourse relationships using entity-based coherence models. However, the method for generating a sequence of sentences for a given image stream is rather ad-hoc. Overall, I believe that this paper can be accepted as a poster paper in its current form.
