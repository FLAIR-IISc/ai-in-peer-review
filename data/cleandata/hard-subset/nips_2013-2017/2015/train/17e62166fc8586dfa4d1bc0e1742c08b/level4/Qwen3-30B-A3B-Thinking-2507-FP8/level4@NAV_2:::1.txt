

This paper addresses the problem of retrieving sequences of natural sentences that describe a given image stream, extending the typical single-image to single-sentence captioning task. The authors propose a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) that integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The work is motivated by real-world user behavior of capturing sequences of images during memorable experiences, such as visiting tourist attractions.

The paper makes a valuable contribution by addressing an under-explored problem in image captioning. The CRCN architecture is technically sound, with the coherence model providing a novel way to ensure fluent transitions between sentences. The evaluation using both quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk demonstrates clear improvements over strong baselines.

Strengths:
- The problem is well-motivated by practical user behavior
- The CRCN architecture is well-designed and integrates multiple relevant techniques
- The evaluation methodology is comprehensive and well-executed
- The paper is clearly written and easy to follow

Weaknesses:
- The related work section does not adequately cite and distinguish from video description research (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014), which directly addresses generating descriptions for sequences of images. Since video content can be viewed as a sequence of images, this line of work is highly relevant but not properly acknowledged.
- The sentence generation method is somewhat primitive, relying on nearest neighbor search and simple concatenation rather than a more sophisticated sequence generation approach.
- The data collection method for the NYC dataset lacks sufficient detail; the authors reuse the Disneyland dataset from [11] but do not provide details about their crawling method beyond "using the same crawling method with [11]". This lack of transparency raises concerns about data provenance.
- Equation (2) appears to have a potential issue as it suggests st can be derived solely from ot, which may indicate a lack of full connectivity in the network.

The paper presents a technically sound approach to an important problem extension. While the work is solid, the lack of connection to relevant video description research and the data collection concerns are notable weaknesses.

Pro arguments for acceptance:
- The extension from single-image to image stream description is a meaningful contribution with practical relevance
- The CRCN architecture is a well-integrated solution to the problem
- The evaluation demonstrates clear improvements over baselines

Con arguments for acceptance:
- The related work section should better acknowledge and distinguish from video description research
- The sentence generation method could be more sophisticated
- The data collection method requires more transparency

Given the technical soundness of the approach and the significance of the problem extension, I recommend acceptance as a poster presentation. The authors should address the related work connection and data collection transparency in the final version.
