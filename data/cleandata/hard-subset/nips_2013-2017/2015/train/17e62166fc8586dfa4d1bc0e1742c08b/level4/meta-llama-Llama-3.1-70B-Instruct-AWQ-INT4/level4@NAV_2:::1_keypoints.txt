- Addresses a significant problem: retrieving a sequence of natural sentences describing an image stream (multimodal sequence-to-sequence).
- Novel multimodal architecture (CRCN) that combines CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Authors provide a clear, concise presentation and relate their approach to prior work.
- Quantitative evaluation uses BLEU and top-K recall; user studies conducted on Amazon Mechanical Turk.
- Demonstrates empirical improvement over baseline methods (log-bilinear multimodal and recurrent convolutional models).
- Technical design is generally sound and well-explained.
- Equation (2) appears incorrect, suggesting an output depends on only one input despite a fully connected network.
- Sentence-generation method is primitive, relying on concatenating sentences from similar training images.
- Lacks detailed comparison with prior work on multi-sentence video description generation.
- Paper may violate anonymity and uses a non-public dataset, limiting reproducibility.
- Overall well-written; recommended for acceptance as a poster subject to revisions addressing comparisons, generation method, and Equation (2).
