- Problem: Extends image captioning from single images to generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams.  
- Proposed method: Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Training data: Uses a large naturally-sourced dataset from blog posts (≈140K images, 20K posts).  
- Evaluation: Shows strong quantitative performance (BLEU, top-K recall) and includes user studies on Amazon Mechanical Turk.  
- Motivation: Clearly articulated relevance for capturing context across related images (e.g., events).  
- Novelty: Reasonable extension of established vision and language techniques with a novel coherence component.  
- Strength: Well-designed architecture integrating image and language models with coherence.  
- Strength: Effective use of a large, naturally-sourced dataset and thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Weakness: Related work in video captioning is omitted despite relevance.  
- Weakness: User study shows only marginal preference over RCN for short streams; statistical significance tests are missing.  
- Weakness: Lacks a detailed ablation study to isolate the contribution of the coherence model and other components.  
- Argument for acceptance: Addresses a novel, important problem with an effective architecture and convincing results.  
- Argument against acceptance: Missing related work, weak statistical support in user studies, and insufficient ablation analysis.
