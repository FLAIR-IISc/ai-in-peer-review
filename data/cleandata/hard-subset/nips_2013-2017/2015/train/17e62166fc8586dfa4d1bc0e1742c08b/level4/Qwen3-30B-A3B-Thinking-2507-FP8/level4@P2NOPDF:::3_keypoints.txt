- Proposes CRCN combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model for image-stream sentence generation.  
- Addresses semantic consistency by explicitly modeling entity transitions across sentences.  
- Quantitative results (Table 1) show strong BLEU performance against baselines but gains are small (1.2% over RCN).  
- Related work misses key prior work (e.g., Li et al. 2015), weakening comparison to unified vision-language frameworks.  
- Human evaluation (Table 2) shows CRCN is statistically indistinguishable from RCN (p > 0.05); claims of “weak advantage” lack statistical validation.  
- No significance testing (e.g., t-tests, confidence intervals) is reported to substantiate claimed improvements.  
- Entity-based coherence model lacks empirical justification—no ablation study isolating its impact.  
- User study sample size (100) is too small for robust conclusions; recommend ≥300 samples or a power analysis.  
- Writing includes subjective phrases (“looks quite good,” “nice algorithm”) that undermine objectivity.  
- Required revisions: add missing literature, perform statistical tests, include ablations for the entity model, increase user-study rigor, and revise language to focus on measurable evidence.
