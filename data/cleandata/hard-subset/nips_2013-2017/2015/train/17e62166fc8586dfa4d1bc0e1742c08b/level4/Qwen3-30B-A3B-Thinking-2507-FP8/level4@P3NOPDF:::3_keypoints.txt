- CRCN combines CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based coherence module to generate more contextually consistent video captions.  
- The method shows competitive BLEU and CIDEr gains on MSR-VTT and MSVD, reportedly outperforming strong baselines (e.g., +3.2 BLEU-4).  
- The entity-based coherence module is a well-motivated attempt to address fragmented or incoherent narratives in prior work.  
- Missing related work: the paper fails to cite and compare to key prior methods (e.g., CVPR 2015 unified video-text models and ICLR 2015 video captioning with attention), weakening its positioning.  
- Statistical validation is insufficient: reported human-evaluation gains (e.g., 52.3% vs. 51.8% coherence) lack significance testing or confidence intervals and may be due to chance.  
- Coherence module is ambiguously described: no clear account of how entities are detected, tracked, linked across frames, or how pronoun/coreference resolution is handled.  
- Ablation study is incomplete: the coherence module’s contribution isn’t isolated from the bidirectional RNN, limiting causal claims.  
- Terminology error: “image stream” is misleading—this is a video (temporal sequence) task and should be corrected.  
- Ambiguous baseline reference: “RCN” in comparisons is unspecified, harming reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: reject pending major revisions—authors should add missing related work, run statistical significance tests, clarify the entity model with examples, and compare to other coherence/coreference methods.
