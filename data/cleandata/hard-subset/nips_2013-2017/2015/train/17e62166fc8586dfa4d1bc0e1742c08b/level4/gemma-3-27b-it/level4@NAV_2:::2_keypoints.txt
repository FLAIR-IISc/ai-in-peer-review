- Problem & goal: Generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences by retrieving and composing sentences from training data.
- Method overview: For each query image retrieve top K=5 image-sentence pairs, embed sentences, model sentence sequence with a bidirectional LSTM, apply ReLU+FC, score image-sentence compatibility, and add a local coherence model.
- Main contribution: Novel method to select and rank relevant sentences for an image sequence to produce coherent descriptions.
- Strength: Proposed CRCN architecture is novel and effective for retrieving coherent sentences.
- Strength: Extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations plus ablation study showing benefits of coherence modeling.
- Strength: Authors plan to release source code and dataset.
- Weakness 1: Incorrect claim that prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” — [5] models intermediate semantic representations that encourage coherence.
- Weakness 2: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2); unclear whether FC layers connect to all sentences or share parameters per sentence.
- Weakness 3: Automatic evaluation should use METEOR rather than BLEU when comparing to a single reference.
- Weakness 4: Use of two linear functions (Wf1, Wf2) in Eq. 2 is unclear since their composition is equivalent to one linear layer; ablation needed.
- Weakness 5: Unclear why same FC parameters are used for BRNN outputs (ot) and local coherence model (q) in Eq. 2.
- Weakness 6: It is unclear whether paragraph vectors [17] are fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.
- Acceptance argument: Addresses a novel problem with a well-motivated architecture and convincing empirical and human-evaluation results.
- Rejection argument: Several technical clarifications and one inaccurate literature claim must be resolved for full acceptance.
- Overall assessment: Significant, well-evaluated contribution that requires a few clarifications to be fully convincing.
