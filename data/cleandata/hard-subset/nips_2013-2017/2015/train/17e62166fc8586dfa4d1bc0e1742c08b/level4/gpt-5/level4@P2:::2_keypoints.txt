- Summary: Proposes CRCN to generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams by retrieving candidate sentences and scoring sequences using CNN image features, a BRNN over paragraph-vector sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid coherence model, optimized with a max-margin objective and beam search; evaluated on large NYC/Disneyland blog datasets with BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and AMT studies showing improvements.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on image-captioning and retrieval-based caption transfer, closest to sequence/video models and entity-based coherence; differs by enforcing explicit local coherence for still-image streams and by generating paragraphs for image streams rather than retrieving image sequences for text.

- Strength: Novel problem setup and architecture integrating BRNN and entity-grid coherence into a unified retrieval-and-ranking framework.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation with multiple automatic metrics, ablations isolating coherence, and user studies demonstrating fluency/coherence benefits.

- Strength: Uses large, practical blog-derived datasets with substantial preprocessing and commits to releasing code/data for reproducibility.

- Weakness: Overstates comparison to [5]; needs to qualify that [5] models topic/semantic structure which can impart cross-sentence consistency.

- Weakness: Figure 2b vs. Eq. 2 mismatch—clarify whether FC layers take joint sentence inputs or operate per-sentence with shared parameters and how tensors are concatenated.

- Weakness: Evaluation metrics—ensure METEOR is used consistently and report statistical significance where feasible.

- Weakness: Architectural justification—two stacked linear layers should be justified (ablation) or include a nonlinearity, and clarify dropout’s role at test time.

- Weakness: Parameter sharing—justify/shared FC parameters between BRNN outputs and coherence vector or provide ablation comparing separate parameterizations.

- Weakness: Paragraph vectors—state whether doc2vec embeddings are fine-tuned end-to-end or kept fixed, and report effects.

- Weakness: Reproducibility/clarity—provide implementation details on κ in data augmentation, parse/mention resolution quality for entity grid, sensitivity to K and beam size, and handling variable-length streams.

- Assessment (Quality): Model and training objective are sound and experiments thorough, but some architectural choices need clearer justification or ablations.

- Assessment (Clarity): Generally well written but must fix Fig. 2b vs. Eq. 2 and add minor preprocessing/training details for reproducibility.

- Assessment (Originality): Novel integration of entity-based coherence into neural sequence scoring for image streams.

- Assessment (Significance): Addresses an important problem with demonstrated gains and potential practical impact, amplified by planned resource release.

- Pros for acceptance: Tackles an underexplored, meaningful task with principled architecture, strong empirical and human-evaluation results, and clear contributions beyond single-image captioning.

- Cons against acceptance: Some prior-work claims need precision, key architectural choices lack justification or ablation, and there are minor clarity issues in figure–equation correspondence.

- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance after authors addressed formulation, evaluation, and related-work concerns; final version should incorporate clarifications and explicitly state paragraph-vector fine-tuning.
