- Tackles a novel and important problem: generating multi-sentence narratives for image streams rather than single-image captions.  
- Proposes the Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN image features, a Bidirectional RNN for text flow, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Leverages large-scale blog posts to create a parallel image–text corpus, avoiding costly manual annotation.  
- Introduces two new datasets (NYC and Disneyland) for the task.  
- Demonstrates CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art methods on retrieval and language metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, R@K).  
- Provides a comprehensive evaluation including automatic metrics and human studies on Amazon Mechanical Turk.  
- Architecture is well-motivated: explicit entity-based coherence addresses multi-sentence fluency challenges.  
- Related work discussion is incomplete—should better connect to video-description literature and contrast sparse image streams vs. dense video.  
- User study improvement of CRCN over RCN is modest (54% vs 46% for short sequences) and needs statistical significance testing to confirm reliability.  
- Overall assessment: strong novelty, solid technical contribution, and thorough evaluation justify acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
