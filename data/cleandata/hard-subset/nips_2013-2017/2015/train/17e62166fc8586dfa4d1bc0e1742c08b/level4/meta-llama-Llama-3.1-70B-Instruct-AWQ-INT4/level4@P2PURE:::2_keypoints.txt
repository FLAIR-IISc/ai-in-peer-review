- Task and method: Generates coherent sentence sequences for image streams by retrieving top-5 similar image–sentence pairs, embedding sentences, modeling sentence sequences with a bidirectional LSTM, applying ReLU+FC, computing image–sentence compatibility, and adding a local coherence model.

- Main contribution: Novel approach to select relevant sentences for an image sequence to produce coherent descriptions.

- Strength: Proposes an effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences across an image sequence.

- Strength: Provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations showing superiority over baselines; includes an ablation removing the coherence model.

- Strength: Commits to releasing source code and dataset.

- Weakness: Claim that prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is disputed — [5] models intermediate semantic representations that provide cross-sentence coherence.

- Weakness: Figure 2b vs. Section 3.3/Equation (2) unclear — diagram implies FC layers connect to all sentences, while equation implies shared parameters applied per-sentence; needs clarification.

- Weakness: Evaluation metric choice — BLEU is questionable with single references; METEOR may be more appropriate.

- Weakness: Use of two successive linear functions (W_f2 and W_f1) in Equation (2) is unclear/redundant; an ablation is needed to justify them.

- Weakness: Clarify why the same FC layer parameters are used for BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence model q (Equation 2).

- Weakness: Unclear whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed; requires explicit clarification.

- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance after rebuttal addressed formulation, evaluation, and related-work issues, but insists on clarifying the paragraph vector training and other points.
