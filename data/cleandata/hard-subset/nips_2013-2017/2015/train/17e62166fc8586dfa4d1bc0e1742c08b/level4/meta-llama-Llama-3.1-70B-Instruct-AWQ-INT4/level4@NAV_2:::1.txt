This paper proposes a novel approach to retrieving a sequence of natural sentences that describe a given image stream. The problem is significant in the research fields of computer vision and natural language processing, and the authors extend previous work by considering both input and output dimensions to be sequences of images and sentences.

The authors provide a clear and concise summary of their main ideas, relating them to previous work in the field. They discuss the strengths and weaknesses of their approach, highlighting the novelty of their multimodal architecture, called coherent recurrent convolutional networks (CRCN). The CRCN integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model to capture the relationships between images and sentences.

The authors evaluate their approach using quantitative measures, such as BLEU and top-K recall, and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk. They demonstrate that their approach outperforms other state-of-the-art candidate methods, including log-bilinear multimodal models and recurrent convolutional models.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with previous work on generating multiple sentences that describe video content, which is closely related to their problem. Additionally, the method for generating a sequence of natural sentences is somewhat primitive, relying on concatenating sentences associated with similar training images.

In terms of technical soundness, the proposed neural network model is well-designed, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their architecture. However, Equation (2) appears to be incorrect, as it implies that the output can be derived from only one of the inputs, which is not the case in a fully connected network.

Overall, the paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the authors provide a solid contribution to the field. However, there are some limitations and areas for improvement, particularly in terms of comparing with related work and refining the sentence generation method. I would recommend accepting this paper as a poster paper, with some revisions to address these issues.
