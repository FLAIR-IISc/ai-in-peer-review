- Presents CRCN: CNN image features + BRNN sentence flow + entity-based local coherence for describing image streams with retrieved sentences.  
- Novelty: explicit entity-grid-style local coherence integrated into a multimodal retrieval framework for paragraphs.  
- Candidate sentences retrieved from visually similar images and assembled via divide-and-conquer beam search under a max-margin objective.  
- Data built from segmented and summarized blog posts using LSA, doc2vec embeddings, and coreference parsing.  
- Strength: extends single-image captioning to image-stream–paragraph generation, a practically important task.  
- Strength: technically sound model combining visual features, BRNN, and coherence signal; coherence is well-motivated for discontinuous image streams.  
- Strength: scalable data-construction pipeline and solid engineering choices (BPTT, RMSprop, dropout, initialization).  
- Strength: consistent improvements on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, and AMT user preferences, especially for longer sequences.  
- Weakness: retrieval-only candidate generation limits expressivity, propagates dataset bias, and fails when correct sentences are absent.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion and direct comparison to multi-sentence video-description (video-to-paragraph) literature and baselines.  
- Weakness: Eq. (2) fusion appears to underutilize coherence — unclear interaction between sentence content and coherence features.  
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns — newly crawled NYC dataset lacks crawl/preprocessing details and reuse of prior proprietary data risks de-anonymization.  
- Weakness: standard metrics (BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR) do not fully capture paragraph-level fluency/coherence; AMT helpful but automatic coherence metrics and significance reporting needed.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to travel blogs (NYC/Disneyland), so claims of domain-agnosticism need cross-domain validation.  
- Quality: model and optimization are sound, but more ablations needed (fusion design, κ effect, doc2vec vs other encoders).  
- Clarity issues: need precise description of entity-grid construction, coreference resolution, doc2vec training details, and divide-and-conquer search parameters.  
- Originality: meaningful contribution in combining entity-based coherence with multimodal CNN–BRNN for image-stream description; retrieval aspect itself is not new.  
- Significance: practically relevant with convincing gains, but impact would increase with broader evaluation and public data/code release.  
- Suggestions: strengthen coherence-content fusion (condition st on q, attention/gating), explore hybrid generation/paraphrasing, add automatic coherence metrics and statistical significance, and release data/code or provide exact crawling recipes.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept (poster) — important and solid work but needs clearer fusion design, reproducibility fixes, and broader evaluations.
