- Problem: generates multi-sentence natural language descriptions for image streams, extending beyond single-image captioning.  
- Proposed model: Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN image encoders, bidirectional RNN language models, and an entity-based local coherence component.  
- Training data: leverages blog posts as naturally occurring image–text sequence pairs.  
- Technical quality: strong integration of established components applied novelly to sequence-to-sequence image description.  
- Experiments: comprehensive evaluation using BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR/top-K recall and user studies on Amazon Mechanical Turk.  
- Results: CRCN outperforms strong baselines, with notable gains in retrieval metrics.  
- Writing/clarity: generally well-written with clear motivation and helpful figures, though Sections 3.1–3.3 need more intuitive explanation of coherence integration.  
- Originality: meaningful contribution by focusing on image-stream sequence generation, an underexplored problem.  
- Related work gap: insufficient distinction from video captioning literature and limited discussion of how image streams differ from videos.  
- Data methodology weakness: incomplete description of dataset collection (e.g., NYC crawl details) and dataset availability not addressed.  
- User study weakness: short image sequences (5 images) may not adequately test the coherence model’s benefits for longer sequences.  
- Practical significance: promising applications (travelogues, social media summarization, photo albums) and realistic use of blog data.  
- Coherence model: entity-based component effectively promotes fluent transitions between consecutive sentences.  
- Scope limitation: experiments largely focused on tourism-related blog posts, potentially limiting generality.  
- Recommendation: technically sound and practically valuable; acceptance recommended after clarifying video-caption distinction and dataset collection details.
