This work investigates sentence generation from image streams. The authors propose a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN), comprising convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model.

Overall, this is a strong contribution, although it could be improved in several areas. First, the related work section omits several relevant papers in the video-to-sentence domain, such as “[Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework]”. 

While the quantitative results appear promising, the user study presented in Table 2 demonstrates performance comparable to one baseline, RCN. A statistical significance test is necessary to determine whether the observed improvement is reliable. 

In summary, the proposed algorithm represents a well-designed approach to sentence generation from image streams. The quantitative results are encouraging, but the user study reveals only a marginal advantage over existing baselines.
