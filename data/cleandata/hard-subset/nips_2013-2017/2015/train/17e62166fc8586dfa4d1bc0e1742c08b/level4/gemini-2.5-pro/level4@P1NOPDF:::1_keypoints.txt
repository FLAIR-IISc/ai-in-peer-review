- Addresses an important problem of retrieving sentence sequences for image sequences, related to video description but missing citations to that literature.
- Paper is well-written, clearly states the problem, and builds the solution on solid techniques.
- Proposes CRCN, a technically sound neural architecture for modeling image-sequence to sentence-sequence relationships.
- Strength: effective modeling of discourse/coherence via an entity-based coherence model.
- Sentence generation is simplistic and ad-hoc—selecting sentences by nearest training images and concatenating them.
- Dataset and reproducibility issues: reuse of non-public blog data and unclear crawling method hinder reproducibility.
- Anonymity/double-blind concern: dataset statement suggests the authors may be the same as a cited non-public dataset, compromising review integrity.
- Possible technical error in Equation (2): it implies state s_t depends only on output o_t, questioning recurrence.
- Overall: core ideas are meritorious but weakened by generation, related-work, and reproducibility/anonymity issues.
- Recommendation: accept as a poster.
