- Paper tackles generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences using a retrieval of image-sentence pairs plus sequence modeling to select and order sentences.  
- Core contribution: combines image-sentence compatibility scoring with a local coherence model to maintain semantic consistency.  
- Strength: novel and effective architecture that integrates retrieval- and generation-based techniques.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation (quantitative, qualitative, human study) and ablation showing importance of the coherence model.  
- Strength: authors commit to releasing code and dataset, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: claim about prior work [5] lacking coherence is overstated; [5] models intermediate semantic representations that imply topic coherence.  
- Weakness: Figure 2b seems inconsistent with Section 3.3 / Equation (2); clarification needed about connections and parameter sharing.  
- Weakness: BLEU may be inappropriate with single references; METEOR should be considered instead.  
- Weakness: Equation (2) uses two consecutive linear functions (Wf2 and Wf1) that could be collapsed; need ablation to justify design.  
- Weakness: unclear why fully connected layer parameters are shared between the bi-RNN output and the local coherence model; rationale required.  
- Weakness / question: unclear whether paragraph vector [16] was fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer recommends acceptance after authors addressed concerns; asks final paper to explicitly state whether the paragraph vector was fine-tuned.
