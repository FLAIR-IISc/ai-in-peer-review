- Task: Generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences (e.g., blog posts) via retrieval of sentences from similar images.
- Method: For each query image retrieve K=5 similar images and their sentences, embed sentences, model sentence sequence with a bidirectional LSTM, apply ReLU and fully connected layers, and score compatibility with the image.
- Coherence: Incorporates a local coherence model to enforce compatibility between adjacent sentences.
- Contribution: Novel architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for image streams.
- Evaluation: Comprehensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations plus an ablation study (removing the local coherence model) showing superiority over baselines.
- Reproducibility: Authors commit to releasing source code and dataset.
- Weakness: Claim that prior work [5] lacks coherence mechanism is disputed since [5] models intermediate semantic representations that provide coherence.
- Clarity issue: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2) regarding fully connected layer connections vs. parameter sharing.
- Evaluation metric: Suggests METEOR would be more appropriate than BLEU when only a single reference sentence is available.
- Model design question: Unclear why two sequential linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) are used in Equation (2) since they can be collapsed; recommends ablation to justify.
- Parameter sharing question: Unclear why the same FC layer parameters are used for both BiRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence model q.
- Training detail missing: Whether the paragraph vector [16] was fine-tuned or kept fixed during training is not stated.
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance after authors addressed concerns, but requests final clarification on paragraph vector fine-tuning.
