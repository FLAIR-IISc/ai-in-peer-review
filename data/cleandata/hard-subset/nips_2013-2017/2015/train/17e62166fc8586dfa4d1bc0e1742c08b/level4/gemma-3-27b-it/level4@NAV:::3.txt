This paper presents a method for generating sentences from image streams, introducing a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) comprised of convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. 

Overall, this is a promising contribution. However, the manuscript could benefit from a more comprehensive review of related work in the video-to-sentence generation field, particularly including studies like those that jointly model deep video and compositional text. While the quantitative results appear favorable, the user study presented in Table 2 indicates performance comparable to the RCN baseline. A statistical significance test is necessary to confirm the reliability of any observed improvements. In summary, the proposed algorithm demonstrates potential for sentence generation from image streams, but the user study reveals only a marginal advantage over existing approaches.
