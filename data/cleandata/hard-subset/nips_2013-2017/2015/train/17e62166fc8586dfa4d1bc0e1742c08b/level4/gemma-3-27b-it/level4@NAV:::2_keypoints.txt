- Task: generate a coherent sequence of sentences describing an input sequence of images (e.g., blog posts).
- Approach: retrieve K=5 visually similar images and their sentences per input image, embed sentences, model the sequence with a bidirectional LSTM, apply ReLU+fully connected layers to compute image–sentence compatibility, and incorporate a local coherence model between selected sentences.
- Contribution: a method to select the most relevant and coherent sentences from retrieved candidates to form a fluent, contextually appropriate description.
- Strength: novel, effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences from image sequences.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation (quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, human eval) and an ablation study showing the coherence model’s impact.
- Strength: authors will release source code and dataset.
- Weakness 1: claim that prior work [5] lacks coherence is questionable since [5] uses an intermediate semantic representation that promotes coherence.
- Weakness 2: Figure 2b visualization appears inconsistent with Section 3.3 and Eq.(2) regarding FC connectivity versus parameter sharing.
- Weakness 3: with a single reference sentence, METEOR may be a more appropriate automatic evaluation metric than BLEU.
- Weakness 4: the use of two sequential linear functions (W_f2, W_f1) in Eq.(2) is unclear since they can be collapsed; an ablation is suggested.
- Weakness 5: unclear why FC layer parameters are shared for both the bi-RNN output (o_t) and the local coherence component (q).
- Weakness 6: unclear whether the paragraph vector [16] was fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.
- Post-rebuttal: authors clarified formulation, evaluation, related work, and that paragraph vector [16] is kept fixed; reviewer recommends acceptance but requests clearer exposition for a polished paper.
