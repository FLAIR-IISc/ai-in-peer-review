The paper attacks the problem of describing a sequence of images from blog posts with a sequence of consistent sentences. To address this, the paper proposes first retrieving the K=5 most similar images and associated sentences from the training set for each query image. The main contribution lies in defining a method to select the most relevant sentences for the query image sequence, providing a coherent description. For this, sentences are first embedded into a vector space, and then the sequence of sentences is modeled with a bidirectional LSTM. The output of the bidirectional LSTM is fed through ReLU and fully connected layers, then scored with a compatibility score between image and sentence. Additionally, a local coherence model [1] is included to enforce compatibility between consecutive sentences.

**Strengths / Positive Aspects:**

*   The paper proposes a novel and effective architecture to retrieve coherent sentences for an image sequence.
*   The paper provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluation, demonstrating the superiority of their approach against several baselines, especially those *without* coherence modeling. An ablation experiment removing the coherence model [1] further supports this claim.
*   The authors promise to release source code and the dataset, which will facilitate reproducibility and future research.

**Weaknesses / Questions / Unclarities:**

1.  **Related Work (Line 94):** The paper claims [5] lacks a mechanism for coherence between sentences. However, [5] *does* predict an intermediate semantic representation of videos, which inherently encourages coherence across sentences by modeling the overall topic of the multi-sentence description. This claim should be re-evaluated.
2.  **Figure Clarity (Figure 2b & Equation 2):** Figure 2b does not accurately reflect the description in Section 3.3 and Equation (2). The figure implies fully connected layers connect to *all* sentences, whereas Equation (2) suggests shared parameters across sentences, connected to a vector representing a single sentence. This discrepancy requires clarification.
3.  **Evaluation Metric:** While BLEU is used, METEOR (Lavie et al., 2014) is often a more reliable metric for evaluating natural language generation, especially with a single reference sentence, as it considers stemming and synonymy. Consider reporting METEOR scores in addition to or instead of BLEU.
4.  **Linear Function Redundancy (Equation 2):** The use of two linear functions (W<sub>f2</sub>, W<sub>f1</sub>) sequentially in Equation (2) is unclear. Since two linear functions can be collapsed into a single one, the benefit of this structure is not immediately apparent. An ablation study demonstrating the impact of these layers would strengthen the argument for their inclusion.
5.  **Parameter Sharing (Equation 2):** Why are the same parameters of the fully connected layers used for the bidirectional RNN output (o<sub>t</sub>) and the local coherence model q (Equation 2)? The rationale behind this parameter sharing is not explained.
6.  **Paragraph Vector Training:** Is the paragraph vector [17] fine-tuned during training, or is it kept fixed? This detail is important for understanding the model’s learning process.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents an interesting approach to a challenging problem – generating coherent descriptions for image streams. The proposed architecture, CRCN, shows promising results, and the extensive evaluation supports the claim of its effectiveness. However, several points related to clarity, correctness, and justification require attention. Addressing these concerns will significantly strengthen the paper.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   Novelty: The work tackles an under-addressed problem and proposes a unique solution.
*   Strong Empirical Results: The model outperforms strong baselines across multiple evaluation metrics, including user studies.
*   Ablation Study: Demonstrates the contribution of the coherence model.
*   Potential Impact: The ability to generate coherent descriptions for image streams has applications in various domains, such as automated storytelling and image retrieval.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Minor Technical Inaccuracies: Issues with related work claims and figure clarity.
*   Lack of Justification: Unclear rationale for certain design choices (e.g., two linear layers, parameter sharing).
*   Limited Evaluation Metric: Sole reliance on BLEU may not fully capture the quality of the generated text.



**Post-Rebuttal Assessment:**

Having reviewed the rebuttal, I recommend this paper for acceptance. The authors have successfully addressed the concerns raised regarding the formulation, evaluation, and related work. They clarified the inaccuracies in the initial claims and provided satisfactory explanations for the architectural choices. The promise of releasing the code and dataset further enhances the value of this work. The paper proposes an interesting new model to retrieve coherent sentences for an image stream and convincingly evaluates it.
