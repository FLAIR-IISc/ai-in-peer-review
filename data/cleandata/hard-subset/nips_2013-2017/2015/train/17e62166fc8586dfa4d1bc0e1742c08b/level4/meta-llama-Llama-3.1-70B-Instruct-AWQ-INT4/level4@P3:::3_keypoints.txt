- Proposes CRCN, a multimodal architecture combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model for retrieving sentence sequences for image streams.  
- Trained on a large unstructured blog dataset (20K posts, 140K images).  
- Task motivation: generate a sequence of natural-language sentences describing a user’s photo stream; extends both input and output to sequences.  
- Evaluation uses quantitative metrics (BLEU, top-K recall) and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.  
- Quantitative results show CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art baselines (log-bilinear multimodal, RCN).  
- User study results are similar to RCN in Table 2, requiring a significance test to confirm improvements.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of the approach and well-presented experiments.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion of related work, notably video-to-sentence generation (missing relevant prior work).  
- Overall contribution is solid and promising; minor revisions could make it a strong paper.  
- Strengths for acceptance: novel sequence-to-sequence extension, thorough evaluation, competitive experimental results.  
- Weaknesses for acceptance: insufficient related-work coverage and missing statistical validation of user-study claims.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
