- Proposes CRCN, a multimodal architecture for retrieving sequences of sentences for image streams, extending beyond single image–sentence tasks.  
- CRCN integrates CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Trained on a large blog-post dataset (20K+ posts, ~140K images).  
- Novel and well-motivated approach with clear, detailed method and training description.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation using BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall, median rank, and AMT user studies.  
- Quantitative results show CRCN outperforms state-of-the-art baselines (Kiros, Karpathy, Vinyals).  
- AMT user studies generally prefer CRCN, but performance is similar to RCN in Table 2.  
- Related work section is incomplete; missing relevant video-to-sentence studies (e.g., "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text…").  
- User study comparisons require statistical significance testing to confirm improvements.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to expand related work and provide significance analysis for user study results.  
- Strengths: novelty, clear exposition, and thorough evaluation.  
- Weaknesses: incomplete related work coverage and uncertain user study significance.
