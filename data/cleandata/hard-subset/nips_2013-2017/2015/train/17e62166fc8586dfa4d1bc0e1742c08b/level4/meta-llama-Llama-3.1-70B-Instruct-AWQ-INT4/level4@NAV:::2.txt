The paper tackles the challenge of generating a sequence of consistent sentences to describe a sequence of images from blog posts. To achieve this, the authors propose a novel approach that first retrieves the K=5 most similar images and associated sentences from the training set for each query image. The primary contribution of the paper lies in introducing a method to select the most relevant sentences for the query image sequence, providing a coherent description. This is accomplished by embedding sentences in a vector space and modeling the sequence of sentences using a bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) network. The output of the bidirectional LSTM is then passed through a rectified linear unit (ReLU) and a fully connected layer, followed by a compatibility score calculation between the image and sentence. Additionally, a local coherence model is incorporated to enforce compatibility between sentences.

Strengths and positive aspects of the paper include:

- The proposal of a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for an image sequence.
- The provision of extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed approach over several baselines that do not utilize coherence. The authors also conduct an ablation experiment to evaluate the impact of removing the coherence model.
- The commitment to release the source code and dataset.

Weaknesses, questions, and areas of unclarity include:

1. The paper claims that there is no mechanism for ensuring coherence between sentences in [5]. However, [5] predicts an intermediate semantic representation of videos that is coherent across sentences by modeling the topic of the multi-sentence description.
2. The correctness and clarity of Figure 2b and its description in Section 3.3/Equation (2) are questionable. The figure implies that the fully connected layers are connected to all sentences, whereas the equation suggests that the parameters are shared across sentences but only connected to the vector representing a single sentence.
3. A more suitable metric for automatically evaluating the generated sentences is Meteor (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/), especially when there is only a single reference sentence, rather than BLEU.
4. The application of two linear functions in Equation (2) (W{f2}, W{f1}) behind each other is unclear, as two linear functions can be reduced to a single linear function. An ablation study demonstrating the benefit of these functions would be valuable.
5. The use of the same parameters for the fully connected layers in the BRNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model q (Equation 2) requires clarification.
6. It is unclear whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed.

After reviewing the rebuttal, I recommend accepting the paper. The authors have successfully addressed issues related to formulation, evaluation, and related work. However, to strengthen the paper, the authors should incorporate the promised changes and clarify the following points:

- Whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed.

The paper proposes an innovative model for retrieving coherent sentences for an image stream, which is convincingly evaluated. Nevertheless, to establish the paper's credibility, several clarifications need to be made.
