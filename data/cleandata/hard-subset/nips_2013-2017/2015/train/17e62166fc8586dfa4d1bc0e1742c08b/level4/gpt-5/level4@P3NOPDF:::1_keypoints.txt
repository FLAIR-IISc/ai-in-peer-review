- Tackles retrieving sentences for image sequences and composing coherent multi-sentence narratives using a CRCN model with entity-based coherence; clear writing and sound approach.  
- Strength: Clear problem statement and relevant task bridging image captioning and video description.  
- Strength: CRCN architecture is well motivated for aligning image and sentence sequences and modeling discourse relations.  
- Strength: Integration of entity-based coherence is a principled way to capture discourse beyond single sentences.  
- Strength: Presentation is generally easy to follow.  
- Weakness: Fails to cite and discuss related multi-sentence video-description work (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014); novelty relative to video captioning is unclear.  
- Weakness: Final sentence generation is primitive (per-image retrieval + concatenation), underusing the discourse model and risking redundancy, incoherence, and error propagation.  
- Weakness: Equation (2) as written implies s_t depends only on o_t, undermining sequential modeling—notation/definition needs correction/clarification.  
- Weakness: Dataset/anonymity concerns—reusing Disneyland blog data and newly crawled NYC data without disclosing crawling details risks identifying authors and hinders reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility limited by lack of dataset release and unspecified crawling/preprocessing/filtering details.  
- Request: Clarify how the entity-based coherence model is integrated at training and inference (representation learning, scoring, or post-hoc).  
- Request: State whether any global coherence-aware selection or reranking is performed, or if outputs are strict concatenations of independently retrieved sentences.  
- Request: Clarify training objectives and loss functions, how cross-modal alignment and coherence signals are balanced, and provide full recurrent formulation if Eq. (2) is schematic.  
- Suggestion: Expand related work to include multi-sentence video description and discourse-aware captioning and delineate methodological differences.  
- Suggestion: Replace per-image independent retrieval with sequence-level decoding or coherence-aware reranking that optimizes visual relevance, fluency, and discourse coherence.  
- Suggestion: Add an ablation study to quantify the contribution of the entity-based coherence component versus image–sentence alignment alone.  
- Suggestion: Improve reproducibility by detailing the crawling pipeline, dataset statistics, and committing to public release or a replication protocol upon acceptance.  
- Suggestion: Correct or clarify Equation (2) and ensure the architecture description unambiguously shows a properly connected sequence model.  
- Overall: Important problem and solid core ideas, but current generation strategy, relation to video-description literature, notation, and dataset/anonymity issues need significant improvement.  
- Recommendation: Accept as a poster conditional on fixing Eq. (2), strengthening related work, and mitigating anonymity/reproducibility concerns.
