This paper proposes a method for generating sentences from an image stream using a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN). The model integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model.

Overall, this is a solid contribution with a novel algorithm. The quantitative results appear strong. However, the work could be improved by addressing the following points:

1.  The discussion of related work is incomplete. Several relevant papers on video-to-sentence generation are missing, for example, "Jointly Modeling Deep Video and Compositional Text to Bridge Vision and Language in a Unified Framework."

2.  While the quantitative metrics suggest significant gains, the user study in Table 2 shows that the proposed method performs similarly to the RCN baseline. The authors should conduct statistical significance tests to verify whether the reported improvement is reliable. Without such analysis, the claims of superiority are not fully substantiated by the human evaluation.

In summary, the paper presents a promising algorithm for sentence generation from image streams, but the experimental validation requires more rigor to fully support its claims over baselines.
