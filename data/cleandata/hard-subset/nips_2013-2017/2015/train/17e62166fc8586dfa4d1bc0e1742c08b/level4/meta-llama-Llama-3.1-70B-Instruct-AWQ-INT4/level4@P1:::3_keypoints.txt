- Proposes a novel task: generating coherent sentence sequences from an image stream (extension of image-to-sentence generation).
- Introduces CRCN: a coherent recurrent convolutional network combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Trained on a large dataset of blog posts with images and evaluated with quantitative metrics and user studies.
- Quantitative results show CRCN outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines, including single-sentence image captioning methods.
- User study results (Table 2) show CRCN performs similarly to baseline RCN, so improvements may be marginal in human evaluation.
- Reviewer requests a statistical significance test to verify whether the user-study improvements are reliable.
- Related work section is weak on video-to-sentence literature; missing discussion of relevant work such as Xu et al. [33].
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and explains the model and experiments effectively.
- Experiments and results are well-presented and easy to follow.
- Novel architecture with potential applications in image captioning, video summarization, and HCI.
- Recommendation: accept but revise to strengthen related work discussion and add significance testing for user-study claims.
