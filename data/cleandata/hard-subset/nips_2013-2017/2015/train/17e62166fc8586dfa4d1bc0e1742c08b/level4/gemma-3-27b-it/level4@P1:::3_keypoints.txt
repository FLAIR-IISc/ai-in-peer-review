- Problem: Generates coherent sequences of sentences from image streams, an important extension beyond single image–single sentence models.
- Method: Proposes CRCN combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Motivation: Well-motivated architecture that integrates established components in a novel way for stream-level description.
- Data: Uses large, readily available blog-post image–text pairs as a pragmatic training corpus.
- Quantitative results: Demonstrates strong performance versus existing baselines on automatic metrics.
- User study: Shows only marginal improvement over the RCN baseline, especially for shorter image streams.
- Significance testing: User study lacks statistical significance tests to confirm reported improvements are reliable.
- Coherence effect: Evidence suggests the coherence model provides larger benefits for longer image streams.
- Related work gap: Related video-to-sentence literature (e.g., Xu et al. 2015) is missing and should be discussed.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel problem focus, technically sound CRCN design, strong quantitative results, and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited user-study significance and incomplete related-work coverage.
- Overall: Solid contribution likely worthy of acceptance if it addresses missing related work and adds statistical tests for the user study.
