- Proposes CRCN, a novel multimodal architecture combining CNN image features, bi-RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based coherence model for sequence-to-sequence image-to-text generation.  
- Leverages blog posts as text-image parallel data to train on sequences of images and associated text segments.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance with quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall), retrieval accuracy, and AMT user studies, outperforming baselines without coherence modeling.  
- Ablation study shows the coherence component significantly improves retrieval metrics, validating its contribution.  
- Authors plan to release source code and dataset, improving reproducibility and community impact.  
- Questioned claim that prior work [5] lacks coherence mechanisms; [5] may implicitly model topic progression and multi-sentence coherence.  
- Figure 2(b) initially seemed inconsistent with Equation (2) and Section 3.3 regarding connections and shared parameters; authors have clarified this in rebuttal.  
- Suggestion to prefer METEOR (or emphasize it) over BLEU for single-reference sentence-sequence evaluation due to better semantic sensitivity.  
- Two sequential linear layers (Wf2 Wf1) in Equation (2) appear redundant; an ablation showing benefit of the two-layer design is requested.  
- Rationale for sharing parameters between BRNN output (o_t) and coherence model (q) in Equation (2) is not clearly justified.  
- Unclear whether paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or fixed during training—important implementation detail for reproducibility.  
- Data augmentation using top-κ sentences lacks justification, particularly the choice κ=3.  
- Claim that the model is “completely unsupervised” is misleading since training uses text-image parallel blog data (weak supervision).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-evaluated, addresses most reviewer concerns in rebuttal, and recommended for acceptance.
