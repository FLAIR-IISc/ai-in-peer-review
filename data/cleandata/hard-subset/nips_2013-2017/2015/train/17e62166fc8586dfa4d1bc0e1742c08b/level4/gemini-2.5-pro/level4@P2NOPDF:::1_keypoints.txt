- Addresses an important CV+NLP problem: generating coherent sentence sequences for image sequences.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly states the problem and solution.  
- Core contribution: Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) to learn image-sequence → sentence-sequence mappings.  
- Strength: integration of an entity-based coherence model that improves discourse-level, story-like descriptions.  
- Missing related work: fails to cite/discuss multi-sentence video description literature (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014), undermining novelty assessment.  
- Simplistic generation method: uses retrieval of nearest training image sentences and concatenation, not leveraging CRCN’s generative potential.  
- Reproducibility and anonymity issues: dataset not public, crawling methodology insufficiently described, and indications the authors may be linked to cited dataset paper, breaking double-blind review.  
- Potential technical error: Equation (2) suggests state st depends only on output ot, omitting dependency on previous state st-1—needs clarification/correction.  
- Recommendation: accept as a poster, conditional on addressing related work and clarifying Equation (2).
