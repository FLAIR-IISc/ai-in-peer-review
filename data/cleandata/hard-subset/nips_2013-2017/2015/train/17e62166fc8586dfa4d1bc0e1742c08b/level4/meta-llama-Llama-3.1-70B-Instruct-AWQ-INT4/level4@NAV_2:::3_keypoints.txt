- Proposes generating sentence sequences from image streams, extending input and output complexity beyond existing methods.
- Introduces CRCN, a multimodal model combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence component.
- Trains CRCN on a large unstructured dataset of 20K blog posts with 140K associated images.
- Evaluates with quantitative metrics and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies, claiming superior performance over state-of-the-art baselines.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the approach and model design.
- CRCN is a novel combination of existing techniques with thorough related-work discussion.
- Technically sound: claims supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.
- High significance: addresses an important CV+NLP problem and offers results likely useful to other researchers.
- Lacks detailed description of preprocessing steps for blog posts (e.g., text segmentation, summarization).
- Should discuss limitations and potential future directions more explicitly.
- Some paragraphs are overly long and would benefit from improved readability.
- User study could be more robust (larger sample size and more detailed analysis).
