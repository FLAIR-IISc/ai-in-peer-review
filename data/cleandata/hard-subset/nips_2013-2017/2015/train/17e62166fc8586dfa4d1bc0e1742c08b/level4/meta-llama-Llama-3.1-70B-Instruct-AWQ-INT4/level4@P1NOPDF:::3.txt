This work investigates the task of generating sentences from an image stream and proposes a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) that integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model.

Overall, this is a promising study, although there are several areas that can be improved upon. Firstly, the authors have missed citing several related works on video-to-sentence generation, such as "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework." Including these references would strengthen the paper by providing a more comprehensive overview of the existing literature.

While the quantitative results of the proposed method appear impressive, the user study presented in Table 2 reveals that the performance of CRCN is comparable to one of the baseline methods, RCN. To confirm whether the observed improvement is statistically significant, the authors should consider conducting a significance test. This would provide more robust evidence for the effectiveness of their approach.

The proposed algorithm for generating sentences from an image stream is innovative, and the quantitative results look promising. However, the user study only demonstrates a marginal advantage over the baseline methods.
