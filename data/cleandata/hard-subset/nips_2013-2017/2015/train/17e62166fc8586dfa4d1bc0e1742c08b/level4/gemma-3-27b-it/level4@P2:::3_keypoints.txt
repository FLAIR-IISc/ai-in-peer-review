- Problem: Extends single-image captioning to generate coherent sequences of sentences describing image streams.  
- Method: Proposes CRCN combining CNN image encoders, bidirectional RNNs for language, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Data: Trains on a large corpus of blog posts, leveraging their inherent storytelling/image–text pairing.  
- Results: Shows strong quantitative improvements on benchmarks and promising but less conclusive AMT user study results.  
- Strength: Explicitly modeling inter-sentence coherence is a valuable and well-motivated contribution.  
- Strength: CRCN architecture appears sound and yields gains over strong baselines.  
- Strength: Using blog posts as scalable, narrative-aligned training data is a clever choice.  
- Weakness: Omits discussion of relevant video-captioning literature (e.g., Xu et al., 2017).  
- Weakness: User study differences between CRCN and RCN are marginal and need statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks detailed analysis of failure cases and error modes.  
- Weakness: Insufficient exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly the κ augmentation parameter.  
- Recommendation: Solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address related work, statistical tests, failure analysis, and κ sensitivity.
