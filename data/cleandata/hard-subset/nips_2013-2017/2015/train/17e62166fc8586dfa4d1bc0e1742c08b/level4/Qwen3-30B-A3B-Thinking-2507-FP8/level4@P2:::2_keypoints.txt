- Problem: Generate coherent natural-language sentence sequences for image streams instead of single-image captions.
- Proposed method: Introduces Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Evaluation quality: Thorough quantitative (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall), qualitative examples, AMT user studies, and ablation showing coherence model contribution.
- Clarity: Well-written and organized with clear figures and reproducible implementation details; minor ambiguity between Figure 2b and Equation (2).
- Originality: Novel extension of image captioning to multi-image/multi-sentence generation and novel integration of entity-based coherence with multimodal networks.
- Significance: Addresses a realistic user scenario using large-scale blog datasets (20K+ posts, 140K images) with unsupervised training from blog text.
- Strength: Novel problem formulation filling a gap in literature.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation including human studies and ablations.
- Strength: Ablation study convincingly shows the usefulness of the coherence model.
- Strength: Practical applicability to real-world blog data without manual annotation.
- Strength: Clear presentation of architecture and training procedures.
- Weakness: Claim about prior work [5] lacking sentence coherence is questionable and needs clearer differentiation.
- Weakness: Ambiguity in how Figure 2b maps to Equation (2) regarding layer connections and shared parameters.
- Weakness: Unclear rationale for using two sequential linear layers (Wf2 Wf1) versus a single layer; needs ablation.
- Weakness: Unclear whether paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or fixed during training.
- Weakness: Reliance on BLEU; METEOR suggested for more nuanced coherence evaluation against single references.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance—technically sound, well-evaluated, and addresses an important gap.
- Pro-acceptance: Novel task, strong empirical results, clear coherence contribution, scalable to large real-world data.
- Con-acceptance: Minor clarifications needed on relation to prior work and specific architectural choices.
- Rebuttal: Authors addressed reviewer concerns (coherence relation to prior work and fully connected layer design), strengthening the submission.
