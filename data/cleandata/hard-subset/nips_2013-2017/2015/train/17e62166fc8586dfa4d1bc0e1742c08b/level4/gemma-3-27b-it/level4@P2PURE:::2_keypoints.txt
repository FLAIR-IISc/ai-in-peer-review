- Problem: retrieve coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences by first retrieving K=5 nearest training images/sentences and selecting the best sentence sequence.  
- Method: embed sentences, model their sequence with a bidirectional LSTM, apply ReLU + fully connected layers to compute image–sentence compatibility, and incorporate a local coherence model between sentences.  
- Strength: Proposes a novel, effective architecture that explicitly models coherence and outperforms baselines in quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations.  
- Strength: Includes an ablation study removing the coherence model and commits to releasing source code and dataset.  
- Concern: Claim that prior work [5] lacks sentence-coherence mechanism is debatable because [5] predicts an intermediate semantic representation that can encourage coherence.  
- Clarity/consistency issue: Figure 2b seems inconsistent with Section 3.3 and Equation (2) regarding whether FC layers connect to all sentences or only to individual sentence vectors with parameter sharing.  
- Evaluation metric: METEOR would be more appropriate than BLEU given the use of a single reference sentence.  
- Modeling question: The rationale for two sequential linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) in Equation (2) is unclear since they can be collapsed; an ablation study is suggested.  
- Implementation question: Why are the same FC-layer parameters used for BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence model q, and was the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned or kept fixed?  
- Outcome: After rebuttal, reviewer’s concerns were addressed and the paper is recommended for acceptance, but final-version clarification is requested about fine-tuning the paragraph vector.
