

This work investigates sentence generation from image streams through a novel coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN), integrating convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. While the proposed approach presents a valuable contribution to the field, the following points warrant attention for improvement:  
* The related work section omits key literature on video-to-sentence generation, including the recent framework "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework."  
* Although quantitative results demonstrate strong performance, Table 2 indicates the user study shows comparable results to the RCN baseline. A statistical significance test is required to confirm whether the observed improvements are reliable.  
The algorithm offers a promising solution for sentence generation from image streams, with generally strong quantitative results, though the user study reveals only a marginal advantage over baselines.
