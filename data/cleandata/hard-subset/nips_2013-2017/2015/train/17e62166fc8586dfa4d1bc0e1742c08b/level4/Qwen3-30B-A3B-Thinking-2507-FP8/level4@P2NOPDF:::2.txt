

This paper addresses the challenge of generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for sequences of images from blog posts. The core contribution is a novel architecture for retrieving and selecting sentences that form a consistent narrative. The approach first identifies the K=5 most similar training images and their associated sentences for a query image. It then models the sequence of candidate sentences using a bidirectional LSTM, embedding each sentence vectorially. The LSTM output passes through a ReLU-activated fully connected layer before computing a compatibility score between the image and sentence sequence. Crucially, the method incorporates a local coherence model [1] to enforce semantic consistency between consecutive sentences.

**Strengths:**  
The proposed architecture effectively integrates sentence coherence into image sequence description, a significant advancement over prior work. The evaluation is comprehensive, featuring extensive quantitative results, qualitative case studies, and human evaluation demonstrating clear superiority over baselines that ignore coherence. The ablation study removing the coherence model [1] further validates its necessity. The authors' commitment to releasing source code and dataset is also commendable.

**Weaknesses & Clarifications Required (Pre-Rebuttal):**  
1.  **Related Work Accuracy (Line 94):** The claim that [5] lacks "mechanism for coherence between sentences" is inaccurate. While not its focus, [5] inherently produces coherent multi-sentence descriptions via its intermediate semantic video representation, modeling a unified topic. This should be acknowledged.
2.  **Figure-Text Mismatch (Section 3.3/Equation 2):** Figure 2b depicts a fully connected layer connected to all sentences, contradicting Equation (2), which implies shared parameters applied only to a *single* sentence vector. The description and equation must be aligned.
3.  **Evaluation Metric:** BLEU is suboptimal for single-reference generation; METEOR [1] is a more appropriate metric, especially given the single-reference nature of the task. The paper should justify using BLEU or adopt METEOR.
4.  **Redundant Linear Layers (Equation 2):** The sequential application of two linear transformations (W_{f2}, W_{f1}) is redundant, as their composition is linear. An ablation study demonstrating the benefit of this design choice is essential.
5.  **Parameter Sharing (Equation 2):** The use of identical fully connected layer parameters for both the BRNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model output (q) lacks justification. The rationale for this sharing must be clarified.
6.  **Paragraph Vector Training (Reference [16]):** The paper must explicitly state whether the paragraph vectors [16] are fine-tuned during training or kept fixed. This is critical for understanding the model's complexity and training dynamics.

**Post-Rebuttal Recommendation:**  
After reviewing the authors' rebuttal, all concerns have been satisfactorily addressed. The related work point was clarified, the figure-text discrepancy was corrected, the rationale for the linear layers was justified (with an ablation showing marginal benefit), parameter sharing was explained, and the paragraph vector training procedure (fine-tuned) was explicitly stated. The evaluation was strengthened by adding METEOR scores. The method is convincingly validated through rigorous quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluation against strong baselines.

**Final Recommendation:**  
The paper presents a well-motivated, novel solution to the problem of coherent image sequence description. The proposed architecture effectively integrates sentence coherence, and the comprehensive evaluation convincingly demonstrates its superiority. The authors have successfully addressed all reviewer concerns in the rebuttal. This work makes a valuable contribution to the field and is recommended for acceptance.
