- Tackles retrieval of natural sentences for image sequences, an important CV/pattern-recognition problem.  
- Closely related to video-description literature (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014) but fails to cite that line of work.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem statement and solid technical approach.  
- Proposes CRCN model that effectively captures relationships between sequences of sentences and image sequences.  
- Model architecture is technically sound and models discourse relationships using entity-based coherence.  
- Sentence-sequence generation approach is primitive/ad-hoc: map each image to similar training sentences and concatenate.  
- Data collection description raises anonymity concerns—language suggests authors may have reused/collected data from [11] without disclosing methods.  
- Equation (2) seems problematic: it implies st depends only on ot, suggesting the network may not be fully connected.  
- Despite issues, reviewer recommends acceptance as a poster paper.
