This paper addresses the task of retrieving natural sentences that describe a sequence of images and generating a coherent sequence of sentences, a problem with significant implications for computer vision and pattern recognition research. Notably, this task bears resemblance to video content description, where a video can be decomposed into key frames representing individual shots. However, the authors have not referenced relevant studies in this area, such as [Rohrbach+ 2014].

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear problem statement and a solid technical approach. The proposed CRCN neural network model effectively captures relationships between image and sentence sequences, leveraging entity-based coherence models to describe discourse relationships. The model's architecture is technically sound, but the method for generating sentence sequences appears somewhat simplistic, relying on direct associations between sentences and training images, followed by concatenation in the order of corresponding images.

The authors' data collection process, as described in Section 4.1, raises concerns regarding anonymity, as the dataset and crawling method are not publicly disclosed, and the paper's authors appear to be closely affiliated with the authors of [11]. Furthermore, Equation (2) may contain an error, suggesting that the network's connectivity is incomplete. Despite these limitations, the proposed model demonstrates technical merit and effectively captures discourse relationships. However, the sentence generation method is somewhat ad-hoc. Overall, this paper may be suitable for acceptance as a poster presentation in its current form.
