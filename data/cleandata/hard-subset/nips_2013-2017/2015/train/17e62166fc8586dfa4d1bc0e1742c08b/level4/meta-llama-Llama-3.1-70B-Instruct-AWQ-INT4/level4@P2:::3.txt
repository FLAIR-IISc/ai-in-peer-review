This work proposes an approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream, extending both input and output dimensions beyond previous studies that typically focus on the relation between a single image and a single sentence. The authors design a multimodal architecture called coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts, consisting of over 20,000 blog posts with 140,000 associated images.

The paper is well-motivated, and the proposed approach is novel and interesting. The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of their method, including the architecture of the CRCN model and the training procedure. The experimental evaluation is thorough, with both quantitative measures (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall, and median rank) and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk (AMT).

The quantitative results show that the proposed approach outperforms other state-of-the-art candidate methods, including the log-bilinear multimodal models by Kiros et al. and the recurrent convolutional models by Karpathy et al. and Vinyals et al. The user studies also demonstrate that the proposed approach is preferred by users over the baseline methods.

However, there are a few areas that can be improved. Firstly, the related work section is not exhaustive, and some relevant studies on video-to-sentence generation are missing, such as "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework". Secondly, while the quantitative results look promising, the user study results in Table 2 show that the proposed approach performs similarly to one of the baseline methods, RCN. A significance test is needed to verify whether the improvement is reliable.

Overall, this is a nice work that proposes a novel approach for sentence generation from an image stream. The quantitative results look good, but the user study results need to be interpreted with caution, and further analysis is needed to confirm the significance of the improvement.

Strengths:

* Novel approach for sentence generation from an image stream
* Clear and detailed explanation of the method
* Thorough experimental evaluation with both quantitative measures and user studies

Weaknesses:

* Related work section is not exhaustive
* User study results need to be interpreted with caution, and further analysis is needed to confirm the significance of the improvement

* The proposed approach is novel and interesting, and the experimental evaluation is thorough.
* The quantitative results show that the proposed approach outperforms other state-of-the-art candidate methods.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The related work section is not exhaustive, and some relevant studies are missing.

Recommendation:

* Accept the paper, but request the authors to address the weaknesses mentioned above, particularly the related work section and the user study results.
