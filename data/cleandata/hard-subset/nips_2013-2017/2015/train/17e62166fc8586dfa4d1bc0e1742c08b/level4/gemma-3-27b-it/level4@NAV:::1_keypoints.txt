- Addresses an important problem (retrieving/generating descriptions for image sequences) but closely relates to video captioning and fails to cite related work (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014).  
- Paper is clearly written and logically structured with a well-defined problem and solution.  
- Proposed CRCN model is technically strong and effectively captures relationships between image sequences and sentences.  
- Integration of entity-based coherence models appropriately addresses discourse-level relationships.  
- Sentence generation is basic and ad hoc, relying on direct associations to similar training images and simple concatenation by image order.  
- Dataset transparency is lacking: the collected/reused dataset is undisclosed, the original crawling method ([11]) is not detailed, and possible author overlap raises anonymity concerns.  
- Equation (2) appears potentially flawed, implying incomplete connectivity (st seems derived solely from ot).  
- Overall recommendation: accept as a poster paper.
