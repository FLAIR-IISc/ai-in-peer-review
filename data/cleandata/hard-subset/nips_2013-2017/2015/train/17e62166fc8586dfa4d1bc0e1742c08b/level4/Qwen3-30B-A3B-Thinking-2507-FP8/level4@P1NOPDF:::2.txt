

Strengths: The paper proposes a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentence sequences to describe image sequences from blog posts. It provides comprehensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluation, demonstrating clear superiority over baselines that ignore sentence coherence. The ablation study removing the local coherence model [1] further validates the contribution. The authors also commit to releasing source code and the dataset.

Weaknesses:  
1.  **Related Work Claim (Line 94):** The paper states [5] lacks "a mechanism for coherence between sentences." However, [5] models a coherent intermediate semantic representation for videos by predicting the topic across the multi-sentence description, implying inherent sentence-level coherence. This claim requires clarification.  
2.  **Figure-Text Mismatch (Section 3.3 / Eq. 2):** Figure 2b depicts fully connected layers linked to *each* sentence vector, suggesting per-sentence connections. Equation (2), however, describes shared parameters across sentences with connections only to a *single* sentence vector (represented by the bi-LSTM output). This inconsistency needs resolution.  
3.  **Evaluation Metric:** BLEU is suboptimal for single-reference sentence generation; METEOR (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) is a more suitable metric for automatic evaluation, especially when only one reference sentence is available.  
4.  **Redundant Linear Layers (Eq. 2):** The sequential application of two linear functions (W_{f1}, W_{f2}) appears redundant, as their composition is equivalent to a single linear function. An ablation study demonstrating the specific benefit of this two-layer structure would be valuable.  
5.  **Parameter Sharing (Eq. 2):** The paper uses identical fully connected layer parameters for processing both the bi-LSTM output (o_t) and the local coherence model output (q). The rationale for this shared parameterization requires justification.  
6.  **Paragraph Vector (Ref [16]):** It is unclear whether the paragraph vector model [16] is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed. This detail is critical for reproducibility and understanding the model's learning dynamics.

Post-Rebuttal: After reviewing the authors' rebuttal, I recommend acceptance. The authors effectively addressed all concerns regarding formulation clarity, evaluation methodology, and related work. The paper presents an interesting new model for coherent sentence retrieval over image sequences, convincingly evaluated against relevant baselines. To ensure the paper's full clarity and reproducibility, the specific status of the paragraph vector (fine-tuned or fixed) must be explicitly stated in the final version.
