- Paper addresses image-stream captioning by generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions using a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN).
- CRCN integrates VGG-based CNN image features, a BRNN over doc2vec sentence embeddings, and an entity-grid local coherence model.
- Training data are weakly aligned image–text streams mined from blogs with heuristic paragraph alignment and LSA-based summarization; inference uses K-NN retrieval plus divide-and-conquer beam search.
- Evaluated on NYC and Disneyland tourism datasets with BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, and AMT user studies; shows improvements over strong baselines.
- Novelty: explicitly models discourse coherence across sentence sequences, extending single-image captioning to image streams.
- Strength: timely and relevant problem formulation (photo albums, travel blogs).
- Strength: sensible and novel model design combining BRNN and entity-grid with a structured max-margin objective.
- Strength: scalable, pragmatic data sourcing from large-scale user-generated blogs without manual labels.
- Strength: quantitative gains across automatic metrics and retrieval measures; ablations indicate BRNN and coherence help.
- Weakness: related work coverage misses important video-to-text sequence models (e.g., Xu et al., AAAI 2015) and needs clearer contrasts.
- Weakness: AMT human evaluation shows marginal improvement over RCN and lacks statistical significance testing.
- Weakness: experiments confined to two tourism domains, raising questions about cross-domain generalization.
- Weakness: pipeline depends on heuristic image–text alignment and LSA summarization without analysis of alignment/summarization noise or its effect.
- Weakness: some implementation details missing (doc2vec hyperparameters, BRNN architecture choices, κ data augmentation, beam-search complexity/runtime).
- Quality: technically sound with thorough quantitative evaluation, but human-study claims require statistical testing and error/sensitivity analysis for noisy components.
- Clarity: generally well organized and understandable; related work and preprocessing/evaluation details should be expanded.
- Originality: combination of BRNN over sentence embeddings with entity-grid for multi-sentence retrieval is novel and meaningful.
- Significance: important task likely to inspire follow-up work, but impact is partly limited by weak human-evaluation evidence and narrow domains.
- Pros summary: addresses underexplored task, thoughtful model integration, improvements over baselines, scalable data collection.
- Cons summary: incomplete related work, marginal AMT advantage without significance tests, limited domains, reliance on heuristics lacking analysis.
- Recommendations: expand related work to include video-to-text models and clarify differences; add statistical tests and confidence intervals for AMT results.
- Recommendations: report full AMT results for longer sequences, analyze preprocessing alignment/summarization quality and sensitivity, and provide more implementation details for reproducibility.
- Overall: promising and well-executed work that needs stronger related-work framing and more rigorous human and preprocessing analyses to fully substantiate claimed gains.
