- Paper proposes a novel method to retrieve coherent, consistent sentences for an image sequence by embedding sentences and modeling sentence order with a bidirectional LSTM plus a compatibility score with images.  
- Main contribution: an effective architecture combining sentence embeddings, a bi-directional LSTM sequence model, and a local coherence term to retrieve coherent multi-sentence descriptions.  
- Evaluation is extensive: automatic metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall), qualitative results, and human studies on AMT.  
- Results show the method outperforms baselines that do not model coherence, and human evaluation prefers the generated sentences.  
- Ablation experiments (removing the coherence model) further demonstrate the coherence component’s effectiveness.  
- Authors initially misstate prior work: [5] does model topic-level coherence via intermediate semantic representation and this should be clarified for fair comparison.  
- Model description inconsistency: Section 3.3/Equation (2) conflicts with Figure 2b about whether fully connected layers connect to all sentences or only per-sentence with shared parameters.  
- Metric choice concern: BLEU may be insufficient when only a single reference exists; METEOR (or other suitable metrics) should be considered/emphasized.  
- Design choices need justification: why two linear functions in Eq.(2) and why parameters are shared between BRNN output and the local coherence model; an ablation showing their benefits is suggested.  
- Post-rebuttal, authors addressed several formulation, evaluation, and related-work issues but some clarifications remain.  
- Clarify whether the paragraph vector is fine-tuned or fixed during training and justify the choice.  
- Strengths summarized: novel effective architecture, comprehensive evaluations, and commitment to release code and dataset.  
- Weaknesses summarized: needed clarifications on comparison to prior work, model description, evaluation metrics, design choices, and paragraph vector handling.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on the authors resolving the remaining clarifications and providing the requested experimental/ablation details.
