This paper studies sentence generation from an image stream and proposes a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that combines convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. Overall, it is a solid and interesting approach.

Strengths:
- The architecture is well motivated, and the integration of an entity-based local coherence model is appealing for maintaining coherence across sentences.
- The quantitative results are strong and suggest the method is effective.

Areas for improvement:
- Some relevant prior work on video-to-sentence generation is missing. In particular, please discuss and position your method relative to “Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework.” Including this and related references would better situate the contribution.
- While the automatic metrics look good, the user study in Table 2 indicates performance similar to the RCN baseline; the observed advantage appears weak. A statistical significance test is needed to determine whether the reported improvements are reliable. As it stands, the human evaluation does not clearly demonstrate a meaningful gain over the baselines.

In summary, this is a nice algorithm for sentence generation from an image stream with promising quantitative results. However, the human study shows only a modest advantage over baselines, and the paper would benefit from a more complete discussion of related work and statistical testing of the user study outcomes.
