- Addresses image-sequence captioning, relevant to video captioning but fails to cite related video-captioning work.
- Paper is well-written and the problem is clearly stated.
- Proposed CRCN model effectively learns relationships between image sequences and sentences.
- Model architecture is technically strong and leverages entity-based coherence modeling.
- Sentence-sequence generation is simplistic/ad hoc: sentences linked to similar training images and concatenated in image order.
- Equation (2) may be incorrect, implying s_t depends only on o_t and a not fully connected network.
- Dataset issues: Disneyland blog reused and NYC data newly crawled, but dataset isn’t public and [11] lacks crawling details.
- Raises concerns about possible author overlap/conflict of interest and compromised anonymity.
- Overall recommendation: suitable for acceptance as a poster.
