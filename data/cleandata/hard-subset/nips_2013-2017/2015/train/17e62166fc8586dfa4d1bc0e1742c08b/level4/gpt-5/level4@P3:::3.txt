Summary
This paper introduces the problem of describing an image stream with a sequence of natural sentences. The authors propose a multimodal architecture, the coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN), which integrates: (i) CNN image features (VGG), (ii) a bidirectional RNN over sentence representations (doc2vec paragraph vectors) to model content flow, and (iii) an entity-based local coherence model (Barzilay and Lapata’s entity grid) to encourage fluent sentence-to-sentence transitions. The approach retrieves candidate sentences from a large corpus of blog posts and ranks sentence sequences by a learned compatibility score with the image stream. The training objective is a max-margin loss over aligned and misaligned image–sentence-sequence pairs. Experiments on NYC and Disneyland travel blogs (~20K posts, ~140K images) report improvements over strong baselines (e.g., Kiros et al., Karpathy & Fei-Fei, Vinyals et al.) on BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR and retrieval metrics, complemented by AMT user studies.

Relation to prior work
The paper is well grounded in retrieval-based captioning (e.g., Ordonez et al.; Karpathy & Fei-Fei) and multimodal RNNs for image captioning (Karpathy, Mao, Vinyals). It also relates to video description (Donahue et al., Rohrbach et al.), but differs by addressing multi-sentence outputs for image streams and explicitly modeling coherence. Please consider discussing additional video-to-text work that models temporal structure, such as Venugopalan et al., “Sequence to Sequence – Video to Text” (CVPR 2015), and more recent video-captioning frameworks that incorporate sentence-level structure. The entity-grid coherence model is a distinctive contribution relative to both image and video captioning literature.

Quality
- Methodology: The overall design is sensible: retrieve candidates per image, then score global sequences by combining per-sentence compatibility (CNN·BRNN) with a coherence score. The use of paragraph vectors is pragmatic for sentence-level modeling. The entity grid provides a linguistically motivated coherence signal.
- Technical details: A few points would benefit from clarification:
  - Equation (3): The coherence vector gk is dotted with each image feature vlt and then summed over t. Why should a single sequence-level coherence vector interact with each image independently, rather than with an aggregate image-stream representation (e.g., sum/mean of vlt or an RNN over images)? Please justify this design or report ablations.
  - BRNN inputs use separate forward/backward input projections (Wfi, Wbi). An ablation comparing shared vs. separate projections would strengthen the claim that this empirically helps.
  - Preprocessing: The segmentation heuristic (assign paragraphs to nearest image by index distance) and LSA summarization introduce potential noise. Some quantitative analysis of segmentation/summarization quality, or a sensitivity analysis to κ (number of summary alternatives), would help assess robustness.
  - Training: The structured loss samples 100 negative examples per positive. How sensitive are results to the number and mining strategy of negatives? Is length normalization applied to avoid bias toward shorter/longer sequences?
  - Evaluation: BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR are limited for paragraph-level fluency/coherence. Consider coherence-specific evaluation (e.g., entity-grid ranking tests) and/or ROUGE-L at paragraph level to corroborate the intended gains.
- Empirical results: The quantitative gains over baselines are convincing on retrieval metrics; improvements on language metrics are smaller. The AMT study shows only a modest advantage over the BRNN-only variant (RCN), especially for short sequences. Statistical significance tests (e.g., binomial test or bootstrap) are needed to establish reliability. The additional AMT with longer sequences is promising; please report full statistics (n, CI, p-values).

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well organized. Figures help convey the pipeline. Some writing issues (e.g., minor typos/grammar) can be cleaned in revision. Please also:
- Provide pseudocode or a brief description of the divide-and-conquer + beam search procedure with complexity.
- Specify doc2vec training details (corpus sizes per domain, window size, DM vs. DBOW, epochs).
- Clarify whether the model is “end-to-end”: most components are trained jointly except the CNN (pretrained) and doc2vec/coherence extraction. State explicitly what is learned jointly vs. fixed.

Originality
- Task: Extending single-image, single-sentence captioning to image streams with multi-sentence outputs is a meaningful and underexplored direction circa 2015.
- Approach: Integrating an entity-grid coherence model into a deep multimodal architecture is novel and well motivated. The dataset construction from blogs is also useful, though domain-specific.

Significance
The proposed framework can impact story-like visual description and retrieval applications. The gains on retrieval metrics and the user preference trends for longer sequences indicate practical value. However, significance would be strengthened by:
- Broader evaluation beyond tourism (or at least cross-domain tests to support the “unsupervised and applicable to any domain” claim).
- Analysis of error types (e.g., content mismatch vs. incoherent transitions).
- Demonstrating that coherence modeling yields consistent benefits across diverse topics and longer narratives.

Pros
- Timely and important problem: multi-sentence descriptions for image streams.
- Thoughtful integration of CNN, BRNN, and a principled coherence model.
- Solid retrieval performance, reasonable language metrics, and user studies.
- Useful blog-derived dataset and a scalable retrieval-plus-ranking pipeline.

Cons
- Limited empirical advantage over RCN on language metrics and short-sequence AMT; significance not established.
- Heavy reliance on heuristic text preprocessing; robustness analysis is limited.
- Coherence scoring design (Eq. 3) is not fully justified; alternative formulations untested.
- Domain coverage restricted to tourism; generalization claims not demonstrated.
- Related work on temporally structured video-to-text could be expanded.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add statistical significance testing for AMT and report confidence intervals.
- Evaluate coherence explicitly (entity-grid discrimination tasks) and include paragraph-level metrics (e.g., ROUGE-L).
- Provide ablations: (i) coherence term removed/varied; (ii) alternative coherence–image interactions; (iii) shared vs. separate BRNN input projections; (iv) sensitivity to κ and segmentation quality.
- Discuss and, if possible, test generalization on at least one additional domain.
- Expand related work to include sequence-to-sequence video captioning (e.g., Venugopalan et al., CVPR 2015) and clarify the distinction from this work.

Overall assessment
A well-motivated and generally sound contribution on describing image streams with coherent multi-sentence text. The integration of entity-based coherence into a deep multimodal framework is novel and promising. I recommend acceptance if the authors (i) provide significance analyses and stronger coherence-focused evaluation, (ii) clarify and ablate key modeling choices, and (iii) improve the discussion of related temporally structured captioning work.
