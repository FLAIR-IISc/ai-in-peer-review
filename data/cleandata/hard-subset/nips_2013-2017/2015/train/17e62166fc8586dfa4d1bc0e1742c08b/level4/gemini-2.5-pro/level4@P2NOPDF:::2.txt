### Review

**Summary**

This paper addresses the problem of describing an image sequence (e.g., from a blog post) with a sequence of coherent sentences. The proposed method first retrieves the K=5 most similar images and their associated sentences from a training set for each image in the query sequence. The main contribution lies in a novel method for selecting and ordering the most relevant sentences from this retrieved set to form a coherent final description.

To achieve this, sentences are embedded into a vector space using paragraph vectors. A bidirectional LSTM then models the sequence of candidate sentences to capture long-range dependencies. The output of the Bi-LSTM for each sentence is passed through a fully-connected layer with a ReLU activation and is then used to compute a compatibility score with the corresponding query image. To further improve the quality of the output, a local coherence model [1] is integrated to promote consistency between adjacent sentences.

**Strengths**

*   The paper introduces a novel and effective architecture for generating coherent sentence descriptions for an image sequence. The combination of retrieval, sequence modeling with a Bi-LSTM, and a local coherence model is well-motivated.
*   The authors provide comprehensive evaluations—quantitative, qualitative, and human-based—that demonstrate the superiority of their approach over several baselines that do not model coherence. The inclusion of an ablation study, which removes the local coherence model, further strengthens the paper's claims regarding the importance of this component.
*   The commitment to release the source code and dataset is commendable and will be a valuable contribution to the research community.

**Weaknesses and Questions**

1.  **Comparison to Related Work:** The claim on Line 94 that prior work [5] has "no mechanism for the coherence between sentences" seems to be an overstatement. While coherence may not have been the primary contribution of [5], that work models the topic of a multi-sentence description via an intermediate semantic representation, which inherently enforces a degree of thematic coherence across sentences. The authors should clarify this distinction and position their work more accurately.

2.  **Clarity of Architecture:** There appears to be a discrepancy between Figure 2b and the description in Section 3.3 (specifically Equation 2). The figure suggests that the fully-connected layer's input is a concatenation of all sentence representations, whereas Equation 2 implies that the parameters are shared across timesteps but the operation is applied to each sentence's vector individually. Please clarify this aspect of the architecture.

3.  **Evaluation Metric:** The authors rely on BLEU for automatic evaluation. For tasks like this, especially with a single ground-truth reference, METEOR is generally considered a more suitable metric as it better handles synonymy and stemming. The authors should consider reporting METEOR scores or provide a stronger justification for their choice of BLEU.

4.  **Model Design Choices:**
    *   In Equation 2, it appears that two consecutive linear transformations (W_f1 and W_f2) are applied. Without a non-linear activation function between them, these two layers would collapse into a single linear transformation, making the two-layer structure redundant. If there is a non-linearity (e.g., the ReLU mentioned in the text), it should be made explicit in the equation. An ablation study justifying this architectural choice would be beneficial.
    *   Could the authors explain the motivation for sharing the same fully-connected layer parameters for both the Bi-RNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model's input (q_t) in Equation 2? These two components seem to model different aspects (sentence-image compatibility vs. sentence-sentence coherence), and using separate parameter sets might be more effective.
    *   It is unclear whether the paragraph vector model [16] used for sentence embeddings is fine-tuned during training or if pre-trained, fixed embeddings are used. Please clarify this detail.

### Post-Rebuttal Recommendation

After reading the author's rebuttal, I recommend this paper for **acceptance**.

The rebuttal successfully addressed most of my concerns regarding the model's formulation, the evaluation metrics, and the positioning with respect to related work. This paper presents a novel and interesting model for generating coherent descriptions for image sequences, supported by convincing experimental results.

I trust the authors will incorporate the promised changes and clarifications into the camera-ready version. I would also ask them to explicitly clarify the following remaining point in the final paper, as it was not addressed in the rebuttal:

*   Is the paragraph vector model [16] fine-tuned during training, or are its parameters kept fixed?
