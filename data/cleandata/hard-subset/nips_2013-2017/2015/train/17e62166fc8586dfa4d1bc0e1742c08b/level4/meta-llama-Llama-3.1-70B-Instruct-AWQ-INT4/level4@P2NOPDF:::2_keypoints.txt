- Problem: Generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences from blog posts.  
- Main idea: For each query image retrieve K=5 nearest training images and sentences, then select the most relevant sentences for the image sequence.  
- Model: Sentences embedded in vector space and modeled with a bidirectional LSTM; BRNN outputs passed through ReLU and a fully connected layer to compute image–sentence compatibility.  
- Coherence: A local coherence model is added to enforce compatibility between consecutive sentences.  
- Strength: Novel and effective retrieval architecture specifically designed to produce coherent sentence sequences.  
- Strength: Extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations showing improvements over baselines that ignore coherence.  
- Strength: Ablation study removing the coherence model validates its contribution.  
- Strength: Authors commit to releasing source code and dataset.  
- Clarification: Claim that prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is overstated—[5] models intermediate semantic/video topics and may capture coherence.  
- Inconsistency: Figure 2b and Section 3.3/Equation (2) conflict—figure implies FC connects to all sentences while equation implies shared parameters applied per sentence.  
- Metric choice: BLEU may be inappropriate with single reference sentences; METEOR is suggested as a better alternative.  
- Equation (2) concern: Two successive linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) seem redundant; need ablation to justify benefit.  
- Parameter sharing: Using the same FC parameters for BRNN output o_t and the local coherence model q needs justification.  
- Paragraph vectors: Unclear whether paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed during training—requires clarification.  
- Decision: After rebuttal, reviewer recommends acceptance; authors addressed formulation, evaluation, related work, and clarified paragraph vector usage.
