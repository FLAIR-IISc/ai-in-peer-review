This paper addresses the problem of retrieving natural sentences that describe a given sequence of images and forming a sequence of sentences. This problem is significant in the research fields of computer vision and pattern recognition. However, it is closely related to generating multiple sentences that describe given video content (e.g., [Rohrbach+ 2014]), as single video content can be regarded as a sequence of images, with each video shot being well described by a key frame. Notably, none of these related works have been cited in this paper.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clearly stated problem and a solution that employs a set of solid techniques. The proposed neural network model, named CRCN, effectively captures relationships between a sequence of natural sentences and a sequence of images. The model's architecture is technically sound, and it successfully describes discourse relationships using entity-based coherence models.

However, the method for generating a sequence of natural sentences is reasonable but somewhat primitive. It involves directly associating each natural sentence with training images similar to a given image and then concatenating these sentences in the order of the corresponding images.

In Section 4.1, the authors mention reusing the blog data of Disneyland from the dataset of [11] and collecting new data for NYC using the same crawling method. However, the dataset has not been disclosed, and the corresponding paper does not provide details on the data crawling method. This lack of transparency raises concerns about the anonymity of the authors, as it appears that they may be the same individuals as those in [11].

Furthermore, Equation (2) seems incorrect, as it implies that st can be derived solely from ot, suggesting that the network is not fully connected. Despite this, the proposed neural network model is technically sound and effectively describes discourse relationships using entity-based coherence models. In contrast, the method for generating a sequence of sentences for a given image stream is somewhat ad-hoc. Overall, I believe that this paper can be accepted as a poster paper in its current form.
