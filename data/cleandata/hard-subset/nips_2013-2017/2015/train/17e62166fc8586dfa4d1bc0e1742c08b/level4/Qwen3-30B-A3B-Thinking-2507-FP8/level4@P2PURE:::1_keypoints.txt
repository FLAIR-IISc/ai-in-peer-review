- Addresses retrieval of natural sentences for image sequences and forming coherent sentence sequences.  
- Closely related to video multi-sentence generation work (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014) but fails to cite this literature.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and presents a well-defined problem.  
- Proposed CRCN model effectively captures relationships between image sequences and sentence sequences; architecture is technically sound.  
- Uses entity-based coherence models to model discourse relationships effectively.  
- Sentence generation is primitive/ad-hoc: picks sentences from similar training images and concatenates by image order without modeling temporal or contextual dependencies.  
- Dataset and anonymity concerns: reuses Disneyland blog data, newly collects NYC data, but dataset not publicly released and crawling methodology in [11] is not detailed, raising authorship anonymity issues.  
- Possible error in Equation (2): it implies s_t depends only on o_t, suggesting the network may not be fully connected.  
- Given methodological limitations and citation gaps, recommendation is acceptance as a poster.
