- Addresses generation of sentence sequences for image sequences; a relevant CV/pattern-recognition problem.
- Fails to cite or discuss related video description literature (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014).
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and the problem is effectively formulated.
- Main contribution is CRCN, a neural model learning relations between image and sentence sequences.
- Architecture is technically sound and effectively incorporates entity-based discourse coherence modeling.
- Final sentence-generation method is primitive: retrieve sentences from visually similar training images and concatenate them.
- Dataset and anonymity issues: newly collected data not public, crawling methodology under-specified, suggesting possible authorship of cited prior work and anonymity breach.
- Equation (2) seems incorrect (implies st depends only on ot), contradicting the claimed network connectivity.
- Conclusion: model handles discourse well but generation is ad-hoc; reviewer recommends acceptance as a poster.
