

The paper addresses the problem of generating a coherent sequence of sentences describing a sequence of images from blog posts. It proposes first retrieving the K=5 most similar images and associated sentences from the training set for each query image. The main contribution is a novel method for selecting the most relevant sentences to form a coherent description. Sentences are embedded into vectors, and the sequence is modeled using a bidirectional LSTM. The bi-LSTM output is processed through a ReLU-activated fully connected layer and then scored via a compatibility metric between the image and sentence. Additionally, a local coherence model [1] is integrated to enforce sentence compatibility.  

Strengths:  
- The paper introduces a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for image sequences.  
- It provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluation, demonstrating superiority over baselines that ignore coherence. An ablation study removing the coherence model [1] further supports the approach.  
- The authors commit to releasing source code and dataset.  

Weaknesses/Questions:  
1. Line 94 claims [5] lacks "a mechanism for coherence between sentences." While not [5]'s focus, it models a coherent intermediate semantic representation for videos via topic modeling across multi-sentence descriptions.  
2. Figure 2b does not align with Section 3.3/Equation (2). Figure 2b suggests fully connected layers connect to all sentences, whereas Equation (2) indicates shared parameters across sentences, connected only to a single sentence vector.  
3. Meteor (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) is a superior automatic metric for generated sentences, especially with a single reference sentence, compared to BLEU.  
4. The sequential application of two linear functions (W_{f2}, W_{f1}) in Equation (2) is redundant (equivalent to a single linear function). An ablation study demonstrating their benefit would be valuable.  
5. Why are identical parameters used for the BRNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model (q) in Equation (2)?  
6. Is the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned or kept fixed?  

Post-rebuttal: After reviewing the authors' rebuttal, I recommend acceptance. The authors successfully addressed issues regarding formulation, evaluation, and related work. The paper proposes an interesting new model for coherent image sequence description, convincingly evaluated. To ensure clarity, the authors must clarify in the final version whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or fixed.
