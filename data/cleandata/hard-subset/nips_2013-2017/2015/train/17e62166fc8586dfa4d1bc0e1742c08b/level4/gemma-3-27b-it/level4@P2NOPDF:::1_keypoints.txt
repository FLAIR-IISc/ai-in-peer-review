- Addresses retrieving and sequencing sentences for image sequences, but closely related to video captioning (e.g., Rohrbach 2014) which is not cited.
- Fails to engage with relevant video-captioning literature despite strong relevance.
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and defines the problem well.
- CRCN model is technically sound and effectively models relationships using entity-based coherence/discourse modeling.
- Sentence generation is simplistic: sentences tied to nearest training image and concatenated by image order, lacking advanced sequence modeling.
- Dataset transparency is poor: reused and newly crawled data are not publicly available, and prior work [11] lacks crawl details.
- Possible author overlap and missing dataset details raise anonymity and reproducibility concerns.
- Equation (2) appears flawed or unclear (hidden state st seemingly determined solely by ot) and needs clarification.
- Overall sentence-sequencing method feels ad hoc despite being functional.
- Recommendation: accept as a poster rather than oral due to the above issues.
