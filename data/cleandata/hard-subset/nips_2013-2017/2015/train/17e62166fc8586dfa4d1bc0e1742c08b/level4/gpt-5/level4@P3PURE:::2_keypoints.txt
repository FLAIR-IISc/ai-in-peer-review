- Paper tackles describing a sequence of blog-post images with a sequence of mutually consistent sentences via retrieval-and-ranking.
- For each query image the method retrieves K=5 nearest training images and their sentences, then selects sentences for the whole sequence to ensure coherence.
- Sentences are embedded and modeled with a bidirectional LSTM (BRNN); BRNN outputs pass through ReLU and fully connected layers and are scored by an image–sentence compatibility function.
- A local coherence model is incorporated to enforce compatibility across adjacent sentences.
- Strength: Proposes a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent multi-sentence descriptions.
- Strength: Evaluation is extensive—quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, human studies, and an ablation removing the coherence model.
- Strength: Authors plan to release source code and the dataset.
- Weakness: Claim that prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is questionable since [5] models topics to produce coherent multi-sentence outputs.
- Weakness: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2); figure suggests FC layers connect to all sentences while equation implies shared parameters applied per-sentence.
- Weakness: Automatic evaluation should use METEOR (especially with single reference) rather than BLEU.
- Weakness: Eq. (2) applies two linear functions sequentially (W_f2, W_f1); composition is linear so the benefit is unclear—an ablation is needed.
- Weakness: Why are the same fully connected layer parameters used for both BRNN output o_t and the local coherence model q in Eq. (2)?
- Weakness: Clarify whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed.
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance; authors addressed formulation, evaluation, and related-work concerns but must clarify paragraph vector fine-tuning and incorporate promised changes in the final version.
