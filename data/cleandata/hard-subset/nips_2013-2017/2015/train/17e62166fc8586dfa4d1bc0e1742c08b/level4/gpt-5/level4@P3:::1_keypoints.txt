- Paper extends image captioning to image streams using a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNN features, BRNN over paragraph vectors, and an entity-grid coherence model.  
- Training data built automatically from blogs by segmenting around images and summarizing segments to one sentence with augmentations.  
- At test time, candidate sentences are retrieved per image from nearest neighbors and sequences are ranked by a learned compatibility score mixing image–sentence match and coherence.  
- Related to retrieval/embedding captioning and multimodal RNN/LSTM work; should compare more to sequence-to-sequence video-to-text methods and paragraph-level outputs.  
- Strength: clear architecture and sensible integration of entity-based coherence with CNN/BRNN for multi-sentence outputs.  
- Strength: practical, scalable training pipeline deriving image–sentence pairs from noisy web blogs.  
- Strength: strong quantitative evaluation (BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr/retrieval) and AMT studies on two datasets, with useful ablations.  
- Weakness: Generation is largely retrieval-based, limiting novelty, synthesis ability, and risking redundancy and stylistic copying.  
- Weakness: Equation (2) and FC layer description are ambiguous—unclear parameter sharing, indexing, and whether s_t depends on all o_i or only o_t.  
- Weakness: Scoring function repeats the same coherence term at each time step, potentially biasing by sequence length and entangling global coherence with per-image matches.  
- Weakness: Claim of being “completely unsupervised” is overstated given heuristic pairing and supervised NLP components; better framed as weakly/self-supervised.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns—NYC dataset not released and crawl/processing details insufficient; phrasing may break anonymity.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear but needs explicit tensor shapes for Eqs. (1)–(4), handling of κ augmentations, and search/beam complexity and runtime.  
- Clarity: Provide more text preprocessing details (tokenizer settings, handling images without nearby text, LSA parameters, parsing/coreference error rates).  
- Clarity: Specify whether doc2vec is trained per-domain or with shared vocabularies.  
- Originality: Novelty is in integrating known components for coherence-aware multi-sentence retrieval; components themselves are established.  
- Significance: Task is important and results are promising; impact would increase with dataset/code release and extensions beyond retrieval.  
- Suggestion: Clarify Eq. (2) and parameter sharing; add schematic showing s_t dependencies on neighboring o_t and q.  
- Suggestion: Normalize/reweight the coherence term to avoid length bias and consider modeling transitions directly.  
- Suggestion: Report runtime/search efficiency, analyze failure cases (scene changes, pronoun resolution), and expand related work to include S2VT and similar video-to-text models.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept (poster); technically sound with useful results but needs clarifications, stronger related work, and better reproducibility.
