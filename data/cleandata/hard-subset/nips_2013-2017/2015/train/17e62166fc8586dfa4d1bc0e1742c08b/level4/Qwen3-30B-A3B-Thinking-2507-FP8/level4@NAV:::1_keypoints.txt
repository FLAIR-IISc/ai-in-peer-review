- Addresses retrieval of natural sentences for image sequences; problem important and closely related to multi-sentence video captioning but fails to cite that prior line of work.
- Paper is well-written and clearly states the problem.
- Employs solid technical approaches.
- CRCN model effectively captures relationships between image sequences and sentence sequences using entity-based coherence models for discourse structure.
- Sentence generation is simplistic/ad hoc: each sentence is picked from a similar training image and sequences are concatenated by image order.
- Equation (2) appears questionable, implying s_t depends only on o_t and suggesting incomplete network connectivity.
- Dataset disclosure is insufficient: Disneyland blog data reused and NYC data collected via crawling, but crawling details and datasets are not provided.
- Lack of dataset details raises anonymity concerns that the authors may be same as prior work [11].
- Overall verdict: acceptable as a poster with minor revisions.
