- Proposes CRCN: a multimodal model combining convolutional image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and a local coherence model to describe image sequences with coherent sentence sequences.  
- Trains CRCN on large unstructured blog-post datasets and evaluates with quantitative metrics and Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.  
- Main contribution: defining a compatibility score that balances individual image–sentence fitness and coherence between consecutive sentences to select relevant sentences for an image stream.  
- Strength: novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for image sequences.  
- Strength: extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations showing improvements over several baselines.  
- Strength: authors plan to release source code and dataset.  
- Weakness: incorrect claim that prior work [5] lacks any coherence mechanism—[5] models topics and produces coherent multi-sentence outputs.  
- Weakness: Figure 2b and Equation (2) lack clarity/correctness—figure suggests full connectivity while equation implies parameter sharing per sentence.  
- Weakness: BLEU may be a poor automatic metric, especially with single references; METEOR or other metrics would be preferable.  
- Weakness: unclear rationale for stacking two linear functions in Equation (2); an ablation study is needed to show their benefit.  
- Weakness: need justification for using identical fully connected layer parameters for BRNN output o_t and the local coherence model q.  
- Weakness: unclear whether paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.  
- Reviewer states rebuttal addressed formulation, evaluation, and related-work concerns and recommends acceptance.  
- For acceptance: novel architecture, thorough evaluation, and relevance to vision–language tasks.  
- Against acceptance: unresolved clarity issues in related work claims, figure/equation presentation, evaluation metric choice, architectural justification, and paragraph-vector tuning.
