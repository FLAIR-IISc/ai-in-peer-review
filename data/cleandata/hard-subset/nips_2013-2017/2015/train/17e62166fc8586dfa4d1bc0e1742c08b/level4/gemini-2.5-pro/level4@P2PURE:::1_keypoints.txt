- Addresses sentence retrieval/sequencing for image sequences but omits related video-description literature (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly states the problem, and uses solid techniques.  
- Main contribution is CRCN, a neural model that learns image-sequence-to-sentence relationships and uses an entity-based coherence model to capture discourse.  
- Final sentence-generation method is primitive/ad-hoc: retrieve one sentence per image from similar training images and concatenate them.  
- Experimental methodology and anonymity concerns: dataset collection/crawling details are insufficient and likely indicate author overlap with cited work [11], compromising blind review.  
- Potential error in Equation (2): implies s_t is derived only from o_t, contradicting the claim of a fully connected network.  
- Recommendation: accept as a poster due to a strong model but weaknesses in generation method, missing citations, and anonymity/reproducibility issues.
