

Strengths:  
This paper addresses the challenge of generating coherent sentence sequences to describe image streams from blog posts, proposing a novel framework that retrieves K=5 semantically similar image-sentence pairs from training data and models sentence coherence via a bidirectional LSTM. The key innovation lies in the integration of a local coherence model [1] to ensure semantic consistency across generated sentences. The evaluation is comprehensive, including quantitative results (BLEU, METEOR), qualitative analysis, human evaluation, and ablation studies demonstrating the necessity of the coherence model. The authors commit to releasing code and dataset, enhancing reproducibility. The approach significantly outperforms baselines that neglect coherence, establishing a strong empirical foundation.

Weaknesses and Clarifications Needed:  
1. **Related Work Accuracy**: The claim that [5] lacks "mechanisms for coherence between sentences" (Line 94) is inaccurate. [5] explicitly models topic coherence across multi-sentence descriptions via its intermediate semantic representation. This misattribution should be corrected to avoid misleading readers.  

2. **Figure-Equation Mismatch**: Figure 2b depicts fully connected layers connected to *all* sentences in the sequence, while Equation (2) implies shared parameters connected *only to a single sentence vector*. This inconsistency requires clarification in the revised manuscript.  

3. **Evaluation Metric**: While BLEU is standard, METEOR is preferable for single-reference generation tasks (e.g., blog descriptions) due to its handling of synonymy. The authors should justify their choice of BLEU or include METEOR results to strengthen quantitative claims.  

4. **Redundant Linear Layers**: Equation (2) applies two linear transformations (W<sub>f1</sub>, W<sub>f2</sub>) sequentially. Since two linear layers are equivalent to a single linear layer, the benefit is unclear. An ablation study comparing this design to a single linear layer would clarify its necessity.  

5. **Parameter Sharing**: The same fully connected layers are used for both the bi-LSTM output (o<sub>t</sub>) and the local coherence model (q). The rationale for this shared parameterization is not explained. A brief justification would improve clarity.  

6. **Paragraph Vector Fine-Tuning**: The paper cites paragraph vectors [16] but does not specify whether they are fine-tuned during training or kept fixed. This is critical for reproducibility and should be explicitly stated.  

Post-Rebuttal:  
After reviewing the authors’ rebuttal, I confirm that all weaknesses except point 6 have been adequately addressed:  
- The [5] misattribution was corrected.  
- The figure-equation discrepancy was resolved via revised figures/text.  
- The METEOR justification was added, and the authors acknowledged BLEU’s limitations.  
- The ablation study for linear layers (point 4) was provided, showing marginal gains but retaining the design.  
- Parameter sharing rationale (point 5) was clarified.  

**However, point 6 remains unresolved**: The manuscript must explicitly state whether paragraph vectors [16] are fine-tuned or fixed. This is a minor but essential detail for replication. The authors should add a sentence in the methodology (e.g., "Paragraph vectors [16] are fine-tuned during training") or state they are fixed.  

Conclusion:  
This paper presents a compelling solution for coherent image-sequence description with rigorous evaluation. The authors have successfully addressed all major technical concerns raised in the review. The sole remaining requirement—clarifying paragraph vector fine-tuning—is straightforward to resolve. The work is well-motivated, technically sound, and advances the state-of-the-art in multimodal generation. I recommend acceptance pending this minor clarification.
