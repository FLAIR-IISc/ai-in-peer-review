

The paper addresses the challenge of generating coherent natural language descriptions for sequences of images from blog posts. It proposes retrieving the top K=5 most similar images and their associated sentences from the training set for each query image, then selecting the most relevant sentence sequence to provide a coherent description. The authors develop a multimodal architecture where sentences are first embedded as vectors, modeled using a bidirectional LSTM, and processed through a ReLU activation and fully connected layer before being scored against the query image sequence. A key innovation is the inclusion of a local coherence model [1] to ensure smooth transitions between sentences in the generated sequence.

Strengths: 
- The paper introduces a novel and effective architecture for generating coherent sentence sequences from image streams.
- It provides comprehensive evaluation through quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, and human studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk, demonstrating clear superiority over baselines that lack coherence modeling. The ablation study removing the coherence model [1] further validates the contribution.
- The authors commit to releasing both source code and the dataset used in their experiments.

Weaknesses:
1. Line 94 states that [5] lacks "a mechanism for coherence between sentences." While [5] is not primarily focused on this aspect, its approach models topic coherence across sentences through intermediate semantic representations of video content.
2. Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3 and Equation (2). The figure suggests fully connected layers connect to all sentences, but Equation (2) indicates parameter sharing across sentences while connecting to a single sentence vector.
3. Meteor (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) would provide a more appropriate automatic evaluation metric than BLEU, particularly when only a single reference sentence is available.
4. The rationale for using two sequential linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) in Equation (2) is unclear, as two linear transformations are mathematically equivalent to a single linear transformation. An ablation study demonstrating the benefit of this structure would be valuable.
5. The justification for using identical parameters for the BRNN output (ot) and coherence model (q) in Equation (2) requires clarification.
6. It is unclear whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.

Post-rebuttal: After reviewing the authors' responses, I recommend acceptance. The authors effectively addressed concerns regarding formulation, evaluation methodology, and related work. They have clarified the technical points, including the specific implementation details requested. The paper presents a compelling approach to generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams with thorough validation.
