- Addresses sequential image description problem and proposes CRCN combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Important extension of single-image captioning to image streams with practical motivation (photo/blog streams).
- Architecture is technically sound and the local coherence model for fluent transitions is a valuable contribution.
- Uses blog data as a practical text-image parallel corpus for training.
- Demonstrates quantitative and user-study improvements over baselines.
- Overlooks relevant video-captioning literature, which closely relates to image-stream description and should be discussed/cited.
- Sentence retrieval method (assigning and concatenating similar training sentences) is simplistic compared to the overall model complexity.
- Reliance on pre-trained paragraph vectors creates dependency on their quality and domain relevance; limitations should be discussed.
- Potential error in Equation (2) that may limit information flow and network expressiveness; needs clarification.
- Potential breach of anonymity: dataset reuse and overlap with prior work ([11]) and crawling method raise blind-review concerns.
- Recommendation: accept as a poster conditional on addressing related video-captioning work, dataset transparency, Equation (2) clarification, and discussing paragraph-vector limitations.
- Reviewer view balances strengths (novel approach, CRCN, coherence model, results) against weaknesses (related work omission, retrieval method, anonymity, Eqn issue).
