- Addresses image-sequence to sentence retrieval/generation, a significant CV/NLP problem.  
- Closely related to video captioning literature; missing discussion and comparisons to that body of work.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presents its contributions.  
- Introduces CRCN, a neural model for modeling relationships between image sequences and sentences.  
- Incorporates entity-based coherence modeling, a valuable discourse-aware contribution.  
- Core idea of learning image–sentence associations is sound.  
- Sentence generation is simplistic (retrieve similar sentences per image and concatenate), risking poor coherence and naturalness.  
- Data collection/reproducibility concerns: dataset not public, prior paper lacks crawling details, and possible authorship overlap raises anonymity doubts.  
- Equation (2) appears incorrect/unclear (s_t seems dependent only on o_t); needs clarification and confirmation of network connectivity.  
- Architecture is technically promising but current limitations (generation method, dataset transparency, equation clarity) justify not accepting as a full paper.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept as a poster to obtain feedback and allow revisions addressing data availability, equation clarity, and generation strategy.
