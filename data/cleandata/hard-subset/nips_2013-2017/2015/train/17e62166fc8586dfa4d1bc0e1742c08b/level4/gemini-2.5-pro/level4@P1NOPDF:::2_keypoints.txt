- Task & approach: Proposes retrieving K=5 nearest training images/sentences per query image and selecting the most relevant sentences to form a coherent multi-image description via sentence embeddings and sequence modeling.
- Model details: Embeds candidate sentences, passes them through a Bi-LSTM, applies ReLU and a fully connected layer to compute image–sentence compatibility, and adds a local coherence term from prior work.
- Main contribution: A novel method for selecting a coherent sequence of sentences across an image stream.
- Strength: Novel and effective architecture for coherent sentence selection.
- Strength: Extensive quantitative, qualitative, human evaluations and an ablation study showing the coherence model’s contribution.
- Strength: Authors will release code and dataset.
- Weakness: Related work claim overstates [5]’s lack of coherence mechanism; [5] models intermediate semantic/topic representations that induce some coherence.
- Weakness: Clarity issue — Figure 2b vs. Section 3.3/Equation 2 conflict about whether the FC layer operates jointly or per-sentence with shared parameters.
- Weakness: Evaluation metric — BLEU used, but METEOR may be more appropriate and better correlate with human judgments for single-reference settings.
- Weakness: Model architecture — two consecutive linear layers (W_f1 and W_f2) are redundant unless nonlinearities or other justification provided; request ablation to show benefit.
- Weakness: Parameter sharing — unclear why the same FC parameters are used for both Bi-RNN outputs and the local coherence term; authors should justify.
- Weakness: Implementation detail — unclear whether paragraph vectors [16] were fine-tuned or kept fixed during training.
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance after authors addressed main concerns.
- Post-rebuttal request: Include promised changes and clarify whether paragraph vectors were fine-tuned in the final paper.
