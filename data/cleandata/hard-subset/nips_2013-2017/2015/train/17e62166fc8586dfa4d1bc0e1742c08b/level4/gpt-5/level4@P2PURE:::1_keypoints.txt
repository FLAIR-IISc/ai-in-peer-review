- Addresses retrieval of natural sentences for image sequences and assembly into coherent sentence sequences; important but closely related to prior video-description work (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014) which is not cited.
- Paper is well written, easy to follow, with a clearly defined problem and solid technical approach.
- Proposes CRCN neural model that learns relationships between image sequences and sentence sequences; architecture appears technically sound.
- Uses entity-based coherence models to effectively capture discourse relationships.
- Sentence-sequence generation method is simplistic/ad hoc: each sentence is retrieved based on similarity to individual images and concatenated in image order.
- Possible anonymity breach: dataset description reuses and extends prior crawling method without disclosure, suggesting overlapping authorship with [11].
- Equation (2) may be incorrect or misleading because it implies s_t depends only on o_t, raising concerns about network connectivity.
- Overall recommendation: technically sound but has weaknesses in sentence generation and dataset handling; acceptable as a poster.
