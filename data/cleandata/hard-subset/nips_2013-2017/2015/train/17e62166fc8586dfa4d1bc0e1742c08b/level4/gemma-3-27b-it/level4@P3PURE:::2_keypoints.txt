- Task: Generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences (blog posts) by retrieving candidate sentences per image and selecting a coherent sequence.
- Method: Retrieve top K=5 similar images/sentences per query image, embed sentences, model the sequence with a bidirectional LSTM, apply ReLU and FC layers, score image-sentence compatibility, and incorporate a local coherence model between selected sentences.
- Contribution: Novel method to select relevant sentences from retrieved candidates to ensure coherence across an image sequence.
- Strength: Introduces an effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for image streams.
- Strength: Comprehensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations with an ablation study showing benefit of the local coherence model.
- Strength: Commitment to release source code and dataset for reproducibility.
- Weakness 1: Claim that [5] lacks sentence-coherence modeling is questionable since [5] predicts intermediate semantic video representations that capture topic coherence.
- Weakness 2: Figure 2b conflicts with Section 3.3/Equation (2): the figure implies fully connected layers connected to all sentences, while Eqn (2) implies parameter sharing and per-sentence connections.
- Weakness 3: BLEU is a suboptimal automatic metric here; METEOR would be more appropriate given single-reference evaluation.
- Weakness 4: Unclear rationale for two sequential linear functions (Wf2 and Wf1) in Eqn (2) since they can be collapsed; an ablation is needed to justify them.
- Weakness 5: Unclear why the same FC-layer parameters process both BRNN outputs (ot) and the local coherence model q in Eqn (2).
- Weakness 6 / Clarification request: Was the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned during training or kept fixed?
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance after authors addressed concerns about formulation, evaluation, and related work, but requests explicit clarification on paragraph vector fine-tuning.
