

This paper addresses the problem of retrieving natural sentences that describe a given sequence of images and forming a coherent sentence sequence. This task is significantly relevant to computer vision and pattern recognition, though it closely parallels the generation of multiple sentences describing video content (e.g., [Rohrbach et al., 2014]), where a video can be decomposed into shots and each shot described by a key frame. Notably, the paper fails to cite this related line of work.

The paper is well-written and accessible, with a clearly stated problem and a solution employing solid technical methods. The proposed CRCN neural network model effectively captures relationships between sequences of natural sentences and images. Its architecture is technically sound and incorporates discourse relationships through entity-based coherence modeling.

However, the sentence generation method is relatively straightforward and lacks sophistication: each sentence is directly associated with training images similar to the input image, and sequences are concatenated in image order without further refinement. In Section 4.1, the authors state, "We reuse the blog data of Disneyland from the dataset of [11], and newly collect the data of NYC, using the same crawling method with [11]." The dataset has not been disclosed, and the cited paper [11] does not detail the data collection methodology. This indicates the authors are likely the same as those in [11], undermining the double-blind review process. Equation (2) appears incorrect, as it suggests $s_t$ depends solely on $o_t$, implying the network is not fully connected. While the proposed model is technically sound and effectively models discourse coherence, the sentence generation approach is ad-hoc. Given these concerns, the paper would be suitable for a poster presentation.
