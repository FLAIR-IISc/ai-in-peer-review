This paper investigates sentence generation from an image stream and introduces a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that combines convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. Overall, it is a solid contribution, but there is room for improvement:

- Several relevant works on video-to-sentence are missing, e.g., “Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework.”
- While the proposed method achieves strong quantitative results, the user study in Table 2 shows performance similar to the RCN baseline. A significance test is needed to verify whether the observed improvements are reliable.

In summary, this is a nice algorithm for sentence generation from an image stream: the quantitative results are good, but the user study demonstrates only a weak advantage over the baselines.
