- Presents a novel method to generate consistent sentences describing an image sequence from blog posts.  
- Method: retrieve top-5 similar training images/sentences per query, embed sentences, bidirectional LSTM, ReLU+FC, compute image–sentence compatibility, add local coherence model.  
- Key contribution: selecting the most relevant sentences for a query image sequence to produce coherent multi-sentence descriptions.  
- Strength: proposes a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for image sequences.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation (quantitative, qualitative, human) showing superiority over baselines that lack coherence modeling.  
- Strength: includes an ablation study assessing the impact of removing the coherence model.  
- Strength: authors commit to releasing source code and dataset, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: disputed claim that prior work [5] lacks coherence—[5] models intermediate semantic/topic representations that yield coherence across sentences.  
- Weakness: apparent discrepancy between Figure 2b and Section 3.3/Equation (2) regarding connections between the fully connected layer and sentences.  
- Weakness: BLEU may be inappropriate with a single reference; reviewer suggests using METEOR for automatic evaluation.  
- Weakness: use of two sequential linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) in Equation (2) is unclear—an ablation study should justify this design.  
- Weakness: need clarification on whether the same parameters are used for the FC layers in the BRNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model q (Equation 2).  
- Weakness: unclear whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed formulation, evaluation, and related-work concerns; reviewer recommends acceptance but asks that promised changes be incorporated and paragraph-vector tuning be explicitly clarified.
