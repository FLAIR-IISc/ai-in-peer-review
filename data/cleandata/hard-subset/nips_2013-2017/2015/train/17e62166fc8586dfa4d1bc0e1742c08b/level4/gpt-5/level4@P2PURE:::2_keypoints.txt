- Task/contribution: Retrieves candidate sentences for each image and selects a coherent sequence using sentence embeddings, a BiLSTM + ReLU + FC scoring pipeline, and a local coherence model for adjacent sentences.  
- Strength: Proposes a novel, effective architecture for retrieving coherent multi-sentence descriptions.  
- Strength: Provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, human evaluations and an ablation study showing benefit of the coherence model.  
- Strength: Authors commit to releasing source code and dataset.  
- Weakness 1: Claim that prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is inaccurate—[5] models intermediate semantics that yield cross-sentence coherence.  
- Weakness 2: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2): FC layer connectivity vs. shared per-sentence parameters is unclear.  
- Weakness 3: Automatic evaluation should use METEOR rather than BLEU when only a single reference is available.  
- Weakness 4: Sequential application of two linear mappings (Wf2, Wf1) is questioned since their composition is linear; an ablation is needed to justify them.  
- Weakness 5: Unclear why the same fully connected parameters are used for both BRNN outputs o_t and the local coherence model q in Equation (2).  
- Weakness 6: Unclear whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed.  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance after satisfactory responses; requests promised revisions and final clarification on paragraph vector fine-tuning.
