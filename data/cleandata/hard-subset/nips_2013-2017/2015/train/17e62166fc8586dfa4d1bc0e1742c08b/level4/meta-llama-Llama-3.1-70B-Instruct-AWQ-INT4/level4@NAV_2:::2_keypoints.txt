- Proposes a method to describe a sequence of blog-post images with a sequence of coherent sentences by retrieving K=5 nearest image-sentence pairs per query image.  
- Embeds sentences into vectors and models the sentence sequence with a bidirectional LSTM to ensure coherence.  
- Processes BRNN outputs through ReLU and a fully connected layer, then scores image–sentence compatibility.  
- Incorporates a local coherence model to enforce sentence-to-sentence compatibility.  
- Introduces the Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and a local coherence module.  
- Extends prior image-captioning/multimodal work by focusing on sequence-level descriptions rather than per-image captions.  
- Provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations showing superiority over baselines that lack coherence.  
- Includes an ablation study removing the coherence model and promises to release code and dataset.  
- Strength: novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences across image sequences.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation including human studies and tackling a novel, challenging multimodal problem.  
- Weakness: minor clarity/correctness issues, notably a discrepancy between Figure 2b and Section 3.3/Equation (2).  
- Weakness: unclear rationale for using two linear functions in Equation (2) and for sharing fully connected layer parameters between o_t and the coherence model q.  
- Overall quality: technically sound with supported claims, well-written but needs minor revisions for clarity.  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the noted clarity and correctness issues.
