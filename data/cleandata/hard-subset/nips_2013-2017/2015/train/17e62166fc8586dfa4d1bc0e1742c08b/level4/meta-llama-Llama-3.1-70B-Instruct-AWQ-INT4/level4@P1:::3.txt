This paper proposes a novel approach for generating sentence sequences from an image stream, which is an extension of the traditional image-to-sentence generation task. The authors design a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) that integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN model is trained on a large dataset of blog posts with images and is evaluated using both quantitative measures and user studies.

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed model and its components, as well as the experimental setup and results. The quantitative results show that the proposed method outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines, including methods that generate single sentences from images.

However, there are a few areas that can be improved. Firstly, the authors could provide more discussion on the related work, particularly on video-to-sentence generation tasks. For example, the work of Xu et al. [33] on jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework is not mentioned.

Secondly, while the quantitative results of the proposed method look promising, the user study results in Table 2 show that the proposed method performs similarly to one of the baselines, RCN. To verify whether the improvement is reliable, a significance test is needed.

Overall, the paper presents a nice algorithm for generating sentence sequences from an image stream. The quantitative results look good, but the user study only shows a weak advantage over the baselines.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach for generating sentence sequences from an image stream.
* The CRCN model is well-designed and effectively integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model.
* The quantitative results show that the proposed method outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines.

Weaknesses:

* The related work section could be improved by including more discussion on video-to-sentence generation tasks.
* The user study results show that the proposed method performs similarly to one of the baselines, and a significance test is needed to verify whether the improvement is reliable.

Originality:

* The paper proposes a novel approach for generating sentence sequences from an image stream, which is an extension of the traditional image-to-sentence generation task.
* The CRCN model is a new architecture that effectively integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model.

Significance:

* The paper presents a new approach for generating sentence sequences from an image stream, which has potential applications in image captioning, video summarization, and human-computer interaction.
* The proposed method outperforms several state-of-the-art baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating coherent and fluent sentence sequences from image streams.

Clarity:

* The paper is well-structured and easy to follow.
* The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed model and its components, as well as the experimental setup and results.

Quality:

* The paper is well-written and free of errors.
* The experimental setup and results are well-presented and easy to understand.

Recommendation:

* The paper is a good candidate for publication, but the authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above, particularly the related work section and the user study results.
* A significance test should be performed to verify whether the improvement of the proposed method over the baselines is reliable.
