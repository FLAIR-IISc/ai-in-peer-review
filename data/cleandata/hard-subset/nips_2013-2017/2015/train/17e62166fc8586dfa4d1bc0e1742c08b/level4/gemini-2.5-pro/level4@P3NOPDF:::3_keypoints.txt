- Task and proposal: Introduces CRCN to generate descriptive sentences from image streams by combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Novelty: The entity-based local coherence mechanism is a novel and promising idea for improving narrative consistency.  
- Architecture: The CRCN design is well-motivated and provides a comprehensive framework for the problem.  
- Quantitative results: Demonstrates strong performance on automatic evaluation metrics, outperforming several baselines.  
- Missing related work: Literature review is incomplete; key video-to-text works (e.g., Venugopalan et al.) are not discussed or compared.  
- Human evaluation: User study shows CRCN only marginally outperforms the RCN baseline in human judgments.  
- Statistical significance: The paper lacks statistical significance testing for the human evaluation, so coherence improvements are not rigorously validated.  
- Conclusion: Solid contribution with strong quantitative results, but claims about improved coherence need better related-work comparison and rigorous statistical analysis of human studies.
