- Problem: Generating a sequence of natural-language sentences to describe a stream of images, extending single-image single-sentence captioning.
- Proposed model: Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN) combining CNN image features, bidirectional RNN sentence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Key innovation: Extends both input (image stream) and output (sentence sequence) dimensions to better mirror real-user photo sequences.
- Contribution 1: Introduces a new problem formulation for image-stream → sentence-sequence description (claimed first work on this specific task).
- Contribution 2: Develops an end-to-end CRCN architecture integrating CNNs, BRNNs, and a coherence model.
- Contribution 3: Leverages large-scale blog data (20K posts, ~140K images) to train without manual annotations.
- Evaluation: Shows improvements on BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall and via Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.
- Ablation: Comparisons (CRCN vs RCN vs 1NN) demonstrate the added value of the coherence model.
- Writing: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and explains technical details of the CRCN components.
- Strength: Novel, well-motivated formulation reflecting real-world behavior; technically sound and well-validated.
- Weakness: Insufficient citation/discussion of related video-captioning literature (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014).
- Weakness: Dataset collection methodology is vague, [11] lacks detail, and no public dataset release hurts reproducibility.
- Weakness: Generation approach is simplistic (K-NN on fc7 + sentence concatenation) and appears to use the coherence model mainly for ranking rather than sequence construction.
- Weakness: Notational issue in Equation (2) implying st derived from ot alone; requires clarification.
- Overall recommendation: Solid contribution; should be accepted as a poster at NIPS.
