This paper proposes an approach for retrieving a sequence of natural sentences for an image stream. The authors design a multimodal architecture called coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. The CRCN model is trained on a large dataset of unstructured blog posts, consisting of 20K blog posts with 140K associated images.

The authors' problem setup is motivated by the fact that general users often take a series of pictures on their memorable moments. They aim to automatically produce a sequence of natural language sentences that best describe the essence of the input image set. The CRCN model is a novel contribution, as it extends both input and output dimensions to a sequence of images and a sequence of sentences.

The authors evaluate their approach with quantitative measures (e.g., BLEU and top-K recall) and user studies via Amazon Mechanical Turk. The results show that their approach outperforms other state-of-the-art candidate methods, including log-bilinear multimodal models and recurrent convolutional models.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach and its components. The experimental results are also well-presented, with a thorough comparison with baseline methods.

However, there are a few aspects that can be improved. Firstly, the authors could provide more discussion on the related work, particularly on video-to-sentence generation. For example, the paper "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework" is not mentioned.

Secondly, while the quantitative results of the proposed method look quite good, the user study in Table 2 shows that it performs similarly to one baseline, RCN. A significance test is needed to verify whether the improvement is reliable.

Overall, this is a nice work that contributes to the field of image-to-sentence generation. With some minor improvements, it has the potential to be a strong paper.
