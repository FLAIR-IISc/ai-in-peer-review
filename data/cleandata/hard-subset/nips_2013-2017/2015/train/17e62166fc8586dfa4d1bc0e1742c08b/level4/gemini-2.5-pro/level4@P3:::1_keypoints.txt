- Tackles a novel, significant problem: retrieving coherent sentence sequences for image streams as an extension of single-image captioning.  
- Proposes CRCN: multimodal architecture combining CNN image features, BRNN for content flow, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Trains on a large-scale blog-scraped dataset and performs retrieval-based generation by selecting K-nearest candidate sentences per image and re-ranking combinations.  
- Evaluated extensively with automatic metrics (BLEU, R@K) and human AMT studies, showing superior performance to adapted baselines.  
- Strength: Explicit coherence model complements BRNN to handle abrupt visual changes between images.  
- Strength: Well-written, clearly organized paper with detailed experimental setup.  
- Strength: Thorough comparisons against strong baselines on a real-world dataset.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion and comparison to related video-description literature despite conceptual similarities.  
- Weakness: Retrieval-based generation limits expressiveness and creativity; outputs restricted to sentences seen in training rather than word-by-word generation.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns and potential breach of double-blind anonymity due to unclear dataset collection details and reliance on [11].  
- Weakness: Ambiguity in Equation (2) — unclear whether final scoring integrates full RNN sequence outputs.  
- Overall recommendation: More strengths than weaknesses; recommend acceptance (likely as a poster) after addressing reproducibility, related-work discussion, and architectural clarifications.
