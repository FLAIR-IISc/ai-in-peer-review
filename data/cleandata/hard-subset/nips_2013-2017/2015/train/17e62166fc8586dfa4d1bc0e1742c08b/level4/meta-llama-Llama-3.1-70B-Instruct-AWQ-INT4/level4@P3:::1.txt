This paper addresses the problem of retrieving natural sentences that describe a given sequence of images and forming a sequence of sentences. This problem is significant in the research fields of computer vision and pattern recognition, and it is closely related to generating multiple sentences that describe given video content. However, the authors do not cite any relevant work in this line of research, such as [Rohrbach+ 2014], where single video content can be regarded as a sequence of images.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the problem is well-stated and solved with a set of solid techniques. The proposed neural network model, named CRCN, acquires relationships between a sequence of natural sentences and a sequence of images. The architecture of the proposed model is technically sound, and it also describes discourse relationships well with the help of entity-based coherence models.

However, the method for generating a sequence of natural sentences is rather primitive. Every natural sentence is directly associated with training images similar to a given image, and all the sequences are simply concatenated in the order of the corresponding images. This approach may not be robust and may not generalize well to different scenarios.

In Section 4.1, the authors mention that they reuse the blog data of Disneyland from the dataset of [11] and newly collect the data of NYC using the same crawling method. However, the dataset has not been disclosed, and the corresponding paper does not describe the details of how to crawl the dataset. This raises concerns about the anonymity of the authors and the reproducibility of the results.

Equation (2) might be incorrect, as it implies that st can be derived from only ot and thus the network is not fully connected. This needs to be clarified by the authors.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the proposed model is well-designed. However, the method for generating a sequence of sentences is rather ad-hoc, and the lack of citations to relevant work in the field is a major concern. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, but the results may not be robust and generalizable.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a new neural network model that acquires relationships between a sequence of natural sentences and a sequence of images. However, the method for generating a sequence of sentences is not novel and is rather primitive.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses a significant problem in the research fields of computer vision and pattern recognition. However, the results may not be robust and generalizable, and the lack of citations to relevant work in the field is a major concern.

Overall, I think that this paper can be accepted as a poster paper as is, but it needs to address the concerns mentioned above to be considered for a full paper.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper addresses a significant problem in the research fields of computer vision and pattern recognition.
* The proposed neural network model is technically sound and well-designed.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The method for generating a sequence of sentences is rather primitive and may not be robust and generalizable.
* The lack of citations to relevant work in the field is a major concern.
* The dataset has not been disclosed, and the corresponding paper does not describe the details of how to crawl the dataset, which raises concerns about the anonymity of the authors and the reproducibility of the results.
