This work investigates sentence generation from image streams, introducing a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) composed of convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. 

Overall, this is a solid contribution, though several areas could benefit from further development. First, the related work section omits relevant papers in video-to-sentence generation, such as those employing unified frameworks for jointly modeling deep video and compositional text. Second, while the quantitative results appear promising, the user study presented in Table 2 indicates performance comparable to the RCN baseline. A statistical significance test is necessary to determine if the observed improvements are reliable. In summary, the proposed algorithm demonstrates a good approach to sentence generation from image streams, and the quantitative results are encouraging; however, the user study reveals only a marginal advantage over existing baselines.
