- Novel problem: formally defines and tackles image-stream-to-sentence-sequence generation, an important next step beyond single-image captioning.
- Proposed CRCN model: multimodal architecture combining CNN image features, a BRNN for cross-sentence semantic flow, and an entity-based coherence model for smooth transitions.
- Explicit coherence modeling: integrating a classic NLP coherence model is a key differentiator that prevents disjointed, independently generated sentences.
- Creative weak supervision: uses blog posts as scalable weakly-aligned image-text training data with a reasonable preprocessing pipeline.
- Strong empirical results: CRCN significantly outperforms strong baselines on two new datasets (NYC, Disneyland) using BLEU, R@K, and user studies; ablation validates component contributions.
- Missing related work: related work section should discuss video description literature (e.g., Xu et al., AAAI 2015) and compare modeling challenges with image streams.
- User study concerns: preference margins for short sequences are small; statistical significance testing (e.g., binomial/chi-squared) is needed to validate user-study claims.
- Retrieval/search clarity: divide-and-conquer beam search is briefly described; needs more detail on beam size choice (M=50), pruning risk, and trade-offs between search complexity and performance.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel, and well-motivated paper with strong results; weaknesses are minor and addressable.
