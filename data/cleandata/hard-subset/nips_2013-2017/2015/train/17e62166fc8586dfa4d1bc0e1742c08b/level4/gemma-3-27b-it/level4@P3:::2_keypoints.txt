- Problem: Generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for a sequence of blog-post images by retrieving consistent sentences.
- Method overview: For each query image, retrieve top K=5 similar image–sentence pairs from the training set and select the most relevant sentences for the sequence.
- Sentence modeling: Embed sentences into a vector space and model the sequence with a bidirectional LSTM to capture sentence-level context.
- Scoring and architecture: Feed biLSTM outputs through ReLU and fully connected layers, then compute compatibility scores between images and sentences.
- Coherence model: Incorporate a local coherence model to enforce compatibility between consecutive sentences.
- Strength: Proposes a novel and effective architecture (CRCN) for retrieving coherent sentences for image sequences.
- Strength: Provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations showing superiority over baselines, especially without coherence modeling.
- Strength: Includes an ablation study demonstrating the contribution of the coherence model.
- Strength: Authors plan to release code and dataset to facilitate reproducibility.
- Weakness: Incorrect claim about related work [5] lacking coherence—[5] models intermediate semantic representations that encourage coherence.
- Weakness: Figure 2b and Equation (2) are inconsistent—figure implies full connectivity across sentences while equation implies shared per-sentence parameters.
- Weakness: Evaluation relies on BLEU; METEOR should be reported or considered since it handles stemming and synonymy better with single references.
- Weakness: Equation (2) uses two sequential linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) that can be collapsed; unclear benefit without ablation evidence.
- Weakness: Unexplained parameter sharing—the same FC parameters are used for biRNN outputs and the local coherence model without justification.
- Weakness: Unclear whether paragraph vectors are fine-tuned during training or kept fixed; this affects learning interpretation.
- Overall assessment: Interesting, promising approach with strong empirical support but needs clarifications and justification for certain design choices.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel problem focus, strong empirical and user-study results, ablation evidence, and practical impact potential.
- Arguments against acceptance: Minor technical inaccuracies, insufficient justification for some design choices, and limited evaluation metrics.
- Post-rebuttal conclusion: Reviewer recommends acceptance after authors addressed concerns and clarified architecture, evaluation, and related-work issues.
