The paper addresses the problem of generating a coherent sequence of sentences to describe a sequence of images, such as those found in blog posts. The proposed approach first retrieves the K=5 most similar images and their corresponding sentences from the training data for each input image. The core contribution lies in a novel method for selecting the most relevant sentences to create a cohesive description. This is achieved by embedding sentences into a vector space and then modeling the resulting sequence using a bidirectional LSTM. The LSTM’s output is processed through ReLU and fully connected layers, and a compatibility score is calculated between each sentence and the input image. Furthermore, a local coherence model [1] is integrated to ensure consistency between adjacent sentences.

**Strengths / Positive Aspects:**

*   The paper introduces a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences given an image sequence.
*   The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation, including quantitative, qualitative, and human assessments, demonstrating the superiority of their approach over several baselines that do not incorporate coherence modeling. An ablation study specifically examining the impact of the coherence model [1] is also included.
*   The authors have committed to releasing both the source code and the dataset used in their experiments.

**Weaknesses / Questions / Unclarities:**

1.  Line 94 states that [5] lacks a mechanism for sentence coherence. However, [5] does predict an intermediate semantic representation of videos, which inherently provides coherence across sentences by modeling the overall topic of the multi-sentence description.
2.  Correctness/clarity: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with the description in section 3.3 and Equation (2). The figure suggests a fully connected layer connected to all sentences, while Equation (2) indicates parameter sharing across sentences, with connections only to the vector representing a single sentence.
3.  Given the use of a single reference sentence, a more appropriate automatic evaluation metric would be Meteor (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) rather than BLEU.
4.  The rationale for applying two linear functions (W{f2}, W{f1}) sequentially in Equation (2) is unclear, as two linear transformations can be consolidated into a single one. An ablation study investigating the benefit of this dual-linear structure would be valuable.
5.  Why are the same parameters used for the fully connected layers applied to both the bidirectional RNN output (o\_t) and the local coherence model q (Equation 2)?
6.  Was the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned during training, or was it kept fixed?

=== post rebuttal ===

After reviewing the rebuttal, I recommend this paper for acceptance. The authors have successfully addressed the concerns raised regarding the formulation, evaluation, and related work. 

The paper proposes an interesting new model to retrieve coherent sentences for an image stream, which is convincingly evaluated. However, to be a convincing paper, several clarifications have to be made. Specifically, please clarify whether the paragraph vector [16] was fine-tuned or kept fixed during training in the final version of the paper.
