- Task and contribution: Proposes CRCN to generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams, extending single-image captioning to sequential input/output.  
- Model: CRCN integrates CNN image features, BRNN for sentence-sequence context, and an entity-based local coherence model to enforce smooth sentence transitions.  
- Retrieval framing: Method retrieves candidate sentences per image from a blog-derived corpus and ranks whole candidate sequences with CRCN for visual relevance and textual coherence.  
- Evaluation: Extensive experiments on two large blog datasets using automatic metrics, qualitative examples, and an AMT user study show CRCN outperforms strong baselines.  
- Novelty/significance: Tackles a novel, practical problem (story-level photo description) with clear real-world relevance.  
- Technical contribution: Effectively combines deep vision/language models with an explicit entity-based coherence mechanism tailored to multi-sentence description.  
- Ablation: Comparison with a variant lacking the coherence component (RCN) demonstrates the importance of modeling sentence-to-sentence transitions.  
- Reproducibility: Authors plan to release code and dataset, aiding follow-up research.  
- Clarify architecture: Figure 2b and Equation (2) appear inconsistent about whether FC layers act on whole sequences or per-sentence—figure/text should be aligned.  
- Justify design choices: Provide ablation comparing two consecutive FC layers vs a single FC (with matched capacity/dropout) and quantify gains from sharing FC parameters between BRNN outputs and the coherence model.  
- Related work positioning: Soften the claim that Donahue et al. [5] has "no mechanism for the coherence between sentences" and clarify differences between explicit entity-based coherence and implicit recurrently learned coherence.  
- Missing detail: Clarify whether paragraph vectors used for sentence representations are fixed or fine-tuned during CRCN training.
