Summary
This paper addresses the task of generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for sequences of images from blog posts. For each query image, the method retrieves the K=5 most similar training images and their associated sentences. Sentences are embedded into vectors and modeled with a bidirectional LSTM to capture sequence-level dependencies. The bi-LSTM outputs are passed through a ReLU and fully connected layer, and a compatibility score is computed between each image and candidate sentence. A local coherence model [1] further enforces sentence-to-sentence compatibility to produce a coherent narrative across the image sequence.

Strengths
- The paper proposes a novel and effective retrieval-based architecture that explicitly models inter-sentence coherence, a key requirement for multi-sentence descriptions.
- The evaluation is thorough, including quantitative metrics, qualitative analysis, human studies, and an ablation showing the contribution of the coherence model [1].
- The promise to release code and the dataset will make the work reproducible and useful to the community.

Weaknesses and Questions
1) Claim about [5] (line 94): The paper states that [5] has “no mechanism for coherence between sentences.” While coherence is not the primary focus of [5], it does predict an intermediate semantic representation for videos that captures topic-level consistency across sentences. Please clarify this point and more precisely distinguish your contribution from topic-level modeling in [5].

2) Figure 2b vs. Section 3.3 / Equation (2): Figure 2b appears to show fully connected layers connected to all sentence representations, whereas Equation (2) suggests that the parameters are shared across time steps and applied only to the vector for a single sentence at a time. Please reconcile the figure with the text and equation, and state clearly whether the fully connected parameters are shared across positions and whether any cross-sentence connections exist at that stage.

3) Evaluation metric: BLEU can be brittle, especially with a single reference. METEOR (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) is generally considered more suitable for sentence-level evaluation with limited references. Please report METEOR (and, if feasible, other diversity- or recall-oriented metrics such as CIDEr or ROUGE-L) to provide a more robust assessment.

4) Two successive linear layers in Equation (2): Applying W_f2 after W_f1 without a nonlinearity between them is equivalent to a single linear transformation. What is the motivation for stacking them? If a nonlinearity is intended, please specify; otherwise, an ablation demonstrating any benefit of two linear layers would be helpful.

5) Parameter sharing between BRNN output and local coherence model: Why are the same fully connected parameters used both for the bi-LSTM output o_t and for the local coherence model q in Equation (2)? Intuitively, image–sentence compatibility and sentence–sentence coherence are related but distinct signals. Please justify this design choice or consider an ablation with separate parameters.

6) Paragraph vector [16]: Is the paragraph vector fine-tuned during training or kept fixed? Please clarify the training protocol (e.g., initialization, layers updated, learning rates), and, if possible, report performance differences with and without fine-tuning.

Additional Comments and Suggestions
- Provide more detail on the retrieval stage (e.g., features used, distance metric, and whether K=5 was tuned). A brief sensitivity analysis on K would strengthen the design choices.
- Clarify the exact form of the compatibility score between image and sentence embeddings, including normalization, margin or loss, and negative sampling strategy, if any.
- Include a brief error analysis highlighting typical coherence failures (e.g., entity drift, temporal ordering) and how the local coherence term mitigates them.

Post-rebuttal Update
After reading the rebuttal, I recommend acceptance. The authors addressed key issues regarding the formulation, evaluation metrics, and related work, and clarified several implementation details. The empirical results, together with the ablation on the coherence component, convincingly support the contribution.

Please ensure the final version:
- Corrects the Figure 2b and Equation (2) inconsistency and clearly states parameter sharing choices.
- Reports METEOR (and, if possible, additional metrics) alongside BLEU.
- Provides a precise discussion of how [5] handles topic-level consistency and how this work advances beyond that.
- Clarifies whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or fixed, and briefly reports any observed impact on performance.
- Includes links to the promised code and dataset.

Overall Assessment
The paper presents a well-motivated and carefully evaluated approach to retrieving coherent sentence sequences for image streams. The architecture is sound, the coherence modeling is novel in this context, and the empirical evidence is strong. With the requested clarifications and metric updates, the paper will make a valuable contribution. I recommend acceptance.
