The paper tackles the task of producing a sequence of mutually consistent sentences to describe a sequence of blog-post images. To this end, for each query image, the method first retrieves the K=5 most similar images and their associated sentences from the training set. The core contribution is a mechanism for selecting the most relevant sentences for the entire query image sequence to yield a coherent narrative. Sentences are embedded as vectors, and the sentence sequence is modeled with a bidirectional LSTM. The bi-LSTM output is passed through a ReLU and a fully connected layer, then evaluated via an image–sentence compatibility score. A local coherence model [1] is additionally incorporated to enforce inter-sentence compatibility.

Strength / positive aspects:
- The paper introduces a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for an image sequence.
- It offers extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations demonstrating superiority over several baselines that do not model coherence, along with an ablation removing the coherence model [1].
- The authors commit to releasing the source code and dataset.

Weaknesses / Questions / Unclarities:
1. Line 94: The paper states that [5] has “no mechanism for coherence between sentences.” While coherence is not the main contribution of [5], it does predict an intermediate semantic representation for videos that maintains coherence across sentences by modeling the topic of the multi-sentence description.
2. Correctness/clarity: Figure 2b does not appear to match Section 3.3/Equation (2). Figure 2b suggests the fully connected layer connects to all sentences, whereas Eq. (2) implies parameter sharing across sentences with connections only to the vector for a single sentence.
3. For automatic evaluation of generated sentences, METEOR (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) is preferable to BLEU, especially when only a single reference sentence is available.
4. Why are two linear functions (W{f2}, W{f1}) applied sequentially in Eq. (2)? Since the composition of two linear maps is linear, the benefit is unclear. An ablation demonstrating the utility of these functions would be informative.
5. Why are the same fully connected parameters used for both the BRNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model q (Eq. 2)?
6. Is the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned or kept fixed?

After reading the rebuttal I recommend acceptance. The authors have satisfactorily addressed issues related to formulation, evaluation, and related work. Please implement the promised changes in the final version and also clarify the following point in the final: 6. Is the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned or kept fixed? The paper presents an interesting new model for retrieving coherent sentences for an image stream with convincing evaluation, but several clarifications are needed to make the paper fully convincing.
