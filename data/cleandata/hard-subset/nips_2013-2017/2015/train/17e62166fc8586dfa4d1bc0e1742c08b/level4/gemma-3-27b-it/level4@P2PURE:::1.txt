This paper addresses the problem of retrieving and sequencing natural language sentences to describe a given sequence of images. This is a significant problem within computer vision and pattern recognition, closely related to video description (e.g., [Rohrbach+ 2014]). A video can be considered a sequence of images – specifically, video shots which can be represented by keyframes – yet the paper does not cite relevant work in this area. 

The paper is clearly written and easy to understand, with a well-defined problem and a solution employing sound techniques. 

The proposed CRCN model effectively learns relationships between sequences of sentences and images. Its architecture is technically well-designed and effectively incorporates discourse relationships through entity-based coherence modeling.

However, the sentence generation method is relatively simplistic: sentences are directly associated with training images similar to the input, and sequences are formed by simple concatenation based on image order. 

Regarding data collection, Section 4.1 states, "We reuse the blog data of Disneyland from the dataset of [11], and newly collect the data of NYC, using the same crawling method with [11]," but the dataset itself is not publicly available and [11] lacks details regarding the crawling procedure. This overlap in authorship between this work and [11] raises concerns about a potential breach of anonymity.

Furthermore, Equation (2) appears to be flawed, as it suggests that *s<sub>t</sub>* is determined solely by *o<sub>t</sub>*, implying a lack of full connectivity within the network. While the proposed neural network model is technically sound and utilizes entity-based coherence models to good effect, the method for generating sentences from an image sequence feels *ad hoc*. I recommend acceptance as a poster.
