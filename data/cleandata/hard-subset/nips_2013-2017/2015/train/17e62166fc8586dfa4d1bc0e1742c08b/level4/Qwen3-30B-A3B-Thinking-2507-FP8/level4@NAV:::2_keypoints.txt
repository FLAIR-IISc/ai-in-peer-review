- Task & approach: Generates coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences by retrieving K=5 similar image–sentence pairs per image and selecting sentences with a novel coherence-aware method.  
- Model: Sentences are embedded and modeled with a bidirectional LSTM; outputs pass through a ReLU FC layer and are scored by an image–sentence compatibility metric, with an added local coherence model.  
- Strength: Proposes a novel, effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for image sequences.  
- Strength: Provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations showing superiority over baselines that ignore coherence.  
- Strength: Includes an ablation study demonstrating the coherence model’s contribution.  
- Strength: Authors commit to releasing source code and dataset.  
- Weakness: Claim that prior work [5] lacks coherence is disputable—[5] models coherent intermediate semantics via topic modeling across sentences.  
- Weakness: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2) regarding fully connected layer connections and parameter sharing.  
- Weakness: Evaluation metric choice: METEOR is a better automatic metric than BLEU for single-reference sentence generation and should be used.  
- Weakness: Equation (2) applies two sequential linear functions (W_f2, W_f1), which is redundant unless justified; an ablation is needed.  
- Weakness: Unclear why identical parameters are used for the BRNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model (q) in Equation (2).  
- Weakness: It is not specified whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or kept fixed—clarification required.  
- Outcome: After rebuttal, reviewer recommends acceptance; requests final-version clarification on whether paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or fixed.
