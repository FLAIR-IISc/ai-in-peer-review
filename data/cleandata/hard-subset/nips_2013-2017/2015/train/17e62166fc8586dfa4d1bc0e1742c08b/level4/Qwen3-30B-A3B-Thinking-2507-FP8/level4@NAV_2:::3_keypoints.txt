- Proposes CRCN: integrates CNN image representations, bidirectional RNN language modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model for generating sentence sequences from image streams.  
- Extends single-image captioning to sequence-to-sequence generation for image streams, motivated by event photo collections.  
- Leverages blog posts as large-scale, natural text-image parallel data — a novel data source vs. prior captioning work.  
- Contributions: (1) defines the sentence-sequence-from-images problem, (2) develops a unified CRCN architecture, (3) demonstrates results on large-scale blog datasets.  
- Evaluation uses automated metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, top-K recall) plus Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-structured, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Original compared to video-to-sentence work by explicitly modeling coherence to handle abrupt visual changes in image streams.  
- Related work omission: does not discuss important prior video-text work by Xu et al. [26], which significantly overlaps.  
- Empirical concern: user study shows CRCN is similar to RCN (without coherence model), implying marginal benefits from the coherence component.  
- Methodological gap: no statistical significance tests reported to verify whether observed improvements are reliable.  
- Overall significance is high; recommend minor revisions to address related work and add significance testing before acceptance.
