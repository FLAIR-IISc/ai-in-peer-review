- Extends image captioning to sequential image streams, an important and relevant problem for CV and NLP.  
- Fails to cite related video-captioning work (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014), overlooking relevant literature.  
- Paper is well-written with clear problem statement and motivation.  
- Proposes CRCN: a technically well-designed architecture combining CNNs for image representation and RNNs for language.  
- Adds an entity-based local coherence model, a key contribution to maintain consistency across sentences.  
- Sentence retrieval and sequence construction (concatenate sentences from similar images) is simplistic and may limit nuance.  
- Reproducibility concerns: reused dataset and newly crawled data lack public availability and detailed collection procedures; potential author overlap threatens anonymity.  
- Possible flaw in Equation (2) suggesting limited connection between st and ot, raising concerns about network connectivity.  
- Overall approach is technically sound but the sequence-generation method feels somewhat ad-hoc.  
- Strong quantitative and qualitative results reported, outperforming baselines.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, solid CRCN architecture, strong results, effective coherence modeling.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing video-captioning citations, unclear/ unavailable data, simplistic retrieval method, potential equation error.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept as a poster.
