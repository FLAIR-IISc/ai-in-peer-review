The paper addresses the task of producing a sequence of coherent natural-language sentences for a sequence of images drawn from blog posts. The method first retrieves, for each query image, the K=5 most similar training images together with their associated sentences. The core contribution is a mechanism to select and assemble the most relevant sentences into a coherent description for the entire image stream. Sentences are embedded in a vector space and their sequence is modeled with a bidirectional LSTM. The bi-LSTM outputs are passed through a ReLU and a fully connected layer, and a compatibility function scores image–sentence alignment. In addition, a local coherence component [1] is incorporated to encourage sentence-to-sentence consistency.

Strengths / positive aspects:
- Introduces a new and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentence sequences conditioned on an image stream.
- Provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human-subject evaluations demonstrating advantages over several baselines that omit coherence, along with an ablation that removes the coherence model [1].
- The authors commit to releasing the code and dataset.

Weaknesses / questions / points needing clarification:
1. Line 94: The paper states that [5] has “no mechanism for the coherence between sentences.” While coherence is not the main focus of [5], it predicts an intermediate semantic representation for videos that captures a topic across multiple sentences, which does impart cross-sentence coherence.
2. Correctness/clarity: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2). The figure suggests the fully connected layers connect to all sentences, whereas Eq. (2) indicates parameters shared across time that operate on a single sentence vector at a time.
3. For automatic evaluation, METEOR (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) is generally preferable to BLEU, especially when only one reference is available.
4. Why apply two linear mappings in Eq. (2) (W{f1} followed by W{f2})? Since a composition of linear maps is itself linear, the benefit is unclear. An ablation demonstrating the impact of this design choice would be informative.
5. What is the rationale for using the same fully connected parameters for both the BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence vector q in Eq. (2)?
6. Are the paragraph vectors [16] fine-tuned within the model or kept fixed?

=== post rebuttal ===
After reading the rebuttal, I recommend acceptance. The authors satisfactorily resolved issues concerning the formulation, evaluation, and positioning relative to prior work. Please incorporate the promised revisions in the final version and also explicitly clarify point (6): are the paragraph vectors [16] updated during training or frozen? Overall, the paper presents an interesting new model for retrieving coherent sentence sequences for image streams with convincing empirical support, but a few clarifications are necessary to make the presentation fully compelling.
