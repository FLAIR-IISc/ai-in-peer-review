- Task: generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams; authors propose CRCN for this problem.  
- Model: CRCN combines a CNN for image features, a BRNN for sequence context, and an entity-based local coherence module.  
- Data: large-scale blog posts (NYC and Disneyland) are used as natural image-text training corpora.  
- Training: model optimized with a max-margin objective to rank correct image-sentence alignments higher.  
- Evaluation: tested on two large datasets with quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, retrieval) and AMT user studies, outperforming baselines.  
- Strength — originality: novel and significant move from single-image captioning to multi-image narrative generation.  
- Strength — architecture: technically sound integration of CNN+BRNN with an explicit coherence model to produce fluent multi-sentence text.  
- Strength — data collection: effective, scalable use of unstructured blogs as in-the-wild parallel data.  
- Strength — evaluation: comprehensive comparison using multiple metrics and human studies.  
- Weakness — related work: misses discussion of relevant video description literature that models temporal visual-semantic relationships.  
- Weakness — user study: preference gains for CRCN over RCN are small for short sequences; statistical significance tests on user data are needed.  
- Weakness — retrieval/search: pragmatic divide-and-conquer beam search at test time needs discussion or analysis of how often it misses optimal sequences.  
- Overall: well-executed, valuable contribution with minor gaps in contextualization and experimental analysis.
