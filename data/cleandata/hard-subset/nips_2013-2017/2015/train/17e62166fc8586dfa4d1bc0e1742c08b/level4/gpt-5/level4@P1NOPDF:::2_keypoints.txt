- Task & goal: Retrieve K=5 similar training images per query and select mutually coherent sentences to describe an image sequence.  
- Core method: Embed sentences, model their sequence with a bidirectional LSTM, apply ReLU + FC, score via image–sentence compatibility, and enforce local coherence [1].  
- Strength: Proposes a novel, effective architecture for retrieving coherent multi-sentence descriptions.  
- Strength: Extensive evaluation—quantitative, qualitative, human studies—and an ablation removing the coherence model.  
- Strength: Authors commit to releasing source code and the dataset.  
- Weakness: Comparison to [5] is overstated; [5] models intermediate semantic representations that can yield cross-sentence coherence and the comparison should be nuanced.  
- Weakness: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3 / Eq. (2); the depiction of the FC layer’s connectivity needs reconciliation with the formal description.  
- Weakness: Evaluation metric choice—report METEOR (better for single-reference generation) or justify using BLEU.  
- Weakness: Architecture question—two sequential linear transforms (W_{f2}, W_{f1}) lack clear benefit without an intervening nonlinearity; ablation needed.  
- Weakness: Parameter sharing concern—the same FC parameters are used for BRNN outputs and the local coherence model q; justify or show results without sharing.  
- Weakness: Clarify whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance; authors addressed concerns but must incorporate changes and explicitly clarify point (6) in the final version.
