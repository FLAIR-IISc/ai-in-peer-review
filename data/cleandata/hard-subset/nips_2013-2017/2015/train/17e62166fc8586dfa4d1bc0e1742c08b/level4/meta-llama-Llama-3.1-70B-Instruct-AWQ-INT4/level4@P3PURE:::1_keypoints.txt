- Addresses retrieving natural sentences for image sequences and forming sentence sequences; problem is significant in CV/PR but closely related to video captioning (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014), which is not cited.
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, with a clearly stated problem and solid techniques.
- Proposed CRCN model effectively captures relationships between image and sentence sequences and is technically sound.
- Uses entity-based coherence models to describe discourse relationships successfully.
- Sentence-sequence generation method is reasonable but primitive/ad-hoc: match each image to similar training sentences and concatenate by image order.
- Dataset concerns: reuses Disneyland blog data from [11] and collects NYC data via the same crawl, but the dataset and crawling details are undisclosed.
- Anonymity concern: undisclosed dataset and reuse of [11] data suggest the authors may be those of [11].
- Technical issue: Equation (2) appears incorrect, implying st depends only on ot and that the network may not be fully connected.
- Overall recommendation: acceptable as a poster paper despite the noted shortcomings.
