- Task: Generate a coherent paragraph describing an unordered stream of images rather than independent captions for each image.
- Method: Proposes CRCN combining CNN image features, a bidirectional RNN for sentence flow, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Output generation: Retrieval-based—candidate sentences are retrieved from training data and CRCN ranks/selects the best sequence.
- Data: Trained and evaluated on two large blog datasets (NYC, Disneyland) assembled from blog posts; human evaluations conducted on MTurk.
- Strength: Novel and significant problem formulation, moving from single-image captions to sequence-level descriptions.
- Strength: Technically sound architecture; integration of explicit entity-based coherence is novel and useful for discourse quality.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation against baselines using multiple automatic metrics and human studies on real-world data.
- Strength: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and experimentally well-described.
- Weakness: Omits connection to extensive video description literature, which is conceptually similar and should be discussed and compared.
- Weakness: Retrieval-based sentence generation limits expressiveness and cannot produce novel sentences tailored to inputs.
- Weakness: Reproducibility and anonymity concerns—dataset collection details are undisclosed and data is not publicly available.
- Weakness: Potential technical inaccuracy in Equation (2) that may prevent coherence features from influencing sentence-level representations; needs clarification.
- Overall recommendation: Valuable contribution but with significant issues to address (related work, dataset disclosure, technical clarity); acceptance as a poster recommended pending fixes.
