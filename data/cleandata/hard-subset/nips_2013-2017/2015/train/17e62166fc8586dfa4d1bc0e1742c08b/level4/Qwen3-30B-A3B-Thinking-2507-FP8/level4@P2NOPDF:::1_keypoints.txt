- Addresses the task of generating natural language descriptions for image sequences, an important problem in CV and pattern recognition.
- The work closely parallels established video captioning research (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014) but fails to cite relevant prior work, undermining its novelty claim.
- The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the problem.
- The proposed CRCN model is technically sound and captures relationships between image sequences and sentence sequences.
- Use of entity-based coherence models to represent discourse relationships is a notable strength.
- The sentence generation method is overly simplistic: it matches sentences to similar training images without using sequential context and concatenates sentences by image order, failing to model narrative flow.
- Section 4.1’s claim of using the “same crawling method” as [11] lacks disclosure; dataset collection methodology is not described and may violate anonymity or proper attribution.
- Equation (2) appears inconsistent (s_t depending only on o_t), suggesting a possible error or mis-specified connectivity that requires clarification.
- Experimental setup is reasonable and technical contributions are valid despite issues.
- The paper could be acceptable as a poster, but the citation gap and data collection concerns must be addressed in revision.
- The work would benefit from stronger engagement with video captioning literature and a more rigorous sentence generation mechanism.
