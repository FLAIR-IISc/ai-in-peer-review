- Problem: Retrieve a sequence of natural-language sentences to describe a stream of images, aiming for coherent multi-sentence narratives rather than isolated captions.
- Proposed method: CRCN combines a CNN for image features, a bidirectional RNN for content flow, and an entity-based local coherence model to ensure smooth sentence transitions.
- Training and evaluation: Model trained on large blog-post dataset (one newly collected) and outperforms baselines on automatic metrics and human AMT evaluations.
- Novelty claim caveat: The paper claims to be first at sequence-to-sequence image description but neglects related video description work that addresses similar problems.
- Distinction needed: Authors should clarify how sparse, user-curated photo streams differ from dense video frames and why the coherence model is better suited to the former.
- Strength — significance/originality: Tackles an important next-step problem and introduces a novel integration of BRNN and explicit entity-based coherence modeling.
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow regarding problem, method, and experiments.
- Strength — quality: CRCN is technically sound, experiments are thorough, and results convincingly show the benefit of modeling sentence-to-sentence transitions.
- Weakness — related work: Overlooks substantial video description literature, weakening positioning and novelty claims.
- Weakness — generation method: Uses a two-stage retrieval-and-rerank approach, so it’s not fully generative and is limited by corpus sentences.
- Weakness — reproducibility: New dataset collection lacks public release and referenced collection method is insufficiently detailed, hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness — technical clarity: Equation (2) is unclear about how image and text representations are combined in the fully connected layers.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a significant problem, introduces an effective CRCN architecture with coherence modeling, and presents strong empirical evidence.
- Arguments against acceptance: Overstated novelty due to omitted related work, reliance on retrieval rather than generation, and dataset/reproducibility concerns.
- Overall recommendation: Solid, valuable contribution worth acceptance (possibly as a poster) if authors address related-work, generation-limitations, reproducibility, and technical clarifications.
