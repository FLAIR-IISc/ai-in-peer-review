- Problem/method: CRCN retrieves K nearest training images per query, then assembles coherent multi-sentence descriptions using VGG fc7 features, a BRNN over paragraph vectors, and an entity-grid coherence model, trained with a max-margin loss and approximate beam search.
- Evaluation/data: Tested on large blog-derived NYC and Disneyland datasets against strong baselines (CNN+RNN/LSTM, log-bilinear, global matching) with ablations.
- Strength: Timely move beyond single-image captioning to coherent multi-sentence descriptions of image streams.
- Strength: Novel architecture combining BRNN content-flow modeling with an entity-grid coherence model in a unified scoring framework.
- Strength: Construction/use of large, realistic blog-based image–text datasets and practical candidate generation/search.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation using BLEU/CIDEr/METEOR, retrieval metrics, ablations, and AMT human studies showing consistent gains.
- Weakness: Related work nuance—claim about [5] lacking coherence modeling should be softened to acknowledge its temporal semantic representation/topic continuity.
- Weakness: Clarity issue—Figure 2(b) appears inconsistent with Eq. (2); data flow and parameter sharing between FC layers, BRNN outputs, and coherence vector need clearer depiction.
- Weakness: Metrics—METEOR should be highlighted (especially with single reference) and consider reporting diversity/novelty or coherence-sensitive metrics while noting limitations.
- Weakness: Architecture ablation—two linear layers in Eq. (2) are justified by dropout but require an ablation (one vs. two layers) or brief supplemental justification.
- Weakness: Parameter sharing—using the same FC for BRNN outputs and coherence vector needs motivation and empirical evidence about whether separate parameters hurt performance.
- Weakness: Paragraph vectors—clarify whether doc2vec paragraph vectors are fine-tuned during CRCN training or kept fixed and discuss potential gains from fine-tuning.
- Weakness: Generality/data assumptions—reliance on blog-aligned image–text and accurate parsing/coreference may limit robustness across domains and under parsing noise.
- Quality: Technical approach is sound with appropriate training/optimization/search details; minor clarity issues remain.
- Clarity: Paper is readable but should tighten Section 3.3 vs. Figure 2(b), related work around [5], and state paragraph-vector fine-tuning; add a schematic for candidate generation/search.
- Originality: The task formulation and integration of an entity-grid coherence model with CNN/BRNN scoring for image streams are novel.
- Significance: Practically relevant and understudied task with consistent gains and promised datasets/code likely to increase impact.
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses important problem, compelling integration of vision/language/discourse, strong empirical results, scalable search, and realistic datasets.
- Cons against acceptance: Needs clearer architectural descriptions and ablations, potential limited generalization due to retrieval/domain assumptions and noisy parsing, and a minor overstatement about prior work [5].
- Recommendation/post-rebuttal: After rebuttal issues were addressed, reviewer recommends acceptance and asks the final version to clarify Figure 2(b)/Section 3.3, refine discussion of [5], state paragraph-vector fine-tuning and impact, and justify/ablate the two FC layers and parameter sharing.
