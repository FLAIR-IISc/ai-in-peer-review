- Tackles retrieval of natural sentences for image sequences and assembly into sentence sequences; important problem but closely related video-description work (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014) is not cited.  
- Paper is clearly written, easy to follow, and the problem is well formulated.  
- Proposed CRCN models relationships between image and sentence sequences; architecture is technically sound and captures discourse via entity-based coherence models.  
- Sentence-sequence generation method is reasonable but fairly rudimentary: each sentence tied to similar training images and concatenated by image order.  
- Dataset issues: reuse of Disneyland blog data and newly collected NYC data with no release or crawling details; suggests author overlap with [11] and compromises anonymity.  
- Possible error in Equation (2): it implies s_t depends solely on o_t, suggesting the network may not be fully connected as intended.  
- Overall judgment: model is solid but sentence assembly is ad hoc; recommend acceptance as a poster.
