- Addresses retrieval/composition of natural sentences for image sequences, an important CV/PR problem.  
- Omits related work on multi-sentence video description (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014).  
- Paper is well written; problem and approach are clearly presented.  
- CRCN architecture is technically sound and models sequence-to-sequence image–sentence relationships effectively.  
- Entity-based coherence models successfully capture discourse relations.  
- Sentence generation is primitive/ad hoc (one sentence per image with simple concatenation), likely limiting coherence and diversity.  
- Data collection/disclosure concerns: reuse of Disneyland blog data and newly collected NYC crawl are undocumented.  
- Possible double-blind breach / authorship overlap with prior dataset; lack of dataset release hampers reproducibility.  
- Equation (2) appears incorrect or ambiguous (s_t seems to depend only on o_t); needs clarification on connectivity.  
- Overall: strong architecture but issues with sentence-generation pipeline, related-work coverage, and dataset/anonymity; reviewer leans to accept as a poster.
