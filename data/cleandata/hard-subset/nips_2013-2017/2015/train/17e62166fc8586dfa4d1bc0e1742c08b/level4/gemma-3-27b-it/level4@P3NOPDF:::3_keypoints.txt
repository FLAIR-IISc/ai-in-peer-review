- Proposes CRCN combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model for image-stream-to-sentence generation.
- CRCN is well-motivated and clearly presented, showing promising potential.
- Quantitative results are strong and generally support the method’s efficacy.
- Related work section is incomplete; key video-to-sentence publications are missing and should be contrasted.
- User study shows only a weak advantage over the RCN baseline; differences are not substantial.
- No statistical significance testing (p-values or confidence intervals) is reported for the user study.
- User study lacks essential methodological details (participant demographics, instructions, evaluation criteria).
- Presentation and reproducibility of the user study need improvement.
- The paper should discuss limitations, especially for complex or ambiguous visual scenes.
- Recommendation (accept/reject/weak accept) depends on authors’ revisions, particularly addressing statistical concerns.
