This paper tackles sentence generation from an image stream and introduces the Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN). The model integrates convolutional neural networks for visual encoding, bidirectional recurrent neural networks for sequence modeling, and an entity-based local coherence component to maintain discourse-level consistency. The approach is well-motivated and technically sound.

Strengths:
- Clear problem formulation and a principled architecture that explicitly targets local coherence.
- Strong quantitative results on standard metrics, indicating the model’s effectiveness.

Areas for improvement:
- Related work coverage: Important prior work on video-to-text is missing. In particular, please discuss and compare against “Jointly Modeling Deep Video and Compositional Text to Bridge Vision and Language in a Unified Framework,” and situate CRCN’s contributions relative to such approaches. Clarifying conceptual and empirical differences would strengthen the paper’s positioning.
- Human evaluation: Although the automatic metrics look good, the user study in Table 2 shows performance close to the RCN baseline. Statistical significance testing is needed to establish whether the observed improvements are reliable. Reporting p-values or confidence intervals would help interpret the practical importance of the gains, and discussing effect sizes would provide further context.

Overall, this is a nice piece of work with a compelling architecture for generating coherent sentences from image streams. The quantitative results are promising, but the human study currently indicates only a modest advantage over baselines. Expanding the related work discussion and adding significance analyses would substantially improve the paper.
