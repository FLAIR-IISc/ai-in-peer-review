- Paper addresses generating multi-sentence descriptions for image streams by retrieving and assembling sentences from blog corpora (CRCN: CNN + BRNN + entity coherence).
- Training uses max-margin alignment on weakly supervised image–sentence sequences from crawled blogs; inference retrieves K-NN sentences per image and assembles with divide-and-conquer beam search.
- Experiments on NYC and Disneyland domains show improvements over strong single-image captioning baselines on BLEU/METEOR/CIDEr, retrieval metrics, and AMT user studies, with larger gains for longer sequences.
- Important problem closely related to multi-sentence video-to-text work, but that literature is under-cited and under-discussed.
- Strength: clear problem formulation extending single-image captioning to streams with multi-sentence outputs.
- Strength: technically sound multimodal architecture combining CNN features, a BRNN for content flow, and an entity-grid coherence model.
- Strength: sensible training objective, practical inference, large-scale weakly supervised data use, and comprehensive automatic plus human evaluation with ablations.
- Weakness: test-time sequence construction is primitive—nearest-neighbor per image and concatenation make the system effectively retrieval-based and limit expressiveness/robustness.
- Weakness: insufficient citation/discussion of multi-sentence video-description literature despite close connections.
- Weakness: dataset disclosure and reproducibility issues—Disneyland reuse and new NYC crawl are not detailed, raising anonymity and reproducibility concerns.
- Weakness: possible error/ambiguity in Equation (2); it appears to imply s_t depends only on o_t and needs clarification of dimensions and dependencies.
- Quality: components are standard and well-integrated; entity-based coherence is appropriate and implemented cleanly, but decoding strategy is ad hoc and could be improved.
- Clarity: paper is generally well written, but should expand related-work on video descriptions, clarify dataset crawling/preprocessing, and fix Eq. (2) and dimensionality explanations.
- Originality: novel extension of captioning to image streams with neural entity-grid coherence is a useful contribution; retrieval-based assembly is less novel.
- Significance: task is important and results are promising; resolving dataset and decoding issues would increase impact for applications like photo storytelling.
- Specific suggestion: correct/clarify Eq. (2) and explicitly state variable dimensions and how coherence influences all positions.
- Specific suggestion: cite and compare to multi-sentence video-description work and clarify differences (retrieval vs. generation, entity coherence).
- Specific suggestion: replace per-image NN retrieval with stronger sequence-level decoding (global CRF/ILP or neural sequence generation with coherence features).
- Specific suggestion: provide more dataset/crawling/segmentation details or release data/tools while preserving anonymity; report sensitivity to K, beam size, and sequence length.
- Specific suggestion: discuss cross-domain generalization since paragraph vectors are trained per domain.
- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated task, solid architecture integrating discourse coherence, strong empirical and human-validated results, clear writing.
- Cons for acceptance: simplistic retrieval-based decoding, weak engagement with video-to-text literature, dataset/anonymity/reproducibility concerns, and ambiguity in Eq. (2).
- Recommendation: Accept as a poster contingent on improving decoding, clarifying equations, expanding related work, and addressing dataset disclosure/reproducibility.
