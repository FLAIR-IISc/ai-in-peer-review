This paper presents a method for generating sentences from image streams, introducing a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) comprised of convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. 

Overall, this is a solid contribution, though several areas could benefit from further development. Specifically, the related work section would be strengthened by including recent publications in video-to-sentence generation, such as work on jointly modeling deep video and compositional text. While the quantitative results appear promising, the user study (Table 2) indicates performance comparable to the RCN baseline. A statistical significance test is necessary to confirm the reliability of any observed improvements. In summary, the proposed algorithm demonstrates potential for sentence generation from image streams, but the user study provides limited evidence of a substantial advantage over existing approaches.
