- Task & contribution: Generates coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams by retrieving K=5 candidate sentences per image and selecting the most relevant, coherent sequence.  
- Model summary: Candidate sentences are embedded, passed through a bi-LSTM, then ReLU + fully connected layer to score image compatibility, with an added local coherence component to enforce adjacency consistency.  
- Strength: Proposes a novel, well-motivated architecture for retrieval-based coherent multi-sentence generation.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation (quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, human study) with an ablation showing the coherence model’s benefit.  
- Strength: Authors plan to release code and dataset, aiding reproducibility and community use.  
- Weakness: Related-work claim (that [5] lacks any coherence mechanism) is overstated—[5] may implicitly model coherence via intermediate semantic representations.  
- Weakness: Model description ambiguity—Figure 2b vs. Section 3.3/Equation (2) conflict about whether the FC layer connects across all sentences or is applied per time step; needs clarification.  
- Weakness: Evaluation uses BLEU only; METEOR (better for sentence-level/single reference) should be reported for robustness.  
- Weakness: Two consecutive linear transforms (Wf1, Wf2) are effectively linear; the rationale or ablation for this design choice is missing.  
- Weakness: Unclear why the same FC parameters are shared for bi-RNN outputs and local coherence inputs—separate transformations might be preferable.  
- Weakness: Implementation detail missing—whether paragraph vector sentence embeddings are fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance after rebuttal; camera-ready must clarify model architecture (point 2) and paragraph vector training status (point 6), and preferably address the other concerns.
