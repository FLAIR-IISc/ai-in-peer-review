This paper investigates generating natural-language sentences for an image stream. It introduces a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) that combines convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence component.

Overall, this is a solid contribution, but it could be improved in the following respects:
- Some relevant video-to-sentence literature is missing, e.g., “Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework.”
- Although the quantitative metrics for the proposed method look strong, the user study in Table 2 shows performance similar to the RCN baseline. Statistical significance testing is needed to assess whether the observed improvements are reliable.

In sum, this is a well-conceived algorithm for generating sentence sequences from an image stream. The quantitative results are encouraging, but the user study indicates only a modest advantage over baselines.
