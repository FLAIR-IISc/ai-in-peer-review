This paper studies sentence generation from an image stream and proposes a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN) that combines convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model.

Strengths:
- The problem is well motivated, and the CRCN architecture is coherent and clearly presented.
- Quantitative results are strong, indicating that the proposed method performs well on standard metrics.

Areas for improvement:
- The related work on video-to-sentence generation is incomplete. In particular, the paper “Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework” should be cited and discussed, along with other relevant prior work.
- While the quantitative results are promising, the user study in Table 2 shows performance similar to the RCN baseline, suggesting only a weak advantage over baselines. A statistical significance test is needed to verify whether the observed improvements are reliable.

Overall assessment:
A nice algorithm for sentence generation from an image stream with good quantitative performance. To strengthen the contribution, the authors should broaden the related work coverage and provide statistical significance analysis for the user study results, especially given the close performance to the RCN baseline.
