- Summary: Retrieval-based method that selects K=5 similar images per query, embeds candidate sentences, models sequence dependencies with a bi-LSTM and local coherence term to produce multi-sentence image descriptions.

- Strength: Proposes a novel retrieval architecture that explicitly models inter-sentence coherence for multi-sentence descriptions.

- Strength: Thorough evaluation including quantitative metrics, qualitative analysis, human studies, and an ablation for the coherence model.

- Strength: Authors plan to release code and dataset, improving reproducibility and community utility.

- Weakness 1: Clarify claim about [5]—it does model topic-level consistency; distinguish the paper’s coherence modeling from [5]’s topic-level approach.

- Weakness 2: Resolve Figure 2b vs. Eq.(2) inconsistency—state whether fully connected parameters are shared across time and whether cross-sentence connections exist.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation metric concerns—report METEOR and, if possible, CIDEr or ROUGE-L (and diversity/recall metrics) in addition to BLEU.

- Weakness 4: Two successive linear layers in Eq.(2) are redundant without a nonlinearity—clarify intent or provide ablation showing benefit.

- Weakness 5: Justify or ablate sharing fully connected parameters between bi-LSTM outputs and the local coherence model, since compatibility and coherence may require distinct signals.

- Weakness 6: Clarify paragraph vector [16] treatment—whether it is fine-tuned or fixed, training protocol details, and performance impact of fine-tuning.

- Additional: Provide details on the retrieval stage (features, distance metric, tuning or sensitivity analysis for K).

- Additional: Specify exact form of image–sentence compatibility score, including normalization, margin/loss, and negative sampling strategy.

- Additional: Include brief error analysis showing typical coherence failures (entity drift, temporal ordering) and how the local coherence term mitigates them.

- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance after authors addressed formulation, metrics, related work, and implementation clarifications.

- Final-version requirements: fix Figure 2b/Eq.(2) inconsistency and parameter-sharing statement; report METEOR (and other metrics if feasible); precisely discuss [5]’s topic-level handling vs. this work; clarify paragraph vector fine-tuning and its impact; include code and dataset links.

- Overall Assessment: Well-motivated, carefully evaluated approach with novel coherence modeling and convincing empirical support—recommend accept.
