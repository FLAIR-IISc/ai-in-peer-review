- Addresses generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams (beyond single-image captioning and video captioning).  
- Proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN): CNN image encoding + bidirectional RNN sentence modeling + entity-based local coherence.  
- Leverages blog posts (image-text sequences) as training data to learn natural inter-sentence transitions.  
- Identifies a clear gap in literature: sharper visual transitions in image streams make coherence modeling crucial.  
- Novel integration of established components into a coherence-focused architecture.  
- Demonstrates approach on two large datasets (NYC and Disneyland) totaling ~20K posts and ~140K images.  
- Evaluation is comprehensive: BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR metrics plus Amazon Mechanical Turk user studies.  
- Strong empirical evidence and ablation studies show importance of bidirectional RNN and coherence model.  
- Writing, figures, and implementation details are clear and largely reproducible.  
- Originality is high with proper differentiation from video and single-image captioning work.  
- Broad significance for social media summarization, photo albums, and HCI; method is unsupervised and domain-agnostic.  
- Weakness: sentence-sequence generation via K-nearest sentence retrieval is simplistic and needs stronger justification.  
- Weakness: claim about vanishing gradients preventing finer-grained methods is not well-supported or discussed.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to alternative sequence generation approaches (e.g., seq2seq with attention).  
- Weakness: dataset collection/crawling methodology for NYC data is under-specified and dataset not released, hurting reproducibility.  
- Minor presentation issue: Equation (2) formatting seems incorrect and may imply a non-fully connected architecture.  
- Suggest expanding discussion of applications beyond tourism and clarifying dataset and sentence-generation details.  
- Recommendation: suitable for NIPS poster pending minor revisions addressing dataset clarity and sentence-generation justification.
