Summary
The paper addresses the problem of retrieving natural sentences to describe an input sequence of images and composing them into a coherent multi-sentence narrative. This is an important and timely task at the intersection of computer vision and natural language processing. The proposed CRCN model learns relationships between sequences of images and sequences of sentences, and incorporates entity-based coherence modeling to capture discourse-level relations. The paper is clearly written, and the overall approach is technically sound.

Strengths
- Clear problem statement and motivation; the task is relevant to both image captioning and video description.
- The CRCN architecture is well motivated for aligning image sequences with sentence sequences and for modeling discourse relations.
- The integration of entity-based coherence models is a principled way to encode discourse structure beyond single-sentence descriptions.
- Presentation is generally easy to follow.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Relation to prior work: The task is closely related to generating multi-sentence descriptions of videos (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014). A video can be decomposed into shots, each represented by a key frame, making the proposed setting highly comparable to multi-sentence video description. This line of work is not cited or discussed. The paper should (i) cite the relevant literature and (ii) clearly articulate what is novel relative to multi-sentence video captioning and why operating on image sequences differs methodologically or empirically from operating on videos.
- Retrieval/generation strategy: The method for producing the final sentence sequence is rather primitive. Each image is matched to similar training images to retrieve a sentence, and the resulting sentences are concatenated in image order. This per-image retrieval with simple concatenation underutilizes the proposed discourse model and risks redundancy, incoherence, and error propagation. A more principled sequence-level inference (e.g., global optimization, coherence-aware reranking, or joint decoding over the entire image sequence) seems warranted.
- Equation (2): As written, it appears to define s_t solely as a function of o_t. This suggests the state at time t does not depend on previous states or inputs, which would undermine the sequential modeling claims. If s_t is intended to be a recurrent/latent state, it should depend on s_{t-1} and the current inputs. Please clarify or correct the notation and ensure consistency with the architecture description.
- Data and anonymity: Section 4.1 states that the Disneyland blog data from [11] are reused and that NYC data are newly crawled using the same method as [11]. However, the dataset is not disclosed and [11] reportedly does not detail the crawling procedure. This both hinders reproducibility and risks compromising anonymity by implying overlapping authorship with [11]. Under the double-blind policy, please (i) avoid self-identifying statements, (ii) provide sufficient methodological detail for crawling without revealing identity, and (iii) state a clear plan for data release upon acceptance.
- Reproducibility: Beyond anonymity, the lack of dataset release and unspecified crawling details limit reproducibility. Even if the data cannot be released during review, the paper should provide enough information (sources, query terms, filtering rules, preprocessing steps, statistics) for others to reconstruct or approximate the dataset.

Requests for clarification
- How exactly is the entity-based coherence model integrated at training and inference time? Is it used to learn representations within CRCN, to score candidate sentence sequences, or only for post-hoc analysis?
- Do you perform any global coherence-aware selection or reranking across the full image sequence, or is the final output strictly a concatenation of independently retrieved sentences?
- Please clarify the training objective and loss functions, including how cross-modal alignment and coherence signals are balanced. If Equation (2) is only schematic, provide the full recurrent formulation.

Suggestions for improvement
- Strengthen the related work section with multi-sentence video description and discourse-aware captioning, and delineate the differences and advantages of your setting.
- Replace per-image independent retrieval with a sequence-level decoding or reranking procedure that explicitly optimizes a global objective combining visual relevance, fluency, and coherence (entity transitions, discourse relations).
- Provide an ablation study to quantify the contribution of the entity-based coherence component versus image-sentence alignment alone.
- Improve reproducibility by detailing the crawling pipeline and dataset statistics, and by committing to a public release (or a well-specified replication protocol) upon acceptance.
- Correct or clarify Equation (2) and ensure the architecture description unambiguously reflects a properly connected sequence model.

Overall assessment
This paper tackles an important problem and presents a technically sound model that thoughtfully incorporates discourse coherence. The writing is clear and the core ideas are solid. However, the current sentence sequence generation strategy is ad hoc, the connection to closely related video-description work is underdeveloped, the notation around Equation (2) is potentially incorrect or misleading, and the dataset/anonymity issues are significant. With stronger sequence-level inference, a clearer technical presentation, and a more thorough positioning relative to prior work, this would be a compelling contribution.

Recommendation: Accept as a poster, conditional on addressing the technical clarification of Equation (2), improving the discussion of related work, and mitigating the anonymity/reproducibility concerns.
