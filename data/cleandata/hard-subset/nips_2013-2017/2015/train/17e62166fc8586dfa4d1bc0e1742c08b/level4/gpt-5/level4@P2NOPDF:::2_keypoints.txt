- Task & approach: Retrieves K=5 nearest training images per query and selects/sequences their sentences using sentence embeddings + a BiLSTM to produce coherent multi-sentence image-stream descriptions.
- Core model: BiLSTM outputs passed through ReLU and a fully connected layer, scored with an image–sentence compatibility function and augmented by a local coherence model.
- Strength: Proposes a novel, effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences across image sequences.
- Strength: Provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations and an ablation showing the coherence component’s value.
- Strength: Commits to releasing source code and the dataset.
- Weakness/clarify: Claim that prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is overstated—[5] models topics across sentences and induces some coherence.
- Weakness/clarify: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Eq. (2); clarify whether the FC layer is shared/applied per sentence or connects across sentences.
- Weakness/clarify: With single-reference evaluation, METEOR may be more appropriate than BLEU—please report METEOR.
- Weakness/clarify: Eq. (2) uses two linear layers in sequence without nonlinearity (redundant); justify the design or include an ablation.
- Weakness/clarify: Explain why the same FC parameters are shared for BiRNN outputs o_t and the local coherence model q, or discuss alternatives.
- Weakness/clarify: State whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.
- Outcome: Post-rebuttal, the reviewer recommends acceptance provided the authors implement revisions and explicitly clarify whether the paragraph vector is fine-tuned.
