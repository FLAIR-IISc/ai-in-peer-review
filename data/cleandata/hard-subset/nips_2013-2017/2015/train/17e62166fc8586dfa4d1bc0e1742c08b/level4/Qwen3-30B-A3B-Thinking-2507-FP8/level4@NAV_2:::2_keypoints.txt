- Task: generate coherent natural-language descriptions for image sequences by retrieving top K=5 similar training images/sentences and selecting the most relevant sentence sequence.
- Method: multimodal model embedding sentences, modeled with a BiLSTM, passed through ReLU and a fully connected layer, then scored against the query image sequence.
- Key innovation: incorporation of a local coherence model to ensure smooth transitions between sentences.
- Strength: proposes a novel and effective architecture for producing coherent sentence sequences from image streams.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation (quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, AMT human studies) showing clear superiority over baselines; ablation confirms coherence model’s contribution.
- Strength: authors commit to releasing source code and the dataset.
- Weakness: incorrect claim that prior work [5] lacks coherence mechanism—[5] models topic coherence via intermediate semantic video representations.
- Weakness: inconsistency between Figure 2b and Section 3.3/Equation (2) regarding whether fully connected layers connect to all sentences or parameters are shared per sentence.
- Weakness: BLEU is a suboptimal automatic metric when only a single reference is available; METEOR would be more appropriate.
- Weakness: unclear rationale for using two sequential linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) in Eq (2), since two linear transforms are mathematically equivalent to one—requires ablation justification.
- Weakness: need clarification on why BRNN output (ot) and coherence model (q) use identical parameters in Eq (2).
- Weakness: unclear whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.
- Post-rebuttal: authors adequately addressed formulation, evaluation, and related-work concerns; reviewer recommends acceptance.
