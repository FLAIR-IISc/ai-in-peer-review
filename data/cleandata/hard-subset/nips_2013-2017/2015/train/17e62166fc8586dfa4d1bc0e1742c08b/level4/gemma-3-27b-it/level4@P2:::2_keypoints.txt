- Problem: Proposes generating coherent sentence sequences for image streams (e.g., trip photos) using blog posts as a text-image parallel corpus.  
- Method: Introduces CRCN combining CNN image features, BRNN language modeling, and a local coherence model; retrieves candidate sentences per image then selects the most coherent sequence.  
- Positioning: Extends single-image captioning and relates to video-sentence work, highlighting the challenge of abrupt visual changes in photo streams.  
- Evaluation: Shows improved BLEU and top-K recall and positive AMT user study results, with qualitative examples.  
- Strength — Novelty: Tackles a relatively unexplored and logical next-step problem in image description.  
- Strength — Evaluation: Comprehensive quantitative, qualitative, and user-study evaluation.  
- Strength — Ablation: Includes ablation study removing the coherence model to isolate contributions.  
- Strength — Resources: Authors plan to release code and data.  
- Strength — Baselines: Strong comparisons against established image captioning methods.  
- Weakness — Related work: Claim that [5] lacks sentence coherence is inaccurate; [5] models topic-level coherence.  
- Weakness — Figure vs. text: Figure 2b conflicts with Section 3.3 and Eq.(2); needs clarification.  
- Weakness — Metrics: BLEU may be insufficient; METEOR could provide better human correlation.  
- Weakness — Eq.(2) design: Two sequential linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) may be redundant; requires ablation/justification.  
- Weakness — Parameter sharing: Rationale for using same FC parameters for BRNN output and coherence vector q is unclear.  
- Weakness — Paragraph vectors: Unclear whether paragraph vectors were fine-tuned during training.  
- For acceptance: Addresses impactful problem, CRCN is well-motivated, strong results, and authors responsive to feedback.  
- Against acceptance: Clarity/consistency issues in model description and potential design redundancies weaken the presentation.  
- Overall: Valuable contribution with minor clarifications needed; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Post-rebuttal: Authors addressed main concerns except clarification on paragraph vector fine-tuning remains.
