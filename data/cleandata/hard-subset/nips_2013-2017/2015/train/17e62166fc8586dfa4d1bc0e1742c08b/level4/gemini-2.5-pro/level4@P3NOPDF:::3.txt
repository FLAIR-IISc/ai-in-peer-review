This paper addresses the task of generating descriptive sentences from a stream of images. The authors propose a Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for visual feature extraction, bidirectional recurrent neural networks (RNNs) for temporal modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model to ensure narrative consistency.

Overall, this is a solid contribution with a well-designed model. The approach of explicitly modeling local coherence through an entity-based mechanism is novel and addresses an important challenge in generating paragraph-level descriptions for visual data. However, the paper could be strengthened in a few key areas.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Novel Architecture:** The proposed CRCN architecture is well-motivated. The combination of CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and a dedicated coherence model provides a comprehensive framework for tackling the image stream-to-text generation problem. The entity-based coherence model, in particular, is an interesting and promising idea for improving the narrative flow of the generated text.
2.  **Strong Quantitative Results:** The method demonstrates strong performance on automatic evaluation metrics, outperforming several baselines. This suggests that the proposed architecture is effective at capturing the relevant semantics and structure for this task.

**Areas for Improvement:**

1.  **Missing Related Work:** The literature review appears to be incomplete. Several relevant works on video-to-sentence generation are not discussed or compared against. For example, the authors should consider citing and differentiating their work from key papers such as "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework" (Venugopalan et al.). A more comprehensive discussion of related work would better situate the paper's contributions within the existing landscape.
2.  **Statistical Significance of Human Evaluation:** While the quantitative results are promising, the user study presented in Table 2 is less conclusive. The proposed CRCN model performs very similarly to the RCN baseline in the human evaluation, showing only a marginal advantage. To validate the effectiveness of the proposed coherence model from a human perspective, it is essential to conduct a statistical significance test on these results. This would help clarify whether the observed improvement is statistically reliable or falls within the margin of error. Without this analysis, the claims regarding improved coherence are not fully supported by the human evaluation.

**Conclusion:**

In summary, this paper presents a novel and interesting algorithm for generating sentences from an image stream. The quantitative performance is strong, but the claims of improved coherence are only weakly supported by the human evaluation. The paper would be significantly strengthened by including a more thorough comparison with related work and providing a rigorous statistical analysis of the user study results.
