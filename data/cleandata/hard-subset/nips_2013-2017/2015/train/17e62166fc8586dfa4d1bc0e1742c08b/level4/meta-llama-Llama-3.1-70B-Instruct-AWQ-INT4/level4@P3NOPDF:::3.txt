This paper explores the task of generating sentences from an image stream, proposing a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) that integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model.

Overall, the work is well-motivated and contributes to the growing field of vision-to-language research. However, there are several areas that can be improved upon:

* The authors could strengthen their literature review by incorporating more related works on video-to-sentence generation, such as "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework." Including these references would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the current state of the field and help situate the proposed method within it.

* Although the quantitative results of the proposed method appear promising, the user study presented in Table 2 reveals that the CRCN model performs similarly to one of the baseline models, RCN. To verify the reliability of the improvement, it would be beneficial to conduct a significance test. This would provide a more robust assessment of the proposed method's performance and help to establish its advantages over existing approaches.

* The algorithm for sentence generation from an image stream is a notable contribution of this work. While the quantitative results are encouraging, the user study only demonstrates a weak advantage over the baseline models. To further demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, the authors may consider conducting additional evaluations or providing more in-depth analyses of the results.

By addressing these areas, the authors can strengthen their manuscript and provide a more compelling case for the proposed CRCN model.
