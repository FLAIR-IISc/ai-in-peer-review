- Task: generate coherent sentence sequences describing image streams from blog posts.  
- Method: for each query image retrieve top K=5 similar training images/sentences, embed sentences, model sentence sequence with bidirectional LSTM, apply ReLU+FC and compute image–sentence compatibility.  
- Novelty: primary contribution is a method to select the most relevant sentences for an image sequence to produce coherent descriptions, with an added local coherence model.  
- Strength: proposes a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences across an image sequence.  
- Strength: provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations showing superiority over baselines, plus an ablation on the coherence model.  
- Strength: authors commit to releasing code and dataset.  
- Weakness: claim that prior work [5] lacks coherence mechanisms is disputed because [5] models topics for multi-sentence coherence.  
- Weakness: Figure 2b and the description of Section 3.3/Equation (2) are unclear—figure implies FC layers connect to all sentences, equation implies shared parameters applied per sentence.  
- Weakness: BLEU may be inappropriate for single-reference evaluation; METEOR is suggested as a more suitable automatic metric.  
- Weakness: Equation (2) uses two linear functions (W_f2, W_f1) in succession, which is theoretically redundant; an ablation is needed to justify them.  
- Weakness: need clarification on why the same parameters are used for the FC layers on BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence model q in Equation (2).  
- Weakness: unclear whether the paragraph vector [16] embedding is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.  
- Outcome: after rebuttal the reviewer recommends acceptance, noting the authors addressed formulation, evaluation, and related-work concerns but should incorporate promised changes and clarifications.
