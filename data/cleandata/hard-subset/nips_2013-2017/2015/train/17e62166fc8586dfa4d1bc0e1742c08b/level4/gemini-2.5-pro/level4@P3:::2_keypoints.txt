- Tackles a novel and significant extension of image captioning: generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams.
- Proposes CRCN: a multimodal architecture combining CNN image features, a BRNN for content flow, and an entity-based local coherence model.
- Uses a retrieval-based pipeline: candidate sentence retrieval per image followed by CRCN ranking to select a visually relevant, coherent sequence.
- Evaluated on a large-scale blog-derived dataset, showing applicability to real-world user-generated content.
- Thorough experimental evaluation with multiple quantitative metrics (BLEU, CIDEr, METEOR, retrieval), qualitative examples, and a Mechanical Turk user study.
- Ablation study comparing CRCN to RCN demonstrates the contribution of the entity-based coherence component.
- Commits to releasing code and dataset, supporting reproducibility and future work.
