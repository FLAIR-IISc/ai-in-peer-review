- Addresses retrieval-based multi-sentence description of image sequences; important task but fails to cite/position itself relative to video-to-text literature (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014).
- Paper is clearly written and problem formulation is sound.
- CRCN model learns image-sequence-to-sentence-sequence relationships; architecture is technically sound and entity-based coherence modeling is appropriate.
- Sentence generation is rudimentary (retrieve-and-concatenate); does not explicitly optimize global coherence or avoid redundancy—joint generation or re-ranking recommended.
- Section 4.1 raises reproducibility and anonymity concerns: dataset reuse/new collection not released, [11] lacks crawling details, and wording suggests author overlap that may violate double-blind review.
- Equation (2) notation appears incorrect—implies s_t depends only on o_t; clarify intended dependencies and correct the equation.
- Overall verdict: solid model and presentation but weaknesses in related work engagement, generation strategy, and dataset disclosure; suitable for acceptance as a poster.
