- Task: Generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences (e.g., blog posts) by selecting sentences retrieved from similar training images.  
- Core method: Retrieve K=5 nearest images per input, embed candidate sentences, model the sentence sequence with a bidirectional LSTM, then apply ReLU, fully connected layers, and a compatibility score to select sentences.  
- Coherence: Incorporates a local coherence model building on prior work [1] to enforce sentence-to-sentence cohesion.  
- Main contribution: Novel sentence-selection architecture using sentence embeddings + BiLSTM-based sequence modeling for coherent retrieval-based description.  
- Evaluation: Comprehensive—quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, human evaluation, and an ablation study showing the local coherence model’s contribution.  
- Reproducibility: Authors commit to releasing code and dataset.  
- Strength: Demonstrates superiority over baselines that don’t model coherence explicitly.  
- Weakness: Claim that [5] lacks coherence mechanism may be inaccurate since [5] predicts intermediate semantic representations that capture topic coherence.  
- Weakness: Figure 2b vs. Equation (2) mismatch—figure suggests FC layer connected to all sentences, equation implies shared parameters per sentence; needs clarification.  
- Weakness: BLEU used for automatic evaluation, but METEOR may be more appropriate for single-reference settings.  
- Weakness: Equation (2) uses two consecutive linear layers (W_f2 and W_f1), which are algebraically redundant; an ablation is needed to justify this design.  
- Weakness: Unclear rationale for sharing the same FC parameters when processing BiRNN outputs (o_t) and computing local coherence score q—explain parameter sharing.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed—clarify this.  
- Post-rebuttal: Authors addressed earlier concerns; reviewer recommends acceptance but asks final paper to clarify paragraph vector training and address raised points for polish.
