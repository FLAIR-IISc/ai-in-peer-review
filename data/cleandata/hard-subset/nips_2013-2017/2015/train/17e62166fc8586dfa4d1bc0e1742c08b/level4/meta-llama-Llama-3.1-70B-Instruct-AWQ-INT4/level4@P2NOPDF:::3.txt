This work explores the task of generating sentences from an image stream, proposing a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) that integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model.

Overall, the work is well-motivated, although there are several areas that can be improved upon. Firstly, the authors could strengthen their literature review by incorporating additional related works on video-to-sentence generation, such as "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework." Including these references would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the current state of research in this field.

While the quantitative results of the proposed method appear promising, the user study presented in Table 2 reveals that the CRCN performs similarly to the RCN baseline. To verify the reliability of this improvement, it is essential to conduct significance tests. Furthermore, the user study only demonstrates a weak advantage of the proposed method over the baselines, which somewhat tempers the impact of the quantitative results.
