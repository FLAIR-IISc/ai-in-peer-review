- Paper: proposes retrieving K=5 nearest-neighbor images/sentences per image and selecting sentences to form a coherent narrative using sentence embeddings, a Bi-LSTM, ReLU+FC layers for compatibility scoring, and a local coherence model.

- Strength: introduces an innovative, effective architecture for generating coherent multi-sentence descriptions.

- Strength: evaluation is thorough (quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, human judgments) and includes an ablation study showing the coherence model’s contribution.

- Strength: authors commit to releasing source code and dataset.

- Weakness/Question 1: Claim that reference [5] lacks a coherence mechanism is likely overstated since [5] models topic via an intermediate semantic representation that promotes coherence.

- Weakness/Question 2: Apparent discrepancy between Figure 2b and Section 3.3/Equation (2) — figure suggests joint FC input from all sentence representations while equation implies per-sentence parameter sharing.

- Weakness/Question 3: METEOR would be a more appropriate automatic evaluation metric than BLEU for single-reference scenarios.

- Weakness/Question 4: Rationale for stacking two linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) is unclear because their composition is a single linear transform; an ablation study is needed.

- Weakness/Question 5: Need clarification why the same fully connected layer parameters are used for both the Bi-RNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model input (q).

- Weakness/Question 6: Unclear whether paragraph vectors from [16] are used as fixed features or fine-tuned during training.

- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer now recommends acceptance, authors addressed major concerns, and reviewer asks authors to incorporate revisions and clarify whether paragraph vectors are fixed or fine-tuned in the camera-ready.
