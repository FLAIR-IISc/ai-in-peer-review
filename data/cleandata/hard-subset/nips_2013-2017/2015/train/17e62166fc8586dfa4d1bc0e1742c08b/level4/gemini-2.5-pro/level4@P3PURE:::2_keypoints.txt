- Paper proposes retrieving K=5 nearest training images/sentences per query and selecting/re-ranking those sentences to produce a coherent description for an image sequence.  
- Core model: sentence embeddings fed to a bidirectional LSTM, outputs passed through ReLU and a fully connected layer, with final selection using an image–sentence compatibility score plus a local coherence model.  
- Strength: novel, effective architecture for retrieving coherent multi-sentence descriptions.  
- Strength: extensive evaluation (quantitative, qualitative, human studies) showing superiority over baselines and an ablation removing the local coherence model.  
- Strength: authors plan to release source code and dataset.  
- Weakness/Question 1: Claim that prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is inaccurate since [5] models topic across sentences, which enforces some coherence.  
- Weakness/Question 2: Apparent inconsistency between Figure 2b and Equation (2): does the fully connected layer take all sentences jointly or apply shared parameters per time step?  
- Weakness/Question 3: BLEU was used for automatic evaluation; reviewer asks whether METEOR was considered, as it may be more suitable for sentence-level/single-reference settings.  
- Weakness/Question 4: Equation (2) uses two consecutive linear transforms (W_f1, W_f2); since their composition is linear, motivation is unclear and an ablation versus a single linear layer is requested.  
- Weakness/Question 5: Equation (2) implies the same FC parameters are applied to both Bi-RNN outputs (o_t) and local coherence representations (q_t); reviewer asks for justification of this parameter sharing.  
- Weakness/Question 6 / Clarification: Is the paragraph vector model [16] fixed or fine-tuned on the target dataset during training?  
- Post-rebuttal recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance after the authors’ rebuttal addressed concerns, asks authors to incorporate promised changes, and requests final clarification on whether paragraph vectors are fine-tuned.
