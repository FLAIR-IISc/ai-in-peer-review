The paper tackles the task of generating a coherent sequence of sentences to describe a sequence of images from blog posts. The approach first retrieves, for each query image, the K=5 most similar images and their associated sentences from the training set. The main contribution is a method for selecting the most relevant sentences across the entire image sequence to produce a coherent description. Sentences are embedded into vectors, and their sequence is modeled with a bidirectional LSTM. The BiLSTM output is passed through a ReLU and a fully connected layer, then scored via an image–sentence compatibility measure. In addition, a local coherence model [1] is incorporated to enforce compatibility between adjacent sentences.

Strengths / positive aspects:
- The paper proposes a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for an image sequence.
- It provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations demonstrating superiority over baselines that do not model coherence, along with an ablation study that removes the coherence model [1].
- The authors commit to releasing the source code and dataset.

Weaknesses / questions / unclear points:
1. Line 94: The paper states that [5] has “no mechanism for coherence between sentences.” While not the primary focus of [5], that work predicts an intermediate semantic representation for videos, which yields cross-sentence coherence by modeling the topic of the multi-sentence description.
2. Correctness/clarity: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2). Figure 2b suggests the fully connected layer connects to all sentences, whereas Equation (2) implies shared parameters across sentences that are applied only to each sentence’s vector individually.
3. For automatic evaluation, METEOR (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) is more appropriate than BLEU, especially when only a single reference sentence is available.
4. Why are two linear mappings in Equation (2) (W{f2}, W{f1}) applied sequentially? Since a composition of two linear functions is still linear, the benefit is unclear. An ablation showing the contribution of these layers would be informative.
5. Why are the same fully connected parameters used for both the BRNN output o_t and the local coherence model q (Equation (2))?
6. Is the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned or kept fixed?

After reading the rebuttal, I recommend acceptance. The authors have satisfactorily addressed issues related to the formulation, evaluation, and related work. Please make the promised revisions in the final version and also clarify the remaining question: Is the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned or kept fixed? The paper introduces an interesting new model for retrieving coherent sentences for an image stream and provides convincing evaluations, but several clarifications are still needed in the final version.
