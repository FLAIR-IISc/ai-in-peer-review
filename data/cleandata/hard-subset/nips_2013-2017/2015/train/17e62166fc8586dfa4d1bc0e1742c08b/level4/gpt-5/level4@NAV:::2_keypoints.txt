- Task and approach: retrieve K=5 similar training images per query, embed candidate sentences, model sentence sequence with a bi-LSTM, pass outputs through ReLU and a fully connected layer, score via image–sentence compatibility, and add a local coherence model to enforce inter-sentence consistency.

- Core contribution: mechanism for selecting the most relevant sentences across an image sequence to produce a coherent narrative.

- Strength: novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent multi-sentence descriptions.

- Strength: extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations showing superiority over baselines, plus an ablation removing the coherence model.

- Strength: authors commit to releasing source code and dataset.

- Weakness/question 1: Claim that prior work [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is disputable because [5] models intermediate semantic representations that maintain multi-sentence topic coherence.

- Weakness/question 2: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2); figure implies FC connects to all sentences whereas Eq. (2) implies parameter sharing and per-sentence connections.

- Weakness/question 3: Automatic evaluation should prefer METEOR over BLEU when only a single reference sentence is available.

- Weakness/question 4: Sequential application of two linear functions (Wf2, Wf1) in Eq. (2) is unclear since their composition is linear; an ablation is needed to justify them.

- Weakness/question 5: Unclear why the same fully connected parameters are used for both the BRNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model q.

- Weakness/question 6 / clarification requested: Is the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned or kept fixed?

- Post-rebuttal: reviewer recommends acceptance, states authors satisfactorily addressed formulation/evaluation/related-work issues, and asks authors to implement promised changes and clarify point 6 in the final version.
