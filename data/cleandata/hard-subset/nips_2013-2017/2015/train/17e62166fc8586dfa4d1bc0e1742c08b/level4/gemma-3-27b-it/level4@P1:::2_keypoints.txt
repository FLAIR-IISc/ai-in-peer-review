- Task: Generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image sequences from blog posts.  
- Method: Retrieve top K=5 nearest training images/sentences per query image, embed sentences, model sentence sequence with a bidirectional LSTM, pass through ReLU and FC layers, score image–sentence compatibility.  
- Addition: Integrate a local coherence model to enforce compatibility between adjacent sentences.  
- Main contribution: A method to select the most relevant sentences for an image sequence to produce coherent descriptions.  
- Evaluation: Extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations, plus an ablation study removing the coherence model; authors promise to release code and dataset.  
- Strength: Novel and effective architecture combining CNNs, RNNs, and coherence modeling for an underexplored problem.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation showing superiority over baselines lacking coherence modeling.  
- Weakness: Claim that prior work [5] lacks coherence modeling is inaccurate, since [5] models intermediate semantics that provide topic-level coherence.  
- Weakness: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2) — unclear whether FC layers connect to all sentences or share parameters per sentence.  
- Weakness: Automatic evaluation should use METEOR (especially with single reference) rather than BLEU.  
- Weakness: Equation (2) uses two linear functions (Wf1, Wf2) in sequence with no explained benefit; an ablation is needed.  
- Weakness: Unclear why identical FC parameters are used for BRNN output (ot) and local coherence output (q) in Equation (2).  
- Weakness: Unclear whether paragraph vectors [17] are fine-tuned or kept fixed during training.  
- Overall assessment: Paper is compelling, technically sound, empirically strong, and warrants acceptance pending minor clarifications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel problem focus, well-motivated architecture, strong empirical results, and practical significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Requires minor technical clarifications and correction of the inaccurate claim about [5].
