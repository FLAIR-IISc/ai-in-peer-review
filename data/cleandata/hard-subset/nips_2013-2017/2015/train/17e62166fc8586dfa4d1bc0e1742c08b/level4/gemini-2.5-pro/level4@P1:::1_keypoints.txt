- Tackles an important, novel problem: generating paragraph-like sentence sequences for image streams.  
- Proposes a technically sound CRCN architecture combining CNN visual features, a BRNN for text flow, and an entity-based coherence model.  
- Inclusion of an explicit coherence model to handle discourse is a major strength.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.  
- Omits discussion of relevant video-description literature (e.g., Rohrbach ICCV13, Venugopalan CVPR15), which is highly related.  
- Final sentence generation is simplistic—relies on retrieval of nearest-neighbor sentences and re-ranking rather than true generative modeling.  
- Reproducibility and anonymity concerns: relies on a non-public Disneyland blog dataset and provides insufficient data-collection details.  
- Possible error/confusion in Equation (2): formulation suggests no temporal mixing in final layers and needs clarification.  
- Overall: valuable and novel contribution with sound core model, but weakened by related-work omission, ad-hoc generation, and dataset concerns; recommend acceptance as a poster.
