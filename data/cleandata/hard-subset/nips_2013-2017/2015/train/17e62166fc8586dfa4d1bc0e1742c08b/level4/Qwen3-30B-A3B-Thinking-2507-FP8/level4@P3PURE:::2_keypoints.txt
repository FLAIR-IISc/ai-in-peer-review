- Task: generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for sequences of blog post images.  
- Retrieval: for each query image retrieve K=5 similar training images and their sentences.  
- Main contribution: method for selecting the most relevant sentences for an image sequence while ensuring coherence.  
- Model: sentences embedded as vectors, sequence modeled with a bidirectional LSTM.  
- Scoring: BRNN output passed through ReLU-activated fully connected layer and a compatibility score between image and sentence representations.  
- Coherence: incorporates an existing local coherence model to enforce compatibility between consecutive sentences.  
- Strength: novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences across image sequences.  
- Strength: extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations, plus an ablation removing the coherence model.  
- Strength: authors plan to release source code and dataset.  
- Weakness: related work claim — paper incorrectly characterizes [5] as lacking coherence mechanisms; [5] models intermediate semantics that imply coherence.  
- Weakness: discrepancy between Figure 2b and Equation (2) about how the fully connected layer connects to sentences/parameters.  
- Weakness: BLEU is suboptimal with single references; METEOR would be a more appropriate automatic metric.  
- Weakness: sequential linear layers (W_{f2}, W_{f1}) appear redundant; need ablation to justify both.  
- Weakness: unclear rationale for sharing the same FC layer parameters between BRNN output and the local coherence model.  
- Weakness: unclear whether paragraph vector embeddings are fine-tuned or kept fixed during training.  
- Outcome: after rebuttal authors addressed concerns (including paragraph vector issue); reviewer recommends acceptance.
