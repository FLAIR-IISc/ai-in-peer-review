

This work investigates sentence generation from image streams by introducing a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN), which integrates convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. Overall, the approach is well-motivated, though it could be strengthened in the following aspects: * The related work section omits key prior research on video-to-sentence generation, such as "Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework." * While the quantitative results appear promising, the user study in Table 2 indicates the proposed method achieves performance comparable to one baseline (RCN), raising questions about the practical significance of the gains. Statistical significance testing is required to confirm whether the observed improvements are reliable. The algorithm effectively generates sentences from image streams, with strong quantitative results, but the user study demonstrates only a marginal advantage over baselines.
