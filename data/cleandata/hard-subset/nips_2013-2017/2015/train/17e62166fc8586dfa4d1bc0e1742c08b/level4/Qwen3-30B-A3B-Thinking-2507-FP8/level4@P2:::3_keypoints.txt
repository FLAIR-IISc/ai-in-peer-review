- Problem: Addresses generating coherent sentence sequences from image streams rather than single-image captions.  
- Method: Proposes Coherent Recurrent Convolutional Networks (CRCN) combining CNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and an entity-based local coherence model.  
- Data: Trains on large-scale user-generated blog data (20K posts, 140K images) to avoid manual annotation.  
- Results: CRCN outperforms strong baselines on BLEU, CIDEr, top-K recall and in AMT user studies.  
- Originality: First explicit treatment of image-stream→sentence-sequence generation, distinguishing from video-captioning due to abrupt visual changes.  
- Novel dataset approach: Blog-based collection is a scalable, novel solution to data scarcity.  
- Significance: Demonstrates practical tourism-domain use (NYC/Disneyland) and suggests unsupervised cross-domain transferability.  
- Quality: Architecture is well-motivated and technically sound; coherence model validated by ablations and yields significant retrieval gains.  
- Clarity/Reproducibility: Paper is well-organized with clear formulation, detailed methodology, and a describable preprocessing pipeline.  
- Weakness — Related work: Insufficient comparison to prior video-to-sentence work (notably Xu et al., AAAI 2015).  
- Weakness — Statistical rigor: User study lacks significance testing (p-values/confidence intervals) to validate preference differences.  
- Weakness — Scope: Needs discussion on generalization beyond tourism (e.g., personal diaries, events).  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with revisions to (1) add Xu et al. (AAAI 2015) to related work, (2) include significance tests for user studies, and (3) elaborate on domain generalization.
