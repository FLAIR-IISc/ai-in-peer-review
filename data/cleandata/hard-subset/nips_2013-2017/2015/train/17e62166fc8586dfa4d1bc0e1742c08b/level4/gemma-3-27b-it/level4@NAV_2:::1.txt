This paper addresses the problem of retrieving natural language sentences to describe a sequence of images, formulating it as extending image captioning to the sequential domain. This is a significant problem with clear relevance to computer vision, natural language processing, and understanding how people document experiences with image streams. The work builds upon the growing body of research in image captioning but distinguishes itself by tackling the sequential aspect, a logical next step. However, the paper overlooks connections to related work in video captioning, where the input is inherently a sequence of visual frames (e.g., [Rohrbach+ 2014]), and fails to cite this relevant literature.

The paper is well-written and clearly presents the problem and proposed solution. The authors articulate their motivation well and the technical approach appears sound. 

The proposed coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN) is a technically well-designed architecture that effectively integrates convolutional neural networks for image representation, recurrent neural networks for language modeling, and an entity-based local coherence model to improve fluency. The inclusion of a coherence model is a key contribution, addressing the challenge of maintaining consistency between sentences in a sequence—a point well-motivated given the potential for abrupt changes in visual content between consecutive images.

The sentence retrieval and sequence construction method, while reasonable, feels somewhat simplistic. Directly associating sentences with similar training images and concatenating them sequentially may limit the potential for more creative or nuanced descriptions.

A concern regarding reproducibility arises in Section 4.1, where the authors state they reused a dataset from [11] and collected new data using the same crawling method. The lack of publicly available data and limited details about the crawling process, coupled with the potential for author overlap, raises questions about anonymity and reproducibility. 

Equation (2) appears to have a potential flaw, suggesting a limited connection between `st` and `ot`, which might restrict the network's full connectivity. Despite this, the CRCN architecture is technically sound and the entity-based coherence model is a valuable addition. Overall, the approach to generating sequences feels somewhat ad-hoc. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a novel and important problem – extending image captioning to image streams.
*   Presents a well-designed and technically sound architecture (CRCN).
*   Demonstrates strong quantitative and qualitative results, outperforming baselines.
*   Effectively incorporates a local coherence model to improve sentence fluency.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Fails to adequately address or cite relevant work in video captioning.
*   Data collection and availability are unclear, potentially compromising reproducibility.
*   Sentence retrieval method is relatively simplistic.
*   Potential error in Equation (2) raises concerns about network connectivity.

I believe this paper could be accepted as a poster presentation.
