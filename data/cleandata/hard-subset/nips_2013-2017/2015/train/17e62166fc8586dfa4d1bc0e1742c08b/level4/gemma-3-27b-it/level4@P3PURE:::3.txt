This work studies the problem of generating sentences from an image stream, proposing a coherent recurrent convolutional network (CRCN). The CRCN architecture combines convolutional neural networks, bidirectional recurrent neural networks, and an entity-based local coherence model. 

Overall, this is a strong contribution, though several areas could benefit from further development. First, the related work section omits several relevant papers in the video-to-sentence generation field, such as “[Jointly modeling deep video and compositional text to bridge vision and language in a unified framework]”. Second, while the quantitative results appear promising, the user study presented in Table 2 indicates performance comparable to one baseline, RCN. A significance test is necessary to determine if the observed improvements are statistically reliable. 

In summary, this paper presents a well-designed algorithm for sentence generation from an image stream. The quantitative results are encouraging, but the user study reveals only a marginal advantage over existing baselines.
