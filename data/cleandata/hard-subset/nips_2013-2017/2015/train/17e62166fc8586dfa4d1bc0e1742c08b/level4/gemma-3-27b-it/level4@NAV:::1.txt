This paper addresses the important problem of retrieving and generating natural language descriptions for a sequence of images, a task relevant to computer vision and pattern recognition. While significant, this work shares strong connections with the field of video captioning – where a video can be viewed as a sequence of images or shots, each described by a keyframe – yet fails to acknowledge related research, such as [Rohrbach+ 2014]. 

The paper is clearly written and logically structured, presenting a well-defined problem and a solution employing sound technical approaches.

The proposed CRCN model effectively captures the relationships between image sequences and corresponding sentences. Its architecture is technically strong, and the integration of entity-based coherence models effectively addresses discourse relationships. 

However, the sentence generation method feels somewhat basic. It relies on direct associations between sentences and similar training images, with sequences formed simply through concatenation based on image order. 

A concern arises regarding dataset transparency. Section 4.1 describes reusing and collecting data, yet the dataset itself remains undisclosed, and the original crawling methodology from [11] is not detailed. This, coupled with potential author overlap with [11], raises questions about anonymity. 

Furthermore, Equation (2) appears potentially flawed, suggesting a lack of full connectivity within the network as *st* appears to be derived solely from *ot*. Despite this, the model’s technical foundation and handling of discourse are positive. The sentence generation approach, however, remains *ad hoc*. Overall, I recommend acceptance as a poster paper.
