The paper addresses the problem of generating a coherent sequence of sentences to describe a sequence of images, such as those found in blog posts. The proposed approach first retrieves the K=5 most similar images and their corresponding sentences from the training set for each query image. The core contribution lies in defining a method for selecting the most relevant sentences for a given image sequence, thereby ensuring a coherent description. This is achieved by embedding sentences into a vector space and then modeling the resulting sequence using a bidirectional LSTM. The LSTM’s output is then processed through ReLU and fully connected layers, and a compatibility score is calculated between each image and sentence. Furthermore, a local coherence model [1] is incorporated to enforce compatibility *between* sentences.

Strengths / positive aspects: The paper presents a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for image sequences. A comprehensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluation demonstrates the superiority of the proposed approach over several baselines that do not explicitly model coherence. The authors also include an ablation study, specifically removing the coherence model [1], which strengthens their analysis. Finally, the authors have committed to releasing both source code and the dataset.

Weaknesses / Questions / Unclarities: 1. Line 94: The paper states that [5] lacks a mechanism for sentence coherence. However, while not its primary contribution, [5] does predict an intermediate semantic representation of videos, which inherently encourages coherence across sentences by modeling the overall topic of the multi-sentence description. 2. Correctness/clarity: Figure 2b appears inconsistent with the description in section 3.3 and Equation (2). Specifically, Figure 2b suggests fully connected layers connect to all sentences, whereas Equation (2) indicates parameter sharing across sentences, with connections only to the vector representing a single sentence. 3. Meteor (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) would be a more appropriate automatic evaluation metric than BLEU, especially given the use of only a single reference sentence. 4. The purpose of applying two linear functions (W{f2}, W{f1}) sequentially in Equation (2) is unclear, as two linear transformations can be collapsed into a single linear transformation. An ablation study investigating the benefit of this configuration would be valuable. 5. Why are the same parameters used for the fully connected layers when processing the BRNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model q (Equation 2)? 6. Was the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned during training, or was it kept fixed?

=== post rebuttal === After reviewing the rebuttal, I recommend the paper for acceptance. The authors have successfully addressed the concerns raised regarding the formulation, evaluation, and related work. 

Please also clarify the following point in the final version: Was the paragraph vector [16] fine-tuned or kept fixed? The paper introduces an interesting and well-evaluated new model for retrieving coherent sentences for image streams. However, several clarifications were necessary to make it a convincing contribution.
