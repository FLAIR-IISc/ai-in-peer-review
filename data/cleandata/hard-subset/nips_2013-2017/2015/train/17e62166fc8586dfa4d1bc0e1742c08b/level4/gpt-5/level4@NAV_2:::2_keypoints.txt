- Task: generate coherent natural-language sentence sequences for image streams from blog posts.  
- Method: retrieve K=5 nearest training images per query image with their sentences, then select and assemble the most relevant sentences for the whole stream.  
- Sentence modeling: sentences embedded into vectors; sequence modeled by a bidirectional LSTM.  
- Scoring: bi-LSTM outputs pass through ReLU and a fully connected layer; a compatibility function scores image–sentence alignment.  
- Coherence: incorporates a local coherence component to encourage sentence-to-sentence consistency.  
- Strength: introduces a novel, effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentence sequences conditioned on image streams.  
- Strength: provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluations and an ablation removing the coherence model.  
- Strength: authors commit to releasing code and dataset.  
- Weakness: claim about prior work [5] lacking coherence is overstated—[5] predicts intermediate semantic representations that impart cross-sentence coherence.  
- Weakness/clarity: Figure 2b seems inconsistent with Section 3.3/Equation (2) regarding whether FC layers connect to all sentences or operate per time step with shared parameters.  
- Weakness: automatic evaluation relies on BLEU; METEOR would be preferable with single references.  
- Weakness: unclear rationale for two successive linear mappings in Eq. (2) (W_f1 then W_f2); composition is linear—ablation suggested.  
- Weakness: unclear why the same FC parameters are used for both BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence vector q in Eq. (2).  
- Weakness: need clarification whether paragraph vectors [16] are fine-tuned during training or kept fixed.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer recommends acceptance after authors satisfactorily resolved issues; requests promised revisions and explicit clarification of whether paragraph vectors are updated.  
- Overall: interesting new model with convincing empirical support but requires a few clarifications in final version.
