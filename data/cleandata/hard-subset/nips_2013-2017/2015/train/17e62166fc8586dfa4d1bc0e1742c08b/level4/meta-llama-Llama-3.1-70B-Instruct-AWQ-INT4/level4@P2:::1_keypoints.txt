- Addresses retrieval of natural sentences for image sequences; significant to CV and pattern recognition but misses related work (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014).  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Proposes CRCN neural model that learns relationships between sentence sequences and image sequences; architecture is technically sound and uses entity-based coherence to model discourse.  
- Sentence-generation method is primitive/ad-hoc: each sentence is tied to similar training images and sequences are simply concatenated by image order.  
- Use of blog data is reasonable but lacks detail on crawling, raising anonymity concerns and possible overlap with authors of [11].  
- Equation (2) may be incorrect, implying st depends only on ot and that the network is not fully connected.  
- Overall recommendation: acceptable as a poster paper.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10; Clarity 8/10; Originality 6/10; Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a significant problem and presents a technically sound model that captures discourse relationships.  
- Arguments against acceptance: ad-hoc generation method, data-crawling/anonymity issues, and a potential equation error.
