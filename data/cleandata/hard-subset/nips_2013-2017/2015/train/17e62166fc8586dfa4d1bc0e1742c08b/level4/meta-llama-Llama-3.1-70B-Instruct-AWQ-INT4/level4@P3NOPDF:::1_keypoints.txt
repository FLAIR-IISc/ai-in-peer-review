- Addresses important problem of retrieving and sequencing natural sentences for image sequences, related to video description literature.  
- Fails to cite prior work on multi-sentence/video description (e.g., Rohrbach et al. 2014 and related shot/keyframe approaches).  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, with a clearly stated problem and solid techniques.  
- Proposed CRCN model effectively captures relationships between image sequences and sentence sequences and models discourse via entity-based coherence.  
- Network architecture is technically sound overall, but Equation (2) appears incorrect/oversimplified (implies st depends only on ot), suggesting possible model connectivity issues.  
- Sentence-generation method is primitive/ad-hoc: maps each image to similar training sentences and concatenates them in image order.  
- Dataset provenance is unclear: reused Disneyland blog data and newly crawled NYC data are undisclosed, crawling details missing, raising anonymity/conflict concerns with [11].  
- Despite limitations and minor flaws, the paper demonstrates understanding and could be accepted as a poster.
