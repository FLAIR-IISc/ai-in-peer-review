- Addresses an important problem (retrieving sentences for image sequences and assembling multi-sentence descriptions) relevant to CV and video description literature.  
- Omits citation of closely related video work (e.g., Rohrbach et al., 2014), a notable related-work gap.  
- Paper is well written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Proposes the CRCN neural network that learns relationships between image sequences and sentence sequences.  
- Architecture is technically sound and, with entity-based coherence models, effectively captures discourse relationships.  
- Sentence generation strategy is primitive: per-image retrieval followed by naive concatenation, limiting coherence and expressiveness.  
- Dataset and anonymity concerns: reused/collected data not disclosed and crawling procedure unclear, suggesting potential self-citation/anonymity breach with [11].  
- Technical issue: Equation (2) appears incorrect or misleading (s_t seems derived solely from o_t) and needs clarification or correction.  
- Overall recommendation: accept as a poster given strengths and the identified issues.
