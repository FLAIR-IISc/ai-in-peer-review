This paper addresses the problem of retrieving a sequence of natural sentences to describe a given sequence of images, effectively forming a paragraph. The problem is significant and relevant to the fields of computer vision and natural language processing. The paper is well-written, and the proposed approach is explained clearly.

**Strengths:**
*   The problem is well-stated and is tackled with a set of solid techniques.
*   The proposed Correspondence Recurrent Neural Network (CRCN) model for learning relationships between image and sentence sequences is technically sound.
*   The use of an entity-based coherence model to capture discourse relationships is a strong contribution.

**Weaknesses and Concerns:**
*   **Missing Related Work:** The paper fails to cite or discuss a closely related line of research: generating descriptive sentences for video content (e.g., [Rohrbach+ 2014]). A video can be seen as a sequence of images (key frames from video shots), making this body of work highly relevant to the problem addressed here. The omission is a significant oversight.
*   **Primitive Generation Method:** While the CRCN model is sound, the method for generating the final sentence sequence is rather ad-hoc. For a given input image, a sentence is selected based on its association with similar training images. These individually retrieved sentences are then simply concatenated in the order of the input images. This generation process seems less sophisticated than the modeling component.
*   **Potential Anonymity Breach:** In Section 4.1, the authors state: "We reuse the blog data of Disneyland from the dataset of [11], and newly collect the data of NYC, using the same crawling method with [11]." The dataset from [11] is not public, and that paper does not detail the crawling method. This strongly suggests that the authors of this paper are the same as those of [11], which compromises the double-blind review process.
*   **Possible Error in Equation (2):** The formulation of Equation (2) may be incorrect. As written, it implies that the state `s_t` is derived only from the output `o_t`, which would mean the recurrent network is not fully connected. This appears to contradict the model's description.

**Conclusion:**
The paper presents a technically sound model for a significant problem. However, it is weakened by a failure to acknowledge related work, a somewhat primitive sentence generation strategy, and a serious concern regarding anonymity. The potential error in Equation (2) should also be addressed. Despite these issues, the core technical contribution is valuable. I believe this paper can be accepted as a poster presentation.
