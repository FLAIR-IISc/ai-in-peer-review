This paper tackles the problem of retrieving natural sentences that describe a given sequence of images and forming a sequence of sentences. This problem is significant in the research fields of computer vision and pattern recognition, and is closely related to generating multiple sentences that describe given video content (e.g., [Rohrbach+ 2014]). Notably, single video content can be regarded as a sequence of images, as video content can be separated into multiple video shots, with each shot being well described by a key frame. However, the authors have not cited any work in this line of research.

The paper is well written and easy to follow, with a clearly stated problem and a solution that leverages a set of solid techniques. The proposed neural network model, named CRCN, effectively captures relationships between a sequence of natural sentences and a sequence of images. The architecture of the proposed model is technically sound, and it successfully describes discourse relationships with the help of entity-based coherence models.

The method for generating a sequence of natural sentences is reasonable but somewhat primitive. Specifically, every natural sentence is directly associated with training images similar to a given image, and all the sequences are simply concatenated in the order of the corresponding images.

In Section 4.1, the authors state, "We reuse the blog data of Disneyland from the dataset of [11], and newly collect the data of NYC, using the same crawling method with [11]." However, the dataset has not been disclosed, and the corresponding paper does not provide details on how to crawl the dataset. This raises concerns that the authors of this paper may be the same as those in [11], which would compromise the anonymity of the submission.

Furthermore, Equation (2) appears to be incorrect, as it implies that st can be derived from only ot, suggesting that the network is not fully connected. While the proposed neural network model is technically sound and effectively describes discourse relationships with the help of entity-based coherence models, the method for generating a sequence of sentences for a given image stream is somewhat ad-hoc. Despite these limitations, I believe that this paper can be accepted as a poster paper in its current form.
