

The paper addresses the problem of generating a coherent sequence of sentences to describe a sequence of images from blog posts. It proposes first retrieving the K=5 most similar images and associated sentences from the training set for each query image. The main contribution lies in defining a method to select the most relevant sentences for the query image sequence, ensuring coherence. Sentences are embedded as vectors, and the sequence of sentences is modeled using a bidirectional LSTM. The bidirectional LSTM output is processed through a ReLU-activated fully connected layer, followed by a compatibility score computed between the image and sentence representations. Additionally, a local coherence model [1] is incorporated to enforce compatibility between consecutive sentences.

Strengths:  
- The paper proposes a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for image sequences.  
- It provides extensive quantitative, qualitative, and human evaluation, demonstrating superiority over baselines that do not incorporate coherence. An ablation study removing the coherence model [1] further validates the approach.  
- The authors plan to release source code and the dataset.

Weaknesses and Clarifications Required:  
1. **Related Work (Line 94)**: The paper claims [5] lacks "a mechanism for coherence between sentences." While [5] does not focus on sentence coherence, it models an intermediate semantic representation for videos that inherently ensures coherence across sentences by capturing the topic of the multi-sentence description. This should be acknowledged.  
2. **Figure 2b vs. Equation (2)**: Figure 2b suggests the fully connected layer connects to all sentences, but Equation (2) indicates shared parameters across sentences and connections only to a single sentence’s vector. This discrepancy must be resolved.  
3. **Evaluation Metric**: BLEU is suboptimal for automatic evaluation when only a single reference sentence is available. METEOR (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) would be a more appropriate metric.  
4. **Redundant Linear Layers (Equation 2)**: The sequential application of two linear functions (W_{f2}, W_{f1}) is redundant, as their composition is equivalent to a single linear layer. An ablation study demonstrating the necessity of both layers is required.  
5. **Shared Parameters (Equation 2)**: The same fully connected layer parameters are used for both the BRNN output (o_t) and the local coherence model (q). The rationale for this sharing must be clarified.  
6. **Paragraph Vector [16]**: It is unclear whether the paragraph vector embedding from [16] is fine-tuned during training or kept fixed. This must be specified.  

Post-rebuttal:  
After reviewing the authors' rebuttal, I recommend acceptance. The authors successfully addressed all concerns regarding model formulation, evaluation metrics, and related work. The paper presents a compelling model for coherent sentence retrieval over image sequences, supported by rigorous evaluation. The requested clarifications (particularly on the paragraph vector [16]) have been adequately resolved in the revised manuscript.
