- Proposed method: retrieve K=5 nearest neighbor images/sentences per query image, embed sentences with paragraph vectors, model candidate-sentence sequence with a Bi-LSTM + FC(+ReLU) to score image–sentence compatibility, and use a local coherence model to select/order final sentences.

- Strength: Novel architecture combining retrieval, sequence modeling (Bi-LSTM), and a local coherence module to produce coherent multi-sentence image-sequence descriptions.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation (quantitative, qualitative, human) and ablation study showing importance of the local coherence component.

- Strength: Authors commit to releasing source code and dataset.

- Weakness: Overstatement regarding prior work [5] lacking any coherence mechanism; prior work models topic via an intermediate semantic representation that imparts thematic coherence—positioning should be clarified.

- Weakness: Architectural clarity issue—Figure 2b suggests FC input is a concatenation of sentence representations, while Eq.2 implies per-timestep shared-parameter application; authors should clarify the intended computation.

- Weakness: Evaluation metric—reliance on BLEU with a single reference is problematic; METEOR (or justification for BLEU) should be reported/argued.

- Weakness: Eq.2 appears to apply two linear transforms (W_f1, W_f2) without an explicit nonlinearity between them, which would be redundant; the presence/location of the ReLU should be made explicit and ablated if needed.

- Weakness: Questionable parameter sharing—using the same FC parameters for Bi-RNN outputs (o_t) and local coherence inputs (q_t) may be suboptimal since they model different aspects; consider separate parameter sets or justify sharing.

- Weakness: Unclear whether paragraph vector sentence embeddings are fine-tuned during training or kept fixed; this needs explicit clarification.

- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer recommends acceptance, states most concerns were addressed in the rebuttal, and requests the authors to explicitly clarify the paragraph-vector fine-tuning in the final paper.
