This paper tackles the problem of describing a sequence of images from blog posts with a sequence of mutually consistent sentences. To this end, for each query image the method first retrieves the K=5 most similar images and their associated sentences from the training set. The core contribution is a mechanism for selecting the most relevant sentences for the entire image sequence in a way that yields a coherent multi-sentence description. Sentences are embedded into vectors, and their sequence is modeled with a bidirectional LSTM. The bidirectional LSTM outputs are passed through a ReLU and a fully connected layer, and then scored via an image–sentence compatibility function. In addition, the method incorporates the local coherence model [1] to enforce compatibility across adjacent sentences.

Strengths / positive aspects:
- The paper proposes a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for an image sequence.
- The evaluation is extensive—quantitative, qualitative, and human studies—demonstrating superiority over several baselines that do not model coherence. An ablation study removing the coherence model [1] is also provided.
- The authors commit to releasing source code and the dataset.

Weaknesses / questions / clarifications:
1. Line 94: The paper states that [5] has “no mechanism for the coherence between sentences.” While explicit inter-sentence coherence is not the main contribution of [5], that work does predict an intermediate semantic representation for videos that is coherent across sentences by modeling the topic of the multi-sentence description. Please nuance the comparison accordingly.
2. Correctness/clarity: Figure 2b does not appear to match Section 3.3 / Equation (2). Figure 2b suggests that the fully connected layer connects to all sentences, whereas Eq. (2) implies that parameters are shared across sentences but applied only to the vector representing a single sentence. Please reconcile the figure with the formal description.
3. Evaluation metric: METEOR (http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~alavie/METEOR/) is generally a better automatic metric than BLEU for sentence generation, especially with a single reference. Please report METEOR or justify the reliance on BLEU.
4. Architecture design in Eq. (2): Why apply two linear transformations (W_{f2}, W_{f1}) in sequence? Since the composition of two linear maps is still linear, the benefit is unclear without a nonlinearity between them. An ablation showing the effect of these layers (and the presence/absence of intermediate nonlinearities) would be informative.
5. Parameter sharing: Why are the same fully connected layer parameters used for both the BRNN output o_t and the local coherence model q in Eq. (2)? Please justify this choice or report results without sharing to quantify its impact.
6. Paragraph vector [16]: Is the paragraph vector fine-tuned during training or kept fixed? Please clarify.

=== Post-rebuttal ===
After reading the rebuttal, I recommend acceptance. The authors have satisfactorily addressed issues concerning the formulation, evaluation, and related work. Please incorporate the promised changes in the final version and explicitly clarify point (6): whether the paragraph vector [16] is fine-tuned or fixed. The paper presents an interesting and well-evaluated model for retrieving coherent sentences for image streams; with the above clarifications included in the camera-ready, it will be a strong contribution.
