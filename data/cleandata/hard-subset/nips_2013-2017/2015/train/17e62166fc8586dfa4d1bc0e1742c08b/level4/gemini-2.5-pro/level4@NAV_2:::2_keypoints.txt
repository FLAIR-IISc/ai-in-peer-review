- Task and contribution: Proposes CRCN to generate coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams by retrieving candidate sentences and selecting/ordering them with a CNN+BRNN+entity-based coherence model.  
- Output scoring: Final paragraphs are ranked by a compatibility score combining image–sentence relevance and textual coherence.  
- Strength — novelty: Introduces a novel and well-motivated problem moving from single image-to-sentence to sequence generation.  
- Strength — architecture: CRCN’s integration of CNN, BRNN, and an entity-based local coherence model is appropriate for the task.  
- Strength — experiments: Extensive, convincing evaluation against baselines with quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, and an AMT user study.  
- Strength — ablation: Ablation study isolates and demonstrates the value of the coherence component.  
- Strength — reproducibility: Authors plan to release code and dataset, which benefits the community.  
- Weakness — prior work comparison: Comparison to Donahue et al. is oversimplified; need a more nuanced discussion about their implicit coherence mechanisms.  
- Weakness — architectural clarity: Figure 2b and Eq. (2) appear inconsistent regarding how fully connected layers are applied—clarify whether FCs operate on whole sequences or per-sentence with shared params.  
- Weakness — FC design: Using two consecutive linear layers (Wf1, Wf2) is unexplained since two linear transforms collapse to one; request ablation/justification.  
- Weakness — parameter sharing: Rationale for sharing FC parameters between BRNN outputs and the local coherence model is unclear—justify versus separate parameters.  
- Weakness — evaluation metrics: Relying on BLEU may be insufficient; include metrics like METEOR that correlate better with human judgments for single-reference generation.  
- Weakness — implementation detail: Clarify whether paragraph vectors used for sentence representation were fine-tuned during CRCN training or kept fixed.  
- Overall conclusion: Compelling model and strong evaluation, but the contribution would be strengthened by addressing architectural, evaluation, and prior-work comparison clarifications.
