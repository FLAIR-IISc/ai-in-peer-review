- Task & method: Generates coherent multi-sentence descriptions for image streams by retrieving top-5 similar training images per query and selecting sentences using sentence embeddings, a BRNN, and a local coherence model.
- Main contribution: A model that scores candidate sentence sequences for coherence by combining BRNN outputs with local coherence features.
- Strength: Proposes a novel and effective architecture for retrieving coherent sentences for image sequences.
- Strength: Extensive evaluation (quantitative metrics, qualitative examples, human study) showing superior performance over baselines that do not model coherence.
- Strength: Includes an ablation study validating the coherence model’s contribution.
- Strength: Authors plan to release source code and dataset, benefiting the community.
- Question: The paper’s claim that Donahue et al. [5] has “no mechanism for coherence” is disputed—[5] may implicitly model coherence via intermediate semantic representations; please clarify the distinction.
- Question: Apparent discrepancy between Figure 2b and Eq. (2): do the fully connected layers take concatenated {o_t} or apply shared weights independently to each o_t?
- Suggestion: Use METEOR (more reliable than BLEU for single-reference evaluation) to provide a more robust automatic assessment.
- Question: Eq. (2) uses two linear layers (Wf1, Wf2) whose composition is linear; an ablation study is needed to justify the two-layer design beyond dropout.
- Question: Why share the same fully connected layer parameters for both BRNN outputs (o_t) and the local coherence vector (q) in Eq. (2)? Provide intuition for this parameter sharing.
- Question: Are paragraph vectors [17] fine-tuned during CRCN training or kept fixed after pretraining?
- Concluding remark: The model is interesting and convincingly evaluated, but several clarifications are required to make the paper more convincing.
