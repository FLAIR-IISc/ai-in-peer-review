- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity for latent variable models.  
- Develops a general inferential framework (decorrelated score test) for low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Decorrelated score test is asymptotically normal and attains the semiparametric information bound (optimal).  
- Algorithm converges at a geometric rate and achieves a (near-)optimal statistical convergence rate.  
- Framework is general and applicable to various latent variable models.  
- Theoretical results are thorough; computational and statistical analyses are impressive.  
- Regularity/verification conditions are reasonable and can be checked for specific models.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, well-organized methodology section.  
- Provides a detailed and helpful comparison to related work (e.g., tensor methods, post-selection).  
- Main limitation: no empirical results or simulations to demonstrate practical effectiveness.  
- Potential concern: verifying theoretical conditions may be difficult for some latent variable models.  
- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add empirical results and more details on verifying theoretical conditions.
