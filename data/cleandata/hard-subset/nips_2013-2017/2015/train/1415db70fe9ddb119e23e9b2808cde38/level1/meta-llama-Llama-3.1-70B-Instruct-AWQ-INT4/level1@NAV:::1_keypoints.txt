- Summary: Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity, achieves near-optimal convergence, and introduces a decorrelated score for inference within a unified estimation-and-uncertainty framework.  
- Strength: Novel algorithm leveraging sparsity for efficient high-dimensional latent-variable estimation.  
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical guarantees including convergence rates and asymptotic normality.  
- Strength: Unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment.  
- Strength: Empirical demonstrations on Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.  
- Weakness: Relies on several strong assumptions (sparsity of true parameter, Lipschitz continuity) that may not always hold.  
- Weakness: Potentially high computational complexity in very high-dimensional settings.  
- Weakness: Limited number of simulations to validate empirical performance.  
- Originality: Novel contribution in incorporating sparsity into high-dimensional EM and unifying estimation and inference.  
- Significance: Broadly significant with potential applications across machine learning, statistics, and signal processing.  
- Clarity: Well-written and clearly organized with detailed explanations.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Pro arguments: Novel method, strong theoretical support, and empirical demonstrations.  
- Con arguments: Restrictive assumptions, computational demands, and limited simulations.
