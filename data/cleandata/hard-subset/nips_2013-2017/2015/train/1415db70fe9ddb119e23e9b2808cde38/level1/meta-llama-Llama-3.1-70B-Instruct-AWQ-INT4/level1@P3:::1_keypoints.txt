- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity in parameter estimation.  
- Establishes general theory for the algorithm, including convergence rate and asymptotic normality.  
- Develops a decorrelated score test for hypotheses on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Algorithm shown to converge at a geometric rate to an estimator with (near-)optimal statistical rate.  
- Theoretical results are rigorously proven with clear assumptions and conditions.  
- Methodology is original and the decorrelated score test is a novel contribution.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Significance is high with potential broad applicability in machine learning, statistics, and data science.  
- Clarity of exposition and presentation of theoretical results is strong.  
- Limitation: relies on the assumption that the true parameter is sparse.  
- Limitation: requires a suitable initialization that may be difficult to obtain in practice.  
- Lacks a detailed empirical or methodological comparison with existing methods.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication, with a suggestion to add more comparisons and discuss real-world limitations.
