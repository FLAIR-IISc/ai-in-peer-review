- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that adds a truncation step to enforce sparsity and proves geometric convergence to a near-minimax optimal estimator.  
- Develops a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional parameter components and establishes its asymptotic normality for valid inference.  
- Extends prior analyses of EM from low-dimensional to high-dimensional settings and addresses non-concave likelihoods in latent variable models.  
- Connects the work to high-dimensional inference literature for convex methods (e.g., Lasso) while distinguishing itself by handling non-convex EM.  
- Significant: tackles a long-standing, important problem by providing a unified framework for estimation and inference in high-dimensional EM.  
- Original: introduces truncation within EM for sparsity and a provably valid inferential procedure for a non-convex iterative algorithm.  
- High technical quality: rigorous theoretical analysis with clearly stated conditions, covering both computational and statistical properties.  
- Clarity: well-written, well-organized, and clearly motivated with contributions and literature context.  
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees require initialization inside a basin of attraction, with no practical guidance for finding such an initial point.  
- Weakness: main technical conditions are general/abstract and may be hard to verify for new latent variable models beyond the provided examples.  
- Overall: landmark contribution whose strengths in significance and originality substantially outweigh the minor practical and verification concerns.
