- Main contributions: a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step enforcing sparsity and achieving near-optimal statistical rates with geometric convergence under suitable initialization.
- Main contributions: a decorrelated score statistic enabling hypothesis testing and the first computationally feasible framework for simultaneous estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Applications: framework instantiated for Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regression models, showing favorable comparison to existing methods.
- Strength — Technical novelty: truncation in EM is innovative and addresses high dimensionality with rigorous computational and statistical guarantees.
- Strength — Unified framework: combines parameter estimation and uncertainty quantification for high-dimensional latent variable problems.
- Strength — Asymptotic inference: decorrelated score achieves the semiparametric efficiency bound for optimal inference with high-dimensional nuisances.
- Strength — Theoretical rigor: detailed convergence and statistical guarantees with comprehensive proofs.
- Strength — Literature positioning: effectively extends prior work to high-dimensional settings and fills gaps in computational/statistical guarantees.
- Weakness — Practical validation: experiments are limited and lack demonstration on real-world or more complex latent-variable datasets.
- Weakness — Initialization dependence: algorithm requires suitable initialization but offers little practical guidance for obtaining it.
- Weakness — Implementation complexity: truncation and decorrelated score computations may be non-trivial for practitioners.
- Weakness — Sparsity assumptions: reliance on sparsity for parameters and score may not hold across practical applications.
- Acceptance arguments: addresses a theoretical gap, provides a general and impactful framework, and advances estimation and inference theory.
- Rejection arguments: limited empirical validation and restrictive assumptions/initialization needs could limit practical applicability.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on encouraging more empirical results and practical guidance in future revisions.
