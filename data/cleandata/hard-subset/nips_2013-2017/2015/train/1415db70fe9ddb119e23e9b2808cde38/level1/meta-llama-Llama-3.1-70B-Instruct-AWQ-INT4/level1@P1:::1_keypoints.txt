- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and improve statistical convergence rates.  
- Develops a decorrelated score test for hypotheses on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Provides a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment.  
- Main contribution 1: EM algorithm converges geometrically and attains (near-)optimal statistical rates.  
- Main contribution 2: Decorrelated score test is asymptotically normal and attains the semiparametric information lower bound.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and a well-organized methodology section.  
- Theoretical results are comprehensive and impressive, with reasonable conditions verified for specific models.  
- Methodology is computationally efficient and applicable to a broad family of latent variable models.  
- Strength: unified approach combining estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Strength: decorrelated score test achieves asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency.  
- Limitation: assumes the sparsity parameter is known, which is unrealistic in many practical settings.  
- Limitation: lacks numerical experiments demonstrating empirical performance of the algorithm and test.  
- Related work: provides a comprehensive review and clearly positions the proposed framework against existing methods.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant contributions, novel methods, and strong theoretical guarantees.  
- Rejection arguments: reliance on known sparsity parameter and absence of empirical validation.
