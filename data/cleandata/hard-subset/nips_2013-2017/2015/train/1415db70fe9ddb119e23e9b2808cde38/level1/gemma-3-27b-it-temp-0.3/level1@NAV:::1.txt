## Review of “High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality”

This paper tackles the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors make a significant contribution by proposing a modified EM algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and then providing statistical and computational guarantees for this algorithm, including asymptotic normality for low-dimensional parameters. This addresses a key gap in the literature, as existing EM theory largely focuses on low-dimensional settings.

The paper builds upon prior work establishing convergence of the EM algorithm (e.g., [2, 18, 25, 30]) and extends it to the high-dimensional regime where d > n. The proposed truncation step is a novel approach to address the challenges posed by high dimensionality and is well-motivated. The authors carefully establish conditions under which the algorithm converges at an optimal rate (p s* log d/n) and demonstrate asymptotic normality, a crucial result for statistical inference. The application to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models provides concrete examples of the framework’s utility.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The high-dimensional EM algorithm with the truncation step is a novel contribution.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical analysis, including convergence rates and asymptotic normality.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The results have implications for a broad class of latent variable models commonly used in various fields.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-organized, and the notation is consistent.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Condition Complexity:** The technical conditions (3.1-3.3, 4.1-4.5) are numerous and can be difficult to verify in practice. While the authors demonstrate verification for specific models, the general applicability might be limited by the difficulty of checking these conditions.
*   **Constant Factors:** The constants involved in the convergence rates and asymptotic normality results are not fully characterized, making it difficult to assess the practical performance of the algorithm.
*   **Initialization Sensitivity:** The algorithm's performance relies on a "suitable initialization" (init ⇤ /k ⇤k 2 < ), which is a common requirement but could be a practical limitation. The paper doesn’t offer guidance on how to obtain such an initialization.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant open problem in the field of latent variable modeling.
*   The theoretical results are rigorous and provide valuable insights into the behavior of the EM algorithm in high dimensions.
*   The proposed algorithm has the potential to be useful in a wide range of applications.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The technical complexity and stringent conditions might limit the practical applicability of the results.
*   The lack of detailed discussion on initialization strategies could hinder the adoption of the algorithm.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field and should be **accepted**. While the technical complexity and condition verification present challenges, the novelty and theoretical rigor of the work outweigh these concerns. The paper provides a valuable foundation for future research on high-dimensional latent variable modeling and inference. I recommend the authors consider adding a discussion on practical initialization strategies and providing more insight into the constant factors in their theoretical results to further enhance the paper’s impact.
