- Paper proposes truncated EM (hard-threshold T-step) for high-dimensional latent-variable models, analyzing exact and gradient M-step variants.  
- Shows geometric contraction plus near-minimax estimation rate O(√(s* log d / n)) up to log factors under local curvature/Lipschitz and good initialization.  
- Develops a decorrelated score/Wald test using a Dantzig-selector for orthogonalization, proving asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency.  
- Framework instantiated and verified for Gaussian mixture models (GMM) and mixtures of regressions, with explicit SNR and other condition checks.  
- Builds on low-dimensional EM analyses (Balakrishnan et al. 2014), debiasing/desparsification literature, penalized EM, IHT, and contrasts with tensor methods claiming sharper rates.  
- Conceptual strength: simple, practical T-step modification that enforces sparsity and cleanly separates optimization vs. statistical error.  
- Theoretical strength: novel entrywise (ℓ1) control of sample/population M-step discrepancy and geometric contraction proofs for both M-step types.  
- Inference strength: first asymptotically valid, efficient testing procedure for low-dimensional components in nonconvex latent-variable settings.  
- Generality strength: abstract framework with concrete verifications for important example models.  
- Clear exposition of optimization–statistics tradeoff, basin-of-attraction role, and tuning of bs.  
- Major weakness: requires initialization inside a relatively small ℓ2 basin; no practical high-dimensional initializers or theoretical guarantees provided.  
- Major weakness: algorithm depends on knowing bs ≍ s*; no analysis of sensitivity to mis-specified bs or adaptive/data-driven selection strategies.  
- Proofs analyze a resampling/split-sample EM (sample-inefficient) rather than the standard single-sample iterate; no theory or experiments confirming single-sample behavior.  
- Assumptions scope: strong SNR required for GMM—need discussion of tightness, component overlap, and performance degradation with lower SNR.  
- Inference assumption: sparsity of orthogonalization vector w* required—need discussion/examples where s_w* is small vs large and interpretability in mixtures.  
- Computational concern: Dantzig program involving d×d matrices per test can be heavy; need discussion of scalable solvers, nodewise/approximate variants, and their effect on inference.  
- Methodological comparison lacking: clearer theoretical and empirical comparison to ℓ1-penalized EM, proximal EM, and IHT/projection methods is needed.  
- Empirical evaluation weak: request simulations comparing classical EM+post-thresholding, ℓ1-penalized EM, and truncated EM, testing sensitivity to initialization and bs, and assessing Type I error/power.  
- Quality: technical development appears sound and well-structured, but some proofs are deferred and resampling reliance should be sketched in main text; fix typos/incomplete refs.  
- Clarity: paper is dense with heavy notation—add symbol table, running example, and more intuition for key conditions and Tn(·).  
- Originality: high — novel combination of truncated EM, sharp high-dimensional rates, and semiparametric-efficient inference for latent-variable models.  
- Significance: high — provides estimation and valid inference tools for widely used high-dimensional latent-variable models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, general framework; near-optimal rates with geometric convergence; first efficient inference in this setting; concrete model specializations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on strong initialization and known sparsity; resampling-based theory instead of single-sample analysis; limited empirical validation; strong SNR and sparsity assumptions may restrict applicability.  
- Key questions for authors: provide a theoretically justified high-dimensional initializer; quantify robustness/adaptive selection for bs; extend results to K>2 components and other missing-data models; comment on multi-coordinate inference and FDR control.  
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance conditional on clarifying initialization and adding empirical validation.
