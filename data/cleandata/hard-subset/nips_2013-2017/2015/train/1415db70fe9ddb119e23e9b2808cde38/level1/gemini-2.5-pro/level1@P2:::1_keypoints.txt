- Proposes a High-Dimensional EM algorithm that inserts a truncation (hard-thresholding) step after M-step to enforce sparsity.
- Proves geometric convergence of the algorithm (with good initialization) to an estimator achieving near-minimax statistical error sqrt(s*log(d)/n).
- Develops a decorrelated score test for inference on individual parameters of high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Shows the test statistic is asymptotically normal and attains the semiparametric information bound.
- Extends debiasing/desparsifying ideas to non-convex EM outputs and provides entrywise error analysis tailored to truncation.
- Validates the framework on high-dimensional Gaussian mixture models and mixtures of regressions.
- Theoretical claims are rigorous, assumptions are clearly stated and standard for the field.
- Analysis cleanly decomposes error into geometrically decaying optimization error plus final statistical error.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and accessible to readers with relevant background.
- Highly original: first general theoretical framework for EM in d >> n and first computationally feasible inference procedure for EM outputs.
- Significant impact for researchers (blueprint for other models) and practitioners (practical algorithm with uncertainty quantification).
- Pros: fundamental contribution, strong technical results, high impact, clarity and novelty.
- Cons: requires a good initializer without a general provable procedure provided.
- Cons: inference relies on a sparsity assumption on w* that may be hard to verify or not hold broadly.
- Cons: practical guidance for tuning parameters (sparsity level, lambda) is limited.
- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept.
