- Proposes a truncated EM algorithm for high-dimensional latent variable models (d > n) to enforce sparsity.  
- Establishes statistical and computational guarantees: convergence rates and asymptotic normality for low-dimensional components.  
- Addresses an important open problem by extending EM theory to high-dimensional settings.  
- Builds on prior EM convergence results ([2, 25, 30]) and connects to high-dimensional statistics literature.  
- Presents a general framework applicable to multiple models, illustrated with Gaussian Mixture and Mixture of Regression.  
- Offers detailed theoretical analysis with concrete error and inference bounds.  
- Provides clear and comprehensive related-work discussion positioning the contribution well.  
- Includes detailed algorithms and mathematical presentation that make technical arguments followable.  
- Relies on several strong, potentially hard-to-verify assumptions (Conditions 3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5), especially about sparsity.  
- Constant factors in the bounds may be large and could hurt practical performance.  
- Computational complexity and scalability, particularly of the truncation step, are not sufficiently addressed.  
- Lacks empirical validation on simulated or real data to demonstrate practical feasibility and performance.  
- Notation is dense and could be clarified to improve readability.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong and novel theoretical contribution that outweighs weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify/justify assumptions, discuss computational complexity, add preliminary experiments, and simplify notation.
