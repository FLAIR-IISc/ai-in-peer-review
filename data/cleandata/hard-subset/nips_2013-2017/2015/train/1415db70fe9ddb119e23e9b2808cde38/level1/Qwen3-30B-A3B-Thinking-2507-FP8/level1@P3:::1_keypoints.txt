- Addresses EM in high-dimensional settings (d > n) by proposing a sparsity-enforcing truncation step within EM.  
- Establishes geometric convergence of the algorithm with near-optimal statistical error rates.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for asymptotic inference on low-dimensional components in latent variable models.  
- Provides the first simultaneous computational and statistical guarantees for high-dimensional EM, including semiparametric efficiency.  
- Differentiates from prior low-dimensional EM and tensor methods by enforcing sparsity along the solution path.  
- Builds on and extends high-dimensional inference literature to nonconvex latent variable models.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, decomposing estimation error into optimization and statistical components.  
- Proofs handle the technical challenge of the truncation step and verify conditions for key examples (Gaussian mixtures, mixture of regressions).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear definitions, notation, and intuitive explanations.  
- Major strengths: theoretical completeness, novel truncation technique, and efficient inference procedure.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of practical implementation (choice of sparsity parameter, initialization), no empirical validation, and unclear M-step computational complexity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance solving a fundamental problem with minimax-optimal rates; broadly applicable framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical utility untested due to lack of experiments and potentially restrictive initialization assumptions.
