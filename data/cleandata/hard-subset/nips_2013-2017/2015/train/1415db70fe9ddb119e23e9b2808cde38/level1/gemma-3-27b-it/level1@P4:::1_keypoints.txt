- Core idea: introduce a truncation step in EM to enforce sparsity, enabling statistical-rate convergence and asymptotic normality.  
- Novelty: extends recent low-dimensional geometric convergence results to the high-dimensional regime via truncation.  
- Relation to prior work: situates contribution relative to Lasso-type and tensor methods, filling a gap for computationally feasible inference in latent variable models.  
- Applicability: framework demonstrated on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models, suggesting broader applicability.  
- Theoretical strength: delivers comprehensive analysis including parameter convergence rates and asymptotic normality for hypothesis testing.  
- Optimality claim: inference results positioned in terms of semiparametric efficiency.  
- Clarity of assumptions: assumptions are clearly stated, though many conditions are required.  
- Readability: mathematical presentation is dense with heavy notation that could be streamlined.

- Unknown constants: constant factors in convergence rates are not fully characterized, complicating practical assessment.
