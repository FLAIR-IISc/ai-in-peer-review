- Addresses a major gap by providing theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity.
- Establishes geometric convergence for parameter estimation and asymptotic normality for inference of low-dimensional components.
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM analyses (Balakrishnan et al., 2015; Taku et al., 2018) to the high-dimensional regime.
- The algorithm is well-motivated and tackles both non-convexity and high dimensionality simultaneously.
- Theoretical analysis appears rigorous, providing optimization and statistical convergence rates under clearly stated conditions.
- Conditions (3.1–3.3) are reasonable and well-defined, forming a solid basis for the results.
- Provides a framework for valid statistical inference in high dimensions, not just estimation.
- Includes practical examples (Gaussian mixture and mixture of regressions) to demonstrate applicability.
- Presentation is dense with complex notation, making the paper challenging to follow.
- Related work discussion is incomplete, lacking coverage of recent methods like spectral approaches and sparse factor analysis (e.g., Chen et al. 2019).
- Assumptions on sparsity level and Fisher information are restrictive and their real-world implications are underexplored.
- Proofs are extensive but require significant expertise to verify fully.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, and practical significance for high-dimensional inference.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited clarity and restrictive assumptions needing further justification.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and discuss assumption limitations.
