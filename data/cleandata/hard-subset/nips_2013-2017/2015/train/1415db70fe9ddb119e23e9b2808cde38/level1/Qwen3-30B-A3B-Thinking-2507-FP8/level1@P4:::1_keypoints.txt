- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation (T-step) to enforce sparsity along the EM path.  
- Establishes geometric convergence of the algorithm and near-optimal statistical rate s·log(d)/n for parameter estimation.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing of low-dimensional components with asymptotic normality and optimal variance.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees (Theorems 3.4 and 4.6) under well-motivated conditions, verified for Gaussian mixture and mixture regression models.  
- Fills a gap in literature by extending EM theory to high-dimensional latent variable models where d >> n.  
- The results match minimax lower bounds and apply to a broad family of latent variable models, making the framework widely applicable.  
- Main computational concern: paper lacks detailed complexity analysis, especially for the cost of the truncation step and M-step implementation.  
- Lacks empirical validation on real datasets and comparisons with existing methods (e.g., gradient EM with sparsity).  
- Some technical conditions (e.g., Condition 3.3) may be hard to verify broadly; verification is shown only for specific models in the appendix.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel theoretical contribution with high relevance and a clear case for acceptance despite limited empirical and complexity analysis.
