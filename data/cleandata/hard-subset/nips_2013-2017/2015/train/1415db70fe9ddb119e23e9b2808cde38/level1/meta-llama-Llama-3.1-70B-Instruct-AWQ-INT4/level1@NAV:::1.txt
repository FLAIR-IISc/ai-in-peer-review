**Paper Review: High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation. The algorithm is designed for inferring high-dimensional latent variable models and is shown to achieve an estimator with a near-optimal statistical rate of convergence. The authors also propose a decorrelated score statistic for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters. The paper provides a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment in high-dimensional regimes.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel algorithm**: The proposed high-dimensional EM algorithm is a significant contribution to the field of latent variable models. The incorporation of sparsity structure into parameter estimation is a key innovation that allows for efficient estimation in high-dimensional settings.
2. **Theoretical guarantees**: The paper provides rigorous theoretical guarantees for the proposed algorithm, including convergence rates and asymptotic normality of the estimator.
3. **Unified framework**: The authors provide a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment in high-dimensional regimes, which is a significant contribution to the field.
4. **Applications**: The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm on two high-dimensional latent variable models, namely Gaussian mixture model and mixture of regression model.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Assumptions**: The paper relies on several assumptions, including the sparsity assumption on the true parameter and the Lipschitz continuity of the population version lower bound function. While these assumptions are reasonable, they may not hold in all cases.
2. **Computational complexity**: The proposed algorithm may be computationally intensive, particularly in high-dimensional settings.
3. **Limited simulations**: The paper only provides a limited number of simulations to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

**Originality**

The paper is original in its proposal of a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation. The authors also provide a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment in high-dimensional regimes, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Significance**

The paper is significant because it provides a novel solution to the problem of parameter estimation in high-dimensional latent variable models. The proposed algorithm has the potential to be widely applicable in many fields, including machine learning, statistics, and signal processing.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a detailed introduction to the problem, a clear explanation of the proposed algorithm, and a thorough analysis of the theoretical guarantees.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting this paper for publication in the conference proceedings. The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of latent variable models and provides a novel solution to the problem of parameter estimation in high-dimensional settings.
