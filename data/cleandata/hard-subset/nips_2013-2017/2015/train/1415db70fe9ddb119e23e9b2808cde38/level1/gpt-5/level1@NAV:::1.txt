This paper develops a high-dimensional theory for EM in latent variable models. The main ideas are: (i) a truncated EM algorithm that inserts a sparsity-enforcing T-step after the E- and M-steps, with analysis for both exact and gradient-ascent M-steps; and (ii) a decorrelated score (and Wald) inferential procedure for low-dimensional components in the presence of high-dimensional nuisance parameters, leveraging a Dantzig-type construction. Under suitable initialization and model-specific regularity, the estimator converges geometrically and achieves near-minimax ℓ2 error O(√(s* log d / n)), and the score statistic is asymptotically normal with semiparametric efficiency. The framework is instantiated for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions. Relative to prior work, this extends population-to-sample EM analyses in low dimension (e.g., Balakrishnan et al.) and debiased inference for GLMs (van de Geer, Zhang & Zhang) to nonconvex latent-variable settings, addressing a gap not covered by tensor methods or convex-penalized approaches.

Quality: The technical development is careful, with clear separation of optimization and statistical errors, verification of conditions for exemplar models, and semiparametric optimality. Assumptions are, however, strong: (a) a basin-of-attraction initialization; (b) high SNR/separation for mixtures; (c) sparsity of the efficient influence direction w*; and (d) a resampling analysis (fresh data per iteration) used to simplify proofs. Practical translation to a single-sample EM is hinted but not fully proved. Tuning of the truncation level b_s near s* and the Dantzig parameter also needs more guidance.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-organized, but notation is heavy. It would help to streamline conditions, provide a practical recipe for initialization, b_s selection, and stopping, and improve reference formatting.

Originality: High. The sparsity-truncated EM with rigorous rates, coupled to decorrelated score inference for latent models, is novel.

Significance: High. The results offer the first computationally feasible route to optimal estimation and asymptotically valid inference in high-dimensional latent-variable models, likely to influence both theory and practice.

Pros for acceptance:
- New algorithmic framework with geometric convergence and near-minimax rates.
- First asymptotically valid, semiparametrically efficient inference for latent-variable EM in high dimension.
- Covers both exact and gradient EM and two canonical models.

Cons for acceptance:
- Reliance on resampling analysis; single-sample guarantees need strengthening.
- Strong assumptions (initialization, SNR, sparsity of w*).
- Limited empirical validation and practical tuning guidance for b_s and Dantzig parameter.
