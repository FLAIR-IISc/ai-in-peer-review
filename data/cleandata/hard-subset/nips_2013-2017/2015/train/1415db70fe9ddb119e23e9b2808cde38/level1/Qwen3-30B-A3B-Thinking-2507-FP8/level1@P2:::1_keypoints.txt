- Introduces a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, achieving geometric convergence to a near-optimal estimator (p(s* log d / n)).  
- Proposes a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing of low-dimensional components that attains the semiparametric efficiency bound.  
- Fills a gap in theory by providing the first rigorous guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models, unlike prior low-dimensional EM analyses.  
- The truncation (T-) step is a novel technical contribution enabling analysis and performance in high dimensions.  
- Contrasts with tensor-based methods (suboptimal in high d) and Lasso-based inference (inapplicable to nonconvex latent-variable likelihoods).  
- Theoretical results are rigorous with detailed conditions (Conditions 3.1–3.3) and full proofs in the appendix.  
- Establishes both computational guarantees (geometric convergence) and statistical guarantees (near-optimal rate of convergence).  
- Provides a unified framework handling both exact maximization and gradient-ascent implementations of the M-step.  
- Presentation is clear and well-structured with precise pseudocode (Algorithms 1–3) and consistent notation.  
- Some technical conditions (e.g., Condition 3.3) are complex and may be difficult for non-experts to verify.  
- Paper demonstrates applicability to Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models but leaves broader model applicability open.  
- Sparsity assumptions on w* (Assumption 4.5) may be restrictive for certain applications.  
- High significance: addresses an important open problem in high-dimensional statistics and advances theory for widely used latent variable models.  
- Could benefit from additional empirical validation to complement the strong theoretical contributions.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant; reviewer recommends acceptance.
