- Tackles an important problem: theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Main contribution: high-dimensional EM with sparsity-inducing truncation and both statistical and computational guarantees.  
- Establishes asymptotic normality for low-dimensional parameters despite high-dimensional nuisance parameters.  
- Builds on prior EM convergence work but extends it to the d > n regime via truncation in E and M steps.  
- Correctly identifies key high-dimensional obstacles: ill-defined MLE without regularization and nuisance-parameter difficulties for inference.  
- Achieves sharper statistical rates (p s* log d / n) than tensor-based high-dimensional methods.  
- Connects appropriately to sparse estimation literature (e.g., Lasso, Dantzig selector).  
- Novelty: truncation step is a crucial and clever innovation enabling guarantees.  
- Theoretical rigor: detailed analysis with geometric convergence rates and verifiable technical conditions.  
- Generality: framework applies to multiple latent variable models (e.g., GMM, mixture of regressions).  
- Practical relevance: algorithm potentially useful across many high-dimensional applications.  
- Presentation: well-written, organized, and technically clear.  
- Related work: thorough and well-positioned within the literature.  
- Complexity: heavy theoretical machinery and many conditions may hinder practical adoption by non-experts.  
- Initialization sensitivity: performance depends on a “good” initialization and the paper gives little practical guidance for obtaining it.  
- Computational cost: truncation step may increase runtime and needs a complexity/optimization discussion.  
- Strong sparsity assumptions: assumptions on s* and w* are strong and require more discussion about robustness when violated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental open problem with substantial, rigorous technical contributions and broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: theoretical complexity, initialization sensitivity, and potential computational burden may limit practical impact.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add practical initialization strategies, analyze computational complexity and optimizations, discuss sensitivity to sparsity violations, and clarify notation.
