

**Review**

This paper proposes a high-dimensional extension of the expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm for latent variable models, with theoretical guarantees for both parameter estimation and statistical inference in high-dimensional settings. The authors introduce a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates a sparsity-enforcing truncation step, and establish geometric convergence rates to an estimator with near-optimal statistical rate of convergence. They further propose a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing of low-dimensional components, establishing asymptotic normality with optimal limiting variance.

The paper makes a significant contribution by extending the theoretical understanding of the EM algorithm to high-dimensional settings, where previous work (e.g., [2, 18, 25, 30]) was limited to low-dimensional regimes. The truncation step (T-step) is a key innovation that enables the authors to handle high-dimensional sparsity, which is not addressed in prior work. The paper carefully distinguishes itself from related work on high-dimensional inference (e.g., [4, 13, 15, 26, 32]), showing that their approach is applicable to latent variable models where existing methods (which rely on convex optimization) cannot be directly applied. The comparison with tensor methods ([1, 9]) is particularly compelling, as the authors demonstrate that their approach achieves a superior statistical rate of convergence.

**Strengths:**

1. **Quality:** The theoretical results are rigorous and well-supported. The paper establishes both computational (geometric convergence) and statistical (near-optimal rate) guarantees with careful analysis of the interplay between optimization error and statistical error. The proofs in the appendix are comprehensive, and the authors provide a clear decomposition of the error terms (optimization vs. statistical). The decorrelated score statistic is well-motivated and the asymptotic normality result is a significant contribution to inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.

2. **Originality:** The paper is highly original in addressing the high-dimensional extension of the EM algorithm, which has been a significant gap in the literature. The truncation step is a novel technical innovation that enables the authors to establish the desired theoretical guarantees. The decorrelated score statistic for latent variable models is also a novel contribution, as previous high-dimensional inference methods primarily focused on generalized linear models.

3. **Significance:** This work is significant because it provides the first computationally feasible approach for optimal parameter estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional latent variable models. The framework is broadly applicable to a variety of models (Gaussian mixture, mixture of regression), and the results have clear implications for practitioners dealing with high-dimensional data. The paper establishes the first theoretical guarantee of parameter estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional regimes for the EM algorithm.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Clarity:** While the paper is generally well-written, some technical details in the proofs (particularly in the appendix) might be challenging for readers unfamiliar with the EM algorithm in high dimensions. The authors could provide more intuitive explanations of the truncation step and its role in the convergence analysis. The notation for the truncation operation (lines 2.2-2.3) could be clarified.

2. **Empirical Validation:** The paper focuses primarily on theoretical contributions without extensive empirical validation. While the theoretical guarantees are strong, it would be valuable to include some numerical experiments to demonstrate the practical performance of the proposed algorithm and decorrelated score statistic on synthetic or real data.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Novel extension of EM algorithm to high-dimensional settings with rigorous theoretical guarantees
- First work to provide both estimation and inference guarantees for high-dimensional latent variable models
- Well-motivated and technically sound approach with clear distinction from related work
- Broad applicability to a variety of latent variable models

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
- Limited empirical validation beyond theoretical claims
- Some technical details in the appendix might be challenging for some readers
- The truncation parameter (bs) selection strategy could be more thoroughly discussed

Overall, this is a strong theoretical contribution that addresses an important gap in the literature. The paper is well-written, rigorously argued, and makes a significant contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistics. While the lack of empirical validation is a minor weakness, the theoretical advances are substantial enough to warrant acceptance at NIPS.

The paper is highly relevant to NIPS, as it addresses a fundamental statistical problem with a novel methodological contribution that advances the state of the art in high-dimensional inference. The results will be of interest to researchers working on latent variable models, high-dimensional statistics, and optimization.

**Recommendation:** Accept
