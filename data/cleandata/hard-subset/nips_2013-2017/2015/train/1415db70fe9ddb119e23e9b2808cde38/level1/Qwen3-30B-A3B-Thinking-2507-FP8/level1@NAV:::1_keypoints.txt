- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that enforces sparsity via a truncation step.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM convergence theory to high dimensions with rigorous statistical and computational guarantees.  
- Key contribution: algorithm achieves geometric convergence and near-optimal statistical rate sqrt(s* log d / n).  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for asymptotic inference of low-dimensional parameter components.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis decomposing estimation error into optimization and statistical components.  
- Proof techniques are sound and specifically address high-dimensional challenges, including entrywise statistical error.  
- Presentation is clear, with well-organized algorithms and consistent notation.  
- Originality: first framework for optimal estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models via EM.  
- Applicability: framework covers Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions and attains semiparametric efficiency.  
- Weakness: requires restrictive sparsity assumption on w* for the decorrelation approach.  
- Weakness: limited empirical validation beyond theoretical results.  
- Weakness: potential computational concerns for the truncation step at very large d.  
- Overall assessment: substantial, technically strong contribution warranting acceptance.
