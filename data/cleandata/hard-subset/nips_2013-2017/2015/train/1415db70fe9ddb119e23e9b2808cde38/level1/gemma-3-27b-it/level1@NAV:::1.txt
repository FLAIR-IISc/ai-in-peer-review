## Review of "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models, a significant gap in the existing literature. The authors contribute a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and demonstrate both geometric convergence for parameter estimation and asymptotic normality for hypothesis testing of low-dimensional components. The work builds upon recent advances in EM analysis (Balakrishnan et al., 2015; Taku et al., 2018) which established guarantees in the low-dimensional regime, and extends these results to the much more complex high-dimensional setting.

The strengths of the paper are substantial. The proposed algorithm is well-motivated, addressing the challenges of non-convexity and high dimensionality simultaneously. The theoretical analysis appears rigorous, establishing both optimization and statistical rates of convergence. The provided conditions (3.1-3.3) appear reasonable and are well-defined, laying a solid foundation for the derived results. Importantly, the paper goes beyond estimation, providing a framework for valid statistical inference, a crucial element often missing in high-dimensional model settings. The inclusion of applications to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models lends practical relevance to the theoretical results.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses. While the technical details are present, the presentation is dense and can be challenging to follow. The numerous conditions and technical notations require significant effort from the reader.  The discussion on related work could be more comprehensive. For instance, recent advancements in sparse factor analysis and related topics (e.g., using spectral methods, Chen et al. 2019) are not thoroughly discussed. The assumptions regarding the sparsity level and the Fisher Information in Section 4 are restrictive and their implications for real-world applications are not fully explored. More intuitive explanations of these assumptions would be valuable. While the authors provide proofs in the appendix, verifying the correctness of the proofs requires significant expertise in the field.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** Addresses a significant gap in the literature regarding theoretical guarantees for EM in high dimensions.
*   **Technical Soundness:** Rigorous analysis with clearly stated conditions and convergence rates.
*   **Significance:**  Provides a computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions, opening up possibilities for new applications of latent variable models.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Clarity:** Dense presentation and complex notation make the paper difficult to follow.
*   **Assumptions:** Restrictive assumptions require further justification and exploration.



**Overall Assessment:** I believe this paper represents a strong contribution to the field. While the presentation could be improved, the technical novelty and theoretical depth justify acceptance, despite the challenges in fully verifying all aspects of the analysis without significant effort. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and address the limitations of the assumptions.

*   Taku, K., et al. (2018). On asymptotically optimal confidence regions and tests for high-dimensional models.
