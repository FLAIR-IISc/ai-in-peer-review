- Contribution: Proposes a high-dimensional EM with a truncation (hard-thresholding) step, analyzes exact and gradient M-steps, and proves geometric contraction plus near-minimax error sqrt(s* log d / n) (up to log n), with semiparametric-efficient decorrelated inference; verified for Gaussian mixtures and mixture-of-regressions under SNR/sparsity.

- Strength: Clear, unified algorithmic idea (EM + truncation) yielding both computational convergence and sharp high-dimensional statistical rates.

- Strength: Analysis covers both exact and gradient M-steps with explicit contraction factors tied to smoothness/strong concavity.

- Strength: Entrywise (ℓ1) control of sample-to-population M-step deviation enables sharp high-dimensional rates and careful treatment of support evolution under truncation.

- Strength: Provides asymptotically valid inference for low-dimensional components using nuisance-orthogonalization that attains the semiparametric efficiency bound.

- Strength: Theoretical applicability demonstrated for canonical models (Gaussian mixtures, mixtures of regressions) with explicit conditions.

- Strength: Conceptually bridges the computational–statistical gap for nonconvex high-dimensional latent variable models.

- Weakness: Requires initialization within a basin of attraction; no general provable high-dimensional initializer provided beyond model-specific/high-SNR assumptions.

- Weakness: Main rate theorem uses resampling/sample-splitting across iterations to control dependence; extension to full-sample EM is not fully argued.

- Weakness: Practical guidance on tuning (truncation level b_s, Dantzig λ, gradient step size) and sensitivity analysis is lacking.

- Weakness: Sparsity assumption on the orthogonalization vector w* (s_w* = o(√(n/log d))) may be restrictive; paper should discuss when this is natural.

- Weakness: SNR/separation assumptions for mixtures (e.g., large r) may be strong; tradeoffs between SNR and sample size need clarification.

- Weakness: Paper is notation-heavy with many derivations deferred to the appendix; bibliography has incomplete entries and some relevant literature is missing.

- Weakness: No empirical validation — synthetic/real experiments, sensitivity to initialization/b_s, or coverage comparisons are absent.

- Quality assessment: Technical development is solid and well-motivated, with careful decomposition of optimization and statistical errors; reliance on resampling remains a limitation.

- Clarity assessment: Algorithm boxes help but exposition is dense; assumptions, initialization, and tuning need clearer presentation and typo fixes.

- Originality assessment: High — integrating truncation into EM with sharp high-dimensional rates and decorrelated inference for latent models is novel.

- Significance assessment: High — provides a computationally feasible path to optimal estimation and valid inference for high-dimensional latent-variable EM.

- Pros for acceptance: First unified estimation + inference theory for high-dimensional EM; near-optimal rates and semiparametric efficiency; covers exact and gradient EM with canonical model verifications.

- Cons for acceptance: Practical aspects (initialization, tuning, experiments) underdeveloped; theory relies on resampling; some assumptions may be restrictive.

- Suggestion: Provide concrete, implementable initialization strategies with guidance and provable guarantees where possible.

- Suggestion: Add synthetic and real-data experiments comparing to tensor/spectral methods and penalized EM, including inference coverage and sensitivity analyses.

- Suggestion: Discuss automatic selection of b_s and λ (cross-validation, stability, theory-driven heuristics) and sensitivity to these choices.

- Suggestion: Clarify how the analysis extends to the no-resampling/full-sample algorithm and whether rates still hold.

- Suggestion: Broaden related work to include iterative hard thresholding, Javanmard & Montanari debiasing, and penalized EM literature; fix and complete references.

- Suggestion: Expand discussion on when the sparsity of w* holds in common latent models and explicit SNR vs. sample-size tradeoffs.

- Overall verdict: Strong theoretical contribution, with recommendation to accept conditional on improved clarity, practical guidance, and empirical validation; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
