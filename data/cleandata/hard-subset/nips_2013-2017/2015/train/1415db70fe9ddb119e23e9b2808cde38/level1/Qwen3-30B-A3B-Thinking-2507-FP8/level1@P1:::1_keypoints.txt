- Problem: Extends EM to high-dimensional latent variable models with sparsity.  
- Proposal: Introduces a high-dimensional EM algorithm that adds a sparsity-enforcing truncation (T-step).  
- Convergence: Proves geometric convergence to an estimator with near-optimal statistical rate.  
- Inference: Proposes a decorrelated score statistic for testing low-dimensional components with asymptotic normality and optimal variance.  
- Novelty: First theoretical extension of EM to high-dimensional regimes; T-step is a key technical innovation.  
- Comparison: Shows advantage over existing convex-based high-dimensional inference methods and superior rates vs. tensor methods.  
- Theoretical rigor: Comprehensive proofs and clear decomposition of optimization vs. statistical error.  
- Broad applicability: Framework applies to Gaussian mixtures, mixture of regressions, and other latent variable models.  
- Significance: Provides the first computationally feasible approach for both estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Clarity issue: Some technical details and notation (especially the truncation operation) are hard to follow in the appendix.  
- Empirical validation: Lacks extensive experiments demonstrating practical performance on synthetic or real data.  
- Tuning: Selection strategy for the truncation parameter (bs) is not fully discussed.  
- Overall judgment: Strong theoretical contribution; recommended for acceptance.
