- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity and attains near-optimal statistical convergence rates.  
- Provides a comprehensive theoretical framework establishing convergence guarantees and statistical properties of the algorithm.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing and confidence interval construction for low-dimensional components.  
- Shows the decorrelated score test achieves the semiparametric efficient information lower bound (optimal inference).  
- Framework is broadly applicable to a wide family of high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Paper is technically rigorous but highly complex, making proofs and analysis difficult for non-experts.  
- Relies on several modeling and data assumptions that may not hold in practical settings.  
- Computational complexity and scalability to large-scale applications are not thoroughly addressed.  
- Strong case for acceptance based on novelty, theoretical foundations, and broad applicability.  
- Concerns against acceptance center on technical accessibility and the realism of assumptions.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and significant contribution, but reviewers should weigh technical complexity and assumptions.
