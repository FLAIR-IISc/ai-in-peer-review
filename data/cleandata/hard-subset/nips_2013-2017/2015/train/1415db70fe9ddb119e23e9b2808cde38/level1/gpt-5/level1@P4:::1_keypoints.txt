- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm that adds a truncation (hard-thresholding) step after each E/M-step to enforce sparsity.  
- Proves geometric convergence of iterates to an estimator with near-optimal rate O(√(s* log d / n)) (up to log n) under local initialization and regularity conditions.  
- Analyzes both exact M-step and gradient-ascent M-step (stepsize 2/(μ+ν)).  
- Introduces a decorrelated score and Wald test for low-dimensional components using a Dantzig-type estimator of a projection vector w.  
- Shows the test statistic is asymptotically normal and attains the semiparametric efficiency bound.  
- Extends prior EM analyses to the p ≫ n regime via truncation and entrywise statistical error control.  
- Contrasts with GLM debiasing work by targeting nonconvex latent-variable likelihoods and estimators not necessarily global optima.  
- Compares with tensor methods, arguing better rates in some settings.  
- Verifies conditions and demonstrates results for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions under SNR and sparsity assumptions.  
- Technical analysis is carefully stated with explicit conditions: Lipschitz-gradient variants, local strong concavity/smoothness, entrywise concentration, and initialization in a basin.  
- Uses sample-splitting (resampling) in analysis to control dependence across iterates, noting it may be conservative.  
- Truncation analysis via support evolution requires bs ≈ s* and tracks ℓ1 interaction between truncation and estimation errors.  
- Inference proof links Qn gradients to score and Fisher information and requires concentration/Lipschitz conditions on Tn(·) and sparsity of w*.  
- Assumptions are strong and include good initialization, local strong concavity, SNR, sparsity of θ* and w*, known/upper-bounded s*, and sufficiently large n relative to s*, s*w, log d.  
- Constants and extra log factors are not optimized but do not undermine main claims.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear algorithms and conditions, but presentation is dense and relies heavily on appendices.  
- Practical guidance is limited for selecting bs, step sizes, Dantzig λ, stopping criteria, and sample-splitting implications.  
- Initialization strategies (e.g., spectral/tensor warm starts) are not sufficiently discussed for reproducibility.  
- The truncation-EM framework and decorrelated score for EM/Q-function are novel contributions.  
- The work is significant for filling a gap in estimation and inference for high-dimensional latent-variable models, with potential broad impact.  
- Strengths: general framework, near-optimal rates, semiparametric-efficient inference, concrete model applications, and careful entrywise error treatment.  
- Weaknesses: strong initialization and sparsity requirements, need to know s* (or tight bound), conservative sample-splitting, restrictive assumptions (w*-sparsity, high SNR), and limited empirical/tuning guidance.  
- Suggested improvements: provide practical initialization procedures, data-driven tuning and stopping rules, discuss removing/mitigating sample splitting, expand empirical evaluation (misspecification, comparisons), and clarify per-iteration computational complexity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important gap with strong, general, and carefully proved results achieving optimal rates and efficiency.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical applicability limited by strong assumptions and limited tuning/empirical validation; sample-splitting and technical conditions may reduce real-world utility.
