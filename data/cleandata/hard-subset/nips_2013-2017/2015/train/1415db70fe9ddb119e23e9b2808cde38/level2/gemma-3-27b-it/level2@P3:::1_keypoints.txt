- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity for d > n settings.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: statistical rate of convergence and asymptotic normality for parameter estimation and hypothesis testing.  
- Extends prior EM theory (low-dimensional) to high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- General framework demonstrated on Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.  
- Well-motivated problem and significant theoretical contribution.  
- Clear presentation and apparently rigorous mathematical derivations.  
- Novelty: asymptotic normality results enable hypothesis testing in high-dimensional EM.  
- Weak connection to recent high-dimensional techniques (post-selection inference, Lasso debiasing); discussion is cursory.  
- Theoretical conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) are numerous and complex, lacking intuitive explanation.  
- No sensitivity analysis showing robustness to violations of assumptions.  
- Hidden constants in asymptotic notation (C, C0) are not discussed; practical impact unclear.  
- Computational cost of the truncation step is dismissed as “reasonable” without detailed complexity or scalability analysis.  
- No empirical evaluation or simulations to validate theoretical claims or assess practical performance.  
- Strength: fills a gap in understanding EM behavior in high-dimensional regimes.  
- Strength: generality across multiple latent variable models increases impact.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound solution advancing EM theory in high dimensions.  
- Argument against acceptance: lack of empirical validation raises concerns about practical applicability.  
- Argument against acceptance: complex conditions and opaque constants may limit usability of results.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper with significant impact potential but practical and exposition concerns.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions including expanded related work, intuitive explanations of conditions, sensitivity discussion, discussion of hidden constants, empirical simulations, and detailed computational complexity analysis.
