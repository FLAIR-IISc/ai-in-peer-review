- Introduces a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation (T-) step to enforce sparsity, enabling analysis when d >> n.  
- Establishes geometric (optimization) convergence and near-optimal statistical error √(s* log d / n) for parameter estimation (Theorem 3.4).  
- Proposes a decorrelated score statistic for low-dimensional inference that attains asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency (Theorem 4.6).  
- Fills an important gap: previous EM guarantees were limited to low-dimensional settings (d << n); this work extends theory to high-dimensional latent-variable models.  
- The truncation step is a novel technical device that allows tracking sparsity along the solution path.  
- The framework is general and validated for multiple models, including Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions.  
- Strong theoretical contributions with well-structured theorems that decompose optimization and statistical errors.  
- Rigorous technical proofs are provided in the appendix, with careful verification of conditions for specific models.  
- Conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) are well-motivated but technically heavy, which may hinder immediate implementation by practitioners.  
- Presentation is clear and organized, stating main results upfront before technical development.  
- Significant impact: provides unified estimation and inference tools for high-dimensional latent-variable problems relevant across applications.  
- Some minor weaknesses: needs more intuition on why truncation is necessary beyond a technical explanation.  
- Practical guidance is lacking on choosing the sparsity parameter bs relative to true sparsity s*.  
- Could benefit from a brief discussion of computational complexity compared to alternative high-dimensional methods.  
- Meets NIPS evaluation criteria: quality, clarity, originality, and significance are all satisfied.
