- Presents a novel theoretical framework for parameter estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Proposes a High-Dimensional EM algorithm that adds a truncation step after each M-step to enforce sparsity and proves geometric convergence to a near-minimax optimal estimator.
- Develops a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional components and proves asymptotic normality achieving the optimal semiparametric information bound.
- Framework is general and illustrated on high-dimensional Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regression.
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM theory (e.g., Balakrishnan et al. 2014) to the challenging d >> n regime via the truncation modification.
- Significance: provides the first comprehensive, computationally feasible framework for optimal estimation and valid inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Originality: introduces an intuitive iterative truncation for EM and a novel inferential procedure for outputs of non-convex high-dimensional algorithms.
- Technical quality: rigorous, unified analysis with clear decomposition of optimization vs. statistical error; main theorems are strong and formally verified for examples.
- Weakness — Initialization: theoretical guarantees require a good initial estimator within a certain radius; paper should discuss practical initialization methods (e.g., spectral heuristics).
- Weakness — Lack of empirical validation: entirely theoretical; small simulation studies would help verify convergence rates, error scaling, and confidence interval coverage.
- Weakness — Clarity on assumptions: several complex technical conditions need more intuitive explanation for practitioners (e.g., sparsity scaling like s_w* = o(sqrt(n/log d))).
- Weakness — Tuning parameters: method depends on tuning (sparsity level bs, Dantzig selector lambda); include guidance on practical selection (cross-validation or data-driven methods).
- Overall assessment: excellent, impactful work that substantially advances theory; strongly recommended for acceptance.
