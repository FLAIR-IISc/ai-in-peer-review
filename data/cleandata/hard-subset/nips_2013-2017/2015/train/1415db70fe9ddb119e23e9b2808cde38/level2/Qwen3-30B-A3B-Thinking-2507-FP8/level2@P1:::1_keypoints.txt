- The paper extends EM to high-dimensional latent-variable models via a truncation step, decomposing estimation error into geometric optimization and near-optimal statistical terms.  
- It introduces a decorrelated score statistic that enables asymptotic inference in high dimensions and is proved to attain the semiparametric efficiency bound.  
- The framework is validated on Gaussian mixtures and mixture-of-regression models with all technical conditions checked and concrete statistical rates derived.  
- The manuscript is well-structured, clearly written, situates related work effectively, and uses consistent, well-motivated notation and lemmas.  
- The paper omits discussion of the computational overhead of repeated truncation (support selection) and how its cost scales with dimensionality.  
- The theoretical analysis assumes knowledge of true sparsity s* (b_s = Θ(s*)); the paper should address data-driven selection of b_s and its effect on guarantees.  
- Empirical validation is limited — add simulations comparing to tensor or gradient-EM methods and evaluate finite-sample performance of the decorrelated score test.  
- Novelty/significance: first simultaneous computational and statistical guarantees for EM in d ≫ n regimes, with a broadly applicable inference framework for latent models.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — technically solid, clear, and impactful; noted weaknesses are minor and revisable.  
- Minor fix: Clarify whether b_s must equal Θ(s*) or if modest overestimation (e.g., O(s* log d)) suffices.  
- Minor fix: Reword abstract to state the decorrelated score test is the first for high-dimensional latent-variable models.  
- Minor fix: Discuss how truncation affects the basin of attraction and initialization conditions.
