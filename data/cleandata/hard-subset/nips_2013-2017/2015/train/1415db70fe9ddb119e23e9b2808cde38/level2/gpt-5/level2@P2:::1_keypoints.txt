- Paper proposes a truncated EM algorithm (E/M-step + hard-thresholding T-step) for high-dimensional latent variable models enforcing sparsity and proves geometric convergence with near-minimax ℓ2 error rates (√(s* log d / n) up to log n).
- Introduces a decorrelated score test via a Dantzig-selector orthogonalization and proves asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency for low-dimensional components amid high-dimensional nuisance.
- Instantiates the framework for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions, verifying technical conditions and deriving claimed rates.
- Extends Balakrishnan et al.’s population-to-sample EM theory to high dimensions by adding truncation and entrywise error control; adapts debiasing/desparsification ideas to latent-variable likelihoods.
- Provides sharper (near-optimal) rates under sparsity compared to tensor methods and handles inference.
- Clear unified analysis giving simultaneous computational (geometric contraction) and statistical guarantees with near-optimal rates.
- Handles both exact and gradient M-steps with explicit contraction constants and separation of optimization vs. statistical error.
- Strong inference contribution: decorrelated score/Wald tests with provable asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency under high-dimensional nuisance.
- Verifies conditions and explicit rates for two canonical latent models, using technically nontrivial entrywise (ℓ∞/ℓ1) control for truncation.
- Main theory analyzes a resampling EM (fresh data per iteration), not the standard single-sample EM; transfer to practical EM is not fully developed.
- Requires good initialization within a basin of attraction; practical provable initializers and empirical sensitivity to initialization are not provided.
- Sparsity tuning parameter bs must match s* order; guidance for selecting bs when s* is unknown is missing and hard-thresholding may be brittle.
- Inference requires sparsity of w* and solving a Dantzig program involving conditional expectations/second derivatives; computational feasibility and stability are not demonstrated.
- No empirical results are provided to assess finite-sample behavior, tuning robustness, or CI coverage.
- Technical development is careful with correct use of EM identities; reliance on entrywise control uniform over s-sparse inputs may be hard to verify beyond showcased models.
- Paper is well organized and readable though some notation/constants and tuning guidance should be streamlined.
- References list and some symbols/formatting need cleaning.
- Novel synthesis: truncated high-dimensional EM with rigorous computational-statistical guarantees and a new EM-based decorrelated score for latent models.
- Addresses an important gap in statistically optimal estimation and valid inference for high-dimensional latent variable models; likely influential.
- Suggested improvements: add empirical experiments, discuss practical initializers and bs selection, provide Dantzig tuning guidance and computational details, and extend/clarify non-resampled EM theory.
- Minor fixes: correct typographical/reference errors and define norms/constants consistently.
- Pros for acceptance: strong novel theoretical contributions, broad impact, careful general analysis.
- Cons for acceptance: lack of empirical validation, resampling vs. practical EM gap, sensitivity to initialization and tuning.
- Overall recommendation: leaning accept for strong theory, but needs experiments and practical clarifications to be a clear accept.
