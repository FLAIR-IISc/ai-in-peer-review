- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity via a truncation step to enforce sparse parameter estimates.  
- Introduces a truncation-based approach that yields improved statistical rates of convergence in latent variable models.  
- Develops a decorrelated score test for inference on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Main contribution 1: EM algorithm with geometric convergence and (near-)optimal statistical rate of the estimator.  
- Main contribution 2: New inferential procedure for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional parameter components.  
- Provides thorough analysis of computational and statistical properties, including convergence rate, statistical error, and asymptotic normality.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regression models.  
- Strength: Novel, well-motivated approach to high-dimensional EM for latent variable models.  
- Strength: Rigorous, thorough theoretical analysis supporting the method.  
- Strength: Empirical demonstrations on relevant real-world model examples.  
- Weakness: Assumes a specific sparsity structure on the true parameter, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Lacks comprehensive empirical or theoretical comparison with existing methods.  
- Weakness: Some technical conditions and assumptions may be difficult to verify in practice.
