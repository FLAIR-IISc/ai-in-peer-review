## Review of "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper addresses a critical gap in the theoretical understanding of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm – its behavior in high-dimensional settings with latent variable models. The authors propose a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and provide theoretical guarantees for both parameter estimation and hypothesis testing. This is a significant contribution, as existing theory largely focuses on low-dimensional scenarios.

The core idea of truncating the E and M-steps to promote sparsity is well-motivated and appears to be a key enabler for achieving statistical rates in high dimensions. The authors demonstrate convergence at a geometric rate and establish an estimator achieving a near-optimal statistical rate of convergence (p s* log d/n). Furthermore, the development of a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing, with established asymptotic normality, is a valuable addition. The paper builds upon recent work by [2] extending their low-dimensional results to the high-dimensional regime, and clearly articulates the differences and improvements. 

The paper is technically sound, with a clear presentation of the algorithm and a rigorous mathematical analysis. The authors provide detailed conditions (3.1-3.3, 4.1-4.5) and carefully outline the assumptions required for their theorems to hold. The inclusion of implications for Gaussian Mixture Models helps ground the theory in a concrete example. 

However, the paper's density and technicality could be a barrier to broader understanding. While the notation is generally standard, it is extensive and requires careful tracking. The connection between the theoretical conditions and practical implementation choices (e.g., selecting the truncation parameter) could be made more explicit.  A more intuitive explanation of why the truncation step leads to improved statistical performance would also be beneficial.  Furthermore, while the authors acknowledge limitations, a discussion of potential challenges in applying this algorithm to more complex latent variable models would strengthen the paper.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant open problem in the theoretical analysis of the EM algorithm.
*   Presents a novel and well-motivated algorithm with strong theoretical guarantees.
*   Provides a valuable contribution to statistical inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.
*   Builds upon and extends existing literature in a clear and logical manner.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   High technical density may limit accessibility.
*   Practical guidance on parameter tuning and model selection could be improved.
*   Discussion of limitations and potential challenges could be more thorough.

**Overall:**

Despite the density, this is a strong paper with significant theoretical contributions. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and provide more practical guidance. The authors have presented a valuable advancement in our understanding of the EM algorithm in high-dimensional settings, and this work is likely to be influential in the field.
