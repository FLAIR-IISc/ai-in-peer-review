

This paper proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, establishing both statistical estimation guarantees and inferential procedures for high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors build upon [2] which established low-dimensional EM convergence rates, extending it to high dimensions by incorporating sparsity through a truncation step in the E-step and M-step. The key contributions are a high-dimensional EM algorithm with geometric convergence to an estimator achieving near-optimal statistical rate of convergence (p(s*·log d/n)), and a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional parameter components.

Strengths: The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by addressing a critical gap in the literature for high-dimensional latent variable models. The truncation step is a novel and clever approach to incorporate sparsity into the EM algorithm, enabling the authors to establish rigorous statistical and computational guarantees. The framework is general and applied to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models, with detailed theoretical analysis. The decorrelated score statistic for inference is particularly valuable as it addresses a gap in the literature for high-dimensional latent variable models, where standard inference methods fail due to non-convexity and high-dimensional nuisance parameters. The paper thoroughly contextualizes its work against related literature, including [2] for low-dimensional EM, [23] for high-dimensional mixture of regression, and the broader literature on debiasing methods for high-dimensional inference.

Weaknesses: The paper claims to establish "the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions" but does not provide a minimax lower bound to confirm the "near-minimax-optimal" rate claim in the abstract and Theorem 3.4. This weakens the significance of the rate improvement. The assumptions (Conditions 3.1-3.3, 4.1-4.5) are quite strong, particularly Condition 3.3 requiring an L1-norm statistical error bound, which may be difficult to verify in practice. The connection between the high-dimensional EM algorithm and the decorrelated score statistic could be more clearly explained, as the latter appears somewhat disconnected from the former in the presentation. The comparison with [23] is somewhat superficial; while the paper notes [23] provides support recovery but not parameter estimation guarantees, it doesn't fully clarify how the authors' framework differs in terms of both the algorithm and the theoretical guarantees.

The paper's claims about novelty are generally well-supported, but the assertion that their approach is "the first" for high-dimensional inference in latent variable models could be strengthened with a more thorough literature review. While the paper cites [23], it doesn't discuss whether other works have addressed inference in high-dimensional latent variable models using different approaches.

Constructive suggestions: The authors should provide a minimax lower bound to confirm the optimality claim. They should also discuss practical considerations for choosing the sparsity parameter bs in the truncation step, as this is crucial for implementation. The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with [23] to clarify the distinctions in both algorithmic approach and theoretical guarantees. A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the truncation step and how it scales with dimension d would also be valuable for practitioners.

Overall, this is a strong theoretical contribution that addresses a meaningful gap in the literature. The paper is technically sound, well-organized, and makes a significant step toward making the EM algorithm applicable to high-dimensional settings with rigorous statistical guarantees. While some claims about novelty and optimality could be strengthened with additional evidence, the paper's core contributions are valuable and well-justified. The framework is general enough to be applicable to a broad class of latent variable models, which enhances its significance. The theoretical results are well-motivated and presented with sufficient detail for experts to follow the proofs.
