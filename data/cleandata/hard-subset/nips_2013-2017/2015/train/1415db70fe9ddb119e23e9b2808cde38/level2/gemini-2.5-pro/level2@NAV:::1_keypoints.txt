- Presents a general theoretical framework for EM in high-dimensional settings (d >> n) with guarantees for parameter estimation and statistical inference.
- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, proving geometric convergence to a (near-)minimax optimal estimator given proper initialization.
- Develops a decorrelated score test based on the estimator and establishes its asymptotic normality for testing low-dimensional parameter components.
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM analyses (e.g., Balakrishnan et al. 2017) to the high-dimensional regime.
- Originality/significance: first unified framework for both optimal point estimation and valid asymptotic inference for EM in high dimensions.
- Quality: rigorous, technically impressive analysis with clear assumptions and application to models like Gaussian mixtures.
- Weakness: no experiments or simulations to validate empirical convergence, finite-sample performance of the score test, or initialization sensitivity.
- Weakness: theoretical conditions are abstract; main text lacks intuition on when assumptions (e.g., Lipschitz-Gradient, Concavity-Smoothness) hold.
- Weakness: reliance on good initialization is a practical challenge that is not fully addressed.
- For acceptance: novel, significant theoretical foundation and high-quality technical analysis offering a major advance.
- Against acceptance: absence of experimental results prevents assessment of practical implications and robustness.
- Summary: strong theoretical paper meriting acceptance, but should include a simulation study in the final version.
