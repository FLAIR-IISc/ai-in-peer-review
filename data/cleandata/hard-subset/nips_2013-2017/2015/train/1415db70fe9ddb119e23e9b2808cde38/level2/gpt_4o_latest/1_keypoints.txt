- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, offering theoretical guarantees for parameter estimation and hypothesis testing in latent variable models.  
- Main contributions: (i) high-dimensional EM with truncation achieving (near-)optimal statistical convergence; (ii) a decorrelated score statistic that is asymptotically normal and semiparametrically efficient for low-dimensional components.  
- Framework applied to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models, demonstrating generality.  
- Strength — Novelty: fills a gap by extending EM to high-dimensional settings and introducing truncation for sparsity.  
- Strength — Theory: provides geometric convergence rates, near-optimal statistical rates, and an asymptotically normal, optimal score statistic.  
- Strength — Generality: applicable to a broad family of latent variable models.  
- Strength — Comparison: thorough comparison to tensor methods and low-dimensional EM; well-cited related work.  
- Strength — Clarity: clear stepwise description of E-step, M-step, and truncation with exact and approximate implementations.  
- Weakness — Empirical validation limited to Gaussian mixture and regression experiments; lacks real-world applications.  
- Weakness — Initialization: performance depends on suitable initialization; lacks practical guidance or robust initialization methods.  
- Weakness — Presentation: dense mathematical exposition may be hard for readers unfamiliar with high-dimensional statistics; needs simpler explanations or examples.  
- Weakness — Scalability: computational scalability for very high-dimensional datasets not thoroughly discussed or demonstrated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance extending EM to high-dimensional settings with rigorous guarantees and broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation and practical implementation details, plus reliance on good initialization may hinder real-world adoption.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on addressing practical limitations — add empirical results and practical guidance to improve impact and accessibility.
