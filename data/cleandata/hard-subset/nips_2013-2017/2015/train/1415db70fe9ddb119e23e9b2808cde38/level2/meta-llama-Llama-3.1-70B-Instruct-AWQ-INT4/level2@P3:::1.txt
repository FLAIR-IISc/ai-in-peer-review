**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm for inferring high-dimensional latent variable models. The algorithm incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation and establishes a general inferential theory for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment. The authors provide a theoretical guarantee for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional regimes, which is the first of its kind. They also propose a decorrelated score test for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel Algorithm**: The paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation. This algorithm is the first to establish a theoretical guarantee for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional regimes.
2. **Theoretical Guarantee**: The authors provide a theoretical guarantee for the EM algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field of statistics and machine learning.
3. **Decorrelated Score Test**: The paper proposes a decorrelated score test for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters. This test is a valuable contribution to the field of statistical inference.
4. **Broad Applicability**: The algorithm and theoretical guarantee are applicable to a broad family of statistical models with latent structures.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Technical Complexity**: The paper is highly technical, and the proofs and derivations are complex and difficult to follow.
2. **Limited Simulation Studies**: The paper does not provide extensive simulation studies to demonstrate the performance of the algorithm and decorrelated score test.
3. **Assumptions**: The paper relies on several assumptions, including the sparsity assumption on the true parameter and the assumption on the Fisher information matrix. These assumptions may not hold in all cases.

**Originality and Significance**

1. **First Theoretical Guarantee**: The paper provides the first theoretical guarantee for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional regimes, which is a significant contribution to the field of statistics and machine learning.
2. **Novel Algorithm**: The proposed high-dimensional EM algorithm is novel and incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation.
3. **Decorrelated Score Test**: The decorrelated score test is a valuable contribution to the field of statistical inference and provides a new approach for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.

**Clarity and Organization**

1. **Clear Introduction**: The paper provides a clear introduction to the problem and the proposed solution.
2. **Well-Organized**: The paper is well-organized, and the sections are clearly labeled and easy to follow.
3. **Technical Proofs**: The technical proofs are provided in the appendix, which makes the paper easier to read and understand.

**Recommendations for Improvement**

1. **Simplify Technical Proofs**: The technical proofs are complex and difficult to follow. Simplifying these proofs would make the paper more accessible to a broader audience.
2. **Provide Simulation Studies**: Providing extensive simulation studies would demonstrate the performance of the algorithm and decorrelated score test and make the paper more convincing.
3. **Relax Assumptions**: Relaxing the assumptions on the sparsity of the true parameter and the Fisher information matrix would make the paper more applicable to a broader range of problems.
