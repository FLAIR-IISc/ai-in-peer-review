- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity for parameter estimation and hypothesis testing in latent variable models.  
- Establishes geometric convergence rates and asymptotic normality, claiming near-optimal statistical rates and inferential validity.  
- Builds on prior EM work for low-dimensional settings and fills a gap for high-dimensional latent variable inference.  
- Connects to high-dimensional estimation literature (e.g., Lasso, tensor methods) and highlights novelty in providing inference tools.  
- Novelty: introduces a computationally feasible truncation-based EM addressing high-dimensional latent variable estimation and inference.  
- Theoretical rigor: provides strong theoretical guarantees under stated conditions (convergence, rates, asymptotic normality).  
- Generality: framework applies to multiple latent variable models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, mixture of regressions).  
- Inference: develops a decorrelated score statistic enabling hypothesis testing in high dimensions.  
- Presentation: paper is well-organized and clearly describes methods and theory.  
- Condition complexity: theoretical assumptions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) are numerous and may be hard to verify generally.  
- Sensitivity to initialization: algorithm requires a good initializer but offers little practical guidance on obtaining one.  
- Unspecified constants: rates include unspecified constants (C, C0) whose dependence on d, n, s is unclear.  
- Computational cost: truncation step may add substantial overhead in very high-dimensional settings; complexity discussion is limited.  
- Limited empirical evaluation: lacks simulations or real-data experiments to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, offers rigorous theory, and provides novel estimation/inference tools.  
- Arguments for rejection: complex conditions, no empirical validation, sensitivity to initialization, and potential computational burden.  
- Overall assessment: a strong theoretical contribution with significant potential, tempered by practical limitations and heavy assumptions.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing initialization guidance, clarifying constant dependencies, discussing computational complexity, adding empirical validation, and expanding discussion of condition verifiability.
