- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that integrates sparsity and gives simultaneous estimation and uncertainty assessment for latent variable models.  
- Introduces the first theoretical guarantee for EM in high-dimensional regimes.  
- Proposes a decorrelated score test for inference on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Method and theory are applicable to a broad family of latent-structure statistical models.  
- Paper is highly technical; proofs and derivations are complex and hard to follow.  
- Limited simulation studies are provided to empirically demonstrate algorithm and test performance.  
- Relies on strong assumptions (sparsity of true parameter and Fisher information conditions) that may not always hold.  
- Provides the first theoretical guarantee for EM in high-dimensional settings, a significant contribution.  
- The proposed algorithm is original in incorporating sparsity into high-dimensional EM.  
- The decorrelated score test is a valuable new tool for statistical inference in this setting.  
- Introduction is clear and sets up the problem and solution well.  
- Paper is well-organized with clearly labeled sections.  
- Technical proofs are deferred to the appendix, aiding readability of the main text.  
- Recommend simplifying technical proofs to improve accessibility.  
- Recommend adding extensive simulation studies to better demonstrate empirical performance.  
- Recommend relaxing sparsity and Fisher information assumptions to broaden applicability.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
