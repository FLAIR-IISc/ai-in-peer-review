- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity via a truncation step to address lack of theoretical guarantees for EM in high dimensions.  
- Establishes two main contributions: (i) a high-dimensional EM algorithm with geometric convergence to a near-optimal estimator.  
- Establishes (ii) a decorrelated score statistic for asymptotic inference on low-dimensional components that attains semiparametric efficiency.  
- Theoretical results are strong and clear, with key guarantees stated in Theorems 3.4 and 4.6.  
- Properly contextualizes the work against prior low-dimensional EM analyses, explaining how truncation overcomes high-dimensional challenges.  
- Presents a general framework and verifies conditions for Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models in the appendix.  
- Main strength is the unified framework delivering both optimal estimation and valid inference, valuable for practitioners.  
- Claims to be the first computationally feasible approach for high-dimensional latent variable models with these guarantees.  
- Theoretical arguments and conditions are sound, well-motivated, and adequately referenced.  
- Minor weakness: lacks empirical validation or discussion of practical/computational implementation of the truncation step.  
- Well-written, logically structured paper that addresses a meaningful gap and is recommended for acceptance with a minor suggestion to include empirical results or computational discussion.
