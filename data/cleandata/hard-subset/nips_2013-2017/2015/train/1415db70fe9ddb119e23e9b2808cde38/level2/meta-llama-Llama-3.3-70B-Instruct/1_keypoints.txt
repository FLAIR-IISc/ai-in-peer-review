- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity, converges geometrically, and attains near-optimal statistical rates.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing and confidence intervals on low-dimensional components.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition of algorithm and theoretical guarantees.  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis including convergence rates and statistical guarantees.  
- Applies framework to a broad class of models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, mixture of regressions).  
- Assumes the sparsity parameter is known, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Lacks extensive numerical experiments to validate the proposed method empirically.  
- Could provide a more detailed discussion of limitations and potential extensions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/significant contribution, strong theory, broad applicability.  
- Arguments for rejection: unrealistic assumption about known sparsity, insufficient empirical validation, limited discussion of limitations/extensions.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address weaknesses.  
- Requests to authors: add additional numerical experiments and expand discussion of limitations and extensions.
