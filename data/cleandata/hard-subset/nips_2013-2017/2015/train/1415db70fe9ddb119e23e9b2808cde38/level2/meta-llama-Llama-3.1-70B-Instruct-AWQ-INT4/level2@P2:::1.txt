This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm for inferring latent variable models, which incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation. The authors also develop an inferential procedure for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters. The paper provides a unified framework for both parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment in high-dimensional regimes.

The authors' contributions are two-fold. Firstly, they propose a high-dimensional EM algorithm that converges at a geometric rate and achieves a (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence. Secondly, they develop a decorrelated score test for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters. The authors also establish the asymptotic normality of the decorrelated score statistic.
