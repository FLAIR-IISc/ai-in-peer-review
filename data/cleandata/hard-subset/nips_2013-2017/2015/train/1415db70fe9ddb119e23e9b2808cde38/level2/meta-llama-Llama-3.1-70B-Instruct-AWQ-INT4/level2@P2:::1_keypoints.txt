- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity into parameter estimation.  
- Algorithm converges at a geometric rate and achieves a (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence.  
- Develops a decorrelated score test for hypotheses on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Establishes asymptotic normality of the decorrelated score statistic.  
- Provides a unified framework for parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment in high-dimensional regimes.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and methods/theory are well-explained.  
- Theoretical results are clearly presented with proofs in the appendix.  
- Assumes the true parameter is sparse, which may not hold in all practical settings.  
- Algorithm requires a suitable initialization that may be difficult to obtain in practice.  
- Lacks numerical experiments to demonstrate empirical effectiveness of the proposed method.  
- Original contribution: EM algorithm that explicitly accounts for sparsity, distinct from existing EM variants.  
- Significant potential applicability (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, mixture of regressions) and important inference result.  
- Quality: 9/10; Clarity: 9/10; Originality: 8/10; Significance: 9/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept; suggest adding numerical experiments and considering relaxation of the sparsity assumption.
