- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, enabling parameter estimation and hypothesis testing when p > n.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: geometric computational convergence, near-optimal statistical rates, and asymptotic normality of estimators.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for valid hypothesis testing in high dimensions.  
- Extends prior EM convergence analyses to the high-dimensional setting and applies to models like Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions.  
- Novelty lies in the truncation idea that achieves sparsity and statistical-rate optimality in EM.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with error decomposition into optimization and statistical components.  
- Framework is broadly applicable to a family of latent variable models.  
- Addresses an important and challenging open problem in statistical learning.  
- Technical presentation is dense and requires strong background; many conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) are hard to parse.  
- Several assumptions are strong (e.g., Assumption 4.5 on Fisher information, scaling requirements, small s*_w) and may limit applicability.  
- Statistical rates include unspecified constants (C, C0); dependence on problem parameters is not fully clarified.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; more experiments would strengthen practical claims and test accuracy of hypothesis procedures.  
- Notation is heavy and sometimes inconsistent, which impedes readability.  
- Condition 3.3’s use of the l1 norm needs more discussion on practical/computational implications.  
- Relationship and comparison to tensor methods should be expanded and made more detailed.  
- Minor presentation issues: unusual notation in Eq. (1.1) and brief discussion of algorithmic limitations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fundamental problem addressed, significant and technically sound contributions, generality and inference procedures.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong assumptions, technical density, limited empirical validation, and accessibility concerns.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, discuss assumptions/limitations, and add illustrative empirical results.
