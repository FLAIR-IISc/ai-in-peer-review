- Contribution: Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and provides statistical estimation guarantees and inferential procedures for latent variable models.  
- Contribution: Shows geometric convergence to an estimator attaining near-optimal statistical rate (p(s*·log d/n)).  
- Contribution: Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing of low-dimensional parameter components.  
- Strength: Fills a theoretical gap for high-dimensional latent variable models with a novel, clever truncation step enabling rigorous statistical and computational guarantees.  
- Strength: Framework is general and instantiated for Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions with detailed theoretical analysis.  
- Strength: Thorough contextualization with related literature and useful connection to debiasing methods for high-dimensional inference.  
- Weakness: No minimax lower bound is provided to substantiate the “near-minimax-optimal” rate claim, weakening the optimality assertion.  
- Weakness: Assumptions (Conditions 3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) are strong and may be hard to verify in practice, notably Condition 3.3 requiring an L1-norm error bound.  
- Weakness: The link between the high-dimensional EM algorithm and the decorrelated score statistic is not clearly explained and feels somewhat disconnected.  
- Weakness: Comparison with [23] is superficial; distinctions in algorithmic approach and theoretical guarantees are not fully clarified.  
- Weakness: Claiming to be “the first” for high-dimensional inference in latent variable models needs a more comprehensive literature review.  
- Suggestion: Provide a minimax lower bound to confirm optimality claims.  
- Suggestion: Discuss practical selection of the sparsity parameter bs in the truncation step and implementation guidance.  
- Suggestion: Clarify the connection between the EM algorithm and the proposed inference procedure, and give a more detailed comparison with [23].  
- Suggestion: Include discussion of computational complexity and how the truncation step scales with dimension d.  
- Overall: Strong, technically sound theoretical contribution with broad applicability, but novelty and optimality claims would be strengthened by additional evidence and clearer exposition.
