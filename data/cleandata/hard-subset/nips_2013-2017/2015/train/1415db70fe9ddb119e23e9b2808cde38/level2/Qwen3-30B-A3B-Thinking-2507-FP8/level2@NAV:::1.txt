

This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating sparsity structure through a truncation step, addressing a significant gap in theoretical guarantees for EM in high dimensions. The authors establish two main contributions: (i) a high-dimensional EM algorithm with geometric convergence to a near-optimal estimator, and (ii) a decorrelated score statistic for asymptotic inference on low-dimensional components, achieving semiparametric efficiency.

The paper is technically strong, with clear theoretical results (Theorems 3.4 and 4.6) establishing both computational and statistical guarantees. The authors properly contextualize their work against prior research, explaining how their truncation step overcomes high-dimensional challenges that existing low-dimensional analyses [2, 30] cannot address. The framework is general and applied to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models, with conditions verified in the appendix.

The main strength is the unified framework providing both optimal estimation and valid inference, which is significant for practitioners. The paper makes a clear contribution by establishing the first computationally feasible approach for high-dimensional latent variable models. The theoretical arguments are sound, and the conditions are well-motivated, with proper references to relevant literature.

A minor weakness is the lack of empirical validation. While the theoretical results are strong, some readers might appreciate seeing how the algorithm performs in practice. The authors could add a short empirical section or discuss potential computational challenges in implementing the truncation step.

The paper is well-written, logically structured, and addresses a meaningful gap in the literature. It provides a significant contribution to statistical inference for high-dimensional latent variable models, with results that could be widely applicable.

Overall, this paper makes a strong contribution with well-supported theoretical results. The proposed framework is novel, technically sound, and addresses an important problem. I recommend acceptance with a minor suggestion to include empirical validation or computational discussion.
