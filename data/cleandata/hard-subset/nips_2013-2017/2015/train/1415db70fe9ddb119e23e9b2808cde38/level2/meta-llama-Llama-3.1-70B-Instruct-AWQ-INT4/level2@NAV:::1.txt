This paper presents a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation for latent variable models. The authors propose a truncation step to enforce sparsity, which allows for improved statistical rates of convergence. They also develop a decorrelated score test for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.

The paper makes two main contributions: (1) a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that converges at a geometric rate and achieves an estimator with the (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence, and (2) a new inferential procedure for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's computational and statistical properties, including its convergence rate, statistical error, and asymptotic normality. They also demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through applications to Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regression models.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to high-dimensional EM algorithm for latent variable models.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithm's computational and statistical properties.
