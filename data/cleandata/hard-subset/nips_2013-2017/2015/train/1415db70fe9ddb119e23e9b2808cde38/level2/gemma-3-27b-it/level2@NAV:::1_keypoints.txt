- Tackles theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models, extending analysis to the d > n regime.  
- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and enable theoretical guarantees.  
- Establishes statistical convergence and asymptotic normality for low-dimensional parameter components.  
- Shows near-optimal statistical rate p(s* log d)/n under specified conditions.  
- Derives a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing that attains asymptotic normality and the semiparametric information bound.  
- Applies the framework to Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models to demonstrate applicability.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with clearly stated conditions, detailed proof outline, and solid connections to prior EM work.  
- Major strength: provides a unified framework for both estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent-variable models.  
- Limitation: assumptions are numerous and potentially restrictive.  
- Limitation: empirical evaluation is limited to two examples and could be expanded.  
- Limitation: notation is dense and can hinder readability.  
- Limitation: practical implications and tuning of the truncation parameter (bs) are not fully explored.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify notation, better discuss parameter tuning, and expand empirical validation.
