- Proposes a High-Dimensional EM algorithm that adds a hard-thresholding step after the M-step to enforce sparsity.  
- Proves geometric convergence from a good initialization to an estimator achieving near-minimax error rate sqrt(s*log d / n).  
- Develops a decorrelated score statistic for asymptotic inference on low-dimensional components and proves asymptotic normality with semiparametric-optimal variance.  
- Framework is general and demonstrated on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models.  
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM analyses to the high-dimensional regime via a novel truncation step requiring refined entrywise (l_infinity) error control.  
- Adapts high-dimensional inference techniques (e.g., debiased Lasso ideas) to non-convex latent variable models and estimators produced by iterative algorithms.  
- Significance: provides the first rigorous general framework for statistically optimal estimation and valid inference for EM in high dimensions.  
- Originality and technical depth: introduces and analyzes sparsity-inducing truncation within iterative EM and controls support/error propagation — a substantial technical contribution.  
- Generality: results are framed under abstract conditions on the population Q-function and M-step error, making them potentially widely applicable.  
- Weakness — initialization: theory requires a good initial estimator but the paper lacks discussion or methods for obtaining it (e.g., spectral/tensor approaches).  
- Weakness — clarity of assumptions: high-level technical conditions are abstract and need more intuitive explanation for when they hold in specific models.  
- Weakness — resampling-based analysis: proofs use a resampling variant (fresh data per iteration) that differs from standard practice; the paper should discuss this gap.  
- Weakness — tuning parameters: practical guidance for choosing sparsity level and Dantzig lambda is lacking despite theoretical scaling.  
- Overall assessment: technically deep, significant, well-written work that substantially advances theory for EM in high dimensions and is a strong acceptance candidate.
