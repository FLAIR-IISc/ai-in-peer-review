## Review of "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models, a significant gap in the existing literature. The authors propose a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity and establish both statistical rate of convergence and asymptotic normality for low-dimensional parameter components. The work builds upon recent advances in EM analysis (e.g., [2, 30]) by extending them to the d > n regime, a critical extension for modern applications.

The key contribution lies in the carefully designed truncation step, which facilitates both statistical and computational guarantees. The authors demonstrate, under specific conditions, that the algorithm achieves a near-optimal statistical rate of p(s* log d)/n, where s* is the sparsity level.  Furthermore, they derive a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing, demonstrating asymptotic normality and achieving the semiparametric information bound. The paper applies its framework to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models, illustrating its practical applicability. 

The paper is technically sound and well-motivated. The theoretical analysis appears rigorous, with clearly stated conditions and a detailed proof outline. The connection to existing literature, particularly the work of [2] and the extensions thereof, is well-established.  A major strength is the effort to provide a complete framework encompassing both estimation *and* inference, addressing a crucial need in high-dimensional modeling. 

However, the paper suffers from some limitations. The assumptions, while standard, are numerous and potentially restrictive.  While the authors mention applying the framework to other models, a more comprehensive empirical evaluation beyond the two examples would strengthen the findings. The notation is dense and, at times, makes the paper challenging to follow. Furthermore, while the theoretical results are strong, the practical implications of the truncation parameter (bs) and its tuning are not fully explored.  A discussion on efficient methods for selecting this parameter would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a critical open problem in high-dimensional latent variable modeling.
*   Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees for both estimation and inference.
*   Novel algorithmic approach with a well-motivated truncation step.
*   Clear connections to and extensions of existing literature.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Numerous and potentially restrictive assumptions.
*   Limited empirical evaluation.
*   Dense notation and challenging readability.
*   Lack of discussion on practical parameter tuning.

**Overall:**

Despite some limitations, this is a valuable contribution to the field. The theoretical advancements are significant, and the proposed algorithm offers a promising approach for tackling high-dimensional latent variable models. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**, particularly focusing on clarifying the notation, providing a more detailed discussion of parameter tuning, and potentially expanding the empirical validation.  A revision addressing these points would substantially strengthen the paper and increase its impact.
