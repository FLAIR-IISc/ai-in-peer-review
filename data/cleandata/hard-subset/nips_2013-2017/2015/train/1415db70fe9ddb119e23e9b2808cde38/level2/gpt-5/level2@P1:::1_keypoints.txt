- Paper develops a high-dimensional EM with a truncation (T) step enforcing sparsity, proving geometric convergence to a sparse estimator with near-minimax error ~√s*·√(log d / n) (up to log n factors).

- Analysis covers both exact and gradient-ascent M-steps, giving simultaneous computational and statistical guarantees via a population-to-sample geometric-convergence framework.

- Proposes a decorrelated score-based inference procedure for low-dimensional components with asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency, instantiated for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions.

- Extends Balakrishnan et al. (2017) to high dimensions by adding sparsity truncation and entrywise control to track supports, and adapts de-biasing ideas to latent-variable models using EM score/Fisher identities.

- Conceptual strength: simple, general augmentation of EM (T-steps) that is computationally light and theoretically tractable.

- Theoretical strength: clear separation of optimization vs. statistical error, geometric convergence within a basin, and near-optimal rates for a broad class of models.

- Inference strength: first EM-based decorrelated score achieving semiparametric efficiency for latent-variable models (novel contribution).

- Generality: unified conditions (Lipschitz-gradient, smoothness/strong concavity, entrywise sample-to-population M-step error) verified for key models.

- Attention to high-dimensional specifics: entrywise concentration, support evolution under truncation, and basin-stability conditions are carefully treated.

- Initialization concern: requires ℓ2 initializer within a constant of truth; practical high-dimensional initialization strategies and dependence of required SNR on d,n should be clarified.

- Resampling vs. practice: main theory uses a resampling (fresh-sample) variant; need clarification/statement of analogous bounds for single-sample iterative algorithm and key technical obstacles.

- Choice of bs: theory assumes bs ≍ s* but s* is unknown; sensitivity to mis-specification and data-driven selection/adaptation of bs need guidance.

- Sparsity of w*: inference requires w* sparse with s*w = o(√(n/log d)), which may be restrictive; provide intuition/examples and discuss alternative debiasing constructions.

- Computational cost of inference: solving the high-dimensional Dantzig selector may be heavy; discuss practical solvers, complexity, and lasso-type approximations with guarantees.

- Extra log n factor: estimation rate has an extra log n—clarify whether this is an artifact of resampling/union bounds and whether it can be removed with refined analysis.

- Empirical validation missing: add simulations to confirm geometric behavior, scaling of estimation rates with d,n,s*, and coverage/power of the decorrelated score vs. alternatives.

- Quality assessment: technically careful and consistent with literature; effective use of Qn/Tn identities for inference; conditions are numerous but interpretable and model instantiations are valuable.

- Clarity: well organized but notation dense; recommend summarizing key constants/assumptions and providing a practical tuning recipe for bs, stepsize, and Dantzig parameter.

- Originality: high — first extension of EM theory to high-dimensional sparse settings with a simple truncation mechanism and new inference guarantees.

- Significance: high — bridges computational EM iterates and statistically optimal estimation/inference in high dimensions with broad applicability.

- Pros for acceptance: novel general framework, first semiparametrically efficient EM inference, near-optimal rates, geometric convergence, covers multiple M-step implementations.

- Cons against acceptance: reliance on nontrivial initialization and SNR, resampling-based analysis, restrictive w* sparsity, computationally heavy Dantzig step, and lack of experiments.

- Concrete suggestions: provide explicit initialization strategies and SNR/separation requirements per model; state a non-resampling theorem (even weaker); add simulations for rates and inference and bs sensitivity; discuss Dantzig solver options and lasso alternatives; clarify support-recovery results under beta-min conditions.

- Overall evaluation: strong theoretical contribution with clear novelty and impact; recommend acceptance conditional on added empirical validation and clearer practical guidance.
