- Introduces a sparse EM algorithm that enforces sparsity by truncating parameters each iteration and a decorrelated-score-based asymptotically normal test for individual entries.  
- Main theoretical result: geometric convergence of the sparse EM estimator to the true parameter up to statistical error depending on iteration count.  
- Statistical error scales with sqrt(sparsity) and the infinity-norm of the M-step estimation error, which is typically logarithmic in ambient dimension.  
- Convergence proof relies on standard low-dimensional EM conditions (Q-function smoothness, near-quadratic behavior, and initialization in a basin of attraction).  
- Novel technical requirement is infinity-norm control of the M-step error, required specifically for sparse vectors.  
- Raises a critical question whether iterative truncation is necessary, or if standard EM followed by a single post-hoc truncation could achieve similar guarantees.  
- Provides compelling theoretical evidence (linear regression special case) that standard EM fails and iterative truncation can be necessary.  
- Omits related sparse-optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 on truncated gradient), weakening contextualization.  
- Experimental section lacks details on initialization procedures, undermining interpretability and reproducibility of results.  
- Empirical validation should include direct comparisons between sparse EM and standard EM with post-hoc truncation to clarify the necessity of truncation during iterations.  
- Recommendations: add experiments comparing truncation strategies, cite relevant sparse-optimization work, and specify initialization methods.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and theoretically rigorous contribution to high-dimensional learning, but practical impact depends on resolving the role and necessity of the truncation mechanism.
