This paper studies EM in a high-dimensional environment. Convergence results have been established, and a prediction process has been developed.

The authors should discuss under what conditions we can obtain the initial solution, \beta^{\rm init}, which satisfies the requirement given in line 60 on page 2.

Two algorithms are proposed. Is there any practical guidance on which one to use?

How are $\hat s$ and $\lamdba$ selected in practice? How sensitive are the results to the choice of these tuning parameters? Is there a way to choose them in a data-dependent manner? In the existing literature on high-dimensional estimation, selecting tuning parameters is extremely important for achieving good sampling properties.

The uncorrelated score test is similar to [*]. This fact should be mentioned and not ignored. Is there a need to develop something new to implement the existing prediction process in the current context?

The main theoretical results are valid under a large number of technical conditions. The authors should discuss whether these conditions/validations are satisfied for the secret variable model under consideration.

[] A general theory of hypothesis testing and confidence regions for sparse high-dimensional models. Yang Ning, Han Liu This paper extends [2] to a high-dimensional environment. It also applies Ning and Liu's work to the problem of estimation. This paper connects existing results in a clever way. However, it should be clarified what is truly new in this paper and why the application of existing tools is insignificant. The presented paper is a "trailer" for longer supplementary material. This trailer should contain more information about the technical challenges required to establish the main results.
