This paper introduces a modified Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm designed for parameter estimation in high-dimensional, sparse settings. The authors establish convergence conditions to a global solution and provide a convergence rate analysis. Furthermore, they develop a statistical testing framework to assess the sparsity of the resulting estimator.

The paper is well-structured and provides a sufficient overview of related work. However, the presentation is quite dense, and sections 3 and 4, in particular, present significant challenges in terms of readability. Greater simplification would substantially improve accessibility for a broader audience. 

I have several specific questions and concerns regarding the methodology and practical implementation:

Firstly, the authors claim in line 66 that the achieved estimation error of $\sqrt{s \log(d/n)}$ is minimax-optimal. However, the proof of this claim is not included within the main manuscript (and I did not examine the supplementary material). A clear and concise demonstration of this optimality would significantly strengthen the paper.

Secondly, Equation (3.8) introduces the estimator $\hat{s}$, which is itself a function of the true sparsity level *s*. In a real-world application, *s* is unknown. The paper does not detail how $\hat{s}$ should be practically selected. Furthermore, obtaining an appropriate initial value $\beta^{init}$ satisfying $||\beta^{init} - \beta^*||_2 < R/2$ (as mentioned in line 275) poses a practical challenge. Guidance on how to determine a suitable $\beta^{init}$ would be valuable.

Finally, the purpose of the truncation step within Algorithm 1 is unclear. What consequences would arise from omitting this step from the proposed EM algorithm? A clearer explanation of the truncation step’s role in the algorithm's performance would be beneficial.

---

**Post-Rebuttal:**

The authors’ response to my concerns was satisfactory, and I have increased my score from 6 to 7. This is a statistically rigorous work, but its density makes it challenging for machine learning researchers to fully grasp. Additionally, the practical utility of the proposed method for real-world applications remains somewhat uncertain.
