A sparse Expectation-Maximization (EM) procedure is proposed, modifying the classical EM algorithm by truncating the parameter vector at each step to a desired sparsity level. Additionally, an asymptotically normal statistic based on a decorrelated score is introduced for testing whether a specific entry of the parameter vector is non-zero.

The sparse EM algorithm provides a guarantee that the estimate converges geometrically to the true value with respect to the number of iterations, up to a statistical error term that scales with the square root of the sparsity level and the infinity norm of the statistical error in the M-step. Notably, this error term is typically only logarithmic in the ambient dimension. This result builds upon recent similar findings for EM in low-dimensional settings.

Similar to previous work, the analysis assumes that the Q function is sufficiently smooth and nearly quadratic within a certain basin of attraction, and that the algorithm is initialized within this basin. A new condition is introduced, requiring control over the infinity norm of the error in estimating the M-step. This condition is specific to sparse vectors, but for many models, estimating M at all possible vectors is as feasible as at sparse vectors. For instance, this holds for the Gaussian mixture example and the non-gradient version of the mixture of regression model. This raises the question of whether the truncated EM algorithm is necessary, and whether a similar guarantee could be obtained by performing ordinary EM under the same conditions, followed by a single truncation step. However, the authors' observation that linear regression is a special case seems to argue against this possibility.

Some related work in sparse optimization, such as "Sparse online learning via truncated gradient" by Langford et al. (2009), may be relevant and worth discussing.

The experimental section should specify the initialization method used. The proposed sparse EM variant is an important contribution to parameter estimation in high-dimensional settings, with the convergence guarantees being a significant result. However, the necessity of the sparse variant of EM to achieve these results is not entirely clear.
