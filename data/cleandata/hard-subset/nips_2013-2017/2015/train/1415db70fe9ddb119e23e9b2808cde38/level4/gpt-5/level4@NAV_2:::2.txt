This paper introduces a variant of the EM algorithm tailored to high-dimensional latent variable models with sparse parameters. The authors establish conditions under which the method converges (claimed to the global solution) and derive geometric rates together with near-optimal statistical error bounds. In addition, they present a hypothesis testing framework that facilitates assessing whether components are zero, thereby enabling checks on sparsity in the estimator.

The paper is well structured and the related work is adequately covered. That said, the presentation is very dense—especially Sections 3 and 4—and some arguments are difficult to follow. Streamlining the exposition would significantly improve readability.

- In line 066, the manuscript asserts that the rate sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal. I could not locate a clear proof or citation for this claim in the main text (I did not consult the supplementary material). Please provide a transparent justification or an explicit reference in the main paper.
- In Eq. (3.8), the tuning parameter ŝ depends on the unknown sparsity level s. How should ŝ be selected in practice? Additionally, how can one practically obtain an initialization β̂_init that satisfies ||β̂_init − β*||_2 ≤ R/2 (cf. line 275)?
- I am not fully convinced about the necessity of the truncation step in Algorithm 1. What theoretical or empirical failure modes arise if the truncation step is omitted in your EM procedure?
