- Proposes a sparse EM that truncates the parameter vector each iteration and an asymptotically normal, decorrelated-score statistic to test a single parameter entry.  
- Provides a guarantee that the sparse-EM estimate converges geometrically to the truth up to a statistical error term.  
- Statistical error scales as sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity-norm of the M-step error, which is typically logarithmic in ambient dimension.  
- Builds on recent EM convergence results from low-dimensional settings.  
- Analysis assumes the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin of attraction and requires initialization inside that basin.  
- Introduces a new condition controlling the infinity-norm error in estimating M, claimed to be needed only for sparse vectors.  
- Notes that for many models (e.g., Gaussian mixture, non-gradient mixture of regressions) estimating M at all vectors is as easy as at sparse vectors, questioning the need for per-iteration truncation.  
- Asks whether ordinary EM plus a single truncation at the end could yield similar guarantees; linear regression example argues against this simple alternative.  
- Recommends discussing related sparse-optimization work such as Langford et al. (2009) on truncated gradient.  
- Experimental section should state the initialization method clearly.  
- Concludes the sparse-EM variant and convergence results are important contributions, but the necessity of the sparse truncation remains unclear.
