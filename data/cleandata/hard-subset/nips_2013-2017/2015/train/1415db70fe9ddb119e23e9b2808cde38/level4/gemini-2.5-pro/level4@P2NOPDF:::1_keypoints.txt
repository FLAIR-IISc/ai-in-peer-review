- Studies EM for sparse high-dimensional latent variable models, giving convergence guarantees and methods for hypothesis testing and confidence intervals.
- Main weakness: unclear novelty—authors must specify which components are new versus direct applications of existing results.
- Inference appears to reuse Ning & Liu’s decorrelated score framework; the paper must cite this work and clarify any non-trivial adaptations (e.g., inverse covariance estimation, score analysis).
- Two proposed algorithms: the paper lacks practical guidance or empirical criteria for choosing between them.
- Tuning parameters (e.g., ŝ, λ) need data-driven selection methods (e.g., cross-validation) and a sensitivity analysis.
- Strong initialization assumption (β^init near truth) requires discussion of when such an initializer exists and practical ways to obtain it.
- The many technical assumptions should be justified and checked for realism/verifiability in concrete models (mixture models, mixed-effects models).
- Presentation is thin on technical depth in the main text; key lemmas, proof sketches, or intuition should be moved from the supplement into the paper.
- Overall recommendation: promising but requires substantial revision to clarify novelty, justify assumptions, and provide practical implementation guidance.
