The paper investigates Expectation-Maximization (EM) in high-dimensional settings, establishing convergence results and developing an inference procedure. 

The authors should elaborate on the conditions under which an initial solution, $\beta^{\rm init}$, satisfying the requirement on line 60 of page 2 can be found.

Two algorithms are proposed; practical guidance regarding which algorithm to employ in different scenarios would be valuable. 

Furthermore, the method relies on parameters $\hat{s}$ and $\lambda$. How should these be selected in practice, and how sensitive are the results to their choice? Is a data-dependent method for parameter selection possible? Given the crucial role of tuning parameters in achieving good sampling properties within the existing high-dimensional inference literature, this aspect requires further attention.

The decorrelated score test is identical to that presented in [*]. This connection should be explicitly cited rather than omitted. Additionally, the authors should clarify what novel developments, if any, are required to adapt the existing inference procedure to the current context.

The core theoretical results are predicated on a substantial number of technical conditions. The authors should discuss the plausibility of these conditions and assumptions within the context of the latent variable models considered.

[] A General Theory of Hypothesis Tests and Confidence Regions for Sparse High Dimensional Models. Yang Ning, Han Liu. This paper extends [2] to a high-dimensional setting and applies the work of Ning and Liu [] to the problem of inference. The work represents a clever combination of existing results. However, the precise novelty of this paper and the reasons why applying existing tools is nontrivial should be more clearly articulated. The submitted paper currently feels like a condensed overview of the extensive supplementary material; the main paper should include more detail regarding the technical challenges involved in establishing the central results.
