- Paper proposes a sparse EM variant with a truncation (T-) step keeping bs largest coordinates to enforce sparsity in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Proves geometric optimization convergence and near-minimax estimation rates ~sqrt(s* log d / n) (up to log factors) under initialization and regularity conditions.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score for inference on low-dimensional components with asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM analyses to d >> n with sparsity and adapts debiasing ideas to non-convex latent-variable settings.  
- Demonstrates framework on high-dimensional Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions, covering exact and gradient-ascent M-steps.  
- Novelty: conceptually clean, broadly applicable sparsity-enforcing EM modification with finite-sample guarantees.  
- Theory: clear decomposition of optimization vs statistical error, geometric rates, near-optimal rates, and efficient inference results.  
- Generality: unified analysis for exact and approximate M-steps with conditions that guide model-specific verification.  
- Clarity concern: Sections 3–4 are dense and hard to parse; suggest moving technicalities to appendix and adding intuition boxes and toy examples.  
- Practical guidance lacking on choosing bs; recommend cross-validation, information criteria, stability selection, or adaptive thresholding and a sensitivity study.  
- Initialization requirements are strong (within R/2 of β*); request model-specific initializers (spectral, method-of-moments, k-means, Lasso) and multi-restart defaults.  
- T-step justification should be made explicit: without truncation iterates become dense, statistical rates degrade, and key lemmas fail; add an ablation “with vs without truncation.”  
- Minimax claim needs explicit lower bound statement, assumptions, precise pointer/proof, and clarification of the “near-” (extra log factors).  
- Theory vs practice: main theorems analyze a resampling variant for simplicity—clarify which guarantees extend to the single-sample algorithm and empirical behavior.  
- Applicability: limited real-data experiments and tuning burden; more comparisons to ℓ1-penalized EM, tensor methods, and desparsified procedures requested.  
- Quality: technically solid and careful; assumptions standard though potentially involved to verify; inference results especially compelling.  
- Clarity: overall organized but technical sections require more intuition, diagrams, and explicit pointers to proofs.  
- Originality: high — sparse iterative truncation in EM plus decorrelated inference for latent-variable models is a novel synthesis.  
- Significance: strong theoretical contribution as the first general framework for optimal estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent-variable EM; practical impact depends on initialization and tuning.  
- Specific author requests: provide minimax proof/pointer, practical bs and initialization recipes, T-step ablation, and guidance on checking key conditions and constants.  
- Pros for acceptance: advances theory for high-dimensional EM, modular framework, semiparametric efficiency for inference.  
- Cons for acceptance: dense exposition, limited practical guidance and real-data validation, and some in-text claims (minimax) need clearer support.  
- After rebuttal: authors addressed main concerns (truncation role, practical guidance); reviewer raised score from 6 to 7 but still notes practical challenges and need for more empirical validation.
