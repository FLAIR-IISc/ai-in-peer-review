The authors propose a modification of the EM algorithm for sparse and high-dimensional parameters, deriving conditions for convergence to the global solution and the rate of convergence. They also construct a statistical test framework to verify the sparsity of the estimator. The paper is well-organized, and the related work is adequately summarized. However, the content is dense, particularly in sections 3 and 4, which may be challenging for readers to follow. Simplifying the presentation would be beneficial.

Several aspects of the paper require clarification. Firstly, the authors claim that the rate sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal (line 066), but the proof is not provided in the main manuscript. A clear proof or a reference to the relevant section in the supplementary material would be helpful.

Furthermore, the choice of ^s in Eq.(3.8) depends on the unknown sparsity s. In practice, it is unclear how to select ^s, and it is also uncertain how to find an appropriate initial value beta^init that satisfies the condition ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 (line 275).

Additionally, the importance of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not clearly explained. The authors should elaborate on the consequences of omitting this step in their EM algorithm.

After the rebuttal, my concerns were adequately addressed, leading me to increase my score from 6 to 7. While the paper is statistically sound, its readability and practical applicability for real-world machine learning applications are somewhat limited.
