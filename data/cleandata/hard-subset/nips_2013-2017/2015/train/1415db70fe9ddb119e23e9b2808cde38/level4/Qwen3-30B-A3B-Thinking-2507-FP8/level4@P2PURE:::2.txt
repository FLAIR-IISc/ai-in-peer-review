

The paper proposes a modification of the EM algorithm for sparse high-dimensional parameter estimation. The authors establish conditions for convergence to the global solution and derive the convergence rate, while also constructing a statistical test framework to assess the sparsity of the estimator. The paper is well-organized and adequately summarizes related work. However, the content is dense, particularly in Sections 3 and 4, which are difficult to follow; greater simplification would benefit readers.  

- While the authors claim in line 66 that $\sqrt{s \log(d/n)}$ is minimax-optimal, this proof is not provided in the main manuscript (supplementary material was not reviewed). Please clarify and provide this proof explicitly.  
- In Equation (3.8), $\hat{s}$ depends on the unknown $s$. How should $\hat{s}$ be selected in practice? Additionally, how can an appropriate initial value $\beta^{\text{init}}$ satisfying $\|\beta^{\text{init}} - \beta^*\|_2 < R/2$ (line 275) be obtained practically?  
- The importance of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is unclear. Please explain what occurs if this step is omitted in the EM algorithm.
