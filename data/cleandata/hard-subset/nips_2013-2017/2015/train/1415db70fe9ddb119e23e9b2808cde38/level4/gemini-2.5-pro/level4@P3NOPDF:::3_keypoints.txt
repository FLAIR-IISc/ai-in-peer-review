- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that enforces a desired sparsity level via a truncation step after each iteration.  
- Introduces an asymptotically normal, decorrelated-score statistic for testing if a specific parameter entry is non-zero.  
- Main theoretical contribution: geometric convergence of the sparse EM estimate to the true parameter up to a statistical error.  
- Statistical error scales with sqrt(sparsity) and the infinity norm of the M-step error, typically logarithmic in ambient dimension.  
- Extends recent EM analyses from low-dimensional to high-dimensional sparse regimes, a timely theoretical advance.  
- Analysis assumes the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin of attraction and requires initialization within that basin.  
- New technical condition: infinity-norm control of the M-step error (only required for sparse vectors), and authors note practical equivalence of sparse/non-sparse M-step estimation in some models.  
- Raises a key question about whether iterative truncation is necessary vs. running standard EM then applying a single final hard-thresholding step; requests clearer delineation of model classes where iterative truncation is essential.  
- Suggests connecting to broader sparse optimization literature (e.g., truncated-gradient methods) for additional context.  
- Experimental section lacks details on initialization strategy, which is crucial given the theoretical reliance on being within the basin of attraction.
