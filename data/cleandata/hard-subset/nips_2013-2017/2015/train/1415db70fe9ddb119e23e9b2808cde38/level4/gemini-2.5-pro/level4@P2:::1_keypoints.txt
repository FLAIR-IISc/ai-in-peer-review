1. Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step that enforces sparsity and converges geometrically to a near-optimal estimator (√(s* log d / n)).  
2. Develops a decorrelated score-based inferential procedure for low-dimensional components and establishes asymptotic normality.  
3. Applies the general framework to Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models, extending EM theory to high dimensions.  
4. Strength: tackles an important, under-addressed problem—EM in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
5. Strength: provides strong theoretical guarantees (geometric convergence, near-minimax rates, valid inference).  
6. Strength: presents a general framework with clear technical conditions and model applications.  
7. Weakness: requires a near-true initialization but gives no practical guidance or proof that such an initializer can be obtained for the considered models.  
8. Weakness: novelty of the inferential procedure is unclear—needs explicit citation of related work (e.g., Ning & Liu 2017) and clarification of technical challenges due to nonconvex, local-optimum estimators.  
9. Weakness: lacks practical guidance on M-step choices (exact vs. gradient ascent) and on selecting tuning parameters (sparsity level bs, Dantzig λ), including data-driven selection and sensitivity.  
10. Weakness: main text defers verification of complex technical conditions to the appendix and should include intuition or sketches to make claims more convincing.  
11. Conclusion: strong theoretical contribution but should address initialization feasibility, clarify inferential novelty, and reduce overreliance on the appendix to be a very strong paper.
