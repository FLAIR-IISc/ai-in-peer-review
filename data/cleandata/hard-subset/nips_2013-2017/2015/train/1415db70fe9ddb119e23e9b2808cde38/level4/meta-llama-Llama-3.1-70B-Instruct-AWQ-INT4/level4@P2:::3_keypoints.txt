- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector at each step to enforce a desired sparsity level.  
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse EM estimate to the true parameter up to a statistical error term.  
- Introduces an asymptotically normal decorrelated-score statistic for testing and constructing confidence intervals for low-dimensional components.  
- Demonstrates the method on Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regression models.  
- Analysis assumes the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin and requires initialization inside that basin.  
- Introduces a new infinity-norm control condition on the error in estimating M, required only for sparse vectors.  
- Raises the question whether iterative truncation is necessary versus running ordinary EM then a single truncation.  
- Provides comparisons with related work (tensor methods, sparse optimization) but could deepen connections.  
- Experimental section is limited and lacks sufficient details about initialization used in experiments.  
- Suggests further discussion of connections to other sparse techniques (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 truncated gradient).  
- Overall, a significant contribution to high-dimensional estimation and hypothesis testing, though some methodological and empirical questions remain.
