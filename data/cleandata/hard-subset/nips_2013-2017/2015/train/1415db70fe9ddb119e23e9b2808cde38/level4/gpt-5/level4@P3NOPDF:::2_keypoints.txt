- Proposes a sparsity-aware EM for high-dimensional sparse parameters with global convergence guarantees, explicit rates, and a sparsity testing framework, but Sections 3–4 are dense.
- Addresses an important, timely problem of scalable, theoretically grounded EM in high-dimensional sparse regimes.
- Provides nontrivial theory including global convergence under identifiability and explicit convergence rates.
- Introduces a practical statistical testing framework to assess estimator sparsity.
- Paper is well organized and positioned relative to prior work.
- Main accessibility issue: technical assumptions and proofs are heavy; need high-level intuition, key special cases, and relocated technical lemmas.
- Practical guidance lacking: hyperparameter selection and initialization strategies are nontrivial and need concrete heuristics, sensitivity analyses, and ablations.
- Empirical scope limited: needs stronger baselines (proximal-gradient, coordinate descent, penalized likelihood, EM variants), runtime reporting, and robustness checks under misspecification.
- Minimax-optimality claim needs clarification: specify norm/model/class/assumptions and provide a formal statement, proof sketch, or precise citation.
- Choice of s-hat (Eq.3.8) is underspecified: propose principled selection methods (CV, BIC/EBIC, stability selection) and quantify sensitivity/impact of mis-specification.
- Initialization requirement (||β_init − β*||2 < R/2) needs practical methods (spectral/method-of-moments, Lasso surrogates, multi-start) and theoretical/empirical evidence on basin coverage.
- Truncation step needs intuitive and formal justification; include ablation comparing no truncation, soft-thresholding, and the proposed truncation and explain which theoretical results fail without it.
- Presentation improvements: add roadmap figure or boxed assumptions, pre-theorem intuition paragraphs, and move detailed proofs to supplement while keeping sketches in main text.
- Notation clarity: unify notation and explicitly define all constants (including R) when first introduced.
- Empirical recommendations: run experiments across varying n, d, s; plot ℓ2-error vs sqrt(s log d / n); include real-data case studies and practical gains over off-the-shelf methods.
- Report computational cost, wall-clock times, scalability strategies, and provide code/scripts for reproducibility.
- Reproducibility needs: document hyperparameters, selection criteria, initialization, random seeds, stopping criteria, tolerances, and sensitivity to these choices.
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution that improves EM understanding in sparse high-dim settings but needs clearer exposition and stronger practical validation; score raised from 6 to 7 after rebuttal though practical usefulness remains somewhat uncertain.
