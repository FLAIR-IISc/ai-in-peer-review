- Proposes a modified EM algorithm for high-dimensional problems with sparse true parameters.  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing convergence to the global optimum and deriving convergence rate.  
- Introduces a statistical testing framework to assess sparsity of the estimated parameter.  
- Paper is well-organized with rigorous theoretical treatment and solid positioning within related work.  
- Main weakness: presentation is dense and Sections 3–4 are highly technical and hard to follow.  
- Needs clearer intuition and simplification to improve accessibility for broader ML audience.  
- Major clarification requested: justify the minimax-optimality claim for the rate sqrt(s*log(d/n)) with proof sketch or precise citation.  
- Practical concerns: selection of ^s (depends on unknown true sparsity s) — request guidance on data-driven selection (e.g., cross-validation).  
- Practical concerns: initialization requirement (beta^init within R/2 of beta*) is strong — request a practical procedure to obtain such an initializer.  
- Clarify the role of the truncation step in Algorithm 1: why it’s necessary and consequences of omitting it (convergence, statistical rate, numerical stability).  
- Post-rebuttal: authors’ responses were satisfactory and strengthened confidence in the contributions.  
- Final suggestion: incorporate rebuttal clarifications, discuss limitations and practical implementation challenges to improve accessibility and utility.
