- Presents convergence guarantees for high-dimensional EM and a novel inference procedure adapting decorrelated score tests to latent-variable models.
- Technically significant: extends Ning & Liu (2017)’s high-dimensional inference framework to the EM/latent-variable setting.
- Lacks explicit verification that Assumptions 1–4 hold for concrete models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, factor analysis); must show or restrict the applicable model class.
- Requires practical guidance on obtaining the required initial solution β^{init}; paper does not explain feasible initialization strategies or whether the condition is restrictive.
- Proposes two algorithms (EM and a modified variant) but fails to advise when to use each; needs comparison on computation, robustness, and sparsity regimes.
- Omits guidance on selecting tuning parameters \hat{s} and λ (selection methods, sensitivity of inference, feasibility of data-dependent/adaptive tuning), a critical gap for valid high-dimensional inference.
- Attribution and differences with Ning & Liu (2017) are insufficient: must explicitly cite that work and clearly explain what technical adaptations were needed for the latent-variable/EM context.
- Main text defers too much to the supplement; should include core derivations, sketches validating assumptions for target models, and algorithmic/initialization details so the paper stands alone.
- Overall: promising contribution but needs substantial revisions—practical tuning/initialization guidance, precise novelty claim vs. prior work, and concrete justification of assumptions—to meet conference standards.
