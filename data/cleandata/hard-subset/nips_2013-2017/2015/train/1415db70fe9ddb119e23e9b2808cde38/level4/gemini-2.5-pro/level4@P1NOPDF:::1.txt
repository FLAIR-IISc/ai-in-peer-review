This paper investigates the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in a high-dimensional setting, establishing convergence guarantees and developing an associated inference procedure. While the paper combines existing results in a clever way, several points require clarification to strengthen the contribution.

**Practical Implementation and Tuning**

*   **Initialization:** The theoretical results rely on an initial solution, $\beta^{\rm init}$, that satisfies the requirement in line 60. The authors should clarify the conditions under which such an initial solution can be found in practice. This is a crucial step for the method's applicability.
*   **Algorithm Choice:** The paper proposes two algorithms. It would be helpful if the authors could provide practical guidance on how to choose between them, perhaps by outlining the scenarios where one might be preferred over the other.
*   **Tuning Parameters:** The paper needs to provide a clearer methodology for selecting the tuning parameters $\hat s$ and $\lambda$. How sensitive are the final results to the choice of these parameters? The authors should discuss whether data-driven selection methods, such as cross-validation, are applicable. As the choice of tuning parameters is of utmost importance for obtaining good sampling properties in high-dimensional inference, this aspect of the proposed method warrants a more thorough treatment.

**Assumptions and Theoretical Guarantees**

The main theoretical results rely on a number of technical conditions. It is not immediately clear whether these assumptions are realistic or easily verifiable for the specific latent variable models considered (e.g., mixtures of regressions, latent graphical models). The authors should provide a more thorough discussion on the validity and practical implications of these assumptions within the context of these models.

**Novelty and Contribution**

The paper's primary contributions appear to be the extension of prior work [2] to a high-dimensional setting and the application of the inference framework from Ning and Liu [*].

*   **Attribution:** The decorrelated score test used for inference appears to be directly adopted from the work of Ning and Liu [*]. This connection should be explicitly acknowledged and cited.
*   **Novelty of Application:** The authors should clarify what, if any, novel technical developments were required to adapt this existing inference framework to the current latent variable model setting. What are the key technical hurdles in applying these existing tools to the high-dimensional EM problem, and how does the paper overcome them? Simply applying existing methods is not sufficient without demonstrating that the application itself is non-trivial.
*   **Technical Depth:** Currently, the main paper reads as a high-level summary of the results, with most technical details relegated to the lengthy supplement. To be a self-contained and convincing contribution, the main paper should provide more insight into the technical challenges encountered and the novel techniques developed to address them.
