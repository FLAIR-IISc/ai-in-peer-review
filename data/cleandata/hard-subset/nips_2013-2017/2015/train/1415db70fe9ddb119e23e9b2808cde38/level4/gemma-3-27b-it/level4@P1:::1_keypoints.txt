- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity with statistical and computational guarantees.  
- Establishes asymptotic normality and inference procedures for parameter estimation in high dimensions.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM theory to the high-dimensional regime and leverages recent high-dimensional inference tools.  
- Well-motivated problem addressing a gap between theory and high-dimensional practice.  
- Presentation is clear and logically structured, though somewhat dense.  
- Initialization requirement is strong and under-discussed; practical methods to find a valid initial solution are needed.  
- Two M-step implementations are given but the paper lacks guidance on choosing between them and their trade-offs.  
- Sensitivity to tuning parameters (ŝ and λ) is not addressed; needs data-dependent selection strategies.  
- The connection to Ning & Liu (2017) is noted but should be explicitly cited and differentiated, explaining novel adaptations for latent variable models.  
- Main theoretical results depend on many technical conditions that are not shown to hold for the specific models studied.  
- Requires a more detailed discussion of the plausibility and implications of technical assumptions for GMM and mixture-of-regressions.  
- Relies heavily on supplementary material for key details, giving the paper an incomplete feel.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, principled combination of techniques with theoretical guarantees and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient practical guidance (initialization, tuning), incomplete novelty discussion relative to prior work, and limited validation of technical conditions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing practical implementation, tuning, clarity on novelty, and validation of technical assumptions.
