- Proposes a High-Dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step that enforces sparsity and provably converges geometrically to a near-minimax estimator.  
- Develops a decorrelated score statistic with asymptotic normality for inference on low-dimensional components.  
- Validates the framework on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regressions examples.  
- Strength: addresses an important, hard problem and delivers significant, seemingly sound theoretical results for non-convex, high-dimensional latent-variable models.  
- Strength: provides a unified framework guaranteeing near-optimal estimation and valid asymptotic inference; paper is well-organized and surveys related work thoroughly.  
- Major concern: algorithm requires an initial estimate within a specified Euclidean radius of the truth, but the paper does not propose practical initialization methods.  
- Major concern: truncation sparsity parameter bs depends on unknown s*; paper lacks guidance or theory for data-driven selection (e.g., cross-validation).  
- Major concern: motivation for the truncation (T-) step versus standard L1-penalized M-step is unclear, and the effect of omitting T-step is not discussed.  
- Major concern: sections with theoretical conditions and proofs are dense; the paper needs more high-level intuition (e.g., role of l_inf-norm in Condition 3.3) for accessibility.  
- Minor point: the near-minimax-rate claim (sqrt(s* log d / n)) should be supported with a specific citation of the corresponding lower bound for the considered model class.
