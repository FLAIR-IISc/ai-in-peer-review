In this paper, the authors propose a modification of the EM algorithm tailored to the case where the true parameter is sparse and high-dimensional. They establish conditions for convergence to the global solution and derive the rate of convergence. Additionally, a statistical test framework is constructed, enabling the assessment of the sparsity of the estimator.

The paper is well-organized, and the related work is sufficiently summarized. However, the content is very dense, and some sections (notably Sections 3 and 4) are challenging to follow. Further simplification would be beneficial for readers.

Several aspects of the paper require clarification. Firstly, the authors claim in line 066 that the term sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal, but the proof of this statement is not provided in the main manuscript (this reviewer did not consult the supplementary material). A clear proof of this assertion would be appreciated.

Furthermore, Equation (3.8) reveals that ^s depends on s, but in practice, the true value of s is unknown. The authors should provide guidance on how to choose ^s in practical scenarios. Additionally, it is unclear how to select an appropriate initial value beta^init that satisfies the condition ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 (see line 275).

The truncation step in Algorithm 1 also warrants explanation. The authors should elaborate on the consequences of omitting the truncation step in their EM algorithm.

After reviewing the authors' response, this reviewer found the explanations satisfactory, leading to an increase in the score from 6 to 7. While the paper is a solid contribution to the field of statistics, its readability for machine learning researchers is limited. Moreover, the usefulness of the proposed method for real-world applications is somewhat questionable.
