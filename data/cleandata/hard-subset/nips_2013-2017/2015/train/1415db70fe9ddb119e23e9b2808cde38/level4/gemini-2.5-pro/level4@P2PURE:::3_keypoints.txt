- Introduces a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector to a target sparsity at each iteration.  
- Proposes an asymptotically normal test statistic based on a decorrelated score for hypothesis testing of individual entries.  
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse EM estimate to the true parameter up to a statistical error.  
- Shows the statistical error scales with sqrt(sparsity) and the infinity-norm of the M-step estimation error (typically log-dimensional).  
- Analysis builds on standard smoothness / near-quadratic Q-function assumptions and requires initialization inside a basin of attraction.  
- Introduces a new key assumption: infinity-norm control on the M-step error for (sparse) vectors.  
- Raises a central question about whether iterative truncation is necessary versus running standard EM and truncating once at the end.  
- Argues the linear regression special case suggests post-processing EM may not suffice, supporting the iterative approach.  
- Recommends citing sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., 2009) to strengthen related-work connections.  
- Requests clearer experimental details, specifically the initialization procedure used.  
- Overall: provides valuable high-dimensional convergence guarantees for sparse EM, but the fundamental necessity of iterative sparsity induction remains not fully established.
