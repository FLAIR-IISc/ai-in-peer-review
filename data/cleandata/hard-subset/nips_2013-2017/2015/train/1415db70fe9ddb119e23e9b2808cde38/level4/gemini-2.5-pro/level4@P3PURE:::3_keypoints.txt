- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector at each M-step to a specified sparsity level.  
- Introduces an asymptotically normal test statistic based on a decorrelated score for testing individual parameter significance.  
- Theoretical guarantee: sparse EM converges geometrically to the true parameter up to a statistical error term depending on sqrt(sparsity) and the infinity-norm of the M-step error.  
- Shows the infinity-norm M-step error is typically logarithmic in ambient dimension, extending EM convergence results to high dimensions.  
- Analysis assumes Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin and requires initialization inside that basin; adds a new infinity-norm control condition on the M-step.  
- Key concern: infinity-norm condition may be as hard to achieve for sparse vectors as for dense ones in many models, questioning the necessity of iterative truncation.  
- Paper argues linear regression as a counterexample to the “EM then truncate once” baseline, but this argument needs further elaboration to fully justify iterative truncation.  
- Suggests connecting the method to sparse optimization literature (e.g., truncated gradient methods) to strengthen the discussion.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contributions for high-dimensional EM, but necessity of the iterative sparse-EM procedure vs. a single final truncation is not fully established.  
- Minor: experiments should explicitly report the initialization procedure used.
