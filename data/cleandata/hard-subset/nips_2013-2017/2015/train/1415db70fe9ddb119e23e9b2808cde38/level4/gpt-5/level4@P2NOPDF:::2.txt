This paper proposes a modification of the EM algorithm tailored to high-dimensional settings with a sparse true parameter. The authors establish conditions for convergence to the global optimum and derive convergence rates. They also present a hypothesis-testing framework to assess the sparsity of the estimator.

The paper is well organized, and related work is adequately reviewed. However, the presentation is dense—especially Sections 3 and 4—and some arguments are difficult to follow. Further simplification and clearer guidance would help readers.

Main comments:
1) Line 066 claims that the rate sqrt(s log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal. I could not find a proof in the main text (I did not read the supplementary material). Please provide a clear proof or include a precise citation within the manuscript.

2) In Eq. (3.8), the quantity \hat{s} depends on the unknown s. How should \hat{s} be selected in practice? In addition, how can one obtain an initial value beta^{init} that satisfies ||beta^{init} − beta*||_2 < R/2 (line 275) in practical settings?

3) Please clarify the importance of the truncation step in Algorithm 1. What happens if it is omitted? How would convergence properties or statistical guarantees change without truncation?
