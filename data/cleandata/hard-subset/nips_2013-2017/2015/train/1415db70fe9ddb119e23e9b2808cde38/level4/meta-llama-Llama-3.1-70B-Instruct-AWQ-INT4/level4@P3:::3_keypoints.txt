- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that enforces sparsity by truncating the parameter vector each iteration.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis: convergence guarantees and asymptotic normality of the decorrelated score.  
- Claims the estimator attains (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence.  
- Demonstrates the framework on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models.  
- Paper is well-written with clear methodology, justified technical assumptions, and thorough related-work review.  
- Criticism: the necessity/importance of the sparse/truncated EM variant to the results is not fully clarified (assumptions resemble low-dimensional EM).  
- Counterargument: showing linear regression as a special case and that a single truncation is insufficient supports the need for repeated truncations.  
- Major omission: experimental section is missing; needs initialization details and empirical comparisons to alternatives.  
- Suggested related work: discuss sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 truncated gradient) for additional context.  
- Overall assessment: significant theoretical contribution that, with minor revisions (experiments and related-work expansion), could have major impact.
