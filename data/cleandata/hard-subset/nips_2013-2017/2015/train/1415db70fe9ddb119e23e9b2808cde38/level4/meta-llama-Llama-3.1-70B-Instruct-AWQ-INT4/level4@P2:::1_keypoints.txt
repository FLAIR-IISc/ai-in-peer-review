- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity and provides a unified framework for estimation and uncertainty assessment.  
- Two main contributions: (i) an EM algorithm with geometric convergence and (near-)optimal statistical rate; (ii) a new inferential procedure (decorrelated score test) for low-dimensional components.  
- Clear, detailed presentation of methodology and thorough review of related work.  
- Discusses implications and examples for Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regressions.  
- Strengths: novelty in high-dimensional EM, unified estimation+inference framework, clarity, and comprehensive related-work review.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of how to obtain the required initial solution β^{init} for convergence guarantees.  
- Weakness: needs practical guidance on algorithm choice and on selecting tuning parameters (ŝ and λ), including sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: should acknowledge and cite existing literature on decorrelated score tests (e.g., Ning and Liu 2017).  
- Weakness: should discuss whether the technical conditions for main results hold for the specific latent variable models considered.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (Rating: 8/10, Confidence: 8/10).
