- Addresses a major gap by studying EM in high-dimensional latent variable models where dimension exceeds sample size.
- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that enforces sparsity via a truncation step.
- Establishes geometric convergence and optimal statistical rates for the proposed algorithm.
- Develops an inferential procedure for testing hypotheses about low-dimensional components.
- Builds upon and extends prior work (notably [2] and Ning & Liu) to the high-dimensional regime.
- Paper is well-structured with a clear introduction and rigorous presentation of methodology and theory.
- Truncation step is a key innovation enabling sparsity in the EM framework.
- Demonstrates applicability to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.
- Practical implementation guidance is lacking, particularly how to obtain an initial solution β^init meeting stated conditions.
- Needs discussion on when to use Algorithm 2 versus Algorithm 3 in practice.
- More guidance required for choosing sparsity parameter bs and tuning parameter λ, including sensitivity and data-dependent selection methods.
- Should explicitly acknowledge and more thoroughly discuss the connection to the decorrelated score test from Ning & Liu and novel challenges in this context.
- Theoretical results depend on technical conditions that need verification for the considered models (e.g., Gaussian mixture, mixture of regressions).
- Heavy reliance on supplementary material makes it hard to fully assess contributions from the main manuscript alone.
- Overall assessment: strong, rigorous, and novel contribution but would benefit from more practical guidance and clearer delineation of novelty beyond applying existing tools.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm with convergence guarantees, optimal rates, inferential procedure, rigorous analysis, meaningful extension of prior work.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient practical guidance (initialization/tuning), limited discussion of inference challenges, technical conditions need further justification, over-reliance on supplement.
