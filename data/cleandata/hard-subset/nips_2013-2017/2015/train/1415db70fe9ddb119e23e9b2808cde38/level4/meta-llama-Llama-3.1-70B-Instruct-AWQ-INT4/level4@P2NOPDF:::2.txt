The authors propose a modified EM algorithm for sparse and high-dimensional parameters, providing conditions for convergence to the global solution and deriving the rate of convergence. They also construct a statistical test framework to verify the sparsity of the estimator. The paper is well-organized, and the related work is sufficiently summarized. However, the content is dense, particularly in sections 3 and 4, which may be challenging for readers to follow. Simplifying these sections would improve the paper's clarity.

Several aspects of the paper require clarification. Firstly, the authors claim that the rate sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal (line 066), but the proof is not provided in the main manuscript. A clear explanation or reference to the supplementary material would be helpful. Secondly, the choice of ^s in Eq.(3.8) depends on the unknown sparsity level s. The authors should provide guidance on how to select ^s in practice. Additionally, it is unclear how to choose an appropriate initial value beta^init that satisfies the condition ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 (line 275).

The truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not well-explained. The authors should elaborate on the importance of this step and the consequences of omitting it from the EM algorithm.

After the rebuttal, the authors' response addressed these concerns, and the score was increased from 6 to 7. The paper is a solid contribution to the field of statistics, but its readability and practical applicability for machine learning researchers and real-world applications are limited.
