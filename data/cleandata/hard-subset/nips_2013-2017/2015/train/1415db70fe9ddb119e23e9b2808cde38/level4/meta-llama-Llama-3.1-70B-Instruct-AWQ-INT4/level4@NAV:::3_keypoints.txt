- Proposes a truncated EM algorithm enforcing a fixed sparsity level each iteration and a decorrelated-score-based asymptotically normal test for single-entry nonzero significance.  
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse EM estimator up to a statistical error term.  
- Statistical error scales with sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity-norm of the M-step estimation error, which typically grows logarithmically with ambient dimension.  
- Analysis extends recent low-dimensional EM guarantees to high-dimensional sparse settings under similar smoothness and near-quadratic Q-function assumptions.  
- Requires initialization inside a basin of attraction and imposes a novel infinity-norm control condition on M-step errors tailored to sparse vectors.  
- The infinity-norm condition is argued to hold in examples like Gaussian mixtures and the non-gradient mixture-of-regressions model.  
- Raises the question whether iterative truncation is necessary versus running ordinary EM then a single truncation, with linear regression as a counterexample hinting necessity.  
- Suggests citing related sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 on truncated gradient) for broader context.  
- Experiments should state the initialization procedure; overall contribution to high-dimensional estimation is valuable but the essentiality of the sparse EM variant remains unclear.
