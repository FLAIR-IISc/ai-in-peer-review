This paper proposes a modification of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm tailored for high-dimensional, sparse parameter estimation in latent variable models. The authors demonstrate convergence conditions to a local optimum and derive the rate of convergence, along with a statistical testing framework for assessing estimator sparsity.

The paper is well-organized and provides a comprehensive summary of related work. However, the density of the content, particularly in sections 3 and 4, presents a challenge for readers; increased clarity and simplification would be beneficial.

Specifically:

*   Although the authors claim a minimax-optimal convergence rate of  √(s\* log(d/n)) (line 66), the justification for this claim is not explicitly presented within the main manuscript (acknowledging that the supplementary material was not reviewed). A clearer explanation or reference would strengthen this assertion.
*   Equation (3.8) introduces a sparsity parameter estimate, ^s, which depends on the true sparsity level, s\*.  In practical applications, s is unknown.  Could the authors clarify how ^s should be chosen in practice? Furthermore, how does one determine an appropriate initial value, β<sup>init</sup>, satisfying ||β<sup>init</sup> - β\*||<sub>2</sub> < R/2 (line 275)?
*   The significance of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 remains unclear. An explanation of the consequences of omitting this step – what behavior would be observed without truncation – would enhance understanding of its purpose.
