- Introduces a sparse modification of EM with theoretical guarantees for convergence to the global optimum and derived convergence rates, plus a framework for testing sparsity.

- Paper is well-organized and cites related work; theoretical contributions appear sound.

- Presentation is very dense; sections 3 and 4 are difficult to follow and need more intuition and simplified exposition.

- Claim that the rate sqrt(s * log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal requires a proof or a specific citation in the main manuscript.

- Practical concern: choice of the parameter ^s (Eq. 3.8) depends on unknown true sparsity s and needs guidance for real-world selection.

- Practical concern: convergence requires an initial beta^init with ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2; authors should describe how to obtain such an initialization and analyze sensitivity to it.

- Role of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is unclear; authors should justify its necessity and explain theoretical/empirical consequences of removing it.

- Overall: strong, rigorous theoretical contribution, but dense math and insufficient practical guidance limit accessibility and real-world applicability.
