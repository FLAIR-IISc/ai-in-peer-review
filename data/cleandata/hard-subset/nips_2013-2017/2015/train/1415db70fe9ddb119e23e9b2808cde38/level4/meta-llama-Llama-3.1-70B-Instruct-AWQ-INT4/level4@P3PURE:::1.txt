The paper studies EM in a high-dimensional setting, establishing convergence results and developing an inference procedure.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should discuss the conditions under which the initial solution, $\beta^{\rm init}$, can be found to satisfy the requirement in line 60 on page 2. Furthermore, since two algorithms are proposed, it would be helpful to provide practical guidance on which one to use in different scenarios.

Additionally, the authors should provide more insight into the choice of $\hat s$ and $\lambda$ in practice. Specifically, they should discuss how sensitive the results are to the choice of these tuning parameters and whether there is a data-dependent way to select them. This is particularly important in high-dimensional inference, where the choice of tuning parameters can significantly impact the quality of the results.

The authors should also acknowledge that the decorrelated score test is equivalent to the one in [*] and provide a proper citation. It would be beneficial to discuss whether any new developments are required to apply the existing inference procedure in the current context.

The main theoretical results rely on a large number of technical conditions. The authors should elaborate on whether these conditions/assumptions are satisfied for the considered latent variable models.

While the paper extends [2] to a high-dimensional setting and applies the work of Ning and Liu [] to the problem of inference, it would be beneficial to clarify what is novel in this paper and why the application of existing tools is non-trivial. The paper effectively combines existing results in a clever way, but it is essential to highlight the technical challenges that were overcome to establish the main results.

Finally, the submitted paper appears to be a condensed version of the supplementary material. To make the paper more self-contained, the authors should include more information on the technical challenges and key insights that are relegated to the supplementary material.
