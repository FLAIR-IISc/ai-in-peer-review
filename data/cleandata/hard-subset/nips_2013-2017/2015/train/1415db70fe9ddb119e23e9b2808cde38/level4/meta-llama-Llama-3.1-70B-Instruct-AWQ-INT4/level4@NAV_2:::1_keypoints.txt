- Studies EM for high-dimensional latent variable models and develops convergence results plus inference procedures.
- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that enforces sparsity and converges at a geometric rate.
- Shows the estimator attains (near-)optimal statistical convergence rates.
- Develops a decorrelated score test for hypotheses on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.
- Proves simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the algorithm and inference procedure.
- Establishes asymptotic normality of the decorrelated score statistic.
- Interprets EM as a perturbed gradient method with a key truncation step to enforce sparsity.
- Applies the framework to Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models, giving the first computationally feasible optimal estimation and inference for these high-dim models.
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and detailed technical condition analysis.
- Missing discussion on conditions and practical methods to obtain the required initial solution β^{init}.
- Two proposed algorithms are presented but it is unclear which should be used in practice.
- Lacks practical guidance on choosing tuning parameters (ŝ and λ) and sensitivity to these choices.
- Decorrelated score test resembles Ning & Liu (2017); the paper should cite and clarify differences.
- Main theoretical results depend on many technical conditions; authors should discuss whether these hold for the considered models.
- Recommendation: acceptable with minor revisions—address initialization, algorithm choice, tuning-parameter guidance, citation/comparison to Ning & Liu (2017), and discussion of technical conditions.
