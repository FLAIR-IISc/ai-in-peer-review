This paper studies the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in a high-dimensional setting, establishing convergence results and developing an associated inference procedure. The work extends existing literature (e.g., [2]) to the high-dimensional regime and applies the inferential framework of Ning and Liu [*]. While the combination of these existing tools is clever, the paper's primary weakness is a lack of clarity regarding its novel contributions and the technical challenges involved.

Specific points for the authors to address:

1.  **Initialization:** A key requirement for the theoretical results is an initial solution, $\beta^{\text{init}}$, that satisfies a specific condition (line 60, page 2). The authors should discuss under what conditions such an initial solution can be found in practice.

2.  **Algorithm Choice:** The paper proposes two algorithms. Practical guidance should be provided on how to choose between them.

3.  **Tuning Parameters:** The choice of tuning parameters, $\hat{s}$ and $\lambda$, is critical for the performance of high-dimensional methods. The authors should provide guidance on their selection in practice. How sensitive are the results to this choice? The paper would be significantly strengthened by a discussion of data-dependent selection methods, as the choice of tuning parameters is of utmost importance for achieving good statistical properties in high-dimensional inference.

4.  **Attribution and Novelty of Inference:** The proposed decorrelated score test appears to be identical to the one introduced by Ning and Liu [*]. This prior work must be explicitly cited and its contribution clearly acknowledged. Furthermore, the authors should detail what novel technical developments, if any, were required to adapt this existing inference procedure to the current context.

5.  **Verification of Assumptions:** The main theoretical results rely on a large number of technical conditions. It is crucial for the authors to discuss whether these assumptions are satisfied for the specific latent variable models considered, as this determines the practical applicability of the theory.

6.  **Clarity of Contribution:** The main paper reads as a summary of results, with the majority of technical details deferred to the supplementary material. To allow for proper evaluation, the main text should provide more insight into the key technical challenges and the novel techniques developed to overcome them. As it stands, it is difficult to assess why the application of existing tools is non-trivial in this context.
