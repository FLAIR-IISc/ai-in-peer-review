- Paper proposes a high-dimensional EM with a hard-thresholding (truncation) step to enforce sparsity and proves geometric convergence to near-optimal rates √(s*)·√(log d / n) for exact and gradient M-steps.
- Builds a decorrelated score and Wald test for low-dimensional components, showing asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency.
- Extends low-dimensional EM theory to high dimensions by incorporating sparsity and separating optimization vs. statistical errors.
- Strength: Addresses the gap between practical EM algorithms and statistical guarantees in high dimensions.
- Strength: Clear optimization–statistical error decomposition with geometric contraction and near-minimax rates.
- Strength: Unified analysis covering both exact and gradient-ascent M-step implementations.
- Strength: Provides asymptotically normal, efficient inference for low-dimensional parameters with high-dimensional nuisances.
- Strength: Illustrative applications to Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions demonstrate scope.
- Weakness: Main results require a good initializer with bounded relative error; paper needs concrete procedures, citations, and sample/SNR regimes guaranteeing such initialization.
- Weakness: Lacks practical guidance on choosing between exact and gradient M-steps (complexity, stepsize robustness, sensitivity to smoothness).
- Weakness: Insufficient advice on tuning s_hat and λ, their sensitivity, and data-driven selection strategies and interaction with theoretical assumptions.
- Weakness: Attribution for inference methods is incomplete; must explicitly cite Ning & Liu and de-biasing literature and delineate what is new vs. reused.
- Weakness: Technical conditions (Lipschitz, concavity, Tn concentration, statistical error bounds) need model-specific summaries of constants, sample regimes, and SNR in the main text.
- Weakness: Practical issues—stepsize selection for gradient M-step, robustness to misspecification of µ/ν, per-iteration cost of E/M/T, and effects of mis-specifying s_hat—are not fully addressed.
- Quality: Technical development is solid and carefully structured but would benefit from moving key lemmas and model-specific conditions into the main text with proof sketches.
- Clarity: Paper reads like a trailer for a long appendix; needs more intuition about basins of attraction, the T-step’s role, and support stability through entrywise control.
- Originality: Combining EM with iterative truncation and unified analysis in latent-variable models appears novel, but the inference component must better credit prior decorrelation/de-biasing work and highlight new technical contributions.
- Significance: Framework is potentially important and broadly applicable if practical initializers and tuning rules can be provided.
- Pros (summary): Addresses central problem, achieves near-optimal rates, geometric convergence, semiparametric-efficient inference, and offers a general framework with two M-step variants.
- Cons (summary): Relies heavily on technical conditions with limited main-text guidance, unclear practical initialization and tuning paths, and unclear novelty/attribution for the inference piece.
- Revision requests: Add explicit initializer guidance and references, practical rules and sensitivity analyses for s_hat/λ/stepsizes, explicit citation and delineation of Ning & Liu-style methods, and main-text summaries of verifiable conditions, sample size/SNR regimes, and key constants.
