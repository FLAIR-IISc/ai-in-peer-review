- Proposes a high-dimensional EM with an iterative truncation after each M-step that enforces sparsity and attains geometric convergence and near-minimax error sqrt(s log d / n).  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for asymptotic inference on low-dimensional components; shown to be asymptotically normal for hypothesis testing and CIs.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM theory to the high-dimensional regime by controlling entrywise (l-infinity) M-step error and adding iterative truncation.  
- Demonstrates generality by instantiating the framework for Gaussian mixtures and mixture-of-regressions.  
- Strength: first comprehensive framework giving both computational (geometric) and statistical (near-optimal) guarantees for EM in high dimensions.  
- Strength: rigorous technical analysis decomposing optimization and statistical errors and providing valid inference for a nonconvex estimator.  
- Strength: provides a computationally feasible inference procedure for nonconvex high-dimensional latent-variable models.  
- Weakness: motivation for iterative truncation is limited; unclear if single-shot truncation or regularized EM could achieve similar guarantees.  
- Weakness: insufficient connection to broader sparse optimization literature (e.g., IHT, truncated gradients, projected gradient methods).  
- Weakness: theory requires initialization within a basin of attraction; paper needs practical guidance and provable methods for obtaining such initializations and to detail experimental initialization.  
- For acceptance: novel, technically deep, and addresses a fundamental problem with practical inference implications.  
- Against acceptance: iterative-truncation justification and strong initialization reliance limit perceived practical impact.  
- Overall: very strong theoretical contribution with addressable presentation and motivation gaps; recommend acceptance.
