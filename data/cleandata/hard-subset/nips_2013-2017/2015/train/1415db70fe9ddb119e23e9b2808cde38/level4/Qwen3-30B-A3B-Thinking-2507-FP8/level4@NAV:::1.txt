

The paper examines EM in a high-dimensional setting, establishing convergence results and developing an inference procedure.  
Authors should clarify under what conditions an initial solution $\beta^{\rm init}$ satisfying the requirement in line 60 (page 2) can be obtained.  
Two algorithms are proposed; practical guidance for selecting between them is needed.  
How should $\hat{s}$ and $\lambda$ be chosen in practice? How sensitive are results to their choice? Is there a data-dependent method for selection? In high-dimensional inference literature, tuning parameter choice is critical for achieving good sampling properties.  
The decorrelated score test replicates the approach in [*]; this must be properly cited, not omitted. What novel developments are required to apply the existing inference procedure to the current context?  
The main theoretical results rely on multiple technical conditions. The authors must discuss whether these assumptions hold for the considered latent variable models.  
The paper extends [2] to high dimensions and applies Ning and Liu's work [] to inference, combining existing results cleverly. However, it must clearly articulate the paper's novelty and why applying existing tools is nontrivial. The submitted work functions as a "trailer" for supplementary material but should contain more detail on the technical challenges required to establish the main results.  
[*] A General Theory of Hypothesis Tests and Confidence Regions for Sparse High Dimensional Models. Yang Ning, Han Liu.  
[] A General Theory of Hypothesis Tests and Confidence Regions for Sparse High Dimensional Models. Yang Ning, Han Liu.
