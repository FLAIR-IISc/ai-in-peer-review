- Proposes a sparse EM that truncates the parameter vector each iteration and an asymptotically normal, decorrelated-score test for individual parameter non-zeroes.  
- Proves geometric convergence (in iterations) to the true parameter up to a statistical error ~ sqrt(sparsity) * ||M-step error||_∞, with the latter typically logarithmic in ambient dimension.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM guarantees under standard assumptions: Q-function smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin, and initialization inside that basin.  
- Novel technical requirement: control of the infinity norm of the M-step estimation error, which is specifically relevant for sparse vectors.  
- For many models studied (e.g., Gaussian mixture, non-gradient mixture of regressions), estimating the M-step at all vectors is as easy as at sparse vectors, calling into question whether iterative truncation is necessary.  
- The linear regression special-case example provides evidence that a simple post-EM truncation may not suffice, supporting the need for further justification of the algorithmic choice.  
- Related sparse-optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., "Sparse online learning via truncated gradient", 2009) should be incorporated in the discussion.  
- Experiments must report the initialization method used.  
- Conclusion: the convergence guarantees are a meaningful contribution to high-dimensional learning, but the necessity of the sparse EM variant remains unclear.
