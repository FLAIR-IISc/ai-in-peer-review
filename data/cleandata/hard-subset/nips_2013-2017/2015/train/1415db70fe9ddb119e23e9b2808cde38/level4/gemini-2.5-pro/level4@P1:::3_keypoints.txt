- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm that adds a truncation step after each M-step to enforce sparsity.  
- Introduces an inferential procedure using a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing on individual parameters, shown to be asymptotically normal.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: under a good initialization and Q-function smoothness/strong concavity, estimates converge geometrically to within a statistical error bound.  
- Statistical error achieves near-optimal rate scaling as sqrt(s*) · sqrt(log d) for sparse problems.  
- Extends EM theory to the high-dimensional regime with both estimation and asymptotic inference guarantees—a novel and impactful contribution.  
- Raises a key question about the necessity of iterative truncation versus a single post-hoc truncation, given the M-step for dense vectors can be as easy as for sparse ones in some models.  
- Argues iterative truncation is likely necessary to keep the solution path in a region where infinity-norm control of the M-step’s statistical error holds, with linear regression as a motivating counterexample.  
- Suggests adding discussion of related sparse optimization methods (e.g., truncated gradient literature) to better contextualize the algorithmic contribution.  
- Requests clarification of the initialization procedure used in experiments, since theory depends on a good initialization for practical reproducibility.  
- Concludes the work is technically sound, original, and of high significance for high-dimensional latent variable models.
