This paper proposes a modified Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for scenarios involving sparse, high-dimensional parameters. The authors establish conditions for the algorithm's convergence to the global solution, derive its rate of convergence, and construct a statistical framework to test the estimator's sparsity.

The paper is well-organized and sufficiently summarizes related work. However, the content is presented in a very dense manner, which makes some parts, particularly Sections 3 and 4, difficult to follow. The manuscript would benefit from simplification to improve its readability.

- The authors state in line 066 that the rate sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal, but I could not find a proof for this claim within the main manuscript. Could the authors provide a clear justification for this?
- In Equation (3.8), the parameter ^s depends on the true sparsity level *s*, which is unknown in practice. How should ^s be chosen in a practical setting? Similarly, the analysis requires an initial value, beta^init, that satisfies ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 (line 275). How can an appropriate initial value be found in practice?
- The motivation for the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is unclear. Could you explain the importance of this step and what would happen if it were omitted from the algorithm?
