- Addresses estimation and inference for high-dimensional latent variable models via regularized EM, with convergence guarantees and inference using a decorrelated score test.  
- Strength: synthesizes and unifies existing high-dimensional estimation and inference techniques for a timely, significant problem.  
- Novelty unclear: paper appears to largely combine prior work (e.g., [2] and Ning & Liu); authors should explicitly cite and clarify what is truly new.  
- Need to articulate new technical challenges in adapting decorrelated score/inference methods to the regularized EM setting.  
- Heavy reliance on many technical assumptions; the paper lacks discussion of whether these are realistic or verifiable for target models.  
- Initialization issue: theory assumes an initial estimator near the truth, but no practical method or conditions are provided to obtain such a start.  
- General assumptions need mapping to concrete models (mixture, probit) and assessment of whether they are standard or particularly strong.  
- Practical guidance is lacking on when to use the two proposed algorithms and their computational/stability trade-offs.  
- Tuning parameters (sparsity level and regularization λ) lack data-dependent selection guidance and sensitivity analysis (e.g., cross-validation recommendations).  
- Presentation imbalance: main text is too high-level and defers key technical intuition and contributions to the supplementary material.  
- Overall: technically sound and important, but requires clearer novelty framing, better assumption justification, and stronger practical implementation advice.
