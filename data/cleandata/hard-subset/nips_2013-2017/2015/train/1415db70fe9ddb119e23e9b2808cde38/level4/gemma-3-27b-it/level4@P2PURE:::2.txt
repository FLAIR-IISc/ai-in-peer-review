This paper introduces a modified Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm designed for parameter estimation in high-dimensional, sparse settings. The authors establish conditions under which the algorithm converges to a global solution and provide a convergence rate analysis. Furthermore, they develop a statistical testing framework to assess the sparsity of the resulting estimator.

The paper is well-structured and provides a sufficient overview of related work. However, the presentation is quite dense, and certain sections – particularly sections 3 and 4 – are challenging to follow; increased clarity and simplification would benefit readers.

- The authors claim in line 66 that $\sqrt{s \log(d/n)}$ is minimax-optimal, but I could not locate a proof of this claim within the main manuscript (I did not review the supplementary material). Could you provide a clear demonstration of this result?
- In Eq.(3.8), the estimate $\hat{s}$ is dependent on the unknown true sparsity level *s*. How should $\hat{s}$ be selected in practical applications? Additionally, how can one determine an appropriate initial value $\beta^{init}$ that satisfies the condition $||\beta^{init} - \beta^*||_2 < R/2$ (as stated in line 275) in a real-world scenario?
- The purpose of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is unclear. Could you elaborate on the consequences of omitting this truncation step from the proposed EM algorithm?
