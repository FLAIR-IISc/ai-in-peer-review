- Contribution: Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step that enforces sparsity and (with proper initialization) converges geometrically to an estimator achieving near-minimax rate sqrt(s log d / n).
- Contribution: Develops a decorrelated score test for low-dimensional components whose statistic is asymptotically normal and attains the semiparametric information bound.
- Novelty/approach: Extends low-dimensional EM theory to the high-dimensional sparse regime via truncation and fine-grained entrywise error analysis, and adapts debiasing techniques to non-convex EM local optima.
- Strength (Significance): Tackles an important gap—EM theory in high-dimensional settings—providing the first computationally feasible method with guarantees for both estimation and inference.
- Strength (Quality): Theoretical treatment is thorough, proving geometric convergence, near-optimal estimation rates, and asymptotic normality/efficiency for inference.
- Strength (Generality): Framework is developed under general conditions and demonstrated on nontrivial examples (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, mixture regressions), suggesting broad applicability.
- Weakness/1 (Initialization): Main results require an initial estimator within a specific radius of the truth; the paper lacks concrete, provable polynomial-time procedures (e.g., spectral/moment methods) to obtain such initialization.
- Weakness/2 (Tuning): The method depends on tuning choices (sparsity level bs and Dantzig lambda) with no guidance on data-driven selection (e.g., cross-validation), limiting practical usability.
- Weakness/3 (Originality of Inference): The decorrelated score construction closely resembles existing high-dimensional inference frameworks (e.g., Ning & Liu 2017); the paper should cite these works and clarify its specific technical innovations for handling EM’s non-convex/local solutions.
- Weakness/4 (Technical conditions): Analysis rests on high-level conditions (Conditions 3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.4); the main text needs more intuition or proof sketches to justify these assumptions for the studied models.
- Weakness/5 (M-step guidance): The paper gives exact and gradient-ascent M-step implementations but lacks discussion of computational/theoretical trade-offs and when one approach is preferable.
- For acceptance: Addresses a high-impact problem with comprehensive, technically solid results that bridge optimization and high-dimensional statistics for EM.
- Against acceptance: Inference novelty is not clearly situated in prior work and practical details (initialization, tuning) and presentation of assumptions need strengthening.
