This paper introduces a modified Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm designed for scenarios with sparse, high-dimensional parameters. The authors establish convergence conditions to a global solution and characterize the convergence rate. Furthermore, they develop a statistical testing framework to assess the sparsity of the resulting estimator.

The manuscript is well-structured and provides a sufficient overview of relevant prior work. However, the presentation is quite dense, and certain sections – particularly sections 3 and 4 – present challenges in terms of readability. Increased clarity and simplification would benefit a wider audience.

I have several specific questions: First, the authors claim minimax optimality with a rate of sqrt(s*log(d/n)) on line 66, but this claim is not substantiated within the main text (I have not reviewed the supplementary materials). A clear demonstration of this result would be valuable. Second, in Eq.(3.8), the estimate ^s is dependent on the unknown true sparsity level, *s*. Guidance on practical selection of ^s is needed. Additionally, the requirement for an initial value beta^init satisfying ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 (line 275) raises a practical concern – how can such an initial value be determined in real-world applications? Finally, the purpose of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is unclear. What are the consequences of omitting this step from the proposed EM algorithm?

**Post-Rebuttal:**

The authors’ response adequately addressed my concerns, leading me to revise my score from 6 to 7. This is a statistically sound contribution, though its accessibility to researchers primarily focused on machine learning could be improved. The practical utility of the method for large-scale, real-world applications remains somewhat uncertain.
