- Proposes sparse EM with iterative truncation and a decorrelated score for testing; proves geometric convergence to the true parameter up to a statistical error scaling with sqrt(s) and the infinity-norm M-step error (log factor).  
- Extends low-dimensional EM theory under smoothness and quadraticity of the Q-function within a basin of attraction, adding a new requirement to control the M-step error in infinity norm.  
- Authors argue M-step infinity-norm control is feasible for sparse vectors in examples (Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture regression).  
- Major concern: unclear whether iterative truncation is necessary—ordinary EM followed by a single truncation might suffice.  
- Analysis does not show iterative truncation provides a fundamental statistical or computational advantage over EM + final truncation.  
- Linear regression example supports the view that final truncation suffices, weakening the paper’s central claim.  
- Related work discussion is underdeveloped; should engage sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al. 2009) and compare to Lasso/thresholding-based sparse EM methods.  
- Experiments omit crucial initialization details, compromising reproducibility and whether basin-of-attraction assumptions hold in high dimensions.  
- The decorrelated score is a useful technical contribution, but its dependence on or benefit from the sparse EM procedure is unclear.  
- Strength: solid geometric convergence guarantee for high-dimensional settings; Weakness: necessity and novelty of iterative truncation not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Minor issues: missing experimental initialization, incomplete related work, and unclear linkage between decorrelated score and sparse EM.  
- Recommendations: theoretically and empirically compare sparse EM vs EM+final truncation, clarify why iterative truncation is required, specify initialization procedures, and expand related work.  
- Overall: marginal contribution unless a clear, demonstrable advantage of iterative truncation over simpler alternatives is presented.
