- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation (T-) step enforcing sparsity and proving geometric convergence to near-optimal statistical rate O(√(s* log d / n)).  
- Introduces a decorrelated score test for low-dimensional components that attains semiparametric efficiency.  
- First framework providing simultaneous computational and statistical guarantees for high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- The T-step is a clever technical innovation enabling analysis of sparsity along the solution path.  
- Demonstrates applicability to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models with conditions verified in the appendix.  
- Main theorems decompose estimation error into optimization and statistical components, achieving near-minimax rates.  
- Algorithms and the decorrelated score test are clearly presented and well-motivated (Algorithms 1–3, Equations 2.5–2.7).  
- Paper is technically sound, well-structured, and provides rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Lacks discussion of practical initialization strategies to obtain required warm-starts (||β^init − β*||/||β*|| ≤ ε).  
- No practical guidance comparing the two M-step implementations (exact maximization vs. gradient ascent) in terms of complexity or empirical performance.  
- Omits discussion on selecting tuning parameters (ŝ and λ) and sensitivity; needs data-dependent selection methods (e.g., CV or stability selection).  
- The relation to Ning & Liu [27] is unclear; authors should explicitly state novel technical challenges in adapting the decorrelated score method to latent variable models.  
- Main paper should briefly state whether model conditions hold for the showcased examples rather than leaving verification only to the appendix.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strong theoretical contribution with suggested clarifications to improve practical relevance and novelty exposition.
