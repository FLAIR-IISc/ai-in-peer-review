This paper proposes a modification of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm tailored for high-dimensional problems where the true parameter is sparse. The authors establish conditions for the algorithm's convergence to the global optimum and derive its rate of convergence. Additionally, they develop a statistical framework to test the sparsity of the resulting estimator.

The paper is well-organized with a sufficient summary of related work. However, the technical exposition is very dense, and some parts (mainly Sections 3 and 4) are difficult to follow. The paper would benefit significantly from greater clarity in these sections.

- The authors claim (line 66) that the rate sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal, but a proof is not provided in the main manuscript. While this may be in the supplementary material (which I did not review), including a brief justification or a clear citation in the main text would strengthen the paper.

- The practical implementation of the algorithm raises a few questions. In Eq. (3.8), the parameter ŝ depends on the true sparsity s, which is unknown in practice. How should ŝ be chosen? Furthermore, the convergence guarantee requires an initial value β_init satisfying ||β_init - β*||_2 < R/2 (line 275). Could the authors provide guidance on how to find a suitable initialization in practice?

- The importance of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not clearly explained. Could the authors elaborate on its role and discuss the consequences of omitting this step from their proposed EM algorithm?
