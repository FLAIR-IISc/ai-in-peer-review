- Paper proposes a high-dimensional EM with hard-thresholding that attains geometric convergence and near-minimax estimation rates, plus a decorrelated-score inference procedure that is asymptotically normal and semiparametrically efficient, demonstrated on Gaussian mixtures and mixture regressions.

- Strength — significance: closes an important gap between practical EM use and theoretical guarantees in high-dimensional settings by providing unified estimation and inference results.

- Strength — technical quality: proves geometric convergence and near-optimal sqrt(s·log d / n) estimation rate for a nonconvex procedure and develops an asymptotically normal, efficient inferential method.

- Strength — generality: framework is presented generally and validated on multiple latent-variable models, suggesting broad applicability.

- Weakness — novelty/connection to prior work: needs clearer articulation of unique technical contributions beyond existing decorrelation/inference frameworks (e.g., Ning & Liu 2017), especially how those methods were adapted to the nonconvex, local-optimum EM output.

- Weakness — initialization: theory requires a good initializer; authors should describe practical procedures (e.g., spectral methods) and whether they satisfy the required radius.

- Weakness — choice of M-step: paper should provide practical guidance and trade-offs between exact maximization and gradient-ascent M-steps (computational vs statistical aspects).

- Weakness — tuning parameters: selection of sparsity level bs and Dantzig lambda is not discussed; need principled, data-driven selection methods and sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness — clarity of assumptions: high-level conditions should be accompanied by intuition about their reasonableness and scope across latent-variable models.

- Weakness — paper organization: main text is high-level; include key proof ideas or condensed technical insights from the appendix to better illustrate challenges and novel solutions.
