- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector each iteration to enforce a target sparsity.
- Introduces an asymptotically normal decorrelated-score test for assessing whether a specific parameter entry is non-zero.
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse EM iterates to the true parameter up to a statistical error scaling with sqrt(sparsity) and the M-step estimation error (typically log dimension).
- Builds on prior low-dimensional EM analyses and assumes smoothness and near-quadraticity of the Q-function within a basin of attraction plus initialization inside that basin.
- Adds a novel condition controlling the error in estimating the M-step specifically for sparse vectors, with verification for Gaussian mixtures and a non-gradient mixture-of-regressions variant.
- Raises the natural question whether iterative truncation is necessary versus running standard EM followed by a single truncation, with a counterargument based on linear regression where single truncation can fail.
- Suggests discussing broader sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., truncated gradient) to situate the work.
- Requests clarification of the initialization procedure used in experiments.
- Argues for acceptance based on technical soundness: rigorous analysis and convergence/inference guarantees.
- Argues for acceptance on novelty: truncation inside EM and the decorrelated score are novel contributions.
- Argues for acceptance on significance: provides a computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions.
- Argues for acceptance on clarity: paper is well-organized and clearly written with thorough technical presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance include insufficient convincing evidence that iterative truncation is essential, incomplete related-work discussion, and lack of experimental initialization details.
