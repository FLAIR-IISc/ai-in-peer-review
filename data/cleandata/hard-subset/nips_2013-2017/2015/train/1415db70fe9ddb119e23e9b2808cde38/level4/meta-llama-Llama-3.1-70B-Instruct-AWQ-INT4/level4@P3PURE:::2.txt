This paper proposes a modification of the EM algorithm tailored to high-dimensional, sparse parameter estimation. The authors establish conditions for convergence to the global solution and derive the convergence rate. Additionally, a statistical test framework is developed to verify the sparsity of the estimator.

The paper is well-organized, and the related work is adequately summarized. However, the content is dense, and certain sections (notably sections 3 and 4) are challenging to follow. Further simplification would be beneficial for readers.

Several aspects of the paper require clarification. Firstly, the authors claim in line 066 that the rate sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal, but the proof is not provided in the main manuscript (this reviewer did not consult the supplementary material). A clear proof of this statement would be appreciated.

Moreover, in Eq.(3.8), ^s depends on the unknown sparsity s. In practice, how should ^s be chosen, given that s is typically unknown? Furthermore, it is unclear how to select an appropriate initial value beta^init that satisfies the condition ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 (line 275).

The truncation step in Algorithm 1 also warrants explanation. What are the consequences of omitting this step in the EM algorithm? A clear justification for its inclusion would be helpful.

After the rebuttal, the authors' response was satisfactory, prompting an increase in the score from 6 to 7. While this is a solid statistical paper, its readability and practical usefulness for real-world machine learning applications are somewhat limited.
