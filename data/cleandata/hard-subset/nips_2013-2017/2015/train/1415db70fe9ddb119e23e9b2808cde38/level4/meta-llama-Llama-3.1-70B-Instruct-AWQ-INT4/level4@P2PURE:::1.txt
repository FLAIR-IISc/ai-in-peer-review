The paper studies Expectation-Maximization (EM) in a high-dimensional setting, establishing convergence results and developing an inference procedure. To further improve the work, the authors should discuss the conditions under which the initial solution, \beta^{\rm init}, can be found to satisfy the requirement stated in line 60 on page 2.

Two algorithms are proposed in the paper, but it is unclear what practical guidance the authors can offer on choosing between them. Additionally, the paper lacks clear guidance on how to choose the tuning parameters $\hat s$ and $\lambda$ in practice, as well as an analysis of the sensitivity of the results to these choices. In the context of high-dimensional inference, the selection of tuning parameters is crucial for achieving good sampling properties. The authors should investigate whether data-dependent methods can be used to select these parameters.

The decorrelated score test appears to be identical to the one presented in [*]. This similarity should be explicitly acknowledged and cited, rather than being overlooked. Furthermore, the authors should clarify whether any novel developments are required to apply the existing inference procedure in the current context.

The main theoretical results in the paper rely on a multitude of technical conditions. To strengthen the paper, the authors should examine whether these conditions are satisfied for the latent variable models considered in the paper.

The paper builds upon the work of [2] and applies the framework developed by Ning and Liu [] to the problem of inference in a high-dimensional setting. While the paper effectively combines existing results, it would benefit from a clearer explanation of its novel contributions and why the application of existing tools is non-trivial. The submitted paper serves as an introduction to the extensive supplementary material, but it should provide more insight into the technical challenges that were overcome to establish the main results.
