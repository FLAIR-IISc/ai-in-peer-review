This paper proposes a modification of the EM algorithm tailored to high-dimensional settings where the true parameter is sparse. The authors establish conditions for convergence to the global solution and derive convergence rates. They also develop a statistical testing framework that allows one to assess the sparsity of the estimator.

The paper is well organized, and the related work is adequately summarized. However, the presentation is very dense, and parts of Sections 3 and 4 are difficult to follow. Additional simplification would help readers.

- On line 66, the authors claim that sqrt(s·log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal. I could not find a proof of this in the main manuscript (I did not read the supplementary material). Could you provide a clear proof?
- In Eq. (3.8), the quantity \hat{s} depends on s, but s is unknown in practice. How should \hat{s} be chosen in applications? Also, how can one obtain an initial value β^init satisfying ||β^init − β*||_2 < R/2 (see line 275) in practice?
- I do not understand why the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is important. What happens if the truncation step is omitted from your EM algorithm?
