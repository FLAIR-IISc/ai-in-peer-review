- Proposes sparse EM: EM modified to truncate the parameter vector to a desired sparsity at each M-step.
- Introduces an asymptotically normal test statistic based on a decorrelated score to test if a specific parameter entry is non-zero.
- Main theoretical result: sparse EM estimate converges geometrically to the true parameter up to a statistical error term.
- Statistical error depends on sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity norm of the M-step's statistical error, which is typically only logarithmic in ambient dimension.
- Extends EM convergence guarantees from low-dimensional to high-dimensional sparse settings.
- Analysis assumes the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin of attraction and requires initialization inside that basin.
- New key assumption: infinity-norm control on the M-step estimation error (formally required only for sparse vectors).
- Practical concern: whether iterative truncation is necessary versus running standard EM then truncating once.
- Authors argue iterative truncation is necessary, citing sparse linear regression where a single final truncation is known to fail.
- Minor suggested improvements: connect to iterative hard-thresholding/truncation literature (e.g., Langford et al. 2009).
- Minor suggested improvement: explicitly state the initialization procedure used in experiments for clarity and reproducibility.
- Overall: significant theoretical contribution for high-dimensional parameter estimation, with a reasonable justification for iterative truncation though practical necessity wasn’t immediately obvious.
