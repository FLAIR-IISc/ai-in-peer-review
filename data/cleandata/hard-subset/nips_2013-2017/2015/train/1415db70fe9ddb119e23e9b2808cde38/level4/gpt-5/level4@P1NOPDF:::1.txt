This paper studies expectation–maximization (EM) in a high-dimensional setting, establishes convergence guarantees, and develops an inference procedure. The topic is important and the results are potentially useful, but several clarifications are needed.

- Initialization: Please discuss the conditions under which one can construct an initial solution, beta_init, that satisfies the requirement stated on p.2, line 60. How can such an initializer be obtained in practice, and how can a practitioner verify that it meets the stated requirement?

- Choice between the two proposed algorithms: Is there practical guidance on which algorithm to use in different scenarios? For example, are there trade-offs in terms of computational cost, numerical stability, or statistical performance that would help a practitioner decide?

- Tuning parameters: How should s_hat and lambda be chosen in practice? How sensitive are the results to these choices? Is there a data-dependent way to select them? In the high-dimensional inference literature, tuning choices are often crucial for good sampling properties, so concrete guidance (and, if possible, theoretical or empirical sensitivity analyses) would be valuable.

- Decorrelated score test: The decorrelated score test used here appears to be the same as in Ning and Liu, “A General Theory of Hypothesis Tests and Confidence Regions for Sparse High-Dimensional Models,” and should be cited explicitly. Please clarify whether any new ingredients are required to apply their inference procedure in your latent-variable/EM context, or whether it is a direct application.

- Assumptions: The main theoretical results rely on many technical conditions. Please discuss whether these assumptions are satisfied for the latent variable models you consider, and delineate the regimes under which they hold. Highlight any conditions that may be hard to verify or potentially restrictive.

- Novelty and contribution: The paper extends [2] to the high-dimensional setting and applies the results of Ning and Liu to the inference problem. While the combination is clever, the paper should more clearly articulate what is genuinely novel here and why applying these existing tools is nontrivial (i.e., what specific obstacles arise and how they are overcome). As written, the main paper reads like a “trailer” for the long supplementary material; the main text should include more of the core technical ideas and challenges required to establish the results.

Finally, please ensure consistent and explicit citation of Ning and Liu’s work where the decorrelated score is used.
