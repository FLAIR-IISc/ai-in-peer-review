The paper studies EM in a high-dimensional setting. Convergence results are established and inference procedure developed. The authors propose a novel high dimensional EM algorithm which naturally incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation, and develop a decorrelated score test for testing hypotheses related to low dimensional components of high dimensional parameters.

The paper provides a general theory of the expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm for inferring high dimensional latent variable models. The authors make two main contributions: (i) they propose a novel high dimensional EM algorithm which converges at a geometric rate and attains an estimator with the (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence, and (ii) they develop a decorrelated score test for testing hypotheses related to low dimensional components of high dimensional parameters.

The authors establish simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the proposed high dimensional EM algorithm and the respective uncertainty assessment procedure. They prove that the iterative solution sequence of the high dimensional EM algorithm converges at a geometric rate to the true parameter, and that the decorrelated score statistic is asymptotically normal.

The paper also discusses the relationship between the proposed high dimensional EM algorithm and existing work on EM algorithms. The authors note that their framework adopts the view of treating the EM algorithm as a perturbed version of gradient methods, but with a key ingredient of a truncation step that enforces the sparsity structure along the solution path.

The authors apply their framework to two high dimensional latent variable models, namely Gaussian mixture model and mixture of regression model. They establish the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions for these models.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear and concise presentation of the main results. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the technical conditions required for their results to hold, and discuss the implications of their results for high dimensional latent variable models.

However, there are some issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, the authors should discuss under what conditions can we find the initial solution, β^{\rm init} that satisfies the requirement in line 60 on page 2. Secondly, there are two algorithms proposed, and it is not clear which one to use in practice. Thirdly, the choice of the tuning parameters, $\hat s$ and λ, is not discussed in detail. The authors should provide guidance on how to choose these parameters in practice, and how sensitive the results are to the choice of these parameters.

Furthermore, the decorrelated score test is similar to the one proposed in [Ning and Liu, 2017]. The authors should cite this work and discuss how their test differs from the existing one. Additionally, the main theoretical results hold under a large number of technical conditions, and the authors should discuss whether these conditions are satisfied for the considered latent variable models.

Overall, the paper provides a significant contribution to the field of high dimensional statistics, and the authors should be commended for their work. However, some issues need to be addressed to make the paper more complete and self-contained.
