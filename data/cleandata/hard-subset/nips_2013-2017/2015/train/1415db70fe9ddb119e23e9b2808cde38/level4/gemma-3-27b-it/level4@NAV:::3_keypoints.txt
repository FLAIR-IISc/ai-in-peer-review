- Proposes a novel sparse EM algorithm that enforces sparsity on the parameter vector at each iteration.  
- Introduces an asymptotically normal statistic based on a decorrelated score to test significance of individual parameters.  
- Proves geometric (linear) convergence to the true parameter up to a statistical error term.  
- Statistical error scales with the square root of sparsity and the infinity-norm M-step error (often logarithmic in dimension).  
- Analysis builds on prior low-dimensional EM convergence results and requires smoothness, near-quadratic Q-function behavior, and good initialization.  
- Adds a new condition controlling the infinity-norm error of the M-step estimator specific to sparse settings.  
- Notes that for many models (e.g., GMM, non-gradient mixture of regression) estimating M for all vectors is not harder than for sparse vectors, raising questions about the necessity of per-iteration truncation.  
- Authors’ linear regression example argues that a single final truncation may be insufficient, supporting the truncated-EM approach.  
- Suggests adding discussion of related sparse-optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 on truncated gradient).  
- Experimental section should explicitly state the initialization strategy used.  
- Overall: valuable high-dimensional convergence guarantees, but the unique advantage of sparse EM versus standard EM with post-processing remains somewhat open.
