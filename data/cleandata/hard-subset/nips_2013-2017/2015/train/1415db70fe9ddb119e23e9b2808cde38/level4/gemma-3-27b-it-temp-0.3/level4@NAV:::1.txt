The paper investigates Expectation-Maximization (EM) within a high-dimensional framework, presenting convergence guarantees and an associated inference procedure. 

A key point for clarification: the authors should elaborate on the conditions under which an initial solution, β<sup>init</sup>, satisfying the constraint detailed in line 60 of page 2 can be reliably found.

The paper proposes two algorithms; providing practical guidance regarding which algorithm is preferable in different scenarios would be beneficial. 

Further, the practical selection of parameters  ̂s and λ requires discussion. An analysis of the sensitivity of the results to these tuning parameters is needed, alongside exploration of data-dependent methods for their selection – a crucial aspect of high-dimensional inference to ensure desirable sampling properties.

The decorrelated score test presented is identical to that found in Ning and Liu [*], and this connection should be explicitly acknowledged with appropriate citation.  It would also be valuable to detail any novel developments required to adapt existing inference procedures to the specific context of this work.

The core theoretical findings are predicated on a substantial number of technical assumptions. The authors should address the validity of these conditions when applied to the latent variable models under consideration.

This work builds upon and extends the framework of Ning and Liu [2], applying their methodology to a high-dimensional problem. The paper demonstrates a clever combination of existing results. However, a clearer articulation of the specific novel contributions and the non-triviality of applying established tools in this setting is needed. The current manuscript feels somewhat like a condensed preview of the more detailed supplementary material, and the main text would benefit from a more comprehensive presentation of the technical challenges overcome to achieve the central results. 

[*] Yang Ning, Han Liu. *A General Theory of Hypothesis Tests and Confidence Regions for Sparse High Dimensional Models.*
