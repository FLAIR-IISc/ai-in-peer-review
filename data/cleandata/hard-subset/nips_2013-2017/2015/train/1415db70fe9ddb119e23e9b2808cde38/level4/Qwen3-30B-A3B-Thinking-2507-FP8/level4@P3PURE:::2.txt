

The paper proposes a modified EM algorithm tailored for sparse, high-dimensional parameter estimation. The authors establish convergence conditions to the global solution, derive the rate of convergence, and develop a statistical test framework for assessing estimator sparsity. The manuscript is well-structured, and the related work is thoroughly summarized. However, the content is highly dense, particularly in Sections 3 and 4, which are challenging to follow; additional simplification would significantly improve accessibility.

- The minimax optimality of √(s log(d/n)) is asserted in line 066, but the proof is not provided in the main manuscript (supplementary material was not reviewed). Please clarify and provide this proof.
- In Equation (3.8), the estimator ^s depends on the unknown sparsity level s. How should ^s be selected in practice? Furthermore, how can one obtain a practical initial value β^init satisfying ||β^init − β*||₂ < R/2 (line 275)?
- The necessity of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is unclear. What specific issues arise if this step is omitted?

After the rebuttal, I am satisfied with the authors' responses and have raised my score from 6 to 7. This is a strong statistical contribution, though the paper remains challenging for machine learning researchers to digest. The practical utility of the proposed method for real-world applications also warrants further justification.
