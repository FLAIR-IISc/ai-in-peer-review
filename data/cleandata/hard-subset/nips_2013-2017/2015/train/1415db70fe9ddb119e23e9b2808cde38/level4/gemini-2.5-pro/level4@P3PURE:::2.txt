This paper proposes a modification of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm for scenarios involving sparse, high-dimensional parameters. The authors establish conditions for the algorithm's convergence to the global solution and derive its rate of convergence. Additionally, they construct a statistical testing framework to assess the sparsity of the estimator.

The paper is well-organized and provides a sufficient summary of related work. However, the content is very dense, making some sections (primarily 3 and 4) difficult to follow. Greater simplification of the exposition would be helpful for readers.

**Questions and Points for Clarification:**

1.  While the authors argue in line 066 that the rate `sqrt(s*log(d/n))` is minimax-optimal, a proof for this claim could not be found in the main manuscript. Can the authors provide a clear proof or a more direct reference for this result?

2.  The practical implementation of the algorithm raises some questions. In Equation (3.8), the parameter `^s` depends on the true sparsity `s`, which is unknown in practice. How should `^s` be chosen in a real-world setting?

3.  Similarly, the convergence analysis requires an initial value `beta^init` that satisfies the condition `||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2` (line 275). Could the authors provide practical guidance on how to find an appropriate initial value that meets this criterion?

4.  The importance of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not clearly explained. What are the theoretical or practical consequences if this step is omitted from the proposed EM algorithm?

**Post-Rebuttal Assessment:**

The authors' response was satisfactory and addressed the questions above, leading to an increased score. This is a solid statistics paper, though its dense theoretical nature may make it challenging for a machine learning researcher. Furthermore, the practical usefulness of the proposed method for real-world applications remains somewhat questionable.
