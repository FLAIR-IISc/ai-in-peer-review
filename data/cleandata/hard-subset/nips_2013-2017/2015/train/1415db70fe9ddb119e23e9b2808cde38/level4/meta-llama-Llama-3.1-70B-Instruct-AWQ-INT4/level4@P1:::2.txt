High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality

This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation for latent variable models. The authors provide a general theory of the EM algorithm, establishing simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the proposed algorithm and its applications to a broad family of latent variable models.

The paper is well-organized, and the related work is sufficiently summarized. However, the contents are dense, and some parts, mainly sections 3 and 4, are challenging to follow. More simplification would be helpful for readers.

The authors demonstrate that the proposed algorithm converges at a geometric rate and achieves an estimator with the (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence. They also propose a decorrelated score statistic for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters. The paper establishes the asymptotic normality of the decorrelated score statistic, and its limiting variance is optimal in the sense that it attains the semiparametric information bound.

One of the main contributions of this paper is the development of a general inferential theory of the EM algorithm for parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment of high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors provide a unified analytic framework that establishes simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the proposed high-dimensional EM algorithm and the respective uncertainty assessment procedure.

The paper also discusses the implications of the proposed method for real applications, including the estimation of latent variable models and the construction of confidence intervals. However, the usefulness of the proposed method for real applications is somewhat questionable, as it requires careful tuning of parameters and initialization.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation for latent variable models.
* The authors establish simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the proposed algorithm and its applications to a broad family of latent variable models.
* The paper develops a general inferential theory of the EM algorithm for parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment of high-dimensional latent variable models.

Weaknesses:

* The contents are dense, and some parts, mainly sections 3 and 4, are challenging to follow.
* The paper requires careful tuning of parameters and initialization, which may limit its usefulness for real applications.

Suggestions for improvement:

* Simplify the presentation of the technical results, especially in sections 3 and 4.
* Provide more intuition and explanations for the truncation step in Algorithm 1.
* Discuss the practical implications of the proposed method, including the choice of parameters and initialization.

Overall, this is a solid statistics paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of high-dimensional EM algorithms. However, it may not be easy to read for a machine learning researcher, and the usefulness of the proposed method for real applications is somewhat questionable.
