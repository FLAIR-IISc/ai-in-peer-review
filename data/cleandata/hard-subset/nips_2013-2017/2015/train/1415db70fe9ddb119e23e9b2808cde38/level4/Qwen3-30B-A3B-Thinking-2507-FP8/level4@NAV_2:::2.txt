

The authors propose a high-dimensional extension of the EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure for latent variable models. They establish theoretical guarantees for both parameter estimation and statistical inference in high-dimensional settings, addressing a significant gap in the literature where existing results are limited to low-dimensional regimes. Their framework provides the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions, with theoretical results showing geometric convergence rates and near-optimal statistical rates of convergence. The work builds upon recent advances in low-dimensional EM analysis [2, 18, 25, 30] but introduces novel truncation steps to handle high dimensionality, which is a critical contribution distinguishing it from prior work.

The paper is well-structured and provides a thorough review of related work, particularly highlighting how their approach differs from existing methods in high-dimensional settings. The theoretical results are comprehensive, establishing both computational and statistical guarantees for a broad family of latent variable models.

However, the technical content in Sections 3 and 4 is exceptionally dense, making it challenging for readers to follow the intricate proofs and theoretical developments. More intuitive explanations or simplified presentations of key arguments would significantly improve accessibility. 

Specific concerns include:
- The claim that the statistical rate √(s* log d/n) is minimax-optimal (line 066) lacks clear proof in the main manuscript; the reference to supplementary material is insufficient for reviewers to verify this claim without extensive additional reading.
- The sparsity parameter ^s in Equation (3.8) depends on the unknown true sparsity level s*, raising practical implementation questions. The paper does not adequately address how ^s should be selected in real-world applications where s* is unavailable.
- The paper fails to explain how to obtain an appropriate initialization satisfying ||β^init - β*||_2 < R/2 in practice (line 275), which is crucial for the convergence guarantees.
- The necessity of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not sufficiently justified; a clear explanation of the consequences of omitting this step would strengthen the theoretical foundation.

After the rebuttal, I found the authors' responses satisfactory, and I have increased my score from 6 to 7. The paper presents a solid statistical contribution with rigorous theoretical analysis, though it may be challenging for ML researchers to fully appreciate due to its technical density. The practical applicability of the proposed method for real-world applications remains somewhat questionable, as the paper does not provide extensive empirical validation or clear guidance on implementation in applied settings.
