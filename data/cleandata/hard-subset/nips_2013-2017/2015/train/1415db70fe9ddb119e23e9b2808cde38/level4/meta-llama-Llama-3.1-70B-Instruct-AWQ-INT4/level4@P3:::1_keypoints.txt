- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity and provides simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees plus uncertainty assessment.
- Two main contributions: (i) an EM algorithm with geometric convergence and near-optimal statistical rate, and (ii) an inferential procedure for hypotheses on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM results to the high-dimensional regime (d ≫ n) and leverages Ning & Liu’s framework for inference.
- Strength: establishes a general inferential framework for simultaneous estimation and uncertainty quantification in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Strength: provides a unified analytic framework and demonstrates effectiveness on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion on how to obtain an initial solution β^{init} that satisfies the required condition for algorithm convergence.
- Weakness: two algorithms are proposed but the paper gives no practical guidance or trade-offs to help choose between them.
- Weakness: tuning parameter selection (ŝ and λ) is not addressed; no practical or data-dependent selection strategy or sensitivity analysis is provided.
- Weakness: the decorrelated score test used is the same as in Ning & Liu and the paper fails to cite/discuss this or clarify what is novel in this application.
- Weakness: main theoretical results rely on numerous technical conditions; the paper should discuss whether these assumptions hold for the considered latent variable models.
- Overall quality: technically sound and a significant contribution, but needs more practical guidance and clearer exposition of novelty.
- Pro-accept points: significant contribution, unified analytic framework, and successful application to two high-dimensional latent models.
- Con-accept points: heavy dependence on initialization, lack of algorithm-selection guidance, missing tuning-parameter methodology, and limited novelty in the inference component.
