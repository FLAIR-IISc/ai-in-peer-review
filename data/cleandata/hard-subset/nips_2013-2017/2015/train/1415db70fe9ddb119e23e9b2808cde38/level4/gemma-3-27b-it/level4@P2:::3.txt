This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm and a corresponding inferential procedure for latent variable models. The core idea is to incorporate a truncation step into the standard EM algorithm to enforce sparsity, enabling statistical guarantees in high-dimensional settings where the number of parameters exceeds the sample size. The authors establish geometric convergence for the estimator and derive an asymptotically normal test statistic for low-dimensional components of the high-dimensional parameter.

This work builds upon recent advancements in understanding the convergence properties of the EM algorithm, particularly extending results previously established for the low-dimensional regime [2, 18, 25, 30]. Like prior work, the analysis relies on smoothness and near-quadraticity assumptions regarding the Q-function within a defined basin of attraction, and requires appropriate initialization. A key contribution lies in establishing statistical guarantees despite the high dimensionality, which is achieved through the sparsity-inducing truncation step. The authors demonstrate their framework's applicability to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses an important problem:** Theoretical understanding of EM in high dimensions is lacking. This work provides a significant step towards filling that gap.
*   **Novel approach:** The truncation step is a clever way to induce sparsity and enable theoretical analysis in high-dimensional settings.
*   **Strong theoretical results:** The geometric convergence guarantee and the asymptotic normality result are valuable contributions.
*   **Generality:** The framework appears applicable to a broad family of latent variable models.
*   **Clear presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly outlines the key ideas and theoretical results.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Necessity of truncation:** While the truncation step is key to the analysis, the authors’ discussion leaves open the question of whether the sparse EM is strictly *necessary* to achieve the claimed guarantees, or whether similar results might be obtained with standard EM followed by a single truncation. Further clarification on this point would be beneficial.
*   **Related work:** The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive discussion of related work in sparse optimization, particularly methods that utilize truncation or similar techniques. For instance, works like Langford et al. (2009) on truncated gradient descent might be relevant.
*   **Initialization details:** The experimental section (or a supplementary material) should explicitly detail the initialization procedure used. The sensitivity of the algorithm to initialization is a critical aspect that needs to be addressed.
*   **Statistical Error Dependency**: The statistical error term’s dependency on the infinity norm of the statistical error in the M-step is not intuitive. More clarity can be provided on this dependence and why it affects the convergence rate.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a fundamental open problem in statistical machine learning.
*   The proposed method and theoretical results are novel and significant.
*   The analysis is rigorous and the results are well-supported.
*   The framework has the potential to be widely applicable.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The necessity of the truncation step could be clarified further.
*   The related work section could be more comprehensive.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant theoretical contributions. The proposed high-dimensional EM algorithm and the associated inferential procedure represent a valuable addition to the toolkit for analyzing latent variable models. While some minor clarifications and extensions could further strengthen the paper, the current version is of high quality and merits acceptance.
