**Summary of the Paper**

The authors propose a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation for latent variable models. They establish a general theory for the EM algorithm, including convergence guarantees, statistical rates of convergence, and asymptotic normality. The authors also develop a decorrelated score test for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.

**Strengths**

* The paper provides a comprehensive treatment of the EM algorithm in high-dimensional settings, addressing both computational and statistical aspects.
* The authors establish a general framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment, which is applicable to a broad family of latent variable models.
* The paper includes a detailed analysis of the proposed algorithm, including convergence guarantees, statistical rates of convergence, and asymptotic normality.

**Weaknesses**

* The paper is dense and technical, making it challenging to follow, especially for non-experts in statistics.
* The authors do not provide a clear proof of the minimax-optimality of the sqrt(s*log(d/n)) rate in the main manuscript.
* The choice of the sparsity parameter ^s and the initial value beta^init is not clearly explained, and it is unclear how these parameters should be chosen in practice.
* The importance of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not clearly explained, and it is unclear what happens if this step is skipped.

**Originality and Significance**

* The paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation.
