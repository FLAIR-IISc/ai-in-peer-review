- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that enforces sparsity via a truncation step and a decorrelated score statistic for inference of low-dimensional components.
- Establishes simultaneous computational and statistical guarantees for parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Extends low-dimensional EM theory to high dimensions, achieving near-optimal √(s* log d / n) statistical rates under sparsity.
- Proves geometric convergence to a neighborhood within the minimax-optimal statistical error with appropriate initialization (Theorem 3.4).
- Shows the decorrelated score statistic is asymptotically normal and attains the semiparametric efficiency bound for inference (Theorem 4.6).
- Framework is general and applies to multiple models, including Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions, with careful verification of technical conditions.
- Highlights the necessity of the truncation step for both computational and statistical guarantees; ordinary EM plus a single truncation is insufficient, especially for nonconvex M-steps.
- Provides the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Technical exposition and proofs are clear and rigorous.
- Experimental section is brief and would benefit from more implementation details, especially on initialization and truncation parameter selection.
- Suggests strengthening related work by connecting to sparse optimization literature (e.g., truncated gradient methods).
- Requests clarification on whether the truncation step is required for all high-dimensional latent variable models or only specific examples.
- Assessed as highly significant for bridging computation and statistics in high-dimensional latent variable modeling.
- Meets conference quality criteria (technical soundness, clarity, originality, and significance) and is strongly recommended for acceptance.
