The paper investigates EM in a high-dimensional regime, establishes convergence guarantees, and develops an accompanying inference procedure.

The authors should clarify under what assumptions one can obtain an initial estimator β^{init} that meets the requirement stated at line 60 on page 2.

Two algorithms are proposed; is there practical guidance on when to prefer one over the other?

How should one select the tuning parameters \hat s and \lambda in practice? How sensitive are the results to these choices? Is there a data-driven procedure for selecting them? In high-dimensional inference, tuning selection is crucial for good sampling performance.

The decorrelated score test coincides with [*]. This should be explicitly cited rather than downplayed. Is any new development required to adapt that existing inference method to the present setting?

The main theorems rely on many technical conditions. The authors should discuss whether these assumptions hold for the specific latent-variable models considered.

[] A General Theory of Hypothesis Tests and Confidence Regions for Sparse High Dimensional Models. Yang Ning, Han Liu. The paper generalizes [2] to the high-dimensional setting and applies Ning and Liu [] for inference. The work synthesizes existing results in a clever way, but the precise novelty and why applying known tools is nontrivial should be explained more clearly. The main submission reads like a “trailer” for the long supplement; this trailer should include more details on the technical challenges needed to establish the central results.
