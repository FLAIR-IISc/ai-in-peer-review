- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity for latent variable models.  
- Provides a general theoretical framework establishing simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees across a broad family of latent variable models.  
- Proves geometric convergence of the algorithm and (near-)optimal statistical rate of the resulting estimator.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for inference on low-dimensional components and proves its asymptotic normality.  
- Shows the decorrelated score achieves the semiparametric information bound (optimal limiting variance).  
- Develops a unified inferential theory for parameter estimation and uncertainty quantification in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Paper is well-organized with adequate related work, but sections 3 and 4 are dense and difficult to follow.  
- Method requires careful tuning of parameters and initialization, which may limit practical usefulness.  
- Suggests simplifying technical presentation, adding intuition for the truncation step in Algorithm 1, and discussing practical parameter/initialization choices.  
- Overall a solid statistical contribution that may be hard for ML researchers to read and of uncertain practical applicability.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, strong theoretical guarantees, and general inferential theory.  
- Rejection arguments: dense, hard-to-follow sections and reliance on careful tuning/initialization.
