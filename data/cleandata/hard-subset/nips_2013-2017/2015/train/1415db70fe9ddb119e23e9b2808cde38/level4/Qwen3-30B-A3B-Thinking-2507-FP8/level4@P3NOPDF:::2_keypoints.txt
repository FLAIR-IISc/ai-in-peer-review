- Proposes a modified EM algorithm for high-dimensional sparse estimation with convergence guarantees, rates, and a sparsity test.  
- Theoretical contributions are significant and the related work is thorough; organization is generally clear.  
- Sections 3–4 are technically dense and impede accessibility; manuscript needs simplification and stronger motivation.  
- The claim of minimax optimality for rate sqrt(s log(d/n)) was asserted but not shown in the main text, undermining self-containment.  
- Practical tuning question: estimator hat{s} in Eq. 3.8 depends on unknown s and the paper gives no guidance for selecting it in applications.  
- Practical initialization issue: convergence requires an initial beta^{init} within R/2 of beta*, but no feasible strategy for obtaining such an initializer is provided.  
- Truncation step in Algorithm 1 lacked justification—unclear what fails if truncation is omitted (convergence, consistency, or sparsity loss).  
- Rebuttal resolved key theoretical concerns: minimax rate established in revision and truncation shown necessary to avoid non-sparse estimators.  
- Despite fixes, the paper remains highly technical and likely inaccessible to non-specialist NeurIPS readers; needs more intuition and examples.  
- Practical relevance is unclear: manuscript does not convincingly show why this modification outperforms existing sparse EM methods in real settings.  
- The sparsity test is useful but requires concrete demonstrations of utility for model selection or practitioner interpretation.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution; merits a 7 (Strong Accept) after revisions, but needs improved exposition and application context.
