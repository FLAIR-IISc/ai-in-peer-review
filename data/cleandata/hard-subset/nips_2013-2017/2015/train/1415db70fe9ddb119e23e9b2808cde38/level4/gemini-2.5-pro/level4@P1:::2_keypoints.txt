- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm that inserts a truncation step after E- and M-steps to enforce sparsity.  
- Proves geometric convergence to a solution achieving a (near-)minimax statistical rate in sparse high-dimensional settings.  
- Develops a decorrelated score test for asymptotic inference on individual parameters.  
- Strength: addresses a significant gap by providing unified computational and statistical guarantees for EM in d >> n regimes.  
- Strength: algorithmic idea (truncation) and simultaneous computational/statistical analysis are original and non-trivial.  
- Strength: technical results are comprehensive, covering convergence rates and asymptotic normality of the test statistic.  
- Weakness: Sections 3–4 are dense and hard to follow due to many technical conditions, assumptions, and specialized notation; needs more intuitive explanations.  
- Weakness: Practical applicability is unclear because key tuning/assumption choices may be infeasible in practice.  
- Practical issue: sparsity parameter bs in Eq. (3.8) depends on unknown true sparsity s*—guidance on selecting bs (e.g., cross-validation) is needed.  
- Practical issue: convergence requires an initialization already within R/2 of the truth—methods to obtain such an init for complex latent models should be discussed.  
- Weakness: the necessity and intuition for the truncation step should be clarified—what fails or degrades without it?  
- Weakness: claim of (near-)minimax optimality for rate sqrt(s* log d / n) should include citation or brief justification for the latent variable model class considered.
