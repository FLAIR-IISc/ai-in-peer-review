- Proposes a sparse EM that truncates the parameter vector each step to enforce a desired sparsity level.  
- Introduces an asymptotically normal test statistic based on a decorrelated score to test if a parameter entry is zero.  
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse EM to the true parameter up to a statistical error term that scales with sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity-norm M-step error.  
- Notes the statistical error term is typically only logarithmic in ambient dimension.  
- Builds on recent EM convergence results in low-dimensional settings and requires the Q-function to be smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin of attraction, with initialization inside that basin.  
- Introduces a new condition controlling the infinity norm of the M-step estimation error, tailored to sparse vectors.  
- Raises the question whether ordinary EM followed by a single truncation could achieve similar guarantees, noting the paper argues linear regression provides a counterexample.  
- Suggests adding discussion of related sparse optimization work (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 truncated gradient) for context.  
- Requests that the experimental section specify the initialization method used for sparse EM.  
- Concludes the sparse EM variant is a valuable contribution with important convergence guarantees, but the necessity and specific benefit of the sparse truncation need clearer justification and discussion.
