This paper investigates the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm within a high-dimensional framework, establishing theoretical guarantees on convergence and developing a corresponding procedure for statistical inference.

A discussion is needed regarding the conditions under which the initial solution, $\beta^{\rm init}$, can be found to meet the requirement specified in line 60 on page 2.

The paper introduces two distinct algorithms. It would benefit from practical guidance on how a practitioner should choose between them.

The manuscript should provide clarity on the practical selection of the tuning parameters $\hat s$ and $\lambda$. How sensitive are the final results to the choice of these parameters? Furthermore, is a data-driven selection method feasible? The choice of tuning parameters is a critical issue for achieving desirable sampling properties in high-dimensional inference, a point well-established in the literature.

The decorrelated score test presented appears to be identical to the one introduced by Ning and Liu [*]. This prior work must be appropriately acknowledged. The authors should explicitly detail what novel technical contributions were necessary to adapt this existing inference framework to the current problem setting.

The primary theoretical findings rely on a set of technical conditions. It would strengthen the paper to include a discussion on the extent to which these assumptions hold for the specific latent variable models under consideration.

Overall, the paper extends the work in [2] to a high-dimensional setting and leverages the inferential framework of Ning and Liu [*]. The strength of the work lies in its clever synthesis of existing methodologies. However, the paper's core novelty needs to be articulated more clearly, specifically highlighting why applying these established tools to the present context is a non-trivial endeavor. Currently, the main manuscript reads like an overview, with the substantive technical details deferred to the lengthy supplementary material. To better showcase the paper's contributions, the authors should incorporate more detail on the key technical challenges and innovations directly into the main text.
