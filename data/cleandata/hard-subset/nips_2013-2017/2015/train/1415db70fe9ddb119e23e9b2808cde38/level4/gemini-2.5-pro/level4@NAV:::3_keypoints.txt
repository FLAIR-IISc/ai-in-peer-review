- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that applies a truncation operator to the parameter vector at each iteration to enforce sparsity.
- Introduces an asymptotically normal test statistic, from a decorrelated score, for inference on whether individual parameters are non-zero.
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse EM estimator to the true parameter up to a statistical error bound.
- Characterizes the statistical error as proportional to sqrt(sparsity) times the L-infinity norm of the M-step estimation error, which is typically only logarithmic in ambient dimension.
- Analysis relies on standard EM assumptions: smoothness and near-quadratic Q-function within a basin of attraction and a suitable initialization.
- Adds a new crucial assumption requiring L-infinity norm control over the M-step estimation error for sparse vectors.
- Notes that for many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture-of-regressions) the M-step computational cost is the same for sparse and dense vectors, prompting questions about the necessity of iterative truncation.
- Raises the open question whether running standard EM and truncating only the final estimate could yield comparable guarantees; authors counterargue using linear regression as a special case.
- Recommends situating the method within the sparse optimization literature (e.g., truncated gradient methods) to strengthen related-work discussion.
- Requests that experiments explicitly report the initialization strategy to ensure reproducibility.
- Concludes that the paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to high-dimensional EM, but the essential role of iterative truncation in achieving the results remains unclear.
