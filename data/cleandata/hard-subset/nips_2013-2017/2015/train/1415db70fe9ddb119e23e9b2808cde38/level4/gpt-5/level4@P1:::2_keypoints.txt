- Proposes a truncation-augmented EM algorithm for high-dimensional sparse latent-variable models that enforces sparsity at each iterate.  
- Establishes geometric convergence to a local solution under suitable initialization and derives near-optimal statistical error rates.  
- Develops decorrelated score and Wald tests for valid asymptotic inference on low-dimensional components with high-dimensional nuisances.  
- Framework supports both exact M-step maximization and gradient-ascent implementations; illustrated on Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM analyses to the high-dimensional regime by enforcing sparsity throughout iterations and controlling entrywise statistical errors.  
- Claims estimation rates of order sqrt(s* log d / n) (up to log factors) and adapts debiasing ideas beyond convex GLMs to nonconcave latent-variable likelihoods.  
- Theoretical development is careful and technical, with explicit assumptions for EM stability, statistical error control, and concentration for the score test.  
- Results cover both optimization vs. statistical error decomposition and asymptotic normality with semiparametric efficiency for targets.  
- Paper is well organized and related work is adequately covered, but Sections 3–4 are dense and hard to follow.  
- Needs more exposition, intuitive explanations of key conditions, and practical guidance on tuning and initialization for wider ML readership.  
- The truncation-augmented EM algorithm and unified analysis for estimation and inference in high dimensions are novel and original.  
- Practical usefulness is uncertain due to sensitivity to initialization, tuning, and sparsity specification, and complexity of assumptions.  
- Request to justify minimax-optimality claim (sqrt(s* log(d/n))) with a self-contained argument or clear citation to known lower bounds.  
- Ask for practical rules to choose truncation level ŝ (cross-validation, stability selection, etc.) and discussion of sensitivity to misspecification.  
- Ask for concrete initialization strategies and diagnostics to achieve the required ||β^init − β*||_2 ≤ R/2.  
- Request clarification of why truncation is essential versus using ℓ1 penalties or no explicit sparsity—does truncation enable computational contraction, refined entrywise control, or both?  
- Suggest providing ablation studies or theoretical counterexamples to demonstrate the necessity and impact of truncation.  
- Pros: unified estimation and inference guarantees, handles exact/gradient M-steps, tackles nonconvex latent-variable settings, careful technical comparison to related work.  
- Cons: dense presentation, limited practical guidance on ŝ/tuning/initialization, unclear necessity/impact of truncation, and uncertain real-world applicability without more empirical evidence.  
- After rebuttal the reviewer raised score from 6 to 7, acknowledging the paper as a solid statistics contribution though not easy for ML researchers and with practical usefulness still somewhat questionable.
