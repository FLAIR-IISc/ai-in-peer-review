The paper studies the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models, addressing a significant gap in the literature where theoretical guarantees are lacking when the dimension exceeds the sample size. The authors propose a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating sparsity and establish convergence rates, alongside an inferential procedure for testing hypotheses about low-dimensional components. The work builds upon and extends existing results, notably [2] and [Ning & Liu], to this challenging high-dimensional regime.

The paper is well-structured, clearly presenting the methodology and theoretical results. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and highlights the contributions. The proposed algorithm is a logical extension of the classical EM, with the truncation step being a key innovation to enforce sparsity. The theoretical results—geometric convergence and optimal statistical rates—are significant and rigorously presented. The application to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models demonstrates the broad applicability of the framework. 

A potential weakness lies in the practical considerations of implementing the algorithm. Specifically, the authors should discuss under what conditions one can find an initial solution,  β<sup>init</sup>, satisfying the requirement outlined on page 2, line 60. Providing guidance on selecting appropriate initialization strategies would enhance the paper's practical value. Furthermore, the paper would benefit from a discussion on which of the two proposed maximization implementations (Algorithm 2 vs. Algorithm 3) is preferable in practice, and under what circumstances. Similarly, more discussion on choosing the sparsity parameter  bs and the tuning parameter lambda is warranted.  How sensitive are the results to these choices, and is there a data-dependent method for their selection? The importance of tuning parameter selection in high-dimensional inference is well-established, and addressing this point would strengthen the work.

The connection to the decorrelated score test from [Ning & Liu] should be explicitly acknowledged and discussed more thoroughly. While the authors apply this existing test to the specific context of the high-dimensional EM algorithm, clarifying what novel challenges arise in this application and how they are addressed would be beneficial. 

The theoretical results rely on several technical conditions. The authors should provide a more detailed discussion of whether these conditions are satisfied for the considered latent variable models—specifically, verifying these conditions for the Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models would bolster the claims.

Overall, this is a strong paper that addresses an important problem with a rigorous theoretical framework. It extends existing work in a meaningful way, combining results from [2] and [Ning & Liu] to achieve a novel contribution. While the paper currently feels like a detailed outline with substantial material deferred to the supplementary material, the core ideas are sound and the theoretical results are compelling. Addressing the points raised regarding practical implementation and clarifying the novelty beyond applying existing tools would further enhance the paper’s impact.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with convergence guarantees.
*   Optimal statistical rates of convergence established.
*   Development of an inferential procedure for testing hypotheses.
*   Rigorous theoretical analysis and clear presentation.
*   Extends existing work ([2], [Ning & Liu]) to a challenging setting.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of practical guidance on initialization and tuning parameter selection.
*   Insufficient discussion of the challenges in applying existing inference procedures.
*   Technical conditions need further justification for specific models.
*   Paper relies heavily on supplementary material, making it difficult to fully assess the contribution based on the main manuscript alone.
