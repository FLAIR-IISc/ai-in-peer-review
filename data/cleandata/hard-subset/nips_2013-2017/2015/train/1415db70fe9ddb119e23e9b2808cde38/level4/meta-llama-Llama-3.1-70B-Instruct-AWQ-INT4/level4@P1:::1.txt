The paper studies EM in a high-dimensional setting. Convergence results are established and inference procedure developed.

The authors propose a novel high dimensional EM algorithm which naturally incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation. The algorithm is built upon the E-step and M-step of the classical EM algorithm, with an additional truncation step to enforce the sparsity structure. The authors also propose a decorrelated score test for testing hypotheses related to low dimensional components of the high dimensional parameter.

The paper establishes simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the proposed high dimensional EM algorithm and the respective uncertainty assessment procedure. The authors prove that the iterative solution sequence of the high dimensional EM algorithm converges at a geometric rate and attains an estimator with the (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence.

The decorrelated score test is used for testing hypotheses related to low dimensional components of the high dimensional parameter. The authors prove that the proposed decorrelated score statistic is asymptotically normal. Moreover, its limiting variance is optimal in the sense that it attains the semiparametric information bound for the low dimensional components of interest in the presence of high dimensional nuisance parameters.

The paper applies the proposed algorithmic framework to a broad family of high dimensional latent variable models, including Gaussian mixture model and mixture of regression model. The authors establish the first computationally feasible approach for optimal parameter estimation and asymptotic inference under high dimensional settings.

However, there are several concerns and questions that need to be addressed. Firstly, the authors should discuss under what conditions can we find the initial solution, \beta^{\rm init} that satisfies the requirement in line 60 on page 2. Secondly, there are two algorithms proposed, and it is not clear which one to use in practice. Thirdly, the choice of $\hat s$ and $\lamdba$ in practice is not clear, and the authors should discuss how sensitive the results are to the choice of these tuning parameters. Finally, the decorrelated score test is the same as in [*], and the authors should cite this fact and discuss what is new in their paper.

In terms of technical conditions, the main theoretical results hold under a large number of technical conditions. The authors should discuss whether these conditions/assumptions are satisfied for the considered latent variable models.

Overall, the paper combines existing results in a clever way and extends [2] to a high-dimensional setting. However, it should be made more clear what is exactly novel in this paper and why is application of existing tools nontrivial. The submitted paper is a "trailer" for the long supplementary material, and it should contain more information on the technical challenges required to establish the main results.
