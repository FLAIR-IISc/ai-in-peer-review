

This paper proposes a modified EM algorithm for high-dimensional sparse parameter estimation, establishing convergence to the global solution under specific conditions and deriving the convergence rate. It also introduces a statistical test framework for assessing estimator sparsity. The manuscript is well-structured, with a thorough related work summary. However, the technical density—particularly in Sections 3 and 4—makes the arguments difficult to follow; additional simplification would significantly benefit readers.

Three critical points require clarification:  
1. Line 066 claims the rate √(s log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal, but this result is not derived in the main text (supplementary material was not reviewed). Could the authors provide a clear justification or reference for this claim?  
2. Equation (3.8) defines ŝ in terms of the unknown sparsity level s. How should ŝ be practically selected in real applications? Similarly, Section 3.2 (line 275) requires an initial estimate β^init satisfying ||β^init − β*||₂ < R/2. How can such an initialization be obtained in practice?  
3. The truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not justified. What specific issues arise if this step is omitted during EM iterations?  

After the authors' rebuttal, I find their responses satisfactory, and I have raised my score from 6 to 7. The paper presents a strong theoretical contribution with solid statistical foundations. However, its accessibility for ML practitioners remains limited due to the dense exposition. Furthermore, the practical utility of the method for real-world applications requires further justification.
