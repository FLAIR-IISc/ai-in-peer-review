The paper analyzes EM in high-dimensional regimes, establishing convergence guarantees and proposing an inference procedure. 

Please clarify the conditions under which one can obtain an initialization beta_init that meets the requirement stated on line 60 of page 2. What models, signal-to-noise and sample-size regimes, or algorithmic schemes ensure such an initializer is available in practice?

Two algorithmic variants are presented. Is there actionable guidance on when to prefer exact maximization versus the gradient-ascent M-step (e.g., in terms of computational cost, stability, and empirical accuracy)?

How should s_hat (the truncation level) and lambda be selected in practice, and how sensitive are the results to these choices? Is there a data-driven strategy (e.g., cross-validation, stability-based selection, information criteria, or theory-guided plug-ins) that achieves good finite-sample behavior? In high-dimensional inference, tuning is crucial for valid sampling properties.

The decorrelated score test appears to coincide with the construction in [*]. This should be explicitly cited. Beyond attribution, please clarify what additional technical work is required to adapt this inference framework to latent-variable EM estimators (which need not be global optima), and which parts are genuinely new versus direct applications of prior results.

The main theorems depend on numerous technical assumptions. Please discuss in the main text whether and how these assumptions are verified for the latent-variable models considered, and delineate the regimes (e.g., sparsity, separability, SNR, sample complexity) where they hold.
