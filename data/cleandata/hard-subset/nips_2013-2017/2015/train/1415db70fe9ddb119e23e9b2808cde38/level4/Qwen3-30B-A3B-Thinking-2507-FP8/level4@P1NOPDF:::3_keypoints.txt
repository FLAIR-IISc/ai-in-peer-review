- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that enforces sparsity by truncating the parameter vector each iteration and introduces a decorrelated-score-based asymptotically normal test statistic for individual entries.  
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse EM estimate to the true parameter up to a statistical error that scales with sqrt(sparsity) and the infinity-norm M-step estimation error (typically logarithmic in ambient dimension).  
- Analysis extends low-dimensional EM results, assuming Q-function smoothness, near-quadratic behavior in a local basin, and initialization within that basin.  
- Key novelty is requiring an infinity-norm bound on the M-step error that only needs to hold for sparse vectors.  
- For many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture regression) M-step estimation over all vectors is no harder than over sparse ones, raising doubt about the truncation’s necessity.  
- Authors’ linear regression counterexample supports the need for iterative truncation, but the paper does not conclusively show standard EM followed by one truncation would fail under the same conditions.  
- Paper should cite sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., "Sparse online learning via truncated gradient", 2009) to contextualize truncation.  
- Experiments must specify the initialization procedure used.  
- Overall: technically promising convergence results, but the fundamental necessity and role of the sparse EM procedure remain insufficiently justified.
