- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector at each iteration to enforce a desired sparsity level.
- Introduces an asymptotically normal test statistic, based on a decorrelated score, for testing whether specific parameter entries are non-zero.
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse EM iterates to the true parameter up to a statistical error term.
- The statistical error scales with the square root of the sparsity level and the infinity norm of the M-step estimation error (often logarithmic in ambient dimension).
- Analysis relies on standard assumptions: smoothness and near-quadraticity of the Q-function within a basin of attraction and initialization inside that basin.
- Novel analytical condition: need to control the infinity norm of the M-step error specifically for sparse vectors.
- Notes that for many models, estimating the M-step for all vectors is computationally comparable to estimating it only for sparse vectors (illustrated by Gaussian mixtures and a non-gradient mixture regression case).
- Raises the question whether iterative truncation is necessary, or if standard EM plus a single truncation could suffice.
- EDIT/response: linear regression as a special case provides evidence that a single truncation may be insufficient, supporting the need for truncated EM.
- Suggests citing related sparse-optimization work (e.g., Langford et al., “Sparse online learning via truncated gradient,” 2009).
- Requests clarification in experiments about the initialization strategy used.
- Concludes the paper is an important contribution to high-dimensional learning, though the necessity of the sparse EM variant remains somewhat unclear.
