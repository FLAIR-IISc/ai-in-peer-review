- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and provide the first theoretical framework for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Algorithm achieves geometric convergence to a near-optimal estimator with statistical rate O(√(s* log d / n)).  
- Introduces a decorrelated score test for low-dimensional hypotheses that attains semiparametric efficiency.  
- Framework validated on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regressions models.  
- Theoretical guarantees are comprehensive: both computational (geometric rate) and statistical (near-minimax optimal rate) are established.  
- Originality: first to jointly address optimal estimation and asymptotic inference for high-dimensional latent variable models via EM; truncation step is a novel technical contribution.  
- Significance: fills an important gap in high-dimensional latent-variable inference and can outperform tensor methods that give suboptimal rates.  
- Manuscript is very dense, especially Sections 3–4, and would benefit from more intuitive explanations and clearer main-text proofs.  
- Proof of minimax-optimality is not clearly presented in the main text and is relegated to supplementary material.  
- Practical issues: choice of sparsity parameter ŝ depends on unknown s* and is not guided for practitioners.  
- Practical issues: initialization requirement (||β^init − β*||_2 ≤ R/2) lacks concrete implementation guidance.  
- Truncation step is critical but its necessity and effect on guarantees if omitted are not sufficiently explained.  
- Empirical validation is minimal; lacks real-world case studies and comparisons to other high-dimensional inference methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong, novel theoretical contribution; general applicability; semiparametric efficiency of the score test.  
- Arguments against acceptance: excessive density hindering accessibility, insufficient practical guidance, and limited empirical evidence.  
- Overall assessment: solid and valuable theoretical contribution but hampered by dense presentation and limited practical/empirical support; reviewer recommends acceptance (score 7) contingent on clarifications and improved accessibility.
