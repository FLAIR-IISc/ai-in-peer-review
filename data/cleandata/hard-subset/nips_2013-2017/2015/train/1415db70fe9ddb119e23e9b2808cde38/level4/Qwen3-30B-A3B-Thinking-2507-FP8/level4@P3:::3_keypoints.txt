- Proposes a high-dimensional extension of EM that enforces sparsity via a truncation step at each iteration.  
- Establishes geometric convergence to a near-optimal statistical rate O(√(s* log d / n)).  
- Derives a decorrelated score statistic that is asymptotically normal and semiparametrically efficient.  
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM analysis to settings with d » n.  
- Provides the first computationally feasible approach for optimal parameter estimation in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Presents the first theoretical framework for asymptotic inference with high-dimensional nuisance parameters.  
- Develops a unified analytical framework applicable to multiple models (Gaussian mixtures, mixture of regressions).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with clear conditions (3.1–3.3) verified for specific models.  
- Proof techniques carefully handle truncation-related technical challenges and entrywise statistical error rates.  
- Paper is clearly written; algorithms and theoretical results are well-explained and reproducible via the appendix.  
- Original innovations include truncation integrated into E/M steps and the novel decorrelated score for inference.  
- Results are significant for high-dimensional latent-variable modeling and relevant applications (e.g., genomics, imaging).  
- Main weakness: limited empirical validation and lack of detailed initialization/performance comparisons.  
- Minor concerns: need to clarify when the statistical error ε decreases with n and further justify per-iteration truncation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strong, novel, and technically sound contribution.
