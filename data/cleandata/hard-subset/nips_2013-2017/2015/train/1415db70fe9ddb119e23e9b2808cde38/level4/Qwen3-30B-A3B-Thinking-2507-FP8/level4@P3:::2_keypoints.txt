- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step achieving near-optimal estimation rate p(s* log d / n).
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic enabling hypothesis testing/inference for low-dimensional components in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Provides unified theoretical guarantees for both estimation (geometric convergence, decomposition of optimization vs statistical error) and inference (asymptotic normality achieving semiparametric efficiency).
- Extends EM analysis to high-dimensional regimes (d ≫ n) by inserting sparsity-enforcing truncation in E and M steps.
- Establishes a (near-)minimax optimal rate improvement over low-dimensional results (p(s* log d / n) vs. p(d / n)).
- First computationally feasible approach for asymptotically valid inference in high-dimensional latent variable models, with rigorous proofs and well-structured conditions.
- Strong significance: applies to broad families (Gaussian mixtures, mixture of regressions) and advances both computational and statistical theory.
- Quality and originality are high, with thorough appendix proofs and insightful comparisons to prior EM and debiasing/tensor methods.
- Main weakness: presentation is dense and hard to follow; truncation step and decorrelated score test lack intuitive motivation and examples.
- Technical concern 1: claim of "near-minimax-optimal" rate lacks an explicit minimax lower bound derivation or brief justification in the main text.
- Technical concern 2: the sparsity parameter ŝ depends on unknown s* and the paper does not discuss practical selection strategies or how to obtain the required initialization.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, justify the minimax claim or cite/derive bounds, address selection of ŝ and initialization, and add intuitive explanations/examples.
