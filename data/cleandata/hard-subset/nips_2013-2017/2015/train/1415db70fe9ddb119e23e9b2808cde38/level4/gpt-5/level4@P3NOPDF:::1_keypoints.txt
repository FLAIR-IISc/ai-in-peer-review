- Summary: Studies high-dimensional EM, provides nonasymptotic convergence guarantees and an EM-based inference procedure for p ≫ n latent-variable models.

- Strength: Fills an important gap by giving theoretical guarantees and inference for high-dimensional EM.

- Strength: Gives general conditions for EM’s statistical and algorithmic guarantees with applications to latent-variable models.

- Strength: Attempts to unify convergence analysis and inference in one framework.

- Initialization concern: Results critically depend on an initial estimator β_init in the basin of attraction; need concrete conditions (signal, sample size, curvature) and provable initialization strategies.

- Initialization concern: Clarify whether multiple random starts with validation can reliably find admissible initializers and how many starts are needed at target dimensions.

- Two-algorithm guidance: Provide comparisons of per-iteration complexity, memory, typical iterations, conditions favoring each algorithm, and empirical comparisons on representative models.

- Tuning parameters ŝ and λ: Provide principled, data-driven choices (e.g., λ ≈ c·σ√(log p/n) with σ estimators), methods for choosing ŝ (CV, IC, stability selection), and sensitivity analyses for mis-specification.

- Tuning impact on inference: Quantify how incorrect ŝ/λ affect estimation, Type I error, power, and coverage/FWER under orthogonality assumptions.

- Relation to prior work: Explicitly cite and delineate novelty relative to Ning & Liu’s decorrelated-score framework and state what is new here.

- Relation to prior work: Specify nontrivial steps to adapt prior de-biasing methods (e.g., verifying Neyman orthogonality, l1-rates for EM-produced nuisance estimators, controlling E-step plug-in errors, dependence from pseudo-complete data).

- Variance estimation / sample-splitting: Clarify whether novel variance estimators or sample-splitting/cross-fitting are required or avoided, and why.

- Assumptions and scope: For each target model class, verify technical assumptions (local strong concavity, smoothness, gradient stability, contraction, sub-Gaussian tails, eigenvalue bounds) and state parameter ranges where they hold.

- Assumptions and scope: Summarize implied sample complexity, SNR thresholds, and scaling in (n,p,s) for canonical examples and compare to known bounds.

- Exposition and supplement: Move more intuition and key technical highlights into the main paper, clearly state assumptions, give high-level proof sketches, and distinguish new contributions from imported results.

- Additional suggestions: Provide a theorem with sufficient conditions and a concrete initializer with probability bounds.

- Additional suggestions: Add ablations and sensitivity analyses for tuning, model misspecification, and failure modes (outside basin, weak signals, heavy tails).

- Additional suggestions: Report empirical comparisons of the two algorithms across initializations and tuning with wall-clock times and iteration counts.

- Additional suggestions: Clarify notation early (define ŝ and λ), fix typos, and ensure complete citations to Ning & Liu and related EM/de-biasing literature.

- Overall assessment: Important and promising work that needs clearer novelty relative to de-biasing literature, concrete initialization/tuning guidance, and transparent verification of assumptions in the main text.
