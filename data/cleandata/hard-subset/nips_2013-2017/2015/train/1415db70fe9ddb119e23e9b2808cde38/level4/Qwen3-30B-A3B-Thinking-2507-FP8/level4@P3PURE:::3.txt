

The paper proposes a sparse EM procedure that enforces sparsity by truncating the parameter vector at each iteration to a specified level. It also introduces an asymptotically normal statistic based on a decorrelated score for testing whether a specific parameter entry is non-zero.  

The convergence guarantee for the sparse EM states that the estimate converges geometrically to the true parameter (in the number of iterations), up to a statistical error term scaling as √s multiplied by the infinity norm of the statistical error in the M-step, where s is the sparsity level and the M-step error typically scales logarithmically with the ambient dimension. This result builds upon recent low-dimensional EM guarantees. As in prior work, the analysis assumes the Q-function is sufficiently smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin of attraction, with initialization contained in this basin. The novel condition introduced is an infinity norm control on the error in estimating the M-step. While this condition is required only for sparse vectors, for many models (including the Gaussian mixture example and the non-gradient mixture of regression model), estimating the M-step over all vectors is no harder than over sparse vectors. This raises the question: is the truncated EM algorithm truly necessary? Could similar guarantees be achieved via standard EM under the same conditions, followed by a single truncation step? The authors' observation that linear regression constitutes a special case where this alternative approach fails provides a compelling argument against the single-truncation strategy.  

The discussion should address related work in sparse optimization, such as Langford et al. (2009) on "Sparse online learning via truncated gradient." The experimental section must specify the initialization procedure. While the convergence guarantees represent an important contribution to high-dimensional learning, the necessity of the sparse EM variant for achieving these results remains unclear. The paper's focus on sparse EM as the core mechanism for obtaining these guarantees warrants further justification.
