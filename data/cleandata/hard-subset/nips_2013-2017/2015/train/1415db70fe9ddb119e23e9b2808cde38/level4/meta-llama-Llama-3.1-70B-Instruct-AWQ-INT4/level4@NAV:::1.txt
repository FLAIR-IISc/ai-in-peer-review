The paper explores Expectation-Maximization (EM) in a high-dimensional context, providing convergence guarantees and developing an inference procedure. 

To enhance the paper's clarity, the authors should elaborate on the conditions under which an initial solution, \beta^{\rm init}, can be obtained that meets the requirement stated in line 60 on page 2.

The authors propose two distinct algorithms, but it would be beneficial to provide practical guidance on selecting the most suitable one for specific applications.

Furthermore, the choice of parameters $\hat s$ and $\lamdba$ in practice requires careful consideration. The authors should discuss the sensitivity of the results to these tuning parameters and explore the possibility of selecting them in a data-dependent manner. In the existing literature on high-dimensional inference, the choice of tuning parameters plays a crucial role in achieving desirable sampling properties.

The decorrelated score test bears a resemblance to the approach in [*], and this connection should be explicitly acknowledged. It would also be helpful to clarify whether any novel developments are necessary to adapt the existing inference procedure to the current context.

The paper's primary theoretical results rely on a multitude of technical conditions. The authors should discuss the validity of these conditions/assumptions in the context of the considered latent variable models.

While the paper extends [2] to a high-dimensional setting and applies the work of Ning and Liu [], it would be beneficial to clearly articulate the novel contributions of this paper and why the application of existing tools is non-trivial. The paper effectively combines existing results, but its relationship to prior work could be more clearly defined. The main text should provide a more detailed overview of the technical challenges involved in establishing the primary results, rather than relegating this information to the supplementary material.
