- Paper proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with an iterative truncation (T-step) to enforce sparsity and a decorrelated score statistic for inference on low-dimensional components.
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM theory to the challenging d >> n setting and frames results in a general theoretical framework.
- Main innovation is introducing and analyzing an iterative truncation step within EM iterations as an alternative to standard regularization.
- Significance/originality: fills a gap by providing the first computationally feasible method with guarantees for estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent-variable EM.
- Technical quality: proves geometric convergence (with suitable initialization) and near-minimax estimation error of sqrt(s*log d / n).
- Inference quality: decorrelated score statistic is asymptotically normal and attains the semiparametric information bound.
- Clarity: paper is well-organized, presents general framework before model-specific examples (e.g., Gaussian mixtures).
- Concern: necessity of iterative truncation is unclear—authors should justify why single-shot truncation or a standard/regularized EM would not suffice in the nonconvex EM setting.
- Literature connection: should situate the T-step within existing sparse optimization/online learning methods (iterative hard thresholding, truncated gradients, etc.).
- Initialization: theoretical guarantees require an initialization in a basin of attraction; the paper should specify practical procedures to obtain such initializations and report them in experiments.
