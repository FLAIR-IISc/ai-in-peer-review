A sparse EM procedure is proposed which modifies classical EM by truncating the parameter vector at each step to a desired sparsity level. Also proposed is an asymptotically normal statistic based on a decorrelated score for testing whether a specific entry of the parameter vector is non-zero. 

The guarantee provided for the sparse EM states that the estimate converges geometrically to the true value (in the number of iterations), up to a statistical error term which is proportional to the square root of the sparsity level and the statistical error in the M-step, which is typically logarithmic in the dimension. This builds on recent similar results for EM in the low-dimensional regime [2, 18, 25, 30]. As in prior work, the analysis assumes smoothness and near-quadraticity of the Q-function within a basin of attraction, and initialization within that basin. A novel condition concerns control of the error in estimating the M-step, specifically for sparse vectors. While the authors note this condition holds for the Gaussian mixture example and the non-gradient version of the mixture of regression model, a natural question arises: is the truncated EM algorithm truly necessary? Could a similar guarantee be achieved by performing standard EM under analogous conditions, followed by a single truncation step? The authors' point regarding linear regression, where a single truncation step would not suffice, provides a strong counterargument. 

It may be worthwhile to discuss related work in sparse optimization more broadly, such as the work of Langford et al. [Sparse online learning via truncated gradient, 2009] as a representative example. The paper would also benefit from clarification on the initialization procedure used in any experimental evaluation.

This work proposes a sparse EM variant for parameter estimation in high-dimensional settings. The convergence guarantees represent an important contribution to high-dimensional learning, though the necessity of the sparse variant for achieving these guarantees remains an open question.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a rigorous analysis of the proposed high-dimensional EM algorithm, establishing convergence guarantees and asymptotic normality results.
*   **Novelty:** The introduction of the truncation step to enforce sparsity within the EM algorithm is a novel approach to addressing challenges in high-dimensional settings. The decorrelated score statistic is also a valuable contribution.
*   **Significance:** The results provide a computationally feasible approach to optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions, which is a significant advance over existing methods.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a logical flow of ideas and a thorough presentation of the technical details.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Necessity of Truncation:** The paper doesn't fully convince the reviewer that the truncation step is essential, and a potential simplification using standard EM followed by a single truncation is not entirely dismissed.
*   **Related Work:** A more comprehensive discussion of related work in sparse optimization might strengthen the paper.
* **Initialization Details:** More details regarding the initialization procedure would be useful.
