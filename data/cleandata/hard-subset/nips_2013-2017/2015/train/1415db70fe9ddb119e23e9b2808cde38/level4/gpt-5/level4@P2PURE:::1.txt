The paper studies the EM algorithm in high-dimensional settings, establishes convergence guarantees, and develops an associated inference procedure.

- Initialization: Please clarify under what conditions one can construct an initial estimator β^{init} that meets the requirement stated on p.2, line 60. Provide concrete strategies or references for obtaining such an initializer in practice.

- Choice between the two algorithms: You propose two algorithms; please offer practical guidance on when to use which (e.g., differences in computational cost, statistical efficiency, robustness, or required assumptions).

- Tuning parameters: How should ŝ and λ be chosen in practice? How sensitive are the results to these tuning parameters? Is there a data-driven approach (e.g., cross-validation, information criteria, stability selection) that achieves the claimed guarantees? In the high-dimensional inference literature, tuning selection is crucial for good sampling properties; please address this explicitly.

- Inference procedure: The decorrelated score test appears to be the same as in [*]. This should be clearly cited rather than downplayed. Beyond adopting that test, what new technical ingredients are needed to apply the existing inference procedure in the present latent-variable/EM context?

- Assumptions: The main theoretical results rely on many technical conditions. Please discuss whether and how these conditions are verified for the latent-variable models you consider, ideally with concrete examples.

- Novelty and presentation: The paper extends [2] to the high-dimensional regime and applies Ning and Liu [*] to inference. The combination is clever, but the paper should more clearly articulate what is genuinely novel here and why applying these tools is nontrivial (i.e., what specific obstacles arise and how you overcome them). The submission reads as a “trailer” for a long supplement; the main text should include more detail on the key technical challenges and proof ideas underlying the principal results.

References:
[*] A General Theory of Hypothesis Tests and Confidence Regions for Sparse High-Dimensional Models. Yang Ning, Han Liu.
