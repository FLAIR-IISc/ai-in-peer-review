- Proposes a high-dimensional extension of EM that incorporates sparsity for latent variable models.  
- Establishes theoretical guarantees for parameter estimation and statistical inference in high dimensions, addressing a gap in prior low-dimensional results.  
- Claims first computationally feasible method for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions with geometric convergence and near-optimal statistical rates.  
- Introduces novel truncation steps to handle high dimensionality, a key technical contribution distinguishing it from prior EM analyses.  
- Paper is well-structured with a thorough related-work review and clear positioning versus existing methods.  
- Theoretical results are comprehensive, covering both computational and statistical guarantees for a broad class of models.  
- Sections 3 and 4 are technically dense and hard to follow; more intuitive explanations or simplifications are needed for accessibility.  
- The minimax-optimality claim (√(s* log d/n)) is asserted but lacks clear proof in the main text, relying on supplementary material.  
- The sparsity tuning parameter ^s depends on unknown s*, and the paper does not provide practical guidance for selecting ^s in real applications.  
- The paper does not explain how to obtain the required initialization (||β^init - β*||_2 < R/2) in practice, which is critical for convergence guarantees.  
- The necessity and consequences of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 are not sufficiently justified; more explanation is needed on what happens if it is omitted.  
- After rebuttal, reviewer found responses satisfactory and raised score from 6 to 7, but practical applicability remains uncertain due to limited empirical validation and implementation guidance.
