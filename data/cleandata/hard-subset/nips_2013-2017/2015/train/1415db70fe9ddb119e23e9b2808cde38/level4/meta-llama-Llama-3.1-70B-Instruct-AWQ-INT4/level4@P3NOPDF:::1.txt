This paper explores the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in a high-dimensional setting, establishing convergence results and developing an inference procedure. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the EM algorithm's behavior in this context.

However, there are several aspects that require further clarification. Firstly, the authors should discuss the conditions under which the initial solution, \beta^{\rm init}, can be found to satisfy the requirement stated in line 60 on page 2. This detail is crucial in understanding the applicability of the proposed method.

The paper presents two algorithms, but it is unclear what guidance the authors can offer on choosing between them in practice. Additionally, the selection of the tuning parameters, \hat s and \lamdba, is not thoroughly explained. The sensitivity of the results to these parameters and the possibility of choosing them in a data-dependent manner are essential considerations that need to be addressed. In the existing literature on high-dimensional inference, the choice of tuning parameters is critical to achieving good sampling properties.

Furthermore, the decorrelated score test appears to be identical to the one presented in [*]. The authors should properly cite this work and acknowledge its contribution. It is also essential to discuss whether any new developments are necessary to apply the existing inference procedure in the current context.

The main theoretical results rely on a large number of technical conditions. The authors should provide a clear discussion on whether these conditions are satisfied for the considered latent variable models. This analysis is vital to understanding the applicability and limitations of the proposed method.

While the paper builds upon existing results, such as [2] and [], and applies them to a high-dimensional setting, it is not entirely clear what novel contributions this work provides. The authors should explicitly state what is new and nontrivial in their application of existing tools. The submitted paper appears to be a concise summary of the main results, with the majority of the technical details relegated to the supplementary material. To improve the paper's clarity, the authors should include more information on the technical challenges required to establish the main results in the main text, rather than relying solely on the supplementary material.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting analysis of the EM algorithm in a high-dimensional setting. However, addressing the above concerns and providing more clarity on the novel contributions and technical challenges will significantly strengthen the paper.
