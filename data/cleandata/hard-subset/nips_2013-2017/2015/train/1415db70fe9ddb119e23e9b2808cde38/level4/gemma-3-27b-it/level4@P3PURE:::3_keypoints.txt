- Proposal: a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector each iteration, plus an asymptotically normal test statistic based on a decorrelated score for testing individual entries.  
- Convergence: estimator converges geometrically (in iterations) to the truth up to a statistical error term that scales as sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity-norm of the M-step error (typically log dimension).  
- Analysis assumptions: follows prior EM work—requires Q-function smoothness/near-quadraticity in a basin and initialization inside that basin.  
- New requirement: control of the infinity-norm error in the M-step estimation, specifically needed for sparse vectors.  
- Practical question: for many models (Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture of regressions) estimating M over all vectors is no harder than over sparse ones, so it’s unclear if iterative truncation is necessary vs. ordinary EM followed by one truncation; linear regression example offers a counterargument.  
- Related work: paper should discuss connections to sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 truncated gradient).  
- Experiments: the initialization procedure must be explicitly stated in the experimental section.  
- Overall: important contribution to high-dimensional learning, but the necessity of the sparse-EM variant to achieve the results is somewhat unclear.
