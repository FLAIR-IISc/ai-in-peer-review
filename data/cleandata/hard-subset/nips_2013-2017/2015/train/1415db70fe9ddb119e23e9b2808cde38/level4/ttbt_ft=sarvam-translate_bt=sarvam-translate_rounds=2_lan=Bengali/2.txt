In this paper, the authors propose a modification of the EM algorithm where the actual parameter is sparse and high-dimensional. The authors show some conditions for convergence in its global solution and determine the rate of convergence. Additionally, a statistical testing framework has been created, which is capable of testing the sparsity of the estimate.

The paper is well-organized. Relevant work is presented concisely. However, the content is very dense, and some parts (mainly, sections 3 and 4) are difficult to follow. Simplification would be helpful for readers.

- Although the authors argue in line 066 that sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal, I did not find evidence of this in the original manuscript (I did not read the supporting material). Can you prove this clearly?
- In equation (3.8), ^s depends on s, but in real-world scenarios, we do not know s. How should ^s be chosen in practice? Also, how can we find a suitable initial value beta^init such that ||beta^init - beta*||_2 is less than R/2 (see line 275)?
- I did not understand why the transaction step is important in algorithm 1. Can you explain what happens if the transaction step is removed from your EM algorithm?
