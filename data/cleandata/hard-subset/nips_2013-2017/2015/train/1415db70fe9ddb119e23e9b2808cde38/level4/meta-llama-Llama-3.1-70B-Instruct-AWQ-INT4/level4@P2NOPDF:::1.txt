This paper studies the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional settings, establishing convergence results and developing an inference procedure. To strengthen the paper, the authors should discuss the conditions under which the initial solution, \beta^{\rm init}, can be found to satisfy the requirement stated in line 60 on page 2.

The paper proposes two algorithms, but it is unclear what guidance the authors can offer for choosing between them in practice. Furthermore, the selection of tuning parameters $\hat s$ and $\lambda$ is not clearly explained. It would be beneficial to provide insight into how these parameters are chosen and how sensitive the results are to their selection. In the context of high-dimensional inference, the choice of tuning parameters is crucial for achieving good sampling properties. The authors should consider discussing whether these parameters can be chosen in a data-dependent manner.

The decorrelated score test is identical to the one presented in [*]. This connection should be explicitly acknowledged and cited, rather than being overlooked. The authors should also clarify whether any novel developments are required to apply the existing inference procedure in the current context.

The main theoretical results rely on a large number of technical conditions and assumptions. The authors should discuss the validity of these conditions for the considered latent variable models, providing insight into their practical implications.

The paper builds upon the work of Ning and Liu [] and extends the results of [2] to a high-dimensional setting. While the paper effectively combines existing results, it is essential to clearly articulate what novel contributions this paper makes and why the application of existing tools is non-trivial. The submitted paper appears to be an abstract of the supplementary material, and as such, it should provide more information on the technical challenges involved in establishing the main results.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting application of EM in high-dimensional settings, but it requires further clarification and discussion on the topics mentioned above to strengthen its contributions.
