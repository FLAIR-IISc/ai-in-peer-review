- Proposes a sparse EM procedure that truncates the parameter vector each iteration and an asymptotically normal decorrelated-score test for single-coordinate significance.
- Main theoretical guarantee: sparse EM iterates converge geometrically to the true parameter up to a statistical error scaling like sqrt(s) times the M-step l∞ estimation error (usually log-dimension dependence).
- Extends recent EM convergence results from low-dimensional to high-dimensional sparse settings.
- Analysis assumes the Q-function is smooth and approximately quadratic in a basin of attraction and requires initialization inside that basin.
- New technical requirement: l∞ control on the M-step estimation error (only needed on sparse vectors).
- Raises the question whether per-iteration truncation is necessary if M-step l∞ control already holds broadly—could a single final truncation suffice?
- EDIT: Linear regression as a special case provides a convincing argument that single truncation may be insufficient, supporting iterative truncation.
- Recommends discussing related sparse-optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., 2009) due to conceptual similarity of iterative truncation.
- Empirical section should state the initialization strategy and report sensitivity to different initializations because theory depends on basin membership.
- Overall: provides valuable high-dimensional convergence guarantees, but the necessity of truncation at every iteration remains somewhat unclear under the stated assumptions.
