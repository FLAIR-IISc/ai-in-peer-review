- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that enforces sparsity by truncating the parameter vector each iteration and an inference procedure using a decorrelated score with asymptotic normality guarantees.
- Main theory: under smoothness and local strong concavity, sparse EM iterates converge geometrically to the true parameter up to a statistical error term.
- Residual error scales with sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity-norm M-step error, which is typically logarithmic in ambient dimension.
- Extends EM convergence analyses from low-dimensional to high-dimensional sparse regimes.
- Introduces a new requirement: uniform infinity-norm control of the M-step estimation error restricted to sparse vectors.
- Questioned whether iterative truncation is necessary versus running standard EM and truncating once at the end.
- Linear regression special case supports iterative sparsification as crucial for optimization and statistical control.
- Inference contribution: decorrelated score for single-coordinate testing addresses a gap in high-dimensional latent-variable models and aligns with debiased/orthogonalized methods.
- Needs clarity on how nuisance components are estimated within EM and on conditions ensuring valid variance estimation and coverage.
- Strength: clear algorithmic idea with geometric convergence to a near-optimal statistical floor depending logarithmically on dimension.
- Strength: principled inferential procedure enabling coordinate-wise hypothesis tests.
- Request to more precisely articulate when per-iteration truncation changes the optimization trajectory versus single-shot truncation.
- Ask to clarify basin-of-attraction size, required initialization, and how initialization is performed in experiments.
- Ask for concrete M-step infinity-norm error bounds for each example, with dependence on sample size, dimension, model parameters, and uniform/high-probability statements.
- Ask how sparsity level is chosen in practice (known vs. tuned) and for sensitivity analyses.
- Related-work request: compare to truncated-gradient, proximal/hard-thresholding sparse methods and situate the inference method relative to general debiased/orthogonalized frameworks.
- Empirical-eval requests: report initialization strategy, compare standard EM vs EM+single truncation vs proposed sparse EM, show sensitivity to sparsity and sample size, and report computational overhead.
- Overall: promising and relevant with solid theoretical and inferential contributions; main open issues are necessity of iterative truncation and clearer guidance on initialization, M-step error control, and tuning.
