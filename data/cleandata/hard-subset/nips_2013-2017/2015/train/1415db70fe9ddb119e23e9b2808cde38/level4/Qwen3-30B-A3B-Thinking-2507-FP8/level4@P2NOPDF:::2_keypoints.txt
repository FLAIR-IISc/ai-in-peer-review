- Introduces a modified EM algorithm for sparse, high-dimensional parameter estimation with global convergence guarantees and derived convergence rates.  
- Proposes a statistical testing framework to assess the estimator's sparsity pattern.  
- Paper is well-structured with a thorough related work section.  
- Sections 3 and 4 are technically dense and difficult to follow; simplification would improve accessibility.  
- Concern 1: Claim that rate √(s log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal lacks an explicit minimax lower bound and matching upper bound in the main text.  
- Concern 2: Equation (3.8) defines ^s using unknown sparsity s; practical selection of ^s when s is unknown is not addressed.  
- Concern 2 (cont.): Convergence analysis requires an initial estimate β^init with ||β^init − β*||₂ < R/2, but how to obtain such an initial value in practice is unclear.  
- Concern 3: The truncation step in Algorithm 1 is critical, but the consequences of omitting truncation (theoretical/practical) are not explained.  
- After authors’ rebuttal, reviewer raised score from 6 to 7, indicating concerns were satisfactorily addressed.  
- Paper provides a strong statistical contribution with rigorous theoretical foundations.  
- Technical depth and heavy notation may limit accessibility for ML researchers.  
- Practical utility is somewhat unclear, especially regarding feasibility of required initial conditions and sparsity estimation in real-world settings.  
- Recommendation: Add clearer discussion of implementation strategies and concrete examples comparing advantages over existing methods.
