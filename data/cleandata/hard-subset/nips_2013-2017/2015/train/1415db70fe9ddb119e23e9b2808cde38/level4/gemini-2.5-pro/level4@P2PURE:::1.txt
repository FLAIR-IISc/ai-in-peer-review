This paper studies the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in a high-dimensional setting, establishing convergence results and developing a corresponding inference procedure.

A key requirement for the theoretical results is an initial solution, \beta^{\rm init}, that satisfies the condition in line 60 on page 2. The authors should discuss under what conditions such an initial solution can be obtained.

The paper proposes two distinct algorithms. It would be beneficial for the authors to provide practical guidance on how to choose between them.

A crucial aspect of the proposed method is the selection of tuning parameters $\hat s$ and $\lambda$. The authors should provide practical guidance on their selection and discuss the sensitivity of the results to this choice. Can these parameters be chosen in a data-dependent manner? In the high-dimensional inference literature, the choice of tuning parameters is of utmost importance for achieving desirable statistical properties.

The decorrelated score test employed in this work appears to be identical to the one proposed by Ning and Liu [*]. This should be explicitly cited and discussed. The authors should clarify what, if any, novel technical developments were required to adapt this existing inference procedure to the current context.

The main theoretical results are contingent on a number of technical conditions. The authors should discuss whether these assumptions are satisfied by the latent variable models considered in the paper.

In summary, this paper extends the work in [2] to a high-dimensional setting and applies the inference framework of Ning and Liu [*]. While the combination of these existing results is clever, the paper's novel contributions need to be more clearly articulated. Specifically, the authors should explain why the application of these existing tools is non-trivial. The main paper currently reads as a "trailer" for the extensive supplementary material; it would be significantly strengthened by including more details on the key technical challenges encountered and how they were overcome to establish the main results.
