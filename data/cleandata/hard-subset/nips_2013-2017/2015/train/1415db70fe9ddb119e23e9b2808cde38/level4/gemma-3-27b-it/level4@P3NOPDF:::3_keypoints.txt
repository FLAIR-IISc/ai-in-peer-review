- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates parameters each iteration to enforce sparsity, plus an asymptotically normal, decorrelated-score-based test for individual parameter nonzeros.  
- Main theoretical result: geometric convergence of the sparse EM estimate to the true parameter with a statistical error term.  
- Statistical error scales with sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity norm of the M-step error, often only logarithmic in ambient dimension.  
- Analysis builds on prior EM convergence work and requires smoothness, approximate quadratic Q-function, and initialization inside a basin of attraction.  
- Novel requirement: control of the infinity norm of the M-step estimation error, which is critical for sparsity guarantees.  
- Question raised whether iterative truncation is necessary since estimating M across all parameters can be computationally similar to sparse vectors for many models—could standard EM plus one final truncation suffice?  
- Authors counter this with the linear regression special case arguing a single truncation may be insufficient.  
- Related-work coverage is weak; should discuss sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 truncated gradient).  
- Experiments omit the initialization scheme, a crucial detail for reproducibility and understanding practical behavior.  
- Overall: important theoretical advance for high-dimensional learning, but the practical necessity of sparse EM versus post-hoc truncation and stronger related-work/experimental details remain open issues.
