- Contribution: Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with truncation that converges geometrically to an estimator with near-optimal statistical rates.  
- Contribution: Introduces a new inferential procedure for testing low-dimensional components, providing the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions.  
- Extension: Extends prior low-dimensional EM theory to high-dimensional latent variable models where sparsity is required.  
- Key technique: Uses a truncation step to enforce sparsity during iterations, enabling statistical and computational guarantees.  
- Strength: Provides a comprehensive theoretical framework that unifies estimation and inference for high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Strength: The truncation-based high-dimensional EM is a practical solution for enforcing sparsity in high-dimensional settings.  
- Strength: The decorrelated score statistic is shown to be asymptotically normal with optimal limiting variance (semiparametric information bound).  
- Strength: Framework is general and demonstrated on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models.  
- Weakness: Lacks guidance on how to obtain an initial solution β^{init} satisfying the required condition for convergence.  
- Weakness: Offers two M-step algorithms (exact maximization vs. gradient ascent) but no practical guidance on when to use each.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of tuning parameter selection (ŝ and λ), their sensitivity, and data-driven methods—crucial for high-dimensional inference.  
- Weakness: The decorrelated score test appears to rely on Ning and Liu [13], and the paper does not clearly explain the novel adaptations needed for the high-dimensional EM context.  
- Overall: Strong technical quality and clear writing, but practical implementation details and clearer articulation of novelty need more attention.  
- Significance: High — provides the first computationally feasible method for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.
