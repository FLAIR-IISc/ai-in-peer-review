

The paper proposes a sparse EM procedure that modifies classical EM by truncating the parameter vector to a desired sparsity level at each iteration. It also introduces an asymptotically normal statistic based on a decorrelated score for testing whether a specific parameter vector entry is non-zero.  

The convergence guarantee for the sparse EM estimator states geometric convergence (in iteration count) to the true parameter value, up to a statistical error term proportional to the square root of the sparsity level and the infinity norm of the statistical error in the M-step. This error is typically logarithmic in the ambient dimension. This result extends recent low-dimensional EM guarantees, relying on similar assumptions: the Q-function must be sufficiently smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin of attraction, and the algorithm must be initialized within this basin. The novel contribution is the requirement for control over the infinity norm of the error in estimating the M-step. While this condition is only necessary for sparse vectors, for many models (including the Gaussian mixture example and the non-gradient mixture of regression model considered), estimating the M-step at all vectors is as straightforward as at sparse vectors. This raises a critical question: is the truncated EM procedure truly necessary? Could a similar guarantee be achieved via standard EM under the same conditions, followed by a single truncation step? The authors' observation that linear regression constitutes a special case provides compelling evidence against the viability of a simple post-EM truncation approach.  

The discussion should incorporate related work in sparse optimization, such as Langford et al.'s "Sparse online learning via truncated gradient" (2009). Additionally, the experimental section must specify the initialization method used. While the convergence guarantees represent an important contribution to high-dimensional learning, the necessity of the sparse EM variant for achieving these results remains unclear.
