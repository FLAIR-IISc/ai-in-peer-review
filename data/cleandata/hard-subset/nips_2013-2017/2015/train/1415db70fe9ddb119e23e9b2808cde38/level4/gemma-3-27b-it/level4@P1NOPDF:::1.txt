The paper studies Expectation-Maximization (EM) in a high-dimensional setting, establishing convergence results and developing an inference procedure. 

A key question arises regarding the practical acquisition of an initial solution, $\beta^{\rm init}$, that satisfies the requirement outlined in line 60 on page 2. The authors should discuss under what conditions such an initial solution can be found.

Two algorithms are proposed. Providing practical guidance on which algorithm to use in different scenarios would be beneficial. 

Furthermore, the practical selection of $\hat{s}$ and $\lambda$ is unclear. How sensitive are the results to the choice of these tuning parameters? Is there a data-dependent method for their selection? As the choice of tuning parameters is critical for achieving good sampling properties in high-dimensional inference, this warrants further investigation.

The decorrelated score test, as presented, is identical to that found in [*]. This connection should be explicitly cited, rather than omitted.  Additionally, the authors should clarify whether any new developments were necessary to apply existing inference procedures within the current context.

The main theoretical results rely on a substantial number of technical conditions. The authors should discuss the plausibility of these conditions and assumptions holding true for the latent variable models under consideration.

This preview should contain more information regarding the technical challenges overcome to establish the main results.
