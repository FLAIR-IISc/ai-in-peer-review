- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm and a decorrelated score test for high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Develops a general theory for the EM algorithm with convergence guarantees, including geometric convergence and a (near-)optimal statistical rate.  
- Provides a framework for asymptotic inference enabling hypothesis testing and confidence interval construction via a decorrelated score statistic.  
- Paper is well-organized and contains a thorough related-work review.  
- Sections 3 and 4 are dense and may be difficult for non-experts; presentation could be simplified.  
- Main contributions are the algorithm and the decorrelated score test, with provable convergence and asymptotic normality of the test statistic.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis of both the algorithm and inference procedure.  
- Strength: empirical demonstrations on Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regression models.  
- Strength: fills a gap by providing a computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and inference in high dimensions.  
- Limitation: sparsity tuning depends on unknown true sparsity; more practical guidance on choosing it is needed.  
- Limitation: algorithm requires careful selection of the initial value; authors should discuss initialization strategies.  
- Related-work review is thorough but could better contrast advantages and limitations relative to existing methods.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution to high-dimensional statistics but may be hard for non-experts to read.  
- Review outcome: Arguments for acceptance (rigor, empirical validation, fills gap); arguments against acceptance (density, tuning/initialization issues, limited method comparisons).  
- Rating: 7; Confidence: 4.
