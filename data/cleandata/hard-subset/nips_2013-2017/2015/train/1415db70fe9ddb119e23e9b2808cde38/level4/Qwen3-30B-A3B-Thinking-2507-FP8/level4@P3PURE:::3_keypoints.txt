- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that enforces sparsity by truncating the parameter vector each iteration and introduces an asymptotically normal, decorrelated-score test for individual parameter significance.

- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse EM iterates to the true parameter up to a statistical error term scaling as √s times the infinity-norm M-step error, with M-step error typically growing logarithmically in ambient dimension.

- Analysis builds on low-dimensional EM theory and assumes the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin of attraction, requiring initialization inside that basin.

- Introduces a novel infinity-norm control condition on the M-step estimation error; this oracle is required only for sparse vectors.

- Notes that for many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures and non-gradient mixture-of-regressions), estimating the M-step over all vectors is no harder than over sparse vectors, questioning the algorithmic necessity of truncating each iteration.

- Raises the key question whether standard EM followed by a single truncation could yield similar guarantees, challenging the need for iterative truncation.

- Presents linear regression as a special-case counterexample where single-truncation fails, supporting the argument that iterative truncation may be necessary.

- Requests inclusion of related work in sparse optimization (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 truncated gradient) in the discussion.

- Experimental section lacks specification of the initialization procedure; this should be reported given the basin-of-attraction requirement.

- Concludes that while the convergence guarantees are an important contribution to high-dimensional learning, stronger justification is needed for treating sparse EM as the core mechanism for those guarantees.
