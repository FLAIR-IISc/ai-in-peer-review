The paper explores Expectation-Maximization (EM) in a high-dimensional setting, establishing convergence results and developing an inference procedure.

To improve the clarity of the paper, the authors should discuss the conditions under which the initial solution, \beta^{\rm init}, can be found to satisfy the requirement stated on line 60 of page 2. This explanation would provide valuable context for readers.

Two algorithms are proposed in the paper, but it is unclear what practical guidance the authors can offer on choosing between them. The authors should provide more insight into the selection process, as this information would be helpful for readers seeking to apply the algorithms in practice.

The choice of the tuning parameters, \hat s and \lambda, is also not clearly explained. The authors should elaborate on how these parameters are chosen in practice and how sensitive the results are to their selection. Furthermore, it would be beneficial to discuss whether there is a data-dependent way to choose these parameters. In the existing literature on high-dimensional inference, the choice of tuning parameters is crucial for obtaining good sampling properties.

The decorrelated score test is identical to the one presented in [*]. The authors should cite this fact rather than omitting it. Additionally, they should clarify whether any new developments are necessary to apply the existing inference procedure in the current context.

The main theoretical results rely on a large number of technical conditions. The authors should discuss the validity of these conditions and assumptions for the considered latent variable models, providing readers with a clearer understanding of the results' applicability.

The paper builds upon the work of [2] and applies the results of Ning and Liu [] to the problem of inference in a high-dimensional setting. While the paper cleverly combines existing results, it would benefit from a clearer explanation of its novel contributions and why the application of existing tools is non-trivial. The main paper appears to be a condensed version of the supplementary material, and as such, it should provide more information on the technical challenges overcome to establish the main results.
