

A sparse EM procedure is introduced, modifying classical EM by truncating the parameter vector to a specified sparsity level at each iteration. Additionally, an asymptotically normal test statistic is derived using a decorrelated score to assess whether a particular parameter entry is non-zero.  

The convergence guarantee for the sparse EM states that the estimate achieves geometric convergence to the true value (in iterations), up to a statistical error term scaling with √s (the sparsity level) and the infinity norm of the M-step statistical error, which is typically logarithmic in the ambient dimension. This extends recent low-dimensional EM results. As in prior work, smoothness and near-quadratic behavior of the Q function in a basin of attraction are assumed, along with initialization within this basin. The novel requirement is infinity norm control of the M-step error, which applies specifically to sparse vectors. However, for many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures and non-gradient mixture regression), estimating the M-step across all vectors is as feasible as for sparse vectors, raising the question: is the iterative truncation essential? Could similar guarantees be achieved via standard EM under identical conditions followed by a single post-iteration truncation? The authors’ observation that linear regression constitutes a special case provides compelling evidence against the single-truncation alternative.  

The discussion should reference related sparse optimization work, such as Langford et al. (2009) on sparse online learning via truncated gradient. The experimental section must clarify the initialization method. While the convergence guarantees represent a significant contribution to high-dimensional learning, the necessity of the sparse EM variant for achieving these results remains uncertain.
