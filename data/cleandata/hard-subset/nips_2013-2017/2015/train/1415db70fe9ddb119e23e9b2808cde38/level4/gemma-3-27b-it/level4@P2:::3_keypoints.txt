- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that inserts a truncation step to enforce sparsity for latent variable models.
- Establishes geometric convergence of the estimator and derives an asymptotically normal test statistic for low-dimensional components.
- Framework builds on prior EM convergence results and requires smoothness, near-quadraticity of the Q-function, and appropriate initialization.
- Demonstrates applicability to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.
- Addresses an important open problem: theoretical understanding of EM in high dimensions.
- Truncation step is a novel and effective mechanism to induce sparsity for theoretical analysis.
- Provides strong theoretical results (geometric convergence and asymptotic normality) that are valuable contributions.
- Framework appears general and broadly applicable across latent variable models.
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presents ideas and theory.
- Unclear whether the truncation step is strictly necessary versus standard EM followed by a single truncation; needs clarification.
- Related work in sparse optimization (e.g., truncated gradient methods) is insufficiently discussed and should be expanded.
- Experimental/appendix lacks explicit details on initialization and sensitivity to initialization, which is critical.
- The dependence of statistical error on the infinity norm in the M-step is unintuitive and needs clearer explanation.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a fundamental problem, offers novel methods and rigorous analysis, and has wide applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: necessity of truncation not fully justified and related work coverage is incomplete.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper with minor clarifications needed; merits acceptance.
- Reviewer suggests checking related reference: Langford et al. (2009) on truncated gradient descent.
