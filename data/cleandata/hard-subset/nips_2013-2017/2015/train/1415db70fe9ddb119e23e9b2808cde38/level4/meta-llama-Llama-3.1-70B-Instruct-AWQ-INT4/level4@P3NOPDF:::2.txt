This paper proposes a modification of the EM algorithm for sparse and high-dimensional parameters. The authors provide conditions for convergence to the global solution and derive the rate of convergence. Additionally, they construct a statistical test framework to check the sparsity of the estimator.

The paper is well-organized, and the related work is sufficiently summarized. However, the content is dense, and some sections (notably sections 3 and 4) are challenging to follow. Simplifying these sections would improve readability for readers.

The authors claim that the rate sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal (line 066), but the proof is not provided in the main manuscript. It would be beneficial to include a clear proof of this statement or provide a reference to the supplementary material where it is proven.

In Eq.(3.8), the choice of ^s depends on s, which is typically unknown in practice. The authors should provide guidance on how to choose ^s in real-world scenarios. Furthermore, it is unclear how to select an appropriate initial value beta^init that satisfies ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 (line 275). A discussion on this topic would be helpful.

The truncation step in Algorithm 1 appears to be crucial, but its importance is not clearly explained. The authors should elaborate on what happens if this step is omitted from the EM algorithm.

After the rebuttal, the authors addressed some of the concerns, which led to an increase in the score from 6 to 7. While this is a solid statistics paper, its readability for machine learning researchers could be improved. Additionally, the usefulness of the proposed method for real-world applications is somewhat questionable.
