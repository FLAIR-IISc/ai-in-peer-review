- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that enforces sparsity via a truncation step for latent variable models.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic that is asymptotically normal for low-dimensional components with high-dimensional nuisance.  
- Proves geometric convergence of the algorithm up to a statistical error term proportional to sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity-norm M-step error.  
- Shows the decorrelated score attains the semiparametric information bound (optimal limiting variance).  
- Demonstrates the framework on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models.  
- Claims simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for estimation and uncertainty assessment.  
- Strength: clear contribution to inferential theory for high-dimensional latent-variable estimation and testing.  
- Strength: potential wide applicability of the algorithm and decorrelated score in high-dimensional settings.  
- Weakness: relies on a strong assumption that the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin and requires initialization in that basin.  
- Weakness: requires infinity-norm control on M-step error only for sparse vectors, but this condition may be no easier than global control for many models.  
- Weakness: unclear necessity of iterative truncation—could ordinary EM followed by a single truncation yield similar guarantees?  
- Suggestion: add discussion of related work in sparse optimization and its relevance to the method.  
- Suggestion: report and justify the initialization used in experiments.  
- Suggestion: provide stronger justification and discussion of why truncated EM is necessary and implications for high-dimensional learning.  
- Argument for acceptance: establishes a general inferential theory and provides a novel algorithm with theoretical and practical promise.  
- Argument against acceptance: strong assumptions and unclear necessity of key algorithmic choices weaken the paper’s generality and contribution.
