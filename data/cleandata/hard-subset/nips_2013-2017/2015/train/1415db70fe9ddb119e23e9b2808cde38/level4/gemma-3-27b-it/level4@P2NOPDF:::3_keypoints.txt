- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that enforces parameter sparsity by truncating parameters each iteration and a decorrelated-score-based asymptotically normal test for individual parameter significance.
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse EM to the true parameter up to a statistical error term tied to sparsity and M-step infinity-norm error.
- Statistical error scales with the square root of sparsity and the infinity norm of the M-step error, which is often only logarithmic in ambient dimension.
- Analysis builds on prior low-dimensional EM convergence results and assumes smoothness, near-quadratic Q-function in a basin of attraction, and proper initialization.
- Novelty is an added condition controlling the infinity-norm error in the M-step estimation specific to high-dimensional sparse settings.
- Authors show that estimating the M-step error for all parameters is not much harder than for sparse parameters in examples (Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture of regressions).
- Raises the question whether full EM followed by a single final truncation could suffice, but authors argue truncated EM is necessary (analogy to linear regression).
- Suggests missing discussion of related sparse optimization work (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 on truncated gradient).
- Experimental section lacks a description of parameter initialization, hindering reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: provides a valuable theoretical convergence guarantee for sparse EM in high-dimensional learning and is a worthwhile contribution.
