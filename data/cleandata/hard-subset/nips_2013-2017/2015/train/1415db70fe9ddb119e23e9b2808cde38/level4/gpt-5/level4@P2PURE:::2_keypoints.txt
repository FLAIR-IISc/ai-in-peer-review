- Proposes a modified EM algorithm for high-dimensional problems with sparse true parameters, proving global convergence and convergence rates, and developing a statistical test for estimator sparsity.  
- Paper is well organized with adequate related-work coverage, but presentation is dense and Sections 3–4 are hard to follow; needs simplification.  
- Reviewer requests a clear proof for the claim (line 66) that sqrt(s·log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal, which is not found in the main manuscript.  
- In Eq. (3.8), \hat{s} depends on unknown s; reviewer asks how to choose \hat{s} in practice.  
- Reviewer asks how to obtain an initial β^init satisfying ||β^init − β*||_2 < R/2 in practice (line 275).  
- Reviewer questions the necessity of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 and what happens if it is omitted.  
- After rebuttal, the reviewer found the authors’ responses satisfactory and raised the score from 6 to 7.  
- Overall assessment: a solid statistics paper that is hard for ML researchers to read and whose practical usefulness for real applications is somewhat questionable.
