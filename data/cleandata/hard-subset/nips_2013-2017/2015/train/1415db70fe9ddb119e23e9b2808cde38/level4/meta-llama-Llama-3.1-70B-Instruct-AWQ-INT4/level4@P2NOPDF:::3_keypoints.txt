- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector each step to enforce a target sparsity.
- Introduces an asymptotically normal test statistic using a decorrelated score to test if a specific parameter entry is non-zero.
- Proves geometric convergence to the true parameter up to a statistical error term depending on sqrt(sparsity) and the infinity-norm M-step error (typically log dimension).
- Builds on prior low-dimensional EM analyses by assuming the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin and requiring initialization inside that basin.
- Introduces a new condition controlling the infinity-norm error in estimating M, stated to be necessary only for sparse vectors.
- Questions raised whether truncation at every step is necessary, since estimating M at all vectors can be as easy as at sparse vectors in many models.
- Notes that linear regression as a special case provides evidence against sufficiency of a single post-EM truncation.
- Suggests discussing related sparse optimization work (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 on truncated gradient).
- Requests the experimental section to specify the initialization method used.
- Acknowledges the paper’s important convergence guarantees for high-dimensional learning but finds the necessity and specific advantage of the sparse EM variant unclear.
- Recommends more discussion on the necessity of iterative truncation and the relationship to existing sparse optimization literature.
