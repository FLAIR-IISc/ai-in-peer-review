- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector each step and a decorrelated-score statistic for testing a single parameter entry.  
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse EM estimate to the truth up to a statistical error scaling with sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity-norm error in the M-step (typically log-dimensional).  
- Builds on recent low-dimensional EM analyses, assuming the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin and that initialization lies inside that basin.  
- Introduces a new condition controlling the infinity norm of the M-step estimation error, tailored to sparse vectors.  
- Notes that for many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture of regressions) estimating M at all vectors is as feasible as at sparse vectors, calling the truncation requirement into question.  
- Raises the question whether ordinary EM followed by a single truncation could achieve similar guarantees, though linear regression as a special case suggests truncation at each step may be necessary.  
- Recommends discussing related sparse-optimization work such as Langford et al. (2009) on truncated gradient.  
- Requests the experimental section to explicitly state the initialization method used.  
- Concludes the sparse EM variant and convergence guarantees are an important contribution, but the necessity of the per-iteration truncation remains unclear.
