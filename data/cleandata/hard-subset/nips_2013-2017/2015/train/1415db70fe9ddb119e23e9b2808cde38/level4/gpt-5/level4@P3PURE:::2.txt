This paper proposes a modification of the EM algorithm tailored to settings where the true parameter is sparse and high-dimensional. The authors provide conditions under which the algorithm converges to the global solution and derive the convergence rate. They also construct a statistical testing framework to assess the sparsity of the estimator.

The paper is well organized, and the related work is adequately summarized. However, the presentation is very dense, and several parts—primarily Sections 3 and 4—are difficult to follow. Additional simplification and clearer exposition would significantly improve readability.

Major comments and questions:
- Line 066: The authors state that the rate sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal. I could not find a proof or a clear justification of this claim in the main manuscript (I did not read the supplementary material). Please include a self-contained proof or a precise pointer in the main text that makes this minimax-optimality claim clear.
- Equation (3.8): The quantity ^s depends on s, yet s is unknown in practice. How should ^s be selected in real applications? In addition, how can one find a practical initialization β^init that satisfies ||β^init − β*||_2 < R/2 (see line 275)? Please provide concrete guidance or data-driven procedures for choosing ^s and for obtaining such an initialization.
- Algorithm 1 (truncation step): I did not understand why the truncation step is essential. What failure modes arise if the truncation step is omitted from your EM algorithm? Please explain the role of truncation in the theory and in practice, and clarify whether the guarantees (e.g., convergence rate or global convergence) would fail without it.

Overall assessment:
- Strengths: Solid statistical contributions with convergence analysis and a testing framework; thorough coverage of related literature.
- Weaknesses: The exposition—especially in Sections 3 and 4—is challenging to follow, making the work less accessible to ML researchers. The practical usefulness of the proposed method for real-world applications is uncertain without clearer guidance on tuning, initialization, and the necessity of truncation.
