The paper studies Expectation-Maximization (EM) in a high-dimensional setting, establishing convergence results and developing an inference procedure. 

A key question arises regarding the initialization of the EM algorithm: under what conditions can an initial solution, $\beta^{\rm init}$, satisfying the requirement in line 60 on page 2 be found? The authors should address this practical consideration.

Two algorithms are proposed, and it would be helpful to have practical guidance on which algorithm is preferable in different scenarios. Furthermore, the method relies on tuning parameters $\hat{s}$ and $\lambda$. How are these parameters chosen in practice, and how sensitive are the results to their selection? Given the importance of tuning parameter selection for achieving good sampling properties in high-dimensional inference – as demonstrated in existing literature – it would be valuable to explore data-dependent methods for choosing these values.

The decorrelated score test presented is identical to that in Ning and Liu [*]. This connection should be explicitly acknowledged with a citation, rather than being omitted.  Additionally, the authors should clarify whether any new developments were necessary to apply the existing inference procedure within the current context. 

The primary theoretical results rely on a substantial number of technical conditions. The authors should discuss the plausibility of these conditions and assumptions specifically for the latent variable models under consideration.

This paper extends the work of Ning and Liu [2] to a high-dimensional setting and applies their inference framework to this problem. The combination of existing results is clever, but the paper would benefit from a more explicit statement of the contributions: what precisely is novel in this work, and why is the application of existing tools non-trivial?  The submitted paper currently feels like a condensed overview of the extensive supplementary material; the core paper should include more detail regarding the technical challenges involved in establishing the main results. 

[*] A General Theory of Hypothesis Tests and Confidence Regions for Sparse High Dimensional Models. Yang Ning, Han Liu.
