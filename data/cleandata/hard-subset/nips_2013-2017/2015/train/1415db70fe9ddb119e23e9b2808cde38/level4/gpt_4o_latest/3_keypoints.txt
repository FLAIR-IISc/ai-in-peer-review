- Introduces a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector each iteration to enforce sparsity.
- Proposes an asymptotically normal test statistic from a decorrelated score to test if a specific parameter is non-zero.
- Proves geometric convergence of sparse EM to the true parameter up to a statistical error term.
- The statistical error scales with sqrt(sparsity) and the infinity norm of the M-step estimation error, typically logarithmic in ambient dimension.
- Assumes Q is smooth and approximately quadratic in a basin of attraction and requires initialization within that basin (similar to prior EM work).
- New requirement: control of the infinity-norm error in estimating the M operator, applied to sparse vectors.
- For many models, estimating M for all vectors is no harder than for sparse ones, raising doubts about whether sparse EM is strictly necessary.
- Question posed: could standard EM followed by a single truncation achieve similar guarantees? (Authors later counter with linear regression as a special-case objection.)
- Suggests discussing related sparse optimization work (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 truncated gradient).
- Experiments should explicitly state the initialization method; overall contribution is significant but necessity of the sparse EM variant is uncertain.
