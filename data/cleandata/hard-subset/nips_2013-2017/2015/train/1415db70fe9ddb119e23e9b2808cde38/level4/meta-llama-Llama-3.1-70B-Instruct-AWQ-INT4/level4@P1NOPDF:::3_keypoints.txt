- Proposes a sparse EM that truncates the parameter vector each iteration and a decorrelated-score-based asymptotically normal test for a single parameter entry.  
- Proves geometric convergence of the estimate to the truth up to a statistical error term scaling with sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity-norm statistical error in the M-step.  
- Statistical error in the M-step is typically only logarithmic in the ambient dimension, yielding favorable high-dimensional behavior.  
- Analysis extends recent low-dimensional EM results and assumes the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin of attraction, with initialization required inside that basin.  
- Introduces a new condition controlling the infinity-norm error in estimating the M operator, which is stated to be necessary only for sparse vectors.  
- Notes that for many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture of regressions), estimating M at all vectors is as easy as at sparse vectors, questioning the need for per-iteration truncation.  
- Raises the question whether ordinary EM followed by a single truncation could yield similar guarantees; authors argue linear regression example counters this.  
- Suggests related sparse-optimization work (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 on truncated gradient) is relevant and should be discussed.  
- Requests the experimental section to state the initialization method used.  
- Concludes that the convergence guarantees are important for high-dimensional learning, but the specific necessity and advantage of the sparse EM variant remain unclear.
