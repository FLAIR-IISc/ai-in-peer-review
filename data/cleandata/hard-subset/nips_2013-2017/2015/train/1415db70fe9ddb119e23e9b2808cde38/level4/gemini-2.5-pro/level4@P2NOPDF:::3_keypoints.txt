- Introduces a sparse EM algorithm that applies hard-thresholding after each M-step to enforce a target sparsity level.
- Proposes an asymptotically normal decorrelated score statistic for inference on individual parameters (testing non-zero coefficients).
- Main theoretical result: non-asymptotic geometric convergence of the sparse EM iterates to a neighborhood of the true sparse parameter.
- Statistical error scales with the square root of the sparsity and the infinity norm of the M-step statistical error, often yielding favorable (e.g., logarithmic) dependence on ambient dimension.
- Extends EM theory from low-dimensional to high-dimensional sparse settings under standard assumptions (smoothness, local strong concavity, good initialization).
- Introduces a key new assumption requiring infinity-norm control over the M-step’s statistical error.
- Raises a critical question about necessity of iterative truncation: could standard EM plus a single final hard-thresholding yield similar guarantees?
- Notes the authors’ linear regression example supports iterative thresholding but requests a more explicit justification comparing single-step vs iterative truncation.
- Suggests broadening related work to include iterative truncation/thresholding literature (e.g., truncated gradient methods).
- Requests details on the experimental initialization procedure given the theoretical basin-of-attraction requirement for reproducibility.
- Summary: important theoretical contribution to high-dimensional latent-variable learning, but needs stronger motivation and contextualization of the iterative truncation scheme.
