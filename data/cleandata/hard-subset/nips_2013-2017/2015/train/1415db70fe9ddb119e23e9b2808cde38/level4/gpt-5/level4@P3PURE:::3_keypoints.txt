- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector at each iteration to a prescribed sparsity level.  
- Introduces an asymptotically normal test statistic based on a decorrelated score for testing if a specific parameter entry is nonzero.  
- Main theoretical guarantee: geometric convergence to the true parameter up to a statistical error of order sqrt(s) times the ℓ∞ norm of the M-step estimation error (typically log-dimensional).  
- Analysis relies on the Q-function being smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin of attraction and requires initialization inside that basin.  
- New technical condition is ℓ∞ control of the M-step estimation error; in many models this uniform control over all parameters is as easy as for sparse vectors, questioning the need for iterative truncation.  
- Raises the question whether ordinary EM followed by a single truncation would suffice; authors argue (via linear regression special case) that a single post-EM truncation is insufficient.  
- Recommends discussing related sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 truncated gradient).  
- Experimental section should specify the initialization used.  
- Overall assessment: convergence guarantees are an important contribution to high-dimensional learning, though the necessity of the sparse EM variant itself remains somewhat unclear.
