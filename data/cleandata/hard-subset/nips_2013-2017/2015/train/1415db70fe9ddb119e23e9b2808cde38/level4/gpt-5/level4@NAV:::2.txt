In this paper, the authors introduce a variant of the EM algorithm designed for settings where the true parameter is high-dimensional and sparse. They establish conditions under which the method converges to the global solution and provide the corresponding convergence rate. They also present a statistical testing framework that enables assessing the sparsity of the estimator.

The paper is well structured, and the related literature is sufficiently covered. However, the exposition is quite dense, and some parts (notably Sections 3 and 4) are difficult to follow. Additional simplification would help readers.

- Although at line 066 the authors claim that sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal, I could not find a proof of this in the main manuscript (I did not read the supplementary material). Could you provide a clear proof?
- In Eq. (3.8), ^s depends on s, yet s is unknown in practice. How should ^s be chosen in real applications? Also, how can one obtain an initial value beta^init satisfying ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 (see line 275) in practice?
- I do not see why the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is essential. What happens if the truncation step is omitted from your EM algorithm?
