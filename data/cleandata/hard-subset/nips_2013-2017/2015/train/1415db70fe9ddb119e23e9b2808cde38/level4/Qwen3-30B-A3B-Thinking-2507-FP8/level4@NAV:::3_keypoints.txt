- Method: sparse EM truncates the parameter vector to a set sparsity level at each iteration and derives an asymptotically normal decorrelated-score test for individual nonzero entries.  
- Convergence: estimator achieves geometric (iteration-wise) convergence to the truth up to a statistical error ~ √s ⋅ ||M-step error||_∞ (typically logarithmic in ambient dimension).  
- Extension: result generalizes recent low-dimensional EM analyses.  
- Assumptions: requires Q-function smoothness and near-quadratic behavior in a basin of attraction, plus initialization inside that basin.  
- Novel technical requirement: infinity-norm control of the M-step statistical error focused on sparse vectors.  
- Question of necessity: for many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture regression) estimating the M-step over all vectors is as feasible as for sparse ones, so is iterative truncation essential?  
- Evidence against single truncation: the linear regression special case presented suggests a single post-EM truncation may not achieve the same guarantees.  
- Related work missing: should reference sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 on truncated gradient).  
- Experiments: initialization procedure must be explicitly clarified.  
- Verdict: convergence guarantees are a significant contribution to high-dimensional learning, but whether the sparse-EM variant is strictly necessary remains uncertain.
