- Strength: Proves convergence guarantees for EM in high-dimensional settings.  
- Strength: Proposes two algorithmic variants plus an associated inference procedure.  
- Strength: Attempts to leverage decorrelated-score methodology for valid post-selection inference.  

- Concern (Initialization): Results rely on a strong initializer beta_init; the paper must state concrete algorithms/conditions (spectral, method-of-moments, gradient EM, sample-splitting), sample size and SNR required to obtain it.  
- Concern (Algorithm choice): Authors should provide practical guidance and comparisons (computation, robustness, statistical efficiency, stability) to decide between the two proposed algorithms and identify regimes where one dominates.  
- Concern (Tuning): The procedure depends on s_hat and lambda; the paper must recommend data-driven selection strategies (CV, information criteria, theory-guided scaling), discuss sensitivity, and show how valid inference is retained.  
- Concern (Relation to prior work): Explicitly cite and compare to Ning & Liu’s decorrelated-score framework, clarifying what adaptations or new technical work are required for the EM/latent-variable setting.  
- Concern (Assumptions): Map technical assumptions to the studied latent-variable models, state regimes where they hold, and provide a checklist or table verifying conditions (RSC/smoothness, gradient stability, Fisher info invertibility, sparsity).  

- Novelty: Clarify which EM analysis components are new versus direct extensions, and identify the key technical bottlenecks and proof ideas that address nonconvexity and E-step errors.  
- Presentation: The main paper needs clearer intuition, highlighted technical challenges, and a compact proof sketch for central results rather than deferring to the supplement.  
- Overall assessment: Promising and timely work, but must operationalize initialization, give algorithm/tuning guidance, properly position the inference method relative to Ning–Liu, and verify assumptions for target models to strengthen impact.
