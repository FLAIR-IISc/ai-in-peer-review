In this paper, the authors propose a modification to the EM algorithm specifically designed for scenarios where the true parameter is sparse and high-dimensional. They present conditions under which the algorithm converges to the global solution and derive its rate of convergence. Furthermore, they construct a statistical test framework to evaluate the sparsity of the resulting estimator.

The paper is well organized and provides a sufficient summary of related work. However, the content is quite dense, and certain sections (particularly sections 3 and 4) are challenging to follow. Increased simplification would significantly benefit the reader.

- On line 66, the authors claim that $\sqrt{s \log(d/n)}$ is minimax-optimal, but I was unable to find a proof of this statement within the main manuscript (I did not review the supplementary material). Could you please provide a clear demonstration of this result?
- In Eq.(3.8), $\hat{s}$ depends on $s$, yet $s$ is unknown in practical applications. How should $\hat{s}$ be selected in practice? Additionally, how can one find an appropriate initial value $\beta^{init}$ that satisfies $||\beta^{init} - \beta^*||_2 < R/2$ (as stated on line 275) in a real-world setting?
- I do not understand the rationale behind the truncation step in Algorithm 1. Could you explain what would happen if we were to omit this step from your EM algorithm?
