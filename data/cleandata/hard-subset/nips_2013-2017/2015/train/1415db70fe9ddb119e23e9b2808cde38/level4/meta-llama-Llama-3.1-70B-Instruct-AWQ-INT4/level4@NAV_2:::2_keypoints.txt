- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity into parameter estimation.  
- Two main contributions: (i) an EM algorithm with geometric convergence achieving (near-)optimal statistical rates, and (ii) a new inferential procedure for low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Paper is well-organized with a thorough related-work review, but sections 3 and 4 are dense and hard to follow.  
- Authors should simplify presentation to improve accessibility.  
- Framework supports two M-step implementations: exact maximization and approximate maximization via gradient ascent.  
- Demonstrated on Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models, showing empirical effectiveness.  
- Establishes a unified analytic framework providing simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees.  
- Proves the iterative solution satisfies a high-probability error bound and that the decorrelated score statistic is asymptotically normal.  
- Claims to be the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions for the considered models.  
- Concern: the (near-)minimax-optimal rate claim lacks a proof in the main manuscript—need explicit proof or clear reference.  
- Concern: sparsity parameter bs depends on unknown true sparsity s; provide practical guidance for choosing bs.  
- Concern: requires an initializer with bounded relative error; unclear how to obtain such an init in practice.  
- Concern: the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is crucial but not well explained—need discussion of consequences if omitted.  
- Overall: solid statistical contribution, but readability and practical guidance should be improved.
