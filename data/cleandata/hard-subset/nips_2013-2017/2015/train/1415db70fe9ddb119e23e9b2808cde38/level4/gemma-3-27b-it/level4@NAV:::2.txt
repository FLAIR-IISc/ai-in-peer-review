This paper introduces a modified Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm designed for sparse, high-dimensional parameter estimation. The authors establish convergence conditions to a global solution and provide a convergence rate analysis. Furthermore, they develop a statistical testing framework to evaluate the sparsity of the resulting estimator.

The paper is logically structured and presents a comprehensive review of relevant literature. However, the presentation is quite dense, and sections 3 and 4, in particular, pose challenges for readers; increased clarity and simplification in these sections would be beneficial. 

- The authors claim minimax optimality with a rate of sqrt(s*log(d/n)) on line 66, but this result is not demonstrated within the main manuscript (and I did not examine the supplementary material). A clear proof of this claim would strengthen the paper.
- Regarding Eq.(3.8), the estimate ^s is defined in terms of the unknown true sparsity level, *s*. Guidance on practical selection of ^s is needed. Additionally, the requirement for an initial value beta^init satisfying ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 (line 275) is difficult to satisfy in practice; clarification on how to obtain such an initial value would be helpful.
- The purpose of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 remains unclear. An explanation of the consequences of omitting this step from the proposed EM algorithm would improve understanding of its necessity.
