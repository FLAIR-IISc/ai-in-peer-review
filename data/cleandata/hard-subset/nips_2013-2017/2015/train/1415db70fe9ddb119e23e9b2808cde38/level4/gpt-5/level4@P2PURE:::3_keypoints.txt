- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector each iteration to a target sparsity.
- Introduces an asymptotically normal test statistic using a decorrelated score to test whether a coordinate is nonzero.
- Proves geometric convergence of sparse EM to the true parameter up to a statistical error of order sqrt(s) times the infinity norm of the M-step error (typically log-d dimension dependence).
- Builds on recent low-dimensional EM guarantees, requiring the Q-function to be smooth and approximately quadratic in a basin of attraction and initialization inside that basin.
- New technical ingredient: infinity-norm control on the error in estimating the M-step operator for sparse vectors.
- Notes that in many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture of regressions) estimating the M-step for arbitrary vectors is no harder than for sparse ones, questioning necessity of truncation.
- Raises the natural question whether standard EM followed by a single truncation could achieve similar guarantees; authors counter with linear regression as a special-case argument against single truncation.
- Suggests discussing related sparse optimization work, e.g., “Sparse online learning via truncated gradient” (Langford et al., 2009).
- Requests that experiments clearly specify the initialization used.
- Overall: presents important convergence guarantees for high-dimensional learning, but the essentiality of the sparse EM variant remains somewhat uncertain.
