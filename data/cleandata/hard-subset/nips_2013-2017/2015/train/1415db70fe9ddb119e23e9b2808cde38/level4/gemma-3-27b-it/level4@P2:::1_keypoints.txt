- Paper studies EM in high-dimensional latent-variable models and proposes a sparsity-promoting truncated EM algorithm with convergence guarantees and inferential tools.  
- Core contribution: a high-dimensional EM algorithm that attains near‑optimal statistical error rates (scaling with sparsity s*, dimensionality d, and sample size n).  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing geometric convergence under suitable initialization and establishes optimization and statistical rates.  
- Develops a decorrelated score statistic for low-dimensional hypothesis testing in high-dimensional parameters, proving asymptotic normality.  
- Builds on and extends prior work, notably Balakrishnan et al. [2] and techniques from Ning & Liu [1].  
- Strength: addresses an important gap—theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional settings.  
- Strength: mathematically rigorous analysis leveraging recent advances in high-dimensional statistics and optimization.  
- Strength: practical relevance by providing an inferential procedure useful for hypothesis testing and confidence intervals.  
- Strength: clear connections to existing literature with appropriate acknowledgments.  
- Weakness: requires a specific initialization—paper lacks guidance on how to obtain or approximate this in practice.  
- Weakness: presents two M-step variants (Algorithms 2 & 3) without practical guidance on when to use each.  
- Weakness: tuning parameters (e.g., sparsity bs, lambda) critical but selection methods and sensitivity analysis are not provided.  
- Weakness: novelty is not fully clarified—need clearer articulation of what is new versus modular reuse of existing tools.  
- Weakness: many technical conditions are assumed; the paper should more directly verify or summarize these for the models studied (Gaussian mixture, mixture of regressions).  
- Weakness: connection to Ning & Liu’s decorrelated score work should be explicitly cited and differentiated.  
- Argument for acceptance: addresses a fundamental theoretical gap with technically sound and potentially impactful results.  
- Argument for rejection: practical issues (initialization, tuning) and heavy reliance on supplementary material limit accessibility and practical usefulness.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with a lean toward weak accept if authors address practical implementation details and clarify novelty.
