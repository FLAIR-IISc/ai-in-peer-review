- Proposes a modified EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity in high-dimensional latent-variable models.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: geometric convergence (with appropriate initialization) and near-optimal statistical rate O(sqrt(s·log(d)/n)).  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional components.  
- Clearly identifies a gap in literature and situates the contribution within related work.  
- Demonstrates strong, rigorous theoretical analysis of EM and its properties.  
- Paper is technically dense; Sections 3–4 are challenging to follow and need clearer exposition.  
- Claim of minimax optimality is asserted without explicit proof or citation in the main text.  
- Practical implementation concerns: choice of sparsity parameter \hat{s} (depends on unknown s) is not addressed.  
- Practical implementation concerns: required initialization condition (||β_init − β*||_2 < R/2) may be difficult in practice.  
- The intuitive role and necessity of the truncation step are not well explained; no illustration of outcomes without it.  
- Strength: tackles an important, timely problem in high-dimensional EM.  
- Strength: novel truncation approach and comprehensive estimation + inference framework.  
- Argument for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution with rigorous proofs and potential impact.  
- Argument for rejection: high technical density, limited accessibility, and insufficient discussion of practical applicability and optimality justification.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add minimax justification/citation, practical guidance for \hat{s} and initialization, explanation of truncation omission consequences, and more intuitive examples.
