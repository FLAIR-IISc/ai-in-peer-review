- Proposes a high-dimensional EM variant that enforces sparsity by truncating the parameter vector after each M-step.
- With good initialization and local smoothness/concavity, the algorithm converges geometrically up to a statistical error of order sqrt(s*)·ε∞ with ε∞ ≈ sqrt(log d / n).
- Framework covers both exact maximization and gradient-ascent implementations of the M-step.
- Introduces a decorrelated score (and Wald variants) for inference on low-dimensional components, yielding asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency under standard assumptions.
- Theory is demonstrated for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of linear regressions with near-minimax rates.
- Extends low-dimensional EM analyses (e.g., Balakrishnan et al.) to high dimensions by maintaining sparsity along the optimization path via truncation.
- Adapts debiasing/decorrelation techniques from high-dimensional GLMs to latent-variable, nonconvex settings.
- Clear algorithmic template (E-step, M-step, truncation) and clean decomposition of estimation error into optimization and statistical components.
- Provides explicit Lipschitz-gradient and concavity/smoothness conditions for both exact and gradient M-steps.
- Inferential theory carefully handles nuisance parameters and achieves optimal variance under a sparsity condition on the decorrelating weight.
- Questionable necessity of per-iteration truncation vs. a single post-hoc truncation; authors should state implications explicitly.
- The key ε∞ requirement is an infinity-norm control on Mn(θ)−M(θ) for s-sparse θ; clarity needed on whether truncation is essential for theory or analysis convenience.
- Main theorem is proven for a resampling/sample-splitting variant—need clarification whether identical rates hold without resampling and how constants/log factors change.
- Empirical section lacks details: initialization strategy, sensitivity to sparsity level bs and stepsize η, and comparisons to ℓ1-penalized EM and tensor methods.
- Should discuss connections to existing truncation/projection methods (e.g., truncated gradient literature) to situate the T-step.
- Technical development appears sound with clearly stated assumptions, though verifiability of the sparsity assumption on the decorrelating weight should be discussed for presented models.
- Paper is well-organized but dense; recommend collecting main assumptions and indicating where resampling is analytic only, plus detailing experimental tuning.
- Novel contribution: truncation-enabled EM for high-dimensional rates paired with an inferential procedure for nonconvex latent-variable models.
- Potentially impactful unified framework for optimal-rate estimation and valid inference in high-dimensional latent-variable models.
- Acceptance merits: strong general theory, coverage of both M-step variants, relevance to common latent-variable models; rejection concerns: need clearer justification of truncation and stronger empirical validation.
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance contingent on clarifying truncation vs. post-hoc sparsification, resampling assumptions, and strengthening experiments.
