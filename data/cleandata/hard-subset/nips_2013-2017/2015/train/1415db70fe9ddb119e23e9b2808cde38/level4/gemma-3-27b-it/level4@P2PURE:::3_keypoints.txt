- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector to a target sparsity at each iteration.  
- Introduces an asymptotically normal test statistic based on a decorrelated score for testing individual parameter entries.  
- Proves geometric convergence of sparse EM to the true parameter up to a statistical error term.  
- The statistical error scales with sqrt(sparsity) and the infinity-norm error in the M-step, typically logarithmic in ambient dimension.  
- Analysis builds on prior low-dimensional EM work and requires the Q-function to be smooth and approximately quadratic inside a basin of attraction.  
- Requires initialization within that basin of attraction.  
- Novel technical condition: must control the infinity-norm of the M-step estimation error for sparse vectors.  
- Authors note that for many models estimating the M-step across all vectors is computationally equivalent to estimating it for sparse vectors, raising questions about the necessity of iterative truncation versus single post-EM truncation.  
- Linear regression is presented as a counterargument suggesting a single truncation may be insufficient.  
- Related work should better cite sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., 2009).  
- Experiments should report the initialization method used.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution to high-dimensional learning, but the practical advantage of the sparse EM variant beyond its convergence guarantee remains unclear.
