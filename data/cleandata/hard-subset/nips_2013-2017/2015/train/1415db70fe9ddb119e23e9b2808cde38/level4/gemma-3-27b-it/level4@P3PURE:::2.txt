In this paper, the authors propose a modification of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm tailored for scenarios where the true parameter is sparse and high-dimensional. They establish conditions for convergence to a global solution and derive the rate of convergence. Furthermore, they construct a statistical test framework to assess the sparsity of the resulting estimator.

The paper is well organized and provides a sufficient summary of related work. However, the content is quite dense, and certain sections – particularly sections 3 and 4 – are difficult to follow. Greater simplification would enhance readability for a wider audience.

- On line 66, the authors claim that $\sqrt{s \log(d/n)}$ is minimax-optimal. However, I could not locate a proof of this claim within the main manuscript (I did not review the supplementary material). Could the authors provide a clear and concise demonstration of this optimality?
- In Eq.(3.8), $\hat{s}$ depends on the unknown true sparsity level *s*. How should $\hat{s}$ be selected in practical applications? Additionally, determining an appropriate initial value $\beta^{init}$ satisfying $||\beta^{init} - \beta^*||_2 < R/2$ (as stated on line 275) poses a challenge in practice. Can the authors elaborate on strategies for choosing such an initial value?
- The purpose of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is unclear. Could the authors explain the consequences of omitting this step from their EM algorithm? What behavior or issues would arise if the truncation were not implemented?
