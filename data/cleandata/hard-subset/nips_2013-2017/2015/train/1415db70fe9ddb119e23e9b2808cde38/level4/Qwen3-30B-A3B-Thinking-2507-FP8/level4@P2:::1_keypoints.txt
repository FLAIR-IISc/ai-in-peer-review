- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with truncation steps that enforces sparsity and achieves geometric convergence to a near-optimal estimator.  
- Proposes a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing of low-dimensional components, claiming asymptotic optimality.  
- Provides unified computational and statistical guarantees for estimation and inference, applied to Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions.  
- Main strength: first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Truncation enables sharper statistical rates (p(s* log d / n) versus p(d / n)), extending low-dimensional EM analysis.  
- Lacks practical implementation guidance and empirical comparison between the two M-step implementations (exact maximization vs. gradient ascent).  
- Does not adequately address selection and sensitivity of tuning parameters (sparsity \hat{s} and truncation threshold \lambda).  
- Decorrelated score test seems largely a direct application of Ning and Liu [26]; need explicit citation and clarification of novel theoretical work.  
- Relies on many technical conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5); model-specific verifications are mostly in the appendix and should be summarized in the main text.  
- Reads like a "trailer" for the supplement and insufficiently highlights the nontrivial technical challenges (e.g., truncation’s effect on the iterative path and need for entrywise error analysis).  
- Overall: theoretically significant and original, but requires clearer practical guidance, explicit novelty claims for the inference component, and better presentation of technical verifications.
