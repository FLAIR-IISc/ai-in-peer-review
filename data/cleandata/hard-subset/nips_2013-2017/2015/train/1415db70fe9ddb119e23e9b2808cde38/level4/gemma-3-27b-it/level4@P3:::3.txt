This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity, along with a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters. The work addresses a significant gap in the literature, providing theoretical guarantees for EM in the high-dimensional regime where the number of parameters exceeds the sample size.

The authors clearly summarize the limitations of existing EM theory, which primarily focuses on low-dimensional settings and global optima, and appropriately position their contribution as closing the gap between computation and statistical guarantees. They build upon recent work [2, 18, 25, 30] establishing convergence properties of EM in lower dimensions, extending these results to the high-dimensional case under sparsity assumptions. The proposed algorithm modifies the standard EM procedure by truncating the parameter estimates at each iteration, effectively promoting sparsity and enabling statistical analysis.  The paper then develops a novel inferential procedure, leveraging the estimated parameters to perform hypothesis testing with asymptotic normality guarantees. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Contributions:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical analysis, establishing geometric convergence rates for the proposed algorithm and asymptotic normality for the proposed test statistic. The achieved statistical rate of convergence (p√s\*log(d/n)) is near-optimal, addressing a key challenge in high-dimensional inference.
*   **Generality:** The framework is presented as general, applicable to a broad family of latent variable models, as illustrated by its application to Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regression models.
*   **Novelty:** The combination of a truncated EM algorithm *with* accompanying statistical guarantees, and the development of a decorrelated score statistic for high-dimensional latent variable models, is a valuable contribution.
*   **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written, with a logical flow of ideas and careful mathematical derivations. The notations are clearly defined.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Necessity of Truncation:** While the truncation step is well-motivated, the paper could more explicitly address *why* a truncated EM is crucial for obtaining the theoretical guarantees. A deeper discussion of whether similar guarantees could be achieved with standard EM followed by a single truncation step would strengthen the argument for the proposed algorithm. The authors touch on this point in the discussion of linear regression, but expanding it would be beneficial.
*   **Related Work:** The paper references related work in sparse optimization but could benefit from a more thorough discussion. Specifically, while Langford et al. (2009) isn't directly comparable, exploring the broader literature on sparse optimization techniques, particularly those relevant to iterative algorithms, might offer further context.
*   **Initialization:** The paper mentions the importance of initialization but does not detail the specific initialization strategy used in the experiments. This information is crucial for reproducibility and understanding the practical performance of the algorithm.
*   **Computational Cost:** The introduction of the truncation step introduces additional computational cost, and the paper does not explicitly address this. A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the algorithm would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant open problem in the field of latent variable modeling, providing the first theoretical guarantees for EM in the high-dimensional regime.
*   The proposed algorithm and inferential procedure are novel and offer potential improvements over existing methods.
*   The theoretical results are rigorous and well-supported by mathematical analysis.
*   The applications to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models demonstrate the practical relevance of the framework.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The necessity of the truncation step could be better justified.
*   The related work discussion could be more comprehensive.
*   The lack of details regarding initialization and computational cost limits the assessment of the algorithm’s practical applicability.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant theoretical contributions. The proposed high-dimensional EM algorithm and the associated inferential procedure represent a valuable addition to the toolkit for statistical inference in high-dimensional latent variable models. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically addressing the points raised regarding the necessity of the truncation step, a more thorough discussion of related work, and providing details on the initialization strategy used in the experiments.
