- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating sparsity and a new inferential procedure for low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.
- Two main contributions: (i) a high-dimensional EM with geometric convergence and near-optimal statistical rates, and (ii) a decorrelated score statistic achieving the semiparametric information bound.
- Extends theoretical guarantees of EM from low-dimensional to high-dimensional regimes (d ≫ n) where traditional approaches fail.
- Paper is well-structured and provides thorough comparisons with seminal and recent related work.
- Theoretical framework is comprehensive, offering both computational and statistical guarantees.
- Establishes a near-optimal estimator rate of s* log d / n, an improvement over d / n in low-dimensional settings.
- Decorrelated score statistic achieves semiparametric efficiency, a notable theoretical result.
- Framework is general and demonstrated for multiple latent variable models (Gaussian mixtures, mixture of regressions).
- Paper is technically dense (especially Sections 3–4), making it hard to follow without background in high-dimensional statistics.
- Lacks clear practical guidance for choosing the sparsity parameter ŝ and obtaining a suitable initialization β̂^init in real applications.
- The truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not sufficiently justified; effects of omitting it are unexplained.
- Initially missing a proof of the claimed minimax optimality (sqrt(s* log(d/n))), though the reviewer notes the rebuttal satisfactorily addressed this.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution, suitable for NIPS, recommended for acceptance provided practical implementation concerns and clarity are improved.
- Remaining concerns after rebuttal: readability for typical NIPS audience and uncertain practical utility for real-world applications.
