- Proposal: a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector each step to a desired sparsity and a decorrelated-score-based asymptotically normal test for entry-wise nonzero parameters.  
- Convergence result: iterates converge geometrically to the truth up to a statistical error term scaling with sqrt(sparsity) and the M-step error (typically logarithmic in dimension).  
- Assumptions: follows prior EM work—Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin of attraction and algorithm is initialized inside that basin.  
- Novelty: introduces a new condition controlling the M-step estimation error specifically for sparse vectors.  
- Criticism: for many models, estimating M at sparse vectors is not harder than at arbitrary vectors, raising doubt about the necessity of per-iteration truncation.  
- Question: could ordinary EM followed by a single truncation achieve similar guarantees? authors argue linear regression suggests single truncation may be insufficient.  
- Related work: missing discussion of sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 on truncated gradient).  
- Experimental concern: experiments should explicitly state the initialization procedure used.  
- Overall assessment: provides important high-dimensional convergence guarantees, but the need for the sparse-EM variant versus simpler alternatives remains somewhat open.
