- Presents a general theoretical framework and a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity.
- Shows parameter-estimation guarantees: with suitable initialization the algorithm has geometric convergence and produces near-optimal statistical rates.
- Proposes a decorrelated score test for inference on low-dimensional components and proves its asymptotic normality.
- Demonstrates the framework on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models.
- Extends Balakrishnan et al.’s low-dimensional EM analysis to the high-dimensional (d >> n) regime.

- Strength: tackles an important, understudied problem and provides computationally feasible algorithms with rigorous computational and statistical guarantees.
- Strength: offers a comprehensive theoretical toolkit that covers both estimation and inference for high-dimensional latent-variable models.
- Strength: novel adaptation of high-dimensional inferential techniques to an estimator arising from a non-convex, iterative procedure.

- Improvement: provide practical guidance on obtaining the required initialization (e.g., spectral or moment-based procedures) that meet theory’s radius requirement.
- Improvement: give data-driven recommendations for tuning parameter selection (sparsity level bs, Dantzig λ) and discuss sensitivity to these choices.
- Improvement: discuss practical trade-offs between the two M-step implementations (exact maximization vs. gradient ascent) and when to prefer each.
- Improvement: explicitly relate the decorrelated score test to prior high-dimensional inference work (e.g., Ning & Liu 2017) and clarify the paper’s unique technical contributions.
- Improvement: better explain the technical challenges introduced by the truncation step and analyzing an estimator not satisfying standard KKT conditions.
- Improvement: make high-level technical assumptions (Conditions 3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.4) more accessible by providing intuition on their strength and applicability to latent-variable models.
