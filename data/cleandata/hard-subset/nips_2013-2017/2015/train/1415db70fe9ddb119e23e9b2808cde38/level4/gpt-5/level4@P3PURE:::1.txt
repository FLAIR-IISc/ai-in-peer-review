This paper studies the EM algorithm in high-dimensional settings, establishes convergence results, and develops an associated inference procedure. The topic is timely and the results are potentially impactful. The paper combines existing ideas in a thoughtful way, but several clarifications and additions are needed to assess novelty, applicability, and practical relevance.

Major comments and questions

- Initialization. Please discuss the conditions under which one can find an initial solution beta_init that meets the requirement stated on line 60 of page 2. Concretely:
  - What structural, sample size, or signal strength assumptions guarantee that such an initializer exists?
  - What algorithmic procedures or references can practitioners rely on to obtain a valid beta_init in the models you study?
  - How sensitive are your convergence guarantees to deviations from this requirement?

- Choice among the two proposed algorithms. The paper presents two algorithms. Please provide practical guidance on when to use which method. For example, delineate their computational and statistical trade-offs, regimes where one dominates the other, and any differences in robustness or tuning sensitivity.

- Tuning parameters. The procedure depends on s_hat and lambda. Please address:
  - How these parameters should be chosen in practice.
  - Sensitivity of estimation, testing power, and coverage to these choices.
  - Whether there are data-dependent selection rules with theoretical support in your setting.
  In high-dimensional inference, tuning choices critically affect sampling properties; clear recommendations and, if possible, theory-backed defaults are important.

- Decorrelated score test and prior art. The decorrelated (orthogonalized) score test you use is the same as in Ning and Liu, “A General Theory of Hypothesis Tests and Confidence Regions for Sparse High-Dimensional Models,” and should be explicitly cited. Please make clear:
  - What, if anything, is novel in your adaptation to the EM/latent-variable context.
  - Whether any new technical developments are required to verify the conditions of that framework here (e.g., control of nuisance estimation error, orthogonality verification, or variance estimation), beyond a direct application of existing results.

- Assumptions and their verification in latent-variable models. The main theoretical results rely on a substantial set of technical conditions. Please discuss whether and how these assumptions are satisfied in the latent-variable models you consider. Ideally, provide explicit verification or succinct summaries (in the main text) that map each assumption to concrete properties of the models (e.g., smoothness, restricted eigenvalue or compatibility conditions, tail assumptions, identifiability, and basin-of-attraction requirements for EM).

- Novelty and relation to prior work. The paper extends your reference [2] to the high-dimensional setting and applies the inference framework of Ning and Liu. While the synthesis is clever, the paper should more clearly articulate:
  - What is genuinely new relative to [2] and to Ning and Liu.
  - Why the application of these existing tools to EM-driven latent-variable models is nontrivial (e.g., unique technical obstacles and how you overcome them).
  At present, the submission reads like a trailer for the long supplementary material; the main paper should convey the key technical challenges and ideas required to establish the results, rather than deferring most crucial arguments to the supplement.

Overall assessment

- Strengths: Timely problem; interesting attempt to merge EM analysis with modern high-dimensional inference; potentially valuable practical procedures.
- Weaknesses: Insufficient discussion of initialization and tuning; unclear practical guidance between two algorithms; incomplete credit and positioning with respect to Ning and Liu; heavy reliance on assumptions without clear verification in the targeted models; main text defers too much to the supplement.

Suggestions for improvement

- Add a dedicated subsection on initialization detailing sufficient conditions, construction methods, and sensitivity.
- Provide a practitioner-oriented comparison of the two algorithms and a brief decision guide.
- Offer concrete data-driven tuning strategies (with citations) and a sensitivity analysis.
- Explicitly cite and position the decorrelated score test relative to Ning and Liu, and state precisely what is new in your implementation or theory.
- Move summaries of assumption verification and key proof ideas from the supplement into the main text to substantiate the claimed contributions and clarify the nontrivial aspects of your analysis.
