In this paper, the authors propose a modification to the EM algorithm for situations where the actual parameter is sparse and high-dimensional. They have outlined some conditions for its convergence in global solutions and derived the rate of convergence. Additionally, a statistical testing framework has been created that enables the testing of the estimator's sparsity.

The paper is well-organized. The relevant work is adequately summarized. However, the content is very dense, and some parts (mainly sections 3 and 4) are difficult to understand. Greater simplification would be helpful for readers.

- Although the authors argue in line 066 that sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal, I did not find evidence of this in the main manuscript (I did not read the supplementary material). Can you prove it clearly?
- In equation (3.8), ^s depends on s, but we do not know s in real-world situations. How should ^s be chosen in practice? Also, how can we find a suitable initial value beta^init that satisfies ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 (see line 275)?
- I did not understand why the truncation step is important in algorithm 1. Can you explain what happens if we skip the truncation step in your EM algorithm?
