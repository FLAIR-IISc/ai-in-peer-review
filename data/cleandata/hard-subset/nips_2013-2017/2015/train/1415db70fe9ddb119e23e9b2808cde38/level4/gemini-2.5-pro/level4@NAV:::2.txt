This paper introduces a variant of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm tailored for scenarios involving sparse and high-dimensional parameters. The authors establish the conditions under which their method converges to the global optimum and determine its rate of convergence. Furthermore, they develop a statistical testing framework to assess the sparsity of the resulting estimator.

The manuscript is well-structured, and the discussion of related work is comprehensive. However, the content is presented in a highly condensed manner, which makes certain sections, notably sections 3 and 4, difficult to follow. The paper would be more accessible to a broader audience if these parts were simplified.

*   While the authors assert on line 066 that the rate `sqrt(s*log(d/n))` is minimax-optimal, a proof for this claim was not found within the main manuscript (the supplementary material was not reviewed). Could the authors provide a clear demonstration of this optimality?
*   In Equation (3.8), the term `^s` is dependent on the sparsity level `s`, which is unknown in practical applications. How should `^s` be determined in practice? Similarly, how can one practically select an initial value `beta^init` that satisfies the condition `||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2`, as specified on line 275?
*   The significance of the truncation step within Algorithm 1 is not clear. Could the authors explain the role of this step and describe the potential consequences of its omission from the algorithm?
