- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector to a prescribed sparsity each iteration and introduces a decorrelated score statistic with asymptotic normality for testing a coordinate.

- Main theoretical guarantee: sparse EM iterates converge geometrically to the true parameter up to a statistical error that scales with sqrt(sparsity) and the infinity norm of the M-step’s estimation error.

- Error term typically depends logarithmically on ambient dimension, extending EM convergence analyses to the high-dimensional sparse regime.

- Analysis assumes Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin of attraction and requires initialization within that basin.

- New technical requirement: an infinity-norm bound on the M-step estimation error, though for many models this is no harder to obtain than uniform bounds (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture of regressions).

- Raises the important question of whether iterative truncation is essential versus running standard EM followed by a single final truncation.

- Provides a persuasive counterexample via the linear regression special case suggesting post-hoc truncation may not suffice and iterative sparsity enforcement can be critical.

- Suggests discussing related truncation-based sparse optimization literature (e.g., Langford et al., 2009).

- Experimental section should clearly state the initialization strategy, since theory depends on starting in the basin of attraction.

- Overall: meaningful contribution advancing high-dimensional EM theory, but clarifying the precise necessity of the sparse variant relative to alternatives would strengthen the paper.
