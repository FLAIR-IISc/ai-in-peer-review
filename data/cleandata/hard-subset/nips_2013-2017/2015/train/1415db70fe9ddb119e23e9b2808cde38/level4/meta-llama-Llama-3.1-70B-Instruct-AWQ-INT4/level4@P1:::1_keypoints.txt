- Paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that enforces sparsity via an added truncation step to the E/M steps.  
- Authors develop a decorrelated score test for inference on low-dimensional components in high-dimensional parameters.  
- Theoretical results: geometric convergence of the iterative estimator and (near-)optimal statistical convergence rates.  
- The decorrelated score statistic is shown asymptotically normal and achieves the semiparametric information bound for low-dimensional components.  
- Framework is applied to high-dimensional latent variable models, e.g., Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions.  
- Claims to provide the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in these high-dimensional latent models.  
- Concern: conditions for finding a valid initial solution β^{init} (required assumption) are not discussed.  
- Concern: two algorithms are proposed but the paper does not clarify which one practitioners should use.  
- Concern: practical selection and sensitivity of tuning parameters (ŝ and λ) are not explained.  
- Concern: the decorrelated score test appears identical to prior work [*]; the paper should cite that and clarify its novelty.  
- Technical concern: main results rely on many technical assumptions; authors should justify these assumptions for the considered latent models.  
- Overall assessment: paper combines existing tools cleverly and extends prior work to high dimensions, but novelty and nontriviality of applying existing tools need clearer articulation.  
- Presentation concern: main text reads like a "trailer" for a long supplement and should include more discussion of technical challenges and proofs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel sparse EM approach, simultaneous statistical/computational guarantees, broad model applicability.  
- Arguments for rejection: unclear initialization, ambiguous algorithm choice, unspecified tuning/sensitivity, potential lack of novelty on decorrelated test, heavy technical assumptions without justification.
