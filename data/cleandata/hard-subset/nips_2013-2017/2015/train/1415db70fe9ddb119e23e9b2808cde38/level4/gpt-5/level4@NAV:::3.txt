The paper proposes a sparse EM procedure that modifies classical EM by hard-thresholding the parameter vector at each iteration to enforce a target sparsity level. It also introduces an asymptotically normal test statistic, constructed from a decorrelated score, for testing whether a particular coordinate of the parameter vector is nonzero.

The provided guarantee for the sparse EM shows geometric convergence of the estimate to the true parameter (as a function of iterations), up to a statistical error term proportional to the square root of the sparsity level times the infinity norm of the M-step statistical error, which is typically only logarithmic in the ambient dimension.

This result builds on recent analogous analyses of EM in low-dimensional settings. As in prior work, the Q function is assumed to be sufficiently smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin of attraction, and the algorithm must be initialized within that basin. The new requirement is an infinity-norm bound on the error in estimating M. Although this condition is imposed only for sparse vectors, for many models estimating M at arbitrary vectors is no harder than at sparse vectors (as in the Gaussian mixture example and the non-gradient version of the mixture-of-regressions model). This raises a question: is the truncated EM algorithm necessary? Could a comparable guarantee be achieved by running ordinary EM under the same conditions and then applying a single truncation step? EDIT: the authors’ observation that linear regression is a special case here appears to be a compelling argument against a single truncation step.

There may be related work in sparse optimization that should be discussed (for example, “Sparse online learning via truncated gradient,” Langford et al., 2009).

The experimental section should state the initialization used. A sparse EM variant is proposed for parameter estimation in high-dimensional regimes. The convergence guarantees are an important contribution to high-dimensional learning, although the necessity of the sparse EM variant for obtaining these results is not entirely certain.
