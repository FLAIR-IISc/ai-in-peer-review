- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that hard-thresholds the parameter vector each iteration to enforce a target sparsity level.  
- Introduces an asymptotically normal test statistic based on a decorrelated score for testing whether a specific coordinate is nonzero.  
- Provides guarantees showing geometric convergence to the true parameter up to a statistical error term proportional to sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity norm of the M-step statistical error (typically logarithmic in ambient dimension).  
- Builds on prior low-dimensional EM analyses, assuming the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin of attraction and requiring initialization inside that basin.  
- Adds a new requirement: an infinity-norm bound on the error in estimating the M-step; this is imposed only for sparse vectors.  
- Notes that for many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture-of-regressions) estimating M at arbitrary vectors is no harder than at sparse vectors, calling the extra condition into question.  
- Raises the question whether truncated EM is necessary or if ordinary EM plus a single truncation at the end could achieve comparable guarantees.  
- EDIT: Observes that linear regression as a special case provides a compelling argument against the sufficiency of a single truncation step.  
- Suggests related sparse-optimization literature should be discussed (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 on truncated gradient).  
- Recommends reporting the initialization used in experiments.  
- Concludes the convergence guarantees are an important contribution to high-dimensional learning, but the necessity of the sparse-EM variant for these results remains uncertain.
