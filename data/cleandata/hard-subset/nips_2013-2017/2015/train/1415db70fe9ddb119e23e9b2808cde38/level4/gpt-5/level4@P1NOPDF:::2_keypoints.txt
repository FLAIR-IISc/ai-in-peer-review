- Paper proposes a modified EM for sparse high-dimensional settings, proves convergence to a global solution, derives convergence rates, and develops a statistical testing framework for estimator sparsity.

- Paper is well organized and summarizes related work adequately.

- Presentation is very dense (Sections 3–4); needs more simplification and intuition to aid readability.

- Claim that sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal lacks a self-contained proof in the main manuscript; reviewer requests a clear proof or sketch in the paper.

- Eq. (3.8) uses ^s that depends on unknown true sparsity s; reviewer asks for concrete, practical guidance on choosing ^s (e.g., data-driven methods, CV, stability selection, information criteria) and analysis of mis-specification effects.

- Initialization requirement ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 is unclear in practice; reviewer requests practical initialization strategies and sensitivity analysis to violations.

- Necessity of truncation step in Algorithm 1 is questioned; reviewer asks for theoretical intuition and empirical comparisons showing what fails or degrades if truncation is omitted.

- After rebuttal, authors addressed main concerns sufficiently to raise the score from 6 to 7.

- Overall assessment: a solid statistics paper but hard to read for ML researchers and with somewhat questionable practical usefulness.
