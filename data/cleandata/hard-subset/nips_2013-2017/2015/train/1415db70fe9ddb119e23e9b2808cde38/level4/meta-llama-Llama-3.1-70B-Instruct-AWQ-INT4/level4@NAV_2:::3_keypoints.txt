- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that enforces sparsity and converges geometrically to the true parameter up to a statistical error depending on sparsity and the M-step infinity-norm error.

- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for testing low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters, shown to be asymptotically normal.

- Main contribution is a general inferential theory for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional settings, providing a unified framework for estimation and uncertainty quantification.

- Provides thorough theoretical guarantees, including convergence rates and statistical error bounds, under well-motivated technical conditions.

- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible.

- Limitation: the necessity and importance of the sparse/truncated EM variant are not fully justified.

- Suggestion: include a more detailed discussion and comparison with related work in sparse optimization to clarify the role of truncation.

- Overall judgment: significant and original contribution, suitable for a top-tier venue; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.

- Reviewer scores: Rating 9/10, Confidence 8/10.
