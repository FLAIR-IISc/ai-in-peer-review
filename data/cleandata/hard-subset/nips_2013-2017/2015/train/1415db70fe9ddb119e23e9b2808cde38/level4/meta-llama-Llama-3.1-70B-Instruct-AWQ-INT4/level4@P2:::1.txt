The paper "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality" proposes a novel high dimensional EM algorithm that naturally incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation. The authors establish a general theory of the EM algorithm for inferring high dimensional latent variable models, making two key contributions: (i) a high dimensional EM algorithm that converges at a geometric rate and attains an estimator with the (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence, and (ii) a new inferential procedure for testing hypotheses for low dimensional components of high dimensional parameters.

The authors provide a clear and detailed presentation of their methodology, including the high dimensional EM algorithm and the decorrelated score test. They also discuss the implications of their results for Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regression models. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough review of related work.

The strengths of the paper include its novel contribution to the field of high dimensional EM algorithms, its clear and detailed presentation, and its thorough review of related work. The authors also provide a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment, which is a significant contribution to the field.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement. The authors should discuss the conditions under which the initial solution, β^{\rm init}, can be found, as this is a crucial requirement for the convergence results. Additionally, the authors should provide practical guidance on which algorithm to use in practice, as well as how to choose the tuning parameters, \hat s and λ. The sensitivity of the results to the choice of these tuning parameters should also be discussed.

Furthermore, the authors should acknowledge and cite the existing literature on decorrelated score tests, as the test proposed in the paper is similar to the one in [Ning and Liu (2017)]. The authors should also discuss whether the technical conditions required for the main results are satisfied for the considered latent variable models.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of high dimensional EM algorithms and provides a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment. However, the authors should address the areas for improvement mentioned above to strengthen the paper.
