- Paper proposes a high-dimensional EM with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and analyzes two M-step variants (exact and single gradient update).
- Shows geometric contraction of iterates up to a statistical error and achieves near-minimax rate ~√(s*)·√(log d/n) (up to logs).
- Proposes a decorrelated score for inference on low-dimensional components and proves asymptotic normality with semiparametric-efficient variance.
- Builds on Balakrishnan et al. for EM convergence and on Ning & Liu / van de Geer / Zhang & Zhang for decorrelation, adapting these to latent-variable EM via Q-function identities.
- Strength: conceptual novelty of integrating truncation into EM and analyzing the optimization–statistics tradeoff in high dimensions.
- Strength: rigorous theory showing geometric contraction and sharp statistical rates for both exact and approximate M-steps.
- Strength: provides inference guarantees (asymptotic normality and efficiency) even when estimator is from a nonconvex EM procedure.
- Strength: abstract conditions are verified for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions and seem portable to other models.
- Weakness: requires an initializer within a relative-error basin; needs concrete model-specific initialization procedures and sample/SNR scaling.
- Weakness: unclear practical guidance on choosing between exact vs gradient M-step and on stepsize selection when constants µ, ν are unknown.
- Weakness: tuning of sparsity level bs and regularization λ is not operationalized; needs data-driven strategies and sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: many technical assumptions and constants are deferred to the appendix; main text should summarize basin radii, SNR/sample-size regimes, entrywise error orders, and w* sparsity requirements per model.
- Weakness: novelty of the inference component relative to prior decorrelation literature is insufficiently highlighted; need to explicitly state new technical ingredients.
- Weakness: main proofs rely on a resampled EM variant—need discussion of applicability to practical single-sample EM and whether sample-splitting is recommended.
- Evaluation (quality): technically sound but practical impact obscured by abstract constants and resampling proof structure.
- Evaluation (clarity): paper is dense; would benefit from proof roadmap, a table of model-specific conditions/rates, and more intuition for Theorem 2.1 and decorrelation.
- Evaluation (originality/significance): novel and nontrivial analysis of truncation-EM and inference for latent models; addressing high-dim latent-variable estimation and inference is impactful.
- Specific requests: provide explicit initializers and guarantees, concrete defaults and sensitivity for (bs, λ, stepsize), and empirical comparisons of Algorithms 2 vs 3.
- Specific requests: move concise model-specific parameter regimes to main text, clearly cite Ning & Liu et al., and clarify new technical contributions enabling inference under nonconvex EM.
- Pros: strong theoretical development with near-optimal rates, first comprehensive inference framework for high-dim latent-variable EM, covers both exact and approximate M-steps.
- Cons: heavy reliance on technical conditions and resampling proofs, limited practical prescriptions for initialization/tuning, and inference largely builds on existing decorrelation methods.
- Overall: strong, timely theoretical contribution that merits acceptance if authors add clearer guidance on initialization, tuning, assumption regimes, and clarify novelty; reviewer is borderline-accept leaning positive.
