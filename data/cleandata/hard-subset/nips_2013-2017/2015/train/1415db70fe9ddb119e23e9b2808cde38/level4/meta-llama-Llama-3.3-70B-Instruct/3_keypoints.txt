- Method: modified EM that truncates the parameter vector each iteration to enforce sparsity, plus a decorrelated-score-based normal test statistic for nonzero entries.
- Convergence: proven geometric convergence to the true parameter up to a statistical error term ∝ sqrt(sparsity) × ||M-step error||_∞, typically with logarithmic dependence on ambient dimension.
- Assumptions: analysis depends on smoothness and quasi-quadratic behavior of the Q function within a basin of attraction and requires initialization inside that basin.
- Key technical condition: control of the infinity-norm error in estimating M for sparse vectors; for many models estimating M for all vectors is comparable to the sparse case.
- Necessity question: unclear if iterative truncation is essential—standard EM followed by a single truncation might achieve similar guarantees, though linear regression as a special case argues against that.
- Literature connection: should discuss links to sparse optimization works (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 on truncated gradient).
- Experiments: need to specify the initialization procedure used.
- Overall assessment: meaningful contribution with strong convergence guarantees for high-dimensional estimation, but the indispensability of the sparse EM variant remains uncertain.
