- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and a decorrelated score statistic for inference on low-dimensional components.
- Addresses a significant gap by providing theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional settings where p > n under sparsity assumptions.
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM convergence results to the high-dimensional regime and establishes geometric convergence rates.
- Proves asymptotic normality for the proposed test statistic and attains near-optimal statistical rates (p√(s*log(d/n))).
- Framework is general and demonstrated on Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.
- Combination of truncated EM and accompanying statistical guarantees plus a decorrelated score is novel and valuable.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and uses carefully defined notation and derivations.
- The necessity of truncation for the theoretical guarantees is not fully justified; alternative strategies (e.g., single post-EM truncation) are not thoroughly ruled out.
- Related work on sparse optimization and iterative sparse methods is under-discussed; references like Langford et al. (2009) need more contextualization.
- Initialization strategy used in experiments is not specified, hindering reproducibility and assessment of practical performance.
- Computational cost of the truncation step and overall algorithmic complexity are not explicitly analyzed.
- Arguments for acceptance: fills an important open problem, novel method and inferential procedure, rigorous theoretical support, and relevant applications.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient justification for truncation, incomplete related-work discussion, and missing experimental/complexity details.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address truncation justification, related work, initialization, and computational cost.
- Reviewer confidence: 7/10.
