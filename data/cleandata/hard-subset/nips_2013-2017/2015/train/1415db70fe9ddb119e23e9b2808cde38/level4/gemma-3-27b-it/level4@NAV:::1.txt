The paper investigates estimation and inference for models in a high-dimensional framework. It presents convergence guarantees and a corresponding inference procedure. 

The authors should elaborate on the conditions under which an initial solution, β<sup>init</sup>, meeting the requirement specified in line 60 of page 2 can be identified.

Two algorithms are proposed; providing practical guidance regarding which algorithm is preferable in different scenarios would be beneficial. 

Further clarity is needed regarding the practical selection of  ̂s and λ. An analysis of the sensitivity of the results to these tuning parameters, and potential data-driven methods for their selection, is crucial, given the importance of tuning parameter choice for sampling properties in related high-dimensional inference literature.

The decorrelated score test presented is identical to that found in Ning and Liu [ * ]. This connection should be explicitly acknowledged with a citation. The authors should also clarify whether any novel developments were necessary to adapt existing inference procedures to the current problem setting.

The core theoretical findings rely on a substantial number of technical assumptions. A discussion addressing the validity of these conditions specifically within the context of the latent variable models considered would strengthen the manuscript.

This work builds upon and extends Ning and Liu [2] to a high-dimensional context, and leverages their prior work for inference. While the paper intelligently combines existing results, a more precise articulation of the novel contributions and the non-triviality of applying existing tools to this problem is needed.  The paper currently feels incomplete, functioning primarily as a condensed overview of the more detailed supplementary material; the main text should contain a more comprehensive presentation of the technical challenges overcome to achieve the central results. 

[ * ] Yang Ning, Han Liu. *A General Theory of Hypothesis Tests and Confidence Regions for Sparse High Dimensional Models.*
