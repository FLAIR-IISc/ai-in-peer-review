- Proposes a "High Dimensional EM" algorithm that adds a hard-thresholding (truncation) step to enforce sparsity and proves geometric convergence and near-minimax estimation under conditions.  
- Develops a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing and uncertainty quantification of low-dimensional components in high-dimensional EM outputs.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM theory with new techniques to handle high-dimensionality and sparsity.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: first comprehensive framework giving both computational (convergence) and statistical (near-optimal rates, asymptotic normality) guarantees for sparse high-dimensional EM.  
- Novel methodology: simple truncation step plus decorrelated inference for a nonconvex iterative procedure.  
- Demonstrates generality by applying the framework to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.  
- Well-organized manuscript with clear positioning relative to related work.  
- Presentation is dense and technically demanding; abstract conditions reduce accessibility for non-specialists.  
- Practicality concerns: requires an initial estimator within a specific l2-ball of the truth, but offers no practical method to obtain such initialization.  
- Hyperparameter bs depends on unknown true sparsity s*; paper lacks guidance on data-driven selection or sensitivity to bs.  
- Needs clearer intuition and justification for the truncation (T-) step and comparison to alternatives like l1-regularized M-steps.  
- Minimax-optimality claim (sqrt(s* log d / n)) should be supported with a citation or discussion of the lower bound for the considered latent-variable class.  
- Overall: strong theoretical advances unifying estimation and inference, but limited immediate practical impact due to dense presentation and strong assumptions.
