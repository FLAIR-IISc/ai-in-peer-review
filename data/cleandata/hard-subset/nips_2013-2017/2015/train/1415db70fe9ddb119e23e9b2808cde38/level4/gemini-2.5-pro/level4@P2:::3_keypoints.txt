- Proposes a High Dimensional EM algorithm that adds a truncation step after each M-step and proves geometric convergence to a near-minimax estimator in high dimensions.
- Develops a decorrelated score statistic for testing individual parameters, shown to be asymptotically normal for valid inference.
- Extends EM theoretical guarantees from the low-dimensional to the high-dimensional (d >> n) regime.
- Significant: provides simultaneous computational (geometric convergence), statistical (near-minimax rates), and inferential (asymptotic normality) guarantees for high-dimensional latent-variable estimation.
- Technically novel and rigorous: key innovation is iterative truncation with L-infinity error control and analysis of EM local optima for inference.
- General and complete: framework stated under verifiable conditions and demonstrated on Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regressions.
- Weakness: needs stronger motivation and intuition for why iterative truncation is necessary versus a single final truncation.
- Weakness: should better situate the algorithm within sparse optimization literature (e.g., Iterative Hard Thresholding, truncated gradients).
- Weakness: clarify Condition 3.3—whether its requirement for any sparse input is a technical convenience or verifiable in practiced examples.
- Weakness: experiments should specify initialization method because theoretical guarantees are local and depend on starting within a basin of attraction.
- Overall: strong, substantial theoretical contribution with high likelihood of acceptance subject to the requested clarifications.
