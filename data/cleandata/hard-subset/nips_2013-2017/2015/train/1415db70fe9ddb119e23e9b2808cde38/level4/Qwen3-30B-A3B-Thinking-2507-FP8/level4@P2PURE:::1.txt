

The paper investigates the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional settings, establishing convergence results and developing an inference procedure. The authors should elaborate on the conditions under which an initial solution $\beta^{\rm init}$ satisfying the requirement in line 60 (page 2) can be obtained. Two algorithms are proposed; practical guidance on selecting between them is needed. Additionally, the paper must clarify how to choose $\hat s$ and $\lambda$ in practice, assess the sensitivity of results to these tuning parameters, and discuss data-dependent methods for their selection. Given the critical role of tuning parameter choice in high-dimensional inference literature for achieving good sampling properties, this discussion is essential.  

The decorrelated score test is identical to that in Ning and Liu (2017). This should be properly cited, not omitted. The authors must address whether any novel developments are required to apply the existing inference procedure within the current context.  

The main theoretical results rely on multiple technical conditions. The authors should discuss whether these conditions are satisfied for the latent variable models under consideration.  

The paper builds upon Ning and Liu (2017), extending their work to a high-dimensional setting and applying it to inference. While the combination of existing results is clever, the paper must more clearly articulate the exact novelty and explain why applying these tools nontrivially in this context. The submitted work functions as a "trailer" for the lengthy supplementary material, yet it should contain a more detailed discussion of the technical challenges required to establish the main results.
