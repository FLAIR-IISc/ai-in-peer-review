- Proposes a modified EM algorithm for sparse high-dimensional parameters, gives conditions for global convergence and derives convergence rates, and builds a testing framework for estimator sparsity.

- Paper is well organized and summarizes related work adequately, but the presentation is very dense and Sections 3–4 are hard to follow.

- Claim that the rate sqrt(s*log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal lacks a self-contained proof or a clear pointer in the main text; include either a proof or precise reference.

- Equation (3.8) uses ^s that depends on unknown s; the paper needs guidance on how to choose ^s in practice (data-driven selection).

- Need concrete procedures for obtaining an initialization β^init satisfying ||β^init − β*||_2 < R/2; provide practical initialization methods.

- The truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not justified: explain why it is essential, what failures occur without it, and whether theoretical guarantees rely on it.

- Strengths: solid statistical contributions, rigorous convergence analysis, and a useful testing framework; thorough related literature coverage.

- Weaknesses: dense exposition makes the work less accessible to ML researchers and practical usefulness is uncertain without clearer tuning, initialization, and truncation guidance.

- After rebuttal: main concerns were addressed and score raised from 6 to 7, but the paper remains difficult to read and its practical applicability still somewhat questionable.
