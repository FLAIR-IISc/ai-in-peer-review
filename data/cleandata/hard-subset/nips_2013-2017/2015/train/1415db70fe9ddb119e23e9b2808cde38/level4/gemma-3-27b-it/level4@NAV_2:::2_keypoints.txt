- Addresses EM for high-dimensional sparse latent variable models and introduces a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: convergence rate and asymptotic normality of the estimator.  
- Shows (near-)optimal statistical rate O(sqrt(s*log(d/n))) with appropriate initialization, where s is sparsity and d is dimension.  
- Proposes a decorrelated score statistic for inference on low-dimensional components, claiming semiparametric information bound optimality.  
- Well-structured, clearly motivated, and situates contributions within existing EM and high-dimensional statistics literature.  
- Technical contributions are significant: both statistical and computational guarantees are established.  
- The truncation step is a key innovation for handling high dimensionality and inducing sparsity.  
- Paper is dense and technically demanding; complex notation and derivations may limit accessibility.  
- Theoretical results lack more intuitive explanations to aid broader understanding.  
- Claim of minimax-optimal rate is stated without explicit connection to minimax lower bounds in the main text.  
- Practical guidance is lacking on tuning the sparsity parameter bs (Eq. 3.8) when true s is unknown.  
- The required initialization (||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2) is strong and not accompanied by strategies to obtain such an estimator.  
- Needs discussion of the necessity and role of truncation and of algorithm behavior without truncation (what goes wrong and how truncation fixes it).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of algorithm, rigorous theoretical guarantees, significance for EM in high dimensions, and technical soundness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility, insufficient practical guidance on tuning/initialization, and need for more intuitive explanations of key components.
