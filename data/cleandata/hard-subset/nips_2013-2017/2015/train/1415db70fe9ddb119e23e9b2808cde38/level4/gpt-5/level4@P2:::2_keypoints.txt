- Summary: Proposes a sparsity-promoting high-dimensional EM with a truncation (T) step, analyzes exact and gradient M-steps, shows geometric convergence and near-minimax estimation rates ~√(s* log d / n), and develops semiparametrically efficient decorrelated-score inference; illustrated on GMMs and mixtures of regressions.

- Strength: Provides a unified computational–statistical analysis with explicit optimization/statistical error decomposition.

- Strength: Achieves near-optimal estimation rates under sparsity and asymptotically valid, efficient inference.

- Strength: Treats both exact and gradient M-step implementations with clear technical conditions and model instantiations.

- Strength: One of the first general high-dimensional inferential frameworks for latent-variable EM estimators that are not convex global optima.

- Weakness: Readability — Sections 3–4 are dense and hard to follow; needs more intuition and a proof schematic.

- Weakness: Tuning bs depends on unknown s*; requires principled, data-driven selection methods and sensitivity discussion.

- Weakness: Initialization requirement (‖β_init − β*‖_2 ≤ R/2) is strong — needs practical, validated initializers (e.g., method-of-moments, spectral, L1 warm-start).

- Weakness: Role of the T-step unclear — must clarify why truncation is essential and show ablation (EM without truncation or L1 alternatives).

- Weakness: Minimax optimality claim needs a self-contained argument or precise citation and clarification of log(d) vs. log(d/n) notation.

- Weakness: Sparsity assumption on w* (for Dantzig selector) is crucial — discuss when it holds, diagnostics, robustness to misspecification, and finite-sample basin violations.

- Quality: Technically sound with careful separation of errors and convincing asymptotic inference under assumptions; would benefit from more empirical validation and tuning guidance.

- Clarity: Generally well organized and thorough related work, but recommend moving technical conditions to the appendix, adding intuition, figures, and a tuning/initialization subsection.

- Originality: Novel truncation-augmented EM plus decorrelated-score inference for latent-variable models, distinct from prior low-dimensional EM and debiased-Lasso work.

- Significance: High theoretical significance; practical impact depends on initializer/tuning availability and more experiments.

- Pros for acceptance: Strong, general theoretical contributions; near-optimal rates and semiparametric efficiency; applicable across models and M-step variants.

- Cons for acceptance: Steep readability curve, limited practical guidance, and limited empirical/real-data evaluation.

- Requests for revision: Provide minimax justification/citation, concrete procedures for choosing bs and initializing β with sensitivity analyses, empirically justify the T-step, and improve exposition with a proof roadmap.

- Overall recommendation: Lean positive; reviewer raised score from 6 to 7 after rebuttal — solid statistics paper but practical utility remains somewhat uncertain without more guidance and experiments.
