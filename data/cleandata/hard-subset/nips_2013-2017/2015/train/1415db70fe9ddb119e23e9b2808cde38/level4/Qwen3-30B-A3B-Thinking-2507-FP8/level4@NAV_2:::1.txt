

The paper develops a general theory for the expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models. It makes two key contributions: (i) a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating sparsity through truncation steps, which converges geometrically to an estimator with near-optimal statistical rates of convergence, and (ii) a new inferential procedure for hypothesis testing of low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters, establishing the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions.

This work extends previous research on the EM algorithm in low-dimensional regimes [2, 18, 25, 30] to high-dimensional settings, where traditional maximum likelihood estimation becomes ill-posed without sparsity assumptions. The authors address this challenge by introducing a truncation step that enforces sparsity structure during the iterative process, enabling both statistical and computational guarantees where none previously existed.

Strengths:
- The paper provides a comprehensive theoretical framework unifying parameter estimation and inference for high-dimensional latent variable models.
- The high-dimensional EM algorithm with truncation offers a practical solution to sparsity constraints in high-dimensional settings.
- The decorrelated score statistic establishes asymptotic normality with optimal limiting variance, achieving the semiparametric information bound.
- The framework is general and applicable to multiple latent variable models, as demonstrated with Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.

Weaknesses:
- The paper does not sufficiently discuss how to find an initial solution $\beta^{\rm init}$ satisfying the requirement in line 60 on page 2, which is critical for practical implementation as convergence depends on good initialization.
- Two algorithms are proposed (exact maximization vs. gradient ascent for the M-step), but the paper lacks practical guidance on which to use in different scenarios.
- There is insufficient discussion on tuning parameter selection ($\hat{s}$ and $\lambda$), their sensitivity, and data-dependent methods. In high-dimensional inference, tuning parameter choice is crucial for good sampling properties.
- The decorrelated score test appears to be a direct application of Ning and Liu's methodology [13], but the paper does not clearly articulate what novel aspects were needed to apply this existing inference procedure to the high-dimensional EM context.

The paper has strong technical quality with well-supported theoretical claims, and the writing is clear and well-organized. However, the practical implementation aspects require more attention, and the novelty could be more clearly articulated by detailing the specific challenges that needed to be overcome to apply existing inference techniques to the high-dimensional EM framework. The significance is high, as it provides the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.
