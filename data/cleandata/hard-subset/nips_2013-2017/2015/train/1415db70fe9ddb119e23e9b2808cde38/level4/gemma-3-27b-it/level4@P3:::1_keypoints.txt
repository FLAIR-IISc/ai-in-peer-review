- Presents a theoretical framework extending EM to high-dimensional latent variable models, filling an important gap in the literature.  
- Establishes convergence guarantees for a sparsity-enforcing modified EM algorithm and provides estimation guarantees.  
- Develops an inferential procedure for low-dimensional components, including asymptotic normality of a decorrelated score statistic.  
- Builds on prior low-dimensional EM results and connects to recent high-dimensional inference literature.  
- Clear and well-structured mathematical presentation with easy-to-follow algorithm descriptions.  
- Practical relevance: enables hypothesis testing in high-dimensional latent-variable settings.  
- Relies on a specific initialization condition; lacks discussion of practical strategies to find suitable initial solutions.  
- Presents two M-step implementations (exact maximization and gradient ascent) but gives no practical guidance on choosing between them or trade-offs.  
- Omits discussion on selecting tuning parameters (e.g., sparsity bs), sensitivity to these choices, and use of data-driven methods like cross-validation.  
- Insufficiently discusses and credits the relationship to existing inference frameworks (e.g., Ning and Liu) and what is novel in this latent-variable setting.  
- Relies on many technical conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) without thorough verification for the example models (Gaussian mixture, mixture of regressions).  
- Paper feels incomplete with many details relegated to the appendix; core text should be more self-contained.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant theoretical gap, provides rigorous technical results, and offers a useful inferential tool.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak practical guidance on initialization/tuning, insufficient connection to prior inference work, and heavy dependence on appendices.  
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical contribution but requires major revisions to address practical guidance, clarify relationships to prior work, and strengthen assumption verification.
