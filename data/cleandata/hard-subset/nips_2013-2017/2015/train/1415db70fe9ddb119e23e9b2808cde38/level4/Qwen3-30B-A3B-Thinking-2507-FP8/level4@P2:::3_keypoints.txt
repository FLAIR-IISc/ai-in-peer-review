- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm that enforces sparsity via a truncation step at each iteration.
- Provides computational and statistical guarantees showing geometric convergence to an estimator with near-optimal statistical rate.
- Develops a decorrelated score test for low-dimensional components, proving asymptotic normality with optimal limiting variance.
- Extends recent low-dimensional EM theory to high-dimensional regimes where MLE is non-identifiable without sparsity.
- Shows the method attains the minimax-optimal rate √(s* log d / n), significantly improving over prior low-dimensional rates.
- Claims the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Establishes simultaneous computational and statistical guarantees: estimation error decomposes into geometric optimization error and statistical error.
- Shows the decorrelated score achieves the semiparametric information bound, enabling optimal inference with high-dimensional nuisance parameters.
- Theoretical analysis is comprehensive, covering both exact and gradient-ascent M-steps.
- Empirical illustrations provided for Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regression models.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and effectively contrasted with tensor-based and Lasso-based alternatives.
- Main weakness: necessity of truncation at every iteration is not fully justified; post-EM truncation may need stronger analysis.
- Experimental section omits critical details about the initialization procedure for EM.
- Recommendation: strengthen comparison/analysis of iterative vs post-processing truncation and report initialization details.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution suitable for NIPS, pending minor clarifications on truncation and experiments.
