- Addresses EM in high-dimensional latent variable models where parameters exceed sample size.
- Introduces a sparsity-enforcing truncation step within EM to enable high-dimensional use.
- Proves convergence rates and asymptotic normality, enabling valid hypothesis testing.
- Demonstrates near-optimal statistical rates under appropriate initialization and truncation.
- Claims general applicability across a broad family of latent variable models.
- Well-structured manuscript with comprehensive related work positioning.
- Presentation in sections 3–4 is dense and technically heavy, limiting accessibility.
- Practical guidance is limited: selecting sparsity level (bs) and initialization (beta^init) is underdeveloped.
- Dependence on unknown quantities like true sparsity s* complicates real-world application.
- Minimax optimality claim needs clearer justification and citation in the main text.
- Importance of truncation step was initially under-explained—negative effects of skipping it unclear.
- Rebuttal clarified minimax meaning, pointed to literature, and discussed sparsity selection methods.
- Rebuttal provided practical initialization suggestions (small random vector, preliminary screening).
- Rebuttal explained that without truncation EM can converge to suboptimal solutions in high dimensions.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, valuable algorithm and inference tools, rigorous analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: high technicality, limited practical implementation details, reliance on unknown parameters.
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution that requires better accessibility and practical guidance.
- Recommendation: Accept (Score 7/10).
