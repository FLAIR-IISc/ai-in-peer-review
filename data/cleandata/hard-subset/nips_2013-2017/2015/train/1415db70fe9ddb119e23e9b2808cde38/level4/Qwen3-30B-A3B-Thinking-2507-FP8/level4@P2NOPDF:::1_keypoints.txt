- Addresses EM in high-dimensional settings with convergence guarantees and an inference procedure, but overall novelty and technical contributions are not clearly articulated.  
- Requires an initial solution β^{init} (line 60) but gives no discussion of how to obtain it; authors must state existence conditions and construction methods.  
- Proposes two algorithms without guidance on when to prefer one; needs practical recommendations based on problem characteristics (e.g., sparsity, sample size).  
- Tuning parameters \hat{s} and λ lack justification; must explain practical selection, sensitivity to choices, and feasibility of data-dependent methods (cross-validation, stability).  
- The decorrelated score test is identical to Ning & Liu (2014) and must be explicitly cited rather than presented as novel.  
- Must clarify what new technical developments are necessary to apply Ning & Liu’s framework to latent variable models instead of merely adapting existing tools.  
- Main theoretical results rest on many technical assumptions; authors should assess whether these are verifiable and plausible for specific models (Gaussian mixtures, factor models).  
- Paper’s core novelty is obscured and reads like a “trailer” for the supplement; the main text lacks sufficient technical detail to justify theoretical claims.  
- Should provide concrete examples of nontrivial challenges in adapting Ning & Liu’s methods (e.g., handling latent variables in decorrelation, dependency structures).  
- Authors must explicitly: (1) specify conditions for obtaining β^{init}, (2) provide tuning-parameter guidance, (3) cite/contextualize the decorrelated score test, and (4) detail technical hurdles in adaptation.  
- Supplementary material should not replace the main text; key insights and essential technical arguments must be included in the paper itself.
