- Summary: Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with sparsity, provides convergence guarantees, statistical rates, asymptotic normality, and a decorrelated score test for low-dimensional hypotheses.  
- Strength: Comprehensive treatment addressing both computational and statistical aspects of EM in high dimensions.  
- Strength: Establishes a general framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment across many latent variable models.  
- Strength: Supplies detailed analysis including convergence guarantees, rates of convergence, and asymptotic normality.  
- Weakness: Paper is dense and technical, making it hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Weakness: No clear proof in the main manuscript that the sqrt(s*log(d/n)) rate is minimax-optimal.  
- Weakness: Choice of sparsity parameter (s^) and initialization (beta^init) is not clearly explained for practical use.  
- Weakness: Importance and consequences of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 are not clearly justified.  
- Originality/Significance: Novel high-dimensional EM incorporating sparsity with a broadly applicable theoretical framework.  
- Quality of writing: Well-organized but very dense; needs more intuitive explanations and illustrative examples.  
- Quality of writing: Should clarify choice of sparsity parameter and initialization procedure.  
- For acceptance: Provides a comprehensive, general framework for high-dimensional EM with uncertainty quantification.  
- Against acceptance: Presentation is overly technical, lacks key theoretical proof, and leaves practical parameter choices unclear.  
- Rating: 7 — solid contribution but hindered by density, presentation, and some theoretical/practical gaps.
