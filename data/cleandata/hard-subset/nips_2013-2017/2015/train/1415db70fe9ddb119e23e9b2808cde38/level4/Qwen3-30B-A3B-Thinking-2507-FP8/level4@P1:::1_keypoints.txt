- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, proving geometric convergence.  
- Achieves (near-)optimal statistical estimation rate O(√(s* log d / n)).  
- Introduces a decorrelated score test for low-dimensional parameter inference with asymptotic normality and optimal limiting variance.  
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM theory to high-dimensional latent variable models, filling a gap in the literature.  
- Demonstrates applicability to Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models.  
- Provides a unified analytic framework that covers a broad family of latent variable models.  
- Theoretical contributions include simultaneous computational and statistical guarantees and sophisticated proof techniques.  
- Claims to be the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Assumes availability of an initial estimator β^init satisfying a relative ℓ2 error bound but does not explain how to obtain it in practice.  
- Presents two algorithmic implementations (exact maximization and gradient ascent) without guidance on when to use each or their computational trade-offs.  
- Omits practical discussion and guidance on tuning parameter selection (ŝ and λ), sensitivity, and data-driven selection methods.  
- The decorrelated score test closely resembles Ning and Liu [27] but the connection and required novel developments are not sufficiently acknowledged.  
- Technical conditions are verified in the appendix but not adequately motivated or discussed for their plausibility in the main text.  
- Main text reads like a "trailer" for supplementary material; key technical details (especially analysis of truncation effects) should be included in the paper.  
- Overall quality: technically sound, well-organized, and clear, though some passages need tightening for readability.  
- Originality and significance: strong extension to high-dimensional settings with high potential impact, but inference component builds on existing methods and needs clearer differentiation.
