- Introduces a sparsity-aware EM that truncates to a prescribed sparsity each iteration and an inferential decorrelated-score producing asymptotically normal tests for individual coordinates.  
- Proves geometric convergence of sparse EM up to a statistical error scaling with sqrt(sparsity) times the sup-norm M-step estimation error, which typically grows only logarithmically with ambient dimension.  
- Analysis extends low-dimensional EM results and requires the Q-function to be smooth and approximately quadratic in a basin of attraction, with initialization inside that basin.  
- New technical ingredient is sup-norm control of M-step approximation error for sparse vectors, but computing the M-step at arbitrary points may be no harder in many models, questioning the necessity of truncation.  
- Raises concern whether standard EM followed by a single post hoc truncation would suffice (edit: linear regression example weakens the post-hoc truncation argument).  
- Suggests citing related sparse-optimization work (e.g., Langford et al., 2009 truncated gradient).  
- Requests that experiments explicitly describe the initialization strategy.  
- Verdict: useful high-dimensional convergence results, but the essentiality of the truncated EM variant for the stated guarantees remains unclear.
