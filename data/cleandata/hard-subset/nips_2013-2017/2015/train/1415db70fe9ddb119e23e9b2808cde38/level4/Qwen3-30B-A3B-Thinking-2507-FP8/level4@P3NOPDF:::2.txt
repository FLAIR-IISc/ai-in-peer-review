

This paper proposes a modified EM algorithm for high-dimensional sparse parameter estimation, establishing convergence guarantees to the global solution, deriving convergence rates, and introducing a statistical test for estimator sparsity. The contributions are theoretically significant, and the organization is generally clear with a thorough related work summary. However, the technical density, particularly in Sections 3 and 4, substantially impedes accessibility. The manuscript would benefit from greater simplification and motivation to aid readers.

The minimax optimality claim for the rate $\sqrt{s \log(d/n)}$ (line 66) is pivotal but appears unsupported in the main text. The argument states this rate is minimax-optimal without providing a proof or reference. While supplementary material may contain this, the main manuscript should explicitly establish this foundational result to maintain self-containment and credibility. Please clarify this in the main text.

Regarding practical implementation, two critical questions arise:
1.  The estimator $\hat{s}$ (in Eq. 3.8) depends on the unknown sparsity level $s$. How should $\hat{s}$ be selected in real applications? The manuscript does not address this tuning challenge, which is essential for usability.
2.  The convergence analysis requires an initial estimate $\beta^{\text{init}}$ satisfying $\|\beta^{\text{init}} - \beta^*\|_2 < R/2$ (line 275). Given that $\beta^*$ is unknown, how can such an initial value be feasibly obtained? The paper lacks practical guidance for this step, limiting the method's applicability.

The truncation step in Algorithm 1 also requires justification. The paper does not explain why this step is necessary. Specifically, what occurs if truncation is omitted? Does the algorithm fail to converge, produce inconsistent estimates, or lose sparsity guarantees? A brief discussion of the truncation's role in preventing numerical instability or ensuring sparsity recovery would strengthen the methodological contribution.

The rebuttal adequately addressed the minimax optimality claim and the role of truncation, resolving those concerns. The revised manuscript now clearly establishes the minimax rate and demonstrates that omitting truncation leads to non-sparsity in the estimator. These clarifications significantly improve the paper's technical rigor.

Despite these improvements, the paper remains highly technical and challenging for the broader NeurIPS audience. The dense presentation of proofs and notation, while mathematically sound, creates a steep barrier for readers outside specialized statistics. Greater emphasis on intuition, high-level motivation, and concrete examples would enhance accessibility without compromising rigor. The current formulation is likely to be appreciated primarily by statisticians, potentially limiting its impact in the machine learning community.

The method's practical relevance for real-world applications is also somewhat unclear. While the theoretical framework is strong, the manuscript does not sufficiently demonstrate *why* this specific modification is preferable to existing sparse EM approaches (e.g., Lasso-EM) in practice. The statistical test for sparsity is a useful addition, but its utility in guiding model selection or interpretation for practitioners requires more concrete illustration. Without evidence of tangible advantages over simpler alternatives on meaningful datasets, the practical motivation remains underdeveloped.

In summary, this is a strong theoretical contribution with clear mathematical advances in sparse high-dimensional EM. The revised manuscript addresses key technical concerns raised in the initial review. However, the paper's accessibility for the NeurIPS community and its demonstrated practical utility for machine learning practitioners require significant strengthening. The theoretical results are sound and novel, but the presentation and application context need refinement to maximize impact.

The paper merits a **7 (Strong Accept)**, reflecting the solid statistical contribution. The score was increased from 6 to 7 following the rebuttal, as the critical theoretical points were resolved. The primary remaining limitation is the paper's density and the need for clearer articulation of practical benefits beyond the theoretical setting. With minor revisions to improve exposition and application context, this work would be a compelling contribution to the field.
