- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with truncation to enforce sparsity when d >> n.  
- Two main contributions: truncated EM achieving near-minimax statistical rates, and a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing.  
- Theoretical guarantee: estimation error decomposes into geometric optimization error plus statistical error √(s* log d / n) (Theorem 3.4).  
- Proves asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency of the decorrelated score statistic (Theorem 4.6).  
- Technical conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) are carefully stated and verified for example models in the appendix.  
- Properly handles nonconcavity and high-dimensional nuisance parameters absent in prior low-dimensional analyses.  
- Provides entrywise error characterization (not just ℓ2), important for analyzing the truncation step.  
- Well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and readable technical exposition.  
- Novelty: truncation step and high-dimensional inference for latent variable models are new.  
- Distinguishes itself from prior work (low-dimensional EM and tensor methods) with better statistical rates.  
- Significance: first theoretical EM guarantees in high dimensions with broad applicability (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, mixture of regressions).  
- Strengths: near-minimax rates, semiparametric efficiency, unified framework, rigorous proofs.  
- Weaknesses: lacks empirical validation, assumes sparsity (s* << d), needs complex condition checks, and requires specific initialization.  
- Overall: high-quality, original, and significant theoretical contribution; recommended for acceptance.
