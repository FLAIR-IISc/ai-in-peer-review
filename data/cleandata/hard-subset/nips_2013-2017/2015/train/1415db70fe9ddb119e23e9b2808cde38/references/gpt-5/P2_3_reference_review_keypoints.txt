- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation (hard-thresholding) step to enforce sparsity, analyzed for both exact and gradient M-steps.
- Proves geometric convergence to a basin near the truth and near-minimax estimation rates O(sqrt(s* log d / n)) up to log factors under regularity and initialization.
- Constructs a decorrelated score statistic for inference on low-dimensional components and proves asymptotic normality achieving the semiparametric efficiency bound.
- Verifies conditions and provides instantiations for Gaussian mixture models and mixtures of regressions.
- Extends low-dimensional EM analysis (Balakrishnan et al. 2017) to high dimensions and connects EM Q-function derivatives to score/information for debiasing-style inference.
- Claims sharper high-dimensional rates and likelihood-based targets compared to tensor methods.
- Hard-thresholding step is reminiscent of IHT/gradient hard-thresholding in sparse M-estimation, but that literature is under-discussed.
- Strength: fills an important gap by providing rigorous high-dimensional computational and statistical guarantees for EM, including inference.
- Strength: clear optimization-statistics decomposition yielding geometric optimization decay plus entrywise statistical error and near-optimal rates.
- Strength: general framework covers exact and approximate M-steps with explicit constants and regularity conditions.
- Strength: strong inference result—decorrelated score achieves semiparametric efficiency adapted to latent models.
- Strength: concrete scaling, SNR/separation conditions, and examples for GMM and mixture regressions.
- Weakness: main theorem (Theorem 3.4) is proved for a resampling variant (fresh data each iteration) rather than the practical single-sample algorithm.
- Weakness: initialization requirement (close to truth) is strong in high dimensions with no constructive, guaranteed initializer provided.
- Weakness: sparsity parameter b_s must match s*; paper lacks guidance on data-driven selection or robustness to mis-specification.
- Weakness: inference assumptions (sparsity of w*, bounded Fisher information, sample-size scaling) may be restrictive and need clearer verification in main text.
- Clarity issue: notation is heavy and sometimes cumbersome; a symbol table and algorithm schematic would help.
- Presentation issue: references are inconsistently formatted and some related work (IHT literature, L1-penalized EM) is missing or under-cited.
- Originality: novel joint computational/statistical EM analysis in high dimensions and nonconvex debiasing for latent models, but related projection-based sparse optimization and penalized EM work should be contrasted.
- Significance: potentially substantial if extended to single-sample guarantees and feasible initialization strategies; currently limited by practical gaps.
- Empirical gap: no experiments or simulations to validate finite-sample behavior, sensitivity to initialization and b_s, or comparisons to alternatives.
- Suggestion: provide non-resampling guarantees (or sketch), or discuss practical implications and cross-fitting/sample-splitting as remedies.
- Suggestion: add constructive initialization procedures (e.g., spectral+thresholding) with theoretical guarantees for example models.
- Suggestion: propose adaptive or data-driven methods for selecting b_s and analyze robustness to over/under-estimation.
- Suggestion: clarify when w* is sparse in examples and give explicit bounds on s_w* relative to model/SNR.
- Suggestion: consider cross-fitting or sample-splitting for Dantzig estimator to relax inference assumptions.
- Pros summary: novel, principled extension of EM to sparse high-dimensional settings with near-optimal rates and semiparametric efficient inference.
- Cons summary: reliance on resampling analysis, strong initialization and tuning assumptions, missing experiments and related-work citations, and notational/presentation issues.
- Overall recommendation: technically strong and potentially impactful but needs extensions to non-resampling setting, empirical validation, and clearer guidance on initialization/parameter selection; reviewer leans toward weak accept conditional on these fixes.
