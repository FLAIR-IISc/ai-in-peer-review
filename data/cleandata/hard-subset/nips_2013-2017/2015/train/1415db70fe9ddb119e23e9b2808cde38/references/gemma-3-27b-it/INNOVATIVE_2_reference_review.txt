This paper tackles a significant and challenging problem: establishing theoretical guarantees for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models. The core idea of incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity is novel and potentially impactful, addressing a major gap in the existing literature where guarantees are largely absent when *d* > *n*. The authors provide a unified framework and establish convergence rates and asymptotic normality, a strong result. The connections drawn to existing work, particularly [2], are well-articulated, and the paper clearly highlights its contributions beyond the low-dimensional setting.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The truncation step within the EM algorithm is a genuinely new idea for addressing high dimensionality. This isn’t simply an application of existing techniques; it’s a modification of a fundamental algorithm to overcome a known limitation.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper presents a rigorous theoretical analysis, establishing both convergence rates and asymptotic normality, which is a substantial achievement. The conditions are clearly stated, and the connection to existing theoretical results is well-made.
*   **Significance:** Establishing theoretical guarantees for EM in high dimensions is a significant step forward. This could unlock the practical use of EM in a wider range of applications where *d* > *n* and has the potential to influence subsequent work in this area. The optimality claim regarding the score statistic adds to the significance.
*   **Generality:** The framework is presented as general, applying to multiple models (Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression), and the authors explicitly state the potential for extension.
*   **Clear Structure:** The paper is well-organized, with a clear delineation between the algorithm, the theoretical results, and the specific applications.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Condition Complexity:** The assumptions (Conditions 3.1-3.3 and 4.1-4.5) are numerous and, while standard for this type of theoretical work, may be difficult to verify in practice. The specific requirements for the sparsity level (s\*, w\*) in relation to *n* and *d* seem particularly restrictive.
*   **Practical Considerations:** The paper focuses heavily on theoretical guarantees. A discussion of the practical implications of the truncation parameter *b<sub>s</sub>* and its sensitivity to the choice of initialization would be valuable. It isn't clear how easily this algorithm would be implemented and tuned in a real-world scenario.
*   **Empirical Validation:** While the theoretical analysis is strong, there’s a noticeable absence of empirical validation beyond the illustrative examples in the Appendix. Demonstrating the performance of the algorithm on simulated or real datasets, even with limited scope, would significantly strengthen the paper.
*   **Limited Scope of Models:** While the paper claims generality, only two models are fully explored. Expanding this to a third model, even in the appendix, would bolster the claim of generality.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The core idea of the truncated EM algorithm is genuinely novel and addresses a fundamental limitation of the standard EM in high-dimensional settings.
*   The theoretical guarantees – convergence rate and asymptotic normality – are substantial and represent a significant contribution to the field.
*   The framework is well-defined and has the potential to be extended to other latent variable models.
*   The paper is clearly written and well-organized, demonstrating a deep understanding of the underlying theory.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The conditions for the theoretical results are complex and potentially restrictive, raising questions about their applicability in practical scenarios.
*   The lack of substantial empirical validation weakens the claim of practical relevance.
*   The reliance on sparsity assumptions, and the stringent requirements associated with them, could limit the applicability of the algorithm.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some weaknesses, this paper presents a significant and novel contribution to the field. The core idea is highly innovative, and the theoretical guarantees are impressive. While the conditions and practical considerations are areas for improvement, the potential impact of this work justifies acceptance. The focus on theoretical advancement, even with limited empirical validation, is acceptable given the novelty and significance of the results. I believe this paper pushes the boundaries of what's known about the EM algorithm and opens up new avenues for research.

**Recommendation:** Accept. While minor revisions to address the practical considerations and expand the empirical validation would be beneficial, the novelty and theoretical contribution warrant acceptance to NIPS. I would encourage the authors to include a discussion section addressing limitations and future work, particularly regarding the challenges of parameter tuning and condition verification in practical applications.
