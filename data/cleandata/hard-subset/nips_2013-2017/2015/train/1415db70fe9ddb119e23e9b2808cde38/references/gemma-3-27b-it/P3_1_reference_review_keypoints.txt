- Tackles EM in high-dimensional regime (d > n) by proposing a truncated EM that enforces sparsity and provides estimation and uncertainty guarantees.  
- Proves convergence rate O(p s* log(d/n)) and asymptotic normality for low-dimensional inference, achieving near-minimax optimality.  
- Novel truncation step effectively balances exploration and exploitation to enable theoretical analysis.  
- Framework is general, applicable to Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions, and handles exact/approximate M-steps.  
- Strong, detailed theoretical analysis with extensive proofs and clear positioning relative to prior work.  
- Presentation is highly technical with dense notation, making the paper hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Choice and tuning of truncation parameter bs are crucial and potentially sensitive; practical guidance is lacking.  
- Some assumptions (e.g., sparsity of leading eigenvectors) are strong and may not hold broadly; robustness under violations is not discussed.  
- Limited empirical evaluation—no simulations or real-data experiments to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Could benefit from deeper comparison to other high-dimensional techniques (spectral methods, variational inference).  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, original, and significant contribution, but dense and lacking empirical validation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a long-standing open problem, provides strong theoretical guarantees, and advances computation-statistics interplay.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to complexity, uncertain practical performance, and restrictive assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, with suggestions to add small-scale empirical results and practical tuning guidance for bs.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
