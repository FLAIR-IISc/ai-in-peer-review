- Summary: Proposes a high-dimensional EM with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, proving convergence rates and asymptotic normality for latent variable models (GMM and mixture regression).

- Strength: Addresses a significant gap by providing theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable settings.

- Strength: Introduces a novel and well-motivated truncation step to handle high dimensionality and enforce sparsity.

- Strength: Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with statistical convergence rates and asymptotic normality under clear conditions.

- Strength: Offers a unified framework that covers both exact M-step maximization and gradient-ascent implementations.

- Strength: Shows estimator achieves semiparametric information bound, indicating optimality.

- Strength: Paper is generally well-structured with clear algorithms and mostly consistent notation.

- Weakness: Extremely technical with many conditions and dense notation, limiting accessibility to non-experts.

- Weakness: Verification of conditions in appendices is limited; practical implications of sparsity assumptions (e.g., on w*) are under-discussed.

- Weakness: Rates involve unspecified constants (C, C0, etc.), leaving practical magnitude unclear.

- Weakness: Truncation may add computational overhead; the paper lacks complexity discussion.

- Weakness: Limited empirical validation—no simulations to illustrate convergence or finite-sample behavior.

- Quality: Technically sound with rigorous proofs and complete analysis of estimation and inference guarantees.

- Clarity: Clear for experts but too dense for broader audiences; more intuition and examples would help.

- Originality: Highly original extension of EM theory to high-dimensional regimes with a novel truncation approach.

- Significance: Potentially very significant for research and practice by enabling valid inference for high-dimensional latent variable models.

- Arguments for acceptance: Fills critical theoretical gap, presents a novel method, provides rigorous guarantees, is broadly applicable, and has high potential impact.

- Arguments against acceptance: Accessibility issues due to technical density, limited experiments, no computational-cost analysis, and assumptions that may be hard to verify.

- Overall assessment: Strong recommendation for acceptance with suggested revisions to improve intuition, condition discussion, and add empirical validation.

- Confidence: 9/10
