- Paper proposes a truncated (hard-thresholded) EM/gradient-EM for high-dimensional latent-variable models to enforce sparsity along iterates.
- Authors prove a nonasymptotic error decomposition: geometric optimization error plus statistical error ~√(s* log d / n), near-minimax under local regularity and initialization.
- They construct a decorrelated score using Q and a Dantzig-type estimator to obtain asymptotically normal, semiparametrically efficient inference for low-dimensional components.
- Framework covers exact and gradient M-step variants and is illustrated on two-component spherical Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions.
- Extends Balakrishnan et al. (2017) from low- to high-dimensional EM by adding an iterative hard-thresholding step.
- Builds on de-biasing/desparsification literature but adapts the orthogonal score to latent-variable models via EM identities.
- Claims faster rates and broader applicability than tensor methods when higher moments are poorly behaved.
- Strength: ambitious unification of computational and statistical analysis for nonconvex, high-dimensional latent-variable estimation.
- Strength: clear separation of optimization vs. statistical error with geometric convergence for exact and gradient M-steps.
- Strength: novel semiparametric-efficiency inference result for this model class.
- Strength: insightful use of entrywise (ℓ1) error control to manage truncation effects.
- Major concern: main convergence theorem relies on resampling (sample-splitting) across iterations; no non-resampled guarantee or degradation analysis is provided.
- Assumptions are strong and insufficiently transparent, notably the basin-of-attraction initialization requirement without concrete implementable initializers.
- Condition of local strong concavity/smoothness of Q(·;θ*) may be unrealistic in many latent models and seems to require strong SNR for GMMs; clarity for mixture of regressions is lacking.
- Critical inference assumption: sparsity of the orthogonalization vector w* may not hold in realistic designs and needs discussion/characterization.
- Tuning/algorithmic parameters issues: bs must be within a constant factor of s* (unknown) and stepsize for gradient EM requires unknown population constants.
- Computational cost: solving a Dantzig selector on d−1 variables per inference step may dominate EM work; frequency and practicality of estimating w are unclear.
- No experiments: absence of empirical validation on finite-sample performance, sensitivity to initialization/bs, or coverage undermines practical claims.
- Scope limited: analysis mainly for simple two-component spherical, equal-weight GMM with strong SNR; unclear extension/constant scaling for more general mixtures, missing data, or HMMs.
- Presentation issues: notational glitches, typos, incomplete references, and heavy notation need cleanup; some standard identities are stated without proper caveats.
- Question 1: Provide a theorem/sketch for the non-resampled algorithm and required mixing/martingale arguments and rate penalties.
- Question 2: How to choose bs in practice without knowing s*—any data-driven selector or continuation schedule?
- Question 3: How plausible is sparsity of w* for the considered models; can s_w* be characterized for examples?
- Question 4: Is w estimated once or iteratively; what are computational costs when d ≫ n?
- Question 5: Propose/justify a concrete initializer (e.g., spectral/method-of-moments) with provable closeness under given SNR.
- Suggestions: add synthetic/real experiments comparing penalized EM, tensor methods, and de-biased GLM baselines and report error, support recovery, coverage, and runtime.
- Suggestions: provide a non-resampling result or practical heuristics approximating theoretical requirements.
- Suggestions: discuss/adaptivity to unknown sparsity and tuning of bs and Dantzig λ (cross-validation, information criteria) and analyze effects.
- Suggestions: clarify assumptions and constants for mixture of regressions and consider weaker SNR regimes.
- Suggestions: expand related work to include hard-thresholding/projection methods and penalized EM and contrast analyses.
- Evaluation: strong theoretical development but hampered by strong assumptions, resampling dependence, and lack of experiments.
- Overall recommendation: Weak reject; promising and novel but needs non-resampled guarantees, clearer/weaker conditions for broader models, and empirical validation to be competitive.
