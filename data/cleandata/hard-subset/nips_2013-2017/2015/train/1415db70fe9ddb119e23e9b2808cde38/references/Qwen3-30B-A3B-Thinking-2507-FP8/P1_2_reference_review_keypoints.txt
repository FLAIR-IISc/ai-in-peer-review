- Addresses a gap: proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with truncation to enable theoretical guarantees when d > n.  
- Contribution 1: high-dimensional EM with truncation achieves near-minimax parameter estimation rate s* log d / n.  
- Contribution 2: proposes a decorrelated score statistic that attains asymptotic normality and the semiparametric efficiency bound.  
- Provides a unified framework offering both statistical and computational guarantees; applied to Gaussian mixtures and mixture regression.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with clear conditions (3.1–3.3) and detailed proofs in the appendix.  
- Carefully distinguishes the new high-dimensional analysis from prior low-dimensional EM results; truncation fundamentally changes the analysis.  
- Achieves near-optimal statistical rates and optimal inference variance, addressing high-dimensional nuisance parameters.  
- Well-organized paper with clear notation; key technical concepts (truncation, decorrelated score) are well explained.  
- Appendix is comprehensive, enhancing reproducibility and model-specific verifications.  
- Methodological novelty: extending EM to high dimensions and introducing a decorrelated score for latent variable models.  
- Distinguishes itself from tensor-based and convex high-dimensional methods and provides a unified estimation+inference approach.  
- Addresses an important practical gap given EM’s widespread use in modern high-dimensional applications.  
- Weakness: needs more intuition on why truncation is necessary and how it affects convergence and rates.  
- Weakness: theoretical conditions are abstract and may be hard to verify in practice; sensitivity to sparsity misspecification is not explored.  
- Weakness: paper is dense and may be inaccessible to practitioners; practical guidance for choosing sparsity parameter b_s is limited.  
- Weakness: truncation idea parallels existing thresholding concepts (e.g., Lasso), so novelty is not wholly unprecedented.  
- For acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, novel high-dimensional EM design, and optimal inference procedure.  
- Against acceptance: technical density for a broad audience, limited emphasis on practical implications and applications.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel, and significant contribution to high-dimensional latent variable modeling.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
