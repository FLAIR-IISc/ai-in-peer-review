

# Review of "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper proposes a high-dimensional extension of the EM algorithm with truncation steps to enforce sparsity, and establishes statistical guarantees for both parameter estimation and inference. The main contributions include a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step, theoretical guarantees of geometric convergence to a near-optimal statistical rate, and a decorrelated score statistic for inference.

## Strengths
The paper addresses an important gap in the literature by extending EM to high-dimensional settings with theoretical guarantees. The truncation step is a novel technical contribution that enables sparsity enforcement. The theoretical results for both estimation and inference are comprehensive, and the paper provides concrete implications for specific models like Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression. The comparison with related work is thorough, and the paper clearly articulates how it advances the state-of-the-art.

## Weaknesses and Concerns

### 1. Formatting and Notational Inconsistencies
The paper contains numerous notational inconsistencies that undermine its technical credibility. The use of "⇤" for the true parameter (e.g., "⇤ 2 Rd") is confusing and non-standard - the asterisk symbol is typically used for duals or as a superscript, not as a parameter symbol. The paper should adopt standard notation (e.g., θ* or θ^*). Similarly, the sparsity parameter "bs" is poorly chosen (should be s_b or s_t) and inconsistently used throughout the paper.

### 2. Technical Errors in Key Equations
Several critical equations contain mathematical errors that affect the paper's validity:
- In Equation (1.1), the statistical error term is written as "p s⇤ · log d/n" instead of the correct "√(s* log d / n)"
- In Equation (3.10), the statistical error term is "p bs+ C 0/ p 1" which should be "√(bs + C_0/(1-ρ))" 
- The paper uses "⇥" and "⇤" symbols in equations (e.g., "⇥ trunc( ,S) ⇤ j = j · 1{j 2 S}") which are not standard mathematical notation and likely represent typos for brackets.

### 3. Unclear Justification for Key Technical Choices
The truncation step (T-step) is central to the paper's approach, but the paper fails to clearly justify why this specific truncation procedure is necessary or optimal. The paper claims it "effectively enforces sparsity," but doesn't establish that alternative truncation strategies would not work equally well. The choice of "bs" as the sparsity parameter is arbitrary without clear theoretical justification.

### 4. Overstated Claims
The paper claims "near-minimax-optimal" statistical rates, but doesn't explicitly state the minimax lower bound for the specific models they consider. Without this comparison, the claim of "near-minimax-optimality" lacks concrete foundation. Similarly, the claim that their approach "establishes the first theoretical guarantee of parameter estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensional regimes for the EM algorithm" is questionable - [23] has established related results for high-dimensional mixture of regression models.

### 5. Inadequate Treatment of Key Assumptions
The paper relies heavily on Conditions 3.1-3.3, but these conditions are not properly justified for the specific models they apply to. The verification of these conditions for Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models is relegated to the appendix without sufficient detail in the main text. For instance, Condition 3.3 requires "M( ) - Mn( )_1 ≤ ε" but doesn't explain how the statistical error ε scales with n, d, and s* for their specific models.

### 6. Formatting and Presentation Issues
The paper contains numerous formatting inconsistencies in mathematical expressions. For example, the paper uses inconsistent spacing around mathematical operators (e.g., "k ⇤k 2" should be "||θ*||_2"). The reference to "Algorithm 1" is made without including the algorithm in the main text, which is a significant omission for a methodology paper.

## Overall Assessment

While the paper addresses an important problem and contains novel technical elements, the numerous notational inconsistencies, mathematical errors in key equations, and lack of sufficient justification for critical design choices significantly undermine its technical credibility. The paper's claims about "near-minimax-optimality" and "first theoretical guarantee" are overstated without proper supporting evidence.

The paper would benefit from a thorough revision to fix these technical errors and clarify the theoretical justification for the truncation step. As it stands, the paper's technical content is marred by numerous minor but significant flaws that make it difficult to assess the validity of the main claims.

## Recommendation

Reject

The paper's technical errors and notational inconsistencies are too severe to be considered acceptable for publication at NIPS. While the problem addressed is important and the general approach is interesting, the execution contains too many fundamental technical flaws that prevent the claims from being properly evaluated. The authors should revise the paper to fix these issues before resubmission.
