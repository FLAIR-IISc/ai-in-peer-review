- Paper proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a hard-thresholding truncation step to enforce sparsity along the solution path.
- Two M-step variants are analyzed: exact maximization and a single gradient ascent step.
- Theorem 3.4: ℓ2 error at iteration t decomposes into geometric optimization error plus statistical error O(√(s*)·ε), yielding near-minimax rate √(s* log d / n) for GMM and mixture-of-regressions.
- For inference, a decorrelated score statistic tailored to the EM/Q-function is proposed, using a Dantzig-type estimator for the nuisance projection vector w.
- Theorem 4.6: under conditions, the decorrelated score is asymptotically normal and attains the semiparametric information bound, enabling valid tests and CIs for low-dimensional components.
- Builds on low-dimensional EM analyses and debiasing literature, extending decorrelation to non-convex EM/Q-function settings and connecting to iterative hard thresholding ideas.
- Technical novelty: unified framework coupling high-dimensional EM with hard thresholding and simultaneous computational/statistical guarantees.
- Inference novelty: decorrelated score tailored to latent-variable/EM context achieving asymptotic efficiency with high-dimensional nuisance.
- Generality: covers exact and gradient M-steps with model verifications for GMM and mixture-of-regressions.
- Careful separation of optimization vs statistical errors and explicit entrywise control to analyze truncation.
- Main theoretical results rely on a resampling/sample-splitting scheme; unclear if guarantees hold without resampling.
- Requires an initializer within constant relative error; no concrete, computationally tractable high-dimensional initialization method is provided.
- Tuning/practicality issues: bs needs knowledge of s*, stepsize needs µ, ν, and Dantzig tuning depends on unknown quantities; no adaptive calibration guidance.
- Inference relies on sparsity of w* (s*w = o(√(n/log d))), but plausibility beyond provided examples is not discussed.
- No experiments or simulations to assess finite-sample performance, tuning sensitivity, or coverage of proposed tests.
- Manuscript has clarity and formatting issues, dense notation, and some obscure explanations of the truncation step and support evolution.
- Quality: theory is careful but practical impact is reduced by resampling and strong initialization assumptions; some model verifications deferred.
- Originality: novel combination of EM with truncation and decorrelated inference in latent-variable settings.
- Significance: potentially impactful if resampling can be removed and practical tuning/initialization provided.
- Key suggestions: extend analysis to no-resampling EM, provide concrete initialization and tuning guidance, discuss Dantzig computational cost and Lasso alternatives, and include simulations.
- Pros: solid novel theory, asymptotically optimal inference, applicable to important models.
- Cons: reliance on sample splitting and strong initialization, limited practical guidance, strong sparsity assumption on w*, and presentation issues.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept pending empirical validation, removal/clarification of resampling, and improved practical guidance.
