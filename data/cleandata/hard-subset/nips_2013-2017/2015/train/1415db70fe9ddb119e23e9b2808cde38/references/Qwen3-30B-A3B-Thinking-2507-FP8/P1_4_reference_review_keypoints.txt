- Paper addresses EM in high-dimensional settings (d > n) with a novel truncation step enforcing sparsity and provides theoretical guarantees.
- Establishes statistical and computational convergence rates, achieving near-optimal O(sqrt(s* log d / n)) estimation error.
- Develops a decorrelated score statistic yielding asymptotic normality for low-dimensional components and enables inference.
- Framework is applied and verified for Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models with detailed conditions in the appendix.
- Technical quality is high: rigorous proofs, clear decomposition of optimization vs. statistical error, and well-structured theory.
- Original contribution: first theoretical guarantees for high-dimensional EM with sparsity and novel truncation-based algorithm design.
- Significant impact: fills a key gap for high-dimensional latent variable models, with implications for genomics, finance, neuroscience, etc.
- Clarity and presentation are excellent: clear problem framing, consistent notation, thorough related-work comparison, and reproducible appendices.
- Weakness: lacks empirical validation or simulation studies demonstrating practical performance and truncation effects.
- Weakness: relies on strong assumptions (sparsity, minimum signal-to-noise, Dantzig selector sparsity) with limited discussion of practical implications.
- Weakness: does not quantify computational complexity or compare runtime to standard EM, leaving practical cost unclear.
- Weakness: provides no practical guidance for selecting the sparsity tuning parameter b_s.
- Recommendation: accept — theoretical contributions are substantial and justify publication despite noted limitations.
- Pro acceptance: first high-dimensional EM guarantees, near-optimal rates, first inference framework, comprehensive theory, and broad applicability.
- Con acceptance: missing empirical validation, strong assumptions may limit practicality, computational/tuning issues not addressed.
