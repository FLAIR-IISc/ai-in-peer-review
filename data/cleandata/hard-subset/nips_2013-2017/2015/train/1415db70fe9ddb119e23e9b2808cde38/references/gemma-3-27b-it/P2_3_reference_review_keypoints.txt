- Tackles extending EM theoretical guarantees to high-dimensional settings (d > n) with a sparsity-enforcing truncation step.  
- Proposes a truncated EM algorithm and analyzes parameter estimation rates plus asymptotic normality of a decorrelated score for low-dimensional components.  
- Demonstrates applicability to Gaussian Mixture Models and Mixture of Regression Models.  
- Fills a critical gap in EM theory for high-dimensional problems.  
- Truncated EM is a sensible, novel algorithmic contribution to enforce sparsity.  
- Provides rigorous convergence rates and near-minimax optimality, decomposing error into optimization and statistical components.  
- Presents a general framework applicable to multiple latent variable models.  
- Well-connected to existing literature, including insightful comparison with tensor methods.  
- Mostly clear presentation with standard notation and logical structure.  
- Paper is highly technical with heavy notation and many conditions, which may overwhelm non-expert readers.  
- Theoretical results depend on multiple conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) that need careful verification beyond the provided GMM example.  
- Practical issues: selection of sparsity parameter bs and Dantzig selector tuning epsilon, plus computational cost of truncation and gradient-ascent M-step, are not fully addressed.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; more simulations or real-data experiments and CI accuracy checks are needed.  
- Quality rated 4/5: technically sound but many conditions complicate robustness assessment.  
- Clarity rated 3/5: organized but dense; needs more intuitive explanations and worked examples.  
- Originality rated 4/5: novel high-dimensional EM theory and decorrelated score statistic.  
- Significance rated 4/5: provides a theoretical foundation for EM in high-dimensional practice with broad potential impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fills theoretical gap, strong guarantees, practical algorithmic improvement, overall clear motivation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high technical complexity, reliance on numerous conditions, and limited empirical validation.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add intuitive explanations, expand empirical validation, and discuss practical parameter tuning.
