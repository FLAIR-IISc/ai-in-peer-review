

# Review of "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper addresses an important gap in the literature by extending the EM algorithm to high-dimensional settings where dimension d exceeds sample size n. Previous theoretical work on EM (e.g., [2, 30]) established guarantees only for low-dimensional regimes (d << n), but no theoretical guarantees existed for high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors propose a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and establish both statistical and computational guarantees for this algorithm. They further develop a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing in high-dimensional settings, which is novel as previous inference methods for high-dimensional models didn't apply to latent variable models.

**Strengths:**
1. The paper makes a significant contribution by addressing a clear gap in the literature. The high-dimensional EM algorithm with truncation is a natural and clever extension of the classical EM framework.
2. The theoretical results are comprehensive and well-structured, with clear separation of estimation (Theorem 3.4) and inference (Theorem 4.6) guarantees.
3. The paper thoroughly differentiates itself from related work, particularly [2], by explaining how their framework handles high-dimensional challenges through the truncation step.
4. The authors provide detailed verifications of technical conditions for two specific models (Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression), demonstrating the practical applicability of their framework.
5. The decorrelated score statistic achieves the semiparametric information bound, which is a strong theoretical guarantee for inference.

**Weaknesses:**
1. The paper lacks empirical validation. While the theoretical results are compelling, the absence of experiments on synthetic or real data makes it difficult to assess the practical utility of the proposed method. The authors should include at least a small simulation study to demonstrate the performance of their algorithm.
2. The assumption on the sparsity parameter bs (Equation 3.8) requires bs to be of the same order as s* (the true sparsity level), which is typically unknown in practice. The paper doesn't discuss how to choose bs in real applications.
3. The intuition behind why the truncation step (T-step) is necessary for high-dimensional settings isn't fully developed. The paper states it's "key" but doesn't provide clear reasoning for why this specific modification is needed.
4. The paper claims "near-minimax-optimal" rates but doesn't clearly state the minimax lower bound for the specific models they consider, making it hard to assess how close they are to optimal.
5. The technical conditions (Conditions 3.1-3.3, 4.1-4.5) are quite strong and may not hold for many practical models. The paper doesn't discuss the scope of models for which these conditions can be verified.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**
1. Include empirical results demonstrating the performance of the proposed algorithm on synthetic data, comparing it to baseline methods where possible.
2. Discuss practical strategies for selecting the sparsity parameter bs, perhaps through cross-validation or other data-driven approaches.
3. Provide more intuitive explanation for why the truncation step is necessary in high dimensions, possibly by comparing the behavior of the standard EM algorithm versus the proposed high-dimensional version.
4. Clarify the minimax lower bounds for the specific models considered to better establish the optimality claim.
5. Discuss the limitations of the technical conditions and provide a more detailed discussion of which models might satisfy these conditions.

**Overall Assessment:**
This paper makes a valuable theoretical contribution to the literature on high-dimensional latent variable models. The technical results are sound and the framework is general enough to apply to a broad family of models. However, the lack of empirical validation and the strong assumptions on sparsity parameter selection limit the paper's practical impact. With the suggested improvements, particularly including empirical results, this would be a strong candidate for NeurIPS.

**Recommendation:** Weak Accept

The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution that addresses an important gap in the literature. While the theoretical results are strong, the lack of empirical validation and the strong assumptions on sparsity parameter selection prevent a stronger recommendation. The authors should address these limitations in a revised version, particularly by including empirical results to demonstrate the practical utility of their method.
