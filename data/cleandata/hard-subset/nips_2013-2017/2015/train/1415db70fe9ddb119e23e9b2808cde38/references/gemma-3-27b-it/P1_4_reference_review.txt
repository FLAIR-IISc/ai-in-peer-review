## Peer Review: High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models, a regime where existing theory breaks down. The authors propose a novel "high-dimensional EM" algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and demonstrate simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees. Specifically, they prove a convergence rate of O(p s* log(d/n)) for parameter estimation and establish asymptotic normality for a decorrelated score statistic used for hypothesis testing of low-dimensional components. The work builds upon recent progress linking EM to proximal methods and extends it significantly to the high-dimensional setting, addressing a crucial gap in the literature.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the existing literature. It acknowledges the limitations of traditional EM guarantees in high dimensions and builds on recent work by [2] (geometric convergence in low dimensions) and [30] (convergence conditions).  It differentiates itself by addressing the sparsity induced by high dimensionality and providing *statistical* guarantees, not just convergence, in this regime. The authors also aptly compare their work to recent advances in high-dimensional inference, like debiasing and desparsification methods [13, 26, 32], and tensor methods [1, 9], highlighting the unique contributions of their approach - particularly its applicability to non-convex latent variable models.  The comparison to [23] is also valuable, pointing out the advantage of establishing parameter estimation guarantees alongside support recovery.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a Fundamental Gap:** The paper tackles a long-standing open problem – establishing theoretical foundations for EM in high dimensions.
* **Novel Algorithm:** The introduction of the truncation step is a clever way to handle sparsity and enable theoretical analysis.
* **Strong Theoretical Results:**  The convergence rate and asymptotic normality results are significant contributions, offering concrete guarantees for both estimation and inference.
* **Generality:** The framework is presented in a general way, with applications to Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regression models demonstrated. This suggests broader applicability.
* **Clear Connections to Existing Literature:** The authors thoroughly connect their work to relevant research, showcasing awareness of the field and the novelty of their approach.
* **Well-defined Assumptions:** The conditions (3.1-3.3, 4.1-4.5) are clearly stated, albeit detailed, and provide a roadmap for applying the theory.
* **Optimality:** Establishing the optimality of the decorrelated score statistic (reaching the semi-parametric information bound) is a strong result.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Technical Complexity:** The paper is *very* technical and requires significant background knowledge to fully grasp.  The numerous conditions and the density of notation may make it difficult for some readers to follow.
* **Assumptions:**  While well-defined, the assumptions (especially 4.5) are quite strong and might not hold in all practical scenarios.  The sparsity requirement on the nuisance parameter (s*w) feels particularly restrictive. The authors acknowledge this but could benefit from discussing how sensitive the results are to violations of these assumptions.
* **Practical Considerations:** The paper focuses heavily on theoretical guarantees.  A discussion of practical implementation challenges (e.g., tuning the truncation parameter *bs*) and computational cost would be valuable.
* **Empirical Validation:** The paper lacks empirical evaluation. While theoretical guarantees are paramount, demonstrating the performance of the algorithm on simulated or real datasets would significantly strengthen the paper. A brief experimental section comparing the proposed algorithm to existing methods would be highly beneficial.
* **Limited Model Scope:** While the authors mention broader applicability, the detailed analysis is limited to two models. Expanding these examples, or providing guidance on extending the framework to other models, would enhance its impact.

**Arguments for/against Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Fills a critical gap in the theoretical understanding of EM in high dimensions.
* Provides strong, mathematically rigorous results with clear convergence and optimality guarantees.
* The proposed algorithm is novel and potentially impactful.
* The work is of high theoretical quality and demonstrates a deep understanding of the field.

**Con:**

* High technical complexity could limit accessibility.
* Strong assumptions may restrict applicability.
* Lack of empirical validation.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical paper addressing a significant open problem. The technical depth and rigor are commendable, and the results are important contributions to the field.  However, the high level of complexity, strong assumptions, and lack of empirical validation are concerns. 

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. The authors should consider the following:

1. **Improve Clarity:** While avoiding oversimplification, the authors could consider adding more intuitive explanations of key concepts and assumptions.
2. **Sensitivity Analysis:** Discuss the robustness of the results to violations of the key assumptions, particularly the sparsity requirement on the nuisance parameter.
3. **Practical Discussion:**  Include a discussion of practical implementation considerations, such as parameter tuning and computational complexity.
4. **Empirical Illustration:**  Even a small-scale empirical evaluation, perhaps on simulated data, would substantially strengthen the paper and demonstrate the practical relevance of the proposed algorithm.
5. **Expand Examples:** Briefly discuss how the framework could be extended to other latent variable models.



I believe addressing these points would significantly enhance the paper and solidify its position as a valuable contribution to the NIPS community.
