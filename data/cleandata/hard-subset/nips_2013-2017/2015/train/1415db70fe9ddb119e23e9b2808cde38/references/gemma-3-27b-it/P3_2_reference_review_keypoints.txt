- Problem & contribution: Proposes a “high-dimensional EM” with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and provides statistical convergence rates and asymptotic normality for a decorrelated score, applicable to GMM and mixture regression with exact or gradient M-steps.  
- Extension of prior work: Extends low-dimensional EM theory to high dimensions and situates the approach relative to Lasso-based and tensor methods, with a relevant comparison to prior high-dimensional mixture regression work.  
- Strength — fills gap: Addresses the open problem of theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Strength — algorithmic novelty: Truncation step is a theoretically grounded, novel mechanism to enable sparsity and analysis.  
- Strength — theoretical breadth: Provides a unified, rigorous analysis covering estimation, inference, and computational/statistical trade-offs.  
- Strength — optimality: Decorrelated score achieves semiparametric information bound, indicating optimal inference.  
- Strength — generality: Framework applies to multiple latent-variable models and is well-presented.  
- Weakness — readability: Highly technical with many conditions and dense notation, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness — strong conditions: Some assumptions (e.g., condition 4.5 and s*w requirements) appear strong and their practical validity is unclear.  
- Weakness — limited experiments: No empirical evaluation on synthetic or real data to validate truncation benefits or the decorrelated score in practice.  
- Weakness — initialization sensitivity: Requires strong initialization; lacks practical guidance on obtaining it.  
- For acceptance: Novel, rigorous, impactful theoretical contribution that addresses a significant challenge.  
- Against acceptance: Technical complexity, strong assumptions needing scrutiny, and lack of empirical validation weaken practical impact.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper meriting acceptance, but should add discussion of practical implications of assumptions and ideally some empirical results.  
- Recommendation & confidence: Strong Accept; Confidence 8/10.
