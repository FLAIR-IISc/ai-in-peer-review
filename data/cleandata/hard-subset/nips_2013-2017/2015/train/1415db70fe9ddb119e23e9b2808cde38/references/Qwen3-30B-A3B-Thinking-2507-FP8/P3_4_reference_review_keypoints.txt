- Summary: Develops a high-dimensional EM with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, proving geometric convergence, near-minimax statistical error O(√(s* log d / n)), and asymptotic normality attaining semiparametric efficiency; applied to Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions.

- Strength 1: The truncation step is a novel, necessary innovation for high-dimensional sparsity and the proofs handling its effect are technically sophisticated.

- Strength 2: Provides unified computational (Theorem 3.4) and statistical (Theorem 4.6) guarantees, decomposing error into optimization and statistical components; achieves semiparametric efficiency.

- Strength 3: Framework supports both exact maximization and gradient-ascent M-steps and verifies conditions for key models, showing practical applicability.

- Strength 4: Clearly situates contributions relative to prior low- and high-dimensional EM/inference literature.

- Weakness 1: Verifying technical conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.4) is complex and may limit applicability to other latent-variable models beyond the examples provided.

- Weakness 2: The truncation parameter bs depends on unknown quantities like s* and the paper lacks practical guidance (e.g., cross-validation) for selecting bs; theoretical value may be conservative.

- Weakness 3: The claimed near-minimax error rate lacks explicit comparison to minimax lower bounds for the specific models (no direct lower-bound justification provided).

- Weakness 4: No comparison of computational complexity with alternative high-dimensional methods (e.g., tensor methods or penalized likelihood approaches), raising scalability concerns.

- Critical assessment: Theoretical contribution is compelling and fills a real gap; truncation is natural and proofs are solid, but practical impact is limited by parameter-selection and comparative/empirical analyses.

- Suggested improvements: Add practical strategies for choosing bs, explicit minimax lower bound comparisons, computational complexity analysis versus competitors, and at least brief empirical/simulation results.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance conditional on addressing implementation concerns (especially bs selection); theoretical novelty and rigor justify publication.
