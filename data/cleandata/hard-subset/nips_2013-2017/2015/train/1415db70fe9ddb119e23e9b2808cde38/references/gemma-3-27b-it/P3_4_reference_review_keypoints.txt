- Paper addresses lack of theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional (d > n) settings by proposing a sparsity-enforcing truncation in a "high-dimensional EM" algorithm.  
- Establishes convergence rate ~ p√s * log(d/n) and asymptotic normality of a novel decorrelated score statistic for inference on low-dimensional parameters.  
- Truncation step is a simple, novel device that enforces sparsity and enables theoretical analysis.  
- Decorrelated score statistic enables valid hypothesis testing with high-dimensional nuisance parameters and attains the semiparametric information bound.  
- Framework is general and applicable to multiple latent variable models (GMMs, mixture of regressions, etc.).  
- Paper builds on and properly situates itself within prior EM convergence literature and provides clear notation and organization with an extensive appendix.  
- Main strength: first strong theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional regimes, with rigorous, near-optimal statistical results.  
- Main weakness: high technical complexity limits accessibility to non-experts.  
- Main weakness: theoretical results rely on several strong, hard-to-verify technical assumptions (notably sparsity of nuisance parameters/Fisher information).  
- Main weakness: constant factors in asymptotic rates are not quantified, leaving practical significance unclear.  
- Main weakness: truncation increases per-iteration computational cost; practical trade-offs are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Main weakness: empirical evaluation is limited; more experiments and comparisons on realistic datasets are needed.  
- Argument for acceptance: solves a long-standing open problem with novel, rigorous contributions and potential practical impact.  
- Argument against acceptance: strong assumptions, technical complexity, limited empirical results, and computational concerns may reduce practical applicability.  
- Overall assessment: strong, important theoretical contribution; merits acceptance despite limitations.  
- Recommendations: clarify/illustrate assumptions and verification in practice (or simplify for specific models).  
- Recommendations: discuss and, if possible, quantify constant factors in rates and strategies to minimize them.  
- Recommendations: expand empirical evaluation and compare to existing methods across datasets and truncation settings.  
- Recommendations: analyze computational trade-offs of truncation and propose mitigation strategies.  
- Recommendations: add a short practical guide for choosing key parameters (sparsity level, truncation parameters).
