- First comprehensive inferential theory for high-dimensional EM; introduces a truncation (T-) step to enforce sparsity.  
- Unified theoretical framework proving geometric convergence to near-minimax rate O(√(s* log d / n)).  
- Establishes asymptotic normality of a decorrelated score statistic achieving semiparametric efficiency.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with careful comparison to prior low-dimensional and tensor-method work.  
- Technical rigor: detailed, entrywise error analysis suited to high-dimensional settings.  
- No empirical validation—paper is purely theoretical with no simulations or real-data experiments.  
- Missing practical implementation details: no guidance on choosing sparsity parameter bs or on computational complexity and truncation cost.  
- Limited discussion on how realistic or restrictive the technical assumptions are in practice.  
- Insufficient comparison to other high-dimensional approaches (e.g., sparse EM for mixture of regression [23]).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant theoretical contribution that fills an important gap.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Suggestion: Add discussion or methods for selecting the sparsity parameter bs (potentially data-driven).  
- Suggestion: Include a small simulation study (e.g., Gaussian mixture) to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Suggestion: Expand discussion of the practical relevance/restrictiveness of technical conditions, possibly with a summary table.  
- Suggestion: Strengthen empirical and theoretical comparison with other high-dimensional latent-variable methods, particularly [23].
