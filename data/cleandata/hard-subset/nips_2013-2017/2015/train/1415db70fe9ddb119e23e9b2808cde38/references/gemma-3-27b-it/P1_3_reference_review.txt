## Peer Review: High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality

**Summary:**

This paper addresses a significant gap in the theoretical understanding of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm – its behavior in high-dimensional settings. While EM is a cornerstone for latent variable models, existing theory largely focuses on the low-dimensional case where guarantees are tied to global optima. This work proposes a modified EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and develops a comprehensive inferential theory establishing both statistical and computational guarantees for parameter estimation and uncertainty quantification in high dimensions.  The key results include a statistical rate of convergence of  O(p s* log(d/n)) and asymptotic normality of a decorrelated score statistic for low-dimensional parameters, achieving optimality in the semi-parametric information bound. The authors demonstrate their framework on Gaussian Mixture Models (GMM) and Mixture of Regression Models.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The authors correctly position their work within the existing literature. They build upon recent progress in understanding the convergence of EM in low dimensions [2, 18, 25, 30] by tackling the high-dimensional challenge.  They differentiate themselves from [2] by addressing the d > n regime and enforcing sparsity through truncation. Compared to methods like tensor decomposition [1] and approaches utilizing debiasing or post-selection [4, 13, 15, 16, 17, 19, 20, 22, 23, 24, 26, 29, 31, 32], the authors provide a unified framework for EM specifically, dealing with the non-convexity inherent in latent variable models and offering a novel inferential approach directly applicable to the EM output.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a Crucial Gap:** The paper tackles a fundamental open problem in statistical learning - theoretical guarantees for EM in high dimensions.
* **Novel Technical Contributions:** The introduction of the truncation step is a clever approach to enforce sparsity and enable rigorous analysis. The development of the decorrelated score statistic is a significant contribution for inference in the presence of high-dimensional nuisance parameters.
* **Comprehensive Theoretical Analysis:** The paper provides a detailed and mathematically rigorous analysis, establishing both statistical rates of convergence and asymptotic normality.  The unified framework is appealing.
* **Clear Presentation (mostly):** The paper is generally well-structured, and the algorithmic descriptions are clear.
* **Demonstrated Applicability:** Applying the framework to GMM and Mixture of Regression models strengthens the paper and shows its practical relevance.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Technical Density:** The paper is extremely dense and requires significant effort to fully grasp. While rigor is appreciated, some parts could benefit from more intuitive explanations. The numerous conditions (3.1-3.3 and 4.1-4.5) are presented without much justification beyond their technical necessity.
* **Parameter Tuning Sensitivity:** The performance heavily relies on correctly tuning parameters like the sparsity level *bs* and the step size *⌘*. The paper provides guidelines, but a more practical discussion of sensitivity analysis and robust parameter selection strategies would be valuable.
* **Computational Cost:** While the statistical rate is optimal, the computational cost of the truncated EM algorithm, particularly the T-step, is not thoroughly discussed.  A comparison with other high-dimensional estimation methods (e.g., variational inference, spectral methods) in terms of computational complexity would be helpful.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The paper focuses primarily on theoretical guarantees.  While the application to GMM and Mixture of Regression is noted, concrete experimental results demonstrating the performance of the algorithm on real or simulated datasets are lacking.
* **Assumptions:** The numerous assumptions required for the theoretical results (particularly in section 4) are quite strong and might limit the applicability of the framework in practice. A discussion about the robustness of the results to violations of these assumptions would be beneficial.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a fundamental open problem with significant theoretical and practical implications.
* The technical contributions are novel and substantial.
* The rigorous analysis provides strong guarantees for the proposed algorithm.
* The framework is general and applicable to a wide range of latent variable models.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper is highly technical and may be inaccessible to a broad audience.
* The lack of extensive empirical validation limits the assessment of practical performance.
* The strong assumptions required for the theoretical results raise concerns about the applicability of the framework in real-world scenarios.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant theoretical contributions to the understanding of EM in high dimensions.  While the technical density and lack of empirical validation are drawbacks, the novelty and importance of the results outweigh these concerns. The work is a valuable addition to the NIPS community.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I recommend acceptance contingent on the following revisions:

1. **Improve Clarity:** Add more intuitive explanations for key concepts and technical details.  Provide more motivation for the various conditions imposed.
2. **Empirical Validation:** Include at least a limited set of experiments on simulated or real datasets to demonstrate the performance of the algorithm and validate the theoretical results.
3. **Computational Cost Discussion:**  Provide a discussion of the computational complexity of the algorithm and compare it to other relevant methods.
4. **Sensitivity Analysis:** Briefly address the sensitivity of the results to parameter tuning.
5. **Assumption Discussion:**  Discuss the robustness of the theoretical results to violations of the assumptions, or provide guidelines on how to verify these assumptions in practice.
