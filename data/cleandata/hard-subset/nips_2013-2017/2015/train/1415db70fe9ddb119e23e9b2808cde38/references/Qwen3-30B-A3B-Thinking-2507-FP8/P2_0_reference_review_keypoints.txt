- Addresses the lack of theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models (d >> n).  
- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and enable analysis.  
- Establishes statistical and computational guarantees for parameter estimation under the proposed algorithm.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing that achieves asymptotic normality and the semiparametric information bound.  
- Achieves near-optimal estimation rate O(√(s* log d / n)) and provides a unified framework for estimation and inference.  
- Verifies theoretical conditions for Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.  
- Builds on low-dimensional EM theory and contrasts with tensor methods and Lasso debiasing approaches.  
- Novel technical contribution: the truncation step and detailed analysis of optimization vs. statistical errors.  
- Paper is technically rigorous, well-written, and provides precise conditions and proofs (with appendix references).  
- Main weaknesses: lack of empirical validation on synthetic/real data and potentially restrictive assumptions for the decorrelated test.  
- Originality: first theoretical guarantees for high-dimensional EM and novel inference approach for non-convex latent variable models.  
- Scope limitation: demonstrated on two specific models, so broader applicability is claimed but not extensively shown.  
- Practical limitation: reliance on sparsity and minimum signal-to-noise ratio assumptions may restrict real-world applicability.  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: high-quality, significant theoretical contribution; recommended for acceptance.
