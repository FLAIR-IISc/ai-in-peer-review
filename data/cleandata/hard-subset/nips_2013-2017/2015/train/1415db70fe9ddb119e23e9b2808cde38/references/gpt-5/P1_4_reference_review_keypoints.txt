- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation (hard-thresholding) T-step, analyzes exact and gradient M-steps, and proves a nonasymptotic error split: geometric optimization decay + statistical error ≈ sqrt(s* log d / n).  
- Introduces a decorrelated score test using a Dantzig-selector orthogonalization vector, proving asymptotic normality that attains semiparametric efficiency; examples: Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions.  
- Builds on low-dimensional EM and desparsification ideas, adapting them to latent-variable settings and aiming for better rates than tensor/moment methods.  
- Strength: addresses estimation and valid inference for high-dimensional latent-variable models with a clean T-step algorithm and general analysis covering both M-step variants.  
- Strength: provides nonasymptotic theory with explicit optimization/statistical error decomposition and near-optimal rates for sparse parameters.  
- Strength: inference result is semiparametrically efficient and ties Q-derivatives explicitly to score and Fisher information.  
- Weakness: requires strong assumptions including a good initialization in the basin of attraction, but offers no practical, guaranteed initialization strategy for the considered models.  
- Weakness: tuning/practicality issues—sparsity level bs must match unknown s*, several bounds need constants (μ, ν, ρ), and guidance for tuning is limited.  
- Weakness: resampling-based analysis is impractical; implications for single-sample algorithm runs are unclear.  
- Weakness: inference needs sparsity of w* (inverse information) which may be unrealistic in mixtures; lacks discussion/empirical evidence on this.  
- Weakness: no empirical evaluation—missing simulations for rates, support recovery, inference coverage/power, and robustness compared to alternatives like ℓ1-penalized EM.  
- Weakness: clarity problems—typographical artifacts, undefined/misaligned symbols, inconsistent norms, and malformed/incomplete references.  
- Missing comparison/theory for ℓ1-penalized EM (soft-thresholding) vs hard truncation and discussion of bias/variance/support recovery trade-offs.  
- Quality: theoretically plausible and technically nontrivial; conditions are clearly stated and verified for examples in the appendix, but reliance on unverified constants/tuning weakens empirical credibility.  
- Clarity: overall structure is reasonable but dense; algorithm is clear yet guidance on tuning/stopping is minimal and references need fixing.  
- Originality: integrating hard-thresholding into EM plus decorrelated-score inference for latent-variable models is novel and valuable.  
- Significance: potentially high impact if results generalize and are implemented robustly, enabling estimation and valid inference in high-d latent-variable models.  
- Key suggestions: provide practical initialization methods with guarantees, adaptive bs selection or refitting, compare to ℓ1-EM, clarify Dantzig practicality/tuning (or nodewise alternatives), discuss w* sparsity/approximate sparsity, translate resampling bounds to single-run results, and add simulations for GMM and mixture regression.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept (leaning accept) conditional on adding simulations, clarifying initialization/tuning, and fixing presentation; reviewer confidence: moderate.
