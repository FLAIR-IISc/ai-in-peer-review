- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm that adds a hard-thresholding (truncation) step and a decorrelated score for inference under sparsity when d ≫ n.  
- Provides a unified analysis with an optimization–statistics error decomposition showing geometric contraction from suitable initialization.  
- Shows near-minimax estimation rate O(√(s* log d / n)) (up to logs) under stated conditions.  
- Proves asymptotic normality of a decorrelated score for a low-dimensional component, achieving the semiparametric efficiency bound despite high-dimensional nuisance.  
- Covers two M-step implementations: exact maximization and one-step gradient ascent.  
- Verifies required conditions for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions under separation/SNR and sparsity; relates Q-function derivatives to log-likelihood (Thm 2.1).  
- Uses a resampling/split-sample variant of the algorithm for theoretical analysis (technical convenience).  
- Builds on population-to-sample EM analyses and high-dimensional debiasing literature, contrasting with tensor/spectral methods and penalized-EM approaches.  
- Claim of novelty: hard-thresholded EM combined with decorrelated-score inference for latent variable models appears new.  
- Technical arguments are plausible and assumptions are explicit (Lipschitz-gradient, concavity/smoothness, entrywise M-step error, concentration/Lipschitz of Tn).  
- Inference result carefully treats nuisance adjustment and links to Fisher information, attaining efficiency.  
- Key caveats: requires good initialization within a basin, strong SNR/separation, truncation level b_s ≍ s*, and sparsity of the decorrelation vector w*.  
- Main estimation theorem proved for resampling variant; unclear whether identical rates hold for standard single-sample EM iterates.  
- Inference conditions involve several opaque quantities (ξEM, ξG, ξT, ξL) and sample-size scaling, though verifiable for the two examples.  
- No empirical evaluation is provided; experiments (estimation, support recovery, type-I error/power, coverage) and baselines are recommended.  
- Presentation issues: heavy notation, formatting artifacts, incomplete references, and limited practical guidance on tuning b_s, stepsizes, and Dantzig tuning.  
- Practical concerns: computational cost of Dantzig/Hessian computations, robustness to misspecified sparsity, and scalability not discussed.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject; strong theory but needs empirical validation, clarification of resampling vs standard EM, and improved presentation to be NeurIPS-ready.  
- Suggested improvements: add thorough experiments, clarify applicability to standard EM, provide guidance on tuning/initialization, and address computational costs.  
- Questions for authors: (1) Do results extend to non-resampling EM iterates? (2) How to choose b_s without knowing s* and robustness to mis-specification? (3) Which initialization strategies have guarantees for the two models? (4) Can nodewise regressions replace the Dantzig step and what is the complexity? (5) How sensitive are results to model misspecification and label switching?
