- Tackles theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent-variable models (d >> n), proposing a truncated EM algorithm with inferential theory and asymptotic normality.  
- Claims convergence to a statistically optimal rate (stated as p√s* log(d/n)) for parameter estimation and asymptotic normality for a decorrelated score statistic.  
- Framework is general and applicable to models like Gaussian Mixture Models and Mixtures of Regression, and supports exact or gradient-ascent M-steps.  
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM convergence results to the high-dimensional regime, addressing a critical literature gap.  
- Differentiates from tensor methods and high-dimensional GLM inference, highlighting novel challenges due to latent variables and non-concave likelihoods.  
- Strength: Addresses an important open problem in theoretical latent-variable modeling.  
- Strength: Provides a strong, rigorous theoretical framework with convergence rates and asymptotic results under verifiable conditions.  
- Strength: Proposes a novel truncated EM algorithm to enforce sparsity in high dimensions.  
- Strength: Broad applicability across M-step implementations and multiple models.  
- Strength: Establishes asymptotic optimality of the proposed score test.  
- Strength: Presents a detailed, well-structured mathematical analysis of statistical and computational trade-offs.  
- Weakness: Paper is mathematically dense with many technical conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) that may overwhelm non-expert readers.  
- Weakness: Constant factors in rates (C, C0, etc.) lack explicit dependence on parameters like sparsity and SNR, limiting practical interpretability.  
- Weakness: Insufficient practical guidance on implementing the truncation step and choosing the sparsity parameter bs.  
- Weakness: Limited empirical validation—no simulation study to demonstrate algorithm performance or validate asymptotic normality.  
- Weakness: Condition 4.6 is stringent, requiring true parameter sparsity w* = o(√(n/log n)), which restricts applicability.  
- For acceptance: Significant theoretical advance, potential high impact, and sound methodology argue in favor.  
- Against acceptance: High technical barrier, lack of empirical evidence, and the stringent Condition 4.6 argue against.  
- Overall: Significant theoretical contribution whose importance and rigor outweigh technical complexity and limited empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: clarify and give intuition for technical conditions; discuss dependence of constants on key parameters; provide implementation guidance for truncation and bs selection; explain and justify Condition 4.6; consider adding simulation results.
