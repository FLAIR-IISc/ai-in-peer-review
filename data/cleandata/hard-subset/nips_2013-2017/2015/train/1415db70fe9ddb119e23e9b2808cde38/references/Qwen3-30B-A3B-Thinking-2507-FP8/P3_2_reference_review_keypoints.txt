- Paper develops a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, providing estimation and inference guarantees for latent variable models.  
- Main contributions: near-minimax optimal parameter estimation via high-dimensional EM and a decorrelated score statistic with asymptotic normality for low-dimensional inference.  
- Framework validated theoretically for Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.  
- Novelty: first theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable settings.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: error decomposition into optimization (geometric) and statistical (near-optimal) components.  
- Provides asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency for the proposed decorrelated score statistic.  
- Thorough comparison with related work, highlighting differences from low-dimensional EM, Lasso-based, and tensor methods.  
- Technical rigor: well-motivated conditions and verification for specific models relegated to the appendix.  
- Practical relevance: truncation step is a natural way to impose sparsity in high-dimensional applications.  
- Weakness: key assumption verifications are deferred to the appendix rather than shown in the main text.  
- Weakness: no computational complexity analysis or discussion of iteration counts and impact of truncation on runtime.  
- Weakness: lack of guidance on choosing the sparsity parameter bs in practice (e.g., cross-validation).  
- Weakness: claim of "near-minimax optimality" lacks an explicit minimax lower bound for the considered models.  
- Weakness: sparsity assumption on w* (s_w = o(√n/log d)) is not justified for the specific models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures).  
- Practical applicability concern: results depend on a suitable initialization within a basin of attraction, with no practical method provided for high-dimensional initialization.  
- Ambiguity about the minimax claim: statistical rate √(s* log d / n) is plausible but needs formal lower bound comparison.  
- Clarity issues: intuition for the truncation step and its effect on convergence is insufficiently explained.  
- Clarity issues: the decorrelated score statistic (eq.2.7) is dense and needs a more intuitive explanation.  
- Clarity issues: relationship between population and sample M-steps and differing norm choices (ℓ1 vs ℓ2) is confusing.  
- Suggestion: include key verification steps for Gaussian mixture and regression models in the main text.  
- Suggestion: add computational complexity analysis including iteration counts and per-iteration cost.  
- Suggestion: provide practical guidance for selecting bs (e.g., cross-validation or data-driven methods).  
- Suggestion: clarify or provide minimax lower bounds to support the "near-minimax" claim.  
- Suggestion: justify the sparsity assumption on w* or discuss implications if it fails.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant theoretical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Pro arguments: first theoretical guarantees for high-dimensional EM, novel truncation for sparsity, first inference framework for such models, strong theoretical rates and efficiency.  
- Con arguments: deferred assumption verifications, missing computational and practical details, ambiguous minimax claim, and unjustified sparsity assumption on w*.
