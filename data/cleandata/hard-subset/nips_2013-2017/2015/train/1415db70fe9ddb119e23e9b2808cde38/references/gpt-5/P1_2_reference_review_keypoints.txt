- Paper proposes a high-dimensional EM with a hard-thresholding truncation (T-step) and a decorrelated-score inference based on Q-function derivatives.
- Theorem 3.4: under suitable initialization/regularity, iterates converge geometrically to a sparse estimator with error ~sqrt(s* log d / n) (up to log n).
- Analysis covers both exact M-step and gradient-ascent approximate M-step.
- Inference: a Dantzig-selector-based decorrelated score yields asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency for low-dimensional targets (Theorem 4.6).
- Instantiated for Gaussian mixture models and mixtures of regressions.
- Extends low-dimensional population-to-sample EM theory to high dimensions with sparsity.
- Differs from penalized or l1-EM by using hard truncation and analyzing support dynamics along EM trajectory.
- Connects to debiasing/desparsification literature by adapting decorrelated scores to latent-variable models via Q-function identities.
- Conceptual advance: simple truncation integrated into EM achieving near-optimal high-dimensional rates.
- Unified computational-statistical guarantees: decomposition into geometric optimization error and sharp statistical control.
- Inference contribution: tailored decorrelated score with asymptotic normality and efficiency.
- Generality: framework formulated to be verifiable model-by-model.
- Relation to prior work is thoughtfully discussed.
- Strong assumptions: uses resampling/sample-splitting across iterations to control per-iterate error, raising practicality concerns.
- Initialization requirement: needs starting point within a basin of attraction; concrete feasible initializers are not provided.
- Tuning and sparsity knowledge: requires truncation level bs ≈ s* and choices for η, Dantzig λ, stopping rules; practical guidance is absent.
- Inference assumptions: sparsity of w* and heavy Assumption 4.5 may be strong; sample size requirements could be stringent.
- Scope limited: only two models worked out; other latent-variable models (e.g., probit mixtures, HMMs) not addressed.
- No experiments: lacks synthetic/real-data validation for estimation, support recovery, contraction, and inference calibration.
- Presentation: notation-heavy, incomplete references, and missing connections to iterative hard-thresholding literature.
- Theory appears technically careful and largely sound, but reliance on resampling and strong SNR/basin assumptions is a limitation.
- Recommend including proof sketches, assumption/rate summary tables, guidance for bs/η/λ/initialization, and discussion of removing sample splitting.
- Originality: novel combination of EM with hard truncation and decorrelated Q-derivative score for latent-variable inference.
- Significance: bridges computation and inference in high-dimensional latent-variable models if made practical.
- Questions for authors: can resampling be removed or mitigated; what concrete initializers guarantee the basin condition; sensitivity of inference to w* sparsity; practical selection of bs and λ; per-iteration computational cost vs l1-EM.
- Pros: strong theory, simple T-step modification, covers exact and gradient EM.
- Cons: heavy assumptions, reliance on resampling, no experiments, limited model coverage, missing tuning guidance.
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on adding experiments and clarifying practical aspects (initialization, tuning, single-sample analysis).
- Reviewer confidence: high on theory assessment; medium on practicality.
