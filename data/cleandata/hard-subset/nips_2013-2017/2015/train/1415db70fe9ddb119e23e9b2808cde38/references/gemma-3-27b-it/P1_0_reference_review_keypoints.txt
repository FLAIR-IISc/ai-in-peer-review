- Tackles EM behavior in high-dimensional regimes (d > n) via a modified EM with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, with theory for estimation and asymptotic normality, applied to GMMs and MRMs.
- Addresses a critical open problem in theoretical understanding of EM in high dimensions.
- Introduces a novel truncation-based algorithmic modification to handle high dimensionality and induce sparsity.
- Develops a unified theoretical framework linking computational convergence of the algorithm and statistical properties of the estimator.
- Achieves a (near-)minimax-optimal statistical rate (p s* · log d / n).
- Establishes asymptotic normality and proposes a decorrelated score statistic for uncertainty quantification and hypothesis testing.
- Paper is well-written with clear notation and concise algorithm presentation.
- Thoroughly connects to and compares with related work (e.g., [2], [25], [30]).
- Highly technical presentation may limit accessibility to non-experts in statistical learning and optimization.
- Results rely on multiple conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) that need verification for specific applications; only GMMs are worked out in detail.
- Constant factors in theoretical bounds are not explicitly quantified, limiting assessment of practical implications.
- Empirical evaluation is limited; more simulations or real-data experiments would strengthen the paper.
- The sparsity assumption on the nuisance parameter w* (Assumption 4.5) may be restrictive and needs discussion of practical impact.
- Overall assessment: strong and important contribution with substantial theoretical advances; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to clarify conditions, discuss sparsity implications, and add experimental evidence.
- Recommendation and score: Strong Accept, 8.5/10.
