- Summary: Proposes a "high-dimensional EM" with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, proving parameter convergence rate O(p s* log(d/n)) and asymptotic normality for a decorrelated score statistic for inference.

- Relation to Prior Work: Extends low-dimensional EM theory to high-dimensional sparse settings, situating itself among debiasing/desparsification and tensor methods while providing statistical guarantees not present in prior EM analyses.

- Strength: Addresses a fundamental open problem by providing rigorous theoretical foundations for EM in high dimensions.

- Strength: Introduces a novel truncation-based algorithmic step that enables sparsity control and theoretical analysis.

- Strength: Delivers strong theoretical results including convergence rates and semiparametric-optimal inference via a decorrelated score.

- Strength: Framework is fairly general and demonstrated on Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions, suggesting broader applicability.

- Strength: Thorough literature connections and clearly stated (if numerous) assumptions guiding applicability.

- Weakness: Very technical presentation with dense notation and many conditions, which may limit accessibility.

- Weakness: Some assumptions are strong (notably the sparsity requirement on nuisance parameters, Assumption 4.5) and may be restrictive in practice.

- Weakness: Lacks discussion of practical implementation issues such as tuning the truncation parameter and computational cost.

- Weakness: No empirical validation — simulated or real-data experiments are missing to demonstrate practical performance.

- Weakness: Detailed analysis is limited to two models; guidance on extending to other latent-variable models is sparse.

- Pro argument: Fills a critical theoretical gap with rigorous, novel, and potentially impactful results for high-dimensional latent-variable inference.

- Con argument: Accessibility, assumption strength, and absence of empirical results reduce immediate practical impact.

- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contribution deserving acceptance, but needs clarification and empirical support to maximize impact.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, discuss assumption sensitivity, address practical implementation/tuning, add empirical illustration, and expand guidance for other models.
