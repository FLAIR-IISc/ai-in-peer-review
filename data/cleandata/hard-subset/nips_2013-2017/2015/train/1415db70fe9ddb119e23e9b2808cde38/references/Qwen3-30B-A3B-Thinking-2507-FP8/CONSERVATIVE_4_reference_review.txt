

# Review of "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper proposes a high-dimensional extension of the EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, claiming to establish the first theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors present a framework that achieves a geometric rate of convergence with a statistical error of √(s*log(d)/n), and propose a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing.

**Strengths:**
- The paper is well-organized and clearly presents the theoretical framework.
- The authors properly cite relevant prior work, including [2] for low-dimensional EM analysis and [23] for high-dimensional mixture of regression.
- The theoretical results are formally stated with clear assumptions and proofs in the appendix.
- The application to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models demonstrates the framework's applicability.

**Weaknesses:**
The paper suffers from several significant concerns that undermine its contribution as a substantial advancement in the field:

1. **Overstated novelty:** The authors claim to provide "the first theoretical guarantee of parameter estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensional regimes for the EM algorithm," but [23] already analyzes gradient EM for high-dimensional mixture of regression with support recovery guarantees. The authors' distinction between "support recovery" and "parameter estimation" appears artificial, as the latter typically requires the former. The truncation step they introduce is a simple heuristic not substantially different from standard sparsity-inducing methods.

2. **Lack of empirical validation:** Despite claiming "computationally feasible" approach, the paper provides no computational experiments or comparisons with existing methods. Without empirical demonstration of the method's practical performance, the theoretical claims remain unverified.

3. **Unjustified theoretical claims:** The statistical rate √(s*log(d)/n) is standard for sparse high-dimensional models and is not novel. The paper fails to clearly demonstrate why their method achieves this rate more efficiently than existing approaches (e.g., L1-regularized EM, which has been well-studied).

4. **Questionable innovation:** The truncation step (line 6 of Algorithm 1) is a straightforward modification to the standard EM algorithm. This is not a significant methodological advancement but rather a simple heuristic that could be applied to many optimization algorithms. The authors do not convincingly demonstrate why this specific truncation is superior to other sparsity-inducing techniques.

5. **Inadequate comparison with alternatives:** The paper mentions tensor methods [1] but fails to properly contrast their statistical rates (e.g., d^6/n for tensor methods vs. √(d/n) for their method). The comparison is superficial and doesn't address why their approach would be preferred in practice.

6. **Unverified assumptions:** The theoretical guarantees depend on several technical conditions (Conditions 3.1-3.3, 4.1-4.4) that require model-specific verification. The paper only verifies these for Gaussian mixture models in the appendix, without demonstrating how widely applicable these conditions are.

**Significance concerns:** While high-dimensional latent variable models are important, the paper's contribution does not substantially advance the state of the art. The statistical rate they achieve is standard, and the computational advantages they claim remain unverified. The decorrelated score statistic is essentially a standard debiasing technique adapted to EM, not a novel contribution.

**Recommendation:** The paper presents an incremental extension of existing EM methodology with limited novelty and insufficient validation. The theoretical claims are not sufficiently supported by empirical evidence, and the methodological innovation appears overstated. While the paper is technically sound and well-written, it does not provide a compelling advancement over existing methods for high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors should either provide empirical validation of their method or significantly strengthen their theoretical claims by demonstrating clear advantages over established approaches.

The paper would be significantly improved by:
1. Providing computational experiments comparing their method with existing approaches
2. More clearly distinguishing their contribution from prior work on high-dimensional EM
3. Demonstrating why their truncation step offers advantages over standard sparsity-inducing methods
4. Providing a more thorough comparison with tensor methods and other high-dimensional approaches

As a skeptical reviewer who prefers established methods and rigorous validation, I find this paper's claims overreaching and its contribution insufficiently validated. The paper is not sufficiently novel or impactful to warrant acceptance at NIPS.
