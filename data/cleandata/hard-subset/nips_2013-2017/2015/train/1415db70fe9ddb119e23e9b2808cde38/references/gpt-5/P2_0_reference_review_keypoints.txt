- Proposes a high-dimensional sparse EM with hard-thresholding and analyzes exact and gradient M-steps, yielding nonasymptotic error bounds decomposed into optimization and statistical errors.  
- Obtains statistical rates ~sqrt(s* log d / n) and geometric optimization convergence under model conditions.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for inference on low-dimensional components achieving semiparametric efficiency and asymptotic normality.  
- Verifies framework for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions under separation/SNR and sparsity assumptions.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM theory to high dimensions with entrywise control tailored to truncation.  
- Contrasts with convex-penalized and tensor methods by targeting nonconvex latent models and achieving near-minimax rates without high-order moments.  
- Strength: clear conceptual advance providing a principled sparse EM with unified estimation and inference guarantees.  
- Strength: solid theory showing geometric optimization error decay and near-optimal statistical rates.  
- Strength: generality across exact and gradient EM variants with unified conditions and proofs.  
- Strength: relevant to an important gap between EM algorithms and high-dimensional statistical guarantees.  
- Weakness: strong assumptions and local analysis requiring good initialization and separation/SNR conditions.  
- Weakness: main estimation theorem relies on a resampling version of the algorithm rather than single-sample EM.  
- Weakness: inference step may be computationally heavy (Dantzig selector, Tn) with no empirical stability/finite-sample evidence.  
- Weakness: verification limited to two models; broader claims would benefit from more examples or a recipe for checking conditions.  
- Weakness: dense notation and many technical constants; better exposition and connections to iterative hard-thresholding literature are needed.  
- Quality: technically strong analysis with appropriate entrywise concentration and semiparametric-efficient inference, but practical gap due to resampling and initialization reliance.  
- Suggestions: extend analysis to single-sample EM or provide empirical evidence, give concrete initializers, discuss inference computational cost and alternatives (e.g., Lasso), relate to IHT/HTP, and study robustness.  
- Overall: solid, novel theoretical contribution with high potential impact; recommendation leans toward acceptance if resampling, initialization, and computational/practical issues are clarified.
