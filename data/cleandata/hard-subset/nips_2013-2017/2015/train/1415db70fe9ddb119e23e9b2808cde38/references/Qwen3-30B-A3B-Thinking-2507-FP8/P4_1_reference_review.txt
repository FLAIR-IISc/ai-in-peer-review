

# Review of "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper proposes a high-dimensional extension of the EM algorithm for latent variable models, addressing the gap in theoretical guarantees for high-dimensional settings where the dimension d exceeds the sample size n. The authors introduce a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and establish both computational and statistical guarantees. They further develop a decorrelated score statistic for asymptotic inference on low-dimensional components of the high-dimensional parameter.

The paper builds upon prior work on the EM algorithm, particularly [2] which established convergence rates for low-dimensional settings (d << n), and extends it to high-dimensional regimes. The authors correctly note that existing results for the EM algorithm (e.g., [18, 30]) do not address high-dimensional scenarios where sparsity regularization is necessary. The paper also differentiates itself from tensor-based approaches [1, 9] which suffer from suboptimal statistical rates in high-dimensional settings.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical guarantees with well-defined conditions. The main results (Theorems 3.4 and 4.6) establish geometric convergence rates for the high-dimensional EM algorithm and asymptotic normality for the decorrelated score statistic. The proof techniques appear sound, with detailed arguments provided in the appendix.

2. **Novelty:** The paper makes a clear contribution by extending the EM algorithm to high-dimensional settings with sparsity, which is a significant gap in the literature. The truncation step to enforce sparsity along the solution path is a novel technical contribution. The decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing in high-dimensional latent variable models is also new.

3. **Statistical and Computational Guarantees:** The paper establishes a decomposition of the estimation error into optimization and statistical components, with the latter achieving a near-minimax optimal rate of √(s* log d/n). This is a significant improvement over low-dimensional results which achieve √(d/n) rates.

4. **Theoretical Completeness:** The paper provides both point estimation guarantees and asymptotic inference results, which is rare in high-dimensional statistics. The decorrelated score statistic achieves the semiparametric information bound, demonstrating optimality.

5. **Clarity:** The paper is well-organized, with clear definitions, theorems, and proofs. The algorithm is presented in pseudocode, and the notation is consistently used. The appendix provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Practical Implementation Details:** The paper lacks guidance on choosing the sparsity parameter bs in practice. While the theoretical analysis requires bs to be sufficiently large (equation 3.8), the authors don't provide practical methods for selecting this tuning parameter.

2. **Assumption Complexity:** The technical conditions (Conditions 3.1-3.3, 4.1-4.5) are quite complex, and it's not immediately clear how restrictive they are in practice. The paper verifies these conditions for specific models (Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression), but it's unclear how broadly applicable the framework is to other latent variable models.

3. **Comparison with Alternative Methods:** While the paper compares with tensor methods, it doesn't provide a thorough comparison with other high-dimensional approaches for latent variable models (e.g., the gradient EM with regularization in [23]). A more detailed comparison would strengthen the argument for the proposed approach.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

- First theoretical framework for high-dimensional EM with statistical guarantees, addressing a significant gap in the literature.
- Establishes near-minimax optimal estimation rates in high-dimensional settings.
- Develops a novel decorrelated score statistic for asymptotic inference, which is the first such result for high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Provides rigorous proofs for both computational and statistical guarantees.
- Theoretical results are verified for two important latent variable models (Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression).

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

- The paper is highly technical, which may limit accessibility to the broader NIPS audience.
- The assumptions required for the theoretical results are complex and may be restrictive in practice.
- The practical implementation details for choosing the sparsity parameter are missing.
- The comparison with related work could be more thorough, particularly regarding alternative high-dimensional approaches.

The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of the EM algorithm in high-dimensional settings. The technical results are sound, and the paper addresses an important gap in the literature. The theoretical guarantees provided are substantial improvements over existing results for high-dimensional latent variable models. The paper is well-written, with clear organization and sufficient detail for reproducibility.

The paper is clearly within the scope of NIPS as it addresses fundamental theoretical questions in machine learning and statistics. The significance of the work is demonstrated by the fact that it establishes the first computationally feasible approach for optimal parameter estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.

Overall, this paper makes a strong contribution to the field with high technical quality, novel theoretical results, and significant implications for statistical inference in high-dimensional settings. The weaknesses identified are minor compared to the substantial theoretical advances presented.
