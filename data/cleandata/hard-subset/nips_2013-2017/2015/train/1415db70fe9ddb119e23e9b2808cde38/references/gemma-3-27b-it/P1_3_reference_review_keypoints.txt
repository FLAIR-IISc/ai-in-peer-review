- Summary: Tackles EM in high dimensions by proposing a truncated (sparsity-enforcing) EM, proving statistical convergence O(p s* log(d/n)) and asymptotic normality of a decorrelated score, and demonstrating the framework on GMMs and mixture regression.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on low-dimensional EM theory but handles d > n via truncation, distinguishing itself from tensor and debiasing approaches by offering a unified EM-specific inferential framework.

- Strength: Addresses a fundamental open problem in theoretical EM for high dimensions.

- Strength: Introduces a novel truncation step to enforce sparsity and enable analysis.

- Strength: Develops a decorrelated score statistic for valid inference with high-dimensional nuisances.

- Strength: Provides comprehensive, rigorous theoretical analysis including rates and asymptotic normality.

- Strength: Clear algorithmic presentation and applicability demonstrated on GMM and mixture regression.

- Weakness: Paper is technically dense and would benefit from more intuitive explanations and justification of many conditions.

- Weakness: Performance depends sensitively on tuning parameters (sparsity level and step size) with limited practical guidance.

- Weakness: Computational cost, especially of the truncation step, is not thoroughly analyzed or compared to alternatives.

- Weakness: Limited empirical evaluation—lacks experiments on simulated or real data to validate theory.

- Weakness: Strong technical assumptions (esp. in Section 4) may restrict practical applicability; robustness to violations is not discussed.

- For acceptance: Addresses a significant open problem with novel, substantial technical contributions.

- For acceptance: Provides rigorous statistical and computational guarantees and a generally applicable framework.

- Against acceptance: Highly technical and potentially inaccessible to a broad audience.

- Against acceptance: Lack of empirical validation limits assessment of practical performance.

- Against acceptance: Strong assumptions raise concerns about real-world applicability.

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel theoretical contribution that merits acceptance but needs clarity and empirical support.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Revision 1: Improve clarity with more intuitive explanations and motivation for key conditions.

- Revision 2: Add empirical validation (simulated or real experiments) to support theoretical claims.

- Revision 3: Provide a discussion of computational complexity and comparisons to other methods.

- Revision 4: Include sensitivity analysis and practical guidance for parameter tuning.

- Revision 5: Discuss robustness to assumption violations and how to verify assumptions in practice.
