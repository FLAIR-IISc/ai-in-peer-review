- Tackles the open problem of providing theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models via a truncated EM enforcing sparsity, with convergence and asymptotic normality results applicable to GMM and mixture regression.  
- Introduces a novel truncation step to enforce sparsity and enable theoretical analysis.  
- Establishes strong theoretical guarantees: near-optimal convergence rate (p√(s* log(d/n))) and asymptotic normality of a decorrelated score statistic, separating optimization and statistical error.  
- Provides a unified framework applicable to multiple latent-variable models.  
- Clearly positions the work relative to prior literature and connects to perturbed gradient methods.  
- Delivers a comprehensive and detailed theoretical analysis with proofs in the appendix.  
- Results depend on many technical conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5), which are complex and need more intuitive explanation.  
- Practical selection of tuning parameters (batch size, step size, Dantzig selector parameter) is not sufficiently addressed.  
- Truncation step increases computational cost; the paper lacks discussion of computational complexity and optimization strategies.  
- Strong and potentially limiting sparsity assumptions for both true parameter and Dantzig solution.  
- Paper is technically sound and complete, but theoretical sections are dense and could use more intuition alongside notation.  
- The combination of truncated EM and high-dimensional guarantees is original and significant, especially the decorrelated score inference contribution.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental open problem, novel algorithm, strong theoretical results, and careful analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong assumptions, unclear tuning in practice, and insufficient discussion of scalability/computational cost.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept, with minor revisions to provide practical guidance on parameter selection and discuss computational implications of truncation.
