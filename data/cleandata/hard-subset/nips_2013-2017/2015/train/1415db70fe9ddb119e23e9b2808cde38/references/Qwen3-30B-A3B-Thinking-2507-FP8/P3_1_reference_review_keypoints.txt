- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity in d >> n settings.  
- Establishes geometric convergence to a local optimum with near-minimax statistical rate s* log d / n.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing that attains the semiparametric information bound.  
- Validated theoretically on Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models, giving first high-dimensional EM guarantees for latent variable models.  
- Significant theoretical contribution filling a gap in understanding EM in high-dimensional regimes.  
- Truncation (T-step) is a novel, well-motivated mechanism enabling sharper s* log d / n rates versus d/n.  
- Presents a unified framework covering both exact maximization and gradient-ascent M-step implementations.  
- Clearly separates and analyzes computational versus statistical error terms critical for high-dimensional analysis.  
- Decorrelation-based asymptotic inference is a strong, technically impressive contribution (Theorem 4.6).  
- Thorough comparison to related work, distinguishing from tensor methods and low-dimensional EM results.  
- Lacks empirical validation on real or synthetic data to demonstrate practical performance and verify rates.  
- Relies on strong, possibly hard-to-verify technical conditions (e.g., Condition 3.3 requiring model-dependent statistical error ε).  
- Does not thoroughly address computational cost of truncation (top-b_s selection may be O(d log d) per iteration).  
- Assumes true parameter sparsity without discussing robustness to non-sparse truths or practical selection of b_s.  
- Limited practical implementation guidance (stepsize choice, handling ineffective truncation, etc.).  
- Theoretical development is rigorous with refined entrywise error analysis and justified use of ℓ1-norm bounds.  
- Demonstrates that truncation enables finer-grained analysis not possible with loose ℓ2-norm bounds.  
- Situates work well in literature, extending [2] and improving over tensor methods, but could engage more with recent high-dimensional gradient-EM work [23].  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions, scoring high on quality, originality, and significance.  
- Suggests adding empirical experiments, guidance for choosing b_s and stepsize, verification details for assumptions, discussion of non-sparse cases, and explicit statement of limitations.
