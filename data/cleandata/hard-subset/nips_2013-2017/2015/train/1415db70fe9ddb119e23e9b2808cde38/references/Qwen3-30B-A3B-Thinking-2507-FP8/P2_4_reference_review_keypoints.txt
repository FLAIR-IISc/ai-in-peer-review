- Addresses gap for EM in high-dimensional regimes (d ≫ n) by proposing a truncated EM algorithm that enforces sparsity and provides both estimation and inference results.  
- Theorem 3.4: algorithm converges geometrically to an estimator with near-minimax rate O(√(s* log d / n)).  
- Theorem 4.6: decorrelated score statistic is asymptotically normal and achieves the semiparametric information bound.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with clear assumptions and detailed proofs; conditions verified for Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized; notation and figures (optimization vs. statistical error) aid understanding.  
- Key novelty: truncation (T-step) that enforces sparsity within EM iterations for high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Novel inference contribution: decorrelated score tailored to latent variable models, extending beyond prior GLM-focused methods.  
- Framework is general and broadly applicable to many latent variable models.  
- High theoretical and practical significance for both theory and practitioners working with high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Main weakness: lack of empirical validation — experiments are missing for conference submission.  
- Additional concerns: required technical conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) may be restrictive and sensitivity to violations is not studied.  
- Practical guidance is limited for choosing the sparsity parameter bs despite theoretical guidance (Eq. 3.8).  
- Overall assessment: outstanding theoretical contribution; recommendation: Accept.
