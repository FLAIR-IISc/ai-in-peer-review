This paper tackles the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models, a regime where existing theory breaks down. The authors make a significant contribution by proposing a truncated EM algorithm and establishing statistical and computational guarantees, including an optimal convergence rate and asymptotic normality of a decorrelated score statistic. The work is well-motivated, addresses a crucial gap in the literature, and demonstrates a deep understanding of the interplay between optimization and statistics.

**Strengths:**

* **Important Problem:** The paper addresses a fundamental limitation of existing EM theory, extending it to the high-dimensional setting where it is arguably most needed.
* **Novel Approach:** The truncation step is a clever idea that enforces sparsity and allows for theoretical analysis in high dimensions.
* **Strong Theoretical Results:** The convergence rate of p s⇤ · log d/n is near-optimal, and the asymptotic normality result is particularly valuable for inference.
* **Unified Framework:** The authors provide a general framework applicable to various latent variable models, as demonstrated with Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.
* **Clear Presentation:**  The paper is generally well-written and the algorithms are clearly presented. Mathematical notation is standard and accessible.
* **Connections to Related Work:** The authors diligently relate their work to existing literature, highlighting the improvements and differences.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Technical Complexity:** The theoretical analysis is dense and requires substantial background knowledge to fully grasp. Some proofs could benefit from more detailed explanations of key steps.
* **Constant Factors:** While the asymptotic rates are optimal, the constants involved (e.g., in the truncation parameter) and the conditions required for them to hold could be difficult to verify in practice.  A discussion on the tightness of these constants and their practical implications would be helpful.
* **Initialization Sensitivity:** The initialization requirement (init ⇤ /k ⇤k2 <  ) is fairly strong. Exploring the robustness of the algorithm to different initialization schemes would be valuable.  Is there guidance on how to choose a suitable initialization?
* **Computational Cost:** The truncation step adds computational overhead. A discussion of the practical implications of this overhead, and potential strategies for mitigating it (e.g., efficient implementation of the truncation) would strengthen the paper.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** While the theoretical results are strong, the paper lacks empirical demonstrations to support the practical effectiveness of the proposed algorithm and its associated statistical tests.  Including simulations or real-world experiments would significantly enhance the paper's impact.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a significant theoretical advance in a challenging area.
* The proposed algorithm and its theoretical guarantees are novel and potentially impactful.
* The work is well-motivated, technically sound, and clearly presented.
* The results have the potential to advance the state-of-the-art in high-dimensional latent variable modeling.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The technical complexity may limit the accessibility of the paper to a broad audience.
* The strong initialization requirement and the lack of empirical validation raise concerns about the practical applicability of the algorithm.
* The constant factors in the theoretical results and the computational cost of the truncation step require further investigation.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant theoretical contributions. The proposed truncated EM algorithm and its accompanying theoretical guarantees address a crucial gap in the literature. While the technical complexity and lack of empirical validation are weaknesses, the novelty and importance of the work outweigh these concerns.

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.
