- Summary: Proposes a truncated high-dimensional EM algorithm with near-optimal convergence rate and asymptotic normality, applicable to models like GMMs and mixture of regressions.  
- Contribution: Fills a theoretical gap by analyzing EM behavior in high-dimensional latent variable models and providing inference tools.  
- Theoretical strength: Offers rigorous statistical guarantees for estimation and inference, including p√(s* log(d)/n) rate and asymptotic normality.  
- Framework: Presents a unified analytical framework that generalizes to multiple latent-variable models.  
- Writing: Well-written and organized; math appears sound though detailed checking is nontrivial.  
- Related work: Thoroughly situates the paper within EM theory, high-dimensional statistics, and sparse recovery literature.  
- Technical complexity: Analysis is intricate and may limit accessibility to a broader audience.  
- Assumptions: Some assumptions (e.g., on the Fisher information and sparsity of w*) are strong and may not hold in practice; implications need more discussion.  
- Computational cost: Truncation step increases computational overhead; practical impact and optimizations are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Empirical validation: No simulations or experiments provided to support theoretical claims or asymptotic normality in finite samples.  
- Quality (NIPS criteria): Strong — technically sound and comprehensive, with limitations acknowledged.  
- Clarity (NIPS criteria): Good for a theoretical paper but mathematically challenging.  
- Originality (NIPS criteria): Very strong — novel analysis of EM in high dimensions.  
- Significance (NIPS criteria): Strong — results potentially impactful for high-dimensional latent-variable applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a fundamental open problem, provides strong guarantees, offers a general framework, and is original and significant.  
- Arguments against acceptance: High technical complexity, strong/possibly restrictive assumptions, and lack of empirical validation.  
- Overall assessment: Significant, original, and technically sound contribution; recommends adding simulations and discussion of practical assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
