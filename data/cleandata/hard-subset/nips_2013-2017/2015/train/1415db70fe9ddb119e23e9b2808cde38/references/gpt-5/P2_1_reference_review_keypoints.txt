- Proposes a truncated EM algorithm with a hard-thresholding T-step for high-dimensional (d >> n) latent-variable models to enforce sparsity along iterates.
- Provides unified nonasymptotic analysis for exact and one-step gradient M-steps, decomposing optimization (geometric) and statistical errors and achieving near-minimax rate O(√(s* log d / n)) with appropriate initialization/tuning.
- Develops a decorrelated score test using a Dantzig-selector orthogonalization vector w, proving asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency for low-dimensional components under high-dimensional nuisance.
- Extends Balakrishnan et al.’s population-to-sample EM framework to high dimensions via truncation and entrywise error control, and connects to debiasing/desparsification literature while moving beyond convex GLMs.
- Differs from l1-penalized EM work by using hard-thresholding and, importantly, providing valid, semiparametrically efficient inference for nonconvex latent-variable likelihoods.
- Technical arguments are precise and build on clear population conditions (local strong concavity, Lipschitz gradients) and l1 statistical error control for M-steps.
- Key assumptions are strong: good initialization within constant error, correct tuning/knowledge of sparsity bs ≈ s*, sparsity of w*, SNR/separation, and use of resampling/sample-splitting.
- Core theoretical claims are supported but many proofs and model-specific verifications are deferred to appendices/longer version.
- Presentation is generally organized but dense, with heavy notation, LaTeX artifacts, and missing intuition/guidance in the main text.
- Novel contributions include unified hard-thresholded EM analysis, efficient inference in nonconvex high-dimensional settings, and coverage of both exact and gradient M-steps.
- The theoretical contribution is substantial and could influence theory and practice if accompanied by empirical evidence and practical guidance.
- Strength: first unified computational-statistical theory for high-dimensional EM with sparsity-enforcing truncation and near-minimax rates.
- Strength: provides geometric convergence guarantees and asymptotically normal, semiparametrically efficient tests for low-dimensional parameters.
- Strength: applicable to Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions under separation conditions.
- Weakness: no empirical validation, no comparisons to l1-EM or tensor methods, and unclear robustness to bs mis-specification or initialization.
- Weakness: analysis relies on resampling; guarantees for the standard single-sample EM are not explicit.
- Weakness: strong/opaque assumptions (initialization, SNR, sparsity of w*, basin parameters) with limited practical verification guidance.
- Weakness: tuning choices (bs, stepsize, Dantzig parameter) lack data-driven procedures and sensitivity analysis.
- Suggestions: add simulation studies comparing to competing methods, demonstrate tuning/init sensitivity, and report wall-clock times.
- Suggestions: discuss/prioritize practical initialization strategies (spectral/tensor, method-of-moments, l1 warm starts) and data-driven rules for bs and Dantzig tuning.
- Suggestions: clarify extension of results to single-sample EM and improve exposition by fixing notation/artifacts and moving key lemmas into the main text.
- Recommendation: borderline/weak reject as submitted; would lean to acceptance if experiments and clearer tuning/initialization guidance are added.
- Reviewer confidence: medium.
