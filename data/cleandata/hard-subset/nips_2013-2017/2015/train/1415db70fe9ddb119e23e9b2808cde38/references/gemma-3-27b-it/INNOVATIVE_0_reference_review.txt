This paper tackles a challenging and important problem: establishing theoretical guarantees for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional settings, where the number of parameters far exceeds the sample size. The authors correctly identify a gap between the computational reality of using EM (which often gets stuck in local optima) and the statistical guarantees that require global optimality. Their proposed high-dimensional EM algorithm, incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity, is a novel and potentially impactful approach. The derived statistical rates – achieving p s⇤ · log d/n – are compelling, and the accompanying asymptotic normality result for a decorrelated score statistic represents a significant contribution to inference in latent variable models.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The work is genuinely novel. Extending theoretical guarantees for EM to the high-dimensional regime is a significant undertaking, and the proposed truncation step offers a clever way to address the challenges posed by high dimensionality.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper presents a substantial amount of theoretical analysis, establishing convergence rates and asymptotic normality. The conditions are clearly stated, although complex.
*   **Significance:** Successful theoretical guarantees for high-dimensional EM have broad implications for various applications relying on latent variable models, including mixture models and beyond. The development of a valid score test is particularly valuable.
*   **Unified Framework:** The framework is designed to be general, accommodating different M-step implementations (exact vs. gradient ascent).
*   **Clear Connection to Existing Work:** The authors clearly position their work relative to prior research, acknowledging limitations of previous approaches and building upon existing results (e.g., [2]).

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Technical Complexity:** The paper is dense and technically challenging. While the rigor is commendable, it also makes the paper difficult to fully grasp without significant effort. The numerous conditions (3.1-3.3, 4.1-4.5) and their implications aren't always immediately clear.
*   **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper focuses almost entirely on theoretical results. While applying the results to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models is a start, more comprehensive empirical validation – perhaps comparing the performance of the high-dimensional EM algorithm to existing alternatives on synthetic and real-world datasets – would strengthen the claims. Showing how sensitive the performance is to the choice of truncation parameter `bs` would be useful.
*   **Conditionality:** The theoretical results rely on a substantial set of conditions. Demonstrating that these conditions hold in realistic scenarios, beyond the simplified examples presented, requires further investigation. The assumed sparsity structure and the need for careful initialization are potentially limiting factors.
*   **Practicality of Implementation:** The T-step introduces an additional parameter (`bs`) that needs to be tuned. A discussion of how to choose this parameter in practice, or its sensitivity to the results, would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a fundamental open problem in statistical machine learning.
*   The proposed approach is novel and theoretically well-motivated.
*   The achieved statistical rates are optimal or near-optimal.
*   The work has the potential to significantly impact the field of latent variable modeling.
*   The detailed theoretical analysis is impressive, even if it demands significant reader effort.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of substantial empirical validation leaves the practical relevance of the results somewhat unclear.
*   The technical complexity might limit the accessibility and impact of the paper.
*   The strong reliance on numerous conditions raises concerns about the generality of the results.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant potential. While the technical complexity and limited empirical validation are drawbacks, the novelty, theoretical rigor, and potential significance of the work outweigh these concerns. The paper represents a bold attempt to bridge a critical gap in our understanding of the EM algorithm, and the results are compelling. I believe the paper warrants acceptance despite its flaws. The program committee should encourage the authors to consider providing a more accessible implementation and more extensive empirical results in future work.

**Rating:** Accept.
