- Paper addresses theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models, proposing a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity.
- Establishes statistical convergence rates for parameter estimation (near-minimax) and asymptotic normality of a decorrelated score statistic for low-dimensional inference.
- Presents a unified theoretical framework applicable to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.
- Introduces a truncation-based algorithmic innovation that enables theoretical analysis in high dimensions.
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees and achieves a near-optimal rate (reported as p√s* log(d/n)).
- Develops an inferential procedure (decorrelated score) to quantify uncertainty in high-dimensional EM estimates.
- Clearly situates the work relative to prior literature and highlights advantages over existing results.
- Significant contribution that fills a critical gap in understanding EM behavior in high dimensions.
- Technical presentation is dense and highly mathematical, potentially limiting accessibility.
- Some theoretical assumptions (e.g., Fisher information conditions and sparsity of w*) may be restrictive and their practical sensitivity is not fully explored.
- Lacks practical guidance on parameter tuning (truncation level bs, stepsize η) and sensitivity to these choices.
- Empirical evaluation is limited or absent; no experiments on synthetic or real data to demonstrate practical performance.
- Heavy and dense notation makes the paper difficult to follow without substantial effort.
- Overall quality of technical analysis is strong and proofs appear well-structured.
- Clarity is moderate; more intuitive explanations would help readers.
- Originality is high due to combining truncated EM with comprehensive estimation and inference theory.
- Significance is high given potential impact across ML, statistics, and bioinformatics.
- Arguments for acceptance: fills theoretical gap, strong guarantees, practical algorithmic idea, novel inferential framework, unified applicability.
- Arguments for rejection: high technical complexity, lack of empirical validation, potentially restrictive assumptions.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept, with request to add discussion of practical parameter tuning and illustrative examples/sensitivity analysis.
