

# Peer Review: "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper addresses a critical gap in the literature by developing the first comprehensive inferential theory for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors propose a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity, establishing both statistical estimation guarantees (geometric convergence to a near-minimax optimal rate) and inferential guarantees (asymptotic normality of a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing).

**Strengths:**

1. **Significant Theoretical Contribution:** The paper fills a major gap in the literature. While existing work on EM algorithms (e.g., [2, 25, 30]) focuses on low-dimensional settings, this work provides the first rigorous statistical guarantees for high-dimensional latent variable models. The truncation step (T-step) is a clever and novel approach to handle high dimensionality, addressing a fundamental challenge in this domain.

2. **Comprehensive Theoretical Framework:** The paper establishes a unified analytic framework that simultaneously provides statistical and computational guarantees. Theorem 3.4 proves geometric convergence with a near-minimax optimal rate of O(√(s* log d/n)), while Theorem 4.6 establishes asymptotic normality of the decorrelated score statistic with optimal variance (attaining the semiparametric efficiency bound).

3. **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper correctly identifies and addresses key challenges in high-dimensional inference for latent variable models. The comparison with existing work (particularly [2] for low-dimensional settings and tensor methods for high-dimensional latent variable models) is thorough and well-motivated.

4. **Technical Rigor:** The theoretical analysis is rigorous, with careful attention to the challenges posed by high dimensionality, including the need for entrywise statistical error analysis (vs. the ℓ₂-norm used in [2]).

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Empirical Validation:** The paper is purely theoretical with no empirical results. While this is acceptable for a theoretical contribution, the absence of any numerical experiments to validate the practical effectiveness of the proposed method weakens the impact of the theoretical results.

2. **Practical Implementation Details Missing:** The paper does not address how to choose the sparsity parameter bs in practice, nor does it discuss the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm. How does the truncation step affect computational efficiency compared to standard EM? The paper would benefit from a brief discussion of practical implementation considerations.

3. **Assumption Discussion Limited:** While the paper verifies technical conditions for specific models (Gaussian mixture, mixture of regression), it lacks a thorough discussion of the practical relevance and restrictiveness of these assumptions in real-world applications. The paper should elaborate on how realistic these conditions are for typical high-dimensional latent variable problems.

4. **Comparison with Alternative High-Dimensional Approaches:** The paper contrasts with existing low-dimensional work and tensor methods but could better contrast with other high-dimensional approaches for latent variable models, such as the sparse EM approach in [23] for mixture of regression.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality theoretical contribution that makes a significant advance in the understanding of EM algorithms in high-dimensional settings. The paper is well-motivated, technically sound, and addresses a well-defined gap in the literature. The theoretical results are impressive and fill an important void in the literature on latent variable models.

While the lack of empirical validation is a notable limitation, the strength of the theoretical contribution is substantial enough to warrant acceptance. The paper makes a clear and important contribution to the field that will likely influence future work on high-dimensional inference for latent variable models.

**Recommendation: Accept**

**Specific Suggestions for Improvement:**

1. Add a brief discussion of how to select the sparsity parameter bs in practice, perhaps with references to data-driven methods.

2. Include a small simulation study demonstrating the practical performance of the proposed algorithm, even if just for the Gaussian mixture model.

3. Expand the discussion of the practical relevance of the technical assumptions (Conditions 3.1-3.3, Assumption 4.5), perhaps with a table summarizing how restrictive they are for specific models.

4. Strengthen the comparison with other high-dimensional approaches for latent variable models, particularly [23] which also considers high-dimensional mixture of regression.

The paper is well-written, well-organized, and makes a significant theoretical contribution to the field. It represents a major step forward in our understanding of EM algorithms in high-dimensional settings and will likely be a valuable reference for future work in this area.
