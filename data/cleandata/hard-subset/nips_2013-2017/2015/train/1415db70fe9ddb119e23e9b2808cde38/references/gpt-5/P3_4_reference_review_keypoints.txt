- Paper proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation (hard-thresholding) step to enforce sparsity and analyzes exact and gradient M-step variants.  
- Establishes nonasymptotic error decomposition: geometric optimization contraction plus √(s* log d / n) statistical term, yielding near-minimax rates at stationarity (Thm 3.4).  
- Develops a decorrelated score test based on the Q-function that attains asymptotic normality and semiparametric-efficient variance for low-dimensional components (Thm 4.6).  
- Instantiates the framework for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions under standard separation/SNR assumptions.  
- Conceptual novelty: simple, effective EM modification mirroring hard-thresholding that yields sparse iterates and sharp entrywise control.  
- Strong theoretical contributions: clear optimization vs. statistical error decomposition, geometric contraction, and control of support evolution under truncation.  
- Inference contribution: unified conditions and verification for two canonical latent models, linking Q-function gradients to the score/Fisher information.  
- Framework scope: model-agnostic form with potential extension to other latent structures.  
- Main theoretical weakness: convergence proofs rely on resampling/sample-splitting (fresh data per iteration), which is statistically inefficient and diverges from standard EM practice.  
- Initialization issue: results require an initializer within constant relative L2 accuracy, but concrete, feasible initializers with guarantees in high-d regimes are not provided.  
- Tuning b_s (sparsity level): theory needs b_s ≳ s* but s* is unknown; no practical adaptive selection or path/stability procedure is proposed.  
- Strong structural assumptions for inference: sparsity of w* (Fisher-information-related) and multiple ℓ∞ concentration/Lipschitz conditions, which may not hold for mixtures—lack of discussion/examples.  
- Limited model scope: GMM analysis assumes known isotropic covariance and equal mixing; extensions to unknown covariance, unequal mixing, or K>2 components are not addressed.  
- Computational practicality: Dantzig program for w is a high-dimensional LP—concerns about cost and numerical stability and lack of scalable approximations (e.g., nodewise regressions) discussion.  
- No empirical results: missing simulations to demonstrate estimation rates, resampling vs. no-resampling behavior, sensitivity to b_s, and inference coverage/power; no comparisons to penalized EM or tensor initializers.  
- Presentation issues: malformed/incomplete references, missing discussion of related high-dimensional nonconvex/sparse optimization works, and condition-heavy organization without early concrete verifications.  
- Overall quality: technically sound and well-motivated, with an appropriate population-to-sample translation, but limited by sample-splitting reliance, strong inference assumptions, and lack of experiments.  
- Clarity: organized and readable for experts, but would benefit from an assumptions-to-models mapping, clearer initialization discussion, and fixed references.  
- Originality: novel combination of EM with hard thresholding and semiparametric-efficient inference in high dimensions; decorrelation adapted to EM/Q-function is principled.  
- Significance: potentially important if non-resampling theory or empirical justification and practical guidance (initialization, tuning, computation) are provided.  
- Pros summary: addresses an important gap, solid theory with clear optimization/statistics interplay, and efficiency guarantees applicable to canonical models.  
- Cons summary: relies on iterative sample-splitting, strong structural/model assumptions, limited scope, and no empirical validation or practical guidance.  
- Key questions for authors: (1) Can you justify or prove the same rates without resampling? (2) What practical initializers meet the basin proximity requirements? (3) How sensitive is performance to b_s and can it be made adaptive? (4) Can inference be implemented via nodewise regressions instead of LPs, with theory intact? (5) How do results extend to unknown covariance/mixing or K>2 components?  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept if authors justify the non-resampling setting and add convincing experiments; otherwise lean reject due to missing empirical validation and practical guidance.
