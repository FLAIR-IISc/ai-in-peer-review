

# Review of "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional extension of the EM algorithm that incorporates a truncation step to enforce sparsity, addressing a significant gap in the literature for high-dimensional latent variable models where dimension d exceeds sample size n. The authors develop a unified framework for both parameter estimation and statistical inference, establishing near-minimax optimal rates for estimation and asymptotic normality for a decorrelated score statistic that achieves the semiparametric information bound.

The paper makes several significant contributions that directly address a major limitation in the existing literature. While previous work (e.g., [2]) established results for the low-dimensional regime (d << n), this paper extends these results to high-dimensional settings where d >> n by introducing a truncation step in the E-step and M-step. This is a bold and novel approach that enables the authors to establish both statistical and computational guarantees for the high-dimensional EM algorithm. The decorrelated score statistic they propose for hypothesis testing in high-dimensional latent variable models represents another significant contribution, as previous approaches for inference in high-dimensional settings primarily focused on generalized linear models rather than latent variable models.

The paper's theoretical results are rigorous and well-structured. The main theorem (Theorem 3.4) establishes the convergence rate of the high-dimensional EM algorithm as a geometric decay in optimization error plus a statistical error term proportional to sqrt(s*log(d)/n), which is near-minimax optimal. Theorem 4.6 establishes the asymptotic normality of the decorrelated score statistic, achieving the semiparametric information bound. The authors provide careful verification of their assumptions for specific models (Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression), demonstrating the framework's applicability.

This work stands out from previous NIPS papers on EM algorithms (e.g., [2], [18], [30]) by addressing the high-dimensional regime, which has been largely unexplored. While [2] established convergence rates in low dimensions, this paper's truncation-based approach for high dimensions is a fundamentally new direction that opens up a range of new theoretical and practical possibilities for latent variable models.

The paper's main strengths are its bold theoretical contribution, the clear novelty of the truncation-based high-dimensional EM algorithm, and the comprehensive framework that unifies estimation and inference. The authors provide a thorough comparison with related work, clearly articulating how their approach differs from and improves upon previous methods.

Potential weaknesses include the absence of empirical validation (though the paper is primarily theoretical), and the technical nature of the proofs which may make the paper challenging for some readers. However, as a reviewer who prioritizes novel ideas over methodological perfection, I find these weaknesses less concerning given the paper's significant theoretical contribution.

The paper's significance is substantial. It establishes the first theoretical guarantees for both parameter estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional latent variable models, which is a major gap in the literature. The near-minimax optimal rate of convergence (sqrt(s*log(d)/n)) represents a substantial improvement over the p(d/n) rate established in low-dimensional settings. The decorrelated score statistic achieving the semiparametric information bound is a particularly strong theoretical contribution.

This paper would be a valuable addition to NIPS as it introduces a novel approach to a fundamental problem in statistics and machine learning. The authors have successfully bridged the gap between computation and statistics in high-dimensional latent variable models, which is a significant contribution to the field. The paper is well-structured, technically sound, and makes a clear, significant contribution to the literature.

The paper's novelty and theoretical significance far outweigh its minor weaknesses. The authors have provided a bold, novel approach to a significant problem, establishing both estimation and inference guarantees in a previously unexplored high-dimensional regime. This work represents a substantial advancement in our understanding of EM algorithms for high-dimensional latent variable models.

**Recommendation: Accept**

**Strengths:**
- Novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with truncation step
- First theoretical guarantees for estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models
- Near-minimax optimal rate of convergence (sqrt(s*log(d)/n))
- Decorrelated score statistic achieving semiparametric information bound
- Comprehensive framework applicable to multiple models (Gaussian mixture, mixture of regression)

**Weaknesses:**
- No empirical validation (primarily theoretical)
- Technical nature may be challenging for some readers
- Some restrictive assumptions (though verified for specific models)
