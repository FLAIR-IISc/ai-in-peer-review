- Proposes truncated EM (T-step: hard thresholding to top bs entries) to enforce sparsity, analyzed for exact and gradient M-steps.
- Proves geometric contraction of optimization error within a local basin and near-optimal statistical error ~ sqrt(s* log d / n) up to logs.
- Develops a decorrelated score/Wald inference for latent-variable likelihoods that is asymptotically normal and semiparametrically efficient with high-dimensional nuisance.
- Applies the framework to Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions.
- Extends Balakrishnan et al. (2017) from low- to high-dimensional EM and introduces truncation with nonasymptotic iterate analysis.
- Adapts desparsification/debiasing ideas to latent-variable models via Q-function derivative identities.
- Claims sharper high-dimensional rates and valid inference compared to l1-penalized EM and tensor/moment methods.
- Conceptual strength: a simple, implementable T-step enforcing sparsity along EM iterates.
- Theoretical strength: clean decomposition of optimization vs statistical error and explicit geometric convergence rates.
- Inference strength: unified decorrelated score that handles high-dimensional nuisance without requiring a global convex optimum.
- Framework strength: transparent conditions (Lipschitz-gradient, local concavity/smoothness, ℓ1 statistical error) verified for canonical models.
- Bridges computation and statistics in regimes where MLE is ill-defined and penalized likelihoods are nonconvex.
- Major limitation: requires initialization inside a local basin and sufficiently large SNR for mixtures; no concrete high-dimensional initializer with guarantees provided.
- Main estimation analysis relies on resampling/sample-splitting EM, not the single-batch algorithm practitioners use; single-batch extension omitted.
- Tuning concerns: sparsity bs and other parameters depend on unknowns; little guidance on data-driven selection or adaptivity to s*.
- Inference scalability: requires solving a Dantzig problem per target; computational/numerical stability and nodewise-Lasso alternatives are not discussed.
- Strong assumptions for inference (sparsity of w*, eigenvalue and small-o conditions) are required but only briefly justified beyond simple settings.
- No empirical evaluation: missing finite-sample error/CI coverage studies and comparisons to l1-EM, gradient EM, tensor initializers.
- Presentation issues: notation-heavy, typographical errors, incomplete references, and missing explicit regularity assumptions for Q-function identities.
- Suggestions: provide analyzable spectral/moment initializer, single-batch proof sketch, practical rules for bs, consider nodewise Lasso/CLIME alternatives, clarify σ estimation and robustness to misspecification.
- Overall assessment: strong, original theoretical contribution with high potential but contingent on adding practical initialization, single-batch justification, and experiments; borderline accept.
