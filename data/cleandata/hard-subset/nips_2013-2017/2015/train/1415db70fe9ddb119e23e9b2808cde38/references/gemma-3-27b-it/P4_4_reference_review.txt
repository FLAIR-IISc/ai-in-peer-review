This paper addresses the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors propose a truncated EM algorithm and establish statistical and computational guarantees, including a convergence rate and asymptotic normality for a decorrelated score statistic. The work is well-motivated, tackling a significant gap in existing literature where guarantees are lacking when the dimension exceeds the sample size.

**Summary of Ideas & Relation to Prior Work:**

The core idea is to modify the EM algorithm by truncating the updates in the E-step and M-step to enforce sparsity. This addresses the ill-posedness of maximum likelihood estimation in high dimensions. The authors build upon previous work characterizing convergence rates in the low-dimensional regime [2, 18, 25, 30]. They acknowledge [2] as a key starting point but highlight that their truncation step necessitates new analysis to achieve comparable results in high dimensions. The paper also draws connections to recent work on post-selection inference [13, 15, 16, 17, 19, 20, 22, 24, 26, 32] and tensor methods [1, 9], contrasting their approach with these alternatives, emphasizing the applicability of their framework to a wider range of models and the non-convexity inherent in latent variable estimation.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses an important open problem:** The paper tackles a crucial and long-standing issue: providing theoretical foundations for EM in high dimensions.
*   **Novelty of the truncation step:** The introduction of the truncation step is a key innovation, enabling sparsity control and facilitating statistical analysis.
*   **Strong theoretical results:** The authors derive a geometrically convergent rate and demonstrate asymptotic normality, providing rigorous guarantees for their algorithm and inference procedure.
*   **Unified Framework:** The authors present a unifying framework that allows for both exact and approximate maximization in the M-step.
*   **Detailed Analysis:** The paper includes a thorough theoretical analysis, with multiple technical conditions and a detailed proof outline.
*   **Clear Connections to Related Work:** The authors carefully position their work in the context of existing literature, clearly outlining the contributions and differences.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Technical Complexity:** The paper is densely technical, which may make it difficult for a broader audience to fully grasp the details. The numerous conditions (3.1-3.3, 4.1-4.5) require careful consideration.
*   **Assumptions:** The results rely on a number of assumptions, some of which (e.g. sparsity constraints on the w vector in Condition 4.5) might be strong for certain applications. The implications of these assumptions could be discussed more thoroughly.
*   **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The paper primarily focuses on theoretical guarantees. Empirical validation, even on simulated data, would strengthen the findings and demonstrate the practical performance of the algorithm.
*   **Notation:** The numerous notations can be cumbersome. While standard in the field, an appendix summarizing key symbols could be helpful.
*    **Presentation of Conditions**: While thorough, the presentation of the technical conditions and their justifications in the supplementary material could be streamlined for better readability.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a fundamental problem in statistical learning with significant theoretical importance.
*   The proposed algorithm and analysis are novel and represent a substantial contribution to the field.
*   The mathematical results are rigorous and provide strong guarantees for the algorithm's performance.
*   The paper is well-written (albeit dense) and clearly articulates the contributions relative to existing work.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The high level of technical complexity may limit the paper’s accessibility.
*   The strong assumptions may restrict the applicability of the results to certain problem settings.
*   The lack of empirical validation makes it difficult to assess the practical impact of the work.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong technical paper that makes a significant contribution to the theory of the EM algorithm in high dimensions. While the technical complexity and strong assumptions are potential drawbacks, the novelty and rigor of the results outweigh these concerns. I recommend **acceptance** with the caveat that the authors might consider providing more intuitive explanations of the key assumptions and exploring empirical validation in future work.
