- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and enable computation when d >> n.
- Provides an error decomposition for estimation into optimization (geometric) and statistical (near-minimax p(s* log d / n)) components (Theorem 3.4).
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for inference that achieves asymptotic normality with optimal semiparametric variance (Theorem 4.6).
- Presents a unified theoretical framework applied to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.

- Technical analysis is rigorous with carefully stated conditions and detailed proofs; estimation and inference results appear sound.
- The truncation step and the decorrelated score for latent variable models are original methodological contributions.
- Results are significant: first theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models and near-minimax rates plus valid hypothesis testing.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, with consistent notation and comprehensive appendices.

- Lacks any empirical validation (simulations or real data) to demonstrate practical performance.
- Does not provide practical guidance for selecting the sparsity tuning parameter bs in implementations.
- Verification of conditions is limited to two models, so the claimed generality is not fully demonstrated.
- Relies on several technical conditions that may be hard to verify in practice and need more intuitive discussion.

- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance despite missing empirical results due to the paper's substantial theoretical contributions.

- Pro: first theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Pro: novel truncation step enabling sparsity with convergence guarantees.
- Pro: near-minimax optimal statistical convergence rate p(s* log d / n).
- Pro: first decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Pro: rigorous analysis showing asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency.
- Pro: well-written, organized, and technically sound.

- Con: missing empirical validation (simulations/real data).
- Con: limited scope of model verification (only two models).
- Con: no practical tuning strategy for bs.
- Con: technical conditions may be difficult to verify in practice.

- Overall: a substantial, novel theoretical contribution likely to be foundational for high-dimensional latent variable modeling; acceptance justified despite empirical omissions.
