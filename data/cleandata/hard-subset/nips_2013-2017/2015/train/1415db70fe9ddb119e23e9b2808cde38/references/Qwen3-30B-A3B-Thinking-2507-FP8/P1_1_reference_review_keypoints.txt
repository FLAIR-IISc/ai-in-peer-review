- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and achieve near-minimax estimation rates.
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for testing low-dimensional components with asymptotic normality and semiparametric-optimal variance.
- Extends low-dimensional EM convergence theory to the high-dimensional regime, providing unified estimation and inference guarantees.
- Establishes near-minimax estimator rate O(√(s*log d / n)) and geometric convergence of the algorithm.
- Verifies theoretical conditions for Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with detailed proofs and clearly distinguishes from prior low-dimensional work.
- Novelty: first high-dimensional EM with sparsity-inducing truncation and first decorrelated score for latent variable models.
- Significance: addresses a fundamental gap in high-dimensional latent variable inference with potentially broad impact.
- Conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) are complex and may be hard to verify for new models.
- The paper claims “near-minimax-optimal” without explicitly stating the minimax lower bound for comparison.
- Notation is dense, especially for the decorrelated score, and could use higher-level intuition.
- The role and impact of the truncation step on convergence are analyzed but lack an intuitive explanation.
- Lacks empirical/simulation results to demonstrate practical performance and compare to alternatives.
- Truncation might cause suboptimal performance in some practical scenarios (not explored).
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with rigorous results but limited practical validation.
- Recommendation: accept — important theoretical advance expected to influence future work in high-dimensional statistics.
