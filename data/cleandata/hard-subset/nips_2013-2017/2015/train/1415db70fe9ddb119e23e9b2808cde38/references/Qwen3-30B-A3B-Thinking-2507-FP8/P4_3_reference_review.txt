

# Review of "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper addresses a critical gap in the theoretical understanding of the EM algorithm for high-dimensional latent variable models (d >> n), where previous work has primarily focused on low-dimensional settings (d << n). The authors propose a high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates a truncation step to enforce sparsity, establishing both computational and statistical guarantees for this algorithm. They further develop a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional components of the high-dimensional parameter. The main contributions are the first theoretical guarantees for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional regimes, including geometric convergence rates and near-minimax optimal statistical rates (s* log d/n), along with a novel inferential framework for high-dimensional latent variable models.

The paper builds upon previous work on the EM algorithm in low dimensions, particularly [2] which established geometric convergence rates for low-dimensional settings. The authors correctly identify that [2] and other works ([18, 25, 30]) only address the low-dimensional regime (d << n), where the maximum likelihood estimator is well-defined without sparsity assumptions. For high dimensions, they note that the standard EM algorithm is not well-defined without regularization, and the existing approaches for inference (e.g., [13, 15, 16, 26, 27, 32]) primarily focus on generalized linear models rather than latent variable models.

**Strengths:**
1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical guarantees with detailed proofs in the appendix. The authors carefully establish conditions (Conditions 3.1-3.3) for the high-dimensional EM algorithm and verify them for specific models (Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression) in the appendix. Theorem 3.4 establishes the geometric convergence rate and near-minimax optimal statistical rate, while Theorem 4.6 proves the asymptotic normality of the decorrelated score statistic.

2. **Novelty:** The paper is the first to establish theoretical guarantees for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional regimes. The truncation step (T-step) is a key innovation that enables the sparsity-enforcing mechanism, which is crucial for high-dimensional analysis. The decorrelated score statistic for inference in latent variable models is also novel and addresses a gap in the literature.

3. **Significance:** The results have substantial implications for high-dimensional latent variable models, which are widely used in applications like clustering, topic modeling, and causal inference. The near-minimax optimal statistical rate (s* log d/n) represents a significant improvement over previous approaches, which would have achieved slower rates (d/n) in high dimensions. The framework is general and applicable to a broad family of latent variable models.

4. **Clarity:** The paper is well-structured with clear definitions and notation. The main algorithm (Algorithm 1) is presented concisely, and the theoretical results are carefully stated with precise conditions. The appendix provides sufficient technical details for reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
1. The paper could better motivate the specific choice of truncation parameter (bs) and its relationship to the true sparsity level s*. While Condition 3.3 and the discussion in Section 3.1 explain the requirements, a more intuitive explanation would strengthen the paper.

2. The connection between the decorrelated score statistic and existing methods (like those in [26, 32] for generalized linear models) could be more explicitly discussed. While the paper notes the differences, a more detailed comparison would clarify the novelty of their approach for latent variable models.

3. The computational complexity of the proposed algorithm is not explicitly analyzed. While the paper focuses on statistical guarantees, a brief discussion of computational efficiency compared to alternatives would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- First theoretical guarantees for EM algorithm in high-dimensional settings (d >> n)
- Near-minimax optimal statistical rate (s* log d/n) for high-dimensional latent variable models
- Novel decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing in latent variable models
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with detailed proofs in the appendix
- Applicable to a broad family of latent variable models (Gaussian mixture, mixture of regression)

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
- The truncation step's parameter selection (bs) could be better justified
- Limited discussion of computational complexity
- Some technical details in the appendix may require significant effort to verify

The paper makes a significant contribution to the theoretical understanding of the EM algorithm in high-dimensional settings. The technical results are sound, the novelty is clear, and the significance is substantial for a field where high-dimensional latent variable models are increasingly important. The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and makes a strong case for its contributions. The minor weaknesses noted do not detract from the paper's overall quality and significance.

This paper addresses a critical gap in the literature, provides rigorous theoretical guarantees, and establishes a framework that will likely influence future work on high-dimensional latent variable models. It is a strong candidate for acceptance at NIPS.
