- Extends EM to high-dimensional latent variable models (d >> n) using a truncation step enforcing sparsity in E- and M-steps.  
- Achieves (near-)minimax parameter estimation rate s*·log(d)/n with combined statistical and computational convergence guarantees.  
- Proposes a decorrelated score statistic for inference on low-dimensional components that attains the semiparametric information bound.  
- Validates framework on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models.  
- Truncation step is the critical novel innovation enabling high-dimensional EM beyond prior low-dimensional analyses.  
- Uses refined entrywise ℓ1 error analysis and cleanly decomposes optimization error (geometric decay) from statistical error (optimal rate).  
- Technical results are rigorous with detailed proofs and model-specific condition verifications in the appendix.  
- Original contribution: first computationally feasible high-dimensional EM with sparsity enforcement and associated inference tools for latent variable models.  
- Significance: addresses important applications (genomics, text, images) and matches minimax lower bounds up to logarithmic factors.  
- Clarity concern: intuition for the truncation step and the relationship between truncation parameter bs and true sparsity s* could be clearer.  
- Assumptions concern: relies on many technical conditions that must be checked per model and sparsity assumptions (e.g., for w*) need better motivation.  
- Practical concern: lacks guidance on choosing bs, empirical effects of truncation on convergence, and comparisons of computational complexity to alternatives.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, well-written, and a significant theoretical contribution to high-dimensional latent variable inference.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
