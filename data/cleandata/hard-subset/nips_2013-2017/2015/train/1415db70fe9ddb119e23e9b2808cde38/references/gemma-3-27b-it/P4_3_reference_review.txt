This paper addresses the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors present a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm, incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and establish statistical and computational guarantees, including asymptotic normality for low-dimensional components. The work builds upon previous results for low-dimensional settings [2, 18, 25, 30] and attempts to bridge the gap between computation and statistical optimality in high dimensions.

**Summary of Contributions:** The core contribution is a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step, accompanied by a rigorous theoretical analysis. The authors prove a statistical rate of convergence of p(s* log d)/n, where s* is the sparsity level, and establish asymptotic normality for a decorrelated score statistic used for hypothesis testing. The framework is general, applicable to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models (illustrated in the paper), and accommodates both exact maximization and gradient ascent implementations of the M-step. 

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Theoretical guarantees for EM in high dimensions are largely absent, and this work directly tackles that gap.
* **Novelty of the Approach:** The truncation step is a key innovation to enforce sparsity and allow for theoretical analysis. This is a non-trivial addition to the standard EM framework.
* **Rigorous Theoretical Analysis:** The paper presents detailed mathematical proofs and establishes strong theoretical results, including convergence rates and asymptotic normality. The separation of optimization and statistical error is well-defined.
* **Generality:** The framework's applicability to different models and M-step implementations is a strength, increasing the potential impact.
* **Clear Connection to Existing Work:** The paper clearly positions itself within the existing literature, citing relevant work and explaining how it improves upon previous results, particularly [2].
* **Well-defined Assumptions:** The conditions required for the theorems are explicitly stated and appear reasonable, although their verification requires careful model-specific analysis (demonstrated in the appendices).

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of the Presentation:** The mathematical notation and derivations are dense and can be difficult to follow without a strong background in statistical optimization and high-dimensional statistics. While thorough, the presentation could benefit from more intuitive explanations.
* **Dependence on Initialization:** The results rely on a “good” initialization (within a relative error of ε). The practical implications of this assumption, and methods for obtaining such an initialization, are not fully explored. It is often challenging to find good initializations in high dimensions.
* **Strong Assumptions on Sparsity:** The sparsity level (s*) and structure are crucial for the theoretical guarantees. The practical relevance of these assumptions and robustness to deviations from sparsity are not thoroughly discussed.  The requirement that w⇤ be sparse (Assumption 4.5) is particularly restrictive and deserves more justification.
* **Computational Cost:** The truncation step adds computational overhead to the EM algorithm. A discussion of the practical computational cost and scalability of the proposed algorithm would be valuable.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The paper focuses almost entirely on theoretical analysis. Empirical validation on real or simulated datasets to demonstrate the performance of the algorithm and the accuracy of the asymptotic results would significantly strengthen the work.
* **Repetitive Discussion of Related Work:** The related work section is somewhat verbose and repeats similar points across different paragraphs.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper tackles a fundamental and challenging open problem in statistical machine learning.
* The proposed approach and theoretical results are novel and significant.
* The mathematical analysis is rigorous and appears sound.
* The work has the potential to advance the field of latent variable modeling and provide practical tools for high-dimensional data analysis.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The complexity of the presentation might limit its accessibility to a broader audience.
* The strong assumptions on initialization and sparsity could limit the practical applicability of the algorithm.
* The lack of empirical validation weakens the claims of practical relevance.
* The high level of mathematical detail could be better balanced with intuitive explanations.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper is a technically strong and theoretically significant contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistics and latent variable modeling. The novelty of the approach, the rigor of the analysis, and the potential impact of the results make it a worthy candidate for acceptance to NIPS. However, the authors should consider addressing the weaknesses mentioned above, particularly by providing more intuitive explanations, discussing the practical implications of the assumptions, and including some empirical validation, to further strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
