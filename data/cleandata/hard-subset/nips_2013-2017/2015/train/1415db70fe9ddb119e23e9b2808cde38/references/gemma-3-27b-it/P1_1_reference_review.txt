## Peer Review: High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality

**Summary:**

This paper tackles a critical gap in the theoretical understanding of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm – its behavior in high-dimensional settings for latent variable models.  While EM is widely used, theoretical guarantees largely exist only for low-dimensional cases and global optima, which EM doesn’t necessarily find. The authors develop a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and establish both statistical rate of convergence *and* asymptotic normality for the resulting estimator and hypothesis tests.  They provide a unified framework applicable to Gaussian Mixture Models and Mixture of Regression models, demonstrating practical relevance.  The work builds on prior advancements [2, 25, 30] but significantly extends them to the high-dimensional regime, addressing limitations of existing approaches like tensor methods [1, 9] and Lasso-based methods [15, 16].

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a Significant Gap:** The paper directly tackles a major open problem in statistical learning – providing theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models, where existing theory fails.
* **Novel Approach:** The introduction of the truncation step to enforce sparsity is a clever and effective approach to overcome the challenges of high dimensionality. It’s well-motivated and crucial to achieving the results.
* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The authors provide rigorous theoretical analysis, establishing both statistical rates of convergence (p√s* log(d/n)) and asymptotic normality for the estimator and related score statistics.  The conditions are well-defined and appear reasonable.
* **Unified Framework:** The ability to accommodate both exact maximization and gradient ascent implementations of the M-step is a strength, increasing the practical applicability of the theory.
* **Optimality:** Establishing asymptotic normality with variance achieving the semiparametric information bound is a significant result, showcasing the efficiency of the proposed method.
* **Clear Presentation (mostly):** The paper is generally well-structured.  Algorithms are clearly presented, and the notation is mostly consistent.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Technical Density:** The paper is *extremely* technical, requiring a strong background in high-dimensional statistics and optimization.  The sheer number of conditions (3.1-3.3, 4.1-4.5) and their associated notation can be overwhelming.  More intuitive explanations of these conditions would be beneficial.
* **Condition Verification:** While the authors mention verifying conditions for Gaussian Mixture and Regression models in the appendix, the details seem somewhat limited. A more thorough and accessible discussion of how these conditions hold in practice for common models would strengthen the paper. Specifically, the implications of the sparsity assumption on w* (Assumption 4.5) are not fully discussed and could be a barrier to adoption.
* **Constant Factors:** The rates and bounds presented often involve unspecified constants (C, C0, etc.).  While standard in theoretical analysis,  providing some indication of the magnitude of these constants (even if just through simulations) would be helpful in understanding the practical relevance of the results.
* **Computational Cost:** The truncation step, while crucial theoretically, might introduce computational overhead. The paper does not address the practical implications of this overhead.  A brief discussion of computational complexity would be valuable.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** The paper focuses primarily on theoretical results. Including some limited empirical validation (even on simulated data) demonstrating the convergence behavior and the accuracy of the asymptotic normality results would significantly strengthen the work.



**Quality:**

The paper is technically very sound. The proofs appear rigorous (though a complete verification would require significant effort). The analysis is complete in the sense that it establishes both estimation and inference guarantees. The authors are careful to state their assumptions and limitations.

**Clarity:**

The clarity is good *for an expert in the field*. However, the sheer technicality and density of notation make it challenging for a broader audience.  More intuitive explanations and more detailed examples would improve clarity.  The organization is logical, but the numerous conditions and lemmas interrupt the flow somewhat.

**Originality:**

The work is highly original. Extending the theoretical understanding of EM to the high-dimensional setting, coupled with the novel truncation step, represents a significant contribution.  The paper effectively builds on existing work but significantly advances the state of the art.

**Significance:**

The results are potentially very significant. Providing theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models will be impactful for researchers and practitioners alike. The ability to perform valid statistical inference in this setting is a major advancement. The results could lead to more reliable and interpretable models in a wide range of applications.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a critical gap in the theoretical understanding of a widely used algorithm.
* Presents a novel and effective approach to overcome the challenges of high dimensionality.
* Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees for both estimation and inference.
* Offers a unified framework with broad applicability.
* Demonstrates potential for significant impact on both research and practice.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* High technical density may limit accessibility.
* Limited empirical validation.
* Lack of discussion on computational cost.
* The assumptions could be difficult to verify in practice.



**Overall Assessment:**

I strongly recommend acceptance.  Despite the technical challenges, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. The theoretical results are impressive, and the potential impact is substantial.  I believe that the strengths of the paper significantly outweigh the weaknesses.  I would recommend that the authors consider addressing the weaknesses identified above (particularly by providing more intuitive explanations and some empirical validation) in a revised version of the paper.

**Confidence:** 9/10
