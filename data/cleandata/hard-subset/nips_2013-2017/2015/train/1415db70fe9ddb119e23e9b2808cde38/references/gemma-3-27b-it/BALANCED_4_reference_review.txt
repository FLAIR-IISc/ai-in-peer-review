This paper addresses the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors propose a high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity and establish statistical and computational guarantees, including convergence rates and asymptotic normality for hypothesis testing. The work builds upon existing literature on EM convergence in low dimensions [2, 18, 25, 30] and addresses the gap in high-dimensional settings. It’s a significant and relevant contribution, as theoretical understanding of EM in high dimensions is limited.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The introduction of the truncation step within the EM framework to handle high dimensionality is a novel and promising approach. This addresses a key limitation of existing theory.
* **Strong Theoretical Results:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical guarantees for both parameter estimation (convergence rates) and inference (asymptotic normality of a decorrelated score statistic).  The achieved rates (p s⇤ · log d/n) are near-optimal.
* **Generality:** The framework is general and applicable to a range of latent variable models, demonstrated through applications to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.
* **Technical Depth:** The analysis is technically sophisticated, addressing the complexities arising from high dimensionality and the non-convexity of the likelihood function. The careful characterization of the iterative solution’s support is particularly noteworthy.
* **Clear Connection to Existing Work:** The paper clearly positions itself within the existing literature, acknowledging prior work and highlighting its contributions. The comparison with tensor methods [1, 9] and debiasing/desparsification techniques [4, 13, 15, 16, 17, 19, 20, 22, 24, 26, 29, 31, 32] is well-articulated.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Technical Complexity:** The paper is dense and mathematically challenging. While this is expected for a theoretical work, it might hinder accessibility for some readers. More intuitive explanations of key concepts, particularly around the truncation step and the decorrelated score statistic, would be beneficial.
* **Assumptions:**  The numerous technical conditions (3.1-3.3 and 4.1-4.5) while necessary for rigorous analysis, feel somewhat overwhelming. While the authors address verification for specific models in the appendix, a clearer discussion of the practical implications of these conditions, and their likelihood to hold in common scenarios, would strengthen the work.  The condition on s⇤w in Assumption 4.5, relating to the sparsity of the score function, particularly requires justification for its plausibility in various applications.
* **Computational Cost:** The paper focuses primarily on theoretical guarantees. A discussion of the computational cost of the truncated EM algorithm compared to standard EM or other high-dimensional alternatives would be valuable.  The impact of the truncation parameter bs on performance isn’t fully explored.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:**  While theoretical guarantees are the main focus, the lack of any empirical results (even on small-scale simulated data) weakens the impact.  Demonstrating the practical benefits of the proposed algorithm through simulations would be a significant improvement.
* **Notation:** The notation is very heavy, and occasionally difficult to follow, even for readers familiar with the field.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper tackles a fundamentally important and open problem in statistical machine learning.
* The theoretical results are strong, novel, and potentially impactful.
* The generality of the framework is a significant strength.
* The technical analysis is rigorous and well-executed.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper's high level of technical complexity and numerous assumptions may limit its accessibility and immediate applicability.
* The lack of empirical validation weakens the practical impact of the work.
* The computational cost of the algorithm is not discussed.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality theoretical paper that makes a significant contribution to the understanding of the EM algorithm in high-dimensional settings. The rigorous analysis and novel results are commendable. While the paper’s technical complexity and lack of empirical validation are weaknesses, the strengths outweigh these concerns. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Improve Accessibility:** Provide more intuitive explanations of key concepts, particularly around the truncation step and the decorrelated score statistic.
* **Discussion of Assumptions:** Provide a more detailed discussion of the practical implications of the technical conditions and their likelihood to hold in real-world scenarios. Justify the sparsity assumption on the score function (s⇤w).
* **Computational Analysis:**  Include a discussion of the computational cost of the proposed algorithm and the impact of the truncation parameter bs.
* **Empirical Validation:**  Add a section with simulated experiments to demonstrate the practical performance of the algorithm and validate the theoretical results.
* **Notation:** Streamline the notation where possible to improve readability.  A more consistent use of notation would be helpful.
