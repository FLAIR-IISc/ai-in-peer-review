- Proposes a “high-dimensional EM” algorithm with truncation in both E- and M-steps to promote sparsity and enable estimation when p > n.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for asymptotically normal inference on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Provides a comprehensive theoretical framework proving geometric convergence and near-optimal error rate √(s log d / n) under suitable initialization.  
- Extends EM convergence theory from low-dimensional settings to high-dimensional latent variable models, filling a notable gap.  
- Demonstrates applicability to Gaussian Mixture Models and Mixture of Regressions.  
- Shows strong theoretical rigor and unified analysis of computational and statistical guarantees.  
- Claims statistical optimality and provides inferential tools for uncertainty quantification in high dimensions.  
- Necessity and advantage of iterative truncation (sparse EM) over a single final truncation remain unconvincing and under-discussed.  
- Needs clearer comparison and theoretical/empirical justification against standard EM followed by one truncation step; linear regression is only briefly noted as a counterexample.  
- Relies on an initialization within a basin of attraction; practical strategies and implications for real-world use are insufficiently addressed.  
- Technical conditions are numerous and complex; the paper would benefit from simplification or more intuitive explanations.  
- Omits related work in sparse optimization (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 truncated gradient methods) that shares conceptual links.  
- Experimental validation is absent; details on initialization used in proofs/simulations and reproducibility are missing.  
- Computational cost of performing truncation iteratively is not analyzed and should be reported.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on authors addressing sparse-EM necessity, clarifying technical conditions, adding experiments, and detailing initialization and computational cost.
