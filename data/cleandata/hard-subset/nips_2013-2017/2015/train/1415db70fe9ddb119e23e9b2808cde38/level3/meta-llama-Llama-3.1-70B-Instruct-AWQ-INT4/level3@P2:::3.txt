This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation. The authors also develop an asymptotically normal decorrelated score statistic for testing hypotheses about low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters. The proposed algorithm and inferential procedure are applied to a broad family of high-dimensional latent variable models.

The paper makes two main contributions. Firstly, it proposes a sparse EM procedure that truncates the parameter vector at each step to enforce a desired sparsity. This approach is shown to converge geometrically to the truth up to a statistical error term that scales with the square root of the sparsity level times the infinity-norm M-step error. Secondly, the authors introduce an asymptotically normal decorrelated score statistic for testing whether a specific parameter entry is non-zero.

The paper assumes that the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin of attraction and requires initialization within that basin. A new technical condition is introduced, which controls the error in estimating the M operator, required only for sparse vectors. The authors note that for many models, estimating M at all vectors is as easy as at sparse vectors, which raises the question of whether ordinary EM followed by a single truncation could achieve similar guarantees.

The paper provides important high-dimensional convergence guarantees, but the specific necessity and advantage of the sparse EM variant remain uncertain. The authors suggest that citing related sparse optimization work, such as Langford et al. (2009) on truncated gradient, could provide additional context. The experimental section should report what initialization was used.

The strengths of the paper include its novel approach to incorporating sparsity structure into EM algorithm, its ability to provide high-dimensional convergence guarantees, and its application to a broad family of latent variable models. The weaknesses of the paper include the uncertainty surrounding the necessity and advantage of the sparse EM variant and the lack of experimental results.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to incorporating sparsity structure into EM algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The paper provides high-dimensional convergence guarantees, which is an important result for high-dimensional data analysis.
* The paper applies the proposed algorithm and inferential procedure to a broad family of latent variable models, which demonstrates its practicality.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper raises more questions than it answers, particularly regarding the necessity and advantage of the sparse EM variant.
* The paper lacks experimental results, which makes it difficult to evaluate the practicality of the proposed approach.
* The paper assumes that the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin of attraction, which may not be true for all models.

Overall, I recommend accepting the paper, but with the condition that the authors address the concerns raised above, particularly regarding the necessity and advantage of the sparse EM variant and the lack of experimental results.
