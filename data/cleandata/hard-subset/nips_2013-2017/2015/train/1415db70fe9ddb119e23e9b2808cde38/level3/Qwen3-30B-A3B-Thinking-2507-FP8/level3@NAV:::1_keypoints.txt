- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, providing computational convergence guarantees and statistical optimality.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM analysis to high-dimensional settings, proving geometric convergence and introducing a decorrelated score test for asymptotic inference.  
- Builds on Ning & Liu [27] for high-dimensional inference while addressing latent-variable-specific computational challenges.  
- Main contributions: a novel sparse high-dimensional EM, theoretical guarantees for parameter estimation and inference, and a computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation/inference in high-dimensional latent-variable models.  
- Theoretical results are strong, achieving near-minimax optimal rates and semiparametrically efficient inference.  
- Major omission: no practical procedure or concrete conditions provided to obtain the required initial estimator β^init.  
- No guidance on when to use exact maximization versus gradient-ascent implementations of the M-step.  
- Tuning parameter choices (ĥs and λ) lack justification; need sensitivity analysis and data-driven selection methods.  
- The decorrelated score test appears to be a direct application of Ning & Liu [27], but the paper fails to explicitly acknowledge or clarify any new development required for latent-variable models.  
- Technical assumptions (Conditions 3.1–3.3) need more careful justification and verification for the specific latent-variable models considered.  
- The primary technical novelty is the truncation step in EM; the paper should better explain why applying Ning & Liu’s framework to latent-variable models is nontrivial.  
- Recommend expanding the main text to include more technical details (now in the supplement) and clarifying practical implementation and attribution.  
- Overall: technically sound and potentially impactful, but requires substantial clarification of practical details and clearer attribution before acceptance.
