## Review: High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality

This paper tackles the challenging problem of applying the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm to high-dimensional latent variable models, a setting where theoretical guarantees are largely absent. The authors propose a modified EM algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and provide convergence guarantees along with a framework for statistical inference. The work builds upon recent advances in understanding the EM algorithm's behavior, notably [2, 25, 30], extending them to the high-dimensional regime. It also connects to the broader literature on sparse estimation and inference, referencing relevant work on Lasso and related techniques [8, 13, 26].

The core contribution lies in demonstrating that, with appropriate initialization and a carefully chosen truncation parameter, the proposed algorithm converges at a geometrically decreasing rate to an estimator achieving a near-optimal statistical rate of convergence (p√s log(d/n)).  Furthermore, the paper introduces a novel statistical test framework for assessing the sparsity of the estimator. The paper is well-organized and the related work is sufficiently summarized, demonstrating a good understanding of the existing literature.

However, the paper suffers from a significant issue: density. Sections 3 and 4, detailing the theoretical results, are exceptionally dense and difficult to follow. The numerous conditions and technical notations require considerable effort from the reader, hindering accessibility. Simplifying these sections with more intuitive explanations and breaking down complex proofs would significantly improve clarity. 

A key concern is the claim of minimax optimality (p√s log(d/n)). While stated prominently, the manuscript lacks a proof establishing this optimality. A clear justification or citation to a relevant result demonstrating this claim is crucial.  Practically, the choice of the truncation parameter ^s (Eq. 3.8) is problematic as it depends on the unknown true sparsity level *s*. The authors need to address how this parameter can be estimated in practice.  Additionally, the requirement for an initial beta^init satisfying ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 seems difficult to satisfy in practice and requires further clarification on how to obtain such an initialization. Finally, the role of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not fully clear; a discussion of the algorithm's behavior *without* truncation would be insightful.

Post-rebuttal, I’ve raised my score to 7. While the paper presents a solid statistical contribution, its complexity makes it challenging for a broader machine learning audience. Its practical usefulness remains questionable due to the difficulties in parameter tuning and initialization.  The work is a valuable addition to the theoretical understanding of the EM algorithm, but requires significant effort to digest and apply.
