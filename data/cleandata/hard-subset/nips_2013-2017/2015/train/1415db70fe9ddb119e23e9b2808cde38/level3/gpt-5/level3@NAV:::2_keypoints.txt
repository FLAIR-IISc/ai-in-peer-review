- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with an added truncation step to enforce sparsity, proving geometric contraction and near-minimax estimation rates.
- Presents an inferential framework using a decorrelated score that gives asymptotically normal tests for low-dimensional components to assess sparsity patterns.
- Extends Balakrishnan et al.’s “population to sample” EM analysis to the p >> n setting by controlling entrywise errors and support evolution.
- Connects to high-dimensional inference via Dantzig-type projections, yielding sharper rates and valid inference without needing a global optimum of a convex surrogate.
- Theory is careful and technically strong; Lipschitz/concavity and statistical-error conditions are clearly stated and verified for GMM and mixture regression.
- Two claims need clarification: the paper sometimes implies convergence to a “global solution” though theorems only show contraction to a sparse fixed point within a basin.
- The minimax-rate claim (√(s log d / n)) requires a precise lower bound or citation; no clear proof/citation found in the main text.
- Sections 3–4 are dense; a high-level roadmap, clearer role for each assumption, and intuition for the truncation step would improve clarity.
- Request ablation/intuition: why hard-thresholding vs. ℓ1-penalized M-step and what fails without truncation.
- Practical tuning concern: Eq. (3.8) uses ŝ depending on unknown s* — authors should specify practical selection (e.g., path + BIC/stability selection or adaptive thresholding with theory).
- Initialization concern: need concrete, model-specific procedures to obtain β_init with ||β_init − β*||2 < R/2 and sensitivity analyses.
- Ask for empirical/theoretical impact of omitting the T-step (ablation study requested).
- Originality: novel combination of EM with iterative truncation and semiparametric-efficient inference for latent-variable models in high dimensions.
- Significance: statistically important, but practical usefulness is uncertain due to tuning and initialization requirements.
- Pros: strong theory, clear relation to prior EM and high-dimensional inference, general framework covering exact and gradient M-steps.
- Cons: dense presentation, unclear practical tuning/initialization, limited accessibility to a general ML audience.
- After rebuttal the reviewer raised score from 6 to 7 — authors addressed several points but practicality and accessibility concerns remain.
