

# Review for "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure through a truncation step in both E-step and M-step, establishing geometric convergence with near-optimal statistical rates. The authors further develop a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing of low-dimensional components in high-dimensional parameter spaces. The work extends previous low-dimensional EM convergence analyses by [2, 18, 25, 30] to high-dimensional settings where sparsity assumptions are necessary for meaningful estimation.

**Quality**: The theoretical contributions are substantial. The paper establishes the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional latent variable models. The convergence rate analysis (Theorem 3.4) is technically rigorous, decomposing estimation error into optimization and statistical components. However, the claim that the statistical rate √(s* log d/n) is minimax-optimal lacks explicit proof in the main manuscript, which is a significant gap. The authors should provide a clear minimax lower bound proof or cite an appropriate reference. Additionally, the practical concerns regarding the tuning parameter ^s in Eq. (3.8) (which depends on unknown true sparsity s*) and the initialization requirement ||β^init - β*||_2 < R/2 need more thorough discussion. The truncation step's necessity (what happens without it?) is not adequately addressed.

**Clarity**: The paper is well-organized with comprehensive related work, but Sections 3 and 4 are exceptionally dense and technically challenging to follow. The theoretical conditions (Conditions 3.1-3.3, 4.1-4.4) are abstract and lack concrete examples for readers to grasp their implications. The connection between the theoretical framework and practical implementation could be clearer. The paper would benefit from a more intuitive explanation of the truncation step's role, particularly why it's necessary for high-dimensional convergence.

**Originality**: The work makes a clear contribution by extending EM algorithm analysis to high-dimensional settings with sparsity, which is a significant gap in the literature. The decorrelated score statistic for inference in high-dimensional latent variable models is novel. While the truncation step is a natural extension of sparsity methods, the integration into the EM framework with rigorous convergence guarantees represents a meaningful advance over prior work on high-dimensional M-estimation [13, 15, 26, 32].

**Significance**: The paper addresses a fundamental gap between computation and statistics in high-dimensional latent variable models, which are increasingly important in modern applications. The theoretical framework could potentially impact fields like genomics and signal processing where high-dimensional mixture models are common. However, the practical utility is questionable given the complex initialization requirements and tuning parameters that depend on unknown model parameters. The paper's focus on statistical theory rather than practical implementation limits its immediate impact on ML practitioners.

**Strengths**: The paper is well-written with comprehensive related work coverage. The theoretical framework is unified across multiple latent variable models (Gaussian mixture, mixture of regression). The two main contributions (high-dimensional EM algorithm and decorrelated inference) are clearly articulated and well-motivated.

**Weaknesses**: The main weaknesses are the lack of explicit minimax optimality proof, insufficient discussion of practical implementation details, and the dense presentation of technical conditions. The truncation step's necessity is not demonstrated, and the practical concerns regarding tuning parameters and initialization are not adequately addressed.

**Arguments for acceptance**: 
- First theoretical guarantee for high-dimensional EM algorithm with sparsity
- Unified framework for both estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models
- Rigorous convergence analysis with geometric rate and near-optimal statistical rate
- Novel decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing

**Arguments against acceptance**:
- Lack of minimax optimality proof for the claimed statistical rate
- Practical implementation concerns regarding tuning parameters and initialization
- Overly dense presentation of technical details in key sections
- Questionable practical utility due to complex requirements

**Recommendation**: I recommend acceptance (score 7/10). This is a strong theoretical contribution to the statistical analysis of high-dimensional latent variable models, addressing an important gap in the literature. The paper makes significant advances in understanding the computational and statistical properties of the EM algorithm in high dimensions. While the practical implementation challenges and dense presentation are notable weaknesses, these do not undermine the fundamental theoretical contribution. The paper would benefit from addressing the minimax optimality claim and providing more practical implementation guidance in the final version. This work represents a valuable addition to the theoretical foundations of high-dimensional statistics, even if its immediate applicability to ML practitioners is limited.
