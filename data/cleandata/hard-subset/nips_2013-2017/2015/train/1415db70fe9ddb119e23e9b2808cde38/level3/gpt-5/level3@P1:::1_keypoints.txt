- Main contribution: high-dimensional EM with truncation that yields geometric convergence to a near-optimal sparse estimator and an inference procedure (decorrelated score/Wald) with asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency for low-dimensional targets.  
- Scope: analysis covers exact-M and gradient-M EM, provides sample- and population-level contraction and entrywise statistical control, and demonstrates results for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions.  
- Relation to prior work: extends Balakrishnan et al.’s “population to sample” EM to p ≫ n via truncation and adapts Ning & Liu’s decorrelated-score ideas to EM through a Q-to-score/Fisher bridge.  
- Advantage over tensor methods: achieves substantially sharper high-dimensional rates.  
- Strength: clean algorithmic idea (E/M + truncation) with unified analysis for both exact and gradient M-steps.  
- Strength: sharp estimation rates O(√(s* log d / n)) up to logs, with explicit optimization vs statistical error decomposition and geometric contraction.  
- Strength: inference attains asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency for low-dimensional parameters amid high-dimensional nuisance.  
- Strength: conditions are explicitly stated and verified for representative models.  
- Weakness: requires strong assumptions (SNR for mixtures, sparsity of w* for orthogonalization, and a good initializer) with limited practical guidance on meeting them.  
- Weakness: tuning and implementation choices (sparsity level ŝ and Dantzig λ) lack concrete data-driven rules and sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: key technical ideas (support evolution under truncation, entrywise M-step error control) are deferred to the supplement, reducing assessability.  
- Weakness: relationship to Ning & Liu’s decorrelated score is insufficiently explicit about what is reused versus what is novel.  
- Quality: technical development appears sound with appropriate concavity/smoothness and Lipschitz conditions and a convincing optimization/statistical error decomposition.  
- Clarity: high-level narrative is clear but practical guidance and some notation should be elevated and summarized in the main text.  
- Originality: truncated EM analysis with entrywise control for optimal rates is novel; adapting decorrelated-score inference to EM/latent-variable settings is nontrivial.  
- Significance: first comprehensive estimation-and-inference theory for high-dimensional EM across latent-variable models, with likely methodological and theoretical impact.  
- Suggestion—Initialization: provide concrete, model-specific initializers, finite-sample heuristics, conditions ensuring constant-relative-error, and robustness experiments.  
- Suggestion—Exact vs gradient M: give practical guidance on when to use each, step-size selection (e.g., η = 2/(ν+μ)), and wall-clock tradeoffs.  
- Suggestion—Choosing ŝ and λ: recommend overspecifying ŝ modestly (e.g., 2–4×s*), use validation/BIC or stability selection for ŝ, and set λ proportional to σ̂√(log d/n) with validation tuning.  
- Suggestion—Decorrelated score/prior art: explicitly cite Ning & Liu and state precisely which parts coincide and which are new (Q-to-score bridge and tailored concentration results).  
- Suggestion—Assumptions: summarize regimes where conditions hold (e.g., SNR thresholds, n ≳ s* log d) and discuss practicality of w* sparsity.  
- Suggestion—Exposition: move proof sketches for truncation/support evolution and entrywise concentration into the main text and add a practical-guide subsection.  
- Pro arguments for acceptance: solid, timely theory bridging computation and inference; clear algorithm with strong guarantees; semiparametric-efficient inference.  
- Con arguments against acceptance: heavy reliance on strong SNR and sparsity assumptions, insufficient practical guidance/empirical validation, and partial novelty in inference requiring clearer attribution.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution, recommendation to accept pending clearer attribution, enhanced practical guidance, and improved exposition/empirical support.
