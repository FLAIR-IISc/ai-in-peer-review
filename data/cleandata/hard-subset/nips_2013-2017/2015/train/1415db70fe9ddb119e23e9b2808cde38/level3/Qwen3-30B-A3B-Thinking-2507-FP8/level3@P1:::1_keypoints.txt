- Develops a general theoretical framework for high-dimensional latent-variable models using a truncated EM algorithm that enforces sparsity, proving geometric convergence and near-optimal statistical rates.  
- Proposes a decorrelated score test for low-dimensional inference, establishing asymptotic normality with optimal limiting variance.  
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM analyses to high dimensions, achieving sharper p(s* log d / n) rates by incorporating sparsity via truncation.  
- Strength: unified framework for parameter estimation and inference with clear decomposition of optimization vs. statistical error.  
- Strength: rigorous proof of asymptotic normality for the decorrelated score statistic and demonstrated applicability to Gaussian mixtures and mixture regression.  
- Major concern: lacks practical guidance for obtaining the required initializer β^{init} satisfying the prescribed ℓ2 closeness.  
- Major concern: no guidance on when to use exact M-step maximization (Algorithm 2) versus gradient-ascent M-step (Algorithm 3), or their computational trade-offs.  
- Major concern: tuning parameters bs (sparsity level) and λ (Dantzig selector) lack data-driven selection methods and sensitivity analysis.  
- Major concern: insufficient acknowledgement and explanation of the connection to Ning & Liu (2014) and the novel adaptations needed for latent-variable, nonconvex settings.  
- Major concern: numerous technical assumptions (Conditions 3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) need clearer justification in the main text and better verification for considered models, especially sparsity of w*.  
- Key technical innovation: the truncation step that enforces sparsity along the solution path requires novel analysis to handle evolving support sets.  
- Suggestion: move critical technical details (e.g., proof sketch for Theorem 3.4 and verification of assumptions) from the supplement into the main paper.  
- Overall verdict: important and non-trivial theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing implementation and exposition concerns.
