- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, achieving geometric convergence to a near-optimal estimator with error depending on sparsity and M-step infinity-norm error.
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing that is asymptotically normal and attains the semiparametric information bound for low-dimensional components.
- Theoretical contributions: provides rigorous convergence guarantees and statistical error bounds for high-dimensional EM where prior EM lacks theory.
- Novelty: embedding truncation inside EM iterations is an innovative way to incorporate sparsity vs. post-hoc approaches.
- Inferential procedure: the decorrelated score enables principled hypothesis testing in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Generality: framework applies broadly to models like Gaussian mixtures and regression mixtures.
- Related-work positioning: clearly situates improvements over low-dimensional EM results and tensor-based methods.
- Weakness: not convincingly shown that truncation at every iteration is necessary; one post-EM truncation might suffice.
- Weakness: experimental section omits critical details about the initialization method required for convergence guarantees.
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited; lacks comparisons to ordinary EM and other sparse methods (e.g., Langford et al., 2009).
- Weakness: computational overhead from per-iteration truncation is not analyzed, raising scalability concerns.
- For acceptance: makes significant theoretical advances filling a gap in high-dimensional EM methodology.
- For acceptance: offers a valuable high-dimensional inference tool via the decorrelated score.
- For acceptance: well-integrated into literature on sparse optimization and latent variable models.
- Against acceptance: necessity and practical benefit of iterative truncation remain unclear.
- Against acceptance: experiments are underdeveloped, missing initialization details and comparative analyses.
- Against acceptance: lacks adequate treatment of computational feasibility for very high-dimensional problems.
- Recommendation: accept conditionally, requiring clarifications/improvements on initialization, experimental comparisons, and computational overhead analysis.
