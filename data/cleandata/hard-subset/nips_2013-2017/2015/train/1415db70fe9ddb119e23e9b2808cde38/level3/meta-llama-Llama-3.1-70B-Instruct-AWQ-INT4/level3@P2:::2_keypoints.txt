- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that enforces sparsity and a new inferential procedure for testing low-dimensional components.  
- Establishes a general theory showing geometric convergence and (near-)optimal statistical rates for the proposed EM estimator.  
- Constructs a decorrelated score statistic that is asymptotically normal and attains the semiparametric information bound for low-dimensional components.  
- Provides a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty quantification in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models.  
- Paper is well-organized with thorough related-work discussion, but Sections 3–4 are very dense and hard to follow.  
- Practical concerns: Equation (3.8) requires an estimate of sparsity s that depends on unknown true s*, with no guidance for choosing it.  
- Practical concerns: initialization requires beta^init within R/2 of beta*, but no practical method is provided to obtain such an initializer.  
- Unclear role and necessity of the truncation step in Algorithm 1; authors should clarify or provide simulations showing its effect.  
- Claim that the sqrt(s * log(d/n)) rate is minimax-optimal is made without a proof in the main manuscript; a clear proof is requested.  
- Overall assessment: solid and significant theoretical contribution but practical usefulness may be limited due to implementation guidance and exposition density.  
- Reviewer raised score from 6 to 7, citing the significance of contributions despite the concerns.
