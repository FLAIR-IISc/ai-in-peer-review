- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step enforcing sparsity, proven to converge geometrically to an estimator with near-optimal statistical rate.  
- Develops a decorrelated score test enabling valid hypothesis testing and uncertainty quantification for individual parameters from the sparse EM estimator.  
- Extends theoretical analysis of EM (e.g., Balakrishnan et al. 2017) to the high-dimensional, non-convex, iterative setting and adapts high-dimensional inference tools.  
- Significance: tackles a key problem at the intersection of computation and statistical theory, with potential impact for researchers and practitioners.  
- Quality: provides comprehensive theoretical guarantees—computational (geometric convergence), statistical (near-minimax rates), and inferential (asymptotic normality).  
- Originality: novel combination of iterative truncation with EM and a decorrelation step for inference on a non-global, non-convex estimator.  
- Lacks practical guidance on obtaining a suitable initial estimator β^{init}; conditions and concrete methods should be clarified.  
- Needs advice on choosing between maximization and gradient-ascent M-steps, including practical trade-offs and recommendations.  
- Requires guidance on selecting tuning parameters (sparsity level bs and Dantzig penalty λ), including data-dependent selection and sensitivity analysis.  
- Should better situate the decorrelated score test relative to existing M-estimator inference (e.g., Ning & Liu 2017) and clarify novel technical contributions for the non-convex/local-solution setting.  
- Improve clarity by providing intuitive discussion of high-level technical assumptions and moving key proof sketches (especially on iterative truncation) from the supplement into the main paper.
