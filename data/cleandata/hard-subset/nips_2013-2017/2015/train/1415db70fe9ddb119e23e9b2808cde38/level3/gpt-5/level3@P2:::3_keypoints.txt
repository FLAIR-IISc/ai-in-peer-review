- Proposes a "sparse EM" with an added truncation (T) step to keep top-b_s coordinates each iteration.  
- Proves geometric contraction of iterates within a basin of attraction up to an irreducible statistical error.  
- Final estimator achieves near-minimax rate ~ sqrt(s*)·sqrt(log d / n), for exact- and gradient-EM variants.  
- Introduces a decorrelated (debiased) score using a Dantzig-type estimator for low-dimensional inference in latent-variable models.  
- Shows the score is asymptotically normal and attains the semiparametric information bound, enabling optimal tests and CIs.  
- Framework instantiated for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions, verifying abstract conditions and SNR regimes.  
- Extends low-dimensional nonasymptotic EM theory to high-dimensional sparse settings.  
- Connects to de-biasing/desparsification literature but adapts methods to nonconvex latent-variable likelihoods via Q-function identities.  
- Truncation step conceptually related to hard-thresholding and truncated gradient methods.  
- Claims sharper rates and genuinely high-dimensional coverage compared to tensor methods.  
- Strength: general, technically careful framework with simultaneous computational and statistical guarantees.  
- Strength: novel entrywise/componentwise concentration analysis of the sample M-operator tailored to sparse inputs.  
- Strength: asymptotically optimal inference (semiparametric efficiency) for low-dimensional targets with high-dimensional nuisances.  
- Strength: concrete model examples that illustrate verification of conditions and resulting rates.  
- Strength: helps close gap between computation and high-dimensional statistics for EM-like nonconvex problems.  
- Weakness: unclear necessity/advantage of iterative truncation vs. one-shot post-EM truncation; requests empirical/theoretical ablation and a concrete counterexample.  
- Weakness: Condition 3.3 restricts entrywise control to sparse inputs, raising questions whether truncation is analytically convenient but unnecessary; asks for discussion on relaxing this.  
- Weakness: strong dependence on initialization; asks to report experiment initializers, propose practical initializers, and provide sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: limited guidance on tuning b_s, step sizes, and Dantzig parameter; requests practical selection strategies and robustness checks.  
- Weakness: some theory uses resampling—clarify which results hold for single-sample-pass algorithm and provide empirical evidence.  
- Weakness: inference depends on sparsity of the decorrelating vector w*; asks for plausibility discussion, empirical estimates of s_w*, and alternatives like nodewise regressions.  
- Weakness: Gaussian mixture guarantees require large SNR; requests empirical performance as SNR decreases and boundary delineation.  
- Quality: technically sound with careful nonasymptotic reasoning, but assumptions need clearer articulation and practical verifiability.  
- Clarity: notation-heavy; recommends summarizing key conditions in a compact table and explicitly stating which results require resampling.  
- Originality: original combination of EM analysis with iterative sparsity-enforcing truncation and semiparametrically efficient inference.  
- Significance: high — provides a template for optimal estimation and valid inference in high-dimensional nonconvex latent-variable problems.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong theory (near-minimax rates, optimal inference), general framework, and advances over prior low-dimensional EM analyses.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient empirical justification for iterative truncation, heavy reliance on initialization, resampling-theory vs practice gap, and limited tuning/robustness details.  
- Requested revisions: add EM+one-shot and iterative HHT baselines, provide a concrete counterexample, cite truncated-gradient/IHT literature, report initialization strategies and sensitivity, give tuning guidance for b_s and Dantzig parameter, and clarify resampling requirements.  
- Overall recommendation: important and timely contribution with a lean toward acceptance pending clarifications and stronger empirical support.
