- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that enforces sparsity via a truncation step for latent variable models.  
- Introduces a new inferential procedure for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Establishes a unified analytic framework that provides simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees including (near-)optimal statistical rates of convergence.  
- Builds on prior EM work (e.g., [2]) but extends to high-dimensional settings and connects to tensor-method approaches.  
- Requires several technical conditions (Conditions 3.1–3.3) that may not hold broadly.  
- Numerical experiments are limited and need expansion to better demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Comparative evaluation against existing methods (including [2]) is insufficient.  
- Paper is original in its algorithm and inferential contributions.  
- Addresses a significant problem with potential practical impact for high-dimensional data.  
- Well-written, clearly organized, and mathematically rigorous with detailed proofs in the appendix.  
- Recommend adding more numerical experiments and comparisons to baseline methods.  
- Recommend discussing the limitations and applicability of the technical assumptions.  
- Recommend providing more details on the truncation step and its effect on iterations.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on revisions to address the listed weaknesses.
