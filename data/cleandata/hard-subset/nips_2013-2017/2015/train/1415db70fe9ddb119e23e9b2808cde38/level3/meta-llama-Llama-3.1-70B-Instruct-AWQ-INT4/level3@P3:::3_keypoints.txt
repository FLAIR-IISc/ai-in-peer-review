- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity and a general inferential framework for simultaneous estimation and uncertainty assessment.
- Main contributions: (i) HD-EM with geometric convergence achieving (near-)optimal statistical rate; (ii) a decorrelated score statistic for inference on low-dimensional components.
- Decorrelated score shown asymptotically normal with limiting variance attaining the semiparametric information bound.
- Methodology is well-explained, paper is well-written, and notation is consistent.
- Provides detailed comparison with related work and highlights advantages of the approach.
- Technical conditions (population Q lower bound, sample vs population M-step errors) are reasonable and well-motivated.
- Sparsity assumption on the true parameter is standard but may not hold in all practical settings.
- No clear guidance on choosing tuning parameters (sparsity parameter, M-step stepsize).
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity of the proposed HD-EM algorithm.
- Provides detailed analysis and applications to Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regression models.
- Quality score: 8/10 — technically sound but with noted practical gaps.
- Clarity score: 9/10 — clear exposition and consistent notation.
- Originality score: 9/10 — unified framework and general inferential advances for latent-structure models.
- Significance score: 9/10 — likely to impact high-dimensional statistics and future research.
- Arguments for acceptance: unified estimation+inference framework, general inferential results for broad latent models, significant field implications.
- Arguments for rejection: reliance on sparsity assumption, unclear tuning-parameter selection, missing computational-complexity analysis.
