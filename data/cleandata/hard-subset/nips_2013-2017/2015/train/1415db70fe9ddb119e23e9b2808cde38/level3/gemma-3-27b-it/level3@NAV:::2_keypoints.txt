- Core contribution: modified EM with a truncation step, theoretical convergence guarantees to a global solution under specific initialization/conditions, and a convergence rate; plus a statistical test for estimator sparsity.  
- Paper is well-organized and cites relevant related work.  
- Strength: attempts to bridge computation (EM) and statistics (global-optimum guarantees); truncation is a sensible sparsity-inducing device.  
- Sections 3–4 are very dense and hard to follow; notation should be simplified and key steps given more intuitive explanations.  
- Multiple conditions (3.1–3.3) are presented without sufficient motivation, making their necessity unclear.  
- Claim of minimax optimality (√(s·log(d/n))) lacks a clear proof or citation and needs justification.  
- Practical issue: the estimated sparsity level (^s) in Eq.(3.8) depends on the unknown true sparsity s — no guidance on selecting ^s in practice.  
- Practical issue: the required initialization (||β^init − β*||_2 < R/2) lacks guidance on how to obtain such an initializer in realistic settings.  
- The role and necessity of the truncation step are not fully demonstrated; an ablation study or discussion on omitting it is needed.  
- Overall: technically solid and valuable to statistics, but dense with limited practical guidance and likely limited impact for a broader ML/NIPS audience; score raised from 6 to 7.
