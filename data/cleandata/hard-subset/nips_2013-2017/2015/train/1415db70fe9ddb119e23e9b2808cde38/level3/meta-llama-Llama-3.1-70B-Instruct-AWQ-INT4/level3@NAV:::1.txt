**Paper Review: High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation. The algorithm is designed for inferring high-dimensional latent variable models and provides a general theory for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment. The authors establish simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the proposed algorithm and demonstrate its effectiveness on two high-dimensional latent variable models: Gaussian mixture model and mixture of regression model.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel algorithm**: The paper proposes a new high-dimensional EM algorithm that effectively incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation.
2. **Theoretical guarantees**: The authors establish simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the proposed algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field.
3. **Broad applicability**: The algorithm is designed for a broad family of statistical models with latent structures, making it a valuable contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistics.
4. **Asymptotic normality**: The paper establishes the asymptotic normality of the proposed decorrelated score statistic, which is a crucial result for inference in high-dimensional settings.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Technical assumptions**: The paper relies on several technical assumptions, such as Conditions 3.1-3.3, which may not hold in all cases.
2. **Limited guidance**: The paper could provide more guidance on how to choose the tuning parameters, such as bs and λ, in practice.
3. **Sensitivity analysis**: The paper could benefit from a more thorough sensitivity analysis to assess the robustness of the proposed algorithm to different choices of tuning parameters and initializations.
4. **Comparison to existing work**: While the paper compares the proposed algorithm to existing work, a more detailed comparison to other high-dimensional EM algorithms would be helpful.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that effectively incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation. The algorithm is designed for a broad family of statistical models with latent structures, making it a valuable contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistics. The paper also establishes simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the proposed algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Significance**

The paper addresses a significant problem in high-dimensional statistics, namely, the development of a computationally feasible approach for optimal parameter estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional latent variable models. The proposed algorithm and theoretical guarantees have the potential to impact a wide range of applications, including genomics, finance, and social network analysis.

**Recommendations**

1. **Provide more guidance on tuning parameters**: The paper could provide more guidance on how to choose the tuning parameters, such as bs and λ, in practice.
2. **Conduct sensitivity analysis**: The paper could benefit from a more thorough sensitivity analysis to assess the robustness of the proposed algorithm to different choices of tuning parameters and initializations.
3. **Compare to existing work**: A more detailed comparison to other high-dimensional EM algorithms would be helpful.
4. **Consider extensions**: The paper could consider extensions to other high-dimensional models, such as generalized linear models or nonlinear models.

**Rating**

I would rate this paper as follows:

* Quality: 8/10
* Clarity: 9/10
* Originality: 9/10
* Significance: 9/10

Overall, I would recommend this paper for publication in a top-tier journal, with some revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
