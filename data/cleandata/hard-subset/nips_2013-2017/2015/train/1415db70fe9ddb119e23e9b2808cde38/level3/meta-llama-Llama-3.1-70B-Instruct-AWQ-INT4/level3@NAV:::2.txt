This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation for latent variable models. The algorithm is shown to converge at a geometric rate to a global solution with a near-optimal statistical rate of convergence. Additionally, the paper develops a decorrelated score test for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of the high-dimensional parameter.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear summary of related work. The technical conditions and assumptions are well-defined, and the proofs are thorough. The paper makes significant contributions to the field of high-dimensional statistics, providing a general framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment.

However, the paper is dense and may be challenging for non-experts to follow, particularly in Sections 3 and 4. The authors could consider simplifying the notation and providing more intuitive explanations for the technical conditions and assumptions.

The reviewer has several questions and concerns:

1. The claim that the algorithm achieves a minimax-optimal rate of convergence is not fully justified. The authors should provide a clear proof of this claim.
2. The practicality of the algorithm is questionable, as it requires the choice of a tuning parameter that depends on the unknown sparsity level.
3. The initialization of the algorithm is not fully explained. The authors should provide more details on how to obtain an initial estimate that satisfies the required conditions.
4. The role of the truncation step in the algorithm is not fully understood. The authors should provide more explanation on what happens if the truncation step is omitted.

Despite these concerns, the reviewer believes that the paper makes significant contributions to the field and is a solid statistics contribution. However, the practicality and usefulness of the algorithm are questionable, and the paper may be more suitable for researchers in the field rather than practitioners.

Rating: 7/10

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper makes significant contributions to the field of high-dimensional statistics.
* The algorithm is shown to converge at a geometric rate to a global solution with a near-optimal statistical rate of convergence.
* The paper provides a general framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment.

* The paper is dense and may be challenging for non-experts to follow.

* The role of the truncation step in the algorithm is not fully understood.
