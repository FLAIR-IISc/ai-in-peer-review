- Proposes an RPN that shares full-image convolutional features with the detection network, enabling nearly cost-free region proposals via a fully-convolutional, end-to-end trained network that predicts bounds and objectness.
- Introduces a simple alternating optimization scheme to train the RPN and Fast R-CNN to share convolutional features.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains motivation, methodology, and experimental setup.
- Provides thorough analysis including ablation studies and comparisons to state-of-the-art proposal methods.
- Results show competitive accuracy with existing methods while being significantly faster.
- Lacks discussion of how to find the initial solution for the RPN, which is crucial for algorithm convergence.
- Presents two algorithms but does not give practical guidance on which to use in different scenarios.
- Does not provide clear guidance or sensitivity analysis for tuning parameters (e.g., ŝ and λ).
- The decorrelated score test is not novel and should be cited/acknowledged.
- Main theoretical results rely on many technical conditions that may not hold for all latent variable models and need further discussion.
- Unclear which parts are genuinely novel versus clever combinations of existing results; more detail on technical challenges is needed.
- Technically sound with claims supported by theory and experiments; paper is well-organized and readable.
- Could benefit from more discussion on limitations and potential extensions.
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address the noted practical and theoretical concerns.
- Pros: novel approach, thorough evaluation, potential to improve object detection accuracy and efficiency; Cons: missing practical guidance (initialization, algorithm choice, parameter tuning) and theoretical caveats.
