- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with per-iteration truncation to enforce sparsity and a decorrelated score statistic for low-dimensional inference.  
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse-EM estimator to the true parameter up to statistical error and establishes asymptotic normality for the inferential procedure.  
- Achieves high-dimensional statistical rate O(s* log d / n) rather than the low-dimensional sqrt(d / n) rate.  
- Builds on EM convergence work (e.g., Balakrishnan et al. 2017) and connects to sparse optimization/truncated-gradient ideas.  
- Strength: First theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent-variable models, covering both computation and statistics.  
- Strength: Decorrelated score statistic attains the semiparametric information bound, giving efficient inference.  
- Strength: Analysis applies to Gaussian mixtures and mixture-of-regressions, showing broad model applicability.  
- Strength: Clear decomposition separating optimization error and statistical error in convergence analysis.  
- Weakness: Necessity of the sparse-EM truncation is questioned—M-step may be as easy to estimate at dense vectors for many models.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether ordinary EM followed by a single truncation could match guarantees; provided counterexample is underdeveloped.  
- Weakness: The novel infinity-norm control requirement for M-step error lacks justification over standard L2 control.  
- Weakness: Experiments omit initialization details, important given reliance on basin-of-attraction assumptions.  
- Weakness: Paper doesn’t fully explain why truncation is necessary when M-step could incorporate sparsity directly.  
- Quality: Theoretical claims and proofs are rigorous, but reliance on initialization within a specific basin limits practical applicability.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written with careful notation, but experimental and implementation-condition details need strengthening.  
- Originality & Significance: Novel extension of truncation ideas to EM with new high-dimensional inference guarantees; practical advantage over simpler alternatives remains uncertain.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance conditional on clarifying and justifying the necessity of sparse-EM, strengthening the counterexample, and reporting initialization details.
