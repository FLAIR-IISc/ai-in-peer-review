## Review of “High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality”

This paper tackles the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models. The core contribution lies in a modified EM algorithm incorporating a truncation step, coupled with theoretical results guaranteeing convergence to a global solution under specific initialization and conditions, and deriving a convergence rate. An additional, valuable contribution is a statistical test framework for assessing estimator sparsity. The paper is well-organized and provides a sufficient summary of related work, referencing key papers in the field like those by Balakrishnan et al. (2013), Tappen & Bai (2013), and others. 

The strength of this work resides in its attempt to bridge the gap between computation (the locally optimal EM algorithm) and statistics (global optima typically required for guarantees). The proposed truncation step is a sensible approach to induce sparsity and facilitate analysis. However, the paper is incredibly dense and Sections 3 and 4, in particular, are difficult to follow without significant effort. Simplification of the notation and more intuitive explanations of the key steps would greatly improve clarity. For example, the multiple conditions (3.1-3.3) are presented without sufficient motivation, making it challenging to grasp their necessity.

A major concern is the claim of minimax optimality – specifically, the stated convergence rate of  √ (s * log(d/n)). The manuscript lacks a clear proof or citation establishing this optimality; providing this justification is crucial. Furthermore, the practical implementation of the algorithm raises questions. Eq.(3.8) introduces ^s, the estimated sparsity level, which depends on the unknown true sparsity ‘s’. How is ^s chosen in practice? The initialization requirement – obtaining an initial beta^init satisfying ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 – also lacks practical guidance. How can this be achieved in realistic scenarios?

Finally, the role of the truncation step itself in Algorithm 1 isn’t fully clear. What happens if this step is omitted? A brief ablation study or discussion regarding its necessity would be beneficial. 

Post-rebuttal, I’ve increased the score from 6 to 7. While technically solid and a valuable contribution to the statistical literature, the paper’s density and lack of practical guidance likely limit its impact on the broader machine learning research community. Its practical usefulness remains questionable. It feels more geared towards a statistical audience than a general NIPS readership.
