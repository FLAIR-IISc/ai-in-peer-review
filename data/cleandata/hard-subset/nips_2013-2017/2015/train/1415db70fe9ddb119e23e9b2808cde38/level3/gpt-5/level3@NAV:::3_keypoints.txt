- Proposes sparse-EM with per-iteration hard-thresholding and a decorrelated-score inferential procedure for testing coordinate nonzero-ness.  
- Provides high-dimensional theory: with initialization in a smooth nearly-quadratic basin, sparse-EM contracts geometrically to truth up to a statistical floor scaling like sqrt(s*) times the M-step’s ℓ∞ error, giving near-minimax rates.  
- Introduces a Dantzig-based decorrelated score, proves asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency, extending debiasing to latent-variable EM.  
- Framework handles both exact and gradient M-steps and is demonstrated on Gaussian mixtures and mixture-of-regressions.  
- Analysis is technically careful and introduces a new ℓ∞ control condition for the M-operator on sparse inputs.  
- Achieves geometric convergence plus optimal statistical rates and a semiparametric efficiency result.  
- Unclear necessity/advantage of per-iteration truncation versus ordinary EM followed by a single truncation; needs theoretical or experimental comparison.  
- Heavy reliance on initialization within a basin and a resampling-based proof; discuss implications for practical single-sample runs.  
- Paper is well organized but dense; experiments should precisely state initialization, sensitivity to sparsity level bs, and stopping rules.  
- Recommend adding a schematic of assumptions (Concavity-Smoothness, Lipschitz-Gradient variants, Statistical-Error) and how they are verified.  
- Originality is high: novel sparse-EM contraction analysis and decorrelated score for latent-variable EM.  
- Suggest citing related truncated-gradient sparse optimization work (e.g., Langford, Li, Zhang ICML 2009).  
- Significance is high: important high-dimensional convergence and inference guarantees for a widely used algorithmic paradigm.  
- Pros: geometric contraction with near-minimax rates; asymptotically normal, semiparametrically efficient decorrelated score; covers exact and gradient M-steps.  
- Cons: uncertain benefit of iterative truncation; strong dependence on basin initialization with limited practical guidance; experiments and baselines need clarification.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on clarifying the role of truncation and strengthening experimental comparisons.
