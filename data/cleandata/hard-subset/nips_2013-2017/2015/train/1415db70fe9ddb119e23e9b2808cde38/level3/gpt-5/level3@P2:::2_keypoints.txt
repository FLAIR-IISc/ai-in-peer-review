- Paper develops high-dimensional theory for EM with a truncation (hard-thresholding) step, analyzing exact and gradient M-step variants and proving geometric convergence and near-minimax rates O(√(s* log d / n)).
- Proposes decorrelated score and Wald inference for a low-dimensional component with asymptotic normality attaining the semiparametric information bound.
- Builds on Balakrishnan et al. (2017) by introducing truncation and entrywise concentration to extend EM analysis to d >> n.
- Connects Q-function derivatives to likelihood score/Fisher information and uses Dantzig-style regression to estimate the decorrelating direction.
- Compares to tensor methods and ℓ1-penalized likelihood, noting tensors give suboptimal rates and penalized likelihood lacks inference guarantees.
- Strength: clear E/M/T algorithmic template with analysis for both exact and gradient M-steps.
- Strength: sharp decomposition of geometric optimization error and statistical error yielding near-optimal high-dimensional rates.
- Strength: strong inference contribution—decorrelated score achieves semiparametric efficiency.
- Strength: unified framework instantiated for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions with explicit, checkable conditions.
- Strength: good literature coverage and positioning relative to EM and high-dimensional inference work.
- Weakness: Sections 3–4 are dense; suggest roadmap of assumptions, move technical constants to appendix, and add a proof-structure figure.
- Weakness: paper should include an ablation/remark quantifying what fails without the T-step (e.g., O(√(d/n)) rates or basin escape).
- Weakness: analysis uses a resampling variant—need clarification on extending bounds to single-sample-pass EM and extra dependence terms.
- Technical ask: provide a precise minimax lower bound or citation to justify the claimed (near-)minimax rate for the latent models studied.
- Technical ask: practical guidance for choosing bs (since bs ≳ s* but s* unknown)—suggest cross-validation, stability selection, shrink-from-large bs, or Lasso-based seeding.
- Technical ask: provide constructive initializers per model (spectral/tensor moments, k-means, ℓ1-penalized warm-start) and state separation/noise conditions ensuring basin initialization.
- Technical ask: quantify the T-step’s role in support evolution and formally show iterates without truncation fail to maintain sparsity/entrywise bounds.
- Technical ask: give data-driven tuning for the Dantzig selector λ and discuss sensitivity.
- Evaluation — Quality: technically sound with careful optimization/statistical control and compelling inference theory; tighten minimax claim.
- Evaluation — Clarity: overall organized but main theory is hard to parse; fix garbled references and improve exposition.
- Evaluation — Originality: novel combination of EM with iterative truncation delivering estimation and asymptotically exact inference.
- Evaluation — Significance: important bridge between computation and statistics for high-dimensional latent models; practical uptake needs clearer initialization/tuning and experiments.
- Pros for acceptance: strong general theoretical contributions, semiparametric efficiency, multi-model applicability, exact/gradient M-step coverage.
- Cons for acceptance: dense presentation, limited practical guidance and empirical validation, minimax claim needs clearer support.
- Post-rebuttal: authors clarified tuning and initialization and committed to improved exposition; reviewer raised score from 6 to 7.
- Overall score: 7 (accept); Confidence: 0.7.
