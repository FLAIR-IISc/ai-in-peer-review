The paper "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality" proposes a novel high dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation and establishes a general inferential framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment. The authors make two main contributions: (i) a high dimensional EM algorithm that converges at a geometric rate and attains an estimator with the (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence, and (ii) a decorrelated score statistic for testing hypotheses related to low dimensional components of high dimensional parameters.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and their contributions. The methodology is well-explained, and the use of notation is consistent throughout the paper. The authors also provide a detailed comparison with related work, highlighting the differences and advantages of their approach.

The technical conditions proposed by the authors are reasonable and well-motivated. The conditions on the population version lower bound function Q(·; ·) and the statistical error between the sample version and population version M-steps are necessary to establish the convergence guarantees. The assumption on the sparsity of the true parameter is also reasonable, as it is a common assumption in high-dimensional statistics.

The main results of the paper are impressive. The authors establish a geometric convergence rate for the high dimensional EM algorithm and show that the estimator attains a (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence. The decorrelated score statistic is also shown to be asymptotically normal, and its limiting variance is optimal in the sense that it attains the semiparametric information bound.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the authors provide a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment, which is a significant contribution to the field. Secondly, the paper establishes a general inferential framework for a broad family of statistical models with latent structures, which is a major advancement over existing approaches. Finally, the authors provide a detailed analysis of the high dimensional EM algorithm and its applications to Gaussian mixture models and mixture of regression models.

However, there are also some weaknesses in the paper. Firstly, the authors assume that the true parameter is sparse, which may not always be the case in practice. Secondly, the paper does not provide a clear explanation of how to choose the tuning parameters, such as the sparsity parameter and the stepsize in the gradient ascent implementation of the M-step. Finally, the paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the high dimensional EM algorithm, which is an important consideration in practice.

In conclusion, the paper "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality" is a significant contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistics.
