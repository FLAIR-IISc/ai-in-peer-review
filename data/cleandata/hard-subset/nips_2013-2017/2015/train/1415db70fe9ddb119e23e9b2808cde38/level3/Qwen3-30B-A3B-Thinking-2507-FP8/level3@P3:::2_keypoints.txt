- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and provides theoretical guarantees for estimation and inference when d >> n.  
- Main theoretical contributions: convergence to a global solution up to statistical error, rate O(sqrt(s*log(d/n))), and a framework for statistical testing of sparsity.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM theory to high-dimensional latent variable models and connects to high-dimensional inference literature.  
- Strength: well-organized paper with thorough related work and technically sound theoretical results applicable to multiple latent variable models.  
- Strength: unified framework demonstrating generality via examples (Gaussian mixture, mixture of regression).  
- Weakness: very dense technical sections (3–4) with heavy notation that impede accessibility for non-experts.  
- Weakness: minimax-optimality claim (rate sqrt(s*log(d/n))) lacks a self-contained proof in the main text; appendix proof is not easy to verify.  
- Practical concern: sparsity parameter b_s in Eq.(3.8) depends on unknown s*, so no guidance on selecting b_s in practice.  
- Practical concern: initialization requirement ||β^init − β*||_2 < R/2 lacks practical guidance on how to obtain such an initialization.  
- Practical concern: role and necessity of the truncation step are insufficiently explained and not empirically justified.  
- Significance: important theoretical advance for high-dimensional latent variable models, but limited practical utility due to missing implementation details, scalability discussion, and empirical validation.  
- Verdict: solid statistical contribution (score raised from 6 to 7) better suited to a statistics venue than an ML conference; needs clearer presentation and practical guidance for ML audience.
