## Review: High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality

This paper tackles the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models, a setting where existing results break down. The authors propose a high-dimensional EM algorithm incorporating a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and develop a framework for both parameter estimation and hypothesis testing. The core contribution lies in establishing statistical and computational guarantees – geometric convergence and near-optimal rates – for this modified EM algorithm, alongside a decorrelated score test with asymptotic normality.

The work builds on the recent progress in understanding the EM algorithm's convergence properties, particularly [2], by adapting their approach for high-dimensional settings. However, simply applying existing techniques isn’t sufficient, as the truncation step introduces new technical challenges. The authors explicitly recognize the gap between low-dimensional EM theory and the high-dimensional reality, and propose a pragmatic solution. The connection to existing work like [15, 16, 17, 24] on high-dimensional inference is also well noted.

**Strengths:**

* **Important Problem:** Addressing the lack of theoretical foundations for EM in high dimensions is significant, as the algorithm is widely used.
* **Novel Approach:** The truncation step is a reasonable and effective way to incorporate sparsity and enable analysis.
* **Strong Theoretical Results:** The established convergence rate and asymptotic normality results are valuable contributions.
* **Generality:** The framework is presented as applicable to a broad family of latent variable models.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is largely well-written and the algorithmic steps are clearly described.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Initialization Conditions:** The requirement for “appropriate initialization” (line 60, p.2) needs further clarification. Practical guidance on obtaining such an initializer, beyond stating its relative error bound, is missing. How easily can this initialization be obtained in practice for the considered models?
* **Algorithm Selection:** The paper doesn’t provide clear guidance on *when* to choose the exact maximization vs. gradient ascent implementation of the M-step. What are the trade-offs and recommendations based on problem characteristics (e.g., dimensionality, smoothness)?
* **Parameter Tuning:** The choices of ĥs and λ are critical. The paper acknowledges sensitivity but provides limited practical advice for their selection. Data-dependent methods would be highly beneficial.
* **Ning & Liu Citation:** The authors should explicitly cite the decorrelated score test’s equivalence to the method in Ning & Liu [*] and detail any modifications made for this specific context. Was any development required beyond direct application?
* **Assumption Validity:** A more thorough discussion of the numerous technical assumptions is needed. Do they realistically hold for the latent variable models (Gaussian mixture, mixture of regression) considered, or are there scenarios where they might be violated?
* **Novelty Clarification:** While the truncation step is novel, the paper needs to more clearly delineate *why* applying existing tools (like those from [2]) is nontrivial.  A deeper discussion on where previous approaches fail specifically because of the high-dimensional setting is needed.
* **Proof Detail:** The paper relies heavily on a long supplement. Key technical arguments and proofs should be at least sketched in the main paper to improve accessibility and allow for better assessment.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**  The paper addresses a crucial gap in the literature, presents a theoretically sound and potentially practical algorithm, and demonstrates significant technical achievement.

**Arguments for Rejection:** The lack of practical guidance on initialization, parameter tuning, and algorithm selection, alongside the heavy reliance on supplementary material, could limit the paper’s immediate impact. The authors need to address the points raised regarding assumption validity and novelty with more detail.



Overall, this is a strong paper with the potential to be a valuable contribution. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above would significantly strengthen the manuscript and increase its impact. I recommend acceptance with major revisions.
