- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity and a general theory for high-dimensional latent variable inference.  
- Two main contributions: a computationally feasible EM with geometric convergence and near-optimal estimation rate, and a new inferential procedure for low-dimensional components.  

Strengths:
- Provides a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty quantification in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- The proposed algorithm is computationally feasible and achieves (near-)optimal statistical convergence rates.  
- Establishes a first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions for a broad family of models.  

Weaknesses / areas for improvement:
- Lacks clear guidance on how to obtain the required initial estimator β^{init}.  
- Does not state when to use each of the two proposed algorithms or compare their pros and cons.  
- No detailed guidance on choosing ĥs and λ, sensitivity analysis, or data-dependent tuning methods.  
- Fails to explicitly cite or acknowledge the relationship between the decorrelated score test and Ning & Liu, and whether new development was needed.  
- Requires many technical assumptions for the main theorems without discussing whether they hold for the considered latent variable models.  
- Needs a clearer explanation of novel contributions relative to existing work (e.g., why applying previous tools here is nontrivial and what new insights are provided).  
- Key material is relegated to a long supplement, making it hard for readers to follow the main ideas and proofs.  

Overall assessment:
- Makes a significant contribution to high-dimensional statistics but needs more practical guidance and clearer exposition.  
- Rating: 8/10; Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  

Minor revisions requested:
- Provide guidance on obtaining β^{init}.  
- Compare the two algorithms and their advantages/disadvantages.  
- Discuss selection and sensitivity of ĥs and λ and offer data-driven choices.  
- Acknowledge and clarify relation to Ning & Liu’s decorrelated score test.  
- Discuss validity of technical assumptions for the models considered.  
- Clarify novel contributions relative to prior work.  
- Expand the main paper to include more details on technical challenges and proofs.
