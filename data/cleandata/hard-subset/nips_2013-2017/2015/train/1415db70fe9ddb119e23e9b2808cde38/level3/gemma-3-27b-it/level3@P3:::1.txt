This paper addresses the challenging problem of applying the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm to high-dimensional latent variable models, a setting where theoretical guarantees are largely absent. The authors propose a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, and a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing of low-dimensional components. This work builds on recent advancements in EM analysis (e.g., [2, 25, 30]) and seeks to extend them to the high-dimensional regime.

**Summary of Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** The lack of theoretical guarantees for EM in high dimensions is a significant gap, and this paper makes a valuable contribution towards filling it.
* **Novel Algorithmic Approach:** The truncation step within the EM algorithm is a sensible approach to induce sparsity and improve performance in high dimensions.
* **Asymptotic Normality Result:** Establishing asymptotic normality for the estimator, and consequently enabling hypothesis testing, is a key achievement.
* **Broad Applicability:** The framework is presented generally and illustrated with Gaussian Mixture Models and Mixture of Regression Models.
* **Connections to Existing Work:** The paper clearly positions itself relative to existing literature, including [2] and methods for sparse estimation like the Lasso.

**Summary of Weaknesses:**

* **Practical Initialization:** The requirement for an initial estimator β<sup>init</sup> satisfying ||β<sup>init</sup> - ⇤||/||⇤||<sub>2</sub> < ε (line 60, p.2) is stated but lacks practical guidance. How does one obtain such an initial estimator in practice?  A simple strategy like randomly initializing with a small norm, or using a sparse regression approach to get a preliminary estimate, should be discussed.
* **Algorithm Selection Guidance:** The paper presents two M-step implementations (exact vs. gradient ascent) but lacks concrete guidance on when to choose one over the other. Factors like computational cost, model complexity, and desired accuracy should be considered and discussed.
* **Parameter Tuning:** Choosing ĥs and λ (implicitly present through the sparsity parameter bs and the tuning parameter in the Dantzig selector) is crucial. The paper doesn't offer readily applicable rules of thumb, and assessing sensitivity to these parameters is limited. Data-dependent selection methods, like cross-validation or information criteria, would be beneficial.
* **Ning & Liu Citation:** The decorrelated score test matches the method in Ning & Liu [*], which should be explicitly cited within the main body, not just in the abstract. It’s important to clarify whether any new developments were needed to adapt their method to this latent variable setting.
* **Assumption Verification:** The numerous technical assumptions required for the main theorems are a potential barrier to application. The paper provides verification for specific models in the appendix, but a more thorough discussion of the conditions' plausibility for a wider range of latent variable models is needed.
* **Limited Technical Detail:** The paper relies heavily on a lengthy supplement for key technical details and proofs. While supplements are acceptable, more substantial detail within the main manuscript would improve clarity and accessibility. Key steps in the proof of Theorem 3.4, for example, would benefit from elaboration.
* **Originality Nuance:** While the combination of EM with sparsity and decorrelated inference is novel, the paper needs to more strongly articulate *why* applying existing tools (like those from Ning & Liu) is non-trivial in the context of latent variable models. The challenges imposed by the latent structure and the non-convexity of the likelihood should be emphasized.



**Detailed Comments and Questions:**

* **Initialization (p.2, line 60):** What specific strategies can practitioners use to obtain an initial estimate β<sup>init</sup> satisfying the stated condition?  Is a simple L2-norm constraint sufficient?
* **M-Step Choice:** Under what conditions is the exact maximization (Algorithm 2) computationally feasible? What are the trade-offs between exact maximization and the gradient ascent approach (Algorithm 3) in terms of accuracy and computational cost?
* **Parameter Selection:** The sparsity parameter *bs* appears crucial. How is it chosen in practice?  Is there a relationship between *bs* and the true sparsity level *s*<sup>*</sup>?  Similarly, the tuning parameter in the Dantzig selector (2.6) needs practical guidance.
* **Ning & Liu Comparison:** Explicitly state the correspondence to the work of Ning & Liu [*] and clearly articulate how the latent variable structure introduces additional complexities that necessitate the presented framework.
* **Assumptions:** The assumptions, particularly 3.1-3.3, seem strong. Can the paper provide examples of latent variable models where these assumptions are violated and discuss the potential consequences?
* **Proofs:** While a supplement is acceptable, core ideas behind the proofs of Theorems 3.4 and 4.6 should be sketched within the main paper. This enhances understanding and credibility.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a significant open problem.
* Presents a novel and theoretically sound algorithm.
* Establishes important asymptotic guarantees.
* Well-written and clearly positioned within the relevant literature.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Lack of practical guidance on initialization and parameter tuning.
* Limited discussion of assumption validity.
* Heavy reliance on supplementary material.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant theoretical contribution. However, addressing the weaknesses regarding practical implementation details and improving the clarity of the technical arguments would substantially enhance its impact. I lean towards **Accept**, contingent on the authors addressing the raised concerns, especially those related to practical initialization, parameter tuning, and a more thorough discussion of the assumptions. The explicit connection to Ning & Liu should also be clarified within the main text.
