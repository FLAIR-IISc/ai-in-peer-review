- Paper develops a unified theory for high-dimensional latent-variable models fit by EM, proposing a truncated EM (E/M/T) with sparsity and an asymptotically normal decorrelated-score inference procedure.  
- Truncated EM guarantees geometric optimization error decay and near-optimal statistical error O(√(s*)√(log d / n)) under suitable initialization and assumptions.  
- Inference uses a decorrelated score from Qn(·;·) shown to be asymptotically normal and attain the semiparametric information bound.  
- Analysis handles both exact M-steps and gradient-ascent M-steps and covers models like GMM and mixture of regressions.  
- Strength: fills an important gap by providing computation-statistics guarantees for EM in p ≫ n settings with latent structure, including inference.  
- Strength: clean separation of optimization vs statistical error with geometric convergence and near-minimax rates under explicit conditions.  
- Strength: inference is semiparametrically efficient and computationally feasible.  
- Strength: framework treats both exact and gradient M-steps and illustrates multiple models.  
- Major weakness: heavy technical assumptions (basin, strong concavity/smoothness, Lipschitz-gradient tied to the M-map, sparsity of w*, resampling) need clarification on necessity and plausibility.  
- Request: provide interpretable, model-specific conditions (SNR/separation, design, sub-Gaussianity) and explicit constants/scalings for GMM and mixture regressions.  
- Concern: sparsity assumption on the nuisance orthogonalization vector w* is nontrivial; discuss when it holds and how to diagnose it.  
- Concern: analysis uses resampling/split-sample EM — clarify whether single-sample iterates satisfy the same bounds or can be handled via martingale/leave-one-out techniques.  
- Major issue: initialization assumed within a constant ℓ2 radius but practical constructions are deferred; need concrete initialization recipes.  
- Clarity issue: many key arguments/conditions are deferred to the supplement; include concise proof sketches and an assumptions/sample-size summary table in the main text.  
- Practical guidance missing: hyperparameter choices (bs, Dantzig λ), gradient EM step size, and when to prefer exact vs gradient M-step.  
- Originality: truncated EM and support-evolution analysis appear novel and technically substantial.  
- Originality caveat: decorrelated score matches Ning & Liu’s orthogonal score approach — explicitly cite and map the connection, and delineate new contributions.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for high-dimensional latent-variable theory and practice, but practical adoption needs clearer initialization/tuning guidance and more experiments.  
- Specific request: provide practical initialization recipes per model (e.g., spectral/k-means++ for GMM, tensor or lasso-based pilots for mixture regressions) with required SNR/design conditions.  
- Specific request: give guidance on exact vs gradient M-step usage and recommend step-size choices (e.g., η = 2/(ν+μ), backtracking, Barzilai–Borwein).  
- Specific request: advise how to choose bs and λ in practice (overspecify bs ≈ 1.5–3·ŝ, λ ≍ σ̂√(log d/n), use multiplier bootstrap/CV) and report sensitivity studies.  
- Specific request: explicitly state assumptions and sample-size scaling for GMM and mixture regressions in the main text.  
- Specific request: add a paragraph clarifying novelty vs Ning & Liu and the new elements needed for latent-variable/EM settings (Theorem 2.1, concentration/Lipschitz for Tn, non-global optima).  
- Experiments: expand comparisons to ℓ1-penalized EM, gradient-EM with ℓ1 penalty, tensor methods, and evaluate calibration (size/power), robustness to bs/λ and initialization.  
- Pros for acceptance: advances EM theory in high-dimensions with estimation and inference, near-optimal rates, semiparametric efficiency, and a generally applicable two-variant M-step framework.  
- Cons for acceptance: heavy technical/opaque assumptions, insufficient practical initialization/tuning guidance, reliance on resampling in analysis, and insufficiently explicit relation to Ning & Liu.  
- Questions for authors: can proofs adapt to single-sample iterates without resampling, is sparsity of w* realistic and diagnosable, can bs be adapted online with guarantees, and how does performance degrade under low SNR where local strong concavity fails?  
- Minor comments: fix malformed references and explicitly add Ning & Liu (2014/2017), Zhang & Zhang (2014), van de Geer et al. (2014); move Theorem 2.1’s proof sketch and implications into the main text.
