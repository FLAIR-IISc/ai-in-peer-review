- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity for latent variable models.  
- Shows algorithm converges at a geometric rate to a global solution with a near-optimal statistical rate.  
- Develops a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional components.  
- Paper is well-organized with a clear related-work summary; technical conditions and proofs are thorough.  
- Provides a general framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment—a significant contribution to high-dimensional statistics.  
- Paper is dense and may be hard for non-experts, particularly Sections 3–4; notation should be simplified.  
- Authors should provide more intuitive explanations of technical conditions and assumptions.  
- Claim of achieving minimax-optimal convergence rate is not fully justified and requires a clear proof.  
- Practicality concern: the algorithm needs a tuning parameter that depends on unknown sparsity level.  
- Initialization is not fully explained; authors must detail how to obtain an initial estimate meeting required conditions.  
- The role of the truncation step is unclear; authors should explain consequences of omitting it.  
- Reviewer views the work as a solid statistical contribution but questions its practicality for practitioners.  
- Overall rating: 7/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contributions, geometric convergence with near-optimal rate, and unified estimation/uncertainty framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dense presentation, questionable practicality, unclear initialization, and unclear truncation role.
