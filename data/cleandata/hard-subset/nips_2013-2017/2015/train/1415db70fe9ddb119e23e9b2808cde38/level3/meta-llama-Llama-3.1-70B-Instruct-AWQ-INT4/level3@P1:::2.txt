**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm for inferring high-dimensional latent variable models. The algorithm incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation and establishes a new inferential procedure for testing hypotheses related to low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters. The authors provide a general theory of the EM algorithm, including simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the proposed high-dimensional EM algorithm and the respective uncertainty assessment procedure.

**Strengths of the Paper**

* The paper is well-organized, and the related work is sufficiently summarized.
* The authors propose a modified EM algorithm tailored for sparse, high-dimensional parameters with conditions guaranteeing convergence to the global solution and derived convergence rate.
* The paper constructs a statistical test framework to assess estimator sparsity.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

* The contents are very dense, and Sections 3 and 4 are hard to follow and need simplification for readers.
* The claim that sqrt(s * log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal is not clearly proven in the main manuscript.
* The practicality of the algorithm is questionable, as Eq.(3.8) uses ^s that depends on unknown s, and it is unclear how to choose ^s in practice.
* The initialization concern is also raised, as it is unclear how to obtain an initial beta^init satisfying ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 in practice.
* The role of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not clearly explained, and it is unclear what happens if truncation is omitted.
