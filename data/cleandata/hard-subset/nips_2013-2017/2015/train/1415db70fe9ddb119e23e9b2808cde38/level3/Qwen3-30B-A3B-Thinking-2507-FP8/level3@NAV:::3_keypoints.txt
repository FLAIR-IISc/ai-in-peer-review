- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm with a truncation step and provides convergence guarantees plus a decorrelated score for inference in high-dimensional latent-variable models.  
- Extends prior EM convergence results to high-dimensional regimes where d > n, filling a literature gap.  
- Establishes geometric convergence with a near-optimal statistical rate O(√(s* log d / n)).  
- Shows the decorrelated score statistic attains asymptotic normality with optimal semiparametric variance for inference.  
- Framework applies to models like Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions, offering a computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and inference.  
- Key concern: necessity of iterative truncation is unclear—ordinary EM followed by a single truncation may suffice for many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures).  
- Authors’ later counterexample (linear regression) does not fully settle when iterative truncation is essential.  
- Technical assumptions (smoothness and near-quadratic Q-function in a basin of attraction) are restrictive and may limit practical applicability.  
- Infinity-norm control on M-step error for sparse vectors appears to be a technical artifact rather than a fundamental requirement.  
- Experiments omit initialization details, harming reproducibility.  
- Missing citations to related sparse optimization work (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 on truncated gradient).  
- Overall assessment: technically strong and novel (Quality: Strong, Originality: Strong) but clarity and demonstrated necessity are moderate, reducing perceived significance (Clarity: Moderate, Significance: Moderate).
