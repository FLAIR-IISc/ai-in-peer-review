- Main contributions: propose a high-dimensional EM with iterative truncation achieving geometric convergence and near-optimal estimation, and a decorrelated score statistic for asymptotic inference attaining the semiparametric information bound.  
- Scope: results established for broad latent variable models including Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions in d >> n regime.  
- Quality: rigorous theoretical guarantees for optimization and statistical errors with detailed proofs (Theorems 3.4 and 4.6).  
- Originality: novel truncation step in EM and extension of decorrelated score inference to latent variable models.  
- Significance: first computationally feasible approach providing optimal estimation and asymptotic inference for high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Clarity: well-written, consistent notation, and comprehensive appendix proofs.  
- Weakness: necessity of iterative truncation is not convincingly established; simpler "EM + single truncation" might suffice in many cases.  
- Weakness: experimental section lacks crucial details on initialization required for theoretical guarantees.  
- Weakness: missing citation of related sparse optimization work (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 on truncated gradient methods).  
- Counterexample concern: authors’ claim that linear regression necessitates iterative truncation is not fully convincing and needs clearer intuition.  
- For acceptance: strong theoretical contribution, technical soundness, and advancement of high-dimensional EM understanding.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient comparison to simpler alternatives and incomplete experimental/practical details.  
- Suggestion 1: compare theoretically and empirically with "EM followed by single truncation" and characterize when iterative truncation is necessary.  
- Suggestion 2: report and justify initialization method used in experiments.  
- Suggestion 3: cite Langford et al. (2009) and related sparse optimization literature.  
- Suggestion 4: clarify and strengthen the linear regression counterexample and its intuition.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the above concerns.
