- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates a truncation step to enforce sparsity in parameter estimation.  
- Algorithm is shown to converge at a geometric rate to a local maximum with theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Achieves a near-optimal statistical rate of convergence, improving over existing methods for high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for testing low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Decorrelated score is asymptotically normal with a provable limiting variance and attains the semiparametric efficient information bound.  
- Paper analyses relationships to classical EM and tensor methods, arguing superior effectiveness for high-dimensional latent models.  
- Strength: novel, effective algorithm with strong high-dimensional convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical analysis of both estimation and inference procedures.  
- Limitation: requires a good initialization but provides no clear method for choosing it.  
- Limitation: unclear how well the algorithm generalizes to settings beyond high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Limitation: lacks comprehensive experimental evaluation, so practical performance is uncertain.  
- Limitation/question: unclear whether the sparse-EM variant is necessary or if ordinary EM plus a single truncation could achieve similar guarantees.  
- Overall: important theoretical contributions, but the specific necessity and practical advantage of the sparse EM variant remain uncertain; further research needed.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
