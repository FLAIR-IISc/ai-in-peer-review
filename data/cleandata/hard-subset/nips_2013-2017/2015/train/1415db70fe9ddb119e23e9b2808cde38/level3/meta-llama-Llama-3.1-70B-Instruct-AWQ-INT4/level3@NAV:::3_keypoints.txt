- Proposes a novel high-dimensional sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector each step to enforce sparsity.
- Develops a general theory for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models with simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees.
- Introduces an asymptotically normal, decorrelated score statistic for testing individual parameter entries.
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse-EM estimator up to a statistical error scaling with sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity-norm M-step error.
- Shows the decorrelated score achieves semiparametric information lower bound for low-dimensional components amid high-dimensional nuisances.
- Strength: provides high-dimensional convergence guarantees, a significant theoretical contribution.
- Strength: offers a thorough, clear analysis of the algorithm and its theoretical properties.
- Weakness: assumes the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin of attraction, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: requires initialization within that basin, which is challenging in high-dimensional settings.
- Weakness: introduces a new technical condition—infinity-norm control on M-operator estimation error—needed only for sparse vectors.
- Question raised: whether ordinary EM followed by a single truncation could achieve similar guarantees; authors counter with a linear regression example but further study is needed.
- Originality/significance: novel approach with important implications for high-dimensional latent-variable inference.
- Suggestion: provide more discussion on practical implications and limitations of the algorithm.
- Suggestion: investigate the ordinary-EM-then-truncate alternative more deeply.
- Suggestion: expand experimental details, especially initialization and full simulation results.
