- Tackles theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Proposes a sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector at each iteration to enforce sparsity.  
- Introduces a new technical condition controlling the infinity-norm error of the M-operator for sparse vectors.  
- Proves geometric convergence up to a statistical error term scaling with sqrt(sparsity) and the infinity-norm M-step error.  
- Develops an asymptotically normal score statistic for hypothesis testing of model parameters in high dimensions.  
- Truncation-at-each-step is a clever idea that enables the analysis and is a notable advance.  
- Unclear whether iterative truncation is necessary versus running standard EM followed by a single truncation; provided counterexample (linear regression) is helpful but limited.  
- Related work on sparse optimization (e.g., truncated gradient) is only briefly discussed and could be expanded.  
- Paper is well-organized and rigorous but dense; theoretical sections and numerous conditions/notations can be overwhelming.  
- Experimental details, especially the initialization procedure, are insufficiently described.  
- Overall a significant technical contribution advancing the state-of-the-art, though practical advantages of the sparse EM variant need stronger justification.
