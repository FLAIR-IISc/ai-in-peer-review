- Proposes a high-dimensional truncated EM that enforces sparsity after E- and M-steps and proves geometric convergence plus final error ~ sqrt(s* log d / n) (up to logs) under regularity and suitable initialization.
- Introduces a decorrelated score and Wald inferential framework for low-dimensional components achieving asymptotic normality with semiparametric efficiency variance.
- Theory covers both exact maximization and gradient-ascent M-step implementations and is illustrated for Gaussian mixture models and mixtures of regressions.
- Main novelty: computationally feasible EM with explicit sparsity control and high-dimensional rates, extending EM analyses beyond low-dimensional/local convergence results.
- Extends high-dimensional semiparametric inference to latent-variable models with non-convex likelihoods and non-global estimators, contrasting with convex GLM approaches.
- Argues advantages over tensor methods in statistical rates and applicability.
- Technical strength: tailored truncated EM with clean decomposition of optimization vs. statistical error and near-minimax rates.
- Inference strength: score test that attains semiparametric information bound in presence of high-dimensional nuisance parameters.
- Presentation strength: paper is well structured and accurately positioned relative to prior work.
- Weakness: Sections 3–4 are dense and layered technical conditions obscure main ideas.
- Suggestion: add a running intuitive example (e.g., two-component spherical GMM) alongside conditions to clarify verification.
- Suggestion: provide an “at-a-glance” table summarizing constants and scalings to aid readability.
- Suggestion: move technical lemmas/resampling proofs to appendix and focus main text on high-level proof flow.
- Suggestion: include a short “how to use” recipe covering choice of bs, initialization, step-size, stopping, and inference steps for practitioners.
- Practical guidance lacking for tuning parameter selection, initialization, and diagnostics for condition validity.
- Minimax optimality claim unclear: no self-contained lower bound provided for the paper’s latent-variable settings; need a theorem or citation and resolve log(d) vs. log(d/n) notation.
- Choice of truncation sparsity bs is impractical since s* is unknown; request practical selection strategies and sensitivity analysis.
- Initialization requirement (||βinit–β*||2 < R/2) needs practical, verified methods (e.g., spectral/MoM, k-means) and quantified SNR/separation/sample-size conditions plus empirical sensitivity plots.
- Role of truncation unclear: request theoretical/empirical ablation showing necessity (without truncation error may scale as sqrt(d/n) and break high-dimensional guarantees).
- Main theorem proven for resampling version; need clarification whether analogous guarantees hold without sample splitting and under what conditions.
- Inference computational cost concern: Dantzig-style step may be heavy; discuss alternatives (nodewise Lasso, CLIME, ridge) and effects on theory/runtime.
- Technical development is strong but assumptions (sparsity of w*, separation/SNR, basin) should be made more transparent.
- Core theory sections are hard to follow; readability would benefit from the suggested clarifications and practitioner-oriented materials.
- Originality: high — likely first general high-dimensional EM with near-optimal rates and asymptotically efficient inference for latent-variable models.
- Significance: high for theory/statistics community; practical impact depends on added guidance and lighter-weight inference options.
- Pros: novel truncated EM with rigorous guarantees, asymptotically efficient inference, solid literature comparisons.
- Cons: dense presentation, need nontrivial initialization and sparsity knowledge, potentially heavy inference step, unclear minimax claim.
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer raised score from 6 to 7 — recommends acceptance but urges addressing clarity, practicality, tuning, initialization, and truncation experiments for camera-ready.
