- Proposes a general theoretical framework for estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models, with applications to Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions.  
- Introduces a high-dimensional EM algorithm that adds a hard-truncation step to enforce sparsity and proves geometric convergence to near-minimax error sqrt(s log d / n) given suitable initialization.  
- Constructs a decorrelated score statistic for testing individual parameters, proves its asymptotic normality and attainment of the semiparametric efficiency bound.  
- Significant and timely problem: advances theoretical understanding of EM in the d >> n regime and connects computation with statistical guarantees.  
- Methodological originality and technical depth: novel truncation-EM approach, nontrivial analysis for a nonconvex iterative estimator, and combined computational/statistical guarantees.  
- Broad applicability: framework based on high-level Q-function conditions suggests potential use beyond the provided examples.  
- Major practical gap: assumes a strong initializer within a basin of attraction but gives no practical method (e.g., spectral) to obtain it.  
- Lacks guidance on tuning: no procedures or sensitivity analysis for choosing sparsity level bs or Dantzig selector tuning λ (e.g., cross-validation).  
- Algorithmic choices unclear: no discussion of trade-offs or recommendations between exact M-step and gradient-ascent variants.  
- Positioning versus prior work weak: decorrelated score test closely resembles debiasing/desparsifying methods and needs explicit citations and clearer articulation of novelty in adapting to nonconvex iterative estimators.  
- Reliance on abstract technical conditions: key assumptions (Conditions 3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.4) are verified in the appendix but deserve more intuition and main-text summary.  
- Paper organization hampers evaluation: critical technical verifications are outsourced to the supplement, reducing self-containment and readability.  
- For acceptance: strong, novel, and rigorous contribution to an important problem with significant theoretical advances.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient practical guidance, unclear novelty in the inference component, and heavy reliance on the appendix for core arguments.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to improve practical guidance, clarity on novelty/positioning, and relocation/summary of key technical verifications into the main text.
