Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes a high-dimensional EM framework that augments the E- and M-steps with a truncation step to enforce sparsity, provides two M-step implementations (exact maximization vs. gradient ascent), establishes geometric optimization error decay plus near-optimal statistical error O(s*^1/2 log d / n)^1/2, and develops a decorrelated score-based test for low-dimensional components that achieves semiparametric efficiency. The theory is illustrated for Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions. This work connects to Balakrishnan et al. on EM’s sample/population analysis (low-dimensional), to debiasing/desparsification for high-dimensional inference (e.g., van de Geer et al.; Zhang & Zhang), and to tensor methods for latent variables (Anandkumar et al.), while aiming to cover nonconvex latent-variable likelihoods where convex global optima are unavailable.

Strengths: Clear conceptual contribution of a truncation-equipped EM with entrywise error control; simultaneous computational and inferential guarantees; two concrete latent-variable examples; asymptotic normality with efficient variance.

Weaknesses and requests for revision:
- Initialization: Please clarify precise conditions and practical procedures for constructing β^{init} that meet the basin requirement (line 60, p.2). E.g., for GMM: spectral/k-means-based centers with thresholding under a stated separation/SNR; for mixture of regressions: method-of-moments or spectral experts warm-start followed by a few penalized EM or proximal gradient steps. State explicit SNR, separation, and sparsity conditions under which these inits land in B with high probability.
- Algorithm choice: Provide guidance for when to use exact vs. gradient M-steps (closed-form M-step available, conditioning, per-iteration cost, memory constraints). For the gradient version, give step-size rules (theory-backed fixed step, backtracking) and practical defaults.
- Tuning: Explain how to choose s_hat and λ (for the Dantzig step) in practice, assess sensitivity, and offer data-dependent rules: e.g., s_hat via EBIC/stability selection/path-based selection with holdout; λ ≈ c·σ̂·sqrt(log d / n) with variance estimated from residuals, or via cross-validation. Discuss robustness of rates and tests to moderate mis-specification of s_hat and λ.
- Decorrelated score: Explicitly cite that your test coincides with Ning & Liu’s decorrelated/orthogonal score framework and state whether any novel development was required to adapt it to latent-variable EM (e.g., using Qn and Tn to access score/Hessian via Theorem 2.1, handling nonconvexity and sample splitting).
- Assumptions: Many technical conditions (Conditions 3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.4; Assumption 4.5) are strong. Please discuss their plausibility for the considered models (e.g., sparsity of w*, SNR/separation, sub-Gaussianity, eigenvalue bounds), and provide empirical checks when they are mildly violated.
- Exposition: Move key proof sketches and technical challenges (support evolution under truncation, entrywise concentration) from the supplement into the main text, and include a practical algorithm section with defaults.

Qualitative evaluation:
- Quality: The analysis appears sound but depends on stringent assumptions that need clearer verification and practical guidance.
- Clarity: Generally well organized; add the requested guidance and proof sketches to improve reproducibility.
- Originality: Novel synthesis of EM with truncation and inference for latent variable models; inference component closely follows Ning & Liu—clarify incremental novelty.
- Significance: Potentially impactful for high-dimensional latent-variable estimation and inference if practical guidance is strengthened.

Pros: solid theory; near-optimal rates; efficient inference; relevant models. Cons: practical initialization/tuning unclear; reliance on strong assumptions; novelty of inference relative to Ning & Liu not fully delineated; key details deferred to supplement.
