- Presents a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with truncation to enforce sparsity and provides theoretical guarantees for parameter estimation and hypothesis testing in latent variable models.
- Proposes a decorrelated score test for inference on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.
- Addresses a significant gap by analyzing EM convergence and asymptotic normality in the challenging d > n regime under sparsity assumptions.
- Builds on low-dimensional EM convergence literature and contrasts with high-dimensional methods like Lasso and tensor approaches.
- Theoretical rigor: comprehensive analysis establishing convergence rates and asymptotic normality under reasonable assumptions.
- Novelty: first to provide guarantees for EM in high dimensions and introduces a truncation step to attain optimal rates.
- Generality: framework claimed to apply to a broad family of latent variable models, suggesting wide applicability.
- Clear organization and related-work positioning, making the contribution's context evident.
- Presentation is very dense and technical (Sections 3–4), hindering accessibility and intuitive understanding.
- Claim of minimax-optimal rate (sqrt(s * log(d)/n)) lacks an explicit minimax lower bound or clear proof in the manuscript.
- Practical implementation concern: algorithm requires estimating sparsity level s (or using ^s) but gives no guidance on choosing it.
- Initialization sensitivity: requires a close initial estimate (||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2) with no practical strategy provided for obtaining it.
- Role of truncation unclear: no ablation or discussion on the impact of removing the truncation step and its necessity for convergence.
- Specific requests: provide minimax optimality justification, methods to estimate s in practice, practical initialization strategies, and analysis of removing truncation.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, novelty, and potential high impact on high-dimensional inference.
- Arguments against acceptance: dense mathematics limits accessibility, practical gaps in implementation (sparsity, initialization), and unclear minimax/truncation claims.
- Overall assessment: solid and novel theoretical contribution but needs improved accessibility and practical guidance.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify presentation and address minimax, sparsity estimation, initialization, and truncation issues.
- Score: 7/10 (Strong accept, raised from initial 6 after weighing theoretical strengths).
