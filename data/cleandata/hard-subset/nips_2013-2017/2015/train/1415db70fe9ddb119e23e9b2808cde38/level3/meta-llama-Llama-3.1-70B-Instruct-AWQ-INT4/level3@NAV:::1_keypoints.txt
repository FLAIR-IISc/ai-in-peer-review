- Summary: Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity for parameter estimation and provides theory for simultaneous estimation and uncertainty assessment, demonstrated on Gaussian mixtures and mixture of regressions.  
- Strength: Introduces a new sparsity-aware high-dimensional EM algorithm.  
- Strength: Provides simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the algorithm.  
- Strength: Algorithm applies to a broad family of latent-variable statistical models.  
- Strength: Establishes asymptotic normality for a decorrelated score statistic enabling inference.  
- Weakness: Relies on technical assumptions (Conditions 3.1–3.3) that may not always hold.  
- Weakness: Lacks practical guidance for selecting tuning parameters (e.g., bs and λ).  
- Weakness: Would benefit from more thorough sensitivity analysis to tuning and initialization.  
- Weakness: Comparison to other high-dimensional EM methods is limited and could be expanded.  
- Originality: Novel algorithm and theoretical guarantees represent a significant original contribution.  
- Significance: Addresses an important problem in high-dimensional latent-variable inference with broad application potential.  
- Recommendation: Add guidance on tuning-parameter selection.  
- Recommendation: Include more sensitivity analyses for robustness to tuning and initialization.  
- Recommendation: Provide more detailed comparisons to existing high-dimensional EM algorithms.  
- Recommendation: Consider extending the approach to other models (e.g., GLMs or nonlinear models).  
- Rating: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10; recommended for publication with revisions.
