- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with sparsity-inducing truncation that achieves geometric convergence to a near-optimal estimator.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score test for asymptotic inference of low-dimensional components, achieving semiparametric efficiency.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees (Theorems 3.4 and 4.6) giving both computational convergence rates and statistical optimality.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM convergence results to high-dimensional sparse settings, addressing a key gap in the literature.  
- Verifies assumptions and theory for Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models, demonstrating model-specific applicability.  
- Lacks practical guidance on initialization—no concrete high-dimensional initialization strategies (e.g., spectral methods) are provided.  
- Offers insufficient guidance on choosing between exact M-step maximization and gradient-ascent implementations in practice.  
- Does not provide data-dependent procedures or sensitivity discussion for tuning parameters (sparsity level and Dantzig selector λ).  
- Should explicitly cite and clarify the connection to Ning & Liu [26], explaining what new theory was needed for latent variable, nonconvex settings.  
- Assumptions (Conditions 3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) are technical and their practical plausibility beyond the examined models is not discussed.  
- Key technical challenges (e.g., analysis of the truncation step) are deferred to the supplement; the main paper could include more accessible detail.  
- Recommendation: technically strong, novel, and significant; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address practical implementation and clarity.
