- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with sparsity via truncation in E- and M-steps enabling analysis when d >> n.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score test for low-dimensional inference with proven asymptotic normality and semiparametric optimality.  
- Provides a unified theoretical framework giving both computational and statistical guarantees for latent variable models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, mixture of regressions).  
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM theory to high-dimensional settings — a significant theoretical advance.  
- Offers a clear decomposition of estimation error into optimization and statistical components.  
- Requires an initial estimator with small relative error but provides no practical method for obtaining such initialization.  
- Fails to advise when to use exact maximization (Alg.2) versus gradient ascent (Alg.3) for the M-step.  
- Does not provide guidance on choosing tuning parameters (bs, λ), sensitivity analysis, or data-dependent selection methods.  
- Omits explicit citation/context of Ning & Liu (2017) and does not clarify whether new developments were needed to apply the decorrelated score to latent variable models.  
- Many technical assumptions (Conditions 3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) are only verified in the appendix rather than in the main text for key models.  
- Relies heavily on a lengthy supplement; main paper is not sufficiently self-contained.  
- Pro: first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Pro: framework is applicable across multiple models and achieves semiparametric efficiency for inference.  
- Con: practical utility limited by missing implementation details and buried verifications.  
- Overall: important and novel theoretical contribution but needs major revisions to add practical guidance and surface key technical verifications.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions.
