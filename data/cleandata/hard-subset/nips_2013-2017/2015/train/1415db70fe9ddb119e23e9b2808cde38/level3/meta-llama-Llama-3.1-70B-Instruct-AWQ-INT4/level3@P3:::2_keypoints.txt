- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity for settings with d >> n.  
- Establishes a general theory guaranteeing convergence to the global solution and deriving convergence rates.  
- Algorithm converges at a geometric rate and achieves a (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence.  
- Develops a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional components.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with well-defined notation and detailed proofs in the appendix.  
- Provides a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment — a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Sections 3 and 4 are very dense and hard to follow; presentation and intuition behind proofs should be simplified.  
- Claim that the rate sqrt(s * log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal is stated without a clear proof.  
- Use of notation ^s in Equation (3.8) depends on unknown s; practical selection of ^s is unclear.  
- Assumption that an initial estimator satisfies ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 lacks guidance on how to obtain such an initializer.  
- Role and necessity of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 are not well explained; consequences of omitting it are unclear.  
- Practical usefulness is questionable because the method requires a good initial estimate and careful choice of sparsity parameters.  
- Technically sound overall, but should discuss limitations and potential applications in more detail.  
- Significant theoretical advance for high-dimensional EM, but additional work needed to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Reviewer rating: 7, citing both strong theoretical contributions and concerns about presentation, missing proofs, and practicality.
