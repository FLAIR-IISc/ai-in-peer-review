- Introduces a modified EM algorithm with a truncation step for sparsity and a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Claims near-optimal statistical convergence rates and computationally feasible estimation and inference.
- Novel truncation step is a creative mechanism to enforce sparsity in EM.
- Decorrelated score test meaningfully addresses inference with high-dimensional nuisance parameters.
- Strong technical contributions: rigorous derivation of convergence conditions and statistical rates.
- Comprehensive theoretical framework covering both computational and statistical guarantees.
- Demonstrates versatility via applications to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.
- Clear positioning within related work and differentiation from low-dimensional and tensor-based methods.
- Paper is dense and mathematically intricate, reducing accessibility for non-expert readers.
- Claim of achieving minimax-optimal rate lacks a formal proof in the main manuscript.
- Insufficient practical guidance on selecting critical parameters like sparsity level and initialization.
- The role and importance of the truncation step need deeper explanation and empirical analysis.
- Lack of empirical results on real-world datasets limits demonstrated applicability to practice.
- Post-rebuttal: authors addressed some concerns (truncation, minimax claim) but paper remains highly theoretical and hard to access.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to improve clarity, practical guidance, and empirical validation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel rigorous theory, fills a gap in high-dimensional latent variable modeling, and could spur further research.
- Arguments against acceptance: dense presentation, limited practical guidance/real-world validation, and unproven minimax claim in the main text.
