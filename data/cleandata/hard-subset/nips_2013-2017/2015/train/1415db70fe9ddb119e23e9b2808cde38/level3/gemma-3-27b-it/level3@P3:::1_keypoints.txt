- Addresses an important gap: provides theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Proposes a sensible algorithmic innovation: a truncation step in EM to enforce sparsity.  
- Establishes asymptotic normality for the estimator, enabling valid hypothesis testing.  
- Framework is broadly applicable and illustrated on Gaussian Mixtures and Mixture of Regressions.  
- Clearly situates the work relative to prior literature and sparse estimation methods.  

- Initialization guidance is lacking: no practical method given to obtain the required β^init satisfying the stated closeness condition.  
- No clear guidance on choosing between the two M-step implementations (exact maximization vs. gradient ascent).  
- Parameter tuning is underdeveloped: rules of thumb or data-dependent methods for bs and the Dantzig tuning parameter are missing.  
- The decorrelated score test corresponds to Ning & Liu and needs explicit citation and discussion in the main text.  
- Many technical assumptions are strong; broader discussion of their plausibility across latent models is needed.  
- Heavy reliance on the supplement: key technical details and proof sketches (e.g., Theorem 3.4) should appear in the main paper.  
- Originality needs clarification: explain why adapting existing inference tools to latent-variable, nonconvex EM settings is non-trivial.  

- Question on initialization: what practical strategies (e.g., sparse regression, L2 constraints) can produce the required β^init?  
- Question on M-step choice: when is exact maximization feasible and what are the accuracy vs. cost trade-offs?  
- Question on parameter selection: how to choose bs relative to true sparsity s* and tune the Dantzig selector in practice?  
- Request to explicitly compare and contrast with Ning & Liu and explain additional complexities from latent structure.  
- Request examples where assumptions 3.1–3.3 fail and discussion of consequences.  
- Request to sketch core proof ideas for main theorems in the manuscript.  

- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a significant open problem with a novel, theoretically grounded algorithm and important asymptotic results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient practical guidance on initialization/tuning, limited discussion of assumptions, and overreliance on supplementary material.  

- Overall: strong paper leaning toward accept, contingent on addressing practical initialization, tuning, assumption plausibility, and clarifying the Ning & Liu connection.
