This paper proposes a novel high dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation, along with a new inferential procedure for testing hypotheses for low dimensional components of high dimensional parameters. The authors establish a general theory for the expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm in inferring high dimensional latent variable models, making two key contributions. Firstly, they propose a high dimensional EM algorithm that converges at a geometric rate and attains an estimator with the (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence. Secondly, they construct a decorrelated score statistic for testing hypotheses related to low dimensional components of the high dimensional parameter.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough summary of related work in the field. The proposed algorithm and inferential procedure are well-motivated, and the theoretical guarantees provided are a significant contribution to the field. However, the contents of the paper are very dense, particularly in Sections 3 and 4, which may be challenging for readers to follow. The authors could consider simplifying these sections or providing additional explanations to aid understanding.

One of the main strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment in high dimensional latent variable models. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their framework by applying it to two high dimensional latent variable models, namely Gaussian mixture model and mixture of regression model. The proposed decorrelated score statistic is shown to be asymptotically normal, and its limiting variance is optimal in the sense that it attains the semiparametric information bound for the low dimensional components of interest in the presence of high dimensional nuisance parameters.

However, there are some concerns regarding the practicality of the proposed algorithm. For instance, Equation (3.8) uses an estimate of the sparsity level s, which depends on the unknown true sparsity level s*. The authors do not provide guidance on how to choose this estimate in practice. Additionally, the initialization condition requires an initial estimate of the parameter beta^init that satisfies ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2. It is unclear how to obtain such an initial estimate in practice.

The role of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is also not entirely clear. The authors claim that this step is necessary to enforce the sparsity structure, but it is unclear what happens if the truncation step is omitted. The authors could consider providing additional explanations or simulations to illustrate the importance of this step.

In terms of the minimax-optimality of the proposed algorithm, the authors claim that the sqrt(s * log(d/n)) rate is minimax-optimal, but no proof is provided in the main manuscript. The reviewer requests a clear proof of this claim.

Overall, this paper makes a solid contribution to the field of statistics, but its practical usefulness may be limited. The proposed algorithm and inferential procedure are well-motivated, but the density of the contents and the lack of guidance on practical implementation may make it challenging for machine learning researchers to adopt this work. However, the reviewer acknowledges the significance of this contribution and raises their score from 6 to 7.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel high dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation.
* The authors establish a general theory for the expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm in inferring high dimensional latent variable models.
* The proposed algorithm and inferential procedure are well-motivated, and the theoretical guarantees provided are a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The contents of the paper are very dense, particularly in Sections 3 and 4.
* The practicality of the proposed algorithm is unclear, particularly with regards to choosing the estimate of the sparsity level s and obtaining an initial estimate of the parameter beta^init.
* The role of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is not entirely clear.
* The minimax-optimality of the proposed algorithm is claimed, but no proof is provided.
