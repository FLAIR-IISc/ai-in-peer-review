

This paper proposes a modified EM algorithm for high-dimensional parameter estimation with sparsity structure, establishing convergence guarantees and a statistical rate of $O(\sqrt{s \log d / n})$. The authors also develop a statistical test framework for assessing estimator sparsity. The paper is well-organized with comprehensive related work coverage, addressing key gaps in high-dimensional latent variable models.

The theoretical contribution is strong, providing the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional EM settings. The modified algorithm incorporates a truncation step to enforce sparsity, with convergence guarantees and convergence rates established under appropriate conditions. The statistical test framework for low-dimensional components represents a notable advance, particularly for latent variable models where such inference was previously unaddressed.

However, the paper suffers from significant clarity issues. Sections 3 and 4 are exceptionally dense, making the technical arguments difficult to follow. The claim that $\sqrt{s \log(d/n)}$ is minimax-optimal lacks proof in the main manuscript, which is a serious omission. Practical concerns are substantial: Equation (3.8) uses $\hat{s}$ that depends on the unknown true sparsity level $s$, creating a circular dependency in implementation. The initialization requirement $||\beta^{\text{init}} - \beta^*||_2 < R/2$ is theoretically convenient but lacks practical guidance. The role of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 is also not adequately explained—what happens if it's omitted?

While the paper makes a solid theoretical contribution to statistics, its practical utility for machine learning researchers is questionable due to implementation challenges and the need for prior knowledge of sparsity. The dense presentation further limits accessibility. The paper would benefit from significant simplification and clearer explanation of practical implementation details.

The paper scores highly on quality (solid theoretical foundation) and originality (novel framework for high-dimensional EM), but lower on clarity (dense presentation) and significance (limited practical applicability). The theoretical contribution is valuable, but the paper's accessibility and practical relevance make it borderline for an AI conference. I've raised my score from 6 to 7 based on theoretical merit, but the paper would be stronger with improved clarity and practical implementation guidance.
