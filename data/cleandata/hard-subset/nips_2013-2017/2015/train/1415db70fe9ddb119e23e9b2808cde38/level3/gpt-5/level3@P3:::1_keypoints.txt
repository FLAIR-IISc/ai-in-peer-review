- Paper proposes a truncated high-dimensional EM (exact M-step or one-step gradient) that contracts geometrically and attains near-minimax error √(s* log d / n), plus a decorrelated score/Wald test that is semiparametrically efficient; instantiated for sparse GMMs and mixtures of regressions.  
- Extends population-to-sample EM analyses to high dimensions via hard-thresholding and links to debiasing/desparsification literature; testing method adapts Ning & Liu’s decorrelated score to the EM/Q setting.  

Strengths:
- Clear decomposition of optimization vs statistical error with explicit contraction factors for both M-step variants.  
- Hard-thresholding/truncation integrated into EM is simple and effective; support-evolution analysis is technically nontrivial.  
- Establishes asymptotic inference with semiparametric efficiency.  
- Provides concrete high-dimensional rate verifications for GMM and mixture-of-regressions examples.  

Weaknesses / requested clarifications:
- Initialization: requires βinit in a local basin ||βinit−β*||2 ≤ κ||β*||2; practical initializers suggested (PCA/top eigenvector + thresholding for GMM; moment methods or a few EM iterations + weighted Lasso for mixtures; generic PCA→moment→threshold recipe).  
- M-step choice: use exact maximization when Q gives closed-form or convex subproblems (better contraction); use gradient ascent for complex Q, streaming/mini-batch or memory-constrained settings (requires step-size tuning).  
- Choosing ŝ and λ: ŝ should be ≥ C·s* but not too large; practice via pilot penalized M-steps, stability selection, or held-out likelihood; λ for Dantzig typically ≍ C√(log d / n) with data-driven variance proxy and sample-splitting to calibrate.  
- Relation to Ning & Liu: the testing construction matches Ning & Liu’s decorrelated score; novelty lies in linking EM-Q derivatives to true score, verifying high-dimensional conditions for nonconvex EM iterates, and proving efficiency.  
- Assumptions: verified for the two examples (local concavity, Lipschitz gradients, concentration, sparsity, SNR/sub-Gaussianity) but are somewhat restrictive (balanced mixtures, high SNR, Gaussian noise); robustness and constants need discussion.  
- Novelty vs prior work: main new contributions are truncated EM with sharp support/statistical error control and the first high-dimensional inference theory for EM-based estimators; testing machinery itself is mostly reused.  

Suggestions to improve clarity:
- Move main conditions and proof sketches (with explicit constants for the two models) into the main paper.  
- Add a practical section/pseudo-code covering βinit construction, default ŝ, λ, η, early stopping, and M-step choice; include sensitivity studies.  
- Explicitly cite Ning & Liu (2017) and clarify what is reused versus what is new.  

Pros:
- Strong theory with sharp rates and efficiency, and a simple implementable algorithm that fills a gap for high-dimensional latent-variable inference.  

Cons:
- Heavy technical assumptions and limited practical tuning guidance; testing novelty is limited; many details are in the supplement.  

Overall: High-quality and significant work; recommend acceptance pending clearer practical guidance and explicit attribution to Ning & Liu.
