## Review of "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors propose a sparse EM procedure, incorporating a truncation step to enforce parameter sparsity, and develop an asymptotically normal score statistic for hypothesis testing on model parameters. This is a significant undertaking, as existing theoretical results for EM largely break down when the dimensionality exceeds the sample size.

The core idea of truncating the parameter vector at each EM iteration is a clever approach to induce sparsity and enable theoretical analysis. The paper builds upon recent work characterizing the convergence of EM (e.g., [2, 25, 30]) by introducing a new technical condition relating to infinity-norm control on the error in estimating the M-operator, specifically for sparse vectors.  The resulting convergence guarantees—geometric convergence up to a statistical error term scaling with sqrt(sparsity) and the infinity-norm M-step error—are impressive and represent a notable advance. The establishment of asymptotic normality for a score statistic is also a valuable contribution, addressing a key need for statistical inference in high-dimensional settings.

However, the paper leaves some questions unanswered regarding the necessity of the proposed sparse EM *variant*. The authors acknowledge that estimating the M-step at sparse vectors is often as easy as at full vectors for many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures), which raises the question of whether a standard EM algorithm followed by a single truncation step could achieve comparable results. Their counterexample involving linear regression is helpful, but a broader investigation would strengthen the argument for the benefits of the iterative truncation. The discussion of related sparse optimization work (like [Langford et al., 2009] on truncated gradient) is brief and could be expanded. 

Regarding clarity, the paper is well-organized and logically presented, though dense. The theoretical sections require careful reading. Providing more details on the initialization procedure used in the experimental section would be beneficial. The notation is fairly standard, but the sheer number of conditions and variables can be overwhelming. 

**Overall:** This paper presents a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of EM in high dimensions. The results are important and advance the state-of-the-art. While the specific advantage of the sparse EM variant over simpler approaches remains somewhat uncertain, the rigorous analysis and novel insights make this a worthwhile submission.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and technically sound approach to high-dimensional EM.
*   Strong theoretical guarantees on convergence and asymptotic normality.
*   Addresses a significant gap in the literature.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The necessity of the iterative truncation step, versus a single truncation after standard EM, isn't fully justified.
*   The paper is dense and requires significant effort to follow.
