- Presents a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step that enforces sparsity and provable geometric convergence under certain conditions.  
- Shows the estimator attains a near-optimal statistical rate sqrt(s* log d / n) and develops a decorrelated score test for inference on low-dimensional components.  
- Contrasts this work with prior low-dimensional EM analyses and high-dimensional convex inference methods, addressing non-convex latent variable models.  

- Theoretical significance and novelty: provides unified computational and statistical guarantees for high-dimensional EM and a simple truncation idea for sparsity.  
- Clear organization and strong literature review that situates the contributions relative to prior work.  
- General framework demonstrated on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regressions models, suggesting broad applicability.  

- Main theoretical sections are dense and rely on abstract technical conditions, making intuition and accessibility limited.  
- Strong practical assumptions: requires a near-true initialization ||init - *||_2 < R/2 with no practical recipe provided.  
- Tuning parameter bs depends on unknown true sparsity s*, and the paper lacks practical guidance (e.g., cross-validation) for selecting it.  
- Unsupported claim: the asserted (near-)minimax optimality of sqrt(s* log d / n) is stated without proof or citation.  

- Request a formal proof or citation that sqrt(s* log d / n) is minimax-optimal for the considered models.  
- Ask for practical recommendations for choosing the sparsity parameter bs in implementations.  
- Ask for strategies to obtain an initialization satisfying ||init - *||_2 < R/2 and discussion of its practical feasibility.  
- Ask what happens if the truncation (T) step is omitted: does the algorithm still converge and with what statistical rate?  

- Overall: technically deep and novel with important theoretical contributions, but dense presentation and practical limitations (initialization, tuning) reduce accessibility and practical impact; nevertheless merits acceptance.
