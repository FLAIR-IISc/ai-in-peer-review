- Proposes a novel sparse EM algorithm that truncates the parameter vector at each step to enforce sparsity.  
- Develops an asymptotically normal decorrelated score statistic for testing low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Shows geometric convergence of the sparse EM up to a statistical error that scales with sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity-norm M-step error.  
- Applies the algorithm and inferential procedure to a broad family of high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Assumes the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic within a basin of attraction and requires initialization inside that basin.  
- Introduces a new technical condition controlling error in estimating the M operator, required only for sparse vectors.  
- Notes that for many models estimating M at all vectors is as easy as at sparse vectors, raising doubt whether ordinary EM plus a single truncation could suffice.  
- Questions the specific necessity and practical advantage of the proposed sparse EM variant.  
- Recommends citing related sparse optimization work (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 on truncated gradient) for context.  
- Requests the experimental section to report the initialization used.  
- Strength: novel incorporation of sparsity into EM with high-dimensional convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: applicability across a broad family of latent variable models and provision of inferential tools.  
- Weakness: lack of experimental results to demonstrate practicality.  
- Weakness: strong assumptions (smooth, nearly quadratic Q-function and required initialization) may not hold universally.  
- Overall recommendation: accept conditionally, asking authors to address necessity of sparse EM and add experimental validation.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
