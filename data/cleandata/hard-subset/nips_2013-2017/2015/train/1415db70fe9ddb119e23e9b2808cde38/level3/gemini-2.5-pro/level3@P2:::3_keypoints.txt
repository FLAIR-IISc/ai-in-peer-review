1. Proposes a High-Dimensional EM algorithm that adds truncation after each M-step to enforce sparsity, achieving geometric convergence and near-minimax error sqrt(s log d / n).  
2. Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing on individual parameters, shown to be asymptotically normal for valid inference.  
3. Extends non-asymptotic EM analysis (e.g., Balakrishnan et al. 2014) to high-dimensional settings with sparsity and l_infinity-norm analysis.  
4. Strength — Significance: tackles an important open problem by providing a computationally tractable algorithm with strong theoretical guarantees.  
5. Strength — Quality: provides comprehensive theory including computational convergence rate and near-optimal statistical rates; inference result is semiparametric-efficient.  
6. Strength — Originality: novel unified framework guaranteeing geometric convergence, optimal rates, and valid inference for a non-convex high-dimensional procedure.  
7. Weakness — Algorithmic motivation: iterative truncation at every iteration is not sufficiently justified versus simpler alternatives (e.g., single truncation or l1-regularized EM).  
8. Weakness — Technical assumptions: Condition 3.3 restricts l_infinity control to sparse theta and may be an artifact of the analysis rather than a fundamental necessity.  
9. Weakness — Related work: literature on truncated/projected gradient methods (e.g., Langford et al. 2009) is relevant and should be cited/discussed.  
10. Weakness — Experiments: paper must specify how the required good initialization is obtained to ensure reproducibility and practical relevance.  
11. For acceptance: first feasible, theoretically-backed framework for optimal estimation and valid inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
12. For acceptance: substantial technical contribution analyzing a non-convex iterative procedure in high dimensions.  
13. Against acceptance: iterative truncation lacks full motivation and some assumptions may be proof artifacts, weakening necessity claims.  
14. Conclusion: overall a very strong paper with addressable weaknesses; recommendation is to accept.
