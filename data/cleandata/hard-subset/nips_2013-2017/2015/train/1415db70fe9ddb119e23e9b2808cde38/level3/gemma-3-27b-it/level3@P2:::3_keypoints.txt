- Addresses lack of theoretical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Proposes "sparse EM" with a truncation step to enforce sparsity during iterations.
- Develops an asymptotically normal decorrelated score test for low-dimensional components amid high-dimensional nuisances.
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM convergence and statistical-rate results to the high-dimensional regime.
- Proves geometric convergence with appropriate initialization and near-optimal statistical rate p s* · log d / n.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, well-written, and carefully justified.
- Introduction of truncation is a novel technique to handle non-convexity and sparsity in high dimensions.
- The decorrelated score test has practical value for hypothesis testing and confidence intervals in high-dimensional latent models.
- Framework is general and illustrated for Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression examples.
- Paper appropriately builds on and cites relevant prior EM and high-dimensional statistics literature.
- Unclear necessity/advantage of iterative truncation versus running standard EM then truncating once; needs stronger justification.
- Requirement for “appropriate initialization” (basin of attraction) may be difficult in practice and needs discussion of strategies.
- Technical conditions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) are dense and could use more intuition and verification guidance for specific models.
- Lacks experimental validation to demonstrate practical performance and benefits of sparse EM and the score test.
- Should cite related truncated-gradient work (e.g., Langford et al., 2009) to contextualize the truncation strategy.
- For acceptance: significant theoretical advance, novel methods, and rigorous analysis justify publication.
- Against acceptance: practical advantages not fully established, no empirical results, and complex technical conditions.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify truncation necessity and initialization guidance.
