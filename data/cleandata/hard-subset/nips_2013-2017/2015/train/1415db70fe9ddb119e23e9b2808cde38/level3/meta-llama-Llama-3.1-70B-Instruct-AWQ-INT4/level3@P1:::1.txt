This paper proposes a novel high dimensional EM algorithm that naturally incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation. The authors establish a general theory of the EM algorithm for inferring high dimensional latent variable models, making two main contributions: (i) a novel high dimensional EM algorithm that converges at a geometric rate and attains an estimator with the (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence, and (ii) a new inferential procedure for testing hypotheses for low dimensional components of high dimensional parameters.

The main strengths of the paper are:

1. The authors provide a unified framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment in high dimensional latent variable models.
2. The proposed high dimensional EM algorithm is computationally feasible and achieves an estimator with the (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence.
3. The authors establish the first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensions for a broad family of statistical models.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

1. The authors do not provide clear guidance on how to obtain the initial estimator β^{init} that satisfies the requirement mentioned in the paper. It would be helpful to provide more practical advice on this matter.
2. The paper does not explicitly state when to use each of the two proposed algorithms in practice. A clear comparison of the two algorithms and their respective advantages and disadvantages would be beneficial.
3. The authors do not provide a detailed discussion on how to choose ĥs and λ in practice, assess sensitivity to these tuning parameters, and offer data-dependent selection methods. This would be a valuable addition to the paper.
4. The paper does not explicitly cite that the decorrelated score test matches the method in Ning & Liu and does not state whether any new development was needed to apply it here. A clear acknowledgement of the relationship between the two methods would be appreciated.
5. The paper requires numerous technical assumptions for the main theorems, and it is not clear whether these assumptions hold for the latent variable models considered. A more detailed discussion on the validity of these assumptions would be helpful.
6. The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the novel contributions relative to existing work (e.g., Ning & Liu). It would be helpful to clarify why applying those tools here is nontrivial and what new insights are gained from the current paper.
7. The paper leaves key material only in a long supplement, which can make it difficult for readers to follow the main ideas and proofs. It would be beneficial to expand the main paper to include more details on the technical challenges and proofs.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of high dimensional statistics, but could benefit from more detailed explanations and discussions on the practical implications of the proposed methods.
