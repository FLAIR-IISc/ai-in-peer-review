- Paper proposes a high-dimensional EM with a hard-thresholding step that converges geometrically to a near-minimax optimal estimator (rate sqrt(s* log d / n)).  
- Develops a decorrelated score-based inference procedure with established asymptotic normality for low-dimensional components.  
- Framework is general and demonstrated on high-dimensional Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of linear regressions.  
- Significant impact: provides a computationally tractable EM-based method with guarantees for estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Strong technical rigor: decomposes error into optimization and statistical parts, proves geometric convergence and near-optimal statistical rates despite non-convexity and truncation.  
- Novelty: analyzes a non-global, non-convex estimator (via EM + truncation) and shows it suffices for valid high-dimensional inference.  
- Major practical gap: requires an initial estimator within constant relative error of the truth, but the paper gives no practical guidance on how to obtain such initialization.  
- Need guidance on algorithm choices: no discussion of trade-offs between exact M-step and gradient-ascent M-step or when each is appropriate.  
- Tuning parameter issue: sparsity level bs and Dantzig parameter λ depend on unknown theoretical quantities; no data-driven selection strategies or sensitivity analysis provided.  
- Positioning: should explicitly cite Ning & Liu (JASA 2017) and clarify that the main novelty is applying decorrelated score inference to a local EM solution rather than introducing a new inferential construction.  
- Technical assumptions unclear: more intuitive discussion needed on conditions like Lipschitz-Gradient, Concavity-Smoothness, and when local strong concavity of Q holds in practice.  
- Paper organization: too much critical material in the supplement—include high-level proof sketches and explanations of how truncation perturbations and statistical errors are controlled.
