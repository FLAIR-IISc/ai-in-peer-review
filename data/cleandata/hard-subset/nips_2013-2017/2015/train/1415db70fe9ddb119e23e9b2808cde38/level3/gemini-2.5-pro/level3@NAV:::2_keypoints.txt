- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce parameter sparsity when d >> n.
- Introduces truncation as a core technical innovation and provides theoretical analysis showing geometric convergence to a near-optimal estimator.
- Develops a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional components in the high-dimensional parameter.
- Extends prior low-dimensional EM analyses to the high-dimensional, non-convex, regularized regime and bridges computational algorithms with statistical theory.
- Manuscript is well-organized and situates contributions within related work.
- Main weakness: Sections 3–4 are theoretically dense and difficult to follow, limiting accessibility.
- Practical aspects are under-addressed, reducing direct applicability.
- Missing proof or clear reference for the claimed near-minimax statistical rate sqrt(s * log(d/n)).
- Unclear how to select the sparsity hyperparameter ŝ in practice since it depends on the unknown true sparsity s.
- Needs discussion of practical methods to obtain an initial estimator β^init within the required radius for convergence.
- Requires clarification of the precise role of the truncation step and the algorithm’s behavior if that step is omitted.
- Conclusion: solid and original theoretical contribution, but clarity and practical-implementation clarifications are needed to increase impact.
