- Tackles theoretical guarantees for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models, where existing results fail.  
- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity.  
- Develops a framework for both parameter estimation and hypothesis testing (decorrelated score test).  
- Proves geometric convergence, near-optimal estimation rates, and asymptotic normality for the modified EM.  
- Builds on prior EM convergence work but adapts techniques to handle truncation-induced technical challenges.  
- Claims general applicability across a broad family of latent variable models.  
- Paper is generally well-written and algorithmic steps are clearly described.  
- Initialization requirement is unclear; practical guidance for obtaining the required initializer is missing.  
- No clear guidance on choosing between exact maximization and gradient-ascent implementations of the M-step.  
- Critical tuning parameters (ĥs and λ) are sensitive and the paper gives limited practical/data-dependent selection methods.  
- Needs explicit citation and discussion of equivalence to Ning & Liu’s decorrelated score test and any required modifications.  
- Requires deeper discussion of whether the technical assumptions realistically hold for the considered models or may be violated.  
- Should better delineate why existing low-dimensional tools fail and where high-dimensional truncation introduces nontrivial obstacles.  
- Heavy reliance on the supplement; key technical arguments and proof sketches should appear in the main paper for accessibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important gap, provides solid theoretical results, and proposes a potentially practical algorithm.  
- Arguments for rejection: lacks practical guidance on initialization, tuning, and algorithm selection, and over-relies on supplementary material.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution but should be accepted only with major revisions to address the listed weaknesses.
