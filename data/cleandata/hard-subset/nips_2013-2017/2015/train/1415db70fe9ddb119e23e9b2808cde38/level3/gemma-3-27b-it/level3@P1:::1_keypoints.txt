- Paper proposes a theoretical framework for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models with a truncation step to enforce sparsity.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing of low-dimensional parameters and proves asymptotic normality.  
- Establishes statistical rates of convergence for the modified high-dimensional EM algorithm.  
- Addresses a gap in literature that previously focused on low-dimensional EM guarantees.  
- Builds on prior EM convergence work and contrasts with tensor and Lasso-based alternatives.  
- Theoretical rigor: theorems and conditions are clearly stated and proofs appear rigorous (in appendix).  
- Novel contribution: truncation-enforced high-dimensional EM and the decorrelated score test are valuable extensions.  
- Addresses an important problem: provides statistical properties for EM in high dimensions.  
- Generality: framework applied to Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models, suggesting broad applicability.  
- Lacks practical guidance on initialization, selection of sparsity parameter bs, and step size η.  
- No sensitivity analysis or data-dependent tuning procedures for key hyperparameters.  
- Comparison to related work is insufficiently detailed, especially adaptation from Ning & Liu and explicit citation/differences.  
- Numerous technical assumptions (3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.5) need clearer practical interpretation and validation.  
- Heavy reliance on a lengthy appendix limits accessibility and understanding of core ideas.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, fills an important gap, and has broad applicability.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient practical guidance, extensive technical assumptions, and accessibility issues due to appendix dependence.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with potential impact but should be revised for practical guidance and clearer positioning vs. existing methods; reviewer recommends weak accept conditional on revisions.  
- Reviewer confidence: 7/10.
