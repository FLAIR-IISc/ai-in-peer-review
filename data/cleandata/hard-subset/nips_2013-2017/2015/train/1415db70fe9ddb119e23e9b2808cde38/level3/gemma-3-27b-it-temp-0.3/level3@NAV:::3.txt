## Review: High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality

This paper tackles the challenging problem of theoretical guarantees for the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models. The authors propose a sparse EM procedure, incorporating truncation at each step to enforce sparsity, and derive a novel asymptotically normal, decorrelated score statistic for hypothesis testing on specific parameter entries. A key theoretical result establishes geometric convergence of the sparse-EM estimate to the truth, scaling with the square root of sparsity and the infinity-norm M-step error. 

The work builds upon recent advances in understanding EM convergence (e.g., [2, 25, 30]) by extending these results to the high-dimensional regime. It addresses a significant gap in the literature, where existing statistical guarantees often rely on low-dimensional settings or strong regularity assumptions. The introduction of the truncation step is a central contribution, aiming to overcome the challenges posed by high dimensionality. The authors also correctly identify that estimating the M-operator is often as easy at sparse vectors as at all vectors, raising a valid question about the necessity of the truncation.

**Strengths:** The paper presents a technically rigorous analysis with strong theoretical results. The geometric convergence guarantee and the asymptotically normal score statistic are valuable contributions. The authors clearly articulate the challenges of high-dimensional EM and propose a reasonable approach to address them. The connection to existing work is well-established, and the discussion of potential limitations (e.g., the necessity of truncation) demonstrates intellectual honesty.

**Weaknesses:** While the theoretical framework is compelling, the practical necessity and advantage of the sparse EM variant over standard EM followed by a single truncation remain somewhat unclear. The authors acknowledge this, but a more thorough empirical comparison would strengthen the argument. The technical conditions (especially Condition 3.3) appear quite strong and may limit the applicability of the results. The experimental section is lacking detail regarding the initialization procedure used, which is crucial given the sensitivity of EM to initial conditions. The paper would benefit from citing related work on truncated gradient methods, such as Langford et al. (2009).

**Quality:** Technically sound and well-supported by theoretical analysis. The work appears complete, although further empirical validation would be beneficial.

**Clarity:** Generally well-written, but the technical details can be dense. More intuitive explanations of the key conditions would improve readability.

**Originality:** The combination of sparse EM, asymptotic normality results, and convergence guarantees in the high-dimensional setting is novel.

**Significance:** The results are potentially significant for researchers working on high-dimensional latent variable models. The theoretical framework provides a foundation for further research in this area.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Provides important theoretical guarantees for EM in high dimensions.
*   Introduces a novel sparse EM procedure and a decorrelated score statistic.
*   Addresses a significant gap in the existing literature.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The practical advantage of the sparse EM variant remains uncertain.
*   Technical conditions may be restrictive.
*   Lack of detail in the experimental section regarding initialization.



**Overall:** I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The authors should address the concerns regarding the necessity of truncation, provide more details on the initialization procedure, and consider citing relevant work on truncated gradient methods. A more thorough empirical evaluation would also strengthen the paper.
