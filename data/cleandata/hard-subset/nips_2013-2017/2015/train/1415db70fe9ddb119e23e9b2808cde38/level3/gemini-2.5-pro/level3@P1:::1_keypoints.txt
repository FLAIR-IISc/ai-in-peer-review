- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a hard truncation step and proves geometric convergence to a near-minimax estimator (~sqrt(s log d / n)) for both exact and gradient M-steps.  
- Develops a decorrelated score test for inference on low-dimensional components, proving asymptotic normality and semiparametric efficiency.  
- Applies the framework to Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regression models and frames results under general conditions (Lipschitz-Gradient, Concavity-Smoothness, Statistical-Error).  
- Strength: addresses a significant gap by providing a unified, computationally feasible theoretical framework for estimation and inference in d >> n latent variable models.  
- Strength: provides strong theoretical guarantees—simultaneous geometric computational convergence and near-optimal statistical rates—for a nonconvex EM-type estimator.  
- Strength: presents a general analysis template with verifiable conditions, making the approach potentially applicable beyond the two examples.  
- Weakness: offers little practical guidance—no data-driven procedures or sensitivity analysis for tuning parameters (sparsity level bs, Dantzig λ) and no guidance on choosing between M-step implementations.  
- Weakness: relies on an initializer within a certain radius of the truth but gives no practical method to obtain such an initialization.  
- Weakness: originality of the inferential procedure is unclear and appears closely related to existing debiased/desparsified M-estimator literature (e.g., Ning & Liu 2017; van de Geer 2014; Zhang & Zhang 2014) without explicit clarification of required innovations.  
- Weakness: main theorems depend on many high-level technical conditions; the paper needs more intuition on their restrictiveness and implications for the specific models.  
- Weakness: organization and clarity suffer from critical details being relegated to the appendix; the paper should better explain key technical challenges (e.g., analysis of the truncation step and the role of the l1 statistical error).  
- Conclusion: paper is theoretically deep and important but should address practical guidance, initialization strategy, and clarify novelty relative to prior high-dimensional inference work before acceptance.
