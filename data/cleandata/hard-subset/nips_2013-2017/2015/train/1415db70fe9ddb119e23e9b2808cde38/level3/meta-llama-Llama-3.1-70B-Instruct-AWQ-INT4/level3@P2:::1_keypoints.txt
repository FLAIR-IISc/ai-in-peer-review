- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity for latent variable models.  
- Main contributions: (i) algorithm with geometric convergence and near-optimal statistical rate, (ii) a decorrelated score statistic for low-dimensional inference.  
- Provides simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for estimation and uncertainty assessment.  
- Analysis relies on technical conditions (Lipschitz continuity, concavity, statistical error bounds).  
- Requires the true parameter to lie in a basin of attraction and bounded sample–population M-step error—assumptions that may be hard to verify.  
- Demonstrates framework on Gaussian mixture and mixture of regressions, showing practical applicability.  
- Builds on existing work (e.g., Ning & Liu’s decorrelated score); should explicitly acknowledge and clarify novel extensions.  
- Compared to tensor methods, offers computationally feasible estimation and (near) optimal rates in high dimensions.  
- Lacks practical guidance: tuning parameter selection and sensitivity analysis are insufficient.  
- Key technical material is deferred to a long supplement; more in-paper details of proofs/challenges would help.  
- Arguments for acceptance: unified framework for estimation and inference, simultaneous guarantees, empirical demonstrations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong/unverifiable assumptions, limited practical implementation advice, insufficiently clarified novelty relative to prior work.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address assumptions, practical guidance, and clarification of prior-work connections.
