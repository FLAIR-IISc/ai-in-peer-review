- Proposes a modified EM algorithm with a hard truncation step to enforce sparsity in high-dimensional settings (d >> n).  
- Shows geometric convergence under conditions and with suitable initialization, achieving near-minimax statistical error rate (sqrt(s* log d / n)).  
- Develops a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing and uncertainty quantification of individual parameters, claiming asymptotic normality and optimal semiparametric variance.  
- Extends low-dimensional EM theory to the high-dimensional sparse regime and links to debiased-Lasso-style inference for nonconvex EM local optima.  
- Strength: addresses an important open problem and provides a unified framework for estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Strength: technically strong—detailed analysis of convergence rates and asymptotic properties of the inferential procedure.  
- Strength: well-organized presentation with clear separation of computation/estimation and inference sections.  
- Weakness: dense, notation-heavy theoretical sections lacking high-level intuition—readability and accessibility could be improved.  
- Weakness: repeated claim of (near-)minimax optimality lacks a formal lower-bound proof or specific citation for the latent-variable model class.  
- Weakness: practical guidance missing for selecting the sparsity parameter ^s, which depends on unknown true sparsity s*.  
- Weakness: strong initialization assumption (beta^init within R/2 of beta*) is unrealistic without a practical procedure to obtain such an initializer.  
- Weakness: insufficient algorithmic justification for hard truncation—needs discussion of alternatives (e.g., l1-regularized M-step) and consequences of omitting truncation.
