- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation step to enforce sparsity and a statistical inference framework for latent variable models.  
- Proves geometric convergence and a near-optimal statistical rate of √(s log(d/n)) for the proposed algorithm.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional components in a high-dimensional parameter space.  
- Addresses a significant literature gap: standard EM convergence guarantees do not extend to d > n.  
- Correctly situates work relative to prior results (e.g., refs [30, 25, 2]) and clarifies why earlier analyses fail in high dimensions.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis with clear convergence guarantees and logical organization.  
- Strength: comprehensive related-work discussion demonstrating command of the field.  
- Major readability issue: Sections 3–4 are densely notated and hard to follow, reducing accessibility.  
- Major missing justification: claim of minimax-optimality for the √(s log(d/n)) rate lacks proof or citation.  
- Practical concern: sparsity parameter \hat{s} depends on unknown true sparsity s, with no method provided to estimate it in practice.  
- Practical concern: convergence guarantee requires an initializer β^init within R/2 of β*, but no strategy is given to obtain such an initialization.  
- Methodological gap: the necessity and impact of the truncation step are not analyzed—unclear if omission still yields convergence or changes the rate.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong and valuable theoretical contribution, but limited immediate impact due to missing practical guidance and some unsubstantiated claims.  
- Acceptance arguments: rigorous theory, fills high-dimensional EM gap, novel decorrelated score test, solid literature context.  
- Rejection arguments: dense exposition, unproven optimality claim, unresolved practical issues (sparsity estimation, initialization), and limited discussion of truncation's role.
