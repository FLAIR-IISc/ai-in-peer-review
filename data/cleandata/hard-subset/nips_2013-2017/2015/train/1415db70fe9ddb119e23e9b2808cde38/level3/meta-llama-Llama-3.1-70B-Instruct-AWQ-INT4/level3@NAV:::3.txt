This paper proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation. The authors provide a general theory for the EM algorithm in high-dimensional latent variable models, including simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the proposed algorithm and a respective uncertainty assessment procedure.

The paper's main contributions are two-fold. Firstly, the authors propose a sparse EM procedure that truncates the parameter vector at each step to enforce a desired sparsity level. This approach allows for the establishment of significantly improved statistical rates of convergence. Secondly, the authors introduce an asymptotically normal, decorrelated score statistic for testing whether a specific parameter entry is non-zero.

The authors prove the geometric convergence of the sparse-EM estimate to the truth up to a statistical error term scaling with the square root of sparsity times the infinity-norm M-step error. They also establish the asymptotic normality of the decorrelated score statistic, which achieves the semiparametric information lower bound for the low-dimensional components of interest in the presence of high-dimensional nuisance parameters.

The paper's strengths include its ability to provide high-dimensional convergence guarantees, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical properties.

However, there are some weaknesses and limitations to the paper. Firstly, the authors assume that the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin of attraction, which may not always be the case in practice. Additionally, the authors require initialization within this basin, which can be challenging in high-dimensional settings. Furthermore, the authors introduce a new technical condition, infinity-norm control on the error in estimating the M operator, which is required only for sparse vectors.

The paper also raises some questions, such as whether ordinary EM followed by a single truncation could achieve similar guarantees. The authors argue that linear regression is a counterexample, but further investigation is needed to fully understand the implications of this approach.

In terms of originality, the paper's contribution is significant, as it provides a novel approach to high-dimensional EM algorithm and establishes new theoretical guarantees. The paper's significance is also high, as it addresses a difficult problem in high-dimensional latent variable models and provides a new framework for simultaneous parameter estimation and uncertainty assessment.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their proposed algorithm and its theoretical properties. The paper's contributions are significant, and it has the potential to make a substantial impact in the field of high-dimensional statistics.
