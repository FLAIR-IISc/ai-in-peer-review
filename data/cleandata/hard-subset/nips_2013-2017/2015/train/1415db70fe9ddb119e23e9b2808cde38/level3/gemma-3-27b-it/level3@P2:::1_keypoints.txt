- Presents a theoretical framework for EM in high-dimensional latent variable models, addressing a gap in existing literature.  
- Proposes a truncated EM algorithm that enforces sparsity and provides convergence guarantees.  
- Develops a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing on low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with convergence rates and asymptotic normality under stated conditions.  
- Builds on and extends prior EM convergence and post-selection inference results, situating the work within existing literature.  
- Potentially high impact, offering a theoretically grounded EM approach for high-dimensional models.  
- Lacks practical guidance on obtaining the required initialization (β^{init}) and how to meet the initialization requirement.  
- Does not specify how to choose the sparsity parameter ĥs or the gradient stepsize λ, and lacks sensitivity analysis.  
- Insufficiently explains the novelty over Ning & Liu (2017) and the non-trivial adaptations needed for this framework.  
- Presents an extensive list of technical conditions without thorough discussion of their applicability to the considered models.  
- Heavy reliance on a lengthy supplement; key proofs and model-specific details are not included in the main paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fills a significant gap, provides rigorous guarantees, introduces a novel test, and has broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing practical tuning/initialization guidance, unclear novelty relative to prior work, weak validation of assumptions, and over-reliance on supplementary material.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution but limited practical relevance until tuning, initialization, and clarity issues are addressed.  
- Recommendation: Weak Reject; authors should address practical guidance, clarify novelty, justify assumptions, and move key content into the main paper.
