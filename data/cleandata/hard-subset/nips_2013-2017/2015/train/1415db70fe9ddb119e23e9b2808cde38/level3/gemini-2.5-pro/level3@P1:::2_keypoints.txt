- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation (T-) step to enforce sparsity for d >> n problems.
- Provides unified theoretical analysis showing geometric convergence from a suitable initialization.
- Shows the estimator attains near-minimax statistical rate ≈ sqrt(s* log d / n).
- Develops a decorrelated score test for inference on low-dimensional components of the high-dimensional parameter.
- Proves the test statistic is asymptotically normal and achieves the semiparametric information bound (statistical efficiency).
- Framework is general and illustrated on Gaussian mixture and mixture-of-regressions models.
- Builds on prior low-dimensional EM analyses and high-dimensional inference techniques, extending them to non-convex latent-variable settings.
- Strength: tackles an important, challenging problem and delivers both estimation and inference guarantees for a computationally feasible algorithm.
- Strength: rigorous, comprehensive theoretical analysis with verifiable conditions and formal proofs (Theorems 3.4 and 4.6).
- Strength: well-organized paper that clearly situates contributions relative to existing literature.
- Weakness: theory sections are very dense and rely on many abstract technical conditions, reducing accessibility for a general ML audience.
- Weakness: strong practical assumptions—requires an initial estimator within a specific l2-ball, but gives no practical initialization method.
- Weakness: requires specifying a sparsity parameter bs on the order of the unknown true sparsity s*, with no guidance on selecting bs in practice.
- Question: please provide citation or argument that the sqrt(s* log d / n) rate is (near-)minimax-optimal for the considered latent-variable models.
- Question: clarify the necessity and role of the truncation step and what would happen if it were omitted.
- Overall: significant theoretical contribution with dual estimation/inference guarantees, but dense presentation and unaddressed practical issues limit broader ML impact.
