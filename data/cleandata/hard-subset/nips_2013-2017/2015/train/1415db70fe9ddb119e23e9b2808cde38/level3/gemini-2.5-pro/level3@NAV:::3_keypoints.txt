- Proposed a sparse EM algorithm with hard-thresholding at every iteration and proved geometric convergence to a near-optimal estimator.  
- Introduced a decorrelated score statistic enabling asymptotically normal hypothesis testing for low-dimensional components.  
- Extends Balakrishnan et al. (2017) low-dimensional EM analysis to high-dimensional settings (p >> n).  
- Novel inference framework adapting high-dimensional debiasing ideas to local optima of non-convex latent variable models.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: provides computational and statistical guarantees for EM in high-dimensional regimes.  
- Analysis appears technically sound and addresses a major gap between EM practice and theory.  
- Weak motivation for truncating at every iteration; unclear why iterative truncation outperforms running EM then truncating once.  
- Relies on a new infinity-norm control condition for the M-step error that may negate the claimed computational advantage for models like Gaussian mixtures.  
- Needs stronger connection to prior work: truncation relates closely to truncated gradient methods (e.g., Langford et al., 2009) and should be cited/discussed.  
- Experiments should explicitly state initialization, since theoretical guarantees depend on it.
