- Paper proposes a high-dimensional EM with an added truncation step to enforce sparsity, plus two M-step implementations (exact maximization and gradient ascent).  
- Theory shows geometric optimization error decay and near-optimal statistical error O(√(s* log d / n)).  
- Develops a decorrelated score-based test for low-dimensional components that attains semiparametric efficiency.  
- Illustrates theory on Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions.  
- Connects to prior EM sample/population analyses (Balakrishnan et al.), debiasing/desparsification inference (van de Geer; Zhang & Zhang), and tensor methods for latent variables (Anandkumar et al.).  
- Strength: clear conceptual contribution of truncation-equipped EM with entrywise error control.  
- Strength: provides simultaneous computational and inferential guarantees for latent-variable models.  
- Strength: gives two concrete examples and establishes asymptotic normality with efficient variance.  
- Weakness/request: Clarify precise initialization conditions and practical procedures to construct β^{init} that land in the basin, with explicit SNR/separation and sparsity requirements.  
- Weakness/request: Provide guidance on choosing between exact and gradient M-steps, including conditioning, per-iteration cost, memory tradeoffs, and step-size rules for the gradient version.  
- Weakness/request: Explain practical tuning of ŝ and λ (Dantzig step), provide data-dependent selection rules (e.g., EBIC, stability selection, λ ≈ c·σ̂√(log d / n) or CV), and assess sensitivity to mis-specification.  
- Weakness/request: Explicitly cite Ning & Liu’s decorrelated/orthogonal score framework and state whether adapting it to latent-variable EM required novel developments (e.g., use of Qn and Tn, handling nonconvexity/sample splitting).  
- Weakness/request: Discuss plausibility of strong technical assumptions (Conditions 3.1–3.3, 4.1–4.4; Assumption 4.5) for the target models and give empirical checks when assumptions are mildly violated.  
- Weakness/request: Improve exposition by moving key proof sketches and technical challenges (support evolution under truncation, entrywise concentration) from the supplement into the main text and include a practical algorithm section with defaults.  
- Quality assessment: Analysis appears sound but relies on stringent assumptions that need clearer verification and practical guidance.  
- Clarity assessment: Generally well organized but would benefit from the requested practical guidance and proof sketches for reproducibility.  
- Originality assessment: Novel synthesis of truncation-equipped EM and inference for latent-variable models, but the inference component closely follows Ning & Liu—clarify incremental novelty.  
- Significance assessment: Potentially impactful for high-dimensional latent-variable estimation and inference if practical initialization/tuning guidance and assumption verification are strengthened.  
- Pros: solid theory, near-optimal rates, efficient inference, and relevant models.  
- Cons: practical initialization/tuning unclear, reliance on strong assumptions, and inference novelty relative to Ning & Liu not fully delineated; many details deferred to supplement.
