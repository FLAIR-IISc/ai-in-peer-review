- Proposes a High-Dimensional EM algorithm that inserts a hard-thresholding step after each M-step to enforce sparsity, with proofs of geometric convergence to a near-minimax optimal estimator.  
- Develops a decorrelated score statistic for inference in high-dimensional latent-variable models and proves its asymptotic normality for hypothesis testing and uncertainty quantification.  
- Extends non-asymptotic EM analysis from low-dimensional to sparse high-dimensional settings and adapts debiasing/desparsification ideas to the nonconvex latent-variable context.  

Strengths:
- Addresses an important gap by providing a computationally feasible EM procedure with theoretical guarantees for d >> n including both estimation and inference.  
- Presents comprehensive, seemingly sound theory: geometric optimization rate, control of statistical error, and attainment of sqrt(s log d / n) statistical rate.  
- Framework is general with verifiable conditions and demonstrated applicability to Gaussian mixtures and mixtures of regressions.  

Weaknesses / Suggestions:
- Unclear necessity of per-iteration truncation: Condition 3.3 only requires sparse inputs to the M-operator, but for canonical examples Mn(θ) seems no harder to handle for dense θ, so truncation may be a proof convenience rather than fundamental.  
- Lacks comparison to a simpler baseline (run EM to convergence then single hard-threshold): needs theoretical or empirical justification (or counterexample) for iterative truncation.  
- Should relate the algorithm to existing sparse optimization methods (e.g., Iterative Hard Thresholding, truncated gradients) and cite relevant literature like Langford et al. (2009).  
- Initialization details are insufficient: experiments should state and justify the initialization method to verify it meets the basin-of-attraction assumption and ensure reproducibility.  

Overall:
- Strong, novel paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions; main shortcoming is insufficient justification for iterative truncation—recommend acceptance if addressed.
