The paper "High Dimensional EM Algorithm: Statistical Optimization and Asymptotic Normality" proposes a novel high dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity structure into parameter estimation for latent variable models. The authors make two main contributions: (i) a high dimensional EM algorithm that converges at a geometric rate and achieves an estimator with the (near-)optimal statistical rate of convergence, and (ii) a decorrelated score statistic for testing hypotheses related to low dimensional components of high dimensional parameters.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical guarantees. The authors establish simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the high dimensional EM algorithm and the respective uncertainty assessment procedure. The analysis is based on several technical conditions, including Lipschitz continuity, concavity, and statistical error bounds.

One of the key strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unified framework for both point estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensional latent variable models. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their framework by applying it to two high dimensional latent variable models, namely Gaussian mixture model and mixture of regression model.

However, the paper requires several technical assumptions to hold for the main theorems, which may not be trivial to verify in practice. For instance, the authors assume that the true parameter lies within a certain basin of attraction, and that the statistical error between the sample and population versions of the M-step is bounded. While the authors provide some guidance on how to choose the tuning parameters, more practical advice and sensitivity analysis would be helpful.

Moreover, the paper builds upon existing work, such as Ning & Liu's decorrelated score test. The authors should explicitly acknowledge this connection and clarify what new developments were needed to apply this method to high dimensional latent variable models.

In terms of novelty, the paper's main contributions lie in its ability to provide a computationally feasible approach for optimal parameter estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensional latent variable models. While existing methods, such as tensor methods, have been proposed for estimating latent variable models, they are often limited to low dimensional regimes or have suboptimal statistical rates.

To further strengthen the paper, the authors could provide more details on the technical challenges and proofs, rather than relegating key material to a long supplement. Additionally, more practical advice and sensitivity analysis would be helpful for readers to implement the proposed algorithms in practice.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

1. The paper provides a novel and unified framework for both point estimation and asymptotic inference in high dimensional latent variable models.
2. The authors establish simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the high dimensional EM algorithm and the respective uncertainty assessment procedure.
3. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed framework by applying it to two high dimensional latent variable models.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

1. The paper requires several technical assumptions to hold for the main theorems, which may not be trivial to verify in practice.
2. The authors could provide more practical advice and sensitivity analysis for readers to implement the proposed algorithms in practice.
3. The paper builds upon existing work, and more explicit acknowledgement and clarification of new developments would be helpful.

**Rating:** 8/10

**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the concerns mentioned above, provide more practical advice and sensitivity analysis, and clarify the novel contributions relative to existing work.
