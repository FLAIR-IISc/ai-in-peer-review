- Paper proposes a high-dimensional EM framework with a truncation step to enforce sparsity for estimation and inference in latent variable models.  
- Introduces a decorrelated score test for hypothesis testing in high-dimensional latent variable settings.  
- Claims to be the first computationally feasible method achieving simultaneous optimal estimation and inference in these settings.  
- Strength: Novel truncation step enabling sparsity and improved statistical convergence rates over prior low-dimensional EM work.  
- Strength: Provides a unified theoretical framework combining computational and statistical guarantees for estimation and inference.  
- Strength: Applicable to multiple models (Gaussian mixtures, regression mixtures), demonstrating broad generality.  
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical results including geometric convergence and asymptotic normality of the score statistic.  
- Weakness: Unclear conditions and practical methods for obtaining the required initialization β^{init}, despite its importance to guarantees.  
- Weakness: No practical guidance on choosing between the exact and gradient-ascent M-step implementations.  
- Weakness: Lacks procedures or discussion for selecting hyperparameters (ŝ and λ) and their sensitivity in practice.  
- Weakness: Decorrelated score test closely resembles prior work and is insufficiently cited or distinguished for novelty.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on technical conditions whose real-world applicability is not well discussed.  
- Weakness: Main paper omits key technical details and proofs, relegating them to the supplementary material and reducing accessibility.  
- Pro: Addresses an important gap by extending EM to high-dimensional settings with strong theoretical guarantees.  
- Con: Practical limitations (initialization, hyperparameter selection, algorithm choice) and reliance on supplements limit immediate applicability.  
- Recommendation: Major revision requested to address initialization, algorithmic guidance, hyperparameter selection, citation clarification, and to include more main-paper details.
