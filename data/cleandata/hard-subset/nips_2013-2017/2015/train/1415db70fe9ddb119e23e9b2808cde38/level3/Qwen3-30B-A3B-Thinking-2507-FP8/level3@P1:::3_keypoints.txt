- Two main contributions: a sparse high-dimensional EM with truncation achieving geometric convergence and near-optimal statistical rate, and a decorrelated score statistic for asymptotically valid inference achieving the semiparametric information bound.  
- Extends prior EM convergence results to high-dimensional, sparse settings where sparsity is critical.  
- Provides a unified theoretical framework giving simultaneous computational and statistical guarantees: error = geometric optimization error + statistical error O(√(s* log d / n)).  
- Verifies detailed conditions and results for specific models (Gaussian mixture, mixture of regressions).  
- Introduces a novel ∞-norm control condition on the M-operator estimation error applicable to sparse vectors.  
- The iterative truncation step is theoretically justified and argued necessary; authors show standard EM plus single truncation can fail (counterexample with linear regression).  
- Questionable necessity of iterative truncation for many models where estimating M is as easy at all vectors as at sparse vectors.  
- The linear regression counterexample needs stronger justification to rule out simpler approaches.  
- Lacks detailed comparison of computational complexity between the proposed algorithm and simpler alternatives.  
- Experimental section is underdeveloped and omits critical initialization details required for basin-of-attraction guarantees.  
- Misses connections to relevant sparse optimization literature (e.g., truncated gradient methods), weakening positioning within related work.  
- High significance: fills an important gap by providing the first unified framework for optimal estimation and inference in high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Strong technical quality: rigorous proofs and comprehensive conditions, though theoretical sections could be made more accessible.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution worthy of acceptance, contingent on addressing the methodological necessity, experimental, and related-work weaknesses.
