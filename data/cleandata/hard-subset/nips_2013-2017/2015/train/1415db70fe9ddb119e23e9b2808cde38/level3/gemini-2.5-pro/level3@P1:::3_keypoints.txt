- Proposes a high-dimensional EM algorithm with a truncation (T-) step after each M-step to enforce sparsity and a decorrelated score statistic for inference on low-dimensional components.
- Proves geometric convergence of the algorithm under regularity and suitable initialization.
- Shows the final estimator achieves near-minimax error rate ~ sqrt(s log d / n).
- Establishes asymptotic normality of the decorrelated score and that its variance attains the semiparametric information bound.
- Demonstrates the framework on high-dimensional Gaussian mixture and mixture of regressions models.
- Addresses the important open problem of theoretical guarantees for EM in the high-dimensional (d >> n) regime.
- Novel algorithmic idea: combining EM with iterative hard-thresholding; technically rigorous analysis controlling support evolution and entrywise errors.
- Provides a unified framework that goes beyond estimation to enable valid uncertainty quantification (p-values, CIs) for non-convex high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Questioned necessity and advantage of truncating at every iteration; unclear if single final thresholding or L1-regularized EM could match guarantees.
- Analysis relies on a new technical condition (Condition 3.3) that only holds for sparse inputs, raising motivation and practicality concerns for iterative truncation.
- Does not sufficiently acknowledge prior work on iterative hard-thresholding and truncated-gradient methods (e.g., Langford et al., Blumensath & Davies).
- Requires a good initialization inside a basin of attraction; paper should clarify practical initialization methods used (e.g., spectral methods) and discuss obtainability.
- Overall assessment: technically deep, significant contribution with useful unified theory for estimation and inference; recommend acceptance.
- Main weakness: motivation for iterative truncation needs clarification and comparison to simpler alternatives, but concerns are addressable in revision.
