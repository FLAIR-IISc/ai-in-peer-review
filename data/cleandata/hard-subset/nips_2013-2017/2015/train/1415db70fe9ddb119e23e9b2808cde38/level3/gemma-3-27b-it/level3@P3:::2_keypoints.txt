- Proposal: Modified EM algorithm with a truncation step to induce sparsity for high-dimensional latent variable models.  
- Core contribution: Truncation enables convergence rate analysis and asymptotic normality of the estimator.  
- Additional contribution: A novel statistical testing framework (decorrelated score test) for assessing estimator sparsity.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends low-dimensional EM guarantees to high dimensions and contrasts with Lasso-type debiasing and tensor methods.  
- Claimed advantage: Achieves sharper statistical rates than tensor-based approaches.  
- Strength: Well-organized manuscript with comprehensive related work and a unified framework applicable to multiple latent variable models.  
- Strength: Detailed theoretical analysis for Gaussian mixture and mixture of regression models.  
- Strength: Derivation of the decorrelated score test is a valuable inferential addition.  
- Weakness: Paper is technically dense; Sections 3–4 are hard to follow and notation is complex.  
- Weakness: Numerous technical conditions feel overwhelming and lack guidance on practical implications.  
- Concern: Minimax-optimal rate claim lacks a formal proof or explicit citation in the main text.  
- Concern: Practical selection of the estimated sparsity level (s-hat) is not discussed; no heuristics or adaptive methods provided.  
- Concern: Obtaining required initialization (beta_init within R/2 of truth) seems impractical; sensitivity and guidance are missing.  
- Concern: Role and necessity of the truncation step are not fully explained; no empirical comparison with/without truncation.  
- Overall assessment: Solid theoretical contribution but limited accessibility and immediate practical usefulness; major revision needed for clarity and practical guidance.  
- Recommendation: Initial score 6 raised to 7 after rebuttal; acceptance contingent on addressing clarity, tuning guidance, and truncation explanation.
