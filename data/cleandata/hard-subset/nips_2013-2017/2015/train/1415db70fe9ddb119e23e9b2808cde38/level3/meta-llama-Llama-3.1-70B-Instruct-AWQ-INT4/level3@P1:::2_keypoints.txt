- Proposes a novel high-dimensional EM algorithm that incorporates sparsity for latent variable models.  
- Establishes an inferential procedure for testing low-dimensional components of high-dimensional parameters.  
- Provides a general theory with simultaneous statistical and computational guarantees for the proposed method.  
- Paper is well-organized and related work is sufficiently summarized.  
- Modified EM algorithm has conditions guaranteeing convergence to the global solution and derived convergence rates.  
- Constructs a statistical testing framework to assess estimator sparsity.  
- Sections 3 and 4 are very dense and hard to follow; need simplification.  
- Claim that sqrt(s * log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal is not clearly proven in the main manuscript.  
- Practicality concerns: Eq.(3.8) uses an estimated sparsity ^s that depends on unknown s, unclear how to choose in practice.  
- Initialization issue: unclear how to obtain beta^init satisfying ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 in practice.  
- Role and necessity of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 are not clearly explained; consequences of omitting it are unclear.  
- Notation and terminology may be unfamiliar; paper would benefit from more intuitive explanations and examples.  
- Topic and contributions are relevant to the NIPS community (high-dimensional, sparse estimation, EM theory).  
- Overall balance: novel algorithm and theory vs. dense presentation and practical gaps.  
- Reviewer score: 7/10 — promising contribution but needs clarity and practical guidance.
