- Proposes a modified EM algorithm for high-dimensional sparse parameter estimation with convergence guarantees and statistical rate O(√(s log d / n)).
- Develops a statistical test framework for assessing estimator sparsity and testing low-dimensional components.
- Paper is well-organized with comprehensive related work coverage and addresses gaps in high-dimensional latent variable models.
- Strong theoretical contribution: first computationally feasible approach for optimal estimation and asymptotic inference in high-dimensional EM settings.
- Modified algorithm includes a truncation step to enforce sparsity, with convergence guarantees under appropriate conditions.
- The statistical test for low-dimensional components is a notable advance for latent variable models.
- Significant clarity issues: Sections 3 and 4 are exceptionally dense and difficult to follow.
- Claim that √(s log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal lacks a proof in the main manuscript, a serious omission.
- Practical concern: Equation (3.8) uses ĥs that depends on unknown true sparsity s, creating a circular dependency.
- Initialization requirement ||β^init − β*||_2 < R/2 is theoretically convenient but lacks practical guidance.
- Role and necessity of the truncation step in Algorithm 1 are not adequately explained; unclear consequences if omitted.
- Practical utility for machine-learning researchers is questionable due to implementation challenges and need for prior sparsity knowledge.
- Dense presentation limits accessibility; paper would benefit from simplification and clearer implementation details.
- Reviewer scores: high on quality and originality, lower on clarity and significance; overall score raised from 6 to 7 based on theoretical merit.
