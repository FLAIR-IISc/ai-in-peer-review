- Proposal: modified EM algorithm tailored for sparse, high-dimensional parameters with conditions guaranteeing convergence to the global solution and derived convergence rate.
- Additional contribution: construction of a statistical test framework to assess estimator sparsity.
- Strength: paper is well organized and related work is sufficiently summarized.
- Weakness: contents are very dense; Sections 3 and 4 are hard to follow and need simplification for readers.
- Question: claim that sqrt(s * log(d/n)) is minimax-optimal—no proof found in main manuscript; reviewer requests a clear proof.
- Practicality concern: Eq.(3.8) uses ^s that depends on unknown s—how to choose ^s in practice?
- Initialization concern: how to obtain an initial beta^init satisfying ||beta^init - beta*||_2 < R/2 in practice?
- Algorithmic question: role of the truncation step in Algorithm 1—what happens if truncation is omitted?
- Post-rebuttal verdict: reviewer raised score from 6 to 7; views paper as a solid statistics contribution but hard for ML researchers and of questionable practical usefulness.
