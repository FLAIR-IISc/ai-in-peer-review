- Proposes a sparse EM procedure that truncates the parameter vector each step to enforce a desired sparsity.
- Introduces an asymptotically normal, decorrelated score statistic for testing whether a specific parameter entry is non-zero.
- Proves geometric convergence of the sparse-EM estimate to the truth up to a statistical error term scaling with sqrt(sparsity) times the infinity-norm M-step error (typically log-dimensional).
- Assumes the Q-function is smooth and nearly quadratic in a basin of attraction and requires initialization within that basin.
- New technical condition: infinity-norm control on the error in estimating the M operator, required only for sparse vectors.
- Notes that for many models (e.g., Gaussian mixtures, non-gradient mixture of regressions) estimating M at all vectors is as easy as at sparse vectors, calling into question the necessity of truncation.
- Raises the question whether ordinary EM followed by a single truncation could achieve similar guarantees (authors later argue linear regression is a counterexample).
- Suggests citing related sparse optimization work (e.g., Langford et al. 2009 on truncated gradient).
- Experimental section should report what initialization was used.
- Overall: provides important high-dimensional convergence guarantees, but the specific necessity and advantage of the sparse EM variant remain uncertain.
