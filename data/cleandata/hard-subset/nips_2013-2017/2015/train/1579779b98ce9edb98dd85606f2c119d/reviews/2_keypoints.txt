- Paper proposes jointly training all decision-tree parameters (splits and leaf posteriors) via a single optimization with an upper-bound approximation for tractability.
- Well written overall, but contains potential flaws in the theoretical reasoning.
- Derivation is clean, but experiments and their presentation need improvement.
- Investigating joint training for decision trees is an important and original research direction.
- Proposed solution is valuable but has notable shortcomings that affect significance.
- Potential flaw in the empirical loss bound: the argument around l.216f is confusing and an example shows the claimed upper bound may not hold; Prop.1 proof in supplement is incomplete.
- Some leaves receive no data points, which may indicate issues with the objective or initialization; the fixed-assignment heuristic seems suboptimal and could be better motivated (e.g., EM/CCCP).
- Questioned necessity and impact of the efficient loss-augmented inference: depth ≤16 makes brute-force feasible, so the effect of restricting search to a Hamming ball is unclear and needs broader validation.
- Terminology clarification requested: what exactly is meant by “leaves” in the context of the inference discussion.
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a greedy training of non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization.
- Limitation: tree depth must be pre-specified; this should be discussed and evaluated more carefully with experiments varying depth.
- Minor suggestion: replace argmax with max in the loss bound expression.
- Request for additional experiments: runtime comparison and more thorough empirical evaluation; reviewer may adjust score after a clear rebuttal.
