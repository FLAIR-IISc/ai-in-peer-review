- Proposes a global algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions by optimizing an upper bound on empirical loss, regularized with L2 and optimized via SGD.  
- Elegant formulation connecting tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical results are unconvincing: global optimization only significantly outperforms greedy trees on a few datasets and is more computationally intensive.  
- Stable SGD description is unclear—need details on how data points are assigned to leaves and a pseudo-code for the procedure.  
- Clarify Figure 2: state whether results use SGD or stable SGD and define what an "active" leaf means.  
- Algorithm 1 (line 10): explain how Θ is projected onto the simplex in practice and note that this enforces each row sums to one.  
- Minor issues/typos to fix: line 407–408 confusing phrasing about features; replace "argmax" with "max" in Eqs. (7)–(9); Figure 1 never referenced; several corrupted/misspelled sentences (lines 141, 238, 301, 404) and undefined acronym FSGD (line 360).  
- Supplementary material corrections: line 29 sign notation should be "=" not "∈"; line 30 must replace sgn(Wx) usage inside loss with Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)) as indicated.  
- Overall: problem formulation is elegant but experimental evidence does not convincingly support global optimization over greedy methods.
