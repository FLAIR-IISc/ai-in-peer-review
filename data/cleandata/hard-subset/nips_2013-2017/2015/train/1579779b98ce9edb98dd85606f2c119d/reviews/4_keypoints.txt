- Proposes global optimization of decision trees by reformulating as latent structured prediction using sign vectors as latent variables.  
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and approximates it with a differentiable (still non-convex) upper bound.  
- Minimization via SGD requires solving successive loss-augmented inference problems, for which efficient algorithms are provided.  
- Main contributions: casting DT optimization as structured prediction and developing efficient optimization algorithms.  
- The approach is novel, interesting, and raises several follow-up questions.  
- Sparse gradient updates: each SGD step appears to change only one node's weight vector, a property beyond mere computational advantage.  
- This sparsity suggests the possibility of growing an infinitely deep tree from zero weights, letting complexity increase adaptively.  
- It’s unclear whether the method would behave like known greedy strategies or prefer adding nodes over revising existing ones; this needs analysis.  
- Ambiguity about zero-weight nodes (paper implies taking the right branch) is problematic; should instead allow output at the node and define pruning/splitting behavior precisely.  
- Practical concern: plain DTs are used for interpretability, but L2 regularization likely yields dense, non-interpretable splits.  
- L1 regularization might enforce sparsity but could degrade performance; this tradeoff must be tested.  
- Experimental weaknesses: insufficient detail on baselines and pruning strategies for the naive/greedy method.  
- Recommend comparing to additional simple global strategies (e.g., multiple greedy trees on random subsets) as fair baselines.  
- Clarity issues: inference descriptions (Section 6.2 and 6.4) could be summarized/made more explicit.  
- Title mentions forests, but the paper does not address or evaluate forests.  
- Post-rebuttal: zero-weight initialization remains a critical case; random init may hide this, but zero-to-nonzero growth is an interesting direction.  
- Overall: clever and efficient reformulation, but practical usefulness and experimental validation are currently unconvincing.
