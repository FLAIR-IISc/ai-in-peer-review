**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a non-greedy algorithm for learning decision trees, which optimizes the split functions at all levels of the tree jointly with the leaf parameters based on a global objective. The authors establish a link between the decision tree optimization problem and the problem of structured prediction with latent variables, and formulate a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss. They use stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to optimize the surrogate objective, which enables efficient learning of deep trees. The experimental results show that the proposed non-greedy decision trees outperform greedy decision tree baselines on several classification benchmarks.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel formulation**: The paper presents a novel formulation of decision tree learning that associates a binary latent decision variable with each split node in the tree, which enables efficient learning of deep trees.
2. **Theoretical analysis**: The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm, including the derivation of the convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss and the proof of Proposition 1.
3. **Experimental results**: The paper presents extensive experimental results on several classification benchmarks, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed non-greedy decision trees.
4. **Efficient optimization**: The authors propose an efficient optimization algorithm based on SGD, which enables fast training of deep trees.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Computational complexity**: Although the authors propose an efficient optimization algorithm, the computational complexity of the algorithm is still relatively high, especially for deep trees.
2. **Hyper-parameter tuning**: The paper requires tuning of several hyper-parameters, including the regularization constant ν and the learning rate η, which can be time-consuming and may not always lead to optimal results.
3. **Limited comparison**: The paper only compares the proposed non-greedy decision trees with greedy decision tree baselines, and does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art algorithms.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper presents a novel formulation of decision tree learning that enables efficient learning of deep trees. The proposed algorithm is original and significant, as it addresses the limitations of traditional greedy decision tree algorithms and provides a new perspective on decision tree learning.
