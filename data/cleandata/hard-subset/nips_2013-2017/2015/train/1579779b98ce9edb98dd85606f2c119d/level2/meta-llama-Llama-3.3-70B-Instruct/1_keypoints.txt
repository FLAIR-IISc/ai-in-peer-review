Proposes a novel non-greedy global objective to jointly optimize split functions and leaf parameters in decision trees.  
Uses a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling efficient training with stochastic gradient descent.  
Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
Demonstrates improved performance over greedy decision tree baselines on multiple classification benchmarks.  
Paper is well-written with a clear, concise methodological explanation and comprehensive related work.  
Strengths: novel, well-motivated approach; clear methodology; thorough related work; strong experimental results.  
Weakness: optimization is non-convex and may get stuck in local optima.  
Weakness: selection of the regularization parameter ν is crucial but lacks clear guidance.  
Weakness: computational complexity may be high for very deep trees or large datasets, raising scalability concerns.  
Overall: significant contribution with potential to improve decision trees but needs further work on limitations and scalability.  
Arguments for acceptance: novelty, experimental gains, and clarity of presentation.  
Arguments for rejection: non-convexity, unclear ν selection, and potential high computational cost.  
Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations and provide guidance on choosing ν.
