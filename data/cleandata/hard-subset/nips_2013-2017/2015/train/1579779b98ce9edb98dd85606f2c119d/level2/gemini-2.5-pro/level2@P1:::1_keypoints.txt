- Proposes a non-greedy framework that jointly optimizes all oblique split functions and leaf parameters by casting tree learning as structured prediction with latent binary path vectors.  
- Derives a convex–concave upper bound as a surrogate objective for the tree's empirical loss.  
- Identifies naive optimization as exponential in depth and contributes a "fast loss-augmented inference" algorithm reducing per-SGD-update complexity to quadratic in tree depth.  
- Empirical results on multiple benchmarks show consistent improvement over greedy baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, greedy CO2), with better generalization and reduced overfitting for deeper trees.  
- Technical quality is high: the formulation is elegant, grounded in structured SVM literature, and the surrogate bound derivation is sound.  
- The fast inference algorithm is a crucial practical contribution that makes non-greedy training scalable.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized; figures (e.g., Fig.2, Fig.4) effectively illustrate performance gains and computational benefits.  
- Highly original: reframes decision tree learning as latent structured prediction, opening new optimization avenues.  
- Potentially significant impact: could change how trees and ensembles are trained, improving performance or reducing ensemble size.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, practical SGD-based algorithm with fast inference, strong empirical validation, and excellent exposition.  
- Weakness: method requires initialization from a greedily trained tree and lacks analysis of sensitivity to poor initializations.  
- Weakness: the "Stable SGD" (SSGD) variant is somewhat ad-hoc; needs deeper analysis or a more principled regularization-based alternative and clearer SGD vs SSGD comparisons.  
- Weakness: experiments focus on single trees; discussion or preliminary experiments on integration with ensembles (Random Forests, boosting) would increase practical impact.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths (novelty, technical quality, significance) outweigh minor weaknesses; strongly recommend acceptance.
