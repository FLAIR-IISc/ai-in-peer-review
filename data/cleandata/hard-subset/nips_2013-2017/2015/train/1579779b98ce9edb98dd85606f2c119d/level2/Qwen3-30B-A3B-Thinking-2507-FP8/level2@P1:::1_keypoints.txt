- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree learning method that jointly optimizes split functions across all tree levels rather than the standard greedy node-by-node procedure.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables, providing a new theoretical framework.  
- Formulates a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss that enables efficient training via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Key technical contribution: reduces loss-augmented inference complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d² p), making deep tree training practical.  
- Empirical results on multiple classification benchmarks show consistent improvements over greedy baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique).  
- Proposition 1 clarifies how regularization affects the bound’s tightness and supports the theoretical claims.  
- Strength: novel theoretical connection and well-justified upper bound formulation.  
- Strength: efficient O(d² p) algorithm enabling practical deep tree optimization.  
- Strength: consistent empirical gains and general applicability to any loss function.  
- Weakness: limited demonstration of scalability to very large datasets (millions of samples).  
- Weakness: insufficiently detailed comparison to CO2 and other recent non-greedy methods (e.g., Mondrian processes, hierarchical mixture of experts).  
- Weakness: need more systematic analysis of the hyperparameter ν and its effect on tree structure, margin, and generalization.  
- Weakness: some technical passages (especially Section 5 and the proof of Proposition 1) are hard to access and would benefit from more intuitive explanations.  
- Suggested improvements: add large-scale experiments, elaborate on CO2 relation and other non-greedy methods, provide a systematic ν study, and include more intuitive proofs and practical implications.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, original, and significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
