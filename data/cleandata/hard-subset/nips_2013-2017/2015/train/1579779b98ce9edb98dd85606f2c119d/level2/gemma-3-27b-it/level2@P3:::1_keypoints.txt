- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree induction that jointly optimizes all split functions and leaf parameters via a global objective.
- Frames tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables, providing a strong theoretical foundation.
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling efficient optimization.
- Implements optimization with stochastic gradient descent and O(d^2 p) complexity for training reasonably deep trees.
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvement over greedy baselines across multiple benchmark datasets.
- Provides insightful analysis of the regularization parameter ν and its role in tree pruning.
- Includes a clear and detailed complexity analysis and well-written technical exposition.
- Limited discussion and comparison to recent differentiable or fully end-to-end differentiable tree approaches (e.g., softmax/continuous splits).
- Mentions kernelization but lacks practical implementation details or empirical evidence of benefits.
- Experimental section could be strengthened by ablation studies to isolate component contributions.
- Needs more thorough sensitivity analysis for learning rate η and regularization ν.
- Greedy initialization of W is used but not fully justified or explained.
- Details about handling inactive leaves with SSGD and criteria for switching updates are unclear.
- Overall: strong, theoretically grounded, and empirically effective work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the above gaps.
