- Proposes non-greedy training of oblique decision trees by modeling hard splits as latent binary variables and optimizing a joint global objective.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound surrogate optimized by SGD with row-wise norm constraints on split weights.  
- Introduces a fast loss-augmented inference by restricting to a Hamming ball of radius 1, yielding O(d^2 p) per-sample updates.  
- Proposes a Stable SGD (SSGD) variant that fixes leaf assignments for several updates to reduce inactive leaves.  
- Experiments on SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST show single trees trained with this method outperform several greedy baselines and a greedy depth-1 upper-bound.  
- Shows the row-norm regularization parameter ν controls pruning and overfitting.  
- Contrasts with prior greedy induction, oblique trees, and soft hierarchical models; frames the approach as latent structured prediction with margin-rescaling.  
- Strength: provides conceptual clarity by unifying hard-split trees with structured prediction.  
- Strength: technical novelty in the convex-concave bound and efficient Hamming-ball (radius-1) inference for deeper trees.  
- Strength: practical training recipe (SGD + row-norm projection) and an SSGD variant addressing inactive leaves.  
- Strength: preserves fast O(d p) test-time inference unlike soft trees.  
- Weakness: the fast radius-1 bound is looser than the full bound; the optimization gap is not quantified and needs ablation.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees on SGD/SSGD convergence and generalization under the norm constraint.  
- Weakness: missing ablations on SSGD scheduling, initialization sensitivity, and individual component contributions (norm projection, fast inference, SSGD).  
- Weakness: baselines focus on single trees; no evaluation as base learners in ensembles or comparison to the strongest practical single-tree baselines.  
- Weakness: runtime analysis limited to one dataset; broader profiling (d, p, k) and wall-clock vs accuracy curves are needed.  
- Weakness: leaf softmax costs scale with k; no discussion of approximations for large label spaces.  
- Weakness: potential for trivial tightening by scaling W addressed by row-norm constraints, but alternative regularizers (L1, path-wise, annealing ν) are not explored.  
- Quality: methodology and experiments are sound and careful but would benefit from additional ablations and broader baselines.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written; adding pseudo-code for fast inference and explicit SSGD scheduling would improve reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel link between oblique trees and latent structured prediction plus efficient Hamming-ball inference.  
- Significance: practical, scalable non-greedy tree training is useful and potentially impactful for ensembles.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled formulation, efficient algorithm, consistent empirical gains, and clear exposition.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited theoretical characterization of the fast bound, missing ablations, and incomplete runtime/ensemble evaluation.  
- Suggestions: add ablations (full vs fast bound, SSGD schedule, initialization), runtime vs accuracy curves, ensemble/base-learner experiments, and discussion of generalization and alternative regularizers.  
- Overall: solid, timely contribution; lean positive but contingent on clarifying ablations and runtime characterization.
