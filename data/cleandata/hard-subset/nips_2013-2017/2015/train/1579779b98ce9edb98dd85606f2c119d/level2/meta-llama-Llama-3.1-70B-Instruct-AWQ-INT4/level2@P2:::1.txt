This paper proposes a novel algorithm for optimizing the split functions at all levels of a decision tree jointly with the leaf parameters, based on a global objective. The authors establish a link between the decision tree optimization problem and the problem of structured prediction with latent variables, and formulate a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss. They also propose a stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithm to optimize the surrogate objective, which enables efficient training of deep trees.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to decision tree optimization, which addresses the limitations of traditional greedy algorithms.
* The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of the proposed algorithm and its components, including the convex-concave upper bound and the SGD optimization procedure.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in improving the accuracy and generalization performance of decision trees, compared to several baseline methods.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a certain level of familiarity with structured prediction and latent variable models, which may make it difficult for some readers to follow.
* The authors could provide more discussion on the relationship between their approach and other non-greedy decision tree algorithms, such as the one proposed by Bennett (2000).
* The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, including more datasets and baseline methods, as well as a more detailed analysis of the results.
