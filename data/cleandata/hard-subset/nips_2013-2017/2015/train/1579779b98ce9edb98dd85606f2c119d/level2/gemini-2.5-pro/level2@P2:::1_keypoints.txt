- Summary: Reframes decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent path variables, derives a convex-concave surrogate loss optimized by SGD, and uses a Hamming-ball (radius 1) approximation to make loss-augmented inference practical, yielding better test accuracy than greedy baselines.

- Strength 1 (Novelty): Original and insightful formulation connecting global tree optimization to latent structured prediction.

- Strength 2 (Technical/practical): Sound derivation and a crucial fast loss-augmented inference algorithm that reduces complexity from exponential to polynomial, making the method usable for deep trees.

- Strength 3 (Empirical): Thorough experiments show consistent accuracy and generalization gains over axis-aligned and greedy oblique baselines.

- Strength 4 (Clarity): Well-written exposition that clearly motivates the problem and explains the technical contributions.

- Weakness 1 (SSGD details): The Stable SGD variant is described vaguely—no precise criterion given for when leaf assignments are updated, hurting reproducibility.

- Weakness 2 (Initialization sensitivity): No analysis or experiments on sensitivity to initialization (greedy vs random), leaving unclear whether the method merely fine-tunes greedy trees.

- Weakness 3 (Ensemble context): Lacks discussion on integrating non-greedy trees into ensembles (Random Forests/Boosting) and the potential practical impact.

- Weakness 4 (Regularization clarity): Would benefit from explicitly relating W‑norm regularization and the nu parameter to SVM-style margin intuition.

- Conclusion: Strong, principled, and practical contribution with minor requests for clarification; recommends acceptance.
