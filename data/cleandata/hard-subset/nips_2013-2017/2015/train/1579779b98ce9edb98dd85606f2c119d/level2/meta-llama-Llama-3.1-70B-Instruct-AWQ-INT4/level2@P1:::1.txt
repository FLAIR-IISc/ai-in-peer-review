This paper proposes a non-greedy algorithm for learning decision trees, using stochastic gradient descent to optimize an upper bound on the empirical loss of the tree's predictions on the training set. The algorithm addresses the limitation of conventional greedy decision tree induction methods, which optimize the split functions one node at a time. The authors establish a link between the decision tree optimization problem and the problem of structured prediction with latent variables, and propose a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss. The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm on several benchmark datasets, showing that it outperforms greedy decision tree baselines.
