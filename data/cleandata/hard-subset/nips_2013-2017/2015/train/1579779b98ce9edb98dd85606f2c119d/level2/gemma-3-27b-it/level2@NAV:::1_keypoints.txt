- Addresses greedy decision tree limitation by jointly optimizing split functions using a structured-prediction-with-latent-variables formulation and a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Core technical contribution: reduces gradient computation to O(d), enabling training of deep trees with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Insightful connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction, providing a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Convex-concave upper bound and gradient-complexity reduction are significant technical achievements.  
- Empirical results on benchmark datasets show the non-greedy approach outperforms several greedy baselines (including CO2).  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written.  
- Needs a more intuitive explanation of why the structured-prediction connection enables efficient optimization.  
- Explanation and justification of Proposition 1 and the regularization term feel hand-wavy and require expansion.  
- Experiments lack detailed ablation studies on the regularization parameter (ν) and the effectiveness of the Stable SGD (SSGD) variant.  
- Limited discussion of potential limitations and directions for future work beyond the brief extensions mentioned.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve theoretical clarity and add ablation analysis.
