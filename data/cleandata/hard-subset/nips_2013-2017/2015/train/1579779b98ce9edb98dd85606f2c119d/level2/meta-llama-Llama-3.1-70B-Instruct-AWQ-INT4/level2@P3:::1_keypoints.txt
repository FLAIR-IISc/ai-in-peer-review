- Summary: Proposes a non-greedy decision-tree algorithm that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters via a latent-variable structured-prediction view and a convex-concave upper bound, trained with SGD and outperforming greedy baselines.  
- Strength: Novel formulation associating binary latent variables with split nodes enabling efficient deep-tree learning.  
- Strength: Thorough theoretical analysis, including derivation of the convex-concave bound and proof of Proposition 1.  
- Strength: Extensive experimental results demonstrating effectiveness on several classification benchmarks.  
- Strength: Efficient SGD-based optimization enabling fast training of deep trees.  
- Weakness: Computational complexity remains relatively high, especially for deep trees.  
- Weakness: Requires tuning multiple hyperparameters (e.g., regularization ν and learning rate η), which is time-consuming.  
- Weakness: Limited empirical comparison—only compared to greedy decision-tree baselines, not other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Originality/Significance: Original and significant contribution addressing limitations of greedy decision-tree algorithms and offering a new perspective.  
- Quality: Well-written, well-organized, with clear algorithmic and theoretical exposition.  
- Clarity/Presentation: Clear structure, concise writing, and helpful figures/tables.  
- Pro acceptance: Novel, significant approach with strong experimental support and effective training for deep trees.  
- Con acceptance: High computational cost, hyperparameter tuning burden, and incomplete comparison to SOTA.  
- Recommendation: Recommend accept, with suggestions to reduce computational cost and provide broader comparisons.
