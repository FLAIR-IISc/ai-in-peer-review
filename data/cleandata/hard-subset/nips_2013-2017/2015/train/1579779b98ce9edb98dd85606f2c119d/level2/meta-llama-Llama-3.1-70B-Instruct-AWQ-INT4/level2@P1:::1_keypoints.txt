- Proposes a novel non-greedy algorithm for learning decision trees using SGD to optimize a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Addresses limitation of conventional greedy decision-tree induction by jointly optimizing splits rather than node-by-node.  
- Establishes a significant link between decision-tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Algorithm is well-motivated and supported by clear mathematical formulation.  
- Empirically outperforms greedy decision-tree baselines on several benchmark datasets.  
- Paper is technically sound and demonstrates effectiveness on multiple datasets.  
- Writing is clear but assumes familiarity with structured prediction and latent variables, which may limit accessibility.  
- Algorithm complexity and limited intuition/explanations make it harder to understand why it works.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion of algorithmic limitations and potential directions for future work.  
- Original contribution: novel algorithm + conceptual connection to structured prediction.  
- Significance: potential for wide practical use due to improved performance over greedy methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical link, and empirical improvement over baselines.  
- Arguments for rejection: high technical barrier for readers, algorithm complexity, and insufficient discussion of limitations/future work.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
