- Proposal: non-greedy training for oblique decision trees via latent structured prediction and a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Surrogate: bound supports general losses and is optimized with (stochastic) gradient descent under per-node norm constraints.  
- Inference: efficient loss-augmented inference via radius-1 Hamming-ball reduces latent search and yields O(d^2 p + k) per SGD step.  
- Practical methods: initialization via greedy/CO2 and a “Stable SGD” schedule to prevent leaf collapse.  
- Empirics: experiments on SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST show consistent accuracy and scalability gains over greedy axis-aligned trees, OC1, random oblique trees, and CO2.  
- Prior work: builds on ID3/CART, OC1, max-margin trees and links decision trees to latent structured SVMs; contrasts with soft-split methods (HME); CO2 is a local comparator.  
- Technical soundness: formulation, bounds, norm constraints, and complexity analysis are well-motivated; non-convexity acknowledged and handled with reasonable algorithmic choices.  
- Empirical support strengths: consistent test accuracy gains, demonstrated runtime scaling, and reduced overfitting via norm constraints.  
- Missing analyses: no quantitative comparison of radius-1 approximation vs exact loss-augmented inference on accuracy.  
- Missing ablations: no thorough experiments isolating SSGD vs SGD, initialization effects, or sensitivity to ν.  
- Missing baselines: no empirical comparison to margin-based (SVM-at-nodes) or soft decision-tree (HME) methods.  
- Clarity: generally well written with clear algorithms; notation is heavy but consistent; minor presentation issues exist.  
- Originality: novel combination of global latent-structured objective, tractable Hamming-ball bound for inference, and practical SGD/SSGD training; structured-prediction link is elegant.  
- Significance: improving single-tree induction non-greedily is impactful for efficiency, interpretability, and as base learners in ensembles.  
- Pros: principled reformulation, efficient deep oblique tree training (O(d^2 p)), consistent empirical gains, generality to arbitrary losses and extensibility.  
- Cons: lack of empirical exact-vs-approx inference comparison, limited ablations, single-tree focus (no ensembles), missing baselines, and sparse reproducibility details/no code.  
- Questions for authors: impact of fast vs exact bound on accuracy; SSGD effects and ν sensitivity; initialization sensitivity; ensemble integration; bias regularization and feature scaling; regression experiment.  
- Suggestions: add ablations (fast vs exact, SSGD vs SGD, initializations), include at least one ensemble experiment, compare to margin-based and soft-split baselines, provide implementation details and release code, and discuss theoretical/generalization implications of norm constraints.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated contribution that advances non-greedy tree learning; with broader experiments and clarity on the fast bound’s impact the paper merits acceptance (reviewer leans toward accept).
