- Introduces a novel non-greedy framework for learning decision trees by jointly optimizing split functions and leaf parameters.
- Formulates tree learning as a structured prediction problem with latent variables.
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a tractable surrogate objective.
- Optimizes the surrogate objective with stochastic gradient descent.
- Develops a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm that reduces gradient-update complexity from exponential to polynomial in tree depth.
- Empirically shows improved generalization and reduced overfitting on multiple benchmark classification datasets versus greedy methods.
- Original and significant contribution, offering a principled alternative to decades of greedy tree induction.
- Technical work is sound, with a well-executed surrogate derivation and efficient optimization algorithm.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivating the problem and presenting theory and experiments.
- Experiments are limited to single trees; evaluation as base learners in ensembles (e.g., Random Forests) is missing.
- Method is initialized from a greedy tree; sensitivity to initialization lacks ablation or discussion.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, compelling work likely to inspire further research; strong recommendation for acceptance.
