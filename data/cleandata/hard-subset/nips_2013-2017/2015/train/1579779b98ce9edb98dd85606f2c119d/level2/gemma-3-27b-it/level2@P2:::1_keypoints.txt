- Novel formulation: casts decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables, enabling non-greedy global optimization.  
- Theoretical foundation: derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and reduces gradient complexity, facilitating deeper trees.  
- Optimization method: joint optimization of split functions and leaf parameters via the bound and SGD.  
- Empirical performance: non-greedy trees consistently outperform greedy baselines on several benchmarks.  
- Regularization analysis: provides insight into the effect of the regularization parameter ν.  
- Writing and presentation: generally clear exposition and good connection to structured prediction literature.  
- Practical algorithmic contributions: addresses computational complexity and proposes a fast loss-augmented inference routine.  
- Stable SGD (SSGD): introduces SSGD to address optimization challenges but implementation and benefits need clearer exposition.  
- Missing related work: lacks comparison to recent differentiable/continuous-relaxation tree methods (e.g., Popescu et al. 2017, Fathi et al. 2018).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance depends on ν and the paper lacks a systematic hyperparameter sensitivity study.  
- Scalability concerns: scalability to very large datasets (millions of samples) is not convincingly demonstrated; runtime eval is limited.  
- SSGD evaluation gap: needs clearer motivation and empirical comparison of SGD vs SSGD convergence and performance.  
- Loss generality untested: claim of handling arbitrary losses is unsupported by experiments beyond cross-entropy.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and potentially significant, but weakened by related-work gaps, hyperparameter sensitivity, and limited scalability evidence.
