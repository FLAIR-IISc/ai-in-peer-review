- Proposes a novel algorithm that jointly optimizes split functions at all tree levels and leaf parameters using a global objective.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss as a surrogate objective.  
- Introduces an SGD algorithm to optimize the surrogate, enabling efficient training of deep trees.  
- Experimental results show improved accuracy and generalization versus several baselines.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly explained, and technically sound.  
- Clear exposition of algorithm components, mathematical derivations, and optimization procedure.  
- Includes helpful figures and tables that illustrate the method and results.  
- Assumes reader familiarity with structured prediction and latent-variable models, which may hinder accessibility.  
- Lacks deeper discussion comparing to other non-greedy decision tree methods (e.g., Bennett 2000).  
- Experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive (more datasets, baselines, and detailed analysis).  
- Original combination of structured prediction and latent-variable ideas applied to tree optimization.  
- Addresses an important problem with potential applications across domains (vision, NLP).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; overall rating 8/10.
