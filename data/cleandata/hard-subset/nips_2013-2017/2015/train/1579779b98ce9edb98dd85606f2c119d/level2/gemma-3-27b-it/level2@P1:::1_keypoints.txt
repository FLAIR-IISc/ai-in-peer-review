- Proposes a novel non-greedy method to jointly optimize split functions and leaf parameters for decision trees under a global objective.  
- Connects decision-tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables and derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Introduces an algorithm enabling efficient SGD training for deep trees by reducing gradient computation complexity to O(d).  
- Empirical results on benchmarks show the method outperforms greedy tree baselines and the CO2 comparison isolates non-greedy benefits.  
- Training-time comparisons demonstrate practical efficiency gains from the proposed complexity reduction.  
- Well-motivated work that addresses a key limitation of greedy decision tree induction; the structured-prediction connection is insightful.  
- The claimed complexity reduction from O(2dp) to O(d2p) is crucial for scaling to deeper trees.  
- Paper needs clearer motivation and intuition for the hard norm constraints on W and how they prevent overfitting.  
- The Stable SGD (SSGD) algorithm is described briefly; implementation details and rationale (e.g., how long assignments are fixed) are missing.  
- Lacks a thorough ablation study comparing SGD and SSGD across configurations to substantiate SSGD’s effectiveness.  
- Related work is comprehensive but should discuss recent advances (differentiable trees, neural decision forests, regularization/split-criteria techniques).  
- Potential extensions (kernel trick, sparsity regularization) are mentioned only briefly and need further elaboration.  
- Some equations involving latent variables and the upper bound are not presented as clearly as they could be; Fig.1’s tree navigation would benefit from a visual illustration.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong novelty, theoretical soundness, improved efficiency, solid empirical gains, and broad significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: technical density may limit accessibility, limited ablation/evaluation of components, and incomplete related-work context.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand regularization explanation, provide SSGD details, and strengthen the related-work discussion.
