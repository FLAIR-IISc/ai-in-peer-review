- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision-tree training method by formulating tree learning as latent structured prediction.  
- Frames optimization with a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective for joint optimization of splits and leaves.  
- Introduces an efficient "fast loss-augmented inference" that reduces SGD update complexity from exponential to polynomial (quadratic) in tree depth.  
- Empirically outperforms greedy baselines (axis-aligned and oblique) across several benchmark classification tasks.  
- High technical quality: sound formulation, correct application of loss-augmented inference, and complete end-to-end work.  
- Experimental claims are well-supported, including ablation against a greedy CO2 variant and empirical validation of complexity gains.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized with consistent notation and effective figures.  
- Presentation could clearer summarize trade-offs between the different upper bounds and specify which bound/algorithm produced main results.  
- Highly original: novel connection between decision-tree induction and latent structured prediction, distinguishing it from prior heuristic or intractable non-greedy methods.  
- Significant potential impact: makes practical global non-greedy optimization for deep trees and could influence ensemble/base-learner design.  
- Strength: principled global formulation that moves beyond greedy heuristics.  
- Strength: scalable, practical algorithm enabling deep non-greedy trees.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains and clear isolation of non-greedy benefit via CO2 comparison.  
- Weakness: relies on greedy initialization—needs analysis of sensitivity to initialization and whether optimization finds qualitatively different trees.  
- Weakness: SSGD variant is heuristic—requires deeper characterization and sensitivity analysis of its scheduling/behavior.  
- Weakness/Suggestion: experiments focus on single trees; a brief discussion or small-scale ensemble experiments (e.g., Random Forests) would strengthen practical impact.
