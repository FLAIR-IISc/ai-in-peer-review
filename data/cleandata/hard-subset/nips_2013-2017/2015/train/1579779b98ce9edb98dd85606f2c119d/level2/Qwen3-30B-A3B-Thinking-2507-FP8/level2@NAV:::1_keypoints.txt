- Proposes a non-greedy method that jointly optimizes split functions across all tree levels with a global objective instead of greedy one-node-at-a-time training.  
- Establishes a novel connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss that can be optimized with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Key technical achievement: reduces computational complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), enabling feasible training of deep trees.  
- Experiments on multiple classification benchmarks show non-greedy trees consistently outperform greedy baselines in test accuracy and generalization.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with rigorous proofs of the upper bound and complexity analysis.  
- Insightful, well-motivated connection to structured prediction and clear algorithmic presentation.  
- Practical implementation details provided, including a Stable SGD variant to handle inactive leaves.  
- Limitation: claims applicability to general loss functions but experiments only use classification/log loss.  
- Limitation: insufficient analysis of sensitivity to the regularization parameter ν and its effect on tree structure/performance.  
- Comparison to CO2 is limited, and the paper lacks direct comparison to other non-greedy methods (e.g., Bennett).  
- Verdict: technically sound, reproducible, and practically useful—recommended for acceptance; suggests expanding experiments and deeper analysis of regularization impact.
