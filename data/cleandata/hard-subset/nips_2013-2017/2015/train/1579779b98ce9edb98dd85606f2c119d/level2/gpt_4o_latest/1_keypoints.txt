- Introduces a novel, non-greedy global optimization formulation for decision tree learning tied to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Proposes a convex-concave surrogate objective and reduces gradient computation from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), enabling scalable training.  
- Uses SGD to train deep trees efficiently on large datasets.  
- Experiments show consistent superior generalization and reduced overfitting versus greedy baselines across multiple benchmarks and depths.  
- Method is practically relevant for large-scale and deep-tree applications.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear methodology, theory, and experimental setup.  
- Still incurs higher computational cost than greedy methods, especially for shallow trees or small datasets.  
- Experiments focus on single-tree performance; lacks comparisons within ensemble frameworks (random forests, boosting).  
- Performance depends on tuned hyperparameters (regularization ν and learning rate η), raising usability concerns.  
- Insufficient implementation details or released code, which may hinder reproducibility.  
- Suggested improvements: add ensemble experiments, analyze hyperparameter sensitivity or adaptive tuning, and release implementation/code.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address reproducibility and hyperparameter issues.
