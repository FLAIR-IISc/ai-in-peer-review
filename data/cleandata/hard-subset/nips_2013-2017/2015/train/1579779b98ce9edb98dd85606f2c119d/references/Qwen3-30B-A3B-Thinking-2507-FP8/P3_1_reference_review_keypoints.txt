- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree training framework by casting tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables and optimizing a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Key technical contribution reduces non-greedy training complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d² p) via a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm, making deep non-greedy trees feasible.  
- Demonstrates strong theoretical grounding: derivation of the upper bound, connection to structured prediction, and insights about bound tightness (Proposition 1).  
- Presents thorough experiments comparing to strong baselines (OC1, random oblique trees, CO2) across multiple datasets, showing consistent generalization improvements.  
- Introduces practical implementation details (Stable SGD) to handle inactive leaves and other real-world issues.  
- Complexity claim needs nuance: the O(d² p) analysis is superficial and, with d = O(log n), effectively yields O(p log² n); this clarification is missing.  
- Fails to adequately compare with the more recent Co2 Forest (Norouzi et al., 2015), leaving the relationship to CO2 extensions unclear.  
- Does not address whether SGD converges to a global optimum; limitations of local optimization for the non-convex problem are not fully discussed.  
- Scalability concerns: experiments use moderate-sized datasets (e.g., 55K), and the paper lacks analysis of scaling to very large datasets and mini-batch SGD costs.  
- Overall quality: technically sound with rigorous derivations and well-supported experimental claims.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized, though some technical details (complexity analysis) need clarification.  
- Originality: novel framing and an efficient algorithm for deep non-greedy trees, meaningfully advancing prior work.  
- Significance: enables practical deep non-greedy tree learning with potential impact on applications requiring compact, high-accuracy trees.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant technical contribution with minor fixable issues (complexity clarification and stronger related-work comparison).
