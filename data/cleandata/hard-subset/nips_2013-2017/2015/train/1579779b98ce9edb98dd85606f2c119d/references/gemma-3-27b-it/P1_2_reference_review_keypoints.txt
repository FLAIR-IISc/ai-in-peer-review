- Proposes non-greedy decision tree learning via structured prediction with latent variables and a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Optimizes the bound with SGD and reduces computational complexity from O(2^{dp}) to O(d^{2}p) for deep trees.  
- Introduces a norm constraint on split weights (W) as principled regularization tied to bound tightness.  
- Empirical results show consistent test accuracy improvements over greedy baselines and CO2 across multiple datasets.  
- Correctly positions the method relative to prior work (Bennett, Mondrian processes, soft-splits, max-margin methods) and cites relevant literature.  
- Establishes a clear connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction frameworks.  
- Strength: novel theoretical framing enabling joint (non-greedy) optimization of tree parameters.  
- Strength: substantial computational efficiency gains that make non-greedy optimization practical for deeper trees.  
- Strength: principled regularization to prevent overfitting and encourage compact trees.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation of technical content.  
- Weakness: actual implementation appears complex (SGD with projection and latent-variable handling) and engineering effort is underreported.  
- Weakness: reliance on greedy (CO2) initialization raises concerns about initialization bias and sensitivity.  
- Weakness: brief discussion of hyperparameter tuning (ν and η); lacks validation strategy and sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: limited ablation study—insufficient isolation of contributions from components like norm regularization and fast loss-augmented inference.  
- Weakness: practical scalability concerns remain because O(d^{2}p) can be a bottleneck for very high-dimensional features or very deep trees.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach with significant efficiency improvements and strong empirical performance.  
- Argument against acceptance: implementation complexity, initialization bias, and insufficient hyperparameter detail may limit adoption.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper; recommended Accept with minor revisions.  
- Revision request: provide more implementation details and engineering considerations.  
- Revision request: investigate sensitivity to different initialization strategies.  
- Revision request: expand hyperparameter tuning details and validation strategy.  
- Revision request: include ablation studies to isolate component effects.  
- Revision request: discuss scalability limitations and possible mitigations (e.g., feature selection).
