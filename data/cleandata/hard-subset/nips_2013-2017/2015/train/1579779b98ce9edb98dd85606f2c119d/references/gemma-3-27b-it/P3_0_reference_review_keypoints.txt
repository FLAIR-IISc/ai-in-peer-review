- Novel formulation: casts decision tree learning as a structured prediction problem with latent variables, enabling joint optimization of split functions.  
- Optimization technique: derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Complexity reduction: achieves a key gradient-computation speedup (from O(2dp) to O(d2p)), aiding scalability to deeper trees.  
- Empirical results: shows consistent test-accuracy improvements over greedy baselines and CO2 across multiple benchmarks; includes an ablation on regularization ν.  
- Relation to prior work: correctly situates the method relative to NP-hard optimal tree construction and connects to structured prediction and CO2 literature.  
- Strength — novelty: the structured-prediction framing is theoretically sound and brings new optimization tools to trees.  
- Strength — scalability: the gradient-computation reduction is a significant practical contribution.  
- Strength — clarity and experiments: clear writing, understandable derivations, and strong empirical validation.  
- Weakness — implementation: the fast loss-augmented inference and overall implementation appear complex and under-detailed.  
- Weakness — ensembles: lacks direct comparison to strong ensemble methods (e.g., well-tuned Random Forests), limiting practical evaluation.  
- Weakness — hyperparameters: performance is sensitive to regularization ν and needs more guidance on selection and dataset variability.  
- Weakness — model class: split functions are linear thresholds; extensions to kernels are discussed only at a high level.  
- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically grounded contribution with promising results but practical adoption concerns.  
- Recommendation to authors: add implementation details and comparisons to ensemble methods in the final version.  
- Reviewer decision: Strong Accept.
