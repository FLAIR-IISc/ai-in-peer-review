- Proposes non-greedy decision tree learning by formulating it as structured prediction with latent variables and optimizing a convex-concave upper bound via SGD.  
- Key technical contribution: reduces loss-augmented inference from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p) using a Hamming ball constraint.  
- Empirically outperforms greedy baselines on multiple benchmark datasets while retaining tree-like computational efficiency.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical foundation, including a proof of the upper bound and analysis of how regularization parameter ν affects solutions and implicit pruning.  
- Thorough complexity analysis and clear algorithm description (Algorithm 1); supplementary material supports reproducibility.  
- Conceptually novel: links decision tree learning to structured prediction and offers a general non-greedy optimization framework (CO2 is a greedy special case).  
- Framework is flexible (handles arbitrary loss functions) and suggests promising extensions (sparse learning, kernels).  
- Experimental comparisons omit some recent non-greedy baselines (e.g., Mondrian Forests, Hierarchical Mixture of Experts).  
- Lacks thorough exploration of hyperparameter (ν) sensitivity and its effect on performance/pruning.  
- Scalability not empirically validated on very large datasets (beyond ~55k examples), despite claims of larger speedups.  
- Theoretical treatment focuses on binary trees and does not analyze complexity scaling or provide a clear path for N-ary trees.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, well-written, significant contribution; recommendation: Accept.
