- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree learning framework using structured prediction with latent variables and a convex-concave surrogate loss optimized by SGD.
- Key technical contribution: reduces computational complexity from O(2^{d p}) to O(d^2 p) via a "fast loss-augmented inference" algorithm, enabling deeper trees.
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over greedy baselines and the CO2 greedy approach on benchmark datasets.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized; surrogate objective and optimization are explained clearly.
- Provides practical insight on regularization (norm of W) and guidance for tuning the regularization parameter ν.
- Limited novelty within structured prediction: techniques used (latent variables, convex-concave bounds, SGD) are established elsewhere.
- Complexity reduction caveat: relies on the fast inference algorithm and the paper doesn’t fully discuss hidden constant factors or practical performance trade-offs.
- Comparisons omit state-of-the-art tree/ensemble methods (e.g., XGBoost, LightGBM), weakening claims of practical significance.
- Results appear sensitive to the regularization parameter ν; robustness across hyperparameter settings is not fully explored.
- Initialization dependence: uses CO2 greedy initialization without investigating alternative initialization strategies or their effects.
- Quality score 8/10: technically sound with valuable complexity reduction but not groundbreaking.
- Clarity score 9/10: explanations and presentation are strong.
- Originality score 7/10: novel application to decision trees but core methods are not new.
- Significance score 7/10: meaningful potential but limited by baseline comparisons and tuning needs.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a longstanding limitation, is theoretically sound, computationally efficient, shows empirical gains, and is well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: not a major advance in structured prediction, lacks SOTA comparisons, and exhibits hyperparameter/complexity concerns.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions and requests to compare against stronger baselines, discuss practical implications of the complexity simplification, and expand on hyperparameter robustness.
- Suggested future directions: explore sparsity-inducing regularization, kernel methods, and alternative initialization strategies.
