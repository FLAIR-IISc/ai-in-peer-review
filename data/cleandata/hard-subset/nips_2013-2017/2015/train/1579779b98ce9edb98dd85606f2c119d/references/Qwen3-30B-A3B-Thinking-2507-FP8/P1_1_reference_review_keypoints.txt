- Summary: Proposes a non-greedy decision tree framework framing tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables, using a convex-concave upper bound and SGD, and reducing loss-augmented inference from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p) via a Hamming-1 search; shows improved generalization vs greedy baselines.

- Strength — Technical contribution: Introduces an efficient O(d^2 p) loss-augmented inference algorithm that makes non-greedy learning practical for deeper trees.

- Strength — Novel formulation: Connects decision tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables, enabling a principled convex-concave optimization approach.

- Strength — Experimental validation: Empirical results show consistent generalization improvements and reduced overfitting across multiple datasets and tree depths.

- Strength — Presentation: Paper is well-organized with clear exposition and useful SGD pseudocode.

- Weakness — Theoretical guarantees: Lacks convergence and generalization guarantees for the proposed optimization beyond the presented upper bound.

- Weakness — Ablation study: Insufficient analysis of the effect of the regularization parameter ν across datasets.

- Weakness — Inference speed: No comparison of inference-time speed with greedy trees, an important practical metric for deployment.

- Weakness — Related work comparison: Does not compare against some recent non-greedy tree methods, limiting contextual evaluation.

- Quality: Technically sound with rigorous derivations and correct complexity analysis, but would benefit from deeper theoretical analysis of the optimization.

- Clarity: Generally clear and reproducible, though the structured-prediction section may be hard for readers unfamiliar with the area and the latent-variable/tree relationship could be clearer.

- Originality: Makes a meaningful and novel contribution—especially the O(d^2 p) algorithm—and offers a fresh structured-prediction perspective on non-greedy trees.

- Significance: Has strong practical implications due to improved generalization and opens directions like sparsity-inducing regularization or kernels.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance; strengths and empirical results outweigh the noted weaknesses.
