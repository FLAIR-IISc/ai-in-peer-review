- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree framework linking tree learning to structured prediction with latent binary split variables and optimizes a convex-concave upper bound via SGD.  
- Key technical contribution: reduces loss-augmented inference complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d² p), making non-greedy optimization practical for deeper trees.  
- Introduces Stable SGD (SSGD) to address inactive leaves and improve robustness of training.  
- Empirical results: non-greedy trees consistently outperform greedy baselines on multiple datasets (SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST) across depths.  
- Theoretical foundation: solid connection to latent-structured prediction with a well-justified convex-concave bound and clear proofs.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized with precise notation and thorough algorithm description.  
- Lacks comparison to more recent non-greedy/deep tree methods (e.g., neural decision forests), weakening significance claims.  
- Does not provide convergence guarantees for the optimization procedure or theoretical generalization bounds versus greedy methods.  
- The O(d² p) complexity claim needs further detailed justification and explanation.  
- Scalability concerns: experiments use moderate-sized datasets and do not demonstrate behavior or bottlenecks on very large-scale data.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (ν and η) is not thoroughly analyzed across datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant, scalable technical contribution with strong experiments and clear writing.  
- Suggested improvements: clarify fast inference complexity, add comparisons to recent methods, analyze ν’s effect on active leaves, discuss large-scale scalability with runtimes, and include hyperparameter sensitivity studies.  
- Final assessment: strong, elegant, and practical contribution likely to influence interpretable and tree-based ML research.
