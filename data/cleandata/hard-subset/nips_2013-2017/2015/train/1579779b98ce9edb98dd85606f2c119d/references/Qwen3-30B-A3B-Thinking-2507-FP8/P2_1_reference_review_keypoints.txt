- Proposes a non-greedy global optimization for decision trees that jointly optimizes split functions under a global objective.  
- Establishes a novel connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Formulates learning as optimizing a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss (equation 7) and proves its validity.  
- Key technical contribution: reduces optimization complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d² p), while maintaining O(d p) inference complexity.  
- Demonstrates improved generalization over multiple greedy baselines across several datasets.  
- Theoretical framework is rigorous with detailed complexity analysis and supporting proofs in the supplement.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, with careful hyperparameter tuning (notably regularizer ν) and comparisons to relevant baselines.  
- Provides clear algorithm descriptions (Algorithm 1) and implementation details sufficient for reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; the structured-prediction connection and technical contributions are well-explained.  
- Original contributions include the convex–concave formulation and the polynomial complexity reduction, distinguishing it from prior work.  
- Significance is high given decision trees’ widespread use and the method’s applicability to any loss, classification and regression.  
- Suggests promising extensions (sparse split functions, kernel methods) that could broaden applicability.  
- Scalability to extremely large datasets remains insufficiently addressed beyond shown benchmarks.  
- Comparison to Bennett’s earlier global tree optimization could be more detailed to clarify distinctions.  
- Practical impacts on inference speed and memory requirements are not discussed and should be evaluated.  
- The "stable SGD" variant lacks clear motivation and explanation compared to standard SGD.  
- Recommendation: accept — the paper is a substantial, well-validated, and potentially impactful advance in decision tree learning.
