This paper proposes a non-greedy approach to learning decision trees, framing the problem as structured prediction with latent variables. The core idea is to establish a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss and optimize it using stochastic gradient descent (SGD). A key technical contribution lies in reducing the computational complexity from O(2dp) to O(d2p), enabling the training of deeper trees. The authors demonstrate improvements over greedy baselines on several benchmark datasets.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Formulation:** The connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables is a significant contribution, offering a new perspective on a classic problem.
* **Efficiency Improvement:** Reducing the computational complexity from O(2dp) to O(d2p) is crucial for scaling to deeper trees, addressing a key limitation of previous approaches.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experiments demonstrate consistent performance improvements over various greedy baselines across multiple datasets, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed method.  The analysis comparing depth and regularization is insightful.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, with a clear explanation of the problem formulation, proposed solution, and experimental results.  The inclusion of Algorithm 1 is helpful.
* **Thorough Related Work:** The authors provide a comprehensive overview of related work, including both classic and more recent approaches to decision tree learning, and effectively position their contribution within the existing literature.
* **Addressing Overfitting:** The regularization parameter ν directly addresses the potential for overfitting, and the empirical results demonstrate its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of the Derivation:** While the authors state the complexity reduction, the path from the initial formulation to the simplified O(d2p) complexity is not fully detailed within the main body.  More explanation of the modifications to the surrogate objective would be beneficial.  The reference to the supplementary material is necessary but adds a barrier to understanding.
* **Limited Comparison to Stronger Baselines:** While the comparison to greedy baselines is strong, the paper doesn’t compare to more recent, advanced decision tree or ensemble methods (e.g., gradient boosting machines like XGBoost or LightGBM).  These methods are highly competitive and would provide a stronger benchmark.  Specifically, demonstrating whether the proposed method can *outperform* these highly optimized implementations would substantially strengthen the paper.
* **Dependency on Initialization:** The reliance on initializing the non-greedy process from a greedy tree (specifically CO2) could introduce a bias. It would be beneficial to explore alternative initialization strategies or demonstrate the robustness of the results to different initialization methods.
* **Practical Considerations:** The paper focuses heavily on theoretical aspects and algorithmic complexity.  A discussion of practical considerations such as memory usage and the sensitivity of the method to hyperparameters beyond ν would be valuable.
* **SSGD Details:** The description of Stable SGD (SSGD) is somewhat brief. A more detailed explanation of how the leaf assignments are updated and the criteria for triggering an update would enhance understanding.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to a fundamental machine learning problem.
* The efficiency improvement is substantial and enables the training of deeper trees.
* The experimental results consistently demonstrate improved performance over standard baselines.
* The paper is well-written and provides a thorough overview of related work.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The complexity of the mathematical derivations may be a barrier to understanding for some readers.
* The lack of comparison to state-of-the-art ensemble methods weakens the impact of the results.
* The dependency on greedy initialization and limited discussion of practical considerations raise concerns about the generalizability and usability of the method.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel contribution to the field of decision tree learning. The theoretical insights and efficiency improvements are significant. However, the lack of comparison to stronger baselines and the limited discussion of practical considerations prevent me from strongly recommending acceptance at this time.  

**Recommendation:** Reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

1. **Expand the explanation of the complexity reduction:** Provide more details about the modifications to the surrogate objective that lead to the O(d2p) complexity within the main text, or provide a more comprehensive explanation in the supplementary material.
2. **Compare to state-of-the-art methods:** Include a comparison to highly optimized ensemble methods like XGBoost, LightGBM, or CatBoost.
3. **Investigate alternative initialization strategies:** Explore different ways to initialize the non-greedy process to assess the robustness of the results.
4. **Discuss practical considerations:** Address issues such as memory usage, hyperparameter sensitivity, and scalability to larger datasets.
5. **Elaborate on SSGD:** Provide a more detailed description of the leaf assignment update mechanism in SSGD.
6. **Address reviewer questions.** A response to the reviewer is essential to demonstrate a willingness to address feedback and improve the paper.
