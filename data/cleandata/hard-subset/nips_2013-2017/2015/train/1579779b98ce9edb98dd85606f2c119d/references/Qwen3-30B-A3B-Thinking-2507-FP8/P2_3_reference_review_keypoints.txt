- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree learning formulation as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss (Eq.7) with rigorous proof.  
- Develops an efficient SGD algorithm with fast loss-augmented inference, reducing complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p).  
- Empirical results show consistent better generalization than greedy baselines (SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST).  
- Comparison with CO2 isolates and supports the advantage of the non-greedy approach.  
- Theoretical analysis and complexity arguments are clear and convincing.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Key concepts, algorithms (Algorithm 1), and figures (e.g., Fig.2) effectively illustrate results.  
- Original contribution: novel connection between decision trees and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Technical innovation: loss-augmented inference complexity reduction to O(d^2 p) is novel and impactful.  
- Significance: addresses long-standing greedy optimization limitation and supports general loss functions.  
- Strengths include theoretical novelty, efficiency, strong empirical performance, clarity, and practical integration potential.  
- Weaknesses: limited to binary trees; hyperparameter selection (ν, η) lacks detail; limited comparisons to recent non-greedy methods; unclear practical speedup on very large datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify hyperparameter selection and discuss the binary-tree limitation more thoroughly.
