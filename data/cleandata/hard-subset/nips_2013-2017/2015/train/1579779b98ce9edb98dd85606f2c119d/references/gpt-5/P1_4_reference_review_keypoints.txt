- Proposes non-greedy training of decision trees by casting hard splits as latent variables and optimizing a convex-concave surrogate upper bound.  
- Exact loss-augmented inference (Eq.7) is exponential O(2^d p); authors restrict search to a Hamming ball radius 1 (Eq.13) to obtain O(d^2 p) training while keeping a valid bound.  
- Regularize per-row norms ‖wi‖2 ≤ ν to control W scale; Proposition 1 shows scaling tightens the bound but hurts optimizability.  
- Introduces Stable SGD that intermittently freezes leaf assignments to prevent leaf deactivation.  
- Empirically, non-greedy oblique trees outperform axis-aligned trees, OC1, random oblique splits, and a greedy CO2 baseline on several LibSVM datasets.  
- Demonstrates runtime advantage of fast loss-augmented inference versus exact, especially for deeper trees.  
- Relates to greedy tree induction, oblique/margin-based trees, soft-tree/HME models, and latent structured SVM literature; Hamming-ball approximation is novel in tree learning.  
- Strength: principled, theory-grounded formulation linking hard trees to latent structured prediction.  
- Strength: algorithmic contribution that reduces exponential complexity to practical polynomial time for training.  
- Strength: well-motivated regularization and practical optimization heuristic (Stable SGD).  
- Strength: consistent empirical improvements and informative analysis of ν and runtime scaling.  
- Weakness: Hamming-ball radius-1 restriction may be a crude approximation; no quantification of bound looseness or frequency of missing global maximizer.  
- Weakness: method depends on CO2 initialization; no ablation on sensitivity to initialization or random starts.  
- Weakness: unclear whether greedy baselines had comparable pruning/regularization to match effective capacity.  
- Weakness: limited experiments (single trees, few datasets); no comparisons to modern differentiable trees, post-pruning, or ensemble (forest) results.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees on convergence or generalization for the nonconvex (S)SGD optimization.  
- Reproducibility: technically sound derivations, but some algorithmic details (SSGD schedule, step sizes, stopping criteria, per-leaf updates) need expansion.  
- Clarity suggestions: clarify cost and mechanics of fast loss-augmented inference (node inner products), provide proof sketches/intuition for bounds, and specify SSGD schedule.  
- Originality: moderately high — specific latent-variable surrogate and Hamming-ball inference appear novel for non-greedy tree learning.  
- Significance: moderate-to-high — practical non-greedy training with improved single-tree accuracy and enabling complexity reduction, but broader impact limited by experimental scope.  
- Questions for authors: incidence of global maximizer outside Hamming ball; sensitivity to initialization (random W/Θ); whether greedy baselines were equivalently regularized/pruned; Θ regularization details; extension to forests.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled novel formulation, practical efficient algorithm, and consistent empirical gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited experimental breadth and baseline matching, no bound-looseness analysis, reliance on strong initialization.  
- Recommendation: Accept (weak accept to accept); confidence 4/5.
