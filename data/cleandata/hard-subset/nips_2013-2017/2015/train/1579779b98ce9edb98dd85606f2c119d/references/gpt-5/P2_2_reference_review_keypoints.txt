- Proposes non-greedy training for oblique decision trees by casting splits as latent variables and optimizing a convex-concave upper bound with SGD.  
- Rewrites sgn(Wx) as argmax over binary codes h to frame tree induction as latent structured prediction with a loss upper bound involving loss-augmented inference.  
- Introduces a Hamming-ball (radius 1) restriction to reduce loss-augmented inference complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p) while preserving the upper-bound property.  
- Regularizes per-row norms of W to prevent trivial bound tightening via scaling; optimization uses SGD with projection and a "Stable SGD" variant that intermittently freezes leaf assignments.  
- Empirical results: non-greedy trees outperform strong greedy baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique, CO2) across multiple multiclass datasets and depths.  
- Maintains fast test-time inference complexity O(d p) and provides analysis of norm constraint effects on pruning/overfitting.  
- Situates work relative to prior art: links to latent structured SVMs and improves on CO2 by jointly optimizing all splits for deep trees.  
- Strengths: principled latent-structured formulation, novel fast loss-augmented inference, practical training procedure, and consistent empirical gains.  
- Weaknesses: experiments focus on single trees with no ensemble comparisons; practical impact vs ensembles not fully quantified.  
- Ablation gaps: sensitivity to initialization, separate effects of full vs Hamming-bound, row-norm constraints, and SSGD not thoroughly quantified.  
- Theoretical gaps: no generalization bounds or convergence guarantees; non-convex landscape remains.  
- Reproducibility concerns: missing implementation details (SSGD update schedule, mini-batch sizes, momentum); code or more specifics would help.  
- Quality: derivations appear sound and experiments support claims, but limited ablations/ensemble comparisons weaken broader impact.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well organized; would benefit from more pseudo-code for fast inference and SSGD scheduling details.  
- Originality: novel combination of latent-structured formulation and fast upper-bound enabling joint deep-tree optimization.  
- Significance: likely to influence follow-on work on non-greedy oblique trees and structured surrogates, though ensemble comparisons would strengthen practical relevance.  
- Recommendation: lean accept — solid methodological contribution with demonstrable benefits.  
- Minor suggestions: clarify SSGD schedule, report wall-clock comparisons to greedy training, add initialization/ensemble ablations in supplement, and fix reference formatting.
