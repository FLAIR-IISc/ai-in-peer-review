- Proposes a non-greedy, globally-trained decision tree method that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters via a latent-variable structured-prediction formulation.  
- Casts binary split decisions as latent variables and derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss (Eq. 7).  
- Introduces a computationally efficient Hamming-1 surrogate bound (Eq. 13) enabling loss-augmented inference in O(d^2 p) per update.  
- Uses SGD with per-row norm projections on W to update oblique splits and leaves while preserving O(dp) inference complexity.  
- Proposes a “Stable SGD” (SSGD) variant that freezes leaf assignments for several steps to prevent premature leaf deactivation.  
- Empirical results show consistent test accuracy gains over axis-aligned trees, OC1, random oblique splits, and CO2 across depths.  
- Demonstrates reduced overfitting and interpretable effect of per-row norm regularization (ν) on pruning.  
- Connects to latent structural SVMs and loss-augmented inference rather than soft probabilistic gating (HME).  
- Extends prior CO2 work to joint, non-greedy optimization across deeper trees with scalable inference.  
- Technical contributions include two surrogate bounds, efficient loss-augmented inference, and per-row projection scheme.  
- Practical algorithm design is sensible: SGD+projections, SSGD stabilization, and initialization from greedy trees/CO2.  
- Evaluation scope limited to single trees; lacks discussion or experiments on integration with ensembles (RF/boosting).  
- Missing comparisons to modern strong baselines (e.g., gradient boosting, multiclass SVM/linear models) for contextualization.  
- Ablation analyses are limited: gap between Eq.7 and Eq.13, SSGD vs. SGD impact, and sensitivity to initialization not fully explored.  
- No convergence guarantees for the non-convex optimization; empirical convergence diagnostics or bound-over-time plots are absent.  
- Generality claims (arbitrary losses, regression) are not supported experimentally; only multiclass log-loss shown.  
- Reproducibility details lacking: SSGD cadence, momentum/mini-batch sizes, hyperparameter search ranges, and code availability.  
- Clarity is generally good but would benefit from a proof sketch of Eq.7, explicit SSGD freeze details, and clearer initialization/hyperparameter reporting.  
- Originality: novel latent-variable tree formulation and Hamming-1 loss-augmented inference at scale relative to prior work.  
- Significance: improves single-tree training with potential transfer to ensembles and hierarchical models, but broader baseline comparisons needed.  
- Suggested additions: ablations on bound choice/SSGD/initialization, at least one boosted-tree comparison, training curves for the surrogate bound, and code release.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled formulation, efficient algorithm, consistent empirical improvements, and preserved fast inference.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited baseline coverage and ablations, missing regression experiments and some practical details.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept to accept (high reviewer confidence).
