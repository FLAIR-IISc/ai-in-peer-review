- Introduces a novel formulation of decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it with SGD.  
- Major technical contribution: reduces loss-augmented inference complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p).  
- Mathematical derivations (eq.7) and Proposition 1 are rigorous and provide intuition on W's scale and bound tightness.  
- Fast loss-augmented inference makes non-greedy learning of deep trees feasible.  
- Empirical results show consistent generalization gains over greedy baselines on SensIT, Connect4, Protein, and MNIST.  
- Norm regularization of W (eq.9) addresses overfitting in a principled way.  
- SSGD variant is a practical extension that prevents abrupt leaf-assignment changes.  
- Insufficient contrast and explicit comparison with Bennett’s earlier global tree optimization work.  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees comparing SGD solution quality to the optimal tree.  
- Experiments lack statistical significance tests to support overfitting claims.  
- No measured runtime comparison against greedy training to quantify practical efficiency.  
- Claims about extension to N-ary trees are mentioned but not detailed or evidenced.  
- Overall significance: makes non-greedy tree learning practically viable and scalable, handling general loss functions.  
- Conclusion and recommendation: technically sound, practically important work with minor gaps; recommend acceptance (NeurIPS).
