- Proposal: Cast induction of hard oblique decision trees as latent-structured prediction, optimizing a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss using h = sgn(Wx) as latent variables.
- Hamming-ball speedup: Restrict loss-augmented inference to a Hamming ball radius 1 around sgn(Wx) to reduce complexity from exponential to O(d^2 p) per SGD step.
- Regularization & optimization: Use per-row ℓ2 constraints on W and an SGD with projection; introduce Stable SGD (SSGD) that temporarily freezes leaf assignments to prevent collapse of active leaves.
- Test-time efficiency: Maintains hard splits and O(d p) inference, preserving traditional tree inference speed vs. soft-split models.
- Empirical results: Consistent test accuracy improvements over axis-aligned CART, OC1, random oblique splits, and CO2 across multiple LibSVM datasets and depths, with reduced overfitting.
- Relation to prior work: Connects global non-greedy tree optimization, margin-based trees, soft-split probabilistic trees, and structural SVMs with latent variables; CO2 is a depth-1 greedy special case.
- Conceptual strength: Principled, neat reformulation linking loss-augmented inference and margin-rescaling to hard tree learning.
- Practical strength: Hamming-ball trick is a clever tractable approximation; implementation via projected SGD is simple and pragmatic.
- Limitation — fixed topology: Method only optimizes split and leaf parameters for a fixed tree structure (initialized greedily); it does not learn topology.
- Limitation — limited theory: Only scale monotonicity shown; lacks generalization (margin/Rademacher) and convergence guarantees; trade-offs involving ν need analysis.
- Limitation — complexity clarity: Ambiguity about computing full sgn(Wx) and loss-augmented search without evaluating all m inner products; need explicit O(d p) implementation details.
- Experimental gaps: Missing ablations (no-Hamming vs Hamming), SSGD vs vanilla SGD, sensitivity analyses for ν and η, wall-clock comparisons, and leaf-inactivity studies.
- Baselines & positioning: Need clearer comparisons to other global/near-global oblique methods and ensure CO2 uses identical initialization/tuning due to sensitivity to init.
- Objective mismatch: Leaf log-loss and surrogate bound are not calibrated to misclassification; discussion on leaf calibration or margin effects is lacking.
- Quality: Technically sound latent-variable reformulation and bounds; empirical section solid but theoretical and experimental breadth underdeveloped.
- Clarity: Generally well written; navigation function and loss-augmented inference clear, but complexity/accounting and fixed-topology scope should be explicitly stated.
- Originality: Novel combination of latent-structured prediction for hard trees and the Hamming-ball speedup; well positioned relative to CO2.
- Significance: Advances non-greedy training of hard trees with a practical scalable approach that improves single-tree accuracy while preserving fast inference.
- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to initialization; empirical gap between full vs Hamming-ball inference; SSGD leaf reassignment frequency and trigger; provide confidence intervals over random splits.
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated paper likely worthy of acceptance pending clarifications on complexity, broader ablations, and explicit acknowledgment of fixed topology.
