- Overall: Novel, theoretically grounded non-greedy decision tree method; strong submission but needs revisions.  
- Summary: Formulates tree learning as structured prediction with latent split variables and derives a convex-concave upper bound optimized by SGD.  
- Complexity: Proposes an important complexity reduction from O(2dp) to O(d2p), enabling deeper trees.  
- Novelty: Original connection between decision trees and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Addresses limitation: Directly tackles the inability of greedy methods to account for downstream tree structure.  
- Efficient optimization: Offers practical algorithmic improvements to make joint optimization feasible.  
- Regularization: Uses norm-based regularization to control overfitting and improve generalization.  
- Clarity: Well-motivated problem statement and clear relation to prior work.  
- Experimental scope: Empirical evaluation is promising but limited and lacks comparisons to modern tree methods.  
- Baselines: Missing comparisons with state-of-the-art methods like XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Results appear sensitive to regularization parameter ν and need deeper analysis.  
- Scalability concerns: O(d2p) complexity may still be a bottleneck for very high-dimensional or extremely deep trees.  
- Ablation study: Lacks thorough ablation to isolate contributions of surrogate objective and regularization.  
- SSGD explanation: Motivation clear but implementation details and quantitative benefits over SGD are under-explained.  
- Initialization: Needs discussion on dependence of performance on greedy-tree initialization versus random starts.  
- For acceptance: Significant theoretical contribution, addresses a longstanding problem, promising empirical results, and clear exposition.  
- Against acceptance: Limited experimental comparisons, hyperparameter sensitivity, scalability issues, and insufficient algorithmic detail on SSGD.  
- Recommendations: Expand experiments with modern baselines and high-dimensional datasets; analyze ν sensitivity; discuss scalability; add ablations; elaborate and quantify SSGD; discuss initialization effects.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
