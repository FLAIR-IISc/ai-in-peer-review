- Proposes a non-greedy training method for decision trees by jointly optimizing all split functions via a latent-vector formulation and a global surrogate objective.  
- Introduces a latent-structured-SVM-style upper bound on empirical loss, yielding a piecewise linear convex–concave surrogate optimized with projected SGD.  
- Controls bound tightness and regularization by constraining per-split L2 norms and a scaling parameter ν.  
- Reduces exponential loss-augmented inference complexity by restricting search to a Hamming ball radius 1 around sgn(Wx), achieving O(d^2 p) per-update cost.  
- Proposes “Stable SGD” (SSGD) that temporarily fixes leaf assignments to avoid deactivating leaves during training.  
- Empirically, non-greedy oblique trees outperform greedy baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique) and CO2, especially at larger depths and with less overfitting.  
- Maintains O(dp) test-time inference and supports general loss functions beyond 0-1.  
- Connects to prior NP-hardness and non-greedy tree work, and distinguishes from soft/probabilistic trees by preserving hard splits and test-time efficiency.  
- Adapts established structured-prediction and latent SVM ideas specifically to decision trees with a practical fast loss-augmented inference.  
- The surrogate bound and optimization are technically sound, and the Hamming-ball restriction preserves validity.  
- Lacks theoretical generalization analysis (e.g., margin-based bounds) and formal conditions linking the surrogate to test error.  
- The role and trade-offs of ν (tightness vs optimization smoothness) are discussed qualitatively but not rigorously analyzed.  
- Initialization uses CO2; no ablation quantifies gains from joint optimization vs initialization effect.  
- No ablation comparing full loss-augmented inference to Hamming-ball approximation on shallow trees.  
- Experimental scope is limited to multiclass classification; regression and comparisons to strong modern baselines (e.g., gradient boosting) are missing.  
- Key practical details (SSGD schedule, preprocessing, hyperparameters) and reproducibility information are insufficiently specified.  
- Complexity and implementation details for fast inference (algorithm, constants, caching, memory) are not fully described.  
- Claims of generality to any loss and kernelization are not empirically validated.  
- Paper is well written; however, it should add pseudocode for fast inference and SSGD and clarify caching and split-evaluation costs.  
- The approach is original in synthesizing latent-structured optimization with an efficient Hamming-ball inference for deep oblique trees.  
- Improving single-tree training non-greedily is significant and likely to be adopted, but broader impact would benefit from ensemble/regression experiments and stronger baselines.  
- Suggested author actions: provide ablations (initialization, Hamming-ball vs full, SSGD vs SGD), report wall-clock/memory, add regression experiment, clarify feature scaling and ν effects, and consider sparsity penalties and brief boosted-tree comparisons.  
- Recommendation: solid contribution leaning toward acceptance, contingent on clearer fast-inference/SSGD descriptions and additional empirical analyses.
