- Tackles non-greedy optimization of decision trees via a new structured-prediction-with-latent-variables formulation.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it with SGD.  
- Key technical contribution: reduces computation from O(2dp) to O(d²p) using fast loss-augmented inference.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvement over greedy baselines across multiple datasets.  
- Positions work well within long-standing decision-tree literature and prior global-optimization and structured-prediction methods.  
- Useful comparison to CO2 demonstrates benefits of full non-greedy optimization.  
- Notes limitations of prior soft-split and online-learning approaches for efficient tree induction.  
- Strength: novel conceptual framing enabling application of structured-prediction techniques.  
- Strength: important efficiency improvement enabling deeper trees and larger datasets.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance and informative ablation studies on regularization.  
- Strength: clear exposition and well-presented mathematical derivations.  
- Strength: thorough related-work discussion.  
- Strength: practical SGD algorithm with a Stable SGD variant for implementation.  
- Weakness: O(d²p) claim may hide large constant factors; practical complexity needs more discussion.  
- Weakness: performance appears dependent on greedy (CO2) initialization; sensitivity to poor/random init is unclear.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons to modern gradient-boosting benchmarks (e.g., XGBoost, LightGBM).  
- Weakness: choice of L2 regularization on weights is not fully justified; other regularizers (e.g., L1) not explored.  
- For acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with significant optimization and empirical contributions, clearly written.  
- Against acceptance: concerns about practical constant factors in complexity and reliance on greedy initialization / limited modern baselines.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper, valuable contribution, recommended for acceptance (Rating: Strong Accept, 8/10).
