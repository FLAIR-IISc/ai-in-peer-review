- Proposes non-greedy decision-tree learning by framing splits as latent variables and optimizing a convex–concave upper bound via SGD.  
- Key technical contribution: reduces loss-augmented inference complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), making deep non-greedy trees practical.  
- Empirical results: consistently better generalization than greedy baselines across multiple datasets and depths (includes CO2 as a greedy special case).  
- Practical implementation: clear algorithmic details and demonstrated scalability (time advantages for deeper trees shown).  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees that non-greedy optimization outperforms greedy methods or attains/global optimum proximity.  
- Incomplete comparison to prior global optimization work (Bennett); unclear how this method improves on Bennett in efficiency and accuracy.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity not thoroughly analyzed—regularization ν affects bound tightness/pruning but practical sensitivity is not reported.  
- Limited theoretical analysis of generalization properties (no relation drawn between norm regularization, VC-dimension, and pruning).  
- Overall assessment: strong technical and empirical contribution but needs stronger theoretical justification and fuller comparison to prior non-greedy methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add comparison to Bennett, more theoretical/generalization analysis, and hyperparameter sensitivity discussion.
