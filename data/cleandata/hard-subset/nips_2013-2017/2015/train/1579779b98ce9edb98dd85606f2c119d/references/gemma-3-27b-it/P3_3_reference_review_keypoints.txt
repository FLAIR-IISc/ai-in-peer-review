- Novel framing: formulates non-greedy decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Optimization: introduces a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling SGD-based optimization.  
- Complexity reduction: reduces worst-case complexity from O(2^{dp}) to O(d^2 p), making non-greedy optimization practical for deeper trees.  
- Empirical gains: shows consistent test performance and generalization improvements over greedy baselines across benchmarks.  
- Regularization: uses norm regularization (and analyzes ν) to address overfitting concerns raised in prior work.  
- Presentation: paper is generally well-written with clear mathematical derivations and a digestible algorithm.  
- Relation to prior work: correctly situates contribution among NP-hard global optimization, structured prediction, and relevant prior methods.  
- Implementation complexity: surrogate objective, loss-augmented inference, and SSGD implementation appear complex and under-discussed.  
- Scalability concerns: O(d^2 p) still limits very deep trees or high-dimensional data; experiments lack large-scale datasets and broad runtime comparisons.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: method appears sensitive to regularization parameter ν and lacks robustness analysis or automated tuning strategies.  
- Limited baselines: missing comparisons to modern ensemble/state-of-the-art methods (e.g., XGBoost, LightGBM, advanced random forests).  
- Initialization dependence: method relies on greedy initialization (CO2) and the impact of this dependence is not fully explored.  
- Pro acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, significant complexity improvement, empirical wins, and built-in regularization.  
- Con acceptance: practical applicability limited by implementation/scalability, missing SOTA comparisons, and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: run larger-scale scaling experiments; compare to modern boosting/forest methods; analyze ν robustness and tuning; discuss implementation overhead in detail.
