- Tackles greedy decision tree limitation by proposing a non-greedy framework using structured prediction with latent variables.
- Formulates a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss optimized via stochastic gradient descent.
- Key technical contribution: reduces computational complexity (reported from O(2dp) to O(d2p)), enabling learning of deeper trees.
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over greedy baselines and benefit relative to CO2.
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly organized.
- Addresses a fundamental problem in decision-tree learning with potential for significant impact.
- Novel combination of structured prediction and decision-tree learning with a useful complexity-reduction technique.
- Implementation appears intricate due to multiple components (surrogate objective, latent variables, SGD, Stable SGD), risking reproducibility issues.
- Performance is sensitive to regularization parameter ν, and tuning/choice of ν appears ad-hoc.
- Comparisons are primarily to simpler greedy baselines; lacks evaluation against strong baselines (Extremely Randomized Trees, Gradient Boosted Trees, Random Forests) and runtime comparisons.
- Scalability experiments are limited to modest datasets; need discussion/demonstration on truly large-scale data (millions of samples).
- The benefit and behavior of Stable SGD (SSGD) are not well explained; more insight into optimization dynamics is needed.
- Technically sound and rigorous, but method complexity increases risk of implementation errors.
- Clarity is good overall, but the interplay among latent variables, surrogate objective, SGD, and SSGD could be made more accessible.
- Originality is strong given the novel formulation and complexity reduction.
- Significance is high if the method proves scalable and robust; observed generalization improvements are promising.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental problem, novel and theoretically sound, promising empirical results, practical complexity reduction.
- Arguments against acceptance: implementation complexity, hyperparameter sensitivity, limited strong-baseline comparisons, unclear scalability to very large datasets.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: add implementation details for reproducibility; include stronger baseline comparisons (e.g., Random Forests, Gradient Boosting); expand scalability discussion; clarify Stable SGD benefits; clarify choice and influence of ν.
