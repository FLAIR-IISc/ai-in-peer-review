- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree learning method by framing tree optimization as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Develops a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss (Equation 7) that can be optimized with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Key technical contribution: reduces loss-augmented inference complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), enabling deeper trees.  
- Theoretical derivation and proof of the upper bound are rigorous and provided in supplementary material.  
- Complexity analysis of the fast loss-augmented inference is thorough and well-motivated.  
- Experimental evaluation on four benchmark datasets shows consistent generalization improvements over greedy baselines across depths.  
- Ablation studies (Fig. 3) analyze the effect of the regularization parameter ν on tree structure and performance.  
- Implementation details and Algorithm 1 are sufficiently clear for reproducibility.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with consistent notation and clear figures (notably Fig. 1).  
- Balances theoretical depth and practical implementation, making contributions accessible to a broad audience.  
- Novel connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- The convex-concave formulation and the O(d^2 p) inference algorithm are original and practically significant.  
- Method is flexible (works with any loss function) and amenable to extensions (sparsity, kernels).  
- Limited comparisons to other non-greedy methods beyond Bennett [1] and CO2.  
- Lacks a deeper explanation of why non-greedy optimization improves generalization beyond regularization arguments.  
- No runtime comparisons with baseline methods (only internal training time reported).  
- Discussion of practical applications is brief and somewhat superficial.  
- Focuses on binary trees; N-ary extensions are noted but not demonstrated.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — significant, original, and well-executed contribution to decision tree learning.
