- Novel non-greedy approach: formulates decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables to enable joint optimization of split functions.  
- Optimization method: develops a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it with SGD.  
- Complexity improvement: reduces computational complexity from O(2dp) to O(d²p), making optimization feasible for deeper trees.  
- Empirical gains: shows improved test performance and generalization over greedy baselines on multiple benchmark datasets.  
- Related work placement: correctly situates contribution amid prior non-greedy and structured-prediction literature, noting NP-completeness of global optimization.  
- Distinction from prior methods: differentiates from multi-linear programming, Mondrian processes, and hierarchical mixtures of experts.  
- Inspiration: leverages connections to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Strength — novelty: joint optimization of splits departs significantly from sequential greedy methods.  
- Strength — efficiency: complexity reduction is crucial for scalability.  
- Strength — theory: provides a solid theoretical foundation via the convex-concave bound and insights on the effect of W’s norm.  
- Strength — experiments: includes useful ablation on regularization parameter ν.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written with consistent notation.  
- Weakness — implementation: overall algorithm remains complex; fast loss-augmented inference and SSGD details need clarification.  
- Weakness — hyperparameters: performance sensitive to ν; lacks systematic analysis of other hyperparameters (learning rate, momentum).  
- Weakness — baselines: missing comparisons to stronger methods like extremely randomized trees and gradient boosted trees.  
- Weakness — limitations discussion: insufficient discussion of limits, e.g., linear split functions may not suit all datasets.  
- Overall assessment: strong, promising contribution that merits acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel formulation, scalability, strong empirical results, clear presentation.  
- Cons against acceptance: implementation complexity, limited state-of-the-art comparisons, hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (8/10).
