- Addresses greedy decision tree limitations by proposing a non-greedy learning framework using structured prediction with binary latent variables for splits.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling joint optimization of tree splits.  
- Key algorithmic contribution: reduces optimization complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d² p), making deep non-greedy trees practical.  
- Empirical evaluation shows consistent generalization improvements over greedy baselines across standard datasets.  
- Provides analysis of the regularization parameter ν and its effect on tree complexity and implicit pruning.  
- Positions work well within prior literature, contrasting with greedy methods, global optimization approaches, and related structured prediction techniques.  
- Strength: novel formulation connects decision tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Strength: substantial practical efficiency gain that enables deeper tree training.  
- Strength: strong empirical results supporting effectiveness.  
- Strength: useful regularization analysis and generally clear writing with helpful algorithmic detail.  
- Weakness: comparisons to baselines are somewhat limited; more non-greedy or recent methods should be included and baseline tuning described.  
- Weakness: performance sensitive to ν; lacks practical guidance beyond validation tuning.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns remain for very high-dimensional data since p can still be a bottleneck.  
- Weakness: description of Stable SGD (SSGD) is brief—needs more detail on leaf update criteria and runtime impact.  
- Weakness: theoretical derivations (upper bound, relation to regularizer, Proposition 1) need more thorough justification.  
- Overall: strong contribution with significant theoretical and empirical merits, recommended for acceptance pending minor clarifications.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel valuable approach, improved generalization, crucial algorithmic improvement for depth, well-written exposition.  
- Rejection concerns: ν sensitivity, limited non-greedy comparisons, and high-dimensional scalability warrant further investigation.  
- Reviewer rating: Strong Accept (7/10).
