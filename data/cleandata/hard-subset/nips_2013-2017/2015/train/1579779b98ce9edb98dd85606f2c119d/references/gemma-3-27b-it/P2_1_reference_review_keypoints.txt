- Paper proposes a novel non-greedy decision-tree optimization framework using structured prediction with latent variables to jointly optimize splits.  
- Authors derive a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and use SGD for optimization.  
- Key technical contribution: reduce computational complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), enabling deeper trees.  
- Theoretical grounding provided, including derivation of the bound and discussion of regularization (Proposition 1).  
- Empirical results show consistent improvement over greedy baselines and reduced overfitting; ablation on regularization ν included.  
- Writing is generally clear and the introduction effectively frames the problem and contributions.  
- Novelty: the connection to structured prediction with latent variables is original and insightful.  
- Complexity of implementation is high compared to standard greedy algorithms, possibly hindering practitioner adoption.  
- Comparisons are limited mostly to greedy methods and one older non-greedy method (CO2); lacks comparison to recent competitive tree-based methods/ensembles.  
- Performance appears sensitive to regularization parameter ν; tuning details and dataset-specific impact are insufficiently detailed.  
- Scalability to very large datasets (millions of samples) is not thoroughly discussed despite runtime comparisons on smaller datasets.  
- Some proofs (e.g., Proposition 1) are relegated to supplementary material rather than included in the main text.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, and promising; reduction in complexity is a key strength despite implementation and comparison shortcomings.  
- Quality: strong; Clarity: good but some dense sections (e.g., Alg 1); Originality: high; Significance: good for decision-tree learning.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theory, significant complexity reduction, superior empirical performance to greedy baselines.  
- Arguments against acceptance: implementation complexity, limited state-of-the-art comparisons, and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand comparisons, detail hyperparameter tuning, and include essential proofs in the main text.
