- Proposes non-greedy training for oblique decision trees by casting internal split decisions as latent binary variables and optimizing a global surrogate objective from structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Leaves produce class probabilities via softmax and the empirical loss is upper-bounded by a convex-concave surrogate optimized with SGD and per-row norm constraints on W.  
- Key technical contribution: restrict loss-augmented inference to Hamming distance 1 from sgn(Wx), reducing naive O(2^d p) search to O(d^2 p) per step while still upper-bounding the loss.  
- Introduces Stable SGD (SSGD) that temporarily fixes example-to-leaf assignments to prevent many leaves becoming inactive and improve training stability.  
- Experiments on LibSVM datasets (SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST) show consistent test-accuracy improvements over axis-aligned greedy trees, OC1, random oblique trees, and CO2 across depths.  
- Relates to prior work: extends structured SVM/MMN ideas to hard-split trees, contrasts with soft-split HME/EM methods, and differs from prior global approaches (MIPs, max-margin trees) and CO2.  
- Strength: clear conceptual bridge from trees to latent structured prediction with a justified surrogate and regularization.  
- Strength: practical and efficient Hamming-ball inference makes the approach scalable and maintains O(dp) test-time inference.  
- Strength: SGD with per-row projection is simple and effective; SSGD addresses an important training instability.  
- Strength: empirical analysis includes effects of regularization ν and shows less overfitting than greedy baselines.  
- Weakness: Hamming radius-1 restriction loosens the bound; no ablation quantifying effect on final accuracy versus full bound.  
- Weakness: experimental scope limited to single trees; no experiments with ensembles (forests/boosting) where modern practice lies.  
- Weakness: baselines omit more recent oblique or differentiable tree methods; OC1 and axis-aligned trees are somewhat dated.  
- Weakness: reproducibility lacking key training hyperparameters, initialization details, and no code release.  
- Weakness: statistical rigor limited — no error bars or multiple seeds reported; sensitivity to initialization unclear.  
- Quality: derivations and complexity analysis are sound and algorithm coherent; proofs provided in supplement.  
- Clarity: paper is well written and organized; suggested additions include pseudocode for fast inference, clearer SSGD schedule, and exact optimization settings.  
- Originality: novel adaptation of latent structured prediction to hard-split trees with tractable Hamming-ball inference.  
- Significance: improves single-tree training in a scalable way with practical impact, though showing ensemble benefits would increase relevance.  
- Questions/suggestions: report sensitivity to initialization, results with full loss-augmented inference on small trees, training-time comparisons to baselines, calibration of leaf probabilities, per-node ν schemes, and SSGD convergence diagnostics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear formulation, solid technical advance, consistent empirical gains, and practical scalability likely to inspire follow-ups.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental breadth, missing ablations on tightness vs speed, and incomplete reproducibility/statistical analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with acceptance recommended, subject to adding ablations and fuller training/reproducibility details.
