- Paper proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree learning framework by framing tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.
- Authors derive a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimize it using stochastic gradient descent.
- Key technical contribution: reduce optimization complexity from O(2dp) to O(d²p), enabling feasibility for deeper trees.
- Experiments show consistent improvement over greedy baselines and demonstrate benefits over CO2 (full non-greedy optimization advantage).
- Paper situates itself well among prior work, contrasting with greedy methods, Bennett’s non-scalable approach, Mondrian processes, and hierarchical mixtures.
- Strength: latent-variable formulation is a clever connection to structured prediction techniques.
- Strength: significant computational efficiency improvement makes non-greedy optimization practical.
- Strength: strong empirical results across datasets supporting effectiveness.
- Strength: paper is well-written with clear explanation of the bound, loss-augmented inference, and optimization.
- Strength: thorough ablation study on regularization and the Stable SGD variant.
- Weakness: theoretical justification for the L2 regularizer (and why L2 specifically) is insufficiently rigorous.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to state-of-the-art tree-based methods like XGBoost or LightGBM.
- Weakness: method depends on initializing from a greedy tree (CO2); sensitivity to initialization is unclear.
- Weakness: O(d²p) may still be a bottleneck for very high-dimensional data or very deep trees; scalability concerns remain.
- Weakness: potential sensitivity to hyperparameters (regularization ν) needs more robust analysis.
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with practical efficiency and promising empirical gains.
- Against acceptance: limited SOTA comparisons, weak regularization theory, reliance on greedy initialization, hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Overall assessment: strong paper warranting acceptance with minor revisions.
- Recommended revisions: add comparison to gradient boosting machines, provide stronger justification for L2 regularization, analyze initialization sensitivity, and discuss scalability/future work.
