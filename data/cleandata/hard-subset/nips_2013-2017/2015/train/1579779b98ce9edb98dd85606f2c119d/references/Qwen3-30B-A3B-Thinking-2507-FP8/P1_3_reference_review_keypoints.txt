- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree learning framework by casting tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Main technical contribution: fast loss-augmented inference algorithm reducing complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d² p) by restricting search to Hamming ball radius 1.  
- Enables practical learning of deeper non-greedy trees while retaining O(d p) inference complexity at test time.  
- Method is general to any loss function and uses regularization parameter ν to control tree complexity.  
- Empirical results show consistent generalization improvements over greedy baselines and reduced overfitting across multiple benchmarks.  
- Theoretical derivations and complexity analysis are rigorous and supplementary proofs are provided.  
- Algorithm descriptions are clear, precise, and implementable; paper is well-written and well-organized.  
- Lacks direct quantitative training-time comparisons with greedy methods despite theoretical analysis and a figure reference.  
- Does not compare against modern state-of-the-art tree ensembles (e.g., XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost).  
- Missing deeper analysis explaining why non-greedy optimization improves generalization beyond regularization intuition.  
- Experiments use moderate-sized datasets and do not demonstrate scalability to million-example industrial datasets.  
- Does not explicitly analyze or report prediction-time performance, though inference complexity is likely unchanged.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, significant complexity reduction, practical algorithm, and consistent empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparisons to modern methods, limited large-scale validation, missing explanatory analysis, and absent training-time metrics.  
- Overall assessment: strong technical contribution that overcomes prior complexity barriers and merits acceptance despite experimental limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept.
