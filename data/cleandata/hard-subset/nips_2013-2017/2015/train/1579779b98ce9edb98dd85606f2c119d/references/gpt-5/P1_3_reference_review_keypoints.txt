- Proposes non-greedy training for hard-split decision trees by casting splits as latent variables and optimizing a global surrogate via SGD.
- Upper-bounds empirical loss with a piecewise-linear structured-prediction surrogate: loss-augmented inference minus inference score using latent binary vector h.
- Naive loss-augmented inference is exponential in depth; paper reduces it to O(mp) by exploiting tree structure.
- Introduces a radius-1 Hamming-ball surrogate variant yielding O(d^2 p) loss-augmented inference.
- Uses per-row norm constraints on W motivated by a proposition that larger ||W|| tightens the bound but becomes uninformative in the limit.
- Trains with projected SGD and a Stable SGD (SSGD) heuristic that fixes leaf assignments temporarily to avoid inactive leaves.
- Maintains test-time inference efficiency O(dp) while enabling joint non-greedy training.
- Empirical results: single-tree method generally outperforms CART, OC1, random oblique trees, and CO2 across depths and datasets, and shows less overfitting.
- Connects to latent structured prediction and margin-based learning; positions CO2 as a depth-1 special case and contrasts with soft-split HMEs and prior costly non-greedy methods.
- Strengths: novel latent-variable formulation, principled surrogate, fast radius-1 inference, practical training heuristics, and clear extension paths (sparsity, kernels).
- Weaknesses: final objective is non-convex with no convergence guarantees; impact of radius-1 restriction on optimization quality is unquantified.
- Experimental scope limited to single trees; lacks comparisons to common ensembles (random forests, gradient boosting) and large-scale/high-dim settings.
- Ablation studies are limited: unclear contribution of SSGD vs. SGD, sensitivity to ν and other hyperparameters, and variance/stability across runs.
- Complexity narrative could be clearer about when O(2^d p), O(mp), and O(d^2 p) bounds apply.
- Reproducibility details are sparse (mini-batch size, momentum, epochs, initialization, stopping criteria) and figures rather than numeric tables reduce precision.
- Quality: surrogate and radius-1 bound are sound; algorithmic derivations correct and implementable; main caveat is heuristic SSGD without deeper analysis.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized; would benefit from pseudocode for fast loss-augmented inference and explicit SSGD scheduling details.
- Originality: novel and meaningful generalization of CO2—first clear latent structured surrogate for hard, non-greedy trees with radius-1 trick.
- Significance: advances single-tree learning and could aid ensembles; practical impact would be higher with ensemble/large-scale evidence.
- Suggested experiments: ablate full vs radius-1 bound, SGD vs SSGD, ν effects and leaf activity, initialization sensitivity; add numeric tables, runtimes, memory, and ensemble comparisons.
- Pros for acceptance: principled formulation, non-trivial technical contributions, consistent empirical gains, and maintained test-time efficiency.
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical breadth, heuristic SSGD without theory, and insufficient reproducibility/numerical reporting.
- Recommendation: Weak accept; reviewer confidence high on methodology/theory, medium on empirical breadth.
