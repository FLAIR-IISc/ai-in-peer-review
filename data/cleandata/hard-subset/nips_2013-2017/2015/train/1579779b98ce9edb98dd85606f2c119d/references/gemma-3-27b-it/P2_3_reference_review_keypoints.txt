- Tackles the limitation of greedy decision tree learning by proposing a non-greedy optimization framework.  
- Frames decision tree learning as a structured prediction problem with latent variables, enabling new optimization tools.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and uses SGD for optimization.  
- Achieves a computational complexity reduction from O(2^{d}p) to O(d^{2}p), improving scalability to deeper trees.  
- Empirical results show improved performance over greedy baselines (OC1, random forests, CO2) on benchmark datasets.  
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly motivated, and logically organized.  
- Includes discussion and empirical evaluation of norm regularization (ν) and its impact on generalization.  
- Practical scalability to very large, high-dimensional datasets is not thoroughly addressed; experiments use modest datasets.  
- Performance is sensitive to regularization hyperparameter ν, requiring validation tuning without detailed practical guidance.  
- Lacks comparison to modern state-of-the-art tree methods (e.g., XGBoost, LightGBM), limiting practical benchmarking.  
- The Stable SGD (SSGD) variant is empirically useful but lacks strong theoretical justification and reads as heuristic.  
- Method depends on initializing W with a greedy tree; influence of different initializations is not investigated.  
- Derivations are clear though some sections (e.g., upper bound derivation) could be simplified for readability.  
- Originality: novel formulation and significant complexity reduction represent important contributions.  
- Significance: promising foundation for non-greedy trees, though not yet shown to outperform top practical methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability, include comparisons to XGBoost, and strengthen SSGD justification.
