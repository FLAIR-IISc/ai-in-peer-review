- Proposal: non-greedy decision tree learning via structured prediction with latent variables and a convex-concave upper bound optimized by SGD.
- Relation to prior work: improves on Bennett and Hierarchical Mixture of Experts by offering a practical optimization with theoretical guarantees, tying to structured prediction literature.
- Theoretical quality: technically sound with proofs of the upper bound and Proposition 1 showing the bound tightens with larger W.
- Algorithmic complexity: fast loss-augmented inference reduces complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), making deep trees feasible.
- Experiments: benchmark results (SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST) show non-greedy trees generalize better across depths than greedy baselines including CO2.
- Limitations acknowledged: requires tuning regularization parameter ν and explains its role in implicit leaf pruning.
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written, clear notation, reproducible algorithms, and transparent experimental setup.
- Originality: novel framing as structured prediction with latent variables and the Hamming-ball radius-1 restriction for efficient inference.
- Significance: substantial — offers a theoretically grounded, efficient alternative to greedy induction that reduces overfitting and supports general losses.
- Strengths: theoretical novelty, efficient O(d^2 p) algorithm, strong empirical gains, clear presentation, comprehensive baseline comparisons.
- Weaknesses: missing comparisons to XGBoost/LightGBM, limited discussion of single-tree vs ensemble practicality, sensitivity to ν under-analyzed, experiments not scaled to very large datasets.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; add discussion comparing ensembles and consider including XGBoost/LightGBM comparisons.
