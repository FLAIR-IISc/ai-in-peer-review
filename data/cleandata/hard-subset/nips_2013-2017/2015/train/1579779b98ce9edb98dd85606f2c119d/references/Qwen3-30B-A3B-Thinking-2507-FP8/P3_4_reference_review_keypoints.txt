- Reformulates decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables and proposes a convex-concave upper bound enabling SGD optimization.  
- Main technical contribution: reduces computational complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), enabling training of deeper trees.  
- Introduces a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm (Section 6.2) that is clever and effective.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements over greedy baselines and reduced overfitting across multiple benchmark datasets.  
- Uses norm regularization (ν) to control tree complexity; empirically shown to help (Figure 3).  
- Claim of “non-greedy” optimization is misleading because loss-augmented inference still follows a greedy path traversal.  
- Theoretical analysis is incomplete: Proposition 1 about bound tightness as ||W|| increases lacks analysis of effects on convergence and relation to true loss.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and scale (mostly LibSVM datasets); scalability to much larger, real-world datasets is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Runtime/scale evidence (e.g., Connect4 experiment) is insufficient to prove practical scalability for large applications.  
- Insufficient comparison and explanation of relationship to prior work (e.g., CO2/ Norouzi et al., 2015); CO2 presented as a separate baseline though claimed to be a special case.  
- Paper omits discussion of limitations: handling of categorical features is unclear and sensitivity to different loss functions is not analyzed.  
- Overall: technically sound with meaningful contributions and validated improvements, but some claims are overstated and the evaluation and theoretical discussion need strengthening.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify the “non-greedy” nuance, deepen the relation to CO2, and expand experimental validation.
