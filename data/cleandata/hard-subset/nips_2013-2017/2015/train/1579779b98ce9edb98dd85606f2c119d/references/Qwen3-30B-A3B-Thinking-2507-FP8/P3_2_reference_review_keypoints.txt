- Proposes a non-greedy decision-tree learning method by casting tree induction as structured prediction with latent variables and a convex-concave upper bound for SGD optimization.  
- Introduces an algorithm with O(d²p) complexity, making deep tree learning computationally feasible versus naive O(2^d p).  
- Strong theoretical derivation connecting decision trees to structured prediction; addresses prior work's computational limitations.  
- Well-organized, clearly written paper with supplementary material that supports reproducibility.  
- Empirical results show consistent generalization improvements over greedy methods on multiple benchmarks.  
- Experiments are limited to classification and binary trees; lacks substantive regression results.  
- Fails to compare against state-of-the-art gradient-boosted tree methods (e.g., XGBoost, LightGBM).  
- Theoretical rationale for improved generalization (e.g., "cooperation between split nodes") is underdeveloped and superficial.  
- Scalability concerns remain: O(d²p) assumes small d, unclear behavior for very deep trees or extremely large datasets; no large-scale runtime metrics provided.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel, and significant contribution likely suitable for NeurIPS.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, requesting comparisons to gradient-boosted methods, regression experiments, deeper theoretical analysis, and more discussion/metrics on scalability.
