- Proposes a non-greedy decision-tree optimization that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters under a global objective.
- Establishes a novel connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.
- Formulates a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss that can be optimized efficiently with stochastic gradient descent.
- Main technical contribution: reduces gradient computation from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), making deep tree training practical.
- Handles general loss functions and enables efficient training on large datasets.
- Builds on prior work (e.g., Bennett, structured prediction literature) but significantly advances efficiency and scalability.
- Theoretical foundation is sound with clear proofs of the upper bound and complexity analysis.
- Experimental results show consistent superiority over greedy baselines (CO2, OC1, axis-aligned trees) with better generalization and less overfitting.
- Exposition and algorithm descriptions are clear and well-structured.
- Significance is high: addresses a fundamental decision-tree limitation, maintains computational advantages, and opens new research avenues.
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent deep learning–based tree methods (e.g., neural decision forests).
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of potential failure cases and scalability limits on very large datasets beyond Connect4.
- Weakness: could better contextualize relations to recent tree-based ensemble methods.
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution, meets NIPS criteria, recommend acceptance.
