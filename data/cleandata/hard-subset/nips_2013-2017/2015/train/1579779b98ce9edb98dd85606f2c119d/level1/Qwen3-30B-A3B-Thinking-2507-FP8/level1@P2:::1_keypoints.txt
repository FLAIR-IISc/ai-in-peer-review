- Proposes joint non-greedy optimization of split functions and leaf parameters for decision trees instead of standard greedy induction.  
- Establishes a novel connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss (surrogate objective) and justifies it theoretically (eq.7, Prop.1).  
- Optimizes the surrogate with stochastic gradient descent and reduces computational complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), enabling efficient deep-tree training.  
- Empirically outperforms greedy baselines across multiple classification benchmarks.  
- Builds on prior non-greedy and structured-prediction work (Bennett, Yu & Joachims) and extends CO2 to a truly non-greedy method.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear proofs, complexity analysis, and careful treatment of regularization/hyperparameters (ν, η).  
- Presents a complete, reproducible solution including a precise algorithm (Algorithm 1) and helpful figures (e.g., accuracy vs. depth).  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized; problem formulation and notation are clear.  
- Originality: the theoretical reframing bridging trees and structured prediction is a significant, generalizing contribution beyond prior limited approaches.  
- Significance: addresses long-standing limitations of greedy tree induction, offers principled regularization via W-norm, and is applicable to large-scale problems.  
- Strengths summarized: insightful theory, efficient O(d^2 p) algorithm, consistent empirical gains, clear exposition, and analysis of regularization/pruning.  
- Weaknesses: lacks comparisons to modern tree ensembles like XGBoost/LightGBM.  
- Weaknesses: could better justify why their specific structured-prediction formulation yields the proposed upper bound rather than alternatives.  
- Weaknesses: experiments limited to classification; no regression benchmarks to demonstrate full generality.  
- Weaknesses: training-time speedup analysis lacks detailed breakdowns for larger datasets beyond Connect4.  
- Recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance (NIPS) despite noted comparison shortcomings.
