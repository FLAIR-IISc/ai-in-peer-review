This paper presents a novel approach for non-greedy optimization of decision trees, which addresses the limitations of traditional greedy algorithms. The authors establish a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables, and propose a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss. They develop an efficient algorithm for optimizing the surrogate objective using stochastic gradient descent (SGD), which enables effective training with large datasets.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The introduction provides a good overview of the problem and the limitations of traditional greedy algorithms. The related work section is thorough and provides a good context for the proposed approach.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant. The authors propose a novel formulation of decision tree learning that associates a binary latent decision variable with each split node in the tree. They also develop a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss, which is optimized using SGD. The fast loss-augmented inference algorithm is also a significant contribution, as it reduces the computational complexity of the algorithm.

The experimental results are impressive, and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The authors compare their method with several greedy baselines and show that their approach achieves better test performance across multiple datasets. They also demonstrate that their approach is less susceptible to overfitting and achieves better generalization performance.
