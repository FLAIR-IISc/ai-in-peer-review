- Proposes non-greedy joint optimization of split functions and leaf parameters for decision trees instead of traditional greedy induction.
- Frames tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables and derives a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss for optimization.
- Key technical contribution: reduces gradient computation complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), enabling feasible training of deeper trees.
- Introduces a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm that makes SGD-based training efficient.
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements over greedy baselines on multiple datasets (SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST).
- Provides an ablation study on regularization (ν) and comparison to a greedy special case (CO2) to isolate method benefits.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and includes useful supplementary material.
- Method is general (works with any loss) and has practical significance for large-scale problems and future extensions (sparse splits, kernels).
- Connection to structured prediction is stated but not deeply justified or connected to specific techniques.
- Limited comparison to other non-greedy methods beyond older work (e.g., omits discussion/experiments with recent approaches like Mondrian Forests).
- Insufficient analysis of scalability with respect to dataset size; experiments use moderate-sized datasets only.
- Overall recommendation: technically strong, significant contribution with convincing experiments — reviewer recommends acceptance (Strong Accept).
