- Proposes a novel non-greedy optimization method for decision trees linking tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Introduces a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective.  
- Optimizes the surrogate objective efficiently using stochastic gradient descent (SGD).  
- Develops a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm that reduces computational complexity.  
- Algorithm scales to large datasets due to SGD-based training.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and thorough related work.  
- Technical novelty: binary latent decision variables for split nodes and the surrogate formulation.  
- Experimental results show superior test performance, reduced overfitting, and better generalization vs. greedy baselines.  
- Strength: addresses limitations of traditional greedy decision tree algorithms.  
- Strength: strong linkage to structured prediction and a principled surrogate loss.  
- Strength: significant empirical performance and computational improvements.  
- Weakness: assumes binary trees; unclear extension to non-binary trees.  
- Weakness: no theoretical analysis of SGD convergence provided.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to other non-greedy decision tree algorithms.  
- Overall ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10; reviewer recommends acceptance.
