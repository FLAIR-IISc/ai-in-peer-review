- Proposes a novel non-greedy algorithm for optimizing decision trees by formulating learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Shows that finding optimal oblique (linear-combination) splits is related to structured prediction and derives a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with a concise introduction.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and provides a good overview.  
- Problem formulation and proposed algorithm are clearly and thoroughly explained.  
- Technical soundness is good; proof of the upper bound is provided in the supplement.  
- The approach is highly original and offers a new perspective on decision-tree optimization.  
- The work is significant and demonstrates effectiveness on several benchmark datasets.  
- Strength: clarity and quality of exposition.  
- Strength: comprehensive related work and solid mathematical framework.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with decision trees and structured prediction.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of time and space complexity.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed robustness analysis to noise and outliers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, clear presentation, empirical effectiveness.  
- Arguments for rejection: missing complexity and robustness analyses and some assumed background knowledge.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication.
