- Proposes a non-greedy decision-tree learning algorithm framing tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables and a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Jointly optimizes split functions across all tree levels, enabling training of deep trees via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Key technical contributions: derivation of the convex–concave upper bound and efficient O(d) gradient computation.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over greedy baselines and analyze the regularization parameter ν and its pruning effects.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and presents a novel theoretical perspective on decision-tree learning.  
- Related work coverage is insufficient, lacking depth on recent non-greedy methods beyond CO2 and missing other relevant structured-prediction/optimization works.  
- Complexity analysis is brief and should include a more detailed breakdown, especially constant factors.  
- Empirical evaluation should include more diverse and higher-dimensional datasets and comparisons to more recent state-of-the-art methods.  
- Explanation of “Stable SGD” (SSGD) is too brief and needs clearer rationale and implementation details.  
- Notation can be dense and would benefit from simpler variable names or clearer explanations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses greediness limitation, offers a novel/theoretically sound approach, and shows clear empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete related work, insufficiently comprehensive empirical evaluation, and some technical explanations lack detail.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
