- Tackles greedy decision tree induction by proposing non-greedy joint optimization of oblique split functions and leaf parameters.
- Frames tree learning as structured prediction with latent routing decisions per input.
- Empirical loss is intractable; authors derive a convex-concave upper bound inspired by latent structured SVMs.
- Optimizes the surrogate objective using stochastic gradient descent.
- Naive loss-augmented inference is exponential in depth; authors propose a fast inference constrained to a Hamming ball radius 1.
- Fast inference reduces per-step complexity to O(d^2 p), making training of deep trees practical.
- Thorough experiments against multiple baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique, CO2) show consistent test accuracy and generalization gains.
- High technical quality: sound formulation, correct derivations, and well-supported claims.
- Exceptionally clear writing and presentation, with helpful figures and algorithm box.
- Highly original connection between decision trees and latent structured prediction, and novel constrained loss-augmented inference.
- Significant potential impact: more accurate, compact, interpretable single trees and avenues for extensions (L1, kernels).
- Missing analysis of sensitivity to initialization and how much improvement comes from non-greedy refinement versus greedy start.
- Lacks exploration of using non-greedy trees as base learners in ensembles (practical relevance).
- Computational trade-off concern: O(d^2 p) is better than exponential but costlier than greedy O(d p log N); wall-clock comparisons would help.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept (clear, significant contribution).
