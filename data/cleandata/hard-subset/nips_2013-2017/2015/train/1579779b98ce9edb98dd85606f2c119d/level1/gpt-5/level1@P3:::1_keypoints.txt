- Proposes non-greedy training for oblique decision trees by treating hard split decisions as latent binary vector h and framing learning as latent structured prediction.  
- Derives a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss similar to latent SSVMs and introduces two surrogates: a full bound and an efficient radius-1 Hamming-ball bound.  
- Radius-1 bound makes loss-augmented inference tractable (O(d^2 p) per example) while retaining hard routing at test time.  
- Training uses SGD with per-row L2 norm constraints on W and a “Stable SGD” (SSGD) variant that intermittently fixes leaf assignments to avoid collapsed leaves.  
- Empirical results on LibSVM multiclass datasets show consistent improvements over CART, OC1, random oblique trees, and a greedy CO2 baseline, especially at moderate-to-deep depths.  
- Paper situates work well among non-greedy/global tree methods, soft/probabilistic trees, hierarchical mixtures of experts, and margin-based tree literature.  
- Strength: elegant, novel latent structured prediction view and a clever radius-1 Hamming-ball bound for efficiency.  
- Strength: practical per-example complexity and hard routing retained; per-row projection regularizes splits and implicitly prunes.  
- Strength: empirical sensitivity study over ν and training-time analysis supports practicality.  
- Weakness: radius-1 surrogate is looser than full bound; needs ablation over radii (r=0,1,2) to quantify trade-offs in tightness, accuracy, and runtime.  
- Weakness: potential dependence on initialization and nonconvexity; should report sensitivity to random W and different greedy seeds and consider multiple restarts.  
- Weakness: limited baseline scope—should evaluate effect in ensembles (Random Forests/Boosting) and compare to soft trees/HME for accuracy vs inference cost.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical generalization or convergence analysis under per-row L2 constraints; could include margin/Rademacher or convergence discussion.  
- Weakness: SSGD heuristic underspecified (duration of fixed assignments, stopping criteria); needs empirical ablation vs SGD.  
- Weakness: implementation/complexity claims unclear—clarify sparse projection implementation and report wall-clock times on larger datasets.  
- Quality: method is technically sound; bounds and gradients are well-motivated and fast inference trick is correct under modified bound.  
- Clarity: generally clear and organized; suggest adding pseudocode/schematic for fast loss-augmented inference and explicit path-index definitions plus SSGD scheduling details.  
- Originality: latent structured prediction formulation for hard oblique trees and the radius-1 efficient bound are novel contributions.  
- Significance: advances single-tree training practically; greater impact if shown to improve ensembles/boosting.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled formulation, efficient training, hard routing with O(dp) inference, consistent empirical gains, and clear literature connections.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited surrogate/radius exploration, no ensemble/boosting evaluation, underspecified SSGD, and missing generalization/convergence analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: leaning accept, contingent on addressing ablations, initialization sensitivity, SSGD details, and broader baselines in the final version.
