- Presents a novel non-greedy training method for decision trees that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters.
- Formulates a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss to enable global optimization of the tree.
- Connects the method to structured prediction with latent variables, providing theoretical grounding.
- Derives an efficient O(d) gradient computation via a reformulation, enabling SGD training of deep trees.
- Builds on and positions itself against prior greedy and improved-greedy decision tree work.
- Empirical results show performance gains over several greedy baselines, including CO2 (Norouzi et al. ’15).
- Writing is generally clear and the technical contributions are well explained.
- Strength: addresses long-standing sub-optimality of greedy induction with solid theory and promising experiments.
- Strength: the gradient speedup is a significant practical advancement for non-greedy optimization.
- Weakness: some theoretical justifications (e.g., Proposition 1) need more elaboration and intuition.
- Weakness: experimental section lacks a detailed ablation study on the regularization parameter ν.
- Weakness: comparisons are relatively narrow; more recent forest-based methods should be included.
- Weakness: current method is restricted to binary classification; extensions to multiclass/regression are needed.
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments and clarify theory.
