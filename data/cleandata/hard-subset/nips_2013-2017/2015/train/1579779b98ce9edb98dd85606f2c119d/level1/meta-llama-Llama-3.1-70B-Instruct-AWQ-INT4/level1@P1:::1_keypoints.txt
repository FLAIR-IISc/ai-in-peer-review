- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree algorithm that formulates tree learning as a global optimization minimizing empirical loss.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Develops a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it with SGD to enable learning of deep trees.  
- Builds on greedy methods (ID3/CART) and structured prediction literature; the global formulation is claimed novel.  
- Strength: efficient optimization via the convex-concave upper bound and SGD.  
- Strength: joint/global optimization of split functions and leaf parameters may improve generalization.  
- Strength: framework is flexible and supports arbitrary loss functions for various ML and vision tasks.  
- Strength: experiments show the non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines on several benchmarks.  
- Weakness: time complexity O(d^2 p) can be slow for very deep trees.  
- Weakness: requires tuning multiple hyperparameters (e.g., regularization ν and learning rate η).  
- Weakness: joint-optimized split functions may reduce interpretability compared to traditional trees.  
- Pro acceptance: empirical gains and novel global optimization formulation justify publication.  
- Pro acceptance: convex-concave bound enables practical parameter optimization with SGD.  
- Con acceptance: computational cost for deep trees raises scalability concerns.  
- Con acceptance: hyperparameter tuning burden may limit practical usability.  
- Con acceptance: reduced interpretability is a drawback for some applications.  
- Quality: paper is well-written with thorough experiments demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Clarity: well-organized, easy to follow, and uses consistent notation.  
- Originality: the global formulation and convex-concave bound represent a novel departure from traditional tree methods.  
- Significance: approach could lead to better generalization and inspire further research in ML and computer vision.
