- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree learning method framing tree split optimization as structured prediction with latent variables.
- Jointly optimizes split functions at all tree levels using a convex-concave surrogate objective and stochastic gradient descent.
- Derives an efficient gradient computation claimed to be O(d) in tree depth, enabling scalability to deeper trees.
- Technical derivation of the surrogate objective and gradient computation is sound and convincing.
- Demonstrates empirical improvements over greedy baselines on several LibSVM benchmark datasets.
- Experiments lack thorough ablation analyses (e.g., effect of regularization parameter ν) and comparison to more recent tree methods like gradient-boosted trees.
- Authors acknowledge remaining computational challenges for very deep trees despite optimizations.
- Paper is generally well-written and organized with clear motivation.
- Some mathematical derivation sections are dense and would benefit from more intuitive explanations and earlier placement of pseudocode.
- Notation is consistent but the introduction of many variables requires careful reading.
- Joint optimization of splits is not entirely novel, but the structured-prediction formulation with latent variables is a fresh contribution.
- Efficient gradient computation combined with the formulation makes non-greedy optimization feasible for relatively deep trees.
- Approach is flexible (works with general loss functions) and addresses greedy optimization's tendency to find suboptimal trees.
- Performance gains are statistically significant but not large in magnitude.
- Limited discussion on hyperparameter tuning and broader related work (e.g., deep-learning-based tree methods).
- Overall assessment: technically strong, empirically validated, and promising—reviewer recommends acceptance.
