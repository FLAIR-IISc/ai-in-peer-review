- Novel theoretical connection between decision tree induction and structured prediction with latent variables, centered on a convex-concave upper bound (Eq. 7).  
- Major computational advance: reduces gradient computation from exponential O(2^d p) to polynomial O(d^2 p), enabling deep non-greedy tree learning.  
- Solid experimental validation on multiple benchmarks (SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST) showing non-greedy trees generalize better than greedy baselines.  
- Practical algorithmic contribution: Stable SGD (SSGD) variant that mitigates inactive-leaf issues and is clearly described with pseudocode.  
- Paper clearly differentiates and improves upon prior global optimization work (e.g., Bennett 1995).  
- Strengths summarized: novel theory, significant complexity reduction, consistent empirical gains, practical algorithm, and useful regularization.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison — omits modern ensemble baselines like XGBoost and LightGBM.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees on convergence and formal generalization bounds for the non-greedy optimization.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical limitations such as memory requirements and scaling with very large feature spaces.  
- Weakness: insufficient engagement with prior critiques (Murthy & Salzburg 1994) about overfitting and how the regularization fully addresses them.  
- Overall assessment: substantial, well-executed contribution that outweighs weaknesses; recommendation to accept.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theory, computational tractability, consistent empirical improvements, and a practical, stable training method.  
- Arguments against (minimal): missing comparisons to state-of-the-art ensembles, no convergence/generalization proofs, and incomplete response to historical overfitting concerns.
