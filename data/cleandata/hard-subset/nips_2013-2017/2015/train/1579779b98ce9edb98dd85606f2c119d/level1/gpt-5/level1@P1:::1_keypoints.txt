- Main contribution: Proposes non-greedy training for oblique decision trees via a latent-variable structured-prediction formulation with a convex–concave upper bound and a radius-1 Hamming-ball to reduce per-example complexity to O(d^2 p), trained with SGD and a stabilized SSGD variant.
- Relation to prior work: Connects hard-decision trees to structured SVM literature; novel relative to MILP/max-margin and soft-split models, with CO2 [18] as a depth-1 special case.
- Soundness: Surrogate bound is standard and scale-dependent (handled via norm constraints); fast Hamming-ball bound and SGD/projection updates are valid; optimization is non-convex but piecewise linear and tractable per-example.
- Empirical results: Shows consistent accuracy gains and reduced overfitting on several LibSVM datasets, analyzes ν effects and runtime scaling, but lacks variance/error bars, ablations, and ensemble comparisons.
- Limitations: No theoretical generalization or convergence guarantees; sensitivity to ν and learning-rate schedules is underexplored.
- Clarity: Paper is well written overall, but needs a worked example of loss-augmented inference, explicit bias handling in the algorithm, SSGD schedule details, and discussion of Θ regularization/temperature scaling.
- Originality: Novel formulation and efficient Hamming-ball inference extend prior depth-1 work to full trees and tie tree induction to latent structured prediction.
- Significance: Improves single-tree accuracy while preserving O(d p) inference, relevant for interpretability and settings where ensembles are impractical; greater practical impact would follow ensemble experiments.
- Pros: Principled latent-structured approach, efficient loss-augmented inference, per-example gradient cost independent of leaf count, scalable SGD training, and empirical gains over greedy baselines.
- Cons: Experiments limited to single trees on mid-scale data, missing ablations and statistical significance, limited theoretical analysis, and some implementation details omitted.
- Suggested improvements: Add ablations (SSGD vs SGD, initializations, ν sweep, Hamming radius), report variance/significance, compare to ensembles/boosting/GBRT, provide runtime/memory figures, explore sparse W (group-lasso), and release code.
- Recommendation: Strong paper with principled, efficient method and solid empirical benefits; recommend acceptance pending expanded experiments (especially ensemble comparisons).
