- Proposes a novel non-greedy method that jointly optimizes split functions in decision trees, linking tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.
- Formulates a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss that enables efficient optimization via SGD.
- Demonstrates a reduction in gradient computation complexity (reported from O(2dp) to O(d2p)), allowing scalable training of deep trees.
- Empirical results show improved performance over greedy baselines across several datasets, though gains are modest.
- Paper connects well to existing literature in decision trees and structured prediction and includes both theoretical and empirical analyses.
- Strength: clear novelty in departing from greedy induction and leveraging structured prediction techniques.
- Strength: theoretically motivated derivation and convincing analysis of gradient complexity.
- Strength: scalability claim backed by complexity reduction and SGD-based training.
- Strength: experiments and writing are generally clear and accessible.
- Weakness: empirical improvements are not dramatic and lack comparison to stronger state-of-the-art methods beyond greedy baselines.
- Weakness: sensitivity to the regularization parameter ν raises concerns about robustness and practical usability.
- Weakness: reliance on greedy (CO2) initialization with unclear impact; unclear whether random initialization performs similarly.
- Weakness: focus is mainly on binary classification trees; extensions to N-ary trees and regression are not thoroughly discussed.
- Weakness: some SSGD algorithmic details are insufficiently specified (e.g., criterion for "negligible improvement"), hindering reproducibility.
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, scalable, and empirically promising work addressing a significant problem.
- Against acceptance: modest empirical gains, hyperparameter sensitivity, limited comparisons, and missing algorithmic details.
- Overall assessment: solid and innovative contribution but with modest results and reproducibility/robustness concerns — reviewer recommends weak accept and suggests expanded experiments, hyperparameter analysis, and clearer limitations/future work.
