- Proposes non-greedy training for oblique decision trees via a global surrogate objective using binary latent routing variables and a structured upper bound.
- Derives two bounds: a full bound (max over all binary codes) and a fast bound restricting loss-augmented inference to a Hamming ball radius-1 for tractability.
- Fast bound yields O(d^2 p) per-example updates for depth d and feature dimension p.
- Optimizes with Stable-SGD and enforces per-row norm constraints to prevent trivial scaling and to regularize split weights.
- Theoretical formulation linking sgn(Wx) to argmax over latent codes and the structured upper bound is sound and well-motivated.
- Complexity analysis and the fast bound as a compromise between tightness and tractability are carefully argued.
- Experiments on several LibSVM multiclass datasets show consistent single-tree accuracy improvements over axis-aligned greedy trees, OC1, random oblique splits, and CO2.
- Training-time scaling benefits of the fast bound are convincingly demonstrated.
- Does not report accuracy trade-off between fast vs full bound.
- Empirical scope is limited to single trees on a handful of datasets; no regression tasks included.
- No statistical significance testing reported.
- Limited ablations: impact of Stable-SGD vs plain SGD, initialization sensitivity, and hyperparameter effects not quantified.
- Baselines include strong greedy and oblique single-tree methods, but comparisons to ensembles (random forests, boosting) and margin-based split-node methods are missing.
- Paper is well organized and generally clear; Algorithm 1 and proofs are accessible.
- Clarity could improve with more intuition for the navigation function f, examples of off-path bit cancellation, and full hyperparameter details.
- Reproducibility suffers from missing training protocol details and Stable-SGD schedule; code release recommended.
- Originality: novel combination of latent-structured surrogate, per-row norm constraints, and radius-1 loss-augmented inference tailored to trees.
- Potential significance: practical value for large-scale classification if method scales to forests and larger tasks; surrogate could enable kernelized or sparsity-regularized extensions.
- Strengths: principled structured-prediction framing, efficient fast bound with rigorous complexity reduction, consistent empirical gains, sensible regularization.
- Weaknesses: narrow empirical evaluation, missing ensembles/regression, insufficient ablations and sensitivity analyses, lack of generalization theory.
- Suggested extensions: train and evaluate forests, include regression tasks, add statistical tests, ablate Stable-SGD/initialization/ν, and quantify full vs fast bound accuracy/runtime trade-offs.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel efficient surrogate, theoretical clarity, and consistent empirical improvements.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical breadth, missing key comparisons and ablations, and unquantified fast vs full bound impact.
- Recommendation: weak accept pending modest empirical extensions; reviewer confidence medium.
