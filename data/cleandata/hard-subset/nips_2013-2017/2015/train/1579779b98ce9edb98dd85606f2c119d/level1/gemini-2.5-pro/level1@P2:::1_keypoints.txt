- Proposes a novel non-greedy algorithm for training oblique decision trees by optimizing all splits and leaves jointly.  
- Frames tree learning as structured prediction with latent routing variables and expresses empirical loss in terms of these latents.  
- Derives a continuous, piecewise-smooth convex-concave upper bound as a surrogate objective optimized by SGD.  
- Addresses exponential complexity of loss-augmented inference with a fast approximation restricting search to a Hamming ball radius 1.  
- Fast inference reduces per-update complexity to O(d^2 p), making deep-tree training practical.  
- Empirical results on four datasets show the non-greedy trees outperform several greedy baselines and generalize better.  
- Paper is technically strong: sound formulation, principled surrogate, and a clever practical approximation.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with consistent notation and a useful algorithm summary.  
- Highly original: connects decision tree induction to latent-variable structured SVMs and differs from prior non-greedy heuristics.  
- Potentially significant: offers a modern, principled alternative to decades-old greedy tree induction and suggests many extensions.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detail and ablation on initialization sensitivity and its effect on final performance.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to single trees; no comparison to ensembles like Random Forests or Gradient Boosted Trees.  
- Weakness: The "Stable SGD" heuristic to prevent inactive leaves is under-justified and needs empirical comparison to standard SGD.  
- Weakness: Lacks absolute training-time comparisons against fast greedy baselines, only shows relative speedup of fast inference.  
- For acceptance: novel principled formulation, practical solution to exponential bottleneck, strong empirical gains, and promising future directions.  
- Against acceptance: limited evaluation scope (single trees) and incomplete description/justification of initialization and SSGD.  
- Overall assessment: strengths substantially outweigh weaknesses; work is complete, impactful, and likely to influence tree-based model training.
