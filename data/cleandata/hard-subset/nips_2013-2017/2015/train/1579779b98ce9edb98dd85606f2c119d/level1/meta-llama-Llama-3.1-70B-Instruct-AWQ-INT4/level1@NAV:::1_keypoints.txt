- Proposes a novel non-greedy algorithm that jointly optimizes split functions across the whole tree.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it with SGD to learn deep trees.  
- Builds on prior decision-tree work but generalizes to any loss while preserving O(d p) inference complexity.  
- Strength: joint optimization improves generalization versus greedy methods.  
- Strength: applicable to both classification and regression with arbitrary loss functions.  
- Strength: SGD enables efficient training of deep trees.  
- Weakness: requires careful hyper-parameter tuning (e.g., regularization ν and learning rate η).  
- Weakness: computational complexity of O(d^2 p) can be high for deep trees.  
- Weakness: may not scale well to very large datasets due to quadratic growth with training examples.  
- Originality/significance: novel formulation and theoretical link to structured prediction are meaningful contributions.  
- Acceptance trade-off: novelty, generality, and efficiency favor acceptance, while hyperparameter sensitivity and scalability concerns argue against.
