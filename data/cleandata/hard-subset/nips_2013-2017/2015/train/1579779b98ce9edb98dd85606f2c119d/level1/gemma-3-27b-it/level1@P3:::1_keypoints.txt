- Proposes a novel non-greedy algorithm that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters via a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Connects decision tree optimization to structured prediction with latent variables, a promising and original direction.  
- Derivation of the convex-concave upper bound and gradient-complexity analysis are well-explained and technically sound.  
- Reduces gradient-computation complexity from O(2dp) to O(d2p), enabling training of deeper trees.  
- Demonstrates feasibility of training deep trees with stochastic gradient descent and shows consistent empirical improvements over greedy baselines.  
- Empirical evaluation is reasonably comprehensive but would benefit from stronger scalability tests on larger datasets.  
- Paper lacks detailed discussion of the assumptions behind the upper bound and how violations affect performance.  
- Regularization parameter ν is introduced without rigorous theoretical justification beyond Proposition 1.  
- Ablation study is limited; specifically, the impact of SSGD (Stable SGD) and greedy pre-training is not isolated.  
- Writing and organization are generally good but repetitive in places and could be clearer.  
- Dense notation makes some derivations hard to follow without careful attention.  
- Algorithm 1 is helpful but lacks explanatory comments for each step.  
- The section on fast loss-augmented inference feels rushed and would benefit from expansion or a visual example.  
- Insufficient discussion and comparison with other structured-prediction methods (e.g., CRFs) and deeper exploration of connections to Mondrian Processes.  
- Potentially significant contribution to overcoming greedy-tree limitations, likely of interest to researchers and practitioners.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen theoretical justification, expand ablations, improve clarity, and better situate related-work comparisons.
