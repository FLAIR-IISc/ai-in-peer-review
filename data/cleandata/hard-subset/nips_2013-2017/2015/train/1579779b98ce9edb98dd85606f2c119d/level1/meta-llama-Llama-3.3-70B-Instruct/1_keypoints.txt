- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree learning method that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters across the whole tree.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Introduces a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective.  
- Optimizes the surrogate using stochastic gradient descent (SGD), enabling scalable training.  
- Demonstrates efficient learning of deep trees and scalability to large datasets.  
- Well-written paper with clear exposition and a thorough related-work section.  
- Experimental results show the non-greedy approach outperforms greedy decision tree baselines on classification benchmarks.  
- Method handles any general loss function without compromising tree inference efficiency.  
- Provides a natural regularization mechanism for joint tree parameter optimization to mitigate overfitting.  
- Main weakness: the optimization is non-convex and SGD may not reach a global optimum.  
- Assumes fixed tree depth, which may be unrealistic in some practical settings.  
- Potential sensitivity to hyperparameters (regularization constant, learning rate).  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept; scores — Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Overall assessment: significant, original contribution with promising accuracy/efficiency gains; limitations should be addressed in future work.
