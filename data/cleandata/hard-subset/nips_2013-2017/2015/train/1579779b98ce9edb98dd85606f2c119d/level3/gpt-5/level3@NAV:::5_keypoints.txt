- Proposes a global algorithm for learning oblique decision trees by optimizing a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss with per-node L2 constraints via SGD; casts tree induction as structured prediction with latent h = sgn(Wx).  
- Key technical contribution: reformulation enabling loss-augmented inference and a fast Hamming-ball-constrained variant that reduces complexity to O(d^2 p).  
- Situates work with structural SVMs and latent-variable methods; relates to prior non-greedy tree learning and soft-split models.  
- Empirical evaluation compares to axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique, and CO2 baselines; results are mixed—substantial gains on only a subset of datasets and higher computational cost.  
- Theoretical/formulation quality is solid: bounds and complexity analysis appear technically sound.  
- Reproducibility concern: crucial optimization details, especially for “Stable SGD” (SSGD), are under-specified.  
- Request to provide precise SSGD description and pseudocode: initialization and updates of data-to-leaf assignments a(x), update frequency, stopping rule, minibatch handling, and how SSGD ensures all leaves receive updates.  
- Clarify Figure 2: specify whether results use SGD or SSGD, whether “fast” loss-augmented inference was used, and define “active” vs. “inactive” leaf.  
- Algorithm 1 ambiguity: state whether Θ rows are projected onto the probability simplex per row or optimized unconstrained with softmax; if projection is used, provide the practical projection routine.  
- Clarify relationship between the “tightness” parameter ν and pruning: how ν is chosen and whether pruning is comparable across baselines.  
- Numerous minor presentation issues and typos that hinder understanding (confusing phrases lines 407–408; replace “argmax” with “max” in Eqs. (7)–(9); Figure 1 never referenced; corrupted sentences lines 141, 238, 301, 404; undefined FSGD at line 360).  
- Supplementary corrections requested: line 29 sign relation should be “=”, not “∈”; line 30 should use Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)) inside the loss, not sgn(Wx).  
- Originality: latent-structured formulation and bound-driven optimization are novel and elegant beyond existing work like CO2; Hamming-ball restriction is a neat idea.  
- Significance: moderate—principled and potentially influential, but current experiments do not convincingly demonstrate superiority over strong greedy/ensemble baselines and training cost is higher.  
- Pros: elegant principled formulation linking trees to latent structured prediction; clear surrogate objective; extensible framework.  
- Cons: inconsistent empirical gains and higher compute cost; missing algorithmic details (SSGD, Θ updates) hinder reproducibility; presentation and typo issues.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject unless authors strengthen experiments (including runtime/accuracy trade-offs vs strong baselines) and provide the requested clarifications and corrections.
