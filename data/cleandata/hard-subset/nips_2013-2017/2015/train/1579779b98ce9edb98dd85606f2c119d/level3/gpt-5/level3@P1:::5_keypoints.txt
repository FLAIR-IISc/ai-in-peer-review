- Proposes a non-greedy training algorithm for oblique decision trees by treating split decisions as latent binary variables and optimizing a global surrogate objective.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and uses SGD with per-row L2 constraints on split weights W.  
- Introduces radius-1 loss-augmented inference to reduce per-step training complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p) while keeping test-time inference O(d p).  
- Proposes Stable SGD (SSGD), which periodically fixes leaf assignments to stabilize training.  

Strengths:
- Elegant, principled latent-structured formulation that supports arbitrary loss functions.  
- Efficient per-example optimization via the Hamming-ball radius-1 trick.  
- Clear motivation and role for per-row norm constraints in controlling the bound and generalization.  
- Practical training details (initialization, projections, momentum/minibatches) make the approach usable.  
- Empirically, non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines on some benchmarks and may reduce overfitting.  

Weaknesses / Requests for clarification:
- Empirical gains are mixed and computational cost is higher; need quantified training-time and wall-clock trade-offs versus baselines for matched accuracy.  
- SSGD is under-specified: require precise initialization/update rules for leaf assignments, update frequency/criteria, whether sgn(Wx) or alternative is used, and pseudo-code including stopping and inactive-leaf handling.  
- Define “active” vs “inactive” leaves and report their counts/evolution during training.  
- Clarify whether Figure 2 uses SGD or SSGD and define “active leaves” if shown.  
- Algorithm 1 ambiguity: clarify whether Θ are unnormalized log-probabilities (softmaxed) or probabilities and explain any simplex projection (method and frequency).  
- Missing ablations: compare full bound (Eq.7) vs Hamming-ball bound (Eq.13) for accuracy/training-time trade-offs; report effect of ν and its interaction with depth on accuracy/active leaves.  
- Missing baselines: compare to soft decision trees/HME (EM-trained) and strong greedy ensembles (e.g., gradient-boosted trees) under matched test-time budgets.  
- Theory/optimization: need discussion of SGD/SSGD convergence on the nonconvex objective and empirical stability evidence (loss curves, variance across runs).  

Quality:
- Surrogate objective and radius-1 complexity reduction are sound, but experiments need stronger validation and more ablations to justify claims.  

Clarity:
- Generally well written but SSGD description is too high-level; provide pseudo-code, data preprocessing details, train/val/test splits, and hyperparameter search ranges.  

Originality:
- The latent-structured view and radius-1 loss-augmented inference for hard-split trees are novel adaptations of latent-SVM/structural SVM ideas.  

Significance:
- Potentially impactful if empirical gains are consistent; current significance is moderate pending stronger empirical guidance on when to use non-greedy training.  

Specific questions for authors:
- How sensitive is performance to initialization (axis-aligned vs CO2 vs random W)?  
- Does ν act as implicit pruning—report accuracy vs number of active leaves for varying ν?  
- What are memory and training-time footprints versus OC1 and random oblique trees at depth 12–18 on large datasets?  
- Are there failure modes where SSGD irreversibly collapses many leaves to inactive, and how to prevent this?  

Minor issues / typos:
- Equations (7)–(9): use “max” not “argmax” on RHS.  
- Figure 1 is introduced but not referenced later; reference when f(·) is defined.  
- Several corrupted/misspelled sentences (lines 141, 238, 301, 404) and undefined acronym “FSGD” (line 360).  
- Lines 407–408 phrasing about features is confusing—reword.  
- Supplement: fix sign notation and consistency of loss notation (use Θ^T f(sgn(Wx))).  

Pros:
- Principled global objective, tractable surrogate, O(d p) test-time inference, and clear regularization knob for pruning/generalization.  

Cons:
- Mixed empirical gains, higher training cost, unclear SSGD/implementation details, and missing important ablations/comparisons.  

Overall:
- Solid and elegant formulation with novel ideas but currently borderline due to insufficient empirical validation and presentation details; stronger experiments and clarifications needed.
