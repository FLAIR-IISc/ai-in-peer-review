- Novel formulation: casts decision tree learning as structured prediction with binary latent split variables.  
- Optimization: derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss optimized by stochastic gradient descent.  
- Computational contribution: reduces gradient-update complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p) with a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm.  
- Theoretical grounding: provides solid analysis (Proposition 1) and shows norm regularization helps generalization.  
- Empirical weakness: performance gains over greedy baselines are inconsistent and significant only on a few datasets.  
- Comparison weakness: CO2 (a greedy variant) comparison does not convincingly show the value of global optimization.  
- SSGD unclear: Stable SGD description lacks detail on leaf assignment, constraints, and transitions; pseudo-code needed.  
- Figure 2 ambiguous: unspecified whether results use standard SGD or SSGD and “active leaves” is not defined.  
- Algorithm 1 unclear: no practical explanation of how Θ is projected onto the simplex or how row-sum constraints are enforced.  
- Minor issues: multiple typos, unclear phrasing, and errors in supplementary material and figure references.  
- Significance concern: higher computational cost than greedy methods and limited empirical gains raise questions about broad applicability.  
- Recommendation: technically sound and novel but needs clearer exposition and stronger empirical validation; currently borderline (recommend rejection pending revisions).
