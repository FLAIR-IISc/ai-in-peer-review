- Proposes a global, non-greedy training objective for oblique decision trees by casting routing as latent binary variables and optimizing a convex-concave surrogate.  
- Introduces a tightened Hamming-ball bound to reduce loss-augmented inference from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p) per example.  
- Uses per-row L2 constraints on W for regularization and proposes “Stable SGD” (SSGD) that freezes leaf assignments intermittently to avoid collapsed leaves.  
- Derivations and latent-variable formulation are sound and well-grounded in structured prediction theory.  
- Complexity reductions and the role of W scaling and regularization are clearly motivated.  
- SGD with per-row L2 projection is appropriate; SSGD idea is reasonable but underspecified.  
- Empirical gains over strong greedy baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique, CO2) are modest, inconsistent, and not fully quantified.  
- Runtime/accuracy trade-offs and statistical variability are not reported, weakening the empirical case.  
- Core formulation and connection to structured prediction are clear and well-explained.  
- Important algorithmic details are missing: precise SSGD pseudo-code, initialization/update/schedule of a(x), stopping criterion for reassignment, and definition/measurement of active vs inactive leaves.  
- Figure 2 needs clarification on whether curves are SGD or SSGD, plus reported ν and η per depth and definition of “active leaf.”  
- Algorithm 1 needs clarification on whether Θ is projected or left unconstrained and the exact projection operator if used.  
- O(d^2 p) inference claim should include a short pseudo-code sketch showing enumeration of d+1 candidate leaves and caching inner products to justify complexity.  
- Novelty: framing tree routing as latent variables and applying loss-augmented inference is novel; Hamming-ball bound is a neat contribution.  
- Significance: could influence oblique tree training if empirical benefits and computational overhead are convincingly demonstrated.  
- Current evidence: experiments do not convincingly show robust accuracy advantage across datasets and depths.  
- Requests: provide SSGD pseudo-code, track active leaves over training, compare SSGD vs SGD on accuracy/stability/runtime/inactive leaves.  
- Experimental requests: report mean/std over runs, significance tests, wall-clock times per epoch and total, accuracy vs time plots, sensitivity analyses for ν and η, and ablation over Hamming-ball radius.  
- Implementation requests: clarify use of momentum/mini-batches/regularization for Θ and initialization of W and Θ (from CO2) and sensitivity to initializer.  
- Minor issues/typos: fix Figure 1 references, correct argmax vs max in Eqs. (7)–(9), proofread corrupted lines (141, 238, 301, 404), reword confusing phrasing (407–408), define/replace undefined “FSGD.”  
- Supplementary fixes: replace “∈” with “=” where appropriate and replace sgn(Wx) with Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)) for consistency.  
- Pros: elegant principled connection to latent structured prediction, well-motivated surrogate, efficient O(d^2 p) inference, potential beyond depth-1.  
- Cons: modest/inconsistent empirical gains, missing SSGD and algorithmic details, limited ablations, and unquantified computational overhead.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; strong formulation and novelty but incomplete empirical support and missing algorithmic details need addressing.  
- Reviewer confidence: high on methodological assessment; medium on empirical interpretation pending clarified experiments.
