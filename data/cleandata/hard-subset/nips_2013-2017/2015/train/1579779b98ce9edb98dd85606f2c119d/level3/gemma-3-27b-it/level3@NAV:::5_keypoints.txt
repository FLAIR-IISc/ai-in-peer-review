- Proposes a novel algorithm for global optimization of decision trees with linear split functions, framing tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Core idea is elegant and provides a principled approach to joint optimization of split and leaf parameters, addressing limits of greedy induction.  
- Theoretical contribution includes the upper-bound derivation and analysis reducing per-iteration complexity to O(d²).  
- Introduces “Stable SGD” to address inactive leaves, a promising direction but described insufficiently.  
- Empirical evaluation is unconvincing: only modest gains over greedy baselines on few datasets and substantially higher computational cost.  
- Reproducibility/clarity issues: “Stable SGD” lacks pseudo-code and detailed explanation of data-point assignments.  
- Figure 2 is unclear about whether results use SGD or Stable SGD; definition of an “active” leaf is missing.  
- Algorithm 1 needs clarification on how projection of Θ onto the simplex is implemented (line 10) and should note it enforces row-sum-to-one.  
- Several minor errors: inconsistent equation formatting (argmax vs max in Eqs. 7–9), incorrect/missing references to Figure 1, and typos/corrupted sentences in paper and supplement.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation linking to structured prediction, solid theoretical analysis, and potential for future research.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical gains and high cost, unclear Stable SGD description, and numerous minor clarity/errors; significant revisions required.
