This paper proposes an algorithm for jointly training all decision-tree parameters, including both splits and leaf posteriors, via a single optimization with an upper-bound approximation for tractability. The authors establish a link between the decision tree optimization problem and the problem of structured prediction with latent variables, and formulate a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss.

The main ideas of the paper are well-motivated and well-explained, and the authors provide a clear overview of the existing literature on decision trees and structured prediction. The proposed solution is valuable, but has some notable shortcomings that affect its significance. The derivation of the upper bound is clean, but the experiments and their presentation need improvement.

One potential flaw in the empirical loss bound is the argument around line 216, which is confusing and seems to be incorrect. The proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement is also incomplete. Furthermore, the authors note that some leaves receive no data points, which may indicate issues with the objective or initialization. The fixed-assignment heuristic seems suboptimal and could be better motivated, for example, by using EM or CCCP.

The necessity and impact of the efficient loss-augmented inference are also questioned. Since the tree depth is limited to 16, brute-force inference is feasible, and the effect of restricting the search to a Hamming ball is unclear and needs broader validation. Additionally, the terminology used in the inference discussion is unclear, and it would be helpful to clarify what exactly is meant by "leaves" in this context.

The paper is missing a baseline comparison to a greedy training of non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization. Moreover, the limitation that the tree depth must be pre-specified is not thoroughly discussed and evaluated. The authors should provide more experiments varying the depth and discuss the implications of this limitation.

The paper is well-written overall, but some minor suggestions for improvement include replacing argmax with max in the loss bound expression.
