- Proposes a novel approach to decision tree learning by framing it as a structured prediction problem.
- Formulates a convex–concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss and optimizes it using SGD.
- Demonstrates non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines on benchmark datasets, though improvements are relatively small.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.
- Introduction and related work provide good background and thorough literature review.
- Technical sections give detailed, clear descriptions of the proposed algorithm and components.
- Establishes a meaningful link between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.
- Use of a convex–concave surrogate enables efficient optimization with SGD.
- Provides experimental validation and a detailed analysis of computational complexity.
- Concern: test accuracy improvements are modest, limiting practical impact.
- Concern: surrogate objective may not always provide a tight upper bound on true empirical loss.
- Concern: computational complexity could be problematic for large-scale applications.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; address small accuracy gains, surrogate tightness, and provide more complexity analysis.
