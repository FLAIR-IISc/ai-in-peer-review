- Proposes an elegant non-greedy decision tree optimization via a novel convex-concave upper bound, linking tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.
- Establishes a theoretical foundation for joint optimization of split functions across all tree levels, departing from conventional greedy methods.
- Frames the problem as structured prediction with latent variables, enabling efficient SGD optimization with O(d²p) complexity per gradient update.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and technically sound.
- Theoretical development is thorough and appropriately connected to structured prediction literature (e.g., Yu & Joachims, 2009).
- Experiments show consistent test accuracy gains over greedy baselines and reduced overfitting, especially for deeper trees, across multiple benchmarks.
- Strengths: clear theoretical motivation, elegant mathematical formulation, accessibility to the ML community, ability to handle arbitrary loss functions while retaining computational efficiency, and reproducibility.
- Main weakness: absolute performance gains are modest (typically 1–3%), which may limit practical impact.
- Insufficient analysis of whether global optimization might increase overfitting despite regularization; needs more detailed study of generalization gap across depths.
- Suggested future work: post-optimize the last split and leaf nodes with standard methods to potentially improve results.
- Overall assessment: original, high-quality contribution likely to spur further research and a strong candidate for publication despite modest empirical gains.
