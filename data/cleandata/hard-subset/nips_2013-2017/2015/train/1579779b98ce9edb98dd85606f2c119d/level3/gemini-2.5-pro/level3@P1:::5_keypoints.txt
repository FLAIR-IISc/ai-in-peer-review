- Proposes a novel non-greedy algorithm that jointly optimizes oblique split functions and leaf parameters via a surrogate objective.  
- Frames decision tree learning as structured prediction with the example's path as a latent variable.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it with SGD.  
- Introduces fast loss-augmented inference that reduces gradient-step complexity from exponential to O(d²p), enabling deeper trees.  
- Positions the method as a principled global alternative to greedy induction (CART/ID3) and some non-greedy approaches.  
- Strength: elegant, theoretically grounded formulation that could enable extensions (e.g., kernels, alternative regularizers).  
- Strength: principled global optimization with L2-norm regularization on split weights to mitigate overfitting.  
- Strength: computational improvements make global optimization practical for moderately deep trees.  
- Weakness: empirical results are unconvincing—performance gains over greedy methods are small given higher complexity and cost.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to modern, optimized tree/ensemble implementations (e.g., Scikit-learn, LightGBM, GBDT).  
- Weakness: Stable SGD (SSGD) is insufficiently described; leaf assignment a(x) initialization and update schedule are unclear.  
- Major request: provide full, clear pseudocode and details for Stable SGD, including how/when a(x) are set or updated.  
- Major request: clarify whether Figure 2 results use standard SGD or SSGD, since this affects interpretation.  
- Major request: formally define "active leaf" (e.g., a leaf assigned at least one training example during an epoch).  
- Major request: clarify how Θ (softmax leaf parameters) updates respect simplex constraints—projection or implicit handling?  
- Minor fixes: use max instead of argmax in Equations (7)–(9).  
- Minor fixes: reference Figure 1 in the text; define acronym FSGD explicitly; address multiple phrasing/clarity issues noted (lines 141, 238, 301, 407–408).
