- Proposes joint, non-greedy global optimization of decision trees by training splits and leaf posteriors with a single global objective.  
- Frames decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables (paths as latent variables).  
- Derives a continuous, convex-concave upper bound surrogate to make the non-convex, discontinuous loss tractable.  
- Optimizes the surrogate with stochastic gradient descent to scale to large datasets.  
- Provides an efficient loss-augmented inference algorithm that reduces complexity from exponential to polynomial in tree depth.  
- Strength: original and significant direction beyond greedy tree induction, promising for more compact and accurate trees.  
- Strength: insightful novel formulation connecting trees to structured prediction and enabling use of loss-augmented objectives.  
- Strength: generally clear presentation and clean derivation of the surrogate objective.  
- Major weakness: potential flaw in the core theoretical bound (Eq. 7) and an incomplete, unconvincing proof of Proposition 1.  
- Weakness: some leaves receive no training data, suggesting issues with objective, initialization, or SGD dynamics.  
- Weakness: the proposed Stable SGD heuristic for fixing leaf assignments is ad-hoc and lacks principled motivation; EM or CCCP might be more appropriate.  
- Experimental weakness: unclear necessity and impact of the fast loss-augmented inference (Hamming ball radius 1) — need comparisons or deeper-tree experiments.  
- Experimental weakness: missing baseline of a greedy oblique-tree trainer without CO2 initialization to isolate gains from non-greedy optimization.  
- Experimental weakness: tree depth must be pre-specified; paper should discuss this limitation and evaluate broader depth ranges for overfitting effects.  
- Experimental weakness: lacks runtime comparisons against standard greedy algorithms to assess practical trade-offs.  
- Minor issues: clarify "solving (11) for every j" (which leaves), and use max instead of argmax in the loss-bound expression.  
- Recommendation: provide a rigorous, complete proof for the theoretical bound and strengthen experiments with additional baselines, depth analyses, and runtime comparisons.
