- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree learning method that optimizes an upper bound on empirical loss via SGD, framing tree learning as structured prediction with latent binary split decisions.  
- Develops a convex-concave upper bound and an efficient loss-augmented inference algorithm (O(d²p)), enabling deeper tree training.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: novel, well-motivated connection between decision trees and structured prediction, building on prior CO2 work.  
- Empirical claims are weak: significant outperformance over greedy baselines appears only on a few datasets, with marginal gains elsewhere.  
- Computational cost is substantially higher for deeper trees, raising practical concerns about scalability.  
- Lacks statistical significance testing to support claimed performance improvements.  
- Description of Stable SGD (SSGD) is unclear: no definition of how/when leaf assignments are fixed or what “active leaf” means.  
- Algorithmic detail missing: Algorithm 1’s projection of Θ onto the simplex is unexplained (implementation and intended constraints on Θ rows).  
- Specific corrections requested: clarify whether Figure 2 shows SGD or SSGD; define FSGD; fix typos and confusing phrasing at cited lines; correct supplementary-material expressions (= vs ∈, replace sgn(Wx) with Θ^T f(sgn(Wx))).  
- Strengths: elegant theoretical formulation, novel efficient inference algorithm, and potential for extensions (sparse splits, kernels).  
- Weaknesses: unconvincing experimental validation, higher computational cost, unclear algorithmic components, and several minor errors—warranting major revisions before acceptance.
