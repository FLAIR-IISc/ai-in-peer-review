This paper proposes a novel approach to decision tree learning, framing the search for the best fixed-depth decision tree as a structured prediction problem. The authors develop a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss of the tree's predictions and optimize it using stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The paper also introduces a regularizer on the norm of the weight matrix to prevent overfitting.

The proposed method is related to previous work on structured prediction with latent variables, but it improves upon prior art by enabling efficient learning of deep trees and handling any general loss function. The paper also discusses the connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction, and it presents a novel formulation of the decision tree learning problem that associates a binary latent decision variable with each split node in the tree.

The experimental results show that the proposed non-greedy decision tree algorithm outperforms typical greedy tree-building methods on test accuracy, although the improvements are generally small. The results also suggest that the non-greedy approach is less susceptible to overfitting and achieves better generalization performance at various tree depths.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed method is well-motivated and theoretically grounded, and it addresses a significant limitation of traditional greedy decision tree algorithms.
* The paper presents a clear and concise formulation of the decision tree learning problem as a structured prediction problem.
* The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, and they demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, there are also some weaknesses:

* The improvements in test accuracy over greedy tree-building methods are generally small, and it is not clear whether the proposed method is significantly better in practice.
* The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method, although it mentions that the time complexity of the fast loss-augmented inference algorithm is O(d2p).
* The paper does not discuss the potential applications of the proposed method in real-world problems, although it mentions that decision trees are widely used in computer vision and machine learning.
