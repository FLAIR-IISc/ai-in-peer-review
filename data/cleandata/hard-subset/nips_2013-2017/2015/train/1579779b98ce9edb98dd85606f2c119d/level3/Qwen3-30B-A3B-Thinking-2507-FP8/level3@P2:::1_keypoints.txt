- Proposes a novel non-greedy framework to jointly optimize split functions and leaf parameters for decision trees.  
- Connects decision tree optimization to structured prediction with latent variables and formulates a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Key technical result: reduces gradient-update complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), enabling training of deep trees.  
- Uses stochastic gradient descent to efficiently optimize the proposed upper bound.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with a clear proof of the upper bound and thorough complexity analysis.  
- Empirical results show consistent, though modest, improvements over greedy baselines on multiple datasets (SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST).  
- Method appears less susceptible to overfitting but requires careful tuning of the regularization parameter ν and can produce inactive leaves.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and reproducible for experts.  
- Originality: provides a substantially more efficient global optimization approach compared to prior non-greedy work and innovatively leverages structured prediction.  
- Significance: method is scalable, general to various loss functions, and likely to spur further research in non-greedy tree learning.  
- Strength: elegant theoretical connection between trees and structured prediction.  
- Strength: significant computational efficiency improvement enabling deep tree training.  
- Strength: clear experimental demonstration of consistent gains over greedy methods.  
- Weakness: empirical gains are modest (typically 1–3% accuracy), potentially limiting immediate practical impact.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of whether global optimization increases overfitting, especially for deeper trees.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to recent tree/forest methods (e.g., Mondrian forests, Lakshminarayanan et al. 2014).  
- Suggested improvement: try dropping and re-optimizing the last split and its leaf node after global optimization to potentially boost performance.  
- Suggested improvement: conduct a more thorough investigation of overfitting behavior for deeper trees.  
- Suggested improvement: include comparisons to recent non-greedy/online tree methods like Mondrian forests.  
- Overall evaluation: high-quality, novel, and well-executed work that merits acceptance and is likely to be influential.
