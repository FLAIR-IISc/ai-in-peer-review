This paper proposes an elegant and efficient non-greedy method for optimizing decision trees with a fixed structure. The authors establish a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables, which enables them to formulate a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss. This bound is optimized using stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to find a locally optimal configuration of the split functions.

The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain their contributions and the advantages of their approach. The technical details are sound, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their algorithm.

The experimental results are somewhat disappointing, as the performance of the non-greedy decision trees is not significantly better than that of the greedy baselines. However, the authors provide a thoughtful analysis of the results and suggest potential ways to improve the performance of their method.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it does not fully evaluate the impact of global optimization on overfitting. The authors mention that the norm regularization provides a principled way to tune the tightness of the tree's fit to the training data, but they do not provide a thorough analysis of the effect of regularization on overfitting.

A potential suggestion for improving the results is to drop and re-optimize the last split and its leaf node using standard methods after global optimization. This could potentially improve the results by allowing the tree to adapt to the local structure of the data.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors make a significant contribution to the field of decision tree optimization. The paper is likely to spur further research in this area, and the authors' suggestions for future work are well-motivated and promising.
