- Proposes a novel, non-greedy algorithm that globally optimizes split functions and leaf parameters for a fixed tree structure.  
- Reframes decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables, treating each data point's path as a latent variable.  
- Derives a convex-concave surrogate (upper bound) for the discontinuous empirical loss, inspired by latent structured SVMs.  
- Optimizes the surrogate objective using stochastic gradient descent (SGD).  
- Introduces a "fast loss-augmented inference" algorithm that reduces per-step complexity from exponential in depth to polynomial, enabling practical training of deeper trees.  
- Positions the method as more scalable and general than prior non-greedy approaches and a principled improvement over greedy algorithms like CART/ID3.  
- Method is technically sound, with careful complexity analysis and a solid optimization procedure.  
- Experimental results show consistent but often modest improvements over strong greedy baselines across datasets.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with ideas presented step-by-step and algorithms well explained.  
- The approach is highly original, applying structured prediction ideas in a novel way to global tree optimization.  
- The work is significant conceptually and could spur further research and extensions (e.g., different regularizers or kernels), though practical impact is tempered by modest empirical gains.  
- Suggestion: consider a hybrid post-processing step (freeze upper levels, greedily re-optimize lower splits) to potentially boost final accuracy.  
- Suggestion: provide a more direct overfitting analysis (e.g., train–test accuracy gap vs. tree depth) to support claims about better generalization from norm regularization.  
- Overall recommendation: strong conceptual and technical contribution that merits publication despite relatively small empirical margins.
