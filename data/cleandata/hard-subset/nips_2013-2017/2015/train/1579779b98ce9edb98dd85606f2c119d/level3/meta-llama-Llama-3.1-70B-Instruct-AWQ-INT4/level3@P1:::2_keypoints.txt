- Proposes joint optimization of split functions and leaf parameters for decision trees using a single upper-bound approximation for tractability.
- Casts decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables and derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.
- Optimizes the bound with stochastic gradient descent to find locally optimal split configurations.
- Paper is well-written with a clean derivation and clear notation.
- Theoretical reasoning has issues: argument around line 216 is confusing and a counterexample suggests the claimed upper bound may not hold.
- Proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement is incomplete.
- Experimental presentation needs improvement: some leaves receive no data, indicating objective or initialization problems.
- The fixed-assignment heuristic appears suboptimal and lacks sufficient motivation.
- The claimed necessity of efficient loss-augmented inference is questioned since brute-force is feasible for depth ≤ 16.
- Missing important baselines, notably greedy training of non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization.
- Limitation that tree depth must be pre-specified is not discussed or empirically explored; should evaluate varying depths.
- Empirically, non-greedy jointly trained trees outperform greedy decision tree baselines on several classification benchmarks.
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
- Suggestions: fix theoretical flaws, improve experiments/presentation, add baselines, evaluate depth selection, replace argmax with max in loss bound, include runtime and more thorough empirical comparisons.
