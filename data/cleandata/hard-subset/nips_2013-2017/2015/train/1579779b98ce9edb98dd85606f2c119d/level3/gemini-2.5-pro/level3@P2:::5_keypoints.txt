- Novel formulation: casts global oblique decision-tree learning as structured prediction with latent routing variables.
- Technical contribution: derives a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective.
- Scalability trick: fast loss-augmented inference reduces per-update complexity from exponential to quadratic in tree depth.
- Optimization: objective optimized with SGD, making method applicable to large datasets.
- Generality: framework handles multiple loss functions, regularization, and potential extensions (sparse splits, kernels).
- Empirical weakness: experimental gains over greedy baselines (e.g., CO2) are marginal and inconsistent across datasets.
- Cost vs benefit: proposed method is substantially more computationally intensive, with small accuracy improvements that may not justify complexity.
- Clarity issues: description of Stable SGD (SSGD) is vague—initialization and update schedule of leaf assignments a(x) are unclear.
- Missing experimental detail: Figure 2 does not specify whether results use standard SGD or SSGD, hindering reproducibility.
- Undefined term: "active leaf" appears in Figure 3 without definition in the text.
- Algorithm question: Algorithm 1’s gradient update for leaf parameters Θ needs clarification given Θ are unnormalized softmax logits.
- Minor corrections: replace argmax with max in Eqs. (7)–(9), reference Figure 1, define "FSGD", and fix typos/awkward phrasing.
- Supplementary fixes: correct notation in supplement (use equality for sign constraints; pass Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)) to loss).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, principled formulation and a sound, practical technical advance (fast inference).
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical support and significant clarity/reproducibility issues that need addressing.
