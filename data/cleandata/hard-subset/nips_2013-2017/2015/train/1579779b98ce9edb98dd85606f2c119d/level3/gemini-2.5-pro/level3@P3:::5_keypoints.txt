- Proposes a novel non-greedy global optimization for oblique decision trees by framing tree learning as structured prediction with latent split outcomes.  
- Derives a convex–concave upper bound surrogate on empirical loss and optimizes it with SGD under L2 constraints to jointly learn split weights and leaf parameters.  
- Introduces a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm that reduces per-step complexity from exponential to quadratic in tree depth.  
- Strength: original and elegant connection between decision trees and structured prediction, giving a coherent global objective.  
- Strength: principled continuous surrogate enabling gradient-based scalable optimization and potential extensions (different losses, sparsity).  
- Weakness: empirical results are unconvincing—performance gains over greedy baselines are often marginal despite higher computational cost.  
- Weakness: clarity and reproducibility issues—Stable SGD (SSGD) is poorly described, figures lack details, and the manuscript has typos.  
- Ask authors to analyze when and why the global method outperforms greedy approaches and identify data characteristics that favor joint optimization.  
- Request clear specification of SSGD: how initial assignments a(x) are determined, the precise criterion for “negligible improvement,” and pseudocode for reproducibility.  
- Ask to state whether reported "Non-greedy (ours)" results use standard SGD or SSGD and to define the term “active” leaf.  
- Clarify Algorithm 1 line 10: confirm Θ updates are plain gradient steps with no projection (softmax ensures valid probabilities).  
- Technical correction: replace incorrect uses of argmax in Eqs. (7)–(9) with max (objective depends on maximum value, not the arg).  
- Minor fixes: reference Figure 1 in text; fix corrupted sentence at line 141; correct sgn(Wx)TWx notation; rephrase line 301; define FSGD; clarify line 407–408 about ν and generalization.  
- Supplementary corrections: use “=” in the sgn definition and verify consistency of Θ^T f(g) in the derivation.  
- Conclusion: theoretically strong and original work, but needs clearer exposition and stronger empirical evidence to justify practical advantages over greedy methods.
