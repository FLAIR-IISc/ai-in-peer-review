- Proposes an elegant, technically sound method for globally optimizing decision trees, addressing a long-standing challenge.
- Introduces a non-convex relaxation that enables joint optimization of split functions and leaf parameters versus greedy methods.
- Draws an insightful connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.
- Derives a tractable surrogate objective amenable to stochastic gradient descent.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, making technical details easy to follow.
- Demonstrates awareness of both strengths and limitations of the proposed approach.
- Core idea is a significant contribution given decades of reliance on greedy algorithms.
- Achieves a key technical improvement by reducing gradient computation from O(2^d p) to O(d 2^p) (enabling deeper trees).
- Thoroughly covers related work and clearly differentiates from prior global-optimization attempts.
- Includes a sensible regularization term to mitigate overfitting.
- Experimental results show positive but modest improvements over greedy baselines.
- Suggests that post-optimizing final splits and leaves could further improve empirical performance.
- Lacks a detailed analysis of potential increased overfitting from global optimization and the generalization gap.
- Needs a more thorough study of the effect of different regularization strengths and the impact of parameter ν.
- Calls for a detailed ablation study to quantify contributions of components (e.g., upper bound form, hard constraints on W).
- Strengths summarized: novelty, technical soundness, clarity, computational efficiency, and strong link to structured prediction.
- Weaknesses summarized: modest empirical gains, incomplete overfitting analysis, and insufficient ablation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, clearly presented, and likely to stimulate further research.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental empirical gains and incomplete analysis of generalization/overfitting.
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen empirical and generalization analyses.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
