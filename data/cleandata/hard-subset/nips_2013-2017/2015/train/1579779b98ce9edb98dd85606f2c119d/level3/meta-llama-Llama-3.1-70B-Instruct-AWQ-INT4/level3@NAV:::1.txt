This paper proposes a novel approach to decision tree learning, where the split functions at all levels of the tree are optimized jointly with the leaf parameters according to a global objective. The authors establish a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables, and develop a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss. They also propose a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to optimize the surrogate objective.

The paper is well-written, and the ideas are clearly presented. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and demonstrate the advantages of their approach over existing methods. The experimental results are promising, showing that the non-greedy decision trees outperform greedy decision tree baselines in several classification benchmarks.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors propose a novel and elegant approach to decision tree learning, which has the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in this area.
* The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the ideas and a thorough review of related work.
* The experimental results are promising, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the performance of their approach.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The experimental results are somewhat disappointing, as the authors note that the score is considered appropriate but not outstanding.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their approach, which is an important consideration in practice.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the potential applications and implications of the proposed approach.

Suggestions for improvement:

* The authors could consider dropping and re-optimizing the last split and its leaf node using standard methods to potentially improve the results.
* It would be important to evaluate whether the global optimization increases overfitting, and to provide a more detailed analysis of the trade-offs between the proposed approach and existing methods.
* The authors could consider providing more details on the implementation of their approach, including the choice of hyperparameters and the computational resources required.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a novel and elegant approach to decision tree learning. While the experimental results are somewhat disappointing, the authors provide a thorough analysis of the performance of their approach and highlight the potential advantages of their method. With some revisions to address the weaknesses and suggestions for improvement, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
