This paper proposes a novel approach to decision tree optimization, formulating the problem as a global optimization task with a fixed tree structure. The authors introduce a non-convex relaxation of the decision tree optimization problem, which enables the use of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) for efficient optimization. The proposed method is shown to outperform greedy decision tree baselines in several benchmark datasets.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The experimental results are somewhat disappointing, as the performance gain over greedy baselines is not as significant as one might expect. However, the authors do demonstrate that their approach can lead to better generalization performance and reduced overfitting.

One potential limitation of the proposed method is that it may not always find the optimal solution, as the non-convex relaxation may lead to local optima. Additionally, the authors do not provide a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of their approach, which could be an important consideration for large-scale datasets.

A potential suggestion for improving the results is to combine the global optimization approach with standard decision tree optimization techniques. For example, after performing global optimization, the last split and its corresponding leaf node could be dropped and re-optimized using standard methods. This could potentially lead to improved results, as the global optimization approach may not always find the optimal split at the last level of the tree.

It is also important to evaluate whether the global optimization approach increases overfitting. The authors provide some evidence that their approach can lead to reduced overfitting, but a more thorough analysis is needed to fully understand the impact of global optimization on overfitting.

Overall, this paper presents a novel and interesting approach to decision tree optimization, and the authors provide a clear and well-written explanation of their method. While the experimental results are somewhat disappointing, the paper is likely to spur further research in this area, and the authors' suggestions for future work are well-taken.
