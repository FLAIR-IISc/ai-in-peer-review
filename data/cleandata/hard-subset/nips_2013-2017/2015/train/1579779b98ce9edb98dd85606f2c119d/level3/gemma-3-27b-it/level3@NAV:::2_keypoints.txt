- Tackles joint training of decision tree splits and leaf posteriors with a novel upper-bound-based optimization; motivation to improve over greedy induction is strong.  
- Paper is clear and well written, and shows improvements over greedy baselines.  
- Theoretical reasoning about the empirical loss bound is shaky; provided example suggests the claimed upper bound may not always hold.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 in the supplementary material appears incomplete.  
- The fixed-assignment heuristic is weakly justified; alternatives like EM or CCCP were not considered or explained.  
- The necessity and impact of the efficient loss-augmented inference are not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Experiments only explore small tree depths (≤16), where brute-force is feasible, questioning the benefit of restricting search to a Hamming ball.  
- Clarification is needed on what is meant by “leaves” in the inference discussion.  
- A crucial baseline is missing: greedy training of non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization.  
- Pre-specified/fixed tree depth is a limitation that needs more discussion and experiments varying depth.  
- Experimental validation is insufficient overall; runtime comparisons and deeper tree exploration are recommended.  
- Minor suggestion: replace `argmax` with `max` in the loss bound expression.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting and potentially valuable but current theoretical and experimental issues lead the reviewer to lean toward rejection, open to reconsideration after a strong rebuttal.
