- Novel formulation: decision tree optimization posed as a global optimization problem with a fixed tree structure.  
- Method: introduces a non-convex relaxation that allows use of stochastic gradient descent for optimization.  
- Empirical result: method outperforms greedy decision tree baselines on several benchmarks but gains are modest.  
- Generalization: provides evidence of better generalization and reduced overfitting, though evidence is limited.  
- Limitation: non-convex relaxation can lead to local optima and may not find the true optimum.  
- Limitation: no thorough analysis of computational complexity or scalability to large datasets.  
- Suggested improvement: combine global optimization with standard re-optimization (e.g., drop and re-optimize last split) to boost performance.  
- Suggested evaluation: more comprehensive analysis of whether global optimization increases or decreases overfitting.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Strength: experiments demonstrate the method’s effectiveness, and the paper is well-organized.  
- Weakness: empirical improvements over greedy methods are underwhelming.  
- Weakness: missing complexity analysis and incomplete treatment of overfitting concerns.  
- Originality: novel approach—using non-convex relaxation + SGD for decision tree optimization.  
- Significance: likely to stimulate further research and has potential to improve tree-based methods.  
- Acceptance case: novel, clearly presented method with supporting experiments.  
- Rejection case: modest empirical gains and missing analyses (complexity, overfitting).  
- Recommendation: accept but request addressing limitations and providing more thorough complexity and overfitting analyses.
