This paper proposes a novel approach to decision tree learning by framing the search for the best fixed-depth decision tree as a structured prediction problem. The authors establish a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables, and formulate a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss. This bound is optimized using stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to find a locally optimal configuration of the split functions.

The paper's main contributions are: (1) a new formulation of decision tree learning that associates a binary latent decision variable with each split node in the tree, and (2) a novel optimization algorithm that uses SGD to minimize the upper bound on the empirical loss. The algorithm is shown to be efficient and scalable, with a time complexity of O(d2p), where d is the tree depth and p is the number of features.

The authors also present empirical results on several benchmark datasets, demonstrating that their non-greedy decision tree algorithm outperforms typical greedy tree-building methods on test accuracy. The improvements are generally small, but the authors argue that their approach provides a more principled way of optimizing decision trees, and that the norm regularization used in their algorithm provides a natural way to control the tightness of the tree's fit to the training data.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to decision tree learning, which is distinct from traditional greedy methods.

* The optimization algorithm is shown to be efficient and scalable, with a time complexity of O(d2p).
