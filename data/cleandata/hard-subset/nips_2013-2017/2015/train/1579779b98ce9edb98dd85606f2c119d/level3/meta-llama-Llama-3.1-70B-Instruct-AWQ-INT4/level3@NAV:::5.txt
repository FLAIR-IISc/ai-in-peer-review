This paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions by optimizing an upper bound on empirical loss, regularized with L2 and optimized via stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The authors establish a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables, and formulate a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed approach. The authors provide a thorough review of related work and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several benchmark datasets. The use of SGD for optimization enables effective training with large datasets, and the introduction of a regularizer on the norm of W helps to prevent overfitting.

However, there are some concerns regarding the experimental results. While the authors claim that their non-greedy decision trees outperform greedy decision tree baselines, the results are not convincing on all datasets. In some cases, the performance difference is not significant, and the computational cost of the proposed approach is higher than that of the greedy methods.

Additionally, there are some minor issues with the paper. The description of the stable SGD algorithm is unclear, and the authors should provide more details on how data points are assigned to leaves and a pseudo-code for the procedure. The figure captions are also unclear, and the authors should specify whether the results use SGD or stable SGD and define what an "active" leaf means.

Overall, the paper proposes an interesting approach to decision tree learning, but the experimental results need to be more convincing to demonstrate its effectiveness.

**Strengths:**

* The paper proposes a novel algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions.
* The authors establish a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.
* The use of SGD for optimization enables effective training with large datasets.
* The introduction of a regularizer on the norm of W helps to prevent overfitting.

**Weaknesses:**

* The experimental results are not convincing on all datasets.
* The computational cost of the proposed approach is higher than that of the greedy methods.
* The description of the stable SGD algorithm is unclear.
* The figure captions are unclear.

**Acceptance:** Borderline

**Recommendations:**

* Provide more details on the stable SGD algorithm and a pseudo-code for the procedure.
* Clarify the figure captions and define what an "active" leaf means.
* Provide more convincing experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* Consider reducing the computational cost of the proposed approach to make it more competitive with greedy methods.
