This paper proposes a novel approach to decision tree learning, framing the search for the best fixed-depth decision tree as a structured prediction problem. The authors establish a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables, formulating the tree's empirical loss as a convex-concave upper bound. This approach allows for the joint optimization of split functions and leaf parameters according to a global objective, promoting cooperation between split nodes and leading to better generalization performance.

The paper presents a thorough analysis of the problem, including a clear formulation of the decision tree learning problem, a discussion of related work, and a detailed description of the proposed approach. The authors also provide a comprehensive evaluation of their method, comparing it to several greedy baselines on multiple benchmark datasets.

Strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed approach is novel and well-motivated, addressing a significant limitation of traditional greedy decision tree induction methods.
* The authors provide a clear and concise formulation of the decision tree learning problem, making it easy to follow their reasoning.
* The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness on multiple benchmark datasets.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* The computational complexity of the proposed approach is high, especially for deep trees, which may limit its applicability in practice.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the time complexity of their algorithm, which would be helpful in understanding its scalability.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of decision tree learning, proposing a novel approach that addresses a major limitation of traditional methods. While there are some limitations to the approach, the authors provide a thorough evaluation and discussion of the results, making this a strong paper.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and well-motivated approach to decision tree learning.
* The authors provide a clear and concise formulation of the decision tree learning problem.
* The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating its effectiveness on multiple benchmark datasets.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The computational complexity of the proposed approach is high, especially for deep trees.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the time complexity of their algorithm.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should provide a more detailed analysis of the time complexity of their algorithm and discuss the implications of the high computational complexity on the applicability of their approach in practice.
