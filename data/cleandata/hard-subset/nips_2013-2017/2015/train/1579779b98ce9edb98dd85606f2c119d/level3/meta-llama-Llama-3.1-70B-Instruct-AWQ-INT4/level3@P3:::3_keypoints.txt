- Proposes framing fixed-depth decision tree learning as a structured prediction problem with latent variables.  
- Introduces a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and a non-greedy SGD-based optimization to find locally optimal split configurations.  
- Method (non-greedy decision tree learning) links decision-tree optimization to structured prediction and enables efficient optimization techniques.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and explains relation to prior work effectively.  
- Provides a thorough computational-complexity analysis and shows efficiency even for deep trees.  
- Experimental evaluation on benchmarks shows the method outperforms greedy baselines in test accuracy, though improvements are generally small.  
- Main contribution is the structured-prediction formulation enabling SGD for tree learning.  
- Limitation: experiments and formulation are restricted to binary classification; multi-class extension is claimed but not demonstrated.  
- Limitation: method requires careful tuning of hyperparameters (regularization ν, learning rate η) with no sensitivity analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient, effective approach with strong complexity analysis and empirical evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited to binary tasks and unclear hyperparameter sensitivity/generalization to multi-class.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
